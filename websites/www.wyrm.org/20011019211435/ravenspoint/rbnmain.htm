the Moonlit Trod Club

Arts and Entertainment - WWMoonlitTrodClubRbN - Local Changeling Freehold and club, owned by Autumn Lander. Mortal Perspective- (In the window is a sign stating"Help Wanted") As you enter through the double doors, you step into the foyer of a stylish brick edificeknown as the Moonlit Trod Club. You then walk down two steps, which reach the oak dance floor of the club. There are tables, set for 2-6 people and a few lounge chairs set around the edge of the dance floor. The lights are low, with many silver, star shaped lights hanging from the ceiling, and other tiny lights which seem to float like fireflies, but a majority of the illumination coming from a large fireplace (one a person could stand in) at the back of the room. On each side of the fireplace are sets of doors. The doors to your left lead to the kitchens, room with a pool table and smaller bar, and restroom areas, while the door to the right is marked "Personnel Only." This door leads to the owner's living quarters on the top floor and business office and music room on the bottom floor. On the right-hand wall, there is a bar and bartender, at the customers' service. The walls are painted aquamarine near the floor, which proceeds to a deep blue, then violet as it reaches a violet/black ceiling. There are plants and flowers dotted at equal intervals around the edges of the room, actually planted into round circular  depressions filled with soil in the floor of the Club.
Changeling/ Enchanted Perspective:
            ONLY THOSE OF THE DREAMING CAN VIEW THIS LOOK AT THE CLUB!
(Help wanted sign is still there, only it is now made of wood and engraved in calligraphic lettering) As you enter through the double doors, you step into the foyer area of a stylish brick edifice known as the Moonlit Trod Club. In the foyer area there is a space for wet cloaks, and a door to the side where any chimerical animals may be kept or doctored, if need be. The lighting in the club is still dim, but warmth emanates from the large hearth on the back wall. Hanging over the hearth is a shield bearing the arms of House Fiona, as well as a crossed silver broadsword and bronze rapier. On each side of the fireplace are sets of doors, with wrought bronze hinges in the shape of lion's claws. The room that served as a game room is the Duchess's private audience chamber. Tapestries hang along the walls, depicting the key events in Changeling history, such as the Shattering and the Resurgence. The dance floor is covered in marble, and surrounding it are chairs and tables of the finest quality mahogany. The plants and flowers turn into columns of the Corinthian style, that support the high ceiling. 

The City Park

Arts and Entertainment - WWCityPark RbN - The park is large. Very large. It is possible to see from one side of the lake to the other, but not at night and even during the day someone's identity would be completely hidden if they were at the other end of the park. There are a number of paths, an old fashioned playground off to one side. There are benches in grassy areas which the paths pass through, looking out on the water. Or you could take a walk through the woods, which may not be old growth, but have grown up into a dense second growth forest, in which it is easy to get lost should you go too far. There are two large meadows, and on a hill that looks down to the lake there is a memorial for all those from the city who died in World War II, as well as a place to sit and feed the pigeons, of which there are a prodigeous amount.

Ravenspoint beach

Arts and Entertainment - WWTheBeach RbN - The Ravenspoint beach is a beautiful bit of land that stretches for three miles along the coast until reaching the rocky Ravenspoint cliffs. The sand is gone over by the sand-sweeping machines twice daily, and the ocean is a sparkling blue that makes for wonderful sunrises and sunsets. There is a boardwalk  beginning at the aquarium and ending near the cliffs and private beach homes there. There are a few piers jutting out over the ocean, and down at the aquarium end, past the aquarium itself, is the harbor.
The boardwalk itself is a nice place, with games and food stands and all sorts of booths. It is a popular area to be in, and is usually fairly crowded from 10 AM until after the stars come out. There are regular police patrols to ensure security, but the area isn't exactly high priority. Have fun!

NighthawksDiner

Arts and Entertainment - WWNighthawksDinerRbN - Nighthawk's has been in Ravenspoint since somewhere around the beginning of time. It's a nice place, a "greasy spoon," as they're called. Inside there's music playing, usually "oldies" rock or jazz. The juke is chrome and glass and plays single records, probably dating back to the sixties or so. There are booths and chrome stools at the counter to sit at, no chairs on their own. All the upholstery is in red leather. Behind the counter Fred, (7'6") is always engaged in some activity, from cleaning to performing the job of short-order cook or waiter. Fred takes care of hamburgers, fries, and the occasional grilled cheese or hot dog. He manages drinks, and they have a full bar. For everything else he calls the orders to the kitchen.

Rules of Conduct

Character sheets for visitors are not required unless you intend to engage in some kind of confllict with the other players.  Don't expect to start trouble in the rooms with an unapproved character v.s. an approved one and survive if you don't have your running shoes on.

The Veil and Masquerade are enforced here.  Please do not bring blatant veil rippers and mask breakers to the RbN and expect them to last too very long.  White Wolf 2nd ed. and Revised Ed are used whenever possible.  Mage, Werewolf, Vampire, Sorcerer and Changeling and some of the Eastern style creatures are acceptable PC types.   Wraith is not accepted as a PC type.  Hunters are currently being play-tested here, with the understanding that hunting the other PCs is not really going to endear them to anyone and they could be reduced to NPC only status at any time.

Any character can be refused to be cleared at any time.  The STs do not have to give a reason - if we do not want a certain type of character in our stories, they will not be allowed.  This does not mean your character cannot come in and chat in character - merely that they will not be actually taking part in any action scenes without the consent of everyone involved.

NPCs listed in room descriptions are for all players, to enable them to limitedly run those characters.  Do not assume they are either human or inhuman.  Just have them get your coffee or fix your car.  Not everyone is here to be part of the plot.  They also serve who stand and wait.

Time: When entering a room, assume that it is in Eastern Standard time.  If it is 3:30 pm, it is 3:30 pm.  That means, Vampires, take note that when the sun's up in RL, it's up in the game rooms.  Vampires who wish to play during the day may open a room and tell anyone who enters the time in that room.  Vampires may NOT enter an ongoing scene and force the players already there to move the time ahead.   They can ASK.  But they should not expect to get their way.  It has been ruled that if you foolishly insist on entering a gameroom while the sun is up, the other characters in the room may get out marshmallows and toast them on you as you burn.
