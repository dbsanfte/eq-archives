Experience Points

All characters begin with zero experience points unless rewarded starting experience points based on rank, as determined by the current guild master (GM).

The bonus earned experience point rule presented in the Player’s Handbook is not used for this campaign.

Please note that Specialty Priests (as per Faiths and Avatars) use the Druid Experience Point table for advancement.

Races, Classes, and Kits

Characters may be any race presented in the Solon races section. The GM must approve exotic character races. Characters may be single or multi-classed, regardless of race. Multi-classed characters may split their experience points between their classes in any way (they are not required to do an even split). You may choose one kit for your character from the published handbooks that is appropriate for your character.

You can not create your character from the Skills and Powers text. This book is a rough draft, at best. Any psionics must follow the rules presented in this text, however.

Psionic wild talents are NOT used for this campaign setting.

New Attribute -- Perception

All characters have a seventh attribute or stat, Perception, which is used to determine surprise in combat situations and to determine what particulars a character may notice in any given situation.

Hit Points

All characters begin with 75% of their maximum rolled Hit Points. For example, a ninth level Fighter could have a maximum of 90 rolled Hit Points, therefore the fighter in this campaign would have 75% of 90 (67.5, rounded up to 68), plus any additional Hit Points for Constitution.
