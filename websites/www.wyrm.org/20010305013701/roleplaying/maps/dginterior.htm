The Interior of the Dragongate Inn

A Note to all Rafter Leapers - THERE ARE NO OPEN BEAMS!!  If you jump in the rafters in this in and you are more than 1 ft tall, you are going to brain yourself pretty good.  The 2nd floor is making a ceiling to the tavern and resteraunt area of the in.  If you start leaping around in the rafers, you are going to encounter laughter and ridicule if you do not heed the 'no rafter' warning.  If you want to jump in the rafters, try the barn.  No one serves drinks there, however.

There is always at LEAST one bouncer on duty at any given time.   Don't make him or her come and get you.

NO ONE but staff is allowed in the Kitchen unless being escourted thru by one of the staff.  Don't break the rule or the Kitchen staff will assume you are the latest food delivery.  Don't try the patience of the Head Cook, Bill.  He demands privacy when he's cooking.

Floors are sturdy wood planking, sanded smooth.  No rushes on the floor.  Walls are stone.  There are, for those of you who see magical writings, invisible runes inscribed on all the windows (all windows come equipped with sturdy shutters) and over all the lintels. 

OOC: coinage in Onaris
in the dragon gate area, copper peices are divided into 10 bits.   one gold is equal to 20 silvers is equal to 200 coppers. to remember it easily, think of a copper peice as a dime, a silver as a buck, and a gold as 20 bucks.  HOWEVER, value is a little different when you consider what it will buy. in those terms, since a copper will buy quite a bit compared to our modern dime, you might want to think of a copper as being around a dollar in value.
