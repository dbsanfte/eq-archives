1's and 20's

A combat ("to hit" or parry) roll of "1" is always a miss and a natural 20 is always a hit or successful parry.  Likewise, a proficiency check or perception check roll of "1" is always successful, and a 20 is always failure.

Critical Hits

A critical hit is a successful natural attack roll of 18 or higher that hits and opponent’s AC by 5 or more. A critical hit doubles dice damage (only the dice, not the bonuses). With the High Mastery skill (single-classed warriors only), a character can score a critical hit with a 16 or higher.

Death and Dying

From 0 to -10 Hit Points (HP), a character is "hovering on death’s door." The character is unconscious and dying: he or she will lose 1 HP per round until he or she reaches -11 and is dead. Raising and Resurrection is (nearly) impossible. These spells do not exist except as direct intervention by a deity. If a player character dies, the death is permanent unless the DM creates an adventure that would justify the return of the character (a very special case). Reincarnation is possible as a way to cheat death, but as it circumvents the Powers, the results are usually only a partial success (as per the spell description).

Firearms

Firearms are not available or operative in this campaign setting.

Gating

Extraplanar creatures may be summoned or banished from Solon, but while on the plane they may not use their gate abilities to summon reinforcements and so forth.  Only high level mages, clerics, and powerful magical items may summon past planar boundaries.

Level Draining

Level draining abilities do not exist in this campaign setting. Instead, DMs are encouraged to replace this ability with something equally or more dangerous (blindness, again, disease, paralyzation, insanity, and whatever else a devious DM can imagine).

Local Fighting Style

Any character can choose to take the Local Fighting Style weapon proficiency as per page 78 of Combat and Tactics. This style confers only one of the possible bonuses listed and only under certain conditions, as per the description. Only one fighting style can be used per round.

Material Spell Components

These are not used in this campaign setting.

Magical Item Saving Throws

If the character survives, so do the character’s magical items. If a character is killed by something that could affect his or her items (fireball, disintegration, etc.) the DM should require item saving throws. This applies to NPC’s in particular (an enemy wizard slain by a fireball should not have much left to loot).

Maximums

A character’s THAC0 and Saving Throws can never be better than "1" including all bonuses. A character’s Armor Class can never improve beyond -10, including a situational and magical bonuses.

Poison

Lethal poison which requires a character to make a save or perish does not exist in this campaign. Lethal poison reduced the character’s Hit Points to zero (0) minus d8 and the character is dying (losing 1 HP per round).

Protection Items

Magical protection items (such as Rings and Cloaks of Protection) will function together; however, a character can only use a maximum of two (2) such items at any given time. Additional items beyond two (2) provide no benefit.

Scrolls

A 9th level or greater Priest or Wizard can make a scroll of any spell they know how to cast. A scroll requires one (1) day of work (about 8 hours) per level of the spell to prepare. The spell is then cast into the scroll. Protection scrolls require ten (10) days to make and may require special preparations (consult the DM).

Two-Handed Weapon Style

Normally this provides an initiative bonus for weapons that require two hands to use. Since we are using an alternative system where weapon speed factors are not used, this style provides a +1 "to hit" and damage, if chosen. If a character uses two weapon proficiency slots in this skill, the bonus is increased to +2 (the maximum for this skill).

Weapon Damage

The master list presented in the Combat and Tactics text is the official damage list for weapons.

Weapon Specialization

A non-warrior may have Proficiency in a weapon. For an additional weapon proficiency slot, a non-warrior may declare a "Weapon of Choice", granting a +1 "to hit" and damage, but no other bonuses. A non-warrior may only have one weapon of choice. A warrior (single or multi-classed) may have expertise in a weapon. Expertise provides the same number of attacks per round as a specialist, but without any other bonuses. A warrior (single or multi-classed) may also declare a weapon he or she holds expertise in as a "weapon of choice", as indicated above. A warrior (single or multi-classed) may only have one weapon of choice, but may gain expertise in any number of weapons, as limited by weapon proficiencies. A single calss warrior may specialize or master one weapon (as per the Combat and Tactics rulebook). A character can choose any number of fighting styles, but can only use one fighting style at a time. Combining two or more styles is not possible.
