The Charter of the Twilight Company

Mission statement:

We are a guild building off the ashes of a few former guilds; a twilight mixture of races, classes, and alignments, bound together by friendship and mutual support.

Our aims are to hunt and raid together and to provide crafting support. We consider courtesy to be paramount in dealings with others.   

Many of the guild members come from a roleplaying background, so we encourage roleplaying as much as possible, and discourage obvious out of character talk in the open channels.

We will understand that this is a game, only a game, that it does not come before our lives, and that a sense of humor is a good thing to have.   Above all, the Company must have fun. 

Officers duties and titles (who's in charge)
Officers are appointed by the Guildmaster - simularly, they can for any reason have their officership revoked.   Junion officers are appointed to assist the guildmaster in guilding and are recognized as officers but have no official duties other than to guild people they know are to join the Company.

Qualifications to join (how do you get in)..
We have no level limit, but!! ...we only take applications from those we've hunted with, as in you have to be sponsored into the guild by a current guildmember. Real Life Friends are acceptable.  People should not expect to receive any sort of guild handmedowns of any great worth until they have attained a level where their corpse will not vanish after 30 minutes. We also do not take level into account for purposes of deciding rank or worth to the guild, the idea is to be a 'fun' guild, not an 'uber' guild.

Proceedures for being disbarred or leaving the guild. (how do you get out?)
We will not tolerate members dragging our name through the mud and any who do so will be punished.  Expulsion of the offending character, then of all of the characters of that one player, or reparation of damages at your own cost are some of the possible punishments. 

Anyone who wants out may leave at any time, we are not expecting people to make some kind of lifelong commitment.  However, circumstances of your leaving will determine whether or not you get a welcome back later.  

If you leave to go on a Raid and join another guild temporarily, that is acceptable, but please remember to let us know by email, the forum, or tell an officer so that it may be noted.  Suddenly seeing a former member wearing another guildtag is something of a shock to the system.

 

The charter is not carved in stone. Things can change at any time.  The Company is a living, breathing thing and is not static.
