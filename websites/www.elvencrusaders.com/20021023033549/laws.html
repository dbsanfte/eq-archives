New Member Recruitment Guidelines

No more alts will be allowed into the guild based solely on the fact that they have a member in EC.

All alts must adhere to the same requirements as new recruits.

All new recruits are required to post their interest in the guild and their personal stats to the guild board.

Then an officer will contact them in game.

Minimum level requirement is level 15.

Characters under 15 are welcome to apply for the Elven Recruits. The recruits are a training and proving ground for young Elven Crusaders. Level requirments for recruits is 1-25. Between 15 and 25 they either graduate to Crusaders or leave the Recruits.

This gives characters a chance to decide how they will play that character.
It also gives them enough time in the PVP environment to understand how to fight. By lvl 15

most have decided where they stand as far as being PK or Anti.

NEW 12-8-01-Officers will not share their Officer character with anyone else. This is for security and diplomatic reasons. Officership brings with it alot of responsiblitites. Sharing Officer characters with others could lead to demotion or removal from the guild.

NEW 12-10-01-All Officers must visit the new Officers Guild Message board at least once a week and post.

NEW 12-10-01-All Officers must attend Officer meetings. Meetings will be once a month. Three missed meetings and you will lose officer status.

NEW 12-10-01 All current and prospective members are required to read the laws page and click the link at the bottom of the page aknowledging that they accept and will abide by the laws of the Elven Crusaders.

A list of questions will be asked of all new recruits prior to induction into the guild.

 

1-Why do you want to join the Elven Crusaders?

2-Have you been in any other guilds, which ones?

3-Have you ever PKd before (random pk, not self defense etc...)

4-What is it that you expect from the Elven Crusaders as a Guild?

5-Are you willing to obey the rules of the guild and our officers?

6-Are you an elf? Class, Race, Level?

7-Will you visit the guild web site at least once a week? (a requirement)

 

It is also required that a prospective member spend some time grouping with our members

 and or be well known and trusted by a member.

A recommendation from a guild mate or one of our allies is necessary to join the Elven Crusaders.

 

General Guild Policies

The Elven Crusaders are an aggressive anti PK Elven only guild.

We KOS known PK's, PK Guilds, personal enemies of our members and of the Elven Crusaders as a Guild.

The Elven Crusaders members are to be honorable at all times. Treat each other and our allies with respect at all times.

Elven Crusaders DO NOT BEG. Our members are very generous and will offer items to those who are deserving of them.

If you are in need of an item you can post your need on the trade board and if someone can help they will contact you in game.

We use the PK's tactics to take them down but we live by a different code.

 We do not corpse camp (wait till they have their weapon before you kill the PK again).

We do not get involved in zone shout wars. Zone shouts only serves to make the PK look bad. If anything at all needs to be said in the zones, let it be "Another PK has been slain by the Elven Crusaders".

We do not use foul language or cuss and avoid street slang as much as possible. We are a Role Playing guild.

When grouping with each other be courteous. If their is no specified loot order, only loot every 5th or 6th time depending on the amount of people in your group. For example, if there are 4 people in your group, loot every fourth time. Of course if mobs are left to rot, ask the group if it is ok to loot. Never loot before all of the mobs in the battle are dead. Your group mates lives could be at risk.

If there are any disputes among guild mates, please, contact myself, puzzikat or any officer to have it settled diplomatically. We do not want anyone to be upset about something and not have it resolved. If you feel something is wrong in the guild, let it be known to someone who can do something about it. We are friends as well as guild mates. Lets do what we can to help each other. Group as often as possible together and lend a hand when you can. No one expects that you should leave your current group to go and buff or port or anything like that, but when you have time it is nice. Most of us already do that, but I wanted to reinforce the idea.
