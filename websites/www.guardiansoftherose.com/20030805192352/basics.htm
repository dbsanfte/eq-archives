Here you will find our recruiting policy, guild rules and Raid rules.

The guild rules are basic, simple and should be easy for all.  They exist for the benefit of all.

First off...  Recruitment.

At this time we are recruiting only levels 45 and higher.

Our recruiting policy has changed recently and is as follows:

All new members will be with us for 2 weeks on a "probationary" basis to begin with.  During this time you should talk to other members, group with them if you can and cooperate in guild events (if any are scheduled during this timeframe).  Be sure to follow game rules and follow raiding instruction and just basically get along.

After your 2 week "probation" is up, we will confer to speak about how you are getting along in the guild.  As long as you haven't been a nuisance or a disruption to the rest of the game, followed the rules and got along with the rest of the guild we can consider you a permanent member of our family and at this time we will take in any of your alternate characters regardless of level.

Guild Policy

1. Use your common sense.

2. Know the game's rules and play with good etiquette.  Kill-Stealing and being a nuisance to other players and being a compromise to other gamers' fun  will get you reprimanded and/or removed.

Advice:

     a.)  If you have an issue with another player, keep it out of public channels, please restrict it to /tell.  No one wants to hear  people ranting about other people, and badmouthing someone in /OOC makes you look like an ass too.  I can't tell you how many times I have walked through a zone and been spammed with an argument I didn't want to hear.  And how childish and foolish the parties appeared to me and all others around.

     b.)  Noting the above... there is a /petition command, use it if you feel you have a problem that talking out with your adversary isn't going to fix, such as harassment by them, repeated KSing (and KSing isn't someone walking into your "camp" and taking an UNENGAGED mob before you can pull it), ninja-looting, repeated training.  GM's WILL investigate these things if you send them a /petition.  If you have a person who is harassing you /report them and petition a GM and then use /ignore.  It's as simple as that.  These functions were added to the game for a reason.

     c.)  Share the problem with the guild if you feel you need to talk about it....  vent, but don't let the problem take over.  Also an eye-for-an-eye, getting back at soandso by repeating on them whet they have done for you isn't the way to go.

3.  If you have an issue about another guild mate, please leave it out of guild chat.  Keep it to yourself and try to work it out between the two of you.  If you need help working out the issue contact one of the guild officers or leaders via /tell and we can attempt to work out the situation in a private channel or even if you want us to assist before the issue gets tough contact us.  We do not want a war inside our ranks, we will assist where we can.

4. Try to help out your fellow guildies.  (we do have community property, and low characters can be assisted, please assist if you have extra equipment)

5. Play nice.  We do have people contact us regarding our guildies if they act inappropriately.  We do not ask for people to watch our guildies, but most people in the game know a member of another guild somewhere and word spreads.  Bring us a good name.

6. Make an attempt to participate in guild events (you don't have to do all events, it's not a requirement, but it helps the rest of us if you try).  Raids are fun, but not alone hehe.  We know you have lives, so if you want to raid, please work with us all to set up times that work for everyone.  If you cannot come, please give notice so we can work things around the open space.  Other members need raids to help them get their Epic pieces, you will too eventually.  Most people will be reluctant to assist someone who has not helped out once or twice so at least keep that in mind when events come about.

7.  Members MUST register with our message board.  All raids and guild events are posted there a week ahead of time.  Contact me Ostarra for passwords or registration info.

8.  Members are encouraged to /gu speak often.  This will help you make friends faster.   We don't ignore /gu tells, but will miss tells if we are busy.  If you have something to say, go ahead and say it, if we don't respond, don't feel discouraged.  If you need help, ask, if you don't get a response, Try again =).   We try to help out when we are not busy, but of course there are days where we can't pull away from what we are involved in. 

9.  Guild Grouping.... 

     a.)  Experience groups... Loot system is pretty much FFA (free for all) depending on the area.  It's up to the party of course, but most often we FFA in xp grouping.

     b.)  Loot groups...  Turn based looting or ML assigned.  Hard drops are rolls. 

     c.)  Raid Groups...  Need before Greed, if you don't need it, you don't get it unless it's a duplicate.  ML always assigned.  Rolls on drops over need.  Epic pieces rolled on between raiding class in need.

     d.)  These are basic rules, but are subject to change during the events or situations.  We work to make things amicable for all involved.

10.  HAVE FUN!  This is a game and that's what it's about.   Don't take things too seriously, it's not worth the aggrivation.

 

Raid Rules

You will have your job during a raid and you will be informed of what will be needed of you.  Please stick to what is asked of you.

Assist who you are asked to assist.  The person pulling is not necessarily the person you want to assist as puller may be off scouting another mob at the time you assist, reason being, we don't want you sending off a pet only to have it get killed by whatever they were targeting at the time and have an angry mob we aren't prepared for come back to eat it's owner.

Keep /rs spam to a minimum, unless it has to do with the raid situation (chat in /gu for random other stuff so everyone can weed out what they need to see in /rs).  If you have an idea, or disagree with raid leader's decision, do not state so in /rs, send the Raid leader a direct tell and they will keep your advice in mind.

Most important thing during a raid is to follow the instructions given by the raid leader.  Going your own way can mean your life or the life of your raid members.
