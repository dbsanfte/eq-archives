Applying for membership

The first step in applying for membership is to post an "application" on the Recruiting Forum. Please include the following information:

Character Name:

Class:

Level:

Any Alternate characters you might want guilded:

Typical Play time (both weekday and weekend):

Time Zone:

Everquest Experience (i.e. how long you have been playing, etc.):

Past Guild Experience (please include any guilds you are currently a member of):

How did you hear about Foundation Rathe:

What members have you adventured with:

After you have submitted an application, you must contact me (Mithandriel) in game so I can go over some fine points about our guild (some of which will be explained below).  As some point we will have an informal "interview" so that I can get to know you better.

Sponsored Applicants

We prefer that applicants be somewhat familiar with our guild and know at least one member who would act as your sponsor.  A sponsor is someone that you know fairly well and can attest to your ability as a player and your character as a person.  If you are interested in joining Foundation Rathe and you think someone will sponsor you, please contact that person and ask them if they would be willing to do that. Assuming the person is willing to sponsor you, please be sure to include that in your application.

As a side note, I take the word and opinions of my members very seriously so having a sponsor makes admission into FR much easier.

Non-Sponsored Applicants

We certainly welcome all members of the Everquest gaming community to apply to Foundation Rathe even if they do not know any members personally or have only had limited exposure to us or our members.  Please follow the link above to our forum and fill out an application.

Raid Participation - Currently not required

Before an applicant can be admitted for membership, they will be expected to attend either some of our scheduled events, informal xp groups or just do some plain old adventuring with our members. Please take a look at the Foundation Rathe Raid Calendar to see what events we have coming up.  If you plan on attending a scheduled event you must contact me (Mithandriel) prior to the day of the event to let me know you are attending. If I am not available, you can contact any member of FR and let them know you are applying for membership and wish to attend a specific raid(s) and they will then inform me.

It will be the responsibility of the applicant to stay informed and contact us about raids. We will not hunt people down to let them know when and where we are raiding.

There is no specific number of required raids that need to be attended. That will vary by applicant and depend on how much you interact with our members during the raid(s) and what type of impression you make.  In general, sponsored applicants are usually admitted after less raids than a non-sponsored applicant.

Becoming a Probationary Membership

The final decision whether to accept a member is made by me and is based on sponsorship, input from members as well as my impression of the applicant from our conversations.  Once I determine that the applicant is of acceptable caliber, they will become a probationary member.

The probationary period will last for 30 days during which time the member will be further evaluated by me and the members of the guild. It is expected while online, a probationary member will make every effort to attend as many raids as possible as well as interact with other members on non-raid days.  The probationary member must also reach level 20 before they may become a full member.

A probationary member can be removed at the end of the 30 day period or at an earlier time if they prove that they are not FR material. Reasons why a probationary member would be removed can range from breaking a guild rule (intentional KSing, training, camp stealing, etc.) or failure to attend sufficient raids while online.  The probationary period may also be extended if the member is not online with enough frequency or does not attend enough raids.

Raid Points/Looting

Loot decisions are based on a point system however need is also taken into consideration.  Members begin accumulating points as soon as they are formally inducted into the guild as a probationary member.  Points are given for attending raids, participating in guild xp groups, helping members as well as other reasons. Points are then deducted for items that are awarded during a raid or "purchased" from the guild vault.  Probationary members may not "purchase" an item from the guild vault and are only entitled to items that drop during a raid that they attended.

Dual Membership  

Members of Foundation Rathe are not permitted to have dual membership in any competing guild. Members are permitted to have characters in other guilds that we have formal or informal alliances with. Some exceptions may be made with "non-competing" guilds and will be made on a case-by-case basis.  

Prospective members should indicate ANY current guild affiliations with any characters they play prior to applying for membership and then a determination will be made whether that dual membership will be acceptable or not.

Level/Class Requirements

At this time, the level requirement for joining Foundation Rathe is 15th level or comparable Everquest experience (i.e. experience from other servers, higher level characters, etc.) .

There is also no class restrictions either. We do welcome all classes.

General Information

Epics - Foundation Rathe is committed to helping it's members complete their epic weapon quests as much as possible.  With that in mind there may be certain epics that may not be able to be completed at this time or in the near future or other epics where there are several members still waiting to complete theirs.

Velious Faction - Foundation Rathe is mainly aligned with the dwarves of Thurgadin and at war with the giants of Kael Drakkel.  Our main goal is the completion of the various Thurgadin class-based armor quests as well as the Tunare quests found in the Plane of Growth. We will also at times hunt in PoG however those of good alignment will not be forced to ruin that faction.

As a secondary result of fighting the giants, we are partially aligned with the Claws of Veeshan however we do hunt the dragons of Velious at this time.  It is expected at a later date when we decide to hunt in the Temple of Veeshan that we will commit fully to the CoV faction.

Members of Foundation Rathe are expected to improve their Thurgadin faction and actively work on the various quests available there.  If you have already improved your Kael Drakkel faction to a point where you are able to do quests there and do not wish to change factions, then that must be discussed with me during the admissions process.

Luclin - At this time, there are no specific faction plans that center around Luclin.  As more information becomes available about the various factions and the pros and cons of each faction, a  plan will be made at that time. We as a guild plan to explore and adventure in the various Luclin zones in the near future.

Tips/What we expect - I'll just give a few ideas about what we look for and what is expected of people that wish to join our guild.

What we look for:

  • We look for members that want to be part of a team/family based environment. With that in mind, we are looking for people that want to work together and adventure with their guild all the time and not only at the times that is beneficial to them.  We expect the highest level of participation from our members as well as a commitment to the guild.  It is only through team work and commitment that any guild will thrive.
  • We look for mature members (this has nothing to do with chronological age). We want members that are mature enough to understand why things are done and why specific rules are in place. We also want members that will follow directions.
  • FR fully supports the RTA and the various calendars and rotations that are set up for the mutual benefit of the Rathe community. Those members of the Rathe that do not want to abide by the rules laid out by RTA should seek another guild.

What we do not want:

  • We do not want members that are only out for themselves or only join FR because they think they can receive loot or help on their agenda only.  If you are someone that expects to receive priority in loot or must have your epic now, then FR is probably not the place for you.  
  • FR also believes strongly in appropriate player conduct. We do not support nor do we tolerate any form of kill stealing, camp stealing, intentional training, invoking PNP (except if no other solution can be found) or any other activity that is generally frowned upon by the Rathe community. If you are someone that has participated in these activities knowingly, then FR is probably not the guild for you.

Some tips:

  • Be open and honest - If you had a bad reputation or had a falling out with your prior guild, then be open about it. Everyone has a past and while some are more colored than others, we try to give everyone the benefit of the doubt. Being honest shows that you want to get past that and be judged on who you are today.  If something has happened in your past that we need to know about, we will probably find out eventually.
  • Be yourself - Don't try to act differently to impress people. Just be yourself and act accordingly. If you are the right fit for the guild that will show and everything will go much smoother.  
  • Loot Whores - There is probably no bigger turn off than a loot whore. The first question of an invite should not be "will I get loot." This only shows that you are more interested in getting loot than it is about you getting to know the guild.  You may get loot but that should be of secondary concern to interacting and showing the guild what you are made of.

I look forward to hearing from you.
