Foundation Rathe Loot Rules

Overview

Looting is based on an integrated point system across all classes.  This system is commonly known as a "Dragon Kill point" or DKP system.  There are several different types of DKP systems. Some are based on a bid system where members bid points and the highest bidder receives the item. There are also straight point systems where the person with the highest point total receives the item. Foundation Rathe uses a modified straight point system where need is also factored into the equation.  This will be explained below.

Accumulating points

A member will receive points for various guild activities.  These activities range from general raid points, XP groups to specific "kill" points.  The points will be described below.

General Raid - .40 per hour

XP group - .50 points per day (must have at least 4 guild members for a minimum of 2 hours)

Helping a guildmate with epic progress or completing a quest - .25 points (assuming this is something that only requires 1-2 groups or less).

Kill Points - These will vary by the specific target and will only be awarded for successful slaying of that target.  The value of the specific NPC will be based on the difficulty as well as importance of that NPC (loot, quest implications, etc.).

Members will also receive points for trading in items or donating items to the guild (assuming the items have value)

Point values may change over time as certain NPCs lose their importance or become easier to defeat

Using points

Any time an item is awarded, the member will lose points. Item values will vary based on the rarity of the item, fair market value, tradeable/No Drop status as well as the benefit that item provides.

Some general point values

Planar BP/Robe - 8 points

Thurgadin/PoG BP/Robe - 10 points

Amulet of Necropotence - 15 points

How loot is awarded

Whenever an item drop occurs, the person in charge of looting (generally the guildleader however it can also be a class leader or raid coordinator) will make the decisions based on three factors:

  1. Member point totals
  2. Need
  3. Benefit to the guild

When the item drops the member will inform the loot officer that they are interested in the item. They must also inform the officer what the item will be replacing.  The loot officer will then review the point totals of all members interested then determine who is eligible. From that point, the officer will determine based on need as well as benefit who will receive that item.  In cases where the point totals are relatively close, the item will generally be awarded to the person with the most need.  Some items have specific value to a certain class and in those cases the item will be awarded to that class even if a member of another class that can use the item has a higher point total

Some examples of how the system works:

Example 1 - Need

Caster A has 30 points and has a plane level robe. Caster B has 25 points but no plane level robe.  A caster robe drops that is useable by both casters and has no special innate benefit to the guild (i.e. an ability that would make encounters/raids easier). The robe would be a slight upgrade to Caster A but a large upgrade to Caster B. In this case Caster B would receive the item due to need even though Caster A had a slight point advantage.

Example 2 - Point difference

Take the same two casters from the first example but in this case Caster B only has 5 points. In this case the robe would be awarded to Caster A because they had amassed a much higher number of points than Caster B.

Example 3 - Benefit to the guild

A Wizard has 30 points. A Necromancer has 10 points. They both have comparable robes. A quest robe drops in Kael Arena that is useable by both.  Even though the Wizard has a substantial point difference over the Necromancer, the robe would be awarded to the Necromancer because it would allow him to cast Conjure corpse which would have a very large benefit to the guild.

Even though this does not cover every possible scenario, I hope it gives you an idea of how items are determined.

How are raid/item values determined?

Generally the value of a mob and the items that are dropped are valued so that the total DKP gained = total item DKP awarded.  This is done to stop inflation/deflation which can cause this type of system to become invalid.

For example:

Mob A has a loot pool of 8 items. On average the mob drops 3 items per killing with the average DKP item value of 10 for a total of 30 DKP. This mob takes 30 people to kill.

(Average Item DKP  * Number of items dropped)/Number needed to kill NPC = NPC DKP

(10 * 3)/30 = 1

Items that are random drops cannot be calculated this way but can be calculated based on their value relative to other items.

Please keep in mind that both item values and raid values will vary over time and can be changed at any time based on need, value and availability. 

Alternate Characters, Playing Multiple characters at once

One point total will be maintained for each member no matter how many alternate characters they have. Any time a guild member participates in an event regardless of it they play their primary or an alternate they will receive points. Alternates are entitled to loot on raids however they will have secondary consideration over primaries.  In some cases, an alternate may be given looting privileges over a primary if the member has a distinct point advantage over the other member (most of that time this will only apply to probationary members), the alternate is of need to the guild or that person was specifically asked to play that alternate at a specific raid.  Points will be deducted from the members total for any item awarded to any alternate character. 

Players may purchase items from the guild vault for their alternates however the item must be in the vault for no less than 3 weeks and only if no primary  has expressed interest in the item. If more than one member is interested in the item, the relative level of the alternate will be considered as well as the point totals of each member.

E.g. - The guld vault has a Lamentation in it and two members express interest in the item for their alternates. The point totals are relatively close. One member has a 25th level warrior and the other has a 40th level ranger. The item would be given to the member with the 40th level ranger because their character is of a much closer level to where you would be able to obtain and benefit from the item.

Players with multiple accounts/computers that can play more than one character at a time will be allowed to and will receive a bonus to their point total for that raid. Anyone that plays more than one character will be given .5 point multiplier per character during the raid (i.e. if you played two characters at a raid for 4 hours you would receive 2.4 points instead of 1.6 (.4*4*1.5=2.4)).  Please keep in mind that if there is a limitation on group space, a member may only be able to play one character even if they have the ability to play more than one.
