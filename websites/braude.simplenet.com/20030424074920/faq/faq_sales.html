1. What are dedicated servers?
A dedicated server offers you the opportunity to be your own site administrator and allows you to manage your own server, resources, users, and services.

Dedicated servers are incredibly flexible to meet your advanced hosting needs. Run Active Server Pages, Cold Fusion, database applications, chat servers, mail system applications, and more. You can even configure a dedicated SimpleNet server to stream media.

A dedicated server is yours to use exclusively, so you have 100% of the processor and 100% of the available disk space working for you. Additionally, the bandwidth for the server is 100% yours. You are not sharing your bandwidth with anyone else.

To maximize network uptime, your SimpleNet Server is connected to the Internet via three backbone providers.

These providers include:

  • Sprint - OC3
  • UUNET - DS3
  • AT&T - OC3

In addition to providing this high level of redundancy, our sophisticated routing techniques ensure that your data will take the fastest, most reliable path to the Internet. There is no specific limitation on how many domains can be hosted on your server. The limitation is related to the hardware capacity of the machine.

If you host numerous domains, or if the domains you host run many CGI pages, databases, or other active content, these applications will tax the server and put a practical limit on the number of domains you can effectively host on the server.

2. Where is the server located?
Our Access Center is a world-class facility and was the first of its kind in San Diego, California. Completed in March 1998, our 2,000 square foot, 150 rack Access Center meets the high security and protection demands of any Internet presence. The Access Center has redundant systems for connectivity, electrical power, air conditioning, and fire suppression.

We monitor our Access Center 24 hours per day, seven days per week, and 365 days per year. Our staff is committed and trained to provide you with continual, high-level network monitoring and load management of your connection. In addition, we offer engineering support for outage response, network performance analysis, and network planning.

The Access Center features a fault tolerant power supply with an Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) connected to a 450kW diesel generator. Each rack has its own dedicated circuit and is conditioned by the UPS before reaching customer equipment. The UPS will provide power for over 30 minutes during peak load to allow time for the diesel generator to start up and provide power for as long as required.

4. What kind of technical support is offered?
SimpleNet Servers is an advanced program for customers who are looking for more flexibility for their hosting needs and are comfortable being their own server administrator.

Support for SimpleNet Servers is offered primarily by email although telephone support is available from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Pacific Time, Monday through Friday. For the fastest response, customers should use email.

Each dedicated server includes 24 hours a day, seven days a week, year round monitoring at no additional charge. SimpleNet Servers will also reboot servers upon request at no charge. Should a customer's server require reinstallation of the operating system, SimpleNet Servers will reformat and reinstall the operating system for a $100.00 per incident fee.

SimpleNet Servers provides technical support for the supplied administration tool on the servers. This support covers the use of the tool for adding and deleting domains and host names, adding and deleting users, FTP accounts, starting and stopping web and FTP service, and enabling and disabling logging.

There are some advanced features not included in the web interface that require familiarity with Linux and typical Linux services. While we do not offer technical support for such features, we do provide technical assistance for issues relating to the web interface and its use. In addition, the following PDF manuals are available for your reference:

RaQ 4 Manual : ftp://docs-pdf.sun.com/816-1820/816-1820.pdf
