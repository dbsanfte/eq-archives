The Lost Order of Akalabeth's Mission Statement:

The Lost Order of Akalabeth is a mature, multiple-game online community. Freejack, Brimstone, Ralineth, Ghoti, Brewman, Tasslehof, Zari, Carnifex and Avenger founded LOA in 1997. Guild members are held to a very strict and high level of honor, maturity, and dedication to guild, members and other players. Above all, we adhere to our motto, "Friendship Transcending Worlds".

LOA is at present composed of three chapters, one for each of the main games we are involved with; Ultima Online, Everquest, and Asheron's Call. In Everquest, we play on the Veeshan server. If you wish to join us, we do require that you also play on Veeshan.

LOA is devoted to role-playing and to ensuring the maximum enjoyment of EverQuest for as many players as possible. We strive to uphold the maturity and professionalism of our guild, both in role-playing and through our behavior with  fellow players. We are a guild firmly rooted in the concept of diversity for all that the term implies. We encourage our members to pursue any race or class so long as they continue to be fair and honorable players, and to respect fellow guilds and players.

If you join the Lost Order of Akalabeth, you will be immersed in a tradition of friendship, honor, dedication and motivation. We truly enjoy the spirit of camaraderie that has grown up among us over the years, placing this above all else. If you honor each other and our guild, you will surely make a name for yourself in our ranks and in our history.

Participation is Key:

The most important aspect of our guild and our survival is participation. We have created several ways for you to participate such as:  IRC, guild mailing lists, guild events, recruiting, advertising, etc. If you make an attempt to meet others, IN and OUT of game, and show the guild that you truly care - you will do just fine.

We realize that each person has his or her own amount of time that can be given towards our guild. Some can play for one hour per week, others have given several hours per day! You simply give what you can, and we will respect you for this.

Out of Game Participation:

  • Guild and Chapter Forums
  • Advertising via Newsgroups, chat rooms, EQ pages, newspapers, message boards, etc.
  • Weekly meetings in mIRC
  • Mailing List Participation
  • Stories, "Mission Reports", Poems, and Songs about our Guild and its Members

All LOA chapters hold weekly on-line meetings in IRC (a client can be found at www.mirc.com). Our public chat room is #Akalabeth and is used to discuss all guild-wide issues, meet new friends, hang out with old friends, and more. We normally have about 20 to 40 members online at any given time.

For our Wednesday night meetings (10 PM Eastern), we have a private room setup named #Hall_of_Akalabeth. While in this room, you must be granted permission to speak on a topic; this helps minimize channel traffic. ANY member can approach the podium and voice their thoughts, concerns, opinions, etc, simply private message the person hosting the meeting for permission to speak.

Weekly EQ faction meetings are held in room #loa-eq, which is not restricted-access but may be moderated. If you have topics or ideas that you would like to see discussed in our meetings, send them to EQ Agenda (eqagenda@lostorder.com).

Sometimes our chapter leaders are not able to make it to a meeting. When this happens, the senior member at each meeting will be required to host that meeting to the best of their ability.

If you are unsure about using IRC or registering your nickname under MYSTICAL.net, simply contact one of our members, or post a question on the Conclave.

The Application Process:

Before you become an initiate of this guild, many things must first take place. We need you to fill out the application form for our review. After that, you should meet up and party with members of the Order in game. This will give our members a chance to get to know and get comfortable with you and your play styles.

We look for several characteristics in a person AND his online persona. To name just a few, we seek those who are loyal, dedicated, honorable, responsible, professional, caring, brave, honest, giving, and this list goes on and on. If you  believe in these things, you are one step ahead of becoming a member in our Order.

There are three phases in the application process, Petitioner, Initiate and Commoner. The Petitioner phase lasts 7 days. In order to progress to the Initiate phase, you must attain three (3) positive vouches. These vouches usually come from members reviewing your application, so please be sure to take some time and effort when filling out your application.

The Initiate Phase lasts approximately 15 days. You must receive at least six (6) positive vouches from people different than those who vouched for you in the Petitioner phase. If you are unsuccessful in receiving six positive vouches, you will go through another 15 day Initiate period, where you must again receive six positive vouches. When in IRC, append [INIT] to your nick.

The Commoner Phase lasts approximately 30 days. You must receive at least eight (8) +1 votes during the three day voting period in order to be promoted to a full member. The voting for the Commoner phase occurs on the 14th, 15th, and 16th, of every month. If, because of the time you applied, you miss the mid-month voting period you will be voted on during the next voting period. The Commoner Phase is special in that you will be, for all intents and purposes, admitted to the guild short of gaining full membership privileges. You will be tagged in game. When in IRC, append [CMNR] to your nick.

If you are IN game often, follow the guild policies, and strive to better the entire guild, you will find that the rewards are sweeter than you could have ever imagined. By giving your time and loyalty to our Order, you will find that our members will always be there for you, through thick and thin. If you do not put forth a strong effort to participate, we will drop you from the applicant process, and say goodbye to you. The key to this stage is getting to know our members, and striving to show them that you are a team player. Also, make sure that you ask them to report your actions and deeds to the High Council and general membership - this helps us keep track of your good faith and participation.

We wish you good luck, friend! Maybe in time, you too will wear the title of the Lost Order Akalabeth. Until that day, I fare thee well.

After you are accepted:

Once you are accepted into our family, many things will take place. You will be added to our mailing lists, and granted private access to our discussion forums.

Guild Mailing List:

Announcements are sent to a mailing list that contains all the members of the LOA. We use this list to inform our members of meetings, new applicants, promotions, missions, changes, etc. This mailing list is PRIVATE and cannot be joined without going through the list owners.

Using ICQ:

Many of our Guild members have ICQ. We ask that when you become a member, you add your fellow members to your contact list, as well as the High Council members. This way, we can all communicate with each other and have a better chance of knowing who is online from our Guild. ICQ can be found at www.mirabilis.com and is currently a free download and service.

We ask that as you add each member to your list, that you rename the entry to reflect the LOA suffix. (i.e. Brimstone should read: Brimstone_LOA). This process will help you to identify a guild member much faster in the case of an emergency.

Choosing a Race and Class:

We realize that EverQuest has several classes to choose from, as well as alignments and races. The LOA doesn't want to be a strict guild with limitations on our members. You have full control over who and what you want your characters to be. Good, bad, ugly, etc. All up to you. NO MATTER WHAT you choose to play, we will still require you to be a good PERSON behind the character. Don't curse at others, don't be rude or insulting, don't be an idiot, etc. Be worthy of trust, always.

Policy on Guard Killing:

In general, the LOA does not condone repeated killing of static "Guard" NPC's who are placed in low- and mid-level zones for the protection of other players ("guard camping"). This practice is unfair to low-level characters who rely on the guards to provide a "safe spot" to rest and meditate, as well as a spot for them to retreat to when they pull more combatants then they can safely handle (ie, a "train"). It is our opinion that Norrath holds plenty of options for high-level characters to gain wealth and experience without endangering the lives of others.

Examples of NPC's covered under this policy would include (but not be limited to) the Gnome, Dwarven, Barbarian, Erudite, Halfling, Troll, Ogre, and Dark Elf guards in their respective newbie zones, High and Wood Elf guards in Greater Feydark, Human Guards in the Freeport, Queynos, and High Pass zones, as well as Human Guards at the outposts in the Commonlands and the Karanas.

Given that guards may be encountered anywhere within a zone, it should be stated that we would never ask a member to not act in their own self-defense. If a guard must be killed to save the life of a guildmate or friend, so be it. It is the intentional and repeated "camping" of guards, especially those with a static "spawn point", which we find objectionable.

Policy on Player Killing:

So long as it is within your character’s profile to be a murderer, social outcast, etc., and so long as you strive to uphold the role-playing elements of our guild and EverQuest, you may do whatever you see fit within the game. Since EQ has a PK-switch, it is fair play for you to engage in PKing and PVP within EQ.

Leaving Our Guild:

If at any time you wish to be removed from our guild, simply email your chapter leader. We will remove your info from all areas of our site. We will not give you a hard time about leaving - things come up and we will respect your wishes.

Negative Guild Actions:

  • Personally insulting fellow members of the LOA.
  • Disrespect of the Members, Leaders and Founding Fathers of this Guild.
  • Dispersal of information to other Guilds or players regarding the LOA, this includes any internal communications, tactics or strategies.
  • Cause harm to guild members or diplomats within the game unless it is in self-defense.
  • Rude statements and/or foul language around people who do not appreciate it. Get to know people first, before you go running off at the mouth.
  • Disregard for Chapter or Guild Policies.
  • Inactivity.

  • Improper use of in-game guild resources (i.e. housing, guildstones, etc.) or out of game resources (i.e. mailing lists, forums, etc.)

Interaction with Other Guilds:

Any interaction with another guild in regards to forming an alliance or a joint operation should be forwarded to the chapter leader or to any available members of the Norrathian Council. At no stage should a member of the guild make any promises to form an alliance without approval from one or more members of the Council. If your 'character' wishes to ally with someone else, we have no problems with it - so long as you keep the secrets of the LOA sacred.

Rule and Policy Violations:

Any member found to be in violation of guild policy and/or rules would, depending on the severity of the infraction, be issued a warning. If the violation is severe enough, the individual will stand in judgment before the High Council. The strongest penalty is banishment.

Proposed Looting Rules:

The below is not official policy of the Lost Order at this time, but we strongly encourage all members and prospective members to abide by it or a similar system. Thanks to Dearg Maul for drafting this policy.

First I'd like to clarify and repeat the looting system that I think is fairest to use when we have a full-LOA group. With mixed guild groups, the system is commonly /Rand 100 for each item and nobody gets 2 items until everyone has at least one. A much better system can be used with a full LOA group. When the group gets a magical item, they run through a series of quick questions in order. And it can be done very quickly...

First of all, ignore issues of rarity, ignore secondary characters and everyone must get 1 item before anybody gets 2.

The Scenario 1-4 rules:

  • 1. Does a party member deserve this item without question? Maybe we're camping that particular item for them, maybe we realised they have not gotten anything in months... This could include giving the item to someone for their secondary or giving it to somebody who is not even in the group. This only works with a unanimous agreement and anyone can nominate another player as being deserving. Often it is obvious that someone is desperate for a particular item, make their day by just giving it to them.

*Assuming this is not the case*

  • 2. Does anyone plan on wearing or using that item themselves? In other words, can you wear/use it and is it going to be used regularly. Weapons, armor, usable items, and weight-reduction bags all count. You do not plan on trading the item or giving it to a secondary, it is meant for the character in the group who got the item and that character does not already have a better item. If multiple people in the group fall into this category, we randomly roll /rand 1 100 to see who gets it.

*Assuming everybody already has that item or nobody can wear/use it*
 

  • 3. Does anybody want that item? Typically, It will be for trade/sale, a secondary character, or to give to a friend not in the group. Everyone interested randomly rolls.

*Assuming NOBODY is interested in the item*

  • 4. If nobody wants an item, usually because it would count as their loot and people don't want to waste their "looting ticket" (Everyone must get 1 item before anybody gets 2), then the item is considered as free loot and whoever wins a random roll gets it. That item does not count as loot and the same person could enter later random rolls. This usually happens with the Ritualist in Lower Guk...

Most of the time, the situation will fall into scenario 2 or 3. Once you get an item, you should not enter any further random rolls until everyone has gotten an item. Scenario 4 free loot items are the exception. These are simple "rules" and 95% of the time, the questions will be:

  • A. Can/Will you wear or use this item? Roll randomly among those that say yes.

If nobody says yes:

  • B. Do you want this item? Roll randomly among those that say yes.

 

Ok, the next element is not a series of rules, but some honor guidelines.

First of all, if you have gotten an item in a group, do not enter random rolls until you are fairly sure that everyone else in the group has gotten an item as well, this could be two days later... If you want an item and can wear it, but somebody else REALLY can use it better, let them have it (a typical example could be a cleric and a warrior both wanting a Thick Banded Belt, it would be nice of the cleric to just let the Warrior have the belt).

Finally, if somebody has already gotten their loot for this "round" but some new item comes up that they are the only one to need, they can ask the group to /rand their earlier item in exchange for being given the new one. This assumes nobody else wants the item under scenario 2. For example, imagine a group of 3 people, Warrior, Cleric, Druid. Only the Warrior has gotten something so far under scenario 3 (it's for trade/sale). They then find an item that ONLY the warrior wants under scenario 2. He asks if he can take the new item and the others can /rand his original item between them. No problem you say, what if the first item was a crap monk item but the new one is a Flowing Black Silk Sash... The other two could wear or use that sash for an incredible trade and by the basic rule of "nobody gets two items until everyone gets one", the warrior is theoretically eliminated from the roll. It can get iffy but don't let your greed get the better of you.

Basically, the idea is to try to make sure that everyone gets something, and preferably something they want to use themselves. But be generous and allow people to switch a scenario 3 item for a new scenario 2 item if nobody else wants that new item under scenario 2. Does this make sense? All this relies on YOU to be generous and to consider your friends needs, it shouldn't be too hard... Some items are just amazing: Ysehkas, Thick Banded Belts, FBSS etc. To be honorable, all those who fall under scenario 2 could have the option of joining the random roll and if they win, giving any previously won items back to the group to be redistributed.

One other point, if you get an item, but then trade that item away, you should NOT enter the roll for the next one the group gets under scenario 2. So lets say a tank gets a FBSS one day and trades it for two swords and a shield, they should not enter the roll for another FBSS until everyone else who can wear it in the group has gotten one. These are not rules but suggestions. The scenario 1-4 rules can be broken if people agree unanimously. Its not worth ruining someone elses day by being greedy... If you want an item for a secondary character, I'm afraid you'll have to wait to get it under scenarios 1, 3 or 4.
