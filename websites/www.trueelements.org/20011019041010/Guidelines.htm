HOW TO BECOME A MEMBER OF TRUE ELEMENTS

First, you must meet the Guidelines for Guild membership:

  1. You must play the game well, either by roleplaying, or hunting, or exhibiting good group hunting tactics
  2. Meet at least 1 officer, hunt with at least 1 member and, when possible 1 officer at least once
  3. Must be able to act in a MATURE manner.

After you've read our guidelines below and decided we might be for you, it's time to meet us and for us to meet you. Becoming a member of True Elements should be fun, so we ask that you hunt with at least 1 member, and when possible, 1 officer. If the hunt goes well, the member you hunted with will sponsor you for membership, at which point, you will be guildtagged for a one (1) week probationary period. This week will give us a chance to evaluate you, as well as you to evaluate whether or not you will fit in with us.

If you decide you would like to join us, please post an introduction for yourself to the forum. If after the probationary period no one has any negatives to say about you, and you wish to continue your membership, you are a full-fledged True Elements member.

GUIDELINES:

We have now set our guidelines, but none of these are set in stone. We want to be able to alter our outlook on the game at will, without being constrained by an unchangeable set of rules.

One guideline is set in stone, and can expressed this way:

"Have fun! If you don't like this guild, and aren't having fun, get your happy arse out!"

Out of Character: The guild is NOT to be used to promote anything OUT OF CHARACTER that a person is doing. That means no posting URL's for your other web sites in the forum, no using the guild's name for commercial ventures, nothing that doesn't concern in-game is to be sponsored or supported by the guild. While it is not discouraged, it is also not encouraged.

Kill-stealing and camping rotations: Kill-stealing is a Guild-bannable offense. By Kill-stealing I mean deliberate and repeated acts of taking another person or groups kills out of turn, or "hogging" all the kills in a camped area, either by intentionally attacking all spawns in the area, or mana dumping damage spells onto anything that moves. Bragging about doing so, OR threatening to bring a load of higher level Guild characters in to make sure you take all the kills is ALSO wrong. Even in a situation where you are the victim of kill-stealing, you do not and SHOULD not threaten to kill-steal in retaliation. You are free to challenge the offender to a duel, call a GM, or try to resolve the situation by discussing it in /tells.

All Guild members, upon entering an area camped by multiple groups/individuals should seek to form a rotation upon entering the area. Once formed, stick to the rotation. If you mess up, apologize, and offer to sit out a spot in the rotation. If another group breaks the rotation, ask them to change their behaviour. DO NOT get into a verbal argument with these people, and keep the verbal abuse guideline in mind. Only after attempting a rotation should you engage in a free-for-all.

Forum Use: The forum is not to be used for personality conflicts. Problems between 2 guild members are not to be argued about in the forum. It is to be used for communication of ideas, events, support, etc. of the whole guild and its members in-game activities. It is not to be a forum for complaints. Complaints are to be sent to an officer via email or by discussing the problem with an officer or the Leader in /tells. The officer contacted with a complaint will be responsible for either dealing with the complaint or, if necessary, passing the complaint to the leader. Arguments between 2 or more players are to be resolved by the parties involved, when at all possible. Please feel free to contact any of the officers or the Leader with a problem, but do not use the forum as a venting platform.

Verbal abuse: No member of True Elements should engage in verbal abuse, except when roleplaying. This means public verbal abuse that can be heard in /shout, /ooc, /say, anything that can be overheard by anyone other than the two participants. No member should also engage in private verbal abuse, and any logged instance of said abuse will be adjudged by the council and treated accordingly.

Bannable offenses: Any offenses that are bannable by Verant, are not Guild-endorsed, and may lead to disciplinary actions. Some examples are:

  1. Intentional, hurtful kill-stealing (more on this in another topic)
  2. Cheating (Intentionally using hacks or bugs in the game that Verant warns or bans people)
  3. Trading illegal, obscene or copyrighted material using EQ as a trading vehicle. This includes pornography, warez or anything else traded between two people. Guildsay is not the place for this, and the guild has NO connections to this. If you are caught doing this, you are on your own.
  4. Harassment. This includes sexual, verbal or any type of harrasment that can get you banned. We are here to have fun, and there is no reason to destroy another's fun, whether in the guild or out.

Guidelines for Officers - Adding new members:
We must be selective in who we sponsor for membership. Just because we have one good hunt with someone is not reason enough. When we add a new member, we should do the following:

  1. Make sure they play well
  2. Let them meet some members (more than you) - use email or the forum to schedule a hunt or whatever it takes to get them together with members that are of similar levels.
  3. Get 1 officer to guildtag them for a 1 week probationary period
  4. Ask the guild in the forum and/or guildsay if anyone has a problem making them a member
  5. AFTER this information is received, the prospective member can be added. When the time comes for the guild to vote, one of the sponsoring officers must post a note to the forum asking for any objections to be mailed to the Guild Leader or sponsoring Officer(s). If none are received within 1 day, membership is finalized and will commence at that moment. The officer who adds the new member will be their go-between in case they don't get the answers and support they need through forum, guildchat and email.

 

GUILD TREASURY LOAN GUIDELINES:
This guild's focus is not to "twink" characters, so iems should be earned in one form or another.

Those who wish to donate items to the treasury must post those items to the Treasury Thread on the Forum. Anyone who wishes to borrow this item must either post their intent to the forum, or give the owner a /tell in-game, and it is up to the two members to arrange the transfer. Once you have taken possession of an item, you must post it to the Treasury thread, showing that you currently have possession. You are then responsible for that item, either by returning it to the original owner, or transferring it to someone else.

MATURE ACTIVITY
Being mature adults, we created this Guild also to develop mature friendships and/or relationships. Being mature adults gives us license to act and voice ourselves as such but only if such verbiage is invited by the targeted member of the Guild. As in real life, we understand too that there are REAL PEOPLE behind the keyboards with REAL FEELINGS and we are sensitive to this. We also understand that this is a game, and as such HAVING FUN is the order of the day.

DUES

Any member found trading for, or soliciting such material using guildsay OR Everquest in general, will be expelled from the guild. What you do outside of Everquest is your business... but your conduct in-game/in character reflects directly on the guild and can affect us all.
