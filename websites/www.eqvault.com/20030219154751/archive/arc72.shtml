GM Atrulen's Leaving - The Tribunal
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:11 PM PST
Menuven sends the following report:

An army of men and women converged in three areas of the land of
Norrath this eve. Specters of great power loomed, the gods of our world
taking semi-mortal form to test our mettle. Three times they were defeated.
Three times they rose again. Until the final battle, in the forest of
Nektulos, where the Ghost of the dead God Atrulen hovered over the masses,
and screamed its last cry of death, but also of thanks.

As this god graduated to higher positions, he left behind many tokens.

Multiple items were left by his cohorts. These minions of doom terrorized,
but were felled by the might of many on the Tribunal. All Hail God Atruelen,
and may you rest in peace, as you leave this plane of existence.


/OOC:
Tonight (November 3-4), GM Atruelen left the Tribunal. He has a new
job now, but many will miss him.

In his final event, multiple high level specters and other creatures,
controlled by other GMs and Guides, fought. No experience was gained or
lost, as far as most could tell (although some claim otherwise), but all had
fun. Wizards and druids were kept VERY busy teleporting dead people back,
and never once did they charge.

Although I only attended the last third of the event (as I did not
know the rest had occurred), the amazing power of these things was
astounding. Yon To, a skeletal shadow knight, kept things interesting along
with a fellow Skeletal Paladin, as they dueled, and killed PCs by the
hundreds. The Planes hardly compared to the carnage at the Wizard Pyramid in
Nektulos Forest. A plot for the regular person would be to zone in, see Yon
To, live long enough for him to cast once or twice on you, and then return to
home point, only to try it again.

Eventually, these two were slain. Miggn was the name of the Paladin,
I believe.

Then, Atruelen's Ghost arose. It took a substantially less amount of
time for this one to be killed, as everyone was coordinated into a myriad of
groups.

I died once, and had time to TP back into WC, and run back. In the
end, I landed the final blow, and since there was no loot rules for this, got
the loot before any realized it was dead. (No, I did NOT Ninja-loot)
In any event, dropped was the following:

Bracer of Scavenging
Mithril Breastplate
Mithril 2-Handed Sword
Bag of the Sewn Evil Eye
Cobalt Boots
Hammerhead Helm

Menuven
The Tribunal
(Dragon2120@aol.com)

P.S. Good Luck, Atruelen. We'll miss you!!
Thanks for the news!

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:36 PM PST
Several posts were written today by Verant's Gordon Wrinn. Here are quotes and excerpts of them directly from the Gameplay Board:

  • Hybrid Experience Solution?
    Yes, it is possible to use math to see that levels don't move around. It's *possible* to do virtually anything given sufficient resources. That said, as mentioned in the FAQ, the experience system is not subject to post-production changes. The experinece system as it exists is part of the game that we will not modify.

    It's not appropriate for me to continue trying to make arguments to justify the differing experience requirements, as that suggests that something could happen that would cause us to change our minds. The experience system, as it exists, is absolutely firm: As firm as the game's name being "EverQuest", or it being a game that encourages grouping.

    I've tried to address this issue in the past through a number of different methods, but what it comes down to is that it absolutly will not be changed. I am indeed sorry if you disagree.

  • EQ a Level 50+ Club Now?
    Please remember that the lower levels are a transitional period. People move through them, and once they are through them, they move on to other places. For this reason, there's always been suitable in-game content for lower level folks, as people keep moving out of the lower levels.

    It's the high level game that is most in need of new content due to the growing number of "members" of that range.

    That said, we'd certainly like to see more dynamic events for the lower level folks. We have the ability now to limit who can attack gm-controlled NPCs, and it would definitely be nice to employ that more. Of course, that's gm-controlled NPCs only. Computer controlled NPCs remain attackable by everyone.

  • Burnout
    Just my opinion, but if you play for the express purpose of maxing out, you're setting yourself up for disappointment. Remember back before the level limit was raised when thousands of people were perfectly happy not to advance at all for months when they were 50th level, and how some of those same people got upset a week or two after we raised the limit when they weren't advancing fast enough.

    If someone feels free to go out and have fun without advancement, I don't see why they can't do the same when advancement can occur. It's my feeling that if people play the game to adventure and hang out with friends like they were doing before they could advance, advancement would still occur without the need to look at the bar after each kill.

  • Epic Quest Items
    [Quote from original post: You have yet to address the issue of the useless effect of the Necro Epic Weapon (beyond saying that the save per tick would be fixed). It is still a useless effect, can you please state if this has also been repaired, or does the necro community need to keep posting why this effect is trash? ]

    Once the save per tick problem is fixed in the next patch, I completely disagree that a free darkness series spell is "useless". That series is one of the staples of the necromancer class, and a way to cast it once or twice, even with a long casting time, for free, is quite a boon.

    I understand that there are those who disagree, but there have been people who've disagreed with the usefulness of every epic quest weapon that's been found so far. In short, it's not something we're considering changing.

    [Quote from original post: Agreed. Int doesn't have to be changed, but 40 more HP to give it 100 total HP/mana like the other weapons would be great.]

    We are modifying the stats on virtually all of the weapons, including the ones that haven't been found. I think that the cleric weapon is the only one that's not being modified.

  • Dex and Procs
    [Quote from original post: Why is it that dex does not affect how a weapon procs???]

    It does. The example in your post is not a large enough model to see the difference. You could certainly proc 5 times in a row even with minimum dex, but the chance is far less.

    Remember that previous successes or failures have nothing to do with the chance of future successes or failures. The fact that you just proc'd does not make the chance of your next proc any closer or further away, probabiltywise.

    Regarding the random number generator, people think that because streaks happen that there's some problems. Random does not mean equal and regular distribution over the course of the sample. In any 10 flips of a coin you still have a .1% chance of never having it come up "heads". The fact that you may sit down and flip a coin 10 times and not get "heads" doesn't mean that your coin flip isn't random.

  • Daylight Savings
    I've lived in Arizona and Indiana.

    Indiana doesn't change because they are so close to the time line, that if they did it would still be light out at 10pm.

    Michigan (where I've also lived) does change for daylight savings time. It's very eerie to be sitting outside at 10pm reading a book.

    Arizona doesn't change due to the heat. Arizonan's don't want more "daylight" in their day. The sooner it gets dark in the summer the sooner it cools off and people can go outside. It gets dark around 8:30pm in the summer, and it's still frequently above 100F (~38C) at midnight.

    But to answer your question, yes, we've changed our clocks. We're back to GMT-8.
