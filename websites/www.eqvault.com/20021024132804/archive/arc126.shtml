What Is the Point of EverQuest?
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 8:22 PM PST
Gordon Wrinn answered one of the deepest questions one can ask about EverQuest, that is what is the point of it all? Here's his response from the Gameplay board:

    Whats the point of Everquest? I have not seen anything about the enviroment that promotes roleplay. All I see is CAMPING for ITEMS and EXP. Even in groups thats all the game is about.What is the point of getting plane gear? Plane gear is suppose to be the best gear in the game and since this game is all about camping gear why should we camp this last tomb of gear if we will never be able to use it anywhere better?

    That's one of the interesting things about MMORPGs: They are what you make of them. If all you choose to see or partake in is "camping items and exp", then that's all there is here for you. On the other hand, if you choose to believe and pursue something more, you can get as much out of the game as you allow yourself to.

    Is there a feeling at Verant that there is much room for impovement or is it true that you guys are satisfied with the current state of the game as Brad likes to hint at?

    It would be entirely arrogant of us to try and say that "EQ is perfect". EQ is however, extremely successful, and fulfills the general goals that was laid out for it. EQ generally adheres to the vision of the perfect game, but there is and always will be better ways to do things than what we chose for this incarnation.

    Will you try to do things different on future games or keep the same attitudes and practices towards players?

    I think it makes sense that any future products that we create in the MMORPG genre will try to capitalize on EQ's strengths and leave behind its weaknesses, much as competitor’s products will try to do the same. As an example, we would try to retain the things about EQ that makes it a very compelling game to play, and might try to rid future products of the aspects of EQ that people tend to be unhappy with (e.g. downtime). Though downtime is integral to EQ at this point, and will remain a facet of EQ, future products might focus on limiting its impact.

    -Gordon

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 8:19 PM PST
On the Gameplay board, Gordon Wrinn has been posting up a storm. Here's his latest posts:

    On New Servers
    We have 4 new servers planned over the next several weeks. Two of them will probably be split-servers to take overflow population from some of the busier servers. We'll release more information as to times that these will come online when we have something firm.

    In addition to bringing up the new servers, we're going to be changing some colors on the server-selection screen in order to try and get new players to choose less-crowded servers. We think that there's a possibility that people new to the game are largely choosing the more-crowded servers thinking "If everyone is on server X, it must be the best server".

    Catering to Uber Guilds
    [Responding to claims that Verant "caters to the uberguild"] I don't think that we are "catering to the uberguilds". We said back in the begining that there was creatures in Norrath that would take waves and waves of people to kill, and even mentioned a few times phrases like "very large organized guilds".

    And we're not consistently beefing up these mobs as you state in your post. They've really only been upgraded a few times.

    Why is it that you think that these creatures should be pushovers for you just because you can't get that number of people together? Don't tell me that you don't want them to be pushovers, just possible. They are possible, and take the same amount of work based on experienced no matter what the size of the guild going after it.

    Why Can't You Loot Other Player's Kills?
    There are things that take more than one group to kill. People would be pretty well up-the-creek on creatures like that if corpses didn't automatically unlock after a given time. It also ads a nice aspect to player interaction. Following a group around and looting their leavings can be a good way to make money.

    Sure, like anything else there are some downsides to this, but overall I think it works out ok.

    On Gods and Dragons
    [Quote from original post: I'm not asking that all dragons/gods pop during primetime, like they do now during the day, but maybe one or two. Or actually have a somewhat set time, like once a week, where they will pop.]

    Though we really can't do anything at present about the fact that they pop whenever the zone is restarted, they do have the opportunity to spawn at other times in the week, and on a random timer at that. Prior to last week, the servers went nearly a month without being patched, and each server spawned dragons several times during that period.

    People who are online most of their natural lives will probably always have an edge in fighting creatures such as that, but I don't think that other people are locked out of the opportunity.

    Vendor Locations
    Not every city has everything. That's just one of the design aspects of the game, and is there to encourage people to travel around to get the goods that they need. It's that way to add some distinction to the cities and give people reason to visit them.

    Planet of Sky Equipment
    [Quote from original post: "Equipment from the Plane of Sky makes all other items look like rusty daggers."]
    Actually that's a misquote. The real one was something along the lines of "Some of the Plane of Sky stuff makes other stuff look like rusty daggers". Note the subtle difference.

    Yes that quote still applies.

    Soundblaster Bug Update
    I posted the other day that we are working closely with the Creative Labs folks on this. *Most* people cease to have a problem if they update both the LiveWare AND the drives. In the event that that doesn't work, there's a work-around (not a fix, a work-around) to get you by in the meantime. Here's a quote from the other post:

      ---SNIP---
      SB Live users need to get BOTH the latest LiveWare AND the latest drivers. This fixes the crashing problem for many people. Some still crash, but the Creative Labs people were not sure how that could still happen with the proper LiveWare and drivers installed. One work-around for these people is to go into their "Control Panel\Multimedia\Audio Tab\goto Playback 'Advanced Properties'\Performance tab" and then lower the "Hardware Acceleration" slider down one notch to "Standard Acceleration".
      ---SNIP---

    We'll continue to work on this until it is resolved.

    Spell Issues
    [Quote from original post: Oh and BTW Abashi. If those wizzies, Druids, mages, or necros have 200WIS/INT they can do the Ice Giants fairly easy. My bud which is a 49 Druid soloes em all the time. As a matter of fact he is bound just outside the Ice Giant castle for this reason.]

    Notice that I said the ice giants INSIDE permafrost, not the ones outside. The point I was trying to make is that solely comparing classes based upon what they can solo in a wide-open outdoor area isn't going to get you much sympathy here, just as solely comparing them on the basis of what type of group is required in a cramped indoor location will do the same.

    Yes, casters do, and will always, have an advantage in wide-open outdoor areas, but when they get indoors, they need the melee classes. I don't think that this is quite the epiphany that you make it out to be.

    Buffing Mobs
    Well we don't ever want Dragons or Gods to become yard trash, but we are sensitive to the concerns that many people have about us buffing them all up in light of the new level limit. Aside from that, we aren't really prepared to release further information on it.

    GM Favoritism
    People claim favoritism simply because there's no reasonable defense against it. There's often no way to prove that something didn't happen, and we frequently won't stoop to the level required to refute individual claims. People making the claims don't even have to produce any evidence. Other people reading it just assume that since they heard it on the Internet, it must be true.

    Like I've said before, if anyone has a specific information concerning favoritism or any other customer service staff misconduct, send me mail and I'll get it to the appropriate people.

    More on Togor/Code Issues
    The entry in the data file didn't matter before, as it was all controlled in code. It's now controlled by the spell database, because the code-cap had to be raised to allow for new spells that do more. Thats why the controlling factor was moved.

    Please read that closely. I've explained it many times.

    Stop the Farming!
    To be honest guys, the quickest way to stop the farming is to quit buying their wares. It's a demand-driven business. Now if so many people are refusing to quit buying their stuff that it creates a big enough demand to encourage the farming, I wonder if the "majority" doesn't in-fact approve of it.

    Thoughts? (Click here for that thread)

    On Server Downtime
    There's always at least 5 minutes notice for any intentional outage. The servers are brought down by script to insure that this happens. Now does this mean that no one gets cut off? No, it's still possible that someone just logging in gets cut off without notice, but that problem will exist no matter how we do it.

    The point of the language is to illustrate the urgency of the situation.

    Hint Book Inaccuracies (Click here for thread)
    I've never bothered to look at the hint book, and really am not concerned with what it may or may not have said. I'll take your word for it that it says what you say it does, and decline to comment further. Whatever the experience requirements are, that's what they are, that's the way it is, and it won't be changing. The contents of the hint book aren't going to change, and neither are the experience requirements to put them in line if any discrepancy exists.

    As we didn't have creative control over any of this documentation, it's very likely that it contains information that might not be entirely correct. It is also very likely that it contains information that we didn't want released. The hint book itself was written in late beta, and since the game has continued to evolve after print, I'm sure that there are many things in it that are obsolete.

    As for the question in general, there's really nothing wrong with it, I just don't want to be roped into answering all of the other questions that would result from it. My recommendation to you would be to kill a few things and draw your own conclusions.

    Repop'ing the Planes
    The GMs won't manually repop the planes after a scheduled outage, and are only doing so after an unscheduled outage as a courtesy.

    Lack of Class Quests
    I'm aware of several identifiable groups that currently don't have any quests. Our Live Team will address these issues as they can, and there is always a need for additional quests in game.

    On Scheduling
    Every company has their own guidelines to determine what constitutes "Scheduled". Before I came to Verant, I did some consulting for a wireless/satellite data Communications Company in Chicago. Outages there had to be "scheduled" 3.5 weeks in advance. Any outage taken without being scheduled that far in advance was considered an "emergency" outage.

    Though I think 3.5 weeks would be a little extreme for our purposes, I do think that we've been throwing around the term "scheduled" a little too freely. The fact does remain that we would do those updates regardless of what we call them, but I'll see if we can get the terminology a little closer to reality.

Proving that no illness can keep a good man down, Gordon Wrinn was back on the boards today responding to the concerns that the players of EverQuest have raised. Here's his latest from the Gameplay board:

    Spell Feature Request
    [Quote from original post: Feature: Allow the ability to cast spells directly from spellbook, with certain circumstances, and at a large casting time delay penalty (a penalty similar to the meditation time of the spell, or more, would be appropriate). ]

    The spell-system of EQ is built around memorization, and would not easily adopt changes to the core architecture such as this. Also, the effort required to implement something along these lines wouldn't really return any advantage substantial enough to justify it, since any net-gain in time or delay would have to be zero in order to maintain balance. In other words, even if we did it, the penalty associated with casting directly from the spell-book would have to be equal to the time it would take to forget a spell, mem/cast/forget a new spell, and re-mem the first one.

    Warrior Vision
    [Quote from original post: Verants Vision of warriors was to make them the one class that was totally dependant on other classes.]

    That's a pretty half-empty way to look at the glass, coming from the class that is considered the most valuable in the game by most people I've seen polled. Of course that means "most valuable to the group", which if it is the "group" part of that that you disagree with, I think anyone could have told you in the first days of EverQuest that Warriors weren't exactly designed to be solo.

    Corpse Issues
    [Quote from original post: Gordon, you have stated that Verant's opposition to a /drag command (drag corpse without looting rights) is that you want people to develop "trust" within the game. Yet, we constantly see server banner messages that shout "Trust NOBODY!!! Don't give ANYBODY your password!!!".]

    Passwords (out-of-game) and corpses (in-game) are two separate things, so the argument really doesn't apply.

    Here's the deal. If you consent the wrong person, he's going to steal from you. Don't do that, and if you do, and he does, then report him to a GM.

    You are fully in control of who you give consent to, and always have been. The /corpse ability (dragging) is relatively new to EverQuest, and was put in to help people get their corpses out of inaccessible areas without having to wait for a GM/Guide. At this point, it works well for that use and we decline to invest further time into it.

    Stating it as plainly as possible, we consider the issue closed.

    Pet Bug?
    [Quote from original message: Anyone notice how when you kill the master of the pet, the pet doesnt also die like our pets do?]

    It's not a bug, but rather another case where NPCs do not follow the same rules that PCs do, such as not being able to drown, fall for damage, etc.

    Just an FYI, people did not level faster than we thought they would. They're pretty much right on schedule. MOST players are 25-35.     --
    The reason is our Vision(TM) of a Warrior (which certainly isn't original, but rather is quite traditional): They hit stuff with weapons, and they get hit.

    I'm sure you can find instances (dunno about a bazillion :)) where you think we were wrong and we don't or where we didn't change something some people thought we should, but I can also find examples of the opposite (not a bazillion either :))

    Basically, everyone knows that the experience needed at every level is a curve. Well, it's not so much a curve, as it is a series of straight lines. As you level, the "curve" gets steeper. However, due to the formula used, in the first level after the curve gets steeper, you must "make up" all of the experience as if the curve had been that steep all along. As such, a "Hell Level" is the first level where a new experience curve took affect. Ideally, it would have been nice if the formula spread the additional experience required over all the levels of the next range, rather than requiring it all in one level, but the outcome is the same.

    As for 50+, it's probably safe to say that experience required in those levels won't be on the same curve as 45-50, and as such, any time the curve steepens, the "Hell Level Phenomenon" will be present for the first level of the new curve.

    Basically, this is just the way it is, and we won't be readjusting the experience curves. They haven't been changed since release, and no one ever said that progression would be linear.

    On Rangers
    Guys, if you take my comments regarding this issue IN CONTEXT you'll find that I was talking about aspects of the game that didn't turn out necessarily the way I personally wanted them to.

    On Overcrowding
    I'm reposting something I wrote tonight in responce to another thread in order to make sure no one misses it. The thread is/was about overcrowding and the math involved in determining how many servers we need and such:

    ------

    Actually, I think the math is as follows:

    (a) Target peak usage per server : 1800
    (b) Current number of servers : 28
    (c) Average total users online during peak hours : 50,000

    And I'll also add some additional but not necessarily relevant to the math data, which we've released before and which continues to grow quite rapidly:

    (d) Current number of subscribers: 196,000

    So, that means about 26% of our player base plays during peak hours. 25-30% is, btw, pretty typical for MMORPGs. I just wanted to go over that briefly as well since I've seen all sorts of formulas and calculations posted in overcrowding discussions.

    Anyway, back to the relevant math:

    c / a = 27.7, being the number of servers we'd need to have to maintain Target peak usage per server.

    Now, before everyone gets too excited or upset at this math, there are definitely a few issues here to go over:

    First, the peak usage per server is NOT equal. Some servers go over 2000 while some average 1200 or so.

    Second, one can argue the 1800 target number in a couple of different ways.

    When we first started thinking about target server capacities (pre-beta) we said somewhere between 1000 and 1500. As we entered beta and these numbers became less theoretical we changed our target to 1500. Then, as the game matured after launch and the level spread grew (meaning there are lower level players, mid, and high, spread out over varying zones, depending generally on the intended level ranges of those zones) we changed our target peak usage number to approximately 1800.

    But there are some problems with that, of course. First, when we launch a new server, it tends to fill up with, of course, newbie players (in terms of their level). This means low-level zones are full and mid to high level zones empty. So you have worse crowding until people spread out level-wise. Second, you could simply debate whether 1800 on a mature server is really a good number.

    In principal I think it is IF people are really spread out. And, of course, they're not. Some dungeons and outdoor areas have a LOT more players than others even though both have similar target level ranges.

    So, this is why that even if you do the correct math (which I believe I've done up above), which concludes with 28 servers we're just about where we should be, there is still definitely a crowding problem.

    I will reiterate our plan to deal with the problem:

    1. Launch at least one more server and fill it with players from the more populated servers. These players will be taken from the group that plays during peak hours so that we don't pull people who play during off peak-hours and risk creating an under-population problem. Looking at the numbers right now I think the Druzzil Ro endeavor was fairly successful: Fennin Ro has 1670 players right now (with about 47K online), while Bristlebane (a server we didn't pull players from) has 1867.

    2. Continue to re-tune and revamp underutilized zones. We need to entice our players, compelling them to spread out to many of the zones that just aren't used much (for example, Najena, Permafrost, Mistmoore, Kerra Ridge, etc.)

    3. Launch the Expansion which will increase the size of the world by over 33% and add more than 25 new zones (all with different level spreads, ranging from newbie zones all the way up to level 40-60 zones).

    So, in summary, that's our plan and that's how we're looking at the situation and how to address it. Your feedback and comments are welcome.

    Blast from the Past
    [This was part of a server wide broadcast from Aradune on Friday, March 26, 1999.]

    "As for total number of servers, don't worry. The others aren't full yet and we can bring new servers online very fast. FYI the servers themselves can handle more than this no problem. It's a matter of crowding. I imagine it bothers some people more than others. So this has all just been a suggestion. I'm just trying to help "

    Just to put my broadcast in perspective, that was back when we were well under the number of servers, the amount of bandwidth, the places to put server farms, etc. we had planned (even though we had severe problems GETTING the bandwidth we'd been promised initially, but that's old news).

    Since then the game has sold well above and beyond our expectations and beginning late last year approximately it's taken longer to get new servers up because of the housing and bandwidth issues.

    We have been and continue to play 'catch-up' to some extent.

Gordon on Customer Service
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:27 PM PST
Gordon Wrinn responded on the Gameplay board to a post about some of Verant's policies. Here's the thread:

    Abashi could you please justify Verant's guilty until proven innocent policy?

    That's not our policy at all. Our policy could better be defined as "Neutral until proven one way or the other". If someone is "proven guilty", then they are frequently removed from our customer-base. If they are "proven innocent", they'll usually get a reimbursement.

    The best analogy for this type of operation would probably be a bank.

    Why do you choose to ruin the play experience of honest people to prevent dishonest people from cheating?

    We don't "choose to ruin" anyone's play experience. However we must have a set of policies to insure everyone receives the same treatment. Now that doesn't mean that by treating everyone the same that the outcome will be "fair", but at least it is uniform.

    He returned and *poof* all his equipment from two separate characters were gone
