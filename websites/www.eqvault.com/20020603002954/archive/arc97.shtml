    Test Server News
    [38 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:50 PM PDT
    Gordon Wrinn made the following announcement on the Gameplay Board concerning the Test Server:

      Hello all,

      After a good deal of discussion on the Test server status, we've decided that having the right or the ability to do something doesn't necessarily mean that we should. Our thoughts were that everyone was aware of the risks of playing on the Test Server, and always did so with the understanding that something such as a character-wipe was always a possibility. It's apparent at this point, given feedback from players on Test, that many didn't understand the risk.

      That said, we do need to restore the integrity of the testing environment in order to insure that we are able to catch and address issues prior to them going to the live servers. Test has served well in this capacity since release, however item loot issues and other factors have reduced Test's ability to do this. Given these issues, we still feel that a complete character-wipe represents the best possible solution for continued functionality of the Test server. However, this is a case where, in the interest of keeping our current testers and players satisfied, that we need to compromise on the solution.

      After our discussions today, we've decided that an item-wipe, without deleting characters, will accomplish the majority of our goal to restore the Test server as a functional testing platform. Given that, the EQLive and Operations teams are currently at work restoring all characters that were wiped yesterday, with the exception of characters known to be artificially buffed. This process should be completed later today.

      Furthermore, in order to allow those that did not understand the purpose or risks associated with the Test server a chance to avoid future problems of this nature, we will be allowing built-up (not buffed) characters a one-time chance to move when the next split-server comes up, sometime in the near future. As Brad mentioned in his message earlier, we do recommend that those who are on Test more for playing, and less for Testing, seriously consider moving when we open registration. The possibility of a future wipe, whether it is a player or item wipe, is not necessarily unlikely in the future.

      We hope that the players on Test will find this compromise acceptable and continue to participate in the testing process. Furthermore, we apologize for the insensitivity of our previous decision to completely wipe the server.

      On behalf of the EverQuest team,

      Gordon Wrinn
      Internet Relations Manager
      Verant Interactive, Inc.

    Gordon Infobits
    [6 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:41 PM PDT
    Several different issues and topics were addressed by Gordon Wrinn this evening on the Gameplay BoarD:

    • Necro Res Too Powerful?
      [Quote from original post: But think long term, when there are many level 60's, and you realize that those people don't mind losing the exp. They'll get it back in no time and sit with maxed exp most the time, just like when level 50 was the cap and you could not mind getting killed much because you would be maxed again soon.]

      Level 50 non-Kunark characters and level 60 Kunark characters cannot be sacraficed to produce the component necessary for the necro resurrection. In other words, a sacrafice requires an actual sacrafice on the part of the target character. :)

    • Test Server Status
      It's being left up while the guys work on the data. We've had to build the program to go through the characters one by one and remove items. It's neither a clean process nor is it fast. That part is done.

      The next thing that has to happen is that all the character names have to be loaded into the used-names list. That takes a while since the Test server has so many characters.

      We'll get them restored as quickly as possible, it's just not something that we can nail down to a specific time.

    • Race War Servers
      [Quote from original post: The only reason that I wanted to join Vallon was because it was a more realistic server. I like the added danger of having to negotiate for you life, and then having the possibility of loosing and taking a good stash from someone that I kill. Most classes will even be nerfed in PvP in what they can obtain. For instance, my cleric has blind, when it is on no one can remove any items. Without item loot it makes them less worthwhile.]

      I recommend that you give it a shot before calling it quits or calling it boring. On Tallon, I know of several people who make thousands of PP a night just by PvP. There's also a lot more PvP on Tallon since people don't run and bag at the first sign of opposition.

      That said, I still think that PvP needs more risk. I've recommended making it so that everyone can only bind in the city, in order to give someone a real incentive to stay alive, but that's met with some serious opposition here on the boards and in the office as well. We're still thinking about it though.

    • More Spell Drop Issues
      [Quote from original post: Abashi, I understand you said that spell drops are very low in some areas (Skyfire, Chardok) and higher in other(s) (you didn't say, but player discussion seems to say Sebillis is higher). Why are the rates different? How does this improve gameplay?]

      An equally valid question might be, why should they be the same? We don't like the idea of making zones one-stop shopping for everyone. It's problems along those lines that lead to things such as tons of people in lower guk, but nobody in Kedge. Just like any other loot, the spells are spread out. Some zones will have more than others. Some won't have any.

      [Quote from original post: Having it this way just makes it more frustrating for players in Skyfire and Chardok. It incents overcrowding in Sebillis, just like the "everyone in sol b and lower guk" problem before Kunark. I thought you wanted to spread players out?]

      Sebilis isn't the only zone that they appear to drop in more frequently. I suggest that people go looking around a bit more. As for Sebilis being overcrowded, most servers are reporting around 45 people there during peak hours. It can support far more than that comfortably.

      In any case, why don't you check out Charasis?

    • Spell Drops
      [Quote from original post: Yes or No: Should a single level 51 drop only necro spell be worth MORE than a SSOY, FBSS and SMR? If the answer is no]

      That's a valid opinion. Who's to say how much these items *should* be worth. The players. The players decide what they are willing to pay for items based upon supply and demand. In this case, supply is a relative constant, while demand will diminish over time as people fill thier spellbooks, but continue to find spells thus allowing them to trickle down.

      We are happy with the rates that the spells are dropping currently, based upon the fact that demand is still higher than the supply. We do not, as mentioned before, want to make them common by any means.

    • Mobs Regenerating
      [Quote from original post: Abashi, level 45+ MOBs regenerate *faster* than a bow can inflict damage! (no flame)]

      It really depends on the NPC. NPCs can have individually set regen rates, for both a peace time, and when they are mad at someone. For instance, it's not unusual to see an NPC that will regen at 50 points per tick while idle, but only 1 point per tick while in combat. Another NPC might regen 200 per tick regardless of the state of the NPC.

      It's one of the things that we have the ability to set in order to adjust the difficulty of an encounter.

      In other words, I'd recommend that you try another NPC for your test.

      [Quote from original post: Remember, Abashi, archery unlike melee skills does *not* have a 2* weapon damage bonus.]

      Though the formula that you are using may or may not be correct, there certainly is a bonus that applies to ranged weapons, but it's not as large as that for melee weapons. This was part of what was needed to balance archery.

    • Same Game at Level 45+?
      [Quote from original post: Is it the same game at 45+?]

      Though I don't agree with the general purpose of this post, I would say that the answer to the question would be "no",but allow me to clarify:

      As characters progress in the game, strategies that they found viable at lower levels might not be the most successful strategies to use at higher ones. Players have to adapt to overcome new situations in order to remain effective. As an example, a warrior can find a bunch of things to fight at, for instance, 20th level and below, and have a reasonable chance of soloing those creatures. As they get higher in level, they start to require groups, and particularly, healing classes, to be more effective.

      We didn't aim for one strategy to work throughout the life of a character, and in every encounter. We certainly expect players to learn new ways to overcome the increasing challenges of the game.

    • Rogues, NPC Pets, Doors
      [Quote from original post: You are killing a mob, it walks through a door you can't open, now you get a huge train.]

      Yes, you are correct. A rogue, a key, a root, a mez, and a stun, can all address this issue quite nicely.

      [Quote from original post: However, it's stupid to REQUIRE a locked door to be passed to get to major parts of the game. That's nothing but affirmative action for rogues.]

      Heh, perhaps. Of course I think the main resistance to this idea is the fact that it's something new. Had this been around since the game launched (as it probably should have been), it wouldn't be such an issue, but rather just how the game is played.

      I do however look forward to seeing more people experiencing how good rogues are for the group. Most people I have heard from, who have experience grouping with rogues, agree that a well-played rogue is a wonderful addition. However, there are a lot of people resistant to the idea of grouping with them based upon anecdotal evidence when the game was first released. They just think that "rogues suck" and aren't open to other possibilities.

      It will be nice when people see how valuable they really are, even when a door doesn't need to be opened.

    • "I Quite Because of Research"
      I'm not sure what you mean by "time sink". Does it take time? Sure, though everything takes time. Games are made to take time, it's the way they work.

      I personally have never found research that difficult. I get the components as I need them, and would see what I could throw together on my periodic trips back to the bank. Of course, you don't have to research. You could simply choose to buy those spells off of other people making them, as there are plenty of people out there who are spell merchants for fun.

      I think that most would agree that it is a good thing that we give you other things to do besides kill things non-stop.

    • Customer Service
      [Quote from original post: I am a 29 Shaman and have done 5 Totemic armor quests with no problems, am a Barbarian and have plenty good faction. I gave Vrynn- 1 Banded leggings(reg.ones, not large), 1 Powdered Dufrenite, 1 Snoball from goblin Wiz in Perma, & 1 Bone Bards from unrest. He didnt give me the leggings and I know 100% I gave all the right components.]

      I am indeed sorry that you had a problem here. However we've never run across a situation where an NPC would intermittently not give out a reward due to a bug, which is probably why the GMs couldn't assist you in this matter.

      In order for reimbursements to take place, loss has to be done via a known or verifiable bug, and there has to be sufficient evidence that the bug occured. Our logs are useful to verify many things of this nature, however not *everything* that can posssibly happen is logged.

    • Scale of Wolf Broken?
      I would like to thank everyone for following up in this post. Like most of those who followed up, I haven't found a problem with the spell and move faster than spirit of wolf in several different tests. I suppose that it is possible that there is some dynamic that's not being properly reproduced, so if anyone finds any more information let me know and I'll send it to the Test team.

    • More on Death Experience Loss
      [Quote from original post: I've been seeing snippets of posts by people who want exp loss removed on EQ Vault...it seems to be a growing sentiment.

      The title pretty much says it all...are you people crazy? Take out exp loss and strategy and tactics pretty much go out the window. When you are fighting, goal #1 is to stay alive, with killing the mob as a secondary goal. (Some exceptions, eg Gods and Dragons). Take out exp loss and that's reversed. This leads to people just not trying as hard, making accomplishments much less rewarding.

      Even if you don't agree with that...just think about it. The death system and exp loss are an integral part of EQ. No matter how much you hate it, it's going to be there. And it's the same with any online RPG. Don't want a death penalty, play Quake. You are supposed to *care* about your character in EQ, as with any RPG.]

      Very well-stated Kaisharga. Thanks for posting.

    • Removing Experience Penalty on Death
      I don't think that I'll ever get everyone to agree, but my point has been, if a situation is too risky for your taste, then either don't put yourself in the situation, or take steps to mitigate the risk if fulfilled.

      Many aspects of the game can be reduced to the statement above. We try to give you options in order to empower you to make the decision that is right for you. As it applies to death and experience loss, if experience loss on death is too severe for your taste, don't put yourself in a situation where you can die, or find someone who can rez you if you do.

      The amount of risk that you assume is up to you.

    Chalk It Up To Experience
    [6 Comments] - Aurum @ 11:20 AM PDT
    Should you gain experience when you die? This very unusual thought was posted on the Gameplay Board recently. Here's the original post followed by Gordon's reply:

      In real life, when you fail, you gain experience. If you get hurt doing something incorrectly, you gain knowledge and people tell you to "chalk it up as experience!"

      In EQ, I believe that when you die, you should NOT get an exp loss, but rather get experience! Yes, you heard me right!

      NOT A LOT OF EXP FOR DEATH
      I don't believe you should get a great deal of exp for a death, but you should get some. If you die with a corpse of yourself somewhere else, you should get no exp...this keeps you from sitting afk at an aggro spawn.

      In life you gain experience by making mistakes; in EQ, you lose experience by making mistakes. Mistakes in life are beneficial in terms of experience, but not in EQ.

      I think that Verant would only go with this if it was made more difficult to level...requiring more experience to level, or reducing the amount with each kill.

      This is partially tongue-in-cheek, but we could give you experience for failure, but we'd have to delete your character when you die. Certain departures from reality are necessary in gameplay in order to make up for the fact that reality sucks.

      Actually, now that I think about it, you do lose "experience" when you fail in real life. This is because experience in game is really a measure of advancement, not a measure of skills and abilities. In life, there's really no way to quantify advancement, but, for instance, if I was *really close* to a promotion, and then I tell- off my boss, I'd probably find myself further away from promotion, thus further away from advancement. I might even lose a level.

      -Gordon

    Gordon Infobits, Part 1
    [3 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:58 PM PDT
    Here is part one of today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

    • Why does numerical spell damage go unreported to other players?
      [Quote from original post: I asked one of Verant's EQ level designers this question at E3 in LA and his response was that they just hadn't gotten around to this feature yet and that it would possibly create more spam. But I suspect another reason Verant hasn't added it. Spam by reporting dual wielding pets/meleers far out strips a 5 second cast time spell.]

      There are many places in game where we could have provided numbers but elected not to. This is one of those places. To be honest, we didn't even want to report the damage to the casters, but did so based upon public request.

    • Customer Service Continued
      [Quote from original post: If that 2 bucks a month would hire 10 more GMs customer service people?]

      Actually we're hiring far more than that for the money that you are paying now. Our goal is to reduce response times for both /petitions and email.

      That said, regardless how many GMs we bring in, we'll still always have issues where GMs must refuse item or experience reimbursements. These issues exist regardless how many GMs we have, and will probably pop up on the boards here for some time to come.

    • Minor UI Improvement Suggestion
      This is a great idea. I think Ctrl-click would make more sense from an interface perspective. I'll go talk to the client programmer about it now.

    • Customer Service
      Though I can't comment on the specifics of the original authors post, we are working constantly and consistantly to improve our customer-service organization. This includes hiring more GMs, tech support people, content-specific GMs, and supervisors. This should improve response times, both in and out of game.

      Of course, most of the customer-service complaints that I've seen are in regards to being refused a reimbursement of items or experience. As our policies are pretty solid in this area, aside from enhancing the consistancy of those judgements, these issues will probably always remain.

    • Spellbook for Pre-35
      Though I appreciate your opinion, we don't have any plans to remove the need to look at the spell-book pre-35. Think of having to do that as one of the disadvantages of being a lower level caster.

    • 51+ Spells Uber "Lewt" Now?
      [Quote from original post: 51+ spells are now UBBER LEWT! Tanks rip casters off for spells now! FIX IT! Just today some tank comes wandering thru zone auctioning spells I have no idea how he got, a couple of which were for my class so i asked price. He says OFFER! So I offer 1,000pp which i think is way too high.. get a response of "LAF!"]

      Since EverQuest began, casters have participated in rolls for melee items, then sold them to those who could use them. I'm not really seeing a problem with the opposite happening here. Certainly there are those in the group who would never think of rolling on an item that they couldn't use, just as there have been casters who did just that when melee item were found. It seems to me that it all balances out in the long run.

      The spells seem to do a good job of spuring on the player-driven economy.

    • Abashi: Dead Wrong & Loving It
      [Quote from original post: Abashi, you're dead wrong.]

      Wouldn't be the first time :)

      [Quote from original post: I would contend that if you completely removed the experience penalty for death, you would see no adverse impact on a player's enjoyment of the game -- the few masochists out there that are disappointed to see the experience loss go would be more than made up for by the players that view experience loss as wasted time.]

      I suppose that everyone is entitled to their opinion, and I'm sure that removal of experience loss on death would be well-received by the majority in the short term. However, we also need to look at the impact on the long term game. Lessening the challenge associated with reaching a goal makes the reaching of the goal that much less fulfilling, which leads to less player-satisfaction overall.

      There are other games out there, and others coming out that have little-to-no penalty for death, doing things incorrectly, etc. I guess only time will tell which method leads to greater player enjoyment overall.

    • Igoring the X-Team Aspect of the TZ/VZ Move?
      The reason that the move was being considered was for loot rules, not for server environment. If people chose to move based upon that and that alone, they made the decision for the wrong reasons. We don't want to set a precident of allowing moves based upon not liking a server's environment. It would not be a good precident to sent, and we'd just end up moving people from one server or another every week.

      Now in regards to cross-teaming in general, I expect that it will fade on VZ once coin-loot is implemented. It did on Tallon, so I expect it will follow suit on Vallon.

    • Will Tuning Meeting Highlights Be Posted Regularly?
      From time to time, depending on what news can be announced something like this might be forthcoming. Once before I had a suitable batch of information of this type and everyone appeared to like it pretty well.

      The number of different items that we discuss and resolve (agree on a solution) varies from week to week, but I'll continue to announce what I can.

    • Is the EQ Code Base Entirely Licensed?
      [Quote from original post: Is the EQ code base entirely LICENSED? This is not a joke post, nor sarcasm, as I really want to know why the programming staff keeps telling us that "certain things can not be changed with in the current code base."]

      No, EQ was built by people in-house.

      As a programmer, you should understand that "can't be changed" really means "cannot feasibly be changed". Let's face it, we're developers, if it can be done with a computer we can do it. That said, not everything is cost-effective to do, and not every good suggestion is good for EverQuest.

      There's also the matter of dependencies. Often one particular suggested change is based upon an assumption that was made deep in code. The only way to change the requested facet of the game would be to modify that particular assumption. Unfortunately, this also affects every other facet that was based upon that assumption. It's often not as simple as putting in one line of case-specific code.

      Finally, it would be a bad decision on our part to make drastic changes to the core game. After all, the game itself is successful, relatively bug-free, and generally well-liked by the players. By going into the core game, we take the risk of upsetting a delicate balance, affecting certain aspects of the game in both negative and positive manners. This also has the potential to create bugs that are much worse than the aspect of gameplay that we were trying to address. Any time a change is made you run the risk of the "solution" being worse than the "problem" it was trying to cure.

    PvP-Teams (VZ/TZ) Status

    Gordon shed some light as to what is the current status of the big Vallon Zek/Tallon Zek transfer option. Here's his original post in it's entirety:

      Hello all,

      Due to the results of the /movelog command executed by players on the PvP-Teams servers the weekend before last, we are reconsidering our decision to do the move. Out of nearly 1800 accounts across TZ and VZ that requested move, only 278 asked to be moved from TZ (Coin only) to VZ (Coin and Item loot), while 1513 requested a move in the other direction.

      The result of completing this move would most likely result in crowding on the Tallon Zek server while leaving Vallon Zek nearly empty. While we have considered allowing a smaller number of people to move from Vallon to Tallon, we've concluded that that would most likely be more destructive to server morale than our current plan.

      We'll wait a few days to view comments here prior to finalizing the decision. Please followup here with your thoughts.

      -Gordon


    Gordon Infobits
    [2 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:55 PM PDT
    Gordon was back answering questions and posting to the Gameplay Board today. Here is all the latest:

    • Guild Housing (Officially Canned)
      [Quote from original post: It would be nice to have a place called home not just the city where you started. You can rest there without being attacked, store food and supplies. Why not other games have it?]

      Though we considered the idea in EQ, we ended up waiving it in favor of spending more time on the adventure aspects of the game. Overall I think it was a good choice given our goals and timing constraints.

    • eBay Question
      [Quote from original post: I am kinda getting bored with my character. Is it against the rules to trade accounts with another player? Now before you say no as fast as you can, think about it. You are only trading an in game commodity for another in game commodity, that being characters. Almost like trading all of your gear for all of someone else's gear, and trading all of your 'class A' exp for all of their 'class B' exp.

      Yes, this is also against the rules. Though I appreciate your arguments, trading a sword for another sword in game (perfectly acceptable) is not the same as trading characters or accounts. Transfer of accounts has always been prohibited.

      It's not just the philosophical ideals concerning gameplay, but the amount of fraud that results in such transactions. Even with our policies as they are, completely forbidding character sales and account transfers, we still receive a number of emails daily citing fraud in relation to account transfers.]

    • Cleric Armor
      [Quote from original post: I'd like to know why the cleric arms from the Ostiary in the library in Sebilis were nerfed, without any warning. These arms had the effect of Valor, which could be cast every 6 seconds. After the patch last week, they now take twice as long to cast, which in essence has rendered them useless, since Remedy and Superior Heal can be cast much faster with great hp return, even tho they cost mana and the arms do not. These were one of the nicest cleric pieces in the game, and like everything else that proves useful to a class, wound up getting nerfed and made useless without warning or explanation.]

      First, I apologize for the fact that this wasn't listed in the patch message.

      As for why they were changed, as you mentioned, use of the arms for the purpose of healing rather than for the "buff" that it gave was the primary motivation. The fact that that whole series increases current hitpoints in addition to max hitpoints was based on it being balanced by mana usage. Since the arms do not use mana, the casting-time becomes the entire cost. A cost of six seconds was too low for what it granted.

    • /Friends Increasement Status
      [Quote from original post: 20 friends just isn't enough for a happy-go-lucky flesh-pressing baby-kissing ogre warrior like myself. What is the status on increasing the size of the /friend list?]

      We've had to back-burner (postpone indefinitely) any plans to due this due to some technical issues. If we take it up actively again in the future I'll be sure to let everyone know.

    • Spell Drops
      We've tested and found the spelldrops appropriate at this time. They did increase, but as I said they weren't being increased to the point that people would find them plentiful by any means.

    • Tuning Meeting
      [Quote from original post: Gordon, It's Monday. How did that tuning meeting go?]

      The tuning meeting went well. I think I'll post a summary a bit later.

    • Linux Port & Development Question
      [Quote from original post: I know this is cluching at straws, but any chance of having EQ run on Linux?]

      We've no plans at this time to develop or release additional platform ports of the EverQuest client.

      [Quote from original post: Also, what system was EQ developed on? If you can reply to that one without revealing too much...]

      We use different tools for many different aspects of EQ. Outdoor geometry, indoor geometry, object building, character building, texture art, etc., all take place in different software packages. As for actual programming, it's done primarily in a combination of C and C++.

      As for what particular hardware platform is used for each thing, it varies between WinTel stuff, various Unix platforms, and the like.

    • The Simple Truth
      [Quote from original post: The more time spent regaining levels and leveling itself, the more months you spend on the game, and the more money Verant gets.]

      Actually we prefer to look at it from the standpoint that the longer we can keep you entertained, challenged, and intrigued, the longer you'll play the game. We believe that things earned are cherished more than things given, which is why some work is necessary in order to advance.

      [Quote from original post: Now for some, the fact that Verant MAKES you spend more months and months to "win"(achieving level 60) in this game and getting more money from its accounts may bother alot of people.]

      There is no way to "win" in EverQuest. Though some might hold "level 60" as the main goal they wish to reach, research shows that people don't "reach 60 and quit", or previously, "reach 50 and quit" like posts of this nature purport. We don't "make" you spend time to do anything. You choose a goal that you want to acheive and make the decision to do whatever is necessary to achieve it.

      [Quote from original post: The simple truth is, exp loss will not change...]

      Very true, but as mentioned above, it's not some devious thing to "make" you play longer. It's a risk that is present in order to provide challenge, making it difficult to obtain goals, which a big part of the reason that people play the game.

    • Caster Loot
      I guess that one of the situations is that things that are considered "good" caster loot aren't very diverse. Int casters want int, and that's it. Wis casters want wis, and that's it. Now, since the int/wis softcaps weren't changed in regards to Kunark, a lot of people are running around right up against 200 points. There's only so much more that can be done with them.

      Melee characters on the other hand need weapons, AC, and every other stat they can get. As a caster, I really don't care about AC items that I can get, since if I get hit I'm dead anyway.

      Basically, one of the cool things about being a melee class is that more options are open to you in regards to equipment. I think that casters will always be at a disadvantage in this area.

    • The Hole
      [Quote from original post: Has anyone taken a look at the items that will be coming from the hole to prevent more post-release item nerfs? You know what I'm talking about. Robe of Living Fungus, Wind Saber, Rod of Mourning, Vambraces of Mourning, Manastone, Mistwalker, etc..]

      Sure, we've looked at the items, but as always I cannot guarantee that post-release adjustments won't be necessary. As always, 250k+ people can find more issues than any test team could. I certainly wish this wasn't the case, but the only way we could eliminate the need for any post-production changes with certainty would be to stop any post-production enhancements to the game. I doubt anyone would like that option either.

    • Unreliable Binds
      I've heard reports about this from time to time. To my knowledge, our testing team hasn't been able to find a reliable way to reproduce this. It would be nice to hear if anyone has a way to help us track this issue down.

    • Disappearing Boats
      We're working on the problem with the disappearing boats. In the meantime, feel free to petition a GM should they appear inoperational. I do apologize for the delay in addressing this issue.

    • Trolls and Centaurs
      Centaurs are ornery and irrational creatures. It seems that they are prone to fits of rage for little to no reason, and have been known at times to attack nearly anyone. We think that it has something to do with tension in the area created by the number of people encroaching on their hunting grounds.

      As for your LD and where your corpse went, LD's will happen from time to time in internet based games for a number of reasons. As we don't have control over the majority of individual LDs, we don't do resurrections for most of them. Corpses have also been known to end up strange places when dying while LD. If it ends up someplace inaccessible (like on top of a mountain that a player cannot climb), the GMs will be happy to move it.

    • Shackle of Ro Not Fixed?
      [Quote from original post: Not to be a pain in the tuckas, but still no finger bones to complete the quest. If it does work and we are just looking in the completely wrong place tell us...You don't need to tell us where, just confirm it is working...]

      Can anyone attest to finding fingerbones? In the meantime, I'll ask the designer of this quest to take a look.

    • Crashing
      I'd be interested to know if your crashing is sound-related. Do you still have the problem if you turn off all sound support in the Windows control panel? Is your crashing zone-specific?

      I'd really like to help solve the problem, however as we don't have a whole lot of people having crashing problems, it suggests a driver or hardware-specific issue. We are continuing to work on the sound-related issues, and will probably have a new patch up in that regard in the next couple of weeks.

    • Track Corpse for Necros
      [Quote from original post: Just wanted to remind you/inform you that this spell has been broken since the release of it. It does nothing when cast. This has been verified on all servers as far as I heard.]

      Yes, we're aware of the problem. It's in our bug database. If I get an ETA on it I'll be sure to let everyone know.

    • Lack of Paladin Weapons
      I had the guys run off a report of weapons that Paladins can wield. I've a list of well over 300 1HS and 2HS weapons in game for them, and over 150 1HB and 2HB weapons. It's hard to say how many of them are class-specific.

      That said, just looking at some of the other things that have been released, it appears that there might be (stress *might*) some disparity between SK and Paly weapons in Kunark. Of course, that doesn't necessarily mean that there is a problem. If you recall, SKs were quite vocal about the lack of 1H weapons in game for them prior to Kunark.

      I'll mention this thread to the guys, but just to be clear, I'm not promising anything, and usually won't give periodic updates on item-related issues.

    • Rain Spells
      I've read the original author's post, and from what I see it's pretty accurate. Rain spells consider all targets in their field, whether or not those targets can actually be damaged. This is the way that the rain spells work. Now if this makes them completely "useless" in all cases, perhaps we need to look at them again, but at the moment they are working as designed.

    • Spellbook Follow-up
      The spellbook obscuring the view is part of the "penalty" that lower-level casters pay in exchange for the power that they will receive later in life. From an RP perspective, the caster was so focused on meditating that they perceived little about what was happening in the world around them. Back in early beta we didn't even allow casters to speak while medding.

      I think most are happy that we changed this stance in the interest of gameplay.
