Solusek Ro Split?
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:13 PM PDT
The idea of a split for the Solusek Ro server came up in a recent thread that Gordon responded to on the Gameplay Board. The following is an excerpt from the first post followed by Gordon's reply:

    I walk into Qeynos hills isee 10 people fighting for pyzjn and another 10 fighting for the miller spawns

    When people choose to go to the most popular areas to fight the most popular critters, of course there's going to be a line. That's why areas are considered popular, because there's always people there.

    At the same time that you have 10 people fighting for both of those spawns, you'll find other areas empty. People who make the choice to visit them can find more than enough to do.

    The point being that even on the most underutilized areas, the popular areas are still popular. There's always places to go where you don't have to fight over spawns if you're willing to do it.

    -Gordon

About DataRate.txt
[13 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:35 PM PDT
Gordon spoke about the datarate.txt file found on the EverQuest client in a recent response posted to the Gameplay Board:

    Please can I have the number into this file explained to me?

    Someone stated that it would help with dumps to Servers Selection Screen with cable modems.

    I have the same problem.

    It's a way for you to tell our server how fast you can accept data. The default setting of 5 is roughly where it should be for a 28.8K modem.
    If you are running something faster than than, feel free to bump it up. If you start having system problems, getting knocked off, or anything of the sort, bump it down a notch.

    I ran 9 on a 512K DSK connection without a problem. Your miliage may vary. Don't set it above 9 or below 3.

    The main benefit of bumping it up is faster zoning, and less of a chance of getting kicked off in high-bandwidth encounters (such as dragon raids).

    -Gordon

Jeff Buttler Interview At PC.IGN
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:28 PM PDT
PC.IGN recently interviewed Verant's Jeff Buttler about their upcoming EQ expansion titled the Scars of Velious. Here's a snippet from the interview:

    IGN PC: What was the decision behind making Velious basically a land-only upgrade instead of adding any new races or classes? Do you think Velious has more to offer in terms of plot and depth than the original EQ or the Kunark expansion pack?

    Jeff Butler: The Velious storyline largely precluded a new player race. Historically, the presence of the dragons, and the remote location of Velious prevented other intelligent races from establishing a foothold there. Insofar as additional classes are concerned, we are pretty satisfied with the 14 currently available, and haven’t made any plans to include more in the foreseeable future. In terms of plotline, Velious does indeed offer greater depth, with nearly the entire continent embroiled in age-old conflict.


Gordon Infobits: Saturday Edition
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:43 PM PDT
Today on the Gameplay Board, Gordon Wrinn stopped by and had the following to say:

  • Complaints Against Abashi
    [Quote from original post: Abashi didn't even know that a snared and fleeing MoB stopped when he got to really low on health.]

    Point of order here Arkanon. I've seen you make this comment in nearly a dozen threads over the past couple of weeks. I'm not sure where you are getting it from, but what you mention is hardly an EQ secret.

    I imagine that it may have come from a misquote in regards to the bard/chains issue, however that issue dealt with the amount of HP the snared creature had when they stopped, not the fact that they stop at all.

    [Quote from original post: Oh, come now. A level 50ish necro could not possibly be ignorant of the fact that snares stop runners ]

    My point exactly.

  • Apologizes & Public Reprimands
    [Quote from original post: After playing EQ on and off since release, I've finally given up on ever being right and a GM wrong. No matter how blatant of a skrew up a GM makes they will never get publicly reprimanded]

    Companies don't publicly reprimand employees. It's just not professional, and not to mention, it's actionable. These issues are dealt with in private, and believe me, we do deal with them when they come up.

    [Quote from original post: no matter how badly Verant skrews up we will never get an apology.]

    That however is certainly not true. I know that I've posted and apologized many times when decisons that were made were in the wrong. Heck, they are human, and everyone makes mistakes from time to time. Not every mistake is one where we are going to rush off and terminate someone. Most of them can be chalked up to new situations where there are substantial shades of gray.

    There are nearly 20K requests for assistance of one kind or another per day on the servers alone. The fact that the number of people who are dissatisfied by their service is so small is quite commendable. That said, we're always working to improve customer service, but I do think that it is a given that no matter how good customer service is, there will always be people who are dissatisified and bring it here to the boards. Likewise, no matter how well trained the staff is, there will always be mistakes made from time to time. All we can do is consistantly strive to reduce the number from week to week.

  • Bug That Needs Acknowledgement
    I've heard of this before. It sounds like an NPC group problem. NPCs decide whether or not they are going to attack each other based on faction, but decide whether or not to assist based on what we call a Group ID. I'll see if one of the guys can look into this tomorrow.

Event Discussion
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:22 PM PDT
Lady Dae made a lengthy post on subject of event organization. Here's the complete post:

    There seems to be some very heated discussion on this board about the rules, the decisions, and the handeling of the events.

    Events are an ongoing process where we run events, make mistakes, learn from the mistakes, and make changes so that the next events we run have less mistakes.

    Are we ever going to run a perfect event that pleases every customer in every aspect?

    I doubt it.

    We will strive for it, but with some 300,000 players, we cannot please everyone.

    With the discussion of level divisions for events, the one currently in question is levels 29 to 38. Could I simply change the rules to state that the event is now levels 34 to 38?

    Sure I could.

    What did I just do? I just told the levels 29 to 34 they could not participate and took away a chance for enjoyment for those players. Does a level 29 have a chance against a level 38?

    Probably not.

    Why do I let the Level 29 enter?

    We have schedueled the time of an Event Master, a Game Master, and Guides to running this event. If a level 29 wishes to participate, he knows that his chances of winning are slim if he faces a Level 38.

    I feel that giving the level 29 an option to participate in this event is better then having him sit on the sidelines dreaming of the day that he can also enter an event.

    Cutting the levels allowed into an event does not give us the ability to double the amount of events we run.

    Twinking is part of EverQuest. Norrath is a have and have not society just like the real world. It is also a "Grey" topic, not a black and white. If a warrior comes into the event in a full set of Crafted Armor, when everyone else is wearing chainmail, I advise the player that he cannot enter in crafted. The customer may not have another set of armor that he can simply wear. What did I just do?

    Told another customer that he cannot enter this event.

    My dedication is to allowing as many players to enjoy events as possible. This will not be hacked to pieces regardless of how many posts are made, emails are sent, or people stomping their feet.

    Perhaps in the future, we will offer some variations of the rules to have both non-specific equipment events, and specific equipment events.

    If you do not like the rules, or the way that VI is handeling the events, I ENCOURAGE you to run your own events.

    Design your event, place your own limitations on levels, equipment, spells, classes, and other factors. Set up a date and time. Set up your own entry fee, go out and get your own prizes to offer. Post your message here, the same that I do, and run your own event.

    We have, and are still making mistakes, as you will find yourself doing when running events. We are not "Gods" who do everything perfect, we do make errors.

    There have been some mistakes made by myself, and the events staff in the Test of Tactics - Best of the Best. We are going to finish this event, compile all data received, look for feedback on this board, and make revisions for the next Test of Tactics - Best of the Best.

    Perhaps the next Test of Tactics will allow full range of spells, including all enchanter spells, all activatable items, and almost no rules for those involved.

    Perhaps we will start another series of events that will be run with very limited selection of items and gear.

    We shall see, the Events Staff will review all data and feedback after the events, as we have always done when the events are completed.

    Good luck, and see you on the battlefield

    Lady Daegarmo
    Online Events Coordinator
