Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:37 PM PDT
The Government's top secret posting machine, aka the Gordon Wrinn project, was tackling posts on the Gameplay Board at full force today. Here's the lowdown:

  • Tinting the Colors
    There may very well be other ways to do the tinting (I'm *not* an engine programmer :) but our textures were designed with the way our engine handles tinting in mind. Take our standard plate armor texture. It looks very much like silver, where in fact it is various shades of gray with highlights to make it appear metalic.

    The steel plate texture really isn't suited to turning the brightness up (as your suggestions do), or at least wouldn't look right when it got up there. As I mentioned before, the best bet if we decided to implement white-plate armor would be to create new textures for that purpose. However we need to remain mindful to avoid getting into texture-swapping issues for the Voodoo1 cards.

    [Quote from original post: Besides, I stood within a few inches of you at E3 and I let you live]

    You should have introduced yourself. I met a bunch of people there, all of them being very supportive.

  • Fair is Fair
    [Quote from original post: Ya know.....that bad thing about all the complaining here is that once one class A is beefed up, they will better than another class B, C and D in some way. Then Class B and C will complain and they will get beefed up and be better than Class A and D. THEN Class D complains and gets beefed up and is better then Class A and Class C. THEN Class C complains.....See where this is going??]

    You've hit the nail on the head. This is exactly what happens, and it's often not even the same group of people, just other people who play the class that felt that the first "beef up" did nothing to help the class, and want something else "beefed up" that they feel is important.

    This isn't a planned sequence of events and I doubt that very many do it consciously, it just works out this way. This is also the same phenomenon that makes "nerfing" necessary in some cases, making exclusively "tuning up" issues a bad long term plan.

    One thing that pretty much everyone understands and agrees with is that some classes must be better at some things than other classes, and must be bettered at some things by other classes. The disagreement that comes in is the specific instances that their particular class is chosen to be inferior, and also the extent to which a penalty makes up or fails to make up for an advantage.

    One frequent issue that comes up is "the list". Someone perceives their class as the last on the list, and uses that as the explanation for why their class should be buffed up. For instance, lately I've seen many complaints from people who state that the ranger is the weakest of the hybrids in overall power. Given that we can't get anyone to agree on "equality" (and therefore don't aim for equality as a goal), there will always be this perceived list. Some class will always be at the bottom of this perceived list and not be happy about it.

    These are the reasons that I stress that equality of the classes is not our goal. We don't want the classes to be able to do the same thing to the same proficiency, at any given time, because to do so would invalidate entire class-based system.

    Balance is not a single point, it is a range in the overall purpose of the class, made up by its offensive power, its defensive abilities, its special skills, and its utility (what it contributes to others).

    [Quote from original post: The problem is that Verant has succumbed to whining and class envy in the past, so the flood gates are now open]

    That's the impression and the hesitation that I was talking about earlier. There are a bunch of people out there who felt that we "gave in" on many issues that have come up in the past 6 months, and feel that sooner or later we'll give in on any issue. But we've never just "given in" on an issue, and have explained the reasoning we followed before deciding to make an upgrade.

    Many people think that we don't understand their class issue or that we are missing the point, and if only they can explain the situation better that we'll have no choice but to agree. It's part of the player's passion about the game, but it's also the same passion that keeps us firm on some issues.

    We investigate these issues as they come up. A number of people here at the company read the boards and are as well-versed in the player's concerns as I am. Sometimes though, as painful as it is to hear about how people are upset or how they are leaving the game, we disagree. We don't like to disagree. We don't like get any satisfaction out of telling someone no.

    Sometimes it is just necessary.

  • Taunting
    Though I don't want to get too deep into the mechanics, everything taunts the same amount according to damage with the exception of spells which can have specific amounts of "taunt" set on a per-spell basis.

    In other words, if a ranger and a warrior both hit for 200 damage in a 10-second period, they both have taunted the same amount. What is important is the amount of damage (taunt/hate/etc) done over a period of time. Creatures generally perceive those who have done a lot of damage recently over those who have done more damage total.

    Rangers, rogues, and monks, who typically dual wield 1H weapons usually catch the most heat here because the speed of 1H weapons usually has them hitting closer to the average damage of their weapons over a given time period. In other words, due to the speed of their weapons they have a better chance to make their average. In other words, over 20 seconds, a warrior might do 1000 damage, and a ranger 900, but at the 10 second mark the ranger might have been outdamaging the warrior.

    All things being equal, creatures that have freedom of movement will go after the character that they hate the most, whereas creatures that are somehow encumbered (rooted, snared, etc.) will go after the character that is the closest. Try it out sometime.

  • The 'Evolving' Story Line
    [Quote from original post: You have mentioned this so called "Evolving EQ Story Line" in several past posts, but 99% of the players have no idea what this is. It seems a shame that you are putting a lot of resources into this storyline, so why waste this effort?]

    You're right. It is a little bit frustrating to receive complaints about how things should change and how plots should evolve, then get more complaints every time we make a change or evolve a plot. We are, however, committed to moving the stories along.

    One day someone notices that a camp that used to have one thing in it now has another thing. It's up to them to try and figure out why that is the case. Some will assume that we did it for some out-of-game reason, to hurt their ability to solo, to kill them so that they'll play the game longer, or to upset them in general. Some others will assume that there must be a reason for it, and will seek out information about the area.

    So the effort is not a waste, it's for that second group of people.

    [Quote from original post: Why dont you guys come up with a method of informing the player base as to what the current storyline developments are as I'm sure most if not all would be interested in it.]

    This is definitely planned. We're still working on a format for releasing this information. I would caution that even when we do this, we won't be releasing information about what something is about until it has already transpired. Ideally we will provide enough clues in game so that people who wish to participate can figure out what the deal is and increase their chances of being there when everything happens. It's after that that we could have a tale told explaining what everything was about.

  • Lord Nagafen Claws YOU for 225pts of Damage
    There are cases where we placed numbers in game due to popular opinion, even though our feeling was that this was not completely in line with what we had originally planned. Though these cases exist, it doesn't mean that we would make the same exception for every case of that in the game.

    In other words, reporting the strength of melee attacks in numbers rather than in a generic qualitative context (Lord Nagafen scratches you, versus Lord Nagafen claws you, versus Lord Nagafen nearly decapitates you) if the exception to the rule, not the rule itself.

    It was decided at the time that points were put in for melee damage that spells would remain mysterious in nature. Another exception to the rule is reporting spell damage done by the player casting the spell (Hit by non-melee for 81 damage!). This was put in during beta to assist in the testing of spells, and was subsequently left in as a matter of convenience.

  • Verant Admits Downtime is a Problem?
    That post had nothing to do with downtime, but addressed that the E3 showing showed off dungeons and such that are meant to be tackled by groups. Since the characters were going through the areas solo to show them off, certain adjustments to the characters were necessary.

    For instnace, part of Charasis was shown. I think that everyone is well aware that any solo character, as the only person in that dungeon, wouldn't stand a chance under normal circumstances.

    The purpose of showing off those areas was to show the art, and some of the action part of the game. I think that most are aware that there is more to the game than pure unadulterated action.

  • Necro Bug?
    [Quote from original post: The Lvl 44 Necro Spell Asystole is doing FULL dmg in PvP. I know that PvP sometimes seems less important then the other issues, but a full damage spell is extremely unbalancing. Many have /bug'd and /feedback'd this and have still gotten no response.

    If you would PLEASE look into it I would appreciate it!

    ===from another thread===

    There is also a Necro in my guild that said some of the Lifetaps are doing 100%. I have not tested the lifetaps however Asystole I have tested.

    I know that PvP sometimes takes second seat to the other servers, but we have been trying to]

    Is there anyone else who can confirm this? I just checked on VZ and found it doing the reduced damage that it should be doing.

  • Banning Incident
    As we've mentioned before, we don't comment on personal account issues with anyone but the owner of the account. If they then wish to post the exchange of information, they can then do that at their discretion. It doesn't appear that the owner of the account is even among the people here.

    That said, I would like to stress that the existance of the profanity filter is not a license to be vulgar online. It exists to spare people from most conversational and unintentional profanity, not specific and directed harassment. As many people here on the board can attest, profanity filters are very easy to get around intentionally by those who aren't able to express themselves without resorting to that type of behavior.

    EQ is a family game and we won't tolerate directed harassment. Sure, the occasional s-word or f-word isn't going to get someone more than a friendly reminder or a warning for the first offense, however strong directed sexually explicit commentary, real-world racial slurs, or other things that are similarly offensive and undesired could very well result in a suspension or banning on the first offense.

    There are some things that are just not acceptable or tolerated for any reason.

  • Invisibiltiy Nerfed?
    [Quote from original post: Question - Why do you keep making invisibility/camo worthless ?
    Then the game started, very few things could see through it.
    As the game progressed, magic casters could see through it.
    Then - non-casting lvl 35 npc's started seeing through it (giants)
    Then you made zones where EVERYTHING sees through it.
    Now - raptors in an outdoor zone?]

    Like everything else in the game, nothing is a sure thing except the fact that nothing is a sure thing :) We may have increased the number of things that can naturally see through invisibility with Kunark, but as for the rest of the world I think that your observations are mostly based on what you've witnessed as leveling up.

  • Summoned Items
    [Quote from original post: Why is it that the summoned items by mages and such don't have a 'NO RENT' tag? This become very confusing and can lead to deception. Imagine if I summon up a coldstone. Then I get soem sucker to buy it for even 10 PP!? You can't expect people to know every item out there and what it does or the fact that it is summoned. C'mon, put a NO RENT or SUMMONED tag on them... please! :)]

    To be honest, I'm not sure if this is the case for all summoned items (I've only looked at a few myself), but they show as summoned if you cast identify upon them.

  • Huge Experience Loss to Gullerback
    This is one of the long-standing myths in EQ, that what kills you has some bearing on how much experience you lose from the death.

    Experience loss on death is based solely on your level and the amount of experience that you have (total). It may appear that you lose more in certain cases, but this is due to the resolution of the experience bar and also where you are in the particular level.

  • Timorous Deep
    We should have pre-announced changes about Timorous Deep due to the nature of the changes there.

    However, this isn't really the same thing. The dark elves moving into that camp are part of the evolving storyline of the game. These types of changes never get announced, as it's meant to be discovered and investigated by the players as they come upon the new situation. In this particular case, the Dark Elves moved in and took over the camp there.

    As one customer mentioned the other day about this issue, "Dark elves don't send out press releases." :)

  • If we found a problem with a class, would you not fix it?
    I didn't say that we wouldn't fix it if we came across a glaring problem, I said that we'd be hesitant. We'll always do what we think is best for the game, even if it does cause us an embarassment or future problems.

    We really don't have any problem admitting when we are wrong. I've taken heat a few times for things like that myself.

  • Bad Wizard Experience
    I'm really sorry that you had a bad experience here. I get a little upset myself when I manage to die with a full load of mana. This doesn't really outline any problems with the wizard class though. I think that any class would have been in deep trouble in the situation that you mentioned.

  • A GM Mishap?
    Well, the story here is very different from that that was told to the GM. In any case, the GM did ask the original author to email the screenshots (that he indicated he had) to the eqmail@verant.com address. As of yesterday the GM still hadn't received the mail.

    [Quote from original post: The problem is GMs have noone that they answer too and If they do, players have no way to contact that supervisor. Oh - and I have no idea if the story is true or not - i just know that the setup as far as customer service and levels of escalation, is unlike any successful service provider I know of.]

    We certainly do have escalation levels much like other customer service organizations, but it is true that we do have to limit access to them. GMs are empowered to make decisions on their server. If you think that decision was incorrect, the proper place to address your issue is the eqmail@verant.com address. It will be forwarded to the appropriate person, who will review the issue and get involved if the complaint has merit.

    The reason why we have to limit access to higher levels of escalation is that our service is very service-intensive, meaning that a large percentage of people require assistance regularly. In many cases, the assistance that is requested cannot be given due to concerns about fairness and the integrity of the game. If we allowed unhindered escalation, people would start "climbing the chain" every time they were turned down hopeing to get the answer they want further up.

    Most other organizations don't have this problem because they are able to render assistance to the satisfaction of the customer in the vast majority of cases, with escalations being the exception to the rule. If we did not empower GMs to make decisions, escalations would be the rule, in which case we'd have to higher more supervisors than GMs, and then there'd be a question of how easy would it be to get to the supervisor's supervisor :)

  • Regent Symbol of Innoruuk
    [Quote from original post: Before you post you should know what you are talking about. Abashi said in the post one very pissed of cleric that it was being changed because it was unbalanceing.]

    Just to clarify, I said that it was changed because the wrong spell effect was assigned. When people asked why we didn't let those who had already earned it keep it the way it was (like Guise), I then replied that it was changed retroactively because it was too unbalancing.

  • Verant's Stance
    [Quote from original post: To this end we have set up a calendar to schedule planes time and a rotation to deal with who gets turns on the different dragons. This calendar has been a point of debate for awhile and has gone through a couple revisions, however, the community as a whole agrees that it is the best solution for fairly distributing time for these much sought after camps. My question is this, when a server community decides to institute something like this, which is, in spirit, what Verant is attempting to do with PnP rules, can that community count on any support from Verant to keep such a system in place?]

    The "server community" that you speak of in this case seems to be the group of large guilds. This system really isn't very fair for those in the smaller guilds or those without guilds who have as much rights to play the game and participate in various adventures as anyone else.

    Though we commend any steps that people take to increase their respective abilities to work together, and certainly won't step in when *all parties are in agreement*, we can't bind dissenters to the decisions of the large guilds on the server. In the event that people to not agree, the rules that we've set forth are the ones that apply.

  • Nerfing Solo'able Zones
    [Quote from original post: Can anyone explain why there is such a witch hunt on all the soloable areas post 50? I picked a necro because i like to solo. All the spots are getting nerfed one after the other and it isnt funny anymore. Guards (ok i agree) , Brownies, now the raptors. Is it part of The Vision (/rude) that noone solo anymore?]

    Not at all. All classes that were previously able to solo can still solo. We don't, however, want people to be able to solo without reasonable risk, at least not less risk than a group player faces in a typical group encounter.

    Soloing should get more and more difficult with each level. What I mean is that soloing a level 45 creature when you are level 47 shouldn't be as easy as soloing a level 35 creature when you were 37 was.

  • Idea Suggestion
    It would be very hard to come up with new information that could give a particular player an idea how they would fair against a creature since there's no way to take into account several variables that can't be quantified in code. For instance, there might be some encounters where a solo rogue might not stand a chance, where a solo necromancer might have no problem. Tactics would need to be considered as well, for instance a necromancer might have no problems in a particular encounter if they kite, but might not stand a chance if they fight toe to toe.

    We all know that the /con message is tied solely to level, but in a way is kind of represents the chances that you have at optimal efficiency. A 50th warrior needs to be in a full balanced group to function optimally, whereas a level 1 warrior functions optimally as a soloer.

    /con is probably as good as it can get without just tossing it out the window and having it report the level.

  • The Vision (TM)
    I get the impression that some think that we have a short document someplace stored under lock and key in a vault entitled "The Vision(tm)" :)

    The Vision(tm) is not a simple easily quantifiable thing that can be put up on a web site for consumption and debate.

    It is the original EQ design document and world history (several hundred pages). It is every email that has been sent to the EQ team's mailing list. It is every agreement and idea that came out of every team meeting. It is the understandings reached over the course of hundreds of late nights of feasting on cold pizza and warm soda. It is the desires of the playerbase as a whole. It is a living idea and philosophy that constantly changes to adapt to new situations while remaining a firm point of reference that guides what can or cannot be. It is a fluid and complex collection of thoughts that represent the passion and ambition of those behind it.

    It is a goal.


  • Overcrowding
    [Quote from original post: The server isn't crowded, the zones are. Bull. They also said back in November that they would keep the peak populations at 1800, even after the expansion. I just want them to keep that promise.]

    I do sympathize with those on the more crowded servers. We do think that the expansion definitely has people spread out more and has aleviated much of the problem. I do, however, want to clarify the quote above. When we say things like "should" or "probably" or state a goal it's not unusual for people to take it as a promise.

    In this case we've said that our target population is 1800 per server. We also said that we wouldn't raise that target when we produced the expansion. This remains our target, but we didn't "promise" to reduce every server to 1800 users at peak like the quote suggests.

  • The Lost Corpses
    [Quote from original post: The issue I see with the lost corpse issue is that you underrate the impact of the loss. You have posted that it's invonvenient but not that big a deal to replace the items, where many if not most post-50 chars seem to feel it would make them leave the game rather than spend months re-camping items.]

    We haven't underrated the impact of the loss. It hurts to lose a corpse, it hurts bad. However it is true that one people are in the high levels that allow exploration of some of the areas were that risk is not insignificant, they usually have some backup equipment. It doesn't equate to starting over from scratch. They also typically have friends who can also help should this happen.

    Corpse loss is part of the risk that people absorb when entering an area that they know little about, or an area known to be extremely dangerous. It is the various risks themselves that make an area "extremely dangerous". An area with equal risk of death, but without the risk of possible corpse loss, is not as dangerous as an area that has that risk.

For them, the leveling is the "fun" that the game provides. I just got an email today from a gentleman who has 4 character over 50th level, and I would guess that he falls in this playstyle.

    Other playstyles do exist.

    For instance, a common playstyle is the guy who hates to level, but wants to be as powerful as possible, so powers up his character to max and then goes off to have "fun". These are the people who have one high-level, maybe a low-to-mid level secondary, and primarily play their high-level character.

    There are other people for whom the concepts of leveling and power are only a secondary part of the game. They play first to have fun and worry not about maximizing their XP acquisition rate. What this type of gamer and the one above find "fun" are often the same thing, however the one above doesn't begin to have this fun until the character is maxed. We typically don't know what level these people are as they rarely talk about it, and if queried, usually have a higher p-time to level ratio than the other types.

    So, to recap, if I had to place labels on each type of gamer:

    1. The Powerleveler (Leveling is fun)
    2. The ???? (having trouble coming up with a label) (Doesn't like leveling, thinks adventuring, socializing, and exploration is fun, but can't have that fun until the character is maxed.)
    3. The Social Gamer (Is not concerned with leveling, and thinks adventuring, socializing, and exploration is fun).

    Note: Let's not confuse this with "The Roleplayer" which really isn't a playstyle in this regard. Most roleplayers would fall under #3, but I have met many 1s and 2s as well. Also, the playstyles aren't firm, but more of a scale that wraps back to #1 after #3. Players do fall in between the different numbers. In addition, there are a couple other less common playstyles, such as the Experimenter, who has a bunch of characters, low-to-mid level.

    Giving levels 51-60 a steep experience requirement should increase enjoyment for those close to a #1, would not affect those close to a #3, and I can see how it might make things difficult for those who tend towards #2. Our assumptions would be that there are more people who have tendencies towards #1 than #2.

    -Gordon
