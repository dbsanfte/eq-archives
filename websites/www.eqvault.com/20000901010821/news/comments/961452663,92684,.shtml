How does GM abuse result from Verant's email network? Well, GM (and Guides) often redirect valid requests to various Verant email, when they know full well that those emails won't be answered. Those emails follow:

questhelp@verant.com
eqmail@verant.com
eqcs@verant.com

Furthermore, GMs abuse players by making it MORE DIFFICULT to get redress for problems, either real or perceived. Instead, we were to redirect to one of the emails above if the issue needed further examination and a GM was not present.

What are the practical consequences of this setup? The most noticable is that players who have completed quests, but not received the appropriate items often have to wait a long time to get their items, if they get them at all. If you ask a Guide or GM for help, you just get redirected again.

This is one that I experienced directly a few times as a Guide. The most glaring example of this kind of behvaior involves a bug with an NPC named "scraps" in East Freeport. At least 2 people a day were killed by this bug, and guides could do NOTHING about it! Terrible!

Interesting to note is that Verant says the onus is on the player to prove a bug death, but players have few tools to prove such deaths.

Again, the practical effects of this were particularly glaring in one routine case that because of the rules, forced me to abuse a player. A player claimed he had fallen for 10K in fearplane. With the resurrection time limit in force, I couldn't guarantee a resurrection in the time alloted.
