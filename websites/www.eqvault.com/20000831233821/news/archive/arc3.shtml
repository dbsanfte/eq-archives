Gordon Infobits
- Aurum @ 5:45 PM PDT
More posts to report tonight that Gordon made on the Gameplay Board today:

  • Multiple Copies of Zones
    [Quote from original post: This would be great, end camping, end reserve dates for planes, and the play nice rules would cease cause there would be nothin to complain about.]

    It would only really "end camping" to the extent that "giving everyone their own NPC to camp" ends camping. It might end lists, but it wouldn't really end camping. It would have the effect of blowing up the game economy.

    [Quote from original post: Why again can't Verant make it so that there are multiple copies of a zone?]

    We "can't" due to the server load that maintaining a dynamically generated zones would require. There would also be problems for people who wanted to "meet up" in a dungeon, as they would have a hard time getting in the same copy.

    It is, however, closer to the point that we "won't", due to its overall impact upon the game. Everyone exists in the same world, and it is that world that we have to manage. Multiple copies of zones would cause the world to dynamically fluctuate in size and content making it impossible to manage.

  • Particle Graphic Changed?
    That's strange. I've also never seen any effects while it [particle effects] was set to off. If you have any further info, I'd be happy to look into it.

  • Request From a Charity Buffer
    That's a good thought. I'll mention it.

  • Two Weapons and a Shield?
    You aren't dual-wielding with the weapon in the ranged slot, it just shows up when you put it in. It's the same thing that causes a bow to magically appear when shooting an arrow. The character using the bow won't switch back to melee weapons until he's in melee range and initiates an attack.

  • Character Missing
    There was a bug that allowed this to happen at one time under very special circumstances, but that was corrected 6 or 7 months ago. Let me ask, were you moved from one of the other servers to Druzzil?

  • Trolls & Ogres
    [Quote from original post: So what I am proposing is, that Trolls have a larger set of racial abilities and hence Ogres are not in balance. Especially when regen is factored in, which makes soloing more practical.]

    As other people have mentioned here, ogres also cannot be stunned from the front. Strength and stamina is also significantly higher than in trolls. Troll stats are more in line with the barbarian in that regard. Finally, it's fairly well known that trolls require more experience to level than ogres. As mentioned in the FAQ, though differing experience requirements can't be traded directly for primary balance components, they do help buffer things such as racial abilities.

  • Magicians Pets Too Powerful?
    I agree that pets are a bit too powerful at level 50, however since we've reduced their power at least 3 times since release, we're a little apprehensive about doing so further. Instead we reduced the amount of the gain in power from 50 to 60 for those pets, both magician and necro. At 60th level, their power is about right where they should be for that level.

    One thing that I do want to note here is that though the pets are a bit too powerful at 50th, it doesn't create a "balance" issue in the greater sense of the word. The power of those pets does not invalidate the need for other classes in the group. For instance, you never see someone ooc, "group looking for tank or mage/necro with pet for Sebilis run". There's also issues in the game that often make pets more dangerous to have than not. These issues serve to balance the pets, though they are powerful in one-on-one combat. Players on the PvP servers will note that their pets do less damage in PvP combat. This is for the reason just mentioned.

  • Monks and Parry Skill
    Yes, you are correct, monks don't parry. I was just rattling off defensive skills and slipped that one in accidentally.


Camping Discussion
[11 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:44 PM PDT
Gordon started a discussion on camping today's on the Gameplay Board. Here's what he said:

    I had a thought earlier and figured we could have a bit of a discussion about camping. Now before everyone jumps in and says, "because the game forces you to", let's agree that for whatever reason, people decide to camp. Perhaps so many people are camping that newcomers feel that they have to camp, but you still have to make that choice: Am I going to camp or am I going to adventure? Perhaps the game encourages it by rewarding camping. If that is the case, wouldn’t the logical solution be to remove those rewards? More on this later…

    So here's some questions:

    1. Why camp?

    Is it because you feel it is more profitable? Is it more profitable to sit in an overcrowded dungeon fighting over spawns and being on loot lists than moving out into the lesser-used areas of the world? I'm reminded of a recent Kunark review where the reviewer, an avid EverQuest player, indicated that there's more than enough room in Kunark to go off someplace with a group, but he couldn't get his group to do anything but sit in one area and camp a spawn.

    2. Why not spread out to some of the lesser-used areas?

    This question will inevitably bring about discussions of bindpoints, risk, reward, etc, or talk about how loot "sucks" in other areas. However, at the same time it begs the question, why do people choose to do something that they find boring and without challenge. Again I ask, is loot in any unused area really worse than what you'll get by camping a crowded dungeon, fighting over spawns, and being on a list? Certainly risk is less, but risk is essentially a small amount of experience in a well-balanced group. Do you feel that there is more net gain in the crowded dungeons? Can two groups taking turns killing 3 critters that spawn every 20 minutes in a room really be more profitable than going off someplace on your own, even given increased risk?

    3. Do you actually enjoy camping?

    There are people who do, and I'd like to get an impression of how many people honestly prefer camping. We've made several changes in the past few months to make camping less profitable, leading to a slew of complaints from this group, so I know that they are out there.

    4. What do you propose should be done?

    Some say that random loot tables are the only way to stop camping, but people don't just camp loot, they camp experience as well. We've all seen people move through the game staking out solitary 6 minute spawns for the purpose of camping experience.

    Keep in mind that anything you suggest has to scale to 2K people online per server, all trying to do the same quest.

    ---opinion---
    My feeling is that people camp because they feel it is more profitable, whatever "profitable" means to them. I personally consider "having fun" to be "profitable", to an extent greater than getting experience or loot. I won't go in certain dungeons because they are camped. I enjoy places where you zone in and you might be the only group there. Other people however often value loot and experience over fun, or believe that they can only have fun by acquiring loot or experience. Some people feel that they can only go have fun once they've acquired all of the loot and experience that they'll ever need.

    I really don't think that random loot is an option for loot-camping, as people will still pick a spawn to camp and kill there until they get the item. Certainly, it won't lead to everyone camping the same NPC, but will still result in all of the NPCs being camped. I feel that as long as there exists the relative assurance that "If I spend enough time in this one area, I will sooner or later get the item".

    As far as experience camping goes, I feel we can only get rid of it if we make it less profitable than other things people could do. Perhaps if we could programmatically detect XP camping, we could put something in to counter it. We've been adjusting spawn times that were set incorrectly on some NPCs in the world, causing them to spawn too rapidly, leading to it being a profitable camp, but what else do you think that we should do?
    ---opinion---

    My last question is:

    5. If we do whatever is necessary to get rid of camping, by making it less profitable, how many people will that *really* upset?

    People camp because it's easier, faster, safer, and more profitable to do so than to move through areas and do something more exciting. People dislike camping because it's boring (without challenge), yet this situation is created by it being easier, faster, safer, and more profitable. Completely randomizing loot will only make more NPCs worthy of camping, not reduce the amount of camping going on.

    Logic suggests that if people camp because it’s easier, faster, safer, and more profitable, the way to stop camping would be to make it harder, slower, more risky, and less profitable. Yet, any time we've done that with an aspect of the game, it creates a *large* number of upset people here on the boards. If we make something harder, we're trying to eliminate soloing. If we make it slower, we're trying to hurt the casual gamer. If we make it more risky, we're trying to make you die more, which is supposed to make you play longer and pay us more. If we make it less profitable, we're trying to force everyone to join uberguilds.

    In all honesty, any time we’ve made something harder, slower, more risky, and less profitable, we’ve been trying to motivate people to play the game in a manner that we would consider more fun. Should we stop trying to do that? If yes, should we stop trying to address camping?

    -Gordon

    August 17th, 2:00pm
    ------------------------

    *Updated Patch Message*

    Some additional changes were included in today's patch that were not listed in the patch
    message earlier today. This patch message is a replacement for the one posted earlier:

    *Miscellaneous Changes*

    - /trackplayers now supports a "GROUP" option, allowing trackers to exclude all player
    characters, except ones in their current group.
    - The EQCLIENT.INI has some new features, including one allowing logging (/log) to be on by
    default. Updated documentation for the EQCLIENT.INI file is at the end of this patch
    message.
    - Activatable items, such as Journeyman's Boots, can now be activated by the corresponding
    number-key if placed in a hotbox.
    - Items that are "out of charges" now display "Charges: None" when inspecting the item.
    - Attack-speed buffs and debuffs will now stack correctly. In other words, lower level
    spells will no longer overwrite higher level spells of the same type.
    - The "U" key (short for "use") has had its functionality enhanced. It now does the
    equivalent of a single left-click and single right-click at the center of the screen. In
    addition to opening doors, it will now consider and inspect PCs, consider NPCs, and use
    bankers, merchants, and guildmasters.
    - Fixed the way that spell-effect icons are displayed on the UI. Previously, after adding
    several "buffs" to a character, one of the icons would be placed down in the chat window.

    *Chat/Login Server*

    This patch contains eqmain.dll, which is used by the EverQuest login/chat frontend. The
    following changes have been implemented:

    - #help now accepts arguments. #help #command will return help for the requested command.
    - Errors issued within the chat/login server frontend now display an "Error Number". Please
    have this error number ready when requesting technical assistance.

    *Looking for Group Flag*

    Players can now indicate that they are "looking for a group" by typing /LFG. This command
    will flag your character in both zone-specific /who lists, and the server-wide "/who all"
    list with "LFG". The LFG flag will remain active until you either enter a group or log out.
    Players can search for other players who are LFG by using filters in the /who command. For
    instance, to find rangers between level 40 and 50 who are looking for a group across the
    server, type "/who all ranger 40 50 lfg".

    It is our hope that this flag will make it easier for players to seek out group members,
    allowing for more adventuring with less preparation time.

    *Updated EQCLIENT.INI Documentation*

    If the file does not exist it will be generated by eqgame.exe with default values. If an
    entry in the file is missing it will be added by eqgame.exe with default values.

    The [TextColors] section contains entries for various text colors in the client. All colors
    are to be set with Red, Green, and Blue values, in decimal. Valid settings for each color
    range between 0 and 255. "Black" (0 red, 0 green, and 0 blue) will be white in overlay mode,
    and black in normal mode.

    Here is a list of user colors to use as a guide when editing the file:

    1 - Say
    2 - Tell
    3 - Group
    4 - Guild
    5 - OOC
    6 - Auction
    7 - Shout
    8 - Emote
    9 - Spells (meming, scribing, casting, etc.)
    10 - You hit other
    11 - Other hits you
    12 - You miss other
    13 - Other misses you
    14 - Some broadcasts (duels)
    15 - Skills (ups, non-combat use, etc.)
    16 - Disciplines or special abilities
    17 - Unused at this time
    18 - Default text and stuff you type
    19 - Unused at this time
    20 - Merchant Offer Price
    21 - Merchant Buy/Sell
    22 - Your death message
    23 - Others death message
    24 - Other damage other
    25 - Other miss other
    26 - /who command
    27 - yell for help
    28 - Hit for non-melee
    29 - Spell worn off
    30 - Money splits
    31 - Loot message
    32 - Unused at this time

    The [Defaults] section contains some default setting for the client. Here is a list of valid
    settings:

    RunMode - TRUE for run, FALSE for walk.
    ScreenMode - NORMAL for the dafault window view, FULL for the full view with overlays
    Log - TRUE for logging on, FALSE for logging off


    - The EverQuest Team

Gordon Wrinn made a few more posts recently on the Gameplay Board. Here's the skinny:

  • Play Nice
    [Quote from original post: BTW, this maybe shoulda been on the rants board, but perhaps GW will come down and answer this mere mortal....why why why do you create rules that are detrimental to the players? Play nice started off as a way to prevent KSing and verbal abuse. Play nice should not be exploited as a means of allowing everyone the same chance at a spot in line for toilet paper...err...getting xp.]

    The Play Nice Policies were created because a growing number of customers were being subordinated by other, more powerful, groups of players. These other groups would claim "ownership" of spawns, loot drops, and even entire zones, refusing to allow other people a chance to experience them. On some servers, guilds had certain areas camped 24x7.

    The play nice policies, in regards to spawns and camping, are designed to promote equal access to the areas. If a group can get into a room, they have as much right to be there as anyone else. Are the policies perfect? No, of course what is? No matter what policy we adopt, there will be people who are helped by the policy, and others that are hurt. Sometimes the policy may help them in one instance and hurt in another.

    As I've mentioned in the past, if anyone has any ideas for policies that are easy to understand, are enforcable, do not require a larger CS team than before or after the Play nice policies, and will please everyone, I'd be happy to discuss them.

  • Reimbursement For Next Patch Spell Change?
    We're fairly open about the fact that things in game are subject to change at any time. This includes the prospect of both positive and negative changes. People making trades in game need to be aware that that there exists a chance that any item is likely to drop in value or usefulness after you buy it. It's sort of like buying stock in a company in that regard.

    The spell was never supposed to be usable by magicians, who ended up able to use it only because the level restriction was transposed by the designer. I really wish we'd caught this sooner.

  • "Witch" Post Removed
    Hello all, I just deleted a post suggesting that a new class ("Witch") be created. The thread was deleted because it degenerated into the disparaging of practiced religion (Wicca).

    As a note to the original author of that post, as that certainly wasn't his intention, we wouldn't create a class by that name due to the fact that it does apply to a currently practiced religion. The same doesn't apply to reference to "priests" or "monks", as those are nondescript titles in nature, and as such not indicative of any particular religion.

  • Wizard Draughts
    The Draught line was created to give wizards the option of doing less damage at similar mana/damage ratio compared to ice comet, and also to give them similar spells across the three elements that the wizards control. The reasoning behind "less damage" was to reduce the amount that the wizard would aggro the target. Had we wanted to continue along the same lines as ice comet, we could have given similar damage spells with equal damage, mana, and casting time requirements.

    In this case, the additional time required to stack two draughts versus one ice comet is what you "pay" for the reduced amount that you aggro the target.
