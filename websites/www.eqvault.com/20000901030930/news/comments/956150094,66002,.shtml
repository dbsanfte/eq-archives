Role playing is something you find a group to do, not a whole server. I have found that just adventuring with one other friend and roleplaying off each other is enough to have a "great time roleplaying". If I join a random group it soon becomes appearent wether or not this is a role playing group...if its not I still have a good time, thats why I enjoy EQ. I still put out a couple funny lines or whatnot and can get even a dead serious group chuckling or role playing a bit. Even in these "cold" groups you can really liven things up by roleplaying taunts and such while fighting. Work on some fun emotes, honestly I find the best way to get the people you are playing with into a roleplaying mood is with humor. Just don't expect everyone to stop playing, head to the nearest bar, nock back a few and spend the evening getting drunk and telling tales of adventure. If thats what you really want, go to the entrance to very heavily populated zones with a couple kegs and start getting all the folks who are waiting for a group into a drunken furor. This is your only captive audience. Other tips on getting your roleplaying fix in: join a role playing guild and role play, but for godsakes don't join a roleplaying guild just so you can all sit in guildsay and bitch about how nobody is roleplaying. I had a great roleplaying experience that just existed within tells from an unknown person. I was playing my shadow knight and someone was shouting for a cure disease...I offered to cut off the offending limb but he refused, my character (a troll) accused him of wanting to keep such a good meal all to himself. This started a roleplaying thread which lasted quite some time and we both had a good experience, and this diseased fellow wasn't role playing at all until I came along. There's another hint for ya, many people are more than willing to roleplay during the course of their adventures, all you have to do to key them off is to start roleplaying to them.

in conclusion: be entertaining, lead by example, don't make people stop what they are doing, don't assume that because someone isn't roleplaying that they won't...and don't bitch about people not roleplaying, man this takes a good attitude and positive energy to make it a good and fun experience for all.

In concluding my conclusion: NEVER ROLEPLAY AN EVIL RACE/CHARACTER AS AN ANTI-SOCIAL CUSS...you'll role play with yourself in isolation, what fun is that?

#16   Name: Elan (jch@ficsgrp.com)   Date: 4/20 @ 14:29 PM PST
Hmmmm. Defining an approach for a serious role-play server in Everquest. OK. My ideas would be:

People and leadership are the key. Start hunting through the Guide community to find the most effective role-players. Have GMs look at Players on their servers also. Spend some time watching and finding people who are truly role players. And then make them Guides on the new server.

These guides will have a different twist. They will be ENCOURAGED to make their guide character their play character and to interact with people. They will buff people who are role-playing. They will be role-playing a leader of their community. Make them Dukes and Lords. Form nobles counsels. It takes no new coding, just some inventive player guides. And promote worthy players to the rank of noble (make them Guides) on rare occasion where it is truly justified and the player wishes to do so.

There will be more Guides than on any other server. Several per race in fact. With a Senior Guide of each race. These seniors can work out promotion tracks through the noble titles based purely on role-playing. The GM-Admin simply makes sure that each city has a Senior to lead it (and changes them when necessary). These nobles will generally stay around their home city, but they will venture out at times. They will role-play always and they will watch for others to do the same, rewarding those who do, and perhaps penalizing those who do not.

Coding changes? Remove /roleplay. It is on all the time. That should be simply enough. Leave the communications channels. Even /ooc. People can turn it off if they wish. No change there. One modification to faction processing. With a high enough faction, city guards will always take your side. If both are that high, they stay out of it. Hopefully that is not a huge change. And lastly, give the GM-Admin one important tool a player faction edit tool. It is simply an editor that alters factions on the database. That is all the coding changes.

Allow the nobles of a city to declare a person welcome or not. If the noble counsel for a city so votes, the GM will edit the faction of a player to make them neutral in a city or killed on sight. Unruly behavior is reason enough for banishment from a town. Laws are enforced but people can make judgements. Make all the Guide nobles very popular with their home city guards. If they take a side in any fight, the guards always aide them. And they can get into fights. Make the server pure PVP. No restrictions at all with full experience loss but no looting. Just as the nobles can grant favors such as buffs, so can they punish. And if a player causes havoc with fellows, he may soon find himself unwelcome in any city in the land. The nobles will banish him to play as an outcast and in extreme cases might even call quests and offer rewards for his head.

An occasional race war? Sure. The nobles of two cities could declare a war to begin two days hence and lead armies of players against one another all across one of the continents. Those wishing not to participate could stay away. Guilds based on cities of loyalty? Perhaps. It would be a great way to track those who wish to take part. And let players create guilds that are aligned with none of the nobles. A guild of outcasts then? It could happen. The key is you have people making decisions. Watching when necessary the same way guilds today might watch for exploiters except this time as avengers who might take sides in a fight or even vote to expel or offer bounty for someone.

Would there be calls of favoritism by the nobles? Absolutely. And thats not a bad thing. People will find communities they mix with. It will not necessarily be races based. A dark elf may simply be hailed as a hero of Freeport and welcomed in. Perhaps his own people will disavow him. And people who do not want to have such interaction dont have to. There are lots of regular servers out there for them. But relationships will be critical. It all boils down to having that first good set of nobles. Start looking for them now. And I am sure they can come up with far better than I was able to dream up here. This is only a start. Some ideas might be thrown out entirely. Other might be expanded upon significantly. But if you find the proper leadership and give them the authority to act amazing things might happen. Some of the Text MUDs have been going strong for a very long time due to a loyal base that is permitted to act. Pick your started set. The people who are already acting in the way you would envision a server to behave and them let them lead by example and enforce the example where necessary.

Oh, and finding those 5000 players willing to try it? A quick note in the upgrade text pointing to the final and then a follow-up poll a week or so later asking who would be interested ought to give some indication using tools that already exist. *wink*
