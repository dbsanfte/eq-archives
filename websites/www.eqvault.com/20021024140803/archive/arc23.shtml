On Mouselook Changes
- Aurum @ 7:48 PM PDT
Alan talked about the mouselook changes yesterday on Developer's Corner:

    This is in regards to the mouselook changes. I almost exclusively use the fourth view (first being first person, second being overhead, third being chase and fourth being the movable, stationary camera). This view is not almost unusable for me. I have to be able to see my character when in this view. Because the camera is movable, I don't know which way my character is facing unless I can see him. I also don't know how close I am to falling off that cliff or falling into the water unless I see my character. When casting a spell on the run, I need to be able to see just how close that mob is to me. Currently, I can only see myself if I zoom way out, this distorts the distance and is very unclear. Currently, when I zoom in to my character, the camera is located way above my head and looks straight out, missing my character. Before, it would angle down so that you would always be in view of your character. The fifth view was very similar, but the camera was not as high. This fifth view currently is way above the characters head, which is almost unplayable.

    Please look into this, please please please give me a view that allows the floating camera but also let's me see my character at all times.

    Thanks
    Gokusprout
    160ish days played on Lanys T'Vyl

    You can use the ALT and UP or DOWN arrow keys in that view to raise or lower the height of the camera. This will allow you to position the camera where you need it to be able to see your character or not as the need arises.

    Alan

    Miriamele-Brell wrote:

    Thanks for the review. The only correction I would make would be that ALT 5 didn’t center the view for me when I was trying this out. The Home key does, however.

    Using these keys you can put your camera pretty much anywhere you want to.

What Inspires Your Character?
- Aurum @ 7:16 PM PDT
The question was recently asked by Alan on the Dev's Corner. Here's Alan's post on the issue:

    I had initially intended to ask you what inspires you when you start a new character, in light of the soon-to-arrive Firiona Vie server. But when I wrote the subject line for this post it inspired another question. So I'll ask you both questions.

    1) When I approach the prospect of creating a new character for a new game, whether it's a massively multiplayer on-line game, a pen and paper game, or even a single player computer game, I usually go through a few steps when deciding what sort of character to play. I know from my time playing these games that most people have preferences, ideals and even checklists of things that they go over when thinking up a new character concept. But the key for me has always been finding a spark of inspiration from somewhere, often from the game background. It's usually an attitude that I want to play, lending to a sort of personality that leads to a certain class or race. Or sometimes I just want to play a basher rather than a healer, or a caster rather than a sneaker. But at the start of it all there needs to be that spark.

    For example my gaming group (pen and paper folks) recently re-started our Inhumaniods campaign. It's a third Edition D&D game where each of us plays a 'monster' among a tribe of monsters. When the GM warned us that the next games would be hard and we'd need backup characters (always a nice thing to hear... :) ), I thought about what I wanted to play if my current character were to be killed. I realized that I'd been playing casters, bards, and other physical weaklings in all of the last few games. I had been playing the thinker in the group. Even my 'monster' character speaks perfect common and refused to give in to the grunting of his fellows.

    So I went to the other extreme and created a barbarian. He's brutish, aggressive and rash, everything that my last five characters haven't been. He should be a lot of fun to play, even if I'm planning not to need him. Now that's not the most interesting character inspiration I've ever had, it's just the most recent. :)

    So I'm wondering if any of you have a sort of process that you go through to come up with character backgrounds or to decide what your next character will be. I thought it might be interesting to hear about the inspirations for your favorite character ideas.


    2) That was the intended question. But the subject line seems to ask a completely different question. What inspires your character? Some characters are inspired by bloodlust (as is my newest one). Some are inspired by things that happen to them during actual gameplay. Think about some of our favorite fictional characters. Frodo wasn't so much inspired at first, but as he went he drew strength from the bravery of his companions and was inspired to bravery himself by them. Batman, well, we know his inspiration. Every character that has ever come alive for us in tales or as characters in roleplaying games has been motivated by their past or events of their present.

    So what has inspired your characters?

    Alan

All About the Roleplaying Server
- Aurum @ 2:49 PM PDT
A lot of talk has been going on about the Firiona Vie server on the Developer's Corner. Here's the first update:

    Because this comment is so long, I'll respond within the text.

    Seninye wrote:

    I have been looking forward to a server where my roleplaying would not be derided, and where I would be likely to run into someone to roleplay with more than about once every blue moon, as is my current situation.

    I must be special. I've never been derided for roleplaying. And I've run into a lot of roleplayers while playing EverQuest. But I'm glad that we have the chance to offer an alternate rules server that you'll enjoy playing on.

    So you can understand my utter glee and anticipation when I heard that Verant was going to promote a server for RP. I thought I wouldn't care what bells and whistles it might come with, or what restrictions there might be to help safeguard its particular nature, as long as it was a home for players like me, where we would be more confident roleplaying, and more assured of finding one another.

    However, I must admit to being a teensy bit disappointed upon hearing that there would be "alignments" and that these would govern who a character could or could not group with, or even guild with. I fear that this will prevent some beautiful roleplaying opportunities, such as an infiltration by an enchanter or a rogue with a mask, or a heroic rescue by a wizard/druid of an "evil" person, because he believes anyone can be redeemed, or even the possibility of teaming up, despite moral differences, because of an important common goal. The mind calls up a myriad of beautiful emotional conversations that will be reduced to "you don't fool us, enchanter!" upon realizing that an invitation to group cannot be extended to the fellow High Elf for some mysterious reason. Similarly, is it really roleplaying to be able to tell at a glance if someone is mean or nice? It's so much more roleplaying to gradually find out the hard way!

    Well, it will indeed mean that the rogue can't infiltrate a group using his mask (assuming he's infiltrating a group that he can't join because of an alignment restriction). But your heroic rescuer can certainly rescue anyone he wants, he just can't group up with some people. He can still blast the enemy that is harming the "evil" person, or lend aid in other ways.

    And one of the bigger internal arguments here has been the one you touched on, the idea that there might indeed be situations where a paladin might want to aid a group of dark elves against some greater evil.

    He still can. He just can't group with them and share their experience points. He won't be able to cast protective spells from his god on them, nor will they be able to taint him with many of their evil magics. But they can still work together if they feel the need.

    Removing the ability for evil and good to 'casually' group together for experience gaining is important here. The idea would be that if you see a paladin and a shadowknight working together, you can be assured at least to some degree that they are not doing so for reasons that involve only gaining experience points. You can have some faith that they are indeed working toward some important goal.

    In exchange for that we also have to give player characters the ability, basically, to see alignment. It's a tradeoff that we've chosen to make.

    At some point, Verant has to trust that the roleplayers can govern themselves, and one another, to accomplish the same thing. I approve of such things as not being able to bind or auction outside a city and not having an automatic common language, but in the case of grouping or guilding, I don't think an attempt should be made to hard code roleplaying. I am willing to trust Verant that it may be necessary at the beginning, to discourage the usual crew of dewds who flock to a new server not to enjoy its theme but merely to climb to the top faster than anyone else to pronounce their uberdom, however I think perhaps some thought should be devoted to lifting the grouping restriction after six months or so, after the server has been able to establish itself as a haven for roleplayers.

    Interestingly enough, the idea behind binding limitations and restrictions to auctions isn't very different than the idea behind the inclusion of alignments and their restriction on grouping and guilding. The idea is to make the world seem more like it should, make it seem a little more real. Limiting auctions should make cities seem more like the centers of activity that they really should be. It also means that it will be harder to auction things. The tradeoff is 'realism' for convenience. On the other servers the convenience was chosen over the 'realism' thing. Usually when these two things conflict it is indeed better to choose convenience when you are trying to get people to pay money for your game. :)

    Restrictions on grouping lend to a more internally consistent world, one where dark elves don't freely associate with high elves and clerics to Rodcet Nife aren't bringing worshippers of Bertox back to life on a whim. The trade off for that more consistent world is that we put a few hitches into some of the more unusual roleplaying situations.

    Let's talk about your idea of lifting these convenience-for-realism restrictions after a certain time…

    Additionally, I also think that the no-character-transfer to this RP server should also be lifted in the same time period. There are many roleplayers who likely won't feel that they can give up their lives on other servers to start over again from scratch. Let the server have the establishment period, and after six months, the only people who will want to transfer are going to be roleplayers.

    If you are having a good time playing a character on another server, why would you change servers? I can think of two reasons off the top of my head: To play with friends that have moved and because you like the idea of the rules for that server. Now if we start peeling away those rules after a given time, well where's that unique rule set that you were interested in the first place?

    And I think perhaps you misunderstand the idea behind these rules. We are purposefully trading out the convenience rules that exist on other servers to promote a set of rules that is more internally consistent. We are doing so because we think that such a server will appeal to a large enough group of people to support such a server. If any of these rules were of the sort that we thought should be removed after a little while, then we probably wouldn't want them in the first place.

    And especially when it comes to transferring characters. A server like this is designed to only allow one character per account because we believe that it fosters a sort of community. It urges people to be responsible for what they do because they can't just jump to another character at any time. Working your way up levels is important to establishing who you are and who you know in this community. Having a 50th level Ogre Shaman suddenly appear in the world rather ruins that whole scheme.

    And let's not forget that this server will have a very different economy with the trivial loot code and the removal of most no-drop items. Letting characters pop into or out of that economy isn't a good idea.

    For me this is a compromise, of course. I originally thought the best way to make a roleplaying server would be to have it transfer-ONLY for the first six months, with a bit of a selection process to approving the transfer (only moving established roleplaying guilds from other servers, for example, or people who test it at length, knowing it will be wiped but wanting to roleplay anyway) instead of making it merely expensive.

    We just can't start any sort of 'selection process' for characters (or players) on this server. The overhead would be unbelievable. And, well, what sort of rules would you use to determine if some guild, player or character was or was not a roleplayer?

    An unrelated topic that I am suddenly reminded of: has it ever occurred to Verant to put up a new server only open to brand new accounts? I still longingly remember the awe-inspiring days when EQ first opened, when no one knew what they were doing, and everything was amazing. I am sure new players do not experience that to the extent that we early players did, and putting up new servers doesn't bestow that opportunity for them properly because of all the experienced players that flock there as well. New players will never know the thrill of discovery (yes, I am talking about the ones who don't even know spoiler sites exist yet - in my opinion those things are the bane of EQ, and I still find myself drawn to them now and then), never know that the assumption a human character should fish all night because she can't see to hunt is actually incorrect (yes, I did that and loved it!), never know that they are supposed to hand in DF belts instead of selling them to other characters, never escape the sight of twinks charging around the newbie hunting grounds in an effort to escape those levels as fast as possible, etc, etc etc. After it's been up long enough to homogenize with the other servers, then it can be opened to any age of account to play on, followed after an appropriate interval by permitting transfers, when it would become a normal server, and a new newbie server could open up. I'd go so far as to suggest that such a server should be opened with every expansion pack, as this is when new players are most attracted to the game, and happens at suitable intervals. But the trend seems to be that new servers opening up are theme servers, which I also agree with, as with a game like EQ, I think everyone tends to enjoy it more if they are playing it with people who share a compatible play style.

    An interesting idea. But, as one of those people that has indeed played the game when nobody knew anything about anything, you can still have that "Oh wow!" feeling if you are a new player on an older server. Experienced players actually add to that excitement most of the time. There's a whole different kind of awe inspired in a new player when he sees his first epic weapon and the Troll that's carrying it. If the new player wants to play the game in the clueless wonder that some of us first experience it, he still can. We certainly don't show up at peoples' houses and hold their eyes open while paging through spoiler sites…

    Enough rambling; this is far more than I meant to express, and though my purpose is to have this read by people in a position to actually contemplate implementing my solutions rather than simply to be posted in the forum, I hope I haven't made it too lengthy to escape even their attention.

    Thanks for EQ!

    long time player
    roleplayer
    utterly silent on forums but constantly looking

    Well, you got both attention from us and a posting on the boards. :)
    I think we all understand your point of view on these things. But we have to choose a course at some point and steer that course. We're in a privileged position right now, one where we can indeed experiment with new ideas. We just can't implement all the ideas we have, so some have to be left out.

    Thanks for your long time playing, I hope you'll enjoy playing EverQuest for many more years. :)

    Alan
Another bit was posted by Brad this morning:
    A few follow-ups based on questions and concerns I've seen in emails and posts:

    1. Only Magic, Lore, and NoDrop are exempted by the TLC -- normal loot is still there, including coins.

    2. We may have to limit shouts and ooc if players use it to get around the /auction limitations, but we really don't want to, because stifling communication is anti-community building. We remain undecided on this issue.

    3. It would indeed be easy for us to launch a PvP server with this ruleset. At this point, however, we have no specific plans to do this -- it's simply too soon after the launch of SZ to make plans for another PvP server. That said, I wouldn't rule it out completely -- we'll continue to listen to the player base in an attempt to gauge the level of interest in this and other ideas.

    thanks,

    -Brad
Last but not least, one more update from Alan:
    Chabak wrote:

    I was wondering how alignment and faction work together. Do NPC's treat you differently based on your alignment, or is that still specifically a faction thing? Kinda curious how those 2 facets of the game will interact.

    Alignments and factions are completely separate things. NPCs are not going to see, react to or have alignments. Alignments are only going to apply to player characters. We'll have more detail for you about the whole system once the server is set up for testing.


    Dagny-Eci wrote:

    Question about trivial loot code:

    What about quests that require items off of "trivial" mobs? For example today as part of the quest to gain entry to Veeshan's Peak (very high level zone) I had to kill a monster that was very very trivial to anyone who has any business going to Veeshan's Peak. How do you address this concern.

    Another one is the part of the Soulfire Quest that requires an item from a level 10'ish mob. Soulfire is part of the paladin epic so to lock out someone who missed the quest at the "proper" level from ever obtaining their epic seems a little shortsighted.

    I suppose you can say that people should think ahead and do those parts of the quest as they level up, but honestly that's kind of a cop out. No one can foresee all the quests they might need to do at a given level before they level.

    You'll have to find someone of the 'appropriate' level to help you out. Keep in mind that very few items will be no-drop on this server. So if you 'need' an item from an NPC that is too low level for you to get it off the NPC yourself, you can hire some folks that are in that level range to get it for you. You become the wizened veteran that doesn't have time to do petty things and pays people to do them for you. This is a nice little side effect that I rather like.

    Alan

Firiona Vie (RP Server) Details
- Aurum @ 6:49 PM PDT
At long last it seems everyone's favorite EverQuest covergirl has finally had a server named after her. Here's a very informative post from Brad directly off of the Dev's Corner:

    Hello all,

    Given that we are very close to launching the new server into live beta, I wanted to talk a bit about it and give you a ‘closer to final’ feature list (although it’s still very subject to change).

    First, just what are we going to call it? As I’d mentioned in an earlier post, we do hesitate to call it an ‘RP’ server for two reasons:

    1. We feel all of our servers are Role Playing Servers and that EverQuest is indeed a Role Playing Game.

    2. We feel it’s pretty much impossible to define what Role Playing is verbally, much less via a rule set.

    The idea then came up to call it the ‘Alignment’ server, since a character’s alignment is pretty key to many of the features we’re implementing. But, to me, that still doesn’t speak to what the server is really all about. From my perspective it’s an RP server, an Alignment server, a hard-core ‘Seige Perilous’ style server, and, quite simply, an experiment server, where we’re implementing some potential ideas for the long-term future, as well as some features we’d probably have put in the game originally, given 20/20 hindsight.

    So, from here forward, I’m just going to call it by its server name, Firiona Vie (in fact, while we’re waiting on the Nameless to create more gods, we plan on naming future servers after Heroes and other prominent mortals in Norrath’s illustrious history). We don’t mind if you still call it the RP Server, or whatever else you’re so inclined, but we’re going to stick to using its ‘proper’ name.

    Lastly, before I list the features, I do want to say the following again: This feature set will undoubtedly be controversial. It will also undoubtedly NOT be consistent with many players’ views as to what an RP server or any other server should be. In fact, this is (combined with having less servers overall in the past) why we’ve been hesitant to launch such a server. But we’re going forward with this anyway, and we hope it will make an interesting and even possibly better EverQuest. And we hope that some if not many players will enjoy it.

    At the very least, it sure will be a different server

    -Brad

    --------

    Firiona Vie Server Feature List:

    This is a new server, not a split server. Nor will there be any transfers to this server.

    This is a PvE (Player vs. Environment) server, NOT a PvP (Player vs. Player) server. In other words, it’s a standard ‘blue’ server with the same rules and features regarding PvP as the vast majority of our servers. It is not one of the ‘Zeks’.

    Auction restricted to city zones: Qeynos, Surefall Glade, Highkeep, Freeport, Rivervale, Erudin, Halas, Neriak, Grobb, Oggok, Greater Faydark, Ak'Anon, Kaladim, Felwithe, Paineel, Cabilis, Kael and Thurgadin.

    Beneficial buff spells will not work on characters 20 levels lower than the caster.

    Very few items will be No-Drop. Exceptions will include things such as newbie notes and epic weapons.

    Each character will be assigned an "alignment" upon creation. This alignment will be described in brief on the character creation screen (in the same location the their Deity is described).

    Each character will be able to change their alignment once any time after they reach 10th level. The player will be able to chose from a list of alignments based on their race, class and deity.

    A character's alignment will determine whether or not they can group or guild with other characters.

    A character's alignment will determine an experience bonus or penalty when grouping with other characters.

    A character's alignment will determine if they can cast beneficial spells on other characters (including resurrections).

    Language skills will improve only through group chat in the same zone and from /say.

    Languages will only improve 1 point each hour and will be capped at level*5+5 skill points.

    Common will not exist. A Human language will be added and only made easily available to certain races.

    One character per account per server.

    The Trivial Loot Code will be in effect in all zones.

    Bind Affinity will be restricted for all characters to locations where melee characters can bind on other servers.

    /emote will be language specific

    All characters will be /roleplay.

    /alignment will display the alignment of your character and probably a more detailed description of that alignment.

We are the worst defensive melee and in the middle of the pack, offensively. Basically, we have very little to offer a group or a raid other than 3rd place damage and snare. Bard Item Effect Stacking
- Aurum @ 8:21 PM PDT
One of the most active topics Alan tackled on the Developer's Corner today was that of the issue of Bard Item Effect Stacking. Here's the intitial post:

    Many bards have noticed that something went wrong with the emergency patch last night. Here's what happened.

    We had to emergency patch to remove an evil duping bug. I'm not going to detail that out, as those details aren't pertinent to the tale at hand.

    We were also patching to remove some bard stacking problems. There was a way that a bard could, basically, cheat the system and double up on buffs.

    To fix this problem we changed the way that spells cast from items work for bards. Bards using items that cast spells (not songs) will have those spells act just like regular spells, where they were acting like songs before. Bard songs stack with just about anything, and spells have their own sets of stacking rules. Bards casting spells from items were able to ignore the stacking restrictions of those spells.

    We changed that. Bards using spells from items will find that they stack just like the spells normally do, not like bard songs.

    However, one of the components of the patch didn't make it to the servers.

    That component is the one that tells songs cast off of items to act like songs, not spells.

    Ok, this is getting complicated. Basically, right now songs cast from items are acting just like spells cast from items, and are trying to stack like spells not like songs. This is causing a lot of item-cast songs to not stack as they should.

    Having just brought the servers down, we are very reluctant to do so again right away. We're not sure when the update needed to fix this will happen, but it might not be until the next regular patch. Please bear with us until we can get this patched. It took us a long time to remove this 'perk', so maybe you can forgive us for taking a little while to fix the fix…

    Alan

Trivial Loot Code and Luclin
- Aurum @ 6:55 PM PDT
The Trivial Loot subject was brought up by Brad McQuaid in his first visit to the Developer's Corner in a while:

    Hello all,

    I wanted to update you all on our current thoughts regarding the Trivial Loot Code as it relates to Shadows of Luclin zones.

    As per my last couple of 'Ask Brads', we've made both an effort to explain what the Trivial Loot Code does and why we were considering it for all Luclin zones. I'd also mentioned, however, that if most players appeared to be against its inclusion we'd not use it.

    After talking with players, reading emails and feedback, and then very recently running a poll, it is indeed apparent that the majority of our playerbase does not feel that the Loot Code's advantages outweigh its restrictions.

    In fact, after polling over 200,000 players, most want the looting system left alone, almost as many want us to explore a different solution for bottom feeding and rampant farming, and a minority were in favor of the Trivial Loot Code as it exists today in the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains (the remainder either didn't have an opinion or were unfamiliar with TLC).

    Well, so be it :)

    We will head back to our drawing boards and keep brainstorming on this issue, but at this current time we do NOT intend on implementing TLC for the zones in our upcoming expansion, the Shadows of Luclin.

    I do want to thank everyone for all the feedback and discussion regarding this issue -- there was a lot of debate surrounding this issue, and we've done our best to pay attention.

    -Brad McQuaid
    Chief Creative Officer & Vice President
