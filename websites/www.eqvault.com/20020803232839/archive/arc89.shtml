First Xegony All-Norrathian Drinking Gathering
[1 Comments] - ShadowLight @ 9:05 AM PDT
This weekend on the Xegony server the First Xegony All-Norrathian Drinking Gathering will be held. It will be on Saturday, the
12th of August.

**************************************************
* First Xegony All-Norrathian Drinking Gathering *
**************************************************

Hail citizens of Xegony!

The time has come to sit back and forget about spawns, loot, experience and
money for a while. You are all invited to the first Xegony All-Northian
Drinking Gathering.

*********************
* What is the Idea? *
*********************

The idea was conceived by 2 fellow adventurers, Balkin Ironfist and Ellegon
Grossfuss. We are both Master Brewers and enjoy having a good time without
constant competition. So we wanted to organize a gathering where we can hand
out free drinks and have fun with old and new friends.

*************************************
* Where will the Gathering be held? *
*************************************

We decided to take West Freeport, as it is relatively easy to reach with
boats and TP's and is a newbie zone, therefore there shouldn't be wandering
MoB's killing drunk people. We are aware that certain races or classes are
KOS in Freeport, but there is no place where EVERYBODY is safe. Just watch
out for the guards if your race/class is disliked! :-)

******************************
* How do I come to Freeport? *
******************************

You can either travel by foot and boat, or you can ask your friendly Druid
or Wizard for a Teleport. We are also trying to set up a taxi-service with
volunteer Teleporters. More about that in the section "How can I help?"

*************************
* What does it cost me? *
*************************

Besides some time, absolutely nothing. The alcohol will be given away free
of charge. You can tip the brewers, breezers, binders or the teleporters if
you want, but it's not at all necessary. This will NOT be a gathering for
the rich or to make money. It's only purpose is to have fun.

****************************
* When will it take place? *
****************************

The First XANDG will start on Saturday, the 12th of August at 6 pm PST (9pm
EST, 2am GMT).

*******************
* How can I help? *
*******************

The easiest way to help is: Spread the word! Tell all your friends, your
guild members and the people you meet in the zones. Post it on your guild
boards and websites!

In addition, we still need a lot of help from everyone. We decided to search
for some services to offer and therefore look out for:

- Brewers: Everyone with the brewing skill can attend and distribute his/her
drinks to the attending people. We don't care about your skill, as even Bog
Juice is alcohol :-) All we ask, is that you accept the fact that we're not
SELLING beverages. Again, this should be a free event, just for the fun of
it.

- Foragers/Gatherers: A lot of the higher skill level drinks need
ingredients that can't be bought. Good examples are Vegetables (very much
needed), Fruits, Berries, Froglok Meat, Snake Scales or Royal Jelly. So
everyone who can forage or collect these ingredients is welcomed to donate
or sell them to us. Yes, we will buy them. The simple reason is, that when
we make high end brews (like Hulgarsh, Ginesh, or Bleeding Brains), we will
most likely gain skill levels (we are both around 140 brewing, but not
maxed). Since we don't want to take advantage of your generosity, we are
willing to pay 1 or 2 plat per stack of Vegetables and also some pay for the
other ingredients. Of course we will gladly accept donations :-)

- Taxis: We want to offer a taxi-service between the various parts of
Norrath to Freeport (respectively Commons) and maybe back. Therefore we
would need some volunteer Druids and Wizards playing Taxi. We would allow
the teleporting personnel to collect tips if offered, but the TP itself
should be free of charge.

- Breezer/Clarity: The taxis will need a lot of mana and would very much
like a breeze or even a clarity cast on them. If you would be able to help
them a bit, please do so.

- Binders: Some of those who attend might like a bind in Freeport. We want
to offer this to the ones asking for it. If you can cast Bind and will do it
FREE of charge, why not join in the fun?

If you would like to help us in any way, please contact Balkin and Ellegon
at xandg@hotmail.com!

********************************
* What will I get for helping? *
********************************

No money, and no exp. If a player tips you, then so be it. But we ask anyone
participating, to offer his services for free. I'm sure you see the good
will behind this event and the possibility to make some new friends.

****************************************
* You still have additional Questions? *
****************************************

No problem. Contact us at xandg@hotmail.com!
Thanks guys!

Gordon Infobits: The Sequel
- ChrisLTD @ 6:11 PM PDT
The sequel to the story of Abashi on the Official Boards:

  • Where's my Manastone or Moss Covered Twig

    [Quote from original post: Verant claims that these items where broken or unbalanced,so they removed them from the game.If these items are "Broken" or "Unbalancing",then why was the manastone left in game to current owners?And if the Moss Covered Twig is a mistake by a game designer that didnt know the rules,then why havnt they recieved retro-active changes?]

    Not everything that is found to be unbalancing is given retroactive changes. This is actually our LAST option when it comes to dealing with these issues as they come up. The manastone was one of the times where we decided to leave the existing item in game, and just barred it from the planes and from Kunark. As for the moss-covered stuff, we're still deciding what, if anything, to do about them.

    [Quote from original post: Then everyone will someday have a chance at it if they want it.]

    I've mentioned several times in the past that no one is entitled to any item. Items change in game all of the time. New items come in, old items go out. One day the Guk Ghoul Lord might run out of his endless supply of swords and that will be it. That's how it's supposed to work.

    [Quote from original post: So where's my Manastone? Where's my Moss Covered Twig?]

    If they are floating around on your server, you might be able to acquire them from another player.

    [Quote from original post: The vision only awards those who play 24/7, are in Uberguilds]

    People who play the game more will get more items. People who have a lot of friends and make allies will have access to more things. There really isn't a problem with either of those. You *should* be able to go more places and do more things if you have friends. You should be able to get more things quicker if you play more.

  • Gordon, come to CT and watch me duel a 60 warrior
    [Quote from original post: Abashi said that monks do the best toe to toe damage. He did not say they had enough hps to withstand the warriors damage long enough to kill them]

    Not to mention, even if buffs were added to the monk until they had the same number of hitpoints, they'd be pitted against each others armor in an unequal fashion. The only way to test this is versus the same NPC. I might try that out.

  • Abashi, this is inconsistent...
    [Quote from original post: Manastone was "overpowering" in only a very certain set of circumstances. The robe also has a casting time on it, where the stone does not.]

    Not to mention, the Robe is extremely rare on a very difficult encounter. The manastone was neither rare nor difficult to obtain.

  • Cursed Items off the very hardest creatures to defeat...
    Those items you mentioned aren't cursed, as Arkanon_Brell said, they just have drawbacks. Many things in game have advantages and disadvantages for their use, they are just more pronounced on some of these items. The pronounced drawbacks allow higher stats to be placed upon the items. Another nice thing about drawbacks is that it creates doubt as to what constitutes the "best" items, leading to diversity in what people wear.
    Cursed items, on the other hand, have yet to make an appearance in game. The Rod of Anhilation was *supposed* to be cursed, but somehow the curse didn't make it on to the weapon.

  • Post-24-Hour Decay: LET ME LOOT IT!
    [Quote from original post: I think a good alternative to a body decaying after the time limit is up is to allow ANYBODY to loot the body when the time limit is up. The stuff is going to be lost for good anyway, right? Why not allow by chance some passerby to snag up some items?]

    Simply, this isn't something that we'd do. Besides creating a new phenomenon of corpse-camping, it would basically result in items being handed out to players who didn't earn them. At least when you trade for or are given an item, you are earning it from the perspective of the trader or giver. Merely being able to loot anyone's corpse after a given amount of time would amount to the items being handed out.

  • Feydedwar drops the FA quest stuff!

    [Quote from original post: So we are supposed to camp the dragon?
    Rare random spawn?]

    Rare random spawns really can't be camped. The challenge in this case is to happen upon him, and get everyone together to kill him before he depops.

    [Quote from original post: ABashi would you please tell someone to make this damn dragon permanent when he pops? Like EVERY other dragons?]

    "Every other dragon" is not "permanent" when they pop. It's in an outdoor zone, and is a chance encounter. It's going to take some work and some haste to take him down.

  • Bottled Mana? Can you give us a hint at least? Please?
    Enchanters have a line of spells (e.g. Thicken mana, purify mana, etc) that creates different components that will, as trade skills are adjusted, be usable when crafting items that are magical.

  • VERANT: Unacceptable customer service!!
    I'm sorry, but the patch message was very clear, there is no reimbursement for items dropped upon the ground. This is absolutely firm. This policy is necessary in order to follow the item reimbursement policy publicized a few weeks ago.

    [Quote from original post: "In addition to there being no reimbursement for accidentally dropped items, all items dropped will disappear immediately. Dropping an item is now the same as hitting the destroy button with it."]

    Not everything disappears when it hits the ground. Some things, for instance, fall through objects onto world geometry below. Other things do just disappear, timing out as trash in the world. Yes, dropping an item should be considered basically the same as hitting destroy for all intents and purposes. Don't drop anything that you want to keep.

    Perhaps the patch message should have included reference to items that were purposely dropped.

  • Hey, good news: Combat speed debuffs
    One of the programmers thinks that he has the reverse stacking problem with "attack speed debuffs" (Deeds/Insects line) cracked. It should be going on the Test server soon.

Gordon Infobits
- ChrisLTD @ 1:55 PM PDT
The story of Abashi on the Official Boards:

  • Brad or Gordon on trade skills ?
    Blacksmithy is being worked on currently, with plans for tailoring and pottery shortly thereafter. Jewelry is probably on the bottom of the list of revamps right now due to the fact that it is already so useful. It's without doubt the most robust trade skill currently in game.
    We do however have plans for the "Bottled Mana" series of spells in these revamps

    [Quote from original post: Also the JC skill is capped at 200. Wasn't Alchemy upped to over 200 ?]

    I don't believe that alchemy was, but poison was bumped over 200. Increase of the skill caps on the trades might be revisited during the revamp. Poison had components that made skill over 200 necessary. The other skills are currently built already taking into account the 200 cap.

    [Quote from original post: As a Master Smithe, the toolboxes were the best thing that ever happened.]

    The toolbox recipe was obviously broken, as it allowed instant conversion of money to more money with minimal effort. None of the trade skills in game are designed to function in this way.

  • Gordon: Why don't hotkeys work when meditating?
    Originally, the idea was that people wouldn't be able to do anything else while medding. As we loosened up on this gameplay aspect, and started allowing people to do things, we probably just forgot to re-enable those hotkeys. I imagine that if you can click the buttons with a mouse, you should be able to hit the corresponding number on the keyboard and have it work. I'll forward this to Roy.

  • Patch Message here *sigh*
    [Quote from original post: No mention of the /roleplay fix taking place though. Did VI forget this promised fix yet again?]

    Even though it worked correctly on the Test server during the last patch, it didn't work on Production because one necessary file didn't get patched. Considering that I'd already announced once that it was fixed, I figured I'd wait until I saw it myself before announcing it again.

    [Quote from original post: It should be noted however that the dragons can be engaged from much further away than is intended. Rather than hold off implementing these dragons for another week, we've decided to implement them now and fix the problem in the next patch. Enjoy
    This actually scares me..]

    That just means that you can hit and be hit by the dragons from further away than it should be. That will be corrected in a future patch.

    [Quote from original post: Does this mean the group switch did not make it in with the trackplayer command?]

    No, that won't be in until the next patch, but it will go in.

  • Emerald Jungle update
    The "chr" files contain references to the NPCs that can be *displayed* in a zone, not the placement of NPCs and the loot on them. That happens server-side and does not require a client update.

  • Who'll stop the rain?
    Zones have chances daily chances to rain based upon the zone and the season on the server. Some zones rain more than others (the Karana's come to mind), whereas others don't get it that much. It is indeed to add atmosphere to the game.
    [Quote from original post: Who'll stop the rain?]

    A druid of the appropriate level

  • Workaround that could allow >20 items in one inventory slot, potion stacking
    Well, one note on potions, we've considered a workaround to make them "stackable" in one fashion or another. If we can't directly make them stackable (and the new programmer "Roy" is working on this), we've started talking about allowing shaman to stick 10 potions of one type in a container, combine them, and produce a 10 charge potion.
    It's a thought, we'll continue working on this issue.

  • Do some testing for us Abashi
    [Quote from original post: Here is what I propose Abashi. Do some testing with a lvl 60 monk and a lvl 60 warrior. Equip both with comperable equipment. I suggest using a jade mace and knuckle duster for the monk and a jade mace and crystalline short sword for the warrior as this is relatively balancing in the difficulty of obtaining those items. Then equip them both in their fear/hate planar armor. This should be pretty balancing as these armor sets are basically of equal difficulty to obtain.]

    If you take two classes with near identical skill, equip them with weapons that do nearly the same damage, and exclude special attacks, disciplins, etc., they are going to do nearly the same damage. That's a given.

    However that's not the appropriate way to test. You need to take into account that monk-specific weapons of a similar *quality* are more powerful than warrior-specific weapons of the same quality. For instance, if you equip one with the best items in the game for their class, you need to do the same for the other.

    I should mention that the top two or 3 non-Veeshan's Peak weapons for monks haven't even been found yet.

  • A request for the /help command. Stop the spam!
    [Quote from original post: While I really think this was a great addition I think it really needs to be broken up into two commands. Perhaps it could be split into /help and /emotes. When you get spammed with 153 commands (yes, I counted), you aren't getting much help.]

    Good idea, I think that it would probably require a bit of hardcoding, but I'll forward it to the client programmer.

  • What Recourse Do I have?
    [Quote from original post: Two more spawns go by, a total of 9 gobs, and a monk comes in and asks me if his group could have the room. I say, not right now, I'm camping these. He questions if I get XP and I say I do. He leaves. He returns with 5 other group members and they sit down. I remind them that I am camping the room.]

    Just a minor reminder here that no one can claim the rights to a particular spawn. If people choose to play in areas where other people are also contesting the same spawns, it would be good to work this stuff out without involving the GMs. Once they get there, the decision would be binding and might be one that neither group ends up liking.

    [Quote from original post: My pal arrives and we get some kills. We lose some kills too. Then the personal attacks start coming. Meanwhile, more than an hour has passed and the one GM online has not responded to me. this could have been settled long ago with some mediation by a GM. I'm sure witnesses thought we all nuts.]

    I'm sorry that someone wasn't available to intervene in the matter. The GMs deal with many of these issues on a daily basis. It sounds like, however, that you all were working together in the end, both getting a few kills now and again. One aspect of massively multiplayer games is that people are exposed to each other and either learn to work together or not.

    [Quote from original post: So is this what I pay my money for? Harrasment at every turn, simply because I had found a room that I could solo in and not spend hours trying to get on a list someplace? No GM assistance at all, not even a "Hey, you should really move on...".]

    You do pay to play a game with other people in the same world, and that has both advantages and disadvantages. Your opponents could have felt the same way that you do about the situation, and you all pay the same money to play. We have to take into account the needs, desires, and rights of all of our players.

    [Quote from original post: Please note this is not a flame. This is honest criticism. I am an unhappy customer, complaining about a flawed product.]

    Your post doesn't denote any flaw in the product. You wanted to kill something, another player wanted to kill the same thing. There are other places in the world where both groups could have gone and never run into a contested situation such as this, but instead the choice was to fight over one spawn.

    Again, I'm very sorry that a GM couldn't be present to resolve the matter for you in a timely manner.

    [Quote from original post: Camping is part of The Vision (tm) and is bound to lead to that kind of situation.]

    That's not appreciated. We've elaborated many times on what our goals were for the world. The fact that people choose to camp rather than adventure is something that we are trying to change regularly. You might note that every change we make to discourage camping leads to a slew of posts here on the board from people who are angry about the change. If you don't want to camp you can *choose* not to camp and go someplace you can adventure without camping. There are many places in game to do this, you just have to open yourself to a bit more risk.

  • Abashi - Why doesn't O'Kiel's Radiation get any upgrades?
    [Quote from original post: Basically, it was just a little freebie they threw wizards. ]

    More accurately, it was a little freebie we decided not to take away from wizards. Back in early beta, when the class roles were still being defined, that was the first damage-shield that was created and it was given to wizards. Shortly after that, it was decided that Wizards wouldn't have buffs to cast upon others. Rather than take that particular spell away, we decided to leave it in to help the wizards get through their first couple of levels.

  • What's the plan Abashi?

    [Quote from original post: Since bards did not get the relative increase in effectiveness the other hybrids got, what is the plan to for bards? Is it your intention for bards to be less effective than pallies, rangers, and s/k's?]
    Bards aren't really hybrids, and in fact aren't really like any other class in the game. They really can't be compared directly to hybrids. Even if they could, who's to say that this change would make them less effective?

    [Quote from original post: 1) Fix the broken lullaby & chains songs. This would increase the relative power of bards, because we have learned to get by without these songs for so long.
