European Fan Faire Cancelled!
- Aurum @ 3:50 PM PDT
SOE's Cindy Archuleta just sent me an email announcing that the Fan Faire event scheduled for next month in Paris, France has been shelved. No exact reason is given, but the letter promises this is only a delay:

    Sony Online Entertainment and Ubi Soft today
    announced the postponement of the European Fan Faire. Our European
    community of EverQuest players has seen much growth in the past year and we
    are excited about all of our initiatives in this market and beyond. While
    we regret the delay in bringing what has been an incredibly successful and
    one-of-kind event in the U.S., we know that all of our international
    initiatives will enhance the player experience, increase our international
    fan base and lead us overseas in the coming months.

    We want to thank all of the fans who had
    planned to participate in this inaugural event and will be sending a full
    refund in addition to a special gift from our fan faire team. While we are
    disappointed that we cannot meet you next month, we hope that our presence
    in Europe continues to grow. We will be able to better serve you by
    postponing this event to a more suitable time and place for everyone. We
    apologize for any inconvenience.

    EverQuest boasts more than 410,000 players
    on a worldwide basis including the U.S., England, France, Italy, Germany,
    Canada, Singapore, Zimbabwe, Saudi Arabia, and New Zealand. We recently
    announced the availability of Game Cards on retail shelves worldwide giving
    cash holders the ability to play in 90-day increments. And, the initial
    phase of localization involving both human and automated translating agents,
    beginning with French, German, Japanese and Korean is well underway. Sony
    Online Entertainment is continuing to build a bridge between nations and is
    actively working toward new server deployment plans with Europe as a primary
    target.

    This winter, all EverQuest players can look
    forward to the launch of EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin. The new
    expansion pack will introduce an enchanting moonscape packed with new
    adventure zones, new items, creatures and spells, a new playable character
    race and more.

    Thank you for your continued support!

    Cindy Archuleta
    Community Relations Manager
    Sony Online Entertainment
Sorry to hear it!

Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:17 PM PDT
The Roleplaying Server and a few spell issues are the highlight of today's infobits:

    Fear and Bear Form
    [Quote: Just went on a raid thru that old portal-o-fun in Freerott. During the buff phase the lady necro at the portal spawned and got off a fear on me. Well, actually more like 3 or 4 before the mop-up squad noticed her. The last of the fears actually managed to stick. When it hit, my bearform (spirit of the great bear) was over-written.     - Tyck of RN ]

    --------------------------------------------
    quote:

    This is a new one to me. I honestly have no idea why this is happening, but I'll look into it and try to get it fixed as soon as possible.

    - Lawrence Poe
    --------------------------------------------

    On Roleplaying Server
    [Quote: I realize I sent these questions earlier, but I haven't seen a response While the issue of the "Examine" option has been discussed, I haven't seen a leaning to keeping it and was wondering if I am one of the few that feel this option should be removed.

    The questions are:

    1. Will we be allowed to have identical names? (Are there not many Johns in the world?) ]

    Nope. Sure, it's possible to have the same name as someone else in real life. But there are two reasons why it can't happen in Norrath. Character models are not people. I have several friends named Brian, and I can tell one from another at a hundred paces pretty easily. Unfortunately in Norrath that's not as easy. Names help to differential characters.

    But more importantly, having the same name as someone else would be a real mess. It helps a lot if every character has a unique name so the server can tell one of you from another.

    [Quote: 2. What are the penalties for not Rping? What are the guidelines for being in character? (I am tired of being berated by people who say - what level are you and then scoff when I say I am 18th season or I am in my 18th cycle of training. Or have names like Illeatyourliver or Priesto). ]

    Well, that's the real meat of the whole debate, for me at least.

    If you can describe to me how to distinguish roleplaying from not roleplaying definitively in every instance, then I'll come up with a set of punishments.

    [Quote: 3. Is it even being considered to do away with the examine option? If someone wants to see what I have on - they can look at my picture or ask me. Perhaps, make it so if someone wants to examine they need your permission. I equate someone examining me as the same as going through my clothes, lifting my cloak out of the way to see my tunic, etc. . . and I feel that is rude (As do everyone who asks permission to examine and doesn't just examine at will) and in no way necessary to a total RP server. ]

    Another endless argument. The emotional response to being inspected can't be overcome. But inspection is in the game because it allows characters to 'see' what they should already be able to see that isn't displayed by the game engine. Yes, you CAN see a tunic under a cloak, unless the wearer of that cloak is spending all of his time holding it tightly closed with both hands. And, well, in Norrath you can't even see that cloak. Inspection allows people to actually see what you are wearing. If you find that rude, then it is probably best that you turn off your inspection messages.

    Let's not get into this debate again, since it's one of those that just never ends…

    [Quote: 4. While I agree that for religion it would be most unlikely that an Iksar would worship a Halfling god, I do not think it is too far fetched to believe the same Iksar would not believe in any god. (Right now, if you are an Iksar - you believe in Cazic-Thule) Can you make it so every race has at least have the option of being agnostic? (Clerics, Paladins, Shadow Knights and Necromancers aside of course) ]

    The world of Norrath is largely built with some assumptions in mind. Player character Iksar, for example, all worship Cazic-Thule. Those that do not are outcasts. While there are certainly some valid and interesting ideas about allowing PCs to play outcasts, setting that up isn't as easy as just flipping a switch. We'd have to redesign a whole bunch of stuff. After all, such Iksar would be KOS in Cabilis.

    The same pretty much goes for all of the other requests for changes (Gnome Shadowknights, Dwarven Bards, X race worshipping Y god…). It's not that there is no way that such a thing could happen. It's just that Norrath exists with things as they are and such changes wouldn't necessarily be improvements, they'd just be changes.

    [Quote: Again these are a repeat of the questions I asked earlier, but haven't seen any answer in the previous postings. (Plus I hope to put the RP Server News discussion back near the top.)

    Thanks and keep up the good work.

    Skrax Swifttail - Monk on Fennin Ro ]

    Sorry I missed it the first time.

    Character Transfers & Hotkeys
    Stewed231 wrote:

    [Quote: Hi guys,

    My characters have been transferred, and thank you very much for the service.

    One thing I didn't know - ALL my hotkeys are gone.
    I know they're dtored on users PCs, not servers, so is there a way for me to map the appropriate file that contains all my hotkeys to the character on the new server.

    Thanks. ]

    You're hot keys and other information for each character is stored in an *.opt file.

    For example: absor_02.opt

    The _02 is the number of the server the character is on.

    Now that you've had your character moved to a new server you should see two *.opt files with that character's name on them in your EverQuest directory.

    First, make a copy of both files to someplace in another directory, so you can replace them if my advice turns out to be goofy…

    Then just figure out which file is the older one. That file should be the one from your old server, with your old information on it. Delete the newer *.opt file and rename the old one to match the new one.

    For example.

    absor_02.opt Dated 3-3-2000
    absor_34.opt Dated 8-19-2001


    The _34 must be the new file for the character on the new server (make sure to log into the character once, or this file might not exist).

    I would delete the absor_34.opt file and rename the absor_02.opt file as absor_34.opt

    Now, I haven't tried this myself, but it should work. And if it goes all wrong, just copy and paste the reserve files back into the directory.

    About Denizens
    Antsy wrote:

    [Quote: ...it looks like an animal...but cockatrices apparently aren't animals. Is this intentional? ]

    That's a mystical beast, much like the unicorn, and not an animal.

    The Reaver
    [Quote: I think the idea of situational weapons is a great one, however, I just don't see how a sword that grants +12 to all saves (ie makes you tougher just by holding it) could NOT be magical. Psionically imbued perhaps?

    Just my two cents,

    Aillron Lifereaver ]

    Well, therein lies the problem.

    It might indeed be magical (though it could be argued that it isn't). But being magical and being magical in the way needed to hit creatures that are resistant to non-magical weapons isn't the same thing.

    Perhaps there is a magical gem of protection on the weapon, and otherwise it's not magical. So it can offer protection but still not be able to harm creatures that are immune to normal weapons.

    Unfortunately, the denotation of 'magical' in EverQuest means both, or either… The two concepts are marked with one bit of data.

    In this case the weapon does indeed have some magical qualities, but the blade itself is not magical.
