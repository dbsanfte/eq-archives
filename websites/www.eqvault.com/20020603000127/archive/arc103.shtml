Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:54 PM PDT
Gordon has been posting up a storm on the Gameplay Board today. Here's all the latest quotes and excerpts:

  • How EQ Has Evolved
    [Quote from original post: What happened to the old days (keep in mind, I don't know the REAL old days). In my limited memory, I cannot remember a time in the life of EQ where so many stupid patches and rules have been placed forth.]

    That's why they call them the "Good Old Days". People by nature tend to remember the things that they were fond of in the Good Old Days. It's a defense mechanism of the human psyche that allows us to move forward and embrace the present without dwelling unnecessarily on the problems of the past. I believe that it's called "Golden Age Syndrome".

    For us, the downside is that people forget the problems of the past, and therefore don't remember all of the changes made to the game that were embraced by nearly everyone.

    [Quote from original post: NOW, I hear a rumor of a patch being placed where NO EXP = NO LOOT?!?!?!?!? I don't understand.]

    That's just a rumor. It's one of the things that we did consider at one time, but think it would be too drastic of a change to the core game at this point, and might cause more problems then it solves.

  • Certain Race/Class Combos Not Allowed
    [Quote from original post: Why is it that certain class/race combos are not allowed?]

    In order to insure class interdependence, and also provide and balance abilities available to the different races, we have to place some limits on the class selections that a person can make. We draw these lines based upon what makes sense from a roleplay standpoint, as well as to insure that the different classes available to each race is spread out fairly well.

    The ideas that you present however are very good, and though not compatible with EQ as you mention, could be modified for use in future products.

  • Gordon Still Here
    Just to put everyone at ease, no Verant personnel were affected adversely by the news that was announced today and covered by various journalistic. The EverQuest operations and development teams are quite well intact and will continue to provide you with the services that you deserve.

    We'll release more information in the not-too-distant future.

  • Penalty for Dying
    [Quote from original post: Why do you penalize the players so heavily time wise for dying. I know its your vision but why? Do you think it draws people back. It doesn't. What draws me back is the people and interaction why not enhance that -vs- penalize us for the occasional or not so occasional death.]

    This issue has come up in several threads over the past couple of days, many that I've posted in. I'd like to see them consolidate to make having a real discussion a bit easier :)

    Anyway, your question above was, "Why do you penalize the players so heavily time wise for dying". Setting aside for the moment that I don't think that calling the penalty a penalty of time is appropriate since there is no "end" to the journey in the game, "heavily" is a subjective term. What one person thinks is a heavy penalty might be no-sweat for another. Since people have differing opinions on what these subjective terms mean, we set a value, and provide methods that allow the mitigation of the time involved.

    The concept of risk and reward is very much part of the core of EQ. In general, one taking greater risks should have the ability to reap more rewards. Players determine what risk is acceptable to them when they play the game. The player who chooses to play without clerics or paladins around to do an XP rez face greater experience losses on death, but reap the reward of having less restrictions on the way that they play. Conversely, the person who chooses the reward of power-soloing to into the mid-50s will face the risk of greater experience losses on death since they don't have friends to rez them.

    There have been a lot of posts here recently from people who have asked us to lessen the risks involved in the game without reducing the rewards. All the while, they have been in control of the amount of risk that they subject their characters to. I understand that it is human nature for people (including myself) to want to have their cake and eat it too, but rewards without risks make for a boring game.

  • Damage Shields
    Damage shields still allow interruptions to take place.

    Back in beta, they used to shield you from all damage and interruptions. I recall that I was able to take a wizard, load on diamondskin, and conflag an ice giant to death before it could get through the diamondskin.

  • Possible bug related to Tashania recast delay?
    Interesting problem. I'll bring it to the attention of GZ and the client programmer. Thanks for letting us know.

  • Recent Layoffs at Sony
    [Quote from original post: Recent news: Layoffs at SONY. What does this mean for Everquest? What does this mean for Everquest? Does it end?]

    There are more than a few inaccuracies in the article, of which I really can't comment on specifics at this time. However, just so everyone is aware, EverQuest operations and development will not be adversely affected by any of what is going on.

  • PoF Items Question
    Considering the numbers of new items from Kunark that are neither nodrop or lore, we probably won't be applying those tags to anything that has been missing them in game all of this time.

    For those on the PvP servers where loot is still a possibility, it's basically another case where you decide what risk is good for you and play the game to that end. If the possibility of losing an item in PvP combat is too great, it is you choice as to whether or not you want to wear it in a situation where PvP is possible.

    Cases like this where players make a decision about the level of risk that they are willing to accept are quite common in game.

  • Nerf the Ring of the Ancients
    Considering how easy it is to reach 200+ int, I'm surprised that people would KS a rare spawn to get an item that is a mere +1 INT better than something else easily obtainable by them. In any case, as I mentioned in a thread yesterday, we're pleased with the rate that JBoots are coming into the game at the moment. If the fact that casters want to wear this ring is partly responsible for the speed that jboots are coming in game, we probably wouldn't want to change it.

  • What is the point of having two slots for bracers, but not sleeves?
    Basically, it's just the way it was designed, and besides, two different sleeves would look funny :)

  • Flamin' Sword Idea
    Well, though not precisely a pet from a gameplay standpoint per se, it is a pet from a game engine standpoint, so having more than one at a time would be prohibited.

    I'd think of the spell more like a DOT spell than a pet.

  • Blood Ember Vambraces Weight
    After a second look we've decided to lower the weight on the breastplate and helm. It will go live in the next item patch.

  • Rune Series Bug?
    I read that thread. It's a fairly compelling argument, enough so that I collared the programmer to talk about the issue. The programmer confirmed that the damage is applied to the skin AFTER damage mitigation takes place. In other word, Rune III on a warrior will typically last longer than Rune III on an enchanter, since the warrior is covered in armor and has high defensive skills.

    I haven't seen any other posts besides this one mentioning that they've seen it wear off due to misses, but if some can corroborate this situation I'll see if I can run some tests myself.

  • Verant & Rangers: Both Right?
    Though the numbers are arbitrary, this is probably the best argument I've seen yet on the subject. Of course we have commented in the past that if you are getting hit too much, maybe you should back off for a few combat rounds and let the critter get stuck on someone who can take the damage. The frequent counter to this is that they shouldn't have to back off for a few rounds, yet other classes have to do this all of the time. A wizard can't just go in chain-casting ice-comment and expect to be ignored. A necro can't stack 5 DOTs on the critter plus have a pet beating on it and remain safe. Palys and SKs frequently don't have the problem of attracting unwanted attention, but Bards certainly do.

    How about just backing off for a few seconds?

  • Patches During Daylight
    [Quote from original post: Any major telco/ISP i've worked with does their maint work during low usage hours, ie: 3-6am local time when the majority of their customers aren't using their service. pay someone the OT 3rd shift money to come in and do your patches then ]

    3-6am is still pretty busy on the servers. In any case, 3rd shift money hasn't anything to do with our selection of downtimes. It's a combination of the inevitability of affecting people when taking a game down that is played internationally, meaning that it's always someone's primetime play time, and the fact that we like to be sure that we have the entire EverQuest team at arms length when making a patch in case anything unforeseen happens.

  • Resurrect
    [Quote from original post: Does Verant Actually Believe that Everyone Can Get a Resurrect Everytime They Die?]

    Everyone, everytime? Of course not. Enough of the time to mitigate some of the experience loss to the point that it encourages people to make friends with classes that can rez? Sure.

    As with many other issues, EverQuest players are responsible for determining the level of risk that is acceptable to them. If the risk of dying without a rez is too great to be palatable, then players need to take steps to mitigate that risk. They can do this by adventuring with cleris, paladins, and the occasional necro who might be conned out of an essense emerald, or by adventuring into situations only where the death potential is managable.

    I know that I've said the same thing as above for many different issues lately, but it seems that a lot of the complaints lately are on issues where the amount of risk encountered is primarily controlled by the player.

    Players are in control of their destiny and the amount of risk that they are willing to absorb.

  • Today's Patch
    It's nothing game-affecting for most people, just a new eqgame.exe for to solve a frequent crashing issue being experienced by a few people.

  • Getting Banned From EQ
    To try and reassure you, we don't ban people on a whim, as it would make no sense for us to kick out paying customers without reason.

    People who are suspended or banned frequently come to this board to plead their case to the public. As a matter of personal and professional policy, I won't comment publicly on the specifics of any individual customer's account, but I do investigate each issue as they are presented here so that I can feel confident when I say what I said in the first paragraph.

    As I mentioned above, I can't come out here and defend these issues as they come up. It's not appropriate to address customer's private issues even if they choose to bring them out in the public light first. I will however be happy to address these issues with the account holder in email, or put their request into the hands of someone who can answer their questions.

  • The Zek Servers
    As I've said in previous posts, the only thing that we have planned currently is allowing people to move between TZ and VZ to determine what loot rules people as a whole generally prefer. Any further plans in regards to server rulesets will need to wait until this happens.

    As for RZ going coin-only, that too will need to wait until we have more data. I've received a ton of emails both ways on the subject, and am aware of the concerns of both sides. I do hope that there is a solution to be found that will please both groups, but as mentioned above, any further plans will be on hold pending the results of the current one.

  • Resist Rates on Necro Darkness Series
    The resist rates for the spell is the same as other magic-based spells such as fear, or root. Targets to not get dual saving throws for the spell. It certainly lands more than half the time on low greens assuming that the target isn't immune to magic.

    Personally, I didn't use the spell regularly until the high 30s due to the resists and mana costs. After that it became quite reliable and economical, and I rarely get a resist on a non-immune blue.

  • EverMon a cheating program?
    Though I can't endorse any specific program, we expose certain server status information to the outside world for those who are willing to write programs to display it, such as on some of the major websites.

    Use of these programs is not prohibited.

  • Alchemy Prices
    [Quote from original post: Abashi- Take ten minutes to fix Alchemy prices.!!! Abashi... ye promised TWICE that ye would get alchemy prices dropped. Please dont put it off AGAIN.]

    Please don't read promises into situations where they haven't been made. I said that we'd *look into* the prices to see if they needed to be lowered, I did not say that they *would* be lowered.

    It's still on the plate, and I hope to have an ETA in a few days. And just a note, it's definitely more than a 10 minute process.

The Standard: Sony Cleans House
[42 Comments] - Aurum @ 10:55 AM PDT
It was announced and reported today that Sony acquired Verant Interactive. While we've heard a little about what will be happening to Verant, we know now a little more about recent events at the Los Angeles Sony Online offices. Here clip from the exclusive article found on The Standard:

    EXCLUSIVE Soon-to-be-appointed Sony Online Entertainment chief Kelly Flock is cleaning house, shuttering the last remaining office at the electronics giant's online-gaming unit in Los Angeles, according to Sony sources.

    More than 50 employees – one hired as recently as Tuesday – were given their pink slips at 2 p.m. PDT today, following a terse 10-minute speech given by Flock to a roomful of crestfallen employees.
Here is another very interesting excerpt from the article:
    The bloodletting in the Sony Online Entertainment saga provides clues as to what Flock's new strategy for the fledgling company might entail. The core focus of operations will center on the games-developer studio Verant in San Diego, which is charged with creating more hardcore online games, a genre with which Sony has done well. Most notably, the subscription-based medieval adventure game EverQuest serves more than 500,000 subscribers who pay $9.89 a month for the game, plus up to $50 for the off-the-shelf software.

    Other games coming down the pike include the sequel EverQuest 2, a new game called Sovereign, a Star Wars-related game and a proposed project connected to the upcoming PlayStation 2.

    Flock previously ran the Sony PlayStation gaming studio 989 Studios but left earlier this year after Sony Computer Entertainment America rolled the unit back into the parent company. He has since been in discussions with Stringer and was lured back to the firm in April to lead Sony Online Entertainment.

    Insiders say Flock didn't think much of the online entertainment unit's free offerings, which were advertising-supported, and chose instead to focus his energy on more-lucrative subscription games.
It should also be noted that the layoffs will probably have little to no effect on EverQuest and Verant.

Also, for those underage the article contains a few four letter words from the ex-employee comments. Be forewarned.

Sony Pictures Entertainment Acquires Verant
[14 Comments] - Aurum @ 10:10 AM PDT
It is now official, Sony Pictures Entertainment has bought out Verant Interactive, Inc. Important questions pertaining to Verant's future is answered in the following press release sent to me by SOE Executive Vice President John Smedley:

    SONY PICTURES ENTERTAINMENT ACQUIRES
    ONLINE GAMING COMPANY VERANT INTERACTIVE &
    APPOINTS KELLY FLOCK AS PRESIDENT & CEO OF
    SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT (SOE)

    Verant Becomes Part Of SOE As Sony Pictures Entertainment
    Takes Steps To Strengthen Its Interactive Gaming Leadership Position

    CULVER CITY, CA, May 31, 2000-Sony Pictures Entertainment (SPE) announced
    today it has acquired the online games company Verant Interactive, Inc. and
    named prominent digital gaming executive Kelly Flock as president and CEO of
    Sony Online Entertainment (SOE) to bolster SOE's position as the dominant
    cyber-destination for interactive game enthusiasts.

    Verant, a San Diego-based development studio that creates and markets
    cutting-edge online games, will be merged into SOE as part of SOE's effort
    to concentrate on becoming the premier attraction for interactive gaming
    enthusiasts from its new San Diego headquarters. With a staff of more than
    150 software developers, Verant has been responsible for operating the
    popular online games EverQuest, TanarusT and SovereignT. Verant President
    John Smedley will remain with SOE as executive vice president and chief
    operating officer.

    Mr. Flock will supervise the development of engaging new interactive games
    by SOE and Verant, and will oversee SOE's award-winning Web site, the
    Station@sony.com, which features EverQuest, the top-selling online PC game
    title, and such popular attractions for non-paying, casual users as Wheel of
    Fortune, JEOPARDY! and Trivial Pursuit Online.

    Yair Landau, president of SPE's recently created Sony Pictures Digital
    Entertainment (SPDE) unit, which includes SOE, said the acquisition of
    privately-held Verant and Mr. Flock's appointment reinforces SPE's
    determination to fortify its online businesses and enable SOE to deliver the
    most exciting, cutting-edge, network gaming to interactive consumers.

    In Mr. Flock's previous role as president of the interactive game company
    989 Studios, he oversaw the rapid growth of EverQuest into an interactive
    gaming powerhouse. Under his supervision, EverQuest broke all
    subscription-based online gaming sales records, reaching more than 250,000
    paid subscribers and 350,000 units sold. During peak periods online, more
    than 60,000 people can play EverQuest simultaneously, acting as adventurers
    and dragon slayers in cyberspace. Average playing time per player is about
    20 hours a week.

    "As the pre-eminent executive in the interactive gaming community, Kelly
    Flock is perfectly suited to build on Sony Online Entertainment's leadership
    position and lift it to new levels of excellence," said Howard Stringer,
    chairman and CEO of Sony Corporation of America (SCA). "With all of his
    wonderful skills, talent and creativity, we're delighted that Kelly will
    continue to work his digital magic in the extended Sony family."

    "We're delighted to welcome Verant and Kelly into the fold at a time when
    interactive gaming is an increasingly important part of our business
    portfolio," said Mel Harris, co-president and chief operating officer for
    Sony Pictures Entertainment. "Considering that consumers worldwide spent
    nearly $7 billion on computer games last year, we look to Kelly and his team
    at SOE to broaden our base and make us a pre-eminent force in this
    fast-growing business."

    "The acquisition of Verant is a very natural fit for us, considering the way
    our companies have worked together on games like Everquest. And, we've been
    very interested in having Kelly join us for some time," Mr. Landau said.
    "Kelly is a world-class executive who commands enormous respect in the
    interactive gaming universe. As we focus more and more on SOE becoming a
    pure gaming company, we're pleased to bring aboard both a high-profile
    industry leader like Kelly and to acquire an innovative gaming leader in
    Verant to oversee both our 'casual' gaming, led by such tent-pole properties
    as Wheel of Fortune and JEOPARDY!, and our pay programming led by the
    hot-selling EverQuest."

    "I continue to believe that online gaming is the fastest-growing, most excit
    ing area of interactive entertainment," Mr. Flock said. "It is a great
    honor to be able to rejoin the pioneers at SOE and the EverQuest development
    teams, under John Smedly's direction at Verant, in furthering our goal of
    facilitating the next evolution in games. Sony Online Entertainment has the
    edge in technology and content, and I am excited by the opportunity to
    create and lead the next mass-market category of gaming."

    The appointment of Mr. Flock, a longtime Sony executive, as president of SOE
    coincides with a shift in emphasis and location for the interactive gaming
    concern. The company has sharpened its focus on SOE as a creator and
    distributor of all kinds of networked gaming, and has moved its headquarters
    from New York to San Diego, where Mr. Flock will be based.

    Mr. Flock was appointed president of Sony Imagesoft in March 1995.
    Imagesoft evolved into Sony Computer Entertainment America's (SCEA's) sole
    North American development studio before launching as 989 StudiosT, an SCEA
    wholly-owned independent PlayStation publisher, in April 1998. In that
    position, he was responsible for day-to-day operations of the
    cross-platform, multimedia entertainment software company and reported
    directly to Ken Kutaragi, the chairman and chief executive officer of Sony
    Computer Entertainment America, Inc., who was recently named to the Board of
    Directors of Tokyo-based Sony Corporation.

    Before joining Sony, Mr. Flock held various senior executive positions at
    LucasArts Entertainment Company and other interactive entertainment
    companies, including Electronic Arts and Activision. During his tenure with
    LucasArts Entertainment Company, the company emerged as one of the leading
    CD-ROM publishers in the industry. He obtained his B.A. in English
    literature from San Francisco State University.

    [Quote from original post: Isnt everquest supposed to be fun?]

People will quit these games and go on to the next one when the fun that they get from playing it is no longer worth the things that are necessary for the fun to exist in the first place. Our goal of course is to make sure that it takes a long time for the average person to traverse that course.

PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Change Pending
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:00 PM PDT
Gordon announced new information concerning some changes coming to the Race War servers. Here's the post from the Gameplay Board:

    Hello all,

    Today we've been brainstorming about what has become known as the PermaMez bug present on the PvP-teams servers. Without going into specifics on how the bug is used, basically, a player can use the interaction between illusions and the mesmerize spell to cause an opponent to be permanently mesmerized. If you play on the PvP Teams servers, you are probably somewhat familiar with this issue.

    The issue that makes the bug possible is deeply woven into the fabric of how illusions work, making it something that cannot be addressed directly. Initial ideas (ruled unacceptable) on how to fix this issue included:

    1. Turning off illusions entirely on the PvP Teams servers.
    2. Making stun and mez spells invalid in PvP combat.
    3. Making an illusion cause a team-switch. In other words, if a dark elf cast "Illusion: Human", they would become a human in all senses, able to attack and be attacked by people not on the human team.
    4. Making an illusion cause a team-switch, but only for the purpose of Mez and Stun spells. This idea would lead to situations where one person could attack someone who couldn't attack them in return.

    The final solution that we've arrived at is the best method that we've thought of to address the issue, and the one that will most likely go live unless someone here can bring something to our attention that we've overlooked. The solution is:

    On PvP Teams servers, casting a *non-beneficial spell* upon another *player-character* will result in any active illusion upon the caster, even illusions that have other effects, being canceled.

    "Illusion" in this case is defined as any spell effect that makes you look like something different than you actually are. This would include all of the enchanter illusions, as well as spells such as treeform, wolf form, the Call of Bones series, and Item-activated illusions such as the Guise of the Deceiver, etc.

    If anyone has any questions or comments on this change, or can think of any issues that make the solution worse than the problem it solves, please post follow-ups in this thread.

    Thanks,

    -Gordon

    The post that was replied to was about the experience curve, not a class. The quote, "with a ranger of all things" was speaking to the high experience requirement of the class, not the class itself. Had the first character been a SK or Paladin, the quote would have been similar.

  • Root and Snare
    [Quote from original post: Shame on you, Abashi...you're a necro, and you know this to be false. Dark a mob and send your pet in, then run away and cast a dd or a dot at it...why is the mob running at you now?]

    If you look at the mob, it will usually turn, stop, and hit the pet a few times, then turn and continue pursuit. It doesn't just chase after the necro ignoring getting hit in the back by the pet. It's more than the taunting being done by the pet. The NPC wants to kill the necro, but by virtue of being under a slowing spell settles for hitting the pet every few steps.

    The effect is not as pronounced (or as useful) as when rooted, but it exists.

  • Changing Servers
    Over time, servers develop their own atmosphere or community based upon the personalities of those playing on them. With a relatively small population of players on a server, people build (or hurt) their reputations, and live and work in concert with others that they have in essense "grown up" with.

    We feel that this community is one of the things that is responsible for the success of EQ. People are not faceless characters cloaked in anonymity, but instead must reap the rewards or consequences of their actions. This, over all, tends to encourage people to behave more honorably in their adventures.

    Based upon the same logic that we use in regards to not changing names unless they violate the naming policy, character moves are likewise prohibited as it allows someone to escape their reputation, and also breaks down the positive aspects of a community.

    We have in the past allowed characters to be moved under certain limited circumstances but that started causing more problems than it solved, and we stopped doing them. The only reason that we allow them now is for the purpose of a spreading out population, based upon the logic that enough people are moving from the server that reputations tend to follow the move. We are also planning on allowing some PvP players to move to another ruleset in the near future, but that is the extent of the program.

  • Experience Loss
