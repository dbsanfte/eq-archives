Caster Balance Follow-up
- Aurum @ 7:26 PM PST
A second follow-up was posted in the Caster Balance section of the Developer's Corner today. As always, the responses were crafted by Alan VanCouvering:

    Hello and thank you for letting the casters know you are looking into things.

    I do have a favor to ask however. On various sites, there has been a lot of question and I will admit flaming that these fixes seem to be aimed that the 55-60 crowd. While it is only logical that if people continue to play their characters the majority of the players will be in this group. There are people who enjoy playing the levels below this. I for one have repeatedly played characters up and then started a new one.

    I would just like to have an answer saying that yes we are looking at ALL levels of play and balance issues.

    Thank you for your time.

    We're looking at the whole thing, just as we did with melee.

    The conclusion might be that only changes are needed post 50. It might not. But we're not neglecting any stage of the life of the caster.

    vallon_sk wrote:

    Clerics, shaman and druids are Pure Casters??
    I've been under the impression since I started in '99, that wiz, mage, ench, and necro's are the only Pure Casters-as they have no other abilities but their magic (with out going into huge discussion that necro can 'tank/melee" with their pet-to a degree). And if a shaman is a Pure Caster, than where do the beastlords fall? And why not add Shadowknight/pally to the list.
    Just curious.
    Thanks.

    Let's nail down some terminology, for everyone's sake. I'll edit Rich to make sure he follows the same terminology. :)

    Pure Caster means any non-hybrid, non-melee (Necromancer, Magician, Enchanter, Wizard, Cleric, Druid and Shaman).

    Int Caster are Necromancers, Magicians, Enchanters and Wizards.

    Wis Casters (or Priests) are Clerics, Druids and Shamans.

    Hybrids are Bards (though they will probably have to be considered separately for most things), Rangers, Paladins, Shadowknights and Beastlords.

    Melee are Monks, Rogues and Warriors.

    Alan

Caster Balance Update
- Aurum @ 4:31 PM PST
Alan brought us this big update on the caster balancing issue started a few weeks ago:

    Most of you probably remember the melee balancing changes we made last year. We'll I have some information for you from the fellow that spearheaded that project, and is leading the caster balancing work.


    quote:
    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Caster Balance

    It's been clear for awhile now that priests and casters haven't been as well balanced as we'd all like them to be. Balance is a delicate issue and any major changes to overall class power are always researched, debated, and taken slowly. There are some really big improvements in the works now for helping out with that balance, and we expect those to be on the Test server in the near future. These changes won't all come in one huge patch, but will show up a bit at a time until casting classes are right where they should be in relative power.

    We are taking the needs and issues of casters seriously, and we think you'll be very happy with the results you see after we finish our caster balance effort. We hope you'll be patient just a bit longer, while we take the time to get everything implemented, balanced, and tested.


    Ancient Spells

    One specific area we are addressing for casting classes revolves around how much improvement they get from raiding and acquiring high-level items. When a melee class begins to acquire items from the hardest monsters, they experience a tremendous growth in power. A melee character sporting Vulak weapons and Temple of Veeshan loot is incredibly more powerful than player who only owns average quality items. Casting classes don't achieve nearly the same amount of increased power from raid items, and rapidly fall behind melees in relative power when they start raiding. It doesn't make sense for a wizard who has helped in slaying hundreds of dragons to be using the same spells as someone who has yet to take part in raid encounters.

    To address this problem, we've introduced the new "Ancient" category of spells recently. Ancient spells are rare, high-quality spells that can only be obtained from the most powerful monsters. These spells are improved versions of spells already available to each casting class. They won't give casters any brand-new, or out-of-class abilities, but are intended to make the casters better at what they already do. Much like a warrior improves himself by getting a better sword from a dragon, a wizard can now acquire more efficient spells in the same way.

    As you might expect, Ancient spells are going to be rare and take a certain amount of effort to obtain. We don't expect many casters to ever get all the Ancient spells for their class. This is a change from the traditional approach we've had to spells, which has allowed every caster to feel entitled to get every one of their spells. We'll continue to make core spells available to casting classes off of vendors, or as relatively frequent drops off of monsters, but we want to be clear that Ancient spells are rare and powerful items of treasure, and we intend them to be very hard to get.

    Only the first set of Ancient spells have been added to the game. In the coming weeks, we'll continue to add more Ancient spells to the game and expand the selection for casters and priests. It takes some time to get the spells balanced, tested, and placed on monsters - so expect to see them coming in a few sets at a time.

    One last thing to note about Ancient spells- the power of these spells will vary depending on what monster drops them. You can expect Vulak A'err to have more powerful Ancient spells than you might find on Trakanon, for instance.

    Rich Waters
    Lead Designer, Systems
    Sony Online Entertainment



    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------


    Thanks for the update, Rich.

    Alan

Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 8:47 PM PST
Three updates to report to you tonight from the Developer's Corner:

  • Fan Faire and Mennix
    You can register for the Seattle Fan Faire at the Fan Faire site. Don't miss your chance to meet Mennix in person!

    Speaking of the, um… handsome Halfling, he's written another column.

  • Warder Duration Update
    So I assume the same thing goes for wizard fammiliars right?
    I never paid much attention as I dont keep my fammiliar around for 10 real life hours.

    Correct, and for the same reason.

  • Another Legends Follow-up
    Does this mean that we are locked into the Legends server for 6 months? What if we get there, find it empty, not what we thought it would be and not worth the $40 per month that we thought it would be worth (obviously this is a subjective choice on the part of the player, whether he thinks its worth the $40 per month to continue or not), or begin to miss friends, or have a change in our financial situations after 2-3 months and want to cancel and go back to the server we came from? Will we not be allowed to do so for 6 months?

    Please clarify this. This is important in deciding whether to "try out" this new server or not. Your original advertisement stated that you would be able to go over to Legends, and if you didnt want to continue, for whatever reasons, you would be able to return to your server with your gear, and whatever gear and levels you obtained on Legends. This original advertisement sounds more like or similar to a "money back guarantee", ie, if it isnt what you wanted, you are free to return with no penalties for your time and money spent. Whereas the 2nd set of rules you just released sounds like the phone company telling you "no you cannot cancell, you are locked in for a 6 month contract".

    Let me try to clear this up.

    If you cancel your Legends subscription, for whatever reason, we will stop billing you and your subscription will end at the end of the period you've paid for. If you sign up, pay for the first month, then decide to cancel after two weeks we won't bill you again and when the month is out your access to the servers will be gone. Also at that time, under most circumstances, we'll move your characters.

    However, we will be looking at the characters as they move. We will be looking for situations that indicate that someone is using the Legends service as an attempt to bypass the spirit of the rules.

    If we notice a character moving off Legends after cancellation with items that can not be obtained on the server they are moving to we will examine the situation. Legends Calendar Information
- Aurum @ 3:55 PM PST
Alan added this post to the Developer's Corner earlier this afternoon regarding a new feature of the Legends service:

    I just wanted to pass this information on to you all. We'll be adding it to the Legends FAQ shortly.

    -------------------------------------------------------------
    quote:

    Question: The Legends service has a Calendar of Events that will be listing approved player submitted events, as well as GM run occasions, Dynamic Events and other fun stuff. Since this calendar can include player events, will these be enforceable by the GMs in terms of rights to particular monsters, encounters or zones?

    Preamble: Such issues are governed by the EverQuest Play Nice Polices as described at http://everquest.station.sony.com/support/policies/conduct.jsp. We encourage our players to be familiar with these policies as, with any server, they will very much govern player conflict resolution on Legends servers.

    Answer: In the past EverQuest Customer Service did not recognize player created web calendars for spawn contention resolution. The difficulty with multiple calendars, who hosted said calendars and a myriad of other issues made such enforcement not practicable. Given that the Legends service Calendar of Events is a single source, monitored and approved by EverQuest Customer Service, it will be a legitimate factor for the resolution of altercations. However, it will not be the only factor.

    Legends Customer Service is very interested in the development and maintenance of the player community. In the interest of fair play, we will consider additional factors when finding resolution to such issues as spawn, encounter or zone contention. Such factors could include the ability of the force present to tackle their scheduled target, the current and past conduct of the players or guilds involved in the dispute as well as any record listed on the Calendar of Events. As with any resolution, we will look to find one that is fair, unbiased and mutually equitable to all parties involved. A lofty goal, but one that we hope, with the help and cooperation of the players, to attain.

    As an addendum, I would like to point out some guidelines we are working on for the Calendar of Events. We will have more specifics ready for everyone at launch. While we will strive to approve submissions in an impartial, unbiased fashion, there are certain things we will not allow. The Calendar is mainly a tool to inform players of events that are open for others can participate in. While notifying others of your intent to tackle a raid zone is nice, if this activity is not open to others, we may not post it. The constant monopolization of encounters will not be allowed. Also, while the fact that you may be logging in and Looking For a Group in Sebilis tonight might be nice to know, it may not be appropriate news for the Calendar of Events.

    We look forward to seeing you on Stormhammer.

    Respectfully,

    Lead GM Bordael

    No, they don't.
