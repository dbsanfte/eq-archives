#10   Name: Sarkoth (sarkoth_eq@hotmail.com)   Date: 5/2 @ 7:10 AM PST
While there are a couple of minor historical inaccuracies in Taronga's editorial, I feel it does a reasonable job of putting the Iksar faction issues in real life terms that are easier for people to comprehend. Although "equat[ing] EQ with real life is inherently flawed," people need to have examples they can conceptualize from when dealing with fiction.

A great example of this was Gene Rodenberry's basing the "factions" in the original Star Trek series on Cold War geopolitics: the Federation represented the "free world"; the Klingons, the Soviet Union; the Romulans, the Chinese, etc. Parallels needed to be drawn with examples everyone could relate to. What better bad guy, for American audiences during the Cold War, could there have been to pattern villains after than the Communist Bloc?

Yes, real life doesn't always make for the best of examples, but if it lays a foundation for allowing people to get a grasp of why things are in a work of fiction/fantasy, I say go for it.

I suppose someone complained about why Verant chose to make the Iksar KOS everywhere and the author of the editorial piece chose to approach the issue from a RP/historical side. Granted, not everyone who plays Everquest believes in roleplaying or seeing Norrath in those terms. That said, it is the author's right to express his/her opinions from that standpoint. It is NOT the only viewpoint on this issue so if you think this needs to be talked about in terms of numbers, balance and other practical factors, feel free to post your own editorial.

IMO, Verant needed to balance out the number of outstanding characteristics of the Iksar race as player characters. Being KOS to everyone should be the least of the downsides to playing an Iksar. Look at what we get for this "penalty":

1. regeneration
2. infravision
3. innate mastery of the swimming skill
4. a slight AC bonus due to our scaly hides
5. 4 great zones right outside our city gates
6. plenty of mobs to kill, each generally dropping good coin
7. numerous quests within the city itself

Being KOS is not a big deal, and I suspect those who complain about it most are players who have never played an dark race before. There are significant downsides to playing an Iksar than the faction issues:

1. a horrible experience penalty, on par with trolls
2. Iksar, as a race, will have a hard time being prolific-- we have no druids or mages to teleport us around and our best healer is a shaman (and that means no rezzes or revives)
3. we can't wear plate- no biggie
4. we live on a very hostile continent

All in all, I think the positives and negatives balance out and we just have to live with the hand that Verant gave us.
