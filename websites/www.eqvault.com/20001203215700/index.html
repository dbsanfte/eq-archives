EQ GameSpot's Online RPG of the Week
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:41 PM PST
Andrew Park informed me that GameSpot is featuring EverQuest as their Online RPG of the Week. The first few pages of the article contain a write-up of EverQuest's history followed by a Q&A session with Verant's Jeff Buttler. Here's a clip:

    GS: Thanks for taking the time for this interview. EverQuest has been a remarkably successful game, and it's changed a great deal since its initial release. Would you say that the game is more or less where it should be in terms of development? What's the best change that's been implemented since its release?

    JB: I continue to be pleased with EverQuest as it matures, and I am fairly satisfied with the game at this stage. That said, we look forward to refining and adding to the game in the future.

    In my opinion the best change since release was the implementation of randomized spawns and loot drops for the Kunark expansion.
To read the rest, use the link provided in the above.


Absor Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:36 PM PST
Here's more quotes and excerpts written by Alan VanCouvering and found on the Gameplay Board:

  • More on Magician Epic Quest Weapon
    Ok, I had a few long and informative talks with the guy that designed this quest. I'm certainly not going to give you any hints, but I'll try to respond to these questions as best I can.

    -windigo- wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "here is whats #$^ed about this quest
    burning embers don't drop or have yet to be emplemented(and they love to say)"]

    They do drop, they have been implemented. Really, they do and they are.

    [Quote from original post: "Dirt of underfoot same as burning embers" ]

    Again, they drop. In both cases, you might be looking in the wrong place…

    [Quote from original post: "earth elemental mastery staff should be dropping off menions of innoruuk instead of off the god himself==lame rare drop that noone on my server has." ]

    Actually, the wording on that part of the quest was left intentionally vague to encompass potential changes to that plane (at some far future date). Sure, it's hard to get. It's supposed to be hard to get.

    [Quote from original post: "if this wasn't enough we get to quest twice in air for our last part ....crown and the elemental master himself who spawns with no indication that he will after you turn in the air components .......yeah!! big fun and wait till you turn in the items at the end and face the 4 elements themselves and get your ass whipped and loose a years worth... i love it" ]

    It's worth the work. Trust me…

    Cardulus said:

    [Quote from original post: "on alazazams this one guy wrote he talked with the guy that made the quest and said that the burning embers and dirt of underfoot IS dropping (doubt it) and the staff will drop off minions later but only inny for now..... not sure if i belive it but if the staff should drop off minions it should of AT THE TIME THE QUEST WAS RELESSED" ]

    No. It is supposed to be right where it is. When/if at some later date it might drop off something different, but it will be something equally difficult. The staff is right where it belongs. It sounds like someone did talk to the guy that made the quest, because the answers were all correct.

  • Patch Problems
    Ok, let's try this in an orderly fashion…

    If any of you have tried playing after the latest patch, with nothing running in the background, and with the latest sound and video drivers, and you are still having this problem, send me the results of your DXDIAG and I'll see what I can do. avancouvering@verant.com

  • Mage FA Question
    We've looked at this, and it's working just fine. All the parts are dropping as they should, folks just are not finding them. I can't give clues, but the stuff is there.

  • Sound Problems
    Try here.

    http://www.esstech.com/Technical/drivers/downloadable/drivers/driver.htm

  • Patch Issues
    Xenomorpheus wrote:

    [Quote from original post: "After the patch the other day, sound was messed up for a LOT of people. Verant and some others pointed the blame elsewhere, saying over and over to update Sound Drivers, and install DX7."]

    And about 90% of the problems that we have encountered were fixed with updated drivers.

    Sure, there are/were a few bugs on our end, we never denied that. But most of the problems were fixed by the solution that you so readily mock.

    [Quote from original post: "- Fixed some problems with sound volume being set above the maximum or below the minimum.
    - Fixed a problem that caused sound volume to be reset regardless of the slider setting upon zoning or entering game.
    - Fixed a bug that would cause some sounds to be unduly amplified whenever the volume slider was moved." ]

    You will note that these problems are non-critical problems. We're glad they're fixed, but the more important problems were the ones where people were locking up or having terrible video problems. Most of those were driver problems.

  • Hiding Players/See Invisible Spell Issue
    [Quote from original post: Hiding is not a spell. You are physically out of the line of sight of others.

    See Invis is not X-ray vision!]

    There's an easy answer to this.

    Yes, see invisible IS an x-ray vision spell that only sees living things.

    The simple reason for the way see invisible works is that there is no way that we were going to allow a way for anyone to be completely invisible.

  • Weapon Scale
    Take a look at the new way items scale. I've seen some of them, and they look a lot better.

Patch Message: 11-21-00
[64 Comments] - Aurum @ 9:41 AM PST
Today's Patch Message is now up:

    November 21st, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    *Patch Day*

    Today's patch addresses a few problems noted by customers since last week's big patch. The following changes were made:

    - Fixed some problems with sound volume being set above the maximum or below the minimum.
    - Fixed a problem that caused sound volume to be reset regardless of the slider setting upon zoning or entering game.
    - Fixed a bug that would cause some sounds to be unduly amplified whenever the volume slider was moved.
    - Fixed a bug causing pink fringes to show on whiskers in association with mipmapping.
    - Fixed a bug with /usercolor causing settings to be saved in the wrong slot.
    - Fixed weapon scaling problems with off-hand weapons and those carried by dwarves.
    - Fixed a bug that caused the client to display an amount of experience in slight disagreement with the server.
    - Fixed the Paladin Epic Quest reward, which was procing a spell that would do around one million points of damage if left to its own devices.
    - Fixed a faction problem that made some quests designed to be doable by anyone, regardless of race or class, undoable.

    In other news, the Tarew/Povar split server, Xev, will be up following the patch today. Xev is named for Xev Bristlebane, the god of fortune. We would have named the new server under its full name if not for an attempt to avoid confusion between the existing "Bristlebane" server (named for Fizzlethorpe Bristlebane) and Xev.

    - The EverQuest Team

Absor Infobits
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:49 PM PST
A few more posts were made this evening by Alan on the Gameplay Board:

  • Graphic Issue Comment
    [Quote from original post: The requirements are for "DirectX Compatible" video and sound cards. No specific version of DirectX is given, just the DirectX specification as a whole.

    If your video card or sound card manufacturer stopped staying up to date with the DirectX specification, your card is no longer DirectX Compatible and you no longer meet the minimum system requirements.

    Quite simple, and clear.]

    Exactly, Drysart.

  • "Make an EQ Movie!"
    I could have a script for it by the end of the year...

  • Logic Problem
    I read it, and I was going to reply, but I'll do it here.

    With a game like EverQuest, where every item that you see needs to be an actual 3D model (unlike other games where items are essentially flat sprites), we have to keep the number of models as small as possible. Each time you enter a zone the game loads up these models into your video memory. There is only so much memory there for us to use, especially since we have to stick to the system requirements for the game as it was released (at least for basic EverQuest…). It was decided early on that some items (cloaks, shoulder pads, etc.) just couldn't be displayed. Cloth items, it was decided, wouldn't be displayed either, also for reasons of these very same restrictions.

    We don't like these restrictions any more than you do. But we just can't display everything.

    Fortunately, we have pushed the technology a bit, and with Velious we've included more textures. Hopefully in the future we can push your machine even further…

Alan VanCouvering, Verant's Community Relations Manager, stopped by the Gameplay Board and posted the following there:

  • Outstanding Issues After Last Patch
    MipMapping: As another poster suggested, you may need to fiddle with your settings. Now that things are working better, some settings (especially the gamma slider) probably need to be adjusted. And anyone seeing black sky or other odd things should update their video drivers.

    Video Modes: Nope. The 'fix' for that is something set in the far future. We'd love to fix it, but redesigning the entire interface takes time.

    Bulk Purchases: Another thing we want to do, but it's not high on the priority list.

    Paladin 2H piercing: Velious.

    On Line Store: Dunno, still trying to get answers on that one.

    JBoots: They're fine as is.

    Bank: Another thing we want to do that has a low priority.

    Banners: I can't say that I like then either, but they seem to be here to stay.

    Music: *shrug* I don't know what to say…

    Mouse Wheel Support: I'll put it on my list, but there aren't a whole lot of places where the wheel would be useful…

    Bug/Feedback: Good idea, I'll mention it to Roy. But, again, low priority item.

    Graphics: That sort of things will get upgraded as much as it can. But we have to keep the game compatible with the original minimum system requirements.

    Epic Weapons: *sigh* "They do not cause video lag like swaying trees." I'll say it again, swaying trees have only a very tiny effect on framerate (the animation for all the trees loads ONCE). It's the volume of trees that is the issue, not the swaying. As for the epic weapons… I think they're fine as they are, but I'll bring it up (and yes, they would cause MORE of a framerate issue than the fact that trees sway, since they have a particle effect).

    Grimstaff: I agree, when blind you should still see text. I'll add it to my list, but again it's not going to make it to the high priority list.

  • Driver Problems
    If you are still having problems after updating your drivers, send us the results of a DXDIAG and we'll take a look at it. Make sure to include a description of the problem and your system configuration. Send it all to eqtech@verant.com

  • Unfair Attack Thread
    I'm closing this one.

    It's unfair to attack Rowyl here, since he's not going to be able to go into details here (and neither am I). I'll say it again, we won't discuss the details of anyone's account in a public forum.

  • Mapable Key Problem
    We're working on some problems with the mapable keys. I'm pretty sure, however, that the backspace key is not going to be user mapable. You'll need to find another key, I think… You also won't be able to map ESC or /. But you will be able to map the Caps Lock key.

    All to be fixed, soon. :)

  • Game Exploits
    That's right. In any case where you think someone is exploiting the game, do this:

    Make sure any useful text is in the visible chat window and do /report [exploiters name].

    Then send a /petition with complete details of the event.
