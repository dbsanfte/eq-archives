Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 8:41 PM PST
New information was posted throughout the day by SOE's Alan VanCouvering on the Developer's Corner. Here is the scoop:

  • Cazic-Thule Changes
    I was excited to hear about the changes to the zone, but quite dismayed to discover that despite its location on Antonica my manastone no longer works here.

    I don't think I'm alone among the players in my belief that while manastones would not be functioning in any alternate planes or expansion zones, they would continue to work in zones in the "old world".

    Was this simply an oversight, or is the reduction in the number of zones where this item can be used intentional?

    ------------------------------------------
    quote:

    Since the time the manastone was introduced, the world of EQ has changed enormously. Players now have hit point totals that were impossible back in the day. Spells like Complete Heal, Torpor, and Nature's recovery make the mana/hit point heal ratio totally insane these days. Beyond that, the improved efficiency and availability of regeneration is also incredibly higher than it was pre-Kunark.

    Manastones are crazy broken today. The speed, convenience, and efficiency they bring would unbalance today's high end player beyond repair. As an example, with the use of a manastone, a high end cleric can go from no mana to full mana in under a minute.
    ------------------------------------------

    While we all understand that there was an implication that we'd let manastones continue to work in the old world, the new Temple of Cazic-Thule is basically a new world zone. It's a zone designed for the same high end people and content that we think manastones break. And, well, it seems to me to be a better idea to make a good zone than to worry about one "crazy broken" item.

    :)

  • Caster Balance Comment
    Just wanted to express my surprise and joy that you corrected the mana cost of Glamour of Kintaz for us "Chanter" types :) It just cost too much mana to use that spell before in places we needed it like the King Room in Seb and Jugs. That definetely corrected the usage problem between Dazzle and Kintaz we had before this, Hats off Gents! Well done!

    Ldar

    You'll probably see a lot of changes like that. Some of them will trickle in and some will come in bigger chunks.


  • Class Specific Items
    Some folks have been asking about the idea behind class-specific loot (or the lack thereof). Questions like this one:

    Sekira-Xev wrote:

    Was marking Rowyls Armguards usable by Clerics and Shamans intentional or a mistake? Please tell me it was a mistake. This was one of the only high-end druid only items in the game, named after the same Druid that druid armor in Thurgadin was...

    It was also the ONLY item that a druid had any hope of getting from *Omitted MOB for spoiler purposes but you know who*. Now priority will be given on this item to Clerics and Shamans because, as we all know, they are vital and important classes and druids are sideliners.

    Others asked why there were not more class-specific loot drops in general.

    Rich Waters had this answer to such questions:

    ----------------------------------------------------
    quote:

    Class specific items are good in cases where they are not going to be No Drop. When they can't be traded, it's often a very frustrating situation if you have no one present who can use the item in question. Having an item that your group earned and no one can use is very annoying, as you put in the appropriate effort and the reward rots on the corpse.

    The Rowyl's Armguards are a good example. The item was changed because that's a case where 45 or more people participate in an event and on some servers the armguards were beginning to rot as the guild druids all acquired the item. Making the armguards useful to more classes lengthens the amount that can drop before a given group becomes saturated with them.

    - Rich Waters
    ----------------------------------------------------

    I suppose there will always be a fine line between the problem of classes wanting the same items and the frustration of having items drop that nobody in the group can use.

    Lawrence Poe (not worthy of a full quote… :)) mentioned the revamp of Hate Plane and how the class-specific loot their wasn't so well received. Both make very good points. Obviously no decision on such things is going to be able to please everyone. But we'll continue to do our best to make the decisions that work out best for the game.

Update on Pets and Group Experience
- Aurum @ 4:42 PM PST
Alan had this for us today concerning a couple of pet experience issue:

    Hello.

    I have an update, rather a correction, on one of the answers to the questions that the Mage Compendium folks sent in.

    I was just informed by Scott Hartsman that I made a mistake about the pet experience thing. Pets only take experience from a group if they outdamage the entire group. If they do, then they gain a share as if they were a group member. If they do not, they take no experience at all.

    This is how it's been working the whole time, and it makes a lot more sense. I'm sorry for the mistake.

    Alan

Mage Q&A Follow-up, Part 3
- Aurum @ 7:03 PM PST
A third follow-up was made by Alan to the gigantic Mage Q&A section on the Developer's Corner this afternoon. Read all about it here:

    Hello,

    Some over on the Magecompendium are proposing the idea that the more we get attention from you about our concerns the more we get hit with the "nerf-bat" as some call it. Meaning Verant will punish us for complaining.

    I saw those comments too. I'll address this issue further down.

    I, of course, dismiss that logic. But then I see this type of comment from you:

    "If the pet is indeed too powerful, perhaps implying that the pet class is too powerful, then there is an experience penalty."

    ...and I have to wonder.

    I think most (if not all) Mages agree that post 50 pets fall behind in the melee damage ratio (in contrasting pc melee and pet melee) significantly. I'd say the majority of Mages are calling for some sort of upgrade to pet's power post-50.

    So I hear.

    We also have to constantly fight others perceptions of it being a negative in having a mage in a group, whether that perception is accurate or not. (pet pathing = trains, pets "stealing" exp, pet's breaking mez...)

    This perception can make it difficult to get in a pick up group.

    This also seems to be true. I'm not at all sure how to change any inaccurate perceptions, but we're always working to fix real issues. Hopefully the balancing stuff will correct some of this.

    In trying to deal with the perception that we are a penalty to a group's exp you suggest that, perhaps, we're overpowered.

    We'll not really. I was saying that if the pet is constantly outdamaging melee classes, then it might be true that the pet and caster combined are too powerful. However, according to reports from mages, post 50 this just isn't the case. My point there is that if pets are too weak, then I can't see a reason for anyone to be worried about them taking experience.

    This feeds the theory that if we speak out we get beat down. A theory I still don't want to believe. But one that seems to fit in this instance.

    That's not an impression that I want you to have, obviously. It's also not true. Sure, sometimes we take a close look at a situation and find loopholes or bugs that we hadn't noticed before. But I wouldn't think that would be an issue (because we all want bugs fixed and loopholes closed… right?). I know that players and designers don't always see eye-to-eye on what is a bug and what is not. But we don't just go 'nerfing' a class just because they raise an issue.

    Also, it's important to note here that I'm not a designer. I'm supposed to be here to get your issues to those designers. To do so I need to understand those issues. If I sometimes question what I'm told (by players or designers, in equal portions), that either means that I just don't understand what I'm being told (not uncommon), or that there is something wrong with the logic of the argument. In either case it's important to me to ask questions. If I'm out here in this thread trying to poke holes in the logic presented to me, that's only because I see those holes. If you feel I'm wrong, great, let me know. But if I don't point out what I see you can't set me straight.

    On the other hand, if I can find serious and irreparable errors in ideas that I'm asked to present (either to the designers or to the players) then I'm going to point them out.

    Are our pets over or underpowered post 50? Is it fair to penalize us for using all of our tools at hand?

    I think these are two different issues.

    I disagree. The quality of your tools is most certainly something that should be linked to any penalty for using those tools. Heck, using your argument you seem to be saying that it wouldn't be right to give a bonus for a class that only had terribly underpowered tools at their disposal. If we invented a… er… peasant class, let's say, and gave it very weak skills all across the board, but gave them an experience bonus for doing the majority of damage in a group, would that be unfair?

    And, no, we're not introducing a peasant class…

    And it doesn't matter if our pet rarely out damages a melee in a group, it's that darned perception we fight, much like hybrids did in their day of exp penalties.

    Agreed. As I said before, I should never have brought it up. Interestingly enough, we already mentioned this whole exp thing a while ago, explained it in detail. But I guess people forgot about it.

    I suppose I find it interesting (though not amusing) that only now that I've pointed it out are people aware of it. This probably means that there are few times when the pet takes experience, and those times when it does (perhaps Epic pets?), the pet was ripping stuff up so fast that the increased exp was easily offsetting any loss to the pet. Otherwise people would have noticed it without me pointing it out. So it seems very likely that this pet exp thing has little or no impact on the game as it is now (except for soloing pet users, who have always taken steps to avoid the penalty anyhow).

    In my opinion, I think pets should be given a melee upgrade AND the exp should be dropped for grouping only, not soloing.

    I'm not saying that I disagree with you. Really, it's not my job to agree or disagree with you. It is my job, though, to find the arguments for and against such a change and present them to the team. I have my personal opinion on the matter. Until I have all the facts I can gather, though, that opinion doesn't enter into the picture. After that, well, I feel it's also my job to express my opinion once the facts and arguments are brought out.

    In fact, if both of these were done, grouping would become a more attractive alternative to soloing, since soloing with a stronger pet would make it tougher to out damage it and stop the pet from taking exp. I believe that is closer to Verant's Vision for Everquest.

    Thank you for your time.

    Ansi Firetosser

    p.s. I still wish to promote the pet's being indifferent to all mobs to avoid the perception (real or not) that any bad pathing means death for a group.

    The indifferent thing is a whole nother discussion…

    Thanks for the comments.

    Thanks to everyone for their comments.

    Alan

More Mage Talk
- Aurum @ 9:08 PM PST
Another follow-up was published to the Magician Q&A section this afternoon. This time around Alan posts more comments from players and responds to them:

    We received more than one hundred comments since yesterday on this topic, and most were very insightful. Thank you for taking the time to make comments.
    Obviously I'm not going to post all of the comments. But here are some of them:

    Alan,

    The point I think you are missing is that no matter how many justifications you come up with, we get passed up in groups simply because there is the possibility that we'll take more Exp than a single member.

    Justified or not, we are once again less group-friendly, and we are stuck soloing again.

    --Fabmatil Fabulous
    53 Gnome Magician, The Tribunal

    I wanted to start with this one because it might very well be the strongest argument of the bunch. It also points out that I probably shouldn't have even answered that question. Often the questions that people think they want answered the most are exactly the ones that shouldn't be answered.

    You are probably correct, justified or not (which is what I wanted to discuss), there will be a perception issue based on the exposure of this particular game system (where until now I suspect that very few people even thought about it when creating or filling out groups). However, it may not be true that clear understanding of the issue will lead to misperception. So at this point the best thing to do seems to be continue the discussion and see what comes of it.

    Alan,

    In terms of pets outdamaging melees, I can think of situations where this could be likely to happen, yet I think even you would agree that the pet shouldn't be taking xp from the group. Two of my best friends in EQ play a mage and shaman as their mains, my main character is a warrior. (we are all 3 currently lvl 57) Now, the mage would likely feel that her rogue pet would be the best choice when the three of us are hunting as a trio. Also, say we take on a fairly tough mob, and I decide I should go defensive so the shaman doesn't have to work as hard to keep me healthy. This causes my damage output to be reduced, yet it will be my tanking ability that ALLOWS the rogue pet to do as much damage as it does. It will almost certainly outdamage me in such a fight, and the shaman will need his mana to heal me, not to DoT or nuke, and the mage can't nuke too hard or I lose aggro. So, is it really fair that the pet takes a share of the exp in this situation? I strongly feel the answer is no. Such a situation could occur in a full group as well. Say, Warrior, mage, druid, shaman, enchanter and bard. There, that's a full group and many of the same arguments apply. A rogue pet could still outdamage everyone in a long fight, as the casters wouldn't want to spend all their mana nuking just to be sure they do damage, possibly taking aggro from the defensive tank, the bard probably couldn't outdamage even a defensive warrior if he tried with melee, and DoT songs might take aggro, etc.

    In the situation you described, though, the pet is essentially as valuable as a rogue would be (the warrior would be the reason that a rogue would be able to deal out as much damage as he would). Add to that the ability of the mage himself, and you might indeed have a single character that is able to deal the damage of two warriors. Of course all of this is situational, which makes it very hard to evaluate.

    Raw damage is not the best measure for a class's contribution to the group. Enchanters and clerics rarely do ANY actual damage in a fight, yet no one would suggest they are less valuable than the melees. Sure, if a pet is outdamaging a monk and/or rogue in a group, maybe it's overpowered, but I can't picture many situations where that's even possible (except maybe epic or rogue pet, and the monk and rogue have no haste, while the mage can buff his pet with haste, or the melees are a several levels lower than the mage).

    I really feel your arguments are based on the design belief that mage pets should never be more valuble than an actual melee. I agree with that completely, but damage is NOT the only measure of value to a group, or even the primary measure in some situations. If pets are powerful enough that they can outdamage melees in some situations, then what that says to me, is that mages are a powerful class, not that a group should suffer an exp penalty because of that power. Should a rogue get more exp because he does so much more damage? Rogues may outdamage a defensive warrior by a factor of two, but it is the warrior's taunting ability that makes this possible, so the warrior is AT LEAST as valuable to the group as the rogue.

    I'm certainly not going to insist that damage dealing is the only measure of the worth of a character. It isn't. But it's ingenuous to suggest that it's not one of the most important things. The bard, for example, isn't so much about damage dealing as support and improving the damage dealing of others, something that just counting up comparative damage can't measure. But if a class has the potential to deal out the most damage in nearly any group, then that should certainly be looked at.

    The bottom line, is that pet damage will considered as part of the caster balancing. Once casting classes are considered 'balanced', that should be the clue that no further penalty to groups should be applied, just as the hybrid exp penalty was removed when they were considered balanced. For soloing, it's probably fine as it is, as long as mages can still gain exp at a rate comparable to other classes that are supposed to solo as well as they can.

    Thank you for continuing to discuss this.

    Olav Trygvarson, Barbarian Myrmidon
    Balthozzar, Human Channeler
    Falcon's Pride
    The Nameless

    It's hard to argue with your bottom line. It's certainly not out of place to use an experience penalty for certain abilities or modes of experience gain (such as soloing, which we already do when we give a bonus for being in a group or bonuses for fighting in a dungeon).

    But one of the points I was trying to make was that the way this pet experience things works it seems rather self-regulating. If the pet is indeed too powerful, perhaps implying that the pet class is too powerful, then there is an experience penalty. If not, then there is no penalty. The key here seems to be that perhaps pure damage output isn't the best way to judge such a thing.

    Saeldar wrote:

    While I understand your explanation as to why pet are taking experience, I have to disagree with you.

    The way you look at it is in my opinion wrong. You the mage and the pet as 2 different entities, and they are not, the class magician like all other classes are incomplete without their pet. You yourself have often stated that mage do less direct damage then wizard because they have a pet doing damage, you often when talking about the mage class say how their pet are the cornerstone of the class. Why is it that the primary ability of the class takes experience away when being used.

    Isn't the class balanced when USING the pet? Or is it balanced when NOT USING it? If it's the second then I understand the pet taking xp, totally justifieable that if the class is balanced without the pet, that when a pet is summoned it overpowers the class and they pay an xp penalty for it. If the class is balance with pet in mind then there are no reasons why it shoudl take xp. No, why because those are abilities that were used to balance the class, like pets for pet classes.

    It is wrong for a class to be penalized for using any of it's abilities. If you say that mage are overpowered because of their pets then this is an entirely different discussion in which you'll probably be alone in thinking so, as most people playing EQ would agree that mages are not overpowered, they are about just right. Sadly there is an xp penalty for them to be about just right.

    thanks.

    Actually, I am most certainly not looking at the pet and the caster as two separate entities. I'm looking at the pet and seeing that it might be as valuable as a single character, and then adding on the abilities of the caster.

    What I've been seeing a lot of is comments like: "Without the pet, a mage is a just a weak wizard." And that might be true, but in no case am I considering just the mage any more than I am considering just the pet.

    I sort of covered the "…balanced when NOT USING…" the pet thing above. And, while it is true to say that it is unfair to penalize a class for using its abilities if without that penalty there is a balance, it's also not fair to ignore the balancing ability of those penalties. There is a penalty for using spells like Lich, for example. The penalty for the mana gain is the hit point loss. That's always part of any balancing consideration.

    -Aldur- wrote:

    Whilst it is fair to say that a magician who outdamages a melee is doing more work in the group, this is not the whole picture.

    Firstly the magician epic is one of the true power upgrades that any caster gets. A magician with an epic pet will deal more damage with their pet than one without, baring missing some critical pet haste spells, that the magician really should have.

    In this way it is more a question of equipment. A level 60 rogue with Sleepers weapons does not recieve or take more experience than a rogue with very little other than their epic, yet the total damage output will be different to the extreme.

    This is a good point. When it comes to the epic pet it might not be fair to consider it's powerful abilities in any other fashion than one would consider any other class with its epic. The fact that it produces a pet means that it inherits the drawbacks for being a pet. But we should also be keep in mind that the mage epic might be such a powerful pet just because it also has the potential to have the experience drawback.

    Also levels is not taken into account. A level 60 magician joins a slightly unbalanced group whereby the melees are level 55. The magician's pet is expected to do a higher proportion of damage in respect to level 55 people than it would to similar level people. This covers the pet doing greater damage, there is no need for a double penalty.

    I'm not sure this is relevant. If a pet's power is balanced by it's sometimes taking experience, then that shouldn't change based on the level of the party.

    Until experience awarded is proportionate to the damage dealt by individual members (massively unfair to priests and enchanters), this is not a good way of doing things. Magician's and necromancers are damage dealers, their aim in groups for the most part is to do as much damage as possibly, giving them or the group an exp penalty when they achieve this aim is unfair.

    Imagine this, if you introduced rules that stated that stated that damage output had to be in the way VI demanded, rogues had to out damage monks who outdamaged warriors who outdamaged rangers, who out damaged SK's who outdamaged paladins. If one class damage output was higher than it should be there was an exp penalty, because clearly the gear they have makes them overpowered with respect to the group they are in.

    Again, I'm not sure this is a valid situation. For pet classes this potential experience thing was figured in.

    This is not good. If the magicians and necro pets are doing too much damage, then their power alone needs adjustment, the exp penalty for pets should be removed.

    I can agree with that. IF they are doing too much damage…

    Let me stress again that I'm just looking for discussion here. I'm posing questions and ideas to hear what you have to say about them. I'm not telling you that anything will change or that it won't. I just wanted to get feedback and discuss this topic. Thank you for taking the time to discuss this with me. And keep sending in comments.

    Alan

    Well now that Cazic Thule has all of a sudden become a 50+ dungeon I have a couple questions. Where do I go now for the mid 30's level quest armor for shadowknights. Mage Q&A Follow-up
- Aurum @ 7:21 PM PST
Alan made this follow-up to the lengthy Mage Q&A posted earlier this week. The complete post can be read here:

    I wanted to talk a bit about the idea of pets taking experience in groups. I'm talking as Alan at this point, not so much so as a member of the EverQuest team, so I'll be presenting my own arguments. I'd like to hear comments about what I have to say.

    Obviously several players of pet classes have sent me comments about the idea that pets can take experience. A sample would be:

    "Followup) Right here, we have a break in vision between design and practice. These two questions are lumped together in order to discuss them both at once. For myself, a level 56 Magician, it is trivial for me to evade this 50% penalty for the pet's "hard work" by simply destroying my pet, wasting 1 malachite, and finishing the mob off myself while it runs. This has not drastically improved my soloing ability. What it has done is permitted me to consider 'light blue' mobs as viable targets for experience, just like every other class in Norrath can do. With my pet chewing up half my experience for light blue, I must either outdamage my pet via spells (costly), chain cast pets (costly in Malachite), or simply to not use my pets up until the finishing blow.

    As for a pet ever taking experience in a group, this concept reeks of the same problems socially as the Hybrid experience penalties did. Exactally what reason would a group have for selecting a Magician over a rogue, monk, other melee, wizard, or any other class that would not suck experience away from the group as a whole? Because it makes it easier to fight? It can be said that a well balanced group will be able to outdamage a pet. However, consider that higher in levels, and at it's peak the Epic Pet, these pets become harder to outdamage if they're used properly. It would simply break my heart to hear "Get the fire pet out, it doesn't do as much damage as your rogue pet". By stating and verifying that This Is So for experience penalties, you condem all Pet classes to go Petless, something that at least for Magicians and Beastlords would go against their very design.

    Rhasin Firestorm"

    I cut this out of your response, Rhasin. I'm sorry about not posting the whole thing, but I wanted to focus on this particular issue.

    Your first paragraph talks about how some folks find it better to work around the experience issue by various methods. While we really don't want to create game mechanics that players find a desire to 'work around' there is some value to this. First, since this is part is about soloing, even with the potential experience loss, mages are not the worst at soloing. So it could easily be argued that removing this penalty from soloing pet casters would simply improve them as soloers, something they already do fairly well.

    But folks using work around methods are, basically, being less efficient when it comes to use of mana. Recasting pets and nuking to do more damage than the pet are mana expensive techniques when compared to keeping one buffed up pet and letting it do most of the work. This means more work for the pet caster and more downtime for more experience. While I'm certainly not going try to convince you that work arounds are good things, these seem to be balanced with the way things work. More effort, more time, more experience (no greater risk).

    As for groups, this is where it's less complicated.

    If a pet is doing the most damage in a group, then there is an issue. If the group has a melee class, say a monk or a warrior (or even a rogue), and your pet is doing more damage than such a melee character, then that pet alone is of as much or more value to the group than any other member. Add to that a mage's casting power and you are looking at a single character providing more value to a group than any other single character could. If this happens a lot and if in a lot of groups the pet ends up outdamaging the melees, then I would begin to wonder that the class in question was overpowered, being able to deal out melee damage above those of actual melee classes as well as having spells.

    If the pet is not outdamaging the melees of your group, then there is no problem here.

    But if it is, then there is a serious balance issue. The thing that counters the power of that pet class in that case is the experience going to pets.

    The only time that I see this as a legitimate issue for groups is when there is no melee class in the group at all. In that case it is the pet that really makes the group viable (unless they are doing something like an AoE group without a tank, in which case the pet won't be there anyway). So in that case I'm not sure that it's unfair to allow the pet to suck up the experience, it's replacing a melee and makes the group viable.

    Ok, this is getting confusing. Let me try a different approach, see if it's any more comprehensible.

     If pets are able to deal more damage and are more valuable than (or as valuable as) a melee class, then the experience cost is justified in all cases.
     If pets are doing less damage than the damage dealers in a group, then they won't be taking more than 1% (though it should probably just be 0%) and there is no issue
     If pets are less valuable than a melee and is in a group with no melee, then that group is using the pet to replace a melee character. In this case it might not be fair that the pet takes a share of the experience, but it might be.
     The classes that use pets in their soloing as the primary damage dealing tool are at least average or better soloers as things are now (with the possible exception of Beastlords, who I'm not real sure about right now). This would indicate that the penalty is fine as it is.
     The 'practice' vs 'design' thing is a valid issue, and is something that I'm not real fond of. But on its own isn't a reason ignore the arguments above.

    I'd be interested to hear discussion about those points (civil discussion…). As I said, these are just my thought processes on this issue. I'm curious to see where I'm making errors, if any.

    Oh, and I did indeed mean the line of spells for Mages not the Shaman spell of the same name… Malo is just the short name I use for them (mostly just a shortening of the Malosi/Malosini spells). I apologize for using short hand, I shouldn't do that here.

    Alan

Small Developer's Update: Mage Q&A
- Aurum @ 8:07 PM PST
Alan only bothered to make one more update to the Developer's Corner this evening. As you can tell by the size, it's not much from him, but we suppose it'll have to do. You may commence reading as soon as you get your bifocals lenses:

    I recently responded to an email list of questions from the folks at the Mage Compendium. I wanted to copy it here. Some of my comments may seem out of context because it's basically the last response in an email conversation.

    ---

    First, let me mention that I was looking at an old list of questions before. Ronaldor pointed me at the correct list. Thanks Ron. :)

    Unfortunately, this list is a heck of a lot longer. It also contains a lot of questions that are opinion based rather than fact based. Right now our feeling is that casters need some work, and that includes mages. But I don't have any hard numbers, solid figures or anything like that to present about, for example, how warriors and magicians compare for damage output. Heck, I'm not even real sure if such a comparison is a fair one, but I'll leave that to the systems expert. Answers to questions like that will have to wait until the balancing stuff is done.

    So what you're getting here is opinion mixed with fact. I know that might be confusing, so I'll try to be as clear about what is my opinion and what I know as fact. I apologize for not being able to answer all of the questions. With such a long list I decided I'd rather answer most of the questions now and get back to you on the rest.

    Questions For The Magician Class

    General

    1) All classes in EverQuest have a role that grows with them as they progress upwards in levels. A different role for each different class, some healers, some melee damage dealers, some magic damage dealers. What is the intended role for the Magician Class to fill, in relation to other classes like Warriors, Wizards, or Monks? –Tuni Gearspinner

    A} Magicians are about pets and blasting things, along with a few debuffs and damage shields. Of course they also summon a bunch of stuff. But this question seems to be about combat potential. Pets allow a mage to deal melee damage and tie up an enemy while regaining mana, a sort of living (kinda} melee DoT. So I suppose I see their 'role' when it comes to combat as both melee and magic damage dealers with some utility. I get the feeling that I'm missing the point of this question, since that answer seems pretty obvious.

    2) Do you feel Magicians are fulfilling this role well in comparison to other classes filling their roles at present? –Tuni Gearspinner

    A} Obviously we feel that there are some adjustments to be made. I understand the problems post 50, for a lot of casters. And I understand how twinking items affects, or rather doesn't affect, casters. The team understands it too. You'll just have to give us some time to work on it.

    3) Would you say our role defined by Question 1 has changed for Levels 50 – 60? -Ronaldor Vladimir

    A} In practice, yes it has. I'm not adverse to roles changing over the growth of a class. Shamans, as a simple example, start out beating up on things with clubs and sticks and pointy things at low levels, but lose that as they grow. At low levels a druid is nearly as effective at healing as a cleric, but that too changes as they advance. I'm not so much worried about a change in roles. I am, however, worried that mages suffer from a lack of a role at levels post 50. That's one of the things that will be looked at as caster balancing moves forward.

    4) Where exactly do we stand compared to Warriors, in a typical high-end dungeon encounter in terms of damage output? -Euriped Moped

    A} We'll find that out as the balance process continues. Right now, I don't know. But I would warn you about such a comparison. It's certainly of some value, but conclusions shouldn't be drawn without considering all the factors. Also, we're talking about different kinds of damage. Against different NPCs and/or with different equipment any such comparison will be different.

    5) Does Verant truly believe we are still overpowered after three expansions, especially from level 50 upwards? -Biggerin Allahya

    A} Not really (that's my opinion, but I'm pretty sure most folks on the team would agree). Again, we're going to have to determine things like that in a more quantitative way than just "believe". Personally, I think magicians are pretty much in line up to about 50. After that, if a few issues could be worked out, I think they'd be right back in line. But don't discount the value of being able to solo pretty easily in the early levels. Such ability doesn't define a class, nor does it mean that a class should be underpowered later. But it has value as well.

    6) Does anyone on the EverQuest live team play a Magician as his or her main character? -Kalryn Mavric

    A} Yes.

    7) Was chain casting pets intended to be a primary ability of the magician, specifically 50+? –Zebanar

    A} Intended? Probably not during the design of the class, though three or four years later that's hard to tell. Is it a strategy that we are aware of now? Sure. Primary ability? In so much as pets are a large part of what Mages do, yes. THE primary ability? Probably not.

    8) Given the increased competition in the MMORPG market, what specific measures, if any, does Verant plan to implement that will improve the quality of their software development process? -Razamatazz Pizzazz

    A} We have made many internal changes to our development process over the past few months based on our Luclin experience. We will let our customers judge as to their effectiveness by the quality of adventures in Norrath over the years to come.

    9) What is Verant’s view of Mages? Are we perceived as unrepentant whiners or are we hard-core players that have valid concerns about our class, or something in between? –Faustian

    A} Seriously? :) There are two answers to this question. The simple company platitude, assuring you that we value the opinion of all our customers and that none of them are considered whiners in any way. The other answer is to just point out how silly it is for you to ask a question like this when you know there is only one answer that I'm going to give you.

    People are people. I don't look at all the players of one character class as anything at all. I look at individuals and deal with them that way.

    10) Do you feel magicians are balanced against other player classes in a player versus player environment? –Aedwen

    A} We readily admit that EverQuest is not geared to be a PvP game. When we talk about 'class balance' we are going to be primarily talking about PvE EverQuest. That being said, we know there are some bugs that cause problems in PvP for Mages, such as the whole issue with charming pets. Barring those bugs, and assuming that we do a good job balancing the casters, then the answer would be yes. Yes, they are as balanced as any other class in PvP, with advantages against some folks and weaknesses against others. But I'm certainly not an expert on PvP.

    11) Will any of those monsters, which I could quite easily suggest as being summoned, be changed to summoned monsters as there are not actually that many summoned monsters in the game? E.g. Golems, Goo, Rockfiend, just to name but a few. –Runemaker

    A} Golems are created, not summoned. Goo… who the heck knows where Goo comes from. Rockfiends are natural creatures (meaning that they don't come from somewhere else). We may make some adjustments to certain NPCs at times to make them 'summoned' or not, and since Norrath is a fantasy world it would be just as easy to say that Goo is indeed summoned from someplace. It's also just as possible to say that some elementals are summoned and some are natural. In the end it really has to be decided by the designer of a given zone whether or not certain NPCs will be affected by summoned-specific spells or items. We'd certainly look over any suggestions for changes, as long as those suggestions take into account the overall balance of the game and zone.

    12) Since melees have enjoyed several upgrades, and everyone has been able to gain 10 more levels since the 66% damage spell cap was introduced pre Kunark, are there any plans to remove the spell/pet damage cap in the player versus player environment? –Mavermage

    A} Not that I know of. And we're not really going to be able to look at how casters match up in PvP until after we've balanced them for PvE. But we've certainly talked a little bit about the 66% thing. We're not inclined to remove it, it has value. But we might alter it in some way to make it more functional. I don't have any more details since we've just been tossing the idea around a bit.

    13) Because Magicians lack defensive spells e.g. root, snare, or blind and the ability to escape or avoid combat when we don’t have a pet present, should we not be more adept offensively especially from level 50 upwards?

    A} Magicians lack defensive skills as part of their class design. "More adept offensively" compared to Wizards? Compared to Enchanters? Compared to Magicians as they are now? We'll look all that over when we review caster balance. But just because a class doesn't have root doesn't, on it's own, mean that the class should have some sort of extraordinary damage dealing ability.

    Pets

    1) Is there any chance that in the future the experience penalty for a pet doing over 50% of the damage while soloing to any monster will be removed? -Ghalon Soulburn

    A} There's always a chance. Again, we'll look at that as well when we review casters. However, remember that whatever 'soloing ability' Mages have now (whether considered great or average) would be dramatically increased if pets suddenly stopped earning experience for all of their hard work and sacrifice.

    2) How much experience does a pet take from a group is it still 1% on average (Various player tests have indicted that a pet takes the same amount of experience as a player)? –Ascii

    A} A pet will take a fair share of the experience (as if it was an additional party member) IF the pet does more damage than all other group members (individually). If any party member outdamages the pet, the pet only takes 1% of the experience.

    3) Will the new pet animations for the new pet graphics be fixed/added in the near future? –Pitounet

    A} The new pet animations will be added. Right now, though, we don't have a timeframe this.

    4) When will /target once again target the pet and not the pet's master? –Toem

    A} We got this one already. - Pets can now be targeted by typing their full name after the /target command (or at least enough of it to distinguish it from the owner)

    5) Will a pet health bar ever be added either to the group display or the individual character display? –Ronaldor Vladimir

    A} It might. It's not something we're averse to doing at all. But you certainly won't see it before you see the new interface. There's no point in adding it to the old one.

    6) Will chat filters ever be added for pets so that we may turn off melee battle spam, but sill be able to see our pet’s melee damage and spell effects? –Kalamos

    A} More filters is something we want to do for several cases where they are needed. Again, this will probably have to wait until after the new interface is done.

    7) As a magician, my pet is my lifeblood. It is what sets me apart from being a poor man’s Wizard. Does Verant believe our pets (Particularly 50+, and our Epic pets) are overpowered, and if so in what situations (i.e. experience grouping, raiding or soloing)? -Silvasa T`Karr

    A} We're looking at that right now. Reviewing pets is part of the process. I could give my opinion, but it might vary from the opinion of the guy sitting next to me, whose opinion might be different than the next guy. Instead, I'll wait and see what kind of results come out of the review.

    8) Will a pet equip armour, jewellery and weapons that are not NODROP and receive the statistical benefits they offer? –Siadhail

    A} A good rule is this: If it will equip a weapon it will probably equip most items, if that item is better than one it may already have. Better in the pet's mind might not match what the owner thinks is better, and the value of some items to pets is questionable (giving your pet + Int items won't make the pet choose better targets…). Also keep in mind that the value that your pet gains from the use of a piece of armor probably won't be the same value that a PC would gain from it. Skill levels have a lot to do with how a character absorbs or avoids damage.

    9) Why can pets no longer taunt a monster off of any player character that has aggravated a monster (No matter how slightly) that is within the monsters melee range? -Nandail Mandragoran

    A} I don't have an answer for this question at this time.

    10) Currently there is a player versus player bug that allows Bards and Enchanters to destroy player pets at will through the use of Charm will this be addressed? –Grumples

    A} Yes, it will. I don't know when.

    11) Will the Magician epic quest ever have Quillmane changed to a triggered spawn, or have a similar monster added to Southern Karana that can be triggered? –Ronaldor Vladimir

    A} We don't have any plans to change that part of the quest. My suggestion, at least for younger magicians, would be to get the cloak when you're of a level to gain experience in the zone. Play there and have fun, snag Quillmane while you're there.

    12) What is the difference between "/pet follow" and "/pet guard me”, they appear to work the same? –The Dweller In The Darkness

    A} No difference at all, really. There may have been a time when "/pet guard me" would cause the pet to attack anything that came near the pet owner, but now both commands do the same thing.

    13) Do you have any intention on improving current pet control mechanisms in the near future, and/or adding new controls and /pet commands to pet control, if so what do you have in mind? –Noonie Vilotskies

    A} Not really. The current control commands fall within the range of effective but not too effective. Remember, part of the value of a live player over a pet is that the player is smarter (though maybe not as easily controlled…).

    14) Is there a code imposed limit on the number of pets that can be actively engaging a single monster, and if so what is that limit? –Istaron

    A} Yes. No more than four pets can engage a single target. This is basically to prevent use of only pets to defeat opponents. We think it's important to have some player characters involved in the combat at times. :)

    15) Why has pet offensive and defensive abilities not been scaled up with the expansions to meet the needs of the new encounters, especially from level 51 upwards? –Eandori

    A} See question #7 about pets. This is a repeat (with a little more spin on it…).

    16) Is there any plan to try and improve pet pathing in the future, if so what is it? –Kysis Ka Tra

    A} "Pet pathing" isn't something that we can just flip a switch and make better. It's a complicated system. We make improvements where we can.

    17) Has the epic pet been altered in any way since the Magician Epic quest went live, and if so in what way? –Ronaldor Vladimir

    A} Not that I know of. I suppose it would help if you told me what you think had been changed.

    18) Why are pets taking so long to buff themselves? –Halves

    A} There are a LOT of pets in EverQuest. Pets casting buffs use up bandwidth and CPU cycles. Zones with a lot of pets could be adversely affected by all the pets casting stuff. We increased the time that all pets will take between buffs to reduce resources used.

    19) Now that pets are capable of zoning with their masters why don’t they? - Fadzen Silverthorn

    A} Some do, some don't. The familiar is, essentially, a buff with a visual representation. The warder zones, but it loses its buffs when it does so. In reality all pets need to be 'recast' when the owner zones. The warder gets all tiny when he zones and the buffs needed to bring him back to speed are very much in line with a Mage recasting his pet. Basically, we decided not to dramatically change that part of gameplay.

    20) Are there any plans for removing random generated pet names, and instead displaying them similar to how Beastlord pets are “Character's pet/elemental”? –Noonie Vilotskies

    A} We've encountered nothing but trouble once we started naming familiars and warders after their owners. It sorta messes with things that refer to entities by name. Right now I don't know of any plans to change the way other pets are named.

    21) The Monster Summoning line of spells is in effect no different to our normal pets except in the fact they look different, will they ever be changed to behave more like the monsters they actually are? -Zebanar

    A} We don't have any plans to change Monster Summoning. And if by "behave more like the monsters they actually are" you mean "do a ton more damage and have a ton more hit points", then the answer is no. Changes to these pets might happen during the balancing process, but any such changes would probably be small when compared to "the monsters they actually are".

    Spells

    1) My question is does Verant believe our current summoned item line to be as useful as it should be compared to other class’s utility spells? -Aammelno

    A} Well, that depends on the utility spell in question. Spirit of Wolf? Probably not. Ultravision? Sure. True North? Absolutely! :) We'll certainly be looking at this as well during the balancing pass.

    2) Are there any plans to change Call of The Hero so that it will function in all zones without trivialising the game encounters, if so what are your current ideas to achieve this? –Qalenie

    A} We've fiddled around with the restrictions in a few zones since this question was written. I'm sure this too will be examined when looking at caster balance, but right now I know of no plans to change the way Call of the Hero works.

    3) What line of spells are our "class defining spells"? –The Dweller In The Darkness

    A} Pets, bolts, summoned items, Malo… There are probably those that use damage shields a lot and might call them defining… I would avoid getting hung up on the whole idea of "class defining".

    4) Why do Druids receive spells vs summoned beings before we do in terms of levels? –Ronaldor Vladimir

    A} Because Druids despise the unnatural and Mages work with the unnatural.

    5) Will we see the Mage class receive more elemental DD spells like ice, lightning? We should have control of elemental spells as magicians are Elementalists. –Ielen

    A} Unlikely. It's a good idea to avoid using labels like Elementalist when talking about class balance. And besides, Elementalist could mean just about anything. I see it as "controller of elemental beings." Wide spread damage types is pretty much the purview of the wizard. But I'm not going to rule it out completely. Who knows what solutions will come from the balancing.

    6) Why can a level 49 Mage cast for 810 damage (72% as good as Wizard’s damage at level 49) but a level 60 mage can only cast for 1005 damage (48% as good as Wizard’s damage at level 60)? –Eandori

    A} Another thing to be reviewed when balancing casters. But it's best to avoid comparisons like this. You're not including all of the factors that make up these spells like casting time, recast time, type of damage, mana cost, range… the whole thing. Just tossing out damage numbers isn't enough. We'll take a more in-depth look at this sort of thing and make the adjustments we think are needed.

    7) Why is there a 6 level limit for what a caster can land spells on, but no apparent cap exists for melee? –Eandori

    A} There isn't really a six level limit. Here's how it works: There is a level limit for spell effects that has a minimum of six levels. A spell will not affect an NPC that is more than 1.3 times your level, or 6 levels higher, which ever is greater. So at 15th level it's 6 levels. At 40th it's 12 levels. Caster Balance Update
- Aurum @ 4:31 PM PST
Alan brought us this big update on the caster balancing issue started a few weeks ago:

    Most of you probably remember the melee balancing changes we made last year. We'll I have some information for you from the fellow that spearheaded that project, and is leading the caster balancing work.


    quote:
    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Caster Balance

    It's been clear for awhile now that priests and casters haven't been as well balanced as we'd all like them to be. Balance is a delicate issue and any major changes to overall class power are always researched, debated, and taken slowly. There are some really big improvements in the works now for helping out with that balance, and we expect those to be on the Test server in the near future. These changes won't all come in one huge patch, but will show up a bit at a time until casting classes are right where they should be in relative power.

    We are taking the needs and issues of casters seriously, and we think you'll be very happy with the results you see after we finish our caster balance effort. We hope you'll be patient just a bit longer, while we take the time to get everything implemented, balanced, and tested.


    Ancient Spells

    One specific area we are addressing for casting classes revolves around how much improvement they get from raiding and acquiring high-level items. When a melee class begins to acquire items from the hardest monsters, they experience a tremendous growth in power. A melee character sporting Vulak weapons and Temple of Veeshan loot is incredibly more powerful than player who only owns average quality items. Casting classes don't achieve nearly the same amount of increased power from raid items, and rapidly fall behind melees in relative power when they start raiding. It doesn't make sense for a wizard who has helped in slaying hundreds of dragons to be using the same spells as someone who has yet to take part in raid encounters.

    To address this problem, we've introduced the new "Ancient" category of spells recently. Ancient spells are rare, high-quality spells that can only be obtained from the most powerful monsters. These spells are improved versions of spells already available to each casting class. They won't give casters any brand-new, or out-of-class abilities, but are intended to make the casters better at what they already do. Much like a warrior improves himself by getting a better sword from a dragon, a wizard can now acquire more efficient spells in the same way.

    As you might expect, Ancient spells are going to be rare and take a certain amount of effort to obtain. We don't expect many casters to ever get all the Ancient spells for their class. This is a change from the traditional approach we've had to spells, which has allowed every caster to feel entitled to get every one of their spells. We'll continue to make core spells available to casting classes off of vendors, or as relatively frequent drops off of monsters, but we want to be clear that Ancient spells are rare and powerful items of treasure, and we intend them to be very hard to get.

    Only the first set of Ancient spells have been added to the game. In the coming weeks, we'll continue to add more Ancient spells to the game and expand the selection for casters and priests. It takes some time to get the spells balanced, tested, and placed on monsters - so expect to see them coming in a few sets at a time.

    One last thing to note about Ancient spells- the power of these spells will vary depending on what monster drops them. You can expect Vulak A'err to have more powerful Ancient spells than you might find on Trakanon, for instance.

    Rich Waters
    Lead Designer, Systems
    Sony Online Entertainment



    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------


    Thanks for the update, Rich.
