Client UI Discussion - Long
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 8:10 PM PDT
Rowyl, one of the EQ Live team's Senior Programmers, started a discussion thread on the Gameplay Board about EverQuest's User Interace (also known as UI). Here's post #1 in this long thread:

    My name is Roy, and I am a Senior Programmer on the EQ Live team. My main focus for now is the client program including the User Interface.

    Some of the first things I've done are now live and I've seen the many reactions to them. I've been reading the boards and even implemented some of the suggestions already (they will go onto test tonight or tomorrow).

    Be aware that while I am reading the boards, I can not respond to everything. I will read what I can, and respond when I can. If I were to spend the time needed to reply to everything, then I would not have any time to code the changes and fixes needed. My boss would not be too happy with me, if that were the case...

    Hopefully, we can work together to make the UI as good as it can be.
Here's post #2:
    Albrecht_Hammerfell: having more then 8 spells from your spell book available to cast at any one time is NOT part of EQ

    -ArchAngelis-: Log control has been added to the eqclient.ini file already, should be on test soon.

    Others: as has been said, I am primarily working on the UI, I won't be addressing issues such as class balance and problems with quests or items.

    -Rowyl
Here's post #3:
    Using activatable items from the hotbutton panel is in and should be on test. I'm looking into why the hotkeys don't operate from a keypress while in the spell book.
    ---
    Looking into the Ctrl-1 thru Ctrl-8 for memed spells.
    ---
    The main issue with selecting party members pets with the F keys is that the client doesn't currently track the info. I will look into it and see if this can be done. As usual, no promises.
    ---
    Looking into the shift click while buying to buy stacks of 20. One thing to keep in mind, if you don't have enough money for all 20 it will just cancel the sale. This will only work on stackable items.
    ---
    [Quote from original post: 1. When emoting, the text of the emote YOU typed used to be blue. But when others saw it, it was white. Similarly, when you saw other's emotes, they were white. Now all emotes are the same color.]
    I had to make "classes" of text, and in order to keep it from being a separate color control entry in the ini file
    for every text message printed (meaning hundreds of entries) I had to group stuff together. I thought it made sense
    to put all emotes into a class together. This reason applies to alot of the messages.
    [Quote from original post: 2. With my color User_11 RGB as specified in Gordon's file, full red only, the text is still WHITE on messages where others are hitting me. This is probably just a bug.
    3. Wasn't the duel color (User_14) yellow?
    Some of the messages are sent from the server with a color tag, I have already fixed most of these and it should be on test.
    4. Was all spell activity really dark blue? I must be losing my marbles, that seems wrong.]

    See my responce to 1 above.
    [Quote from original post: 5. When you do a /who friend all, your friends list comes back white, rather than the color of all other friends queries (again, likely just a bug).]
    The "local" /who command is handled by the client, the "/who all" commands are all handled by the world server. Those will be colored properly soon.
Here's post #4:
    Just a note, hotkeying a backback slot is just not possible.

    -Rowyl
Here's post #5:
    Couple things I wanted to address:

    Cancelling a spell you already cast and having to wait until the casting time is done is part of the game. If you click the wrong spell you have to wait...

    I doubt I will be able to increase the number of hotbuttons per page above 6 pages of 6 buttons. It would change the player data format stored ont he server, and that could cause serious problems with character data. I might be able to make it 4 pages of 9 (still having a total of 36 buttons, just more available at a time). I am still investigating this one.. I'll let you know what comes up.

    Several of the other things asked for here are either in or going in this week. Like a /cast 1-8 for casting memmed spells, /book # to open your book on page #, /sit, /doability # (so if you have tracking set on ability button 1, then /doability 1 would invoke tracking), more control of certain text message colors, the spell and target being mentioned in the "casting" message, custom emotes that include the target name (/em gives @ the look of death and @ is frozen in his tracks would replace the '@' with the current target), and more... some of these are on test already, and some will be there later this week.

    -Rowyl
Here's post #6:
    Just to reply to the people that didn't like my reply about stopping spells.

    It's part of the game. If you click the wrong spell, it's a penalty that you have to wait thru. I'm sorry that it's not the way you would like it, but there have to be drawbacks to making mistakes. And I realize that sometimes it's not a mistake so much as the situation changing after you cast.

    I play a caster, and I would like to make this change, also, but it's just something I have control over. The designers of the game want it this way.

    -Rowyl
Here post #7:
    I am familiar with ShowEQ, and how it does it's pet stuff. The pet does have the ID of it's owner in it's packet, but player packets down't have the ID of their pet. What needs to be done is that the client needs to search the current mob database for any pets that belong to people in the current party. For your own pet it does this when you create it. The client also needs to keep updating this info as your party changes and as your party members create and destroy pets (either via pet spells or charm).

    Again, ShowEQ is just going thru it's entire database and when if find s a pet it sticks it under the pets owner, but as I've explained above it's not the same as tracking the pets for the functionality asked for in this thread. That said, I still plan to look into this feature because I think it would be useful.

    -Rowyl
Here's post #8:
    Regarding the text box in higher resolutions.

    One of the other programmers here is working on that right now. It's not as simple as one might think at first glance because users can and do switch modes back and forth (sometimes they are forced to by the game itself like for inventory mode), and thus the text needs to be "re-wrapped" for the narrower box when in that mode, and then expanded back out when you return to the higher res fullview. It's almost working though and should be on test later this week.

    -Rowyl

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 7:56 PM PDT
Gordon has kept busy on the Gameplay Board this evening with several new posts made to address several issues. Here's the scoop:

  • Gordon "Again" Mistaken?
    [Quote from original post: b) Random loot table. With this system any mob at a given level range has a slight chance of droping a rare item for a given level range. You've shown you can do this somewhat. Level 1-7 mobs tend to drop rusties while 8-19 drop bronze and 20+ drop Fine Steel. So instead of camping X mob for X amount of time you "do" dungeons in the traditional fasion known as dungeon crawling. This would incourage more social interaction because caster's would get melee only items and melee would get caster only items. Thus you have a working trade system. How rare something actually is remains a unknown until a lot of the same item goes into circulation. Supply and demand will determine the trade price.]

    Loot tables are already somewhat random (or contextually random) for some good loot in Kunark, however, random loot for special quests just makes the quest meaningless. Quests have a story behind them, and that story is based off of who drops what, who give what info, and who fulfills the quest. It would be quite disappointing for a quest to just send someone in to randomly slay creatures in a particular area.

    People camp quest items because they want the reward from the quest, and whatever it takes to be assured of getting that reward is worth the effort. Increasing the availability of that reward only reduces the amount that people want it. Camping only decreases in this case because people who want the item are then more likely to have it.

    [Quote from original post: Gordon your sadly mistaken. ]

    Is it just me or do a lot of posts here begin with this, or with "You've missed the point"?

  • Frustrated Warrior Vents
    [Quote from original post: Why don't you start a group? It's a startling concept and one that most people never catch on to. I've been in zones where you'll see a wizard, shaman, warrior, and druid all yell at the same time for a group for like an hour and never once consider joining up to make their own group. It's pretty amusing when I see that. Just make one, it isn't that hard.]

    I've seen this several times myself. It's one of the things that I hope the LFG flag will address. If there's a bunch of people standing around in a zone looking for a group, existing groups should be able to see them and pick them up quickly, or they can form amongst themselves.

  • UI Fixes
    [Quote from original post: At the online game company I worked for (not verant), we experienced much the same thing. We had all the staffers working our heinies off to get the big stuff done, and it never seemed to end, with something else "big" always popping up that we'd have to deal with. But eventually we had it all in place, all the big stuff was working, and we could FINALLY move on to the secondary things we'd been shelving.

    Looks like Verant has recently hit that same point. They have really changed gears in the last few weeks in particular, and are doing a lot of smaller things that I'm sure they were forced to put on the back burners. Remember the patch where they fixed a bunch of typos? That's a very low priority task. Or the text color changes in the latest patch? Again, low priority.

    The fact that they are addressing low priority fixes (in spades, I might add) means that they are finally over the hump and the high priority project list has finally stopped increasing on a daily basis. ]

    That's a very good description, so good in fact that I'm not sure what I can add to it. It is very nice to be able to go back and start addressing that "would be nice" list that has been building for some time. They often give good bang for the buck, but are often hard to justify when you have a lot of high priority things on the docket.

    I'm glad that we're able to start working on some of the wonderful ideas that have been on the back burner for some time.

  • Using Different Spells for Different Creatures
    [Quote from original post: Well, some creatures may have a lower resist to a certain spell type. So that is your reason to use certain spells over another.]

    Bingo. Thanks Fal.

  • Naming Pets
    [Quote from original post: Just as a minor touch to the pet classes, could you implement a command that lets you change the name of the pet? Might be kinda fun to do. :-)]

    We couldn't allow people to choose the name of their own pet, as it would increase the number of naming issues by an order of magnitude. Also, changing the name of a pet after summoning isn't an option, due to the way that the zone keeps track of NPCs. The only possible change that we could even look into would be for people to choose an NPC name from a list of acceptable names, causing future pets to be named in that fashion, however something like this isn't likely to be prioritized anytime soon.

  • Archery Bug/Nerf?
    The problem regarding being unable to shoot an arrow after casting a spell is unintentional. The dev team is working on the issue and it should be fixed soon. The problem is occurring because we just moved some components of archery from the client to the server. This will let us do some neat things that we weren't able to do before. For instance, we can now place randomly triggered effects upon bows and have them work.

  • Group Mana Bars
    We won't be displaying mana bars to everyone in the group. Hit point bars make sense as you should be able to tell by looking at someone how healthy they are. Mana, however, is another thing.

  • Request to Share Smithing Revamp Details
    If I do that, and then we miss it by a week or two, the net result would be people that people were upset. As it is, I'd prefer to let you know that we are working on it, and when we get it in, everyone who's interested should be pleased :)

  • Camping
    [Quote from original post: Abshi I dare you to come camp Glacier bear with me on your DAY OFF Come and see how fun it's to camp it... I'll bring the beer and nacho.. you bring yourself. Hell let's make it a GM even... first one to SEE the bear win a prize!]

    I'm sorry Microbe, but I don't camp. I prefer to play the game, have fun, and if I happen across anything rare, it's that much better. I think that if I were to go out specifically looking for something rare, I'd be setting myself up for disappointment.

  • Wife Balance Problem :)
    Thank you for reporting this balance issue. We'll get right on it.

  • No Shiverback Change?
    We have to look at everything available to the monk, not just his armor, when deciding whether or not a change is justified. Yes, I'll agree that some shiverback components are of lesser quality than some other non-planar items that are available, however, even so, the monk is as solid defensively as we want them to be. While one could argue that we should increase shiverback to a level that it's more powerful than the mundane stuff, one could also argue that we should lower the power of the mundane stuff and keep shiverback as it is. We're currently running defensive tests of all the melee classes to ensure that they end up in the list where we feel that they should fall.

    The indication from the last tuning meeting was that a monk in full shiverback had enough defensive power as they should.

  • You Have Been Disconnected
    [Quote from original post: Why cant it be made so that when I make water and I make food it will stay on my character for a 45 minutes or 30 is that so much to ask. I don't exactly like spending 10 minutes making food and water everytime I go LD and its even more irritating when your in a group fighting and you have to create food for yourself why they are in need of help]

    Summon food and items are intentionally less-reliable than "real" items of the same nature. It's one of the balancing factors for having them there to begin with. Now it was never the intention for people to lose them when accidently going LD, however LDs remain a part of life on the internet, and there's no way to determine the difference between an accidental and intentional LD. There was one time when we allowed people to keep summoned items for an LD, but had to change it when we noticed our LD logs increase exponentially for the purpose of keeping the summoned items.

    [Quote from original post: There ought to be like a 30 minute timer before pets and summoned items automatically poof. This was the most promising of the threads.]

    Unfortunately, this isn't possible, as characters aren't in the world 30 minutes after they disappear. As long as characters are "in the world", the servers have to keep track of what they are doing and what is happening to them. Once they are removed from the world, they then do not exist. We can't age a timer for someone who is not in the world.

  • GM Faction Issue
    [Quote from original post: After speaking to a very nice GM by the name of Valdelmar, I was told that they STILL would not fix it, unless I was a wizard trying to do the Ice Comet quest. I mentioned the posts on here, but he simply said that the Lead GM said he couldn't do it.]

    I've spoken to the GMs about this, and word will be spread that this faction needs to be reset. I apologize that you've been given the run-around. Please petition again when you get in game.

  • RP Server
    [Quote from original post: Actually, the FAQ seems to have quite a few loopholes in this area, focusing on enforcement and definition of roleplaying rather than what most of the RPers that post here are asking for... A label, nothing else]

    I've addressed the idea of a "label, nothing else" before. Unfortunately, it has been shown that without enforcement, some aspects of the player community would not respect the label.

    Take these boards as an example. Flames, name-calling, profanity, and general discourtesy has never been "allowed". In fact, rules prohibiting such conduct have always been part of the Station Terms of Service. However, since these rules were not enforced, it wasn't very long before the board was dominated by it's former problems. Only now, after we've expressly tied account status to the boards and enforced it by means of login restrictions have they started allowing for better discussions.

    The same issue exists with the idea of an unenforced "roleplay only" server, and since the idea of an enforced roleplay-only server isn't feasible, it's not something that we can do.

  • Long Q&A
    [Quote from original post: 1. Is is possible to have armor and weapons be right clicked and have them appear directly on your body, instead of having to drag them to your inventory picture. This would help elliviate some of the time it takes to loot your own corpse. Backpacks and other items can be right clicked directly into inventory slots, so why can't equipment be right clicked directly on?]

    No, it used to be like this in early beta, but was purposefully changed to it's current state. It simulates some time necessary to reequip items, rather than having it more or less instantaneous.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Will we ever be able to use the hot buttons while meditating? I had read that this was being worked ]on a while ago, but I wasn't sure if it was implemented. Also, will Hot buttons be able to be used on the Merchant Purchase screen as well (see the next question)??

    Maybe, it's on Roy's plate. There's no gameplay reason why we wouldn't allow it since we allow you to use them by clicking them with the mouse, so the only thing left is to see if there's some technical reason, and if not, to implement it.

    [Quote from original post: 3. Will we ever be able to purchase multiple items at once (food, water, and trade skill items)?]

    See #2 above.

    [Quote from original post: 4. Is there a way to have food that will give temporary boosts to Ability scores? I think it would be cool to eat a piece of Bread of Stamina, or Mana Muffins. Is this possible to add to the game, if it isn't already a part of the Cooking skill?]

    This is not in our current plans, though we like the idea and will keep it in mind.

    [Quote from original post: 5. Is there a way to increase the Hide/Sneak speed for Rogues??]

    Already in. Rogues above 50 sneak at progressivly faster speeds, until level 60 where they can sneak at a full run.

    [Quote from original post: 6. Why does sitting increase the aggro distance on enemies, especially green ones?]

    It really doesn't, however, sitting PCs are automatically added to the hate-list of any NPC in the awareness radius. Normally, an NPC that isn't mad at you, but would attack you anyway, becomes aware of you before they react to you. So, in other words, if the awareness radius is 200 ft, and the reaction radius is 100 ft, a wolf would attack a standing person once he crossed into reaction range. However, when the person is sitting, they are added to the hate list at the awareness range. The ranges themselves don't actually change.

    This is intended.

    [Quote from original post: 7. Why is it that Monsters have the ability to turn around immediately?]

    See the FAQ. NPCs have to be able to do things PCs can't to remain competitive.

    [Quote from original post: 8. Does anyone else think the monsters ability scores are unfairly high?]

    Same as #7

    [Quote from original post: 9. Where stunning creatures goes (like above with bash), how come players never know when they have actually stunned creatures?]

    There is a text message indicates this.

    [Quote from original post: Why do the monsters stun and sometimes cause cahracters to spin?]

    Because being stunned allows the player to make no further inputs for the duration of the stun. As such, any turn that you've begun, will continue while stunned. You'll never get spun if you are simply facing the creature. You only get spun if you are turning at the time you are stunned. I've always thought that this was a neat effect.

    [Quote from original post: 10. Why is it that monsters don't have to follow the same rules of hit detection that players have to conform to?? I cannot tell you the number of times I have seen monsters go through walls, monsters standing inside of one another, and monsters that pass right through players?]
    See #7.

    [Quote from original post: 11. I have noticed that small races sometimes have problems climbing onto ledges, when exiting the water from swimming. This is especially true for the Halas zone, where my Halfling has to try numerous times to get out of the water at both ledges. Often while trying to climb onto the ice near the Halas dock, I will fall (for 1 pt of dmg) under the ledge, and then be under the water in front of the ledge. Usually I have this happen four or five times to try and get up the ledge. Is this a known problem?]

    It's a known problem, and unfortunately cannot be fixed without man-months of work on each zone (basically rebuilding each one from scratch) followed by excessively long downloads.

    [Quote from original post: 12. The bugs involved with boat trips? I am sure I don't need to mention the numerous bugs associated with the boats, especially having to do with characters appearing to be swimming and sharks appearing ON the boat. Have these been looked at. A friend of mine said that he had seen a response that in order to fix these the whole zone would have to be redone. Is this true? Or, is there a remedy in the works?]

    Sharks appearing on the boats is not a bug, but a consequence of the design. As for other boat related issues, you'll have to list them one by one.

    [Quote from original post: 13. The zone doors in some zones are ridiculously small, and this can often cause people to "get stuck" while zoning, and cause them to be sent back through the zone the other way. This happens frequently in the West Karana/Qeynos Hills zone]

    This particular zone is on our plate to fix. No ETR.

    [Quote from original post: 14. Has anyone else noticed a lack of Guides/GMs during late night hours? I usually play from 1am EST till about 6am EST daily, and usually it is rare to see one Guide on at that time, if any at all.]

    We're always working to improve coverage in the off-hours. We just moved a bunch of GMs around to fill that area better. As for Guides, they are players just like yourselves. We take every opportunity to recruit off-hours guides and do the best we can in that area.

    [Quote from original post: 15. This question driectly relates to #14 above. I myself tried to appply to be a Guide using the re-designed form that was announced last week. I logged in and created a password for the guide program, and then began filling out the questionaire. I was about half way done filling it out, when because of pressing issues at work I decided to finish it later. I had never atually submitted the form, but when I tried to log in again and fill it out, it said I had already submitted a form. I would still like to try and fill it out again, but it continues to say that someone with my account name has already filled out a Guide application. I would hate to have to wait until the next form comes out some months from now to try and apply again. I also seem to remember this same problem with the last guide app I tried to submit.]

    When filling out the guide account, it only allows a station name to be entered once in order to prevent multiple applications submitted by the same person. What most likely happened in this case, is that the application was read as completed and submitted for grading.


    If a situation such as this occurs, the applicant may write appadmin@verant.com and supply the station name and email address used when filling out the application and ask that we delete it so they may resubmit. If they wish to wait, the application will go into the queue to be graded and will fail as incomplete and once the database is purged of failed applications they will be able to resubmit the application.

Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:28 PM PDT
Gordon got an early start on the boards today, so here's the latest quotes and excerpts from the Gameplay Board:

  • Twinking
    Our stance on twinking is covered in the FAQ at http://everquest.station.sony.com/forum_faq.html.

  • Shaman Group Buffs
    [Quote from original post: Why should shamans, the king of buffs, get the worse end of the deal when it comes to group buffs?]

    It makes sense for single-target buffs to be more effective than group buffs. It's one of the tradeoffs that are in the game. The group buffs have the benefit of affecting a larger number of people with one cast, and therefore less mana, while the single-target buffs have the benefit of being stronger.

  • Najena Revamped?
    [Quote from original post: There are definitely things happening on the Tarew Marr server. New items are dropping and rubicite (sp) is dropping as well from what i am told.]

    The revamp of Najena is still planned, but this is not it. This is a bit of a special event.

  • Druid Quest Armor
    [Quote from original post: Why is there no quest armor in the game , like warriors crafted, shamans totemic, cleric, sk rouge, pally, ranger, etc.]

    Check out the last section of the FAQ at It covers the "why isn't there...." questions fairly well.

    In short, there just isn't. There's no mandate anywhere here saying "There will be no mid-level druid class-specific armor". There might be quests/armor along those lines some day, just as there might be anything else in the game.

  • EverCamp
    [Quote from original post: So, I have to *SIT* and wait 7 hours for the spawn]

    You aren't required to "sit and wait" for the NPC. You are only required to be there when the NPC spawns. That can happen at any time, and you could choose to leave and come back later to try and complete the quest. Now, if the item is such that you *choose* to sit and wait, you've made the decision that the item is "worth it", which is your decision to make.

    [Quote from original post: From what I understand, most quests include a *long* camp to get an item. If you want to control how often the quest is done, just give it more requirements (20+ no drop item list).]

    More requirements do not limit the amount of items that come into the world. The proliferation of items in the world is tied only to the amount of time it takes to get the rarest item. Even if the quest had 20 one-hour steps, taking you at least 20 hours to complete it, the rate at which items from the quest can come into game is one/hour. The more items that come into the game, the less valuable the items, and the less prestige garnered from acquiring it.

  • Delaying Attacks & Haste
    We purposely don't show this information, as it's meant to be derived from experience with the various haste effects. One thing to remember is that items with "Effect: Haste" do not stack, so only the most potent haste will take effect. Haste spells do, however, stack with haste items.

  • PnP Protects Bad Players?
    I'm not sure why you say that it "protects" the people taking inappropriate actions. No part of the Play Nice Polices allow that it was that the person was doing. It is, however, true that these things must be witnessed, as we can't allow one person or group of people to adversely affect the account-status of another on word alone. As I'm sure you can imagine, there are people who would use that as an offensive tool if we allowed it.

    [Quote from original post: I turn to my left to see Balorf, running by with his train of literally 50 or more Monsters.]

    I'd like more information on this if possible. Code was put in a long time ago, before public release of the product, that keeps people from forming a train this long. That many NPCs are not supposed to pursue any one player-character. Once enough NPCs are chasing a player, the others will consider the situation "well in hand" and go back about thier business.

  • Is the Rain Gone?
    Rain can't turn on or off if you are looking at your spellbook at the time. I assume that this is probably what you are experiencing. The rain should stop within a few seconds after looking up from your book.

  • Concerning GMs
    [Quote from original post: There was a lvl limit to who could attack them, these GM's made it so only the lower level people could attack. They didn't kill many people,]

    We've recently implemented the ability for GMs to limit the level of PCs that can attack them when in an NPC form. Hopefully this change will allow us to perform more quests, events, raids, etc., for lower level characters without fear of a higher level one coming in and nuking the NPC.

  • "Wrath of Spoolie Gee"
    [Quote from original post: Is it the "Wrath of" part that was "bad"? or was it that we mentioned the name of a NPC in our guild name (spoolie gee is a NPC is east commons). Anybody know?]

    It would be the NPC. We don't allow guilds named after an NPC.

  • Vampe Embrace Castable on Others?
    [Quote from original post: The other day I was flipping through my spell book while medding in timorous and just happened to come across vamp embrace. I wondered to myself, now why the heck do we have such a useless spell, meleeing lvl 50 mobs isn't a hobby I plan to take up. I think this spell would be much more useful, not to mention necs becomming more popular groupers, if we could cast vamp embrace on others.]

    It's a fairly useful spell at the level you get it. It does become less useful as you level up and give up melee, but that is as intended. We really have no plans at present to further increase necro abilities and benefits, as it's hardly appropriate for the class.

  • Spell Mess Up?
    [Quote from original post: My question,why even have Draught of Jiva at lvl 55 when Voltaic Draught in the same thing at 54? make no sense.]

    Jiva does a little something extra.

    [Quote from original post: The Vision(tm) hard at work]

    Must you camp the boards looking for an opportunity to make a snide remark?

  • SK Pets
    The pets are plenty strong. Remember that SKs have the same combat skills as the Paladin, and have a way to inflict unresistable damage besides. The pet is of course not supposed to tank for you like a necro pet does for the necro.

    Personally I think that the 44th level pet is too strong for the SK.

  • GM Item Replacements
    [Quote from original post: Why GM can replace items from a mob stuck in the wall, but cannot replace items from corpse that poofed due to a bug ?]

    If she's stuck in a wall, the GM can get a look at the items, see what she had, and correct the situation, assuming she is still there when the GM is called. The GM cannot however tell what happened or what was dropped on a corpse that disappears.

    There's also the issue that there is currently no known bug that will cause a corpse to disappear before it is looted. If one exists, we haven't found anyone who was able to reproduce it. There was one at one time: It was related to DOT spells and was corrected.

    There are, on the other hand, two ways that a corpse could legitimately disappear following death. One, it could time out if the items aren't looted soon enough after death. Secondly, it could disappear immediately if everyone in the group that did the most damage died before the creature did. Both of those are acceptable and part of the way that the game is designed. Neither result in the creation of items for replacement.

    GMs will help in situations where you have an item *in your possession* that disappears. However the GM can't get involved until that item is actually in your possession.

  • Problems on The Nameless
    We've been looking into why this problem is happening on the Nameless. We're not quite sure what's causing it yet, but as soon as we track it down we'll get it fixed. I apologize for the problems.

  • Skills
    The patch message that indicated that numbers would be opened up for skills was incorrect. This was one area of the game where we decided not to release the actual numbers of skills. Overall, I think that its worked out. Those who care about the numbers can keep track of them on skill-increase messages, and those who don't aren't hit with them every time they look at their skill tab.

  • Wizard's Role in Norrath
    [Quote from original post: Allow each wizard damage spell a 5% chance to do up to double it's normal damage. Not very unbalancing, but gives a bit of an edge to wizzies where they are supposed to have it: The ability to deal Fast Magic Damage.]

    Though 5% is a bit over-the-top for this sort of thing, I wouldn't necessarily have a problem with suggesting something along these lines. However, I do wonder what the response of the community would be. Chances are, if that Ice Comet suddenly does 2000 damage, the wizard will die shortly thereafter.
