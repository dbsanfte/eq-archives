Pet Resist Changes
- Aurum @ 10:10 PM PDT
The focus of one of the latest Developer's Corner updates was once again on pets and more specifically the upcoming resist changes. The information is as follows:

    Dear Mr Absor

    I am writing this in hope of a response to my question..

    With the upcoming changes to pets that Mr Rich posted about on the mage/necro class boards, i am left wondering that although the changes will be benefical in some aspects, i am puzzled about this:

    Will pets lose there (near) immunity to spells of same type eg. Fire pet getting hit with fire spell...
    Because if thats the case this is a bad bad change, some mobs (like the one in CoM) REQUIRE fire pets because of the nasty fire AE it casts which can kill players in seconds.

    Although i genurally like the changes this is puzzling me, will the pets lose there immunity

    Sinceraly...
    Atermis
    Atermis Entreri
    50th Magician
    Innoruuk Server

    All of this is still in testing, so when all is said and done none of it, part of it or all of it may go Live.

    Right now its working like this.

    Summoned pets will use his owners resists and level against NPC spells unless the pet has a natural resistance that makes them immune or mostly so. Note: This only affects summoned pets and it will no affect spells cast by PCs - we dont want to make a change that might dramatically change PvP until we know more about the effect it might have.

    For example, fire elemental pets have incredibly high fire resistance, but low resistance to other type of magic. If someone tries to cast a fire-based spell on this pet, it will use its innate fire resistance because it's so high. If someone tries to cast a poison spell on the pet, it will use it's owner's current poison resistance score instead.

    Weve also added a bit more information to the /pet report health function. It will list the pets current effects along with its health.

    And whats with the Mr. Absor? My dad is called no, wait, he isnt. Hes called Mr. VanCouvering

    Oh well. :)

    Alan

EQ Real-time Strategy Game & Console Project Announced
- Aurum @ 9:17 PM PDT
The world of Norrath continues to grow and expand in exciting new directions. In an announcement released today, Sony Online Entertainment revealed plans to develop an EverQuest RTS game and begin work on an EverQuest console title with two new developers respectively.

Here is the press release:

    SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT PARTNERS WITH LEADING GAME DEVELOPERS TO BRING PLAYERS THE BEST IN GAME ENTERTAINMENT
    -Leader in Massively Multiplayer Online Gaming To Bring New Genres of Gaming to Consoles and PCs-

    SAN DIEGO, CA  July 18, 2002  Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming, today announced deals with Snowblind Studios Inc. and Rapid Eye Entertainment Inc. to create original content for the PC and console markets under the SOE label. Rapid Eye Entertainment is currently developing a real-time strategy, PC-based EverQuest game while Snowblind Studios is developing an EverQuest-based product for the PlayStation 2 computer entertainment system. Further product information will be announced at a later date.

    "We've made it a point to work closely with leading developers to create the best possible gaming experience," said Russell Shanks, Senior Vice President and Chief Technology Officer, Sony Online Entertainment. "As we expand our product line into new genres, our mission is to deliver compelling content and online capabilities to PC and console systems worldwide."

    Combining the experience and the expertise of the SOE development team with other top developers opens the market to new possibilities in gaming. By expanding into new genres of gaming, SOE is well poised to take advantage of the rapidly growing market that recently broke new ground in entertainment by surpassing movie box office receipts.

    Founded by Mark Caldwell in June 2001, Rapid Eye Entertainment (www.rapideyegames.com) is a game developer dedicated to expanding gameplay and pioneering new game genres. Caldwell was an original member and leader of the team behind the highly acclaimed Might and Magic series of role-playing games and the Heroes of Might and Magic series of turn-based strategy games. Headquartered in San Diego, Calif., the company's initial focus is a unique twist in the real-time strategy category.

    "Rapid Eye is dedicated to working on the cutting edge of gaming," said Mark Caldwell, founder and president, Rapid Eye Entertainment. "Our partnership with Sony Online Entertainment allows us to work on projects that will change the face of gaming, delivering great new content to a broader group of gamers."

    Snowblind Studios, best known for the development of Baldur's Gate: Dark Alliance, a widely successful role playing game for the PlayStation 2 system, is an award-winning entertainment software company founded by industry veterans Ryan Geithman and Brian Sostrom in 1997. Based in Bellevue, Washington, Snowblind Studios has developed titles for Nintendo 64 and the PlayStation 2 system.

    "EverQuest has taken the world by storm," said Ryan Geithman, president, Snowblind Studios. "We're excited to be working with Sony Online Entertainment to expand the EverQuest phenomenon to new platforms and new audiences."

Never Give Out Your Password
- Aurum @ 9:39 PM PDT
The scammers have drawn the ire of one Alan VanCouvering today. Hear what he had to say about it in the lone update made to the Developer's Corner:

    Let me start by saying this: Never give out your password.

    I certainly admit that the scammers are getting more creative, and theyve learned how to spell.

    The latest scam (which I cant display properly here) is an offer of a free copy of Ruins of Kunark. This is a pretty clever scam. Theyve copied the art and our web site to present a reasonable looking email, represented as coming from our mailing list.

    There are two key things about this scam that identify it as such.

    The first is the email address that it was sent from. The copy I have here was sent from Freeq account [eqaccountstatus@vgnmail.com]. It isnt too hard to spoof an email address and make it appear to be from a Sony Online email address, so this wont always be a clue. Just because it has an email address that appears to be from us doesnt mean it isnt a scam. In this case, however, the fact that the email address is definitely not ours is a good hint that something is up.

    The second, and most important clue, is that it asks for your EverQuest account information. The key to these scams is always to get your account name and password. Never give out your password.

    I suspect that those of you that read this board or one of the fan sites, that you already know never to give out your password. So Id like to ask you to do me a favor. Warn a friend. Its never fun to hear that a friend lost all of his or her stuff because of one of these scams.

    Never give out your password.

    Here is the text of the scam (at least they were kind enough to link to our store ). And in a boldly ironic move, they linked to the EULA.
