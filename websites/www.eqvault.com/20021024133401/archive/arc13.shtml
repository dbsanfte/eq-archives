      And if the "EverQuest" universe of Norrath were a country, its per-capita gross national product would be $2,266--comparable to the 77th richest country on Earth and ranking it between Russia and Bulgaria.     Brad McQuaid & Jeff Butler Announce New Company
    - Aurum @ 6:48 PM PST
    Well its official, former EverQuest gurus Brad McQuaid and Jeff Butler have launched a new company and have given it a name. Read all about it in this press release:

      "EVERQUEST" VETERANS BRAD MCQUAID AND JEFF BUTLER FORM SIGIL GAMES ONLINE, INC.

      SAN DIEGO, CA -- January 22, 2002 -- Brad McQuaid and Jeff Butler, formerly of
      Sony Online Entertainment and key figures in the development of the popular "EverQuest"
      series, today announced the formation of Sigil Games Online, Inc. The new company
      is dedicated to the production of massively multiplayer online role-playing games.

      Sigil's official website is set to launch this spring, and the company will begin
      adding designers, artists, and programmers to its staff later this year.

      McQuaid and Butler are both creators of the massively multiplayer runaway hit
      "EverQuest," developed by Verant Interactive. The game shattered all multiplayer
      game records after its release in 1999, exceeding all industry expectations and
      inspiring three top-selling expansion packs.

      McQuaid is President and CEO of Sigil, and Butler has assumed the role of Vice
      President.

      About Brad McQuaid
      Brad McQuaid was the co-designer of "EverQuest," managing the development team
      from the project's inception until its launch. He later became a founder of Verant
      Interactive and its Vice President. After Verant's acquisition by Sony Online
      Entertainment, Brad McQuaid served there as Vice President of Premium Games responsible
      for "EverQuest," "Sovereign," "PlanetSide," "Star Wars: Galaxies," and several
      currently unannounced projects. In addition, McQuaid was the company's Chief
      Creative Officer. He resigned amicably from the company in October 2001. McQuaid
      is one of the most respected online game developers in the industry, featured
      in with numerous articles in publications such as "The Wall Street Journal" and
      "Time Magazine." "PC Gamer" named him one of the "Next Game Gods" in their November
      2000 issue.

      About Jeff Butler
      Jeff Butler joined Verant Interactive as Customer Service Manager in early 1999,
      and was promoted to Producer in 2000. Butler managed the EverQuest Live teams
      dedicated to ongoing content and development while simultaneously producing the
      three most recent EverQuest expansions, "EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin," "EverQuest:
      The Scars of Velious," and "EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark." Jeff Butler resigned
      from SOE amicably in December 2001.

      Multi-component spells (even Snare spells) check to see if they break at regular intervals. Most spells with a Snare component have been specially coded to bypass that check. Some have noted that, for example, the Necromancer epic weapon effect worked the same way until it was given special coding to change that.

      Whether or not Insidious Retrogression should be changed, I don't know. I've certainly passed your concerns to the team.

      Chill/Ignite Bones - the illusion effect of these two spells are blocking the Dragon/Gaint Bane line of the Wizard class.

    Changes Coming to Cleric Epic Quest
    - Aurum @ 8:47 PM PST
    Alan VanCouvering detailed the changes coming to the Epic Quest for the Cleric class this evening on the Developer's Corner. The following is his complete post:

      Normally this isn't something I'd do. But, well, I'm going to outline the changes we're making to this quest in rather close detail. But first, let me try to explain the concern that players had with this quest.

      With arguably the most popular Epic Quest item, the Cleric trying to gain his Epic item usually finds plenty of people to help in the process. Clerics are commonly the first in line with guilds to help with their epic quests. So Clerics tend to get further in their epic quest faster than many others. This has lead to a very nasty 'bottleneck' for Clerics trying to complete the quest. The Ragefire portion of the quest has been creating a lot of static among players.

      So we have decided to make a change to the end portion of this quest. To reduce confusion and to help people understand why we're making this change, I'm going to give out a few specific details. These changes will go Live with the patch tonight.

      For those that are not interested in quest details, consider the rest of this post SPOILER INFORMATION:


      First, the 'merchant' version of Zordak will now appear at a different location in SoldungB. He will appear once each day (except after a server comes down, then he will spawn in one to three days). The appearance of this NPC will no longer require the absence of Lord Nagafen.

      The cleric still turns in his Shimmering Pearl to Zordak. But now the merchant Zordak will return a Swirling Pearl to the Cleric and disappear.

      At that point the Cleric will need to head out and look for him. A killable version of Zordak will appear independent of the pearl turn-in every few days (except after a server goes down, then he will appear a little faster). Killing Zordak will transform him into dragon form, and the Cleric (with help…) must kill him immediately.

      The new dragon Zordak will drop a heart that is a container that requires the Swirling Pearl to be combined in it to create the Heart that is turned in to Omat.

      The rest of the quest remains the same.


      The goal of these changes is to reduce the problems with the bottleneck that existed before. They will not remove that bottleneck, but hopefully will remove the nasty competition for a single spawn with so many people willing to be… rude to each other.

      Alan

    - Aurum @ 7:47 PM PST
    Absor returned to the topic of the Beastlords in one of his updates on the Developer's Corner today. The question followed by his answer may be viewed here:

      If Beastlords are so monk-like, why don't they have Feign Death or Instill doubt? Or in the case of spells a snare spell?

      Fleabitten Furball

      Because they are not Monks, they are Beastlords. More than any other 'hybrid', with the exception of Bards, Beastlords are their own class. They are a hybrid in the sense that they are partially melee and partially spell-caster, and they do indeed share some of the abilities of other classes.     End of the World Party Report - Druzzil Ro

    Laozee Mcharma sent us this report of the party that took place on Druzzil Ro:

    End of the World postponed!! Party goes on anyway!

    Much to the chagrin of our prophets, the world did not end last night as was originally forseen, proving once again the failings of prognostication and the fickle nature of the Gods. The time of blackness that was seen by our Oracles was postponed for Norrath, and our party planned for the eclipse of Luclin seemed in imminent danger.

    The discovery by our prophets about the postponment of the end came after our announcements and plans were made concerning the party, and it seemed that the Gods of Norrath sought to spoil our plans to congregate and show our solidarity.

    Undaunted, the Defenders of the Rose still gathered in West Freeport, and what WAS an "End of the World Party" became a "The World's NOT Ending Party". Either way it was still an occasion worth celebrating, so we continued on with our plans and celebrated in style.

    All of our family of guilds was represented there (Thank you Sisterhood of Roses and Legacy of the Rose), as well as members of many other guilds of Druzzil Ro. The turn our was greater then we expected considering the circumstances, and we partied long past the time when the moon was expected to go black. The Defenders of the Rose would like to thank everyone who was in attendance, especially those with other guild affiliations who came to aid the celebration.

    The Defenders are still planning another "End of the World Party", should the Oracles predict yet another dark time approaching, and although it might be difficult to surpass the success of this party, we hope that the next one will prove to the Gods that we don't fear them and that they will not damage our comraderie or thwart our plans for greatness.

    Laozee Mcharma
    Shaman, Defender of the Rose
    Druzzil Ro server
