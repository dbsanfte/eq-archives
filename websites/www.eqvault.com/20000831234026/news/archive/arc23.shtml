Gordon Infobits
[14 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:07 PM PDT
Here's all the latest from Gordon Wrinn on the official EQ Gameplay Board:

  • Final Fix to Overcrowding?
    [Quote from original post: The FINAL fix to overcrowding, soloing, aggroing greens, and all other problems! Make greens give experience.]

    Soloability in its current state is as intended. The classes who were designed to solo can do so efficiently. Some other classes have developed strategies that allow them to solo under certain circumstances, but not as efficiently. Finally, most if not all of the classes have the ability to kill an occasional experience-giving critter, but not to the extent that they could really say that they are "soloing".

    My point here is that since soloability isn't broken, giving every class the ability to go "soloing" (Meaning efficiently killing creatures for the purpose of advancement) really wouldn't fix anything.

    Finally, we really don't want to encourage people to bottomfeed (killing things much lower level than you, thus denying the people who should *really* be killing those things the experience of doing so). Bottomfeeding is a fairly present problem these days, to the extent that we've actually considered making it so that things that don't give experience won't give loot either.

  • NPCs
    I have to go with Xvarious here. NPCs finish their casting cycle and only then turn around to flee. It has to do with the way NPCs process commands. They have a list of commands they execute, and only certain things can interrupt that list. In casting, the only thing is a bash. Even death causes them to finish their spell as I recall :)

  • AE Spells on PvP
    Though this explanation doesn't apply to RZ, on the Teams servers, it is an incentive to group within your own team. By choosing to group outside your team, those spells become less effective.

    I will however chat with the team on Monday about it. As I recall, of the 3 types of AE spells, one of them (radiate out from caster) doesn't affect group members. The other two (radiate out from target, and rain spells) do.

  • Signs EQ is Rotting Your Brain
    I've a couple:

    1. I was intently concentrating while playing the game one night, when my wife came over, gave me a kiss, and told me that she was going to the store. I asked her if she'd pick up a couple of stacks of jasper while she was there.

    2. My wife would take insane joy in talking to me while I was playing EQ (again concentrating like mentioned above). On more than one occasion, I responded in /gsay to things that she said. My group thought I was losing my mind... If they only knew :)

  • Even More Monk Issues
    [Quote from original post: Why can Monks get weapons that are stronger than their fists?]

    Simply, because if we provided monks weapons that were not as good as their fists, there wouldn't be a point to them.

    [Quote from original post: Correct me if I'm mistaken, but I thought the idea of a monk is that they fight best with their fists, not with a weapon. This basically makes monks blunt-wielding warriors that hit harder and can't take as many hits.. Variety is a good thing, and the more the better.]

    Monks do wield a considerable amount of power with their fists, giving them an advantage over all of the melee classes when naked. The difference in power between the fists and the weapons is not so great that a monk would be at a *huge* disadvantage by choosing to shun weapons.

    [Quote from original post: Why don't Monks get any more damage upgrade on their fists post 50? I hear they're 14/30 at 50, and 14/28 at 60.]

    Though the weapon damage on the fist does not go up, they do get better damage tables, thus yeilding more damage. In other words, a level 60 monk will still hit harder than a level 50 monk.

  • 3 Million a Day for Verant
    Though I do recogize that this is a humorous post, it is strangly in line with some serious suggestions that I heard a few weeks ago.

    At E3, I was asked a number of times by other game developers why we don't do something along these lines. Some suggested that we should sell the ability to level faster, others suggested that we should sell items and characters.

    Though we could certainly make more money doing something along these lines, we really don't feel that doing something of this nature would be fair or good for the game. We feel that people should be able to expect similar rewards for similar time invested and similar risk taken. Doing something along these lines would just reduce the only deciding factor to those with a bigger wallet.

  • More Monk Issues
    [Quote from original post: Shiverback has way better AC than cured silk. ;P But it's not quite as good as other non planar armor, which is the problem.]

    By the way, someone a post a few months ago outlining each piece of shiverback and comparing it to a piece of non-planar monk wearable armor that was preferable in most cases. I sent it to myself in an email so that I could hang onto it.

    I'm having trouble finding that email. Can anyone send me that? Please be concise. The EQLive team was looking for it as well.

  • More Level 50+ Spell Droppings
    [Quote from original post: From having played the first 50 levels where spells were always available, we all assumed that every caster of a certain level is supposed to have all their spells. To a caster, spells are what gear is to a meleer. To have the best spells readily available is like having all the best melee items in the game available on vendors.]

    That's a good point Utay. Players have always viewed spells as "entitlements", espicially wis-casters who didn't have to worry about research. We're trying something different here. My character was 43rd level before he got his 39th pet. This situation really isn't that much different.

    Players can generally survive and advance using a combination of their 49th level spells and the spells that are purchasable if need be. Finding a spell will just make it that much easier, in the same fashion that a melee character doesn't have to have the absolute best weapon in existance in order to be effective.

  • Monks
    Monks are supposed to do more melee damage than warriors, which is why monk weapons are better than warrior weapons. If a warrior can suddenly do the damage of a monk, while having a LOT more hitpoints, a warrior would be preferential in a group in all cases. The compelling reason to have a monk would be missing.

    This is the reason that the staff was overpowered for warriors.

  • Walking Sleep - Turgur's Insects
    [Quote from original post: I didn't see anything in the patch message that said this was fixed. Verant could we please have an update!?!?]

    This hasn't been fixed yet. We'll definitely let you know when we've made some progress on it. In the mean time, I'd recommend that people don't use this weapon when there's someone in the group that can cast a better attack speed debuff.

    In light of news of some new programs of this type coming on the market, we just wanted to let you know that we will vigorously investigate these issues as they come to our attention. Anyone found to be using such a program (macro programs, packet sniffers, etc.) will be immediately and permanently banned from EverQuest.

    Please, let's play fair.

    -Gordon

GroupExp v 1.0 - 25 May 2000
----------------------------

This is the first "production" release. I think the majority of the kinks
are now worked out.

1. All valid Race/Class combinations should now be properly recognized.
2. Default action for the key is now Calculate, vice Exit.

remaining reflects the fact that you are about 40% thru the level).

group composition (you no longer have to make all your changes, then hit
the Calculate button).
5. Added the ability to view Current xp, Next Level xp, and xp Remaining for
each player in the group.
6. Added Labels at the top to make the interface more clear.
7. Added the ability to minimize the window if desired.

Known Issues:
1. GroupExp does not enforce level restrictions (i.e., you can group with
anybody, and anything you kill will give you experience). I may fix this
at a later date, but I am fairly certain the players know who they can
group with, and what they can kill. =)

    Though I don't want to get too deep into the mechanics, everything taunts the same amount according to damage with the exception of spells which can have specific amounts of "taunt" set on a per-spell basis.

What is important is the amount of damage (taunt/hate/etc) done over a period of time. Creatures generally perceive those who have done a lot of damage recently over those who have done more damage total.

    Rangers, rogues, and monks, who typically dual wield 1H weapons usually catch the most heat here because the speed of 1H weapons usually has them hitting closer to the average damage of their weapons over a given time period. In other words, due to the speed of their weapons they have a better chance to make their average. In other words, over 20 seconds, a warrior might do 1000 damage, and a ranger 900, but at the 10 second mark the ranger might have been outdamaging the warrior.

    All things being equal, creatures that have freedom of movement will go after the character that they hate the most, whereas creatures that are somehow encumbered (rooted, snared, etc.) will go after the character that is the closest. Try it out sometime.

    The streaks that you mention really aren't indicative of a problem with our random number generator, but rather that it's working ok. You seem to be asking that we introduce code to thow some assurances into the equation, but that would actually break the random number generator. Right now, no one can predict if a creature is going to drop the rare on the next spawn. That's how it should be.

    [Quote from original post: And at the same time, you don't know when you'll get what you want. But you still won't spend 20 hours in a row camping for nothing.]

    Your assumption seems to be that if someone wants an item, they are entitled to it. That's not how we feel. There still has to be an element of chance involved. Increasing chances of acquiring an item on each spawn does nothing to reduce camping. In fact the perceived entitlement to an item actually encourages it. People camp things like the runed cowl because they feel relatively certain that they can get one in X hours. The only way to get rid of camping entirely would be to 1) Make items so common that they no longer mean anything, 2) Make it so that they are lible to drop anywhere (we are testing this idea for some things in Kunark, but will still have the best items dropping off of named critters), or 3) Make items so rare that no one would camp them (for instance, if the average camp time on the Runed cowl was 100 hours, very few peopel would camp it, more likely people would just happen across it and get lucky).

    None of these options used exclusively appeals to us. What we are trying in Kunark is making nice items drop randomly, but keeping the best items on the rare mob, but at a rarer rate than usual. By doing this we make it so that you don't need to have absolute best item to be competative, you can get a good one just by fighting. However if you do want the best weapon/item out there, it might take a bit of specific planning and execution, or maybe some luck :)
