Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:24 PM PDT
Here's today's round of the Gordon Infobits, a compilation of excerpts and quotes from recent postings by Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • EverQuest Soundtrack
    I believe that all of the songs in EQ also play on the website at www.everquest.com :) I'm not sure if the music would make for an entire soundtrack. Maybe if someone expanded on the existing works :) (Note from Aurum: We have two zips containing EverQuest's music in our Files section)

  • Hasten Still Bugged?
    I'll have the test team check this out. Is this only on Brell? Or other servers as well? Is he getting stuck somewhere, or does he keep zipping around?

  • South Karana Test Server Issues
    I'm sure SK has a much lower population on the test server, and they might have gotten some good rolls when you've been there. This could contribute to the observations.

    However, just to be sure I've forwarded the post to the Test and Live teams to doublecheck.

  • Enviromental Effects: Can an Iksar survice in EverFrost?
    Environmental effects from cold are something that wasn't put into the game. If we were to do something like this, we might have to make it so that Trolls can't go to Lavastorm or something of that nature. This is one of those times that realism takes a back seat in the interest of gameplay, like when we let full plate wearing encumbered clerics swim.

  • Fear Vs. Hate
    Interestingly enough, we got the impression over the past several weeks that people found the drop rates in Fear to be acceptable, and hate unacceptable. Prior to that, the general feeling was that drops in Fear were bad, but Hate was great. At that time, we adjusted both of them in opposite directions. This time however, last patch fixed Hate, but didn't change Fear.

    I'm starting to think that the problem at this point might be coming from the standpoint that "equality between the planes" hasn't been acheived.

    The truth is that both planes are now engineered to drop the same amount of armor (on average) in the same period of time. They are basically equal now with possible exception to a surplus of cleric armor that might be dropping in Hate due to public reports.

  • Dungeons and Kerra Isle
    The dungeons are designed with the idea that there will be several groups all "doing" them at the same time. In your example, the idea would be that some other group should have cleared out the area behind you by the time you need to run. Of course then you train and kill them, so you might have a point :) Let's give it some time however, we are committed to having people use this and some of the other underutilized dungeons. If we need to make further changes to make that happen, we will.

  • Symbol of Naltron bugged?
    Well it's designed so that if you want the best thing on you, it's going to cost enough that you'd consider it "expensive". Looking at the lineup you've posted, and remembering it from my leatherskin days, it looks like Jasper may actually be underpriced, but I don't think we'll be changing it at this point.

    One good piece of news, the next in the line of Symbol's will use the same reagent and last longer.

  • Who's the Tank: Ranger or Cleric?
    [Quote from original post: The Cleric class is suppose to be a plate armor class, and the Ranger is a chainmail class. And from my understanding plate armor is suppose to be superior to chainmail. That and I'm all for my healer to have more AC.]

    This is correct, and that was the motive behind our changes. Now we realize that there are pieces of armor that the ranger can wear that use the plate texture, but that is *really* chain-based, and was set to use the plate texture since it looked better.

    There needs to be an advantage to being a plate class, and that's why the cleric armor was boosted to bring it in line with the hybrids.

Gordon on Items in the Plane of Sky
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:18 PM PDT
Verant's Gordon Wrinn talked about items in the Plane of Sky on the the boards today. Here's his post:

    Hello all,
    I've noticed that quite a few people are upset about the quality of some of the Plane of Sky items that have been found so far.

    Part of the problem is due to the fact that the most powerful items still have yet to be acquired. Once you start seeing these items come out of the plane, you will understand what I meant when I said "There are items in the Plane of Sky that make other planar items look like rusty weapons".

    The other part of the problem is that some of the items that have been found so far are indeed underpowered. Since we were entering uncharted territory with many of our ideas for these items, we erred on the side of making them underpowered, thus avoiding the problems that occur should we have to downgrade them later. That said, while some of the items that have been found so far are appropriately powered, many are not.

    Though I will not comment on any particular item at this time, we will be reevaluating the Plane of Sky items in the near future, probably after the initial excitement from the Kunark release.

    -Gordon

King Tearis Thex addressed Greater Faydark in front of Felwithe this evening. Accompanied by Firiona Vie, he toasted the crowd. Although I was disconnected before the zone crashed, I did catch his toast-

King Tearis Thex shouts, 'To all of you, I give my best wishes. Though I am your King, It is not necessarily I who has brought you here today. It is your hearts, your spirits... the very things that make you of the Fier'Dal and Koada'Dal nations, that have brought you here.'

Gordon Infobits: Weekend Edition
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:51 PM PDT
On the official Gameplay Board, Verant's Gordon Wrinn has made several posts today. Here's a rundown of all of them:

  • Opening Kunark on Test Server Early?
    [Quote from original post: As a bonus to your Loyal customers is there any reason why you couldn't turn on Kunark on the Test Server before the rest of the servers? That way we would get to try out Kunark before it actually goes live on the real servers.]

    To be honest, with over 30 servers online, we'll still have to be on our toes to insure that severe problems don't happen when thousands of people go to the new lands at once.

    To funnel 50K+ people into one server for that purpose would make that task impossible.

  • Lack of GM Events?
    Please keep in mind that just because it doesn't end up on the front page of a news site doesn't mean that it didn't happen. I ran over two dozen dynamic quests, some of epic proportion on Vallon Zek over September and October, and I believe only two made it to the news sites.

    Of the quests that have been reported to the news sites lately, the majority have run across all servers, Bristlebane included.

  • Werewolves
    [Quote from original post: Just wondering if their were bigger plans for werewolves originally, for example, were player's going to get lycanthropy?]

    We were playing around with the idea of lycanthropy in the earliest stages of beta. The models were added to all of the zones at that time. Lycanthropy turned out to be something that we ended up not implementing, but the existence of the werewolf model made for a nifty illusion.

  • Iksars and Rogues
    [Quote from original post: Hmm... lizards seems perfect for rogues, they're fast, agile, and they just look nasty! So why no Iksar rogues? I hope they come along later like Erudite SKs (Though sooner after RoK release than ERU SKs did from EQ release).]

    They were a very close-knit society for a very long time, every one of them being loyal to the main city from which they ruled the lands with an iron fist. I guess that their society was just a bit too harmonious for an underground group of thieves to form.

  • Drops in Hate Broken Again?
    Drops work on the basis of percentages. For instance, an NPC of type X might have a Y% chance of dropping a piece of armor from armor group Z.

    This and other random systems depend on a roll of a virtual die. There will certainly be times that you catch good or bad rolls. These good or bad rolls can run in streaks just due to the nature of randomness.

    Basically what I'm saying is that you cannot determine the correctness of the drops based upon one clearing of the plane, or even 10. Believe it or not, there is still a miniscule chance that an entire plane clear could result in zero drops, by the same fashion that it is possible that everything could drop something.

  • Carrying Items
    Right now there is a maximum number of items that can be possessed by a character, given all inventory slots and bank slots available to them. The way data is entered or extracted from the character file optimized based upon this theoretical maximum. As such, we really wouldn't be interested in increasing the number of items that can be stored as part of the character.

  • Can Clerics Heal Pets?
    Clerics should have no problems healing a mages pet. There's no level requirement or anything of that nature. I imagine in this case he was just getting beaten on faster than you could mitigate the damage.

  • Necromancer Quesiton
    [Quote from original post: Have any human necros out there ever have trouble with running out of mana constantly? I have only played a DE necro and was wondering how much of a hinderance the lower int turns out to be, since Iksar's are as smart(dumb) as humans.]

    The "point of diminishing returns" on the INT stat is fairly easy to reach regardless of your race. I don't see it being a problem for the Iksar.

  • Responding to Suggestions/Spelling Issues
    Well, in response to at least one of the many good suggestions in this thread, we did recently task someone with going through all of the text in the game for the purpose of fixing grammar and spelling.

Gordon Infobits
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:57 PM PDT
As always, Gordon Wrinn has been keeping busy on the Gameplay Board. Here are quotes and excerpts from today's posts:

  • Gordon on Several Topics and Issues
    We've no new illusions planned beyond what may be included with the Kunark release.

    The Lull series is not Harmony, and as such suffers some disadvantages. It's not meant to make single pulls possible in all (or even most) situations. It's also why Harmony is outdoors only.

    We'd love to see more use for the mana vial series, and hope to when the guys get a chance to take another stab at trade skills. Poison is planned first after the expansion, but we'll see what happens after then.

    We feel that the enchanters pets (and the enchanter class) are balanced. The enchanter pet is a supplement to the class, not a major component of it. We do expect that most enchanters will dump the pet for most dungeon situations later in life, as they can be a tad unruly.

    Religion change won't be an option in EQ.

    No ranger-only skill is planned.

    SK-only weapons (quest based) are planned, though I can't comment further on them at this time.

    Though there are FA-class weapons for other classes out there (PoS mostly), we're also planning on creating "Epic Quest" weapons for all of the classes. This is not however the type of thing that we'd make regular updates on.

  • RoK Shipping & Order Confirmations
    Confirmations just started going out today, and the computers are plugging away as we speak. Hang tight in the meantime, the confirmations are going out in no particular order, and you should get yours soon.

  • Aging Armor?
    Back in EQ's design phase we opted out of item upkeep based upon the philosophy that people shouldn't have to worry about losing something once they earned it. In retrospect it is quite possible that such upkeep could help establish and maintain the importantance of trade skills, though a change like this would not be possible in EQ at this juncture. It is certainly a philosophy that we can keep in mind for future products that we'll be involved with.

    [Quote from original post: limiting ertain classes from wearing banded, i.e. shawman, bard, and making reinforced armor a little more appealing to utilize the toiloring skill alittle more,no more big gap between cured silk and backpacks.]

    Reinforced armor was a fairly recent addition to the game compared to banded, it probably wouldn't have been fair to take that away from them at the time.

  • Why does life leech cost more than 315 mana?
    All hybrid spells are subject to a mana penalty curve associated with their level. As the level goes up, the mana penalty decreases. The early SK lifetaps have a spell-specific additional mana penalty associated with them. Life Leech does not have this additional mana penalty, but is still subject to the general hybrid mana penalty.

  • Buying Levels For Money?
    [Quote from original post: That's right, all those rich people can twink their new characters with a few thousand pp, go buy some gems, and do the crafted quests OVER AND OVER. They don't care if they sell at a loss, it's easy experience, and it's NICE. Furthermore, this holds true for a LOT of class armor quests.]

    I'm only marginally familiar with the crafted quests, and correct me if I'm wrong, but don't all of them require a dropped item in addition to gems? The inclusion of a dropped item (e.g. something that must be adventured for) goes a long way to quell our concerns.

  • Moving Characters Between Accounts?
    I'm sorry, but we do not allow characters to be moved between accounts. The prospect of allowing that opens too many doors for potential problems.

  • Another Nerf Needed!
    Rofl. Might as well I suppose, someone nerfed my stock portfolio today. Actually it's been an ongoing persistent nerf for about the last month and a half. [Note from Aurum: My portfolio was nerfed all week too!]

  • Reclaim Energy Issues
    As a level 1 spell, it's really as useful as we want to make it. We don't want pets turning into a mana battery. That might drastically change gameplay for certain encounters. For instance, someone might find it more efficient to terminate his pet right before the end of battles in order to avoid sharing experience with it.

  • Will Verant turn off faction hits for major GM events?
    No. For events such as this we prefer that they take place with at least some semblance of character. There's also the fact that faction effects just can't be turned off, much like XP loss.

  • Verant's View on Trading Accounts
    Accounts are non-transferable per the license agreement, so this would apply to both the selling and trading of the accounts. On another note I do suggest that you be very careful regardless what you decide to do. We've seen many people scammed by people claiming that they'd trade accounts.

  • Is it possable to gain faction with dungeon creatures?
    In some cases it is possible. Dungeon creatures that have faction often have a way to reduce the faction. For instance, the well-known "Live Froglocks of Guk" faction can be reversed. One thing to keep in mind is that there are thresholds to certain factions, meaning that there is often a limit to how good, or in some cases, how bad it can get. Finally, some factions can't be improved if you let them get below a certain point. For example, if someone bombs a building here in the US, we don't care how much he gives to charity after the fact, he's still no good :)

  • Some of the expansion spells will only be found off planes mobs
    This is not at all true. Though it is true that some of the expansion spells will be treasure drops, many will be purchaseable or questable. I can see how you might come to this conclusion, but please keep in mind that the plans are *currently* the only place you can find 50+ NPCs in abundance. That's why you are seeing the spells drop there.

  • Lack of, um, Breats on Iksar Females
    Er... They are reptiles. :) To my knowledge, reptiles have never had need for such endowments.

  • Is Kerra Isle Fixed?
    The alternate data set should be active on all servers. I'll ask the GM staff to doublecheck.

  • Moving NPC Corpses
    GMs cannot (meaning that they do not have the ability) to move NPC corpses. What further actions that a GM would be able to take would be up to the GM there at the time. If the corpse was there in the wall, there's a very good chance that the GM couldn't have targeted it either. Did you try the /loot command?

    Of course now that the corpse is gone, there's really nothing further that a GM could do in regards to this issue.

  • Cleric Quest Armor
    You mean the stats on the individual pieces of cleric armor? We're pretty much happy with the way they are. If you compare the different class-specific armors, and look at the values of the primary stats granted, the cleric armor is right up there with the other peices. Mid level armor doesn't grant the super stat buffs that many are asking for.

    There are many +WIS items out there, but you might have to trade AC and appearance for them.
