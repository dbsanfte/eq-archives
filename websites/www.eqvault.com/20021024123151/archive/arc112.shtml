Gordon Infobits
[12 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:03 PM PDT
Hot off the pages of the Gameplay Board, here is today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Pet Regen Rates
    Pet regen rates haven't changed. I imagine that you might have gotten a new or higher level pet. Though newer and higher level pets do get more hitpoints, their regen rate is always the same number of hitpoints per tick.

  • Hangman's Noose (haste) From Trakanon Seems to be Broken
    I've forwarded this to the test team so that they can have a look. I'm not sure off the top of my head what level haste this item is supposed to have, but assuming that your times are correct it does appear to be broken.

  • Clarifying Previous Post
    [Quote from original post: A few weeks ago 2HS was fixed by changing the minimum damage that it would do. Was this change for 2HS only, or does it apply to weapons of all types?]

    It applied to all 2-Handed weapon types.

    [Quote from original post: There are many relatively slow 1H weapons (delays of 35-40) that did not perform as well as their lower-delay cousins. Do high delay 1H weapons now perform as well as low delay 1H weapons of the same ratio?]

    Lower delay weapons still have an advantage over their high delay counterparts of the same ratio due to the way the damage bonuses are applied.

    For instance, assume that a 8-point bonus will be applied on a weapon. In 4 seconds, a 20 delay weapon would apply that bonus twice, whereas a 40 delay weapon would apply it only once. However, if the 40 delay weapon were 2-handed, it would apply a larger bonus.

  • More on Corpses Decaying
    [Quote from original post: Reason Why Corpses Should NOT Disappear. Though I have not personally ran into the problem of having a corpse disappear, I am sure I will. But is this realistic? No! Though the flesh and bone of a corpse may rt all it wants, the fact is: ARMOR DOES NOT ROT AS QUICKLY AS FLESH!So why not have the corpses turn into skeletons after a while? The armor would still be on it, and we can all live in peace.]

    Corpses decay in EQ due to the need to maintain a playable framerate for people in the game. As many people have mentioned in some other threads, the number of corpses in the vicinity directly impact the framerate that people experience. Each corpse must be drawn on the screen. Packing tons corpses in close proximity basically has the same effect on framerate as packing a ton of live characters in the same area.

  • AE Mez
    [Quote from original post: This is one I cannot fathom for the life of me. in another post, it was mentioned that a pet is "part of the person" by a GM when explaining why pets could not be charmed. Ok...doesnt make total sense to me...but ok.

    That being the case, why in blazes are group pets...including my own little sword and shield...effected by a spell that doesnt hit group members? If they are a part of me...or even if not...they sre still a member of the group.

    Abashi, could you either;

    A. Look at this and see if a change is needed or,
    B. Give me a rational explanation why it is like this?]

    Does it actually hit them, meaning that they get mez'd and stop fighting, or are they just counted in the total number of criters that can be affected?

    [Quote from original post: To put it another way, if I use color flux and color shift while two pets are after the mob, I now have three hostile mobs (two pets and the origional) on me untill the pet owners can call them off....which is not always that fast. So in short, yes it hits them and they become the enemy as soon as the mez breaks. And since I cant control my pet, I have to run and or kill it.]

    Well that certainly doesn't sound right. I'll have the test team check it out and see what we can do.

  • Tranquil Staff Proc is Still Broken
    Ack, I knew I forgot to announce something. The staff is fixed, but you'll need to get ahold of a GM to trade it out for a working one. The current one you have thinks that it is out of charges.

  • A Big Problem with Gyrating Goos
    [Quote from original post: Gyrating Goos, a level 28ish mob [in dalnir] now proc a poison which does 800-900 points of damage!]

    Ouch. That certainly doesn't sound correct. I've got the live team checking it out now. We'll do a special zone-patch for that once it is corrected.

  • Rotting Corpses
    [Quote from original post: Blah, its NOT fun and exiting to have your corpse rot!!! Dont even try to tell me that! You cant say, i know its exciting for you]

    Very true, in the same fashion that getting severely injured or killed in extreme sports isn't fun either. However, the prospect of that, however remote, does lend a good amount of excitement to the endeavor. If we start retrieving corpses in order to eliminate corpse loss, then the challenge and discretion imparted to its prospect is also null and void.

    Given that corpses must disappear for multiple technical reasons including framerate, and the fact that we cannot build a server to track an infinite number of corpses, we've engineered the possibility of a lost corpse and items as part of the game in the same fashion as done by other games in the genre.

    As always, I would recommend that if a specific aspect of the game provides more risk that is palatable for you, you might not want to engage in that aspect. In other words, if a dungeon is basically unexplored and not populated with people that can help you, you might want to visit places that are safer.

  • Login Server Inaccurate
    There's really no way for the client to determine whether it is down, or just busy and not responding in a given amount of time. Either case provides the same information back to the client, that being that the login server is unreachable.

    Perhaps we should change the message so that it points people to the network status page in the event that the outage is planned.

  • Howling Stones
    [Quote from original post: The reason for this is simple: THE QUEST FOR THE KEY IS TOO DIFFICULT!]
    I've asked the Live team to take a look at the rate at which Charasis keys are entering the world.

    [Quote from original post: last i heard if a rogue picks it group is transported in, and i believe it is the same with the key. otherwise as you say it wouldn't be worth it, the jade chokidai prod is generally camped for people who want to use it... its worth a decent ammount, and you have to use one to just get a key?]

    That teleporter being pickable was a mistake which is why we did those corpse recoveries for the few rogues who picked into the zone.

    As for the keys, each key transports one person, so everyone in your group will need one. Also keep in mind the difficulty in doing a corpse recovery in a zone that has a keyed entrance. Make sure that you have some friends with their own keys as well :)

  • Track Corpse Bug?
    [Quote from original post: While you are taking a look at the:

    1) Spell Drops
    2) Uber Guards (that see Invis.)

    Please do us a favor and fix Track Corpse as it hasn't worked since the expansion came up! You cast this spell and it does absolutely nothing except put an icon on your screen.

    Since you gave us the spell...please go about the process of testing it out and providing a fix for it.]

    I've forwarded this to the test team to check it out. Thanks for the info.

More on Melee Binding
[65 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:55 PM PDT
Gordon Wrinn again address the subject of melee players paying an NPC in a city for a bind on the Gameplay Board. Here's part of the original post followed by Gordon's response on the thread:

    That's Verat's customer service policy, it seems. You take what they offer, or get out. You do NOT want fries with that, but you DO want a chocolate milkshake. This is NOT Burger King, you have it our way or you don't get the damn thing.
    This is from EQVault:

    ------------------

    Poll Results & New Poll Question
    [12 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:35 PM PDT
    The results are in from last week's poll that asked if you thought melee classes should be able to pay an NPC for a bind in a city. The results are:

    Yes: 76.6% - 3264 votes
    No: 23.3% - 996 votes

    --------------------

    Add to this the vast number of threads posted on this board about melee binding, and if you go through them all, you will find similar percentages. About 70% of the posters are strongly in favor of it, becuase they are damn tired of trying to find a bind at off-peak times. About 10% are againt teh idea. The remaining 20% don't really care, but see nothing wrong with it.

    So, this is an example of an issue that a fairly large percentage of players feel fairly strongly about, in general. There has been NO EXPLANATION WHATSOEVER of why having an NPC in the city that charges 20pp for a bind, or other similarly limited and expensive solutions to this problem, would be in any way negative. Here, in fact, is the latest response from Verant, and is typical of anything else they've previously said on the issue:

    -----------------

    I'm sorry guys, but the binding issue is absolutely a dead horse, which is why I'm not jumping in the threads to remind everyone about that more than once a month or so.

    The game has been out for over a year. In that time, melee characters have survived and prospered, even though the binding arrangements haven't changed. Melee characters will continue to thrive without implementing something such as this that would drastically change the pace of the game, game balance, class interdependency, and the penalty for death.

    Binding is an advantage of being a pure caster in the same fashion that the ability to dish out melee damage, wear protective armor, etc., is an advantage of being a melee player. My advice to you is to make friends with clerics, wizards, druids, and anyone else who can make your travel go quicker.

    -Gordon

    ----------------

    That's their response to the concerns of the players. No real reason is given, except to say that giving the melee classes the ability to bind themselves would change the game balance, with which most people would agree. He conveniently ignores the plethora of suggestions that do not involve giving the ability to melee classes and thus avoiding the balance problem.

    This is a rant about Verant's attitudes towards it's customers. I am SICK and TIRED of being treated like a small retarded child by a company that I am paying for a service. It seems the vast majority of what Verant calls customer service amounts to either patting me on the head and telling me it'll all be okay, or snidely insulting my intelligence.

    There are times where majority opinion does not necessarily point to something that is good for the game, and it is in these cases where, though the outcome might be a bit painful, we need to decide what is in the best interest of the game as a whole. I would imagine that we could get a similar number of people agreeing if the question was "Is your class underpowered?", or "Should it be easier to level and acquire good equipment?", or "Should new characters start out with some money?"

    In this case, we believe that wide-scale melee binding, whether in the form of allowing an NPC to bind melee characters in city zones, or allowing casters to bind melee characters anywhere, would be bad for the game as a whole. It reduces class interdependency, the penalty for death, the incentive for people to protect the melee characters at all costs, and the incentive to avoid getting in over your head.

    -Gordon

New Patch Message
[35 Comments] - Aurum @ 11:14 AM PDT
A new message is up on the Patch Server:

    May 16th, 10:00am
    -------------------

    ***Patch Day***

    *General Changes*

    - Sounds have been updated for NPCs in Kunark. Your Iksar should no longer sound like humans.
    - Dark Elven worshipers of Solusek Ro and Bristlebane will no longer be natively KOS to the outpost in the Overthere. Take care that if you have taken actions allowing you to be judged sufficiently by your deeds, you may still find yourself KOS.
    - Increased the number of frightfingers in the Plane of Fear. Previously only 3 frightfingers would pop per respawn.
    - Several problems with the guards that were buffed up last week have been addressed. Some guards that should not have been buffed have been fixed. In addition, due to the level of the new guards, they all gained the ability to innately see-invisible. The level of those guards has been reduced so that they no longer automatically get this ability, making it a bit easier to sneak by them.
    - Many quests have been updated and/or fixed, both in terms of ease of completion and reward.

    *Item Updates*

    - Corrected a typo that had the Canyoneer's Pike marked as NO RENT, causing it to disappear on logout.
    - Corrected the model for the Axe of the Iron Back. It was showing as a 2H sword when equipped.
    - The Halcyon Broadsword was mistakenly marked as an artifact. This has been corrected.
    - Corrected item icons for various pieces of Burynai Hide Armor. It was displaying improperly in the inventory slots.
    - The Skull Mask of Innervation has been changed so that it must be worn to activate its effect.
    - Corrected a problem with the Scourgetail Whip whereby the damage and delay figures were transposed yielding a 30 damage, 5 delay weapon.
    - The "Illusion: Iksar" spell scroll is now droppable/tradeable.
    - The Goblin Eye Poker now looks like a dagger instead of a small sack.
    - The Cone of Mystics, which incidentally also looked like a small sack, now looks as intended.
    - Corrected a typo that had the Goblin Skull Crusher's effect set to the "Lull" spell, which is not appropriate for a melee weapon. The "Lull" effect has been replaced with an appropriate effect.
    - The Pouch of Mistletoe Powder will now impart its effect on the targeted character.
    - Mosquito Wings are available in game once again.
    - Many items had incorrect usage restrictions, and have been repaired.

    As always, we strive to avoid making negative changes to items that already exist in game. However, there are cases where a particularly unbalancing error has been made, such as with the Scourgetail Whip mentioned above, where the item in question must be fixed in interest of maintaining game balance.

    We will continue to repair problems, add content, and enhance gameplay in both Kunark and EQ proper. We appreciate everyone's support.

    Thank you,

    The EverQuest Team
