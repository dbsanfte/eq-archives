IMPORTANT: New Info & Policy Changes - eBay Forbidden
- Aurum @ 5:23 PM PDT
I just receive an important letter from Verant President John Smedley addressing several of EverQuest's major issues. Topics included in this letter are Kunark, login server changes, ShowEQ and hacking and eBay. It is now forbidden to auction EverQuest acounts and items on eBay for money. Here's the letter:

    Dear EverQuest Players,

    An exciting time is now at hand. Within the next 2 days the Login Serverswill be coming down and we will be doing a patch that will add the EverQuestStore into the front end of the game. At that time, the first product we will be offering will bethe EverQuest: Ruins of Kunark (RoK) upgrade for existing customers. This will allow existingcustomers of EverQuest to purchase RoK for $16.95 (Plus S&H). In addition, we will offer variousshipping options depending on which country you live in. EverQuest: Ruins of Kunark will be launchingon April 24th. We want to insure that this product is in everybody's hands on or before that date.

    In addition to this change, we will also be modifying our User Agreement andSoftware License to add in some additional Terms and Conditions that we will require everyone toagree to before playing EverQuest.

    The first of these changes concerns the selling of EverQuest Characters andItems outside of the game (i.e. things like Ebay). Here is the text of this change:

    "You may not sell or auction any EverQuest characters, items, coin orcopyrighted material."

    You may ask why we are doing this. There are many reasons, but first andforemost of them is the amount of trouble this is causing our Customer Service group. Simply put there area lot of people out there who defraud others and we are being put in the middle of it, and we don'thave the time or the resources to assist people with these disputes when they arise. The next reason is amore philosophical one and that is that we believe people should have to earn their items and characters in thegame rather than from buying them outside the game. Obviously the second point can be argued from otherperspectives that we do in fact respect, but we wanted you to hear ours.

    The next change to the User Agreement and Software license concerns ourefforts to stop people from hacking EverQuest and from doing malicious acts that we feel can affect EverQuest as anongoing business concern. We have developed the technology to check for these hacking tools/programs and report that fact back to us. Withoutgetting into the specifics of this technology, I can say that we in no way will search a user's harddrive(aside from the EverQuest directory during the patching process), registry nor will we send back any information other than the fact that auser is in fact running one of these programs (specifically we are NOT sending information about everything the user is running back to ourservers).

    It's disturbing that the amount of recent developments in the hacking community force us to do this. Many will say "ShowEQ" isn't worth worrying about. To us, it's cheating and it's not something we can allow in a game like EverQuestwhere it affects other people's enjoyment of the game. In addition, althoughthis is the first time we'll be acknowledging this, ShowEQ isn't the worstoffender. Recently we had someone attempting to bring down our servers withanother malicious program. We were able to quickly identify what was goingon and insure this didn't happen again, but nonetheless the potential isthere and we need to be able to quickly identify and stop these types ofprograms. Again, I want to say it clearly that we will in NO WAY send any information back tothe Everquest servers other than information regarding programs that we feel interfere with the intendedoperation of EverQuest. We have had this technology available for some time, but recently we've found it's going to beneccessary to go to greater lengths to stop this kind of thing. To those ofyou concerned about privacy (and rightly so) I want to say that we feel it'sneccessary to do this but we want you to be informed that we are going totry these measures to protect both ourselves and the game's players.

    Here is the text of this change:

    "You hereby grant us permission to download Game-related files toyou. You also grant us permission to access, extract and upload(i) Game-related data as part of the patching process and (ii)data relating to any program that we, in our reasonable discretion,determine interferes with the proper operation of EverQuest."

    Thanks,

    John Smedley
    President and CEO
    Verant Interactive, Inc.

Very big needs indeed. Thanks John!

I posted this on my server board (xeg)...and I'll add it here:

Quote from John Smedley:
"The next change to the User Agreement and Software license concerns our efforts to stop people from hacking EverQuest and from doing malicious acts that we feel can affect EverQuest as an ongoing business concern. We have developed the technology to check for these hacking tools/programs and report that fact back to us. "You hereby grant us permission to download Game-related files to you. You also grant us permission to access, extract and upload (i) Game-related data as part of the patching process and (ii) data relating to any program that we, in our reasonable discretion, determine interferes with the proper operation of EverQuest."


Personally, I'm rather bothered by this. Verant apparently feels that the EQ community is guilty until proven innocent by checking our hard drives while we are playing. While I can understand their security concerns, it seems extreme to me that they would go to such measures as invading personal computers and going through whatever files they see fit regardless of the assurances given by Verant.

Not only are they admitting having the ability to go through your hard drive, but they are now announcing that they will do so by going through game related files and others "at their discretion" as noted in part (ii). Now, legally, giving them permission to do so using their discretion means they can pretty much do whatever they want and frankly speaking, I don't believe for an instant that they will ultimately do what they want with whatever information they see fit to retrieve, regardless to my wishes as a paying customer.

As some of you may or may not remember, Blizzard did more or less the same thing via Battlenet to Starcraft and Diablo players and only admitted to doing it after it was brought out by former employees and players who had discovered the process. That was the last day I played Diablo or SC on Bnet.

Now, I'm about as ardently opposed to hacking as about anyone could be. I remember the hacking problems on Diablo and how it ruined the game, just as it did on OSI's UO. I feel that Verant should take appropriate measures to protect its interests and its intellectual property and hardware. That said, I DO NOT feel it is an appropriate measure to go through my files on the assumption that I may be running a hack program.

On a matter of principle, I will shortly be quitting this game. I have nothing to hide on my hard drive, but, I still am fundamentally uncomfortable with someone prowling through it. In fact, I feel it is downright wrong.

I loved the game and the friends I've made and I will miss them. I never played to level or get items or make plat, rather, I played to have fun, meet people and discover as many of the hidden facets within Norrath as I could. Its unfortunate that things have come to this, but, I knew from the beginning that the sun would set eventually on my EQ playing days. However, I also realize that the sun will still be shining in the real world, I will always have my friends; my girlfriend, family, and ferret will all still love me (in different ways of course... =p), and that there are always adventures in the real world to find.

#54   Name: jrapp ()   Date: 4/5 @ 12:30 PM PST
I would like to address the privacy concerns raised by the two additional EULA clauses as I see them as an attorney. The first clause states the following:

"You hereby grant us permission to download Game-related files to you. You also grant us permission to access, extract and upload(i) Game-related data as part of the patching process and

This clause does not define the term "Game-related data." Since the term is not defined, the user can not really know which data they are agreeing to let Verant download or upload. Unless the term is defined somewhere else in the EULA, Verant can interpret the phrase "Game-related" data as broadly as possible.

The second clause states:

"You hereby grant us permission to download Game-related files toyou. You also grant us permission to access, extract (ii)data relating to any program that we, in our reasonable discretion,determine interferes with the proper operation of EverQuest

I think the intent of Verant with this clause was to prevent programs like "ShowEQ." However, Verant's lawyer's have drafted this language so broadly as to cover data from just about any program. I really have no idea if this language allows Verant to obtain data from programs such as Excel, Word, etc. The language that gives this clause such a broad interpretation is "any program" coupled with "in our reasonable discretion." I really do not know from this language which programs I allow Verant to access data from.

I am in no way suggesting that Verant would improperly retrieve data from a user's computer. All I am suggesting is that Verant's lawyer's have drafted the EULA so broadly as to allow it to download or upload just about any data in its discretion. Put another way, if users theoretically had a lawyer representing them, there is no way that lawyer would allow such a broadly drafted clause.
