Gordon Infobits, Part 2
- Aurum @ 7:53 PM PDT
Mr. Wrinn has been busy on the boards. Since the first infobit post today he's made even more posts on the Gameplay Board. Here's his latest:

  • I Have A Cable Modem, Why Can't I Download Kunark?
    The Ruins of Kunark upgrade is huge. The problem isn't necessarily a problem with *you* downloading it, it's you and the 50K other people that would want to :)

    Though the amount of bandwidth that we have is immense, it is nonetheless finite.

  • Is there danger in binding in Kelethin trees?
    [Quote from original post: I was planning on binding myself at the druid guild in Kelethin. Is there any danger gating and rematerializing such that I fall through the floor or anything? I just don't want to die when doing a simple gate.]

    I've never heard of anyone having this problem.

  • Is the 'Male Barbarian Invisible Shield Bug" Fix in RoK?
    This is indeed fixed in the expansion files.

  • Paladin's Armor of Ro: Will it be fixed?
    [Quote from original post: OK they fixed the melatite problem, so the quests are theoretically doable, but several of the molds are still nigh-impossible to get due mainly to 6 hour + spawns. (undead cyclops and ancille cooks come to mind right now.) Just wondering when they will be fixed, if ever. Thanks!]

    There are several spawn-problems associated with the armor of Ro quests. These are scheduled to be patched when we do data next, which I'm hoping (*kneels*, *pleads*) will be early next week.

  • I Ordered 3 Copies of RoK, Why can't they all ship at once?
    Due to the automated nature of the order fulfillment process necessary to insure that orders are shipped in time, and our agreement with the fulfillment house, we only support one order per account.

    I understand that this might be a little problematic for people with multiple accounts, but this is the way we needed to do it in order to insure timely delivery.

  • Now that Verant has a "store" setup, does this mean we'll see other EQ merchandise eventually?
    It's certainly a possibility :)

  • What Servers Are Getting Split?
    [Quote from original post: Hey all, just wondering if anyone had any insite into what servers are gonna get the split in the next few weeks? I can't troll all the message boards and websites, and I'm not sure if someone (ie. Gordon, Brad, John) has given any kind of list.]

    We haven't really decided this issue yet. It's not very beneficial for us to talk about it until we have the server ready to go. We'll be sure to let you know when we've decided :)

  • Risk: Randomly Dying
    Risk is an integral part of games such as this. Now risk that can always be quantified, calculated, anticipated, and avoided is no risk at all. Hence, we have things that introduce unanticipated risk.

    A good example of this is when Cazic Thule screams "ELWITHEOS!", which is followed immediatly by your character falling over dead. It is a risk of going into the Plane of Fear.

    "Fault" really isn't an issue.

  • Questions Answered
    [Quote from original post: 1. Per a post I read recently by Gordon, I think it was, plus mana items have a built in Anti-Twink feature. Cool, I think thats good. Before I ask my question I want to say I do NOT want a formula or exact numbers. Does this feature tapper off at higher lvls, or would say even a LvL 50 be effected by this? Such as a LvL 50 INT caster with 200 INT gets more mana from an item then a LvL 50 INT caster with 180 INT]

    Though not affected directly by a stat, the amount of mana received is affected by total mana. Now since someone with 200int has more mana than someone with 180, this would have the *potential* to affect the amount of mana received from +mana items. However it is highly unlikely that someone would have so many +mana items, and so little total mana that the mana items would be scaled. The important thing here is that it does work correctly.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Did I miss a patch message somewhere or are casters suposed to come back from death from a creature with no mana? I know Duels cause you to come back OOM, but I thought we still came back with mana if a creature kills us in a regular fight. I died 2 times yesterday. Once with my necro and once with my wizard both came back OOM. Neither were dueling.]

    It depends which server you are on and how you die. As a rule, if you die in a manner that does not cause XP loss, you'll respawn with zero mana. To my knowledge, there are no exceptions to this rule.

    [Quote from original post: 3. This one is a technical and fesability question. While low priority, I know many people who would love to see more graphics for weapons, armor and such. I know you can only do so much because of memory constrants on older systems and time required to make the textures and new looks. If you had the extra man hours to make the graphics (not fancy partical effects for everything including rusties, just things like make the Staff of the Wheel look different from a water crystal staff or Different looks for rare plate armor other then color) could this be implemented as an optional download? Maybe put an option to download a few new graphics every month or two as your people have the time to draw them up. That would let your people work on it as they have time, avoid a big single download, let the people who can not or do not want to use the new graphics use the old graphics, and quite honestly do something this game is ment to do, make a lot of people happy (btw I think it already does that REALLY good) I for one love to look different and for people to be able to see that I have achieved a rare or hard to get item.]

    As we've mentioned before, EQ needs to continue running under the same system specifications as when it was released. That is what ties our hands in regards to new graphics. With the expansion, we do expand upon those graphics due to our ability to change the required system specs, however objects that can move into areas where they can be viewed by people with the old system specs must remain viewable by them. Optional patches don't work in this case, as people without the required system will download it, and then they will cease to run. For these reasons as well as others, global graphics have to remain more or less static.

    [Quote from original post: 4. do you like soft or hard shell tacos?]

    Soft. Definitely.

  • Investigate Tashan
    [Quote from original post: Currently it seems that this debuff does not lower the MR accurately ( 65 is the rumor, hard to tell in PvP because of the % being reduced ).]

    Debuff spells aren't subject to the % reduction in effectiveness. Only damage dealing spells are.

    [Quote from original post: Gordon, could you state your 'view' on the MR of high creatures ( those 49+ ? ) or could we possibly setup some type of poll to see how many enchanters feel they are an effective part of the party when dealing with planes/giants/dragons and what their feelings on MR are?]

    Certain special encounters (dragons, gods) have high resistances (to the point of being immune) to all but one type of magic. Normal monsters typically don't have the incredibly high resists across the board.

    We realize that this sometimes limits the damage-dealing potential of some classes, however those classes typically have either other ways of dealing damage (e.g. pets) or have other uses besides dealing damage (e.g. buffs).

  • Why Isn't There Cool Chest Armor for Casters?
    Generally, because we don't want pure mages to look like fighters.

#9   Name: Aslidan (j_cross@hotmail.com)   Date: 4/7 @ 8:46 AM PST
I just wanted to address a few of the comments above and throw in some of my thoughts on random uncontrollable risk:

Rupert, first off, game design is not an exact science and your quote from Ken is just his opinion. Yes, he helped to design one hell of a game, but his method and view point is not the be all and end all of game design. In addition, you are not comparing apples to apples so to speak. How can you compare a static single player experience with pre scripted events to a MMORPG? The constraints of the project are entirely different along with the goals for the final design.
Second, what's this about attacking their process? I'd say the architecture control is very sound in that it has yet to have major hacks like UO or Diablo. Some thought must have gone into its implementation to prevent this, not just random chance. Yes, they have had some problems with version control and update patches, but they have also corrected the problems and moved on. Also, your comment about developer attitude is completely off base. Could you please point me to any other game developer that has as much interaction with their players and takes such an active part in dealing with issues associated with the game? Just because they don't pander to your wants / needs / gripes doesn't mean that their attitude sucks.
Finally, when did not knowing how to play chess equate to someone being an idiot?

DamianoX, I think you are missing the deeper understanding of RP and world generation that the developers have included in the game. Since you cited D&D I'll attempt to use that as a basis for the explanation. From your post I gather your GM/DM used modules or had a fairly linear story line approach to his sessions. He/She set you a task for the game session and you went about achieving it. You expected the task to be challenging but within your capabilities to achieve. Fine, that's one way to play. However, consider the alternative of a non linear story line where you don't go from B1 Keep on the Boarder Lands, to B2 (dang, forgot the name), etc. Look at the world of Greyhawk, or the Forgotten Realms as examples of non linear story lines. You are given a world to work with and not a set path to follow. It is entirely up to you to follow a course of action that your character can achieve or run off to the Tomb of Horrors equivalent as a level 4 fighter. This gives you much more freedom in your choice of role playing actions, but also forces you to deal with the consequences instead of being shepherded into situations within your capabilities.

Finally, lets just talk about random risk and the effect it has on Everquest. You both complain that it detracts from the enjoyment of the game but I (of course) would argue just the opposite. Rupert, you referred to it as EverCamp in your post, but the random encounters are designed especially to help prevent and modify that situation. What enjoyment is there in camping a spawn you can easily handle for hours on end? None. However, how many fights do you remember where you were easily handling something then its big brother suddenly joined in, but you still won the fight? Those are the battles that people remember and make the game fun. To have them, you have to allow for the option to fail. What's the point in playing if you know the outcome of every battle you get involved with? And what about higher level monsters wondering through typical newbie areas? More stuff to keep you on your toes and provide motivation for achieving higher levels perhaps? I still remember having the Mino Lord tear through Steamfort after me as I ran for help. Yes I died, but I loved watching the higher level group that came and killed him. For that matter, I still enjoy going back and beating his a** just for old times sake. The encounter taught me not to get so involved in a combat that I don't pay attention to what's going on around me, and it gave me something to strive for instead of just level X.

Oh well, didn't mean to write a book.

Oh BTW the reason uncontrollable risk is a problem:

When you die you lose experience. When I was playing I often only had 5 hours a week to play. When I died I lost on average 1-2 hours worth of work (at level 20+). This means that I spend my time leveling only to die and be back where I was.

Because I'm a game designer, and I get my joys out of designing games, I could spend that five hours doing something meaningful that ISN'T WASTED because of something I cannot avoid.

Bottom line: you cannot avoid dying, thus you are penalized for something you cannot avoid. See you cannot have both. You either have death be common and the penalty inconsequential, or you have death rarely and the penalty very consequential. In EQ they have death common AND the penalty consequential.

This is why people say this is a hard-core player-only game. Because those of us without the hours cannot get very far past a certain point (around level 15-20) without sacrificing our job, our marriage, our family, or our other hobbies so that we can add more hours to this game.

Needless to say I don't consider the money I paid a complete waste, EQ has refined my game design knowledge by a VAST amount. Too bad the knowledge cost them my $9 a month.
