PC Gamer Previes EQ II
- Aurum @ 9:45 PM PDT
PC Gamer has taken an upcoming look at EverQuest II exploring everything from the graphics to the gameplay mechanics. An excerpt from this preview follows:

    Aside from letting you own these vehicles, EQII will also introduce player-owned property. Buy a plot of land and build a one-room (upgradeable) building, then invite friends to locate nearby. After a set level, the game will introduce a guard tower complete with a patrolling guard, plus other civic buildings as the community grows. With a set number of big structures available in the world, you can be sure power-players will perform a land rush at EQII’s launch.
EverQuest II is slated to launch late next year.

Bazaar Functionality Follow-up
- Aurum @ 6:49 PM PDT
Alan returned to the bazaar topic on the Developer's Corner to respond to the feedback sent in already:

    Thanks for all the feedback so far. Keep it coming.

    olavf-saryrn wrote:

    First, let me qualify this by saying I don't have a charater on test server so I am sending this in hopes you can pass it on to the dev team.

    2 issues come to mind that you might want to look into.

    First, the traders sachels weigh 25 stone empty, that is 200 stone in weight before anyone even puts objects into them....How will characters be able to move with full packs?

    Second...what if I want to sell 10 slot tailor made backpacks, or smithed tool kits, or any other containers? Can containers be placed in containers in /trader mode?

    Just a couple of thoughts.....

    Weight isn’t an issue in the Bazaar. When people carrying too much weight started to die going down a step, we took out a lot of the restrictions for weight in that zone. :)

    Bags can’t go into bags. There are two reasons for this. One is that it would lead to infinite storage capacity. Just as importantly, putting containers inside containers was known to cause the world to collapse back when it was possible (all sorts of ‘bad things’ would happen to characters and their stuff). And, well, nobody really wants that.

    For the PoTC quest there is a great demand for foraged items so my first thought was to try and sell these.
    If I set my character to sell food will the character starve or eat his/her profits away ?
    Does the old rule of the food in the first inventory slot being eaten first still apply ?

    Hugs
    Mellissa
    Druidess of Tunare on Tunare

    As far as I know, your character will continue to eat in the normal fashion. So you might have to worry about eating yourself out of business. At least folks selling baked goods will be eating well while they work.

    RZ_Ceric wrote:

    Are items only in the trade satchels available for people to buy?

    What if you have something in the bag that you forgot to price, will that be sellible for some default price?

    Do the bags have to be in your inventory or can they be in the bank?

    Only items in trade satchels can be sold, and those satchels must be on a character that is standing in a stall. So, no, you can’t sell things that are in the bank, and you’ll have to remember to get the items you want to sell into a satchel and priced.

    Alan

New Trade Federation - Karana
- Aurum @ 9:27 PM PDT
Tithonus brings us word of a new trade federation being started on Karana:

    Greetings Tradesmen of Karana!

    As many of you know, the Karana server is one of the few original servers to open in 1999, and since then the Karana economy has developed and blossomed to the bustling and busy state it is in today.

    My name is Tithonus Arcanus and I am pleased to announce the creation of a new Guild/Community Organization for the Karana server called the Norrathian Trade Federation. Our goal is to preserve and enforce the legendary Karana economy, and in doing so help those new players who cannot afford what they desire to create savings accounts with us that will accumulate interest. We will operate and function like a normal guild, but we will also host a Karana Community trading forum online, similar to the one hosted by KCoG, but more specific to the needs and wants of the tradesmen of Norrath.

    Anybody who plays on Karana can become a local member of the forum simply by posting, no applications are needed for use of the trading categories. On the site the Karana community can trade or sell items, quests or services to each other, or browse items quest opportunities and services that the Trade Federation will be offering players of Karana.

    If you would like to begin posting on the Forum, or just browse! (The more participants the better!) You can check us out at:

    http://go.to/tradekarana

    Thank you for your time and happy trading!

    -Tithonus Arcanus
    Leader of the NTF
    Proud citizen of Karana
Thank you!


Board Thread of the Day
- Moony @ 5:50 AM PDT
My favorite ogre wrote a letter that inspired today's Thread of the Day. Has EQ had a positive or negative impact of a relationship in your real life? Are you with someone, or no longer with someone as a result of playing?

Below is the thought provoking letter. Thank you Howse Ogre for making me laugh once more!

Yargh. Hi der Jeff Green. I am Howse Ogre. One of ma stoopid dorfs wanted me send you dis lettur him wrote from inside ma backpack.

OOC

Speak on brother! I’ve never been compelled to reply to an article in a magazine because if I have 45 minutes on the computer, I would rather work on my tradeskills in EQ or solo a few monsters with my 44 Beastlord. However, as the husband of a non-gaming wife, your Greenspeak “SimHusband” CGW August 2002, warrants a response from me. I am a Major in the United States Air Force and during the evening’s I am Howse Ogre, the mojo merchant with his backpack of stoopid dorfs. My wife doesn’t play computer games (FreeCell doesn’t count) and I venture to say I or Everquest have caused her to loathe them. In order to maintain a somewhat stable household and to avoid coming home from work to find the computer in the trash and computer disks broken, I now have a “family friendly” EQ schedule: Monday, Wednesday, occasional Friday and a “raid” on the weekend. When we met, some 7 odd years ago, I didn’t own a computer, and I was a bona fide club hopper. We partied, that is what we did…but the computer gamer in me was always there; just sort of repressed as I lived in my “informative” years.

My wife is under the opinion that my “newfound” joy in computer games is a recent phenomenon-it’s not. I grew up typing (unsuccessfully) games into my Vic 20, competing in arena events in Auto Duel on my Apple IIe, and recording reams of NPC conversation in the Ultima series. I’ve owned the gamut of platforms from a Vic 20 to a TRS-80 to the Amiga and Apple IIe to my current rig, AMD Athlon. So this computer gaming thing was not an overnight, “Wow, check out the graphics on that xxxx” during a stroll through Wal-Mart.

I have earnestly tried to get the wife interested in anything fantasy related in hopes that she may convert. I built her a Magic the Gathering deck based on Angels because she likes Angels, I created her an Everquest character named Vogue and attempted to get her to play Grim Fandango. However, I’ve given up hope of converting her after a recent comment she made regarding a recent movie I saw, Lord of the Rings. She asked me, “So, how was Lord of the Apes”. /e sighs.

Now, I wont go so far to say that I am making progress, I still get my subscription to Computer Gaming World, I haven’t got any looks from sitting at the computer typing this letter on my non-computer day…yet, and she showed interest when I told her I won the roll for a Temple of Veeshan BP. But the most progress comes from her chuckles when she reads Greenspeak. Jeff (I feel I can call you that since we seem to be computer gaming kin) what I need you to do is put some subliminal messages in your Greenspeak article. Something that will cause the non-gamer in the dentist waiting room to read your article, and decide to go out and buy a computer and the latest game. Because, lord knows, I wouldn’t know what to do if the wife called me at work oneday and said, “Baby, DING! I just got level 15 with my gnome shadow knight. Uh-oh, damn, I just died to Froglok in Upper Guk. Can you take some leave and come home and help me on the corpse recovery. As a matter of fact, dear, let’s get a second computer so we can play together when the boy goes to bed…” /e drools


- Howse Ogre and him stoopid dorfs

Poll: How many pairs of boots do you have in your inventory?
- Moony @ 6:39 AM PDT
I was packing my bags to fly to California for vacation and faced a mind-numbing dilemma....which shoes should I take? As I looked at my outfits and at the line of shoes that begged to caress my feet along the shores of sunny CA, I gave pause to reflect on how many pairs of boots my characters have in their inventory.

Picking shoes for a vacation isn't quite as detailed as picking boots for a raid. After all, I don't have to worry about which resists I'll need, or if I want more AC, dex, or HP. I did have to consider some of the same questions my fashion challenged characters face though. Do these shoes match my outfit? And let us not forget...do these shoes make my feet look big?

(Aurum asked me if I forgot to pack the sunscreen on vacation. He seems to think I'm suffering from sun stroke or something.)

We're thrilled to announce that 'AVATARS OFFLINE', the first full-length documentary on the MMORPG phenomenon, is now available for purchase on DVD exclusively at www.avatarsoffline.com.

- The First Couple of EverQuest: They met in Beta Phase I and are now happily married.

- Addiction Controversy: Can games be made to be addictive? What constitutes addiction?

- The Cross-Gender Phenomenon: Female Wood Elves with a twist!

- Dark Age of Camelot: Meet Mark Jacobs, Sanya 'Tweety' Thomas and Scott 'LumTheMad' Jennings.

- Lineage The Bloodpledge: 2 million subscribers and counting.

- Star Wars Galaxies: See the Star Wars universe come alive in amazing footage and go behind the scenes with Haden 'Shug_Nix' Blackman and Tom Sarris!

And much more!

We're very excited about finally being able to share the film with the public and look forward to the community's feedback!

Sincerely,

Daniel Liatowitsch
director, "Avatars Offline"

New Interface Up On Test
- Aurum @ 9:22 PM PDT
Alan sent us news that the new interface is up on the Test Server by way of the Developer's Corner:

    ** Monday, June 24 2002.

    Welcome to the open beta phase of testing for EverQuest's new interface!

    It still has a few rough edges and some pieces that aren't complete, but after using it in-house for a couple weeks, we'd like to see how well it does in front of a larger audience.

    By now, you've downloaded all of the graphics and layout files. If you choose to run the new interface, please edit your EQCLIENT.INI file and, under the defaults section, add the line:

    NewUI=TRUE

    ...and feel free to have a look around!

    As always, your feedback is welcomed through the Developer's Corner.


    * Random new toys worth checking out in the new interface:

    - Item Inspection: Alt-Leftclick now pops up the inspect box and keeps it there.

    - Quantity Selection: You can now type numbers when referring to stackable items and coins.

    - Key Mapper: Every top level window still has a hotkey you can use to open and close it, and all of the keys can still be remapped through the Options screeen. (The hex nut icon in the window selector, or Alt-O by default). To turn the window selector itself on and off, the default key is Alt-W.

    - Mousewheel: ...should automatically work in every UI screen that has a scroll bar. (including Chat, Merchants, Looting, etc.)


    * For those interested in developing custom skins for the interface:

    You'll notice that there is now a directory called UIFiles beneath your EverQuest install directory.

    The default skin (aka "The New UI") is stored in the directory called, predictably "default," and we've included a document called "AboutSIDL.doc" in the UIFiles directory.

    If you make yourself a new skin, please create a separate directory for it in the UIFiles directory. Once the skin is in there, you can switch to it by typing "/loadskin "

    Working with the skins at this point is not an activity for the faint of heart, but if you're inclined to hack around in those files, AboutSIDL will hopefully help out a bit.

    This one we definitely can't just sign "- The EverQuest Development Team."

    Many, many people have contributed to this effort, be it artwork, technology, or suggestions.

    Mobhunter Interviews Verant/SOE's Scott Hartsman
    - Moony @ 4:34 AM PDT
    Verant/SOE's Technical Director, Scott Hartsman answered a few questions for an interview with Mobhunter Here is just a taste:

    One long-standing complaint of EQ players is the pathing of the monsters. Can you explain how pathing works, and how it can go bad? Can we ever look forward to a redesign of pathing in EQ, or will we have to wait for future Verant products?

    If I had to give a definitive answer, I'd have to say... "It's magic!" ..and then you'd probably look at me funny. I don't want to launch into a full-on explanation of how our pathing works, other than to say that pathing is an attribute of the zone, not the individual NPC. The thing that makes a mob path badly is that generally it has faulty information on the shortest distance between point A and point B.

    A better system could make up for that sort of thing, and we'd love to make tweaks like that, but it's hard without breaking the whole thing in new and exciting ways. We definitely recognize it as an area that's ripe for improvement. I wouldn't rule it out as something we'd really like to look at.

    The hard part is to make a better system that would take all of the existing pathing work into account. (It wouldn't do us much good to write a system that would cause every zone in the game to need to be repathed - That would be an even more immense undertaking.) I sincerely hope we have the time to improve this at some point.
