EQ Real-time Strategy Game & Console Project Announced
- Aurum @ 9:17 PM PDT
The world of Norrath continues to grow and expand in exciting new directions. In an announcement released today, Sony Online Entertainment revealed plans to develop an EverQuest RTS game and begin work on an EverQuest console title with two new developers respectively.

Here is the press release:

    SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT PARTNERS WITH LEADING GAME DEVELOPERS TO BRING PLAYERS THE BEST IN GAME ENTERTAINMENT
    -Leader in Massively Multiplayer Online Gaming To Bring New Genres of Gaming to Consoles and PCs-

    SAN DIEGO, CA  July 18, 2002  Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming, today announced deals with Snowblind Studios Inc. and Rapid Eye Entertainment Inc. to create original content for the PC and console markets under the SOE label. Rapid Eye Entertainment is currently developing a real-time strategy, PC-based EverQuest game while Snowblind Studios is developing an EverQuest-based product for the PlayStation 2 computer entertainment system. Further product information will be announced at a later date.

    "We've made it a point to work closely with leading developers to create the best possible gaming experience," said Russell Shanks, Senior Vice President and Chief Technology Officer, Sony Online Entertainment. "As we expand our product line into new genres, our mission is to deliver compelling content and online capabilities to PC and console systems worldwide."

    Combining the experience and the expertise of the SOE development team with other top developers opens the market to new possibilities in gaming. By expanding into new genres of gaming, SOE is well poised to take advantage of the rapidly growing market that recently broke new ground in entertainment by surpassing movie box office receipts.

    Founded by Mark Caldwell in June 2001, Rapid Eye Entertainment (www.rapideyegames.com) is a game developer dedicated to expanding gameplay and pioneering new game genres. Caldwell was an original member and leader of the team behind the highly acclaimed Might and Magic series of role-playing games and the Heroes of Might and Magic series of turn-based strategy games. Headquartered in San Diego, Calif., the company's initial focus is a unique twist in the real-time strategy category.

    "Rapid Eye is dedicated to working on the cutting edge of gaming," said Mark Caldwell, founder and president, Rapid Eye Entertainment. "Our partnership with Sony Online Entertainment allows us to work on projects that will change the face of gaming, delivering great new content to a broader group of gamers."

    Snowblind Studios, best known for the development of Baldur's Gate: Dark Alliance, a widely successful role playing game for the PlayStation 2 system, is an award-winning entertainment software company founded by industry veterans Ryan Geithman and Brian Sostrom in 1997. Based in Bellevue, Washington, Snowblind Studios has developed titles for Nintendo 64 and the PlayStation 2 system.

    "EverQuest has taken the world by storm," said Ryan Geithman, president, Snowblind Studios. "We're excited to be working with Sony Online Entertainment to expand the EverQuest phenomenon to new platforms and new audiences."

Never Give Out Your Password
- Aurum @ 9:39 PM PDT
The scammers have drawn the ire of one Alan VanCouvering today. Hear what he had to say about it in the lone update made to the Developer's Corner:

    Let me start by saying this: Never give out your password.

    I certainly admit that the scammers are getting more creative, and they’ve learned how to spell.

    The latest scam (which I can’t display properly here) is an offer of a free copy of Ruins of Kunark. This is a pretty clever scam. They’ve copied the art and our web site to present a reasonable looking email, represented as coming from our mailing list.

    There are two key things about this scam that identify it as such.

    The first is the email address that it was sent from. The copy I have here was sent from “Freeq account [eqaccountstatus@vgnmail.com]”. It isn’t too hard to spoof an email address and make it appear to be from a Sony Online email address, so this won’t always be a clue. Just because it has an email address that appears to be from us doesn’t mean it isn’t a scam. In this case, however, the fact that the email address is definitely not ours is a good hint that something is up.

    The second, and most important clue, is that it asks for your EverQuest account information. The key to these scams is always to get your account name and password. Never give out your password.

    I suspect that those of you that read this board or one of the fan sites, that you already know never to give out your password. So I’d like to ask you to do me a favor. Warn a friend. It’s never fun to hear that a friend lost all of his or her stuff because of one of these scams.

    Never give out your password.

    Here is the text of the scam (at least they were kind enough to link to our store… ). And in a boldly ironic move, they linked to the EULA.

Bazaar Functionality Follow-up
- Aurum @ 6:49 PM PDT
Alan returned to the bazaar topic on the Developer's Corner to respond to the feedback sent in already:

    Thanks for all the feedback so far. Keep it coming.

    olavf-saryrn wrote:

Yargh. Hi der Jeff Green. I am Howse Ogre. One of ma stoopid dorfs wanted me send you dis lettur him wrote from inside ma backpack.

OOC

Speak on brother! I’ve never been compelled to reply to an article in a magazine because if I have 45 minutes on the computer, I would rather work on my tradeskills in EQ or solo a few monsters with my 44 Beastlord. However, as the husband of a non-gaming wife, your Greenspeak “SimHusband” CGW August 2002, warrants a response from me. I am a Major in the United States Air Force and during the evening’s I am Howse Ogre, the mojo merchant with his backpack of stoopid dorfs. My wife doesn’t play computer games (FreeCell doesn’t count) and I venture to say I or Everquest have caused her to loathe them. In order to maintain a somewhat stable household and to avoid coming home from work to find the computer in the trash and computer disks broken, I now have a “family friendly” EQ schedule: Monday, Wednesday, occasional Friday and a “raid” on the weekend. When we met, some 7 odd years ago, I didn’t own a computer, and I was a bona fide club hopper. We partied, that is what we did…but the computer gamer in me was always there; just sort of repressed as I lived in my “informative” years.

My wife is under the opinion that my “newfound” joy in computer games is a recent phenomenon-it’s not. I grew up typing (unsuccessfully) games into my Vic 20, competing in arena events in Auto Duel on my Apple IIe, and recording reams of NPC conversation in the Ultima series. I’ve owned the gamut of platforms from a Vic 20 to a TRS-80 to the Amiga and Apple IIe to my current rig, AMD Athlon. So this computer gaming thing was not an overnight, “Wow, check out the graphics on that xxxx” during a stroll through Wal-Mart.

I have earnestly tried to get the wife interested in anything fantasy related in hopes that she may convert. I built her a Magic the Gathering deck based on Angels because she likes Angels, I created her an Everquest character named Vogue and attempted to get her to play Grim Fandango. However, I’ve given up hope of converting her after a recent comment she made regarding a recent movie I saw, Lord of the Rings. She asked me, “So, how was Lord of the Apes”. /e sighs.

Now, I wont go so far to say that I am making progress, I still get my subscription to Computer Gaming World, I haven’t got any looks from sitting at the computer typing this letter on my non-computer day…yet, and she showed interest when I told her I won the roll for a Temple of Veeshan BP. But the most progress comes from her chuckles when she reads Greenspeak. Jeff (I feel I can call you that since we seem to be computer gaming kin) what I need you to do is put some subliminal messages in your Greenspeak article. Something that will cause the non-gamer in the dentist waiting room to read your article, and decide to go out and buy a computer and the latest game. Because, lord knows, I wouldn’t know what to do if the wife called me at work oneday and said, “Baby, DING! I just got level 15 with my gnome shadow knight. Uh-oh, damn, I just died to Froglok in Upper Guk. Can you take some leave and come home and help me on the corpse recovery. As a matter of fact, dear, let’s get a second computer so we can play together when the boy goes to bed…” /e drools


- Howse Ogre and him stoopid dorfs
