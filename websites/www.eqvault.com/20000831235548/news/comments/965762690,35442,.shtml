Gordon: Why doesn't pressing a hotkey activate an item?

That said, the idea for the key to operate as a right-click for activatable items is a good one, and I'll forward it to the programmer who's handling UI changes.

Gordon Infobits
- ChrisLTD @ 1:51 PM PDT
Here you will finda summary of the posts made by Abashi on the Official EverQuest Forum:


My experience with the "New" Verant Customer Service
[Quote from original post: So I ask you Abashi, you call this "fair" treatment? Consistent treatment? You ask for names all the time I give you names.]

The GMs have to do the best job they can given the information available to them at the time. As I wasn't there at the time, I really can't comment upon what actually happened. However, I think that it would be prudent to say that if everything were as clear in game as your accounting of it seems, then there would have been no reason for you to come to the boards with this.

Things, however, are rarely this clear in practice. Had decisions gone in another direction, there would most likely have been someone from the other side of it here to take issue with the way it was handled. The PvP environments are very difficult to manage. One would think that people on those environments would be more open to conflicts with other players, however that's not the case. A full 90% of petitions on those servers deal with some type of PvP harassment issue. As I said in the begining, GMs have to make the best decision that they can given the information at hand at the time.


Beautiful commentary on Verant's "it's a grouping game" logic
[Quote from original post: Why do you assume that people won't interact unless they're 1/6th of a siamese sextuplet?]

We don't, we assume that they will "group" even if the game mechanics don't support it. Once this happens, if the game doesn't support and provide challenge for the group, they won't enjoy themselves.

Since they (being the majority of player) are going to group anyway, we decided to make sure that the game engine was primarily in support of it.


Planning ahead
[Quote from original post: Why is the code so unflexible that your only answer is "That isn't possible" every time someone suggests an improvement or change?]

It's not inflexble, it's just not infinitely flexible. At some point in development you have to begin placing limits on what you want to accomplish. If you don't place those limits on what the software will handle, you end up with "feature creep", where feature after feature is layered onto the product until the point that forward progress stops. One decision often affects several other subordinate decisions.

Certainly, it's easy to look back a year down the line and say that several things should have been done. Afterall, hindsight is 20/20. As it is, we can do nearly anything with the code. The question remains, do the benefits for what we could do outweigh the time, effort, and the problems that result from doing it. Sometimes that answer is no.


Zone camping at the teleport points.
[Quote from original post: As I understand zone boundry camping and teleport location camping is in violation of the current rules.
Is there anything that can be done to remove these individuals that continuously do this?]

This behavior is not allowed on the PvP servers. If it's happening, you should /petition to get the incident witnessed. Assuming that it can be witnessed, appropriate action will be taken.

[Quote from original post: Does a character enter the game before control is available?]

Only in cases of excess internet latency.

[Quote from original post: Serveral Guilds are known to condone this behavior.]

If that's found to be the case, the guild will be disbanded.


Annoying thing about /hidecorpses
The command simply stops the client from drawing the corpses. They technically still exist even though you can't see them. This was necessary in order to have corpse-related spells and commands continue working.


Gordon, please add grouping level rules to FAQ
[Quote from original post: I've never been able to find a definitive answer to the question "who can group with who and still get XP?". Could you please add this to the FAQ? The rule I've heard most is everyone will get XP if the highest group member level is 1.5 times the lowest group member level or less, or if the difference is 3 or less.]

The easiest way to tell is to kill something. If everyone gets a "gained experience" message, then they all get their full share of experience. Contrary to popular belief, experience isn't scaled after the fact.


Abashi, Why are these 2 spells like this?
[Quote from original post: Invoke fear is a level 34-49 spell (depending on class) and takes 100 mana with a cast time of 2.5 seconds, and has a maximum duration of 42 seconds.
Trepidation, level 56-57, takes 200 mana, with the same cast time as invoke fear, but lasts for a maximum of only 36 seconds. Why is this? The later version (which takes more mana, and has the same cast time) actually lasts for a shorter amount of time, and has no benefits over the older one.]

There are other ways that spells can be improved beyond that done by casting time, duration, and mana usage. There is something about Trepidation that makes it better than Invoke Fear.


Abashi Why do dark races get so little option on classes?
[Quote from original post: Abashi Why do dark races get so little option on classes?
If you look at it. light races can play any single class they want to play. Human SK human Necro. but i notice that dark races get left out. they get left out in the hybrid aspect very badly. here is why.]

"light" races accounts for 3/4ths of the races available in game. It would make sense for them to have more classes available to them. Of course it's not really fair to include humans in the list as they can be anything but a shaman.

In any case, the game really isn't "light" vs. "dark". There isn't an alignment system that differentiates between the concepts of "good" and "evil". The faction system in game is far more complex than that, and makes more sense overall. Would dark elves consider themselves evil? Probably not. From their point of view the high elves probably look evil. Ogres and Trolls just don't have very discriminating diets, and there's no "evil" in consuming your prey. But I digress...

The class restrictions were decided based on what made sense, and what would be balanced. This places restrictions on what combo you can be.


Mana Regen ABASHI please read
[Quote from original post: So before you say that it will ruin the balance of the game by putting mana regen items in the game, look around and see what kind of hitpoint regen items you have out there. Hitpoints are the same thing to a Tank, as Mana is to casters. WHy not give an enchanter a Complete Mana reload spell that would act like a Complete Heal?]

HP Regen and Mana Regen are two different things. Mana is, in the end, the standard that defines what groups can do and how often they can do it. HP regen is really only important in the absense of mana, as with mana you can instantly heal someone.

If you want to increase the rate at which you regen mana, you'll have to see one of the classes that can help you in that department.




Question About EQ Graphics
The world has to look the same (e.g. same geometry, same textures, same models, etc.) for everyone who can view the particular thing in question at any given time. With Kunark, we decided to increase the quality of the graphics, which is why we increased the system specs for the Kunark zones. EQ Proper, however, must remain the same in order to support those having the original system specs.
