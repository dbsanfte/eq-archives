Gordon Infobits, Part 2
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:09 PM PDT
Gordon has made several more posts since the first infobit post today. Here's what he's posted since the original news post:

  • More on International Shipping
    [Quote from original post: I can order RoK and have it shipped to a friend in the USA for $7, he can then send it to me for $10-$15, and it will get to me as fast (or faster) as the $57 S&H quoted to Australia. Does Verant have any stance on this - is it perfectly ok for international customers to do this (ie no legality issues)?]

    We wouldn't have any problem with this. Our responsibility for the package would end upon it arriving at your friends in the USA. What he does with it after that is up to him.

    As for your other questions, I believe Brad's post (part of which you've quoted) contains all of the information available to us at present. I apologize that I'm unable to elaborate further.

  • Kerra Isle Revamp
    The revamp of Kerra will be going to the live servers tomorrow morning. There have been quite a few new things done with this zone. As for your specific questions, we prefer to leave them up to the players to figure out. I will say that we didn't overlook anything in regards to the Paw of Opalla, but I can't say whether or not it will still be obtainable after the zone goes live.

  • Why is CON not adjusted for group size?
    There's a learning process. Players have to learn how to react in certain situations, and those decisions directly affect the difficulty of an encounter. For instance, I've seen a group of 18 attack and kill a dragon. I've also seen a group of 40+ fail to kill the same dragon. There's no way that an automated system can take group size and tactics into account and always provide an accurate /con statement, nor would we want it to.
    --
    Realizing that my list message might not have been too clear, by saying "nor would we want it to", I meant that we don't want to tell you in advance what the outcome of a battle will be to a mathematical certainty.

    The /con message should represent the difficulty of an encounter assuming that the entity engaging in it is using the appropriate complement of people and tactics. Sometimes a solo player is the appropriate complement, sometimes it's a group.

  • Icon Issues
    Your point is well-received. Though the looks of those items aren't to be changed at this juncture, our team has a directive to avoid using high level item graphics for lower level items going forward.

  • Future Server Splits
    We haven't yet commented on what servers will be slated for split. We won't likely do so until the servers are ready to go.

  • Expansion Spell: Flaming Sword of Xuzl
    Though I can't vouch for everyone's individual tastes, everyone around here thinks that it's pretty cool :)

  • When will the retail version be on shelves and what races can the Iksar be?
    The retail version is expected to go on sale in stores on 4/24, however I can't answer your question about classes at this time.

  • Data Patch Tomorrow
    We are indeed including data (items, spawns, etc.) in the maintenance tomorrow.

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:14 PM PDT
Verant's Gordon Wrinn has made several new posts on the Gameplay Board today addressing several topics and issues. Here's a quick rundown of them:

  • Caster Cap Question
    [Quote from original post: Just wented to know if you can tell us whether or not the int "cap" of 200 (and similar stats) will be raised in the expansion.]

    Casters will continue to get more mana per level, but there's no reason to raise the "int cap" that people speak of.

  • Why Should Anyone Under Level 40 Get RoK?
    There will definitely be areas that you fight in and explore. We're starting an entirely new race over there, so lower level areas were a must. The new lands aren't exclusively high level.

  • Server Limit
    1800 was the target that we agreed on pre-expansion, and we have no plans to raise that target after the expansion is released. We're pretty confident that the 4 new servers that we are bringing up (2 of which will be populated by allowing people from the more crowded servers to move to them) will help aleviate the existing overcrowding problems.

    We will continue to address these and other issues as quickly as we can.

  • Skin Like Steel and Bravery Bug?
    [Quote from original post: I have to tell them NOT to cast SLS while I'm in the group, or they will be hurting, not helping. Shouldn't SLS be cancelled by bravery? Or is there a component of SLS that I don't know that causes it to be stronger? (damage absorption, duration, some non-obvious number, because clearly SLS is inferior when comparing to bravery in hit points).]

    The spell hierarchy compares all of the effects of the spell to determine the cases where one spell overwrites another. In this particular case, it sounds like the duration of Skin Like Steel is causing it to overwrite Bravery.

    That said, it's not unusual to have to educate other classes that you group with on the capabilities of your class. My character, a necromancer, is sure to remind druids to avoid snare and other casters to refrain from root. Enchanters frequently have to remind all of their party members not to attack the mezed critters. In this case, you may have to remind druids above your level to forget SLS while in your group.

  • Kunark Spells
    I should also clarify that we won't be commenting on them until a reasonable number of people have them and what they *really* do is widely known. This probably won't happen for some time after the expansion is released.

  • Rogues can be banned for pickpocketing?
    No, they can't. I discussed this issue with the customer service team today, and they are in the process of getting the word out to the GMs and Guides to be sure that everyone is clear.

    What rogues (or anyone for that matter) could be banned for is being anti-social and trying to cause problems, though these actions have to be judged at the time that they are committed.

    For instance, there's no problem with you pickpocketing MobX. However if you chose to following around a group emoting "/em steals 3 platinum from a_gnoll_scout00", while taunting them in /say with "Haha, I'm stealing all your money", thus trying to ruin their gameplay experience, it could be considered harassment.

  • Verant.com Updated
    We recently updated our web site at www.verant.com. We have quite a few job listings posted, as well as some other interesting information. We've also posted our "Featured Site" for the next week or so, da Grobb Skwad, a well done guild site for those greener of skin.

  • Give Clerics a Turn Undead Ability
    Well we won't have this in the form that you ask, but there is a pretty nifty anti-undead spell that clerics can look forward to at later levels. I apologize, but as it is part of the expansion, I can't really comment on it quite yet.

Brad Reveals Info about Stats!
[23 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:20 PM PDT
Brad McQuaid had a big change of heart regarding Verant's policy toward releasing information about stats. Here's his post directly off the Gameplay board:

    In working with the people doing the strategy guide for Kunark we decided to provide them with general information as to what the stats do.

    I'm going to post them here.

    This is a change in the sense that we were not going to discuss them or release more info. We changed our minds.

    That said, we're not posting any formulas, and it's absolutely true that some stats have considerably more direct impact on gameplay than others.

    We're posting this for clarification, and because previous explanations have not been accurate (manual, etc). We will not, however, engage in debate and our stance on how much some stats affect game play is that it's by design to inhibit some aspects of twinking. Are there better attribute systems out there? Yeah, but we're not going to change something as fundamental as a stat system in EQ after the game's been out for more than a year.

    Anyway, here you go:

    DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows

    STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

    STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

    INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights

    WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

    CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

    AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

    -Brad

Thanks Huntre and Shadow!

Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 2:35 PM PDT
Verant I-Net Relations Manager Gordon Wrinn spent some time on the Gameplay board this weekend. Here's this weekend's round of infobits:

  • Any plans to implement a quest to change starting stats?
    We have no plans for this. It was part of the core design of EverQuest that base stats would be fixed.

  • The Consider
    The /con system is an indication of level. It makes no note of special skills, abilities, or attacks. For example, some encounters of a specific level might be more difficult than others.

    As people level up, they get stronger. However NPCs get stronger on a steeper scale. For this reason, the /con system goes from being representative of solo abilities to being representative of group abilities. The rate at which this happens varies for all of the classes.

    NPCs of a higher level MUST be stronger than a solo player, or groups will tear through them with no problems and no risk. For this reason, the /con system is not broken. At lower levels it fairly well represents the abilities of a solo player. At upper levels, it very closely represents the risk to a full balanced group.

  • On releasing Kunark early for those who ordered on net
    Actually I think it was Brad that mentioned that. We're still aiming for it, but it's too soon to guarantee it, so the official date is still 4/24.

  • Can you tell me how to get Kunark?
    There will be two ways to get to Kunark. Teleports and ships. If you haven't purchased Kunark, the teleports just won't work for you, and if you are on the boat, you'll be transported back to the dock when the boat zones to a Kunark zone.

  • Why Even Have Zones?
    Massively Multiplayer RPGs have a long development cycle compared to other games, and the graphics engine is the first thing that is designed. Back when development started on EQ, the development team had several goal for operation of the game. For example, they wanted to minimize the effect of internet latency. They also wanted to have each "area" have its own look-and-feel, with its own set of appropriatly placed NPCs.

    At the time, the "Zone" technology was the best technology available to meet the goals of the development team. Of course since then, new technologies have been developed, and though it makes sense that we would take advantage of them in future products, EQ will always have zones. You just can't change graphics engines on the fly post-production.

  • Blacksmithing Bug?
    The "Block of Ore" listed in the blacksmithing manual is just for the purpose of scaling measurements. For instance, the book says "3 large bricks = 1 block", therefore when a recipe calls for "a block of ore", you can substitue "3 large bricks".

    You can't combine 3 large bricks to make 1 block. 3 bricks is the equivalent of 1 block.

  • Are Ogre's "unstunablitiy" by design?
    Yes, this is by design. It is one of the advantages of their large size that offsets the disadvantages. I think that it could be arguable that they should be stunnable while shrunk, but it might be too late to see that if it isn't there already.

  • Summon Corpse Nefed?
    I'm not sure where the rumor came from, but the spell hasn't been changed to self-only. As a precaution to insure that something inadvertent didn't happen, one of our testers checked it out. Nothing has changed with the spell.
