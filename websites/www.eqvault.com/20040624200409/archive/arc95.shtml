Patch message: 08-01-00
[6 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:20 PM PDT
New messages are now up on the EQ Patch Server. Here they are:

    August 1st, 7:00am
    ------------------

    **Patch Day**

    Greetings everyone!

    Today's patch contains some changes that we are sure everyone will enjoy:

    - Canned emotes (e.g. /point) now work on NPCs. In addition, the range for canned emotes has been increased.
    - /who all "guildname" should now work for players who have /roleplay turned on.
    - Preferences for the mouselook will be saved in your options file. Those who turn mouselook off each time when starting EQ will only have to do it once more.
    - The "Chat Filter" tab in "Options" now has an option to filter messages caused by damage shields hitting others.

    *Fast Drop*

    Our most exciting change today is an option that allows players to avoid accidentally dropping items on the ground. The new client allows players to set "Item Dropping" preferences on the "General" options tab. The three options that can be set are:

    - FAST DROP: Items are dropped without confirmation.
    - CONFIRM: A confirmation box will be displayed whenever you try to drop an item.
    - NEVER: You are not allowed to drop items on the ground.

    Alternatively, you can also set these options using the /fastdrop command. The arguments are:

    - ON: Same as option "Fast Drop" on the General Tab.
    - OFF: Same as option "Confirm" on the General Tab.
    - NEVER: Same as above.

    Please note that whatever your setting, coin will always be dropped without confirmation. Additionally, the FastDrop option only applies to items being dropped upon the ground. Handing items to NPCs will still work regardless of your FastDrop setting.

    It is highly recommended that you use option "CONFIRM", as GMs will not reimburse any items that are accidentally dropped.

    - The EverQuest Team

    July 29, 4:30pm
    ---------------

    *EMERGENCY PATCH*

    Due to problems encountered in the most recent patch, you may temporarily see problems with spell icons displaying for certain stackable spells. We apologize for the inconvenience. This issue will be addressed in the next patch.

    - The EverQuest Team


Gordon Infobits
[8 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:16 PM PDT
The changes to the official boards are now in place and Gordon has been busy answering questions about them. Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Board Policy Question Answered
    This board is incredibly active, so I understand where you are coming from. The anti-bumping language in the rules of conduct is meant to apply more to the person who will go around to a dozen threads on the same subject and keep them bumped to the front constantly.

    The moderators will certainly give the benefit of the doubt to everyone here since the thread-index almost encourages it.

  • You CAN post with an inactive account?
    [Quote from original post: I have access to a second account which for now I have let lapse. Payment ran out on it on July 25 and it can still post. I was going to leave a message with it but that account was used by my nephew and his Chat name was too close to his user name... No use tempting anyone.... )]

    We're checking into why this might be the case now. It's probably a problem with information synchronizing with the database on the message board.

    Thanks for the info.

  • Acceptable Customer Service?
    I certainly would have liked to see something a little more firm. I think that the GM that made the announcement had heard that they would be coming down soon, and wanted to give more than the standard 3-5 minutes notice if possible. It would be normal for the GM not to know exactly when the server would be down, though I think that they probably should have given a timeframe for what qualfied as "shortly".

    Concerning not being able to answer all the /tells, as much as we'd like to, we just can't. Any broadcast, regardless of content, usually results in a /tell from about 10% of the people online at the time. That's a sizeable number of /tells. The best they can do in this case is see if the same questions are being asked in a number of the /tells and try to follow up on the issue in a subsequent broadcast.

  • Patch Changes
    Oops. Sorry, I knew I forgot to mention something. Indeed, ranged weapons will no longer use their full delay when you are not capable of using the weapon due to being too far to too close.

    In addition, we also stealth-fixed a problem with skill-caps on poison. They not correctly to up to where they should.

  • More Board Clear-ups
    I've seen a few emails and received a few posts regarding threads showing up as read before you've read them. As the boards have been reorganized, I imagine that your browser thinks that the links have been accessed on the basis of them having the same name as something you've read previously.

    Clearing your history through your browser should fix this up.

  • The Critic
    [Quote from original post: 1> As long as you continue to speak to customers with the same tone you used when dealing with these examples, players are going to remember said examples. I’ve said this before, but here’s a slightly different angle: if I said some of the things said in that FAQ, in the tone they are said, I would have been fired – immediately & without question – from any CS department I have ever worked for. And I have every reason to believe this would be true in the vastest majority of CS departments in this country. Need an example? Divine Intervention (final question under 4.1).]

    I understand what you are saying AuntieShaynta, but you'll also notice that in cases where we've tried to be extremely delicate with issues, we're usually jumped on for not giving a straight answer. Sometimes a straight answer requires that we be very blunt, and sometimes bluntness is taken with offense. In the particular case you quote, I was looking for a way to indicate that there will be no change, not "no change" in the "lets discuss it for a long time and we might change it" sense, but in the "this will never ever change" sense. There's only so many ways to state an absolute negative.

    [Quote from original post: To again quote myself, if I were in Brad’s position no quest would go live until I personally tested it. ]

    While I will not deny that there is much improvement to be had in our testing department, there's certainly too many quests for Brad to test them all personally. However you are correct that someone should.

    [Quote from original post: Players are going to remember these examples as long as Verant in general and you in particular continue fertilizing their memories of these examples – by continuing the same behaviors.]

    I understand that, but I would ask that the players also take note of progress that we make. We are all people, and people make mistakes. The point that we stop learning from our mistakes is the point that you should get worried.

    [Quote from original post: Thank you for your time.]

    And thank you for yours.

  • Cannibilize Fizzle Rate
    There's no spell-specific fizzle rate tied to that spell. Spells have an increased chance to fizzle during the level that you first become able to cast the spell. As you get at least a level higher, you should notice your ability to cast it improve greatly.

  • Changing Your Chat Handle on Official Boards
    I've noticed a number of questions here regarding changing your chat handle. You can change your chat handle by going into the EverQuest chat server, and typing:

    #rename OldName NewName

    The chat handles are currently copied over to the board database once per day, so it might take a little while for your change to be reflected in your posts. We're considering increasing the number of times per day that the database is copied, so the duration you will have to wait to see your new name reflected might decrease in the future.

  • Question About the Live Servers
    [Quote from original post: I'm curious... has there ever been a change in the Test server that HASN'T come to the Live Servers soon after?]

    Off the top of my head, I do recall a DOT change that was put on Test. The version that finally made the production servers was much different from the original version on test.

    [Quote from original post: I'm getting the very creepy feeling that Verant just sticks things on Test for a couple days before putting it Live, without looking at the effects it has on Test, just to cover their bums.]

    Well, I certainly couldn't say that we haven't made mistakes in the past. We do however continually try and improve our processes when we do make those mistakes.

  • Board Notice
    We ran into a few problems getting the board reconfigured. We think that we have them all set now. If you notice any problems with the boards over the next day or two, please email me at gwrinn@verant.com.

    As a note, we now have a few people moderating the boards to remove posts in violation of the new rules of conduct. We appreciate everyone's cooperation in keeping these boards free of the discourteous banter that was present before.

  • What Happened to XX-Board?
    [Quote from original post: I found the Newbie Board to be a great place to ask 'dumb' questions. No one would flame and people in general were nice. It was a good forum to ask some basic advice and/or questions about the game that might not be found in an FAQ, but considered 'common knowledge' by posters in the General Forum. Why was the decision made to delete this forum?]

    We wanted to limit the number of forums based upon what we could actively moderate. Given that flames won't be tolerated here any more, this might be a good place for the questions that you mention. However, if the Newbie Zone was really enjoyed that much, we could certainly think about bringing it back.

  • Official Welcome Message to New Boards
    Welcome to the new EverQuest forums. As mentioned yesterday, posting to all forums, with exception to the “Customer Service / Technical Help” forum, is restricted to active EverQuest account holders. At this time, we are also implementing strict moderation on all EverQuest forums in accordance with the rules of conduct at http://everquest.station.sony.com/s_conduct.html.

    I’d like to begin by telling you about some new features of the boards. In the upper right-hand corner of the screen, under the “Post New Topic” button, you’ll find a link to the new EverQuest Forum FAQ. In addition to containing information regarding the new rules in place on the message boards, this FAQ also contains useful information on some subjects that tend to come up frequently on the boards. On the upper left-hand side of the screen, you’ll notice a link to “EQModerator”. This link will take you to a page that will allow you to email the moderators of the boards in order to report posts that violate the message board rules listed in the FAQ and the Rules of Conduct. The EQModerator email address is *only* to report violations of those rules and to request moderator support in a particular forum. At the top of each page you’ll note a line that reads “Posting to this forum is subject to the EverQuest Rules of Conduct”. We highly suggest that everyone read all of these documents prior to posting on the boards.

    We’ve taken great steps to ensure the security of Station information here on the boards. You’ll notice that when posting to the restricted boards, your Chat handle is displayed as the author of the post. You can change your chat handle once per week in the EverQuest Chat Server. Type #help for a list of commands available to you in the Chat server. Please note that posting in the “Customer Service / Technical Help” forum will display your Station Name rather than your Chat Handle, as the forum currently allows posts from the general public regardless of account status. In the near future, we will change that forum to use a chat handle if one exists.

    Please note that the new message board rules will be enforced immediately. There is to be no more name-calling, personal attacks, or rampant profanity on these boards. People who post in a prohibited manner will run the risk of losing access to their EverQuest accounts. We regret having to tie the two services together in this manner, however given the option of either doing this to allow the boards to serve their purpose, or shutting them down, we feel that this is a much better solution, as it allows us to serve our customers to the greatest extent possible.

    These boards exist as a resource for current EverQuest subscribers. I would like to thank everyone for helping ensure that they fulfill their purpose. We look forward to recommending these boards to our subscribers as a whole in the near future.

  • Boards are Set
    We won't be wiping again at this point :) (we hope)

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:34 PM PDT
Changes to the official boards were the big topic in Gordon's latest posts on the Gameplay Board:

  • Even More on Boards
    That's a good question tgmcclosky. I'd hate to come in and say X posts in Y minutes/hours/etc, then see everyone worried about or intentionally going up against some limit.

    It's going to come down to the intent of the poster, and whether the activity is disruptive to the normal activity on the board.

    I think that these changes will make the boards much more useful overall, as after we get over the initial hump, we'll have a much better signal-to-noise ratio than we have currently. We'll also be providing an email address for the moderator, one that will actually be manned, in order to give people an opportunity to help us with the moderation effort.

  • Answer to a Board Question
    Yes, as long as you have time remaining on your EQ account, you will continue to have the ability to post.

  • More on Boards
    An honest post does not require personal attacks, profanity, real-life threats, and general spamming. In fact, it would be true in our opinion that any post worth an honest read does not contain any of these.

    It's not at all our intention to police the expression of ideas or opinions. We just want them to be expressed in a manner that is helpful to the game as well as to other players.

  • More Board Changes Q&A
    [Quote from original post: Does this mean you'll be paying more attention to the message boards?]

    While we already pay a *lot* of attention to the boards, I would say that the quality of that attention will improve. I typically read about 20 threads for every one that I can respond to due to the tone of the content. I'd like to see that ratio get much better.

    [Quote from original post: How about posts suggesting well thought-out game improvements?]

    I'd like to give a lot more feedback in this area, even if it is to say that it wouldn't be part of EverQuest. There's a lot of good ideas out there, so many in fact that there's no way we could implement them all. Of course it's no secret that we have several other as-yet-unannounced games in development, and some of those ideas might apply there.

    [Quote from original post: Or the post asking for an update to the client software. Even without more spell slots there's still requests for a better hotkey setup, a better system of targeting, easier shop interface, seperate chat boxes for guildchat say or shout. All of these are things commonly found in older and supposedly inferior MUD's.]

    Incidently, you just may be seeing some improvements in this area over the next couple of months.

    [Quote from original post: Will these things get more attention once the trash posts are gone?]

    Yep, that's certainly the plan. I'll look forward to everyone's help.

  • Security on Official Boards
    [Quote from original post: Have you ever heard of packet sniffers?????]

    The password exchange is encrypted. The chances of having someone on a subnet capable of sniffing with any success and having sufficient knowledge, hardware, and ability to cause problems in that area is exceedingly low.

  • FAQ Question
    [Quote from original post: Abashi how do you want players to add FAQ topics now?]

    The FAQ will basically be for issues that keep coming up here on the boards, with things like melee binding and experience tables. I don't think that the FAQ that I was working on was appropriate for that type of thing. Maybe we start up a daily "Did you know?" thread :)

    As for future additions to the forum FAQ once it is publicized, we'll be adding issues to them as they show themselves to be persistant in nature. For instance, it would be premature of us to add things like class-skill cap issues to the FAQ as they are not yet frozen.

    Hello everyone,

The flames and personal attacks that are now so common on these boards will no longer be allowed to continue. We will also be posting a FAQ that not only explains the new rules that will be observed and enforced on the boards, but also goes into verbose explanation of some of the issues that come up here quite regularly (e.g. melee binding, experience requirements, etc.)

    As a symbol of our renewed committment to return the boards to a standard of acceptability, we will be completely wiping the boards at the time of the change.

    Well, it's our opinion that 'challenging = fun for many people'. It's also our opinion that 'too easy = people becoming bored and leaving'. And finally, since we're doing word equations, 'unbalanced = no fun = dead game', 'monty haul = short term lots of fun, long term total boredom.'

  • Monty Haul Server Suggestion
    In a nutshell, the reason we wouldn't want to do this is that it cheapens the game.

    We don't want a monty haul server, we don't want a 'real easy/level fast/collect the phat l00t' server.

    It's the same reason we don't have level-up potions or gates or some such on Test.

    It's the same reason I throw a fit when Veeshan's Peak screen shots are shown, and why we don't like spoiler sites.

    It cheapens the game. It makes the sense of accomplishment a player gets on a regular server signficantly less.

    And, as mentioned, it would also just fill up with the lazy power gamer.

    People would rip through the content on the easy server, cheating themselves of a good sense of accomplishment, and leave.

    I'm sure the flames will follow this... 'you are just greedy and want people to play a long time!'

    Well, yeah, in a sense. One of the challenges to creating MMORPGs is PACING the game. Players can always consume content faster than a team can create it... you know, the 'it takes 6 months to write a novel and 2 weeks to read one' argument.

    But another key aspect to PACING an MMORPG is balancing risk and reward and character advancement such that players get a deep sense of accomplishment. Yes, this keeps players around longer AND makes a better game, IMHO.

    And that was our goal -- to create a persistent, massively multiplayer game, where people would play for months, even years. We did not set out to create D2, great game that it is.

    Ah, this rears its head again.
