    [Quote from original post: "the biggest problem is absor said they knew our spells were weak and our new scars spells would fix our complaints."]

    I don't remember saying anything of the sort.

  • A Small Idea
    I have no idea if this can be done. I'll add it to my list and see if it's possible.

    What are the implications for a change like this as it regards PvP?

  • EQMacros
    I'm certainly not going to discuss EQMacros.

    But tell me this, what features do you think we have not included that would aid the people that you speak of? We're certainly willing to talk about enhancements that help handicapped players.

  • Sound Problems
    Try asking on our tech support board. My first (and only) suggestion would be to reinstal your sound drivers.

  • DX 7 and DX 8
    We are aware the Microsoft has just released DirectX 8.0. Because we have not tested the new patch with this version of DirectX, we are recommending that you use DirectX 7.0a with EverQuest. You can still download DirectX 7.0a at Tucows at the following URL:

    http://cyberlynk.games.tucows.com/preview/58542.html

    Versions of DirectX are inherently backward compatible, but we have not done any sort of intensive testing with this version specifically. We have done a bit of testing, and we've heard that it works fine from some folks on the test server. But we still recommend using 7.0a with EverQuest.

  • Deciding When to Respond
    The posts that I can answer easily and quickly get answered easily and quickly. I can give my opinion with assurance that my answers will be correct. To answer for the company, I need to be more certain.

    A lot of the complicated issues that you mention require that we sit down and talk about the implications and the mechanics behind those sorts of changes. I can't just pop off with an answer.

    But I am also extremely aware of my very small number of posts on the boards, and how very few of the heated issues I've responded to. I'm sorry about that. I certainly want to respond to more of them, and I will in the future. I take my responsibility to our customers seriously. Perhaps too seriously at times, causing me to hold off responding to an issue until I think I understand it intimately.

Preperation for Next Patch
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:32 PM PST
David Starr, of Verant Interactive, made the following announcement on the Gameplay Board tonight:

    Preparation for the next patch:
    Greetings Windows 98/95 users.

    To play the game after the next patch you will need to update to DirectX7.0a if you have not done so already. Before you do this I would want you to make sure you know what video card and sound card driver versions you use.

    Here is two ways to determine what sound and video game drivers and cards you have.

    Click on start; go up to the run box. In the Run window, type DXDIAG than hit ok. This will launch the DirectX diagnostics tool. Click on the tab that says Display to see what video card you are using and what is the latest driver you are using. . Click on the tab that says Sound to see what sound card you are using and what is the latest driver for the sound card. If you are using a 3D-accelerator card it will show up as another Display number. You will have a Display 1 and a Display 2. Please copy the information down for both.

    Please write this information down on a piece of paper with a pen or pencil. Remember to write down the number of the driver version you are using.

    Another way is at your desktop right click on My Computer. Scroll down to properties, click on properties and go up to device manager. Please click on device manager. In the device manager, if you click on sound, video, and game controllers, you can determine what audio device you are using. If you click on display adapter you can see what video device you are using. If you click on either device and then click on the driver tab you can determine which version you are using. If you are using a 3D-accelerator card it will most likely show up in your sound, video, and game controllers section.

    After you find out what video card or sound card you have, please visit our main web site and visit the support section and examine the sound and video FAQ’s. This will usually have locations for the latest drivers for your cards from the manufacturers’ web sites. You may want to write that information down as well.

    Now for the update information:

    For the full version download for DirectX 7.0a, go to this URL. 6.3MB download.
    http://download.microsoft.com/download/win98SE/Update/7.0a/W9X/EN-US/DX7Aeng.exe
    If you have DirectX 7.0 on your system already, there is a DirectX patch to upgrade to 7.0a. 234KB download.
    http://download.microsoft.com/download/win98SE/Patch/7.0a/W9X/EN-US/DI7AENG.exe

    It is up to you what you will want to download. I would recommend the full version of DirectX7.0a.

    ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    At this time I do not recommend downloading Direct X 8.0. Please do not download and install it this brand new DirectX version. Many of the Video and Sound Card manufacturers have not made drivers that work with EverQuest and DirectX8.0. Please note there is a difference between compliant and compatible.
    ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    After you have updated to DirectX7.0a please try to play the game. If you have problems we recommend that you reinstall your video card drivers and sound card drivers. Since you have written them down, you will have a good idea of what version number you need. If you have visited our web site looked at the Video and Sound FAQ’s under support, you should now know what web site you need to visit to download the proper or latest Video Card Drivers. There is a chance that after the patch you might again need to update and/or change your driver versions.


    --------------------------------------------------------
    There is a slight chance you might want to reinstall or uninstall DirectX due to corruption issues. An uninstaller is available off the Www.downloads.com web site at
    http://download.cnet.com/downloads/0-10107-100-2023210.html?tag=st.dl.10001_103_1.lst.td_2023210

    Follow the directions carefully. Please do this as a last resort measure. It might help but it might hurt as well. PLEASE FOLLOW THE DIRECTIONS CAREFULLY. You are playing with your registry. You do not want to mess it up.

    Good Luck
    David Starr
    Verant Technical and Customer Support
Thanks Gordon.

Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 12:14 PM PST
Gordon stopped by the Gameplay Boards this morning and had the following to say:

  • Graphic Annoyance
    EQ uses a mesh based character model system. In other words, with the exception of helms, the character geometry does not change to show new armor, instead new textures are mapped over existing geometry. This has the nice effect of ensuring that character geometry is contigious, meaning that your arm, for instance, is always attached to your body.

    Helms are currently achieved by changing the geometry of the head, but results in all helm geometry being the same. We are currently experimenting with equipment-based geometry that attaches to the current character geometry, which will let us do some more exciting things in the future. The new helms to be included in Velious will use this technology.

  • EQ Too Bright
    If the in-game gamma adjustment doesn't work currently for your card, it should after the patch on Tuesday.

  • Huge Experience Bug?
    It's not a bug. Experience loss on death is a numeric value calculated as a percentage of the numeric value required by a human warrior to level in the previous level. In other words, if you die in level 55, the experience you lose is based on the experiece a human warrior would have required to level from 54 to 55. It's not calculated as a percentage of experience required for the current level, so the number of "bubbles" or fraction thereof may fluctuate.

    There is a cap of the maximum amount of experience (as a numeric value) that you can lose from a death, which classes hit in the mid-50s.

  • GM Availability Toward Non-US Players
    Servers have nearly 100% coverage during primetime. There are GMs on 24x7, but they do float around to take care of situations as they come up. You may consider dropping by chat and seeing if there's one there who can arrange assistance in game. Failing that, and assuming that you play during off-hours, you can email eqmail@verant.com, and see about setting up a scheduled time that one of the floaters can come in game and give you a hand.

    That said, though I don't have a list of skill caps handy at the moment, it's very possible that you've hit the caps for the skills that you mentioned. They do not all increase the assumed 5pts/level and are dealt out slower, usually begining at 40th level depending on your class.

Gordon Infobits: Saturday Edition
[10 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:00 PM PST
A few more board messages were posted by Gordon Wrinn today on the Gameplay Forum:

  • Another DX8 Question
    Mensha's post pretty much covers it. We've been working on getting the DX7 stuff put together for some time and have invested quite a bit of testing into it. It is very likely we'll migrate to 8.0 in the future, but the step we are taking now is about as large of a step as we'd like to do at one point.

    We're very optimistic about the reports we've gotten from those checking it out on the Test server and are looking forward to taking the changes Live.

  • DX 8.0 Question
    As a general rule, you're better off with the drivers directly from your card manufacturer as they build releases and respond to issues very quickly. As Yense said, the DX installation sometimes takes a preferred driver, which is why the order of install becomes important. If you try it and it works in whatever order you've done it, that's fine, but if you have problems be sure you do it in the perscribed order.

  • "What does it matter?"
    [Quote from original post: The problem lies in the design of EQ. It changes our desired formula of 3(B)=A to more like 6(B) = A.]

    I don't think it has so much to do with how much you play per week, but rather how much you play in one sitting, and what level you are.

    If player B plays 1.4 hours per day, it may very well be 6B=A if A does his 30 hours in only two days, especially in the 50+ level range. However if B plays his 10 hours in two days, he probably does better than 3B=A if A is playing 4.2 hours 7 days per week.

    Sure, there's a certain amount of "overhead" involved with getting on, getting together, and getting ready to do something. That overhead varies with the size of the encounter, and thus the level of those involved. This makes sense as those who reach the levels where the increased overhead is present are typically those who play more. That combined with the steepening experience curves keep things pretty relative. For instance, the "average 40 hour per week player" might have a character around 55 and level once a week, while the "average 10 hour per week player" might have a character around 30 and level once per week.

    I put "overhead" in quotes, because many times that "overhead", or things thought to be "overhead" by "powergamers" are actually what the "casual gamers" crave. It's the time spent to get together and plan a big adventure, and is just as important to the game experience as killing something. It's part of the adventure.

    In any case, the average EQ player plays over 20 hours per week, with the majority of that being done on days off. The game is centered, by means of advancement and acquisition quite well around that figure. It fits the vast majority of players, and works well for the populace of EQ. Baring those who powerlevel up in a few days with the help of guilds or friends, advancement means something. Items mean something. This is a very important tenent of EQ, and we're not going to be ones to risk messing that up by fooling around with advancement curves.

  • Procs Random
    It's definitely a neat idea, though not within our current abilities. We can currently only attach one spell to an item. It is possible that in the future we'd look into expanding in that direction.

  • EQ vs. AC Essay
    Just a note, we've been watching this thread with some interest in house. As a matter of professional policy, we tend to watch these from afar and let players draw their own conclusions, but I did want to let people know that we are paying attention.

    One question I do have though... While I'd never propose that our policies are any better or worse than those of another company, I do wonder at JCDDreamer's last post here he said:

    [Quote from original post: EQ does, trying to help out a bit... that was their mistake.]

    and

    [Quote from original post: The reason we have so many problems with EQ... is BECAUSE they are trying to help. ]

    and

    [Quote from original post: EQ made a mistake, by caring about some individuals... ]

    Does everyone feel this way more or less?

Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:17 PM PST
Gordon stopped by the Gameplay Board a few times today and wrote the following messages:

  • SoV Q&A
    Actually I believe that all classes will benefit from SoV-specific features.

    I doubt that any of them will widely swing the scales of balance, but there will certainly be some benefits. I would however caution that if you are hinging your decision to buy SoV on the basis of new powers alone, you're probably setting yourself up for a disappointment. Obviously, nothing we can do will widely change the game from what it is now. That wouldn't be fair for those who do not buy SoV to acquire any new abilities, features, equipment, etc., that is available there.

    It's important to mention that nothing included in SoV is being proferred as a "fix" for any current issues deemed important by the players, except perhaps for high level overcrowding, camping, and other "space" related problems.

  • Letter From GameSpot Editor
    Thanks for the clarification and explanation Jennifer.

  • Experience Res
    Policy-wise, no you should not get an XP res. People choose where to bind, and it is their responsibility to ensure that they bind someplace safe.

    However, if someone gets in a bind-death loop and can log on another character and get a GMs attention, they'll try to help you out of the loop rather than have someone get into a situation where they cannot play their character any longer, but the experience loss from getting in the loop in the first place is the penalty for binding in a dangerous spot.

  • Casual Gamer Statement Response
    [Quote from original post: As a paying customer I disagree. the FACT is, a casual gamer who plays 3 hours a day as compared to a hardcore one who plays 10 hours a day, you would think that the 3 hour guy has a 30% shot at getting what the other guy gets. This is not the case.]

    I didn't say that was the case. Sure, there's a certain amount of overhead in getting together to go do things, especially at the higher levels. My point was that if your focus *is* on getting the "what the other guy gets", you are a power gamer, not a casual gamer, even if you only play 2 hours at a sitting.

    In my opinion, being a power-gamer or casual-gamer has nothing to do with how much time you can play. It's about your goals while you are playing.

  • "Thunder Kick?"
    These are the same types of suggestions that come up once in a while in regards to critical hits. People don't like to see "crits" that are no higher than their average blow, but it can happen. A crit just means that you're hitting for more damage that hit than you would have if you hadn't had a crit that blow. On *average*, you will get much higher crits than your average blow, because your blows remain average.

    The same is true for Thunderkick. You will have the occasional bad roll, but your average one will be much higher than your average unassisted kick. This is how they are designed to work.

  • Necro Issues & Skeleton Ilusions
    I spoke with some Necros this weekend about some of the disadvantages associated with being a Necromancer in the large encounters. One of them stated that they already had enough disadvantages considering that they "couldn't solo much beyond 57th level".

    I thought he did a pretty good job of supporting my position: The disadvantages that necromancers have in multi-group encounters in part makes up for the tremendous advantages they have early and most the way through their life. While I understand that classes want their advantages to be absolute and their disadvantages to be meaningless, the disadvantages wouldn't mean much if that were the case.

  • Cleric Issues & New Helmet Models
    We'll have new helmet models with Velious. That said, I don't think that the changes to Mark of Karn or Donals arms constitute any real "nerf" to the class. Both issues represented something that created untenable problems in game that had to be addressed.
