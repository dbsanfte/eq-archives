Gordon Infobits
- ChrisLTD @ 7:26 PM PDT
Abashi made the following posts on the Official EverQuest Forum:

  • Abashi listened to hybrids finally! Thanx
    [Quote from original post: Abashi THANK YOU for putting a larger mana pool on test server. Please consider making this live as it will not address the xp issue, but it certainly will go a long way to fixing it. Also consider unnerfing the spell mana requirement, they should take the same mana as the parent class.]

    Just to stress, this is in testing and may or may not make the live servers. I thought I'd announce it to give the players some idea of what we are working on. Just to elaborate further on what is in testing:
    1. The mana pool for a hybrid will be the same as the pool for the parent caster, assuming the same amount of int/wis. This is balanced by the fact that there are not nearly as many +int/wis items available for hybrids as pure casters, and that they'd have trouble equallying out in that department. Previously, hybrids had the equivalent of 8 levels less mana than a caster of the same level assuming the same amount of int/wis.
    2. Spells cast by hybrids will still cost more than the same spell cast by the pure caster. The idea here is that since that is the casters specialty, they will always be better at casting then their hybrid brethern.

  • Druid Wolfform Variety
    [Quote from original post: I was just wondering if it was feasible to do other types of forms. I.E. Form of the Bear]

    This is covered to a certain extent in the Online FAQ. Basically, only so many character models are available in each zone, with some being available in all zones. The "wolf" is one of these global models, whereas the "bear" is not. An illusion spell can't make you look like something that doesn't exist in the zone.

  • Gating mobs! Hello, the patch message said this was fixed.
    Indeed, only pets no longer gate. NPCs in Kunark are designed to under certain circumstances

Official Board Policy Update
[10 Comments] - ChrisLTD @ 11:34 AM PDT
Straight from Abashi on the Official Boards:

Hello all,
We're quite pleased with the way the boards are working out so far, and hope that they will continue in this fashion. That said, there are a couple other issues that we need to address in order to facilitate the board's continued usefulness:

Nerf Requests: We're going to begin closing requests to nerf others classes. It becomes a fairly common occurance when it's apparent that we aren't going to upgrade another class. Nerf requests are very inflamatory and are a breeding ground for all-out flame wars. As we don't want to have to liberally ban people if it can be handled via other means, closing the threads is probably the best action we could take.

Dead issues: We'll also begin closing threads, explicitly or implicitly addressed to us, that we consider dead issues, issues that we've addressed already and consider "locked down". You all can feel free to continue discussing the issue amongst yourselves, but if it's a "Verant please read!" thread, and we consider the issue closed, we'll close the thread as well. We're going to do this in hopes of making room for new threads on other subjects that just can't seem to draw much interest on their first go-around. Rather than have a dozen threads pop up asking us to continue explaining the issue, this might be the best course of action.

In addition, I'm going to create a "dead issue" section of the FAQ for these things as they continue to come up. We realize that not everyone can read everything on the boards every day, so we should have the data available someplace.

Concerning the "dead issue" stuff, please take note that it only speaks to threads addressed to us. Like I said in the original post, feel free to discuss it amongst yourselves.
What I'm trying to avoid is this:

1. Post shows up asking for an issue to be addressed. We address it.
2. More posts show up asking for it to be addressed further, we clarify.
3. Yet more posts show up demanding it be addressed. We run out of ways to address the issue and sooner or later stop responding.
4. More posts show up asking why we're ignoring the issue. We post that it's already been addressed several times.
5. More posts show up saying that it wasn't addressed well-enough to begin with, yet we've said all that we can say on the issue.

Where does it stop? A solution is needed, and this one seems better than having a bunch of "why are you ignoring..." posts on the front page.

-Gordon

Burning the midnight oil with Gordon Wrinn
- ChrisLTD @ 10:22 PM PDT
I have survived another night of patrolling the Official Boards to bring you this information:


  • Test Server characters quietly moved to production servers
    They weren't "quietly" moved as you indicate. It was published publicly that testers would be given the opportunity to move. We moved the majority of them in the /movelog to Terris Thule, but there are some that were missed that are being handled individually.

    Artificially buffed or equipped characters are not being moved in the process. Of the characters that are being moved, the only items they are taking are the ones they earned after the item-wipe.

  • Guide Program
    [Quote from original post: I went to the guide applicaiton page and found it was unavailable. The page says it will be available before the end of june. I can assume the date has been pushed out? :-) Any ETA on when we can apply?]

    Yeah, it was pushed out a bit. We decided that a revamp was in order since the game had been out a while. It should be back up in the next couple of weeks.

  • Can armor have REcast time instead of just a long casting time?
    Unfortunately no, we have no way to place a recast time on spell effects attached to items. I agree with the author that this would be a better solution than long casting times in many cases. With any luck, we may some day gain that ability, but I'm unable to promise that as a planned upgrade.

  • Larger Mana Pool Is the Solution to Hybrid Exp Mess... Heres Why
    [Quote from original post: The solution is giving hybrids a much larger mana pool.]
    Though I don't think that it's right to match up a mana pool versus an experience penalty, you might be right that a larger mana pool might be appropriate for Hybrids.

    Upon looking at the issue, it does appear to be a bit of a double mana-penalty, by virtue of the fact that they get less mana, and their spells also cost more. Given this, we've increased the hybrid mana-pool on the Test server to see its effect upon the environment.

    [Quote from original post: Life Leech is taking a percentage of total mana!!.]

    There are no spells that use mana based on a percentage of what you have. All spells use a numeric value of mana, plus a percentage modifier of that numeric value in the case of hybrids.


  • Eliminate Twinks?!
    Though this thread doesn't appear to be asking for any official response, I thought I'd mention that our position on twinking is in the FAQ.

  • SRZ Players LOSING EVERYTHING when zoning!
    [Quote from original post: 1. It has been well over 12 hours since this was posted. And a similar msg was posted on the General Discussion forum. Neither has been responded to. Why not? Is this how you expect to keep customers happy?]

    We're set up to provide customer-service in-game, by phone, by email, and on the net, in that order. The customer service team will respond to issues on the boards as they can, but we can make no promise that every issue will be addressed. Things such as item-reimbursements *cannot* be handled via a post on a public board. You'll need to contact a GM in game.

    [Quote from original post: 2. Is there any chance that the items could be replaced BEFORE I can get home and log in this evening? Which may be quite late. If not, why not?]

    Though you might be able to arrange something via email, or at least arrange a time to meet in game via email, reimbursement requests have to be handled online.

    [Quote from original post: 4. I will definitely be mad if I am to be expected to petition and await a response from a GM this evening]

    All of our customers would like to have an immediate response to all of their issues. I do wish that that was feasible, but it really isn't. The GMs in game will be the best route to get you cared for quickly.

    I've checked with the GMs on the subject and they were aware of the problem. Apparently a hardware failure was to blame. The failure was resolved, and those who experienced a problem due to it will be restored. You just need to contact a GM in game.

  • The FAQ states NO DROP is now for quests...
    [Quote from original post: Can you please change the planar armor and other planar items to not be NO DROP? Now that the Kunark armor is out and available, I am sure that some people who are not going to use their planar armor pieces anymore would appreciate the ability to trade the planar items to someone else.]

    NODROP was originally created as a way to insure that the actual character was present to complete a quest. It was later applied to certain items. Going forward, we're leaning in the direction of using it in the manner for which it was originally created. We however won't be changing existing items in this fashion. Planar armor is a bit of a status symbol anyway.

  • 10000 damage, or not 10000 damage..
    [Quote from original post: btw, I'm pretty sure all 10k falls are classified as bugs.]
    No, 10K is just the maximum damage you can take when falling. There are certainly places in game where people can fall for 10K legitimately.

    As for the original authors issue, I wouldn't be able to take a look without a /loc. I haven't heard a place were falling damage was greater than it should be before, so it would be interesting to take a look

  • Wurmslayer
    [Quote from original post: Apparently on test its now 2hs. Can anyone confirm ? the changes were retroactive]

    What we *really* need is a "polearm" skill. We really don't like the idea of paladins wielding daggers or rapiers, but really wouldn't have a problem with them using lances and polearms. Unfortunately, new skills aren't technically feasible at this time.

    In any case, the change to 2HS was a mistake, and it's been switched to 1HS.

  • Proving You Are KSed
    [Quote from original post: I tell him the story, along with the fact that I have witnesses and screenshots. He has the audacity to tell me he thinks that I AM LYING and that I can fake witnesses and screenshots. So my question is this Verant. Unless you put a guide in every single zone every minute of the day, how can you enforce PnP and prevent KSing? Without a guide to witness the event we don't have "sufficient" proof for you. Now I have my PAW and the guy who KSed me has been severely blacklisted, but I think this brings up a valid point that still exists. Thanks for your time.
    Please understand that we cannot allow players, even with witnesses, the chance to adversely affect the account status of another player. It's one thing when it's the player verus a game mechanic, but when the issue is one player's word versus another, a CS Rep needs to witness the incident before action can be taken. Screenshots of KS incidents really don't show what is actually going on. All they really show is that one character is killing an NPC.]

    This does mean that people get away with KSing a single creature. However, after a while and a number of reports, they will soon develop a reputation for KSing and make it far more likely for them to get themselves caught.

    I certainly hope that the guide didn't incidate that he thought that you were lying, but rather that doesn't have a way to tell what really happened. People often confuse the later as an accusation of lying.

  • Please, a Rallos Zek /move update. Rumors are bad.
    I've nothing further to announce in regards to my post the other day at this time. I posted what I did to get peoples thoughts and see whether or not that would address the concerns that have been raised.
    We still haven't made any decisions regarding any potential Rallos move at this time.

  • Mana regen items, errr Lack there of.
    [Quote from original post:1) Why are the mana regen items not being put back into the game. During beta, as you recall, some items had a mana regen effect that was not unbalancing. These items went along with all the melee health regen items that are dropping and have been dropping since the full release of EQ and later release of Kunark. Health regen is to the melee class as mana regen is to the pure caster class. I find it a rude and insulting practice that Verant would not equal (or balance if you please) the main factors of character time sinks inherent in class dynamics. The pure classes are not so beneficial for their narrow specialization in the first place. With this said, I propose that class specific items be placed in game that have mana regen effects. Mainly for wizards who have nothing but mana to defend them selves even against lowly greens when they are trying to med. This is not unbalancing in no way. It does not step on the enchanters’ toes because like regen on rubi and other items that stack with regen it too would stack with the clarity type spells and bard songs.]

    Though I don't recall any items in beta that increased those rates, if they were in, they were in proir to the bard and enchanter song/spells that grant the ability.

    Improving mana regen rates is the bailiwick of the enchanter and bard. That said, xenosoma's post is on target.

    [Quote from original post: "Improving mana regen rates is the bailiwick of the enchanter and bard."
    actually all pure casters have ways to increase their mana regen (all except mages)]

    I probably should have said "Improving mana regen rates of others..." And as a note, I don't believe that clerics or druids have the ability to improve their own either, unless you were drawing a distinction between int and wis casters.


  • Why does there have to be downsides on the best Armor?!
    [Quote from original post: Why does there have to be downsides on the best Armor?!]

    Basically, in order to make "best" an opinion. If there were no downsides, everyone would make the same choices regarding armor, and each new level of armor would make the previous one obsolete. Take the new Kunark armor as an example. Most of it doesn't have any stats, but it's high in AC and has a spell attached. This may not be the "best" armor to wear, espicially if you want stats. Yet if all you want is AC, then it may be the best.

    The concept of "best" is subjective in this case, allowing each player to make decisions that best suits their particular style and specific encounter.

  • Abashi, Your explaination for druid planar armor weight doesn't hold water
    [Quote from original post: The weight is intentional to provide downsides to wearing this armor???? Okay, why is it that druids are the ONLY class to have a downside to wearing planar armor? Can you name another downside to wearing planar armor suffered by any other class?]

    It seems to me that druids have ports, sow, tracking, respectable dots and dd's, good faction, can wield some pretty nifty weapons for a caster, and are the "Masters of Versatility" if there ever was such a thing.

    Perhaps druids have abilities or combinations of abilities that make some special disadvantages necessary.

  • SoV
    We're not prepared to comment on how testing for Scars of Velious will be conducted. I can however mention that in the event that we bring current players in for those purposes, we will most likely give preference to those who've been on the Test Server since launch.

  • Level limits on Dungeons
    [Quote from original post: Has this been asked before? Why arent there lvl limits on dungeons? If the highest lvl monster in a dungeon is 30, no one more than say 7lvls higher should enter.]

    Well some dungeons are pass-through, like Blackburrow and lower Guk, so that certainly wouldn't be an option for those zones. The other issue is that while minimum levels make sense in the world, such as with the planes, there's really no RP reason I can think of that would prohibit someone from entering a particular dungeon.

    Perhaps something along those lines as an *exception* to the rule, such as if a dungeon was overseen by an omnipotent being who demanded fair fights, but a large-scale implementation really wouldn't work in EverQuest.

  • Why doesn't Harm Touch do more damage than anything else for its level?
    [Quote from original post: Why doesn't harm touch do more damage than any other player-initiated effect for a particular level? Shouldn't it be the single most powerful damage source in the game? After all, it's only usable once per day.]

    No, it shouldn't, not for an instant-casting spell. Imagine what would happen if 6 SKs got together and decided to harmtouch the same critter at one time.

    [Quote from original post: Why doesnAlso, why isn't lay on hands a complete heal by the time a paladin reaches 50? It really ought to be.]

    Well probably the same reason that HT isn't the strongest DD. Both abilities remain a very powerful and aren't really in any need of improvement.

    The craft skill designers indicate that those items can be crafted and sold for a profit cheaper than they can be bought. Do you not find this to be the case?

When my character was in the "research levels", I saved every words/rune/and mage research component I found, and traded them with others to get what I needed.

    [Quote from original post: 2. God specific items. Allow me to compliment you on how well these were done. This was one of the best ideas I have seen to date and I hope you implement them for all priest classes.]

    That's certainly the plan

    [Quote from original post: 3. Druid quests. Could use a few more, this is everQUEST after all. I realise that the quest rewards can not be equivalent to some dropped equipment but how many clerics, shamans and others do you see in thier quests armor even at very high levels simply because of looks/pride? I see quite a few.]

    Yes, we agree, there is always room for more quests.

    [Quote from original post: 4. Shaman jaundiced bone bracer. just a bit overpowered. Since getting this bracer my shaman has stopped grouping. I can go to sky fire and easily solo any mob here that doesn't summon. I don't even kite anymore, just stand and fight with constant clicking of the bracer. If you intended shamans to never solo, this was good.]

    It does have a fairly long casting time. Don't you get interrupted?

    [Quote from original post: 5. Inappropriate spells or effects. Summon food and water for druids? druids forage. Levitate and water breath for enchanters? I thought enchanter were masters of the mind, not the body. banish undead series for necros? well ok... maybe. If you want the effect then I think the spell should at least be named and implemented differently. (example: necro versus cleric ressurection)]

    Well I suppose that inappropriate is a matter of opinion, but we won't be removing those spells from those classes

    [Quote from original post: 6. The planes. Great zones but why make them class specific? why not make all mobs have drop tables like the chests and phoboplasms and end the complaints of "my plane is harder then yours". It would also encourage more grouping at the high levels I think. Currently, it is very rare to find an enchanter, warrior, cleric and such in fear or vice versa.]

    We honestly don't want the planes to be one-stop shopping for all. Ideally, people with items in one plane will need the help of people who have items in the other. That way people work together.

  • Even More on Boards

    Yes, as long as you have time remaining on your EQ account, you will continue to have the ability to post.

    [Quote from original post: Have you ever heard of packet sniffers?????]
