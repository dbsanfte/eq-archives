New Patch Information
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:50 PM PDT
Back on the Gameplay Board, Hobart published the following announcement regarding the upcoming patch:

    The patch introduces all the DirectX7.0 or greater enhancements. It also introduces a number of User Interface (UI) changes.
    All EverQuest players will need to upgrade to version 7.0 or greater of DirectX™. It is also highly recommended that players check for updated sound and video drivers from their manufacturer. We also highly recommend that players install Directx7.0 or greater and then their latest video or sound card drivers after Directx7.0 or greater as been successfully installed.

    Players can download DirectX7.0a from http://www.microsoft.com/directx

    If players do not know what brand of sound card you have, you can right-click on "My Computer", go to properties, click on "Device Manager", and open "Sound, Video, and Game Controllers". If you use a SBLive sound card, you can run "LiveUpdate" from the Windows Control Panel off of your start menu. View our Sound FAQ (http://everquest.station.sony.com/s_sound.shtml) for driver locations and more details.

    Those players with the SoundBlaster Live series of sound cards should also have LiveWare3.0 installed.
    They can be found at http://www.creativelabs.com

    Next, go to the website for your 3D accelerator. There are a number of different manufacturers, and it is preferred that you visit the website for the manufacturer of your card. You can at your option go to the chipset manufacturer site. Most will be able to use either www.3dfx.com (for Voodoo cards) or www.nvidia.com (for TNT/GeForce cards). We recommend those that have NVIDIA chipsets goto the NVIDIA website listed above. Download and install the latest drivers for your card and operating system. See our Video FAQ (http://everquest.station.sony.com/s_video.shtml) for more details.

    Many players have the latest drivers and directx7.0 or greater installed on their systems. However, they still experience some graphical anomalies. At this time it is recommended that they reinstall DirectX7.0 or greater and then install their latest video drivers next. This has been proven to fix the majority of graphical problems.

    We also suggest that players take some time before the patch on Tuesday and login to the Test Server to make sure their systems are up-to-date. You can log on to the Test Server by running the testeverquest.exe. We recommend that before doing this that player's back up their eqclient.ini file to a different directory. The changes on Test Server will alter this file, and when returning to the Live Servers will result in their settings being set back to default.

    If after you have completed all the above updates and are still experiencing graphical or sound problems, please do the following.

    1. Run DXDIAG.EXE from your Start menu (click start, run, then type dxdiag.exe)

    2. Click the "Save all Information" button at the bottom of the program window.

    3. Save the file as dxdiag.txt

    4. Email that file to eqtesting@verant.com along with a complete description of your problem, any text from any error messages that are presented, and a phone number that you can be reached at if you are willing to assist our test department over the phone.

    5. If you experience a crash to the desktop while running EverQuest, please email your dbg.txt file from your EverQuest directory to the email address above.

Gordon Infobits: Morning Edition
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:18 AM PDT
Here are the late night posts made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • BotB: No Magicians?
    Only two Best of the Best tournements have been held: Warriors and Rogues. Magicians will get held at some time.

    Now assuming that you are talking about the Test of Tactics (something else entirely), I imagine that someone had a magician. If now, you could always have formed a team of your own :)

  • Velious Zones
    Pretty much every thing in that post points to it being quite possible that the poster isn't in the beta.

    First, there are no "mirrored zones" (e.g. Zones from old EQ that are flipped, reversed, and have a different texture set). Though this has been suggested many times by the players as a way to add zones quickly, we've never done it.

    Secondly, there are no Planes that cause a rez effect to be placed upon you on entrance. I'm not sure where that came from.

    In regards to item drops, they are progressing along nicely. Suffice it to say that we want to limit the amount of info about items that gets released, so most of the best stuff won't be put in until right before Velious goes live.

  • Playing Large Races
    There are supposed to be disadvantages to playing large races, and confined areas are supposed to be more dangerous than wide-open ones. Getting blocked in by NPCs and not being able to escape is part of that design.

  • Test Server Tip
    [Quote from original post: So if you do go to test server to help test the Direct X changes, don't use a character name you have on a live server.]

    Good advice. I believe I mentioned this in the patch message, but helping get the word out further always helps.

  • TestEQ Problems Under Win2k?
    Are you running the EQGame executables, thus bypassing the patch? If so, don't do that. You need to run either "Testeverquest.exe" or "EverQuest.exe" to enter the game. Running Testeqgame or eqgame will only cause problems.

    That said, any problems caused should be easily recitified by merely rerunning the patch. Doing one shouldn't break the other as long as you patch.

  • Necro Epic Quest Question
    [Quote from original post: "Can the right click darkness effect be used while running?"]

    No, no effect with a casting time can be used while running.

  • Emotes
    You can only put two commands on one line when one of the commands is "/pause". The syntax to do so is:

    /pause #, /command

    The pause command must come first followed by a comma to separate it from the next command. The pause is, however, executed after the command. It's a little kludgy, but it works.

  • Gordon: Here to Stay
    [Quote from original post: Hey Abashi when you leave can I have your blue name?]

    Leave? I'm not leaving. I'm just going to spend the vast majority of my time working on other projects. You can bet that I'll still come back once and a while to see how things are going and solicit ideas.

    These boards are a tremendous resource for ideas, which will continue to be important to me in my new position

  • DX Test
    [Quote from original post: From what I understand, EQ will cease to work when DX 7.0 update is installed on PCs with inadequate hardware.

    Because most of us are not sure if our current hardware will be DX 7.0 compatible, will you give us a way to test this? ]

    Run TESTEVERQUEST.EXE from your EverQuest directory and follow the instructions on the patch server. The Test server is currently running under the new requirements and is an excellent place for you to test your configuration out.

  • DX Test Failed?
    Be sure that you've installed the latest video and sound drivers *AFTER* you upgraded DX7. The DX7 installation sometimes overwrites preferred files. These will get set back the way they should be following installation of the correct drivers.

    Secondly, if you could email the dbg.txt file and dxdiag.txt file (see the test server patch message for details) to eqtesting@verant.com along with a description of the problem, that would be appreciated.

Banned For Posting on Public Board?
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:18 PM PDT
Gordon drafted an interesting response to a thread on the Gameplay Board centering on a recent banning controversy. Here's the original post followed by Gordon's response:

    There is a story on the rant site of www.lumthemad.com. It claims that some poster decided it would be a good idea to post up Verant's Guide Policies on a non-VI/Sony site and was banned.

    Well, good for Verant. I actually think someone who is caught violating a NDA or something of this nature should get banned, regardless of where they posted.

    What I find unbelieveable is the article claims that ANYONE who posted even a reply to the thread was tracked down and banned!

    It claims that Verant employees stated the site owner released the IP's of all posters involved and Verant banned the user's based on this information. Problem is the site owner posted his own thread, denying Verant's claims and asking for a VI rep to contact him.

    Is any of this true? I personally find many of Verant's past actions offensive and thoughtless, but I refuse to believe even VI could stoop so low or they would make false claims.

    Frankly, I will be amazed if this is true. :(

    I'm not sure where this came from. First, no one has been banned (yet). There are three player accounts that are currently suspended, but they all play from the same location, and correlate to information on the site and ICQ profiles related to the posters.

    The action is being pursued because account warnings from our warning database were copied/pasted on the message boards there. Guides and GMs have access to that warning info, and the location where those three accounts post from is also the same location as the Guide.

    I'm not sure who got the idea that the operators of that site provided us with IP addresses, but they haven't. Of course if they are willing to provide the IP address of the person who posted the account warnings, it will be the final piece of information to correlate the data to 100% certainty. We already have "beyond a reasonable doubt", and though I could establish that if I were to discuss specifics, we can't do that as a matter of professionalism.

    -Gordon

Discussion of the Week: Expectations
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:30 PM PDT
Gordon started a new Discussion of the Week this evening on the topic of expectations:

    Ok, it's been a while since we had one of these, but reading the posts lately I thought it might be a good time to discuss expectations.

    It's been surmised by many gurus in the MMOG world that the most important job of a game developer is to manage expectations. MMOGs are fairly new in the grand scheme of computer games, and I think that there are still many people who do not know what to expect, or have expectations that could be considered "unreasonable" on a large scale, while seeming perfectly "reasonable" to the individual.

    To date there has not been an MMOG market leader that has had "good customer service" from the perspective of many of the people playing the game. Yet when these games are no longer the market leader, their customer service image somehow improves. Is this indicative of policy changes in regards to no longer being the market leader? I don't think so. I think that overall the policies remain the same, and that those who are left once a game leaves the limelight have expectations more inline with what is considered realistic by the people playing the game.

    The expectations of those playing EverQuest are as diverse as the makeup of the player base. We have people who are brand new to MMOGs, those that are veterans, and those that are somewhat in between. Customer service, however, must keep in mind that service provided to one player does not only affect that player in the MMOG environment, but everyone else, either directly or indirectly as well. For instance, a policy to give items whenever a claim of loss is presented can have a dramatic effect upon the game economy, and the game overall, if abused.

    No, we do not think that all players are liars like has been suggested in the past, however the question is, what percentage does it take to ruin a game? Many of the complaints received by GMs, and on this board as well, are rooted in player interaction. Soandso stole a kill, or ninja-looted, or cheated in a transaction, or harassed, zonecamped, bindcamped, hurled profanity, etc. With the number of player-interaction related complaints, is it not reasonable to assume that the percentage of those who take advantage of a situation is high enough to have a negative impact upon the game if those same people’s actions are rewarded? I believe that is a reasonable assumption, so why does the expectation exist?

    While I’ve used an example regarding expectations and customer service, it’s not limited solely to that. Expectations regarding the game exist in every facet. Some people expect to be able to acquire every item, complete every quest, to be the center of the world, the hero, and to in short, win the game. While these are good goals, the same expectations are often shared by others as well. In fact, if one person can achieve all that’s been mentioned, it’s very easy for all or a large number of people to have those same expectations. What happens when two people’s expectations conflict directly with each other? What happens when it’s 200K people who all have conflicting expectations? Can we make everyone the center of the world? No, because the center would become quite crowded. All we can do is attempt to manage the expectation, and provide content that encourages people to be satisfied or modify their expectations in a direction that does not conflict with the expectations of others.

    Meeting everyone’s expectations are the domain of the single player game. They are designed to make the player the hero or the center of the world. In single player games, one person “cheating” affects no one else. It’s even considered normal since virtually every game out there comes out with “cheat codes”, the use of which carries no stigma. Everyone is equal, because what I do on my PC in the privacy of my own home has no bearing on what you do on yours. You do not exist in my world, nor I in yours.

    Take class issues as an example. Suppose we have 4 classes, A, B, C, and D. (note: I’m avoiding actual class names to try and keep things on track). If A > B > C > D, A is perfectly happy with its relation to all others, while B is happy in regards to C and D, while C is happy in regards to D, and D is unhappy. Elevating D to the point that D > A upsets the other 3 classes, while pleasing D. This process can continue ad infinitum. Of course the easy solution would be to try and come up with A=B=C=D, however “equality” cannot be achieved without truly making all of the classes identical, at which point we would not have classes. If there are going to be differences, which is of course our goal, there will always be those with the opinion that the differences themselves are not equal. That is of course why we strive for balance, but while “equality” is a statement of fact, “balance” is an opinion that we have.

    I’ve used two examples, one of customer service and the other of class balance, to illustrate situations in which expectations can and do conflict. I don’t want to turn this into a class balance or customer service discussion, but would like to keep on the subject of expectations in all facets of the game. I normally end my opening post of one of these threads with a question, and the obvious question here is “What are your expectations?” However, I think that that question would yield replies along the lines of “I expect to be able to find a group”, or “I expect to be rezed if I die to a bug.” But this question isn’t that simple, and all I can ask when you reply is that you keep in mind that for every expectation that you have, there’s someone else with an equal and opposite expectation.

    There’s no perfect formula for meeting or exceeding expectations. Be certain that if there was, we would certainly employ it. Just try to look at your expectations not on the individual scale, but on that of the game as a whole. What happens if your expectation becomes policy? What happens if your expectation is applied to everyone playing the game? Are any one person’s expectations right? Is anyone wrong? I think that the answer lies somewhere in the middle. It’s not black and white or right or wrong. It’s not that simple.

    -Gordon

    An update for the data structures to allow for more in-depth quests.

    The DirectX 7a update.

    An update that should reduce the amount of NPC warping.

    An improvement to the movelog function that should reduce the chances of requests being dropped from the logs.

    NOTE: After this patch, you must DirectX 7a installed to play EverQuest.

    There will be more information in the patch message.

    Some of the changes we are making could use some stress testing. If you have time before the next patch, please take a few minutes and play on the test server a bit, we would like to see how these updates react when the servers are under full load. Please take special note of any video or sound related problems and send a detailed note to eqtesting@verant.com.

    More in-depth how? Like just more items to fetch for Mr. Quest NPC? Or what?

    And as for guilds…

    We had to set a size limit for guilds. The rule has always been ten people to form a guild. We probably should have been enforcing this more stringently from the start, but there were other priorities. Too many guilds does cause a performance problem, and forcing people to stick to the rules should solve that problem.

    If you are forming a guild and need to meet the ten member on-line requirement, if you can get them together during the hours that the GM is on-line for your server, just petition. A guide can verify the ten people and they can take that information to the GM. Your guild members can leave after that. So you do not have to wait for the GM that is actually going to do the guild creation, so your wait time shouldn’t be too long.

    If you cannot organize your guild to gather during the regularly scheduled time for the GM of your server, you can contact eqguilds@station.sony.com. We will work with you to make your guild.
