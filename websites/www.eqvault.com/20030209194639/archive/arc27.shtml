Scam Warnings from Auric
- Aurum @ 7:21 PM PDT
More scam warnings came in from Auric today. Here's the complete post by way of the Developer's Corner:

    Hello all. I have some more scams to let you all know about.

    ----------------------------------------

    quote:


    Hi,

    I read the thread on the developers corner about the scams, and I have a few more for you (and some other things);

    First of all, it's very possible for people to send mail that appears to be coming from Verant - all it takes is telnet, and knowledge of how
    the SMTP protocol works (which is used to send mail).

    An example:

    telnet <mailserver>.domain.com 25

    The mailserver will respond with a line
    220 software/version sometext

    Then you identify yourself to the server, usually
    most mailservers will correctly insert your IP address
    if you enter a bogus name, but for demonstration purposes:

    HELO workstation.verant.com

    Mail server will respond by saying

    220 welcome workstation.verant.com [real ip address]

    From this point on, you can 'fake' a message like so:

    MAIL FROM: <ceo@verant.com>
    RCPT TO: <recipient@of.email.com>
    DATA

    Here the mailserver will tell you to enter your message

    From: "Verant CEO" <ceo@verant.com>
    To: recipient@of.email.com
    Subject: you win a lot of stuff

    Message...

    --

    What will happen with most Windows mail clients (such as outlook, eudora, etc.) is that the From address of the actual message is displayed. The only way to find out whether the message is a fake or not is to look at the entire message, with all message headers. This does take an above-average level of knowledge about how e-mail works, so a lot of people won't bother with it, and assume the message is from Verant.

    One solution for this would be to include a URL in every official Verant mailing (something like "www.everquest.com/mailing/<whatever> ) where people can verify that the email did indeed originate from Verant - maybe a bit of overkill, but I have received more than 1 email scam of the type you listed.

    A few new scams that are popping up in the actual game is people waiting for lvl 19 characters to hit lvl 20, then they con up some story about how, when you use your password as a last name, you instantly zoom to level 50. Once that is done, the lvl 20 person is tricked into giving his station name with the /afk trick you listed. I'm not too sure about this one since I heard it from a friend
    who heard it from a friend, you get the drift :)
    Yet another one, people get conned into doing a /say <200 X characters> station name <10 X characters> password or something along those lines - the 'reward' would be 100kpp.

    Anyhow, just figured I'd explain that mail thing, since it is rather
    easy to do, and maybe something could be mentioned about checking
    the message headers :)

    Have a good one and tell everyone at Verant to keep up the good work, and tell the coders to drink more coffee - I can't wait for SoL :)

Ask Brad: 09/01/01
- Aurum @ 6:59 PM PDT
Brad McQuaid kicked up another edition of his popular Ask Brad posts on the Developer's Corner:

    Before going into the Ask Brads I received, I wanted to first post this email I received about the trivial loot code and Luclin:

    It completely destroys the role-playing feel of the game. It is also not farming to go get a specific item that you wish to wear from a lower level zone and then leave. Since I have played pretty much since the beginning, I was one of those people who killed for everything I wear and readily recognize that wearable equipment choices are very hard to come by. Any lower level item that is farmed now is due to a mistake in its placement or a mistake in not having another choice of similar equipment, farming is a very, very rare problem for anyone and can always be handled by your play nice policy anyway. I am also a very average player . A 34 year old truck driver that works full time and have my characters at high level and in a good guild through time played and being a good group member. I really think you should ask what everyone thinks, if an average player thinks the trivial code is awful you might just have made a bad mistake.

    Good points. What we're trying to do is twofold:


    1. Stop a small minority of high level players from storming into low and mid level zones at launch and then publishing all the treasure to spoiler sites. Yes, we know that will happen anyway, the information coming from level appropriate players, but it will happen more slowly. Right now, when we revamp or launch a new zone, it's filled with lookey-loo high level players who just blast everything. This inhibits the appeal of exploration and discovery for the players for whom the zone was designed. For example, when we re-vamped Runnyeye, this is exactly what happened. And while there were still some players who went into the Warrens and Stonebrunt and blasted everything, they didn't get any significant loot and therefore couldn't talk much about it.


    2. The loot code should hopefully allow us to put some decent items in low and mid level zones. Now, an important point is this: the items will still be measurably less powerful than those found in tougher, high level zones. Also, the items will probably not hold much appeal for mid level players on very old servers due to the fact that better items are available because of the trickle down nature of the economy. But, we are trying to make a better, more interesting EverQuest, and we think the loot code will contribute to this, especially on newer, younger servers.


    One of your points is that heavy bottom-feeding farming only occurs if the item is in a low or mid level dungeon and is still attractive to the high level player because there is no better item in a higher level zone. This is an excellent point, and when these instances occur, we should correct them (by either moving the item if it's too powerful, or by putting a better item in a higher level zone that is equivalent in nature).


    Our plan with Luclin is to make sure there are more attractive items for high level players so that they won't feel they 'need' to adventure in zones that are too low level for them. If we can accomplish that, then, in my opinion, there would be no legitimate reason for a higher level player to be blasting mobs in a low or mid level zone, and therefore the loot code will interfere with very few players' enjoyment of the game. (By the way, I've seen the argument that the high level player has the 'right' to enjoy all content in the game and therefore has equal rights to be in the new zone as another player for whom the zone was designed and populated -- I must respectfully disagree -- the character who is in a zone designed for his level range should have the right of way).


    We are aware this is a controversial issue, but honestly, I think most of the controversy comes from the fact that we've inadequately explained how the system will work, why we're doing it, and who it would really affect. I've received many emails and read many posts regarding the subject, and there are players who are both for and against the system. Going forward, Absor and I will attempt to better explain the system.


    I will say that I'm generally against making any sweeping changes to the game because disrupting the status quo of a game more than two years old is not usually a good idea. When we do make big changes (for example, removing the class based experience penalties) it's typically after a lot of thought and consideration and after concluding that, overall, the change would make a better EverQuest long term. If we are unable to communicate the benefit of this new code in Luclin to most of our players, we will reconsider it.

    ----

    In one of the "Ask Brad" columns someone asked the question about "Are you doing the moon of Norrath because you have run out of continents to add?" Your answer was along the lines of "There is a globe in skyfire mountains, check that out."

    Well I was porting someone to Skyfire the other day and thought I'd study the globe, because I was sure there was undiscovered continents. I was right, directly on the other side of the world from Antonica is a land mass about the size of the original EQ game, also with a couple of small islands between it and Odus. So that would answer the question of "If the world is round how come I can't go from Odus to Faydwer/Kunark?" "Because there is a big chunk of land you don't know about yet."

    But what I would like to know is (and I know you'd have a slight idea by now), will this last continent be expansion number 4? (Don't suppose I'll get an answer for that one.) Or I guess you could even make this the setting for EQ2. At least then people would be familiar with the world and could leave some sort of connection between the first and second generation EverQuest.

    -Geddine

    You’re right, you won’t get much of an answer because it’s simply too early to talk about unannounced titles or products that ‘might’ happen. What I can say is this: we think EverQuest has a very bright future ahead of it and we intend to turn the game and the world into a franchise – in other words, we are very committed to EverQuest for the long term.


    To that end, we’ve thought about and planned all sorts of stuff as it relates to Norrath and the universe it’s in. The globe you saw is just one example of how there might be more to the world than most people think. Our opening of one of Norrath’s moons in the Shadows of Luclin is another. And trust me, we’ve got all sorts of crazier ideas too :)

    You're correct that a high level character doesn't have the same option. But what we're really talking about here, for the most part, is what the player wants not what the character wants.

    A player with no high level characters that wants to experience high level content just needs to gain experience on one of those characters and he can see that content.

    A player with no mid level characters that wants to experience a level-restricted quest just needs to gain experience on a low level character and he can see that content.

Questing and the Trivial Loot Code
- Aurum @ 1:46 PM PDT
Alan addressed another piece of player-submitted feedback on the Developer's Corner this afternoon. Check it out:

    _junior wrote:

    There is a new, fairly involved quest, in the Stonebrunt Mountains. It appears to be a fairly interesting quest, and I'm hoping that the end result is nice, although its still too early to say at this point. There are, however, some problems that I'm seeing with it.

    The problem revolves around the trivial loot code, unfortunately. The quest requires players to retrieve items from the Tower of Frozen Shadows, a zone in Vellious that is appropriate for players from roughly level 30 to level 40. The quest also requires players to loot items from the spiritlings in the Stonebrunt Mountains. Unfortunately, the level on these creatures is low enough that your typical player who can actually visit the Tower of Frozen Shadow and retrieve one of the needed items is probably not going to be able to get what he or she needs from the spiritlings due to the trivial loot code.

    There is also an additional item worth mentioning, although I'm not yet certain if its a problem or not. Further along on the quest an 'apparition' will spawn. Apparently there are at least two types of apparition that spawn. One automatically attacks the player that spawned it, while the purpose of the other one is not yet understood. Both are apparently fairly easy to dispatch, and both drop trivial loot items. The apparition that aggros is apparently meant to be killed, so the fact that it has an item that is impossible to loot by anyone that can complete the quest is very strange. The other (non-KOS) apparition apparently has an item that falls under the trivial loot code as well, although not enough is known about the quest, yet, to know if it is meant to die in battle with a player that is unable to loot it.

    At any rate, it is my personal suspicion that this quest needs to be looked at, and possibly have the tags on some of the items changed so that they can be looted by those players who can reasonably be expected to complete the quest.

    There is one assumption that you're making here that I want to correct first.

    Quests are not always designed to be completed all at one time. Many quests are designed for the character to grow into, so that a character might begin a quest at a certain level that he can't complete until he reaches a higher level. A lot of people see quests as something to be done all at one time, and that's just not the case.

    So it might indeed be true that some quests in areas that use the Trivial Loot Code can't be completed by folks that didn't discover the beginnings of the quest in that zone when they were of a level to do so.

    I understand that this may seem unfair to some of you. I've received a few comments about "punishing people for leveling" in regards to the Trivial Loot Code. Let me address that issue while I'm here.

    The Trivial Loot Code is indeed a restriction based on the level of the character. But it isn't a punishment, as if gaining levels was a bad thing. It is simply a way of keeping content available to those characters of the level that the content was designed for. It's really no different than the fact that low level characters are restricted from accessing high level content, or that enchanters are restricted from accessing certain quests designed for paladins, or that worshippers of a certain deity are restricted from using items designed for worshippers of another deity.

    All of that content is available to the player. If a player wants to wield a Fiery Defender, he'll need to be playing a paladin. If a player wants to have a character that can visit the Plane of Hate, he'll have to level him up. And if a player wants to do a certain level restricted quest (and, by the way, this isn't the only kind of level restricted quest in the game…), he'll have to be playing a character of the appropriate level.

    Back to _junior's question, though…. In this case there is indeed a problem with this quest. We'll take a look at it and see if we can get it fixed for the next patch.

    Alan

Patrick Baldwin Slays Dragon
- Aurum @ 7:30 AM PDT
A touching story was published on EverQuest.com telling the story of Patrick Baldwin and his wish Verant helped come true. I won't spoil all of it, but here's a brief excerpt:

    Patrick Baldwin made a wish. As a 13 year old fighting a battle against Muscular Dystrophy, the Make-A-Wish Foundation® stood ready to grant almost any wish to the young man. If he desired, Patrick could have met a celebrity, taken a trip to anywhere in the world, or become a radio disc jockey just to name a few. However, Patrick didn't want any of that. He wanted to hunt dragons.

    The Make-A-Wish Foundation was founded in 1980 as a means to grant children with life-threatening illnesses a way to realize their dreams. This charitable organization has made the wishes of more than 80,000 children come true in the past twenty years. This year, Make-A-Wish made Patrick Baldwin's dream to meet the developers of EverQuest® a reality.

GU Comics Closes
- Aurum @ 4:39 PM PDT
Woody Hearn announced today that the popular GU Comics website will be closing down:

TITLE: GU Comics closes it's doors.

After more than a year and some 18 million+ page views, I feel that GU Comics has run it's course.
There are a ton of people I'd like to simultaneously thank and say goodbye
to; but for the sake of brevity, I'll do that else where and at another
time.

I would like to thank Everlore at this time though. They were wonderful
hosts for my little web comic. I appreciate all the hard work they put into
keeping GU live. You guys own. Thanks.

And to the fans... I love you guys. At the darkest moments and the lowest
point you were there to lift me up and support my work. I enjoyed the time
you shared with me, and I hope I brought atleast one smile to you. And,
hopefully helped you realize that EQ is a game... and that it's okay to take
yourself lightly.

Thanks again and maybe I'll be seeing you around.

Woody Hearn - Former Cartoonist
GU Comics
Take it easy, Woody!

European Fan Faire Cancelled!
- Aurum @ 3:50 PM PDT
SOE's Cindy Archuleta just sent me an email announcing that the Fan Faire event scheduled for next month in Paris, France has been shelved. No exact reason is given, but the letter promises this is only a delay:

    Sony Online Entertainment and Ubi Soft today
    announced the postponement of the European Fan Faire. Our European
    community of EverQuest players has seen much growth in the past year and we
    are excited about all of our initiatives in this market and beyond. While
    we regret the delay in bringing what has been an incredibly successful and
    one-of-kind event in the U.S., we know that all of our international
    initiatives will enhance the player experience, increase our international
    fan base and lead us overseas in the coming months.

    We want to thank all of the fans who had
    planned to participate in this inaugural event and will be sending a full
    refund in addition to a special gift from our fan faire team. While we are
    disappointed that we cannot meet you next month, we hope that our presence
    in Europe continues to grow. We will be able to better serve you by
    postponing this event to a more suitable time and place for everyone. We
    apologize for any inconvenience.

    EverQuest boasts more than 410,000 players
    on a worldwide basis including the U.S., England, France, Italy, Germany,
    Canada, Singapore, Zimbabwe, Saudi Arabia, and New Zealand. We recently
    announced the availability of Game Cards on retail shelves worldwide giving
    cash holders the ability to play in 90-day increments. And, the initial
    phase of localization involving both human and automated translating agents,
    beginning with French, German, Japanese and Korean is well underway. Sony
    Online Entertainment is continuing to build a bridge between nations and is
    actively working toward new server deployment plans with Europe as a primary
    target.

    This winter, all EverQuest players can look
    forward to the launch of EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin. The new
    expansion pack will introduce an enchanting moonscape packed with new
    adventure zones, new items, creatures and spells, a new playable character
    race and more.

    Thank you for your continued support!

    Cindy Archuleta
    Community Relations Manager
    Sony Online Entertainment
Sorry to hear it!
