#17   Name: Canuck (canuck@bound4doom.com)   Date: 4/18 @ 3:46 AM PST
Note: This is a repost of my comments on the OOC board at www.ralloszek.net.

If you care to view the (overwhelmingly positive) replies the thread begins at:

http://www.gamers.com/messages/message_view.asp?name=rzooc&id=zzoeq

By the way... Though it was written for Rallos -- The same applies to all PvP servers.

<><><><><>

Dear Reader,

I'm sure we can all agree -- pk, anti-pk, and us non-fanatical folks in between -- that PvP in Norrath is seriously screwed up.

We have naked mages scouring the countryside seeking out half dead targets with the sole purpose of picking through their belongings for nifty trinkets, guilds that have been reduced to nothing but an extra chat channel and a pool of potential grouping partners, the list goes on and on.

The question is... WHY?!?

We have an incredibly beautiful world at our disposal, a decent variety of races, classes (flawed but still there), spells and weapons, and more than enough players. In short... All the ingredients needed for a persistently evolving environment appear to be present.

So what the Hell went wrong?

Can we blame the PKs? After all... Surely Darkenbane are the ones that screwed everything up with their naked mage style gankings, right?

Or maybe it's all because of the pacifist roleplaying crowd that label everyone daring to kill another player as an antisocial 12 year old bastard with no life?

No. As much as I'm sure it pleases both camps to blame the other the simple fact is that there is no meaning to PvP because there are, by and large, no consequences to PvP.

Think about it... You're in Butcherblock and one of your guildmates sends a message over the guild channel because he's just been killed by a random PK in East Karana. Are you seriously going to pick up and run over there to hunt the murderer?

No. And you won't do it because there is no damn reason to! So what if you kill him? He has nothing to lose so he can take a thousand deaths and still flip you off every time he's about to die. It is MEANINGLESS!

So, aside from a few pissing contests, we spend the lion's share of our time playing what someone aptly refered to as "the blue game" until we're so damn bored we have to create roleplaying events!

I don't know about you, dear reader, but I bloody well expected every minute of every day to be a roleplaying event on Rallos! When CoA go up against Darkenbane it is supposed to be an event. NOT the exercise in futility it currently is!

So. Giving death a little meaning is the only way I see to restore purpose to PvP while simultaneously reducing the NMS (Naked Mage Syndrome). Here, in a nutshell, is how I'd see it implemented:

1) Upon PvP death there is an immediate experience loss. The precise amount can be debated. But roughly 25% that of a PvE death sounds quite reasonable to me.

2) Random loot of one item (including bagged stuff) + Coin

Now it's rather easy to guess some of the more obvious objections to this and I'll try to address those immediately in order to save you all a little typing.

First. Getting whacked by one cast due to being weak from fighting a critter.

I am no programming genius but I'm pretty gosh darn sure that it wouldn't be that complicated (or require oodles of resources) to implement a system, based on the current group aggregate damage concept, where the death penalties (experience loss, looting, etc) only take effect if the player (or group) doing the killing have caused 51%+ damage to the target. Only the
winner (or a member of the winning group) should be capable of looting the corpse - Aside from the victim, of course.

It would also be a nice feature if the penalties only took effect if the target was at 75%+ health when the attack was initiated (unless they
themselves drew first blood, of course). This would require additional coding but, if Verant is willing, would well be worth the effort.

Next. Zone (druid ring, wizard spire, etc) camping.

Is it really that hard to make it so a character cannot be attacked (even by critters) after zoning until either a set amount of time (10 to 20 seconds should do it) has elapsed OR the player tries to move/speak (ie: touches his
keyboard or mouse)?

I can't for the life of me understand why this hasn't already been addressed.

On to... Bind point camping.

This could be taken care of similarly to zone camping. I merely have it as a separate matter because my personal belief is that anyone binding themselves in an easy to find spot (ie: as soon as they enter a zone) suffers from a mental deficiency and could use a few deaths to knock some sense into them.

I'm flexible on this one. ;-)

Now. Duels, Arena Battles, etc.

Regular death penalties do not take effect under these circumstances. Not even looting.

I'd also advocate an extra /guildwar command that would enable opposing guilds to do battle without any serious consequences.

I'm sure I've forgotten a few of the more common objections. But I'm equally certain that you, dear reader, will not hesitate to point them out to me. I will naturally reply to these concerns to the best of my ability. I will NOT respond to stuff that's already been covered - Unless it's creative, of course. ;-)

Kumbaya,

Canuck
An Island Of Sanity In A Sea Of Madness
