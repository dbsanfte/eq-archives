Important Update from Abashi
- Aurum @ 9:13 PM PST
Abashi kicked this up on the Developer's Corner earlier this afternoon:

    Hello All,

    We apologize for the problems some of you have experienced getting into EverQuest after the launch of Luclin. As of just a bit ago, we patched a new graphics DLL that fixes the problems some of our users (AMD users in particular) were experinceing crashing to desktop. This does not mean that all crashes to desktop have been fixed, but this code had the potential for a lot of side effects, and fixing it is a big step forward.

    In the coming week you will see us concentrating on fixing many of the problems that remain. We are also hard at work on optimizations for both framerate and loading time. Load time is a particularly big target for us to work on, and we have some promising results in development. Our goal is to have that code out to you as soon as possible, but it needs to really be tested properly before we will feel comfortable doing that.

    In addition, we're also working on server side stability in a few zones, most notably Hollowshade Moor. These should be taken care of very rapidly as well.

    Thanks very much for your patience.

    The EverQuest Team

Shadowhaven Problems with AMD Chips
- Aurum @ 8:04 PM PST
EverQuest, more specifically the Shadowhaven zone, is having some problems with certain AMD chips. Here's the situation update from Gordon:

    Hello all,

    For those not up to speed on the issue, users of K6, K6/2, and some Athlon processors are crashing when attempting to enter Shadowhaven.

    Just wanted to let you know that we're still working on this issue. We've acquired several systems with this problem from EQ players in the area and are close to a solution. We think that the problem has to do with floating-point calculations and some issues that were present in the K6 and early Athlon lines. We'll be sure to keep you informed as we have more information.

    Thank you for your continued patience.

    -Gordon


Big Luclin Update, Part II
- Aurum @ 7:41 PM PST
Alan made this announcement recently on the Developer's Corner:

    We're still working on some issues and we've fixed others. Keep in mind that we have a bunch of people working on things, so most of these items are on the top of someone's list.

    While some of these issues only affect a tiny portion of our customers, we want to give a full and complete rundown of issues we are aware of. We will be keeping you updated as things change. Also, check the patch messages for more information.

    Fixed since release (in no particular order, by the way):

    - Bard songs and group buffs (these were related problems)
    - Familiars no longer get added to hate lists and don't cause you to crash if you die
    - Wolf form factions with the new models are now in line with intent
    - Several face/hair issues, such as not being allowed to choose bald and some facial hair.
    - /follow bug that caused folks to crash
    - Transparence issues with i810 display adapters
    - Crash bug related to root and gate
    - Various item and content fixes
    - Crash problems in certain zones


    Stuff in the works, some of these will be fixed with the patch on December 8th (again, in no particular order, most are priority one for the person working on them):

    - K6 Processors: We're working to fix, or provide a work-around for problems AMD K6 users are experiencing when entering some zones. Shadowhaven is one of those zones.
    - Reducing loading and zoning times
    - Crashes occurring for some people when logging in
    - Some players are crashing in Hollowshade Moor
    - Some characters being 'stuck' if game crashed while playing that character
    - Slow framerate on some fast computers
    - Large fonts and text that extends outside their boxes is affecting a few users.
    - Choppy, buggy, faint or flat sounds for some sound configurations.
    - A few people that don’t have Shadows of Luclin installed are seeing "Entering Luclin" when logging in and then crashing
    - Some systems have mouse pointers which are being lost or duplicated
    - Characters changing to wolf form look tiny
    - Some people are seeing Vah Shir as male humans
    - Some spells (mostly 'bolt' spells) not displaying effects
    - A bug exists that causes random crashes when exiting game to server select, and then going back into game.
    - Working on getting facial hair tinting for the half-elves, dark elves, high elves, and dwarf females working
    - Faces that sometimes don't remain as selected
    - A bug that was giving classes that had the old experience penalties more alternate advancement experience than they should have been getting.

    Note: Those without Velious should be able to access the Alternate Advancement interface by hitting the 'v' key.

    Also note: The inability to adjust gamma setting with some cards is an issue with cards that just don't support that ability. It's not really something we can fix in code. We are investigating to see if there are any possible workarounds out there for these older cards that are no longer supported by the manufacturer.

    Thank you all for your patience. We are working hard to get these issues straightened out as soon as possible.

    Alan

Alan on the Bazaar
- Aurum @ 9:10 PM PST
A new update appeared on the Developer's Corner that dealt with the Bazaar zone. Read all about it here:

    It was my understanding that in the Bazaar zone, you would be able to set up your character as an NPC selling your equipment. I honestly can't tell you whether this was an official Verant statement, but I was curious whether a) this is true, and b) if so, when is it scheduled to be implemented?

    It was certainly on our desired feature list. And we are still working on adding it to the game.

    We just didn't have time to get all this code working properly for release. Because of the high potential for duplication of items or even item loss when dealing with this kind of code, we've decided it was best to hold on to this feature until it was done. We plan to get it into the game as soon as we can. We decided that delaying the ship of Luclin for this feature, as much as we wanted to get it in, would be a bad idea because players often plan time off around the release of a new expansion and we didn't want to change the date and upset those plans. And rushing a feature like this would lead, at the minimum, to a badly implemented feature. So we thought it was prudent to hold off until it was ready.

    Alan

New Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 3:25 PM PST
Here's some of the more recent excerpts from Alan off of the Developer's Corner:

  • New Interface
    Resulka wrote:

    I have to admit that I'm sort of disappointed that there has been no official word on what happened to the new user interface we were told was coming. I mean, with as large as this release has been, I'm sure it had to be put on a back burner for later...but since you advertised it quite a bit, why didn't we hear something like, "We are working on getting this out to you as soon as possible."

    Was it scrapped?

    Below is the quote we received from these boards concerning the UI...

    "Yes, if you read that carefully you noticed that I said something about a new interface. We will be releasing a new and impressively customizable interface to all EverQuest players with the release of Shadows of Luclin. I don't have a lot of details for you about the new interface at this time. I can tell you that we think you'll be very impressed with it. The main feature of this new interface will be the customizability that it gives the players. We're still building it, so I can't really elaborate, but there is no doubt in my mind that you'll be impressed, or in the least intrigued."

    So, what happened?

    Well, we decided to hold off on the new interface. It's going to be rolled out to all our customers, so it's not really a Luclin feature. Because we decided to make the new interface available to everyone, we had to make sure that it would work with all of the old commands. And since it wasn't a feature specifically for Luclin, we decided not to spend too much Luclin production time on its creation.

    We also wanted to take the time to let folks try the interface and give us feedback before it is finalized. We just couldn't do that under the time constraints of producing Shadows of Luclin.

    When we have a better idea when we'll be able to release the new interface, I'll let you know.

  • Old and New Faces
    When you are fixing the face command pleeease look into this problem:
    I turned on the new textures and played around a little, and changed my face a couple times and decided there are too many things I don't like about the new dark elven female (the char I was on), so I logged out and turned it off. Then when I logged back in, I was on the old character model but with a NEW face! And on the old models I can't pick a new face so I will have to log all the way back out, turn the new model on, log in and try to guess which new face corresponds with my old face, log back out, turn on the old model again, and log in to see if I was right. Add to that the fact that I freeze up every other time I try to enter the character select screen, it could take hours to get my face back!! Arrgh!

    The old and new faces are linked. Each new face corresponds to an old face, ones that they were based on. So when your old characters are given their new models, they are given a face that was designed from their old face.

    This also means that when you change your new face, you also change your old one. If you really want the old face back you just have to change the new face back to the one that matches your old face. So if you think that there is a chance that you'll want to revert to the old models, take a screenshot of your new face when you first log in with that character and change back to that face if you decide to use the old models.

    But remember, in the near future we will be turning off the ability to change your face at will, so make your decision as early as you can.

Luclin Update
- Aurum @ 8:13 PM PST
The current status of the 3rd EQ expansion pack was revealed by Mr. VanCouvering this afternoon on the Developer's Corner. Read all about it here:

    I just wanted to inform you of some of the items that we are aware of and are working to fix. We will be patching as many fixes as we can tonight.

    Bard Songs – Bard songs are broken. Bards are having trouble getting songs to start or to take hold.

    Group Buffs – there is a problem that seems to be preventing anyone from casting group buffs.

    Crashing when Zoning – some zones are causing some folks to crash and not be able to get back to play with those characters.

    Bolt Spells – some spells are not displaying their effects.

    Mouse pointers – something is up with mouse pointers. For some they get an extra, others get none.
    - NOTE: Changing resolutions seems to fix this problem for some people.

    Intel 810 and ATI Rage cards – and possibly others, are displaying 'black boxes', often where a texture should be transparent.

    /facepick – sometimes after changing your face it doesn't stay changed.
    - NOTE: This seems to be an issue with folks wearing helmets when making the changes. It might help to remove helmets before changing faces. It might also help to zone once before putting that hat back on.

    I'm sure there are other issues being fixed right now, but those are the ones I have at hand.

    I also have a few tips and suggestions for folks. But first I want to respond to this:

    -Cragganmore- wrote:

    The back of the Luclin CD states: ADDITIONAL LUCLIN SURCHARGE REQUIRED. Is there some new monthly charge associated with EQ that has not been announced before? I have read all the web sites and do not recall seeing anything about this.

    There is no extra surcharge required to play Luclin. I honestly don't know how that got on the CD case, but it's not true.

    And what are you doing reading the back of your CD case anyhow… :)

    Tips and reminders

    DirectX 8.1 is required to play EverQuest. I wanted to make that clear. Problems like "No 3D devices found" and many others can be fixed by upgrading to DX 8.1. Run DXDiag.exe and make sure the version says "DirectX 8.1 (4.08.01.0881)".

    For those using NVIDIA cards, the 23.11 drivers have proven to give much better performance in EverQuest. You can find a link to them here on our driver support page.

    Clipping Plane! Remember, you can pull in your clipping plane if you are having problems with your framerate.

    Choosing which models to load. Ester asked me to make sure that everyone understood that when we talk about going to the "options" to choose which models to display, we mean the options BEFORE you log in, not the options in game. This is done before you log in because it has to know what models to load before you log in.

    Another tip for the more advanced computer user – It might be a good idea to turn off your swap file, run a defrag and then turn your swap file back on. It's nice to have an unfragmented swap file. If you don't know how to do this, then don't worry about it. :)

    Oh! And, yes, there is a typo in the printed manual that comes with the game. I apologize to all of the budding young makeup artists out there for calling you Rouges rather than Rogues. :)

    Alan

    There's some confusion about the information that's been released on the Test server over the past couple of days, and that confusion is resulting in some people having trouble playing EverQuest. To clear it up:
