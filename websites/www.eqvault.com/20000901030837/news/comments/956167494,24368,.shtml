#14   Name: Marithea Sparrowhawk (pootersox@yahoo.com)   Date: 4/19 @ 13:46 PM PST
Serendena is ABSOLUTELY on target here. NOTHING I would argue with in her comments.

So far as Zenarchy's post is concerned, I do have several issues with his (her?) comments.
1. Owning a spawn. Certainly if a group of appropriate level is AT a spawn attempting to get the item for members of THAT group who ACTUALLY CAN USE IT, saying "We're camping X" to the zone is legitimate, along with explaining that you'll move on as soon as you've looted your special loot. HOWEVER, as soon as each member of the group (NOT their other characters, their guildmates, or their little brothers) have obtained the item that they can use, it's time for them to move on. That's one element of sharing the spawn. A second element is the case of a group spawn that is experience rather than loot based. THAT kind of spawn you negotiate a fair sharing process. Any other approach to loot spawn or experience spawn is selfish. NO group owns a spawn. NO guild owns a spawn.

2. Twinking. Twinking is a kind of cheating (and I admit that my current main character is twinked-- not from any of my other characters but from friends lending her armor and from generous strangers handing her things or arranging for her to get to loot highlevel mobs for nodrop items, like the map on the ghoul messenger). Certainly the level 50 can and should be generous and give items he/she cannot use to those who can. One need not always be mercenary. However, to say "rude whiners of this game have CAUSED twinking" is to indulge in a distortion of logic so bizarre that I cannot find a name for it in the textbooks. Whining is annoying. Players, especially newer ones who don't know the economy and haven't discovered maximum eq and other sites w/ current prices NEED to ask what the value of something is. I do it all the time, and I've been playing since May '99. And yes, I've even told players that I'd seen certain items at much less than they were asking. I've also overpaid when it's an item I really wanted (like BI boots).

I do agree that begging for buffs, res's and TP's can get out of hand. And yes, whenever possible, offer to pay. And ALWAYS try to ask in full sentences and politely, ideally in character (as in "Excuse me, but might I solicit a gift from Tunare of Wolf Spirit? I shall gladly pay a tribute to Her.) I've been very lucky to have run into a generous cleric when I'd gotten a groupmate killed who was bound 1/2 a world away-- got an exp. res for a level 19. AND he refused payment, saying "That's my job; I'm a cleric." Further, when someone is about to make that long trek for corpse recovery, simple fellow-feeling ought to lead us to cast SoW without being asked and certainly without expecting payment. Often you will never see that person again; other times you'll receive more in return than you could possibly have asked up front. (Like barbed arms and legs as a gift... like a 100 plat piece of jewelry b/c you'd once helped a jewelry maker 10 levels earlier... these things have happened to my characters.)

Conclusion: nice is better, not b/c of some Verant Play Nice Policy but because it makes Norrath a better place and EQ a better game to play.

My 5 plat...
