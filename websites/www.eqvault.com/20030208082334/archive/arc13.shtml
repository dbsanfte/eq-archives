    ----------------------------------------
    quote:

    ----------------------------------------

    And swaying trees doesn't?

    /poke Absor

    Aladain
    60 Cleric

    No, they don't.

    You see, it's just like an animated gif. Once loaded up, it just does its thing. The server never has to track where those trees are because they don't move. The swaying isn't really movement, it's just frames of an animation.

    The pet's movement, though, not only has to be tracked by the server, it has to be sent to everyone (you've already got all the data you need for those trees once you zone in). Each time the pet moves, the server has to tell all the clients in visual range that it's moved. But if the server knows the pet is sitting there in one place... well, then it acts much more like a tree.

    I can only hope that made sense.

    Alan

    Grand Master Glox sat silently staring into the black waters of the Cabilis aqueducts, his thoughts tracing and retracing the recent visions that had upset his nights. The Lord Cazic was trying to tell him something, but he could not yet decipher what it was. The hand on his shoulder startled him and he turned quickly, striking the intruder with a quick blow to the chest. The figure crumpled to the cold ground.

    Glox stood over him with a look of horror, it was one of his own pupils he had struck. He lay bloodied and bruised at Glox's feet breathing heavily with much effort, he was obviously dying. The pupil reached up toward Glox with something in his hand. Glox took the items from his hand and peered at them intently. There were two items, one was a glistening serpentine scale, the other an ancient stone symbol. Glox recognized the symbol almost immediately with horror. It was the symbol of the Soriz, the ancient Iksar tribe that had been the slaves of the Shissar empire. Glox looked down at his pupil again. "Where did you get this?" The dying Iksar used his last ounce of energy to point up toward the night sky and the moon of Luclin. "sSs... Ssraeshza... has... returned..."

    February 4th

    ** Changes to the EverQuest patcher **


    A little while ago we combined our Test patcher with our Live patcher. The intent was to remove the confusion that having two patchers can cause and to encourage more people to play on the Test server.

    Well, we're going back to the old way. We are going to split the Test patcher and the Live patcher back into two separate programs.

    This means that in order to access the Test Server you will need to run TestEverQuest.exe, which you will find in your EverQuest directory. To play on the Live Servers just start EverQuest as you always have.

    There are a few advantages to separating the two patchers. Splitting them up makes it a little easier, as a player, to be sure that you are headed to the right server when you double click the icon. It's also a little more intuitive for those that want to run the game in two directories.

    Using two directories for EverQuest does use up a lot of hard drive space. However, for those with enough space that play regularly on both Test and on Live servers, this is a safer and faster way to work. Using the same directory means that each time you change servers (Live to Test or Test to Live) you will have to patch. The data on the Test Server is very rarely the same as that on Live servers.

    And for us internally it makes it easier to test new patchers. It gives us one more step for testing. Now we can push a patcher to the Test Server and try it out there after internal testing and before it goes to the Live servers.

    In any case, we've decided to move back to the old way. Starting on Monday, February 4th, 2002 you will need to run TestEverQuest.exe to patch and play on the Test server, and Everquest.exe to patch and play on the Live servers.

    * Patch Message Addendum *

    The command line argument "/test" should no longer be used to play on the Test Server. Anyone using this should remove this argument from their shortcut. Please manually select "TestServer" from the Advanced Options window to use the Test Server. On Monday, February 4th, TestEverQuest.exe will once again be used to patch and play on the Test Server.


    - The EverQuest Team

Absor Infobits - Monday Edition
- Aurum @ 6:31 PM PST
Three new updates were spotted on the Developer's Corner this afternoon. The highlight of the new batch of posts was the Cleric Epic Quest update:

  • Cleric Epic Quest Update
    "Perfect, except for the fact that it can't be done. There isn't a way to link looting to items in the inventory with the current game system. Nobody knows what the future holds, but right now we can't do things like this."

    I beg to differ. We can only loot ONE LORE item. How would the "loot table" know I have one already unless they were not linked? I would beg to differ with your above statement saying it can be done with this chunk of code relating it specifically (through a Lore type link) to the pearl and the box. No, I am not a cleric, but I have played with them extensively.

    Jenrai of the Nexus
    Ranger lost forever in the 45th Circle of Life
    Emarr

    I just wanted to apologize for saying "can't be done" in this post. Obviously given the time and resources such a thing could be done. I should know better than to say "can't", especially considering the talent on our team.

    The proper response is that it can't be done easily with existing code. It would require new code to compare a list of dissimilar items (Lore only works on duplicates of the same item) and then only allow looting if one of the listed items exist in inventory.

    It can't be done with the Lore code as it is. In fact, I suspect it would be easier and more secure to write a new code for this.

    However, there are other ways to handle this situation. Many of the other suggestions would not require new code, just a few new items. And, well, we have to look and decide if this is actually a problem that needs fixing. We're talking about the order of the quest, not the difficulty.

    In any case, we're still evaluating this quest.

  • What is beneficial?
    I have a question regarding the caster archtype alternate advancement ability spell casting deftness. It states that this only works with beneficial spells. In most cases it is clear cut what is a beneficial spell, and what is not, however, in the case of the necromancer, there is some ambiguity I think. So my question is, are the various tap spells that benefit the caster but harm the target considered beneficial or not? Also, are the new group taps considered beneficial? Are some considered beneficial and some not? If so, which ones? I think this question is quite pertinent for any necromancer to have answered, since the value of this ability greatly depends on what spells are considered beneficial.

    Best,
    Lambik

    p.s. Are necro pet's lifetaps still being looked at?

    A 'beneficial' spell is one that helps the caster. This will exclude spells that harm others. Tap spells are not 'beneficial' spells. Beneficial is a category that we use to define spells that are essentially not damage-dealing spells.

    Yes, we're looking at the lifetap thing. I don't know when we'll get it fixed, though.

  • Frostreaver
    Haldraeger-oop wrote (about Frostreaver):

    [Item statistics cut]

    Class: WAR PAL SHD
    Race: HUM BAR DWF TRL OGR IKS

    ==================================================

    Can anyone tell me why a High Elf, Half Elf, Erudite, Dark Elf, Wood elf, Halfling, Vah Sir can't use this Axe? Why isn't it ALL/ALL?

    From a roleplaying perspective why would a Dwarf take an axe from an brethren Dwarf?

    Elves, Erudites and Halflings don't have the mass to wield the axe. Only the bulkier races can use it.

    I have no idea why a Dwarf would take an axe from a Coldain. I certainly can't see why a Dwarf would want to kill one to take his stuff. But that doesn't mean that a Dwarf would be physically unable to use an axe built specifically for use by the Coldain. And I'm sure there are some folks out there playing Dwarves that do have a reason to take the axe, the hard way if needed.

    "It's a trick, get the axe." – poorly quoted - Ash

Beastlords with Holes?
- Aurum @ 7:44 PM PST
In the most recent update to the Developer's Corner, a few Beastlord issues were addressed by Alan and company. Catch the complete post here:

    The team has been thinking about these issues as well. I have a few answers for you. I'll put them in with your questions.

    Just a couple of Beastlord Issues that I would like to bring up.

    - Beastlords do not get the Hybrid ability to not have to wait for weapon delay to refresh before attacking after casting a spell. This ability was given to Rangers/Paladins/ShadowKnights a while ago. Were we overlooked? Or is this intentional?

    That got overlooked. We should have Beastlords delayed at the same rate as other hybrids when we can. How soon I don't know.

    - No level 50+ Disciplines. All Hybrids have 2 class specific Disciplines, except for beastlords. Are these in the works or will we never get any Class specific Disciplines?

    They are designed and in the hands of the programmers. With any luck (not that we have a lot of that…) they'll be in with the next patch. No, I'm not going to give any hints about what they are. :)

    - At level 49 Beastlords are given the Shaman spell Frenzy, this is a great AC buff with some nice stats but it does not stack with the Shaman spell Focus. All of the higher versions of this spell (Rage/Voice of Berserker) were recently changed to be able to stack with Focus, was this spell intentionally left out? Or was Frenzy forgotten?

    We'll take a look at it. This is one of those cases where some measure of 'hard coding' will be needed to make a change. It seems like a reasonable request, but with the dependence on coding time I can't possibly tell you when we'll have a chance to look at it.

    - Finally, Beastlords currently do not have any Class specific AA abilities in the 3rd Tier. All of the AA abilities that we do have are just general Hybrid stuff. Are there any Beastlord specific AA abilities in the works?

    These have not been designed yet. We intend to do so, time permitting. This, however, might take a little while.

    Thanks for taking the time to read this post, the Beastlord class as a whole is great and with just a few holes patched it will be even better.

    Zoka

    Thanks for the comments.

    Alan
