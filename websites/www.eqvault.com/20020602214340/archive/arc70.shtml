Gordon Infobits: Saturday Edition
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:42 PM PDT
The following is this Saturday's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Epic Quests
    [Quote from original post: Frankly your odds of actually getting the loot needed are slim to none when every other mage on your server is vying for same thing. ]

    As I mentioned in another post, this is how it's supposed to work. We *understand* that everyone wants these items. We created them in order to ensure that that would be the case. However we cannot *give* them to everyone without there being serious reprocussions on the balance of power in the world.

    Remember that once these items go in, unless we create UberEpic quest items at some point in the future, they represent the most powerful item for your class in the game. Other held-items pale by comparison. We just can't have everyone running around with them and maintain some semblance of a game.

  • DirectX Upgrade
    [Quote from original post: While visiting test, I noticed that the frame rate does tend to get a bit jerky]

    There are some DX related framerate issues on Test that we're working on, and intend to fix before bringing the new changes live.

  • "Putting Two and Two Together"
    [Quote from original post: -Brad has often said that more and more people are coming into the game.
    -Gordon has often said that they wish to limit the rate at which rare items enter the game.

    Well, maybe, if they put their heads together, and thought about it, they would see that they need to UP THE RATE RARE ITEMS ENTER THE WORLD AT!]

    Your point suggests that new people are all joining and wanting a piece of finite server resources, which isnt' the case. This is already happening. To use your two points above, we've added more servers, so technically items are entering the "game" faster, just by virtue of there being new servers.

    Now on a per-world basis (which is what is important since the economy is measured on a per world basis), the rate at which items come in has also gone up, not by means of percentages, but as a result of item-carrying creatures being killed at a faster rate.

    [Quote from original post: Frankly, I see NO advantage to restricting the rate at which powerful items enter the world, except to keep people playing for longer, which is, of course, the Vision in a nutshell. ]

    Let's imagine a world where: Everyone has everything. Everyone starts at level 60, has an unlimited supply of platinum, and can buy everything off of vendors. Who would play on this server? A lot of people in the beginning, but that would soon drop off to nearly nothing when people realize that there is no challenge in obtaining anything. Item value, and in this case that value is the prestige in overcoming a challenge and having something that isn't too common, is inversely proportional to the availability of the item. This same formula can be applied to levels as well, which describes the "race to level" that exists on new servers.

    So technically, yes, limited item availability is about getting people to subscribe to the game longer. Just as the experience curve does the same thing. But neither of these have a direct correlation. Item availability and speed of advancement correlate directly to challenge, and the prestige associated with overcoming that challenge. It is the challenge that directly correlates to the length of time that you play the game.

    Games are about overcoming challenges. If we cease to provide you challenges to overcome, you will not play the game.

  • Karana
    Karana was split very recently in the grand scheme of things. As it turns out not nearly as many people chose to split as we would have liked, even upon subsequent movelogs where we let it be known that we had tons of room and would be able to service all-comers.

    Secondly, the "2500" figure that was being reported wasn't accurate. There's a problem that we're trying to nail down that causes the world server to stop releasing people when it is supposed to. When this happens, people can't get back into the server, and each attempt causes the population to increment even though they aren't successfully getting back in. We're trying to find out what causes this. It currently happens on one server once or twice a week, making it pretty intermittent.


New Patch Messages Up
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 4:36 PM PDT
Two new Patch Messages went up last night containing some important information:

    October 13th, 7:01pm
    ------------------------------

    *Important System Configuration Announcement*

    As many of you know, over the past couple of months we've made numerous enhancements to the EverQuest client. In addition to things such as new /-commands and other changes of that nature, we've enhanced the UI itself to allow moveable and resizable windows, among other things. At the same time we've been working on a brand new sound engine, and have been further concentrating on optimizing and expanding the capabilities of the EverQuest client.

    For most applications, the feature set of a version of DirectX is limited to the lowest common denominator between the version that the software is compiled under, and the version running on the client. This means that though you may be running DirectX 7.0a already, and are receiving advantages associated with the more advanced code, the feature-set of DirectX used when in EverQuest is limited to version 5.2.

    This limits the type and breadth of enhancements that can be made to the client. Given that we feel that EverQuest players would enjoy continuing to see new enhancements for the client, we will soon begin compiling the client under DirectX 7.0a, and implementing the feature-set available within that version. When we do this, everyone will need DirectX 7.0a (or higher) to play EverQuest.

    Here are some of the possible benefits from DirectX 7.0a:

    - Better performance (Graphics and Sound)
    - Gamma Correction working in D3D
    - Proper Mip-Mapping in D3D
    - Fixes fogging on some cards. (Some cards have no fogging at all and look pretty bad)
    - Allows us to use newer interfaces to support such features as full-screen anti-aliasing, and alternate Z-buffering methods, which tend to look better.

    Changing to DX7 does have a potential downside. Those using older or discontinued video or sound cards that do not have DX7 compatible drivers may need to upgrade to continue playing. However, we feel that the benefits that will come from upgrading to this newer version of DirectX outweigh the potential problems.

    We plan to begin requiring DirectX7.0a when the EverQuest client is next patched, in two or three weeks. We wanted to be sure to give everyone enough time to check their version of DirectX and install the newest version. You can run DXDIAG.EXE in your Windows\System directory to check your DirectX version and get the newest version of DirectX from http://www.microsoft.com/directx if it is needed.

    Please keep an eye on the patch message for further information regarding the planned implementation of DX7.

    - The EverQuest Team

    October 13th, 7:00pm
    ------------------------------

    *Epic Quests*

    Due to some difficulties regarding the Epic Quests implemented two patches ago, the epic quests for the Monk, Wizard, and Druid will not be completable until the next patch. While we would like to have implemented updates right away for these issues, we are mid-way in our conversion to support the DirectX changes that have been announced.

    We appreciate your patience.

    - The EverQuest Team

Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:57 PM PDT
Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Deleted Posts
    [Quote from original post: When customers have legitimate concerns, do you just delete posts at a whim?]

    The thread had far too many personal attacks in it. New posts to the thread were either additional personal attacks or complaints about the personal attacks. Though that might not have been the original subject of the thread, that's what it turned in to and why it was deleted.

    I'm not going to spend a whole lot of time sending individual posts to the moderator for cleaning. If a thread seems to attract more offending posts than proper ones, we'll just delete the whole thread.

  • Halting a Spell
    While I've never heard of a 2 second pause being insufficient, I have seen this effect before and imagine that it has something to do with the way prediction is implemented. The client and server do not send position data constantly while you are running, but instead update position data either at a regular interval, or when something significant changes. Basically, the server thinks you are still moving even though from your point of view you are stopped.

    The trick here is to be sure that the server receives the "I stopped" packet before it gets the "I started casting" packet. The best way around this I've found is to turn slightly upon stopping. I always had a much easier time of getting my spells off once I started doing that.

  • Spellcasting Bug
    This has happened a few times in the past, and is caused by a network problem on our end. It's a fairly quick fix not requiring server downtime. We apologize for the problem.

  • Working as Intended
    [Quote from original post: It procs a dmg absorption shield and Berserker Strength. When the shield is used up, the Strength componenet is still active.]

    You might want to take another look. The berserker strength spell does include a "skin", however the strength portion does not remain when the shield is used up.

    That said, we are kicking around the idea of removing that component from the bard songs based on it being a bad combination.

  • The Wishlist Board
    I've posted before on the subject. Though people here read those boards regularly, we'll rarely respond to things on the wishlist board. Wishes, by their nature, are things that are unlikely to happen. Responses on that board would typically be along the lines of "I'm sorry but..." or "That's a good idea but..."

    The board would become a negative focus because we would constantly be justifying why wishes either don't fit into EQ, or are good but not something that we are likely to implement. Right now those boards are a good idea resource, but I fear that they would degenerate if we were over there responding to posts regularly.

Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 5:54 PM PDT
A massive number of new posts were recently made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • Possible Coin Bug
    There was a bug at one point that would crash characters if there were a large number of coins on the ground in the viewable area. I'd thought that all known instances of this problem were corrected. I'll forward the thread to Ester and ask her to have a look.

  • Giving All Classes Copies of Other Weapons
    [Quote from original post: Why not give All casting classes +20 to INT/WIS and MANA+50? ]

    We sincerely dislike the idea of making items mere copies of other items available to other classes. It's often a cheap way to go, and we prefer variety.

  • Online Store Not Secured?
    [Quote from original post: Ok I just received an email from their support people. If you right click on the page and select properties, it show that the page really is secure. I don't know why the lock doesn't show though. ]

    Frames.... The status bar will only show a lock if the primary frame is secure. The primary frame in this case is the NavBar at the top of the page.

    The page on which you enter your credit card info is however secure.

  • Stacking Problems
    [Quote from original post: (yes I'm stacking same - same runes) ]

    I've not heard of problems stacking identical words/runes before. I imagine that you might have run into a situation where you had two runes of the same name (aiza, beza, etc.), that weren't really identical.

    I'm going to look into having the problems with unmarked differentiated runes and see if we can get their names changed.

  • Guides and Ressurection
    [Quote from original post: When a guide performs a ressurection on a player due to non-avoidable deaths such as system disconnects or bugs, the guide or GM still uses the level 49 cleric spell ressurection. However, with the new spell at 56 could it be adjusted then to allow guides to grant this ressurection spell over the 49? ]

    While substandard XP return from a GM or Guide resurrection was a problem about six months ago, it wasn't precisely a problem related to the level of the spell. It was a case where the XP return was being modified by the XP modifier for the zone, causing it to return less experinece.

    In short, this was fixed several months ago, and GM and Guide resurrections have been returning 100% of lost experience since then.

  • Veeshan's Peak Changes
    I believe that it was said when we were looking into the issue that existing items wouldn't be changed. I understand that you and your team worked for the items, and now there are new items to get. This is one of those cases where the door swings both ways: There are items in game that no longer drop, or were reduced in effectiveness, where the stats on existing items weren't changed. Now there are items that were enhanced where existing items weren't changed. There are people in your guild have the "Guise of the Deceiver" and a couple of other items that aren't available any longer, and we haven't asked them to trade those in.

    I'm sure that you and your guild will go in and get the newer items regardless, you now have some lesser items that you can pass down or sell to others that you work with.
    --
    [Quote from original post: I believe i read Someoen You Can pettiton a Gm For a replacement ]

    That's only in the case where an item displays "out of charges" when attempting to proc. Those items have to be traded out once the fix is made and moved to the live servers.

  • PoS Belts
    I know that when the belts were designed it was determined that they'd have some of the best values out there. If they are indeed less than the FBSS they are certainly something that we should look into.

    Anyone have the names of the belts handy? I'll look them up.

  • Bard Song #12 Nerfed?
    I recall this same topic coming up about 6 months ago. I'm not sure what the circumstances are that is causing people to think that the spell has had its range reduced, but we haven't adjusted it.

  • Renting Rooms
    [Quote from original post: I remeber Eons ago verant Claimed we would be able to rent rooms And such In everquest but that it would be very expensive This was like as Eq was released Whatever happened to this? another broken or forgotten promise?]

    It wasn't broken or forgotten, but was ruled out as a feature in early beta. We've said since then that it wasn't something that was going to be part of EverQuest. Though we still like the idea, again it's an issue of compatability with the game world.

  • More on Epic Quest Weapons
    [Quote from original post: What was decided in the meeting that was supposed to happen today?]

    The specific changes made won't be something that will be announced. While I'm aware that there are plenty of spoilers out there and enough people who know the stats on the items that have been found, having the information *available* and *forcing* people to read it (by virtue of it being quoted in one of my posts) are two different things entirely.
    --
    Just to clarify, though we won't be announcing the specific changes, we'll tell you if there are any negative changes once everything is worked out.

  • Bugs Have To Go!
    This reminds me of a /petition that was filed once and later posted on the humor section of the guide board. The petition read "I died to a bug". I don't recall the exact text, but apparently several /tells were exchanged before the guide became aware that the person had died to a beetle in Unrest.

    The /petitioner was of course being humorous :)

  • A Bug?
    [Quote from original post: "This isn't a case of difficulty. The names aren't over skeletons heads by design. Skeletons are supposed to be inconspicious."]

    The point here was that we didn't want them sticking out like a sore thumb when they are sitting as a pile of bones on the ground. The idea was that they'd leap up and surprise someone who wasn't paying close attention to the situation.

    I'll tell you what, I'll talk to the guys about displaying their name if they are standing, but not when they are a pile of bones. I'm not sure if we can queue off of the position, but I'll check. Would that work out?

  • Accidental Movelog Activation
    [Quote from original post: But if Torvy splits in the far future, it won't still split me because I entered that a long time ago will it? ]

    No, you'll be fine. Though the /movelog command is always active, we always clear the files before officially opening registration for a move.

  • When is the Direct X 7.0 Patch?
    It's looking to be around 2 to 3 weeks. I hope to get it more widely publicized in the next day or so.

  • Con'ing Yourself
    [Quote from original post: I /con white to myself but my dots/spells always stick to me on the first cast.. for FULL DAMAGE..]

    First, DOTs are all-or-nothing spells and always do full damage (notwithstanding PvP) assuming that the target lives to see it.

    Second, you probably have resists lower than the creatures you are fighting. Try having someone cast a resist spell on you and see how you do.

    Finally, as for it being the first cast, versus a subsequent cast, it's all random...well, as random as it can get given constrants on computed random values.

  • Epic Quests
    [Quote from original post: Having to get [spoiler removed] a rare drop from a rare spawn is ridiculous]

    The epic quest rewards are designed to be rare. It isn't expected that they will be something that everyone can get. While I appreciate the different points of view regarding items that are this rare, this is how we envisioned these items. They are meant to be roughly one step below an artifact by current definition.

  • FAQs
    Most of the dead issues are transient in nature, only sticking around for a week or two, making a FAQ an inappropriate place for them. They are also typically class-based, and as I want the FAQ to be of interest to all, haven't listed most of them.

    Things like the melee binding, which is more persistant than the antagonist of a scary movie, are the types of things that I wanted in the FAQ.

    We will be freezing certain aspects of the game, particularly certain class-issues around January. It might be time to create a class-based portion of the FAQ at that time.

  • Higher Level Spells
    The components don't resist separatly. Spells only save versus one resist, and only save once unless special code is put in to have them do something else, such as with Lullaby.
    The magic resistance probably makes it a bad spell component combo. When the coder comes in tomorrow I'll see if that has any possible positive effect. If not, we'll look at another potential component.
    --
    [Quote from original post: You don't understand how charm work. At every tick (or so) your charmed pet will make a MR check to see if he can break your charm. That is why charm duration appears to be random. Lowering his MR will generally make the charm last longer. ]

    That's certainly a possibility, but I'll find out for certain tomorrow.

  • Win 2K
    [Quote from original post: I used to run EQ under Win2K professional but found that it was not entirely supported. I'd just like to know whether the client will be more Win2k friendly once we patch to a DX7.0 version.]

    We're setting up a Win2K testbed to check out the new client stuff. We'll be sure that everything is compatible before making the changes live.

  • "What Draws Social Degenerates to EQ?"
    [Quote from original post:social degenerates]

    While I wouldn't use the term "social degenerates", I do think that "grief players" more appropriate. EverQuest tries to limit the extent that grief players can affect the normal player's gameplay, which leads to other, hopefully less-intrusive methods of acting out.

    I attribute the "grief player" phenomenon to both anonymity, and a reduced self-esteem in real-life. Given anonymity, and the lack of real consequences (besides perhaps removal from the gaming environment), some people try to create or improve their own self-esteem by affecting others. Affecting others adversely provides stronger and more immediate feedback than doing so positively.

  • Global Graphics File
    There are two issues. One is textures. When EQ was released the system requirements were a 4MB video card (which has 2MB of texture memory). Any time there are more than 2MB of textures that need to be viewable, the card (assuming that it is a voodoo1) swaps textures in and out to disk, causing a large drop in framerate. Increasing textures to the point where it is likely that that occurs causes decreased performance for those closer to the minimum specs.

    The second issue is models. Global model definitions are kept in a global file, or files now. These files are quite large, often being something that we don't want to modify and require download of too often. Optional downloads aren't an option in this case because we can't have two separate versions of the same file pass as "valid".

  • Broken Quests Notice
    [Quote from original post: B4 I put my foot in mouth, is there a place where we can find out about bugs/brokens quests. If there isn's there should be. This would be a perfect example of "wasting time" trying to do something that cant be done. Please inform us, it's our right to know what doesnt work in the game.]

    While I agree that broken quests are a sincere pain, it would be very difficult to publicize the existance of a broken quest without that publication containing spoiler information. We are making some changes in the way that we handle things in the near future that should drastically reduce our propensity to implement broken quests and take care of the ones that are currently having problems.

  • NPCs Vs. PCs
    Given that we want EverQuest to promote grouping, the only way we could equalize NPC power at a given level with PC power at a given level and maintain that would be to increase the number of NPCs in nearly every encounter. Since PCs usually find ways to take on singular NPCs even when they are grouped (and we've designed for that), equal NPC power would trivialize most major encounters.
    --
    [Quote from original post: Only when mob AI is capable of using player tactics can you let things be equal.]

    That is, of course, another good point which I mentioned in the FAQ but didn't mention in my last post. It's certainly an issue that we need to take into account, along with that of the prospect of multiplying the number of NPCs in the world by six, and reducing the experience given by each NPC by the same factor.

  • Non-destructive Backspace

    At some point in the future we will most likely post some basic fiction guidelines. Until then, a good guideline is to refrain from violence against children.

    We have some good plans for tracking in the future, but I apologize for them being something that we can't implement "now" per your request.

    We'll release more information when we are certain of what we can implement. It's being done by Rowyl, so given his previous contributions, I would expect something good.

  • "Persistant Worlds Require Persistant Players"
    [Quote from original post: A virtual world that is operational 24 hours a day everyday can be extremely demanding on the players.     Since players cannot play 24 hours a day, they have had a tendency to build friendships with other players who play on similar schedules. That's really one of the nice things about being in a world with enough other people: The opportunity to find other people with similar interests and lifestyles.

Velious will be an expansion-only product. In other words, someone buying EverQuest who also wants access to Velious will have to buy the full box (which includes Kunark), plus the Velious expansion.

    [Quote from original post: The Scars of Velious will be a traditional expansion in that it will add new zones, mobs, items and even some new character models. It will not up the XP Level cap or give any new playable races or classes]

    Actually Velious will indeed include the ability to exceed level 50, even if you don't already have Kunark.

  • Expansion Status
    The expansion is coming along very well at the moment. Everything is ahead of schedule right now with no problems expected. Besides the additional art and content, there's also a significant amount of work to be done on the operations side, which is going along quite well as well.

  • Shaman Issues Recap
    I'd like to get all of these shaman threads under one if we could. Personally, I'm having a hard time sorting out what the real issues are, because I'm seeing as many contradictions out there as the ones that are being attributed to me.

    On one hand, someone makes a complaint about the damage that the weapon does, so I say that shaman don't do an appreciable amount of damage via melee.
