Bard Spell Issues, Part Deux
- Aurum @ 3:11 PM PST
The current spell problems with the Bard class received further attention by SOE's Alan VanCouvering on the Developer's Corner. Here's the skinny:

    Last night we took down each server for a short time to patch a fix for bards that couldn't sing songs. We also fixed a faction problem with wolf form.

    We are aware of other issues, and we're getting them fixed as fast as we can.

    Thanks for your patience.

    Alan
Another update was posted an hour later:
    "Last night we took down each server for a short time to patch a fix for bards that couldn't sing songs. We also fixed a faction problem with wolf form."

    With all due respect, it is absolutely imperative that you understand that your server reset last night did *NOT* fix the bard song problem. I logged on and tested it after the server reset and found the problem remains, and even replied to your first post to make sure you understood that the problem remains. Every bard player on the bard messageboard continues to experience this problem. My level 60 bard with 236 days played is stuck in town because he is completely helpless without songs. This is why it concerns me that you somehow think the problem has been fixed.

    Please listen to us when we tell you it is broken instead of trying to tell us it has been fixed. It is difficult to remain patient when you seem to think that the problem has been resolved when it clearly has not.

    I would be more than happy to demonstrate this problem to you or do anything else in my power to help you identify this problem and get it resolved. Thank you for your time.

    Suladan
    Innoruuk


    Actually, that was an error on my part.

    We did indeed fix a bug with bards, but it wasn't a complete fix. I'm sorry, I misunderstood what I was told last night.

    We will be patching again tonight to try fix as many things as we can. Certainly the broken bard songs are on the priority list, and we hope to be able to get a fix for that in tonight.

    Alan

EverQuest No Longer Supports Windows 95
- Aurum @ 7:50 PM PST
If you are running Windows 95 on your computer, you probably no longer are able to run EverQuest on that machine. On the Developers Corner, Alan had this to say about it:

    Because Windows 95 does not support DirectX 8.1 and our new and improved EverQuest engine requires the use of this version of DirectX, we can no longer support Windows 95.

    We apologize for this inconvenience but in order to play EverQuest and enjoy the benefits of the new engine you must upgrade to Windows 98 or greater and install DirectX 8.1.

    Alan

Patch Message: 12-01-01
- Aurum @ 6:45 PM PST
Regarding the eqclient.ini, this message was published to the Patch Server:

    *EQClient INI Setting*

    There's some confusion about the information that's been released on the Test server over the past couple of days, and that confusion is resulting in some people having trouble playing EverQuest. To clear it up:

    The GraphicsDLL setting in your EQClient.ini file should read "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx7.dll" regardless of what server you are playing on or what version of DirectX you are running. If you are having problems getting in to EverQuest, specifically, problems crashing prior to reaching character select, please check and set this entry in your EQClient.ini as necessary.

    Also, as a reminder, all EverQuest subscribers will need to have DirectX 8.1 or better installed on their system in order to play beginning Tuesday morning. The latest version of DirectX can be found at http://www.microsoft.com/directx.

    - The EverQuest Team

Supported by a complete graphic overhaul, the enhanced gameplay featured in Luclin will allow EverQuest players to witness their world in unprecedented splendor.

The Vah Shir are agile warriors and silent stalkers fighting for the honor of their king and kin. This new catlike race allows players to hunt the dark side of Luclin with their excellent night vision.

This will allow players to traverse greater distances in shorter periods of time and even battle from their mounts.

This texture upgrade will allow EverQuest players to view Norrath with unparalleled clarity and beauty, enriching the overall gameplay experience.

  • Server Problems
    We're very sorry about the problems some folks are experiencing this morning. We've found a fix for those that are having a problem being kicked out after they choose "play online".

    Here's a copy of the patch message.

    quote:

    November 29th, 1:30 pm
    ------------------------------

    ** For those having problems logging in **

    If you are having a problem that causes you to be kicked out of the game after selecting "Play on line", there is a solution to this problem.

    Delete the file "bmpwad6.s3d" from your EverQuest directory.

    This should solve the problem.

    - The EverQuest Team

    ------------------------------

    *Your help is requested*

    As we announced previously, we are beginning testing of the new DirectX 8 graphics engine that will become a central component of EverQuest. This new graphics engine will go live mid-next week, and will require that all EverQuest players have DirectX 8 or better installed, along with suitable drivers from your video and sound card manufacturers.

    The new engine will include many features including a new visibility solution which will dramatically increase framerate in some areas of the game, and in addition will eventually support cards featuring Hardware Transform and Lighting (GeForce/Radeon or better cards).

To do this, please launch TestEverquest.exe from your EverQuest directory, and edit the line in your EQClient.ini in your EQ directory from "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx7.DLL" to "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx8.DLL".

    Thank you for your help.

    Let me start by restating that we appreciate any help we can get with this. Thanks.

    There are a couple of important things to be sure of when you try this out.

    1) You need to have DirectX 8.0 or better on your system.
    2) You need to make sure you have drivers for you sound and video cards that support DX8
    3) You then just have to edit your eqclient.ini file, changing "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx7.DLL" to "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx8.DLL". All you really need to do is change the 7 to an 8. :)

    If you experience problems, the first thing to do is make sure you've got steps one, two and three right. After that, send us your information to the email listed above. And if you can't get it working and you just want to play, all you have to do is go back and change that 8 back to a 7 and you'll be using the old dll file.
