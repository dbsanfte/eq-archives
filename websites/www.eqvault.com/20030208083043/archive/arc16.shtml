Sudden Changes, Part 3
- Aurum @ 9:29 PM PST
Speaking of the Developer's Corner, another recent update was made to the Sudden Changes section. Check it out here:

    I know of the binding NPC's intresting though I had to pay for the spell and level my self up to get it. Shouldent it cost something? Would it not be to unfair for the binding npc to ask for 5gold or something?

    Yes, you did have to level up a bit and pay a few gold to earn the spell as a caster. But for you the spell is far more versatile than the soulbinders, easily justifying the effort and cost. While we certainly could have charged for the service from an NPC, we decided just to make it free. So far it seems as though this was the right decision.

    I think we're pretty happy with the soulbinders as they are, so I don't think you'll see any changes to them.

Luclin Update #5
- Aurum @ 8:29 PM PST
Another large Luclin Update was posted by Mr. VanCouvering on the Developer's Corner today. It's brings us up to speed on where things are at with the 3rd expansion:

    I apologize for the length of time since the last update. We've been trying to get a good grip on these problems, but some of them are elusive and have evaded our efforts thus far. We have made good progress on several of them, and expect to have some good results for you soon. Please note the italicized comments next to some of the entries.

    We appreciate your patience as we work these few remaining issues out.

    ---

    We're still working on some issues and we've fixed others. Keep in mind that we have a bunch of people working on things, so most of these items are on the top of someone's list.

    While some of these issues only affect a tiny portion of our customers, we want to give a full and complete rundown of issues we are aware of. We will be keeping you updated as things change. Also, check the patch messages for more information.

    Stuff in the works, some of these will be fixed with the next patch (in no particular order, most are priority one for the person working on them):

    - Reducing loading and zoning times (Note: we have a possible improvement for this on the Test server as of 1-8-02 and hope to have it fixed Live soon)
    - There is a very short pause for some players when other players enter a zone (Note: we have a possible fix for this on the Test server as of 1-8-02 and hope to have it fixed Live soon)
    - Crashes occurring for some people when logging in
    - Some characters being 'stuck' if game crashed while playing that character
    - Choppy, buggy, faint or flat sounds for some sound configurations.
    - Some systems have mouse pointers which are being lost or duplicated
    - Characters changing to wolf form look tiny
    - Some spells (mostly 'bolt' spells) not displaying effects
    - Faces that sometimes don't remain as selected
    - New Necromancer group debuff spells are not working/displaying properly (all fixed at this time except for Mind Wrack)
    - A few players are being disconnected at random
    - Partial transparencied NPCs are still showing up half invisible/behind things they're supposed to be in front of.
    - Wizard pet buff spells do not appear to be functioning properly
    - Druid pets, either charmed or 55 castable, cannot be buffed
    - Familiars die when the wizard cast invisibility. When this happens no harm comes to the wizard, but the plan it to have them remain when the caster is invisible
    - Luclin ports trying to take people that don't have Luclin (or the Luclin portal stone fragment) and dumping them at the 'safe point' in the zone
    - Spell Reinforcement: Only the person who has the AA has their buffs last longer. There is no change in the most practical case: A person with the AA buffing another person. The other person does not get the added time
    - Slow framerate on some fast computers*

    *Every bug has its story. This one begins as an incompatibility between EverQuest, AMD processors and Umbra (a technology that, ironically, speeds up framerates). There is a known problem in the Umbra engine that was causing the crashes that some AMD users were originally seeing in Shadowhaven. In our desire to make sure that both Intel and AMD users could enter the game, we introduced another condition that unfortunately slowed down framerates in certain circumstances. We have one person working on nothing but this at the moment and he's made significant progress. We expect this problem to go away soon.


    Fixed since release (in no particular order either):

    - Bard songs and group buffs (these were related problems)
    - Familiars no longer get added to hate lists and don't cause you to crash if you die
    - Wolf form factions with the new models are now in line with intent
    - Several face/hair issues, such as not being allowed to choose bald and some facial hair.
    - /follow bug that caused folks to crash
    - Transparency issues with i810 display adapters
    - Crash bug related to root and gate
    - Various item and content fixes
    - Crash problems in certain zones
    - K6 Processors: We're working to fix, or provide a work-around for problems AMD K6 users are experiencing when entering some zones. Shadowhaven is one of those zones.
    - A bug that was giving classes that had the old experience penalties more alternate advancement experience than they should have been getting.
    - Some people are seeing Vah Shir as male humans
    - Working on getting facial hair tinting for the half-elves, dark elves, high elves, and dwarf females working
    - Some players are crashing in Hollowshade Moor
    - A few people that don’t have Shadows of Luclin installed are seeing "Entering Luclin" when logging in and then crashing
    - A bug exists that causes random crashes when exiting game to server select, and then going back into game.
    - Large fonts and text that extends outside their boxes is affecting a few users
    - New Bard Area of Effect songs are acting strangely


    Note: The inability to adjust gamma setting with some cards is an issue with cards that just don't support that ability. It's not really something we can fix in code. We are investigating to see if there are any possible workarounds out there for these older cards that are no longer supported by the manufacturer.


    Tech Tip
    - Pauses with Win 98/ME ONLY: A possible solution for those with a fair amount of RAM (technically, anyone with 128 megs or more…) who are seeing pauses while the hard drive runs a bit, might benefit from this simple change (it's also easy to undo). It might also improve load and zone times.

    Add the following line to the [386Enh] section of the SYSTEM.INI file: ConservativeSwapfileUsage=1

    For best results, change only this setting (don't change a bunch of other stuff at the same time) and see how it performs. And if it isn't better or seems worse you can just erase that line from your System.ini file. I should tell you that this change might make EverQuest better and it might also cost you some performance with your system overall, though this does not seem to be the case for most people. Keep that in mind if you decide to use this tip.


    Thank you all for your patience. We are working hard to get these issues straightened out as soon as possible.

    Alan


Patch Message: 01-08-01
- Aurum @ 2:08 PM PST
This was the message that was published on the Patch Server this morning:

    January 8th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------
    ** Items **

    - All beastlord-usable items are now useable by their requisite races
    - Regular, medium quality, high quality, mithril, brellium and adamantite rings, as well as their enchanted versions are now stackable. (this time we mean it)
    - Made Rune Encrusted Short Sword rentable (was No Rent, should not have been)
    - Fixed Shadowscream and all related recipes so that they return the correct mallet
    - Reduced the price of the Fuligan Soulstone of Innoruuk and Cloudy Stone of Veeshan
    - Reduced the price of the first two keys for the Plane of Air
    - Coterie Mask of the Dead has been retroactively changed to have 5 charges as originally intended
    - Gauntlets of the Blood Fiend - Warrior has been removed from the wearable classes
    - Shiny Trinket of Speed - An appropriate level of haste has been added to this item


    ** Interface **

    - Added /tgb as a short version of /targetgroupbuff

    - /shownames now works with the following parameters:
    no parameter = display the current setting.
    off = don't show player names over their heads
    1 = show only first names of players
    2 = show First and Last Name
    3 = show First, Last, and Guild (if any)
    4 = show Title (if any), First, Last, and Guild (if any)

    Also, the LFG, AFK and LD tags will always show if names are not off. There will no longer be brackets around them, and LFG will now appear at the end of the name with the AFK and LD tags (instead of being a * at the beginning)


    - A player character will always have a name over its head, even if they are skeletons (unless /shownames is off), with the exception of some rare cases when players are turned into certain NPCs
    - Added /ttell. This will send a tell to the player character that is currently your target. The command will also allow corpses as a valid target. /ttell'ing a player's corpse will auto-tell that player (this was actually patched earlier along with an emergency patch)
    - Added IRC style '/me' as a synonym for /emote (this was actually patched earlier along with an emergency patch)
    - Added '/send' as a synonym for /tell (this was actually patched earlier along with an emergency patch)


    ** Spells **

    - Necromancer Group spells should now be working, they were only affecting the caster. This includes spells such as: Degeneration, Succession of Shadows, Crippling Claudication, and Zavfeer's Theft of Vitae. These spells have also had their duration increased. Mind Wrack will be fixed soon.
    - Shadowknight "Torrent" spells have also been fixed and have had their duration increased as well
    - Flowing Thought effects will now stack
    - Meditating on a horse occurs only while not in combat
    - Mark of Retribution should work as intended
    - The Decession spell now requires you to be in the same group
    - Levitation can no longer be used in The Deep
    - Fixed a problem that was still allowing people to Cast Eye of Zomm in Ssra Temple
    - Fixed a problem that was still allowing people to cast Call of the Hero in Ssra Temple
    - NPCs that cannot be stunned will once again take damage from damaging stun spells (this was actually patched earlier along with an emergency patch)
    - Critical heals and nukes now display damage numbers (this was actually patched earlier along with an emergency patch)


    ** Zones **

    - Characters will no longer take falling damage when in the Bazaar
    - Characters can now bind in the Nexus


    ** Alternate Advancement **

    - Made it so the percentages of Spell Casting Fury Mastery scale a bit more
    - Fixed Soul Abrasion to work with a few spells that it didn't work with before
    - Dire Charm *should* no longer break (this was actually patched earlier along with an emergency patch)
    - Mental Clarity and Body/Mind Rejuvenation now give mana regeneration both when standing and medding, as intended


    ** Miscellaneous **

    - Added a few new socials: /nudge, /pat, /flex, /fidget, /purr and /smack
    - Changed the automatic targeting code so that it won't automatically target someone that cast a beneficial spell on you
    - Adjusted the timing of the Luclin Spire activations to more closely match the announcements by the Scions. This has the net effect of reducing the time between portal activations


    - The EverQuest Team

  • Texture Cache Follow-up
    I'm not sure if this has been asked before, but why were/are the textures loaded over the network in the first place? It seems like a waste of bandwidth to have relatively static data sent over the network every time a client connects, as compared to storing it on client machines and updating it with the patcher. My guess is that it was done that way to keep disk space requirements down, since we didn't all have hard drives in excess of 20GB back when Everquest was first released. Am I right?

    Whatever the answer is, I appreciate the addition of the texture cache. It's also amazingly cool that I have a way to pose this question to an authoritative source. Other commercial software shops could learn a few things from you guys. Maybe you should write a book. :-)

    No textures are loaded over the Internet. It's all on your hard drive. They are, however, loaded into RAM, and that's what takes time. The process is something like this (and I might be wrong on some of the fine points...)

    The data for the textures is stuffed onto your hard drive when you install the game, but they are installed in a compressed format (it's more complicated than just compression, but that's the basic idea).
    When you load the game, these files are decompressed (again, not the right term, but close enough) and put into RAM.
    It takes time to decompress these files. It also takes time to copy them into RAM.
    The texture cache just decompresses these files and stores them in a format that can be copied right into RAM. But putting all that data into RAM still takes time.

    So you're right, we are trying to use as little hard drive space as we can during installation. But all the textures on your hard drive, and they are indeed updated with the patcher and stored on your hard drive.

    Heh, I might be authoritative, but I don't know much. Just enough to ask the right questions of the right people.

    And I don't much like reading technical manuals, I sure wouldn't want to write one. :)

  • Sudden Changes
    Firstly, I would like to thank you for your continuing effort to keep EverQuest alive. I once wondered if EverQuest would become a one-off game, which fades out after a year or so. So far you guys have been great!

    One little thing that I have noticed in the recent era - you have been changing 'little' things which we (players) had so passionately argued for in the past. For instance the stackability of pelts and trade skill items, and the recent addition of 'Soulbinders'.

    In the past, as you know, quite a lot of us requested for such 'little' changes that brainstormed the players. With these issues, the voices on the players' end were nearly always unanimous (unlike with class balancing issues where there were usually conflicts among players). A pattern then emerged gradually - you always refused to change them by saying that things were to remain as they were for the best. This pattern repeated itself over and over.

    I have been seeing the changes being applied gradually, though. Is this a general change in attitude among the developpers? I don't believe that one side was wrong up until a certain point and suddenly become right - so I'm sure you would see what I mean when I say that it seems the general attitude you have towards the game seems to be different from that of, say, this time a year ago.

    Either way, it's a good thing. I think it is. And I want you to know that I love EverQuest. I think you have done VERY well overall. We all know nothing/noone is perfect - and within the limits you have done an excellent work. At least, you are looking at (or reading a message from) a person who's enjoying the work you have done. I just really wanted to ask that question just because I'm somewhat a little surprised to find the changes occuring in the areas where you so PASSIONATELY refused to change in the past.

    I'm not really expecting a reply on this, to be honest, but I would want to know (before the day I die) how things advanced for you guys within the team. I would never find that out unless you tell the story, and you are likely not to bother talking about these minor issues unless asked.

    Once again, I would like to thank you for continuing to improve EverQuest. You guys are really wonderful!

    Carline Clair

    Well, there really are two considerations involved here: team dynamics and the health of the game itself.

    Probably the most important thing is the team dynamic. We are just like any other group of people. One day we can all agree that the sky is blue. But over time we can be convinced, as individuals or as a group, that the sky is in fact yellow. As the team changes, as the game expands, and as the game itself changes, so do we. Especially when you're talking about the soulbinder thing. I think it's fair to say that we've been on the fence about it as a group for a long time. There really are reasonable arguments for and against doing it. We have debated this issue in several forms since I started here.

    Very few changes that we make are sudden. But for those that aren't here, in meetings and sitting around the lunch table, I can see how it might seem like a sudden switch.

    I think it's also important to understand that once we add something like this, it's fair to say that we really can't take it back. So when we're on the fence, when arguments on both sides are equal and the net result is hard to determine beforehand, it is probably always better to make the mistake that is reversible.

    Focusing on the soulbinders as an example, we don't know how these NPCs will affect the interaction between players. We don't know how it will affect the economy for lower level PCs, who can often make a good living being helpful to others by binding them. On the other hand, adding these NPCs reduces the frustration level for some players that play at off-peak times when finding a bind can be a problem.

    You also talked about how these changes are being applied gradually. We never really want to introduce a ton of changes at one time. Despite the seemingly popular desire for soulbinders, I'm already getting plenty of mail from people that don't like it (those are the people that made money selling binds). And I, for one, am convinced that this is one change will affect the social dynamics of the game, perhaps in a negative way. Before making any other changes that might affect the social nature of the game, it seems like a wise idea to examine the effects of any similar changes made previously.


    So we make changes one at a time when we can. It makes it easier to judge the overall effect on the game world.

    I read plenty of message boards. I think I'm fairly well aware of the desires of those that post about EverQuest (though I am also aware that there are many people that just play the game and never come to message boards at all, either to post or read, and we have to try to be aware of those people too). There have been a few times since I took this job that we've implemented a change based on what seemed like the desire of the majority of players, only to receive an overwhelming amount of email from people that didn't like the change. It is very hard to claim that any suggestion has the near unanimous support of the player base. So we try to be careful making changes. EverQuest is a great game right now. It's hard to say what changes might make it better and what changes might only seem to make it better. We have to use our judgement on these things as best we can.

    Anyway, those are my thoughts on the issue. :)
