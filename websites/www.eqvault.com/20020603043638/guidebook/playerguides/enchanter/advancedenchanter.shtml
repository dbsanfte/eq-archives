Advanced Player Guide: Enchanter

Author: Vendan Fivemiles (N/A)   Organization: N/A

Introduction
So, you want to be an Enchanter do you? Like to play one of the most powerful, rewarding and well-designed classes in the game? Want to be able to charm that fearsome Hill Giant and send it on a rampage? Want to make your companions invincible and endow them with supernatural physical prowess? With time, you can do all of these things and more.

The Enchanter class is powerful, but it is not power in the sense that we can destroy a monster with fire, ice or lightning, or create fearsome, unshakable pets to do our bidding. The power of an Enchanter is subtler. Our power comes into play indirectly for the most part. By increasing and streamlining the efficiency of the group, we can drastically tip the scales in the players' favor. Be forewarned though, as incredible as it sounds to you right now, the advancement of an Enchanter can be a very frustrating and difficult undertaking. It requires a good deal of practice to get the skills down, as well as patience.

Whether you are merely a casual player, new to the game or in for the long haul from level one to fifty, I hope this guide will provide you with the basic knowledge and skills to become a great Enchanter.

Race Selection
For starters, lets cover the race selection, which is the first step you must take to become an enchanter.

Enchanters can be: Dark Elves Erudites High Elves Humans Gnomes

Each race has its particulars, Dark elves, Erudites and Gnomes all have high starting intelligence but suffer from low charisma. Humans have average stats while High Elves have good starting intelligence and the highest charisma. Some races have special abilities, such as the Dark Elves with Ultravision and hide, Gnomes, the ability to tinker see in the dark like a High Elf with Infravision. Humans and Erudites have normal eyesight, and cannot see in the dark without assistance. Stat-wise, High Elves are arguably the best enchanter race, with Dark Elves being quite popular as well. Nevertheless, variety is the spice of life and eventually any race can achieve both high INT and CHA. Choose what appeals to you most is the best advice here.

Bonus Point Distribution
Once you select a race, you will have a number of bonus points to add to your stats in any way you decide. The stats highlighted in green are the Primary Stats, which is to say they are the most important to the success of your character.

Primary Stats
The two most important stats to an enchanter are charisma and intelligence. Charisma affects several things, namely the prices at which NPC vendors will buy and sell goods, the chance of a target resisting your charm spells and the duration of the charm, and lastly the targets chance of resisting a mesmerize spell. Intelligence will affect how much mana your character has and how quickly certain skills will increase. It will also decrease your fizzle rate. Both are important and its best to only spend your skill points on these two stats. Eventually you will obtain magic items and spells that increase your INT and CHA. It should be noted that generally it is easier to get items that have more CHA than INT. Therefore, it is not a bad idea to spend a few more points on INT than CHA unless you choose a race that has high starting INT.

Secondary Stats
All other stats are pretty much secondary. Agility is useful for dodging and having a decent armor class, but as a pure-caster, you will rarely be able to go toe-to-toe with a monster, especially at high levels. Strength is useful to have when carrying loads of loot out of a dungeon, but this is only a benefit, not something you should spend valuable points on. Strength also gives a slight melee damage bonus as it increases your ATK rating. Stamina affects total hit points and stamina loss from combat, jumping and swimming. These are not activities you will be spending great amounts of time doing as an Enchanter. Wisdom is reputed to decrease the failure rate on jewelry making, but other than that, it is of little use to an Enchanter. Dexterity directly affects how often a weapon with a special effect or 'proc' will go off, pure-casters get very few weapons that have 'procs' and are generally ineffective in melee combat, so again this is an unimportant stat.

Religion
For a long, long time the benefits of being an agnostic Enchanter were praised and the Enchanters that did follow a deity expressed their woe. This was due largely to the fact that with their illusions, enchanters can enter any city, even a city that is hostile to the characters true race. Some NPC's in the game follow a particular God that dislikes other Gods and its followers, often to the point of killing them on-sight. Enchanters that followed unwelcome Gods could be 'sniffed out' by the guards, shopkeepers and other NPC's of the hostile city, and thus be killed.

Due to this fact, there was no reason to follow a God other than for role-playing reasons. Now the game designers have thrown in a twist, and that is Deity-specific items. It's largely unknown at this point in time what and how deep of an effect this will have, but it's a safe bet that there will be some items that you may or may not be able to use based on your religious choice. I would recommend you choose what feels right.

The Beginning
Once you have chosen a name for your new character you can select your starting location and begin playing. First things first, you should locate your guild and hand in your note to get your robe. You should have a dagger in your inventory, as well as two spells. Follow the instructions on your guild note and from there find a trainer who can teach you your first skills. There will also be a book that turns you into a +PvP player, I would not recommend you do this, as there are generally very few +PvP players around. Also, scribe the two spells into your spellbook.

You will only have 5 points to spend at first, so spend them wisely. I suggest placing 1 point in piercing, 1 in sense heading (it is a good idea to begin practicing this skill early), and the rest on your magic skills. Every time you gain a level, you receive 5 more points to train with at your guild. Once you reach a certain level in a skill, it will cost you money to continue to train that skill.

Alteration is among the most commonly used magic skill for Enchanters. It is related to all of our spells that increase or decrease abilities and the majority of our spells are Alteration based. Conjuration is important to Enchanters because it is what the Mesmerize series is based upon. Abjuration is related mainly to shielding type spells and Evocation is for all DD spells. Divination is related to all illusions, invisibility and sensory enhancement spells and the Color series. Spend one point in Channeling as well.

At level 4 you will get Meditate and you will need to put only one point in this skill. Do not put more than one point, as it will increase automatically when you meditate.

From there head out of your city and into the world to gain some experience. Basically, at this point in time all you need to do is cast your two spells and attack with your dagger. After you have collected enough loot from your slain foes, head to the nearest merchant and sell it all. Continue this until you have enough money to buy the rest of your spells. Also, make sure to use the points you gain from leveling-up each time. Eventually you will reach level 5, which means your 'newbie' area can no longer meet your experience needs, so you must head out into the larger areas. By then, you should have the general feel of playing a low-level enchanter. At these levels, you can choose to solo or group with other adventurers.

The Progression of Your Character
You have probably heard or read about all the amazing things enchanters can do. Well, the fact is at low levels you cannot cast those powerful spells for obvious reasons. You might be led to think it's a terrible, weak class. I would classify the lower levels as a very trying time in a young Enchanters career. You have few of the class-defining spells that make the enchanter so sought after later on. In fact, you might wonder what the purpose of an enchanter is at all in these stages. Don't fret though, if you were determined enough to make it this far, you can get through these times and it will be worth it. Trust me. After level 12 things get better because you'll have Charm, a very fun and useful spell. You will also have some new illusions to entertain you and your friends with as well as Bind Affinity, giving you the ability to bind yourself wherever you choose.

After level 16, things should really be starting to come together, you will have a more powerful mesmerize spell, a stronger DD spell and you'll be able to fight in a few new dungeons. In addition, the newly implemented Breeze spell, a lower level version of Clarity, should help you find groups more easily. At level 16 you will also be able to obtain the Mesmerisation spell, a powerful Area-Effect mesmerize. It will allow you to be able to Mesmerize multiple foes at once, stop trains and generally save lives. You must be careful when using this powerful magic, as you yourself can fall victim to the effects of it if you are too close to the target.

Level 20 on up as I remember it, I really began to love the class. At 20, you'll be able to turn into a troll or ogre, and you will be able to heal yourself in the form of the troll's natural regeneration ability. This perk is incredibly useful. You'll also get an upgrade to Tashan and your second Color series spell. Another great thing about level 20 is you get your first charisma buff; Sympathetic Aura.

The 'Magic' Level
Level 29. You've made it this far, and now you get your reward: Clarity. Possibly one of the most sought-after spells next to Spirit of Wolf and Teleportation, it causes your mana to regenerate at a much higher rate. By casting this on all the spellcasters in your adventuring party, you will have much less downtime recovering from fights and be able to gain more experience due to this fact. It's probably the most beneficial spell in your book, and just think, you never have to ask for it again. It's right there in front of you. Now you will understand why many level 29+ Enchanters go anonymous due to the large number of requests you will get for this spell.

You'll also have another fantastic spell, Augmentation. This gives a bonus to your agility, causes you to attack slightly faster and prevents stamina loss from attacking and swimming. It has a good duration and is cheap in terms of mana usage. It won’t hurt to cast this on the whole party as well, as the agility bonus can help everyone.

Level 29 And Above
From here on you should really have a good understanding of the Enchanter class. In your mid 30's you will spend a good deal of time in dungeons honing your Crowd Control skills and you will definitely notice that your melee skills are largely ineffective. At around level 30 or so, most Enchanters choose to undertake their quests from the Temple of Solusek Ro. There are some nice rewards you receive from these quests, most notably the Incandescent mask and Rod of Insidious Glamour. These items remain useful all the way to level 50.

Series Spells
Enchanters will get many spells that are part of a series that progress as you level. I'll now cover some of the most useful of these series spells.

The Tashan Series
This series of spells; Tashan, Tashani and Tashania, lowers the magical resistance of a target. The majority of Enchanter spells are based on magic, so it's a very good idea to cast this spell on the target you are fighting. Enchanter spells have a tendency to be resisted easily, so you can only benefit from using them. They are very low cost in terms of mana, even Tashania, a level 44 spell only takes 30 mana. If you are soloing, this is a good spell to grab the monsters attention. Also, be advised this spell can have a 'taunt' effect, especially at high levels. This means you should allow the fighters to attack the intended target for a few seconds before you cast, or else, the target might choose to attack you instead.

The Haste Series
These spells are highly valued by melee classes, as they speed the rate at which they deliver their attacks. It truly is amazing to see how much difference this type of enhancement can make on a party. Your first one will be Quickness, which you get at level 16. It provides a minor upgrade to a targets attack speed. By comparison, at level 49 you'll have Swift Like the Wind, the most powerful pre-expansion speed buff. Certainly the expansion will bring some amazing new spells, but as of now this spell can transform a high level fighter into an incredible killing machine.

The Rune Series
This is a set of spells that provide you or your target with an absorbing shield of 'freebie' damage. When cast you will see a text message stating that you are invulnerable when you are attacked physically by an enemy. This can be a major lifesaver. It does not make you immune to magic, however. It requires a semi-precious gem of some sort depending on which version of the spell you use. Currently there are four versions of this spell.

Mental Enhancement
Enchanters are unique in Norrath, as they are the only class that can increase your mental attributes. They can increase INT and WIS. As of now, we only have one INT and one WIS buff spell, which grant the target a +12 point bonus. Others are likely slated for the expansion, however. We can also increase our own charisma as well as others, an ability we share with Shamans. There are 3 spells in this series, Sympathetic Aura, Radiant Visage and Adorning Grace, with the latter providing a massive 40 points of CHA. There are likely to be further versions of the CHA buff spells in the expansion.

Shielding Series
All intelligence-based casters; Necromancers, Wizards, Magicians and Enchanters have a self-only armor class, hit point and magic resistance boosting series of spells. They are very useful and it is a good idea to keep one cast on you at all times. At higher levels, these spells provide a nice bonus to the caster. Enchanters also have a series of AC boosting spells, while they are not anywhere near the power of a Cleric AC buff, they are very useful. They can be cast on anyone, but certain classes have their own AC boosting spell as well, thus making ours incompatible with them. This series is best used on melee classes in the absence of a Cleric.

Magic Resistance Series
Enchanters also get several spells to increase a players magical resistance. They start out being minor, but at level 49 you will have a wonderful spell that in one cast gives a large bonus to everyone in your group at once.

The Root Series
Enchanters also get a series of spells commonly known as Root. What it does is causes a target to stand in place, but you are still able to attack it unlike Mesmerize. If you are in melee range, however, it may still attack you. This spell can be useful to prevent an enemy from escaping, and for classic 'root and nuke' tactics. Root is the basic spell at level 8, while at level 49 Paralyzing Earth is the highest form.

The Color Series
This is an extremely useful and potent set of spells. At first, its uses might not be readily apparent, but as you grow you will soon understand just how crippling it can be to a foe. What it does is blast strands of color out from you in all directions, stunning the opponents around you. It has a small area of effect, but anything in that area that does not resist the spell will be stunned. The applications of this series are great, it can stop monsters from casting spells, prevent them from escaping, save your fighters from taking damage and give you the extra few seconds you need to cast a Mesmerize spell, or escape if necessary. You will have 3 of these spells, with the first lasting 1-3 seconds at level 4, and the final version at level 44 lasting around 12 seconds.

The Debuff Group
Enchanters get a set of their own spells as well as sharing some Shaman spells that have but one purpose: to lower the physical abilities of a target. At first, they start out weak, but as you progress, they become incredibly powerful. These spells can remove over 100 points from a targets stats, namely STR and AGI. Many have found that against computer-controlled enemies they have little to no effect, but against other players, there can be no denying the devastating power of these spells. Imagine a warrior wearing heavy platemail armor, carrying a large sword. He's strong and if he got close enough he could really hurt you. Now imagine that same warrior, but with severely crippled strength and agility. With all that armor and gear, he can no longer move due to its weight. You can now destroy that opponent at your leisure from a safe distance.

We also have a set of spells that will slow the speed at which an enemy attacks. This can be very useful when fighting tough opponents with many hit points. When used in tandem with the Haste series, the results can be very devastating.

The Counter-Magic Series
Most spellcasting professions have at least one spell to cancel the effects of magic. This can be done on party-members or on themselves. Enchanters are specialists in this field. We get a series of spells that at high levels, can remove all buffs, debuffs, and sometimes Damage Over Time spells that could kill you or someone else. You can also take away any buffs an enemy might have, which can be helpful at higher levels when enemies have some powerful buffing spells of their own. You may not cast these spells on players that are not in your group.

Direct Damage and Damage Over Time Series
Enchanters are by no means masters of causing damage, at least not directly. However, we do have some respectable DD spells, and our DOT spells are decent. Chaotic Feedback, the first DD you get, causes about 42 points of damage at level 8. Discordant mind, the level 44 DD causes 520+ points of damage. All Enchanter DD spells are magic based, and 'all or nothing' spells, meaning that if they cause damage it will be full and any resists will cause no damage at all. They also have the secondary effect of interrupting the casting of any target that is hit at just the right time. This is a useful benefit when you get the timing right. These spells are highly resistible, and have the unfortunate side effect of being a 'mega-taunt', meaning it really ticks the bad guys off! Use them wisely; never cast them at the start of a fight, rather save them as a finisher to quickly kill your enemy when it is low on life. Enchanter DD spells also have a Recast Time, which gets increasingly longer as you get higher level DD's. This means that you must wait for a few moments before you can recast the spell.

Our Damage Over Time spells, or DOT's, cause minimal damage when compared to other classes DOT spells, but ours also have the secondary effect of lowering the strength and agility of the target. This effect stacks with our debuff series of spells. Some find the DOT's to be very handy, as they take only a small amount of mana to cost, some prefer not to use them. While they don't cause heavy damage, they do have the benefit of being largely non-taunting, in other words, you can usually cast them at the start of a fight without fear of retaliation from your foe. With a recent patch, you will now get a message in yellow text that informs you when your DOT has worn off.

The Enchant Metal Series
This set of spells is another perk that makes the Enchanter class famous. With them, we can enchant precious metals to make magical jewelry to sell or trade to other players. Many wise and skillful Enchanters have made their fortunes from this practice. At higher levels, some very powerful and expensive pieces of jewelry can be made. Jewel Crafting is a very costly and time-consuming skill to practice, so it's best to start it later on in your adventures. Over the course of your travels, you will likely get many requests for the enchantment of metals and the crafting of jewelry.

The Illusions
These are what make the Enchanter class so fun in my opinion. We have the ability to transform into any race of Norrath, including several magical creatures. Often these illusions grant you a unique ability, for instance Illusion: Dark Elf will grant the caster Ultravision. Illusion: Barbarian gives you the racial ability to Slam. At high levels, you will be able to transform into elementals, wolves, and even a werewolf. In some instances, you will be able to enter a dungeon unharmed under certain illusions.

The Animations
This is nothing more than a magically animated weapon and shield, summoned to serve you. It cannot heed your commands and will only assist you if you are attacked. It is has low HP, and often can be more of a liability than a benefit. It should not be used in dungeons unless you are very high level or very confident of your skills. The reason being is that it will attack an enemy until it is slain and you cannot tell it to stop. This could mean doom for you and those around you if it chases a fleeing creature into the depths of a dungeon, as any monsters it comes near will attack your pet and kill it and then come after you.

Why do we have a pet when we can charm virtually any creature in the game? It helps tremendously at lower levels. You should be able to solo fairly well with the right buffs on your pet. In mid-levels, from about 29-39, its not very useful compared to the beasts you will be warring against. However, at very high levels, 44-49, the pet again becomes extremely powerful and useful. With the proper buffs applied, this pet can do some amazing damage.

To summon one, you will need to buy a number of Tiny Daggers. These can be found in various locations around the world and are inexpensive. It's a good idea to stock up on lots of these if you can afford it, as traveling to the merchant that sells them becomes inconvenient when you begin adventuring to far-off lands.

Animations can dual-wield at level 29, bash and kick and double attack. They can also hit magical creatures. To get rid of your Animation, you will have to zone, cast invisibility on yourself or use the spell Reclaim Energy. With a recent patch, you will now get a message in yellow text informing you when your pet’s buffs have worn off.

The only way to get an animation to engage an enemy is for you to be attacked, or it to be attacked first.

The Mesmerize Series
This is basically the class-defining spell, the power that makes the Enchanter so sought after. There are many spells in this series, and they will be able to hold a target for a longer duration as you advance in levels. Mesmerize is a level 4 spell, and only holds a creature for a few seconds, while at level 49, the potent Dazzle will hold a target for up to 120 seconds. The area-effect version of this spell, Mesmerisation, is an extremely useful spell. It should be noted that the single target Mesmerize spells generally are 'mega-taunt', and the area effect Mesmerize spell does not seem to taunt foes as badly.

Tactics and Skills

Crowd Control
Let's begin by examining the primary purpose of the enchanter class. This main purpose is known as Crowd Control. Enchanters can do many things, but this is where we shine. What it entails is reducing the number of enemies you are fighting at one time. How it is done is through a series of spells known as the Mesmerize line.

These powerful spells allow you to 'pause' an enemy, causing them to stand in place and not attack for the duration of the spell. It is a crippling attack to an enemy, allowing an enchanter to turn impossible odds into a fight that can be dealt with by your companions. A monster that is Mesmerized or 'mezzed' will be frozen in place until the spell wears out or it is attacked. Casting any type of spell that causes damage will also wake up an enemy that has been Mesmerized. A target that has had a DOT spell cast upon it will not be able to be Mesmerized for more than a few seconds.

Debuffing
One thing you can do while a monster has been Mesmerized, is debuff them. This is a wide ranging term, but basically what it means is to reduce the fighting ability of the target monster, whether that be its strength, agility, intelligence or otherwise. Many classes have the ability to debuff, and it's a common practice to do so while an enemy is Mesmerized.

Another tactic you can employ with Mesmerize is Memory Blur. This is mainly used when you need to escape but don't want to zone, or when you are pulling but only wish to pull a few creatures and not have to deal with the one that was Mesmerized later on. What it does is 'wipes' the 'hate list' that the monster has. Since you cast a Mesmerize spell on it, it would normally come straight to you as soon as the spell wore off. If Memory Blur is employed, that monster will forget you. There is an area effect version of this spell, Mind Wipe, which can be used in conjunction with the Mesmerisation spell.

Targeting
Targeting your foes can sometimes be difficult, especially when they 'stack' together or bunch up. For singles, the quickest way to select a target is to face it and press F8. When facing multiple enemies that have bunched up there are several ways to target them. One popular method is to press F9 and switch from a first-person point of view, to an overhead point of view. It will often make it much easier to select the mob you wish to Mesmerize. Some people prefer to wait until the fighters of the group have caused damage to one, and then begin to Mesmerize the others.

Group Cooperation
This is paramount to successful Crowd Control. If the group does not work together then you will have a very hard time and run great risk of being slain. First, the group must acknowledge one person as the puller and assist target. A puller is the person, usually a melee class, who will bring the enemies to the group. He or she will usually select one and begin to attack it. Now if this person brings one monster, it's easy for everyone to target that creature and kill it.

Unfortunately, in the great world of Norrath things do not always work as planned. Often the team will have to contend with 3 or more creatures, and as the Enchanter, it is your role to lock them down with your Mesmerize spells to prevent the group from taking massive damage or being killed. Now, when 3+ mobs are all attacking at the same time, how will the group know which ones to attack and which ones to let you Mesmerize? Simple, this is what the /assist command is for. The puller should already be attacking one creature, and the other fighters in the group now target him or her with the appropriate F key and type: /assist [enter]. This will automatically set them to attacking the target of the puller. Alternatively, you can create a special hotkey to perform the assist function. This is essential and once you get into the higher levels, it will become second nature for you and your companions.

Meditation And Timing
Eventually you will get into some pretty tough and heart pounding fights that will have everyone on the edge of their seat. While very exciting, you will be in great peril until you have won. Under normal conditions an Enchanter can usually manage to Mesmerize the extras, debuff and maybe use a DOT or DD spell. Sometimes, you just can't afford the mana.

In these situations you need to know exactly how long your Mesmerize spells will last, and you will need to sit down and meditate in the middle of a fight to ensure you have enough mana to continually keep a target Mesmerized without it wearing off. It takes practice to do this, but it is an essential tactic. Clarity will usually ensure you have sufficient mana but even with it, an Enchanter could quickly become exhausted in the tougher battles you will face.

If you are facing an opponent that seems to be resisting your attempts to Mesmerize, it's a good habit to re-apply the mez a bit earlier to compensate for any resists. Always cast Tashan when you are controlling a large number of enemies.

Taunting
This is something that is often overlooked, but things will go much smoother if the melee classes that possess this skill use it. When a target is Mesmerized, it will wake up and generally be very angry with the Enchanter for doing this. Sometimes you will take quite a beating from monsters when they break mez. One way to counter or at least reduce this is to have the fighters Taunt the creature before it wakes up. This way the Enchanter can usually sit down and rest for a moment while the fighters do their work. It will take a bit of time to get used to it, but people will eventually realize its importance.

Buffing
You might think it strange to cover this as a tactic or skill, but I've seen many players that do not buff as effectively as they could. As an Enchanter you will have several buffs your companions will desire at all times, if not more. A good way to apply them is to first cast them on yourself and then go down the line using the Function keys, i.e., F1(you), F2, F3, etc. This way you will know when everyone’s buffs are wearing out since you were the first to receive the buff. When using a pet, it's often hard to target it to for buffs, a quick and easy way to target it is to press F1 twice. Setting this rotation is a good way to get a smoothly running group.

Pet Usage
Sometimes you will not be able to group for various reasons. There are ways you can use a pet effectively. Our pets have very low HP when compared to a Necromancer or Magician pet. They will never be able to 'tank' for us as those classes pets can. Even at low levels, you will probably have to take a few hits or your pet will be destroyed.

There are several ways to counter this and use the pet effectively. One is to chain-cast Rune. It would be expensive to do so, but it’s a sure-fire way to keep your pet alive. Another tactic is what's known as Fear Kiting. What you do is engage a monster and let it hit you so your pet attacks it. Then you stun it with one of your Color Series spells. After this you back off as quickly as possible and cast your highest-level fear spell.

In theory, the target will run away while your pet hacks it from behind. At lower levels you could run behind and attack the creature too, but you will not do significant damage. When the fear spell expires you simply stun it and fear it again. It takes a while to perfect this strategy but it does work and you can solo in your high 20's and low 30's in this fashion. It works best in large, open areas. In addition, your pet should be buffed as much as possible.

While doing this you can cast your DOT spell or a DD if you have the time. Another strategy is to team up with another caster and play 'ping pong', essentially taking turns nuking and confusing the monster, all the while the pet is attacking it. Druids and Necromancers complement the Enchanter class very well, and working in tandem can create a formidable force.

Charm
This is a very difficult to master tactic, but with luck and experience, you can do some incredible feats. When charming, it's best to get as much charisma as possible to ensure you are not resisted as much and get good duration’s. Charm is a very random spell, sometimes the charm lasts 5 seconds and sometimes it lasts more than 5 minutes.

Some Enchanters have been able to solo the fearsome Spectre island in Oasis. It can be used as a way to augment you normal Crowd Control routine. Example: you face a large camp of monsters and instead of trying to mesmerize all of them, you charm one and send it against the target. You've not only managed to reduce the number of enemies you are fighting, but have also increased the strength of your group’s offense.

As stated before, this is a very random spell, and it's definitely not as reliable as mesmerize. Also when a monster breaks free of your charm, it will be very unhappy with you and try to slay you. If this happens you can either run, or stun it and charm again, or just mesmerize it. This is a spell you should play around with and practice, as it's very useful and powerful, but also very dangerous.

Spell Research
At level 16, you must begin to research your own spells. To do this you will first have to train at least one point in the skill, but I would recommend you place all available points into the skill. Next, you will need to obtain a copy of the Tome of Endless Enchantments. Once you have done those two things, you now only need to venture forth into the world and seek research material.

At around level 16 or so, you will likely notice that creatures you kill tend to have strangely named pages, runes and small pages that are torn in half. These are the aforementioned research materials. Enchanters will require the torn in half pages, a left side and a right side. Once you have a pair who's numbers match, you place them in the Tome of Endless Enchantments and click the 'combine' button.

If your skill is high enough you will succeed and have a new spell to scribe into your book. If you fail the materials will be destroyed and you must seek them again. Fortunately, they are common and you can often find them for sale on various merchants, or get them from other players. You should keep the Tome on you at all time, in case you find the materials you need while on an adventure.

Even once you have the spells you need for your level, you should continue to collect them, your skill in research will increase. Once you reach a skill level where it says 'This item is trivial for someone of your skill.' you then no longer need to collect those pages, but rather move on to the next set.

Specialization
All magic users have the opportunity to specialize in one magic skill. The effects of specialization are not completely understood, but it is generally assumed you will cast spells for less mana if they are based on the skill you specialized in. You will get this option at level 16.

To select a skill to specialize in, you need to venture to your guild and train points in it, you should train at least one point in all of the fields. However, to lock the specialization in a field you must do one of two things; repeatedly cast a spell that is based on the skill you wish to specialize in until the specialization skill is above 50, or train the skill above 50.

After that, the other skills will all cap out at 50 eventually, and the one you chose to specialize in will continue to increase. Most Enchanters choose to specialize in Alteration, as the majority of our spells fall under this skill. Conjuration is another option if you'd like to be a Mesmerize specialist. Divination could lead you to become a stun specialist using the Color series. It's not recommended that you specialize in Evocation or Abjuration, as we have few spells that fall under this skill.

Jewelry Crafting
Although anyone can make jewelry, only Enchanters can make magical jewelry at their leisure. Anyone else would have to request us to Enchant their metals. This being so, we pretty much have the monopoly on the jewelry market. It is possible to become a master jewelry crafter at level 1, but you would not be able to make any magical jewelry until you got your first Enchant metal spell.

I'll only give a rough outline on how to begin this skill, as there are resources that are much more detailed available should you be seeking knowledge of this skill. To start, you should train at least 1-10 points in the skill. Then you will need to buy a jewelry kit. Purchase of the book, The Legacy of Jewelcraft, is optional. After you have bought the kit, you need to buy precious metals and gems.

These can be found in most large cities, though some have a more extensive selection than others do. You will have to start out first with silver, as it is the easiest, and work your way through the metals. As a rule of thumb, the NPC merchants sell the metals and jewels in order of price, lowest to highest, from left to right like a book. This order also relates to the difficulty level of the materials. Therefore, you should start with the least expensive metal and jewel, and make them until they become trivial.

You can opt to Enchant the metals at this point, but you will advance your skill slower. Most people usually sell their first attempts back to the merchant and recover some of their expenses.

It's a fairly easy and fun skill to get the hang of. When working with large stacks of materials, you can hold down Ctrl and left click to select only 1 out of the stack, rather than using the scroll bars. This will save you a lot of time.

Conclusion
While this is by no means the ultimate guide to playing your character, I hope you have gained some insight into the class and how it works. Just remember it's impossible to master everything in one day, but you should now have a few advantages at the start of your journey. If you get frustrated take a break and think things through, and the solution will come to mind. With a bit of luck and a keen mind, you will do quite well in your career.

-From the Scrolls of Vendan Fivemiles, Human Enchanter of the Test Server
