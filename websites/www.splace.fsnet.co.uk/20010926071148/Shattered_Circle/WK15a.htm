Dungeon Master:

As Karg, Aeriel and Varistan leave the room; Rupert sits down on one of the bunks, and after much searching, and a frown towards his sister, buries his nose into a book. The rest of you gather round the table …

Lindornea'en:

Lin, in trying to catch up, guzzles down a mug... the Elf makes a nasty looking face afterwards. "I knew I'd pay for it..." He looks ripe to just spit the strong 'elixir' right back out. After finishing it off with a gulp, he shakes his head vigorously, removing the stars from his vision... After a few more blinks, he looks ready to attempt the penny throw.

 

 His toss looks better aimed at Mickey than the cup.

Mickey Donatelli:

Mickey smiles, remembering to swig down half a flagon since Dolly just made us all drink. He smacks his lips, enjoying the taste a bit more now that he has a couple in him. He has lost track of the turns, but sees the penny lying by the cup and quickly snatches it up.

 

He bounces it from a foot further back this time and... still makes the mark. He sips from his flagon and grins as he licks away a tuft of foam at the corner of his mouth, "You're drink Dolly...hoosh' turn issit'?"

Dolly:

Taking a drink as Mickey gets the coin into the pot, Dolly holds out her hand for it.

 

Placing the pot on the table, with a smug grin, she bounces the coin; it hits the side of the pot, and lands on the table. Shrugging her shoulders, she takes another long drink of ale.

Yanish:

Yanish stands on a chair, so as too retrieve the coin. Once done she returns to her place and flips the coin into the bucket. With a giggle she says to Mickey this could be a long night for you. Taking a small sip of her own mead, she produces the small skull from her pocket rolling it around on her palm she places it under one of the cups on the table retrieving two more similar ones. She shuffles them about and says, "Since you’ve been so lucky thus far tonight possibly a 1 gold piece wager on finding the skull would be something you would like to try."

Mickey Donatelli:

Mickey slugs back another half flagon as per the rules and the skill of Yanish. He picks up the penny, still unsure of whose turn it really is, and easily plinks it into the tiny bucket. His lopsided grin never wavers as he watches Yanish pull out her own 'game'...

 

"Dolly love, ish' your drink again..."

 

He watches all three (six?) cups shuffle beneath the skilled hands of the halfling, then holds up his one gold coin from earlier. He places it on the table next to the cups and says,

 

"What’s one gold coin? Sure, I'll play...umm...it's under number two..."

Aeriel:

The winged elf steps into the dormitory room, the smell of ale striking her tender nose. She blinks, looking at the game being played and the demeanour of some of the players (which seems to have changed in the short time she was gone).

 

"Oh!" Aeriel exclaims. "Hast thou begun a new game?" She looks at the table curiously, stepping closer. "Did someone win the last?"

Yanish:

Yanish flips over the cup Mickey pointed to and displays the skull. "Well, well look at that, I always thought you were lucky!" Fishing into her newly acquired funds, Yanish pulls out a gold coin and flips it into the bucket. "Look at that, a coin of a gold and a drink are yours this round." When the winged elf re-enters the room, Yanish makes a better attempt to hide her fascination than last time. To the group she says, "Mickey has already proved how easy this game is who else wants to give it a try? Perhaps Mickey, you would like to try it again? It’s a good thing I just got paid, I don’t know if I could afford playing against you if I hadn't. However be that as it may, what say we raise the prize to 4 gold pieces this time. For the night grows late and we might be on our way at first light. So who wishes to try for some easy money?" Taking another sip of her mead she looks from one face to the next with large innocent brown eyes.

Mickey Donatelli:

Mickey laughs as he collects his one gold coin from the cup. He quickly drinks the remainder of his flagon and then releases a very audible -BBUUUUUUUURRRRPPPPP- as he tries to cover his mouth with one uncooperative hand...

 

"I'm tha' winner. Winnin' this - winnin' that - say, do Angels play gamesh? Betcha I could win those too, gosh knows I'm flyin' already..."

 

Smiling through heavy lidded eyes, he tosses the coin to the tabletop and misses the table completely. He laughs even harder, rolling off the chair to collect his little gold coin. Crawling back to the table, he places it and the first one on top and giggles, "There ye' go - four coins...anna I pick nummer' three..."

 

It's obvious that Mickey won't last another round...

Aeriel:

Aeriel covers her mouth, her eyes going wide as she sees Mickey's antics. Her wings instinctively fold tighter to her body as she looks at the drunken man in horror and disgust, her face becoming pale, almost as if she is about to become ill.

 

"Thou... thou'rt drunk!" she exclaims, stating the obvious. "Drunk most foul!"

Lindornea'en:

Lin sits, listening to his inner feelings, trying to feel the taste of the 'Drink' he's imbibed... Without further comment, he drops the mug to the table, "I'm out... I can't understand how humans stomach this stuff... If y'change to wine, tell me." He considers Yanish's game, and sighs. Not clouded in the least, he sees the confidence game for what it is. The elf offers the Halfling a grin and amused shake of his gold-haired head.

 

The return of Aeriel breaks the monotony of seeing Mickey only further make a fool of himself, and gives him something interesting to place his blue eyes upon. Behind a grin, he states, "Oh, he's probably more than drunk. Especially when he's playing games for money with a Halfling..."

Varistan:

A few minutes after Aeriel enters the dormitory, Varistan squeezes between Karg and the doorframe holding a large flagon of ale.

 

Ignoring the antics of the others, he deposits all his gear at the foot of one of the bunks and sits heavily on the edge of the bed. He watches the others impassively, thoughts of the past and of the current situation occupying most of his attention. He takes long, deep draughts from the flagon in a steady rhythm.

Mickey Donatelli:

Mickey watches the little halfling carefully.

 

He's having a great time, and only one of his Gold coins is free of the heavily knotted pouch at his side - but Father had warned him of the little tricksters even before he knew the Familia of the Guild...he's ready for anything...

 

"Nummer' THREE! Well...do I win again?"

 

He looks back at the elf-angel with a broad sloppy smile. He may be drunk 'most foul' but the half-elf feels like he's in heaven.

 

"Are there a lot of angels in your Heaven Aeriel?"

Aeriel:

Aeriel crosses her arms over her small breasts, her gaze narrowing in disgust. "I knowest not what thou speaketh of," she says to Mickey in a cold voice, "but I am not of the other realm and knowest not these 'angels' thou dost speak of!"

 

She looks at the others, then back at Lin. "Are these the sort of disgusting ground-dwellers," she makes the term an insult, "that thou dost associate with? Art thou not ashamed?"

Mickey Donatelli:

Mickey’s smile drops from his face as he sees that Aeriel holds him only in contempt. He hiccups once, then slumps quietly into a nearby chair. The young mans brow furrows as he ponders her harsh words...

 

* She sounds so much like my Mother! *

 

His humanity has taken the best of him yet again....Unfortunately, he is too drunk to fully comprehend the reason for his shame - but a part of him feels it none the less...

 

He turns over his empty flagon with a quick glance to the angel. Looking back to the games, he says thickly,

 

"I dunna' want to play no more... some think me a fool for even playing at all. This ain’t no fun no more...did I lose? ...musta lost..."

 

He leaves the two gold coins on the table and wanders as far from the group as he can before collapsing on the lower bunk. He manages to struggle off one of his boots, but gives up on the other before falling backwards into a spinning room of comatose darkness..."...I'm sorry Mama... zzzzzzz..."

Lindornea'en:

Lin holds up his hands, as if to say he's just as surprised by this as Aeriel. A slight smile still remains upon his features, however, as he states, "Hey, I've only known them for ..." He ticks off something on his hand, "Thirty-Six hours... and even that is being Generous. I'm personally amazed that he could suck down Four Flagons of that stuff... Damned if he'll remember it in the morning, either."

 

He smiles helplessly, and finishes off with, "Besides... I wasn't particularly here for them, anyways."

Aeriel:

The woman puts her hand over her mouth as she realizes what she just did. She didn't hear the words he whispered but knows that she just hurt Mickey deeply, though she's not sure why. And how did she just injure the others? She looks at Lin, hearing his words, but her eyes wide with her guilt.

 

"Oh by the Goddess, what have I done?" She whispers, tears starting in her eyes. "I apologize... I knowest not thy ways..." She looks at the rest of the group. "I should judge not thy ways... I am so sorry..."

 

Her voice fails as she turns and puts her face to the wall, her wings sagging as she cries.

Yanish:

Yanish flips the third cup over the skull isn't under it. Her smile disappears however as Micky looses both interest in the game and consciousness. Scooping up the coins she grumbles something about "bloody drunks, the bet was 4 gold!" She considers waking the oaf and demanding the rest of her pay, then the winged elf begins too talk as if she been the one drinking. Yanish forget the other two coins for the moment anyway, and walks over strange elf. "The creatures wings do seem like that of an angle" Yanish thinks as she approaches. She reaches out a timid hand to touch one of the vivid white wings only to pull her hand quickly back before it made contact. Instead she says "Do not cry Aeriel, the fault was not your own. Mickey was not himself. His state might not have been completely his own doing either. However be that as it may, you have done no wrong. The night does grow late, and I too am off to bed, perhaps on the marrow you could tell me a little of where you come from." With a flushed face she turns and stalks to her bed muttering about what a fool she must appear.

Lindornea'en:

Lin's smile disappears with the opening of floodgates across the room. He seems lightly confused at this outburst... And just like Yanish, has to put in his own words. Unlike the halfling, his touch alights on her chin, lightly turning her face to gaze at his questioning look. The elf says, "No-one can avoid judgement. Lots of 'Human' ideals are silly... same with Elves... and so on. That cannot be turned aside. I can tell you this much... You really didn't hurt Mickey, just got him to see his current situation in a different light. There is nothing wrong with that. Even *IF* he remembers it, he'll realise just how much of a fool he was being... and that has nothing to do with if you had or had not just walked into the room." A grin comes to his face, "So cheer up, kid. We 'Ground-Dwellers' are a reasonable lot, once you get past the initial shock... Just remember, not everyone is a monk..." He snickers at the whole thought of that...

Varistan:

The beginnings of a grin start to curl Varistan's mouth as he listens to Lin, and with the effects of the alcohol having set in, it never even crosses his mind that maybe he should keep his mouth shut.

 

"Don't let Lin fool you, sweetling. We're a reg'lar band o' saints compared to other groundlings you could meet. Reasonable's not what most reasonable people would choose to call us ground dwellers."

 

With a snort of amusement, he finishes off his flagon with a final gulp, dropping it clumsily on the floor where it tips and falls, rolling loudly across the room until it clinks against one of the walls.

 

He sighs, all hints of amusement once again absent from his face. "Yup, jus' a bunch o' saintly Paladins, we are." He flops down onto his back, hands behind his head, staring up at nothing.

Rupert:

On his bunk despite the commotion, Rupert falls asleep, his book in his lap, his spectacles still on his face.

Karg:

Seeing the others drifting off one by one, Karg moves to face the troubled elf. He puts one hand on her shoulder and the other gently lifts her chin so that she is looking at him. The warm smile on his face seems quite out of place. "Aeriel do no wrong, no need mourn." He does what he can do to comfort her, finally asking, "Aeriel tired? Karg take Aeriel to room?"

Aeriel:

Aeriel looks at Karg, wiping the tears from her face. "In sooth, I am most tired. Mayhap that be best."

 

She looks at Lin. "Thy words are most wise, my elder. I've not been among thy kind much, but I shall learn."

 

She looks to the retiring Yanish. "And thank thee, little one. I shall speak with thee anon as I am able. Like my friend, thy words ring true, and I thank thee."

 

She sighs, her wings drooping. "I must away to my own bed. I bid thee all goodnight and hope to see thee on the morrow." With that, the winged elf departs for her own quarters.

Karg:

Karg escorts Aeriel out of the room and down the corridors to the room set aside for her use. When they reach it, Karg says, "Aeriel seek Karg if need rises." As soon as the girl is safely in her room, Karg makes his way to his own quarters. Entering, he nods to the brothers already there as he doffs the heavy bears hide armour. Seeking his own bed, he prays to Shyeth for strength and success in the aid of the kin of spider. Then, knowing that he would need all the rest he could get, he gives himself over to the embrace of sleep.

Dolly:

Dolly watches the antics of the others from sitting on her bunk. It is clear that there is no need for yet another attempt to placate Aeriel so she just frowns at Rupert's sleeping form, and calls "Good Night" a s Karg and the winged one leaves.

 

Taking another swig from her flagon she sets it down and says, quite softly “Well that's screwed that for an evening", before retiring.
