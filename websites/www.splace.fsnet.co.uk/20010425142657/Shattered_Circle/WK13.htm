Dungeon Master:

It takes you the rest of the morning and most of the afternoon to get back to the monastery. You make several stops along the way, as the terrain isn’t the best to be carrying a stretcher. Jarrod wakes a couple of times, but is still to weak to talk much to you, and all you manage to get out of him is that he was attacked by some hideous creature, before he drifts back into sleep. At one point you feel that you are being followed, but you find nothing to back up this feeling.

 

As you come down the hill and approach the monastery, Abbot Jengor runs out to meet you. “You found him!” he comments excitedly, “I knew you would”. The smile falls from his face as he sees the extent of Jarrod’s injuries, “What happened to him”, he asks worriedly, “no, no wait, let us get him inside, then we will sit and eat and you can tell me”. With this he bustles you into the door of the monastery.

 

As you enter, there is a smell of fresh bread in the air that plays at your hunger glands, you see that the place is furnished minimally; under Abbott Jengor’s instruction two brothers take the stretcher off you, and disappear off down the hallway. Abbott Jengor leads you into a mess room, with long tables running the length of the room; he beckons for you to sit down, whilst sending one of the brothers off for food and drink.

 

Looking around you see that the other occupants of the monastery are dressed in a similar fashion to Karg, although the hide varies from person to person, with wolves, deer and mountain lions all playing a part in it somewhere.

 

Once he has seen you all sat down, Abbott Jengor sits too. “So tell me”, he says, “what happened? Where did you find him?” Just as he finishes his questioning, a large pot of stew, fresh bread and cheese are brought out of what you imagine is the kitchen at the far end of the room.

Dolly:

Dolly makes sure she gets a seat next to Mickey ..... and allows her hand to rest casually on his knee.

Mickey:

Mickey is one of the first seated at the long table, and shows no shame for the greed as he grabs large portions of cheese and bread. He sniffs at the stew, then realizing that this IS a monastery, he trusts it enough to begin shovelling quickly with the simple wooden spoon provided by the monks...

 

After a few minutes of focused eating, he slows a bit, then addresses the host of this dinner party - Abbot Jengor,

 

"He fell in a hole, was jumped by a monster..."

 

Before he can finish, he yelps, and then bounces up and down in his seat just as Dolly sits next to him at the table. He sputters, he blushes, he looks over at Dolly in surprise...then smiles. He takes another large bite of bread and says,

 

"Maybe you should tell him..."

Dolly:

No, Lets let Karg tell him. After all he acts for the abbot." and she gives his knee a light squeeze.

Lindornea'en:

In his typically disengaging way, Lin lays down upon a bench... Taking up whatever space he feels like. Being as crass as he seems to be, the Elf is slightly less so this time, as he spaces himself off from the main cluster... Just close enough to keep him within the conversation. In a rather mercenary tone, Lin comments, "You weren't paying us to tell you how we got him back, just to get him back..." There are the edgings of a smile upon his fair face...

Rupert:

Rupert watches his sister in growing dismay. Shaking his head, he reaches into his coat and removes... an apple. Then a broken quill. A scrap of paper. Finally, he draws out his book. He tears a piece of paper out and hands it over.

 

"I drew a picture of the areas we saw, the webs and the creatures. I do hope you can read my handwriting, but it should be a good narrative."

 

He pushes his glasses onto his nose and smiles.

Yanish:

Yanish sits up straight on the bench, in an attempt to make herself seem taller. Pride shines in her light brown eyes. With a casual glance at Abbot Jengor she thinks, "Thought I was going to steal your money or end up dead did he! Well now what do you have to say?!" She sticks her tongue out at him; quickly catching her self she hides the gesture by raising her goblet of wine. Having more interest in the rest of her money than telling tales as a bard might, Yanish let the rest of the group spin the tales of their adventure. Agreeing with the elf's statement and not his posture she chimes in "We graciously thank you for the meal and drink, but might I enquire about the remainder of our payment."

Karg:

A weary Karg trudges on to the monastery. He dials up a smile for Jengor as the man runs to greet them. "Karg glad see home again." he says in return. Karg gladly passes the burden off to the two brothers and spends some time working his shoulders, his efforts rewarded by the sound of joints cracking.

 

Karg takes his seat at the table and considers Jengor's question. "We find tunnel in circle of broken stone. We go down, find spider people. Spider people tend Jarrod. Spider people talk of great evil, want us go back, help fight great evil. Karg give oath to return, repay care of Jarrod. Karg no know what happened Jarrod. Spider people no say. Jarrod no say."

 

Karg finds his attentions diverted by the smell of the stew. He helps himself to a hardy serving by dipping his bowl into the pot. He also takes large chunks of bread and cheese as well. Eating noisily and with little concern for manners, his spoon left untouched, Karg repeats the performance. When the second helping is done, a third follows soon after.

Varistan:

Varistan sits with the others, keeping his opinions to himself while he eats, occasionally wiping his mouth with the back of his hand.

 

He perks up a bit with interest when Lin and Yanish mention payment, but still says nothing. He's not too worried about receiving his share. These monks seem honest enough.

 

When Karg finishes his report, Varistan finally breaks his silence, starting off with a loud, contented belch. "Yeah, that just about sums it all up. Only we have no idea why your Jarrod was there in the first place, or what exactly happened to him. Those bugs who had 'im weren't too generous with information."

Rupert:

Rupert looks up from where is yet again sketching something in his book. "They volunteered information but then again, neither did we, as I recall," he states. "And I'm not sure that going back into that place is something I want to do, personally."

Dolly:

"We didn't actually ask that many questions either. We got Jarrod and got him back here as quick as we could. So I guess there are still a lot of unanswered questions."

 

Dolly helps herself to stew and bread, "You want me to reach a bowl for you Mickey?" she asks.

Varistan:

Varistan bristles at Dolly's words.

 

"It's not like I didn't try to get some information from them bugs. And it didn't help that none of the rest of you seemed to give a damn about findin' out what was really goin' on."

 

He falls into a sullen silence, debating with himself whether or not it would be worth the risk to return to the Circle. For reasons he doesn't even understand, he feels more angry at himself than anyone else.

Aeriel:

"Oh! Am I disturbing thee, Abbott?" says a hesitant voice. In the doorway to the room stands a frail elf with long braided white hair and large blue eyes. A light blue toga, somewhat ragged and tied over one shoulder, is her garment. Most noticeable about her are her wings- beautiful and white, the right one bandaged. She looks at the gathered strangers with trepidation.

 

"I knew not that thou had guests, Abbott Jengor," she continues quickly. "But I could help not noticing the food and thought, thought to mayhap break my fast. But I would not disturb thee and thy guests."

Karg:

As the frail looking elf enters, a smile graces Karg’s rough features. He waves her over, "Karg glad see Aeriel again. Aeriel come, eat." Karg looks at the others. Addressing them as a group, he makes the introductions, "We welcome Aeriel, Aeriel foundling of monastery." Then he returns his attention to the winged elf, "Aeriel, Karg travel with these, find Jarrod." With wonderful economy, Karg points at each of his companions and saying their names without further embellishment. Sliding over slightly to give her some room, Karg returns his attention back to the meal.

Aeriel:

The winged Elf's face lights up as she sees Karg, her left unbound wing flexing slight as she recognizes him. She tosses her head, the long braid falling between the wings and moves forward, looking at the group. She sees humans, part humans, part part humans, the little one and an Elf. She smiles hesitantly at him.

 

Folding her wings behind her back, she hesitates before sitting. "It is well to see thee, Karg," she replies in her softly accented voice. "Thy mission to save Jarrod was most talked of here." She looks shyly at the others. "In sooth, foundling may be most well in describing mine own situation, I do fear. I want not to disturb thy meal or thy conversation."

 

She still does not sit, looking about at this room of strangers.

Yanish:

As the winged elf walks into the room, Yanish's spoon drops into her now forgotten stew. With mouth agape she stares at the creature with amazement. Stuck up elves like Lin, she had seen from time to time in her village. But the winged elf is something new. Stammering a bit and talking mostly to herself she says, "I thought they were story creatures created by drunken bards." When Karg mentions her name, Yanish's awe breaks and her mouth snaps shut. Amazement still shines in her eyes, but she says to Aeriel, "Aeriel, it is a pleasure to meet you. Perhaps latter if it please you we might talk." She falls silent as Karg moves on with his introductions.

Dungeon Master:

Abbott Jengor takes the piece of paper off Rupert, nodding his head in thanks. He studies it closely for a moment, before placing it on the table in front of him. He listens to what Karg tells him of the circle of broken stone and the spider people. Abbott Jengor smiles at Karg when he mentions the oath, “you’re lessons here have taught you well my son, and yes, yes you must go back and help these people”.

 

He looks around the table at the strange collection of people before him and then continues, “You are good people, and yes I have the payment that I promised to you, did you want it now?” he asks questioningly. Before anyone can answer he beckons to one of the brothers and with a quick word in his ear, the brother disappears off out of the common room.

 

Abbott Jengor listens to the bantering across the table, “Please, now is not the time for arguing, it is the time for celebrations, but it sounds to me like this is a race in trouble and in need of our help”, turning to Varistan he adds, “I would also find it hard to believe that they would have tended to Brother Jarrod, if they were the ones to cause him injury”.

 

Before the Abbott can continue he hears the soft tones of Aeriel at the doorway, giving her a beaming smile he beckons her in, “No, my dear, you are not interrupting anything, please come and join us”. He smiles, as Aeriel heads over to Karg and he sees the welcoming between them, “she has been lost without you around Karg, I am wondering if maybe she should join you on your return to the stone circle, I think it will help in building her confidence back up”. Seeing the young elf hesitate, in his warmest voice he says, “Aeriel, these people are our friends, they mean you no harm, and they have assisted us in finding our missing brother, please sit down and join us”.

Varistan:

Varistan forgets about his brooding when Aerial walks into the room, his eyes going from narrow and stern to open and wide. Curiosity and wonder is marked clearly on his face. He mutters under his breath, "damn, that's somethin' you don't see every day."

 

His anger and frustration momentarily forgotten, and distracted by this unique stranger, he forgets to tinge his response to the Abbot with his customary sarcasm. "Aye. You're probably right. I still don't trust 'em completely, but they seem to have done alright by Jarrod."

Aeriel:

The winged elf sees all the attention focused on her and blushes a deep red, the colour even reaching to her wings. She looks down at her feet for a moment, overcome with embarrassment.

 

Aeriel finally looks up at Yanish. "I thank thee for thy welcome, Little One. Thou art most kind." She looks again at Yanish. Is this a child, she wonders? No, she seems to sure of herself to be a child. Perhaps she can find out later.

 

She sits carefully, spreading her wings slightly so can sit, then folding them back again. She then looks at the Abbott. "Stone circle?" she asks, looking around, then back at the Abbott. "But if thy brother is safe, what purpose is there in a return to such a place?" Her eyes, which are slightly larger than normal, are quite wide now with her curiosity.

Lindornea'en:

Lin returns that slight smile... but, unlike the rest, he seems content to pawn the situation off like he's been in this particular stew pot before. Any watching the Elf might notice his secretive glances at the Winged one, however...

 

His mind filled with stories of the Ancient, he knows what she is... but had never before thought to see one... Curiosity fills his thoughts just as surely as the rest, but he's quite capable of hiding it...

Mickey:

Mickey has been slurping and chomping even as the newcomer enters and introductions are made. As his name is mentioned, he raises a hand in salutation, his eyes never leaving the bowl before him. He glances at Varistan as the man belches, and a moment later, releases one of his own. It is then he finally looks up to the new voice before him...

 

"...uh... ummm... pl-pleased to meet you m-m-miss Ariel..."

 

He drops the spoon into the now emptied bowl and rises from his seat as he speaks. He bows like a proper gentleman, then nearly falls to the floor as he sits down to close to the edge of the bench.

 

His eyes are wide, not in awe or shock, but in that she looks SO MUCH like his lost mother...an angel of pure elfin beauty...it is truly love at first sight.

Dolly:

Dolly nods a greeting to the frail looking elf with the wings, and smiles ruefully to herself at Mickey’s reaction.

 

*First spider people then bird elves, whatever next* she wonders to herself.

Mickey:

Mickey manages to overcome his heart, and tears his eyes away from the angel before him. Looking to one side, he catches the expression on Dolly’s' slanted smile. He leans closer, then whispers,

 

"Forgive me Dolly - she’s' just so...well, you know. Still, I know nothing of the girl, and I have travelled with you for a couple of days now...

 

- a quick glance to the feathered wings -

 

"...you still hold a very special place in my heart. It's just that...I umm... she looks so much like Mother! Isn't she beautiful?"

 

Mickey knows his words are fractured and unhelpful. He is still too young to control his fickle nature, and hearts are bound to be broken.

Rupert:

As Aeriel enters, Rupert's eyes widen. Frantically he begins searching his pockets, before finding a small book. He looks at the young elf and hastily thumbs through the pages, settling his spectacles on his nose to better read. He smiles a greeting to her but says nothing before going back to reading...

Aeriel:

Aeriel looks about her as she awaits an answer, still obviously embarrassed by the attention. *They must think that such as I am a freak,* she wonders. She sees the one ground elf return her smile and feels better. She feels better too that Karg is next to her as she tentatively helps herself to some food.

 

As Mickey addresses her she cannot help but smile. Such an awkward lad! Aeriel wonders if it is the mixture of the pure and impure blood in him, as it appears he is only part elf. And the woman next to him- an unusual looking creature who seemed to know him.

 

As she looks around she realizes she is staring as much as the others and blushes yet again.

Mickey:

Turning back to the fair elf-angel, Mickey smiles his best smile, unconsciously running his fingers through the tangles of his blonde hair. He pushes the empty bowl away, and says to her,

 

"..ummmm, yeah. The Circle is apparently home to some weird bug-people. They were caring for Jarrod until some of his own kind came to find him...somebody like us.

 

"Seems they have troubles of their own down there, some sort of evil beasties roaming their halls eating all the people...probably just a really big bird.", he laughs uneasily at his own attempt at humour, then with a slight blush, continues,

 

"Since they took such good care of the shepherd Jarrod, we offered to help. Return the favour with a little monster-hunt if you will. Hopefully, they won't hold that poor bugger in the pit against us..."

 

He reaches into his pocket and pulls forth the ancient key he found. He sits daydreaming in the memory of that first room. The smoke, the dragon carving, the pit he fell into and nearly died in...

 

His smile fades. Inwardly, he worries now that he may look incompetent yet again should they return. He wishes so badly to impress Aeriel, so he tells her of their battle in the dark, the discovery of hidden and ancient armouries, and his keen discovery of the Key.

 

"I'm hoping its the very one to open the locked door beneath the smoking dragon keystone. It certainly looks right..."

Aeriel:

Aeriel listens intently, hardly touching her food. The feathers on the outside of the unbound wing quiver from moment to moment as she hears the tale.

 

As Mickey finishes the story, her eyes widen in concern. "Mayhap I did understand not thy tale," she interjects quickly. "Didst thou say this was under the ground, in holes and caves?" The twitching of her leading feathers increases slightly. "Is- is it a small place?" Her voice shakes slightly as she asks the question.

Mickey:

Mickey smiles as the young lady expresses her interest, then frowns slightly with concern as he watches her tremble like a fearful robin beneath the paws of a cat. "Yes...it's under the earth. The entry was found beneath a fallen stone of the circle. But it is not small and cramped, why, the stairs alone were wide enough for two men!"

 

He stands and walks around the table, asking Rupert for his sketch of the complex as he does so..."Thanks, Rupe..."

 

"You see here, this room was fairly large, but it holds a ten foot ceiling height by my recollection. The halls are not unlike those of a castle, although a little aged and worn. It is dark, but we were never far from the sunlight. There was room enough for Karg, so it's not that bad..."

 

He tries his best to reassure her, but he knows that this may well prove to be a difficult trip for Aerial, even if we encounter nothing...

Aeriel:

The young woman nods, hearing the concern in Mickey's voice. She glances over at Karg, realizing how big a space he would need to be comfortable. At barely 5', one as slight as her should be able to function.

 

Aeriel looks at the others, realizing what a little fool she must appear to be. "Not as small a space as one might think," she agrees, trying to calm herself. "In sooth, the Goddess watches for me, as did she when I was taken. And I am not helpless, I assure thee! I pray I shall not be a burden to thee."

Karg:

Karg smiles down at Aeriel, trying to put aside her discomfort. "Aeriel no fear. Cave big place." His eyes drift over towards Mickey, but he doesn't look directly at the halfbreed, "Karg watch Aeriel, keep Aeriel safe. Karg sure none dare hurt Aeriel."

Lindornea'en:

Lin finally sits up, watching the spectacle before him... Especially Mickey. He wonders, if not for the first time, what would happen should someone give him a slight tap on the shoulder... Always like a spring wound too tight. The elf walks over to the conversation.

 

In the blink of an eye, Lin turns on the charm, something those who've thus far haven't seen. The elf bows before the Winged one, lightly taking one angelic hand, he places a soft kiss upon it. With an assurance matching Karg, the High elf says, "Nothing to worry about. I have my doubts that our 'Friends' have released much of anything. Besides... We may more be interested in just finding out what it'd be. For all we know, they've been scared by a Dung Beetle." He sits calmly down at the table... finally joining the 'party'... though, the sneaking suspicion of why could be ascertained... [*grin*]

Aeriel:

Aeriel smiles gratefully at Karg. "Thou art my saviour, Gentle Karg," she tells him. "I thank thee."

 

As Lin takes her hand and kisses it, she blushes again- obviously something she does easily. She meets the elf's eyes shyly. You do me honour, Noble Sir," she replies in very careful and accented Elvish.

 

The Avariel looks back at the rest of the party, ruffling her wings slightly. "All of you have made me most welcome, and I thank thee," she says, reverting back to the common tongue. "I shall join thee in thy quest, if that be thy desire. Mayhap him that doth seek my doom will follow me not where we go, nor chance to kidnap me again with thy presence."

Dolly:

Dolly catches Aeriel and Yanish’s eyes and barely suppresses a giggle.

 

*Young men and their Hormones* she thinks to herself * They think they look good, but don't realise what fools they make of themselves. Especially that obnoxious Lin.*

Karg:

Karg's eyes beam at Aeriel's comment. As she speaks of he that would be her doom, Karg growls like the bear he reveres. "Karg give oath, Aeriel safe while Karg live. Aeriel no fear Gree'da chanter." He brings his hand down on the table with a thud, underscoring the seriousness of his resolve.

Lindornea'en:

Lin smiles back at Ariel, he returns (in Elvish), "The honour is all mine, Lady Arvynquesse" [Heaven Feather ;)] His attention seemingly returns to the matter at hand, "Now, we have decided to return? .. It looks to me at least a small recon mission is in order. And along those lines, we should also choose a leader... Someone that seems to command trust within the group." His gaze sweeps over the whole of the conglomeration of individuals... and stops at Karg, "You've done well so far, at least that I've seen."

Aeriel:

Aeriel blushes yet again at Lin's comment to her. In common, she responds. "Oh, by the Goddess, I am not worthy of that!" she replies. "Thou art far too kind to one most far from home," she adds in High Elvish. Her pause indicates that she has to think to speak in that tongue.

 

She smiles at Karg, even as she is startled by his actions, her left wing slightly unfurling and showing some of its white feathery beauty. "Oh! But thou wouldst make a most fine leader, Karg," she quickly replies. "Thou are brave and kind and gentle to others in need, all qualities of a leader most grand."

Karg:

Karg looks at Lin in surprise, then looks to Aeriel as well. He seems rather confused by the suggestion, seeing himself more a loner than a chief.

 

"All do what Karg say? Karg chief?" He looks around, wondering if the others are willing to commit to following his commands as well.
