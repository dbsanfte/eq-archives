Dungeon Master:

Making your way single file, carefully down the stairway, you come to a very narrow landing where the steps double back on themselves and continue down. Approximately 100 feet below you find yourself in a chamber filled with a blue haze, making it impossible to see more than a few feet in any direction. The haze smells of strange spice, smoke, and dank copper

Karg:

Not liking the confines of the depths, Karg hangs back and ends up near the end of the group. Entering the chamber, he mutters low tones in his native tongue it sounds almost a dirge. He drops to his hands and knees at the foot of the stairs and sniffs loudly, but the stench prevents him from learning anything. "Karg no think Jarrod choose come this place. Jarrod here, Jarrod snared."

Micky:

Unable to see clearly, Mickey stays close to the left wall as he works his way east. He turns to Lin and Yanish as they reach the first door(?) whispering quietly,

 

"What say we let the lady have first shot at this one? Of course, even if she finds it safe, we can still add our own opinion..."

 

He peers back down the line of adventurers, wondering at their reactions to the strange mist. He notices Karg squinting more than the rest of us - oh, of course, he's the only human among us. A little louder, but still a whisper,

 

"You okay Karg? Should we strike forth a light?" He looks back to Yanish and Lin, awaiting his turn at the inspection of the first door of the quest.

Lindornea'en:

Lin nods his assent to Mickey's idea. The elf glances annoyed back at Karg, though, as the Barbarian continues to not keep his tone low. He sighs, and pulls out two swords... One from across his back, the other hidden beneath his cloak, "I prefer to be ready, should the need arise..."

Karg:

Karg turns at the sound of Micky's voice asking about light. "Karg no need light. Karg wait here, keep back safe. No near Karg without warning." He hefts the massive club and moves aside a bit so as not to stand right at the foot of the stairs.

Varistan:

Varistan continues to follows along the wall to the left, trailing behind Mickey and Lin. Making no comment at Mickey's suggestion, he simply waits for his chance to give the door a careful inspection.

Gob:

Seeing everybody disappear down into the depths Gob follows along and at the bottom waits beside Karg deciding that it is definitely better to hang out with the big guy.

 

"Hey Karg - this place is a little on the dingy side - want to help lighten the mood with another one of your clan songs?"

Dolly:

Getting to the bottom of the stairway Dolly strains to see into the room. All she can see is haze and the pretty boys heading off left. So suddenly thrusting her Axe to the right she takes a step or two in that direction and then waits for something to happen.

Karg:

At Gob's suggestion, Karg makes a sound deep in his throat, an impressive imitation of a bear. In a low voice, he responds, "Karg no sing, Karg need hear noise in dark. Gob be quiet too."

Yanish:

Upon hearing the half-elfs suggestion, Yanish swallows the lump in her throat and silently glides down the stairs. Standing next to the mouse, she stands on her tiptoes and gently places a finger to his lips in nice but firm way of saying (silence is golden). Turning her attention to the door indicated she checks it for any traps, if none can be found she will check if the door is locked. If the door is unlocked she will open it.      

Dungeon Master:

The door opens into a hallway. To the east seems completely blocked by thick strands of pearl-gray webbing.

Gob:

Seeing all these big folks arguing about being quite and being nervous about being in a hole - we need something to lighten the mood Gob thinks to himself.

 

With a burst of inspiration he pulls around his little lute and with a flourish strikes a raucous chord and launches into a popular folk dancing tune.

Dolly:

At the first sound of the Chord, Dolly swings around Axe ready. By the time she has committed to the blow she realises it is just the stupid halfling messing about. She just manages to abort the blow, inches short of the halfling....

 

"Idiot" she calls softly and without affection as she turns to watch her side again.

Yanish:

Unshouldering her pack, Yanish rummages through it and pulls out a small flask of oil and her flint. To the Elf and Half-elf she says in a quite voice. "Flame works well on webs, but its kinda ruff on your vision. If either of you have a better way to get through, now would be the time to say it." With that said she waits patiently watching the webs for movement.

Gob:

Stopping in mid song at Dolly's hiss Gob quips - "music is the soul of life and should be enjoyed in all places at all times."

 

Gob picks back up in his song

Karg:

As Gob takes up the song again, Karg growls a quiet oath in his native language. Reaching out, he grabs the neck of Gob's lute with one massive hand and holds it tightly so that the strings grow taut. "Gob be silent." He retains his hold on the instrument, "Karg need break stick?" he asks in a calmer voice.

Gob:

Gob looks quite upset at Karg's threat but he straightens up and with a look of utmost indignation ...

 

"My dear sir - if you break my lute you shall be required to pay for it - but regardless I have my mouth organ or my fine voice if need be - you need to learn to respect other cultures and to learn when a bit of music can help lighten the burdens of your day"

Dolly:

Dolly turns back to Gob and smiles her crooked little grin. "I can break either of those while Karg works on the banjo, if you like. I think now would be a good time for you to respect his" nodding at Karg, "culture. Don't you?" Her smiles stays crooked but now Gob can see more of her uneven teeth poking through the gap between her thin lips.

Gob:

Looking at Dolly

 

"That may be so but likewise mine as well - i was selected for this mission as well because I can bring cheer and be a chronicler of the history in the making - Now I suggest you all get on with creeping around holes whilst I help to inspire bravery and to keep the moral high!"

Lindornea'en:

Lin gives an annoyed glance over his shoulder, "I know you mean well, Mr. Bard, but if I was down here... with you playing, I could probably track you for a Half-Mile." He goes back to readiness, waiting for the Shortie to open the door, "So, I advise you shut it."

Dolly:

"Not often I agree with him." she remarks conversationally, "And its you that wants to respec' cultures, not me. I just want you to shut up." She starts to turn away but then turns back for one last comment. " Oh, and by the way. That's the last time I am gonna ask you"

Rupert:

Moving along behind his sister, Rupert gives a snort. "Maybe we can all be quiet," he adds. "There is time for frolic and time for work. In this case, work means not giving a recital while we are trying to sneak, eh?"

Karg:

Karg looks down at the insufferable Gob, he asks quietly "Gob buy new club when Karg break old one over head of Gob?" He lets go of the instrument and proceeds to ignore the little fellow.

Lindornea'en:

Lin snickers at Karg's comment... "Man knows how to bargain."

Gob:

Gob shakes his head in amusement and with no less of a voluminous voice continues

 

"My dear lady if there is anyone here they already know we are here from the antics of everyone trooping down the stairs like an army - if you were sensible only a single person would have scouted ahead - preferably the adorable Yanish who as the knack of silent moving - as however we are already known to be here - then let us announce our presence as guests and visitors not an invading army - after all we are here to find a lost man of the cloth not here to steal and rob. And yes my dear barbarian I do have another Lute back home with which to practice - this one here (Giving the little lute a strum) is my travelling instrument. If you break it you will need to make amends for it unless you have no honor"

Karg:

"Gob no play, Karg no break. Gob choose play, Gob choose break. Karg honor not in question."

Gob:

"Ah I understand now - you don't like the instrument - of course you come form a clan of chanters - Then let me inspire with the voice of acapella my beefy friend"

 

God slings the lute back around his back and stops to pause for a moment and then Starts a battle chant he learnt as a youth from his instructor in singing

Micky:

Oblivious to most of the conversation behind him, Mickey has been quietly standing close to Yanish, intently staring down the Eastern passage beyond the first door.

 

Again, another dispute - this group would destroy itself in less than a fortnight if left alone, who needs spiders? Still, he watches closely for some sign of life...(Do arachnids generate heat?)

 

He hears the badly tuned music, and the angry growling of Karg. He hears the talk of the values of clubs versus banjos, and then bows his head when the halfling speaks of our 'loud' entrance to this eerie place. Mickeys' skills are yet untested, and not much more than that of a rank amateur....

 

The moment of silent self-deprecation passes as he looks down in admiration to the tiny halfling Yanish. So talented! There is still much to learn...

 

"Lets' torch it. I say, better safe than sorry. The rest of them are debating music theory and cultures, let's press on, they'll catch on soon enough."

 

He deftly produces his tinderbox in the nimble black gloved hands. He was always better at locks than moving and hiding anyway...

 

"A light, Miss Yanish?"

 

He looks back at Varistan and Lin with a nod. The argument beyond shall certainly desist once the cries of the burning drown it out...

Dolly:

"OK, Mr Music Man, You come and stand here in the middle of the room, and see if you can hear the nasty monsters creeping up in you in the dark, while you are singing and playing that bloody banjo."

 

She reaches across to grab Gob and move him into the center of the room, but misses her grasp.

Yanish:

Yanish flashes a smile to the mouse as he offers a light. Using her dagger Yanish slices a small piece of her cloak off and stuffs it into the oil flask. After the half-elf lights the wick, she throws the lit flask at the webs.

 

And prepares to leap out of the way if any flaming eight legged critters come running.

Dungeon Master:

The flask hits the webbing and lands about two feet inside, as it hits the floor the bottle smashes and the oil catches fire instantaneously. The flaming oil slowly seeps towards you, but the flames seem to be having no effect whatsoever on the webbing.

Yanish:

As the flames and flask pass through the web, Yanish voices what her mind thinks. "The webs seem to be an illusion, however stay alert. I have done what I can; now I leave the glory and boasting for the warriors among us. From what I have heard thus far, boasting seems to be most of your best asset." She steps aside to allow the rest of the group to walk into the hallway. Now well lit with the flames.        

Dungeon Master:

The flames lick around the webbing, and the smoke billows past you looking for an exit.

Gob:

Yet again interrupted from his singing Gob looks at Dolly and shakes his head ruefully

 

"My fairest maiden, well lady anyway, I have seen no signs that would indicate any such beasts - this passage is obviously made by intelligent beings of some kind - no the lair of a bear etc - besides if you wish to creep around as though afraid of the very shadows then so be your courage - let us go forth boldly and announce our presence like men afraid of naught - as in the tales of old - fearless, on a heroic mission not a thieves creeping in the dark - where is your sense of adventure and your spirit of fun?"

Dolly:

"OK Mr Music. Lets do it your way. Yanish has opened the door - lets proceed - you go first ..... And I will stand a few yards behind you making a racket."       

Gob:

"That would indeed be fun but I doubt such headstrong individuals would appreciate being led by someone of such short stature - though if you wish for myself to take the reigns as the nominated leader of this band of merry folk then I shall happily take the reigns"

Dolly:

"No, No Mr Music, what a thought." and she is almost laughing, and certainly shaking her head - "You really are a bit slow aren't you? I was offering to let you walk through there first - so that you can meet what ever is in there first and see if you can hear it while I am singing behind you. A sort of experiment to prove to prove how good your morale is. Then we can keep doing it until you have decided how well you can hear while I am singing.

Micky:

Mickey looks back with a smile upon the raucous Gob and his bold opinion of an open, honest entry. Sure, lose the element of surprise - wonderful idea...

 

Shaking his head, he realizes that with all the arguing, fire and smoke, that element has long been lost already. The little man is just anxious with his ever burning curiosity, what better way to shut him up?

 

"Good idea, Dolly. Yanish already burned out any possible arachnid threats Gob, why don't you bravely lead us forward from here?"

 

He looks to Lin, Varistan and Yanish with a wink,*This'll be fun to watch*.

 

"You go on ahead Master D'smacked, I'll be right behind you..."

Varistan:

Ignoring the bickering of the others, Varistan peers down the hallway, still unable to see much of anything due to the flames and the obscuring billows of smoke.

 

He coughs as smoke wafts past him on its search for an exit, and while the others argue about sending Gob down the passage, Varistan takes it upon himself to close the door.

 

"We won't be going down this way just yet, unless any of you can walk through fire and breath smoke."

 

He coughs again, waving more smoke from his face, and begins to move further along the wall.

 

"So just shut all your holes, and let's finish our search of this room in the meanwhile, eh?"

 

He continues to move, searching the wall and the floor in front of him for anything suspicious as he goes.

Gob: 

Smiling Gob looks at Mickey and Dolly

 

"Why thank you both of you for your support I know I will be able to count on you as loyal subjects - and Dolly Think singing would be a great idea would be happy to help you tune your voice - anything in particular you wish to sing?"

 

With a wave in Varistan's direction

 

"I wish yon sir here could make up his minds - first he is opening up passage ways and plunging into these holes and then he wants us to close them up - without a few tons of rocks and mortar I do not see how we can close up these holes?"

Micky:

Ignoring Gob, Mickey nods seriously at Varistan as he moves along the wall to the next portal. Of course we don't really want to burn up the only musician in the group, even if he is a bit off-key...

 

Without a word, he follows, watching as Varistan inspects the second door. Always good practice to watch anothers technique, never know what you'll learn...

Lindornea'en:

Lin sighs, listening to the stupidity flowing about the room... Figuring this to be the best time for a little 'Call to Action' he throws a comment over a shoulder to the 'Woman' and Karg, "You're going to let the weak elves... Derivatives included, lead the way again?" a slight grin breaks out on his fair face, before following Mickey and Co.

Gob:

"Apparently the lady thinks that an indomitable halfling should lead the way - what do you think?"

Dolly:

Looking across at Lin Dolly responds quickly. "Yep! Do you think I'm as stupid as the sawn off music man, here? 'Though you haven't shown any great signs of it yet - you are supposed to be good at sneaking, well according to the stories. Just not bright enough to do it too well I suppose." she smiles as sweetly as she can, which gives a fairly strange effect for those who are close enough to see. "So why should I want to deprive you of the practice you so clearly need?"

Karg:

Karg looks at Lindornea'en, or at least in the direction of the voice, with confusion on his face. Of course the weak should be in front. Not only does it allow the strong to attack from a position of strength, but by removing the weak, it also makes the tribe stronger. What's that term the lowlanders used with their tame beasts, culling the herd.

 

Karg responds quietly, "Karg let weak walk ahead. Give weak chance prove not weak."

Mickey:

Mickey stands near Varistan as he inspects the second door and hears the conversation clearly, and even though he can see none of the participants, the voices are easy enough to recognize...

 

Even as the other thief examines the door, Mickey looks it over himself. Looks like a door. No traps... is it even locked?

 

Then he hears the words of Karg, "...the weak shall lead..."

 

He looks to the floor in shame. The Bear-man is right, his skills as a thief are that of an amateur, had then been better, perhaps his parents would have survived...

 

Still, he wonders. Looking back over his shoulder to the north, he questions the actual experience of ANY in this room. * Are they really any better than me, or is it just so much boasting? *
