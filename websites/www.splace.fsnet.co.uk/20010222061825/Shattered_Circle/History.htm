A Brief History

     Civilizations ebb and flow like the tide.  When an elder race diminishes and fades, thousands upon thousands of years pass before younger races move into the forgotten region.  Of the elders whose grand cities crumbled thousands of years before, few clues remain, save for the occasional dolmen, standing stone, or henge.  Such is the case of Cahervaniel.

     The Circle of Cahervaniel, so named by early Suloise settlers, is a simple ring of crudely carved (or much eroded) standing stones measuring some 20 yards in diameter.  Generations of sheep herders have moved their flocks over the hill and through the circle, sometimes resting in the cool shadows cast by the old stones at midday  Never once has there been any indication that the site was anything more than an innocuous crumbling, ruin, just one of many that are so prominent in this part of the world.  In all the years that sheep have grazed near the stony fingers of the Circle, things have been quiet, at least on the surface ....
