RAID ETIQUETTE

 

 

ARRIVING

 

The gathering location is posted on the raid calendar, make sure you are there at least half an hour beforehand, the posted time is the start time. If you cannot be there, get to a druid ring or a wizard spire an hour before, pay attention to guild chat as to who is offering ports. Announce that you require a port and from where, if no response send a polite tell to a guild druid who seems to be porting asking if they’re coming your way, they may have missed the guild line request while porting, or may have organised another druid to head to your location.

 

Say so early if you have already get a port!

 

 

GROUPING

 

When you arrive drop your group, turn off anon and role-play, and turn on your LFG flag.  Make sure you have joined the raid channel, this is where the raid leader announces groups. Groups are set up to be generally self sufficient exp-style groups, tank, healer, support (enchanter shaman) and melee. The MT group gets the kills in order to systematically announce loot. Remember the raid is not about getting your alt points, if you notice your group getting a few too many kills, lay off the nuking for a while.

 

 

BUFFING

 

The group leader sends tells to the group-buffers for buffs, e.g. aegolism, khura’s focusing, group stamina, circles, c3, etc. The haster's and c2’ers will be announced, send 'a tell' to them for your buffs, don’t use the raid channel. If a vital buff gets dispelled, say so over the group line and the leader will ask the buffer for a new group buff.

 

Make sure you’re near your group leader while buffing is going on!

 

The guild provides peridots, pearls, etc. so ask the raid leader for a few stacks if you need them for the raid.

 

 

MOVING

 

Pay attention to the raid channel for instructions to move. Don’t wander off exploring, try not to get left behind, if you do, ask your group which direction they went. If you get caught behind repops don’t train, die like a man and get dragged through for a rez. Remember your loc, die in the open, send the raid leader a tell and consent your dragger as soon as possible.

 

  

KILLING

 

The MA and MT are announced on the raid channel, sometimes a shadow knight is the person you are assisting while you clear down, and the main tank will be a warrior.

 

Make sure you have attack_on_assist turned to off, so that when you press assist it doesn’t turn auto-attack on. You can type /assist off or fiddle with your ini file.

 

Wait for the MA’s call to attack, they need to build up aggro so the monster is facing them for the whole fight. Check your target with what he has announced on the raid channel.

 

Melee should be fighting from max melee distance, the MA closest to the monster. This means step back until it says you are too far away, then step in a little.

 

Melee should always fight BEHIND the mob, opposite the MA unless he tells you otherwise.

 

Large races please bring shrink potions, 20 melee behind a monster gets a little crowded with 3-4 ogres.

 

If you get aggro, turn attack off, feign, hide, step back.

 

If the monster runs in a dungeon or a plane, don’t chase if you’re under level 55 or a non-feigner, whichever seems sensible.

 

 

SPELLS

 

Dots are a big no-no on raids. An unmezzable monster may arrive and you need to switch target, and your current target needs to be mezzed, which it cant while dotted. Also be careful of epic dots if you’re a ranger. AE spells and proccing weapons are a bad idea too.

 

The call to nuke at 50% health is announced on the raid channel.

 

 

LOOTING

 

The MA group loots corpses to announce the items, the cash goes to the guild bank for peridots and other costs. If your group gets the exp, the leader loots and announces the drops. If nothing significant just hand the cash and items to the raid leader.

If the raid leader awards you an item, it's perfectly ok to pass if you don't think you would use it, or you have your eye on a different item. Send the raid leader a tell and they will re-assign the item.

If you haven't been asked to loot, DON'T

See "raid loot rules" under "guild rules" on the message board for more information.

 

COMMUNICATION

Raid channels are a marvellous way of cutting down shout and ooc spam for the other people in the zone who aren’t on the raid. That doesn’t mean you get to spam the raid channel. Keep announced actions there to a minimum, e.g. tash, slow, cheal, person dead. This is where the instructions are being announced so keep it nice and quiet.

 

Try not to give instructions or become a “pop-up officer” over the raid channel. The raid leader or the puller will call camp, move, evac, charge. Unless of course there is a horde of giants coming over the horizon from behind and no-one else is looking.

 

 

DYING/WIPEOUT

 

Should you get aggro from melee damage, nuking, debuffing, stand still, the tanks will get it off you, or it will be mezzed. Running around doesn’t help. Stand and die if you must. Try to die in the open any time you die. Expect to die on a raid, it’s not basic exp grind, things can go wrong. Wait patiently, a rez will be coming as soon as the monster is dead.

 

Should the raid leader or puller call camp, that doesn’t mean you, it means the vital clerics. You get to look after the clerics while they sit. Taunt for your life, cast ae’s, all bets are off, keep the clerics safe.

 

Should you wipe out and need to walk back for summons, again ask for ports and say where you are, stick together. Running anywhere is a lot more dangerous naked.

 

NEVER EVER gate out of a raid even if you think you will die, chances are you will not be able to rejoin again.

 

 

LEAVING

 

If you can gate, do so. Sometimes the clerics have bound outside and may need a lift so be nice to them. Send polite tells to a wizard for a TL stating your destination. He will probably have bind, Gfay and DL memmed.

 

Thank the raid leader for a wonderful raid and anyone else you felt needs a special thanks, it really makes a difference to the people playing.

 

 

LATECOMERS & EARLY LEAVERS

 

Both of these cause MAJOR disruption to raids. If you know in advance that you will be late or cannot stay till the end, please let the raid leader know prior to the raid so they can accommodate.

 

If you are a latecomer DO NOT expect us to break raid groups to come and get you, find your own way there.  A senior member of the raid will co-ordinate you getting to the group with minimal disruption.  Please stay in close contact and do exactly as asked.

 

RL does take over at times and people do need to leave early, but please have the courtesy to let the raid leader know 'in tell' that you are leaving.  A message in the raid channel can be easily missed or cause a chain reaction of people leaving.
