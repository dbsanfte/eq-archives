Dungeon Master:

Uelwen watches as Karg bends to assist Jarrod. She listens intently as you argue between yourselves over what must be done, before she speaks up …

 

“We are not an unreasonable race and I understand you concerns” she says. “Maybe it will be better for him to be in his own surroundings”. She looks over at Vald before turning back to you and continuing, “Will you return to help us!” There is a sound of desperation in her voice as she asks this question

Varistan:

Varistan throws up his hands, expelling a sharp breath of frustration and mumbling "Hells, for a reasonable race it sure is a pain in the arse to get a straight answer out of 'em.

 

" Speaking more loudly now, he continues with "Let's get 'im back to the monastery, then. Maybe we can hear the whole story from 'im on the way and then we can decide to come back or not."

 

He holds out his hand towards Uelwen. "And can I get me sword back."

Micky:

Mickey giggles behind one hand as the bug-thing pleas in near desperation to the group and ignores any of our questions. Varistan is right - they ask a lot for very little. Best to leave now and then question Jarrod along the way. The shepherds' experience will influence greatly the young thief’s decision as whether or not to come back here, but he leaves the way open anyway in the name of possible future profit...

 

"Yes, we'll be back, but we must first tend to our own. The monastery is not far from here, and we shall return by this time tomorrow. Be ready then to answer our questions...ALL of our questions..."

 

Mickey then turns his back on the bugs, and does what he can to help Karg secure a litter of some form to help carry the injured shepherd upon. “Lets get out of this hole, I miss the sunshine... “

Yanish:

Yanish blinks as the bug woman stares at her. She thinks to herself, "I really must work on sticking to the shadows, and not attract so much attention." Shaking her head in frustration, both at herself the groups situation, Yanish steps forward. Addressing the leader she says with a slight bow, "I do believe if the proper reward were offered more of these warriors would be more convinced to assist you. However I would like to thank you for taking care of the fallen brother. I myself am not a warrior, however if a few of these strong men or women choose to return and assist you, I will be among them." With that said she waits for the group’s departure and the rest of her pay.

Karg:

In response to Uelwen's request, Karg nods. "Karg be back." he says with finality.

 

Karg looks over the spun silk. There's no way that would work to carry Jarrod back. He pulls at the sling on his back, pulling out a couple of his small spears. Maybe if he tied them together, that'd work to support Jarrod’s weight. Or maybe he should just carry the brother out and find some wood to use then. He looks over his companions, wishing some of them would be using spears or staves as weapons.

Lindornea'en:

Lin leans against his staff, and then notices Karg glancing about for more long posts. He sighs disgustedly and holds out the thing, "I'll be wanting this back."

Dolly:

Dolly watches as they start talking about ways of getting Jarrod out of here. "I am not sure you are going to get anything like that round the corners and up the stairs." She comments as Lin and Karg start talking about staves and sticks and the like.

 

"Maybe I should carry him 'til we are outside. Then there will be enough space to build something, or speedy there," nodding to Lin, "Could go get us a donkey or a pony from the Monastery and we could take him back in comfort."

Dungeon Master:

"Karg be back"

 

Uelwen cocks her head to one side, and her mouth changes in what could possibly be a smile, “you are a good man”, she says to him, “like your friend here”, she adds, gesturing down to the sleeping Jarrod. Ignoring Varistan for the moment, Uelwen looks to Mickey, “Yes, I will answer all the questions I can on your return”. Turning to the young lass, as Yanish speaks up, she nods in acknowledgement before shaking her head and answering, “Unfortunately we have little to offer such as yourselves, but I will try to make it worthwhile for you to assist us”

 

She walks over to Vald and with a few clicks and sounds he disappears off. Uelwen crouches at Jarrod’s cot side and gently wakes the sleeping man, “Your friends are here Jarrod, and they are going to take you home”. Vald returns with a crude stretcher, made out of the same webbing as everything else around you, and Varistan’s sword. Uelwen stands, and taking the stretcher from Vald gives it to Karg, “I hope this will be of some help to you”. She nods at Vald, and he returns Varistan’s sword to him.

Micky:

Mickey sees the others have the invalid shepard well in hand (or stretcher as space allows), so he turns from the group and wanders back to the main entry room. Looking down, he sees the dead Chiton...

 

Looking back quickly over his shoulder, he hears the others discussing poles, staves and how best to transport the man. He decides to act quickly, and drags the corpse over to the edge of the still opened pit. Once there, he looks back once more, then casually nudges the body over the edge with the heel of his boot...-SQUILCH-...

 

He turns and looks down at the puntured remains lying among the rusted spikes below. Hey, accidents happen, best not to make new friends into old enemies because of a little past ignorance....

 

* It was Gob's fault anyway...* , he thinks sadly.

 

He stands with his back to the pit waiting for the others to catch up and climb up out of this dark little place. It sure will be nice to see the sky again...

Varistan:

Varistan, not even offering a nod in thanks for the return of his sword, waits impatiently for the others to gather up the injured priest. Finally losing patience, and feeling not entirely welcome in the presence of these insectile people, he belatedly follows Mickey back to the main room.

 

Entering the room where they were initially attacked, he finds Mickey standing in front of the pit, and absolutely no sign of the body they had left behind. Confused just for a moment, he pieces together what Mickey had done, his only reaction a crooked grin and a wink for the quick-thinking Mickey.

 

"Let's get out of here, hey? The others should be out quick enough." And with that he heads up the stairs to the surface.

Yanish:

Yanish not having the size nor strength to help, gets out of the way and quickly follows Varistan back to the main chamber and up too the wonderful outdoors. To herself she thinks "The blood smear and drops will quickly lead our new found friends to their comrade, best to be gone when that happens." To Varistan she says "Well all in all that worked out pretty good. However I will be glad to have a pint of ale and a good meal in front of a fire." As an after thought "I wonder what ever became of Gob?"

Varistan:

Varistan grins down at Yanish.

 

"Yeah, worked out alright. Don't think it's over yet, though. Karg at least will be back, an' I'll probably come with 'im."

 

Realizing that what he just said might sound a bit altruistic, he adds "I don't care for those bugs too much, but there's gotta be something we can get out of this."

Yanish:

Nodding in agreement Yanish says, "I will probably return as well. Though as for reward, from what that bug woman said any ways, I don’t expect much. They don’t seem very well off." With a smile and a wink she adds "And charity doesn't pay all that much."

Varistan:

"True enough. But who knows what we may find down there? Besides, I got no other prospects. Nothin' that I'd prefer to this, at any rate."

Micky:

Mickey follows Varistan and Yanish back up the stairs, each footfall being one step closer to sunshine or rain, and either way, he welcomes both happily...

 

He listens to the conversation turn on the topic of coin, and looks back over his shoulder into the darkness below. The Chitines are ugly, and probably poor as well, but they are so different from us...

 

"Cash or no, I can't wait to get back here and see more of that place. The pit was a little unexpected, but did you see that dragon smoker? Never did get to use my key..."

 

Poor Mickey, when he's down he can't wait to get out, and it seems he's once out, he's just as eager to get back down again. At the top of the stairs, he looks to the four horizons and then settles back against one rock to watch the turning of the skies,"...so many interesting things down there. Think they built all this?"

Rupert:

Rupert nods in agreement as they decide to return and come back. To the half elf, it does indeed seem the best of all possible solutions. He tucks his journal back in his coat after a few notations and follows the others.

Karg:

In response to Uelwen's compliment, Karg shrugs. "You help Jarrod, Karg help you." He nods in thanks when the makeshift stretcher is handed over. Setting it down near the pallet, he looks to move Jarrod. Noticing that Mickey's already left and seeing Varistan following suit, Karg motions to those remaining. "Karg need help." He moves his hands gently under Jarrod’s shoulders, cradling the head with his forearms. He nods towards Jarrod’s feet.

 

Once Jarrod is on the stretch, Karg balances his club across the staves awkwardly and then [assuming someone takes the other end], lifts it slowly. He starts to push it through the door. "Karg leave now, return soon."

Lindornea'en:

Lin, without comment, picks up the other end of the stretcher. He raises an eyebrow at any of the expected glances... as if to say 'what are you looking at?'

Dolly:

Dolly lets the others leave first and assumes the rearguard position, taking care to keep an eye out behind her.

Rupert:

Rupert hesitates until he sees his sister is bound and determined to bring up the rear. He takes lets the others pass and follows, walking in front of her.

 

"At least we've accomplished one duty, haven't we Sis?" he remarks.

Dungeon Master:

“Thank you” Uelwen says as you get ready to leave, “Vald will guide you out, and I look forward to meeting with you once again on the morrow”, with a few words in their own language to Vald she disappears into the back room, and Vald leads you back down the corridor.

 

Before you head through the door into the main room, Vald stops you. “Please”, he says in broken common, “don’t let us down”. With that he closes the door behind you, and you make your way back up to the surface careful not to upset Jarrod too much.

 

On reaching the surface, Varistan, Mickey and Yanish are standing amongst the stones waiting for you. Jarrod groans on the stretcher at the brightness of the sunlight.

Dolly:

"Yep, we have that Rupe. It will be good to get him back to the Monastery. Then we can decide whether we come back with Karg or head off out on our own." She responds as they walk.

Micky:

Mickey catches the words of Dolly and turns to address her as the group slowly ambles away from the circle and back towards the Monastery. With a wink he says,

 

"I would hope you come back with Karg and the rest of us. I tell ya' Dolly, I haven't felt this safe with a woman since my Mother died. It felt right having you back us up down there against those bug-people. Heck, Rupert might even find a library somewhere under all that rock..."

 

He walks with a happy step, his golden hair glowing in the sunlight. Seems pretty-boy Mickey has a bit of an Oedipus complex, and has his eye on a new 'Mother'...

Dolly:

"We will have to see, my pretty." she calls back to Mickey. "We can talk about it over an ale tonight at the Monastery." She says winking at him.

Lindornea'en:

Lin keeps up his part of the stretcher... matching Karg's paces with his own, and besides bitching quietly about how the man is constantly keeping the stretcher higher than he's used to, follows along without incident. He looks visably sick when Mickey and Dolly pass comments back and forth, however...
