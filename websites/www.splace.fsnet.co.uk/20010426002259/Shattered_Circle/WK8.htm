Dungeon Master:

A full search through the debris uncovers a rusted iron key, several yellowed, ivory charms carved to resemble grinning skulls only one-half inch wide, and a total of 13 gold and 36 copper pieces.

 

Varistan: On close inspection of the southern door, with ease you remove a hidden needle. There is a stain upon the needle, but the lethal poison once coating it has evaporated in the Age since living creatures passed through this door.

 

Opening the door slowly you see that the ten foot square room once held shelves and racks on every wall – though now all the shelves on the eastern and southern walls have collapsed into a tumble of debris and dust on the floor. The racks on the west wall remain intact, and hold several dozen rotted wooden spears with rusted iron tips.

Micky:

When Mickey finds the rusting key, he smiles proudly. Perhaps this is the one for the dragons head across the way. He collects the key and the tiny little ivory skulls. He examines each one carefully as the others explore the 'armory' of rusted swords. He then scoops up the money, and as he drops it into his pouch, tells everyone,

 

"I am only holding the money, not claiming it. At the end of all this, the survivors shall each receive a fair share. You have my word as Donatelli...

 

"About these... skulls... any idea what they may be for? Some form of identification in the realms of the underdark maybe? I have thirteen of them, anybody want one?"

 

He offers a palm of tiny ivory carvings for any who wish to examine them closer. Once the others have thoroughly searched the armory (hopefully, without incident), Mickey leads the group across the room back to the eastern door and the ornate dragon lintel. There, he will try his newfound key of rust....

Karg:

Karg looks around at the room almost disappointed that there's nothing to bash aside. At Mickey’s assurances, Karg laughs. "Karg give money to church. Money no good to Karg. Karg think skulls bad. Karg no want no skulls."

Lindornea'en:

Lin raises an eyebrow, "For once, The Lout and I are in perfect agreement. Skulls are usually denoting death, or infernal watchers. I'll not touch the damned things." He finally crosses the threshold of the door, and enters the room... giving Mickey and his 'skulls' a wide berth.

Dolly:

Grinning at the response of the others, she responds, "Let's have a look then, Mickey." as she reaches out a hand for one of the skulls.

Yanish:

With the possibility that the skulls might be used in some sort of game, Yanish steps forward and holds out her little hand. "If you can spare one luv would you mind passin me a skull?" Studying the skull she racks her brain over any stories she might have heard about small skulls. While doing so she ponders the idea of creating a game using the small skull. One that would possible bring a little coin her way.

Rupert:

Rupert holds his hand out too for one of the little artifacts. "Knowledge is power, you know," he remarks, reaching into a pocket for one of his books. "If you always hide from the unknown, you shall never learn."

Varistan:

Wordlessly, Varistan plucks one of the skull charms from Mickey's palm, taking a moment to look it over as he rolls it around in his fingers before pocketing it with a shrug.

 

That done, he tentatively follows Lin into the room and makes a search through the dust and aged weapons, kicking aside pieces of debris to see if anything is hidden in the clutter.

Karg:

Karg looks at Lin with an air of challenge about him. "Karg no know lout. Elf insult Karg?"

Dungeon Master:

In addition to scores of broken and useless weapons, a thorough search of the small room uncovers three long swords, five daggers, one spear, and one two-handed sword that have not rusted to uselessness over time. On close inspection each weapon bears a carved skull in its pommel or shaft.

Lindornea'en:

Lin snorts at the comment, his quiet laughter hissing about the air. With only a look over his shoulder at Karg, Lin retorts, "It's only an insult when it holds very little Fact. [And with a Imitation of the Barbarian] Elf was under impression that was Truth." He raises an eyebrow mockingly, and continues into the weaponry... The elf compares the insignia. "Mickey... bring yourself, and your skulls over here..."

Yanish:

Yanish looks up from studying the small skulls and watches the different items unearthed from the destroyed arms room. "Would anyone mind if I took one of those dagger? A lady can never have to many dagger."

Varistan:

Wordlessly, Varistan scoops up one of the daggers and slips it into his belt, hardly giving it a thought. But his eyes gleam at the sight of the longswords. Taking one up, he eyes it appraisingly, and gives a grunt of curiosity when he sees the skull, mentally comparing it to the charms that Mickey had gathered. He shrugs, and sheathes the skull blade into his scabbard, keeping his own sword bare and in hand.

 

He grins as Yanish expresses a desire for the daggers, and moves back out of the room, waiting for the others to claim the remainder of the weapons.

Rupert:

Rupert looks long at the little skull in his hand. "Hmmm..." he says as he takes out his spectacles and looks closer. "I wonder if this was a symbol for a group or unit? The skulls on the weapons might have been identifying marks of a group while these little skulls functioned in the manner of passes or identifying badges?"

Micky:

Mickey hands out skulls to those who wish, snickering behind the back of the giant Karg. Superstitious one isn't he...? The half elf is quick to notice the discovery of the weapons, and even quicker to note the lack of aging.

 

Either these are recent, or these are magical.... Either way, they hold value, so he grabs up a longsword. "Nobody minds if I take this do they? Let me know..."

 

Then he walks over to Lin, his eyes eagerly searching the room for signs of traps, hidden doors, recent habitation... just the eager viewing of a strange place.

 

"What have you found Lin? My skills are at your disposal...."

 

Then he realizes that he has misheard the words of the elf. The Elfin accent is still too thick to be easily reconciled. Blushing, he hands one of the skulls over to the ranger and mumbles a quiet word of apology....

Dolly:

*Here we go again* she thinks to herself as she listens to Lin's poor attempt at humour and the extra insults included within it. *I wonder if he realises what an arrogant prat he makes himself sound? Still he's not having a go at me this time. I wonder if he realises that soon he won't have anyone left here that likes him.* Dolly takes one of the daggers and inspects it briefly, before sticking it through her belt.

Lindornea'en:

[I was wondering that myself... ;)]

 

Lin sighs and shakes his head at Mickey. He motions towards one of the weapons, "Would you like to compare, or should I?"

Micky:

Mickey nods in understanding, he was going to do that, given the time... he looks carefully at the skull 'badge' and the skull on the weapon. More than likely a matched set, but what does it matter? Shrugging, he gestures to the weapons still lying on the floor,

 

"Not sure what it is you're looking for Lin, why don't you take one of those blades and show me. Looks like a match, but my sight is not as keen as your own..."

Varistan:

Varistan waits just outside the door, listening to Mick and Lin, and then voices his opinion.

 

"The little skulls match the skulls on the blades. Mystery solved. Let's get movin'."

 

With that, he moves back to the main room, crossing the floor to the door behind which they had set the fire, curious to see if the fire is still burning.

Yanish:

Yanish waits until those that wanted a dagger have claimed one, then she scoops two up and follows Varistan out of the room. Leaving the remaining weapons to be divided by those more interested in talking than actually equipping themselves.

Micky:

Leaving the ancient armory, Mickey quietly follows Varistan and Yannish back over to the first opened doorway. The air has cleared significantly since we first entered, although the heavy and acrid smell of smoke lingers.

 

"Are the fires out Varistan? You going to go first...?"
