Dagger Rock   <-- Click here for map

 

     The town of Dagger Rock boasts a growing population now in excess of 200 inhabitants.  It owes its name to a strange rock formation, in the middle of the Silverfish River on the northeastern edge of town, which markedly resembles a very large stone dagger stabbed straight into the heart of the stream.   Most of the townspeople make their livings by selling and bartering their goods and services to others in the town.  Some sell their crops (mostly grains) to the cities in the south.  Sometimes, a small barge or ship stops at the docks, and the crew spends their time and money at one of the local inns or taverns, but this is a rare occurrence.

     There are various shops in Dagger Rock including a General Store, Blacksmiths, Bakers, Wine and Spirit Shop, a Clothier, and Leather Goods Shop.  Also found in the town is a Bank, a Mason, Stables, Carpenters and a Church.  There are two Inns, The Hearthfire Inn, and The Happy Half-Ogre Inn and Tavern, as well as Dagger Rock Tavern. 

     The mayor of Dagger Rock is called  Winston Jacob and Captain of the Guards is Sidon Bearclaw, both reside in the town itself.

 

 

Circle of Cahervaniel   <-- Click here for maps

 

     White shafts of stone thrust up from the earth, decayed and broken.  Their circular arrangement at the top of a grassy hill suggests the last oral remnant of some long-rotted giant in the earth.  Though some columns are bent, and others lean against their companion stones in the thirty-foot-diameter circle, only one stone has truly fallen over.

     The fallen column is partly shattered, and chunks of stone are scattered down the hillside.  The unshattered portion has slid down the hillside as well, looking almost like a disembodied step.  A dark gap remains in the earth where the stone recently stood, a black cavity in the truest sense of the word.

     A gap measuring five feet on a side remains where an ancient dolmen recently fell.  The cavity is utterly dark, however, rounded steps are visible within the gap, plunging into the earth at a steep angle.  Cobwebs thick with stone dust, smooth sharp corners, and the smell of earth, moisture, and rot is strong in your nostrils.
