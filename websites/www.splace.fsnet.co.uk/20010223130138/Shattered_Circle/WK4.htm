Dungeon Master:

The night passes without too much trouble, although the sounds of wolves can be heard in the distance. You break your fast and Karg leads you further into the foothills.

 

The day turns hotter with hardly a cloud in the sky, and you stop for a bite to eat around lunchtime. At about two hours past midday you see the Stone Circle as you peak one of the hills, from this distance it is a beautiful sight, but as you get closer you see that most of the stones are decayed and broken.

 

As you are almost on top of the stone circle you notice that although some columns are bent, and others lean against their companion stones in the thirty-foot-diameter circle, a chunk of white stone has actually fallen from its position upon the crown of the hill and slid downward some twenty feet. Though the upper portion is completely shattered, the square base forms a rough step about four feet high, five feet wide and five feet long.

Dolly:

As Mickey wakes her Dolly rolls over and grabs her axe, coming to her feet fast and ready. Shaking her head slightly at Mickey she says with a slight smile, waving her axe "Well I warned you not to wake me too fast - I nearly helped out with your morning shave then ....."

 

Nodding good morning to Yanish, Dolly carefully straps herself into her armour and goes about the watch duty.

 

If the others don't wake Rupert she will leave him asleep and carry the watch alone, making sure to wake him twenty minutes or so before the others.

 

When its time to wake the others she - will do so carefully, with the point of her boot, starting with Karg. (gently - not a kicking)

Lindornea'en:

Lin quietly watches the reactions of everyone throughout the trip. He doesn't really say anything unless spoken to... and for that matter, it'd depend on the question.

 

At the point they reach the stones, He takes the time to give them the once over, before piping up with, "This'd be the place that the Lost one disappeared from?"

Micky:

Upon seeing the Circle for the first time, Mickey pauses.  This is a place of great mystery and power, and it makes him feel small by comparison...

 

He follows the party down to the base of the hillside, then breaks from the group so he may thoroughly investigate the fallen stone 'step'.... (Observation Skill Check)

 

He then moves up the hillside to study the ring in even greater detail, noting the placement to the surrounding landscape and it's relation with the heavens above. (Observation Check, again)

 

"Well Lin', no one is really quite sure where the lost shepherd disappeared from, but this seemed the most probable starting point..."

 

The blonde half-elfs' words trail off as he continues to scrutinize the Circle of Cahervaniel for clues as to its reason for being here, as well as any hidden signs of the missing clergyman...

Karg:

Appearing much like a hibernating bear while asleep, Karg never the less comes immediately awake at Dolly's gentle prodding. Karg decides to stay up through the night and meets the dawn with clear eyes.

 

Leading the others onwards to the stone circle, he keeps a ready look about the countryside, not nervous or scared, but very watchful. At Lindornea'en's question, Karg growls out an answer without taking his attention off the surrounding lands. "Somewhere, Karg, Brothers, not know for sure."

Micky:

Mickey continues touching and investigating the Circle of ancient stones. He has heard so many stories and legends of this place, yet all were so conflicting that he often wondered if it were truly a place at all. He walks over to stand by the huge bear of a man, Karg.

 

"You, more than any of us here, would know the truth of this place. Tell  me Karg, what is the significance of this place to your people and your beliefs?"

 

He hopes to puzzle a little more of the history from the simple words of the barbarian priest, trying to understand the truth of Jarrods disappearance...

Yanish:

Yanish passes the day chatting in a soft tone with Mickey, who was so kind as to share his food and tasty wine. Since he expressed interest in her ability to walk up on the group. She spends the long hours of travel explaining games she played as a child, such as lost and found a hiding game. Though her favorite has always been climbing, which she demonstrates by scampering up a large tree. As her story and the journey come close to the end, a tear trickles down the side of her dirty face. The memories she has seem to hold more than just games and fun. Upon seeing the fallen stones she searches her memories for past tales and stories to see if she can recall any that might help explain the stones significant.

Lindornea'en:

Lin's searchings pull him up around the fallen stone. The elf gazes down at his findings... He stands back up, and calls, "Found a Possibility..." Anyone that comes over is greeted by Lin pointing down at a hole, then the Elf returning to gazing into the Darkness... trying to figure out anything specific about the area just out from the thing.

Dungeon Master:

Anyone going over to find out what Lin’s possibility is, finds a gap measuring five feet on a side remains where the ancient dolmen recently fell. The cavity is utterly dark, however, rounded steps are visible within the gap, plunging into the earth at a steep angle. Cobwebs thick with stone dust, smooth sharp corners, and the smell of earth, moisture, and rot is strong in your nostrils

Micky:

Seeing the hole almost at the same moment as Lin, Mickey stands close to the edge peering down the steep ancient stairs into the cold darkness far below. As the others gather to examine the elfs find, Mickey ribs him,

 

"Did you do this? You knocked the stone over, didn't you? These rocks are very old Lin, you can't just lean on them like that, they'll fall over..."

 

 He says it all in levity, trying to find some humour within the haughty elf as he looks to the 'step' lying at the base of the hillside. Of course, this is a serious matter, with grave implications for the missing shepherd and his flock.

 

"Seriously though Lin, you want to go down and look a little deeper with me? I'll even take point if you're too scared."

 

He smiles over at the stern faced elf, awaiting opinions and ideas from the rest of the group as well, especially the massive Karg, *does he know about this? do the legends of his tribe even mention such a staircase?*

Lindornea'en:

Lin laughs at the comment, for once releasing his frosty exterior, "Actually... I was banking on the Idea that you'd shown your true strength, and heaved it, unceremoniously, down the hill."

Gob:

The circle of stones is an impressive site and the diminutive Bard racks his memory for a scrap of poetry, A piece of lore or an old song but nothing comes to mind.

 

Taking a few minutes Gob pulls out a little roll of parchment and begins to sketch the stones taking pains to get the morning shadows right.

Karg:

At Micky's question, Karg looks confused, trying to figure out just what it is that Micky is asking. Finally, he shrugs. "Karg see this place before. This place be here always, Karg know no more. Karg people no sing of this place."

 

As the hole is found, Karg moves closer. He keeps clear of the hole as much as possible while still drawing near. As Lindornea'en jokes of great strength, Karg turns an appraising gaze upon the elf, wondering how his own strength might compare to the elfs. Pushing the thought aside, Karg's nostrils flare as he nears the hole. "Bad place." he announces as he points at the hole.

Lindornea'en:

Lin raises an eyebrow at Karg, "That'd be the most common idea of such a Place, I'd wager. And, as far as for scouting out the place, yes Mickey, that would be a good Idea... be we should keep our options open before blinding diving down into holes." He picks up a rock, and sends it spiraling down the stairs...

Dolly:

Dolly walks quietly with Rupert, enjoying the walking and watching Yanish at her childish antics. Occasionally she raises an eyebrow at her constant babbling and cavorting, but refrains from commenting.

 

Dolly hangs back a bit at the stone circle. There was no real reason to use this place to start the search - someone had plucked a name out of the air - and the rest had gone with the hunch. Fair enough, one place is good to start as another, but excited by a few stones and hole was, perhaps, over doing it.

 

Ambling across she listens to the discussion and the schoolboy ribbing between Lin and Mickey, waiting to see what they will get up to next. When the foolish Elf tosses a stone down the hole, she knows he really is a few coppers short of a silver. He talks about scouting, then destroys any chance of it by deliberately tossing the stone and making a racket.

 

Raising her eyebrows in disgust, Dolly comments, "Would you have been happier with a door knocker? Then you could announce, officially, that we are here too."

Dungeon Master:

The rock tumbles down the stairway, you hear it click, click clicking on the steps as it falls, then there is silence.

Micky:

Mickey looks back to Lin without hiding his expression of disappointment. True enough, it may have released any traps hidden in the stairs themselves, but what of the death often set above the normal step of a man? Even worse, what of the beasties in the dark that now know of our presence? He looks back to the hole, knowing that eventually somebody is going to have to go down there...

 

"I'm going in. Please, keep your voices low, maybe Lins' rock didn't wake anything up yet..."

 

He starts slowly down into the yawning tunnel, deliberately and slowly placing each foot he scans the walls, the ceiling, the way ahead for the traps of death. A small pile of pebbles scatter as he slips on a stair, his blue jerkin flashing from beneath his robes. Mickey is neither silent nor hidden, and unfortunately, not very experienced in finding the traps of the Ancients...      

Lindornea'en:

Lin considers telling Dolly to stuff it, but figures his time isn't worth the trouble. "Anything unfriendly would already be alerted by his Bellowing, and your Clomping. So, Just shut up, and wait." Without further words, the elf slides in alongside Mickey... Taking it slow, and remaining relatively quiet...

Rupert:

Standing next to his sister, Rupert just shakes his head. It seemed obvious to him that they were all going to be going down there eventually. Although he personally saw no problem with throwing the pebble down, he also saw Dolly's point of view.

 

..."Anything unfriendly would already be alerted by his Bellowing, and your Clomping," said the Elf.

 

"Or your big mouth," Rupert states softly, putting away the book in which he was sketching the area. He looks at Dolly. "If they don't get killed  right off, maybe we should follow, don't you think?" he asks.

Varistan:

Varistan remains silent through the day's travel and the searching of the Circle. Having spent most of his life among the streets of cities, he was feeling somewhat out of place in the wilderness. The spaces were too open, and the sounds too unfamiliar.

 

With the discovery of the hole, Varistan moves up with everyone else to investigate. Not one to be left out of the action, he draws his sword and follows Lin and Mickey down the stairs. He makes no real effort to move silently, considering such precautions pointless after Lin's stone toss and having made note of Mickey's accidental scatter of pebbles rattling down the steps.

 

However, he does make as meticulous a search of the stairway and descending tunnel as possible, looking for any sign of suspicious cracks, wires, scratches, or scuffs.

Dolly:

"Yus Sah!" grins Dolly at Lin's reply, the sarcasm clear in her voice.

 

Then turning to Rupert, "Yeah, I suppose so, no point them trying to scout now, is there?"

 

And advances towards the hole ...

Yanish:

Silently Yanish slips in behind Dolly, though any traps would more than likely already have been sprung, she still studies the ceiling and walls for anything that might cut her life’s thread. She thinks to her self "Either rock or elves charging down into the unknown should set off any nasties, but best to be careful." Her small form is quickly engulfed in shadows as she slips into the darkness.
