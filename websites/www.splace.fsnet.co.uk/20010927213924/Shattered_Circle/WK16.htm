Dungeon Master:

With the ale drunk dry, you fall like flies, one by one into bed. You are woken at daybreak, by chanting, and should any of you choose to investigate, you discover that this inharmonious chanting is coming from the chapel, where a congregation of the brothers are gathered for Morning Prayer.

 

Unable to sleep through the noise, despite the foggy heads of some, you make your way into the commons, where you are welcomed by the smell of freshly baked bread. After a little while the chapel empties, and most of the congregation join you in the commons. A few strange looks are thrown your way, but as the Abbott and Karg enter, they come over and join you at the table, and a short while later the table is laden with cheese, cold meat and fresh baked bread.

 

“So”, the Abbott starts, “have you all decided what you will do? Will you be joining Karg in returning to the Circle of Cahervaniel?”

Karg:

Rousing himself early, Karg makes ready for the day. Being one of the first of the brothers to reach the chapel, Karg joins in with the chanting enthusiastically. He'd given much thought to their plight and had sought out Shyeth's wisdom late into the night. The prayers helped to convince him that his choices left no taint between him and his god.

 

Afterwards, Karg meets up with the Abbott and follows him into the commons. Seeing the others there, some looking worse for the wear, he nods and smiles. He then sits down and begins to help himself to the food, again showing that his background has left him with little concern for table manners or the correct use of eating utensils.

 

He interrupts his repast as the Abbott asks the question. He looks over the others closely, wondering again if it's wise to put his trust in these people.

Aeriel:

Aeriel comes awake slowly, sitting up carefully to favour her injured wing. She removes the bandage and examines it, rearranging feathers, tenderly feeling the scars over the broken bones. The healers had assured her that it would be fine, but she worries still.

 

Standing in her small room, she spreads her wings, wincing as she feels the pull on the injured one. Soon, she thinks. Soon she may be able to test it on the winds.

 

But first... there are other considerations. Through the haze of early morning thoughts comes the knowledge that she has agreed to go with Karg... and other ground-dwellers... to go beneath the earth to right wrongs.

 

Kneeling in prayer, the Avariel calls on her goddess, asking for strength, asking for courage.

 

Then, dressing herself in her thin clothing, she hangs her short sword from her waist and adds her daggers. "Mayhap I shall be a help, not a hindrance," she tells herself as she leaves for the common dining area...

Lindornea'en:

Lin yawns and sits up... Eyes narrowing, and slight confusion upon his face, as he tries to work out where he is... Making a mental note to never again drink Human Alcohol, the Elf stands up, and throws on a shirt.

 

Having nothing better to do, he follows the procession to the 'food court’; sleep still hidden behind his blue eyes... along with the occasional yawn. He half-heartedly pokes at the food... taking his time in 'choking' down what was offered. The question almost takes him off guard, thought-processes re-awaken, as he works to think his feelings through... As yet, undecided upon the matter. He closes his eyes, and looks to... almost meditate...

Dolly:

Dolly wakes to the chanting, and rolls out of bed. Making sure her gear is still there, she goes to look for a bucket of water to pour over her head.

 

Heading off into the refectory she sits down and makes a hearty breakfast, pausing to greet the others as they arrive. She shows no symptoms of having a bad head.

 

At the Abbot's question she grunts "Sure. Why not?" almost before she has thought about it. Then shrugs her shoulders and gets on with her meal.

Aeriel:

Aeriel walks gingerly so to spare the injured wing she has unbandaged but feels no discomfort from it. Her normally braided white hair hangs down over and between her wings as she enters the dining area.

 

“...Have you all decided what you will do? Will you be joining Karg in returning to the Circle of Cahervaniel?” Aeriel hears as she sits carefully. She hesitates, then reaches for a bowl of porridge to cover up her momentary indecision. She thinks about this group- the Little One, the part bloods, the others... and Karg. She looks at him first, seeing the quiet determination in his face.

 

"In sooth, I cannot hide forever here," she says, looking down at her food. "The one that seeks me will seek me 'ere I stay here or go with thee. And thy path is one of good." Aeriel looks up at Karg. "As thou didst save me, then I can but follow thee. I did say the eve before, if all will have me, then I pledge to go on this quest. And may the Goddess bless us."

Mickey Donatelli:

Mickey opens his eyes, then quickly snaps them shut to block the daggers of light that seek to destroy his mind. He rubs his eyes, opens them a little slower this time, and looks down at his boot lying on the floor nearby...

 

* How did that get there? *

 

He sits up v-e-r-y slowly, banging his head on the upper bunk regardless. Rubbing both temples, he says little, and nearly falls from the bunk as he gropes blindly for his errant boot. Pulling it on, he stands a little too quickly, then finds himself clinging to the edge of the top bunk like a drowning man to a life preserver...

 

After a few minutes of death-nausea have washed through him, he struggles on the boot, and then slowly stagger steps his way to the common room...

 

* Oh God, it's even brighter in here..."

 

He finds a place at the table, and nearly hurls again at the sight of all the food. Ignoring painful protests in his lower gut, he forms a small carbo-load of sandwiches from the bread and meats, and then dutifully chokes them down with a flagon of WATER...

 

He hears the yelling monk, then looks around to the others wondering how they can handle such noise so early in the morning. He sets his meal aside, undoes the knots on his money pouch, and pulls out two gold coins. Without a word, he slides these across the table to Yanish, and offers a lopsided and painful smile as he whispers, "...for you Yanish, to cover my ill chosen bet..."

 

He looks over at Aeriel, who is just responding to the Abbots question, and holds the brief and broken memory of an angel looking down on him in disgust. His face is puzzled, was it her? He chews thoughtfully and deliberately, the meal more of a function of recovery than any real pleasure. He swallows, chases it with MORE WATER, then answers in turn,

 

"I already said I was going before we ever left the place, they’re expecting me...yeah, I'm still going, even in my current condition...hopefully it'll keep me out of trouble..."

 

He runs his hand through his tangled blonde hair as he begins chewing another small sandwich, and smiles over at Dolly and her brother. Reaching into one pocket, he pulls out a tiny vial...

 

"Hey Rupe, I forgot to ask you last night, you know what this is? I sure don't, why don't you keep it until you can figure it out..."

 

He hands the Vial he found under the Circle across the table to the bookworm, waving it just under his nose trying to get his attention away from the latest publication he can't seem to put down...

Rupert:

"Hmmm?" Rupert looks up from his book, surprised. Of all there, he had slept completely and without incident. He can't help but smile as his companions come in one by one, especially the half-elf who had had so much to drink the night before.

 

He takes the vial and holds it to the light, settling his spectacles firmly on his nose. With a frown of concentration, he uncorks it and mells it, then holds it up again.

 

Corking the vial, he reaches into his coat and removes... a small fan... then an apple core... then finally yet another small book. He flips through the pages, reads, then smiles.

 

"Although it is difficult to be fully sure," he states with authority beyond his years, "I would guess that this is a common yet valuable potion that one would inbibe to heal wounds. Quite valuable," he adds, handing it carefully back to Mickey.

With a growl she re-sheaths it and pulls the pillow over her head hoping for at least a few more hours sleep. The pillow does little to muffle the sound and Mickey slamming into the bed helps even less. With wild hair and a temper to match she says to no one and yet all. "If we are to travel to day back to that pit, wouldn't a few more hours sleep in a warm bed seem a better course than banging about and what this howling nonsense I hear?" Not waiting for an answer she stomps down the halls toward the dinning hall. Pulling herself up into a chair she grumbles about the lack of chairs in this world meant for her kind. When Mickey passes the two gold coins her way Yanish looks offended. "If I had wanted the coin I would have retrieved them. The ill placed bet was not yours but my own." She does however drop the gold into her bag saying, "When we are done with this quest and both of us still live, I shall buy you and I a drink and meal with this coin fit for a king. So don’t do anything dumb and get yourself killed lest you wish to loss all your coin." When the abbot inquires about returning to the pit Yanish now in a better mood answers "With brave men such as these before me I should have nothing to fear." Her tone seems to hold a bit of jest in the sentence.

Mickey takes the vial back from his scholarly friend and says in a whisper, "Yes, but will it cure a hangover?" He smiles, showing that he knows better, then pockets the vial. Lets' hope we don't have to use it anytime soon...

 

As Yanish tells him the bet was her loss, he looks confused. The memories are broken and fuzzy, so he just shrugs as she tells of her offer to keep the coins until such time she can buy the two of us dinner..."

 

If you're buying dinner Yanish, that’s reason enough to survive a journey through hell. My Da' always said -Never pass up a free meal..."

 

Even as he says this to his little friend, he packs two more of the little sandwiches away in a cloth from his pack. No telling when we'll get lunch - is this a union operation?

Aeriel:

The winged elf looks around, having announced her decision. She smiles at Yanish, recognizing the humor in her comments. She then looks over at Mickey, her expression more concerned.

 

"Art thou feeling better this morning?" she asks, pausing to take another bite of her food. "I hope thou dost not hold against me mine own comments to thee last night." She looks embarrassed. "I fear I have not seen much of how thy kind does find amusement and thy state did cause me to make some unkind comments. Wilt thou forgive me?"

Karg pats Aeriel on the shoulder as she pledges herself to the mission and gives her a nod of approval. His eyes move from face to face as others speak of their intention to return as well. He turns his attention to Varistan and Lindornea'en. "Varistan, Lindornea'en, stay or go?"

Dungeon Master:

The Abbott nods at Rupert's request to stay at the monastery and is pleased to hear that the rest of you will accompany Karg back to the Circle of Cahervaniel. He excuses himself from the table and heads off into the kitchen, leaving you all to finish breaking your fast.

 

A few minutes later, the Abbott comes back to the table carrying 7 small packages, he hands one to each of you before saying, “I am not sure how long you will be gone, but in these packages you will find 2 days worth of food rations. I will meet you in the main entrance in 15 minutes to see you off”.

 

You all finish up, collect your equipment, and head to the entrance hall where the Abbott is waiting for you. He hands a small jar of what looks to you like flour to Karg and says, “you will know when this is required” before addressing the rest of you. “I wish you all the best in your travels my friends, please take care of yourselves, and I hope to see you all returned safely to us shortly”. To Karg (and out of earshot of Aeriel) he says, “please look after our winged friend, I feel the path ahead is going to be difficult for her”, he finishes by saying to you all, “I have spoken to Jarrod this morning, and he sends his well wishes, and would like me to pass on his thanks to all of you for returning him safely to us”. With this he opens the door and bids you all farewell.

Karg:

When the Abbott hands Karg one of the small packages, Karg nods and places it in his belt pouch. Having few trapping to contend with, Karg is at the main entrance a bit ahead of the others. He spends the time tasting the wind and studying the sun, still early for some, but not Karg. As the rest appear, including the Abbott, Karg takes the jar without question. Karg wasn't sure what it was or what it was far, by Shyeth had taught him to have faith in the man.

 

Stepping aside to share a few private words with the Abbott, listening intently to his words about Aeriel. Pulling a knife from beneath the hide armour, Karg's eyes lock on the Abbott’s. With unwavering gaze, the knife slides along Karg’s left forearm, leaving a trail of welling blood. The pain is as nothing beside the intensity in the barbarian’s eyes. "Karg protect Aeriel until blood of Karg go cold." In a gruesome rite of his former life, Karg licks the blade clean before sheathing it again.
