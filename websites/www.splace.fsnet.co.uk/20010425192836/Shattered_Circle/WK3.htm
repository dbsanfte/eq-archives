WK3 A Night in the Foothills

Dungeon Master:

The day passes by without any major incidences; you break briefly for lunch in a shady copse that gives you a break from the afternoon sun. As the sun starts to fade, Karg has found a circle of rocks for you to camp in, which will assist in keeping out the chill of the foothills.

 

Although wood is sparse you find enough to light a small fire, and to keep it going through the night.

Varistan:

Varistan slumps down by the fire and breaks out his food. He eats sparingly, saving as much of it as he can, and between bites mumbles with his mouth full, "I'll be takin' first watch. It suits my reg'lar sleepin' habits."

 

He sighs dramatically. "Too bad it slipped my mind to grab some whiskey back at Dagger Rock. Would have been a nice way to spend my wakin' hours durin' the night."

 

With that said, and hurriedly cramming a last bite into his mouth, he moves to one of rocks, deposits his pack at its base, and deftly hops up onto the top of the rock. He sits, managing to look quite comfortable, and scans the area as far as he can see.

Dolly:

"Shall we do last watch Rupe?" asks Dolly. She follows up with "Who's getting the fire going and doing the cooking?" to the group generally.

Yanish:

Yanish watches as the group begins to make camp. With the smell of a fire and possibility of missing a meal, her stomach rumbles. This helps her decide now would be a good time to make her presence known. Not making any attempt to conceal her movement or sound, she begins to whistle a tune from her youth. Thinking "Best to be on the safe side with this group, would be a shame to die by some cow herders hand just because he was startled." Reaching into her cloak, she pulls the letter out and begins to walk into the fires light.

Karg:

Karg finds a campsite for the group and starts a meagre fire. Enjoying a bit of dinner, he settle with his back to one of the stones, his keen eyes keep a close watch on most everything. "Karg watch after scarred one." He settles back and pulls his hood low over his face, but there's a strange feeling of alertness about him. It's almost as though he's still watching from the dark recesses of the hood.

 

Suddenly, he lurches forward, hood thrown back and resting on his haunches. His nostrils flare about the same time a faint whistling can be heard. His hand drops to the grip of his huge club. He faces off to his right in time to see a little halfling lass enter the fires light. He snarls slightly, then snorts. In a rough bear voice, he asks, "You be who?"

Varistan:

Varistan crouches atop his stone perch, bow drawn and arrow targeted on the halfling woman.

 

"Answer quick now, little maiden, afore I punch this arrow through your pretty little head."

Yanish:

As the large oaf jumps up from his rest, and then another draws a bead on her with his bow, Yanish again thinks to herself "Perhaps joining this mission was a bad idea." Out loud she says "Oh my, all of this show for one as small and harmless as myself. I would hate to think how jumpy you would be if I were a real threat. Like a kobold, probably make you wet yourselves." She finishes the statement with a grim chuckle. To the group in general she says, "As to who I am, the names Yanish. However the real question should have been why am I here. Which of you is Karg? I have a letter from Abbot Jengor which should answer any further question.” She holds the letter up to show the Abbot’s seal. However she doesn’t move any closer, just in case the need should arise for a quick departure.

Varistan:

Laughing at Yanish's insult, Varistan relaxes the pull on his bow. "The little filly's got a mouth on her, that's for sure.

 

I like her." He flops back down into a sitting position. "I say we keep her."

Lindornea'en:

Lin remains sitting, leaning against a tree... Basically ignoring the whole thing. With Varistan's comment, however, he feels the need to retort, "Who said anyone had a vote in this matter?"

Varistan:

Varistan laughs again, this time at Lin's comment. "Jus' a figger o' speech, Lin. Don't go losin' your famous superior Elven cool over it. Yeah wouldn't want to bring yourself down to the level o' the likes o' me, now would ya, Elf?"

 

He accentuates the last word, rolling it off his tongue like a vulgarity, and barks a curt laugh.

Micky:

Mickey has said little through the afternoon, leaving the bully banter to Dolly and Varistan. His earlier admiration for the Elf has long since been replaced by more bitter feelings made possible by the the mans attitude.  Perhaps Father was right, a gentle Elf like Mother is a rare find indeed...

 

He sits up quickly with his hand on the sword hilt as the small lady whistles her way into our campsite. She is accepted soon enough, even with the mundane protests of the haughty Elf.

 

Varistan has taking a liking to her, and as Mickey sits on the grass, he finds himself feeling the same.

 

"Welcome to our camp Yanish." seeing the letter still held in her hand, he is quick to notice that it does indeed bear the seal of the Abbot.

 

"The big furry one over there is Karg - the one that looks more like a bear than a man. After you give him he letter, feel free to come join me for a glass of wine and bit of supper. Oh, and ignore Lin, he's like that with everyone..."

 

Mickey only glances towards the Elf before returning to his place by the fire. He pulls a wineskin from his pack and takes a short draw.

 

"Wake me for third watch, would you Dolly?"

 

He smiles at the ugly girl, her tenderness as Rupert's sister having shown a greater beauty within. He then pulls forth some dried meats and unleavened bread from his pack and begins preparing a small meal...

Rupert:

Rupert agrees with his sister to do the last watch and generally is unhelpful in setting up camp. He seems to have no skills whatsoever in such matters. Indeed, he is more in the way than he would be if he simply stayed out of such matters.

 

Rebuffed and out of his element, he settles himself to read.

 

His reading though is interrupted by the appearance of the little woman. He hears the calls and counter calls, the agreements and disagreements. He doesn't speak up until the Elf voices his opinions.

 

"If such a small girl had wanted to do us harm, would she approach boldly or slit our throats as we sleep," he calls out. "Or are we so afraid that we cannot learn to trust?" That last seems aimed at the Elf.

Dolly:

Dolly watches the halfling approach, hand not far from her axe, but relaxes at the sight of the letter. She listens to the banter between Lin and Varistan but decides to leave them to it this time, and settles down to pull a small sack from her backpack.

 

Withdrawing a square of canvas some needles and a number of fine threads, she proceeds to thread a blue silk into a needle and start work embroidering a part finished flower.

 

When Mickey suggests that she wakes him for the third watch she responds, "Not me, I'm on last watch with Rupe. I think Karg said he was doing second watch. You get to wake me up instead." and she smiles wickedly, "Just don't wake me too early, lover boy. Nice and slow, with a mug of ale, would be good." before she returns to her embroidery.

 

She looks up sharply and aware at Rupert's comments to Lin.

Lindornea'en:

Lin rolls his eyes, "Has society befallen some great tragedy that everyone must either mistake, or twist the words from my mouth?" He looks to Varistan, "First off, only a fool would take such as an Insult... by Calling me Elf, you only reaffirm what I am. That does not bother me, nor add a verbal attack in any way." His attention swaps to Rupert, "And Second, Boy, I was talking of Karg's status as the Leader... It seems Abbot Jengor appointed him for such a purpose. I said nothing about Trust, but I can talk of Fools, if You'd like me to."

Yanish:

Yanish feels the tense moment pass as most of the odd collection of races replaces their weapons and resume making camp. Focusing on the large brute that was pointed out as Karg, Yanish slowly walks toward the hulking human. For a fleeting moment she wonders if the creature can even read. Not being very good herself, she decides to continue walking toward him hoping he will at least recognize the seal. When one of the half elves offers a glass of wine, her eyes never leave Karg, but to the elf she says in a husky voice "Throw in some of that meat and bread and you have a deal love. As for the Elf's nasty attitude, I have seen their lot before. All high and mighty because of their long lives, but a blade can cut that life thread just as easily an quickly as any other mans. Keep your nose out of the clouds elf, it might not help you in your quest to be rude all those long lonely years, but at least you might live to see them."

Dolly:

Dolly looks up somewhat sharply at Lin's comment, but then continues with her embroidery....  

Karg:

Karg moves forward and takes the letter from Yanish. He holds it very close to his face and you hear a snuffling sound, almost as though he's smelling the document. After a moment, he rolls it up, "You hunt Jarrod with us?"

Rupert:

"I said nothing about Trust, but I can talk of Fools, if You'd like me to."

 

Rupert represses a smile as best he can, then gives up. "Please do talk of fools, Sir Lin," he retorts. "You seem to have more experience in that than the rest of us."

 

He looks over at Karg, almost dismissing the subject as he pushes the glasses back up on his nose. "Can you really read that, dear fellow, or should I assist you?"

Karg:

"You want read note, you read for all ears." Karg passes the paper over to the elfling.

Yanish:

Yanish is a little unsettled as the large human takes the letter and begins sniffing it. However she quickly puts all thoughts of leaving out of her head and replies "Yes I come to hunt Jarrod with you." She waits patiently hands on hips for the letter to be passed and red aloud.

Lindornea'en:

Lin shakes his head at the comments that were returned. Figuring it's not worth the trouble to reply, he goes back to his lazing against the tree...

Broki:

Following along behind everyone, Broki seems got up in the idea of making a ballad for his new kingdom, and doesn't even seem to notice the childish fighting amongst the others.

 

As they settle down for the night, Broki returns to the real world and begins to notice the comments and barbs being exchanged by the others.

 

But before he can say anything, Karg leaps up, and then he hears the whistling and grabs his weapon. As Yanish enters camp and hands over the letter, Broki relaxes.

 

"Welcome, lass. I hope you bring a little more maturity to this group. These children 'ave been arguing over some o' the dumbest things since we stopped to make camp. It's enough to make ye pull yer beard out!" Broki says with a welcoming smile for the girl.

 

Giving the others a hard look, Broki announces,

 

"Seems to me the abbot sent her, so she's one of us. Now, ya'll should get to sleep, as I'll be waking ye up in a bit fer yer watches."

 

With another look around at everyone, Broki grabs his weapons and some food to snack on, and heads out to the edge of the camp for his watch. He will look for a large boulder or such to sit on.

Rupert:

Rupert takes the letter and examines it, allowing his glasses to slide down his nose. It serves to make him seem somewhat older than his teen years. He breaks the seal carefully, then clears his throat.

 

"Its addressed to you, Karg," he says formally. "It says, 'Please allow the halfling female,' I assume that's her, 'to accompany you on the quest to bring back brother Jarrod. Though her size is small, I believe her knowledge of the surrounding area and other skills many be of some use in this endevor.'"

 

Rupert grins, then continues. "'Keep an eye on her though. I don't believe she is dangerous but your purse might end up lighter if you aren't wary.'" He giggles. "Then it says 'Good luck and God speed."

 

He shrugs, handing the letter back to the little woman. "It would seem you are to travel with us," he concludes, still grinning.

Micky:

Mickey makes a second 'sandwich' and walks over to the cute little halfling who has now Officially joined our group. He hands her the small meal, and begins chewing his own. Hearing the reply from Dolly, he shudders involuntarily.

 

Distress crosses his tender young face, whether from the fear of being woken by Karg (a kick in the head?), or the terror of waking Dolly (do I have to kiss her?), it's hard to tell, but he looks uneasy.

 

Through a mouthful of food, he replies,

 

"...mmph...wha? Oh yes, Karg has second watch - of course. Umm, I'll just stay up. I'll wake you when it's time Dolly. Unfortunately, I have no ale, you'll have to provide your own."

 

He turns back to Yanish, smiling as he wipes his mouth. He prides himself on his 'trail-sandwiches', each one made with a tiny dollop of Mothers special mustard paste...

Dolly:

"Awwww" says Dolly in response to Mickey's response, but with a bit of a grin on her face. She embroiders carefully and slowly, taking occasional bites from her rations.

 

When the light gets too bad for embroidery, she will pack it away and draw out a blanket. Removing her armoured she will wrap the blanket around her and settle down for the evening. Her armour is stashed along side the pack, which she uses as a pillow. Her axe is by her side, ready to hand.

Lindornea'en:

Lin sighs, letting the others make nice. The elf says, "Wake me for third watch, also." Before 'dozing' off against the tree... his cloak acting as the only padding between himself and the bark.

Yanish:

Yanish's face colors a bit as the last of the letter is red. Quickly shrugging it off she says in a light crisp tone, "The abbot was probably referring to my skill with the dice, however he must have meant common folk unlike the smart, brave warriors I see before me. May haps along the way one of you would mind playing a lady at cups or dice." When the elf hands the meal he made to her, she says with a voice thick as honey and just as seductive "Why thank you, its good to see both kindness and trust in these hard times by the way my young knight I never caught your name." Shooting a ice filled scowl at the elf laying against the tree she says "Some here could learn from your kindness. As for which watch I hear everyone speaking of, I my self am not tired as of yet and would like to stay up with whom ever is on first watch." That said she finds a spot near the kind half elf and quietly engages in quite small talk.

Gob:

Sitting off by himself Gob keeps a quiet eye on the goings on around him.

 

Keeping up a quiet tune as he practices little bits and pieces and generally just tuning out and enjoying the rest.

Micky:

Mickey smiles as Yanish expresses her opinion of Lins attitude. Good, she already is one with the majority. He takes another swallow from his wineskin, then politely passes the skin to the tiny halfling.

 

"Mickey Donatelli, but you can call me mouse. As far as the watch schedule goes, let me see if I've got this now... Varistan, then Karg, then myself and Lin... followed by Rupert and Dolly, who likes a glass of ale for breakfast!

 

Looking to the half-orc, he smiles confidently,

 

"Got it right that time love?"

 

Turning back to Yanish, he bows and holds forth his hand,

 

"May I say that it is both a pleasure and an honour to be travelling with one of such talent. All day with Lins' incessant noise it was little wonder we couldn't hear you, but still, your approach to camp was done with such finesse! You had Varistan on his feet only after you stood by our fire!"

 

Sensing he is somewhat exaggerating, and rambling on and on about quite alot of nothing, he flushes beet-red,

 

"...that is to say...umm, I'm a bit of a Security Specialist myself, perhaps we could compare technique. I mean to say - ummm..."

 

He quickly takes a large bite of his sandwich, his mouth now too full to fit any more foolish words or boot leather...

Dolly:

"Yep that's about it, I think" Dolly smile "Just remember not to wake me too fast" says she licking her lips suggestively.
