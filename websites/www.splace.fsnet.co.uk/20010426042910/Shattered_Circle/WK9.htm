Dungeon Master:

You pick up the oddments that you find in the rooms and discovering there is no other exit you make your way back into the main room.

 

Avoiding the body of the creature and the pit trap you make your way over to the first door. As Varistan opens it slightly, a plume of smoke seeps into the room. Opening it a little more you see that the oil has burnt and the flames have died, but there seems to be no dint in the webbing other than the sheen of black where smoke has turned it off colour.

Lindornea'en:

Lin takes particular note that no-one else is afflicted with anything bad, before picking up the last Longsword, and spinning it into his back-sheath. The elf keeps his original sword out, though... He deems it unimportant enough to comment on Varistan's attitude, however. But, the elf has no problems at all letting the two half-breeds act as mine detectors in the hallway... [*Grins* Momma didn't raise no fools now... ;)] 

Yanish:

With a seductive glimmer in her eyes, Yanish turns to face Varistan saying; "You were so very brave to go charging into that last room, perhaps your bravery and knowledge of tactics could lead us down this next hall way. My strong knight. I will follow close behind you so as to stay protected by your might." 

Varistan:

Varistan looks down at Yanish, a look of honest perplexity on his face. Two women in two days making doe-eyes and fawning over him? True, Dolly had seemed to lose all interest in him, but still... Something wasn't adding up.

 

Shrugging his shoulders, Varistan turns back to the door and squats down, looking across the floor. Seeing nothing out of the ordinary, other than the strange blackened webs, he rises, nods to Mickey, and moves down the hallway. He makes no real attempt to keep to the shadows or move quietly, figuring that anything here that hasn't been disturbed yet isn't going to be disturbed by him walking. Besides, if something is disturbed and comes for him, little Yanish will be that more likely to go unnoticed and possibly be able to strike a killing blow.

Yanish:

Yanish follows Varistan down the web hall way, she scans the floor, walls and ceiling for any traps that may still lay in wait.        

Dungeon Master:

There is nothing of any interest down the corridor, and you hear no unusual sounds ... the web looms in front of you!

Varistan:

Varistan eyes the web quizzically, and tentatively touches it with the tip of his sword.

Dungeon Master:

The sword sticks to the web, but with little strength you manage to pull it free

Karg:

Karg gives Lindornea'en a harsh look, but holds his temper in check. Apparently, the elf's lack of response to Karg's question shows that he regrets insulting Karg by calling him a lout. At least, Karg thinks it is an insult, whatever it's actual meaning. Doing his best to dismiss such thoughts, Karg watches idly as the others poke and prod the weapons. Karg would have none of it, evil place and evil creatures make for evil weapons. Looking almost bored, Karg follows Varistan and Yanish into the corridor with the web, not granting Lindornea'en any more of his attention.

Rupert:

Rupert reaches for his spectacles (again!) and examines the web. "Hmmm. Perhaps a very large spider? Cobwebs wouldn't grab as easily, I might surmise." He reaches into his pocket and pulls out... a handkerchief. He reaches into another pocket and pulls out... his book. Opening the book, he reads a moment.

 

"Fire. Fire should do it. Does anyone have fire or should I produce some?" 

Dolly:

“Err Rupe, We already tried that. Perhaps something else might work?" she say gently

Micky:

Mickey stands nearby in the North hall watching all of this uneasily. The 'web' behaves like that of a sorcerer, yet it does not burn. Flame proofed? In any case, it has to magical in nature, for there are no spiders (yet) around to claim Varistan's sword...

 

"Hey Rupert, can you tell if this 'web' is enchanted? Even better, if it is, can you undo it?..."

 

He waits for the bookworms answer, then offers another suggestion regardless.

 

"I also have a key I found in the last chamber. It might fit the lock in the dragons mouth above the East door...you know, the one that had the bad head cold?"

 

Mickey fails to mention the third choice of the southern door. Having made that one unapproachable when he sprung the pit, he doesn't want to deal with those mistakes just yet...

Rupert:

Rupert blinks at his sister's soft rebuke. How had he missed that? "Hmmm. Pity, that," he responds. "I have magical fires but they function as mundane ones and would not likely have effect either. No, I fear I have neither the means to detect its true magical nature nor to dispel it. That would require one of more training than I. A pretty puzzle indeed."

 

The half-elf looks in surprise at Mickey. "Why, such an idea! Perhaps that makes sense. The path that is locked versus the path that leads to death. I for one like that idea. Mayhap we should try your key and see if it leads to more beneficent situations?"

Dungeon Master:

The blade slices cleanly through the first foot of webbing, and it falls to the wall, opening a path through.

Dolly:

Dolly looks across as the blade slices through the webbing, and sees that there is yet more. Roughly she comments, "By Varen's titties, how much more of the stuff is there?"

Varistan:

"I hope Varen don't have huge titties" says Varistan, stealing a look at Dolly's chest and shaking his head in disbelief. He then proceeds to hack his way through the webs.

Lindornea'en:

[Heheh...]

Lin comments, "Far too much. There is definitely something that don't want to meet in there... Little spiders don't do that much work. Even a Colony." He points back towards the 'Armoury', "I'd guess that unless it was constructed by our friend, that's decaying in the dark back there, We have much, much more nasty things within."

 

[Master of the Obvious.]

The elf pauses, thinking along those lines, "I wonder if our missing brother was caught by the Feuyaerea (Disgusting Ones)... and brought through here." His eyes slide down to the stone floor, which only elicits an annoyed sigh, "How I hate stone."

Dungeon Master:

Varistan manages to slice cleanly through another foot before moving forward and attempting the third. He swings his blade, but this time instead of cutting through the webbing, it tangles and will not budge.

Dolly:

Dolly looks down at her own chest and grins - "Apparently they were huge ...." she comments quietly, "According to the legend she fed a whole village through a winter of fasting ..."

Yanish:

Yanish looks down at her own chest, perhaps not as large as Dolly's but in relation to her size quite supple. Shrugging off the thought of nursing a village, Yanish steps silently forward and passes one of the oil flasks to Varistan. In a quite voice she says, "Place but a few drops on your blade and light it. As soon as it comes free smother the flames, to much heat can ruin a blade plus the light will ruin an night vision." Stepping back a few paces she waits for Varistan to free his weapon and hand her remaining oil back.

Karg:

Karg stumbles along in the background, listening to the bantering of the others. "Karg no know Varen. From sound of it, Karg want know Varen." He grins a bit.

Varistan:

Neglecting to say thank you, Varistan takes the oil from Yanish and follows her instructions, attempting to pull his sword free from the web. A grin tugs at his lips at Karg's assessment of this well-endowed Varen.

Lindornea'en:

Lin comments, "Why am I not surprised."    

Dolly:

"Nearly as good over here, Hon." calls Dolly in response to Karg's comment

Dungeon Master:

You watch as Varistan once more attempts to put flame to the webbing. With Yanish kindly holding the sword hilt, Varistan carefully puts a couple of drops on the blade, avoiding contact with the webbing. Handing the vial back to Yanish he removes his flint and steel, and the small spark catches quickly.

 

Without much thought he smothers the flame with his cloak as Yanish tries to pull the sword free …. All to no avail!

Yanish:

Yanish shrugs, as the flames have no affect on the web. She says to Varistan "Possibly brute strength might work where flames do not, maybe you could try some of your charm to persuade Karg to pull the blade free." That said, she tucks the remaining oil back in her cloak and moves out of the way just in case Karg decides to help out.

Varistan:

Feeling a bit frustrated, Varistan yells "Karg! Pull!" and points to his stuck sword.

Karg:

Karg glances at the youthful seeming woman, surprised at the invitation. The women of his people are tall, lithe and strong, not short and dumpy like this one. No, entirely too dwarf-like for his tastes. He makes a show of having to look way down to check her out. "Dolly too short, Dolly need find dwarf-man."

 

As Varistan yells out his order, Karg looks mildly offended at having demands shouted out at him. "Varistan move away, Karg try." He orders back, believing in giving as best as he gets. "Varistan hold club, no stick club in web." He adds. Gripping the hilt of the sword in both hands, Karg pulls. Having little luck, he redoubles his efforts, and redoubles them again. His arms strain with the effort, his face red and his breath husky, Karg gives a beastial yell and gives the blade one last, ultimate effort......

Dolly:

"Oh Yeah? And where do I find one of them, big boy?" Dolly replies to Karg, not in the least offended.

Dungeon Master:

Karg gives a beastial yell and gives the blade one last, ultimate effort …

 

… and the blade comes free. Karg stumbles back with it, but manages to keep his footing and avoid the webbing. Thankfully no one was silly enough to stand immediately behind him!

Yanish:

Yanish walks over to Mickey, "Well big boy now that Varistan has gotten unstuck, what were you saying about a key?"

Varistan:

Exchanging Karg's club for his sword, the only thanks he gives a brief nod, Varistan stubbornly turns back to the web and again attempts to cut his way through.

Dungeon Master:

Varistan manages to cut through the last couple of feet of webbing, which falls to the wall leaving a passage through. Once through to the other side there is a loud screech as a couple of things come hurtling towards you, one carrying a sword the other a javelin. Fortunately for Varistan, they misjudge, and miss him.

Varistan:

Varistan takes a step back in surprise as the two creatures run at him, causing his wildly swinging sword to fall short as he sweeps his weapon at each in turn.

Dolly:

Dolly pushes her way to the front and tries to bat the attacking creatures away with the flat of her Axe

 

"We have come in search of one of our own" - she shouts - "We don't want to hurt you, but we will if we have to ..."

Dungeon Master:

The creatures hesitate, looking at each other, then back to Dolly with a quizzical look on their faces ...

Karg:

"Karg no know where find dwarf-man. Karg look, let Dolly know Karg find one."

 

As the blade suddenly comes free, Karg staggers back, only barely managing to keep from falling over with the effort. Karg swipes the sword for his club. He keeps his face impassive at Varistan's curt acknowledgement of the feat, but has lips twitch as Varistan goes about cutting the webbing again. Not a man to learn lessons easily. Still, Karg is surprised at Varistan's success with the webbing. Getting Varistan's attention, Karg gives him a curt nod to reward his efforts.

 

At the screech, Karg turns his attention to the fore, his hands tightening on the handle of his club. What with Varistan in the way and the webbing above, Karg brings the club up in an undercut swing, but he misses widely.

Micky:

Mickey had been daydreaming of experiences yet unknown to a man of his tender years, when Yanish surprises him with her question about the key. Blushing in embarrassment,(*Does she know what I was thinking?*) he begins to stammer,

 

“...umm, yeah, a key. I found it..."

 

Before he can respond completely, the webs are broken and Karg and Varistan attacked. Even as Dolly ducks through, Mickey steps back and works his way back to the safety of the rear flanks. He will stand close to Rupert, 'protecting' the Mage while his sister attempts to negotiate with the enemy(?)...

Yanish:

Slipping one of the skull daggers from her cloak, Yanish readies if for throwing just in case the negotiation between Dolly and the new creatures goes bad.

Varistan:

Varistan, surprised at Dolly's halting of the fight, shares the creatures' quizzical expressions as he looks at her.

Lindornea'en:

Lin is half-surprised to see Dolly more interested in parlay than making war. Something he was pretty sure was a natural 'Fighter' trait... the surprised look on his face doesn't last but a moment, before composure is regained. He says, in his own annoying way, "Interesting Idea... now where are you going to take this tree, you've unearthed? I don't suppose your brother has a magic to make the negotiations easier?" He glances back at the half-elf, letting his question speak louder than any commands he could have issued.

Dolly:

"What bloody tree?" responds Dolly never taking her eyes from the things that had been attacking.

Lindornea'en:

Lin sighs, "I guess in dungeons, no one can hear you make metaphors... I _said_ Now that you have their attention, what are you going to do with it..."

Rupert:

Rupert goes quickly from being acutely embarrassed at the constant breastial (sic) references to the appearance of the creatures. As usual, he frowns at the Elf's absurd comments but he figures that he best ignores them as long as Dolly chooses to do so.

 

Which brings him to Dolly. Just what the heck is she doing?

 

Stepping forward as best as possible, he chooses to answer all. "I've no magic to help negotiate, but I could choose to use methods that would aid in surrender should our opponents fail to see the wisdom of my sister's words." He removes his spectacles and regards the creatures. "So. What say you to my dear sister, creatures unknown?"

Dolly:

Dolly shrugs "Just seemed to me that if someone had hacked their way through my wall, I would feel aggrieved too. But talking ain't my bag - better get motor mouth over there to have ago" gesturing behind her to Lin, "Maybe they know where his tree is." There is a funny edge to her tone as she says the last bit.

Lindornea'en:

Lin rolls his eyes, and sighs disgustedly. He mutters, "Barbarians" to himself, and motions towards the two stunned 'Whatsits', as if she should be getting back to business...

Dolly:

As Lin is behind Dolly, she doesn't see his look or signs. So she continues to watch the 'things' ignoring him completely.
