Celestial Rising are a mature guild of players we will only accept people under the age of 18 if their maturity has been proven to us,. We will accept new members from level 10 upwards.  All potential members must read and agree to the following :

 

GUILD RULES

Recruitment

While we accept members from level 10 upwards we are in the main a mid/high level guild.
 

Once invited into the guild you will be placed on a probationary period of 1 month, this is to ensure that new members fit the Guild and equally that the Guild fits new members.  Probationers rules are as follows :

 

Probation member's details will appear on the members table with the title of PROBATIONER

Once invited, the new member is expected to group as often as possible with other CR members

Probationers should not play in /anon or /role modes.  This is to enable members to see where everybody is

 

(Members' alts are obviously unaffected by this probation period)

 

As a new member you are obligated to:

 

Register an account on EZBoard  - For communication (please make your EZBoard registered user name the same as your primary EQ character).  All guild information will be published on the Message boards, and you are encouraged to participate on the boards. 

Register a Magelo Account - Keeping an up to date Magelo account enables us to see what equipment you are in need of for planning future raids and assigning loot (see raid loot rules)

 

Rules of Conduct

 

This section could just be a reference to the EverQuest site.  In short

 

Don't train people on purpose

Don't KS

Don't give yourself and Celestial Rising a bad name with rude behaviour in trades, OOC, shouts or public chat channels

Don't bicker about camp sites, but state your right

Don't let your first action be to /petition and call a GM if any of the Rules of Conduct are broken by others causing harassment to you.  Seek diplomacy, call in friends or Guild Officers, then as a last resort /petition

Many, many problems can be solved by the /ignore command

 

Guild Chat

The guild chat is our common line of communication when online. Here is how we can use it:

 

SOCIAL:  Chat and joke, any normal socialising

HELP:  Request help for ports, price checks.  We are here to help each other.  However DO NOT see this as a service which you at any time can request and expect delivered

IGNORE:  You are of course allowed to turn off guild chat if you are in a place where you do not need disturbance.  Expect people to do this on raids

RAIDS:  The guild say may be 'borrowed" by a majority of people on a raid where fast communication is needed.  If so, please try and keep socialising and jokes to a minimum

SILENCE:  The Guild Leader or any Officer can call "Guild say SILENCE".  This basically means that information necessary for us all to hear are being relayed and just for a short period we need not to be interrupted

MOTD:  The guild MOTD is displayed each time you log onto a character that is a CD member.  It will often contain important information that the whole guild needs to know so please read this

 

The Message Boards

You are obliged to read the boards.  This is our primary means of communication!  To do this, you must make an EZBoard account using your primary EQ character name as your EZBoard login name.  For access to member protected areas, post your name under the 'New Applications & Access Requests' topic and state that you want in.  These boards are password protected and an officer will arrange for access to be given.

On the boards, use normal rules of conduct.  Use them to debate, arrange raids, chat or suggest improvement if there is something you don’t like.  Proper categories have been made for you so please use them! 

Do not use the boards as a place for personal vendetta.  This doesn’t mean that every sentence needs be polished and politically correct, get to the point and say what you mean, but please be constructive in all cases!

 

Grouping

Grouping is the thing in EverQuest.  Always try and group with the guild  ! Always ! 

If you are moving into a new place, see if other guildees want to go with you.  For example post on the boards asking if anyone wants to camp any area you wish to go to.

Loot has been the reason for most disputes on Norrath.  In Celestial Rising we do not want to be known as 'Ninja Looters', 'Loot Whores' or similar.  Be fair and have a general rule that those that CAN and WILL use, will roll on an item. 

However be responsive to the group you are with, there is a thriving economy in EverQuest and many items can fetch a good price on auction.  In many groups all members are allowed to roll for any item. 

If you enter a group where the object is to gain wealth, be sure you agree upon the rules before entering the group.  This will prevent any disagreement later on.

 

Raiding

Anyone in Celestial Rising can set up a raid involving a lot of people.  In fact we encourage people to do this.  There are however some rules you most apply to :

 

Be sure that everyone joining knows the objective for the raid.  This might sound formal, but it need not be.  Having fun is a good enough reason for going!

Never let down a guild member.  This can be about joining the raid, help with corpse retrieval or similar promises.  You do not leave the raid or let down a guild member for anything.  That being said RL is more powerful than Lord Nagafen and will always win but please make sure everyone is aware that you are leaving

You are now the warlord!  This takes planning.  Planning is best done on the boards.  If you are looking to recruit many people for your raid you have best give at least a weeks notice.  The Officers  and Raid Leader will always help you plan and organise events, just ask

There are many things to learn about multi-group raids which you will learn as a CR member.  The most important one is 'THE WARLORD IS GOD!'  all members on the raid must follow his instructions to the letter.

 

Raid Loot Rules

 

Celestial Rising ONLY

 

Celestial Rising only raids have a 'no roll' policy.  Loot is assigned by either the Guild or Raid Leader.  In order for this to be a fair and efficient means of assigning loot it is the responsibility of ALL guild members to ensure that their Magelo profile and epics page are updated on a regular basis.  Any queries on the assignment of loot or the fairness of this policy should be addressed with the Guild Leader, but we have been running this policy for several months now and all members are happy with it

 

Even with this policy in place the following things are adhered to:

 

All drops are announced publicly

In a multi group raid situation all cash is put into Raid Funds for future events

Any loot that is not assigned will be distributed to the appropriate Class Banker

 

Pick Up Raids

When raiding, loot rules are simple:  We are not loot whores.  If the raid has been set with a particular loot goal in mind, then loot will be reserved and published so all attending the raid are aware.  All other loot will come under the following rules:

 

In all circumstances check the drops and announce them.

Everyone who CAN and WILL use an item will roll.

Rotation means, that once you have won a roll on a type of equipment you loose your right to roll on that type until all who CAN and WILL use that type of equipment have won such an item.  There are four types of equipment:

Normal Armour

Breastplate

Robe

Weapons

For example you win a piece of armour and next a weapon drops.  In this case you are allowed to roll on the weapon drop again with a chance to win it.

Honour cannot be explained, it is just a common trait for Celestial Rising members.  There are no rules for this it simply has to be lived, honour makes people pass on rolls for one or the other personal reasons.

 

The main objective of the guild is to have fun, these rules are here to try to make that easier for all guild members and the people they group with.  Above all else we are here to help one another in the land of Norrath.

Class and Level

Age

Playing Time
