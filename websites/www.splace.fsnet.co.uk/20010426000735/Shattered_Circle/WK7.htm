Dungeon Master:

The mist lays heavy on the floor, but is slowly dissipating. Yanish riffles through the body of the dead chitine.  She tucks the dagger into her belt, and the only other things of value to be found are the shield and armour.

 

As Varistan heads slowly around the wall of the chamber followed by Lin, Mickey makes his way to the south door. Suddenly, there is a loud click, and on looking toward the origination of the noise, you watch Mickey disappear from view with a yelp!

 

The pit is approximately 10ft x 10ft x 10ft, and Mickey’s tunic is torn and his skin seriously grazed by one of the rusted spikes in the bottom (-1 HP Mickey).  As the mist billows down into the newly created hole, you get an itchy feeling that you are not alone down there.

Yanish:

As Mickey disappears down the pit Yanish shakes her head. "Caution and stealth are not the mouse’s best traits." Checking the now mist free floor for any other traps, Yanish walks over to the pit. Leaning over to the pits edge she looks down and asks, "Mickey are you alright?"

Lindornea'en:

Lin joins Yanish at the edge of the pit, the elf wears a smirk. He puts his swords away for a moment, and pulls out his quarterstaff, reaching it down for Mickey to grasp onto. "Nice job. but, I thought you were supposed to avoid traps?"

Dolly:

Once she sees there are no more enemies ready to attack out of the fast disappearing mist, she allows her guard to drop slightly.

 

Just as she is relaxing Mickey disappears down the pit, from the reaction of the others she can guess its not too serious, but walks across to offer a him a hand, over the lip of the pit.

 

She is also ready to grab the staff and help Lin should the task of hauling Mickey up on his staff prove too much for him.

Karg:

At the sound of the yelp, Karg starts moving in that direction, his club held firmly at the ready. Unsure of the footing in the dim light, he drags his feet along the ground in small steps. Reaching the edge of the pit, he stops and peers into the dimness. "Mickey hurt?" he asks in a low voice.

Micky:

Mickey stands in the corner, terrified. He tries to blend with the wall, and fails. As the faces of Lin, Dolly and Karg lean into view, he fails at even standing quietly and a small whimper of shame is heard below...

 

Putting on his bravest smile (and wanting out of the hole RIGHT NOW), Mickey grabs Lins' quarterstaff and scrambles up with a sheepish smile over to Dolly. Hearing Kargs concern, he only mumbles,

 

"...I'm fine, more pain in the ego than the arm..."

 

Turning, he looks back down into the pit. His eyes search the dark corners fearfully, trying to justify his paranoia. He doesn't want to come off as a complete idiot. There was something down there...right?

 

Looking across at the door, he lowers his head quickly. * Great, now how do we get THAT open? *

Dungeon Master:

Peering back down into the hole, you see the skeletal remains of a humanoid. What used to be clothing of sorts, lies in tatters, along with the remnants of leather sandals, and a small leather pouch. The stench from the pit, is almost unbearable.

Micky:

Without a word, Mickey sits on the edge, turns, and lowers himself back down into the pit. He opened it, he'll take the risk.

 

He moves cautiously past the rusted spikes to the ancient corpse. Using his dagger, he lifts the pouch and hefts it into one hand. What is the value of broken pride?

 

He looks back up to the others, then opens it, pouring the contents into one gloved hand...

Lindornea'en:

The elf shakes his head, and sighs. He leans into the pole, waiting for Mickey to finish his 'exploring'... Along with half a mind to let someone else help the Mouse out next time.

Dungeon Master:

A couple of coins (2gp, 3sp) and small glass vial fall into your hand. The vial is stopperred and contains a clear liquid.

Yanish:

Yanish moves around the pits edge and inspect the door for any possible locks or latches that might keep it closed. If the switch for the pit is found, she will wait for the mouse to exit before testing it. To Mickey she says "Glad to hear your alright mouse, she shoots him a warm smile."

Yanish:

Unable to find any way to unlock the door and with the knowledge that Mickey is ok, Yanish heads across the room (F2) and attempts to unlock that to. As she walks Yanish studies the floor and walls for any more nasty surprises.

Dungeon Master:

You hear a small click as you successfully pick the lock, and the hinges squeal as the door is pulled open. Within, a tumble of dust and debris cover a toppled stone table and bench. What might be shattered pottery shares the floor with the skeletal remains of several humanoids.

Yanish:

When the door opens, Yanish turns to the party and says. "Would any of you strong warrior types like to move forward in this place, or are we going to spend all day pacing around this dusty room?" To the barbarian she says in a soft honey filled voice "How about you go check out that room big boy, I'm sure nothing in this place would dare take you on. As big and strong as you are a creature would run in fear at your foot steps."

Rupert:

In the pregnant pause between Yanish's comment and Karg's reply, Rupert sneezes loudly. He still has one of his ever-present books in his hand and is sketching the area on paper as the others explore.

 

"Excuse me," he manages before he sneezes again.

Lindornea'en:

Lin snickers, and says, "There is none, for you..." knowing full well that he's going to get it sooner or later. The elf helps Mickey back up (should he not want to continue spelunking), again.

Varistan:

Before Karg can reply to Yanish, Varistan steps forward and grumbles "might as well send in an ox as Karg. Unless that's your plan for discovering more pit traps." He snorts a curt laugh, and without a further word moves past the others and steps up to the doorway. He pauses momentarily, makes a quick study of the floor beyond the door, and steps through, sword held at the ready.

Micky:

Mickey happily takes Lin's staff (again) and climbs back up out of the pit. He stands nearby (J5) and seems more interested in his findings than the opening of the south door. When Rupert sneezes, he mentions a distracted, "Bless you..." but never even looks back to the frail mage.

 

He holds the vial up, shaking it, examining closely. He uncorks it, and smells the clear liquid inside. Shrugging, he tastes the rough surface of the cork... Sure, it could be poison, but if the body in the pit is any indication, then this 'assassin' is even less competent than the Mouse. At least Mickey survived the fall...

Dungeon Master:

Mickey the liquid in the vial smells slightly of peppermint.

 

Varistan, amongst the debris on the floor you can see remnants of rusted weapons and broken shields aswell as the skeletal remains of several humanoids.

Yanish:

When Varistan charges into the room, Yanish says "Well bold Varistan, are you to be my knight in shining armour? Its good to see not all the men in the party cower behind the women. As for Karg, he may be as large and strong as ox, however he is much more intelligent and brave if he chose to enter the room in pursuit of fame and glory so be it."

Karg:

At the suggestion of Yanish, Karg finds himself overwhelmed with the suggestion the pretty little runt makes, of he charging to the fore. Something about it strikes him as odd, but now that the idea has hold of him, the feeling of oddness can't get more than a fleeting grasp upon him. Hefting his club, he charges on into the next room and giving a good approximation of a bear growling.

 

When he gets in there, he looks about, seeing if any dare stand in his path.

Varistan:

Varistan, standing just inside the doorway, turns and looks at Yanish with his crooked grin. "Knight in shining armour?" He hawks and spits, wipes his mouth with the back of his hand, shakes his head in amusement, and takes a quick step to the side when he sees Karg coming for the room, allowing the big brute to enter unimpeded.

Micky:

Mickey pockets the tiny glass vial, wondering at it's true potential. (Probably just an extract for cooking.) He looks across the room just as the bear-warrior Karg charges into a room with the noble (but crude) Varistan...

 

Not wanting to miss on any of the glory (or the loot), Mickey nods to Yanish as he passes, then follows into the ancient room beyond. Upon seeing more corpses, he kneels and begins a careful inspection of each with his black dagger.

 

* Who knew grave-robbing was so profitable...? *

Gob:

Gob quietly looks on as the pain in his arm refuses to quit nagging him and shakes his head at the sight of his so called companions looting dead bodies - did these people have no respect for the dead?

 

In a loud voice Gob announces

 

"I can see I made a mistake in joining on this quest and that my meagre talents such as they are a hindrance to such a group, I bid you all farewell and good luck on your journey"

 

Gob promptly turns around and disappears up the stairs back into the sunlight and the crumbling circle of stones and begins to retrace the parties footsteps back towards town.

Dolly:

As Gob makes his speech Dolly responds.

 

"I'm sure you talents will come in useful sometime- but singing and playing while the 'specialists' are having a look around wasn't a good idea and we tried to tell you that. You wouldn't listen, but we all rallied round and got you out of that mess. Didn't we?

 

If you stick around you might actually learn how, and when, to use your talents in a way that they are useful. Run away and you won't learn anything. Now if I were you, I would shut up, get back in place and wait for someone to have the time to sort your arm out."

Gob:

Looking back over his shoulder at Dolly's rebuke Gob counters.

 

"Sorry but hanging out with people that loot graves is not my idea of finding a worthy ballad - I shall take my leave and move on"

Dolly:

Dolly shrugs her shoulders at the departing halfling, "What's he whingeing about now - no one has even found a grave let alone, looted one. You think he mean that bloke in the pit? That isn't a grave - that is a trap. But maybe we ought to take the bones back to the monastery later? Or maybe get Karg to bury them?      

Varistan:

Varistan pokes his head back outside the door at the new round of complaints from Gob. "Ah, let the little speck go. Those bardic types never have any taste for reality. Even shit smells like roses in their songs."

 

Not even knowing if Gob can still hear him or not, Varistan adds "have a fun trip back, speck. Hope you don't run across no hungry bears or wolves."

 

Turning back into the room, he joins Mickey in searching through the debris.

Lindornea'en:

Lin sighs, and comments, "The dead need nothing in the Afterlife, and more than likely, would rather their implements from the past life to be used by another, since they could not. Being a little peevish, aren't we... Bard?" He doesn't run after the half-pint, though... letting him figure out his own path.

Micky:

Mickey stops searching the corpses just as he realizes we are about to lose Gob. The words of the others confuse him - they feel much as his Father would have, the dead have no need of money or weapons. Then the nagging doubt of his Mother creeps up on him. If she were here now, she'd probably slap the snot out of him for playing with the 'icky dead things'...

 

He stands, and bolts up the stairs after the little bard. Stopping him on the landing, he lays a firm hand on his shoulder and begins a plea for his return,

 

"Gob! Wait... don't go yet. I hadn't thought you would find this offensive, but if it troubles you I will stop. How's your arm?... Look, Gob, I really liked it back on the trail when you would sing for us... maybe your just looking at this in 'too extreme a light' as my Da' used to say.

 

**In every Ballad of Glory there are parts that are left unsung just as there are verses of heroism. A talented song-writer such as your self should be able to take the good for his lyrics and ignore the more seedier side of the adventure. **

 

Surely, you remember the songs of the Warrior Quesnef - they sing of his valor and his blade, but not once mention the carnage or his loss..."

 

Mickey slows his words, realizing his rambling. It has been nice having a little minstrel with us, but if the small man chooses to leave us, perhaps it would be better not to stand in his way,

 

"Gob, I guess I've said all I can, other than - I'm sorry. Is there anything I could do to convince you to stay with us just a bit longer? I'd love to hear the first verse of how we slew the bug-man..."

Karg:

Having gone charging into the next room, Karg has no notion of Gob's leaving. Of course, if he did know, would it have made a difference?

Gob:

Gob pauses for a moment looking at Micky and then sighs and looks in indecision.

 

"Your a good man Master Donatelli! Would be happy to share a mug of ale with you any time. My concerns are petty in the eyes of most but I was taught to believe that you don't take what isn't yours and to accept any gift freely given, is why I don't charge when I sing but happily take donations from people who appreciate my art.

 

Master Donatelli - what is it that makes these people think this missing monk is in a hole in the ground that has shown no real signs of being disturbed - is there any evidence that he was taken away? Have we looked or searched the area to find any signs of him? The commission was to find a missing monk not to go exploring strange ruins and take things that do not belong to us - I am not abandoning my quest as I promised the Abbott but choosing a course that seems more sound - will you join me Master Donatelli?"

Yanish:

Watching Gob turn and leave the ruins, Yanish shakes her head. To herself she thinks "Pity to see him go, his antics were quit amusing. But perhaps it is safest for both himself and us." When the halfling disappears up the stairs with Mickey in pursuit Yanish blends into the shadow, staying close to the wall (H13), and walks into the room with Karg and Varistan. While entering she attempts to examine the walls and floor for any traps that may still lay in wait.   

Micky:

Mickey shakes his head.

 

"No Gob, I feel safer with the group, even if they are on the wrong trail...

 

He watches Gob shrug and turn to walk away. A shame to lose one to anything other than a hard fought battle. The Mouse looks dejectedly back towards the dark stairwell and frowns unhappily...

 

* This HAS to be the place. I felt it days ago back at the Inn when we were first hired on. Gob has just given up too soon... *

 

“GOB! Perhaps this IS where the shepherd vanished from; maybe we just need to do a bit more exploring! Gob?...HEY GOB D'SMACKED!!!"

 

Mickey only sighs heavily as he sees the small man continue walking, all the yelling and logic in the world can't derail the determination of a halfling. Shaking his head one last time, Mickey returns to the cellar under the circle and back into the room just opened...

Varistan:

Varistan moves slowly across the room, sifting through the remains and the debris until he comes to the door in the south wall. He studies it carefully, searching for any signs of a trap.

Dolly:

Dolly moves up to the doorway of the room, "You coming, Rupe."

Rupert:

Rupert looks up from where he has been kneeling, his fingers in the dirt. "Oh! Coming, Sis," he replies, standing quickly and brushing his hands off on his coat. Not satisfied with that, he rummages in his pockets for something better to wipe his hands. His first pocket yields a half-eaten apple, the second a shiny rock, the third an advertising poster for a performance by the Halfling Halfwits.

 

Finally, he finds a bit of cloth and wipes his hands, hurrying to follow Dolly.

Dolly:

Dolly smiles as Rupert hurriedly cleans up and starts heading along with them.

 

*Whhooo hoo - gonna be hard stopping him getting too distracted - gotta keep a closer eye on him.* She thinks to herself

Gob:

Gob waves as Mickey disappears into the hole and one by one begins to examine the stones that are scattered around wishing he had paid more attention to his Pa when it came to outdoor work. Gob moves quickly trying to find anything that looks odd or out of place in an expanding spiral from the circle of stones, staff in hand just in case any more of those rather painful bug creatures should put in an appearance.
