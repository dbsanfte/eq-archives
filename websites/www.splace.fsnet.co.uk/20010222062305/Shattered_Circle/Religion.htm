Shyeth, He Who Provides

Since before written record, despite the capriciousness of kings and warlords, Shyeth has been honored far and wide.  And why not?  Who can hope to live even a single day should Shyeth not grant the boon of His gifts?  The food they eat, the water they drink, even the very air they breathe are but the least of His gifts.

And what of those who serve in the name of Shyeth?  They have spread far and wide, and even the powerful treat them with respect for when they are treated poorly, when Shyeth is disrespected by the mistreatment of His servants, crops become blighted and wither in droughts, wells run dry or the water becomes tainted and the cattle fall diseased and barren.  And so, the faithful of Shyeth have grown strong and rich, a force to be feared and appeased and few are those who don't bow at the alters of Shyeth and help to fill his coffers with gold and silver.

But Shyeth is a distant god, giving His gifts and leaving the guidance of his flock to his faithful and many have grown proud and willful and they abuse that which has been given them, often squandering the gifts to Shyeth on drink, or women of ill-repute or any of the other base pleasures of the flesh to which men are drawn.

Other Limitations Clothing - When in public, the priests of Shyeth wear yellow robes covered by a green, sleeveless over tunic.  When one reaches a position of power among the order, the robes are highlighted with threads of gold, the over tunic with strands of silver.

Instead of blessing water as some religions do, the followers of Shyeth consecrate specially ground flour, using it in their rites and rituals.  It is said that the restless undead are unable to abide it's touch.  At these rituals, the priests also wield shepherds crooks and pendants of bronze carved with wheat and the sacrifice of herd beasts or bounties of the field are common.

 

 

Kephian Order

 

The Kephian order is an offshoot of the followers of Shyeth.  To date, it is limited to a single, tiny monastery, nestled in the hills at the edge of the lands of the savage Gralk tribes.

A young and idealistic Shyeth priest, Kephan, grew weary of the excesses and abusiveness of his brothers.  His superiors would not grant him release from his oaths, but sent him instead as a missionary to the savage Gralk tribesmen.  It was thought he would soon find his head on a pike, trophy for some grunting shaman.  But apparently Shyeth was not yet done with young Kephan.  He built a small hold of lovingly shaped wood.  It was a small thing at first, but it called to a few of the savage people and the hold soon became a small monastery, far from the meddling of the civilized churches of Shyeth.  In these times, it is made up solely of those who have sundered their ties to their Gralk brethren and have sworn allegiance to Shyeth.

The followers of Kephan's teachings are looked down upon by the more civilized priests of Shyeth, though they contend that they are far more in tune with Shyeth's teachings than the rest and there is much ill feeling between the two.  So, while there is rarely blood drawn between them in these days, there is little friendliness.

 

Shape Changing  (example: Bear Clan)

 

Level

Physical

Attack and Damage
1st

Assumes the aura of a bear, but his body can clearly be seen through the aura with the exception of his hands which have now turned into claws 

Attack:  claw/claw 

Damage:  1d3/1d3

2nd

The aura is still there and clearer in definition, although his body can still be seen through it.  Again his hands are physical claws

Attack:  claw/claw

Damage:  1d4/1d4
3rd

The aura is even more defined, but again body can still be seen.  Now though the head is a solid bear head along with the hands still as claws

Attack:  claw/claw/bite

Damage:  1d4/1d4/1d6
4th

The aura is again more defined, with solid bear head and claws

Attack:  claw/claw/bite

Damage:  1d4/1d4/1d8
5th

The aura is more defined still, and now along with the solid bear head and claws, the whole arms have taken on solidity as the bear

Attack:  claw/claw/bite/hug

Damage:  1d6/1d6/1d8/2d4 with two successful claw hits of 18+
6th

It is difficult now to see the man form through the aura, and the head, claws and arms are solid bear form

Attack:  claw/claw/bite/hug

Damage:  d6/1d6/1d8/2d4 with one successful claw hit of 18+
7th

Full Bear Shape change (All basic equipment, weapons and armour meld with the change)

Full Brown Bear Stats for attack and Damage
