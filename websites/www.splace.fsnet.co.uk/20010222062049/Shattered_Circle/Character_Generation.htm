Character Generation

To create a character to play in my AD&D game, proceed, in order, through the following steps.

 

Step1 : Ability Scores

Your character needs scores for Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. All characters have 80 points to distribute as they choose between the above abilities.  In the case of choosing an 18 Strength, 1 point will buy you each 10%.

 

Step 2 : Choose a Race

Once chosen (and I will only allow the Race's listed below) adjust the character's Ability scores according to the race chosen:

Dwarf                 Con + 1, Cha - 1

Elf                       Dex + 1, Con - 1

Gnome                 Int + 1, Wis - 1

Half-Elf              no adjustments

Halfling               Dex + 1, Str - 1

Human                no adjustments

 

Step 3 : Select a Class/Kit

Select a class that is available to your character's race.  I will also allow kits in my game, as long as I have the details.  I do hold most of the Kit manuals.  If you are unsure, please do no hesitate to contact me.

 

Step 4 : Choose an Alignment

In selecting you alignment, please abide by class restrictions:

Paladin                lawful good

Ranger                 lawful, neutral, or chaotic good

Mythos Priest     any acceptable to deity

Bard                    any neutral combination

All others            any

 

Step 5 : Record Saving Throws and THAC0

Consult your Players Handbook for these details, or I will fill them in for you.

 

Step 6 : Hit Points

To make life a little easier all my first level characters start out on maximum hit points including any Con adjustment.

Warrior               1d10

Priest                   1d8

Rogue                  1d6

Mage                   1d4

 

Step 7 : Record Base Movement

Find the character's base movement rate in the Players Handbook and record it.

 

Step 8 : Select Proficiencies

Consult your Players handbook to determine the character's weapon and non-weapon proficiency slots.  Add the character's number of languages known to his number of non-weapon proficiencies.

Select weapon proficiencies.  If your character is a fighter, you may select a weapon specialization.

Select non-weapon proficiencies.  Record their relevant abilities and check modifiers.

 

Step 9 : Equip Your Character

Determine your character's starting funds using the list below:

Warrior            5d4 x 10gp

Wizard            (1d4+1) x 10 gp

Rogue               2d6 x 10gp

Priest                3d6 x 10 gp

Then go shopping :0)
