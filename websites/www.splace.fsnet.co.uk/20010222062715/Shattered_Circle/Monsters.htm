Monsters

Chitine

     Chitines are small humanoids who, when fully mature, possess four jointed arms that allow for inhuman movement.  Their faces are human, although they have multifaceted eyes and mandibles protruding from their mouths.  Long, stringy black hair falls in a tangle from their skulls and grows down their backs like the mane of a horse.

 

Encountered: Prime Material Plane, Oerth, Toril
Strength:    6-18
Dexterity:    8-19 (+1)
Constitution:    7-19 (+1)
Intelligence:    8-18
Wisdom:    6-18
Charisma:    3-16 (-2)
Thieving Skills: PP -5%, OL -5%, FR/T +10%, DN +5%, CW +80%
Class Option: Warrior (12), Thief (15)
Typical Alignment: Any
Starting Age: 20 + 3d4
Maximum Age: 10 + 1d100
Average Height: 41 + 1d6 (m/f)
Average Weight: 65 + 5d4 (m/f)
Movement Rate: 12, Web 9
Initial Languages: Drow, Svirfneblun, Underdark Common
Advantages:

AC 9, Save vs. Web at +4 if save is called (automatic save if not),

May use all four arms to attack, Thaco at –1,

But if they attack with three and a shield Thaco is Normal..  
Disadvantages: -1 to Attack and Save in Sunlight
Special Abilities:

A chitine can spin sticky spider webbing at the rate of 1 foot/round.  The webbing is spun from an orifice in the chitine's stomach.  The webbing, in combination with oils secreted from the chitine's body, can be hardened and shaped into a variety of tools, clothing, and traps.  A chitine's skin secretes an oil that negates the adhesive effects of their own webbing.  Meanwhile, the palms of their hands and soles of their feet are covered in dozens of tiny hooks that allow them to climb textured surfaces with no loss of speed.
