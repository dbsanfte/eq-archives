Dungeon Master:

The evening passes without a hitch, and Yuri personally shows you all to your rooms.

 

In the morning you wake to the smell of fresh baked bread, and making your way downstairs, find a great spread of cold meat, cheese and fresh bread, with jugs of ale or herbal tea to wash it all down.

 

Once you have gathered your belongings you group ready to set off. Before you go Yuri hands you each a small packet some dry meat, bread and a little cheese (enough for 3 days). Karg then guides you out of town to the East and you find yourself moving along a dirt road into the foothills.

 

It is a clear day, and you can see the Stickler Mountains way off into the distance.

Micky:

Mickey bounces from his bed, taking the stairs two at a time to stand once again in the Common Room. He makes a small meal of the fresh bread, cheese and meats, then pulls a chair near to the window, basking in the morning sun even as the others grumble and stumble into the room. From the looks of Varistan, it must have been a hell of a party...

 

With a big smile, he thanks Yuri for the rations, then when all are ready, falls into step right behind the barbarian Karg, taking the number two position. For most, it looks to be a day of promise...what's the ugly girl smiling about?

Lindornea'en:

Lin stood and stretched, his body taught from sitting in the cross-legged style he learned to sleep in. Two blinks, and a smile cross his face, as his blue eyes greet the day. The Elf calmly walks downstairs noting the odd conglomeration of people running about the Inn. With a sigh, he chokes down what food he can, muttering to himself occasionally about finer fare.

 

He lets Karg run the show... for now, as they take off for the Site. With a nod towards the Barmaid for the Rations, he silently trails along behind the Barbarian...

Gob:

Awakening with curiosity for what the day has in store Gob awakens ready to face the day. Starting the morning off with a few minutes of finger exercises to keep them supple for plucking the strings of an instrument or palming a small gem as the need may arise.

 

Gathering himself together he neatly packs his kit and heads downstairs, seeing everyone eating and bustling to get ready, being small of stature Gob is easily overlooked in the scheme of things and to announce his presence he sits at a table has a small bite of food and then launches quite loudly into a Poem recital, a popular tale of a small band of men setting off on a noble quests that comes to a tragic end.

When everyone is outside and getting ready to set off Gob is quite amused at the people lining up like some sort of army and instead decides to or jog as the case may e besides Karg.

 

"Hey Karg, tell me a bit about your people, their battle chants and songs a people as strong and tall as yours must number many fine warriors and hunters!"

Micky:

Mickey glances to his side as the limber elf easily matches his stride only paces behind Gob and Karg. He smiles easily as he tries to recall the proper pronunciation of the mans name.

 

If only mother had taught him the proper elfin inflection! The memory eludes him, so he shrugs and settles for,

 

"Morning Lin, you sleep well enough?"

 

Mickey adjusts his pack, and extends a firm handshake,

 

"Don't believe we were properly introduced yesterday, Mickey Donatellis' the name, but you can call me mouse. How is it you came to hold such a burdensome namesake, Lindorm'aen... Linnnormeea... -Lin?"

Dolly:

Having given up on the disfigured man's body, Dolly spends the evening with a few beers and dinner before retiring.

 

Sleeping well, she awakes early as is her wont. She washes, dresses and repacks her bag before heading down stairs. On the way she bangs on Rupert's door to make sure he is awake and getting ready.

 

This morning, rather than yesterday's street clothes, she wears a suit of studded leather armour and carries a shield and battle axe. Her slightly greasy red hair hanging down below her unfastened leather helmet.

 

She makes a good breakfast with a plateful of cheese and meat and a couple of large hunks of bread - all washed down with a jug or two of ale. She grabs a smaller plate with a bit of everything and a tankard of ale for Rupert, should he get down late.

 

As she eats she smiles gently at the excessive energy of the one that calls him self Mickey Mouse. *Bouncing about like a lamb in spring, Hmm what a shame that so much energy should be wasted - pehaps I had better see if he needs 'tiring out' tomorrow night.* she thinks to herself.

 

Later as the party leave she smiles again at the macho jostling to be at the front, and settles in beside Rupert to enjoy the walk.

Broki:

Grumbling to himself and scowling at people as he stomps down the stairs, Broki pulls out a chair and drops into it.

 

As he drains a mug of ale, he leans back, eyeing the food on the table.

 

"It be too early ta be up" he grumps, reaching for a piece of cheese and another, unclaimed, mug of ale. Definitely not a morning person, he only grunts a yes or no to any questions.

 

As the other prepare to set off, Broki grabs a handful of meat and cheese and puts it all between a couple slice of bread. Wrapping this up in a semi-clean cloth, he puts it in his jerkin for later.

 

Grabbing his equipment he follows the others, but settles in at the back of the group, and keeps an eye on the rear.

Yanish:

Safely concealed in the alleys shadows, Yanish watches the odd collection of races as they leave the inn. She thinks, "Well the invitation for a free nights sleep was not a trap after all." Yanish Scratches her back  where the straw rubbed her skin, she begins to regret the decision to sleep in the stables hay loft instead of the warm room that was offered by Abbot Jengor. However her smile returns as she pats the fat purse hidden deep in her robe. After catching Jengor as he was leaving the inn and accepting his reluctant offer for her assistance, she look forward to what might lay ahead, that and the rest of her money!

 

A dwarf seems to be the last of the party as he comes out of the in grumbling. Perhaps the inn was not as comfortable as she had thought, the tunnelers mood seemed as foul as her own this morning. Keeping him just within eyesight, Yanish begins to follow.

 

Jengor hand be very clear when he said to let Karg know she had been add to the list of adventures, however he hadn't said when. With the letter from Jengor in hand she still wondered why he had thought it necessary. If Karg had accepted the strange group, she saw no reason why he wouldn't accept her as well. But as most things in life she decided to play it safe.

Lindornea'en:

Lin's eyes narrow as he casts a sideways glance at Mickey. "It's only a problem, to those who are too far out in the Sticks, to know Elven. And, before you slaughter my name anymore.. try ... Lin... Oor... Iian." He sighs disgustedly, noting the slight elven heritage within this one, "I'm to assume that you haven't studied half of your Lineage, hmm?"

Varistan:

Varistan wakes with a headache and fuzzy memories of the night before. Crawling from bed, he rubs the sleep from his eyes and peers out his window, squinting against the brightness. He curses, noting the height of the sun. Like a damned fool he'd overslept.

 

Quickly gathering his gear, he trots down the stairs and wolfs down a quick meal and a mug of ale. He begins to head for the door, has a second thought, and chugs down a second mug to help appease his pounding head. He grabs up two handfuls of meat and cheese, continuing to eat as he heads outside.

 

Seeing that the others were all accounted for and just beginning to head out, he slips in at the back of the group with Broki. He preferred being in a position that enabled him to watch his own back and to keep all the others in sight.

 

He looks at all of them now, memorizing each face and wondering which ones he could count on if things got ugly.

 

His gaze falls on Dolly in her studded leather, looking even more imposing than she did before. Seeing her, some of his fuzzy memories begin to attain more clarity. Had she and he...? No, somehow he didn't think so. Something he had said had seemed to cool her off considerably. He shrugs. Story of his life.

 

He walks in silence, feeling the strangest sense of regret mixed with immense relief.

Rupert:

Rupert is rudely awakened by his half-sister's knock early in the morning. It is quite obvious that the half-elf is not an early riser. Especially not after drinking far more than he is used to.

 

Wandering in to the common room, he realizes that he is not the only one with fuzzy memories and an aching head. He manages a soft good morning to Dolly but grimaces at the ale, choosing instead to find a cup of tea. A very strong cup of tea.

 

For not the first time he is amazed at his sister's mode of dress. No faint maiden, she! He, of course, wears no armor and only has a strong oaken staff along with his pack and coat with its many pockets.

 

Rupert also manages a good morning to Varistan, despite the other's attentions to Dolly. Or was it the other way around? He's no longer sure.

Micky:

Mickey looks to the ground as Lin... Oor... Iian, slowly pronounces the name for him as if he were a small child just learning to speak. He looks up after a few moments, with a shameful glance of admiration towards the true-blood elf beside him.

 

Mothers family abandoned her after her betrothal to his 'human' father, and the ways of the elves were never a warm topic of discussion in the family home.

 

Sadly, Mickey really didn't know much of his elfin lineage...

 

Mickey says nothing more to the man, not wanting to lose any more respect than he already has. He wants badly to be accepted by the Elf, perhaps he can teach more of the ways of his Mothers people.

Lets start off with what you know." Lin quirks an eyebrow at the Rat, his mind trying to decipher such a nickname...

Thank you sir a most entertaining song - In fact i believe I could work a pleasant counter melody to it. Slinging his quarter staff across his back Gob pulls out his little lute and strums a few chords that would accompany Karg's voice quite well.

 

Now if you wouldn't mind could you sing the first part again so i can be sure I have the right chords my good sir?

His eyes pop wide in startlement at the sudden bellowing of Karg's preamble to song, but otherwise he shows no indication of surprise. When the little halfling sings out his improvised verse, Varistan chuckles and shakes his head. The diminutive entertainer had balls, that was for sure.

 

He continues to listen to the others, his interest even more highly perked by Mickey's and Lin's conversation of parentage. "Lins' not an oaf - he just... well, he didn't know..."

"No Gob, they were neither heroes nor villains. Father ran a shipping company, Mother loved him not for his money or deeds, but just because he  was who he was...  There was never a love greater...

 

"Our business was forever plagued by burglaries, and that's how I came to be in my current profession. Theft deterrent.

 

"Some months later, I was approached by two men who 'recruited' me into a 'Security School', a way to learn and improve upon my natural talents.

"Enough of me. How about you Gob? Ever take singing lessons? They say it helps, you know. Tone and Inflection...",he says, smiling down at the little man.

I am not Clumsy in either word or action. Maybe it is you that is reading into things?"
