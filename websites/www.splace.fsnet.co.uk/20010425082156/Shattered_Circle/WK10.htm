Dungeon Master:

As the two parties stand there looking at each other, another 6 of the creatures appear from the northern passageway, they too just stand there, although weapons are at the ready. They seem to be talking to each other, in some sort of clicking, high-pitched type language, and one of them disappears back up the passageway. He is not gone for long, but returns with another, of which you assume is their leader. He seems to speak to the others as they move back to let him through to you.

 

“Who are you, and what are you doing here?” he asks in broken common.

Micky:

Mickey looks to the creatures (chitines?), then to Dolly. This is a tense moment. Are her skills refined enough to handle this situation with the delicacy required? Knowing this to be a matter or Charisma and charm, Mickey confidently walks forward, standing next to her for support. Bowing slightly, he speaks,

 

"Forgive our intrusion, kind sir. As the young lady has stated, we are from the surface and seek one of our own. A shepherd is missing, he was last seen in this area...do you know of this?"

Dolly:

Dolly takes a step back, clearly leaving the talking to Mickey. However, she stands behind him, axe in hand, watching the creatures for any sign of attack.

Dungeon Master:

The chitine captain, looks over the party, and nods at Mickey’s question, “If it is a male like you that you are looking for, then we may be able to help, but you must give up your weapons before I take you to see him”

Karg:

After his first failed attack, Karg pulls his weapon up short and stops his efforts. When the thing speaks, Karg shrugs, unable to really understand it's words, but he gets the gist. With as much trouble as he had understanding the thing's speech, Karg decides it's best to let someone else do the talking.

Yanish:

Yanish catches just enough of the creatures speech to know she's not giving up her weapon. Moving forward, pushing the tree with legs out of her way if necessary, Yanish, using the meekest voice she can muster, says to the leader; "You completely out number us, plus you know this ruin better than any here. There’s no need to take our weapons please just show us to the one we seek and we will leave your territory as fast as we can."

Dolly:

"Or fetch him out to us, so that we can take him back to his people."

Rupert:

Rupert listens, frowning. Carefully making sure that his glasses are safe in his coat, he looks at the 'creatures.' "My dear companions," he tells his friends, "Why argue over giving up weapons. Why don't they simply take me to see our poor lost companion? I will be unarmed and then I can see for myself."

Varistan:

Varistan looks at Rupert, his eyes wide. The man either had himself one monstrous set of balls or was a few bricks short of a wall. Either way...

 

"Uh uh. No way, Rupe. No one goes alone with these guys."

Dolly:

Dolly looks over at her brother, sharply. "No, I don't think that would be a good idea, at all. If you go, I will have to go with you."

Rupert:

Rupert holds out his hands. "Please. We're not going to give up our weapons and they're not going to give us what we want. I think its a good compromise."

 

The half elf looks at his sister. "Come on, Dolly, I don't need you to go to. Surely I can take care of myself, can't I?"

Dolly:

Mummy would never forgive me if I let you go off alone like that, you should stay here with the rest of us and let them fetch him out."

Varistan:

"Listen to your sis, Rupe. The girl talks sense. There's nothin' to stop them from feastin' on your bones once they have you to themselves."

 

Then Varistan tries unsuccessfully to suppress a smirk. "Besides, we wouldn't want your mummy gettin' upset."

 

Then he's all business again as he speaks to the creatures. "If you've got someone here then bring 'im on out. We'll jus' stay right here and no one will get hurt."

Dolly:

Dolly turns slightly and raises her middle finger towards Varistan, before returning her attention to the things facing them.

Varistan:

Varistan snorts a laugh at Dolly's crude gesture, trying to keep a straight face in the midst of this serious, and no doubt delicate, situation.

Lindornea'en:

Lin keeps his mouth shut (for once) and lets everyone else cut into the other. He doesn't let up on a slight grin, however...

Micky:

Father once taught him, 'Only a fool surrenders his weapon in home of an Enemy.'... These Chits had already proven their urge to attack first and question later, as was apparent by the loss of Gob. Sure, it wasn't a mortal wound - but it scared the bard enough for him to seek the path of daylight.

 

He is with the group. Holding tightly to the black longsword, his mouth is set in a grim frown of negative response. Well, if diplomacy breaks down, at least we have Dolly on our side...

Dungeon Master:

The chitine captain listens intently to Yanish, cocking his head to one side as if in thought. The others continue the bantering in the background.

 

“OK” he says finally, “but the one of your kind is in no state to be brought out here, you must come with me, but you must swear that your weapons will stay sheathed”

Rupert:

Rupert smiles, winking at his sister. "You see," he states, "I thought that they might be reasonable creatures. I suggest that we do as they say."

Varistan:

Not liking the situation one bit, but unable to argue with the creature's reasonable request, Varistan moves to sheathe his sword, only to find that he had forgotten about the skull sword already placed in his scabbard. Momentarily perplexed, and not wanting to hinder himself by carrying yet another uncovered weapon in his belt along with the dagger he had claimed earlier, he carefully leans his longsword against the wall and takes a few slow side-steps away from it. No sense in making any sudden moves around these things.

Micky:

Mickey reluctantly sheathes his longsword, then discreetly checks that his boot-dagger is still readily available. This is not what he expected at all.

 

* I thought we would kill all the monsters, not talk with them... *

 

He smiles openly, even though his stomach lurches from simply looking at these bug-headed creatures. He thinks of a warm sunny day and a very large magnifying glass - and smiles even wider...

Karg:

Not having any way of sheathing his club, Karg settles for holding it in his off hand in a casual manner. "Karg ready." he announces to no one in particular.

Lindornea'en:

Lin slides his sword through his belt, unwilling to leave it behind. He crosses his arms, and waits patiently (surprisingly) for the show to get on the road.

Dolly:

"Maybe Rupe" Replies Dolly as she tucks her axe handle down behind the hand grip of her shield, and slings the whole lot over her shoulder.

Yanish:

Yanish, places the dragon tooth dagger back in her cloak next to the others. Slipping back down the hall she lets one of the warriors take the lead, "The time for talking is over now, I have done my part." She thinks.
