OK, some progress has been made on this, and we're getting close to consensus.

Here's the loot policy so far:

(1) When joining a group led by a non-NOR, it is our responsibilty to ask what the groups loot policy is, and follow it, no matter how stupid. If you don't like it, you can just leave the group. If there isn't one, suggest ours, but wait for agreement.

(2) When a NOR is a group leader, it is his responsibility to explain the loot policy to each new person that enters the group, before the first piece of loot drops.

(3) No one may end up with an item they cannot use unless no one else in the group can use it either. Only the characters actually in the group (or groups if mulit-group raid) have a right to the loot, unless no one wants it.

OK, these 3 rules I'm sure of, unless someone has an amazing argument against them. The next two rules I'm not sure about:

(4) Any "good" item should be considered group loot no matter who gets it via autosplit. If only one person can use it, they get it. If more than one can use it, they "roll" (guess next copper drop or whatever).

(5) For items that several players can use, once someone has won a "roll" they don't get another roll till everyone has something. Don't know how this works with some things only I can use, and some I want to roll for. anyone?

VERY IMPORTANT for this discussion. IT DOES NOT MATTER what policy is "fair" or "makes sense", I don't even want to read arguments about that. ALL THAT MATTERS is what other guilds will be OK with at high levels. THE LOOT IS NOT IMPORTANT, not pissing other guild members off is all important.

OK, what do other guilds expect to see? Suggestions?

All of the above sounds fair and honest to me. The first three being very important I agree. First and foremost looting policies should be established in any group prior to hunting. This has never been any sort of problem for me, as I follow the group loot philosophy which tends to be the norm in higher level parties.

I am not trying to push my values on to anyone else (okay maybe I am) but to me it is more important that loot goes to the one who needs it most rather than the one that receives it. And yes I like having the "extra" roll that is exclusively for those that have voiced a need. To be honest, I have never seen a group function differently

Perhaps an example would better put forth the type of attitude toward loot that to me demonstrates the NOR spirit. Prepare Mhair!! You have a starring role

Ok we are out in a full group tonight hunting dulls, etc around the Innis area. For those of you not familiar with these particular mobs, they have very nice drops. All of Innis is a breeding ground for 32 to 43ish level hunting parties. We have a full group. A druid, several wardens, Mhair, and I. The entire time we are there we get only one rare drop. It is a sword. I of course pass immediately before the stats are even read because I am piercing. The sword is 16dps 3.5spd or something and gives a +6 str plus another bonus I cannot recall at the moment. It is a dang nice sword. There are several people in the group that wield blades, both the wardens, the druid, the hero, etc. The call is made for who have a need and can use it. What does Mhair do? He passes saying the sword is better suited for the Hero in the group. Everyone who "can" use it follows suit and passes it to the Hero. Mhair dual wields blades mind you. The hero of course was ecstatic. No roll was ever even necessary at all. What impression do you think he left with of people in NOR?

To me this is how things should be. Now if there had been two heros in the group? Well that would have precipitated a roll. Or often times one might pass if they know the other is of a higher level and could use it sooner.

To me it is less about the rules and more about the feelings behind our actions. At the sake of being redundant, to me the game is not about loot. It is about having fun and spending time with friends that I care about. I am not saying that you should pass on every good drop that comes around so people will like you. I am saying be honest with yourself about who needs it the most and act accordingly.

Obviously, I haven't gotten to the point where good loot drops, so none of my groups have announced loot rules, but I like what I read on that website. Where something very desirable or rare can only be used by one class or profession, let those in that class or profession roll for it. If one person doesn't need it, they can drop out of the roll. And once someone in that class or profession has won something special, they have to wait for the others in their class or profession to get special stuff before participating in the class lotto again.

Other items, not restricted, should be rolled for by the whole group, excluding those that have already won something in a prior group lotto. When everybody has won something in the group lotto, everybody starts all over again.

As previously stated, what is important is having a loot policy that reflects NOR values and is acceptable to other guilds. I think the above does reflect NOR values, and should be acceptable to most other guilds.
