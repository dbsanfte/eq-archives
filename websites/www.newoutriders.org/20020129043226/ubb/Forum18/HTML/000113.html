D R A F T

Proposed Dungeon Masters Guild Structure for DAoC Realm

The DMG has two separate roles: trade skills, and knowledge of game
mechanics and content. The purpose of the trade skills section is to
encourage the development of trade skills and their use to benefit
all members of the guild. The purpose of the game knowledge section
is to provide a home for those who enjoy playing the game in an
analyzed, efficient, and disciplined manner, given that NOR is not a
power gaming guild and many NORbies do not enjoy playing that way.


NOTE ON DMG TITLES

The NOR charter defines DMG ranks. These ranks are held in
addition to one's true NOR rank, indicating the additional skills
and responsibilities that a player takes on. There will be three
paths of advancement: class mentoring, raid (RvR) specialty, and
trade skills support. The Rank equivalencies are as follows:

Knight (KP) Knight Protector, Assistant Artisan
Champion (QS) Class Specialist, Raid Specialist, Guild Artisan
Baron (QM) Class Mentor, Raid Mastet, Trades Master
Count (DM) Dungeon Master

With the exception of DM, the DMG titles are honors held in
addition to one's usual rank. Also, multiple positions are
possible (Due mainly to tradeskills). The specific title shall be
considered an "award" under the charter, and would be use in your
NOR EPI as such. For example, if Count Skorj were also a Guild
Artisan (as an Armorsmith) his NOR EPI would be

*NOR/CT-DAoC (QS) Guild Armorer

If Squire Elite Aleena were an assistant guild armorer (tailoring)
and Raid Master, her NOR EPI would be:

*NOR/SE-DAoC (QM) Raid Master, Assistant Tailor

DMG titles are not directy a NOR rank, as is shown, and aare specific
to one branch. However, a NORbie with a DMG title higher than his or
her NOR rank will generally be earning faster advancement because of
the duties of that DMG position.


GAME KNOWLEDGE

(KP) Knight Protector
* Understands the tactical roles of characters in combat (tank,
healer, nuker, melee damage dealer, etc). And how to organize
an efficient party from an arbitrary collection of classes,
especially oddball combinations.
* Understands his or her own class thouroughly from this
perspective, including the different spec choices available and
the consequences of these choices on the roles that character
will play.
* Able to assist newbies in understanding these two elements. Can
explain the point allocation choices available to his or her
class, and the roles that should and should not be attampted as a
result.
* Has a basic understanding of game mechanics as relates to his
or her class, and can recommend appropriate low level hunting
grounds based on armor and weapon types vs monster type and
starting location.
* Participates regularly in guild raids and recruiting events,
and occasionallly organizes them.

(QS) Class Specialist
* Thoroughly understands a class from newbie to high level,
including all class specific quests and important allocation and
faction choices.
* Has a thourough understanding of the game mechanics as relates to
that class (what all the numbers mean, what changes based on the
specific details of the opponent, etc).
* Follows the class-related websites and news from Mythic, informs
the guild through the message boards and other means of changes
relevant to that class.
* Provides mentoring based on this knowledge to members of his or
her class.
* Regularly plays alts of various levels in order to be able to
group with and assist NORbies learning the game.
* Organizes recruiting events and participates in guild raids on a
regular basis.
* Suggests to the appropriate Class Mentor players who should be
Knights Protector.

(QM) Class Mentor
* Thoroughly understands a group of related classes (all melee,
healer, caster, or rogue) at the Class Specialist level.
* Follows the class-related websites and news from Mythic, and
updates the guild when necessary (i.e., when no Class Specialist
for that class is active).
* Is available when needed to assist a NORbie having problems with
his class, either in questing or allocation choices. Makes this
a priority in adventuring.
* Collects hand-me-down class specific items that are not currently
needed until such time as they are (or until the durability is
used up), establishing a guild vault character for this purpose.
* Regularly reports to the DM the status of recruiting from that
class group, and recognizing problems if folks are leaving, along
with a roster of all DMG members in that class group.
* Acts as a recruiting officer for members of that class group
(devises recruiting events and makes sure they happen regularly).
* Responsible for recognizing and "promoting" up to Class
Specialist, with approval from the DM or Branch Leader.

(QS) Raid Specialist
* Can organize and lead a multiple-group raid. Understands how to
create effective groups from 20 or 30 people who actually show up
for a raid, whatever their class and level.
* Understands the logistics of a successful raid (how to get
everyone to where they need to be at the time they need to be
there). Works with guild craftsmen to provide seige weapons
when appropriate.
* Is expert in one specialy of RvR: keep taking or defense, total
knowledge of one frontier zone, etc. (If there is ever PvE
content at this level, the title of Quest Specialist will apply).
* Regularly organizes NOR Raids, and organizes Alliance raids at
the direction of the Raid Master, and participates in rectuiting
events.
* Recomends to the Raid Master players who should be Knights
Protector.

(QM) Raid Master
* Has overall responsibility for all guild raids on a server,
although leadership of individual raids may be delegated to a
Raid Specialist.
* Acts as Liaison to allied guilds (once the alliance has been
established and approved by the high council).
* Responsible for keeping "raid loot" that has yet to be assigned
to a player (or guild in an alliance). There is likely to be
much less of this unless high-level PvE is added.
* Responsible for policy on use of any goodies that can be
purchased with Realm Points for use by the guild. Responsible
for raising cash for a raid fund when necessary for building
siege equipment (or whatever) and the use of this fund.
* Regularly reports to the DM the status of raids (success,
faliure, reputation of the guild), the use of the raid fund, and
a roster of DMG members involved in raids. Maintains raid point
list if that ever becomes necessary.
* Provides constructive criticism about raids afterwards to all
involved.
* Provides leadership of all guild raiding activities by insuring
that raids are organized frequently, and recognizing and
"promoting" up to the level of Quest Specialist with the approval
of the DM or Branch Leader.

(DM) Dungeon Master
* Has the knowledge to substitue for Trade, Class, and Raid Masters
as necessary. Approves all "promotions" within the DMG, and
tests memebers as necessary before promotion.
* Collects the reports of all Trade, Class, and Raid Masters,
providing the report if necessary, and reports the collected data
and DMG roster to the Realm Leader, Branch Leader, and Herald on
a regular basis.
* Provide oversight for the entire DMG: makes sure that DMG members
doing what they need to, especially those at QM level. Promotes
up to QM level qithin the DMG and may replace QMs for non-
performance with approval of the Branch Leader.
* Insures that guild events happen regularly and are fun for those
involved, insures that criticism coming from the DMG after events
is always constructive and no abusive.
* Looks for alliances that would be beneficial to the guild, and
brings them to the attention of the Branch Leader and Realm
Leader for High Council Approval.


TRADE SKILLS

(KP) Assistant Artisan (Armorer/Tailor/Weaponsmith/etc)
* Has a skill of at least 400 in the selected trade.
* Willing to help others get started in this trade and explain all
of the basics.
* Provides high quality items of the first two material types to
low level NORbies at a discount, and is elibible for guild grants
to develop the skill (both things to be clarified by the Trades
Master).
* Participates in trade-related guild events.

(QS) Guild Artisan
* Has a skill of at least 650 in the selected trade.
* Follows the trade-related websites and news from Mythic, informs
the guild through the message boards and other means of changes
relevant to that trade.
* Maintains a mule character (always in the city) stocked with
items of the third through fifth material types, so that this
resource is always available to characters needing upgrades.
(Of course, the crafting character might *be* a mule instead).
* Provides these items at cost or discount to NORbies, and
maintains a small pool of guild money, to "float" poor characters
needing items and collect donations, and advance the chosen trade.
(again, by policy created by the Trade Master).
* Collects hand-me-down crafted items that are not currently needed
until such time as they are (or until the durability is used up).
* Organizes trade related guild events
* Recognizes and reccomends to the Trades Master players who should
be Assistant Artisans.

(QM) Trade Master
* Has a strong knowledge of all trade skills, and follows trade-
related game changes for all trades, and provides updates when
necessary (i.e., when no Guild Atrisan for that trade is active).
* Maintains an inventory of the best trade items in the guild bank.
* Manages the financing of tradeskills on that server (or servers).
Sets policy for how tradeskill financing will be handled (how
will money be collected, how much will trade items be subsidized).
* Responsible for recognizing trade skill specialists and "promoting"
up to Guild Artisan with approval from the DM or Branch Leader, and
for devising new kinds of tradeskill-related guild events.
* Reports the members of the trade skill organization and a summary
of skills and inventory available on that server (or all servers)
to the DM on a regular basis.
