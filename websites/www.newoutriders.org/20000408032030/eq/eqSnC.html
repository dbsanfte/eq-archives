Norrath Duchy on Everquest:  Duchy Specific Standards and Conventions

 Here are some items of importance to help you within the Norrath Duchy.

 Guild functions:  Everquest has guild functions built right into the game!  What does this mean to you?  When you are invited to join the guild, you will see a yes/no selection box appear.  A guild officer or GM must invite you while in the same game zone as yourself.   Select yes to become of member of *NOR/ according to Everquest’s guild rules.   Note that your squiring ceremony is your true induction to the guild;  the game function merely places the New OutRiders name over your head while you are traveling in Norrath.

 Once you are a member of the guild, you will be able to speak on the guild channel and hear others.  To hear others, all you need do is have your guildchat filter turned ON.  To speak to other members of *NOR/, simply type /guildsay and your message, or you can also set your default chat mode to “guild”; then everything you type without chat filter of /gsay /tell or /say will go to the guild channel.

 Everquest denotes one person of *NOR/ as the GM.  This person will generally be the Duke or Duchess of the Duchy.  Only the GM can appoint officers (Everquest’s definition of officer is a member who can invite others into the guild as regular members).  Appointed officers can invite players into the guild, but not as officers.  Please note that this Everquest definition of officer is not the same as *NOR/’s definition of officer.  A *NOR/ officer is any member of the rank Herald/Heraldess, BA/BS, CT/CS, etc.    Not all officers of *NOR/ are denoted EQ officers.  The purpose of game officers is merely to assist the DK/DC in tagging new members when they are far away from the DK/DC in the game, or when the DK/DC is not ingame when someone needs to receive the guild tag.

 Names:  You can have up to 8 names on your account.  Any of them can be tagged as guild members ingame, regardless of race, class or level, so long as they meet the following requirement: the alternate names must be so close to your original name that it is unmistakably you (your main character), i.e., the alternate name must be spelled nearly the same and when pronounced, is also nearly the same.  For example, Aragorn is a *NOR/ member, and could have alternate characters in *NOR/ in the game named Arragorn, Aaragorn, Aragourn, etc.  Please check with you DK/DC to determine if your alternate name is appropriate for guild tagging. Alternate characters are NOT to be roleplayed differently (in terms of their behavior towards other members of *NOR/) but can be roleplayed differently due to race and class.

 Helping others: As a new player in Everquest, you will find your guildmates to be very helpful!  This is what we do, of course.  You are expected to do likewise, but within the limits of your race/class/level.  No one is expected to give the shirt off his or her own back.  However7, while traveling about, help others as best you can, through buffing (casting beneficial spells upon them), healing, or assisting with mobs that are within your range of ability to combat.  Answering players questions about locations, traveling, quests, armor, weapons and items is also a good way to show your *NOR/ colors.  We are very proud of our knowledge and skills in the game in the Norrath Duchy!  We have a great reputation in this world, and expect everyone to contribute to the furthering of it.  Remember that as in other games,  the golden rule is always a good guide.  Treat other players as you would like to be treated in turn.

 Combat etiquette:  Kill stealing is expressly forbidden by *NOR/.  If another player or party has attacked a mob, before you and/or your party, and has not asked for your help, nor appear to need it, do not attack their target.  If you do more damage than the party, you would have gained the experience and stolen their kill.  Respect the rights of others, but expect your own rights to be respected as well.  If you are uncomfortable with the behavior of any of your party members, leave the group if you cannot convince them to behave better.

 PvP:  PvP is an optional activity.  Many of our members choose to duel other players to hone their skills and test their abilities.  You are never required to combat another player unless it is your choice to.  Just beware that there are arenas in the game, places in which you become PvP, involunarily, as soon as you enter them.  There are such areas in the major cities in the game, but not only there.  You will see a message telling you that you have entered a PvP area if you should do so.

 Economy:  Norrath has a unique economy.  You will need loot and money to improve your armaments and weapons, obtain magical items, do certain quests, obtain food and drink, buy services from other players and buy spells if you are a casting class.   The Norrath Duchy of NOR/ has a great reputation for generosity as well.   Many members freely give items and money to other guildmates and non-NOR/ players in the game.  Some donate items and money to the *NOR/ treasury, which was established to help out younger members, rebuild a character’s equipment in the case of a lost character disaster, and to fund guild expertise in the trade skills.  However, no member is required to give money or items to other members of the guild or to players in Everquest in general.  You are encouraged to do so, if you have no need of a particular item, nor a need for the cash proceeds from its sale.

 Events: From time to time there will be guild sponsored events.  You are encouraged to participate in such events, as they allow you an opportunity to spend time with guild mates that you would not normally be able to travel with due to level differences.  You can also plan and conduct an event of your own, if you have an idea for one.  Any member can run an event.

 To find other guild members in the game, simply type /who all new.  Remember that if you use the anonymous function, which removes your level and class from your character info available to others, you also remove yourself from being recognized ingame by other guild members.

 Please visit our message post board often!  There you will find current events in the Duchy, as well as news of events, items available and information about the various aspects of the game.  Other members of *NOR/ will be glad to help you with obtaining information about an item or quest, but be advised that the web contains a wealth of information as well.  Some quests are lengthy and involved, and it is more practical in some cases to read a detailed description at one of the many well-known web sites than to try to get this information in the game via tells.  But ask your guild first, and you will be pointed in the right direction!  Or ask on the message board, many of our members visit that site every day.
