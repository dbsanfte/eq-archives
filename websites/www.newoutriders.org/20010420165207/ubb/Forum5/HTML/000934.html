Greetngs one and all,

After the long discussion on our wake up procedures it came to me, hmm what kind of procedures do we have for Going to Bed...

I have some habits I have picked up over the years. One thing which is very important for me going to sleep is Music. Yes that is correct, I find that without my nightly visits from Creed, Eric Clapton, Stevie Ray Vaugh, Days of the new, Scorpions, Cinderella, Robert Plant, Santana, Metallica(the Orion instrumental is best), The Cult, and many others I cannot sleep, so before going to sleep I find 5 CD's and place them on the tray and set it for Random. Then march off to bed. With the music drifting over the air, I can find my way to visit the sandman. This ritual has been going on for years, even as a child I had a music clock/radio and would set it for the 1-2 hour countdown and goto bed. When I travel I carry a CD player with a favorite CD(recently been Santana's new release), and with this playing I am soon asleep. In the army during AIT, I had to sneak my CD player out of my locker, hiding it under my pillow, with only one ear phone in my ear to listen to the music.

Another ritual I follow is reading, I find that reading 20-30 minutes before going to bed is necessary too a good nights sleep.
Of course this can have drawbacks, as some books can just draw you in, then you find yourself unable to put it down and next thing you know that Alarm clock is going off with the silly country toons. One way I have found to speed up the process is too read such books as SQL 7.0 Adminstration, or Study guide for Microsoft NT server/workstation. Such books after 30-40 pages can place you in a deep deep coma.

---------------------------------------------Some side notes when unable to sleep, I have been known to drink that Niquil, have a soda, or juice of some sort ready to wash that taste away.

So now you can see why I do not use Music I enjoy to wake up too, if I did it would have the complete opposite effect.
---------------------------------------------

So I only have one question who is going to sing me to sleep at the bash.....

One methos I've found to be quite effective is to play EQ until I can no longer see straight or keep my eyes open. I have a hard and fast rule to gauge when I'm tired, the third time my face hits they keyboard I'm off to bed. Another timing mechanism to let me know it's time for bed (while playing EQ of course) is to get a random /tell asking if I'm busy or something to that extend. This will invariably happen right as my head is banging off the keyboard for the third time. After shaking my head I"ll read the tell and usually respond with the standard, "what do you need?" I should really know by now the answer as it's only one of two things usually. The most common being "can you powerlevel/clarity/buff/whatever me?" but a close second, and this is how I know I'm minutes away from sleep, is "we need an enchanter for <insert good camp that you would usually wait month to get into here>" Upon reading those words my eyes slam shut not to open until the last possible moment the next morning, or more often than not, in a few hours.

That's just what works for you, your mileage may vary, and all aplicable taxes do apply.
