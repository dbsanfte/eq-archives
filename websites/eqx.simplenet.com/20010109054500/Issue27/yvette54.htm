ISSUE # 27June 20th - June 26th

Yvette's Tower - part 5

The seventeen strong party of men stomped across the East Plains to the continental divide led by Porthios. Even in the seasonal downpour Bastion's eyes never dimmed, knowing that the hands of the Faceless One had guided him this far with a corrupt priestess feeding him numbing leaves between lashings from the flagellants. He thought it comical that the followers of the god of light were so brutal, just as brutal as any Lord of Terror, with less mercy upon the enemy. The words of the Light Bearer burnt into his skull like hot knives and the lashings broke away at his resolve but he would never tell Porthios what lay ahead. Occasionally Bastion would consider if the Cardinal had seen the future and knowingly condemned him to this fate, knowing that the Wood Elves would never see the treasure the scroll was sent for; knowing that Bastion would take it and run. He wondered how far behind Yvette and the mercenaries were, and if they would catch up before it was too late. Just as he was cursing the gnome for a fool for sending him, his prayers were answered. In the late evening mists large figures could be seen moving in on the war party. The group spread into a tight formation as the shouts of the Ogres could be heard. "The Scourge Gromlyko, Sir," the head Ranger told the Paladin. "He has burnt many of the farms here. Be ready for Pagan Shaman Magic."

The paladin nodded. "I am always ready for anything, that is why I am still alive."

Tale by Ian Ash.
