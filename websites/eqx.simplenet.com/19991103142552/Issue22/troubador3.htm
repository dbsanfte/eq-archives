ISSUE # 22May 16th - May 22nd

The Troubadour: the passionate bard

"Damsel, it were better to fall to be your prisoner than to many another, for I trow your prison should not be so hard to me as it should if I were taken by the English."

And she answered, "I have seen some no long since that I would you were my prisoner." 		"Would you, " I asked her, "put them in evil prison?"

"Nay," she said, "I would keep them as I would my own body."

I said, "Happy is he that might come into so noble a prison." 		The Book of the Knight of La Tour-Landry, ed. Thomas Wright, Early English Text Society, OS 33 (1868; reprint, Millwood, N.Y.: Kraus, 1973), p. 18.
