ISSUE # 73

May 8th - May 14th


Desolate in Love

Who can want this dark enchanted stranger
        A portent of the coming world
Is she my mirror, my own night splendor
        The face I keep behind my shield
And in her step I hear the calling
        The roar of fanfare, the chants of war
As she breaths, I watch the sun die
        Of shame, its heat so mild and low

With her hands she conducts the armies
        Of bone and scrap, with rusty sword
Her eyes are traps, each dark and endless
        The place I seem to have lost my soul
A voice like music, so magical
        That words like "plunder" sound like song
And in her wake lay crimson puddles
        Casting rainbows sharp as swords

I'm damned for nothing, a crime of chance
        For witnessing her obsidian skin
I'm damned to follow when once I was strong
        A simple pawn whose spells can't win

    Poem by Archanalia
