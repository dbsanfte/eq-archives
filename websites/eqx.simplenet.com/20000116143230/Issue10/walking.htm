ISSUE # 10Feb. 21st - Feb. 27th

Walking in the Light - the story of Indreju Drochnatiar

I write this so that all may know of the great and noble deeds that this brave sole has attempted to complete, and with the hope that all can learn from what happened to this great warrior.

The Inn of the Wayward Traveler was not an exceptional inn by any means, in fact it was really beneath his normal standards. Unfortunately a lone traveler can not always afford better, and there was worse. Luckily there were no rats to be seen, and the food was cooked satisfactory. Indreju sat by himself in a small booth in the corner of the inn listening to the beautiful bard that had a voice that seemed to caress his ears like the touch of silk to skin. She was really the most beautiful human that he had ever seen, long dark hair and skin that looked to be golden from just the right amount of exposure to the sun.

Just when he thought of maybe approaching her to see if she new any songs from his homeland, Faydwer, a large man who had the smell of too much brandy approached the tall High Elf and spoke in a loud slurred speech, "Get out of here elf! We don't want your kind in the city!"

Realizing the large, bulky man was speaking more from his abundance of alcohol that night, Indreju responded in a warm friendly voice, "Friend, allow me to buy you a drink, and then in the morning I shall continue my travels to the north, and leave your fine town behind me." 		Instantly he realized his mistake as the man burrowed his eyebrows and demanded, "You will leave now, or we shall gut your elven hide like the animal you are!" At this, a group of the drunks friends stood up from their positions around a table near the bard, and started to bring their hands down to the hilts of their weapons. Weapons the innkeeper had not required them to remove as he had Indreju when he arrived.

"Maybe we should just gut him anyway? He looks like he might actually be a fight, not like the last foolish elf we killed." One of the drunks friends interjected into what Indreju was considering a dangerous situation. And well he should, he had been required to leave his sword outside with his war horse, and although he still wore his chain armour, this would not stop the group of drunkards.

The others were starting to draw their weapons as the large man turned to talk back to them, "A High Elf fight! Hah! What would they know of fighting, unless it is to use magic and steal babies in the night as all the other elves do." With the large mans attention turned away, Indreju took the opportunity to quickly run to the back of the inn, past the startled innkeep.

With shouts of, "Get the elven bastard!", the drunks immediately started to follow him through the kitchens and out the back door. It was still moderately light out, and the fresh autumn air was already several degrees cooler then that of the inn. The change helped awaken Indreju's senses as he ran to the stables, to find his horse and try and escape the mob of drunks. 		The noise that Indreju heard from behind him enforced the need for him to escape, as his attackers made their way clumsily through the kitchens. Rushing into the barn that this inn called a stable, the rough smell of animal waste assaulted his nostrils with a putrid stench. His horse was sitting in the very back stall, and the stable boys had yet to get to him, being as the inn had been relatively full that night. "Quickly, is there another exit from the bar... I mean stables?" he asked the boy.

"Aye sire, through that sliding door, " the boy answered pointing to a door that was barely visibly only by a crack slightly larger then normal in the wooden planks. Indreju said his thanks and started to throw a boy a small coin when he realized that he left his figure of Fennin Ro back on his table. He could not leave it, and yet could not go back to retrieve it. Cursing under his breath and wondering what possible crime against his gods he could have done to deserve this, he mounted his horse and prepared to leave out the back.

The shouts of the drunks from outside told him that someone was still sober enough to check the stables, and that his time was running out. Vowing to his god to return later to retrieve the artifact, he kick the door open and raced out the back just as he heard the drunks get to the front of the stables. Barely making an escape, and wondering how to retrieve his precious artifact.
