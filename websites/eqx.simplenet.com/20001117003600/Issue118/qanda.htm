Q: 'Ullo, I am a gnomish wizard, and I 'ave a few questions. I heard about all these normal gods, Bertoxx, Solsusek Ro, and them, but who is Xegony and Druzzil Ro and them?

Durand

A: Now there is a typically gnomish question arising out of insatiable curiosity and sigh, this half elf bard is not going to be able to help all that much. A good friend of mine, when I asked about Xegony, described him most succinctly as “a weirdo”! Beyond that, I don’t have any information on him. Sadly, I don’t have a lot more information on Druzzil Ro except that it sounds like he might be related to Solusek Ro and thus might have some connection to elemental characteristics. I would love it if anyone reading the column would help fill in the blanks on these members of the Norrathian Pantheon.

Q: I use 2 different types of wrist armor (leather and black iron), and I notice that the actual wristband displayed tends to switch quite often (by zoning, logging on, etc) from wrist slot 1 and 2 (and vice versa). Which wrist graphic is 'typically' the one displayed? Slot 1 or 2?

Malise of Tunare

A: Tagad, the Pillar of Wisdom, back from a week’s breather from my constant questions, says that the wrist graphic that is displayed is the one on the right, which is slot 2. If the wrist item you want showing is in slot 2, you shouldn’t have a problem. However, a word of warning -- if you loot your corpse it is possible that the order of looting will place the item you had in slot 2 in slot 1 and the item that was in slot 1 will end up in slot 2, thus changing the graphics.

Q: Why have Karana if nature is run by Tunare? There's no need for Veeshan either, cuz birds (which are animals) hang out in the sky. And the Tribunal? Isn't that one of the Marr's subordinates? Oh, and what is Druzzil the god of? And Tallon or Luclin for that matter?

Enderzai

A: Karana, the god of weather and rain, governs an entirely different sphere of Norrath than does Tunare. If Karana did not exercise his will over the weather, the world would be sucked into a massive tempest, and quite frankly, there’s plenty of rain as it is without going to such extremes. Tunare, while of course concerned with watching over nature, is the Mother of All and thus limiting her influence to just the animals takes away from her power of generativity for all living beings. I think Veeshan might dispute your assertion that there is no need for her, as she is the Goddess of the DRAGONS for heaven’s sake, not the birds, and although she lives in the Plane of Sky, her concern is for the superiority of everything draconic. Now, the Tribunal, I’m a bit hazy on but Tagad, aka a Pillar of Wisdom, tells me that the Tribunal is made up of masked beings, who focus on retribution, vengeance and punishment. They are also known as the Six Hammers and I think the Marrs wouldn’t really like to share tea with them very often. Ok, moving on to Druzzil Ro -- that one, as mentioned above, I’m not sure about although with the last name Ro, my guess is there is a connection to Solusek. Tallon Zek, however, is definitely one of the twin sons of Rallos Zek and is in charge of either strategy or tactics, but I’m not sure which and so far, I haven’t wanted to risk my pretty neck to try to find out. Finally, another Pillar of Wisdom, tells me that Luclin is not a “who “ at all, nor a god, but is one of the discovered moons of Norrath. I wonder if there are other moons?

Q: Hello! Biffin hears that you are a wise bard from Deputy Tagil in Rivervale and you might be able to help the Biffin with his problem! Biffin's sister Kizzie Mintopp in Rivervale makes Honeyjum. One day Biffin was stealing the Honeyjum but he was caught by Kizzie! She then took his footcomb! Without his footcomb, Biffin has itchy feet forever! Biffin wonders what you do about itchy feetsies? Biffin's fleas are getting mad at him because he does not have his footcomb. What do you do when your fleas are mad? Biffin does not want them to leave him, they are his friends! Also Biffin wonders if you will grow a beard? No female Elves Biffin has run into will grow a beard for him :( Biffin finds that meanie weenie!

Biffin Mintopp Druid of the 31st season/Veeshan

A: Oh my dear, now see what happens from a little innocent thievery of Honeyjum? I hope you have decided that when you figure a way out of this dilemma with Kixxie that next time you will ask for the Honeyjum politely? However, it wasn’t very nice of Kixxie to steal your footcomb and I think she owes you an apology, a large backpack full of Honeyjum and a pleasant little footcombing session to make up for the inconvenience to you and your fleas. If she adamantly refuses, then in order to keep your fleas, you should be sure not to bathe your feet at all and perhaps you might check Runnyeye for a sharp flat rock that could serve as a substitute comb. Fleas usually don’t last long as potential friends in my vicinity for some odd reason, so I think I’m not the best person to give advice on what to do when they’re mad. ( I think my response might be too violent for a sweet halfling such as you!) So perhaps you might want to sit in a quiet sunny spot and chat with your fleas to calm them down and build their trust again. They would probably enjoy a long story in just the same way I enjoyed your letter. Immensely! Sadly, however, despite the fact I am totally charmed by your name and your demeanor and would love to hear more about your fleas, I cannot grow a beard to persuade you I am not a meanie-weenie. My explanation may seem a bit indelicate, but, simply put, bards with beards end with up with spittle in the little chin hairs after blowing a horn or tooting a flute. Can you imagine anyone who would want to get within 6 feet of me if I had a beard in such condition? *Shudders at the thought!* So you see, I’m not being a meanie to refuse to grow a beard, but rather I’m being a very considerate bard who would simply prefer to avoid grossing you out. Thank you for writing and I hope you can find a comb your fleas will enjoy.

Q: Hmm. This may violate your bias toward mystery, but I am an SK who has been playing for several weeks. I am constantly surfacing random loot items that I have no idea what they are for, and usually, actually, have no use for. I end up auctioning them, but my most frequent auction response is: "I want it, but tell me how much you want to charge...I don't know." I, of course, don't have a clue, and don't want to cheat or be cheated. Is there a good source out there for uses and approximate auction value of non-weapon, non-armor items? (Examples include: Embalming Dust, Utricating Hairs from Spiders, Bloodstones, etc. -- some are worthless, and some, like bloodstones, I can find a use for but no price even after lots of web surfing.

Beraan

A: There are of course, many spoiler sites available for EQ as far as information on various non-weapon, non-armor items goes and you might want to follow the links from EQ Vault or the main EQ website to find some of the sites. However, as far as I know, there is no good source for item prices as the economy varies from server to server and even varies from day to day on a given server. You can, of course, decide to try to sell high and then lower your price if you find no takers, but if you are concerned about a fair asking value, you can wander among the hangers-on in Freeport and get a variety of opinions before trying to sell the item or you can check the various public message boards and ask for opinions on the current sales price of the item you want to be rid of.

Q: I know that a player can have 8 different characters on each server. That tells me that the information for each character is kept on that server. My question is, if I go on vacation and take EQ with me and load it on my brother's computer (not copy the game, just load and play while I'm there). Can I access and play my characters as normal?

Elexwiz

A: Yes, indeed, you can feed your EverQuest addiction even on vacation! Smiles. All of your character information is held on the Verant servers, not your home computer. If you do want to play while on vacation, simply take your EQ disc with you, install it on your brother’s computer (assuming he has the system specifications the game needs) and patch the files for the updates. At 56K the patch might take around an hour, so do allow the time for that. Once you are patched, you’re ready to give your password and log on. A cautionary word here -- it is best to not share your password with anyone and to make sure your brother’s computer is free from hacking viruses that might give away your password to someone looking for it. Once you are on, then have a great vacation playing EQ ! :)
