The Dwarven Bard - part 2

The next morning, in the cold light of day, the idea seemed like career suicide. She spent the day in uncharacteristic silence, but Thagor did not mention it. He took her on a tour of Kaladim, showed her all the changes that had occurred since she'd been there last, and proudly displayed the forge he had established with his brother and the money he'd gathered in the years of his adventurous youth. In the late afternoon, he begged off to go conduct services in the local temple. He was still a cleric, and took his duties to his god most seriously. Alone in his house, Elnore put her head in her hands and tried to think of a way to save her reputation. She would never, if she lived as long as a High Elf, live this one down.

The Politics of Fear - Role Playing a Troll

In my third installment of role-playing races articles I deviate from following Oghma's order of race coverage and have found myself writing from the Swamps near Grobb. Amidst the happy frog people and jolly Fungus men that inhabit the swamps of Innothule, dwell Trolls. Trolls are almost exclusively born to serve Cazic-Thule, God of Terror. Some EverQuest literature refers to Trolls as anarchists, while other classic references would create an image of cunning evil beasts.

    Article by Ian Ash


Trollbreak

Renthiel struggled to clear his head. The pain, instead of focusing his thoughts, was dragging him perilously close to unconsciousness. He couldn't let that happen though. Ever since he was captured and beaten, he had hung on desperately to avoid losing consciousness. His meager few spells would disappear from memory the moment he gave in. He could not let that happen. Not while his troll captors held his precious spell book... if they hadn't destroyed it already.

    Tale by Snicker


A Troll in Kelethin - Part 1

She lay hidden behind thick foliage, blending into the forest like only a wood elf ranger could, her quick elven eyes keeping a close watch on the band of orcs despite the darkness. She almost chuckled it was so easy. The orcs, though quiet when moving through their forest home, had become noisy and boisterous now that they were near their target. A fire burned in the small clearing where nine of them had made camp, heating the bodies of the orcs and making it even easier for her eyes to pick them out. Their grappling hooks and climbing gear plainly stated their intentions to make a raid on the nearby treetop city of Kelethin.

    Tale by Ramos
