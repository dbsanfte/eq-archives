ISSUE # 20May 2nd - May 8th

Venire's Promise - part 2

The leaves of the trees sang a familiar song as I made my way down the path to the Stables. I traveled that path every day to work, and could prove it by the week’s worth of footprints in the dry ground. I always stayed close to the harbor to see if any familiar mariner’s, in from long trips, were ashore. I would often dream of voyaging to far off lands, fighting sea monsters, and hunting treasure in dungeons, rich with treasure. I observed one of the seamen hauling a trunk on his shoulders as he paraded up the plank of a huge freighter. The man stood at least six and a half-foot tall. His shoulders measured as wide as I was tall, his hand the size of a cantaloupe. Veins the size of a tree branch bulged out on his muscular arms displaying years of hard labor. "Balin" I shouted as he waved.

"How goes my little pirate?" Balin chuckled and waved with his free hand. With a shrug of his massive shoulder, Balin tossed the two hundred-pound oak chest to the ground like it was made of balsa. "No time for stories today lad, I’m way behind schedule!" he barked.
