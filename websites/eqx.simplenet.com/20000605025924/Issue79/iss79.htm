What do you mean you can't define Role playing?

Verant has presented us with an interesting quandary. They have proclaimed role playing to be this undefineable, nebulous thing. Yet they are perfectly willing to label their game an RPG and provide switches. Just what is going on here? To kick start things, I'm going to paraphrase the definition of 'role play' from a psychological journal. To wit: Role play is the act of defining an imaginary persona, replete with desires and motivations for the express purpose of acting out an imagined situation. Now while this is obviously intended to be very general, it certainly is applicable. To paraphrase, you create a persona with motivations, and then stick with it. Contrary to Verants objections, they have done just that.

    Article by Ahtenret

Balancing and/or Strengthening the Classes: Caveats to Consider

There has been an extensive and ongoing debate about strengthening and balancing the various classes. The most popular of these, so far as I have seen, surrounds the Warrior class. I have been following this and other discussions for some time now, and have noticed that there are a number of things to be said about it. This article has two parts. First, I would like to summarize some of the points made about strengthening/balancing the classes. Second, and my main focus, is centered on an issue which I have not seen discussed: How strengthening the classes would result in a need to similarly recalibrate monsters' statistics and abilities as well.
