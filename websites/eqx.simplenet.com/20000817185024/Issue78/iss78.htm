Switch off, brain off

Let's talk switch shall we. No, not the once widely discussed PK switch, devised to protect players from the marauding Ultima Online huns. I'm referring to the newly implemented RP (Role-play) switch.

The greatest criticism of the switch seems to be that now that it exists those who do not wish to role-play in this game (which is by definition a "role-playing game") do not have to. Now they just don't turn on the switch and they can talk about any idiotic thing they want. Different game, same old problems. The power gamers think role players are a bunch of idiots. The role players wish those who don't get it would get lost. Is EverQuest Still in Beta?

One of the more annoying trends that has developed over the past several years in PC gaming is the tendency for companies to release products that are, quite simply, not finished. Generally the consensus logic behind this seems to be that problems can just be patched in the future.

Sometimes it’s done by accident … a broken quest in a game, not enough QA ("Quality Assurance"), etc. And sometimes it’s simply done to get a product to market, because development-level companies do have some pressure to get some revenue on the books (especially when it can take years to develop a single project). In 20 years of PC gaming (and a stint working at a software store in college) I’ve seen both, with products running the gamut from small tweaks needed to obviously unfinished products hitting the shelves. In fact, come to think of it, I can just barely remember the last game I bought that didn’t have at least one patch released subsequently. I think it was pong for the Atari.

    Article by Jerkius

Letters to the Editors

Please direct this to Ahtenret:

re: The Day I Almost Quit EverQuest

Please understand, there are no dedicated areas for Role Players, thus there is no mandate that Role Players be accepted. There is only the compromise of mutual tolerance. Be appraised that, while the charmed and amused will /tell you how much they are enjoying your role playing banter, the annoyed and irritated generally will not, if they're sociable, well-adjusted people. You managed to find one who is neither, apparently. My point is, just because a lot of people don't tell you you're annoying, it does NOT follow that you aren't annoying a lot of people.
