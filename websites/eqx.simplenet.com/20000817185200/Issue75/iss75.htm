The Romance of Role-playing

What is it about role-playing that grabs our attention? Why are we all salivating for a game which essentially is no different than a MUD or a good session with a paper and pencil RPG? What is it about this experience which drives us to buy $500 worth of upgrades to our PCs with just the promise of a new online experience?

My first experience with fantasy literature was reading Tolkien's The Hobbit. In it, a small, unassuming homebody finds himself thrown into experiences which would reduce most individuals to quivering heaps. By sheer luck, stamina, and desire, he wins his way to a dragon hoard and witnesses a battle of five separate armies, then finds his way home with his share of the dragon's riches...

Roleplay Isn't a Waste of Time

I could hear the slimed slippery sound of my pursuer's webbed feet whapping against the rock floor of the cave. Then, as I slid across the narrow plank, the noise was closer, now a thwacking on the wooden span high over the murky stream below. My hands pounded my drum harder and faster as if they could speed my feet along faster than the magic of the song was already doing. I could almost feel the sticky tongue glomming onto my sweatsoaked hair streaming behind me. Would I make it? Where was the gate? It had to be there . . .
