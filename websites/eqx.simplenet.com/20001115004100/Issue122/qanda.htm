Q: I am a level 13 enchanter, and I wonder if equipping my pet with a weapon at this point will do anything. If I equip it with a sword, the normal axe is not replaced with a sword, and I cannot give it a second weapon until 24.
Shantak, 13th necromancer

A: From what the Pillar of Wisdom, Kirbol, tells me, your pet is actually quite fussy about those cheap weapons you might be foisting off on him. Let’s say the sword you give him is worth less in coins than what he comes with. Well, then, he’s going to turn up his nose at your offer and totally ignore it. Now, however, let’s say you offer him something more valuable than what he has, and all of a sudden he’ll take your weapon advice and use your gift. Hmmm, sort of like when you go to an elven hall for dinner. If your wine is better, they open it and drink it and if it’s worse than what they have, they hide it away. Since you do have to wait for a second weapon until level 24, you might want to experiment with some 2h weapons to see if the damage is higher and if your pet appreciates them more. (I think someone is going to have to open a pet obedience school -- pets being picky over a weapon is simply out of line!)

Q: I have a question for you. I've played this game for some time now, and written countless stories detailing my characters adventurers, et al. So tell me, is it Teir'Dal, or Tier'Dal? I seen Tier'Dal in a lot of places, but in the game itself I see Teir'Dal often as well. The same applies for Feir'Dal. Also, while on the topic, any idea what Vie means? As in, Koada'Vie, or Firiona Vie.
Thank you,
Whyte Tyger

A: From everything I can tell, Teir`Dal is the correct spelling, the same going for Feir`Dal. I really don’t know the linguistic derivation of Vie -- the King of Felwithe was named Vie (Firiona’s father), so the only thing I can surmise is that at some point the elves visited France in another realm and liked the sound of the French word for “life” so much, that they took it on as a unique surname among elves. Of course, it’s possible I’m wrong on this, but doesn’t it make sense?

Q: This isn't a question, I just wanted to mention a couple of things that were brought up in the last Q&A. First, you seemed to refer to Nillipus as a halfling. Nillipus is a brownie that runs around Rivervale (the halfling town) and steals jumjum from them. Second, someone mentioned using the mouse to look and move around as opposed to the keyboard. I had assumed the vast majority of people do this, but perhaps I was wrong? In my opinion, this is by FAR the easiest way to move around in EverQuest, and allows an easy way to strafe (just use the left/right arrows while holding down the right mouse button), which is important for snatching glimpses of what lies around the corner in that nasty dungeon, or more importantly, seeing where you're going when you're running through a dungeon (as in, away from big nasties). Also, using the mouse to look around gives you much more control than pressing separate keys for up, down, left, and right, plus you control at what SPEED you look around. Great if you run into something nasty and need to turn around in a hurry to run. While I don't want to encourage a Quake mentality in playing, those who play it will recognize the importance of being able to maneuver quickly and accurately in a tight situation.
Wolveryn, Half-Elven Bard of Xegony

A: You are right, I was quite unobservant -- a miserable failing in one so highly paid as a source of information. Thanks for writing and thanks also for talking about the use of the mouse while playing. It sounds like it can be a great way to go.
