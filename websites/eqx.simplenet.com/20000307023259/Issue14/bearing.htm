Bearing Witness

Well, first off I want to thank all of you that told me of their experiences with Ultima Online as it relates to the issues of the lawsuit. I am grateful to you all for helping me out with this.

I suspect that the only way to do this is to list the complaints again, and add your responses. And, of course, my comments. There is no way that I can include all the responses, so I will use the ones that are representative. I also want to be clear that those that responded to my article are certainly not a fair representation of the general population of UO players. So it’s obvious that the responses will be skewed, and this would never get me college credit in a statistics class. 		1) The defendants claimed that the game would be playable "24 hours a day, everyday". The plaintiffs say that it cannot due to frequent server crashes.

Most of you had something to say about this one. For example:

As for not being able to play 24 hours a day 7 days a week, this claim is bogus as well. What the document should have stated is that you are not able to play on a given shard of your choice 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. It is very very very rare that all shards are inoperative at once so a player can still play, just not with his usual character. While this may be frustrating, it in no way violates any of your rights. 		(Sorry to have sounded like a OSI rep in that last paragraph. I hate it when my regular shard is down, but I can still play on any of the others. I would be willing to bet that OSI/EA uses a similar argument as I did).

Each shard undergoes weekly maintenance, but will often crash at unscheduled time as well. My main shard crashes usually at least 3 time a day (it is one of the worst ones). Before (around XMAS) it crashed upwards of a dozen times a day.

Kevan Dettelbach
