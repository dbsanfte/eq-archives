Q:  Okay, right now all the languages I know were taught from other players, everything from Barbarian to Elder Dragon. Okay, I know that Barbarians start out with Barbarian but how does one learn Elder Dragon and such. No one starts out with it, it has to be learned in the game. Same with others like Lizard, Combine, Dragon, Elder Erudiin, Elder Elvish, on and on, how does one learn these besides from a player, maybe only certain races can learn a certain language (Only Erudites can learn Elder Erudin). How do I do it?

Kilaan

A:  As far as I can tell, and as far as the various Pillars of Wisdom mentioned while they were all under fire and not wanting to divert their attention to my questions any more than necessary, languages must be learned by listening to someone whose skill in the particular language is higher than your own. Your skill list will show you all the languages you can learn (by the way, this list is not limited to just the languages you would expect your race and class compatriots to speak) and you may put one practice in any language you want to learn but it isn't necessary. While some early players must have found a way to solo learning a language, my guess is that it was slow and laborious. Now, you can watch for language fairs and classes where friendly folk will get you off to a good start in whichever language you are choosing to learn. Another way to learn a language is to find a friendly expert in the selected language, group with him or her, and while away some friendly hours with some flowing spam. A pack full of vittles and cold drinks is vital for a venture like this to succeed, or so I'm told.

Q:  I am a low level druid. I was wondering about what level could I start to buy armor and how expensive it is. Thanks for your time.

Teawen

A:  You can start to buy armor at any point. Most players when they begin playing will skip cloth armor, saving their coins for a decent weapon or spells and leather armor. Another option is to take up the tailoring skill and collect the skins off creatures you kill to make tattered or patched armor to get you started. After that, of course, armor gets more and more expensive, but as you gain in levels you will also be fighting monsters that drop armor pieces. It's also good to seek out the quests for your class and race, as often you will be able to gain better armor and better weapons than by purchasing them. A cautionary note: Remember that through level 5 your corpse will poof thirty minutes after you die if you haven't looted it. If you have equipment you don't want to lose, make sure you are bound near where you are fighting and remember to put all your spare stuff in the bank. Once you hit level 6, you'll have lots more time to recover your corpse so save that cool piece of equipment your best friend gave you until you hit that level.

Q:  What combo or race/class is allowed to own pets? What are their limitations/costs? How do you go about creating a pet?

Jestak

A:  Druids, shamans, necromancers, magicians and enchanters can all have pets. The pets are summoned with spells and usually some sort of reagent, i.e., bone chips, tiny daggers, gems, etc., is needed to successfully call one. Pets are limited by their owner's class, level and skills, and their forms will be class specific. Pets may be armed but never, ever, ever give one a weapon you cherish as they are notorious for absolutely refusing to return any item you might hand them.

Q:  How do you get married in EQ? Do you get a GM to officiate? Thanks.

Do feel free to write your own ceremony, bribe the guards to ignore out-of-favor races, and hire a good bard to commemorate your nuptial day. Finally, should you decide to share a last name with your bride or your groom, send in a petition for assistance and a GM or Senior Guide will help you with your names.

Q:  Greetings, I am Tenalain, 16th level Warrior from the server of Povar. My question is more of a complaint... Recently I lost quite a large amount of my belongings due to a server going down. Although the GM did announce this, I camped only to find upon my return that some of my possessions had gone. I /petitioned 7 times and only ever got 2 answers, one from a guide the other from a GM saying I would get compensation... I waited 2 hours but didn't get anything. Now I hear that just a few nights ago the GM's were taking control of Sand Giants at the Oasis and getting people to levitate them. I pay my fees expecting help, not this kind of attitude. On another note, don't you think a group should be able to do a /who on anyone in the group and not get an anonymous reply?

Tenalain

A:  Oh dear, it does sound like you didn't get the service promised you and I guess I'd be perturbed too if that happened to me. Although it may seem slightly rude to the more courteous among us, there is a method to try to get help from very busy and/or often absent guides or gms. If you should lose your belongings, do as you did and put in a petition immediately saying what happened and what you lost and ask to speak to the GM. If no GM is on but you do get a response from a guide, ask the guide to tell the GM about your problem, but do not wait for the GM to get back to you. You may ask for the GM's email address and you should definitely ask when the GM will be on the server (the normal GM shift is 2 pm to 11 pm pacific time, five days a week). Then come back every day and petition again to talk to the GM. The keys here are persistence and politeness. I don't know how long the logs are kept but have heard that it is for a fairly short time, so it is important to very civilly pester the GM until you have a response as to whether he or she will be able to restore your items. The good thing about this is that when the GM does get to you and has verified the loss of your items, then it doesn't take too long to get things back to rights again. This might sound like you'll always get your stuff back but in my own experience I know that most times my guildmates and I have lost items in trade window bugs or by accidentally dropping them on the ground and watching them poof, the word has been that those items aren't trackable and thus no replacements. If you get hit by a bug zoning or camping, however, and items disappear or if they morph into something completely different, the guides and GMs usually will try to figure out what happened and help you if they can. I do agree with you on the /who issue. Lots of my guildfellows use the roleplay toggle which makes them anonymous and I don't even know they are on sometimes.

Q:  Maybe you can shed some light on the issues of how players/guilds handle access and play on the planes on different servers. On several servers I have heard that guilds are very protective about plane access once they have broken them. Just because the planes have very interesting loot and are difficult to play, why are they somehow played or treated differently then other tough zones ? These policies on some servers seem to be causing more troubles as greater number of players are high enough to want to venture forth into these new an exciting planes. Any thoughts oh -great bard ?

Relseg

A:  I don't know about the great bard part, but heck I always have thoughts about stuff. (See reference to "the real truth" at the beginning of this column.) Mainly, I think you have written in about one of the most difficult tensions that players face as they rise in level and want to venture into the lairs of the dragons and onto the planes. I've heard that on some servers, a group or a guild will lay claim to an entire zone and threaten anyone who comes there without their permission with blackballing from future trips, refusal to help on corpse retrievals, etc. Other groups don't seem to mind company as much and generally will decide ahead of time how much they will help each other, who gets to loot, etc. Some servers have experienced nasty thievery when a group finally kills a dragon only to lose the loot to someone who snuck in and grabbed it while the group was deciding how it should be split. There have also been issues with a group taking on a dragon or breaking a zone only to die en masse and then a group or guild waiting in the wings comes in and tries to complete the foray gaining the benefits from those who died without giving anything back to them. As far as anyone has ever told me, however, no group owns a zone or an npc and that very general policy creates a major dilemma for anyone called in to mediate the disputes that inevitably arise in the high level zones. Let's say that a guild had been attempting a plane for a long period of time, hours and hours, with many deaths. Another group from half way around the world comes on, fresh and with full inventories of equipment and rushes into the plane, now more than half empty with just a few juicy mobs left to do, and proceeds to kill the highest monsters and take the loot the first group had been working toward for hours. Is this fair? Should there be some sort of intervention? Is this just competition or is it really one group exploiting the work of another? I don't have any answer for you and quite honestly, I haven't seen an official response to this Ghordian knot of issues either. My own feeling is that if a zone takes a major effort to "break" it, there should be some basic protection in place for the group which has done so. Even if they are all dying off, over and over again, I feel that the group should have the right to invite others to help or to keep working away at it on their own. That kind of effort is the stuff of sagas for future generations and it seems to me that it would be fair to find a way to protect it. Does this mean I think the same rules should be applied to the non-plane or non-dragon zones? Nope, because those zones, as difficult as they may prove depending on what a group is trying to accomplish, don't take that initial special effort to get started. I sure would love to see the players and Verant work to clarify this problem before every high level player is furious with every other high level player over an experience in the planes. It seems like developing a policy on high level areas that we can all understand would really help.



Thanks all for writing in. I look forward to hearing from you soon.

Nep
