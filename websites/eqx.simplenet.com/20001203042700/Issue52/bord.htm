ISSUE # 52

December 12th - December 18th


Bord's Gait

A dwarven one called Bord TwoAxe
Was stayed near Qeynos-town.
When moanings did his ears fall on--
A bardling casted down.

"What fate met I in Commons East,
while hunting with a friend.
A bear, so great, and griffon too
Didst send me to my end!"

The bard went on with tale of woe,
"And now my pain is great-
For now I be in Qeynos-town,
Not close to Freeport's gate!"

Bord did hear this sad, poor tale
And in his heart found pity.
"Come bard, and follow me this day,
I'll lead to Freeport city."

So Bord prepared the journey sack
Of muffins and of water.
For bard was penniless and sore,
After his own slaughter.

The two dashed east from Qeynos Hills
with Selo's song were speeding.
And soon were met by Bord's allies,
A warrior and a halfling.

The warrior tall with name of Bear,
Lent strength unto their fight.
The halfling quick bore magic strong
And hastened them with light.

The two, now four, drove eastward then
Through vast Karana plains
To river serpentine where Bord
Turned north and ran again.

By river's edge they journeyed long
Below the sight of foe.
For certain death awaited them
If on the plains they strode.

Past bandits, gypsies, griffons too
The band did keep their pace,
Till ancient bridge of wood and steel
Did rise before their face.

They crossed and ran though light woodlands
And dodg-ed cyclops' paw.
Then ran northeast to canyon deep,
Into Beholder's maw.

A minotaur did block the way
But Bord he had no fear.
A mighty dwarven cry he hurled
That deafened mino's ear.

Fast Bord then led them right and right
Across both rock and slide.
Till goblin's entrance cave was seen
Then Bord pressed on inside.

In darkness bardling quaked and feared
While goblins cast their spells,
But Bord's stong hand reached back and pulled
The bard on through those halls.

A misty light broke caves despair,
And oh! What vision blessed!
A vale were haflings walked about--
A vale where we could rest.

Then Bord stood tall in dwarven pride
The journey nearly done,
And caught the bard with mirthful eye
And said, "I leave you, son."

"The trip you make has not much left--
one danger there remains.
The giants, orcs, lions, and gnolls
Of Western Commonlands."

The bard took little ease from Bord
For giant's foot he feared
Would crush him quick as had the claws
Of griffon his flesh teared.

But Bord he laughed and clapped bard's back
"My friends will see you through.
Tween bear and half, they know the path
You need me not, Tis true."

Reluctantly the bard went on
Ahead of half and bear
"Watch out!" they cried when giant spied,
but bard was almost there.

Ran, and ran, and ran he did,
The bard was fast as wind.
So did he find his corpse to loot,
With help from kindly friend.

Bord, and bard, and Bear, and half
Did journey on that day.
And gave their time in generous port,
To ease an early grave.

So learn from Bord, you heros bright!
The kindness bravery brings.
And then you too may find your name
In songs that Jithri sings.


    Tale by Jithri, Bard and Wanderer
