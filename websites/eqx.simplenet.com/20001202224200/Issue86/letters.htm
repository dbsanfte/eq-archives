Dear Oghma,

I often sit down, when I am a bit tired of playing EverQuest, and read your articles. Most of the time I agree with what you write, and so also in the last article in the 85th edition if the EQExpress. EverQuest is my third online game so far, UO I played for one week, before putting it away, whilst Realm, with it's simplistic graphics kept me going for quite some time. But, as all games do, Realm got old, but there is ONE thing that I still admire both the Realm staff and the staff of Sierras next game, Middle Earth: Comunication. Verant lacks much of this. Why you might wonder. They have frequent interviews and they post updates every time they even just added a rock to EverQuest's landscape... but what they don't do, is when small things go wrong.

Last week, a 1000+ (maybe more) players were kicked off EverQuest and were unable to log in, the game stated that our accounts were expired. Having played Realm, I stayed generally calm, and tried to calm the EQVault message boards down too, saying it would probably be up soon and that the Verant staff were probably working on it right now. But what I had expected to happen, didn't happen. Nobody from Verant came down from their pedestal to tell us what was going on, or later when the server was back up, what had happened. THIS is what annoys me about Verant, it has to affect the WHOLE of the population of Norrath, or they can't be bothered to give an explanation on what happened or an estimated time of when the problem will be fixed. I personally remained calm, since I have been through such problems before, but some people went totally insane, calling Verant and it's employees names. Verant has one of the worst customer service (next to UO) I have ever seen, and that I think is appalling and helps to ruin the game for some players.

Noor Stormweaver

Dear Noor,

I haven't personally had any problems with the guides or GM's but I know many who unfortunately have. I think Verant's Customer Service does need work, and I certainly hope that the recent request for more GM's and Guides will address this. I do think they do a lot of communication with players and I think they are very receptive to players comments and suggestions, as witnessed by the recent warrior and rogue enhancements, the call for more GM's, frequent quest updates/fixes, and the opening of two race war servers. I'd like them to be a little more up front when they make a mistake, but I'll have to admit I'd be leery of admitting to the pottery sketch blunder. The silence from them is maddening, though. I won't agree that it's appalling, but it needs work. It feels like you speak from the frustration of a player like myself, who loves the product but is frustrated with some of the issues.

When Brad did eventually come on to the chat servers after the TZ problems and said "I'm sorry", he did an excellent thing, and those customers who weren't complete idiots recognized that. You probably had left by then, as many did. Some of the things you were waiting for, acknowledgement of the problem and what was being done to address it, were later posted on EQV and EQSS. The Tallon Zek problem was addressed on alt.games.everquest and posted on EQV on Saturday, July 31st. The account expired problem was posted about somewhere, but should have been made into patch note, as those who had their accounts messed with should know right away. I'd like to comment a little about the guide/GM issue, though.

I know how hard it is to be a guide, since I have done similar jobs elsewhere. On Tuesday, August 6th, a post from Smedley on EQV showed an example of what a guide has to deal with, but I don't think it went far enough. If a player contacts a guide they are almost always already very upset, and, to be honest, feeling incredibly self-righteous. Guides have to approach every customer patiently, thoughtfully, and with humor and compassion EVERY TIME. I work in a field with good customer service, and know what it is like to be out on the front line dealing with complaints. (A slight brag here - since I became a library director, my library has gotten "excellent" customer service comments every month since I arrived, and has never gotten a rating below that "excellent".) It is very difficult to be a guide or GM, and I'm not sure I'd have the stick-to-it-iveness that many of the guides have shown - remember, a guide gives up 10 hours a week of play-time for the dubious advantage of having the power to help frustrated and angry players while not being about to re-imburse or directly affect the game itself. It's a volunteer position, and as such does not have a lot of the tools that a GM can handle.

The Guides need every bit of support that Verant can give them. They need to know more about what can happen every patch, and what every patch contains. They need to get the training which allows them to keep their balance when everyone freaks out, and there needs to be enough guides to handle the load. The hiring process for guides is necessarily strict, as you need someone who is both ethical, knowledgeable about the game, an excellent communicator, even-keeled, and willing to give up 10 hours a week of their own time to help others. If an applicant is let in lacking one of these qualities, the whole program suffers. It's not an easy task at all to find and hire good guides.

This reply wound up being longer than I thought it would, sorry! To boil down my response, I guess you can say: I agree with you, I hope it will be addressed, and letters like yours are good for the game. Thank you.
