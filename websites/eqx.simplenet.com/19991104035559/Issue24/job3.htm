I Need A Job!

The Interview

Hopefully based on how you write your resume, and how well you do your homework, you'll get a few interviews. Before you meet for your interview it's important that you prepare for it. It's worthwhile to know a little about a company before you visit them. I was usually asked, "Why do you wish to work for us?" or "Do you know what we do here?"

So before your interview call the company's main number, and ask the operator if their company has a web site. Web sites are a great place to gain knowledge about a particular business. If there is no web site, ask the operator a few questions about the company. The only dumb question is the one that never got asked. 		An interview is not all about what you have done, or what you can do. This is a misconception and I'm living proof of it! When you're sitting in front of that interviewer you're selling a product. You're selling yourself. It is important that you present yourself as a "you just got to have" commodity. This is not something you can learn over night. You need to practice. If you can find strangers to practice this on, do it!

Let me list a few things I look for in an interview.

1. You're personal appearance. What you're wearing, posture, how you present yourself. Sit up straight. Look and feel important, but don't act cocky. Feel relaxed! Trust me, they are just as nervous as you are. It might show as impatience or some other form of body language, but I can assure you its pure nervousness. 		2. When given the opportunity to describe yourself do it! Talk about yourself until he/she shuts you up. My last interview I actually talked about my hobbies and myself for over twenty minutes. I had all three of them on the edge of their seats. They were my audience and I was achieving my best performance ever, and all this time not having anything to do with qualifications of the job. They are more interested in you as a person.

3. If asked a situational question like "What is the most difficult situation you have ever been faced with, and what steps did you take to solve it?" answer in detail. These are questions targeted at finding out how well you solve problems. They don need to have anything to do with the trade you are applying for. It could be how you solved a particular task or assignment in school. They want to see how you solve problems. This is very important for a help desk position.
