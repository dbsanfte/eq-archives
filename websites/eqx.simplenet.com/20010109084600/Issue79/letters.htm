I was sitting here enjoying the EQX issue 78 when I came across Ariel's reply to Ahtenret's posting in the "Letters to the editors". I'm stunned to read her (his?) take on role-playing. I had to read it all over again to make sure I had not misinterpreted the whole thing, but no. I'm truly amazed by the fact that someone thinks that role-playing is "inappropriate" or a waste of scroll space, and this in a role-playing game! That's right, it's a role-playing game, this is not my statement, but the Verant's, and since they made the game I assume they know what they wanted it to be. I'm not saying that everyone should role-play, I'm not a die hard role-player myself, but I think at least there should be no criticism directed to people who choose to do so. After all, the game was designed with role-playing in mind. The suggestion about a channel for role-playing is absolutely absurd. If anything and that idea isn't a good one either, there should be a channel for people who like to talk about stats of the different weapons, spoilers for quests etc. Personally I think you should take all of those discussions into /tell if you so desperately need to know if you really have to best armor money can buy.

I would also like to address another thing; the role-play switch. Hot topic indeed it seems, and I get the feeling this new feature is not appreciated at all. Well, my short take on the whole thing is that in a game where most people can't tell the difference between a role-played troll and a genuine psycho, this might be semi-good solution. The fact that I can now maybe (I say maybe because I've found that many people have it turned on, even though they don't role-play) find someone to adventure with that can appreciate role-playing. It's in indeed a sad day when people feel that there is a need for such a feature in an RPG-game, but I think with the current situation, having the switch might better than not having it.

Some of you might think I am "true" role-player, but I am but an apprentice in the area. I try to role-play when the opportunity is given, but you can catch me from time to time asking someone what level they are in /say. (I try to minimize this, but it slips from time to time =) The few role-playing situations I have been involved in have been great fun, and every time I log on it's with the hope of meeting interesting characters. To all of you that haven't tried role-playing I strongly suggest you try it, not because I say so, but because you might find a new, fun dimension of Everquest.

Thanks for your time,

Kevarien,
Half-Elven rogue

I like to roleplay my druid, but find that I can't because I'm too afraid to venture anywhere or if I dedicate a week to learning another trade I'll fall way too far behind. I just wish verant had allowed those of us who are casual gamers a way to get a sense of accomplishment in leveling instead of making those of us who only get to play maybe 20-30 hours in a week have to spend a year or more getting up just to lvl 20 something, and let us be able to roleplay. I mean sure I'd like to have to level, but I wish there was some sort of timer or something that would allow those of us who play less a week to be able to have fun by getting more exp for a kill. then when we start playing more in a week we must revert to normal growth patterns. Good question. I think Everquest is an outstanding game. It's well worth looking into. What you have here at EQX, are a group of people who are dedicated to pushing for an even better game. We come across as malcontents sometimes, but we have the interest of furthing Roleplaying at cost of possibly losing the PK crowd, if it comes to that.

The biggest detriment of the game currently is that you'll find folks hunting down something of comparable level to themselves and killing it for no reason. No "Let the damned beasts be slain for defiling our lands". More like "Gnoll. Get 'em before someone else does."

That's not to say that the environment is bereft of Roleplaying. I've had a great time with many of the folks I've met. And unless you turn on your PK switch, the PKers can't hurt you. I encourage you, and any others who are curious but who have not played, to give the game a try.
