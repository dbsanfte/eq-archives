Movie Review

 

Well, Sony has finally given us our first real taste of what EverQuest will be like.  All the comments about Sony's EverQuest Movie have been raves!  Finally,   after   all   the discussions, and comments from the EverQuest development team, we have something we can sink our teeth into.

The movie is really a grouping of short clips; basically, showing the same character (except in the case of the dark elf thrown in) moving through various terrains, a couple of spells cast, with a few monsters also included to give us a glimpse as to what we will have to face.  The movie finally ends with a battle between this same character and a Red Dragon!

I must say that the movement was superb!  Smooth, steady, with some nice,     well     rounded,   character

limb movement as well.  In addition, I found the monsters to have nice definition, and movement also.

Finally, the Red Dragon at the end was superb!  This is what a dragon should look like...  Roughly 4 times the height of the player character (Ed: I think it was more like 20 times the height), and I would guess at 10-15 times the mass!  Now, I have heard some complaints that the dragon was too easy to kill, only taking 1 sword strike, and a couple of fireball blows, however, as was also pointed out, what was displayed in the movie was actually only the opening blow, and then the death strike.   This sequence was edited down to keep the movie size as small as possible.

All in all, I would have to say that the EQ   development   team   did  a phenomenol    job   with   this  movie,

and the timing couldn't have been better, only a day or so after the projected release date of the closed beta applications.  These clips went a long way to keep the large following that EQ has already developed happy, and content, at least for a little while longer.

Some of the things that I think we can look forward to in the next movie are much more detail on spell casting, and a larger variety; more depth, and variety on the monsters to be encountered; and possibily some PC and NPC interaction.  These are the issues that will keep the EQ fans appeased until the open beta release.
