Razor's "Free Entertainment 101"

My son, knowing what I was about to do, was having trouble keeping a straight face, so I shove him in the direction of "Parts" to get his memory. The clerk motions me to a line of computers. Each computer has fancy screen savers with fancy twirling logos. Which of course are all password protected so you have to ask for a salesperson (and also fun to hack, and change the configurations around.. but that's another article). He points to the first computer in the lineup.

"Now this is one of our big sellers. It's a three hundred-megahertz Pentium computer with one hundred and twenty-eight megabytes of RAM, four gigabyte hard drive, thirty-two speed CDROM, tape backup," he says with a snaky salesman grin.

"Umm. Is that fast?" 		He looks at me for a second, as if something inside of his head clicked. I think I could almost read his mind.

Oh this is a sucker.

"This is the fastest computer on the market. Only I'm afraid this might be too much speed for you. Most people can't use this computer because it's just too fast."
