ISSUE # 20May 2nd - May 8th

Letters to the Editors

This is in response to "Will Everquest Succeed?" and I couldn't help but get in my two cents worth...

The PK switch - I believe that in the end this function will merely provide sanctuary for players until they feel that they have risen above newbie status. Eventually, unless you are heavily into merely chatting and role-playing, the player is going to have to enter the fray to fully experience the game. So even though you won't get wasted on your first day in by some rampaging PK'er, how aggravating will it be to have built yourself up to a comfortable level only to have the same thing happen many many game hours later? Unless you're willing to devote yourself to a lifelong commitment of associating will non-PK players only, the PK issue is still alive and well.

[Continued...] 		Server Caps - I sang Sony's praises when I heard about this decision. However, now that I think back on this, I realize that this could bring up an annoying issue... what if you've found a server you like (either for ping or attitude) and have settled in nicely, only to find that the server is usually already at the cap when you logon to play? I'm curious to know how Sony is going to deal with this...

The one consolation I have is the knowledge that Sony is the sort of company that focuses on quality, and if anyone can do it they can.

libra

btw - great job on EQX, I just finished catching up on all the back issues. 		I just finished catching up on my reading of EQX. Of particular interest was Issue 16's article: Beta Testing 101 Article by Wynn Orill. This article was an outstanding guide for all upcoming Beta Testers for EQ. Sadly, the disappointing failure of UO was caused in no small part by the lack of the developers to continue beta testing the game before final release. Of no less importance was the failure of many players to adequately test the game, rather than using the Beta as an opportunity to play the game before charges were implemented. Wynn has an excellent point that WE the Beta Testers for EQ will have a profound impact on determining the future success of the game. He brought up points that I had already considered in my thoughts about the Beta, but he brought up many that I hadn't even dreamed of! I urge you to seriously consider running this article again (perhaps many times!) Especially as we receive word that the Beta has, or will soon, begin. I don't have to stress the importance of good solid Beta testing to most of the EQ community, but perhaps everyone can learn a little more about what a good Beta Tester should be trying to accomplish. More importantly, those who will be new to Beta Testing will certainly gain a great deal of insight into what their role in the Beta should be. Keep up your fine work.

Cheers,
Kel'anth
