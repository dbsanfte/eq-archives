Q: Dear Nepenthia, I was surprised to read that your Pillars of Wisdom could not help you with the question of how someone might go about gaining mastery in a language without divesting one's fortune to his trainer in the process. For indeed, the Gods have seen fit that this not be the case. As a humble and usually financially strapped cleric of Karana, I recognized early on my need to learn an assortment of languages. After all, how can I bring Karana's message of redemption to the trolls of Innothule if I cannot speak their language? And of course, as a cleric, there was no way I could afford the funds my trainer was demanding for lessons. It turns out that a teacher of a language may improve the student's skill in that language up to two points higher than the skill level the teacher currently possesses in that language. Using this fact, a pair of linguists may teach each other any language they desire. The process proceeds thusly: First, two linguists with a skill of 5 in a language (awful) give each other lessons (spam each other) until one or the other sees two skill point improvements. At this time, they both take a break (zone) and begin anew. The student becomes the teacher until the former teacher has gained a skill level two points higher than the current teacher. After another rest period (zone), they switch roles once again. This process can be time consuming with the required rest period (zoning) but it works. Eventually both parties will have mastered the language in question. I hope this tidbit of information will help any others who have an interest in languages but do not possess the funds necessary to pay to their trainer.
May the winds of Karana whip through the skirts of many a half elven bardess very soon,
Searle Hearthfire, Cleric of Karana, The Nameless

Q: Subject: EQX: Some answers to #122 that you might have missed. Tithing in Neriak Question:
Tithing is the time honored tradition of giving money to the church. In this case, Lokar To'Biath (The NPC in Neriak 3rd gate who likes Red Wine) wants you to Tithe 70gp to locate the Scribes of Dal. This money is to be used to quench the thirst of Lokar...err...the Thirst of Innoruuk. This tithe makes you more much popular with the regular residents of Neriak (That's because giving money makes people like you more). Learning Languages: Languages can be learned many ways. One way is to go to your trainer and put one point into the skill. The trainer teaches languages based on your level. You get to learn a new language every few levels you go up. Another way is to find another player who wishes to share that language with you in GROUP or TELL messaging. (SAYs do not work). Another way to learn a language is to sit in the camp of NPC's that speak the language that you want to learn. (I learned ORC by sitting invis in their camp for 20 minutes) Btw, this is VERY dangerous. I would hate to be the first person who learned Elder Dragon. But to answer the question about getting to 100+. You do not need to go to your trainer and spend hundreds if not thousands in plat to get a language to master. All you need to do is group with a friendly person who has the same level of language as you and spam each other. If you are not the same level the highest you can get is to the same amount as the teacher. If you ARE the same level then you can spam them to 2 points above what you know. I assume that from listening to the language long enough you will pickup the diction better and become a better master at it. Then you both zone and do it again. If you both start out at 1, then after many sessions and 50 zonings you can both be at 100! Phew! What a job! But the only thing it costs you was water and food. Note: Since languages don't have a skill number associated with them (Like fighting skills) it takes a little practice to equal out your skills before you can start teaching at the 2 point zone.
Forbin, Channeller on Mith Marr
