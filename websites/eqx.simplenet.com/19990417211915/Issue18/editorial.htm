ISSUE # 18Apr. 18th - Apr. 24th

EDITORIAL

Welcome to Issue 18!

First and foremost: Nice new site, Sony. I like it. And all the new information and screen shots are wonderful as well. You have again succeeded in grabbing our interest and giving us more to consider while we wait for EverQuest.

As I sit and write this, I am watching the editors cut of BladeRunner. And as Roy destroys his maker, I think of EverQuest. Why? Because I’m supposed to be writing an editorial, that’s why. But also because we, as the players in EverQuest, have the opportunity to destroy the game.

Despite the continuing sales of Ultima Online, there are many that say it is dead. That its children have destroyed it, taken all its beauty and its joy. Perhaps they are right. But certainly there have been more complaints about that one game than any other that I am aware of. It is my opinion that the game designers are ultimately responsible for her death. They made some poor choices, for reasons that may or may not justify them.

But the players are not without fault here. They have allowed themselves to be the unwitting tools in the destruction of something that they enjoyed. I can lay no blame with them, however. They simply acted as they chose. Nobody in authority was there to teach them. Nobody explained the idea to them.

Now Roy is hunting Deckard through the abandoned apartment complex. Roy taunts his prey. He wants to make certain that Deckard is suffering. He wants to know that someone else is feeling his pain. Pain brought on by those that made him. Those that decided he would only live four years. And all Deckard can do is try to survive.

Now that’s a scene that I would love to see in EverQuest. A battle, not only of strength, but of morality and willpower. Roy is resigned to his death. Deckard refuses his. "Quite and experience to live in fear." With that line, Roy gets the chance to explain why he is putting Deckard through all of this pain. And then, in the end, he does the human thing, and lets him live. He allows his story to affect a life, without having to destroy it.

To bring a tale to life, that’s role playing. Certainly the genre requires conflict. Every good tale requires conflict. But it does not require killing.

If pitting your fighting skills against another is what you want, there are many games out there designed for that. Play them. I do. But when in a role playing game, try the role playing idea. You may actually enjoy it.

Hey, Slingblade is on! I wonder... :)

Thanks for reading.

   Absor
