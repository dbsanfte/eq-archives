ISSUE # 10Feb. 21th - Feb. 27th

Letters to the Editors

Concerning Death and Everquest

I just read the article "News Worthy" in EQX Issue 9 and I would like to respond to it.

I see that the game Everquest has Gods in it and that provides the perfect opportunity to incorporate death and death penalties in a role-playing sense. This is how I see it. Every God has a belief (Death, Decay, Life, Arts etc.) and their own agenda. With that said every God also tends to favor a particular "Class" or maybe a "Race". 		When a character is made, a player is allowed to "choose" a God for their character. The character must then stay within the guidelines of the Gods belief and agenda to stay in favor. While that character is following the God and is in his/her favor, resurrection is 100% guaranteed upon death. If the character should ever fall out of favor (or not have a God at all) then resurrection has a percentage chance of failing and the character is dead forever. This allows another form of role-playing in the game and it also would help to keep the game under control. If done correctly it can also keep rampant player killing down but not eliminated; the God system would have to be setup correctly. When a character dies and is resurrected he/she should lose 1/10th of their levels rounded down, and all items that they are currently carrying. This allows a character of 1st through 9th level to die without losing levels and 10th through 19th to lose one level etc. This causes fear to step in (but only to a point) to keep the "player" under control. It also allows new characters the ability to flourish and possibly survive for an extended period of time.

I hope Everquest has a death penalty that will keep players from attacking anything that walks or breaths. This is not role-playing, it is action playing, and does not belong in a ROLEPLAYING game. Don't get me wrong, there should be lots of action in the game but the players should have to at least think about the possible penalties should their character die.

Thanks for listening, Leanthar
