Q: Quick roleplaying question - would a wise-cracking, ladies' man Monk be considered inappropriate based on EQ's history? I'm relatively new to Norrath and cannot find any concrete evidence either way. The character would still stay true to a lot of Monk cliches - he would just be a lot more vocal about them.
Joe Z.

A: I don’t have a hard and fast answer for you on this one but my interpretation would be as follows: Every class will have members which follow the ideals of the class slavishly, i.e., in this case perhaps it would be expected that a monk would naturally be more introverted, focused on his or her causes, the silent solitary type, etc. However, I can’t think of rules that would preclude anyone from roleplaying the character as an iconoclast or rebel or even as a kind of a fringe member of the class. It makes sense to me that a monk could indeed be a ladies man, even if his guild had tried to make him repress such ideas, and it makes sense that a ladies’ man would be a more extraverted and vocal person. Roleplaying in Norrath, from what I have seen, is rarely highly stylized and I know of no set of rules that have to be applied to developing a character. Most people who roleplay try very hard to bring life to their character as an individual rather than trying to match up with some standardized version of proper roleplay. Of course, I could be saying this because I happen to find ladies’ men charming and would hate to lose even one such from the lands, but such is not the case. Rather, to me, it would be extremely boring to greet each monk on the road and find that their guilds had brainwashed them so deeply that no remnant of individuality, much less flirtation, remained. So, I say -- go for it. Play your monk the way you imagine him and enjoy bringing life and breath to him.

A: The Macro Issue
Macros can be highly effective in making your character fight better, and especially work better in teams. Here are some answers and tips.

To hotkey any button simply left click and hold the button until it releases and becomes your cursor. Move it over to your hotkey pad and left click over the slot you want it to be in and it will drop off your cursor. This works for spells, social commands, and inventory slots.

Spells - I use my hotkey pad for spells and organize them by purpose. I have my "combat pad" which currently holds the spells I find myself as a cleric using most often in combat. I also have a "Buff pad" which has all the buffs I cast during combat downtime. Just right click and hold on the spell from the 8 spell slots after you have memorized it, and bring it down to a hotkey pad. I also have on my combat pad the single most important grouping social button you can create - /assist.

Social commands - From your windowed screen, you will see buttons on the middle right which say "Combat" "Main" and "Socials". Click on the socials and you will see a few rows of buttons that do some commonly used commands like AFK. These buttons can be hot-keyed, and also can be edited to create your own socials. You can put any /command in there and make a button of it.

To create a social macro, right-click on a social button that you wish to edit or change. In the middle "text" box will appear a multi-line form with a heading on top. Edit the heading to label the button so that you will be able to distinguish it from your other buttons, and label it, of course, with something you can remember. For example, I label my /assist key "Assist". Below the heading you can enter a multiple number of commands, one per line. /say, /g, /assist, /who all friends, all are good candidates for macro buttons. Each line is just that - one line. The text doesn't wrap. Also Each line is short - keep it brief, and use more than one line do accomplish a long action.

Here are some of the more useful uses of the social macro:

Pet commands - I have a mage who has a complete bank of pet commands. When I need to, I switch to the pet command bank and hit the appropriate key, such as the "PBO" key (/pet back off). I have the "PA" key (/pet attack) on my combat bank of buttons.

Assist - /assist targets and attacks what your target is attacking. Sounds a bit confusing, but here is how I use it. When grouped I usually pick out one person to be the attacker. That person selects what the group is to kill by attacking it. Once the fight starts, I target that person using the appropriate Fkey and then hit the assist button. If the whole group does the same, we are all attacking the same creature. Once that foe goes down, the targeting party member attacks another foe and we all switch to that one.

When not grouped and someone is running past me yelling for help, I try to click on the person and heal them. However, sometimes I target their attacker by accident, so I just hit the assist key and now I am targeting the person attacked, i.e., the poor schmuck running past me. It's much quicker that running and trying to click on some guy fleeing for his life. If that person then turns to fight and I pitch in to help, I can monitor the character's health bar by flicking back and forth between our mutual foe and the foe's target - the character.

Multi-line social commands -Here is one of my favorites:
/say My God, what does a dwarf have to do to get some peace and quiet around here?
/em grumbles.
/em hunkers down to contemplate the ways of Brell
This is my meditate in the Crushbone throne room button J. You can make multi-line social by merely adding commands to each line. Creative players make the combination of emotes, says, and commands fun. My /assist hotkey has a /say I will help you, my friend!

A friend of mine has a complicated sword drawing and attacking emote which is pretty cool.

Combat commands - for leaders and those of you that group often, common group messages such as "Out of mana", "ZONE", "RUN", and "Heal please" are great hotkeys.

Inventory slots - Inventory slots can be right click, hold, and dragged just like any button. This is the simplest method to switch weapons - you just must make sure that you keep that slot open all the time for the switch. Make that inventory slot a hotkey, and hotkey your held weapon also. It's a more complicated dance for two-handed vs. one-handed switches, I haven't puzzled it out yet, but I know it can be done. Another thing handy is the use of a inventory slot for potions.

Q: I have a question about Social Hot Keys. I have been trying to make a social with more than two lines in it. Something like Line1: /s Tag your It.Line2: /s Can't tag the butcher. But when I run this hot key, it only is showing the first line. (I have checked both mine, and my roommates screens). Is there anyway to get multiple lines in these hot keys to work? Another question on the same topic... Is it possible to ad a target to the phrase? Eg) /s Tag you're it <>translates into: Ranini says "Tag you're it Nepenthia"
Ranini

A: Ok, I spent a considerable time last night near the orc lift in Kelethin, driving passersby and a very cute gnome "socials" partner crazy with my attempts to find some way to use the social keys as you have described. As you discovered there is no way to set multiple lines and have more than one of them show up using /tell or /say. There is also no way to wrap a long sentence to more than one line. It's the Anti-Bard League at work again! So to do as you would like, you have a couple of options. If you are trying to use the multiple socials with someone not in /emote range, you will have to do each phrase as a separate social and then set each social to a hot key. When they are set, you simply click the hot keys in order as fast as you can. Answering your second question, I could find no way to use target with a socials button except if you are using a pre-programmed emote. In that case, if you target a player and hit a socials hotkey or button, it will name the player as part of the emote. One thing to remember: If you are within /emote distance, you can have each line of a social box with a separate emote and they WILL all show up to those around you. Thus you could type: Line 1: /em hollers, "Tag you're It!" followed by Line 2: /em notes, "Can't tag the butcher" and put both phrases on one hotkey. You can also use preprogrammed socials on hotkeys and set off 5 at once that way.

Q: Hi Nep! Just found out about your column. I am wondering how to set up my hotkeys to switch between my weapons and my instruments quickly...is there a way?
Thanks,
Oriley, 8th Level Bard, Mithanial Marr server

A: Yes there is a way and I must acknowledge it was taught to me way way back in beta by an old crotchety bard who would rather keep his nose tucked in a book of lore than answer 3948495 questions from me. However, he did graciously teach me how to use my hotkeys and I pass this learning along to you. First, the best thing to do it make sure most of your inventory keys and your melee slots are empty. It just makes it easier. Then choose the bank of hot keys you wish and click on melee one to slide the slot over to the top left position on your hot key bank. Next click on the melee two slot and move it over. Then pick four numbered slots from inventory and fill the rest of your bank with those slots. Once that is done, put your weapon in the first melee slot. Put your instruments in the inventory slots that match the numbers on your hot key slots. You will then see your weapon in the top left corner and your instruments in the lower boxes. To move it, click on the weapon, move it and click on the instrument of choice, exchange them, and move the instrument up to the second melee slot (where instruments insist on going). Others may have even better ways to handle this but I find that I keep my most used spells on hotkey bank 1, my instruments and weapons on hotkey bank2, and a set of travelling hotkeys on hotkey bank three. The one drawback of this arrangement is that if you have a light, water, food and a backpack, there isn't too much more room for carrying stuff. But, what the heck, bards travel lightly.

Q: I often wondered if there was a simpler way to switch between your "hotboxes" or whatever they're referred to. It seems really difficult to keep moving my mouse over to the little arrow in the middle of battle or if I needed to say something I put in a macro.
Thanks.
Dadien

A: Yes, there is a simple keyboard method to access the different banks of hotkeys. If you hold down the shift key and type 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6, you can gain quick keyboard access to each of the sets of hotkeys. I know I have a set of hotkeys for quick access to useful bard songs, another set up for melee weapon and instrument changes, and a third one set up for traveling, so the shift key is definitely a help.
