ISSUE # 24June 1st - June 5th

A View to Temptation

"Oh be quiet before I get sick" laughed Serat. "It may be true that occasionally the prey hesitates, but I like to think it is out of fear and respect and not for these feminine trappings."

My cousin, Darius, Turlas Delk the innkeeper, one of his servants and many of the merchants had remained throughout the events. Beside Darius was Hameil, an apprentice merchant. Hameil was having trouble with his guild because of his wandering ways and lack of study and it was doubtful that the merchants would promote him. 		Hameil could not move. Darius prodded him. "We must now leave to inform the guards of these warriors."

"She will do us no harm" said Hameil.

"I am going, now!" urged Darius. "And for yourself and for the sake of my sister you should be off as well." 		She was beautiful. More beautiful than any women Hameil had ever imagined. In the dim lighting he could discern the reflection of the fire dancing on her features. The strong defining lines of her face were offset by the smooth curves of her breastplate armor. Beneath the steel defense could be seen a silken shroud. That Hameil was stricken became obvious.
