The Dwarven Bard

In the mountains of Butcherblock, a cool breeze arose, and blew north across the land. It ruffled the long grass, rustled among the leaves of the trees, tangled the beards of the dour guards that manned the lonely wayposts. It passed over the hills and rocky outcroppings, carrying the scent of coal and metal working from the forges, and dipped into valleys to pick up the scent of the wild flowers and the tang of the fiery breath of drakes. The breeze blew north, and swirled into the pass to Greater Faydark, where a lone Wood Elf stood, gazing across the distance toward Kaladim, mighty home of the Dwarven race.

    Tale by Aklar


I came - I tried to conquer - I had to share

A long time ago when I was a child my dad read to me the book, "The Hobbit." At the age of seven, I was transported to the world of dragons, wizards and high adventures! This is where my love was born for the world of fantasy books. At the age of eight my dad bought me a great game called "The Dark Tower," again it was involving adventure and fighting and victory of conquering the Dark Tower! By the time I was 12 years old, I was soon involved with a cousin making endless maps of dungeons and places to explore, creating a few characters and trying out Dungeons and Dragons for the first time.

    Article by Jaxy

The Family Visit

"Turn off that computer!" Chris shouted at the top of her lungs.

Jeff did his best to ignore her request, and continued to play. There was something about spending the day at his mother in-laws that just did not appeal to him on a Saturday. It wasn't so much the long drive to get there, or that his wife's mother was a huge nag. It was just the fact that he would have to sit there and listen to her parents talk about all the things Jeff didn't do right as a husband. Lectures on how they should spend their money, and what investments they should be making.

    Article by Razor
