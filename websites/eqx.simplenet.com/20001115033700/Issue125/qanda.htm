Ho and hiya, Neppy here! And I had the name first! A perfectly good name for a halfling rogue who manages to weasel her way into all kinds of interesting situations.

Well, Nepenthia-of-the-nearly-copycat-name is off to Lake Rathe to sulk and drown her sorrows over having no questions to answer although I s’pect she is prolly getting some rays and practicing her swan dive off the aviak boards when the birds are busy elsewhere. So anyway, there wasn’t any Question and Answer column last week and I got to thinking, hmmmm, how come she never answers the important questions, the ones I hear popping around Norrath? For instance, has anyone ever asked her if Fippy the Gnoll’s blue fur grows naturally that way or whether they have a dye shop in Blackburrow? And then I wonder if that ogre lady loose in Freeport ever got her “love” questions answered? (Who could possibly put answers to love questions into ogre-speak?) Nooooo, Nepenthia’s always just doing those high and mighty issues, like ummm how to learn languages or what to do with the long pointy end of a spectre’s tail. Sooooo, here’s the questions you always wanted to have answers too. And if you want Nepenthia’s fancy-falutin’ verbosity and continuous requests for a dress, then you’d better write to her so she has a reason to head back to town.

Q: Is it true the gnolls of Blackburrow dye their fur blue and have to have a touchup job every other week?

A: Yes, haven’t you ever noticed the peculiar blue of the water when you fall off the cliff and land right smack in the middle of some pissed off gnolls? Well, the deal is, the gnolls aren’t mad at you because you fell in. What they are really angry about is that you might notice the water is tinted by runoff from the furdresser’s shop hidden away in the cliffs there. Now, you might ask, why would the gnolls want blue fur? I mean, it doesn’t exactly set off those fangs does it? Well the answer is actually found in their music. If you listen carefully sometime (better hide to stay alive, of course), you will find the gnolls favor gothgnollpunkrock music and decided that dyeing their fur blue would set them aside from the Abergnolly and Fitch clans over in the Karanas. Leave it to the gnolls to be slightly out of date. My guess is that it won’t be too long until they all have long blonde fur to go with their toothy grins and go around barking Hanson songs. As for the touchup job? Well of course -- just check out Fippy’s belly sometimes. One day it’ll have all these little black roots and the next pure blue. I’ve seen it myself!

Q: I heard there was an ogre lady in Freeport crying in her beer because she couldn’t find a mate. Whatever happened to her?

A: Well you’d have to ask Nepenthia about that. All I know is that one night I saw the ogre lady and Nepenthia, skinny arm in fat arm, nearly rolling down the street, singing a song that had a chorus something about males and pig’s tails and bash’emgud. They stumbled into a bar and when I slipped in to wangle a free beer, Nep already had her lute out and she and ogre lady were singing, err hollering actually, a duet about getting laid. Well, blush, I won’t go into the details on that song. But you can bet there was a lot of attention focused on the darling duo. Although, quite honestly, I think it was the novelty of an ogre singing -- and not all that badly either -- that really got people listening and not the ogre’s hunt for a mate. However, I slipped outside to inspect, err protect, a few pockets and when I got back, the ogre lady was arm wrestling with the hairiest-toed halfling I’ve ever seen and Nep was batting those eyelashes at an extremely inebriated darkelf who seemed to think she was his mother. *Shakes her head* Oh it was quite a night I tell you. But, next morning, as I was sneaking… err walking out the gate, I noticed ogre lady ambling out toward the hills looking as close to a cat that got all the cream as a pock-marked grotty ogre could manage. I think that ogre lady has a new appreciation for bards’ songs now. And NO, I’m not saying Nep charmed the halfling. Oh no, not me.

Q: Is it true Aradune had to bribe a gelatinous cube to spraypaint his name all over the Qeynos catacombs?

A: You think I’d touch that? What with green flaming swords appearing out of the ether just on a whim? Whaddya think I am? Nuts? Well yeah, I am. Goes with my job. Anyway, yeah, Aradune was sitting out under a tree in Qeynos one day and realized he was totally tired of his goody-two-boots-and-flaming-sword image and decided he wanted to go hip. Well hmm as hip as a ranger can imagine. I mean, there’s no way Karana’s favorite would go dancing bare-chested in a Neriak bar, is there? Well so he got to thinking about what a cool dood would do. (Took him a minute to respell dude but he finally managed to overcome his innate good taste in spelling.) Graffiti he thought, that’s it -- I need to get some spraypaint and get busy. Let’s see, I can do “DuNE waz here!” And maybe “Mastah DuNE! He RoCKS!” Yep, Ara was really getting into the spirit of it and strolled into the shop for a can of green spray paint. Well the shopkeeper, and I’m not gonna name names, got huffy and told Aradune he didn’t sell THAT kind of thing. Aradune was momentarily discouraged but you gotta remember this is a ranger and ranger’s always get their …. well whatever it is they’re looking for. So he headed down into the ‘combs and came across a cube slime-ing up the wall in green. Aha, he said (well hmmm I kinda doubt Aradune would say Aha, but then again, this IS a story), just the right color, you must be the fellow, err quivering mass of jello I was looking for! Would you spray some graffiti for me? The gelatinous cube ignored him for a bit, and then tumbled around to him and said, “It’ll cost ya Dood!” Aradune stood for a moment, probably debating gelatin flambé against hip grafitti while he considered the cube twisting and rocking there, and finally said, “Ok 10 gold for one, 19gold and 89 copper for two!” “Done” the cube cried and immediately started squeezing out the rough lettering for the now-cool Aradune graffiti. Now you might want to know how I know this? Hah! I’ll never tell.

Q: Why are all the sand giants male? Why aren't there any FEMALE giants? God knows they can't have sprung up from ogre females... or can they ?

A: Well, it’s true all the sand giants you see are male and considering the girth of their bellies and those ridiculous cowpies of cloth around their heads, you gotta wonder if there used to be some female sand giants who took one look, tucked up their skirts and ran away as fast as the little hummies and dorfs do in Oasis. (Darn I listened to the ogre lady one song too many!) Ever run between a giant’s legs? Now that is even scarier than looking straight on at one. And don’t look up! Well, then again, nothing like one of those bare chests to send any possible female giant screaming and running to hide on the backside of the stone pillars. So, do the male sand giants come from ogre females who spent one too many nights in an inn in Freeport? Could be! I doubt it though. The sand giants either had very expensive orthodontia or they came with naturally good teeth which would rule out the ogre genetic component completely. The tale I heard about the birth of sand giants however, is an old one. Way, way back when the high elves took dresses away from the half-elves, it is said that the half-elven bards went stark raving crazy, ended up down in the jungle and were never seen again. So sad, too bad, but then the tale goes on to mention that nine months and seven days later, strangely clad giants emerged from the jungle ready to wreak havoc on the world and its inhabitants, particularly high elves with long snooty noses. The moral of the fable? Never underestimate the ire of fashion-deprived half-elves. (Smoothes her pretty silk robe and contemplates poor Nepenthia and her battleweary togs. Poor dear.)

Physta the Favorite Druid
