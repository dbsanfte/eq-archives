ISSUE # 100

November 13th - November 19th


Of the Lost Temple


'Neath murky Feerrotts darkened sky
Where green vines creep and frogloks cry,
There looms a temple large and old
That many secrets still does hold.

For Cazic Thule, this keep was made,
In forgotten ages the stones were laid,
And no one knows who built these halls
Or who inscribed the mighty walls.

Ancient dangers dwell within,
Where no one but the brave have been,
They guard the courtyards and the throne
And fool is he who comes alone.

Armed with teeth and claws and spells
Death lurks in the rooms and wells:
Vicious lizard, golem, ape,
And Fear itself in monstrous shape.

There is no pity for the weak,
For those who run and cry and shriek,
And only with both luck and skill
Will you live to make the final kill.

But in spite of death and pain and fear,
Many come to battle here,
And in days gone-by those of patient might
Even find the Rubicite.

Sought by many, found by few
Red as the blood of me and you,
This was the prize for which they all did scheme,
That filled their every thought and dream.

Such dreams did drive the best ones mad
With yearnings for what could be had,
And many a brave and goodly deed
Was here betrayed by lowly greed.

The age of Rubicite is past,
It was too good, or we too wicked, for it to last,
Yet armor is still wrested from the dead:
Dark ravenscale replacing red.

Aye, in this lost temple 'neath Feerrott's sky,
They still come to fight, and come to die.
Some find fortune; some find a friend,
And some find only a bitter end.

Still the creatures of the God of Fear
Will never shed a single tear,
For dwarf and gnome and man and elf
Who braved the halls of Cazic Thule himself.

    Tale by Teona
