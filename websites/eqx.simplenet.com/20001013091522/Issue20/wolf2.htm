ISSUE # 20May 2nd - May 8th

A Bards Last Tale

I shushed her. The pain of what she had witnessed was too great. I waited and soothed her. Soon she passed out. I was afraid she would never awake again. But I still held her. I looked down at her face. The world would be lesser without her. She was fair, her skin light and her hair dark. Her eyes were of the deepest green, though her lids were closed. And she was a priestess of the Quellious. Or at least I guessed so by the shortness of her hair and the style of her sandals. Quellians often chose such simple but durable trappings.

While I was thinking about what I knew Quellious, she awoke. She was feverish now. 		"Thank you for staying with me. You are very kind."

"I could never leave you alone. Though we have never met, I feel that I owe you at least this much."

She smiled. She was very pale from the loss of blood, and she was also very weak. But she continued her tale. 		"I had finally convinced Orm that we should head back to the inn, the Red Ferret, and seek shelter. Certainly the beast was beyond our power to defeat. But as we headed for the inn, it howled again. Orm was certain that he knew where the sound came from. He drew his blade and ran off after it. I tried to keep up, but no one can keep up with Orm at a run. Orm is a great warrior, and I guess he thought he could defeat the creature. I think we both knew that it would never stop hunting us.

Tale by Absor.
