ISSUE # 24June 1st - June 5th

A View to Temptation

Soon after seating myself in my accustomed place I took stock of those gathered. Only a fellowship in the corner drew my attention as the others were familiars.

By the firelight I could distinguish that all seated most certainly were adventurers. If they had ever called a town home then they were surely far away from it. It seemed there was a heated discussion and soon their banter began to cause distress amongst the patrons. Calmly, I struck up a conversation with my cousin at my side. I was careful so as to not appear to be staring in the direction of the strangers. I would talk and listen at the same time. 		There were at least two men and a women seated. A fourth figure, shrouded in flowing cloth was indistinguishable. One of the men, the extremely large one, slammed his mug to the table and seemed to be laughing at the others.

It was at that moment the woman rose from her seat and with a warriors yell threw her hands to the air and then quickly brought them to the top of the table in front of the man before her. I realized a sword of black metal had been plunged through the wooden table splitting the rock foundation beneath. "It was by the sword fool!" 		Many quickly found the exits while others stood as if frozen in ice.

In the instant I heard a chuckle from the smaller of her companions I also witnessed the black sword withdrawn from the crumbled rock. The warrior sat far back in his chair with the blade at his throat. "Nay, I did not mean to insult you mighty Serat. It is only that you have many weapons and that I, a lowley warrior have but few. You have your beauty as well as your sword. I admit, I concede, I am envious. I am as jealous as..."
