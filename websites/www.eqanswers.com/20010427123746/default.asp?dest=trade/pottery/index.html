Mastering Pottery

Pottery is now once again gaining some life as a trade skill. It used to be that it just did no have the profitability as the others, or the usefulness of the other skills. It also did not lend much to the entertainment of EQ for some people. The only one really using their pottery were bakers who needed the items for baking.

However that is now changing, seemingly over night (actually over patch)

There is now becoming a higher demand for this skill. Not just rouges but also enchanters are seeking the simple vial, only made by PC’s with the pottery skill after now. The new spell line of Crystallize Mana has sparked life back into all those potters out there. This guide will lead you there.

Making the items

In order to start your career to becoming a successful potter one must have the essential components. However, even before hand you must at least put 1 point into your potter skill, which can be learned from any of your guild masters. Because Pottering is not the easiest skill to improve in level, I would strongly suggest you put whole 20 points into the skill before trying to attempt anything further.

Creating the finished product has two generally two major steps. The first one is you must mold the clay into your desired product, then fire the unfinished mold to make the finished item. Because the second step requires a firing sheet to fire the unfinished mold, it is suggested not to be done for skill gaining due to the cost involved.

Here are the two steps and what takes place in both, please follow this as the guide will refer to it.

Pottery Wheel - Used items
Sketches
Clay
Flask of Water

Kiln - Used Items
Firing Sheet
Unfired mold (item was result of step one)

To work these two tools, bring your clay, sketch, and water to the pottery wheel. Place the correct components and amounts into the wheel and hit combine. If you are successful you will have an unfinished mold, now take the mold and correct firing sheet to the kiln. Again if successful skill role, you will now have the finished product.

 

Small Jars - Level 1-31 - Novice

Equipment needed
Block of Clay
Small Jar Sketch
Flask of Water

You are at skill 20 if you followed the above method, now keep doing small jars Intl they become trivial at 31. Remember not to fire (kiln) your unfinished products, just sell them back to the merchant, Intl you can start making useful items like Vials that can sell to other PCs.

Medium Jars - level 31-36 - Junior

Equipment needed
Block of Clay
Medium Jar sketch
Flask of Water

This may not increase your skill much but its worth it to save the money and less skill failures, so keep working with medium jars Intl it becomes trivial to you.

Pie Tins - Level 36-115 - Senior

Equipment needed
(2) Block of Clay
Pie Tin Sketch
Flask of Water

Its time to start doing some serious mouse clicking so prepare yourself. You’ll need to load up on tons of the components listed above; Pie tins have a long trivial gap.

Vials - Level 115-148 - Master

Equipment needed
Small block of Clay
Vial Sketch
A skin or Pelt (anything except ruined)
Flask of Water

Now you can start making profit with this trade skill by creating the Vials and selling them back to Players for a good profit. Remember you should fire these now with the correct firing sheets so you get the finished product.
