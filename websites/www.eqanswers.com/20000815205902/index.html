GM Events
How do I find out more about GM hosted events? I have been playing EQ since release but i always missed those events. Is there anywhere i can find out when is the next event on my server (Mith marr). Thanks

"Events", meaning things like the tests, will soon be posted on the EverQuest.com web page. Quests, however, are another matter, as they are never pre-announced. You'll only be able to find out about them by either being on and involved at the time, or by reading up on them afterwards. We'll soon have quest-stories posted on the official website.

Play Nice
BTW, this maybe shoulda been on the rants board, but perhaps GW will come down and answer this mere mortal....why why why do you create rules that are detrimental to the players? Play nice started off as a way to prevent KSing and verbal abuse. Play nice should not be exploited as a means of allowing everyone the same chance at a spot in line for toilet paper...err...getting xp.

The Play Nice Policies were created because a growing number of customers were being subordinated by other, more powerful, groups of players. These other groups would claim "ownership" of spawns, loot drops, and even entire zones, refusing to allow other people a chance to experience them. On some servers, guilds had certain areas camped 24x7.

The play nice policies, in regards to spawns and camping, are designed to promote equal access to the areas. If a group can get into a room, they have as much right to be there as anyone else. Are the policies perfect? No, of course what is? No matter what policy we adopt, there will be people who are helped by the policy, and others that are hurt. Sometimes the policy may help them in one instance and hurt in another.

As I've mentioned in the past, if anyone has any ideas for policies that are easy to understand, are enforcable, do not require a larger CS team than before or after the Play nice policies, and will please everyone, I'd be happy to discuss them.

Wizard Draughts
You thought they were small and swift, didn't you ? He he, wrong:

1) Post-50 Wizards don't need the missing large DD spells, because a double cast of Draught does the job (so Draughts are "large" if cast twice...) but
2) Draughts are swift in single-cast, but "slow" in chain-cast, and this might be the hidden reason why some post-50 Wizards feel less effective than before. I suspect Draughts need a cast time of 2.5 instead of 3;

The Draught line was created to give wizards the option of doing less damage at similar mana/damage ratio compared to ice comet, and also to give them similar spells across the three elements that the wizards control. The reasoning behind "less damage" was to reduce the amount that the wizard would aggro the target. Had we wanted to continue along the same lines as ice comet, we could have given similar damage spells with equal damage, mana, and casting time requirements.

In this case, the additional time required to stack two draughts versus one ice comet is what you "pay" for the reduced amount that you aggro the target.

Reimbursement for Mistakes
According to the Bug board, this spell was a mistake, and should have been labeled as 61 and NOT 16. Ok valid mistake.. However those of us that traded VALUABLE rare drop spells for this are basically being told, tuff luck it's being changed.

We're fairly open about the fact that things in game are subject to change at any time. This includes the prospect of both positive and negative changes. People making trades in game need to be aware that that there exists a chance that any item is likely to drop in value or usefulness after you buy it. It's sort of like buying stock in a company in that regard.

The spell was never supposed to be usable by magicians, who ended up able to use it only because the level restriction was transposed by the designer. I really wish we'd caught this sooner.

Deleted Post
Hello all, I just deleted a post suggesting that a new class ('Witch') be created. The thread was deleted because it degenerated into the disparaging of practiced religion (Wicca).

As a note to the original author of that post, as that certainly wasn't his intention, we wouldn't create a class by that name due to the fact that it does apply to a currently practiced religion. The same doesn't apply to reference to 'priests' or 'monks', as those are nondescript titles in nature, and as such not indicative of any particular religion.

Archery Change
There was a hidden nerf in this patch - when you shoot a bow you turn towards the mob you are shooting at. Well, not anymore!!! You now don't turn to face the mob. This allows mobs who run to get out of the line of sight and you miss more.

Quite frankly this is the first I've seen on the subject. I'll check into it tomorrow.

PvP Teams by randerson at 8/12/00 5:24:00 PM PST   

Gordon 'Abashi' Wrinn responded today about some PvP concerns and asked for suggestions.

PvP Teams
Hello,

I posted this in another thread but it seems to warrant its own.

The biggest difficulty in regards to PvP in EverQuest is that EverQuest is, and will remain, a primarily PvE (Player versus Environment) game. The addition of PvP to the game should not change the basic fundemental mechanics of EverQuest. PvP in EverQuest has to result in 'EverQuest with PvP' rather than 'PvP EverQuest', if you get my point.

One of the largest issues on the PvP Teams servers is the subject of cross-teaming. Players of different teams get together for battles. Due to the PvE focus of the game, this has to be allowed for sake of game balance. Though some have suggested that cross-teaming, either in groups or guilds, not be allowed, it's something that will need to remain for the PvE game.

The problem comes in when cross-teaming is applied to PvP. Cross-teaming creates a situation that is often referred to as the 'invulnarable healer' scenario. Basically, one group of PCs (Team A) attack a group of people on Team B, while supported by a healer from Team B. In this fashion, you have a situation where a member of one team is taking an active role in the deaths of members of their own team.

There is another 'invulnarable healer' scenario that is more common and often just as bad as the cross-teaming scenario. This one revolves around a group of players from Team A attacking a group from Team B, while supported by a healer/buffer who is far superior in level to anyone from Team A or Team B. Gordon Responds by Gendarin at 8/11/00 2:51:00 PM PST   

Gordon 'Abashi' Wrinn responded today on the Official EverQuest Message Boards. This page will update each time a new response has been made, please click continue to view the preceding ones.

Another Damage Test
I have not done any extensive tests mind you, but my L60 warrior with a 3/10 primary and 16/30 secondary weapon got owned by a L60 monk with both a RFS and a Tranquil staff. We only dueled 4 times, the first was close, but the latter three I died while he had 1-3 bubbles of health left.

One of the weapons combos I tested, with both the monk and the warrior actually was the moss-twig/jade mace combo. That twig has some serious problems.

Exp Loss in Arena
I play a shaman, and I’d love to be able to participate in PvP combat. However, because the shaman has both pets, and DoTs, I can’t /dual someone without the possibility of causing them an experience loss death. , I was wondering if it would be possible to at leas turn off ALL experience loss in the Arena?

There's no XP loss for any death encountered while in the PK region of the Arena. Feel free to battle to your hearts content inside there

Monk vs Warrior Test
Gordon Thank you for doing a test for us.

For the past several weeks, the complaints by monks here on the board (most of the complaints anyway) have been that they no longer outdamage warriors. The tests that I did were only to confirm that that wasn't true. Yes, I conceed that monks cannot takes hits as well as warriors, but that's never been where the disagreement has been. When people said, 'Monks no longer outdamage warriors', I had to disagree, which is why I ended up testing it out. However, when people now say, 'We can't take hits like a warrior', I agree with that.

Dealing out more damage than the warrior doesn't mean that you'll beat the warrior in a duel, nor that the ratio of damage dealing to damage taking capability is constant. The benefit of extra damage has more weight than the disadvantage of being defensively inferior.

I'm sure that some would say, 'If a warrior can solo creature X in two minutes and be almost dead at the end, then a monk should be able to do the same in a minute and end up almost dead'. This would be an example of constant ratio between offense and defense and the two classes. This however isn't the case. If the ratio were constant as suggested, there would, again, be no reason to have the warrior, because the monk's other skills would make them preferable in all cases. Not to mention, there's an added benefit any time a creature is killed faster than normal, a benefit that can't be measured directly against the power of the class, but more against its usefulness to the group. Would a group, for instance, like to kill things in 2 minutes and end up OOM and near death at the end? Or would the rather do it in 1 minute and end up the same way? They'd want it done faster in all cases. That's what monks and rogues add as melee classes.

Concerning everyone saying that the test wasn't 'valid', I fully agree with that from a scientific standpoint. Many more combats would have to be carried out than the ones I could do over a couple of hours yesterday. To be honest, its not really my job to test things of this nature, I just do it as needed so I can speak with first-hand experience. Though the specific percentage point differences can't really be considered averages, I do think that the test fairly well answered the question: Do monks outdamage warriors?

There was one interesting thing in the test that I didn't mention in the post yesterday, concerning the one time that the warrior outdamaged the monk. The warrior was using his best combo out of the 5 weapon combos I tried, the monk his worst (bare knuck). The damage ratios were determined by seeing how long it took for the character to kill the NPC in question. The monk completed the task in under two minutes in every case but one, where it took 2:03. The warrior was over two minutes in every case, with its fastest time at 2:01. The monk's best time, by the way, was 1:30.

Pets in Arena
Would Verant make a second copy of the Arena that allows pets? It could be exactly the same and the zone entrance could be from the current Arena; the only difference would be that pets would be summonable.

Pets work in the Arena. Initially when the Arena was put together, pathing data wasn't included. That pathing data was put in a few months ago, and since then pets have worked properly.

Arena Dueling
Since this is the case, I was wondering if it would be possible to at leas turn off ALL experience loss in the Arena?

There's no XP loss for any death encountered while in the PK region of the Arena. Feel free to battle to your hearts content inside there

Fate of the Twig
Based on the patch message, I am guessing you made the decision not to make any changes to the existing twigs and branches in the game. Can you confirm this?

I've answered this question on the boards about 5 times in the past two weeks. No, we have not decided what to do with the item, if anything. When we do, we'll be sure to let everyone know.

Aug 8th 6:10pm

Modified/fixed the way attack speed spells stack, so higher level (better) hastes will overwrite lower level hastes, slows will always overwrite hastes, and higher level slows will overwrite lower level slows.  So it should now work as it was intended.
