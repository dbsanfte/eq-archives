Mastering Poison

The mixing of vile smelling concoctions together to create strong and potent poisons is a rogue’s favorite pastime. Taking their sharp bladed edges and lacing them with the deadly poisons, they just wait to step out of the shadows to take their next victim. Make and Apply Poison are both skills only received by the rogue class and are developed at different character levels. Apply can be learned at the level of 18, while Make Poison can be used at 20. This makes for an interesting concept as lower level rogues can use a poison delevoped from another higher rogue.

When you have created a poison and applied it to a dagger of your choice, you then have a chance of the poison infecting your opponent. Unfortunately, the poison currently acts like a spell effect and can therefore be interrupted while moving or hit. It has been thought among many that movement shouldn’t affect whether or not your poison seeps into the victim when you inflict damage. It can also be resisted, which prevents lower level rogues from taking out higher level creatures more easily with a poisoned blade. It has been learned that Apply Poison doesn’t actually affect the weapon you currently are holding. Instead it affects the primary weapon slot that you used. This was believed as a way to prevent rogues from poisoning others' weapons and creating an unbalanced issue.

After you successfully poison a blade and the effect of the poison has gone off, it will no longer affect the weapon. Furthermore, the poison vaporizes once you venture into another zone, and is suggested to only poison weapons when you will be staying inside the current zone for a while, or you know you’ll be fighting something soon.

Things you need

Like any trade skill it is strongly suggested to place skill points into poison before you start out. With poison I would recommend to put in a full 20 points just because those first skill levels can become very hard to achieve.

To make poison you will also need different components. Most of these can be bought from Gindlin Toxfodder inside a small hut located in Western Karana (-2980.34, -13399.48). The first things you need to buy are a Mortar and Pestle to combine the component in and a suspension fluid; both can be bought from Gindlin. The second things you need are a vial made by potters and the actual poison components.

The vial needs to be made by master potters and above; these vials cannot be purchased. There are three different types of vials that can be made for use in different types of poison. The most common two are the poison and lined vials. It has been rumored that the sealed vial cannot currently be used. If you see yourself using a great deal of poison in battle, then it would be suggested to master pottery first before you try mastering poison.

For more information on pottery recipes consult the pottery guide.

The final ingredient for poison is the actual poison itself. When mixed with the other components it creates the final, deadly liquid. These can be found throughout Norrath and can take a rogue quite a long time to find everything he needs. When everything has been gathered, place the components inside your Mortar and Pestle and press combine. Hopefully you will receive a successful messag; if not, keep trying!

Asp Poison – 20 –24 – Beginner

Equipment needed
Mortar and pestle
Poison vial
Suspension vial
2 Asp Poison sacs

If successful this poison will cause your target to writhe in pain as the poison seeps through its body. Causing fast acting damage in one strike, this can do up to 205 direct damage. The Asp Poison Sacs can be found on different Cistern Asps, which wander throughout Northern Ro, Southern Ro, and the Oasis of Marr.

Basilisks poison – 24-32 - Novice

Equipment needed
Mortar and pestle
Lined Poison vial
Suspension
2 Basilisk eyestalks

This poison will cause your target to lock his muscles, causing it to move at snail's pace. This lets you easily escape if things get out of control. The basilisk eyestalks can be found randomly on different basilisks located in Lavastorm.

Crystal Eritus – 32-44 – Intermediate

Equipment needed
Mortar and pestle
Lined Poison vial
Suspension
2 Crystallized marrows

A direct damage force between 98-205 damage, including a short stun. It is a very useful poison to apply to those extra frisky opponents. The Crystallized Marrows can be found on deadly ice bone skeletons, which wander in different zones throughout Norrath, including the depths of dungeon Befallen.

Putrid Bane – 44-56 – Intermediate Continue

Equipment needed
Mortar and pestle
Lined poison vial
Suspension
2 Putrid bile’s

This poison will do upwards of 245 damage, inflicting your opponent over a period of time (around one minute). A great poison--if successful--to start out in a fight. The Putrid Biles can be found on Fire drakes inside Lavastorm.

Sweet Lathyris – 44-66 – Advance

Equipment needed
Mortar and Pestle
Lined poison vial
Suspension
2 Ichors
1 Giant wasp venom sac

Not the most useful poison, as it is highly resistible. It will cause the target to get dizzy which creates a longer lasting stun and will let you hit the target without it hitting back, but it will go right for you when it stops. The effects also downgrade the targets AGI by 20 and AC by 10. Ichors can be found on Giant wooly spiders wandering Everfrost and Wasp venom sacs can be found on Giant Wasps.

Lixt Wing – 66-85 - Advance Continue

Equipment needed
Mortar and Pestle
Lined poison vial
Suspension
2 Embalming dusts

Clerics aren’t the only ones that can deal out massive damage to walking dead. Lixt wing will affect only undead creatures for a massive 310 direct damage; this is a very effective spell and isn’t resisted very often. Embalming dusts can be found off undead themselves but only on Mummies and Dark bone skeletons.

Festering Nettle – 85-100 – Master

Equipment needed
Mortar and pestle
Lined poison vial
Suspension
2 Ashroots

Congratulations on getting this far! I imagine you are having much fun running around gathering poison components (hehe). The Festering nettle will cause your target to lower all its resistances by 30 except poison. Ashroots can be gather from Fire imps inside Lavastorm.
