Becoming A Shaman

Forward:

This guide is geared towards players who have a good working comprehension of EverQuest game fundamentals but wish to achieve additional information on classes that they might want to play. If you are a new player in EverQuest, then I suggest reading the EverQuest Manual and looking at our new player section before continuing.

If there are any errors or comments for this guide please send them to: news@eqanswers.com

Overview

Witch Doctors of Norrath. The Shaman of the Tribe is both revered and feared for his ability to call upon the spirit world.  Their unmatched abilities to cure illness, disease, and other forms of magic and to aid their battle members make the Shaman a powerful force in the land of Norrath.

The Path of a Shaman is a lifetime of learning of the spirit realm. If you choose to become you will soon feel the true power that the spirit world can bestow upon you and your comrades. A Shaman’s power is a combination of many classes. They are typically a “group” magic class. They do, however, excel at a few things.

Statistic Buffs
The key role of the Shaman is boosting the fighting abilities of your party before battle. No other class can bolster your group’s agility, dexterity, strength, stamina, or charisma like the Shaman.  Additionally, he can increase hit points, armor class, attack speed, and all resistances (magic, disease, poison, fire, cold).  A Shaman can transform an otherwise average warrior or monk into a killing machine.  Although uneasy with the source of your magic, monks, paladins, warriors, shadow knights, and rangers are always assured by your presence.

Statistic De-buffing
At the onset of battle, the Shaman is most effective.  In addition to your ability to bolster your party’s abilities, you have the unmatched skill of reducing any adversarial beast to a whimpering heap. Whether it is reducing a target’s attack speed, lowering its resistance to magic attacks, or halting its retreat, the Shaman has a vast array of magic to call upon during battle. Damage over Time As you mature as a Shaman and learn the intricacies of the spirit realm, you will be capable of summoning the greatest of poison and disease to cast upon your enemies.  These spells will wreak havoc on a monster during the course of battle.

Travel
The Shaman also excels at traveling, either solo or with a group.  At level 9, you receive the movement increase spell, Spirit of the Wolf.  Later, you receive Invisibility and Ultravision (perfect night vision).  Both are excellent aids in travel.

Healing (early)
While later in life the Cleric class will outshine you in healing during battle, you can also keep your party members alive this way.  Often, from levels 1 through 29, you will be asked to perform this role.

Tanking (early)
Very early in your life, you will be capable of being the front line in an attack.  Your ability to wear chain mail, combined with decent armor class and hit points, allows you to engage closely in battle.  However, it is not long before you should drop back from the main line and allow the warriors and monks to assume their rightful place.

Disadvantages
With the exception of statistical buffing, the shaman class holds no truly unique advantage over other classes.  This is generally attributed to game balance, but can be particularly frustrating when another class gains a skill that had been unique to you for many levels, such as Magicians being given the Malaise line of spells. Further, the Shaman class does not enjoy a robust Direct Damage line of spells.  Actually, except a level 1 fire-based spell, we only receive cold-based attacks.  Additionally, the DD spells we do receive are not terribly mana efficient. However, a well-played Shaman is a valued and desired class for any situation.

Myths and Controversy

Statistics have no meaning
A much-debated topic in Shaman circles is the effectiveness of statistics.  Many tests, heated posts, and poor assumptions led to the release of what each statistic does by the game’s designers. By their own admission, the statistics “may not be the greatest statistic system”, however, they do have an impact on game play.

Below is a brief summary of what each statistic does:

DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how hard you hit with bows
STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills.
STA: affects how many hit points you have and how long you can hold your breath.
INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights.
WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.
CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular).
AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit.

Disease line of spells is useless
This stems from the long duration of the spell series.  Most of the disease-based spells last 18-ticks (108 seconds).  The problem is most battles in a well-balanced group do not last near this length of time. However, in certain situations, this line of spells is useful.  During a protracted battle, the amount of damage balanced against the low mana cost of these spells makes them useful.  They also stack with all of your other DoT spells.  A shaman can really create a lot of damage on an NPC this way, and it makes the Shaman a solid solo class.

Shaman pets are terrible
This would be true if the Shaman class was a pet class.  Necromancers and Magicians get their pets very early in their life, and they are meant to provide a protection for the robe- wearing casters. We obtain our pets at level 34, and it is summoned at a much lower level than ours. When a Shaman realizes that the pet, with it’s reduced hit points and armor class, is more akin to a Damage over Time spell than a warrior, he will find it to be quite useful. Besides, a Shaman can take a hit without crumbling, unlike our pure casting brethren.

Shamans have terrible Direct Damage spells
Our Direct Damage (DD) spells do not have the same power or efficiency of Wizards or Magicians.  Our power and usefulness has been described above.  However, they are quite adequate for a nice “finish” to a mob when needed.

Character Creation

Creating a Character can be a daunting undertaking, for it can be straightforward and complex at the same time. You can create your character depending on what type of situation or role you want to classify in.

Race

Another large factor in creating your Character is deciding what race to play. This is not only a personal choice depending on how you want to role-play, but it will also affect how you play. Each race has diverse starting attributes, location, and disadvantages over others. Typically, evil races are harder to play than good ones, but the final decision is left up to you. It is suggested you take a hard look at what one race offers over another, and most significantly, if you think you will have fun with it.

All three of the Shaman races are large and allow for the nice ability to Slam.  Slam allows a Shaman to interrupt NPC casters, preventing them from dealing serious damage at a bad time.

Barbarian
Certainly the most popular race for the Shaman class is the Barbarian.  Starting in the far northern city of Halas, the Barbarians enjoy the most fertile hunting grounds and surrounding areas in all of Antonica, Odus, and Faydwer. The Barbarians are essentially neutral among the “good” races of Norrath.  This will allow great freedom of travel throughout the land. However, they share with their Human cousins a lack of night vision.

Ogre and Troll
Starting in the southern portion of Antonica, Ogres and Trolls offer a nice role-playing aspect to many players.  While considered “lesser” by many in Norrath, these races provide some nice starting points for Shaman.  Ogres have decent to nice base statistics and Trolls receive regeneration natively. The areas surrounding Oggok (Ogres) and Grobb (Trolls) offer nice hunting areas for gaining experience.  While not as nice as the areas surrounding Halas, an Ogre and Troll may find themselves traveling far to find good hunting grounds.  Fortunately, we receive Spirit of the Wolf to assist in this matter.

Iksar
Also perceived as a “lesser” race, the Iksar offers another easy role-playing aspect.  The Lizardmen of Kunark enjoy THE most fertile early hunting grounds found in all of Norrath. While their starting statistics are not attractive at first glance, the areas for hunting and the regeneration spells and items available to Shaman more than compensate.

Attributes

One of the most significant parts of character generation is choosing where to put your bonus points. This will let your further modify your character’s role to be more helpful in diverse situations. A Warrior might add more to his Strength, so he can cut deeper into his quarry. While a Necromancer would be much more interested in increasing his magical powers and spreading the evil deeds with Intelligence. A thing to keep in mind, you can even further increase your attributes with magical items found inside the game, so incase you make a oversight, it is not very hard to correct.

Note: It is recommended that you spend as many points into Wisdom as allowed and place the remainder into Intelligence, as you are capable of increasing every other statistic through spells we obtain.

Strength

Influences your maximum and average damage ratios, the more strength you have, the more likely you will hit harder. Additional strength will let you learn your offensive skills faster, and let you carry more weight.

Stamina

Affects how many total hit points you have, and how long you will be able to hold your breath under water.

Agility (Valuable for Shaman)

Increases your percentage in how difficult it is to hit you, as well as how much damage you take when you are hit. Also, affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills.

Dexterity

Influences how often your weapon proc (spell) will engage, and how quickly you learn weapon skills. Will also increase how much damage you hit with bows, and how fast you learn rogue skills.

Wisdom (Valuable for Shaman)

Directly affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, including rangers. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher then intelligence. Here is the most influential attribute you can place points into, as it will directly affect your mana pool.  More mana equals more firepower and availability to buff and de-buff.

Intelligence (Valuable for Shaman)

Directly affects the amount of mana of magi classes, including bards and shadow knights. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills. I would recommend you place the last 5 points into Intelligence to assist you in learning skills.  Also, every other statistic listed can be increased via a spell we receive as we gain experience.

Charisma

Affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you. Also effects saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

Combat

Much of your time spent inside EverQuest will be involving combat situations. To be prepared for combat, one must know the basics of it and EverQuest itself, which we won’t describe here, for obvious reasons. What we will divulge, is common tactics used to take down foes more hastily and safer.

Solo

Only certain classes can become an effective soloist and a Shaman does do it quite successfully. The life of someone who chooses to be by herself is one of immense risk but also of great reward. You can reap much more experience faster, letting you build your levels more rapidly then someone who says inside the warm refuge of a group. Of course, without that group you have no one to save you, and soloist tend to die much more often then someone inside a group. The second disadvantage is, most high-level items are found on creatures that require a group’s effort.

Group

Fighting inside a group can have many advantages like taking down higher-level creatures, resulting in more experience for some classes, better loot, and the protection of having a group behind you. Some people also like the group social aspect of it and find adventuring solo can become very boring and tiresome over time. If your class has the ability to solo well, and you are after experience then you may want to go with that.

There is no doubt, going solo will result in faster and more experience than inside a group. Sooner or later though everyone will need to group, as after level 40 everything becomes too hard to take down by a single person.

General

The combat life of the Shaman is constantly evolving.  Therefore, it is quite difficult to describe combat tactics.  The versatility of the Shaman prevents the class from becoming boring, to say the least.

In the early levels, a Shaman is quite adept at imitating a Warrior.  You may find yourself at the front of the battle, cracking skulls with your staff. As you move from level 9 through level 24, you will be called upon to play the role of healer.  Beyond level 24, a Shaman tends to just cast buffs on the party, de-buff the mob, occasionally heal a party member, and poison the offending beast. When the Shaman achieves level 34, he not only receives a nice set of spells, but gains his first pet: the ever-present wolf.  The feeling of independence and achievement is astounding. At level 39, a Shaman truly comes into power.  The spell line creates a vast array of play options for the Shaman, as we can slow mobs’ attack speed and buff and poison at will.

Note:  It is generally agreed within the Shaman community that the levels from roughly 16 to 24 are very discouraging to the Shaman.  The spells we receive are less than exciting.  However, to the Shaman that survives this brutal period, he attains great power.

Team Work

Without question, a Shaman’s main ability is to enhance the party through buffs.  A quality shaman will time all of his buffs by first casting them upon himself and ensuring that at no time is a party member without these life saving assists. It is also important for the Shaman to observe the entire battle.  An overall picture of the battle will ensure that you can deliver just the right spell at just the right time.  Whether it be a well timed heal, a quick DD, or a root of a mob, the Shaman must always consider the big picture and react quickly and surely.

Spell Usage

As a Shaman, you not only carry responsibility for keeping the party buffed at all times, but you will be called upon to heal your party members and deal damage to your adversaries.  Just remember that mana = life for a caster so use it wisely.  Because of the added burden of using mana before, during, and after battle, it is particularly nice to receive Regeneration and Cannibalization at level 24.  Regeneration adds to your hit points and Cannibalize takes from hit points and adds to mana.  The combination, while sometimes tedious, gives you more mana for your role than you normally have.

Anti Magic

The Malaise line of spells can become very useful down the road while fighting even, yellow, and higher-level NPCs. It will lower your target’s magic, fire, cold, and poison resistance, resulting in less chance for spells to get resisted.

Slow

The most under valued spell line in the game may well be the Shaman’s series of slowing a mob’s attack speed.  They are the best in the game.  First you will receive Drowsy, but later the Insect line of spells comes forth (Tagar’s, Togor’s, etc).  Do not neglect this line of spells.  Use them at all times to prevent damage to your party.  When a mob is dealing less damage, you and your healing partners (clerics and druids) will be using less mana.

Special Information

The Shaman class has the unique trade skill of Alchemy.  A variety of potions (both useful and not) can be made solely by the spirit realm practitioners.

Spell Information

Here we will describe the first two ranks of spells you are eligible to buy, including which ones to buy and the ones to avoid. You can find all these spells at your local guildhall, or near it.

First Rank (Levels 1-4)

Burst Of Flame (Single Target Damage)

Fire attack. 5 damage (+1 per 2 levels. Max 7 damage) Must have.

Cure Disease

The more powerful diseases will require multiple castings to remove. Worth buying.

Dexterous Aura

+10 Dexterity. Dexterity increases your ability to hit, especially with ranged weapons. Not critical to buy, but nice.

Endure Cold

+15 Resist Cold. Not critical early, but you should pick this up later.

Flash Of Light

Blindness. This lowers the target's ability to hit. Casting is still possible while blinded. Blind works as a nice poor-man's fear. This is also probably one of the most effective and overlooked dueling spells in the game. Worth Buying.

Inner Fire

+1 AC, +10 HP. Max +3 AC, +20 HP. Note:  Can be used as a heal spell. Must have.

Minor Healing

+ 10 HP. Must have.

True North

Face North. This handy little spell turns the caster around to face directly North. This spell is the replacement for the formerly unimplemented Sense Magic Not particularly useful.

Strengthen

+10 Strength.  Strength does not increase maximum damage, it instead increases the probability of doing maximum damage for the weapon type. It will also increase carrying capacity, which is very important in those long dungeon hauls. Worth buying.

Second Rank (Levels 5-8)

Cure Poison

Each casting lowers the strength of the poison one rank (example: Weak poison becomes Feeble poison after the first cast, then vanishes on the second.) Must buy.

Drowsy

Decrease attack speed. A good debuff is you are in a group but tends to be slow to cast while soloing. Must buy.

Endure Fire +15 Resist Fire. Worth buying.

Feet Like Cat

+10 Agility. Agility is used for AC calculation and is the prime statistic for dodge and a few other defensive combat abilities. Must have.

Fleeting Fury

+15 Strength, +20 Dexterity, +6 AC. It has a very short casting time, and duration (18 seconds). Worth buying.

Frost Rift

Cold attack. 13 damage (+1 per level. Max 15 damage). Must buy.

Gate

Teleport to homepoint. Not useful until you receive Bind Affinity, unless you use it as an escape spell.  However, gating out from your party is generally frowned upon, as you are one of--if not the--healer. Must buy.

Scale Skin

+6 AC. Requires Snake Scales as a component (Easy to get in any newbie zone). Worth buying.

Sicken

Disease attack. 8 damage, 2 damage per tick for 21 ticks. Somewhat useful.

Spirit Pouch

Summon Container. Creates a bag with 4 large spaces and makes the items in it weightless. Important: If you leave the game or crash, the pouch disappears along with any items in it. Somewhat useful.

Summon Drink

Summon Water. While time-consuming, it can save you money. This water cannot be used for blacksmithing purposes.  Will disappear when you log. Somewhat useful

Conclusion

The Shaman is a wonderful class to play, and one with many possibilities to role-play. As stated previously, the life of a Shaman is constantly evolving, providing fresh opportunities for combat beyond the normal acquisition of levels or items. As with any class in Everquest, the most important part is to pick a class and race you are willing to play to its full extent and enjoy your time doing so.
