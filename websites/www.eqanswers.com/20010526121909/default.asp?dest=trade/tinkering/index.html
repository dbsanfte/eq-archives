Mastering Tinkering

Taking scraps of metal, bolts and other pieces like gears and sprockets, then combining them into a gadget is what many generations of the gnomes have passed down. Through its lifetime it has sprang up new and interesting inventions, letting people know far and wide that the gnome's are the true Inventors of Norrath's mysterious world. Tinkering can only be received by the Gnome Race, and cannot be started till the 16th level. Since gnomes have the natural ability to tinker with items, they have a starting skill of 50, which will allow you to get started right away. Tinkering is a very expensive trade skill; maybe more then jewel craft, as each component is costly and some times have high rates of failure. It is not uncommon for master tinkers to spend hundreds of platinum pieces to gain a single skill point, and is not recommended for financially unsecured gnomes.

However, it is still to be said that because you start out with a skill level of 50, you can still make some very useful and creative items. Check out the full list of recipes here, for more information.

Making the Items

Tinkering requires the use of a toolbox to build the components together. Both the toolbox and most of the major components can be found at the windmills, located in steamfont. Any gnome should know where these are located, as they are a prime lower level hunting ground.

Gnomes start out with a level 50 skill already in tinkering so its easy to jump right in, but tinkering requires a lot of money, and for the reward its more of a novelty trade skill in higher levels rather then one that is worth it to lower levels. Two of the better items you can make later on with a higher skill level are the Aqualung and Rebreather. Both of these can be very useful when exploring underwater dungeons like kedge, and it is much anticipated that the new expansion, Ruins of Kunark will have a vast amount of under water exploring.

Flameless Lantern - Level 50-68 - Novice

Equipment needed
Toolbox
1 Large Lantern
1 Twine
1 Firewater

The Flameless Lantern will illuminate with the same strength as a greater lightstone, and is a worthy addition to any blinded human. To make this item combine one large lantern that can be bought at most general merchants along with twine and one bottle of firewater. To make the firewater Combine one bottle of gnomish spirits and a normal flask of water inside your toolbox.

Standard Bow Cams - Level 68-75 - Junior

Equipment needed
Toolbox
1 Gears
1 Grease
1 Gnomish bolts

Any advance bower will need your services to make faster bows and these can be easily sold to fletchers around Norrath to cover some of your cost. To make the item combined gears, along with some grease and gnomish bolts inside your toolbox.

Stalking Probes - Level 75-102 - Senior

Equipment needed
Toolbox
1 Bottle
1 Metal Rod
1 Firewater

The Stalking probe can be a very useful item at times. It will summon up to Five mystical eye's of Zoom, allowing you to take control of it and scout out areas for a limited amount of time. When used under certain circumstances it can make your life a lot easier, like finding out there is really two creatures instead of one inside that room. To make a stalking probe, combine a normal bottle, which can be bought at most bartenders, and general merchants, along with a metal rod, and one bottle of Firewater.

Aqualung - Level 102-148 - Master

Equipment needed

Toolbox
1 Fine Coral Mesh
1 Silk Lined Steel Helm
1 Metal Twine
1 Gnomish Bolts
1 Metal Rod
1 Fresh Fish

Congratulations on getting this far! As you can tell by now Tinkering is a very hard and expensive trade skill, but the road you must travel has just gotten started. You can now make items worthy of selling on the open market, but they come at a steep investment price, and a high risk of failure. The Aqualung, will give you 50 charges of Breath of the Dead, which will let you stay under water for long periods of time safety. These items are very popular among parties ready to raid the famous underwater dungeon kedge, and most likely will be widely used inside Ruins of Kunark. To create an aqualung requires many components, first you need a Fine coral Mesh with, a silked lined steel helm, some metal twine, gnomish bolts and a metal rod, all of these can be bought at the tinkering merchants located in Steamfont and Ak'anon. The final item is a fresh fish, which can be bought at a vendor or by catching it yourself. And, what better time to get out that dusty collapsible fishing pole and animated bait.
