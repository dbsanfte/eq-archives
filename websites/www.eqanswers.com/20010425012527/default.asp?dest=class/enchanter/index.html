Becoming A Enchanter

The mystical illusions of life; twisting and curving all aspects of what we see, hear, and even what we think. These powers are left up to the Enchanters of Norrath, capturing the curiosity of others and jealousy of some. Becoming an Enchanter will open up the doors to the true power of the mind, allowing you to weave your magic into others and take control of them. An Enchanter inside the world of EverQuest is something that is needed among every group--especially among the higher levels. The Enchanter can control numerous NPCs at once, and having one Enchanter inside a group can save the rest. Their spell books are filled with spells that can drastically alter the outcome of the battle. Either by its wide variety of distraction spells like Mesmerise or the ability to even take control of another enemy and force it to kill others is a great power indeed.

The Enchanter is not an easy class to play. The player must also be intelligent and know how to control and tame the powers of an Enchanter. Everything with an Enchanter is based on timing; you need to know when to cast certain spells and when to not. Along with timing gives you the power to receive time for you and your party to think. You can distract the NPCs so that they will stop fighting and stand there looking off into the stars, or you can stun them repeatedly so that your warriors can bash into a pulp. One thing you need to learn while playing an Enchante--or any class for that matter--is what your role is inside a group.

First to clear something up... Enchanters can solo quite well, but they weren't meant to be a non-sociable class. They are suppose to and have been made to play inside a group. Only in a group can the true powers of an Enchanter shine. Hopefully this guide will teach you some aspects of how to play an Enchanter for what it's worth. The best way to truly find out is going out and experiencing it for yourself.

Myths and Controversy

  • All my spells get resisted

    Some of the enchanter spells are indeed highly resistible, but most of them are not. If you are fighting from within your level range, you should have absolutely no problem with getting through an NPC's resistance. One thing you must always do before attacking is Tashan the NPC to lower its magic resistance. This will greatly reduce any chance of your spells getting resisted before hand. Another thing that will play a large impact with spells is the amount of Charisma you have. Charisma affects your entire mind spell series like Charms and Mesmerizes. It is highly recommended to get as many Charisma items as you can find, or your spells will resist more, and your charm spells will fade more quickly.
  • Charm spells are too risky

    The Charm spell line can be at times very risky if you do not know how to control the spell itself. Below the guide will tell you how to use this spell in its full glory and how to use it safely.
  • Our Direct Damage is Worthless

    Sadly half of this is true. Enchanters were never made to be great masters of damage; we leave that up to the Wizards. The mana to damage ratio cannot even be compared to the other pure casters because ours are extremely weak and inefficient. However, they are not completely worthless. Many times, especially in the lower levels, you will find that they can be used under certain circumstances that will affect the final outcome of the battle. For example, if an NPC is fleeing and everyone else is out of mana, or if you need to finish something off quickly.

Character Creation

Getting down to making you character is a big decision on what type of Enchanter you want to play. This guide is more aimed at the Enchanter who wants to group and use the variety of mind control spells that the Enchanter has. However, there are different types of Enchanters you can play--most players who want to solo will put more into Intelligence then Charisma. The final decision is up to you.

Race

This is not only a personal choice depending on how you want to role-play, but it will also affect how you play. Each race has different starting stats, advantages, and disadvantages. Here we will list some to you, and hopefully you’ll make the correct choice for yourself.

 

Dark Elf

  • One of the most popular choices for creating an Enchanter is the Dark Elf. The starting stats for this race aren't bad, but in my personal opinion they lack the charisma that lower level Enchanters need badly. They aren't very liked among the other races, but that doesn't really matter with your illusions. They also have the advanced ability to see using ultravision.

Gnome

  • Like the Dark elves, they aren't a bad choice for playing an enchanter, but they lack the base Charisma that is needed.

Erudite

  • One of my favorite choices for creating an Enchanter is the Erudite. They have high Intelligence, and better yet, high starting charisma. This lets you not worry about items so much in the lower levels and will give you a better ability to balance both INT and CHA. Erudites however are human and blind at night. Of course this really shouldn't matter much to an Enchanter. With your illusion spells this corrects that problem, and you also obtain different sight spells like ultravision.

High Elf

  • Another very popular choice for Enchanters clearly because of the great starting location and base stats, and they are overall a great race to play. They are well liked among many except in the evil towns. They can see at night and have the highest charisma base stat.

Human

  • The least favorite among Enchanters is the humans. They lack in the stats, cannot see at night, and don't make out to be a very good Enchanter. It has been said Humans get no experience penalty... this is false. The lack of stats you receive for a minor experience adjustment is not worth it anyway.

Stat's

Strength: This gives you more power in your melee combat and lets you carry more stuff. Both of these I don't see Enchanters needing much; even a solo Enchanter shouldn't be engaging with melee style combat.

Stamina: This stat is pretty much pointless for an Enchanter (most likely all classes), as it only gives a limited amount of extra hit points and increases your stamina while swimming and fighting. Keep moving on…

Agility: This isn't a very useful stat to the Enchanter, as we won't be getting hit very often, and most Enchanters use Rune spells to counteract getting hit along with different AC buffs.

Dexterity: Will increase your procing speed on your weapons and affects how often melee skills rise. This is a pointless stat to the Enchanter.

Intelligence: The most powerful stat to other casters. It doesn't really make a massive difference towards an Enchanter. Other casters use Intelligence to nuke in which case they need a lot to destroy a foe. However, with an Enchanter we rely much more on other mana efficient spells like Charm to take out creatures.

Charisma: One of the best stats you could ever invest in with an Enchanter. Charisma means everything to an Enchanter; don't let other people fool you. The more you have the better. It greatly affects our mind spells, and having less then 140 charisma at higher levels will make your spells highly resistable and not worth much inside a group.

Group Combat

Enchanters shine inside a group--they were built for a group, and their arsenal of spells was made to interact within the group. Group combat for an Enchanter can become very confusing at times. It can put a lot of pressure on the Enchanter to try and control everything around the group, which is most likely attacking them. Group combat is something that you need to experience for yourself. You can read everything you'd like on group combat, but it won't matter til you try it for yourself.

Mesmerise
These spells give you time to control the situation and time to think of your next move. The ability to distract NPCs and let them idly wait for your group to finish off one of their friends is very powerful. Mesmerise, when casted upon an NPC, will make it stop what they are doing and stand there for 16-30 seconds unless they are attacked. You can cast non-damage spells on them like Tashan and other debuffs, and there is also a chance for it to wipe the hate list. In most situations the puller of the group will bring two or more creatures with him. Not to worry, you can just Mesmerise the ones that your group isn't attacking and concentrate your efforts onto one. Mesmerization is an AE spell, which has a large radius and will stun the caster if you are inside that radius. This can be a very useful spell inside a dungeon when you want multiple things to stop quickly.

Charm
Turning the odds is what Charm is about. Taking two creatures and turning them against each other, which can win a battle in itself. It has been a known fact that NPC creatures hit harder and most of the times have higher amounts of hit points for their levels than an animation or elemental of the same level. With charm you can take control of NPCs and let them do your biddings for you. They make wonderful pets. Unfortunately, the influence of Charm doesn't last a long time, but more likely will last through the entire battle. Charm like many of the other Enchanter spells is greatly affected by the amount of charisma you have, so it is suggested to wear as many CHA items that can be found to make Charming much longer lasting and effective. One thing that I have seen many Enchanters do is Charm a creature and bring it into the second battle. This is one of the worst tactics you could ever use because there is a high chance that the Charm will break during the battle, and you'll have two creatures attacking you.

Fear
One of the more useful spells for solo combat; Fear can become your best spell quickly in wide-open areas. Fear, when casted successfully, will make the NPC to turn and flee in terror, giving you the ability to cast spells on it, or a more used tactic is getting your animation to hit from behind. It can be also useful in groups when you want to fear something away. Fear and the Druid/Ranger spell Snare can be a deadly combo.

Stun
Color Flux, Color Shift, and Color Skew are the three spells that can become the three most powerful spells that you have in your spell book. Combined, they can control a fleet of NPCs attacking a party and turn them into mindless punching bags. These spells will act like an Area effect Stun spell that centers around the caster. It also has a fairly small radius, so it's best to get right in the middle of the fight. It will, however, not affect the caster or his party members. The first spell you receive is Color Flux at level 4, then Color Shift at 20, and finally Color Skew at 44. They have slightly long recycling times but can be all casted in order, which can be devastating when combined. A simple tactic is to do Color Flux, then right after Shift, wait for it to recycle and then do Color Skew, and then repeat.

Animations
Taking a dagger and magically creating our personal bodyguard. The pet spells of the Enchanter, but unlike others, they do not respond to our commands for they have no mind. The only purpose they serve is to protect the Enchanter at all costs and will engage in combat if anything hits the Enchanter. Unfortunately, because we cannot control what actions they do, these aren't too helpful at times and can be downright deadly inside a dungeon. These are more likely to be used in open areas where you don't run the risk of pulling other enemies back like you do inside a dungeon. A commonly used tactic among the solo Enchanters is to buff your animation with haste spells, and then starting with Tashan on a creature, waiting for it to attack you and then Fearing it while the animation hits it from behind. Many people use this tactic called reverse kiting, and it is the most common to people whom tending to solo.

Illusions
One of the most interesting abilities that the Enchanter has obtained is to create illusions and project it among themselves. They can turn into any race and can even turn into different NPC races like skeletons and elementals. Many of these illusions aren't simply just for looks; they can let you into places that you could not traverse before due to faction. Not only that, but some even come with the effects of the illusions, like turning into a troll will give you the ability to gain hit points faster, or making yourself into a fire elemental will give you an unlimited damage shield. Many of these spells are under estimated and can become a very useful tool to the Enchanter.

Conclusion

The Enchanter is a fun and powerful class to play. It is one that makes the player to think about the situation before he moves. One that you need to understand how to control and use effectively, else it becomes very useless to the group. The class is somewhat more interesting then the others like playing a Warrior which some say is boring and doesn't take a lot of brain power. The Enchanter is something that everyone should experience. It is a long haul to become a great asset inside a group. You may find yourself depressed with the lack of power in the early levels, but those who stick with it earn the greatest reward in the long run.
