Becoming A Warrior

Warrior, the ultimate weaponsmaster and the powerhouse of any group. As a warrior you are the one thing starting between your group and a grisly death at the hands of whatever monstrosity you are fighting. You search the lands of Norrath for new ways to test your skill in battle and so that the glory of your strength may be known to all.The warrior is the master of battle, you have no need for magic which you consider the cowards way of fighting, after all what honorable person would consider fighting in a way where you never actually meet your opponent in face to face battle?

As a warrior you can look forward to wielding the mightiest weapons in Norrath and wearing powerful armor forged by the gods themselves. You have the ability to take damage better than any other class and, when properly armed, deal it out better than any other class. You gain all of your combat skill well before any other class and have a natural resistance to magic which grows every level.

A simple warning before you continue reading this guide: if you wish to solo at all at the mid to high levels then a warrior is not for you. Due to their massive amounts of hit points it takes a warrior as much as a half an hour to rest up un aided before he enters battle once again. A warrior has one primary function in Everquest, flesh shield for his party, it might not sound all that glorious but when a hoard of blood thirsty beasts are descending upon your group and you manage to deflect or absorb most of their blows you know the feeling of true strength.

You will have the most exciting role in battle since you are always in the thick of things and in most cases the battle focuses on what you are doing. Many say that they could never play a class that has so few options in battle, what they don't understand is that since you don't have to worry about casting spells, watching other peoples health, or twisting songs you can concentrate on what is actually going on in battle to make sure that the party is not overwhelmed. Many times a party's leader is a warrior since it is you who give the order to Evac, heal someone, or root a creature before it starts to run away.

Myths and Controversy

  • Since you are in the thick of battle you will always be the first to die

    This simply is not true, since most people in your group understand that you will be the one taking all the damage you are usually the one that gets the most buffs and who the healers concentrate keeping alive. When the going gets real tough and the party needs to run you will usually make it out since you have more hit points than the ranger or bard in your group and are more likely to survive the mad dash to the zone line.

  • You can not deal out the damage of rangers or monks
    • Until the higher levels

    This is not true, you gain dual wield and double attack so early that until 35 or so you are doing much more damage than rangers or monks. Once things get into the higher levels though you will be out damaged by them unless you have a planar weapon. In the end this is the best, if you did the most damage of any class hands down then no one would ever be able to taunt anything off you.

  • Warriors are no fun at high levels since they have so few abilities


    • If you talk to any high level warrior they will tell you that no matter what class you play you will always feel at the high levels that your class is boring and that you are doing the same old things. Warrior more than make up for the lack of skills with all the new weapons and armor they can obtain. Once you reach the planes you will discover that each class has one or two weapons that they can use, you on the other hand can use most weapons dropped, many of the new weapons are WAR RNG, WAR SHD, or just plain WAR.

    Character Creation

    Now it is time to experience playing a warrior, but before hand you must first create it. Creating your character can be simple and complex at the same time. Warriors luckily can concentrate on three main skills since wisdom, intelligence, charisma, and dexterity have very little bearing on a warrior.

    Race

    This is not only a personal choice depending on how you want to role-play, but it will also effect how you play. Each race has different starting stats, different advantages and disadvantage. Here we will list some to you and hopefully you’ll make the correct choice for yourself.

    Half Elf

    • The best choice over all if you are looking for balanced stats and a small experience penalty, most of your main stats average out at around 90-95 with a half elf. They also have the infravision of their pure blood relatives but do not have the high agility of wood elves nor the wisdom of high elves.

    Wood Elf

    • Wood elves are not usually the best choice for a warrior due to their low strength and stamina, the main two stats for a warrior. They do have a natural agility of 100 though, and they are gifted with the abilities of infravision, foraging, and hiding.

    Barbarian

    • Barbarians are a nice choice for warriors since they have a high starting strength and stamina and since they are a large race they have the ability to slam which is the same as bash but without having to use a shield.

    Dark Elf

    • Dark elves are a great class to play if you are going for good looks and you enjoy to roleplay your character. They have a low strength and stamina but they have a high agility and they see with ultravision which is a great advantage when you are pulling things at night.

    Dwarf

    • An excellent race to start out as for a warrior due to their high starting strength, stamina, and dexterity. They also are a small race which makes for good maneuverability in cramped dungeons such as Guk and Solusek.

    Gnome

    • Gnomes are an okay race for warriors since they have a nice starting agility and can see with infravision. Unfortunately few people play them due to the fact that they have an extremely low starting strength.

    Halfling

    • Many warriors are starting to choose halflings as their race these days since they have rather nice starting stats and their natural ability to hide and sneak make up for a warrior's lack of invisibility spells.

    Ogre

    • The best possible race if you are going for brute strength and the ability to take far more damage than other races. Ogres also, due to their size, can slam which is an indispensable ability at high levels since it means the difference between a creature pulling off a superior heal on itself or not.

    Troll

    • Another good race due to their ability to slam and to see in the dark. While they do not possess the obscene strength and stamina of Ogres they do have the ability to regenerate so that soloing is possible at some levels.

    Stats

    Stamina: The single most important stat for a warrior, the more hit points you have the more damage you can take and the longer you can last in battle. A high stamina combined with a high AC makes a warrior near unstoppable.

    Strength: The second most important skill for a warrior since this determines how much damage you are doing in battle, the extra damage you deal from a high strength might not seem all that good on a single hit but spread out over an entire battle it will definitely make a difference.

    Agility: As with stamina this is a good stat for warriors to have, though most people are just beginning to realize that straight AC is much better to have than just high agility so do not sacrifice AC to have a high agility score. Agility also affects how often you dodge, though since you barely ever dodge it is usually not worth pumping this stat up with just that in mind.

    Dexterity: Increases the amount of times your weapon procs and greatly increases the chance of double attacking and getting a critical hit. Dexterity may also affect your chances at getting a crippling blow but this has yet to be tested.

    Conclusion

    The Warrior is one of the most exciting classes to play, you are constantly in the heat of battle and right on the front line of raids. There is nothing quite like seeing a crippling blow for 350 damage that kills a monster who would have otherwise slaughtered your party. You are the ultimate tank and are welcome in any group over most other melee classes. You have the pride of knowing that you are a truly essential part of the battle and that without you your group would be doomed to painful repetitive deaths.
