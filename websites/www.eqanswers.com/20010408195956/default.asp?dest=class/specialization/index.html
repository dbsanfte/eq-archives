Specialization

Specialization is a very important skill which allows the caster and priest type classes to use their spells more efficiently both in terms of mana and fizzle rate. A magician casting Lava Bolt will burn less mana and fizzle less if his evocation is specialized than if it were not. As well as the fact that it can further be specialized into his primary specialization which will allow him or her to use even less mana. In short, it is a very important skill that allows you to make better use of your casting skills.

How does it work?

You are allowed to specialize in all types of specialization to the skill of 50. However, only one can go past 50 and become your primary specialization. With this being said, decided which skill to use as your primary specialization can be a difficult decision. If you do screw up or decided you want to change it later, there is a quest located in the Temple of Ro that will reset all your specialization skills to 49 or 50. Just keep two things in mind: Your Specialize: Alteration and your Alteration skill are two completely different things. Resetting the Specialization Alteration skill will in no way effect your actual alteration skill. Secondly, there are more than one specialization skills. Thus, you will be raising all of them but only one can surpass the skill limit of 50.

When do I get Specialization?

Caster types that use INT as their mana source gain the skills at level 20. While the priests that use WIS as their mana source yield the skills at level 30.

How do I raise them?

The most common way is by spending valuable training points in the skill you plan to have as your primary specialization in order to get that past 50 so you don’t mistakenly raise the wrong specialization skill above 50. Specialization skills will rise naturally with the appropriate casting as well. In other words, Summoning Bread would raise Specialize conjuration. So in short, depending on how you want to do it, there are two good ways to practice the skills.

What skill should I have as my Primary Specialization?

That’s up to you. It’s important to keep your class in mind and what spell you will yield the most at higher levels. Below I have given the top two suggestions to use for each class.

Wizards:

Evocation – Since the primary roll of a wizard is to nuke, this is your best bet. This is the skill you absolutely should specialize in since it will give you a very good advantage with your devastating DD nukes.

Alteration – The only other skill a wizard should even consider specializing in is Alteration. The only reason for this is to improve their evacuation spells. Although, by doing this you lose a big edge on your nuke spells. So to get the most out of Specialization you should honestly go with evocation.

Magicians:

Conjuration – I personally prefer Conjuration in this field since it offers better mana cost for those already fairly low casting blade spells. They take an unbelievably short time to cast for their great damage. This will also help when you have to resummon a pet midbattle. However, the downside to this is until your in your 40’s specializing in Evocation would probably seem the better choice. But, once you reach your high 40’s you will probably be thankful if you did go with Conjuration. Just remember, it all depends on if your going to use bolt series or blade series when you hit level 50.

Evocation – This all comes down to personal taste. Evocation is for your bolt line of spells. While some magicians prefer to use the Blade type of spells (which are summon based) there are those whom prefer to use the fire bolts. Be warned that the fire bolts are resisted far more (magic and fire) than your blade line of spells. Again though, the Bolt of Lava at level 50 will do far more damage than your last blade spell. However, Lava Bolt will also take twice as long to cast.

Enchanters:

Alteration – Most enchanter spells are based on Alteration. Root, dots, charms, haste, slows, and just about every other enchanter spell is based on Alteration. This seems to be the number once choice at least till you hit level 50.

Conjuration – Depending on your game style I have been told numerous times that Enchanters tend to use the mesmerize series of spells more often than not. If this is true, you may want to focus on this. Just remember you can always change, as long as you don’t mind building a new skill back up. Just depends on your personal gaming style.

Necromancers:

Alteration – Necromancer DoTs, charm vs undead, and lifetaps all come from Alteration. You will find this would probably be your best bet. These are used in most combat situations that include both grouping and soloing your necromancer.

Conjuration – Or you could always go with Conjuration which would cover other spells like disease and other "off brand" spells. This will also cover the summoning of your pet.

Clerics:

Alteration – This covers your heals, your resurrections, your roots, and a few other important spells. If you plan on grouping this is the skill to go with. At level 50 I am sure you will appreciate this the most.

Evocation – This is for the clerics that want to solo. Although not as useful at the 40+ levels, it’s still useful. This covers your nukes. Remember, a clerics nuke burns mana fast.

Druids:

Alteration – Because druids have a big decision to make, I went ahead and listed three skills they should choose from. This one is for druids that like to play healer and buffers during fights. If your dealing out damage shields 10 times a fight then this is a very good choice indeed. This covers your buffs and heals as well as the gate line of spells.

Conjuration – This is for the kiting druids. This is your massive line of DoTs. So it comes down to personal taste if you want to go with the offensive side of the druid. DoTs work nicely in groups as well, just not as efficiently when your with other druids.

Evocation – This is the other side of the offensive druid. These cover the DD spells druids will get. Druids DD spells are not very mana efficient as it is so you may quite possibly prefer this considering the DD spells do deal good damage. Although it is more difficult to kite with these, these would work in soloing or grouping rather effectively.

Shamans:

Conjuration – These are for shamans that will be using a lot of DoTs or kiting a great deal. Shaman DoTs fall under this category thus most shamans tend to lean towards conjuration.

Alteration – However, alteration is also a very good choice. The buffing and healing type spells are found within this skill. Either choice is good for a shaman. It all depends on what kind of shaman you will be playing
