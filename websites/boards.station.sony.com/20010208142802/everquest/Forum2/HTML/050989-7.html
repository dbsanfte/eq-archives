I'm going to break this into two sections, "major" and "minor". The major issues are the ones that really make us viable as a class. The minor ones are, well, things I'd REALLY like to see but of little overall import.

MAJOR ISSUES:
1. Al'Kabor line. It's been detailed above, so I won't clutter the board by repeating information.
2. Uber HP mobs. Even on full burn, assuming no resists, we can't even begin to scratch them without going OOM. Once OOM we are 100% useless to the group and the raid. Can't even Evac (like you would from an encounter like this. The more popular method is to save the clerics and die). If we sit to med, instant agro and we find ourselves at our bind spots. Solution? Already in game: Harvest. More on this later.
3. Normal exp grinds. We cannot keep up with the melee classes here thanks to our mana constrainsts. We sacrifice a LOT to be "the masters of magical damage". Low low HP and AC, mana constraints, had to stare at a spell book until 35, etc. I believe to compensate we should be able to do considerably more damage than the melees are capable of. Not only in the "short term - IE manadump, agroed and dead" but in the long run, the normal single mob pull exp grinds. After all, I made a wizard because I wanted to do THE damage. Solution? Again, Harvest. See number 5 for a proposed solution.
4. Spell fizzles. Atol's Spectral Shackles comes to mind...
5. Harvest, a spell that most pre-50 Wizards have memorized at all times. Roughly three quarters of a bub of mana... 10 second cast time, 10 second stun. 10 minute recast time. This spell is great. The ability to pull additional mana out of the air in a sticky situation is truly amazing. Off set and balanced by the 10 second cast and stun. I like that. I find myself WANTING to cast it but unable to risk being stunned. This spell is amazingly balanced, and I tip my hat to the design team. HOWEVER, the 10 minute recast time seems to only be in place soas to not make us solo kings. I can appreciate that and wholeheartedly agree. But in a group it should function differently. In a group it should not have that restriction. And so I propose two possible solutions, one of which may not be possible to code and the other trivial. ONE: reduce recast time by 90 seconds for every member of the party other than yourself. It makes sense that a larger group would be pulling faster and killing more. Thus, with a six man group Harvest would have a recast time of 600 - (90 * 5) = 150 seconds, or two and a half minutes. This would GREATLY enhance the wizard's mana supply and therefor the amount of damage he can generate for the group (and therefor his appeal to the group). It also does not enhance that same wizard's ability to solo. AT ALL. However, this may not be possible to implement under the current system. So the second solution I propose is this: Scale the amount of mana harvested by the number of members in the group. Above, in a six man group the wizard can harvest four times faster so this way he should get four times as much mana - but only once every 10 minutes. Perhaps you should also increase the length of the stun, to keep the same balance as the spell now has.

MINOR ISSUES:
1. Our spell lineup seems to indicate that we are the king of self only sight. True North, See Invisible (not self only but a sight spell none the less), Halo of Light, Heat Sight, Chill Sight, the Bind Sight series, and the Glimpse Series... And Sense Summoned. Which brings me to the issue at hand. Where is our Sense Animal? Sense the Dead? Sense Plant? It seems only natural to me that the king of self only sight would be able to sense these things as well as Summoned.
2. Sphere of Light. This has always seemed a bug to me. It irritates me to no end that I am able to conjure something out of thin air using Divination. This spell should be on the Conjuration skill. Not only for roleplaying purposes but for gameplay as well. Until the upper 50s wizards have TWO, yes that's right. TWO. 1 + 1.. Spells that use the Conjuration skill. Eye of Zomm and Halo of Light. Both have long reloads and for their level, hefty mana costs. Thus it becomes quite difficult for a wizard to practice his Conjuration skill. Why do we want to, you ask? Because of point number 3:
3. Flaming Sword of Xuzl. At level 59 we get this "pet" to throw at uber HP mobs or 100% magical resistant ones. Unfortunately, the mana cost of this spell is way to high for the life expectancy of this non-flaming Flaming Sword. One riposte or AE and its toast. And even if it survives, it suicideds when the mob goes down (rendering it totally useless in normal exp grinds). 700 mana down the drain when we could have fired off two Lures or three Draughts. Please either up the life expectancy of this "pet" (increased HP, level, AC, etc. and no suicide) or decrease the mana cost.
4. More teleport spots would be nice. I liked the "old world" where there were portals everywhere except near Qeynos and I knew I had at most two or three un-SoW'd zones (smaller zones, too, I might add) to run. With the addition of Kunark and Velious I find myself relying more upon my Bard, Ranger, Druid, and Shaman friends for traveling than on my portals. Would be nice if this were not so. /e daydreams of how things used to be.
