- Add in Cold Based spells into our arsenal of Damage spells. Make them approximately 5 levels higher than the previous Fire Based Spell but keep them at the same damage level. Hell you could even start them off at the same damage as the previous spell, and then incriment the damage per level at the same rate as the previous one did. Just give us a bit more variety. This would make Mages a bit more useful on Nagafen and Talendor Raids. As it is we can't do anything at all (pets get feared).

- Power of Pets post 50 is rediculous. After the patch to make Pets Duel Wield and making them Pets instead of NPCs, I have noticed a substantial decrease in their power. Add that to the fact that the 49 pet costs 200 mana, maxes for 56 (earth pet), and has ~2500 HPs, and then the next upgrade (for earth) costs 300 mana, maxes for 58, and has ~2600 HPs. Umm, we get an additional 2 damage and ~100 HPs for 100 more mana? How is this "balanced"? At the very least lower the mana cost back to 200. Or increase the Max damage by 4 and add 500 HPs. If a Mages pet is his focus of Power, then as it stands we have less power at 60 when compared to others at 60, while when the cap was 50 we were among the more powerful classes available.

- Malo-line spells. The main idea behind this series of spells is to lower overall resists. Well being Magic Based it is useless against things that are of High MR. We get an Unresistable one of these at level 60, but by then it's too late to be overly useful. Please add in another version of this spell at or around level 53 that is Lure-Based. Make it Double the Mana cost of the level 51 version, and take off 20 points of Resists, making it -40 instead of -60. Upgrade the Mala spell (level 60) to take off -40 instead of -35 but leave it as is in the case of Casting Time and mana cost.

- Magician Spells being given out like Candy to other classes.

You yourself said the following: "DESCRIPTION: Perhaps there is a story reason for giving bards the ability to turn into werewolves. But we should be very careful when we give the more class defining abilities to other classes. The ability to look like a werewolf is the signature illusion for enchanters. Even if we don’t remove this lute, we most certainly should think twice before giving such abilities to classes in the future."

Here is a list of some Magician Abilities given to other classes:
Summon Arrows (Level 29 summon) - Tolan's Darkwood Bacer
Pouch of Quellious (Level 55 summon) - Silver Moon Wrist Wraps
Eye of Zomm (Level 8 summon) - Singing Steel Helm
Shield of Flame (Level 20 Damage Shield) - Singing Steel Bracer
Summon Throwing Dagger (Level 16 Summon) - Mrylokar's Bracer

And this is but a few. The big one there is the level 55 spell being on a mildly common drop from Plane of Growth (not to mention the ability to be quested for via Velious Armor quests). If you are going to go ahead and do that to us, then give us that spell on a vendor. As it is, I have not seen in yet, and this is from about 5 eight hour Jugg Raids, and countless hours in Skyfire. If you worried about Enchanter Illusion Spells being given out then at the same time worry about Magician Summon Spells being given out. It's only the right thing to do.

- Resist Rates on Rain Spells.

A scenario: Single pull Sea Fury. I sic my pet on him and let him build up a little aggro. I decide to use a rain spell so I Malosi. I then cast the Rain spell. Wave 1: RESIST. Wave 2: FULL DAMAGE. Wave 3: PARTIAL DAMAGE. I nuke twice, FULL DAMAGE on both. I cast Rain spell again. Wave 1: RESIST. Wave 2: RESIST. Wave 3: PARTIAL RESIST.

You see a pattern? I could spend a day in OOT hunting Seafuries for you and log all my Rain Spell Damage if you want. And let's not forget that the Seafury could move out of the way, virtually negating 1 or even 2 waves. Whereas if I decided to use just straight DDs then I would almost never get resisted after casting Malosi (95% of the hits are Full damage. 4% are partial. 1% is Full Resist).

- Call of the Hero. I think an upgrade should exist, and that the current version should be scaled to level 52. The Upgrade should be level 56. For the Upgrade it should have the ability to summon group members across Zonelines. Make the component cost 4-5X the cost of the first. Double the Mana cost. Make it so that Mages would only use this spell on things like Complete Group Wipeouts. Make the Recast time like 5 mins or so. Adding all that into it would offset it's power. Moving the first one to level 52 would insure that many more mages would be able to get groups. If your focus here is to increase the viabilty of classes in a group, doing this would be enough for plenty of mages.

- Elemental Armor Series. We get the first spell at level 20. It gives +20 to Fire and Cold resist. The upgrade is 24 levels later. It gives +30 to Fire and Cold resist. No other Upgrade exists. This seems a little but much for a good series of spells. Magicians command the Elements so they should get protection of sorts from them, but ony 2 spells in the series, 24 levels apart and only a measly +10 to both resists is a joke. Move the Elemental Armor (level 44) spell to level 34. Make an Upgrade at level 49 that gives +40 to both resists. Make another upgrade at level 55-57 that adds +50 to both resists. Make it so other Resist spells that offer less of a resistance rate do not overwrite it.

Add in a counter line that gives us the ability to bestow this type of spell on others, at a higher mana cost and reduced resist. Like +20 FR and CR for 100 mana at level 29. +30 FR and CR for 200 mana at level 44. +40 FR and CR for 400 mana at level 54.

This is not an unreasonable request given that Druids and Clerics get a similar line of Resist Specific spells, both that are targettable and, in the case of Druids post 50, which adds it to the entire Group.

- Modulation Rod. Good spell. I have my own version of crack (clarity) to give people. Too bad it's useless to me. Make an upgrade that adds in an additional Charge at level 57 or so.

- Mana Regen. If anything add in a quest at around the level 49 level (make part of it Planar specific so only 46+ can complete) that would get us a Magician Specific Robe that had virtually no stats (About the same as an Oracle Robe) and Effect: Summon Modulation Rod. Make the cast time like 20 seconds. This in itself would be a form of Mana regen, and it would be specific to Mages above level 46 so it wouldn't be as overpowered as people would think it is, especially given such a long cast time.

- Magician Epic. I know you said no Epic item discussion on here but I have to add this. At 500 mana this thing is a pain to summon. What increases the frustration is the fact that even after summoning it we have to wait 20 seconds for our Pet to arrive. This is obviously an oversight. If we have to spend the time to cast it, that should be enough. All the other Right-Click Epics take less overall time to summon and in some cases can be done twice in the time it takes a mage to Summon, Equip, and Right Click, then wait for the pet to arrive. Make the Right-Click instant cast.

That is it. Thank you for your time.

I will agree with you that there is very little overall that is wrong with the balance of this class....pre-50 anyways. Post 50 is a different matter. For one thing our 50+ spells are rather bland and unexciting for the most part. Summon Booze and fireworks? Summon a bag full of arrows. Uh...yay? Mages are not always welcome either, as you state, because of their pets. In fact there are instances where a Mage's pet is specifically prohibited(Hate for example). The reason is usually terrible pathing and aggro problems. Without the pet the Mage is a poor second to a Wizard although can still be useful to the group because of damage shields, dispels, and Malise. In fact the only time a Mage is considered essential to a group is one that has Call of the Hero. It is for that one spell alone that Magicians are invited into a group. You state that we're all the more welcome because of the Malise line....not if it gets resisted all the time. I can't use it in Hate at all at level 52. This is a minor complaint though. Let's address some other issues.

Fear: Yes, pets get feared and run. I don't find this a problem. I take preventative measures against this by having someone cast Magic Resist buffs on the pet. I keep a focus item with Reclaim Energy Handy to desummon it if it gets feared. If it's out of range of the focus item I use the effect on Earthen Boots. Pet gone...no more problem. Resummon it and send the new one in again. I don't see the need for any anti-fear buff that is castable by mages.

No Rent Items/LD: Doesn't bother me...much.

Rain Spells: I find that they are too mana inefficient for the damage they do, especially since I am almost guaranteed to have at least one wave resisted...and more often than not multiple waves resisted. An extra wave as well as tuning the resist rates a bit would help. Resists on this seem to usually be all or nothing. Very rarely do the waves ever hit for partial damage.

Summoned Procing Weapons: We had a summoned weapon that procced for a very long time. We got used to it. Then you took it away. Yes we can still summon it but it's not practical or useful anywhere outside the Hole. The idea of being able to equip pets with weapons is very intriguing to me. I'd like to see more weapons that are pet only with procs on them. Stuns, DD spells, Self Haste, Malise, Fear, Renew Elements, etc. I'd also like to be able to equip my elementals with 2 handed weapons please. The 50+ spells are pretty barren of any useful summons spells. This would largely solve that issue. Don't tell me Summoning arrows or shurikens are useful. It's convenient but hardly anything worth getting excited about.

Pathing: Needs improvment. 'Nuff said.

50+ Fire pets: I recently acquired this spell so I was able to finally get some first-hand experience with it. Yes, it's very situational. No, they can't out-tank the 49 firepet. Damage output is less overall than the 49 pets and it really isn't useful when soloing. Was it intended the 50+ firepets do less damage than the 49 pets? Anyways, what they do well is interrupt casters. I love this pet...just wish it wouldn't move into melee range immediately. Can you make it so the 50+ fire-pets hang back and cast until something moves in to melee with them or attacks the caster?

Spell Drops: I like the idea. I hate the implementation. They're far too rare and too random. I can spend hours killing monsters that are supposed to drop spells and only see 2 or 3 if I'm lucky. Some areas are better than others true. What's worse is the spells that drop aren't likely to be ones I can use. It would alleviate some of the frustration if I knew the mobs were going to be dropping ONLY spells for my particular class. Either increase the drop rates quite a bit or else have mob X only drop class X's spells. That way it sitll takes time to get the spells but I know if I stay there long enough I will come away with something for my effort that I can actually use!

DoT's: I love em. Yeah, they aren't the most mana efficient but they're something different...if they aren't resisted they do full damage. They don't generate the aggro a full nuke would either.

High level Pet Healing: As much as I'd like it I'd have to say no. It would make Magicians too powerful.

Beneficial Buffs: Sometimes I get beneficial buffs cast on me by well meaning players...for example clerics, that overwrite my own or I simply don't want...but can't remove them! I should always be allowed to remove benefical buffs.


There have been a LOT of other really good suggestion given which, unfortunately, I did not write down. I hope you've been taking notes. There are other issues on my mind but those have largely been discussed already.
