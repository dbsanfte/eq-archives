Something I haven't yet seen covered is mid level armor options for paladins. With the recent introduction of craftable plate, it has become easier for paladins to obtain decent armor for the levels 25-46 (at which point planar armor replaces the class specific armor, in our case, Armor of Ro). While those with lots of money may find more viable options earlier on, you will find that (and I don't mean to generalize, but I think this is true) people who play paladins tend to want to earn their equipment more than people that play other classes (again, this is a generalization; it just turns out that people with this mentality are more likely to initially choose a paladin as their first class when first starting the game). Anyway, back to the point: Ro armor is, especially in comparison with most other class armors, too hard to obtain. Warrior crafted armor is by far the simplist (maybe too simple, but that isn't the point here... nor do I wish to see any other classes get nerfed). I would say that the Ranger armor quests should set the standard of difficulty for the rest of the class armor quests (with the exception of the ridiculously long camping time involved in obtaining Ivy greaves; God forbid you should not attack him within the first 10 seconds of his spawning, either! ... I think I'm going to the ranger board when I'm done here). EACH piece of Ro armor requires that a piece of Ronium be created, requires the following ingredients:

1. 2 bars of enchanted platinum
-this is alright, since most other armors require a gem of some sort, and this costs rougly the same
2. 1 bar of melatite
-this is ridiculous: not only do the clockworks range in level from 25-40, but they're GNOMES! Gnomes have done NOTHING wrong, and there is no reason that paladins should have to hunt down their creations
3. 1 sands of Ro
-I suppose this is alright given the volume of Sand Giant hunters that this can be gotten from, but I don't think a component required for EVERY piece of armor should be on this high level a creature
4. 1 soil of Underfoot
-again, ridiculous: WHY should we have to kill fairies? They have done nothing wrong! What would possess any holy warrior to tell his disciple, "Go out and slaughter some fairies, then return with their remains"???? I suggest this be changed to some other component... I would suggest brownies, but then we again have the problem of a high level creature carrying a component meant for EVERY piece of armor. Maybe something from goblins would be more appropriate (or that could replace melatite, since you'd still have to go to Sol. A to get it)
5. 1 mistmoore granite
-this wouldn't be so bad if mistmoore weren't so nasty. The other problem is how deep into mistmoore one must go in order to obtain these. I would suggest either removing the No Drop tag on these (I don't like this, because then people will be attempting to make a profit on paladins, and we'll be back to the problem of not having "earned" your armor), or making all gargoyles in Mistmoore drop this (currently only the level 30+ gargoyles deep in the castle drop it; the ones in the courtyard should as well, but maybe more rarely)

Aside from Ronium creation, we ALSO need to obtain through other quests the molds needed for this armor, and then smith it ourselves. Since the smithing skill check was removed, I don't mind this part, but some of the armor molds are ridiculous:

Bracer of Ro: requires 2 deepwater goblin nets
-these nets drop off a 2 hour spawn in Lake Rathe. 2 nets per bracer means 8 hours spent getting the molds, plus the added time of getting the ronium. On top of that, if the goblin is on land, and he is rooted or snared, when he is near death (less than 25% health remaining), he will suicide and this results in NO nets. This is obviously a bug and requires fixing, but the point is, one should not have to invest a minimum of 6 hours in getting a piece of armor.

Vambraces of Ro - requires 2 rotten shark meat
-again, a couple things make this a poor quest, especially in comparison to some other class quest armors. Firstly, there is another despawning problem with the shark, this time with its corpse: when the shark dies, if it is not looted IMMEDIATELY (by this I mean the corpse is checked and the meat is grabbed within 2 seconds of death), the corpse sometimes disappears. Also, the shark is again level 30+ (last I checked I believe it was 34-36). It is my belief that these armors should be attainable in the late 20s (not necessarily on your own, especially not for more crucial pieces such as greaves and breastplates), and the creatures that drop components should reflect this range; I would knock 3-5 levels from the shark if I were to alter this quest in any way. I don't mind the 2 hour spawn time so much, but I would also like it if he dropped the meat every time; current drop rates tend to be 50%. It's a big shark, how hard can some meat be to find? There isn't much reason to make a paladin who wants his armor kill the same shark 4 times over 8 hours.

If paladins are to learn their role of the tank, good armor is needed early on. While the fine plate that has been added helps in achieving this goal, it is my opinion that Armor of Ro (why is it called that, anyway? Ro is a wizard deity... just because we go to his temple to obtain it (why, too, is that?) doesn't mean it should be named after him) should not be as difficult as it is to obtain. Ronium is far too hard for the level this equipment is geared at, and at least 2 of the quests for molds have bugs and long camp times which make them either very difficult or very frustrating. I ask that this be examined in detail and reviewed so that the quests do not become oversimplified, either. Thank you for your time and patience, and I hope to hear something concerning this topic soon.

Post 50 issues are certainly important. Please do not forget many others aren't quite at that level yet and DO have issues as well. I applaud them for bringing up issues I might deal with some day, but mid-levels should be examined as well.

Balance:

Warrior crafted armor is, and should be, better than paladin Ro armor. It is part of them being a better tank. However, warriors can and do get crafted quite easily by the time they reach mid 20's in level. Paladins are still finding it difficult to get Ro even into their late 30's.

Ro armor quests are simply so difficult and time consuming that Ro armor is amazingly rare when compared to crafted. End result? Ro armor sells for twice the price, or worse, paladins buy something else. With more and more post 50 characters being developed, Deepwater Armor (which drops) is becoming as common as Ro. As such it is almost as cheap as Ro.

Proposed Solution:

Increase the spawn rates of the 'key' monsters needed to complete the quest (e.g. Nightfall Giant, Goblin Netmasters, Undead Cyclops, etc.). Drop rates on these guys should also be automatic. The mistmoore granite should drop from those easier gargoyles in the front yard as well. The melatite should be dropping from the various goblins.

When all is said and done, paladins still have to cough up 2 bars of enchanted platinum for each piece and NONE of the key items would come any easier, just more frequently.

In a game called "Everquest" isn't the point to encourage the young paladins to actually perform some quests? Instead, due to extreme difficulty, and a prize that is less protecting than crafted (which can be had 10 levels earlier and/or for half the price), the game has evolved into "Neverquest".

Just my $.02 on the idea of a protective discipline for high level Paladins: First off, i really like the idea, as it matches well with the persona of a noble knight, and matches the defensive/protective nature of a holy knight.

How I think it should work: When activated on a target, the paladin takes 100% of the damage directed at the target for a period of time (I won't try to determine what that time should be), with the following limitations:
1. The paladin must remain within a certain proximity to the target to be effective (I can't very well step between you and an incoming sword/fireball/whatever if i am on the other side of the room.)
2. The paladin cannot protect the target from AOE effects and will not take damage from anything that occurred prior to the activation of the discipline (like dots). Possibly this should only be effective against line-of-site type spells, as other spells may be able to just hit the target straight out, but then, maybe the paladin can intercept some of those. This would be especially important in order to prevent abuse of cannibalize type spells.
3. The paladin cannot cast or attack while using the discipline - he/she is concentrating all effort on defending the target. Possibly riposte should be allowed though, as it is a type of defensive skill.
4. The paladin cannot protect the target from damage caused by falling.

One other alternative that had occurred to me was that the paladin could target a mob and take all of the damage output from that mob for a time. I suspect that this would be rather impracticle, since most uber mobs would kill a paladin rather quickly in that situation, and it may also be rather unbalancing (There is probably a potential for abuse that hasn't occurred to me in there somewhere), but there it is anyway.

Just some thoughts, and thanks for the hard work Absor.
