Hi Absor,
I posted this suggestion to the necromancers on raids thread part 3 or something like that already. It is a suggestion that might help to balance a little the use of the necromancer on an epic mob encounter, but will have minimum to no effect on other encounters and will not introduce the unbalacing effect of unresistable damage. Before getting to this I'd like to say some things regarding the necromancer's role in general.

The necromancer has actually always been a support caster in a group, as well as a damage doer, this is clear because from the beginning necromancers have had the ability to heal a little as one of the core abilities they bring to a group. In patches since release the necromancers grouping abilities have been improved with the addition mana transfer spells, which when combined with the call of bones type of spells can be used to help out a group remarkably if the necromancer just focuses on using his lifetap--bond of death lines of spells to keep himself healthy while generating mana. The ability to debuff in the form of the asystole line was also added, but was really not the most significant group specific enhancement. Thus, to recap, in addition to doing some damage, the main thing a necromancer brings to a raid group is the ability to use his own life to replenish others and to fill the mana of other casters, and then use his own spells to efficiently replenish that life.

Now, let's think about lifetaps a little, how they worked in the past, how they work now, and the balance issues involved. Lifetaps and lifetap dots were unresistable in the beginning by any mob. Arguably, this was a bug, however, more likely it was an oversight brought on by the original intentions for the working of these spells. I believe that it is likely lifetaps were irresistable and remain so within the lvl cap except for immune mobs because the game designers wanted necromancers to have a guaranteed way of healing themselves as long as a mob is engaged, after all, can other classes heals be resisted? However, a consequence of this original working of these spells was that they made for unresistable damage, thus they were turned into all or nothing spells that generally function 100% against vulnerable mobs within the lvl cap. This balancing was definitely needed, and lifetaps should never become irresistable forms of damage again, for the simple reason that a group of necromancers casting them together could take on things well beyond what other balanced groups of similar lvl could do. But at the same time, this very reasoning has crippled the necros role as support caster against mobs that resist the lifetaps. Thus, whatever change would be implemented would have to respect both the idea that necros do not get unresistable damage, but at the same time allow them to use their support abilities as intended while engaged to a mob.

Therefore, I have a proposal that I believe can respect the two aspects of balancing necros I discussed above, but first an observation. Bond of death line of spells is the most efficient spell to use for the necromancer's group support spells, and is a spell very representative of what the necromancer is about, being over time and a lifetap. Furthermore, I have noticed that if I am in a raid with a higher lvl necromancer there too, that if I cast this spell it will not take hold on the target, but still I will gain it's healing on myself, even though it does no damage to the target in this instance. As an extension of this, I propose that the healing aspect of this spell be made guaranteed provided there is a valid target(NPC, not PC in non-PVP) to cast it on, whether the damage part of the spell is resisted or not. By implementing this idea, the necromancers efficacy against boss mobs is still limited by the fact that the rate of healing he can muster on himself is limited by the speed of the dot, and no form of irresistable damage is added to the game. Moreover, this change will have no affect whatsoever on necromancers outside fo raids, and will not increase their ability to inflict damage in that context, but will allow them to utilize their primary group abilities during the fight, which are still quite balanced with other support casters in this role. Absor, I hope you will read this and consider the suggestion, and pass it on, I believe it is the balanced solution to the problem of necromancers feeling useless in the epic encounters.

Statement of problem:

Everquest is a game based in grouping and social contact.

Necromancers are a soloing class.

Conclusion:

Necromancers need to be modified in order to fit into Everquest.

Detail:

Necromancers are able to solo because of having three things, a pet, fear, and snare.
These give a necro the choice to solo to 50, if he/she so chooses. The problem comes about when a necromancer wants/needs groups. If the reason a necromancer cannot find a group is he/she soloed too long and now lacks the skills to be accepted into a group, or just do not have the contacts to find a group, once again because of soloing, then they should have to deal with their choice.

However, groups do not choose necromancers to join their groups, in many cases forcing the person to solo regardless of the necromancer’s preference.

DoTs don’t damage fast enough to be effective in a group, or will get the necromancer killed, which will slow the group. Necromancers do not nuke well (or at all). Necromancers can transfer mana, but not as quickly as a mage can with mod rods. Necromancers have pets, but they are of limited use indoors and are out matched by mage pets. Necromancers can snare and fear, but snare is by no means unique and fear again is of limited use in most situations involving a group. Necros also have a mez spell, but I have not seen people hunt post 50 with out an enchanter. What is the thing that makes necros unique so that they will get one of the highly prized spots in a group of 6? That is the problem with necros in groups. Other classes do every thing we do better in groups.

The argument about soloing does not hold valid either. Druids and wizards can quad kite, but are still able to find groups because of their abilities. Monks can solo post 50, to a point, and are able to limit down time with bandages and mend. Good enchanters can solo with their mind-bending spells. I’ve seen many bards soloing with their charming. With the changes made to the necromancer class, I do not think we are the best soloing class now (this is up to personal opinion and I’m sure people on both sides could have very good arguments to support their view).

Necromancers are lacking a nitch of their own in groups. Verant seems to be VERY against giving necromancers anything new, for fear of necros using it to solo better. I agree that our soloing ability is as high, if not higher, then it should be, but look and see that we are not alone in this any longer. We used to be the most over powered class in the game that is true, but is it fair that we have to pay for past power when the classes are so much closer to balance? Necromancers are now under-used for groups. We are just not needed in groups. And if you don’t want us to solo, then what are we left with?

Please put some time into these concerns. Address them instead of dismissing them. Verant seems to be against anything that might encourage any more necromancers. (I can show you how dumb our epic looks as proof). What about the necromancers who have invested a lot of time trying to group and make friends there? We are getting left behind by our friends because they can get spots, and we cannot.

Maybe let us give mana faster (same ratio), but we need to be the best at something. Right now we do a bit of it all, but at high levels, that does not cut it. If there were more bards on my server, I doubt I would get any groups compaired to their unlimited mana song. We just cannot group well enough yet with the tools that have been given to us to warrent a spot in a group (given a choice between other classes).
