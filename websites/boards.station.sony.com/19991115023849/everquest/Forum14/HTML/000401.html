What is the best tactic for healing my tank(s) without pulling all the MoBs on me? I can handle the single kill fine with my tanks taunt or using root. However, with multiple baddies i always draw most with my first heal. Please help!

There are a couple things you can do. One, make sure your group understands the problem. Most the tanks I group with will hit every mob a little so that it agros on them. This helps a lot. Second, use divine barrier and divine aura. They are anti-taunts, and will get most mobs off you. Third, tell your party you are being attacked. Every group I have been in will come get it off you. If they don't have a free healer, they are dead.

Station Member 		posted 09-17-99 01:54 PM           
The absolute best way is to put druid/mage damage shields on your tank before pulling. You will almot *never* get a mob on you after healing if the tank has a good damage shield on him. Damage shields are essential in the higher levels to keep your cleric alive and ticking.

kolour
Station Member 		posted 09-17-99 02:29 PM           
Here is another idea. Use one heal early on a tank here a tank there. Space out the healing early.
I used to pop on heal on the guy getting hurt the most early. Bubble and half down gets a heal. I then switch to the next guy and wait for him to be 2 bubbles down. Heal. By this time your first melee monster will be to about 2 bubbles down. Root the mob if possible. Then you can heal and wait as needed.
Seems to work fairly well, by this time, the mob will be half or lower and not a threat. Med time. ) Hope this might be another tactic you can try. Just stay safe, hard to find a good healer these days.
