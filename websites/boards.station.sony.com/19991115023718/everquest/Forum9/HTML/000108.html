It has come to my attention from reading many of the messages on this board is the fact that, in general, wizards are considered in the majority by other classes to be kill stealers. As a wizard i find this a rather irritating viewpoint as i and others have tried very hard to fight fairly and not steal kills. Granted that i am no level 50 wizard, but in my travels i have only come across 1, maybe 2, true kill stealing wizards. One of the only reasons i can come up with for this invalid assesment is that because we do so much damage and posess the ability to easily steal kills from even higher level PCs it is automatically assumed that we would. This is not true for many of us. The only other reason i can come up with is based on another valid mistake. Because we have low HP and little melee skills it is important that we use a good deal of strategy to do as much damage before we actually come into contact with are targetand are forced to fight in close quarters. Because of this we try to attack from the greatest possible distance thus allowing us to do a great deal of damage while the target is running toward us. Unfortunately for some reason, monsters are visible at much longer distances that PCs. So often if we see something in the distance that we would like to attack we stay well out of range and fire. Because our spells do so much damage we usually kill whatever we aim at before the misunderstanding can be cleared up. This is, in my experience the most valid argument that has led up to the misconseption that the majority of wizards are kill stealers. Whether you agree or disagree with this i suggest that you post your view on the matter here as i would like to know what people think and where this whole thing came from.

There are actually 3 seperate issues here:

1. Is it immoral for a caster to sit at a spawn point invisible, waiting on the MOB to show up, letting others kill things in which he's not interested? I can see both sides of the coin here. He's camping, just like everyone else...he's just not killing stuff in which he's not interested. Using the spells at your disposal is certainly valid. If you don't like this...you're a ranger. You should have Hide. <shrug>

2. What about camping stuff solely for loot? I'd love to see Verant implement a feature such that you only get loot from stuff from which you also get exp. Or maybe only apply that to good loot. (Hey...if I can't get batwings, it'll seriously hurt my attempts to enhance my culinary talents).

3. Casters can, and do, kill-steal from non-casters. Wizards get the reputation for that because, realistically, that's what Verant designed us for. This tends to be the worst at camps. Crushbone and Highpass are, off the top of my head, the worst places in the game for this.

Issue 3 is really the big one this thread is revolving around. What can be done about it?

1. Nerf the wizard class even further. We have the most limited spell selection in the game. Worst hit points, worst combat tables. In exchange for this, at the highest levels, our damage spells are about 20% better than a magician's. It'd be hard to make the class any less powerful and more useless to a group (which is what matters) without just totally deleting them.

2. Change the exp/loot system. Note: going to a cumulative group thing will not help. If I want to KS, under those rules, I can easily find 3-4 anti-social bastards who would be happy to group up and help. In which case, the same problem exists.

3. If you want an item that's heavily camped, just buy it, and then go enjoy playing the game. If you don't enjoy camping and competing for kills, then don't. There are lots of places which aren't camped. Admittedly, the loot is not as good. But the Fun is a lot better.
