It simply boils down to who the target customer base is, and what their gaming habits are.

If the target base is the average player (by Verant's own statistics) who can play 2-3 hrs a day and a little more on the weekend who can usually become involved in a group for the duration of their time online ...

Then every important or necessary or top notch piece of content in the game should be eventually accessable via this playstyle.

You can argue all you want that "uber" encounters "should" give greater reward. They should drop MORE ... just not BETTER or EXCLUSIVE.

When Absor aruges that 90% of the client base "just doesn't choose to do what it takes to experience that content" ... he misses the point entirely. The point isn't to alienate or frustrate 90% of your client base by moving exclusive content outside of their normal gaming habits and then justify it by claiming, "well, you could if you WANTED to invest the time and effort."

90% of your client base doesn't WANT to invest that kind or effort in your game. This is who you're trying to PLEASE and RETAIN as customers. This demographic is your PRIMARY CONCERN.

Yes, it would be NICE if you could keep the "ubers" happy as well. So, you offer them something ... uber encounters that drop 5-7 times as much loot ... but the SAME loot ... as those encounters geared to be hard for the full group of average players.

You don't RESTRICT items, quests, loots, gear, etc TO those that can go on multiple uber-raids, or blithely state that "there are other ways of getting most of those items" (like buying them). It's OKAY that you have zones and MoBs that REQUIRE uber-raids to take down .. but it is NOT OKAY that you have those uber-encounters drop EXCLUSIVE or NECESSARY or WAY COOL items that REQUIRE uber-raid events to achieve.

Everquest is designed around the 6-person group concept. Their target audience spends 2-3 hours at a sitting in the game on average. The mechanics of the game "should" be that an average group of 6 with 2-3 hours to invest can accomplish any single goal or encounter in the game in some fashion.

Uber encounters should produce a greater QUANTITY of drops ... but nothing EXCLUSIVE. Otherwise, you're pandering to very small, exclusive clique of players .... and guess what?

Your claims to the contrary notwithstanding, most of your customer base can SEE that you are.

The very fact that uber encounters exist, AND drop exclusive and necessary loot for many aspects of the game, demonstrates that you are indeed pandering to that tiny segment of your customer base.

NOTHING, essentially, meaningfully, should be out of the reach of an average group of 6. Might take longer, and require more patience, but the game should be designed around the average 6-person group, not the uber elite and THEIR gaming habits.
