With all due respect I would have liked the following to be included about levels, classes and experience:

Levels 1-20. Every class will be able to solo (i.e. kill blue mobs 1 on 1 given full health, mana, buffs, etc. at start of battle) given the equipment that coin loot can purchase from merchants. This will become harder as you approach 20 and some higher blue mobs (ones nearer your level) may be too tough a challenge to some race/class combinations.

Levels 21-30. With appropriate equipment warriors, rogues, and wizards will no longer effectively be able to solo. Unless battling undead it will become difficult for clerics to solo.

Levels 31-40. Hybrid classes, although still capable of soloing become markedly inefficient as they increase to level 40. Beyond these levels
a monks ability to solo is very small.

Levels 41-50. Hybrid soloing becomes very situtational (i.e. posible against certain blue mobs in this zone, but not other blue mobs in this or other zones). The only classes that are designed to solo at this level are druids, magicians, and necromancers. Additional classes that can solo, but may require more selective hunting are Shaman, Enchanter, Bard, and Enchanter.

Levels 51-50. A Ranger becomes a damage supporting melee class and are not intended to take hits beyond level 50. Clerics become the most desired healers. Druids and Shaman now enter there role as supporting healer and buffer/debuffer (Shaman) and nuker/evacer (Druid).
At this level of play an Enchanter may become a necessary group member if one wishes to explore certain areas of some dungeon zones. Paladins and Shadowknights are designed to supplement their damage dealing in groups by also taking damage (although a Warrior is preferred in this capacity) and by use of their spells. Monks are a valuable addition as they inflict the second-highest amount of melee damage and posses some skills that can make hunting areas safer for them and their partner. Rogues inflict the greatest amount of melee damage and provide useful reconaissance and recovery skills. Bards, through the use of their many songs, enhance almosy any group situation.

Alan, this is what came out of my head with no planning or revisions. Now, I *could* write a level 56-60 part to this summary, but I have one problem: how do I say with a straight face that "A Paladin gains buffs and heals nearly as good as a Cleric, including the highly sought-after spell Resurrection. This makes a Paladin a tremendous asset to any group that does not have a Cleric." I *could* do a white-wash of the problems that plagues all the classes that are not (clr/war/rog/ench/brd). The BEST I could come up with (with a clear conscience) is the following:

Levels 55-60. These levels are primarily designed for muliple-group 'raids' and groups that contain the most 'balanced' mixture of classes. At this level a group of 1 warrior, 2 rangers, 2 druids and a shadowknight will not be able to perform with nearly the efficiency of a group that consisted of a warrior, a rogue, an enchanter, a cleric,a bard, and a wizard for example. This is by design in order for the game to remain a challenge for more balanced groups. At these levels it is more useful to perform 1 task very well, then 3 tasks moderately well. Warriors will 'tank' better than Shadowknights and Paladins. Rogues will inflict more damage than Rangers or even Monks. A Cleric will be a much more efficient healer than a Shaman or Druid. These differences continue to increase until at level 60 there is a noticeable separation in the specialized classes ability to perform a function over the same task being performed by a more general class (i.e A Rogue being 'specialized' in inflicting melee damage will become better at this relative to the ability of a Ranger to do the same). Groups may seek out members who can fill one particular role rather than a generalist who has more flexible (but less focused) abilities. Solo classes may continue to solo. But doing so at these levels is very risky. Therefore these classes gain some abilities that make them useful to groups, although they will never be as useful (in general situations) as the classes that are designed to need groups to fight with.

I VERY much would have liked to have seen such a piece of Info in the FAQ. It is as optimistic about the 'end-game' levels as it can be. If I had known the above I either A) would have selected a different class, or B) quit after my free month.

I honestly hope this has been helpful.
