DAMAGE INCREASE

Flying Kick:

This is going to be first as it is the most necessary.

PROBLEM: Special Attacks were included to enhance the overall damage capacity of the class. Unfortunately, do to the random number generator used to calculate damage, more often than not, the overall damage done with this attack is less than that attained through the use of Hand to Hand, One Hand Blunt, or Two Hand Blunt methods.

SOLUTION: Enhance the overall damage capacity of this attack through the use of a MINIMUM and MAXIMUM damage output.

I have worked on the numbers here and the best solution I can come up with is this:

MINIMUM: lvl/2 (If not acceptable, lvl/3)
MAXIMUM: (lvl x 2)+10

I can understand the point of having the minimum damage set at 3 but only at the beginning of the learning process. As one continues to learn the skill, the chance of the grazing attack should be limited OR it should get to the point where although the grazing attacks do exist, they’re precise enough to do considerable damage to indicate his/her growing prowess in the art. This minimum damage would exist as a hard cap thus meaning that although STRENGTH and ATTACK modifiers exist, they will not and cannot be added to the minimum.

You will also notice that I have included a maximum. The reasoning behind this that 1) it shows us as being willing to give something up to gain something and 2) I did a poll of the monks 51-60 and the highest Flying Kick that was reported was a 149 and that was with a max STR and the epic ATK bonus. So, the max hard cap does not affect us in the slightest. This maximum damage would exist as a hard cap thus meaning that although STRENGTH and ATTACK modifiers exist, they will not and cannot be added to the maximum in order to exceed this value.

Another point that needs to be added here is that the AVERAGE needs to be rescaled and have a better probability of reaching the average needs to be applied. I, at the level of 51, find that I rarely, like Raster rare, ever hit triple digits…maybe once per real life day if that much. I also find that I hit the minimum or 30 and below to the minimum around 75% of the time. I generally punch for more than I kick and very rarely match or exceed that supposed average. Something needs to be done to address this. The whole intention of a special ability is to add to the overall damage output. And although our specials do indeed do that, the time we sacrifice in-between the cycle time on Flying kick by no means equals the efficiency of the attack.

I also suggest that something along these lines be looked at for the other special attacks of this class.

Disciplines:

PROBLEM: These special attacks came about as an answer to the casters new found power with Kunark. And though they do indeed increase our ability as massive damage dealers, there are ways of making them more efficient and desired to the community without unbalancing the class itself. As it stands, the bulk of the monk community only use 3 or the given Disciplines.

THUNDERKICK (52)

This discipline can be improved via two different options.

Option A:

Delete this as a discipline and add it as an ability once the player achieves level 51. This would be added to the ability list much like Triple Attack for warriors and Double Backstab for the rogues. A check would be made every time the player successfully makes a Flying Kick skill check. Upon doing so, the player would have a percent chance (suggest 15-20%) to have that attack as a THUNDEROUS KICK. This ability would act much like a warrior’s Critical/crippling attacks but with an increased percent chance due to the long recycle time and inaccuracy of the attack. This is perceived as a balanced method as warriors have access to very fast weapons when compared to Flying Kick and therefore have an opportunity to attack more often. Thus, the higher percent chance of scoring a Thunderous Kick is balanced. Higher Percent vs. More Attacks. As above, a minimum and maximum value would be added to the attack to ensure the full usefulness of the ability. (see below) Also, it will be necessary to rescale the average damage output of this ability as was done with the proposed changes suggested to Flying Kick above.

MINIMUM: Level
MAXIMUM: (Level x 3)+20

This is considered to be the most acceptable option as it gives the player a better sense of accomplishment upon reaching the higher levels and it adds to the abilities of the player which ceased after exceeding level 35. Also, with the damage MINIMUM, it allows us to be able to do something special and have it actually affect our damage output instead of landing a Thunderous kick for 10 which we can already do withough this ability.

Option B:
Give this discipline a 14 second duration so as to allow for multiple Thunderous Kicks thereby making the discipline more worthwhile. As above, a minimum and maximum value would be added to the attack to ensure the full usefulness of the ability. (see below) Also, it will be necessary to rescale the average damage output of this ability as was done with the proposed changes suggested to Flying Kick above.

MINIMUM: Level
MAXIMUM: (Level x 3)+20

STONE STANCE (51)

In order to balance the addition of receiving Thunderous Kick as an ability at level 51, I suggest moving Stone Stance to level 52. This change will only affect a small proportion of the monk players as it only constitutes a change in skills/abilities for one level. This is also deemed balancing as the other two melee classes do not receive disciplines until level 52.

ASHEN HAND (59)

This ability increases the damage substantially on the next successful Eagle Strike. It also provides the chance to kill the target outright. Duration = Instant. Recycle Time = 72 minutes.

PROBLEM: Ashen Hand requires the use of Eagle Strike, the lower version of Dragon Punch.

The main issue that Monks currently face is dealing damage in the end game. Prior to Kunark, and the changes in the melee skill caps, we were ahead of all other classes in dealing melee damage with the exception of a backstabbing rogue (which is as it should be).

Since our damage was based on our higher skill caps, and those skill caps were raised for all other melee classes with the Kunark expansion, the gap has closed dramatically. Damage dealing is the only potential a monk adds to a group, especially due to changes in the way the Feign Death skill works, and mobs' reactions to Feign Death.

Currently, a monk offers very little to a group due to the changes in damage. A warrior has higher hitpoints, higher AC, and higher magic resistance -- and thanks to the loss of a skill cap advantage for monks and new, extremely high ratio Velious and Kunark weapons for warriors, monks no longer have a clear advantage in dealing damage over a warrior.

Because the skill caps cannot be raised for monks to re-establish damage potential from that side due to programming issues, we would like to propose the following changes for the monk class.

DAMAGE INCREASE

1) Some type of upgrade to damage output.
Rogues are incredibly powerful when they are behind a mob. Warriors are incredible tanks. Monk damage -- either with an "uber" staff or with epic fists -- should be halfway between a rogue's potential when the rogue is behind a mob using a Ragebringer and a warrior who is attacking with his/her best weapons. Keep in mind 10/16 and 13/20 weapons for warriors when rebalancing our damage. There are many ways to tune this damage, but PLEASE do not resort to another UBER-rare staff that will only balance the 1% of the monk population lucky enough to get one of these drops.

2) Raise fist damage post-50 for those without epics.
While the delay continues to drop, the damage never increases once a monk hits level 50. Very few monks use fists anymore, but this would make the RP'ers and casual players a tad more viable near the end-game, if they ever get there.

3a) Encourage usage and training of lesser monk special attacks.
Either allow us to use one type of strike and one type of kick at the same time by unhooking the special attack re-use timers post-50, or give the possibility of attack "strings". (This is the preferred solution, but would be harder to implement.) If a monk successfully uses Flying Kick on an enemy -- connects for damage -- then give a chance (based on Double Attack skill, preferably) for the monk to automatically use Dragon Punch/Tail Rake. If Dragon Punch/Tail Rake connects for damage, give a slightly lesser chance of doing an Eagle Strike. This is not only an interesting solution, it would make raising all of the monk special attack skills more important. This would be a post-50 enchancement, or when a monk reaches 200 skill in Flying Kick.

--- If this is technically not reasonable from a coding standpoint, then a secondary option would be:

3b) Minimum damage on Flying Kick.
It won't make a huge difference in our damage output, but it will at least make us FEEL better. There is nothing worse than doing a flying kick for 3 damage as a post-50 monk. Even making this minimum damage cap post-50 only would be an improvement, and would not unbalance the pre-50 game where monks did have an advantage.

Damage Increase Suggestions:

To recap, a level 50's fist is a 14/30 weapon, which reduces (in delay only) to 14/26 at level 60. One suggestion is to have the damage increase (as it does for the rest of the levels) post 50 as well, so that a Monks fist at 60 is 16/26.

One of the best suggestions that has come from the many Monks is to have the main hand damage bonus applied to the off hand.

This is becasue Monks are the masters of Duel-Wield. We get it as a level 1 skill, and gain 7 points per level, capping faster and higher than any other class. This indicates a natural affinity for the monk to use his off-hand, a kind of natural/trained ambidexterity.

Allowing us to strike just as hard with the off-hand as the primary will solve the damage-dealing problem and bring us up to the correct damage level of being between warriors and rogues.

If this change is made, you will need to remove MNK as a class capable of using the moss-covered twig, a weapon changed to be off-hand only because of the large amount of damage it does when a damage bonus is applied.

Other suggestions on ways to increase damage include having a minimum damage of level/2 for special attacks such as Flying Kick, or add some kind of level damage bonus to the special attacks, but the off hand damage bonus is the most favoured solution.

4) Improve Feign Death.
Leave it as it currently functions in all but two areas -- If the monk is not removed from the mob's haste list and does not get a "has fallen to the ground" message, give the monk an automatic "Evade"-type effect. In other words, allow a monk to sacrifice a couple of combat rounds to lower him- or herself on the hate list. This would DRAMATICALLY increase monk survival rates on uber-mobs, when we're causing intense aggro with epic fists or other high end weapons. It also provides a check to ensure that monk damage is not overly high, if Verant is still worried about that. Finally, since this is linked to a SKILL, it still has a chance to fail, just like a rogue's evade skill. Also, fix the "no failure message, but the mob just keeps beating on me after the eighteenth feign attempt in a row" problem that I and many other monks have encountered in the post-50 world, especially while grouped in Velious.

One possible suggestion was to have something like "%T has seen through your ruse" when a Mob attacks after a succesful feign, simalar to the fact that Rogues know when they have sneaked succesfully, and another for resisted Spells not breaking Feign.

5) Fix the Celestial Fist effect.
Celestial Tranquility is a great effect on our epic, but it stacks with almost NOTHING. Most monks rarely solo post-50, and most common group buffs will keep the effect from working. (Call of Earth, enchanter haste spells, most bard songs, Speed of the Shissar, etc.) At least alter it so that even if the haste is not usable due to other, better buffs then the additional effects -- the extra +AC and +ATK -- will stack anyway, similar to the way the Robe of the Whistling Fists worked. It's pretty bad when a well-meaning ranger tosses me Call of Earth and costs me 54ATK and 12AC. The ranger buff especially does not make sense because Call of Earth is NOT a Haste Buff, and the Celestial Tranquility Effect will indeed stack with the upgrade, Strength of Earth.

Also the Celestial Fists wearers, felt that a graphic for the hands would be better than the hand wraps.

6) Increase Warrior, Paladin, and ShadowKnight taunting skills.
Yes, I know this isn't an enhancement to MONKS, but it would DEFINITELY help us out. WE ARE NOT TANKS. Unfortunately, it's very hard to taunt off of us due to our extreme aggro, ESPECIALLY with our low-damage, low-delay epic fists. PLEASE, PLEASE, PLEASE implement this one change if you don't make any other!!

7) At least consider spreading the weight limit increase out over the post-50 levels.
Few monks will make 55. Even fewer will make 60. Therefore, weight increases at those two points are not terribly useful. A 2lb increase every other level (i.e., 52, 54, 56, etc) would result in a weight limit of 24 at 60. A 1lb increase every post-50 level would be another way to implement the changes, and would also result in a weight limit of 24 at level 60. The following are additional information gathered from other Monks posts, covering Skills, Disciplines, Armour and Quests.

SKILLS

Mend.
Perhaps Mend at a skill of 201+ can have a small chance of curing disease/poison. The increase chance to cure will rise as the skill goes above 201+.

Skill Caps.
Kick caps at 250. So why does Flying kick, eagle strike, and dragon punch all cap at 230?

Also, it was recently announced that they upped the caps for the Hand to Hand skill to equal 1hb/2hb, but all that was raised was the 60 cap, the 50 cap is still only 225 compared with 1hb/2hb cap of 240, so the 50 cap needs to be increased to 240 as well.

Higher Dodge, Riposte & Block.
This is a concern for high level Monks in regards to the Big Game Hunts.

With higher skills in these areas, this would give Monks a unique way to tank better - Plate classes absorb damage with their higher hps, Rogues use stealth (evade) to not get hit, and monks just flat out use speed (block/dodge/riposte) to make the mob miss them.

Either increase the 3 skills from there current caps of 225/230 to 252, or have higher percentage chances of working (if that is not taken into consideration with a higher skill)

DISCIPLINES

Thunderkick
This needs to be worked on, as currently it is being reported that 24 damage comes up the majority of the time. Possible suggestions include it becoming an innate or a timed discipline where every kick within the disciplines duration can be a thunderkick, {b} the minimum damage on TK to be 1 to 1.5 * level, (so a 60th level Monk will not kick for less than 60-90 damage with it), {c} add a short stun to Thunderkick, or {d} make TK a special attack after flying kick, gained at 50+th level or so.

These suggestions assume that the damage is not based from the FK damage table, and flying kick is not improved in any of the above afore mentioned ways.

We would like all the disciplines to receive an increase in length, and looking at the new hybrid disciplines, no one else has every discipline last for short periods of time.

ARMOUR

Monk armour needs some natural AC progression. It would be nice to see all cresent pieces (obtainable at around level 30) being 1 point more AC then cured silk (obtained at level 15) shiverback (obtained at 46+) being 1-2 AC greater than cresent, and Velious quest armour (obtained at level 50+) being 1-2 greater than shiverback. Each piece should have a higher AC than the same piece of the previous type.

Also, why does our equipment seem to have less (overall) in stats, resists, and clickable effects then other melee classes?

Make them have a slight effect (whether attack raised by 1 to 10, a small self only heal or even an effect like summoning bandages), or alter the stats enough to make the Monks of that level want to do the (35hr average long) quest, rather than the all to easy Gem Inlaid Guantlets, which provides a very simalar quest result in a fraction of the time.

I'll be discussing some balance issues as well as possible fixes that I and many others have noticed recently with the implimentation of many new aspects of high end level gameplay and many new npc changes.

Issue 1. High level tanking.

Monks tank with the ability to live through 1, and sometimes 2 rounds of melee combat, the same as rogues, and even pure casters
To me, this is unbalanced, as monks are supposed to be the second best tankers out there being the middle man of pure melee.

Directly in relation to tanking ability comes the factor of who will be primary target of these mobs.
It will always be monks in the beginning of fights due to their slightly better melee output and lack of a hate list reducer.
Feign is no longer an option as it used to be against lower level mobs, so should a monk do what they are designed to do, which is a litle more damage output, their going to die right away and no healer will be able to keep them alive....hence they have contributed nothing other than what damage they could muster before they died, which won't be much.

Monks need a way to counter their higher damage over warriors in order to be a viable class at end levels.
Feign used to be a reliable hate list reducer, which would essentially void their extra damage, and make them a weak warrior in every aspect who has feign. While feign is nice to have, in end game, it doesn't help us not be a worthless class in a fight.
Much like all the magic based classes who are upset over high resistance rates on uber mobs, monks usefulness drops drastically, with the exception that monks have no additional benefits to offer a group as casters do.

This is the main problem with monks. We need to be able to live and be of some use.

Absor, if you take one thing away from this thread, I think most would agree it should be:

More damage

Nearly as important (if not more for some, case dependent) would be aggro reduction, especially if we get our wish of additional damage.

This relates to the 51+ levels only, mainly nearer to 60. Monks were balanced at 50 when they maintained a skill cap advantage. The vanishing of the skill cap advantage, along with warriors/rogues gaining additional damage abilities at 55 and 60 as well as weapons very nearly as good as monk weapons (if not better in some cases), led to monks falling behind in the damage curve to where they are now.

Even with the small advantage that they have, monks have a hard time dealing with unwanted taunt. There are 4 classes that get both dual wield and double attack. These can be thought of as the damage dealing melee classes. These classes are rogue, ranger, warrior, and monk. Warriors WANT aggro, as they are also the best tanks. The other 3 classes do not want aggro when a defensive class is present (mainly warriors). Rangers and rogues get jolt/evade to offload aggro. Where does that leave monks?

We are in nearly the same sitation as rogues were pre-evade. The only way for monks to get lower on the hate list is to stop attacking or try to feign death off the hate list. Reducing attacks to reduce aggro defeats the purpose of having a monk in the group. We would then be doing warrior damage or lower.

Feign death might be an option if it actually worked well as an aggro reducer, especially on high-end mobs. For example, it is said that feign is just about useless on the velious uber-mobs for aggro reduction. The result is that monks get unwanted aggro and either die within seconds or soak up healing mana that would be better spent on a warrior.

Feign death seems to not work very often as an aggro reducer, especially as the mobs increase in level. It works fine when I'm by myself, but in a group situation I can feign death multiple times and the mob keeps attacking me. Each time I feign it costs damage, as auto attack must be turned off first (and if you don't wait for a time before feigning, it will never work...why must there be a delay?). Even if it DOES work, there's only about a 30% chance it will reduce aggro at all.

Given these factors and a 10-second recycle time, feign is not a reliable aggro reducer. Monks sorely need a way to reduce aggro, especially if we receive a damage increase. Otherwise it would always be better to add a warrior over us in a group for melee purposes.

One note about taunt. Weapon skill seems to play no part in mob aggro. This was great when monks had a weapon skill advantage, but now they do not. The result is that not only do monks not do as much relative damage, but they actually have MORE relative taunt! I'm not sure this was ever considered.

My evidence? My most striking story is this. 2 monks, both level 56 with tranquil staves. The only differences were weapon skill and haste. One monk was at 252 skill (2-handed blunt), the other at about 85. The monk with 85 skill (me) had 20% more haste than the other monk. The monk with 252 skill had to do at least 3-4 times the damage I was doing, but I had the mob's attention 100% of the time. This is when I figured that actual damage done (ie. weapon skill) had no bearing on taunt. It seems the only factors in generating melee taunt were the number of attacks and the weapon ratings.

Even if an aggro-reducer wasn't implemented for monks beyond what we have now, I would hope any kind of damage increase was done in a way that didn't increase aggro (like increasing the minimum damage on flying kick, for instance...for starters ).

If we are to continue to accumulate aggro with no way of reducing it, then we would like a way to avoid the resulting attacks. Additional AC/HP is not what many monks are after, nor in the spirit of the class. Additional damage avoidance abilities would help us and make sense as well. The caps on block/dodge/riposte could be increased (they're not 252 yet), or perhaps some other method.

There are other points I could mention later, but I wanted to state my main balance concerns for now. Monks should fall closer to the middle of the damage difference between warriors and rogues, not barely more than warriors (unless we were very good tanks, which we're not). We also need a way to offload aggro without sacrficing any damage advantage we have, or it would just always be better to replace us with a properly equipped warrior for melee.
