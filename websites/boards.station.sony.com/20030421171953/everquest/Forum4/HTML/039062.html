You say your problem is not being able to have the warrior take agro? As an SK you must know that you are one of the most agro creating classes in the game. Warriors can only generate hate through there weapons and their taunt skill. Both of which you have. If you want the warrior to establish himself as the MT allow him to work up a high lvl of agro before you unleash the agro spells you love so much. Otherwise it will be like trying to freeze butter in the desert.

Taunt works by adding "points" to you under this mob's imaginary hate list. How I like to look at it is like a set number of hate points per mob. For example, say Emperor Crush has 100 hate points to give out. Let's say you are the only person who has touched the mob, well then you have all the points. Get it? So let's not say that you and a cleric are fighting a mob. Though the fight the cleric has healed you a bit when you needed it but no too much. Therefore you would have 75 points and the cleric would have 25.

This brings us to your situation. Let's say you pulled the mob (100 points to Erebus). The wiz nukes more dmg than you are doing (85 points to the wizzy and 10 for you and 5 for the war also doing dmg). The mob will agro whoever is on top of its list. This being the wizzy. The wizzy stops nuking and the warrior taunts and gains the majority of the points. Then you cast super agro spells and wa la! You have the agro again.

I hope it wasn't too confusing. Just trying to help.

quote:
if the Wizard nuked 3 times for 3300 damage total, and I taunted once (3301) that I would pull aggro immediatly since I'm top the highest number now?

That is correct. That wizzie had better not be still casting/stabbing, though... with such a small headstart with aggro back on you, the wizzie is going to be in trouble again, if she so much as pokes the mob with a pointy stick.

Some things to keep in mind:

1) If that wizzie with aggro has taken a severe beating, and is very low on hitpoints... Taunt will not save her. Low-HP aggro will keep that mob stuck to her until she is dead, or healed adequately.

2) Taunt has a "check" each time it is pressed. It either works... or it does not work.

Which leads to...

quote:
if the mob is beating on you, theres no reason to taunt

If you are Main Tank... this is correct. Mindlessly mashing that Taunt button when you already have aggro only means that the button will be greyed out, when someone inadvertantly pulls the mob from you... and you need to get it back fast.

(Obviously, in order to get skillups with it... mindlessly mash it when you can... not when it counts )

3) All bets are off, in Dreadlands. It sounds like you are after the really fast xp. If you don't want to slow down (and you still outdamage the Warrior even without taunting/spells), you are MT anyway, like it or not.

To preface, I am a 56 SK.

Firstly, Erebus, allow me to point you to www.shadowknight.org. You will find much useful information there, from your fellows who know your class extremely well.

Secondly, Taunt is a check, as someone had said. It doesn't matter if a Wiz Ice Cometted three times or a Monk hit it for 1 damage, all that matters is whether the Taunt is successful.

Thirdly, both Monks and Rogues have ways of dropping aggro, Evade and FD. They have to take some responsibility for their own actions as well.

Fourth, it depends in terms of who should have aggro. When I am in a PoP group and there is a warrior who is approximately the same level/ac/hp, it makes sense for me to just tank. No ping pongs, no dead dress wearers, mob sticks to me.

However, let us say there is a 60 warrior with 200 ac and 1000 more hps than me. He is clearly the better tank, while I have the better aggro abilities. So I "aggro boost" him. I cast 2 high aggro spells as the mob is incoming. Then I leave attack off for a short bit, take a couple hits, as the warrior taunts off me. Now the Warrior has SK levels of aggro. Mob doesn't come off on the slower or healer or anybody, and we use the better tank (due to hp/ac/level). Everybody is happy.

But that is in PoP, where the mobs double for 600+ on TANKS. In the Dreadlands, it really doesn't pay for you to bother with this. Just MT if the warrior can't hold aggro.
