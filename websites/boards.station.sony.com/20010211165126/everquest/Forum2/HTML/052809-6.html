In general, I'm happy with the warrior class right now. One of my high level characters is a level 60 warrior, and I have seen a couple of things I would like to comment on.

1. The level 60 warrior discipline (aggressive) is bugged. It is supposed to increase your damage output by about 30-35% and also increase the damage mobs do to you by about 15%, and last for 3 minutes. Based on quite a bit of my own testing, it does not add ANYTHING to the warrior's damage output, but mobs do hit the warrior a lot harder (It appears to me that the damage the warrior takes may be increased by a lot more than you guys intended). Basically, we get the bad part but not the good.

2. Drop rates on cobalt bracer and (ESPECIALLY) cobalt helm are too low. Cobalt bracer drop rates are just a little to low, IMHO, but cobalt helms almost do not drop at all. With RoK having been out for almost a year now, I still have only heard of ONE cobalt helm dropping on my server. There may be 1 or 2 more that I don't know about, but that's still too low. I know quite a few level 60 warriors (some of them in uberguilds!) that are still wearing a skull-shaped barbute, because anything better than that is extremely hard to get. No level 60 should be using a SSB unless he recently lost his corpse.

3. Taunt does not work as well as it should post 50. A warrior's entire usefulness to the group depends on his ability to take the hits. Post 50, ALL of the classes that get taunt have this problem, but at least hybrids can taunt with spells in an emergency. Warriors can do nothing but click the taunt button and hope. This means that the warrior's ability to taunt in a real emergency is the worst of the tank classes. Monks and casters get ripped to pieces in many cases because we simply cannot taunt.

4. Warriors got absolutely nothing new post 50 in Velious, while all casters and hybrids got new spells and/or abilities. If you felt that warriors were too powerful and needed to be "stealth nerfed" I can understand (although I don't agree that warriors were too powerful). However, it would have been nice to at least get a FUN new ability post 50, even if that new ability is useless in combat. One of the rough things about playing a warrior is that gaining levels usually means nothing but a few more hp, some skill increases, and a discipline that often is useless. It would be nice to have one new ability post 50 (even something that is useless in combat) that would be just for fun. Maybe a warrior-only animation that would look ferocious and scary?

5. Although I am currently happy with the warrior class, I am a bit concerned about the recent incredible series of hybrid enhancements. Before I say anything more, let me say that I play a paladin at high levels also, so this is not just a warrior speaking that is trying to make his class powerful. Many hybrids right now are asking to have their hp, ac, and melee skills increased to be nearly the equal of warriors. The warrior class is very good, but they are a one-trick pony. People invite warriors to groups right now because they are the best tank in the game. Hybrids are more of a jack-of-all-trades type. My concern is that if paladin and shadowknights get any enhancements to their tanking ability the usefulness of the warrior may quickly evaporate. After all, who would want a warrior in their group when a paladin could tank almost as well, and also offer the following: 90% experience res, root parking (very nice for crowd control when no enchanter is present), superior heal, stun, buffs, Lay on Hands (vastly underrated ability -- even clerics do not get a nearly instantaneous heal that heals far more damage than superior heal does. Paladins may be the best class in the game for saving a player who is on the verge of death). Again, don't get me wrong on this one. I'm happy with the balance between warriors and hybrids right now. I just don't want to see changes in the future that might weaken the warrior class more than you might realize.

I apologize for the long post, but I've been playing a warrior almost every day since release, so I can't help but have a few ideas and thoughts here.

This is off a post from the Everlore Warrior page. I found that it contained a number of good suggestions and thought that it should be posted here. It was originally written by Ugglie Bogmonster from Quellious and posted on Everlore at http://www.everlore.com/guides.asp?ID=323258

--------------

I’m suggesting that warriors get a makeover and get a few skills on each of the timers (the kick/slam timer, the taunt timer, and the disarm timer) that they have now. I’m not asking for a bunch of extras, or unbalancing uber-skills, or anything like that. I’m suggesting that Verant substitutes out a few of the automatic skills (critical hits, crippling blows, and bezerker frenzy in its current form), and substitutes in some equally useful skills, but put them on the timers. I’m also suggesting that they put our class defining ability, the bezerker frenzy, on a button, instead of having it go off automatically.

This way warriors are constantly forced to make small tactical decisions, which are fun, interesting, and helpful to the group. If he gets badly jumped while pulling, he hits his guard button, giving him a defensive edge while he flees to his group. If they are fighting a named uber-mob, he may use his feint skill to lower its AC, allowing it to be hit a bit easier by his group mates. If they pull a handful of mobs, he may use instill doubt to frighten one off (this is a risk!), then taunt another off the wizard (as soon as it recycles), then hit guard again to survive getting whacked by four other baddies. If half a dozen low blues get pulled, he may push the bezerker frenzy button, giving his group an edge, taking out one of the mobs early in the fight. That one will take a while to recycle, but it was a worth while decision.

So after the overhaul, warriors would have kick, slam and guard on one timer, taunt and instill doubt on another timer, and disarm, feint, and charge on the third timer. And bezerker frenzy is now a button (and a tactical choice).

In addition, my suggestions have ease of implementation in mind, so that it would not take a team of programmers two weeks to change this stuff, with extensive play testing

Which way is more fun? More group friendly? More hard decisions?

In making these suggestions, I have a concern about fun. I think the warrior class should be a more fun class to play and, well, a LOT of the skills you get as a warrior go off automatically. Riposte is a great skill, as is dual wield, and double attack, and a bunch of others. Crits and crippling blows do some nice extra damage, too. But I’m not getting to decide when to use any of these, really, and I’d just like to make a few of those ‘hard decisions’ that Gordon Wrinn has spoken about along the way. In addition, most players who choose the warrior class enjoy strategy and tactics, and allowing them to make some tactical choices during combat would be VERY enjoyable.

In addition to all of that, warriors go loooooooong periods of time between getting new skills. You get double attack at level 15, then riposte at 25. Ten levels later! Ack! Well, at least you only have ten more levels before you get the ultimate in do nothing skills, disarm. Good thing its only seventeen more levels before you get your first discipline. Now patience is a virtue, but this is a bit much. Putting the skills I’ve suggested for the timers into the game would lower this extreme spacing between skills significantly.

Speaking of disarm, is anyone of the opinion that it accomplishes anything positive? It appears to me that if I have a Hill Giant hitting me for 80 pts a shot while wielding a FS scimitar, and I disarm him, he is still hitting me for 80 pts a shot and at the same exact speed. I know warriors who think disarm is so useless, they don’t even have the button on their hot key bank. If disarm caused some beneficial effect (making the mob run for a bit, or lowering its AC, or stunning it because of surprise, or something…) we’d all love it more. So disarm could be improved a little. Am I wrong about this?

Bezerker Frenzy, much like Harm Touch or Lay on Hands, should be a powerful tool to use in a save-your-skin type situation. I think it should be on a timer, much like HT or LOH, and recycle appropriately. If it were on a button, I would love the ability to decide when it’s used, not have it just go off automatically when I’m down to 40% of my hit points. I would love to see it become more powerful as warriors gained in level, either in its raw power, or in the reduction of its recycle time so it could be used more often. This change probably reduces the power of bezerker frenzy (as it could not be used as often), but would be worthwhile. This change is useful, it’s fun, and when to use it is one of those tactical ‘hard decisions.’

I think that Verant really hit upon a great idea when it improved the bind wound skill for warriors. This post-50 skill increases the skill cap to 200, and the maximum bind wound percentage to 70% of your hit points. Simply brilliant, and perfect for a class that lives on the battlefield and would certainly have the best skills as field surgeons, medics, first aid providers, and battlefield wound dressers. The problem is that it’s not that useful in the post-50 world, but it would be at lower levels. I just think that this should be implemented much earlier, perhaps as early as level 1, because it is group friendly (reduces the group down time between fights), gives warriors something to work on during those incredibly long gaps when they are not getting any new skills, and is unique to the class.

I would also like to suggest that a “Guard” skill be implemented. Warrior’s who use this skill take a somewhat defensive fighting posture for a few moments. This skill is on the same timer as kick/slam/bash, and raises the warriors AC by 20. Thus a warrior has to make one of those ‘hard decisions’, and it’s a fun tactical decision. Do I go for offense, and kick? Spell interruption, and slam? Or defense, and guard?

I think that instill doubt should be put on the taunt timer. Should the warrior taunt the mob into attacking him, or (in some tactical situations) attempt to frighten it away? Again, a fun, hard, tactical decision.

Putting instill doubt on the taunt timer is, to me, the most obvious skill that a warrior could possess, and warriors should be the unquestioned masters of it. Now I know that practically no one thinks ID is a great skill, and using it has its dangers, but from a role-play perspective, is there anything in the game more frightening that an ogre warrior in full crafted with a gigantic Ex Axe?

I believe that feint should be introduced as a warrior skill, and it should be put on the disarm timer. In this skill, the mid-level warrior feints his opponent out of position, temporarily lowering the opponent’s AC. Again, if put on a timer, it forces one of those ‘hard decisions.’

I would also like to suggest a charge skill, which would raise the warrior’s attack skill for a few moments. As I’ve suggested giving up crits and crippling blows, this offensive skill should not be a problem.
