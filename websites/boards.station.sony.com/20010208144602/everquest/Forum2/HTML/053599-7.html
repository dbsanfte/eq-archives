a_burly_gnoll

the 75% offense/defense was not applicable - it was just an example as to how percentages work *IF* they were the same thing.

Defense is made up of MANY variables. You say offense and defense are apples and oranges. You are correct.

But what I listed in my first post were all elements that contribute to Defense ability *as a whole*. Hit Points, Defense skill, AC on armor, Dodge, Parry, Riposte all contribute to defense and are all related to one another - they are ALL APPLES - or parts of apples. Of course my calculations had some assumptions (which I DID say) - for instance - we know that Riposte does not contribute to defence as much as AC on armor, but my first calculation ASSUMED it contributed just as much.

Think about this: if I have 75% of the ac of a warrior (the warrior has 133% of my AC), and 75% of their hit points (ASSUMING hit points and AC contribute equally to defense), I do NOT tank 75% as well. For me to tank 75% as well as a warrior I would have to have 75% of their hit points and 100% of their AC, or vice versa. They are multiplicative.

Lets assume 4 melee hits attempt to hit a warrior and a paladin.
Lets be simple and say each 'check' has a 50% chance of working for the warrior.
Warrior:
hit 1: passes AC check, misses warrior entirely (Miss)
hit 2: AC check fails, then dodge check fails, passes parry check, misses warrior entirely(Parried)
hit 3: AC check fails, dodge fails, parry fails, riposte passes, misses warrior and he hits in return(Ripostes)
hit 4: all checks fail, warrior is hit (lets ignore how much he is hit for based on AC for this)
50% chance of AC check passing
IF that fails, 50% chance of dodge
IF that fails, 50% chance to parry
IF that fails, 50% chance to riposte
Now, 50% chance for each is silly, but if these numbers were true - what is the chance the warrior would get hit?
ANS: the warrior would get hit 6.25% of the time. Figure it out.
Now, I dont' know exactly how the combat system works in EQ - but if things are checked sequentially like that- that is what it would look like. (the percent chance of dodge, parry, riposte - which are really quite low passing percentages - are probably set independently and should be added together in that case - I dont' know though)

OK Paladin has 75% of the warrior skills - 75% of 50 is 37.5% chance of the check for each passing:

Same as above as warrior, but each skill has only a 37.5% chance of working, instead of 50%.
What is the chance the paladin will get hit?
100-(100* 0.375) = 62.5% chance the paladin would fail the AC check
62.5 - (62.5*0.375) = 62.5 - 23.4 = 39.1% chance the paladin would fail both AC and dodge check.
39.1 - (39.1*0.375) = 39.1 - 14.7 = 24.4% chance the paladin would fail AC, dodge, parry.
24.4 - (24.4*0.375) = 24.4 - 9.15 = 15.25% chance would fail all 4 checks.
Therefore the paladin would get hit 15.25% of the time.
But, the warrior would get hit only 6.25% of the time.
Strange isn't it? The paladin gets HIT more than TWICE the amount the warrior does. The warrior takes LESS THAN HALF the damage the paladin does.

Obviously - if these numbers were true, the paladin under 50% as good at tanking as the warrior.

Capiche?

I had this idea last night while thinking over the thread. I think it could make the warrior class more entertaining to play and more capable of meeting their potential, without unbalancing the game.
There would be a new warrior only trade skill called Armoury, or something along those lines. It would be a lot like smithing, but using strength as it's primary attribute (it makes a lot more sense for smithing to require brawn over brains anyway). It would allow warriors to make armor and equipment for themselves out of items that would drop randomly, much as research components drop for intelligence casters. Purely as an example, the cobalt bracer might involve combining a brick of cobalt ore (an uncommon drop in Kunark zones), a 10 charge shrink potion (assuming shamans can make such a thing... don't know alchemy all too well), a vial of viscious(sp) mana from an enchanter (or similar item), a bracer mold, and perhaps some other items. They would be combined in special forges kept in warrior guilds across Norrath. Items meant to be rarer, along the lines of a cobalt breastplate, might require several cobalt blocks, gems, and a not terribly rare drop off of a major mob like Trakanon. Weapons could be created in a similar way.
There would be several purposes behind the addition of this skill. First of all, to give warriors a skill they can use outside of combat. Warriors currently have nothing they can do outside of combat on their own that is not time prohibitive. Secondly, warriors would be able to create some of the items, both weapons and armor, that define their class, much as intelligence casters must research many of their spells. Third, warriors would be more capable of actually attaining the equipment they need to be effective in their class's role, instead of it being restricted to warriors who belong to uber guilds or who have other characters that are casters and can afford to buy or camp it.
Other classes would benefit from having more effective warriors in their group, and several classes could benefit by being able to sell warriors trade skill items needed to make their items. They only people that wouldn't benefit would be those people that currently make an outrageous amount of money camping warrior items they don't need.
