I'm going to go out on a limb here and go through what I think are some fundamental game mechanics involving healers in EQ.

Healers get hated by lots of critters in EQ, very quickly. When the chips are down, the critters will go for someone. When the healer helps out, the critter typically switches to the healer.

When the healer gets mobbed, what happens next?

Cleric: Divine Aura to buy time while critters get taunted off or the cleric runs for it.

Druid: rather than going crazy with heals, simply evacuate the entire party.

Shaman: (pre-nerf) Rage and wade in with the tanks.

This spell seems obviously intended as a last-ditch defense spell. The duration on it exceeds any possible mana total found in EQ, and by quite a margin. # 2. DRAIN ALL REMAINING MANA. Either this or #1 is required for balance I think. Both would be fine.

# 3. VERY SMALL MANA DRAIN. It must be small to allow someone who is low mana on mana to cast this spell and keep it active for a reasonable period of time. I mean like 1-2 points per tick for the level 19 (rank 5?) spell. Tricky to get this right.

# 4. LONG RECAST DELAY. Simulates medding to full before using again. Because as shaman increase in levels, we get slightly better at regenerating mana, and the chance that some one nice has cast Clarity, Breeze, etc. on us increases, the recast delay should be less as the spell rank increases. For example: Frenzy (Rank 5) recast delay 2 1/2 min. Fury (Rank 8) rd = 2 min. Rage (Rank 11) rd = 1 3/4 min. Voice of Berzerker (Rank 21-level59) rd = 1 1/2 min.

The spell needs to actually run AT LEAST 2 - 3 min, and closer to its original duration would be much better.
