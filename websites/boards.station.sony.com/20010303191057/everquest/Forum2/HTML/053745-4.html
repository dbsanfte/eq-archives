My comments will strictly address one facet of Bardic life, melee effectiveness. In the spirit of taking a look at what is inherently "wrong" with the Bard class I think most everything stems back to a decision made in Beta 2, if I remember correctly, that Bards would not get Double Attack, but rather Dual Wield exclusively.

Let's examine this. Dual Wield (DW) grants a person the ability to attack with two weapons. Double Attack (DA) is a chance to attack twice with any given swing of a weapon. EQ's combat was designed around the idea that the right hand weapon (primary) would do the majority of the damage for ALL classes in the game, with the stipulated exception of two-handed weapons. Most classes would only wield one weapon and the pure melee (Warrior, Rogue, Monk) could attack with 2 weapons. The Ranger hybrid would be allowed to also utilize 2 weapons at a time and have DA, but would sacrifice armor to gain this ability.

In early Beta Verant was concerned with class balance and felt that Bards were already the superior hybrid as far as casting ability. Therefore they tried to limit the offensive melee efficiency of Bards by not giving them DA as every other melee and hybrid class gets, and by excluding them from using 2 handed weapons. This was a poor decision for the following reasons.

1) It too severely impacted a Bards offense. Which has been exposed in post 50 Norrath.
2) It had a secondary effect of lowering the effective AC of a Bard to little more than that of a chain wearing class as Bards would seldom utilize a shield, as then they would lose a significant portion of their offensive effectiveness.

So what should Verant have done? Well Verant could have given Bards Double Attack but capped it at a level that accomplished the same offensive result they were looking for. This would allow Bards to utilize a shield like all of the other “defensive” hybrids. They could have excluded Bards from having the ability to Bash with the shield, and also maintained the prohibition against two-handed weapons.

How could Verant now correct this issue and what ramifications would it have? This would assume that Verant does consider Bards as having too low of a defensive rating compared to other hybrids and pure melee. This is what I would do:

1) Determine a Double Attack cap for pre and post 50 Bards that places them in the correct position on the melee offensiveness table that holds to the “Vision.”
2) Phase in Double Attack and eliminate Dual Wield for Bards by taking the Bards current skill in DW and transferring it to their new DW skill (not to exceed the hard cap), or if that is not feasible then give Bards the maximum DA skill for their respective level.
3) All weapons currently equipped in the off-hand when this change is phased in either drop to inventory or are automatically placed on the mouse cursor for the player if there is no room in inventory for the item.

This would allow Bards to increase their AC by utilizing a shield and Verant would not need to fudge around with Defense caps and other skills. This would also allow Bard to maintain their current level of offensive output.

I play a L 40 bard on Karana, and overall I'm pretty happy with the class, although I can't comment on the high level game.

I think the role of a bard is to enhance a group without detracting from it. Bards may not have the most powerful abilities, but we are never OOM, and are always adding to the group, not sitting around medding. This is a strength: We have staying power, and a weakness: our abilities tend to be weaker over the short term than 'pure' classes. I'd like to see any changes to the class reflect this. At higher levels, bard songs seem to weaken compared to casters while melee skills weaken compared to melee and hybrid classes. I think bards could use some minor tweaks in both areas to make us a bit more useful.

I have a few suggestions that I think would make bards more useful:

1. Better defensive skills. I see bards as depending on finesse rather than brute force in combat. It makes sense to me that we would be able to fend off our opponents blows and strike back when they are off guard. We should gain parry around L 40 with a cap of 150+ and riposte at 50 with a similar cap. This would not make us into tanks or infringe on anyone's role in combat, but would give us a bit more staying power.

2. Better Crowd control.

a) Better charm is probably out of the question since it is so powerful at high levels. Longer duration would infringe on the enchanter's role, but I think a fixed duration (3 ticks) for the L39 charm would not be overpowering. Charm does not stack, so you would still lose control for at least a short time.

b) Better mez. As it is, a bard can only keep 1 or 2 mobs (because of resists) out of combat while effectively removing himself from combat. I think that a bard who dedicates himself to CC should be almost as useful as an enchanter since the enchanter has the option of charming, buffing, stunning, etc while selectively mezzing mobs. Pixie strike can't have it's resist rate decreased because it also has the effect of a manaless stun which interrupts casting. This is the reason the AE mez has to become weaker at higher levels, it cripples caster mobs. I would like to see an increase to the duration of our 2 mez songs to allow us to throw in the occasional haste, or to affect more mobs if we spend all of our time mezzing. A 1 tick increase at levels 40, 50, and 60 would not turn us into CC gods like enchanters, but would make us more useful in these situations. Another idea would be to add 1 or 2 ticks if you are playing a wind instrument. This would make bards more effective at CC when dedicating themselves to this role, but would also detract from our other roles in the combat. Longer duration would have no impact on on interrupting casting. This is the only song line where I would favour greater duration.

3) Increased skill caps. Safe fall should cap somewhere around 75. Sneak, hide and forage at 100. SF of 75 is still lower than rogue & monk caps (I think rogue SF should be capped higher as well, but that's another thread). Sneak and hide are of dubious use for non-rogues since they can't be used together. Bard hide is below some racial skill levels. A forage of 55 is just above some racial limits, and is so unreliable most bards never use it. I spent 6 hours foraging for an ancient coin in EJ (a bard quest!) and ended up having to get a druid to forage one for me. I could not even reliably maintain my food level during that period. I understand that we are jacks of all trades, masters of none, but having multiple useless skills does not benefit us in any way.

4) More diverse instruments. Possibly player crafted. I can see drums that double as a shield, a lute that can be used as a 1hb (ka-bong! :-) ). There are instruments like these in the game, but they are invariably god drops. Mid level weapons with an instrument effect could have a lesser effect than an standard instrument. eg: if a regular instrument doubles a song effect, a specialized weapon could add 30 or 50%. This would make melee possible while adding a slight bonus to our songs.

5) Better stacking. Bard songs will stack with many effects if they are applied after the effect, but not before. EG: Hymn will stack over regen, but not vice versa. This means that bards limit which spells can be cast at any time, especially in combat. If 2 effects are not too powerful in combination, I don't see why the order of application should matter.

Things I would NOT like to see:

1) Massive increases in melee damage. Bards have good damage output as it is. I don't see any need for melee in line with a ranger or warrior.

2) Any additional automation. Bards require a certain amount of skill. A cleric who cannot react fast enough to heal someone in combat before they die should not be playing a cleric. Someone who has trouble with swapping instruments, and twisting songs should not play a bard. Hotkeys already make this easier on the hands. This aspect is one of the defining qualities of a good bard, please don't remove it.

A MINIMUM requirement for "balance" is this --
If there is a spell effect type that both bards and some mana caster class(es) get, and the mana classes can maintain or repeat the effect indefinitely using only a small portion of their mana recovery capability, then the corresponding bard song (using at least a full third of the bard's capacity) should be AT LEAST as effective.

Examples that satisfy this condition include resistance buffs, regeneration, charming, DOTs, mana regeneration, stamina regeneration, and targeted spell interruption. These are balanced.

Examples that fail to satisfy this condition include damage shields, haste and slow songs, targeted and AE mesmerization. (Haste would be fine if it stacked with other haste; slow and targeted/AE mez would be fine if they didn't overwrite longer-term superior spells.)

Examples that fall ludicrously short of satisfying this condition include Chains, stat buffs, AC buffs, cure poison/disease, and (at higher levels) direct damage.

Again, a point that must be repeated because it doesn't seem to have registered yet is that bard songs are not automatically "free" compared to mana-using spells. There is a whole continuous range of mana efficiency for spells. At one end, you have spells that give long-lasting, powerful effects for very little mana (e.g. snare, Augmentation, most caster stat/ac/hp buffs). At the other end you have spells that give very short-lived effects at very high mana cost (e.g. Complete Heal, Ice Comet, Wondrous Rapidity). Bard songs fall IN THE MIDDLE of this range, requiring very considerable resource investment (equivalent to 1/3 of a mana caster's long-term mana regeneration rate) for an effect that lasts (minus interruptions) for an entire encounter, or an entire downtime between encounters. Their power should be tuned accordingly.

Absor has mentioned in previous postings that he doesn't like the blurring of lines between a classes traditional roles, i.e. the Warrior as ultra-combat jockey, the Rogue as the sneaky thief, etc. In that vein, he has mentioned (realize I'm not quoting here, just paraphrasing) that he doesn't like us having ANY rogue skills.

I would accept a nerf that takes away my ability to hide, sneak, pick pockets, find/disarm traps, even pick locks, so long as that reduction in abilities were compensated by the addition of skills with realistic level caps that are more appropriate to our class. I have read several that I would definitely agree with, with good arguments for them above, and here are some of them:

1. Feign Death: Whether its the Monk equivalent or one crafted solely for bards, its something that does make sense for our class. I'm not talking of the monklike, "I possess such perfect disapline I can stop my own heart" kind of Feign death, I am thinking of the more dramatic acting type death as described by one poster above. I still think it would be more useful than any orgue skill we currently possess.

2. Parry and riposte: Many have made game and technical arguments for their addition at earlier levels and with greater caps than currently exist. Please refer to them for those sorts of arguments, but the conclusion that seems to be drawn is that allowing bards of lower levels to be able to avoid taking damage would not be overpowering. I would agree that bash, double attack, and other damage dealing skills are not our province. But one additional argument I would add for parry and riposte is the fact that, as actors and entertainers, we would learn stage fighting from an early age. Getting parry at 25-30 and riposte at 40-41 would not be completely out of line, therefore, to balance the rogue skills we lose and/or overpower the other melee classes (I have no idea when others get their skills, I'm not really sure when we get ours except that its late in life. DO NOT take the suggested levels as a set-in-cement kind of proposal and start flaming, please).

If your vision of bards is this itinerant wandering storyteller/minstrel, we are better scroungers than that. Raising this cap to 75, 85, 100 would not be overpowering and is a skill we actively use a great deal (indeed, we MUST use it as part of our quest for Nostrolo's Tamborine, a fact that I actually congratulate Verant on!).

4. Increased song effect time: Absor has already mentioned that this may be addressed. I hope so, as it is one of the greatest problems that I can see with the bard class. Even adding a few seconds to effect time would greatly enhance out ability to be useful.

5. Specialization: I see casters running around specializing in one aspect of magic (abjuration, etc), why not bards? The obvious lines of demarcation are the various instruments. Why can't bards decide to specialize in an instrument and then have a greater effect with that instrument's songs?
