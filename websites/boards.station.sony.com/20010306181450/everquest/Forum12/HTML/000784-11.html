There are a lot of people (myself included) that think warriors and other melee plate-wearers need to be able to taunt more. And yes, this DOES belong in the wizard thread, because it's an idea on how wizard's could cast more with less taunt AND help the party.

Two new spell lines --

1) Rebound line of spells: A line of DD spells (low-mana cost, medium damage, starts around 29) that you target ON A PARTY MEMBER ONLY. Cast the spell. The spell "Bounces" off the target, and hits the mob that the party member is attacking. (Similar to the "/assist" code -- if party member is not attacking anything, no effect). The damage of the spell is added to the PARTY MEMBER'S hatelist, NOT the wizards.

If this spell is deemed "too powerful" then you could always add a second resistance check -- the normal "fizzle" check already in game and a second check relating to the party member being cast on.

Pros: Increases tank's taunting ability. Improves wizard's ability to chain-nuke without aggro. Assists clerics and enchanters with maintaining focus on the primary tank.
Cons: Might be a ***** to code. Might be considered a form of reverse crowd-control -- an enchanter stops all the mobs, the wizard makes sure they're all hitting the right target.

2) Radiation spells: Not a buff, but a PARTY MEMBER targetted AoE. Works on the same code as a wizard's current PBAoE (AoE spells that radiate from the caster, like Supernova -- fixed damage, unlimited number of targets, short range, higher mana cost). However -- the damage done by the spell is added to the TARGETTED PARTY MEMBER'S aggro, NOT to the wizards aggro.

Pros: Warriors finally get an AoE taunt, with the assistance of a wizard. All the "adds" of a pull end up on the party tank, NOT the enchanter. Wizard can do more damage (a LOT more, in an AoE party) without generating significant personal aggro. <If this is thought too powerful, possibly a check can be put in against mob intelligence, where they have a chance to figure out where the damage is really coming from>
Cons: This could also be considered a form of reverse crowd control, but I don't think it really is...

On fizzles or failures, the mob could figure out the damage's true source -- after all, there shouldn't be a TOTALLY safe line of spells in the game. Neither spell should be castable on pets, either -- PC party members ONLY.

Think about how great this would be in the post-50 world when warriors are having trouble taunting something: Wizard adds 1,000+ damage to the warrior's damage list in a single spell. Bingo! The mob is definitely paying attention to the warrior now...
