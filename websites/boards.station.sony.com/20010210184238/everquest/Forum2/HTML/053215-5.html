Rangers - offensive
Bards - all around utility (and songs)
Paladin/SK - defensive

Now, with this is mind, why is it that, although Paladins and Shadow Knights are supposed to be THE defensive hybrids, BOTH those classes are the ONLY hybrids that do not have a defensive discipline!

Rangers, at level 60, get Weaponshield. This allows them to parry ALL attacks directed at them for 15 seconds, making them invulnerable to melee attacks in that time.

Bards, at level 55, get Deftdance. This gives them the ability to automatically dodge EVERY attack AND automatically duel wield (off hand chance to swing will be 100%) for 10 seconds, making them invulnerable to melee attacks in that time and doing increased damage.

Now Paladins DO have a defensive skill at 60. Sanctification, making them immune to MOST (and I need a pally to tell me what most includes) types of harmful spells. If this makes them immune to "uber" mob AoE spells, this is an excellent ability. This, however, SILL precludes them 'taunting' a mob off a warrior to share damage, or off a spell caster to keep them alive because, even WITH a higher defensive skill, Paladins just aren't going to last against mobs that may very well be over 60.

Shadow Knights, on the other hand, do NOT have a defensive ability per se. Although it can be argued that Leechcurse, the level 60 discipline that heals self for every point of damage done, CAN be argued to be defensive, it is by no means an effective defensive discipline.
