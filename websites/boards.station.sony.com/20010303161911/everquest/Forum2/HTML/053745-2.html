I really hate to sound like a broken record in bringing up something that's already been discussed, but I don't think anyone has adequately explained our concerns about Chains (the bardic version of snare).

Absor wrote, "Well, it may have a much shorter duration than a druid snare, but you can also re-snare it, what, every 6 seconds? But I guess I can see that there may be too many restrictions on it. But keep in mind that this is not really intended to be a song that you can lock down something forever with. There has to be a pretty serious limitation on songs ('free' is always a dangerous thing when you are talking balance)."

Druid/ranger Snare, necromancer/shadowknight Darkness, and wizard Bonds of Force effectively slow a monster's movement rate for a set period of time. Druid/rangers can "lock down" a mob for 10 minutes (fixed duration) at a very minimal mana cost. Necromancer/shadowknights can "lock down" a mob for a fixed duration (length of time varies according to which level darkness they use, as does mana cost). Wizards can "lock down" a mob for a set period of time (length of time increases as they level) at a very minimal mana cost. Bards, on the other hand, have a snare effect that breaks randomly, and can even break immediately upon application to the monster. This is a balance issue. All other classes that have some method of slowing the monsters down have an effect that will last a guaranteed length of time. I cannot perform my duties as a snarer in group anymore, even when singing this song repeatedly...the monsters can still get away. Couple the random duration of this song with its naturally high resist rate and it's almost worthless.

You mention a limitation on songs as they are "free". Keep in mind the mana cost on everyone else's snare effects, they are extremely small. Even IF this song was never resisted AND had a fixed duration a bard has to give up 1/3 of his song ability to use this. What Verant seems to fail to understand is that bard songs are not free. We can only maintain three at a time, therefor by keeping a monster snared we can only hope to keep up two other songs. Again, this is a balance issue...the cost to the bard for using this song (1/3 the bard's song potential) is much higher than that for any mana-using caster. Let's assume it's a necromancer doing the snaring using the high-cost Dooming Darkness effect...120 mana for about 1.5 minutes of snare effect. That's less than 5% of their total mana, given a very realistic 2500 total mana pool. That necromancer will recover the mana used in about 12 seconds while meditating with Lich on.

I don't know if I can explain it any more clearly than that, but having five classes in the game with a guaranteed duration snare versus bards having a highly-resisted and random duration snare is extremely unbalanced. That's like making everyone's Root spell work except wizards.

I'm very concerned by this notion that non-bards have, that our songs are "free" because they don't use mana.

Imagine a caster with
Max mana of 10, unbuffable
Mana regen rate of 10 per tick, unbuffable
3 second casting time on all spells
Max 18 second duration on all spells
All spells cost 10 mana.

That's a bard. The spells are not FREE -- it's just that we can only spend mana at one rate, and we regen at that rate.

This is NOT a particularly favorable ratio of spending rate to regeneration rate, when compared to other casters doing similar things. ANY caster (including hybrids) can regenerate mana used for snaring, stunning, mezzing, etc. far faster than they need to spend it to make the effect work. Bards have the WORST mana-efficiency of any class, hands down.

Let's talk Chains again. A bard responsible for Snaring will melee while singing Chains continuously. That means he's using 100% of his mana-equivalent for snaring. In return, he can manage to keep most mobs snared about 80% of the time.

A ranger in the same role will cast Snare until it sticks. Even if it takes 2 tries on average (50% resists, much higher than you would really see), that's only 30 mana. The ranger will recover that mana, even while fighting, in 3 minutes without any mana-regen buffs. Typically, the ranger will recover all of the mana spent on Snare without incurring any downtime or time not meleeing.

If you ask me which of these two Snares is FREE, it's pretty obvious to me: the ranger's Snare is free. He doesn't have to give up anything at all to guarantee that the mob will not run away at full speed, 100% effective for 10 minutes. No extra downtime, no reduced melee ability (other than the time to cast Snare once or twice at the beginning of the fight), nothing worth noticing.

This makes no sense. The bard spends 100% of his magic capacity to get an 80%-effective Snare, while the ranger gets a 100% effective Snare for no cost.

Absor, which part of this cost analysis do you disagree with?

Absor,

You used the word "free" in response to the bard snare. Please understand that bard songs are NOT FREE!

Every song a bard sings takes up one third of his/her potential contribution to a group other than melee damage. This is a very high cost compared to what casters can do.

I play a 52 bard and a 52 chanter. With my chanter, I can keep my tanks at 60% haste (SLTW), MR debuff the mobs by 33 (tash), lock down three mobs reliably forever (dazzle & med), and keep myself and other casters in clarity (clarity). I can do this while being at a net positive in terms of mana. If there are caster mobs present (theft of thought!), or if the puller brings less than three at a time, my chanter regains mana even more quickly, allowing me to slow the mobs by 65% (shiftless deeds), buff the group's MR (group magic resist), throw around a component free rune, stun mobs reliably, and do all sorts of other stuff.

I can maintain all of the above contributions to my group at all times with my chanter, and every one of them is about TWICE as good as the contribution my bard can do to a group, with the exception of clarity, which is closer.

The mana regeneration ability of a 50+ spell caster allows them to easily do more than three effects simultaneously, whereas a bard can only do 3 things at once. In most cases, the things that a chanter, shaman, cleric, necro, or other caster does are actually better, in a quantitative sense, than what a bard does.

For actual details on how to balance a bard's ability, look at Thott's power curve analysis. There are concerns on the bard boards as to whether you have read it - please indicate that you did, as it is crucial to understanding how bards are not balanced.

Absor: Your comments on snare and haste were very dismaying to me. Snare is, in my opinion, the most egregiously unbalanced song in our songbook, and for you not to implicitly understand our concerns says to me that we're never going to get balanced properly.

Here is a comparison:

Druid/Ranger:
1. Needs to snare on average twice to have it take effect.
2. Cost is 30 mana (regens in 3-30 seconds, call it 10 at high levels), plus 5ish seconds of casting.
3. Snare lasts 10 minutes, fixed, no question.
4. Length of effect/Cost of effect: 600/15 = 40:1 effectiveness ratio(!)
5. Snare does not need to work for 10 minutes, but this allows the caster to cast at his leisure, and to regen the mana whenever is convenient. Assume 30 seconds of required snaring, ratio is 2:1, with an assurance that it will work and the convenience of casting it whenever it best fits in.

Bards:
1. 30-40% resist means that, in order to snare a mob for 30 seconds, it is necessary to sing it every single song attempt (3-4 seconds), and even then the resist will sometimes allow mobs to run (3 resists in a row is not terribly common but still possible, it happens about 5% of the time we sing this song 3 times in a row, or roughly 15% of the time we try to lock down a mob for 30 seconds). This means that we CAN'T snare a mob as effectively as any other snaring class, period, based on the resists and the short duration alone.
2. To attempt to lock down a mob for 30 seconds, we need to sing this song for 30 seconds. This is an effectiveness ratio of 1:1, half as good as the ranger/druid. We can melee for all 30 seconds, the ranger/druid is casting for 5 and doing something else for 25, so that is a small benefit, but does not make up the shortfall. Plus, we do not have the convenience of choosing WHEN to sing it, we HAVE to sing the song at the end of the fight, no matter what else may be required. This means that our snare is vastly less efficient than other classes, and is in addition far less convenient and far less flexible.
3. In ADDITION to both of the above, every other snare in the game has a fixed duration. Ours has a random duration of 0 to 12 seconds. This means that roughly 1/4 of the time, the snare "wears off" BEFORE WE'VE HAD TIME TO SING IT AGAIN. And THEN we have to face the resist check!

The net effect is that we have to devote vastly more of our resources to a snare that DOESN'T WORK.

I am referring here to a level FIFTY ONE SONG. If you can't see that this is blatently unbalanced, there is really no hope.

To parse your statement:

"Well, it may have a much shorter duration than a druid snare, but you can also re-snare it, what, every 6 seconds?"

Recast time isn't the point. The Druid can recast every 2.5 seconds. The point is the utter ineffectiveness of the song.

"This is not really intended to be a song that you can lock down something forever with."

But the point IS to keep the mob from running. This song doesn't DO that. And the concept that we can do this "forever" is irrelevant. If ALL that a druid/ranger does is: snare, med, snare, med, they will use a total of 5% of their casting potential to keep a MOB snared forever, and IT WON'T MOVE. We will use 100% of our casting potential, and it will get away repeatedly. And what is the value of snaring a MOB indefinitely in any case?

"free' is always a dangerous thing when you are talking balance"

Bards songs aren't FREE. They cost 3-4 seconds of our time to cast them, they last 12 seconds. We can only do (on average) THREE THINGS AT ONCE. Compare this to ANY other high-level casting class.

I know that bards are MEANT to be a group class, but the fact of the matter is that post-30 (and REALLY post-50) bards do not make very good group members, especially if there's an enchanter in the group.

In "broken" groups bards continue to excell, because we can provide the hastes, slows, snares and crowd control which are missing from the group. Once you add an enchanter to the group, our main addition to the group would be in damage dealing (sucky) and that's about it.

It appears that many of the Kunark (if not SoV)dungeons are not really do-able without an enchanter. Now the addition of the level 53 mez song has helped this somewhat, but once again, this means that your group has to be broken in order for the bard to be useful.

There must be some way to make bards useful in a group that already contains an enchanter.

One method is the "haste-stacking" method which would allow the bard's haste songs to stack with other haste spells. Haste-stacking for all our haste songs is not the answer, because lower-level bards would become essential to any group becuase no-one is near the haste cap at low levels.

The current SoV haste-stacking songs would be a great way to solve this problem if they were slightly higher hastes (say 10-20% at level 50) and if they did not require an instrument in order for them to be used. Making them instrument-only just makes for a zero-sum (or negative-sum) situation where the damage you lose from the bard not being able to melee is counteracted by the extra melee damage form the tanks you're now hasting. You could always make the extra haste increase by using an instrument, but apparantly that's broken too.

I understand tat all this haste-stacking sounds dangerous to Verant, but it really wont make that much of a difference at the very higheset levels anyway becuase they're alrady at the haste cap. Once you hit the haste cap, haste stacking won't work. If you limit haste-stacking to only the high-level bard songs then a bard might actually be wanted instead of another melee/pet class

Other ways of making bards more effective in the group is to make songs scale better, as has been said many times before in this thread. If the damage shield, DoT and regen songs could be made to increase exponentially instead of linearly that would perhaps make up for the exponential increases in mob strength between 50-60.

Allowing the level 34 cantana song, Niv's, rune and heal songs all stack with each other would also really help (as well as the level 55 cantana as well perhaps). Some decent regen stacking might allow the clerics to save a bit of mana from time to time as well.

Of course, the main solution to almost all the balance problems in the high-level game would be to remove the complete heal power from the clerics, and to rescale the mobs from that point on, but I guess we don't want to go there.

Thanks for listening,
