I would first like to thank Absor for being willing to sift through all these opinions, rants, and pleas to find something worth taking to Verant. I will attempt to avoid ranting too much.

I myself have not thoroughly read every single post on here, but I have sifted through them and completely read a good number of them. I may or may not repeat someone else's recommendation or suggestion but this is what I feel could be done to make the Paladin class a more balanced partner in the Everquest community.

I think the main problem stems from the fact that every class needs to have an identity and a purpose to set them apart a bit. I don't mean that there cannot be any overlap, but a class needs to have a purpose that is primarily theirs. This purpose does not have to be defined by abilities alone, but their abilities should reflect this.

In my eyes, and I believe Verants as well, Paladins are the great defenders of the innocent and just. We are not meant to be the greatest damage dealers out there nor are we meant to be wizards in which to blow our opponents to dust. The one area in which the Paladin does become the aggressor and charge forth in holy vigor is when fighting evil. In regards to this we should have abilities, equipment, and quests that shine on this aspect.

What is evil though exactly? Currently Verant has pretty much defined the evil that Paladins combat as being undead. While I agree that this is certainly correct at least in part, I would at a minimum extend this list to include our greatest opposition and challenger, the Shadowknight. In truth were I to define it, it would be more like undead as well as any follower to Innoruuk, Cazic-Thule, and Bertox. It could also just be undead, Shadowknights, and clerics of the previously mentioned gods.

I think something that lets us be more effective in facing these opponents would be beneficial as well as enhancing roleplay. The following are my recommendations/suggestions:

Give Paladins a chance to perform critical/crippling blows against these types of aggressors. Such an ability is obviously very limited in that it only works against these types of opponents and is still limited by our significantly lower damage output than pretty much any other melee/hybrid class other than bards.

Give the Paladins a few more unique spells that are not superceded by other classes spells. I am not talking about a large number, but maybe 4 or 5 scattered throughout the Paladin's life. Preferably make them along the lines to fulfill our class's purpose. Perhaps a smite type DD that only affects the above mentioned evils, or maybe a resist fear line that makes the Paladin immune or highly resistant to fears spells for a short duration.

Remove the lvl 55 Paladin discipline and put something a little more purposeful in its place. My suggestion would be to give the Paladin a discipline keeping in line with his particular role . My thoughts on this would be something like
Heroic Aura where the Paladin has a large increase in all defense skills and AC and attracts the attention of all mobs within a certain radius to attack him. This does not really increase any damage he is doing to the enemy but gives those around the Paladin a few seconds to breathe and either run, letting the Paladin make his heroic last stand to save others, or gives them a fighting chance to regroup and cast a heal or two. I am REALLY in favor of something like this, as I think that it would add a lot of flavor and fun and let Paladins enjoy their one small moment of glory without unbalancing the game.

Give Paladins better and more equipment. This is by far one of the biggest complaints I have with the Paladin class. There are numerous WAR SHD and even RNG weapons out there that are significantly better than what Paladins can use. On the armor side, I am not seeing a significant number of WAR PAL armor that SHD can't use. I can understand Rangers getting better weapons, offense is after all, on of their main attractions. Shadowknights however, everyone keeps referring to as one of the "defensive tanks". Personally I think this is a fallacy. The only reason they are claimed under that umbrella is because they wear plate and can't dual-wield so sometimes use a shield. And yet, when you look at their spells and the weapons they can use, they are FAR more offensively oriented than Paladins. Heck, even their Harm Touch ability is an offensive attack. They are not a "defensive tank". They are a middle of the road tank. They have better defense yet less offense than a Ranger, but have better offense and SHOULD HAVE less defense than the Paladin. The problem is because everyone has thrust them into the role of "defensive tank" there is a serious problem of balance between them and Paladins. The change in the defense caps for the hybrids were fine with the exception that I think the Shadowknights should have had an equal split in upgrades to defense and offense. Give them the same number of points in upgrade but split them and put half in each. Sorry starting to rant so I'll stop that

Back to the equipment thing, Paladins should have armor much better than Shadowknights can use if we are going to be denied better weapons and yet they are going to be allowed to use them. Also, what about shields? We need more, and we need better, and we should have better ones than the Shadowknights do based on my previous statements of difference between Paladins and Shadowknights.

Gods and follower specific items. I like these. What I don't like is that their don't seem to be one for every combination out there at least at this point. I also don't like that some of the best Paladin gear out there is usuable only by one race or religion and on the Shadowknight side, their equivalent item is usuable by all races and religions of Shadowknights(Shield of the Stalwart Seas/Obtenebrate Mithril Guard for examples). I would like to see armor, shields, and weapons that are reflective of our devotion to our particular gods and I would like to see that they do have some differences but I also want it to be fair to everyone. I don't think that just because one Paladin follows Brell or Tunare they get the most awesome sword in existence and in return the other god's Paladins make do with a semi-standard sword from their god. I would also like to see armor and shields with the crests of their god, and perhaps color as well. Like white and gold for Mithaniel Marr and green and silver for Tunare, etc.

Anyway, these are my ideas and I appreciate you taking a look at them. I am excited and pleased that Verant is actually taking the time to do something about setting right the few problem areas there are so that we can all enjoy Norrath a little more.

Ah, the paladin issues thread. I've been waiting for you...

I will mention one thing here: I began writing this several weeks ago. A few days ago, I read on EQ Caster’s Realm that several changes to several classes had appeared on the Test server. Since it has been repeatedly stated that nothing on Test is ever final, I am not going to take the reported Test changes into account and modify this list.

First, the good:
I was only level 15 when the increased hybrid mana pool was implemented many, many months ago. Seeing 1/5 of my mana bar vanish with every Greater Heal now, I can only imagine how rough paladins, and other hybrids, had it before this went into effect. This was a GREAT change. This was probably the best change ever made for hybrids.

I am also glad that we hybrids finally got disciplines with Velious. I was always confused as to why, even though I swung my sword just as much as the warriors, I could not grasp such simple concepts as swinging it four times in a row, or parrying a whole lot for a short time...But more on that later.

The Lay Hands improvement was needed, and well received. Prior to this change, our "defining" skill was, after level 40, only a glorified partial heal. I am quite happy now that I can fully heal myself when I have full group buffs up.

The removal of the class-based experience penalties: wonderful.

Paladin spells are generally pretty good all-around. I have no serious complaints with the current spells, just a suggestion or two where I think they may be lacking.

There is one last thing that I'll stick in right here since I don't know where else to put it. In the SK Talk thread, you mentioned something in your summary to the effect of "SKs and paladins are defensive hybrids, with Rangers being the offensive hybrids." Since paladins and SK concerns are similar, I thought I'd mention it. Granted, SKs can wear plate, but really, can they defend themselves for any amount of time in a high-level fight? Does a 75-point lifetap keep them alive for even a combat round longer when monsters frequently hit for 100+? Take a look at all the spells a SK has. Harm Touch, pets, dots...Tell me how many of them are defensive in nature.

Here is how the majority of people who I have talked to view hybrid melee abilities:

Rangers are a pure offense class, with their offense being melee-based. Their spells are for augmenting the group and helping during downtime.

Shadowknights are a pure offense class, with their offense being both spell and melee based. "I'm going to kill you before you do the same to me, just cause I'm mean!"

Paladins are primarily a defensive class. All our spells pretty much revolve around allowing us to take a tremendous amount of damage, and helping others doing the same during combat. Heals, buffs, stuns, Lay Hands... All the tools in our arsenal seem designed to help us hang in a fight for as long as possible and deal as much damage and absorb as much as we can over time. We try to accomplish through magic what warriors accomplish through brute strength, higher HP and AC, and better skills.

Anyway, I thought I would go ahead and mention that now. In keeping with the defensive tank nature of paladins, I now will submit the areas where paladins could really use improvement.

---Melee Skills

Having just recently gotten myself to planes/dragon/serious dungeon level, I am starting to truly notice a major discrepancy in my offensive and defensive skills. This is not only in comparison to other classes, but especially in comparison to monsters. I'll get right down to it, since my post is going to be long...

Paladin offensive and defensive skills seem geared to a game where 50 is still the level cap. With typical monsters in Kunark, Velious, and other 50+ areas far exceeding the skills of 50th level, we begin to fall much further behind in offense and defense. I have no problems with monks, rogues, and warriors blatantly out damaging me with their specially developed skills. I accept the fact that when I chose to be a paladin, I sacrificed the time I could spend into developing special skills in return for developing my spell casting ability. What I object to, however, is the fact that non-hybrid tanks still blatantly out damage me when they are *not* using their special skills, and I am using every tool at my disposal. The problem gets worse when even yard trash monsters become unstunnable. Robbing paladins of their Stun spells pretty much robs them of their usefulness. Got a cleric? Paladins get out-buffed. Got any other melee class except a SK? Paladins have less AC (never under normal circumstances should rogues, rangers, and monks have more AC! This is ridiculous!), and "it's more efficient if we Complete Heal the warrior!" So, what do we contribute to the fight? Extremely minor melee damage, since we can't even hit the things we're fighting, and *MAYBE* the occassional heal during the downtime afterwards.


--Offense
Paladins should get an upgrade to offense in some form or another. We simply are not very effective at hitting monsters in melee combat after level 50. Our offensive skills are too low when compared to a monster's defensive skills. We simply can’t do much damage at all. Obviously, if we can't damage a monster, we can't get it angry enough to attack us. What good is a tank who doesn't tank?

-Weapon Skills
If you are hesitant to simply upgrade Offense, perhaps weapon skills could be increased by a fair amount above their current cap. That way, if my understanding of the system is correct, we would at least be able to hit the monsters more, if not for more all-out damage per swing. I would be quite content if I could at least HIT what I am swinging at consistently. It is quite frustrating to be hacking away at the shins of a giant, yet still be inept enough with a weapon that I flat-out miss a target that large as much as I do. It kind of makes me feel bad. I'd like to think that, if I had attacked a huge monster which was standing stationary in front of me and the monster didn't parry, dodge, or riposte, I was at least coordinated enough to still *hit* it.

-Bash
A paladin, being one of the only two classes who frequently uses shields after level 20 or so, should be quite adept at bashing things in the head with a large blunt object. I propose two things:
1) Paladins should have a Bash cap equal to warriors.
2) Bash should have its damage upgraded.
One would think that spinning around and slamming a large, dense chunk of solid metal into a monster's head would cause an appreciable amount of damage. The most I have EVER heard of anyone bashing for is eighteen damage though. Bash is the only way a paladin can consistently supplement his damage output. Paladins need every point of extra damage in order to coax monsters to attack them. I do not foresee that increasing the maximum bash damage from eighteen to thirty or so would truly have an unbalancing effect on battles with level 50+ monsters. Since no one except paladins and shadowknights use shields at this point anyway, the total group damage output will not increase by much. It would help increase our low damage output somewhat, and at the same time, bashing for 30 or whatever would at least make paladins *feel* like they might be doing their share of damage in a post-50 battle.


--Defense
Paladins are defensive tanks. However, our defense seems, just like offense, still be geared for a level 50 cap in a level 60 cap game. A 50+ monster's offense seems to be able to easily override our best defenses. "Grrrr Bark Bark Grrr Oh I'm sorry, were you wearing armor there? Oops." (insert sickening crunching sound here)

-Defense
Our defense skill in general could use an upgrade, perhaps much closer to warrior levels.

-Parry
Using a shield and seeing as much combat as we do, one would think that we would be able to anticipate where a blow was going to land and somehow be coordinated enough to deflect it. Our Parry skill should be closer to or equal to warrior-level, especially considering that we *are* typically holding a shield.
I really don’t know how it could be coded, but isn’t there some way our Parry skill could be increased to near-warrior level when we are using a shield? I currently do not notice any difference between using a two-hand weapon and using a one hand/shield combo. I parry almost exactly the same amount in any given fight no matter what my weapons are. It just really seems to me that we should be able to parry more with a shield.

-Dodge
This seems to not happen very often, at least to me. Perhaps parry or riposte catches hits before I get a chance to dodge them. The cap seems appropriate though, since we are not quite the same brawny hulks that warriors are, but we are still wearing heavy plate armor that they are wearing. Still, I wouldn't protest a slight upgrade here.

-Disarm
My disarm skill is capped. I have disarmed exactly two things in at least 72 hours of pure combat: an Orc Pawn and a Greater Kobold. Raise the skill cap so we can disarm fine steel weapons (which seem to triple a monster’s damage output) with some sort of regularity. Do something to make this skill worth clicking. Perhaps, once the button is pressed, it would stay greyed out for ten or fifteen seconds, and any successful attacks during that time might have a chance of disarming the creature? Or perhaps when the monster parries our attack with a weapon? Just do something to make this skill useful…


--Disciplines
As I said earlier, I am very happy that paladins finally got disciplines with Velious. I also am pleased that they followed the same "pure melee with a magical bent" that our class follows. However, I have always been puzzled as to why, after swinging our weapons just as many billions of times as the warriors, monks, and rogues, that we cannot figure out how to swing it four times in a row, or how to focus our attention and parry a flurry of blows for a time. In my opinion, paladins should get at least one, possibly two of the regular warrior disciplines, which would leave us with half the disciplines of a non-hybrid melee. Really, how horribly would it unbalance things if paladins could parry everything for ten seconds in one battle every hour? Following are the disciplines which paladins should at least be considered for.

-Riposte Discipline
It would be nice to occasionally get a massive burst of attacks. Perhaps then we could even participate in raids against nasty melee fighters such as Venril Sathir, among others...

-Parry Discipline
In keeping with our defensive nature, this discipline is only logical. After angering a half-dozen monsters with a well-placed heal or stun, we could use this spell to give us those extra few moments for the heal to arrive before the ensuing flurry of angry blows takes us down.

-Dodge Discipline
I am less in favor of having this discipline added, since it would simply be the same thing as the parry discipline. Also, as I stated before, I wouldn't expect paladins, with their lesser strength, to be able to dodge hits while decked out in their Sunday best plate mail.

---Spells

All in all, I think paladins get the most useful batch of spells compared to other hybrids' spells. For the most part, they are all great. There is slight room for more improvement, though. Our role in a group is to take the damage meant for others and to save others in a pinch, right?

--Casting Speed
Some of our more advanced spells are too difficult to cast during combat. Since our Channeling caps at a fairly low point compared to pure casters, our chances to recover from a hit are lower. Our chances to be hit, however, are much higher. Since we cannot specialize, our fizzle rates are also much higher. Perhaps Greater Heal and Superior Heal should be given slightly faster casting times, so that getting in that last-second heal in a pinch isn't as tremendously difficult as it is now. Paladins give up *effectiveness* for *speed*, which sounds like a reasonable opportunity cost to me. Clerics can heal infinite hit points with a single spell, but it takes a fair amount of time to cast. They trade off *speed* for *effectiveness*. I really can't envision how a half-second or even full second casting time decrease on either Greater Heal or Superior Heal would be overpowering. We will still be tremendously limited by our tiny mana pool (in comparison to our parent caster class), our fizzle rate would still be the same, and we still run the high risk of being interrupted by a monster while we are casting or getting beaten down immediately after healing someone. With this slight change, though, we just don't have quite as high a chance of failure...

Speaking from personal experience, it is EXTREMELY frustrating when it appears I will get the saving heal in on a group member, only to have the spell fizzle at the last possible moment, or to have a monster suddenly turn and focus on me and interrupt me at the last possible moment. Consequently, after missing the heal, the person usually dies.

Also, I have noticed that after level 45, even when I am standing totally still, I frequently have to “regain my concentration and continue casting.” It has happened to me too many days in too many different zones and while using too many different spells for it to be just a bad run of luck with the random number generator. This might just be something that I alone have noticed… Strangely enough, I now fizzle more with my level 30 spells than I did when I had just scribed them at level 30. Take a look at this, would you?


--Current Spell Issues

-Yaulp 2
The effect on Yaulp 2 seems to wear off as soon as it is castable again. If I remember correctly, Yaulp 1 seemed to last for an extra two or three seconds after the spell gem returned, usually long enough to properly time another cast in between swings. This spell also seems to get interrupted or fizzle on me a lot more than a 15 mana, half-second casting spell should...

-Lull and Soothe
About ninety percent of the time, these spells fail and end up only creating trains. Perhaps the instant aggro component upon failure could be toned down, or the resist rate lowered. This is very rarely useful as it currently is.

-Flame of Light
No one uses this spell. I don't think a single 50+ paladin on my server even has this spell scribed. This isn't speculation either; I made a list of every single 50+ paladin on my server and I have talked to them all too. Simply put, for 85 mana (a very noticeable amount for paladins) and 2.5 seconds of casting time, this spell isn't worth it. Compared to the hitpoints of monsters by this time, this spell is only a drop in the bucket, especially considering that with a two-handed weapon and a double attack, we can typically do more damage in the same amount of time. We could also throw that mana to a likely much more useful stun. Reducing both the casting time and mana cost by at least half, or increasing the damage by a decent amount is the only way level 50+ paladins might even bother to scribe the spell. We could then use it as a supplemental damage boost when using two-handed weapons.

Currently, the only use this spell has is to bewilder newbies by going anon, hanging out an an orc camp, wearing a mismatched assortment of armor, duel wielding some steins, and alternating between nuking orc pawns, healing, and using 2HSers. "What class is he?!" "I didn't think wizards could use 2HS!1111!" "i heard they added a new class see i told you i was right!" Etc. etc.

Oh, and I have heard that the spell also has no sort of effect attached. It's just a casting message and a damage message. That's pretty...boring....

-Expulse Undead
Unless I am hunting in Kithicor Woods in night and I need a big spell to pull with, I never have this spell memmed. The four second casting time makes it fairly impossible to cast during combat. Perhaps the casting time could be reduced just slightly?


--Proposed New Spells

Sometimes, Stun and Taunt just isn't enough. Monsters just get insanely angry at some people some times for apparently no reason, and it is very hard to "convince" them to turn around and swing at you instead. I personally think that warriors should be able to AE Taunt. Assuming that they get that skill, why shouldn’t a paladin be able to accomplish through magic what a warrior can do with brute strength? If paladins are expected to tank as well as warriors, but we cannot survive as much direct combat as they can, ways are needed to alter the combat scenario. Here are some suggestions. I am trying to present the widest range of options and ideas from my fellow paladins here. I am not necessarily suggesting that we get *all* these spells.


-Area Effect Stun
In emergencies, when a pull has turned bad, and the casters need just a spare second or two extra to evac or complete heal, paladins are mildly good at stopping one monster with stuns for a short amount of time. *ONE* monster. Clerics have an AE stun called "Stun Command" at 55. Perhaps paladins could have a downgraded version of that spell after 55? You know, "Stun Suggestion" or something, heh. The stun time could be reduced while keeping the mana costs the same and increasing the recast time slightly. The 150 mana cost would certainly be prohibit a paladin from using this spell continuously. Not every group has the luxury of having an enchanter... This would certainly ease their burden, and no 55+ group would turn down an enchanter or cleric on the basis that the paladin has one measly little AE stun for temporary crowd control...

-Knockback or Knockback/Stun spell
Again, in those situations where some monster just won't leave somebody alone, a paladin could vent a nice chunk of his mana to forcibly and physically push a monster away from his victim, stunning the monster momentarily and perhaps long enough to get a root cast to hold the critter there. There could also be a minimal direct damage component to the spell, just enough to get the monster angry with the paladin. Again, this would require a sizable amount of mana (100+), so it would have limited application. Also, it would be quite helpful when a monster is trying to flee, and you can just feel that root won't hold and a regular stun won't do the job. Dash out in front and *BAM*, you get an extra fifteen feet and a few precious seconds in which to hopefully save the group.
Basically, it's just another form of very temporary crowd control for those times when enchanters and other crowd control specialists are not a disposable luxury...

-AE Root
Paladins get Root at 22. We are forced to cope with what is really a level 9 spell until extremely late in our lives. At level 54, we FINALLY get an upgrade to Root: Enstill. Especially when my group has needed to make a hasty run for their lives, I would have given anything for an AE root. Again, it would be just another minor crowd control utility spell that would help paladins keep their groups alive. An AE version of regular Root would be really nice.
Oh, and speaking of Enstill, as I recall, somebody mentioned in the Wizard (?) thread that Enstill didn’t seem to work any better than Root…I can’t say that I’ve tried it myself, I just thought I’d remind you just in case…

-Tremor
Tremor is a level 34 cleric AE DD spell. Since paladins typically get in the thick of combat much more than clerics do, giving paladins this spell at say, level 50, to me, makes sense. Perhaps lowering the casting time from 4 seconds to 3 seconds would be helpful as well, so we could cast it in combat. The only reason I suggested this spell is as an alternative to the AE stun spell above, just for use in those times when extra damage is highly needed, or when paladins could taunt multiple creatures upon themselves and help save the group. Also, this would nicely complement (a slightly upgraded) Flame of Light in helping raise a paladin’s damage output during combat.

-Minor Magic Resist Debuff
Since practically all of a paladin's offensive-style spells are magic based, perhaps a cunning paladin somewhere along the way got the bright idea to stroll in an enchanter guild and scribe a basic magic resist debuff spell. I somehow don't see this one happening, but it's a thought. Stuns are the only viable way we can get monsters to attack us a lot of the time. Unfortunately, monsters seem to be highly magic-resistant, which is really too bad since, as I said, practically every single one of our “offensive” spells are magic-based. There are many times when I have been in combat and the enchanter has been too busy with crowd control to debuff whichever target the tanks were fighting. The level 20 enchanter spell Tashani (23 magic resist debuff) seems like a good candidate to me.

-Conjuration Spell with a Purpose
Currently paladins have a grand total of three conjuration spells. None of them are useful in any way, unless the paladin is on a corpse recovery alone, in the dark, in the rain, and he needs an emergency weapon and lightsource. Of course, by the time he gets these spells, he would be an easy snack for any monster he encountered while naked...Perhaps paladins could learn how to conjure up a magic/lore/nodrop/norent shield, or a nice shield/weapon combo. The shield could have relatively low AC but decent resists, notably magic, poison, and disease. (After all, those are the things a paladin is familiar with and should know how to counter.) It would also be nice if the shield was of the glowing, see-through type and generally looked really neat. Just a thought...

Perhaps, also, a paladin could conjure a nodrop/norent "Shard of Faith" or something similar, putting his faith in his god into tangible form and creating a range slot item with some minor stat boosts, such as some minor + wis and + str.

Heck, I’d even be happy with Summon Food and Summon Drink.

Honestly, as it is, my conjuration spells stay back in a very dark, very lonely corner of my spell book.... They would love some friends to keep them company and perhaps move them closer to the rest of the spells. Really, they would. Please make my conjuration spells feel loved.

-Atone or similar memory-blur style spell
Frankly, paladins have a hard time travelling and exploring things on their own. Without SoW or some form of invis to the living, we have the hardest time of all spell using classes (and of most of the non-casting classes as well) travelling outside of cities. Supposedly, paladins make up with this by having really good faction. However, the one monster in a zone that hates us seems to *always* be red, have a massive aggro range and a bewilderingly fast and powerful attack. “A Scowling_monster00 claws you for…well, a whole lot. Really, it’s so much that you’ll be dead before you finish this sentence. Good bye.” Go ahead, try to explore Kunark or Velious alone as a paladin who doesn't own Journeyman Boots and can't afford a pack full of SoW potions. Maybe a downgraded version of Atone, with a higher resist rate or casting time, could help us out...Just something to keep from training a hundred things upon ourselves as we travel. Green things still hurt, especially in the expansions...

I somehow don't see a spell of this type becoming a reality. It would be great, but I don't think I can actually picture it happening. I just personally have wanderlust some days, and feel like taking a break from endless killing sprees so I can explore the fantastic world of Norrath. I don't always have somebody to travel with, and I doubt I'll get Jboots any time soon. Perhaps with some form of mem blur, I can leave the murderous dinosaur/giant/whatever else behind, run out of aggro range before it recovers, and explore some more in peace... I'm sure that there are many others who feel this way as well.

---Equipment
Melee class fighters are absolutely, with the exception of monks, totally dependent upon their equipment. No matter how you want to debate the wording, when it comes down to it, paladins are a melee class who spends their entire life in melee combat. Let's face it: paladins have a pretty rough time, especially in their early to mid lives, when it comes to equipment. Finding usable items isn't difficult; finding *useful* items is the hard part.


--Item Stats in General
Most items with useful stat boosts are crutches in combat. There is an extreme lack of +Wis, +Str, +Sta gear with decent AC in the game. I think that everyone would agree that those three stats are the core stats a paladin needs to succeed. There is a surprisingly much larger number of + STR + Int gear with decent AC than there is of + Wis, + Str or +Sta. I mean, heck, even Valorium Armor has two pieces with +5 Int apiece! Paladin gear seems to be geared majorly towards + Cha (useless in combat), + Int (useless in combat), and + Agi (of seemingly dubious usefulness). Most items that do have +Wis are low or no AC items with +Int and other useless stats as well. What this means is that a paladin is forced to live with roughly the same base Wis and Str for his entire life. AC is critical to our class, since we are expected to tank just as well as a warrior. We are continually forced to choose between surviving a melee or having nice stats but getting our heads taken off when it comes time to fight.

Paladins need more items that help support both our melee and casting skills in combat. Frankly, as much as you may claim otherwise, EQ is, at the core, a game about killing monsters.
