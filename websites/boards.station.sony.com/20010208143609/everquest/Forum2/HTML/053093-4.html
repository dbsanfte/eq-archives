Many things discussed, many things touched upon that I think are worth discussing.

There are a few issues that need to be addressed that I think people aren't keeping track of, however.

1 Taunt - Yes, Taunt is ineffective at high levels. It would be good if it could be improved so that warriors aren't just mashing the taunt button every few seconds. Perhaps a new version of taunt that has a longer (say, twice as long) cycle time, but has a significantly higher chance for forcing the warrior to the top of the hate list. That way choosing to hit 'taunt' becomes an actual TACTICAL decision as opposed to what it mostly is now: Mash the non-grey button!

2 Disarm - Yes, disarm is a 'useless' skill as far as its original intent (disarming the enemy to reduce their damage output). It has limited usefulness in a very specific situation (mobs armed with low-delay fine steel, for instance)

However, I know that I always use disarm. Why? because it's another skill that bumps me up the hate list, I've found. Maybe i'm giving away some kind of 'uber warrior secret' (yeah right), but I have found that if i do a properly timed taunt/slam/disarm, I can force a mob to turn to me at pre-45 levels. post 45 is still broken, taunt wise, but this >is< a valid use for disarm that I think people are missing.

Re: crippling blows. Well, generally i couldn't care less about crippling blows, but when you combine it with the discipline that sets all blows to be criticals, it becomes the exact 'do or die' discipline that someone was talking about. I've seen a single warrior use that combo for amazing amounts of damage in seconds.

What would I like to see improved for warriors?

Taunt.

Yeah, the epic weapon could be better, yadda yadda, etc, etc. But if you want to know what skills/abilities as a class I think need improvement:

Taunt.

That's it.
