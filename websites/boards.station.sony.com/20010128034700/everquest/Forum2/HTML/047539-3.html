Shafticus -- distance from the mob is a factor in who it chooses to attack. (Damage and taunt, sure, but also just plain old distance.)

When you tell a pet to /pet attack from long range, it moves toward the mob, and starts hitting it at its maximum melee range. For whatever reason, that melee range is larger for mage pets than for shaman pets. (Necros are in between; not sure about other classes.) Since the pets are fairly close in power, the difference in distance means the mob is hitting the closer pet.

One trick for dealing with this is to not tell your pet to attack until you've got the mob where you want it. Your mage can then have the pet /follow him right "into" the mob. Tell it to attack then, when it's really close. The other way to do it is for your puller to stop on the far side of the pet, just at the mob's melee range, so that the mob stops on top of the pet. Then you tell it to attack.

The tanks can help by trying to stand farther away from the mob than the pet (difficult if not impossible with a mage pet at max range; easier with others).
