Executive summary
=================

* Don't mess with specialization, it will annoy many more people than it will please.

* Increase drop rate of earth staff off Inny, it's unfair to have hundreds of rogue/shaman/monk/cleric epics to each mage epic.

* Make call of hero work in Sky to increase the attractiveness of this underutilized zone and to give mages a class specific tool that will help them and their friends reach an otherwise super hard to reach part of their epic quest.


Long version
============

The ability to change specialization has been in game for a while, so many conjuration specialists might have changed over to evocation by now. Making changes to spells at this stage would likely annoy those people a second time, as well as annoy the evocation specialists.

It's not fair or balanced by any stretch of the imagination that there are a hundred each of shaman, rogue, monk and cleric epics to every one mage epic weapon in existence.

Our epic definitely needs fixing by increasing the drop rate of the earth staff, and we need confirmation that it was fixed - anecdotes by players who have seen it drop "twice in the last week" won't cut it because of selection bias (30 servers, 52 weeks in a year, even with a 1% drop rate you'll hear these stories all the time). The drop rate on the earth staff is ridiculous for a mob that spawns once a week, let alone that Inny is totally out of reach for many mages due to the way guilds arrange to share the planes.

Also, to help with the mage epic, make call of the hero work in Plane of Sky (the crown is on island 7, almost all raids run out of steam long before that because people have real lives too). The zone is very underused right now because it is a huge time, exp and platinum sink, for relatively meagre rewards compared to Kunark, Velious etc.

My vision of a mage is that of a conjurer. He summons forth his elemental and prepares it for battle with summoned weapons, armor, and items appropriate for the foes he will face. He summons items for his comrades which will assist them in the upcoming battle. During battle he controls his elemental warrior, assisting his friends by using his control of the elements to inflict further damage upon his foes.

Thus, there are 3 aspects to a mage: pets, summoned items, and elemental mastery. To tune the mage, I think these concerns should be addressed in order. [I would leave the discussion concerning the epic weapon out of this particular debate].

Pets First - Sugggestions include better ways to control pets, to buff pets, and to heal pets. More summons we can give to pets. The main idea here is to make the pet useful in all situations and give the mage the tools he needs to prepare his pet for any battle. Several pet buffs are live on test, and a new pet heal seems like a good bet. Due to pathing, mages need better ways to control pets. Suggestions include: having the pet summon clear pet aggro, a leash spell to keep pet from wandering off, or a command to stop pets from attacking under any circumstance. Another idea would be to buff up the rage of zomm spell (and maybe make it a line of spells), so when a mage can't summon a pet, he summon up this type of pet. Also, a pet spell which casts fast (2 second cast time) and has a high taunt would be a great addition. This would give mages a method of escape which fits in with their abilities. The pet would tie up the mob for several seconds and give the mage a chance to run or gate.

Also under pet problems is the post-50 firepet. Extensive testing has revealed that the pet can't melee effectively, that even when it is not meleeing that it is easily outdamaged by the other pets, and that it will cast disruptive AE spells and dispells so it cannot be used in a group setting effectively.

Next, summons. No line of spells in the game has such potential and such poor realization. The fear that summoned items will be too powerful is just that a fear. With twinking, the ability to control the proc level of weapons, and the ability to make summoned items no rent, lore, and no drop, this should not be an issue. Mod rods were a good idea, make it a line of spells with a lower level version and a higher level version. Give our weapon line of spells some life with good +resist gear for the ammo slot and proc'ing weapons that we can give to pets or [heaven forbid] even to other players vs. certain mobs. Throw in a couple of general purpose and fun summons and mages should rub their bellies with contentment.

Third Elemental mastery comprises DD, bolts, damage shields, and DOT's. With the above ideas implemented, mages' DD and bolts need very little fixing. The Blast School of magery identifies these problems: variety of DD (evoc and conj only), mana efficiency of DOT's, lack of DD at certain levels, drop in blast-o-matic rankings post 50. Personally, other than fixing the DOT's that were given to us, I don't think the blast school is going to get their concerns addressed. Nor do I think they need to. Blasting is fun, but ultimately it is not the best way to do damage. At low levels mages rely upon their blast power to solo. But the general consensus is that mages have enough firepower to support their main source of damage - the pet. I think this is appropriate. A look at rain spells may be warranted because of high resists and the ability of the target to move making them un-mana efficient. This may not be true, with earth pet and malaise stuck, they may be ok. At least look at them.

Ultimately, to be balanced, mages needs pets for every situation including areas with bad pathing, and I would contend an escape pet type of spell. Second, Mages need summoned items that are useful at the level they are gained and that are desired by the players under the right circumstances. +Resist gear which could go in ammo slots for raids is one good example. There are numerous others examples.

Well, gotta run...

Absor,

I play an Arch Mage on Vallon Zek, a PvP server. From my experience I can add the following to this discussion:

1) In 60 levels, the only defense a mage has is his/her pet and gate. Thats it. Every other pure caster gets some kind of damage absorbtion, root and stun/mez. The justification has always been 'we have the strongest pets' and 'our earth pet casts root and air stuns.'

Eliminate the pet from the situation (pet dies, you zone, pet is feared or rooted or mezzed, etc.) and the justification no longer applies, and you're left with a defenseless magician, something you can basically say for NO other class in the game.

2) By the same token, Magicians are severely handicapped in PvP if their pet is removed from combat (root mez fear etc). Yes, you can annul or pumice, but while youre doing that you're also allowing your opponent to get in a free attack(s).

Either GZ or Gordon stated pets comprise 50% of a magicians power. Tell me, what other class can have its PvE or PvP power cut in half by ONE spell?

3) The only summons I use at 60 are: ring of levitation, rod of modulation, quiver of marr (rarely, for those cheapskate warriors), gift of xev, and occasionally shard of the core for resists. The type of summons that I know I'd appreciate are an improved shard of the core with better resists (since the current spell is only 34, it stands that a later spell could grant better bonuses), or an improvement to the rod of modulation, such that it confers greater health to mana/ summoning cost efficiency or that it is no longer lore (I would really like better efficiency though - if necros get improved mana transfer spells, and rods of modulation were introduced *prior* to the necro line, surely this lends some weight to the notion that this was our ability first, and perhaps an upgrade as they have recieved is not too unrealistic?).

I can also tell you that the way you have designed Velious encounters makes my mana far more useful if given to a cleric then if I actually do anything with it.

PvP suggestions:

1) Perhaps improve our shock line with greater pushback or something, that would really aid us in PvP. Wizards get stuns, even shock of lightning excels at this, and enchanters chaotic feedback (level 8 nuke) is an almost guaranteed unresistable interrupt. Our low level shocks are easily channeled through, and our high level ones are slow and easily 'stepped back' into position to channel through.

2) Why are the only useful PvP spells I get: Mala, scars of sigil, scirocco, scintillation, and char? In 60 levels, thats all? Everything else is easily resisted by anyone with half-decent gear or just impractical (Manastorm, seeking flame of seukor, shock of steel, all fairly useless based on casting times, LoS, resistance checks, etc.).

3) I would bear your children if you would give us some kind of pet MR buff, or a pet with naturally high MR. Other than the epic pet, even our level 60 pet is not too unlike the abilities of the necro pet, with the major difference being more HP. Doing something special to our pet would help make the pet conjuring aspect of this class more unique (since you gave their pets procs and spell buffs nearly identical to ours). This would help tremendously in both PvP and PvE. Yes, I know about the fearless pet, which is admittedly a very nice pet, but does nothing regarding a simple root spell.

4) Magicians have the fewest defensive options in PvP than any other casting class, and I would say any other class, period. Should I be thankful we received the gate spell?


My interpretation of the class can be summoned as this: we have a few slightly varying pets, and many useless ones (broken dyzils, one shot rage of zomms, 52, 58 fire pets that are 'situational' says Gordon/GZ but I've yet to find anything appropriate for them). We have some varying DD's, only 3 or so of which see much use. We have some good AE's. We have about 4 useful summons. We have good fireshields and a debuffthat while good, is useless in the presence of a shaman (mala vs malo). We have ONE spell that makes us desired on raids. Other than gate, shielding, see invis and invis and those sort of spells, I think that's all this class boils down to right now. Its not bad, but a little defense would go a long way.

I suspect there's a slight problem with the drop rates. My guess (from observation) is that research components all come from one loot table with uniform probability. It's a big list of all the runes, pages, words, etc, and you just pick one once you make the "drop a research component" roll. However, all the spells but mage pet spells use unique components. A bigger can of worms is balancing the drop rates against the actual server population that needs them.
