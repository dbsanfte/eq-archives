Guilds come in all flavors. But generally the strength of guild starts to be felt post-50. Discipline also picks up. If you wanna raid, you gotta have discipline. Learn to work with people.

I always consider it a triumph when a raid officially gets under way less than an hour after the planned start time.
Between those who show up 15, 30, 45 minutes late and belatedly decide to join in or need a port and the constant reshuffling of groups due to the people who apparently planned to be there for 15, 30, 45 minutes total -- it's an exercise in chaos.
The challenge for every guild is getting people in the habit of planning ahead, arriving on time, planning to stay for at least a few hours and listening to what the raid and group leaders tell them.
We've done a disasterous "learning raid" on Befallen for our teens, and a great mini-raid on Paw where everyone got experience and good loot. But those who went to Befallen and also came to Paw had learned their lessons about all of the above -- and that's half the point of raiding below 50. The other half is having fun, and they all did.
It won't ever necessarily be pretty, but it is a game and it will hopefully be fun for everyone.

You missed a few.

There's the "zeg" guilds.

This is a guild that tries to be high end but doesn't have the skill/time/gear to pull this off.

So their version of raiding is getting as many possible people together to swarm en mass on the nearest target to kill it. Don't confuse high end guilds with zerg guilds, a lot of them detest zerg mentality and only do so on VERY high end stuff which requires massive amounts of people. These are guilds who can kill targets with a few groups that other guilds find necessary to "zerg"

Then the anti zerg guilds. They usuall have high requirements, lower then average membership and stress quality above all else, there are few of them and not overly sucessful, the only good anti zerg guild that has made a powerful presence of themselves (meaning, they're equipping people with vex thal gear) is science of war on legends. Though I'm sure they're others.

Then there's the epic+ guilds.

These guilds are those that have the numbers and power to deal with epic and above encounters. They're generally equipped will enough to kill kunark targets with ease, can raid old planar zones efficiently, and can go after most midrange velious targets. They can get sleepers tomb keys, but don't have the strength to kill higher end luclin bosses. They're usually in tier 2 PoP zones, with maybe a handfull of people in tier 3. They're more casual than raiding guilds, but more structured and regimented, and can get do higher raids than the casual guilds. This is mine.
