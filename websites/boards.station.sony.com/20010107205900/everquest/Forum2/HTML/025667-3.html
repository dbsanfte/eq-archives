Maybe I'm just missing something.....

It's been stated many times that inspecting doesn't affect anything and causes no harm. I agree, in principle.

Those who make this statement then go on to declare that since inspecting doesn't affect anything there is no reason to require permission to do it, and there is no reason to allow the person being inspected to have a choice about whether to allow the inspection or not. Ok, I can follow that line of reasoning, again in principle.

My question, though, is as follows...if inspecting doesn't affect anything and causes no harm (which we all agree is the case) then why NOT require permission or allow the person being inspected to have the choice? Is the ability to inspect other players without their consent somehow REQUIRED by some aspect of gameplay? Would limiting this ability somehow lessen the enjoyment of the game?

I personally don't see the reason for inspecting others without their consent, and I've never been able to come up with any valid reason why the ability to inspect without consent provides a benefit to anyone other than beggers and scam artists. If there ARE true benefits then please share them with the rest of us. I'm willing to listen to reasonable explanations.

JUST GET RID OF THE STUPID MESSAGE AND EVERYONE WILL STOP WORRYING ABOUT IT!

(Ok, now that I have gotten that off my chest ...)

You shouldn't need permission to inspect someone because inspecting is not invasive.

Character graphics are extremely limited. There are only four different graphics for armor and many pieces do not show at all. The 3D rendering you see of someone is what you can might see of a person at a distance or a glance.

Inspection is analagous to looking at someone who is a few feet away. You can see that they are wearing some jewelry, a scaley belt, and that the tunic they wear appears to be sewn from the skin of some beast. The amount of detail you get is still minimal. You don't see the item stats or the contents of the person's backpack. All you see is a name. There is no reason you should be made aware every time someone looks at you.

Character profiles are also a great way to convey more information about your character. The inspection message inhibits people from seeing this information.

Drawing a parallel to another game: Back in UO, the on screen character was a fairly small and low res image. If you actually wanted to see what someone looked like, you would bring up a paperdoll. Nobody *ever* got mad about people bringing up their paperdolls. They didn't even know when it happened, unless the person subsequently made some comment about their equipment.
