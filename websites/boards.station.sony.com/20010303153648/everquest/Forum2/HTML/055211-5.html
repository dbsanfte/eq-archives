Here is a summation of the balance issue, using 50+ hybrids as the model.

Defense skill grants 3 numeric AC per point. Offense grants 1 numeric ATTK per point and weapon skill grants 2. Therefore, a single point of defense is equivalent to a point of offense & weaponskill.

If a Knight has 252 Defense and 230 Offense and Weaponskill, he will gain 156pts of AC and 90pts of ATTK.

If a Ranger has 200 Defense and 252 Offense and Weaponskill, he will gain 0pts of AC and 156pts of ATTK.

The Ranger does have higher Double Attack and Duel Wield skills, which reflect higher damage dealing but it is my understanding that this is balanced by lower AC armors and HP totals resembling MNK and ROG class. Correspondingly, the Knight's lower damage potential is balanced by higher AC and greater HP pools.

The disparity between Hybrid Offense/Defense numeric values is not addressed properly. Rangers have a deficit of 90 numeric AC/ATTK points. This can be addressed by either adjusting the Defense skill to make up the defecit, or since the weapon/offense skills are maxxed, the difference may be made up in Duel Wield and Double Attack skills.

Spells:

Ensnare 51st - This spell fizzles way too much. When I was 50th, my 9th level snare fizzled once a month with low wisdom; it was rock solid reliable, as a spell of this nature should be. Today at 60th, with 130+ wis this spell still fizzles up to 40% of the time. This is unacceptable.

Drones of Doom 54th - This spell is hideously underpowered for the level. The 341 damage it applies over its duration is under 10% of my melee damage potential. The casting time, mediocre mana ratio and difficulty of mastering conjuration make this spell a white elephant in the ranger repetoire. This spell should have been Drifting Death at 59 and Skin Like Nature at 54th instead of Skin Like Diamond.

The defense argument is very simple.

There are 3 factors

1) Ranger Defense skill was completely ignored with RoK, while Knights received significant chages to Offense and defense.
2) Parry, Dodge and Riposte were ignored with RoK. Parry and Dodge were increased to the same proportion as they existed pre-RoK after Verant realized that they messed that one up. However, Riposte was never increased to the same proportions pre-RoK.

4) Rangers have noticibly few HP than Knights.
5) Due to having high offensive abilities, plus a few very high aggro spells, like snare, Rangers incurr a good deal of aggro. To solve this aggro problem Rangers have to back out of fights, which lessens their damage output, for which they sacrificed defensive abilities (notably Plate armor).
6) Jolt doesnt work on velious for the most part.
7) Rangers are a fine class and can do very well if played intelligently. But the disparity in Defense and similarity in offense between the Hybrids is unjustifiable.

Since Rangers are Offensive and Knights are Defensive, it is reasonable that Rangers should defend as well as Knights offend.

Finally, please, please get the team to make archery as a whole more fun and less heartbreaking for a ranger (how would rogues feel if backstab was partially broken, almost useless in terms of damage dealt beyond level 20 or so, and massively expensive to use anyway to its full damage dealing potential via high-end arrows.)

How about: Minimum damage - 2x Arrow dam + any attack/dex bonuses applicable (for all bow-using classes.)

Double Attack (ranger only) - kicks in when d.a. kicks in at 17 or if you feel stingy when off-hand double attack kicks in (29?)

Chance for Crips based on same chances warriors get in melee (ranger only)

Damage bonus based on the same tables that govern the bonus for main hand melee, but capped earlier (40 or so?) if you feel this would be overpowering (all melee classes, perhaps, but ranger only if it would be perceived as too powerful to be implemented across the board.)

In general I feel it's a shame that rangers have to wait till 40 before archery becomes even remotely close to being tactically useful...and surely there's a world of difference between 'tactically useful' and 'primary form of combat'?

Finally, rather than fiddling with Jolt (an inelegant and non-RP solution to a critical problem) why not just give us a form of Evade at level 35 or so, low enough for us to train it up before the hell levels? This would be so much more in keeping with the class. Could be called something other than Evade to make it distinctivelt rangerly, but surely this would be a 'cleaner' solution to the almost crippling aggro problem?

Once again, thanks for listening. Great to have a real opportunity to get such things off our collective chest!
