Graverobber wrote:

>"First off, Lich series. If the "bugged" 1:1 ratio is what is preventing Necros from gaining any sort of upgrades, then fix it. There are ways around it such as increasing HP gear and asking for Talisman and Symbol type buffs. Although personally, I think the spell is actually fine at a 1:1 ratio, seeing as I have thousands upon thousands of mana points versus my thousand HPs. So even at a 1:1 ratio, I will only gain 1300 mana for 1300 life, and that is about all of my life. heh heh But, if this is the one thing that is blocking any kind of fix to the class, then Id rather have it returned to normal. It may be a nerf of sorts, but it can be dealt with."<

Well, fixing Lich and other changes or improvements are linked in a way, but not directly. They are linked because they represent part of the whole of the power of the class. So, yes, fixing Lich might allow some improvements in other areas. But just because we fix it (IF we fix it) does not necessarily mean an improvement in some other area. And, yes, a 1:1 ratio for hit points to mana seems reasonable enough on it's own. But you can't forget that hit points can also be restored with mana. And since hit points gained for mana spent is at a much greater ratio than 1:1, we have to keep in mind that mana (or hit points) can be generated out of nowhere as it is… It's a complicated thing, and we'll have to look at it very carefully. It's also probably the kind of change that would be hard to test, since it's effects would be wide spread. Testing can make sure the changes all work as intended, but how they would affect the fabric of the game is going to be hard to tell.

And keep in mind the paragraph I wrote above. Allowing mana to be given to others at a better ratio could be a mess, since the cleric can turn mana back into hit points very efficiently. We really need to make sure that mana isn't too easily created out of thin air. Obviously a fix to the Lich series would give us more room to work here…
