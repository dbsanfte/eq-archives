The most literal translation of who has "rights" on a mob is that the first person to "engage" the mob has rights.

Sometimes engage means be hit by the mob.
Sometimes engage means damaging/casting on the mob.
Sometimes engage means being able to actually take the mob down (ie/one person kiting Lodi around waiting for a full force to help him drop Lodi has not engaged Lodi).

That's the official spin on it.

**********************************

Player rules on camps and mobs are a bit different but a bit simpler.

Players are supposed to respect each others static camps - ie/a mob or group of mobs spawn in a small, easily defined area belong to the folks who are camping them whether they are engaged or not.

Thus, if I and my group have laid claim to orc camp One, then we "own" all of the orcs in the location accepted by the player population as "ocr camp one." If someone comes along and starts pulling single orcs out of our camp, while they are not KSing by Sony's rules, they are KSing by the player's rules.

Sadly, not everyone accepts or cares that there are other players out there and they are perfectly willing to roll through you, me and anyone else who gets in the way of thier play time. They will invoke the PNP (play nice policy) whenever they want to steal pulls from the camps of others and scream to the whole zone that so-and-so is a camp/kill stealer when someone dares to do the same thing to them.

EQ is no different from RL - @#$holes are everywhere.

SirAndrewWard,
You have the right take on it from your post. The rules SOE has for the PnP only are part of the story though. Yes, there is not an official camp policy. The players have their own unwritten rules though.

In the example in an earlier post a group was hunting at "orc camp one" another player comes along and starts pulling orcs from this camp. They are not breaking the official rules, however, they are breaking the unwritten player made rules.

The results of each sort of behavior is different. Break Sony's rules and you can be suspended or banned. Break the players rules and you can find difficulty finding groups and also find yourself on no rez no port lists.

It's not a perfect system, but it does happen. My personal system is to use common courtesy as much as possible and just ignore the worst behaved players.

One thing to remember about EQ is that what sets it apart from playing a single-player RPG on your computer is that it's a social game.

It does, in fact, matter what other players think of you if you want be as good at the game as you can.

Your character's reputation with other players is more valuable than any item in the game -- because it is that reputation that will get you on the raids where the really good stuff is found.

Make a lot of enemies, and you can expect to either quit in frustration over finding you can't solo your way to the really good stuff, or pay $50 to move your character to another server where you haven't trashed your reputation -- without a single piece of the equipment you trashed your rep for.

Some rules may be strictly player-enforced, but it is wise to respect them.

Reputation > all.
