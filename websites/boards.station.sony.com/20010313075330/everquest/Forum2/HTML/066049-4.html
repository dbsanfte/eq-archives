If you really want to quibble at specifics I can make it easier for you. You want a better metaphor? Let's say you are a master gambler.

You've been playing in a Casino for about 10 hours. You arrived with $600, you win some and you lost some, but the pot for each has been steadily increasing and so has the ante. The pot is now $145,000.

You're playing blackjack. Your faceup card is a king, the facedown is an Ace. This is almost a surefire win. The people you play with (for all you know, they might be Verant) are not very good at Cards, you've been able to scam them several times. Suddenly the dealer tells you an Ace is now worth 15 points, new house rule. There goes that chance at the pot.

You have $20,000 left. The pot is now $150,000. You figure, what the heck, I'm very good at blackjack and these guys aren't, I can pick up some more cash, just have to remember the new Ace rule. The Ante is about $1,000 a game now.

Your next hand nets 14 points, you call for a draw, it's a 7 (which is 21, or Blackjack, a great hand) so you just made more money... except the dealer changes the rules again, and you lose. The Ante keeps increasing. At times you get lucky, and win even with the changing rules, but usually you lose more than you put in. You are good at blackjack, you have no idea what the house is playing, but you figure since you are a good blackjack player and everyone here sucks, you SHOULD be able to make an extremely profitable night.

It goes on and on and you get more frustrated. Sure, you've earned a lot of money, but no where near what you could have. A few strings of losses in a row and you're ready to call it quits, even with a half a million on the line.

That a better situation? Replace money with emotional satisfaction. We are extremely happy when we do well. We are insanely happy when we do exceptionally well.
