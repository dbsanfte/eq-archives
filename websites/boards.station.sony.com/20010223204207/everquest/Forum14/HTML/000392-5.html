Ok, I've played a fair number of EQ classes over the past year and am currently making my way through the levels with a cleric. I enjoy much of a good cleric's duties.

However, in all fairness, I was very surprised and a bit disturbed by the recent patch message. It seems that Verant left all the problems with the cleric class unaddressed. This might be understandable if Verant honestly believed clerics did not need some remedy. However, as Absor's original post that started this thread clearly shows, Verant has already recognized some of the key problems with the cleric class. Yet, they have left all of these problems untouched.

Now, as to specific issues. The #1, big kahuna, monster problem with clerics has to be mana regen. Clerics desperately need some form of mana regen. Of course, it should NOT stack with clarity or breeze, but have a similar effect. It could either be a spell (Divine Faith) that opens a clearer path between cleric and deity or a skill such as an advanced meditate (Commune) that allowed a double-rate mana regen when sitting and sticking one's nose into that infernal spellbook.

I honestly don't mind staring into that book and monitoring group HP bars during battle. What I do mind is that, usually being the only cleric in a big group, I am ALWAYS out of mana, even with very high wis and wis and mana bonuses. I almost never spend mana buffing and heaven forbid I use one of the dd or stun line spells in combat. ALL of my mana MUST be strictly preserved for healing. Faster mana regen gets me more into the game and the action. I will be more able to use a variety of my spells, because I won't be at less than full mana with every pull.

Look, clerics are the big healers. We all get that. Just like Wizards are the big nukers. No one tops a Wiz at nuking. However, while a Wiz gets a mana regen capability to keep them nuking and allow them to occassionally buff or take on other casting activities, clerics are stuck with zilch in the mana regen category. From a game-play and balance perspective some mana regen capability for Clerics is absolutely essential. It is the number one reason I get frustrated in-game.

From a roleplay perspective, it is easy to think of clerics gaining a greater connection to their deity through a skill or spell. I always imagined that a Cleric's meditate was like prayer to her/his god. Step that up a notch to communing with her/his god. It only makes sense. Drop it into play at the mid- to high teens, but definitely no later than level 20. Meditate times start to be ugly from there up.

It can be self-only, so we don't become mana crack dealers like some other classes and don't affect the clarity/breeze economy.

Standing meditate? Who cares? Really. That is a waste of programming time. Help clerics with mana regen. Before you do ANYTHING ELSE! HELP CLERICS WITH MANA REGEN!

A cleric can live just fine without invisibility, with low combat skills (why would I want to melee?), and without many of the other items listed on the talking points. However, a cleric cannot live (nor properly do her/his job) without MANA! Given that the other healing classes have a mana regen capacity and, after this patch, have improved heals, why, oh why, can't clerics be given some mana regen boost? Isn't that now an essential change in order to restore/preserve balance?

By the way, I like the previously posted idea of boosting the healing bonus for clerics from 15 to 20 percent. That would definitely help make clerics more uniquely valuable, especially at levels below C-HEAL. However, and once again, this must be considered secondary to a mana regen boost.

Seeking mana from Verant,

Pat
