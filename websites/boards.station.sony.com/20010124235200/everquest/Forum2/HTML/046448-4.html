Well folks,

First to the bard issue, TO clarify, it is only affecting Movement songs augmentation due to drums, NOT all drum songs.

Having said that I do agree that it is a big issue and deserves fixing ASAP.

On the snare/root issue, Last night I teamed up with a L40 ranger, (L39 ranger myself) and we took an even con Ry'Gorr legionaire. Under normal cercumstances this would be either a long kite, or a quick deah if share didnt hit, Lots of running around, and a good chance of agroing surronding mobs.

The difference,

Once the mob was snared and rooted I would shoot the crap out of it, maintaining Root and Stinging Swarm, Whialse my partner hit it with Flame Lick (lowers ac by a little) maintained snare and dropped in the DD's

Little or no running around, much quicker death for the mob, and the situation under controll.

Some people have noted that snare and root drop with a DD, well this did happen a few times, however not every time, sometimes only snare would drop and other times only root It looks like the %chance for a DD to break root has been passed on to snare.

What is the effect of this?

Well in a well controlled group not realy that much. both druids and rangers can snare/root so as long as you qagree beforehand who holds wich responsibility, Usually Ranger to snare Druid to root, all should be kewl.

Whare it would have an effect is with the Snare/Fear tactic. you see when using this tactic you are more often than not in a group with a number of casters, and usualy only yourself as the tank, sometimes a Full tank class also. This means that if a caster drops a 500point DD on the mob, the mob resists the DD and breaks snare, the caster may well be toast.

Also consider that rangers of L39 and above now have Call of Sky which gives there weapons a 35 point DD now if this proc gives snare/root a chance to break they will be asked not to use it, especialy in large encounters.

Finally Druid SOLO kiting is made harder with this, consider, Snare, Root, DOT, Nook, Mob breaks snare and root, Druid backs up, Fizzle, resist, snared, Root, DOT, nook, Rince and repeat.

The safest way to deal with this would be to remove the "break" chance from snare, that way the unanounced part of the change is removed.

On the whole I like this change, snare is a bit more variable now but the additional safety of being able to root and know the snare will probably still be there is good.

Best regards

Darg
