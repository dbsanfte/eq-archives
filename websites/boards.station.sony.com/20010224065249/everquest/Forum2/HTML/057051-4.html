A thought or two...if you're going to make necros into a support class, give us some powerful support spells.

For example, some have suggested we get boon of the garou, or a targetable version of vampiric embrace. A variation on this:

Spell: Negative Energy
- mana cost: 400 (tenative figure only -- obviously we can only estimate what the overall effect, and thus the necessary mana cost, might be in a variety of situations)
- duration: 3 minutes
- causes two effects on the targeted party member:
- Lose 25 hp per tick
- Proc a 150 point disease-based lifetap off the primary hand. Proc rate would result in about 4 per minute given a delay of 20, FBSS haste, and a dexterity of 100.

- does not stack with boon of the garou (for obvious reasons)
- Possibly make the spell castable on players only to eliminate soloing concerns

The total potential this spell would have at the given proc rate would be to tap 1800 hps over 3 minutes and lose 750, net effect +1050 hps on the tank. However, in a normal group, not all battles will be 3 minutes long; many may be 1-2 minutes. Consequently, the total tap may very well be around 900 hps, which helps the tank very little as he still loses 750 hps to the spell.

Of course, with haste and dex buffs, the spell could become very powerful. This requires, however, that you group with said classes in addition to the tank, and healer, etc. Synergies are good in what is primarily a grouping game.

The key effectiveness of this spell would come in raids, where shaman and enchanters are almost guaranteed to be present. Additionally, ubermob fights are also likely to last a decent length of time (especially those in velious).

Recovery:
Pump mana and summon corpses. Repeat ad infinitum.

Prep: DMF x 50
Clearing yard trash: no impact
Main encounter: no impact
Recovery: mind numbing boring
