I share the oppinion of most who have posted here about the unnecessary difficulty of the Faydedar spawn.

The druid epic in general is designed around good fundementals of the druid class (ie ability to travel, ability to track, ability to forage), which is the way all of these class defining epics should be. But the way that these fundementals were used in the epic has me a bit confused.

First I would like to say that this druid epic quest can be completed relatively fast with an unreal amount of luck, I personally completed the 'cleansed spirit of antonica' portion of the quest within 4 hours, and that includes traveling time, I was just lucky enough that all three corrupted were up when I got there or it only took a bit of time before they did come up. This is how I started my druid epic (on a *this might be fun* type of note), but after getting the elaborate scim and the corrupted seafury cyclops and camping the corrupted Baracuda for plenty upon plenty of hours (still no corrupted) my attitude and 'fun' factor of the quest has gone downhill.

The only parts of the quest that I am looking forward to right now is the slaying of Ulump and the dungeon run to kill the Corrupted Seahorse, just because Ulump is (from what I hear) on a 3 day spawn timer, so he should be up when I need to kill him, and the seahorse is supposedly not a long camp, but getting to him is a challenge which should be fun. The rest of the quest that I have left to do will be long solo camps.

Verant has tried and tried (and suceeded in most cases) to make EQ a group game, where people HAVE to be in a group to do stuff. To achieve this they have molded the classes to make them dependant on one another, and in the case where one class can solo, they have made the ability to solo scarce and hard (through *nerfing*). One would conclude that this means that Verant is against solo'ing and pro grouping. Well, the druid epic seems to be designed to make the druids trying to complete it (50+) to SOLO. Most of the mobs that have to be killed in this epic quest are soloable by 50+ druids that have any inclination in how to play their class. This also goes against what Verant has been trying to do for the duration of EQ's life.

The foraging aspect of the quest is also badly implimented because for one it is a large unproductive time sink because a druid does NOT spend enough time in a zone they dont hunt in to forage these rare items. Verant know's that a druid has teleports to almost every corner of Norath and also SOW to make them run faster, so why oh why would a druid stay in misty thicket, Greater Faydark, Everfrost, or Innothule Swamp long enough to forage these items??? The longest I spend in any of these zones is the amount of time it takes me to run (w/SOW) from the druid gate to Rivervale for example, or from the South Ro zoneline to Guk zone. Very little time when, from my personal experience, you have to spend over an hour in each zone to forage these items, less if you are very lucky and more if you are not lucky. It comes down to Unproductive and therefore UN-FUN time spent in a game that is supposed to be a GAME (game = FUN, at least normally).

So as I agree with using the druid's class defining skills alot in the druid epic quest, I do not agree with the way most of these skills have been used in this quest setup.

Now to address the problem with Faydedar...well, I'm not gonna try too hard because most of the people reading this thread would have already read what has to be said. But I would just like to say that Epic quests WILL be completed, there is no getting around that, and they also will be completed in short amounts of time if the person performing them has the proper resources to do so (i.e. member of a large organized guild, or lots of money and friends). Verant, there is no getting around the fact that NPC's will be killed and they will be killed fast if enough people want them killed. So when we come to the epic quests, you have high level mobs that have to be killed and in some cases several of these high level mobs. So this is acceptable and expected considering the value of the item that is rewarded in these epic quests. But making these High Level mobs UNAVAILABLE to the people who have to kill them, the people who only have that mob left to kill, to complete their epic quest is not wise in the design of the quest.

Putting all the problems with the druid class, too many, too powerful, too soloable, too whatever...

To make one classes epic weapon unatainable to the majority because of the rarity of this ONE spawn is like taking some of the fun out of this game.

This quest so far has been reasonably fun (to myself) because I for one dont have a hefty schedual and I was able to read while I foraged/camped the corrupted. So I was entertained by other things while my character was preparing for the FUN things to do. I see my needed slaying of Faydedar ont he horizon and I feel like giving up on this quest. If your intention was to make the druid epic one of the hardest to get, you suceeded, but not because the mobs are hard to get to or hard to kill, but because they are just...not there...


Bottom line is, in the creation of this druid epic quest, Verant has created situations where players of Everquest have to CAMP certain spawns for unnecessarily high amount of hours, SOLO mobs that give absolutley no productive reward like experience or loot besides the quest peice (these are solo'd because they absolutely dont need a group to take out if the druid is 50+), and spend hours upon hours of UNPRODUCTIVE and UN-FUN time in a *game* foraging a rare item.

These situations -> CAMP, SOLO, UNPRODUCTIVE & UN-FUN seem to me to be situations that Verant has been trying to avoid and eliminate in the game of Everquest for the past two and a half years. Now I dont claim to be an expert, but is this not contradicting or hypocratic?!?!?
