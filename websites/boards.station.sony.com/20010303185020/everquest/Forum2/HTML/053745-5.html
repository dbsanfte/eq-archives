1 small point: The big problem is that bards seem to be designed to enhance parties over being individually useful. However, the conflicts with spells turn grouping into an exercise in frustration as you try to determine which two or three songs won't overwrite a better spell, or keep a spell from casting effectively, or even have ANY decent effect on the proceedings.

If necessary, make the stacking component less than the unstacked version... so that a 10% haste song would add 5% or even 3% to a haste-buffed character. Enough to make it worthwhile and the bard an obvious asset, rather than a distant choice for filling party positions.

Hastes are not the only songs where this is an issue, however. Chains seems to overwrite snare, letting a creature free after as few as a second or two, even if it has been snared previously (in this case, being used by a bard who wants to use the slow component in a non-AE song). Besides Selo's I can't think of a single effect we do that is nearly as powerful as the caster version of that effect, so either we stack them, or become the partial-party class... since we can perform adequate (not great, just adequate) fill-in for a misformed party.

I have read or skimmed most of the messages up to this point, and because many of them seem to be focused on the high levels (50-60) I wanted to make sure that the lower levels get covered too. So here are my comments on the level 1-45 life of the bard. I play a level 46 bard with over 85 days logged. (No comments on efficiency please, I play for fun not power leveling.)

BARDS ROCK. This class is really fun to play. The fact that a bard gets a new song every level, while the power of older songs increases, really makes playing a bard a lot of fun. I have played other classes, and no other class has been as fun to play as my bard. In addition, I have found it very easy to find a group as a bard, as all melee types want haste, and all casters want the mana song.

So if "fun factor" of a class can be considered as part of balance, as I feel it should, then the bard (up to level 46) is excellent. So please keep that in mind as you read these comments. Up to level 46, my bard has been nothing but fun to play.

I do have a few comments on various songs from level 1-46, which may help in balancing and fine-tuning the class:

Level 6 : HYMN OF RESTORATION : Great song! But once you get in a group with a druid or shaman, it can be a pain to use. It *does* stack with other regen spells, but they will not take hold if you are singing this effect. I always have to educate druids and shamans about this, and it would really be great if I didn't. Not really a BALANCE issue but certainly one that affects me a lot.

Level 15 : KELIN'S LUCID LULLABY : Area Effect Enthrall. I honestly cannot figure out how to use this song. It seems that this may be one of the few bard songs only useful for a very short period of a bards life, say from level 15 to 25 or so. This seems inconsistent with most other bard songs, that either get an upgrade or continue to be useful. I have tried singing this song full time while using weapons, singing this song full time with a lute, twisting this song with a lute and the healing song, and in my personal experience none of this works. Monsters either resist the song outright, or "throw" the song off immediately after it takes effect. In practice, my group does much better if I use other combinations of songs and weapons, and just forget about this song entirely. As a suggestion, perhaps make it require a string instrument (can currently be sung), lasting only one pulse so it must be constantly refreshed, affect a maximum of 4 creatures (like rain spells), and decrease it's resistance. This would allow a bard to use this song for small crowd control, at the expense of his other benefits to the group (since he could not melee and would have to sing this full time.) Enchanters may feel that this infringes on their ability, but I would point out that a group with an AE Mez enchanter is much better off in this situation, because the Enchanter can provide other buffs (haste, etc) for the party during the battle and still Mez, whereas the bard could not.

Level 16 : TAREW's AQUATIC AYRE : Party Water Breathing. In practice this song is only useful as a backup if someone's water breathing spell wears off, simply because of it's low range. Even auto-follow does not keep people within adequate range to receive the benefits of the song, which means as soon as you start moving several members of your party start to drown. Increasing the range of this song would make it more useful, particularly in "quick water transit" situations.

Level 21 : MELANIE's MELLIFLUOUS MOTION : Party Random distance teleport. (ie- Yonder) I admit, I cannot find a good use for it in combat situations. The several times I used it in an emergency, it only served to scatter my group to the five winds and make a bad situation even worse. But this is a WONDERFUL song for getting people unstuck. I am frequently receiving requests from people to come help them get unlodged from behind an oven or a small hut. Whereas normally they would have to wait many hours for assistance from a guide or GM, this song allows players to receive more immediate help from their local bard. Please consider this before making any changes to this song. (I've read a few).

Level 22 : ALENIA's DISENCHANTING MELODY : Party Strip Enchantment. I have had nothing but bad experiences when using this song. I believe its design was to strip negative magical effects that have been cast on your party, at the expense of also stripping buffs. So in a situation where someone had been rooted, or has heat blood (DoT), the bard could save the day by whipping out this song. Unfortunately, I have never been able to use it for this purpose. In the time it takes to communicate that someone needs the song, get out my lute and beginning singing, strip off all BENEFICIAL buffs first, and then FINALLY strip off their negative buffs, that person is usually dead, or no longer needs to be stripped. In fact, I may have actually killed them by stripping off their valor, or their symbol spell. This is a really bad way to make friends. My only suggestion here is to have this song strip in reverse order, so that the last buff/debuff to be cast on a player is the first one to get stripped. Or perhaps have this song strip ALL BUFFS AT ONCE from everyone in the party. This might still go very bad, but at least I could get to the source of the problem quicker.

Level 24 : LYSSA's VERACIOUS CONCORD : Party See Invisibility. Originally this song was a bard's only way of seeing invisible. However, a while back the Level 19 bard invisibility song was changed to also give the see invisibility component. Since that addition, I have had no reason to memorize this song. Perhaps giving it an additional minor effect (glimpse?) would give this song some sort of use.

Level 27 : SOLON's SONG OF THE SIRENS : Single Target Charm. This song feels a bit overpowered, at least up to level 38 or so. The reason I say a "bit" is that I have used this song to good effect in party situations, where it was basically a damage control song. In those cases, I do have to suffer the few seconds of taking abuse from two or more monsters as I try to get off a re-charm. It is the solo situation where this song really borders on being overpowered. This won't be a popular opinion. But here's why - A level 35 bard can solo two hill giants (not 1, not 3) using this song. It definitely takes practice, and has a definite risk associated with it, but it is very doable. I am not aware of any other class that can do this at this level range. Note that the later charm, which uses mana, deals with this fairly well. You can get off about 30 "pulses" of charm and then you are DONE singing charm for the next hour and a half. Perhaps adding a mana requirement (5 mana?) to this 27 charm would deal with the problem in a similar fashion. (I can hear the screams now.) However, I do feel I should reiterate that the bard LOSES the power of this song at about level 38, where monsters become too high level to be affected by this charm. In the interest of balance, you should note that this is about the same level where some other classes GAIN the ability to solo two or more creatures (Quad kite, etc.) Just wanted to throw that in for reference.

Level 31 : AGILMENTE's ARIA of EAGLES : Party Levitation. Great song, but limited in usefulness by its range. When a party follows you on bard speed, they go just outside the reach of this song. This means we usually just get someone to cast levitation (spell) on the party instead of using the song. Increasing the range to that of bard speed would let a skilled bard move his party across rocky landscapes very quickly, by twisting levitation and speed. This seems like it would be a good thing.

Level 34 : CANTANA OF SOOTHING : Party Mana/Health/Stamina regen. I am confused about this song. It requires a stringed instrument, but does not stack with hymn of regeneration. So if you need healing, you are better off singing Hymn (healing) that has a much greater effect.
