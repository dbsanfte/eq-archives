Most of the items on the final list have very little to do with the balance of the class post 50, where most of the conern is. Obviously, the healing changes on test are important, however, I don't think it may be enough in a grouping or raiding environment. Additional solo power is not needed, so suggestions about Sow II really aren't necessary to balance the class. The following are a few items that would help out the class in a group or raid:

1) Stacking of the Skin line of spells with the Cleric Hero line. The Shaman Talisman spell stacks and so should ours. With the inflation of mob hps and difficulty in Kunark and SoV I really don't see this as unbalacing to the PvE portion of the game and also helps out with Druid desirablity on raids as an additional buffer. As it stands now, a ranger can outbuff (Call of Earth and Strength of Nature) a druid on a raid as clerics are always on raids and overwrite most of our buffs. If it is viewed as being unbalanced for lower level PvE then an alterantive suggestion would be to allow them to stack beginning with the lvl 49 spell Skin Like Nature and above.

2) I accept my back up healing status in a group, clerics reign supreme here as they should. Appropriately, it takes a shaman or druid more mana to heal the tank than a cleric. This is fine. The one problem I see here is mana regeneration, which is the absolute key to dungeon crawling. (mana regen dictates the pace at which a group can kill) In addition to clarity/clarity 2 buff, a shaman can increase his mana thru Cannabolize to help offset the additional mana he neads to act as a back up healer ( a great line of spells which should not be given to anyone else), while inside a druid has no such assistance. Suggestion is to allow the post 50 druid wolf forms to be cast indoors. Those wolfforms have a small mana regen component that stacks with clarity. The problem with this is the spell then effectively becomes an indoor castable self only sow. I am not sure what solutions to this are appropriate or even possible, so will leave it up to additional comments from both a balancing perspective and a coding one. (i.e. is that an unbalacing factor and if yes, is it possible to code to remove the sow effect when cast indoors)

3) Prior to 53, as a high level druid, I pretty much had four spell slots permanently memmed with lvl 34 or below spells. (ensnare, greater healing, fury of the sky (a great addition), and spirit of the Wolf) In a dungeon take out sow for an evac and the principle remains the same. All of these are great spells, but it just seems odd that even post 50 we so heavily rely on lower level spells. This point is more of an observation as I have not in detail reveiwed the spell lists for other casters to see if it is the same for them, but it just seems odd to me. This situation changes when we get to 53 (soon 51) with superior heal, but perhaps an upgrade to Fury of the Sky is also in order. My suggestion would be to have a longer stun effect, but there may be several ways to upgrade the spell.

Well that is all that I can think of, and perhaps my suggestions are way off based, as always your mileage may vary.
