Kheldan,

I'm not sure I understand your point. The economics in Ultima Online were completely different (and far more dynamic and interesting) than everquest economics. In Ultima Online, you didn't have "uber" items. Armor and weaponry was disposable, and because of how easy it was to die from a pk gank people did not go out with the few uber items they had. The only time I'd bring out a bow of vanquishing was during the 30 minutes before the servers were taken down. Ultima Online economics revolved around money and land. That's why the game was remarkable. You could own houses of various types, sell anything and everything on vendors. Hold events in your home, decorate, and train etc...

And that's how it should be. You could play the game how you wanted to play it. What can you do but camp spawns hours on end in everquest? Trade skills? Compare the usefulness of alchemy in everquest and in Ultima Online. No comparison.

Nerfing was a big problem in UO too. They made PK'ing way too hard, increased the penalties for killing way too much. Problems with bugs plagued the game for a long time, house break-ins etc... But the principle of not making the game driven by items paid off by allowing the player to BREATHE once in a while and take a break from farming spawns.
