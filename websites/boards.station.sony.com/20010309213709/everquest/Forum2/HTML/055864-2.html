Overview of the Necromancer: How can we be improved?

Disclaimer: I do have a 51st Level Dark Elven Necro, so if I comment on spells/concerns that pertain to 52-60th level necromancers, I am using information drawn from both personal feelings and from information gleaned from www.eq.stratics.com as well as other sources. Flames will be ignored.

Objectives: to discuss Necromancer’s weaknesses and overall concerns.

A few weeks ago I had a chance to go to the Plane of Fear for the first time, and I had a blast. We cleared the plane and then went after Cazic himself. A fellow necromancer volunteered to take the first Death Touch. CT screamed out “Zarc!” and with that, my friend was dead. Trembling the way that only a Plane n00b can tremble, I sent my pet in as my fellow guildmates charged in for the kill. I stood back and waited until CT was down to about 75% health before I used my 49th level Drain Soul on him. After having waited about a minute, I tapped him again knowing full well that Chain-Tapping a god was a good way to get killed. When the fight was over, I had only cast twice, the CT lay dead, and we rejoiced.

My next question would be, “What’s the matter with this?” I was able to cast on a god, and I felt useful to both my party as well as to the raid as a whole. I don’t feel that my ability to cast on a god was overpowered, and I had fun.

Why is it that Verant feels the need to downgrade my ability to contribute to a raid? Even if all of our lifetaps were 100% resistible (within the 6 level limit), this still does not give us an unfair advantage. If you chain-cast any spell on a mob, you run the risk of having that mob turning its attention on you: chain-casting on a mob = angry mob = soon to be dead caster. Period.
I understand that Verant does not want a group of 25 n00bie necros to chain-tap a Sand Giant to death, and I realize that doing so would violate the risk vs. reward idea. Tenth level necros should not be able to gang up on deep red-con giants in Oasis and reap incredible experience/coinage with complete impunity. /agree

Why does Verant so fear a 57th necromancer’s ability to cast Vexing Mordinia upon an Epic Monster in Kunark/SoV? So what if a necromancer can lifetap a mob. Verant is perfectly willing to allow that same necro to take part in a raid on Cazic Thule. That same Necromancer’s spell will stick to one of the Old World gods, why not all of the other mobs? The level 57th necromancer has to play by the same rules as any other caster: chain-cast and run the risk of being killed. Is Verant worried that any given 50+ necromancer will have an unfair advantage if he can affect one of the new 100,000 hit points mobs? I have serious doubts that any given level 57th necromancer will have enough mana to chain-cast his
459 mana/1000 hp leech/tap often enough to take down an Epic Mob, let alone any mob. And even if the caster had enough mana to chain-cast his tap often enough to place a serious dent in one of the new supercharged epic mobs’ health, he will eventually earn the wrath of said mob: again, chain-casting on a mob = angry mob = soon to be dead caster.
