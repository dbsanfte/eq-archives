(Small) Eureka:

I like doing "in case of emergency" damage and then don't mind sitting out, because I am very broad with my definition of "emergency" (it's anything that will interrupt a group's pulling flow, not just anything that will kill the group). Some wizards think they should be doing "routine" damage: moderate damage to all mobs, but being able to sustain such casting for long periods of time (hence the requests for "self-only clarity" effects of some sort).

My proposal--two new types of blast, to be added to whatever spell rank wizards start seriously lagging in both emergency and routine damage (early post-50 levels is my intuition):

Type I--"Sprinting"
Damage: Huge. Enough to put us back in our glory days of doing around a third of a standard xp mob's hit points.
Cast Time: Fast.
Refresh Time: Minimal.
Mana Efficiency: The downside of the spell--not impressive.

This is the spell type which allows us to blow the holy hell out of mobs in an emergency, despite the ramping up of hit points. (I'm not talking about +32k-hp ubermobs, but rather typical xp-group fighting.) We sacrifice mana efficiency for insanely fast and lethal damage. I leave the precise definition of "insanely fast and lethal" to game designers.

Type II--"Long-Distance Running"
Damage: Middle of the road.
Cast Time: Decent.
Mana Efficiency: The good part--Simply amazing. these would be the most mana-efficient non-AE spells wizards would get. Close to, perhaps on par with or even better than, DoT spells.
Refresh Time: The downside--I envision these spells as taking at least 15 seconds to refresh, possibly longer. The idea being we can cast only one, or maybe two, per pull.

This is the spell type which allows us to continuously deal moderate, efficient damage in a standard xp'ing group, without sniveling about how low we are on mana. The long refresh time exists to ensure that we can continuously contribute a group which pulls a stream of low blues, but can't chain-cast the spell to take maximum advantage of the efficiency.

My thought is that these two spells allow for both an improvement to our current "kill this now" ability, and an improvement to our endurance, without radically changing any game mechanics, without making Harvest a "lay hands" ability, and without really affecting the spells we would want to cast soloing.

I'm also pretty sure most players will hate this idea, so I think I might be on to something

Thank you for letting everyone know that you are reading, or at least skimming, the posts that are being made, Absor. It is nice to know that our opinions are being listened to. I am sure we would all like for you to post more frequently so that we can “discuss” things more, but it is understandable that your time is very, very limited at the moment.

Okay, on to the subject matter.

Wizards, as has been said numerous times already, are a very specialised class. With this high level of specialisation, we expect to be vastly superior to other classes at what we do. However, we are not. Well, not to a great extent. We are on a rough parity with Druids in regards to teleporting, they have more ports and some great locations, but we get some really nice ones as well. Northern Ro for example. So I have no problem with ports, apart from those with high risk involved, but that has been covered earlier. Our other main ability, DDs, is where we should truly stand separate from every other class. Yet we don’t. Our spells are better. For the most part doing a little bit more damage with faster cast times and reduced mana costs, but the advantage is not enough. Considering everything that has been sacrificed by the wizard class to “specialise” in DD spells, we are not good enough to justify it. Several things have been suggested about how to remedy this. From increased damage to reduced mana cost and increased mana regeneration. Personally, I would prefer the reduced mana cost or, even better, improved mana regeneration. Increased damage would be nice, but it would not stop you going out of mana very quickly and getting back into the fight quicker. The problem with increased damage and the reduced mana cost is that both would make wizards much better at soloing. This might not be a very good thing, but it could be a very bad one. Of the three ideas, increased mana regeneration is the best. It would allow you to solo faster, granted, but it would not increase your ability to solo tougher mobs very much. It would however allow you to contribute more damage during combat and be ready to fight again within a reasonable amount of time.

One idea that has been mentioned that I like a lot was the one about melee resistant targets. This is a great idea. It also makes perfect sense. How is it possible for people to fight with physical weapons creatures and beings that are not truly there? Think about it. With creatures like ghosts and spirits, how can a warrior swinging their sword or a monk kicking it or a rogue backstabbing it do any damage to it? They shouldn’t be able to. It is a creature of spirit, it doesn’t have a corporeal body for them to damage, so they should not be able to. In other words, to fight these creatures, you would need spell casting classes. It would be the only way to fight them. However, like wizards have lures to cast on “uncastable” mobs, there would be classes and specific weapons that would be able to fight the non-corporeal entities. The classes I have in mind are paladins and shadow knights. Both have training in the “supernatural” that would give them the knowledge required to fight them. However, they would also need specialised weapons to harm the creatures. Something like a “Ghost Sword.” This sword would appear as just a hilt and would not be able to hit normal targets, because for them, the sword does not exist. However, against non-corporeal targets, the sword would work, and extremely well at that.

Another point that is really strange. Casting mobs. These mobs are spell casters and yet they can melee as well as a fighter, have thousands of hit points and can also cast spells as well. The also, I might add, are usually able to summon opponents to them. Why? Why do casting mobs have high hit points? Especially “pure” casters. They shouldn’t. They should be a lot weaker physically than their melee counterparts. To have them otherwise doesn’t make much sense. Now we come to the casting mobs and their ability to summon opponents. Again, why? Why would any spell caster want an opponent closer to them? Surely it would make more sense to have an inverse-summon opponent for casting mobs? That way they could send melees away from them. I realise that this idea probably wont be liked very much, but it would make much more sense. It would also allow for a reduction in the hit points of caster mobs since they could effectively avoid melee combat for the most part.

On the issue of soloing, wizards have some severe disadvantages to compensate for the high amount of damage that we can do. One. You need some form of SoW. From either another player, a potion, or else journeyman boots. Without a form of SoW, soloing is much more difficult, if not impossible. At least I think so. Two. You are very, very, vulnerable to attack between fights. If something comes across you just after a fight and you are low on mana and meditating, there is a good chance you will die. You have no pet to buy you time to med for a little longer or to make a run for it. So med time for a wizard is very dangerous and due to the nature of our class, downtime is most of what our time soloing is spent on. One solution, to help against wandering mobs, would be to cast invisibility on yourself. However, this doesn’t always help. With the way zones are now, you are likely to meet both living and undead opponents and you cannot hide from both. There is also the fact that a lot of monsters can see through invisibility with no difficulty. So yes, wizards can solo, but it is not as easy as some people think.

One idea that I have been toying with that could help spell casting classes is the ability to split an opponent. What I mean by this is, is that some mobs have high melee and magical abilities rolled into one. My idea is that through a spell, a wizard could strip the “magic” from a monster. This would creature two mobs. One which was “pure” melee and had 90% of the mobs original hit points and the other which would be a “pure” caster and would have the other 10% of the hit points. This would allow raids to work more effectively since you could have your spell casters quickly destroy the “magic” of the opponent, this reducing it capacity to harm your people. What would be left would be an extremely tough physical opponent that could then be dealt with. Of course, there would be some mobs that you would want to split in such a manner, but for targets up to 100k hit point, it could be very useful.

One issue that is mentioned several times during this talk is our lack of diversity. For this I have some more ideas for spells, apart from those I have already mentioned earlier.

Mirror image.
-Level 4. This spell would create a duplicate (not shown) of the wizard. The number of “images” created would be one per every two levels. Any attack that would succeed in harming the wizard would cause an “image” to vanish. If the wizard is hit by an area effect spell, all “images” are destroyed.

Web.
-Level 39. This spell would stick a target to the ground, rendering them unable to cast or attack. The target would be able to try and escape from the web once every twenty seconds or else once for every successful attack.

Barring The Gift.
-Level 52. This spell would stop the target from casting spells or using magical abilities for the duration of the spell.

Prismatic Sphere.
-Level 51. This spell creates a magical shield that greatly increases magic, fire and cold resistances.

Prismatic Blast.
-Level 55. This spell throws a blast of energy formed of the three different types of DDs and the wizard’s disposal, magic, fire and cold. Each of the three types would be able to do a maximum of 500 damage.

Prismatic Storm.
-Level 60. This creates a storm which is made out of the three elements at a wizard’s disposal, magic, fire and cold. Each element has the potential to do 800 damage to mobs in its area of effect.

I do have other ideas, but this post is long enough as it is, so I will end it here.

Okay... I've thought about this for quite some time now, and I do have to admit that I think the class is in need of a general boost in power at levels beyond 20, or 30 at the very least.

It's been said many times that we are the one class that really, *really* needs a group to survive. To that end our primary role in a group is to zap a monster *fast*. With the exception of a few rather incredibly common spells like invisibility and levitation, and evac spells (which druids almost seem to get a better deal on, but I think it's an acceptable trade-off considering we get planar travel, even if it's a mixed blessing), we don't have *any* other means of contributing to a group. We are quite and thoroughly bound by our ability to turn manna into damage.

Taking a slightly different approach from the rest of the people here, I'd been trying to figure out why the wizard class exists, _at all_, rather than what it's "defining" role is. It's what I feel is a more apropos approach to the issue--analyzing the need for a class from a design standpoint. I *can* agree that having a class which can deal damage significantly faster than the rest is a unique enough concept to warrant the existance of the class, BUT... with the wizard class, gradually and over time but only *mildly* due to changes to the other classes, the end result is that the concept simply hasn't been properly followed through with completeness.

The close proximity in spell damage output that the other pure caster classes (and at some levels where they exceed those of the wizard) makes the existance of their pets *overshoot* balance, and creates a situation where the wizard simply winds up being notably less effective than the other pure caster classes. It didn't become immediately obvious until the Velious uber-mobs entered the picture, but it is the *same* problem that plagues the class at nearly all levels above 20.

Without pets we are *quite* dependent on groups, which is fine considering it's been stated the game is meant for people to play in groups. However, it's *not* fine that our overall effectiveness makes us the *least* useful to groups. Druids, shamen, bards, paladins, necros, shadowknights, and even clerics, can in any given fight, perform several *interchangeable* roles because they have a lot of the same abilities, although in differing amounts, so they can be easily fit into a group. Pet classes can add quite a bit of value simply because they effectively bring an extra *tank* with them, even if it is a somewhat weak one compared to the warrior class. Two group members for the price of one. Wizards simply can *not* compete for a spot in a group against that with the spell damage being *sooooo* similar, and having almost no other role we can perform in a given scenario *but* blasting. All of the wizard's existance is focused down to one thing, and that's nuking, and while we do it faster, we should be doing it a lot *better* than we currently are.

Absor, I've seen you say very nearly that much yourself, and while it may pain you guys greatly to do it, you simply must _accept_ that it's the only way to "fix" the class. We *have* to have groups, but simply don't have what it takes to be useful enough to others to get into groups, so long as there is literally anyone else left in the zone.

Now it sounds good and logical in _theory_ that the wizard class is one that should be nearly as necessary as a cleric, in that our ability to do damage quickly lets us take an active role in determining the overall outcome of the fight. Playing my wizard, if an additional mob shows up, I am ready and quite able to get it to go where I want it, and make it STAY there with Enstill and a DD or two. With the exception of certain types of mobs, I'm able to effect a change to the fight that makes a serious difference to the outcome. We're *better* at it than the other classes, but not by enough to make the other classes interested. Fear gives the same effect, with less control. Mezmerizing mobs gives a nearly identical effect, and enchanters are outright better at it. Mages can use pets that can *root* at a relatively early level. Neither of these classes can do it as quickly, but yet again, it's not much less quickly at _all_. Their other benefits very strongly offset the minor increase in time used. We can Evac, but so can druids. Druids can *also* employ direct damage, DoT, and healing magic. (I know this seems to be a reiteration of the last paragraph, but stick with me, there's a point to it.) In *theory* we're useful, and in very specific, pre-planned, and ideal circumstances we can *be* quite essential to a group, as "The Insurance Policy" because we have the greatest (by a very, *very* slim margin) ability to make the *key* changes to a fight scenario to keep a bad skirmish from becoming an absymal rout. Ours is not the straightforward role of a Big Gun, with magic substituting for swordwork, but that of a player of chess, spotting the key moments where very particular things need to happen (...mob #4 has to go away NOW! ...party is *clearly* doomed, Evacuate! ...drop the bomb and *voila* the monster is running for the hills!), and seeing them through. However, this is not theory, it's an actual game in practice, and players of the game simply do not believe in "Insurance". Not only that, but probably >95% of the fights in EverQuest are *not* carefully pre-planned, made up of groups of people who have been specially selected for the fight, or are by any measure ideal conditions. In _theory_ we work, but sadly, *not* in practice, and *that* is why serious changes need to be made to the class.
Our maximum damage output needs to be increased across the board, even if it means getting monsters seriously pissed off at us even more often than they are now, because after all, _every_ class has the ability to keep us in one piece when we overburn (they just gripe about it sometimes). We need to be able to do more damage across the board from 20 up to get groups. Period.

I *do* have a solution to what I think the Velious uber-mob issue is, based partly on a post on the very first page of this one, and I think it will work, and makes sense from both a tactical perspective AND a role-playing one. With the entrance of Velious uber-mobs to the game, skirmishes changed from a matter of players effectively doing their maximum damage possible in a nearly fixed amount of time, to how much damage can be dealt over a long period of time. The wizard class is sooooo shafted (I'm not trying to be rude, but it's the best word to express it that I know of) in the change of gameplay that it's just horrifying. Over time, with blasting AND meditation taken into effect we max out at somewhere in the neighborhood of just eighteen points per second. Literally every melee class is capable of this by level 30. Every class but the wizard is quite capable of this by 40 because they're slinging a sword, or they have a pet who might as well be.

Our problem is that we just plain run OUT of manna, which you guys have said you're not willing to increase because it would increase our total damage output. Well, YES. That *has* to be done, because classes that _aren't_ completely manna-dependent have *their* total damage output distinctly increased simply because the fights last longer, giving them more time to do the damage. We can stand with them best of them for about a minute to a minute and a half, and then beyond that, we taper right down to zero damage output, and *stay* there.

Now extending the analysis of fights to include those battles that go on for an extended period of time from the start, leads me to believe that possibly an analysis of the time *before* a fight is in order. If damage over time is the primary focus, then it logically follows that exp gain over time should be looked at as well, and then the answer becomes fairly apparent.

It makes complete sense to me that a class which is essentially based on careful planning and flawless execution would be doing something to *prepare* for major battles long before any other class would consider doing so. That preparation time would very logically be spent *hoarding up manna* since *manna* is our _entire_ stock in trade.

The concept of manna storage devices has been breached with the Mage's modulating rod. Apparently the developers decided it was a good one, since it's still here, so I'm not breaking wholly new ground with this, but very simply, wizards should be given the ability to store enough manna to last through a long fight.

Give wizards quests for a series of devices which are NODROP and only wizard useable that can store *charges* of bundles of manna, which become drained when they camp, and would perhaps take most if not all of a wizard's manna to give one charge to at a time, with each charge restoring between a 1/5 (early devices) manna to 1/2 manna,
Yes, it is a *grievous* amount of time spent meditating back from nothing to gain a small advantage later, and with this in mind I think the increase in output is quite handily offset by the downtime *before* a fight.

Giving the idea a bit more thought (and taking into account what is more likely feasable without massive changes to the game engine) give us a container (or possibly a holdable, but a container seems fine to me) which can hold *charged* NODROP NORENT gems, one or two slots at the lowest level (to increase our damage output enough *at key points and key points only* in keeping with our "Insurance" role), and eight slots at the highest level. The gems themselves should be reasonably common, and not nodrop when *uncharged*, but a wizard's manna should belong to that wizard alone, so once charged they should be both nodrop and norent (to keep people from casually storing them up while waiting for groups over a period of days, and to keep people from making mules for storing massive stockpiles of wizard manna). The devices should come at first with quests which are nearly as time-consuming as the Staff of the Wheel (but steer clear of the random drops which require extended camps, common items, but a lot of them, because these devices must necessarily be very magically complex, as the quest for Ice Comet is), and with the highest eight-slot container be as difficult as an epic quest (but not the WIZARD epic quest, which Cazic Skin drops make far far *far* more difficult to achieve than epics of other classes--it should be something we should achieve after massive study and effort, not as a result of a long patient *wait*) You've got a nice storyline going with the "voices" in stones of Velium, so fill in a little more detail. This won't scale as for down as I really think it needs to go, but make various sized velium *crystals* available in the game much in the manner of style of various sized chunks of ore, that wizards will gain spells (though RESEARCH ONLY, make us have to use that research skill for this, indeed make the spells nodrop once researched so a wizard must learn it for themselves, since they're storing their own particularly unique manna into gems, and every wizard is different) to modify and empower into the various sizes of nodrop norent manna batteries (heh. Not the gnomish kind). With the gems used at the eight-slot level giving back about 1/2 manna, this increases the output of a wizard by five, with the downtime effectively increased by ten, or by *twenty* if you care to add a recast delay similar to harvest to the spell we fill the gems with. In summary, let wizards do the dramatically higher damage necessary to keep up with the other classes, at the expense of having to spend quite a bit of time *earning* that ability by not being able to engage in fights in any real capacity while wizards, balancing out our ability to gain abnormally high levels of exp from having this extra manna due to the time taken.

Now I'm pretty sure I'm over-explaining this by now but I'm going to continue, because I think this is a really winning idea. Al'Kabor has been running around trying to find they key to eternal life for some time, and the best he has come up with so far are a series of spells which while bigger and more powerful than other similar spells, have shown a distinct tendency to cost an abnormally (and you have to admit, it's enough to make them less valuable overall) high amount of manna for their increased damage output. Raistlin Majere. Ever heard of him? The mage from Dragonlance who started out as a pitifully-ill boy who had to rely on his brother for everything, and then grew into a pitifully-ill Archmage who challenged the gods.

He is my image of what a wizard should be. Arcane spellcasters (yes, I have the 3rd Ed. D&D PHB) should be indispensable to a high-level group. In EQ, ALL arcane spellcasters (necromancers, magicians, enchanters, and wizards) should be equally valuable to a group. However, in the end wizards got the short end of the stick because of our over-dependence on mana and the lack of some other method of contributing to a group. Well, here are my suggestions. You can adopt one or more of them.

**Mana Batteries**
Make a wizard-only item that can be purchased in a wizard’s guild (probably just Erudin) or accessible in a mid-teens quest. The item can be a piece of equipment or an inventory item that is capable of storing mana that are no drop. Whenever we have downtime, these things recharge mana on their own, and when we right-click them, they empty themselves and restore some of our mana (I don’t know how much, maybe a bub or two? Anything, but make it relative to level)

**Percent-based Spells**
Give us spells that reduce a mob’s HP and a wizard’s mana pool by a certain percentage. A good suggestion would be 34 level % spell that reduces a white/black mob’s hp by 10% while reducing the wizard’s mana pool by the same percentage, with a change of 1% of damage and –1% mana per level. For example, a level 34 wizard casts this on a level 34 mob, he slices off 10% of that mob’s health and it costs him 10% of his mana. If he casts it on a level 39 mob, only 5% of the mob’s health is sliced but he uses 15% of his mana because the mob is five levels above him. If he casts it on a level 44 mob, it slices 0% of the mob’s HP and he has just wasted 20% of his mana. However, if he casts it on a 29th level mob, then he takes off 15% of the mob’s hp instead of 10% and it costs only 5% of his mana because of the difference of five negative levels. Minimum mana cost is 1%. This can be potentially overpowering, so maybe a long repop and/or casting time will balance it out.

**Fade/Shadow Step/Yonder**
Fix these spells. Give us some form of control, such as teleporting in the direction we are facing, fix the Z-axis bug (can’t teleport up, only down or across level surfaces), and, above all, ADD A MINIMUM DISTANCE FOR THE TELEPORT!!! There was nothing I hated more than shadow stepping a total of TWO FEET while being attacked by a goblin battlemaster in Lake of Ill Omen. The Z-axis correction isn’t such a big deal, but for goodness sakes, a teleport does us no good if it teleports us two feet to our right, opening up a better attack path for the goblin. Neither does it help us if we end up just outside the same ruined fortress that we just escaped (read: the ruins in Lake of Ill Omen).

**Dragonform**
I read this on another thread somewhere, and it sounds pretty cool. I mean, dragons in common high fantasy are the masters of magic, especially magical damage. Dragonform would transform wizards into drakes or wyverns. While in dragonform, the wizard gets improved Melee, AC, and HP (turning him into a nuking tank; bonuses depend on which dragonform). He can still cast spells, for the most part, but he cannot meditate or access spellbook to change spells. For balancing, while in dragonform wizards can’t access inventory (and thus right-clickable items), cannot cast self-protection spells (any already on him at dispelled), teleport spells dispel dragon form, and once he has sustained a certain amount of damage (half or a quarter of normal HP, for instance) he reverts back into normal form with all the HP loss and is stunned for a certain amount of time. AC bonuses from armor do not affect him in dragonform, though sustained magical effects and bonuses (like the +10 int from a Stein of Moggok) still apply.

Other than giving wizards more options within a group (allowing it to take the role of a tank or nuker), it is also gonna be hella fun to play, and gives us something else to look forward to other than Sunstrike or Lures. I’m sure 60 Wizards would love appearing in newbie areas as sky drakes and wyverns.

**Bestow Power**
While this could be considered in the realm of an enchanter, how about a spell or skill that permits wizards to enchant a warrior’s weapon with a particular nuke that acts as a limited duration proc. The nuke must be something like one half to three-fourths the wizards level. For example, if a 60 wizard bestows a warrior’s sword with the Lvl. 29 DD Inferno Shock, then for the next, say, 30 seconds or a certain number of hits (whichever comes first), every successful hit the warrior makes with that particular weapon deals the same amount of damage as the spell Inferno Shock. Again, this may be considered unbalancing unless there is some sort of penalty involved, so I suggest that the wizard sacrifice one of his nukes (his own choosing, must be in spell slot) for the duration of the spell, or else give this spell some big reagent.

**Damaging Root**
How about a Root/Enstill-type spell that deals constant crushing damage for the duration of the spell? Again, this is also potentially unbalancing, but considering Root is perhaps the one of the most-used spells by low-level wizards, we should have SOME sort of insight as to how it works. So, a Root spell that lasts as long as the Lvl. 4 Root, but with a stronger resilience (i.e. does not break after just ONE hit. Arrgh), and deals low DoT. This is just a random idea, not at all necessary, and perhaps won’t even help in advancing wizard group usefulness.

**Al’Kabor**
Two ideas for this. Firstly, make Al’kabor a high-hp spawn that can’t cast or hit for sh*t in a common newbie zone so that we wizards can beat the cr@p out of him until you fix the Al’kabor line of spells. Secondly, FIX THE AL’KABOR LINE OF SPELLS so that they have a lower mana cost and higher damage. Actually, fix ALL area effect spells so that they don’t have such a high aggro, and so that we can cast them and be useful to a group during an overpull.

**Quicken Spell**
Give wizards a skill that allows them to halve the casting time on maybe two or three spells per day. Once clicked, the next spell the wizard casts takes half as long to cast. The Quicken Spell button repops every 8 or 12 hours, depending on how many Quicken Spells the wizard can use.

**Instant Spell**
Like Quicken Spell, wizards can instantaneously cast one spell per day. Again, this can be an evac, or it can be a last-minute nuke.

At least some of these should help wizards get into groups, especially at higher levels, but I personally think that Verant screwed up in splitting the basic "mage" class into four specialty sub-classes, and they ROYALLY screwed up the wizard’s role. If there is ever an EQ2 (a completely new world, the way the upcoming UWO: Origin is to Ultima Online), I hope they keep the classes simple by keeping one universal mage class and then letting him choose a specialization later on, so that we can get pure summoners (essentially magicians, but able to control more than one pet but having very little nuking ability), pet-nukers (ideal combo for magic-users), necros, and enchanters.
