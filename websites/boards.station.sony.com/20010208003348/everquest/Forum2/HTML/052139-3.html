4) Enchantments. There really needs to be more spells involving the enchantment of spells into items (and not just the 'enchantment' of gems and metals and vials of mana for tradeskill use, I am not referring to *permanent* magical items. The game already has a bewildering overabundance of those...).

A standard example I have used for the last year, a Levitate variant spell for an Enchantment-based caster would require a special tailored cloak (add a Griffon Feather in with the pattern and silks) and would replace the tailored cloak with the 'Winged Cloak' which would have the always on effect of Levitation when equipped and would
'lose it's enchantment' when the user had to zone or log (just like an illusion spell) and be replaced in inventory with the non-enchanted tailored cloak again.

Other utility spells or buffs or even attacks could be 'enchanted' into special items in this manner as well. Just as the Magician uses a different means to cast 'Levitate' and 'Enduring Breath' (via Rings of Flight and Waterstones) so too would the Enchanter place these effects into items for a time, creating magical items of these effects instead of just casting the spell like a Wizard or Druid would.

One spell could have several effects as well via this means. If an Enchanter cast 'Enchant Weapon' while holding a special rod he smithed out of 'shaped driftwood' and 'an aquagoblin gemmed eye,' it would be coded to replace that rod with a Water Crystal Staff. Like the cloak above, that Staff would remain until the user zoned or logged, at which point it would
'disenchant' back into the rod. If the Enchanter cast the exact same Enchant Weapon spell while holding on his cursor an axe smithed from a
'charred haft' and 'fire-forged granite' from Lavastorm, it would be replaced by a Polished Granite Tomohawk for the time. If he cast this same spell with nothing on his cursor, it would fail. If he cast it while holding a Rusty Longsword on his cursor, he would get a fail message, since the spell wouldn't be coded to respond to this 'ingredient.'

The downside to this sort of 'Enchantment' idea is that the Enchanter already has a plethora of spells that work just like anybody elses, so it would seem pointless at this late date to add a spell that confers (for example) a Water Breathing enchantment on a special tailored Fishscale Mask when the Enchanter can just as easily cast Enduring Breath at 12th level anyway.

GoM should be +1; the others should be increased by 1. Then Gift of Magic would be compelling to cast.

Alternately, the suggestion that Gift of Magic acts as a mana transfer is good. If we can't do anything but buff in Velious, we might as well be able to give our excess mana to the wizards.

Being limited to magic as our only spell line (ignoring Tashan's "poison-based" nature), Enchanters should be the masters of magic and mana. The only thing enchanters lack, mana-wise is a way to give mana, directly, to another. Clarity is like a mana "DoT"; we could use a mana "DD". But it's not all that vital.

There are already dozens of comments on the uselessness of various lines of spells, so I won't rehash them. I got over it by simply pretending that the spells never existed.

Because of the level-based limits on the mez line of spells, high-level enchanters facing mobs which can span both sides of the dividing line but who cannot detect the level of the mob (i.e. because the mobs are all blue /cons) are at a severe disadvantage versus lower-level enchanters who themselves sit on the dividing line, level-wise -- once the spell hits the mob, the mob completely resists the spell, but, instead of ignoring the enchanter, the mob aggroes as if the spell were resisted normally. (As an aside, does it really make sense for a creature to be concerned about something to which it is completely immune?)

Someone suggested that having notifications about whether or not a spell would be effective would be good (a la Theft of Thought). This would be nice (quick and easy, actually), but there might be another way to address the issue which gives enchanters more to do (which may be good or bad, depending on the enchanter...). Provide a spell, Evaluate, which characterizes the various resistances that a mob has. When cast on a mob, the spell reports which kinds of things a mob is immune or highly resistant to. In this way, an enchanter can know beforehand, when facing a mob which is questionable in his susceptibility to things, whether or not he can mez or charm the mob, or if the mob is highly resistant to magic. Because Wizards are supposed to be the masters of knowledge, I would advise giving them a similar spell, but make it more general, across all the different resistance types (but it wouldn't report things that only an enchanter would be interested in, such as immunity to mez or charm). Shamans might be another candidate for this type of spell.

Beyond this, the only thing which is of any real concern is the Velious anti-enchanter measures. Either give us a way to make Clarity II and Haste potions in concert with Shamans, so that no one even bothers inviting us, since that's really the only thing we're needed for, anyway, or find some other way to make the area challenging that actually allows us to do something in a more proactive role.

Making the game harder is fine. Changing us from being one of the most active classes to being one of the most inactive is disappointing. Unfortunately, I don't have any good, easy suggestions on exactly how to do this. The only ways I can imagine at this point would wind up completely altering Velious, and I know that won't happen.

Thanks for listening.

It seems to me that balancing the enchanter class has a lot to do with the power of the traditional "ideal" fighting group - enchanter, cleric, tanks. How many want to risk death by playing in a less than optimal group? If you make us less wanted make sure you leave us something we can do... Velious is a good example of how *not* to do this; the insane MR of MoBs means enchanters can't do much of anything there, with or without a group.

You can reduce the dependence on clerics by making the enchanters more robust (more AC items, less aggro on Tash, debuffs, resists..). Groups would probably be happier with having a shaman or druid for healing if the enchanter wasn't so fragile. If you want to reduce reliance on enchanters, make places where people can get good experience without them -- make more spots people can camp that provide a steady stream of single or double MoBs(no tediously long spawn times, no train danger), make haste potions that stack with haste items, and make clarity potions like SoW potions. So we see no reason to make anything except the gods themselves immune to this if they meet the level requirement for the spell. You usually get a message "this mob cannot be mesmerised with this spell" that message should mean you cant use dazzle and lower, but you can use either GoK or if the mob is 54+ Rapture. No zones should be filled with mobs that are immune to those, only some boss mobs (gods)should be.

Fix targetting on stacks of mobs. Add a cycle nearest, or something. Add a way to Identify your target on mobs with no names above their heads. Targetting is and enchanter staple, and as it stands, it is not good.
