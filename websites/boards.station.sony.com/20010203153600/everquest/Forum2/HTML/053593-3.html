--> Stuns for monks - correcting some misinformation that has been posted so far.

Currently the only way for a monk to stun is to use the level 59 discipline - Silent Fist. This discipline has a reuse time of 7 minutes at level 59 and 6 minutes at level 60.

Silent Fist is not an automatic stun: on the next Dragon punch that hits the opponent, there is a "chance" that it will stun them.
--
When dealing with the average fight, a monster may attempt to heal, gate, or cast significantly powerful spells upon you many times in a single battle. It becomes obvious that one chance to stun them per 6 minutes of gameplay is pretty limited in value.

All large races able to use slam may attempt a stun or casting interrupts roughly once every 6 seconds.

At level 55, all warriors gain the ability to stun and interrupt with every single kick they do - also roughly one chance per 6 seconds.

Warriors do not sacrifice anything for this innate ability, they use the same kick they have used throughout their careers, but now with a stun chance.

Many classes also have the option of equipping a shield to use the bash skill to attempt to stun or interrupt mobs. This is a tradeoff in that they lose a sizeable chunk of damage output in order to gain a chance to interrupt spells.

--
-Proposed Solution-
At level 55, allow the "Round Kick" skill that monks gained at level 5 to gain the ability to interrupt casters. The trade off here is apparent, as while the monk attempts to interrupt by using Round Kick, he is sacrificing his ability to be using the very powerful and higher damage "Flying Kick" skill that is the hallmark attack of the monk past level 30.

This solution would allow the monk the option of sacrificing significant damage output in order to gain the chance to interrupt spell casting monsters.

The bottom line of this trade off is that the monk would be giving up a chance to kick for 100+ damage to instead kick for roughly 15 points of damage with a chance of interrupting a spell.
