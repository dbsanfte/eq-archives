Shadee, this I COULD be actually a design feature of newer AI (not the newest pathing AI being tested on Test).

One of the things newer MMOGs promise is "smarter" AI. All games since AO have promised mobs that were smart enough to not try and go into an unwinnable situation alone. What you're experiencing could very well be a result of SOE trying something along these lines. Maybe it’s formula based. Maybe it calculates how much damage it’s taking per second versus how many HPs it has versus the amount of damage it’s primary target (as in, your pet) is taking, knows the kind of pet it’s hunting (since each class at each level has different ways of dealing with their pet) projects that curve out and sees the logical conclusion. In fact, at a very simple level, that’s probably how I’d do it, and it’s a pretty quick formula.

Because you’ve mentioned it wasn’t nearly beaten enough to flee in terror, this is the only possibility I can think of.

The other is that it was steadily getting knocked back (or knocking your pet back) and the path it runs on(since all mobs run on rails, calculating the best path on those rails along the way) intersected a junction that sent it off all over the place. If you ever want to get fascinated by just how terrible some rails were laid down, visit the Shadowmen encampment in LFay. Mobs spawn on the top of the ramp. We stand at bottom. We pull from top. They come down, pass right by us (if we’re just outta the way enough) and proceed to cover just about half the zone before coming back. This, btw, is an outdoor zone, and except for the Z-axis change by the height of the ramp, everything else is pretty flat. Thankfully they’re not on the same social aggro as other mobs

In this case though, it sounds like the sent IS on the same social aggro.

So either the AI was written to actually have it go get friends well before most mobs think to flee, or the rails in the place are just as stupidly laid down as they are in others.

quote:
Sigh, now i ask you VI or any other person, "WHAT IS THE DEAL WITH THE PATHING IN THIS ZONE!?!?!?!?!
Is codeing really that hard? or was the programmer suddenly struck with the lazy bug?


*sigh* I wish people understood just how hard some of the simplest problems in real life are to transfer to the digital world. Yes, as a matter of fact, the coding is that hard. Pathing coding is not simply.

Oh I can easily bang out some pathing code that will work virtually flawlessly in all situtations, no matter how many obstacles, players and other mobs you put between your mob and where you want to move to. The problem is it would take several Cray supercomputers to run it in real time.

Pathing code comes 3 ways. You can have your choice of any two ways but they are mutually exclusive of the third. It comes good, fast or cheap (cheap = low CPU load)

You want good and cheap, it's going to be slow performance.
You want good and fast, it's going to be costly in terms the amount of CPU needed to drive it.
You want cheap and fast, well it's not going to be very good at efficiently getting your mob from point a to point b.

Your specific problem is this: Stop hiding behind your pet.

You see PoJ has narrow passages that only wide enough to have a single row of pathing points. So lets look at your problem. You sic pet on mob and melee it a bit, then dot the mob and then sit back to med while pet and DoTs work on mob. Basically in a PoJ hall you look like this

You - Pet - Mob

Where the pet is between you and the mob. Correct? Now consider what happens when the DoT's finally out pace your pets damage and the mob decides it's time to come "talk" to you for a bit. But how do I, the poor mob, get there? Normally I'd go straight to you but alas this stupid pretend kitten is in the way and that pathing branch is there for blocked because while I can walk through players I can't walk through pets. So my only alternative is to run the other way completely around the zone.

So next time instead of hiding behind your pet put the mob between you and your pet and you won't have this problem.
