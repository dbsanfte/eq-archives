I would really like to have feign death lower you on the hate list. Currently, many many times in groups when I feign the mobs just keep beating on me. Thus feign can no long save my life like it is supposed to.

Feign currently should temporarily remove you from a mobs hate list, while you are feigned. Once you get up you have a small chance to mem wipe the mob and be completely removed from the hate list. However, in groups feign is seen through far too often. This is a problem.

I will feign during a battle and the mob will stay on me even though I get no "you have fallen to the ground" message. Once this happens it is pretty unlikely that the mob will ever stop hitting me no matter how many times I feign. I can do this at 80% health or 10% health and it doesn't really matter.

Feign was changed so that mobs wouldn't always forget you once you feigned. Basically they wanted this to save a monks life and not be used for splitting mobs with little risk. This is not how feign works anymore. It very rarely saves a monks life during a battle. Infact it almost never does.

To change this I would like to have feign lower the monk down the hate list of mobs every time you get a successful feign. Even if a mob saw through a successfull feign this should move us down the hate list and the mob would begin attacking the person that is now at the top of the hate list. This would allow us to use feign to save our lives once more.

Feign should still have the normal chance to memwipe mobs etc.

Moving down the hate list should be more reliable than a rogues evade. Moving down the hate list should be more challenging for a rogue because if they are successful they can do insane amounts of damage. Monks don't need to move down the hate list to deal their damage. As such getting down a mobs hate list should be easier than a rogue getting dropped down the hate list.

Doing this would make a monk much more survivable on raid level encounters and would not have any global impacts on balance.

First off I'd like to add my support to Bodhi et al's compilation of suggestions. They are well thought out and presented.

I'd also like to suggest that, in lieu of chained special attacks, to simply decouple our special attacks. The main benefit of this change is to increase the monk damage through our existing abilities and restoring the relative damage capacity of monk in comparison to warriors. A side benefit is the re-affirmation of the monk's combat versatility. Let's face it, Scars of Velious has once again created a disparity between monk, rogue and warrior damage, with monks falling by the wayside.

From what I've gathered, our special attacks max damages are:
Round Kick, 13
Tiger Claw, 13
Eagle Strike, 25
Dragon Punch, 35.
This adds up to 86 additional damage points over pressing the flying kick button, assuming each and every special attack lands, and each and every special attack attains maximum damage.

With this change, and an offhand damage bonus increase for hand to hand combat, would likely retune monk damage inline where it was before SoV.

In response to the 'need for stun' being unsupported i thought i would explain a little.

Shaolin monks are the masters of the body which means that they can use the body to many advantages. This also means that they can use their foe's bodies to their advantage. In combat they are know to disable their foe in any way possible. This can be done through various means. e.g. A sharp kick to the side of the knee will disable the opponent temporarily. This can be likened to the 'stun' in eq.

Something like this would not over balance the class, it would possibly make soloing remotely viable and also bring our worth back to grouping.

Stun may also be implemented so that it is only possible while using fists. There are not enough monks using fists which certainly has been frustrating to be out damaged up to my current lvl by twinks wielding 10k weapons, which completely distracts from using fists. Not that this should be used as a method for stopping twinking, but it certainly would make fists a viable option.
