Youve asked for our ideas and our thoughts. We have given them to you in legion. Some have been well thought out and descriptive, some have been far flung and over the top. We have suggested the removal of the skeleton effect from Ignite and Chill Bones. Im glad to hear that it is possibly being looked into. This would at least allow Necromancers to take an active part in a high level raid, and I mean active as being able to inflict damage on the target helping our groups to succeed. At present, this spell does 510 max damage. This is not an unreasonable amount of damage. As the game progresses and the creatures we encounter continue to advance in number of Hit Points, and amount of resistances, 510 damage is a mere pixles worth in the whole scheme of things. The spell does not change for the better or the worse. The damage is still there but the restrictions placed upon its use are lifted, providing the Necromancer with the ability to be of use.

We have mentioned Lifetaps in every form we have. They are all resisted on "uber mobs". We have gone into length about how the process of the Necromancer breaks down very quickly without the ability to tap targets. I am sure you understand by now how the process works, so I will not go into that. Ideas on this have been stretched and flexed. Some good, some not so good. But it is then your(Verant) job to cull the information for ideas that can be used in the game. I think the lack of postings on this goes to show that we are running low on ideas that are reasonable and fair. If we wished to get outlandish, Im sure the thread could go on for months. But thats not what this is about. This is about trying to find a way to allow Necromancers become useful on high level raids. Its not about new spells, or disciplines, or anything of that nature. What we have now simply does not work in the high level game. It is not too much to ask to have some resemblance of a roll to play. Mana dumping other casters is fine, but if we are to be relegated to such duties, then we should at least be good at it. As of now, were not even that. We are poor mana batteries, period. 300 mana for 100 mana is a terrible ratio for a class that is reduced to doing nothing but that during a fight. The whole Lich process comes to a screeching hault as to avoid ones own death and their own hands or a stray AE spell.

New spells and abilities? Sure they sound nice, and could be considered for future expansions and so on. But at this moment in time, the only thing a Necromancer needs is the ability to do damage to high level encounters. Be it through Lifetaps and Drains(which I would prefer), or by Direct Damage spells such as the Ignite series. Before raids and after raids, everyone has a roll. Be it buffs, corpse dragging/summoning, pulling, whatever. But those who have jobs before the raid are still useful during the raid. The Necromancer is not.

This game is about fun. Reality should never come into play in a fantasy game. What would be the point then? I would play a life simulation if I wanted that. But instead I choose the unreal world of high fantasy. Dragons and demons, elves and ogres. Game balance is a tricky thing, and as these messageboards prove time and time again, people take it to heart when their character, their creation that they created from the ground up, is crippled and made obsolete. You invade their fantasy, their imagination. People tend to take things like this very personal. To be told that "you must pay for your abilities in early life" is infact blaming the player. It is not our fault the Necromancer class is percieved as a god class by many(mostly those who do not comprehend the class). You made the classes do what they do. Even you have to admit that the morning of beta testing, you did not know that Necromancers would be as powerful as they were. Nor did you expect Druids would be so efficient at kiting(they actually were early in the games life). You also didnt know that Wizards would turn out to be as weak as they were. Who would have ever invisioned that Bards and Druids and Wizards could quad kite? Nobody did. An engenious player sat down one day and said "Hey..this might work. I'll give it a try." And so it was born.

My point here is that by removing the Necromancers ability to deal damage based soley on the designs layed out before us, and the methods that have been honed into fine skills by those that came before us, you essentially contradict the game. You made the Necromancer, we just played them. You gave us fear and darkness. You gave us Lifetaps and Drains. You gave us the ability to gain mana by draining our own life. We simply used what you gave us. Albeit there were times when some abilities were used in ways that were not meant to be used, these were taken care of(ie: Feign Death and let the pet tank for you the whole fight). The Necromancer as it stands now in regular play, solo or grouped, is exactly how you made it. Pets have been nerfed multiple times to balance. Spells have been nerfed several times to balance. We play the class you have put before us. To punish us for giving us the class in which we have is a contradiction, especially in the highest most rewarding part of the game.

You see the game as rewarding through all levels, and it can be. But every game must have a goal, otherwise it is just uncontrolled and pointless. People wish to level, people wish to gain items, people wish to see mighty gods lay at their feet. I agree this is not all there is to the game, but it is a goal. And once having reached that goal, the ability to take part in large scale fights where giant creatures do battle with teams of people and rewards and egos are on the line, it would be nice to be able to sit back and take pride in the fact that you helped. You fulfilled your destiny, you killed the evil demi god Venril Sathir and freed thousands from his tyranical reign. But instead, you find out that you helped drag corpses to the priest. You were reduced to after battle coroner. The medic who was a day too late. "This is important too though." you may say. But its not what we do. We are Necromancers. Dark practitioners of Death magics. We know the inner most secrets of life and death. We summon fallen warriors to do our bidding, and we can dispose of them as we see fit. We should not be the god class of dragging body bags. We should be allowed to be Necromancers and fill those body bags.

some random thoughts on the subject...

- re: lifetaps being resisted...I have to disagree that this makes lich useless. Less useful, sure...but chloro or bard heal/mana song can make a pretty decent substitute. Lack of lifetaps does make healing useless though, though if healing is most of what a necro is doing, then there are other problems that need to be looked at (and there are)...

- I don't think twitch should really get any better...right now it serves the purpose of saving people from OOM-ness and reducing downtime adequately. While being efficient mana batteries would make necros desired, it really wouldn't be much fun on raids, as people would want necros to avoid dotting/nuking/etc so as to save mana for twitch...bleh.

- I agree with what's been said on ignite bones -- kill the illusion. If you want to leave some kind of effect on the mob to keep it from being recast in the next so many seconds, or want to increase recast time, fine...but drop the illusion so necros can have a decent general-purpose DD.

- As an alternative to an ignite bones change -- or perhaps, a supplement, depending on what the change is...anyway, I noticed shaman get a 400ish poison DD in velious at 44. Why not make it necro-usable at 53-55 or something? The ratio and damage are worse than ignite bones; I'd hardly say it'd be overpowered.

- good to know dots are being worked on

- immunity to normal fear is a good idea, as is making pets receive group buffs so xobaber can get GRM ...however that doesn't do much for your regular raid encounters, as you still have pathing problems, broken mez problems, etc. some thoughts...

- give necros a temporary pet post-50, one that has 100 hps and dies when its target does, but has a much lower mana cost to account for the fact that you'll be recasting it for each mob. why? well, first of all, it's a lot less costly to /pet get lost when xobaber decides to screw up mezzes if the pet will die anyway, and doesn't cost too much to cast. Additionally, with 100 hps, xobaber will likely die quickly if he switches targets (and taunts his newest one). You could also make the temporary pet die if he gets too far from his master (since he's not supposed to do anything but fight that one target). Finally, the temporary pet could be given better resists for boss mobs (though it should be taken into account that a temporary pet that attacks once cast won't have much opportunity to get resist buffs before being blasted).

- group pet! Make a spell that multiple necros can use to summon a single pet for all of them. The pet might have less damage capacity than the combination of their normal pets, but have better resists, better skills/ac, and be higher level. It would be better suited for boss encounters at a cost of damage, and would also require multiple necros to be truly effective (as opposed to other necro abilities, like DMF or summon corpse, that require only a single necro for the entire raid).

Additionally, each necro could be able to give the pet commands independently, so that commands -- primarily, /pet back off -- can be given multiple times in the space of time that one necro would only be able to give a single command. (each necro would have his own command-recast delay, but would be unaffected by the delays of the others) This would require some communication among necros, but would make the pet much less of a liability on a raid, while not completely eliminating the difficulty of pet control.

- defoliation -- if it looks like a plant, walks like a plant, hits like a plant...

- make it so chanter mezzes can overwrite screaming terror. Right now, if a raid gets a number of adds, and I try to help out with ST, the chanters won't be able to take over mezzing the mobs I've screamed without waiting for my spell to wear off. Considering that the level 4 mez does the same thing, and lasts longer than ST, you'd think mez should've overwrote in the first place...

- ST upgrade? I dunno. This would help groupability, and it's true that necros are a damage machine in groups already...although, with high enough mana cost, the ability to deal damage would be lessened. All in all, I'd say if you're going to do this, do it not to help necros, but to make dungeon groups more viable in the absence of a chanter (as few will do high level dungeons without one atm). If you can't justify it that way, then forget it.

Aatudien, 53 necro, Lanys
