Problem 1. Out of range healers, while not a true exploit does create an unfair advantage. The solution to this is to set a level limit on healing and buffing equal to the pvp limits. while the possiblitly of an oor healer/buffer is possible, it would not pose an advantage that is so great as to make it unfair. A lvl 20 cleric buffing and healing a lvl 12 warrior engaged with lvl 12 opponents is not the same as a lvl50 cleric healing/buffing the same warrior fighitng lvl 15 opponents.

Repost from an external forum:

I'm trying to beat the dead horse again. But Absor throwing another bone to the PVP community to get our hopes up, got me thinking on the issue. In reality I don't expect any changes, but who knows. There are a couple of conditions like new games (Shadowbane and DAoC), and blue servers getting more crowded every day that can motivate Verant to take some action on PVP gameplay issues.


In my opinion class balance is not the main issue in RZ. Everquest is not a one on one game, it is a grouping game. Verant will always balance PVE first, and PvP will be balance only to address exploits and things of that nature.
For example a monk can claim that he is un-effective in PVP. But, What about a Druid + Monk combo? That combo can be very lethal. It is quit obvious that the solo classes (ie. necro, druid, shaman) are more effective in PVP. Those classes have the tools to kill an experiece-give-mob solo due to the wide range of tools at their dispossal. Nerfing PVE capabilities on a class is not the answer.

In my opinion the are two fundamental problems with RZ PVP.
1. Penalty
Basically, the penalty is based on if you are bagged or running on no-drop equipment. Those that have access to no-drop equipment have a huge advantage over those who can't camp those equipment (due to time limitations) or those classes that don't have a good selection of no-drop equipment. That's a big failure, the penalty should be universal. Equality is key. Also, in most cases is not possible to gain retribution. A penalty must be suffered at all times, even if your are naked.

2. Reward
Same as above. Is your target bagged or in no-drop?
Also if you get an uber or good item in PVP, you ganked it. No way a player would risk hours of camping on a few minutes or even seconds. I may be wrong but I think this applies to 99% of the population, there may be 1% truly hardcore. I'm not against of ganking or surprise attacks, however we all know that the odds are stacked heavily on the attacker. I don't think getting a big reward for it is right.

How do we fix this?
1. Penalty
Experience loss + Res. Debuff Effects

I think experience loss makes an excellent Universal Penalty. I would suggest a ratio of 1/5 of a yellow bub at any level. I'm lvl 48, not sure how would this affect the 50+ game. But this figure is not an absolute value and can be improved upon. Pre-Kunark getting experience at 35+ level was a real pain, due to overcrowding and small amount of high level zones. But now, there is a lot of options to gain experience at any level. I was against experience loss on PvP pre-Kunark. Also, this allows the community to somewhat police themselves.

To declare a victor and eliminate any kind of bind-rushing, there should be a strong Res. type debuff upon PVP death (30 minutes or more). Also, if you get killed during this period you would not lose experience, to prenvent bind point camping, deleveling or corpse camping. The debuff should eliminate the ability of a player to operate on PvP or PVE.

2. Rewards
When you go and kill the a named NPC, do you always get the reward you were looking for? No.
Basically I think the best way to handle the rewards is through a Random based system. Like those on the NPCs.
Example:
If I kill a player that is lower level than me. I get a 5% percentage probability of gaining an item from what he had equiped prior to dying (no held items). On the other hand if I kill a player higher level than me, then the probability should be higher (10%). There is a chances that nothing may drop, and a higher chance of an inventory item dropping. That's the basic idea.

Any comments are welcome. Thanks
