My 2cp worth of math:

At level 54, lich drains 24 hps from me per tick for 24 mana.

At the same level, I regen 5 hps per tick sitting.

For the remaining 19 hps per tick, I need to cast bond of death, tapping two points per mana spent. I have to spend half of the mana I get from those 19 hps to replace them, and have 9.5 remaining (again, per tick).

9.5 + 5 = 14.5 mana per tick.

Clarity 2 is 12.

This assumes you have no periods of downtime where you might turn lich off because your hps are getting low. This isn't much of an issue in groups, where you can be buffed to 2k hps and have a steady stream of pulls...but after making/remaking pet (which happens a considerable amount with the flimsy 53 one) or fighting a named, you're going to have some downtime. You can let your hps run down if you like, but it becomes dangerous when that mature wurm resists darkness a few times in a row, or when you find out that the named you just pulled summons.

What's more, however, and a major reason why lich shouldn't be nerfed, is that it gets better when you're grouped with someone who can regen you. For instance, if I'm in a group with a druid who is group chloro'ing (13 points per tick, yes?), I have 18 total hp regen while sitting. Remaining 7 hps are made up with bond, so total 18+7/2 = 21.5 mana per tick. That's a lot, but if you've taken the effort to group, why not? The grouped effectiveness of necros benefits everyone.
