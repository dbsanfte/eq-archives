Ahhh finally we can have a discussion about this, thank you for your response, Alan. Now lets analyze the facts about this line.

>" Magicians also suffer more than any other class from the broken nature of AE Rain spells (hint: pets being a valid target is NOT the real problem)."<

"Grimstaff, I've read your calculations about how you think rain spells should work. If we did it your way they would be way, way, way too powerful."

The problems with this view point are many:

-GZ Himself said they are broken as they are done now, and no offense but I'd say the guy who designed the spell system knows much better about what would work and what wouldn't in terms of balance.
-Verant assumed the spells WERE working as I described until we informed them otherwise in January... which means that you guys thought the concept was balanced and not a problem, why the change?
-There are issues you are not paying attention to here.

--Three waves, each one is resisted separately, so that instead of the damage being added up, it has to be considered in three different packets.
--The damage is over 12 seconds (3 waves 6 seconds apart) so it has a similar effect as a DOT, it might not even take effect due to the creature dying in that time.
--The area is fixed at a given location, and the monster can physically LEAVE the area of effect, thus negating 1 or more waves entirely.
--Like all AE spells, they will affect the caster, and damage him.

In other words, looking at the damage as a single, total package is misleading.

"I've read your posts, both here and on other sites, and I wanted to show you something. You never take your suggestion to its full conclusion."

Actually I think its really you that have not taken it to its full conclusion.

Let's do this. I'll assume that you used the most efficient use of rain spells in your examples. So let's look at the change you suggest using the most effective use of your version of the spell. And, for extra fun, let's take one of the worst ratio rain spells and look at it. Also, keep in mind that at level 29 a magician's DD spells are extremely efficient. So this might be the 'worst case' scenario for your suggested changes to the rain spells:

"Level 29 best blast Inferno Shock (110 mana)
Level 29 Rain Spell Lightning Storm (162 mana)
Blast = 1.85 damage per 1 mana
OLD Rain = 1.77 damage per 1 mana
NEW Rain = 5.31 damage per 1 mana."

This only assumes that you hit with every wave on every target. Now, do you have a problem with PBAE spells? Here's another comparison that you might want to ponder (I run the risk of you freaking out and calling for PBAE to be nerfed here):

Level 24 best blast (Frost Shock)= 187 damage(110 mana)
Level 24 best PBAE (Cast Force)= 101 damage (123 mana)
Max targets hit by PBAE at best estimate = 10
Mana efficiency per point of damage by your system = 9.181 damage per 1 mana
Level 24 Rain Spell (Lightning Storm) = 75x3 (162 mana)
OLD Rain = 1.77 damage per 1 mana
NEW Rain = 5.31 damage per 1 mana

See how that works? PBAE is in ONE shot, its drawback is that you have to be near the targets. Rain does it in THREE shots, the drawbacks being listed above and MUCH more crippling than PBAE.

PBAE is balanced. Rain is not.

It doesn't really matter what you remember GZ saying about this.

Actually it does matter. Its not my recollection, it is the truth, if you want the actual EMAIL I can get that sent to you. The fact is, the word and reactions and ideas of the designer of the spell system for a game has tremendous weight, because he knows the balance of the game and the spells and how they are supposed to work better than anyone else, right?

You cannot simply discount the fact he wanted it changed.

"Keep in mind that rain spells do less damage per tick, therefore their taunt rate is lower than the column spells."

That is the only advantage, and it is easily overshadowed by the disadvantages. Please, Alan, think through this carefully and get back to us on it. Magicians everywhere would really be helped by this.

3 waves on 4 targets. That's the fix. If that is too painful for you, then lower the damage on the rain spells, or make them single target, don't pretend its a valid AE if it gets weaker and less useful the more targets you get.

PLEASE lets have a dialog on this, its been a problem for years that Verant recognized and admitted was a problem.
