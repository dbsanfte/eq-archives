Bfason, I don’t want to turn this into “You’re better than me” match but let me tell you something about SK’s:

1. Mana efficiency and spell utilization
Paladin’s spells are mostly buff and heal oriented (with a little damage thrown in for good measure). Once you cast all your buffs your AC and Hp’s go up. This reduces the damage taken in a fight. The few offensive spells can blind a monster or even do a little damage to it. Paladins have lower down time comparing to SK’s. Also they are more mama efficient for the group’s healer/s.

SK’s “buffs” are more or less limited to “elemental” resistance (cold, disease etc). Not much in the way of increasing AC or HP’s. His spells are mostly offensive oriented.
The offensive spells in question are low level dot’s. They do not cause great deal of damage.
SK’s get “siphon health” line of spells with which to heal themselves. Most SK’s over level 30 that I’ve met stopped using those spells. They are slow to cast and not very mana efficient.
Another spell that supposed to keep SK’s alive is “Vampiric Embrace”. It lasts about one minute. It’s also proc based, meaning it may go off once or twice in a fight, healing a few points (for example about 19 hps at 36). So this spell is just another spell window place holder. Very inefficient. I usually forget to cast it.
SK’s are capable of Fear Kiting. But anyone who has done so will tell you that Fear doesn’t stick most of the time and usually breaks in the first couple of seconds in the higher levels.
So the only real advantage that SK’s have is Feign Death. Lately I’ve noticed that most mobs will keep on chewing off my feet while I’m feigning death. So there goes that idea.
The Siphon Strength spell and Steal Strength proc from the Dark Reaver look nice on paper. Reality is quite different. A mob hitting you for 70 hps will keep on hitting you just as hard after you have reduced it’s strength by 20+ points. It should be weaker and hit for a bit less but it doesn’t.
With the added strength a SK can carry more but his damage output doesn’t seem to be affected noticeably. Pets are nearly useless. They are very incompetent and cause, at most, 40 or 50 damage during a fight (before level 39 pet). To low level players that may seem a lot but at my level it is a drop in a bucket. They do come in handy when the SK had enough and needs to make a run for it. The pet may (and I mean may) occupy the mob for a couple of seconds (literally, it is that weak) giving the SK a bit of a head start. Also since SK’s pet are so much lower than their masters they tend to attract all the “green trash” in the immediate area. In dungeons they are too much of a risk to use and can result in the deaths of the entire group if summoned.

So, paladin’s spells last a lot longer than those of a SK and tend to be more useful.

2. Acceptance.
Groups will usually take paladins over SK’s. Paladins are more group friendly.
Extra/buffer healer is always welcomed. An extra low damage dealer is not.

3. Tanking
Thanks to their buffs paladins can last longer in a fright.
SK’s are more dependent on other people’s buffs and heals than paladins. They are high maintenance class.

4. Taunting
Here is one thing that I think SK’s are superior at: Taunting.
SK’s can taunt an angry mob off a warrior when necessary.
But what good is taunting when you can not take the damage?
If the warrior, with his advanced AC and HP’s, can’t survive the mob what chance does SK have?


In the end, I think Paladins get the better end of the stick.
Why do I keep on playing a SK (especially human SK)? Maybe I’m a little masochistic or I like a challenge or maybe I just need to have my head examined… The truth is SK’s are very rare and very unique.
I bet there is more but my lunch break is almost over.
