Disarm:

This is an issue for all classes that have this skill. Disarming a mob has no effect whatsoever on their combat ability. My suggestion would be to make Disarm work like the Cripple spell when used against mobs that are not wielding a weapon. Currently, disarm does absolutely nothing -- mobs without weapons attack the same as mobs with weapons.

Taunt:

Another multi-class issue, but it affects warriors the most since we do not have spells to taunt with. Our whole job in life is to tank, and yet I find it nearly impossible to taunt any mob over level 50 with me having maximum taunt skill.

Warrior epic:

I know you don't want to discuss epics, but it is one of the most difficult to obtain epics, and at the same time, one of the weakest. Other people have covered this, so I'll leave my input at that.

"Charge" discipline:

This is a really silly discipline. It makes you not miss for 12 seconds, with a recycle time of half an hour. There is never a reason to use this. The damage improvement is generally around 20% for a period of 12 seconds, which is to say about .01% of a typical mob's hit points. Anyway, I would like to see it changed to a 'Spirit of Cheetah' effect. A "get away discipline" that makes the warrior run fast for a short period of time. Warriors have no escape effects, and since pumice got nerfed we're hosed if we run into a SOW speed mob, especially in the huge Kunark/Velious zones (where it's a long way to the zoneline). It doesn't help that JBoots are a lot harder for warriors to obtain than any hybrid/caster class. Changing 'Charge' to a running discipline would be a cool replacement for what is right now a useless discipline. (Ideally, it should stack with, not overwrite, SOW.)

Arrows:

I'm sure rangers must complain about this more, but the way arrows track is really bad. Go into Howling Stones (Charsis) with a bow. Try to pull the entrance mobs. You can't. Even though you have a direct line of sight to them, the arrow will not angle up in time, it will fly straight into the steps at the monster's feet every time. Very annoying to have to make a caster be the puller simply because I can't shoot the target. (Alternatively, giving us access to an easy to get item that casts a some useless effect that's only good for pulling would fix this as well.)

Hit point buffs:

Maybe this is me being picky, but it has always bothered me that, after a full set of buffs, warriors do not have many more hit points than other tank classes. After you add 1500 hit points to both of us, the percentage difference is greatly reduced.

It would be cool if clerics/shaman got a high level hit point buff that gave the target a PERCENTAGE increase based on their 'naked' hit points. e.g., a spell that gives 20% -- if my base HP are 2000 and yours are 1000, the buff gives me 400hp, and gives you 200hp. Thus maintaining the natural hit point differences between the classes.
