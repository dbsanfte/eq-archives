Thanx for asking absor. I play primarily on Rallos Zek but some of my suggestions are for all the Zeks.

1 - AE Spells. Make Point blank AE spells NOT hit our pets OR the pets of those we group with. Make AE Rain or targetted spells NOT hit our pets, our group members OR their pets.

2 - PVP Looting. It should be limited to the person doing the killing or a member of the group the person is with.

3 - Shadowknight harm touch. The discipline should be subject to the 66% damage that ALL attack spells are subject to. The harm touch ability should NOT be available to the shadowknight if they die and don't lose exp(kill themselves by drowning OR the low level newbie killing shadowknights).

Now...ideas I have.

Balancing the naked caster:
1 - If you are killed in a pvp fight, your spellbook remains on your corpse and is only lootable by the victim. The killer(only) has the option to loot you for 5 minutes(real time). If he chooses to NOT loot the corpse within that time, the corpse is locked to EVERYONE for 60 minutes from the time of the attack. That way, if a naked caster comes to PK, all the players need to do is to kill him once and not loot the corpse. And you will have 1 hour grief-free playing time.

2- Make spells lootable. This was argued with the spell guy and he said that if this were implemented, they would have to allow weapons to be lootable as well. Unfortunately that is not accurate. Although weapons can not be looted, they are not SOUL BOUND like spells are. If a PK wizard might lose his ICE COMET spell, he might think twice about attacking. Also the VAST majority of spells are purchasible from vendors where there is an EXTREMELY limited amount of weapons available from vendors for melees. You should be able to choose to loot a spell or an item from someone you kill.

3- Faction hits. Faction hits were tried on Rallos Zek in a half-assed manner. They should be MUCH more complex than just the killing blow gets you the faction hit. First, they should be agressor based. They should be allowed to be forgiven by the victim. There should be a retaliation period where the 2 can reengage each other without having to take additional faction hits. The retaliation faction grace period should be extended to the guilds of both players so that they can assist in vengeance.

Grief PK's:
1 - Zone/TP camping should be bannable...there should be no confusion among GM's and guides on the Zeks. We know the extent of the logging capabilities of the GMs. There should be a logging of anytime a player engages in PVP with another player. Specifically the location of each individual. That way when a GM looks in the logs and sees that player A zones into a druid ring and is attacked within 5 seconds of a person outside the agro radius of the guards there, then that person is zone camping. The logs should be able to show if the victim was idle or not(meaning they do or do not have control of their character).

2 - Attacking through walls. This feature should be disabled. There was an issue with people hiding behind trees and flowers to escape a spell's wrath but that was a far better reality than sitting the bank and being attacked by someone you can't see or attack back.

3 - guildchat monitoring. Guides should have access to the guildchat/tells of people who have been warned for grief PKing. At this point, only GM's have that access.

Just some ideas

1 - Charming/fear. Reimplement them so that they can work in PVP again. Limit charmed attack targets to other PCs. This would eliminate the grief-charming where you would charm someone and make them attack a level 60 guard. This would also eliminate the exploit charming where an enchanter and a warrior could break spawns with ease. This would be very limited above about level 30 anyway since most players at that point have their magic resistance too high for enchanters/bards to land spells even with tashania. But it would open the possibility of charming pets in order to make those classes think twice about summoning them in a PVP situation. This would also make enchanters slightly effective in lvl 30+ pvp where they are currently limited to clarity/haste and dispelling.

The number one problem on the Team servers at present is Immortal healing. Players have become well versed in the art? of exploiting the level and race protections while circumventing them in order to kill other players.

The most egregious example of this, which occurs frequently, would be the guy with two accounts and computers, controlling a low level and high level at the same time. The low level character used to attack players of his level, is invincible, thus immortal, in all encounters, and more than likely will use this power to engage in grief play.

Crossteam healing and buffing, while not as powerful as the above exploit, is in some ways more offensive, as it is OOC, and prevents the team atmosphere that the racewar rules were intended to foster. Players that try to play true 4-team, find that their supposed homelands are actually where their enemies are most powerful, due to the easy access to crossteam immortal healers.

I advocate one of the following remedies for this:

- Character PvP Flag prevents xteam/OOR buffs/heals. This was actually proposed by Abashi quite some time ago, which leads me to believe the implementation would be practical. When a character takes damage in PvP, he can no longer be xteam/OOR healed until zoning. In many ways, this would be the ideal solution, allowing unrestricted grouping for PvE. A variant on this would be to set the flag, only when the pc deals damage to another pc. This would allow xteam/oor heals for defense only, making it more difficult to use the proposed flag to disrupt play.

- Race and level restrictions on heals and beneficial buffs. This would certainly solve the problem, but would have the downside of now longer allowing different races to group for PvE. A side benefit would be an end to the practice of powerleveling.

- Faction hits for xteam/oor healing. Currently xteam/oor healing is the MOST POWERFUL tactic one can use in PvP. Yet there is no consequence at all for using this tactic. Faction hits, similar to assisting in PvE fight, would at least provide a minimal reason not to engage in this behaviour. Ideally, these factions could be displayed in the /con message, allowing players to recognize race-traitors and treat them accordingly.

That is really the only issue that takes considerable joy away from the game for me. I am thoroughly entertained by EQ PvP, and highly skeptical that the upcoming competitors will be able to lure me away, though I'll certainly give them a try. That said, here are a host of minor issues that could use improvement:

- While I appreciate the intent to avoid "sea-lawyers" by keeping the rules of conduct private, it has resulted in much confusion. The majority of players, seem completely unaware of what is legal or illegal. Even worse, some of the less experienced guides do as well. A published code of conduct would reduce the number of petitions greatly.

- Loot and Scoot, a player-adopted code of conduct, based on Verants own rules regarding PvP has gone a long way towards promoting good-natured PvP among like-minded players. Official endorsement of this code would also alleviate the petitioning.

- Thrown weapons should not trigger damage from damage shields

- Damage shields should be limited by the level o f the recipient.

- The Halas arena is bugged. Not only does it not function for PvP, but a duel begun there will cause the duellists to be bugged. In duel mode, but unable to hit each other.

- Protection from PvP until control is regained when zoning would be nice

- Please give thought to game mechanics changes for PvE and their effect on PvP. Seemingly small changes such as the moving of sow to a different spell stack from root, can have great impact in PvP. This change severely reduced the effectiveness of root (no longer removes sow, movement being the key factor of PvP) while increasing the effectiveness of snare (no longer removed by root, still removes sow).

Thats about it, thanks fer having a look at PvP.
