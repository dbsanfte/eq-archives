Here are the Official Rules for this Board, Read them carefully, you well see clearly what this board is supposed to be:

Welcome to the Newbie Zone.
This forum exists to allow EverQuest players to seek advice from other players regarding gameplay in EverQuest.

Examples of permitted discussions include:

- Best place to go for a given level.
- Fun quests to do.
- Discussion of EQCLIENT.INI settings
- Creatures to pursue and/or avoid.
- Strategy for overcoming areas.
- Other things that can be considered player-to-player issues.

This forum will not be read by any official personnel with exception to the board moderator, who will be responsible for maintaining order within the forum. Issues noted within this forum will not be forwarded to the Design, Customer Service, or Management Teams.

This forum is NOT for the following:

- Discussion of any in-game design issues or gameplay-related issues such as spawning, drops, quests, class balance, etc., except as it relates to informative rather than negative posts meant purely for other players of a “newbie”. If you wish to discuss these issues beyond this scope with other players, you’ll need to seek out a private board. If you merely wish to bring something to the attention of the design team, please go here http://www.station.sony.com/bb/dev_submit.jhtml to submit a comment.
- Discussion or reporting of in-game design-based bugs (e.g. Quest XYZ doesn’t work). If you wish to report in-game design-based bugs, please go here http://www.station.sony.com/bb/qa_submit.jhtml .
- Any message addressed explicitly or implicitly to, or posted ostensibly for the benefit of, the EverQuest Design, Customer Service, or Management Teams.
- Posting of issues or disagreements with in-game customer service personnel. Players wishing to report an issue to customer service management should email eqmail@verant.com.
- Commentary on the message-board rules or inquiries into why a particular post may have been deleted or locked. Players with questions or comments on these rules should refer their comments here http://www.station.sony.com/bb/dev_submit.jhtml .

We’re committed to attempting to bring back this forum as many people found it useful, but we cannot allow it to become another “general discussion” or “gameplay” forum. The rules here will be very strictly enforced. Violation of the rules can result in anything from a notation upon the game account, to suspension, to banishment from EverQuest and any game released by SOE in the future.

Please note that “warnings” are only appropriate when it is possible that the person committing an infraction did not know that what they were doing was inappropriate. As such, the first step in addressing problems on this board will more often than not be immediate suspension for a period of 7-10 business days.

We appreciate your assistance in maintaining this forum.

Thank you.

Addendum (added 1-15-03):

Things not to do that might get your post deleted or edited. These are things that are considered general rules of etiquette in most forums, so they should be common sense. But we’ll outline them just in case.

- Bumping a thread just to keep it on the top of the list. If the thread isn’t interesting enough to warrant conversation and remain near the top on its own, then please let it fade away. Repeatedly bumping threads, especially your own, may lead to warnings.

- Repeating posts on the same topic. If there is already a post on a similar topic, please reply to that post rather than making a new one. If the moderators see multiple posts on the same topic, they may delete those with only a few replies. We do this to try to keep the discussions in one spot for ease of conversation and to help keep the boards a little neater.

- Spam. Any form of spam (repeatedly posting new threads) will be deleted. Repeated spamming may lead to warnings.

- USING CAPS. Over use of capital letters is commonly referred to as yelling in text on the Internet. Use of such methods to gain attention to your post in the subject may mean deletion of the post. Repeated use of such tactics may lead to a warning.

- Links. It isn’t possible for us to monitor all links posted in this forum. Therefore, if a link isn’t immediately familiar to the moderators it may be edited out, or the post may be deleted. We will endeavor to check links whenever possible to prevent this. But the risk of links leading to unsavory or even dangerous (for your computer) locations isn’t one we want to take. So please use the ability to create links in your posts wisely. Links to nasty sites will earn a suspension.

- Posts about servers being up or down. We're all frustrated when servers are down. But there is no value to posting about it on this forum. Posts ranting about the servers being down will be deleted. So will posts ranting about people ranting about servers being down.

- Post complaining that others are 'whining' too much. The irony involved in making such a post is just too much for us to handle, and such posts will be deleted.

NOTE: If you post a reply to a thread that breaks one of these rules, you probably shouldn't complain if your comments are lost when the thread is deleted.

ALSO NOTE: We have disabled the ability for users to edit posts. Obviously this is a useful feature. But we have disabled it because it would allow unsavory types to post things that are offensive to others and remove them before a moderator can take action. So it might be useful to write up posts in a word processing program before posting.
