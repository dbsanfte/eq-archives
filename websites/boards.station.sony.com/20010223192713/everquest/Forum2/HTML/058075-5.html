Well, I agree with Grimstaff about Wizards do not suck. I also went through a wizard depression about the effect and abilities of the class I had chosen to play. I also heard in Grimstaff's post about us wizards not having to buff. Aye, this allows us to be more focused in the combat portion of battle, but it also lends to the fact that we are not wanted in pickup groups.

When you are in a group, you are expecting all party members in the group to lend some sort of help in the upcoming battles. Each and every member has some way of helping in the group that they are in that is specific to their class. A cleric heals, a shaman buffs, etc. Our role in the group is nuking. It is our one and only ability. The problem with this ability is it has no way of telling the group that you are doing anything. All they see is, "some_poor_sob01 is bathed in a torrent of fire". There are no quantifiable evidence that the wizard did anything. For all the group knows that downward swing in the life bar of the mob could have been the culmative damage of all attacks by all players in that group. The wizard could have done full damage or he could have done 1 point of damage. How do they know? They surely know when the spell is resisted fully due to there being no spell effect text at all. So pretty much, you will tell them when we are resisted(failed), but you do not tell them how werll you did when you suceeded. This lend to the next point of contention, resists.

First off, I see the need for resists. You are not going to get a full damage nuke on every mob all of the time. Just like a warrior is not going to hit for full on every blow that he lands. But, I as a 52 wizard, have nuked mobs in a newbie zone and had multiple full resists on the same mob. First couple of times this happened, I chaulked it up to being unlucky and shrugged it off. The subsequent times I have had this happen it just makes me mad. I know, I know, NPC follow a different set of rules than PCs and they get more of a chance to live than we do, it has been stated endless amounts of times, but when a lvl 52 PC attacks a lvl 3 mob I do not think that these rules should apply. If you are going to make it so casters cannot hit a mob that is higher than them then you should inbversly make it so lower lvl mobs have less of a chance to resist us too. Not in a mediocre way either, it should be quite significant.

Larger mana pool. I agree with most of the board on this one. I would like to have one but without adding to the exorbant med time penalty that would come with it. rather than make our mana pool bigger, the damage we do accomplish should be in better chucks for the same mana. This is all we do, we have no other way of doing damage at this point in the game. This also covers the mana efficiency portion of the debate.

Make us group friendly. This is by and large my biggest gripe. Unless I bring my own group with me(consisting of friends and guild members) it is almost impossible to get a pick up group. No one want's us unless it is a major raid on a high level mob and it needs to be taken down quickly. With the Velious mobs this is even made more apparent as the amount of damage I can do to them is negligible compared to the melee damage that can be done in the same time. I would like to see some sort of group friendly spells added to our line up, something new, something that will make us wanted in groups. My suggestion (as well as a couple of others on the boards) was to give us the ability to add procs to the melee damage. That way we would be contributing even while we are sitting on our collective arses. It would give us face time so to speak. I am sure that there are other ways to do this, but in my thinking this would be the most direct way of adding value to our class. Just think of the possibilities if you put a high lvl wizard, a shaman and a warrior in a group.

Bottom line is, the last problem in our class is the one that should be looked at first. Verant, you have stated that you frown upon soloing, but since I created my character that is all I have mostly done. I am tired of being the lone wizard that only gets called upon when a large raid is going on. I want to be considered valuable in a xp group. I would like to be able to walk into Karnor's and shout for a group and actually find one.
