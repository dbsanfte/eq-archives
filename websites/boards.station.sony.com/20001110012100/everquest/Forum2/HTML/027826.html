There are multiple parts to this post but I think if you read it you will be excited about the idea and hopefully with your Support Verant might even start to implement something like this. Please comment.

Let's start off with a review of the general type events that seem to cause so much unhappiness. GM spawns ultra high level mob in a low-mid level zone, which then either of it's own will or the GMs comences to wipe out all the low-mid level chars. Eventually word spreads and Uber Guilds/Chars show up and kill the Uber Mob, get some Uber or not-so-Uber loot. Meanwhile the people who were in the zone are busy recovering their equipment off corpses, and if lucky gettin xp rezzes.

Good/Bad points of this type event. Good First:
Introduces a random risk factor
Allows players exposure to mobs they may not otherwise see for quite some time
Breaks the Monotony of hack/slash/med/camp routine

Bad Points:
Little to no creativity involved
High level players take the Reward, low-mid take the brunt of the Risk
Little to no story involved
Lots of upset customers

How can this situation be fixed? Suggestions to follow with an example to tie it all up:

Sciek
Station Member 		posted 11-01-2000 10:57 AM           
Implement a Town Crier in each City. Perhaps have him sell newspapers. The way things are now, if you don't spend a bit of time on these boards, OUT OF THE GAME, you'll probably rarely have a clue when a GM event is about to happen. Newspaper IN-GAME could be used to post articles pertaining to the city the paper is sold in and/or norrath wide events. The Town Crier could randomly shout headlines every minute or so to the ppl in the city as a teaser to get them to buy a paper and as information to the players.

Solicit Quest/Event ideas from the players. Perhaps this would be best handled outside the game on Harpy's Head or something similar. Seen this on Many MUDs and works well. Players submit a story for an event/quest by posting on a specific board. Someone/team reads these ideas and as interesting/feasible ideas are found the ideas are made into actual events. To avoid having people come up with puzzle/riddle quests and then giving spoiler info out to their friends/guilds only a general theme would be posted/used. Specific mobs/items required to successfully complete the quest/event would have to be chosen by VI staff.

Now, how does it all go together you ask? Like this:

Following article runs in the paper for a few days:
Headline: Goblin Unrest Grows Throughout Kunark!

Sneagy Whisperfoot reports after scouting the Frontier Mountains and several surrounding areas, that the Goblins from the Mines of Nurga, Temple Of Droga, Lake of Ill Omen and perhaps beyond seem to be collaborating to achieve some diabolical ends. Messages intercepted after the slaying/capture of Several Goblin Scouts Indicate the Chief Rukgus, Lord Gorelik and several other hig ranking Goblins of the several Kunark Clans have grown tired of the constant raids upon their homelands and are planning some sort of unified action to exact their revenge.

After that another article posts the following:
Headline: New Reports on Goblin Unrest reveal shocking plan!

A message from Chief Ruckgus to Lord Gorelik intercepted today indicates that the goblin clans are readying their best warlords and legions of warriors for an assault on Cabilis as revenge for the injustices they have suffered. The message also referred to some outside help that would be standing by if needed to assist the Goblins.

Now this is how it all goes down: The evening of the event is marked by abnormally large amounts of Goblins forming up at the entrances to the Goblin Caves in Lake of Ill Omen. Just after dark on a rainy night(GM induced if possible) under cover of the diminished visibility a fairly large horde of Goblin Warriors, Warlords, and a few Whipcrackers along with various others head out from the Goblin Caves in Lake of Ill Omen. Slowly at first, until sighted then all hell breaks loose as the hordes attempt to work their way to Cabilis Slowly... Slowly because they are meant to lose. This is where the hint at outside help comes in, as the Warriors and Warlords are finally slaughtered, a few Bloodgills come to their aid from the lake, also a few of the Higher level Goblins pop into the fray to help turn the tide, as the tide inevitably begins to turn against the new horde, Lord Gorelik, Chief Rukgus, and a few other high level gobs come out of the Goblin caves surrounded by an elite guard of level 40ish guards and make a last stand. Inevitably the Goblins are slain after several hours of battle. Over the course of which, mid levels 20-35 or so have been taking out packs of Warriors through Whipcrackers and finding a few slightly nicer drops than normal, the higher levels 40s-50s are occupied with the Bloodgills and finally the named ones and as well find some better loot than normal, perhaps forced load of mithril staff along with the best loot to be gotten off the other named mobs. GMs observing the events could hand out a couple uber items to those few observed by them to have been instrumental in repelling the attack.

End result hopefully: Lots of people of varying levels participating in a major GM Event with MANY people gettin small rewards and a few getting somewhat heroic rewards. Deaths inevitably happen, but through the town criers/newspaper articles over the preceding week, they can't really cry foul in that adequate warning was given/hinted at least. An actual story unfolded and is now a permanent part of Norrathian History the outcome of which wa decided by the Players. Perhaps following such an event a new book could be found on some of the merchants describing the events and naming some of the noteable Players involved.

I am not an expert but here are my suggestions for server wide events for certain Holidays like Halloween:
1.) Offer a program for people to volunteer not to be a “Guide” but to be an “Event Official”. This would allow people to help run an event without giving them special abilities. Offer them an item that is fun but doesn’t impact game play (i.e. a Lore, No Drop helm that looks cool but has no stats). Use these high level characters to assist the GMs or Guides in running scavenger hunts, races, games of hide and seek and the like or give them the a part in a script and let them role-play to further the story along. I would be very interested in doing this myself.
2.) A very high level mob placed in a newbie zone really promotes high zone populations. Instead of putting things like a Dragon in the Lake of Ill Omen where anyone who has the Kunark expansion can reach. How about spawning some ultra rare MOBs in the higher zones so that the High level guilds can go there and play without having to worry about massive lag. These MOBs could be spawned during special event days only.
3.) Don’t be as aggressive in moving very high level spawns to mid level zones. (e.g. A Dragon in Frontier Mountain or and invasion of 10th lvl skeletons in West Freeport, maybe Emperor Crush running through Kelethin…) this would keep the mid level player occupied without causing their immediate death when some 55+ Dragon is dropped into the zone they are hunting in. If these spawns were timed properly it would have the effect of moving the population in waves into the next lower “Level” of zones without massive spikes of 200 players all trying to get into one zone.
Imagine this scenario, Emperor Crush appears in the zone near the POD in Kelethin and begins attacking players a call for help goes out and people in zone run to help, a minute or two later Dark Elves from Lesser Faydark appear in Crushbone, then drop some giants spawn in Lake of Ill Omen and after a minute or two a dragon into FM.
If something strange happened in every zone there would be no need for people to zone to another zone to see what was going on.
4.) Put a Jack-O-Lantern next to all of the PODs. Set them to sell candy or give a Lore item when you say “Trick or Treat”. You collect these from every Jack-O-Lantern on the server and turn them all in to the King Scarecrow to complete the quest.

If the number of events are increased and spread across the server in location, frequency and level there would be less clumping of players in zones. This would also reduce the amount of spectators (which causes lag) since there would be something else to do other than just watch a bunch of high level players try to slay a high level MOB so that they can get their corpse.

I love the type of event Sciek mentioned. How about this as an idea of how to implement it?

At the time the event is ready to start, the GM or Event Manager controlling it look at who is in the zone level-wise and set the number/strength of goblins appropriately. Then the zone is locked so that nobody can zone in, but anyone can zone out if they wish. People trying to zone in from adjacent zones are ported to a 'safe spot' within the zone they were leaving and any agro list they are on is cleared (in case they were trying to avoid a train).

The event starts and runs as you described, but no high levels are able to zone in to ruin the event, or crash the zone. Players who do not wish to participate can zone out if they want. Anyone who dies in the event loses no experience, even if it's a normal mob that kills them, and after they respawn, get summoned back into the zone so they can continue to participate (in case they are bound outside the zone)

Then, and this is the important point, *everyone* who participates in the event gets some small reward, even if they just died and /q out to keep from multiple deaths. The GMs should have a list of everyone who was in the zone when the event started, and can keep track of who participates in the event fairly easily I would think. Or, how about every goblin that is part of this event drops a NODROP/LORE ear, or claw or something. That item can be turned into a GM-controlled NPC for a reward later.

Certain higher-value loot would be handed out at the GMs discretion based on people they saw taking extraordinary measures to roleplay the event or something.

On Cazic, most of the loot from Faydedar was ninja-looted by people who couldn't even use it, the loot from the Scarecrows went to people who weren't as big a part of the event as other players, etc.

Just an idea.

Events really need 3 things.

1) A story
2) A warning
3) A chance

They refused all conversation and slaughtered anyone coming close. Some 50s arrived and everyone went into kill mode.
