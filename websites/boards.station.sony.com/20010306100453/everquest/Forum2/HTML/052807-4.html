The point of these talks are overall class balancing. I think we need to make a point of defining the class before balancing it against other classes. A paladin is a cleric/warrior. A Ranger is a Druid/warrior. A shadow knight is a necro/warrior. These are easy distinctions and the necro does not complain that the sk can darkness. Nor does the Cleric complain that the Pally can cast symbol. So what is a bard? I think a bard is overall an Enchanter/Rogue though not as clear cut as the others, obviously. Many Bard songs have been nerfed or just never have worked properly because of bards stepping on enchanter toes. Why is this even an issue? If bards are part enchanter just like rangers are part druid then they need to be balanced as such. Enchanters complaining because their armored version shares a handful of the same skills should not be listened to anymore than clerics complaining that Paladins can revive. If you take away the enchanter part of the bard then you are left with a very bad rogue with a couple of useful songs but not many. Instead, strengthen the enchanter part of the bard. The ability to AE mez should be given back to the bard in higher levels and please please please do not take away the enchanter abilities they do have when you balance. And do NOT take away any illusion items that drop, you will only be removing the fun of a very complicated and misunderstood class.

Many have also stated in these threads that bards should be able to tank more. I strongly disagree. Please define the bard and then have integrity when you balance. If bards are enchanter/rogues then taking damage is not their job. Some can make an argument for dealing damage with this definition, and I will let them do it. But above this hybrid definition, a bard has the distinction of having songs and instruments. Strengthen these tools. I will not repeat the posts that discuss all of the songs that are not increased by instruments where they should. But it is obvious that a lot of details have been ignored in completing the bard class in favor of fixing other parts of the game. But now is the time to work on the details. Fix the songs that don't work, have instruments increase songs where they should and make available instruments at the midlevels. Keep the class complicated, we like the challenge -- just realize that you are working with a flawed class that needs to be fixed before it can be "balanced".

Ok... I don't pipe up too much but here goes. I have put a lot of thought into this but I'm sure there are more then a few holes in my ideas. In general addressing overall balance for the bard here in the high level game. There are a some bugs (ac, song messages, song effects) that have been laid out very nicely by some of my bretheren here and I didn't want ot get into those.

"Level 55 and 110 days played... but hey I'm a master fisher!"

1) We don't use mana (in general) TIME is our mana. Seeing as a 'average' bard weaves 3 songs, the time for each song is 1/3 of our 'mana.' It seems many people sometimes makes the mistake of looking at a song by itself and not as each song/effect takes 1/3 of our 'mana.'
-a. In relation to songs this means that to balance a song you have look at it in the context of a weave. For instance weaving level 55 mana song, 47 Nivs, 32 mana song is roughly 45hp a tick, clarity1, clarity2, and STA regen. All that for no mana sounds great. Now combine all that, give it as a single spell to a cleric that costs no mana to cast, lasts 3 ticks. But ALL other buffs they have cast on anyone fade halfway through the first pulse.
-b. In relation to items the cast time must be based on the effect in how much 'mana' (time) it takes from us. One solution in my opinion is to make pieces that cast songs we would normally weave with the same cast time as a regular song. Little if any balance problems and efectively allow us to have a larger selection of songs up at once. Bards have no 'down' time. If a group member needs water or food it takes so long to cast it (time with mana song off) that it's all but unusable even in down time in a group.

2. Balancing high level crowd control. Song of Twilight has done great things for us in its current form. Now we want a low resist aoe mez to balance us at high levels as a lesser but viable replacement to an enchanter. Verdant is rightfully worried about the abuse of a manaless aoe mez.
-a. Prefered solution: Compromise with a level 50 or 52 aoe mez (levels chosen because other hybrids got new spells at 50 and 52 is the only post-50 level we get one song) with low to no resist and a duration just slightly longer then Song of Twilight. Make the song a 3 minute recast. This gives a bard the time to mez a pull and start locking down as many mobs as he can with the shorter duration Song of Twilight. The breathing space would allow bard to more effectively utilize Song of Twilight even with poor lag/video card that makes targeting hard with all the mobs moving but not increase the number of mobs we can keep locked down overall. Also with a 3 minute refresh and a 20 second duration it would take at least 10 bards (acounting for a little overlap of mez casting) with great timing to work in tandem to lock down a group of mobs indefinately for no mana. Any server with 10 bards level 53 or higher that can coordinate like that would not evenneed the song

3. Balancing damage output in the 51-60 game. Melee classes (including us to a lesser extent) get new damage tables in these levels providing a drastic increase to damage for pure melee. Casters get better ratio damage to mana spells and a larger pool of mana to draw from. As a hybrid we righfully get a smaller increase to melee tables. Unfortunately as time is our 'mana' the increasing mana to damge scale for casters (and as is appropriate to hybrids to a lesser extent) does not apply to us. And the increase to hybrid mana pools did nothing for us in terms of damge we can inflict through songs.
-a. Complicated fix: Rescale our songs at 51, 55, and 60. Example, Chant of Frost does 1+(level/2) damage for a total of 26 at level 51, 28 at level 55, and 31 at level 60. Change the base formula so for bards level 51-54 the bards level is multiplied by 1.2 then the effect for any level based song is resolved. Add a 1.4 multiplier at 55-59, and a 1.5 multiplier at level 60. Now we get the following numbers for Chant of Frost, 31 at level 51, 39 at level 51, and 46 at level 60. An end result of a increase of 50% to damage output by level 60 without increasing our avalible 'mana' (time). An approprate halfway increase to one side of our damage output and balancing us with other hybrids that recieve melee AND spell increases (better cast ratio/more mana) post 50.
-b. Easist but less effective fix: Make all songs level 51-60 last 4 ticks. A bard would then be balanced with a portion of the melee increase other hybrids get post 50 and by making a song last 4 ticks we can have 4 songs not 3 up at once effectively increasing our 'mana' (time) by 33%. In order for this method to be truely effective our level 51-60 songs need to be tuned and made more useful as this solution does no good if the level 51-60 songs are less useful then the sub-50 songs.
-c. A possible happy medium. Increase the duration of all 3 tick songs to 4 ticks when a bard reaches level 51. Same benefit as above while removing some of the need to retune high level songs to make them something a bard will sing more.

4. Balancing our snare: Our current single target snare songs (Selo's Constant Chain-23 and Largo`s Absonant Binding-51) have a random duration. In order for a bard to have a mob reliably snared he must sing one of the two snare songs non-stop as it could break as fast as 1 second after sticking. By refering back to point one you can see that this effectively causes a bard to use all his 'mana' (time) to provide the same ability to stop a mob fleeing that a druid can provide with a 15 mana snare. Once again not asking to be as good as a pure caster but using all of our 'mana' to snare a mob seems unreasonable.
-a. Solution: Make the level 26 and 51 snare songs have a fixed duration of 3 ticks. Even with no resists a bard will be using 1/3 his total 'mana' (time) to emulate a spell that others can cast for 15 mana. Due to the very real posibility of multiple resists with this song the bard is still not as good as snaring classes but we will once again be a viable, if lesser, alternative when no other snaring class is avalible.
