Great work Absor!! Bearing in mind that you've had to filter & compile such lists for all the other classes, you deserve a medal! Whatever the development team do with the list, you'll always have my respect for the amount of work that you've obviously put into this.

My 55 bard is into her 2-month countdown to oblivion but this list gives me hope that I might take up EQ again & bring her back to Norrath.

Having said that, however, I do have a few points to add and contest (typical huh? )

1) Drum-playing & casting animations
Personally, I think these should have low priority. They have nothing to do with class balance and I actually like the mix of animations for songs. As for the drum, I read somewhere, long ago, that this was difficult to implement properly. As a compromise, why not swap the flute for a tambourine? Nostrolo's Tambourine looks fine when strummed (IMO)

2) Increasing the damage from our damage songs from 50 to 60 to keep in line with other spells would be great. However, it will be uselss unless the resist rate is lowered also. I last tried using the 3 chants at level 52 or 53 (heck, we still have to try soloing, even at that level ) and the resist rate was usually 100%, else very close to it.

3) Song effects on items
Firstly, there aren't "tons" (however many that is ) of battle-useful song effects. I can only think of Niv's on Breath of Harmony and the epic song effect (but then, I'm not a walking EQ item catalogue). Additional songs such as those on Singing Steel BP (Travel Song) aren't going to unbalance the game, however many there are.
Secondly, they are only available at higher levels because of the minimum level required to use them. As things currently stand, post-50 bards need all the help they can get.

4) Kelin's Lucid Lullaby
Re. this song's redundancy after about level 30, you say "....if this is intentional then that's fine" No it's not! Not unless we get an upgrade at higher levels. Song of Twilight is spot-on now, until the need to pin-down 3 or 4 mobs coincides with the ubiquitous lag-spike. We need an AE mez, even if it's only of short duration, to recover from such situations. As it stands, bards are more liability than asset when it comes to crowd control at higher levels.

5) Song Twisting
Just to say that introducing auto-twist in increments seems to be a great idea. Manually twisting at lower levels requires skill. To keep it going for 8 hours plus on a long raid at higher levels requires a brain-dead player with forearms like Popeye (anchor tatooes help but are not necessary). Plus it becomes very anti-social and many of us play bards because we wanted (in the beginning) to be sociable.

6) Fulfil's Curtailing Chant
I think your description of Fulfil's Curtailing Chant is incorrect. Correct me if I am wrong, anyone, but Fulfil's Curtailing Chant is only a DoT, despite the fact that some lists also claim that it also lowers magic resistance. Having it lower MR would keep it in line with the other chants, however.

7) New songs cancelling old songs
"...this is annoying the heck out of bards." No kidding!! This bug alone will stop me from playing my bard again. Every time I get the "perfect" twist I am penalised by a bug!

8) Slow/snare songs
I've not seen this mentioned directly here, but it was on the original list. The high level slow/snare songs are just that - slow AND snare. Therefore, if a group needs slow only, we are limited to using Largo's melodic Binding (level 20) which slows without snaring. However, this is an AE song and therefore potentially limited. If a group needs snare only, the bard is uselss as any slow spells will cancel the bard snare & vice versa. We need ONE targeted slow/snare song and separate, targeted, slow and snare songs to increase our usefulness. The 54 song is fine as it is, being great for AE groups.

9) Increase in melee potential
This has been mentioned a few times in this thread, but my *personal* opinion is that bards are about their songs, not melee. From a balance point of view, our melee skills should just a be a bonus to our songs, assuming we are not using instruments. Certainly at higher levels (from my level 55 perspective), some form of taunt would probably be welcomed, as our songs just don't have the same aggro effect that they had at lower levels and we can take much more damage.

10) Post level-50 role
The list doesn't really address our non-existent role post-level 50 (especially post-54). Making haste songs stackable won't have a great effect when any decent group's tanks are already maxed from a combination of items and enchanter haste.
