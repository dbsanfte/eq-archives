Downside to wizards:

- Roots. Enchanters have that. They are unpredictable and nearly impossible to get off while being beat on.

Sinneed - I appreciate your comments. A year ago while I was in my 20s I probably would have thought,

'yeah, see, someone out there knows that a wizard really is a great class… too many whiners out there'

...if I had read your posts back then that is.

But now, in my mid-40s I just can't agree with you, despite the fact that I’d really like for you to be correct in your views.

I suppose now that I disagree with you, I will most likely be relegated by you as, 'one of those players who can't play a wizard right.' Oh well, perhaps I do still have something to learn about the class...

But, I don't believe that the wizard population, as represented by the many posts on this thread, are suffering from some kind of mass delusion. I think there is a lot of truth to the criticisms pointed out on these threads about our class.

Two fundamental issues:

BAD PRESS
Perhaps wizards are suffering from ‘bad press.’ But if ‘bad press’ is contributing to the problems wizards are having finding a group, you have to wonder why and how that bad press all started. Perhaps it started from some players, that no matter what the capabilities of the wizard are, will always whine. Perhaps it started from insightful players having played the class to the higher levels.

My take on it is that the easily-to-whine players ‘felt,’ early on in EQs life, that something was not quite right with the class. However, through there inability to really flesh-out their arguments, they were perceived mostly as whiners. Later on, these ‘feelings’ were validated by a few very well articulated players that reached the higher levels. Unfortunately by then, any well-construed argument regarding the wizard class was summarily debunked by Verant as just more whining. Now the ‘bad press’ issue is somewhat of a vicious circle made up of both false perceptions, whining, as well as quite a few valid criticisms.

WIZARD CONCEPT
It’s almost a knee-jerk reaction to accept the fantasy notion that a wizard is physically weak. But if able to achieve great knowledge through many hardships and failures, can ultimately become a very powerful being – one that makes the gods pay attention, one who is subtle, quick to anger, and confronts foes with powerful contempt.

I can’t help but believe that Verant, early on, accepted this notion, at least in part, during the development and early deployment of the game – whether they were cognitive about it or not.

As the game evolved however, it became a logical goal to ‘balance’ the classes so that each type of player possessed ‘relative’ power to all the other classes. This, however, is the anti-thesis of the fantasy notion-defining wizards. This issue, to me, is the core problem (at a macro level) regarding the wizard class in EQ.

Wizards start out weak and frail. By level 60, we arguably are less powerful relative to any other class in the game. Why is that? Why is it that when every casting skill is maxed out, wizards spells cast slower, recast slower, get resisted more, fizzle more, get interrupted more than ever before? Why should my level 4 gate collapse EVER at level 45, when standing perfectly still? It just doesn’t make any sense.

Verant is stuck between a rock and a hard place because their initial premise regarding wizards was based on existing fantasy concepts. But their ‘world’ has a vision of its own that cannot mutate this class from existing fantasy into a form workable in the EQ realm that won’t completely unbalance everything.

CONCLUSION
I don’t know what the answer is. I do know that I’m frustrated for having played a wizard so long. I really wish I had chosen to play a different class but I didn’t. I do know that quick and easy fixes will not resolve the fundamental problems with this class – in fact, if Verant were to make Wizards viable, the person or people behind such changes should be highly commended for whatever insights they would have come up with to achieve such viability.

As it stands now, I’ll continue playing my wizard, because I just don’t have enough interest in the game to start a new character from scratch – especially with so many new games looming on the horizon. I’m just gonna play this wizard until another game captures my interest and my time.
