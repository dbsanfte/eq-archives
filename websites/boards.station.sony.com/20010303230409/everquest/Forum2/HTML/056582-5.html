a few more things:

1. I see a lot of people saying that disarm/sense traps is worthless because there are no traps. While this is incorrect (they obviously just haven't been to the right zones), I would like to add a caveat. It only makes sense that a rogue would disarm a trap for more than 30 seconds if he bothered to do so at all.

1. Sense Traps: Extend the range to 'see' them. Make it 75-100 feet, -or- even better, have sense traps work like sneak (in that it's mashed down) until you let up on it, and have it reduce your movement in the same fashion that sneak does (again, can move half rate up to 50, then an additional 5% per level to 60, where you can move at full movement and still be sensing traps), and then when you get within your 'sense' range, you get a warning when there is a trap near.

2. Disarm traps: I can't begin to describe how tedious it is to stand around disarming traps while people run by, or while you sit on a spot with a trap. When a rogue disarms a trap, it should destroy the trap (forcing the trap to wait for a respawn, perhaps 20-30 mins). This does 2 things: reduces the tedium of hitting disarm trap every 30 seconds, and forces a rogue to be more "smart" about his disarming (ie: once a trap is found, he can't just sit around and disarm it, he has to be on his toes, and figure out trap respawn times, etc.)

3. Throwing: The only thing here would be range. I'd really like to see the Mrylo (or assassin's) bracer pick up a spell that summons daggers with a longer range, or perhaps make a quest for a container/pouch that summons daggers (probably not possible, as a right click on a container opens it, and wouldn't cast a spell, it could just be an item with a pouch icon. *shrug*) I see a lot of people asking throwing to do more damage, or backstab damage. Please *don't* do this. For those of us who pull as rogues, this would be unbelievably harmful (try to imagine pulling a mob that summons if you just did enough damage to make it start summoning with a pulling throw...)

4. Hide/Sneak: For the love of Bristlebane, make these two skills work across zones. You have no idea how stupid it feels to ask for an invis when you need to cross into siren's grotto. I would also love it if the hide/sneak speed increased underwater as you leveled as well, but hey, I can suffer at "swimming" at 2 ft per minute i guess... It would also be nice if hide/sneak worked *better* than the camo/sneak combo that rangers/halfling druids have. Something along the lines of mobs with "see invis" capability only have a 50% chance to see a rogue that's hiding/sneaking.

5. Pick Pocket: You know, I'm not seeing how unbalancing it would be to have rogues be able to pick magic items off of mobs that are of the appropriate level. It would return some of the ability to be roguish. As I recall, this "fix" was originally put in to keep rogues from walking up to nag/vox and stealing "uber" items from them. With the level cap on PP, it's not possible to pick uber mobs anyway. Perhaps put in a flag that doesn't allow a rogue to pick no drop items, but allows them the ability (however slight) to pick items that are magical/lore). I believe Bilbo stole items out of Smogg's lair, did he not? *wicked grin*

6. Evade: Do what you gotta do, I'm tired of arguing. Just don't be surprised when you see a lot of dead rogues, and a lot more whining here. In any group, i can easily climb back to the top of the hate list before hide recycles. Rogues currently bring 1 thing to a group: damage. Seems silly to remove that from them, eh?

I hope Absor makes it down this far, and doesn't abandon the thread as a result of some of the inflammatory posts which seem to be sprinkled through it.

There are a few inconsistencies I've seen on the board, and I wanted to try and clarify them, in addition to summing up what I think would help the class. Most of it will have been seen before, so I apologize if I'm being repetitive, but I think it's worth it, in order to make sure there are no misunderstandings.

As far as evade goes, my empirical data is limited to the moderate and low levels of play, as my rogue is not quite 30th. I do hope that the testing groups responsible for checking evade's effectiveness will have done so at a wide range of levels, however. Evade works so seldomly for me that it's almost useless, but at my level it is not so important, as the mobs I am fighting are not yet to the point of being able to kill me in under 10 hits. I'll reiterate other rogues' points however: I soloed my way to 23rd level, (blech), and used evade constantly for most of it (on the off-chance that it would increase, like a skill) and never ever had a mob decide to chase off after someone else. This includes times where I even forgot to turn auto-attack back on! So essentially we had a 20th level rogue standing toe-to-toe with a mob, hitting evade every time it came up, and not attacking, and yet the mob still stayed on me. Again, at my level, it does not matter so much, but the problem of evade scales with level; at higher levels, if a rogue is unable to evade, they are dead. I know that your plans for evade will likely be implemented regardless, but wanted to voice my opinion on it.

Now to the meat of this post, a summary of the requests I found worth discussing:

(1) Stackable poisons. As mentioned earlier, the important thing to realize here is that we are not asking for the ability to apply multiple poisons to a weapon... we are asking for the ability to have our manufacture poisons stack in our inventory. Poison has enough negative aspects, (hunting for every ingredient, a one-shot 'whee' factor, etc.), that simply carrying the results of your labor should not be so difficult. This was argued before with alchemy, and eventually the bearskin potion bag was put in, and potions were allowed to have charges, if not actually stack. I won't bother mentioning the 'bandage-auto-kneel' aspect of applying poison, because I think you already got that point, and it may end up implemented.

(2) Hide/Sneak across zones, when link dead, and when camping out. While some no doubt would disagree, I feel that hide and sneak is one of our class' defining characteristics; we are the only class that can be 'invis and invis to undead' at the same time. There are enough mobs at the higher levels which see through invis that the skills are balanced; I don't feel adding a convenience factor like persistence through logouts and zones will alter that balance. Currently, if a rogue is concerned about zoning into a dangerous area... he gets an invis, so as to not die as soon as the new area loads and his sneak/hide is gone.

(3) An excellent point regarding stats was mentioned earlier. I know the chances of something like this happening are roughly one in a thousand, but, given that you all have made some pretty sweeping changes to things that 'would never ever change', (like experience), I hope that you'll at least consider it: re-evaluate stats. A number of people have made half-elf, wood elf, halfling etc. rogues, because those races fit the traditional ideal of a rogue. Even looking at Verant's concept of a rogue, it seems like a more agile and dexterous character would be best suited for the class. However, when it comes to combat, the only thing that really matters for a rogue is strength. Allowing a barbarian to backstab for more damage (due to higher innate str) is fine, and even logical, but there should be a tradeoff inherent in choosing strength over agility and/or dexterity. Please make these other two stats more useful! I don't really care how it's done, (but just saying 'dex already affects procs' is insufficient), but I feel that these two stats very much fit the swashbuckler image that Verant has crafted for the rogue, and therefore they should figure far more heavily, both in combat (backstab, evade) and utility skills (poison, traps, etc). We cannot all be barbarians and dwarves, and, like everything in Everquest, there should be pros and cons for every choice (you want to be a dwarf rogue? Fine, your high str and dex are invaluable, but your low agility means you won't do XXX as well as a more agile rogue... you want to be a wood elf rogue? Fine... your agility is very helpful, but your backstabs will never be quite as powerful as a barbarian's until you max out your str with stat equipment...).

I'm not looking for new commands or skills, or for the ability for a rogue to solo effectively, (I now exclusively group). I'm just looking for refinement of our current skills, and perhaps a re-evaluation of what role stats play in a rogue's existence.

Thanks,
Skaal
