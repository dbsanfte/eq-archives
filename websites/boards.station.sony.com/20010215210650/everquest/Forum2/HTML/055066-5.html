OK i have to admit that I havent read many of the posts here. But I've been thinking about the issue of balance. Balance is something that is hard to measure, I think the easiest way to to compare 2 classes. The problem with this is that it can be perceived as "class envy," or it may be that the other class is overpowered. But in any case, I dont see a better way but to do a comparisson. I think the most obvious one is between wizards and druids. I choose druids as they are the only other class than can teleport.

So that said lets look at some areas of each class

DD spells: Wizards are clearly stronger here, although not by a margin that makes druid dd spells look weak.

AE spells: Again Wizards are clearly stronger here, although not by a margin that makes druid AE spells look bad. The Jyll's line for wizards is excellent and standsout.

Teleports: close, but I think druids get the edge overall. Whatever lets call it a draw for the purpose of trying to make my point.

Heals: heheh not much arguement here. Wiz get none. Im not going to add changes that are being discussed on the test server.

AC/Health Buffs: wiz gets no group buffs at all. Druids get some excellent group buff spells. True wizards get ok self buffs, but that doesnt help finding a group

Resist Buffs: Druids get significantly better buffs here. Wiz gets elemental armor at 44 but thats about it. Also wiz buffs are self only again.

Damage Shield: no contest here. O'keils isnt much use.

Snare: Interesting area. Druids get snare at level 1, wizards get an AE snare at 29th. For some reason druids dont get an AE snare until 50+ but whatever. Wizard version is also very short in duration. Edge druids.

Root: I have to admit my knowledge of druid root spells is poor, but i'd give the edge to wizards here. but then again, casting root is pretty rare when your 50+ and in a group.

Pets: Druids get that bear pet at 50+ (which is very cool btw) wizards dont really get a pet (flaming sword doesnt really count)

Melee skills: Wizards skill caps are significantly lower than those of druids.

Im sure there are areas I havent covered, but I think overall its clear that druids are the more well rounded class. Let me make one thing clear. I play a wizard, and I dont want to heal. I not asking that wizards get all kinds of new abilities. I just dont see that our DD/AE spells are that much better than all of the other abilities that a druids gets such that things are balanced. Dont get me wrong, I wouldnt trade my wiz in for a druid if you offered me the chance.

I do think wizards should get better resist buffs, and make them group friendly. Since we are the masters of fire/cold/magic DD why would it not make sense for us to be the masters of resisting those things?

Beyond that Im not sure what the right fixes are, Im sure there are LOTS of great idea what my fellow wizzys have posted here.

Flame on if you want, I was just thinking about balance outloud. Sorry for any rambling.

I've been thinking about comparing the recent test server changes, and how they parallel the wizard dilemma. Consider:

Warriors take damage only a little better than paladins/shadowknights, but they deal damage much better. Warriors deal less damage than rogues, monks, and rangers, but they take damage much better. So, warriors have rivals for tanking ability, but those rivals don't come close in damage output. And warriors have superiors in damage dealing, but those classes don't come close in damage absorption.

Let's say magical damage is in two areas, too: fast damage (emergencies) and endurance damage (sustained lesser amounts). Wizards do fast damage only a little better than their closest rivals (mages and druids), but they have much better endurance damage.

Ack--no they don't!

The fundamental problem in a nutshell. My brand new suggestions (I would not want to see all of them):

* Rogues have rivals for damage dealing (monks, primarily), but the rogue is better able to deal that damage *safely*, because of evade--what if wizard blasts inherently caused less monster aggression? They would be able to unload earlier on a bad pull, giving them an entirely new advantage over their closest rivals.

* Better endurance damage--enhanced specialization, new highly mana-efficient blasts with a longish recast time (so only one could be cast on a typical pull), or the idea of going "mana-berserk" and having better mana regeneration when at 40% mana (the idea being you could either have a large reserve, OR put out consistent damage)

* Just bigger and badder--general increases in size, speed, and (maybe) mana-efficiency, so that the wizard's closest rivals are less close (my least favorite).

Lastly, my standard closing: Wizards have definite problems, but they're STILL underrated =P

Another balance issue I didn't see mentioned directly is how our spells scale as the levels go up, especially in the 51+ range. Looking at the level 51+ spells, we have the following:

Draught of Fire: Level 51, does 643 damage at level 51 for 215 mana, cast time 3 seconds.

Voltaic Draught: Level 54, does 658 damage at level 54 for 215 mana, cast time 3 seconds.

Draught of Jiva: Level 55, does 663 damage at level 55 for 215 mana, cast time 3 seconds.

Draught of Ice: Level 57, does 716 damage at level 57 for 215 mana, cast time 3 seconds.


Note that the above scale up VERY slowly, and except for draught of ice, increase by only 5 damage per level for what is virtually the same spell. In this time, how many more THOUSANDS of hit points do the mobs get?

The lure line is even worse:

Lure of frost, does 640 damage at level 52 for 320 mana with a casting time of 4.5 seconds.

Lure of flame, does 640 damage at level 55 for 320 mana with a casting time of 4.5 seconds.

Lure of lightning, does 680 damage at level 58

Lure of ice, does 825 damage at level 60 for 320 mana with a casting time of 4.5 seconds.

Look at that, the damage is flat for the lure line, with a minor 40 more at 58, but then a sudden jump of 145 damage at level 60. Still, the mobs increase their hit points significantly over this period, while the damage output is flat.


Now, the following is the final set, the pillar line. I won't worry about the PB AoE spells for this.

Pillar of frost, does 755 damage at level 51 to 4 mobs with a cost of 325 mana in 4.5 seconds.

Pillar of lightning, does 828 damage at level 54 to 4 mobs with a cost of 344 mana in 4.5 seconds.

Pillar of Flame, does 937 damage at level 57 to 4 mobs with a cost of 364 mana in 4.5 seconds.


Note that the pillar line scales up MUCH better than our direct damage spells.

The point blank spells:

Jyll's Static Pulse does 495 damage at level 53 to all in a radius around the caster with a cost of 285 in 2 seconds.

Thunderbold does 600 damage at level 54 to all in a radius around the caster, pushes the mobs back, and stuns for 3 seconds with a cost of 300 mana in 3 seconds.

Jyll's Zephyr of Ice does 594 damage at level 56 to all in a radius around the caster with a cost of 313 mana in 2 seconds.

Jyll's Wave of Heat does 638 damage at level 59 to all in a radius around the caster with a cost of 342 mana in 2 seconds.


There are level 60 spells, but it is far from the same line as these short cast time spells, with high cast times. Level 60 spells are an upgrade of the level 49 spells overall, and should not be lumped in for comparison purposes. Note that the area of effect spells increase their damage PER MOB, more than the DD spells. This is an issue to be looked at. Even then, it is obvious that the increase per level in damage for the point blank spells is NOT consistant with the nice upgrades in the pillar line. Something needs to be adjusted to scale our spells.

Please note that unlike ALL other classes, there is no item that increases our damage output. Focus items are there to improve the pets summoned, and even though rare and difficult to find, their abilities go up notably with them. Nothing for the wizard class to do the same thing. Just pointing out a fact, it's not envy.
