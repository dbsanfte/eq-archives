I just read through Absor's post and the replies to it.

I wanted to clarify some things.

First, it didn't take 6 months and 300,000 people to find the problem. The problem was only made apparent recently, with Velious and the removal of the HP cap. The breastplate didn't appear as a problem until encounters started lasting much longer than they had previously. We made one attempt to address the issue, found out it wasn't nearly enough, and now are faced with having to do this change.

Removal of the HP cap added more variety to the game -- it changed strategies and tactics. No longer were we forced to simply make mobs kill in a very short period of time. Overall, this is a very good thing for the game -- variety and encounters that require differing tactics is good.

That said, whenever we do change or add behavior or other significant aspects of the game, other unforeseen problems often surface. It's the nature of the genre -- these game's change and evolve, and every change, small or large, can have unexpected ripple effects.

A second clarification: less than 5 or so mobs in Velious were created assuming the breastplate would be used, and this was mostly likely in error (i.e. we shouldn't have made that assumption in those cases). We will be addressing those mobs and tuning them down accordingly.

In summary, I'm sorry we goofed up -- it does happen. It will happen again. It's the nature of the beast, but we will always do our best to minimize these occurrences. And we will also do our best to react as fairly as possible when we are forced to act, attempting to do the right thing for both the individual player as well as the game as a whole.

It is unfortunate that the item was created in the first place, given its potential for problems, and it's also unfortunate that we didn't realize how much more magnified that problem would become when the HP cap was lifted.

That said, again, we're not perfect, and we will continue to make mistakes as long as this game continues to grow and evolve. And, no matter how dedicated our testing staff is, or how carefully we watch for problems on test, we will miss things that 300,000+ players will find. I don't see this changing, and I certainly don't think this is something unique to EQ, but rather something inherent to the genre -- no other type of game changes as often and as significantly, nor involves such a huge community of players who both work with and compete with each other.

I know that what I've said probably won't stop the flaming, and I know this is disappointing to both players who have the item as well as those who were planning on obtaining it one day -- I don't expect to post and have happiness follow -- but I did want to at least try to explain the situation in more detail, and to clarify it. And I also think it's important for us to try to manage expectations as they relate to the future.

-Brad

I wanted to add a little more detail and some of my thoughts about ripple affects whenever significant changes are made to a game like this.

When we increased the level cap, there were unforeseen consequences. And so we had to make adjustments. This understandably upset some players -- in fact, any change to the status quo can be upsetting to many.

Now that we've increased the HP cap, there are also some unforeseen consequences. The breastplate is just one -- the perceived problems you've posted about class balance and usefulness as a result of the HP cap removal might may well be other consequences (and we're already starting to debate and discuss them).

Undoubtedly, we'll have to make more adjustments, bringing the game back closer to balance. Some of these changes will probably be negatively perceived by some players, and hopefully others will be positively perceived. But, honestly and bluntly, the short-term happiness or disappointment is secondary in our decision making process. We fix and adjust and balance, especially after changes like this, to ensure the overall health of the game, because we fundamentally believe that our focus on the game's overall health and balance is what brings long term happiness to our playerbase.
The alternative, really, is a static, unevolving game. If we don't try things out that we never planned to do, like increasing the level cap, or the MOB HP cap, then we risk a stagnant game. The genre is still new, and yes, in a sense, EQ will never be out of 'beta'. It's an experiment in many ways, because something like EQ hasn't really been done before -- there are very few games out there even close to EQ in terms of goals and scope.

The same goes for items. We've tried some new item ideas out in an attempt to make the game more interesting and varied. These include this breastplate, the manastone, mistwalker, etc. And yes, unfortunately, these also caused unforeseen consequences.

I submit, however, when considering the big picture and the game as a whole, it's better that we try this stuff than not. I also believe in my heart, all things considered, that EQ is a far better game today than it was 6 months ago, and waaaay better than it was when we launched. And, overall, I know it will continue to improve, and so also will our future games be all the more better given what we've learned, both from our successes AND our failures and mistakes.

I guess what I'm trying to say, or do, is manage expectations better, going forward. Perhaps another MMORPG will come along that never nerfs, never makes mistakes, but still evolves and expands, with a perfect dev and testing team, eager to try new things, but somehow impervious to failure.

But I really doubt it... years of MUDing and game development experience tell me otherwise. And, in any case, until then, we're going to march forward, doing our best, with a strong commitment to continue to both improve EQ and also expand EQ, be it with more content, more options, improved technology, etc.

Heck, we're already talking about some major additions to gameplay (not just new content) that we're going to make after we make a serious stab at getting the classes balanced in the next few months. I can't go into details yet, but I think they'll be great. But I also think, realistically, that we'll face challenges associated with the changes - in any case, though, we are going to continue pushing EQ beyond its original scope and vision.

I appreciate all of you who've stayed with us this long, and I encourage you to stick with us going forward -- we certainly couldn't have done it without you And while I wish some of your expectations were a bit more realistic and forgiving, I'm at the same time very glad we have this board so you all can keep us on our toes -- it's an invaluable resource.

take care,

-Brad
