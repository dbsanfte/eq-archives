Suggested changes:

Evade - There is a problem that when mobs hit you while you are evading, you get the message "You must stand perfectly still to hide!" Since evade/hide are tied to the same button, it makes sense that you can't hide while moving. However, it does not make sense that you can't evade while getting hit [not to mention that the message is completely awkward]. This is especially harmful during extremely laggy periods when mobs hit you and you evade at what seems like a time that the mob didn't hit you, but it actually did [server lag combined with player lag]. This leads to death. And I would like to call this a bug that could/should be looked into. Failing to evade is perfectly acceptable. However, failing to evade because "You must stand perfectly still to hide!" is probably a bug.

Poison - Having non-stackable poisons with one charge, an application time, and only one application per usage is very unproductive. The only use I could find for poisons is when I need a mega taunt for a certain mob [such as when engaging mob that must be riposted and therefore has to be on me for a certain duration]. I suggest making poisons either non-stackable or have multiple charges. Or let us apply two or more doses at a time.

Sense trap/Disarm trap - During my time in Necropolis [disarming nasty beetle/dd traps], I've noticed that sense trap has a HORRIBLE radius. I would walk down a NARROW hallway, sensing traps every 3-4 steps. The recycle time on sense trap takes FOREVER, so I try to take as few steps as possible. When I think it's safe, I call my group members down. However, more often or not, they step on a trap! That's ridiculous. I don't see how that's possible since this is a NARROW hallway and I'm walking down the CENTER every FOUR steps and sensing everytime it pops up. I suggest a larger sense trap radius and a faster recycle time for the sense trap button to refresh.

Pick Lock - This is one of the worse skills to max, and one of the most necessary to max. Some doors require 210 skill. However, just getting to 201 skill takes HOURS of opening/closing door. I've heard of rogues going on 10 hours of opening/closing doors and getting ONE skill check. That's enough to make even diehard gamers go insane I think. Especially when there's other things that can be done besides standing in front of a door and closing/opening it.

Instill Doubt - This skill has too many checks, takes a considerably long time to take effect, and takes a LONG time to get skill checks. Again, another skill that takes forever to bring up with limited uses. I suggest looking at the skill checks for this one and perhaps reducing some checks for a successful ID.

Weapon Variety - The selection of rogue weapons are appealing. However, considering that rogues are limited to low damage weapons [because of backstab], can we see some more rogue only offhand slashers with higher damage outputs to compensate for the low damage primary pierces? 9 damage piercers [and sometimes even 5 damage piercers] can only go so far when warriors are walking around with 25 damage ONE hand weapons in their primary hands. Of course, we can never get a 25 damage weapon in our primary hand, thus making an offhand weapon with a great damage ratio OFFHAND only [make it a slasher or even a blunt too] to compensate our lack of damage without backstab in primary hand would be useful.

Disarm - Disarming weapons is always useful... Well anyways... Off to next topic...

Kick - Why don't rogues have this skill? It's a dirty and mean thing to do to a mob... Kick them when they're down... A very roguely thing in fact. Not much damage, but a little skill to add to our arsenal wouldn't hurt. A little more damage when the mob is beating on us from the front side would be acceptable. Or when we need an extra taunt to keep the mob on us [when that rare chance occurs and we are needed to use riposte/dodge disciplines for uber mob encounters].

Once a rogue attains double backstab at 55th level and a ragebringer, they are in very good shape and contribute very well to any raid or group. However before this point rogues suffer quite a bit. Ragebringer is a 15 damage weapon with a good damage delay ratio and haste to boot. It's quite an excellent partner to double backstab. However, for all the rest of a rogue's career before this ragebringer and double backstab stuff, a rogue is typically using a 10 damage weapon and puts out rather weak backstab performance. Any group that doesn't need a lockpicker, a trap disarmer, or a sneak/hide person (in other words 98% of the time) will look at a rogue's contribution to the group purely in terms of damage. They will find it marginal when a warrior can do almost as much damage plus tank and taunt, a monk can do almost as much and feign pull, a wizard can do about as much as well as snare, stun, and evac, a mage or necro can do more damage and offer other benefits as well. Plus a rogue can outdamage none of these classes without someone to taunt and reliably present a back to stab. If the monster switches back and forth a lot, backstabs are certain to be missed and the edge deteroirates even further. Frankly, the rogue is pretty much at the bottom of the list from 10-54 in a typical group. It is at this range of levels the class really needs all the help it can get.

1. The most obvious, backstab from 10-54.

Right now from 10-54 a typical rogue's backstab just isn't a big enough contribution to make up for their lack of diversity in battle. Indeed, in battle NO other class is as singularly focused as a rogue. Backstab has to justify that narrowness for groups looking to add members. This is doubly important because you cannot even consider adding a rogue in your group until you have already filled at *least* half the group positions. Towards this end rogues in this level range need to do more backstab damage to justify their existance.

There are several ways to do this:
a) Add in more rogue piercers with good damage/delay figures in the 10-14 damage range designed to be acquired within the 10-54 level range
b) Halve the cycle time on a backstab that fails becuase the mob was facing the wrong way. It is very common for a mob to switch facing too abruptly to catch or it switches facing on the server before the client updates and shows it facing you. Even with the best reflexes in the world, you can have quite a few backstabs spoiled that way. Halving the recycle time when a rogue "frontstabs" instead will reduce the penalty for the miss and offer a very small improvement in face-to-face combat when employed intentionally.
c) Put in a sliding scale on double backstab. Allow a rogue as low as 10th level to double backstab - very rarely - but the chance to double backstab very slowly increases throughout the levels until 55th level where it intersects with the existing double backstab skill

or some kind of combination of the above, whatever.

2. Poison

Simply put, it's nothing but a time, money, and space sink. While the great number of recipes are appreciated, the time, expense, and worst of all, bank and backpack storage demands that the poison craft requires simply doesn't add up to a worthwhile result. Time invested vs. reward is exceedingly bad. Now maybe this is meant to be nothing but flavor, but honestly it has no value at all other than "goofing off" value at any point in a rogue's life, whether 20 or 60.

3. The erudite mask of deception

Is in a really strange place in Charasis. You are most likely to camp the bloke who drops the mask if you need his key. However, as a rogue, your group doesn't need a key, and thus a rogue is unlikely to loot such a mask for himself unless he twists enough arms to get the group to camp exclusively for what most people would consider to be a frivolous item.

Naturally #1 is by far the most important issue, 2 and 3 are kind of side issues.

***A Guide to "Charging" Poisons***

--------------------------------------------------

Some sort of poison combining sack like the Shamen have is gonna be needed of course. Now that that's out of the way, try not to get confused =) (* more later)

For each type of poison, make 10 clones of it. Let's say we use Injected III as our example. Rather then just having Injected_III, we now have Injected_III_1, Injected_III_2, Injected_III_3,...Injected_III_10. As items work on a numbered system rather then by names, it wouldn't look as tacky of course And each item would have the name Injected III (* more later).

At the end of each "effect" for the poison, we'll add this line:

Summon item: Injected_III_

Thus, the Injected_III_10 will 1) poison your blade and 2) summon you another bottle of Injected_III this time being Injected_III_9. Obviously, the Injected_III_1 would have no summon code on it.

* This leaves some interesting possibilites:

1) Since there are actually 10 items, combining 3 poisons could just give you a 3 "charge" poison, rather then having to do it at 5 or 10 at a time. Or combining a 4 "charge" and a 6 "charge" would give you the maximum of a 10 "charge" poison.

2) Having the "item displayer" or whatever it is that shows an item when you right click it, possibly it could examine the last digit(s) on the item, and then include a line that says "Charges: X". Different technically, but from the players viewpoint would look like a Shaman's potions, save the number of original charges on the potion.

That's a neat idea Canbic that I missed over at the Safehouse. Your idea requires 800 new items and corresponding effects though.

Here is the idea I had and referenced through a link above (but Absor doesn't want to follow links so here it is again).

---- BEGIN QUOTE ----

Whenever the idea of stackable or charged poisons is brought up, Verant rejects the notion because it is not possible with the current game engine. Apparently stackable items cannot have effects (alcohol is not an effect then), and poisons cannot be charged because the effect is delayed until the next weapon hit and that doesn't fit in with the charged system.
Never has it been said that Verant is against stacking or multiple charge poisons for balance or other reasons, just that it wasn't technically possible. Well, I had an idea that should work within the current game engine.

There are basically 80 poisons as far as I know (16 known effects at 5 strengths). The first thing to do would be to create 80 new items in the item database to represent a 5 charge version of each of the poison. Next add 80 new recipes to the poison making skill using the mortar and pestle. Each of these recipes would take 5 of the appropriate single charge poison that we already have and create the new 5 charge item. If an extra cost for the convenience of the 5 charge item is needed, maybe the sixth ingredient can be a new extra large poison vial for a couple of plat.

Each of the new items would be expendable and start out with 5 charges of a new spell that summons the original single charge poison. This would require 80 new spells in the spdat.eff file and would be the only sticky spot in my idea. Are we close to any limit in the spdat.eff file now?

In any case, by adding 80 new item table, 80 new recipes, and 80 new spells, poisons are now stackable. I believe these are all simple rows in a SQL (Oracle?) database somewhere. It doesn't seem like it would be too big of a project and it doesn't change the game engine at all, just the data.

The same process could be doubled in size to allow for 10 charge poisons, but that would require a new container, and I really don't want to have 2 containers for poison making.

---- END QUOTE ----

This idea only needs 80 new items and effects.
