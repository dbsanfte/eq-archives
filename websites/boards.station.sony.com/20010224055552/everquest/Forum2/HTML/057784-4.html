"MAges were intended to be a solo class."

I heartily disagree. Magicians are a grouping class that can solo because they do a lot of damage fast, and with a pet can avoid getting hit. Monks can solo for a similar reason, they do damage fast. Also given the fact that pets are unreliable in some situations some people will not bother with the mage.

Why I think magicians are a grouping class:

Magicians lack any form of crowd control, any form of mana regeneration, and any form of travel abilites. Gate is our only escape ability, and all we have for protection is our pet and a couple of self buffs. We are meant to get these things from others. As a warrior must depend on a cleric for healing, a wizard for travel, an enchanter for haste, so must a magician(add clarity from the chanter).

Magicians, (pre-50 anyway), have several very group friendly abilities. Number 1 is fast damage(pet+nukes). A group with a magician kills stuff much faster. Number 2 is the emergency(or possibly primary) tank/rooter/stunner in the pet. Numbers 3 and 4 are the damage shields and resistance debuff. Finally our summons have utility and are helpful to others.

Mages are more powerful in groups. Groups with a magician are more powerful. To me that looks like the design of a grouping class.

Some not so random ramblings of a magician:

We are the master summoner's of the magical arts. We summon wonderous items, powerful pets and fearsome elemental nukes to destroy those who dare oppose us.


PETS:
Increase the damage, hit points and taunt factor of post 50th level pets. Don't need to go overboard here, just increase the pets so it is "as strong in relation to mobs, as the post 50th pets were".

In other words, at 29th I was capable of killing a level 29-31st mob solo, but concentrated on low 20's blue mobs for good experience.

From 50th to 60th, mobs increase in power by approximately 300%. Pets don't increase nearly as much. Fix the disparity. Fire pets are a joke, and post 50th, they were my favorite pet.


SPELLS:
As I increased my power through leveling towards 50th, my spells increased at a commensurate pace on par with the increase in monster ability.

Our spells post 50th do not increase in effectiveness by even close to the 300% the mobs have increased in power. I beseech you, increase the damage of our spells as well as the mana efficiency, to make them as powerful in relation to the mobs, as the first 50 levels of the class.


SUMMONINGS:
It seems other classes can now summon the items which were once the privy to the magician class only. Summoned daggers, food, water etc..

The mage is supposed to be the master of summonings, yet this is not true. In the first 50 levels of the game, our pet is now the only real summoning of note, mod rods the only exception.

The mage as a class is built around the premise of casting now, to obtain something useful later. We store mana now for future use. Our pets are the extreme example. I think additional summons, concentrating on storing mana is a great idea.

Give me an improved modulation rod like device. Stackable to make it useful, without a hit point drain. To balance it, make it costly in terms of mana ratio. Costs 400 mana, and will only restore 100 mana; with this kind of ratio, on a no-rent item, the mage is simply storing mana for later use, not creating a hit point to mana item.

Let me summon useful items. Weapons which only pets can use, but which increase their effectiveness. Procs of elemental type effects. The proc should be often enough to make the summoning worthwhile, but not overpowering. Looking for balance, not /godmode on.

Let the mages summon damage shield items. Click this item, and receive a damage shield. Item costs double the mana of a commensurate level damage shield. Again, the magician is storing mana for later use, the "purpose" of the class, but the ratio to create the item (double mana) and the fact of no-rent, removes the overpowering aspect in relation to other classes.

Some spells in the magician arsenal which would be nice stackable summoned components (as mentioned in the preceding paragraphs), but costing double or more mana as a check/balance, include: Bolt of, Burnout, Malise, Pet CH (costing more mana then a new pet), Rain of, RenewElements/ RenewSummoning, Shock of... The _______ of spells are simply something approximately useful to the level of the summoning. Make the mana to summon the item more expensive then simply casting the spell.

Having a summoned item, capable of summoning an insurance pet would be great for those stints through a hostile area without a pet. Without a fixed duration invisibility, in a situation where invis is required, mages are worse off then any other caster. Make an item, with an instant cast time, prohibitive to summon in terms of the mana cost.

Pet invisioned as something similar to this: High hit point, high ac pet with an incredible taunt ability. The pet has no damage dealing capacity, cannot be damage shielded, and suicides should the mob in question be dealt more than a small amount of damage.

A higher level mage summoning spell, which removes the no-rent tag on an object and renders the object expendable would be interesting, but I won't hold my breath.

This is a long post and I apologize in advance for those who have the fortitude to wade through it.

Some time ago Abashi made a comment that has stuck in my head since and goes a long way to explaining a number of things about class balance and dependency. In answer to a complaint by a mage that we were the only purecaster class that didn't get some form of mana regeneration, he said that not all classes would get everything. For instance, mages didn't get mana regen and clerics didn't get normal invisibility. I always read the reply(which I paraphrased in any case) to mean that in return for the ability to
do some things each class would have to be unable to do something else. With the exception of necromancers, of course (j/k). This is a sensible course I believe and so I've always thought that the lack of many things mages have wanted such as root, snare, escape spells, etc. were balanced in light of other class abilities. So when Velious came out I looked through the spell lists for the various classes and was sorely disappointed in the mage spells as compared to those for the other pure casters. So I started comparing the
abilities of all the caster classes to see how each benefited or lost.

Damage dealing abilities (with my ranking from best to worst)
DD's - all casters get these. WIZ, MAG, ENC, NEC
DoT's - all casters get these except wizards. NEC, ENC, MAG
AoE - all casters get these. WIZ, MAG, ENC, NEC
Damage shields - all caster classes. MAG, ENC, WIZ, NEC (Necro is self
only)
Pets - MAG, NEC, ENC

Buffs:
Fire resist buff - MAG, WIZ,
Cold resist buff - MAG, WIZ, NEC
Magic resist buff - ENC
Poison buff - NEC, WIZ
Disease buff - NEC, WIZ
Melee buff - ENC
AC Buff - ENC, NEC
HP buff - ENC
Haste - ENC

Debuffs:
Fire debuff - MAG, NEC, WIZ (as a component of a DD spell)
Cold debuff - MAG, WIZ (as a component of a DD spell)
Posion debuff - NEC, MAG
Disease debuff - NEC
Magic debuff - MAG, ENC
Melee Debuff - ENC, NEC
Slow - ENC

Combat Assist spells:
Charm - ENC, NEC
Fear - NEC, ENC
Mez - ENC, NEC
Evacs - WIZ
Root - all casters except mages. ENC, NEC, WIZ
Snare - all casters except mages and enchanters. NEC, WIZ

Other capabilities: (these may include spells that assist during combat or
while traveling)
Invisibility - all casters. ENC, MAG, NEC, WIZ
Improved invis - all casters except mages. ENC, NEC, WIZ
Invis to undead - all casters except mages. ENC, NEC, WIZ
Shield skins - all casters except mages. ENC, NEC, WIZ
Armor buffs - all casters except wizards. ENC, NEC, MAG
Shielding spells - all casters. ENC, MAG, NEC, WIZ
Mana regen - all casters except mages. ENC, NEC, WIZ
Health regen - all casters except wizards. MAG, ENC, NEC
Levitation - all casters. ENC, WIZ, MAG, NEC
Enduring breath - all casters except wizards. ENC, MAG, NEC
Travel WIZ

Personal protection: Why do so many mages ask for an escape spell? Well it's because we are the weakest class bar none in the game when in melee. Let's look at some numbers:

At level 40 I had approximately 750 HP and 440 AC. My shield spell and phantom armor give the following additions: 94HP and 37 AC. This equates to an increase of approximately 12.5% in HP and 8.4% in AC.
Now how about the others?
Magician +94 HP +12.5% +37AC +8.4%
Wizard +324HP +43.2% +23AC +5.2%
Enchanter +324HP +43.2% +40AC +9.1%
Necro +324HP +43.2% +37AC +8.4%

I don't know what the actual impact of an increase of 8.4% in AC is - I doubt anyone outside Verant does. Just looking at the actual numbers though I have to say that I'd personally rather have a 43% increase in hit points over the slight increase in AC. Our survivability without a pet is the lowest of any class in the game. Believe me the health regen on the phantom
armor line is a nice addition - but it does not increase my survivability one iota when I'm running for zone with a green beating on me.

Finally, mage research: Well what can I say? We're the only class that needs the previous rank's spells to make our new ones and we also have to do chain research. One item I can understand, but both? At one time I liked the fact
that mage research was the hardest of all - it was kind of a mark of distinction. But where is the balance? What do we get in return? Decent mid
level questable mage armor? The Sol Ro stuff is mediocre to outright miserable (Stone Bracelet). And there's only four pieces.

Why did I do all this? I like backing up my arguments. Most people know most of this stuff, but even I was surprised when I finished going through the spell lists.

So at the end of the day what have we got? From looking at the list its obvious that the mage class is power in the form of damage dealing. Pure and simple. We have the most difficult research. The lowest personal protection in the game. The most limited buffs of all the
casters. No combat assist spells. No mana regen. No Improved Invisibility. No invis to undead. No shield skins. We do have the second best DD's of the pure casters. We have the best pets. We have the best debuff for assisting casters. We need an enchanter to function even halfway efficiently since we have no form of man regen. We have AoEs which are pretty good but we cant use them unless we have an enchanter. Oh, and when we get to level 56 we get Call of the Hero. Notice I'm
not even listing summons. They have no real impact in the game other than to occasionally eke out another player's supplies so he won't have to go to town in the middle of a camp.

Some solutions have been bandied about. Here's mine.

Path pathing. Forget it. Id love to see this corrected but its just not realistic to assume that at this late date it can be done. Almost all dungeons are a problem. I just don't think that Verant is going to repath all of these areas - the cost would be too high. And that's not even counting outdoor areas where pets go buggy
like large portions of Lesser Fay. So what can one do? The spell described on Test for summoning the pet is a good start - but it could be available at a lower level. Here's one simple solution. After several bad experiences I
never went back into a dungeon until I got to level 35. Know why? Because I couldn't keep track of my pet while medding. Take the spellbook out for mages from level 4 on. It's just too darn dangerous for us to go into dungeons when we can't see where our pets are for half or more of our time there. And it's not our fault - we didn't design the pathing. This affects us much more than other classes with the possible exception of necros. So it makes life a little easier in the outdoors while soloing. Go back and look at the list above. Is it that unbalancing?

Why didn't we get Improved Invisibility? What possible reason is there for mages not to have got this? We don't have any other means of protecting ourselves and our armor/hitpoints just don't measure up to the other classes. Of all the casters, mages should have gotten this spell first. Verant can't even use the class dependency issue since the spell is self only. And no - we do not pull up a pet every time we enter a zone for travel - it will get aggroed on by mobs that wouldn't touch the mage.

Maybe it's just because I spend more time reading mage threads than those for other classes, but does it seem like mage items - be they spells or epic quest drops or whatever seem to have the most problems getting put into the game correctly? I don't have a solution for this - but I wish Verant would come up with something. As it is I don't even bother trying quests anymore
till I know that they work. Too much time running around to find out that the quest I'm working on isn't just difficult - it's impossible, because
it's broken.

Finally, and I do mean finally. I've read with some interest the other class threads. The one that caught my eye was the one for the enchanters. In it there was some discussion on what could be done so that the enchanter wasn't
a make or break class for some areas in the game. By this I mean that in some areas there is a very high probability that lack of an enchanter will
mean total wipeouts or the inability of people to form viable groups. Rather than nerfing enchanters or the areas that they're so necessary in, why not consider enabling other classes to make effective use of spell lines that normally lie unused. I'm speaking of the AE spells. Many casters and priest classes have at least one line of AE's but they hardly ever get used. Why?
Because for effective use they must be coupled with AE stuns. Any other course is too dangerous. And who has the only line of AE stuns? Enchanters
of course. And naturally, and I can't say I blame them, they are for the most part reluctant to indulge in AE tactics. If given a choice between an AE group and one where the enchanter is mezzing I have to go with the latter. It's just safer. But if I can't find an enchanter to group
with then I have little option since both sets of tactics are dependent on their presence. If there is one thing I would recommend changing it would be to extend the AE stun lines to another caster class. Naturally I would prefer that mages get it but either wizards or necros would be just as good. Or for that matter any of the priest classes.

So that's my two cents. I tend to run on but I've been pondering these issue
for a while.

Oh can't resist - one last idea. Why not make Quillmane a summoned creature. Then at least we don't have to drag a ranger or druid along on a very boringcamp to find the dang thing. We can use Sense Summoned to find it ourselves.

First, I want to join in the chorus of "Thank yous" among those who appreciate that Verant is working to engage in feedback from people playing their classes. And I recognize that not everyone will agree on possible fine tunings.

Let me add that I dont feel the need to say another class should have something taken away just take make Mages more desirable. Nor do we need alternate vectors of attack (DOTs, cold, wind, etc) if indeed our own vectors of attack can be made to be a touch more friendly to the caster. We would appreciate a role, as do all pure casters, in being able to deal some damage to the large boss mobs that currently really only seek tanks and tank support.

Nor in general do pets need too much work, except A) certain low level pets such as fire and air which are too fragile to use and B) post-50 pets which are also too fragile to use and C) the ever-important issue of control to where having pets would become a desirable thing.

In this post I wish to address only one line of spells, our summonings.

Summons: This line is one of the ones that we rather hope can make us most valued in a group. However, none of the things we summon are of good quality by the time we summon them; the post-50 quiver of Marr arrows are great, but for whom? People love our waterstones, for the two or three rare times they desire them. Coldstones are desirable to races with poor vision. Some spells would be wonderful bumped down a spell ranking; the inclusion of early magical 1hb would be appropriate and is a gaping hole; the fact that the norent status on bags makes them effectively unwanted nullifies what would otherwise be a useful line of spells. And, of course, shard of the core has been appropriately castigated as needing to be in both range or ammo slot, and possibly tradable. In addition to the changes proposed what about:

1. Summon Sewing kit; jewelers kit; portable oven, etc; These mid-range spalls would summon norent equivalents to tradeskill kits. This would make the magician seen as a more valuable companion during downtime while people med.

2. Summon functional NPC: Summon baker, bartender, summon merchant, summon banker. A line of spells that would briefly summon an immobile NPC who functioned as a merchant or banker. After all, since astral projections can come into the world to do commerce, why not summon these commercial beings to do trade with the people of Norrath. The bonuses would be enormous: Most people who wont hunt certain areas frequently because of their remoteness would be more likely to do so if they had these resources available to them. Further, since these NPC merchants would vanish after being dismissed (and they would take the place of a mages pet slot, so a mage would have to unsummon their pet to bring one in), this would help the economy of Norrath from not constantly being flooded; any items sold to these ethereal merchants would be taken out of the game.

3. You shall not evade me! : Obviously a spell in the game: why not have it that if a monster is aggroed, the mage might be able to cast a spell with a low casting time (but high recycle time) that would pull the monster back to them? It would be a different type of crowd control than root or snare; as the monster would be on top of the magician. It would not replace a puller, as the monster not only would have to be targeted by the magician but also already have to have the magician on their hate list. Since call of the hero has become a new post-50 mage spell that makes them desirable in groups, lower levels surely could benefit from the abilityt o bring back a monster that flees or warps. Since magic resist always plays a role, such a line of spells would never be too overpowered as it would require a higher level spell to effectively summon such a creature back.
