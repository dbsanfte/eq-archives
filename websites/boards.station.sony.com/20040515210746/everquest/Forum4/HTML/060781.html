Assuming SUN boxes, if the root disk on TMarr died, even if it was mirrored, a tech has to come in and tell the system to use the mirrored disk as the new root, and set another disk as the mirrored disk.

If they lost a mainboard, they would be looking at 24 hours assuming SUN warranty replacement (possibly even 48 hours if it happened on Saturday,as few companies ship on Sunday). And almost no one keeps a spare SUN mainboard on the shelf, although I suppose SOE might).

Assuming multiple CPU's, the loss of one may still permit uptime but at a limited level of functionality. Loss of more than one would probably render the machine useless for practical purposes.

Loss of a SCSI controller would not be too catastrophic. They would likely have spares on hand.

But in all cases, a tech has to come in (if he is not already there) to make the repairs.

The 2 things you fail to mention, ship, sinc eyou obviously know your stuff..

Redundancy and parity... 2 industry standards when it comes to data such as eq's.

If a hard drive was the failure, parity would minimize downtime to next-to-nothing. (I've had to rebuild arrays using parity, and was shocked how fast this miracle of technology works)..

And if redundancy is practiced, properly (very expensive), an entire system can wipe, and should have a backup right there ready to go.

I deal with important data for a living, and redundancy and parity are 2 words I've begun to use on a daily basis when it comes to backup strategies.

But, alas, your ultimate point is indeed true.. No matter what, you need a hardware tech on site to deal with this.

My estimation is that they don't practice either/or of these strategies, the extended downtime reflects this.
