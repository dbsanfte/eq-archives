*A lengthy plea to the gods of Norrath*

My suggestion is that you make the pets resistant to fear, because undead fear nothing; resistant to disease, because undead are supposed to use disease as a weapon, not be plagued by it (no pun intended); resistant to poison, because they have no blood or nervous system to attack.

I know this is a fantasy world, and "realistic" is a term commonly thrown around, but - while we can throw out realism and suspend disbelief in a fantasy setting - inductive reasoning and logical associations should hold true.

As for the other points, here is what the necromancer really wants (in order of importance):

1) Lifetaps/lifetap dot's that work at least 51% of the time on major enemies. This is our bread and butter line of spells. If one spell line defines the necromancer, this is it. Pets are great to have, but we'd be a mage if we wanted powerful pets. God knows verant nerfed ours to near uselessnes.

2) One "get out of nerf free" card, whereby the necromancer class can play this card at their leisure, when they feel they are going to be nerfed unfairly (again). Ok, maybe this is just a fun one. =)

The real #2) Risk/Reward re-evaluation. Pet improvements and/or dot improvements to bring our damage output inline with the other classes in the game would help. Necromancers can outdamage bards. That's about it. We are pure casters - pure casters devoted to EVIL! We want to kill. At this time, druids, clerics (/boggle), shaman, and every other unlikely class can humiliate us when competing for damage. We do negligible damage in battles with mobs over level 55. Our pets cause more trouble than they are worth in the planes. We cannot safely visit more than three cities and an outpost without struggling for days with newbie quests, only to be beaten down by some unrelated faction within a city. The risk/reward for necros is nowhere near balanced. The necromancer class is the most persecuted. Shouldn't there be a reward for all of our troubles?

3) If not #1 or #2, then at least a different role than mana battery. There are two lines of spells most necromancers would prefer replaced by two new lines. That being the mana transfer spells and the essence emerald spells. Mana transfer spells are a waste! Why convert my mana to a person with a role, when I should have a role myself? This is just plain humiliating to necromancers who have struggled through 50+ levels of prejudice and hatred to end up dumping their hard-earned mana to the high elf wizard so he can get off another nuke, or the dwarven cleric so he can heal someone. Humiliating! We want a job after level 50. Give us something we can do besides tailoring backpacks.

Essence emeralds are too costly. Plain and simple. It's nice to have powerful spells, but why bother memorizing them if their cost is unbalanced. Would anyone in their right mind sacrifice hours of their play time so someone can gain 1000 mana? NO! Make it targetable? Heh... why not just give it to all other classes but necromancer and make it free for them to cast. Who do you think will absorb the high pricetag on this spell? You guessed it - the person who casts the spell, not the one who receives the mana. But, again, this is another case of necromancer mana being useless, so we may as well give it away to someone with a role in the end game.

4) Group DMF. This should be in the game anyway. It has no balancing effect, as the group wouldn't be waiting on necromancer mana anyway. Mana checks are for clerics, enchanters, and anyone else who has a role in the end game.

5) Make plants plants. Yes, a druid had a great point that humans are, indeed, animals, so they should be able to charm them, but this would be unbalancing. Plants in PoG would not! Necromancers don't do all that much in PoG or any decent high level zone, for that matter. If we cannot kill plants with defoliation, then turn it into a disease based dd that works on any type of mob. The damage/mana ratio is not any better than that of any other classes ratios.

6) Stop calling us overbalanced and get someone to play a level 51+ necromancer for a couple of months. With all the resources you have available to you, one would think you could pay someone to play a necromancer. God knows it might come to that if we are nerfed any further.

7) When you're holding an apple in one hand, please discard the butcher's knife in the other. Not only have necromancers received the most nerfs since beta, we have also been cheated out of precious skills by giving us flashy utility spells that none of us use after a week. Examples: voice graft, deadeye, track corpse (that just started working, 6 months after implementation, but is too annoying for anyone to want to use), chill bones (which has yet to drop on my server).

8) Make necromancers groupable with other necromancers. One necromancer per raid is all that anyone wants since you cripple our class with unstackable spells that aren't even that powerful to begin with. Ignite bones would be *decent* if more than one necro could cast. DOT's would be ok if they worked on big mobs, stacked, and didn't poof corpses (which still happens). Our healing spells (which have HORRIBLE ratios, by the way) would be nice if they didn't take so much of our life, stacked with people's buffs, and stacked with other necromancer's spells.

Plus, not only do our dot's not stack with our dot's, but many of our dot's are not compatible with ANY enchanter dot whether it is of a related sphere of magic or not. Shaman get half of the same dot's as we, and they get them sooner. Guess what? They don't stack either.

I realize that necromancers aren't meant to be the most "group-friendly" class in the game, but give us a role, PLEASE! Let us be wanted in a group BEFORE the cr begins. Let two of us group together and not have to waste half of our time coordinating what we're casting and when.

I may have messed up the order a touch, but you can tell which of these is important to our class, and which would just be nice to have. I suppose I should have said first, and foremost, we want to be needed again. I recall being a level 50 necromancer (a couple months into final), entering lower guk and getting /tell flooded from people wanting me in their group. Now, nearly level 60, I enter sebilis - the kunark equivilant of LGuk - and beg for hours before I can find a group if my guild has no openings in their camps. Any other class can enter that zone and be immediately snatched up. Let me repeat that last statement; ANY other class can enter sebilis and IMMEDIATELY find a group.

That is a slap in the face to those of us who worked so hard and spent so much time, effort, and hard-earned money, only to have our frayed ends of sanity snap back in our faces as we realize how much of our lives we've wasted to become useless. If I wanted to be useless, I would never have taken the time to level up; I would have just sat around and roleplayed and done newbie quests.


I ramble. Hopefully you will find one bit of usefull information in my post, Absor. If so, please take it to heart when I tell you that these issues are very important to us as well as you. You offer a product that should build in value and customer satisfaction as time progresses. At this point, you are losing many customers because you have pigeon-holed the requests of 9% of your customer base (9% is close to, if not exactly, the amount of necromancer main characters in the game). We built up our characters only to see their relative power diminish. What is the point?


Please, just give us a role. Mana battery is not a role. Second-best pet caster is not a role. Third-best dot'er is not a role. Worst nuker is not a role. Worst items degrades our role. I could go on...


We want to be needed and useful again. That's not so much to ask.

Personally, this is a niche that I think could be promising for necromancers in groups, especially in the mid-levels, like in the level 40 - 49 range.
