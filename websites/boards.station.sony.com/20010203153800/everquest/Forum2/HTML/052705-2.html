A few things I haven't seen brought up much in the last thread (I've managed to keep up with about 75% of the posts).

*Reducing Tedium*
Two basic ways to reduce tedium:
-Increase duration of our most common buffs (clarity, haste, magic resist) and increase mana cost accordingly.
-Make more of our spells available in group versions. I'd second the moving of group clarity to a lower level to allow it more use, but why not group augmentation, group invis/see invis, group gift? These don't have to be more mana efficient, just allow us to reduce our time spent mindlessly selecting group member1, cast, group member2, cast.. ect. These are downtime / travel spells, I'd rather be spending my downtime chatting with group mates or talking about how we're going to take the next mob, not buffing.

Do group spells or longer durations have balance implications? We're casting these spells anyway, why not give us an easier means to do so. Set the mana cost so the only benefit is reducing tedium and mindless repetition.

I think too often Verant offers the removal of an annoyance (group buffing, longer buff durations, meditating without spell books as the reward for leveling, rather than a positive reward.

The next suggestion definitely has balance implications so I'm not holding my breath, but what the hell.

*Charm Casting Time*
A while back the casting times and recast times of the higher level mesmerize spells underwent a reduction to encourage their use, since enchanters as a whole were sticking with lower level spells exclusively. I wonder if a re-evaluation of the higher level charm spells would also be possible. Charm and Beguile are great, but Cajoling Whispers and Allure have a casting time just long enough to discourage their use (5.5 and 6 seconds respectively). Things get better again with our lvl 53 charm at 4 seconds.

These have very little implication on an intial charm, but faster casting times make re-charming possible in conjunction with our stuns (albeit difficult). Again this is wishlist area, but I don't understand why the increase in diffulculty for 39-53, and then remove it again. Also I'd gladly give up dictate for a general purpose charm spell that affects 53+ but I suspect I'm in the minority on that.

Last topic is a repeat but I thought I'd second it.

*Targeting*
Targeting is difficult due to many reasons: mobs stack, frame rate slow downs, lag spikes, and most annoying - mobs who's bounding boxes seem to extend far beyond what is visible. I'll second two suggestions I've seen earlier in the thread as ways to help.
- Cycle nearest target - pressing the button multiple times cycles from nearest to furthest and back again, within a set radius.
- Anti-assist - works as above but excludes what your main tank (or whatever) has targeted.

That's all I have at the moment. In closing let me say I think overall Verant has done a great job with enchanters and I love playing them. Great work.

-Finias

I have been playing an Enchantress since April of 1999, so I remember playing when we were THE most undesired class under 29th. Even then I was happy to play an Enchantress because I thought the rewards would be worth the suffering. I was correct.

I can't say I really have any complaints about playing an Enchantress pre-50. It is certainly a tough road, but a rewarding one. You play a class which takes itelligence and skill. There are certainly ways to make it easier, but the same could be said of any class.

I think a lot of the previous posts have made good points for improvement, so I won't go into them with the exception of saying that look carefully at some of the longer posts. There are a lot of good suggestions to be found for balancing.

Post-50 has seen a blurring of the lines between classes. This started with RoK and has continued in SoV. In my opinion, the important thing about playing a class is it's uniqueness. Every one of us started a class because something for that class jumped out at us. "Wizards are the masters of big damage," "Magicians can summon anything under the sun," "Druids are one with nature." Along with these perceptions, correct or not, comes certain expectations for abilities, some of which were stated in the manual.

I know I have been rambling but please stick with me for a few more minutes. In my mind, what the post-50 world comes down to is 2 things:

1) Raids
2) Class-defining abilities

Raid complaints/suggestions have been beat to death. For class-defining abilities the case is less clear. We have reached the highest levels possible (unless another level expansion comes out). We deserve to hold the abilities implied by that power and our class.

Each class has certain abilities which they consider class-defining:

Magicians have pets, summoning, and damage shields.
Druids have teleportation, snare, and SoW

Each class has their own opinions. Enchanters have always had a wide variety of abilities which we do well.

Heres what I have as a sort of summary so far. Keep in mind that I wrote this yesterday evening (during Survivor), so it does not take into account posts made after that. I am reading them today, though.

The first thing to think about is how enchanters work against the high-hit point creatures of Velious. Do they have enough to contribute over the course of a fight with such monsters?

Im not sure that enchanters are in need of help in this area. Perhaps the addition of something like a DoT-pet would help.

Really, where the majority of higher level enchanters are expressing their concern is that there are too many creatures that are immune to mesmerization-type (and secondarily charm) spells. As I noted earlier, Im not real sure that we can increase the functionality of those spells because they would simply over-balance the enchanter in those encounters (encounters where one or two very strong enemies are intended to challenge large groups, and one charm or mesmerization spell would eliminate all or half of the challenge).

Im not sure that Ive seen an answer to this situation that will work.

Rampage: This line does not stack with other strength buffs. We should evaluate this, and decide if this is how it should be.

DoT Pet: Something not unlike the clerical hammer spell, to add more to the ability of the enchanter to help during fights with extremely high hit point enemies.

Illusions: Mostly, you want more illusions. There are also a few requests to add effects to some illusions.

Illusion, Wolf: There has been a call to have this spell grant Spirit of Wolf. If it gets no Spirit of Wolf component, then perhaps it could work indoors. Ill have it on the list. I am very much against such homogenization of the classes. In fact, so much so that I dont really think that enchanters should even be allowed to transform into wolves at all.

Illusion, Gender: Seems like a good request.

Illusion, Group Elemental (or race): A spell that grants the elemental illusions (or race) to the group.

Illusion, Change Size: Some sort of illusion that makes the caster bigger or smaller. This is something that I am against, assize change is really the area of the shaman.

Illusion Monster (and any copycat illusion): a spell that lets the enchanter copy the appearance of either a random monster in the zone, or a targeted creature. Faction issues will most certainly disallow this, but its listed.

Major Illusion: If possible, a high level spell (even 60th if needed) that has the functionality of the original minor illusion spells. One that brings back the enormous illusions that were possible with that spell.

Illusion face: Would give the enchanter a random face.

Undead Illusions: I, frankly, feel that enchanters shouldnt have these spells. I will ask that we look at them, and consider that they are the territory of the necromancer.

Phantom armor: It would be awesome to create an illusion spell that allowed the enchanter to look like he is wearing plate. Perhaps a new spell similar to the magician phantom line (perhaps just like it) that has this illusionary effect (and we should also think of adding this to the magician spells...)

Enchanting Items: I will add this to the list as a reminder.

Lull spells: These spells seem to be resisted too easily, more often used to pull than to break up a group. There should be at least a fair chance that the creature resisting this spell will not immediately aggro.

Bard Lute: We should at least look at this loot. Even if we dont remove it (it is a very rare item), we should keep it in mind when making new items. It gives a key enchanter ability to another class.

Voice Graft: We should evaluate the idea of giving this spell to all pet classes, including enchanters.

Whirl til you hurl and Dyn's Dizzying Draught: Players feel that these spells are too easily resisted and too easily broken.

We should consider this approach for mez spells as well.

Mesmerization (Charm) Indicator: I, personally, dont really like this idea. But there is a call for a change of the color of a monsters name when it is under the effects of mez and charm so that people know by looking.

Reoccurring Amnesia: The reports are pretty overwhelming, this spell does not reoccur.

My main disappointment with enchanters is post 55. For 1-55 I thought the class was well balanced and a good contribution to any group. Post 55 though as many people indicate we get reduced to casting C2 and Haste. This is especially true around level's 56+ where mobs become either unmezzable or require GOK/Rapture/Dictate to control. Our primary function has always been in my mind crowd control first (shamans are and should be better buff/debuff). Now at the peak of our levels we have spells that last for a third of the duration of dazzle for three times the mana. I almost exclusively use Rapture because at least I know it isn't going to be resisted *IF* the mob is mezzable. GOK on the other hand will shoot out a lot of resists (at about a bar of mana a cast). Totally going against the increased mana efficiency other classes see (or at least holding the same efficiency as we did prior to ROK/SOV).

A lot of comments on this thread comes from people who are sub 56 who in general view the class as killer or are worried about getting more illusions. If I were in their shoes I would probably be posting the same thing. But after several months at 60 lots and lots of enchanters are hanging up their shoes. Because what made the class fun for the first 55 levels isn't continued for the last 5-6.

And on a closing note the casting times on Resplendent gear is just whacked. 30 seconds for regular clarity?? I can sit down and med for 30secs and get more then enough mana to cast c2. The robe is useless at 30secs as well for the most part. Compared to the other classes SOV gear our effects blow.
