Howdy all! I’m not going to start by saying how long I’ve been playing or how many characters I have or how I consider myself to be very smart about all this…. etc, etc, because it’s soooo annoying. As I've read the posts, I've found myself wanting to argue AGAINST many of the changes that have been suggested. I think the game works pretty good as it is. First, I'll get to the point and give the changes that I DO think are good.

1) Using a shield should provide a block bonus. Creatures in Velious can block our attacks, why can’t we block theirs? Also, being able to block will alleviate the need to increase AC.

2) Bind wound. It DOES work, but the cap should be increased some for warriors. Also, it's not practical to have to carry around two backpacks just for your bandages. Have an item like a "first aid kit". It only takes up one slot, but you can use it to bind wounds. You'd have to have the skill to use it. You'd also have to replenish it with more bandages, or it only has so many "charges", like a splitpaw. But right after a fight where you almost died, you shouldn’t be able to push a couple of buttons and BAM!.. be ready to go.

3)I think it’s a good idea to get 5 points every 10 levels that we can use to buff our base attributes. After all, we’re gaining experience… why can’t we grow stronger or smarter?

4)At level 40 or 45 add a weapon specialization that gives a good bonus for 1H/2H Slash and Blunt ONLY IF you’ve reached master level in all of them.

A lot of other improvements have been mentioned. Some of the other suggestions might sound good on the surface, but they really aren't practical in gameplay. Others are just plain silly. Here are some that stood out, and why I feel they are "less effective."

-Area affect taunt. This would be great! However, I think it would make life a little too easy for the casters. Besides, if there are say 12 characters raiding a room, it's not realistic to expect ALL the mobs to say "He's the tank! Get him" No, they're going to go for the little guy hiding behind the tank, that's nuking them for all that damage.

-Taunt. Leave it the way it is-- it works! The mob’s attack is based on damage. The reason why mobs don’t listen to taunts is because the casters are hurting them more than the tanks. Casters need to use their spells more effectively. Start nuking after the tanks have done a lot of damage. When the mob goes to the caster, the warrior can taunt and it will work perfectly. Why? Because he’s done more damage. This way, the casters help the tanks keep the baddies off them, instead of rendering their taunts useless by doing too much damage. It doesn’t need tweaking, just a strategy a bit different than the rut most groups have dug themselves into. Before you flame, try it.. it works.

-More damage output. A high level warrior can dish out 1200 points of damage. He’s not going to be able to nuke like a wizard, but that’s not his job. Damage output is fine.

-Disarm. This also works just fine. It lowers the damage to mobs that are attacking with a weapon. If they don’t have a weapon, how can you disarm? They’ll do the same amount of damage as before.

-Bind points in the game are fine. The whole point to having a zone far away from any binds is to increase the risk involved. You wouldn't be as worried about dying if you could get bound two zones away no matter where you go.

-Area affect fear. Some want a "Battle Cry" type skill that has the same effect as instill doubt. This doesn’t fit a warrior. A warrior is a stand up, toe-to-toe fighter. Rogues use instill fear to get the mob to turn around so they can backstab and get more damage. Warriors can’t backstab, so there’s no point in having the skill.

-Track. Warriors don’t need a tracking skill. A tracking skill would be useful, don’t get me wrong. All the time I hear "How many HP's do you have? You're pulling!" Then I've wandered around unable to find anything, and ended up pulling the first thing I saw. If you could have the HP’s and AC of a warrior, plus tracking, why would anyone ever play a ranger? Some think that warriors should get a scaled down version of the skill. That’s what rangers have, a scaled down version of the druid skill. Sure, it’d be nice…. But we’re talking balance. Tracking for warriors wouldn’t work. If you HAVE to use it, group with a ranger or druid and have him tag along while you pull.

-Healing Potions are bad. Some say that they would be used just as a backup or for emergencies. I disagree. Need a heal, find a cleric. You could even pay him for it, and it would be cheaper than an expensive potion. Can't find a healer? Sit down and rest. All potions will do is allow the super rich to solo when normally they'd have to group or die. Need to heal when nobody is around? Bind Wound. Besides, this isn't Diablo II. Everquest is geared more to real life, real time role-playing, not comic arcade style diversion. If your group ditches you because you didn't heal fast enough, well they were a crappy group and you should be glad to be rid of them. If you're tanking for them, any group should be happy to give you heals and/or wait for you to heal on your own. I'm sure the game designers thought about this when they were still in the playtesting phase of development, and they left them out for a reason.

-Increased regeneration. Again, leave it the way it is. If you get beat within an inch of your life, it should take some time to recuperate. Again, we're talking about realistic role-playing and combat... not Diablo II.

-Increased HP's. This also is not needed. Of course one warrior is not going to come close to the HP's of a high level mob. Those mobs are meant to be attacked by groups, no soloed. Do you really want to have guys running around that have so many HP's they can solo dragons, or take strolls alone in the planes? Besides, we still have roughly 10% to 15% more HP's than the rest. That's enough.

-Racial bonuses. A dwarf or halfling warrior shouldn’t get any extra help so he can keep up with barbs, ogres, and trolls. They shouldn’t get special attacks, either. Large races get bonuses because of their greater size and strength. The fact is that in a fight, bigger is better. A big ogre is going to hit harder than a little halfling. Big races make better warriors, period. Just as an erudite makes a better wizard because his INT is higher. The little guys don't deserve bonuses just to level the field. Deal with it, or sell your swords and take up baking. Giving bonuses to small races would also leave humans and elves out of luck… shouldn’t they get something, too? The incentive to play a dwarf or halfling warrior is the difficulty. A barb warrior is an easy character to play. If you pick a harder one and do well, it brings more challenge to the game and proves how good you are.

-No Bind and no Gate. Don't get me wrong, it would be cool for us to be able to bind ourselves. But, whether by skill or item, this would make us a little too powerful. This is what casters are for. I've never had a problem getting a bind when I needed one. It thought for a while it would be cool to have an item that had an effect the same as Gate. This also defeats the purpose of group play. At high levels, you're going to be in a group. Fight together and die together, and hopefully you'll have an druid near that can cast succor and save the group.

-No more speed. Some have complained that it's too hard for warriors to travel, and that we run too slowly. Again, that's part of the game. If you need to run across Antonica, make preparations. Get invis and SoW, (I've always gotten one when I asked nicely) or find someone to travel with. Learn to AVIOD the mobs that will kill you. Practice by taking a low-level character to Kunark, and run between Firiona Vie and Ill Omen while avoiding Drachnids and Vekis.

-Somebody suggested infra and ultra vision for large race warriors. That is just silly. Might as well ask for spells. Or better yet, a rocket launcher! Again, that's what makes the game interesting. When it gets dark, it's harder to see. You have to deal with the world around you and adapt to the conditions. Visibility gets worse when it rains, too. Do players want a Finely Crafted Helm of Weather Control?

-Religon. Contrary to what some think, religion DOES affect gameplay. The deity you worship affects faction. It would be impractical to introduce abilities that would allow someone to pray to their god and have him save their butts. Don't rely on god, save your own arse. Also, evil characters are KOS in the Plane of Hate because Innoruk is the prince of hate. Guess what... he HATES everybody, including all those that follow him. What, did you think you could go visit, have some tea and give him a hug? (No, I'm not British.)

-More AC. I think the AC for warriors is fine. Just because we are the main tanks, does not mean that we should tower over the rest. Paladins and Rangers can be powerful fighters as well, and we should share the pedestal a bit.


Well, that's enough complaining for now. Just like everyone else, I think the class is darn near perfect. Just a tiny bit of tweaking is needed. Some of the “wishes” would be really cool to have, but just aren’t practical. A lot more of the requests are a little out there; they would only serve to make the warrior a super-uber badass that would not fit in the game. It's not about having one class dominate the others; it's about the classes teaming up and each performing a role. Some of the ideas on tweaking the crafted armor are good, but that is more of an item balancing issue, so I did not mention it here. You also didn't hear me complain about the quests, because it's going to be a long time before my warrior will be a high enough level to even care. Sorry for all the typos, and I’ll be keeping an eye on the string for all the flames I’m gonna get! Really, I don’t mean to offend anyone… I just think that each idea should be carefully considered… and I have a Dennis Leary-like sense of humor. Thanks to Verant for hosting these forums, hopefully there are some good ideas in here that will lead to good changes in the game. Luck to you all!
