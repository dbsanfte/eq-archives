The Low Levels

Wizards struggle greatly at the lowest levels, more than any other class. Lacking pets, healing, combat skills, armor class, and superior hit points, Wizards die, and die a lot. The Wizard is the least survivable class in the game at the lowest levels, you just get used to it and go on.

Now, again as I noted earlier, if the concept is "start weak, get powerful" we have a working class, but the idea of the classes is "everyone is balanced and viable, especially at the end game." So what we have is a class that just is a punching bag with no pay off later on.

The fix? Well there are a lot of ideas, but here are the easiest ones:

-Make Root less resistable at the initial cast. It should land more reliably, it can hold just like now, but when you cast root, you should have a reasonable expectation that it will take hold. I can't even count the number of times I have had to cast root 4 and 5 or more times before it holds, and sometimes it breaks so fast the monster doesn't even slow down. Root is the Wizard's defense.
-Fix Fade/Shadowstep/Yonder line so it will port up again, like it used to. Make it port around trees, buildings, etc. Further, starting at Shadowstep, have it port a minimum distance of 1 foot per level the Wizard has achieved. Yonder should port 2 feet minimum. This means the Wizard has a reasonable expectation that he will actually GO somewhere when the spell is cast, whereas right now it will quite often dump you 2 feet from the monster.

Here are the other fixes that could be done, ones that you might consider extreme or too much work, but remember this class design concept is NOT viable any more.

-Increase damage at lowest levels. Clerics and Druids should never out damage a Wizard with a single blast, not indoors, not outdoors, not against undead, not ever. That just isn't a rational design concept. If the Wizard class followed the weak/powerful model, we might have something but not the way Everquest is made now.
-Add in small skin spells at lower levels. These could use small reagents and have little damage absorbing components, but that spells the difference between life and death at level 16. Adding in one that uses a Malachite at level 4 (when Magicians get pets) would give Wizards a level of protection that they don't have now.

That's for the lowest levels, below level 20.

Medium Low levels

At level 16, Wizards get their first skin spell and start to measurably out damage their opponents. 16 is actually a fairly good level for Wizards, they have good protection and decent damage output, plus a great AE spell in Pillar of Fire. Unfortunately we run into different problems.

Level 20 gives Wizards self ports, which is pleasent, its a break point many have been striving toward. While we get fewer locations than Druids (can't get to Freeport area until level 24, oddly), Druids are masters of travel. However, there are some real problems.

Force Shock is an all-or-nothing interrupt blast that is highly resistable. Not only does it not get partial resists, but it has a stun/interrupt component that makes it easier to resist. Unlike Enchanters, who can lower their target's resistances, Wizards just have to watch as the spell bounces, hyper-aggroing the monsters a good 30-60% of the time, depending on the monster.

Further, the only other blast that a Wizard has at this point is Tishan's Clash, another highly resisted stun with damage that does less than 40 damage at this level. In addition, for the Wizard to use cold damage, he has to dip to level EIGHT for the 38 point Shock of Frost blast. That is a triple whammy that makes many Wizards flat out quit, no lie.

In order to contribute, Wizards are using spells a full circle lower to hope to hit more often, something Magicians can sympathise with - but we don't have pets nor good damage shields to fill the gap.

At level 24, the new spell is finally cold, after 15 levels without a new one, but it only does 10 more damage than Shock of Force at level 20. There are some AE spells to use, although they aren't very sparkling. The mid 20s are very unpleasent for most Wizards, they struggle with few weapons and no real group friendly abilities.

At level 29, the Wizard gets an evac and 3 group ports, which help in group friendliness. Mind you, hardly anyone evacs at these levels, I have used it 3 times in my career even at level 45. But its something that Wizards bring to the table at least. Level 29 also brings Bonds of Force, an AE snare spell that can be used in a pinch if none of the other five classes that can snare are missing from the group, and can allow the Wizard to quad kite... eventually.

However, the damage output still is not very noteworthy compared to others of the same level range. Further, the 20s bring something painfully to light about Wizards that you didn't run into very often before.

Monsters more than 6 levels or 1.2 times the Wizards level in power than the Wizard have an enormous increase in resistances (it is said not to be 100% but it might as well be, there isn't any point in dumping spell after spell into a target and doing nothing in the hopes of getting a few points through). Hunting in my 20s, it was rare not to face 1-2 monsters PER NIGHT of hunting that were above this cap. While other classes can contribute using pets, healing, damage shields, buffs, or just beating on the brute... Wizards have nothing, not a thing to offer. That is a weakness of the class, but where is it compensated for? To have a distinctive and intentional design flaw like that, one would expect a proportional increase in ability in special cases, but where is it? This is especially painful in the 20s due to the way the formula works, until level 34 or so, the formula doesn't exceed the 6+ level cap.

How to fix this? Pretty simple really.

-Add in a blast or two each level, so that every level the Wizard gets at least 2 Direct Damage attacks, not including stun spells. They should be scattered across types of damage, so that the Wizard never has to dip more than 1 spell circle to get a given type of damage. Either this is a strength of the class or it is not. It should be, and isn't presently.

-Increase the 6+/1.2 cap for Wizards to 8+/1.3 which would have minimal effect overall, but would help these levels significantly. Afraid of rampaging bands of Wizards stalking the land annihilating monsters 8 levels higher than their level? Not going to happen. Resistances, hit points, and damage done by the monsters are far too high, if we could do it, we'd do it to monsters 6 levels over our heads. Ever seen that? Me either.

-Look at AE spells across the first 30 levels, make sure they are all viable and efficient enough to use regularly. If they aren't used at these levels, they will not be in most groups higher, because you get kind of set in your way at higher levels. This is partly human nature and mostly the "if I die I lose a full night's work in xps without a res" effect.

Dream fixes:

-Add in a Lure at level 29. Why Lures? First off, monsters are starting to have notable resistances, and some are even immune to magic (EXG in Sol A) at this power level which shuts out Wizards TOTALLY at this point.
-As an alternate, treat lures as if they are immune to the level cap (only these highly mana inefficient spells ignore the 6+ resist cap).
-Add in a Draught at level 20. Why? because at level 20 your spells are starting to become very slow, so slow that you will find yourself challenged to get off that last blast in an emergency. Due to the 'cannot see your target' effect when a goblin gets his toenail behind a bump in the floor, you must tag monsters NOW to stop them. Further, a spell that casts faster than 3 seconds is never going to be fast enough to be considered quick damage.

Well, that was a lot of reading. Besides one meeting, all I've had time to do today is read these class threads and write up very long replies. So let's get on with it!

In general there are few 'balance' issues in this thread. There are several problems, but things (even seemingly important ones, like the whole Admiral Akbar line of spells) aren't balance issues. And I want to look at the balance issues first.

A bazillion hit points: The biggest balance issue that I see is the problem that we've given the purely mana dependent wizard in Velious. Once the mana is gone and the enemy is only partially wounded, the wizard is no longer contributing to the fight (except that he is meditating in preparation for helping out later). I see this as the single most important issue facing the wizard class as a whole. And, as I'm discovering with most classes, it's mostly a post 50th level issue.

I've seen two basic approaches to a solution to this problem. Unfortunately, both are very dramatic (though it may require a dramatic change to rectify this issue). One is simply to increase the wizard's mana reserve, the other is to create a new line of spells that does a percentage of damage based on the current hit points of the target. Honestly, I don't like either one. Raising the mana pool would be a terrific balancing problem. This not only increases the potential damage output of the wizard while he's fighting the bazillion hit point enemy, but at all other times as well. I can't really see this working.

I admit that the % damage spell idea is interesting. And it does target the problem a lot more specifically. This also seems like an idea that could cause troublesome balancing issues, but far fewer than the mana pool increase. It would require a ton of work, assuming that this sort of spell is even possible…

There is a third idea that I have, but I can't tell you about because it is based on things that you folks don't get to know (sorry). But it is not too far from the percentage damage idea anyhow.

Regardless, this is the main concern that I have about balancing the wizard class. And it will probably remain at the top of the wizard list.

The other more general issue is about the whole level limit cap on wizard (and everyone else's for that matter) damage spells. I can see how this would impact a wizard more than others, since they have little to do when they can't blast things. I'm not sure if this should be changed, but I'll bring it up.

With the list below, a + means it's on the list, a – means that it is not on the list, and a ? means that I don't think I understand the problem and need more input.

These are my ideas for balancing wizards. I'm going to list it out in the following format:
What I don't like
Why I don't like it
What I'd do to fix it

Vision Utility Spells
I'm not just talking about the telescopic vision spells. From what I've read, that's been covered. Specifically I'm talking about the spell Magnify (level 29). This is a telescopic vision spell with the added bonus of having Ultravision. The problem is, it has a short duration, and you can't be very effective when your zoom is that high (your rez/FoV drops, you have no way of guaging your distance from your target, and the like).
I'd fix this by removing the telescope part, and increasing the duration of the spell to match that of Heat Sight (our infravision spell). Infravision is pretty useless for finding your way around some places, and there's not always a mage to get a coldstone from nearby. Basically, I'd like an Ultravision spell for us non-Dark Elves.

Staff of the Wheel
The rod in Erud's Crossing is way too hard to get. The sharks there can see through every type of invisibility, there's a LOT of them, and they're all (reportedly) in their high 40s - low 50s.
I'd remove a few of the sharks, or make it that they can't see through at least invisibility to animals. Also, when I was doing this, a shark flew out of the water and ate me. I think this was an isolated bug but please look into it (this happened several months ago).

Invisibility
This isn't specific to wizards but I think it should be looked at. Invisibility and Invisibility to Undead stack, but effectively cancel each other out (if you have both cast upon you, then EVERYTHING can see you).
Either make it that they don't stack, or make it that they don't cancel each other out. This got me killed when I went after my rod in Guk.

Portals
I don't think it's fair that we have to wait until level 39 to get a group teleport to Western Commonlands. Even level 34 would be more appropriate, considering the volume of people that want to get there (the East Commonlands tunnel is more or less the marketplace of Antonica, if not all of Norrath, and people constantly want to go there to peddle their wares, and druids/higher level wizards aren't always available).
I'd like to see the level on this spell be reduced to 29 or 34.

Harvest
This is a great spell. I have one problem with it though: because it has a 10 minute recast time, if it's removed from our memorized area, when we rememorize it, it will take 10 minutes before we can use it. This is probably intended but I don't think it's right. This is effectively giving us one less spell slot to work with (those of us who use the spell, at least).
I would fix this in one of two ways: either make it that the recast time doesn't tie into the rememorizing time (probably not feasible), or make it a skill available at level 34 (not unlike Mend, Lay Hands, Harm Touch, and other class specific skills).

Gloves
Not specific to wizards in any way. I still wear my Gloves of the Gatecaller for the look, and they weigh 5.5! I'd like to see more black gloves, maybe even with some stats on them.

O'Kiels Radiation
Here's what I like about this spell (and its counterpart, O'Kiels Flickering): It increases the SV Fire of the target. Eventually, it also lasts long enough that it's a pretty effective anti-fire buff... provided that you plan on keeping only one person protected. The spell lasts just long enough for the average battle, so by the time you've cast it on everyone in your group, the first person's begins to fade away.
I'd fix this by increasing the duration of the spell and removing the damage component (or leaving it in, since it doesn't really do much at all).

Rain spells
These spells aren't widely used probably because the situations for which they would be used effectively are so infrequent (let's leave the pet factor out of this). These spells would be best used on a single creature to the effect of a DoT without kiting. The immobile creature (either kept in place by your tank or rooted) will receive a gradually increasing amount of damage over a time (the 4 hits) while the taunt factor is kept low (the time between the hits helps in this). The thing is, it isn't often that when you're fighting with a tank/group that you'll be taking on one creature at a time, and the continuous hits tend to cause root to break and the target often gets out of the range of the spell before he has been hit to the spell's full effect.
I would fix this in one of the following ways:
increase the number of hits and lower the aggro (so you can hit stuff surrounding the main target without drawing too much attention from the tank)

make root have less of a chance of breaking with rains than other spells (probably not feasible, and also probably not appropriate since grouping is being largely encouraged at this point)

have rains be a manner of a chain-DD, keeping the damage isolated to the target, so it would work as if the caster cast a small DD 4 times successively (I don't think this is a good idea but I thought I'd throw it in anyway; might be nice for a new line of spells)
