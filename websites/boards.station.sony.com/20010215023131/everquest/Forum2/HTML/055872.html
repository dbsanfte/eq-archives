I feel the only change that really needs to be given to rangers is the evade skill. This has been said before, but I thought I would post in attempt to try and articulate why this skill is necssary, and balanced for the ranger class.

The real question is, after level 25+, rangers are going to predominantly be traveling in a group, as much out of need as out of desire. A ranger's "group effectiveness" is a MUCH stronger consideration for class balance than say a necromancer, who can act independently of a group if they want, even in the later levels. Hence, while suggestions for ports and binding, and so on, are interesting, the real point of class balance for a ranger is to consider a ranger's contribution to a group, and an average group's desire to take a ranger as one of their members --and this becomes an ever increasing and paramount concern for rangers in the mid to late game.

So the real question is, when in a group, what does a ranger bring to the table? I think all "non-stackable buffs a ranger can cast can be ruled out --which is most of his buffs". This is not to say that they are not useful when alone, but because we are considering a ranger's role in a group, the role he plays from 25+, that in most group situations, especially ones with a cleric, the warrior, ranger, thief, monk, etc, will have the SAME buffs ...the buffs cast by the cleric, or the haste caste by the enchanter, or the buffs of the shaman, or druid, and so on, all better than a ranger's at an equal level--this is how a group works. So what does the ranger add in the way of spells if not his buffs? A ranger adds snare, an extremely powerful mob control device, and critical in dungeon fighting, a ranger can add sow, and a ranger can cast a comparatively light DS to his level on the lead tank, and can be a "top off" healer (not in battle but between to reduce downtime) ..none of these things are things that can't be done better by another potential member of the group, but is nice for the ranger to do because his mana is not as essential to the overall welfare of the group as say, the druid who might want to shed those lesser duties to preserve mana for heals, buffs, evacs, nukes. On test, the ranger offers the same offensive attack as a warrior (minus the crits, crippling blows). A ranger adds increased damage output against ONE mob through his fire based direct damage spells. This is not insignificant, when comparing against other classes, but the advantage of a group is to go through many mobs quickly, and over the period of say ten mobs, the warrior, monk, and rogue will all do more damage overall because they do not have to med back up their enhanced attack, but rather get them as skills, such as crits, back stabs, special attacks, and so on --where as a ranger will find himself having to sit out a battle or two to recover his mana for another burst of increased damage, or simply cast less, lowering his overall damage contribution, possibly below that of a warrior (over say 20 mobs).

The ranger adds increased average damage potential over a Shadow Knight and a Paladin, and would seem a better choice but for the SEVERE defense penalties a ranger suffers. A ranger's skill settings in defense, riposte, parry, and dodge are lower than all the melee classes, not to mention that he is the only chain based melee class aside from a rogue. It can be argued, as far as group contribution goes, a ranger is not competing with the Shadow Knight, Paladin, or Warrior, because he cannot handle their role as damage absorber, but infact, is competing with the role rogue (more like assassin). The classes may seem different, but what they do in a group is strikingly similar. Both are there to add increased damage and not absorb it. While a rogue adds his powerful backstabs, and to a lesser extent, poisoned effects, the ranger contributes lower damage and offers a small selection of lesser utility spells, such as snare, healing, sow, ds. This seems a fair trade off, and both could be tactically important. However --a rogue is also MUCH more defensible than a ranger because of evade, and a ranger's in ability to avoid damage completely negates his ability to deal it, and rather than a group asset, the ranger becomes a group liability, increasing the downtime of the group due to excessive healing needs. I think it is arguable that the more damage a class can potentially deliver, the more that class should have to be protected to maintain balance. A wizard is a good example of this. However, with thieves and monks, they both have their own, powerful, defensive measures.

If Verant feels that a ranger's contributions in his utility spells off-sets his inability to be as damaging as a thief or monk, I can see this, but I do not feel that rangers spell list is so advantageous that he should do less damage AND have no defense countermeasures (Jolt was an attempt to address, but has not been effective) Verant may disagree on this point of defense, but as for me, I would much rather have an shadow knight snaring than ranger, and a monk or rogue filling in as a damage tank because the rogue can scout and picklocks, and the monk can feign pull, and both of them do a LOT more damage than a ranger with less downtime. I think the answer to all this is extremely simple. Give Rangers evade. Gave it to them at level 40, when clerics REALLY start hating rangers. Evade does not take away from the shadow knight, warrior, or paladin because a ranger needs someone to "evade" onto, meaning the role of a damage absorber must still be filled be soemone else. Evade will not raide the ranger above a thief, becuas ethe theif sitll does more damage, still can be invisibile to living and dead a tht esame time, still can pack locks, etc. Evade will simply place the ranger as a more viable option when considering the role of damage tank, making him harder to just "pass over" when making a group.

First, while I did solo quite a bit until my late teens, I'm not too fond of ideas or changes that make me better at soloing. I enjoy grouping immensly, and that's sort of what I gather VI wanted with EverQuest, so the changes I would like to see made should make me more desirable in groups, not more effective as a solo'er.

So, with that said, here's my primary list of desires:

1) Fix tracking. Give us a scrollbar so we can see everything within our tracking range. Allow us to sort by distance and/or con level. Give us more filtering options, like filtering out things that con at a certain level, or even filtering out NPCs like guards and/or merchants. Without this, the track skill becomes useless after a certain level. For example, My tracking is at 180. I can track a merchant on the other side of the zone, but I can't see the orc standing right next to me. I play with my wife, who plays a druid, and she does all of the tracking now as it's much more useful at the lower skill level (she can see things that are closer to us). It's rather embarassing when a group relies on the tracking skills of its druid or bard. Justify its current implementation all you want, but it doesn't work the way it should (if your designers say it works the way it was intended, they should be fired).

2) Fletching needs a dramatic upgrade. I stopped working on fletching at 102, as it was simply not cost effective. It's very difficult to make money from it, as archery is a skill only useful for pulling (more on that later), and thus no-one wants to pay 700p for a high-end fletched bow (that probably cost me 4x that amount to make due to the rediculously high failure rates). Also, arrows are just too expensive, and don't seem to make that much difference in damage anyway (I've hit for 1 point of damage using 1 dmg arrows as well as 5dmg arrows). Since arrows can't be recovered (which has been suggested before, an idea I strongly agree with), and I can get summoned arrows rather easily (and cheaper I might add), fletching as a skill is relatively useless. I'd like to see a dramatic increase in the number of bows that can be made, arrows that proc (which may require the addition of more ranger spells), a decrease in failure rate on the upper range bows, and a decrease in compoent cost for upper range bows and arrows.

3) While I'm only a level 35 ranger, the discussions I've seen on the Trueshot discipline really concern me. Without the benefit of first hand experience, I can't suggest a fix, but I would definately like to see rangers discussing this discipline in a favorable light, so obviously something needs to be done about it. Perhaps the addition of more ranger only/no drop bows with higher damage and lower dly would help this some.

4) We need more HP, or barring that, some additional self only buffs that stack with others to give us more HP. We have the same HP as caster classes at the higher levels? Does this make sense if we're supposed to be an offensive melee class?

5) I would also like to see defense raised to 230, perhaps even higher. This has been suggested before, a flip-flop of the offense/defense of SKs/Paladins. It's been said that we have low AC, low HP, and low defense because we can duel wield. That's too much of a penalty for duel weild, in my opinion. We need to have an increase in HP, AC, defense, or all three; giving us the ability to duel wield in exchange for all 3 just gives us the ability to MAYBE do a little more damage at the expense of being MUCH easier to hit and kill. I mean, come on, bards can duel wield and they can wear plate. We're taking a triple penalty here.

6) All of the other hybrids have some sort of unique skill that's not available in other classes. For example:
Paladins: Lay of hands
Shadowknights: Harm touch
Rogues: Backstab
Monks: Kill a MOB with one strike (Ashenhand) and many many other cool abilities
Rangers: nothing
We do not have any sort of truly unique skill (bards and druids get tracking, and that doesn't have anything to do with combat). We need some sort of ranger-only ability to equal that given to the other hybrids. How about a skill and/or ability that gives us a chance to kill a creature with one shot from a bow? Make it a discipline/skill whatever. You can call it a 'called shot', if you will. I think that Trueshot was meant to be this 'ranger only' ability, but as I've said before, from previous discussions I've seen, it's sorely lacking. Whatever you give us, please don't make it a once-a-day Bind or port. That won't help out my group in a combat situation. I want something that makes me more desirable in groups, not some twiddly utility ability that's readily available from the nearest caster.

7) We should have the dodge/parry/riposte caps of rogues/monks. To quote a previous post: 'Being a dual wielding class, we should be better at skills like parry and riposte over classes that are limited to one weapon. Being limited to lighter chain classes of armor would make you believe we should be able to dodge better then someone in full plate. Its pretty amazing if you look at it from that angle that ranger dodge/parry/riposte are lower then Paladin/SK's'.
Indeed, paladins/shadowknights got an increase to their reposte cap (200) where rangers stayed the same (150). In fact, I think we should have the riposte cap of warriors, as it's almost an offensive skill.

8) We should also have the evade skill. Like rogues, we wear light armor and have a high agility/dex. You would think that this combination would allow us to have a skill like evade (light armor+high agi+high dex=ability to evade/dodge). This might also increase our chances of survival.

9) Archery is broken. The whole 'your target is too high/low' is rediculous, and looks more like the work of a lazy programmer and a shortcoming of the engine than any real believable explaination. I should be able to hit anything as long as I can see a majority of it. Archery is useless right now for anything but pulling. It should be a very viable form of combat for rangers, if not THE main form of combat. If you made archery as effective if not more so than hand-to-hand melee combat, there would be no need for higher defense or hitpoints, we'd kill our MOB's from a distance, which is my vision as a ranger anyway. We should get double/triple attacks with the bow (this has been covered before). We should even get crippling blows with the bow. The ranger should be THE MASTER of the bow bar none, and should be able to do more damage with a bow than any other class, and this damage should be at least as much as you could do in hand-to-hand combat, if not significantly more so.

10) The other hybrids have been given some of the defining abilities of their parent class (i.e. Paladins: resurection, shadowknights: pets, summon corpse). If this is the case, then rangers should be given the ability to perform group ports, or at a minimum, group evacs. This would give us some of the defining abilities of our parent class, just like the other hybrids.

11) The Raincaller bow needs to be modified. Give it a 25 dmg/40 dly with unlimited charges so it will actually be useful and worth questing for.

a) Add Endure Cold at 22: May be useful in some situations, and in-line with what a ranger should be able to do.

g) Add Panic Animal at 22: Totally unnecessary, and not in line with what I see as a ranger. Replace with either charm animal or another DD fire/cold spell. I'd prefer a charm animal spell where the animal didn't agro after the spell wore off. I find it rediculous that shamans get a wolf pet, shadow knights get some sort of pet, but neither druids nor rangers get this ability.
i) Offense and weapons skill made identical to warriors: Excellent start, but with the increase in agro that this will do, we need a comperable increase in defensive skills, or we're going to get killed even quicker. I like the idea of rangers being the high-damage hybrid, but it comes at too high a price.

13) Give us more unique spells (ranger only). With a druid/shaman in the group, my buffs are next to useless, and my DD spells are so weak that I could do more damage in melee in the time it takes to cast them that they simply don't get used. End result: I become little more than a weak tank and a mana sponge. If we had more unique, ranger only spells (perhaps a line of ranger only DD spells that do similar damage to Druid spells at the same level). The new spells are nice, but they have stacking issues (others have given examples) that need to be removed in order to make them more useful in grouping situations. What about a series of ranger-only dots that stack with Druid dots?

14) Give us a few more shaman buffs, like some buffs for dex and stamina. This would round out our selection of buff spells nicely, and make us more useful in groups with druids but no shamans. In fact, we need to have an upgrade to feet like cat, at the higher levels this spell doesn't last long enough and doesn't do enough to make it worth casting.

15) Let us reclaim arrows from corpses. This will offset the cost of fletching these arrows. Perhaps we could recover only those arrows that actually hit.

16) Lower the level at which we get SoW. I know others have debated this, but if we're supposed to be a lightly armored, fast attack unit, then give us the ability to be fast. Move it from 39 to 30.

17) Give us a haste spell at 39 (or 49 even), goes along with the idea that we're lightly armored, fast attack units.

18) A questable cold based spell at 49, like call of flame, is a great idea (to quote a previous post).

19) If we're supposed to be the most offense based hybrid, give us triple attack at 60, and allow us to do critical hits with our primary hand weapon. That would increase the damage output of rangers nicely, making us more desirable in groups and perhaps make us useful on the uber-MOB raids.
