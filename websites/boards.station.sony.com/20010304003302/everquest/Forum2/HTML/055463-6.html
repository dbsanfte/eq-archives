Absor,

Whilst skimming and/or skipping some posts you missed a major issue many many rangers brought up....

How are rangers to survive dealing out damage as an offensive hybrid? Rangers currently take a quadruple whammy in the defensive skills department. We have no Defense increase, Dodge/parry/repost are all well below that of true melee classes, HP's are extrodinarly low for sombody that takes as many hits as a ranger does. The AC on our armor is some of the weakest out there.

Now some of these penalties are needed to mantain class balance and others are not. First off I will explain the ones I agree with, and why. Followed by my disagrements, and thoughts on improvements.

Lack of Hitpoints - This is reasonable, personally rangers do not need the HP's of the other Tank classes and sit somwhere in the middle of the road. Take a ranger unbuffed and unequipped at 60 and you will probabbly find them somewhere above bards/rouges/monks but below pali/sk/war. This is ideal fits us just fine. Disregard all equipment, anyone can gear themselves to skew the stats including HP.

Low Armor Class on Equip - Once again this is just fine. We are a light tank, thus resulting in the use of light armors, similar to that of a rouge. Monks's armor is even lighter, thus resulting in the lowest AC per peice on melee equip. Pali/SK/Warrior armor is plate classed and ultra heavy, as a result it should provide better protection with less movement etc.

No Defense Upgrade - This I orginally agreed with UNTIL pali/sk/bard gained an increase from 230's to 252. Now the basis for my opinion on this is as follows. With a defensive skill in the 230's plate wearing hybrids tanked on par to our damage output as light tanks. 30 points of Defense is more than equal to 40 points of offense. The addition of very strong 1hd and 2hd weapons, as well as extremely good AC +stat bonuses on some sheilds pretty much makes up for some of slack of not having dual weild. Now explaining how I felt the old caps were balanced let me shed some light on what I feel is imbalanced about the "new" caps. While Plate hybrids gained somwhere in the neighborhood of 22 points in defense skill, the ranger only gained 12 points in offensive skills. 12 point in offense would only make a minor change in %dmg for a ranger, whilst 22 points in defense would make a significant change in a hybrid's ability to take dmg. Look at your own systems, people have parsed them out in the past and some of the tables and results are much to long to post here, when asking a dev member may just be that much more expedient. In order to retain the balance in offense/defense Ranger's should scale equally +22 points defense with the other hybrids, putting us at 222 Defense. This change would not make a ranger a tank due to their HP/Armor AC hit. However it would increase the survivabilty without having to rely on a spell, that currently is not a sure thing or even a 25/75 thing.

Dodge/Parry/Reposte skills - Naturally due to their lighter armor, a ranger should have more flexibility in avoiding a opponent. Dodge should be at a level above that of the plate hybrids yet below those of the light pure melees. Parry/reposte "should" be based off the weapon skill of the character, meaning its only natural that a person with higher skill in weapons should parry/reposte better and more frequently than that of sombody less able. Ranger reposte/parry should be above less capable hybrids, but below the more capable pure melees. This skills shouldn't just be a few points above the heavy hybrid tanks, but centered between the heavy hybrids and the light pure melees.

On ranger offense - As a side note I would like to point out that Ranger's while getting dual weild are not instantly 2 times as good as a pali/sk. Far from it in many cases we are only 25% or less better than our heavy hybrids, depending on equipment. This comes from the addition of very good ratio 1 hand weapons, and some very amazing 2handers, in addition to the higher lvl pets of Shadowknights that do dmg like having a similar (somtimes lower lvl) ranger with them as a pet.


Now I am not saying all of the above "should" be implemented, but rather that is my outlook on how the class matches directly with the other melee's. As it is currently rangers rely on a very unstable source for both dmg and evasion of dmg. Spells, by properly balancing the melee skills of rangers you wouldn't need to change jolt, you wouldn't need to shore up rangers with new or diffrent spells that add HP or AC (not that I am saying they are bad).

In closing I'd just like Verant to honestly sit down and test these final changes thouroughly, possibly even allow players to voulntarilly be moved to Test and then returned as they were before the move to their home servers for a period of a few weeks. This I think would most likely help more than any number of internal testers hackin away, its been admited before that active players can find bugs just as well if not better than the testers, and those who have had the most experience could probably give the most feedback when it comes to how their class plays.

Suggestion for Jolt line...

It occurs to me the most important factor in using any skill is practice. Take the spell 'Snare' for example. we get it at level 9, and 30 levels later some people still don't know how to use it effectively. Immagine what would happen if you were to give Rangers snare at, say, level 30. What would happen then? At level 30 we are already at a point in the 1-60 grind where using the Snare spell effectively is essential to individual and group survival. If we were not to get it until then there would be chaos as rangers were rushed to try to learn to use an ability they desperately need.

This is how I see the 'Jolt' spell. at level 55 we have already endured 5-10 levels of ineffective hate-list management, at which point we get this spell. Jolt is a godsend (except possibly in Velious, where it's usefulness is debatable). However, improper use of the Jolt spell can lead to the deaths of other party members, or even the whole party, including the ranger him/herself.

I propose a line of jolt spells, to be as follows:

Level 39: A 'watered down' version of the current Jolt spell. Getting a version of this spell early would allow us to learn how to use this ability in a time period where a mistake is not necessarily fatal.

Level 49: This is when we begin to truely need the version of Jolt that exists currently. Creatues in popular zones for these levels just hit too hard for us to be able to sustain our damage output without risking death. I propose moving the current version of Jolt to level 49 or 50.

Level 55: At this point the current version of Jolt is highly resistable in the sort of creatures groups typically face. Sometimes it just doesn't work. Sometimes you just don't have the precious seconds to waste on a recast when you had to wait so the creature didn't immediately turn on the Cleric when you Jolted. I propose a new, less resistable version of Jolt at this level. This would solve the problems of mana efficiency and lost damage output, as well as any hate-list management issues.

Thank you for your time, I hope you will consider this as a viable alternative.

How do you balance and undefined class?
Please bare with me on this as there are numerous factors adding to this question. I will be aluding to comments made by verant staff but please dont ask me which ones. This goes back to the beginning of the game and has had many replies.

1. Rangers complained that they were no longer an effective tank past level 40. They have the lowest HP and AC by far of all the tank classes. Their low very low dodge and parry caps made them an even worse tank than a thief. This all adds to the nick name "Mana Sponge" as most clerics like to call them. Verants first reply to this was "Who said they were a tank."

Granted you eventually looked back over the class and granted them an increased cap on these skills post 50. By the time they are 60 their skills are competent for the level. But what about the long hard road from 40-60.

2. Rangers always thought (assumed) that they would be the masters of the bow. This idea was further justified when you started putting in many ranger only bows and quests for bows. Even in the beginning the Trueshot was a "signature" weapon of the ranger.

When critical hits for weapons were introduced rangers were granted criticals with only the bow. This once again showed people that rangers were archers. Then we found that warriors also shared that skill. We no longer stood out with the weapon.

The bow also proves to be too cumbersome of a weapon to use as your main weapon because of ammo supplies. You could fill your entire inventory with arrows and run out of ammo within the first hour of a camp.

When rangers complained about this and asked for a summon arrow spell or anything to truely make this skill our signature skill Verant replied "Who said rangers were supposed to be the masters of archery."

After all this when disciplines were given out rangers got "Trueshot" as their main attack discipline. This is a nice skill that makes us usefull for 5 minutes every hour and a half.

3. Tracking was said to be our forte. The masters of the wilderness. There are many problems and issues with this and you are working on some of them. For that I am thankfull. However this is mainly usefull outdoors and does little to help us in the dungeon environments. Even outdoors until some changes are made to the skill there is little to no benefit of having a ranger over a druid.

4. When in outdoor encounters we are still wanted from time to time. We are wanted for tracking, pulling, sows, and the ability to split mobs with harmony. All of these skills are completely useless in dungeons. In dungeons we are reverted to pure tanks since none of the skills we have are usefull here. With our combat skills, hp and ac the lowest of all the tanks and hybrid tanks in the game it puts us at the bottom of peoples wish lists in dungeons. More of a last resort than anything.

Give rangers a self only sow for indoors and increase thier base hp. We cant pull when one stun from a mob will get us killed before we get back to our party. Our hp are just too low for the high level encounters. An indoor version of harmony. In dungeons where mobs are almost impossible to split and mez is useless it would make the ranger truely an asset.

As you can see from above... Rangers arent master archers, they arent master trackers, they arent truly effective tanks and most of thier skills are useless in dungeon settings. You have stated so yourself. So if we arent any of those things what are we?

In the later stages of the game almost every class has something that sets themselves apart. A "signature skill" if you will. In the higher level encounters these people are coveted for them... but the rangers dont have anything and as such arent wanted. After all who wants a mana sponging half tank that cant take a hit.

My advice is this. Picture in your head what you exactly want a ranger to be and make them that....

If you want them to be archers then give them the summon arrows spell at low levels. Give them an added haste and/or damage bonus when using the bow so they can match or come close to the damage output done by dual wielding melee classes.

If you want them to be a tank then increase thier base HP to a respectable level or give them spells like the Shield of Words line that increases their HP a descent amount. The only time I was an effective tank was when we had a cleric and a shaman in the group both hit me with the shield spells. I wasnt a burden on the clerics then. Also increase their dodge/parry/defense caps so that they can be an effective tank.

Above all else give them a signature skill. One thing that sets them apart from the others and will be usefull to a group at all levels of play in all environments.

Until this class is truly defined there can be no balancing.
