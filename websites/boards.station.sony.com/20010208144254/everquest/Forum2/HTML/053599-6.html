Absor -

Let's try again .

What warriors don't need:
- changes to bind wounds (when we're grouped, it doesn't matter 90% of the time)
- invisibility on cobalt legs (90% of the warriors will likely never see cobalt legs anyway and as someone else noted, the only time the legs would be useful is on a corpse run, when we have no armor anyway
- more AC. As people have noted, there appears to be a "soft cap" on AC at 1100. At L54, with only 2 pieces of cobalt (gloves, boots), and still wearing a Skull-shaped barbute (I swear the Kira's in Hate have hidden all the helms , when fully buffed, I can get to around 1050AC. If/when I manage to get either Velious quest armor, Plane of Growth pieces, or more Cobalt, it shouldn't be that hard to hit 1100.
- increased damage. We do fairly well, though frankly, the offense-oriented disciplines are really just eye-candy after Defensive is available.
- new travel abilities. We've done fine to this point, we don't need to run faster, leap continents in a single bound, or anything else like that.

I think there are a few misconceptions:
* Warriors have a huge AC advantage over hybrids. Well, maybe we do over Rangers, but most SKs and Paladins of roughly my level have equal or better AC (in part due to higher availability of their Kunark armor, but also due to the shield). And as someone noted, if a Paladin/SK has an AC of 1100, and we have 1200, I'm not sure how much difference it makes (would be interesting to see hard numbers if anyone has them).

* Warriors have a big HP advantage over hybrids and other melees. Okay, we have a sizeable advantage over monks, rogues, and bards. It might even make sense to bump them up a bit. If I recall correctly, the higher level SK in my guild has more HPs than I do, and has only 100 or 200 less than our higher level warrior. If you take into account the fact that most mobs you fight post-50 hit for at least 100 a pop, that's one extra hit. I don't see this as a big advantage.

* Our epic is middle of the road. Let me give my perception and you can think about it some more . I figure there are a few ways to measure the effects of the epic:
Q1) comparison to existing items already available for that class
Q2) comparison of how having the epic effects a class in a single group, and on a raid.
Q3) difficulty of the mobs to obtain the epic and drop rate from those mobs.

A1) The warrior epic provides a relatively small incremental benefit over other weapons that can be acquired with less difficulty for L50+ folks (A jade mace can be acquired in your 40s).
A2) While the small stat bonuses are nice (especially resistances), they aren't a huge effect. No haste means that you're really not going to do substantially more damage than previously. WHEN the weapon procs, you can taunt about as well as you did with Jace Mace/Lamentation + whatever. On a raid, the incremental damage isn't significant, and the extra taunt effect is marginally useful.
A3) Requires rare drop off Maestro (1 in 10, maybe?), uncommon drop off Spiroc Lord (5th Isle sky), uncommon Queen of Chardok (pre-Velious, deemed the toughest mob in the game outside Veeshan's peak) in an area where fast respawn makes multiple attempts problematic, scales from 2 Kunark dragons (granted, the EJ dragon is probably the easiest of the various Kunark dragons, maybe the fish is easier), uncommon drop from CT's Lieutenants (Larry, Curly, and Moe).

It was stated by Abashi previously that there wasn't really any attempt to balance the quest difficulty or quest rewards across classes (I find this curious). But you must admit there will be comparisons made.

Most of the items for other classes (with the exception of the SKs and Wizards that need rare drops off Cazic Thule, and the Magicians who need the various rare-drop staffs) tend to be "always drops" off difficult mobs. Chardok King for clerics, Chardok Prince for enchanters, for example.

And looking at the effects of the cleric and enchanter items on a raid (mana-free revivisence, mana-free haste), in my opinion, those have a much bigger effect on the game.

I'm _NOT_ suggesting the power of the warrior epic needs to be increased. If you opt to keep it unchanged, I would suggest you make some of the items "always drops" or at least up the frequency some. My personal suggestion would be to have the Spiroc Lord wingblade be an "always drops", since it's such a hassle to get to the 5th isle in sky. Increase the Maestro hand drop rate to 1 in 4 at least (1 in 10 seems unnecessarily cruel, especially when the planes are so crowded). And make the Chardok queen drop rate > 50%. I'd like to see it as an always drop, but if you feel that's too unbalancing, how about 60% instead of 40?

Currently, it's an "Epic Luck" quest. If you're lucky, you get to do everything once. Otherwise, you get the dubious distinction of having defeated some of the most difficult mobs in the game with a band of worthy comrades, enduring many deaths, corpse recoveries, and some frustration, only to decide that it's easier to get one of the new Velious weapons that puts the epic in 2nd place for damage/taunt anyway. And that would be a shame.

Finally, since I've probably worn you out already, please take a closer look at the effectiveness of Taunt, post-L50. I don't feel like I need an AE taunt ability, but when my shaman sticks a slow on a blue creature, it would be nice to be able to have a reasonable chance of taunting off him before he's dead . Maybe something as simple as increase the effectivness of taunt _slightly_ for each level of the warrior above L51. Nothing huge, I'm not asking for 100% effectiveness, just something that would at least let the mob bounce to me once in a while, before it decides it would rather go kill the monk .

A final comment (since I suspect this may be falling on deaf ears anyway)... think carefully about the scenario for which you're tuning...

It's a very different picture to compare L55+ large guild members of different classes than it is to compare the average players of those classes.

A slight increase in the chance of a criticle hit.
A slight increase in the chance of a cripling blow.
A slight increase in the speed of the weapon (until the speed cap is reached, either by skill or by magic, or a combination)
A slight increase in the minimum damage calculation for the weapon.

Criticles:

Speed:

Min. Damage:

Having played a warrior exclusively since May of 1999, I have a few observations to make while my class has the floor. Pretty much everything I have to say is in regards to high-level warriors.

Taunt- At 50+, this skill is very ineffective, especially compared to the 'taunt-like' effects of rogues, monks, and rangers (increased damage output or spell effects which attract aggro). A warrior's ability to attract an enemy's ire should be commensurate with his ability to absorb damage. It is embarassing to be at near-full health at the end of a fight with a lvl 58-65 mob while dead rogues and monks are strewn about. I'd like to see Taunt made more effective, and I am quite keen on the idea of the AE taunt. This would be a big asset.

Disarm- Useless skill, and pointless to even practice. The only possible use for it is in duels, and warriors are hard-pressed to win legitimate duels against non-warriors (paladins, SK's, monks) where spells and spell-like effects overwhelm the warrior's advantages. I suppose if Disarm is intended to be primarily PvP, then it works fine; but it is of no value against NPC enemies. Especially in Velious, where every armed opponent carries a magical weapon that cannot be disarmed.

Balanced weapon damage- I have used a variety of weapons, and I still have found no compelling reason to consider using anything other than dual 1HS as my primary weapons. Even with the changes to 2H weapons, they still generate less damage over the course of a given fight than fast 1H weapons. I'm not comparing apples and oranges either (well, other than in the obvious way); say for example, a pair of Lamentations (not overly difficult to obtain) vs. a Felhammer (very rare). The Lamentations will outdamage the Felhammer by well over 2 to 1 in any prolonged fight against an exp-giving opponent (based on old-fashioned addition of numbers from the combat log, not parsing of data), even though the ratio of the Felhammer is much more favourable. It's all down to frequency of hits; the weapon that strikes more often is going to be more effective. I would like to see 2H weapons become a viable choice as main weapons; not only from a RP/style POV, but as an actual combat asset. This could be done by increasing high-level 2H damage, increasing the skill cap on 2H (probably not the best option), or even by virtue of an auxiliary function (higher critical modifier, higher taunt chance).

Warrior spells- I may be alone in this, but I am not very keen on spell effects from items for warriors that replace the abilities of other classes. Things like Journeyman Boots and similar general purpose items are not a problem; but I don't like seeing warriors with Invisibility and Healing spells built into their gear. There doesn't seem to be a compelling reason for these effects. My own thinking is more along the lines of 'spell effects that enhance the fundamental nature of the class' for item effects. For example, a big ATK buff instead of Invigourate on the high-level warrior chest pieces, or a big poison/disease resist buff on the leg pieces. Strength, Stamina, AC, ATK, speed, and/or resist buffs are what warriors should get as class-specific spell effects. Since most of the warrior spell-effects are not useable in combat anyway, heals and invis are only useful to increase soloing ability, and if you're wearing that armour you sure as hell did not get it solo!

Disciplines- Increase the effectiveness of the offensive disciplines, please. Right now, the only discipline I find very useful is Defensive, and occasionally Mighty Strike. The offensive disciplines rarely pay off in terms of results against their lengthy recharge time, while the defensive disciplines are very useful, even essential, in some fights. I suppose I would just like to see disciplines, especially the top five disciplines, tuned to a point where ANY of them could be considered valuable in a big fight. I would especially like to be given some more payoff for opting to go with an offensive discipline over a defensive one. With Fellstrike, for example, I find that damage is usually EXACTLY four times the base damage, on 6 out of 10 hits. This is worthless to me, as I normally hit for that much or more without a discipline in use (using Lamentations, in this case). I'd like for all the current disciplines to be reviewed and tested to be sure they are working properly; if it's a bug or a glitch, then a perfectly good discipline could be going unused because it's not functioning properly.

I'd like to see Charge replaced altogether, as it goes totally unused once you get past 52nd level. I'd replace it with a discipline that moves you to the top of an enemy's aggro list for every hit you land for 10 seconds, with a 10-15 minute scaling recharge time. This dovetails with my comments on Taunt, above.

Kunark armour- I know it's probably futile, but I'll chime in with my vote to make cobalt helms and bracers just a BIT more available. I can't think of any other Kunark armour pieces that are both so extremely rare and come from such extremely dangerous areas. I'm a big believer in patience, perseverance, and luck, but throw us a bone, here. Reet frogloks and myconids are a big enough risk without item drop percentages below 1%.
