Absor,

Thank you for your response and effort. I would like to address your question of how to make Wizards more group friendly. You are exactly on point with your notion that increasing damage output will do this. However, the increase must be significant enough to act as an incentive for a group seeking out a Wizard specifically for this purpose rather than accepting a more generalist caster class which can provide other benefits as well as nuke. The following is a list of ideas with an EXPLANATION AS TO HOW EACH WOULD MAKE WIZARDS MORE GROUP FRIENDLY WITHIN THE CONTEXT OF THEIR ROLE AS DAMAGE DEALERS:

1. Increase Damage Output by up to 50% more than other casters at comparable level.

This is necessary to make a Wizard more group friendly. If Wizard spells are only marginally more damaging than other casters’ then there is no incentive for a group to take a Wizard over those other classes which can also offer other abilities such as buffs to the group. I offer the concrete figure of 50% as a starting point for discussion. The reason for this is to emphasize that 10% or 20% more damage than other casters is not enough of a difference from other casters to provide an incentive for a group to seek out Wizards rather than more versatile

2. Mana Effeciency.and Stored Mana Benefits

Increased mana efficiency is necessary to make Wizards more group friendly. Conceptually, time is the currency in a MMORPG. Groups generally try to gather the members who will provide the greatest experience in the least amount of time ratio. Wizards are perceived as undesirable because their mana dependency increases the downtime that a group has. Increased downtime equals less experience and therefore Wizards are not as desirable as less mana dependent classes. This is a bigger problem than other casters because of the difference of a stored mana benefit versus a mana burst. For example, A druid casts a sow and it provides a benefit long after the druid has regenerated the mana it cost to cast it. A mage casts a pet, and the pet is around after the mage has regenerated the mana it cost to cast it. By additional example, an enchanter casts a clarity which provides a benefit long after the enchanter has regenerated the mana it cost to cast it. For a wizard the benefit, damage, is an instantaneous one shot benefit which is over the second it is cast. There is no lasting effect. In other words, there is no “stored mana benefit.” Since Wizards do not have the ability to provide stored mana benefits, there mana rate of regeneration should be increased to compensate. This will increase the incentive of groups to invite wizards.

3. Decrease Resists and Remove/Modify Six Level Cap.

Resists equal wasted mana, or damage a wizard is unable to provide. This hits wizards particularly hard because they have nothing else to contribute to a group except their damage. If groups knew that Wizards had a much greater chance of resists than other casters on DDs then this would increase the groups incentive to invite Wizards.

The Six level cap is more situation specific and really concerns aggressive groups which like to pull a lot of yellows and reds. However, these groups are common enough to at least consider either removing the cap or putting a higher spread such as 10 levels on it. Doing so would increase the desirability of Wizards in this type of group because as it stands now, there is no incentive at all for a Wiz to be in this group.

4. Critical Hits.

This is a great idea that some people have though up and seems to make sense as Wizards are the magic counterparts of Warriors. The key issue for this topic is: Will it make Wizards more group friendly. I think it would. Criticals are rare, but in a couple of hours happen with enough frequency that their existence is appreciated by a group. I think in a close situation, where a group is already fairly rounded and seeks a member, it may consider the wizard because of the possibility of a critical hit since it really could tip the tide of battle. For this reason, I think critical DD hits could provide an incentive for groups to consider Wizards.

5. On Screen Damage and how it relates to Group Friendliness.

On screen damage could go a long way towards making Wizards more desirable in a group. Basically, if you increase wizard damage as I indicated in point #1 above and don’t show it, group members may not realize the benefit the wizard brings. The expericed ones would, but lets not start with the assumption that all players are experienced in caster combat. Many of the Melee players may not understand why the wizard is constantly sitting on his tuckhus. If ALL caster damage is shown on screen then group members can observe that the wizard really is contributing his/her share to the fight. Right now, all we see is the Melee damage which really emphasizes in our minds that it is the Melees who are doing the damage, not the casters. If you show those big DD numbers on screen, it will show groups that Wizards are pulling their weight and, thus, increase the incentive for a group to invite a Wizard.

6. Percentage DD

This has been discussed by others and would increase the Wizards’ desirability against uber-mobs while not having a great misbalancing effect on weak mobs. Thus, percentage DDs would increase the desirability of Wizards in groups who fight high hit point Mobs.

7. AE Spells and Aggro

As AE spells stand, they are very undesirable in a group. This is for two reasons: One: They break the Mez and Two: All the Mobs aggro on the Wizard and either kill him or annoy the group by forcing them to save the wizard. While I don’t see a solution to the Mez problem, it seems that an AE spell centered on the tank which causes monsters to aggro the tank could be beneficial for certain types of groups. In other words, those groups without chanters who have tanks and people to heal the tanks. So, an AE spell with aggro on the Tank would increase a Wizard’s desirability in non-chanter groups.

The above ideas would, I believe, increase the incentives for groups to invite wizards and thus would make wizards more group friendly. Most importantly, the above ideas increase group friendliness while in the framework of the Wizard class: Magical Damage Dealers.

I think a random damage component of some sort would be nice because it gets tedious seeing the same dmg over and over. Its very fun for warriors and even the rest of the group to see a warrior try and beat their personal best critical hit when they use a disc. This is something along those lines.

Another possible random modifier could be the Int of the wizard. As masters of knowledge we dont max out our int very often since the popular conception is that Int over 255 doesnt expand our mana pool enough to make it worth the hit to stats and resists. Make a scale that increases the damage of our spells the higher our Int is. Perhaps at 201-210 int we get a .5% increase in damage, at 211-220 we get a .75% increase in damage, at 221-230 we get a 1% increase in damage, at 231-240 we get a 1.5% increase in damage, at 241-250 we get a 1.75% increase in damage and finally at max Int of 255 we get a 2.25% increase in damage. Naturally these percentages can be higher or lower as necessary. Instead of hard percentages each range of Int might have a range of percentages that would be randomly rolled between and added to your base dmg. This would allow for further randomization. I understand this involves a significant amount of coding but its a thought at least.

In my opinion the methods about would not be unbalancing since the absolute highest amount of damage we could do in the example above with a lure of flame for example would be 784 which isnt all that much more damage. Perhaps adding both the group bonus and int bonus would be interesting to further bump up our damage.

"Remember the goal is for we, the Wizards, a one-trick class, to match the damage output of our one-trick melee counterparts, the Rogues."

To match the damage output how? Over the course of 5 seconds? 10 seconds? Over the course of a minute? Over the course of 30 minutes? an hour? If the answer is yes to all of the above, half your nukes would be very efficient dots, and the other half would be very cheap to cast but have a 90% average resist rate.

Furthermore nukes by their very nature have a great deal of flexibility that melee or dots simply do not have. You can choose exactly when you want to apply the damage.

If a wizard and rogue do X points of damage per hour, but the rogue can only do it in a trickle and a wizard can open it like a flood at will, the X points of damage the wizard can do are infinitely more valuable to a group or raid than the X the rogue can do.

Nukes by their very nature offer you a tremendous degree of tactical flexibility, and that should come with substantial drawbacks.

This isn't to say that wizard nukes shouldn't be stronger or more mana efficient. They absolutely should! Wizards should be much much better at their specialty than they are now.

HOWEVER, the point that must not be lost is that a wizard's specialty is nuking, not damage.
