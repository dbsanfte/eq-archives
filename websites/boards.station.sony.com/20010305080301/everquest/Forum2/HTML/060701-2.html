I would suggest a further modification, to prevent a single-team exploit - if you attack a member of a group, you become vulnerable to all the other group members of you own race. This would prevent single-team guild from ganging up on one opponent while his friends have to stand helplessly by and watch him die.

I have been around since the early days of Tallon Zek, and have always loved the server.there have, however, been a few changes that i disagree with.

One of the greatest things about the PvP servers are the risk. I can understand that some people want something of a safety net while at the same time playing on a PvP server. I feel that Item loot was a great part of the PvP experience. Playing a warrior, I came to terms with the fact that casters can be naked, or the possibility that you may get ambushed. Many don't seem to understand that a naked caster is at a distinct disadvantage, and not as big of a threat as some would have them believe. Maybe make a new teams server with Item loot... I am sure some would want to transfer those characters, but alot of people wouldn't mind starting over from scratch on a new server.

If there was to be a new server, I also feel the level cap should be upped... this is a sensitive issue though, as there are many players that feel as though they stand no chance against someone just because that person is higher. If it were possible to make some sort of progressing level range, so the lower level players don't get so discouraged, would add a great new element to the game. High level wars and true guild unity would be some desired results of this.

As it is now, people complain constantly about cross-teaming... though i would never do it myself, it doesn't bother me all that much (I figure, the more enemies, the better). To fix the problem doesn't have to be something nearly as complicated as locking teams, or adding in checks. All that has to be done is remove the faction hit. Some would argue that the faction hit makes for more realistic roleplay. I am under the impression though, that with the faction hit in place, many times a player will opt to not fight someone that they would under normal circumstances. This applies in extreme to the "light" races, where they want to be able to interact with the NPCs in a city dominated by the other players' race. You have to sacrafice a little bit of the Roleplaying to get a lot back.

On the issue of fear and charm... these are special abilities of some classes, and i feel should be allowed. I can see many accidents and hard feelings with fear, and understand the reasons for its removal. Charm, on the other hand, was a great part of the early days on Tallon Zek. Some, naturally, would use it to intentionally make others lose experience... but that is a risk I, for one, am willing to take. I do not see it as being too much different than someone charming a monster, bringing it to where you are rooted, then gating. Things like this will happen, but are definatley acceptable. Many of my friends and I play on a PvP server not to gain exp (or whine at the loss), but to participate in PvP, as we find it to be the most thrilling aspect of the game.

I am glad to see that Verant hs decided to look in to PvP again, as it has been a long time with many changes to the game since last it was tweaked. I will be eagerly awaiting the outcome of this discussion, thank you for listening.
