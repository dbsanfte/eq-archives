Regardless of the balance issues surrounding this particular song, I think this debate has shown how crucial an issue crowd control is. Look at the number of "hot topics" that are directly related:

* Feign-death pulling
* Camping-and-pulling instead of dungeon crawling
* Snaring (another bard issue)
* Mesmerising

Maybe there's some fundamental problem with mob population density or aggro tendencies at the core of this, but what it all boils down to is this:

* A group must find a campsite relatively free of wandering mobs.
* The group must pull a number of mobs not exceeding a certain limit, which depends mainly on the relative strength of the mobs to the party, and the presence or absence of an enchanter in the group. Classes that can FD are good at accomplishing this.
* The group must prevent injured mobs from running away and pulling more mobs back with it. Classes that can snare or root are needed for this.

Dubious strategies (such as kiting) aside, the above is the sine qua non of adventuring in EQ. Either you do this successfully or you die. Therefore, your group must consist of a combination that can perform these functions. Once you've got that covered, then you shoot for things such as:

* Reducing downtime so that you can pull more often and thereby gain more loot and XP. Bards are famous for this, and many other support classes contribute significantly.
* Increasing the number of mobs you can handle, both for safety's sake and for more loot and XP. Enchanters are the strongest here because of their mes abilities, but all classes, of course, contribute.

I think bards are upset because we've recently learned of significant problems with our ability to contribute in two of these key areas: the number of mobs a group can handle (lullaby) and our ability to help prevent runners (chains). We're increasingly dependent on our ability to reduce downtime to provide value to a group. Of course, we can still increase a groups combat effectiveness significantly, but it feels like our overall value has decreased.

When asked about "the Vision" for bards, Abashi said, "That's for you to figure out and establish given the tools that we've provided." Personally, the issues with chains and lullaby have forced me to narrow my vision a bit. I used to think of bards as "jacks of all trades" that could compensate somewhat for the absence of a specialist in any key area. I'm seeing bards in a much more limited support role now.

Food for thought...thanks for listening.
