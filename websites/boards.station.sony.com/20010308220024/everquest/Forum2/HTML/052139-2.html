As 51 lvl enchanter, I am just becoming acquainted to the "end-game" scenario that many Enchanters have mentioned in previous posts. Since this post is about balancing issues, let me direct your attention to the issue of balancing the enchanter class thru the entire life of the class.

I don't think I'm alone (atleast amoung enchanter character players) in thinking that the Enchanter plays the most pivotal role of all within the game, even exceeding the healing role of clerics in a group. My reason for this is that a good enchanter enables the group to take on five mobs which would require three clerics in a group to keep the tanks (and clerics) alive. A poor enchanter will get the entire group killed. If Verant had designed the game to limit encounters to one or two mobs, the enchanters role would be much less pivotal. This is water under the bridge because multiple mob encounters provide the greatest feeling of danger and thus the greatest enjoyment in the game.

I'll be the first to admit that I feel that the enchanter is TOO powerful in many situations especially in the middle level (20-45). However, in the higher levels, the enchanter (along with alot of other casters) has been relegated to the background. This is not necessarily a bad thing yet the WAY it has been done (giving mobs MR or Mez immunity, increasing mob hps to the point at which dozens of ice comets can't kill it) not only ruins the enjoyment many long standing enchanters had, but demonstrates that Verant needs to examine balance of each class within level bands.

Increasing the MR of mobs to our spells in SoV is not necessarily bad. I've been amazed at the fact that my mezzing is not resisted better by mobs in Kunark and the original game areas. However, if you are looking for balance, it is NOT achieved by making one whole portion of the game useless to our class. If you were looking to increase the challenge of mobs to groups having enchanters, you should increase the MR to mobs throughout the entire game to a level where it becomes a challenge (though not the ridiculousness that SoV is designed to).

As far as the illusions go, I frankly am tired of the whole deal. I don't necessarily want any more illusions but I think the excuses that Verant has given then found out to be not truthful reflects pretty badly on your decisionmaking.

Our debuffing ability of mobs is pretty ridiculous as it appears that the mobs have unlimited stats such that our spells have no apparent affect. While it may be useful in PvP, Verant has made clear that the enchanter class is never going to be balanced in PvP by the way your have handled PvP issues with enchanter spells. The previously posted suggestion of debuffing by percentages sounds useful since that is the way the attack speed buffs and debuffs work.

I find it decidely unbalanced the lack of reasonable enchanter-only equipment that can be obtained at the mid-levels (ie prior to becoming planes capable) when compared to every other class in the game. There are three classes that get beat on the most: warrior (taunt), cleric (healing), and enchanter (Tash, Mezz). The AC granting spells that Enchanters receive do not compensate them in a balanced way for the increased AC that each of these other classes can achieve at these levels from farming equipment.
