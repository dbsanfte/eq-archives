Food for thought Absor:

Pets (and their masters) will never be welcome in fights against uber mobs until a couple major problems are solved.

1. Pets contribute more to lag than they do to killing the mob. (see below for explanation)

2. Pets spend more time running away from mobs than they do damaging the mob, and bring friends back to the raid when they stop running.

3. Pets are generally hit by AE damage spells cast by the uber mob more than a tank would be. Level differences and lack of high enough resists/resist gear place pets at a severe disadvantage to an uber mob.

************ Solutions *************
EXPLANATION OF LAG PROBLEM AND FIX:
Every time a character (NPC or PC) takes an action that needs to be reported to other players, it contributes to lag. If I have 20 PC's and pets hitting on a mob, every time a hit occurs, it gets copied to 20 people, creating 20 * 20 = 400 messages. These get filtered out using the chat filter, yes. But the messages still seem to be sent from the server to the client, although they get ignored by the client - this reduces client side lag but does nothing about server lag. Thus, one way to reduce linkdeaths during a raid is to reduce the number of things hitting the uber mob and have them use slower, harder hitting weapons. One guild on my server consistently fails to take down trak despite bringing 60 or so people to the raid. They wonder why half go linkdead almost immediately. The answer is simple.

Take 60 people on raid: 60 * 60 = 3600 messages per "round"

as opposed to

Take 36 people on raid: 36 * 36 = 1296 messages per "round"

The reason pets will always be a problem on uber mobs is that they contribute to this lag without doing a reasonable amount of damage in return. If I have 36 "slots" for the raid before lag becomes unbearable, do I want a pet, or do I want a tank? Simple, when it comes to damage output and hp, tank > pet. The only solution that "balances" this problem is creating a new line of pet with the same damage output per minute (same ratio) but making the pet hit less often for more damage. The pets will do the same damage over time, but contribute far less to the lag problems we see on raids.


FIXING PETS RUNNING AWAY:
The test server has a change that makes a 59th level pet spell that makes the pet immune to fear. The pet classes simply put need resist buffs specifically for their pets. They need to be balanced to increase teh pet's resists to keep the pets in the battle at least as frequently as a tank would be. The current Immune to Fear spell given to Magicians on Test has 2 problems.
First, pet classes begin to have this problem at about 45 or 46, not at 59. Second, immunity is too powerful. Make it an insane buff, but do not allow it to make the pets immune. I'd rather see a + 120 MR pet only buff than an immunity to fear. Yes, this makes them more resistant to magic based ae damage, but see #3 above.

More food for thought Absor:

Recast time is a very effective way to make spells that "tweak" the uber encounter without unbalancing the rest of the game. High impact, low duration, long recast time spells are where it is at. The long recast delay means I will use the spells only when the capability gives me a decided advantage (i.e. only on an uber or close to uber encounter).

The "normal" power curve for spell balance can be exceeded if the recast delay on the spell limits your ability to chain cast it. Some examples are already in game: Dictate, Boon of the garou, Harvest, Dive Aura.

The wizards have a problem for instance in Velious - a full load of mana does nothing to the mobs in question. Rather than go out and do a percent based DD, instead, create an insane damage to mana ratio spell that can only be cast infrequently. Here's how to design the spell:

Figure out how much each class at a given level, when properly equipped, can do in terms of damage per minute.

Determine where you want a wizard to fit in terms of damage per minute and call that X. To make this work, it has to be on the low side of damage output capabilities for the various classes, but still a respectable amount of damage.

Figure out how much mana a wizard will regain per minute. The calculation should have the wizard without claritied, without mana song and should include chain casting Harvest and meditating at all times. Call the mana per minute Y.

Create three DD spells with recast delay of 20 seconds, one each of fire, cold and magic. Each spell does X/3 damage per cast and costs Y/3 mana to cast. Cast times are commesurate with damage. Mana efficiency of the spell is X/Y.

In a long engagement, this gives the wizard a sustainable damage output while retaining some of their fast damage. If they get clarity or bard song, the extra mana can be used to cast other damage spells.

The same technique can be applied to enchanters. You don't want them to enchant mobs in Velious, that much is clear. Instead, make short duration spells that have long recast times. Make several of them. MAKE THEM COMPELLING TO CAST. Boon of Garou is a nice model, although I frequently get told not to cast it because the illusion effects make people sick when they are in werewolf form.
Duration of these spells should be SHORTER than recast delay - they are one shot wonder drugs for who ever they happen to be on.

Overall, I like the list, but there are two things I just have to disagree on.

1> AC/HP. We never asked "to have armor and hit points to match warriors". At least, no one in their right mind did, can't comment on everyone. If we wanted plate-level AC we'd be Bards. What I'd like to see, personally, is instead of all this cloth-based INT-loaded stuff (of which there are now a ton of varieties available), offer us a leather-based set that increases HP, CHA, AGI, whatever. We never asked for the durability of a Warrior. We'd like the durability of a Druid. And, realize that an Enchanter leather-based suit could be inferior in stats to a Druid suit, since part of the bonus could be justified by the higher AC.
After all, you've already DONE this for Necros, they have available higher-AC armors already, and WE'RE the ones who are expected to take a beating.

Heck, if the Resplendent Robe's Rune gets changed to use no component (even if it gets downgraded to a self-only Rune III or something) it'd still solve most of this problem; the ability to self-buff before most fights with a protective shield would reduce the need for a high AC. Of course, this only affects plane-level people, so maybe a new quest... for an item that casts Rune I (self-only, no component, longer casting time) at will? If you made it look like a shield even the people complaining about illusions might like it.

2> Religions/Faction. This isn't just an Enchanter issue, but it's by far the clearest in our case, since we're the class that deals with faction the most, IMHO (Necros and SKs coming in right behind).
Simply put, there are very few bonuses to having a good faction; once your CHA gets above a certain threshold (which varies with faction), you will receive the best prices as long as you're not KOS. Since Enchanters tend to have ludicrously high CHA, we stopped paying retail at about level 20. So, what does having a good faction in our hometown (the main use of religion, it'd seem) gain us? By the time an Enchanter is willing to buy expensive items, faction has become a non-issue.
The other bonuses of choosing a deity: imbued jewelry (most of these aren't very good, I'm sorry to say; the total bonus is unchanged, and the lack of resale value and difficulty finding imbuers of some gods outweigh any personal preference), imbued weapons/armor (doesn't affect non-HIE Enchanters)... not that impressive, IMHO.

The negatives of bad faction, on the other hand, are obvious. My Agnostic friend casts Illusion: Iksar and walks right in the front door of Cabilis while the guards are indifferent, while in my case (follower of Quellious) the guards scowl, ready to attack. Should religion make THAT much of a difference? And if so, why does it only make me Amiable in my home town?

Suggested changes:
> Make deity have larger bonuses to "good" towns. If an Agnostic is indifferent, a follower of Karana could be Amiable in all non-evil towns and Kindly in Qeynos. Or (and I'd prefer this option, actually) decrease the penalty for having an unfavorable deity. Following Quellious shouldn't take you from indifferent to scowls with the Iksar, in my view.
> Improve Imbued jewelry to have better TOTAL bonuses than mundane varieties, in the same way that imbued weapons and armor are superior to non-imbued. This affects all classes equally, and gives more of a reason to not be Agnostic.
