The simplest is just having a warrior break off and tank one down while being healed. With a good number of clerics, this is rarely a problem at all - on some high resist npcs, my guild agreed it was much more efficient to do this than to have the npc beat the tar out of an enchanter trying to mesmerize it. Keeping the enchanter alive as he tried to put under a high resist mob was far more expensive to clerics than keeping a warrior healed for the entire duration of him tanking it. Root parking on non-magic-immune npcs is often more efficient than glamour of kintaz or rapture. You'll hear of many high level enchanters who confess that their favored manner of crowd control on sebilite juggernauts is just that. Despite us being able to mez them, it's not efficient to do so. The problem here is that you can name a large number of classes better at root parking than enchanters due to higher ac/more hps. Bard/necro spells not having the same checks for unmezzables just seems to be an oversight, not really intentional - but it's worth pointing out that these abilities have been in play for a long time without "overpowering" them. When was the last time you heard someone say, "Gosh, good thing we had 5 necros for crowd control - those were a breeze!"? I know I never have. There's other tactics, like kiting the npcs around, that are specific to certain areas - but again, enchanters aren't very good at this.

Make Glamour of Kintaz(level 54) effect giants/other unmezzables. I can't think of a single raid-style encounter this would trivialize, as all npcs you need raids for are higher than GoK's level cap. It may actually make xping in places like Kael or Skyshrine worthwhile as an enchanter.

Give Rapture(level 59) a chance to land on magic-immune npcs, as well as giants/other unmezzables. All bosses in Kunark and Velious are too high level to be effected by this spell. Even in the old world pre-Kunark, I can't see how this would overbalance encounters - Vox and Nagafen would boot characters out of the zone, and Deathtouches from Innoruuk and Cazic make things slightly problematic to 'hold' them. I guess it would make Kedge and Hate easier - but any level 59 enchanter in those places is essentially bottom-feeding. Alternately, just make it not work in the old world. You may not realize it, but our spells function much differently in Kunark, Velious, and Plane of Sky - things in the old world over level 52 are unmezable -and- uncharmable, as they're all considered to be bosses. Lots of fun with rock golems in the Hole that can be up to level 53. Whoops.

Give Dictate(level 60) an instant recast, or a cast time equal to its duration, much like how rapture works. Dictate sounds really strong until you consider that in most cases where it's used, the enchanter is turned into paste about 50 seconds after it is cast. On level 53 or below npcs, we always just use boltrans - enchanters can keep a level 53 npc charmed for hours in the right situations. On higher level npcs, dictate with an instant recast would still be too expensive to keep going indefinitely, but it would be a more viable form of crowd control in raid situations. If it makes you feel better about it, raise its mana cost.

I know it sounds like we're asking for a lot. But really, we just want to be able to play too in Velious. You guys did such a great job balancing how the classes worked in Kunark (well, for the most part - I know some necros who would kill me for saying that)... it's a shame to see you eliminating the roles of classes now.
