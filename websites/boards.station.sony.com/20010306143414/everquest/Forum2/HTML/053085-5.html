Absor,

First of all, these ocmments apply primarily to the 50+ game

With all due respect, if a monk doesn't do damage between a warrior and rogue, what is a monk's role in a group?

A monk is not capable of being a main tank, so that's out. We don't have the HPs to surive against Kunark or Velious mobs in this role. We have high AC, but when we get hit, it's usually for max or close to max damage.

So a monk is a secondary tank in a group. But what would the compelling reason be to take a monk over a 2nd warrior? The warrior does nearly as much damage, and can take a much bigger beating. Since aggro is based mostly on # attacks and not actual damage, warriors have trouble getting a mob off of a monk. What about a rogue? A rogue does about twice as much damage as a monk, and with a competent main tank will take a minimal amount of damage because of Evade. What about a hybrid melee? Sure, the monk does more damage, but the Pallies and SKs tank better than a monk, and all the melee hybrids bring more versatility to the group than a monk does.

OK, what about pulling? Prior to Kunark, monks were desired in groups for feign pulling, a practice which Verant very publicly frowns on. In Kunark and Velious, the advantage in pulling was taken away, both by modification of mob behavior and the impact of higher levels on aggro ranges.

Raally, what all the above posts come down to is defining what a monk brings to a group, either in a standard hunting group or in a raid situation. Basedo n past communications and EQ manuals, the definition that Verant has provided is that we do more damage toe-to-toe than any other class, with only a backstabbing rogue able to surpass us.

Absor,

The roles of classes are easily described here.

Warriors = main tank, great AC, great HP, damn good dmg ability.

Rogues = Supposed to have semi poor AC (not currently) and lower hps than a tank (also not currently, since their HPs usually match a Pally/SK), do Awesome dmg above all other melee (absolutely) and have some useful skills.

Monks = in between these 2 classes. Monks have the HPs of a Rogue, yet get an added attack that is generally only decent (i.e.) flying kick. They have a natural AC bonus to "HIT AVOIDANCE" (there are 2 types of AC, damage avoidance and damage mitigation). That is why their armor generaly BLOWS CHUNKS i.e. 6 ac armor against lets say a warrior 40AC armor piece.

They have 2 defining abilities. Doing DARN GOOD DMG while Dodging/Blocking attacks very well (this makes up for the lack of good AC armor and Lack of a Warriors HPS), and the ability to Feign death. That is what a monk is.

The problem arises when against uber mobs, There is no avoidance of their attacks, they hit every time, and since Monks have low hps and small Damage mitigation AC, they get hit for Full dmg like a caster when they are hit. That is why they ask for higher skill caps, or better armor.

Then when they are being slaughtered against these Uber Mobs, they notice that warriors are doing as much if not more than the monk in Dmg. Why play a monk at all if a warrior can tank, Taunt, Slam, have Unreal AC, and do as much dmg as a monk?

Basically verant gave all mobs over lvl 50 a COMPLETELY overpowering ability to kill a full 45 person raid in a matter of seconds. It is called GATE SUMMONING.

1) mobs over lvl 51 can summon you to them from anywhere in the zone, if you are aggro'd.

2)50+ mobs can easily kill a single character in seconds, especially if there is more than 1 mob hitting for 200+ a hit.

3)caster mobs now can GATE to their origionation point.

Conclusion:

When a wanderer mob adds to your battle, If you do not STUN it out of its casting of GATE (this is only ONE spell mind you), The mob gates to a spot where there are usually 5 to 10 mobs around, then summons characters across the zone to their utter death at its normal attack rate (1-2 seconds a pop).

THAT IS WHY EVERYONE NEEDS SOME FORM OF STUN. It is an outragously overpowered unresistable (if gate completes) anialation of a raid, and since monks have NO ABILITY TO STUN at all innately, they are at a major disadvantage to other melee classes. Stun is not something they need to just say they can do it, everyone needs it, just so you can stop this ONE outragous mob ability (think of the cleric BP and you will understand how bad this ability really is)

I am mainly discussing issues related to the high-level uber game, as thats what Im familiar with.

There is a major problem with Feign Death, mainly it doesnt work. Someone brought up the mob frenzy idea, which is NOT correct. While soloing, I have frequently successfully feigned with well under a bubble left. The problem is lag. My guess would be that lag causes the client and server not the synch the feign, so that while the client thinks it did a good FD, the server still has the monk as a valid target, the monk gets hit and feign breaks. If Im in a fight and there are 5 or so other people there, feign almost never works (meaning no failure message, not interrupted, but I still get attacked). If I step back and turn my back to the fight, it works better. But as a life saving tool in combat, its marginal as is. As an agro reducer, its worthless. Currently, Feign Death is only useful when pulling, soloing or scouting.

The biggest problem I see with Monks is that we dont have a role. Were not tanks; we dont have the HP or AC of a Warrior, not to mention no taunt ability. Monks may do slightly more damage than Warriors, but the difference is minor, and probably shrinking with new, better Warrior weapons out there and triple attacks. Also, Monks usually have fast, agro producing attacks, and no evasion ability (as discussed earlier, FD is useless here) so Monks often must hold back to avoid becoming damage sponges. The net effect is that Monks actually manage to do less damage than Warriors do. Basically, one melee class, Warriors, are better at ALL aspects of combat than another, Monks. That is not balance. Monks do FAR less damage than Rogues, whose Evasion allows them to attack at will.

So what can Monks do? Some scouting, sneak improvements have made Rogues generally better for that. Corpse recovery is now located in Necroland. It comes down to pulling, which is not in the Vision, and Velious was designed to make feign pulling as hard as possible. Its been said that feign pulling makes no sense from a role playing perspective, I disagree. Perhaps in a literal sense, it is silly. Just like a Dark Elf Rogue sneaking right into the front gates of Freeport in broad daylight past KOS guards. Personally, I imagine the Rogue got into Freeport by climbing the wall, or using a disguise, but the game engine cant support it. Feign pulling is in a similar vein, playing dead to draw out the curious. Its a time-honored trick. I just imagine all the standing and flopping isnt taking place.

The Iksar factor: There is no single disparity between two races in the same class as between Iksar and Human monks. Iksar monks get infravision, regeneration (and extra 12 hp/tick at level 60, I believe) and an armor class bonus that maxes out at 50 AC. Their disadvantages are minimal. Faction is a pain in Antonica, but not an issue in Velious and advantageous in Kunark, as Cabilis is a much better place than FV. Fist delay isnt an issue, because at level 60, monks either use Celestial Fists (same delay for Humans and Iksar) or weapons. Lastly, there is an experience penalty (although with the elimination of classed based penalties, current Iksar Monks will actually take less exp to get to 60 than I did). What makes all this worse is that most Human Monks never had the option to chose an Iksar. It would have been an interesting idea to give Iksar Monks different skills than Human Monks, but its too late for that. As it is, the Iksar are considerably more survivable.

One final thing to remember when balancing classes: new equipment disturbs the balance. A Warriors PoH leggings are AC 20, their PoG are 34. A Monks PoF leggings are AC 5, their PoG leggings are 6. That means that in Velious, the armor class gap increased even more. Every time Warriors get new armor, their AC increases by leaps and bounds, while Monks get trivial increases. So even if the classes are balanced today, that balance will be thrown out of whack tomorrow when Warriors get some AC 50 leggings and Monks get AC 8.

What is the solution? Id like to see Feign Death fixed so it works in combat. If there could be some sort of mem blur effect to wipe agro in combat (without making it abusive for pulling), that would be helpful. Perhaps if a Monk is feigned and a mobs agro list is changed, the Monk is dropped from it? Would that be possible? So if I feign, and a Warrior taunts, the mob will forget me.

Monks need a place in the melee hierarchy. Just doing more damage probably isnt the right solution. What would be nice is if Monks were specialized in combat, better at fighting certain mobs. It makes perfect sense that a Warrior in plate armor could fight hordes of Frogloks better. However, someone more agile, not burdened by armor would be better able to dodge a dragons bite, and the protection of plate armor would do little to mitigate the damage from the six-inch fangs and jaws powerful enough to crush a VW.

The idea of a Monks special attacks having other effects is interesting. Even if Monks just got stun, it would be an improvement, and make sense role playing wise. Martial arts teach pressure points, something a knight in plate mail kicking never learned. It would make the most sense to have Dragon Strike or Eagle Claw stun, as a) its more along the lines of a strike to pressure points and b) a Monk would have to trade off damage from Flying Kick. Also, if an advantage was given to Humans, it could be that only Dragon Strike, not Iksar Tail Rake, stunned.

Finally, I hope feign pulling is brought back. My EQ identity is as a puller. Thats what I enjoy, and I spent a lot of time perfecting my skills. Its something unique to my class. Maybe a Warrior is a tougher fighter, and a Rogue can drop mobs faster, but pulling is at least a purpose.
