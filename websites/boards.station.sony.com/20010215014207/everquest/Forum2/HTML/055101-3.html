Absor. These talks are a Godsend. You are actively taking the players opinions, and presenting them at the tuning meeting.
I put myself in your shoes. You played a Cleric, and thus wouldn't know about Monkly issues, or what is important to us, but there were a few things that jumped out at me during the Monk talks.

/**********************************

The Epic Quest.
--------------------------------

Background.

The epic quest actually starts at level 1 with the Headbands and Sashes. The Headbands and Sashes are used for the Robe of the Lost circle quest. You turn them in with drops from drops from Raster of Guk and Targin of SolsekB to Brothers Zephyl and Qwinn. They give you items that you place with shadowsilk and JWW to tailor the Robe (no skill check). You then get the Zan and Fi pipes from Karnors and Chardok, and hand them into Brother Balatin in The Dreadlands for the Robe of the Whilstling Fists. You hand in the Robe of the Whistling Fists during the actual Epic quest for the Celestial Fists.

Problem.

The Robe of the Lost circle quest takes an incredibly long amount of time. Why? Because you can camp Raster of Guk for hundreds of hours and never see him. Just trust me on this one. I know it sounds crazy, but it is true. This is not necessarily a difficult quest because of the skill required to perform it, but because it takes such an incredibly long time to complete due to the No-Show of Raster.

So instead of camping Raster for so long, people decide to take matters into their own hands and slay a member of the Lost Circle and take is robe. Most notably Brother Zephyl. Killing Brother Zephyl is a Dragonraid style encounter. He requires more skill to take down than it takes to kill Minotars in Lower Guk.

Some people view this as a cheat, but the original quest designers obviously meant for the Robe of the Lost Circle to drop from Brother Zephyl in the first place otherwise they wouldn't have itemized him that way.

Solution.

Do not change the drop from Zephyl and Qwinn. The skill required to take down Zephyl is exponentially greater than the skill it takes to sit in Guk for hundreds of hours killing Greens. If anything, buff them up a little more. Increase the spawn time on Raster of Guk for people who want to quest it.

Problem.

People see a level 30 with Celestial Fists and say "BURN THE WITCH!". This is indeed a problem, but their proposed solution is to remove the multi-questing capabilites for all aspects of the Monk Epic Quest (ie. Robe of the Lost Circle, Robe of the Whistling Fists, and Celestial Fists) is not good. Multi-questing is a great facet of Everquest. It allows people to work together to perform "Epic quests".

Solution.

Twinking with the Celestial Fists is bad, and is accomplished by multiquesting. Instead of thwarting twinks by removing the multiquesting capabilities, just make it so you can't recieve the Celestial Fists or Wear them Pre-50. You can't even get the text if you are below level 50, so it makes sense not to be

given them Pre-50.

Total Solution.

Twinking with the Robe of the Lost Circle, the Robe of the Whistling Fists, and the Celestial Fists is a very bad thing. But removing the multiquesting capabilites will hurt more monks than the twinking ever would. I would rather see 10 twinks and 1 deserving Monk, than see no twinks and the deserving Monk go without. Leave it as is.

/**********************************

Damage!

Problem.

We are trumped out by Rogues and the margin between Monk and Warrior damage in diminishing. My maxiumum flying kick at level 60 is lower than the Rogues MINIMUM backstab. I am not asking for a Rogue nerf, but I am saying that Monk damage needs some serious work.

Our Celesital Fists are 9/16 weapons. These are REALLY NICE.

Now compare these to a Rogue weidling an EXQUISITE VELIUM SPEAR (12/20 Main and Offhand Piercing) and a Ragebringer (15/25 Primary Hand and Offhand Piercer).

They will do 1071 damage per second, without a haste item, or the Ragebringer haste effect, from the front of the MoB.

Monks will do 1042 Damage per second without a haste item, or the Celesital Fists haste effect, from the front of the MoB. When you add Flying Kick into the equation, we actually catch up.

Now put the Rogue behind the MoB with a Ragebringer in the mainhand, and you get regular double backstabs for 200+. This puts the Rogue at a conservative esimate of 175% of the Monks damage from the back. Now, that makes some sense, but it really hurts when they also do 100% of a Monks damage from the front.

Do the same experiment with Warriors, but replace the Ragebringer with an Epic blade, and Warriors will do 98-100% of a Monks damage while tanking 165% better than a Monk.

Solution.

Rogues and Warriors do the same damage as a Monk. Monks need an edge, Rogues and Warriors do not need a Nerf. We need to be placed in between Backstabbing Rogues and Warriors. I suggest that we be given Tripple Attack at level 51 and have it cap at 200 at level 60. I also suggest a Minimum damage on Flying Kicks (I kick for 3 pts of damage at level 60), along with Criticals on FLying Kicks starting at level 45. This would put is nicely between Warriors and Rogues and damage dealers without overpowering us.

/**********************************

STOP CASTING A_ORC_ORACLE_00!

Problem.

We can't stop casters from casting. When we say we want stuns, we don't want to immobilize the MoB, we want to interrupt casting. There is nothing more irritating than us Flying Kicking a level 11 orc and him standing there healing himself laughing.

Solution.

Please add a *SLIGHT* knockback effect to our Flying Kick or a 0.1 second stun, just so we can interrupt casting. We are not asking to immobilize the MoB for any amount of time, we just get irritated when we can't interrupt casting.

A way to give a monk a special skill without linking it to a higher level skill (making that skill too powerful), and giving it the limitation of much reduced damage.

/**********************************

GO AWAY! I'M DEAD!

Problem.

Feign Death. A couple of issues. Feign Death is our bread and butter. As you can see from my previous statements, we don't do the damage we are supposed to. We rely on Feign Death pulling. As much as you folks may not like it, we need it and use it every day. It makes us viable. With the release of Kunark, Feign Death lost a little bit of its usefulness with the crazy pathing and MoB behavior towards

Feign Death. With the release of Velious, Feign Death pulling was essentially eliminated because all the MoBs just walk away at once. Also, Feign Death as an "Evade" is useless in a group or when you or

If we Feign and we are already DoTed, make Feign death stay. Our bodies would still be diseased or poisoned even while dead, so the MoBs would beleive it. It makes RP sense.

Fix the pathing in Kunark and Velious, please change the MoB behavior so Feign Death pulling or at least Chain pulling stays viable.
