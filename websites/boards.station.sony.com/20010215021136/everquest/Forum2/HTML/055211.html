Hi all.
You may already have seen a list of some of the things that we are already testing as changes for the ranger class. Essentially, we are adjusting the level at which some of their spells are attained to make them more in line with their power.

Here is a list that I, once again, stole from Everlore because I'm lazy and because it seems accurate:

a) Add Endure Cold at 22
b) Add Skin Like Nature at 59
c) Add FireFist at 22
d) Move Bramblecoat from 49 to 39
e) Add Spikecoat at 49
f) Add resist cold at 55
g) Add Panic Animal at 22
i) Offense and weapons skill made identical to warriors

Let's talk about these changes and others that might be needed to 'balance' rangers.

Keep in mind that I primarily want to talk about 'balance' here. I'll certainly list any bugs that you would like to see addressed, but the focus should be balance. And please be concise when responding. These threads will get very long, and it helps me to be able to read all the responses if they are to the point.

Let's hear what you have to say.

Well I have read through most of the posts above and while I agree with most everything there are a few things I do not agree with.

For the most part, since the latest changes have gone live I believe that Ranger class is very nearly balanced. I am happy with the class as is with some minor tweaking.

-Give Rangers More HP: We are the only melee class that does not recieve significant HP boosts post 50. Pre Kunark we were right on par with the other Hybrids HP wise given same level and Stamina. This should continue post 50 as well. The reason is that HP is the most important thing in combat at these levels. It all comes down to how long you can stay alive. Currently should we get into trouble and find ourselves tanking we have too little HP for clerics to get off their Complete Heals and sometimes even Superior heals. This is what fosters the Mana Drain image. Give us the same HP base as the other hybrids. Regardless of skill caps the HP should be a level playing field among Hybrids. HP is also a major factor in the Hate list. MOBS like to hit things that go crunch and crumple the fastest. More HP's means we are less squishy and less of an appealing target to mobs.

-Defense: I for one do not want to see a defense increase. We don't need it. Quite simply our job is not to tank. We don't need defense if we are not getting hit. I would also like to remind people of the spells we get and the spells that we would be getting if the test changes go live. the Thorncoat series will be improved and existing spells lowered. This means LOTS more AC. Getting Skin Like Nature gives more AC and more Importantly HP regen. Add in Call of Earth and the end result is more AC from spells than the 15 points of defense we asked for when Kunark went live. At lv 53 I have 894 AC self buffed in pretty average equipment. I only have Tolan's Guants and TV Arms and Legs. The rest is stuff that any average Ranger would have at my level. I will strongly argue against Defense increases because we don't need it, and I feel that this would only take away from the areas where we really do need help.

-Move Jolt to lv 49 or 51 and change the resist rate to equal that of the Lifetap series of spells. I.e. nearly unresistable. The reasons are that lv 51 to 54 are accepted as being the hardest time in a ranger's life. This is mostly due to the places we have to fight in. Everything we will fight is either High blue, even, yellow, or red. There are no low blue mobs that we can really fight. This makes mobs hard to hit, hard to dodge/parry/riposte, makes us get hit harder and more often than opposed to a fight with a low blue. Giving Jolt at the start of these levels will considerably ease the uphill battle we face until MOBS beomce low blues instead of High Blues.

-Stacking Issues with Call of Earth and Str of Nature. As it stands now there are several lines of spells that these do not stack with that don't make that much sense given the effects of the spells. These two spells make our group desirability go up quite a bit. Not being able to use them due to stacking issues can hurt us a lot. Call of Earth is a 5pt dmg shield and a 25 AC boost that can be cast on anyone. Very nice spell. But wont stack with the augment line of spells including Speed of the Shissar. If a melee class has to choose one or the other of these spells they will choose the Haste every single time. Str of Nature does not stack with Divine Strength. Little need for our Spell when you get the pally spell.

As an addendum to this I would like to propose that both spells be changed to be "pack" type spells. I.e. one casting will buff the whole group. The reason is that both these spells cost quite a bit of mana. When I am in a group I generally choose to cast this on myself and maybe 1 other tank in the group to avoid going totally OOM. Even with only buffing 2 people I will still go through half my mana bar. This hurts us because we rely on our spells a great deal in combat. I usually pull for my groups. Not only am I very good at it the ranger class has a lot of tools especially for pulling. As such I am casting a lot (DD's, snares, Roots, dmg shields, etc.) and have VERY little time to med. Also I have a much smaller mana pool than a pure caster. I can't wear a lot of wisdom gear so my Wisdon never even comes close to the 200 cap that every single caster makes sure they get to. Even with Clarity 2 on me these 2 buffs will drain my mana very fast and impair my ability significantly. There are 2 ways to fix this problem. 1. Lower the mana cost of each spell by at least half. 2. Make both spells Pack spells so that with one cast of each we can buff the whole group. Also increase the mana cost of each spell to compensate for the pack effect. I prefer the latter method of fixing this.

-Tracking Changes: When these were promised to go live every single ranger in the game wept tears of joy. This alone will go a long way to balancing the class and making things more fun. We are trusting you to follow through with these changes and will keep the faith and hold confidence that you will indeed get these on the live servers. This is just a reminder really that we have not forgotten about them and are eagerly looking forward to them.

-Epic Battles: Not sure if this falls under the category of Balance but I guess it never hurts to ask. Concerning the Epic Quest. There are 2 Bottle necks in the quest. The 1st is Venril Sathir. The second is innoruuk. Both drop a gem that we need to complete our swords. The problem is that currently with each being a once a week spawn only some people ever get the *chance* to fight and kill these guys. I would suggest making these battles triggered Spawns. I am using the Cleric Epic as an example because I feel that the methods used were perfect. The fights in their quest are HARD. But as long as you put for the effort, time, and show enough skill to complete the quest steps everyone has the opportunity to fight the big fights in those quests. Whether or not they win is another matter. But everyone gets the chance privided the have completed all the steps leading up to each battle. Basically what I am saying is that every single Ranger who has completed his Runecrested Bowl and recieved Part of the Nature's Balance gem has every bit as much right to fight a big battle for the second part of the gem as Joe Blow from Uberguild_001 does. Right now though only Joe Blow has the opportunity to even try the fight because his guild camps the respawn to the second on his server not allowing anyone else the chance. You can even change the MOBS in question.

For instance, here are a few ideas:

Playing a Ranger was so frustrating for me at lower levels that I eventually switched to become a bard. I don't know what playing a Ranger is like past-50 but most of the people I know who have tried playing Rangers never made it to level 50 because they were so frustrating to play to begin with.
I honestly believe that Rangers don't just need new stuff above 50, but they need to be revamped from the first level at which they get spells. You could make their level 50+ spells and disciplines alluring, but what good would that be if people quit playing before they make it that far?
Problem 1: Rangers' lower level spells are nearly useless. Their first set of spells includes Skin Like Wood and Minor Healing. By the time a Ranger reaches level 9, these spells are nearly useless. At level 9, the spell fizzles like mad, and past that you can heal almost as quickly by sitting and resting as you can by standing and casting Minor Healing repeatedly for 10hp per cast. Flame Lick doesn't do enough damage to be worth the time taken to memorize it.
The situation doesn't get much better at level 15. Sure, that's when we get Cure Poison, but who's off killing spiders at level 15?
By the time we get any sort of offensive spell or lull spell, the mobs we're fighting resist these spells easily, or they only work on mobs that con green to us. We get Calm Animal at... 39. By that time almost all animals con green to us.
Even worse is that we can't do anything a Druid can't do until... what? Level 49 with Call of Fire?

The solution? Give Rangers some more interesting spells at lower levels. Befriend Animal at level 19. SoW at 22 and Calm Animal at 24.
In addition, the 7-9 levels between spell levels are very, very boring, and by the time we're reaching a spell level like 15 or 22, our spells have gone from useless to embarrasing. Give us a handful of spells to keep the game interesting midway between our 'main' spell levels - say, a couple spells at 19, two or three spells at 24, etc.
Also, give us a few lower-level spells that Druids can't do. Wouldn't True North be a great addition to the repertoire of the level 9 Ranger, a class devoted to tracking and exploring the outdoors? How about some less mana-efficient but still effective healing spells? (Instead of Minor Healing at 9, how about a version of Light Healing that costs 2X mana? Give it a more rangery name, like 'Healing Balm.') We won't supplant the role of Druids in a party, but we can assist them in a pinch.

Problem 2: Tracking is, frankly, broken. Tracking starts to become ineffective before we even reach level 10. We can track a stationary merchant across the zone, but somehow our tracking doesn't detect the Hill Giant about to step on our toes.

Solution: Just add a scrollbar to the tracking list so as our range increases, we can actually track all of the mobs in range. In addition, list the mobs in order of distance, not popping order, so closer mobs are first on the list and more distant mobs are farther away. Right now, older pops (like merchants) crowd out younger pops (things we might actually be hunting or trying to avoid) in our measly short tracking window.

Problem 3: Rangers just... can't really do anything interesting at lower levels. We really need some more unique skills or at least heightened abilities. If all Rangers started with at least a 20 or so in Forage, that would help, and fit with the theme. Better archery starting around level 11 or 15 would help a great deal, too, to make us more unique and compelling.

Right now, all Rangers are are warriors with a handful of pointless cantrips.
