=I would assume, since you are able to gather subscriber info from posting for bans/warns, you would have little or no trouble creating a filter based on poster.=

Just to concentrate on logistics here a second as I don't have an opinion on the idea either way.

It would be extremely hard (ie not practically feasible), to hack up Ultimate Bulletin Board so that every time you went to post it queried live server databases for information on your character level.

Think about it a minute, your screen-name could represent dozens of different characters, spread over different servers and of different levels. How is a bulletin board ever supposed to include the kind of 'intelligence' needed to guess which character's viewpoint you are posting from.

What you would have to do is query all servers to see if X-screenname had a character anywhere >51 and then let that person post on 51-60 board. Ah, but wait - now you also have to let them post on the <51 board since they may well have Alt characters who are of that level.

Now here you have a problem. If Player-X's lvl 60 character is a warrior (randomly picked class), how do you stop him posting in the 51-60 forum in threads about the Enchanter end-game? He knows less about the Enchanter end-game than a lvl 49 Enchanter as he, in fact, doesn't play an Enchanter at all. Now, how do you also stop him posting his level 60 warrior views on warrior threads in the <51 forum? You have to let him in there because his twink is a level 25 rogue - but now he's messing up threads which are not meant for his level.

In conclusion, having a forced system would be too hard to code and too impractical to use. Simply having an extra board for the 51-60 game would, but then everyone would have to be prepared for <51's posting in the high-end board and 51-60's posting in the 1-50 board.

In other words, total segregation simply doesn't work.
