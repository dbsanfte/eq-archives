-- The Uber Velious mob problem --

SUMMARY:

You are aware of our issue against massive hp mobs!

DETAILS:

Whilst I originally promoted % dmg spells, its occured to me that this isn't the actual issue. This is a symptom of an overideing inbalance in the wizard class. It all comes to dmg/time. I already covered it in the post below.

Essentially we cannot consistently dmg at the same rate as other classes over time. Not even in the slightest. The massive hp on velious mobs just serves to highlight this issue. Its not velious specific!

Please bare in mind that the wizard's forte, fast dmg, is actually pretty worthless in most grinding situations.

SOLUTION:

Address this fundamental flaw and I think a lot of other problems will disappear with it.

Using normal spells (not lures) we should over time be able to do as much dmg as monk/rogue. I don't care if a few support classes are required to pull this off (equiv of healer), but right now its chanter or bard only and even then we don't compete on dmg.

Remember its balanced by:

a) controlling aggro.
b) medding to keep at the constant mana ideal.

In fact why not just do the mana pool is joined to health pool thing - then we can just use the healer class framework that exists already. Health would have to be at a 2:1 ratio to equiv mana though as wizzies only have 1000hp. This would make us work in a similar way to melees. In fact there are numerous ways of tying this up. The best bit would be that in emergency we could dump mana/health and seriously jeopardise our life expectancy in doing so (balance). In fact we could kill ourselves!

SUMMARY:

Our equivalents in dmg dealing appear to be monks/rogues. We just can't compete with dmg vs time with them in any sense. Clarity sure isn't as useful as CH...

DETAILS:

People suggest that giving more dmg output will:

a) Unbalance us.
b) Get is killed.

Monks are rogues are very similar to wizards, they do dmg at the expensive of hp (and thats only if they get the aggro!). We do dmg at the expense of mana. One of these expenses costs an awful lot more than the other in terms of downtime...

The exceptional circumstance I referred to being, needing a fast burn down in emergency. Which I feel we should still be able to do, but it might require 15 mins top get back to that state...

SOLUTION:

Address mana cost of evocation spells.

Even if the spells do what seems to be a large amount of dmg in x seconds, remember its balanced by:

a) controlling aggro.
b) medding to keep at the constant mana ideal.
