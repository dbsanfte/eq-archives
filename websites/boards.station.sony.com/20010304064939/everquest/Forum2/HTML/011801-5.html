For anyone new to the issue that doesn't understand what is going on, let me give you the rundown:

- The level 15 Bard song, called Lullabye, is an AE Mez.

- It is not as powerful as the level 16 Enchanter spell, Mesmerize, because it only lasts 3 seconds (all other songs last 9). This means the Bard playing it can do NOTHING else while playing it, and it has a chance to be resisted every 3 seconds, making it very dangerous to use.

- The Enchanter's Mesmerize costs so little mana that meditating while mes'ing will actually INCREASE their mana if they've casted Clarity on themselves. Bard's Lullabye costs 0 mana.

- In a patch last year, an unannounced nerf was made to all Bard mez songs.

- Bards suddenly noticed that their song SAID it was working but the mobs kept hitting them. After several months, they finally convinced GZ to do a public test in January 2000 to see what the problem was.

- This chat is available at http://www.eqsongs.com/NewsArchive/GZ_LullabyLog.htm

- During the chat GZ said that a secondary resist rate had been added to the song.

- The secondary resist rate had no message for it, so the song gave a successful message even when it failed. Upon discovering this, GZ figured that was the whole problem and had the message added 2 weeks later. The song STILL says it is successful, however, it ALSO says it has failed at the same time.

- During the chat, people asked GZ what the resist rate should be. GZ said about 25% on low cons (blue/green) and 35% on white cons. He didn't mention yellow/red.

- GZ also said CHA should lower this resist rate.

- Level 50 Bards ran tests and found that the resist rate against a level 1 mob was 35% and completely unaffected by high CHA

- They also discovered that the resist rate got worse as they tried against higher and higher mobs. The resist rate against exp-giving (blue con) mobs was about 70%.

- 70% resist rate checked every 3 seconds was fatal to the Bard.

- Bards complained until last month, Ester The Tester was set to test the song and find what was wrong with it.

- Ester admitted she was clueless on Bards and didn't know much about the issue.

- Ester later said she thought that since the song was level 15 it shouldn't still be effective against high level mobs.

- Bards explained that ALL spells and songs in the game have their resistance rates dependant on the level of the caster versus the level of the mob and the spell level was irrelevant.

- Case in point, Enchanters use their level 16 AE mez all the way up to 50 with consistent resist rates against blues. Same with Druids and their level 1 Snare.

- Ester never acknowledged these responses so Bards didn't know if she had heard.

- The next day, it was announced that the song was working as intended.

- Having already been told recently that the admittedly accidental nerf to Bard snare song would not be fixed, and having been looked over when the enhancements to the other Hybrids were made recently, the Bards were outraged.

- Many Bards quit EQ that day.

- Other classes agreed that the Bard Lullabye should be functional as Enchanters now have an unbalancing monopoly on Crowd Control. Even Enchanters showed their support, saying that they feel the Bard AE mez should work again.

- When asked why GZ would say one thing and then go back and say something else, Gordon replied that the snippets of chat log that he had been shown did not explicitly state which resistance GZ was referring too. The whole chat log does, of course, but Gordon had not read it.

- Gordon/Ester claimed that the song was not supposed to be effective above level 30 and that the Bard's single-target mez was the "upgrade" to the song. However, it is even weaker because it is single-target only and has the SAME high resist rate as Lullabye.

- Every other Bard song in our arsenal gets MORE powerful as we level, even our level 1 song is used by level 50 Bards, and this was the first time we had been told that one of our songs was supposed to be worthless post 30.

- The song is also worthless pre-30 because of the resist rates, but not as horribly.

- There is no other AE mez songs for Bards, so nothing to truly replace it post 30. There is, however, a blank spot at level 49 that we have no song for.

- It has been suggested that since Verant's main concern is that the song doesn't use mana that mana use be added to it similar to the second Bard Charm song. There has been no answer on this yet.

- The fact that CHA does nothing for the resistance rate at ANY level has not yet been addressed.

What is mana for? It is there to limit the number of effects a caster can keep going in any period of time. For example, a shaman can keep all their stat buffs up but at the expense of their offensive/healing abilities. Over time a caster can only expend as much mana as they med back. The limit on Bard songs is the duration and casting time, as mentioned previously on this thread.

Addressing Lullaby, i will run through some numbers using a hypothetical situation. Suppose a group pulls 4 creatures and proceeds to fight one while the bard uses Lullaby to mez three of them. This song lasts for such a short time that it must be the ONLY song sung (this is the proper trade off for no mana cost, not increased resists). With a primary resist rate of ~5% and secondary resist rate of ~35%, as per the testing done by Kaearia and other bards, you could expect to successfuly mez a mob .95*.65 or ~61% of the time. So, for 3 mobs you could expect to mez all three on one pulse of the song .61^3 or 23% of the time. Now, suppose it takes your group 30 seconds to kill that fourth mob they have engaged, this means Lullaby will pulse 5 times (every 6 seconds), so your chance of keeping all 3 mobs mezd for the entire time is .23^5 = .0007, or .07%! So in this case the song will work less than one-tenth of one percent of the time.

The reason I used an example of a four mob pull in the above is because a level 27+ bard can crowd control with charm in the case of a 2-3 mob pull. Why did i work through all those numbers? To demonstrate that the song is indeed broken. The equivalent Enchanter spell, Enthrall (a level 16 spell which is useful to level 50 and beyond, whereas our level 15 song is only intended to work up to level 30 *boggle*), has a maximum duration of 48 seconds. So to get an ACCURATE comparison of enchanter and bard resists the resist rate of *one* cast of Enthrall should be compared to *eight* casts of Lullaby, since that's how many casts it would take to keep a mob mezd for the same period of time (works out to 99.999%, seriously). Even if Lullaby only had it's 5% primary resist rate, to keep 3 mobs mezd for 48 seconds the overall success rate would only be ~30%. Can you understand now why the secondary resists on bard mez songs make them entirely useless?

Snare-type spells have *always* been considered more useful than root for this very reason. Chains was a bit more effective in that at level 38 and above, the point at which a mob would stop fleeing was at half HP. This effectiveness was offset by the need to cast it repeatedly. It is now nearly useless as both a movement and attack speed slowdown, since it overwrites other classes snares. Not only that, but we have to wait till level 23 to gain the ability to snare, whereas other snare classes get this ability earlier (Necro + Druid at level 1, Ranger level 9, Shadow Knight level 15, Wizards being the exception getting Bonds of Force at level 29).

Well, I hope someone out there finds this post to be useful.
