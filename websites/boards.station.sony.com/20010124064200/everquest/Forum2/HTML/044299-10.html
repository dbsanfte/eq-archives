I don't play a high level Necro, just some low/mid level ones. Just want to get a handle on all of the arguments, so if someone can sanely reply I'd appreciate it.

Lifetaps not landing on "boss" raid mobs-

From what I understood based upon the SK HT resistances which are on the lifetap resist table (unless you use the discipline), and I may be wrong, Lifetaps are not 100% resistable by all uber mobs, they are just appear to be so highly resistant that the number of times they have to be cast to have chance of landing something renders them effectively unusable in practice. Pulling some numbers out of my rear, in theory 90% resistance means that 10% not resisted which may look viable, but not in practice on the battlefield what with casting times, mana consumption and whatnot. This may be a difference between Developer and Player view.

Increasing mana transfer ratios would double the Necro's mana pool -

It would not double the Necros mana pool, but if the efficiency is doubled, then the effect is that they can transfer twice as much mana, similar effect as doubling the mana pool if the mana pool is devoted to mana transfer functions.

Verant's stance seems to be that while you are not that good at mana transfer or with healing but you can do something to contribute to the effort. If you develop friends they will take you with them even if you are not the most effective.

Players stance is that if we are not that effective, in raid situations they don't take 3rd raters so being bad at something for those situations is effectively in practice the same as not doing it at all. Having to rely on being friends with the Raid leaders just to get the chance to be ineffective sucks rocks.

Dots not stacking -

Only one can be cast on a creature, making more than one necro in an encounter less useful. This also affects other DoT casting classes, not just necros. On the other hand if you allowed more than one dot to stack, while not bad with one or two used in a "normal" group combat scenario it, could scale up to be abusive pretty easily with some creative thinking.

Perhaps instead, create some DoTs that do more damage in the early stages and trail off in damage and overall be less effective than consistant damage spells. This would allow others casting the same spell and overwriting to be more useful, eg 100 damage on get go, 50 damage 1st tick 25 2nd, 12.5 3rd, etc. That way if someone else casts again after the second tick and overwriting, they are still adding to the damage instead of just increasing spell duration.


Pet effectiveness -

Sure they die quickly to AoE high level mobs, but who in melee range doesnt? Wizards have to be in melee range in order to land a Lure spell and they are probably less equipped to handle the damage than pets but they contribute somehow. For the small time that the pets are there they can help fill the hate list and do some minor damage. Pathing can still be a problem if your pet is feared.

Corpse Poofing -

It may have been "fixed" for level 55 and above, but since reimbursals for poofed mob corpses arent reimbursed, not many people want to take a chance on this and I don't know anyone who will go through all the effort just to test it out, especially as proper testing requre multiple attempts for different scenarios, let alone repeats of the same scenario to be 100% certain. It still is not "fixed" for lower level mobs. Corpses of high level mobs still seem to "poof" perhaps for other reasons not fixed, perhaps LD members getting the killing blow, dead or LD members's dots or pets doing killing damage or what have you.

On a side note, while Warlock is sexed as male and Sorceress the female counterpart, Heretic is a unisex title. They do need to have male/female titles but no need to weaken a valid point by making up words like Hereticess.

Absor:
I think there are a few things that you need to re-evaluate. I’ll explain my understanding of things and we can see what you think you need to look at.

Issue 1: The necro’s role on a raid.
People are claiming that the only role of a necro on a high level raid is as a mana battery. This would be fine with me if we were able to do that job properly. As it stands a necro cannot use lich while fighting a big monster to keep his own mana up because he cannot tap the creature. The necro is no longer a good mana battery and after he dumps all of his mana he is certainly less than useful. I think you need to get rid of high-level creatures immunities to lifetaps. With Velious you have breached the 32k HP limit. The damage that a necro or even a group of them can do with lifetaps is only a small portion of the life of the new creatures. Kunark is a different story though. The boss mobs die so fast that necros (and I’m sure some other classes as well) don’t have any real need to be there. This is of course a problem that you have fixed with Velious (and thank you very much for that, 15 second boss mob fights are borrrrrrrring), but even in Velious MOB MR is ridiculous.

Issue 2: The tools of the trade.
A necro has a lot of tools at his disposal. We have pets, DoTs, DD, mana and HP exchanges for self and for others. We can summon your corpse and a lot of other fun stuff. Almost none of these skills however are useful during a high level encounter. Lets look at them one by one.

Issue 3: Jobober Vs. Uber_Mob_00
Pet are very useful and one of the mainstays of the necro profession. On large raids they become a lot less useful though. Walking a pet through a zone is most times a nightmare. The pet will wander off or agro things through walls that a PC wont agro. Verant may claim that this is a disadvantage to using a pet but that is just a flimsy excuse to call a bug a feature. When you do get to the uber mob it’s either going to fear your pet away or it’s going to kill your pet in the first 10 seconds of the fight from obscenely high damage AoE spells. You’ve now just wasted 500+mana.

Issue 4: Damage spells.
Mobs at higher levels get some pretty high resists. When you get into the uber mob category the resists are so obscene that only a lure will go through. Everything else is just wasted mana. The only possible way to hit these things is to debuff them, but unless the debuffers are both level 60 and have the supposedly irresistible debuff your not going to touch them. Lower resists a little bit. Make the susceptible to something. Nagafen/Talendor for example should almost never resist a cold based spells yet he is immune to fire spells. From what I hear though the cold resist is still pretty high.

Issue 5: Melee range casting (all casters)
What is it with having to be within melee range to cast on all Boss mobs? Why did you put that in. There is not roleplay reason for that to be in the game and I see no mechanics reason for it either. As for roleplay reason NOT to have it. I’ll be damned if I’m a caster and I’m going to get anywhere near a monster like that. That’s the tanks job. I’ll stand back and nuke it thank you. This is something that I have always been strongly against. Either get rid of it or give casters better defense and AC.

Issue 6: Summoning Corpses
This is a non-issue. This has nothing to do with killing an uber mob. This is not parto f what necros are griping about. We want to be more effective DURRING the battle, not after it.

Issue 7: Corpse Poofing
Yes it still can happen. Why? Because Verant is paranoid that someone somewhere might come out ahead. Here are the ways that I know a corpse can poof:

Scenario 1: The NPC
Any NPC attacking a creature if it gets the last hit will make a corpse go poof. As well if the NPC does the most damage the corpse will poof.

Scenario 2: The hate list
Another way for a corpse to poof is for a pet to get the last hit on a mob after the pet’s owner has been knocked off the hate list. The hate list used to be so short that people who were not constantly casting or hitting the mob would be bounced off the list. If a DoT or a pet kills the NPC while the casters is off of the hate list the game assumes that it was killed by an NPC…POOF

Scenario 3: NPC mode, fear, linkdeath and other “features”
The other way to poof a corpse is for your PC to become an NPC. If for some reason the game thinks that you’re an NPC and you get the last hit. *POOF*. How can this happen? First off as soon as you get feared the game considers you an NPC (a BIG mistake on Verants part in my opinion) So now if you have a DoT on the mob and the DoT kills the mob while your feared POOF! If you go linkdead during the fight you become an NPC. Guess what! Yup if your DoT or pet get the last hit the game considers it killed my an NPC. The same of course goes for ALL the melee classes in the fight. I do not want to hear the line about “we do not want linkdeath to be a desirable state” That’s BS. You’ve already given a once over to enough people who lose all their summoned items due to out-of-game issues that should not have as detrimental effects on your in-game time.

So far as I know not a one of these has ever been fixed. The hate list was extended but that is just a workaround and not a fix. With all the outdoor uber and semi-uber mobs you will have to deal with scenario 1 a lot more often now.
