Charm.

I'm refering to the decrepid AI charmed pets have. Here are some specifics.
1. Telling pets to sit down causes them to sit down for 1-10 seconds. Then they stand up and start walking away from you and dont stop until you say follow (i.e. they try to wander to their spawn point and then decide to sit).
2. Following charmed pets won't cross arbitrary points in zones, instead they choose to take their own path (typically backtracking through the nearest AI path aka railroad track) to get to where you are. Often training the whole zone on you. And once they are next to you they often pace rather then stand stationary. Typically running between you and the nearest railroad track and back again.

You can try this in lots of zones but for specifics you can try Rathe, and Overthere.

Basically pets use "normal" wandering monster AI (meaning they follow the railroad tracks you plant in the zones). They also won't take the shortest distance between it and you, they will take the shortest distance between a railroad track and you.

Forcing players to learn the arbitrary points in a zone which charmed monsters won't cross is NOT something you can claim to be a feature of the game. It is a flaw, a bug, and a needless headache to players that charm.

You should try any zone which has alot of internal pathing lines and walk across them to see what a charmed pet does and tell me it is as intended.

Now before you discount this completely and say "charm was meant to be difficult" keep in mind that the AI is easily abused. You can use the fact that monsters walk to railroad tracks before moving towards you and keep a monster at maximum casting distance. Allowing you to easily charm it again when charm fades. And this is not something you intended.
