I think a big problem with EQ down time is that the risk vs. reward isn't balanced due to the following factor:

Risk is constant.

Reward is random.

If I go on a Planar Raid, I am not going for experience. Raids are a bad source of experience, and a good source of loot. In fact, going to the planes almost means CERTAIN death, but there's a small chance of getting any of the nice equipment that drops in that plane, and less chance of getting a decent amount of exp.

Look around the game and you'll find that there are often many more risks than rewards, and the penalty of a risk (amount of Exp lost for dying) is generally greater, and more constant, than the rewards (amount of exp for killing something, phat lewt dropping off a mob)

The problem that people complain about isn't that there is downtime, I believe. It's the fact that that down time is constant and you know it will happen, and it's the same every time. But you can go out and seek a reward, spend several hours pursuing it, have all the requisite components of a group on hand, be able to gain the reward in every facet except for random chance, and that random chance is what keeps you from getting a reward.

They end up wasting time inside and outside the dungeons looking for a party to adventure with. There are of course balance/abuse issues with these ideas and the fact whether this would help at all considering the "camp" mentality of most players. Would anyone want to leave a good "camp" and go back to the towns? Would people just overcrowd the towns to get these small benefits? As I said, since you used to play a mud Brad, I'm sure you were aware of all of these conventions when designing EQ1, and maybe you considered them but realised they weren't feasable at the time. Downtime as a penalty is part of the primary design of the game, but what often gets missed is that this means we get 'penalized' whenever we decide to do something other than pound on monsters.

The only rewards in the game are experience and loot.. if you are not actively pursuing these, then you are getting the same penalty as if you had made a play mistake!

If you want to encourage a certain behavior, then you MUST reward that behavior with experience or loot. Think exploring is important? Create a small experience reward for the first time you set foot on a new bit of map. Think roleplaying is important? Create a database where people can give feedback on how well they feel each person is roleplaying, and give experience bonuses for people you can consistantly get high roleplaying marks from their peers.

Time as a penalty has some serious game design drawbacks. Create new rewards, or create a new penalty.

-Zaphod

A situation where there was ABSOLUTLY NO CHANCE OF REWARD. I was not traveling thru dengerous territoy nor even in a place where I was not liked (the guy was wrong who was talking about me taking a good char to an evil city - except for the Freeport Militia ubber gate guards).
