I don't mind paying $10 a month,I'd pay A LOT more if necessary, and here's why:

Most players don't even realize - that we pay indirect opportunity costs..

Let's say you value an activity, for example going out with friends $10/hour. Then you'll lose 10$/hour if you can't go out with friends.Or let's assume you can't work because you're doing something else, then you'll lose 20$/hour.

So for EQ:

5*4hours/day +2*8 hours on weekend
= 36 hours * 4
=144 hours a month * 10$/hour
= $1440/month.

These are very reasonable numbers..

Now if you compare the direct cost of $10 to the indirect cost of $1440 you'll see that the direct cost is less than a percent of your investment.

Even if the fee was $30 a month this would NOT triple the actual cost you spend on a game, but only increase it by about 1.5 percent.

That's why I don't mind paying $10 at all, and by the way that's also why downtime (lost time, time that have no or even negative value to me) bothers me greatly.

By the way I did NOT make this theory up myself, this is totally standard biz 101, and it's widely used..

"And we've certainly never stopped calling the game an RPG -- EQ is a game that continues to appeal to all sorts of players, hard core and casual, RPers and power gamers, and everybody else inbetween."

You may have never stopped calling EQ an RPG... but most of the rest of us have. VI has never really seemed to have a good grasp of what an RPG entails. Now on some rudimentary level EQ is a very very base RPG... much the same as Quake. However to be an RPG the equivelant of what MOST people define as the genre you need a LOT more.

Your current setup is supposed to be "player driven" which forces anyone who wants to RP to be not only Player, but to also fulfill the role of GM and frequently NPC. Couple this with the fact that there is no back structure in place to keep the non-RP'ers in their role and it becomes readily apparent why almost no one actually RP's in EQ.

If EQ were truely an RPG you, the controllers of the game world, would be the GM's. The world would change and evolve *influenced* by player actions but not solely as a result of them. This would likely involve hiring a content designer to be the GM for each zone and having them all sit down in weekly meetings to discuss what's going on in their zone, what changes will occur resulting from those events, what other events they'd like to impliment and how it might affect other zones.. this would also include things that happen across zones. Over all of them you would have a lead GM (and when I say GM I don't mean your customer support guys) who makes sure all the stories and events mesh. This would keep the world alive and fresh and the "story" rolling... like in an actual RPG. To keep the non-RPers from destroying the integrity of the world you'd also have to impliment safe-guards. I'm not talking making sure everyon stays in character when in conversttion.. that's impossible. But by limiting their actions to what the characters would do would be quite easy. Druids and Paladins should not be camping "good" NPC's and likewise SK's and Necro's should not be camping "evil" NPC's (actually they don't much).. you did try to limit this with faction hits... but the faction hits are negligable compared to the gathered xp.. and worse than that are frequently easily repaired if needed at all. I'm totally happy with monthly subscriptions and paying for expansions. Personally I would pay a lot more (a rough estimate of my British Telecom pay-per-minute phone charges is around $1500 for under 2 years of solely EQ-playing time).

I've played a lot of games in my life but probably all combined don't even match the time I've spent in EQ. Sure I've had bad times but I'm not so retarded I'd keep returning to a game I didn't enjoy.

What I think Verant need to realise though is, I for one (and I suspect many others) see ourselves as far more than just customers. We are pretty much THE game, you guys just give us an arena to play in so to speak. The disappointing thing is, certainly until recently, how little you guys have acknowledged us.

Obviously you can't have a game run by player democracy. If that were true we'd all be level 100 with deathtouch and GM equipment by now but at the expense of enjoyment. The great thing about EQ is we're all on a more or less equal footing, playing by the same rules. The bad thing is there are still many flaws in the game that don't prevent mud-flation but do directly detract from player enjoyment. Things may be close to being balanced but that's not the same as things are close to working properly - they aren't.

No one should have a single useless spell for instance, highly situational maybe but useless no. Yet many classes have them and worse still they continue to be put in the game. Verant isn't learning from it's mistakes - that's the biggest worry I have.

I see Absor starting a thread for every class. This is great, it's what we've been waiting a long time for. Finally we can clearly see our ideas being taken note of. But it's fine starting something but you have to conclude it also. Personally, as a necromancer, I'm left wondering why I bothered spending hours reading and responding to 'our' thread when there is little evidence to suggest our ideas are being examined. Sorry Absor but saying 'you're collecting ideas' isn't good enough. We want answers, of course we don't expect all the ideas to be implemented (not least because many are conflicting) but I want to see evidence you've taken those ideas to tuning meetings and I want to see feedback on those ideas. How can we possibly expect a 'fair hearing' given that you intend starting a dozen more class threads in the next couple of weeks? It's in none of the classes best interests as we all risk getting drowned out by each other.

Back, sort of on topic, what is Verant doing to address the increasingly sizaeable lvl 50+ playerbase? Kunark was a great start but Velious is an utter joke. The weird thing is Velious IS something that has long been missing from EQ, a continent almost entirely set up for large scale, high level, guild raiding. Only it came too soon. There should have been another Kunark-class expansion first, more places where single 'pick up' groups of lvl 50+ players can hang out. I'm sick of Karnors and growing tired of Sebilis. Not many like Chardok and Charassis tends to be done by guilds. Velious, despite it's vast size, adds NOTHING to the part of the game I spend a lot of my time in - single groups of lvl 50+ players. Who tuned/designed Velious? I sure hope they aren't involved in Star Wars: Galaxies.

What as a player do I have to look forward to in EQ? The Warrens sounds like a mid level joke (judging by your last few attempts at 'old world' zones/re-tuning). There has to be new content to look forward to for pick-up groups of 50+ players, currently the game is getting stale and getting there at an alarming rate. If EQ's future is all about massive raids taking multiple hours then I'm afraid it will have to continue without me as I don't have 8 hours a day to dedicate to it.

Maybe being a ewok won't be so time-consuming...
