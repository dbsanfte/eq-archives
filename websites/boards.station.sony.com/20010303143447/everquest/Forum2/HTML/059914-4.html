Greetings Absor!

Having just finished reading the three pages of posts and incorporating my own ideas, this is what I've come up with (I've only played on Rallos Zek, so my ideas are for that server alone):

Egg-Shaped Pumice Stones
This is probably the one thing everyone agrees on. We really need these back on Rallos Zek (and probably on the teams servers as well). It is the only way for non-casters to level the playing field. It is also the easiest for your team to implement since they were already there once.

Newbie Killing
This is a major problem in that the newbie areas of every zone (except maybe Tox forest) look like battlefields. People will come to the PvP servers when their servers are down just to run around and randomly attack people. It makes getting started incredibly difficult. A good solution would be to simple disallow PvP prior to level 10. I don't know how difficult this would be to code, but you guys are pretty clever and I'm sure you could implement this without too much difficulty.

Ganking/Zone Camping
This is PvP of the lowest caliber. Typically casters will wait at dungeon zone points and blast anyone who emerges low on health. A simple solution would be to make the victim's corpse unlootable unless the assailant did at least 51% of the damage. This will also prevent accidental EXP loss as would-be gankers root someone in front of an agro'ed mob and then wait till the person's health is low enough that they can kill them in one hit. If they fizzle or just miscalculate, then the person dies and suffers EXP loss. The 51% rule would greatly mitigate this situation.

Looting
If the above anti-ganking measure were implemented, I see no reason not to leave Rallos Zek as item loot. For really nice items, however, you could introduce a difficult quest that would allow the person to make a piece of equipment NO-DROP thereby soulbinding it to their person.

PvP Spell Damage
As other people have stated, PvP spell damage is reduce by a flat percentage across the board. This should be reconsidered and adjusted on a sliding scale. At lower levels, for example, it is virtually impossible for a caster to kill a well-equipmed tank. At the upper levels (50+), however, Druids and Necros can kill with a single DoT! In fact, DoTs are FAR more powerful at the upper levels than even Wizard's nukes. You may find that, in the interest of balance, high level DOTs need a steeper damage penalty in PvP.

Portal Camping
This tends to be a real problem. One simple solution would be to create a no-fire zone or perimeter around the portal. Maybe even put some guards there (I don't know how feasible that would be).

That is basically it for now. I have a few other ideas, but they are mostly derivative from the base ideas listed above.

A separate message dealing with enchanter and cleric specific issues from the perspective of a level 57 enchanter on Tallon Zek:

ENCHANTER AND CLERIC SPECIFIC ISSUES:

In the old days before coin-only loot, both of these classes could PvP fairly effectively. After coin-only loot it became feasable to raise resistances because you no longer had to worry about losing your expensive resist gear. This wouldn't be a problem except for 2 issues:

(1) All spells that enchanters get are magic based and "all-or-nothing" in nature. This means that if they are resisted at all, then you get no effect.

(2) It seems like many magic based spells are more resistable than other typses of spells.

The result of this is that anyone with much over 100 magic resist is IMMUNE to enchanters and clerics. Most players over level 50 have significantly more then 100 magic resist. I am a member of guild where the average member is around level 55, and almost all of us have around 150 MR with many of us who are close to 200. High MR is not an atypical special case as Abashi once put it. It is the norm on Tallon Zek. This is because 3 classes of magic based spells are devistating: blinds, roots, and snares. People hate these spells so much that as soon as they can, they raise their magic resist until they no longer have to worry about those spells affecting them. The side effect is that this makes them completely immune to all cleric and enchanter spells as well. In adiditon MR is one of the easiest resistances to raise with gear.

In every battle I am in, I begin with casting my highest level tashan line of spell, then I strip enchantments at least once (often 2 or 3 times) to remove any buffs that might give my opponent magic resist, and yet even after doing this the vast majority of the time that I cast my mez spells they are resisted. My Dazzle spell (level 49 enchanter mez) is resisted better than 75% of the time and that is a pretty conservative estimate, I think it is actually more like 90%. Many battles I've been in have gone exactly like this:

I cast tashania, then strip enchantment, then I go oom casting dazzle over and over again on a blue-con target and get resisted every single time.

That is just plain not fair. I think Abashi or someone said that "some class has to be the worst pker", and that may be true, but when you are completely innefective at PvP combat then the game just becomes no fun on PvP servers. If a class is no-fun then people stop playing that class. There is much evidence that this is happening on Tallon Zek. There is a serious shortage of high level enchanters on Tallon Zek. Before the server went coin-only we used to be a very common class. I am not as "in-tune" with clerics' situation since I don't play one, but they too seem very scarce at high levels, and I hear the clerics I group with complain about how much they suck at PvP because of resistances. This is a problem because enchanters and clerics are essential for the high level PvE game, so all players on PvP servers suffer when people quit playing enchanters and clerics. Verant needs to do something to make PvP more fun for enchanters and clerics so that more people will play these very neccessary classes.

I can't really comment on how to make it better for clerics since I don't play one, but here are some good suggestions for how to fix enchanters:

(1) Make the tashan line of spells more effective in PvP combat. These spells should reduce magic resistances at least twice as much as they do. Even with that, people with good Magic resist (over 100) will still resist magic based spells quite a bit. (I bet it would still be over 50% of the time though) I think this would at least give enchanters a chance at landing some of their spells on a regular basis. I think this would be the most balenced and fair way to increase enchanters PvP effectiveness. They would still have trouble killing most people solo, but at least they would not be the "sitting ducks" that they are now. They would also still be hindered by in PvP by having to first casting a debuff before casting any of their real offensive spells.

(2) Make enchanter Mezzes less resistable in PvP. As I said before mez rarely sticks in PvP.

Although getting mezzed is very frustrating it is not a particularily devistating spell if you consider what we are up against. After level 50, most of our casting opponents can kill us with one spell. Those that can't can do it in 2 or 3 very fast casting spells. Most melees can kill us in a very short time through melee damage. I think it would be balenced and fair for enchanters, who have very little damage dealing ability in PvP, to at least have a way to prevent the devestating damage that opponents can do to them in just one spell, or a few rounds of melee combat.

In addition, there are exploits that enchanters' opponents use to prevent themselves from being mezzed. Opponents who fear getting mezzed often cast low-level poison or disease based dots on themselves (enchanters have no way of removing such a DoT spell from their opponents). The small amount of damge these spells do breaks the mez very quickly. Opponents will often have members of their groups hit them to break mezzes as well. This happens alot, so on top of hardly ever being able to land a mez do to resistances, the mez is often made completely or nearly innefective do to exploits.

In the PvE game enchanters primarily act in the capacity as crowd control. It makes logical sense that this would be our roll in PvP as well. But right now we cannot do that because our mezzes are too easily resisted.

(3) Make enchanter Nuke's less resistable. Enchanter nukes are the weakest nukes of all classes. Not only do they not do much damage, but they have a long cast time... >6 seconds. And a long recast time. The recast time seems to be longer then 6 seconds as well. So not only do our spells not do much damage, but they also cannot be cast that often in a period of time. Our Direct damage spells are also not very mana efficient as well. They do have an instant stun associated with them, which interupts opponent casting, but doesn't actually leave the player stunned for any period of time. In addition our direct damage spells are "all-or-nothing" spells. They either hit or they don't, with no chance for a partial resist. In practice our direct damage spells are almost always resisted in PvP combat. I have heard that enchanter nukes have a double save against magic check, do to the stun component of the spell, and this is the reason they are so highly resisted.

Does it really make sense for one of the weakest, most least damaging spells to be also the easiest to resist? Once some friends did a test. One guy buffed up his Cold resist to 140, and had a wizard cast ice-comet as a cleric healed him. Ice-comet seemed to be doing about half as much damage as it normally does on average (which means a wizard can still do a hefty amount of damage even when faced with high resists) With 140 Magic resist, an enchanter will almost never be able to do any damage to you at all. Contrast that with a necro or druid, no matter how much magic resist you have, 95% of the time they will be able to cast splurt or winged death on you. I don't think these classes need to be nerfed. I just think enchanters needs some way to be able to hit back in some capacity.

BTW, I believe almost everything I've discussed in regards to enchanter DD spells also applies to cleric DD spells, but since I've never played a cleric I can't really speak with much authority on the matter.

Of the 3 remedies I suggested for enchanter PvP I and most enchanters I talk to think the first solution (increasing the effectiveness of the Tashan line of spells) is the fairest, simplets and best solution, but the other 2 would be nice as well. Whatever you do, you need to do something to make it fun for clerics and enchanters on PvP servers, so more people will choose to play these 2 essential classes on PvP servers. They are quite scarce now. Just checking the usage logs of high level enchanters on Tallon Zek won't give you an accurate picture of how bad the problem is. This is because many of the high level enchanters on Tallon Zek have basically quit playing their enchanters (I know this for a fact) but share the account loggin info with their guildmates. Most of the time the enchanter is on he/she is being played by guildmates who need an enchanter for a PvE dungeon raid.
