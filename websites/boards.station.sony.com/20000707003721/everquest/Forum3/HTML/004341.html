You will never, ever, ever be outclassed as a healer. A cleric will always be welcomed in a group and you will always be the main healer and the one groups are looking for.

A druid can provide some healing, a bit of tanking and a bit of spellcasting and are also often welcomed because they can buff and sow as well, but as for a healer (especially at higher levels I am told) a cleric is always the first choice. A druid does in a pinch.

Not saying druids and necros aren't welcomed into a group, but if a group needs a healer, they are going to chose the cleric over the druid. Cleric is probably the most group friendly class there is.

And yes, perhaps it appears that druids and necros get better spells past 50, but they are not the priest class the cleric is. Therefore, they have more attack spells and DOTS that the cleric does. A good cleric knows his role is this: Keep everyone in the group alive. For a druid and necro, those roles are often different in a group setting so comparing the two is comparing two different things, really.

A cleric heals. That's a cleric's job. I assume you know that and know that your job will be to keep everyong alive later on and melee and most other offensive spell casting will be out.

Don't worry, you are the first and best choice. I've been told that at higher levels a cleric who does his/her job well can be the most effective and powerful player in the game.

DoT: Damage Over Time. A DoT spell will do some damage to the target each round until it wears off.

DD: Direct Damage. All the damage this type of spell will do is immediate.

SoW: Spirit of Wolf. Druids and Shamen get this spell. It basically doubles your running speed. Very nice, saves many lives.

Aggro: Each beastie has an 'aggro range' around them, which is to say that if you enter this range, they will become aggressive towards you. In addition, certain things in combat move you up or down on the 'aggro list' which determines who the monster is most mad with at any given time. Things that increase your position on the aggro list (i.e., make them mad at you) are: doing damage, taunting, healing a party member in combat, buffing a party member in combat, debuffing a monster, sitting, being low on health, being physically close to the monster. Things that decrease your position (relatively speaking): waiting for other party members to build up their standing on the aggro list before healing/blasting, increasing distance between you and the monster, and some spells such as memory blur and atone (enchanter and cleric, respectively).

Hope this helps!

Druids and Shamans can help in the healer roles in slightly different ways. Druids have pack-chloroplast, which will give the entire group a strong regen... but this is mostly a good tool for keeping downtime down, and letting people that just get hit slightly in a combat top off their health. It won't make a big swing during the combat itself as something creams into them (will help a little, but won't cover the distance). Shamans can slow the attack speeds of things, causing there to be a need for less healing. The combat until they get that going can be pretty tense though, and if there is no secondary healer they could potentially lose people because of a lot of resists (faced with the choice of mana-dumping and going oom before it dies, or continue trying to slow it and then heal, with possibly one person dying).

We clerics are still supreme because of complete heal, though there are quite a few cases where I can't use it (people that won't survive 10 seconds for it to go off, for instance).
