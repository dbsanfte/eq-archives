1. BUGS / TWEAKS

AE Spells hit group members and / or pets. This makes many AE spells effectively unuseable, despite the fact that many zones and encounters are tuned for the useage of such spells.

Uber Guards. The buffed newbie guards kill the roleplaying enjoyment of city raids that used to occur. (suggest restoring the guards to normal and removing experience gains for killing them)

Fear/Charm: Enchanters would like a way for these spells to be restored, even at a much limited capacity (ie. charmed players should not lose exp, and perhaps should react like an enchanter pet, only attacking who / what attacks the enchanter)


BALANCE ISSUES

Item loot favors casters. Item loot favors the opportunistic. I love item loot, but only if it can be implemented fairly with equal risk to both parties... Risk vs Reward. Exploiters and opportunistic PKs risk nothing.

Newbie killing. Its crazy and the number 1 reason why players who experiment on PVP quit within minutes. On Rallos Zek PVP should not begin until level 5. I have had a newbie killer slay a new character of mine before I had taken my FIRST NEWBIE STEP. I entered the game for the first time on that character and saw Soandso has killed you, LOADING PLEASE WAIT...

Spells do lesser damage in pvp, 66% of the damage that they would do in pve. This flat % for all levels does not solve the problem. For example, a level 4 wizard is worthless at nuking in pvp, but a level 44 wizard can kill a level 40 enchanter in 2 spells. The damage reduction should be on a SLIDING SCALE per LEVEL.

Spell Resistances. Magic resistance items are a must have in pvp to prevent serious spells like blind, root, and mez. As an enchanter, I am often faced with an even con opponent that none of my spells land on, even after I use my MR debuff.

Other spell resistances, like ice and fire, have a far less noticible effect on reducing the damage of those spells.

A deep look at how spells are resisted should be required.


EXPLOITS

Deleveling. This is an exploit achieved by melee characters. A level 20+ melee character will get exp killed repeatedly until they are of a level to attack newbies with NO RISK to themselves.

Just 2 nights ago my level 8 Paladin was attacked by a Yellow con rogue who had a last name. You can imagine how that battle went when I was being backstabbed for insane amounts of damage.

Customer service refuses to punish delevelers, even if they can observe someone purposefully taking advantage of this quirk in engine mechanics.

Exploit: No line of sight required for pvp spells. This causes many deaths in cities where a player has no hope of knowing where their opponent is casting from.

Level limit bypasses: If you are invisible and someone out of range casts upon you, your invis will drop.

CUSTOMER SERVICE

The Rallos Zek rules posted on this website are outdated. Many rules are enforced that appear nowhere in official documentation. This also allows for an incredible amount of inconsistancy on rule enforcement.

There never are, nor can there ever be, enough guides and GMS on Rallos. I've been exploited, trained, more times than I can count. Never did a CS official arrive in time to help me with these issues. Want to catch exploiters? Put a newbie character in some armor and watch players use whatever means they can to kill that player.

Server moves? In the past it was alluded that there might be server moves. Abashi stated that there were enough players of each pvp type that there should be 2 coin only servers and 1 loot server. However, none of the players on any of those servers were allowed to move. Time invested in one's character is absolutely crucial, and people would not give this up even to protest not being allowed to move.

Ach-hem.

Item 1) Z-axis poofing.
This is an Everquest issue in general, but it adversely effects PvP. Simply, when a player is too much higher or lower than you (frequently as little as about 6 feet), they can simply vanish. The server stops sending you information about them, even if you have them targeted, even after the recent improvements to update rate.

The simple fix, IMO, is if you have someone targeted, the server should send you information about them no matter where they are, what altitude, etc.

(I frequently wonder if it's just a bug with the Z-axis, where it miscalculates, thinking that 5' above = 500' above, so it decides the target is out of range and stops sending information, even though they should be in plain sight.)

Item 2) "Immortal buffing".
I call it immortal buffing, because 'healing' is only part of it. This is on Team-PvP where a dark elf heals a human who is killing another dark elf. The dark elf being killed cannot defend himself from the immortal buffer, because they are on the same team. The most reasonable "fix" for this that I've heard is to implement PvP flags -- normally you can crossbuff and heal all you want, but if you are damaged by another player, then for a certain period of time, only people from your team can heal and buff you.

There is also an "out of range" componet to this, but in my opinion as a player who has been on Vallon Zek full time since it opened, OOR buffing is not worth addressing. Same-team buffing is the main problem and the main source of grief players.

Item 3) Guides showing up on ShowEQ.
Not technically a PvP issue, but it's a griefer issue, and griefers like PvP servers. Simply, a guide entering a zone invisibly to scout for exploiters will show up on ShowEQ. The grief players know when to stop causing trains, etc, because of this. Fix this, catch the griefers, everyone is happy.

Item 4) PvP death penalty.
Simply put, there isn't one. Making harm touch not recycle when killed is a good start, but in addition, mana regeneration should be crippled and melee attack speed should be lowered via an un-dispellable "rez effect". Exact details can be debated, but there needs to be SOME death penalty. On VZ/TZ, where items cannot be looted, griefplay is simply out of hand, partially because there is no penalty for dying a PvP death.

Item 5) Unresistable DOT's.
These need to be addressed for PvP. Winged Death, Drifting Death, Splurt, etc, are all 99% unresistable DOT's that do ridiculous amounts of damage for their mana cost/cast time. This wasn't a big deal until Egg Shaped Pumice was removed. Warriors, rogues and monks cannot dispell themselves with the new Crystallized Pumice because they have no channeling skill. A 1 damage hit from a druid will interrupt them 100% of the time.

Item 6) Twinkage.
This is really a consequence of removing item loot from VZ/TZ. Level 20's in fungal regrowth tunics, wielding weapons that are near epic quality with full sets of diamond/blue diamond resist gear. That's just not fun for anyone, and when you do finally kill them, they're back in action 30 seconds later because there's no PvP death penalty. Low levels are completely in favor of melee twinks these days. (Casters can be twinked, but the improvements are very minor.)

Item 7) Casting through walls.
I realize this probably isn't doable, but I'll list it anyway. Currently, a player cannot cast on an NPC through a wall. You CAN cast on another player through a wall, though. This leads to some obvious "grief" tactics and is basically a bug exploit.
