And however beneficial this sounds initially, one can be quick to learn (after controlling or grouping with a ranger character) that the ranger is a nothing more than a combination of an undercasting druid and a weak warrior. Not looking so good anymore is it?

The reply above states that even an upper level ranger is a burden to groups, where as the ranger can neither effectively tank, cast, buff, or heal. And we should all agree. This leads one to ask himself, "What benefits do I have over other classes?" and "How exactly am I beneficial to groups, especially when fighting within a group becomes a necessity?"

Well the fact is that no matter how many bottles of mead a ranger drinks, he feel never better about himself as a character and as a team player?

Today I was playing everquest with my ranger in a group. The group consisted of my ranger, a warrior, and druid, all of which were level 19. "Well", I thought to myself, "this ought to be interesting." And not surprised in the least, I found that my ranger's attack damage was well below par in contrast to the warrior's attack and yet my casting, buffing, and healing skills were much below par in contrast to the druid's casting. And I thought to myself, "What in the hell is the purpose of a ranger...?" Yeah, I can't figure it out either. The tracking function is USELESS (with reasons scattered all over the message boards), and it does not make ranger's uniquely helpful, in the least, while fighting in groups. Archery is not unique to the ranger. Nothing is unique to the ranger. The ranger is inadequate as a group player.

Begin aside...

I understand the concept of hybrid characters; to combine attributes of two characters into one character. For example, incase it isn't clear enough, the ranger character is not unlike the dog called the "****erpoo". Not in that the ranger has an unsual name, but that like the ****erpoo the Ranger class got the butt-end of the deal when it was created. You see, the ****erpoo is half ****er Spaniel and Half Poodle. It could have been called the "Spandle", but instead someone chose to combine the worst parts of both names, the first being "****er" and second being "Poo", to create the terrible name "****erpoo". Now I have a ****erpoo and I can tell you that this dog should not be judged by its ridiculous name. It's characteristics contain the compact size and acute sense of smell of the ****er Spaniel while simultaneously possessing the supreme eyesight and shedless fur coat of the Poodle. Quite a unique package I must say. Accordingly, when the Ranger was created, exciting many players with its hybrid warrior/druid hybrid concept, its characteristics were chosen contain the worst characteristics of both parent classes. Just as the ****erpoo inherited the worst of its parents name.

... end aside.

When fighting in groups the Ranger is an open-wound, a bruise on the warface of the entire party. The ranger has no bussiness trying to take damage while fighting and even less business trying to heal during the aftermath. The warrior and the druid should be ashamed of their ranger child. And they are, this is apparent when a ranger has to bribe a group to let him join the fight. (see above reply)

This is how it should be...

The ranger shouldn't have a hybrid of weakend skills of its parent classes (warrior/druid) but, rather, should contain skills which are unique to the ranger, making him a more valuable team player. Now, I'm not going to spoon-feed Verant a schematic about of how to redesign their ranger, after all it's THEIR job to make all classes of EverQuest equally important. - To create a harmony, if you will, between certain group and guild arrangements. Currently the ranger is a benchwarming waterboy, while the Druid leads in assists and the Warrior in points. The ranger class deserves its chance to shine!

An example of what I am saying is the Armor Class situation Ranger's Deal with. The AC of Ranger armor is far less substantial then other Melee classes and this was explained originally by Verant as a Balance issue. This seems correct. Rangers should have less AC then a Warrior. Then the real truth came out with the release of Kunark. The AC of armor is secondary to the natural AC of the class, as derived from the Defense Skill. Verant admitted that Rangers were not performing correctly by upping the Dodge/Parry skills, but never corrected the issue by doing what they should have done, which is raising our defense skill. So now Rangers have Lower armor AC and lower defense skills. Why? Because if they did raise Defense Skill, other melee classes would have the incorrect notion that Rangers were being made more powerfull. Verant values public opinion over statistical fact.
