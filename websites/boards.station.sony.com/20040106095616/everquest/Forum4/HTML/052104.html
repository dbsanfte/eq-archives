What you are experiencing is the "player-driven" economy.

What should an item sell for? It should sell for whatever people are willing to pay for it, and there-in lies the hard part.

There are several web sites that will show what an item might sell for, but from my experience the prices they quote are seriously out of whack. I believe they get their data from a select few who are trying desperately to skew the numbers in some vain hope of getting more for their inventory than its actually worth.

So in general, you will have to learn an item's value from having some playing experience.

----------

Prices can be affected by NPC vendors as well.

Example:
NPC Joe offers 100pp for an item. If you think a player will ever sell it to you for less than 100pp, don't hold your breath. Some person might do it, but it's probably because they haven't checked what the vendor is offering yet. This has the same effect as 'government subsidies' that prop up prices. Despite the fact the item may not be worth 100pp, the NPCs are acting as a government entity with endless cash who can afford the prop ups.

In a different vein, that same NPC might offer only 3pp for an item worth 100pp. This is actually the proper situation that SHOULD exist. By offering so little the players are pretty much encouraged to NOT sell to NPCs, and instead offer the items up for sale to other players. After all, the best way to have a "player-driven" economy is when players sell to each other and the NPCs are left out of the equation.

It is all supply and demand.

If it sells quickly, it is either (a) in demand; or (b) you underpriced it.

If it sells slowly, you either (a) overpriced it; or (b) is not currently in demand.

You mostly have to do it trial and error. See what other people are selling for.

Keep in mind that if someone has 247 spiderling silks for sale at 20 plat each and there are no other vendors, that those spiderling silks have not sold because they are overpriced, and are not the going rate.

Some things which should be high priced aren't because they drop commonly (what is that Paly weapon as an example??) and don't have that much demand.

Some things were once high priced (Iron Oxide anyone??) and now are not. Blue Diamonds much the same thing. They were once rare and hard to get, now there are so many they are usually just vendor fodder.

As you learn more about gear and the game, you will know when you find a good deal at a NPC merchant. Until then though... don't buy any armor or gear from an NPC unless you know the bazaar price.

Economics 101.

Value is relative not absolute. The true value of any item is what someone is willing to pay for it.

There is no “correct” price in the bazaar. Check and see what comparable items are going for and price accordingly. If your item does not sell in a reasonable time, lower the price. If it gets snapped up immediately, try asking more next time. Just like RL.

The bazaar is hard to learn, and everchanging. It is different from server to server as well. The key IMO to the bazaar is PATIENCE.

You have to scan the prices that people are charging for a period of time. This keeps you from getting ripped off, or just paying X, and then trying to sell it yourself 2-3 months later, and stuck selling for Y.

The same thing applies to selling tradeskill made items like Leather padding, or Grobb liquized meat, for example. Plus the prices are always changing. Example on my server the prices for silk swatches from late spring to end of sept., were running 13-20pp each. Now the prices have fallen back to around 8-11PP each. So you have to adjust to the changes. That was kinda generalized, and the bazaar is way more complex than almost anything in EQ. Good luck on your career as a trader.
