Well, well, well.. time to talk about enchanters.
I've briefly read over probably about half the posts already in this thread, so I'll be touching briefly on a lot of things people have lready said but I'll try not to say the same things over.

I'd like to start by stating that I don't believe enchanters to be any over powered class. As one other person mentioned, enchanters should be the yardstick by which other classes are measured, since as it stands, enchanters and bards are the only two classes that currently reward smart, intelligent playing, and really seperate the people who know their class from those who don't.

Much like clerics, enchanters are considered powerful due to their essential role a group, and the ability to make the impossible possible. There is, however, a few things to consider in this regard. Firstly, the old addage "it's a tough job, but somebody has to do it" comes to mind. Secondly, there is a flipside to all of this. Enchanters are so specialized in what they CAN do, that there are a lot of things they CANNOT do. They cannot effectively nuke (the damage ratio on our spells are not bad, but they're all magic based and highly resistable), cannot really do any sort of pet damage at higher levels (except in highly specialized situations), and as a corralation, cannot really solo. Sure, enchanters can charm-solo, but that's dangerous, and never as xp efficient as grouping (and impossible at most levels due to environments and types of mobs around).

A lot of what I'm saying here can be summed up as follows: enchanters don't need to be changed, at least much. I've seen a lot of people post that enchanters should get controllable pets, get some sort elemental-based nuke... I highly disagree. Giving enchanters the ability to effectively deal damage in a fight WOULD begin to imbalance the class, since it would eliminate the drawbacks we currently have. It would be like giving clerics a nuke that was actually mana efficient.

And the suggestions to give some sort of visual sign that a mob is mezzed? No way. That would take away part of the art of being an enchanter, the art of grouping with an enchanter, and make killing in EverQuest even more mindless and simple than it already is.

As for the role of enchanters on a raid, and the problems of such... firstly, I've read people posting that it's pointless to have multiple enchanters on a raid. Well, to a degree this is true. I whole-heartedly disagree with the people who state that you can only have one enchanter mezzing things due to different mez spells not stacking -- I do this all the time on raids. Entrance is my mez spell of choice, whereas other people prefer Dazzle. We deal with it, and things get mezzed quite effectively. You just need to use your head (there's that skill part of being an enchanter again...) and remember which things you've mezzed, and keep them mezzed. Or if this is really an issue, co-ordinate with other enchanters on the raid, and use the same spell. As for enchanters being useless in the big fights... well... yeah. But it's just part of the class. Should we really be essential to the buffing of the raid, and still be able to nuke with the big boys when it actually comes down to that? Sounds to me like having our cake and eating it too. If you really want something to do on a raid, start chain-casting Rune V on people.

The other thing I saw mentioned that sort of irked me were people suggesting that single target mezzes should be able to overwrite AE mezzes. Damn, that would be sweet, but I think much too sweet. Just start off any dangerous situation with an AE mez, and single target mez everything at your leisure? Sign me up! Seriously though, that would make crowd control entirely too easy.

Now to make a quick checklist of other burning points, and my thoughts on them:

- Lull spells: yeah, they're useless as they are due to their resist rate. Would be nice if we could actually use them.
- Whirl line: it's just plain broken in it's current form.
- Religion/illusions: religion is meant to be a double-edged blade. I'd like to think that your choice of god still matters, I don't see any great need to change this.
- High-level illusions: not essential, but would be sorta nice to have.
- Controllable pets: I covered that above. No way, it would overpower the class.
- Mezzing problems in Velious: certainly should be looked into. I avoid Velious like the plague these days.
- Debuffs: yeah, our stat debuffs (the disempower and weakness lines) don't seem to make any noticable difference in combat. I'm not expecting much, but it would be nice if either the effectiveness of decreasing mobs stats or the actual stat decreases themselves were raised.
- Short spell durations: except for the effect on my epic (which is a pain in the ass), I've never had a problem with spell durations.
- Illusions dropping when zoning/ld: this is a serious point that really needs to be fixed. I think it's wrong that we should be at the mercy of our internet connection when wandering through dangerous places with illusions on, particularly when this sort of behaviour is required for many of our quests and what not. If fixing this would require having illusions stick when logging, zoning, etc. then so be it, but that's certainly the most important aspect to fix.
- Sentinel: remove that spell. It does nothing but lag about a 100 people when someone decides to cast it at the EC tunnel. :P
- Mem blurs: well, mem blurs certainly do seem pretty useless 50+. Not sure if this is intentional or not, but it's something worth bringing up and discussing.
- Berserker Strength line: it would be nice if it stacked with strength buffs. As it stands, they're pretty much unusable. (I currently only use them as self-buffs for the damage absorb component)

So, in closing, I think enchanters as they stand are a beautifully executed class. Lots of fun if doing damage isn't your thing, and rewarding smart gameplay. Some minor tweaking to current spells may be in order, but overall I think they need little in the way of subtractions, but not really much in the way of additions either.

In your post you mentioned people complaining that Enchanters, and some other classes, are overpowered and you may have to nerf us in order to balance the classes.

I just wish that people would realize that a nerf to Enchanters, or any of these classes, will basically be a nerf to groups. I'm sure that if some of our abilities and effectiveness are reduced or removed they won't be given to other classes to compensate for their loss to a group.

What will basically happen is just a loss of the potential power of a well organised symbiotic group. And encouraging us to group and interract is something the designers have always said they are trying to achieve. As such, it seems to me, reducing the effectiveness of groups in general is not conducive to achieving this goal.

Personally, I think the real crux of the matter is that some players feel that groups may tend to choose an Enchanter, or any of the godlike classes, over some other classes when forming a group and that's not fair to the other classes.

And while I can understand that feeling I don't believe the solution to this is to nerf Enchanters, or any other group oriented class like Clerics or Bards, so that they aren't so attractive to a group anymore. As I said, all this does is reduce the effectiveness of groups in general.

Actually the godlike power of the Enchanter that everyone talks about is pretty situational. If you need crowd control, you seek an Enchanter. Otherwise, Druids and Shaman have heals and SoW and tend to get chosen over an Enchanter, even considering Clarity. In a camp where crowd control isn't an issue most groups will choose a Cleric over an Enchanter. You may have a little more downtime, but you will live.

It's a fact that in Everquest some classes are more group oriented and group enhancing than other classes. Many people forming groups will tend to choose those classes over others. It's just the nature of the game.

As in:

Dang, I tried to get in a group in (insert dungeon or plane name here) and there was an Enchanter (or Cleric, Druid, Shaman) and I didn't have a chance. This is a bummer, they should do something about it.

I think that when everyone talks about balancing the classes, what they are really talking about is trying to get the classes to the point where a group won't choose one class over another simply because of their abilities and their perceived potential to enhance a group's chance of success.

Good luck game designers, this is going to be a monumental task.

Without removing these classes defining abilities completely, SoW, healing, Clarity, Manasong, Mez, I just don't see how you can do it. Groups will always want these abilities and spells available to them. And classes who have them will probably always be chosen over classes who don't.

I play the game for fun. I have started several characters and classes. I tend to play each of them according to the situation that exists in any given game session.

If I don't have friends to group with, I play one of my solo oriented characters. If my friends are online, I pull out one of my more group oriented characters.

I don't worry too much about balance. Early on I realized that it's hard to find a good pick-up group in this game. So I bit the bullet and started some characters that I could play effectively when I couldn't find a group. I could then still play and have fun even if I wasn't advancing my "main" character.

I know people have heard this over and over, but I still think it's one of the greatest features of this game, and that is:

You can be anything you want in Everquest. You can be an Enchanter. You can be a Cleric. You can be a Druid. You can BE any of these godlike classes you want to be.

To me, that is the true balance in this game. None of us as players are really denied these godlike qualities and abilities.

All it takes is some time and effort to build one of these characters and you can be as godlike as anyone else.

I realize this post doesn't address specific issues of balance concerning Enchanters, but I just wanted to point out some obvious truths about balance in this game while you are making your decisions about how you will enhance or reduce the power of Enchanters or any other class for that matter.

Make us better, we will love both you and the game.

Nerf us and we will become frustrated with both you and the game.

Just the nature of the beast.

Anyway, my two cents worth.

Good luck and good hunting and see you in the game!
