This post is targetted at the purpose of this thread – the final class balancing of the Necro.
A quick comment: I do understand that there are MANY complexities to balancing a class that most of us are incapable of considering due to the non-transparent nature of the details of the game mechanics. My concern herein is that with the magnitude of the task – issues that were once dealt with will not be reconsidered even though things may have changed significantly since then.

*Alan said:
"Also, we are not looking to make necromancers any more effective at soloing than they already are. And, well, the necromancer is a pretty powerful class as they are now."

Alan, please don't buy into the Necro all-powerful "hype" immediately. Do some reasonable comparisons in situations and environments that VI feels are appropriate for the game they/you envision. Obviously that will be the game you will endeavor to promote, and scenarios other than those are going to become progressively more meaningless.

Our "power" is repeatedly based upon our ability to fight a single, low magic resistant mob in an open outdoor zone, or in a dungeon with pathing problems. This is a small part of a Necro’s life, and it is unfair and unreasonable to summarize our class with such an isolationist view. Consider our power compared to the abilities of the other classes in the same situations.

The progression of "power" of different classes against the Necro class is another consideration, in times gone by things were very different. Changes in the past were made due to game mechanics then which are now very different from when those changes were deemed appropriate. There are some very knowledgeable people on these boards who have been around for a long time. There is a history to the power of a necro that has been discussed in various proses throughout these threads. Zrazzt seems to know as much as anyone about this.

Consider balancing our class according to soloabiliy, groupability, and raidability. Do not forget to consider the effectiveness of multiple necros in a group vs. other classes in the same situations.

In addition, remember our faction and trade/banking penalties.

An interesting side-effect of increasing the group experience bonuses is that Necromancers have less of an opportunity to take advantage of this than more group-friendly classes (by definition). This is actually yet another (small, but tangible) negative effect of being a Necro.

*Alan said:
“The primary issues that I see revolve around the battle with the boss monsters.”

Please do not limit your consideration to the top 1% of players in 1% of scenarios...this is supposed to be the final balance of the class.


*Alan said:
“But I certainly want to hear from necromancers with concerns about all levels of the game. And, once again, I have to warn you that since we will be looking closely at this class we may very well feel that something needs to have its power reduced to achieve balance.”

A very reasonable statement. But look closely – PLEASE. I have no doubt that if you all leave preconceptions at the door, and perform a fair assessment of the Necro class in various scenarios, you will come out with a far more realistic view of where we stand in the GRAND scheme of things.
