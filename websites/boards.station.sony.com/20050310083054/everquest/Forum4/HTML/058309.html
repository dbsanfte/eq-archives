DS are great. One thing I found when PLing my cleric was if you don't do at least 1 point of damage, and let the DS do all the damage and kill the MoB, then you don't get the XP.

If you can, get Temperance. It's an 800 point HP buff, that does not have level restrictions on it. Also try to get a set of armor and weapons that are much higher than your level. That will help take them down faster.

One downside of PLing is that you're skills (combat, spells etc.) do not keep up with your level. So you may have a level 30 caster with only 40 skill in channeling, for example (where he should have nearly 150 skill in it.) That will affect his ability to cast the higher level spells, and make you much less effective in a group your own level. Same with meleers. They may only have 50 weapon skill when they should have 150, which means they will hit much less often. In addition to that, you don't really learn how to play the class well, so you become a detriment to your group.

PLing is fine, I bet we all do it at times (I know I do), but you need to be careful of those things so it doesn't hurt you later on.

If you are trying to get him to your level, don't group. If you want to level with him, wait until he's got a couple more levels before grouping with him.

Around Halas - Blackburrow might be ok for powerleveling, though you might have to go deep. Paludal or Kruns would be better. If you choose Blackburrow be careful - the mobs will run and cause trains or pull in other mobs to the fight.

I'd recommend Paludal on the Shadeweavers Thicket side. You don't have to deal with the horror of the ShadowHaven side of Paludal, and the zone is easy to get to if you need to run. On my server it is empty most of the time. Becareful getting there, the aliens in the Shadeweaver tunnels leading to Paludal can be trouble for you. Get a map, get Spirit of Wolf on both of you, and invis if you can.

Bugs there are sociable, but numerous ones can be easily single pulled. Nothing in the first cavern, except the fish in the water, will attack on sight.

Buff the rogue up, and you pull a bug. Smack on it a little (just a little) to get it mad at you. Keep your pet away. Get the rogue behind the bug and he starts wacking. Heal yourself, then when the bug starts hitting the rogue, heal him. When the mob gets to about 30-40%, call in your pet and help finish it off. If the rogue doesn't get the exp, you jumped in too soon, or did too much damage at the beginning.
