JooJooFlop,

You're just a tad off describing the cat thingy.

You seem to be referring to the Schrödinger's Cat thought experiment to illustrate the uncertainty principle. In short, the uncertainty priciple states that the act of observing quantum particles affects them such that, for example, one can never simultaneously know the spin and the position. Schrödinger's experiment said that you have a cat in a box, and the box is rigged so that opening it triggers a mechanism that will kill the cat. Until the box is opened, the cat exists simultaneously alive and dead, but the act of observing it determines its state, thus illustrating the uncertainty principle.

It's a bit more complicated than that, but those are the major points.

There: everything you never wanted to know about dead cats and quantum mechanics, all rolled into a neat package.
