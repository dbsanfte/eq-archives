>"The buff aspect is a kludge to work around the problem that items cannot have a recast time. Otherwise, they'd just put a 7.5 minute recast time on the breastplate."<

Actually, it's far better than just a longer recast time. If the recast time was 7.5 minutes, the cleric would only be able to use it once in that time. With this change the cleric can heal his entire party (of six) completely in three minutes without using mana.

>"Are you going to nerf monsters to conpensate for this ?"<

Actually, we've already thought of that. There are certainly some monsters in the game that were designed with that breastplate in mind (that alone is an indication of how horribly messed up that item is). We will be looking at those encounters as well and adjusting them as needed.

>"maybe just change the way mobs react to the CHeal....make em REALLY pissed when someone gets fully healed...yes no?"<

We also thought over that possibility. I think it was one of the first ideas we had. But it would allow the cleric far too much control over the monster's behavior. It would allow other sorts of tricks that we don't want happening either.

>"Direct Question : Will the Nerfing of this breastplate be linked to a reduction in the insane Mobflation that has occured ? Say, a 50% reduction in Hitpoints for all mobs over 32,000, coupled with a 50% drop in MR so that a Wizard Lure is only resisted half the time ? "<

Direct answer: No. The existence of most of those creatures is unrelated to the power of the breastplate. But, as I said before, there are some that will be re-evaluated after the breastplate change.

>"It really makes no difference if the cleric uses a BP or spell to CH,…"<

It makes a huge difference. It's called mana. The breastplate is not limited by anything right now but it's 30 second cast time. The cleric is limited by his mana pool.

>"Part 1: Will you be un-able to clear the effect with nulify and like spells?"<

We will make certain that this effect is not removable.

>"Actually, this is a nerf aimed at the top 1% of players, that will only affect the top 1% of players."<

Correct. And even more importantly, if we do not make this change, the game will have to be changed to compensate for it, and that WILL affect every player all the way down the line. This is what we are trying to avoid.

>"As soon as one of them dropped, the entire cleric class knew the implications"<

And that may very well have been true. We were, certainly, slow to take this action. But, well, even when the last nerf took place, there were clerics that had never even seen the item in game complaining about the change and insisting that they would quit the game if we did it. If it was so obviously broken, there were very few clerics out there willing to admit it out loud.

I think, through all of this, that I see a general belief that having the buff affect overwrite another buff is preferred over having the spell fail. That seems the logical choice to me, too.

>" There has never been an effect so permanent in the almost two-year history of the release of this game."<

Tons of them. They are called DoTs.

>"DO NOT MAKE IT INTO A BUFF !
We have so many stacking issues…"<

This effect would stack with anything.

>"Stop inventing invisible bogey men for Cleric Nerfs [1]"<

There is no invisible bogey man. This item is broken. I am not making any excuses about it. Yes, we should have done this a long time ago. But, well, perhaps we'd become a little gun-shy. No matter how necessary the 'nerf', there is always an outcry from a vocal group, and perhaps people here were a bit unwilling to anger customers. That seems understandable to me, but I wasn't here when those decisions were made. But this item MUST be changed. Our options are, really, to let it ruin the game, or to fix it despite the outcry. More people would be upset if we left it in when they saw the long-term effects of this item on gameplay than are upset now that we are (finally) changing it.

Alan
