Station Member 		posted 02-14-2001 07:03 AM           
The game is way out of whack once you level past 50 (or for any level in SoV).

Because teams of warriors with enough healing can kill just about anything in this game, SoV had to be made to be a challenge to this team. Anything less would have been too easy for this kind of combination.

That's the reason that MOBs are so magic resistant, hit so hard, and have so many hitpoints; in order for them to be a challenge, they had to be difficult for the 'best' combination of classes. This leaves all the other combinations unable to do much of anything because they simply cannot compete with the best of the best -- something that is a challenge for the best of the best is completely impossible for anything less.

So how do you fix it? I think Verant can address this issue with the next expansion. In order to make the game fun for ALL players several things need to be done for the next expansion.

-Leave SoV the way it is. This will provide lots of raid content for those who can do it.

-The next expansion must be BIG. I mean, TONS of zones. Why? Because you are going to have to provide content for all types of players, uber-guilds, small groups, and soloers alike.

-Work on creating many more zones like LGuk and SolB; this means near a bind point, balanced for a single group, decent loot drops. By balanced for a single group, I mean that MOBs don't kill anything less than a Warrior in 2 rounds of combat, and don't have extraordinary resists. Let those pickup groups that are only composed of a SK, shaman, necro, two mages, and a bard be able to break an area and hold it. Similar zones should be created for the rest of the level ranges. Old Sebilis and Karnor's are nice, but nobody wants to go too deep without a cleric because CR will become near impossible. Old dungeons did not have this problem -- invisibility worked well, as well as other tools (hide/sneak, feign, etc). These zones are targetted at the high level 'casual' players.

-Several outdoor zones like Lake of Ill Omen, Frontier Mountains, and Burning Woods. Again, no insane magic resistancs, no level 30 mobs hitting in the 100s; provide good hunting areas for those people who don't care for dungeons, or don't have enough time to play for them.

-A handful of lesser raid zones. A pickup raid can do Nagafen, Vox, FGs, IGs, etc. A pickup raid cannot really do Kael, Sleepers, etc. Areas designed with pickup raids would be a nice addition because the casual gamer would stand a decent chance of going on one of these. These mobs should NOT be 100% resistant to all but Lures and should not quad for 800s.

-A handful of 'uber' (for lack of a better word) raid zones. Zones that are designed for guilds like FoH, Triton, LoD. Contrary to popular belief, guilds like these do not sit around and farm the easy stuff because they can, they go for the challenging zones. Provide them with the content to do so; new zones like these in addition to VP and most of the SoV zones should provide plenty of content.

-More plane zones like Fear and Hate, not PoG. Zones that are designed with the 'planes capable but not uber' guilds. Add in more Gods, they are fun to kill (Design them like Innoruuk and Cazic, not like Tunare -- remember, we're not designing these zones with the most powerful guilds in mind).

Those are just the zone designs that should be considered. There are other gameplay issues that will have to be considered to make the package complete.

-At least semi-convenient bind locations. Casual players will always gravitate around the bind locations, provide them with enough were it's fun to travel to the new zones.

-Proper loot distribution. Random loot is a very good thing, and loot from the new expansion should be designed to be similar to loot in the other dungeons. Put the best loot in the uber zones, they deserve it. Put decent loot appropriate for the level of the dungeon in the rest of them; it should be decent enough to improve the character.

-Random spawn locations for NPCs. This will help curb any farming that will be done. Possible negative effect of this would be a higher level character simply slaughtering the entire zone a la the Ancient Cyclops, so perhaps static names spawns are a good thing. Please discuss

-Gear must be improved for the casting classes. Casters are being left far behind melee classes even in the pre-50 game now due to the loot that's dropping. Gear such as spell-haste items, damage increasers, meditation focus items, and resistance reducers (innate effects, not right-click with long casting times) would help immensely. Place variations of these items in many existing dungeons too, to avoid a farming rush into the new zones.

-All new spells placed on vendors. Again, this is to avoid people feeling like they have to sit at a spawn point and kill until they actually get one of their spells. Having spells dropped leads to people simply not having a chance to get them, and let's face it; a caster with all of his spells does not equal a melee character.

-Wide variety of equal equipment. Nearly the same items, different graphic/names. Instead of having Jade Mace be the best Kunark weapon, have 5 of them that are similar.

-Raise the 200 cap on INT/WIS for post-50 characters. It does not fit into the current game, casters need more mana to begin to pull their weight again.

Basically, create zones with the different player groups that you have. Casual, non-dungeon, minor raiders, and uber players alike want to have places to go and have fun. Part of the reason that people are complaining about SoV (aside from casters hardly having any use in many places) is that many of them will never see many of the zones. For a lot of people, they payed full price to play in 25% of the new content, and that's just not right.

Please feel free to comment, I'm open to criticism

I should add here that while I agree with the topic and the majority of the points posted, "class balance" and "game balance" are rather intertwined like the two snakes of the doctor's cadeuceus (sp?) symbol.

The game balance (let's call it encounter balance) we have right now is designed by Verant to be challenging to the most powerful classes - in other words, it's dictated by the class balance we have now. The realities of class balance are meaningless on a sheet of paper, but have everything to do with your ability to impose an effect on the encounters - in other words it's dictated by the game/encounter balance.

So to address the problem of 4000-5000hp tanks+/discipline defensive+complete heal for example, you have mobs that hit for 300-800+ damage. To address the problem of Kunark-style wizard firing squads that kill everything in seconds, you have mobs with 1,000,000 hp and insane resists.

The catch is, there are SO many side effects to these changes, and fixing the side effects would cause more side effects and so on, so Verant basically ignores the side-effects until they get to the point they turn critical. This works against versatile classes because the theory is "well, they can do something else, right? they can still do *something* right?"

Of course, by doing this, the effective, real-world, versatility of classes is reduced until you get to the point where the only thing they have left is the core role, which naturally is always done better by the specialists.

Who can forget the flippant remark "well you can always heal and buff the pets" It says volumes about the road versatility has taken.
