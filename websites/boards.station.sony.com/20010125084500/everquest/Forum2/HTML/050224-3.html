Okay, if you actually get to this point, here is what I would like to see in summons. Obviously you can't have Mages summoning staffs with 5 charges of Ice Comet or weapons that give permanent Haste. But then again it is dissatisfying to be level 51 and be able to summon more of the same "stuff" you were summoning at level 8. (Yes, food, water, bandages, and no weight bag could all be summoned at level 8). So there must be a line drawn somewhere but it can't be too far back.

Some Summons I personally would enjoy seeing (these ideas, most of them, are not my own, just interesting ideas I have seen).

Summon Focus Stone - Summons a No Drop, Lore, Magic stone. To use the stone, hold it in your cursor and click on any spell. The Stone will be given 1 charge of that chosen spell. Alot may say "It has little use if you can't give it to others." and more will say "Way to overpowering" but I think that this is the true meaning of the Magician concept of being able to focus mana into objects. Lets say you put the Spell Gate into the stone. This would allow a quick, clickable way to get out of trouble. Casting times are iffy. The stones may be overpowered if they have no casting times. If casting times were put on all spells within the stone, it would still be useful in those dire situations where you need just 1 more cast but don't quite have enough mana. It would be even better to allow other classes to focus spells into the Mage Focus Stone, but that obviously would never happen, heh.

More Dispell Summoned type weapons. Obviously weapons with Stun, Haste, Fire DDs, Cold DDs, Poison, Disease procs, DoTs (you get the point) would overpower a summoned Sword. But I believe that the Dismiss Summoned on the Sword of Runes is very helpful against what Mages are supposed to be masters against, summoned stuff. Higher level procs in higher level swords would be interesting and useful (sometimes).

Summon GM - Very useful when you're on an island in OoT and get a tell from a GM you petitioned 2 hours ago. (just kidding of course).

Summoned ARMOR series - Any armor, stats don't even have to be incredibly nice (though hopefully better than patchwork). Magic/Lore. Particularly helpful when everyone in your Fear raiding party has lost their corpses. You can suit up your tanks with weapons AND armor. Could make Mages so much more useful on Raids, or at least CRs, heh.

Summon Mob - Summons a single random mob to your side from somewhere in the zone. Would make Mages essential for groups. Or even better, Summon Rare spawn, just stand where the rare spawn pops and cast, voila. 80% spell failure and you summon a Large Rat instead. (a joke)

Summon Mana Stone - "It's back?" No, of course not. The mana stone would be similar to a focus stone, except Mages could "sap" their mana into the stone. Might be an upgrade to the Mod Rod. The mana would be stored in the stone, which could be given to anyone, and once it is activated the mana stored pours out in a 1 to 4 ratio or something.

It would also be interested to see more Mage oriented dungeons. The Hole was interesting, but the bad pathing made pets horrible and a Druid can do just as good damage against a summoned creature as a Mage. Maybe quest spells, that yield special summoning spells. Those spells would then be useful in conquering certain places in dungeons made to be conquered by a multiclassed group (or groups). For example, a zone entirely under lava. A quest mob in the zone (or a rare spawn) will drop a Spell: Summon Lavastone (somethin to that effect, could be a mob anywhere I suppose). As a result, no group would have a chance of raiding the lava zone without a Magician. There are very few if any zones out there that require the abilities of the Mage. A Wizard could do a better job, and you don't have to worry about that pesky pet running into trouble.

Okay, well, there are more ideas for Mages, but alot of them don't deal with Summons. I believe Mages do, however, need a huge revamp on their ability to summon stuff. It is a class defining ability and it seems to fall short of any other class defining abilities (immensely). The problem is that its hard to take a step forward with Mages summons without risk of overpowering. But if we never take that risk, Mages situation will never improve to the norm.

-Esmos Darkmage
