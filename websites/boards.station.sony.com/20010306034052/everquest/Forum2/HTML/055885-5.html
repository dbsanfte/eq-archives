These spells are pretty much required to keep them alive, especially in the post 50 game where you die in seconds.

Magicians, as it is, have the HP of a caster and no way to heal or protect themselves from damage. This accounts for many very quick deaths in 50+ zones, and lots of problems even in level 30 zones. Ever fight 3 level 30 mobs at the same time and simply not be able to do anything but wait for your pet to kill them one at a time? This is more because of our lack of Root, but could also be helped a lot by having damage absobtion.

I like the Armor series that mages get, the regen is nice, but it simply does not compare to the Shieldskin series.

2) Root. Mages do not recieve root in any form. When I was level 10, leveling with my Necro friend, we would talk about how if we recieved root we would be so powerful. Then I understood that a low level mage w/ root could easily kill many mobs with the added time to cast a new pet. We have to revert to our 57 Shock of Steel (825), but most of the time encounters like that are immune to Magic, so we have to revert to our level 52 Char (702).

It would be very nice if Magicians recieved and upgrade to the 52 Char at level 60, even if for the same damage as the Seeking Flame. Having the ability to decide between Bolt or DD would be extremely helpful.

There have been arguments for an Ice line of DD's for mages also, as they are the 'Master of the Elements'.. it would be nice, and helpful, but I'm not going to start hoping just yet .

Summoned line, 52/58 Fire pets, why level 54 spells Voc: Water and Bandoleer of Luclin drop off the 55+ spell table (Juggs etc.), and countless other good ideas listed in this thread.

Keeping in mind this is all about class balance (i.e. not a wish list), here's my, imensely abreviated, list of points:

Increase damage on the anti-summoned range of spells. Not only are summoned type mobs very rare but my 'normal' DD spell of the same (similar) level does more damage, so why would I ever use the anti-summoned spells? Even if you don't include more summoned type mobs at least Mages would then be especially useful against those that do exist.

5) Give mages a (very) limited ability to promote mana recovery. Sorry, can't justify this quickly, going to need to post the full arguemnt:

On reflection the main [but by no means only] advantage of groups is to reduce down-time thus anything I can do to that end is going to render me group friendly. The two main ways to reduce down-time are; to do fast damage (thus shortening the course of the fight), and speeding the recovery of resources (mana & HPs) post combat. Allowing pets to work in all situations achieves the former goal as does targeting mobs by specific damage type (per below) and on the whole Mages are already pretty good at this (with the exception of pet availability restrictions due to pathing). The second technique is where Mages are currently weak, we do little to aid in the recovery of mana (aiding the recovery of HPs is waaaaay beyond of our remit). Mana rods are useful but limited and apart from that we have no ability in this area [that I can think of at the moment anyway]. The first, and biggest, question about this is "should we"!!? This is after all the provenance of Enchanters and it would seriously encroach on their territory for us to have a significant ability in this regard. However this is also an extremely group-friendly ability and an obvious way to encourage groups to take Mages. So to answer my own question, Yes! We should have this ability BUT only in a very limited fashion. Thus we could become Enchanter-lite, never as effective as Enchanters at speeding mana recovery (or Bards for that matter) but better than nothing in the absence of either of these two classes. This achieves two things, it makes Mages significantly more attractive to groups without seriously impinging on another classes ability. There's a really good analogy I like to use in this regard. Druids and Clerics, both heal (i.e. speed the recovery of resources, in this case HPs) but Clerics do it much better than Druids, and the fact that Druids can do it at all increases their attractiveness to groups without seriously undermining that of Clerics. If you need a healer a Cleric's the way to go, but a Druid is definitely better than nothing. Similarly Mages could be 'better-than-nothing' when it comes to speeding mana recovery without ever seriously threatening an Enchanters (and Bards) utility in this role. Thinking about it while several classes can speed HP recovery (Cleric, Druid, Bard, Shaman and even Necro (to a far more limited, i.e. individually, extent only) only Enchanters, Bards and Necros (again to that very limited extent) can speed mana recovery, there's a under-utilised niche' there just waiting to be filled! There's no mana-recovery equivalent of Druids, and Mages fit the bill beautifully. J [Yes, Wizards could claim this also but I see them as a far more focused class (the magic damage equivalent of warriors) to which end they need their ability to do DD improved, not hugely, but definitely improved].

2) Epic: Needing the staff from Innoruuk is insane in its current form. Having to get an item that drops on average 6 times a year is just plain nasty and wrong. 3) Pets: They mana increases of our post 50+ pets does not come anywhere close to the power increase that they receive. Why am I paying double the mana for a pet that is just a couple levels higher and that is basically it?

Also, at the highest levels AE fear is not the worst part of a big game hunt. It is the massive AE's that they cast. Pets have no resistances other than one with fire and one with cold. Pets often last no more than 2 AE's and since many of them are ALSO 100% resistant anything but melee damage and lures, mages are pretty useless. Each pet should have an innate resistance (or at least very high resistance) to something like the water and fire pets. Pets will still die to the massive melee damage but at least they will last awhile first.

It's damage is actually on par with the other pets when it sits there and chain casts, but when it goes to melee (29pts MAX damage) it's damage potential goes WAY WAY down. The fix is simple: up the melee damage.

Lava Bolt - Level 49 - 810 Damage - 300 Mana
Rain of Swords - Level 49 - 3 waves of 324 damage (972 Max) - 375
