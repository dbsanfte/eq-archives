3. level 60 - 300 hitpoint AE lifetap

If the experience cap absolutely will not be removed then here's my 2cp on how to
balance the bard.

1) Loosen up on how bards overall level. The curve is too steep leaving bards everywhere weak upon hitting the ubber levels. Bards should NEVER become as good as any specialist, however, they shouldn't also suffer the indignity of becoming nothing but a mana battery in the end either. Therefore my suggestion to improve bards are thus-wise:
• Eliminate the caps on what rogue skills we have. Allow bards progress as rogues just few levels behind. We only utilize their stealth and lockpick skills anyhow. The truly prized rogue skills are backstab and pick-pockets which we should NEVER get. As it stands there are only three doors in all of Norath which bards can currently open. Stripping the cap and resetting the skill is by far simpler than creating or recalculating a whole lot of doors.
• Remove the warrior disciplines but allow bards to fight using the same general hit/damage tables as warriors. Doing this will give bards the neccessary combative skills to do their jobs without giving away the skills that define and make warriors unique.
• Revamp song stats so that they level better than they currently do. In their current watered down state most song effects are meager. Remember, good bards can only solidly twist three songs at a time. Unlike buffs, when the music stops all benefits end.
• Improve hit points in the same way. Bards needn't be the next in-line to die after the enchanter (enough said on that).
2) Fix the remaining songs needing repairs. Kellin's Lucid Lullaby is an obvious one. In its current watered down state is a suicide song. Agilmente's Aria of Eagles is another song needing repair. The bug fix of Selo'S Song of Travel has crippled both songs. Neither song notifies the recipient that they're about to fall before the levition ends.
3) Add in a create instruments trade skill for us. Why not. The parts are already being sold at the vendors. Let us now make them.
4) Allow the existence of more and well thought out bardic instruments that suit the class. For example:
• a piccolo which would enable the bard to charm like a Pied Piper
• a set of Bag Pipes that would raise the moral of his troops and serve to unerve the enemy
• a lyre that could soothe the savage beast (area effect lull)
• a horn that would summon assistance upon his most dire need
• a metronome to help the avid singer keep pace (lengthen song durations so that a solid fourth can be twisted)
• battle drums that can smite down the enemy when struck (magical instrumental damage)
• bucolic pan pipes that turn friend and foe into happy drunkards (useful when grouped with warriors about to take on casters)
• various instruments that sing a fourth (fifth if he's really good) song into the bard's twist.
• instruments that can function as two of different types (i.e, a chime that doubles as percussion and wind, a balalaika that plays both wind and string, a psaltery can be used for percussion and string, or an oboe that blows as wind and horn)
