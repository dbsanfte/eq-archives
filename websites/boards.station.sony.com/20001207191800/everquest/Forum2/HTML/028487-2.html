Ok, it's not a solution, but I think it's a clear description of the general problem.

Verant predicted that the hybrid classes would be more played because they can do more things (get more “toys” to play with). For example a paladin is a warrior/cleric hybrid. You could play a warrior, but then you wouldn’t get to do cleric things; you could make a cleric, but then you wouldn’t get to do warrior things. From what I’ve read, that’s why hybrids have exp penalties; you get to play 2 classes at once. (Bards are not a mixture of classes per se, but they get a lot of fun toys.)

Why would anyone want to play a warrior, when they can play a warrior and a cleric all at the same time? Well, the experience penalty was added to dissuade people from playing the hybrids. By itself, this makes sense.

However, combine this with the idea that classes need to be balanced, and the argument falls apart IMHO. Hybrids get an ability penalty too; a paladin is not a combination of a warrior and cleric; he is a combination of a weak warrior, and a weak cleric, and can do neither job as well.

Hybrids get a double hit: Experience and ability.

Verant really needs to ask itself this: If there were no hybrid experience penalty, would everyone rush out and make hybrids? I’m sure there would be an increase in them, but, the most “powerful” classes in the game are: Warrior/Cleric/Enchanter for groups, and Necro/Druid for soloing.

Hybrids are hit at higher levels, because the game is tougher at higher levels, and there is less room for unoptimal groups. If the game is balanced, then 2 paladins would be an equal substitute for a warrior and a cleric. Are they?

There is a third 3rd way hybrids are hit along with other classes. It’s generally accepted that a core of a good high level group is a warrior/cleric/enchanter. True, you can get by without them in certain situations, and maybe even do as well in other situations; however, these 3 classes are always desired in a group under *any* circumstance, whereas the remaining classes are desired under *certain* circumstances.

I’d offer this challenge to Verant: Make an area where a Cleric/Enchanter/Warrior group would fail, and a Ranger/Paladin/SK group (or even a Shaman/Rouge/Magician group) would succeed?
