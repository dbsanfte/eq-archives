Well we could be coming at this from a number of directions (what SHOULD wizards be like from a description of their focus in the game manual, what would be COOL for wizards to be like, or what needs to be done to BALANCE them). Given Absor's starting note I'll opt for the third concentration, though I've plenty of ideas on the first two as well.

Ok, in my opinion this is the balance issue with wizards: They have a total devotion to mana for thier abilities. They have no melee, no pet, no neat skills like alchemy. Their mana cannot be used to produce any lasting effects which will affect combat after they run out of mana (I refuse to include the damage shields, they are mostly a joke to me). This causes a problem which I believe is unique to the class where they have a larger period of complete ineffectiveness than any other class while grouping. Wizards are similar to an inverted melee class in many ways, warriors rely on healers for hp, wizards rely on mana regens for mana points. I don't have a problem with that basic concept. However, hp recovery spells are completely effective instantly which allows the melee classes to continue combat without a restorative downtime. I liken harvest to a monk's mend skill, so I have no problem there. The big difference is that mana cannot be reobtained quickly like hp and a lack of mana prevents a wizard from having any effect on the combat. So, in my opinion there are a couple of options:
- Come up with some way that wizards can affect combat while out of mana.
- Come up with some way a wizard can regain mana more quickly (possibly only during a combat situation)
I find it reasonable to believe that, as a class, wizards would have been driven to research ways to conserve or restore their mana supplies, they would have been far more motivated to do so than any other class. So from a game world standpoint I see no problem. For instance, I believe cannibalize makes much more sense as a wizard spell than a shaman spell, I can see wizards viewing mana as supreme and more important than health. However, if the goal is intentionally to make wizards like an inverted melee I can see that won't happen. Looking at the difference between heals and clarity, perhaps instead of giving the wizard the ability to quickly restore his mana, it could be arranged that the spells which other classes get to restore mana would have a bigger effect on wizards than they do on other classes (justifiable for the reason that wizards are so mana-centric that they receive the flow with less lossage than other classes). Although I would be looking more for an increased instant recovery than something like an increased clarity rate. Perhaps cleric spells would heal 75%hp and the wizard would convert 25% of the spell cast on them back into mana? Perhaps wizards could get a spell which, when active on them, reclaimed some of the mana on any friendly spell case at them with the price being a greatly reduced duration or effect?

- The other option is for wizards to be given some things which can affect combat after they run out of mana. Perhaps spells which have a lasting effect on combat (longer rain durations? at higher levels only maybe?) Perhaps adding lasting side effect to some of our DD spells (cold spells reduce dex or slow attack or movement speed, fire spells reduce health regen, lightning spells reduce agility or attack etc.) so that when we are done nuking we know we have debuffed the monster a bit for the rest of the combat? Anything along these lines. I assume things like combat oriented pets are right out, so I'm not exploring those kind of options. Also, anything added to the effects of our DD spells would need to stack with other classes debuff spells (and be weaker than them) so that we aren't stepping on any toes.
- If we can't have either of those then that only leaves increased payback for the spells we get to cast before we run dry. On the issue of us being an inverted melee class (ok, you haven't bought into this I realize, but I really like the idea so let me run with it for a bit) we could be given some mana equivalents of warrior things. When we have < 1 bubble of mana we could go into the mage equiv of berserking (increased int, chance for extra dmage when landing a spell) a real "go out with a bang" sort of thing. We could get the equiv of crits with spells (pretty sure this one has already been floating around). To further steal from other places, maybe we could get focus items like magicians do, but geared towards a particular spell line or damage type. Some other options are spells which do a % of total hp in damage (perfect for those insane hp vellious monosters), a chain damage spell (targets one monster, does damage, 6 seconds later targets a monster near the first and damages it, etc.

Another thing we can target is wizards donations to the group. Wizards have been intentionally crafted as introverts. Heck, even the evil necromancers have waaaay more group friendly spells that wizards. Almost all our buffs are self only and we don't have any group friendly spells like summon corpse or rez. I would argue that, due to the group-centric nature of ever quest, that represents a balance issue. I would like to see us given some group utility outside of evaccing. IN particular I would like to see some things which other classes (like druids) can't already offer. Here are some ideas off the top of my head:
- Give us a spell which moves the targeted person's money into their bank account. Monks will love us.
- Give us higher level versions of our buffs which we can cast on others and which stack with other buffs, but have a short duration maybe 5 to 10 minutes.
- Give wizards a second bind point and a second gate spell. This lets us make round trips between home and dungeon. Very powerful, but very group friendly, AND completely in line with our purported strengths.
- Give wizards the ability to add damage procs to group mates weapons.
- Give wizards the ability to shadow step others (aggro NPCs and PC on pvp, NOT non-aggro NPCs or regular PCs)
- Give us the ability to rclick any scroll we could scribe and cast the spell of the scroll with no mana cost.
- Give us the ability to "scry" locations. Cast once to set up an effect on you with the loc encoded into it. Cast again to see that loc. Click away the effect and recast to set a new loc.
- Give us the ability to teleport someone to the same place as a selected group mate from anywhere in Norrath. (Bascially I believe that where clerics reduce xp down time, wizards should be given the supreme reduce travel time spells).
- Give us self only gates to all major cities. I don't understand why we have been so limited in our self only travel abilities, this is our only real strength besides damage, we should absolutely excel at it.

Also, one final thing. If it is such a wonderful idea to have portals which can get you killed, then give a few to the druids as well, and make sure they are the only ports they get in the general vicinity, so they either use them and risk death, or they walk. Oh, and take away their SoW too. Ok, /rant. Look I really do not like the idea of portals which we can't really use at the levels we get them. We get far too few for that sort of thing. Not only are the danger gates largely un-useable (and unreplaceable, there is no other nearby gate we can choose from), but there are gates like the WK, NK ones which practically overlap. I really think that you guys need to either lighten up and give us more ports OR make the ones we have useable and safe. This danger port thing may make for an interesting concept, but PLEASE try it out on someone else. We don't even get any movement spells like SoW. I think we wizards pay FAR too much in lack of diversity for it to be fair for us to ALSO pay in potential xp loss for using one of our only two strong areas. It would be like giving warriors only 12 or so sets of armor, and making 3 of them kill you some of the time when you put them on. Oh, kill you AND move your corpse someplace dangerous for you to retrieve it. Anyway, I think I've made my point, please fix the ports.

Well those are my thoughts, hope they help you guys decide what to do :-)

Ok, another try at this thread.

Taunt and damage: Several people have asked why a significant upgrade in damage would also increase the taunt factor of our spells. Well guys, if I go bear hunting with a 22 rifle and an AK-47, do you really think that the bear is going care which gun I use. I mean, it's going to just be an instinctive reaction by the bear to run over and maul me to death. The only difference that I see is that I might have a slim chance at surviving the encounter with the AK-47. So my question is why would any spell have a taunt factor greater than ice comet? Believe me when I say ice comet has enough taunts to go around.

Mana and regen ability: If this was an actual role playing game, wouldn't you see huge massive characters as warriors or strong agile characters as monks or the stealthy ones as rogues?
