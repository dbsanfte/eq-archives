-Idea for how to improve Taunt-

...quickly and easily.

Currently, when taunt succeeds, it puts you just above the former #1 guy on the hate list. How much higher isn't known but I suspect it's just +1. When it fails, your position on the hate list remains unchanged. The problem with being placed +1 above the former #1 spot is that if someone is even slightly outdamaging you AND is attacking just as quickly, they immediately re-take the #1 spot and there's nothing you can do about it till Taunt recycles.

To improve it quickly and with a minimum of effort, do this:

1) Make a successful taunt put you 2 hate points per point of Taunt skill above the former #1 spot. This means, when you max Taunt at 200, a successful taunt places you 400 points above the other guy. Kendrick (a Verant guy) said melee hits can range from 50-500 points of hate, and Jolt is -500 hate points, so 400 seems an appropriate amount.

2) On an unsuccessful taunt, add 1 hate point per point of Taunt skill to your current total, but not to exceed the #1 spot's amount, if you aren't that person.

These two changes would have the following effects:

-All classes with Taunt would improve, but warriors would improve the most due to our higher Taunt caps.

-Untauntable ubermobs would still be untauntable, as intended.

-Even if someone is outdamaging you AND attacking as quickly (i.e. pretty much any monk, ranger, or rogue) a successful taunt can keep you above them for at least a little while. It's still not a free aggro control button.

-A failed taunt is no longer completely useless, and, by itself, cannot place you on top of the hate list.

Aside from purely untauntable mobs (either because they are ubermobs or because they are higher level than you, post 50), the worst taunting situation to get into is someone who is attacking just as quickly AND is outdamaging you. Rogues (when they fail to Evade successfully), nearly all post-50 monks, and (if the test changes happen) rangers all cause this situation.

You gave rogues the ability to Evade, which is good, but I'm very concerned that the upcoming changes will cause problems with aggro-management. Rangers can Jolt, but only at 55 and it has resist problems. Some possible changes on test may address this. Monks can FD but in my experience, any monk who needs to FD to "let" me tank will regain aggro almost instantly. Besides, is FD during combat to reduce aggro really its intended use? Seems pretty silly to me.

To use myself as an example, I'm wielding a 9/19 weapon and an 11/22 weapon and I wear a runebranded girdle for haste. Just how much better do I need to go to maintain aggro, and why am I forced into using the fastest weapons I can obtain in order to just plain get hit during combat? Why even make twohanded warrior weapons when they reduce us to a non-tanking DoT?

Good list, Absor, thanks for doing this.

I do have a few comments on some of them, though.

AoE TAUNT: I like this idea. This sounds like a fun new ability that, as Absor said, has its own drawbacks built right in.

INCREASED HP:
While I don't believe that the defensive gap between hybrids and warriors should be closed, I don't believe it should be widened either.

TWO HANDED WEAPONS AND TAUNT: This one could very well end up being a nerf in disguise. Currently, warriors have the worst taunting ability of the 3 heavy tank classes, because taunt+spell+melle (hybrid) is a MUCH better taunt than taunt+melee (warrior). This is already a serious class balance problem post 50 (IMHO). The only reason this problem is not worse than it already is, is because dual wielding taunts better than wielding a 2-hander. If you change it so that 2-handers taunt as well as dual wielding, then warriors will fall even farther behind hybrids at taunting. Be careful what you ask for, my warrior brothers. You might get it. PLEASE DO NOT implement this one.

FURIOUS DISCIPLINE:
This discipline is not very useful anyway, so I never noticed this.

COBALT HELM AND BRACER:
Drop rate on the bracer is just SLIGHTLY too low IMHO, but the drop rate on the helm is obscenely low. The problem here is that the areas where these items drop is not an area that people really do as a one-group camp. These areas are usually done as part of a multi-group raid, often just in passing on the way to Trakanon. Hence, the number of reet frogs killed in a raid is usually very low. I'm glad to see this one on the list.

CRITICAL HITS FOR LOW DAMAGE:
I really don't see a need to change this one. Critical hits are basically eye-candy only (although I like them for that reason). It never bothered me personally to see critical hits for 30 damage. That just meant to me that I got an extra 15 damage that time.

BETTER TAUNT:
This is the big one to me. If you guys at Verant don't implement anything else on this list, PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE implement this one. Taunting is 50% of the job of tanking, so it really isn't a good thing for warriors to be vastly inferior to paladins and shadowknights at taunting. Slightly better ability at taking damage + dramatically INFERIOR ability at taunting = inferior tank. Despite higher taunt skill caps, warriors are inferior taunters because some of the hybrid spells generate a *LOT* of hate, and they generate this high amount of hate EVERY time they are cast, even if the spell is resisted. Procing weapons do not help the warrior very much with this, because the proc is random, and will rarely happen just at the moment needed to save a dying party member.

Level 50+ mobs are much harder to taunt with the taunt button, and this DRASTICALLY increases the importance of taunting with spells, which warriors can't do.

I suggest replacing the level 53 warrior discipline (charge) with a new discipline that would have a 15 or 30 minute re-use time, and would taunt just like a spell does: by simply generating a large amount of hate that would cause the mob to turn on the warrior 90% of the time, and stay on the warrior for a while after that (i.e. don't just hit the warrior once or twice and then go right back to killing the monk).

BERZERKER FRENZY AS A SKILL:
This could be good, or it could end up being a nerf. If you replace any of the disciplines with this new skill, then it WILL BE A NERF. The level 54 warrior discipline turns every hit into a critical hit or crippling blow for a few seconds. This does not add all that much to the warrior damage output, unless the warrior uses it when he is berzerk. If you replace a discipline with "berzerking", then berzerk will no longer stack with the 54 discipline, which will make the level 54 discipline nearly useless. (Useless is a strong word, I know, but the amount of damage it adds would almost never be worth the very long re-use time of this discipline, unless it can be used to turn every hit into a crippling blow. I rarely use this discipline even now. If I can't use it to turn every hit into a crippling blow, then I would *NEVER* use it.)

Again, warriors, be careful what you ask for. You might get it.

RESPECT FROM GREENIES:
I wouldn't really oppose changes here, but this isn't really that important to me. Being the only class with no way to protect myself from hoards of greenies is a price that I have consciously paid for the advantages of the warrior class. It's a big price, but it's one that I knowingly paid when I created my character.

IMHO
====

ITEM: Better Taunt.
DESCRIPTION: Warriors want to be able to taunt more effectively at the higher levels. It may be, though, that the problem isn't with their ability to taunt, but with the taunting of other classes. Also, players are not supposed to be able to dictate the actions of higher level monsters. They are supposed to be smart enough to kill who they think needs killing, and they are not supposed to be tricked by simple name calling… But this should be looked at along with the big picture, perhaps warriors should have a greater ability to taunt.

If the Vision[tm] defines Warriors as 'meat shields' and high level mobs are smart enough to attack other players because of this, then something is REALLY wrong. 'Hi, I'm a meat shield, that's what I'm designed for, but mobs won't attack me so I mill around pointlessly.'

ITEM: AoE Taunt.
DESCRIPTION: The ability to 'taunt' all of the creatures in your combat range. This would have to be a high level ability, most likely a discipline.

This is, IMO, a stupid idea. Based on the Vision[tm] of taunting and mob-intelligence, AoE taunt makes zero sense. Plus, unless taunt OVERALL gets fixed, who cares. It won't affect anything at all.

ITEM: Two-Handed weapons and taunt.
DESCRIPTION: The consensus is that slower weapons taunt less, no matter the damage level generated. If this is the case, perhaps finding some way to make these weapons taunt more would make them more popular.

Fixing OVERALL warrior taunt will fix this.

ITEM: Increased Hit Points:
DESCRIPTION: Some warriors feel that their hit points are too low, at least at the high levels.

Not a bad idea, if not taken to extremes.

ITEM: Berserker Frenzy as a skill.
DESCRIPTION: This has the potential to be significantly overpowering, but it's certainly interesting. The idea is to turn berserking into a skill or discipline with a recycle time and a duration that balances out the fact that they can do this at will. This might also mean the removal of the berserk code for low health. Perhaps the berserk skill would drain stamina and then, when stamina was gone, hit points.

Bad, bad idea. It's not broken, it's effective and interesting as is. Please don't change it.

ITEM: Respect.
DESCRIPTION: The idea here would be that a deadly warrior shouldn't be jumped on by little green beetles when wandering back to his corpse. They should fear his dangerous fighting power. The gameplay point here is that warriors have no way of travelling safely among otherwise annoying greens. Where a caster would go invisible, a warrior has to fight off hordes of weak enemies.

Amen. Make green cons indifferent until player-aggro'ed, at which point they can all gang on the foolish warrior.

ITEM: Equiping shields on the back.
DESCRIPTION: Allowing shields to be used in back slots. A popular idea, and it makes some sense, assuming the shield only gave an armor class bonus from the back.

A very interesting idea, but one that's probably impossible to justify as a class-specific idea, which sucks since sooo many shields will be overbalanced on the backs of many rangers/rogues/paladins/SK's.

SUMMARY:

- Fix taunt
- Add 'Respect'
- Add a few HP's
- Shields on back (if workable)

*** A final note: My biggest beef with the hybrid/warrior issue is this. Both expansion and many patches have introduced many new spells and disciplines for the hybrids, while the warriors got (comparably) little. Is it really unreasonable to throw in _something_ for the Warrior class? I think I speak for all warriors when I say 'Oh Gee, look new ranger/paladin/SK spells.'

Thanks for reading this
