My monk is only 41, but I have friends who are high level monks, and I have discussed issues with them. Most of the suggestions in this thread are geared to 50+ monks, so let me add some things that I would like to see for levels below 50.

1 - Change the stats on the headbands/sashes. They are worthless statwise, even the most difficult to obtain ones. I mean, have you ever seen a level 30 monk wearing their purple headband or red sash? No. How about increasing the stats a little more as each quest progresses, and add an effect on the high end ones. Like make the red sash have a 3-4% haste element. This is hardly overpowering, but is a nice monk-type reward for completing the quests to get this sash. Adding an ultravision or see invis effect to the purple headband would be a nice bonus as well.

2 - as someone earlier suggested, allow monks to quest for the black headband and black/purple sashes. Make it a high level quest (like over level 45) and give the headband/sash nice stats that are commensurate with that level. I would suggest making the quests over 50, but these are on the old continent and doing that would exclude non-Kunark owners. Or make it so level 50s could do it - doesn't matter. Just make these items questable so the monk has a status symbol they can obtain from their guild.

3 - add a minimum damage to flying kick. Perhaps leave it as is from 30 to 40. At 40, minimum becomes level/3 and over 50, it becomes level/2. Add a small chance for a critical kick (less than 5% maybe) post 50. Critical kick does double normal damage.

4 - add a chance for dragon punch and/or flying kick to stun, even if just for a half second - should be enough to interrupt casters.

5 - Quests. Several good suggestions in this thread for quests, but I want to focus on two ideas. First, make armor that fits between Crescent and Pantherskin statwise, but have it questable for monks in their mid 30s to low 40s. Second, a quest for a 100% weight reduction bag that is monk only (NODROP) and all the components are NODROP would be wonderful. Monks are the only class in the game with the weight restriction, yet the best weight reducer in the game is 5250pp, best price. Monks cannot loot because of the weight of coins and items, so most monks that are not twinked do not get a tinkers bag until their 40s. Why not add a quest that monks could do in late 30s, early 40s for a bag that is the equivalent of the tinkers bag. This would be a reward for struggling with the weight restriction all of our lives.

6 - add a hide skill to go with our sneak skill. Sneak is maxed at 113, so why not give the monk the ability to learn the Hide skill, which also caps at 113. The two can be used in conjunction to make the monk invisible to most mobs, but not to undead. And the ability can not be used to evade mobs that have the monk on their hate lists like Rogues can. Rogues still have the ability to move at full speed post 50, monks would not get this either.

7 - up the damage per fist post 50, or add a damage bonus to the offhand post 50, similar to the primary hand bonus below 50.

None of these suggestions would be unbalancing to other classes, but would make the monk a more rounded character and with the slight damage changes we would be a better addition as a damage dealer to high end groups (which is our job after all =)

I believe that monks are meant to be masters of their own body and mind. They master themselves to manipulate people, monsters, and the environment around them. This personal mastery and discipline is also what gives them the intense melee capabilities that they have. All of these things contribute to making the monk class enjoyable.

Some fun enhancements to the monk class could be focused around the ability to protect and cure the body. This is similar to mend works now. First, monks could consume a greatly reduced amount of food and water or perhaps go for days without food and water before being “hungry and thirsty”. Secondly, higher resistances to disease, poison, and perhaps harm touch make sense or maybe this fluctuates with other conditions. Third, a small enhancement to the mend ability at high levels which would allow pressing mend to not only heal the hp, but cure anything afflicting the monk at that time, like poison, blindness, disease, etc. Finally, it would be nice to re-examine the weight limit for monks. Perhaps if this was based on a percentage of strength instead of a fixed number, it would be a greater challenge and more flexible for adding equipment. If a monk were to chose to add AC items only, they would be more limited to weight than if there was a mix of ac and strength. Maybe the limit would be tied to agility too since that is what a monk relies on for his abilities. Basically, monks’ options would be broadened if he focused on building a well-rounded character and tightened if focused on raw AC, which would thus limit equipment choices.

Another area that could be enhanced to make the class more enjoyable to play is focused around attack. There are two contradictions in logic with design of the monk class for attack and damage. First, they are made masters of attack through increased skill levels in weapon and hand-to-hand melee, however, this awesome skill level never allows for critical hits. I would think that a monk would have the ability to score a critical punch, crush, or kick and effectively stun a target for a random duration of time after their skill level reaches its max. Secondly, there needs to be a certain level of skill when a monk simply cannot perform a flying kick and deal out 3 damage. This ability was learned in the low 20’s and by level 60, the monk should get a minimum damage of his level or some other calculation base on his skill. Finally, also in regards to special attacks, I would like to see the monk “internal haste” also apply to his special attacks. Again, at level 60 and max skill in flying kick, I simply cannot do it any better or more often than I could when I learned it.

All of the changes I have mentioned, to me, are focused around making the class more enjoyable to play. Some are focused around simple enhancements that make you think about choices you make. Some are focused around reward for hard work, like doing 60 damage versus 3 on a level 60 flying kick. I have enjoyed the monk class for the most part, but have also become extremely frustrated at times when I max every skill and every quest and yet I struggle to raise my key stats to acceptable levels or I perform a “Thunderous Kick” for 2 damage and listen to the giggles.
