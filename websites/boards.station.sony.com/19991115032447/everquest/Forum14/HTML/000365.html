I play an 18th lvl shaman. I've tried kiting (blue griff) and ended up running for the guards. I haven't played since the patch but it looks just fine to me. I solo a fair bit, group a fair bit, and belong to a guild so I'm looking at this from most points of view other than as a kiter.

When I group, I often volunteer to be the lure. I can re-SOW myself if I need and I have armor enough to handle myself. I use a DOT to get the mob's attention then lead it back to my group. As long as it doesn't take me more than 18 seconds to get back to my group, the DOT does as much or more damage than before. Even if it does take me more than 18 seconds, the mob comes back already damaged...most people I've grouped with consider that to be a GOOD THING [tm]. And that way, the DOT has longer to work even if we kill the mob before the spell expires.

When I solo, I do much the same except instead of leading the mob to my group I lead it to my chosen battleground. Again, it's generally less than 18 seconds so the spell will be doing as much or more damage as pre-patch. If I have enough of a lead I'll root it then stack another DOT then hit it with some debuffs and/or DD's, and finally, hopefully, melee it to death.

I don't see anything in the DOT change that will hurt this kind of style of play. The only style it will really hurt is the hard-core kiters. The ones that rely on DOT for doing most or all of the damage to the mob.

As for how does a druid fight? We have a couple in my guild and I've been fortunate to find a few that I've teamed up with. Druids and shamans are great in a fight as long as you realize a few important things; you have mana AND hitpoints, use 'em both. You have healing spells so you can recover lost hitpoints after a battle much, much, much more quickly than any tank. You have DD and DOT and buff/debuff and shielding spells.

If you've been so one-dimensional with your character as to think that a change to make kiting less effective has destroyed your character then I feel for you. Just don't lump us shamans in with you...we'll continue to play and have fun.

Alise
