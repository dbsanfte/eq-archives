Alright, I was going to throw my own sarcastic reply into the mix as well, but it seems there's plenty of sarcasm already, and Absor asked us to be nice. Won't hurt, so here goes.

I disagree with your opinion of giving teleports to the Shaman class.

You said: "My main char when I played was a druid, and I always looked upon shamans as my brothers of sorts. They, like druids, were very in tune with nature, and we shared some important abilites." This is true. Secondary healing, Wisdom-based Classes, the nature-closeness thing, Spirit of Wolf, similar spell lines, Bears and Wolves (whether forms or pets)... both the Shaman and Druid classes have these. However, if you were to give the Shaman class teleports, you'd be adding a grey area. Right now, someone (anyone) can look at the fourteen (minor correction: You had said sixteen) classes and notice a distinct separation between all of them. Monks and Warriors are similar. They're not the same though. They share some skills, but not all. Same with the Druids and Shamen (Shamans? Shaman? I don't know the plural): if they get too many similarities, people will start going "Druid, Shaman... same thing." And in effect, EverQuest will have lost a class. Not good.

Sure, teleporting would make Shamen (I've just decided to use that one) happy. There are plenty of things that would please Shamen, but far fewer to balance and to fix them. Please, let's leave the proposed additions to Shamen to Absor's Talk Sessions about the classes.

Certainly travel is tedious for the classes that cannot teleport. I imagine it gets that way for the folks that CAN teleport, too (coughgettingtoQeynoscough) sometimes. Really, though, another teleporting class isn't the answer. Do what I do: Stop worrying about the time needed to travel. Don't min-max your playing time with "Must level must level must LEVEL!" frenzy. Chalk some of it up to sightseeing. Look around. See if any of the zone's rare spawns are up. Run in dread from that big ol' cyclops. Oh wait... that last one is just for me, because I'm a level 24 Ranger. They'd use me as a pine-scented toothpick. Either way, don't look at travel as a chore (though it sometimes is. Corpse recovery comes to mind). Think of it as played time. Time playing a game.

Well, there it was. My attempt at a non-sarcastic, conversational, and hopefully informative reply to an issue I felt needed addressing.

Origin and plural of the word "Shaman"
In short: the "-man" part has nothing to do with the english "man". Plural of shaman is shamans.

From Merriam-Webster:
Main Entry: sha·man
Pronunciation: 'shä-m&n, 'shA- also sh&-'män
Function: noun
Inflected Form(s): plural shamans
Etymology: ultimately from Evenki (Tungusic language of Siberia) samAn
Date: 1698

From ENCYCLOPÆDIA BRITANNICA
shaman (from Tunguso-Manchurian saman, "he who knows")
