I thought of something to balance the RACES within the Paladin classes this morning!

We need balance between the Paladins/Clerics within the Paladin/Cleric classes themselves. Dwarfs get the best of both worlds, High Melee stats AND High Wisdom. What is the point of playing an Erudite Paladin/Cleric when a Dwarf has the exact same WIS and better melee stats then you? That is right, there is none.

CHA, even though highlighted as a MAJOR statistic in character generation, means NOTHING to paladins in terms of game play. This is where the Dwarfs succeed in being uber, because the only drawback to their race is low charisma (none of them are INT casters, so INT doesn't matter, it doesn't matter to melee classes anyways).

I propose that the amount of healing bonus on healing spells be increased by 5% for every 10 CHA above 50 CHA, of course stacking with the already 15% bonus of clerics. ERU and HIE pay for low melee stats by receiving reasonable WIS and high CHA, yet the dwarfs come along with about the same WIS and the CHA doesn't matter. Once changed, there will be more "balance" internally between the races of the Paladin and Cleric classes. No longer will you be "stupid" or "a moron" to choose a race other than Dwarf for a paladin. This will also help Gnome Clerics out, because they have more CHA than Dwarves.

Realizing that Paladin's have a mana-penalty already, which limits their amount of casting, and lower level spells. This could fix the problem of paladins complaining about their heals being too *weak*, yet not changing the spell lines.

This would also ensure that even if Druids/Shamen have the same healing spells as a Cleric, the cleric will be the obviously better healer!

Also, this would make CHA a stat that people might think of increasing, instead of just going for all out WIS as a Cleric. Then even low WIS races can compete with Higher WIS races.

Using base stats, this would yield cumulative healing bonus of:

Dwarf Cleric - 15% above normal healing
Dwarf Paladin - 2.5% above normal healing

Erudite Cleric - 25% above normal healing
Erudite Paladin - 15% above normal healing

Gnome Cleric - 20% above normal healing

Half-Elf Paladin - 17.5% above normal healing

Halfling Cleric - 15% above normal healing

High-Elf Cleric - 30% above normal healing
High-Elf Paladin - 20% above normal healing

Human Cleric - 27.5% above normal healing
Human Paladin - 17.5% above normal healing


I wish this game had more choices in it, besides which race you chose at the beginnning and what stats you chose at the beginning. Maybe a Cleric wants to be a better healer, at the expense of a large mana pool. The Cleric would Sacrifice WIS for CHA. Maybe a Paladin wants to focus more on their healing nature than their melee nature. They would focus on WIS and CHA, rather than AC/STR/STA. Why not put more measures in the game where the players can "bend" their class toward a different focal point instead of all the characters ending up the same.

1. Raise the defensive skills. People have stated that a Paladin is 3/4 warrior, 1/4 cleric, and I think we pretty much all agree on that. If we raise the defensive skills (including Parry/Riposte and the like) we will be equal in defensive ability, while having less AC due to equipment. Since it's pretty obvious we dont do much damage, this would fulfill the 3/4 warrior. (same defense and about half as much damage)

2. Defensive Disc. While LOH is stellar for big group situations, we never use it on ourselves, we use it when the cleric cant cast his Cheal quickly enough to heal the warrior. This effectively negates the advantage we have for keeping ourselves alive. If we had a big raid /disc to replace Holyforge, this would go a long way to making Paladins viable large raid members.

3. Give us an effective heal other than LOH before Level 57 (i.e. move sup heal to 53-55) By doing this, we can actually keep other people alive in group situations (Gheal heals about half a bub of life for quite a few warriors from what I've seen).

I'm sure there are more changes that could be made to balance the classes out, but I feel these are the most effective.
