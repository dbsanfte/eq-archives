I only have a low level (18) wizard but here are the main flaws I see in the wizard class from my low level perspective, looking at the list of spells and listening to friends who play wizards. These are, for the most part, listed in the order of their importance to the wizard class.

Portals/Evacs: Portals like cazic have dangerous entry points, portals have bad locations, portals to decent locations come at too high levels. I can see an argument for having a dangerous portal but wizards are the least defensive class in the game so giving them a dangerous portal is just plain mean. Compare wizard portals to druid portals and you see quite clearly that wizards get the short end of the stick here, considering that druids get SoW, SoC, and other speed enhancements to make them easily a better travel class I'd have to argue that wizards should get a better portal spell makeup, at earlier levels. This should include West Commons in their first ring of portals (self, and group) a safer location for the south part of the old world than cazic, better relocs in Kunark, possibly a location closer to Qeynos/Surefall/Halas to make up for shafting them for so long in this area. If you want to put in dangerous portals, give some to druids to offsent their ability to travel the world relativly freely compared to any other class. Wizards seem to get shafted at every turn, even the Dreadlands portal is just further away from anything than the druid portal.

Damage spells aka DDs aka nukes: Wizards should be A-Number One, prime damage dealers at all levels PERIOD. There should be no level range where a magician, who also has a pet, can do more damage in a shorter time frame, for less mana than a wizard. I see no need to justify this, it should just be done.

Utility spells: I've always wondered why O'Kiels Radiation was just one spell and never got any upgrades, a line of damage shields (target only and later group) would go a long way in helping the wizard class. A few more of the group line of resistance buffs (endure fire/cold/poison/disease) look at the relative level of levitate/invis/see invis/endure breath.

Fade Line/Root Line: These need to be re-evaluated for all classes, along with the snare line, as everyone I have ever talked to says the higher level versions are just less effective, more mana intensive, more resistable versions of the lower level spells.

Bind Sight Line: Been said but there needs to be some way to flag the client for more location updates of the target which the caster has bound sight to. This is a bug for wizards, along with every other class who gets this spell.

Worthless AE spells: Centered around caster AEs are rarely used by a wizard (except to farm green dungeons) since the mission of a wizard in a group is to do the most damage with the least aggro and most wizards try to be as far from melee range as possible. I have also seen bugs with the Area Effect spells centered around target where they just don't seem to go off.

Bolts: These can't be counted when you evaluate how much damage a wizard does because they are as unreliable as a bow and arrow. I've seen players, little hills, edge of inclines, foraged food dropped on the ground hinder the path of a bolt (arrows and thrown weapons seem to suffer the same issues.) Yes I understand there should be a penalty for having a more powerful spell than an appropriate level direct damage spell but the acceptable pathing of these needs to be re-evaluated.

Conjuration: OK so it's a rarely used skill by wizards but how about giving them something that costs less mana than eye of zomm to practice with.

In general there are few 'balance' issues in this thread. There are several problems, but things (even seemingly important ones, like the whole Admiral Akbar line of spells) aren't balance issues. And I want to look at the balance issues first.

A bazillion hit points: The biggest balance issue that I see is the problem that we've given the purely mana dependent wizard in Velious. Once the mana is gone and the enemy is only partially wounded, the wizard is no longer contributing to the fight (except that he is meditating in preparation for helping out later). I see this as the single most important issue facing the wizard class as a whole. And, as I'm discovering with most classes, it's mostly a post 50th level issue.

I've seen two basic approaches to a solution to this problem. Unfortunately, both are very dramatic (though it may require a dramatic change to rectify this issue). One is simply to increase the wizard's mana reserve, the other is to create a new line of spells that does a percentage of damage based on the current hit points of the target. Honestly, I don't like either one. Raising the mana pool would be a terrific balancing problem. This not only increases the potential damage output of the wizard while he's fighting the bazillion hit point enemy, but at all other times as well. I can't really see this working.

I admit that the % damage spell idea is interesting. And it does target the problem a lot more specifically. This also seems like an idea that could cause troublesome balancing issues, but far fewer than the mana pool increase. It would require a ton of work, assuming that this sort of spell is even possible

There is a third idea that I have, but I can't tell you about because it is based on things that you folks don't get to know (sorry). But it is not too far from the percentage damage idea anyhow.

Regardless, this is the main concern that I have about balancing the wizard class. And it will probably remain at the top of the wizard list.

The other more general issue is about the whole level limit cap on wizard (and everyone else's for that matter) damage spells. I can see how this would impact a wizard more than others, since they have little to do when they can't blast things. I'm not sure if this should be changed, but I'll bring it up.

Wizards talk too much I've been told this by many people, so I'll be brief.

The problems in the wizard class are twofold:

a) the problems of EQ balance in general are that melee is greater than nuking in almost every circumstance. Who else to show it better than the pure spell-damage class? Why else are enchanters and clerics so important? (to make it a one mob world for the meleers to drop the mobs one by one)

b) the other problem with EQ in general is the downtime. The ports don't help with downtime when dragons and green aggro mobs camp the zone in points for 1/2 of our new world ports. I like cazic port being dangerous-- but only because South Ro was an option. There are no options for Cobalt Scar or Wakening Lands or Skyfire. Except to run.

That's it-- solve EQ's magic vs. melee question, and you will have mostly fixed the wizards. Enable wizard and druid ports to be more numerous and more safe and you will balance class desirability in EQ more. (who cares about being able to port when you have to run from Dreadlands or Iceclad anyway?)

Everything else after that is just gravy (spell effects, faster mana, bigger damage, pets on rain spells)... you screwed up when you made the melee classes get better and better weapons/armor, and upped mob hitpoints and damage output to compensate-- and didn't consider how easy it is (with aggro coding and limited mana pool) to toss ALL MAGIC except that which supports melee (speed, heals, damage shields, buffs) into a second-class (non-essential) way of dealing damage.

I wish I had some quick fix suggestions-- but I honestly don't believe there are any. I commend you for trying, however-- good luck!

Ok this is not my own ideas but some posted on Everlore. Hope you at least look at them cause some of are good ideas...

You know, I have heard a lot of good ideas for balancing the wizard class out a bit. I'm a die-hard wizzy lover, and while it pains me to point this out, wiz's suffer some major problems at critical levels. Against super nasty 100K hp mobs in velious, for example, we can not do too much other than blow off perhaps 5 or 6 K of their hp, if that. Then its back to the books, provided the mob doesn't eat us before then.
The ability to use get less resists is always good, and thats before the lure line of spells. But as far as a complete answer, it falls short of that mark. Heres some ideas I've heard and/or come up with.

A) Make Harvest an ability, like Harm Touch or Lay of Hands, that can be practiced at level 34. As your skill in it rises, so does the amount of mana you regain per use, say, perhaps 11 extra mana per level, with 225 mana starting off at 34. Doing the math, that means about 511 mana back per use at level 60. Also cut down the delay from the original time by a quarter. This means more nukes from us, and makes us look like heroes for throwing down that extra ice comet and a lesser spell while everyone else is oom. AND it frees up a spell slot.
B) Critical hits, but spell wise. This lets us do more than the allowed max damage for the spell occasionally, by perhaps 1/3 more the intended damage.
C) I like this one I've heard. Make a spell for the wiz that allows them to power someones weapon by casting the spell on that person to do additional damage against certain mobs, (i.e. extra damage againt frost giants because a weapon has a fire-based enchantment on it.). Make it group only, and the chances of a wiz getting a velious group just went up drastically.
D) This ones sure to make some people scream about class unbalancing, but here it is: Give wiz's a higher skill cap in medding. At higher levels, a mage, enc, or nec has their pet that can keep contributing to the death of a mob, even while the caster goes oom. A wiz does not. So if they get a higher level cap, or at least a faster med time, (like 8 to 10 mana per click as opposed to the 6 it is. I think...), and with the harvest ability, they can contribute more and more to a group than they could ever before.
E) Just make wiz's spells more powerful by an additional 10%. At least this way our nukes aren't doing marginally more than the mages and druids, and at higher levels, makes up a larger force to recon with. And again, to wiz's, mana and nukes are everything. No pets, DoT's, mez's, Feign Death, hp, ac, buffs, LoH, HT, nothing. Just powerful nukes. Improving on that one area slightly isn't asking a whole lot. If you have a 500 dd, make it do 550. 50 more damage can be a lot in the right situations, and again points to our ability to burn a mob down faster, our only innate ability other than porting.
F) I think that group port spells should start at 24, and not 29. Druids can port also, but wiz's are the masters of the planes and can port anywhere druids can, so why not give them a 5 level advantage? After all, wiz's can port, but druids can port and travel in zone faster, (sow and group wolf form). So its not all that much an unbalance.

Give wiz's a Fire based or Ice based DoT, as it makes sense if you get burned it going to keep hurting, or if you get your arm frozen solid it'll get worse over time. Yes, wiz's are DD masters, not DoT. But if it was a minor one, like comparing the necro 500dd to their regular DoT's, no one would complain. And with that said, think that you'll STILL be hurting the mob even though your face is in your spell book, or sitting there, level dependent.
