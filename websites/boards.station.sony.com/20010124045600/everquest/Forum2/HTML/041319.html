(Disclaimer: I'm a french player, please excuse my english)
The enchanter has been my primary and favorite class since day one. I've played EQ since his launch and I am now after all this time a level 42 enchantress. I enjoy playing EQ every day and it's surely the best game I have ever played. My post is not to tell you how weak my class is, or how you should nerf another one. I know very well how powerful enchanters are. It's not a post about power. I like our mind controler aspect. I have one of the best group in Norrath (having a well trained group is a requisite for an enchanter) and have done the hardest dungeons at the right levels everywhere on Odus, Antonica, Faydwer and Kunark (not Velious since I'm a euro player ). My point here is: I really love this game, and would never have had the idea of writing this post if you hadn't extinguish lately with Velious my latest hopes concerning one important part of my enchanter's enjoyment : illusions. Sure there has been some nerfs in the past but nothing that was enough disturbing to force me to take my computer and write a letter.

To be totally honest I have not chosen the enchanter class for the illusions. It was a nice bonus...no it was a VERY nice bonus to my gaming enjoyment. I'm more the roleplayer type, and being able to change form in a game where customization of my character is not the major feature was a blast. A breeze of fresh air in all the fights in dungeons .
However I think you have been very unfair to us in your decisions on the subject of illusions so far. And lately you have crossed the line for me.

Let me first summarize the history of illusions for enchanters. Please note how things have happened and the feeling the average EQ enchanter player can have in front of Verant attitude.

Like many enchanters I was surprised to discover that we do not have illusions past the level 44 (and only one at 44). When enchanters asked for finishing the spell line we were told that there was technical issues that stopped you adding new illusions in the game: The character global files (a name like that), which contains all the models that should appear in all zones (players and pets) had a size limited by the old 3D cards. EQ had to be compatible for people with those devices.
Fine. Something I can fully understand.

Enchanters have then proposed to Verant many ideas to go around this limitation. For example: illusion gender, illusion copycat : copy living creature (like minor illusion with living things), illusion animation (our pet). I dont know why they were discarded. For me it was a positive thing that players suggest ideas to you, and ideas that can work and are not unbalancing in any way.

With the first expansion (Kunark) we were all waiting for the new illusions. Post 50 spells for enchanters meant some illusions for us ? Nothing. Except illusion iksar. What a surprise to discover that there was indeed some room inside this global file. Ok, you were keeping the room for the new iksar race. Again I can understand, except I do not like very much the fact that you didnt tell us the truth at first.

The first disatisfaction for me came with illusion spectre given to necros. After telling us there was no room for a new model, you change your mind... and obviously not for the one that were asking for it. Let me tell you I felt bad : a pet was most important in your eyes than a player. Ok, ok, I know there is a player behind every pet, but that's the way i felt at this time. Again, I can understand that you chose to give some of this precious room in the global file to necros instead of us (it's your game after all), although i felt a little betrayed.

At this point, we, enchanters were quite confident over the fact that you were giving us in the near future a spectre illusion. There was this time no technical issues, since the model was already in the global file. Second big surprise : you chose NOT to give us the illusion. Reason behind ? You told us there was another technical issue (sigh) : the spectre model has too many polygons and enchanters running everywhere in spectre illusion could create lag.
Even today it's something I simply dont believe. It was blatantly not the true reason, but you didnt want to tell us the real one.
And with Velious Necros got illusion spectre. Hmmm. And you deny it to enchanters again. Hmmm.
Again: Ok, I could try to forget that necros are the only one to get a new model. I will insert here what I think is the true reason, which I can almost accept : A lich/spectre form for necros as the supreme spell of their spellbook should have no rival or else you would lose the really cool and unique aspect of this power. Even if it's only an illusion for enchanters, with no bonuses at all, the fact that another class have it would take a part of the "coolness factor". (please remember this argument when you'll come to the last section of my post)

What irritated me the most on this subject was that you never answered clearly. Abashi said, if I remember correctly that the spell had a "high level of coolness". Sure it had. At the same time you were telling shamen that their shrink spell could not be used outdoor because It would have been purely a fun spell with no purpose and you didnt want that. I simply dont understand this double-reasonning (and I've never played a shaman in my life). We have entertaining-only illusions (all the undead ones which let me KOS with skeletons !). Fun is obviously a good reason for the existence of a spell. Why necros can have fun spells and not shamans ? Little disturbing to me.

Back to our enchanter point of view. Ok so you gave necros a spectre pet, then a pet illusion, everything that was asked by enchanters to have their last illusions has been discarded. Ok.

Now Velious. We were (at least I was) hoping that something would come from this frozen continent. After all the previous requests for completing the illusion line of spell, and the necro issue, I was sure you would do something. Let me tell you that it was a big disillusion.

In velious Druids get a bear pet. Chamans get a bear illusion. No bear illusion for enchanter. Indeed: no illusion at all. Once again you told us that you have found new room in the global file for the bear model. Not for us. I felt very bitter at this point. I had the feeling you were giving purposely the new illusions to all classes except the one that was asking for. But since it's not a reasonnable attitude for a company I think now it's not the case.

All these changes have contributed to a vast disatisfaction concerning the illusion issue. However it's true I was stil the master of illusions. Other classes got new changeform spells ? Good for them. I have no upgrade on this topic ? That's your right to not improve my class. But the last and most important change that came with Velious has simply destroy my last hopes and my actual enjoyment on illusions. You have given the summon monster line of spell to the mages.
This one change has vastly change the way norrath will look like and my illusionnist role in the world. How is it you gave to mages our old proposition of copycat spell for their pet ? How do you expect me not to see this as the ultimate betrayal ? After all this unclear attitude and answers on the illusion issue, I hope you understand how betrayed I can feel. I was -really- having faith in you Verant. I really thought that with Velious we would gain new illusions. We have seen other classes getting illusions with spells, items and so on. But after 2 years I was still hoping for something to come. And you gave more illusions to the mages pet that I could possibly mimic with all my spell line !
This is disheartening.
I have lost the faith.
And it's not the usual complaint: "someone is getting what i wanted yada yada yada". I will explain why I feel the mage spell was a terrible blow to my illusionist aspect. It's absolutely not the same as the previous spells given to other classes. You have given mages a way to create and control virtually all creature types (not stats but appearance) of the world. I know it's not an illusion, but for me really the distinction here is very small. They have access to more appearance than me, the enchantress, will ever be able to imitate, even in my wildest dreams. I feel as if I am not anymore the master of illusions. I know it's a pet but that's how I feel.
And it's worse than that.
You know, there was something "magic" with my class. I could surprise people. You know the feeling it is to be "special" in a world where everyone looks the same ? I know that very soon, due to the number of mages in the game, everyone seeing two monsters fighting again each other wont be stunned anymore. "Oh that's just a mage pet" will be the standard reaction. I used to amaze myself each time I saw my charmed mob fighting another mob. A mob fighting a mob is a terrible sight . Think of Hill giants ! Now it will rapidly become -trivial-. All the mages going across Norrath with giant, spider, gnoll, froglock, griffon pets will trivialized what was something unique and rare.
You have taken this little magic aspect of my class, the "wow" factor that I was so proud about.
And for THAT Verant I blame you so much.

I know many people who chose to start an enchanter for these illusion tricks. With the illusions, ALL the possibly imaginable illusions, given to the pet of a mage, I'm now a second-class illusionist. Not even that. In the game there are NPC enchanters who can turn into aviaks, Sarnaks, gnome robots. I'm limited to a poor set of illusions. I'm ashamed to see what mages can do in comparison.
I know perfectly that you wont change anything. I'm not even asking for it. Mages deserve a good spell like that. But the right move was certainly not giving away all the illusionnist aspect of my class to them. I feel very sad about all this stuff.
You know I have come to the explanation of all of this after some reflexion. I see the big picture now. Look at all the new spells introduced in this game after the initial release, especially the expansion ones: VERY few new spells. What you see is :
- upgrades to old spells
- Spells from one class given to another one
Not new spells.

That's a tendency that I really dont like in EQ. You are overlapping what all classes are able to do. Although it seems good at first sight, I think it's not the right decision. I'm not the integrist enchanter type which think he should be the only one to cast mez: I was very happy that bards get after such a long waiting a correct mez song. Giving mez to necros was however totally inconsistent with the class. You shouldnt blur the frontier between the classes. There is always a roleplay explanation to give something to someone. But you have to draw a line and dont mix everything. Else you are destroying the reason why someone want to play a specific class.
How about giving feign death to enchanters ? It makes sense, it's just an illusion corpse, right ? NO. Because feign death should not be given to everyone. I know it's easy and tempting to get the code of one spell and give it to another class under a new name and hop voila, a new spell is born. You are killing class diversity. Think again at the UO "tank mage" stereotype. We are far away from it in EQ but you are leaning this way. Really it's not the right direction. Please please respect the class specialities. There's no mystery why everyone plays a druid in EQ: they can do every little thing the other classes can, even sometimes better than the specialist classes ! The diversity and a sense of distinction between the classes is -necessary-.
I know it's too late for the summon mob spell. But please acknowledge it was a very bad decision. You didnt had to take a code obviously targeted at the enchanter class (2years waiting...) and give it to the mages.

My fear actually is that the spell design team has moved on EQ2, err... "another project" as you name it. You are not creating new (=potentially unbalancing) spells for the game anymore, but simply moving spells between classes. If it's a design choice, you have to know that this choice for me has been the worse nerf to my enjoyment in the game to date. EQ is not about hack and slash only. I used to love my "surprise them" abilities. They are dead. Charm and illusions in one single move are going to be trivialized. Enchanters were few and their capacities so surprising. Now all the mages out there will make their visual effects common place. End of the "magic feeling" to be a little "special".
Now I know that no new illusions will ever come. Sorry, but I have lost my faith in you. I believe the "no upgrade policy" about enchanters is due to our reputation on the boards to be crucial to all high level expeditions, to be god-like and so on. It was more simple and honest to tell us the truth you know.
Even if new illusions were to come, now that mages have all the possible illusions in a spell line, how could I rival ? I'm not the master of illusion anymore. In my eyes at least.

Thanks for reading me. (and I hope you did)

Silh, a disillusionned enchantress

Mike wrote:

>"Despite his personal feelings Absor *WILL STILL BRING THESE REQUESTS TO THE DESIGN TEAM*."

And I have. Enchanters won't be getting those illusions. The consensus was that they remain unique to those classes. And, it turns out, that I'm not the only person that thinks that undead illusions for enchanters was a mistake.

Though there was some talk of new illusions based on some of the existing global models, there probably won't be any in the near future. I'm sorry.

ScarletGhost said:

>"Please stop with the 'no room' excuse. It's getting old and tired and totally un-believable considering that you clearly can make room for other classes."

Well, it’s a reason, not an excuse. Yes, we made a decision to use freed up memory space to add those two forms for those two classes. That does not mean that we can ignore the initial system requirements.

We may not all agree with that decision, but there are no excuses here.

So far as making bear or spectre available to enchanters as an illusion, the decision is that those forms will remain the distinct province of those classes that have them now.

And, due to the current restrictions, no new enchanter illusions will be added.

There might still be some chance that we'll get Illusion: gender functional and add that. And it might be possible that one or more of the existing global models will be made into illusions, that's still under discussion. But right now, things are probably going to remain as they are.

I know this won't please many of you. For that I'm sorry. And I hope I've explained the technical, gameplay and philosophical reasons for these decisions well enough.

I rather suspect their code IS object-oriented. However, some code is just not that easily swapped out. Consider the following scenario, which is completely HYPOTHETICAL and, although it represents the actual effect seen in-game, probably bears no relationship to the actual code.

Let's say that all reaction code is inside the monsters. Reason: Players choose their own reactions to monsters and players, so its really part of NPC AI. Now, we know that reaction is based on race, religion, and history with a faction. Let's suppose that each monster has a list of reaction modifiers applied to the first two. A sample: a sarnak recruit in the Lake of Ill Omen. Let's say it is -1000 (used to represent KOS) to all PC races and also to goblins and 0(neutral) to everything else. Thus it attacks all PCs and goblins it sees, and ignores all other NPCs. (I've never seen a Sarnak fight an Iksar NPC, but either I may have just never seen the circumstance, or NPC iksar may be a seperate race). Just for the heck of it, it could also be +100 to worshippers of Cazic-Thule and -100 to everyone else. Now, this information is all in the monster object, not the player object. Illusions work (in this scenario) by temporarily changing the race of the character, and thus whenever monsters "query" the race, they get the new value and react to it. In addition, race is probably bound somehow to how the character looks. I.e. you can't just have a "General Race" that looks like a bunch of different stuff depending on circumstance. Absor stated special coding was required to make the two undead illusions. The special coding was probably to make two races (or maybe one if they look exactly the same) just for the illusions, and include reaction variables for monsters for them. The reason you couldn't (in this scenario) make a copycat illusion that didn't alter faction is because to change the way a character is displayed, you'd have to change its race, and now all NPCs would treat it as that race. Thus no Sarnak would attack someone changed to a Sarnak by a spell, because Sarnak don't kill each other. You couldn't work around this by adding new "illusion races" like for the Drybone illusion because you'd have to have an extra illusionary race for every single real race, and even then it would be unclear as to how to get the illusionary race from the value of the copied monster's race. Also, in this case, I can't conceive of a change to only one object that would fix the problem. Actually, you COULD do it by changing the player object... but it would require adding variables for storage of temporary values for race. In this case you could change the client code so the graphic used to display the player was the "apparent" race value, while reaction code continued to use the "real" race. Then Illusion:Copycat could make it you an "apparent" Sarnak (that is, displayed as a Sarnak) without making you a "real" Sarnak (to the reaction code, you are still a human/elf/gnome/erudite/whatever). The problem with this scenario is.... Abashi already said (in regards to keeping track of whether people had already completed a quest) that they CANNOT add new variables to the player code without doing a player wipe. So if they don't have the variable free, and can't add it, they can't do this. I can't really think of any other way to have racial faction modifiers, and display tied to race, and have a copycat illusion under this THEORETICAL construct. So in this sample case, even with well organized object-based code, you are faced with a change you can't make without wiping the player population, something roughly equivalent to dynamiting all the server machines as far as the game goes . It's a shame, actually, that they CAN'T add new variables over the run of the game, because here and especially in quests and in LORE drops there are some variables they really COULD use.
