Some thoughts on a few of the items...

---

Defense skills: I'm 110% for raising these. Would rather have a high level dodge than a low level parry.

---

Aegolism: Please please please please do NOT make this a group buff for a huge amount of mana. As it is, it takes over 20% of my mana to cast, any more would be horrible. Why? Because while it does indeed take a lot of mana currently, I can space out the mana cost of buffing the group by medding in between casts. Think about it, clerics! You are going to have to rebuff when it drops - if you are deep in a dungeon with your mana staying at about 60-70% from healing, do you really want to have to spend nearly *all* of that when group aego falls? This would not only create downtime for the group while they wait for you to med, it creates a dangerous situation if you are attacked by a random wandering mob. LEARN TO SPACE BUFFS. You should never have to redo more than one Aego at a time, and you should have the time to med back the mana for the next one by the time it falls, so that you never have more than one person unbuffed.

Now, as for the Aegolism spell itself, I personally was disappointed with it and would like to see it give more hp/ac than it currently does. It's not an upgrade - and I think that clerics are due for a real upgrade to their buff line at level 60, as opposed to an all in one what we already did buff.

Please don't make this a group buff for 2000 mana.
/begs

---

Shield of Words: Absor, this spell only follows the pattern of the spell line in an artificial sense. Let's say that you go to the store and buy 6 books. They cost you $120. Then you decide you want to get 6 more. The price for 12 books is $240, double the original. Great. Now you want to get 18 books. Following the pattern, the cost for 6 more books is double - $480. Hrm. That doesn't seem right, does it? If we only get 6 books they are $20 each, but if we get 18 they are over $26 each. Are those extra 6 books really good enough to warrant that?

The problem here is not the mana cost itself, it is the mana spent per minute of duration per ac point gained. Armor of Faith takes .09 mana/minute/ac, if you extrapolate it. Shield of words is .13 mana/minute/ac - that's a HUGE difference. Bulwark of faith is a little better, .11 - but clerics don't really get another mana efficient ac buff until Aegis, at .08 mana/minute/ac.

Shield of words is a mana hog; the few extra ac you get are not worth the inflated cost. I consistently used Armor of Faith even though I had SoW. Likewise, I never cast Bulwark of Faith, using Aegis exclusively.

---

Mark of Karn: Along with the pet, this falls into the category of spells that I don't bother casting anymore because they aren't worth the mana cost. Nor do many (if any) other clerics I know cast them. I think that says something.

---

Heroic Bond: When cast by the same cleric, this spell DOES overwrite itself, but will not overwrite Heroism. A level 60 cleric will be able to overwrite a level 59 cleric's Heroism/Heroic Bond, but the reverse is not true. Personally, before I got Aego, if someone joined the group late they got Fortitude - which *is* overwritten, since it has insignificantly less hp/ac than Heroism.

---

Fortitude: I kinda like Fortitude, but only for the reason that I just mentioned above. It is only mana efficient in a group situation for the late comer, or if your group consists of two people. It's great to cast on someone who doesn't have a cleric in their group or who is solo. If the benefits of this spell were upped, then it would replace Heroic Bond. Clerics, do you really want that? I wouldn't.

---

Invisibility: I'd like to see this added to an item. I think putting it on the Donal's bracer is a great idea. You gave warriors invis and shrink, why not give us invis?

---

Clicking off spells: Note that the Armor of Protection line is overwritten by the upper level Talisman line as well, nor does it stack with Yaulp (which is really just silly).

---

A few people have mentioned a group AC buff, I think this would be a great idea... but worry that it would cost too much mana. Personally I think that any spell that takes over 30% of my mana at level 60 is too much.


~Morniel Everhate, Teir'dal Cleric of the 60th Circle

Other people have already adequately dissected Absor's list, so I won't belabor those points. However, what struck me about Absor's list was what was totally missing -- the imbalance between clerics and the other healing classes prior to level 39 (or at least prior to level 34). Shaman get SoW at level 9, druids get it at level 14. Druids then get individual TP at 19 and group TP at 29. Clerics, on the other hand, don't get anything that really distinguishes them, except for Revive at level 29. And frankly, no one gets too excited about Revive because it doesn't restore any experience. The reaction tends to be,"That's all you have? Hmm, let me look for a level 39 cleric."

So let me make a proposal, which would address this problem without having any effect whatsoever on the 50+ game. In fact, this proposal does not nerf any other class, and in fact would benefit every non-cleric in the game.

1. Make Revive a level 19 spell. That is when druids get TP, so why not give clerics Revive? This would make clerics more popular in groups, especially dungeon groups. Because it would be easier to complete CRs in dungeons, it might make some of the underused dungeons more popular, too. This would be a win-win scenario for everyone in the game, and it would not imbalance clerics.

2. Create a new level 29 rez spell that restores 30% experience. At level 29, druids and shamans get GH, and druids get group TP. This would give clerics a little something extra to make them more desirable in a group. Druids and shamans are still rocking at this level, but this would give clerics something that put them in demand.

3. Increase the experience restoration of the level 39 spell, Resuscitate, to 60%. This is really optional, but I like a 30-60-90 progression. That way, there is more separation from the level 29 spell.

4. Leave Resurrection and Revivescence alone. The level 49 spell stays 90%, and the level 56 spell stays 96%. Clerics don't need help competing in the 50+ game, as long as we don't get nerfed on what we already have.

The only real objection to this proposal would be that there would be less experience loss in the the level 29-39 game. Compared to the elimination of the class experience penalties, the reduction in experience loss would be trivial. Furthermore, this proposal would make the game more fun for everyone. Even the most violently anti-cleric druid dies from time to time. If a level 22 cleric could give that druid a Revive somewhere out in the boondocks or on the bottom level of a dungeon, the druid won't complain one bit.

STANDING MEDITATION III
-----------------------

Ranjar, you are entirely correct.

To all of you who say "no, we should have it" I wholly disagree and ask that you consider EVERY possible thing you have to stand up to do, and that sitting down makes either impossible or dangerous.

Aggro and medding in a fight is one prime example. When the cleric has reached a point from healing in a fight that he cannot sit down to meditate without drawing the mob, (which is a problem suffered by EVERY CASTER when they draw taunt) then they have to remain standing or simply get hit for the max damage that creature can pump out in one of its combat 'rounds'. That could mean a quad of max damage (dual wield, double attack) from that nasty mob if you sat down at the wrong time! Giving clerics the ability to simply IGNORE this problem is by far an unbalancing proposal.

What does the cleric do to avoid this? Well, for starters, don't taunt that much. This sounds so simple it's stupid (if it hurts, don't do it) but it's true - just like Rangers who do so much damage in a short amount of time that they complain even Jolt won't save them... why didn't they just turn off autoattack for a second when it started getting bad? A Ranger who lived and did less damage is more useful than one who died. So the cleric learns to use root, enstill, stuns, even Atone to make up for the times that they blundered, or they stand there and take a few hits until someone can taunt the mob off, and wait a long while to sit back down. They screwed up building too much taunt, so they don't get the mana from meditating. If that changes for Clerics, then Druids and Shamans (who have DoTs and debuffs that build taunt even more than cleric healing) are being spit upon.

What about visibility? In the pre-34 EQ, meditation is a complete visibility blind. For those people who are going to turn on attacker chat filters (who really wants to see exactly how often the two group rogues, the group bard, and the group ranger hit, miss, or sing songs? especially when an important 'ADD!' or 'RUN FOR IT' might be groupsaid...) they're even more required to just watch the HP bars and stand up occasionally to get an eye of what's going on. In a mana sense, unless they're casting when they stand up, they wasted mana (could have been meditating) by hopping to their feet. So if "standing meditation" is implemented, why wouldn't a cleric simply turn on their wide-view third-person perspective and monitor everyone and everything around them while healing? I know I would. Again, completely unfair to everyone else to give this to the cleric.

What about those who say "ah, but the pre-34 EQ doesn't matter, this should only be a high level skill!" too, hmm? Double standard. Complain about the 'fun' of being a cleric, but forget entirely that the moment they get Greater Healing, the cleric better darn well have their rear on the ground and nose in a book. What happens to everyone between 19 and 34? It wasn't important for playing a cleric to be 'fun' then, but it is after that? No way in Norrath will I accept that as the answer, even if the game gets three times more fun past that point it still isn't worth it for those who have NEVER GOTTEN THERE. The guy who plays a cleric up to 30 and stops, even though it's his highest level guy, because it "just wasn't fun" compared to any of his alts, isn't going to care whether or not 50+ clerics got something to make it 'fun' for them.

Clerics have fewer spells than others. Many of them are completely ineffectual (lull, soothe, calm) because of their lack of reliability. Many are simply restricted to functionality in certain areas of EQ, or are better done by other classes (flash of light, fear, root) because the cleric's mana is ultimately THE best at handling the heal, hands down. Clerics are incredibly group-reliant. They do not melee well, they do not take damage well, although they generally have a higher AC than the four pure casters.

What does this higher AC boil down to? It's not important that higher AC (higher defense skill, etc.) be upped for the purposes of melee, because melee requires offense skill, a well-suited weapon, and lots of practice. What group-suited cleric actually has a lot of time to spend swinging a weapon that simply won't connect very often, and when it does will likely result in minimal damage? (Strength sacrificed for wisdom: we aren't Paladins.) Adding Bash doesn't fix this, as we shouldn't be up there meleeing in the first place.

The SOLE reason I see to up defense skills at all is merely to give that cleric who got jumped and attacked a chance to take a few more hits than the pure casters would before -requiring- a rescue. Yes, channeling might mean the cleric can heal themselves if they really need it, but it's more important to everyone else that the cleric live so he can keep THEM alive, and self-healing only builds that taunt factor. Root might get it off the cleric long enough to re-taunt, but even during that time it's important to have had the defense skill. Not so the cleric could swing their mace better. Not so they can mix it up next to the Paladin, the Ranger, the Warrior, or by the gods - the Shaman!

What is an EQ Shaman? A ritualist, barbaric, spiritualist "priest". Who do they serve? Justice. Hate. Fear. The Glory of War. These are ideals of which great personal strength and combat ability IS an important part.

What is an EQ Cleric? A holy man/woman, one closer to the God of their people, the speaker of the words of their gods and teacher of those truths. A healer, and in many cases the hand that walks forth to do battle with the scourge that MUST be cleansed, but always with their guard at their arm and men of devotion at their side! (Paladin or Shadowknight) The cleric is NOT the hand that strikes down that scourge themselves, ideally. They are the force of will behind the cleansing, the purification, the Jihad, but they support the arms of devoted warriors who stand to fight. Who do they serve? Love. Life. Truth. Community. Peace. Protection of the weak. Balance. And yes, evil deities: Hate, Fear and Pain, and Death and Decay. But the cleric's role in even these forms of evil worship are not those ultimate hands that strike down the 'good' themselves, but the mouth that speaks the words of Hate, or Fear, or Death, to the true followers who draw their power from it and go out to spread their stain of evil across the land.

I play a Human Cleric of Bertoxxulus. I roleplay him as a Prophet. I try to teach others the truth of their mortality. The phrase most commonly heard by me is this: "There is but one ultimate truth in Norrath, one ultimate truth of the Plaguebringer, and it is this: all things must die." Does that mean I'm going to stand there and kill them with my mace? NO! Does that mean I'm going to smite them with holy-granted power? NO! That DOES mean I'll refute their foolish ways with words, and intelligence, and if they choose to attack I can stun, cancel their magics, heal myself, root, them, and get away long enough to call my fellow faithful to arms. And those who dared attack the Prophet of the Plaguebringer will shiver as the scourge of the Shadowknights of Bertoxxulus strip their very flesh from their bones.

I don't want invisibility as a spell. Put it on my armor, sure. That's an enchanted item that I can seek to aid my calling. I don't have time to learn the spells of invisibility, regardless of what people say about "everyone else gets it"... if that's the case, talk to "everyone" and have them cast it on you. Invis to undead is appropriate, as undeath is a scourge to be cleansed/appropriated and some power over THEM makes sense. I'll not fool mens' minds with invisibility, only to have them distrust the true words I speak about my god as if they were ranting from a deceitful trickster!

I don't want higher offense skills. What cleric stands before their foe and strikes them with the mace or staff to kill them? Clerics are given the ability to kill when needed, and sparingly... (mana expensive) their power is in healing.

I don't want a self-gate to my temple! If you, a cleric, need THAT then you haven't been spreading the word to enough Wizards (or Druids, in the case of Karana and Tunare) yet. Any faithful Wizard/Druid who is a member of your temple should be willing to assist the chosen priests of their order. Gating back to the Temple is not a power given to those who go out into the world and tell - it's given to those who cower within their ivory towers. That is not the Priest!

Yes, I can shove something with my shield. Who can't? But Bash is an offensive skill. Just like I never learned to parry with my mace (Turn aside a blade with a blunt weapon? They'll sever my hand first!) or attack with a staff in each hand, I never learned the proper way to get past a dangerous foe's guard with my shield to strike them. Any fool can run headlong, shield held before them, and any fool can be surprised when the warrior they fight merely side-steps and heaves a foot of steel through one's gut. No bash for clerics.

Why would I bind wounds with a bandage when my god gives me their blessed power of healing? To refrain from its use on the faithful is to spurn such a gift, and even binding the wounds of my god's foes is sacrilige. I have my prayers. Bandages are for the less devout - to spurn my healing ability in favor of a bandage is to call into question my very faith in that which sustains me.

I am the holy speaker of the words of my god. I tell my comrades what the god has decreed, I interpret his wishes in every circumstance. I bless, I provide for, and I defend the swords and arms of the faithful and valiant with my prayers. Praying for each, in turn, is a blessed act - but not all situations allow for this individual attention. A greater prayer, calling for the favor of the divine one upon us all in a group blessing of protection, seems entirely in order; it would, however, be less effective than individually ministering to the needs of my comrades. I would hope that the particular prayers themselves for the many are not commonly known: quests to recover group prayers would be the only way that these should be provided.

Just as the faithful Wizards will port, so too will the faithful enchanters mesmerize my foes. I will smite when I must smite, and I will dismiss the undead when I am called to dismiss: but the enchanter is relied upon for their abilities and I will not ask them to be relied upon for mine. I have no need of mesmerization or root of undead, when my root will suffice for all, and the enchanters' mesmerize will do the same. If there are no faithful amongst the enchanters, perhaps they too are a scourge waiting to be cleansed?

What deity is more divine than my own? I know the spells he provides me, and I believe wholly that they will sustain me. If my god sees fit to reveal a greater mystery and a greater power to mine eyes, then so be it: but what deity is able to reveal to its chosen a mystery that no other deity can pierce? Just as Tunare created the elves, so too was Innoruuk able to warp them into his image. Solusek Ro was able to blast life into a searing death, and even to this day the Sands of Ro yet smolder in their heat. Why should Prexus be the only lord to fathom a secret of the deep, when Karana's continual rains draw from the deep itself? Deity-specific prayers and spells, while possible, open a huge Pandora's Box of problems that only (message-board) Jihad would resolve. Let the faithful chosen bear their Symbols of Faith with pride. Let us earn the station of Regent and beyond with our toils and our faith, and let our god's unique touch reach us through his markers. Is the symbol not enough? Then perhaps a scepter or signet is an appropriate chalice to fill with holy power... but these are but ramblings. I would not dare to be wiser than my god and say 'this, this is where you must bless me'.

I am the Prophet Wascen Witherfist. I am the tainted tongue of the Plaguebringer, but I speak the truth: you and I are mortal. Do not dare redefine me as a loathed Paladin, nor force me to strike out alongside the Shadowknights rather than behind them, for I have chosen my own path.

I am the Cleric.
