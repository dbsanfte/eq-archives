re: lich, and raid stuff

One of the defining features of the high level necro, IMNSHO, is efficient damage, and lich is part of that. We can't do our damage as rapidly as other classes, we aren't the best in pet damage, and our area-of-effect spells, for whatever they're worth, aren't that effective, either. We do our damage slowly, but efficiently, with a variety of dots, and pets that aren't the best -- and certainly not at tanking -- but have a decent damage output. Having a superior rate of mana regen fits into this well, and helps to compensate for the extra difficulties involved in using dots and pets to do your damage. Of course, lich has its own challenges -- often I have to let my hps get below four bubbles before I cast bond of death (since it taps hps much faster than lich can eat them). I also have to turn lich off from time to time between battles because my health is getting low, and there isn't a mob around to tap.

Now, we do have other abilities. Of course, most casting classes have auxiliary abilities as well. And the extras we get that aren't done better by other classes -- mana transfer, DMF, and corpse summoning -- are somewhat marginal in nature, especially within the context of a normal group. Nerf lich, and you are hurting what the necromancer is best at.

I'm sure you can see, within this, the raid dilemma of the necromancer as well. The thing we do best in a well-balanced group -- efficient damage -- is moot on many raids, where pets and dots are often not allowed. Even if they are, the aforementioned problems with dot stacking, pets being feared / lost in pathing / wasted by aoes sharply reduces this power. But, I digress.

In any case, if lich is going to be nerfed, I expect to see it stack with clarity 2. Presently, lich acts as a form of mana regen in conjunction with lifetap dots, with an overall effect somewhat better than c2. This works because bond of death taps 720 hps for 360 mana; you get 720 mana, and spend half of it for bond. If lich converted at a 3:2 ratio, as you say was intended, you would get 480 mana for those 720 hps tapped. Since you had to spend 360 mana for bond, your net change in mana would be 120, instead of the previous 360 -- which would make lich vastly inferior to c2.

Of course, this assumes you are using bond of death (or its upgrade). If you are on a raid that doesn't allow dots, and are using deflux to tap, you are tapping roughly 300 hps for 200 mana. With a 3:2 ratio on lich, you would receive 200 mana for those hps tapped -- no net mana regen at all.

But you say, lich is a conversion spell, not meant for generic mana regen. If that is to be the case, then I shouldn't have to give up a mana regen spell in order to use it. The overall effect would not give necros with a nerfed lich much more mana the way it would if our present lich stacked with clarity 2.

I understand you want to keep the classes in balance, but please consider the effects that your proposed change to lich will have.

OK. Necros have issues with being needed/wanted in groups, especially in raids. Verant's "vision" is that people should group, and they have designed level 50+ Mobs and situations to force people to group, even necros. However, Verant doesn't want to give necros enhancements that will increase their ability to solo. I think we all agree that a necro is among the poorest choice of class for a raid or high level group, as what they do best, Pet, DOT, Fear, are tactics that for one reason or another have serious drawbacks against an uber-MOB, or in high level dungeons. That said, what could be done to improve a necro's group desirabilty, without adding soloing capability? Why not extend the necro's native forte of manipulating and converting forces (e.g. mana, HPs, and to a lesser extent XP) to include the group, and perhaps into areas not yet tapped. The guiding theme behind these new spells would be that they don't help soloing, and that whatever you give to the group comes from either the mob, or yourself, and is of short duration, like our current Vampiric Curse line. Examples would be:

Mana DOT - takes xx mana over xx seconds from mob and splits among all casters in group. Stacks with clarity, but not with Lich (to avoid soloing improvement).

Haste Curse. - Lowers attack speed of mob, and increases haste of all tanks in group. Lasts 45 seconds or so. Stacks with haste items and buffs.

Stamina DOT. - takes xx stamina over xx seconds from mob and splits it among all tanks in group.

Snare Curse. - lowers mob running speed for xx seconds and increases group running speed for same amount. Works only in dungeons to avoid being abused soloing outside. Primarily an escape spell, as you don't otherwise need to run fast in a dungeon unless you are trying to get away.

Targeted Feign Death. - Save cleric on raids gone bad. Downside, maybe necro stunned for xx seconds afterwards so he can't chain cast it, and to give mob a chance to kill necro instead of target.

Resist Curse (one spell for each resist type). Mob resist to xxxxxx lowered and group resist to xxxxxx raised. Lasts 45 seconds. Stacks with other resist spells.

You could take this philosophy and extend it to any attribute in the game (e.g. strength, AGI, INT, WIS, etc.). This would help out the group a lot without infringing on the other buffer's abilities, as theirs doesn't need a mob, and lasts much longer. It also wouldn't have a significant effect with regard to soloing capability.


Just my two cents worth.
