Greetings!

I am glad to see this post and all of the contributions that have been offered here. There are some great ideas that have inspired me to comment and add as appropriate below. Forgive me if I cover any ground already discussed or omit any ideas previously mentioned.

BACKGROUND:
I have played my druid to level 39 through over 80 days of /played time. I'm a casual player and enjoy fishing as much as anything else. Oddly enough, fishing and alcohol tolerance have both increased at the same time and are both maxed at the moment - hehe.

I have also played DnD and ADnD since thier beginning and have played in GenCon tournaments for many years. I understand the concepts behind the huge tasks of creating worlds while making them challenging and fun.

GENERAL IDEAS:
* My druid is completely untwinked. I see no need for uber-gear that I did not earn. I enjoy pursuing the gear with friends and grouping to meet new friends. My friend list has moved to paper long ago. hehe. Unfortunately, so has my ignore list.

* The biggest problem that I have is camps. I do not have the time to sit in UGuk for 30 hours waiting to combat the MOB that might drop the components I need to finish the Paw of Opalla quest. Invariably, there is a high-level character parked there with thier pet guarding the spawn point. Trying to get that camp is insanely difficult. (My 44 warrior has the same problem with the FBSS and trying to get that camp for the last 9 levels - lol - there is usually a high-level mage or necro camping it to sell it).

* I do not disagree with anyone's motivation to play this game. I just find the VI logic difficult to understand. If grouping is the focus, then why is loot sellable? All loot should be NODROP and sellable to merchants only in my opinion and allow the trade-skill items and merchant wares to be fully sellable. I will never see 40,000 plat in my account as a druid. Indeed, among the level 60 friends that I have, only the mages, necros, and a few twinks can claim that bank balance. So be it - again, I do not complain here. I chose the poverty and natural focus of the druid and could roll up a necro easily to fund stuff. But my point is grouping. If grouping truly is the focus of VI's intent, then sellable treasure will only encourage the higher level soloing classes to camp that particular treasure to sell and twink thier alternates. (with a 20% turnover in subscriptions for EQ, it is simple logic that 80% are staying and trying new class/race combinations - but the difficulty in the new class/race encourages that 80% to twink thier alternate with gear that drops from 60th level mobs - I know this is true because I have seen it)

* Each of us has a limited right to play this game in a matter that we enjoy without making it unenjoyable for others. If a person focuses on loot or levelling or fishing, it is completely up to them. I state this only because I do not want my words above to taken out of context or spirit.

DRUID SPECIFIC IDEAS:
* The druid is finely tuned to the forces of nature. He or she draws upon that force for the accomplishment of a task. If we are to compare the druid to the other priest classes (shaman and cleric), we must also understand that thier magical sources originate elsewhere. The Cleric relies upon the force that his or her diety blesses them with and the Shaman upon the herbs and chants that call the assistance of spirits to accomplish a goal.

* In the Dungeons and Dragons world, the druid is the traveller and the one most closely related to nature. To travel, the druid would open up tunnels in the undergrowth of a thick forest enabling his or her group to walk at a regular movement rate through them. This is accomplished in EverQuest with SoW, levitate, camo, and TPs as they assist in guiding the druid's group to and from any point.

With this in mind then, I offer the following ideas that can be implemented. These ideas should meet 2 criteria in my opinon.
1 - ideas should not make any other class less effective
2 - ideas should not make the druid more desireable

Here they are:
* Druids should have the ability to summon any aninmal pet in the zone. Indoors or out, if there is an animal there, the druid knows it. This pet should stay with the druid for the duration that the druid is in that zone and once dismissed, simply goes away. Perhaps it attacks the druid or just poofs. That really does not matter. But the balance is that the druid would need a non-stackable material component (mistletoe was used in ADnD to enhance the power of all druid spells) and, the druid should suffer some sort of ability hit to offset thier concentration on maintaining thier pet. Perhaps a substantial reduction in wisdom or dropping the moverate to precisely that of thier pet. This would make pet choices a bit more thought provoking and reduce the ability to pet-quad-kite or some other rediculous solo strategy while not making the kiting ability completely useless. Druids often kite excess mobs off thier groups as a sort of needed crowd-control in the absence of an enchanter.

* Druids should have the ability to completely heal a group member (not just any target) and, optionally, only outdoors. But the complete heal should not be the same as the cleric's because the channeled forces have different origins. Let the Druid completely heal a group member by casting this spell on a sitting member only. During a period of say 2 minutes, neither the druid nor the target will be able to move, cast other spells, or be effected by PC spells of beneficial value (NPC spell attacks should be more or less effective as well as PvP attacks). The idea is illustrated by considering a visual of the druid conjuring the natural plantlife to encompass and surround the injured target and heal it with it's essence. Essentially rooted, the target must take no action during the healing process and the druid must maintain his or her concentration on the channelling of this power.

* Rooting something means just that. Rooted. Certainly do not remove the other casters' ability to root a target but allow the druids to have a much more effective one. One that has no chance to break every round but one that still suffers the chance to break on a direct damage spell. Leave ensnare the way it is but allow it to overwrite everyone elses snares and slows. Give the druid value in this area and he or she will be a saught out addition to every group.

* As a being tuned to nature, the druid should know and be proficient with travel. Every zone and every nook and cranny should be explored. This idea involves the person at the keyboard to do just that and learn to be the guide for thier groups. No spells are needed here, only the commitment of the player to actually learn the zones. Perhaps making camo and sup camo less random would help, but hearing constant whines in the zone asking where is that or this from druids simply embarrasses me as a druid roleplayer. I usually tell them to camo up and explore and have spent many hours teaching the loc system to those that wanted. But the ability to take on another form does much in the way toward accomplishing this as I cover in the next idea.

* For the roleplay, and the travel, allow the druid to take on the form of any animal in the current zone and allow that form to remain such as long as the PC remains in that zone. If a snake is normally attacked by the guards in West Freeport, then the druid should be attacked if in that form. Likewise, if the druid is of sufficient level to defeat his attacker, then perhaps the snake will win once in a while. And if the scouts in Firiona Vie do not normally attack a leech, then the KOS druid has a tool to gain entry there. Some things see through camo, but others might be indifferent to wasp form. Allow the druid to explore and learn when and where to strategically take that self-illusion on. There are many options for this spell to provide some fun as well as travelling strategies and keyboard education beneficial to the druids' group.

SUMMARY:
The druid can be a useful class in any group that he or she wishes to be a part of. Some people, like myself, are casual players and not bold enough to ask for a group when we only have an hour or so to play. Making the druid a desireable class to have in a group by augmenting and not replacing other benefits found in other classes goes a long way to achieve this goal. I might add that druids also should wear robes as the druids do in DnD but the look of the druid is not as important as his or her contribution to the success of the group. Allow the druid to be the prince of nature again but the king of nothing and the druid will remain an option for the player and not an uber-class.

Peace all. I know many of you will be inclined to flame me and, quite honestly, I and others skip past those whines anyway so go nuts. I simply play a game that enjoy and offer some ideas to make one aspect of it more fun.

Although a druid is not my main character I do have one at level 51. Her primary role is tax and power level support which is a shame. She was a valued member of a party through level 40, maybe even through the mid 40s. What happened??

I have read as many of these posts as possible and everyone seems to think that a couple of healing spells will fix everything. I think the issue is a little deeper, especially if you want to keep the uniqueness of the druid in tact.

A druid for most of their life [assuming you don't get tremendous enjoyment from kiting stupid non-intelligent MOBs around in circles] has a good role in a group:

1) Shields -- skin line.
2) Healing [direct heals, poison/desease heals and regen]
3) Str buffs
4) SOW
5) Dam Shields
6) Shared Wolf Form [great buff for tanks]
7) Resist buffs
8) Nukes
9) DOTS
10) Snare, Root

A druid could be summoned up as a secondary healer, with good blasts, great dam shields, nice DOTs, SOW and Shared Wolf Form.

A little bit of everything, plus SOW and Shared Wolf Form.

After 50 the problem is a relative one.

1) healing, the gap in a cleric and druids healing ability goes from large to huge in the plus 50+ game. A level 60 druid couldn't even keep one tank alive in a real fight. So I agree with everyone that SH at 49 and something a little better at mid 50 would get things back on track.

2) Regen. This line of spells is also not increasing in proportion to the damage and total hit points of a highend tank. But its close.

3) SOW/Shared Wolf. The restriction of this to outdoors had the effect to keep druids a bit more outddors than indoors. However, in the post 44 game groups and multigroup activities tend to force you indoors a lot more. Hence this line of spells is tremendously devalued. I think that there should be a pair of post 50 spells, one a SOW upgrade and one a Shared Wolf Upgrade. This pair should both be castable indoors, last longer than the current ones and survive zoning. This is what makes a druid a druid [or at least one of the most associated things with druids, puppys and SOW]

4) Damage Shields begin to lose there effectiveness in the post 50 game. The mobs are hitting harder and harder, but the dam shield is not raising in proportion. The dam shield has always been most effective on fast hitting MOBs that hit for small amounts. But when MOBs start to hit for 300+ even the a 32 dam shield is not doing much. I would suggest having two post 50 dam shields both of which are percentage based. I.e. a 15% dam shield would do 30 damage if a mob hit you for 200.

So, in summary:

Move SH to 49 and and a better heal at mid 50.

Add a upgrade to SOW and Shared wolf in the 50s that can be cast indoors and lasts longer.

Change the post 50 dam shields to be % based.

Maybe lookminto the regen line and see if it is improving in line with a a tanks typical damage in hps.

None of this takes anything away from other classes, it just says keep the druid being a druid.
