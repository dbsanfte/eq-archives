I would like to make a rational point about Balancing Hybrids.

Lets first look at what each was intended to be: A ShadowKnight is intended to be a Warrior crossed with a Necromancer. A Paladin is a Warrior crossed with Cleric. And a Ranger is a Warrior crossed with a Druid. Hopefully no arguements so far.

Now lets look at defining features of each main caster class and the resultant hybrid.

Necromancers' defining characteristics are Damage spells, and undead pets. ShadowKnights get damage spells and undead pets.

Clerics get heals, including complete heal, and good spells against undead. Paladins get good heals, a form of complete heal (lay on hand) and spells against undead.

Druids defining features are Traveling spells (ports), middling level buffing and SOW. Rangers get a few buffs (very low level), Crits with a bow (have been discussed to death elsewhere) and tracking (needs to be fixed ie. scroll bar).

Does anyone else notice something here. Don't get me wrong I like my ranger and will continue playing him. But it seems to be the only hybrid who inherits nothing from it's supposed parent caster class.

I understand balance, and know that ports at low levels would be overpowering, but how bout some self only stuff that would not realy unbalance.
Nerfed SOW (self only and not quite as good) at 22. Gate at 22. Self only ports and self only bind in outdoor zone at 30 or 39. By that high of level I don't see this as un-balancing, I mean SK get pets, and Pal get CH.

So what does everyone think?

(by the way nothing of what I'm suggesting has andy bearing on me right now my ranger is only lvl 13)

1. When you make a thread titled "A Rational Hybrid Discussion", do not be suprised when a paladin, shadowknight or bard shows up to discuss their views on the topic. Furthermore, do not be suprise when a thread title as such turns out to be what you want for the ranger class only, that these paladins, shadowknights, and bards are going to express their disagreement with. If you want to discuss rangers issues and only deal with ranger issues, title the thread approriately.

2. If you post public your views on a topic, you will get people who will disagree. If you are going to post your views, you have to be willing to justify them. If you are not able to justify them, do not post them. Also, just because someone disagrees with you and expresses their disagreement with you, does not mean that they are giving you an angry responce. You do a dis-service to Mujina by making a reference to his post as "...get angry emotional responces from pallies..." His responce was in no way an angry emotional responce. It was just a disagreement to your views that you failed to justify. You really should appoligize to him.

3. Now, as to rangers receiving bind or teleportation. First, I have to tell you that I believe that is just a pipe dream. Verant will never give rangers these abilities. Does not matter about any arguments you make on balance. The sole reason they would never give us these abilities is external to balance. It is because it would piss off to many other class. Come on now man, every other melee class would be royally ticked off and most likely quit the game if rangers were the only melee class to receive bind. And change that cause a mass amount of people to quit the game is a change that IS unbalanced. Same with teleport. But self-only teleports are even worse for the fact they beneift only that class. No other class would beneift from this change. That would make this change a clear increase in the power of that class and that class only. Any unjustifable increase in power to one class only is an unbalanced increase.

4. There are agruments about paladins receiving rez which is the primary key trait of their caster class parent, clerics. There are agruments about shadowknights receiving pets which is the primary key trait of their caster class parent, necromancer. But both of these give a group a justifable advantage. Paladins have rezs means that a group member may be able to get a rez from someone other then a cleric. A shadowknights pet means more damage done in a quicker time by the group. Self teleports and binds does not help the group in any way.

5. The key trait about rangers, and those that play rangers, are their ability to learn and know the locations that they travel and hunt in. They are guides for the classes. Their abilities and skills leads them to be able to do this effectively. This is more then just a roleplaying factor. It is even in the point of game culture. Those that play a ranger (by the nature of playing a ranger) often know more about any zone an more zones then anyone that plays any other classes. Rangers are often group pullers, this allows them to see more of the zone and its special features then any other person. I cannot tell you how many times that I knew more about the details of where mobs spawned, when they spawned, how they moved then any other person in my group. The ability to teleport will make the majority of rangers lazy in developing this knowledge base. This will intern make them less effective to be the groups puller. It is our familiarization of zones and shortcuts that is our boon. And no matter what you say, given the ability to teleport will make rangers more likely to do that rather then explore the zones and what is there and what is new.

Here's some interesting stuff to throw in the 'rational discussion' stuff - all factual.

With the changes on Test, rangers will always do the same damage as warriors - and sometimes more damage thanks to DD spells when they have the mana. Rangers are getting Offense AND weapon skills raised to 252, along with having Dual Wield skill capped as high as 240. Also, with Jolt being lowered to 51, and a new fire based 'Cinder' Jolt being added at 55 - Ranger surviveability will significantly increase, and as their potential aggro reduces - their potential damage output increases. Another important thing to note is that Rangers can use weapons of the same dmg/dly ratios as warriors. And since mobs only ever hit one thing at a time, despite Rangers lacking defense, their added offense and damage ability is always welcome when a group or raid has a warrior already. (Offense - damage output - stacks. Defense doesn't)

On the other side of the coin, Knights are getting only their Defense raised to 252, while Parry, Dodge, Riposte and Hit Points remain the same. This means that while Rangers are equivalent and sometimes better than warriors offensively, Paladins and Shadow Knights will remain significantly inferior than warriors defensively. Because of this, once a group has a warrior, any high damage dealing melee will be preferred to add to the group over a Paladin or Shadow Knight with their very low dmg output. Why? Offense stacks, Defense doesn't. To compound the offense vs. defense issue - Warrior Kunark class armor has 43 raw AC over Knights, while Knight's class armor has only 17-19 raw AC over Rangers and Rogues.

Last but not least, Rangers got Trueshot and auto-parry disciplines, both extremely useful (the auto-parry being a very good defensive type disc). Meanwhile Paladins got Holyforge - a useless waste, and Sanctification - 20second immune to magical damage only. Hardly useful.
Shadow Knights got a disc wasted on a HT upgrade, it is good though - but useless on the high lvl raids since uber mobs resist it still anyway. And, at 60 they got that lifetap for amount they hit for -for what? 15 secs? Won't amount to much, but arguably better than pally discs.

Ranger discs better offensively (Trueshot, as it should be) and better defensively (Auto-parry, not as it should be imo) than Knight discs.

How's that for rational? ;p
