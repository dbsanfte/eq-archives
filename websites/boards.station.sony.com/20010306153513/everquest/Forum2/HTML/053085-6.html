Well, this is something that I seem to have encountered with the shadow knight talk earlier. Some want offense, some want defense. Being better at both isn't balanced.

My understanding was that the balancing factor was the fact that monks don't have half the hp of a warrior, that means that if we are being hit there is no way for us to tank as effectively as a warrior/shadowknight/paladin who can use a cleric's healing ability to its full potential because of the hp that they have over us. Also the fact that when we get hit, we get harder than these tank classes due to lack of damage mitigation means that lower total health of the monk leaves us laying on the ground, out of combat.

The thing is mobs want to hit monks, and they hit us hard and we don't have the hp to take the hits.

I think another problem is one you mentioned, with the having to look up the "vision" on monks. I think thats the problem, no one knows what the "vision" for monks is, so monks get left out of balance.

From the start I always viewed monks as being defensive masters, sacrificing heavy armor for ability to dodge, this doesn't mean when we get hit we don't get hard, it just means that monks need to be the _BEST_ at avoiding being hit, since we "chose" not to wear armor.

You also will notice a flip flop in beliefs about damage because no one really knows, pre kunark, monks were labeled as master of melee, they did the most damage, with the exception of rogues post evade. After kunark, monks continued to sort of hold this title, though analyzation came to realize that the best weapons availible to the general playerbase left monks wielding the same or like weapons to all other class, meaning they lost their distinction, except the fact that there were a few EXTREEMELY rare monk items that gave them the best advantage, so the in theory were still the masters of melee. With velious, while not completely explored, common drop weapons useable by many non-monk classes and not the monk class and no simular improvement items for monks leaves monks actually lower on the damage scale than they were previously.

Mind you that the reason monks went to weapons is that their hands no longer compared damage wise to the best possible weapons as well as skill wise after kunark came out.

It boils down to, I think monks can be balanced with having superior defensive skills and damage dealing abilities because the design features being, monks can't afford to be hit given their lower hit points and their damage mitigation leaves them being hit for near max damage due to the fact that we don't have the damage mitigation ac and verant seems steadfast against changing this. The final result is the best equiped monk will have roughly 200 less ac than the best equiped warrior a margin of about 15%, and at least 30% less hitpoints than the average warrior/true tank class.

Monks aren't designed to be tanks, they are designed to avoid damage, not take it. Given those balancing features they can reasonably be given significant increases to damage and damage avoidance.

In the end game, a monk won't be chosen for a group over a warrior if they don't do more damage than a warrior, because they don't tank as well and never will.

Sure i can outdamage a paladin or shadowknight but i can't get the mob off a wizard that just overnuked before he/she dies. For this reason monks will always be stuck fighting for that 5th or 6th slot that isn't Tank, Cleric, Enchanter, Evac'er. If monks don't bring something compelling, such as significant damage, then the groups will take a rogue or warrior or any class that can do significant damage.

Balance is having a compelling reason to invite a character beside that the person behind the character is a friend who made a poor choice in playing class.

I know I'm a little late in posting however I'll add my two cents in.

First off, Abashi has many times said that the vision of monks were kings of damage dealing, and, for a brief time we were and were content with that. Not as tough as warriors. As tough, if not tougher than rogues. However, our damage dealing potential was always higher than both.

With the upgrades to rogues their damage approached ours when in groups, but not while soloing. Warriors were never as good as we were as far as damage output per unit time either.

Unfortunately it seems that by the time kunark was released, both rogues and warriors, who were both unpopular classes at one time, were far and above over compensated for their previous deficiencies. Better weapons. Better armor. Better disciplines. All to the point that their combat performance exceeded that of the monk by leaps and bounds.

Now we are being told (or atleast infering your meaning by your responses) that our class has been redefined as being better than rogues but not as good as warriors as far as what our original class defining ability is concerned, that being damage output potential. This is a concern to myself and I'm sure to many others, as I see the question being asked in many posts "What is our new role or class defining ability?"

The last issue has to do with equipment. Every new item that is released that would improve our class has some drawback on it that deminishes us. Usually that is unusually high weight or an exceptionally low AC. Now we are seeing tradeskill items (black panther skin armor to be specific) with better overall stats and AC than our planes gear(an insult to say the least in many respects), further complicated by the fact that no human or iksar can train their skill high enough to make it themselves.

Even the evolution of our class specific gear does not even compare nor come close to the same progression as that of other classes. All other melee classes received armor in Kunark with considerable armor class increase and a right click effect. Monks did not. The progression from Plane of Hate/Fear armor to the armor found in Plane of Growth is as much as 30% increase in armor class, but the same cannot be said for the monk equivalent. There is no need to go in to the weapons with considerably better delays than fists as I have seen others post concerning it.

Lastly, are the disciplines and class abilities. Rogues received a discipline that will double a backstab of 500 damage to 1,000 damage or more, which can land twice in a single round. We receive a thunderkick that consistantly hits for less damage than a regular fly kick with no associated special abilities (like stun, debuff, etc). Warriors receive triple attack at level 60, mind you wielding weapons with better delay ratios than our fists, even with celestial fists.

My suggestions for finding some middle ground are as follows:

1. Do not change the class defining characteristic of monks, which is the king of damage dealing. While I would concede it is too late to nerf warriors and rogues, giving monks triple attack, maybe even at some point before 60 will help keep them on top.

2. Allow the cellestial fist effect to stack with other standard haste buffs, or make the haste effect permanent like that of rogues and rangers.

3. Thunderkick discipline should at the very least be on par with the double damage back stab ability of rogues. Not necessarily do as much, but atleast have a higher minimum base damage, double normal damage, and/or an associated proc.

4. Additionally, fly kick should double attack as per the rogue ability to double backstab.

5. All of our special attacks, or atleast some of them, should be able to use more than one per recycle time. To be able to round kick and fly kick at the same attack round would not noticably increase our damage output but would instill a little meaning in having those skills.

6. While I would not ask for monk items with rediculous damage delay ratios, I would say to slow down on the warrior and rogue stuff. If you are to introduce new items for monks I would only ask that they follow the same progression as any other class. Equal percentage increase in AC and equivalent increases in damage/delay to keep them above that of warrior/rogue.

Hey Absor, Brothers and Sisters, and other EQ addicts.

I play a 36 monk on the Bertoxxulous server, Pridewalker. Yes I'm not uber levled (yet) But I'd like to throw my buck-fifty into the ring for ideas on improving the monk class

1) Like everyone has said, we're supposed to be the toe-to-toe masters of damage. We're also suppose to be the master of unarmed combat. RoK has made us dependant on expensive weapons, a fact I don't like. It kills rp but if I don't go with the trend and spend a couple dozen K on a Wu's stick/KD combo I can't keep up with the rest of the players my level, my damage output is virtually nothing, and people regard me as a weak character. I know better, but when my fists hit for maybe 35 MAYBE 4 times every 3 and a half seconds, when my SK and warrior friends are hitting double 60s to double 80s every three seconds, I feel weak too. I honestly think a 1 point increase in dmg per five levels, with a 3 point increase at 60, should do the job.

2. Is it me, or are Dual Wield and Second attack not as effective as I'd like them to be? My dual is up to the 190s already, and I still only get two attacks about 60% of the time...I won;t even mention how rarely I get the third, or even fourth attack, with second up to around 170. Maybe this is a lower-level concern, and in the highers people always double and often quad. My concern is that the avg warrior my level almost ALWAYS dual wields. Rogues I know almost ALWAYS quad from behind. I mean, I disagree with triple attack as a solution...heck I'd love it, but that's just too much dmg...I think it'd overbalance us..but let our 2nd attack and dual work a bit more effectively please.

3. I share the concern about FD and hate wipes post 32. Doesn't even make sense realistically...OK you're in a fight and suddenly your heart stops beating, you fall to the ground and go still, faking your own death. Your enemies see this, cheer, and wander off. So maybe 5 minutes later you get up. The badies are at the bar down the street. Suddenly, one of them snaps his head up, and the whole gang runs back at you, suddenly sensing you are alive. Um..I don't think this'd happen in rl. Just please consider making feign actually useful again..sometimes it seems my only recourse is to /q or zone, which in some circumstances is ridiculous.

3. Monk_00 kicks A mob_01 for 85 damage!
Warrior_02 bashes A mob_01 for 3 damage!
A mob_01 is stunned!

OK, does anyone else see anything wrong with that? Heck if I took a flying kick to your face you'd likely be unconcious, or at least seriously stunned!

4. Here's my wacky idea: meditate for monks. Not for mana, but HP. Hear me out please: I've always been under assumption we were the "lone ronin" class, built for soloing. but warriors can solo better than me, due to their higher AC and HP. I won;t comment on that, they deserver higher AC (they do wear plate after all), and maybe hp (they are bigger, tougher, healthier sorts). SO they have less downtime. What I'm suggesting is a meditation skill..heck make it level 40+ skill. You have to sit down, click a med button, and NOT move or fight. if you do so and the skill is successful, you regen double hp. OK I do see two problems with this: Iksar already regen, and there are monks out there with Fungi Tunic. Best suggestion I can give for that is let it only recover 50% more hp for Iksar, or have it change monk sitting hp regen to a flat 10 points/tick or so across the board...and have it NOT stack with other regen effects. I dunno it's a litle over the top, but it's a thought.

4. In the avg solo fight against a yellow HG I get swung at 100 times or so. I get hit 50, 40 times I am missed...10 times I block/dodge/riposte. Um...sometimes I don;t see a block or dodge for 10 fights straight. Seems to me a master of the martial arts can pull off a block a LITTLE more offten then that.

5. Gloves can too change h2h stats..the CF now prove that..if you guys have a model, why not make a few more gloves like that?
*cough*extra point of dmg for treant fists*cough*

I mean, I camped for my Treants over 30 hours, and I'd like to feel I got something worthwhile for that. Or maybe 2 points for Shiverback/Grandmaster's/White Lotus/Silvermoon?

And as a personal favor...more robes! it's ALL about kicking butt and wearing a dress Seriously we could use a few new outfits..and I don't feel it's fair that our "uber robe" the ROTWF, looks like a Buryai legion Gi.
