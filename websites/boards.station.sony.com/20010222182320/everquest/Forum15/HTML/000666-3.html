Grr, okay.

It's not a question of greed. It's a matter of trying to send a message to the developers that we feel there is an imbalance. Ultimately the developers will decide, but Absor needs to know to pass our feelings along before that can happen.

To be honest, no one has a clue how the parts (skill caps, hit/miss tables, etc.) add up to the sum of our offensive abilities. But we are very aware of what the final sum is, and many of us feel it is less than it should be.

Sure, some of it is pride. It's hard to feel like you're making any kind of difference for your group when the warrior is tearing through a mob that you miss half the time, for double the damage you're doing.

But look at it this way: you can have a warrior, who tanks just as well (or probably better) than a paladin, but also does good damage. Or, you can have a paladin who can tank well, but spends half his time taunting with spells, taking away even more from his already low damage output.

The warrior keeps the support classes safe, and helps kill the mobs. The paladin keeps the support classes safe (although not any better than the warrior can), and the fights will last longer, costing more mana (meaning more downtime). Which are you going to choose?

Maybe this is how it's supposed to be, but if that's true paladins should have *better* defense than warriors, not the same. If the mob lives longer because less damage is being done to it, we need to take less damage in return, since we'll be taking damage over a longer period of time.

I think the reason people (myself included) are jumping on the offense bandwagon is because Verant seems to be quite adamant about not making us better damage sponges than warriors, or giving us unique spells that stack with everything -- so offense is the only remaining way we can improve our efficiency. But as long as warriors continue to be _significantly_ more efficient for their groups, paladins will continue to have the problems they do now.
