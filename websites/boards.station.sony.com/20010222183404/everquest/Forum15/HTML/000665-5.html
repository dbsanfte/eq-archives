A very nice list that gets most of the bugs and issues. Just 1 comment on the list items:-

a) Mana Costs - It's already been suggested and possibly even agreed that a better solution for Denon's Desperate Dirge is to put it on a timer rather than a large mana cost. Even with that and the patch upgrade in damage I don't see it being used much, but it's still a decent solution.
The lvl39 charm song is then left as the only mana song; there's an easy way to remove the mana cost while making it much less useful for soloing - just reduce the casting range by >alot<. This is a balance issue in some ways as at medium levels bards charm at least as well as enchanters. The positive adjustment I would like to see is removing the hard level cap on this song.


Also please consider adding the following:-

1) Crowd Control - The suggestion that bards could play a more useful role in crowd control made the enchanter list but not the bard list - it should probably be added here. In truth we do not need much but my suggestion is:-
a) removing level caps on charm and mez.
b) adding an upgraded area-effect mez song (Kelin's Illustrious Lullaby?) that has a fairly high resist rate, say 25% on a white with standard magic resist +/-5% per level difference and adjusted for CHA and MR.
c) adding a limited memory blur song; several options here that do not tread on chanters' toes. Perhaps targetting a party member and lowering their hate on the nearest mob; perhaps targetting a mob and lowering the hate of the most hated foe. In either case something that has a chance to pull a mob off a caster (particularly clerics with the patch :-) ) but falling way short of a full targetted blur.


2) Melee Skills - most bards feel their melee skills need looking into in some way. I'm not asking for an increase with no decrease but perhaps thinking about how the skills currently work would be appropriate. Myself I would take riposte away from bards completely (we're less warrior than other hybrids) while giving a small boost to parry and dodge; I'd even suggest lowering our defense a little again (I don't believe we're as good as warriors). Whatever the final breakdown of these skills an examination of them should be on the list.


3) High-Level Song Effectiveness - The issues of post-50 song damage and haste/slow stacking are really special cases of the general problem that has been posted over and over of bards feeling without a good role in balanced high level groups. It's fine to leave these 2 on the list for emphasis but please also add a more general revision of high-level bards and their roles. It is likely that if the crowd control issue, the haste/slow stacking problem and the damage curve issues are all addressed we will feel useful again but if these are found to be impossible or impinging on other classes' areas (or any other perfectly valid reason) then the general problem will still remain and perhaps some of the many alternative ideas people have had would need further consideration.

That's it for now - I was very disappointed in the patch (I play a bard and enchanter, my partner a cleric) which didn't seem to address the concerns I have seen raised but I hope that is because the bigger concerns are bigger problems taking more time to solve. My feeling is that you will address the issues in time as you have for the most part in recent times. I'm worried that you will bow to the "bigger voices, more pressure" syndrome where the less popular classes' issues take lesser weight. We've seen a >big< increase in druid power in this last patch, the most popular class, please do not forget about us.

(-: Zel :-)

ok it is acknowledged that there are stacking problems with Bardic haste songs and that most folks are at the fully haste limit once they hit the big time levels anyway and get various other buffs and items on them.

Currently a bard can do indirect damage via damage shielding and via hasting.

But with stacking and possibly more importantly hast limit issues one of those channels is not really viable.

I propose a set of 3-4 songs which would scale with level, be sung rather than played on an instrument and would provide a damage enchancement ability - a Damage Sword rather than Damage Shield if you will.

The 3-4 songs would be to allow a fire-based, ice-based or magic based option (if you wanted to make the songs resistable unlike a damage shield - i would suggest strongly that this song not be resistable so that it would be of use on raids and in velious) and would add from 5-10 points of damage (off the top of my head) to each successful melee attack as well as a modest boost to a corresponding resistance.

if a baseline melee attack does 50 points of damage (which at higher levels i'm sure is a low estimate) then a 20% haste is in effect generating 20% or 10 more points of damage over time.

this song would have roughly the same effect - it wouldn't be as strong as a 20% haste because it wouldn't improve the chances of a proc for instance or increase the number of critical hits etc.

But it would be damage that the bard could contribute to the party without diminishing the value of the other buffers and hasters or having any stacking issues.

If a bard were twisting this and a group damage shield while meleeing the overall contribution of damage would be rather high certainly those two songs would be worth a lot more damage than the bard him/herself can do directly.

that would leave one twist for something useful like a resist or ST/DEX buff or whatever for melee situations. Alternatively in the abscence of haste buffs a haste/sword/shield song would be of considerable benefit to the party melee.

I think these songs could be filtered at around level 35 (if you go with a single version of the song unresistable and unenhanceable by element type) or every 5 levels starting at 35 with each song adding some damage or effect based on element.
