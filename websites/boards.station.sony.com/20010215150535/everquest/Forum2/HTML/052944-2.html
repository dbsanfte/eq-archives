In designing my uber encounters, I look at each of the players in my game- and their magic items (including that growth potion they got six months ago) and design the encounter to where using every player and every tool will make it easier. I can never require a particular item be present because by the time they get there they may have drunk the growth potion.

My advice to you is to consider your encounters in this way.

Decide how many players of what levels should be able o beat it and then give each one a role in the encounter. And a note: SINGLE mob encounters never worked well in DnD unless they have a lot of abilities (so really they are a group wrapped up in one monster). Either the mob is too tough or it is too weak. Groups of mobs are always more interesting.

One possible "uber" encounter..

0) some mobs that can be snuck by with invisibility
1) some splittable mobs that can be feign pulled out
2) some unsplitable mobs that can be mezed
3) some unsplitable mobs that cannot be mezed which do not have high MR.
4) some unsplitable mobs which cannot be mezed that DO have high MR.
5) the "boss" mob.

So you could have a throne room with the guards at the door pullable.

The courtiers would be a mixture (which could be told apart) of unsplittable mobs of types 3-4.

And a king or queen to be the boss mob.

---

Then you get tricky.
You make the King and Queen tough but not really the boss mobs- instead, the boss mob is the jester or the bishop.

Then trickier still- some traps in the throne room that can be disabled by rogues.

Layer complexity over the encounter. Buffs on the mobs that can be dispelled. Mobs that cast buffs during the battle so dispell continues to be needed.

Mobs that focus in on certain classes.

Mobs that strip enchantments or cast debuffs.

--

For now your uber encounter seems to be one big mob with a lot of hit points that hits hard.

That's just not very complicated.
