Druids have better teleports to start (29). Wizards take the lead by 39. By 52 wizards have a huge advantage.

Both classes get their first group teleports at level 29. At that level, wizards get GFay, N. Karana, and Toxx, while druids get Butcherblock, N. Karan, Toxx, W. Commons, and Surefall Glade. W. Commons is one of the most popular destinations, so druids have a clear advantage. GFay is slightly better than BBM, though.

Later, druids get The Feerot while wizards get Cazic-Thule. In this case, druids are much better off.

Even later, wizards get lots of translocate spells: to Bind, GFay, Toxx, N. Karana, Dreadlands, W. Commons, Great Divide, Cobolt Scar, Nexus, and Group to Bind.

However, there's a lot more to consider when chosing a class, even if teleports are very important to you. Druids are easier to solo & to get in groups. There are a billion druids on your server already. Wizards have a tough life, but are much sought after by level 60.

Here is some basic information about ports that I posted on another thread a while back.

Gates and Rings:

Wizards and Druids can teleport themselves to a number of specific locations around Norrath using their Gate (Wizards)and Ring (Druid) spells. Wizards port to the Wizard Spires and Wizard Platforms (Pyramids) in the old world and Kunark, and the Dragon Rings in Velious. Druids port to the Druid Rings in the old world and Kunark, and share the Dragon Rings on Velious.

Each location requires a diferent spell. They get the first few at 20th/19th level, and add a few more with each spell circle.

Portals and Circles:

Wizards and Druids can take their entire group with them to the same places when they port (assuming the people are close to the caster)using their Portal (Wizards) and Circle (Druids) spells.

Wizards and Druids get their first group ports starting 10 levels after their first solo port spells. Again each Portal location requires a specific spell, and they add several new locations with each spell circle.

These spells take longer to cast and more mana than solo ports.

Evacs and Succors:

At the same spell circle as they gain their first Portal and Circle spells, they also get their first emergency ports. Evacs (Wizards) and Succors (Druids) allow the caster to use a group port which casts noticably faster than Portals or Circles to get their groups out of trouble fast.

The list of locations where these spells can go is much more limited however.

One disadvantage to these spells is that they have a _small_ chance of leaving someone behind.

Translocates:

Wizards (but not Druids) have one additional type of teleport spell. These are called Translocates (often written "TL"). These spells allow the Wizard to teleport a targetted group member to the same basic places (Spires, Platforms, and Dragon Rings) while he himself stays behind.

Again each location requires a different spell. The Wizard gains his first few TLs at level 39 and adds several more with later spell circles.

At Level 50 a Wizard gains the ability to TL a targetted group member to his (the target's) bind point.

At Level 52 a Wizard gains the ability to TL his entire group to their bind points with one spell.

Note 1: Most Translocates require the Wizard to expend a Portal Fragment. These can only be found in the Temple of Solusek Ro, and they cost something over a Platinum each (depending on his Charisma). These fragments weigh nothing, so it is polite to carry a stack with you and give one or more to the wizard along with your donation.

Note 2: The Target no longer needs to be in the Wizards group for him to successfully cast the TL.

Alter Plane:

Wizards (but again not Druids) also have 2 special group port spells which allow them to teleport their group to the Plane of Sky and the Plane of Hate.

These spells work just like other group ports, except they require the use of components which cost aproximately 200 Platinum.

Luclin and the Nexus:

The major Wizard Spires (Tox, GFay, NK, DL, and GD) now have Nexus Scions at them. If you tell them you wish to "journey to Luclin" they will give you a stone. Aproximately every 15 minutes, anyone within the spires who possess a stone is teleported automatically to the Nexus on Luclin.

From the Nexus, in a ddition to being able to head to the assorted Luclin zones though the use of your feet, you can choose to step to 5 individual portals which each Teleport you to one of the four major Wizard Spires. The Scions there tell you which portal each is. For example the Kunark Scion indicates that portal leads to the Dreadlands Spires. These portals also go off about every 15 minutes.

Nexus and Wizard Spells:

Wizards also have a Nexus Gate, a Nexus Portal and a TL Nexus Spell. Unfortunately these spells only work from Luclin. That is, the Wizard can not teleport to the Nexus from Norrath using these Spells.

However all of the assorted port spells do work from Luclin to their target locations on Norrath.

The only way to Luclin aside from the spires is by being bound on the moon and using the basic caster Gate spell (not to be confused with the assorted Wizard Gate Spells.)

Hope this helps.
