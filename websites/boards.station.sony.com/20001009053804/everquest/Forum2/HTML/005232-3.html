Under the current framework, players are rewarded with the best items the game has to offer when they stay in the same spot for extended periods. I can think of alternative methods of acquiring items that would not require camping; however, they do not fit within the current software framework. For example, make the best items in the game quest-only where the quest requires NO DROP items that are at an appropriate drop rate on several MOBs all over Norrath. I truly believe that the best items and loot in EverQuest should come from Questing and not camping. I don't think camping was a bad first attempt at all. However, after implementation, it appears to reward players for staying in one spot for days or weeks at a time.

Amusing that some lessons are never learned...

Magic: The Gathering (trademarked by Wizards of the Coast, just so they don't get grouchy for the mention) made the same mistake: "It's okay for rare cards to be powerful, they'll be rare".

Bzzzt. Wrong. People will do whatever is necessary to get the "best/most powerful/etc" items/cards/whatever. All "rare" does it create friction over access to the items that everyone feels they "must have" in order to keep up with the Joneses.

Personally, I think the 20-part quest idea makes a hell of a lot more sense. And if it takes a powergame one 18 hour session to complete and a casual person 18 one-hour sessions, who cares? They both get a shot at it, and folks are happier. People will do what it takes to get the items anyway. No sense in pissing people off in the process.

My suggestion to the Verant folks: Implement a few of these 20 one-hour step quests, and see how it goes. Add lots of travel, components in Kunark, OoT, Kerra Isle, Permafrost, etc (places you can't quickly get to via teleport . Create quest "trees", where there are 5 steps to the main quest, but each of those requires 2-4 sub-steps, each of which require a handful of obscure items.

There's nothing wrong with an Epic quest, and you can still keep the storylines. Just requires a bit more work than having the quest require 3 items with 20-100 hour camps each.

A good friend of mine plays a monk, and just about quit EQ over the Robe of the Lost Circle quest. And this is a guy who completed the treant fist quest. Food for thought.
