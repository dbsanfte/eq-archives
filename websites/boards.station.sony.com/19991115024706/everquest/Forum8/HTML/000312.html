In North Freeport there is a weapons shop that sells ore. Buy lots of small blocks of ore (as much as you can by in 2's, they stack) and buy water. Then make metal bits at the forge outside.
Metal Bits = 2 small blocks of ore (not stacked, placed seperatley) + 1 water flask (store bought)
Make metal bits to trivial. Do not sell these back as you will need them for everything you do up until armor. Then make scalers until trivial.
Scalers = 1 metal bit + scaler mold + water flask
Then make lanterns, and follow that with skewers until trivial.
Lanterns = Metal bit + Lantern casting mold + Bottle + Flask of water
Skewers = Metal bit + Skewer mold + Flask of water
Then make armor. thats it. Go to traders holiday and ikthar's for molds.

I wouldnt put any points into blacksmithing. I worked it up to 26 without using any points in it by sharpening. Basically small rusty weapons until 21, then large rusty weapons till 26. Fishing is handy for getting rusty daggers. Use 1 sharpening stone and the rusty weapon in the forge. Took me about 130 sharpening stones to get it up to 26, and I did spread it out over 4 days or so. Overall cost was minimal so about 2 plat because I bought rusty weapons from the merchants and sold back the tarnished (weapons off orcs and fishing means you only spend 6-8 gold on stones to get it to 26).
Charisma does make a big difference. So your cost will vary depending on who you buy from. As an enchanter I found my costs were much lower, though my luck getting my skill up with lanterns is bad of late.

You put 10 points toward the skill that means your skill level is 10. It doesnt matter what level you are. To sharpen weapons you put the weapon and the stone into a forge and hit combine. As for whether or not it is a good idea to put points in, it all depends on whther or not you want to spend an hour gaining those points or just go ahead and get the freebees over with by putting points to it when you level. Why not? You really only need to put one point toward any skill so that you "learn the basics" and can gain skill points thereafter much easier. However, throwing 20 points into a trade skill saves you the time and money it would cost to earn those points otherwise. At 20 you need to make metal bits and or sharpen weapons.
