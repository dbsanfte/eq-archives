ITEM: Healing

as stated before, a 39th level cleric should *NOT*, under *ANY* circumstance be a better healer than a level 60 shaman (or druid), let alone two or more shamans (or druids).

my guild has called off four raids in recent history (last couple weeks) because we didn't have enough clerics. this is despite the fact that there was at least 3-4 druids and 3-4 shamans online, but only 2-3 clerics.

the taunt factor of complete heal is also *far* too low. i can do /assist healing with superior heal on people and get pummeled on the next aggro check for the mob. but a cleric can complete heal several times on several targets and never get attacked. i've seen this on trakanon, zlandicar, several named mobs in plane of growth. often times all the druids and shamans are dead at the end of the fight but the clerics all lived. in reality, complete heal should be one of the highest taunts in the game.

ITEM: DoTs and corpse poofing

because of this bug, my guild has *FORBIDDEN* the use of *ALL* DoT spells and effects on raids. it doesn't matter if this is "better" or "fixed", we will get immediately booted from a raid if we use DoTs. if it weren't for this, shamans could add thousands of damage with pox, bane, plague and envenomed bolt. necromancers could add thousands as well with their DoTs.

let's look at some numbers here:

group buffs -

lets look at these numbers carefully. what do we see? well, i see that there isn't much difference between the amount from the talismans (group buffs) and the 34-39 buffs. in fact, the largest difference is between the dexterity buffs at a factor of 10. also, the difference between the jaundiced bone armor buffs and the 51+ single target buffs just isn't enough to justify spending mana. any shaman that can cast all of the group buffs should be fully equipped in jaundiced bone armor except the breastplate (not applicable here) and greaves (casts 34th level stamina buff, health). many shamans will have the spirit weavers breastplate which casts riotous health.

there is no compelling reason for any shaman to cast the mana using spells, either single target or group versions when they can right click their armor for no mana cost at all. certainly using the group buffs saves time, but think about this for a moment. stamina is the only stat buff we have that is beneficial to all classes. everything else is limited in use. strength benefits only melee/hybrids, agility benefits only those with <75 AGI, dexterity only benefits those who process weapon effects or are learning weapon skills and charisma only benefits enchanters and bards (remember, enchanters have their *OWN* charisma buffs - yet shamans have no way to buff wis other than bear form).

compound to the fact that other than stamina (and to a lesser extent agility) all the stats shamans can buff are meaningless, stat buffs just aren't a worthwhile way for us to spend our mana and time. perhaps if buffing STR had the same effect of directly buffing ATK... perhaps if buffing AGI improved the chance to block/parry/riposte/dodge... perhaps if dexterity improved chance to hit... perhaps if enchanters didn't have their own charisma buff... perhaps *THEN* stat buffs would be useful and wanted. but as it stands now, stat buffs are merely a "junk" buff used in front of real buffs when encountering mobs that dispell (nagafen, for example), or for a placebo effect so those tanks think they're more effective.

Hmm….this is going to make me unpopular with some...
I would like to propose that certain ‘sacred cows’ of class balance be reconsidered. There is a statement you hear over and over..."We don’t want to cut into the role of X as primary X (cleric = healer, warrior = tank, etc). I want to reconsider this issue.

1) It has been amply stated that at high levels, clerics and warriors (and in non-Velious zones, enchanters) are assured a place in every group and that the other slots are fought over by the remaining classes. The perception that is created is that The Vision™ only has a place for clerics and warriors at the high levels, and the other classes were just added as an afterthought to pacify those who found the ‘true classes’ too restrictive.
2) A lot of time is being put into discussing minor adjustments to the other classes, so as to make them useful enough to be considered for groups,..BUT THEY ARE OPERATING FROM THE ASSUMPTION THAT THESE CHANGES SHOULD NOT ALLOW THEM TO DISPLACE ANY OF THE CORE (WARRIOR, CLERIC, ENCHANTER) MEMBERS. The operating assumption here is that warriors and clerics are guaranteed a place in a group, but that the other classes are optional and can be dispensed with.

I believe this approach is in error. If true class balance were to occur, a group would need to be made up of healers, tanks and casters. BUT Rangers and Paladins should be able to fill in for warriors, perhaps at lesser efficiency (with rangers maybe doing more damage, but tanking less well or paladins tanking as well, but doing less damage). Or the shamans and druids could fill in for the cleric, with INCREASED DOWNTIME, but NOT a complete inability of the group to operate. This reduced efficiency SHOULD be able to be compensated for by a good group (as is true at lower levels).

In other words...WHY ARE THE CLERICS AND WARRIORS ROLE IN A GROUP CONSIDERED TO BE SACRED AND UNREPLACABLE?


The obvious (knee-jerk) response is...We don’t want to cut down on their usefulness to a group.

But this response only makes sense if there is something special about clerics and warriors that makes them MORE DESERVING of being in a group (aside from the skills they bring to the group). And I don’t see why that should be so. YOU CANNOT HAVE A SITUATION WHERE ALL CLASSES ARE EQUALLY DESIRABLE, AS LONG AS YOU HAVE CLASSES WITH A PROTECTED STATUS THAT CANNOT BE REDUCED. As long as every group must have one of each of a few classes, the remaining classes, BY DEFINITION, must be less desirable to the groups. To make ALL the classes balanced (and not just the less desirable ones) requires either increasing the abilities of the ‘secondary classes’ (EVEN IF THAT MEANS THEY MAY ECLIPSE THOSE OF THE SACRED CLASSES) or nerfing the core classes (which is neither my intent, nor is it feasible, as the mobs are balanced to groups containing these classes, at their current ability levels).


Or, to put it another way..

Goal: Each class should be as close to equally valuable to a large raiding party. The decision of which class to take (as long as the essential roles of tank, healer and caster are filled) should have MORE to do with HOW WELL THE PLAYER PLAYS HIS CLASS, then with the specific class of healer, tank or caster.

Current Situation: Certain classes are essential (warrior, cleric, enchanter (some areas)), other classes are useful (Shaman, Bard, Rogue) and others have little to add (Necro). If you need a healer (at high levels), you are VASTLY better off with a cleric played by some barely adequate E-bayer, then you are with two shamans played by the best players to ever play a shaman.

Fix: Stop ‘protecting’ the status of the essential classes by making sure that the classes with secondary abilities are useless at the highest levels. Sure…Clerics should always be better healers, but shamans and druids SHOULD be able to do it in a pinch (and a slow heal spell that heals the equivalent of ONE hit by the mob is NOT adequate, and neither is one that significantly reduces the tanks effectiveness (torpor). Warriors will always be better healers, but Rangers and Paladins SHOULD be able to stand up to the highest level mobs, with the party changing tactics some to compensate (more healing needed or more caster damage needed).

In the post 50 game, VI nerfed Shaman capabilities, resulting in a Shaman class that is but a shadow of its former self. For example:

Issue: Slow spells are the most effective indirect healing capability that Shaman have, IMO, in the current game. However, if not able to be landed until an enchanter has finished crowd control duties and is sufficiently satisfied that a mob won't agro on them, we're not likely to be able to stick the spell until the second half of the fight (once the fight is "safe" for the group. To be useful, we need to be able to land the spell early, BEFORE the chanter has finished crowd control. This would allow us to HELP keep both the chanter and tank alive...

Recommendation: Shaman need a usable (read low resist) MR or combo (like the Malo line) debuff rather than the useless one we have now. This would allow us to land our Turgurs line and give us a viable role in the "high level" party. If your and the "balance gods" feel that Turgurs is too overwhelming, then provide a lower effect slow spell that is low resist. This is key!

2) Buffs: Shaman currently get moderate capability AC/HP buffs, "the best" stat buffs, the same resist buffs as all the clerical classes, and low powered haste buffs (usually 2 or more generations behind the chanter). However, you have referred to Shaman as the "king of buffs". Can you develop some reasonable rationale for your position? Realistically, Shaman are the "kings" of stat buffs, on par or inferior to or on par with other classes for the majority of other PHYSICAL buffs. Enchanters have been the "kings" of mental buffs (e.g., MR, WIS, INT), but for some reason VI has seen fit to also make them the kings of haste (which would seem to be physical to me).

Issue: Stat buffs have been shown, time and again, to be nearly useless in effect.
