A few comments and ideas...

Warriors are extremely item dependent. This is neither good nor bad, but it's true. While other classes sift through their spellbook AND their item collection, we can only prepare differently for a battle by changing out equipment.

This is compounded by the fact that we have absolutely no variety in the skills we use during a combat... Taunt, Slam(if you have it or until kick stuns), Disarm (for the hell of it, might aggro the MOB more). Positioning our character and timing the use of these scarce few skills is ALL we have to differentiate ourselves from other warriors.

Since the taunt skill itself is fairly useless at higher levels, this also means that the only effective method of taunting is through weapon selection. This forces higher level warriors into a narrower selection out of the pool of weapons out there, which sadly leaves many of the better two handed weapons unused (have you EVER heard a warrior seriously consider using the epic as the two handed version?).

So without proposing an exact solution, here's some things that could help:

Give big hits more staying power on the MOBs hate list. Make two handed weapons viable at higher levels, and make the bigger hitting, higer delay one handers useful.

Create some variety to the taunt skill. Give us more strategic choices. Introduce a higher taunt that has a negative such as an attack debuff, introduce a faster one that has less chance of working... Who knows, it's up to you to determine what has the least impact to the game engine, but as this is our class defining skill (keeping the MOBs on us) it would be nice to have some variety to it.

One other idea... a few folks have been requesting that certain shields be back equipable. I have a better idea that kills severalb birds with one stone:

Introduce a skill in the late 40's/early 50's that allows a warrior to equip a shield WITH a two handed weapon.

Should be easy to implement, the graphic is already empty. Would be like dual wield - once you have the skill you can put the item there. This would add a HUGE amount of variety to the viable equipment for warriors. It would allow non-large race warriors to bash without a major tradeoff in damage, and it would give a hefty AC boost to make up for the damage and taunt issues of two handed weapons. I also think it's hard to claim that this would be unbalancing, as the only benefit is adding AC, and there is a built in penalty by using the lower damage output and taunt capability of the two handers.

OK, I hear alot of Warriors saying that they don't want an AoE taunt because it might be dangerous in certain situations. Maybe you aren't getting the concept here.

Most skills/spells can be dangerous in certain situations. AoE spells require very specific circumstances in order to be useful. More often than not, you can get killed using them. AoE taunt would be somewhat the same, except you would WANT them to attack you (hence, TAUNT). Get it. If you don't want everything around you attacking you, you don't use the AoE taunt.

I also noticed that most of you seem to think that the only place a Warrior will ever fight is in an enclosed area (Dungeon, Castle, etc). In other words, if you have 2 MOBs attacking your Cleric, it would allow you to stand right next to him and taunt both at the same time instead of trying to get them one at a time.

Again, this is a Skill. Every skill requires a little bit of practice to figure out it's usefullness.
