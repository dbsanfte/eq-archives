Coming from someone who actually has a knowledge of the Asian martial arts, and from a more realistic role-playing perspective, I think it would make more sense to allow monk characters to CHOOSE a more defensive or offensive focus when they first create a character. My reasoning behind this suggestion follows: most martial arts have either a "defensive" focus (i.e., aikido) or an "offensive" focus (i.e., karate, tae kwon do, five animals fist, etc.). Why not allow players to choose their skills focus (their "style", if you will), effectively allowing more powerful offensive or defensive effects without making the monk class too powerful.
Of course, this may just be wishful thinking, as making such sweeping changes to the class may be too difficult this late in the game. Of course, you could always add more "martial artist" classes/subclasses in a later release...

A few things to consider when trying to decide if Monks SHOULD be equal or better than rogues in offense.

Monks attack, they can't tank and their only added abilities beyond attacking are Feign Death and Instill doubt. Monk defenses are not superior to hardly any of the melee classes.

The only bonus to playing a monk would seem to be that you are not equipment dependant, except now with these Kunark and Velious "improvements" we are completely dependant on equipment to keep our damage output high enough to make us worth grouping with.

How do you feel if you are a class that cant tank, is not spectacular in defense and is ranked 3rd in damage and only has 2 other abilities to play with besides punching people?

You feel like a monk.

My suggestion is make us less equipment dependant. I love the idea that a previous poster stated, give us the ability to choose a superior fighting style or a superior defensive style. You could even make it like the Magician focus quests for their pets.

The sash and headband quests are horrible. They are in line with the original vision of everquest when all things magical were supposed to be very rare. It just turns out that a fantasy world with all magic items was quite boring. Now that magic is proliforating through the land, they really need to be revamped.

Human monk versus Iksar monk. I have to question the viability of human monks. Their only apparent bonus is the exp penalty being absent.
Please compare the two:
1) Iksar. 3pt regen bonus, infravision, superior newbie grounds, superior armor quests and equipment. Inferior faction adjustments, small exp penalty.
2) Human. No exp penalty. can't see at night penalty.

I can't stress how much regen matters to any melee class. You may think that not having infravision is not a penalty, but it is. When you choose a class that does not have slef infravision in some manner, it increases the difficulty of the game extraordinarily.

So the iksar really has 3 bonuses and 2 penalties while the human has 1 bonus and 1 penalty. Doesn't sound so bad till you look at the actual bonuses and penalties. Faction can be modified and worked around. Getting an item with infravision is NOT easy. My human monk is lvl 23 and still doesn't have one, it is very hard to play at night. And considering the frequency of "bad weather" it is practically night time 75% of the time in EQ.

These are inbalances within the monk class itself. Human monks are just too inferior to Iksar, the Iksar even get better stats..the ones that matter to the Monk anyway. I've heard rumor that human monk skill caps are a little higher than Iksar skill caps. If that is so, that is a nice bonus....once you are in your 40's??? that's a bit of a long time to wait for something to show itself.

So I ask that you not only look at balancing the monk versus other classes, but the monk versus itself as well.

Another thing to look at. All mobs get a defensive bonus when they frenzy. I really find this offensive in the fact that it seems targeted toward starting trains and basically requiring you to have someone who can snare. For an example, take any character and go fight a mob that is a tough fight, but that you can solo. Now, watch what happens when the mob gets to 2 bars of life. Suddenly it starts dishing out higher damage more frequently, now watch what happens when the mob decides to run. You start missing horribly. I have chaced mobs for very long distances because suddenly my blows just mysteriously stopped landing.

All classes except the warrior suffer AGI penalties when they drop below a certain amount of life, this lowers their AC and skills. Warriors seem immune to that effect as does every single mob in the game, from snow bunnies to giants. This really needs to be looked at. Maybe I am just taking a dark view of things, but its only purpose seems to be to allow the mob a higher chance to get away and alert others, causing a train and death for the party. This is true even when the mob is snared, it turns to flee, cant run and you STILL can't hit the sucker.

Suggestion: If you can't up our damage and you can't up our defense, then how about upping our accuracy in attacks? Yes this makes us do more damage, but in quantity not in individual strikes...just a thought.


Vordai.

A few points

Stun effect - i feel that a stun of some sort should be available to ALL classes when they attack with a bare hand or kick. Implemented much as the critial attack is for warriors. This will lessen the "need" a lot of young monks seem to have to acquiring weapons as soon as possible and never using fists. Would also be good for ALL classes while doing a naked corpse recovery run. The stun effect would ONLY occur on those hands or feet USED in combat and would NOT happen if a weapon was weilded (one weapon would mean your empty hand has a chance though of performing a stun). The stun effect could be implemented so it only worked once per 3 fights on average (or something similar). It shouldn't be a monk only ability though as everyone has a random chance in real life of landing a perfect punch while flailing madly :)

Triple Attack
This is an interesting notion and would certainly fit into the roleplay aspect of a blindingly fast martial artist :) However some would see it as giving us an instant 50% boost to our attack damage. To counter that the triple attack would be an automatic ability that is only used if BOTH hands are empty (make it a high level skill too so that its only gained at maybe 45). Weilding one or two weapons would negate the triple attack and we'd just be double attacking like normal. Also while triple attack is in use we could expend stamina faster (since we'd be more energetic) and once stamina is down to 20% (1 bub) left we'd stop triple attacking and only SINGLE attack (not double!). From a roleplay point of view this would equate to a fighter being so worn out they'd have one hand on their knee/leg bent over slightly catching their breathe but still able to swing with their free arm. It could become the monk equivalent of OOM in long fights ;) OOS!

Mend Other
I'd love to see us given the chance, once our mend level is at say 150 (since most skills don't seem to actually improve at all above 100 lets make use of that extra 100 points that we earn :) that we gain a Mend Other ability. This skill would be a seperate skill (instead of adding a target feature to our mend... it would be too dangerous and slow to a player that way) but it would recycle perhaps every 10min instead of 6 and while its recycling we CAN'T use mend on ourselves. So we can use only one at a time and long recycle times mean we aren't a threat to healers in groups, it just becomes an emergency heal that can be applied. Roleplay wise it would be the conditioned martial artist able to gather and project lifeforce/chi to heal externally (someone else) rather than internally (for ourselves).

Disarm
Really, what is the point of this skill at the moment! Mines around 200 and after a dozen disarm attempts on a lowly orc it still has its weapon (and dies to my riposte (yippee a skill at works :)) Make disarm work more effectively.. pretty much a guaranteed disarm on a sub lv10 mob for a lv50-60 char... through to a difficult chance for even cons up to a slim chance for red cons. I think at present its not possible to disarm certain weapons (magic? stat?) from mobs which would be understandable. However add a fumble effect instead.. so if its not possible to disarm a certain weapon but the disarm skill "works" then the mob (and players in a duel) will fumble their attack and lose that attack round (or just that attack) while the opponant regains a grip on their weapon again. It's not quite a full stun effect in that they can still attack with their second hand if they dual weild and still kick. Not sure if it would be something to cause a caster to stop though, perhaps only if the caster is weilding an item.

Thunderkick
You land a thunderous kick for 8 points of damage. More like a whimper. Thunderkick should have a minimum damage level of perhaps 50-80 so that it can still do more or less than a normal kick but will do a guaranteed amount.. after all the thunderkick is applied on our first SUCCESSFUL kick after using the skill, so the kick has already landed and now just needs the bonus Thunderkick applies to it. Disciplines have long recycle times so they should also be better than a regular skill since they are added onto the regular skills. Perhaps Thunderkick = Flying Kick damage (of the successful attack) + 50-80HP damage.

Well, in amongst all the fluff this will probably never get read, but hey, you never know (THROWING crits ffs? where are these people from!).

I am going to make a few statements as I see the vision for the monk class's role in everquest, which you may disagree with perhaps, and show the problems that this creates between the melee classes.

1) Rogues are intended to be the incarnation of damage. No-one should do as much damage as a rogue who can backstab) in the high endgame, and thus require evade so they can do this.

2) Warriors are intended to be THE toughest toe-to toe opponents. This is further exacerbated by /disc defensive at 55, which leaves them as the ONLY ones able to tank large scale encounters which were balanced against warriors max buffed using /disc defensive in the first place. Hybrids don't like this, but its not per-se a problem as the intention is for hybrids to do less damage than warriors anyway in pure melee, though hybrids have many other issues.

3) Monks. Now, with low HP and no taunt, but moderate ac and good defensive skills, monks are not tanks, cheal efficiency dictates this. The way they are setup is to be more damaging than the warrior but less than the rogue (I think this is the case and should be the case.) HOWEVER, with high end warrior weapons and the fact that basically their special attack decreases in significance dramatically in levels past 30 as an overall damage contributor, this is not necessarily the case. The problem is that in high end encounters Monks have no genuine way to remove agro from themselves *or* survive (well, unless you count an *8 sec* survival duration 72 minute reuse discipline ).

The points about special attacks and min damages on FK/TK, combo strikes or offhand bouses is valid, I think and this is the REAL source (apart from the skill cap convergence at 50+). As as an overall percentage of your damage output FK is at a guess twice (if not more) as significant at L30 as it is at L50. (ie: FK is dominated by its magic number (58) and its minimum (3!) at all levels. Unlike backstab, which is the tool through which rogue's damage superiority is achieved, it does not scale with weapon damage or *anything*, and thus as main autoattack damage output goes up its significance overall deteriorates fast).

The weapon dependence of monks is something that is not liked, but somewhat inevitable in expansions since the grass has to be lusher to get the sheep to cross the river (or at least look it :P). Doesn't really fit, but alas that's too late.

This is what I believes motivates alot of the suggested changes in the more thoughtful posts, and is NOT a class envy post (even though I mention monks and warriors), but determining where monks fit into the game. (The designers ideas about roles and players are not necessarily the same. ie: I play a L52 shaman too, and I can tell you catagorically his PRIME role is debuffing, and buffs are nice but secondary.)

Now. The question is do you agree with this vision of the role of the monk in the game, and the rationale as to where monks fall away from this in the higher levels (which is the source of most of these requests)?

The monk class is far from balanced from my view point. And is one of the few classes that are in need of balancing at all levels, not just post-50.

As stated in many threads, the issues of dealing damage and surviving damage are in need of some tuning. I beg to differ on the point of being allowed one or the other but not both. Monks should always be able to dish out slightly more overall damage then a warrior of a comparable level and gear, and at the same time must have a defensive value that is slightly less effective then wearing full heavy armor. That is what equates a monk and a warrior as equals on the battlefield.

Warriors are tanks. They have the AC to survive being hit, and the hit points to endure this for a given length of time. It is their job to taunt and take the damage for the group, and that's it.

Monks, like rogues, are not tanks. We are more like special forces then shock troops.

The only way a monk survives is through their defensive skills i.e. not being hit in the first place. Monks have a large aggro factor, in fact one of the largest for any melee class. When hit, sure we should rightfully take more damage because we have less physical AC. But we should be rarely hit, because we are much more mobile then even a rogue. That is the whole point of martial arts in the first place: avoidance of damage by any means necessary, including the ability to strike fast, hard, and lethally.

However, there is one thing that has got to be changed. Feign death must continue to be a viable group skill post-32 for pulling, else monks have no place, no niche, and no reason to be on any raid ever. Without feign death, the monk is a mana waster for clerics that should only need to concentrate on healing the primary tank.

The only single reason monks were ever invited on dragon and plane raids was to feign pull. That's all we had, and that's all we will ever have. Breaking spawns is the monks role in a group. I'm not saying we should be able to walk through any place without fear, due to some over-powered ability. Just that Verant should accept this as a smart tactic for players, and then plan on its use.

Causing the enemy forces to break off from one another in order to manage the fight to one's advantage, has always been, and shall always be one of the smartest strategic uses of force that can be devised. It is the mainstay for "Guerilla Warfare", in other words, when fighting an enemy that is larger, stronger, or better equipped then oneself. Feign death pulling can be seen as diversionary tactics, one of the first things any tactician learns. Fight smart, and one will always win over brute force.

I didn't create my monk to be some delusional warrior that doesn't believe in armor. I created him to be a puller. My very first EQ character was a Monk. I have been playing a monk a very long time, before most people even knew there was feign pulling. And pulling has always been the key reason to staying with this class.

Melee is not ever about standing toe to toe with something. That is moronic. Melee is a fluid motion, always moving always changing. This is why the warrior class appeals less to me then the monk. I still want to be in the fray, but I desire to have more then just "auto-attack on."

Monks do pay for all these things already. We simply do not have the hp base to survive any hard hitting or lengthy encounter. Not even with mend.

Hail My Brothers and Sisters

I have been lurking and thinking on the subject of finding the yin/yang of our class. Props to all of the folks who have posted lots of great ideas. I think that what Verant really wants to provide us (monks but all players in general) with are choices. Choices that we make, that effect which niche we fulfill. The basic reasoning is that if the decision making is placed in our hands then more issues become internal discussions within our community rather than rants directed towards Verant which promotes dissatisfaction with the game.
So in that light, my suggestion is to allow players to select disciplines in which they can specialize. Specialization in a discipline could do various things either decrease the reuse time of the discipline or enhance the discipline itself or both. Each of the disciplines that we have:

Resistant
Fearless
StoneStance
ThunderKick
WhirlWind
Voidance
InnerFlame
100Fist
SilentFist
AshenHand

Help to deal with the challenges that are presented to us. And, they help to define what momentary niche we fill. Specialization would be a individual reflection of what you want to be. Each of us has a different impression of what that niche is, which is why we have so many varying opinions as to what changes should be made to the class. I say let Verant set the limits, but we make the decision of what we become. What those enhancements to the disciplines should be, I shall leave to debate.
