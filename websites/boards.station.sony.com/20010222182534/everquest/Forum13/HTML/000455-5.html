I have seen countless posts comparing Monk offense/damage and Warrior offense/damage. Monks and Warriors are comparable on their current offensive skills. The biggest point of difference comes from their defensive abilities. Warriors are built to absorb a great deal of damage (highest hit points). Monks *should* be built to avoid a great deal of damage (improved dodge, improved block, etc). FD doesn't avoid damage ... it avoids combat all together.

The problem is that Warriors and Monks seem to have nearly the same defensive skills (Defense, Dodge, Parry/Block, Riposte, etc.) throughout their entire lives. The monk has a *natural* boost to AC when using almost no armor and carrying no equipment, the warrior is more than able to compensate for this by using massive amounts of armor without the carrying capacity restrictions.

Since the focus always seems to be increasing the damage output for monks, they will also be greatly increasing their taunt factor. The Warriors are built to deal with a MOB swinging at them for extended periods of time ... Monks are not.

There should be more focus placed on the avoidance of damage for Monks. They would be able to fill their *nich* by avoiding being hit more often, while the Warrior would continue to fill their *nich* by absorbing damage.

If you want to balance Monks and allow them to be a viable Melee class, look at improving their defensive abilities.

here is a paste from an EXCELLENT article about TAUNTING and HATE LISTS and why monks need much more damage output to make up for their lack of TAUNT skill

remember... im not asking for a TAUNT skill... im simply pointing out the fact that it is a DEFICIENCY for our class that makes us less desirable for grouping

if you dont believe me please read carefully:

---------------------

How Hate is calculated:
This is something most tanks and casters understand. Hate for melee is generally based on two variables, the
speed of the weapon and the potential damage of the weapon (This is why quad misses taunt just as well as
quad max's). Casters also deal with this in the form of spells in addition to worrying about effects that create
extra hate. Snare, stun, debuffs, etc.

How Taunt works:
Taunt (when successful) seems to have a few rules binding it. It will either take the taunter to just beyond
the current highest person on the hate list, or if the difference is too great, will increase their position on the
hate list a certain number of points. This is just speculation based on observation of playing this game since
launch. It may be inaccurate, but observations seem to fit with taunt working in this manner.

Taunting off of another melee'r:
In this scenario, lets assume that only two tanks are in the fight. No other casters will make an appearance.
(This isn't all that uncommon in exp fighting since no in combat heals will be needed). Tank one is the
designated meat shield. Tank two is not taunting (or can't), but his/her weapon configuration is such that
he/she will generate more hate over time than tank one. The fight starts, assuming tank one pulled (I've
observed that the first one on the hate list seems to get a hate bonus, this may be false), tank one will be
taking the hits. Eventually however, tank two will surpass tank one on the hate list. The mob switches over to
tank two. At this point, tank one taunts the mob. But the difference in hate between the two tanks is fairly
small, therefor taunt doesn't increase tank one's hate very much. Very soon after, the mob switches back to
tank two, where it will stay until taunt ungreys. Repeat until tank two or the mob is dead.

Taunting off of a Caster:
While casters do get added to the hate list in pretty much the same way melee's do, the pattern of those
hate increases is vastly different. Melee adds hate steadily in small chunks, casting adds occasionally in large
chunks. Lets assume the same scenario above, but substitute tank two for a caster. The tank has just
engaged in combat. The caster being an overzealous wizard lets say, casts a fairly big nuke. The wizard has
just surpassed the warrior in the hate list by a big margin. Luckily, the warrior taunts successfully, bringing him
past the wizards position in the hate list by increasing his hate rating by a significant amount. The wizard isn't
likely to be stupid enough to cast another nuke right away, so it is likely that taunt will be available for the
next time the wizard surpasses the hate of the warrior.

Taunting in a party situation:
Lets bring tank two from the first situation and add him into the second. Tank one pulls, Tank two surpasses
tank one. Tank one doesn't taunt because he knows that the wizard will probably nuke early again. True to his
nature, the wizard pops in a big nuke, surpassing even tank two by a large margin. Tank one now taunts, and
surpasses the wizard by a small margin and tank two by a large margin. Tank two will be fairly safe from
attack until the difference in hate generation between his/herself and tank one accumulates enough to
surpass tank one's hate.

Party without Taunts:
This is what monks are worried about. Monks don't have a way to jump up the hate scale rapidly since they
do not have taunt. If the wizard were to cast early with only a monk around to pull it off, that monk can only
get the agro back if his/her potential damage surpasses the amount of potential damage that nuke had. Now
lets plop in say 1500 hate points that complete heal would generate on a monk (assuming 1hp - 1 hatepoint).
Will the monk be able to generate that much hate fast enough? Hopefully the monk was already at 1200 hate
before that cast, otherwise the cleric may be dead.


Taunting is so much more than playing whack-a-mole with the taunt key. Taunting involves knowing what the
wield and when to push that taunt button. Getting the mob back off a secondary tank with taunt right when
you see A_Wizard_24 casting ice comet in the first 30 seconds of the fight isn't a good use for it. Know that
when you're the only warrior in a group, putting away your wurmslayer and grabbing that SSoY will make you
a better asset to your group.
