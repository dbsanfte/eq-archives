On aggro:

Verant is right on the money with aggro. The more damage (direct or indirect) you do, the more angry the mob will be with you. Wizards should have to manage that fact. There are ways to deal with aggro both solo and in groups. I've never liked the idea of reduced aggro spells. Wizards should be powerful nukers and MOBs being attacked should hate them.

Aggro should be based on damage done, not potential damage. If I were a MOB getting whipped by a warrior, I don't think I would be all that concerned about that wimpy wizard who I just resisted his Ice Comet for 2 points.

A critical dd hit for a wizard is a TERRIFIC IDEA!

A 5% might be low. I only say that because of the number of spells that a wizard throws on average per hour. If you're a warrior then a critical hit occuring 1 out of every 100 or more hits might be ok because you are hitting thousands of times per hour. For every 100 swings of a warrior, the wizard casts far fewer dd spells. I'm not sure the median number, but for the sake of argument, let's say it was 5. It would then follow that the wizard critical hit should be a percentage 20 times more than the warrior.

I don't know the exact numbers, but I encourage Verant to do the math and balance the percentages appropriately. This is a great idea. But if it only goes off once in an 8 hours session then it will be a dud.

I think this is a great idea. I don't think it fixes everything, but what does? It'd be a nice little extra feature and I've always wished for some kind of critical blast with a variable upper end on the damage.

I wouldn't worry in the slightest on the increased aggro. Already as wizards we have to learn to deal with aggro and just because a foe gets mad at us for a few seconds doesn't mean we're going to instantly die. This happens all the time. Wizards are able to draw foe attention and survive. You let the tanks taunt a foe off, you back off for a little while, you hit the foe with Concussion, you allow another caster to blast to distract, and you use your Skin lines.

I ALWAYS have a skin line up. It's well worth the price. Prior 52nd I always kept Steelskin up, and since 52nd I almost always have Manaskin up. Yes it's expensive but I find it well worth it. It allows me to take the accidental aggro hit or two, concussion the foe, and go on with business as usual.

You can cast an ice comet on a foe without aggro'ing it. You can cast 2 ice comets on a foe without aggro'ing it. Extra damage doesn't automatically make a foe angry, particularly if everyone else is doing their job and you're good at timing.

It's true if you're already pushing the edge on aggro and you get a surprising extra hard hit you may put yourself in danger, but I think that'd add to the excitement. I fully support this idea.

Allowing this to go on a stat like Dex would be a huge boon given how many caster items have this stat, and how we were originally given the impression Dex would be a meaningful stat for a spellcaster. Dexterity so far as I can tell plays 0 role with a wizard the way the game is presently designed, so that'd be a nice thing to have. (Yes, I am a gnome and I do have over 100 dex, so maybe I'm biased!)

Gordon, there is no obvious logical reason why the critical cast should generate more aggro than the regular cast. As far as I know, spells generate a fixed aggro, and a fully resisted spell generates the same fixed amount of aggro as a spell doing full damage; so, why should a spell doing more-than-full damage generate more aggro than the fixed amount ???

But you probably assume that the enhancement must be give-and-take. If it turns out that the enhancement HAS to be give-and-take (extra aggro for critical casts), then DON’T DO IT. It’s part of a Wizard’s basic job to contro aggro (either in order to pass unnoticed or to provoke), and that kind of unpredictability would be a major pain in the neck. But, must the enhancement be give-and-take ? Hmmm...

Let’s separate two very different cases: pre-50 and post-50. Oops, I mean pre-50 MOBS and post-50 MOBS, not pre-50 Wizards and post-50 Wizards. First of all, are you able to code a chance for critical cast which depends on the level of the target mob, instead of depending on the level of the Wizard ?

Pre-50 mobs: let’s admit it, Wizards, whatever their level, do quite enough damage on pre-50 mobs. It would be COOL if the critical cast feature was added (it would confirm Wizards as magical mirror images of Warriors), but it would be essentially nice-to-have, and, if you do it, give a very small chance, far less than 5% as you said; it’s OK, just for the rare fun of it (but no added aggro).

Post-50 mobs: there is this emerging issue of Wizards losing their power against post-50 mobs because of rocketing mob hit points. The happiest (?) of post-50 Wizards (I mean those who fight 51+ mobs, not those who limit themselves to bullying mobs in their high 40s) seem to be those who have accepted good-heartedly the challenge of making AE damage spells their MAIN OFFENSE. But there’s something very wrong with the idea (as I hear them say) that using DD on post-50 mobs is "a waste". Apart fom this minority showing much goodwill, players won’t forgive you for forcing them into that kind of diluted, non-flexible, low-sting AE fighting, and you will have increasing complaints. This critical cast thing can be a solution, if you give a rocketing chance for critical cast when casting on a level 51+ mob, to follow somewhat the mob hit point curve; possibly to the point when the critical cast becomes something to be relied on; I wouldn’t be surprised, considering the current spell stats, if the DD spells actually needed a chance for critical cast (potentially doubling the damage) of about 50% when cast on high 50s mobs.
This could make sense in roleplaying, if you pretend that Wizard DD inherently resonates with the life energy level of their target, and tend to burst into critical damage on the mobs having the highest life energy (i.e. mobs of level 51+).
At any rate, keep a fixed aggro for the spells; you can possibly raise somewhat the fixed aggro of some higher level spells, depending on how often you think they will generate critical casts; but no variable aggro, or forget the “enhancement“.

Oops, last note: I hope critical casts are for DD only, not AE. Pre-50 AE damage spells are bad (I mean, two spells out of three are bad), and need a much deeper fix than the addition of critical casts. Post-50 AE (except AlKabor) is good, and there’s nothing to add.

The nature of the aggro of spells is still a little bit of a mystery and I don't think we've ever figured it out exactly. I'm just posting in reference to a previous post where it was indicated all spells aggro the same, which just isn't true.

The amount of damage does seem to factor into aggro, although I'm not sure exactly how. But I definitely have in the past modulated aggro by casting many smaller nukes vs. a larger one, and if you go to a small enough nuke you can chain-cast without drawing a foe's attention if there are others involved in the battle.

This said, I don't think it's the most important factor and I don't think it'll immediately make the wizard a smear of blood on the ground the second he rolls a critical hit. I can hit a foe with an ice comet and not get attacked and I can hit a foe with a Draught and not get attacked, yet the IC is almost twice the damage of the Draught.

I think there is something that happens when a spell is resisted, it tends to have highest possible aggro. I think maybe this counts as a different "category" of spell. This would be hard to test, but I have a feeling an Ice Comet of 1120 damage does less aggro than a full resisted Ice Comet. I would be willing to bet the resisted spell goes in the "extremely aggro non-damaging spell" category, similar to a stun or a major debuff.

Any wizard who has nuked alongside an enchanter will tell you enchanter nukes have huge aggro, most likely due to the 0 second stun component. I think this spell basically counts as TWO spells for aggro... One as a "high aggro non damage" and then also aggro for the damage itself.

I once was helping a considerably lower level enchanter fighting hill giants. I was casting Ice Comets and he was casting something like a 300 damage range DD. Without exception the hill giant was generally chasing the enchanter instead of me, unless I cast 2 times.

We are all aware of roots and snares being relatively high aggro, along with the shaman slow debuffs, enchanter mesmorization & charm, etc. Some of these DO seem to be variable, however. I think charm aggros more than root, etc. No one knows exactly how these equations work, and it's a very hard thing to test.

But I do remain convinced that on a basic DD which is just damage, the higher damage will cause more aggro, but I also think the critical hit concept will NOT cause that large of a problem, particularly if wizards are already prepared to deal with aggro...

A 48 level wizard is casting a 600+ point DD, conflagration. He gets Ice Comet and is suddenly casting an 1100+ damage DD, almost a 100% improvement. Yet most wizards don't just immediately die because of this. You still space out your blasts. Yes this could cause some additional aggro at a bad time, but I say this is EXACTLY what happens when a spell is resisted, which is also an unforeseen circumstance...

If I'm fighting a foe with my usual mix of stuns & DD, I sometimes get a nasty surprise. I toss a stun out early, with the intention of hitting with Concussion after... The stun is RESISTED, making the foe very angry... He runs at me, hits my Manaskin a couple times, I cast Concussion on him, and he goes back about his business, then I DD him once I feel the tanks have gotten his attention properly.

Having the critical hit would be similar, but at least have the very substantial advantage of DAMAGING the foe, even more than usual. I'd very happily take some added aggro for hurting a foe more vs. NOT hurting the foe at all because of a resist and still having to endure increased aggro.
