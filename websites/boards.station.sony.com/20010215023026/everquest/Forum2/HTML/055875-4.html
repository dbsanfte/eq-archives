As a Paladin of 31 seasons, I cannot speak to the issues of those 50+. It seems there are adequate voices for those issues anyway, with many well-thought posts.

I have a few additions, and a couple different suggestions.

**Equipment**
1. Absor said, on the other thread, "I don't think one set of armor makes a balance issue." (this was in reference to the DeepWater armor, and the ineffective spell effects on the various pieces). Absor, it is not one set of armor. It is *every* set of Paladin armor, at every stage of our careers.

I cannot obtain my Armor of Ro, though I have been trying for levels now. I get stuck on the granite, every time. I simply cannot survive Castle Mistmoore, even in a group of people of similar levels. The only way I may achieve this component is to hope some higher level characters will take pity on me and babysit me long enough to loot a few pieces. On the other hand, I can obtain any piece of crafted I want, at any time, with minimal aid. Others have covered the tedious nature of the components needed for each piece. Crafted is a cake walk in comparison. And I need not ruin my faction with good races, whom I should protect, in order to get the crafted armor.

DeepWater armor comparisons and Valorium comparisons have been made before, in cogent posts, and I will not repeat them here. The end result, though, Absor is that our armor is sad at every point in our lives. It is not merely one set, it is every set.

2. Weapons: I received a Ghoulbane as a legacy from a noble Paladin who left the game, otherwise I could not in good conscience wield it. Verant claims to encourage role play, yet this class-defining weapon, required for our later class-defining weapons, requires us to slay a Knight who has done no wrong. And, it is alone in its field for 1hs weapons for us for many levels. There is no reason to rehash the other posts on the dearth of decent 1hs weapons, except to say that I agree. I also would like to see nice deity/race-specific weapons if possible (the Falchion of the Koada'Vie rots in my bank, it is a useless weapon which I obtained because it seemed the proper thing for a High Elf Paladin to do).

3. Shields: Again, many of us see a Knight in Shining Armor, complete with sword and shield, when envision a Paladin. The shield serves as added armor and also aids us in interrupting casters, with a bit of gratuitous damage thrown in. We need more of them, with effects like other classes receive for their offhand weapons or range weapons (not comparable of course to those special weapons for those classes by any means, but similar in balance to objects casters can hold in otherwise unused slots perhaps). As above, some more deity/race-specific variety would be welcome as well.

**Abilities**
1. Offense/Defense: Many good posts on this balance issue. I tend to side with those who would increase our defensive capabilities to be on a par with warriors, and a modest increase in offense to allow us to wield weapons effectively seems in order as well.

2. Lay On Hands: "Their greatest skill is Laying of Hands, which can only be used once per day, but will heal a character completely." That is a direct quote from the Official Player's Guide. When did Verant lose that Vision? One complete heal, once an EQ day, does not replace any class in any way. Of course, one Donal's Breastplate can replace one Paladin many times over, if this were truly to be our class-defining ability. And it will not take a slot in a group either.

**New Proposed Abilities**

I have a few suggestions, based on my knowledge of historical and fantasy-genre Knights:

1. Shield Snatch: This skill would require a shield be equipped. The purpose of the maneuver is to foul your opponent's shield/defense with your own shield, to allow easier infliction of damage on the foe. When successful, the Paladin's own shield would be unavailable for the duration of the snatch (no ac, no bash, etc.). The target's defense and ac would be similarly reduced, so that blows landing on the target landed a bit better and did greater damage.

2. Rally: A Paladin is a forceful being, beloved by her deity. This skill/discipline would allow her to rally her group mates in the face of danger, raising their morale and giving them the courage to face the battle. It would act as a group-effect Resist Fear of some nature (or some suitable group resist bonus), with perhaps a small atk boost thrown in, for the duration. It could even be further enhanced depending on the deity the Paladin follows (additional small damage shield versus undead for Rodcet Nife, for example).

3. Call Deity: Paladins are dedicated servants of their gods, and are given certain special abilities to reflect that special relationship. Perhaps that can be expanded on in some fasion, allowing them to call on their deity once a day for some form of aid. This could be something that got better with levels (e.g., at 1-9, you received a small heal of some sort; 10-19, a lower-level clerical dd spell would hit your current foe; etc.). I am not speaking of the deity themself coming to your aid, or the granting of some super-powered ability, just a small showing of the gods' love that could prove useful. These could again be tailored to the deity served.

That was all I could think of for the moment, and I thank each of you who have taken the time to post thoughtful ideas and read what others have had to say.

I'm also ignoring the 3 or so AC from the 18Agi added with the Tolan's armor.

Both of the hybrid classes also have self-buffs to enhance their AC values. The best/only self-buff for a paladin is currently Yaulp-III (lvl 56), which would add 12AC to the paladins total. All of the other buffs are sharable / lower level versions of clerical spells (I'll ignore them for the sake of this arguement since they are sharable). Rangers on the other hand have the following self-only buffs to increase their AC: Call of Earth (lvl 50) which would add around 24AC to their total plus Thorncoat which would also add around 32AC. Both of those are self-only and I'll ignore the "Skin like line" (lower level Druid buff which isn't self-only) for the same reasons I ignored the sharable buffs above within the Paladin spell line.

These self-only buffs allow a ranger to add around an extra 56AC to their total AC whereas the Paladin would only be able to add 12AC to their total. After Equipment + Self-only buffs, the ranger is actually ahead by 17AC. The only way a Paladin makes up for this is through the higher Defense skill. Paladins do not get self-only buffs to raise Attack to bring them closer to Rangers/Warriors either. So, while Rangers get a couple of really nice spells to help offset their weakness (defense), Paladins do not get any spells to offset theirs (offense).

So, for the loss of Dual Wield, lower Offense and Weapon Skill caps the Paladin class gets a lower AC rating compared to Warriors in contrast to Rangers who get the Same Weapons Skills, Same Offensive skill, but only a lower Defense Skill compared to Warriors. I guess what I'm getting at is that I just don't see the parity here.
