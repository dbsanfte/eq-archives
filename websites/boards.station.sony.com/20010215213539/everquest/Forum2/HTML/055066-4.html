Its hard to add to what people have already written here. While Grimstaff and I have disagreed on amy things over the years, starting back on the old Graffe board, I think his post pretty much sums up the current issues.

Unfortunately I think Wizards suffer from something no one can do anything about. The fact is, we are the only class that only does damage when we have mana. Because of balance issues, mana is a closely guarded resource by Varent. Increasing mana supply across the board would change everything immensely. Add to this that Wizards are only damage dealers, and you end up with wizards being useless when they are out of mana, which is almost all the time at high levels.

There is no debating that that amount of damage we can contribute to a night of fighting has become much less than all the other damage dealing classes, mostly because of the lack of mana. The efficiency of other damage dealing classes (like warriors, rangers, rogues, etc) are very good because mana from support classes gets turned into HP at a very good ratio. Thus a single cleric supporting a warrior will yield far more damage per hour than the mana spent supporting a wizard. Not to mention that there are few ways to feed mana to wizards.

So..

1. Wizards are a pure damage dealing class
2. We render far less damage in a night than other damage dealing classes.
3. The mana to HP spells are very efficient, and the HP to damage ratio (from fighters and pets) is far more efficient than pure mana to damage ratios (wizards).
4. While this was more balanced before the level 50 expansion, even back then we were on the bottom of the spectrum.
5. Ergo, wizards are not very useful at high levels.

I have read how wizards are multi-dimensional, and a well played wizard can still be a help if played well in certain situations. But we can no longer do enough damage in a short amount of time to make much difference to fights. We also cannot do enough damage over a long period of time for a class whose only skill is damage dealing. Thus, we become second class citizens.

Someone spoke well above... if you moved a couple of ports to druids, you could delete the wizard class and there would be no effect in the gameplay of Everquest. That cannot be said of any other class, and it is a sad thing.

I started my wizard on the day Innoruuk opened and have held my account ever since. Most of the wizards I have known for years are now gone or retired. It is a sad thing. One by one I have seen them disappear from the "/who wizard x y all" list. I have other classes and I play them for fun, but the one I am most attached to is Dilvish. My many friends make it easy on me and I am welcome in any endevor we try, but I know deep down inside that it is their love for Dilvish that matters, not my contributions to battles.

Now to the crux, my recommendation.

I think the spells we have are great in their damage dealing variety. The lures, draughts and 3 kinds of damage spells (magic, fire and ice) are also very good. The only thing that stops us from consistantly doing damage is our (total mana) * (dmg ratio) * (mana regen). There are two solutions, but only one which can be made and not effect the balance. The first one would be to increase our mana, or our mana regen by a huge number in high levels. This I think would probably be unbalancing, as it would allow us the ability to do too much damage too quickly and would turn soloing wizards into power leveling machines and break balance.

The second solution, and the only one which makes sense, so to add two new sets of spells, one at 50 and one at 55.

1. These spells would cover the usual fire, ice and magic ranges.
2. The set at 55 would be lure type spells.
3. They would have fairly long casts, but not too long.
4. They would do medium damage, nothing stellar
5. They would cost VERY little mana. So little mana that we could consistantly fight all night.
6. They would be designed so we would do damage similar to warriors over the same time period. We are, after all, the magic version of warriors.
7. So the casting time, damage, lure qualities and mana usage would need to be balanced to provide this consistant damage.

This would solve the issues of wizards without making us more powerful soloers. You can't solo with that type of spell. You need to make the spells be root breakers 100 percent of the time, and add a special effect to the spell that is added to a buff on the mob which stops them from being rootable. Then make the cast time long enough that a bonded mob will reach you before you can cast it. So if you were soloing and you used one of these spells, the mob would be unrootable (for some time), and since the time it takes to cast the spells is too long, the mob will reach you before you can re-cast. Thus the spells could not be used for soloing. But in groups and large battles we could consistantly use the spells throughout the night, doing equivelent damage as warriors (but slightly less to adjust for that fact that warriors are having their life artifically extended via healing).

If they make this one change, wziards will become viable again. But please note this... these spells must require an extremely small amount of mana for this to rebalance wizards. I am not talking a 5 percent increase in effiency, but a huge jump in efficiency.

When I did my initial post on this division of the thread, I tried to follow Verant's apparent and explicitly stated ideas of the Wizard class and balance.

As such, some things I'd like to see or consider a balance issue I left off or moved to other areas. Damage is one of the primary problems we face in terms of balance, as is the basic conceptual problem of starting out weak and ending up the same as everyone else.

Balance in its very core nature requires that everyone have roughly the same opportunities and a proportional level of difficulties, weaknesses and drawbacks as each other class. No one class can have more drawbacks than any other without a corresponding set of advantages to the other classes. No one class can have clear advantage in any skill or ability or opportunity over other classes without corresponding drawbacks and limitations.

This, then, is the task that Verant has before them, to examine each class with as fresh and unbiassed eye as possible, setting aside all prejudice or previous experiences with the class in an attempt to see them evenly and properly.

In terms of the conceptual design of the Wizard class, we have a distinct and obvious problem. Verant tried to follow the AD&D standard of starting out weak and growing in power, weaker at the beginning than those around them, more powerful at the end than those around them. Even before Kunark came out, this was not the result of the Wizard class, they were never significantly more powerful than those around them. Further, the power level increase was limited to the very end of the Wizard's career, a small handful of levels. The expansions have only made this failure more apparent.


However, the stated intent of Verant is to have balance, for all classes to have a similar level of raw power and ability at the end certainly and throughout the classes presumably (based on the recent experience changes).

The Wizard class, then, is fundamentally in opposition to this concept - one cannot have a class that starts weak and ends up no more powerful than any other under this paradigm. The choices are to raise the power level of the Wizard at the end of its career to be clearly superior in raw power and ability to the others (to compensate for earlier weakness and a long road to travel); or to make the Wizard more powerful and survivable at the earlier levels.

In terms of damage the picture is less clear. Unfortunately, to get a clear picture of the damage inequities it is impossible to show without a host of numbers, mathematical comparisons and most of all class comparisons by level. Since none of these appear to be welcomed by anyone at Verant, I will try to avoid this as much as possible.

The concept of Wizards as we have learned from a variety of posts on here and comments other places by the Verant representatives has followed a basic pattern. Wizards are not meant to be literally more powerful and deadly using every kind of magic than every other class in every situation.

Wizards are said to be the masters of fast damage, putting the hurt on the creature more furiously than any other class.


for party members. Once a Wizard is out of mana, they contribute nothing to a group. The damage, then, is designed to be potent enough in the immediate that the down time waiting for mana to come back is acceptible to a group.

Wizards are designed to have no lasting effect in a fight. The damage is instant, no real buffs This is most apparent in an emergency, when something bad happens the Wizard is conceived to unleash their superior, fast damage to save the group by ending the lives of the opposition.

We then have two questions here:

Do Wizards actually put damage out more powerfully and efficiently in the short term than other classes. Not simply at the end of their career, but is this a trend and design that carries through and builds over time?

Is the concept of having a class built around fast damage and down time to pay for this a valid design for Everquest, especially as it has evolved to at this point?


The damage a Wizard must do to be able to carry this task out, fast and powerful damage, must have two obvious characteristics: be fast and be powerful.

Wizard damage must be more powerful than any other class can offer in the same time frame, or the entire design and concept falls apart. In the time it takes a Wizard to blast something, it cannot be possible for anything to rival that damage, against any target, or a Wizard not only does not do what they are designed to do, nor is any help to a group.

The questions for Verant are these:

-How much damage must a Wizard do on a single blast to contribute properly and effectively to a group?
-How much damage must a Wizard do with his entire bar of mana to be effective to a group and be therefore balanced?
-The damage that is done is affected by resistances. Is a Wizard able to contribute properly in the current design in terms of types of damage across levels and in terms of resistances faced?

note here: the post 34 resistance reduction for Wizards and staff of temperate flux were added in under a correct understanding by Verant that Wizards required help to do their job. The first is almost unnoticable at best, and the second is so minor and insignificant to be meaningless. Looking at these to review their impact and design would be useful with this kind of balancing discussion.

In addition, Wizards have 3 types of damage because they are supposed to be able to target their damage type against resistances, to take advantage of what creatures resist and do not. However, Wizards need to go back as many as 15 levels to find a spell that will be of a given type, in effect negating the benefit this is supposed to give. This, too, should be considered in a balance discussion.

Pets are considered a mob by the spell). If there is no pet, and only one mob, that level 52 spell will do up to 1800 damage if all three waves hit. This is over time as well, so it's not as bad a taunt as most of our DD spells.

So, with this 1800 damage spell at level 52, it seems on the surface that we have the roots of what WOULD be a good level of class balance. If this were made into a "only save once" effect over the duration of the rain, we would see up to 1800 damage, and not just 0, 600, 1200, and 1800 damage done by this line of spells. Partial resists are rare, it's generally all or nothing per wave. If this line were made into a single mob spell, that would move with the mob, it would work like a DoT with a different look, but would provide the damage that would balance our class.

1800 damage is what we look for when we think of being the masters of magical damage. We don't mind it not being a DD, but I at least would like the spell to stick with the solo mob I'm trying to kill. Note that a change to the whole rain line to what I have described would NOT allow this to be used in quad kiting.

The second issue is the speed of damage.

This damage must be faster than others, it must be an amount of damage that will resolve and take effect before others can equal that much damage. From starting to cast until the blast going off, nothing and no one should be able to equal that damage.

However, here we get to a hazy point. How long must this damage be superior and efficient before it no longer is defensible for Wizards? Should this carry over only a single blast? Should it be over 2, 3, 10? How many times should a Wizard have this superiority in order to be useful to a group and be powerful enough for balance?

The questions in this for Verant are these:

-Is Wizard damage fast enough to take effect as desired?
-How many rounds of damage does this have to be true for the Wizard to contribute to the group and fulfil the concept of the class?
-Does this speed exceed the damage that other classes can offer their damage within the range of attacks above?
-How much of a factor does and should mana efficiency take effect here?

Note: Wizards are outgunned in a single blast at some levels. While the damage offered by a Wizard is faster by a few tenths of a second than other classes, the damage is less. Is this acceptible? If the damage is supposed to be more than one blast for a Wizard, then ANY class having more powerful blasts or bolts should be reviewed. The answer to this is obviously for Wizards to get at least the same damage faster and more mana efficiently - against any type of target - not to lower anyone else's damage, since they are balanced based on their present abilities.

Another thing to consider is that the additional abilities of other classes, such as pets and damage shields, DOTs and combat ability must be factored in to determine balance. To leave out anyone's ability assumes you take the Wizard at their best and the other classes at their average or less than best abilities. Balance cannot be achieved in this manner.

A last comment on damage is this: The Wizard damage must compensate for lacking other grouping benefits, and take into account the ability to teleport others in an emergency. Non combat teleports should not be considered in damage assessment because they are two separate settings and do not influence each other. The fact that a class can move to another place quickly in the game has no bearing on the benefit they give a group while gaining experience and in killing.

Non Combat abilities need to be balanced against non combat abilities, and combat abilities against combat abilities.
