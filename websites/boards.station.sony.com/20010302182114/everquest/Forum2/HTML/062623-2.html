I dunno. As a former usability professional (I recovered from that drug induced nightmare) I'm all for making software easier to use. I'm also against making it so damned annoying that expert users curse your name (re: the microsoft paperclip guy).

Actions that have irrevocable (or nigh-irrevocable, is that a word?) and dire consequences should prompt the user for confirmation assuming they are so rare that you hardly ever do them. Eating a potato chip is an irrevocable (heh don’t say it) action, but relatively common and the consequences aren't that bad. Deleting a hard drive partition is a relatively rare action that has some pretty steep consequences, that’s why you type in the volume name before you do it.

Can we think of a real reason you’d want to attack a mega-guildmaster NPCs? For that matter, can you think of a real reason that a level 14 PC would want to attack a Griffin? Or anyone jump off a cliff? Drop your favorite magic item on the ground?

So far Verant has only been willing to provide confirmation boxes for things that can really ruin your day. Accidentally looting a nodrop quest item your guild has worked days at getting is a bad thing. So is dropping your favorite magic item because it fell through a hole in the UI (this actually happened to me). People do accidentally attack the wrong NPCs all the time. But Verant has decided that it’s not a big deal, so they don’t provide a confirmation box for it. -- As much as it sucks to be on the wrong end of it (yeah, I’ve done it too), I think they made the right choice.
