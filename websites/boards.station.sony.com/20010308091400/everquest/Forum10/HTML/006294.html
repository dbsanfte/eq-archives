Tentative Charter for the new guild follows. Yes, It was lifted heavily from the charter of Siebwen Dekan. Comments are appreciated.

--------------

We started as a guild on one of the official EverQuest forums known as The Newbie Zone. This forum can be found on the official web site of the on-line multi-player fantasy game, EverQuest, located at www.everquest.com. All guildmembers are strongly urged to read this site at least once a week, and active participation is appreciated. We owe much to our predecessors of Seiban Dekan, and all guildmembers should visit their website once to meet these marvellous people. The official web address of the Seibwen Dekan is http://www.luclin.com/SeibwenDekan. Their web address is case sensitive. Much of this charter is lifted directly from theirs- Thank you Naked Newbie for allowing this.

Player and Character

It is important to understand the difference between a player of EverQuest and a character of EverQuest. A player of EQ is the real life person who plays the game EverQuest using their own personal computer and Internet connection. A character of EQ is a figment of a player's imagination and is that player's persona and adventurer. There can only be one player, but that one player may have an endless amount of characters.

Roleplaying

The TNZ/Enoz Eibwen (TNZ/EE) is considered a roleplaying guild. The characters that we play are based on roleplaying. This is a concept where creativity and fantasy come from our minds and our hearts. All player characters who are a part of our guild should have some knowledge of what roleplaying is and how it works within the game EverQuest. From day one, a player is to think of their character's background and life story and then effectively act it out while on-line in the world known as Norrath. When dealing with other player characters (PC) inside and especially outside our guild, it is highly recommended that the player roleplays to the best of his or her own ability with the other PCs. As a whole, we do not roleplay non-stop amongst ourselves. It should be noted that continuous roleplaying on-line can burn a player out. Although roleplaying is a requirement, it is not required every moment you are on-line. But whenever you are interacting with an on-line EQ stranger, it is required that you fully roleplay because you all are a representative of our guild. It should also be noted and understood that a player does not go too far or overboard with his or her roleplaying style. Roleplaying must never defy anything within the charter of the TNZ/Enoz Eibwen, or the End User Licensing Agreement (EULA) of Sony/Verant's on-line multi-player fantasy game known as EverQuest. Roleplaying is never an excuse for being rude.

Family Goals

The primary goal of the TNZ/Enoz Eibwen family is to promote roleplaying within the game of EverQuest and to assist naked newbies whenever we can. It is not a goal to seek out every person out there and take away our own time for this. It is understood that all of us have the right to hunt on our own or as a group. We all have the right to not go out of our way to help others. No one may require you to help them. We all cannot play 24 hours a day to level up our characters. Therefore, it is a goal for all of us to occasionally set aside time for helping others rather than hunting. If any player character of TNZ/Enoz Eibwen does a service for any other player character of TNZ/Enoz Eibwen, absolutely no payment must be given or accepted. The receiver of service must not offer any form of payment and the giver of service must not accept any form of payment. We help our family members out for free absolutely. However, it is required to say the universal two words of appreciation and gratitude, “thank you”. Roleplaying is a goal that must prevail above all else. We must all be creative and we must act out our own character's life in game. Whenever we interact with each other, roleplaying is highly encouraged. Whenever we interact with others outside our family, roleplaying is required. It should be understood that we all cannot roleplay non-stop. Too much roleplaying can produce stress upon a player. There is nothing wrong with taking a break and interacting with fellow SD members out of character.

It must be known that there are no beggars in the TNZ/Enoz Eibwen family. A beggar refuses to work hard, and a person who refuses to work hard has no place within our family.

Player Character Initiation

It is required that all players of EverQuest who wish to join the TNZ/Enoz Eibwen family must pass a handful of steps before becoming an official regular member of our guild. From here on, any unique EQ player who wishes to join the Seibwen Dekan family shall be referred as the “candidate”. There is no minimum level requirement to become a regular member of TNZ/Enoz Eibwen.

1.All candidates must visit the official EverQuest The Newbie Zone forum prior to joining. They should read our charter and agree to its terms. The candidate must read thoroughly this charter prior to joining and if any questions arise concerning our charter, the candidate is strongly recommended to contact the guild master or any officer of TNZ/EE. The candidate must also fully understand and accept the charter prior to joining.
2.All candidates must have two unique players, who are already active members of the guild, nominate the candidate for membership. The purpose of nominations is to familiarize the candidate with our guild and to allow the candidate to interact with two unique players who are already members of our guild. This gives the nominating members a chance to get to know the candidate better. A primary goal of the nominations is to hopefully eliminate time-wasting, ridiculous, and illegitimate initiations. Once the candidate has two unique players who are active members willing to nominate the candidate, the two nominating members must notify the guild master (GM) or any officer personally to officially nominate the candidate. The candidate is not allowed to officially announce who nominated him or her. The nominations must directly come from the two unique players who are active members.
3. The GM or the officers reserve the right to cancel or deny regular membership into the our family. Only the GM or any officer may officially invite a candidate into TNZ/EE Once a candidate becomes an official regular member of TNZ/EE, the new member must abide by the guild charter for as long as he or she remains a member.
4. Once a unique player has been successfully initiated and invited into the TNZ/EE family, all characters of that player are automatically initiated and invited to join TNZ/EE. No nominations and initiation quests are necessary or required for all future characters of a player who is already an official regular member or high rank of TNZ/EE.


The Rules of TNZ/EE

Note: Rules containing * may result in immediate ejection from TNZ/EE if violated. All other rules will result in at least one warning of ejection if violated.

1.*All player characters of TNZ/EE must abide by the guild charter. All PCs in general must abide by the official EULA of Sony/Verant. If ever an issue arises between this guild charter and the EULA of Sony/Verant, then the Sony/Verant EULA shall prevail and override the guild charter.
2.All Xev server players of EverQuest who hold a valid and paid EQ account may be eligible to join the TNZ/EE family. The TNZ/EE family does not discriminate real life players based on age, gender, handicap, race, color, national origin, ethnic background, or physical location. The TNZ/EE family does not discriminate EverQuest characters based on level, race, class, or deity.
3.The personal real life of all players in EverQuest must remain private and undisclosed unless the player chooses on their own free will and without coercion to reveal anything. No one is ever required to reveal personal or sensitive information about themselves. If a player tries to harass another player into giving out personal information, the offender will be ejected.
4.*Exploitation of bugs within the game of EverQuest is a violation of the EULA of Sony/Verant. A bug is an obvious in game error resulting from the programming code of the server and/or the client. For example, let's say you kill a very low level mob and it drops a high valued item that it would not normally drop. The correct procedure would be to immediately report the bug by /petition or email. If you purposely do it again, you are exploiting a bug of the game and the GMs of Sony/Verant may ban you from EverQuest.
5.*Kill stealing is not roleplaying at all and is not acceptable within TNZ/EE. If anyone accidentally kill steals, immediately notify the victim and apologize to him or her.
6.*Scamming another player character inside or outside TNZ/EE is not allowed for any reason. If any TNZ/EE player is proven guilty to even attempt a scam but was not successful, that player will be ejected immediately.
7.No player of TNZ/EE shall put another player into what is known as tell hell. “Tell hell” is to send numerous tells to another player without respecting the other player's right to play on-line without being bothered constantly. However, if the receiving player accepts your tells and does not feel bothered, then this rule has not been violated.
8.All TNZ/EE players shall never do anything that would tarnish the reputation of the TNZ/EE family as a whole or any individual player in or out of TNZ/EE. This includes but is not limited to libel, slander, ridicule, or any false expressions of any kind.
9.*Sexual harassment of any kind will result in immediate ejection. Think before you speak or act. Think of the other person first before you say or do something that may offend the other person.
10.If any TNZ/EE character does a service for another TNZ/EE character, no payment of any kind shall be offered or accepted by both parties. All services performed by a TNZ/EE player character are done absolutely free of charge to another TNZ/EE player character. To clarify, if a TNZ/EE player character performs a service to anyone outside TNZ/EE, then donations or gifts are allowed to be accepted.
11.No one is ever required to go out of their way for another. You have the right to decline assistance or service to another player character who requests it from you. We must all understand that we all have the right to our own fair amount of hunting time. However, if you are known to constantly decline services requested of you, then you are defeating the goals of our family and you may be ejected.
12.One of the biggest problems of EverQuest is that spamming is rampant and plagues us everywhere. We must always try our very best to never spam the /shout, /ooc, /auction, and /guildsay channels. Do not have a conversation with one player using /shout, /ooc, /auction, or /guildsay when the /tell channel can easily be used. One of our goals is to limit spamming if we cannot eliminate it. The TNZ/EE shall not gain a reputation as a spamming guild.
13.Twinking is allowed within TNZ/EE.
14.No one in the TNZ/EE family may beg for money, armor, or weapons from anyone inside or outside the guild. Our family shall never produce beggars to be let loose throughout Norrath. However, if someone gives you a gift on their own free will, that is fully acceptable. For example, let's say your character is a young wizard and you really don't have much. A stranger comes along and offers to give you the Robe of the Oracle as a gift. By all means, that would be acceptable. But to go out and start asking people for handouts is a violation of this rule and this family. Beggars must be given nothing at all.
15.No one is obligated to stay with the TNZ/EE family for any minimum amount of time. Any current TNZ/EE player may leave the guild without any reason and does so on their own free will. However, the guild officers or the GM reserve the right to at least ask the departing member the reason for leaving. The departing member also reserves the right to leave without giving reason.
16. Swearing will not be tolerated in guild chat.

Character Promotion

Only the Guild Master of TNZ/EE may promote a regular member to officer status. Promotions are based upon the following:

Your pride of being a member of our family.
Your goals that you wish to accomplish for our family.
Your personality as a person, not necessarily as a character.
Your respect and honor for others inside our family and outside as well.
Your roleplaying ability and skill.
Your communication skills.
Your length of time as an active member. Having more seniority does not matter.
Your devotion to the game as a whole and to our family.
Your character's experience level.

Arbitration

If any player of TNZ/EE has a problem or issue of any kind, please contact an officer or the GM of the guild immediately. The high ranks must be fair to everyone at all times. There shall be no favoritism and all of us are equal. “Officer” or “GM” is merely a title, but in actuality we all are the same as individuals. No one is better than the other.

If you have a problem with any member of TNZ/EEor any issue you wish to resolve with our guild, please contact a high rank immediately. Please treat the high rank the same way you wish to be treated. Explain carefully your problem or issue to the high rank. Email is a recommended form of communication because the /tell channel is limited. Always understand that we are all human beings playing an on-line fantasy game. Nothing is to be taken too seriously even though many of us are serious players.

One important note: If a player is causing a problem for you and you rather not choose to reveal who the offender is, then in all honesty and fairness, nothing much can be done. This is due to the fact that no action can be taken against the offender if the offender is not known.

After grieving your problem or issue, please give the high rank a fair amount of time to act and resolve the matter. Although it is the duty of the high rank to resolve matters as quickly as possible, things still do take time.
