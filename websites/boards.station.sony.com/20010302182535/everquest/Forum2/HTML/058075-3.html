Mad Cow Disease and the Skyfire Evac

Disclaimer: I'm aware that it is debatable whether or not evac locations are considered wizard 'class balance' issues. But just in case they are, here you go:

Ok being the sick EQ addict that I am, I noticed a strange similarity today between the logic behind the skyfire evac location and proponents against the ban of blood donations from US citizens having been in Europe in the 80s and 90s.

Just in case you're not up on the latest mad cow disease issue:

Mad cow disease, at least according to the US press, is spreading in Europe. This has led to concerns about blood donations to the Red Cross from US citizens having extended stays (a month or longer) in Europe during the 80s and 90s.

Here's where my sick addiction to EQ gets involved. I noticed that one of the proponents AGAINST this ban suggested that there are, “…many judgements that can lead to many outcomes in this issue [the blood ban], and that no single judgement is correct.” This person went on to defend her position against the ban, primarily because the ban will cause major blood supply shortages.

Now, according to this person, it should be allowable for those who are possibly infected with mad cow disease to donate blood to the Red Cross. But think about this. Why do people need other people's blood? Usually, because they are in a life and death situation, and external blood is needed to sustain their life. But if the blood is infected with yet another disease that will end your life anyway, then what is the point?

The purpose of an evac is to move the group out of harms way in an emergency, not put them in an even worse situation, much like a blood transfusion is supposed to save your life, not end it in the near future.

Well, I sighed, as this type of logic (or lack of) seems pervasive in all things, real or virtual. It’s no wonder then that certain game design concepts are implemented the way they are. At least a flawed game design implementation based on questionable logic won’t kill you.
