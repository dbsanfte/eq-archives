Mordenkanen,

You lose so much experence when you die because of the intense amount of experence you gain on a hell level. The loss of experence is always a percentage of your total experence. Using the below as an example, because of the HUGE amount of experence gained at level 30 compared to whats required at 31, your exp "loss" at 31 is only visually disappointing. Its not a different percentage based loss.

I dont guarantee the following is correct, but it sure seems like it --

Experence to complete a level is done by the following formula (High Elf Cleric for example):

exp needed = Level^3 * 100 (CLR Modifier) * 10 (HEF Modifier) * Level Modifier

Level Modifier RISES at level 30, 35, 40, and 45, and I think each level 51 and after. Therefore at level 1, use this formula:

1^3 * 100 (Race Modifier) * 10 (Class Modifier) * 1.0 = 1,000 XP needed

Hell levels work like this:

Level 28: 28^3*100*10*1.0 = 21,952,000 Exp
Level 29: 29^3*100*10*1.0 = 24,389,000 Exp (Dif of 2,437,000)
Level 30: 30^3*100*10*1.1 = 29,700,000 Exp (Dif of 5,311,000)
Level 31: 31^3*100*10*1.1 = 32,770,100 Exp (Dif of 3,070,100)

Thats how hell levels work, therefore what you are seeing on 31 is a large VISUAL loss, but your actual loss is static percentagewise based on your race/class. You require more than 2 times your experence to get through 30 than you do at 29.

The way experence is with a warrior/rogue at level 50 vs. a high elf cleric at 50 (XP requirements) are:

50 Wood Elf WAR = 50^3*100*9*1.4 = 157,500,000 XP
50 Dwarf ROG = 50^3*100*9.05*1.4 = 158,375,000 XP
50 High Elf CLR = 50^3*100*10*1.4= 175,000,000 XP

Therefore, with them not changing the modifier, the Cleric requires a lot more work to get to level 50 than the warrior or rogue, HOWEVER, in my *PERSONAL OPINION* with the headaches of downtime, traveltime, etc involved with a warrior/rogue (No regen HP, rare bind spots, etc), Their bonus doesnt bother me, but thats just me.

I think racial penalties should continue to be shared across the group. Unlike hybrid penalties(which had no relationship to grouping value), racial penalties DO indeed help the group.

Racial exp penalties should be shared for 2 reasons:

1)Non-exp-penalty races get hurt in pickup groups.

A Troll shaman will benefit the group more than a Barb will.
An Iskar monk offers more to the group than a human monk.
An ogre warrior helps the group more than a gnome warrior.

In all of these cases, it is adventageous for one to select the exp-penalty race in their group even though they would have to share some of that exp. The better races will lower group downtime and thus the group will get more exp overall, negating the exp loss to other members of the group.

This makes xp penalty races MORE popular to group with(at least in pick-up groups) than non-penalty races. This is because the person without the exp penalty gets the benefit of grouping with the exp-penalty of the race, but no downside.

Why group with a human monk over an iskar? Before, the iskar would take some of your exp, but his natural regen and AC bonuses made it worth it. But with these changes, there is no reason not to choose an iskar over a human to group with, since there is no trade-off for grouping with one. The iskar benefits you more, but now you don't have to pay for the benefit you are getting.

If I'm a human monk, I now bring no advantage to a pick-up group over an Iskar.

I have one char that I only play once a week with 5 friends. This is a dungeon-crawling, well balanced group. It does everything Verant expects people to do under "The Vision".
