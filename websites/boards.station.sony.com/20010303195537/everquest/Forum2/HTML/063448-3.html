It sounds like Verant is making an honest effort here, to clarify both what they expect of the players, and what the players can expect of them. That's obviously a good thing, and I take it as an encouraging sign.

I am of course, like everyone else, slightly hung up on the concept of deleting characters for what are considered to be serious name violations. It's not clear to me why it's necessary to be so confrontational about it. Possibly the thinking is that people will say "hey, if the worst that can happen is they'll change my name when they catch me, why not go for it?", so they feel there needs to be more of a penalty.

That's understandable enough. But no matter how smart you are, or how much you're certain that something is obvious - it is possible to be wrong. Verant has been wrong before, when they were absolutely certain that they were right initially - everyone is. The point is, you aren't always going to be right - put in some safeguards for times when you're wrong.

Another point about names - a lot of people come here from other games, where the kind of names that are unacceptable in EQ are not only perfectly acceptable, they're almost de rigeur. When you first load up and start Everquest, you don't know anything about the game, or conventions, you've never seen the message board, or the last 2 years of name discussions, and you maybe use, in totally good faith, the name you've used for the last couple of years in quake or tribes or UT. By the time you DO figure it out, policy says you aren't allowed to change it (if you petition yourself). A little understanding may be in order here, rather than immediately scaring newcomers off the game by deleting their characters.

And yes, I'm sure it's in the user agreement, and if everyone at Verant who ever loads a new piece of software on their PC sits down and peruses the license agreement in detail before they run the program fine, but honestly, you have to expect people to be human and treat them the way you'd want to be treated yourself.
