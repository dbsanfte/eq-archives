I play a level 30 warrior on Seventh Hammer. While this doesn't by far make me an expert, it does give me a little experience in this class. A couple suggestions for warrior class tweaks that I would make would be:

1) Improve taunt. Do this for all levels. Warriors are suppose to be the group tanks, that's what we do. We are suppose to wade in and slug it out with our foes one-on-one. We are also suppose to be the ones taking the brunt of the damage, so that the thinner skinned casters and not quite as tuff hybrids don't get it. It usually doesn't work that way. All it takes is for a healer to through a healing spell on me and the critter I'm fighting takes off to wail on my healer. Then I have to taunt atleast 3 or 4 times before I can get it of the healer. By this time the healer is low on health himself and has to spend some mana on healing himself.

2) The warrior class specific armor (ie. crafted) should not have att. bonuses for INT or WIS. These do not help a warrior out in combat in any way, and that is the whole reason for a warrior's armor. Rather, the armor should give bonuses only to combat related attributes (ie. STR, AGI, DEX, HPs, STA, etc.)

3)Warriors need some sort of way to sneak past a foe. A sneak skill of some type. Almost all other classes have some way of getting in and getting out of dungeons practically unseen, if they need to. The warrior, however, only has the use of brute force to get clear, hoping that he'll make it before he runs out of health. This makes both corpse recovers (as a warrior you tend to do quite a few of these, atleast if your a good grouping warrior) a bit difficult, especially if you happen to have died deep in a dangerous dungeon which is about 3 zones from the nearest bind location.

4) More bind locations for warriors and other non-caster types. This will make it more likely for most of us who can't bind ourselves anywhere we like to go out explore more of the areas and dungeons around Norrath. Verrant wonders why there are so many under-utilized dungeons, this is why. Especially when your talking about the higher level dungeons. When the closest bind location is 3 or 4 zones away and atleast one of those zones is very hazardous, it tends to keep people from wanting to explore that area or dungeon. Verrant has even introduced area bonuses in these under-used areas to try to make them more inticing to adventures. You want more people to use these areas, make it so that they can be bound closer to them.

5) A few more 1HS weapons for those of us in the mid levels. It seems that the only good weapons for mid-level warriors are 2HS weapons. That's nice, but I like using dual wield, it really helps when fighting casting mobs. Most of the other classes have good 1HS weapons at mid-levels, the warrior class should as well.

Clarification on the few issues warriors have...

New skills are not really needed.. warriors are fairly well balanced under most circumstances. We would however like a few of the things we have work better so we can do our job.... Take damage like no other class!

We have the hit points and armor class to take the hits. Whether or not we get to use them relies on two things.. the effectiveness of our taunt skill, and the 'taunt' factor of our weapons.

The first is used when someone in our group does something that places them instantly at the top of the list.. like a large direct damage spell, or large heal. If it takes more then 3 tries to use the taunt skill to get warrior back on the top of the list, someone is in serious trouble. Best solution for this is increasing the effectiveness of taunt from 40th-60th level.

The second comes into play when there is someone in the group that can consistantly out damage the warrior. Simply increasing the damage a warrior can do will fix the problem, but is unbalancing.. instead, just increase the taunt factor a warrior does with weapons.. particularly 2h weapons. This would allow us to 'do our job', and not infringe on another class.

These two things alone are enough. The warrior is an excellent class. Thank you for your time.
