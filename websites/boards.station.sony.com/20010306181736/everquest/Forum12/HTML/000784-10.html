You want proof, eh? Here's your proof:

From the Everquest Gameplay Forums on 1/15/00:

Oh, by the way, we are considering making spells cast by wizards inherently less resistable. We're actually testing something on test now, but I can't comment further until Tuesday evening.

-Gordon

From the Everquest Patch Message on 1/20/00:

Wizard Enhancement

Level 35 or above wizards will find that their spells (Magic, Cold, or Fire) much less likely to be resisted. This effect stacks with the Staff of Temperate Flux and Enchanter, Shaman, and Magician resistance debuffs. This change was made to increase the effectiveness of wizards at about the level that all spell-casting classes start seeing their resistance rates go up. Wizards specialize in direct magical damage. It makes sense that they should be better at causing that damage than the other classes.

From Casters Realm's talk with GZ on the Wizard Tweak on 1/20/00:

The changes to Wizards mean that for any Wizard above Level 35 they get a lower chance of their Cold, Fire, or Magic spells being resisted. In reality their spells are basically given a built in "debuff" factor. This stacks with any other debuff, such as Tashania, Malosi or Temperate Flux and gives Wizards much better combat effectiveness. It is not a duration debuff. It does not mean when you cast a DD spell the mobs resistance is lowered for other casters in the group.
What the tweak does is when a DD spell is cast it looks at the current resistance of the target, and then the new tweak reduces that resistance amount further, then uses that new reduced resistance within the game formula to determine if the spell lands, or is partially or fully resisted. This process is done each and every time you cast a spell which is enchanced by the new code.

Wizards should now notice the following with these types of spells now:

Green Mobs: Rarely Resited
Blue Mobs: Rarely Resited
White Mobs: Much Lower Resistance
Yellow Mobs: Much Lower Resistance
Light Reds: You will now have a much better chance against these mobs.
Deep Reds: Regular Resistance, with a slight chance of the "debuff factor" assisting.
This tweak for Wizards doesn't change the 6 level cap. It simply makes general combat much easier for the Wizard Class. They should be able to use more powerful spells with less resists on mobs that would be resisted by most other members of the party. It should help to keep the Wizard the true masters of direct damage spells.

From the Everquest Gameplay Forums on 1/21/00:

I didn't really read the other classes so much, but the wizard changes? Pathetic! Slightly increased the AoE and stun area of effect and resistances after lvl 35.How do I survive until 35?

Group? Wizards were affected by resists past about 40th level worse than any of the other classes, as they are the only magic using class without an indirect method of causing damage, and no other combat-related skill besides causing damage. Below 35, the wizards are a very solid class. I hung out with a wizard off and on for nearly 20 levels when building my Necro, and he didn't have a problem.

-Gordon

I hope this helps.
