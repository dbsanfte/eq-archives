I read (something, can't remember what, magazine article I think?) a Verant representative asked if they would ever consider entering ridden creatures into the game, and the answer was an emphatic, absolute, non-changing

no.

I suppose it raises way too many issues. Where can the animal go and what can it do? What happens if that bear you're riding goes into the water? Can you take an animal into town? Into buildings? What are the modifications to battle damage and defense? Does the animal get hit too? Can it fight back? How long can a person keep an animal? Is it like food, it'll go away?

See, in Norrath, people giving quests out have been standing in the SAME SPOT for two years.

Creatures and current modes of transportation are on a SET PATH at all times. (the boats have the highest percentage of bugs, and they're on a set path!)

The world is STATIC, not DYNAMIC. You can't carve your initials into a wall, you can't knock a door off it's hinges, you can't "fly", only levitate a bit. There are no creatures flying around in the skies at all, in fact.

A renegade meteor does not smash into Norrath ever, the Orcs of Crushbone do NOT get any closer to taking over Kelethin (they might as well give up, they've been there for two years, just sitting there... waiting)...

See, it's like an amusement park. Everything's on tracks. On runners.

I'm not trying to be the doomsayer or the big spoilsport or the nasty cynic. I WANT all of these things too, BADLY (check out the thread on "Will Fironia Vie ever be completed?" - it's where this particuar conversation is going on bigtime)... but the reality of it all is this:

until Verant decides to change the game from STATIC to DYNAMIC (involving MUCHO $$$ <they have it>, MUCHO PATCHING <they can do it, even though YOU will complain big time>, and MUCHO TALENT AND CREATIVITY <well, we shall see!>, it ain't gonna happen.

But it's a lovely idea!
