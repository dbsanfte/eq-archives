Here's my thoughts about a few issues that seem to be keys for people regarding how much us Paladins suck, and my opinions about what our job is in a group:

1) We can't taunt as well as warriors.
Of course not! We're not meant to tank stuff if there's a warrior around! Why the hell would you want to taunt something off a warrior? They have more AC and more HP than we do!

2) Our healing sucks... it barely makes a warriors hp bar move.
Our healing is not meant to be used on main battle tanks... that's what clerics are for. We're ok in a pinch, but we're not supposed to replace a "real" healer.

3) Some of our skills are totally useless (Disarm and Archery, for instance).
Not entirely true. Granted, archery is a joke for us... but we can pull with spells so who cares (Cancel Magic is a great pull spell... so is Soothe, oddly enough).
Disarm is another matter, however. Since getting disarm skill at lv40, I have NEVER, not once, EVER successfully disarmed a mob that would give me xp. On a good day I can disarm an orc pawn about 1 in 3 attempts. Seems pretty silly don't it? But here's what some of you may not know: Disarm is a great taunt. Even if it's not successful it taunts almost as much as a successful "taunt" skill use.


So here's what I see as a Paladin's true role in a group:

We are not meant to heal and defend warriors... they don't need to be defended, and clerics are better healers. We are meant to protect and heal casters. No class in the game is as good at doing this as a Paladin. Warriors can't taunt mobs off casters as fast as we can, and rangers can't heal worth a damn... and monks and rogues are even less useful in any kind of protective roll, so we won't even talk about them.

Sure, Lay Hands only heals a fully buffed warrior about half way... they have a ton more hitpoints that we do, why should it be a full heal for them? But how many casters have you LoH'd that weren't completely healed, even from being unconcious? None... we have a ton more hp than casters. And LoH is a great taunt in it's own right... you LoH a 'chanter about to die and suddenly every mob in the room wants to be your best friend.

And our heals suck when used on warriors... but Greater Healing is a signifigant portion of a casters life bar. And we can cast in combat just fine... I regularly get spells off while being whacked by a mob or 2... it's all in where your stats lie. Dex makes it easier to cast under fire.

And we can taunt mobs off casters better than any other class in the game. Stun + taunt + disarm + bash + attack == mob ignores the nice caster. Failing that, root it. There is no better taunt in the game than standing on top of a rooted monster.

So in that role, protecting and keeping alive the casters in the group, we do just fine. Granted, when yer fighting mobs that double and triple attack for 200+ casters don't last long enough for us to help... but when yer fighting stuff like that there should be enough tanks present that casters (unless they are stupid and unload with nukes) should never be high enough on a mobs agro list that they get hit more than once or twice anyway... if they are constantly being attacked, either the tanks or the caster is not doing their job right.


Likewise, we are pretty damned good at interrupting mobs that cast... not at 55+ granted, but how many 55+ mobs are there in the game that you fight without an army anyway? I can't think of that many.

We are also excellent between combat healers... Rangers are laughable in this role, and depending on where and what you fight, Paladin's often have mana left over to heal with and save the clerics mana for when it's really needed.


So to sum up... I think we could do with some better defensive skills (and higher caps) and some useful disciplines. We could also use some better and more obtainable gear... our class specific weapons and armor are either rare enough to be almost unobtainable, or impossible to get at a level where they are truly useful (*cough* Ro Armor *cough*).
But whining about how much DW armor sucks so bad? Puh-leeze... if DW sucked that much, why is EVERY 45+ Paladin on my server wearing the helm and swearing it's the coolest thing they've ever owned?

There are two forms of balance... quantitative and qualitative. I've come to the conclusion that VI is intent on balancing Paladins quantitatively - you throw enough lead on a scale, and it'll eventually weigh as much as a gold bar.

We don't need more spells that another class already has. Frankly, as far as I'm concerned, VI could gut our spellbooks, and leave us with our stuns, heals, and anti-undead spells. That at least might allow them the freedom to strengthen us in the area where we really need it- melee, both in terms of taking damage (although I think we're in fairly good shape here, post-patch) and in terms of hitting a creature, and making it notice us. I've seen enough evidence, posted here and by watching my own results in combat, that we are sorely lacking in terms of being able to hit something... never mind what damage we might do if we do manage to connect.

I know I'm on page 7, but I've got new material to add, honest.

#####
ITEM: New Discipline Idea.
DESCRIPTION: There is an interesting idea floating around for a discipline that would help make the paladin more useful on raids. It would allow the paladin to target a PC and, for the duration of the discipline, take half of the target’s damage in his stead.
####

ACK!!!!

Please, NO! When this was floated on one of the earlier threads, someone (I don't remember who, sorry) explained in great detail why it was a BAD idea for an alleged "raid-friendly" discipline.

He made a number of good points, but the main one was that just being able to spread the damage around would be a huge yawner for people planning a raid. In fact, it's usually better to have a single PC soaking up damage, it simplifies the healer's job, AND it's more efficient if the target has the best AC and dodge/parry skills in the group (*cough*Warrior*cough*).

"I can get hit more!" is hardly a reason to bring a Paladin along... Instead bring two Warriors, and let them trade off taking damage -- again, who needs a Paladin?

Rather, a GREAT idea on the earlier threads for a raid-friendly discipline seems to have been overlooked, which is some sort of "groupmate enhancer" short-duration buff, which might act like haste on melee classes, and some sort of spellcasting boost for caster classes. The RP rationale, which would be PERFECTLY in keeping with the very concept of the Paladin, is that the gung-ho Knight in the group is goading his compatriots on to greater effort, spurring them to give their all (and more) to fight evil (or at least the current mob) in service to the Knight's holy cause.

THAT (appropriately tuned), would be a real reason for people to want a Paladin along on a raid (similar to the cries of joy when an Enchanter joins and can pass out Clarity, or a Shaman joins and can pass out haste and other effectiveness-enhancing buffs).

NOTHING else I can think of that has been suggested would give anyone a good reason to consider a Paladin an equal choice against other classes, without being unbalancing at the same time (e.g., if a Paladin ever had enough damage output to be a tempting groupmate, he'd be cutting into the territory of Warriors and Rogues).

A new, "spur the groupmates" spell/discipline (call it "Holy Cause", perhaps, or "Contagious Fanaticism" ) WOULD be a new, valuable addition to a group, WITHOUT cutting into any other class's territory.

ITEM: Stunning.

What's wrong with the idea of allowing stuns to work on *ALL* monsters? Just make them more likely to be resisted by the higher-end monsters, and far more likely to be resisted on the uber-monsters.

But there should always be a CHANCE, both to reduce the insanity of never-can-be-interrupted-ever mobs, and to add some excitement to those encounters ("maybe we'll get lucky and a rare stun will interrupt this mob at *just* the right time!") And balancing the odds of using stuns on a hard-to-stun mob, versus not even trying and concentrating on damage output, would be yet another strategy choice for a group, always a fun thing.

Making certain mobs totally unstunnable was just a lazy way out for Verant, and rather a "cheat" at that. Give us a CHANCE to have a lucky stun save the day, at least, even if it's a small one.

Plus that will be one more thing that would make a group say, "hey, if we brought a Paladin along, it would add a useful ability to the group..." Right now, when going against a 100% unstunnable mob, they might as well leave the Paladin behind -- and do.
