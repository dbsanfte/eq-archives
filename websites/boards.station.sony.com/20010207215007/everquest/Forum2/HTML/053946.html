From the naming policy:

Names containing titles within them, such as, but not limited to: The, Lord, Lady, Master, King, Knight, Sir, Father (e.g. Sirtallon, Lordeagle, Mothermaggy).

Names that contain sentences, phrases, or more than two words. (Ikillorcs, Ontop, and Petcarbob, Diediedie) Descriptive compound words are allowed, especially in surnames (e.g. Treehugger, Giantslayer)

In the first rule, while "girl" might not be thought of as a title, it can be based on loose interpretation. As far as the second rule, Managirl is obviously in violation if you reference the example of "ontop." By that same rule, Managirl would have been an acceptable surname, but not a first name.

As far as the protocol of changing the name:

For categories 3-10, the Senior staff member will contact you to initiate a discussion about the name change. When you have chosen a new name and have looted any outstanding corpses, the CS representative will perform your name change. Please be aware that if you do not choose a name within a reasonable amount of time, a new name will be assigned to your character. Furthermore, it is your responsibility to inform the Senior staff member of the existence of any unlooted corpses that you own. After your name is changed, those corpses will not be lootable, and no reimbursements will be given.

It sounds as though protocol was followed. The Senior Guide (whom is not a paid Verant employee, by the way) made contact, asked her to come up with a new name, at which point she argued rather than thinking of a new name, then it was changed a further "three minutes later." Perhaps she would have been given more time had she said "OK, but let me think of a new one." Instead, she argued the point.

As far as her current name of Matemp, she can get that changed to something else by petitioning and explaining that a Sr. Guide changed her name to that, and she had now thought up a new one.
