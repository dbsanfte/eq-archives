Create a level 55 troll warrior. Now create a level 55 troll shadowknight. Their hit points are very close. Now give them both Focus, Aegolism, and their Plane of Growth armor and the percentage hit point difference between them drops to about 1%.

Absor, I have an explanation for why some warriors say they can taunt well and some can't. There really is a problem here, but it only exists in certain situations. There are two reasons why some warriors in this thread don't think there is a problem:

1. It depends on the level of the mobs that the warrior is trying to taunt. When my warrior was level 50-55 or so, I hunted in Karnor's almost exclusively. The trouble is that against higher level MOBS (and I'm not speaking just of ubermobs here -- I mean normal, everyday nasties in Sebilis and other places), taunt just has too low a probability of working.

2. It depends on who the warrior is grouped with. You have to realize how taunt works. It moves the warrior (or paladin, or SK, or ranger) to the top of the mob's hate list, but only BARELY. When taunting off a caster, this works fine, as long as the taunt roll is successful, because the caster usually just nukes for a bajillion point of damage and then sits back down. Taunting off of monks or rangers, however, is another ballgame completely. These guys outdamage us AND HIT JUST AS FAST OR FASTER than we do. This means that they get lots of chances to jump over us on the mob's hate list AFTER our successful taunt roll but BEFORE the mob's next attack.

Equipment does indeed make a difference. Dual wielding fast 1H weapons that proc is the best way for a warrior to taunt. But even when you do this, there is still a big problem in the situations I describe above.
