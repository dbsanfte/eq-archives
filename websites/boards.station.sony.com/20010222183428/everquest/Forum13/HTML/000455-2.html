The two greatest points of balance that I feel need to be adressed as a high level monk are damage output and "evade" ability. Both, while already present in some fashion are not up to the standard that I feel they need to be.

Regarding damage output, while monks still stay ahead in terms of weapon ratios (Bo Staff of Trorsmang: 40/35 2hb -Veeshan's Peak drop-, Tranquil Staff: 29/30 fx: 120 dmg-stun 2hb, Celestial Fists: 9/18 h2h), warriors and rouges (without backstab) can get very close with weapons of equal or less difficulty to obtain (Willsapper: 13/20 piercing, Exquisite Velium Warsword: 12/21 1hs, Exquisite Velium Brawlstick: 32/36 2hb).

The best way to resolve this issue is to simply give Flying Kick a minimum damage by level. Why is that? It allows for an across the board increase in damage regardless of what weapon combination you go with (h2h, 1hb, 2hb).

The monk's need for an enhanced "evade" type ability is the issue that definetly needs looking at. Unlike other damage dealers, monks do not have a way of directly lowering themselves off the hate list. With feign death, a monk forces the mob onto another person, but it does not lower them on the hate list (others have to build their agro higher than the monk). While rogues can use evade to reduce their agro fairly quickly (step back, evade, re-engage), monks have to sit fd'ed and wait for people to rebuild agro around them before they may re-engage, thus lowering their overall effectiveness in large-scale encounters.

A few examples with a monk, rogue, and warrior:

The warrior has already established agro and the monk and rogue are just coming in...

Round 1-
Warrior 1200
Monk 0
Rogue 0

Rogue lands a huge backstab. Coupled with his melee, that bumps himself up the list past the warrior. The monk is slowly working his way up the agro ladder due to his higher damage output than the warrior.

Round 2-
Rogue 1700
Warrior 1600
Monk 700

Rogue steps out and evades, reducing his hate to a number lower than the warriors and possibly lower than the monks. Monk keep batting away and
is catching up to the warrior.

Round 3-
Warrior 2000
Monk 1400
Rogue 700* Out of fight

Rogue with lowered hate comes back in. The rogue has essentially lost 1 round of combat for ducking out.

Round 4-
Warrior 2400
Monk 2100
Rogue 900

Later in the fight, the monk has gotten ahead of the warrior and needs to fd.

Round 412097-
Monk 3000 *fd'ed, out of fight
Warrior 2000

Because the monk fd'ed, the agro is shifted to the warrior. However, becaues fd does not lower the monk on the hate list directly, the monk has to sit and wait for the warrior to catch up.

Round 412098-
Monk 3000 *fd'ed, out of fight
Warrior 2400

Round 412099-
Monk 3000 *fd'ed, out of fight
Warrior 2800

After 3 rounds of no damage output. The monk can stand back up and start attacking.

Round 412100-
Warrior 3200
Monk 3000

However, look what happens if the monk starts attacking again off the bat:

Round 412101-
Monk 3700 *fd'ed, out of fight
Warrior 3600

The monk got ahead of the warrior again and needed to feign. In order to avoid this, the monk would need to stay out of the fight not only to let the warrior get ahead in hate rating, but also SIGNIFICANTLY ahead so the monk continue a stream of attacks unmolested. The monk needs some sort of hate reducing factor so he/she doesn't have to make him/herself ineffective by being down a good deal of the fight.

The easiest way to accomplish this is by giving feign death an evade-style hate reduction component. By doing this, feign death can be used more effectively in a fight as a means of avoiding agro, as it was meant to be.

Everything else that isn't bug related - chain attacks, stuns, improved/new disciplines - are just, frankly, just bones. Several things are worth heavily considering in terms of asthetics (spreading out the weight reduction, monk looks), but the only main problems I see with monks in terms of balance are the two things listed above.

How feign works: We'll name the monk in question 'Wu.'

Wu is a monk. He has the feign death skill. Wu is soloing at level 20. He's in oasis and takes on a deepwater crocodile that's level 19. Poor Wu can't seem to solo the croc, so he must feign. As he is the only one who has engaged the croc, he simply turns off attack and feigns death. Wu is a good monk and has maxed his feign death skill, and as luck would have it he doesn't get the failure message. The croc stops attacking him and walks away even though wu has half a bubble health left.

Again, he doesn't get a message and again the fg starts to pound on him. Again, poor Wu dies.

So Wu becomes curious and starts to do a little research. A while later, after new raids and experiences, he notices a trend. He remembers the time at the fire giants and notices that there was a warrior and rogue in his group. He notices that in all the times feign death has failed there has been someone else melee'ing the mob of choice. Even more time goes by and after trying many things, Wu learns that he can successfully feign these creatures off if he leaves their melee range before trying to feign. This action is known as a mob 'frenzying' on a player...this is when a mob will ignore other players and go after a party member of low health. Some mobs will even leave their current target to finish said player off. The only way to get a mob to stop frenzying, is if a) said player is healed b) said player dies c) said mob(s) are killed.

This being the case, it is very hard to leave the melee range of a mob that is frenzied on you.
