Alan, Here's the problem with the hate/heal patch and high end mob encounters in general.


The changes to cleric hate are really only noticeable during encounters with high end mobs. Things like Vox, Nagafen, and other uber named mobs. In these situations problems with the hate changes become apparent. A cleric, druid, or shaman heals once...they get summoned immediately. This can be adapted to with Vox because this mob's health is capped and damage output is reasonable. Not so with other mobs. Velketor for example. The mob has more health than Vox/Naggy combined and quad hits for 840 damage a round as well as casts spells. It's damage output is such that multiple clerics using complete heal must be used to keep one tank alive. Any other healing spell is not an option because it doesn't get the job done. This cannot be done if each cleric gets summoned and killed in seconds after just one heal. Mob hate towards healing in high end encounters needs to be toned down.

This only illustrates another problem with high end mobs, especially those in Velious. Namely the absurdly high amount of hit points and damage output these mobs have. Some of these 'mortal' mobs are tougher than Gods! This is a poor substitute for AI. Your design team should re-think how high-end encounters in general work, especially now that the Donal's BP has been changed. Mob encounters that were designed with that item in mind in it's original state need to be re-tuned immediately.

An example of how a high-end encounter with Velketor might go as the game is now:

You get lots of high level people together, buff them as much as you can, then try to keep them healed and do as much damage as quickly as possible. There's no finesse here, no real strategy...just get in and bludgeon the thing to death. Hard? Yes. Fun? No.


Now, an example of how it might be handled:

Same group of players...they get buffed and go in. Velketor has a few outer guards. When one key mob is killed it triggers another response...reinforments maybe or spell-trap. Players work their way through that encounter which triggers another one. Further down several more mobs await to ambush the players. They are already aggroed but do not rush in...they wait til the players are in range and unleash ranged weapons and spells...then engage the players. The players finally get up to Velketor himself. He has about 100k hitpoints and does about 100-200 melee damage at most during this encounter..same as any other giant. Surrounding him would be 3-4 elite guards that attack with Velketor.

There's no need to keep increasing the health/damage output of mob encounters to make them challenging...just put a little more work into their set-up and design. Another point...there are always going to be a group of players who can beat anything and everything in your game. Eventually they'll find it easy. Such is life. Do NOT re-tune the game with them in mind. They are the elite..the top 1% and do not constitute the majority of your playerbase. Fires of Heaven is without question with that elite 1% in EQ. Please remember that most of us can't compete at that level and to stop making design changes that are targetted with that guild in mind.

Another idea...one that might allow you to challenge average guilds as well as the elite. Scale the encounters. Have a minimum number of mobs, resist rates, health, damage output that scales upwards based upon the average level of the players in the zone. The higher the players the tougher the mob encounter is to compensate. Loot should also be scaled. If you kill a mob on the 'expert' setting it should drop loot that is not available to those who beat it on the 'normal' setting. Dunno if this can be done somehow but it would be a better solution than tacking on another million hitpoints and an extra 1k damage per round. Would be a lot more fun I think for sure.


Anyways, I hope you read this with a mind open to the possibility there needs to be changes and take these suggestions in the way they were meant...an objective and honest opinion with some suggestions for improving the game. You guys have done a great job...but as always your playerbase wants more. 8)
