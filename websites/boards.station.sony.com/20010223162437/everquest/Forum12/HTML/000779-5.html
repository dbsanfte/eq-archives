In the high-level, large mob situation (dragon, epic, etc.), we run into this:
a) Little to no pet damage - Our pets die quickly from overwhelming damage that they can't resist, get hit with fear and run away, or can't participate due to pathing/aggro issues.
b) Little to no DOT damage - Even if corpse poofing didn't scare people off, only the best of our damage-over-time spells hit hard enough to be worth casting. Since DOT spells can't stack with themselves like DD can, just one necromancer can pretty much take care of this. This is assuming the spells can be landed at all (often not the case), and that the fight lasts more than a minute (less than that and the DOT hasn't done appreciable damage).
c) Little to no DD damage - Without our lifetap line, we're left with Chill Bones (55, 500ish damage), Ignite Bones (44, 500ish damage), Torbas Acid Blast (34, 220ish damage), or Shock of Poison (24, 150ish damage) - and we can't use the Bones spells because of the illusion.

It's not that we necessarily need a DD upgrade here, we just need a way to do damage. We have an offensive spell line-up. How are we intended to do damage? If the answer is Pets, that's great - but the pets will need to be much more useable than they are now. If the answer is Damage Over Time, that's okay - it gives one, maybe two necromancers some damage output, that's better than where we are now. If the answer is Direct Damage, that's great - multiple necromancers would have a role, and the Lifetap line is the obvious choice for this rather than putting in new spells.

*** Answer to Absor's Conjure Corpse Question ***

Absor, you wanted to know the logic for providing Necromancers with the spell "Conjure Corpse" at a lower level than 57.

It is this...SKs are a hybrid and, as far as I know, no other hybrid gets it's parent's class' spell until the parent gets an upgrade, if it were available. In reality, the parent is usually 2+ circles above its hybrid counterpart

Here is an example.

SKs get Cascading Darkness at lvl 59
Necros get it at lvl 49
Necros get an upgrade to Cascading at lvl 59, hence SKs don't have their upgrade before Necros.

SKs get Asystole at lvl 60
Necros get it at lvl 44
Necros get an upgrade to Cecessation of Cor at lvl 56.

I guess you see the pattern. I am not as familiar with other Hybrid/Parent relationships, but am not familiar of a time when the hybrid gets any given spell before its parent gets a corresponding, available, upgrade. This is especially true for the issue surrounding Summon Corpse vs Conjure Corpse.

Necros get it at lvl 39 and currently receive an upgrade 18 lvls later. But, remember, 7 of those lvls are post 50 so are much much harder levels than those <50.

SKs get Summon Corpse at 50. One could easily argue that Necros should get Conjure at 51, but the community has reasonably suggested lvl 55, which is a significant right of passage and still 5 lvls past where the SKs get their spell.

Please add this to the list.

I would like to put forth some suggestions that would compensate for many nerfs our class has received and that would bring the necromancer back to being desirable and productive addition to groups in dungeons and in raid situations.


GENERAL SUGGESTIONS:

-reduce the MR of many of the new uber mobs in SOV and ROK, this "mobflation" seems to be the cause of many of the problems for casters and necros in the post 50 game, as well this would help us stick our lifetap line of spells on uber mobs

-put some undead content in outdoor zones in SOV, there are zero undead mobs in outdoor zones in SOV, this is not only a nerf to necromancer but SK's, paladins and clerics who have a nice set of anti-undead spells, also bonechips are not readily available in these zones for necros

-fix the current code that no longer lets you resume your character if you crash or go link dead, both mages and necros use considerable mana to summon pets and suffer because of this attempt to thrwart cheaters who took advantage of resuming characters to dupe

-necro epic - ugly, impractical, very difficult to get, would never use, a complete disaster, there only a few on our server

-increase the drops in the planes, in all the raids I have ever been on I've only seen 1 necro item drop and that was a pair of boots in POG a few days ago, what we see is tank armor over and over again


FACTION:

Why do rangers and druids get a amiable faction with animals and necromancers don't get amiable or at the very least indifferent with the undead?
We are supposed to be the "masters of the undead". Even in skeleton form undead scowl at us. The faction system that is in use in lowerguk with undead would also prevent this from being abused.


PETS & EXPERIENCE:

Currently if your pet does 51% of the damage to the mob it will take half of the exp. This is wrong and needs to be changed. Necromancers shouldn't be competing with their pets to see who can do the most advantage. We are the master of the pets therefore we should get FULL experience for the kill. Pets who are victim of bad pathing in dungeons get me killed all the time, perhaps my pet should take half of my share of exp loss.


PET COMMANDS:

-new pet command /pet defend (toggles on and off)

This new command would allow the pet to continue to fight his chosen target and not defend himself from a mob that has chosen to start beating on him.

The most disconcerting problem necromancers have is the reputation that pets have with breaking mez inside dungeons when attacked by mobs other than the mob that is the main target of the group. Implementation of this single command for pets would make finally make the necromancer group friendly. Also the fact that the pathing is absolutely horrendous in most dungeons, this new command would make up for that.

-new pet command /pet report level

There is currently no way to know a pets lvl unless you sick it on some greens. This command would help.

-increase the maximum damage of the lvl 53 pet, the lvl 56 pet and the lvl 59 pet by 2 damage each.

Consider that it takes 2 times as long to get from from lvl 49 to lvl 59 as it does to get from lvl 1 to lvl 49, is it too much to ask to ask that our pets receive a damage upgrade at lvl 53, lvl 56 and lvl 59?

Look at the progression of HPS that the following mobs have then compare it to the 2 damage increase of our pets:

greater kobold lvl 34 - 1370 HP
lava beetle lvl 42 - 3290 HP
imp protector lvl 48 - 6566 HP
fire giant warrior lvl 51 - 13136 HP

The mobs practically double in HPs every few lvls but necromancers are given pets that that only increase by 2 maximum damage. This list of mobs doesn't even include the new SOV uber mobs. Why is it that a lowly lvl 33 spectre in Oasis can hit for 80+ damage, yet my higher level pet only hits for 49?

-increase the inherent dexterity of our pets or include dexterity as part of the Augment Death pet buff

Recently the Verant nerfed our pets by placing them in a PC model from an NPC model. Now as Abashi admitted, our pets have seriously low dexterity and hardly proc at all when using weapons that proc. -mages get an extensive line of damage shield that are castable on anyone, necros don't get any damage shields, why?

was going to be a few things, but just one for now (brain falling asleep)...

- Pet pathing and pet control, primarily in a raid context. In normal groups, the difficulty with both of the above is understandable (though I do think bad pathing should be fixed whereever possible), because in said groups, the damage done by a broken mez, or a lost pet is limited. The mobs involved are of manageable difficulty (otherwise you wouldn't have a single group doing them). Even at worst, with a total wipeout, you only have six deaths on your hands.

In raids, however, the difficulty with pets stays the same, but the risk is much greater. The mobs hit much harder, are much tougher, and in some cases are in greater numbers. Effective crowd control can be critical, and it's not hard to see where a pet could screw that up. Even worse, if the pet gets lost...the resulting train will not be pleasant. And if there's a wipeout, that's not six people, that's 12, 18, 24+...and a whole lot of downtime for the raid -- not fun.

Consider that many raids already have a good number of melees present, and it's not hard to see why pets aren't allowed at many of them. Hence, the request for better pet pathing and better tools to control them.

Many suggestions for the latter have popped up in mage and necro threads (as pets are a significant issue for both classes), such as /pet noattack (pet will not aggro), /pet nochange (pet will not change targets), etc. Some other alternatives:

- Spell: Control Death -- makes a pet ignore attackers, and prevents it from switching targets. This would slow the pet's attacks by say, 15%, however, so it would only be of significant use when pet control is important.

- Temporary pets -- ones that attack a mob once summoned, and suicide once it's dead. Not a perfect solution, but this prevents issues with a pet being prematurely aggroed by a pull (since it doesn't exist until the owner is ready to attack).

I also agree that the suggested mage spells -- the anti-fear buff, and the summon pet, should be given to necros as well, as pet issues, again, affect us to a significant degree as well.
