I've seen a lot of posts on this and previous ranger talk threads and I have to mention one thing before I voice my ideas. These are supposed to be forums for balancing the ranger class with other classes while bringing them equally up to par. This is not a forum for you to voice what things you think would make the ranger class more attractive to play to you or anyone else. I could go on to cite the silliness of requests for Teleports and such, but I won't, because that would not be productive. Here are my suggestions to make the ranger class more balanced and viable to play among the other classes in Everquest:

1) Defense. Rangers have had the same defense ability since level 39. Granted, no classes received the ability to progress past 200 in Defense until reaching level 51, but rangers should receive a slight increase in this skill. If the changes on test are any indication, virtually all other melee classes (and even bards, who are not primarily a melee class in most circumstances - that is to say, they do not fill the requirement of a tank in most high level groups) are receiving the defense cap of warriors. If this holds true, rangers should have their defense skill cap raised to 225 or 230 to bring them in line with (but still lower than) other melee classes.

2) Defensive skills (parry, dodge, riposte)
It has been stated many times by various VI spokespersons that rangers are designed to be the nimble, chain wearing warriors of the forest, and therefor not meant to be as durable as a warrior. If rangers are meant to be more nimble and avoidant than warriors, I believe our defensive skills should reflect this and be HIGHER than warriors. Defensive skills have a lesser impact on survival and durability than AC, but I think giving rangers skills superior to a warrior's would bring a closer balance to the class, and add some truth to how our class has been defined by the game designers.

3) Spells. Rangers use druidic magic and have several spells that do not continue their "line" after level 50. One thing I would like to see to continue the ranger's use of druidic magic is one or two "pack" spells. I'm not talking about new spells that only rangers get (e.g. Call of the Predator), but other spells like Pack Chloroplast. Granted we get chloro at 55, we should get PC at 60. In most cases, it will not be necessary, as a druid with Regrowth of the Grove may be available, but rangers should have the option to provide this backup role as it shows the natural progression of their magic line.

4) Jolt. This spell is and always has been a hack. I've been following the troubled issue of ranger melee ever since starting my ranger over 18 months ago and it has been made abundantly clear that the code responsible for an individual's placement on the hate list is based on the frequency of hits and POTENTIAL damage that person is dealing rather than the actual damage they are doing to the NPC. This is why low delay weapons, of which rangers get many, generate a much higher place on the hate list. Rather than address this code problem, VI has instituted Jolt, which if successful, removes the ranger from the top of the hate list. Unfortunately, it is resisted over 50% of the time, and when resisted, generates additional aggro. I often use Jolt to pull, as it is a fast casting spell that is less affected by line of sight problems than arrows, and this is ludicrous. In addition to this, Jolt must be recast multiple times during combat, because if a ranger is using weapons that put him on the top of the hatelist to begin with, he/she is just going to get there again after resuming combat. There are really only 2 ways to fix this problem without rewriting major areas of the code which generate the hate list; make Jolt's effect greater and much less resistable, or remove it entirely and replace it with Evade.

5) Buffs. I would like to see some of the buffs available to the ranger post level 50. Some of these I see already going in on Test, such as resist cold and skin like nature, and we will have to see if these actually go live. Rangers get Feet Like Cat at a very early level and yet receive no upgrade to this spell. Granted, druids don't get an upgrade either that I am aware of, but an upgrade to this spell (somewhere in line with Storm Strength in level) would seem to be appropriate to the ranger class.

Damage Mitigaion vs. Damage Avoidance

Ok, as far as ranger defense is concerned, the problem does not lie in the defense skill, or the ability to get high AC. It lies in the underlying code that calculates AC. AC, as we've been told by VI, is made up of 2 numbers, mitigation and avoidance. As it stands, rangers are high avoidance, low mitigation. This worked well pre kunark and all was happy.

Then along came kunark and it messed everything up. The mobs were designed around faster encounters, so hit harder and hit for a way higher percentage. So not only were we getting hit for full (which was pretty normal pre kunark) by every mob, but we got hit more frequently too. Here's a hypothetical example:

Pre kunark lvl 45 mob:
Hit us 45% of the time for an average of 70

Kunark mob lvl 45:
Hit's us for 55% for an average of 110


Now I understand that other classes have to overcome this also. But other classes aren't getting hit for full, and are getting hit about the same as us. Now some want a defense increase, but I hate to tell them that it will do little to no good. I don't notice it a bit the difference between 900ac and 1100ac. I'm still getting hit for full, and getting hit for a pretty high percentage.

I think the answer lies in the code we don't see, and won't show up in numbers that we see. I think what we need is to have our damage avoidance upped to compensate for the higher percentage that mobs are hitting for.
