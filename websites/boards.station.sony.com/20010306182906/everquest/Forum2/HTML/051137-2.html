/agree (hey wow ..)

I don't boggle or anything though. It's just a rather obvious fact that tradeskills were never meant to be a major aspect of EQ, comparably to UO. (I'd say for example, but it's the only example, so I'd be cheating ..)

Tradeskills are based around the central philosophy of the game - kill creatures/go places to get x ingredient for x item. I can see why they've done this, it's a solid design philosophy to reference everything back to your central philosophy (Aka. Vision), however I think in doing so they've lost a lot of player interest in doing tradeskills.

Not saying that Yak does a bad job at all, I think he'd be pretty snowed under trying to do all this stuff, which is probably why there are recipes that don't work after having been in-game for a long time.

But I am saying I'd think harder next time if I was you guys. Tradeskills, particularly not linked to player power (level), are a big drawcard for a sizeable group of people. They are also a good distraction from the normal flow of play. There's nothing wrong with hack'n'slash type games, they're entertaining and can be fun. I wouldn't agree that EQ is just that (It's really in the hands of the player), but it is beneficial to provide the players with the means of distraction from regular gameplay. Increases game longevity = good for customer and company.
Currently the tradeskills seem to point the player back to the regular facets of gameplay. (See how powerful the vision that so many people discount as fluff can be?)

Now does this mean the vision is flawed? Hardly. The whole game would suck if that was the case. No rather they didn't focus heavily on this area during design phases. Someone already mentioned building the game around tradeskills - that would be a pretty bold move. I don't think it would hurt at all though to have a fairly well thought out tradeskills system which was self sufficient in terms of design philosophy, and removed from regular aspects of gameplay. (I.e. Rather than killing something to get something, make another tradeskill create that something. Ultimately everything is made from the same ingredients.)

Amethyr:

I was the one who suggested that a game be built around tradeskills. And you are correct, doing that would be a bold move. What I meant by that probably did not come across in what I wrote.

What I really meant with that was similar to what you said about the Trades apparently not having a heavy focus during initial design. If you are going to have viable trades, then the products of those trades must be integrated into the game at intial design and in every step thereafter.

Thought must be given to each product as to its usefullness within the game structure during design. And, on the same token, care must be taken with the game structure itself, so that it fully encompasses the tradeskill in question.

If you design the structure with no trades in mind, then tack them on after the fact, you (and I mean "you" in the general sense) end up with a disconnected aspect of the game. Some parts may fit, others will be superfluous. And if you code in items that don't fit or aren't usefull, then you have wasted your programmer's valuable time, because those items will not see much, if any, play time.

And then there's the issue of character advancement while pursuing the trades (leveling in EQ's case). If trades are fully integrated correctly, this should not be as big of an issue as it is currently. If you decide to dabble as a tradesman, you're skills as an adventurer will slow proportionately to your dabbling. If you decide to Master the trades, your skills as an adventurer will suffer greatly, but you would be the equal of a Master Adventurer in terms of chosen profession.

I'm also not suggesting that this sort of integration could be accomplished in EQ at this time. It would probably require so much revamping that it would end up being EQ2 anyway. There may be many things that could be done to come close, but I doubt they will be. It will probably take EQ2 or another game designed with Trades in mind from the very start.

Anyway, thanks for the comments, Amethyr, and everyone else for that matter. Good Discussion.

I was in Beta2+, and one of the things I really looked forward to was the tradeskills in EQ (I had given up on UO, pk-fest that it was.) However, upon arrival it turned out that the only general tradeskill in game was (drum roll please......) tailoring patchwork.

*BOGGLE*

Having been a miner/blacksmith/tinkerer in UO, I had looked forward to these skills (ever wonder why the bank in Kaladim sells Pickaxes? Mining was originally planned... And yes, that was from a GM.)

I played all the way through Beta, and while I never reached a very high level I explored most of the 2 continents that I frequented (Antonica for most of it, then Faydwer when they brought it online.)

Tradeskills have always been the red-headed-stepchild of EQ, and nothing has changed. Yes, they can finally (after how long?) make some decent equipment, probably stuff that should have been in in the first place. But no matter what happens, EQ will always be first and foremost a standard hack-slash-heal-repeat game. Tradeskills will probably be broken for the life of the game, they just don't consider them worth the time. (BTW: I'm a 168 brewer, 200 fletcher, 100-something blacksmith, so I'm familiar with broken crap.)
