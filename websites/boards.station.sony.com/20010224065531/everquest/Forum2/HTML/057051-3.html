Group or targetable FD. IF none of the above are done, then perhaps at LEAST make the lifetap line above 50 a different resist line such that they are more likely to land on uber mobs.

I would like to end with 2 comments:

1) Necro mana-battery is really kind of a stretch. If this is a balance issue, remove these lines completely and permit us to grow in areas more applicable to the Necromancer class. A very good point was made before: 800 mana/ necro vs. a different class with a full bar of 2000+ mana...easy pick I think. Please, it's a little too cuddly for a Necromancer. Balance is an issue, but this was added due to the more "group-friendly" vision for the class (which is fine), but there are FAR more characteristic roles we can fill in a group. (some examples are listed above let me know if you want more...).

2) Please recognize that although we have an AWESOME technique for soloing, namely fear kiting, it really shouldn't be the argument that is continuously used to describe the power of a Necromancer. As I am sure you know, this solo technique is not unique to our class.

Fear kiting and dotting is done by Druids all through their lives, with the restriction that the mob must be an animal. Frankly, soloing quickly becomes boring and has a poor exp. pay-off especially now that you have [wisely] increased experience bonuses for groups and certain dungeons.

I feel very strongly that we have reached a point where additional 'tweaking' to reign in our unacceptable power over individual mobs is really beginning to become a little excessive. We are not any more powerful than any other class. In fact, in most situations I would say that an individual of different class would outperform us at any particular function. What we DO seem to excel at is variety. Therein lies our power...and that is already somewhat limited by having only 8 spell slots, and the simple restriction of time on all encounters (especially in a group).

I am struggling to find a facet of our ability that can be described as unbalancing. We have an ability to survive in different environments and situations. Looking at exp gain from soloing, or grouping with 6 necros, planes raids or uber mobs, there is usually a different class that does each desirable function better.

One last time...a mana-battery role is not in keeping with our discipline.

Our power, our variety, should in part and as a whole, remain Necromancy; and we as academics of pestillence and death, must always be Necromancers.

I would like to put forth some suggestions that would compensate for many nerfs our class has received and that would bring the necromancer back to being desirable and productive addition to groups in dungeons and in raid situations.


GENERAL SUGGESTIONS:

-reduce the MR of many of the new uber mobs in SOV and ROK, this "mobflation" seems to be the cause of many of the problems for casters and necros in the post 50 game, as well this would help us stick our lifetap line of spells on uber mobs

-put some undead content in outdoor zones in SOV, there are zero undead mobs in outdoor zones in SOV, this is not only a nerf to necromancer but SK's, paladins and clerics who have a nice set of anti-undead spells, also bonechips are not readily available in these zones for necros

-fix the current code that no longer lets you resume your character if you crash or go link dead, both mages and necros use considerable mana to summon pets and suffer because of this attempt to thrwart cheaters who took advantage of resuming characters to dupe

-necro epic - ugly, impractical, very difficult to get, would never use, a complete disaster, there only a few on our server

-increase the drops in the planes, in all the raids I have ever been on I've only seen 1 necro item drop and that was a pair of boots in POG a few days ago, what we see is tank armor over and over again


FACTION:

Why do rangers and druids get a amiable faction with animals and necromancers don't get amiable or at the very least indifferent with the undead?
We are supposed to be the "masters of the undead". The faction system that is in use in lowerguk with undead would also prevent this from being abused.


PETS & EXPERIENCE:

Currently if your pet does 51% of the damage to the mob it will take half of the exp. This is wrong and needs to be changed. Necromancers shouldn't be competing with their pets to see who can do the most advantage. We are the master of the pets therefore we should get FULL experience for the kill. Pets who are victim of bad pathing in dungeons get me killed all the time, perhaps my pet should take half of my share of exp loss.


PET COMMANDS:

-new pet command /pet defend (toggles on and off)

This new command would allow the pet to continue to fight his chosen target and not defend himself from a mob that has chosen to start beating on him.

The most disconcerting problem necromancers have is the reputation that pets have with breaking mez inside dungeons when attacked by mobs other than the mob that is the main target of the group. Implementation of this single command for pets would make finally make the necromancer group friendly. Also the fact that the pathing is absolutely horrendous in most dungeons, this new command would make up for that.

-new pet command /pet report level

There is currently no way to know a pets lvl unless you sick it on some greens. This command would help.

Consider that it takes 2 times as long to get from from lvl 49 to lvl 59 as it does to get from lvl 1 to lvl 49, is it too much to ask to ask that our pets receive a damage upgrade at lvl 53, lvl 56 and lvl 59?

Why is it that a lowly lvl 33 spectre in Oasis can hit for 80+ damage, yet my higher level pet only hits for 49?

A quote from Absor:
"Also, we are not looking to make necromancers any more effective at soloing than they already are. And, well, the necromancer is a pretty powerful class as they are now."


I have posted here two times already, but I want to stress my point one more time, for the sake of emphasis.

I agree totally with the fact of not making Necromancers more powerful at soloing. Yet I think there is an overall misconception of the difference in power between the classes with respect to soloing. Comparing two ends of the spectrum, Warrior and Necromancer. A 16th level Warrior wouldn't dream of soloing a yellow mob. While a 16th level Necromancer could pull it off with relative ease. So the 16th lvl warrior solos blue mobs. He does so at a much faster rate than the necro soloing yellow mobs. Why, because the necro will use up on average 4 bubs of mana during the battle, and then have to med for awhile. The warrior can take on 4 blue mobs in the same amount of time and lose probaby 2 bubs of health. Net result, same xp roughly for both. Where the Necro edges out the Warrior in ease of soloing is the travel to and from the hunting grounds. The warrior is always exposed, the Necro, can be invis the whole way and mostly unmolested. Is that way more powerful? Not really, considering most casters can do the same when it comes to travel, some have it much better (Druids). All of that notwithstanding, I agree not to increase the viability of soloing.

Which brings me to my second point. Are Necros "pretty powerful" in groups? I think not. Why? Even at the lower levels we are virtually useless. We can sick pet, and snare. Is our pet as good as a Mage's? HA! Not in any way shape or form. Our darkness spells are not as good of a snare as a Druid's until 29th level when we get dooming. What about our wonderful DOT's? Utterly useless. A DOT on a mob in a good group that takes two minutes to do it's total damage to a mob that is probably dead in 30 seconds. As useful as a tit on a boar. Fear kiting? Well now this would be one place we could rock. But unless you are in a group that is in an outdoor flat area, and that isn't totally resistant to the idea, it would make us a very valuable member. How often does that happen? For me, once in 30 levels.

Where is this conceived sense of being "pretty powerful" coming from? I have read where some people want to nerf the pet's from 1-40. Are you crazy? Why? To justify your pet's getting power increases because you are post 50? How is that helping the class? It isn't. That pet is as much of a bane as it is a benefit. You cannot survive without it, yet at times at can be the source of your doom. They are useless in dungeons, and therefore, what good are you as a Necro in a dungeon? ZIP.

Since most of the people posting here have addressed the post 50 concerns (albeit some ideas haven't been all that great), I am addressing pre-50 concerns. Make us wanted in groups! Make our DOTS fast acting. Decrease the duration to 42 seconds, keep damage the same and increase mana by 1/3. In conjuction with this, make them landable on uber mobs, and fix corpse poofing due to DOTs. Give the post-50 a wider selection of DOTs so that on a raid you can have multiple necros, each using a different DOT on the mob.

Fix the pathing problem with pets in dungeons, or give us something with which to summon the pet back to us. Then if the pet was in trouble when it was summoned, a FD will clear it's hate list as well as yours. And make pet's of a certain level immune to fear, unless it is an undead fear. In fact, one way to implement a small nerf to all casters who can fear is to make ALL undead immune to fear except for the undead only fear spells.

And don't touch the Lich line of spells. There is already plenty of risk involved with the reward received.
