OK..interesting topic...have had a few thoughts of my own along related lines:

1) Is the board useful ...yes

2) Is it severerly flawed..also yes.

Before I suggest improvements, let me point out a few observations.

a) Many threads start out with calm, civil comments describing a problem of some sort, for example with a game mechanic or some other feature of the game. The thread might strike a chord with the rest of the community and attracts attention. But, somewhere around page three or four, the posts start to get nasty as the posters begin to feel as though Verant is uninterested in their opinion.

b) Expressing you opinion (in general, not just on this board) generally improves peoples attitude BUT being ignored makes it worse.

c) The General Discussion board has so many topics, etc that two hour old posts are already on page 4.

What does all this mean for the usefulness of this board.

1) Split the general discussion board into three other boards (goal....a post requesting information or assistance should persist at least 12 hours on a given page...more splits may be needed).

a) Discussin of game mechanics (i.e. Healing aggro, stat buffs, /lastword command, etc)

b) Questions and Discussions for the everquest community -no response expected from Verant (i.e. What happened when..., What should I play...., How to...)

c) Requests for input (threads in this area will be started by 'blue names' when they want input into a specific topic..i.e. the iksar quest threads, the class talk threads, the one you should have started on healing, the one you should start following every major patch to get input on the changes, etc).

2) READ the threads in boards one and three on a daily basis, and POST. I dont mean make a careful response to each post in the thread..but just a ...I have read this thread and am considering the issues mentioned...would do wonders to convince the EQ community (righly or wrongly) that you are reading and listening to their concerns. Because if you DONT post, we feel ignored and get upset. But if you DO post, we get the feeling that we are being listened to.



Note: Some of this is based on the assumption that you are actually concerned with input from the gaming community on how to improve your game (and I dont mean catering to the whims of every player, but rather listening to valid concerns and making adjustments to increase the enjoyment and quality of the game). Of course, if not....pretending to listen to our concerns through more frequent posts (Absor has responded to maybe three or four posts in two weeks (not counting warnings and thread lockdowns/ splits)...will still do wonders for customer morale.

Different types of industries have different ways of having consumer feedbacks. Some industries use a sample audience to view their products and comment. This is not really possible in the case of Everquest because of the nature of the product and the sheer amount of bugs and errors that may occur. Therefore, the nature of the product and the clients make it such that the Board are a primary mean of communication.

Why are the boards needed? Verant needs these boards because it needs to know how it consumers feel. Every company needs to know that information. It is not when the consumers are gone that the information becomes apparent. Any company needs consumer feedback BEFORE the consumers are gone.

On the other hand, the board is a somewhat limited way of obtaining information since, by its very nature, it attracts only a limited number of people. I would be surprised to see what percentages of posts are made by the same people who just reply to each other (some sort of weird incestuous relationship where the same people are talking to each other over and over).

The Government started getting worried when it discovered that the composition of the marchers had changed and that people who usually do not complained had started protesting.

I suggest that the number of posts is irrelevant to the importance of a subject but that who is posting is not.

People often make the point that EQ is not a role-playing game. That's largely true, insofar as people do not actually adopt the role you might expect given the typical fantasy background.

However, EQ does have one important aspect of an RPG. It has the close association of the player with their character. Most people don't think of their character as just some sprite off on a video screen. They think of it as themselves. That's why they get so attached to them, and get so emotionally involved with issues about their character. They don't see them as issues about "their character", but as about themselves. Thus, they lose their objectivity.

This point adds a bit of complexity to the player-publisher/GM relationship. There does exist that potential for actual emotional abuse. People learned a long time ago in RPGs that the gamemaster really can't have the attitude that they are God, it's their game, and thus they can do whatever they want, while still retaining a satisifactory game. Publishers of any sort of game where players identify that strongly with their game tokens will have to learn the same thing.

It's true that it's Verant's game, Verant's bits, "we're in their world now", and they can do as they like. But it may not be wise to take all the license that implies. Nor does that necessarily extract the full potential available in the game system (and thus, ultimately, customer satisfication, dollars, and repeat business).
