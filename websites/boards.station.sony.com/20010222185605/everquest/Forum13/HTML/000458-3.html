AoE Taunt: This would be an interesting addition, but I think this would be considered more of a wish-list type item. I would not want to see this added as a discipline...to do so would make sure it would see limited use, and would likely not replace Defensive as the discipline of choice.

Increased Hit Points: At high levels, the supposed hit point advantage of warriors becomes less and less significant. Fighting giants in Kael, for example, a few hundred hit points can be lost in mere moments. It ceases to be a defining class advantage, especially considering the nature of NPC's in Velious. If this is going to be considered a great advantage for warriors, a significant boost in our hit points needs to be made.

Bind Wound Improvement: The idea that increasing our ability to bind wounds at high levels would reduce downtime for a warrior when fighting solo is somewhat off base. At those levels, we're not going to be fighting without a healer At level 54, I can solo the occasional blue, but I'm not going to be doing so unless I'm simply trying to survive a run across a zone. I'd like to see an improvement in this area; it reduces downtime, yes, but it's not going to affect a warrior's desire or ability to solo in a significant way.

Two-Handed weapons and taunt: Barring the rare weapon with a proc capable of drawing major attention, two-handed weapons just don't provide the punch to taunt effectively. Perhaps the taunt value of any hit from a two-handed weapon could be increased, with the current values being multiplied by X to make them effective.

Cobalt Helm and Bracer: I'd really expand this to cover all cobalt armor and beyond. AC is considered one of our greatest assets; keep in mind that a warrior's AC depends almost entirely on equipment (with a minor contribution from agility). To truly consider AC the advantage of a warrior, such equipment needs to be obtainable. The warriors in my guild can claim 2 bracers and perhaps 3-4 helmets have dropped for us since Kunark was released. The total number on the server (and likewise on other servers) is not much more than that. The other cobalt pieces are somewhat more common, but not by a great extent. Most warriors of high level are going to have lesser equipment, and as such, won't enjoy the massive AC advantage attributed to warriors. Look at the drop rates for these pieces, and consider adding additional pieces to help ensure we do typically have a higher AC than other classes.

Critical hits for low damage: I certainly agree with the sentiment that's it can be annoying to see a critical hit for less damage than we might do with a regular hit. Warrior's don't rely on critical hits as they happen all too infrequently (and for too little damage overall) to have any big impact. We typically consider them eye candy, and we're used to it. I'd love to see improvements here (more frequent with a raised minimum damage value) but this isn't what I would consider a burning issue; taunt comes well before this one.

Better Taunt: DING! As some have said, knowledge is the key to using taunt effectively. However, the best comment I've seen on this topic came from a paladin who said they had the advantage here. With the taunt key, melee weapons and spells, they can control their position on an NPC's hate list far more effectively than a warrior can. I think that applies to any other taunting class as well, giving them a significant advantage in this area. Give warriors the tools to exert this same level of control. My suggestion would be a taunt skill that adds a concrete value, not +1 above the next person. Allow that value to increase as the warrior increases in level. By no means do we want an automatically successful taunt...we just want to be at least as effective, if not better, at the job. Currently, we're not.

Piercing Skill Cap: As masters of weaponry, and with a supposedly greater selection of weapons available to us, I think having the higher cap is justified.

Berserker Frenzy as a skill: My vote would be no on this. Unless it were made FAR more effective, it would not replace Defensive as the discipline of choice. If any changes are to be made to this, go with the idea of making it more than mere eye candy. I liked the idea put forth above by Milena, I believe, increasing the health-percentage at which we start 'zerking, with that percentage increasing at higher levels. As a discipline, this would not be used and would have no impact unless, as I said, it were seriously beefed up to justify regular use.

Iksar Planar Armor: Personally, I think the Iksar, in terms of quests, available equipment and general apathy from Verant, have long gotten the short end of the stick. For the purposes of this discussion, however, I'll just say that yes, planar armor (not counting the higher end PoG equipment) should be added for Iksar warriors (...and SKs and Shaman).

Respect: It's a point of annoyance that we're the only class without any inherent means of avoiding conflict while traveling. We have to rely on an escort, spells cast by others or equipment to assist us. To an extent, this is something we sacrificed in choosing the class, but Kunark and Velious have upped the scale of these incidental encounters. This may not be a matter of balance, per se, but rather an issue of a particular part of the game being very tedious and time-consuming, far more so than for any other class.

Nothing to look forward to from 35th to 50th: This was always a general beef for me, though not something to which you can easily give an answer. Discounting Disarm (hehe), there is really only one skill from 25th to 50th, and that's just boring. The suggested idea is interesting, giving value in some situations. I'm sure other ideas could be suggested as well, to simply give us SOMETHING new at these levels.

Equipping shields on the back: A question, do cloaks currently provide an AC bonus only from the back? I like this idea, if only for the fact that it would give me a chance to actually use that silly Nautilus shield I picked up and promptly stored in the bank. My personal opinion is that some/perhaps all shields should be usable in the back slot...without the limitation you suggested. I don't see how this unbalances anything; heck, if this were a warrior-only addition, it gives you a potential way to increase warrior AC in general, a category we should dominate, but strangely do not by any great margin due as I mentioned above.

Riposte damage report: I think this is already reported, though perhaps not in the order some would prefer. This is cosmetic, and really has no bearing on balance.

Things you failed to list, but should have...

1. A question, is there a soft cap on AC of 1100 in any way? If there is an artificial limiting factor of any kind, please factor that into your stance that warriors have a significant AC advantage. A high-level hybrid can reach 1100 AC fairly easily, and if such a limit is imposed, you essentially eliminate any AC advantage warriors should have at high levels.

2. Disarm: It works on a small number of NPCs, and I've never seen that it has a significant effect on ANY NPC, with the exception of those NPCs who dual-wield. It's been said that it contributes slightly to taunting...perhaps slightly, but not enough so that I've ever seen it as a true bonus to taunting. Perhaps removing this skill isn't an option, but I would suggest either boosting it's taunt ability or allowing it to disarm magical weapons into the NPC's inventory. The latter would probably still be a very minor detail, but at least then it would work on Velious NPCs.

3. I touched on this earlier, but please accept the idea that equipment DOES in fact have a great deal to do with the balancing of the warrior class. There is no inherent AC bonus for the class, except by virture of our Defense skill...and if said value is increased for Paladins/Shadowknights that ceases to be a warrior advantage. We rely on our equipment for our AC advantage, but there is very little warrior-specific armor (remember, cobalt is extremely rare) for us to get that helps make AC a defining characteristic. Furthermore, I don't see the problems with spells on our armor...for example, the invisibility on the cobalt legs is a luxury; it simply means we won't have to run to a vendor to buy a bag of potions. At the highest levels, I don't see that as a bad thing, but rather a justifiable reward at those levels. Keep those ideas in mind when evaluating a warrior's AC advantage and the available equipment for us.

Other items have been brought up as well, that may have been missed...this is what I had time to post this morning Back to work! (...until Mongo boss go to der meetin' laytur...)

The biggest problems I see for the warrior class is the armor issue and the taunt issue.

I will not comment on the taunt issue as it has been covered very well by other warriors in these threads. My concern is armor. Cobalt drop rates have been mentioned a lot, but that is just a small part of the larger problem.

The problem is that there is not enough variety of armor for warriors (and all armor wearing classes honestly) and availability of what armor does drop.

The average warrior probably has full crafted by the mid 30s and has little to upgrade to. The next meaningful step up is Indicolite armor from the Plane of Hate. This is no drop armor that many players are never going to see as the planes are typically packed. The next step up is the new Velious quest armor or Cobalt. The Velious quest armor is difficult at best to get and Cobalt has its own set of problems. There is no good armor set in between crafted and planar. This is a huge problem for most warriors who will probably not see Hate armor and will, at most get a piece or two of Cobalt.

So, Verant has created a situation where there are a lot of warriors attempting to get high level armor that is rare. Link that to the way hogh level monsters are designed (to hit hard and fast and require the tank to have high ac.) and there is a problem. The game has been designed so that high level encounters assume the best of gear. Yet the best warrior gear (Cobalt) has the worst drop rate of any class armor and the new Velious stuff is as hard or harder to get and does not address the armor gap between Crafted and planar.

I have two suggestions to help with this problem, and I do think it is the biggest one that effects warriors. Armor and weapons are to a warrior what new spells are to a caster. Yet until the 50s a caster can feel secure that they will get new spells every few levels in a constant manner. Warriors (and other melee) must get equipment to stay in the power curve. Good weapons are not a problem. They are very available at all level ranges. Armor is not.

My two suggestions-
1.) Make a new set of armor quests targeted at the AC range in between Crafted (22ac on the BP) and Indicolite (25 ac on the BP.) Make it difficult and expensive, but make it so that more warriors can get armor that will at least give them a moderate armor class as they approach the 50s.

Please take this into consideration when making the final list. More armor options helps all the classes and gives more flexibility to the melee classes.

Everyone agrees that Taunt is broken, but is it the skill or the action that is the problem? I agree with Absor when he states that just by using the Taunt skill (ie yelling insults at a creature) shouldn't put you at the top of the list all the time. It just doesn't make sense. Another way a Warrior 'taunts' his opponent is by the damage and rate he hits the said opponent. Unless you've got super-weapons that extremely fast at dealing damage, a average Warrior cannot fulfil his role properly.

Looking at this problem from a different angle, a Warrior is supposed to be a fighter who is THE master of melee both in regards to offence and defence (not necessary the greatest damage dealer though) but this is not the case. Warriors get the Double Attack skill at Level 15, which at that time helps us taunt even better, but it isn't until 45 levels later that we finally get a Third attack. From a Roleplaying perspective, wouldn't it seem reasonable that as a Warrior became more experience at his 'craft' he would find ways to utilised his weapons better and be able to hit quicker by adding extra attacks in? Why is it that only right at the end of our careers we finally get given a 3rd attack as some token reward?

Looking at the 3rd Edition D&D rules, Fighters are given a 4th attack at Level 16 (might be 18 not sure, haven't got my rulebook here) but the way it is implemented is that the first attack has the greatest bonus and then each attack after that becomes gradually weaker, implementing something like this into EQ would be quite possible. What I propose is that a Third Attack skill becomes available at Level 40 (or at Level 35 by replacing the Disarm skill) and then a Fourth Attack skill is given at Level 60. Having these extra attacks will help Warriors tremendously in keeping mobs onto us, yes it will make us more powerful but the sliding damage/attack bonus for each attack would lessen this effect.

I would hazard to say to give Monks, Paladins and Shadow Knights a Third Attack too sometime during their career (but not Thieves, by their very nature they are not suppose to be fighters) but never a fourth attack.

From my point of view, this would be the best way to make us as Warriors be able to 'taunt' effectively and do our job properly.
