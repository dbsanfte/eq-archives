This post doesn't address what I think is the biggest enchanter balance issue: We die too much. Many have suggested better defensive skills; I don't think that's the way to go. One of the following would be more consistent with enchanter abilities:

* Illusion: Corpse (i.e. feign death) -- this is a combination of two enchanter specialties (illusions and hate-list manipulation), which I think justifies the giving of a necro/sk/monk ability to enchanters

* Illusion: Gate (like the D&D spell Shadow Door) -- use the same particle effects/messages as the Gate spell, but have the effect of making the caster invisible and wiping the hate list (as with feign death). This could be seen as less diluting than Illusion: Corpse, and the unreliable duration of invis balances it.

* A fast-casting spell that moves the enchanter further down on the hate list (as with rogues' evade or rangers' jolt).

----------------------------
To address a few other points on your list:

* Failure Messages - Memory Blur also gives the same message on success and failure. Blur doesn't get a normal resistance check; since you're being more open about game mechanics (as on the rogue evade skill), it would be nice to know a bit more about how this spell line works.

* Better pet control - I don't care about /pet attack; the only pet command I want is /pet back off, so that I can keep a pet out for extra damage without worrying about it constantly breaking mez.
* Illusion races bug - there is a related graphical bug with elemental illusions: people who zone in after the illusion is cast see the wrong textures.

* Mesmerization spells - on a related point, the Hole operates under old-world rules (level 53+ creatures can't be mezzed), but it was added after Kunark and is designed with post-50 players in mind. Some mobs there can't be mezzed due solely to this rule; there is no way to know besides trying it.
