Just my quick understanding of the differences in tanking ability between Shadowknights/Paladins and warriors. I'm sure the Live team knows all the difference, but I want to be sure everyone is fully aware of just what kind of a gap there is.

#1. Warriors armor is multiplied by 1.6 to figure out total armor class. Pal/SK is multiplied by 1.5. That's 1 extra AC for every 10 AC your armor gives.

#2. Warrior armor is typically 4-9 AC higher than the equavelent Pal/SK armor post Kunark. Bracers are 20 vs 16. BP is 45 vs 40. Non-legs are 25 vs 21 (except for gloves which are 25 vs 16??). Legs are 26 vs 35???. That's a free 43 AC, plus the added 1 AC for every 10 means with just RoK/PoG armors, warriors run 352 AC while Pal/SKs run 265 AC. That's a difference of 87 on visible armor pieces alone.

#3. Warriors have a higher defensive cap than Pal/SK. Warrior defense caps at 250, Pal/SK caps at 230. The extra 20 defense adds 60 AC. This on top of superior armor gives warriors a 147 AC advantage.

#4. Warriors have higher dodge, parry, and reposit skill caps. These are intangable, but definatly effect total damage intake.

#5. Warriors have higher hitpoints than Pals/SKs. Especially considering warriors get additional hitpoint bumps at certain levels that hybrids don't, and the fact that only shadowknights can be large races with huge stamina stats. Dwarves are the only hybrid that start with 100 sta. This makes CHs more effecient on warriors.

#6. Warriors have the Defensive Discipline. This gives them the ability to tank an ubermob while allowing a cleric or a battery of clerics a prayer in hell of keeping them alive. Zretch (my SK) has died as quickly as one combat round to Trakanon. Defensive and huge hitpoint pools give warriors a fighting chance.

In my opinion, Verant should be adjusting #2 and #6 and leaving all else alone. #2 would keep our relationship to warriors about the same as a ranger's relationship to warriors on the offensive side. Same quality of equipment, lacking the skills to use it as effecitvly. (We are knights in shining armor, after all) #6 would give us a fighting chance to actually receive a cleric heal against mobs that hit over 600. I would probably give them the level 52 Defensive Disc. It helps, but it isn't nearly as good as the 55 one. Also double the timer on it so it only refreshes once every half hour.

Anyway, if hybrids are going to get a defensive upgrade, I would give it to them in the armor/disc area. Warriors should be more skilled but in no way should a Knight have inferior equipment. This would still allow warriors to keep their skill superiority, their ubermob tanking superiority (we have a defensive skill, just not the best one), and their hitpoint superiority, making them a more effecient and effective tank in all ways, but allowing hybrids to use their spells to greater effect when versatility is called for. ie, balanced.

1. Defense Skill Cap - Good change, possibly too much (on test). Consider balanced offense/defense instead of all defense upgrade as I think we're between rangers and paladins.

3. Hit Points - not a prime concern
4. Bash skill Cap - raising this makes sense
5. Better shield use - unnecessary and would be too much trouble for little gain

8. Better 2-hander - Personally I feel that 2 handers need their minimum damage raised. Fast one handers are still superior even after the damage bonus adjustments. But for SKs specifically I don't feel we need an "incentive" to use them.
9. Taunt - we can already taunt just fine. It would border on being perverse to add to this
10. Archery - This is unnecessary. Archery is for pulling, and teh damage it does is minimal anyway.
11. Pets - The pets between levels 15 and 49 aren't useful. The pet buff on the test server might address this.
12. Spell Review - The new spells on test are nice, but I'm not sure if the post-50 spell changes were necessary.
13. Casting Times - this is a great idea that would let us actually use teh higher level spells, and I think the changes being tested would work nicely.
14. Lifeleech - this quest is comparable in difficulty and price to the other hybrid spells, and is very powerful for it's level. Nothing needs to be changed here.
16. Lifetap DOT's - these should probably stack with the cleric versions
17. Lifetaps - On test high level SKs now have more powerful lifetaps from the necro line. These aren't very useful because of the long cast times (possibly ties in to #13). This change is kind of pointless, especially since large encounter npc's can't be lifetapped anyway. If Verant feels that they definately should add more lifetaps to our spell list, consider making an upgrade to Life Leech (high mana cost/recast in exchange for lowered casting time)
18. Gather Shadows - Acceptable at the level we get it.
19. Voice Graft - available via an item, not a real issue, but not exactly a balance issue either
20. Vamp Embrace - this spell is fine as is. Upgrading it to necro levels would invalidate Shroud of Death and be unbalancing. Proc rate is pretty random but acceptable.
21. Grim Aura - I don't see the point in this being added (it's on test server now), but others seem to like it
22. Low level evoc's - Not an issue since you can practice this by casting on skeleton pets.
24. Blood Ember - Weight is in line with other class armors since the few pieces that were out of whack have been fixed. Perhaps you meant Darkforge armor?
25. Unholy Aura - This discipline already adds enough damage to make it useful, and lifetapping 5% of the damage it does wouldn't change anything. (That's 75 points at level 60 btw)
26. Leechcurse - This does need to be improved. I support the changes being tested on the test server as a way to balance this.
27. Ashenbone - I always agreed with this.

Within the SK community the 1H + shield vs. 2H choice often falls along large race (can bash without shield) and small race (must use shield to bash) divisions. I believe that these changes would be better brought up as part of more of an overall combat tweak among all non-dual wielding classes than as a class specific isssue. A large percentage of SKs are larger races which do not even use shields and would not benefit at all from any proposed changes to shield use. Likewise many small race SKs use 1H weapons and shields and would not benefit from better 2H use.
