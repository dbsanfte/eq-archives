I'm somewhat reluctant to do this, but since that last thread was corrupted (Innoruuk strikes again, I'm sure!)...

What is a Paladin?
In terms of Game mechanics, we're "Defensive Tanks". In literature and history, we're the defenders of the weak and innocent; the knights in shining armour who save the day, who persevere despite all odds. Lancelot. Roland. Joan of Arc.

The problem is to allow Paladins to fulfill that role, without intruding to far into the realm of those classes that we draw much of our abilities from. How can this be accomplished? At this point, we have spells drawn from the Cleric class, making us the equal of a Cleric much lower than our own level. Spellcasting is not our primary function, so this is appropriate. As fighters, we have some of the abilities of the Warrior, but Match them in no way (the changes on Test not withstanding). We also have our own special ability, granted by our diety- Lay on Hands.

In spite of what we have, we do nothing particularly well. As levels progress, soloing becomes less of an option, yet our appeal to groups becomes equally limited. If there's no Cleric around, then a Paladin might "pinch-hit", provided there's another tank in the group... and provided the opposition isn't too rough. We can fill in for the a Warrior... provided we aren't the main source of melee damage.

How do we fix the Paladin? First of all, let's dispense with the notion of "Parent Classes". This terms does more to hurt Paladins, Rangers and Shadowknights than any other notion in the game. Yes, we all draw abilities from other classes... but so too do all the other classes- anyone can melee to some degree, even if they would be foolish to do so. Does that make the Warrior a parent class to all?

The Paladin needs changes that are in keeping with the role it plays, both in terms of mechanics and "atmosphere". Here is what I suggest:

1) An increase, across the board, in Taunt (this is to all Taunt-capable classes). Give the Warriors a sizeable boost, and a scaled boost to the other classes with the skill. This will help keep "weaker" (in terms of AC and HP) party members alive longer.

2) Implement the defense changes now on the Test server, allowing Paladins to match a Warrior's base defense. Thus, under normal conditions, we will be able to stand in the face of danger just as long.

3) Lift the cap on Paladin's Bash skill. We of all classes are most likely to use a shield- it is a defining characteristic in many ways. Let us keep it effective. The damage resulting is minimal, but the taunt effect is important to our role.

4) Increase our 1HS caps. Frankly, I do not wish to do as much damage as a Warrior (mainly to keep them happy, I'll admit), but I'd like to at least hit the target more frequently. If it is possible to increase our hit frequency without increasing the damage per hit, I would be happy. The again will increase the effectiveness of our taunting. Focus new Paladin weapons on 1HS.

5) Give us some unique, Paladin-only abilities or spells, in keeping with our role. An AE protection spell or ability, that lasts until those under the protection attack would fit us perfectly.

6) Don't give us any more "hand-me-down" abilities. In most cases, the spells we have received to date are really only useful if we are soloing (not likely!), travelling through dangerous areas, or in a group with no Druid, Cleric or Shaman of roughly equal level. The heals we have are fine for "topping up" between fights, to ease the burden on the Clerics.

Some additional words about weapons: Each of the melee classes has a distinct style- Rangers, having Dual Wield, often opt for two fast weapons in place of a single, two handed one. Warriors, having access to some great weapons, are equally comfortable with either, though many prefer the use a single, two-handed weapon to inflict maximum damage. Paladins are blessed with the ability to use a shield and a one-handed weapon, yes are often forced to resort to two-handed ones in order to remain effective. This is due to a combination of (a) the low skill cap on Bash, and (b) the relative scarcity of good 1HS weapons for our class. Even the majority of our holy swords are two handed (why, I'll never know). This, for me, is the greatest issue I have with the class. I don't play a Paladin to be the absolute, undisputed ruler of the battlefield, I play a Paladin because of what the class *is* (and not in terms of mechanics).

I'll try to make it brief. First, let me thank VI for looking into the issue of game balance, which is a problem that can ruin all the fun of the game for hardcore gamers like me.
Next, all I'm going to say only applies to level 55+ (mostly 60, in fact).
The purpose of this post is informative (for pallies and VI), and it's to compare the defensive abilities of various tank classes. I will however also make some suggestions at the end.
Now you should know that my specialty (in RL as in EQ) is to crunch numbers. I do not believe in vague sentences about how this or that class should be a better thank because of AC, or skills, or whatever else, I only believe in log parsing.
I have therefore parsed about 50 Mb of data collected during raids in PoG. This corresponds to several weeks of intensive PoG farming by my guild, and should lead to extremely small statistical error (about 1%). I collected everyone's hits / tanked hits and did various operations (all of which were meant to help paladins, as you will see):
* did not take into account defensive disc. each time a pure tank would use a disc the data would be discarded for the duration of the disc.
* removed the logs of all bosses. this is because some people (e.g. paladins with their epic) tend to over-aggro for very long periods of time, resulting in severe inbalance of tanking during long fights.
Ok so since this is a talk about class balance I compared 4 classes: warriors, monks, rogues and paladins (I did not have enough data for rangers and SKs, sorry).
