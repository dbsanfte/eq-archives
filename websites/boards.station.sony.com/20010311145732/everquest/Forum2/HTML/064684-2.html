bellicus,

You are almost right about copyright but are wrong about how it applies here. Consider the following:

1. Gambit, the EQ character, is *not* a fictional character. He is a persona for a real person. He is not part of a story being sold for profit. If Gambit were an EQ npc, then there'd be trouble.

2. Copyright doesn't protect the name (that's what trademarks are for), copyright protects the 'look and feel' or, as you put it, the 'image and reputation' of the character. Thus, as long as it can be shown there is no relationship between the two characters sharing the same name, there is no copyright violation. Gambit, the EQ character, does not throw high energy cards pulled from his sleeve, he doesn't speak with a French accent (I presume), he doesn't look like the X-Man, etc. He doesn't pretend to bare any relation to the X-Man character. Thus, no copyright violation.

3. Most importantly, copyright means absolutely nothing unless the copyright-holder is losing revenue from the alleged violation. Marvel loses no revenue from Gambit, the EQ character, having that name.
