Look at your life, and identify a problem. Solve it. You'll be amazed.

I quit EQ back in May sometime, with well over 100 days played across a few characters, 90 or so on my primary, in less than a year.

I do believe cold-turkey is the only way to go, if at least for a limited time. I was also playing with my girlfriend (in the same room, at least) for the most part. After a couple months of cold turkey, I returned to the game to start new characters with my girlfriend. Even with our supertwinked druid/ranger combo the game quickly lost our interest.

The primary thing that addicts you, in my opinion, is the interpersonal network, and severing that network of people is required to take an objective look at the game. You quickly realize how empty these in-game relationships and friendships were when you reforge you real-live friend and family ties. If you return to the game fresh (perhaps on a new server, or as new characters) you realize how empty and dull the game is without these pseudo-friendships.

Please don't argue with me that you have great, lifelong friends made through the game. I'm sure some people did, and my strongest friendship to date I met on a local chat BBS when we were in our teens. What really killed these in-game friendships for me was my eventual disgust at the amount of time and effort they put into the world of Norrath. You really have nothing more in common with most EQ people, and once that's taken away, things disintegrate. Furthermore, they often do nothing but serve as reminders for wasted time you wish you could have back.

At any rate, I must agree that the above is correct when saying that it's not Verant's fault. Consider the glurge on the back of the Kunark CD-ROM:

"Welcome to the most immersive online 3D fantasy world ever created. Join more than a thousand people online and experience a never-ending quest so addictive and dynamic, you'll never want to return to the real world again."

They're very clear up front. Kind of scary, isn't it?

I recently quit EQ a few weeks ago (for about the 2nd or 3rd time). The previous times were just a week or so breaks, but so far this break has been final. Canceled my account, uninstalled the game, and haven't been back on.
One thing that make it hard to quit is friends, friends online still ask when I'm coming back, even though I really don't want to. Work has been slow so that doesn't help, but I refuse to play again. I read posts that remind me how tedious and boring the game generally is.
I kind of see EQ like a person who has passed away. Once they are gone, you remember only the good things and filter out any of the bad things. I think playing EQ is sort of the same way, sure anyone can name 10 or more reasons why the game really sucks, and probably 1/2 that of why it's good but you pass off the bad things (prime example is to tell a friend about it, 'sure there aren't a lot of quests, but you can do a bunch!', when we all know the quests are few and far between and 95% not worth doing).

Things to help quit:
get out more, and don't even log on for a minute (we have all been there when you want to play for 10 minutes and end up camping a few hours later). when kids leave home they are supposed to move so many miles from their parents as to 'break free', if they move close by, chances are they will still rely on them more then they should. when you quit EQ, it's too hard to wane off it, you just have to quit. delete the game off your hard drive (that way if you start to reinstall, you may quit before it finishes). you'll find the longer you are away the less and less you miss the game, unlike yearning to play it more and more as time goes on (as most enjoyable things actually work..i.e. can't wait to go snowboardin).
when friends talk about the game, just remember the bad factors of the game and point it out to them (kind of funny how they'll deny most bad facets of the game even when they know they're wrong :)
The way I see it, a lot of people playing EQ really don't want to play, but it's become a habit and they continue to play. The game has a life spawn of about 6-8 months (which is incredible for a game), most everyone I know past that has lost a great amount of interest in the game, yet they continue to play (for whatever reason).
