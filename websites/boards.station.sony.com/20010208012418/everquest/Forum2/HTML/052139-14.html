Quite honestly, I, like many other post 50 enchanters, have felt myself slipping increasingly into the auxilary buffing role that so many others have talked about. The alternative is really mobs so easily controlled (by a good enchanter) that we are relegated to a very tedious buffing role. Swapping 3-5 spells out every 2 minutes is very aggravating... and with the demand for Boon Of the Garou (which isn't a particularly productive spell, truth be told), one more spell slot is gone.

On the plus side, with the way that our Memory Blur series fails consistently on high level spawns, it's relegated to the unmemorized page.

Though if Reoccurring Amnesia actually REOCCURRED, we wouldn't have the instant tashaniaing death scenarios that occur above. Just a thought. Fix that, if you could.

Additionally, I'm of the school that if a monster can do something, there should be a player class that can do it as well. Similarly, that if a player class can do something, an NPC class should be able to do it. (A good example is the MOBs that now cast Gate... a great addition.)

SO...
1. SUMMON. Not Magician summons... I don't want to step on their toes and make the poor class even less desireable (sorry, guys, I still like you). But an ENCHANTMENT type of summon... thus...

A Froglock Ilis Wizard begins to cast the Gate spell.
The_Group_Cleric tells the group, "dammit. he gated, sorry. zone."
You begin to cast Summons of Will.
Maldisdain says, "You will not evade me, A Froglock Ilis Wizard."
<Battle spam>
The_Group_Warrior_With_Epic_Weapons_CoF_and_Haste has slain A Froglock Ilis Wizard.

In short, here is the idea: a single target spell that, maybe, costs a lot of mana, has a long recast time, or just a huge aggro (Of these, I recommend a HUGE aggro and low mana cost & casting time. It makes the game much more epic when classes put their lives on the line for the group.) and that summons a monster back in the same way that high level mobs can summon people to them.

It makes chanters MUCH more useful in high-level single mob situations (such as when a camp has already been broken) and it adds a real FEEL of excitement... like we characters are beginning to become *truly powerful* and not just low-budget would-be mobs.

2. Group Dispel Magic.
I know someone else has already brought this up, but I thought I'd reiterate it because I think it continues in the vein of giving PCs some of the same abilities of high level mobs. SUMMARY:
1. Add a spell "Summons of Will" that calls back a fleeing mob to the enchanter's feet and makes the mob aggro HARD on the chanter. Make it fairly low mana cost and quick cast/recast time.
2. Add an area of effect dispel magic (that would only affect mobs).

After reading through six pages of posts (sorry, all I had time for)....I have noticed that certain things are mentioned repeatedly, and thus I have some hope that these will be addressed. However, the many of these problems are the way the are because the alternatives were seen as overbalancing. With this in mind, even though some parts of this post may seem redundant, I will address these issues once again for two reasons: 1) Repetition stresses the importance. If the majority of those who play the class agree that a spell is useless as written (i.e. Lull), then perhaps it truly does merit change. 2) Although we most of us agree about what the problems are, a nearly endless variety of solutions have been proposed. Hopefully, in the morass of possible fixes for the problems will be one solution that will both satisfy those who play the class and those whose job it is to keep the various classes balanced.

Prior to proposing any changes, I wish to make a brief comment about class balance and enchanters. The statement that they are overstrong at low levels concerns me. This is NOT the case. Granted, under certain circumstances (multi-mob pulls in dungeons) they are indespensible and can make an otherwise disastrous pull survivable. Does this make them overly powerful? NO. It makes them a specialist class. They solo less effectively than most mobs, and if you are NOT in an area where multi-mob pulls are commonplace, you are relegated to you _secondary_ role of buffer/ debuffer. Thus, rather than being overly powerful, they are simply very good at one thing..crowd control.

That being said, the pre-50 levels do not need any major changes to achieve balance. Sure, we cant solo, but that doesnt mean we need better pets, better DD or snare. That is simply the penalty we pay to be so valuable in crowd control (again, we are _specialists_). As I have not played the post-50 game yet, I cannot directly address their issues, but I am concerned that those who have post here believe that our class defining abilities of crowd control have been completely eliminated. In my opinion, this is the equivelent of making warriors unable to hit any of these mobs. Sure, they would still have the secondary abilities of taunting mobs off of the casters and absorbing damage while the wizards kill them, but they would no longer be able to perform one of their primary functions.

The changes I would like to see (roughly in order of priority) are:


Lull: This spell is useless in almost all conditions (the only exception being breaking up a pair of low resistance mobs - and there are other ways of dealing with that situation). The biggest problem is that it is single target, highly resisted and aggro. Of these, the fact that it is aggro is the biggest problem. Keeping it a single target, non-aggro spell with a fairly short duration (say one min, or even random with a check once a tick) would make it useful, but NOT overly powerful, as you try to get the spell cast on all of the mobs in the camp before the first one wears off so that the party can break the camp. Outdoors, you are still much better off with a single casting of Harmony and indoors the spell becomes useful, but still dangerous to use (as the lull wears off of one mob while you are standing in the middle of the camp casting lull on another mob) and limited.


Illusions and Religion: As did many of us, I chose to worship a god before I found out how limiting that would be (I foolishly believed that the illusion spells would still work to allow travel). However, given the mechanics of the game, I do believe that actions should have consequences, and I do not believe that casting a dark elf illusion should necessarily allow one to completely negate ones previous actions. However, the illusions should 'obscure' ones choice of religion. In other words...Casting an illusion should carry with it an emulation of agnosticism. Since I dont know how faction information is stored, I dont know how difficult this is. If faction is stored as a base value (race+religion) modified by subsequent actions, then modification of the religion value is no more difficult than is the modification of the race value. If, on the other hand, the original starting number is lost, and your faction versus any group is stored as a single number (the combined sum of starting faction, including religion, as modified by actions), this is more difficult.
If this is the case, than a more difficult solution may be necessary. One possible solution (which has been suggested before, such as the faith mask (god) suggested yiktopo, which will give the player the starting faction of a newbie character of that religion. To make this spell a little less powerful, I would give it two important weaknesses: 1) It is of random duration (like invis) and 2) it breaks if the caster performs certain activities (like scale of wolf). This spell should be of high enough level that it comes after all of the standard illusions, but NOT post 50.

Copy cat illusions : There are two reasons why enchanters want to have more interesting and varied illusions. The reasons can be summoned up as entertainment and security. Those who want these illusions for entertainment purposes simply want the ability to assume the form and dont care if they remain KOS to the mob and get no benefits from the form. Those who want security want the ability to assume the form of the mob and would like some added benefit, either in the form of benefits (i.e. resistence, str, etc) or faction (no longer KOS to that type of mob). Both of these are legitimate desires, and both can be answered, by providing two spells:

Illusion: Minor Monster (49th). This spell gives the caster the shape and appearance of a randomly chosen mob already present in the zone. As is the case for the magician summon monster spell, the illusion is tinted differently than the mobs already present and thus can readily be distinguished from the native mobs, both by players (thus allowing targeting) and by the mobs (thus giving no faction benefit). This spell neither adds to the global file requirements (or no more so than does the monster summoning spell) nor does it add to the enchanters ability to travel with impunity. This spell would be a logical progression at approx 49th level.

Illusion: Major Monster (55th+). This spell also gives the caster the shape and appearance of a mob already present in the zone. However, it also makes the caster indifferent to mobs of this type. Because coding specific benefits for each illusion type is prohibitive, no other benefits are given. The benefit of this spell (the ability to travel around other mobs of a specific type) is outweighed by the following requirements: 1) The type of mob to be 'copied' must be targeted. 2) The spell has a short range and is actually cast upon the targeted mob (the mob is copied). Although this spell is unresistable and non-aggro, it does have a long casting time (10+ sec). 3) This spell _Does not_ wipe aggro lists..any mob already aggro will remain so, as will any mob whose 'hate list' has picked up your name. The combination of these limits will effectively limit its usefulness to mobs that are low enough in level that you can walk up to spell range and cast the spell, without having them attack you and thus disrupting its casting.

Pets: I can accept the fact that our pets are weak and that we have little control over them. However, I find it ironic that the masters of crowd control have the only pets that CANNOT be prevented from breaking their own mezzes and enthralls. Because they are supposed to be purely defensive, it makes sense that they only come into play when we are attacked, however a way to make them stop attacking mezzed and enthralled mobs would be very useful. The ideal would be for our pets to be unlike every other pet in the game and recognize when a mob is mezzed and NOT ATTACK THEM. However, I find it unlikely that you would give us this. Instead, I would like the abilty to order our pets to back off. No other commands, just this one. Or, as an alternative command, which wipes its aggro list, causing it to forget which mobs have attacked you in the melee (returning it to non-attacking status if neither you nor it take any damage after it is cast).


FInally: Although I know the primary focus of these threads is to balance already existing abilties, rather than to consider whole new abilities which would require a new round of balancing, I would like to propose the following:

Single target invis: A high level invis spell that only affects a single mob. It is cast upon the mob (and thus can be resisted) and prevents that mob from seeing the enchanter. This spell should have a long duration (in line with the benevolence spells) and NOT break when the enchanter casts spells, loots corpses etc. However, this spell will NOT make the enchanter immune to hate lists, etc. Thus, any attack against the mob or any nearby mob will result in the enchanter 'coming to the mobs attention'. Off the top of my head, I would make this a 50th+ level spell.

Mirror Image: This spell is designed to solve the high aggro problems of the enchanter. It is a varient of a pet, and as such you can either have an image or a pet, but not both. It looks like a ghostly (or in some way different) copy of the caster (naked caster look). However, the advantage of this mob is that all spell effects cast by the enchanter appear to have emerged from the mirror image and thus when the mega-taunt spells go off and enrage the mobs, they will attack the mirror image, and not the actual enchanter. This image should have hp approx identical to the enchanter and be buffable, but NOT have any attacks. The marginally higher hp of the mob would be offset by its lack of attacks. An alternative, if readily codable, would be for DD spells to originate from the enchanter, but for the more subtle spells, suchas mez, tash, etc to appear to come from the image.

Magic Items: Many have suggested that the viscous, etc mana line should be more useful, and I suspect that it will become more so as designers find time. However, one idea that has not been used to date, and which has been dismissed as unbalancing and 'making certain classes unncessary' is the creation of items (ideally rings or some such) which can cast effects. The solution I would like to propose is as follows: A magical item can be made which is lore (thus you can never have more than one of a type) and can cast a single effect. To make such an item, you need: enchanted metal, gem, scroll of spell, viscous mana line spell, imbued gem, looted component. Each of these items is there for a specific reason:
The enchanted metal and gem will determine what is made (i.e. ring, etc), as well as adding a specific cost to the item.
The scroll of the spell provides the magic behind the effect.
The viscous mana line spell is to require the involvement of an enchanter of a specific level - in other words, the higher level the effect (or the less available you want the effect to be), the higher level of mana spell needed.
The Imbued or enchanted gem (using the currently implemented spells) is to require the involvement of a caster capable of casting the given spell....In other words, a recipe for a shaman spell, such as sow, would require a shaman to cooperate in the creation of the item by providing the imbued gem.
Looted component: This is simply to reduce the availability of the item. These components can either be common (such as wolf pelt) for some spells, such as a sow or very rare for spells which are more powerful (such as gate). By carefully chosing the component, the availability of these items can be regulated.
The end result of this is the ability to make single use magic items which are lore. Thus, althogh it is still better to have a caster inthe party to actually cast the spell, you have the ability to cast a given spell ONE TIME. This is not unbalancing and will be difficult to exploit, but will result in the creation of a number of useful items. Note: Other items besides jewelry can also be created in a similar fashion, such as bracers with a single charge of a spell. Obviously, any given spell effect will require a specific recipe and you can implement as many or as few recipes as you wish...For example, as a test implementation...You can create five items (for five slots), for some useful, but not overpowering spells (i.e. Invis (silver), Sow (electrum), Clarity (gold), Superior Healing (platinum), and Gate (velium). If this is implemented, some attention should be paid to cost, as few of these spells are going to be worth more than twenty or thirty plat..a fact which currently invalidates the majority of the already implemented alchemy potions...as such, given the presence of a multiple gems, means that silver, electrum and gold are the only metals which can be used (with the possible exception of gates).
