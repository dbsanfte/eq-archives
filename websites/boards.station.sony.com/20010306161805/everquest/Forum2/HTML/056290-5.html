Necromancers are powerful. In terms of "class balance", I do not think there is much that should be added or detracted from Necromancers, as they currently stand. Necromancers do very well soloing. It stands to reason that they should do less well while in a group because of their obvious benefit when alone. However, it is really quite a tragedy that more necromancers are not able to enjoy deep dungeon hunting and well balanced groups as frequently as the other classes. This more social aspect is really what makes EQ the great game it is, and even arguably becomes a necessity to really enjoy the higher levels (epic quests, large raids, etc). I recommend these minor changes:

1. Allure of death line of spells may be "broken" from Verant's point of view, but is, in fact, better implemented the way it is now, than perhaps originally intended. Currently, the loss in hit points is painful, and fact the spell is self only is painful, but this is offset by the accelerated gain in mana --which can make the spell worth it. By increasing the hit point cost, you increase the risk of the spell, and as it stands there are already times I have gone with clarity over the allure line when in a group, either due to risk or pull speed. If the life loss was even more severe than it is now, the spell line would only be a "use when no enchanter is present" solo spell, which actually takes away from one of the very FEW desirable group advantages a necro has, slightly less downtime.

2. Like Shamans and druids received increases in healing to make them slightly more group viable, Necros need increases mezzing to make them more group viable. Screaming Terror helped get into a few groups when I first got it, and this adds an interesting new role to the group Necro. Necros should not be as a good as an enchanter at mezzing. However, it would make them MUCH more group friendly if the disparity between the two classes were not as severe. Groups might look for an enchanter first, but if they couldn't find one, maybe would consider a necro. As it now, if they can't find an enchanter, groups just assume "it can't be done" and go hunt somewhere safer, and more boring.

3. Transfer mana line of spells is a very "necro" like ability. Necros generally don't like the transfer mana role in groups, because it is very boring --but it is also very in character. A necro's enhanced mana generation from eh allure line is bent to aiding others generate mana faster. The necro contributes further by tapping mobs for life, to feed his life hungry mana generation spell. However, these line of spells could be powered up a bit, to make groups a little hungry for them. As it is now, it is more of a novelty rather than something a group would seek out.

4. Necros can heal others by helping themselves. This is role most necros HATE, because lost life means lost mana ...but it is an interesting group role. Necros have very interesting methods of generating life, perhaps a few more ways to lose it by giving it out might make them more group friendly. As it stands now, the transfer of life is inefficient, and relatively slow to the point where it makes it almost pointless to use it, and not something a group would seek. Perhaps a few tweaks here could give the necro a very in character thing to do.

Finally, and nothing to do with group balance, I think the necro resurrect spell (which I haven't used yet), would be very in character if it turned its target into a skeleton for the duration of the resurrection effects. This would just be neat.
