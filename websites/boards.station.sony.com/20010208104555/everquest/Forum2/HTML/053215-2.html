First off the problem with hybrids in general is multipenalites, I went through this last night and looked at our skill levels and it appeared that you guys averaged our skill levels compared to those of our pure classes to determine our melee and casting ability, however thats wrong. At level 50 when hybrids were/are balanced skill differences were alot smaller overall, heres the differences between our melee skills and a warriors at lvl 50, damage output will not be considered in this because that is based soley upon weapon selection/aviabality for how much damage you will do

Those widening caps make us worse than warriors by a large margin because multiple penalites stack, we start off being 88.24% of a warrior in melee (lacking dual weild and kick) now take our penalites into consideration using only the melee skills which we will have in the fight, not including taunt disarm or disciplines, dmg output from weapons is not considered as well, or ac on armor:
92% as good at offense, were now 81.18% as good.
92% defense, 74.69% as good.
92% of one weapon skill, 68.71% as good.
82% in dodge, 56.34% as good
82% in parry, 46.2%
82% in riposte 37.89%
92% of double attack 34.85%
82% of bash 28.58%

That leaves us with 28.58% (being generous as I omitted a few skills there, as well as defensive which makes a huge difference) of a warriors combat ability, this is far from the 75% of them which we supposedly are.

Now I will do the same type of breakdown for spells not taking into consideration the resistability, usefulness or power of the spells in question.

Again, with the exception of our spell selection compared to Necromancers the gaps widened again, not as severley as the melee side of us did but still widened a good deal. Taking into consideration the skills we lack completly we start out with 61.54% of a necro's casting ability, now take into consideration our penalites on the other skills, not taking into consideration mana cost and mana regeneration penalites.
90.9% of the channeling makes 67.7% of a necros ability.
38.8% of the spell selection makes 26.27% of their ability.
86.2% of the meditation ability leaves us at 22.64% of their casting ability.

28.58% of a warrior (before defensive is considered) and 22.64% of a necromancer leaves you with 51.22% of a class when being generous to their skills and completly ignoring some penalites. Verant how can you justify making certain classes this weak? We used to have weapons which were extremly powerful because of no dual weild now (while I wasnt planning on mentioning the weapons by name as I dont want to see them nerfed I will to prove my point) Ill take two weapons: Ebon Mace, and Willsapper
Ebon Mace is a 19/24 weapon, in the hands of a Level 60 SK with 40% haste it does approximatley 32.72 dmg a second after factoring in Double Attack (Eduin posted this same sort of thing earlier on Part One of this discussion, however because he didnt factor in double attack his numbers will be somewhat lower than mine).
Willsapper is a 13/20 weapon which is more common (7 of these on my server since the release of velious, compared to 5 maces since the release of kunark) than an Ebon Mace and can be dual weilded, in the hands of a Level 60 Warrior with 35% haste it will do approximatley 51.8 damage a second (29.78 dmg a second in an sk's hands with 40% haste if you wish to compare)

Now if this isnt bad enough the Ebon Mace is the BEST SK 1h weapon currently in the game with the exception of some stuff possibly found in sleepers tomb. The next closest weapon to an ebon mace in game currently is the Dragon Tooth Shard which comes to 3.1% LESS damage than an ebon mace.

As you can hopefully see Absor or whoever else may be reading this, hybrids (although this has focuses specifically on sk's rest assured were not the only ones with this problem) are in severe trouble, by the time were fighting high level velious encounters were just about as fragile as a caster but we dont even deal the same damage as a warrior using only one weapon. There have been numerous solutions proposed in this discussion so far among them were:
Higher HT
Defensive discipline or something close
Higher skill caps
More hp
More ac
More atk

There were certainly more as well but if you read this thread and listen to the ideas from the higher level sk's (no offense to lower lvl sk's out there but please dont take what they say about the high level game of eq which is generally described as 55+ at face value because they more than likley dont know what there talking about) and do something about it. Absor, you said you knew very little about sk's? Well why not speak to the top sk's on various servers? Im sure most of them (myself included) would be willing to help and suggest ways to improve the class.

Offense, defense, and weapons skills have always been maxed upon reach level 39 and do not increase until you cross 50. It also seems that the spells are supposed to make the difference. Problem has been that most of the spells are simply too watered down to make up for it and there is too much magic resistance in the world.

It seems to me that the hybrids, for whatever they were originally considered, have each become a different flavor.

Ranger = low hitpoint, low AC, good damage machine. They have all the attack skills and their spells are geared toward dealing damage. They are therefore the OFFENSIVE hybrid.

Vampiric embrace is level/2 +1. The proc is truly not that often such that I cannot understand why the SK cannot have that restored to level +1. This spell is usually not memorized. It sucks up too much mana to restore too few hitpoints even after the battle. While all these discussions of specific powers, spells, and skills is fine and good we are all (both players and VI) missing what is core to all these discussions.

**If the game is to be group oriented then there needs to be a role for everyone in a group.**

This in mind it is not really ok to just say "this class is supposed to be defensive" since, in the terms of this game, that could mean a lot of different things. In an effort to clear these thing sup... let's first look at what things a group actually needs:

You can group needs in two ways:

Damage Dealers (melee, ranged, magic)
Damage Absorbers
Utilities (healing/buffing/crowd control)

-or-

Attack (Melee, ranged, magic)
Defense (Taunt, Damage Absorption, Crowd Control)
Utility (Healing, Buffing)

Now - let's look at which class can do what:

Warrior - Best at Melee attack, Best at Taunt, Best at Damage Absorption, Decent with Ranged Attacks. As they can fill two whole areas of a groups needs alone and are the best at them they are highly sought after in groups.

Cleric - Best at Healing, Decent Buffing, Decent Crowd Control, Decent Damage Absorption, Low Melee Attack, Low Magic Attack. As staying alive is one of the main points of grouping the Clerics unsurpassed healing skills make him as highly sought after (sometimes more sought after than the warrior). That little red bar is the only thing standing between us and experience loss after-all.

Enchanter - Best at Crowd Control, Decent Buffing, Low Magic Attack. Highly sought after for the Clarity Spell (which gives clerics the little blue bar that lets them make the little red bar grow)

Shaman/Druid - Best at Buffing, Decent Magic Attack, Decent Crowd Control, Low Damage Absorption, Low Melee Attack, Low Healing. Many groups enjoy having these guys around for the buffs and to have the backup damage, a backup healer and backup crowd control.

Wizard - Best at Magic Attack, Decent Crowd Control. In the cases where a group doesn't have their extra spots filled with multiple warriors (or other "tank") groups like these guys just for the sheer damage output.

The following classes are fairly much left out in the cold unless there is an open "tank" slot that needs filling:

Monk - Decent Melee Damage, Low Damage Absorbtion

Rogue - Decent Ranged Attacks, Decent Melee Damage, Low Damage Absorbtion, Low Taunt (through backstab). Rogues come in handy in those very rare places with locked doors or traps.

Ranger - Best at Ranged Attacks (unfortunalty, anti-perching code prevents this from being any use), High Melee Damage, Decent Taunt, Low Damage Absorbtion, Decent Crowd Control, Low Healing, Low Buffs, Low Magic Attack

Paladin - Decent Ranged Attacks, Decent Melee Damage, Decent Taunt, Decent Healing, Decent Damage Absorption, Low Buffs

Shadow Knight - Decent Ranged Attacks, Decent Melee Damage, Decent Taunt, Decent Damage Absorption, Low Magic Attack, Low Crowd Control

And finally the following classes are also left out in the cold unless another caster is needed:

Bard - Decent Buffs, Decent Magic Attack, Decent Damage Absorption, Decent Crowd Control, Decent Healing, Low Melee Attack

Necro - Decent Magic Attack, Low Crowd Control, Low Melee Attack (through pet)

Magician - Low Magic Attack, Low Buffs, Low Melee Attack (through pet)


Now as you can see things are slightly skewed here as to group usefulness. You have 6 slots in a group. You have 5 classes that are the best at what they do (Warrior's doing multiple things).. leaving one slot open for an additional character who is most likely to be another one of the "best at what they do" classes. If, and only if, one of those classes is not available will one of the lesser classes be chosen... and typically another "tank" over another caster.

I'm not sure if this is something you can really fix as much of what's wrong goes to the core of the game itself. Adding more group slots would be a start as would increasing the abilites of the "lesser" classes so they were as good as the better classes in at least One of their "Best" areas. Then again, if you made the solo game more viable we all could play the game without worrying about all this group balance.
