Eldanon has it right. This is basically a question that could be solved with knowledge of the english language.

"If mages are the masters of sumoning then why cant they SUMMON a corpse?"

Mages are the masters of conjuration. They can conjure, summon, or evoke stuff out of thin air. (IE food, daggers, bandages, arrows, etc.) Basically they are 'summoners' per the LAST (#5) definition of summon in the dictionary. 'To call forth;evoke'.
'Summon Corpse' is not a conjuring type of thing. You are NOT summoning something out of thin air. We are using number 1 or 2 of the definition of the word summon. #1 to call together -or- #2 to request to appear, to send for.
The english language is a complicated thing. They should have called mages the masters of conjuration and name their spells 'Conjure Food', etc. to avoid confusion.
Besides, doesn't the 'Summon Corpse' spell use a 120pp regent? (It the regent consumed or re-usable btw?)
