An issue actually relating to Balance:

Wizards are unwanted in exp groups because they slow the group down. (Specialized AoE maniacs - yes it rocks but it takes a special group, and that special group has strict class requirements which exclude most of the classes in EQ).

The fastest way to gain exp in Everquest is with the following group composition:

1-4: Melee class. 4 rogues is a shredder. 4 monks/warriors - awesome *sustained* damage output. 4 hybrids works too. 3 mage/necro pets and someone to pull works great too.

5-6: Pick any two of : enchanter/cleric/shaman/druid.

An exp group in everquest is a constant stream of blues that the group can take down quickly. The limited sustained damage output of a wizard makes them a hindrance to a group with enough mobs to pull - they will get better exp with a different class. A wizard contributes nothing to a group but damage. Evac is great, but that is more of an exp-loss mitigator rather than a direct contribution to the exp rate.

Wizards are good in groups when:

a) the mob rate is fairly slow, so they have time enough to med back mana spent between pulls

b) the mobs are casters, and this only after level 20 when wizards get their first stun


Wizards are moderately useful in raids. They scale well, since all of their damage spells "stack", but with many/most of the new high-end encounters the mob has so many HP's, or has resistances of such high level, that the contribution of the wizard is meager at best.

The obvious way to increase wizard damage over time is to give them a med bonus. Either give them higher skill caps and advancement rates, or increase Med effectiveness for wizards. Wizards have no way to regain mana other than Meditate.

Some of the proposed suggestions for disciplines are promising as well - disciplines to lower cast time, lower resistance, increase damage, etc.

You're outlining a problem that Verant cannot fix, unfortunately. For mobs with excessive hps, Wizards will NEVER be able to fulfill the pipe dream of "fast damage". In cases like this, and even lesser and far more common cases where the average mob has 15,000+ hp (most of SoV), it's a far better solution to allow Wizards to *gasp* do reasonable damage over time.

Verant, Absor, see any problems with that? Wizards don't offer anything else to your typical grinding group or raid besides nuking. Ports? Ports fall into the same category as unlocking doors, casting invis vs undead on the party, CoH'ing everyone to a spot, etc. They're the "getting there" part and have nothing to do with the "now we're here" part.

Fortunately, Rogues can offer more than just unlocking doors. They do the most damage over time of any class in the game.

Fortunately, Magicians can offer more than just unlocking doors. Their pet gives them superb damage over time and their nukes just add to the total, and they can debuff and provide damage shields.

Fortunately, Warriors can offer more than just tanking. Their melee does damage over time similar to, if not quite the equal of Rogues.

Fortunately, Monks can offer more than just mob separating capability. Their melee power lies somewhere between the similarly powerful Warrior and Rogue.

Unfortunately, Wizards have only their nukes. Similarly unfortunately, Wizard nukes at their typical efficiency, namely a competent L60 Wizard with Clarity 2 using Sunstrike or Ice Spear, amount to not quite half of your average L60 Warrior's damage output. This doesn't take into account ridiculous new weapons from Sleeper's Tomb. Wizards ARE A BURDEN TO THEIR GROUP!

And the solution is to FIX, not "improve", but FIX their efficiency.

I already proposed a proximity-based spell or spells, allowing no increase in efficiency under solo conditions but providing an eight-to-one (close to a Warrior's damage output) efficiency from melee range. Restrictions on recast or damage per cast could be used to prevent the Wizard from doing damage TOO fast (that way, we successfully prevent the Wizard from actually succeeding at what is supposedly his official job in the game).

Calls for a larger mana pool or bigger nukes or even fewer resists are not solutions. My own proposal isn't much of a solution either because under raid scenarios it places the Wizard in a special kind of peril. But efficiency IS the solution, and yes an eight-to-one casting ratio would literally only be as good as a typical Warrior; it would still be inferior to most pure damage classes and Wizards would still have a lack of group desirability to deal with.

I'll try to keep this as short as possible, but I have a lot to say.

The main complaint seems to be that we wiz's have problems finding groups. Verant has always encouraged grouping. It goes along with "The Vision" they had way back when they first came out with this game. So not being able to find a group could be a serious problem.

Personally, I think at the higher lvls, wiz's are pretty well balanced. Yes, we suck early on, but as mentioned before, this weeds out that weak and is probably healthy for our class as a whole. From the mid-30's on, I think wiz's are very good with groups as it is (except in Velious). That's why from here on out, I'm mainly talking about the problems with ahigher lvl wiz.

Complaints about resistances and such were covered before and resulted in our having our spells having a built-in lower resistance and a new line of Lure spells. Yes, they didn't do as much dmg as most of us would've liked, but it was a compromise. A step in the right direction.

Now, the problem is that with Velious, the mobs now have a ridiculous amount of hp. Add to that the high resist rate, and we once again have a problem.

I think the best solution could probably be with the line of spells that was added to our arsenal earlier: the Lure line of spells.

- Lower the Lure resist rate even more. Although they do work a lot better than our normal spells, people have been complaining that they're getting a lot of resists from a "practically unresistable" spell.

- Lower the mana cost for Lure spells. Preferably by half. I know this may seem like a rediculous amount at first, but some of those mobs in Velious have a lot of hp. We would like to do more dmg throughout the longer battles. Even with being able to cast twice the amount of Lure spells, we'll still only be able to take off a tiny amount of hp from the bigger mobs. But at least we'll feel a little better since we'll be busy a little while longer.

- Lower the aggro for Lure spells. This will also make us more benefitial in a group situation, and not really help us while soloing. Unlike most other classes, wiz's have only one main purpose: to do dmg. We don't have the hp's, pets, heals, or other utility spells or skills that other classes use to contribute to the group. We have crap agility, crap AC, and crap hp. If something's pissed at us, we get hit hard, fast, and often. And with no feign or fear spells, there's little chance for escape (this is where the concussion spell comes in, but not all wiz's have that- even lvl 50+ wiz's). Lowering the agro for Lure spells will help us do our job a little better.

- Lastly, I think the casting time for Lure spells should be increased. Right now it's around 6 secs or so. I think 8-10 would be better. The longer casting time will also help justify the lower agro. This will make sure we aren't just chain casting, and it makes Lures significantly less practical for soloing. Yet this won't really hurt us in a group because the mob should be pounding on the tanks.


Okay, in short, Lure's were always meant as a group-type spell. They are used to hit stuff we normally wouldn't be able to hit, and therefore would not be soloing anyway. I feel the changes listed above would make us more appealing for grouping, while not making us overly powerful.
