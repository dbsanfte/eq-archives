Absor, I have tried my best to read the whole thread, and I didn't find these mentioned, so maybe it's worth your reading?

1. -- Pets included in group spells --
With the downgrade of pets to PC abilties, it would follow that they should be included in group spells. You wouldn't have to worry about raising the mana coast of group spells either, because it would balance itself out, as it's a win-win situation. The most common group spells, at least in high levels, are Group Resist Magic and Heroic Bond. If pets got Heroic Bond too, it would prevent some pet classes from begging for Heroism for the pets (yes some do), and save mana from the cleric to do so. Also, and I will discuss this further, it would be greatly beneficial if pets could have Group Resist Magic cast on them without having to beg for single target Resist Magic all the time. I'm sure the chanter would love it if I would stop asking him for MR on my pet.

Of course, this change would affect any and all pets, even those cute little bears. Who knows, maybe they could survive a riposte if they had Heroic Bond?

2. -- Pet MAGIC RESISTANCE is very low --
Pets get slowed left and right, and Grav Flux lands on them almost every time. Grav Flux does no harm to pets, but it carries more than enough agro to cause a pet to ALWAYS switch targets to whatever cast it. Perhaps you should rename the spell to "Herd Pets" because the players almost always resist it, the pets almost never resist it, and the end result is the pets all flock to whatever cast Grav Flux. You should look into raising the magic resistance of pets to a level where spells do not automatically land on them.

3 -- Group damage shields mana ratio --
Clarity, 75 mana
Boon of Clear Mind, 175 mana
2.33 times the mana cost, most group members benefit from clarity, so the group version saves both mana and time

Resist Magic, 85 mana
Group Resist Magic, 200 mana
2.35 times, every group member benefits from +55 MR, so saves mana and time

Heroism, 140 mana
Heroic Bond, 420 mana
3.0 times, every group member benefits from 400hp and 20ac, so saves mana and time

Shield of Lava, 120 mana
Boon of Immolation, 360 mana
3.0 times, only one perhaps two group members benefit from 25pt DS and +25 FR for 6 minutes, so saves time but WASTES mana

What is the idea behind group damage shields anyway? A damage shield is designed for one person only, that being the main tank. The short duration of these spells means that the recipients better get hit often while it's active, or else it's not efficient. A group version of this type of spell ensures that mana is being wasted, as usually only one person is ever being hit.

Lowering the mana cost to 240 (2x the cost of single target) would be a start. But I still don't see the point behind the group version of these spells. They can't really be used for their +FR attribute either, because they last 6 minutes only!

4. -- New spell: Elemental Barrier --
Group version of our Elemental Armor series, entire group gets +50 FR and +50 CR. Very useful for raids (note to self: I might cast more than just COH on raids if I had this). Though not strictly useful in everyday groups, it would be great for raids. It fits in perfectly with the class.

5. -- New pet command: /pet buffs --
Wouldn't it be nice to know what buffs your pet has? It's easy enough keeping track of Burnout and Damage Shields, but when other people buff your pet and keep asking when stuff has worn off, it would be nice to answer "pet needs xx spell" instead of "umm, I dunno"

I don't think any of this is outrageous, it is as you say "minor", but affects the class as a whole.
