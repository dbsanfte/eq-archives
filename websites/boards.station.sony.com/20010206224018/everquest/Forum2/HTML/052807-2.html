Bards are useless on Big Raids, for 50+ characters. The only thing we contribute are high resists, and Thott of Afterlife has demonstrated that any resists over 175 or so are useless anyway.

However, this could be remedied by providing us a high quality damage mitigation song.

Not a Rune type of effect, because high level (50+) creatures hit so hard that a rune would go down instantly, and not a slow, because shamans already do that, and to create stackable slows would probably be unbalancing.

But rather, a song that reduces damage by a set percentage, say 30 percent or so. This would still expose a party to considerable risk, while giving the bard some usefulness against large monsters that require Raid-type assaults to succeed. Make it a level 52 song, since we only get one song at that level currently anyway, while all other 51+ levels have two songs. For balance, keep the duration short so it would require constant playing/singing, similar to the duration on Kelin's Lucid Lullaby.

It fits with the idea of bards as group support and would make bards very useful on large raids, without making us more powerful than other classes.

Please take the time to fully understand the issues that were raised on the Concert Hall. Many of these ideas are well thought out in my opinion. Although I doubt you’ll be able to address all of them I hope you’ll at least give them serious consideration.

I play a 54 bard and of the issues raised, the most important to me are haste stacking and the viability of a bard on epic/uber encounters. Thott has a very good explanation on the history of haste stacking:

http://www.afterlifeguild.org/Thott/haste_stacking.html

The important point is that prior to fixing the way haste stacked a bard/enchanter/shaman could take anyone to maximum haste if they had a haste item when Kunark came out. I can only believe that Kunark was play-tested with this effect in place so the net effect of everyone at max haste could not have been overpowering or Verant would/should have fixed the bug prior to Kunark. My understanding of why the bug was eventually fixed is:
1)Restore relative value of haste items so a Cloak of Flames was actually more valuable than Flowing Black Silk Sash from a haste perspective.
2) Force enchanters to use all of their haste spells since before the fix Augment and a haste item brought a player to maximum haste.

The side-effect of this fix was that bards could no longer take anyone to the haste limit with their songs. While I agree that the best haste buffs should come from a specialist such as enchanters, I would argue that bard haste should stack with other haste effects no matter the level. The benefits to this would be that a bard and a shaman could achieve the same effect as having an enchanter in the group while a bard and an enchanter could achieve the same results that were in effect prior to haste-stacking being fixed. This alone would restore some of the viability of grouping with an enchanter since most of our other effects are dwarfed in comparison. I realize the two Ervaj songs were added for Velious for what appears to be this purpose but I don’t think they do enough. Could you consider at least doubling the percentages if an instrument is equipped and removing the requirement for an instrument all the time? 10% at level 50 for Melody of Ervaj coupled with a 56 shaman buff(Celerity) will almost equal the haste given by the 53 enchanter buff (Aanya's Quickening). This shouldn’t be overpowering with enchanters since a character with a haste item will quickly hit the cap anyway. This also implies that a 54 bard could provide slightly better haste than a 56 shaman if he dedicates 2/3 of his time and uses instruments. If he doesn’t use instruments then he creates the same equivalent haste as the shaman for a higher opportunity cost.

With regards to uber mobs and epic encounters, I’m disheartened to see my role as a resist buffer diminished to the point of non-usefulness. When my guild fought Antonican dragons, my resist songs often made it possible for people to stay in the fight when they normally would have succumbed to fear or be destroyed by the dragons breath. This does not seem to be the case with Kunark/Velious mobs where maximum resists of 255 and an apparent soft-cap of 175 offer the same protection. If this is the case then the new blue diamond jewelry plus other items effectively eliminates this role for me on raids and turns me into a liability rather than an asset. Several of the suggestions such as improving melee damage and mana drain are attempts to provide bards with a job to do again. I believe that two bards should be as effective as one enchanter at draining mana. I’m not sure how to improve our melee output without encroaching on paladins and shadowknights.

Lastly, I would like to state that I really enjoy playing a bard. I understand fully that I’m a support class but always liked the idea that I could tip the scales in favor of my group. By working in the background, by making the group more efficient, and by filling in where needed, I felt I made a positive contribution beyond just a walking mana/health battery. Please give me a role to play in the end-game encounters. Thanks.

Hello Absor. I play a 55th bard and a 52nd bard. I have been playing my bard since 1 month after the release of EverQuest, and I have completed several high end bard tasks, including several Sky Quests, and my Epic Weapon. Thus, I feel I am qualified to comment about bards.

First, let me suggest the one nerf that I believe is justified for our class. Add mana-usage to our 27th level charm song. As it is, this charm is ridiculously powerful, and makes soloing gobs of experience easy. Adding manausage to this song, as in the 37th level charm, would definitely be justified.

However, despite the power of our charm, there are several issues with the higher level game. First, let me address our crowd control abilities.

Our crowd control is perfectly balanced. I can mez 3 mobs consistently, 4 with a bit of luck and while taking a bit of pounding. However, our charm begins to decrease in usefulness as we level. Quite simply, the level cap on charm needs to be raised from 51 to 54 or 55. The level cap on SoT needs to be raised to 57. And, while we're discussing level caps, the level cap on Appalling Screech (Fear) needs to be raised to 55 or 56. The songs are perfect, and just as they should be, however, the level caps simply need reworking.

For ideas on our healing, damage output, haste, and mana regeneration, please refer to the excellent articles Thott linked to. Also note that bards were originally the only class with mana regeneration abilities, and enchanters were given the ability as not to give bards a monopoly. I feel that our abilities should reflect this, and slightly increasing the efficiency of Chorus of Clarity and Cantana would not be out of line.

Please allow the enchanter spells Breeze, Clarity, and Clarity 2 to overwrite Cantana. It is obnoxious as all hell to have to stop my manasong every time some caster in my group wants a c2.

I consider equipment issues to be balance issues as well- if a bards high-end equipment is not as powerful as it should be, then the bard is less powerful than he should be. The opposite also applies. Thus, let me address a few issues.

Melodic Greaves. The only Growth legs without an effect. Why?

Melodic Breastplate. Selo's Song of Travel- useful in theory. However, when you right-click on the Breastplate, your new song immediately breaks the old song, and your invisibility, levitate, and speed immediately drop for the three seconds it takes for the song to recast. An utterly worthless effect, which needs changing or reworking.

Singing Steel Greaves: Summon Food. Need I say anything more?

Singing Steel Breastplate: Invigorate heals less than our songs. Also note that our songs heal the entire party. Also note that, when using songs, we can grant the party mana regeneration and stamina regeneration. Please change this effect to either a 5 second casting time, or make it a self-only Greater Heal. Neither would be unbalancing, and would simply reflect the actual intention of this piece of armor.

Singing Steel Boots: Summon Drink. See Singing Greaves.

Singing Steel Helm: Eye of Zomm. Gosh, this "need I say more" thing is getting pretty old.

Singing Steel Arms: Invigorate. We have songs that do it better, do it for the entire party, and do other things besides. Eliminate or upgrade.

Plane of Air quests:

Mask of the Songbird: Perfectly fine item.

Songweavers Mantle: The AC of 9 is somewhat subpar on this item. Please consider an increase to 12. Considering that Gullerback, a creature I can with little to no trouble, drops a mantle with AC 12, I hardly think that an item which requires a rare drop the the Keeper of Souls would be justified in having a mere 9 AC.

Agilmente's Flute of Flight: Nice item.

These are the only 3 Sky quests I have completed, so I can't comment from firsthand experience on the others. However, the AC of 4 on the Amulet seems underpowered, please consider an increase to 6.

Please consider increasing the Rune on Shield of Song to double it's current strength.

Velious Songs:

Melody and Composition of Ervaj are absolutely useless. My epic weapon adds 55% haste. I usually have either an enchanter or shaman around to buff on top of that, often with Shissar. Most tanks I group with use items with 30%+ haste. When am I EVER going to use an additional 5% haste? I would rather twist *shudder* manasong than these. Please set them as Manadrains, as they were originally.

Occlusion of Sound is nice enough, keep it.

Give us a song. The rest of the Hybrids got one, why can't we? Else, admit we're not a hybrid and give us the levels we should have were it not for our hybrid experience penalty. I applaud you on the removing of it, yet choose whether we are hybrids or not. If yes, give us our song. If no, give us our levels.

Possible song ideas/changes:

50 Melody of Ervaj: Change this to a targetted manadrain.

55 New Hybrid Song: Make this an AE manadrain, one that actually works (unlike Dissension).

60 Composition or Ervaj: Make this a more powerful targetted manadrain.

Adding manadrains to the bard class would give us a nice comfortable niche in raids. Our old niche in raids was to pump the resists of the other fighters. However, with exceptional new resist gear available, a cap of 255 on resists, and a series of diminishing returns past 175, this is no longer necessary. Manadrains would give us a role.

There are several more problems with the bard class, however, I feel that Thott and others have addressed them more than adequately. Please note what I and others have said, and consider balancing the class accordingly.
