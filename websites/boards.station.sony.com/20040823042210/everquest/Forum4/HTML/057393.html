This weekend, some friends and I took a little trip to Veksar. It's an interesting zone, and was a blast to hunt. However, the experience did make me wonder if perhaps I was a bit too low to be in the zone.

The mobs were hitting in the low 300's fairly quickly, so I figured the slow was a better choice. However, given the resists I was seeing, I was lucky to have the slow stick. What would other shamans have done in that instance?

Unfortunately the mobs in Veksar have insane resists to poison and I believe disease as well. I hunt there often with my necro and I never use anything but fire, magic and my DD undead spells for damage. I know that doesn’t help your situation as I don’t think you have fire or magic dots. Your cold DD should work as you mentioned about the JBB.

I also cast my slow which does get resisted once in a while. I would suggest you start out with a debuff which should help your chances of landing a spell.

Your level may also have something to do with what you encountered. The lowest level mobs I believe is the BloodGill shaman which is 44-47. The lowest undead I believe is a Phanton at 45-47 but most of the undead are in their mid to upper 50’s with some in the 60’s.

Unfortunately, your dots are going to get resisted unless you land the debuff first and even then they still might get resisted. Stick with the support role if you are in a group, letting your pet chew on the mob and slowing, healing, and haste.
