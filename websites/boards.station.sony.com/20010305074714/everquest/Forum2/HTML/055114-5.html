After a very bad start, I am quite happy with how rogues ended up. I just have a few minor things I'd like to see addressed.

Evade- The changes they are considering making don't worry me much. Evade was implemented when the hate lists were much shorter. Evade being changed to only lowering you, say, 5 to 8 slots on the hate list wouldn't be much too complain about. However the change is implemented, the problem they are trying to correct is the ability to drop too far on the hate list, not lessen the day to day effectiveness of evade.

At least I hope not. Evade as it is now in a single group situation is fine. It works maybe a little less than half of the time, and it can be "interrupted" by a mob hitting you too soon to hitting the hide key. These are reasonable drawbacks to a very useful skill.


Sneak/Hide- Its been brought up a ton already, but I would absolutely love to see it work across zone lines/LD/camping. If it can't be done for technical reasons, I love the idea of making mobs non-aggro for a few seconds after zoning to give us time to hide. I don't see how that could be abused to give us maybe 5 seconds or so to hide before getting killed if we zone into a hostile environment.

As much as I'd like to be able to sneak faster underwater, it doesn't make much sense. =P

I would however, like to be able to sneak faster in all directions, not just forward. To me, I picture a rogue dodging from corner to corner, checking all around to make sure no one sees him while sneaking around. Sneaking faster only straight forward doesn't make a lot of sense. When I have to sneak out of Sebilis from zone in, I have to strafe and do a lot of aggro-checking because random frogs see invis. I have to pass behind the ones that do see invis so that sneak works and they don't see me. Increased sneak speed in all directions would make this a lot easier, and I can't see a balance reason to not allow it.

I also agree that increased sneak speeds should start sooner. =)


Illusions- Been mentioned before. Enchanters can click-cancel theirs. Rogues and bards have to zone, camp, or wait for theirs to wear off. Is this intentional? I'd love to be able to click cancel them when I need to.

Weaponry- It would be great to see something other than 10 damage weapons for the mid to high level range. It just seems that the overwhelming majority of weapons are of the 10 damage variety. I went from Slime Coated Harpoon to Eyerazzia to Trak Tooth to Blood Point before getting my Ragebringer. I'd imagine a lot of rogues went through a similar progression as high level weapons with anything other than 10 damage are few and far between. A little variety in damage would be nice.

Another thing that bugs me is the fact that weapons like the Trak Tooth and Eyerazzia are primary only. I'm sure there are more examples, but these are the two that stuck in my mind. I can understand making a large weapon like the Crys Spear or the Wurmslayer primary only, but why would a light rapier and an even lighter dagger be primary only? There are lots of better, larger, heavier and faster weapons that can go in either slot. Making these weapons primary only severely shortens their usefulness, especially with Ragebringer in the picture.

Speaking of Ragebringer, it may be too late to get the effect redone, but I'd like to see some sort of noticable effect on it. Spewing fireballs and lightning bolts isn't exactly appropriate for a rogue weapon either, but I'd love something like the subtle but noticable dark blue glow of the cleric epic.


Armor- Mrylokar's boots are extremely rare on Brell. Maybe 3 or 4 on the server. I don't think there are any Mrylokar's Gaunts at all on the server.

SoV armor, for whatver reason, isn't tinted like it should be. Its been mentioned a lot, but rogues shouldn't be bright yellow. =)


Poison- Again, its been mentioned a lot before, but its too time consuming for it to be very useful. I only bother with it in PvP pretty much because the effects on mobs are negligable considering the time it takes to gather components.

They should be made stackable though, like the shaman potions. It can be argued that a rogue is more in need of stacking poisons than shamans needing stacking potions. Rogues have to carry a 6 slot container with them at all times to be able to make poisons. The container must be empty as well in order to have room make the poison. They also have to carry around 2 to 3 components per poison, 2 to 3 different types of vials, and 2 to 3 different types of suspensions (all depending on the level range of the poisons). This could very easily take up 3 or 4 of your 8 primay inventory slots if you plan on using more than one poison. Rogues must then stop what they are doing and, in a hostile environment, combine the components in hopes of making a poison, then wait another 7 seconds to apply it. It is a huge hassle. It would be so much easier to make a few 10 shot poisons while in town so that poisons take up maybe half a bag instead of half your inventory. =) Shamans generally don't make potions outside of town other than resist potions for raids. Shamans don't often use their own potions either, so they generally don't have to carry many around. Stacking just makes it easier to sell. Incidentally making poisons stack would also make them easier to sell, another good reason to do it. =)

Like I said, overall I'm very satisfied with my class. I just wanted to bring up a few minor things I'd like to see changed. =)

DEXTERITY AND/OR AGILITY-
When creating a rogue, the “classic” EQ rogue emphasized dexterity and agility. After two years time the “modern” EQ now focuses on strength. Why? Strength has a marked, sometimes biased, effect on maximum damage, attack rating, and backstab damage. The net result has been race-favoritism among rogues. In fact, many low-strength rogues re-rolled their characters in favor of strength rogues and the predominant advice given to those making a new rogue is to focus on strength. Well I’m a bit disgruntled about how powerful strength has become while the real rogue attributes, dexterity and agility, have been neglected. Strength should not be the end-all for rogues. Various aspects of dexterity’s and agility’s role in the daily rogue grind need to be re-analyzed. Among these is what is known as “processing”. Many tests have revealed a “very” small increase in the process-rate of a weapon at low (~100) versus high (~200) dexterity. Furthermore and as I understand it, the proc-rate of offhand weapons is independent of dexterity. It seems ludicrous that dexterity should have no effect upon proc-rate of offhand weapons and I can only hope that it is not true.
A qualm I have with agility concerns “evading”. I am not sure if there is anyway to test high versus low agility on this particular ability but it is logical that agility should have a effect on a rogue’s ability to evade. Along these lines (and I know this is a toe-stomper to some), large sized rogues should have lower chances of successfully evading compared to medium and small races. This would make being a small, nimble rogue a “worth-while alternative” to the large, clunky rogue races.
This last ‘peev’ is not truly a balance issue but more of a bug. When I’m in dwarf form there are inclines that I can not scale. An example of one is the undead temple in the desert of South Ro. In dwarf form I CAN NOT scale its walls to get to the top of the building. However, when I assume dark elf form I can walk right up this wall. This is not very realistic and needs to be addressed. What benefit should being a dark elf have over a dwarf when it comes to being nimble and fleet-footed. I chose a small class under the assumption that I might be more rogue-like than my larger class mates. The whole agility and dexterity attribute system seems to have been thrown out the window concerning rogue foot-work and body movement. Please address this and give those rogues who are small and who placed emphasis on dexterity and agility at the get-go the benefits of being so. I’m tired of the strength-is-king mentality among rogues.
So to summarize, please 1) reassess the process-rate of main hand weapons so that high dexterity rogues get a “significantly” greater chance at processing than low dexterity and, 2) make offhand weapon proc-rate dependent on dexterity (i.e., just like main hand), and 3) make evasion and other roguish skills of body manipulation agility- (and/or dexterity-) dependent abilities.

POISON-
Rogue poison has come a long way. I was a skilled poison maker prior to the poison system upgrade and am even more skilled now. I love using poisons. I have lots of options now. The only problem is that the options take up a lot of space, time to make, and in the process take up a lot of my sanity. I simply want to address poison by comparing it to alchemy.
Alchemy reagents can be purchased from single merchants on shaman-based cultures. Alchemists use a single bag to combine reagents to produce a potion encased in a vial (where the vial comes from is still subject to conjecture). Alchemists use another bag to combine their unstackable potions into one stacked five- or ten-charge potions. Alchemy costs a lot of money to learn; however, alchemists can sell their trade to other individuals to cover costs of learning alchemy. A caveat of the latter is that alchemists become useful in the trade-economy.
Now rogue poison. Toxicologists must travel to remote, one-of-a-kind merchants to purchase reagents and suspensions. Toxicologists must travel a multitude of zones to collect reagents that drop from various monsters, many of which are low level. The net result being the consumption of vast amounts of time JUST for collection (not for learning the actual skill have you). Toxicologists use a single bag to combine the reagents, a suspension, and oddly enough a vial (unlike the alchemist’s medicine bag, our mortar and pestle does not magically make a vial). Hence, toxicologists must either ally with a master potter or become master potters themselves. Again, the result is a massive consumption of time (I mean I got 140 unfired sealed poison vials that I NOW have to fire them all one at a time in a kiln... please this is overkill). Toxicologists produce an item that is unstackable. Toxicology is cheaper than alchemy but I feel the time sink does not offset this fact. It takes a lot of time to learn this skill, so much so that many rogues decide to learn poison in spurts of every ten levels or so (or worst not at all!) simply because traveling all over hell and high water does not fit in with an experience-driven schedule. Lastly, toxicologists produce an item that is only useable by rogues, many of whom I’ve already stated do not use poisons, with the end result being no interaction of toxicologists with the trade economy.
I compared these two skills in hopes it would reveal the inadequacies of poison. Now I am not really asking for poisons to have a market economy, I’m just asking for a leveling of the playing field here... the time sink for poison is just incredible. Resolving the time-sink is complex but the reward obtained for the time invested is not that great. So here I go... about to beat a dead horse. Rogue poisons MUST have multiple uses. Again and again Verant has insisted poisons can not be made to stack. Ok, I accept this. Lets forget stacking... lets forget charges... I propose a novel, yet simple (I hope its simple anyway) solution to getting multiple uses out of a poison vial... ‘apparent’ charges.
Make the finished poison react differently when it is applied to the weapon. Make the success rate of applying a poison to your blade dependant on the player’s apply skill (this is how it used to be prior to the poison-system patch a few months back). So a level 18 player just starting to apply poison would have, say, a ~50% chance of failing. Whereas, a level 60 rogue with apply skill maxed would have, say, a ~10% chance of failure. Ok easy enough, so here is the big change I propose... make it so when the rogue fails to apply the poison the poison vial is broken and disappears from inventory. However, IF the rogue successfully applies the poison the poison vial is not broken and it remains in the rogue’s inventory. Alright, this is going to be a long post, but please read it

Disciplines -

Level 53 - Counterattack. This one is alright, although phased out by Nimble a couple of levels later, because of Nimble's shorter refresh and longer duration, as Riposte holds little appeal for rogues due to our lack of high damage weapons. Nothing is really needed to improve this, except maybe some high-damage high-delay two-hand piercing weapons for use with it.

Level 54 - Deadeye. I personally never use it - the very tiny extra bit of damage that it does isn't worth giving up the safety cushion that a ready-to-use Counterattack or Nimble give you. Suggestion: Increase the duration to 3 minutes or so.

Level 55 - Nimble. Excellent discipline, the only one I have hotkeyed at all times.

Level 57 - Kinesthetics. Trash, junk, zevel, musor, etc. 18 seconds of guaranteed DA/DW at a level when you're pretty much guaranteed it by your levels of double attack and dual wield skills anyway. Absolutely not worth losing your ability to go into Nimble mode for half an hour. Needs to be dropped down to level 40 or so, when it would still have some use, and have it's duration increased to at least 90 seconds.

Level 58 - Blinding Speed. You'd think no discipline could be less useful than Kinesthetics in it's current form - and you'd be wrong. This is supposed to haste you - but it does not allow you to pass the class/level haste cap, and at level 58, whenever you're hunting, you're grouped - and when you're grouped, you're at haste cap anyway. As a result, you lose your ability to go Nimble for 30 minutes and gain absolutely nothing. Increase duration to 45-60 seconds and make it break haste cap. As an alternative, you can change it into an 18-sec burst of 200% running speed, an escape ability similar to Spirit of Cheetah.

Level 59 - Duelist. No complaints there.

Hide and Sneak -

Whenever we cross a zoneline or go linkdead, hide and sneak drops. This means that in order to enter a hostile zone (Sirens Grotto, Howling Stones, Dragon Necropolis, etc), we must beg a caster for invisibility. Worse yet, if we go linkdead while sneaking through a hostile zone, we are 100% guaranteed to come back dead. Please make it last through zoning/LDing, similar to what you said you were going to do to illusions (which suffer from exact same problem).

Ragebringer particle effect -

The green dot that's supposed to travel along the blade zips through so fast, it's impossible to see unless you look really really well. I suppose it's a low priority issue, but it shouldn't be all that hard to slow it down...

Assassin's Chainmail -

Ummm, ***? Sparkling gold gem-inlaid armor for assassins? I suppose it could make our victims die from uncontrollable spasms of laughter... anyway, it needs to get a dark gray/black pallette swap.

Range on Sense Traps -

EXTREMELY short - in some cases, it appears to be shorter than the trap's activation radius. I have the skill maxed (200), but when I attempted to drag a corpse in Dragon Necropolis from Dominator Yisaki's room to Zlandicar's chamber, a path that takes me through 2 spike traps, I set them off 3 times out of 4 - despite me knowing EXACTLY where they are, and moving a step at a time when I got to area, then hitting sense traps, waiting 10 seconds for refresh, taking another step, hitting sense traps again, repeating this for 5 minutes... and then out of the blue spike trap hitting me and THEN I sensed it. If a wanderer passed by at that time I would be dead in an instant. Sense traps needs a range increase to ~50 feet, and/or reuse time decrease to match that of sense heading.

Instill Doubt -

This skill runs through a bunch of completely ridiculous checks, and you have to succeed in each and every on of them, or you fail - resulting in completely ridiculously low success rate on anything, even the deepest green mobs. Successful instill doubt is an exception, rather than a rule. Here is an outline how it works:

Ensure backstab is refresh - if it is not refreshed, you are GUARANTEED a failure - thus you cannot use backstab and instill doubt at the same time, severely crippling your damage output.
Hit instill doubt - it appears to run a success/failure check on every figure of that little dance you make - /cheer, primary slash, offhand punch, pierce. If you pass them all (which is RARE, even when you're level 60 with 200 skill and using it on a level 10 bandit), proceed to next stage.
Kick is invoked - since we have no kick skill, it uses our offense rating. If you miss the kick, you have failed. It used to be that if you fought a mob that required magic to hit, you'd get the INVULNERABLE message even if you were wearing magic boots, and that would - guess what - cause your attempt to instill fear in the mob to fail.
Fear (the basic, level 4 necro version) is invoked, and checked against target's magic resistanse. If the target resists it, you fail - and resist rates on Fear line are pretty damn high.

As a result, the success rate on Instill Doubt on low blue con creatures hover somewhere around 0.5-1%... if not less. Thx useless skill ^_^

Disguises -

How about some questable masks that allow us to disguise as other races? Gnome, wood elf, dwarf, high elf, barbarian, etc. Maybe set some race restrictions on who can use them... ie, barbarians can't use gnome disguise, and halflings can't use ogre disguise, etc.

Plane of Growth -

Similar problem to bards and warriors - wood elf and half elf rogues can follow Tunare, yet, there are no quests for them in Growth, although there are quests for most other Tunare-following classes (clerics, druids, wizards, magicians, enchanters, rangers, paladins).

Poison -

Far too long of a subject, and I'm tired of writing. Suffice to say, you need to kill a few dozen mobs in order to inflict a small amount of damage on one - and even that is not guaranteed. No one I know uses it - and I know a lot of rogues. It simply isn't worth the time and money.

First off, let me say that, in my opinion, Verant has done a good job at fixing the Rogue class. At the beginning Rogues were very unbalanced and needed a lot of help to make them fun, just like a lot of the other classes. I know this thread is about class balancing and things like that so I will get on with it. This post may seem a bit wordy, but I want Verant to know how I arrive at my ideas and that there is a lot of thought put into them, not just a hasty 'wish list' slapped together.

I think that the Rogue class is almost perfectly balanced as far as the game engine will allow. I, and many other people I am sure, view the EQ Rogue class as an 'Assassin' class. I view Rogues as doing things more stealthy and shady as opposed to the damage dealing, poison using EW Rogue. But since the game cannot be redone from the ground up, I am sure that the Rogue will be staying almost as is until the final days of EQ.

I also believe that 'class balance' is not just about making a particular class more powerful or uber, but it is also about rounding out a class, making the class more believable and fun to play. Take the illusions that Enchanters get. Are some just fluff and don't do much? Yes, but do they make the class more fun to play. You better believe it. I hear Enchanters always wishing they had more illusions because they find them fun. I also wish that Bards had songs that flung people about like the gravity flux spell without hurting them. Fluff? Yes, but it would be a Hoot!! The first time I got a ride by a Tentacle Terror, I almost peed my pants I was laughing so hard (it was outdoors in Unrest, so I went flying hehe). Remember, being balanced does not just mean super-mega-uber-awesome power and skills


Now, onto my suggestions and ideas.


----------- Idea 1 -----------
------ Droppable Chests ------
------------------------------
I think that in many ways it could be easy to allow Rogues to do more Roguish type of things without unbalancing the game. One of these ways would be a locked chest that Rogues can unlock to see what is inside. Right now the only thing a Rogue uses his/her lock picks for is to open locked doors. Putting items in the game that also allow Rogues to use this ability would make it more fun for the Rogue class and make the time spent taking up that lock pick skill more rewarding. Here are the details of this idea.

1- the chests would NOT be static in rooms and such. They would be dropped by mobs. The graphics are there, and so are the sounds. Nothing new would need to be added as for graphics and sound. Not all mobs would be able to drop these chests, and not in all zones either. They could be dropped by named mobs or by normal standard mobs. They would also be random and not drop every time. It would be based on a percentage, much like some items are done now I believe.

2- they would also be NO DROP. This would ensure that no other class could gather up a whole bunch and then sell them off or give them to twinks and such. This would also ensure that they would not be farmed or used as trade bait. It would be a direct reward to a Rogue if he were in the party or nearby. No other class would benefit from this and this would ensure that no other class becomes unbalanced. This would also mean that low-level Rogues could not be given a chest to practice picklocks on. If they are worried that high level Rogues would do this as well (no risk and good reward) just make the chest able to be picked open once, after it has been picked open it would not need a lock pick to open it.

3- they would contain items only usable to Rogues, or a small amount of cash. After all, these would not be rewards for quests or for an extremely tough mob (however, they could be if Verant wished to do so. But ONLY if the item inside was only usable to a Rogue). The Rogue only item would be the only choice because it would be unfair to other classes to deny them items simply because they did not have a Rogue handy. The best item to put in them, I think, would be poison. I know that this seems it would lessen the desire for Rogues to make poison but not so. Why? Because Verant could make the poisons NO DROP and more importantly, they could control what poisons could be dropped. If they feel a particular poison would be too unbalancing, just leave it out. If they think more poison types should be in the game, just put them in there. Making them NO DROP would also ensure that the Rogue who looted the poison either used it or destroyed it. As for skills, the Rogue might not use the 'make poison' skill, but would certainly have to use the 'apply poison' skill. This would be another skill that would be used more. The cash reward (or gems, or food and water, or other misc items) would also be low key. Since the Rogue is the one opening the chest he could keep what was inside all for him if he wanted wanted to. Making the item(s) fairly mundane and coin amounts small would ensure that class balance again is not thrown out of whack.

This idea may seem like fluff and just a 'perk' to the Rogue class, but only if you look at it that way. Remember, Verant could put ANYTHING in these chests that they want. From small amounts of coin, to quest related items, to poison players cannot make, the ideas could be endless.

---------- Idea 2 ------------
----- Stuns from Behind ------
------------------------------
When I think of Rogues, I also think of how they interact with enemies and the like. Backstabbing is in there, as well as disarm, riposte, etc. But I also think that Rogues should be able to 'stun' and opponent from behind as well. After all, we are at a creature's backside 99% of the time anyway, so we should know how to do this. I know Rogues in EQ do not "mug" people, but I always think of how they take down opponents silently, and this is a very effective way. This could be implemented as a skill OR as a weapon. Both aspects are below as different sections, so please read all of it to make sure you see both options explained. Here are the details of this idea.

1- Make it a SKILL or DISCIPLINE. The skill could be introduced at any level, low or high. This skill would be like backstab, in that you MUST be behind the creature to try AND you need a certain type of weapon in your hand (either primary OR secondary could be chosen). The weapon could be a simple blackjack or a club. OR it could be a certain weapon type, ex, a 'heavy' blackjack or a 'bouncers' club. Making it primary could be a way of offsetting the skill, since a Rogue could not backstab while they have a non-piercing weapon in their primary hand. Now, I know that you are thinking "what is stopping a couple of Rogues stunning an opponent so much that nobody in the group takes damage, that would be absurd". I agree. Verant could make the skill similar to a discipline, or make it a new discipline altogether. It could only be used once every X number of minutes. This way the Rogue could help out the group, but not be able to make an opponent incapacitated for such a long length of time to reduce the risk of a fight.

2- Make it a WEAPON. The tougher version to control. Making it a weapon is certainly tougher of the two. Since it is an item that must be "acquired", not all Rogues would have a chance to get it automatically at a certain level by training it. The weapon could be quested for, purchased, or just found off of a mob (this could also be put into a chest from idea 1, see how good idea 1 is!!!...wink wink). Now, controlling how often a person can stun for with a weapon is harder to control as well. The easiest option is a weapon with a proc. I know some people have a problem with giving spells to tanks via weapons, so this would not sit well with them. Also, a Rogue could get lucky and stun with the proc a lot of times in a short period of time, but the opposite could happen as well. Just ask any Rogue who has a Bloodpoint and he/she will tell you that when you are desperately in need of that proc, it just never happens
