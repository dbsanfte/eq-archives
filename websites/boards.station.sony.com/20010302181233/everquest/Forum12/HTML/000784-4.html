Well, both Azmodeus and Dracones have touched on this issue but I don't think it has seen the attention that it deserves. What I believe is the primary problem with wizards and casters in general is that they do not get better as time goes on. I do not mean that they do not get better as they get to higher levels. What I mean is that with the additions of new expansions, new equipment, and new monsters in the game, casters have seen almost no increase in their power over the broad range of levels.

Let me begin with an example of how the power of tank types has increased. When tanks get new weapons, their damage over time in increased. This means that they can dish out more damage and over all, take less damage since dead monsters deal no damage. Now, when an expansion is added (Kunark or Velious) new and more powerful weapons are added. Some may be just as good as currently available weapons but are simply much easier to get at. Once these are in the game, trickle down theory takes over. Many people start getting the new item and the old ones are sold off. What happens to the ones that are sold off? Well they usually go to people who are lower level and can't get the old item themselves. Now that a new better weapon is around, the old one will go down in value so that the lower level tanks can afford to buy them. Now lower level fighter types have improved, as have higher level ones, so tank types across the board have now become stronger due to the implementation of a new weapon.

Casters do not benefit from this at all. The spells that casters get have more or less been fixed from day one (with the exception of 50+ spells that had to be added once those levels became available). Since spells are fixed by level, it is impossible for them to trickle down as equipment does for tank types. This means that if casters are ever balanced with tanks, they will eventually become unbalanced since tanks will eventually get new equipment and casters will be stuck with the same spells.

While casters have equipment that does trickle down the levels, it is largely unhelpful. Caster equipment does one of 2 things. It either gives the casters more manna or it has a right clickable ability. There is really no other benefit to be gained from caster equipment. Stats other than the one linked to your manna are basically worthless (with the possible exception of Charisma for Enchanters) as the benefit of these stats is negated by the fact that casters don't see hand to hand combat. Now, while more manna is nice it really doesn't do all that much. The difference between my level 40 wizard when she is equipped with all her equipment (reasonable equipment for her level) and when she is completely naked is about one to one and a half nukes. That isn't much. And if you consider that I get manna back just as fast regardless of how much you have, they extra manna in the pool is basically just for emergency situations when you have to do a little bit more with your spells. Down time afterwards is also increased due to the extra manna. Right click abilities are nice but do not make insane amounts of difference.

"But my Cleric / Druid / Shaman / Enchanter, seems to be quite powerful enough." The reason these classes are not screaming at being overshadowed is because their power is based on the power of the tanks they work with. Clerics heal tanks so that they can keep fighting. If you can keep a more powerful tank fighting, that healing has done a lot more than the same healing put on a less powerful tank. So in a sense, healing gets more powerful as the tank gets more powerful. Healing also has a built in efficiency increase. A tank that can do more damage will kill a monster faster than one who does less damage. This means that the monster will have less time to do damage to the tank. This means that the healing needed to keep the better equipped tank fighting is less than the poorly equipped tank against the same monster. Better armor for tanks only increases this effect by making tanks take less damage and therefor require less healing. Shamen and Enchanters derive a large part of their usefulness by buffing tanks. Haste style spells are particularly effective since they make a tank do even more damage faster. This scales with the new equipment. A tank with better equipment does more damage faster, which is then bumped to even faster when they get buffed. To illustrate this point further, how often do you see clerics use their nuke spells effectively? In almost every case I've seen, any cleric that uses their manna to nuke is yelled at since their healing is a much much better way of doing damage.

As the system stands, the power of casters (especially ones that don't enhance tanks such as wizards and magicians) is fixed by their level where as the power of tanks is dependent largely on equipment. There are no spells that casters can get that make other spells of the same level obsolete. Caster equipment as it is has no baring on how powerful their spells are. Caster stats have no baring on how powerful their spells are (although they do effect how many spells can cast if they have full manna). This means that there is no way for a caster of a set level to be come more powerful.

As things stand getting new equipment is one of the main reasons to continue playing everquest. It is the driving force behind the game. If no more new equipment becomes available a lot of drive to play the game will be lost. However if new equipment keeps getting put in, casters (especially wizards and magicians) will fall further and further behind in the scale of power. Spells will never do more damage or get more manna efficient since they will not be changed but tanks will continue to do more damage and take less damage as their armor and weapons get improved. This will eventually result in casters becoming worthless except as support for the tanks, (which is something that wizards don't do at all). I really don't want to see this eventuality come to pass.

There needs to be some way of scaling the power of casters to their stats or to their equipment or else they will simply fade into uselessness. I believe that this is a very real concern that needs to be looked into.
