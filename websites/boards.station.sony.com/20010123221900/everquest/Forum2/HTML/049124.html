Phelan42:

Only California is to blame for its current condition. The manner in which deregulation was done in California is the main problem. California is heavily reliant on extra-state generated power. This is because they have obstructed most of the new power generation plans (even alternative energy) for various reasons. California has a bad case of NIMBY (Not In My Back Yard!).

Given that, they created a deregulation system which forced the regional power companies into a locked fee system and a system where they had to buy power on the spot market to cover need. Its exploded in their faces. Even Federal Government has told California essentially "Hey, its your mess! Clean it up!".

A broad spectrum well designed deregulation system can work. Its worked elsewhere - but since it creates a supply and demand economic model to control prices, you've got to maintain supply to keep the prices down. California has grossly failed to maintain an adequate supply of power.

If you think this is bad, wait until there's a water crunch...
