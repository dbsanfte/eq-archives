Issues with the Warrior epic 2-hander:

The proc, Rage of Zek, does 2 things:

1) 175pt fire based dd
2) approx 12 AC debuff, lasting 3 minutes or so

The problems we're seeing are stacking difficulties in the following situations:

a) If two warriors with epics are fighting the same mob, and one warrior procs, the second warrior to proc will get a 'Your spell did not take hold message'. The first warrior will land successful procs on the mob, but the second will not be able to proc while the 'Zek' debuff is present.

b) The Shaman spell Cripple seems to block procs in the same way.

I'm sure the reason the procs don't stack is due to the AC debuff part of the proc. This is understandable, but it also procs the 175pt dd, which I don't think was intended. So in situation 'a', every resisted proc the second warrior has costs the group 175pts in potential damage. And in situation 'b', the single warrior will have all procs nullified.

I imagine that having partial stacking will be nearly impossible due to the coding, but I think most warriors with epics would agree that the 175pt dd is far more important than the 12 AC debuff. I'd really like to see the proc changed to address this. There's no need for warriors' epic DD's to be blocked due to a spell stacking issue.

I like how the proc is a unique spell, and simply removing the AC buff would remove that. How about replacing the AC debuff with something that buffs the warrior instead? Maybe adding 12 AC to the warrior, or a 200pt Rune, or a stackable haste. Anything would be better than current, as long as the proc has no stacking issues with any current buffs or debuffs.

I'm hearing that the 1HS weapons have a similar stacking issue, but I have not been able to test it as of yet. If this is the case, then the above arguments would cover the 1HS as well. Maybe a Haste proc on the 1HS would be a good solution, considering the need to speed those weapons up.

There are a lot of ways to fix this, but please, whatever is decided, make it stackable
