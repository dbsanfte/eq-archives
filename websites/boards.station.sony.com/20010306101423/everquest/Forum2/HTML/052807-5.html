Kaearia and Thott are right on target.

If you only make one positive change to the bard class-- and I think it's obvious that, in the 50+ game, bards need a substantial boost in power-- make it haste stacking.

Bards have significant problems at high levels. In traditional xp groups (tanks/crowd control), the *only* situations in which a bard is preferable to a chanter is where the bard's admittedly impressive (but still around 5th best on the depth chart) pulling abilities come into play or where the group is sufficiently healer-deficient as to have to depend on bard-healing.

(Bard-healing, by nature, cannot be used as spot heals, and therefore if you have a bad run of unlucky mob hits against a player in a primarily bard-healed group, someone's gonna die.)

Otherwise, chanters are flat-out better than bards.

EVEN if bard haste was as fast as chanter haste-- which it's not-- chanters would still be better than bards for trad xp groups. They mez better. Their mana regen is more dependable. Chanter slows are so much better than bard slows, it beggars description.

Are there good things about the bard? Yes. Bards are and remain the best possible puller for AoE groups. In fact, bards are and remain among the best finesse pullers around.

The downside is that finesse pulls are generally only important when your group is doing something that it really doesn't have the levels for, and otherwise you'd rather have a warrior doing faster brute-force pulling of 2-3 mobs at a time.

Where bards really fall down is in raid situations. I have exactly one song at level 55 that constitutes a substantial contribution to a battle against any epic encounter: Occlusion of Sound, because it stacks with everything I've seen and it makes it easier for the casters to do their thing. Either make all of bard hastes stack-- in which case bards will be the optimal 6th man in a group in a VERY limited number of possible groups-- or up the haste percentage on the Ervaj songs to the point where it makes sense to use them. As others have pointed out, this point is around 45%.
