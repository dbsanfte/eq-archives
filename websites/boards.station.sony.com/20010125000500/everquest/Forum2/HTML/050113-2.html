Most of these ideas aren't summons, but hey, how can you blame me

-----------------------------------

An item that is NORENT, LORE, weighs 10 pounds, and has one charge of Bind Affinity. However, just like melee binding works now, you would only be able to use this item in a place that a melee class could normally bind. The spell would be called Summon: Bind Stone, and would cost 300 mana to cast, with a casting time of say 6 seconds, and a recast time of something around 10 seconds.

Example:

Teramenor, a level 49 mage, casts Summon: Bind Stone, and produces a Stone of Binding. He gives it to Thargor the barbarian warrior. They are standing outside, in the Frontier Mountains. Thargor right-clicks the stone, and he is NOT BOUND because he is not in a city zone. (The charge is used up and the stone is now useless.) Teramenor curses Thargor for wasting his mana, and summons another stone. This time Thargor runs to the Firiona Vie docks, and right-clicks the stone. It binds him to the point he is standing on.

This helps reduce the "it's 3am and I can't get a bind" problem, as well as giving mages a new summon.

-------------------------------

(This isn't strictly speaking a "new summon" we'd get, but it would be a nice modification.)

Dimensional Pockets and Dimensional Holes (and in fact, all of our summoned weightless bags) should not be *deleted* when we log out. They should instead be converted to a heavy, NON-weightless, NODROP, non-NORENT bag of the same holding size. Let's say 1/2 pound of weight per slot in the bag, so a Dimensional Pocket (4 slots) would weigh 2 pounds (PLUS the weight of the contents of the bag!), and a Hole would weigh 3 pounds.

This solves the linkdeath problem; you don't lose the stuff in the bag, but now it's weighing you down.

The "unbalancing" downside to this is that you can now go to a newbie area and summon 50 bags, give them to all the newbies, and now the noobs basically have all gotten free containers to hold stuff with. Yeah, I know this isn't major, but it's still worth considering as a factor in changing dimensional bags this way.

-------------------------------

Some UI commands and key combinations that would help mages (and necros and enchanters and shamen and... ).

You know how %t inserts the name of the target? Something like %p (or any %-letter combo hasn't been used) that inserts the name of our pet. This way, I can make custom emotes that do things like:

/em tells %p to kill %t.

This way, if I have a bixie targeted, and my pet's name is Jobaner, people nearby see:

"Teramenor tells Jobaner to kill a bixie."

This is obviously in the "fun" category but it would certainly be nice.

-------------------------------

Something that would make our lives easier when enchanters are mezzing things would be a command like "/pet sleep", which would make our pet *ignore* being attacked, or when we get attacked. i.e. no matter what happened, the pet would not add anything to its own hatelist, nor would it attack or fight anything.

This obviously might make dealing with enchanter situations TOO easy, so it's understandable why you might not want to do it. On second thought, even I think this would make things too easy, but at least the idea's out there in case Verant thinks it's a good plan.

-------------------------------

PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE give us Voice Graft, BUT ONLY FOR OUR PETS!!! I would give anything to be able to have Jobaner speak! Imagine the humor! Such a voice graft doesn't even need to be able to interface with NPCs; anything Jobaner says can be ignored by the game.

-------------------------------

That's all for now.

Good summons are actually one of the hardest things to design for mages, IMO. It's tough to strike a balance between being good, but not overwhelming. I'm also not that keen on summoning things just to cast spells on them. (Storing the spell, as with waterstones, is fine. Just an awkward way to cast, as with See Invis on a staff, is fairly pointless.) That said:

Bags that survive random linkdeath. I've had these spurned even for containing summoned items, just because they were feared and hated so!

A low-level 1hb.

In fact, I'd revise the existing three summoned staves, which just aren't useful as is, even with the added spells, something along these lines:

Level 8: Staff of Tracing.
Currently 2hb, 7.5lb, 7/40, 2 charges of Flare. New Rod of Tracing: 1hb, 6/32 (one point better than a Cracked Staff off any newbie monster), 5 charges of Locate Corpse (so there's something worth "tracing" with it).

Level 16: Staff of Warding (2hb, 8/38, 5 charges of glimpse)
New: 2hb, 9/36 (like Runed Totem Staff, once reasonable loot at this level, now trash even newbies won't touch), proc Ward Undead.

Level 24: Staff of Runes (2hb, 9/36, 1 charge of Cancel Magic)
New: Rod of Runes, 1hb, 5/28 (same as most mid-level loot 1hb staves), give it about 4 charges of Cancel.

Level 34: Staff of Symbols (2hb, 10/34, 4 charges of See Invis)
New: Not too bad, as an awkward way to cast See Invis. At this level, you're not holding weapons anyway. Make it 6 charges, just so you can do let the whole party see your air pet before destroying the staff.

Level 44: Mod Rod (1hb, 8/26, 1 charge of modulation) Good for groups with a cleric, useless solo.

A level 8 1hs sword (consult a melee class for stats , average combat stats, but able to hit magic weapons. It'd be useful at that level (for non-twinks, anyway). Five levels later, everyone's got magic or combine anyway.

High-level 1hs and piercers that melee classes might actually like to hold.

Lower the level of the Kunark "fun" spells.
Gift of Xev (ration kit, or the "Happy Meal" as we used to call it) should be about level 29, not 51. (You can already summon the food and bags by 20.) Bristlebane's Bundle (beer and cake) about 34. Quiver of Marr, 39. Bandoleer of Luclin (3 stacks of knives), 39. Pouch of Quellious (shurikens, which might themselves be tweaked a bit), 49.

Lower the level of Ring of Flight (2 charges levitate, level 39). This is about 20-25 levels past the time all other casters get lev. Originally, as I understand it, it was supposed to be an always-on item, which might justify level 39. As it's just a roundabout way to cast lev, knock it back down to about level 24.

Make the Shard of the Core tradable. As a nodrop, it's fairly pointless. Tradable, it'd only be a mostly-pointless buff. Allow it to be used in range and ammo slots -- typically the only slots you have any chance of beating out the phat lewts people already have occupying the thing.

Remove the in-zone restriction on CotH. The melees will like it better if they don't have to run back to the dungeon. The in-group restriction keeps it from being a teleport.

--
New things:

I'd like to be able to summon all sorts of food, ideally by choice. A zillion spells would clutter up the book; maybe some sort of popup menu so I can summon meat pies or fish fillets or whatever?

The ATM Machine: summon from the bank. An item with a right-click effect that basically lets you access your bank, just as though you had clicked on a banker. Say level 39. Level 60, an item that when equipped lets you act like a banker, so all your group can bank. Now that, they'll love you for Throw in the "merchant stone" so they can sell loot before banking while in the dungeon, and mages become group gods

Spell storage crystal: hold it on the cursor, cast a spell, that spell gets sucked into the crystal and becomes a charge on it, to be cast later by the holder at will. (One charge only). Might need to be LORE for balance. Sort of like a general-purpose waterstone. Say level 39.

Summoned armor. Again, I don't really want one spell for each piece and type of armor cluttering up the spell book. Maybe we can reuse that popup window technology. More likely, just make it an AC buff castable on others. I'd suggest just making the Phantom Armor line not self only, and stackable with other buffs. Plus, stop calling it "phantom" armor -- because it's theoretically conjured, not an illusion. And just for the sake of coolness, combine it with a character graphic of the appropriate type of armor, so the wearer looks like they're in leather/chain/plate.

Money changing. Maybe a weightless money purse. Or, a spell that changes money. Summon Platinum produces 1 pp, requires 10 gp as a reagent. (Scale it by the money present!) About level 24, because you're not going to need it once you get the ATM Machine spell.

A summon-by-name spell, sort of a reverse teleport. Probably requires a confirmation dialog, if not /consent, to avoid annoying players. Bring anyone to your location from anywhere in the world. Upgrade to CotH, which doesn't leave us much of a level range.

A quick-cast uninterruptable travel/escape pet. It's simply not practical to have a pet up while you travel due to bad pathing. It's not practical to summon one every time you zone without carrying around three thousand malachite. (What other class has to have a reagent to zone?) Fix the decoy pet, and make it available at lower levels with about a 2 second cast time. It aggros on the pets onto itself, and lets you run away. Give it enough hitpoints for the mage to have a decent head start.

I'm also trying to avoid making up summons to try to solve mage problems in general, so I'll leave it at this, and go think about it some more.
