Let me preface this by saying my shaman is only 45 right now, so I only have experience during the time when our pets are useful. All my opinions about the L55 pet are based on what I've read in forums.

The main attributes of pets seem to be damage output and survivability.

- In terms of damage output, what reasonable to expect? When I was level 39 I used to solo drolvargs in FV a lot. My tactic was slow the drolvarg, have the pet attack, dot the drolvarg, move into melee. Both I and the pet had a haste buff, and the dog had taunt turned off. This allowed me to tank for the dog. In a normal fight, the dot would do a little more than 1/3 of the damage to the mob, I'd do a little less than 1/3, and the pet would do about 1/3. All this is from memory, I don't have exact logs to back it up. Based on experiences like this during there 30s and 40s, I think most shamans expect the pet to dish out melee damage at about the same rate they can when fight experience giving mobs. Is this the case right now? From what I've heard other people say, it doesn't sound like it.

- As far as survivability goes, shamans in there 30s and 40s quickly learn, the dog isn't a tank. If a mob is on it for more than a couple of rounds, it's going to die. I adjusted my tactics so the dog is never expected to tank. The first thing I do when summoning a dog is to turn taunt off. In any combat situation I make sure either myself, or a real tank is there to be on the receiving end of the mob melee. The problems comes in at high levels that pet level appears to be a variable in the mob aggro equation. The mobs prefer to pick off the weaker targets first. Since the pet HP increases don't keep up with the increases to mob melee, the pets die before mobs can even be taunted off of them. A mob that dies in seconds clearly isn't worth 850 mana.

My solution to this would be to have mobs use the level of there master rather than the level of the pet when determining whether or not to aggro on the pet. For example, a level 55 shaman summons a level 39 pet. He's in a group with the 55 warrior. The warrior and the pet start meleeing a mob. Where previously the mob might aggro on the pet because it's low blue to him, now it's less likely to because it considers the pet to be like a level 55 char (although it still attacks and defends like a 39 pet).

One additional good benefit of this is that it would make pets more usable in dungeons. Right now, the biggest problem for me is that pets tend to aggro things thru walls because they are such low level. There are definitely places where you can stand in dungeons and be perfectly safe. Just by summoning the low blue/green pet you can cause a lethal train. For example, my shaman 45 is grouped with a 45 monk working to kill Xalgoz in the church area of Kaesora. It's pretty tough to do as a duo at 45, but we appear to be able to clear enough spawns to so that we can kill Xalgoz when he spawns. We get all of the spawns broken that we need to, but I decide to summon a pet just to be sure that we can keep up with the spawn. The monk is pulling to the safe area behind the church. The ghosts run but the pathing is far enough away from the church that they can only aggro the roamers (which are all already dead). Xalgoz's room starts respawning and the first pull is a ghost. During the battle, Xalgoz and another skel pop. The ghost runs, my dog follows with the monk - wham - instant train. That's the last time I'll ever summon a pet at that camp.

(continued)

> Yes, it's a 55th level spell. But you also have to keep in mind all of the other things that a shaman can do. Pets are a secondary, perhaps tertiary, ability for your class. It's certainly not going to be as effective a spell as those in your primary area.

Beyond the point that our pet is far from being a secondary ability, at least as far as we are concerned, all the 'other things that a shaman can do' are indeed very present in our mind.

The four main areas that people always associated to shamans are as follow:

· Healing
· Buffs
· De-buffs
· Damage over time

Any group post-55 depending on a Shaman for healing is a group in a precarious position at best. Our top healing spell is the level 53 Superior Healing. Since it comes to us at 53, it gets only minor helps from our nearly max'd alteration skills and specialization. It has a fizzle rate over 25% and barely gets any Mana cost reduction at all. Add to this mix MOBs with thousands of HP hitting for multiple hundreds of damage per rounds and you can see that the shamans healing abilities are inadequate at best.

Buffs... let's face it, the only buffs we shamans have that add anything to another players abilities fall in 2 categories. Useful support buffs like SOW, Levitation, Enduring Breath, Invisibility and some vision spells. Those are hardly unique to the shaman class in anyway though. And buffs useful to tanks: the Talisman line (first acquired at 34 too, same as our pets... secondary ability?), the AC buffs, the haste line. That's about it except for some minor side-effects of the stats buffs. And the good ones have limitations all of their own, too. The Talisman line, unlike the Skin/Hero lines, overwrites the robe casters Shielding spells. The AC buffs are weak at best. And our haste line is a minor subset of the much more powerful haste spells that the enchanter gets. When it comes down to it, yes, a shaman can buff a tank but, in the kind of group make-up the level 55+ games start requiring, his contribution will be minor in effect, at best. Impressive as hell, with a 67 point STR buff, but still minor.

De-buff was were we shinned... let's face it, nothing is cooler than to see a player casts a spell and have the MOB start attacking in slow motion. Or suddenly STOP resisting the DD spells that a damage caster was throwing at it, being hit for full damage every time instead. Of course, those are hardly spells that are specific to the shamans anymore, are they? Neither, since the expansions, is it rare to encounter a MOB that is either utterly magic resistant or, with Velious, specifically SLOW-resistant. Then down the river goes all our de-buffing abilities, first diluted among the other classes, then just plain made irrelevant by design.

And that left DOTs. Shamans always had 3 lines of DOT spells: Poison, Disease... and pets. Yep, our pet is a DOT. Oh, it does have some side abilities that are useful, like a mildly effective taunt by example. But nothing really more significant than its ability to do damage to a MOB over time. Now, the Poison and Disease lines have been hit by all the DOT nerfs over the months, but they are still pretty effective. Nothing devastating or unique, though, especially with the top levels DOTs having been rendered practically inaccessible by the lack of drops. Or considering that using DOTs in a raid setting, with a couple dozen other players whapping on the target, quickly leads to a corpse *poofing* with its loot and a blacklisted caster. That left the pets... but again, not really, because our pet cannot do damage anymore to the MOBs that we encounter.

So, yes, we are acutely aware off all the things a shaman can do...as we get higher and higher in level, gathering more and more power, we can become inadequate at healing, minor at buffing, replaceable to irrelevant at de-buffing and useless at damaging over time. That's a rather paradoxical position for a class, don't you think?

That doesn't mean we don't like our class, though. And that's why we're getting slightly intense in trying to get things fixed about it. And since we would rather have this happen without taking away from other classes' abilities (because there is time when turnabout is NOT fair play), this is why we are pushing for you, Absor, to ADD to our pets, to ADD our class. This is not a balancing issue. This is not a class envy issue. This is just a long postponed, class FIXING issue, one of many for the shamans.
