Many players have commented that fast attacks appear to have a higher "taunt factor" than slow attacks, even when they deal less total damage. a) has a moderate damage/delay rating
b) maximizes the other "taunt factors"
c) can be wielded in either hand
d) is NOT LORE, so a player can wield 2
e) gives a moderate AC bonus, such that wielding 2 is equivalent to a decent shield
f) the weapon should only be usable by the class(es) deemed as the ultimate defensive tanks ( be careful with Paladins and Shadow Knights here)

The idea is to give Warriors the benefit of both high "taunt factor" and the AC of a shield, without altering the damage-dealing balance with other classes. I have seen some 2-handed weapons that give AC bonuses, but have yet to come across any 1-handed ones (please see my apology #3 above).

Lemme spend a moment to talk about taunt.

The hate list is, at least in part, one of the ways that the NPC AI determines what it's going to do. Setting it up so that a warrior, with the push of a button, can determine how the NPC is going to act in a fight would be silly. The more powerful the NPC, the less we want him to be controlled by the desires of the players. After all, the higher level the monster is the more intelligent (or experienced) it is supposed to be. What we really want is for the NPCs to act as much like PCs as they can. How many times to you, as a player character, turn away from the caster that you are destroying just because a warrior type NPC comes up behind you and calls you names (or stick sharp things into your behind, for that matter)? Unless he's going to kill you, you probably don't. Killing the caster is more important.

So giving complete control to the warrior to decide who the NPCs attack would be a bad thing for the game.

There is a delicate balance to maintain, though. And we are aware of it. But you also need to be aware that controlling the actions of the NPCs is always going to be a group activity. If your group works at it they can, for the most part, convince the NPC to attack the target you choose. It requires that, for example, your casters watch how much damage they do and when they do it. Just because they can chain cast direct damage spells does not mean that they should. The same applies to fighter types. It's up to the group to control aggro, not just the warrior.

Corrann wrote:

>"I think what Absor is afraid of the most is if we become too good no one will want to be paly's or SK's or rangers. Don't count on it. Give them better defense, fine. Just allow our AC to matter, and fix whatever is not working in regards to AGI. I don't care if I have only 200hp's more, but I better have 150 to 200 aC more at 60, and it better matter. that's the issue it sounds like."<

Exactly. And those things do matter. Warriors have more AC on their equipment, more hit points, more stamina (were a hybrid gets either Int or Wis). And in all cases a similarly leveled warrior will have better defensive skills. Just looking at hit points is very much underestimating the whole warrior package.

To put it plainly; no, we're not going to explain the AC mechanics in extreme detail. If you observe you should see that the warrior has the advantage in all cases. Don't focus on hit points, as they are only part of the equation.

>"Someone else made a point about my specialization idea. knocked it because of the issue of what about when I get a better weapon? ok, I'll answer that."<

I've been thinking about this whole specialization thing. At first I kinda skipped it because it would require some serious time and effort to make it work. It's certainly not something that would happen any time soon. And, well, think about it. Warriors that are 'specialized' in a weapon are going to be noticeably better using it. Right now, warriors do just about the right amount of damage (again, that's kind of the problem when you're the 'base' class for something). So we wouldn't want to increase the effectiveness of warrior damage. So we'd have to make it so that warriors with specialization do about the same damage that they do now, and we'd have to make it so that they would do less damage than they do now with their non-specialized weapons.

That was my first thought on it, so I skipped it.

But I'll add it to the list. It's still a good idea, in principle. It would allow for warriors to be a little more diverse. Though it would also favor the 'power gamer' that knows which weapons are the best to specialize in when they are given the choice. I would hate to think that some warrior that specialized in one-handed blunt would feel unhappy about his choice later on if he couldn't keep up with other warriors that were using whatever the best option is. That's not a major concern, but it's there.

So I'll add it, as a wish-list kind of item.

On Disarm… well, basically, I see no way to make this skill more viable without making it far too viable. I'll add this to the list, but I don't know that it can be made more useful. No, I will not advocate removing the skill. Remember, taking it out will not automatically entitle warriors to some other skill to replace it, that's not how this works.

I have an idea about critical hits and weapon specialization. I am thinking along the lines of AD&D which this game is based on. I know this game is not AD&D, but the basic concept of similarity between the two games is important.

Critical hits - You would never see a critical hit in AD&D do 12 dmg when your top amount of dmg is 120 dmg with a particular weapon. In my opinion, a critical hit shoud ALWAYS do at LEAST as much damage as the highest amount of damage that particular weapon can do. It is a criitcal hit - it should do damage accordingly. You don't put someone in the hospital and list them in critical condition when all they have done is stub their toe. A fair trade off to ensure the crit hit did at least the maximum dmg of a particular weapon is to reduce the frequency of them. Same for crippling blows...but make them at minimum twice the maximum dmg of the weapon and reduce their frequency as well. A crippling blow should mean just that - MASSIVE damage that cripples or severely damages the opponent.

Weapon Specialization - My idea for that, though it may sound too powerful, is to make the chosen weapon do at least half the dmg it does for a maximum hit. I think Verant is doing a good job by looking at the class issues. I am not trying to suggest that Warriors become all powerful gods with the ability to solo dragons. I am suggesting a more realistic approach to the melee system as far as damage and weapon usage is concerned. Thanks for listening. =)

Here's my thoughts

1) AC & HP's
Warriors should DEFINITELY have greater AC and MUCH greater HP's. I'm talking about perhaps 25% more hp's over hybrids and maybe removing the AC cap for warriors only.

2) Disarm
As we all know it's basically useless except in PVP. I really don't care if it's fixed or not as it neither adds nor detracts from gameplay.

3) Shield on back
Sure, this would be a nice thing. However, unless the AC cap is raise/removed it is useless at higher levels.

4) HP regeneration
I do like the idea of having a discipline or a meditation-like skill to increase HP regeneration.
Or, if HP's would not be raised perhaps warriors could be made to regenerate HP's faster than other classes.

5) Specialize weapons
A good idea, but you screw yourself with the slash only epic weapons if you choose any other skill.

6) Critical Hit damage
Yes, it's irrating to get small critical hits. Someone had posted an interesting idea of adding a stun to any crit/crip hits. That would make good sense as any "critical" hit would stun a creature.

7) Pierce cap
It is ridiculous to have pierce capped as it is. Rogues will out damage warriors with their backstab ability always and some weapons are warrior only piercers.

8) Stats discussion
People talk about uselessness of AGI. All I have to say is that if you want to tinker with how stats effect play at this point, you better be able to let characters reallocate their extra points. People allocate those points according to current coding. To change that coding would REALLY upset a lot of high level people.

9) Sweeping Blow
While this is nice in concept, there is one HUGE problem. No, it's not hitting mez'd MOB's. If you do that you're a fool for using the ability around them. The problem is on any PVP servers it will be an almost useless skill. It's bad enough we have to deal with AE stuns affecting party members. I know the majority of the players are on PVE severs, but please stop to think of the thousands playing on your PVP servers.
