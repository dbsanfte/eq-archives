Over the past year, in one fashion or the other, I have participated in these ranger threads. From the point that I was involving myself I saw many things said by various people that weren't necessarily supported by anything, including logic, yet they were taken as the word of Verant. They were taken even though these people weren't representatives of Verant, and in some cases I saw Verant pick up the ball and declare, that since we had ordained it then it would be so. One of these was the issue of whether the ranger was a tank or not.

I am a bit dismayed. Repeatedly throughout this set of Ranger Talks have I seen this same falsity propogated. I would like to say that I am speaking to not just Absor and Verant, but also to the various other players of rangers and otherwise. The ranger class is a tank. It has always been a tank. It was a tank from level 1 to 50 and it remains a tank. Every ranger, regardless of how they feel they are playing the ranger class is playing it like a tank. Some taunt always, some taunt never, and some taunt under specific circumstances. In any case, taunt isn't the deciding factor of whether a class is a tank.

Are there factors that can be examined to determine of a ranger is a tank? Sure there are. Are they viable factors that are accurate and allow us to draw a solid indisputable conclusion? Yes and no. Yes there are but there will always be someone that seeks to pull gnat **** out of pepper to declare that this or that factor indicates that the ranger class isn't a tank.

Rangers are primarily a chain wearing class. This means that they have a limit on the type of armor they can wear. What this does not mean is that the ranger class can't wear armor that is considered to be plate that provides stats and AC boosts similar to the plate wearing classes.

What the rangers gave up in order to double attack and dual wield was the ability to wear ALL kinds of plate. What I mean by ALL are the types of plate that is most commonly found in all levels of play, from level 1 to level 60. In turn, the ranger class received the ability to have near warrior offense with dual wield and double attack. For the ranger this is a significant trade-off. In pre-Kunark times there were only certain types of armor that a ranger could wear, simply because the armor was so severely limited in selection. There just were so few sets of armor on the rack, if you will, that anyone could wear. The ranger was limited in the armor they could wear out of this selection. Essentially they had their banded armor, their quest armor, their planes armor, and rubicite (while it dropped).

Throughout most of the pre-kunark days the hybrids and warriors had roughly the same HP. The ranger was the only one that showed a marked difference in HP. The ranger also showed a marked difference in AC. Their spells were quite weak and didn't make up the disparity.

Nonetheless the ranger community always acted as a tank. They pulled mobs, taunted off the other classes, including the warrior. Pre-Kunark I always taunted, regardless of whether we had a warrior in the group. Every ranger I knew did the same thing. There was never a time when we didn't consider ourselves to be a tank. When someone called for a tank, rangers were selected, not by their classes' inherent tanking abilities but on how the person played their class. Were they experienced? Were they mature? Did they have a bad reputation? But never, was a ranger excluded (if they were experienced, mature and had a good reputation) as a tank. They just weren't excluded. A ranger could use some of their skill and spells and stand up just like a warrior, SK, or Paladin could.

Now we move into the realm of post-kunark. From the release of the expansion most of the more mature and experienced rangers saw a problem. They would sit in battle and get the **** beat out of them. They would try to get out of the fight and the mobs would pursue. They would enter the battle later then the others and the mobs would pursue. They would cast their spells near the end of the battle and the mobs would pursue. The rangers were trying everything to be able to remain effective in battle. Some even suggested that the rangers give up their class defining skills to become more survivable.

Keep in mind that this all happened overnight. It wasn't something that was progressive and showed a maturing of a class into something else, something better. It was a dramatic change that caused signficiant failure of the ranger class to be effective and survivable in the post kunark world.

When this happened someone identified that the ranger class has problems, specifically with defense and with HP. It was also noted that warrior classes were receiving armor with spells on them that rivaled the spells of the ranger and most certainly the spells on the class armor they were to get.

Aradune came on and told the ranger community to have patients and see what level 60 brought them. One of the players from FoH (Elderan) decided he would use his other accounts and power-level his ranger to 60. He hit level 60 just before a solo necro hit level 60. During this time he played his game in a nomal fashion, participating in his guild events and collecting his Tolans armor and other gear. When he finally hit level 60 he came on and posted what his concerns were with the game only to be out-right attacked by Abashi.

The conclusions he drew then still hold today. The ranger class has problems all the way to level 60. One of the most notable things was that the ranger's AC and HP didn't go up as drammatically as the other hybrids did nor how the warrior classes did. It became apparent that the ranger was suffering and that the suffering was something that was dropped on them like a bomb in the night.

Many threads were started in support and debate of the conclusions that Elderan came up with. As other players moved up near level 60 they also concluded the same thing. Some minor changes were made but most of them were rather off-base and didn't bring the ranger class any closer to balance.

The most notable was that the ranger defense was suffering terribly. Comparative reviews were done for all classes of players (hybrids, warriors, monks, rogues, and even casters (as the ranger has some DD and other buff spells)). In these cases severe disparities were discovered and they were applicable only to the post-kunark expansions.

As time went by and the epic quests were implemented and the slaying of trak and other dragons became a regular things, it became notable that the rangers were excluded from them. They didn't have enough staying power and the amount of resources they were eating up wasn't equal to the amount of value they added to the groups, even the best rangers were treated in like fasion.

Somewhere in this series of debates someone (not a Verant representative), but some player, started saying that the ranger class wasn't a tank. Others debated whether the ranger had actually undergone a forced role change. Most of the rangers participating in these fought the notion that the ranger was no longer a tank and rejected the forced role change.

Alas, though, nothing can deter the ranger class from being the tank that it is. The ranger is a tank, has always been a tank, and always will be a tank. Period. It is only Verant's lack of focus and insight that has led to many of the changes that have fought to pull the ranger out of melee. This is a bad thing. Groups are looking for tanks and to limit the ranger's role as something other then a tank is what is hurting their desire in groups. If the ranger isn't a tank, they what are they? They don't do the damage you think. Just today I saw a snapshot on one of our guild's message boards showing one of our level 59 paladins scoring a crit of 185 against CT in Fear. It shows me and supports what I have said many times in the past, the other hybrids, with the right weapons can do some awesome damage. Rangers on the other hand do small amounts of damage fast, but totaling them up they don't justify the disparity of AC/HP to offensive capabilities. It shows also that the ranger class just isn't the damage dealing powerhouses that some have depicted the ranger as. They don't do the damage the rogue does, not even close. They don't do the damage the monks do, not even close, and both of those classes have signficantly higher ATK and AC then the ranger class has. In other words, if the ranger isn't a tank, then what are they.

Verant said that the ranger, if they were getting slaughtered, they should pull themselves out of battle and cast their spells. This was supposed to provide them with a viable alternative to melee damage (but the fact remains that the rangers gave up plate to get dual wield and double attack). The next thing Verant said was that rangers should pull themselves out of battle and shoot their bow (they postulated this even though the ranger's had already stated that Archery wasn't contributing in any noticeable way to the damage done to a mob during battle--and the fact that Archery had far too many other limitations and restrictions to make it worthwhile)--and Verant returned saying that they would implement double damage for rangers, but there would be serious restrictions/limitations on it. The ranger would have to be 51+, would have to not snare or root the mob, the mob couldn't be moving, and there were line of sight issues. The last thing they did was nerf the trueshot discipline thus all but eliminating the only nice thing about archery. The only thing I, as well as most other rangers could conclude, was that these attempts to extricate the ranger from arms length melee was only hurting their ability to produce damage and to bring value to the groups they were in. It wasn't providing the group with anything additional. In fact, these changes made the groups less effective overall.

In the case of the spell casting, it was uncovered that the ranger class, as well as the other hybrids, didn't have the mana to sustain any type of combat with their spells. At some point Verant decided that it was a double and even tripple penalty to limit the hybrid mana pool by causing them to have less mana per WIS/INT stat point. This penalty they removed. There still remains a penalty on spell casting, actually several. The first is that it costs the hybrid more mana per cast of a spell then the parent class costs for the same spell. The second is that the hybrids have no specalization for casting, and the third is that since the hybrids are all tanks they are fighting all the time and have little time to regen their mana back. This in the end signficantly reduces the effectiveness of pulling the ranger out of battle to cast spells. Almost completely negates it. As well, the fact that spells have high agro modifiers it causes the mobs to turn on them. Since lower HP is also an agro modifier the lack of HP and the spell casting gets the rangers into trouble more times then not.

Verant continued, and continues today in trying to get the ranger out of melee. This doesn't mean that this is a good decision on their part. It seems to have forced all rangers to change the way they played. For 5/6th of their lives they played a tank and to date, they want to remain a tank.

I want to remain a tank. Fix the defense issues of the ranger. There's no reason I have to be so significantly behind the other melee classes, including the rogue and the monk. There's no reason my ranger's defense skill, at level 60 should equal the defensive skill of a level 60 druid or shaman.

I want to also say that many of the changes to spells, many of which I like, except the low level ones, do nothing to balance the ranger class in any way.

My first char was a ranger and my last char will be a ranger. Fix the damn class Verant.
