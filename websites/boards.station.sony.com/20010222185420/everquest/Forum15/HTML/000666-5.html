On White Armor:

If I remember correctly, this item was brought up a long time ago, and while well liked, was not possible at the time. Due to the limitations of the EQ engine coupled with those of the version of DirectX we were using, the colour White was recognized by EQ as an absence of colour. However, they could not assign "no colour" to an item texture, so this idea had to be put away. If you carefully examine Gossamer robes, you will notice they aren't truely white, but a variation of it.

Now that the engine has been updated and we are using DX7, this idea may be feasible, and I for one would love to see it happen, maybe to Armor of Ro as was originally suggested, or to a new armor if this isn't feasible.

Just thought I'd throw that in for no reason, maybe I don't remember correctly and that wasn't why there's been no white armor.

ITEM: New Discipline Idea.

DESCRIPTION: There is an interesting idea floating around for a discipline that would help make the paladin more useful on raids. It would allow the paladin to target a PC and, for the duration of the discipline, take half of the target’s damage in his stead. So if the target is hit for 100 points, the paladin would take 50 and the target would take 50. The restriction on this would be that the paladin had to remain within melee range of the target and that it would not stop AoE spells or environmental damage. The idea is that the paladin is throwing himself in the way to protect the target.

---

I originally suggested this /defend <target> skill, although I got the glimmer of this idea from someone else. There was more to it than just taking 50% of the damage.

As listed above, its usefulness is limited. If the Paladin is protecting a caster (low AC, low HP), taking 50% of the damage will help - but not a lot. The paladin's AC and defensive skills need to be shared. For instance, if the Paladin has a AC 20 shield, the AC of the caster could go up be 20 and the Paladin down by 20. Also, the Paladin should be able to Parry and Riposte for the caster. (Of course, the Paladin can't block for the caster).

It doesn't make sense for 1 hit to hit two PC's, as the 50% dmg sharing implies.

But it would be cool to see.
