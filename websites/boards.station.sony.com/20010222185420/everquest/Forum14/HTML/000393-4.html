Speaking from a cleric's point of view, the only options I see being posted here to make druids more valuable in groups is to increase their healing abilities. Druids already have many advantages overall and have some excellent group skills as well. This does not mean that I do not think they should have some changes made to them that will make them more desireable in groups. Granted, I have not read through every single post so I may have missed a suggestion or two.

Druids and shamans both SHOULD have the current increases listed on the test server. SH at 51 is a good move, even to 49 would not be unbalancing. A spell similar to Remedy at 55 is ok and then a spell that heals around 900 or so for BOTH druids and shamans at 59 or 60 would work as well. That should be the cap on the heals for those two classes. Why? Because that leaves clerics with one heal spell that is better and that is Complete Healing. This is a situational spell at best and not used as much as many people seem to think it is. Most clerics use Remedy and Divine Light or Celestial Healing/Elixir in most group fights for the simple fact that 10 seconds cast time on CH means many dead group members if you are not careful. Clerics do one thing extremely well, and that is heal. If we are creative, we can do a few more things but for the vast majority it is truly med/heal/med/heal etc. Diluting that more could have an impact on clerics in higher level groups.

Please do not take all this as a rant. Just trying to explain a cleric's perspective on the healing issue. No one should be close to a cleric in terms of healing. At the same time, clerics should not be absolutely neccessary for a group to form in a normal circumstance. What I mean by this, is that in Karnors Castle, any group should be able to hunt with any reasonable combination and able to hunt well in most places of the dungeon like the hand room or warlord etc. A cleric should not be required to hunt in most areas of the zone.

To this end, I think that druids should be given a capability that makes them desired in groups AND reduces the need for healing at such levels that requires a cleric. Druids right now are considered the second best at just about every role but have no specific role to call their own. Why not give them a role that is unique and complimentary to other classes without giving away other classes' abilities to make them more desireable in groups? Why not give druids a spell at higher levels similar but complimentary to the shaman slow series of spells. Call it entangle like the old AD&D series of spells. I am not an expert on what exactly the shaman slow spell does so bear with me on this; and I am not familiar with all high level druid spells either but I don't think they have anything currently like what I am thinking of below.

Spell: Engtangling roots (or some other nature name). Roleplaying aspect is that the spell causes vines to grow from the ground tangling up a monsters arms and feet thereby reducing their ability to fight.

The actual effect of the spell would be that it lowers the target's overall ac by a percentage instead of an actual numeric value. This enhances the meleers'capabilities in having higher damage. Even more important overall though, would be the spell having some sort of effect that could help reduce the mob damage output. I think the shaman slow spells increase the delay between the actual mob attacks. For example, if a mob hits every 3 seconds and is slowed it now hits once every 6 seconds or so. Why not make the druid spell do something similar, but instead, it reduces the actual number of attacks in an individual round? If a mob qaud hits, then this could reduce it to 2 hits or in some circumstances to a single hit.
Make it stackable to the shaman slow spells but reduce the effectiveness of both if they are both on the same mob for balance purposes. Test the ratios to make them work but this would reduce the damage a monster does in a round of combat where it should be sufficient for a druid or shaman to act as a healer WITHOUT making them into clerics at the higher levels. It adds a role for them in a group that is unique and compliments other classes' roles. And if I am describing the shaman slow spells when I refer to reducing the number of actual hits per round, then make the druid spell increase the time between attacks. Make the higher level spell reduce attacks by 50% or 75% if it isn't too overpowering. That would definitely make a druid desireable in a lot of groups and it wouldn't really effect their ability to solo since kiting really does not involve getting hit. Make the mana cost somewhat high to offset the advanatgaes of damage. 350 or 400 (similar to complete heal cost).

Another issue I did not see really addressed here, was the high end raids and the role, or lack thereof, druids (or shamans) play on them. Currently, one shaman is usually all that comes on a raid since only one can actually cast slow on a mob. Others are mainly backup healers. Druids, well they seem to be there for ports out at the end of the raid more than anything else or healing casters. And I am not trying to flame either class, just trying to address the issue of how they are seen for higher raids. They SHOULD have a role and an important one like other classes do. Have a "raid" version of the slow spells for both shamans and druids. Make them stackable. The more druids and shamans, the more cumalitive effect of the slow spells. Cap it at 40% or 30% or whatever. Say each druid that casts the raid version slows the mob 5%. Same for shaman. The more of each class you have, the better chance of slowing the boss mob even more. And on the high end Velious mobs, that would definitely have an important effect and help out all raid members. I don't think raids should boil down to 1 or 2 enchanters, 15 warriors, 6 rogues, and 5 clerics.
Heck, add a curse series for necormancers that might reduce the effectiveness of how well a mob hits. Maybe make it miss 20% of the time. That adds something to them for grouping but doesn' take away from other classes.

These are just my two cents worth. Verant asked a while back for ways to make other solo oriented classes more group friendly without stepping too much into the realms of other classes. The argument that druids and shamans need even better heals to keep groups alive because there are few clerics is not really addressing the problem. Why not give you the role and tools to be creative and reduce the damage dealt to within the current capabilities you have to be able to heal the group. It won't be as efficient healing as a cleric but it also shoudln't take all your mana to do it. Just an idea for balance that will add a new dimension to druids and make them more group friendly without making them mini-clerics.
Also, give druids and shamans a group spell buff that cast the equivalent of the resist line of spells (adds 40 resist) but that covers cold, fire, poision, and disease all in one shot? There are better group spells for a single resist like GRM and the druid circle of winter spells, but this would be a general purpose elemental buff. Just an idea for balance.
