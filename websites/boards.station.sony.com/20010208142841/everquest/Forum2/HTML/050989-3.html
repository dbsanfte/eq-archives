Most people are addressing the high level issues. Being a Level 15 Wizard, I'd like to address some issues for us low level people, which can make Wizards extremely discouraging in the beginning. Root, Nuke, Nuke, Nuke, Nuke. Then you get XP and you sit and med for up to a few minutes solid... then go take one more creature.... That's assuming you didn't die because root was resisted or failed early, which happens way too often, resulting in a lot of lost XP. Possible solutions (and I know there may be a lot of issues here I'm unfamiliar with making these impracticle) would be lowering the mana requirements of the level 1-8-ish nuking spells, upping the mana regen rate for low levels a little, or making root a little more effective at these levels.

I feel it should be possible, and not so extremely difficult as it is now, to solo a Wizard at these levels. Not to mention the fact that since we spend so much time medding, at these levels people tend not to like us in groups, because we slow them down (as compared to having another class along with them).

Seeing as quite a few classes have been recieving attention on these boards recently, pertaining to some of their long-standing gripes about their class, I thought I would offer an opinion about my time as a wizard. This is my first time posting to this board. I refuse to flame, yell, and scream, and prefer to discuss problems civilly. So please don't reply to this message if your only contribution is "D00D U R SUX".
I've played since beta 4. I was a wizard in beta, and I've been a wizard ever since the game went retail. Having said that, I'm only a 55 wiz, as I'm not the powergaming type and I like to try other classes/races as well. Regardless, I know the class as well as almost anyone who plays a wizard, -- I speak from experience.

First, we are a pretty decent class. I'm actually quite satisfied. We can solo well at the higher levels, do a pretty impressive amount of damage through most of our career, and the travel abilities are very nice. I don't want to say we "suck" by any means. There have been *very* little changes to our class post-beta, and we're still pretty effective a year+ later. I think that's a sign that we're pretty balanced.

That having been said. ("Uh, oh" Absor says to himself.) Because our class hasn't been touched much since post-beta, there are some pretty glaring issues and rather embarassing problems with our class that have been around since the game first went live.

1. Al'Kabor spells. Al'Kabor, the (supposedly) most powerful wizard in existence, who has an entire line of spells named after him... what an absolute embarassment. I know of NO wizard who can keep a straight face while actually discussing these spells. I even see them being auctioned in Freeport by other classes as "(Whatever) of Al'Crapor," the pet name most of wizzies have for the spell line. The mana to damage ratio on these spells is, for lack of a better word, a complete joke. Let's compare:

Level 51 - Pillar of Frost
750damage or so to 4 targets, 325 mana, 10ft radius of effect

Level 51 - Something of Al'Kabor
720 damage or so to 5 targets, 650 mana, 35ft radius of effect

Twice the mana for the same damage (with an extra target), and a slightly increased radius of effect is NOT worth it under any circumstances whatsoever. Period. For over a year now I've searched for a way to use these spells effectively without success. At lower levels, the discrepancy in damage/mana compared to spells of the same level is even *more* exaggerated - to the point it's laughable. I've been in hunting groups in Seb, etc, which simply *destroy* the Ak'Kabor spells looted without even *asking* if anyone wants them, because they're notoriously useless.

2. Enstill. We're given this spell at level 20, and then given the news that our level 4 root is now no longer going to hold critters as it has before. (At level 20, the effectiveness of root is *severely* reduced. It's sudden, not gradual. It's definitely a "message" from the gods to anyone trying to use it, that it's time to do something different.) This is apparently to encourage us to use the new Root-based spell.

However, Enstill is almost as much of a joke as the Al'Kabor line. At level 20, against a level 20 mob, it is easily resisted 19/20 times. When it *does* stick, if a fly so much as breathes the wrong way on the mob, it breaks. If a spell cannot take effect 90% of the time (and I know the propensity on this board to exaggerate percentages and stats - this is not an exaggeration), and then is completely ineffective the other 10%, it's useless to us. It simply isn't worth the research time invested.

3. Gates. Most are great. One is embarassing and two are deathtraps. When we are given the Skyfire and Cazic gates, gating into either location is instant suicide for a wiz of that level. The Cazic gate aggros immediately (this was supposedly fixed to not happen any longer, but it still does), killing any young wiz who appears before they can react. The Skyfire evac is similar. The Tishan's and Markar's Relocation line (the Kunark evacs) are an evac line - they cost the same mana as an evac, have the same effect as an evac (chance to leave someone behind), and the same casting time. WHY is one of them designed to require vast amounts of preparation, buffing, and strategy before it's safe to zone in there? The evac point for Tishan's is *literally* in the *middle* of one of the most heavily-NPC-trafficed, high-level areas of Skyfire, right by the Veeshan's Peak orb. There's no reason to "evacuate" a group to their deaths.

In the entire zone of West Karanas, one of the biggest, emptiest zones in the game, why oh why is our wizard platform as far east, towards the NK spire, as it can be? Why have that platform at all?
