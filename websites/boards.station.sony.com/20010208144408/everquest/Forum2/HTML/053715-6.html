I am a part time EQ player and have only reached lv20 so far and find the reading in what is in store for me at the upper levels is interesting. The overrideing theme seems to be that the role of the Wizard is undefined since the definition that Verant has given the Wizard clearly does not fit anymore. Several people have given some keen insights to fundamental problems and some innovative solutions. Kudos to them. To the yelling, screaming, and criticizm of authors (rather than the class) even if it is polite, shame on you. I would just like to add my opinions and comments to this discussion.

Epic Quest - I have no fundamental problem with a quest being X amount harder than another quest. But is the risk and time worth the reward? (I can't answer that since I don't know, but a phenomenal risk implies a phenomenal reward.)

Mana Efficiency - this would encompass casting speed, mana cost, resists, fizzles, aggro rate, effectiveness, etc ... Wizards should be the best 'pure' caster in the game. Our friend Al'Kabor, should represent the pinnacle of casters (his spell line not included). If there are two spells that have similar effects (such as damage) the Wizard should do it better than another class and Significantly so. Various aspects of this have been beated to death about this ... and I agree with most of what I have read.

Groupability - Again, beaten to death, but what gives us that Wizardly Aura?

A few other thoughs and ideas:

Tools - As the Class voted most likely to 'search for knowledge', how come we mem 8 spells just like other casters and have roughly the same quantity (albiet, different) of spells as other casters? A wizard should have a spell for every occasion. Should they all be useful in a battle? Heck no! How about a pyrotechnics spell for weddings and parties? Gnomes can make fireworks, so it would be a simple add on. Higher level fireworks spells could be different shapes or motion. A larger range of tools would allows us to customize for zone, the MOB we are hunting, or the composition of the group we are in.
A larger selection of memorized spells would allow us to use the mana we have more prudently by having the ideal spells ready to go. Perhaps a spell slot becomes a double slot every 6 levels or so. That would max out at lv 48 and would let us look forward to some of the intermediate levels between new spells. We could also potentailly get more confused about what spell we put where and cast the wrong one. Perfectly in line with the nature of a Wizard.

Upgrade/New spell lines - In the same vein as Tools. While easy enough to say, they need to be creative so as to not duplicate other classes, which in turn could increase our group ability.
The image of a Wizard in literature is one where the heros/villans are held back from the Wizard by some unseen force. Except for Root, I really don't get that feel and even Root doesn't really capture it. Now most people are opposed to pets and, in the sense of how other casters get them, I am too. But how about a line of shield spells that are centered on the caster and extend outward some distance. This could be considered a non-attacking (okay, maybe just aggro) pet with so many hp before it dies and the MOB is free to attack the caster. The distance it extends out should be smaller than the PB AoE spells so it could allow the caster to use these when he aggos multiple MOBs. Now the Shieldskin line of spells I don't use since a hit still intterupts my spells, so it does not matter that I have extra hit points. You could revamp this line of spells so it extends out for the Wiz and could also be used to protect any group members who are standing within the shield perimeter. Drawbacks to this type of spell could be that the Wizard also moves slower when the shield is up.
A 'search' pet - Again, invisible so no new graphics. Does no damage, dies in a flash, but you could tell it to "/Pet find Kizdean Gix" and it would wander around until it found Kizdean Gix and then tell you his Loc and die, or "/Pet pull A ORC" then it would wander around until it found A ORC, tell you "A ORC incoming master", then aggro it like Eye of Zomm does, die, and the A ORC would come chasing after you. It would give the Wizard the option of being the puller for a group, although the downside of the pet wandering aimlessly around a zone or pulling at a bad time are obvious. You could call it 'Zomm's Seeking Eye' so the pet casters don't get jelous.

Skills - The ability to have a double effect. Single casting time, twice the effect, 3/4 the mana, three times the aggro. Again, would increase our rate and efficience to do damage but also put us at the top of the aggro list.
Also Dual cast, which could be used if you incrase the number of spell slots as suggested above. This could be that the adjacent spell does not have a 'down time' when the first is cast. Then afterward, the usually 'down time' after a spell is cast would be longer. This would allow for combos for exceedingly fast damage ability (Lightning Bolt/Flame Shock), defensive retreat (Root/Fade or Fade/Gate), or combo (Fingers of Fire/Shadow Step).

Anyways, I'm just trying to not only comment and make class suggestions, but to try to help bring an identity to the Wizard class

I don't think Verant doesn't care, else i wouldn't have wasted my time with this list that i Feel ALL casters should be given, not just wizards.
--------------------------
Class Balance Issue: Wizard Disciplines:

Why: Before Kunark, the classes were (supposedly) balanced. We wizards did 1/6-1/3 of the damage of a typical mob pulled for xp, and there were no disciplines. Post kunark, wizards spells did the same amount of damage (look at lvl 60 sunstrike, only a 500 point upgrade to ice comet, which does 1120 at lvl 50 already!… though the mana cost/damage is surprisingly better, thank you for that Verant.)

We wizards are being outdamaged by the second, in long and short term battles. Another wizard posted that the avg rogue in his guild does 80/sec while he was pulling 25/ but ONLY if 2 and only 2 of his lures were resisted, any more, and his dmg/sec took a greater beating.

What we need: Disciplines to make us more capable casters, along the lines of the ones given to warriors and the other melee classes. Personally, I believe that ALL caster classes need these disciplines, and will try to make them as non-class specific as possible.

Lvl 51- Discipline of Resistance: +3 to all saves, just like warrior discipline (reuse time the same as well). Since we casters have to be in melee range to cast on mobs with aoe damage spells, no reason why we should be the least resistant ones to such spells. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 52- Discipline of Void: While under the effect of this discipline, no spells will fizzle, and no spells can be interrupted, even if walking or running. Duration: 3 mins, reuse 15 mins. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels. Our spells will still cause agro, so chain-casting will still be unwanted, it just makes more efficient use of our mana, which all wizards need.

Lvl 53- Discipline of Channeling: When using this discipline the wizard will automatically land all casts against his opponent. In other words he will not miss. This discipline lasts for 14 seconds and has a base reuse time of 30 minutes. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 54- Discipline of Mana: When using this discipline, the wizard will automatically receive the specialization mana-cost for each of his spells. Discipline lasts 2 minutes, reuse: 30 minutes. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 55- Discipline of Redirection: When using this discipline, the wizard will not increase agro when casting. It will be just like if the wizard used concussion 3 times after casting his spell, particularly useful if spells are fully resisted but the full damage is placed in the monster’s agro table and it comes for the wizard anyway. Duration: 3 minutes, reuse 30. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 56- Discipline of Evocation: When using this discipline damage output by the wizard is increased by a certain percentage. Say 10 or 20%. Duration: 30 seconds, Reuse 30 minutes. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 57- Discipline of Duration: When using this discipline, spells cast by the wizard will have an increase in their duration, say 50% that is applied immediately to the spells duration. So if invisibility were cast, and it has a maximum duration of say 10 minutes, it will have a minimum of 5 minutes, and a maximum of 15 minutes when using this discipline. Duration: 3 minutes, recast 30 minutes.

Lvl 58- Discipline of Fell Cast: When using this discipline, a wizard’s damage will be multiplied by 4, but the mana cost will also be multiplied by 4, and specialization will not count when subtracting mana from the pool available. Duration 9 seconds, reuse 15 minutes. Additional note: if the spell hits for 2 dmg, then the spell will hit for 8 dmg with the discipline, but still cost the 4x regular cost. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 59- Discipline of Absorption: When using this discipline, a wizard will be immune to any magic cast upon them, including dots and fears, but of course can be hit by melee. Duration 8 seconds, reuse time 60 minutes The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the wizard gains additional levels.

Lvl 60- Discipline of Haste: When using this discipline, a wizard’s spells will be cast at some fraction of their normal casting speed. Say 60%. I would much rather have caster haste items in the game, but I will settle for getting this discipline at lvl 60. This would be the thing that all casters aspire to, just like pre-kunark everyone aspired to getting Ice Comet. Duration: 3 minutes, reuse delay 30 minutes.

I feel that these disciplines address many of the posted concerns that I have read, including damage output and even my own personal wish: caster haste. I was unable to find the thread at graffe.com that had the disciplines, so I made these ones up (save the one about uninterruptible casting, I remember reading that one over at graffe.com—though I named it myself) based wholly on the warrior disciplines at the same lvls.

The expansions to EQ came with a massive load of new items for all classes. On a closer look on what those items actually do, nukers clearly got the short end.
Melees:
- higher AC
- improved str/dex/agi/sta
- better resist gear
- effect items
- haste items
- new weapons


Nukers:
- better int items
- better resist gear
- new spells

While melees try to max str/dex/agi/sta, the nuker only cares about int for mana. Int is soft-capped, making it useless to go beyond 200. So what good is yet another ring with +5 int instead of +4? The int cap is reached very easily, so the new int items are practicaly useless.

Resist gear is the same for nukers and melees.

With the new effect items, melees can regenerate, buff, snare, stun, nuke, go inivs, gate.. All mana free.
Besides the Staff of Temperate Flux and JBoots I yet have to find an effect item for my wizard. While my spells are more reliable since they don't need to proc, they are all I have and they consume my mana.
No usefull effect items for my wizard from the expansions so far.

Haste. As I understand it, a 20% haste item increases a melee's overall damage output by 20%.
There is nothing even remotely comparable out there for nukers. Spell casting time is unchangeable.

New weapons are used for their better damage ratio compared to the old ones. The nuker's spells didn't bring any increase in the mana ratio, they are rather tools like IVU to make getting around easier. (Melee's can achieve this through effect items.)

Summary
While new items for melees result not only in better defense but as well in higher damage output, the nukers still stand where they were pre-SoV (and for lvl 1-50 pre-Kunark even).
Mobs have become increasingly difficult in the new worlds to keep up with the new gear of the melees.
So either the melee/nuker classes were unbalanced before the expansions or they are unbalanced now.

Fixes
Nukers need an increase in damage output.
Improve their spells. Many good ideas on this board.
Create items for nukers that match the usefulnes of a Iksar BP or a FBSS. Items that decrease casting time, that increase mana regeneration or increase damage of nukes. Clickable items for mana free spells: Pet buffs for mages; Teleports, snare/root for wizards..
