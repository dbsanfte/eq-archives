First, in response to the Raenya flames.

If you want to suggest things like mana regen, more animal forms, or superior harmony or anything else(and thank you to the people who finally proposed ways that harmony could work in dungeons) be my guest. All that I ask is that you *support* your requests. Explain what problem it would address. Explain why it would not make the druid class overpowered. Explain how it could be implemented.

Support your oppinions. Instead of flaming me saying "Who are you to tell us how to post? Who died and made you god?" prove me wrong. Support your suggestions with valid arguments. Use examples and hypothetical situations. If you can't come up with a way to implement something without it being overpowered, or can't see how it would allieviate problems plagueing the class why post it? Wishlists are nice, there are things we'd all love to see that will probably never happen (like more animal forms) but until something drastic changes (like a change to minimum systen requirments) we're not going to see more animal forms.

On the other hand the flames did get people talking, that is a good thing.

These are the things I don't think are going to happen, and why.

1> Superior Harmony, this spell trivializes dungeons. The nature of terrain in indoor dungeons are different than outdoor dungeons. Little chance indoors of something agroing you before you even see it. You seldom get agro'd through walls in indoor dungeons. The spell is beneficial. I don't mean it does something "good" to the target, I mean that it doesn't check for magic resistance and isn't counted as an offensive (mobs do not agro from it). Even if the target is immune to this type of spell the target doesn't agro. Though it doesn't stop agro completly, if you avoid walking *right next* to the mob it will ignore you.

2> Animal forms. New animal forms aren't happening due to global space constraints. There is a finite ammount of space in the global, that space is almost used up. Any new models given would probably go to the enchanters, who are the masters of illusion. It doesn't matter how good an idea for a new animal form is, it's not likely going to happen because of limited global space.

3> Manaregen. The soloability of druids has never been in question. They are a strong soloing class throughout much of their career. The druid problem is late game viability. (The whole thing got started with the "better heals for druids/shamen" threads months ago). Druids already recieve mana regen at high levels. If you are in favor of mana regen at lower levels explain how it would help the deficiencies of the class without increasing power in already strong areas.

"I think this would be cool" posts don't add to the thread. They are for the wishlist forum.

Ok. Example. Bad post.

"I don't think druid charm should agro the mob when it breaks, after all we're masters of nature right? Animals are supposed to love us. I don't see why they agro us. Make all the druid charm spells not agro."

This theoretical post doesn't answer a few questions.
1> What changes if any will be made to charm to compensate for this?
2> If no detremental changes are made, will the spell be overpowered.
3> What would stop the druid from continually recharming and keeping a permanent pet.
4> What do you do about the level 55 spell tunare's request that is supposed to be a non agro charm? It is now twice as useless. What changes do you make to it?
5> If druid charm is nerfed in someway to compensate what happens to the people who already get some benefit from the spell line? For example, if the spell line is made to not agro, but only work on non kos mobs, what about the people who use charm as crowd control in dungeons? What about those people if charm is made to not agro but will only work on greens. Ask anyone who has used the level 55 charm exactly how useful it is.

Maybe a change that is needed is obvious to you. Maybe it isn't obvious to someone else. What if a change would be good for the class, and for the game but doesn't get implemented because Absor didn't see it the same way you did, but you didn't explain your position.

4) I became really excited reading a post about adding a druid skill in the charm area. The duration of the charm animal spells are rediculously short. 6) I am an avid fan of trade skills. I spend a lot of time trime to forage the items I (and others) need for them. It would also have the side benefit of releaving crowdining if everyone had an easier way of reaching other parts of Kunark.

I think that's all for now.

I have a druid on Luclin who just hit 32nd level. Making a suit of crafted armor for my 23rd level warrior.

I'm not big enough to go on Plane or dragon raids.
I've spent the last month teleporting guildmates around getting pieces for Crafted, Ro, and Etched Ivy. I'm still wearing the set of small leather boots I bought at 7th level with money earned from selling the patchwork I got turning in Crushbone belts.

I've never actually been to Surefall Glade. I solo'd twelve griffons (all but one con'd red) and three hill giants from 30th to 31st level. I haven't been asked to join a group in the last week - and I've played at least 3 hours a day over that time.

I played Asheron's Call for a year and left because it was essentially a single-player game you played next to 1500 other people. I don't want to abandon my first character because it's a single-player class.

First, general suggestions (ie, things that I'd like to see, that wouldn't necessarily improve class balance re:grouping):

1.) Non-aggro Charm? Maybe this would be too hard to code, but how 'bout it doesn't aggro on you if it's at full health when charm breaks? Add in some flavor text ("a_brown_bear_14 shakes off the charm and turns on you in rage at its rough treatment") and it *gasp* might actually evoke a sense of wonder ... if that's not outside the scope of the Vision.

2.) Quests? Right now, mid-level druids have a series of quests at the Unkempt Preserver Ring in Rathe Mountains, where we turn in usually one or two useful-to-other-classes items to receive one not-really-useful-and-only-usable-by-us item. Why can't druids have quests that are actually worth the time it takes to do?

3.) Class Armor. As in, we don't have any. This is a common theme, neh? We get the restrictions of being a melee-caster like Shaman (who get decent attack ratings) and Clerics (who get decent armor ratings) but we get leather, the Scimitar, and a swift kick in the pants - the swift kick being that we look like lowbies all the way up to 46th level, and usually even a little past then. At least give us a decent set of mid-level quest armor that's colored ... it can be green or brown leather, or even a colored mask on the "naked" skin. Something, anything, as long as it's not hot pink or lime.

4.) Charm. Dammit, if we're restricted to animals (and if spiders et al aren't animals) and I think outdoors as well, then the spell should not be worse than enchanter spells of the same level. If a 29th level Bard can charm - and KEEP charmed - a Hill Giant, give me one good reason my 32nd level druid with 97 charisma can't touch a griffon. Even if you don't work in no aggro, at least make it not suck.

Now, group-related stuff:

1.) Resistance-buffs. Especially Fire and Cold, probably Magic as well. Poison and Disease can stay more in the realm of the cleric, fine, but ... we get these spells like one or two or more spell tiers after shamans. Why? Shouldn't druids be immune to nature's worst effects? Or how about a single spell to boost both? Or group spells if we're going to get them five levels later than shaman.

2.) This is perhaps unbalancing ... but how about a Rune-type effect? You know, Skin Like <whatever> absorbing like either the first <however much> amount of damage, or some small amount per each hit? Negating 3 points of damage per attack when the mob is hitting for 60 isn't all that overpowering, and would help compensate for our overall crappy healing ability.

3.) How about a low-casting-time, no-aggro spells that are self-never and/or have a self-destructive component? A heal that dumps our health into a groupmate. The key here is low-speed; we'd be able to get the spell off fast, at the cost of personal damage (with the low-aggro to compensate for the fact that we're now nearly dead). Or just something-for-something spells. A debuff that shreds our own stats as it rips up an enemy or a buff that takes from caster and dumps it onto groupmates. Make the effect greater than spells of equivalent level, both to give an incentive to cast and to prevent the druid from casting Sacrifice Warmth to buff cold on groupmates and having the shaman cast Resist Cold on the druid to counter the cold resist loss (tying in with the resistance-buffs above).
