Bard defense already equals paladin defense. It was raised back when SoT was fixed.

Parry/riposte don't help as much as you'd think. They certainly don't result in any significant change in class balance based on the level they're aquired. Asking for them is likely to simply get the level aquired changed and a pat on the head, "see we did something for you, you should be happy now," and nothing of substance would change.

There's no reason to get them earlier. What problem does getting them earlier solve? Will getting them earlier really solve the problem? Requesting the skill earlier is the standard "I want more and I want it now!" feeling that's based on childish emotions and needs. There isn't any real class balance reason to change the required levels.

Now if it could be shown that bard tanking is a significant factor in bard balance, and that that tanking needs to be better in order for the class to be balanced, then changing the skills would be one way to do it. I just don't see any evidence that points in this direction.

Lets not ask for things just for the sake of asking, hoping we will get them. Instead lets ask for the things we actually need to play the class the way it was intended.

A couple of points. One thing which has allways been brought up in bard balance sessions, is that a group get's a large increase in damage from the bard haste songs. Indeed, this was one of the primary reasons that I made a bard. However, in practice, in over 150 days played I've spent about %90 of that time with an enchanter around, for various reasons. Especially on a raid, it is unreasonable to assume that there will be no enchanters present, and with c2, necro pumping etc.. it's extremly easy for them to buff everyone with haste buffs superior to bard buffs. That is where a great deal of us are coming from. On paper, it looks great that a bard has haste songs which improve the damage output of the group. However, since these haste components do not stack with enchanter haste components, and are limited by the haste cap, a bard's contribution to a group is limited to non-haste songs. I would really like to get through to Verant on this issue. I have never really gotten a straight answer to this since I mentioned it in Phase 3 of the beta to Aradune.
Currently, through parsing, a bard does about 1/4 to about 1/3 the damage of a warrior, against a mob, via melee damage. The chant songs rarely land against uber mobs. Most groups, to be properly balanced, only have, at most 4 melee. 5 melee leaves room for just 1 cleric to keep them alive, especially if they will be hasted and drawing aggro. 4 melee is the best situation a bard would get. Replacing 4 melee with a 3 melee and a bard. Your damage output drops from 4 to 3.3. Probably less, since a riposte or two will shred the bard, and force him to withdraw till he gets healed, which other classes can stay in. Still, haste at %30, stacking with everything would them raise the damage output of the group to 4.2. This would be a total increase in group damage output of %5. If the bard doesn't die. This %30 song does not exist at the present time. What happens now is that everyone in the group already has max haste from manaless shissar. Bard haste effects don't stack. Everyone also has maxxed stats from shaman. So bard stat improves make no difference. Bard debuffs don't land on most raid mobs. So, in the end, the bard group is left with 3.3. damage. A loss. Manasong is the only thing a bard has which is not duplicated, overwritten, unnecessary, or not resisted.
My suggestion, if you wanted to make bard haste a factor, would be to come up with a line of bard haste songs, which stack with all enchanter haste, ignore the haste cap.. but do not stack with other bard haste songs. This would allow bards to play a useful role in raids, by assisting their party. Keep in mind though.. that %30 haste results in only a %5 increase in group damage output. Anything less, and adding a bard to a group would still be a loss. I would argue for more.

I can't think of a bard who would object to getting 2h weapon skills and da, over 1h weapon skills and dw. DW basically makes a class limited to 1h weapons = to a class which just has 2h weapon skills. The effect, if all skill levels were equal, would be 2h weapon skills = 1h weapon skills + DW. DA, is the melee skill which makes a difference. As is, a shaman/cleric/druid with comparable offence/2hb skill to a bard would do the same amount of damage.

To the suggestion that our damage songs be upgraded as an improvement. Well, again, that would be only useful in some encounters. And I am not even going to begin to explain my frustration with melee skills. Like many bards, I have devoted a good ammount of my equipment to buffing my cha. Now, it has worked well for me, but not nearly so well as buffing str works for other hybrids, or buffing int, or a mix of the 2.
Why then, would I buff cha? Because it is bard like. Bards make their livings (speaking from an rp perspective) from being able to ammuse and entertain. It could also be argued that charisma is more important to a musician than his playing skill (dex).
What I suggest is this, give bards a GOOD reason to buff cha, which by the way, is a hard stat to get up there.
1. Change our instrament skills to Cha based skill gain.

2. Base our mana off of cha.

3. Make cha have a greater effect on our mind affecting songs.

4. and finally make Cha have a higher influence on our fizzle rates.

This would at least give us more incentive to develope a stat that, as it stands, is really only good for mez and charm. While this may be good for enchanters who get a ton of these types of spells, these only represent a small portion of the bard arsenal.
