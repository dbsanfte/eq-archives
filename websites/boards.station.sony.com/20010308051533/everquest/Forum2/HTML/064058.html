Please, read this post in its entirety before responding.

I am still on vacation, as i am in Oregon with the In-laws untill Mondayish.

I wanted to share my thoughts on what i have learned from not being able to play Everquest for a little over 2 weeks.

1.) Everquest sounds like a fun game, in theory.

2.) I am a little sad about all the time i spent playing Everquest and not spending time with my family, as I have found that spending time with my family has been exceedingly more interesting and "wierd".

3.) Justification of my time spent playing, is difficult, as i have spent a long time playing Everquest just to get nowhere in game.


As i sit here contemplating my return to "real life" as it were. I face the inevitable reality that again, i will be working the late shift, and have little time to spend other than online, and that i will probably end up playing Everquest to ease my late-night boredom again on my return.

However, I am not really sure i will. As i come back to the message boards, i find myself faced with the cold hard reality, knowing that Aradune wants to take his game in a direction completely away from what my initial expectations and hopes were in a three-dimensional orientated fantasy role playing game.

Im sure, from my reading of other posts on this board over the past few weeks, that i am not alone in my dissapointment.

What is odd, is that i cant really finger out any particular reason for the sour-taste i experiance in my mouth when i even think about spending hours a night again on Everquest.

If im forced to think about it though (and im forcing myself to do so) i would have to say that it seems to me, at least that Aradune is trying his absolute hardest to ensure that is takes us longer and longer, and longer to "enjoy" this game.

In fact, it is now taking so long, that I am now associating EQ with being nothing more than a Graphical chatroom than anything else.

As i remember, i spent a lot of time talking to others in /ooc /shout or /gu than anything else in the game. I remember very brief bouts of "fighting" the enemies, and very long bouts of waiting around for my mana to fill, or my health bar to fill.

It is kinda sad that i remember playing the game about a tenth of the time i spent doing other things while playing the game.

If i wasnt playing i was: reading a book, watching MTv Discovery or SciFi, getting a drink, taking a pee, getting something to munch on (usually leftovers), ordering pizza, calling Cox to yell at them out some more for thier crappy service, playing with the cat, tormenting the cat, throwing the cat at the wall for sinking its hyperdermic-back legs into me, or sleeping lightly so i could wake up every now and then to check my life/health bars.

Maybe now that i have a Playstation2 (heh best thing to come out of going to a small bodunk town for vacation), i can play some Armored Core2, DoA2, or maybe some FFIX... Hey, i can even watch a few of the movies i have while playing Everquest...

Now that i think about it, Everquest is the only game that instead of using the CPU to multitask, it uses the USER to multitask during its operation.

Oh well, im done.
