Absor,

While I respect your global desire to improve the product and trust that, in your heart of hearts, that is where you wanted to go... this change was dramatic in effect and modifies a fundamental element of the game. I hope you will agree that it is rather late in the process to make sweeping changes to the game... and this is a sweeping change.

You have stated clearly that you felt the hate from healing was "broken" before. Well, I'm intrigued by this implication. If this is true, then most healers should not have experienced agro-ing from healing... yet I can state unequivocably that EVERY healer I know has experienced agro from healing, of varying degrees. They way it was working before, hate list management was a skill all clerics learned and, by their later levels, had largely mastered. This meant that it was less of an issue for their mental game since it became almost second nature. To replace it, mana and timing management became the order of the day. Now, the job of a cleric has become dramatically more complex by virtue of having to not only relearn the hate management, but to apply this knowledge constantly as it is a much more difficult juggling act.

Perhaps you don't actually perceive the magnitude of this change, but for comparison it is roughly equivalent to, in a game like Quake, suddenly tripling all movement speed of all players (let's ignore the frame rate limitation for sake of this analogy). The result, of course, would be to greatly widen the gap between the average player and the expert player. A very few could handle the extreme speed and would become gods... while the great bulk of the rest of us would find the game much harder because of the increased speed. The hate-list change is similar... now you've made it so the best clerics will be able to overcome the added obstacles, and the rest will be forced to deal with perhaps too many things to juggle. The result will be some clerics leaving, and some players in general leaving... while a select few are excited by the new challenge.

I put it to you that, regardless of the legitimacy of the "fix" on the grounds of repairing a broken mechanic, it does not add to the fun of the game for many players. For that reason alone, it should be removed. You have, in this case, made the game more fun for a handful, and a great deal less fun for many.

I'm glad you're reading the posts... now, I can only hope you actually listen and think about them.

Ok...edited to correct the small error he pointed out..
(excerpted from an earlier post in this thread)

1) Encounters involving one or two mobs with players that are below the 50th level cap.
The new aggro rules work fine here. This is undoubtably the situation that was being considered when the new rules were designed and 'tested' and they make sense here. Aggro is higher, but manageable amd the encounters are not super deadly.
Solution: None needed, although fixes applied to the following solutions will affect this situation as well.

2) Encounters involving many mobs with players that are below the 50th level cap. Note: there are many areas in the world where this situation is unavoidable. Single and double pulling is NOT possible in all areas.

The new aggro rules DO NOT work under these conditions. The problem is that a 1000 point heal generates MANY TIMES more aggro than does a 1000 point nuke or 1000 pts of damage. Scenario: Party beats on mob 1, while mobs 2-5 are mezzed, rooted or allowed to beat on tanks. After a while, someone needs a heal (could be the tank, could be the chanter,doesnt really matter). Cleric heals injured person. Mob 1 has cleric elevated to a higher aggro, but this is offset by previous damage. Mob1 _might_ aggro on cleric, but can be taunted/ damaged off. Mobs2-5 ALSO aggro on cleric, but have no offsetting hate list and thus cleric shoots to the top. If they are free to attack, they do so and the party has NO WAY to get them all off. And, even if they do (re-mez, for example)...As they beat on Mob2, a new heal is necessary. But not mobs3-5 have twice the heal aggro they did earlier...Etc. By the time you get to that last mob, it has the cleric on a hate list equivelent to ALL THE DAMAGE THAT THE PREVIOUS MOBS DID (because all that damage had to be healed at some point). This hate list cannot be offset by simple damage or taunting, and can only be offset by mem blur or Atone (and may require MANY atones, as the mobs dont always cooperate in their positioning). Thus, this situation CAN be managed with great difficulty...and if the cleric has to cast several atones, he isnt healing or medding and may not be able to get off the next heal when it is needed.
Essential problem: HEALS ADD TO THE HATE LISTS OF ALL MOBS HAVING RECIPIENT ON THEIR HATE LIST, BUT DAMAGE AND TAUNTS ONLY APPLIES TO THE ONE BEING HIT.
Proposed Solution: Heal adds to aggro, but at a 1:3 ratio. Under this rule, although each heal will add less aggro, as the cleric casts several heals, his aggro on the unengaged mobs will still be going up, resulting in a more dangerous situation for him (which was your goal, no?) while not inflating his aggro as currently occurs.


Note: Changing the post to refer to PLAYERS below 50 from referring to MOBS below 50 did not IN ANY WAY change the significance of the problem.
