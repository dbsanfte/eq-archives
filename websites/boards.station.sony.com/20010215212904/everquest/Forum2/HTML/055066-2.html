Thanks for the excellent post Grimstaff. I hope it gets noticed.

I made a suggestion earlier that I didn't see anyone comment on, so I'll shamelessly mention it again. What about being able to cast through something like the Eye of Zomm? I think it would be nice if it was a mirror image kind of spell, in that it looked like the caster, but it could look just like the eye and be just as effective.

Basically, this would allow the wizard to go places that he or she would not ordinarily go -- such as into melee combat range. It would also give the wizard the effect of a few more hit points since the monster would aggro on the duplicate (and kill it) before moving on to the wizard. It wouldn't be like a pet since the wizard would be sitting in an out of the way spot (vulnerable to attack) and wouldn't act independently.

Since the wizard is sitting down, why not let him or her meditate at the same time? This would give wiards a benefit over other casters in that they can be casting and meditating simultaneously, but since it takes 12 or more minutes to med at higher levels, it wouldn't really be that dramatic. (In other words, if you can dump all your mana in 30 seconds, you're really only gaining 30 seconds of med time in that 12 minutes.)

I think this would address a couple of wizard concerns: increase mana regen (although not alot) and therefore decrease down time, and it would be group friendly (in a group of casters you can keep watch while the others med). I admit it wouldn't make wizards the most powerful caster hands down, but these advantages combined with some small improvements in DD might be enough to more clearly push wizards to the front when it comes to DD. Add in a few other group friendly abilities and I think we'd be moving alot closer to "balance".

I suppose this would help wizard soloing ability slightly since the wiz would be able to stand more punishment, but I don't think it would be that unbalancing. In groups however, the wizard could do a bit more offensive casting and have a chance to survive aggro as the tanks try to taunt the monster off the duplicate.

Besides, this just seems very "wizardly" to me. :P Take it for what it's worth.

Oh, one other thing I didn't clearly mention. There is a pretty serious downside to using this spell. The wizard is left sitting and vulnerable to attack. This means that you won't use the spell for safe and secure kiting since if you get too far away, you can be killed before you could get back.

As I see it, one of two things could happen if the wizard is attacked.

1) The spell breaks, resulting in the disappearance of the duplicate. If you were fighting some other mobs, they are now running in your in your direction as fast as possible *and* you have to deal with whatever attacked you.

2) The spell does not break, but you are taking "real" damage. You must decide to either cancel the spell yourself (resulting in situation 1) or try to finish off whatever you are doing and run back before you are killed.

Come to think of it, this is very much like Astral projection.
