I think you need to distinguish false information from intentionally misleading information. When a company has intellectual property to protect, they often will create a smokescreen around that information. Some of the information will be intentionally misleading mainly to misdirect attention.

Any information relevant to new spells that you find in a game file on your box is covered by the license agreement and you must have Sony's permission before revealing it. In game spells that are presented as part of the game environment at fan sites get treated differently because they are part lore and part hint. The game file itself is neither--it is protected, licensed property.

When you or anyone else chooses to reveal protected, licensed information, you run two real risks:

1. You could be held liable for the publication of damaging information.
2. The misleading information can be used to prove your source.

Traditionally telephone white pages contained intentionally misleading 'seed' listings. When a competitor copied that information without proving it, then the telephone company would use the seed listings to prove the source of the competitors listings. The infringing company would then have to explain how they could have copied the 'seed' listings without infringing on the original company's intellectual property.

Up until a case just a year or so ago, the originating telephone company's ownership of the telephone listings was protected using this approach. That case made public phone numbers part of the public domain and made them non-protectable. Rest assured that Sony/Verant won't suffer the same fate anytime soon based on fantastic elements such as spell names contained in their speff.dat file.

In conclusion, sometimes companies use intentionally misleading information in order to create a smokescreen around their intellectual property. Publishing their data and specifically the misleading information provides a fingerprint that can be used to trace the information to a source and prove infringement of national and international intellectual property laws.

This file is only for properly informing the client software with what to do (such as display what anims, graphics, spell book info, etc) with further / modifying information from the server.
