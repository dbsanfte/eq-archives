Many of you are completely missing a fundamental point here. A player's contribution to a group cannot be reduced to a simple comparison of defensive and offensive ability. Hybrids, by their very definition, have a diverse range of other abilities.

Paladin:
1. Root parking. Pull 2 mobs? Don't have an enchanter with you? Paladin will root park one of them. For those that can't grasp the importance of this, I'll explain: When 2 mobs are pulled, but the paladin root parks one of them, the damage taken by the group IS CUT IN HALF. That alone is a pretty darn useful to a group.

You must realize how powerful it is to have a major heal THAT HAS A ZERO CASTING TIME. Because of this, the paladin is the single best class in the game for saving a party member who is a few seconds away from death. Countless whole-party wipeouts have been avoided by LoH.

3. Superior heal. Paladin heals between fights help save clerics mana. Superior heal is good enough to be used during battle even.

4. Buffs. Contrary to what many on these boards say, not every group post 50 has a cleric (and after the upcoming changes, clerics will be even less necessary than they are now). When no cleric is present, paladin buffs are very useful.

5. 90% exp res. One of the most group-friendly spells in the entire game. Some warriors would give up all of their disciplines for this spell alone. I've seen people pay clerics 1000 pp for a 90% exp res. That pretty much proves that cleric resses are not always easy to obtain. You can't possibly tell me that this spell isn't useful.

6. Taunting. Paladins can taunt far better than warriors can, because of their spells. All the defensive ability in the world does no good at all if the warrior can't get the mob to hit him and the paladin can. 'Nuff said.

Shadowknights.
1. Lifetaps. The hp advantage of warriors can be easily made up for in most situations by SK lifetaps.

2. Harm Touch. This ability alone is better than
any of the warrior's offensive disciplines because it does its damage INSTANTLY.

3. DOTs. The usefulness of these depends on the situation, and how long the mobs are surviving. But you can't tell me that an 1152 damage darkness/dot isn't useful.

4. Pets. Yes, I know SK pets suck and will cause trouble in some cases. But under the right circumstances, a SK pet DOES increase the SK damage output.

5. Darkness line. Warriors have no way to stop runners. Shadowknights do. Runners can cause serious problems in most dungeons.

6. Feign Death pulling. Many areas are impossible to do without a FD puller to break the spawns. This plus high defensive ability makes SK *MUCH* better pullers than warriors.

7. Taunting. As with paladins, SK can taunt *MUCH* better than warriors. If the 3 heavy tank classes get equal defensive abilities, why would anyone want a warrior as a tank, when they are the worst taunters of the 3?

The warrior disciplines are not all they are cracked up to be either. Some of them are totally useless, one or two are useful only in rare situations, and most of them just do the same one or two things, just in different ways.
Our only really good one was defensive, and that one is getting nerfed, possibly to uselessness (remember it doesn't matter how well the warrior tanks if he can't get a mob to hit him).

Currently, warriors are doing ok because they can out-tank hybrids. But after these upcoming changes, this advantage may disappear altogether. And that would leave warriors seriously underpowered compared to paladins and shadowknights.
