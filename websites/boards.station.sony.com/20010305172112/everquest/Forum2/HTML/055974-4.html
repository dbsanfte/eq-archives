There are a couple problems I've noticed with the cleric class.


1) MoK fizzle rates are pretty nasty. I have my skills maxed but this thing fizzles like crazy. No other spell I have even close to the same fizzle rates, even on higher level spells using the same type of skill. Resists are also pretty high even on much lower level mobs. Since the MoK nerf this has been happening and due to the fizzles and resists is now found on page 50 of many clerics spellbooks.


2) Aegolism- The idea of the spell is great. Everyone loves a symbol/ac/hb combo with a huge duration. It saves buff slots for all the stat warriors out there. The problems with this spell are really too great to make this spell usefull though. First off the spell is very easy to dispell. Also the spell is a single target spell and requires a great deal of mana and 10pp in reagents per cast. Realistically the cleric has two options.
First Option- Cast heroic bond, naltrons mark, and then whatever ac buff the cleric chooses (I usually use bulwark). For a group of 6 all recieving these buffs requires 3050 mana and 10pp in reagents. Duration is about an hour and a half.
Second Option- Cast Aegolism on each person in the group. For a group of 6 this is talking 4500 mana and around 60pp in reagents. Duration is 2 and a half hours.
These two options cannot be combined or interchanged without in effect losing effectiveness of the spells. Heroic bond and naltrons mark are not usefull when some in the group have aegolism.

From a mana and hp gain over time perspective-

The net gain of using a lvl 60 spell over much lower level spells is marginal if any gain at all. Look at the similarities of the mana cost per benefit and divide that by the duration of the spell. Hardly any difference at all.

From a cost effective perspective-

Aegolism requires @ 60pp in reagents. The other option requires @ 25pp in the same amount of time. Not much more needs to be said.

From a practical perspective-

Aegolism is incredibly easy to dispell. I remember right when I scribed the spell that I immediately casted it on myself. I couldn't hardly wait. About 1 minute after casting the spell on myself the mob my group was fighting turned and casted a spell. The message I saw was you feel annulled ... your aegolism fades. It's kinda funny in a way, I swear when I pick the group buff method the group every once in awhile gets debuffed by a mob we are fighting. With aegolism I swear the mobs know it's up, and they just zap 1200hp and a ton of ac immediately. With the group buff method I usually just lose one buff on dispells.

In summary, Aegolism has no advantage over group buffing. There is marginal mana gained but at a heavy reagent cost and is really impractical. I would really hope that a lvl 60 spell would be an upgrade and not a downgrade to the spells a cleric learned and scribed even 8 levels earlier.

There are alot of room for improvement on this spell. Possibly make it a very mana expensive group spell and maybe reducing the duration and reagent cost. Another possibility would be to make the spell a group ac buff, in line with naltrons mark and heroic bond. I personally would prefer the group ac buff, because that would deal with the dispell issue.


3) Possibly having some form of curse spell. Even though the majority of curse type spells are in the necromancer realm, I would think clerics would have some form of curse also. These could be religion/diety specific spells that would lower ac, hit points, or when the npc flees. In roleplay the mob would just seem to be having a bad day. He would be taking more hits than usual (AC), possibly just not feeling well (lower max hp), or just not feeling very motivated (flees at higher health). I would enjoy these types of spells, even if they only slightly effected the npc.

Overall I would say that clerics are a balanced class. Our defensive skills (defensive cap/ dodge/ ect) are where they should be. We are a plate class so yes we enjoy the advantage of a nice crisp ac. This is what keeps us from taking an arrow in the back or one of those dirty a$$ling backstabs, not our combat skills.

36-48 we are fairly balanced. our abilities start to shine, we can finally heal better than 2 of the other priestly classes, we still have a very narrow focus of healing and res, as our nukes, even vs undead are less effecient though being the most effecient DD we have.

49+ we are still balanced. The game at this level is very VERY unbalanced, esp Velious. The resist rates of the mobs makes it such that the only effective method of killing mobs is Melee. Every caster class has problems with fizzle rates, and with spells being resisted. Velious is a hyperexample of this imbalance. In an effort to give the 50+ crowd a challange by beefing up resistance and HP of the mobs, all classes save Warriors and Clerics are largely ineffectual, to the point of being neutered.
