An amended repost of a suggestion I had for an event that would involve a lot more people than the tournaments:

An example of a GM event many can participate in: Many prisoners of varying races have escaped from somewhere like Highhold. They have scattered all over Norrath, but there is a bounty out for them.
Add several NPCs to the heart of several dungeons and cities(Upper Guk, the attic of some buildings in Qeynos or Freeport, deep in Najena, etc.)

Each NPC drops something to prove that they were caught.

Now add a twist: The NPCs are have the typical aggro of their race. If someone indifferent or better to them hails them, they reveal part of a conspiracy that their captors were trying to suppress. When they have given their information, they hand a written description of it to the 'hailer' and despawn (no doing both quests per NPC )

Undoubtedly some information would be lost due to bloodthirsty players, so have another event a little later that gives the community another shot at getting this data. (but only the data that was not obtained in the first event, that should be shared by the PCs. Only repeat the data if the PCs that get it don't share it for some reason)

Eventually, if everyone is sharing their info, the plot can be uncovered, and a major raid can be performed to stop whatever is happening. One NPC in each city must be taken out by a group of players. This NPC will get no help from any of the normal NPCs in the game, so you don't have to wipe out the entire city to get the bad guys.

The benefits of this style event:

1. Since the mobs are non-aggro, you don't wipe out tons of newbies with the event.
2. Since it is wide spread, with varying levels in different dungeons and cities, many people can participate in it, and even newbies can participate in it just by exploring their home cities.
3. Sharing information between people will be encouraged, since it will become obvious their is not second chance at the info.
4. By requiring raids in different cities, but ones in which the locals won't defend the target (who is only hinted at by the evidence, not absolutely fingered - gotta make the right guess or else a whole city aggros you ) you can encourage cross-guild cooperation since even many larger guilds may not be able to field a big enough force to raid Oggok, Grobb or Neriak by themselves without having to wipe out the entire population.
5. By having the finale include each city, every newbie in the game (except maybe the Iksar, although you could probably include Kunark) would be able to witness it.

Perhaps after each city 'boss' is defeated, the final master conspirator could be tracked across Norrath - he could be moving at spirit of norrath speed so groups of people would need to 'pen him in' with some good coordination.

Just a suggestion.
