The word "title" has a whole long list of definitions in my dictionary, but only two apply here:

1. a descriptive name.

2. an appellation of dignity, honor, distinction, or preeminence attached to a person or family by virtue of rank, office, precedent, privilege, attainment, or lands.

Is this a descriptive name? Yes, as are a huge number of other names in Everquest.

Does this meet the standards of the second definition? No. Calling someone "Sir", "President" or "Lady" is using a title. Calling someone "girl" is never a title. It is either a casual chatty way to greet someone or an insult.

The list of examples that Verant used in this rule ALL fall under the second definition. Not ONE is an example of the first. That shows me that Verant was not intending to exclude any name that could possibly fit under any of the roughly 27 different definitions of the word "title" in my dictionary, but that they were intending to exclude honorifics. This is NOT an honorific.

You can continue to disagree with me, but you're wrong.
