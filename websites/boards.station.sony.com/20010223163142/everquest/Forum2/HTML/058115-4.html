Due to a high level of frustration from the (lack of) results from the last necro thread, I have refrained from really reading this one or voicing my opinion. However, nothing ventured, nothing gained.

I did not create a necromancer to become a corpse summoning manastone. I created a necromancer to be a damage dealing master of the undead. Here's what I'd like to see that would resolve my complaints with the end-game for necromancers:

Is there any reason why the level 59 necromancer pet can't be what the level 49 pet was when the game was originally released? Sure, at level 50 in EQ Proper zones, the original 49 pet was overpowered. But the Kunark and Velious mobs are a LOT tougher than their original counterparts. I don't understand why it would be overpowering for the pet to hit in the 70's when we're fighting mobs with 250,000+ hitpoints. Also, the pet requires a peridot to cast, so it should be a LOT better than the level 49 one and right now it isn't. Even with this adjustment, a well equipped warrior or rogue will still outdamage it. This would make the 59 pet a joy to watch in action. Every necromancer with it would be the envy of those without it. Not only would it look like a death dealing machine, it WOULD be a death dealing machine. Add to it either an innate ability or a buff to make the pet resistant to fear, and the pet even becomes useful on dragon raids. I'm not asking for the pet to be IMMUNE to poison, fire, cold, or even immune to fear. I'm just asking for the pet to do something other than run from the mob I'm fighting.

Make lifetaps (nearly) unresistable the same way they were intended to be at the release of the game. However, keep the level cap so that a necromancer can't drain a mob which is more than 5 levels higher than they are. A level 59 necro wouldn't be able to drain a level 65 mob, but a level 60 necromancer would be able to. This would encourage every necromancer to reach level 60, and at the same time solve the complaint that necromancers can no longer do damage on raids. (If I am mistaken about the highest level mob being 65, adjust the level range accordingly.)

These two modifications would "Fix" the necromancer class in my eyes. I would have a reason to put myself through the "hell levels" to become a Warlock. I would be able to do on raids what I created a necromancer to do in the first place. I would no longer be just a corpse summoning manastone. I would once again be a damage dealing Master of the Undead.

The following is just things that I think would be nice:

Quit having pets take half of the necromancer's experience. (This goes for any pet class.) It's a spell, it's a weapon we use. A wizard doesn't have to split his experience with his ice comet. A warrior doesn't have to split her experience with her Ykesha. Why do pet casters have to split their experience with their pet?

Make Vampiric Embrace castable on others. Or, give us an upgrade post 50 which can be cast on others. It's a great spell, if you're a meleer. (And give SK's a non-nerfed version of all our spells. They're necro-warriors after all.)

A version of lich castable on others. Even if you had to make it a nerfed health to mana ratio, this would prohibit clerics from obtaining free infinite mana yet give improved mana regen when no clerics or bards were around.

General game enhancements:
Can necromancer (and all characters for that matter) get more hitpoints? At level 58, I had 831 hitpoints if I was unbuffed & nekkid. With cleric buffs and equipment, I've had up to 2500 hitpoints. But the mobs I'm fighting can hit for 500 points PER HIT. Even greens hit me for 140+ points a shot PER HIT. If I'm just fighting one mob, I'm being hit for 280 to 1000 points per round. I just don't see how it is unbalancing to be able to last more than 3 rounds without having clerics chain casting on you. Every character class needs to have their natural hitpoints at least doubled.

In every other RPG game I've ever experienced, the melee characters tank mobs while the casters stand back and zap. The casters do more damage, offset by the fact that they can't take a beating like the tanks do. But in EQ, if a caster isn't in melee range of a mob, the majority of the spells are resisted. Spells need to be able to hit for full damage from the edge of their spell casting range.

Tanks need to be able to BLOCK a mob. If I'm in a dungeon, an ogre can block me from running down the hallway. The mob can just pass right through him. You told us that mobs have more power to compensate for the fact that your AI can't compete with the human brain. But any time that we use our brain to outthink the mob, it's considered an exploit. (Use an AE to kill a mob trapped behind a locked door? Exploit.) If we're not allowed to use our advantage, then why are the mobs allowed to use theirs? Let our tanks block the mobs and our casters hit them from behind the tanks.

*A lengthy plea to the gods of Norrath*

My suggestion is that you make the pets resistant to fear, because undead fear nothing; resistant to disease,
because undead are supposed to use disease as a weapon, not be plagued by it (no pun intended); resistant to
poison, because they have no blood or nervous system to attack.

I know this is a fantasy world, and "realistic" is a term commonly thrown around, but - while we can throw out
realism and suspend disbelief in a fantasy setting - inductive reasoning and logical associations should hold true.

As for the other points, here is what the necromancer really wants (in order of importance):

1) Lifetaps/lifetap dot's that work at least 51% of the time on major enemies. This is our bread and butter line of
spells. If one spell line defines the necromancer, this is it. Pets are great to have, but we'd be a mage if we wanted
powerful pets. God knows verant nerfed ours to near uselessnes.

2) One "get out of nerf free" card, whereby the necromancer class can play this card at their leisure, when they
feel they are going to be nerfed unfairly (again). Ok, maybe this is just a fun one. =)

The real #2) Risk/Reward re-evaluation. Pet improvements and/or dot improvements to bring our damage output
inline with the other classes in the game would help. Necromancers can outdamage bards. That's about it. We are
pure casters - pure casters devoted to EVIL! We want to kill. At this time, druids, clerics (/boggle), shaman, and
every other unlikely class can humiliate us when competing for damage. We do negligible damage in battles with
mobs over level 55. Our pets cause more trouble than they are worth in the planes. We cannot safely visit more
than three cities and an outpost without struggling for days with newbie quests, only to be beaten down by some
unrelated faction within a city. The risk/reward for necros is nowhere near balanced. The necromancer class is the
most persecuted. Shouldn't there be a reward for all of our troubles?

3) If not #1 or #2, then at least a different role than mana battery. There are two lines of spells most
necromancers would prefer replaced by two new lines. That being the mana transfer spells and the essence
emerald spells. Mana transfer spells are a waste! Why convert my mana to a person with a role, when I should
have a role myself? This is just plain humiliating to necromancers who have struggled through 50+ levels of
prejudice and hatred to end up dumping their hard-earned mana to the high elf wizard so he can get off another
nuke, or the dwarven cleric so he can heal someone. Humiliating! We want a job after level 50. Give us something
we can do besides tailoring backpacks.

Essence emeralds are too costly. Plain and simple. It's nice to have powerful spells, but why bother memorizing
them if their cost is unbalanced. Would anyone in their right mind sacrifice hours of their play time so someone can
gain 1000 mana? NO! Make it targetable? Heh... why not just give it to all other classes but necromancer and make
it free for them to cast. Who do you think will absorb the high pricetag on this spell? You guessed it - the person
who casts the spell, not the one who receives the mana. But, again, this is another case of necromancer mana
being useless, so we may as well give it away to someone with a role in the end game.

4) Group DMF. This should be in the game anyway. It has no balancing effect, as the group wouldn't be waiting on
necromancer mana anyway. Mana checks are for clerics, enchanters, and anyone else who has a role in the end
game.

5) Make plants plants. Yes, a druid had a great point that humans are, indeed, animals, so they should be able to
charm them, but this would be unbalancing. Plants in PoG would not! Necromancers don't do all that much in PoG or
any decent high level zone, for that matter. If we cannot kill plants with defoliation, then turn it into a disease
based dd that works on any type of mob. The damage/mana ratio is not any better than that of any other classes
ratios.

6) Stop calling us overbalanced and get someone to play a level 51+ necromancer for a couple of months. With all
the resources you have available to you, one would think you could pay someone to play a necromancer. God
knows it might come to that if we are nerfed any further.

7) When you're holding an apple in one hand, please discard the butcher's knife in the other. Not only have
necromancers received the most nerfs since beta, we have also been cheated out of precious skills by giving us
flashy utility spells that none of us use after a week. Examples: voice graft, deadeye, track corpse (that just
started working, 6 months after implementation, but is too annoying for anyone to want to use), chill bones (which
has yet to drop on my server).

8) Make necromancers groupable with other necromancers. One necromancer per raid is all that anyone wants
since you cripple our class with unstackable spells that aren't even that powerful to begin with. Ignite bones would
be *decent* if more than one necro could cast. DOT's would be ok if they worked on big mobs, stacked, and didn't
poof corpses (which still happens). Our healing spells (which have HORRIBLE ratios, by the way) would be nice if
they didn't take so much of our life, stacked with people's buffs, and stacked with other necromancer's spells.

Plus, not only do our dot's not stack with our dot's, but many of our dot's are not compatible with ANY enchanter
dot whether it is of a related sphere of magic or not. Shaman get half of the same dot's as we, and they get them
sooner. Guess what? They don't stack either.

I realize that necromancers aren't meant to be the most "group-friendly" class in the game, but give us a role,
PLEASE! Let us be wanted in a group BEFORE the cr begins. Let two of us group together and not have to waste
half of our time coordinating what we're casting and when.

I may have messed up the order a touch, but you can tell which of these is important to our class, and which
would just be nice to have. I suppose I should have said first, and foremost, we want to be needed again. I recall
being a level 50 necromancer (a couple months into final), entering lower guk and getting /tell flooded from people
wanting me in their group. Now, nearly level 60, I enter sebilis - the kunark equivilant of LGuk - and beg for hours
before I can find a group if my guild has no openings in their camps. Any other class can enter that zone and be
immediately snatched up. Let me repeat that last statement; ANY other class can enter sebilis and IMMEDIATELY
find a group.

That is a slap in the face to those of us who worked so hard and spent so much time, effort, and hard-earned
money, only to have our frayed ends of sanity snap back in our faces as we realize how much of our lives we've
wasted to become useless. If I wanted to be useless, I would never have taken the time to level up; I would have
just sat around and roleplayed and done newbie quests.


I ramble. Hopefully you will find one bit of usefull information in my post, Absor. If so, please take it to heart when I
tell you that these issues are very important to us as well as you. You offer a product that should build in value
and customer satisfaction as time progresses. At this point, you are losing many customers because you have
pigeon-holed the requests of 9% of your customer base (9% is close to, if not exactly, the amount of necromancer
main characters in the game). We built up our characters only to see their relative power diminish. What is the
point?


Please, just give us a role. Mana battery is not a role. Second-best pet caster is not a role. Third-best dot'er is not
a role. Worst nuker is not a role. Worst items degrades our role. I could go on...


We want to be needed and useful again. That's not so much to ask.
