To the best of my understanding, a monk's intended role was to be a medium tank with high damage output. At level 50, they fulfilled this role admirable: only a rogue could inflict more melee damage, and with decent equipment a monk's high AC compensated to some degree for their low hit points, enabling them to stand in as a tank to some degree.

Post-50, this changes dramatically due to a convergence of two factors. The first is the skill cap changes introduced with the Ruins of Kunark. Prior to RoK, one of the monks biggest advantages was the fact that while other melee classes capped their skills around 200, monks could reach 230 or even 252 in many of their combat skills. This allowed them to deal more damage and recieve less via passive defensive skills and the defense skill iteself: a 30 point advantage here compensated to a large degree for the shoddy equipment monks were given. After Kunark however, this advantage melted away: because of the 252 limitation imposed on skills via the use of an 8-bit integer to store the relevant value, monk skills simply could not rise to the degree that the skills of other classes did.

Secondly, with Kunark, marvellous new armor and weapons were introduced for every melee and hybrid class: except the monk. Sadly, while cobalt and mrylokar's armor sets were introduced to warriors and rogues, monk's got Crescent: armor that is even worse than the Shiverback which we already had rotting in the bank. To be sure, monks got some spiffy new weapons but so did everyone else. Sadly, a level 60 warrior is able to compete with a monk in terms of damage dealt. Why? Well, owing to the quirks in the EQ combat system, which the item design team does not seem to understand particularly well, the top monk-only weapons (Tranquil Staff, Runed Fighter's Staff, Imbued Fighter's Staff) are outperformed by a multi-class weapon combination like the Jade Mace/Stave of Shielding combo. Warriors have the even more effective Blade of the Sebilite Croaking Dirk/Blade of the Black Dragon Eye combo available. To top it off, Warriors get triple attack, Rogues get double backstab... and monks get their weight restriction eased by 10 stone. This disparity is compounded by the fact that the new weapons for monks all take up significant chunts of their 14 to 22 stone limit: from 5.3 stone for the Jade Mace/SoS to a whopping 10 stone for the tranquil staff. Exceeding their weight limit causes a monk to lose AC. The excuse given is that these weapons are so powerful that there MUST be some drawback. The flaw in this logic of course lies in the fact that other classes have comparable or better equipment available to them, as I demonstrated above, with NO such limitation. You'll note that a warrior does not have to sacrifice damage if he wants to maximize his AC, why should a monk have to sacrifice AC to do damage?

Where did this leave things? Well, warriors are now the preeminent defensive melee class, while rogues grasped the offensive title quite securely. Sadly, the monks are NOT a blend of the two. Rogues can have higher AC than a monk and warriors do comparable damage to a monk. Is there any intention to look into this, or do monks comprise a small enough segment of the population that it is acceptable to allow them to remain a subpar melee class?

The only tests I have come across which dispute my own findings of the damage equivalence between monks and warriors was the test that Gordon ran a while back. I have a few problems taking that test seriously however. The ONLY weapon comparison he actually identified was that of a level 60 monk's fists against dual Short Sword of the Ykesha. This is not a valid comparison: SSoYs were subpar weapons at level 50. If I equip a monk with Cracked Staves and give a warrior those same SSoYs, then the warrior will badly outdamage a monk, it's just not a valid comparison of damage output due to the equipment discrepancy. test out some of the combinations I've listed: a Tranquil Staff against a Sebilite Croaking Dirk/Blade of the Black Dragon Eye combo, a Jade mace/Stave of shielding combo against a Jade mace/High_Ratio_Weapon00. It is possible that my own tests have been executed incorrectly, but my results when high level warriors and high level monks equip comparable weapons indicate that they inflict comparable damage.

To summarize:

Offensive Rankings:
1) Rogue
2) Monk/Warrior

Defensive Rankings:
1) Warrior
2) Rogue/Monk

So, why choose a monk over one of their more powerful brethren?

For the most part, I support the post Authored by Bodi and propogated by others that visit monkly-business.com.

I'd like to add this to the mix though, What is the job of a melee class? Melee classes are supposed to deal large damage with weapons or fists and absorb damage.

As it stands, Warriors are meant to be the damage sponge. They have supperior hit points, armor class and disciplines compared to other classes. They also need to have a means of keeping a mobs attention throught a fight. This is done by having a substantial damage output and the skill taunt.

Next we come to the Hybrids, they are meant to deal good damage, absorb damage and hold a mobs attention. They are supposed to do this job a little less effectively than a Warrior. Hybrids can come close in tanking ability when compared to warriors, but are a bit behind.

Now look at rogues. They are designed to be stealthy and to stay low profile while dealing massive amounts of damage with thier ability to backstab. They are supposed to deal very large damage and absorb damage somewhat less well than a hybrid.

The last type of melee is the monk. It is my understanding that a monk is meant to deliver large amounts of damage while avoiding damage thru their damage avoidance skills and the Feign Death skill. Monks are supposed to deal the most toe to toe damage of any melee class. They are also meant to absorb damage somewhat well and avoid it very well. I belive a monks deffensive ability is intended to be somewhere around that of the best hybrid classes, but not exceed them.

Now take these deffinitions of the melee classes and see how the monk stacks up.

Deffensive Abilities
=================================================
Monk HP fall somewhere below most all other melee classes. In my experience monk HPs are pretty similar to that of a rogue. All hybrid classes outpace monks and warriors do by an even more substaintail margin.

Next, look at armor class. Monks do decent here and fall behind warriors, above some hybrids and are similar to a rogue. From my experience, monk AC falls somwhere in the middle of the melee pack.

Lastly, consider deffensive skills. Monks do standout here. We have the wonderful ability to block incoming attacks. This gives us the opportunity to avoid attacks better than any other class.

So, that being said. Where do monks stand in the deffense category? From my experience, monks are of midrange ability in absorbing enemy attacks. The ability to completely avoid some attacks, offsets a portion of the HP defficency. But, monks tend to take larger damage on each attack that lands and some efficency is lost. Add to this a mediocre AC and I believe you see monks falling somewhere in the lower end of deffensive ability. Adequate for some situations, but poor in many.

I consider monk AC to be close to where it should be, but I think all classes need at least some increase in AC. The ordering is just about right, but I think the gap between the high end warrior and those further down the totem poll should be closed slightly.

I believe a small upgrade to some of the lower end melee is warrented as well, monks included. Nothing overwheleming, but perhaps 3-5% more. Meaning a level 55 monk would gain roughly 45-75 hitpoints base.

Consider the monks ability to mend and we gain some ground relative to a warrior and rogue, but all hybrid classes can regain hit points thru varrious spells. Essentially Mend leaves monks with the same sequential power, but shifts them relative to the 2 other pure melee classes.

Damage Avoidance Skills - A supplement to Deffense
==================================================
Feign death was intended as a way to keep a monk from taking damage, but with the advent of FD pulling and verants disdane for the practice it no longer functions as intended. Mobs of lvl 32+ will most often not be memblurred and in group situations will commonly act as if the monk never feigned death.

Rogues also have a skill that is meant to lower themselves on the agro list of a given Mob. This skill is evade. In it's current form it drops a rogue to the bottom of the agro list allowing the rogue to deal more damage without worry of being attacked for some time. This is soon to be changed, however, and it will only drop the rogue a few notches on the hate list.

It is my understanding that these two skills were intended to have a similar effect in practice although going about it in different ways. As it stands, Feign Death does not achieve what it was intended to an overwhelming majority of the time.

My suggestion is this. Add to the Feign Death ability an agro lowering component. If a complete memblur does not occur on a given mob, the monk should be dropped on the agro list some fraction of what rogues are. This should not affect FD pulling in any way since when doing this, the monk is the only PC on the hate list thus dropping them 0 slots on this list.

Offensive Output
==================================================
Monks are a damage dealing class. We are meant to let the warrior or hybrid take the punishment while we anihilate our common opponent. With our positioning in the deffinsive arena, I believe it is clear that monks are meant to have the second highest damage potential for any melee.

Rogues are supposed to do the absolute maximum melee damage when allowed to backstab. This is justified by the fact that their lack of healing capacity, hit points, armor class and deffensive skills.

Warriors sit on the other end of the spectrum. They are the most deffensively orriented class in the game. They have more hit points, better damage mitigation and higher armor class than any other melee class. Add to this the effectiveness of certain disciplines and you have the best attack absorber in the game. This being said, they need to have some means of holding a mobs attention. They do this by dealing large amounts of melee damage and the taunt skill.

Now lets consider monks. Monks are supposed to deal large damage to make up for their short comings as damage absorbers. So, a monk should do a good deal more damage than a warrior but significantly less than a rogue.

As it currently stands, warriors have the ability to do a large percantage of the damage a monk can. Some of the new velious weapons have better ratios than the absolute best monk weapons currently known to exist. Because of these weapons, I believe monk damage should be upgraded to increase their advantage over a warrior and close the gap between monks and rogues.

Conclusion
=================================================
Monks are a great class and I love playing one. If the things I've outlined are addressed, I believe monks will be balanced. First, slighty upgrade all melee classes Armor Class and Hit points, but increase those on the low end slighly more than those on the top. Second, change Feign Death to work more closely to what it was intended to be, a damage avoidance mechanism. And last, increase monk damage output relative to the new weapons of other class weapons now available with the advent of Velious.

First order of Business.

From the Rogue Talk Thread:
1)
Bind Wounds: Skill cap pre-50 for Warriors and Rogues was 100. We are likely to be moving this to 175, with a max skill of 210. This would mean a 70% binding well before 60th level. We're also adjusting other classes caps higher as well, but in reality, only Monks, Warriors and Rogues used Bind Wounds with any frequency, and only Pure Melee and Priests will be able to bind to 70% of maximum hit points.

Before this change, Monks were the only meele class that could do this. It was one of our few post 50 improvements that everyone liked and appriciated. It made monks more viable to be able to bind themselves up, and thus being a 'less downtime needed' class. We could even bind others in our group to 70%, thus making us group friendly. Now everyone has this. Thus our advantage is gone.

2)
Instill Doubt/Backstab/Special Attacks. We are not going to change the functionality of this, but we are going to make Instill Doubt gray out backstab and vice versa until the timer has expired. This should make it easier to know when you can use the skill, and prevent a lot of dud Instill Doubt attempts.

This has already started an uproar on the monk boards. The problem here is that monks have had this same problem with Instill Doubt not working with our special attacks, thus we worked out that because Flying kick(or any other special) doesnt grey out we can time the Instill doubt and Flying kick to coincide with each other, that is, if flying kick has just been used, its greyed, but Instill Doubt can be pressed then, and by the time The instill doubt animation gets to the final 'kick' that preforms the action, the flying kick button has ungreyed, and thus ID can work. The final motion of Instill Doubt is when it checks to see if Backstab or Flying kick is greyed, if it is, then it always fails, if it isnt, then it has a chance of working. So Making flying kick grey out with ID, will remove it from many monks hotkeys because we have been doing it this way for months, and this is how it works. Thieves could time there backstabs much in the same way we time flying kicks. If you have to make this change to ID for backstab, then please do not change it for flying kick and other monk special attacks.
3)
Haste increase on throwing daggers at higher levels. Similar to the Monk haste on Shurikens.

Noted.

The point im trying to make is that because you are making hybrid meeles equal to a warrior, then the monk is becoming less and less viable as a different class. Monks arent suppose to be tanks, but because we outdamage warriors(sometimes), we typically are. We are in the same boat that rangers were in before with aggro.

Mend.(A once every 6 minutes self heal of 25% of total hps)
Feign Death.
Instill Doubt.

Mend is useful, and once it becomes reliable (pre 100 skill it can worsen your wounds) it is relied on nearly as much as our attack key. But once used, that 6 minutes can last a lifetime.

Feign Death. A necromancer, or shadowknight, can pull 2 or more mobs, get off a darkness spell, which slows one mob, then Feign Death, the monsters all go back to there starting point, but the darkness'ed one is slower to return, and can then be single pulled. The change to mob returning to start points, was directly related to monks use of FD to pull. Now all it is doing is making us less useful to a group.

Please fix Feign Death to always clear aggro. This would definately help monks become more viable..probably would help necros and Sk's too, but if you make it selective it would make the monks FD more powerful then the others.

Instill Doubt. This rarely works, and the prior information suggests that it may be nerfed along with the changes for the rogues.

The monk is deminishing in differentiation. Why play one? what are the advantages? Does he have any skills or abilites that make him stand out? In some cases the warrior can outdamage him, he has a lower AC, he has no particular skills unique to his class except mend. Now the warrior can bind wounds just as well, and the hybrids can reach levels of damage output similar to the monk, especially the ranger, whos weapons typically have ratio's similar to the monks.

Solutions? The monks need to be viable again. We need to be able to contribute to a group, and show a value of uniqueness. To make a monk more group friendly, you could either reduce his mend time, and allow him to mend others, or/and fix Feign death, so that pulling becomes more of an option no matter the zone. To give him uniqueness, and desireability, he needs higher damage output, an increase of 15-20%, as well as giving him enough defensive skills to continue to be the tank he is now and the better tank he will hopefully be after a damage upgrade. Increaseing dodge/riposte/block skills to max would give us the ability to tank much better then we can now, and would offset our low hit points. Ask yourselves what is the difference between a tank with double the hit points or a tank that dodges or blocks half the hits directed at him.
Please keep these ideas in mind when balancing the monk. I also ask that you look at bodi's and Zadikel's post again, as it also points out many things we as a community have aggreed on that we need.
