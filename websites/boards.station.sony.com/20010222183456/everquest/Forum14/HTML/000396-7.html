Here's the disclaimer. I play a 45 Shaman, so my comments, as with those of most of the poters carries with it a certain self interest. Having said that, let me throw my vote behind what I believe is the concensus here.

"Shaman... her primary focus is healing..." Well clearly not. I was in a well balanced group hunting wurms in Great Divide. No cleric, so I along with a 50 Druid functioned as the emergency healer. On double pulls, we had deaths on three occasions that could not be prevented. GH restores 300+ Hps. The wurms were double attacking for 90, with a kick thrown in for 60 or so, and a DD spell for around the same. Even were I able to chain cast GH, I can't possibly keep up with that sort of damage, even with unlimited mana. Not to mention that the long casting duration of GH frequently meant my potential targets had, of necessity, moved out of range. And this was fighting MOBs of around 34-37, with a 5 person group ranging from 44 to 50!!!

The solution, as I see it, is for Shaman, and Druids as the other secondary healers, to get a more efficient spell; perhaps as suggested a percentage heal. Otherwise, even at this level a cleric has become essential to group function.

As to Stat buffs. Right now other than AC speed and HP's they do nothing measurable. Quick show of hands here, how many of you self cast the others when soloing?? Thought so

Solution? Well there are two schools of though, one is to take what we have, and change the algorithm to make them do something measurable. This does not seem too hard to me; the formula to assess damage taken already has a multistep calculation, to extract from that the element due to the buff as opposed to the equipment seems pretty straightforward. Some kind of multiplier here, together with removal of the soft cap would seem to work. The other school of thought is to add additional categories of buffs, explicitly labelled as chance to hit, chance to avoid and the like. This too, doesn't sound very demanding.

Now how about the pets? They add a layer of complexity without much benefit, unless, and only unless another caster is fearing or snaring the MOB. So if the pet is added as a tool for soloing, it is pretty near useless. Even then, as a shaman ages, the pets become progressively worse, a level 44 pet, buffed, cannot solo a green Dire Wolf in Great Divide. Why not? They are both wolves after all, and my pet is armored, speeded and has more hit points.

Solution. There are two viable approaches, first just make the pets better, why can't I summon a blue pet at 45. I'm not asking for equality with Mages or Necros, second tier is fine, not a pet from the bargain bin. The alternative is to drasticaly reduce the casting costs. If Shaman are one with the spirit world why is it so darned expensive to make a call? Why can't I chain cast pets? Why would this be unbalanced?

Damage dealing is another area that clearly needs a rethink. Straight off, disease spells, as they currently play just take far too long. Soloing they have some utility, as most blue MOBs require more than one Poison DOT application. In groups they are hopeless. The poison based DOTs are mostly OK, although clearly there is a gap at 54, where we should get a vendor spell commensurate with the level, as well as a purchasable poison based DD. As to the rarity of the upper level spells, if the statements are accurate, this needs fixing. If spells are only dropped off epic MOBs, then the effect should be equally epic. Double the mana efficiency of the DOT or DD, make effects lat until you log.

Solutions. Well firstly, either reduce the duration and up the effect of disease DOTs, or create two types, one shorter acting (perhaps a fever line lasting 14 ticks) and a second chronic disease line based on Rabies which as I recall lasts an EQ day, but does a smaller amount of damage per tick, with the two disease types not stacking. Why the latter you ask? To be able to remove the accelerated regeneration that most MOBs have when compared to players, thus adding a tactical choice to the situation. Second, make our MalXXXXX line the total debuff package. There seems no game play or role play reason for disease to be exluded here, and I have never personally, nor heard of anyone else csting the disease specific debuffs.

The MOB debuff line needs balancing, both in terms of fizzle rates, resist rates and measurable effects. Incapacitate, according to the tanks, does not have a measuarble effect on MOBs. This does not make sense, even if this is designed primarily for PVP, I fail to see why this cannot affect MOBs. Once again, a simple multiplier in the algorithm would seem to be a quick and easy fix.

Finally there is alchemy. I can only shake my head here. The quite ridiculous cost is one thing. The absurd failure rate once a potion has become trivial is another. The bizarre unavailability of ingredients a third. (Note to the geographically challenged, Halas is a long way from anywhere, and five zones and a lot of running from the nearest spires and ring.) The worthless nature of almost all of the potions a fourth. The worst thing though is the absence of truly useful potions that could be made, albeit at a high cost. Why not a Complete Heal potion, why not a self or group teleport? What is unbalancing about a Clarity potion?

Solution, just rethink the entire skill, make either worth something, or leave it as is, and make it cheaper by a factor of 10.

Let me end with a question to Absor, if all the Shaman on all the servers stopped playing right now, would anyone else notice? How about Warriors, Clerics or Enchanters? Thought so, now tell us one more time about class balance and class playability in the post Kunark era.

/Sigh
