From a traditional roleplaying standpoint most popular fantasy supports the idea of GvE and I think it is hard for most people to actively PK traditionally 'good' avatars if they are good themselves. However, there is some support in the popular fantasy fiction to support team PVP - i.e. in -The Hobbit- the elfs and humans band together to fight *dwarfs* for the treasure under the mountain after Smaug decimates the human town. This shows that Tolkien (the grandaddy of all fantasy writers) had a more complex interaction in mind for the races. Unfortunately (or fortunately, depending on your POV) the Goblin army intercedes to push the conflict back into a GvE standing at the climax, and shows us that even the master had a hard time coming to grips with this.

From a playability standpoint, I think the 4 team concept could work if amended in the following way:

Faction between the teams should be BAD to begin with. i.e. elves should not be welcome in human, shorty, or Dark team towns, similarly shorties should not be welcome in the other three and so on. As it is now, light teams (such as elfs) can freely travel within 'enemy' towns with impunity. The Dark side team (Ogres, Trolls, Dark Elfs and Iksar) do not get this benefit. Hardening the faction could help solve the cross teaming problem because there would be no advantage (or regular opportunity) to mingle with other-race teams. Conflict within home zones would be more intense across the server as other-race teams invaded.

That failing I would like to see a pure GvE server that would prevent X-teaming, imortal healing and the like. This could be a problem if we base it on religion because I can see how the 'agnostic' selection could lead to X-team possibilities if not handled correctly. A simple solution would be to force a good or evil selection (no agnostics allowed on the server) to enforce the team play. You must be on one team or the other - if you want to be agnostic go to a blue server.

My 2pp (I charge by the hour...)

Things about Team PvP that people need to come to grips with:

Item Loot: Not all play PvP just so we can gank and take something for somebody. Some of us like to get immersed in the epic battle of good vs evil. After a kill, I don't even loot, it is a sign of respect and honor. PvP is more than just PK and Lewt.

Teams: The team layout is just wrong. You have the Dark team, clearly superior in many ways. More races, better location and more players choosing to play the dark team. Everquest was designed as a good and evil game. The resources (banks, merchants, boats, cities) in EQ are in twos, not fours. Not only that, three of the teams (light races) will be competing for those same resources while the dark team will have no competition. Without Light team cooperation, the dark team would rule supreme making it totally a dark server with hunted lights.

Faction: If a dark team player kills a light, no big deal as doesn't affect the dark team player since their resources use dark faction. Try being a light player that kills other lights and 90% of EQ is off limits, especially after the newbie guard buff.

Location: While you have 5 races on 2 different teams (elves and shorts) on the tiny island of faydwer, the Humans and Darks, with the exception of halfies, have access to the whole continent of antonica along with the best old world loot.

Kunark: While Kunark brought more hunting grounds, it also brought to light the bipolar nature of Everquest. There are only 2 outposts in Kunark, a light and dark outpost. 3 teams fighting for firiona Vie while 1 team has total, uncontested control over the Overthere outpost.

Everquest has resources in twos. Why are there 4 teams? I am not talking specifically about loot but the very basics of travel and banking. If you deny the basics to a team, they cannot survive and therefore, there will be little healthy competition and players will be unhappy.

My suggestions:

1. Make the server Light races vs. Dark races. Forget about deities and such since programming all that will be a huge time sink and will require much refiguring of starting locations and newbie guilds, plus the faction hits taken for killing a gnome doesn't take into consideration the deity, just that he is a gnome.
2. Disable xteam healing/buffing
3. On PvP death, player comes back as if rezzed with no spells memmed and no disciplines reset. If possible have the person spawn at their newbie bind point with body. If not possible, the body should be unlootable for 30 minutes (to prevent bind rushing).
4. Give players a 10 second PvP invuln flag upon zone in to prevent zone camping. If they cast, move or attack, the flag drops early.
5. Debuff the newbie guards. They are insta death to any character and have completely stopped city raids.
6. Change level limits as follows: Below level 30, +/- 8 levels like now, above 30 can be hit by anyone 22 (-8 levels) higher. This way, invulnerable scouts and healers can be dealt with. Level limits are irrelevant in a two team environment because if a level 30 is attacked, they should be able to count on their team fighting for them. Yes, a level 60 should be able to go into a dungeon and smoke everyone there if they want to, they earned the right to go where they want imo.

Well, those are all my suggestions that I can come up with for now.
