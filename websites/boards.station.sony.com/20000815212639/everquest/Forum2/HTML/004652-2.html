>>>BTW, this maybe shoulda been on the rants board, but perhaps GW will come down and answer this mere mortal....why why why do you create rules that are detrimental to the players? Play nice started off as a way to prevent KSing and verbal abuse. Play nice should not be exploited as a means of allowing everyone the same chance at a spot in line for toilet paper...err...getting xp.

The Play Nice Policies were created because a growing number of customers were being subordinated by other, more powerful, groups of players. These other groups would claim "ownership" of spawns, loot drops, and even entire zones, refusing to allow other people a chance to experience them. On some servers, guilds had certain areas camped 24x7.

The play nice policies, in regards to spawns and camping, are designed to promote equal access to the areas. If a group can get into a room, they have as much right to be there as anyone else. Are the policies perfect? No, of course what is? No matter what policy we adopt, there will be people who are helped by the policy, and others that are hurt. Sometimes the policy may help them in one instance and hurt in another.

As I've mentioned in the past, if anyone has any ideas for policies that are easy to understand, are enforcable, do not require a larger CS team than before or after the Play nice policies, and will please everyone, I'd be happy to discuss them.

Abashi, you're using a blanket rule to correct a specific problem, and that's where the PnP rules fail. The PnP rules DO create more problems than they fix.

The rule should read: Groups and guilds who camp a spawn site in order to monopolize it for extended and unreasonable amounts of time will be required to leave the spawn site and let others have it. If they continue to violate this rule, said guild can be banned from the site for a week (month, whatever).

That would perfectly correct the extended camping problem without hamstringing legitimate campers. I feel that it is wrong to make a group move on after breaking a spawn cycle.

I've had it happen where newcomers arrive at a spawn site and DEMAND access to the site, even though our group has spent an hour breaking the spawn so that we can hunt there without an utter catastrophe happening every thirty seconds. People who work to secure spawn sites should have an opportunity to enjoy it for a little while, not be forced out by someone taking advantage of the situation.

Guilds, as noted above, often use the PnP rules to THEIR advantage by forcing other campers out. Usually, however, it's just one lone pain in the neck who wants all the benefits and none of the work. In one of my husband's parties, a necromancer decided to leave the group, telling the group that he JUST left that they could have "every other spawn" because he was invoking the PnP rules. So they let him have the spawn. THE WHOLE SPAWN. ;> Needless to say, he did a lot of corpse running for a while. That's a very popular tactic amongst groups who've been unfairly made to share spawns. I've seen that more than once.

I don't think PnP really solves anything, as written. I think it only encourages player animosity.

<<As I've mentioned in the past, if anyone has any ideas for policies that are easy to understand, are enforcable, do not require a larger CS team than before or after the Play nice policies, and will please everyone, I'd be happy to discuss them.>>

The part of the PNP that I think gets overlooked by CS is this: "Note: A "group" in this case is defined as a party of one or more characters that are united in a common belief or goal and are capable of completing that goal." This directly addresses the issue of "my group broke a spawn that the person invoking PNP couldn't have broken, they are riding our coattails," and seems often not to get enough attention from the CS rep receiving the PNP petition.

I think when a CS person receives a petition requesting that the shared spawn rule be applied, the CS person should seriously focus on whether the petitioner is "capable of completing that goal." NOT with the room/camp already broken, but when it ISN'T broken -- and if there's any doubt, even going to the extent of repopping the room/spawn (or waiting for it to if code doesn't permit actively making it happen) and asking the would-be sharer to demonstrate their "capability."

If they can do it fine... but if they can't or refuse to make the effort, no sharing. A lot of folks can hold a spawn if it's already broken, but aren't capable of breaking it themselves for a few more levels. Of course, the CS rep would then have to /kill the spares so the original group that broke the spawn isn't penalized.

Not a cure-all, especially for single spawns like AC. And you would still have the folks who resent having to share at all... but at least it would remove the resentment factor arising when the person asking to share wasn't capable of breaking the spawn themselves, and is truly "taking advantage" of other players' work/risk. And simply being informed that in order to share, you are going to have to handle a fully-popped room will discourage this tactic going forward -- for the true abusers, therefore, this should only take once or twice for them to knock it off.

In addition, you could avoid the add-on CS involvement described in a post up above when a share was enforced, the would-be sharer couldn't handle it and died, and re-petitioned because they didn't get "help."

Just my 2 cp.
