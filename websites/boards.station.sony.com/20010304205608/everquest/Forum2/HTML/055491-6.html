Take your average lvl 60 paladin (if such thing exists...) with 4K to 4.5K HPs against a mob quad-hitting him for 400 pts.

So yeah, CH lands and you can add the 2.5K LH healed to his 4.5K HP pool.
Guess what now, mob continues spanking him and his HP bar keeps going down dramatically fast. Cleric starts casting CH... *splat* ... CH lands on a_paladin's_corpse00. Too bad for the pally that he can use his LH only once every 72 minutes, I guess.
So if you add all the HPs "spent" in the fight, and a very short fight to boot, here you get :

4.5Kx2 + 2.5K = 11.5K

Now take your average 55 to 60 warrior, with anywhere from 4.5K to 6K HPs. Let's even take 5K to be on the safe side. Same mob starts hitting on him, given his superior AC and defense skills his HPs start going down much slower than a pally, but still too fast to his taste. So he activates his Defensive discipline, mitigating the damage received by about 40%. In my book, 40% of 5K = 2K. So anyway cleric has all the time in the world now to land a CH on the warrior who at this point artificially increased his HP pool to 7K. Rinse and repeat for the duration of the discipline, 3 mins, and say the cleric had to CHeal the warrior 6 times during that period. Then after he loses concentration, said warrior starts getting hit to hard and dies.
So let's look at the numbers now, total HPs spent over a 3 mins tanking period :

(5K + 2K)*6 + 5K = ***drumroll*** 47K !

I quit playing by Pally about a year ago. He was only lvl 27. My friend was playing a druid, which especially at the time, was FAR more powerful. I didn't want to start another druid, so I started a necro. Wow, am I glad I did. (He's lvl 45 now.) Necros are far more powerful and definitely more fun to play.

One of the big reasons for quitting the pally was the spell rings. I understand that pallys must be worse fighters than warriors to make up for the fact that pallys get spells. It's only fair. At level 22, I was happy to be a pally, because despite the fact that I was a terrible fighter, I could buff myself a little to narrow the gap slightly, and partially heal myself after a fight. Each level after that, warriors fight a little better, but pallys fight only very slightly better, with no new spells. At 27, the light healing spell that we got at lvl 15 is almost totally worthless. The gap between pallys and warriors grows every level up to 30. Maybe the lvl 30 spells would have made it worthwhile, but I'll never know.

My suggestion is this. Space out the pally spells more. Give them one or two of the lvl30 spells at 26 or so. Give them some of the lvl 39 spells at 35 to help get them through the hell level. I can't imagine what it's like playing a lvl29 pally. Of course, that's why I quit at 27.
