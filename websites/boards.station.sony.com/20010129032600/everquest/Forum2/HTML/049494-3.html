I see two /big/ problems with having damage shield credit damage back to the caster:

1.) This would result in large amounts of unavoidable agro back to the caster. I don't think I need to explain how this is bad.

2.) Casters could KS using damage shields. Especially if significantly higher level than the target, a caster's damage shield could easily out damage the target's damage and credit the kill back to the caster. Damage shields would prolly have to be made group-only to prevent this.

And of course crediting damage shield damage to the person it was casted on would only make it easier to powerlevel because then they wouldn't need to do any real damage at all to the mobs they were fighting.

At any rate, consider fully what you're doing to damage shields if you change how they're designed. Personally, I consider damage shields to be one of the fundamental abilities of the druid class, and one thing that makes us desirable in groups. Making them any less useful than they are now would be a very bad thing.

There is always a lot of bickering by players when druids are discussed. Many think that druids are overpowered, others that druids do not provide enough to groups in the upper tier of play. I think this wide belief in druids ability comes from there being two distinct situations...grouping and soloing.

I would agree that in solo play druids are very powerful. They have a wide range of abilities, perhaps more than any other single class. In solo play this gives them a great advantage. The ability to do practically everything well makes a druid almost a one person group.

However, this strength in soloing is a weakness in grouping, specially in the upper levels (post 50) play. It is in this area that groups need classes that are not simply good at a skill, but are excellent at a skill. Thus the recent threads over the need for shamans to recieve an upgrade to their healing spells so that another class other than clerics be capable of providing some sort of true healing.

If you are asking as to what can be done to better druids in terms of solo play, I would answer nothing, as I believe druids are already plenty powerful in that catagory.

If on the other hand you are asking what can be done to better druids in term of group play I think minor additions that would improve one or more of their 'good' skill. More than likely though you would need to decrease other skills (thus hurting solo play) to balance this.

As far as other things I had always wanted to see that, while not affecting the power of the druid class would increase the enjoyment of the game from the Druid perspective. I had always wanted to see mid-level (30 to 40) druid item quests. Please don't feel a need to think that these quests must be for armor only. Perhaps a spell, weapon, or hand held item. First I want to ask you all to keep within the generally existing mechanics. Healing:

Druids (and Shaman) want to be better at healing. Not only would this give them a more active roll at the end of the big raids, while at the same time reducing perceived ‘need’ for clerics at such times. But we also have to keep in mind that healing is the clerics role, and we do not want to infringe on that role.

Move Superior Healing to 49th level.
