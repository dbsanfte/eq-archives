Hey y'all. Just thought I'd throw my 2 cents in on the Wizard tweaking issue. I have a 12th level high elf wizard that I play exclusively on Fennin Ro. After reading the issues that others have posted - my main concern regarding this class is their usefulness to a party/group. I have found that some of my heavy hitting level 12 spells (shock of lightening, etc.) are a great benefit to a group but they are really not that useful when we are in a large battle with mutliple, tough MoBs. The downtime to regenerate mana is the main problem. I nuke a few enemies and then I'm out for almost the rest of the battle - watching my groups health diminish with no recourse. I think that a nice solution to this problem has already been voiced - namely a spell, or line of spells, that allows for the wizard to summon a familiar. Obviously this "pet" would have no melee or defensive functions (that would be intruding on the magician class) but instead, they would act as a kind of focus for the Wizard - offering faster mana regeneration, causing spells to require less mana, and perhaps adding power to a specific type of spell (i.e. fire-based, cold-based, lightening-based). This also makes up for the fact that Wizards do not recieve any focus inventory items in the game that would serve to enhance our spell casting. Another point to make, regarding the Wizard class in the fantasy genre overall, they almost are always depicted as having familiars - it is a staple in fantasy games, literature, and movies.

I see the addition of a familiar line of spells to be the best solution (each type of familiar would have thier own unique properties and obviously there will only be one familiar at a time (the one pet rule)- that way Wizards wouldn't be able to have faster mana regeneration AND enhanced spells or another benefit at the same time - for balance purposes).

Just a thought! But I have seen posts similar to this one - might be a good feature to add.
