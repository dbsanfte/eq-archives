I am heartened to see that Verant is opening considering many of the issues and concerns raised by the player. Unfortunately, I am somewhat disturbed that the response is more akin to a child being given a shopping spree in a candy store. As someone that has only played a shaman since EQ launch, I would like to offer some reasoned comments on Absor's post. While I doubt my comments will be popular with all shaman, I consider my conclusions to be well reasoned, balanced, and reflect the limitation of the game engine running EQ. Following Alan's structure:

ITEM: Healing.

What an issue. Before RoK healing was not a concern. With the lower hitting mobs and less resistance to the slow line of spells it was possible for a shaman to be a healer. Not to say the shaman was a particularly good healer, but a group could kill for xp with a shaman healing. A support group could do dragons/planes with a shaman healing. With the release of RoK we found mobs that attacked faster and for more damage. Mobs also, in general, were more resistant to slow spells. This is even worse in Velious. No longer can a shaman keep as a primary healer. The problem can be fundamentally traced to one thing - Complete Healing. Now, don't think I am begrudging clerics this spell, I am not. But the capabilities of this single spell have clearly impacted the design decisions in RoK and SoV. The power level of mobs are tied to the healing capabilities of this spell, and nothing a shaman has to heal can come remotely close. Now I have seen alot of clerics dismiss CH in the recent discussions on healing upgrades; I wonder what game these clerics are playing - it is obvious that encounters in the two expansions are designed around the healing potential of CH.

Now, the best solution would be to eliminate CH. This is NOT practical given the level of game changes that would need to occur to balance its removal. Given shamans a heal even close to the efficiency of CH is also not a solution. Torpor was a pretty good solution for shaman, but it comes late for most shaman (lvl 60) and has considerable limitations. The solutions offerred on Test, while nice, don't begin to address the problem; they are only small upgrades in efficiency (2.3 hp/mana to 2.5) or offer quicker healing.

I would offer the following as a possible solution:

-- Move superior healing to 49th level.
-- Move the new chloroblast to replace superior healing at 53rd. level.
-- Add a new spell at level 56. This spell should be along the lines of celestial healing: a very efficient heal over time. This helps close the efficiency gap without greatly encroaching on clerics. I would recommend 4 ticks at 180 hps/tick for 200 mana. This gives a respectable 3.6 hp/mana ratio.
-- Torpor : keep as is for now, there is nowhere near a concensus on the useablility of this spell.

ITEM: DoTs and corpse poofing.

I have not seen this happen in last year plus. Only time I have seen corpses poof is when the person who would have be "awarded" the kill is dead/LD.

ITEM: Group buffs

I am going to broaden this to buffs in general. I want to touch on two points: The cost/effect of the given spells and the effectiveness of those spells.

I have posted elsewhere, that I think the mana costs of the post-50 single target buffs are too high (with the exception of manical strength). These buffs follow the the upgrade progression of the previous spells in the line (roughly +9 more stat, +9 more minutes duration). Unlike the earlier upgrades though, they mana increase by +50 instead of +20. The four single target stat buffs (Agi Sta Dex and Cha) should all have mana cost lowered to 120 or duration increased significantly.

The group version - these spell are just near useless. Their mana cost is too high for second rate buffs (3.5x single target cost). No other class pays this type of mana penalty for a gain in casting efficiency. At worst, the multiple is 3x for some of the cleric group buffs, but those are actually useful for everyone in the group. Without going into a long discussion, I would suggest one of two solutions:

-- Change effects to those of the top level single target buff. This would also entail swapping the levels on the single target/group charisma buff.

-- Keep the same effects but lower the mana cost to 250.

Now, on the effectiveness of stat buffs. I am somewhat disturbed that it seems many shaman following this board think all stat buffs are worthless. It is as if they equate not desireable all the time to worthless. This is my take on the individual buffs:

STAMINA - this is an excellent buff. The hp/mana ratio is on bar with other hit point buffs and it stacks with all other hp buffs. This spell is excellent.

DEXTERITY - my tanks ask for the spell, so I gather it has some noticeable effect for them. Not a use all the time spell, but it has it uses (especially with weapons the proc easily).

AGILITY - the AC from this buff is noticeable, but it is unclear how much benefit is derived form 10-11 AC. Maybe a great buff for monks; without extensive understanding of how AGI works it is hard to say it is more than a marginal spell.

STRENGTH - this is a highly situational spell. It can add from 1-5% to average damage. In a constant pull grind situation, this can be alot and this is a nice spell. In a raid, this spell is sort of weak except to increase rogue backstab damage. I think most shaman's concern with this spell is that it is a very small effect over a very long time. Compare that to celerity which is a large effect. One thing to think on - celerity is +50% damage for 16 minutes for 185 mana. Manical strength lasts 120 minutes for 150 mana. For manical strength to be AS efficencient as celerity it only needs to add +5.4% damage. Just something to consider before calling this spell worthless.

CHARISMA - very very situational.

ITEMS: Alchemy.

Store sell back prices do not need to be increased. If anything the problem lies with other skills that give too much return when selling to stores.

The stat/resist potions should really be simplified to just give a plus to one stat or resist. This might help with solving alot of stacking issues.

ITEM: Pets.

Lower the mana cost by at least 200. The power level is pretty much okay - our pets were never very strong. I am somewhat concerned that the recent pet change lowered the max dmg pet by 2, but it is unclear if this was outweighed by the other positive changes. Also regarding pets, would be nice if the level 50 pet buff, Spirit Quickening, had a longer duration.

ITEM: Spell Stacking

This really needs to be addressed. There are just huge stacking issues with top end shaman spells and shaman spells and potions. This really needs to be cleaned up. Focus needs to stack with Avatar and with the rage line. Fixing the potion stacking with these spells would also be nice.

ITEM: Jaundiced Armor.

If Iksar get JB armor I would suggest upgrading ogres/trolls/barbarians to get a nice racial AC benefit. I know it isn't fun to not be able to get some nice items, but iksar have so many other benefits I don't see why they should get the armor too. Someone commented earlier giving Iksar AC bonus was akin to ogres getting no-stun from front. It is quite obvious this person doesn't play ogres much. The no-stun is not a huge benefit - no where near 20 or more AC. (I don't know exact amount).

ITEMS: Alchemy.

A good description of the changes desired. Just want to add a note to the potion stacking. It's not a problem with the "high level" potions -- its a stacking problem with high level spells that raise more than one stat/ability.

For example. The "Null" potion, in any strength, raises Magic Resistance and Dexterity, and lowers Agility. Null will stack with all shaman cast dexterity AND agility buffs. It stacks with other Magic Resistance buffs.

But it does not stack with spells that raise Agility PLUS someting else. The Enchanter epic Speed of the Shissar effect includes an increase to agility, in addition to its stamina regen and haste components for example -- and it overwrite "Null".

Null with stack with Acumen (stamina regen), Deliriously Nimble (agility buff) and Celerity (haste spell) simultaneously, but won't stack with Speed of the Shissar.

Since each potion raises one item by lowering another, it would be difficult to list ALL the potions and ALL the spells they do and do not stack with.

Fixing this will take a serious revamp of spell stacking in general -- or just remove the negative effects to all our potions
