There needs to be something to do with the Mana Potion spell line. The power of the items, and bonuses added should be scaled appropriately to the level of the mana potion used. To restrict the profit potential for this change, remove the gemstone component requirements for these spells! This would allow the potions to sell for reasonable amounts (cost of the vial), so that the costs of the final product (from the trade skills) would not be well out of line with its overall component costs and the rendered effect.

Examples of this could include:

Magic cookies (non-stackable) effect: Strength +3 for 15 minutes (stacks with all strength buffs).

Normal weapon -- effect: becomes magical for 5 minutes and adds 3 damage to the weapon.

Gate and Bind potions (non-combinable). Allows melees to bind and gate.

Hand held Totem -- effect: increases one stat by 4 for 10 minutes, stacks with all buffing spells.

Magic Arrows (stackable) -- effect: arrow does not disappear for 3 shots, but adds 5 to the arrow damage for each of these shots.

Whoa, so very many disgruntled enchanters. I'm going to try very hard to avoid turning this post into a free-association diatribe on "The Way Things Oughta Be" -- in general, I'm very happy with my class, and I think that for the most part, we are "balanced", even on the dreaded continent of Velious. However, there are definitely a few factors which need to be re-examined, many of which have been mentioned in this thread already.

1. Crowd control in the post-50 playing environment: Yes, we're definitely operating under a serious game mechanic handicap when attempting to do our crowd control job in the 55-60 environment. The trouble is twofold; the much-discussed high magic resistance of many Velious mobs, the proliferation of 'giant-flagged' unmezzables, also most noteable in Velious, and the "charm gap" between 54 (Boltran's) and 60 (Dictate). All of these factors serve to inhibit enchanters severely in performing what has become their class-defining duty.
Suggestions - raise the Boltran's cap to 55 or 56, which would make charm a viable alternative for crowdcontrolling those 56+ critters who otherwise require the mana-intensive Glamour of Kintaz to control. It is definitely more high-risk, both to the enchanter and other players, due to the wild card nature of charm, but it would at least give me another option when faced with the dreaded message that my target cannot be mezmerized. No, I'm not talking about high MR (that comes later ) -- these are mobs that are flagged as unmezable. If I can't mez or charm them, I can't risk using my class' other 'definining' ability in combat and reducing innate MR with Tashania.

Which brings us to the next "problem area": the Tashania line, or, as it's come to be known, Tauntania. I know that this is a powerful line (although less powerful, in terms of actual reduction, than the Shaman or Magician Malo lines, but I digress). However, in "ultra mob encounters", or even in the post-55 game environment, the taunt factor on Tash is, quite simply, absurd. There is no spell in the game which enrages mobs quite so much. I could taunt off a cleric chain-casting CH on the lead tank, a lure-nuking wizard, a slowing shaman AND a DoT-crazed necro in a flash by casting tash -- for about .4 seconds, which is how long I'd last when the mob in question turns his quad-for-400 attack on me immediately afterwards. It has gotten so bad that enchanters are faced with two choices at a typical 'epic'-class encounter: cast tash, or live to cast even one other useful spell during the battle. Look into the taunt factor of tash, please. I'm not asking for a free ride to lowered MR, but the current situation is over the top.

And finally - the buffing conundrum. Yes, I know that enchanters are hardly the only - or even the most put-upon - "buffing" class (Hi, shaman!). In a raid situation (which, by the way, is where most enchanters find themselves spending 90% of their time in the post-50 game), there are two spells that we *must* keep on all members of the raiding party (usually between 20 and 40 people): speed (augment, AQ, WR, choose your poison) and clarity 2. Anything else is just gravy.

So, you ask, what are we whining about, when shaman have to cast 4 or 5 buffs for our 2? Duration. Clarity 2 has exactly the same duration as Clarity; While the 'pure haste' speed buffs generally last from 12 to 20 minutes, Augment has exactly the same duration as Augmentation: roughly half an hour, for each. That is, simply, not long enough. An hour would be manageable. Some of the higher level cleric buffs last over an hour and a half.

Suggestions: Re-tune the Gift series. Contrary to your former correspondent's opinion, it is not "awesome" in the least; it costs too much mana for negligible benefit. The mana boost (200 at level 60?) is a joke. The added mana regen is *impossible to perceive*. Yes, we can stack it with Group C2, but let's face it, since we're spending the majority of our bufftime buffing those *outside* the group (remember what I said about raids, and how we spend our post-50 existence), group spells, while very handy *for the group*, are hardly the essence of our buffing duties.

I do not have 400 mana on hand to buff every member of the raiding party with Gift of Brilliance (which I would be hesitant to do even if I was floating in the stuff, since it's such a useless buff). I would far rather spend that 400 mana on a combination-style buff that stacked Clarity 2, Enlightenment and - perhaps - a breeze-like "added" mana-regen that lasted for *at least* an hour. Sort of Clarity 3, with int, that actually lasts.

This would be a *great* spell, and would also fall in line with the other level 60 buffs given to the other buffing classes: Aegolism for clerics (combination symbol/heroline/AC) and Focus of Spirit for shaman (combination Kragg/str/dex). It would not be "unbalanced" in the least.

Finally, on the miscellaneous other issues that have been brought up:

llusions; sure, illusions are fun, but honestly, other than the occasional trip into enemy towns or the utility factor of some of the elemental forms, I don't use them all that much. I've got actual work to do; I don't have time to put on a show for my group/raidmates.

High cost of higher mezes; Oh, after months of grumbling about it, I think we're finally resigned to spending half our mana controlling one or two 56+ mobs with Glamour instead of slacking off with the irreplaceablely perfect, low mana, low resist, long duration Dazzle (I love Dazzle ). It would be nice if higher level mezes could overwrite lower level mezes, however. This would go a long way towards making multiple enchanters of different levels "stack" when on the same raid.

Oh, and one last thing: I HATE BOON OF THE GAROU. The duration is way too short, the recast too long, the benefits too negligible to make me spend a whole spell slot on what is essentially a party trick. Change any of these three factors, and I'll reconsider
