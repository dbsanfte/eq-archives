thanks for this series of topics. Not only is it good PR, but, some things are going to change and that would be great.

I'll be first to admit that warrior is a very well defined class, that has recieved a ton of positive upgrades since retail realease. As for our slot in the class hierchy, we are wanted in groups, essential at high levels for raids, and an entertaining class to play.

But, that's not exactly what you wanted to hear, so... here is my list of complaints, suggestions, and requests.

First off, I wanted to talk about the warrior's epic weapon. It can be changed from a 2 hander, into two 1 handers, and back again. This is a great ability, however, the 2hand 'proc' debuff and the 1hand 'proc' debuff do not stick together. Sometimes it seems that the 1 hand procs wont stick together either :/ This is a major problem when you are in a raid type situation in which multiple warriors have their epic weapon (my guild has 4 warriors with it, so I experience this often). The slow speed of the main hand weapon, as well as the ratio of the weapon make the proc damage ESSENTIAL to keeping up with other melee classes with their epics.

I'd like to propose the removal of the debuff all together in favor of just about anything else. Make the weapon faster, add some damage to the proc or something to that effect. the debuff doesnt seem to taunt (which I think was the oringal design of it) as well as speed, damage, or anything else does. By improving the speed and the damage on the proc, your certainly creating 'taunt'.

Secondly, I'd like to talk about HP regen. Casters get meditate to help in mana regen, which is their means of life. Well, our means of life is our HP and our HP only. When travelling, or when without a cleric, Healing to full can take just about as long as a lifetime. I feel that adding some hp regen, scaled by level, to all warrior classes would certainly help eleviate the 'we MUST have a cleric' mentality... as well as remove some of the binding melee whine.

Third, I know its a dead topic, but binding is an incredible issue with the class. We have no movement enhancing spells, we have no means of healing ourselves, we are unable to hide, sneak, mend, invis, feign death, or anything of the like. Travelling is exceedingly difficult with this class when trying to retreive your corpse. Consider returning the now out of game item 'Locket of Escape'. Obviously with this item, a warrior was able to target themselves and bind themselves to a spot, even if it wasnt in a city. This is a nice perk for the people high enough level to obtain one or two of these items. Personally, I was bound at sebilis as soon as I got my key using this method... it made corpse recovery amazingly less time consuming. Consider making it either purchasble for a large sum from vendors, class creatable, or a drop in a very difficult zone on random mobs (or perhaps a on a 'all 60+' random table). If none of these options are possible, put a no drop item of 50+ charges on an epic mob (not a dragon/venrilsathir, please, but a mini boss of sorts) that can at least grant a _few_ of us this ability.

Fourth, lets talk about taunt. The recent improvement to make it work _sometimes_ on 60+ mobs is good, but, its not what we were really looking for. With the addition of certain weapons for other melee classes it has become nigh impossible to taunt off of some of them, even with _our_ epic weapon. An improvement on this skill would be very welcome. I mean, as a level 60 warrior, I really should be able to pull that krup knight off of a paladin who proc'd one too many times with their new Plane of Growth sword

Also, several of our disciplines either do nothing, or are horribly weak. Specifically when using the level 60 discipline, does it even have an effect, or is it broken? Even when the damage has been parsed, there is so little improvement with that discipline its impossible to tell if it was marginal error or the discipline at work. The refresh times on our disciplines are horribly out of whack, I think and should be reviewed. Also, what happened to velious disciplines? I'd hoped we would have gotten one or two new ones (hello flurry and rampage, those are awesome) but that was overlooked. Yes I'm aware we got the same ones all the hybrids got =P

Now lets talk about balance.

What is a warrior's role in the vision? is it to be the most 'damage absorbing' class? is it to be the one that makes complete heal most effecient? is it supposed to be the class that should do the most damage over time, because they will survive longer?

First, I dont have a clue about 'damage absorbtion' because its been a secret on verants side of the world since the game was retailed realease. We know from beta that there is 'avoidance' ac and 'mitigation' ac. We dont know which is which, how much of each we have, or whether or not it caps at some point on either. This basic understanding of how the game works is more vital than any peice of knowledge a warrior could know.

as for 'balance' an iksar monk, a half elf paladin, a troll shadowknight, and an ogre warrior all have nearly the same ac at 60 with their 'best' gear. Sure, it varies from 1150 (iksar monk with a few velious items) to 1200 (Furor's base ac, unbuffed, from FoH) but how big of a difference is that 50 ac? Is it mitigation or avoidance ac? IF that monk has '1100' and the warrior has '1100' is it the same? How does it affect combat? is there an ac 'soft cap'? If so what is it?

Knowing all of these things is integral to playing the class.

Also in the same vein of 'absorbing damage' should we gain natural resists in all 5 areas? I mean, our job is to be poiosoned, diseased, frozen, burnt, and blasted in place of everyone else. After 60 levels, you'd think I'd have built up a resitance to some of it other than just magic.

Secondly, if we're supposed to take damage, we _MUST_ be able to taunt mobs. This is accomplished by improving the 'taunt' skill, by speeding up our weapons to be faster than most other classes, or adding 'hate' procs to our most common weapons.

If its to do the most damage over time, by surviving longer than most.. Well, I'd say we're close to that. Some tweaking, as well as some 'warrior only' weapons that compare to some of the other classes choices would be advisible in this area

If its making us the best person to 'complete heal' then, we do that pretty well too. We do have on average more HP than any other class, we thank you for it.

In closing, I'd like to make a 'wishlist' set of requests.
Please consider making the AC display OPTIONAL so we can understand avoidance/mitigation ac, as they did in beta.
Please consider putting in a few quests to make the most popular shields (especially the warrior only ones) back wearable.
Please consider once again reviewing and perhaps improving the warrior epic weapons.
Please consider the HP regen idea as mentioned above
Please consider adding some 'ATK' and 'AC' to our berserk state. improving this, scaled by level, would certainly add to the class.
Thanks for reading this 'War and Peace' sized post. I certainly hope to hear your words on parts of it (or all of it!)

The melee classes in EQ are under-powered. Actions and game design by Verant strongly suggest that they have known about this issue for quite some time, yet have not outwardly acknowledged or made moves to fix the problem.

Case 1 (PvP and duels):
In PvP (duels) spell and pet damage are greatly reduced. Why? Because the spell classes are far out of proportion to the melee classes. Why else would you tone down spell effects? Why aren’t all classes on the same relative power curve? This points at a huge design flaw in the various character classes.

Lets use an example of a warrior vs. a wizard. Now, I do agree that a high level wizard with good equipment should be able to defeat a warrior of equal level with good equipment (provided the wizard has at least a little space to move around in and doesn’t have to stand right next to the warrior). So what has Verant done to make this encounter "fair"? They’ve toned down the effectiveness of spells (and pets) when fighting other player characters. This is admission by Verant that the classes are completely unbalanced, plain and simple. Hmm, we can’t have a player caster take down a player warrior in one or two spells, so we’ll just handicap the casters. Did you guys ever stop to think that maybe the current power level of the casters was just about right, but that the melee classes are instead horribly under-powered?? There should be NO change in power of any sort when people duel. Spells should NOT be toned down. Pets should NOT be toned down. The very fact that these rules exist means that other classes should be toned up to match. Again, I still believe that a HIGH level wizard SHOULD be able to defeat a warrior of equal level. The general power-curve of a typical caster starts lower but ramps higher than the power curve of a melee type (which should be pretty linear). However, what you have is a power ramp for melee classes that stagnates at the top of an S-curve. Poor design.

Case 2 (Soloing):
High level melee classes can’t solo a damn thing. In fact, even monsters that con "green" are often too powerful for a well equipped warrior to solo. Meanwhile, druids and other spell casting classes are not only soloing "blue" creatures, but even quad-kiting them! On top of this, even if I do find the occasional "blue" mob that I can solo, the recovery time from such an encounter is about 10 times the recovery period of an equal level caster. Wow, talk about well-balanced classes on Verants part (sarcasm intended). Now, I understand Verants desire to make people group more. In theory this is a noble goal. However, what Verant is actually saying is, "We just want melee types to group more". In reality, I enjoy grouping in EQ. However, the CONSTANT need for a group in this game is at times REALLY ANNOYING! Sometimes I just want to sit down at my computer and go kill some monsters without the setup time it takes to form a party and find a camp.

Case 3 (Armor Class):
Two of the most fundamental attributes of the warrior are AC and Hit Points. Interestingly enough, Verant doesn’t actually seem to think warriors should be able to acquire the equipment needed to attain excellence in these attributes. Now, I’m not complaining about the best armor we can get in the game. What I take objection to is an equal level shaman that can attain AC equal to or better than myself and NOT NEED THE ASSISTANCE OF ANYONE. What is up with this rip-off? A caster can, by himself, get armor that nearly equals my armor that took AT LEAST one full group to acquire. Give some melee types a break and spread the armor around a bit more. On top of that, it seems that all the best warrior armor has to be some of the more rare-drop items in the game. Compare some top-of-the-line shaman armor or bard armor to some top-of-the-line warrior armor and you’ll see a price difference of at least 2x. True, there is a greater demand for the warrior items because there are more warriors. And why wouldn’t there be more warriors? You need a ton of them to be effective because they are so under-powered. If they weren’t under-powered then why decrease the spell effects and pet power? We’re all on the same level, right? (Again, much sarcasm intended.) One possible solution to this problem is to adjust the frequency of dropped items based on how many people in the game can use the item (pretty simple really). The current configuration of the game creates an unbalanced game economy.

And speaking of twisted economies: People use to sell EQ items on E-Bay. At one point I saw a Cobalt Breast Plate (warrior) and an Elder Spiritist Breast Plate (druid) up for auction at the same time. I did not bid on either item, but I was curious to see the final prices of these two highly desired pieces of armor, as there are a large number of people playing these two classes. The final prices were:
ES BP: $535 (approx.)
Cobalt BP: $1008 (approx.)

Wow. Nice economy you guys have created. From this, I’d say both druids and warriors are getting screwed, but warriors are really taking it hard.

Proposed solutions:
1. Increase the number of hit points for melee classes. Warriors should be granted a substantial HP bonus. Rogues and monks should be granted a smaller, yet still significant, bonus. In fact, I would consider raising the HP of most classes with warriors receiving, by far, the biggest gains. An alternative to this would be to GREATLY increase the level range of "blue" creatures for the melee classes to actually represent the REAL challenge that a monster poses and grant experience accordingly.
2. Change the drop tables to reflect the number of characters that are going to want that particular item. This will create a more balanced EQ economy across all classes.

Addressing complaints from other EQ classes:
No, I am NOT saying any of the other classes are over-powered. I’m not saying anything bad about the other classes whatsoever. I’m not saying that ALL of the really good warrior items are harder to get than the items for other classes. I have played many of the other classes in this game, and have friends that have played all the classes I’ve never touched. I’ve seen the power balance in EQ and, quite honestly, it sucks. The melee classes have clearly been ripped-off in my very educated opinion.

The warrior class is a very well balanced class when viewed from the big picture. I'd go so far as to say that there really are no fundamental changes needed to warriors.

1) We are highly desired in both standard 6 man group fighting
2) We are highly desired in any type of large scale raid
3) We are effective in combat from level 1 all the way to level 60
4) What we do best (tank) we do better than anyone else
4) We are fun to play
5) We get all kinds of fun toys

I could spend quite some time arguing why a large number of ideas brought up in this thread (faster healing, aoe attacks, binding, travelling, etc) are completely unneccessary but I'm sure the EQ team will reach the same conclusions.

Having said all that, there are a few tweaks that I sure wouldn't mind seeing.

1) Epic Quest -- Most of the points have been made (queen blade, delay on main hand weapon, etc.). I'll just simply say I agree.

2) Cobalt Helm and Bracer -- Again, ditto what everyone else has said about the drop rates.

3) Disarm -- Useless...then again, so what? It would be nice if this could do something useful, but it's not a big deal that it doesn't. It's certainly not hurting our class.

4) Taunt -- As others have mentioned, a warrior's primary job is to taunt. The changes made to post 50 taunt abilities were great. I think they are almost sufficient. The fact is, if all warriors could taunt as well as we want to, a lot of encounters would be trivialized. The difficulty in taunting some mobs is what makes some encounters difficult. So I feel any wide ranging improvements to taunt would be unbalancing.
What I think would be neat is a taunt discipline, that improves the performance of taunt for a short time (not unlike the SK harm touch discipline). It could do something as simple as remove the higher-than-you level cap on taunt. Perhaps it could raise the success rate of taunt. It could possibly even make taunt succeed 100%. All of these are viable depending on the duration and reuse time of the discipline. By improving taunt through a discipline, you avoid unilaterally increasing the effectiveness of taunt for all combat, and force the warrior to make the tradeoff between increased taunt and increased tanking (defensive) and increased damage (fellstrike, aggressive, etc).

5) Piercing skill cap -- Warriors are the masters of armed combat, skilled with *all* weaponn types. Why is this cap lower than the rest? With the introduction of 2h piercers, and the large number of high quality piercers introduced in Rok and Sov, this needs to be raised to match the other caps.

As I mentioned above, the warrior really is a balanced class. We are fun, we are desired, and we have a role in most situations. Any changes would simply be gravy on top of what I feel is a great class.
