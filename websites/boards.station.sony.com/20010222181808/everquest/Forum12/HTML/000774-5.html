I'm going to go over a couple of things, one that I think is critical to note, and I'm not sure Alan read it before in my original post in the first thread... going to place it here in caps:

1) A LEVEL 55-56 ENCHANTER IS BETTER THAN A 57+ ENCHANTER VS. 56+ CREATURES.

Why is this? Not because the higher level enchanter can't mez more effectively with Glamour or Rapture, but because the 55-56 enchanter can use the system's /con command and determine if the thing they are about to mez requires Glamour of Kintaz vs. Dazzle. So, effectively, they waste less mana because the 57+ enchanter has to cast dazzle first, or just waste mana casting Kintaz on everything.

This is extremely evident in areas like the Mushrooms in Sebilis where only the highest level of the Mushrooms are 56, the rest are below. The 55-56 enchanter doesn't waste the mana on dazzle trying to mezmerize a 56 Mushroom, and doesn't get the possible agro associated with it making it more difficult to recast Glamour. Effectively making the 55-56 enchanter better. This is why spells should tell you beforehand whether or not they can be cast.

This is a VERY important argument toward why we should have a message stating "XXXX cannot be mesmerized with this spell" BEFORE casting.

2) ENCHANTERS NEED A MESMERIZE LINE FOR GIANTS/GIANT SIZED CREATURES

I know this has been mentioned, but what I'd like to see is something where we start to get a "giant" mesmerize starting at 34th. The 34th version is the same as the 4th level mesmerize for effect, but works on giants. Basically the line would be as follows:

Effect: Mesmerize vs. Large - 34th Level
Effect: Enthrall vs. Large - 49th Level
Effect: Entrance vs. Large - 54th level
Effect: Dazzle vs. Large - 60th level

I think this is fair. It would NOT affect encounters like Vox and Nagafen simply because you've flagged those zones already such that anything 53+ in a non-kunark zone CANNOT be mezed, charmed or stunned. It would make us viable in giant situations and gives us the abilities at what I think are appropriate levels.

3) ILLUSIONS

I really could care less about losing undead illusions, although I really don't think they step into the realm of Necromancers. Why? Because we're able to cloud minds, to make you believe we are something we're not. If it happens to be a skeleton, so be it. If it happens to be a log, so be it.

What I'd like to see is MORE illusions. I like most of the suggestions, like Illusion: Gender, Illusion: Corpse and Illusion: Face. Gives us a lot of fun playing variety. I don't, however, think that we should get Spirit of the Wolf or like speed with our illusions.

4) BARDS SHOULD NOT BE NEARLY AS EFFECTIVE AS ENCHANTERS AT CROWD CONTROL!

This is a biggie that I saw Absor mention in his post, giving Bards more crowd control ability. This will simply make the enchanter class utterly undersirable. A bard will then not only be able to crowd control with little to no mana, they will be more effective at it because they can take the hits when their attempt fails whereas an enchanter cannot. This is the primary function of an enchanter, it is the only reason to play the class, if you take crowd control away from us and pass it to others, making them nearly as effective, you will have a reversal effect on the Essential Enchanter... it will become the unnecessary enchanter.
