After reading quite a few of these posts, I feel that I have something to add to the discussion, so I think I will. I play a level 44 paladin on E’ci, and while I can only imagine and sympathize with the paladins whoa are faced with the dilemmas of post 50 life, I think I may have a few suggestions that would make our class more “balanced.”
I think that many of us reading these threads are forgetting what balance really is. Balance is not having one class be as good at any particular skill as another. That only serves to effectively lessen the uniqueness of the classes, and make the game ultimately less replayable. I think that when we think of balance, we should look at the situation. If any two classes are truly balanced, then two classes in the middle of the same problem, will have different, but as close to equal as possible ways of dealing with the situation. Example: a warrior has lower damage output than a monk, but a monk in turn has lower AC. This enables the warrior to slowly finish off a mob taking less damage per round. The monk will take more damage per round, but will finish off the mob faster.
As I see paladins in Norrath, we are the defenders and the protectors of our party. We do not/should not be the ultimate damage producing melee class, nor should we be even considered in the middle of that category. Where we should excel, however, is protecting ourselves and, more importantly, our party.
I read in an earlier post the idea of adding a parrying bonus to Paladins/Shadow Knights when wearing a shield. I think I’ll take that idea and modify it a little. Add a “block” skill to all melee classes. While Monks can already do this without a shield equipped, require the other melee classes to have a shield equipped for the skill to be used, and for skill to be gained in using it. Not only would this enable Paladins to better serve as protectors, it also makes shields a more viable option to dual-wielding classes. If not that, maybe adding a “chance to block” attribute to the shields would be a good option. Giving the shields different blocking chances would be appropriate, as a buckler wouldn’t be as good for blocking as, say, the larger Qeynos Kite Shield.
Another shield upgrade that I would like to propose, even though it is not an original one, is to increase Paladin bashing skill significantly. We, as paladins, trade damage output for defense when we equip a shield instead of an extra weapon, but I feel to remain as a viable choice as a party member, Paladins should more consistently connect with their shields, and possibly have the damage output increased slightly. Since no large races can be paladins, it’s not as if our slamming ability comes into play here, as in the case of warriors and SKs. I have also read much about the inability to interrupt the casting of many end-game monsters, and I hope that this will be resolved, but as I haven’t personally encountered this problem, I don’t feel comfortable commenting on it much.
The Paladins in Norrathian lore are the fighters who will die for honor, duty, and their loved ones. I think that attribute of paladins is mostly left out of the game. I have read talk in this thread of an AE root-type spell. I think that a simple AE root would be very imbalanced, as it would give paladins a too effective way of crowd control. What I propose instead of this is a spell, that although will work as an AE root centered on the caster, the spell will also effect the caster. Additionally, the spell will add a powerful, but slow dot to the caster only. The addition of the dot would make this an emergency only spell. A Divine Sacrifice, you could call it. The rest of the party is left free to flee the scene, but the paladin is left behind to face the wrath of the monsters that were caught in his spell.
I think the addition of more utility spells would also be welcome Paladin spell book. Whenever I am in a party with a cleric (which is nearly all of the time), nearly all of my spells become useless, because the Cleric’s casting ability totally eclipses my own. This is as it should be, but there should be the addition of several spells to both the paladin and cleric arsenal that may not be important enough to the Clerics to use a precious spell slot on them, but would greatly enhance the ability of the Paladin to contribute to the group. Spells like group Invisibility to Undead, more resist buffs (I see that Verant could have already partially addressed this), and maybe a spell that lowers everyone on the target’s hate lest except for the caster would be nice.
Sorry to make this post such a long one, but I love playing my paladin, and I know the strengths and weaknesses of the class very well. I hope that my comments are read, and that they are helpful.

Hybrids all receive multiple penalties to their casting of spells. As is apparent...

Much fewer spells to choose from through out their careers.

Spells are received very late in their careers, often being 20 or more levels beneath them.

Parent classes will always have "the better version" when grouped together.

Spells cost additional mana to cast over their parent counter parts.

Hybrids have significantly less mana to work with based on equipment and specializations of casters.

Hybrids cannot specialize any of their spell circles.

Many individual spells are individually nerfed to be less effective when cast by a hybrid.

Lastly hybrids contend with the fact that 90% of the time they are casting, they are also supposed to be meleeing. This is where I see the need for a balancing improvement to our spells.

What I believe would benefit Hybrids the most on their spell casting needs is a way to "specialize" in Channeling. What this would mean is fewer interupts of course, but more importantly and entirely the point would be Faster Casting times.

For instance, a SK trying to cast a 7 second dot is almost impossible when tanking or soloing. Not to mention how much melee time is lost. More than half the SKs I talk to do not use most of their higher level spells, like cascading darkness, simply because the casting times are just rediculous for spells that may be resisted or over written anyway.

If a Hybrid had the ability to hasten casting times at later levels, making their spells more useful (or just plain useful) and allowing them to get back to meleeing more quickly (or recast again in the event of a resist) it would even out the fact that their spells are 20 levels too weak and only marginally effective.

It would also mean that less tampering would be needed with a hybrids base skills and attributes (the very things pure classes hold against us) as these faster casting times would mean we would be more effective with our watered down spells.

This makes sense to me.

It makes sense that a Cleric, who has gained considerable knowledge in spells would eventually specialize in the healing circle.

It makes sense that a Necro would eventually specialize in one of their circles of study as well.

This makes both characters better, more efficient casters in the areas they most need.

It ALSO makes sense that a Hybrid would specialize their spells for one purpose and one purpose only. To cast faster. So they can get back in the fight and do their jobs as melee's.

I hear complaints constantly from Hybrids about their spells being obsolete far too often.

But when would the superior heal of a Paladin be more effective than the superior heal of a Cleric? (who has more mana and is specialized in that circle)

Answer; when the paladin can get it off faster. Because he has devoted his study to being able to cast lesser spells quickly, in order to get back to the melee. It still cost him more mana, took longer to learn, and insn't as good as CH. BUT, he has an advantage of speed with his lower spell and specialized channeling.

Right now Hybrids are Forced to make hard decisions; Do we cast dooming darkness, with a good chance of getting it off successfully in only two tries? Or do we waste alot of time trying to get cascading off and to stick, when it takes forever to cast?

So why not create a method that allows Hybrids at higher levels to shave off some of the casting time to their spells? Thus allowing those spells to be more useful and in the end, more balanced.

Absor, your brain must be mush by now.

I've been reading these posts and thinking for a couple days, and wondering if maybe we should be looking at this balance issue from a different perspective.

There has been a huge focus on defensive skills in particular, as a solution to make paladins more "balanced", but I think it will only end up making them lopsided. Putting all our apples in one basket, by boosting a couple skills way up and ignoring other problems isn't a great solution, IMO. For just one moment, ignore everything you know about the classes in EQ, and consider this one point:

A paladin is (supposedly) 75%/25% warrior/cleric. I'm not sure what the actual exact split is supposed to be, but I'll use that as a close estimate for now. Anyway, this implies two basic things -- first, paladins have some cleric-like abilities, but their PRIMARY focus is melee. Second, not counting special class abilities (disciplines, LoH, etc.), a paladin should be able to function at 75% of the offensive/defensive capacity of a warrior in most situations.

The key here is efficiency. From a melee standpoint, if a paladin truly functioned at 75% of the overall efficiency of a warrior, he would actually be balanced (assuming the cleric side of things was also balanced). A warrior would still be more desirable to high level groups, because it would mean greater efficiency and less downtime. But a paladin wouldn't be so drastically worse (efficiency-wise), that it would preclude him as a viable alternative.

As it stands, paladins are currently far far less than 75% of a warrior (ironically, mostly due to their awful melee skills). If paladins can't help make a group efficient, they will continue to be unwanted in groups. But skewing their offensive/defensive focus too far (in an attempt to keep from homogenizing the classes) will create an entirely different problem. Just blindly maxing out paladins' defensive skills without looking at their other melee attributes is a BAD IDEA, and won't lead to any kind of true balance.


I propose the following:

Step 1) This is just the starting point, so hear me out. Balance paladins' skills across the board, in every melee respect, to make them exactly 75% of a warrior with the same equipment and stats. This balancing should not include dual wield because I don't think there's any fair way to balance against that. It should, however include pretty much everything else, including crits and class-specific bonuses. I won't bother to suggest numerical values, since only the Verant people know for sure what effect all the numbers have. But do the math, run the simulations, just make sure you get it 100% right. The idea here is (statistically) to make paladins EXACTLY like having 3/4 of a warrior in the group, for BOTH tanking and damage output.

Step 2) Now skew the offensive/defensive focus toward specializing paladins as a defensive tank. But most importantly, keep doing the math, keep running the sims, etc. Make sure that no matter what, the paladin still has the same efficiency impact on a group as if he were 3/4 of a warrior. I'm guessing that you will find you can't take things as far out of whack as they are on the test server right now, without losing the balance of efficiency.

All in all, there have been a lot of good suggestions in this thread, and I don't feel much need to repeat what everyone else is saying. I will, however, repeat and stress one serious issue with our cleric side:

Our level 50+ spellcasting abilities do NOT constitute 25% of our usefulness to a group. It's a joke in bad taste to give us spells that are overwritten by classes that are present 95% of the time, and then tell us that those spells are supposed to contribute significantly to our desirability. How exactly are we supposed to be desired in a group (for our spells), when they already have the same or better spells?

Our high level spells SERIOUSLY need to be re-evaluated, to focus more on spells that help in combat, and group/self spells that are NOT overwritten by other classes' spells. Stuns are about the only useful thing we can bring to a level 50+ group (until level 55, anyway). Flash of light would be a nice debuff (and a great taunt) if it didn't have the tendency to make a mob flee (as if feared) at around 2x SoW speed. How the hell does it navigate all those twisty corridors that fast when it's blind, anyway? A new ATK or STR debuff of some kind would be wonderful, fitting in with our "defender" role. I particularly liked the idea of a buff that would transfer a percentage of the target's injuries to the paladin. Not so useful for the tanks, but a life saver for the casters.

As far as disciplines go, I'm not sure about some of the suggestions. We definitely need something to make us a contributor in epic encounters, but I'd like to think of copying warriors' defensive discipline as a last resort.

Any kind of LoH discipline/ability I'm not too sure about, as it really doesn't have a huge impact on an encounter in its current neutered state. LoH's only real use is to bail out a wizard who nuked too much, an enchanter who temporarily lost control of the crowd, that sort of thing. It's pretty rare that a situation occurs where you need to LoH your entire group at once. Generally if things are that bad, you're all going to die no matter what unless the evac gets off. Besides, you won't find too many people planning a raid saying, "okay, now first we'll want to get ourselves in a really screwed position, where we're all about to die, so the pally can LoH us..."

Anyway, those were the most important thoughts I wanted to share. I hope you're still reading all this Absor. If so, a sincere thanks from all of us for taking the time to listen.

NEW (I think) IDEA: anti-jolt spell.

Background: Jolt for rangers is a Single Target Hate List Reduction at level 55 for 60 mana and 1.5 second cast.

Proposal: Paladins gain a single spell at the same level rangers gain Jolt that does the exact opposite as jolt: increase their position on the agro list by 1 or 2 positions with a quick recast time but for substantialy more mana cost (200-250).
