Wow, in all my years of EQ, never have I been so perplexed in terms of "which side of the fence to stand on".

Having played on test server since EQ's release (I recently left test server after the item wipe), I have had the opportunity to test many things and work very closely with people from Verant. Smedley has called my house, I've traded emails with GZ and Brad, and I've debated many scenarios here on the boards.... usually in favor of Verant's position. Due to this, I've been labled a troll, a Verant Ass-Kisser, etc. However, my arguments speak loudly, and I'm very unbiased.

I've had direct influence on the changes to the wizard class (it is still lacking a bit, but at this point, I dont know what to add without making it too powerful). I had alot of input on the "big warrior change" that finally made warriors a viable class with the fabled "MR, HP, and Crit patch". But, what about rangers?

First off, I'll admit, I dont play a ranger. My main "play characters" are warrior and enchanter. My "test characters" have been rogues, monks, and a little bit of bards, but the bard was a long time ago. To say the least, I really consider myself an expert on the balance of melee classes, and I think this is important to know because of what I'm about to say concerning Rangers.

If you consider rangers in the "melee" department, I think they are fine. This means, melee damage, AC, etc, I think they are fine. While it is true that rangers get their asses handed to them, what would you propose instead? I've read all the arguments. "Raise the defensive caps, but keep AC the same", "Raise AC, but keep the lower defensive caps", "Lower the 'bugged' aggro factor of Rangers", etc. My question is, how much?

If you remove the "disciplines" of the melee classes, the Ranger is VERY close to the melee damage of a warrior, and even surpasses it sometimes if his equipment is better than the warrior's. Rangers have a large abundance of very powerful 1h weapons. Throw in the fact that many of those weapons proc, and you have taunt-city. On top of that, rangers kick and cast aggro spells from hell (DD, and snare, etc). Personally, I think Verant has their back against the wall with Rangers. The last class that had this kind of outcry was the rogue. And now, rogues are overpowered in my mind, and I'm sure Verant is aware of this. The only reason THAT issue isn't being brought up is because of this huge ignorant view that the majority player base has that they "still suck". That is changing very quickly, and soon rogues will be up for a nerf. haha That is a different subject. The problem is, how is Verant going to change the ranger class without overpowering it? Its melee output is very close to a warrior's and when you throw spells in and procs, it surpasses it. Throw in the ability to SoW, Snare, DoT, etc, and you have a very powerful class. Its only downside is its uncanny ability to also get its ass smacked. The problem is that any small change could end up turning into a very big one.

1) I propose that rangers should not get higher AC.
2)I propose they SHOULD get a higher dodge cap, but all other defensive skills should stay as is.
3)I propose they should get a better selection of 2h weapons.
4)Finnaly, I propose they get a new line of spells. These spells would basically INCREASE the taunt ability of whomever it is cast on. Of course, this spell would be group only, and, would not be able to affect anyone unless they are actually using the TAUNT skill. Thus, it would be applicable to SKs, Paladins, Rangers, and Warriors. With this spell (which would have a fast cast time, recast time and mana would have to be balanced, and the duration would be extremely short), the ranger could basically make someone in his group a better taunter for a short period of time.

I originally had this spell idea for Necros; Scent of Blood. This would make the creature smell blood on the recipient, and thus focus more on that person when they activated TAUNT. However, due to the recent changes made to the necro, I feel necros have their place in Norrath and this spell would be alot better suited for Rangers in their current plight.

To be honest, this may not seem like alot. However, after play testing and balancing SOOOOOOO many issues with Verant, I have come to the startling revelation of how MUCH a LITTLE really is in the world of "balance". What seems like a "little" today, is the best thing since rice 6 months from now. If you just start messing with AC, Dmg, Weapons, etc, then it becomes too cut and dry, and eventually, the ranger will be too powerful, I assure you. This new line of spells would encourage teamwork, would require new tactics, and most of all, would help not only rangers, but groups in general, which would make the ranger even more enticing to groups. Hell, a class that could improve the taunt ability of a tank...thus allowing clerics to focus on 1 member for heals? heh Trust me, this would definitely solve the issue, and then add a little extra to the "group friendly" aspect of Rangers.

Just my 2 cents.
