This is the opinion of a level 47 enchanter, who's nowhere near as smart as some of the mathmeticians that have posted here already.

Twipple is right. This doesn't mean he's nice, and he isn't exactly positive why he's right, but he IS right.

Though the reason he's right has been touched on, it hasn't fully been broken down, like I'm about to attempt to do.

THE REASON: The reason that enchanters have become so valuable these days is because, post-Kunark, mobs have double the hit points and double the attack/damage per second, as they used to have. These are approximations, of course, but I think they will serve.

EFFECT 1 of tougher mobs: Warriors and tanks in general are not as effective as they once were. For example, let us say that in the old days, when tanks could tank, a good tank could handle a single mob, solo, but just barely. Now, let's take a Kunark mob, as I described above. Since this mob has twice the HPs, a tank can only tank it until it's half health. But wait, it's hitting twice as hard, while the tank has the SAME number of HPs. That means the tank's dead while the mob is still 3/4 health. Which means, the tank's effectiveness has been reduced to 25% of what it was.

EFFECT 2 of tougher mobs: Wizards are not as effective as they once were. Whereas in the past a wizard with full mana could completely kill a mob, assuming he wasn't killed first, with that mana, now he can no longer do so. Since the mobs have twice the HPs, a wizard can only halfways kill the mob. And with the mob's higher attack, the wizard must be even more cautious about nuking. So, a wizard is only 50% as effective as he once was, and perhaps less so, when you take into account agro and taunt.

EFFECT 3 of tougher mobs: Clerics, while more necessary, are also less effective. Pre-Kunark, they could emerge from a single mob encounter with 4 bubbles of mana, having cast just a single C-heal, while the tanks did their job, and the wizard put the burn on it before it got out of hand. Now a cleric would have to use a lot more mana, since the mobs are doing more damage, and also able to TAKE more damage.

EFFECT 4 of tougher mobs: Enchanters are MORE effective!!! How? Well, in the past an enchanter mesmerizing a single mob was helpful, saving, let's say 25 points of damage per second, throughout the time the mezz was maintained. So, given a 1 minute kill time per mob, an enchanter would have saved the cleric having to heal an extra 1500 points of damage from the second mob.

But NOW, post-Kunark, the same mezz would prevent 50 POINTS OF DAMAGE for 2 MINUTES, because the mobs hit that much harder, and take that much longer to be dispatched. Yet, it's the same spell, for the same mana, suddenly made 4 times more effective.

The same is true of slow spells. Back in the good old days, slow was a handy spell. Slow cast on a mob from my above example would reduce the DPS to 12.5 from 25. Nice, but you really didn't save the cleric a ton of mana, probably not more than it COST you to cast.

However, post-Kunark, you'd be reducing the DPS from 50 to 25 for TWICE as long as before. The same spell is 4 times as effective, though it's the exact same spell for the exact same mana! Consider that it's reducing half from a significantly larger number than it was pre-Kunark.... You know, in the first example it reduced the dps by 12.5, but in the second it reduced it by 25. And it seems like a spell no group could do without.

Haste goes without saying. Haste just helps to breach the gap between mobs and PCs.

Clarity, however, I feel is more important than it was. While mana has always been super important, now the lack of it is more common, due to the toughness of the mobs and the higher mana requirements for killing said mobs. Supply and demand, peoples. The demand for mana is greater now, though the supply is the same. Hence, the enchanter is more important.

I am an enchanter, as I said, and I know that there is a million reasons why we are not "God-Like." But, as I hope I've proved in my text, the problem is that while everyone else's skills and abilities have less of an impact on the mobs, our skills and abilities have MORE of an impact. So much of an impact, that we are considered indespensable, and vital. Rather than being a "nice addition" we are the front corner of the diamond, the heart of the group.

We are enchanters. Who could have thought we would become so important, though we don't do any damage, nor do we take any.
