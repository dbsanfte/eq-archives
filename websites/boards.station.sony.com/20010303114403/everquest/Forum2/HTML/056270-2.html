I believe the only thing that really needs to be balanced about the mage class is their pets. Mage pets are only marginally (if at all) better than necromancer pets, yet mages rely much much more on their pets than necromancers do.

The reason mage pets aren't far and away better than necromancer pets is that they stop melee combat to cast. High level necromancer pets also get to cast (more rarely than mage pets) but their cast is a proc, they still get to do melee damage in the same round that they cast. With pets nearly 10 levels lower than the mage, the pets are almost always casting against creatures much more powerful in level, so they are resisted quite frequently -- making the rounds that pets stop to cast in large part simply lost time. Necromancer pets aren't faced with this problem. Add in the fact that necromancer pets have more hitpoints than 3 of the 4 mage pets (according to GZ) and hit for only one less point max than a mage pet of the same level, and necromancer pets arguably do more damage in a great many situations than mage pets do.

My suggestions to bring pet strength into balance:
1) Reduce the damage output of the necromancer pet.
2) Reduce the total number of hitpoints of the necromancer pet.
2) Give necromancer pets the ability to cast (a resistable lifetap for instance) and make that take up necro pet combat rounds.
3) Make mage pet casts into frequent procs, so that they do melee damage in the same round that they cast.

Please note that I'm not saying mage pets aren't powerful enough, I'm just saying that they aren't properly balanced in comparison to necromancer pets. If mages are the masters of pets, why are their pets not clearly more powerful than necromancer pets?
