I read as much as I could of the two threads and while I could reiterate much of what my brethren have written (I have written the same myself for nearly a year now), I would like to address something that I did not see in either thread.

Many of the items that were initially used by monks (prior to the AC insanity) were AGI items. Agility is claimed as helping to avoid being hit. This is the key to monks. As per the definition offered, monks were meant to deal damage and avoid being hit. Sadly, the AGI function of the formula seems to be non-existent or at least extremely non-impacting.

A key for a monks should be the option to build up AGI to 150-200 (just like casters who build INT/WIS to gain more mana), which would help monks avoid more blows and take less damage from those blows. AC should not be the primary factor in determining if a monk gets wailed on and how how badly. Nearly all of our skills deal specifically with two RL skills, agility and dexterity.

Agility is the ability to move and perform actions with minimal effort. Many of our skills like dodge, block, riposte, and safe fall tend to fall in this type of category.

Dexterity is the ability to manipulate and perform special tasks with our hands and feet. Our special attacks fall into this type of category.

One of the ways that monks could be balanced better is to make our defensive abilities more effective and allow us to escape taking damage as opposed to forcing us to absorb the damage. If a monk chooses to be an AGI monk, he or she should reap the benefit of being able to move swiftly and avoid blows.

In addition, FD should be made as a complete agro wipe that does not fail, at least not when you FD and the mob sees through it. Falling to the ground sucks, but does balance it out. FD could be further balanced, but making mobs leave right away (negating the FD pull aspect, although there are more techniques that just that these days) and possibly make FD impact XP gain (harsh, but if it keeps us alive in all situations, worth it).

Mend. What is the purpose of having mend cap at 200? At 101 is does not fail (DO NOT CHANGE THAT). Perhaps a bonus should be given like reducing recycle time as it gets higher. Maybe 5 minute recycle at 150, 4 minute recycle at 200. Or it could be level based (5 min at 50, 4 at 55, 3 at 60). Mend could even repair more damage at higher skill levels.

There are issues involving weight, damage output, and what not. Everyone here has done an excellent job addressing those, but I felt that these points above would also be worthy of looking into...
