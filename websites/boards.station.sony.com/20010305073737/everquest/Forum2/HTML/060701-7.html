follow up on experience loss as a penelty in PvP...

This would be damn hard to code, as it could be a serious tool of the grief players if done wrong. It would require at least two factors to have a chance of being anything less than a nightmare:

1. the ratio of levels of the victors vs the defeated, factored by % of damage caused/healed to the amount of damage done. Complicated to run the math, and I question where these values would be stored and just how they could be tracked over a period of time.

2. A limitation on just how much exp loss a character could face, based on a percentage decay timer. You get PKed, you start with a 25% reduction in exp loss, that decays 1% per x amount of time. So if at 10%, you get PKed again, and it goes to 35% and the decay starts again, etc.

These two factors combined with a decent Res Effect upon PvP death could work. Alot of work though.

I think a majority of people, if not all, have said that immortal/invulnerable healing is a major issue on TZ and VZ. The contention lies in how to fix this problem.

GvE, or "Good versus Evil" proponents want the server hard coded as two teams, a Light and a Dark team.

Single team proponents want it coded as 4 teams.

4 teamers seem to all want it to be guild based.

Yet, all of them seem to agree that invulnerable healing is at worst an exploit and at best something very undesirable.

Verant has never taken drastic steps in anything related to pvp, with the possible exception of cancelling charm/fear usage on players and removal of egg shaped pumice, so I dont see them doing anything major here. Maybe I'm wrong.

What I think is that a compromise should be reached that will please everyone.

The 3 team ("light alliance" or "good team") people want their alliances, mostly because of roleplaying reasons, and the fact they have played this way almost since the inception of Tallon Zek. They say hard coding the servers as true teams would alienate their playstyle. I agree, it would, but if it isn't done, invulnerable healing against 1 teamers still is possible and still will happen.

The 4 team people want the same thing. They have made an unholy alliance between all teams because of PVP, and ultimate PVE goals such as slaying uber dragons and creatures that have never been killed before (on TZ anyway). They see hard coding 4 teams as a threat. Rightly so, it would dismantle their guild.

The single team adherents (99% of Darks "orange tags", and other single team guilds as HR, LOTU, etc) want 4 teams hard coded. Why? They play that way already, and they like it. Is it in their self interest to have it hard coded? Yes, because then they cannot be invulnerable/immortal healed against.

The root of these team problems is the fact that anyone that is NOT on a single team has an advantage which a single teamer cannot defend against or do anything about, unless they abandon their single team style of play for a 3 or 4 team style. Most single teamers would rather quit playing then do that.

I want to see invulnerable healing go away. In my mind, the simplest solution is an Aggressor Team Flag. If you heal or buff ANYONE not on your own team, it makes you attackable by any team for a short period, of say 10 minutes. This means that the person doing the healing is now hittable by their own team-mates, thus eliminating all invulnerable healing issues. They can become fair game. Of course, faction issues are still a problem, but at least the offending person who immortal heals can pay for their treachery.

Instituting this Aggressor Team Flag also means that the PVE style of 3 and 4 teamers who have made alliances for various reasons, will not be affected in any manner.

The exploit will be over.

I was even told a few days ago by a guide that she had received 5 petitions about a person that day because he continually stunned others in front of mobs until they were dead and they never had a chance to fight to save their own lives. This is the kind of BS that Verant has to stop. Most people truly don't care about dieing at the hands of players. The most you lose is a wee bit of time and maybe a couple platinums. I enjoy PvP action. I'm not particularly good at it, but I do like it. But if it comes to being crapped on by people that are 8 levels above me (AKA you haven't a chance at any level) or not playing, I'd rather not play.

2. Absor wasn't around (at least publicly) when TZ went coin only and the huge outcry that without risk, there is no fun. This game in my mind is really not much fun any longer. I realize that this is the normal progression that after playing the same game for so two years that some things would be difficult. So the options for me are simple. I play when I have nothing else to do and there is nothing I want to see on TV. I'm impatiently awaiting the upcoming competition games to EQ. Now, the reason that I'm ready to quit EQ isn't because of EQ, it is because of Verant. I truly never expect to purchase another game that is put out by VI. I think from a customer service aspect, we get no where near our money's worth. The game is great and perhaps the greatest game ever written. Sadly, there is not enough control over those people who's sole purpose in EQ is to make other's lives miserable.

3. I know a lot of people want the two teams servers to go to 4 teams. We know that almost all of the dark quests do not involve having to go into any of the other teams cities, but a great deal of the lights quests do. So, it really isn't logical for anybody to go and ruin faction with a race that they very well might need later. This is another reason for GvE instead of 4 teams. Not to mention that VI never gave us a storyline that would indicate why Elves and Dwarves hate each other. And why the Dwarves and Gnomes sided, etc.

4. Class balance - basically there is none. I play a class that post 50 I know of few people that are great at PvP - Shaman. Its been stated many times that Poison resists are horrid and our DD is a very inefficient joke. I've been killed by one spell (well 2 if you count root) by Necros, Druids, and Wizards. I've seen a single Necro pet completely rip apart a group of people while the necro is floating around splurting everybody faster than we can nullify it off. The PvP aspect is really not great unless it ends up in a good battle of groups where a test of tactics actually comes into play. Personally, I think that all spells should probably be resisted less (other than lifetaps/WD/Splurt who should be resisted a bit more), but they should do less damage than they currently do. Notice that I didn't talk about pets. Shaman don't get pets, we get companions. And all you have to do is see my green con pet miss and miss and miss against any tank my level to see that in a PvP encounter, they only give the aspect of distraction to the fight.

5. Heck, I think that the following would be good for the PvP servers as a whole. Make PvP not function from levels 1-9. At level 10 you can PvP to your hearts content (that means nobody can hit you, and you can't hit anybody). This would address the stupid newbie preying that goes on now that is responsible for probably 50% of the low level petitions. I also think that unless VI does something to address the post 50 game, I'd rather not have the PvP aspect in it either. Just because I know the shaman life well, I'll list my "Normal" scenario. I see say an iksar monk. I run up to casting range and lead off with e-bolt (resisted unless he's way blue) and then try and stick Turgurs (usually resisted as well). Once he closes the gap, I'm dead. The shaman spells take SO long to cast that getting one cast is a virtual imposibility. Sad to say, but my best defense against a monk is to drink a damage shield potion and start healing myself while he kills himself punching me. It usually doesn't work though. Casters in general are easier to kill, but only if I take the offensive. I'll never stick e-bolt on a necro as they always have DMF. Do you know how hard it is to kill a level 50 necro while having to use cold damage spells that take over 5 seconds and a lot of mana and do about 300 hps of damage? All of this with a necro pet trying to beat and cast the life out of me. Lets just say that Shaman do not have the tools to compete in most 1-1 machups. I make a lot of people run, but almost never get a kill.

- sidenote - For all the people saying how easy it is to get rid of Splurt and WD, there is a certain way that you have to set your buffs, etc for that to be effective. It isn't easy to keep them that way and if you are a buffing class (Shaman, cleric, druid, and enchanter to some degree) you basically can be dead by the time you get to the 10th or 11th buff spot. Now, if Nullify took off damage spells before it took off buffs, then this would work. As it is, only those that are out on raiding parties typically have their buffs set just right.

6. If given the chance to move my characters from TZ to another server where I have friends playing, I would do it in a heart beat. I would even go over completely naked and have no money if that was the only option. I won't start over to move however, so that option is probably not realistic. My thoughts are that VI doesn't have the fortitude to do the things that would make the PvP servers even as good as they were pre-Kunark.

7. Lastly, define in print what the rules of engagement are. There has always been a long list of interpretation as to what was acceptable and what isn't. Is it ok to attack people that are engaged in combat? etc. There are a ton of ideas that can be used on this to make sure that there is little room for interpretation. Since most people truly do not know what is and isn't acceptable, make a /rules command that will bring up the general rules for the server.

Well, I've gone on long enough.
