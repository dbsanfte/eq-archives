Obviously, symbology is terribly personal to anyone, and various groups tend to interpret a symbol in many different ways. As a Master Linguist, I see this often in the Troll language: after all, your word for Hand, "Skorgg," translates roughly into: "That which bashes." Certainly colourful, but considering that in trollish there are 13 names for body parts, 17 different types of weapons, 13 names of cooking utensils, and two names for froglok corpses that also happen to translate into "That which bashes," it can make an Erudite scholar such as myself understand how your vision of a hand can be, er, fist-centric.

Mind you, this is not to knock your vision of the use of your fingers, but I also know that the more common slang in troll for your digits, "Skruggles," are among the 117 words and phrases that translate into "Those things that scratch my fanny." In fact, not to be rude, but it reminds me that if we used trollish Symbology for Hands in our Guild Symbology, we would have to be "The Itching Hand of Mana."
