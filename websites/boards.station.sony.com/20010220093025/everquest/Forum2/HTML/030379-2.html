Abashi stated, "Haste increases for unarmed monks, though hailed for roughly six months now 'suck'."

I was there when it happened, and man was I stoked.

This was before Kunark, though. Kunark changed a lot of rules. Monk fists, by Abashi's own admission, are now considered the default "worst case" at higher levels. The addition of monk 1HB weapons such as Fighting Batons allow pretty much anyone to twink a monk and have damage dealing ability superior to fist damage throughout their career.

When compared to new weapons such as the Tranquil Staff, monk fists are no longer the ideal weapon they once were. This seems to be the focus of the monk whines, not that the fist delay reduction (haste implies that it works similarly to item or spell haste, and is therefore misleading).

The main focus of monk discontent seems to be *not* that our fists are weak (which, unfortunately, they are when compared to top eschelon Kunark weapons), but that other classes can eliminate the need for monks by obtaining damage-dealing ability that is close enough that the monk class limitations (low AC, HP at 60th level) make monks undesirable in groups.

Abashi's response to that particular issue was something I was pretty happy to see. According to tests VI conducted, monks outdamaged warriors by about 1/4. Unfortunately, though, the test data contradicts similar testing done by players, which shows a much tighter margin -- roughly 1/10, in fact.

Anyway, Abashi and Absor, you guys have to deal with lots of negativity from customers about a bunch of different topics. The problem is, whenever the balance of the game is changed, someone is screwed out of some territory. When players feel that their place in the game is threatened, they whine. Something 'fixed' months ago may well become broken again (from a balance perspective) when new content is added. Please try not to get frustrated when your players tell you something "sucks," though. They might be right.
