I would assume that just from the sheer magnitude of posts, you missed some very well reasoned dialog, and a lot of important issues got lost in the mix. So rather than go back and touch on the little issues, I just want to point out what I think are the BIG ONES for the balance of the post-50 bard:

#1: THE PRESENCE OF A HIGH LEVEL ENCHANTER NEGATES THE NEED FOR A BARD. Our hastes are not as good and do not stack, and our Charm / Mez / Fear is inferior and unstackable. A single enchanter can keep up to 3 groups with full haste buffs and clarity, while mezzing and charming. The bard can only help his immediate group, and now is left with few ways to help them, other than resists and manasong. It is rare that one class so clearly negates the need for another, but the presence of an enchanter DOES have this effect in high-level encounters.

#2: BARD MELEE ABILITIES DO NOT INCREASE AT THE RATE OF OTHER CLASSES, POST 50. A bard will always be less effective than other melee classes, but the gap widens to the point that a melee bard is of minimal effect in post-50 encounters, and hence undesirable.

#3: BARD HASTE NEEDS TO STACK IN SUCH A WAY THAT HAVING A BARD IN A GROUP IS DESIRABLE. This means that if you put a bard in a full group, the capability of the group should increase more than putting in another melee type. In other words, a bards combination of melee + haste + songs for everyone in the party needs to be more effective than simply adding another melee type. Whether you require the bard to melee, or use instruments entirely, doesnt really matter, the math just needs to work out that the group is AT LEAST AS WELL OFF WITH A BARD.

(Note: You listed this one, but seemed to miss the reasoning: There is no need for a bard in the group if other haste is available, as haste can be supplied by someone outside the group, and having the bard in the group will lower overall group damage output, even if the bard uses his current (5%) haste stacking song.)

Note that these three issues are all interrelated. The goal is to make bards a desired and useful class in the post-50 environment, particularly in long duration (Velious-style) epic battles that last several minutes.

Having played bards for a very very long time, I'd like to add my suggestions.

Please increase the variety / level range of magic instruments.

Do not increase melee abilities. Too many bards think they are tanks with regen song. Bards are INT / Charisma casters who happen to wear plate. Anyhow, a smart bard who uses instruments can just about out-DoT any class in the game.

Allow bard haste songs to stack with haste spells. Not all melees have haste items, and the argument that stacking bard haste songs is pointless due to haste caps assumes that the melee who is hasted has some haste item.

Please do not allow scripting or macroing of bard songs. Doing this would only fuel the "melee bard" fire as bards would simply hit their "songs" macro key, and then go in and pretend they are a warrior. Juggling songs / instruments is one of the basic aspects of playing a bard. It would be like allowing clerics to have a "heal tank" macro that auto-heals low health tanks.

Please please please play test bards. I do not believe anyone play tests bards to any significant degree. This is evident in the recent "Selos fiasco" where any bard lvl 5 or higher would have caught that bug within 5 minutes of play time.
