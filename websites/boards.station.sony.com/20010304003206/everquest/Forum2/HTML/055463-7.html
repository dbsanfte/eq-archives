Absor, as you skipped my posts, I have truncated them into a helpful organized shortish list for your convenience. The list is not in any order of relevance, it's just a list of the issues I saw you did not bring up, and which I feel are important.

1) FORAGE
---------
Funny thing is, with a 200 skill in forage I forage more gold, flowers, pebbles, barc... well you name it, than food and water. I actually have to buy water these days (it's true). A 30 bard I met while waiting for the Catliff (sp?) in Mistmoore a few weeks ago said all he forages is food and water. Lower cap = better than a grand master 200 skill? Same goes for tracking. Make it possible to filter Forage as with Tracking, if we're supposed to be masters of these things. Let us chose what we are looking for when foraging.

2) BALANCE KNIGHT PARENT GIVEN CLASS DEFINING ABILITY
---------------------------------------------
I stress Rangers should get a similar before/during/after fight group/targetable friendly ability/spell compared to the the class defining abilities the SKs and Paladins who were given from their respective parent classes. Summon Corpse and Ressurect.

No this is not self teleport. No this is not self bind. And no this isn't SoW. It *must* be group friendly or at least usable on other players than self. This should work both outdoors and indoors like our brother/sister hyrbids' given parent abilities.

It's pretty easy to name the ability isn't it? Considering our parent class, considering the group friendly state, considering before/during/after fight and considering that Ressurect and Summon Corpse are, in fact, reasons for chosing a Paladin instead of a Cleric, reasons for chosing a Shadow Knight instead of a Necromancer. What ability in the Ranger parent class that is all this would be a reason for chosing a Ranger instead of a Druid? I know that you know what it is. So why leave Rangers out in the cold. Why make Rangers the only hybrid that does not inherit that ability out of the three?

3) GROUPABILITY IN DUNGEONS
---------------------------
I don't know about you other Rangers out there but I'm an outdoors type of Ranger. I don't like dungeons much. Yet, we are forced to go to dungeons to get most of our best items (and experience, not to forget). And still how group friendly do you consider a Ranger to be in a dungeon situation? None at all. We have pretty much no indoor usable skill or spell. I'd like this to be changed. Indoor group-friendly skill or spell. As others have pointed out, indoors we are reduced to tanks, and we are not tanks.

4) FLETCHING
------------
Could there be a way to make fletching a trade skill worth picking up other than for being able to make your own arrows and a bow? Verant made Alchemy one of the most sweet trading skills from nothing by adding all possibility to create potions with some of the most useful abilities/buffs/spells (Various heals, buffs, SoW, IVU, TP...) I have sold 2 bows and about 200 arrows during my 51 levels. I am not at 200 skill, but a few points from. Once, mind you, once, someone asked me if I could make a bow for him. How often do you see people looking for potions? Bows? Arrows? Could fletching be made a "trade" skill? YES. Let us (no others, us = Rangers) add enchantments to our bows and arrows. Arrows with a Stun proc (if archery is tuned) will sell. Arrows with a DOT will sell. Bows with various procs will sell.

Also note that Alchemy together with (!) right-clickable spell armor for non-casting classes (!) greatly reduced the need for pretty much any of the hybrids (and their buffs) (I'm not counting Druids, Necromancers or Clerics, although I'd think these classes should feel the most "betrayed" by this, though ultimately it hits the hybrids as well).

5) HALF-ELF RANGERS
-------------------
And why oh why are Half-Elves treated like the least worthwhile race in the whole of EQ. We are hit with a wis/mana penalty. Wood-Elves do not. They get additional bonuses (at some point I trust - can't wait to see what this will end up like) cultural fletching and forage at earlier level. For what reason? Half-Elves have better looks? :-) I don't get it.

6) RANGER UNIQUE ABILITY
------------------------
Yes, *unique* ability. Our brother/sister SKs and Paladins get it. Rangers don't. Together with point 2) it really feels awkward. Why are Rangers left out in the cold?

7) TRACKING QUESTION
--------------------
Is it/will it be possible to change the con colors in the Tracking list? As they are now, the blue is so deep/dark blue I can't read what it says, and I have a high-end graphics card / monitor. Can we change these colors in the, say, eqclient.ini file today? Will we be able to do so?

------------------------
The case for hit points:
------------------------

The original design of the hybrids had the Ranger trading Plate Armor (i.e. AC) away for Duel-Wield. Rangers had the same HP as other hybrids, and had higher Defensive skills (was in-character for being light and agile).

Then as they increased AC and HP on Warriors, it became clear that Knights didn't tank significantly better because their quest armor AC was too close to Ivy Etched AC. To compensate, they added more HP to Knights.

The trouble is, that only existed for one class of armor (and it was neglected that Knights got bronze and stuff like that while Rangers were still in banded). Planar Knight armor has significantly higher AC than Thorny Vine. What's more, in spite of Rangers being light and agile, Knights are being given much higher Defense.

What this means is that now Rangers are paying a triple-penalty for Dual-Wield: AC (as originally intended), Defense and HP.

Since Knight AC on Planar, Kunark and Velious armor is significantly higher than the equivalent Ranger armor, this triple-penalty is unjustified.

Further, Rangers are the ONLY melee class past 50 who receive a single HP bump. All others receive at least two.

Finally, this results in Ranger HP being closer to casters than their hybrid kin. Is it really intended that Rangers have caster hit points, and not HP closer to other hybrids?

-----------------------------
The case for raising Defense:
-----------------------------

See above for a description of the triple-penalty.

Why do we need defense raised? We get hit more by mob ripostes because we dual-wield faster weapons. This can result in significantly more hits per fight. Our spells are all high-agro, and so are our weapons. My defining weapon, the weapon Verant designed just for me as a Ranger, has a huge taunt on it.

Finally, it is in-character. Defense is damage-avoidance, exactly what a "light agile" melee class should have. Before the recent proposed changes in Defense for other hybrids and Clerics, high Defense was given to Rogues and Monks (and Warriors just because they're the master tankers). Before level 51, it's given to Rogues, Monks and Rangers. See a pattern?

Finally, after the proposed upgrades on test, we come up with this:

------- Off / Def
Ranger: 252 / 200 : 452
Knight: 230 / 250 : 480
Ranger balance: -28

There is no compelling feature in the Ranger list of abilities that justifies this skill gap.
