What moderndaylegend said.

Listen to your raid leader. Do what they say. Make sure you know what is expected of you, if you are not sure, ask. (As a cleric, are you responsible for your group's health? the main assist? the entire raid?? are you perhaps going to handle cleric buffs instead of heals?) It is ok to admit to not knowing.

Final bit of advice. Seems strange. Seems anti-EQ in a way. But raids are not for the obtainment of gear, money, or exp. OK, sure. There will be times when you may even get all three and usually someone will get at least one. But more often you will die. Die gracefully. Bind in a convenient place. Sometimes the raid leader will tell you where to bind. For Mistmoore it may be in PoK if they have an epic cleric coming along. It may be at the LFay zone in Gfay (so the non-binding classes can be bound there too). Or, as a cleric it might be right outside the entrance to Mistmoore.

When I was GL on FV, we had "family night in the warrens" on a regular basis. Started people raiding around 10 to get used to having multiple groups. Was also a good way for officers to assess the newer members. Were they good at sharing? Could they follow orders?

Oh, hey, be careful of those #(*%&(%(& brownie scouts and Equestrielle the Corrupted on your way in through Lfay. Apparently Equestrielle's agro range and pathing have been fixed somewhat but those brownie scouts can still kill you remarkably fast at 25.

Did anyone mention this?
There are usually certain people who forfil certain function on raids :

1. Puller - usually a paladin or a monk or shadowknight (for Feign Death.)
2. MT (main tank) - it is his job to engage the mob once he is arrives and to hold the aggro so it doesnt run around and squish the casters (often the puller and the MT are the same person)
3. Main healer - he heals the MT
4. Secondary or Patch healer - he uses smaller heals and keeps an eye on everyone else
5. Slower - Shaman or Beastlord usually. He casts slow on the mob to makr it easier to fight.
6. Main Looter - he grabs up all the loot to be divided up later.
7. CC or Crowd Control - usually an enchanter, who will mezz adds.
As first/second healer in groups/raids I have found out the following
1. Wait until the MT has aggro (he often makes a macro to announce this) before doing anything to the mob or it may decide to hit you instead!
2. Sit to preserve your mana. This is more true at lower levels when an enchanter may not be around. Beware, sitting draws aggro.
3. Healing also draws aggro sometimes
4. Watch the chanters! Heal the MT sure, but if a chanters mezz fails he will die VERY quickly. Watch his health bar, escapcially when adds are around.
5. dont expect loot or xp, but it is good to have a method of fairly distibuting the goodies. At your level we just used /ran 100

I can't beleive you all missed the two most important pieces of advice:

1.) Find out who the Main Tank (MT) for the raid and make a social hot key: /assist <MT_Name>. Use this key, and this key only, if you are going to cast any damage causing spells. If you are an enchanter or shaman doing crowd control or slowing this doesn't apply to you, but it certainly applies to all the melee and nukers. Exceptions to this rule exist, as in when warriors/knights are required to 'tank mez' mobs that can't be enchanter mez'd (Ssra Emporer encounter is a good example of this).

2.) Do not overaggro. This includes sitting on pull, overhealing the MT (all healers should be in healing chat channel and its up to you guys to work out a system for keeping the MT alive) and nukers nuking too early. This also applies to those melee types who have weapons that out aggro the MT's. If your MT isn't dualweilding a BoC and a SoD then you certainly shouldn't be because you'll never get aggro off you.

Also, expect things to go wrong. If it's a new zone or new people it'll take time to get things worked out.

Good luck, a well run raid is some of the most fun you'll have in EQ.
