This worn-down nub of a enhancement request is all that is left of a very important balancing issue. The real issue is that magician utility summons are almost valueless to Norrath and even most mages consider them of only limited value for their own personal use. Sure, there is only one 1hand blunt that is summonable, and that isn't until level 44. Oh, the 1hand blunt is moderately popular for distributing on big raids in case of emergency, like mass rezzing casualties. But again, that's high level. First, stats on weapons just are too anemic for the levels the summoning spell becomes available (with the possible exception of the 8 dmg and 26 delay on the 44th-level mod rod summons). Second, people don't want to rely on summoned food, drink, containers, weapons, etc. that could poof out of existence subject to the vagaries of network and server latency. It's kind of the ultimate extreme that summoning a bag full of food, drink, and bandages is considered a very high-level spell. You literally cannot GIVE AWAY this stuff 99% of the time even you try hard.
Some suggested changes to utility summons.
-Create a fairly complete complement of all types of 1hand and 2hand weapons at most spell ranks.
-Improve the dmg/delay stats on these weapons by about 20%. Probably include more procing weapons. Summoned arrows are particularly poor stats right now.
-See if it is feasible to make summoned weapons transform from droppable to No Drop once the summoner hands them to someone else. Perhaps by summoning a weapon with one charge of "Summoned Weapon X". Example, a summoned sword with one charge of "Sword of Runes" and the actual Sword of Runes is No Drop. Then summoned weapons could be significantly more effective without becoming overbalanced in game power.
-Make sure that more boring utility summons like food and drink become more attractive in groups. Not everything has to be No Rent. For instance, a summoned Horn of Plenty could have three charges of Cornucopia (summons a stack of food) and not be No Rent. Relying on it as a food source even when in fear of link death now becomes practical.
-More summoned items with charges or spells of Invis, See Invis, Fire Shield, etc.
-Introduce either summoned armor or summoned items with one to five charges of armor buffs. The Phantom Leather/Chain/Plate/Armor series would be well suited to this.
-Do NOT give other classes armor or other gear that lets them summon things that are more properly "defining abilities" of magicians. Even wield the dread nerf bat in some cases.
Conclusion. It is a very important issue to make utility summons more balanced by making them more desirable. It is a "defining ability" of the mage class even more than pets are, and it has been little more than useless for the last two years. Magicians are very unpopular when trying to find groups, and this single issue and/or pet enhancements and fixes can and should be implemented to reverse this situation.

A couple of things that Alan/Absor didn't post that I think are very important.

Level 12 balance problem. There are a couple of great big holes in the list of bolt/DD spells that magicians get at each spell rank, especially at 12th level, magicians are woefully inadequate compared to wizards' or druids' nuke spells.
At level 16, mages get a DD spell that does 91 damage, but prior to that they just have to limp all the way from 8th level up with a nuke that is good for only 41 to 47 points of damage. And that's a bolt spell, inherently less reliable because it depends on line of sight. Mages get no DD or bolt at 12, but wizards get a DD doing 78 to 83 dmg--almost twice the damage mages can muster.
At almost every spell rank, mage nukes do roughly 80% to 90% of the wizard nukes from the same spell rank. The lack of a bolt or DD mage spell at 12 seems to be a glaring oversight. Maybe the original idea was that a rain spell at 12 would do the job, but in practice it hasn't worked out that way. Mages do get a 12th-level rain spell, but even if the proposed fixes to rain spells are implemented, this falls well short of appropriately matching wizard and druid nukes. Just as importantly, it makes mages too underpowered to either solo or get invited into groups from roughly 11th level until they reach 16th.
Mages get both a DD and a bolt spell at 8, then nothing until 16. I would suggest deleting either the DD or the bolt spell at level 8, and adding it to level 12. A level 12 DD should do roughly 65 dmg, or a level 12 bolt should do roughly 79 to 85 dmg.
There is a similar gap at level 39, but not nearly as severe. Here is the comparison

34 Wizard: nukes for 306 to 320
34 Mage: bolts for 319 to 33 or DDs for 276 to 295

39 Wizard: nukes for 381 to 405
39 Mage: still relies on bolt spell for 333 dmg or Dd for 295

44 Wizard: nukes for 606 to 625
44 Mage: nukes for 580 to 600


PETS
Mages should probably get a pet spell at first level. Necros, the other pet class, get first level pets.
At levels 4 through 8, and maybe even 12, the levels of summoned pets are pretty competitive with the mobs hunted. Except that at the last level before gaining a new spell rank, the existing pets are noticeably anemic and lacking in usefulness. So, an 11th-level mage is still using a pet from an 8th-level spell, and those pets don't keep up very well. That last level before a new spell rank is tough all the way from 3 through 48, but worst in the younger seasons.
At level 34, mage pets suddenly start becoming dramatically more in line with the mage's own level of power. But ONLY if the mage is using the appropriate focus item, of which there are four. The quests for these focus items are kinda tough for a new level 34 mage to have completed, by the way.
With the new relative power of 34th- through 49th-level pets, mages have a more realistic shot at soloing than they did when they were younger. I am going to talk about soloing for a minute, but only because soloing power is a very good measuring stick of power in a group, in this regard. Younger mages who solo aren't being very smart, IMHO. Their pets just aren't good enough. At 34 through 49, it's feasible as long as you have the pet focus items, keep SoW going through purchased potions or whatever, and try to get Clarity whenever possible. Even then, soloing mages will still die pretty frequently. Even played by very smart and skilled players with all the jboots and Clarity in the world. Lacking snare/root/fear spells, a soloing mage is always leading a very risky life.
Earth pets should snare instead of root. Or at least change their root spell to include significant damage, or give them more hit points, or something.
Pets almost never take the hits from a mob and leave the group's tanks to take the entire beating. Even when the tanks don't taunt and the pets do. I think this was unintentional, and the game design is expecting pets to taunt more effectively than they do. Certainly, groups would be much more interested in inviting mages if the pets could be counted on soak off some of the damage from the tanks--thus saving healing mana and downtime. Pets in general are truly awful at taunting. It appears the biggest reason for this is that they always melee from the maximum distance they can be from a mob and still melee it. It's obvious that when mobs calculate who they most hate right now, distance is part of the calculation. If pets were to move to minimum melee distance for whichever weapon they are equipping, their taunt factor would be increased. Or, you could use brute force to improve pet taunt factors by increasing a number in a database.
Conclusions. Pet taunting needs to be fixed/enhanced. Pets prior to 34 aren't quite powerful enough. 34 to 49 pets are adequately powered, but only if the mage has quested for the necessary focus items. There are various issues with 50 to 60 pets that other posters cover with more knowledge than mine.

CALL OF THE HERO
This spell is inappropriately biased to the very highest levels. Levels that the vast majority of players will probably never achieve. It isn't a powerful a spell, and needn't be such rare reward. It really should be thought of as a "defining ability" of the magician class.
I suggest giving a much weaker version of this spell at a much lower level, and giving occasional upgrades every two to four spell ranks. Perhaps start with a 12th-level CotH that has a range of 150 feet and casting time and mana cost that make it feasible in combat. Used creatively and with skill, this could save the lives of tanks and also cut down on the number of runaway trains in many zones. At 25 or 29, an upgrade with a range of 500 feet or so. At 39, an upgrade with a range of anywhere in the same zone. At 49, any zone on the same continent. At 55 or 56, any zone in the world. Possibly at 59 or 60, even the planes. Only the most fearful designer would argue that this is an overpowering capability....but even their arguments should be satisfied when we remind people that CotH requires that the 'Hero' be grouped with the magician FIRST, before anything.

Enough? Trust me, I'm more tired of writing this than you are of reading it. :->

Still, we need a quick recap.
-Give mages an appropriate 12th-level nuke spell
-Changing graphics and other cosmetics should be a backburner priority.
-The class' defining abilities are utility summons and pets, and also CotH. Utility summons desperately need improvement in a variety of ways. Pets need some minor tuning. CotH should be expanded quite a bit. Other classes should not be getting utility summons attached to their gear.
-Check for No Rent upon log-in not upon log-out. Have a 60-minute grace period (not 30-minute). You could make it 120-minute and not unbalance anything but actually achieve humane treatment of the people behind the magician characters.
-Spell drops and research requirements are too harsh and should be eased.
-Area effect spells are resisted too much and should affect all mobs (but not pets) within their area, regardless of how many. Or perhaps much higher resist for the pets and much lower for the mobs.
-Area effect spells should not break mez.
-DoTs are not necessary.
-One or two spells should be added to the pet heal line.
-Mages should be able to SoW their own pets.
-Bard songs and group spells should affect pets.
-Pets' health status should be easily available to group members, and healers should be able to target pets as easily as they target the other group members.
-Many people have noted that pets should be able to be temporarily "muzzled" so that they aren't a threat for constantly breaking an enchanter's mezz. The /pet back off command is extremely inadequate for this purpose.
-Others have posted that uberhigh level magicians should be getting some kind of mana-regeneration enhancement.
-Mobs at higher levels and Velious resist spells too much. (I know, that's a different thread. :-)

This is two years' of accumulated thought and about two hours' of writing. Thanks for reading, but most important thanks for taking what you read to heart (please).

It's almost the opposite, and especially in all dungeons. Mages will never compete effectively a slot until pets are made considerably more welcome combined with enchanter mezzes, have pathing problems dealt with, and are made attractive otherwise to groups by some kind of buffing ability besides the fire shields that are too short duration, cost too much mana, and do insufficient damage.

Discussing the pre=50 game here. Until the higher levels, necros are almost always preferred because their pet is widely perceived to be actually more effective than a mage pet, plus they have the valuable snare capability, plus Fear for emergency situations. Druids actually have the same or slightly better nuking ability as mages throughout most of their careers, can acts as the group healer just as well as a cleric pre-30, have valuable strength and armor class buffs, have a damage shield 95% as powerful as a mage's but longer duration, valuable Snare (as well as Root), regeneration buffs, group evac for battle emergencies as well as making profits from others, and the single most important spell in the game is still SoW because it saves so many lives. Magicians cannot compete. Wizards and mages compete about on a par with each other, except that mage pets and their reputed unpredictability, especially in dungeons, put mages at a disadvantage. Until pets lose their reputations for unpredictability and uncontrollably breaking chanters' mezzes, and the 'class-defining abilities' of mages can compete on a par with druids, etc. "because of their pets, they are..." still going to be UNwelcome enough in any group.

Reduce the mana cost of fire shields to about 60% to 75% of current costs, and/or triple their duration. Make sure that mage fire shields do at least 20% greater damage than druid damage shields.

Give individuals in groups a selfish reason for wanting to invite a mage. An obvious buff that each group member can benefit from. I suggest summoned items with two charges of the Phantom Armor series of spells, and change Phantom Armor to stack with other buffs. The tiny AC buff is welcome, but the Regeneration component of Phantom Armor would be even more welcome. This would be a start to competing evenly with other classes for a group invite. Mages would still lack emergency-type spells, or any speed-restricting spells like Snare, but it would be a start.

Transform the current list of utility summons that mages get into something that has value to others, instead of their nearly-complete valuelessness they have now.

Make pets more reliable and less in conflict with enchanters. Someone's suggestion of /pet back off causing the pet to just not attack for 10 seconds would help, but isn't quite sufficient. Because the pet would still wait 10 seconds then attack the now-mezzed mob it was backed off from. Make it so that once a pet is backed off, that clears its hate list like a memory blur would. Or it will now treat a mezzed mob with the response of "That is not a legal target, master."

If pets get more than ten or twenty feet away from their owners, make them say "Where are you, master?" for everyone to hear.

Make pets report their health to the entire group, and group healers able to easily target pets for heals.
