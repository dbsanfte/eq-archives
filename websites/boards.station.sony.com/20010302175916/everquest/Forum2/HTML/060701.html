Absor,

Let me preface this response with a little information about who I am. My main character is Duhara, a level 60 barbarian shaman on Vallon Zek, who is one of the leaders of Defiant. I have played on Vallon since the day it was brought up. After reading through most of the posts on this thread I believe the following issues will best improve game play on Vallon without a lot of adverse effects.

Issue #1: Cross Teaming and Out of Range healing.
To solve this issue I would instigate a three-part solution:
A) Limit all beneficial spells to the 8 level range that is attached to offensive spells.
EFFECT: Drastically reduces the ability to OOR heal. Yes there is still the case where a level 48 could kill a level 40 with healing from an OOR healer, however, given normal game mechanics this would happen anyway. This solution would eliminate the ability of a level 10 character to wipe out a party of level 15 characters due to the presence of a level 50 druid chain healing and casting damage shields.

B) Apply the PvE method of attaching faction hits to the PvP environment.
APPLICATION: Once PvP combat is engaged (i.e. a PC is damaged by another PC) anyone casting beneficial spells on the combatants is added to the faction list. In addition all PCs who take part in offensive action should receive a faction hit not just the person who lands the killing blow.
Effect: This would in effect make immortal healers think about immortal healing if they realize they will be made KOS in their home towns. While not completely solving the problem at high levels (Most people don’t care about faction) it would reduce the problem.

C) Attach a “traitor flag” to combatants immortal healing
APPLICATION: A flag is activated when a PC Buffs or heals a member of a different team that has been damaged by PvP. When activated the flag will cause that PC to be vulnerable to attack by any race, and thus have their name color switched to some new “traitor” color. As this could be detrimental to faction any kill of a PC flagged as a “traitor” would result in faction hits associated with the last person of another team they healed.
EFFECT: Would wipe about the ability to immortal heal. In essence anyone who heals a member of another team would be fair game to all, and have the faction hits associated with the PC they were healing/buffing.

Issue #2: Lack of a real “Death Penalty” in pvp
PROBLEM: Being killed in pvp combat has no real consequences and is easily exploitable.
EXAMPLES: Tanks bound inside the zone they are PvPing in can endless bindrush with no real consequences. Casters bound close to the combat have no mana but with clarity II or a bard this only stops them for 3-4 minutes.
SOLUTION: Apply a 15-minute “death effect” to all PCs killed in PvP combat. This effect would be substantially worse then traditional resurrection penalties. In addition to standard resurrection penalties I would extend the time frame to 15 minutes, add a major combat speed defuff (Turgurs) and dramatically lower both intelligence and wisdom. In effect this spell would keep the combatant from doing anything in PvP for 15 minutes.

Issue #3: Super Resist items
PROBLEM: The current resist items available to most 50+ characters basically invalidate a vast majority of casting classes from having any offensive abilities in pvp against an opponent of the same level. Enchanters, shaman, and clerics have essentially NO WAY to damage a prepared opponent with magic. Each of these classes has no low resist damage spell, and no way to lower resists enough to make their spells hit. Yes shaman have maloxxxx series but it will never stick, and enchanters have tash; but most enchanters will tell you that even with tash none of their spells will stick. At lvl 60 shaman get malo, however, by level 60 most opponents can easily get their poison/disease resists over 130 and bane/pox/ebolt/plague will never stick on someone with 80+ poison/disease resists. Blue diamond jewelry has essentially made these classes healers and buffers for PvP combat. Druids, necros, mages, and wizards all have some spells that are either unresistable or have a very low resist rate.
SOLUTION: Give each of these classes a low resist damage dealing spell for high mana costs or lower the resist rates on the level 50+ spells.

Issue #4: Z-axis poofing.
PROBLEM: This is an Everquest issue in general, but it adversely effects PvP. Simply, when a player is too much higher or lower than you (frequently as little as about 6 feet), they can simply vanish. The server stops sending you information about them, even if you have them targeted, even after the recent improvements to update rate. (Thanks to Slamz for this)
SOLUTION: Fix the coding 


Issue #5: Guides showing up on ShowEQ.
PROBLEM: Not technically a PvP issue, but it's a griefer issue, and griefers like PvP servers. Simply, a guide entering a zone invisibly to scout for exploiters will show up on ShowEQ. The grief players know when to stop causing trains, etc, because of this. Fix this, catch the griefers, everyone is happy. (Thanks to Slamz for this)
SOLUTION: When guides zone into a zone invisible do not transmit guide location to the client.


Issue #6 Con system
SOLUTION: Make anyone in range white those too high should be red those too low should be green.

Issue #7 Pumice
THOUGHTS: Pumice should remain out of the game or make it self only with a 1-2 second cast time and only 1 charge per stone. Non stackable. For any of the buffing classes pumice basically made our buffs worthless. In the current form melees have to stand still for a short period of time to cast a spell, just like casters have to stand still to cast a spell. Egg shaped pumice is completely unbalancing.

Issue #8: Unresistable Spells
THOUGHTS: There seems to be a lot of people complaining about unresistable dots (Splurt, WD) ect. Each of these spells can be dispelled if you are aware of them, so I see no problem with them. IF you learn how to keep the top two buff spots open when you pvp splurt and winged death are just mana sinks for their casters.

Alan, Absor, et al:

Please do not do anything to compromise the ability of the different light teams to buff/heal each other. The complaints about same-team healers come mostly from a small (but loud) minority who had no complaints about invulnerable healers when they themselves used out-of-range healers, a far more unbalancing exploit.

On Tallon Zek, the whole xteam healing furor only arose when the dark team, previously completely dominant, began to lose that dominance to a united light alliance. Many "dark" character players began an attempt to destroy the light unity by rolling light team twinks who created ligh-on-light PK guilds. When the light alliance proved impervious to these tactics, they began to agitate for code changes to cripple the ability of multi-race light guilds to fight together in PvP. When my guild offered a guildwar to these 4-team guilds, which would remove the invul healer exploit, we were invariably turned down. In other words, it wasn't exploiting that bothered these guilds, it was light teams allied together.

It is hardly a coincidence (which Verant can probably verify) that the 4-team guilds on TZ are primarily founded by (and disproportionately made up of) 2nd characters of dark team players.

Certainly some people do enjoy 4-team PvP for its own sake. But they are still a minority, and the server should not be hijacked by their demands because they are willing to complain more than the majority.

4-team makes no sense from a role-playing point of view, and flies in the face of the background story and atmosphere Verant has created, in which the light races tend mostly to be allies. Many light team quests require good faction with other light team NPCs, which would severely weaken them should 4-team PvP ruin that faction. The distribution of abilities and levelling areas is grossly unequal - the elf-team would be virtually unplayable in 4-team PvP, and elven characters would very likely all but vanish from the server.

If you need to fix invul healing, start with OOR healing and buffing, a far more serious problem. As far as xteam healers are concerned, either code the server as good-vs-evil, or change the code to remove the invulnerability, not the healing. In other words, if a member of team A heals a player from team B, the healer becomes vulnerable to other team A members for some short peiod of time after the heal (say 60 secs).

An alternative would be to base the PvP on guild rather than teams, where each guild can choose to ally itself with one of 2 super-factions.

But please don't destroy the fabric out of which the existing TZ commmunities and friendships are woven.

Thanks,
Astrael
