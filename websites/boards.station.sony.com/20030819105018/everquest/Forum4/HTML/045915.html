Hmm... was that you or someone else who just asked this the other day. No matter. Is asked often enough.

What level of profit are we talking about? Enough to buy your spells each level? Enough for a horse? The high level horse?

Do you want to want to sell to vendors? Or to players in the Bazaar?

At 16 probably the best overall place for loot/exp is the warrens.

For loot to sell to vendors, Butcherblock (although the zone apparently has been having some problems) is a good place. Pristine skunk claws. Wandering Greenbloods. Look for Oak Bark on the ground but sell that to Players, not vendors.

The bazaar standbys are pelts (LQ, MQ, HQ Cat, Wolf, Bear). Silk swatches (people will pay more for already combined silk and it is a no fail combine). Silk thread/padding.

Find a niche and fill it. For a long time I was making amazing money supplying eggs for baking on my server. Then suddenly the market just sort of dried up on me. I do have several stacks available and if no one else is selling I will put them out. Sometimes they sell well, other times they don't.

If this is your first life, don't be so obsessed with making money. You won't have the knowledge or the ability to do so, even with our help here.

I make more money vendor mining than I do hunting. That is for two reasons. (1) I don't play much anymore. I can come in, vendor mine and chat with friends, and leave in an hour. (2) I know what sells well on my server. I look for specific items like crushed topaz which if I find on a vendor for around 26 pp I can sell in the bazaar for about 800 pp (used for Kael/SS armor quests, price used to be higher but has been falling lately). I look for things for high end tradeskillers like iksar blood or akheeva blood. I found a stack and a half of hobgoblin meat the other day which will turn a sweet sweet SWEET profit if my game hubby can manage to make any hobgoblin surprise out of it.

But that is with 2+ years knowledge of the game. And truth be told, I spend money as fast as I get it. When a spell can cost 1-2-20-60 k and a horse costs 10 - 60 k, and I am trying to raise my smithing with blue diamonds, usually 10 combines at a pop (2660 vendor cost for 10). The game hubby sits on a large bank balance. Mine averages about 2-5k on my main, with another 2 k or so on my mining alts.

If you are near them anyway you might try doing some bandit/raider camps in the karanas. They drop bandit sashes which you can turn into Chesgard at the paladin guild in qeynos, getting you bronze weapon types that sell there for a couple plat each. The bandits/raiders themselves also drop occasional bronze.

In east karana you'd actually find some lions and spiders which gave you XP. The spider silk can be sold in the bazaar for around 4 plat and the kitties might drop low/med/high quality cat skins which you can also sell.

You can also "farm" qeynos area vendors for any of this stuff. I've never had good luck finding much on karana area vendors, no one hardly does stuff in those zones (or if they do, they are there for the items so are not selling them to vendors) The city of qeynos itself often has npcs with tons o' bone chips. They sell for around 1.4 silver each and you can sell them in the bazaar for 3 gold or more each.

The few points in tailoring is fairly inconsequential as far as making money. What I found is I sell more skins and spiderling silk, likely for more money, than if I bother to laboriously turn it into leather padding. Tradeskillers wanting padding can buy from you and do that themselves, while other people might want spiderling silk or a skin for something else.

Most skins I sell at 3 or 4 plat, with high quality cat around 14 plat (quivers) and hq bear around 20 plat (backpacks) If I put a vendor up in the bazaar before I got to sleep, in the morning I get on the computer and it was all sold.
Most of that stuff will sell in under an hour.

One little trick to selling this kind of stuff is to have ALOT of it. Don't even bother with silks until you have at least 3 stacks. The more the better. Tradeskillers generally need quantity and are more concerned with being able to buy a few stacks then getting the best price. Furthermore if you have many more stacks than anyone else in bazaar, crank up your price a little. Don't have the most and sell it cheapest. Since you have the quantity, yours is more valuable. Sell it slightly higher price. It'll sell. (Slightly higher, not ALOT higher.)

Go to GFay or Crushbone and kill Orcs.
- Take the CB belts. They can be turned in for faction, experience (not as much as it use to), money, and armor in Kaledum. You can sale the armor to a merchant for money. You can get around 4 to 6 gold a belt this way. The experience and faction are not bad to have. You can sale the belts to other players but the demand has gone down now that the experience has been reduced.
- Take the CB shoulder pads. Turn them in two at a time and get money, faction, and a hammer. Again you can sale the hammer to a merchant for a few more gold.
- Take the scalps. 90% of the time these are left on the Orcs because few people know you can turn them in to a guard in High Hold Pass. You will get money and faction.
- Take any weapon that drops. They are usually a few gold each but will require a few trips to a merchant.
Now none of this is huge piles of money but it adds up quickly.

Another low-level area I like to hunt at that level is EverFrost. Find the path the mobs take in a big circle through the pass and pick a direction. Run in that direction killing stuff. Con the skeletons at a long range. If you see a red one then run the other way.
-Kill Spiderlings and take the spiderling silks. Sale them in the bazaar at your server’s average price, or use them to make leather padding.
-Kill the wolves and take the hides and meat. The meat will sale in the bazaar. The hides/pelts can be tailored into different items. The LQ wolf pelts will make great leather padding.
-Kill the bears and take the hides and meat. Same as above but the meat does not sale as well because it can be purchased in merchants. Polar bear skins can be turned in to a merchant in halas for faction and armor.
-Kill the skeletons for weapons and bone chips.
-Kill the Gnolls for money and items. Loot the NODROP tooth for faction and experience in Halas warrior’s guild.
-Kill the Ice Gobblins for cash and loot. Once you faction is high enough in Halas you can turn in the ice necklace for faction.

If you take the path all the way around you should be getting encumbered after a circle or two. Stop by Halas or POK and dump off your loot and convert the copper into higher coins. This will add up quickly.

As always when hunting in an area that in not really giving you experience you should step aside when someone that can gain experience in that area shows up. I usually just move further from the guards or the zone line and that usually puts me out of their way.

The two above places are just examples, I am sure there are plenty of newbie areas that have the same sorts of setups.
