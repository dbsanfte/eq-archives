Ok, after going over the posts, and playing a wizard up to 50th myself, here are my 2 cents worth.

We have two defining characteristics as a class:
1. Taxi Service
2. Magical Direct Damage

Taxi Service:
Ok, I love this as a defining attribute for our class. Being a "walker of the hidden ways" has a lot of appeal. Sadly, Druids do it as well or better. I have no problem sharing the glory, but if that is fully half of our appeal, at least let us do it right. I saw the new translocation spell as a serious step in the right direction. Now all we need are two additional capabilities:
1. Allow us a spell to port the entire party to OUR bind point.
2. Give us a secondary bind point.
This would allow us the capability to scout out an area, then bring in the rest of the team. By no means a safe operation, but definitely in keeping with the lore of the wizard class as finders of the hidden ways. Also, the second bind point vastly increases our capabilities to shuffle people from one hard to reach area to another.

Miscellaneous Taxi notes:
The Cazic gate is ok where it is. You port, you die, you learn to wait until you are 30th to port there again. Skyfire is similar, but I would still move it or reroute traffic. The WK port location is just silly. Yes, I have heard the "Evil PC needs a safe port" argument, but that is nonsense give the Cazic gate location. Move the WK portal to Qeynos Hills and make it useful. 'Nuff said.


Direct Damage:

I agree that pre-Velious we were fairly ok as far as damage was concerned. Sure, I would burn out my mana and die on a regular basis, but hey, that is the game, right? Now with the uber mobs it is obvious we just can't contribute diddly anymore. There are two ways to repair this:
1. Increase our firepower
2. Make our existing spells more capable

I really like the suggestions I have heard for #1, to provide a percentage based damage spell. While this could be tuned to minimize the impact of existing old world zones, it would at least give us a competitive shot at the larger critters in the new world. I also don't see a chance in Tartarus of VI implementing it.

The other option is more subtle. The key to ALL of our DD capability is not the spell itself, it is the fact that we cast the spell, spend our mana, and we are out of the game. Having more mana, or a way to effectively get it back in a long battle, would go a long way to making us a more reasonable class.

My overall suggestion is to institute a line of familiars for the wizard. These could act as the focus items of other classes. A good start is the Eye of Zomm, a familiar that grants distance vision. As levels increase, familiars could have additional durations and capabilities, including graduated additions to HP, total mana, mana regeneration, and increased capabilities of damage categories. Not a pet, per se, that attacks or defends the caster, but a summoned being that grants additional capabilities until it is killed.

Ideas for the familiar's capability include:
Level 4: O'Kiels - autocasts O'Kiels radiation on you on a timer
Level 8: Eye of Zomm - as is
Level ?: Adds minor HP/HP regen
Level ?: Adds minor Mana/Mana regen
Level ?: Adds % fire damage
Level ?: Adds % lightning damage
Level ?: Adds % cold damage
Level ?: Adds to evocation specialization
Level 34: Harvest - autocasts Harvest effect for you (without stun) on a timer
Level ?: Adds % chance for critical damage on cast
Level ?: Adds major Mana/Mana regen
Offsets to these capabilities could include reducing our resistance to elements (trading damage for vulnerability)

Miscellaneous DD notes:
Make Harvest a skill, not a spell slot. Having 1/8th of our total capability taken up at ALL times is a total waste.
Give us more spell slots. As casters, and only casters, give us the capability to pick the right spell for the right situation. Giving us the capability to make the right, if subtle, choice of the right spell in combat is only reasonable.

Miscellaneous:
Our AoEs are, in my experience, totally useless. Since some seem to like them however, simply leave them alone and fix the more core issues.
I don't want our class to buff others, it is not our style.
I don't want pets.
Remove the Melee Range to Hit rule. This is just senseless.
Definitely show damage to group.

It seems to me that most problems with wizards fall into two categories; one is play balance, and the second is the "fun factor". Let's talk about play balance first.

I have a 53 necro, a 52 wizard, a 46 cleric, and a 42 warrior, so I have broad play experience. Wizards are seriously inefficient in two ways, that are making them less effective than they should be.

First, wizards are totally dependent on mana for doing damage, and dealing damage is their primary reason for existence. However, they are by far and away the most inefficient class in the game. It isn't even close. Let me give you an example.

My necro hunts seafuries in OOT a lot. With pet, bond of death, lich, and pact, I can kill about 20 seafuries an hour, and stay full mana mostly. A seafury has 5K hp, so I can do about 100,000 damage an hour, over a long period of time. And the pet is never resisted, and bond of death is never resisted, so all the mana I spend is well used. With my wizard, it takes 5 ice comets to put down a seafury, assuming no resists (a big assumption). It will take me about 30-60 seconds to kill the seafury, depending on how many times I need to root it. Then I am OOM, and must med for 10 minutes to get back my mana. So, I can kill 4, or maybe 5 seafuries solo in an hour. This clearly shows how unequal the classes are. Now, I don't have a problem with wizards being less efficient than other classes, that is part of the price you pay for SPEED. But a 400% difference just totally sucks. I could see a 200% difference being barely acceptable.

And yes, necros rock. But so do mages, with those huge damage shields. And enchanters get clarity, and they can charm one seafury and make it beat up the other. All of these tactics are FAR more efficient than wizards.

Second example: the high level druid AE spell does more than twice as much damage to a single target as it costs in mana. Multiply that by 4 targets, and they are close to 12:1 mana efficient. I can't think of a single wizard AE spell that does more damage than it costs in mana. And without a truly effective snare, we can't cast them fast enough to get them off against multiple targets, so they can't be used for kiting the way druids can. We've all heard the complaints about resists to Atol's, but few people make mention of how easily the spell is broken. Seems to me it breaks like root, that is, any time you damage the mob it has a chance to break free, which is not true of snare.

Of course, shamans get pets, and huge dots, which are far more mana efficient than a wizard's best DD spell. I have also seen SKs soloing seafuries, using fear, although this is a bit more risky.

So, currently, while other group members in a fight can continue doing damage, and are effective over long periods of time, wizards are mostly a one shot deal, and limited both in their effectiveness in groups, and in their levelling ability, since other classes can outdamage them over time.

Now, pre-Kunark, wizards were able to at least do damage faster than anyone else in the group, and this did have some usefulness. If there was a bad pull and no enchanter for crowd control, a wizard could sometimes burn down one or 2 mobs fast, and let the rest of the group handle what was left. They were also useful in killing casting mobs quickly. But Kunark and Velious have changed this. The major new weapons have drastically increased the firepower of melee classes. This has not decreased the usefulness of enchanters, but all of the other int based classes are suffering in comparison, because we have not seen comparable gains in what we can do, and this is more true of the wizard than any other class. High level pets have gone up in power, damage shields have gone up, necros are mroe efficient. Wizards have gained a little bit on our top end damage, but almost nothing on our mana efficiency because the bigger spells cost more to cast, and nothing really on speed either, because the bigger spells are slower casting. All we really have is a bit more total mana as we gain in levels, and so a bit mroe total damage that we can do before our mana is exhausted and we are rendered comabt ineffective.

When you combine the efficiency problems with the huge inrease in hit points on mobs in Velious, wizards are next to useless there. They aren't even very useful for transportation, because of the poor locations of the wiz ports there.

Now, why is the wizard epic item a WEAPON? What are we going to do, supplement our direct damage with melee? I suppose it gives us something to do once we are OOM. Seems to me a spell focus or mana regen item would be better, like a ring. Where are our rings of power, anyway?

To fix this problem, you should do 3 things.

One, increase the damage our spells do, by 10% across the board.
Increase the damage of the 51+ nukes, by 10% per spell circle.
Reduce the mana cost by 20% across the board.
Increase wizard mana regen, as we are the only class that is soley dependent on mana. Our mana regen rate should increase with level.
Expand the draught line of spells. Add some lower level spells, that cast quickly, but cost less mana and do less damage, to give us more versatility in our choice of nukes. For example, I'd love to see a level 39 spell that does 250 dam for 125 mana, and casts in 2.0 seconds. This would not affect the total amount of damage we can do with our mana, nor would it really affect the speed with which we can do that damage. It would let us get our spells off more easily when we are being hit, or giving us a better shot at cutting down that runner before it gets around the corner and we get that "target out of sight" message.
Add a new line of spells, that cast slower, but are HIGHLY mana efficient. Basically, we trade speed for efficiency. This would let us remain effective in a group for a longer period of time, for long fights. For example, the level 51 draught does around 650 dam for 215 mana, cast in 3.0, about. The more efficient spell would do 600, cost 50 mana, but take 9 seconds to cast, with a slower spell regen as well. Wizards are the masters of direct damage, so give us some serious versatility in what we can do.
Get rid of the useless point blank spells, and add more DDs of different types of damage to each spell circle. We should always have a spell of the proper type within our current spell circle, or at worst the previous spell circle.

Second, our transportation is lacking. The locations of most of the wiz ports are silly. What is the purpose of putting a wiz port out in the middle of commons, or north karana, or west karana? My nearest port to my home city is 2 zones away.

Verant talks a lot about "game immersion". Well, silliness like this really disrupts that ole immersive experience. Levitate, binds, see invis and invis, ports to hate and fear.

That is the kind of spell a wizard would research and create, one that is useful. We are supposed to be in part the travelling class. A spell that will jump you closer to where you want to go is the kind of spell a wizard would create.

Finally, get rid of silliness. No wizard wants a pet, not really. And damage shields are not really our cup of tea either. Although, if we are going to continue to have damage shields, then as masters of damage, give us GOOD damage shields.
Fix manaskin. If I am currently targing a low level mob or player, it is making a level check against the target, even though the spell is self only. I am getting a message that I cannot cast the spell on such a low level target.

Make harvest a skill, not a spell. Increase it's effectiveness with level.
High level casters should gain spell slots. We are mroe experienced, we should be able to remember more spells. For instance, add a 9th spell slot at level 29, and a 10th at 49. I think ALL casting classes would applaud a move like this. It will not have a major impact on game play, it would just be nice to reduce the amount casters have to keep sitting and switching spells all the time. This definitely falls under teh topic of "fun". Tedium is not fun, and the game is overly tedious, in many ways.

I'd really like the game to be that much fun.

Read your post and thought about it for a while. Most of the things there are fine points to bring up. However, I don't seem to see anyone at Verant defining what a wizard is supposed to be. I gave a very plausible definition: magical monk.

Wizards are supposed to dish out more damage than any other casting class. Given a specific target, a monk/cleric should be able to dish out more damage than a warrior/cleric. The same should be true for wizards. Given a specific target, a wizard should be able to dish out more damage than a necromancer, or a magician, or a druid. Period.

We don't get (and I don't want) a whole line of "group-enchancing spells". We need to do efficient damage and we need to do it fast. That is the balance to the class.

Since you don't like the idea of a increasing the mana pool the only option left is to increase the efficiency of the spells. (Increasing the time to cast the spells should *not* be a viable option.) I am fine with going out of mana in a big fight and then meditating to help out later. I am not fine with having another casting class end up dishing out more damage in the end.

It would be a mistake for you to point to the "spells vs. spells" damage and say, "hey, you get lures and stuff and can obviously dish out more damage in a big fight". That is not the whole of it. I trade doing purely magical damage for not helping the group do a greater amount of damage as a whole.

That is what I do not see being addressed.
