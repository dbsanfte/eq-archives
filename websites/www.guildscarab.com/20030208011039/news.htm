Monday, Jan 6, 2002
  Star Wars Galaxies… ~ Rose


Well since the latest talk is all about the upcoming Star Wars Galaxies, I thought I'd share some general info.

For those not in the know, SWG is another MMORG, created by Sony Online with LucasArts. Set in time during the Galactic Civil War (after the Deathstar destruction). You will be able to play a range of classes, with species such as: Bothan, Human, Trandoshan, Twi-lek, Zabrak, Mon Calamari, Rodian, and your favorite I'm sure, Wookie.

Just some questions asked on the SWG FAQ's page. There are a ton here, make sure to check it out!

Are there zones like in EQ?

Not exactly. The "zone model" doesn't really apply to Star Wars Galaxies. We will have contained areas designed for exploration, combat, player interaction, and travel, but the transitions between these areas will be seamless and automatic. Players will feel like they are adventuring across large sections of a planet, rather than hopping from zone to zone.

Will my character be based on a pre-defined archetype or "class?"

No. We want to give players the freedom to create characters with a wide range of skills and abilities. Characters will be defined primarily by the skills they possess and the types of activities they pursue, and not by "classes." We're hoping this results in thousands of unique characters with their own individual strengths.

Will there be name constraints or professional titles?

We will enforce name constraints to help promote a Star Wars atmosphere. You will not see LuukSkywalker777 running around. We do intend on allowing players to attain professional titles based on the skills they choose to focus on.

Can I play a bounty hunter like Bossk or Boba Fett?

Yes, you can. There is a bounty hunter profession in the game, which includes abilities such as tracking your targets. Bounty hunters are not able to take down any target they wish; they are given a list of possible targets and they sign up for a particular one at the bounty hunter's guild. The more experienced you are as a bounty hunter, the more difficult (and lucrative!) the targets offered to you will be. Targets may include computer-controlled NPCs or players who have incurred bounties because of breach of contract or criminal actions

Will I be able to play a Jedi?

Yes. Although the details are still being worked out, we are staying true to the spirit of the films. Therefore, the journey to become a Jedi will be difficult, but those players who choose this path with find it very rewarding.

Can I become Dark Jedi?

Yes. We're creating the Jedi system to take into account Dark Jedi. However, be warned that the life of a Dark Jedi will be very perilous...

And of course… Can I be Luke Skywalker , Han Solo or Princess Leia?

No. To preserve the continuity of the fiction, you will not be able to play the key figures in the Star Wars universe. However, you may cross paths with a few of them…

Hope that helps with any Star Wars Galaxies questions you may have!

Welcome to another edition of Inquiring Minds Want to Know. This interview was with Tolmar, who currently plays with the Dark Ages Scarabs.

Q. How did you come to be in Scarab?
A. I played EQ on SolRo and my soon to be cousin-in-law Sayuri had just joined Scarab. He tried getting me on some raids but I always got left behind. :( I had to wait till he was an elder member to invite me in, but that wasn't till DAoC came out. Sayuri was the one that talked me into playing, and I'm glad he did. Scarab is the only kind of guild I would want to be a part of, and DAoC has the RvR elements that I've grown so addicted to that I could never play a purely PvE game again.

Q. Have you ever been to a ScarabFest? If so, what was the most enjoyable thing you did?
A. Nope. Would love to, but my wife just doesn't understand the idea of gamers getting together to have fun. Doesn't surprise me as she can't understand how I could like computer games so much in the first place. ;)

Q. What online game(s) do you play?
A. My first taste of online gaming was Halflife. The game was great I thought, but the one flaw was that even with cable modem lag was bad enough to make the game frustrating. Little did I know how good I had it once I saw my first relic raid. ;) Anyway, I played EQ for a couple years and was talked into joining DAoC.

Q. In the game(s), what name and type of character do people know you best by?
A. Most people know me as Tolmar. I played hard to get up to 50 in the hopes of going out with the guild and tearing up the RvR action. Little did I know that the Thane was not really the character I envisioned. The hard part was that in PvE the Thane rocked. In RvR though, I find it sad that I have a tough time soloing an enemy tank (and most other classes for that matter =D). I couldn't bear the thought of leveling another tank class to 50 so set my sights on a runemaster that I had floating around level 20 for the longest time. Janderon is now going to be my main for the immediate future.

Q. Why did you choose the class that you did?
A. I chose the Thane originally because I've always like tank classes, but the warrior I played in EQ was too one dimensional for me. I like a bit of magic thrown in. The Thane sounded perfect. Slice things with my axe and cast lightning bolts. Life doesn't get any better than that. ;) In EQ my main was a druid and I wanted a class that could melee this time around.

Q. What is your favorite aspect of the game you play most?
A. The adrenaline rush you get when you come across folks from an enemy realm. Especially when it's a surprise.

Q. What is your least favorite?
A. The fact that people that play 24/7 have a big advantage over you. Not a big deal in PvE type games, but when you have enemies out there that RvR non-stop it's hard to compete with that. Would be nice to have a level playing field at some point. That's why I like the Battleground zones. Everyone is pretty much at the same level and realm ability rating. Makes for much even game play and it then just comes down to skill not time played. Brings me back to those old Half Life days!

Q. What game do you see yourself playing next and why?
A. I will probably play Star Wars Galaxies next. I really prefer the fantasy D&D type games better, but I can see myself getting addicted to the breadth of possibilities in that game. Bounty hunter just sounds plain all out fun.

Q. If you could be any character in real life from the game(s) that you've played, what would it be?
A. I would have to go back to my D&D days to remember that I mostly played fighter types. My favorite character from D&D in fact was named Tolmar, and he was a big 7 foot tall warrior from the frozen northlands of Vlekzed. That would be me in real life. A big imposing figure wielding a huge sword. =)

Q. What is your favorite munchie while at the computer?
A. I don't really have one. I don't tend to munch while playing. What I do consume is beer. Guiness is the elixir of life. ;)

Q. Outside of computers and being with Scarabs, what is your next favorite hobby?
A. I have been a volleyball addict for many years. Before getting married I would play 5-6 times a week. That dropped to once a week afterwards. ;) Unfortunately I just blew out my ACL and MCL playing volleyball. Looks like that once per week is not looking so good anymore.

Q. What do you do in real life as a job and what do you WISH you did in real life as a job?
A. I work in software at Fidelity Investments. Currently I am a project manager still feeling the withdrawal symptoms of not writing my own code anymore. If I could be anything I would love to be in the computer games industry, but I know full well that most don't make enough money to stay afloat, and those that do have to work way too hard. Maybe I'd just like to be independently wealthy so I wouldn't have to work at all. I'm one of those people that could be happy not working!

Q. What is your favorite/best achievement in and out of the game(s)?
A. In game, it would have to be reaching 1100 in armorcrafting. I started out thinking it would be good to work on it. When I saw how long it would take I never thought I could make it. Somehow I put my nose to the grindstone and pushed it out. Out of game, it would be when I was at the height of my volleyball skills I had level A rated players impressed with my game and telling me I was far underrated. I got many invitations to play on tournament teams because of my lower rating. Was a blast to play in those tournaments.

Hope you enjoyed the latest interview! If you would like to be interviewed, please email me.

Rose

DAoC - Since so many test patches have been released, and not everyone always keeps up to date on them. I thought I’d mention a few of the best ones soon to be out. I’ve cut some interesting pieces out of the updates for you:

Sprinting: Sprint can now be bound to a key in the keyboard configuration. Very handy as this will free up an extra slot for a spell or style! Now... where to find a spare key!

Dueling System: By popular request, there is now a way to fight characters from your own realm - a dueling system, where both parties agree to fight.

To start the duel, select the character you want to fight and type, "/duel challenge". The character responds with "/duel accept" to accept the challenge and fight you. They can also type "/duel decline" to reject the challenge. Once the fight starts, either combatant can type "/duel surrender" to surrender the fight.

All duels are to the death. No constitution or experience is lost on a dueling death, but you still must be resurrected by a friendly healer/cleric/druid, or release. A message is printed to all nearby on victory or surrender notifying bystanders of the outcome of the duel.

Oh goodie, now when you are having a tiff with your significant other you can beat each other to a pulp, venting the frustration harmlessly and with no police!

Monster/Name Color Coding: There is now a way to display the names over all in-game models (player, monster, and enemy player). There is a new slash command: "/colornames" that enables the new name coloring scheme:

Monsters: Names are colored by con color
NPCs: Cyan
Realm Enemies: Red
Guild Members: Dark Green
Players: Normal Color (light blue)
Graves/Dead People: Brown
Selected Target: White

Note that when you turn this on, selected target is no longer yellow, it is white. Yellow names are yellow-con level monsters.

To restore the game to the old system, type "/oldnamecolors", which restores the original name coloring. Your name coloring preference will persist across zoning and quitting.

Like I’m not having enough problems with the colored boxes, colored squiggles in the boxes, remembering what each of those squiggles does, my con colors are now reversed and I’m trying to blow up my group mate while wondering how come the monster isn’t dying. Yipe!

Name Toggling: There is a new slash command used to hide player, monster, and grave names. The command is as follows:

/hidenames players | monsters | self | graves | all
/shownames players | monsters | self | graves | all

Shift-N effectively acts like a toggle between "/hidenames all" and "/shownames all". (Example: /hidenames self graves will hide your own name and those names over graves.)

10 Icon/10 Bank Toolbar: You’ll be able to have 10 hotbars with 10 icons each!

There has also been a new slash command added, /qbar which allows you to pick a quickbar with a slash command. Example: /qbar 2 switches to your second quickbar, the same as pressing Shift-2. This change allows you to assign /macro commands that will select between different quickbars. The current quickbar page will now persist across zoning and quitting.

Cool! Now I can Shift 1, cast spell 4, shift 0, buff, /qbar 3, run, Shift 2, eyes. glazing. over.

Well some nice goodies coming out, and hopefully soon! There are always the little fixes they are doing of course, armor dying, monsters, etc. I just picked some of the major fun ones.
