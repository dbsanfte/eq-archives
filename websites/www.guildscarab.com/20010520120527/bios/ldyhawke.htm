Name: Ldyhawke Jymblade

Gender: Female
Age: 26
Height: 4'6"
Weight: 180
Eyes: Blue
Hair: Auburn
Alignment: Lawful Good

   
Ldyhawke's Story
   

Description:

Lady Hawke looks like most normal Dwarfs of her age. She has long hair and wears it in long braids draped over her shoulders. unlike most dwarven females, she lacks a beard. She carrys herself in a rugged manner, having spent the majority of her years in the mines of Kaladim. She has recently added a tatoo on her left hand which is a picture of the sun, moon, and the stars as she firmly belives that her Diety looks down upon her and given her the strength and courage to uphold the values that her guild, Scarab, has asked of her.

History:

Born as Rozella of Kaladim, she grew up the maze of many tunnels and trails. She made it her duty to know the destination of each and every one of them! She lived in relative security with her mother, also a cleric, until one day she decided to expand her knowledge of the area. She liked living in the mines but always wondered about what lay outside her immediate area. One day she loaded up her pack and ventured forth whereupon she walked onto the docks of Kaladim to find a large ferry. Bravely she said a small prayer to her diety, and jumped aboard. She arrived in the town called Freeport with no maps to guide her. It was so unlike the quiet underground she was used to, and at first the hustle and bustle made her nervous. It was not too long before she soon realized that she was lost with no idea as to where she was at.

Luckily for her, she met the acquaintance of a human Paladin who lived in this city, he had a natural charisma about him, and seemed to treat her with high respect, something which other humans did not give her. Instead of wishing to be returned to her underground home, she elected to learn more about the city that she arrived in. After informing her mother of her decision, she took to teaching herself everything that she could about the land she had discovered, while trying to promote the ways of the dwarven Diety. She spent many days with her new friend, and found his faith in Dietys did not clash with hers, and found his sense of honor refreshing. Rozella learned that the city held many secrets that held her fascination for many years. After spending several years as friends of the Paladin, they grew very close, and decided to take up residence on the western edge of the city and share thier knowledge with anyone or anything that would need it. Together they formed a guild with strong roots in Honor and Loyalty, and they married, she took his name, dropping the Rozella, and earned the right of Lady with her station of Guildmaster.

Since then she has been assisting any creatures that come to her for help. She has an open policy of assisting any creatures of any alignment who may need healing. Lady Hawke has an extensive knowledge of healing certain adventurers who were cursed with long-term ailments and then teach them the ways of the land so that they could care for themselves. Due to her views in assisting everything in the land she has made friends with many creatures that live there. Together with Lord Hawke, she makes a solid force agaisnt the evil dealings in this land.

Recent Activity:

Lady Hawke has been involved in many investigations over the last several weeks. Most of the cases she works on are either acting as a knowldege gatherer for the guild, or making money in the fishing industry she recently discovered. She has also been in a number of internal investigations into the guild of Scarab. As a lawful good cleric, she is on the constant look out for signs of activity by known evil groups in the land. She is very intellegent and exceptionally observant, very little escapes her notice. She is very difficult to surprise, and always tries to stay one step ahead of the enemy. She doesn't, however, give this appearance to those who do not know her, which only serves to increase her ability to achieve this. To most she appears to be a simple cleric who speaks freely with you, however has a knack of being able to get people to say things before they realize that they didn't mean to.
