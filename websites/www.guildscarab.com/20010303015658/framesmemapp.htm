What we expect from our members
Be honestly motivated to be a member of the guild. Our guild is a large family and we take pride in the fact that every person in the guild really enjoys being a part of the family. If you want to be a part of the guild, then be a part of the guild. At Scarab, we honestly believe that the person behind the screen is important. We try to look beyond the character, and just like any Pen and Paper gaming group, everyone involved are friends, and such is it with the people behind the characters in Scarab.
The guild has two sets of laws, which dictate our actions: The Code of Conduct and the Code of Chivalry. These codes are the heart and soul of a Scarab member, and are to be followed at all times.

 
Code of Conduct.

Honor...doing the right thing
Valor...Battling the evils of the realm, and dying for the beliefs we serve.
Loyalty...Thy guildmate is thy brother, the life and needs of a guildmate comes before thine own!

 
Code of Chivalry.
Justice-  In courageously seeking the truth, and the path of righteousness, members must strive to escape their own personal gain and instead be just. Members who seek out justice without bending to temptation will be members of renown. Justice serves both HONOR and LOYALTY.

Sacrifice-  Generosity is a main characteristic of a Scarab member.   In order to counter the weakness of gluttony, members are to be as generous as their resources will allow!!!!   Generous members are better able to walk the fine line between mercy and cold justice. Giving rather than taking makes the name of SCARAB known and respected. Sacrifice serves HONOR.

Compassion- The weak and Innocent are our charges, their defense and safety are our duty. The might and wisdom of the SCARAB member allow those not capable of their own well being to stand boldly behind us. The meek may rest weary heads on the strength of our shoulders. Compassion serves HONOR and VALOR.

Spirituality-  Members must seek to be masters of their own minds and bodies.  From this prowess we must seek to humbly serve those to whom we have sworn our lives and duty. Spirituality serves both LOYALTY and HONOR.

THIS IS THE SCARAB WAY!!! To these ends we strive to serve! Thanks to Lord Gregor, who provided the model for this code.

How to Join

We take the recruitment of new members very seriously, and getting into Scarab is no easy task. However, most people who have put in the necessary effort to be admitted to the guild believe it is well worth the wait.
Here are a few guidelines to help you become a recruit, as well as some information about what to expect from the recruitment process.

To become a recruit, you must do the following:

1) Introduce yourself to us on our public message board. Tell us about yourself and express your desire to join the guild. Be sure to tell us your reasons for picking Scarab as your guild of choice, let us know of your current guild acquaintances, and tell us what you hope to bring to the Scarab family.

2) Send an email containing a similar message to the Master of Admissions expressing your desire to join the guild.

3) We are recruiting by invitation only, so if you are serious about joining Scarab you must get at least three elder members to sponsor your admittance into the guild. Please ask them to post a message of recommendation about you on our Recruitment Message Board.
We are a large guild, and we pride ourselves on the fact that we've been able to maintain a family feeling and atmosphere in the guild where everyone knows everyone. We feel that if you are unable to find at least three friends in the guild to sponsor you, this probably isn't the right guild for you
As a general rule, the more friends you have in the guild, the better your chances of being admitted will be.

Once we've received this information and at least three messages of recommendation, we will add you to our waiting list. We usually accept recruits in "batches," typically 3 - 5 new recruits at a time. When the time comes to select the next "batch" of recruits, we will review our waiting list and select the most promising candidates. Please note that the time between "batches" might be several months.
Please note that our waiting list is not on a first-come-first-server priority basis. We evaluate each person individually and select those who most closely match our values and friendships.
So, don't be discouraged if you are "last" on the list. Your position on the list has no bearing in regards to your acceptance into the guild.

If we select you from the waiting list, you will officially become a recruit and we will add you to the "guildstone" (our term for /guildsay chat) but you must go into /anonymous mode so the Scarab tag is not shown above your character's head until you've completed your recruitment period.

Expect to be a recruit for quite some time. The minimum recruitment time is two weeks, but it often takes over a month for a recruit to be admitted into full membership status.
This long waiting period gives us a chance to get to you know better, but more importantly, it gives you a chance to see if Scarab is the right guild for you.

After we feel that we've gotten to know you fully, you'll be presented with a verbal membership test in-game presented by the Master of Admissions. This test will include questions about the nature of the guild, its leadership structure, important guild concepts, the guild charter, and questions about your personal character. If you review the information contained on our Membership page and put forth a good effort toward getting to know your guildmates you'll do just fine on this test.

Lastly, you along with your fellow recruits will be faced with a Recruit Quest presented by the guild for the initiation of the recruits. These quests are detailed, involved, and very enjoyable. They give you a good taste of what being in Scarab is all about.

In conclusion, the # 1 best way to get into the guild is this: Hunt with us, group with us, hunt with us, get drunk with us, hunt with us, and when you've done all that, do it all over again.

The recruitment process is long and detailed, so it will take time for the "paperwork" to be handled. So, your main goal should be that when the Master of Admission says "Do we want to let So-And-So become a recruit?" everyone will say "What? You mean he/she isn't one already? Why on earth not?"

Good luck!

 
Scarab in multiple games
We support our members in their desire to play additional games. We provide Secure boards for communication for games like Diablo, Icewind Dale and Vampire. Scarab members in a Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Games can petition the Scarab Inner Circle to become an officially sanctioned Guild by following these provisions.
1) At least ten Scarab members have played the game for at least three months
2) The in-game world would logically support a guild of Scarab’s nature, beliefs and rules (i.e. no Unreal Tournament, Half-Life, etc.)
3) Follow the Code of Conduct and the Code of Chivalry stated in the Charter.
4) The players ask the Inner Circle to become official emissaries of Scarab to that Game.
If approved by the Inner Circle, the active players in that game would be welcome to:
1) Use the moniker “Scarab” with their names.
2) Apply for official guild status with the new game, under the name Scarab.
3) Recruit members from the game to be part of Scarab. These players would be allowed to participate as a Scarab in ANY of our supported games.
4) Be allowed to select one of their numbers to serve on the Inner Circle as a representative with full voting privileges.
5) Create an organizational structure to facilitate in-game needs. Examples are positions that are not leadership oriented (i.e. not guild master). Acceptable positions are those needed to make game life easier such as controlling the flow of resources (i.e. armourer, treasurer, librarian, etc.)
Although Scarab does believe in supporting as many games as their player’s desire, most resources will be focused on a single MMORPG. Currently this is Everquest. It takes a 75% full membership vote to change this focus.

Current Guild rules and bylaws.
GUILD SERVER - Solusek Ro

GUILD BASE OF OPERATIONS - Jade Tiger, North Freeport

RACE RESTRICTIONS - Scarab does not show prejudice to the player's right to choose a race of their character.

CLASS RESTRICTIONS - Scarab does not show prejudice to the player's right to choose a class of their character.

ROLEPLAYING VS POWERGAMING - Scarab would be considered a light role-playing guild. When at all possible, we ask that members try to be in character. OOC comments are not prohibited by any means, nor do we expect people to be hard-core role-players all of the time. We do not, however, support powergaming. There are times when a touch of powergaming is acceptable, and even needed. Scarab members should not consider powergaming to be a top motivation for their character.

MEMBER INTEGRITY - At NO time will a member of Scarab attempt to hack game code. Members of Scarab will at no time exploit bugs for any reason. Members of SCARAB will not promote bug exploiting, or any other illegal activity as deemed by Verant Interactive, or the Guildmaster of Scarab. Any member found to violate this bylaw will be immediately dismissed from the guild.

Player vs. Player - Scarab is supportive of it's members and their values. The Inner Circle and Guildmaster have concluded that we should be a -PVP guild on Norrath. There are limitations in the way that -PVP players can interact with +PVP players (from the functionality of the game), and thus we need to all be "in the same boat" for optimal group interaction. There is a lot of PVP interaction in -PVP, such as dueling and Guild Wars, which provide honorable PVP fighting situations.

Membership Fees - Scarab has no initiation fee nor does Scarab have any minimum donation required monthly. Members are asked to donate extra equipment to the guild and if they have extra coin donate it to those working on trade skills.

Multiple Guilds - Scarab members may not have alternate characters in different guilds. Scarab members may have more then one character on the Guildstone.

These are the current bylaws for Scarab. Changes may be made periodically by majority vote by the Inner Circle.

 
Current Officer Positions - Please note that these positions are for role-playing and in-game purposes only
Master of Admissions
The Master of Admissions (MoA) will be responsible for the final testing of all recruits before they are made members of Scarab. The MoA will be allowed to join in conversations on the Inner Circle message board but will not have a vote.

Guild Armourer
The Guild Armourer position was created to allow one person to hold all extra armour. If you are looking to upgrade your armour see the Guild Armourer and s/he will see what they can do. This position will also help to coordinate the creation of racial armours.

Guild Blacksmith
The Guild Blacksmith position was created to allow one person to hold all extra weapons. If you are looking to upgrade your weapons see the Guild Blacksmith and s/he will see what they can do. This position will also help to coordinate the creation of racial weapons and shields.

Guild Librarian
The Guild Librarian position was created to allow one person to act as the point person in gathering all magical scrolls, words and runes. They will also work to coordinate helping with the skill of research. If you are looking for help with spells please contact this person.

 
Phases of Membership
RECRUITS
Recruits are added to the guildstone so they may communicate with guild members. Recruits must stay in Anon mode during this time period so their guild tag does not appear. This is a probationary period that will last a minimum of 2 weeks. Some time after the 2 week period they will meet with the Master of Admissions and at least one other member Inner Circle. They will be tested on their guild knowledge. Upon passing the test they will be made a full member of the guild and will be granted access to our secure web pages.

GUILD MEMBERS
Once promoted from the status of Recruit, the member is now a full member of the Scarab Family. Full members are the heart and soul of the guild, and are able to vote for  new Inner Circle members, receive a custom guild pop3 account, and have the bragging rights of being in a tight knit family of true friends and comrades.

ELDER  MEMBERS
Elder members are members who have been in the guild for six consecutive months. Elder members of Scarab can volunteer to serve on the Inner Circle.

INNER CIRCLE
1) Members of the Inner Circle provide leadership through shaping policy and voting on key issues.
2) The Inner Circle is not an in-game role-playing leadership body. Its purpose is to handle issues that affect the guild as a whole (recruitment, website administration, discipline issues, involvement in non-core games)
3) Members of the Inner Circle must maintain an active presence by participating in all official discussions and all votes unless they have given some prior notice that they will be unavailable
4) At times real life may make it difficult or undesirable to participate as a member of the Inner Circle for an extended period of time. In such cases it is acceptable to honorably step down from the position.
5) The Inner Circle will abide by the Code of Leadership. They will not treat their position as one of authority and will at all times perform their duties to the best of their ability and treat all members with respect
6) Inner Circle members have no individual authority. In order to prevent possible abuse, the Inner Circle must approve all actions. There will be an election every 6 months to keep the Inner Circle and the Leader of the IC active and representative of the guild as a whole. If an IC member steps down, that position will not be filled until the next general election. There is no limit to the number of terms a member may serve. The IC is encouraged to delegate certain responsibilities such as recruit mentoring or running quests to volunteers. Willing volunteers are our greatest assets. The IC and Guildmaster will select officers as needed (treasurer, librarian, armourer, player advocates, etc)
7) The Inner Circle will have a secure forum to discuss issues.
8) The Inner Circle will have a ratio of 1:7 with Scarab members. This ratio is to be used when electing a new Inner Circle.

GUILDMASTER
This member is the highest level of role-playing leadership and inter-guild diplomacy, he/she should be well spoken and levelheaded, intimately familiar with guild policy, and be able to role-play as necessary. The Guildmaster is an equal member of the Inner Circle with full voting privileges.
The Guildmaster will hold this position until they decide to step down, the guild decides to stop playing the current game they are actively involved in, or by a vote to remove the GM is passed by a two/thirds majority of the guild.


SCARAB CODE OF LEADERSHIP
Excellence - The tasks and responsibilities of a leader can at times become difficult, time consuming, discouraging, and frustrating. However, no matter what the circumstances the leaders of Scarab perform their duties to the best of their ability to benefit the entire guild. A leader takes no short cuts, does not look for an easy way out, and always tries to do the best job possible.
Service - A leader will always put the needs of members before their own. Leaders will never use their position to degrade or put down another member. A leader in Scarab is not in a position of authority. A leader serves the membership.
Integrity - Leaders of our guild are held to a higher standard. Any action taken by a leader reflects upon the entire leadership body and upon the entire guild. Leaders will at all times think and act carefully with the highest level of integrity. A leader is a leader at all times, even when nobody is looking.
