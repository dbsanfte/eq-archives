Saturday, March 29, 2003
  Inquiring Minds Want to Know… ~ Rose

SCARAB NOTES:

Nothing overly, just to keep watching the Alliance boards for further information.


This times Inquiring Minds Want to Know is with Scarab member Horammer:

How did you come to join Scarab?
It's a lucky story for me. Just fate really, but I'm glad it happened the way it did. One night, about 2 weeks after I started playing EQ, my friends decided Fennin Ro server was too laggy and overpopulated. We decided to switch. After some investigation, I told them Sol Ro seemed to be a good server and we moved there. Many of you know Diison Dawnrunner from Sol Ro EQ. I joined his guild, Norrath War College, when Diison was level 12, lol. After Norrath War College broke up in the fall of 1999, I looked for a new guild. I met Hawke, Borgu, and Ozwyn in the Highkeep basement fighting goblins. They seemed like nice people and they had a good website, so I pestered them until they signed me up a couple months later, in December, 1999J.

What game(s) did you play with the Scarab Guild?
As a member of SCARAB, I have played Everquest, Dark Age of Camelot, and Anarchy Online. Maybe one or two others that aren't springing to mind, but those are the major ones. Actually at Scarabfest last year we also set up a LAN and played a lot of Jedi Knight II and...Stronghold I think it was called, both of which were very cool. I was pretty mean at JK2, but got my butt kicked at Stronghold.

Which was your favorite game and why?
Despite all the various and sundry bitching that people direct at Verant (and much of it deserved), the most impressive and groundbreaking video game I've seen in the last 5 years has undoubtedly been Everquest. Yes, it had and still has several problems, but overall it was an AMAZING experience. Think about it for a minute. Consider the graphics, the real time interaction with an ever more challenging fantasy environment; fabulous group/class dynamics; chatting about all sorts of stuff with your "team" that, in a good guild like SCARAB, quickly became friends.

Until boredom and frustration set in later in the upper levels, EQ rocked SCARAB's world for many happy months of questing and slaying of monsters and I and many others look back on those days of EQ very fondly. The friendships gained I maintain today. Some of the people I met from SCARAB have been some of the best people I have met and I hang out with them in real life.

Also, EQ lent itself quite naturally to providing opportunities to foster SCARAB's principles of Honor, Valor, and Loyalty by rewarding such with medals. The Inner Circle created a variety of medals that were specifically designed to ensure good guild morale, smooth operation of in game events and administrative tasks, and reward those showing loyalty to the guild and willingness to sacrifice for the good of the guild and one's guildmates. This continued SCARAB's tradition of being a friendly and honorable organization, which helps ensures that we have a good reputation among other gamers, and that members of SCARAB are good people that treat each other well and act to help the guild, which is of course, the whole point.

In the game, what name and type of character do people know you by?
In Everquest, my first serious character was named Dinaden Gahalantine, which was a combination of the names of 2 Arthurian knights. He was a bad ass warrior. He wore all blue armor when it was still cool. Ahem. Anyway, after I leveled Dinaden to level 50, I started playing a cleric character of a guildmate that had stopped playing. That character's name was Horammer. I played him to level 52. Clerics rule. That's just all there is to it. I loved playing Horammer, but felt that I had accomplished all I had ever wanted to do or could reasonably even dream of doing in EQ, so I moved on to DAOC. In DAOC I played a thane named Dinaden up until level 20. Today, I am mainly known as Horamir, as I have slightly amended the name of my cleric ;) I do like cleric types in general. They are the Kings in EQ. People want you in their groups, and you are useful and fun to play. I've found that having a decent amount of HP and AC is good, I'm way too much of a perfectionist to play an enchanter or something that die at the drop of a hat, yet I gotta have some spells too!

Have you ever been to a ScarabFest?
Absolutely! After I made the big move out east to work for the government, I have attended several Scarabfests in Atlanta and Washington, DC. I expect to be at the next major Scarabfest in Atlanta over Memorial Day weekend in May, 2003.

What was the most fun moment you had at the ScarabFest?
There are two things that are great about Scarabfests. One was meeting SCARABs that I knew well from EQ but had never met before. Jayfoot, Miri, Dolvin and co. from Atlanta, and Sedrick and Hawke from California, among others, were fun to meet and hang out with.

Although we always play various computer games and chat incessently about games, probably the most fun we have is actually non-game stuff like all gathering together and sharing a good meal and just hanging out and talking. We all went as a big group last year and saw StarWars Episode 2 and had a good time at Dave and Busters (Dolvin is a shareholder in D&B's, natch…)

Also last year, Hawke, Mace, Dias and I I think it was all went to a Sam's Club and bought a bunch of food (a bit TOO much, but hey, better than not enough). We then had a couple great meals together as a guild with about 16 people all eating together. One meal in particular was really good-homemade lasangna made by Jay's wife Celia. Everyone liked that and we were regaled by some great funny stories from Hawke, Ozy, Ven and Ravenn about the early days of the guild, and Hawke gave an excellent retelling of the Llama story (and I could still see the terror in his eyes…).

This year, for SF we are planning to focus more on group outings and meals, since LAN parties tend to burn us out and are generally not as cool for a guild gathering as stuff we can't do when we aren't together, if you follow me.

What or Who got you interested in gaming?
Wow, long story. The short version: I remember being hooked on Pac Man and Space Invaders from an early age on the Atari 2600. Later, when I was in 3rd or 4th grade, my sister's boyfriend's brother introduced me to Dungeons and Dragons. At that tender age, I didn't really have any serious campaigns going on, but my friends and I knew there was something very appealing about it and we stuck with it. Around that same time, the original 8 bit Nintendo Intertainment System was coming out and I was hooked forever on gaming. The Legend of Zelda is still my all time favorite video game, period. It also helped that my best friend that I have known for practically my whole life (we were born the same week in the same hospital in Iowa City) was a huge gaming nerd. Growing up in the 80's with my little income from a paper route, I played just about every good computer and Nintendo role playing game there was. This morphed into computer gaming more in high school and college, as I was never as into the systems that came after the Super Nintendo 16 bit system.

What MMORG game do you see yourself playing next and why?
Well, obviously I suppose I and the guild will be giving the Star Wars MMORPG a shot, although so far, beta chatter is not overly optimistic. But I can't deny the appeal of slicing off heads with a lightsaber or going "growooror" as a Wookie. I mean, who can, really? Also, I have heard good things about Worlds of Warcraft from Blizzard, but that one looks like it's a ways off. Also, Horizons has put out some very cool looking looking ideas for their game, but its been around a long time and they haven't really produced all that much, so it isn't looking like Horizons will actually be appearing anytime soon either.

Favorite website(s)?
Any one that features Patricia Ford is pretty strong in my book. Ok, seriously, I mainly go for the web comics such as PvP Online, Bruno the Bandit, Penny Arcade, Nuklear Power 16-bit Theater, as well as gaming and news sites, the occasional fantasy sports and car website, and of course, the mighty SCARAB boards. The Excite.com homepage is surprisingly good at organizing a customizable array of news links for you and I generally set that as my homepage.

Favorite hobby besides games?
Probably music and DVD movies. I've always been a musician, first being forced to take piano lessons and hating that and not being very good, but later being really unaccountably good on trombone and loving that. These days I play electric guitar halfway decently and am totally addicted to home theater DVD watchin. I like music quite a bit, mainly hard rock, some heavy metal, as well as blues and classical.

What do you do in real life?
I work for the United States Department of Education, Office of Federal Student Aid as a Financial Aid Management Analyst. That is a polite way of saying I am a bureaucrat ;) I am mainly a writer. Our biggest annual project is the Student Financial Aid Handbook, which explains to colleges nationwide how to correctly administer the Federal Student Aid programs for college student financial assistance. I write the Pell Grant section of the Handbook and occasionally call up colleges and prod their lazy butts along on behalf of guildmates who happen to be in college, heh.

Also, my little team analyzes all sorts of FSA web sites and knowledge management solutions that the Dept has going on and implements solutions to enhance communication and operational fuctions. That is a fancy way of saying we have license to look at all sorts of various screwed up FSA (the Office of Federal Student Aid) things and try to improve the way things work, a little bit at a time. Our other major function is to research complex financial aid questions that our Customer Service Call Center cannot answer.

Oh, you meant what do I do that ISN'T completely boring? Well, after work I go home and play with my kitty cat, Coal, and listen to music and watch TV and play video games. Right now I'm plowing through the first 3 seasons of the Sopranos on DVD. Highly recommended.

What state do you live in?
I live in the "commonwealth" of Virginia. Don't ask me why it is still called a commonwealth, that's probably a British remnant, but it is one of the 50 states in the United States. It is one of the two states that surround the Capital of the US, the District of Columbia.

Best thing to do in Virginia?
Since Virginia is one of the original 13 states and has had more American Presidents born within its borders than any other state, there are many interesting historic sites to see here, such as Monticello and Mt Vernon (the homes of famous American Presidents Thomas Jefferson and George Washington), as well as Colonial Williamsburg. The Fashion Center at Pentagon City and Tyson's Corner Shopping Center are two of the nicer shopping malls I've seen on the East Coast, and both are within easy driving distance of Arlington.

Other than that, Washington DC itself is nearby, and is famous for its tourist appeal, featuring many museums, the US Capitol and White House buildings, memorials, and excellent restaurants. Back in Arlington, I live within a couple miles of the Pentagon. My mom toured the Pentagon once, which she said was a very interesting tour, but now after September 11th, they no longer give tours to the public, so that is unfortunately out. And of course, the headquarters of Mythic Entertainment, makers of Dark Age of Camelot is in Fairfax, Virginia, approximately 10 miles from where I live…

Long Live SCARAB!

I’d like to make a note in remembrance to the crew of flight STS-107, who lost their lives on Saturday, February 1. The Columbia was NASA’s oldest shuttle, logging 28 flights since 1981. Columbia was named after the Boston, Massachusetts-based sloop that in 1792 maneuvered past the dangerous sandbar at the mouth of a river extending more than 1,000 miles/ through what is today southeast British Columbia, Canada and the Washington-Oregon border.

You can find complete coverage of the STS-107 at Space.com, including bio’s of the astronauts that are complete with interview clips.

To the crew of the Columbia and to all who have had the greatest honor to visit space:
