The Rules

At the beginning of the game, I will ask the audience a question. The first person from the audience who /tells me the correct answer to my question will become the first contestant who will attempt to become a Millionaire.
NOTE: All questions will be given directly at the Jade Tiger Inn, not via guildsay. So, if you want to participate, you'll have to be there in person.

The first contestant will then join me in the "hot seat" and I will begin quizzing him/her. All questions will be multiple choice and EverQuest-related. Each question will be assigned a money value. The contestant will start at the 100 question and will work his/her way up to 1 Million.

There are 15 questions in all, and their difficulty increases as the show goes on. When the contestant reaches the 1000, 32000, and 1 Million mark (every five questions), they are guaranteed to leave the game with at least that amount of money, even if they answer a following question incorrectly.

During each question, the contestant has the option of simply withdrawing from the competition before answering the question and he/she will get to keep all the money that has been earned up to that point (take the money and run).
The contestant will also have the option of using "Lifelines" that will help reveal the correct answer.

Lifelines

Each contestant has 3 Lifelines that can be used at any point in the game, but each Lifeline can only be used once. Lifelines are used to help the contestant answer a question that he/she doesn't know the answer to.

50 : 50
The 50:50 will eliminate two of the incorrect choices, leaving two choices remaining (one correct, and one incorrect).

 

Call a friend
When this Lifeline is used, the contestant may send a /tell to anyone else who is online at the time to ask for help. This is the ONLY /tell that the contestant will send during the entire game. All contestants will be on their Scarab Word-Of-Honor to uphold this condition. This /tell conversation may only last up to 30 seconds.

Ask the Audience
When this Lifeline is used, each person in the audience will send me a /tell about which answer he/she believes to be the correct answer. I will then tell the contestant what percentage of the audience voted for each of the choices.

More than one Lifeline may be used for the same question, but each Lifeline may only be used once during the entire game.

Other Odds & Ends

When the contestant answers the question incorrectly or chooses to "take the money and run," a new contestant will be chosen from the audience through the same method as before and the game will start over. This will continue until a) we run out of contestants, b) we run out of time, or c) I run out of money.

This game promises to be a ton of fun, and I look forward to seeing you all there!
