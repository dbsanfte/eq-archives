The Bard Recommends: Where the Bard acknowledges the Masters

These lists are meant to represent the best creators of pure fantasy. Much of the work below had a clear influence on the development of EverQuest. If you have any additions, or notice any glaring omissions, email me at the address below.

Literature

Author Work First Book in Series Comments (influences on EQ)
J.R.R. Tolkien Lord of the Rings, Hobbit The Hobbit Do I need to comment? Halflings, Rangers, Treants, Orcs, Dragons, Giant Spiders....
Michael Moorcock Elric, Corum Elric of Melnibone Decadent dark elves and the baddest magic sword of all. Also check out the Corum books.
Fritz Leiber Fafhrd and the Mouser Ill Met in Lankhmar Everybody's favorite thieves, they are in thrall to mighty wizards. Viva Lankhmar!
Raymond Feist The Midkemia Books Magician: Apprentice Pug is eventually the most powerful magic user in fantasy literature. All wizards check it out.
Patricia McKillip The Riddlemaster of Hed The Riddlemaster of Hed Gentle fantasy novels. Great depiction of magical shapeshifting for all you druids out there.
Stephen R. Donaldson Thomas Covenent the Unbeliever Lord Foul's Bane Slightly derivative of Tolkien, these books are masterpieces of characterization. Giants the way they ought to be.
Robert E. Howard Conan, Kull, etc. Conan The Barbarian's Barbarian. He is NEVER getting the bloodstains out of his clothes!
Roger Zelazny The Chronicles of Amber Nine Princes in Amber Not fantasy in purest sense, still there's enough magic and swordplay for anybody. Talk about sibling rivalry!
David Eddings The Belgariad, etc. Pawn of Prophecy Hawke's favorite series. Godslaying and father, daughter sorcerer combo.

Artists

Artist Bound Collection Comments (influences on EQ)
Keith Parkinson Knightsbridge: the Art of Keith Parkinson Painted the EQ box cover. Also painted the ice dragon on the SCARAB home page.
Michael Whelan The Art of Michael Whelan Probably Borgu's favorite artist. Truly a master. Award winning fantasy book cover artist.
Frank Frazetta ICON: a Retrospective by Frank Frazetta The single most influential fantasy artist ever! Remember those Molly Hatchet record covers?

Films

Excalibur Knights in shining armor. Early role for Capt. Picard.
