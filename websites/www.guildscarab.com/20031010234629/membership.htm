How To Join
  Scarab actively recruits in all of the games it is currently playing. To join Scarab please check the Game Message Board and look for a post entitled "Recruitment or How to Join", follow the guidelines laid out in that message and you will be a Regular member in that game in no time at all.

Phases of Membership
Regular members:
• These members wear the SCARAB tag, but may also join other guilds and/or play on other servers in sanctioned (have an Official Scarab presence) games.
• Regular members have access to all non-secure SCARAB message boards.
• Regular Members will be granted access to the secure message board for the game(s) they are involved in.
• Regular members have voting privileges in any game they are involved in. An example of this would be if the Guildmaster(GM) asked the players if they wanted to absorb another guild.
• Regular members may join SCARAB in other games by petitioning the game's GM and Master/Mistress of Admissions(MoA) and following their recruitment system.
• Regular members may petition the Inner Circle to be admitted to Elder member status.

Elder Members:
• Elder members are Scarabs who have chosen to forego joining other guilds and who have dedicated themselves to building the Scarab family.
• Elder members may join another guild in games where there is no Scarab presence.
• Elder Members have voting privileges for any non-game specific vote and any in-game vote for which they are a member of that game.
• Elder members are automatically granted membership in all SCARAB sancioned games.
• Elder Members will receive access to all secure game boards plus the General Secure board and the Scarab Get Together board.
• Only Elder Members may volunteer to serve on the Inner Circle.

Inner Circle:
• Members of the Inner Circle provide leadership through shaping policy and voting on key issues.
• The Inner Circle is not an in-game leadership body.
• Its purpose is to handle issues that affect the guild as a whole (website administration, discipline issues, involvement in non-core games, policies that impact multiple games, etc).
• Members of the Inner Circle must maintain an active presence by participating in all official discussions and all votes unless they have given some prior notice that they will be unavailable.
• The Inner Circle will abide by the Code of Leadership. They will not treat their position as one of authority and will at all times perform their duties to the best of their ability and treat all members with respect.
• Inner Circle members have no individual authority. In order to prevent possible abuse, the full Inner Circle must approve all actions through votes called by the Inner Circle Leader.
• There will be an election every 6 months to keep the Inner Circle and the Leader of the IC active and representative of the guild as a whole.
• If an IC member steps down, that position will not be filled until the next general election.
• There is no set limit to the number of terms a guildmember may serve on the IC.
• The Inner Circle has a secure forum to discuss issues.
• The IC will have a minimum of nine members, with a ratio of 1:7 IC to full members. If the ratio dictates more than 9 members, then as many IC positions that correspond to the ratio will be elected.
• Inner Circle members will be granted access to all Scarab message boards.

Inner Circle Leader:
• This position is voted upon by the Inner Circle Members at the start of each term by simple majority.
• The Inner Circle Leader shall report weekly to the guild on the General Secure board a summary of discussions being held by the Inner Circle.
• The Inner Circle Leader shall call all votes being held on the Inner Circle board.
• The Inner Circle Leader should cast the final vote so that his/her vote may be used to break a tie.

Guildmaster:
• This position is the highest level of in-game leadership and inter-guild diplomacy.
• The Guildmaster should be well spoken and levelheaded, intimately familiar with guild policy and game mechanics, and be willing and able to lead Scarab in in-game activities.
• Only Elder Members of the guild may serve as Guildmasters.
• You can only serve as the GM of one game at a time.
• The GM is the permanent representative of that sanctioned game to the IC and has equal voting rights with elected IC members.
• The Guildmaster is responsible for delegating in-game officers as needed to ensure the success of Scarab.
• The Guildmaster will hold this position until they decide to step down, the guild decides to stop playing the current game they are actively involved in, or by a vote to remove the GM is passed by 3/4 vote of the Inner Circle.
• The Guildmaster of each game will moderate an officer board to discuss in-game issues with their appointed in-game officers.
• The GM is responsible for setting up a system of recruiting in that game consistent with the needs of the guild in that game and in keeping with the Scarab tradition of finding quality members.
• No formal approval of the recruitment policy is required by the IC per se, but the GM should remember that any Regular Members added in one game may eventually petition for Elder Member Status and therefore a diligent effort should be made to find quality candidates.

Game Officers:
• These positions are created by the GM of a game and may be either appointed by the GM or elected from the game's population at the GM's discretion.
• The GM has complete latitude over the role and quantity of the officers for that game.
• Game Officers are not by themselves granted Inner Circle access, however, Inner Circle members may be game officers and are strongly encouraged to participate in games.
