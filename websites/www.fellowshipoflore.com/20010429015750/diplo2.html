Ranks

~ Duke/Duchess of Friends ~

High Council Member. Makes the rules and has the final say if a guild is accepted as an ally. Announces the promotions at the meeting. Takes all problems to the High Council that can not be handled at his level. Has a direct link to the Regent to deal with problems with other guilds.

~ Marquis/Marquise of Friends ~

Knight of the Round Table. Is in charge of the Division in the absence of the Duke when an answer is needed right away. Takes all problems to the Duke for answers that he/she can not be answered at the time.   Works with and trains those of lower rank. Is required to be able perform all duties of ranks below him/her.

~ Count/ess of Friends ~

Serves as second in command of the division, within the guild, is considered a supervisor of those below. Takes any problems that can they can not solve to the Marquise of Friends.

~ Ambassador Extraordinary ~

An ambassador that is often sent to handle duties outside of to the ordinary Has special and often temporary task or responsibilities. At times these responsibilities my last for extended period of times. May represent our guild in negotiating a treaty if need be. Works on assisting and training the Diplomatic Greeters, Officers, and Agents. Works on issues with the high rank members.

~ Ambassador of Friends ~

A diplomatic official serving as permanent head of a branch within the division and is considered a supervisor of those below. Is an authorized messenger or representative of the guild in times of negotiating and formalizing the treaties/alliances with other guilds. Takes any problems that can they can not solve to the Count of Friends.

~ Ambassador At Large ~

An ambassador who is not given a particular assignment but is appointed on a special missions by the Ambassador, Count, Marquise, or Duke of Friends. Assists and trains those in the Greeter and Diplomat levels and assists them in learning and understanding  their duties. Works on issues with the lower rank members.

(Must take accent oath and is also know as a Knight)

~ Plenipotentiary ~

Works with the guilds given by suggestions by the Diplomatic Agent and sees what type of alliance is required between the guilds. Does the same thing as the Agent did but starts to talk with the higher officials of the other guild to see about an alliance. If an alliance is recommended then pass it to the Ambassador of Friends to work out the final procedures.

(Must Attend Knight School)

~ Diplomatic Agent ~

Begins to work with other guilds to form a friendship within another guild. Learns there rules understands what their guild stands for and recommends if the guild should be an allie. Hunts with members of the guild and sees how many follow the rules. If they feel it is a good guild for alliance pass it up to the Plenipotentiary for more work on the alliance.

~ Diplomatic Officer ~

Takes reports from Diplomatic Greeter and assembles them. Finds out the information and does the research if there is a problem with another guild in some way. Passes all the information up to an Ambassador or above to work out

~ Diplomatic Greeter ~

Greets the members of other guilds that attend our guild meetings. Makes new members feel welcome to the guild and important. Gets to know members of unallied guilds send report on different members of non-allied guild to Diplomatic Officer.

~ Diplomat ~

A member who is tactful and skillful in managing delicate situations, handling people, etc. Works as a vehicle for other guilds to get information up the Ambassador. Handles everything with diplomacy and tact. Welcomed members of allied guilds to our meetings or events.

~ Greeter ~

Is the rank you receive when you join the division. Once a member has proven the ability to be a excellent greeter and has shown they understand the meaning of the Knight Code of Honor they are eligible for promotion. Greets members at the meeting and helps them know they are welcome.
