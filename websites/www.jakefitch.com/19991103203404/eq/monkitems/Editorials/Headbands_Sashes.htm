Monk Headbands and Sashes
Many monks in the game believe that the current Headbands and Sashes that are available from our guildmasters are not worth the amount of effort that is required to get them.  That is not even mentioning the fact that we can only currently get half of them through the quests, the only way to get the other ones is to kill a fellow monk, or for the best ones the guildmaster himself.  So I would like to propose a change to the current headbands, that would make them a valuable resource to a monk for his/her entire life.  

First, lets look at some of the background to the monk class in EverQuest and the headbands and sashes themselves.  When one creates a monk they are told that the key statistics for them are strength, stamina, agility, and dexterity.  Of these, monks have pretty much always agreed that stamina is the least important statistic to them.  With strength, agility and dexterity being heatedly debated as to which one is the most important.

That being said lets look at the headbands and sashes as they are currently in the game:
White headband/sash: +1 STA, +1 CHA
Yellow headband/sash: +1 STA, +1 CHA, +1 AGI, +1 HP
Orange headband/sash: +2 STA, +1 CHA, +1 AGI, +2 HP
Red headband/sash: +1 DEX, +2 STA, +2 CHA, +2 AGI, +2 HP
Purple headband/sash: 1 AC, +1 DEX, +2 STA, +2 CHA, +2 AGI, +3 HP
Blue headband/sash: 1 AC, +1 STR, +2 DEX, +3 STA, +3 CHA, +2 AGI, +3 HP
Black headband/sash: 2AC, +2 STR, +3 DEX, +4 STA, +4 CHA, +1 INT, +3 AGI, +4 HP

Now as you can see the headbands and sashes as they are currently implemented don't really help us that much in our most important statistics.  As a monk-only items I feel that they should improve those statistics that Verant has stated are the important ones for monks.

I would also like to point out that currently the highest headband that you can receive via the guild quests is the Purple headband, and the best sash is the Red Sash.  

As for those quests lets look at one of them, and the rewards that we receive from it.  To go from the Red to the Purple Headband you have to give one of the monks at the guildhouse: the dagger of Marnek, the skull of Jhen'Tra, and Zaharn's Coronet.  Just yesterday I saw Zaharn's Coronet being auctioned at the bazaar on the Rathe server for over 250 plat.  Now for turning in these items we receive an additional 1 AC and 1 HP.

Yes, most of the other quests are easier to complete, but many of them require a monk to camp in one area for more than 12 hours to receive the items that they must turn in.  However, it is not the quests that I am complaining about, it is the rewards for those quests.  What I would like to see happen is that Verant would change the headbands and sashes into an item that any monk would be proud to wear into battle, not just because of the work involved in receiving them, but also because they would actually help us in those fights.  Or as many higher levels monks do, not wear them at all because there are so many other items that are better that a high level monk can get that will help them much more in battle.

So here are the changes that I would like to see happen.  First I believe that the sashes and headbands should be made No Drop.  The monk already is the most un-twinkable class, or at least melee class in EQ.  I do not want to see these changes affect that at all.  Also, I would like there to be a minimum level that a monk could receive a headband/sash.  These levels would be below the level that a monk can currently get the headband or sash IF he/she were to collect the items necessary to complete the quest themselves.  As a bonus it would be nice if the items that a monk most collect for the quest were light enough for them to carry themselves, but this really is not necessary.  And finally I would like to see the headbands and sashes to give abilities that a monk would find useful and to affect statistics that Verant has stated, and all monks agree, are important for monks.  Here are some ideas for the headbands and sashes that I had, where level is the level required to finish the quest (turn the items in).
