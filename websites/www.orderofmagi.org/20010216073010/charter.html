Order of the Magi Charter

    When the world was young and the peoples of the world had first begun to have magick, a young high elf by the name of Minviern studied the arts and was found to have a innate ability with them. As time grew on and he mastered all the skills available to him...he yearned to move on, to learn more, but no more was know, or allowed, by the peoples of that time. Slowly and secretly the powers of the black art of necromancy creped into his brain. He left his society and wondered the earth, learning this dark forbidden art. But as all else, in time this too proved to be not enough for his absorbing mind and again he sought more. It was in those years that he discovered a force that controls magick. He called it the Magi. The Magi - a intangible force that is the source of all power. It fuels the mana of men, as well as that of the gods. Ages since the death of this powerful man a few remembered the secret about the source of magi, the few except a society of people sworn to learn and control this ancient force know as magi. Sworn to use this power to their own ends.

General Rules

  1. Role-playing

  • While role-playing is not mandated as a must. It is greatly encouraged.

  •  Definition of role playing: Role-playing does not mean talking with "Thee" and "Tho"...It means adopting a persona for your character and using it when acting as your charter or talking with the /say (regular talk). 

  • A person should only role-play with the /say command and all other commands are used when not role-playing. So using /tell you may be out of charter, but when using /say you are in character. Each character a player has should have his own personality.

  1. Multiple Characters in the Guild

  • Multiple Characters in our guild by the same player are allowed, although some restrictions apply.

  • Your secondary characters must follow all the guild lines for regular admittance although you can sponsor yourself.

  • A Player can only have one character in the guild that is a officer. Officers represent the guild and are not given away lightly. Officer status is given away based on that character and how he or she helps the guild. A secondary character is just that; not the first character and hence forth not the same person.

  1. Chain of Command

  • The Order of the Magi uses a democratic type system for all decisions including the voting of all officers.

  • The voting system consist of this: 1 vote from the guild leader, 3 votes from elected officials, and 1 majority vote from all other guild members and one vote from all other officers.

  • There are 3 votable positions available. These positions are givin officer status as well as having one vote in important issues. The 3 positions listed below.

  • Captain of the Guard. This is the position of the warrior hybrid classes. All warrior hybrid classes are members of the Royal Guard of the Magi and they will vote for one leader. Bards for the time being fall into this class.

  • High Magi. This is the leader of the Magi (caster) circle. Members of this circle are all pure casters such as wizards, magicians, enchanters, etc. They will elect one leader.

  • High Priest. This is the leader of the Priest circle. This circle includes all shamans and clerics.

  1. Helping other guild members

  • All guild members must help other guild members when ever they can, so long as it doesn't cause them to travel out of there way (such as going from Halas to Freeport because someone needs a SOW. Also see twinking

  • Guild members may charge a discounted rate for all skills they use for other guild members although it is recommended that they do the skill for free as long as the player asking for the skill to be done pays for all materials..

  • All teleport spells can be asked for by guild members so long as the caster has a convent means of getting himself back to the zone he is in. ( A one way trip from Halas to Greater Ferwith for the caster does not constitute a easy way back unless he can teleport himself back to a nearby zone.) If a caster can not port you for whatever reason, they may tell you no...so long as they are telling you no because they have something more important to do such as they are in a good group or a plane raid that is acceptable. They do not have to port you.

  • All spells should be cast on other guild members for free, although for some of the more mana draining spells a small fee paid as a courtesy. 

  • Kill stealing from other guild members is NOT allowed in any way shape or form. The penalty for this is severe and can range from item and monetary fines to expulsion from the guild. It is permissible however to help a guild member who's death is almost guaranteed as well as helping kill a creature whose life is well below half for this will not give you xp or looting rights.

  • Twinking is not discouraged so long as it is on a subtle level. Giving away patchwork or bronze weapons is fine, although making the newbies do some simple quest for them is recommended, but giving away a full suit of etched ivy armor and a staff of the observer is greatly discouraged. We DO NOT allow beggars into out ranks. If you are caught begging you will be warned or removed. We DO NOT support the weak.

  • Power leveling is also not discouraged on subtle levels. Bringing a character up 4 or 5 levels in a day is fine, but bringing a charter from level 1 to level 15 is allowed but discouraged because it leaves their skills at a loss and makes them a weaker player because of it. Also see twinking above.

 

Rules for acceptance

 

  1. All potential members must be of good knowlege of the game and of considerable ablity. For this reason all new members must be of the 20th pillar or higher in level. This only applys to your primary charater, all Secondary charaters in the guild can be of any level.
  2. All potential members must of a spell casting class. This includes the warrior hybrid type classes such as rangers, paladins, and shadow knights. Bards are also now allowed.
  3. There may be special circumstances for entry into the guild. If the officers do not know the prospective guild member, the potential member may be required to party with them, or do a small player run quest to prove dedication to the guild.
  4. To apply for membership you can ether be sponsored by a officer or a guild member and they can post your request on the message board. If being sponsored by a guild officer, you can be added to the guild at their digression. If being sponsored by a regular guild member, you must wait for them to submit your request ether on the web page, or via email and then approval of the majority of the officers must be made.
  5. All new characters must submit a email address to the guild leader along with character info. This can be done by emailing orderofmagi@niia.net . 

Officer Rules

  1. All officers must remember that they represent the guild finest and should act accordingly at all times.
  2. Officer status my be given to any outstanding member of the guild so long as a majority of the guild votes for this privilege.
  3. Guild status my be removed at anytime for improper use of your office. This includes bossing around members of the guild for the sheer joy of pushing them around, adding people who have no place in our guild such as warriors, and making a bad example of your self to the outside world.
  4. All officers may add a new guild member at will so long as the new member qualifies in the rules set down above.
  5. Upon the adding of a new member to the guild the guild leader should tell the new member to go to our web page and ether post a message stating who they are and what there email address is or email orderofmagi@niia.net and state who they are what level they are and their email address.
