The Neriak Alliance
by Jack Holt

Chapter 4 - Call To Arms

It took Dvinn two days, but he found the land of the Crushbone Orcs-or more accurately, the Orcs found him. A raiding party came across him in the darkness. They were expecting him, and immediately took him through the pass separating the Elven forest from the mountain stronghold the Orcs called home.

It was surprisingly small. Dvinn was also surprised at how neat it was. He had never seen Orcs take pride in their appearance or their property. Additionally, everything was well ordered. Regular patrols swept the land. Orc slavers drove work parties of captive Elves, Gnomes, and Humans from task to task. On a mountain in the middle of the stronghold a trainer worked with the young Orcs to ready them for battle. A mine, worked by slaves, provided a constant flow of treasure to the Castle that occupied the northeast portion of the stronghold.

A deep, clear mountain river surrounded the castle. Dvinn was taken across on a wooden bridge and turned over to the guards at the entrance. These Orcs were larger than the common Orcs and were designated as Legionnaires. They took him inside the castle entrance where Dvinn finally saw something he would have expected from Orcs. It was a fountain flowing with blood. The Legionnaires explained with pride how victims were regularly sacrificed, and their blood added to the fountain to keep it flowing.

Dvinn then was led into the throne room to meet King Crush. The Orc was huge, and hideous, with the most extended fangs Dvinn had ever seen on one of his kind. The throne he sat upon was carved from a single stone, and was 18 feet high. Flanking the throne were his Emissaries, Oracles, the crown prince Lord Darish, and the king's Warlord. This last Orc did not suppress at all his distaste for Dvinn. He glared at him, and barely nodded when he was introduced.

Dvinn bowed slightly before the king, and gave the communiqué to the King's Emissary. The Emissary promptly opened it and read it to the king. Only then did Dvinn understand why the Warlord hated him so. The king had agreed with Neriak that the Dragoon would lead the Orcs in their quest to destroy Kelethin.

A meal had been prepared, and once again Dvinn was reminded that in spite of the unique things he had observed earlier that these were still Orcs. They ate like a pack of wolves pulling apart a rabbit. Rancid blood was drunk freely, and the meal consisted of foul dead things-snakes, rats, Gnomes, and even one of their own kind. The ambassador's meal was provided by Elven slaves, and consisted of bread, honey, berries, some venison, and wine. He ate it thinking all the while of how glad he would be when he could return to Neriak.

After the meal the Orcs slept through the afternoon. As darkness fell they rose, and a meeting was called to complete the battle plans.

The overall plan was simple. The Orcs of Crushbone were to attack Kelethin, and draw the defenses towards Crushbone. Then the Neriak troops from Lesser Faydark would attack the undefended side of the city and complete its destruction by fire. The Orcs from Lesser Faydark would split into three bands. One would stay in Lesser Faydark to block any interference from the Gnomes. A second band would move to Felwithe and engage its defenders in a diversionary attack. The final band would march to cut off the Pass into Butcherblock so the Dwarves could not interfere. As long as they could keep the Wood Elves, High Elves, Dwarves, and Gnomes separated they could defeat each in turn.

The challenge that Dvinn, and "his" Orcs faced was not getting destroyed before the trap was sprung. It was essential for them to pull away as many of its defenders as possible; but they had to see to the security of Crushbone until the Elves realized the nature of the trap and fell back to defend the city.

An all out frontal attack would not do, but the Orcs knew little else when it came to battle. For several hours Dvinn argued for, and repeatedly explained his battle plan. Half the Orcs would stage an attack on the Orc Lift of the city, and then fall back through an ambush the rest of the Orcs would set up along the way. The elves would be taken by surprise. They would fight through the trap, but the time it took would be more than enough for the Neriak Regulars to burn the tree city to the ground.

Dvinn was not used to having his authority questioned when he made a battle plan, but the Orc Warlord was determined to save face and wrest control of the battle from the ambassador. Dvinn tried to ignore it, and then argued with him. He appealed to the king for a ruling, but the king remained silent.

"We should not split our force!" the Warlord said as he pounded the table. "The Elves are too strong. And I will not turn my back to them and run!" The other Orcs agreed with claps and whistles.

Dvinn had no choice. He would have to assert his authority as if he were an Orc. He rose on a pretense of refilling his cup of wine as the jeering died down.

"Hail, King Crush! I will lead our troops in battle. This ambassador is a coward!"

It was the last words the Warlord would ever speak. In a quick motion Dvinn buried his dirk in the throat of the huge creature. He then seized his sword, and hewed a great gash in its chest. Reaching in, he tore the still beating heart from its chest and threw it on the ground at the king's feet.

There was a moment of total silence. The king's face then slowly drew back in a hideous grin. "Brethren! We have a new Warlord!"

The Orcs cheered and shouted in their foul language, and then descended as one on the body of the former Warlord to devour it.


Niffun was surprised when he awoke in a warm room in Kelethin instead of the forest where he had lost consciousness. Maesyn Trueshot was leaning over him and casting healing spells. Ahnog stood at the bottom of the bed looking concerned, and then relieved when he saw the Halfling open his eyes.

"Welcome back little Niffun," Trueshot said softly and with affection.

"Indeed!" the Dwarf bellowed.

"You are ok," Tureshot said. "With a little rest and food you will be up and around in a few hours."

"The Neriaks! The Orcs! They are coming," Niffun blurted out as he struggled to rise. The Ranger and the Dwarf pressed him back down.

"We know. Ahnog has told us everything. While you were recovering we tracked the Neriak you fought to Crushbone, and messengers have been sent to the High Elves, the Gnomes, and the Dwarves. We are preparing for the assault here. Everything will be fine."

"The Neriak is not just any Neriak Master Trueshot! He is an ambassador and a Dragoon. He resisted all my spells, and he's…"

"Yes, yes," the old Ranger interrupted. "Ahnog has told us."

"The Stormguard will defeat him, his cohorts, and the Orcs," Ahnog said.

"Maesyn, I let him get away. I failed to the get the teleportation device."

"What more could you have done? You survived and we have been warned. You are a hero Niffun!"

"I don't feel like one. I feel… I feel little."

"Nonsense!" Ahnog snorted. "The size of a person is not measured by his stature but by his heart. You took on the Dragoon alone and survived. Many giants have fallen before them."

"I was foolish, and I left you alone. I was wrong. Forgive me?"

"There is nothing to forgive! You did the right thing."

"Thank you Ahnog for saving my life."

"We are even now," the Dwarf said with a smile.

Trueshot stood up. "I need to go. There is much to be done to prepare for the battle. Rest. We will need you."
