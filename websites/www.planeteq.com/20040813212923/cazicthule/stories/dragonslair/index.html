Dragon's Lair
by Delrachnid (Twelve Year)

(To put things in perspective I am an 18th level Dark Elven Rogue)

I have long heard of a fabled Dragon within the land of Norrath and its existence was something that I wanted to make known. Not for any glorious battle to be won. Not for the sake of being the first to lay eyes on it. But I, like many of my dark companions, have sought out the answer to this folktale for one pure simple reason...treasure.

I have loved the coin for quite some time now and Innoruuk knows that a Dragon hoards it with vengeful jealousy according to most tales that I have heard. Should this creature be the only being to have such wealth? I and many of my kind disagree. So I set out to find it. Heading towards, what was believed to be its rumored location, I brought with me a companion that would travel with me to the mouth of the cave, but not within. The area of the Lavastorm mountains was warm to the skin. Some might find it uncomfortable, but nothing that I wasn't use to living underground. Gazing upon a pit of lava I noticed the cave entrance embedded within the rock face. Waving the Enchanter to follow behind me, I walked cautiously along the pit's edge to reach the entrance. I looked into my companion's eyes as I handed over most of my belongings to make it easier for me to traverse within. I assured him that if I was to die that I expected my items to be stored until such time that Innoruuk deemed it necessary for me to return.

As a parting blessing the air around the Enchanter began to distort and I felt my very skin fading away from normal sight. Indeed this was a good way to start.

Holding my breath I headed in. Immediately I was beseiged by the ramblings of the denizens within. Howling echoed throughout this lair as creatures lined the hallways, thankfully ignorant of my existence. I quietly ruffled through my pack in search of my detailed scoutings of this area, but realized that this place was somehow missed. I should be in Solusek's Eye I mused to myself, but this was not it. Listening again to the ramblings I heard mention of Nagafen and concluded that this realm (ooc:zone) was known as Nagafen's Lair.

Feeling a bit uncomfortable and not wanting the Enchanter's magic to fade in this den of beasts, I ran forward, twisting my way through hallways and passages. My ability to track where I have traveled faded as the paths became more intertwined. Finally I was greeted, amidst the deafening howling, with a passageway that looked different from the rest. It felt as if it was pulling me to a different realm.

Gathering myself after feeling disoriented I looked around cautiously. Now this was a place that struck some familiarity. This looked like Solusek's Eye. Strange, however, that it was completely deserted. There were plenty of paths to be explored, hidden walls that led to doors that could not be opened by any normal means, and even an occasional faded colored wall that looked as if it had been placed there magically. Pressing forward I ran through this area confident that I would not be bothered. The layout of the place was incredible. Strange drawings and even stranger symbols lined the walls. The whole place had the appearance of being a deserted camp that was once inhabited by a clan of some sort that worshipped fire.



Then I saw it. My first indication that the fabled beast exists! On a wall within one of the many rooms hung a map. Detailed on it were passages and corridors leading to an open area where a crudely drawn image of a dragon was placed. Inspiring. My heart took a jump as I sketched this evidence into some loose parchment that I brought along. Leaving the room I began to envision this map along with the layout of this area. Not making any discernable connection I decided to do what any confused adventurer would. Run about wildly. After some time I came upon another passageway that seemed to draw me into it. Upon exiting onto the other side I had the feeling I was again in a familiar place. Nagafen's Lair. But this was not the same lair I had come from originally. Somehow it looked different. And different it was!



This area was dark and ominous. The flutter of wings could be heard close by as well as the scurrying of many-legged creatures. Cobwebs lined the walls and quite a few victims could be seen cocooned within the webs. Not wanting to join them in their demise I quickly moved on. Scared would be the key emotion in this realm. Although invisible to normal eyes I had a gut wrenching feeling that my life was close to ceasing. Turning a corner I saw a strange doorway in the distance. Instinctively knowing that this doorway led to that which I had come to seek the most I decided to press forward cautiously. Making sure I was still out of sight I crept towards the opening. Two very large shapes began to take form and my step faltered.



Fire Giants! I cursed my luck as I gazed upon their visages. They didn't seem to notice me, but the fear they brought about in me was very noticable. Just as I was about to turn around and retreat to safer pastures, a mysterious calling possessed my spirit. The call of the coin. I looked long and hard at the doorway ahead of me and then just as long at the two hulking brutes that guarded its existence and the treasures that lay beyond. The unbelievable treasures I thought. Before I knew what I was doing my feet ran me forward towards the doorway and in between the Fire Giants. As both of the beasts' large and powerful fists lowered onto my still invisible head I could only think of one thing before finally crumpling to my death...Dragon treasure.
