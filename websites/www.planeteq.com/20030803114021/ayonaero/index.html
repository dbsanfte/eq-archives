    Updates for August 6 Patch
    7/30/03, 10:44 PST | Kwill

    Now all those times when you had to switch plat from one character to another and had to ask someone to help you, at your peril sometimes, are gone, after August 6 -- shared platinum in the bank. Also, they will implement a command to memorize a spell set, another excellent improvement. But that's not all, there are a number of useful and interesting improvement in this patch:

    From EQLive::

    Upcoming for August 6th, 2003

    It's been a little hectic around here lately, with all the Live, Legends and expansion work going on. We're busy in that good way, where you get a lot done every day but tomorrow there's just as much to do.

    New adventure unearthed in Lavastorm! In the depths of the Lavastorm mountains there is only one ruler. But even his burning breath and power have their limits. Rumor has it that there is wealth and danger in locations even further below the boiling lands than Lord Nagafen can reach. These tales have been spreading like ripples on a calm lake, and their point of origin appears to be an upstart barbarian named Morden. Some say that he's already delved these fabled caverns and taken what there was of value. Other say that he couldn't have carried it all off by himself. But most say that he's a rogue, a teller of tales, and not to be trusted. Will you discover the truth and brave the heat, or will you sit beside your cozy campfire and listen to stories?

    We will be adding platinum to the Shared Bank. In order to allow folks to transfer money between characters on the same account in the same way that they can now transfer items, we will be adding a spot in the Shared Bank for placing platinum.

    New filters and chat message options will be added. We have separated pet melee messages into their own category. You will be able to send them to whichever chat window you want, or filter them out completely in the Options window. We have also added the ability to send /random messages to any chat window you'd like. There is no option to filter these messages, however, to prevent any loss of the information in cases where it is needed (meaning, you can't claim that you didn't see the /random that you lost just to force a re-roll).

    New fletching recipes will be added. We're adding a few new fletching recipes. The resultant items will only be usable by Rangers that worship Karana, and will be comparable to their Tunare counterparts.

    Guktan Illusion will be implemented. Yup, the long-awaited new illusions will be available. While the old Illusion: Froglok spell is just as popular as ever, folks have been asking for an illusion based on the new Froglok models. After our next update, both will be available.

    Adding summoned Burning Affliction items to the Magician's arsenal. Four new summoned items with the new Burning Affliction focus ability will be introduced. This rounds out the Magician's ability to create summoned focus items.

    /memspellset command will be added. This new command will allow you to memorize the spell set you indicate (using the corresponding number) from the command line or from a hot button.

    Also, as a heads up to players that have created their own user interfaces for EverQuest, these are the interface files that we expect to change next week.

    EQUI_BankWnd.xml
    EQUI_BigBankWnd.xml
    EQUI_OptionsWindow.xml

    LDoN: Raiding
    7/30/03, 10:42 PST | Kwill

    From EQLive:
    Lost Dungeons of Norrath - Raid Content
    In designing Lost Dungeons of Norrath, we haven't forgotten about those who enjoy raiding. In addition to the multitude of rewarding single-group adventures, LDoN also includes exciting and challenging events targeted at six groups or less.

    For the first time, raids take place in truly private zones, where your groups can test their mettle and focus solely on the task at hand. Instead of limiting the availability of content by who can get there first, this new system only limits participation based on whether or not you have recently succeeded or failed at each event within a given period. This allows us to place a heavy emphasis on skill, while remaining in control of the influx of new items into the game.

    While our primary focus for LDoN is the single group experience, we are working diligently to ensure that these new raids are centered around the principles of organizational tactics, individual skill, and most importantly - fun.

    We feel that the core content and rewards in the Lost Dungeons of Norrath expansion will entertain and benefit even the most experienced players. We are aware of the desire for high-end raid content and will continue to address and meet those needs over time.

    - The EverQuest Team

    EQ Players in Combat
    7/28/03, 11:34 PST | Kwill

    A nice article by the Syndicate guild:
    EQ Gamers Go To War

    The war in Iraq has touched the lives of many gamers. Guildmates and friends have gone off to war and some of us have lost friends and loved ones as a result. Regardless of where you line up on the politics of the war, no one wants to see the young men and women, doing what they are trained and ordered to do, getting killed. As such, the war has touched the lives of many gamers.

    For The Syndicate the war has an especially personal meaning. A number of their members have already served in Iraq or are currently serving there. Others are destined to be deployed there to relieve troops that will rotate home.

    Syndicate members have served in a number of exciting and critical roles in the war effort. One member was a member of the same unit to which Pvt. Jessica Lynch belonged and that units tragedy and ultimate triumph was personally historic to them. Another member commands a unit of the 1st Armored Division in downtown Bagdhad coming under attack on an almost daily basis. Another member is with a Marine combat unit at an unknown location inside Iraq. Others serve in the Airforce flying missions over Iraq. There are those serving on Aircraft Carriers and other ships of the navy. And still others in all branches of the service havent been heard from in awhile so their location in Iraq and status remains unknown.

    For some gamers, online friends are just pixels on the screen that you see in game occasionally and hunt with. But for a group like The Syndicate, that isnt a guild as much as it is an online community/family, the online friendships take on a much deeper meaning.

    It is for that reason we are proud of the recognition we received for our informal support of a unit of the 1st Armored Division by supplying them with many necessities they couldnt get elsewhere. Safe and sound at home, we take alot of things for granted like cool drinking water. Our troops in Bagdhad are drinking 80+ degree water that is nearly undrinkable without adding some flavor to it since there is no refrigeration available to cool the water from the 110 degree daily temperatures. There is no running water for showers so staying clean is a real challenge. And morale among the troops who sleep in the dirt, live with bugs and get shot at daily is an ever present challenge. Being thousands of miles from home, it can be difficult to remember that there are people who care if you make it back alive. So The Syndicate was very proud to be able to do a small part to help one unit cope with those issues. That help is nothing compared to the sacrifice they are prepared to make but it was very special to be recognized for that help by that unit of the 1AD.

    We are equally proud when the 772nd Expeditionary Airlift Squadron flew an American Flag over Iraq, in honor of The Syndicate, and then presented it to us. That flag holds special meaning to our guild and is proudly displayed on our website.

    As more than one hundred people set off for our 2003 Syndicate World Conference, our members who remain in harms way and those who are soon to join them, remain foremost in our thoughts. Regardless of your political views on the war, perhaps you can take a moment out of your day to remember the men and women, fellow gamers and friends of yours, who are in Iraq and in harms way and hope that they will make it back safely to their families as rapidly as possible.
