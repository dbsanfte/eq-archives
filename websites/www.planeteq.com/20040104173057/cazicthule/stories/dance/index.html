The Dance
by Grayeagle CloudRunner, Blade Dancer

Airiel (my wife -RL-) and I were light of foot,
glad to be away from the snow and darkness of the North
where I had shown her my growing place.

The miles coursed under our feet as we ran with Spirit of the Wolf
across Everfrost, through the Black Burrow, across the Hills of Qeynos,
and into the Western Plains of Kanara.

High Keep was our goal, and closer by the moment.
We decided to take a small risk,
Airiel camo'd herself while I ran in full view..
I was 23rd Warrior, a proven Wolf Brother from Halas,
and I knew nothing here I could not kill or run from.
Little did I know ...

We streaked east, direct,
the shortest run tween the borders of the land.
Comin upon hills we continued our run,
toppin the second I spied a stone arrangement
down in the fog of the valley below.
Without breakin stride I began veering left,
what was that creature below?
An Ogre! He had friends!
I was glad I had cut far left
as I came to passin the camp.

Airiel! My lady was hit and near dead!
I slammed to a stop and whirled in time to see her fall!
Halberd at the ready I charged the evil foe,
intending to stand upon his guts or die beside m'lady.
I am a barbarian.
It is what I do.

I was charmed as a pet to Cheif Goon,
an Ogre who rules that valley.
He it was who struck down Airiel.
I was in the red heat of battle lust
.. even so helpless as a cur waitin on scraps from his master.
I waited.. halberd at the ready, for the charm to break.
I would whet my blade on this 'cheif' of evil.

Charm break, halberd strike, the dance was joined.
All too soon his minions joined the fray
..I went down with my blade well fed,
I had taken a third of the Cheifs life.

This began the trial
..Airiel and I tried for hours to retrieve our bodies.
After 2 more deaths for me,
2 more for the lady Airiel,
..we had succeeded in retreiving Airiel.

She had died at the outer edge of the camp.
I lay at the Cheifs feet.

This is when friends began to hear, and join in a plan.
First Fraye the Druid came to help.
Then Inana, the Lady of Steel and a healer of legend.
Fraye camo'd me and gave me his best running Spirit.
I made a pass through the edge of the camp, grabbing my corpse,
and running like the Wolf south.

The Cheif saw me, invisible as I was to the rest..
..and I died yet again, yet not at his feet, no.
Up the southern hill we had run down hours before.

Inana took charge then..'No more dyin here!'
as she gathered her friends from the corners of the world.

I could do no less, and began to call friends I had grown with..
..from Greater Fay they came, teleported by
Soonar Orlater, Cheif Wizard of the Damned.
Came Bishonen Wizard Friend, Arestes Ranger, Latep Warrior, Dede Paladin,
even Ered Healer, who had heard of my plight.

Soonar said 'We need more warriors,
for these will end us even so!'
after spyin the strength of the evil we faced.

Inana saved us all.
'Wait,' she said ..
'..I have the warriors we need comin!'

Lo.. came Ulath Ironarm!
Kalanon! Scytheiea! Nekoj! Tobias!

These were Legends of the Land,
heard of but never seen by any of us before!

The gatherin for the dance was done.

Inana stated the plan.
The Legends would take the Cheif
and his Shamans..
the rest would take the guards.
No Evil would survive the dance.

Kalanon commented that this was overkill.
'Overkill is Guud!' I exclaimed,
for I would see this evil dead
in their tracks as payback this day.

The two nearby Cyclops wandered near,
and died for their curiosity.
Almost trivial was the dance with them.
It was a small warmup.

'We will take the Cheif and his Shamans,
..the rest of you take the guards'
ordered Kalanon
.. and so the plan of Inana Starshire was enforced.

The dance began.

I have never seen so many ogres
come at me at once in all my journeys.
Bracing for the charge
the smash of armor, blade, and meat
blended with the ground tremble of their rush
in a crescendo of mayhem.

Spells lit the evening sky
and the dance was joined.

Fraye the Valiant was first to die,
followed quickly by Ered, then Latep,
dying as the Cheif himself went down!

M'lady Airiel was feared, and ran.
Arestes and I were both charmed..
I cut Ulath Ironarm down from behind,
striking cowardly as the Cheif would fight.

Released on the Cheifs death,
Arestes and I were truly whirling death,
incensed with rage at bein used.
Guards and Shaman pets fell in our dance,
the last bein the Wolf ..the Cheifs pet.

The evil was dead.
Inana resurrected those who had fallen,
even unto drawing Latep
all the way back from Greater Faydark!

The Dance was Grand.
Legends were made,
the Battle won..
people came to help
just because they could.

I owe much.
