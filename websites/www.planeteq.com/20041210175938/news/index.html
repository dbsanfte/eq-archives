EQ2: Heroic Opportunities
12/8/04, 6:58 PST | Kwill

Guide to Heroic Opportunities (HOs)

by Beck

There you are, outside Stepford City with your fellow adventurers bashing away on bears trying to complete your Clothspinner's armor quest and your group for some reason is getting angry at you for not paying attention to "the wheel". What wheel? Oh that thing in the lower righthand corner of my screen? That my friend, is a heroic opportunity wheel.

Heroic opportunities are buffs and/or enhancements that you will receive if you complete the wheel successfully. They are integral to gameplay and can greatly affect the rapidity of your killing. When soloing, your ability to manage the wheel will be the deciding factor in most cases whether you live or die by that orange con mob you picked a fight with.

The basic heroic opportunity icons on the wheel appear four colors, depending on your archetype:

Blue-Fighters
Green-Scouts
Red-Mages
Yellow-Priests

The middle icon in the wheel delineates the order:

Round Arrow: Icons must be done in order for the heroic opportunity to be successful.

Starburst pattern arrows: Icons can be done in any order.

For example, you are fighting and notice a round arrow wheel with a yellow cup flashing in the wheel, followed by a green cape. This is a starter chain. What this means is the priest in the group needs to cast a group heal with a scout type class in the group backstabbing/sneak attacking for the heroic opportunity chain to be successful. Because it is the round arrow it needs to be done IN ORDER. If it is not done in order, you failed.

Normally you will see an icon flashing in your hotkey bar which corresponds to what is required for the wheel when it is your turn. Unfortunately combat can happen quickly in Everquest 2 and if you aren't paying attention you can miss your chance on the wheel and fail at completing the order and earning yourself or your group a buff or enhancement.

If you are lucky, you will see a coin icon flashing in the wheel. No, this does mean everyone gets money if you complete the chain successfully! It means you have triggered a heroic opportunity shift. This gives the scout in your group a chance to shift to another heroic opportunity using combat arts. It can trigger a rare enhancement if completed correctly. The Scout has to act quickly and has to comlete it before any others have been used to advance the wheel.

gnorrath.com is an indepth site for Heroic Opportunities with much more information, so if you want to learn more, please give them a visit!

EQ2 Patch for Dec.3, Dec. 7, 2004
12/7/04, 11:12 PST | Muskie

Update Notes: December 7, 2004 12/7/2004 3:00 am

Today’s changes include the following:

*** Gameplay ***

- Harm Touch no longer has any chance to fizzle when cast.
- The power cost associated with Shadowknight lifetap procs has been removed.
- The Tainted Caress combat scroll is now available for purchase from the appropriate trainers.
- Experience rewards are now consistent with NPC con colors across all level ranges.
- You can now trade your baby dragon house pet.
- The remainder of the missing suspensions and solutions have been added to the appropriate vendors.
- The boat voyage to the Enchanted Lands will now only allow six players.

The EverQuest II Team

 

Update Notes: December 4, 2004 12/4/2004 3:00 am

Today’s changes include the following:

- The Deflection skill should now be working properly for brawlers, monks, and bruisers.
- The mariner's bell leading to Everfrost has been adjusted to allow those who have already completed the access quest to assist others who have not.
- Firemyst Gully is once again available for play. A quest found within the zone has been adjusted to better reflect its intended challenge.
- The amount of experience rewarded in the Emery Seastrider's Supply Run quest has been lowered, while the amount of coin has been increased. Additionally, the quest now targets sheep instead of wolves.
- Corrected an issue causing players to occasionally wind up in odd locations when exiting their house or other small zone.

The EverQuest II Team

   Saturday, December 4, 2004

EQ2 Patch: Dec 3, 2004
12/4/04, 3:21 PST | Muskie

Update Notes: December 3, 2004 12/3/2004 7:30 am


Today’s changes include the following:


*** Zones ***

- The connections between Vermin's Snye and the Crypt of Betrayal should now be working correctly.


*** Items ***

- Archetype quest rewards on the Isle of Refuge have been changed to NO TRADE and NO VALUE.
- The Black Tainted armor pieces from the Tomb of Valor had their stats reduced and level ranges lowered to be more appropriate for the encounter level range.


*** Tradeskills ***

- Tradeskill recipe books no longer drop on the Isle of Refuge.
- The minimum skill required to harvest objects in Antonica and Commonlands has been lowered.
- A new crop is in bloom! Blue succulents are now harvestable in Nektulos Forest and Thundering Steppes.


*** Spells, Combat Arts, Skills, and Abilities ***

- Inquisitor: Favor of the Repentant spell line's healing component per attack functions once again.
- Wizard: Lightning Flash - Spell damage was adjusted to be more in line with comparable AoE damage spells.
- Wizard: Benumb - Casting time was reduced to one second, reuse time was increased to twenty seconds.
- Wizard: Blazing Intimidation - Casting time was reduced from four seconds to two seconds.
- Druid, Cleric, and Shaman summoned Priest resurrection items should function more reliably and only revive Priest professions.
- Strangling Shot should now be working correctly.
- Taunt abilities have been made more effective.
- Monks and Bruisers can now wield round shields in addition to bucklers.
- Combat arts now have the same probability to hit difficult encounters as spells.
- A maximum limit has been placed on the effectiveness of skill buffs and debuffs.
- Both NPCs and players now do more damage at level 20 and above. Damage and healing spells have been increased in potency accordingly.
- NPC and PC hit points at level 20 and higher have been increased.


*** UI ***

- Fixed "Remove Friend" and "Remove Ignore" buttons on the Community Window.
- Fixed using /tell button in Community Window so it works properly if you have sorted the player list.


The EverQuest II Team
