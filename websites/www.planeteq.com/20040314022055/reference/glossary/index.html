EverQuest Glossary of Terms
EQ certainly has a language of it's own! Slang is something that changes constantly, but here are some terms to get you started in the game. Thank you to Boanerges and Malachy from the Cazic Thule server for their contributions!


10, 20, 30 etc: refers to the amount of mana left in the caster's mana bar

AA Ding: achieving a new alternate advancement level or skill

AA: alternative advancement points

AC: armor class

Aego: Aegolism, a high level cleric buff

AFK: away from keyboard--the person won't be responding to any tells you send them

Aggro Range: how far away the monster is before it will attack you

Aggro: from "aggression," making the monster mad at you and attack. When an NPC that detects a player it is KOS to or is attacked, it is said to have become agro. Also refers to whom the NPC is attacking (ie "I am agro").

Anon: playing without showing level or class

AOE group: Area of effect spell group, generally a group of wizards that take down mobs with AOE spells and no melee characters

Assist: using the /assist player command to make sure all players are fighting the same monster

Avo: Avocet, a type of bird monster

Back Spawn: when monsters you have already killed reappear behind you as you move through a zone

Barbie: a barbarian

Bazaar: zone where people buy and sell in the Luclin expansion.

BB: Blackburrow zone

Beta: EverQuest before it went into commercial production, beta test. Very old school players were in the beta.

Book: portal to the Planes of Power zones.

Boss Mob: refers to the biggest monster in the zone or encounter. Usually named.

Box: one individual using two or more computers to play more than one character at time simultaneously. Commonly you will see 2 boxing and 4 boxing.

BP: breastplate

BRB: be right back

Break mez: to attack a mesmerized monster either intentionally or unintentionallyBrells = Group divine Strength

Buff: add powerful character enhancements from other players or ones self

Burn: When a caster chain casts nukes to aid in the killing of a mob. Can be a command (ie "Burn the mob down")

BW: Burning Woods zone in Kunark

C2 - C5: Refers to the Enchanter line of clarity and its successors. (ie Tranquility = C4)

Camp Check: shouted out to the zone to determine where a group can go to get experience

Camp: a particular spot in a zone where players normally stop and fight for long periods of time. Most camps have names made up by players to describe them (Bar/Arm is the bartender/armorer area in Old Sebilis, for instance). Camps can spring up anywhere that players set up to kill for an extended period of time.

Caster: any class that depends primarily on spells to deal damage and/or survive (old definition could include any class since technically all 15 classes can use spells thanks to clickable armor).

CH: Chain heal. A strategy used to fight upper level monsters in high level encounters, where the clerics heal the main melee one after the other

CH: complete heal

Chain pulling: a feign death player uses his skill to grab more than one mob, and then has another player grab just one and bring it back to the group.

Chanter: enchanter class

Channel: refers to chat channels in the game that can be set up by players

CoH: a spell that allows mages to summon a member of their party to them. If it is far enough, the summoned member can lose agro

Coffin: players can purchase coffins which are used by necromancers to recover corpses in a zone

Con: consider the level of difficulty of the monster. Cons Blue: good experience monster. Cons even: at the same level as the player (also even con). Cons Green: too low a level. Cons Light Blue: just under the optimum experience level for the player. Cons Red: very high level for the player

Corpse recovery: getting someone's corpse out of an unsafe area where they died to a safe area. Also CR.

Crack: mana enhancing spells for casters, given by enchanters

Cultural armor: specific armor made for various races

Cyber: to have virtual sex with another player over tells

DA: Divine Aura. A spell that allows clerics and paladins to become invunerable for 18 seconds. Prevents negative and positive spells from landing as well

DC: dire charm pet, used by high level enchanters

DD: direct damage

DE: dark elf

Ding: achieving a new level

Donation: player wants cash for his or her services

Don't Train Us: a request that a player coming through doesn't bring mobs with them to attack a group

Dot: Damage over time effect

Dots: peridots, used by clerics to buff players

Drow: a dark elf

DS: Divine Strength Paladin HP Buff

DS: damage shield

DT: Death touch

EB: enduring breath for underwater swimming

Ebay: characters that are bought on the auction service and not owned by the original account creator. Not a complement. Ebayed: the character was sold or bought on the auction service

Enrage: when a mob enrages, they ripost every blow, so players remind each other when the mob enrages

Epic: the epic quest weapon

ETA: estimated time of arrival

Exp: experience

Exploit: unintended consequence of an action. Exploits are against the rules of the game and players can be reprimanded by a GM for using them. An example is using fear to send a monster into a wall where they get stuck for an easy kill.

Faction: your standing with various NPCs in the game. Depends on race, religion and who you have allied yourself with in the game

Farm: hunting in an area for items, not for experience. Generally refers to getting multiple items from that area

FD: feign death. The player appears dead to the monsters, and they do not attack. Monks and Shadowknights have this ability. FD puller: feign death puller. A strategy used to split monsters up so the puller can bring just one to the group at a time.

FFA: free for all looting. Players can loot at will when monsters are dead.

Flag: achieved in the Planes of Power through specific encounters, it indicates a player can move on to other Planes in the expansion

FM: full mana for the caster. Used when reporting mana status to the group

FMB: full mana burn

Gem: any precious gems found on monsters

Ghetto Mez: techniques to subdue monsters that are used by players other than an enchanter.

GM: Game Master employed by Sony OnLine Entertainment

Gore: Gornaire, a dragon

Group Bugged: can't disband or leave a group because of technical difficulties

Group Hug: group wide buffs given by a player

GS: groupsay, means use the group say option when talking

GY: graveyard. In some Planes of Power, corpses all end up at the graveyard area after about 20 minutes

Hotkey: programming hotkeys to cast spells and/or give a message

HP: hit points

HT: Harm Touch, a powerful shadowknight spell for doing damage to a target

Hybrid: Class that can melee for damage but has a group of spells from a parent caster class

Inc, Incoming: the puller has found a monster and is bringing it to the group

Inky: a dark elf

Invis: invisibility

IRL: in real life

IV: infravision, the ability to see in the dark

IVU: invisibility to undead

J/K: just kidding

Jboots: journeyman's boots, an old world speed enhancer

KC: Karnors Castle in Dreadlands

KEI, C3: high level mana enhancing spell that can be cast on other players by an enchanter

Key: various keys needed to enter certain zones.

KFC: the bird tower in South Karana. Stands for "Kentucky Fried Chicken."

Kite, Kiting: snaring and / or rooting Mobs and slowly killing them or running them around while raid kills them. Druids are known for "quad kiting" four monsters at one time

Kitty Feet: feet like the cat, a druid spell

Kneebiter: smaller race character

KOS: kill on sight

KS, Kill Steal: When a mob has been engaged by a person or party and another person or party outdamages them and earns the experience and looting rights. Can be grounds for suspension or expulsion from EQ

Lag: a server problem that makes playing slow down on the user's end

Land, Landing: Refers to the success of a spell being cast (ie "I have landed malo on a moss snake")

LD: link death, being disconnected from the server

LFG: looking for a group.

LFTG: Looking for trials group. Refers to the trials encounter in the Plane of Justice, an over 60 encounter.

Lift: the elevators in the wood elf city of Kelethin

Link it: right click on an item's picture so other people can see the description

List: when a particular camp is desirable, players ask to have their names put on a list to be the next person in line for the group. The List Holder keeps the list of names.

Loc: using the location command to navigate. Locs are given in two parts, indicating a positive or a negative on each (pos 1400, neg 750)

Lock the Corpse: keeping a dead monster's inventory open so the right person gets the loot. Done to prevent ninja looting.

Loot: the rewards and items held on a monster

Lore: you can't have more than one of the item

Lowbie: a new player

MA: main assist. Refers to the practice of assisting the melee character that is in charge of doing the most damage. The MA is the person usually with the most hit points and armor class.

Mage: magician

Mana Sponge: when someone takes a great amount of healing in a group. Not a complement.

MB, ManaBurn: using all the player's mana in one kill

Mem: to memorize a spell

Mez: to mesmerize a monster so it does not attack

MGB: Mass Group Buff

Mistell: when a private message goes to the wrong person

MOB: mobile object -- a monster

MQ or multiquest: more than one person helps to complete a quest. Items can be bought as part of a multiquest sale, in which the seller for a fee helps complete the quest.

MR: magic resistance. For equipment and buffs from other players.

Naggy: Lord Nagafen, a dragon

Named: a monster with a specific name or title that usually will drop better loot

ND: Natural Durability AA skill (sometimes has a number 1,2,3 after it indicating skill level)

Necro: necromancer

Nerf: changing an item or an attribute making it less powerful or effective

Newbie Lift: the elevator to Kelethin where new wood elves come down to Greater Faydark to fight

Newbie log: a specific fallen tree log in Neriak Forest where dark elf newbies fight

Newbie: someone new to the game

Ninja loot: grabbing an item quickly away from someone who legitimately deserved to loot it. Can result in suspension from the game if a GM intervenes.

NoDrop: item cannot be traded or sold

No-Rent: the item will disappear after the player logs off

NP: no problem

NPC: non player character

Nuke: Refers to direct damage spells; nuking is used to describe when a caster starts to cast DD spells.

Old School: players that have been in the game for a long time

On Me: something is hitting a caster or the cleric

OOM: out of mana. Not a good thing when you are fighting.

Own: to kill something with very little damage to oneself

Pages: spell components used by enchanters to make spells, found on monsters.

Pally: paladin

Parchment: a no-drop item found in the planes of power that can be turned in for a 60+ level class spell.

PC: player character

Placeholder: a monster that spawns in the place of a named mob

Planes: any planar zone in the old world or the Planes of Power. Generally abbreviated with the initials.

Port: getting ported around to different zones by druids or wizards

Powerlevel: to help someone achieve levels by assist with other higher level characters

PP: platinum, the highest level of coinage in the game

PST: please send tell

Pull: to bring a monster to the group

Puller: person in charge of getting the monster to the group

Raid: a large group of players getting together to fight in a zone. Also refers to setting up a raid with the raid function where more than 6 people can join a group encounter and talk to one another as well as get experience.

Respawn: when a certain monster appears again after a certain amount of time

Reverse Kite: When a mob is snared and feared. Allows the caster(s) to avoid being damaged while attacking the mob.

Rez: ressurection, and gaining back of lost experience, when a player has died. Often specified by the amount of experience gained back from the level of ressurection.

RL: real life

Role Play: to try to get into character during playing.

Roll: using the /random command to roll "dice" on an item to determine a winner. The random command produces a number for each character in a range specified by the group leader. Higest roll wins. Most high end items are rolled for if the group does not know one another. See Seed.

Root: to use a spell to immobilize a monster

Seed, set the seed: using the /random command to set a number players can random to for looting rights to an item. Used as, "the seed is 785." Players then execute the command, /random 0 785 to get their number. The "dice" is rolled by the server and the group sees the number each person has "rolled."

SLW: swift like the wind

SOE: Sony Online Entertainment

SoE: Spirit of Eagle, adds levitation to the speed spell

SolA, SolB: zones in Lavastorm, officially known as Solskek's Eye and Nagafen's Lair

Sold: a character was sold to someone else to play

Soloing: playing by oneself

SoW: Spirit of the Wolf, fast running spell

Spam: to broadcast something over and over

Spires: refers either to the wizard portals which look like spires, or the spires in South Karana where the gnolls gather

Split: getting just one monster from a group

STA: Stamina

STFU: shut the f--k up.

Symbol: refers to the symbol line of buffs clerics and paladins can cast on themselves and other players. Usually costs a gem to cast

Tank: a class that does damage through melee but also keeps an NPC's attention focused on themselves while having the necessary AC to take less damage than normal players (old definition covered all 15 classes since any class can melee).

Tash: Tashania. A spell line that reduces Magic Resistance.

Taunt: players with a taunt skill use it to keep agro on themselves

Tell: private messaging

To loot: to take the items off the monster when it's dead

TOV: Temple of Veeshan, people refer to the armor aquired here as the industry standard (you want ToV grade or better)

Trader, Trader mode: when a player character is selling goods in the bazaar, they are in trader mode.

Train to Zone!: player is bringing a large number of monsters to the zone out area and innocent bystanders could be wiped out.

Train: a large group of NPCs following a PC

Trak: Trakenon, a dragon

Trans: translocation by a wizard, which means the wizard can send you toa wizard portal without leaving his location

Trash, yard trash: lower level monsters usually at the beginning of a zone or playing area

Tree Hugger: druid character

Trinket: loot found on monsters that is sold to NPC vendors. Usually used when doing high level encounters to differentiate between the loot people are wanting and loot that isn't worth much.

Twink: a person who has armor and equipment far above what they could get at their level.

Twist: a bard's ability to sing up to four songs at once during combat

TY: thank you

Uber: very powerful

Uberguild: a powerful guild on a server, usually doing high end encounters

Vendui: hello in Dark elf speech (Drow)

VI: Verant Interactive, the original makers of EQ. Now called SOE

Virtue: a high level cleric buff

Vox: Lady Vox, a dragon

Wanderer: a monster than travels randomly around an area, or a monster that is traveling around a camp spot

Words: spell components found on monsters used in player crafting of spells.

WTB, WTS, WTT: want to buy, want to sell, want to trade

Zerg: Sometimes derogatory, refers to the tactic of sending in an unusually large group of people to attack an NPC.

Zone: EQ's various areas are broken up into areas known as zones. Crossing a zone line can remove you from harm in the zone you just left. When used as a command, this means you should cross the nearest zone line as soon as possible. It is also used as a reference point (ie "Train to zone!")
