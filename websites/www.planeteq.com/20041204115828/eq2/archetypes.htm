Archetypes in EQ2

Choose your profession! When you arrive at the Isle of Refuge, one of the first things you will have to do is choose what type of citizen of Norrath you will become.

Some races do better than others in certain professions. Before choosing a profession, make sure the race you have chosen fits with your playing choice.

What class should I choose?
That depends on your personality. Do you like to socialize? Then choose a group friendly class. Do you want to solo? Make sure you choose a class that has the attributes that allow you to go out alone. Do you want to be a support member of a group? Or the bravest one who goes out and explores, or fights the monsters while others heal you? Do you want to help others? Do you like to fight on the front lines, or stay back and deal damage while assisting the main melee tank?

As a melee fighter, you will be expected to go out and gather mobs into the group ("pull") and fight them while the priests heal and the casters support you with spells. Some melee classes don't "pull" but assist in direct damage. As a priest, you will be expected to pay attention to the health and well being of other players at all time and heal and buff when necessary. As a scout, you will be expected to explore new places and be brave enough to go out and perhaps die finding new places to fight or explore. As a mage, you will be expected to help the group with your spell powers, and particularly with crowd control when fighting multiple monsters.

Fighter
A pure melee character, fighters are the tanks of a group and expect to take the brunt of the damage from the enemy. Their primary attribute is strength. They can use all types of weapons as a fighter class, then specialize in their professions.

Statisical Bonus: +5 Strength, +2 Agility, +3 Stamina

Mage:
As magic wielders, the mage's primary attiribute is intelligence, needed for spell casting power. They can wield a staff and dagger, but do more casting from behind the melee characters in the group than actual physical combat. Choose a Dark Elf, Erudite, Froglok, Gnome, Half-Elf, High Elf, Human, Iksar, and Ratonga for the best luck with the mage class.

Statistical Bonus: +3 Agility, +2 Stamina, +5 Intelligence

Priest:
The healing classes support the fighters in the group by both healing wounds and making their allies stronger with powerful magical spells. Their primary attribute is wisdom.

Scout:
The stealthiest and most cunning of the archetypes, they can easily explore strange places and unknown areas. Using surprise and the skills of trap detection and disarming, they can give a group of fighters much advantage over their opponents. Their primary ability is agility. They can wear medium armor.

Statistical Bonus: +3 Strength, +5 Agility, +2 Stamina

Notes:

Can I solo this class? Scouts aren't the greatest soloers for the simple reason that although they are heavy damage dealers, they don't have the ability to take much damage, and in the case of the brigand/swashbuckler, they are backstabbing which is hard to do solo.
What is the scout's role in a group? Scouts are not in a group to be on the front lines meleeing and taking hits; rather, they are there to assess and enhance combat situations through stealth, disarming traps, tracking, group songs, and to deal a lot of damage to the enemy in a short period of time.

Consult the EQ2 player's scouts's class guide for more information on playing this class.

Artisans:
You don't have to choose to be a fighter; a player may want to create goods for fellow Norrathians. Artisans create furniture, armor, weapons, spells, and many other things needed for life in Norrath. A craftsman's primary attribute is creativity.
