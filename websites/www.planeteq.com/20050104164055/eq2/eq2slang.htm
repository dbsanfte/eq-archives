EQ2 Slang. Acronyms, and Expressions

Every game has it's own language used by players, which evolves over time. If you have played other online games with people, you probably know much of the abbreviations and terms develop with the idea of typing as little as possible in a short time to communicate what you mean. Sometimes all the shorthand can get confusing, so here's a start. Each day new terms and abbreviations come into play, so this list is as current as it can be, given the lightning speed of communication on the net, but it certainly won't be complete. Send in your abbreviations and add to the list!

AC Asheron’s Call game
Aggro Aggression, getting a monster angry or an angry monster ready to attack; aggro range is the distance at which you can approach before the monster will attack
XP Experience
Wis Wisdom
WB welcome back
Wheel heroic opportunity wheel
waypoint system to track npcs or group members (alt W keystrokes)
tier advancing levels for artisans/crafters
station griffin transportation stations in Antonica
train a group of mobs running after a player; training refers to people intentionally pulling mobs onto a player to kill them off
ST secondary tank
SH Stormhaven zone
Regen regeneration
puller person responsible for bringing mobs to the group
Pred predator class
pop monster has spawned
AF Auto follow a player character
oop Out of power (lost spellcasting ability)
med meditation -- refers to taking a break to get power back
NPC Non-player character
Node Refers to objects you can gather/mine/collect/harvest/forest items from
nerf changes in the game made by the developers making something less powerful
NBG Need before greed – a method of distributing game loot to those who need it most
named Monsters with titles or names; generally drop better items and are harder
MT Main tank: the character that takes most of the damage when fighting; other group members assist this player in fighting
mob originally "Mobile object," refers to any monster in the game
PC Player character
MH Main healer
main Someone’s main character, as opposed to an alternate character
Int Intelligence
instance Whichever instanced zone you choose to go into; group members usually check with one another on which instance they should all meet in when zoning
HO Heroic Opportunity
gratz Congratulations
GHO Group Heroic Opportunity
FFXI Final Fantasy Eleven game
Feather A player summoned object that allows one group member to revive another
shard the corpse of a dead player
DPS Damage per second, used to describe melee character abilities
ding Making a level, refers to the sound when leveling
Debuff Removing spells from a player or monster
Debt Experience debt from dying
DAoC Dark Age of Camelot game
CS Customer service
Crows Scarecrow monsters in Antonica
Chanter Enchanter class
buffs Player enhancing spells; players will ask others for their class buffs. Learn all the buff abbreviations on your server so you know what someone means when they ask for something. Don't be afraid to ask what people mean when they abbreviate something.
bio Player has to use the restroom
RL real life
BB Blackburrow zone
Awakening A divine awakening stone from a cleric spell; revives a cleric, cast by a cleric on a group member
Assist Everyone assists the main tank in fighting what he or she is fighting
AQ Armor quests (start at level 20): AQ1, AQ2 and so on
App I, II Apprentice spells
AoE Spell that affects more than one monster when cast (area of affect)
Alt An alternate character made later after a first character

AFK 		Away from keyboard; means that person is not at their computer
WTS want to sell
WTB want to buy
WoW World of Warcraft game
ROFL rolling on the floor laughing
LOL laughing out loud
: P this little symbol is hard to define. It's a face with a tongue sticking out, but its meaning is more like "heh" or "yeah right" and is used to punctuate a statement that might be ironic or silly. Readers, if you can come up with a better definition, I would love to see it.
KS kill stealing: not really possible in EQ2 due to the game mechanics, but people still talk about it sometimes
MMO, MMORPG Massively muliplayer online game, or massively multiplayer online role playing game (pronounced more-peg)
SWG Star Wars Galaxies game
WTT want to trade
bbl be back later
cya "see ya"; from "see you later"
brt be right there
omw on my way
imo in my opinion
btw by the way
GM game master (official staff member )OR guild master
GL group leader OR guild leader
jk or j/k just kidding: since typing doesn't lend itself to expressions, usually this follows something hurtful or mean that has been said when it wasn't serious. Be careful -- two dimensional communicaton can be misunderstood!
lop low on power. A little better than oop (out of power) but still not good. Time for a break! sometimes people will still say oom, which is the same thing, taken from EQ1 where there is mana, not power.
oom out of mana. There's no mana in EQ2, but EQ1 players might still use this expression to mean out of power.
otm on the move; group is moving
yw you are welcome
ty thank you
tank a character that melees. Also meat shield; tank can be used as a verb, as in "to tank a mob" or "a tanking cleric"
uber a very well equipped character or powerful mob
rdy "ready?" or "ready"!
gtg good to go
kk same as ok
spam repeating the same thing over and over again, usually in /ooc out of character or in shout, even worse. Can be a noun or a verb. "stop spamming!"
PvP, PvE player versus player (player killing), player versus environment (no player killing)
twitch refers to the physical ability to use the game controls, such as a keyboard or game controller. Requires manual dexterity, and refers to twitch reflexes which make for quick game play when dexterity is required.
oic "oh, I see"
raid a game encounter with large numbers of players
zerg refers to defeating an encounter with huge numbers of players
nuke to cast damage dealing spells
overnuke casting too many spells at once, causing the spell caster to get aggro from the mobs, taking it away from the main tank
taunt the melee character's ability to focus enemy anger on himself
hotkey making a button or key that automatically creates an action
macro programming a button so an action occurs when you press it
twink in EQ1, someone who has armor above their level, usually given to them by a higher level character. Not possible in EQ2 at the present time
powerlevel when a more powerful character helps a newer one gain levels very quickly; not easy to do in EQ2
two box one player playing two characters at once
kiting pulling mobs around a zone while killing them; a common EQ1 practice for some classes
jboots journeyman boots that add running speed
spawn

monsters appearing.

Backspawn: monsters reappearing after they have been killed once

Spawn point: where a monster normally appears.
noob, noobie an inexperienced player, a new player
gg good going, usually said sarcastically when someone makes a mistake
