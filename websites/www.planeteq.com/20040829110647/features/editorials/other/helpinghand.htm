A Helping Hand by Kwill

There are so many acts of random kindness in EverQuest between players that occur every day in the game, and I hope that you personally have been either a recipient or a giver of assistance at least once in your playing career. Each server really is a small community. Last night, I was playing an alternate character to recover my level 12 corpse which had gotten into a little trouble with the dinosaur things deep in Paludal Caverns -- he shouldn't have been in there in the first place, and they see through sneak! My plan was to log in my 58 paladin, go find my little beastlord's corpse, and drag him to safety. Nothing went as planned, and I managed to fall off the bridge to the Paludal Caverns and get stuck underneath it. I could find no way to crawl out, and was stuck. I gave a tell to a random level 65 magician one zone away, hoping I might get a call of the hero (I wasn't feeling too heroic at the moment under the bridge, mind you). He not only responded to my tell, but came over and got me out. He didn't want a donation for his time -- he was just helping another player out that he didn't know out of the kindness of his heart.

It turned out this magician was tagged in one of the power guilds on my server, whose leader I met at the guild summit. I gave a tell to his guild leader, telling him how kind his guild member had been to me. It turns out that my magician rescuer was an applicant to the guild (which is very hard to get into) and by helping me, he made a very good impression on the guild leader and, I assume, the membership. You never know who you may be helping when you get that random tell. That little alternate character may belong to someone who can help you out sometime, or perhaps that person's main ends up being a desirable group member. At the very least, you have made another friend in the game. Of course I didn't have to give a tell to his guild leader, but that was something I could do to help him and thank him -- and because he was an applicant, my gesture made a much bigger difference in his playing experience than it ordinarily might have.

But my evening didn't end there. I couldn't find my corpse. Another random player in the zone, a level 20 necromancer, enlisted her friend and not only found the corpse, but purchased a coffin for me and got my corpse back to the zone line. Another friendly gesture that made my game play last night a fun time, and not a frustrating experience. No, I couldn't do anything for the necro or her friend like I did for the magician, but that didn't make their experience helping me any less satisfying for them, I don't think. They seemed to be very happy to take some time to connect with another player and have the satisfaction of helping someone in trouble resolve a problem. Last evening, they made a difference in someone else's playing experience.

The design team talked at the guild summit about making a difference in the storyline of the game world -- and by helping a someone out, you just have, because the players make up the game world just as much as the NPCs and the storyline. For my magician friend, helping me out had a personal benefit he didn't expect. But that's not the only reason to do something nice for a stranger. Corpse recoveries, helping a lost character, buffing for no charge, or porting someone make the server community a nice place to be. Every player can probably help out someone else, some time. By helping one another out, everyone has a good time. Because isn't EQ really about the people we play with? It's not just guild mates or group members or people on your friends list. It's the people you run by in the zone or you hear shouting for help that also make up our communities. And one other thing: when you are on the receiving end of a helping hand, be sure to thank your good Samaritan -- it feels good to be appreciated when you make an extra effort. This is my public thank you to those characters on Cazic Thule last night who helped out the little beastlord and the paladin. I appreciate it!
