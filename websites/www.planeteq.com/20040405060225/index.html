Fan Faire!
4/4/04, 17:04 PST | Kwill

For those of you who didn't make it to the 5th anniversary Fan Faire in Las Vegas, you missed a great time! But don't worry, I will fill you in on the highlights.

Just like the rain in EQ when you least expect it, it rained all weekend in the desert ... good thing Fan Faire was in the Las Vegas Hilton and most people didn't have to go anywhere. But just try getting a taxi anywhere else, wow, the lines. But I was busy enjoying the festivities, so who cared if it was pouring outside. SOE pulled out all the stops to make sure the fans had a great time, including a private party at the "Beaches" club which was standing room only, with a very funky band -- and an open bar for everyone. Amazing. The banquets were sumptious and the costume contest as entertaining as always. Host Vannoth knows how to work the crowd and lived up to his reputation for great standup yet again. The random prize drawings always give a few lucky peeps some very phat loot at the banquet as well as some great comedy from the host. He has some of the best and most entertaining EQ improv (even when he's been working 20 hour days, he's still funny)--You go, Thomas!

The server and class banner auction is always one of the highlights of the event, and this year the most coveted banner was Povar's, because Povar su... well I can't say but you attendees know what I mean. Povar is the server they love to hate, only because Povar really brings out the attendees and they are loud and proud at the banquet. That banner is so coveted that it was sold to someone from Tarew Marr, and I don't think it's going to be around for too long after this weekend. Nothing like buying something for the sole purpose of destroying it! But it's all in good fun. Banners don't go cheap, either: some banners sold for over $1K. So if you want one, better bring some money. But all the proceeds go to the Red Cross, so it's fun and for a good cause.

The fire in the hole (literally) didn't stop the fans from seeing the first glimpse of EQII being played live, both in demo and with the press core getting a crack at it. The SOE staff kept their sense of humor after the ambitious computer setup pulled too much power and caught the power cords on fire, but they pressed on, giving the crowd a good show despite the live demo problems (which were pretty few, really). As the lucky lab rats kept playing, an SOE and press core group decided to try a raid, even though it had never actually been done live before. Now that's bravery. It went off flawlessly with much fun had by all in the instanced dungeon. I was not in the raid, but I leveled my character up to level 6, got seasick riding the boat (the ocean is pretty darn realistic!), completed some quests, and got killed a few times too. The new spirit system of death and corpse recovery is very interesting and much easier to deal with than the traditional corpse recovery of EQ. After experiencing EQII, I can say without reservation what a great game it is. The graphics are incredible, the play is smooth, and the world is immersive. The voice overs are a great addition to the game, and make it much more realistic. The acid test was I played for 4 hours non-stop and didn't want to quit (they made us leave!). More later on the game, but I give it an enthusiastic thumbs up.

The live quest as usual was quite challenging and fun, a mix of puzzles, scavenger hunts, creativity, and drama club. My team, the Silver Knights of Mystery, was awesome and especially our team member Iadriss who, when called on to create original Norrathian art, whipped up an oragami Lady Vox. The rest of the team was awesome, too. If you go to Fan Faire, be sure to join a live quest team. But be prepared to do just about anything! The event this year was based around EQ playing cards, which I imagine should be available soon for retail sale (but I don't know for sure). Teams collected playing cards and traded them, so between the quests, finding the elusive live NPCs, and shouting for card trades, everyone kept pretty busy. The prize to the winning team was an Intel motherboard and CPU for each team player -- quite the haul.

And of course there was more; one person can't get to everything because there is so much going on every day. R.A. Salvatore showed up for a book signing and to talk about the new EQ fantasy series, and each day there were packed discussion panels on trade skills, customer service, spells and AA, and more.

As always, Fan Faire is informative, fun, and full of serious EQ fans that aren't afraid to get a little rowdy or dressed up like their favorite class. There are always new things to see and the SOE staff show how much they want to hear from and interact with players that are enjoying the things they create. I was sorry I had to leave (very early!) Sunday morning. Time for a nap!

EQ2's Voiceovers for NPCs
3/31/04, 15:23 PST | Kwill


SAN DIEGO, CA – March 31, 2004 – Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online (MMO) gaming, today announced that EverQuest II will be the first MMO game to feature voiceover for in-game Non-Player Characters (NPCs). The voiceover will be comprised of more than 130 hours of dialog and approximately 1.5 million words (the equivalent of more than 65 feature films worth of content). The addition of voiceover to the game adds a new dimension of realism to the MMO genre that is unmatched by the current generation of titles.

“Including voiceover for NPCs in EverQuest II is an incredibly ambitious project,” said John Smedley, president, Sony Online Entertainment. “Few publishers could even imagine undertaking such a huge project, but we’re committed to taking EverQuest II to the next level. By giving our in-game characters voices for the first time in a massively multiplayer online environment, we are raising the bar for the genre as a whole, breaking down the boundaries between static, text-driven interface and meaningful, cinematic interaction.”

EverQuest II will deliver a unique online gaming experience to players with a cast of more than 100 actors, including well-known Hollywood talent. Featuring nearly 100,000 lines spoken by thousands of characters, the voiceover project is a major undertaking that is sure to mark the beginning of a new era for the MMO genre.

After launch, SOE will continue to add new voiceover content whenever new quests are added to the game. Additionally, EverQuest II players will have the ability to choose whether or not they wish to hear the voiceovers, giving them maximum control over their individual level of immersion and system performance.

About EverQuest II
EverQuest II is the next generation of massively multiplayer gaming, a huge online world where thousands of players can come together for adventure and community. Featuring breathtaking graphics and a vast, beautiful game world to explore, EverQuest II sets the standard for graphical realism as players are immersed in the game’s exciting locales and dangerous lands. EverQuest II brings players into its world with a powerful epic storyline and gives them the power to be the hero or villain in their personal adventure. Players enter this world by creating their own unique character, choosing from 16 races and 24 classes. Players will encounter hundreds of creatures as they travel across majestic landscapes of rolling hills, barren deserts, dense forests, and bustling cities. Thousands of items, hundreds of spells, and unlimited adventure await all who enter the world of EverQuest II. EverQuest II also features:

• A new, massive online world for thousands of players to explore, filled with danger, adventure and mystery
• Voices for almost every non-player character (NPC) in the game, a huge project encompassing nearly 100,000 lines and 130 hours of dialog (the equivalent of more than 65 feature films worth of content)
• Unparalleled character customization capabilities. In addition to thousands of pieces of armor, weapons and items users can equip, players will be able to customize their character’s facial appearance to an incredible level of detail and personalization
• 16 races, 50 levels and 24 classes with a branching system that allows players to choose their class as they advance
• No race/class limitations, allowing players to truly choose who they want to be in game
• Player-owned real estate (such as apartments, homes, and guild houses)
• State-of-the art combat, spell and skill systems
• New user friendly game mechanics with reduced learning curve for both new and seasoned players
• Trade skills available to all player classes
• More than 160 unique creature types, from Orcs and Goblins to huge dragons and menacing Living Statues
• Incentives for current EverQuest subscribers
• A state-of-the-art, cinematic quality 3D graphics engine capable of improving as hardware technology advances
