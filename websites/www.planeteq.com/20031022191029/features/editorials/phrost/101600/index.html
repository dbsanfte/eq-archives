Not an RPG
By Phrost (10/16/00)

There’s been a lot of discussion lately about Verant’s recent ban of a player who wrote a controversial story and posted it to an EverQuest fan site. But so far, just about everyone who’s objected to it has failed to take into account one simple fact. The banning of Myztere isn’t about intellectual property rights, obscenity, or even about a conflict or personal tastes. While I do agree with most of the arguments people have put up against his banning (torturing a level 14 Dark Elf is worse than killing a pregnant Halfling I guess) all of these arguments are pointless once you realize what the truth of the matter is. “What truth is that Meesta Phrosty?” Simple:

EverQuest is not a roleplaying game.

Now take a deep breath, blink a few times, and wipe off the Mountain Dew you just spit all over your monitor… and read further.

Once upon a time there was a man named Gary Gygax. Gary had a vision (not “The Vision™) of what a roleplaying game should be. He filled his world with heroes and monsters and villains. Unfortunately for Gary and his company, TSR, his game took off in that hyper-religious period of time we call “The 80’s”. Reagan was president, Nancy said no to drugs, Tipper was banning Heavy Metal, Tammy Faye was painting her face like a circus clown, and imagination was the enemy of the establishment. (Who needs kids who dream? They won’t grow up and be proper little wage slaves!)

Accusations of satanic heresies (the first refuge of the ignorant) abounded. Dungeons and Dragons was blamed for everything from suicide to teenage pregnancy and the deficit. You see, in the 80’s, much like it is today, many parents paid little attention to their children due to a pursuit of material wealth. So a scapegoat was needed for the parents to blame when their children “went bad” due to this neglect. Heavy Metal was one of these scapegoats, and so was D&D.

Why D&D? Well one of the rulebooks, the Monstrous Manual included (gasp) demons, which as we all know are bad, mmkay? That made them a prime target for the moral crusaders who were looking to “protect” America from anything they thought was “naughty”. God forbid that young adults be exposed to a fantasy evil that they would adventure together in order to defeat.



Fast-Forward to the present day, to EverQuest, and the land of Norrath. Norrath, you see, is much like a fudge-covered Oreo™ cookie: Dark on the outside, with corpses hanging from hooks, torture racks, and blood stains on walls, but wholesome in the middle because everyone is expected to be friendly to each other, regardless of their race’s “history”.

About a year ago, my little inky Necro was the terror of Nektulos forest. I was a fledgling lord of the shadows. I summoned undead to serve the will of the Prince of Hate. I called upon powerful magics in order to further his power. I laughed as my enemies died at my feet, begging for my help… only to be told:

“Dood, u r a dick”.

My guild, the Disciples of Innoruuk, was the terror of the server and rightly so for we followed the will of Hatred. We mocked the petty, inferior races for their weak adherence to “good”. Our sorcerers would make our Ogres and Trolls invisible to trap enemies in the tiny dungeon corridors with vicious monsters. We took what we wanted to, not caring for anyone who stood in our way… only to be told:

“Dood, u guys are dicks”.

Our small band that served the god of Hate persevered in the face of overwhelming odds. Almost no one would ally with us, much less provide our members any assistance if we were in need. In fact, when the more powerful guilds were reaping the treasured Rubicite Armor from the bowels of the lost temple of Cazic Thule (*cough* Farming *cough*) we were forced to overcome our foes through skullduggery to acquire it… only to be told by Verant’s GM’s:

“Dood, u guys have to stop being dicks or you will be banned”.

Apparently Verant had come up with an idea for everyone to “Play Nice”, and this meant that there was no place in their world for a band of murdering Ogres, man-eating Trolls, or manipulative Dark Elves.

It also meant that there was no place for anyone that truly roleplayed.

Somewhere in the recent past, the words “fantasy” and “roleplaying” became interchangeable. Game companies market new “RPG’s” all the time, but are they really roleplaying games? Do you really get to “chose” a role in Final Fantasy 7? Are you free to take whatever course of action you chose in Baldur’s Gate? The answer to both, for the most part, is no. EverQuest is a prime example of this. It’s not an RPG. It’s a fantasy version of Quake with server-stored, semi-permanent characters. Although as a result of the interaction with other people in the game you’re allowed a great deal more freedom than in Final Fantasy, you’re still not allowed to truly deal with that snotty High Elf the way an Ogre would in D&D. The game was never designed to be anything other than Player vs. Environment… and that means it’s not a roleplaying game.

So if you look at the banning of Myztere through this set of eyes you can understand why Verant took the course of action that it did. EverQuest isn’t Dungeons and Dragons. EverQuest will be gone in a few years, replaced by something better. In the meantime, when it comes down to a choice between banning a roleplayer and keeping the moral crusaders off their backs (and out of their pocketbooks), and risking an association with “evil” like D&D that could shorten EQ’s already limited lifespan…

…what would you expect them to do? It’s not really a roleplaying game anyway. Ask Gary Gygax.
