The Relentless: A Thriving Guild on Cazic Thule Server

The Relentless is one of the oldest guilds on the Cazic Thule server, and one of the most successful if you consider longevity and routine high level encounters to be a measure of success. The Relentless is not the biggest guild on the CT server, but it is well respected by the CT server population as being a tough guild with successful players. The guild leader is Thornsong, a 65 druid, who has been playing since May 1999. The guild has been around since May of 1999.

The Relentless can boast that only five members have ever left the guild to join others since it began to raid high end encounters. Thornsong attributes that loyalty to the fact that the guild distributes desirable loot to all members, rather than to a top tier of officers, and the friendships that evolved in real life -- they were a softball team and played Magic: The Gathering together -- before the guild played as a group on the server, solidifying the bonds between the members.

Thornsong and I discussed the guild over an email interview:

Kwill: How many members does the Relentless have?

We have probably 120 members in The Relentless. Of those about 20 are retired but active on the boards, another 20 are semi-active and 82 log on at least 4-5 times a week.

Kwill: How many officers does the guild have?

Eleven:
Thornsong Guild Master
Omalley Internal Relations
Lyddea Board Records
Grizumpy Guild Treasurer
Rjey Forum Guru
Shokwaav Seargeant of Arms
Zith Tactics Coordinator
Mintern Trade Skills Officer
Zaero Webmaster
Samirah Recruiter
Shizda Small Camp Coordinator
Athren Catch up Coordinator

Thornsong, who has a professional career and family outside of EverQuest, feels that his delegation of tasks within the guild makes it possible for him to play less and the members to achieve more.

Kwill: When did the guild actually start?

May of 99. It started as a group of real life friends, mainly a softball team combined with some friends who would casually get together at Robb's house to play Magic The Gathering.

It's kind of interesting as of those 10 people who created our guild, at least half have played dramatic key roles in the history of the server. First there is me, who has built the Relentless up to the guild which was the first to kill Aten Ha Ra on this server.

Then you have Robb aka Kenji who left us to join Cohort [Chalybeuis] and is now their GM. Larry aka Remyth left our guild to join Shadowed Soul, and became an officer there. In fact, he was the swing vote which ousted Shadestorm out of SS, which caused the rise of Element of Darkness.

Then you have the brothers Felix and Ken, probably best known for their toons Solknar and Assassin, who left us to help build STK and left them and came back, left again to help build EA [Erudinum Arcanium], came back, and left again to help the Masks (and are presently back with us).

The Masks splintered on their own to form the Talons. Saze, the main puller and [their] GM, was taught his class by Ken and Felix. So we have been a big part of this server's history.

Kwill: What are your recruitment policies and minimum requirements?

We have always been a mesh of casual players and power gamers, but once we went solo from our alliance with Element of Darkness, have been adding only power gamers to our mix. We are big enough and only want to fortify our raid counts so that we can continue to do things to make our membership happy. We require A LOT of AA to get in our guild. We want people to come in "done" from experience and ready to raid. That is our guild's function now. We raid hard and we raid relentlessly.

Kwill: What is the most difficult thing about leading a guild?

The most difficult thing about leading a guild is getting off and doing your own thing. It's hard to level and to play my class well when I constantly get interrupted with problems. The running joke in my guild is that when you have Thornsong in a group, you need another snarer as I will get wrapped up in a tell and will inevitably miss one.

Kwill: What is your proudest accomplishment?

The proudest accomplishment for me would be waking the Sleeper. Some people look at that as I ruined the game for others, but I look at it as fulfilling a quest that I saw as gamechanging. One that no one else could repeat. I still consider my Primal Velium Battlehammer looted off Ventani as my favorite item.

Kwill: What are your goals for the guild?

Our goals is to kill everything that Verant has put in our way. We want to see every zone, defeat every mob and glory as conquerers. On a personal note, I want my guild to be respected and not hated for walking over people. In the past, some of our greatest achievements, have been acts of kindness, like call of heroing in someone to loot an item that would rot. I hate to see loot rot, we have given items like that to our competition over and over again.

In the past, a guild war of sorts on the server for the right to hunt Trakenon created a conflict between The Relentless and other guilds that wanted the dragon. Thornsong comments, "This was the beginning of us being strong. CC [Cohort Chaylbeuis] and EOD [Element of Darkness] were the two guilds who forged an alliance, and they brought us in as the third. They fought CN [Coterie Nocturn] to a standstill before inviting us. We just pushed them over the top."

It is a tradition with The Relentless to fight for what they think is theirs; early in the game they fought it out with another powerful guild in the Plane of Fear when the other guild zoned in and insisted they couldn't have Cazic Thule. Instead of giving up, they engaged Cazic Thule with the entire temple still up. They died, the other guild died, but so did Cazic Thule at the hands of The Relentless.

Kwill: How would you compare the Relentless with other guilds on the server?

I would say that we are in the top tier of the guilds on the server. I wouldn't say we are better than them skillwise or accomplishment wise, but on par. We all have our good days. The other top guilds are the SS/DA alliance, the CC/Talons alliance, Element of Darkness and Anavon.

Kwill: What other guilds do you admire/respect and why?

What guilds do I admire? I admire guilds like Crimson Talon. This is a small up and coming guild who has managed to keep their core together despite the consistent pressure of members to want to jump to the end game. Of the uber guilds, the one I admire most is The Divine Alliance. I have had some real good friends in that guild, unfortunately most have retired.

Kwill: What would you want someone reading this article to know about the guild?

I hope [our reputation] is a positive one. That has always been one of my goals. I really feel good when I hear stories from my members of being handed 100k to hold for someone as they transfer it to another toon. The reason given, "I know I can trust you cause with your tag I know you wouldn't screw me over."

Kwill: What makes it unique on the server as compared to the other guilds there?

We have a lot of working adults. We probably have less power gamers than several of the other uber guilds out there, BUT, we focus. That there is hard to explain. Let me give some examples. We try to make it so that our members lack nothing and can focus on getting the most out of the game. Any droppable item the guild can afford that would better someone in our guild we will buy it. We try to give them the best gear possible so they don't have to spend time getting it. For example, back before run speed 3, I probably bought 30 pairs of JBoots for guildmates via multiquest. That way they could concentrate on leveling rather than camping a rare spawn. I want productive time out of my members when they are online.

Thank you Thornsong for giving us a glimpse into The Relentless!
