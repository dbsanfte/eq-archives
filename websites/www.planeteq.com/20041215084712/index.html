EQ2: Heroic Opportunities
12/8/04, 6:58 PST | Kwill

Guide to Heroic Opportunities (HOs)

by Beck

There you are, outside Stepford City with your fellow adventurers bashing away on bears trying to complete your Clothspinner's armor quest and your group for some reason is getting angry at you for not paying attention to "the wheel". What wheel? Oh that thing in the lower righthand corner of my screen? That my friend, is a heroic opportunity wheel.

Heroic opportunities are buffs and/or enhancements that you will receive if you complete the wheel successfully. They are integral to gameplay and can greatly affect the rapidity of your killing. When soloing, your ability to manage the wheel will be the deciding factor in most cases whether you live or die by that orange con mob you picked a fight with.

The basic heroic opportunity icons on the wheel appear four colors, depending on your archetype:

Blue-Fighters
Green-Scouts
Red-Mages
Yellow-Priests

The middle icon in the wheel delineates the order:

Round Arrow: Icons must be done in order for the heroic opportunity to be successful.

Starburst pattern arrows: Icons can be done in any order.

For example, you are fighting and notice a round arrow wheel with a yellow cup flashing in the wheel, followed by a green cape. This is a starter chain. What this means is the priest in the group needs to cast a group heal with a scout type class in the group backstabbing/sneak attacking for the heroic opportunity chain to be successful. Because it is the round arrow it needs to be done IN ORDER. If it is not done in order, you failed.

Normally you will see an icon flashing in your hotkey bar which corresponds to what is required for the wheel when it is your turn. Unfortunately combat can happen quickly in Everquest 2 and if you aren't paying attention you can miss your chance on the wheel and fail at completing the order and earning yourself or your group a buff or enhancement.

If you are lucky, you will see a coin icon flashing in the wheel. No, this does mean everyone gets money if you complete the chain successfully! It means you have triggered a heroic opportunity shift. This gives the scout in your group a chance to shift to another heroic opportunity using combat arts. It can trigger a rare enhancement if completed correctly. The Scout has to act quickly and has to comlete it before any others have been used to advance the wheel.

gnorrath.com is an indepth site for Heroic Opportunities with much more information, so if you want to learn more, please give them a visit!

    The State of EQLive
    12/1/04, 11:15 PST | Kwill

    Let's face it, we are all feeling the impact of the release of EQ2. I have been hearing how some high level raiding guilds are disbanding because their players are going off to do other things in EQ2 and elsewhere, and how casual mid-level players are finding it hard to get a group in what used to be crowded hunting spots.

    The raiding guilds are feeling the pinch because of the lack of clerics, for the most part, and the overall lack of players, still perhaps eager to raid, but who just don't have the equipment or the flags to go raid in the zones that need experienced, upper level players. Some guilds are working through the problem and have survived the cataclysm of EQ2, and some have not.

    The casual players are finding on many servers there isn't anyone to group with. Finding a group at the lower levels is getting more difficult. There just aren't the number of players on 24/7 like there used to be.

    So is there a solution? I don't think the horizon is as dark as it might appear. Firstly, EQ2 takes a powerful computer to run. Not everyone can upgrade to play the game, and those folks will still be enjoying EQLive just as much as ever without a state of the art machine that can handle the graphics load EQ2 asks of them. Secondly, EQ2 is not a newer version of EQLive: it's a different game entirely. With more content based on questing, solo content, and small group activities, the raiding folks are probably going to come back to raid-oriented EQLive at some point. Okay, so perhaps when the dust settles and the gloss on the new game gets a little worn people will come back--but until then, how can we play EQLive given the population changes in the game right now?

    Here are some good suggestions from veteran players:

  • Take groups consisting of varied classes, not just the traditional cleric/warrior/enchanter. Other classes can accomplish crowd control, healing, fighting, and other tasks reserved for the power three. It's a chance to experience the game differently than you played it before, and get creative in your encounters. Personally, I have gotten pretty good at soloing my 40 enchanter with charm when I can't find a group. Learn to play your class well, and exploit some of the lesser used abilities all the classes have as a way to still have fun. Some of the best groups are ones that seem like they can never work -- but smart strategies can make up for a lack of healing, crowd control, or a traditional tank.

  • Start a "looking for a group" chat channel on your server, and use it. One was started on the Cazic Thule server and it's a great way to start talking to people across zones. Think about the bazaar concept: if people looking for a group gather in one spot such as the Plane of Knowledge or the Plane of Tranquility, those looking for a group can more easily find one another.

    For some, it will take a little more work now than just going to the old crowded hunting grounds and getting a group there for experience grind. We aren't told how many people are playing or not playing right now, but there are still plenty of people who want to group; you may just have to look a little harder and start thinking about more creative groups. Raiding guilds may need to combine forces and work with each other to experience the end game content -- alliances are a powerful tool.

    Don't forget, another expansion of EQLive is coming out soon -- the EQLive Team is still in there creating content and new adventures for us. EQLive is still a fun and interesting game, with features unique to itself. EQ2 has better graphics, sure, because it runs on a new engine that can handle the textures and shading the old EQLive engine just can't. But if you consider the content and the game play, I think you will find that EQLive still has something to offer. Different designers, different concepts for each game give us a choice. That's always a good thing.

    LDoN Points Issue
    12/1/04, 11:03 PST | Kwill

    There have been some issues with LDoN points, specifically that players cannot see or buy items costing over 1000 points, no matter how many points they have in a specific camp or in total points. After some discussion on the message boards about it, Bulvinne posts on the EQL forums:
    If you sell back items your available point total will NOT update with the merchants until after you have completed your next LDON.

    - AFTER you have gained over 1492 CAMP points in any camp the items you can VIEW is based on your current adventure point total.

    - If at anytime your AVAILABLE adventure points drops below 996 You will not be able to see the higher point valued items available on merchants. Regardless if your career camp total is 5000 or 2000.

    Example: Player has 580 available adventure points. A career total in Mistmoore of 2214. They can see all items in the Butcher Block wayfarer's camp up to 996. They sell back two augments for 700 points each. Their new available total is 1980. Player will not be able to use these points until after they run one more adventure.

    *Note* I know for a fact your points will update if you win or partial win an adventure. I am not certain if the points will update if you lose an adventure.

    So to get your point totals to work, you need to run another adventure after selling back your items to the merchant. Hopefully the devs will correct this "bug" when they have time!
