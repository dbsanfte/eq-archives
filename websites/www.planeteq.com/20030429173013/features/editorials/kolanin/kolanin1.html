Kolanin's Comments

Hello and well met, my main avatar's name is Kolanin, and I'm glad to be known by that name here. Before I go on to the topic I plan on exploring in this, my first article to be posted on Planet EQ, I would like to share a little about myself and my history in the lands of Norrath as a player.

After playing every kind of First Person Shooter and computer Role Playing Game, I tripped upon Everquest. Friends warned me that I could become a hermit thanks to the amazing game and even the box echoed the warning with a quote from a reviewer that said; "Buy this game for someone you love if you never want to see them again!" I looked at the check out girl after chuckling and showed her the quote. She nodded solemnly and admitted that her boyfriend played. I laughed when she said; "See you when the next expansion comes out." Little did I know the correctness of her statement, as just a few weeks later I found myself in her check out line buying Scars of Vellious.

My entrance into the world of Norrath is remembered fondly. Guild less and for the most part clueless I was surrounded by my fellow newbie's in the Greater Faydark, thrilled by the continuous war of Orc Hill and Crushbone, and enjoying myself immensely as I role played my heart out.

Weeks passed in real life, and a role playing romance was developing. It was a great deal of fun, and the hunting partnership was near perfect. My cleric and her bard fought side by side, shared long boat trips across the Ocean of Tears, and discovered new and challenging zones. I shudder at the corpse runs I had to endure at times. The role playing aspect of our hunting partnership eroded and the veil of fantasy lifted, revealing what I had suspected all along. My male cleric had been flirting with a female bard avatar, who turned out to be played by a married man with two children.

If I wasn't so busy laughing myself into hysterics along with my cousin, who was sitting right beside me when the discovery was made, I would have been disturbed by the revelation. Quickly realizing that a friendship emerged out of the whole ordeal, things righted themselves very well and the hunting partnership, changed forever, continued.
In real life a couple of months passed, and I was invited to another server by local friends, where I began playing a Druid named Kolanin, and an entirely new role playing experience began. The quest for levels and the equally important quest for equipment.

Role playing was a novelty on this new server, and there were so many high level characters everywhere that it was staggering. After a few weeks I had met some very good players, and after becoming a friend to one woman living far south of myself, our characters were married. The union wasn't made to last however, and the guild we were members in was rocked by a fairly civil Everquest divorce. The friendship between the other player and I remains, however, and that's something I am very grateful for.

Over a year has passed since that particular episode in my Everquest experience and Kolanin the Druid has been a member in three different guilds, each with different ideas about how the game should be played. Since I began playing this game, it has changed so drastically, that I sometimes feel like it's not the same game I purchased at all. I know this is partially due to the fact that I'm on an old server, but some of the same sentiments are shared by many players.

With the addition of the new, many things become suddenly old, and there has been a lot added to Norrath since I began playing. In the next two articles I'll explore some of the key changes I've found, and how this has become one of the key parts of ongoing discussions on the bulletin boards, especially with regard to the ongoing Everquest II debate.
