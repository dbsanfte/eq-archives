Paths Unchosen, Part II
by Morganth, GM of the New Templars

In Morganth’s last adventure, he had entered a rogue camp of the Horadrim, and had bought goods from his dear friend:
"I'll have 8 thorned roses, 3 ostrich eggs, and 20 ginseng roots, Berrun," ordered Morganth. "Very well..." and Berrun began to package the goods. Berrun placed the bag with the components on his counter and asked for 18 silver pieces. Morganth withdrew that exact amount from his pouch. "Thank you Berrun, I'm sure we'll meet again soon," said Morganth. "I hope so, farewell Morganth," replied Berrun.
Morganth was weary and needed to rest. His acquisition of the components was one goal completed in this evil camp of Horadrim. Morganth could ascribe the murders of many innocent people he has met to the rogues in this camp; and thus he wanted to leave this place as soon as possible.

"Not without rest," the old man mumbled to himself. He was tired and the next town was a few days journey from here. Although he had supplies, his eyes grew weary, and there was no way to survive in the dark forests, even with his magical invocations.

He saw what resembled a tavern and inn to his north. Longing for rest, he began walking in that direction. When he was a bit closer he could see an excerpt from the tome of Miragul on the wall:
Sosanctus Lopus Bracton Limden…

"The nocturnal people of our land are not ones to be afraid of. If a murderer can do his evil deeds in broad daylight, he is a force to be reckoned with."
Ah, yes, Miragul. You know not that name but it is widespread in the land Morganth lives in. He was a sorcerer who’s power was greater than any mage before or after him. He could summon creatures from the deepest depths of hell, such as demons, devils, and other evil minions. The rogues and necromancers across the land often have such plaques of commemoration on the walls of their shops and such, however, the good people of the land despised Miragul. You see: he once cast a spell that included the words of power aforementioned in the excerpt above. Sosanctus Lopus Bracton Limden, it was the spell to open the gates to hell. Despite Miragul’s power, he could not control a spell of such great power, so in fact the gate had sent him to hell. However, the gate remained open, and every millenium the minions come out for retribution.

The year is 987. Morganth knows that in a few years that spell will attempt to destroy his world, but that is why he tries to master as much magic as possible. Perhaps with the help of other sorcerers in the land, this portal will be able to be sealed.

He enters the tavern. On his left he sees a troll staring at him with battle in his eyes. On his right, a couple of thieves that look harmless. Strait ahead a slightly overweight, bald man tending the bar. Keeping his composure, Morganth approaches the bartender. "What does a person have to do to get a place to rest around here…" The bartender laughs, "Thy gold pouch looks full, traveler. Ye’know what I need. 75 gold pieces a night." Morganth checks the ogre on his left. "I just spent 18 silver to buy enough components to light your fine tavern on fire, and you’ll charge me a hundred times that to sleep here?"

Morganth hears the ogre get up from his seat. The bartender shakes his head lightly. "Rungupp, I believe you have a customer." Rungupp, the troll, begins walking towards Morganth. The old man draws some components from his pouch and chants words of power, "Sosanctus Lopus Bracton Limden." Under his breath, he really murmurs the words of power for a resplendent spell that made his body glow. Morganth had not the time, components, nor strength to cast such a strong spell as the demon gate, however, he knew this troll and his lackeys didn’t know any better.

"Boss! It Miragul, he ‘da bad mage. He will open ‘dat gate and kill us, boss!" Morganth grinned. "Aye, that I will, Rungupp. You better put down thy weapon and take a seat. I need rest, and I wish not to be disturbed." The bartender was speechless.

"Bartender, I’ll take the keys for thy best room in this place." The bartender was petrified and he could not speak. "Fine, bartender, I shall wake ye up." Quickly he chanted the spell magic arrow and flung one over the bartender’s head. The heat and speed of the arrow left an abrasion there. Quickly, the bartender woke up from his state of petrification and handed Morganth the keys. With a sedate look, Morganth headed up to his room.

He placed his pouch of components and gold right next to his pillow, and lay down to rest. Within minutes he was asleep and dreaming.

In his head an image appeared. He saw many trolls around a campfire, primeval runes around the trolls. They were dancing and chanting as if performing in some religious rite. It made no sense, however. Trolls had an infidelity to any gods. The trolls worshipped none. The dream continued, and suddenly from the middle of the fire rises a dragon that had skin of fire. The dragon looks to his left and right, and then flies at an innocent troll of the circle. With its claw, the dragon inflicts a lethal blow on the troll, and engulfs him. The dragon then disappears, as do the trolls.

Morganth woke up in cold sweat. He checked his pouch and everything was still there. What could that dream have meant? Morganth had read the most in-depth annals on trolls, and he knew for a fact that they worshipped not a god nor religion. Yet here they were in his dream, worshipping some fire dragon god; and sacrificing to it. The old man knew not what to think, and was still tired… even more so than before. He went to sleep.
