Comparison of Priest Classes
Author unknown, originally from the EQ2 official forums

Like many of you prospective priests, I've been spending a good deal of time attempting to figure out exactly what sort of priest I wanted to be, and flipping between a variety of posts and Gwynet's priest spell lists and so on to help me decide...but still getting nowhere.

Today, I figured that the best way to make my decision was to look at the endgame (level 40+) spells of each class to try and get a better picture of what each priest class was going to bring to the table at that level. Now obviously, I haven't used any of these spells, in fact, I've not played the game yet. However, I do believe we can look at the spell descriptions, the relative levels of the spells and the concentration of spells in various categories from class to class to try and get a general picture of what one class will be skilled at versus another.

All of the opinions in this thread are my own, and all of the spell descriptions are from Gwynet (thanks for posting the list!). I don't claim to be stating facts, these are just my opinions of how the various priest classes will play in the endgame based on the spell descriptions and levels provided. Please note that I also make the assumption that the spells received at lv 40+ are representative of the spells which the priest will primarily be using, because in general they are repeated versions of earlier spells. I also have not made a true 'ranking' of priest classes, but after doing this analysis, I do believe them to be well balanced in what they will bring to the group, though in very different categories.

Common Spell Trends:

lv 39 - Crowd Control utility spell. Most priest classes receive a spell which sounds powerful and related to controlling overpulls or fleeing combat at this level. I know that this spell is before 40, which I designated (rather arbitrarily) as the cutoff for endgame spells, but as the spells given to each class at 39 are all related to Crowd Control and aren't duplicated or repeated later, I assumed them to be significant to the priest's role.

lv 40 - flavor spell 1

lv 45 - flavor spell 2

lv 42 - 'Save-Me' utility spell. All priest classes receive a spell which is most likely a long-recast, self only spell. These spells are hate reducers and are probably to be used when the priest is getting too much agro.

lv 50 -'uber' flavor spell

lv 50 - 'uber' spell. At 50, each priest receives a unique class defining spell (heal, buff or nuke)


I divided the spells from each class into the following categories.

Buff: spell which is cast on a group member and increases his offensive or defensive potential, or gives him the ability to decrease enemy combat potential through procs or reactive effects.

Damage: spell which primarily serves to directly reduce an enemy's health, usually through an attack.

Mitigation: spell which primarily serves to directly prevent damage to a group members health, not by affecting attributes but by directly affecting his health pool.

Death prevention: spell which has the intended effect of preventing the death of a character who has completly run of out health.

Debuff: spell which is usually cast on an enemy and primarily serves to decrease an enemy's offensive or defensive potential, or a spell which causes the enemy to inadvertently increase the combat potential of allies through reactive effects

Utility: spell which benefit the group or character in another manner besides primarily directly affecting player or enemy offensive or defensive potential, also including hate reduction or last ditch effort spells. May have buffs or debuffs as side effects, however the primary effect is not the buff or debuff.

Flavor: spell which has no/little effect on combat but serves to distinguish the character class by adding to atmosphere of the game world.


Priest Class Spell Analysis

Templar
In my opinion, the templar has the most variety and probably potency of healing and is a good defensive buffer who has pretty much no ability to increase group DPS or provide much utility other than keeping the group alive. He wears plate, and will probably be able to save his own behind easily due to the higher AC and great agro avoidance spells.

Specialty: Healing Flexibility, Staying Alive, Armor Class Buffs.

Buff (7): The templars buffs focus on keeping the party alive; increasing HP and AC, and offering mental/arcane protection. Glory of Combat 47 is the only endgame mitigation buff that doesn't require getting hit, and will probably be very valuable. Valor at 47 keeps the templar tied with the mystic and warden for the highest lv AC group buff in game, although Vigilant Benediction at 45 is the only endgame single target buff that just buffs AC, and is probably the best AC buff in game. Overall, his defensive buffing is probably the best.

40 - Praetorate - Str and Wis buff
43 - Shielding Faith - Power and arcane group buff
44 - Sacred redoubt - HP + offense buff
44 - Symbol of Pinzarn - Group HP buffs + can increase AC when hit
45 - Vigilant Benediction - AC buff
47 - Glory of Combat - Buff that gives a chance to heal the party on each succesful attack
47 - Valor - Stamina, mental and AC group buff

Damage (2): Doesn't have much variety in damage dealing; uses single or AoE DD's of only divine type. Likely to be good against undead, but overall, probably near the bottom of the damage dealing scale.

45 - Condemning Smite - Divine DD
46 - Rays of Faith - AoE Divine DD

Mitigation (8/4 instant): Plenty of instant heals, the last at level 46. Has varied sources of mitigation all the way through 50, with some spells which probably offer very power efficent healing (atoning fate/involuntary curate). Gets a regular reactive heal at 50; Focused Benefaction the only level 50 heal which doesn't require immobilization (or at least it doesn't say it in the spell description). Templar has the most spells dedicated to solely to mitigation, meaning that they do nothing besides mitigate, which leads me to believe they would heal for more than a mitigation spell with an additional effect. Only potential endgame drawback is that he doesnt have any single target reactive heals besides his 'uber' heal until 50. Likely though, he's the most well rounded healer with a heal for every situation.

40 - Greater Intercession - Instant heal + can increase AC when hit
42 - crucial Intercession - Group reactive heal
43 - Word of Restoration - Group heal
43 - Greater Amelioration - Instant heal and offense buff
46 - Greater Restoration - Instant heal that can cure minor Noxious effects
48 - Atoning Fate - Heals group when target dies
49 - Involuntary Curate - Can cure trauma and heal when target is hit //cast on enemy
50 - Focused Benefaction - Reactive heal

Death Prevention: The power regeneration in this spell may give a slight edge, but over all, most of these death prevention spells are pretty much the same.

41 - Salvation - Prevents death + heal + power regen

Debuff (2): Templar's debuffs focus on making the party harder to kill, not on making the enemy easier to kill. Mark of Kings at 46 is probably the second best mitigation debuff in the game, sliding in right under the defilers lv 40 Bane of Shielding. Overall a good defensive debuffer, but totally lacking in offensive debuffs.

46 - Mark of Kings - Debuff that can cause regen and AC buff on attacker
49 - Sign of Infirmity - AoE str debuff

Utility (2): Resolve looks to be the best of all of the 'save-me' 42 abilities of all classes, it will likely save the templar many times. The templar's 'CC' utility at 39 is a stun, which may give a nice tactical advantage to a group. He might need it though, as only he and the fury have no AoE hate reduction spell. His 41 Harmony is likely to be castable on anyone, and will probably be a very welcome spell for the other mages/priests in group.

**39 - Prostrate - Stun //special CC ability
*42 - Resolve - Hate reducers and no damage for 3 attacks //'save-me' ability: probably long recast, self-only
41 - Harmony - Hate reducer + heal

Flavor: no transformations, but the acolyte sounds cool. Unlike all of the other classes who must all wait until 50, he gets his 'uber' flavor ability at level 45, holy aura. I don't believe that the acolyte will have any affect on combat, as it is given to the templar at the same time as the other classes get their flavor spells.

40 - Summon Holy Symbol - Summon a holy symbol
45 - Holy Acolyte - Summons an acolyte
45 - Holy aura - covers the templar in a holy aura

Inquisitor: In my opinion, the inquisitor is a very good healer with lots of creative buffs to increase group DPS and protect them at the same time. His debuffs are probably not too powerful, but he has the best assortment of crowd control of all the priests. He will not be a direct source of DPS however, as his own spells aren't structured around damage.

Specialty: Creative DPS Buffs, Haste, Crowd Control.

Buff (7): The inquisitor has buffs which seem to be more skewed towards offense, but boost defense simultaneously. His Feverence at 47 is the highest single target AC buff and wards against trauma (which I believe is melee damage); this is sure to be a potent spell. The group cast Act of Faith (49) is the highest level DD proc. He has no spell which directly decreases damage from a special attack source (such as fire/arcane/disease), only an indirect protection at level 45. Focuses his level 50 'uber' spell on a self-immobilizing haste, providing even more offensive power to the group. Overall, seems to be the second-in-command at offensive buffing, right behind the fury. The inquisitor will likely turn up a groups DPS very nicely.

43 - Vengeance - DD on enemies when attacked
43 - Zealot's conviction - Group HP + chance to haste when attacked
44 - Hallowed Aura - HP and damage buff
45 - Harrowing Inquest - Can drain power and increase arcane mitigation when enemy attacks
47 - Fervence - Sta + AC buff + ward against trauma
49 - Act of Faith - Group buff granting the group a divine DD
50 - Zealotry - Haste + maintening power cost + immobilization for the caster

Damage (3): Like the templar, the inquisitor focuses on divine damage. Heretic's fate at 40 will likely be a power efficient source of damage as its a reactive spell. Just like his good brother, the inquistor doesnt have anything special to bring to the table as far as damage is concerned.

40 - Heretic's fate - DD to enemy allies when enemy is slain
45 - Merciless Invocation - Divine DD
46 - Litany of Torment - AoE divine DD

Mitigation(5/3 instant): With his highest class defining heal being a group heal at 42 (salutary diatribe, a reactive heal), the inquisitor may have to throw more instant heals than other priests. His highest single target class-defining heal is 41 Stinging Penance, which has a DD side effect, and thus might be lower in mitigation power than a mitigation-only heal like some other classes have. In fact, his highest single target heal in general is only lv 42, so he might be fighting an uphill battle on high-con single pulls that deal tons of damage to one target, at least when compared to the mystic and templar. His highest heal is also a haste, so that might help end a battle which is going in the wrong direction, and gives one more haste spell to his roster. Overall I believe the inquistor is probably slightly less potent of a healer than the mystic, which is probably slightly less potent than the templar.

41 - Stinging Penance - Reactive heal + Divine DD
42 - Scolding alleviation - Group heal
42 - Faithful Ministration - Instant heal + offense buff
42 - Salutary diatribe - Group reactive heal
46 - Intolerant Healing - Group heal + haste

Death prevention: Nothing special here.
41 - Redemption - Prevents death + heal + HP buff

Debuff (2): The inquisitors' debuffs are surprisingly defensive, reducing enemy offense, or STR and INT which will likely also reduce damage output. These spells make the party harder to kill instead of making the monsters easier to kill. Vitiation at 49 ties with the Fury for the highest level STR debuff.

48 - Forced Compliance - AoE offense debuff
49 - Vitiation - Str and Int debuff

Utility (5): Wide range of utility abilities, the inquisitor is the only priest who can stun, fear and root individually (not fear+root in one cast). His 39 'CC' ability is a root which will no doubt help make up for some of his potentially lower grade (or at least lower level) healing abilities than other classes. His 42 'save-me' ability isnt quite the get-out-of-jail-free that some other classes have, but his strategy appears to be to kill an enemy who won't leave him alone, instead of making it attack someone else. Has the most crowd control type abilities of all priest classes, and his AoE hate reducer stuns, making it the coolest by far.

**39-Imprison - Root //special CC ability
*42 - Flagellant - Hate reducer + AC buff + DD when attacked //'save-me' ability: probably long recast, self only
40 - Digression - AoE hate reducer + stun
46 - Coerced Repentance - Counterattack when caster is hit
47 - Fearful Conversion - Fear

Flavor: as with the templar, the inquisitor can't transform, but will likely look really cool in plate with an unholy aura and an adept following him around. Like other priest classes, i believe that these skills will have no/little effect on gameplay and instead just be for atmosphere. Don't expect a healing pet (like the mystic kind-of gets).

40 - Summon Unholy Symbol - Summons an unholy symbol
45 - Unholy adept - Summons an adept
50 - Unholy Aura - Covers the caster in an unholy aura

Mystic: The mystic is a potent stat buffer and healer, with a nice compliment of slow causing spells and damage resistance debuffs. His healing power is probably surpassed only by the templar, and his buffing power is probably unsurpassed in its balance. He'll likely be the friend of mages, and he's able to exploit the weaknesses that he causes with his own wide assortment of damage types.

Specialty: Offensive Stat Buffs, Slow, Resistance Debuffs.

Buff (6): Buffs everything, from power to hp to regen, offense to AC. Has divine and noxious resistance buffs, and can also protect against elemental with wards. Has the most variety in types of damage to protect against, but focuses on noxious. Avatar at 47 is the higest STR and AGI buff of priests and is the only direct cast end-game haste from a priest. Runic Talisman at 47 is tied with the templar and warden for the highest group cast AC buff, and is the highest group cast STR buff. The mystic has the market cornered on offensive stat buffs, and has enough HP buffs to go toe-to-toe with the templar; overall he is the most balanced buffer.

43 - Prophetic shield - Group power and noxious resistance
44 - Eidolic Mettle - HP, divine, noxious group buff
44 - Prophecy - HP + Power buff + minor regen
46 - Spirit of the Elephant - Group str and sta buff + ward protecting from elemental and noxious attacks
47 - Avatar - Str, sta, agi buff + haste
47 - Runic Talisman - Group str and AC buff + increased noxious resists every attack

Damage (2): Deals cold, noxious and disease damage (and noxious at 39). This variety may give the mystic a slight edge in finding weaknesses, and will work nicely with his disease debuffs.

45 - Ire of the Grey - Cold DD + snare
46 - Fury of the Ancients - AoE disease DD

Mitigation (6/2 instant): Has his highest group ward at 42, and his highest (regular) single ward at 41, although all but one of his mitigation abilities are solely mitigation, leading me to believe they mitigate more than a spell that does mitigation+effect of the same level. He has the highest mitigation-only single target instant heal in game with Enlightened Healing at 46. Like the other "good" healing classes, he has a class defining heal as his lv 50 'uber' ability. Unlike the templar, he must be immobilized while he keeps his 'uber' ward up. My best guess is that the mystic will mitigate very well because of his (mitigation's spells) focus on pure mitigation, without other effects, but not as well as the templar who has more a balanced distribution of spell levels and types.

41 - Ancestral Aegis - Ward
41 - Rejuvenating chant - Instant heal + noxious cure
42 - Transcendent Blessing - Group heal
42 - Umbral ritual - Group ward
46 - Enlightened Healing - Instant heal
50 - Oberon - Ward - Immobilizes the caster and costs power to maintain

Death prevention: Nothing special here.

40 - Eidolic Savior - Buff that prevents death + adds ward

Debuff (4): The mystics noxious and elemental debuffs give more options for casters in group to exploit than other priests provide. No other priest gets a damage resistance debuff at endgame, and the mystic gets 2 in Howl of the Ancients at 40. His 49 Chemerik is tied with defiler for the highest STR debuff in game, and his AoE slow is the only AoE endgame slow. Strangely enough, he also gets a slow for his lv 39 CC spell, which I believe will be extremely potent, as it immobilizes him (and because all other priests get CC spells at that level). His debuffs will make it harder for the enemy to kill the party, and make it easier for the party to kill the enemy, and in my opinion is the best balanced around debuffer.

40 - Howl of the ancients - AoE Sta, noxious, elemental debuff
49 - Chimerik - Sta and Str debuff
49 - Dreadful Lethargy - AoE slow
**39 - Mourning Soul - Slow but immobilizes the caster //Special CC ability (yes I know that a slow isnt CC, but this is where all priests get their CC)

Utility (4): Unlike all of the other priests, the mystic has NO crowd control spell at all. He spent his lv 39 special CC slot on what is likely to be a powerful single target spell, and thus has little recourse for dealing with overpulls and the like. His 42 Fading Spirit 'save-me' spell seems to be effective, as it both wards damage on self and reduces hate as long as the enemy keeps hitting him; probably the best except for the templars. His AoE hate reducer is nothing special compared to other priests. The 45 Shadowy Attendant could be a real boon, depending on how the spell works in game, I'd assume it performs minor healing/buffs.

42 - Fading Spirit - Ward to the caster + hate reducer when enemy hits //'save-me' ability: probably long recast, self only
43 - Accordant Spirits - AoE hate reducer + minor heal
45 - Shadowy attendant - Summons a pritective spirit that casts beneficial spells on the group
48 - Ursine Prophet - Bear form

Flavor: no idea at all what the 40 Aura Awareness does, possibly a detect good/evil. 50 Form of the Stag could be fun when you're tired of looking like a bear.

40 - Haero's Aura Awareness - See beyond the realm of the living and use the eyes of the spirit ream
45 - Form of the Great Stag - turns the caster into a stag
50 - Talisman of the spiritist - Covers the mystic in a sparkling essence

Defiler: With a full complement of damage spells that also debuff the enemy, and a lv 50 spell which both heals and harms, the defiler is a very offensive priest indeed. While his buffs are probably not as focused on either offense or defense as most other priest classes, his focus is regained when you take a look at his debuffs; he's the clear master of stat debuffing and has the only priest AoE power drain. He has a questionable set of mitigation spells which all cause damage to enemies as they heal, and thus may be a weaker mitigation class. Overall, the defiler is likely to be the most directly offensive priest.

Specialties: Simultaneously Causing Damage And Healing, Stat Debuffing, Draining Power

Buff (5): For the most part, the defilers buffs cause group members to debuff enemies, but he has some HP and stat buffing as well. His 47 Ghastly Shroud is a proxy of the templars mitigation buff Glory of Combat, which basically allows the group to cure itself by hitting enemies, but probably less a potent heal due to its other effects such as STR, AC and causing damage. Tendrils of Terror at 49 brings the defiler back into the shaman role of a slower, albeit through a buff that only works when a group member is hit. Overall, the defiler's buffs don't look to be as potent as those of most priest classes at increasing either defense or offense.

44 - Harbinger - HP, power and defensive buff
45 - Baleful Efflux - Group HP buff + poison ward
45 - Dread Invective - Buff that drains power over time and does a disease DoT to attacker
46 - Voracity - Group str and sta buff - can decrease offense of enemies when hit
47 - Ghastly Shroud - Group Str, AC buff - can cause disease damage to the attacker that is convered into a heal
49 - Tendrils of Terror - Slow and agi debuff when group is hit

Damage (3): With DD's that debuff noxious and arcane at level 44, the defiler might just be a warlock/necro's best friend, especially if they stack (which Iwould imagine they do). Also, like the fury, he spends his lv 50 'uber' spell on a damage spell. Unlike the other fury, or all other priest classes, his spell allows him to play the friend and the foe at once; it's an AoE dot which also heals group power and health, probably making it the most versatile of all the priest 'uber' abilities because it basically damages all enemies and heals all group members power/health at the same time. Ya' can't ask for much more than that. In my opinion, i'd say that the defiler has the edge in causing damage among endgame priest classes.

44 - Ruinous Imprecation - Disease DD + arcane and noxious debuff
44 - Aphotic Corruption - Disease DoT and noxious debuff
50 - Maelstrom of Dismay - AoE DoT that gets converted into power/health. Power cost is periodical and the caster is immobilized //lv 50 'uber' spell

Mitigation (4/2 instant): In my opinion, the defiler is going to be on the lower end of the endgame healing totem pole. He has no endgame group heal. All of his spells but one are mitigation+effect spells, which I think will probably heal for less than a mitigation-only spell (because it's just plain logical that a spell that just heals will heal more than one which heals and does something else too). And to put the icing on the rotten piece of cake, his highest level heal costs him health. Ouch. Having said that, these spells do have interesting offensive effects and might be very useful if there's another healer in the group who's able to pack a bit more healing punch.

40 - Malefic Shroud - Ward + Disease DD for the attacker that heals the target for the same amount
42 - Carrion shield - Ward + Disease DD for the attacker that heals the target for the same amount
43 - Fetid Balm - Instant heal + DoT proc
46 - Sacrifical Allevation - Instant heals that costs health to the caster

Death prevention: Pretty much in line with the other classes, might provide a slight edge as it actually damages whoever attacks the near-death character.

41 - Eerie Aevenger - Buff that prevents death. Summons a shadow to attack the attacker

Debuff(4): The defiler is the master of stat debuffing, almost like the opposite of the mystic who is the master of stat buffing. Between his 47 and 49 spells, he'll be able to reduce every single stat of an enemy, and STR gets a double whammy, being affected by both of his debuffs. Bane of shielding is probably the best mitigation debuff in the game, followed by the templar's lv 46 Mark of Kings, so this in my opinion gives the defiler an edge in debuffing over other priest classes.

40 - Bane of shielding - Debuff that can ward the hit member and do disease DD damage when target attacks
47 - Loathsome Seal - Str, Sta, agi, defense debuff
49 - Abhorrence - Int, Wis and Str debuff

Utility (5): Fear is good, Root is good, but what happens when you cast them both together in one spell? Well if you use 39 Primordial Terror at the right time, it might just be a great CC spell which can keep the caster or other lightly armored folks from being torn apart in an overpull. The AoE Devitalizing Chant at 41 is the only AoE power drain, and the only non-reactive-buff power drain in the priest endgame, and provides the defiler with a unique ability. Crystalize soul 43 is most likely a good downtime healer which will probably be power efficient. The defilers "save-me" spell, Mail of Souls 42 is pretty middle of the road when compared with some other priests that get more protective versions. Overall the utility of the defiler seems decent.

**39 - Primordial terror - Fear + roots when it wears off
41 - Devitalizing Chant - AoE hate reducer, power drain
*42 - Mail of Souls - Hate reducer + disease DD + heal //'save-me' ability: probably long recast, self only
43 - Crystallize soul - Places a soul essence used to heal in the inventory when target dies
48 - Malefic Countenance - Wraith form


Flavor: 40 Turgur's Instant Relief will probably be a bit more fun than another illusion, as it is castable on others, and i'm sure that the illusory hawk image will draw many to the defiler class. As far as the lv 50 'uber' flavor spell, I have no description, but it sounds, well, hot.

40 - Turgur's instant relief - Temporarily forces another person to be sick
45 - Form of the spirit Hawk - turns the caster into an illusory image
50 - Juka's smoldering essence - ?

Warden: Arguably the most versatile priest class, the warden can do a bit of everything. He can buff stats, resists, defense, do direct damage and also has a better variety of heals than average. His defensive AGI and avoidance buffs might be the best in the game, causing monks and bruisers to follow him around like little puppydogs. The only major hole in his repertoire is the lack of decent debuffs, unless for some reason you want to snare mobs. Also, his evac might be very useful depending on exactly how it works.

Specialties: Resistances, Damage Versatility, Snare (may or may not be useful)

Buff (7): The warden has a very balanced set of buffs much like the mystic, but more defensive than stat based. He, like the defiler and templar, has a mitigaton buff and AC buffs. Unlike those classes, or any other priest class for thay matter, he can increase resists against heat and cold, as well as arcane, and also has what is likely to be a very powerful defensive spell, Duststorm at lv 45. I'm guessing that the warden's avoidance+AGI buffs will be very sought after.

40 - Primal Instinct - offense buff
41 - Essence of the Kodiak - HP buff, out of combat power regen + small ward against elemental damage
43 - Praise of the Untamed - Group Power+ elemental buff
44 - Aspect of the Owl - Power, heat and cold buff
45 - Duststorm - Increase avoidance to group, roots the caster
46 - Benison of the wild - Group agi and str + arcane buff, can also heal group when they attack
47 - Spirit of the Oak - Group agi, wis, elemental, AC buff

Damage (3): The combination of two damage types in the warden's endgame spell roster makes him as versatile as the mystic, while all other priest classes must plug away with just one damage type. Additionally, the presence of a DoT and a DD makes he (and the defiler) the only priests with access to both types of spells in the endgame. Dooming swarm at 43 gives him a defense debuff which well likely come in handy, as his regular debuffs seem to well...suck. Overall, the warden might give the defiler a run for his money as both have very versatile damage endgames.

43 - Dooming Swarm - Divine DoT + defense debuff
45 - Winter's bite - Cold DD
46 - Winds of Winter - AoE Cold DD

Mitigation (6/3 instant): At higher levels, the warden seems to break away from one of the potential complaints about the druid class, that their class specific regen heals won't resolve a situation when a character is already low on health and needs protection because regen is too slow. The warden's endgame heals provide an instant heal to kickstart the regen, and hopefully bring a wounded character back into the green before the regen kicks in and protects from further damage. This elevates the warden above the fury and defiler in my opinion, in terms of mitigation. His 'uber' regen ability at 50 immobilizes him, just as the mystic's 'uber' ward at 50, leaving the templar as the only priest with an 'uber' lv 50 mitigation ability that can still move around in battle.

40 - Chloroplast - Group regen + elemental buff
42 - Gale of Healing - Group heal
42 - Chlorostorm - Group regen + ward against elemental
43 - Sylvan waters - Instant heal + regen
46 - Verdant Rapture - Instant heal + regen
50 - Hierophantic Genesis - Regen that immobilizes the caster

Death prevention: pretty much in line with other classes.

41 - Nature's Reprieve - Prevents death + heal + regen

Debuff (2): In my opinion, the warden's debuffs seem as if they will be the least useful of all priest classes. In general, stat debuffs may or may not be useful depending on the way the combat system works, and even if they are, agility is always a suspect stat to buff or debuff. The warden has 2 spells which debuff only agility, and have the additional useless side effect of snare. Pretty much the bottom of the debuffing barrel here. A ray of hope may be found in 49 Tranquilizing Spores, but i'm not really sure if this is a buff or debuff from the description (either way it sounds promising, but not enough for me to think that the warden will be a decent debuffer.

47 - Undergrowth - AoE agi debuff + snare
49 - Seizing vines - Agi debuff + snare
49 - Tranquilizing Spores - Chance of regen + slow on enemy when hit //not sure if this is a buff or debuff

Utility (6): Forget crowd control, the warden simply evacs the group with Verdurous Journey at 39! While I don't know if this is a long cast time spell, or non-combat spell, I do know that it's given at 39 which is when the other priest classes are getting their crowd control spells, so I'd assume it can be used in combat, making the warden the only priest to have an endgame evac. The 42 Verdant Sigh 'save-me' spell is better than average, a regen that also reduces hate with every hit is likely to keep you alive until the mob decides to leave you alone. Unfortunately, Seizing brambles at 41 is possibly the worst of the hate reducers, as its additional effect is a probably useless snare (though that could benefit you if you're so wounded that you're running away).

**39 - Verdurous Journey - Teleports group to safe spot
*42 - Verdant Sigh - Regen + hate reducer when hit //'save-me' ability: probably long recast, self only

44 - Sylph - AoE Hate reducer, snare + teleport of the caster a short distance away

Flavor: Leading the pack with 4 flavor spells, i'm going to go ahead and guess that the Warden will have the coolest spell flavor effects that are castable on others.

40 - Form of the Strange Bear - Changes the target into a strange creature

Fury: The fury seems to be the master of increasing group DPS, with a variety of buffs which all work together to add attacks, cause additional damage and just create carnage in general. Surprisingly, he doesnt seem to have much to contrbute to group DPS on his own, as he has no endgame DD spells except his lv 50 which causes him to remain still, and groups may not like to have their healer focusing so much on DD. He has a strange assortment of mitigation spells like the defiler, which may or may not be effective in healing the group but all have other effects. He also has two interesting spells which may or may not work as i imagine, a group run buff which I believe can be used in battle (crosses fingers) and a group invis. These utility spells are unique enough that he may be sought out for those as well.

Specialties: Increasing Group DPS, Only Lv 50 DD Priest Spell, Interesting Utility Spells

Buff (7): The fury has 7 buffs, and they all have the same purpose, to make your group kick butt. While the inquisitor has indirect DD buffs and reactive damage buffs and the like, the fury increases your damage the old fashioned way; through extra attacks, damage adds and procs. He can buff AC and stats also, but his buffing focus is clearly on damage, and I believe he'll be the best DPS increasing priest.

40 - Bloodlust - Agi and str buff + additional slashing damage
41 - Ferocity of the hunt - HP buff + increase in HP regen and speed out of combat
44 - Vim - Power Buff + power regen out of combat
45 - Fae Flames - adds heat DoT proc
45 - Feral Salve - Instant heal + lion transformation + increase of slashing damage
46 - Ferine Mask - Group Agi, Str, AC buff + additional slashing attack
46 - Ferine Spirit - Group agi, int, wis, arcane buff + potential damage increase

Damage (1): Strangely enough, the master of adding to DPS is able to make very little contribution himself until 50 (or at least his endgame spells would suggest). Pre 40, the fury had a couple of magic based DD's, but since then, he gets nothing, zip, nada until his 'uber' spell which, like the other 'evil' priest classes, is a damage spell. I'd imagine that it is pretty damaging indeed, but i'm not sure how a DD requires power to maintain, that sounds more like a DoT. Maybe it damages in waves. Either way, in my book, i'd prefer that my healer isn't immobilized while casting a DD...it's one thing to slip in a nuke here and there, it's another to forget about everything else and cast a stationary DD.

50 - Porcupine - AoE DD - Costs power to maintain and immobilizes the caster

Mitigation(6): An interesting variety of heals are available to the fury, most of which buff as well as mitigate damage. To me, that means that they probably don't heal as well as migitation only heals. The buffs present on the heals here, however, look to be the best mitigation buffs in the priest endgame. Lv 46 Starfire's AoE HoT DoT will likely be as useful as it is fun to say, meaning that it will be very nice, sort of like a watered down version of the inquisitor's 'uber' lv 50 DoT + power/health regen, except without that pesky immobilization. I think that the fury will probably be on the lower end of ability to actually mitigate damage, but the great buffs on his actual mitigation spells will still make him desirable.

40 - Wild Bloodflow - Regen + damage buff
42 - Owl's restoration - Group heal + defense buff
42 - Howl of the Untamed - Instant heal
43 - Savage Feast - Group regen + sta and str buff when enemy dies
46 - Starfire - HoT DoT + AoE
47 - Ferine Elixir - Instant Heal + str and sta buff

Death prevention: pretty much like the other classes.

42 - Feral Tenacity - Prevents death + heal + defense increase

Debuff (2): The Fury ties with the inquisitor and defiler for having a level 49 STR debuff. Unfortunately for him they also have plenty of other debuffs, while fury fans have to hope that the Irritating Swarm at 47 does big things to enemy offense. Overall the debufs of the fury seem just slightly more potent than those of the warden, but weaker than those of all other priests.

47 - Irritating Swarm - AoE offense and agi debuff
49 - Beastly Intimidation - Str and wis debuff

Utility (4): Much like his nature based brother the warden, the lv 39 utility spell of the fury is not a CC spell. It is a run speed spell, and i'm going to cross my fingers and hope that it works in combat (because most of the other classes get a spell at 39 which serves as a last ditch effort for crowd control). Untamed Shroud at 45 is a group invis, and if i remember correctly this and the run speed boost were thought to be in the domain of rogues. Either these are better than the rogue counterparts (which wouldn't be too much of a shock, as they are very high level), or the rogues are better and these can serve in the case that there is no rogue in the group. The 42 Toxic Quills 'save-me' ability will probably be slightly more effective than the average, because it not only provides an initial DD, but also throws damage onto the mob whenever it hits. Overall, the fury has a unique utility package which will probably make him sought after.
