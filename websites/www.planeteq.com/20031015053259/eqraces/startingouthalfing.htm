Starting Out as a Halfling (EQ Races)

Halflings start out in the city of Rivervale. (Click for more) It will be a bit confusing at first but you will soon learn your way around.

The first thing to do is find your guild leader. You should have started out fairly close to your guild if not in it. If you right click on the 'tattered note' (note name and contents may be different depends on starting guild), there will be a small paragraph detailing the name of the guild your joining and who you are to report to. Look at the name of the person and then set about finding them, they should be close by. Hand in the note by left clicking it, placing it on the person, then left clicking again, a window will pop up with the note displayed, press Trade if your sure its the right person.

You will be given an item usually a tunic of some sort, and then told either to start a small quest, or report to another person. Its usually a good idea to write down the quest items or the name of who you have to go. Before you continue with your starting quests, it might interest you to look for some things to bash. First, right click on your guild master, then on the menu that pops up, click on the weapon type you will be using. (Hold down right click on the weapon you got at the start of the game and it will tell you what type it is.) Then click Train 5 times (You are given 5 training points at the start of the game). This will give you a skill of 5 in that weapon type, it will help you to get started.

Now, exit your guild and explore until you find the Priest of Discord, hes a tall human usually wearing a purple robe. (If you zone into Kithicor Forest walk back to Rivervale by turning around and back the way you came.) When you find him you should see two deputies guarding a large passage. Walk down this passage and after awhile you will zone into Misty Thicket.

Notice the two deputies standing near the passage and the road leading ahead from the entrance. If you find yourself outnumbered you can run to the deputies and they will assist you. Its best to stay near the road so you can find your way back easily.

Open your inventory and put the weapon into your left hand (primary melee slot). Then using the 'action buttons' on the bottom right of your screen select Combat. When you left click on a monster (e.g bat, snake etc), clicking the picture of your weapon in the combat menu will attack it. Before you attack anything 'con' it by right clicking. This will tell you how strong it is. If the text appears white or black, it is equal to your level, if its blue it is below, if its yellow it is 1 or 2 above your level, if its red it is many levels above you. Stick to equal or lower level cons for now.

After awhile you will gain enough experience to level up and will hear a 'Ding', this gives you 5 more training points to use at the trainer, and more hit points, mana (if your a casting class) and slightly higher 'caps' (limits) on your skill ratings/levels.

Check with your trainer as you level up, to see if you can train a new skill. You will see Level 4, Level 10, etc next to the skill, left click on it and click Train if you meet that requirement, and you will now be able to use that skill and to increase its rating/level.
