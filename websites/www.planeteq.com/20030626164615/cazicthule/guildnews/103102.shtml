Strategic Alliance
October 22nd: Tonight was our first foray into Howling Stones. Our first break-in was interesting to say the least. Carse, our entertainer(bard), cried like a girl for heals when his invised popped in the middle of a large group of Scorps. Lumpeey, cleric extraordinaire, refused to pop her own invis to heal him. After a quick rez, we were ready to zone in. Vaughan, the only porter/evacer present, realized he forgot his key, shortly after our valiant guild leader zoned into the dungeon. Fortunately Nato was able to camp out quickly, since another guild had recently cleared the zone-in. After retrieving his key, and enduring repeated verbal abuse, we finally all zoned in and secured the platform. Due to repeated connection problems and LDs, we cut our raid short after travelling a short way into the North Wing.

October 23rd: We took out our frustrations by attempting to annihilate the Darkpaw Gnolls of Jaggedpine Forest. We fought deep into the gnoll caves, killing gnolls as we went, until we arrived in a large cavern with a named gnoll present. We pulled Barduck, and with our little band of adventurers, got him down to about 10% health when a defiler spawned nearby. With our entertainer rooted, our evacer feared, and our ranger charmed, Barduck made short work of Lumpeey, which in turn spelled death for the rest of the party. Thanks to our expert corpse retrieval unit, we were all rezzed and safe within the hour.

October 26th: Grats to Lumpeey on 55.

October 27th: Our first guild raid into Kedge Keep. We successfully camped the mermaid temple and took down several named mermaids, as well as CauldronBoil and CauldronBubble. Guyl temporarily dinged 55 before his token death of the evening.

October 28th: We returned to Howling Stones, with the intent of seeing more of this exciting zone. After liberal use of the Lessor Succor spell, we were able to develop tactics to allow us to break into the North Wing successfully. We had only one death (Guyl as usual), some good druid spell drops (Mask of the Howler and Circle of Winter), and succesfully traversed the entire North Wing. Grats to Guyl on 55 again, we hope it sticks.

October 29th: Our largest guild raid yet, we took our massive group of 8 into the Tower of Frozen Shadow, with the intent of getting some good exp on the higher floors for our mid-40 players, but we soon tired of killing greens on level 2 to get a keyholder spawn. We all ported to Toxx Forest and entered the Hole, which was deserted, much to our surprise. We dropped down and camped the city entrance for nearly 2 hours with no deaths, not even Guyl.

Ronthen Stormfinder - 56 Ranger - Raid leader
