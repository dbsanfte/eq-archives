    EQ2: Nov 30, Dec 1, and Dec 2, 2004
    12/3/04, 3:21 PST | Muskie

    Update messages for the Last 3 days Follow:


    Update Notes: December 2, 2004 12/2/2004 7:00 am


    Today’s changes include the following:


    *** Gameplay***

    - Fixed an issue preventing players from completing the Deathfist Citadel access quest if their journal was full.
    - Lord Chesgard in the Tomb of Valor will no longer respawn immediately after being killed.
    - Ghosts within the Tomb of Valor have begun to wander restlessly.
    - Alethea Augustine in West Freeport will no longer attempt to offer every tradeskill profession to players once a profession has been chosen.
    - Status quests are now assigned based on the character's level rather than their guild's level. Status awards have been adjusted accordingly.


    *** Spells ***

    - Necromancer: Grisly Mark spell line now also decreases Noxious mitigations on its target victim.
    - Inquisitor: Favor of the Repentant and Contrite Grace spell lines had their reprisal damage adjusted.
    - Inquisitor: Penitent's Sermon spell line now properly heals the entire group.


    The EverQuest II Team


    (Previous day's patch notes follow.)


    Update Notes: December 1, 2004 12/1/2004 7:00 am


    Today’s changes include the following:


    *** Gameplay ***

    The con colors of encounters that are level 20 and higher have been modified to more accurately reflect the challenge of the encounter.
    - Red means the fight is extremely difficult to defeat.
    - Orange indicates a very tough battle.
    - Most yellow, white, and high-blue cons remain about the same.
    - Low-blue and green encounters are somewhat easier than before.
    Committing a hostile act against a broken encounter will cause you to regenerate health and mana at the same rate as being locked to that encounter. If no further hostile actions are taken for ten seconds, you will again regenerate at the non-combat rate.
    NPCs that are not part of a locked encounter no longer consume power when casting spells.


    *** User Interface **

    - Using the Auto-Bank feature when your bank is full should no longer destroy a partial stack of items when they are dragged from your inventory.


    *** Characters, Appearances, and Rendering ***

    - Fixed a problem that caused NPCs to sometimes be invisible.


    The EverQuest II Team


    Update Notes: November 30, 2004 11/30/2004 8:00 am


    Today’s changes include the following:


    - Dreena the Mess Cook's respawn time has been changed to a more appropriate value.
    - Several bosses in Permafrost, Runnyeye Citadel, and the Feerrot have been modified.
    - Fixed an issue that caused "a Runnyeye Crusader" to drop treasure he wasn't meant to possess.
    - The Qeynos Catacomb catalog quest should now be updating correctly.
    - Ghostly inhabitants of the Commonlands will now be visible at a distance.
    - Players should no longer get stuck in the middle of the Orcish Wastes access quest if they crashed or went linkdead after the boat encounter.
    - Players should once again be able to access Firemyst Gully.
    - Players betraying Qeynos should no longer be able to get a class quest in Freeport. They will, however, be able to receive their subclass quest once they've become a citizen of the Overlord's city. Those who are stuck in their progression because they started and completed the class quest should contact Customer Service using /petition for assistance.
    - The warlock's Dark Distortion spell line had its damage increased.
    - The sorcerer's Freezing Whorl spell now has an added poison mitigation debuff.
    - The Mystic's Keening Haze spell line's "slow" (attack speed decrease) component no longer stacks with the shaman's Wailing Haze "slow" component.


    The EverQuest II Team

    The State of EQLive
    12/1/04, 11:15 PST | Kwill

    Let's face it, we are all feeling the impact of the release of EQ2. I have been hearing how some high level raiding guilds are disbanding because their players are going off to do other things in EQ2 and elsewhere, and how casual mid-level players are finding it hard to get a group in what used to be crowded hunting spots.

    The raiding guilds are feeling the pinch because of the lack of clerics, for the most part, and the overall lack of players, still perhaps eager to raid, but who just don't have the equipment or the flags to go raid in the zones that need experienced, upper level players. Some guilds are working through the problem and have survived the cataclysm of EQ2, and some have not.

    The casual players are finding on many servers there isn't anyone to group with. Finding a group at the lower levels is getting more difficult. There just aren't the number of players on 24/7 like there used to be.

    So is there a solution? I don't think the horizon is as dark as it might appear. Firstly, EQ2 takes a powerful computer to run. Not everyone can upgrade to play the game, and those folks will still be enjoying EQLive just as much as ever without a state of the art machine that can handle the graphics load EQ2 asks of them. Secondly, EQ2 is not a newer version of EQLive: it's a different game entirely. With more content based on questing, solo content, and small group activities, the raiding folks are probably going to come back to raid-oriented EQLive at some point. Okay, so perhaps when the dust settles and the gloss on the new game gets a little worn people will come back--but until then, how can we play EQLive given the population changes in the game right now?

    Here are some good suggestions from veteran players:

  • Take groups consisting of varied classes, not just the traditional cleric/warrior/enchanter. Other classes can accomplish crowd control, healing, fighting, and other tasks reserved for the power three. It's a chance to experience the game differently than you played it before, and get creative in your encounters. Personally, I have gotten pretty good at soloing my 40 enchanter with charm when I can't find a group. Learn to play your class well, and exploit some of the lesser used abilities all the classes have as a way to still have fun. Some of the best groups are ones that seem like they can never work -- but smart strategies can make up for a lack of healing, crowd control, or a traditional tank.

  • Start a "looking for a group" chat channel on your server, and use it. One was started on the Cazic Thule server and it's a great way to start talking to people across zones. Think about the bazaar concept: if people looking for a group gather in one spot such as the Plane of Knowledge or the Plane of Tranquility, those looking for a group can more easily find one another.

    For some, it will take a little more work now than just going to the old crowded hunting grounds and getting a group there for experience grind. We aren't told how many people are playing or not playing right now, but there are still plenty of people who want to group; you may just have to look a little harder and start thinking about more creative groups. Raiding guilds may need to combine forces and work with each other to experience the end game content -- alliances are a powerful tool.

    Don't forget, another expansion of EQLive is coming out soon -- the EQLive Team is still in there creating content and new adventures for us. EQLive is still a fun and interesting game, with features unique to itself. EQ2 has better graphics, sure, because it runs on a new engine that can handle the textures and shading the old EQLive engine just can't. But if you consider the content and the game play, I think you will find that EQLive still has something to offer. Different designers, different concepts for each game give us a choice. That's always a good thing.

    LDoN Points Issue
    12/1/04, 11:03 PST | Kwill

    There have been some issues with LDoN points, specifically that players cannot see or buy items costing over 1000 points, no matter how many points they have in a specific camp or in total points. After some discussion on the message boards about it, Bulvinne posts on the EQL forums:
    If you sell back items your available point total will NOT update with the merchants until after you have completed your next LDON.

    - AFTER you have gained over 1492 CAMP points in any camp the items you can VIEW is based on your current adventure point total.

    - If at anytime your AVAILABLE adventure points drops below 996 You will not be able to see the higher point valued items available on merchants. Regardless if your career camp total is 5000 or 2000.

    Example: Player has 580 available adventure points. A career total in Mistmoore of 2214. They can see all items in the Butcher Block wayfarer's camp up to 996. They sell back two augments for 700 points each. Their new available total is 1980. Player will not be able to use these points until after they run one more adventure.

    *Note* I know for a fact your points will update if you win or partial win an adventure. I am not certain if the points will update if you lose an adventure.

    So to get your point totals to work, you need to run another adventure after selling back your items to the merchant. Hopefully the devs will correct this "bug" when they have time!


    Backflagging in EQLive
    12/1/04, 10:59 PST | Kwill

    Backflagging

    As you know, when a guild gains or loses members, they often find themselves dedicating a large amount of time re-playing content that they have long since completed in order to gain access to zones for newer members. During our Community Summit this last summer, and in many subsequent discussions, we have heard the recurring desire to find a way to ease the frustrations associated with this backflagging.

    While the issue is not as simple as removing flags altogether (we feel flags are necessary for the zones they are currently attached to), we have looked into ways that we can reduce the frustrations of backflagging and feel that we have arrived at a fair and equitable solution, which is detailed below.

    - A new implementation of the 85/15 rule will be introduced into Planes of Power zones including the Plane of Time. This should resolve the outstanding problems that occur from time to time with the current 85/15 implementation.

    - The new 85/15 will also be introduced for the locked zones in Gates of Discord.

    - An alternate means of flagging guild mates for locked content in Planes of Power and Gates of Discord will be implemented that will allow you to flag raid members for the zone you are in by completing raid content within that zone.

    The implementation varies slightly from zone to zone to fit the story and nature of the zone, but in general will involve killing a raid target within that zone, looting an item, and completing a single group quest to change that item into a zone flag. This only applies to zones that don't already have a single group task or system to gain access.

    We will be opening threads on the forums for you to discuss these proposed changes and to gather your feedback on whether you feel this will hg!elp to resolve the current problems with backflagging. Please join the threads on our EQ forums and let us know your thoughts on these proposed changes. We hope to have these on the test server shortly after Thanksgiving and Live after proper testing.

    The EverQuest Team.

    EQ2 Character Transfers
    11/30/04, 8:58 PST | Kwill

    You can now transfer characters from one server to another in EQ2. No firm dates are set, but will probably take place during a patch. You can find a general FAQ on the official EQ2 site.

    The transfers are voluntary, they cost nothing, but you have to be online when the annoucement is made. You will be told at that time what server you can transfer to. Characters cannot move back once moved.

    However, on November 24 Ryan Schwayder made this annoucement:

    We are continuing to accept character transfer requests on the following servers:

  • Mistmoore to Neriak
  • Antonia Bayle to Neriak
  • Valor to Innovation
  • Runnyeye to Faydark
  • Permafrost to Faydark

    • We do not currently have a firm date for when the actual transfers will take place, but they will occur while the servers are being updated.
