Poll Controversy
6/7/04, 10:51 PST | Kwill

Yes, it was only an in-game poll, not an action, but even SOE asking about selling game items for cash fired up the player base last week. Obviously, SOE was thinking about it. It is a very controversial issue that will affect the game. SOE wants to stem the proliferation of items for sale as it stands now, and with that action shut down the sweatshop grind of paid farmers getting those items. However, by legimizing the sale of items, they will get many more people buying items for cash. Right now, it's a black market. By taking it off the black market, you regulate pricing and keep the fraud out of the equation. But by making it acceptable to buy items for cash, you take away any hesitiation by players to twink themselves out in the best gear by buying it rather than earning it. EverQuest has traditionally been a game that has a level playing field -- it doesn't matter how much money you have in real life, as long as you can afford a computer, a net connection, and the monthly fee. Now theoretically it will matter how much money you have, because those with lots of discretionary income don't have to work for the good items, they can just buy them. An irrelevant argument, because the items can be gotten for free by working in game for them? Possibly. Still, the haves and the have-nots may become much more obvious than before. At the guild summit, the one comment made about the whole issue by the staff was that there is no plan to create items, just provide an opportunity to buy items that already exist for cash. Will it happen? No plans yet, but they are discussing it in house, I think.

   Wednesday, June 2, 2004

Guild Leader Summit This Weekend
6/2/04, 14:00 PST | Kwill

This weekend, SOE will be hosting guild leaders and fan site directors at the SOE offices for meetings and discussions on Saturday. I will be attending, and I will be posting my impressions and accounts of the discussions that go on with the guild leaders and the SOE staff.

The guild leader invitees are from the top guilds on the server, who know the game and represent a lot of subscribers, and are concerned with player progressions to the higher content. Here is a message board posting from Vannoth about how people were selected for an invitation:

Just because we have one event where we invite the top end to be heard does not mean that we are not listening to everyone else. What do you think Fan Faires are for? Isn't this the Fan Faire forum? Do we not have development feedback time at Fan Faire?

The simple truth is that SOE can't invite everyone, and it would be impossible to identify the 50 "best casual" guilds in EQ.

We also understand that the feedback given from the Uber Guilds is not indicative of the entire EQ community.

Also, this is not an uberguild only event, we are also inviting The Safehouse, Druids Grove, The Runes.net, EQVault, Stratics, Mobhunter, berserker.org and a few other sites, so if you wish something to be brought up on your behalf at the event, I suggest you take it to the appropriate site and ask to contribute feedback. Heck you can post it here in this thread, and I'll personally bring it up if its valid.

I wish I could invite everyone, but that's just not an option. Besides, thats what Fan Faires are for. Personally, I feel the EQ Community as a whole should be very happy that SOE is making these strides. I've always been quite proud to be the one who puts on events that connect you, the players, directly to the decision makers at SOE. This event is just the company taking it one step further and proving that we really are interested in what you think and want. I'm very proud that we are doing this event.

I would classify myself as a casual player (and I still do play, five years now!), so as for those concerns, I will be one who will ask questions concerning this player base. I understand that Woody Hearn, from GU Comics, is also attending, and he is a casual player as well from what I have read -- and a very outspoken critic of SOE when he sees the need to be!

As always, I will strive to bring my readers an interesting account of what happens at the Guild Leader Summit.

Updates

From Updates:
June 2, 2004

June 2nd, 2004
----------------------

Collision fixes collide with servers!

Collision
----------------------
- When you are summoned by NPCs or players smaller than you, the collision system will now scan a limited area near the destination to find a place to put you so that you will not be stuck in the terrain.

- A problem related to collision data being out of sync on the client and server has been corrected and should solve the problems many of you have been seeing with NPCs being pushed into walls.

- The sunken NPC and character problem has been much improved. You will still find the occasional NPC at a spawn point that is sunk or elevated slightly, but the NPC should move normally once engaged.

Cabilis Quests
----------------------
- The Battle plans from Dalnir should be a little easier to obtain.
- The loyal follower in Dreadlands should no longer be a merchant with an empty inventory.
- The traitor necromancers seek should be a little easier to find.
- Wyzith's belongings should now combine correctly.

-- The EverQuest Team

Changes on Test
6/2/04, 13:39 PST | Kwill

Proposed Changes

Hey folks,

I wanted to give you all an update on what we are planning for the coming weeks as we work on some persistent in-game issues. We know that many of you are concerned with the current state of the game and we feel confident that the changes we have planned will help to alleviate many of your concerns.

First on our plate, and number one on your list of concerns, is collision in EverQuest. We are working hard to fix the current problems with collision and have many fixes currently on Test. We will most likely be pushing these collision fixes to the Live servers tomorrow, June 2nd at 3AM PDT. Many of the current issues appear to be resolved with this latest push and our programmers are still working hard to track down and fix any remaining issues. For those of you on Test, thanks very much for your help in getting these issues to Grumbuk.

We are also scheduling out many other fixes, with the first round going to the Test server this week, and subsequent rounds as the changes are made. Currently planned to hit Test this week are the following proposed changes;

- Add a quest to allow the exchange of Gates of Discord spells.
- Correct an issue with the berserker epic quest that left some berserkers unable to complete the quest.
- Improve the Eagle Eye spell series.
- Increase the spawn rate of the Shattered Emerald of Corruption.
- Change berserker snare to have a set duration.
- Correct the pathing in Txevu.
Please remember that this is only the first round of planned changes and there are many more to come. As more changes are rolled out to the Test server, I will post them. Please feel free to discuss these changes on the EQ forums at eqforums.station.sony.com.

Thanks for all of your feedback this past week. You have shown, once again, what a strong community EQ has, thank you for being an active part of it!

Brenlo

Letter from John Smedley
6/2/04, 13:36 PST | Kwill

Hello Norrath!

I wanted to announce that we've decided to push Omens of War back to Late August in order to give the EQ team time to respond to important issues that the EverQuest community is raising (specifically including the issues with Gates of Discord). We've heard some concerns from within the community that we feel are important enough to push back our next expansion and spend time fixing the problems you have brought to our attention. At the end of the day, we work for you and it's important for us to demonstrate that we both understand and appreciate that. We're also inviting many members of the community to join us here at SOE for a day to have a direct dialog with the development team about issues that are important to the EverQuest community as a whole. We've already sent out the invitations and are making all the arrangements. In addition, our EQ community managers will be coming up with a list of issues raised on the message boards so that we can come back to you with a specific list of things we're going to address.

I'm sorry if you feel we haven't been listening and reacting appropriately. We do our best to balance all of the needs and concerns you raise along with expanding Norrath and giving you new and wonderful things to do in EverQuest, but we do make mistakes. We've heard you loud and clear.

We have many things planned for EverQuest over the upcoming years, and our goal is to keep you happily playing EverQuest for a long time to come. The best way for us to do that is by first and foremost listening to you.

John Smedley
President, Sony Online Entertainment
