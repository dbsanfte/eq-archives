Newest Expansion: Omens of War
5/6/04, 17:31 PST | Kwill

Another expansion is on the way! And it finally happened: level caps above 65. Here are the features:
Epic Weapons:
Epic weapons are a result of completing an "epic quest." These hand-tailored quests will be available to all classes. The quest themselves will be a combination of solo, group and raid content. However, these weapons are being designed for people at the upper end of the level spectrum.

The weapons themselves will be unique in design. Additionally, the weapons will have specialized characteristics that set them apart from traditional weapons.

Level Cap Increase:
The level cap increase is being designed to allow players the ability to reach levels above 65. With the level cap increase, new items, spells and quests will be available.

Guild Tribute System
This system is extremely similar to the Tribute system that's currently in game. However, the main difference is that Guild Tribute will affect the entire guild, instead of the individual.

Task System:
The task system allows players to go to various zones and request a task. These tasks are being designed for quick completion and are initially aimed at lower level players.

Custom PC Titles:
Players will be able to select titles for their characters based on various accomplishments. These titles will be available across all levels.

New Disciplines/AAs/Items/Spells/Quests/Tradeskills:
New disciplines, AAs, items, spells, quests and tradeskills continue to make EverQuest the most compelling and persistent online world ever created.

Improved Graphics:
Discover rich new zones, detailed environments with expanded graphical features, improved spell effects and all-new NPC character models to fight.

The backstory:
Follow the Wayfarers Brotherhood to the war-ravaged Realm of Discord to help end the tyrannical ways of the Muramites once and for all. Mata Muram, the leader of the Muramites, is rumored to be building the ultimate invading army. It's up to you to thwart his plans.

The Wayfarers Brotherhood is considering meeting the Muramites on their home turf to end their tyrannical ways once and for all, in a place called the Realm of Discord.

The Brotherhood is still puzzled by the realm, unsure if it will take the risk of going there. However, this team of wayfarers is doing what it can to learn from the Taelosians about the dark lands. There has been talk of preparing to build a portal in order to open a way to Discord - much like the Taelosians once did, except this time Norrath would have the advantage of preparation.

Taelosians have told the Wayfarers Brotherhood that the lands of Discord are most surely a twisted and volatile place - even the Muramites seem to fear them. Word tells of constant war there.

The Muramites have been heard commenting that Taelosia is a welcome break from the chaotic Realm of Discord, with its crazed creatures and dangerous magic. Nothing there is at it seems.

Everyone has now learned of Mata Muram, the leader of the legion - a terrible creature seeking to build the ultimate army by any means necessary, including stealing beings from other worlds.

Mata Muram is much older than almost every member of his army, and well protected. He is described by Muramites as unmerciful, powerful, a worthy leader - although there are rumors that he is obsessed with revenge against someone. Some Muramites have been overheard describing Mata Muram as some sort of dragon.

No one knows when the Realm of Discord and its counterpart, the Realm of Order, came into existence. In both are rulers that are all-knowing and all-seeing, yet are never seen or touched - much like the Nameless.

More information is available on the Omens of War website.


Gates of Discord Tuned Up
5/6/04, 17:29 PST | Kwill

Gates of Discord gets retuned!

Greetings Norrath!

Gates of Discord is being tuned to rework many of the experience areas to better balance the expansion for all classes. It is our hope that this re-tuning will allow for more group diversity and better use of individual class skills while maintaining the challenge of the new content. We hope to roll out these changes within the next 2 weeks. Here's a quick breakdown of what's changed -

* Experience has been increased in several zones.
* Natimbi, Qinimi, Barindu, Riwwi, and Ferubi should be tuned more appropriately for their intended level ranges.
* NPCs will no longer hit for max damage as often. Knights in particular should find it easier to tank mobs they previously had difficulty with.
* Spell drops have been increased.
* Zone travel has been made easier.
* NPC innates have been adjusted to behave a little more reasonably.
* Animals in various zones across Gates have been made non-agro.

Note: Please keep in mind that these changes do not affect every zone. We would like to thank all of the folks on the Test Server who have been helping with the re-working of the encounters and we hope that you enjoy the changes when they go live in the very near future.

Thanks all!

The EverQuest Design Team

Some Updates
5/5/04, 15:02 PST | Kwill

May 5th, 2004

Happy Cinco De Mayo!

Surnames
--------------------
You can now use the /surname command to remove your own surname and add a new one. Please remember to read the naming policy as listed on EQlive.station.sony.com as it applies to both first names and surnames. You will still need to ask for CS assistance if you wish to have a name with double capitals or special characters. Please note that this command can only be used to remove or change your surname once per week.

HP Regen
---------------------
- All characters should now regenerate HP faster when sitting. The longer you sit, the faster you regenerate!
- You will not receive increased regen while under the effects of a DOT or while feigned.
- You can now bandage yourself up to 70% of your HP. Folks with the appropriate AA abilities can bandage above 70%. - You can now bandage while sitting.

/rewind
----------------------
/rewind is back and should help if you find yourself stuck. This is not an automatic rewind, you will have to execute the command. We are continuing to work on other collision issues and hope to no longer need /rewind in the near future. This command is only able to be used once every 30 seconds, and in order to make use of it, your character will need to be stationary for at least 30 seconds.

EQII Beta Sign-Ups Start Today

EVERQUEST(r) II BETA SIGN-UPS BEGIN TODAY!

May 3, 2004 - Beta sign-ups begin today for EverQuest(r) II, the next-generation massively multiplayer online (MMO) game from Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE). Thousands of players are expected to apply in order to test the game beginning this June. EverQuest II will be available at retail in North America after the Beta program is complete later in the year. Dedicated, longtime subscribers to SOE's EverQuest Legends service will be the first allowed into Beta, followed by players that fulfilled the requirements of the Lords of EverQuest(tm) special offer. Both groups are being contacted directly by SOE via email so members of those groups need not apply for Beta. Additional Beta testers will be selected from applications received through the EverQuest II website. Gamers interested in signing up for the Beta are encouraged to visit http://www.everquest2.com SOE will notify qualified Beta applicants in stages over the coming months.

About EverQuest II
EverQuest II is the next generation of massively multiplayer gaming, a huge online world where thousands of players come together for adventure and community. Featuring breathtaking graphics and a vast, beautiful game world to explore, EverQuest II sets the standard for graphical realism as players are immersed in the game's exciting locales and dangerous lands. EverQuest II brings players into its world with a powerful epic storyline and gives them the power to be the hero or villain in their personal adventure. Players enter this world by creating their own unique character, choosing from 16 races and 24 classes. Players will encounter hundreds of creatures as they travel across majestic landscapes of rolling hills, barren deserts, dense forests, and bustling cities. Thousands of items, hundreds of spells, and unlimited adventure await all who enter the world of EverQuest II. EverQuest II also features:
* A new, massive online world for thousands of players to explore, filled with danger, adventure and mystery
* Voices for almost every non-player character (NPC) in the game, a huge project encompassing nearly 100,000 lines and 130 hours of dialog (the equivalent of more than 65 feature films worth of content)
* A state-of-the-art, cinematic quality 3D graphics engine capable of improving as hardware technology advances
* Amazing character customization capabilities. In addition to thousands of pieces of armor, weapons and items, players will be able to customize their character's facial appearance to an incredible level of detail and personalization
* 16 races, 50 levels and 24 classes with a branching system that allows players to choose their class as they advance
* No race/class combination limitations, allowing players to truly choose who they want to be in game
* Player-owned real estate (such as apartments, homes, and guild houses)
* State-of-the art combat, spell and skill systems
* New user friendly game mechanics with reduced learning curve for both new and seasoned players
* Trade skills available to all player classes
* More than 160 unique creature types, from Orcs and Goblins to huge dragons and menacing Living Statues

Game Theory in EQII
4/29/04, 8:41 PST | Kwill

Game Theory: A Conversation with Chris Cao, Lead Developer for EverQuest II

At Fan Faire, I had a chance to sit down with Chris Cao, one of the two lead developers of EverQuestII, and talk to him a little bit about gaming design. We were at a Star Trek themed restaurant, and if there is anything that is yet an “undiscovered frontier” it’s the universes that game developers are creating for all of us to play in. EverQuest is five years old now -- old school in the market -- but the field of massively multiplayer online games is just beginning to blossom. The EverQuestII team is working to be again on the cutting edge of this genre, just as EverQuest was. Chris and I talked about what kind of theories and ideas game developers have to consider to create an engaging and sustainable game world that’s fun to play in, ideas that turned out to be more complex than you might at first imagine them to be.

Chris worked on the live team for EQ before moving to the EQII team as lead developer. He came to gaming after being a science teacher in the public schools, finding that his physics background lent itself naturally to scripting of game code, and his life experience lent itself to designing fun, challenging games that have the complexity to sustain themselves over time. He has thought a great deal about complex issues that impact a game of the scope of EverQuest and EverQuest II, including building community, the economy, playability, and how to design games so people want to log in and keep coming back over time. Currently, his job consists mostly of making lists, he told me, keeping the project on track and making sure that everything gets done the way it should be. But his first love is game design and the details that go into creating a world that people want to adventure in.

My first impression of Chris was how thoughtful he was about game design, and the importance he stressed of balancing the game between fun and sustainability. Game designers have to make decisions about how they are going to treat the players as the game progresses. The tone that the developers take makes a huge difference on how the game ends up being played. Will the developers, and later the live team, take a very strict authoritarian role, forcing players to play the game as they envision it, squelching any deviation from the assumed gameplay with nerfs and changes? Or will they be permissive, and change things as the players ask for them, realizing that some of the changes may ruin overall gameplay in the long term? Where do they go when suddenly a new play style in the game shows up that completely differs from the original design idea and changes all the encounters? Chris was very earnest in explaining that many times developers and designers seem to be making changes that at the outset seem unfair, but in fact are making sure that the game remains playable and enjoyable over time. If the game can be “won” in a short period of time, players will leave and find other games to play. Live team changes are a result of many discussions about what will keep the game fun and hard enough to be satisfying to the players over time, without making it too difficult or frustrating. A tough challenge! We both agreed that even the most brilliant group of programmers and game designers can never outguess 450,000 players using all their skills and imagination to make the game into their own personal playground. It’s not possible for the developers to guess every trick and technique good players come up with to conquer a difficult encounter, as they found as EverQuest progressed. Every time the players come up with something new, developers like Chris have to decide how that is going to impact the game now, and in the future. What changes, if any, should be made to make sure the game stays engaging?

Chris is also very interested in fostering a sense of community and friendships among players, working to put elements into EQII that will encourage interactions between individuals. Players of MMOs place value on their player avatars: they have individual personalities that may or may not reflect the personality of the person at the keyboard, and exist strongly as individuals in the game world. Interaction between players is what builds community within the MMO. One important part of EQII that Chris feels will draw on this concept is the player housing feature. In some MMOs, the player housing can be described as a “trophy case”, something a player constructs to show off his or her accomplishments. In EQII, the housing is very personal, in the sense that players can design the layout, the placement of the windows, and the design of the interior, but more importantly are also made for sharing. The house is an expression of the avatar’s personality. A key element is that friends can come into the house to enjoy it, move objects around, and even contribute to the design. The house is interactive, not static. EQ has not survived and prospered over five years just because of the monster encounters; it is because of the relationships player characters have formed in the world of Norrath. Chris hopes to encourage friendships and altruism in the game by incorporating more elements to make cooperating with others rewarding personally. I can’t wait to see what he comes up with in this regard.

Because of the emphasis on friendships and camaraderie, groups and raids are going to be designed to work on smaller scale. There is a theory behind these decisions, as well. Chris feels that large raids end up marginalizing some players. Most people don’t feel very important in a group of 50 to 100 players zerging the mob. In EQII, the emphasis is on smaller raids, up to 4 groups of 6, and many of the encounters can be easily done with just a small group of 6 people. So in changing the game design, he has changed the focus from huge raids to more personal adventures with groups of comrades.

We also discussed the economy of an MMO. The economy in a game becomes very important in the success of gameplay, as a player’s in-game money and gear needs to have value. Chris has looked into economic theory, which helped him to decide which elements can make the economy stronger, thus making the game more playable because buying and selling items actually works.

People’s play styles also come under scrutiny by the design teams. Chris thinks often about what kind of playing experience disparate people want to have. Some just want the hack and slash of a good fighting encounter. Some want the challenge of quests and puzzles. Others want a different type of game, where player characters work at trades or skills within the game world. Chris, as a former schoolteacher, uses his knowledge of different learning styles and diversity to incorporate some of these ideas into his game design, with the goal of making the game interesting and fun for many kinds of people.

Fan Faire is important to him, as it is to all the designers and programmers, because they really get to see what the game fans are like, up close and personal. It’s not just the 18 – 25 year olds playing: it’s the over 50 crowd, professionals, women, men, children, whole families. Designing a game that’s fun and a rewarding use of leisure time for all those types of people is not easy, to say the least. For EQII, Chris has tried to put in a more open ended game, with less linearity than the traditional RPG, to appeal to a wider audience. Tradeskilling, player housing, and other elements are in place to offer alternative playing experiences. He also took into account the casual gamer, who does not want to raid for 5 hours each night. Chris has considered gender roles and the ways men and women might play differently, and incorporated those ideas into his design elements, with the goal of making a game that can be fun in different ways and blend differing player personalities into a complex MMO that is really enjoyable to spend time in, day after day.

We briefly touched on the issue of player-versus-player. Chris personally feels that although PvP can be fun, it also can foster a gang mentality amongst certain players. He hopes to develop an alternative system of PVP that incorporates the excitement and skill of challenging and defeating a real person, but does not end up with a bullying group of players ruining the experience for everyone else.

The bottom line for any game designer is how to keep people coming back who feel that the monthly subscription fee gives them entertainment value. To keep customer loyalty, the design and development teams have to be responsive to customer concerns and yet have a strong sense of where the game is going and how the game universe, in this case the land of Norrath, should function. The game has to be fun, for a lot of people. It has to foster community and a sense of belonging. It has to give you a sense of accomplishment and personal satisfaction. There has to be a desire to log in today, and the day after. To that end, the designers play themselves, and read message boards and fan sites. How does their theory of game design really play out in the real world? The grass roots commentary and complaining is not ignored; the developers really do want to know what the players care about. If players are complaining and asking for changes, that means they care passionately about the game. And there is nothing more rewarding for a designer to log in as just a regular character (which they do!), join a group, fight, enjoy a challenging encounter, and watch their group members have a great time too. Yes, Chris admitted, he cringes when he hears his group members complaining about something he designed, but he doesn’t say anything. He just waits for the next moment when people are having fun again.

Lungi Robe Will Display Again for Monks
4/26/04, 6:43 PST | Kwill

The SOE Live Team has responded to player requests, and changed back the Lungi of the Forbidden robe to show as a robe graphic for the monks, as well as working with the beastlords to make it show up as a tunic for their class. The Lungi of the Forbidden robe was one of the only high end monk robes that displayed as a robe, and is considered something of a status symbol for achieving it. Recent changes to make it displayable for beastlords changed the graphic into a tunic. Monks were not happy with the tunic look. EQ Designer Ryan responded to the customer complaints, and addresed why it was changed initially:
The robe graphic was changed to address Beastlord races that didn't have the robe graphic as part of their model. Several Beastlords were disappointed that they didn't have a graphic for a rather rare item. I wasn't really aware that everyone was so attached to the robe graphic, so I'll be changing it back to the previous graphic and offering a turn in quest for Beastlords to get one with a graphic. Hopefully that should make everyone happy. (about the robe anyway)

Sorry about the confusion over this. -Ryan

An official announcement was made:
Lungi of the Forbidden will display a robe graphic once again after the next update. Beastlords will be able to turn the robe in for a leather looking BP with the same stats, if they so desire. If you would like more details on this change, you can visit the discussion about the item here.

Thanks all!

Brenlo the Forbidden Dancer

Rytan posts that the change went live on test Friday April 23:
It just went up on test today, and should be included in the next live updated. Garath the Trader in Eastern Wastes will offer to swap between the two graphics for you.
Not everyone agreeed the robe should be changed back (of course, this is EQ we are talking about), but clearly the live team is reading the message boards and taking player comments seriously. Good to know!
