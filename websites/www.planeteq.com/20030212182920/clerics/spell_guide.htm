Spell Guide by Dikmer Orless

First off, let me say, I grew up in dungeons. Many tactics used today only apply in outdoor zones, where I tended not to be. I did Black Burrow, Befallen, Najena, Sol A, Unrest, Cazic Thule, Lower Guk and Sol B. About the only real EXP I got from 1 to 50 in an outdoor zone was soloing Spectres in Oasis. I'll try to give you a rundown of the various spells as I remember doing it from 19 on.

BTW, Atone doesn't get an upgrade that I'm aware of. But it's worth getting for when you need it. (like when I once aggroed a Nexus Scion, took me 5 casts, but I eventually atoned her). You wont get this spell til 34.

Lull: Level 56 Word of Tranquility has it's uses (Targetable AoE lull). I've used it in Sebilis to AoE lull 3-4 frogs and pull 1. Other than that, I've never used lull's successfully at all. If you wanna play around with it, go for it, but I didn't find them useful at all.

Roots: Well, there are 2 different camps for root. Some use level 9 "Root" exclusively. Some go for the higher level ones. I personally load the 56th level Parlyzing Earth most of the time. At 19, you don't have to worry about this, just use Root. And keep some kind of root loaded at all times, it WILL save lives.

Nukes: Well, I use Judgement when I use a nuke at all. Never use the Vs. Summoned nukes, just not enough bang for the buck. The Vs. Undead line is nice for when you got an undead critter that needs to die NOW, since that line-up has no recast delay. Lately, though, my contribution to group DPS is my hammer pet and my melee procs. I occasionally bust out a nuke when I'm running FM and wanna blast something for 1k damage. For 19-50, you can solo fairly effectivly with your nukes. Especially 40-50, since spectres die so easily and are decent exp. In otherwords, buy your nukes and use them when the opportunity presents itself. If nothing else, it will raise Evocation.

Summoned: Abundant food/drink. I have these, but never actually used them for the food and water. I prefered to buy the real deal. Before the time of naked corpses not vanishing, I would summon food and water after a death to preserve the corpse in case I died again. (Nagafen raids, Fear raids, etc) It's also good to keep your Conjuration up. My conjuration was a pathetic 105 when the changes came around. It's up to 198 as of today. I fizzled alot of hammers and pets for awhile. Start early with your Conjuration skill, or you'll be wishing you had later. As for the summoned hammers, well, up til recently, I always had some kind of weapon that was better to have than those summoned ones. That may be different for you youngsters now.

Buffs: Get these. You can avoid Fortitude in the higher levels, you'll never actually use it, from 52 to 60 you will be using Heroic Bond almost exclusivly. Also, you can probably pass on symbols until you get to your mid 40's and get Naltron. They don't really do you any good and waste your money and mana. Courage and Holy Armor (and all the upgrades right up the line) will be used. Alot.

Fears: Well, without a way to snare, I never really saw the benfit of having the fear spells. I HAVE them scribed, somewhere. Never actually used any of them other than as an escape tool. Fear and run. If you are duoing with a SK or some other class that can snare, fear kiting is a fairly effective way to get EXP. I just never really had that opportunity. Fear kiting in a dungeon isn't something you wanna try, and as I said, I didn't spend much time outdoors.

Stuns: Ok, here's where it get's tricky. You will want to load level 9 Stun sometimes. Otherwise, nothing stops a critter from gating away and bringing back friends. If your group is melee heavy, you can try relying on bashes and slams, or maybe your enchanter can stun better, but keep this spell handy. The higher level stuns, unfortunatly, are VERY situational. Nothing upsets an enchanter more than you casting Holy Might or some other stun that does damage, and breaking a mez. Best to just bury those stuns and never think about them. Only 2 of our stuns do no damage. Level 9 Stun and level 55 Stun Command (which is a targetable AoE stun).

Heals: Ummm, get every heal you can. Nuff said.

Cures: Well, the only cure I feel is worthless is Cure Blindness. In the lower levels, blindness doesn't last long enough to waste mana trying to cure, and the higher level blinds require multiple casts of a cure to get rid of (3, 4, 5 or more). And, unlike our other cures, Cure Blindness does not get an upgrade that I am aware of. Cure Blindness is a spell that resides permanently in the back of my spellbook.

Divination spells: True North and Sense the Dead, almost totally worthless. Good for raising skill. Invisibility to Undead (and the group version, Sunskin) will come in handy, but only if you can actually cast one, so keep your skill up. We don't get any other divination spells.

Hope this helps.
