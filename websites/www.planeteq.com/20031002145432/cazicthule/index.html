    Fan Faire Notes
    10/1/03, 9:29 PST | Kwill

    I have compiled my notes from Fan Faire Chicago 2003. Read about EQ2, the Art of EverQuest, Lost Dungeons of Norrath, and the Live Team Panel. Pictures are up too!


       Monday, September 29, 2003

    Some Augmentation Notes
    9/29/03, 9:11 PST | Kwill

    From EQLive:
    Using the Same Augment Twice
    You cannot seal two of the same augmentation onto the same item. The duplicate augmentation will not bond. We just wanted everyone to be clear on this point when making plans to spend their adventure points.

    Augmentation Restrictions - Worn Locations
    It is important to remember that the restrictions on an augmentation will be applied to the item that it is bonded with. This includes the worn location. Remember, when you combine an augmentation and an item, the result will have all of the restrictions of both items.

    I'd like to stress this specifically when it comes to worn location.

    If you have an augmentation that can only be used in the wrist location and you apply it to an item that can be worn on the wrist and neck, the resultant item will ONLY be wearable on the wrist.

    Please keep that in mind when augmenting your items.

    Alan

    Augmenting Deity Restricted Items
    Deity restricted items do not accept augmentation items with no deity restrictions. This is a bug, and we should have it fixed when we next patch.

    Fan Faire Report
    9/29/03, 8:16 PST | Kwill

    Two words for Fan Faire -- serious fun! This was my first Fan Faire, and I had a great time. I attended some panels, met and hung out with some great people, participated in the live quest, saw some incredible costumes at the grand banquet, and did some shopping at the trade fair.

    Tom Taylor, who is the events coordinator for SOE, did a fantastic job running the event. His humorous presentations kept 2,000 people entertained for two nights with his terrific mc-ing of the banquet and prize giveaways. And what prizes they were! Logitech wireless mice, GForce video cards, gift certificates to EBGames, wow. Tom is organized, funny, and puts on a great show --and can hold his liquor pretty well too, despite the efforts of many people to try to prove otherwise. He works 16+ hour days to make the show a good time for all, and he does his job well. The other SOE staff members in attendance also did a tremendous job, and were unfailingly friendly, helpful, and fun to be around. Absor did his best to make sure everyone got their questions answered at the panels and things ran smoothly, and all the SOE people seemed to be keeping their sense of humor and having a great time. The press coordinator, the person handing out the t-shirts, the people at the registration desk -- all nice, all polite, and all so very helpful.

    The costume contest on Saturday night was amazing. Not only were the costumes good, they were creative. There was a dead high elf and a dead dark elf, each one dressed rather accurately with a sign saying "corpse" over their heads. The Vah Shir bard, with amazing handmade full plate armor, was something to see. There was an Antonica Scion, two Terris Thules, rangers, a rather attractive wood elf druid, a Vah Shir beastlord with a moving pet (a stuffed tiger on a remote controlled platform -- so cute!), some dark elves and magicians, druids, dwarves, and so many more. Creative kudos goes to the old style shaman in a bear hat who announced he was a shaman with an old video card, so he wasn't displayed as a new model. The winning prize went to a GM dressed as a female dwarf, who very much deserved to win with her elaborate costume (which included a very realistic beard), second to the Vah Shir in plate mail (who sang to us!), and third to the dead high elf, a very attractive girl even if she was a corpse. The worst costume award went to the guy with a beard in a skirt and a helmet sporting two long braids, who announced he was a female paladin. He had everyone laughing, and Tom pretty much lost it for about 30 seconds up on stage -- he wasn't expecting something quite that ridiculous!

    The Live Quest event, held on Saturday, was very hard, and turned out to be a great way to spend 90 minutes. The live NPCs in their elaborate costumes did a fantastic job. Our team did not even come close to winning, but it was still entertaining and I made some new friends, so even if you aren't good at logic puzzles, coming up with pirate slogans, singing, and answering riddles, you should still participate just for the fun of it and to meet people at the Faire.

    Although I did not participate, the Lords of EverQuest tournament, held both Friday and Saturday, was very competitive and looked to be a lot of fun for the participants. The prize of $500 for first place wasn't too bad, either.

    White Wolf games had pen and paper EQ going for most of the two days, and after browsing their wares, it looks to be a top quality product.

    The panels with SOE staff were well run, interesting, and most importantly, drove home the point to me that the SOE designers and Live Team very much care that the game runs the way it should. Scott Hartman, lead technical designer for the Live Team, is willing to answer any question from the audience and look into the problem. He is funny and a people person as well as a great game designer. The Live Team, as well as the LDonN Team, were obviously interested in what people had to say about the game and took notes about questions to take back to the SOE offices. Scott Hartman emphasized strongly that reporting bugs in the game does get to them, so use /bug when you have a problem. Now that LDoN is released, they are starting a huge melee class project, so although the class vs. class discussion was tabled at this Fan Faire, it will be very much on their minds as they continue to work on the game to make it playable, fun and as seamless as possible.

    Fans should know as well that much of the proceeds from the silent auction, which includes the banners and publicity items from the Faires, goes to charity.

    I will post more information on what was said in the panel presentations and photos this week on the site, so check back.

    Is it worth going? Absolutely. Fan Faire is a well run, entertaining event for EQ players of any age, level, or ability. The next one is rumored to be back in Las Vegas -- so I hope to see you there!
