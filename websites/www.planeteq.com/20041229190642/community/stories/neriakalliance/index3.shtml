The Neriak Alliance
by Jack Holt

Chapter 3 - Journeys

Dvinn journeyed for a day in the company of four Neriak regulars. As the evening began they were joined by a small horde of Orcs who spread out in all directions clearing the path through Lesser Faydark towards Greater Faydark. All night they kept up a steady pace.

When morning came the Orcs made camp, but no fires were lit. Although they were more orderly than Dvinn had expected, they were undisciplined by Neriak standards. They complained to three larger Orcs about the march, and about the lack of warm blood and fresh meat for their breakfast before finally settling in for sleep during the daylight hours.

The Neriak guards kept watch. They were expecting the Ambassador to rest, but he did not. Dvinn, not wanting to sleep near the Orcs, moved quietly checking the guard posts on a regular basis.

In the late afternoon his guides for the next leg of the journey arrived. They were three female Wood Elves dressed in black. Part of a bandit community that had a secret camp in Greater Faydark, they too wanted to see the demise of Kelethin.

Dvinn left with them as darkness fell upon the forest. Instead of a march, he and the Wood Elves slipped slowly and silently from shadow to shadow, and thereby entered Greater Faydark.


The portal at the Druid ring in the Butcherblock Mountains closed with a soft glimmer after depositing Niffun and the Dwarf upon the hard ground there. The Dwarf was ecstatic about the escape, but the Druid was perturbed. He had missed his opportunity to get the teleportation device.

"Well done, my friend, well down!" the Dwarf shouted as he embraced Niffun in a bear hug. "Of course, I would have got the better of those devils given time, but don't think for a moment I don't appreciate what you've done. I'm forever in your debt. My children's children will honor you name… errr… your name? What is your name?

"Niffun Tuttle, but I…"

"Well met Niffun! Yes, glad to meet you! Ahnog Nogginbash is the name, and bashing is my game. I'll make a song about all this. A good one! Your name will be sung among the Dwarves, and…"

Niffun rolled his eyes, "Enough of this. We must make haste!"

"Indeed!" the Dwarf replied. "We must warn the Stormguard! They will know what to do with those dark devils."

"Clip, Clap, Stamp, Stomp,

Dwarven feet march to war!

Be they Ogre, be they Orc

They soon shall be our pork!"



 "Yes, yes," Niffun replied. "Sing while you run, please!"

"Of course. Of course," The Dwarf said.

"This way!

This way!

To Kaladim we must go!"



For two hours they ran. Ahnog went on and on about everything! When he wasn't talking, he was singing. The constant verbal barrage overwhelmed Niffun, who was used to being alone. He usually traveled quietly, and few noticed his passing--but not today! The loud Dwarf drew the attention of every creature in the mountain. Niffun was relieved when he saw Kaladim's gate in the distance. He fully intended to leave the Dwarf there and press on to Kelethin to warn Trueshot about the dangers in Lesser Faydark. The Dwarf, however, would have none of it. He spoke briefly with the Captain of the Guard, who in turn went inside the mountain-city to muster Dwarven troops for a march to Kelethin. Ahnog took some armor and weapons, and then insisted on accompanying the Halfling on his journey to warn the Elves.

By the end of the day they reached the pass out of the mountains into the forest. The Druid led the way as they plunged into the darkness. Finally, the Dwarf grew silent, but his armor and heavy feet were anything but quiet. Consequently, the bandits with Dvinn heard him long before he or Niffun were aware they were there.

They didn't want a confrontation, but luck was not with them. As they hid, one of them stepped on a branch and it broke with a loud, distinctive pop. The Dwarf stopped and drew his sword. Niffun, on the other hand, moved silently away from the Dwarf flanking the source of the sound.

Ahnog, in typical Dwarf fashion, moved directly and challengingly towards the sound. The bandits, perceiving that battle was unavoidable, and believing that the Dwarf was alone, split up. One went after the Dwarf while the others led Dvinn away from the battle toward their ultimate destination.

The tactic almost worked. But, unseen and unexpected, Niffun glimpsed the fleeing party as he was flanking the bandit attacking Ahnog. He had not just seen them, but he had also recognized the ambassadorial raiment that Dvinn was wearing. Immediately he suspected a connection with his mission to Lesser Faydark, and left the Dwarf alone to deal with the remaining bandit so he could pursue Dvinn.

The bandits with Dvinn moved quickly believing that they had escaped. Dvinn too thought they had escaped at first, but after only a few moments of evasion his instincts as a soldier told him that he was being hunted. He was only familiar with Greater Faydark through maps, but he thought he could find his own way to Crushbone alone. So, at the first opportunity to do so unnoticed he broke off from the bandits. Maybe the unseen pursuer would follow them.

Niffun paused at the point where Dvinn had separated. His mind weighed the group's separation and concluded that an ambassador would not be left alone. He followed the path the two had taken. It took him 10 minutes to catch up to the point of being able to see them and realize his mistake.

Cursing, he backtracked to pursue the other trail. To add speed, and the power to track by smell, he shaped changed to a wolf and then set out at a quick lope. Dvinn was in unfamiliar territory and wasn't hard to catch up to. Niffun's plan was to follow him quietly, try to determine who he was and what he was up to, and then capture or kill him. He moved out to a parallel course and kept pace with the Neriak as he picked his way through the dense forest.

Suddenly the ambassador stopped. He stood perfectly still for several seconds. Niffun did as well, but he wasn't really afraid that he had been detected. He was a Halfing and a Druid. His ability to hunt silently was something he was extremely proud of.

Dvinn turned slowly in Niffun's direction and froze. Niffun could only see the lower portion of his face. It was pale blue, with red lips that were now turning up in an evil smirk.

"I know you are there." Dvinn said.

Niffun didn't answer, but doubt formed in his mind about whether he had been actually detected or not.

Dvinn slipped his hand under his robe and drew his Dragoon's dirk.

Upon seeing it Niffun understood for the first time that he had hunted an administrator, but found a seasoned warrior instead. "A prize," he though to himself, "a prize indeed! No Elf can call me 'little Niffun' again if I bring in a Neriak Dragoon!"

"Is Neriak so strong that she can send her sons to die in Faydark?" Niffun challenged in return.

"Yes," Dvinn replied. "Not only to die, but to conquer."

"Are you greater than your fathers who were driven from here in shame?" Dvinn's eyes narrowed at the insult, and he chaffed at the arrogance of the challenge. "I shall know that soon," he said as he moved toward Niffun, "but you… you will be dead."

Niffun cast a snaring spell to trap the Dragoon, but it had no effect at all. Mildly annoyed, he cast it a second time; but again, it did not take hold.

Dvinn laughed and closed the distance between them. Still, Niffun was not dismayed. He had time for one more spell, and selected his lightning spell. It struck the Neriak, but had no visible effect. Niffun's arrogance melted away, but not his confidence. Still in wolf form, he savagely leaped for the throat of his foe. At the last possible moment Dvinn parried with his right hand, and buried the dirk in his left hand into the hindquarter of the wolf.

Niffun yelped with pain, first from the slashing wound, and second as his back hit a nearby rock as he was falling. Desperately he fought to rise, but couldn't.

Dvinn stood over him savoring the coming kill.

"Before you die, I think you should know what is about to happen," Dvinn gloated. "Orcs from Crushbone and Lesser Faydark, supported by Neriak Regulars are going to besiege and destroy Kelethin, so your family and friends, with the possible exception of your females, will soon join you. Who would you prefer have your female? Me, or an Orc?" Muhahahahahaha!

Niffun, in pain, but not completely robbed of his senses, retorted. "You mean the Neriak Regulars in Lesser Faydark that we killed today?"

Dvinn paused for a moment and studied the eyes of his adversary, and then slowly grinned. "Nice try. You are lying."

He raised the dirk for the final blow, but it never fell. Ahnog came roaring out of the darkness with a two-handed sword above his head and charged Dvinn. The Neriak was completely surprised. Not knowing whether or not the Dwarf was alone, he fled into the forest.

Niffun yielded to his wounds and drifted into dark unconsciousness.
