The Good Old Days
by Kolanin

I was playing my Druid not long ago, and had to run through the East Commonlands for some reason or another, and was stunned as a realization struck me. There were no players auctioning anything at all, it was like walking into a ghost town. Even the calls for group members for the Orc camps there were absent for the most part. I decided to stick around for a while, tracking low level characters there and buffing them with Druid goodness.

While I was busying myself with that, I started talking to the people in the zone about the old days of the EC Tunnel, when /auc's came so fast and steady, that you were constantly scrolling back up, looking for that one item that caught your eye. Players had even formed a firm etiquette for how you could auction your wares, and there was little tolerance for that one urgent salesperson who wanted more attention by using /shout instead. The realization that those days were gone when someone chimed into the /ooc conversation asking; "What are you guys talking about? Is the EC tunnel here?" I got a few laughs as I replied; "Only in spirit, my friend." I moved on to complete the errand I was undertaking, and began thinking of all the other things that have gone by.

Much later, after some hunting, I was setting up my trader and realized something about the Bazaar. With weight being a factor in the old EC tunnel, prices were limited, there's no way you could carry 50,000 platinum all the way from Freeport to the EC tunnel without the help of at least a couple of burly friends. With the removal of the weight restrictions in the Bazaar, however, why not charge 50,000 platinum for a Cobalt Breastplate? I'm not saying I don't like the bazaar, but I find the prices are largely inflated, and if you can come up with that much platinum, you probably deserve the equipment you're buying.

One glaring example is the abandoned boats that still drift across the various bodies of water on Norrath. I met a good number of people while taking the boat from Butcherblock to Freeport, sure it was simply boring half the time, and I was really reading a good book while checking the screen occasionally, but the only reason anyone would take that boat now, is if you don't have Planes of Power, Luclin, or the platinum for a teleport.

There is another thing; the biggest platinum makers were Druids and Wizards once upon a time. They would ply their trade in a great number of zones, offering teleports in trade for a "donation" that was often "not required, but appreciated." You can still make platinum doing this, but I don't find it's worth the time anymore. One of the good things about the addition of common, convenient transportation is that there are many zones that are largely ignored, since it's such a long journey to them from any "PoK Book", or "Spire." The other day I was asked; "Where is Unrest?" by someone who had never been there after raising one character all the way up to level sixty five, and another into his forties. The days of making one thousand platinum in under two hours from teleports are gone, my Druid and Wizard friends.

The addition of easy transportation in Everquest has changed the game irrevocably, and to be honest, I don't mind one bit. According to several interviews I've read, the old developers for Everquest have been slowly replaced by a new breed. This happens in any workplace, and it's not surprising, as being one of the first programmers for the most commonly played on line role playing game must be one hell of a good tidbit on your resume. The new programmers, and this is something I've seen in many interviews as well, are mostly players themselves, and know well the pain of getting a level twenty nine cleric from Mistmoore all the way to Overthere. Now you can simply Gate to the Plane of Knowledge, then click on the Overthere Stone. Minimize travel time? Increase the pleasure and pace of game play? Why not? I'm all for it, especially when I don't have the time to play like I used to. I'm sure the new programmers for Everquest don't have as much time either, considering how frenzied the pace is rumored to be over there.

There is one more significant change I'd like to mention here, and that's there removal of "Hell Levels". This is where you would have a grand old time getting all the way up into your thirties, and then you hit, say, level thirty nine. You run off, gleefully buy your spells (if you use spells with your class), then return to the group only to find that after an hour of hunting the same things as you were before, that your experience bar seems to be stuck at zero. Hours pass, and finally, you're seeing a little bit of experience there. Most often players would go off and find something else to hunt, a little higher level, and often more risky. If you don't die several times as your group tries to bite off a little more than it normally chews, you quickly realize you've hit a Hell Level. This is where it takes several times the experience to get through your current level, as it did to get through your previous one. No, not say, 10%, 30%, or even 70% more, but, and this may be off a little, more like 300% more.

From what I understand, one of the reasons for this was to slow down your level advancement so your skills could catch up, which in itself isn't a bad idea, but the time investment it took to get through that Hell Level was so much greater, and the frustration for some people was endless. The most common approach to getting through a Hell Level was to go off and try to hunt creatures that were much higher level, and very dangerous. Of coarse death would result quite often, thus making the level that much more hellish.

Quite some time ago, around the introduction of the Luclin expansion, hell levels were removed, and they now adhere to the normal experience progression of all other levels. I've heard people dispute this, but believe me, it's in the patch history (on www.eqlive.com), hell levels are gone, and so is one more thing to blame your bad luck on. Now, the term Hell Level is still used, but these days it refers to a level you're having a lot of trouble (and in which death is so common that it's normality), getting through. Normally this is because of the players you're hunting with, the spot you're hunting in, or your own hunting style, or lack thereof.

There have been a great number of other, more obvious changes, such as the new graphics that came with Luclin, the new level cap of sixty five, armor dying, the new user interface, the addition of charms, and many free zones, such as Jagged Pine Forest and Stonebrunt. There are even rumors that Sony Online Entertainment is working on new Epic Items (often referred to as Epic 2.0), and that we can expect to see a few new free zones in the next few months. Now, don't take my word for it, those are just rumors, but one of the changes that is coming involves the complete revamping of bow usage in Everquest, which will make Rangers directly accountable for the damage they do from a distance. That in itself should be interesting, and it leaves me wondering what other changes are on the horizon other than pathing corrections that are currently being made on the test server. I, and I'm sure I'm not alone in this, see a bright future for Everquest, and am very much keeping my eyes and ears open for the new, as I enjoy the already rich and vast world of Norrath.
