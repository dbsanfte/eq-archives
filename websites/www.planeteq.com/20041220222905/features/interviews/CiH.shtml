Cats in Hats Guild of Drinal Server

By Stryker

As part of our series on the EQ server community, I interviewed the leaders of one of the Drinal server's most successful guilds, Cats in Hats. This guild has accomplished many server firsts, including killing Emp. Ssra, Lord Inquisitor Seru, and the waking Kerafrym, or the Sleeper.

Cats in Hats is organized to have co-leaders, rather than a single guild leader. I spoke with two of the three co-leaders of CiH. The conversation also included Saden, a member of CiH who joined during the first week of its creation on Mithaniel Marr, where the guild originated.

Stryker: When was the guild founded, and why?

Saden: Well we all had the goal of meeting people, accomplishing goals, and having fun. We formed in Mid-July of 2000 on the Mith Marr server.

Stryker: Approximately how many members does CiH have?

Lern: We moved to Drinal to get away from Afterlife [An "uber" guild on the Mithaniel Marr server, which is still active today.], to be able to grow as a raiding guild. We currently have probably 100 on the roster, with about 70 active members.
Trevize: We have more total members, but they float from really active to non-existent for a time.
Lern: We' re in the process of trying to shrink (not recruiting as people leave).
Trevize: /nod…recruiting is completely shut down now.
Lern: PoP kinda hurt us, cause we have more then 60 people on currently so some people can't join the raid [window].

Stryker: Why do you run the guild with three co-leaders, as opposed to one main leader?

Trevize: 'cause 1 leader would go insane =P
Lern: One thing this guild has never had was one solid leader. We've always used a team effort to run it.
Trevize: And we feel a committee for important decisions works better. Brings better balance and keeps the guild's best interest at prime focus.
Lern: The nice thing by doing it this way is we've had multiple officer turnovers. When an officer leaves, the guild doesn't fall apart, we just elect a new officer.
Lern: The guild mostly runs itself, we are just here to point it in the right direction for raids, and to settle disputes.

Stryker: What was the first "uber" mob CiH killed?

Saden: Trakanon was a huge goal for us, and we accomplished that quickly.

Stryker: Who was first in your guild to reach 65?

Lern: Darkpyro was the first on the server, and our guild.

Stryker: Why did you join CiH, on Mith Marr?

Saden: Friends and what everyone else wanted; to see things, to do things, and beat things, be the best, and at the same time have fun.

Stryker: Saden, what is it like to be a member of CiH?

Saden: [I have] always have been proud of Cats in Hats. We all have a great time, we work hard at having fun and it makes us proud to accomplish what we do.

Stryker: What would you want someone reading this article to know about CiH?

Lern: We are a very casual "uber" guild. We don't run it like a military organization. We have many part time players, and people take breaks as needed.
Trevize: That we're normal working people that go to work, school or whatever. Nothing really "different" about being a high end guild except that you are a group of people that decided to kill the bosses. Only difference is the zone we play in.

Cats in Hats, like many successful and powerful guilds, has a policy of mandatory raid attendance as part of the requirements of guild membership. The guild raids almost every night. I asked Saden, "What do you think about the way CiH encourages going to all raids called, even if you don't want to go?"

Saden: People should go to raids when they are called merely for the fact we are a raiding guild and people join to do that.

Stryker: Why did you decide to wake The Sleeper, and what were fellow guild responses?

Trevize: It was an accomplishment in EQ, the ONE AND ONLY thing a guild can do that actually changes EQ. So doing the script and being able to see it was special for me and I was happy to be a part of it.
Lern: To me, being able to permanently change the server is a huge deal. We even had members come out of retirement just to help wake him.
Trevize: As for other guild's responses, many on Drinal hated us for it and flamed us. Many others cheered us on.

Stryker: Before the cleric epic quest was changed by SoE, the Drinal server had a Ragefire list, where clerics would get in line to take down Ragefire to complete their epics. CiH came in and took Ragefire before a cleric camping it could rally the troops. Why did you do this?

Trevize: Hehe, re: Drinal flame boards. Seriously though, this was the act of a few guild members and even a few non-guild members as well. It wasn't something the officers said 'go kill RF'.
Lern: From day one of being on Drinal, CiH has made it clear we do not believe in waiting lists. The Ragefire list was started and we made it very clear at that time we would not be following it. It was only due to the fact that it took months before one of our members actually found Ragefire up for us to kill him.
Trevize: ...but on that note, lists suck. If you want to kill a mob get a force there to kill it first. Sitting around in a zone for hours spamming "this mob is mine" really doesn't mean any thing to me. The mob is yours if #1 you have a force to kill it and #2 are actively killing the mob.

Stryker: With the recent "renovation" of the guild Avatar, a now 60+ guild, do you feel you have competition?

Lern: Avatar was a guild of maybe 10 people when Drinal first opened. [And now that they have grown] they [still] aren't any competition anytime in the foreseeable future for us. Just being 60+ isn't enough, you then need AA skills to beat a lot of the mobs in the game now.

Stryker: What are your personal goals for the guild, now that you have accomplished waking the sleeper, killing Seru, and Emp. Ssra?

Trevize: Simple in my mind... conquer PoP.
Lern: Same, the ultimate goal is to get into the plane of time. That's probably 6 months away.

Stryker: Has CiH killed any PoP gods yet, and are you planning a raid on one of them shortly?

Lern: We've been focusing mostly on letting our members level and work on AA points, while getting their flags for next tier zones.
Trevize: /nod, this week has been get lvl 65 and get AA. Next week starts full time raiding again.

Stryker: What does CiH think about the new "graveyard" effect in PoP? Good/bad?

Lern: I love the graveyard, it lets groups try challenging things without fear of having to find a necro to CR. One thing they could do to improve graveyards is allow people to /corpse themselves once they've run back. Having to sit there waiting for your corpse kinda sucks.
Trevize: I dunno. Some things I like about the GY like Lern said. What I don't like is how lazy some of the players have become, for example, 'Oh I died. AFK for 20 min while corpse goes to GY'. =/ No longer is there the challenge of CR.

Stryker: Has CiH discovered any drawbacks to PoP, specifically the functionality?

Lern: One serious issue we see with pop is the fact that once we've beaten the tier one planes, if a member returns to the game or we recruit somebody we will have to go back and redo all the "easy" mobs we already conquered, so that they get flagged and can join us on the real raids. So right now, we are taking our time to make sure we get as many members flagged now before we move on.
Trevize: /nod, having to re-do a tier 1 plane for just 1 person isn't something I'm thrilled with in the design.

Stryker: And now for a player submitted question: "Would CiH consider having a 'sponsor a player day', in which CiH would rally around one player in Everquest, 50+ but -60, to either get them an epic piece, a nice piece of equipment, or just some experience?"

Trevize: Hehe, I get asked this a lot. General answer is no. We have guild members who need stuff and guildmembers are going to get help in stuff long before we get time to help everybody on Drinal out. However, this doesn't mean that members of CiH aren't helping people out. Personally in the last 3 months I've helped non-guildmembers with 4 epics.
Saden: I'd like to step in here, yes some of us had helped random people on epic pieces, etc., even CoHed friends on different guilds for items we may have not needed just to be nice. Just because we don't need [an item] we won't always destroy.
Lern: Yeah, its really up to the individual guild members. If I am not busy and get asked to help somebody I do my best to help him or her. As a guild though, we just don't have the time to take away from xping/raiding to help others.

Stryker: Well, thank you for the interview guys. It was a lot of fun.
