The Neriak Alliance
by Jack Holt

Chapter 5 - Battle!

In Lesser Faydark the muster was complete, and the march to the edge of Greater Faydark had been accomplished. The three companies of Orcs were deployed, one to the rear, and one each on the right and left of the Neriaks.

As darkness fell they marched to the wizard spires and separated. One company of the Orcs remained there in case the Gnomes marched up from Steamfont. The second broke to the west intent on occupying Butcherblock Pass to keep the Dwarves out. The third marched east toward Felwithe to engage the High Elves and prevent them from rescuing Kelethin. The Dark Elves pressed to the north to Kelethin to assist the Crushbone Orcs.

To the North, the army of the Crushbone Orcs pushed into Greater Faydark. It were separated into two companies. The first one, led by Dvinn, headed south to attack the Orc Lift in Kelethin. King Crush led the second group. They deployed in the forest to set an ambush through which the first group could retreat, and their pursuers could be destroyed.

The first skirmish took place at Felwithe. The Orc Company sent to occupy it attacked from southwest of the walled fortress. Having been warned by the messengers sent from Kelethin, the city was already closed. The troops mustering for the march to Kelethin went to defend the walls of Felwithe. Kelethin would have to defend itself for the time being.

A short while later a second battle began when Orcs arrived at Butcherblock Pass and found it defended by stout Dwarves. The Orcs failed to occupy the pass, but they settled for fortifying the forest just outside the eastern end of the pass.


Dvinn was nervous. They had run across a few Rangers on the way to Kelethin, but no significant resistance. Now he was in sight of the Orc lift, but there was still no sign of resistance. He studied the situation, and decided that the Elves were waiting in the trees with their bows. He would have to force them down and bait them into following him.

"Light fires!" he commanded. "We will rush the lift and set fire to the trees of the city." He said it loud so that the Elves could hear him. The Orcs began lighting torches and several young Orc who were carrying bundles of wood soaked in oil moved towards the front. As they did this all the Orcs began shouting battle cries.

Their challenge was answered by a slow beat of a single huge drum. Doom! Doom! Doom! The Orcs grew quiet and listened to the deep, resonating, rolling sound. Then the singing began-not the light merry singing of Elves, but the deep-throated bass voices of Dwarves.

"All hail the power of Kaladim!

Deep and strong may she ever be.

All hail the power of the Guard!

March! March! March her to the war."



Warned by Niffun and Ahnog, they had marched to Kelethin through Butcherblock Pass a full day before the Orcs arrived there to block it.

Their song infuriated the Orcs. They rushed as one toward the Dwarves. Dvinn screamed for them to stop, but in their hatred for the Dwarves they heeded him not at all.

At that moment the Neriak Regulars reached the other side of the city. They had expected the Wood Elves to be occupied with the Orcs, but instead of finding the city undefended they were ambushed from above and on both flanks with a hail of arrows. They were professional soldiers, and although they were hard pressed they answered with their own bows, and some spells. Nevertheless, the attackers had now become the defenders.

Niffun took his stand with Ahnog and the Dwarves, and thus helped absorb the blow of their first charge. The Dwarven line bent, but did not break. In strong contrast to the Orcs, their ranks were the model of order. Whenever a Dwarf fell, another stepped forward to fill the hole, and the line began pushing the Orcs back. Finally they broke, and began to run.

Niffun and Ahnog took advantage of the chaos to seek out Dvinn. He had rallied some of the legionnaires and had formed a rear guard action to cover the retreat of the Orcs. He had no place to go. The Orcs would kill him for losing the battle, and Neriak would do the same if he returned there. Grimily, he faced the charging Dwarves and prepared to die in battle.

Niffun challenged him directly. "Your plan failed, ambassador."

"You are a Halfling," Dvinn said with surprise. Then, with contempt, he met Niffun's challenge. "Come here little one, and let me finish my kill before I die."

"I challenge you Dragoon--for your dirk."

"And what would you do with a Dragoon's dirk?" Dvinn snarled.

"Destory it, and end its legacy," Niffun replied.

The Dwarves, having dispatched the Legionnaires, surrounded the two in a large circle. Dvinn removed his ambassador's robe, and dropped it to the ground along with his other weapons, and drew his dirk. Niffun, with his Bloodforge Hammer drawn, watched from the other side of the circle.

Doom! Doom! Doom! The Dwarven drums spoke.

When the third "doom" rang out Dvinn charged. Niffun raised his free hand and cast the spell "Fury of Air." The spell hit home, but so did Dvinn's dirk. Ignoring the pain in his upper left chest where the dirk was embedded Niffun swung his hammer. The Dragoon dropped to the ground dead.

The Dwarven drums spoke again-Doom! Doom! Doom!

Niffun was weak from his wound, but strong enough to pull the dirk from his shoulder. He looked around and found a large rock. Pushing past the Dwarves he went to it and placed the dirk on it. With his last bit of reserve strength he raised the war hammer and crushed the weapon with a single blow.

The Dwarves cheered. From behind them the Elves of Kelethin also cheered. After they defeated the Neriaks they had arrived just in time to see the death match. Never again would they call him "little Niffun."

The End
