Shadowed Soul and the Divine Alliance: "Doing More with Less"

by Kwill Heartfyre

In our ongoing series of guild leader interviews on EverQuest servers, I interviewed Pirotess, the guild leader of Shadowed Soul on the Cazic Thule server. Shadowed Soul has an established alliance with the Divine Alliance guild, and many of their high level encounters are done as a two-guild effort.

Kwill: How did the guild get started? How long have you been playing?

The guild got started back in April of '99 but we didn't get our tags for a month or two after that. Bgup, Shendra, Naeen and I lived together so we leveled up quickly and met Caleal, Codas and Geshp in Najena before the bulk of the players on CT got to it. Our crew was actually trying to kill the front ogres and ended up training Caleal and his crew by accident. We talked for a bit while we recovered and decided to work together. In time we worked down and started farming Jboots off Drelzna which back then was the ultimate gear. We learned to play well together and decided to form a guild to facilitate that. Since we didn't have the required 10 people to form the guild we recruited several work acquaintances and took a few days to come up with a name from scratch.

I've been playing EQ since the start of Beta 3 which I believe was in the Sep-Dec 98 time frame.

Kwill: How did the alliance with DA get started? Can you talk about how that works?

The alliance with DA started back in October of 2000. This was about 2 or 3 months after SS went through a split which left us at about 50% capacity. Instead of mass guilding a bunch of players that we really didn't know and growing to the point where the guild and its schools of thought became unwieldy again, we decided to find an ally. We started out trying to get something going with various smaller guilds but unfortunately it never seemed to 'click'. Around the same time we heard that DA was also looking for a way to take it to the next level. If I remember correctly, CN [Coterie Nocturne] one of their former allies, was looking to do more solo instead of being allied.

In any case DA and SS got together and tried a joint raid in the Plane of Hate. To make a long story short, it was a complete disaster and I think it would be safe to say that afterwards neither guild thought it would work out. At the time SS was pretty militaristic in the way we raided and DA was much more relaxed so we butted heads in the fundamental way we wanted to go about raiding. However we persevered and over the following months the officers of the two guilds banged out a plan of attack were we met in the middle as 50/50 partners which has served us quite well to this day.

Kwill: How do you handle the organization in your guild?

I have 5 officers that have different responsibilities in running the guild.

They are:

Chionia - Recruitment Officer
Valkemier - Vault/Merchant Officer
Tritolus - Raid/Loot Committee Chairman
Imrik - Raid/Group Officer
Isifhan - Internal Affairs Officer

Any of the officers can step in for the others if they are unavailable and I can help with any of the responsibilities if need be as well. Primarily I run the alliances raids with Aliudyen, DA's guildleader and cover Foreign Affairs and general negotiations with other guilds.

Kwill: What membership requirements do you have?

Presently we have an official level requirement of 58. However that will be raised and we will be adding some basic AA requirements if we decide to reopen recruitment. Right now it is closed. Past experience has shown me that keeping a tight grip on our class balance is extremely important to the health of the guild. It is my belief that if you get too many of any one class people tend to feel unimportant, unrewarded and unchallenged to excel. This has proven to work very well for us.

If we have an opening for a particular class and the character meets our level requirements they then have to pass through 3 additional stages of the recruitment process. First, they need to complete an interview with our recruitment officer who asks basic recruitment questions to give us an idea of what the lead is looking for in a guild. This helps us determine if we have a possible match. If we feel good about the interview, an invitation to apply is extended and the lead becomes a candidate for a month where we judge their raiding and group skills. At that time a vote is taken by the membership and if it is positive, the candidate becomes an initiate who wears the SS tag and adheres to our ethics and policies. After a month as an initiate a final vote is taken and if positive they become full members with all the privileges that entails.

Kwill: How do you get your members to remain loyal?

In my position a lot of the loyalty of my members is based on trust. If people don't trust me they won't follow me into the mouth of a dragon. :)

Kwill: What have you done as a guild/alliance that you are most proud of?

I'd have to say winning the CT Test of Tactics 3 competition was one of the moments that made me proudest. We were an underdog and ended up beating some pretty stiff competition through teamwork and a good strategy.

Kwill: As on every server, there is always politics involved in high level game play. What is your take on the politics of the Cazic Thule server? Who are the most powerful or influential guilds?

Politics on CT always keep you on your toes. I think CT is very unique in that it has a very large concentration of highly capable guilds. Its pretty rare for a single server to have 7 guilds in Vex Thal for example. This makes negotiating VERY important as its pretty easy for any guild to completely screw the other guilds if they really wanted to.

That said, during Luclin things were fairly amicable between the high end guilds on CT. Rotations were accepted pretty well and we managed to stay out of each others hair for the most part. Prior to Luclin, and now that PoP has launched, that seems to be changing again. To a degree that is understandable since regardless of the vast difference in ethics that drives each of the guilds, each has a responsibility to its members to be successful. For that reason negotiations can get pretty heated at times and quite often of late erupt into all out wars. Like I said it keeps you on your toes. :)

Most powerful and influential? Shadowed Soul and The Divine Alliance of course! =) All joking aside, SS and DA have been around as guilds for longer than any of the other high end guilds so name recognition has certainly been good to us. SS in particular became very well known back in the days when we were the only guild to be killing Nagafen and later Vox regularly until EA came into the picture. We even had the server GM broadcasting each of our major kills for awhile. =) The SS/DA alliance also tends to do things with less people than our competition so we've adopted the motto "Doing more with less" for the alliance.

Past that I'd have to say that The Relentless and Element of Darkness are the guilds we are most often in competition with. Due to the nature of PoP however that could change pretty quickly as we all progress through its storyline.

Kwill: How would you like SS to be thought of by other players and guilds on the server?

I think SS's reputation proceeds us pretty well. We've long been known as a fair and just guild to the general CT populace. Naturally our direct competition wouldn't agree with that as we are often at odds when we have to stick up for ourselves but we always manage to do so without breaking Verant's rules. Many times we have helped less fortunate people and guilds with their epics and encounters and we've stepped aside to let other guilds learn how an encounter works instead of breathing down their necks. A few times we've finishing an encounter off at another guilds request, ressed them up and had them loot the drops. So far our ethics have almost always given us a positive image and I'm happy to be a part of that.

Kwill: Your guild has stayed fairly stable over time. What causes guilds to break up?

Well from first hand experience they typically break up for a couple of different reasons. SS split into EoD and SS because two unreconcilable schools of thought formed and polarized the guild. There were the people who felt we had a right to take what we wanted when we wanted it and could roll over anyone who got in our way. The other school wanted to continue to uphold the good name of SS and not be quite so confrontational to the other people on the server. Naturally there was more to both sides but those were the fundamental differences in the two schools.

The other thing that typically does it is growing too large. Its really hard to keep people happy when they don't see any personal reward for months at a time since loot needs to be spread across so many more people. Loot really is the root of all evil in EQ.

Last but not least, the "grass is greener" syndrome. If you aren't pushing the bleeding edge of the game you're in danger of losing your people to someone who is. Everyone wants to succeed and people will migrate to people who are successful.

Kwill: What is your policy on loot, then?

I'm a pretty big fan of our policy. We split gear with DA 50/50 by randoming the first time we kill a new mob and then winner gets first pick. On the next kill the other guild gets first pick. SS then has a loot committee made up of officers that take tells from all members interested in the items. We consider playtime, gear replaced, number of pieces recently awarded among other things before coming up with the member the item will go to.

Additionally we purchase items for our members as Thorn[song, from the Relentless] does. In fact the vast majority of our cash reserves goes towards buying spells, FT3 boots, mosquito earrings, horses, raid components etc to make our members lives easier.

Kwill: What about people who leave the guild? What happens to the loot you have given them?

Some extremely hard to get or rare items are given on loan to members such as Scepters of Destruction or Blades of Carnage. However we feel that unless we specifically state that an item is on loan when we give it to a member, it is theirs to keep. Our thinking is we wouldn't be giving it to them unless they earned it in the first place.

These days we have very few people that leave the guild to go elsewhere. The last one in recent memory who did was just an initiate. We do have a fair number of people who have left the game but 9 times out of 10 they leave their account for the guild to use. The nice thing about that is it makes it easy for them to return after they have attended to RL and play with us again.

Kwill: Do you consider EQ addictive? Do you have players in your guild that you would consider excessive players?

I absolutely think EQ is addictive, it's an escape from reality. I love EQ addicts since I consider myself one. Unfortunately in reality there is only a small percentage of people in our alliance who would qualify as true addicts and play at that level. I'd say 95% of our membership is 21+ and have jobs and families to attend to so they really cant be true addicts. We have a policy in SS. RL before game, group before guild and guild before self.

Kwill: How do you handle the casual players in your guild that can't play as many hours?

We have quite a few members who work night shifts but they make their contributions during the day or in setting up the raid even though they aren't there for the kill shot. We take that into account and reward them as best we can when they are there when gear drops for them.

People who have RL events like sicknesses, dates whatever dont get rewarded as much as the extremely consistent members but we do try to spread gear around to everyone. Luckily our alliance with DA allows us to have real lives and still be successful since we have enough depth for some of us to be out and still succeed.

Kwill: What are your guild's goals?

Our guild/alliance is about doing more with less. We strive to push ourselves to the edge and beyond. An example of what I'm talking about is we recently had an 80 minute fight in Vex Thal with 40 people. At one point we had 15 mobs mezzed in camp, most of which were splitters. Before we could get camp clear and move up, respawn started and we got in a tight spot as we chain aggroed a boss mob and had to advance through the zone fighting the remains of those 15 mobs, the boss and respawn as it continued. At the end we were all drained but intensely proud that we had lived through it.

While we certainly dont mind server firsts, we don't live for them as some of our competition does. We find that it is rewarding to get to an encounter at our own pace and under our own power instead of making any concessions just to succeed. To us, making concessions cheapens the game and robs us of the pride of success. If we fail an encounter, we hit the boards, work out a new strategy as a team, and apply it the next time until we prevail. While that sometimes slows us down, in the end we often do the encounter itself quicker, with less people and less deaths than the competition. I think we've managed to keep pace with the rest of the pack while maintaining our standards and that makes me happy.

Thank you Pirotess for letting us get to know Shadowed Soul and the Divine Alliance a little better! --Kwill
