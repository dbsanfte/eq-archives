A Cleric PVP Point of view on a RED server by Shesees Awestruck

 

PvP player vs player
PvE player vs everything [mainly mobs]

DoT damage over time
PC Player Character
MB ManaBurn

When asked to write the brief synopsis of pvp on a red server I was faced with a great challenge. I'm not the best pvp'er, but I'm not the worst either. This guide was written from a Clerics point of view, and I'm sure a lot of people will have different views and strategies. Please keep in mind this was written solely for you, the Cleric. Once you've absorbed the knowledge, you can use it to your fullest advantage. I am currently a level 54 cleric. Even though this guide leans towards high level PVP, please remember that all of the spells mentioned weather at high levels or not, are only upgrades or lower versions of what you yourself will encounter in any spell line. What people also may not know, is that PVP on a red server duel or otherwise for any caster-is modified. Any damage output by a Caster will be somewhat nurfed. For example-if you are a level 60 cleric and use Judgement on another PC it will never hit for the maximum damage of 925. The PC could be naked and without buffs, and it would still never hit for 925. The Melee has the advantage in PVP always. Their weapons and damage output has not been nurfed. Yes-they may hit us for the full damage ratio the weapon allows. The player versus player rule on the red server is EIGHT LEVELS. That's right-eight levels. Which means if you are level 10, a level 18 may engage you in PVP. At different levels of the game PVP takes on a new meaning solely because of the spell lines. At level 52 you have reached the end of the PVP line. You are now in range and vulnerable to the best of all spell lines for all classes. Many people before reaching level 52 place 100% of their experience into Alternate Advancement points in the hopes of avoiding PVP confrontation until a later time when they are better equipped with amenities such as run speed III or mana regent in the case of a caster or priest.

Class Bard
Pros There are absolutely hands down NO pros to fighting a Bard in a duel or pvp environment. They are the best PVP class in the game.
Cons If skilled, a bard can "weave" up to 5 manaless songs per session. What does this mean? It means they can have up to 5 spells working to their advantage during the pvp encounter and not break a sweat because they won't be running out of "mana". They can snare you, debuff you, direct damage you, heal themselves and debuff any dots you've decided to throw on them in 1 flawless weave execution. Although the most songs I've seen woven were a maximum of 4, they can most assuredly do 5. Please also remember that bards not only have the fastest sow in the game, they have selos speed, which is like sow x 3. Even if you were to get them to low health, usually a bard just runs off, heals up, uses their track to find you, and engages again.
Do they have a pet? No (*Yumanea's Note: Bards can however charm mobs and other players to make them their pets.)
Things to watch out for He can not dot you, and he is not a caster. However they do have track, and invisibility which makes an ambush easy if you're not prepared
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class If you're going to engage in Bard PVP, out damaging them quickly is the only way to win the situation. Above all, do not let him run. If you have no snare or root, hopefully you can keep up with them after they back down from a pvp fight. Melees have a much greater chance to kill a Bard in PVP because of their damage output. Clerics on the other hand have to wait for spell gems to repop.

Class Beastlord
Pros There are not a lot of Beastlords on the Red servers. They are few and far between. They use a base of mainly poison and cold based Dots and DD's. They are weak in casting damage. You should never die from a Beastlords spell
Cons As with any class that has a pet [except enchanters], pets were designed to be the main tank. Since the last patch Sony has implemented that pets have the SAME resists as their owners. As I have tested since the patch, it still poses very little problem. It's not the resists we're concerned with, it's the melee damage they do. The rule of thumb with ANY class that has a pet is the SAME throughout the game. Get rid of the pet and dump all your mana into the owner as quickly as you can
Do they have a pet Yes-heavy melee
Things to watch out for The Beastlord's pet is actually summoned via ability. The Beastlord also receives a number of Shaman based spells such as Resistance Buffs, Statistic Buffs and Heals. They also have available to them Slow Spells and Spirit of Wolf at Level 39. The Vah Shir are by far the strongest and toughest of the Beastlords. Again-it comes down to damage output. Do you have what it takes in your direct damage spell line at your level to actually do more damage to him than he does to you? Is your mana pool high enough?
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Fear the pet and assess the level of the owner with your direct damage line of spells. If your spells are hitting for a high percentage of damage, and barely scratching the surface, you know you're facing a challenge and need to think quick.
Class Druid
Pros Druids often depend on their DOTS and snare to do most of their damage. Their dots are MR based and this is easily avoidable with a good set of resist gear. Again, jump in with your weapon and "A" key ready. Interrupt them-because no matter what class they are-they can not take melee damage.
Cons Snare is by far and above the most crippling pvp challenge. If you ever find yourself snared, click off all of your buffs and dispell yourself quickly. By the time you remove the snare, the druid should already be in the middle of a new cast. Again-charge the PC and attack, and hope your weapon strike interrupts the cast. Druids are more of a nuisance than a PVP threat. Sow makes it difficult as well
Do they have a pet? Yes, at level 55. Druids can also charm animals.
Things to watch out for As I have said, they have sow and invisibility. At higher levels they get brutal DOT's. Mainly one that I see resisted very little is Winged Death. It's an MR based DOT that does up to 1K in hp damage within a 1 minute time frame. This DOT needs to be dispelled before it leaves you at 25% health with a full mana bar-which wont do you any good at this point because the Druid has just enough of a DD to finish you off. Never be afraid to use your own mana for self heals or dispell during a PVP encounter if you have the chance and time
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Melee damage and your nukes seem to take care of the Druid pretty quickly. Druids at lower levels are really good PVP'ers do not underestimate them. However as they get higher our spell lines are modified and increased in damage output, where as they are still dependent on DOT's.
Class Enchanter
Pros Enchanters were not made for PVP, they are made for crowd control in a mob situation. They have very low AC and HP and can not take much damage before dying a swift death
Cons People underestimate Enchanters greatly. They are infamous for Tash, Cripple, and having pets
Do they have a pet? Yes
Things to watch out for Always fear the chanters pet if you can it is their only source of damage output.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Tash can be immediately cured with cure poison. A lot of people do not realize this-and since they don't see their hitpoints go down, they don't think it's doing any damage. What it's doing is keeping all of your resists low and leaving you vulnerable for further PVP-something you do not want to be. Also-it is very rare that an Enchanter is ever alone. Where there is 1, there is always a Tank and or a Cleric behind them. Before you engage an Enchanter in PVP, make sure you're not outnumbered. They are by far, the most intelligent class in PVP because of their weaknesses, they are forced to re-evaluate their strategies during play.
Class Magician
Pros The ONLY pro in PVP with a Mage is if you're lucky they won't have their pet up-please see below.
Cons The primary focus of a Magician is as a pet class. Magicians have the largest variety of pets in the game. They are the strongest and hardest to kill. My philosophy on pets is to FEAR them always. If your alteration skill is high, and your fear always sticks, this is the first plan of attack. Please remember that Verant just implemented same resist statistics to mimic their owners. Don't expect to fear a Mage pet if the Mage has got on 160 MR or higher.
Do they have a pet? Yes-their Earth pets are the strongest of the 4 types and cast root.
Things to watch out for PVP Mages are infamous for hiding behind a corner, targeting a PC and sending their pet in to attack. By the time you're knocked to your feet, and visually gain your footing, you're going to try to find the owner of that pet. DO NOT WORRY ABOUT THE MAGE. Handle the pet first. You'll be dead before you can get a target on the PC.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class TRY to fear the pet. If you're unsuccessful in fearing try rooting it. The bottom line is, unless you root or fear the pet you will never get a crack at the Mage behind the pet because no matter how you duck and weave or run that pet will always be on you. IF you can get past the pet you have the capability to kill the Mage. Prevent him from casting with melee, dispell and DD him. Mages use mainly MR and FIRE based DD's. The advantage to most Mage damage [aside from their pet] is that it takes them 4 plus seconds to cast. That is more than enough time for us to interrupt them.
Class Monk
Pros The only pro to PVP with a Monk is that they are possibly a few levels lower than you.
Cons This class pure melee. They disarm, have and have high AC. Their sole agenda is to hit you-period. They will interrupt your casting as much as they can.
Do they have a pet? No
Things to watch out for They disarm you frequently-often removing wisdom and statistical armor from your primary and secondary slots. If you do not have at least 1 or 2 open slots in your main inventory field, and your bags are full, you run the risk of losing your primary and or secondary weapon. They will be dropped to the ground and you won't find them in combat, hence a /petition and along wait period after. This bug has supposedly been fixed in patches-however it's always smart to leave at least 2 slots open for your weapons in case of a glitch.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class *GATE*
Clerics never have and never will be able to kill a Monk. Our class differences and abilities are so far beyond comparison we just don't have the damage output to do it. A Wizard or other Melee has the opportunity to kill a Monk because they do have the damage output. I have killed 1 monk in my time. He was an Iksar and 1 level lower than I was. After the PVP encounter was over we compared gear. There were 2 reasons I was able to defeat him. The first was my channeling ability. I had kept my skill up and religiously placed training points in it for such an occasion. The second was that his gear was average-with only moderate AC so my DD's probably took for close to full PVP damage. Keep in mind that I have played on a red server for 8 months now and have only killed ONE Monk lol. What does that tell you?
Class Necromancer
Pros There is a lot you can learn PVP wise from a Necromancer. However you don't want him using you as the target. Other than that-if you can get rid of the pet-they're always at less than full life because they're expecting to LifeTap something-either a mob or PC.
Cons These are the nastiest casters in the game. They can do serious DOT, and DD casting damage in a very short amount of time with very little mana to spend on themselves.
Do they have a pet? Yes
Things to watch out for There are 3 major components to any Necromancer that you need to be careful of. The first is Deflux which is a life tap. Necromancers are infamous for taking life from mobs and gaining strength and health. The same holds true for a PC. If they can do it to them, they can do it to you. The second is Splurt, which is a MR based DOT that does 1100 points of damage over 1.5 minutes. This spell is a creeper, it starts off very slow. You'll know when it truly kicks in because you're health bar will just go down down down. The third is a level 60 spell called Funeral Pyre. It is a HUGE fire based DOT that will hit a mob [remember PVP spells never hit for the full damage] for 2160 in 54 seconds.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Necromancers as I said are the DOT true damage kings. They are more important to dispel than the druid dots. If you are buffed well enough for PVP you could out live a druid dot. Chances of you out living a Necromancer dot while he's sucking the life right out of you and dotting you are slim to none. Rule 1. Get rid of the pet Rule #2 debuff yourself if you're dotted and Rule #3. Hit them hard and hit them fast
Class Ranger
Pros It is very easy to "hill bug" a tracker, mainly a Ranger. It prevents them from properly tracking and casting on you. Rangers are what I like to call the Melee/Druid half-breed. They can duel wield, but they can snare and track as well.
Cons Rangers also pose a slightly higher threat than Druids, because they are melee based not casting based. This is when your channeling skill comes in handy. They are very under rated in the PVP scope of classes and can hold their own.
Do they have a pet? No
Things to watch out for Never fear a Ranger, their fighting style is pretty consistent except if you are in open flat land. Find water or a hill quickly if you can. If you engage in an open area with a Ranger, get ready for a long fight because they are the most persistent class in PVP. It doesn't matter where you run, it doesn't matter where you hide, they will find you.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class They will try to snare you, then they'll melee you. Debuff them, hill bug them nuke them, and when all else fails? Run…. The short in stature [halflings dwarfs, gnomes, etc] PC's on the server will teach you to dodge, weave, jump and move with incredible agility when you're forced into a combat situation. These skills are to be used with the Ranger and many melees to avoid being struck.
Class Paladin
Pros The good thing is, they can not cast anything on you that you aren't prepared for-they are not true casters.
Cons They are the hybrid between the Warrior class and the Cleric class. The bad thing is they have an outrageously high AC level and can self heal quickly.
Do they have a pet? No
Things to watch out for They have a skill that is called Lay of Hands which is a self only complete manaless heal.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class The over all suggestions in this guide have been consistent throughout the classes. The Priest class and ANY melee do not mix in PVP. It is possible to win a PVP encounter with a melee but very unrealistic to expect a good outcome.
Class Rogue
Pros Rogues concentrate on the skill back stab. It is their 1 and only consistent source of damage. Just as a melee is designed to be the main tank, a rogue is designed for additional damage on the mob but in an assist capacity. You'll see where I'm going with this, in a moment.
Cons Their back stabs are UNGODLY. I have never been hit by a melee with such damage in one blow, than I have been backstabbed once by a Rogue. I have seen melee damage in the 200's. I have seen consecutive back stabs for 600+ hp's per stab.
Do they have a pet? No
Things to watch out for Open spaces-there is no way to defend yourself with a Rogue in an open area
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Find a corner and do it QUICKLY. Their job is to nudge and move you into open space just enough to get around you to back stab you. If they can't do that, you'll be able to take them down quickly with just your DD's unless he's 8 levels higher and you're not scratching the surface.
Class Shadow Knight
Pros The only pro to PVP with a Shadow Knight is that they are possibly a few levels lower than you. They are a hybrid class between a Necromancer and a Warrior and get some excellent PVP abilities.
Cons They are a plate wearing class, which means higher AC for them. They have feigned death in case of mob agro while in PVP battle and can disarm you. SK's also have something called Shadowstep. It's a 5 second cast, 10 mana using spell that will translocate them a short distance from the spot in which they stand. Picture the scenario, the PC hits you, shadowsteps, hits you, shadowsteps etc. This could go on for 5 minutes if you're persistent. If you have a see invisible item try to put it on really quickly. At least this way you'll be able to follow the PC.
Do they have a pet? Yes, from level 9 onward
Things to watch out for Disarm and their primary weapon-we are not a melee class-they are. They also have an innate skill called HT or Harm Touch. If used in PVP it is a manaless way to shave up to 300 hp off of his opponent. I can honestly say I have never been harm touched. This skill would be saved for PVP versus a much harder class, such as a Warrior.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Fancy footwork is about the only thing that's going to keep you alive with this class. As I said earlier this is a melee class. Their main function is to come at you like an indestructible brick wall. If you chose to engage in PVP activity instead of gating-the best advice I can give you is to dispel and nuke. That is about all we can do. If you have mana do not waste it on a pet. None of us keep unswerving hammer of faith memmed in a gem slot anyway. I call PVP with a melee class a dance lesson. Nothing but a lot of running around, casting and jumping to get further distance between you and the nasty weapon he's holding. And usually-we do not win. You have a 60/40 shot of winning that fight. And again that's only if your channeling skill is up. Do not engage in PVP with a Shadow Knight and expect a moment's peace to be able to cast free from interruption.
Class Shaman
Pros Shamans are a pretty straightforward class. They can melee about as much as a Cleric can.
Cons Their disease and poison based DOTS will take you down faster than you can say to yourself "what's that icon?" and click on it.
Do they have a pet? Yes-but there will be a 50/50 chance the Shaman did not summon one. Shamans seem to not depend on their pet as heavily on the PVP server as some of the other classes do, such as a Magician [Mage] and a Necromancer.
Things to watch out for Torpor is a healing spell that will almost completely heal a mid 50's level Shaman if cast in PVP. Shamans have the capability to also cannibalize themselves, turning hit points into mana. You may think you've done 80% damage to them when in fact you've only done 40, and then you can watch their heath bar go up unless you interrupt their casting.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class It is a matter of damage versus time [are you getting a sense of strategy theme yet?] with the Shaman. If you see them buffing up in the corner, most likely they will summon a dog. Attack quickly and catch them off guard. Keep your poison and disease buffs on, and you shouldn't have a problem with a Shaman. We make great contenders with a little practice. VERY IMPORTANT NOTE-If you see a Shaman walking away from the PVP fight and his health bar slowly starts to go up, dispel him immediately! He has healed himself with Torpor which is a heal over time. Dispell him and nuke him until you get your SS!
Class Warrior
Pros Zip-Zilch-Nodda-Nothing- if you're lucky, you'll be in a dungeon and he'll be on the human team. They have poor vision.
Cons I think the name of the class says it all, but in case you haven't noticed, we'll get completely wiped out by a warrior unless he's a lower level with mediocre gear.
Do they have a pet? No [Thank Tunare]
Things to watch out for They disarm and don't ever run from PVP
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class The fancy footwork rule applies here. Head for the murky dark water or a cave. The Human team has poor vision. If it is anything other than the human team you may have luck ducking and weaving in and out of foliage or trees. My best suggestion when encountering a Warrior in PVP is to use your highest DD spell and watch his health bar. If it goes down significantly, rest assured his AC is low, and or he isn't wearing any buffs. In this case you want to kite the melee as much as you can-get off as many DD's as possible. Remember when all else fails, GATE works.
Class Wizard
Pros Wizards are just like Enchanters in that they can not take direct damage of any kind. They generally have low AC as well.
Cons These are the most powerful casters in the game. Even though as I mentioned earlier damage scales for the casters have been extremely reduced in PVP they are a force to be reckoned with.
Do they have a pet? No-they have a familiar
Things to watch out for Ice comet is one of the most common DD cast a Wizard has to work with. It takes a low amount of mana and could hit the PC anywhere from 250-700hp's a hit. Wizards also have a spell called Yonder, which is similar to the Shadow Knight shadow step. The final warning is about an AA ability they often work for, which is called Mana Burn or MB. I have been MB'd for over 2000 hp's of direct damage in one cast. Needless to say you'll die pretty quickly if a Wizard decides to use his Mana Burn on you instead of saving it for a mob.
Overall Strategy for PVP'ing this class Wizards are one of the most challenging and more fun casters to PVP. They certainly make you work for a KS or kill shot. Always dispel then try to root the Wizard. Oddly enough whenever I do use my high level root, it tends to stick on the Wizards more than any other class. Prepare to do a lot of chasing with this class. You must be the aggressor.

Spells to keep memmed

Dependent upon if you're a solo cleric or a continually grouped cleric, these change drastically. On a PVP server when soloing it is imperative that you keep at least 7 of the following spells memmed at all times. You may change the order of spell per your preference. Please keep in mind this was based on a level 54 character.

Complete Heal
Fear-for pets and mobs that agro while you're travelling
Divine Barrier
Celestial Heal-in a long PVP battle when you're almost OOM you can usually cast one of these on yourself or group mate
Immobilize-Unless you have a clickable root item in your inventory this also stops the adds while running around in PVP and may even help in PVP
Annul Magic-this is self explanatory
Reckoning
Gate

Since I have a Warden symbol of Tunare I can replace Immobilize with Retribution or Blinding Luminance.

Items to keep in your bag

Unlike the blue servers, on a red server you must have and carry with you at all times, 2 sets of gear. The first set you will wear on a daily basis. It consists of your regular PVE gear. The second set is your PVP gear, which consists of your resist gear. In a PVP environment your resists are double the norm of what is needed on a blue. Carrying around 2 sets of gear can be cumbersome, so this also entails finding weight reduction bags as not to be encumbered. The ideology on weapons holds true as well. For a cleric, the 2HB is critical for damage dealing. 1HB weapons are only used when in PVE for the extra wisdom or resist statistics. However in a PVP situation you will want your highest damage dealing primary weapon and hope that you've had practice points placed in 2HB.

The red servers are much different than the blue servers. There is continual conversation, interaction, friction, defensiveness, more excitement and even fear on a red server. It is almost like a true war zone. "Go to a blue server" is the common reaction of the winner of a PVP occurrence, answering the loser because he was upset and decided to verbally express his discontent out loud in the zone. Tallon Zek is a PVP Teams server. So for the light of heart, and the weak in will, may I suggest you stay on the blue server? The amount of stress and confusion and day to day chaos on a red server will make you miserable. However, if you like challenge and constant adventure the moment you log onto the server, if you're always peaking around corners wondering who's there and constantly being on your guard, if you always wonder to yourself "boy I wish I could just walk up to him and.." then a red server may be the place for you. Please keep in mind the rules on a red server are also very different. It is complete culture shock. I have characters on blue servers as well. Red server characters go to play their "alts" on the blue servers only when they want peace and quiet. I will end this reference with saying there is no feeling of accomplishment like there is on a red server.

I hope you enjoyed the guide
