July 14 Update
7/8/04, 18:59 PST | Kwill

Robert Pfister on the Next EverQuest Update

Dear Friends,

I wanted to pass along a quick update and let you know that we're shifting the July 8 update back a week to July 14. As you know from reading posts from Brenlo or playing on our Test Server, this update is quite extensive. This update will include several community requested features, quite a few bug fixes, and some back-end changes that, while invisible to you, will help us immensely in the future. These continuing community driven changes will help us to maintain a healthy game and to re-affirm to you, our customers and fans, that we do value your input and, when appropriate, enhance the game based upon your suggestions.

As part of our ongoing plan to provide you with the best possible play experience, we've looked at the testing that went on over the last several weeks and determined that these changes should spend another week or so on the Test Server. When we update, we want to be sure that the update enhances your play experience; pushing code too quickly doesn't do that. I know that many of you are eager for the changes you've read about or played on Test, however, I guarantee that the extra week will be well worth your wait. If you simply cannot wait to experience these changes, please feel free to join us on the Test Server. As always, your feedback is not only appreciated, but considered one of the things that continues to make this game so great!

Thanks again for your support and for your patience.

Robert Pfister
Executive Producer
EverQuest


   Friday, June 25, 2004

Interview with George Scotto, SOE
6/25/04, 8:27 PST | Kwill

Recently, I had a chance to interview George Scotto, Vice President of Customer Service and Quality Assurance. My questions focused on the new customer service system and how it is working to cut down on response times and resolution of player problems:

Kwill: How will the new CS tool cut down on response time? What is your goal for response times for different problems?

GS: There are a few big changes that will help cut down on response times. First, CS Assist will allow us to use persistent messaging. We will be able to respond and resolve tickets even when customers go offline. Next, we will know what type of tickets are coming in before reading the actual context, based on what type of category and sub-category are submitted. This will allow us to sort by category and address issues that need immediate attention, such as: harassment or zone disruption. Last but not least, we will be able to sort tickets based on age (time in queue), allowing us to address the oldest ticket in queue. We will now be able to address the oldest ticket in the queue. Our goal is to have an average response time of less than 3 hours and for harassment and disruption issues, we would like it to be even lower.

Kwill: What will be your hierarchy for dealing with player problems? Which problems will take precedence?

GS: There really is not a formal hierarchy for dealing with player problems; however we always prioritize Terms of Service violations (harassment, zone disruption, etc…), “stuck character” issues and problems directly preventing game play.

Kwill: What will happen if a problem is not listed as a category on the CS tool list?

GS: We will create new categories as necessary and ask our customers to let us know what’s missing. If we see a consistent trend of issues that are not included in our list of categories/sub-categories then we will add the category as soon as possible.

Kwill: What other steps is CS taking to make sure that player problems are solved as quickly as possible?

GS: We have recently implemented internal tools that will help our GM’s resolve tickets faster, such as offline reimbursement and “soul-marking” tool. It usually takes a CSR 25-30 minutes to investigate and resolve a reimbursement issue. With the new offline reimbursement tool, our GM’s will be able to resolve these issues in less than 10 minutes.

Kwill: What is the best way for a player to have a problem dealt with in the quickest amount of time?

GS: We ask that players provide as much detail as possible regarding their problem. For example, if they are inquiring about an item loss, we want players to provide: time/date of the loss, which zone the player lost the item, the name of the item, and any other important information that would help a GM to resolve the issue. Also, there are many cases where a player will submit a ticket and ask that their friends also submit a ticket on the same issue hoping for a faster response. In reality, this actually slows down our response times because we read each ticket received. If all our customers could submit tickets on their own behalf, we will be able to help customers a lot more efficiently.

Kwill: What happens when a complaint is made to a GM about behavior or a name problem in the game? Will the response time change for these types of problems?

GS: For harassment issues or naming violations, we address these issues as quickly as possible, as we understand this affects game play for many of our customers.

Kwill: What about support after regular business hours? How will that be handled?

GS: We recently added a graveyard shift in San Diego. The hours are from 10pm to 7am Pacific Time.

Kwill: Will there be a system where players can appeal a decision? How will that work?

GS: The current system is to send an email to eqcs@soe.sony.com or request an escalation to the GM's Supervisor. We will continue to use the same system.

Kwill: Will the server GM handle all the problems or will they go to a general pool, or someone who specifically deals with one kind of problem?

GS: During primetime hours, which are between 5pm - 8pm Pacific Time, GM’s cover their dedicated servers. On the off-peak times, GM’s will handle the oldest tickets in the queue regardless which server the ticket is submitted from.

Thank you George Scotto!
