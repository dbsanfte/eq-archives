Stop bringing the server down, already!!!
Posted by: Druss on Saturday, July 27th, 2002 @ 3:50 PM

We all know what happens after the servers go down in EQ - The inevitable post-patch feeding frenzy by every guild on the server. Within hours of the server coming back up, pretty much everything worth killing is already dead to the hands of the usual suspects. Lord knows, we got caught up in it.

After the patch, we spent our Friday killing the High Priest of Ssra (is it me, or is he like the most overrated "tier 8" mob ever made?) Lord Yelinak, A quick sweep of the Sleeper's crew, and a Zlandicar kill. CC's share of loot for the day came to a Halo of Enlightening Barriers for Grakus, a Sporecaller for Sehria, a Primal Velium Warsword for Druss (yay me!), Gauntlets of Dragonslaying for Parris, A sleeper's key for Whoa, and some assorted crap noone wanted.

Remember...Reduce (the crappy drops on mobs), Reuse (the same tried and true Zerg-o-licious tactics), and recycle (those craptastic drops into peridots whenever possible). Thank god for the new /trader interface, the next best thing to VI implementing 12 slot backpacks. I managed to clear out so much crap that had been rotting in the bank for months, that I can actually stop carrying around those $%&*($&#* Lucid Shards around.

So, anyway, back on topic - we all felt pretty good at the close of last night. We figured tonight might be a relaxing night off, a night where I could blow the dust off that Warcraft 3 jewel case sitting on my desk, or get around to finishing Soldier of Fortune 2 (Fantastic shooter, btw - good level design, plot is so-so, but the weapons are just flat-out the best I've seen in a shooter, ever - GG OICW!). Then we log out, only to see another patch that evening...so tonight...the madness begins again.

BTW - Ragefire spawning 5 minutes before the server going down = Not Funny!

WANNABE of the Week

News: Prepping for VT
posted on 08-01-2002 09:24 AM by Thornsong

Been pulling our hairs out this past week in a mad scramble to finish off key parts before entering Vex Thal. Over 400 shards have been looted by our guild this week alone.

With the patch yesterday we decided to grab some key glowing orbs of luclinite to finish some people off so we headed to Ssra.

Zoning in, I do a quick /w. 283 people in the zone!?! Where do I buy my trader bag? This is prime selling real estate -- especially since the zone is bugged and mobs aren't respawning when you kill them.

Anyway, we are formed up in the basement and pull the triggers - well the zone is so lagged the mobs aren't fighting back so the monks can't pull the triggers -- so we go to them and kill them in place for once.

Glyphed spawns, glyphed goes down flawless. Grats Malheur on the Bow and score 1 Luclinite, the real loot of the night.

Exiled up and down quickly as well. Grats Almir on the shoulders and Tronsmang on the luclinite. That's 2.

Cursed was a tough fight with the lag. He was lagging himself as his AE didn't stay at a pat 24 seconds - would bounce up and down. But people need keys so he had to die. Grats Korkrest on the melee cloak, Lildaanie on the ft4 one, Furly on Goldenrod and Iuvas on Luclinite #3.

Well, we leave Ssra and head to Akheva as we hear luclinite is up there too.

Va Dyn pulled and dead. Grats Coldar on your amulet and Solknar on Luclinite #4.

Itraer who? Went down just as quick as Va Dyn and for once we pulled to Itraer without a train. Grats someone on FT2 neck (I missed the call) and Thundre on Luclinite #5 and the Akheva shard that dropped right after to finish his key.

We checked our numbers and were still strong so we headed to Shei. One of our smoothest fights yet on him and he went down in record time. Grats Manolas and Mirrorpond on Boon of Recovery Spheres, Kaeln on finding harmony with a cloak, and Hierl on Luclinite #6.

Six orbs in a day was enough, we still needed those shards so people headed back out into Norrath to look for more. My favorite quote of the week comes from Grizumpy who said, "I want to lock all the bathrooms in Verant and sprinkle the keys all over North America and make them feel our pain."
