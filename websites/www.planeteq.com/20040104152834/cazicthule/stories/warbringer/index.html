Warbringer
by Penya Dreamlight

The snow had its own illumination that set fire to the soul. The strength required to live is all a barbarian needed to know, at least it was all Warbringer needed. His whole being was set to challenge nature. He fought against the hunger of winter when all that could be done was hunt or stay huddled in the snug houses of Halas like an old woman. He fought against his desire to sit by the fire and drink, exchanging stories with his clansman. Mostly, though, he fought against the cold that had its own deadly power.

His fogged breath preceded him as he tracked the wolves. The winter had been particularly bad and the wolves had run out of prey. Hunger had overridden their natural fear of people and they had turned their attention to the villagers. A couple of children had already been killed within sight of the tunnel entrance to the city.

Warbringer had only recently gone through the trial of manhood, and still needed to prove himself to be included on the more glorious expeditions. It was time to work on making a reputation for himself among his tribe. He wanted to walk into Halas with a pile of skins from the vicious pack. In his heart he knew he was destined to have his name told in the spoken stories repeated to the children and sung by the visiting bards. His name would be carried down generation after generation, giving him immortality.

First, though, he had to find the wolf packs. Warbringer followed the tracks in the freshly fallen snow. From what he could tell, there seemed to be a pack of at least six animals that were prowling close to Halas. Intermixed with the wolf tracks was the evidence of a couple of bear cubs. He saw no sign of the mother bear. That was good. Adult polar bears were simply killing machines and were wildly protective of their cubs.

Finally Warbringer discovered a small group of wolves. With a mighty cry, he jumped amidst the dogs, swinging his mighty two-handed sword. Little subtlety was required in dispatching the snarling creatures. There was subtlety required, however in noticing the human-like tracks to the side of the path. Trails were easy to see in the deep snow surrounding the paths. Traveling through the snow cut out a highly visible trail. On the paths, however, the cover of snow was light. Some sections were actually covered by ice where camps had been made and the snow had been melted away only to re-freeze. It was near these areas that Warbringer could have looked to see if there was anything else in the area. He lacked the experience to know that he should. He also lacked the experience to realize just how far he had wandered from Halas.

He had traveled into far more dangerous ground than he realized.

The barbarian never saw the creature approach that would kill him. He only became aware of it when he felt the bruising force pummel him from behind. He stood to face a frightening skeleton of the undead realm. It's eyes glowed with an internal illumination as it cackled, delighting in finding a living soul to extinguish. Warbringer raised his sword and swung, knocking the creature back a little - but only a little. The vengeful composer responded back with a tremendous blow right into Warbringer's chest. The barbarian could feel his ribs cracking with the impact. The barbarian's next swing held none of the force of the first. From that point the battle could only end one way.

As the years went by, Warbringer's name would be spoken. The fire of the town would light up the faces of the children as the storyteller would say the name of the first victim of the wave of deaths that would follow. Warbringer's death would mark the start of the struggle with the undead musicians as they rose to strike out against all of the living they could find.

It was not the legacy that a warrior with the fire of the north in his heart wanted to leave.
