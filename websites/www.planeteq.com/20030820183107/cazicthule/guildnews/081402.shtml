Tuesday, August 7th, 2002
Nobody Does It Slower....

The folks at the Guinness Book visited our guild today to make their official rounds. The events fortold by Nostradamus, the Book of Revalations, and crazy Chauncey the prognosticator had come to pass, and the Guinness folks had to dutifully record it.

Way back in distant times, when Norrath was new and dinosaurs still roamed the planet, there was a little PalaDINK chick with a dream. The dream to grow and be the best little DINK she could. The details of these times are few and far between. Civilizations have risen and fallen since then. The great ice age has returned and relented 3 times, grinding most records of this mythological period under the stark power of nature. The few details that have been secreted away and passed from father to son to lecherous uncle to police sargeant and then back to father tell us that in these times there were only a slight handful of Norraths, and our own home, Cazic Thule, was one of them. They tell us that in these times, among the first few adventurers of Norrath waded the little lost DINK. While some adventurers raced against the cruel realities of Norrath and built themselves into powerful machines of vengeance incarnate, our little DINK wandered the countryside in search of thongs, good times, backrubs, and a mythical Trout.

As time marched on and other raced like high powered rocket fueled funny cars, our DINK raced along more like a Tonka Truck. Events were stacked against her. Having grown up before such modern convenience as language, it's use often evaded her and left others wondering what to do with words that would make the Ebonics folks stop dead. Hurdles like this merely steeled her rigid resolve to grow at a pace rivaled only by continental drift.

But, as fate and psychic predictions fortold, there came a day, TODAY where our little DINK would carve her name in the recordbooks, in first place before every person who has ever played this game.

And that came to pass, for today, Jenka made level 60. Nobody on any server has taken longer to reach this goal. There were no Alts, no delevelling, no calling it quits and abandoning the game, just an unsteady shuffle to max level that wound it's way just about every direction but straight.

To be sure, there were long pauses in growth at some points. Jenka helped introduce Thund to EQ. When Jenka did this, she was level 54. When Thund made 60, Jenka was still 54. This steadfastness of purpose to meander to where ya wanna go was truly amazing to watch.

Some day, some dedicated individual might set their minds to it and break Jenka's record, but for today, we salute Jenka for an achievement that puts her in a position above all other players of EQ throughout the world.
