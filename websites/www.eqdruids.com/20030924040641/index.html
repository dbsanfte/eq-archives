Lords of Everquest goes Hollywood! 9/23/03

Sony Online Entertainment (SOE) has announced the names of a slew of Hollywood talent who were brought in to provide voice-over acting for use in its upcoming PC game, Lords of EverQuest.


The real-time strategy game, due out this fall, will offer over 75 hours of single player game play, while also supporting a variety of online modes of play for up to a dozen players per game. The actors used in the title include:


    · Fairuza Balk (American History X, The Craft, The Waterboy)
    · Claudia Black (Farscape, Pitch Black)
    · Kevin Conroy (Batman: The Animated Series, Justice League)
    · John Rhys-Davies (Lord of The Rings, Raiders of the Lost Ark)
    · Hudson Leick (Xena: Warrior Princess)
    · Kate Mulgrew (Star Trek: Voyager, Remo Williams: The Adventure Begins)
    · Ron Perlman (Hellboy, Enemy at the Gates, Beauty & The Beast)
    · Katey Sagal (8 Simple Rules For Dating my Teenage Daughter, Futurama, Married ... With Children)
    · Dwight Schultz (Star Trek: The Next Generation, Star Trek: Voyager, The A-Team)
    · Cree Summer (Rugrats Go Wild!, A Different World)
    · Michael T. Weiss (The Pretender)

Lords of Everquest demo has been released 9/20/03

Good news and bad news on this one.  The good news is that there is a Lords of Everquest demo available through Gamespot.

Now for the bad news.  Gamespot will not allow you to download this demo without installing their special download software made by Kontiki.

What this software is doing as a default, is setting to run every time you boot up your computer AND THEN it uses your computer as a partial fileserver to help speed up downloads to other peoples computers (its a file sharing software).

From the license agreement.

...you are agreeing that solely as part of the operation of the Kontiki Delivery Grid Kontiki may through the Kontiki Software access Content stored on your computer (which Content you had previously requested to be delivered and stored from the Kontiki Delivery Grid) and reproduce portions or all of this Content in order to distribute such stored Content to other users of the Kontiki Delivery Grid who request delivery of such Content.
 

This is not made clear unless you read the license agreement very carefully or you go into the advanced settings to change the settings.

Yes you can turn off the file sharing.  But you have to know it is happening in the first place!  Otherwise you have just turned your computer into a file server for this website and didn't probably realize it.

Personally I think this is an extremely poor move on the part of Gamespot, and SOE needs to wakeup to who is distributing their demos.

My recommendation?  If you want to see the demo, setup the Kontiki download, download the demo (it's very large, be ready) and then immediately remove the Gamespot download manager from your system.  When you do, it will ask why you are removing it, make sure to choose the "TOO INTRUSIVE" choice.

Ok, getting off my soapbox for the moment.

More on Augments and slots 9/20/03

Something to be careful with when augmenting your items.  If you put an augment that lists wrist, on an item that is wrist or neck, your item will become wearable ONLY on the wrist.

So when augmenting understand that the augment slots (wrist, ear, arm etc) will override the items slots.

Bug in Deity restricted augmentation 9/16/03

There is a bug currently that if you try to augment an item that has a deity restriction on it, with an augment that doesn't have a deity restriction on it you can't augment.

This is a bug, and should be fixed next patch.
