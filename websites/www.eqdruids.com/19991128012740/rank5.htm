1) Careless Lightning. Finally we get an update to our venerable Ignite spell. This direct damage spell can be cast indoors or out, and it costs 70 mana to cast. It does a range of damage from 2-99 pts. Basically if your target does not resist it will do 94, but if the target resists it will do significantly less. When it does max damage it is an awesome spell with a damage-mana ratio of 94:70, which is pretty good (although not as good as Stinging Swarm, at least its instantaneous). However it will with alarming frequency do 2-20pts of damage, which is an enormous waste of mana. At least it is almost never entirely resisted, unlike:

Twylight : Useful in parties or just dropping the hammer on something you don't like anymore...like a green that used to beat you senseless.

2) Dizzying Wind. This spell is an upgrade to your Whirling Wind spell. It costs 80 pts mana to cast but does a whopping 110 pts of damage. Unfortunately it keeps the weaknesses of its predecessor. It either does max damage, or it does NO damage. Unfortunately if you cast it on monsters that con white to red, there is a very good chance it will be completely resisted. Unlike Careless Lightning, it will not do some lesser amount of damage when resisted, it is all or nothing. It is also an outdoor-only spell. These problems make me favor Careless Lightning over Dizzying Wind. I don’t see the point in using two memorization slots to keep two spells memorized that do basically the same thing.

Note: You may see some reference on other outdated web-sites that say these Wind spells do extra damage to flying creatures. This is simply not true. There may be a possibility that flying creatures have a harder time resisting these spells, but I can’t say for sure.

3) Cancel Magic. An important spell when facing creatures that like to Root you. It allows you to dispel enchantments that have been cast on you. I haven’t had the opportunity to battle-test this spell yet, so further updates will be made.

Twylight :  This spell is must against charming monsters..nothing quit like that tank turning and beating the crap out of you...

4) Barbcoat. An upgrade to our Thistlecoat spell. I was really looking forward to getting this spell and I must admit I am a bit disappointed with it. It adds 10pts to your AC, a slight improvement over Thistlecoat, and it does a measly 2 pts of damage when creatures strike you, instead of 1 pt for Thistlecoat. The mana cost is almost double what Thistlecoat costs, but due to its extremely long duration its not a big deal (simply meditate for a bit after casting it). No reason NOT to use this spell, but I was certainly hoping for a bigger improvement.

Twylight : Self only - ALWAYS keep it up

5) Shield of Barbs. An upgrade to Shield of Thorns damage shield spell. I am VERY disappointed in this spell at this time. I say at this time because it might improve significantly as we rise in levels, but we will have to wait and see. It cost a whopping 60 mana to cast as opposed to 40 for Shield of Thorns and it only does ONE more point of damage! What a joke. It doesn’t last any longer and it does not improve your AC. It will barely last you through a combat. Right now I do NOT use this spell, I continue to use Shield of Thistles.

Twylight: This spell increases from 7 points of damage to 9 as you move up in level.   At level 20 it becomes worth it...but not at 19...

6) Ring of Commons/Karana/Butcher/Toxxulia. These are your first teleport spells. They cost a massive 150 mana to cast but boy are they a time saver. If you look at your map you will see these spells are perfectly spaced for you to get to the major points of Norrath. East and West Antonia, Feydwer, and Erudine. Also, the teleport destination point is in some cases a Druid ring, with druids that you can buy spells and train from.

Twylight : Once I have this, I set my bind to the outside of a zone I'm killing in and ring back to sell.

7) Healing. The next in your line of healing spells. I’m guilty of not checking the exact healing:mana ratio, but it is a major improvement over light healing. You can now effectively heal party members in combat again if you are so inclined.

Twylight : Slow, fizzles more, but useful and makes you a second tier cleric.

8) Superior Camouflage. An improvement over your camouflage spell in that you can cast it indoors. This spell seems to be getting better and better.  Many of the things that can see thru Level 5 camo cant see thru SC.  The only thing that seems to be able to see thru this is high level NPCs and casters...
