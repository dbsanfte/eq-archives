First, a few basics:
 
The rain line of spells is a small radius AE which dumps 3 waves of deadly rain on any targets within its radius. The target may resist each individual wave or not. If the target does NOT resist, it takes FULL damage for that wave. The waves are spaced about a tick apart (6 seconds). One little snafu is that currently the rain appears to be bugged so that it will not strike the killing blow on a mob - if a wave would actually kill the mob, it won't do damage.
 
The problems with using it have always been that (a) it breaks root and the target(s) wander out of the effect after the first wave, and (b) the target resists a lot. Its still tantalizing because of the amount of damage you can dish out for the mana cost...
 
I found a good way to use it for a single mob, if you are tough enough to handle getting whacked around a little (which can be a problem if your mobs are quading for 500+ and land a stun, but otherwise shouldn't be too bad). First, throw up your usual buffs and get your CR up as much as you can easily "just in case" (these are cold based rains, despite the fiery effect shown). Second, snare a mob, then root it. Next if you have a CR debuff, throw that on the mob. Now comes the fun part: get just a bit out of melee range (very close to melee for giant mobs like Wurms) and throw your rain. If the rain breaks the root, IMMEDIATELY run straight at and past the mob until he gets hit by the second wave of rain... the instant the second wave hits immediately turn around and run into/past the mob again so he gets hit by the third wave as well. If you time this right you will NOT be hit by the rains but the mob WILL. For me the timing is sort of natural, if you start just out of melee range and then run/reverse any time a "root breaks" or wave damage message shows. Just have to react fast. Of course if you mess up you will get rained on which can really ruin your hairstyle :P
 
Of course if the root DOESN'T break on the first wave, DON'T run at the mob. Wait until the second wave, and if that breaks root then run past the mob just one time. If the mob stays rooted the whole time, well that's best of all - but kind of rare.
 
I have done this repeatedly on different mobs in different places, and it works quite nicely. Using level 61 Winters Storm, I can almost take down mobs in Skyfire with 2 casts. That's 2550 damage for 500 mana on a good run with no resists. Good way to help get some pearl shards for your cleric's clicky stick, although quading isn't bad either :)
 
Of course, you will still get some resists... and you get whacked around a bit sometimes... but for the right targets (low blues without high CR) this is a very viable tactic.
 
I imagine that on blue servers you could do this to a mob with a tank on it, but on my PVP server it hits everyone around the mob, including other casters pets who love to beat on me after battles, so I wouldn't know. Bah to PVP servers! :) 
 
You can also do this without rooting at all if you can get the timing down to cast so that when it hits the mob is still a short distance in front of you (but close enough to run past before getting hit by the rain yourself).
 
It is also possible to get more creative and actually snare and optionally root MULTIPLE mobs if you can take the hits from them when you run past, but I found this very difficult to actually accomplish, and more dangerous. I'm sure there are some interesting things that can be done with multiple players if they all cast and run through the mobs at the same exact time, or if some players rain while another casts our AE root to root them in place again right after the rain hits. Experiment! :)
 
Anyway just wanted to at least share the easiest way to effectively use it solo, since I've never heard of this specific tactic before (it sounds so simple, but I was always discouraged by failures, resists, etc in the past and so I never figured it out until now, despite playing druids forever).
