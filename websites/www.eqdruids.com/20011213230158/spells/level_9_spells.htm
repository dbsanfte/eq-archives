Ignite:  

Effect:  Decrease Hit Points (HP) from 32 (L9) to 37 (L14)

Skill: Evocation
Allowable Targets: All

Range to Target: 200 feet

Resistance Check: Fire + 25

Mana Required: 30
Spell Duration: Instant
Casting Time: 2.10 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.50 seconds

Spell cast on you: You feel your skin ignite.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> skin ignites.

The next burn spell. It cost 30 mana and consistently does 33 pts (it increases very slightly as you gain in skill) of damage in combat So its slightly more mana efficient than the Burst of Fire spell, and it’s a whole lot more useful to cast one spell for 33 than it is to cast twice for 30, especially when you figure in the odds of interruption. Getting this spell makes Grasping Root a little more effective because you can burn a lot more in the limited time the monster is held in place.

Shield of Thistles:  

Effect:  Damage Shield from 5 (L9) to 6 (L12)

Skill: Abjuration
Allowable Targets: All

Range to Target: 100 feet

Resistance Check: Magic + 25

Mana Required: 40
Spell Duration: 6 seconds (L1) to 360 seconds (L60)
Duration Formula: 7
Casting Time: 2.50 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.25 seconds

Spell cast on you: You are surrounded by a thorny barrier.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> is surrounded by a thorny barrier.
Spell fades: The brambles fall away.

When combined with Thistlecoat, you dish out 6pts of damage every time a monster hits you. Through the course of combat in which you decide to go primarily melee instead of burn, a monster might hit you 10-15 times, so the accumulated damage of this spell is very nice. It costs 40 mana to cast, and if you dish out 50-100pts of damage during a combat with this spell then its more mana efficient than any other spell you have at this point. Unfortunately the duration of the spell will usually not even last the entire length of the combat. This spell is handy if you have more than one monster beating on you (bad news).  Each mob that hits you will be damaged.

This spell can also be cast on others, so it is an excellent spell to buff your primary tank with if you are grouped.  The duration and effectiveness of this spell increase as your skill increases. At 14th level it now does 7 damage combined with Thistlecoat and it easily lasts through combat. 

Purchase at:  Druid Guild

Treeform:

Effect:  Illusion: Tree
Effect:  Root
Effect:  Regeneration for between 720 (L9) to 1800 (L20) Hit Points (HP) in 360 ticks
Effect:  Decrease Fire Resist by 10 (L9)

Skill: Alteration
Casting Area Restriction: Outdoor
Allowable Targets: Self

Resistance Check: None

Mana Required: 30
Spell Duration: 360 ticks (36.0 minutes)
Duration Formula: 3
Casting Time: 4.0 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.50 seconds

Spell cast on you: You have taken root.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> turns into a tree.
Spell fades: You are no longer a tree.

This spell makes you a big tree - like a fun improved camo.  Lasts a long time and seems to fool griffs, hill giants, and a few of the other insta deaths.  Can meditate, attack, and cast while in treeform...Instantly cures poison on a casting...(except some high level poisons) also after a few levels you heal a total of 8 hps a tick.  Some monsters that are already agro can see thru treeform so be careful.  

Regeneration and Chloroplast stack with Treeform.

Be aware that dying in treeform can be a bad thing- your corpse remains in its arboreal state, and becomes very difficult to find if you do not have a loc (It's gotta be ONE of these trees...)

If a merchant won't sell to you, give Treeform a try if you don't have Wolf Form yet- if you can cast it in that zone, chances are good that it will make the merchant more friendly to you. 

Going Treeform:  You cannot go treeform in a zone without trees.  And you must be on a grassy area, standing on sand normally won't work.  You'll find areas that work.

Partial Camo: The nastier mobs CAN see through it...so /con them while in treeform to see about an individual mob, and don't assume you're safe just because you're in treeform. (if the mob cons indifferent, you are ok)

Regen:  You will see an increase in your hp regeneration
 
Fighting:  You can melee, cast and buff in treeform.  You just can't move.

Casting:  You can do just about anything EXCEPT Sow yourself.

Resistance:  You CAN be poisoned while in treeform, and would need to recast to cure it. 

Faction: Makes merchants indifferent to you...which is how it's camo effect is obtained.  So if your faction is worse than indifferent, you can improve your selling prices a bit.  If you're better than indifferent, you'll make it worse.  

Purchase at:  Druid Guild
