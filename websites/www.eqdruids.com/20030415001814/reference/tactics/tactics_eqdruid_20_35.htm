EQ Druids Guide to Levels 20 - 35

Many players find these middle levels to be very enjoyable times in their characters' lives.

During these levels, druids can find themselves "at the top of the world", so to speak. Our soloing ability increases as we advance in levels. Our grouping ability also increases, and because we can heal just as efficiently as a cleric during this period, druids are welcome in almost any group.

One of the goals of this guide will be to instruct a mid-level druid in some the many strategies available to the class during this time.

Another goal will be to provide information concerning hunting grounds and general tips to help reduce the time required to obtain level 35.

Levels 20 - 23

By now, you should have purchased all of your spells up to level 19. Your self-teleportation spells will be one of your greatest assets, as it will allow you to travel to another zone should the one you are currently in is too crowded.

One of the most useful tools a druid receives during these levels is Tracking. This skill allows a druid to locate targets within a zone quickly and more efficiently than before. It is also a useful method of locating rare spawns within a zone.

Tracking begins at level 20 for all druids, and with regular use, advances quickly until it caps at skill level of 125.

Note: You will want to train with a druid trainer when you achieve level 20 (make sure to put one of your training points into Tracking).

Note: The spell Lesser Succor is available at level 18. This spell is considered an emergency spell, and will transport you and your party to a safe spot within the zone. The advantage this spell has over the evacuation spells is that it does not remove your party from the zone, thus reducing downtime caused by travel. We recommend that you purchase it now but hold off on using it until later, unless you frequent dungeons often, or you have a large mana pool.

Recommended Level 18 Spells:
  • Lesser Succor
Recommended Zones:
  • South Karana
  • East Karana
  • Lake Rathe
  • Solusek's Eye (Sol A)
  • Lake of Ill Omen
  • West Karana

South Karana:

Hermit and lower-level aviaks. Stay away from the aviak avocets at this time, unless you have a strong group.
Gnoll spires, grouped.

East Karana:

Various spiders. Use caution when hunting the crag spiders, as they can poison you quickly. The poison is also particularly strong for it's level, so kite them, or have your Cure Poison spell ready.

Lake Rathe:

Aviaks can be hunted solo, or in a group. Use Levitate to assist in pulling should the aviaks be out of range. The tower is good for a group, as there are three spawns. There is also a single spawn near the stone skeleton tower.

Gnoll / undead camp. The camp is located near the South Karana zone. Gnolls populate the camp during the day, and if left alone, will be present during the evening also. The undead spawn in place of the gnolls at night if the gnolls are killed at the right time.

Use Harmony to pull singles, and pull close to the South Karana zone, as the entire camp is very social and can easily gang up on you and your party.

Solusek’s Eye (Sol A):

Window room.

Lake of Ill Omen:

Beginning with the targets closest to where the river enters the lake and work your way deeper towards the mill, the skeleton tower and finally the fort area.

West Karana:

Lars Mcmannus (Barbarian). Killing this NPC lowers barbarian factions, so only do it if you're willing to lose faction with Halas.

Levels 24 - 28

Level 24 is an exciting level for a druid. Not only do additional self-teleport spells become available, but the illusion spell Wolf Form is now at your command.

Wolf Form is generally a superior upgrade to Spirit of Wolf. It gives you a larger increase in movement speed than SoW. It also increases your attack rating, has a longer duration, provides Infravision, and changes your form to that of a wolf. It will also adjust your faction level with many NPCs and mobs.

Note: Wolf Form simply lowers some factions and raises others, so if you original faction standing is not very high, it will drop you to KOS, but if you are their ally in regular form, then you should probably be fine as a wolf.

For a list of areas where being in wolf form makes you KOS (Killed On Sight), click here.

At level 24, druids also receive an upgrade to their root spell in Ensnaring Roots, and an upgrade to the Swarm line of spells in Creeping Crud. These two spells allow you to rot difficult targets more easily than you could previously.

Druids also receive an upgrade to their charm spell in Charm Animals, which will allow you to charm higher-level animals. It will also generally last longer than the previous spell in the line.

Lake of Ill Omen:

Fort with a group, or solo wandering mobs.
Goblin cavern.

Iksar camps, giant fort, goblin camps, and wandering mobs.

Frontier Mountains:

Giant fort is recommended if you can find a group.
Wandering Burynai, brutes, and low-level giants can be soloed.

Everfrost Peaks:

Mammoths can be soloed. They are considered animals, so charm tactics and fear kiting can be utilized. Their tusks are also a nice source of money (they generally sell for around 8 platinum pieces each to a vendor).

South Karana:

Low to mid-level centaurs also are nice hunting.

Twilight Sea:

Elementals are nice targets if you use the Dismiss Summoned spell.

Qeynos Hills:

Millers outside of Surefall, as well as the guards. Only hunt these if you don’t mind working on raising faction at a later date.

Levels 29 - 33

Level 29 is considered another special level. Largely due to your new ability to teleport groups, or Group Gate (GG). This ability is highly sought after by players without the ability to teleport themselves, making it an effective source of income.

Druids receive several upgrades to their spells at this level. Most notably Greater Healing, Immolate, Combust, Shield of Brambles, Bramblecoat, and Ensnare.

Druids also receive the first of their evacuation spells in Succor: East. This spell differs from Lesser Succor in that it completely removes you and your party from the dungeon, and teleports the party to the druid rings in East Karana.

Should your party be hunting within the confines of a dungeon, you should always have this spell, or Lesser Succor ready. At these levels, both spells will normally use less than 20% of your mana pool, or approximately one bubble of mana.

The spell Ring of Misty will make selling and banking much easier. The druid rings are located close to the entrance to Rivervale, and is located in a safe spot within Misty Thicket. Because you will only have to zone once to enter town, using this spell and the Rivervale bank is highly convenient.

Note: You will want to train with a druid trainer when you achieve level 30 (make sure to put one of your training points into each category of Specialization).

Recommended Level 29 Spells:

  • Combust
  • Shield of Brambles
  • Extinguish Fatigue
  • Bramblecoat
  • Ensnare
  • Ring of Misty
  • Circle of Karana
  • Circle of Commons
  • Circle of Toxxulia
  • Circle of Butcher
  • Circle of Surefall
  • Succor: East
Recommended Zones:
  • The Overthere
  • Firona Vie
  • Timorous Deep
  • Frontier Mountains
  • Splitpaw

  • Rathe Mountains

The Overthere:

Many of the mobs will be even con or higher. Find groups until the mobs start to turn blue, then you can begin to solo as well as group. There are plenty of animals within this zone, so fear kiting and charm tactics are viable options.

Firiona Vie:

Wandering singles (drolvargs, spiders) are recommended.

Timorous Deep:

Golas can be hunted solo or grouped. Be sure to bind yourself in Oasis, as Bind Affinity does not work in this zone.

Frontier Mountains:

Remain grouped at the giant fort.

Splitpaw:

Continue to hunt inside near the zone, using the rotting method.

South Karana:

Aviak city (avocets and harriers) or continue at the Gnoll spires.

Rathe Mountains:

The various undead camps can be soloed using harmony. Giant skeletons near the Lake Rathe zone can also be hunted solo, or in a group. Cyclops are best tackled with a group, as they will be highly magic resistant at these levels. Hill giants are best tackled with a group.

Levels 34 - 35

For many soloing druids, level 34 is a god-send. It is at this level that druids can begin to Quad Kite effectively, using the spell Lightning Strike.

For more information on Quad Kiting, read this guide to Quad Kiting.

Druids receive several outstanding spells at level 34.

Strength of Stone is a much needed upgrade to Strength of Earth (level 9). Regeneration helps to significantly reduce downtime, by increasing the natural hitpoints regeneration of you or your party, instead of using healing spells. Drones of Doom is a nice upgrade to Creeping Crud. Greater Wolf Form is a nice upgrade to Wolf Form. Beguile Animals is a nice upgrade to Charm Animals, allowing you to charm higher-level animals.

A new batch of teleportation spells becomes available at this level as well. New ring spells to Velious allows you to get to those areas much more quickly. Additional group gates and another succor spell are also available.

As you continue to advance in level, you will begin to see a greater number of mobs that cast spells. It is at this stage that resistances and the ability to stun become important hunting tools.

Two spells become important when hunting spell casting mobs:

Fury of Air is an extremely useful spell when hunting casters. As with the previous wind spells, it has a stun component which will allow you to interrupt a mob's spell casting. However, due to the stun component combined with the damage component, it does get resisted more often.

Another useful spell against casters is Endure Magic. This will increase your natural resistance against magical spells by 20 points.

Recommended Level 34 Spells:
  • Strength of Stone
  • Endure Magic
  • Regeneration
  • Lightning Strike
  • Drones of Doom
  • Greater Wolf Form
  • Beguile Animals
  • Fury of Air
  • Ring of Iceclad
  • Ring of Great Divide
  • Circle of Iceclad
  • Circle of Feerott
  • Circle of Lavastorm
  • Circle of Ro
  • Circle of Combines
  • Circle of Steamfont
  • Succor: Butcher
Recommended Zones:
  • Several of the zones listed as 29 - 33 will support a druid well into level 40 and beyond.
  • In addition, because Velious zones are now easier to get to, Iceclad Ocean is also recommended.

Iceclad Ocean:

When you get Lightning Strike, you can quad kite dire wolves (mana intensive, usually not doable if you have low wisdom).

Rathe Mountains:

You will be more effective against hill giants in a group. Cyclops can now be soloed without too much difficulty.

Timorous Deep:

Continue to hunt golas. You should be able to solo them relatively easily.

The Overthere:

Sarnaks, cats, succulents, cockatrices, and rhinos can support a druid easily till level 45.
