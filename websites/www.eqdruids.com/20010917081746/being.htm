First, let me thank everyone for all the feedback and messages in world..
Its been a lot of fun..
So I decided to give another little tactic that I use called being picky..  
I've talked with a bunch
of other druids in the higher circles and apparently this tactics here is
simple, seemingly obvious
and yet MAYBE Unique!  I have never EVER seen another person use this
technique so enjoy.
Remember, its almost so obvious of a technique that that is the reason
itself why people are not
seen doing it...

My example will be in the Rathe Mountains in Cyclops heaven!  This works in
ALL outdoor zones
whether solo or grouped!  If your grouped and are taking on big guys
remember to have your party
very far away so that the, " Ahh Damn him/her!" effect will happen..

SCENARIO:

I don't feel like waiting before I draw this sweet lemon yellow Cyclops my
way!  He has a buddy though
and I know that if I draw one that the other will come after me as well! 
So, I select the one I DO *NOT*
want and commence snaring the one eye!  Ok!  Yes it fails and I have to get
distance, but I love a good
rush in game and sometimes it takes two Cyclops bearing down on me before I
feel excited!

Anyway, the one that I don't want has been snared.  The one that I DO want
is now in pursuit at full speed.
I hope you scouted the area before hand, because you need to ignore the one
running after you and get
enough distance between YOU and the one you SNARED so that it says to
itself, "Ahhh Damn him/her" and
goes back to where it was hanging out..

You may have test this a few times to actually find the distance needed
before a creatures says, "Ahhh Damn him/her".
Once you find this. the technique really becomes something that makes you
feel much more.. Egotistical..

NOW, DO NOT forget that the other creature is still ticked off at you.. 
When you go in range, EXPECT to be
attacked and this range is quite large, so I recommend medding to full right
where the first creature falls!
BE ON GUARD!

This technique works great in the outdoor zones and is a complete failure
indoors..

Well.. There it is, both obvious and from what I have seen, VIRTUALLY
unique..
This is great with groups because you can always be picky about exactly what
you want.  This is also a
perfect lure method for Aviak city in those times that two avocets pop up on
the ramp..

A variation of this is simply by words..  If you run in the path of
something that jumps in on the fight, this is
also a good way to remove it from the action.

Snare is sweet..
