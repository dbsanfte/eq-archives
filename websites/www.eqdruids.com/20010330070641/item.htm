Druids are priests of nature.  According to Verant the primary stats for a druid are STA and WIS.  With some skills in most areas (2nd/3rd best DD, tied for 2nd best healer) and the ability to teleport, they are one of the most popular classes to play, accounting for about 20% of the characters on most servers.

As a WIS based caster, the order or importance for statistic value I feel is WIS/AC/STR/STA/CHA/AGI/DEX/INT

There is a perceived difference in performance based on statistics reaching certain levels. The best known level is 75, if you have a statistic at lower than 75, you are taking a penalty in that field. The next lvl seems to be around the 100 mark, some say the 120 mark, and then somewhere between 150 and 200. I would imagine there is an additional benefit to hitting the 200 mark in any category(or there ‘should’ be. Silly Verant and their VisionTM).

WIS affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT

AC to get hit less often and for less damage

STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

 

For the last 2 stats; DEX is how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows: and is really only useful for a druid that has a weapon the processes, not that many of them available, and INT directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights.
