Gateway computers teams up with EQ

Off the CNN website thought folks would like to see the EQ Allies :) :
Gateway enters "EverQuest" world
 

By David Becker
Staff Writer, CNET News.com
January 22, 2002, 12:05 PM PT
 

Gateway has found a new ally in its quest to convince consumers to upgrade their PCs: a half-man, half-tiger beast that wields a mean sword.

The struggling PC maker announced Tuesday a partnership with Sony Online Entertainment and chipmaker Intel to sell PCs optimized for the Sony online game "EverQuest."

Under the promotion, customers who buy a Gateway PC with an Intel Pentium 4 processor and one of two versions of packaged "EverQuest" software will receive several months of free access to the game's online service. "EverQuest" players pay monthly subscription fees of about $10 to access the game's online world.

 

The promotion makes more than marketing sense, as the newest version of the fantasy role-playing game is one of the most resource-intensive pieces of commercial software yet released.

For "EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin" to run at its peak performance, Sony recommends a PC with at least 512MB of memory, a Pentium III chip and a graphics card with 32MB of memory. To reach those standards, Gateway customers must pay more than $1,000 for a custom-configured PC.

"With technologies continuing to expand and gamers demanding more out of their computer systems, we have teamed up with two leaders in the computer industry to deliver a system that answers all of these demands and more," said Scott McDaniel, vice president of marketing for Sony Online Entertainment.

"EverQuest" is considered one of the most successful online games to date, with more than 400,000 paying subscribers currently enrolled.

News Announcement

GuildPortal Grows, Makes Plans
 
CONTACT: Sandra Lewis, Lewis Development, support@guildportal.com
 
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
 
(11/27/2001 Denver, Colorado) – Today, GuildPortal, an online community host for players of Massively Multiplayer Online Games, announced the nearing of 300 hosted guilds and player associations supporting over 2200 users.
 
“This shows the viability of the underlying GuildPortal engine,” said Aaron Lewis, developer of the GuildPortal service.  “The performance has stayed consistent througout the increase in the number of users concurrently accessing the site, keeping each guild’s portal at least as fast as most single-guild hosted solutions.”
 
The service offers forums, voting, events scheduling/notification, member rosters, news feeds specific to the game a guild plays, chat and an integrated e-mail service.  Boasting over 30 different ways to customize the appearance of a guild’s page, GuildPortal claims to be at the top of their game – and yours.  “It’s not just fluff, either.  If you go check out the options for customization on forum solutions, you’ll see that most if it is filler.  It exists solely for the purpose of having something to list in the bi-weekly release notes.  GuildPortal really offers you a full solution,” said Lewis.
 
What’s on the horizon?  “Right now, GuildPortal is working on putting together a package of Web Services based on the .NET platform.  Once that’s done, we hope to be able to work out arrangements with game publishers so that their systems can talk to ours in near real-time.  For example, if you create a guild in, say, EverQuest, our web services could accept information from Verant’s servers, automatically creating your guild’s web presence for you.  Now, we haven’t spoken to any game publishers yet about this, as we want to get a working framework in place before we do.  But it’s something we’re really very excited about.”
 
Currently, GuildPortal offers free community hosting for EverQuest, Dark Age of Camelot, and Anarchy Online.  It is located at http://www.guildportal.com
