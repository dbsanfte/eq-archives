Weapon Classes

RUSTY
Rusty weapons are the most common weapons among low level characters in the
game. Most level 1-8 characters will be wielding some form of rusty or worn
weapon. So what is the difference between these and their steel(normal)
counterparts? In general rusty weapons are slower, heavier, and they do
less damage.  Rusty weapons are not generally sold in stores. Shopkeepers
often have rusty weapons in their inventory because they are sold by
players to the NPC. There are a few shopkeepers however who actually stock
rusty weapons. This doesn't matter much for a pure casting type. A wizard
for example will probably not be depending on his melee skills much after
level 10 or so and a rusty weapon may be all that they ever need. A priest
type class will have to weigh the benefits of new spells and a better
weapon. A melee class should put a better weapon as a top priority. If you
happen to run across a bronze weapon then this might be wise to use in the
interim. However as soon as you get the money a melee class should invest
in a store bought weapon. Rusty weapons can also be used by a blacksmith to
create tarnished weapons.

TARNISHED
Tarnished weapons can be created by young blacksmiths. Tarnished weapons
are made by combining a sharpening stone and a metal weapon in a forge.
Tarnished weapons are in between rusty weapons and bronze weapons in
quality. In general they are 2 points faster than the rusty version of the
same weapon. One rule I have found effective when creating tarnished
weapons is that the faster weapons are more effective than the slower ones.
For example a rusty longsword has a damage factor of 5 and a delay of 35
where a rusty short sword has a damage of 4 and a delay of 28. Both have a
damage:delay ratio of 7. A tarnished short sword has a damage of 4 and a
delay of 26(a 6.5 ratio) where a tarnished longsword has a damage of 5 and
a delay of 33 a 6.6 ratio. That extra .1 might not sound like much but it
makes a difference. More importantly a fast weapon will allow you to get
the most out of your damage bonuses as well as get in more swings, and thus
more chances to hit.

BRONZE
Bronze weapons are a decent intermediate weapon in the game. Bronze weapons
are better than rusty weapons but pale in comparison to steel, fine steel
or combine weapons.

STEEL
Normal(steel) weapons are the base weapon type in the game. This is the
highest quality of weapons that is actually sold by shopkeepers. It is non
magical in nature.

FINE STEEL
Fine steel weapons can be found off many level 20-30 monsters in the game.
Fine steel weapons are not magical and cannot hit magical monsters. They
are however just as good as combine weapons in every other aspect. They are
as fast and as light as combines.

COMBINE
Combine weapons are the only magical weapons in the game that you can find
at stores. They are sold at gypsy camps throughout Norrath. A few zones
worth noting that have gypsy camps are lavastorm, oasis and north karana.
These weapons are generally slightly faster than their steel counterparts
(usually a point or two quicker), they are generally a bit lighter and also
have the added benefit of being able to hit monsters which require magic
weapons to
damage.

WELL BALANCED
Well balanced weapons are a currently the most powerful magical weapons
that you can buy. They are stronger and more powerful than combine weapons
and cost a great deal more. You can find these weapons in the tunnel that
goes between East Commonlands and North Ro. To give an example of what to
expect the well balanced 2 handed axe is damage 16 and delay 48 for a price
of nearly 600 pp.
