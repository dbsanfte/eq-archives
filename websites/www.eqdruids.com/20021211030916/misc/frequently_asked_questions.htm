What should I use at lower levels for a weapon? 1HS, 1HB or 2HB?

One hand weapons are the way to go in my opinion.  They are the ones you will normally use, so you can keep your offhand free for a shield, or magic item.

Now, you need to make a decision.  One hand blunt is the normal weapon of choice of most Druids.  And there is a greater selection of druid-usable one hand blunt items in the game.  But in their mighty wisdom, Verant made our Epic weapon a one hand slash scimitar (I won't guess what was going to make that happen! <grin>). 

So, if you are planning on your epic, I would suggest finding a one hand slash weapon that you can use, and practicing with it.  You don't want to try to learn one handed slash when you are in your 50's, trust me!

At what level do druids learn their teleporting spells?

The Self-Only teleport spells, or "rings", are first gained at level 19. The first set allow you to port to : North Karana, West Commonlands, Tox. Forest, Butcherblock Mountains, Surefall and Combines (Dreadlands). At level 24, you receive self only ports to: Steamfont Mountains, Lavastorm Mountains, The Feerrott, South Ro Desert. At Level 29, you receive the last self only port, to Misty Thicket. The group teleport spells, or "circles", are first gained at level 29. You receive them in the same order as the self only ports, with the addition of Circle of the Combines, (Dreadlands) and Circle of Iceclad Ocean at 34, and Wind of the North (Skyfire Mountains) and Circle of Great Divide at 39, along with Wind of the South (Emerald Jungle), also at 39.  And at 44 you receive Circle of Cobalt Scar.

Are druids a pure caster class?

No. A druid is part of the "Priest" line of classes. Other classes in the “priest line” are Shaman and Clerics. We use WIS as our mana base.  We are also able to wear leather based armors, which is a step up from the “Pure caster” type armor.

What is the difference between Evac and teleport spells?

Evac, or succor spells have a lower casting time, making them ideal for just that, evacuations. Because of this lower casting time, there is a small chance you will leave one of your party behind when you evacuate (which can be the caster also).

Does the druid's grasping roots line of spells work as well as the wizard/cleric root spells?

Generally, no. They tend to break a lot more often, and easily than the Wizard/Cleric/Enchanter "Root" spell. The reason for this, is because our root spells also have a damage component to them.  So when you cast the spell, there is a check against the root being resisted and also a check against the damage being resisted.  If either is resisted, the whole cast is resisted.

What Druid DoT spells stack with each other? What about spell stacking with other classes DoT, i.e.: Bards and Shaman.

Very recently, the game developers have introduced the ability for players to stack the same DoT on a monster.  This had the added (and unforseen) bonus of being able to stack all our DoT's on a monster, in addition to those of another player.  So now, ALL DoT's stack with each other, regardless of line.  So far, we have not had any reports of there being conflicts with stacking DoT's with other classes.  It has yet to be confirmed whether DoT's and items that cast said DoT will stack.

How important a spell is harmony?

Harmony is one of the best spells a druid has.  The ability to pull single pulls from a group of NPCs, without pulling all of them is an incredible benefit as a class. Learn to use the spell, practice with green mobs.  Knowing how to use Harmony is a strength that will benefit you for a long time. Remember though, Harmony is an outdoor only spell.

What is Kiting?

Kiting is a reference to using our Damage over Time spells to kill a monster.  The basic premise of kiting is you sow yourself.  Then snare the monster you want to kill.  Then cast a damage over time on the creature.  Keep running out of range from the monster while the DoT works.  When it is finished, cast again.

What is Quad-Kiting?

Quad-Kiting is the same method as regular Kiting, except you are utilizing a point-blank-area-effect (PBAoE) spell and kiting up to 4 monsters simultaneously.  To learn more about quad-kiting in general, you can read our guides in our library section, here.

What is Rotting?

Rotting, or Root-Dotting, involves rooting a monster, stacking DoT's upon it and waiting for the spells to take full effect.  This is a very effective method of taking down a single monster with a large amount of hit points (such as giants).  It also allows a great level of control while hunting, as the monster is not moving.  Done properly, you can gain slightly less, or equal the amount of experience as you would quad-kiting in the same amount of time.

How do I use pauses in my social scripts?

You may now enter pauses in social batches with the /pause command.  /pause takes an argument from 1 to 600 (600 = One minute), with the value being equal to the number of tenths of seconds (i.e. /pause 100 will yield a 10 second pause).

 What do the different terms on items mean?  No drop, lore, artifact etc?

When you inspect an item (right-click and hold on the item), there are often several different flags printed right below the name of the item:

MAGIC ITEM:  This means that the item is imbued with magic.  If this item is a weapon, it will have the ability to damage creatures that can only be harmed by magic. 

LORE:  This means that you may only have one of these items in your possession at any one time.  If you attempt to acquire another, you will not be able to pick the item up.  This will check your bank account. 

NODROP:  This means that the item cannot be traded to another player nor sold to a merchant.  However, you can hand the item to an NPC, which is necessary for many quests. 

NO RENT:  A no rent item will disappear from your inventory 30 minutes after logging out.

ARTIFACT: This means that the item is unique upon the server that you play, and that there will never be another one given out.  Artifacts are usually earned through participation in dynamic quests that occur in game regularly.  Artifacts however remain so rare that most people may never see one, much less own one, which is why they are called Artifacts of Great Power.

Item Size:  The size of any item will be listed (and for containers it will show what sized items it will hold).  This should make organizing your inventory a little easier. 

A Deity's name: If a deity's name is listed upon the item, it means that only followers of that deity may equip the item or use its effects. 

Weight Reduction:  If a percentage for weight reduction is listed on a container, that container will reduce the weight of any item placed into it by that percentage while it is in the container.

How long will my corpse last after I die?

 

Please note that all times are in real time, not game time:

Level 1 - 5:    30 minutes

Level 6+:       24 hours of online time, or 1 week of offline time, whichever comes first.

 *Note:  Empty corpses that are level 30 or above characters will remain in game for 3 hours.  It is best advised to get your corpse right away.  If that is not possible, remember that one-hour in game is the same as seven hours out of game.  Also, be advised that your corpse decay is still advancing if you leave your computer at the character select screen or play on another character on that same account

What is KEI?

 

KEI is short for Koadic's Endless Intellect, a level 60 Enchanter spell.  It's a group buff that lasts 2 hours and 30 minutes in real time (not game time).  It is, in essence, a combination of Enlightenment and Clarity 2, with a better regeneration than Clarity 2.  It increases your mana regeneration by 14 per tick.  It also increases your intelligence and wisdom by 25 points and increases your total mana pool by an additional 250.

How do you chat across servers?

 

Cross-server chatting is now possible.  This allows you to send tells either to a channel or directly to another character on another server.  To join a channel on another server simply add the server name to the beginning of the channel name and a dot between them.  For instance, /join nameless.auction will join you to the server-wide auction channel on The Nameless.  Another example, /join serverwide.eqdruids would join you to the global EQDruids chat channel.

You can also send direct tells by using a semicolon (;) at the start of the line instead of slash (/).  So to send a tell to me on The Nameless server you would type ;tell nameless.spiritual Hello!.  To send a tell to a default chat channel, you would type /1 Hello! where 1 is the chat channel number (if you have joined more than one channel).

How do I customize my UI?

 

In order to customize your User Interface, you must first download a skin or mod.  EQInterface is a good resource.  Next, create a folder in your EverQuest\uifiles directory.  Unzip the downloaded file into that directory then log into the game.  Use the /loadskin <name> where <name> is the name of the directory where you unzipped the file.  The screen will pause for a moment and will display your new custom UI.

How do I learn a language?

 

To learn a language, you must find a player who is proficient in that language and willing to teach you.  This player can teach you his language +1 skill level above his current skill level.  So, if he had a language skill of 80, he could teach you up to 81.  You can learn up to a skill level of 100 for any language.

 

First you must group with that player and each create a language macro.  Next, both of you must "spam" each other with the messages on that language macro until you have reached the skill limit.  Then you must zone in order for the skill level to "take effect".  Then repeat the process for any other languages you wish to learn.
