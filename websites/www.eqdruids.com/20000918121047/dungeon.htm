The idea is dungeon kiting, which you may find silly.  However, in the overcrowding of today's dungeon it is a very viable option.  I base dungeon kiting in Guk.  Lets say the ranger says, "ouch 3 dars inc" (level 40-43 mob that hits for 80+).  Then if our group had no enchanter, I could snare 2 of the dars, pull them away and let the rest of the group take out the 1.  This raises efficiency greatly, and I can damage the dars while kiting to do my part in the group.  One more time this is not a viable option is if you are the only healer.  Then your tanks will not appreciate you running off in battle and one of them (much, much, much, preferably a ranger) should kite them off.  The two keys to being able to cast on these is to find parts in the dungeon which force the mob to take a long way around.  Rectangular paths are best for this.  

Thank you for your time, Falius Wolfwalker 50th human druid of Bristlebane.
