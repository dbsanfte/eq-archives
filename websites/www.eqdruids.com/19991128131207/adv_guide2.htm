Advanced Guide to Grouping for Druids II

Rupert did a good job with his Advanced Guide to Grouping for Druids but
there are still a couple of things that a Druid should know at any grouping
level.

General:

One very important thing left out of the Guide to Grouping was the
importance of a leader.  Having one experienced person make the calls on
when to pull, when to evac and when to move is priceless in a good group.
If you join a pickup group ask the following questions (in no particular
order):

1) Who's the group leader?
2) Who do I assist?
3) What is everyone's role?
4) How are we looting tonight?

If you don't get decent answers to this initial set of questions...find
another group.  Trust me.  There are more questions you can ask, more
strategies and tactics you can confer about but if they can't answer these
questions without a lot of contention (or blank stares), move on.

During a fight, see if the group members are communicating.  Since it takes
a half dozen casts to get a snare on something, I tend to call out "snared"
when I finally land it.  The puller often calls out what he's swinging at
(or will be swinging at).  Casters report LOM and OOM and so forth.

/assist:

A good grouper learns the power of /assist and social keys.  I create a
/assist PullerName social key to assist the puller and /assist Tank1 and
/assist Tank2 as well.  Get everyone in the party to agree to assist a
designated person (generally the puller).

The Secondary tank can first take a few swings on extra mobs first to get
higher on their hate list before /assisting the primary.  This helps out the
casters and healers in the group (a little anyway).  NOTE: Don't do this if
the enchanter is doing an AoE mez as the first spell.

Crowd control folks can do what they do best...and without party members
breaking their control (by swinging on them) because all the other party
members are swinging on ONE mob.

NOTE:  I often wait a swing or two before hitting /assist.  Sometimes
/assist gives you the wrong mob if the puller is switching from what mob he
used to pull to the real target.  If I remember correctly, it also only
works if the target is in auto-attack mode.

Safety TIP: I used to only use a generic /assist hotkey (no name) after
selecting a party member but switched to using /assist <name> after
accidentally hitting the hotkey twice and doing something stupid to myself.

Primary Healer:

As suggested by Rupert, Greater Heal has it's drawbacks but if you are the
primary healer, keep it mem'd.  The regular 80 pts heal is just too weak to
do much for tanks IMHO.  With good crowd control you should be able to
G'Heal your tank and not get obliterated.

My rule of thumb for G'Heal vs Heal is I use the greater on the tanks when
they get to the 2.5 bubbles of health range.  I use heal on anyone under 2
bubbles.  This sounds kind of backwards but I've lost too many people
because G'Heal took a tick too long to get off.  YMMV.

The trick to G'Heal is the same as the trick with big nukes.  Timing.  Once
the tanks have the target mob aggro'd enough, it wont switch.  Often, if the
secondary tanks take a few swacks at the other mobs (enough to get one hit
anyway) to get on their hate list and they've been damage shielded, the
other mobs may not jump on you for one G'Heal.  And of course, if the
enchanter has mezzed everything, you can safely G'Heal to your heart's
content.

Safety TIP:  If you are the primary healer, you cannot also be the primary
evac person.  If you happen to be the only priest class in the group and you
don't have a wizard then decide which you are.  I've tried filling both
roles at one time and there's nothing more frustrating than hitting your
evac minimum and having to decide "Should I let XXX die or heal him and lose
evac capability".   Sooner or later you decide wrong.

Secondary Healer:

One thing that wasn't entirely clear: your job is to keep the primary healer
alive.  Often times you also pick up one of the casters to keep alive but
generally, your healing mana will be spent on the cleric keeping her alive
until the tanks can pull the aggro'd mobs off of her.

Primary Evac:

My primary evac strategy is to load up on agility and armor class at the
expense of wisdom.  Then the only thing I do is damage shields PRIOR to the
fight and snares once the mob is down to half.  Then I swing a lot and toss
the occasional DD or DOT to finish a mob or the heal to keep the cleric up.
Generally, I finish the fight with 80+ mana.

Safety TIP: The PRIOR to the fight should be common sense but hey...I used
to damage shield the puller on his way back in so it would last longer.  99%
of the time this is okay.  Don't do it as the evacer.  The 1% of the time
it's not okay is the same time you need to evac.  Never ever cast anything
at the start of the fight as Primary Evac.

Evacs generally happen in three circumstances:

1) Bad pull.  These are the easiest.  Puller calls "EVAC" you cast.
Everyone is full health so should survive the trip.  If the puller is
bright, he gives himself enough time to get back for the trip home.  If
not...

2) Runner.  Someone missed a snare, you're all beat up to some degree or
another and the group leader calls out "Evac".  This one is sometimes
iffy...which is why I like having AC, Agility and Dex over Wisdom as the
primary evac.

3) Train.  This happens at the worst possible time...one second you're okay
and the next you're in big trouble.  As above, you're likely to get hit and
this is when you earn your pay as primary evac.

Note:  I almost always use Succor Butcher once I get it.

Snaring or Rooting:

The way spells work right now, just snare.  Not root, not ensnare...snare.
It takes multiple casts to hit but it lasts 2 mins which generally is longer
than the fight will last.  Do NOT let other casters root.

One thing you can do is once the mob turns to run (and just stands there) is
to have the tanks switch to another mob.  I don't know how many times I've
seen tanks and wizards keep hitting/nuking some mob that could easily be
DoT'd, resnared and ignored.

Spells:

One spell not on Rupert's list that I keep up all the time is Gate.  It's my
personal escape for when the zone crashes and I reappear alone or I fall
into a pit or whatever.  It's also useful for when you've been playing
healer, are LOM and the primary evacer just got obliterated by 3 ghoul
wizards chain casting on her.

Well there are more tips so I'm sure someone will come out with a Advanced
Guide to Grouping for Druids III.  There are other high level group tactics
when hunting certain critters or when equipped with certain items.

Good Hunting!
