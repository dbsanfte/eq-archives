Hey just want to share with you a really effective technique I've found that costs almost no mana, and group members take no hits... I call it snare and scare, I don't think you really have a complete description of it, and no doubt u know of it somewhat...
This tactic works with almost ANY group, works best with tanks, wizards aren't really that great for it. This is my own technique that I think is the fastest possible way to level (without being helped by others ;)
 
Step 1: Preparation
Find 1 or 2 other group members. A druid works well, but a tank or magician/necro/enchanter works best with a pet.
Spells I have memmed: Snare, Dot, Ignite, shield of thorns, panic animal, SoW
 
Step 2: Obtain a target
its best to be in the karana's I find for this. I hunt mainly in North Karana, fighting gorge hounds, griffawns, and dark stalkers. Note these are all animals, you don't want to really fight spiders or beetles or anything insect like because they are NOT considered animals.
 
Step 3: Snare
Snare the animal from a distance, I snare first because it gives me a chance to re cast in case the snare is resisted. Dotting can sometimes cause an animal to Lock on to you, so I usually snare first.
 
Step 4:DoT
This is optional. I only really dot if its a yellow or harder. I dot it right after a snare, and its running towards me and my group member.
 
Step 5: Scare
your group member should intercept the animal with their pet or themselves depending on the class, and melee it while you cast Panic animal on the creature. this spell isn't resisted too often, but resisted none the less. If it is resisted, simply re cast. The spell costs 10 mana, and casts in no time.
 
Step 6: KILL KILL KILL
Once its running away, the animal wont attack you or your group members until the panic animal wears off. simply Melee it down to nothing, casting the panic animal spell 2-4 more times.
 
Side Notes:
 
Mana - Mana is VERY efficiently used in this. In the best case scenario, you snare the animal for a cost of 15 points, and then scare it for 10, and usually scare it again for another 10. That's only 35 units of mana, the cost is just 5 more than ignite, but you can do so much more damage through melee.
 
Health - Health is again conserved. I find I can fight yellows with ease, and have the tank sustain usually less than half a bubble of dmg, or less!
 
Time - Doing this technique means you maybe have to med ever 3 - 6 kills just to recoupe.  Your members will be amazed at how many yellows you can take down while burning very little mana, and almost no dmg to your tanks.
 
Enchanters - I have had the pleasure to be grouped with an enchanter once so far, and found I had to modify my tactic a tad with them. once I snare the animal, the enchanter casts his magic resistance reducing buff on the target as well. This makes the target less easily resist your panic animal spell. This is very useful. I also let the animal hit the enchanter once or twice so his summoned animation will attack the target once I cast panic on it.
 
Multiple targets - often multiple monsters may join in on the fight. This can often be a problem. I find the best thing to do is continue on as your doing, and snare the animal which joined in, then scare it as well. Then I might dot the primary animal were fighting to help the tanks kill it faster, so they can deal with the 2nd animal.
If 3 or more join in, its often time to make good use of sow and run for the zone =) As a druid, I often scare off as many as I can before making a dash for the zone.
 
Bad Scenario - once or twice while doing this, in the heat of battle, we notice the animal is panicked and running towards the Cyclops 100ft away! In this case, we keep meleeing it till the panic wears off, then move backwards while meleeing it. I then dot the animal to help the tanks and join the fight. I DO NOT cast panic again, cause the animal often runs in the same direction(in this case towards the Cyclops)
