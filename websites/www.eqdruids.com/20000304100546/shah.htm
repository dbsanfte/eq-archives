Grouping with a Druid

Twylight : I have kept this guide exactly how its was written cause it is good info and it needed a home.

This was written by Shahlateah, Half-Elf Druidess of the 27th Circle.  It is designed to inform other classes of the benefit that may be derived from having a Druid in the group, as well as give suggestions to other group members on how to expect a druid to react in certain situations  Information herein can be used with Shaman classes as well and is mostly set down for OUTDOORS, but some tactics can sill be used indoors.    I also disregard level in this because the Snailing technique does NOT require SoW.

Traveling:

SOW - Spirit of the Wolf
Although a few other classes obtain similar abilities, only the druid is true to this skill.  We have all seen that Bards are far superior with their speed song in later levels, yet there comes many problems associated with songs where a Druid remains master of Speed along with the Shaman.  In many fights, or in traveling, a bard can easily slip out of range and the effect of the song is lost.    If at the same time someone had a SoW cast on them the effect has just been nulled out by the bard going out of range also.  This has been tested and confirmed.    So if you have both a Druid and a Bard in a group, make the initial decision of SoW for when you a hunting, and Bard Speed for travel.

Tips on how to NOT annoy a Druid or Shaman with SoW requests:
1)  IF your not grouped with a DRU/SHM don't constantly go up to one asking for SoW, even if you are donating, it really gets old and don't be surprised to suddenly hear, " I really need to save my mana for my group."

2) IF your fighting RIGHT at a ZONE point, don't ask for as soon as it runs out.  If a time comes up when you really have to zone, SoW wont get you there any quicker right at the zone point.  There is nothing more annoying than being OOM, 20 feet from the zone point with half health and three people give a 'Need SoW' tell.

Rings and Circles - Teleport
With these spells a Druid is able to move almost instantaneously to another continent, and later with the Circle spells is able to teleport a full group as well.  It has been clearly stated that simple possession of this skill does NOT turn a druid into a ferry service for travel.  Requesting teleport should ONLY be in an emergency situation or if you are already grouped with someone going a certain direction.

Succor - Flee
Big BIG trouble has arrived and everyone is almost completely drained.  With luck and enough mana, as druid can Whisk the whole party away to another zone.

Healing:

Druids in combination with the other skills also have many restorative skills.  Healing, curing, and resistance's are part of the ensemble.   Lets point out some things.  First off, let me make a point for all healers.  At the end of a battle, and people have about a bubble of health gone, leave them alone, your time is better off getting your health back while you meditate.   Here is the scenario:
Each player has 1 bubble of health down.  A healer has 1/2 of his mana and heals everyone to full.  Now the healer is OOM and is useless for a while for heals..  A battle immediately starts.  One person gets POUNDED big time!  Healer is OOM and everyone else is full health and NO ONE can get the MOB off this guy before it is killed.  Good-bye party member, you are now dust.     Let those people regain their health while you are on your way to full mana. It makes complete sense!  Its much better to have 6 people regenerating 5 points a round(30 points) then to have one healer completely drained and getting only 5 points.
DON'T top people off, it increases YOUR downtime healers!

Curing and Endurance's can be very vital in a party when fighting certain creatures.  Also later on don't forget your cancel magic spell when fighting creatures who use Damage over time spells(ex.HEAT BLOOD)

Combat:
Where to even begin, I guess here.  I consider myself an excellent druid, which means I still need skill points to become a master and so forth.  In that light, take my confidence and seeming arrogance as instruction.
First off, a druid can be several things to a group.
1) An addition to melee damage and a person to take damage for a bit while things are brought back under control.
2) A healer for the group, or a backup healer if a cleric is grouped.
3) A Spell damage dealer.
4) A sacrificial group member.
5) A battle captain, or tide turner.
6) A fetcher, or lure person.

Dictionary:
Pegged:  Player is hated more than anyone else and anytime he gets close to the monster it will take off toward the pegged player.   One becomes pegged by using certain spells and especially in combinations. Also is pegged by doing more initial damage to a monster.
DoT  -  Damage over Time
BRB -  Be right back, Bathroom Break, or if running a creature it means you to a break and gather your wits because the monster is being taken out of range by the DRU/SHM.
INC -   This just means that a monster has been found and tagged.  IT DOES NOT MEAN GET UP in Druid terms! A monster is tagged so any other people luring should then CEASE hunting and return to the designated camp spot
ENGAGED - Druid luring is IN RANGE NOW.   GET UP and be aware of the battle plans.
RES -  This indicated that a spell is RESISTED.

I guess I'll hit each and then pop in with explanations of various Druidic battle tactics and why they are great.

1)  Partial Tank - Druids have mid range hit points.  They don't have a ton, but they do have enough that they can take quite a few beatings before they need help.  During this time the healers can focus their heals on the real tanks and they can then take over again.

2)  Druids healing spells are gained slower than a cleric but are just as valuable.  No offense intended to other healers and their methods but if your party members are getting hit and you are half health. You should be healing THEM and NOT yourself.   Keeping everyone else alive is your goal as a healer, as the melee and casters of the group the others are to keep you free for healing.  Make a hot key that will notify your group that you are unable to heal, or are engaged.  Mine says simply, " ENGAGED....  ENGAGED...." and the people I group with know that I will not heal myself until THEY are out of danger.    If they see me hit the hot key one time, it means that I am in range of them AND I have a creature on me.  If its in the middle of a battle and I'm being pounded and can't heal, I let out several ENGAGED MSG's and that ALWAYS pulls a TANK, or another group member to my aide.  Let your group know what your messages are for, and get to know their signals because they are vital. As I already said about healers not topping off your health, don't ask or expect a Druid to top your health off UNLESS you are a low hit point caster, and even then, if they don't top you off please don't ask because it's very likely they are more interested in obtaining mana at that moment more than spending it. TARGET OUT OF RANGE - DOH!  When you are in need of a heal, make SURE you are relatively close to your healer.  Sit down a couple of times and LISTEN to the a healer casting HEAL.. Its sound is distinct, and if you hear it and you are the most hurt, or running to a zone point, STOP for a second.. If your not too close to the zone and your health is low. You KNOW your going to die?  Hear that heal spell being cast? STOP.. It might be being cast on YOU and you might just make it.. If you go out of range, the healer still burned his mana and you still died.
Got a designated healer in the group while a monster has you pegged?  If they know their position in the group and are playing as a team, then TRUST the HEALER to heal you when you come in range, and give them time to get their spell off..

3)  Druids have fairly decent damage spells and can do considerably more damage than their melee abilities. IMO one of the best spells Druids have under their belt is their line of DoT spells..  There is nothing more pleasing to here everyone yell OOM, and everyone so low on health to make a run for zone then up comes, 'You gain party experience', a SO-and-SO died.  DoT's are deadly and carry guaranteed damage. Not to mention VERY intimidating in a duel...   Heh, don't duel a druid outside. Even one that cons green.  Druids can solo hard reds NO PROB. Another line of spells we have is Area effect which isn't all that great IMO except to draw the Mobs off others. WIND spells, Nice FULL damage spells that stun a creature for about a second or two.  They ALWAYS deal full damage when they hit and work well at ticking off a monster.  VERY good for Snailing that you will see later. This also interrupts casting and a druid should always use this on an offensive enemy caster when possible. Its also quite entertaining to witness a monster hop in the hair for no apparent reason.

4)  I M O, a druid is a member of the group that is SACRIFICEABLE if at all possible.  I've gotten to the point to where I want people I group with the know to save themselves before dying for me.  The effort they put in saving me is nice, but I would rather be the one to die because I know I have more of a chance of surviving than the average class.  Early on in my levels I obtain a spell called SNARE.  If I get in trouble the first thing I do is attempt a snare spell which will allow me to outrun a creature WITHOUT SoW.  Sometimes its a 50/50 deal, if Successful I live, if not I still may not die.. I've got GATE at level 5 and ROOT and WIND Stun, SoW and later CHEETAH.  My chances of survival are 5 to 10 times better when in RUN AWAY mode.   IF a Druid in your party follows this way and they tell you ZONE.. Do it then, its a whole lot easier for 1 Druid to regain a bubble of experience then to have 2 members die trying to save one.. Now again.. This SACRIFICE deal.. It's MY OPINION. Don't want to do it?   Don't, period.

5 & 6)  These two go hand in hand, A druid can be a highly quick and successful hunter in the outdoors.  Druids have long range DoT spells that really tick monsters off in order to lure.  They can lure out a good distance where a party waits in ambush, and where you can be free from Loose Caster cannons(Kill Stealers). With the hot keys and signals a druid can bring constant picked creatures to a party, fight half of the battle and immediately head back out to retrieve more prey.  Although your party is still camping somewhere else, it still does not cause the clutter of a camping group standing right at a spawn point. In these cases of campers, I personally have a tactic that I use called hit and run..  Its my favorite lure technique to use around people camping a spawn spot.  Its so very fun to hit my cheetah spell, zoom in to a creature that just spawned, whack it, gain some distance, and then do a quick burn so it pegs me.  They never know what happened considering Cheetah is probably SOW x 2.   You don't have to use cheetah but its really funny to hear someone camping a spawn to say you stole it.  I've had several times to reply with, "Yes, I did take that one because I was faster and it had not been attacked yet. You may consider befriending a Druid." 
Fight Moderation, very similar to an enchanter in this idea, a druid can snare, root, and cause creatures to focus attacks elsewhere.  Try letting a good druid be your leader, and especially one who will die for everyone.
Tide Turner, a druid has the ability to lure with mastery creatures in open spaces while the other members concentrate on other creatures.   This is a method calling running a monster.
EX 1.  Aviak City, South Karanas..  I lure 1 creature that is right next to another, both are very tough creatures and it would be better if the majority of the party only had to deal with one at a time.  Druid snares ONE while in range of the group and continues on with the other tagging behind him.   A successful division of the enemy has been made.  Success of BOTH enemy's death is now 100% with NO casualties.
EX 2.  Hey! We were meditating! Who brought that Cyclops by here!  A SoWd Druid can immediately tick the Cyclops off by snaring! Come on Cyclops, lets go for a walk while my group keeps preparing to ANNIHILATE you!!
EX 3.  Creatures friends popping in uninvited!   Same deal, Snare it! Draw it away. When the group is ready bring it back.

All examples are great crowd control!  Many more, that go with differing tactics. Tactics is now my topic..  Enjoy..

Combat Tactics:

Some people have various names for specific tactics, and my main one is Snailing with groups or solo and then baseball with only one other partner.  Every now and then when I see something deep red I know I can take, I will combine Snailing and kiting.

TACTIC 2:    S N A I L I N G

This tactic is BY FAR my favorite and MOST successful of ALL techniques for groups and solo, and I prefer this over Kiting.   When I snail a creature I always DoT the mob first to lure.  I take the creature to where I will be fighting it and immediately snare it.  I will NOT attack it until I snare it.   ONCE creature is Snared it is now considered TAGGED. Upon snare I back up and proceed to decimate.  In groups, do NOT use root in combination with snare, rooting a monster makes it berserk and once its free of root, it also breaks free of snare and it not a pretty sight.

Group Snailing:
DoT, Snare, then WIND!  YOUR PEGGED! Stay JUST in range of creature in order to keep him trailing you.. I usually run backwards in front of my party from right to left and then back again.  Upon a finished circle, RECAST SNARE.  Your party will ANNIHILATE the opponent! After enemy death you should be able to hit the hunt immediately right after.
Healers: Watch the pegged trailers health! Don't let them get too low!  The druid should be in range frequently and therefore SHOULD remain at least at 80% health..
TANKS:  Kind of nice to not take so much damage for a change, no?  Well this time around you are all back stab!    Follow the creature around and get in those back blows!
Casters:  Stay safe from the Deep Red mob your fighting?  Cast medium spells until the creature is half dead.  The druid will stay pegged and you will stay able to let off a big bang at the end.  You don't ALWAYS have to use your biggest NUKE!

Solo Snailing:
DoT your prey, draw to Camp, Snare, Lightning, go far enough away that you barely see the creature, face it, dump down in meditation.     Watch the creatures health bar while you MED, as soon as it moves down you KNOW your prey is now moving close to melee range. Hit 'I' twice so you don't lose your target and then veer off to the right or left..  This WILL take practice!
When you are far enough to cast another snare, do so, then either cast another DoT(if other has had enough time) OR cast a LIGHTNING or WIND spell..  Again, go out of range and med up until time to do again. This works a lot better than kiting IMO and takes less time and gives you opportunity to recover mana in the process.

BASEBALL!(Bear with me on the symbolism)
DeepReds with 2 PPL
Materials needed:  One Druid, One Caster, and one runway.
Druid      =  Pitcher
Caster     =  Hitter
Runway  =  Playing Field!
Creature  =  Ball

DoT Your prey!  Run it to the field and Snare it.
Its a slow ball to across home plate and the Hitter Swings for a base hit!
MEDIUM SPELLS! NO APOCALYPSE!
Hit a base hit, watch the ball start down the field and dump down into Meditation for the next pitch!

The pitcher then runs the ball back down the field where he either uses a big burn, or a DoT if the other has had time.  Then Snare again!

When the ball is really beat up the hitter can start swinging hard for a home run!

KEEP IT snared! DO NOT throw a pitch to the hitter with a fast ball!
He WILL strike out!
