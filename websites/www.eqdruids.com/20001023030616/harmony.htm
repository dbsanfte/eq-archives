Trelemyst's Guide to Harmony

Harmony is basically an area effect lull spell which works by decreasing the
radius of the NPCs aggro range.  In short, if you harmony a group of NPCs
and then attack one, the rest will not attack when they otherwise would
have.  It is very handy in that it allows you to pull NPCs in a controlled
fashion (one or two at a time), allowing you or your group to handle camps
that might have otherwise overwhelmed you at the first pull.  It is one of
the spells that make druids such effective solo artists in the game, and
nice for group work at tough camps.  There are a few surprises that people
may encounter based on spell mechanics, but if you are aware of how the
spell works you will find that the "resistances" other people talk about
don't really happen (NOTE - I have not tested this on reds, only greens,
blues, whites, and yellows).  Following are some hints based on the various
aspects of the spell.

Visual Effects:  There is a nice little effect, a white pulsating light that
radiates out from the waist of the affected NPCs. 
Hints:
 - It is NOT VISIBLE from the outer reaches of the spells range; in other
words, if you are standing as far away as you can to cast the spell, you
WILL NOT see the effect, and you won't know for sure which ones have been
harmonied.
 - Conversely, if you don't see the effect, it doesn't mean the NPC is not
harmonied.  Unless you get the "target out of range" message, you can assume
the target NPC is indeed harmonied.
 - If the NPCs are not attack on sight to you, stand near to them when you
cast, so you can see which ones you've nailed.  The spell is NOT considered
a hostile act by NPCs, so they will not attack you even if the spell didn't
take hold.

Duration:  (Tested by casting on self)  A bit over a minute.  Not sure if it
increases with levels. 
Hints:
 - You have a comfortable window in which you can get off about 3 casts of
harmony before you ought to get down to business. 
 - Alternatively, if you only have to cast it once, you can get 45 seconds
of meditation time for that last little bit of mana.
 - When you pull your prey, PULL your prey.  In other words, drag it back
well away from the rest of the camp.  I've seen people harmony a camp, then
fight the NPC too close to the rest.  Harmony wears off, and now they've got
a full camp thumping their foolish little heads.

Range: Seems to be about 30 or 40 yards. 
Hint:
 - In order to be sure you're getting everything you think you're getting,
target the Middle NPC.
 - You will NOT see the visual effect at the max range.

Spell Radius: Seems to be about 5-8 yards. 
Hints:
 - If you're not sure, or you can't see the effect, etc, be safe and cast it
on multiple NPCs rather than trust the area effect.  One of the spell
failures is when people overestimate the radius of the area effect.
 - If it's something that does not attack you on sight (aviaks, for
instance), you can go stand right up with them, harmony one towards the
middle, and see if there are any you missed by directly observing the visual
effect up close.

Aggro Radius:  Harmony does ~not~ decrease the aggro radius to zero.  In my
testing, it seems to be about one arm-length.  In other words, if 2 NPCs are
standing close enough to each other so that one could touch the other with
an outstretched arm, they are STILL within each other's aggro radius after
reduction by harmony.  In short, if they're standing right next to each
other, you can't pull one without pulling the other.
Hints:
 - If two NPCs are standing right next to each other, you won't be able to
pull them individually.  You cast harmony, see the spell effect on both
NPCs, pull one...and they both come running at you.  Why?  The spell hasn't
decreased the aggro radius enough so they don't overlap.  You ~can~ pull
other NPCs away from these two singly, but those two are a pair.  How to
deal with a pair of high blues left as an exercise to the reader; there are
at least two good techniques.

The Whim of the Fates:  The spell obviously doesn't work on things that
spawn or wander through after you've cast it.
Hints:
 - As mentioned above, PULL when you pull, well back from the camp, and have
a relatively safe fighting spot picked out before you cast.
 - You remembered to keep spirit of the wolf freshened up, right?  And snare
your prey early in the battle, right?  Right?

Final hints:
 - If you're in a group, let them know how the basics of how the spell
works, most importantly to pull the prey back to a safe fighting are.
 - Beware of NPC casters who may be able to Cancel Magic on their buddies
 - It won't work if they're already aggro on your little druid butt, e.g.
chasing or attacking you.
