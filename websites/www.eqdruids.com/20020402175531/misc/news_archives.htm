Healing Spells updated 3/12/02
It was brought to my attention that I had an oops on the spells.  I was listing the information straight from the spell database, and in the case of our healing spells, that added a problem. 

For those that don't know, Druids have a 10% penalty on healing spells.  So if the database shows 500 hps healed, we actually only do 450 hps at a cast.

Anyways, I'm going to leave the base number from the spell database in there still, but under the description I've changed the actual healed hitpoints to the more approximate number.

Cazic Thule redone! 3/8/02
For those that hadn't heard Verant has completely redone the mobs in Cazic Thule.

The items that used to drop there, such as ravenscale armor and such have all been moved out into Feerot onto new mobs.

Gateway computers teams up with EQ

Gateway enters "EverQuest" world
 

By David Becker
Staff Writer, CNET News.com
January 22, 2002, 12:05 PM PT
 

Gateway has found a new ally in its quest to convince consumers to upgrade their PCs: a half-man, half-tiger beast that wields a mean sword.

The struggling PC maker announced Tuesday a partnership with Sony Online Entertainment and chipmaker Intel to sell PCs optimized for the Sony online game "EverQuest."

Under the promotion, customers who buy a Gateway PC with an Intel Pentium 4 processor and one of two versions of packaged "EverQuest" software will receive several months of free access to the game's online service. "EverQuest" players pay monthly subscription fees of about $10 to access the game's online world.

 

The promotion makes more than marketing sense, as the newest version of the fantasy role-playing game is one of the most resource-intensive pieces of commercial software yet released.

For "EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin" to run at its peak performance, Sony recommends a PC with at least 512MB of memory, a Pentium III chip and a graphics card with 32MB of memory. To reach those standards, Gateway customers must pay more than $1,000 for a custom-configured PC.

"With technologies continuing to expand and gamers demanding more out of their computer systems, we have teamed up with two leaders in the computer industry to deliver a system that answers all of these demands and more," said Scott McDaniel, vice president of marketing for Sony Online Entertainment.

"EverQuest" is considered one of the most successful online games to date, with more than 400,000 paying subscribers currently enrolled.

New Section, Updates and misc. 11/17/01
I've added a new section.  It is the Frequently Asked Questions (F.A.Q.) section.  Take a look and let me know if there is something you think should be added.

Several people also asked for a way to donate towards the cost of operating this website.  I gave this some serious thought, and realized that if people wanted to help with the costs, I would be foolish to not let them! <grin>.  Some things I want to say first.  I will never make this site a pay only site (or even a partially pay site).  I will never have popup ads.  I will always have this site where anyone that wants, can come and find the information they are looking for, without a price.  But I am willing to take donations to its operation.  Please don't ever feel obligated to donate, this is purely for those that have asked, or wanted too.  You can donate as low as one dollar. Right now I can accept Paypal donations.  If people wants something other than Paypal, let me know.  You can go to the Support EQDruids page for the Paypal donation button.

I've also added a nice guide from Pelf to our reference section.  Check out his "Sharpening the Sword" guide for some great info on what to do the level before you ding, the level you ding, and level after you ding.

Beyond that, the majority of the structure of the site is done for now.  Between now and the release of Luclin, I'll be doing minor updates, and cleanup.  I'll also be making the site look a little prettier :)

I will keep updating the Hall of Fame and such areas as that, so don't think you can't submit your info, you can always do that!
