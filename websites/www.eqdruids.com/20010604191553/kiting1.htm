I didn't find a tactic description using root and dot though.  I found it fairly efficient for taking down Broon and crag spiders at 24th and higher level.  I'll put a tactic description here in case you'd like to include it.
 
Since DOTs do less damage when the creature is moving kiting with DOTs has become much less mana efficient, almost exactly as mana efficient as direct damage kiting.  Another option to solo those hard hitting creatures you don't want to close to combat with is to use root and dot as a tactic.  I've found it to work well in East Karana on Broon and crag spiders.  Broon hits hard enough that I'd rather not close to combat with him for long, and the spider poison is good to avoid too, doing perhaps 200 points over one minute.  Here's the root and dot tactic as I use it.
 
I use Ensnaring Roots and Creeping Crud for this tactic.   We get them both at 24th level, so this tactic only becomes effective at that level.  I get close to the creature, and start with root.  If the first root holds I put the dot on him and sit and meditate.  If the root holds a whole minute I recast the dot right at the end of the minute.  Our swarm line dots have 13 6 second ticks of damage so the best time to reapply the dot is at one minute seven seconds after the first started.  When I hear him coming I stand up and start the root again, casting again until it holds.  Then I back up out of combat range and sit and med again.  I stand up to recast the dot when necessary.
 
To do this perfectly keep track of two times, time since the dot was applied and time since the root was applied.  Stand up at the one minute mark of each to be ready when you need to recast.  An important buff during this tactic especially in East Karana is staying in wolf form because otherwise there are plenty of lions that will still try to take advantage of a sitting person there.
 
The real efficiency from this tactic is getting the med time in DURING the combat.  The Rank 2 spell Grasping Roots doesn't last long enough for this tactic to be effective because between resists and it possibly breaking every 10 seconds it doesn't allow time to med.  Ensnaring Roots does 35 points of damage for the 60 mana, but the real damage comes from the full damage DOT Creeping Crud, 198 damage for 100 mana.  Ensnaring Roots lasts 0, 30, 60, 90, or 120 seconds.  If the spells is resisted or the Root damage breaks the root immediately after it takes hold the spell lasts 0 seconds.  Otherwise the creature has about a 25% chance to break free with each additional resist roll every 30 seconds.
