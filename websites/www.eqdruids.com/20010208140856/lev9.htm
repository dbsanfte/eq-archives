Some choice zones:
Misty Thicket, Field of Bones

At level 9 you get a good set of spells.  If you have been a cash saving druid you should have no problem purchasing all the 3rd circle spells.  During this time it is helpful to have a same level or one below melee based class to fight with...you really aren't a fully soloable class until level 14 and the purchase of some mighty expensive spells.

The way to experience is low down time on risky and dangerous blues....find a ranger and target some specific mobs that give treasure and are 'risky blues' or 'dangerous blue'...Upper hand blues seem to give significantly less experience.  At about level 10 (especially if you have Runed staff or JaggedPine staff) you can beat up on wisps for great experience and cash.  A small group of druids, shamans, or wizards can usually burn one to death by level 10 with a full group mana dump.

Note that you can solo at this level by taking risky blues, near a zone with the Shield of Thistle, Melee and Burn Method if you wish.  Many times this is safer as other characters are a bit clueless on grouping and might get your tree hugging butt killed.   Just make sure you run for the zone if you are getting whooped and NOT die.   SoT doesn't last long so don't expect to go the full length of a fight if you are the Whiff Master.

This is a confusing time...you feel like melee class with spells...rest assured in the late teens and early 20's - you are a CASTER.  You can hit some monsters but in NO WAY can you tank or be bait.  Always let the tanks start combat then step in and take some whacks...the 18-19 points that you can dish out with a RTS is very helpful...but will not kill a level 22 mob before it can smear you in melee.
