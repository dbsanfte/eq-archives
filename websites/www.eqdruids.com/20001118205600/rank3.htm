Level 9 Spells are available in your guildhall for about 4-5 plat each.

1) Ignite. The next burn spell. It cost 30 mana and consistently does 33 pts (it increases very slightly as you gain in skill) of damage in combat So its slightly more mana efficient than the Burst of Fire spell, and it’s a whole lot more useful to cast one spell for 33 than it is to cast twice for 30, especially when you figure in the odds of interruption. Getting this spell makes Grasping Root a little more effective because you can burn a lot more in the limited time the monster is held in place.   Very, very useful spell.

2) Light Healing. This replaced Minor Healing for me. I don’t think its any more mana-efficient, but it sure is a lot faster. It also makes you a bit more useful in groups as you can now heal party members somewhat effectively during combat.

3) Thistlecoat. Increases your AC by 5 pts plus it does 1pt of damage to any monster that strikes you. The good thing about it is that it combines very will with Skin Like Wood and Shield of Thistles. This spell lasts a long time, and like all buff spells I only memorize it when I need to recast it. Keep this spell up - 1 damage adds up over time.

4) Firefist. Another long lasting buff spell. It increases your chance to hit during melee, which means less whiffs, which is nice 8-). Its pretty expensive mana-wise.  Of our "long-lasting" buff spells, this one has the shortest duration. Its lasts roughly half as long as Skin Like Wood Although its somewhat annoying to have to stop and recast it when your other buff spells are still going strong, it is useful to keep track of about how long you have till all your buff spells wear out (assuming you cast them all at once). It DOES have a noticeable effect. I definitely hit more often for maximum damage than when I do not buff with this spell. If I feel there is a strong likelihood of engaging in melee combat I always cast this spell.  Combined with SoE it makes you a level or so better in melee.  Self only and does not help hit magic only creatures

5) Shield of Thistles.  When combined with Thistlecoat, you dish out 6pts of damage every time a monster hits you. Through the course of combat in which you decide to go primarily melee instead of burn, a monster might hit you 10-15 times, so the accumulated damage of this spell is very nice. It costs 40 mana to cast, and if you dish out 50-100pts of damage during a combat with this spell then its more mana efficient than any other spell you have at this point. Unfortunately the duration of the spell will usually not even last the entire length of the combat. I smile however when a monster dings me for 1 pt of damage and I give him 6pts in return, very nice 8-). I don’t bother using it if I determine that I am going to do a mana burn to take out a monster, as those combats are usually much shorter in duration so you don’t’ get as much help from the Shield of Thistles. Also, if you have more than one monster beating on you (bad news), the Shield gets even better damage/mana ratios. This spell can also be cast on others, so it is an excellent spell to buff your primary tank with if you are grouped.  The duration and effectiveness of this spell increase as your skill increases. At 14th level it now does 7 damage combined with Thistlecoat and it easily lasts through combat. I will even sometimes sit and meditate for a few seconds to recover some of the mana cost by casting the spell before I engage in combat. Still should be considered a combat-duration buff spell however. There is no way you can effectively keep this spell active all the time. Also as you start fighting 10th-20th level monsters its just not feasible for you to melee with them. Getting hit for 40pts of damage just so you can prick them for 7 pts isn't a good trade. However, it remains an excellent spell to buff tanks and pets with prior to combat. Cast it on tanks, cast on yourself, cast on friends.   Damage Shields is one of the reasons you are loved.  Incredibly mana efficient and can help solo and group take higher level monsters.  This line of spells becomes more useful in a solo situation in the high 30s to 50+.

6) Strength of Earth. A buff spell that increases your STR by 9 pts (15 at level 15). I use it though because it does allow me to carry more stuff without getting encumbered. It lasts a good long time so only memorize it when you need it.  It also increases your ATK which is good for connecting with your weapon.

7) Endure Cold. All I can say about this spell is that it is a long lasting buff, so you can cast it in your buff phase. This spell is effective against shamans and wizards.

8) Invisibility to Animals. Makes you invisible to some garden variety low level animals - will not work on high level animals.

9) Treeform. This spell makes you a big tree - like a fun improved camo.  Lasts a long time and seems to fool griffs, hill giants, and a few of the other insta deaths.  Can meditate, attack, and cast while in treeform...Instantly cures poison on a casting...(except some high level poisons) also after a few levels you heal a total of 8 hps a tick.  Some monsters that are already agro can see thru treeform so be careful.  Regeneration and Chloroplast stack with Treeform.
--Be aware that dying in treeform can be a bad thing- your corpse remains in
its arboreal state, and becomes very difficult to find if you do not have a
loc (It's gotta be ONE of these trees...)
--If a merchant won't sell to you, give Treeform a try if you don't have
Wolf Form yet- if you can cast it in that zone, chances are good that it
will make the merchant more friendly to you.  I guess almost everyone likes
trees.
--Treeform overwrites the first spell on you - so make sure you remove anything before you cast it and it will cure any poison in the first slot.

Going Treeform:  Rule of thumb:  if you're standing on grass, you can
treeform.  Can't do it some places, notably the deserts.  In East Karana, I
can treeform if I'm on a grassy section, but cannot on the sand banks near
the water.  You won't find out for sure until you cast; if you cannot, the
mana is spent and you get a message telling you you cannot change form here.

Partial Camo: The nastier mobs CAN see through it...so /con them while in
treeform to see about an individual mob, and don't assume you're safe just
because you're in treeform.

Cure:  Treeform will take off the first ONE of the following things it finds
in your row of buff/debuff icons on the right: SoW, poison, disease.  Make
sure you cancel SoW BEFORE going treeform if you want it to cure the poison
or disease.

Regen:  I've only tested this to level 19 so far.  Here's the rates:
 - 5 hp/tick standing
 - 5 hp/tick melee
 - 6 hp/tick sitting

Fighting:  You can melee, cast and buff in treeform.  You just can't move.

Casting:  You can do just about anything EXCEPT Sow yourself.

Resistance:  You CAN be poisoned while in treeform, and would need to recast
to cure it.  I treeformed and let a chasm crawler bite me until I was
poisoned to confirm this.

Faction: Makes merchants indifferent to you...which is how it's camo effect
is obtained.  So if your faction is worse than indifferent, you can improve
your selling prices a bit.  If you're better than indifferent, you'll make it
worse.  Of course, if you're level 19 or better, just ring back and sell to a
druid.  :)

10) Enduring Breath.  This is spell is not something you need to purchase right off the bat...however it is needed at higher levels for some underwater areas.  Buy it when you have more money than need and you want to go sit at the bottom of the Ocean of Tears.  Also a good emergency spell in Kedge Keep.

11) Starshine. This spell is only useful if you are a human druid.  Makes a light object roughly equivalent to a greater lightstone. This is much brighter than a Greater Lightstone.
