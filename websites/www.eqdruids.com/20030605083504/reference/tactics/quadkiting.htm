The Unabridged Guide to Quad Kiting

 

So you overheard someone whisper something about quad-kiting and how it can speed up his training considerably.  When you asked about this mythical technique they mumble something incoherent and slipped away into the darkness never to be seen again.  Well don’t worry all the information you need is right here.

 

Kiting is the act of slowing down your opponent and increasing your speed so that you can put enough distance between the two of you so you can cast a spell.  Use Snare, and later Ensnare to slow the MOBs* down, and Spirit of Wolf and Wolf Form to speed yourself up.

 

Quad kiting uses the same technique but Ensnaring four attackers at the same time.  At level 34 you get a spell called Lightning Strike.  It’s an Area Effect, Direct Damage spell that does between 172 – 184 damage for mere 149 mana. 

 

“So what?  I’ve had Area Effect spells for a long time now.  What makes Lightning Strike so special?”  Well that’s a very good question and I’m glad you asked.  The answer is simple.  Most Area Effect spells fall into the DoT or Damage over Time category.  This means that your target has to be sitting still in order for it to take the full brunt of the spell.  A Direct Damage spell deals all the damage in one punch and the target can be moving.

 

Basic Theory

 

In a perfect, computer generated, world here how things should work.  Meditate up to full because you’re going to need all the mana you can muster.  Make sure you have your shields up and that Spirit of Wolf or Wolf Form is not going to wear off.  Venture out into your hunting ground and find your first victim, con them to make sure they are within your range, target them and cast Ensnare.  Congratulations, your 1/4th the way there. 

 

Now find or track your next target and cast Ensnare on it as well.  Make sure you don’t loose the first one while trying to find the second.  If you run beyond the creature’s awareness zone they will wonder off aimlessly, with you still very high on their hate list.

 

Now group them together.  One way to do this is to think of the capital letter Y.  The two MOBs are at the tops, one on the left and one on the right.  You are in the middle part.  (Kind of a V shape at this point.)  If one of the targets is behind the other you need to circle to the left or right until they are both equal distance from you.  Back away from the creatures, down toward the bottom of the Y and the creatures will come together as they approach.  When they are close to each other go out and get your third.  Gather these three using the same method described above, finally grab your fourth. 

 

Make sure all four are in a very tight group.  They should all be touching each other.  Run away for a few seconds, stop completely, cast Lightning Strike, and repeat until they are all dead.  WooHoo!  4X exp for 1 trip out hunting.  Sounds good doesn’t it?  Ok, as you might have figured this is just the meat and potatoes of quad-kiting.  There is much more to it than that.

 

Advanced class:  Some of the got ya’s of quad kiting.

 

Alternate methods of gathering:  There is more than one-way to kite your prey.  Use any method you feel most comfortable with.  Some work best in certain areas and certain situations.  Take the time to learn all the different methods, and what works best for you.

 

When you get comfortable with the above method skip the step where you group up the bad guys as you go.  Instead run out and Ensnare 4 MOBs  then group them together.  This can save a lot of time in certain situations but is a bit more dangerous and should be attempted after you get a handle on the technique.

 

The quickest way to gather up your MOBs is to stand still and let them beat on you.  If the big bad creatures don’t hit that hard then you can just stand still for a few seconds and let one or two beat on you while the others are catching up.  You are going to heal up while you med anyway.   Again, be careful!  This is a lazy Druids method and should be used sparingly.  Do not let your health drop below two bubbles if you are going to use this method.  Bad, very bad.

 

While hunting Snow Cougars in Iceclad I noticed that if I had four or more cougars grouped, when they got close to me one would veer off.  As soon as I moved away it would regain its interest and re-join the pack.  This is a feature of the game.  No more than three of one kind of MOB will attach you at any given time.  The easy way to keep them grouped was to just stay far enough away so they don’t scatter.  Make sense?  This is true even when you are casting the spells.  If they are getting close, duck out of the spell and run further away.  In the long run it will save you time.  Having to re-gather in that fourth MOB can be a pain.

 

Another method that works very well is the circle method.  You have four ‘walking experience bags’ snagged.  Run around them in an increasingly tighter spiral and they should be packed close together.  This method takes some practice and is best used in tight confined areas.

 

Outdoors only:  Lightning Strike and Lightning Blast are outdoor only spells.  Kind of makes sense when you think about it.  After all how many lightning bolts have you seen flying around a cave or dungeon?  (Ok, ok, I will admit that I’ve not seen too many Goblins lately either . . . but that’s different!)

 

Spell interruption and Timing:  After a while you will gain a feeling for the timing of the spells.  However in the beginning it might be kind of hard.  When you are running and stop to cast a spell the computer might not have gotten the hint that you have stopped.  The result is an interrupted spell.  Best way to prevent this is when you stop running, turn slightly left or right. Just a tap, this sends a signal to the EQ servers that you have change directions, oh look you stopped!

 

Casting on a hillside can be very tricky.  Sometimes you will slide down just a smidgen and your spells will not resolve.  When on a hill do not turn, look, or move in any way, while casting.  Sometimes your character will slip anyway.  Stick to flat land where possible.

 

With Lightning Strike I find it best (Depending on the speed of a snared MOB) to run until the spell effect stops it’s bubbling, wait until the spell resets, and then cast again.  For some reason this method only works when you are in wolf form.  When in non-wolf form the spell effect doesn’t last as long.  Some snared MOBs are faster than others and you will need to adjust.

 

 

Start out small:  If you have never quad kited before.  Don’t go out and try to round up four on your first try.  First make sure you can take down one bad guy with Lightning strike to make sure you have enough mana.  Then take out another one to make sure the first was not a fluke or a low con.  Get a feel for how to round them up and how far away you need to be to cast the spell.

 

Pull gone bad.  Of course this won’t happen to you, I’m writing this for the other druid.  Nudge nudge, wink wink.  Lets say you are out hunting and have a nice group of four bad guys grouped up ready for you to vanquish, suddenly something pops out of the blue and starts to attack you.  You have five seconds to live.  What do you do hero?  What do you do?

 

First, don’t give up hope.  In the best scenario you can snare the fifth MOB, then re-target one of your original 4 and continue along your merry way.  When the four are dead you can run out of range of the last one.  However if you happen to wonder by the same MOB again it will remember you and attack.  An easy way to clear out the MOBs hate list is to simply zone.  You are a Druid, you can port yourself to a safe zone (West commons are good because you might be able to pick up Clarity while there.)  Then simply gate back.

 

A more mana efficient method is to simply log out.  It takes 30 seconds to log out and about 20 to get back on.

 

Recently I had something happen that made me re-think the philosophy of clearing out the hate list.  I was quad kiting dire wolves in Iceclad.  I had 4 of them rounded up when they started coming out of the woodwork.  A fifth attacked, I snared it.  Then a sixth and seventh were added to the list.  By the time I hit the beach I had NINE in tow.  I stayed along the beach, running in a straight line, and cast Lightning Strike until the original four dropped.  (As long as you don’t change direction or rearrange them too much you will hit the same 4 over and over).  Then I ran to the far side of the island.

 

When I was back up to full mana I ventured out hunting again.  When I was close to my old hunting grounds one MOB from my previous group jumped me.  I snared it and very quickly a second  agro’d me, soon I had a nice group of four all lined up and ready to go.  Cool!  I cut my gathering time down by about 2/3rd.  I’d have them come to me instead of the other way around.

 

I don’t recommend this method all the time because it makes medding a lot more stressful.  At any moment a previous target might haphazardly wander your way and attack.

 

One note about this, if you have more than four grouped, the one that is targeted is not always one that will take damage.  Not exactly what I was expecting.

 

Spells to memorize:

- Lightning Strike

- Spirit of Wolf/Wolf Form.

- Ensnare

 

The rest are up to you and depend a lot on where and what you are hunting and also your comfort level.  Other possibilities are

-         Combust to finish off the lone MOB

-         Your current best shield(s) to beef up HP/AC/ATK.

-         Regeneration/Chloroplast when you get it

-         Spirit of Cheetah to save your hide when things get hairy

-         Greater/Superior Healing

-         Level 5 root spell because of its low mana cost and its fast casting time. 

-         I’ve also had some friends suggest Gate.  What happens if you accidentally snare yourself?  (That never happens right?) Answer, pray to Tunare that your gate spell goes off.

 

As you might have noticed there are more than 8 spells listed here.  Like I said, your selection depends on a lot of things.  Choose wisely young Jedi.  Get a feel for what works best for you.

 

Mana is king:  I would say that you need wisdom of about 170 or better before you even think about quading.  A good rule of thumb is you should be able to take out one MOB with Combust using 60 – 65% of your total mana pool.  This is equal to about three mana bubbles.  Lightning Strike takes up more mana per cast but does the same damage.  (However 184 damage * 4 MOBs  = 736 total damage for 149 mana, this is an extremely efficient spell if used properly.)  (Combust 182 max damage for 110 mana)

 

At level 49 you get the upgrade to Lightning Strike which is Lightning Blast.

     234 mana for 447 X 4 = 1788 DAMAGE!  Now you’re cooking.

 

Even with 200 Wisdom and some extra mana gear you are going to have problems quading at level 34.  Please note that I did not say it couldn’t be done, all I’m saying is that this guide is for a non-twinked player who is just learning.

 

Money is mana:  At 34 you should have most of the group ports spell, you can make a huge amount of money (safely) by porting groups around.  Hint: Bind yourself in a crowded zone that is not very close to a normal travel hub.  When you see a shout for a port for money, jump on it.  When you arrive at the destination you can shout offering ports, if there are no takers you can gate back.  The Bazaar is a great place to earn money while porting.  Use the money to buy Wisdom/Mana gear.

 

Specialization:  If you are going to quad kite, you should give serious thought to specializing in Evocation.  Specialization does two things.  It reduces the mana cost of that type of spells, and it reduces the frequency of fizzles. This is very important when your main spells burn up so much mana.  Specialization does NOT reduce the casting time of a spell.

 

You can specialize when you hit level 30, go to a Druid guild Master and put one point into all your specialization areas.  Then make damn sure that Evocation is the first one to reach 50.  Alternatively you can put one point into evocation then after it is above 51 go back and put one point into the other specialization types.

 

If you screwed up and specialized in something else, don’t worry.  There is a very easy quest that you can do to reset your numbers.  Go here to see the quest.

 

For the Greedy:  Why stop at four MOBs?  Wouldn’t five or ten bad guys make more sense?  Getting greedy now aren’t you?  The answer to this very good question is that Lightning Strike will only hit four MOBs at a time.  If you have five then you will still have one at full health when the other four have dropped.

 

Resistant is futile:  What happens if one target resists Ensnare?  In the immortal words of the Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy “Don’t panic!”  Run away a bit so the other Ensnared MOBs don’t catch up then try casting Ensnare again on the resistant bugger.  Usually you can get it snared before you die.  J

 

If you run into one that is particularly resistant to Ensnare, don’t try to fill out your group.  Odds are good that your health will be low by that point and the seemingly blessed MOB will resist your Lightning spells as well.  It will no doubt require special attention after the other MOBs have dropped.

 

3rd person view:  F9 is your friend.  Many people don’t know that you can change your view however this can be a very helpful feature when quading.  When you are trying to gather up your MOBs using the circle method, it is very hard to do if you have to keep turning around to see where all the bad guys are.  However if you press F9 four times you are at an extreme zoom out and can see yourself and the four MOBs very easily.

 

You can change the angle of view using the mouse.  I said ‘can’ what I meant is that you SHOULD change the angle.  Use it to look around to make sure you are not going to get jumped by other wandering MOBs.  Changing the view is very simple.  Hold the Right mouse button down then move the mouse around.  (and then you do the hokey pokey. . .never mind.)  If you move it left or right the camera will swing that direction always leaving you as the center point of view.  If you move the mouse up and down you will zoom in and out.

 

Each of the four different views offers different angles like very close, top down view, far away left and far away right.

 

If you run in front of a tree or rock or hill the camera will jump ahead of the object so you should still be able to see yourself.  (Branches on the trees will still obstruct your view.)  Also, it is almost always better to have the camera on the downside of a hill.

 

It takes awhile to get used to doing things this way but it is well worth the effort.  Stick with it, play around a little before you head out to get yourself killed.

 

F10 is also your friend:  If you are still using the old interface then make sure you are at least playing in full screen mode.  Pressing the F10 key once will put you in full screen mode.  This view is so much better and lets you see things that you might normally miss.  Pressing F10 again will give you full screen with out all the info boxes.  Pressing F10 a third time will return you to the default screen mode.

Thank you VI for the new interface!  You guys rock!  (/does a little Druid dance around a tree.)

 

From personal experience:  At level 35, with an evocation of 80, wisdom of 170 and no mana gear I was able to kite two foes at a time.  I was learning how to do this in The Overthere.  Most of the time one MOB would stop when its HP fell below a certain level and the other one would keep chasing me.  I’d combust the one that was still moving and by then I’d be out of mana and have to hack them both to death starting with the one with the most HP left.

 

By level 36 I could take down three with very few abandoned pulls.  My first successful quad kite was right after I dinged 37.  (I didn’t even try 4 at level 36 for some strange reason.)  At that point my wisdom was at 196 and evocation was about 105.  I believe I could do much better now, but I guess that arrogance comes with experience. J

 

What to look for in a zone

 

The world has many choices to offer a Druid when they are out hunting.  What I look for in a zone is wide open, flat spaces, abundant MOBs, short spawn times, few if any high level wandering MOBs and the less populated by other players the better.  There are several very good guides on the internet that will give you specific locations.  Keep in mind that the author probably didn’t try out EVERY location.  They might have found one that worked well, but not the one that would work best for you.

 

  • Snow Cougars in Iceclad by the igloos - usually over camped but you can try.

  • Dire Wolves by the port in ring - never camped, great experience.

  • Walrus in Eastern Wastes - wide level range, good exp, hardly ever camped.

  • Spirocs in Timorous Deep – sometimes camped but easy to get from 49 – 51.

  • Wyverns in Cobalt Scar – Very over camped.  Good loot, good exp.  No faction hit but it really helps to have good dragon faction.

  • Ulthorks in Eastern Wastes

  • Tizmaks in Great Divide

And many others.

 

As a general rule the creatures that give the best experience are the ones with the worst loot.  My goal from day one was to find the quickest way to level so that I could join my guild on high-level raids and perhaps be able to twink other players.  If you are looking to make massive amounts of platinum then you can take a break from leveling and farm some of the high buck creatures.

 

Mono-type MOBs:  Try to find an area that has very little variety of monsters.  It greatly shortens the hunting time.  For example, I hunted in The Overthere for a while.  Great experience but found that there are about 10 different types of creature wandering around at any given time.  I’d be trying to gather some Thorny Succulents and have to avoid the Spiked succulents, Sarnak Knights, Sarnak Berserkers, Kunark Rhino’s, Sabertooth Tigers, Sabertooth lions, Stoneeler Cockatrices. . .etc.  This can greatly increase your hunting time and also increase your abandon rate.  Which leads me to my next topic.

 

Time:  I was looking to maximize my time in the game.  I looked at the root/rot, /nuke, playing in groups and other methods and found that quading is the absolute best method to level quickly from 34 on up.  If you find a better method, please let me know.

 

Quad = (Time it takes to meditate up to full) + (Time it takes to hunt) + (time it takes to gather up the MOBs) + (time it takes to wipe them off the face of Norrath) + (Time it takes to find a safe spot to med.) + (Time it takes to re-cast the buff/enhancement spells)

 

For Root-n-Rot it goes something like this.

R-n-R = (time to Med to full) + (Time to hunt) + (Time to Root and cast Dot’s)(Wait for DoT’s to work)+(Fine a safe place to med)

 

I was never able to take out 4 mobs with root and rot in the same amount of time I could to do one cycle of Quad kiting.  There is less downtime with R-n-R but the result is slower overall.

 

The smaller the numbers the better off you are.  With Quading you cut way back on most of those numbers.  Even at level 44 I was getting a full blue bubble of experience per trip and each trip had a cycle time of about 12 minutes + 3 minutes hunting.  This puts a level at about 5 hours of work.  Not too shabby.  You could cut this in half if you had Clarity or KEI. 

 

Root-n-Rot lets you take on a higher level MOB but it takes a long time for the Damage over Time spells to work and even if you med while the Swarm is doing its thing you are still going to need to med afterwards.  (Don’t get me wrong, this is a fantastic method for Druids below 34 and high level Druids as well.)

 

Snare-n-Nuke.  You might as well Quad.

 

Death:  If you die, you just wasted about an hour or more of your time.  Suck it up, death is part of the game.  Learn from your experience (or loss of it, hahahah) and move on.

 

Some methods to avoid death.  Depending on the landscape you can use levitate to get out quickly.  Some people have Spirit of Cheetah memorized at all times.  Some players have gate memorized even if the six-second casting time is a bit long for an exit strategy, and some players run to the zone.

 

Heal and the world loves you! Quad and the world will hate your guts.

 

What other class can solo 4 MOBs at the same time at such a low level?  Of course other races will hate you for this.  However it is more likely that other races will hate you for a different reason.  Druids have a bad reputation.  There are so many bad Druids out there that it’s deservedly so.  Some Druids are notorious for training**.  When a pull goes bad, and it will at one time or another, don’t drop your problem in someone else’s lap.  Make a hotkey that says “Train to Zone!  Move it or loose it!” or whatever is appropriate for your current hunting situation.

 

Don’t drop your pulls on someone else and if (god forbid) it does happens make sure you help that person any way you can.  It’s your fault they got in trouble.  Arrange a rez or port them back to their body, whatever is needed.

 

Don’t kite past groups when your MOB will agro on them.  Know when your MOB will agro on others.

 

The little train that could. . .wipe out an entire party:

Do not assume that you are alone. A simple warning that falls on deaf ears is ten times better than dropping a gaggle of wyverns on an unsuspecting group that results in their Total Party Wipe Out.

 

Not everyone who steals your pulls is doing it deliberately.  The graphics in the game are not perfect and sometimes you do not appear on another players screen.  If they pull one away, gently ask them to be more careful in the future.  (I’ve made several friends in game this way.)  Odds are pretty good that someday you will be the one stealing from someone else.

 

Now at the very end of this tutorial I’d like to mention that a vast majority of the idea within this document are not mine.  I did not invent quad kiting all by my little lonesome.  Most of the information in this guide can be found elsewhere on the Internet.  However it is spread out all over the place and very hard to piece together.  I did a lot of digging to find this information and test it to see what worked best.  There is still a bunch more information out there that I have not found yet.

 

Good luck and happy hunting.

  

*MOB: Short for Mobil OBject.  Back in the Beta days when the creatures didn’t have name tags floating above them the players had to name them something.

 

**Train:  An angry group of monsters that are hell bent on crushing everything in their path including innocent players that just happen to be in the wrong place at the wrong time.  If you hit the zone with 10 mad warriors on your tail they will forget about you and pick on anyone who happens to be around.
