Only fight creatures that might contain decent treasures.  This is mainly gnolls, goblins, kobolds, and decaying skeletons.  Don't fight anything yellow.  At level 1 everything is even.  Don't spend a dime - you need your cash for spells.   Have a friend make you some patchwork armor or buy some if you are rolling in the dough.  Do not group.  You need all the money and treasure you can get.   Right before level 5 suicide for food near your starting point.  If you are a wood elf I would consider hoofing it to Rivervale or another newbie area that isn't as crowded and contains better treasure monsters....run back to the guards when you get beat up or trained.  NEVER lose your corpse.  You do not lose experience at death until level 5.

The Elf zones are way too crowded...I highly recommend hopping the boat going to Freeport or possibly Rivervale.

If you are a half-elf  - make a level and hoof it over to Halas/Everfrost - best newbie zone in game...when you are done there at level 6 or so....go to the shaman guild in Halas, buy gate, and go home!

They've changed the rules a little on how death, food, and newbie items work
since release:

Levels 1-3:
You get all your newbie items (except guild tunic) back.

Level 4:
You get all your newbie items (except guild tunic) and food back.

Level 5:
You get nothing back. But you don't start losing experience until level 6.

So if you're a hungry newbie, suicide before hitting level 4, not 5.

Badger's Bear steaks

Ingredients:

    1 level 3-4 Druid

    1 level 3-4 Tank

    1 brown bear or similar level animal, yellow or low red

    1 snare spell

    1 panic animal spell

    1 flame burst spell

 

Recipe:

    Track down your dinner and immediately sauté with a snare...turn and run until you get a safe enough distance away to cast, but still within easy range of your tank. cast panic animal, which will make it turn and start to flee at slow speed. Allow your tank to tenderize the animal by beating it thoroughly for 30 seconds, at which time you cast another panic animal. roast thoroughly with a flame burst to keep it's attention upon you if it turns at bay, and recast snare and panic animal as needed. Enjoy. Note that this can work without the tank element for yellows, but is considerably riskier. This tactic is even effective at higher levels.
