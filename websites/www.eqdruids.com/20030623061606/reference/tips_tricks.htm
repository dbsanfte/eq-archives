Casting

 

If you get charmed by a mob you will cast the spell that is in the number 8 slot, so if you have a heal in that slot you will heal the mob, if you have a nuke spell in that slot you will end up nuking your party. But if you have a buff spell in the 8th slot you can't cast anything that will hurt your group.

 Grab a KEI, load up your cheapest, fastest casting spell for the skill you wish to raise and head to the Plain of Knowledge zone.

Begin casting whatever spell you have chosen, on yourself., be it a de-buff, DD or DoT, the spell cast will not effect you but will still raise the skill. Buffs will work as normal.

Ducking Interrupts Casting

Jumping (spacebar) will also interrupt spells.

If you need to cast after running, tap a short turn before casting to avoid having an interrupted spell. (This forces the server to update your location so it doesn't think you're still running)

/tgb on - lets you cast group spells on another group without having to be invited

alt + # (1- 8) casts the spell in that spot.  So holding the alt key and pressing 8 would cast the spell I have in my 8th spot.

Arrow keys left and right flip through the pages of your spellbook if you have it up

/book 5 opens your spellbook to page 5.

Cure disease will cure Slow

Cure poison will cure Tash

If the cleric has divine aura on, or the bard is playing their divine aura spell, they will not teleport with you nor will they succor.

General

 

Control and a click picks up one item out of a stack in your inventory.  Shift and a click picks up the entire stack.

Clouds always move east to west (in case you don’t have a sword to drop and point north lol)

My druid keeps her Crucible of Escape (right-click 10-second-ish Lesser Succor) in her last inventory slot.
 

You can left-click-and-hold an inventory slot and drag-and-drop it into an empty hot button. So, her Crucible of Escape is always available for use without opening her inventory.

 /SHOWGRASS OFF reduces lag in newer zones with moving tree branches like Jaggedpine.

To get into The Hole, go down to the bottom where the pond is in Paineel. When you are in the water, in the upper right portion of the rocks there is a hole you can squeeze through. Wolfform helps in doing this. Keep trying and eventually you'll make it through.

/ttell. Targeted tell. Useful for sending tells to the owner of a corpse. Works for the living too

For characters with pets, pressing the respective group select function key (F1 - F6) twice will target their pet, provided the pet is within 50 feet of you.

Pressing F1-F6 targets respective person in group

Pressing 1-10 initiates respective Hotkey

When creating the macro's, you can put " /sit off " on the first line to save yourself time having to find your stand hotkey

F7 targets the closest player in view
F8 targets the closest NPC in view

/assist <target> will tell you what target the person has.

/assist with a mob targeted will tell you who the mob is attacking

To not auto attack when you type /assist, type either /assist off or in your EQClient.ini file change AttackOnAssist=TRUE to read FALSE

To check if you are hidden or sneaking type /target <your name> If it targets you, you are not invisible.

/rt will return a tell to the last person that gave you a tell

If you are on a high-speed connection, find the file datarate.txt in your EQ Directory and change it so the value is 9. This will cause larger packets to be downloaded to you (decreasing lag)

Stand/Sit shortcut key is ctrl-s
Open Spellbook is ctrl-b
Invite/accept group is ctrl-I
Disband a group is ctrl-d
Toggle run/walk ctrl-r
Open your abilities window ctrl-a
Camp shortcut is ctrl-x

%r returns the race of a player character.
%s returns the subjective pronoun (he, she, or it).
%o returns the objective pronoun (him, her, or it).
%p returns the possessive pronoun (his, her, or its)
%m gives back your pets name

' is a shortcut for /say
: is a shortcut for /emote

Hold shift down and hit pageup or pagedown and you will scroll the main chat window up or down 1 page at a time.

Hold shift and up or down arrows will cycle through the messages you have typed

Ducking significantly reduces falling damage when running on hilly terrain

To stop moving quickly while on a horse, just click off the horse icon

/doability # will perform the numbered ability

Sitting causes Agro with mobs. This can work to your advantage if the mob doesn’t summon. If you need to keep a mob off the tank for a moment, sit and as it comes towards you, stand back up. This is known as ping ponging the mob. The mob will go back and forth between you and the tank.

Large sewing kits can hold giant items. This is very useful at times.

Numlock will turn autorun on 

Stats

 

I'm still surprised that many players don't know about player-made stat foods and drink.

For example, if you keep a Holy Cake and a Kaladim Constitutional in your top inventory slots, they will add +10 to ALL your saves. How much would you pay for a magic item that gave you +10 to all your saves and didn't take up any equipment slots?

Want to add +10 to your intelligence and wisdom? Buy a Misty Thicket Picnic and a Qeynos Afternoon Tea.

Look at these new recipes from PoP:

Hero Sandwich: wt 1.0, small, miraculous meal, STR 10, DEX 5, STA 5, WIS 5, INT 5, AGI 5, HP 30, mana 30

Tormentor Stir Fry: wt 2.0, large, miraculous meal, DEX 8, AGI 8, STA 8, HP 50

Just keep force-feeding yourself on cheap food and drink and you will never consume the stat food.

The EQ Traders web site has baking and brewing recipes for all known stat foods and drinks.

Communication

 

/ttell is the same as just /tt

 

Once you remember to use it (you can use it on corpses) it's amazing. Ever talk to someone with a horribly complicated name to spell at a glance? Target them and /tt. 

Shift and up and down arrows will let you scroll through what you have previously written. Comes in handy when someone has missed your tell or reply.

In Game Commands

 

/shownames off turns all names off so you don't get lag from all the names. Good for raids.

/hidecorpses all or /hidecorpses allbutgroup works well to get rid of dead bodies that you don't need to loot and are interfering with targeting.

/shownames 1 (reduces everyone’s name to just their first name)
/shownames 2 (reduces everyone’s name to first and last)
/shownames 3 (Shows title and whole name)
/shownames 4 (shows guild, title, and wholename)
 

I use /showname 1 most the time, really makes bazaar shopping easier...

 

In laggy zones or on raids, just type Ctrl - (minus) to lower clip plane without having to switch to options.  Ctrl + (plus) moves the clip plane back out. 

/hotbutton *Name_With_No_Spaces* *First Line Can Have Spaces*
 


Thus...


/hotbutton Help /g HELP ME!!!


Gives you a hotbutton that when depressed says


"HELP ME!!!" to your group.

 When shopping (or looking through your inventory bags) pressing the "Alt" key lists the name of all the items in the container your mouse is hovering over. 

/target will target the closest thing whose name is unique. This works on mobs too. For example. There are 2 players named Fred and Fredrick on the raid, both not in the group. Fredrick needs healing. Type '/tar Fredr' and as long as Fredrick is within 50 feet of you (forwards or backwards - doesn't matter) she will get selected to be your current target. If you type '/tar Fred' and both Fred and Fredrick is within 50 of you, your current target will not change since 'Fred' is not a unique name 

Looting and finding corpses

 

Targeting your corpse and typing /loot will also loot for you 

To find a corpse that is missing, make a hotkey with /target <player name> and run around hitting the hotkey. When you are in range of the corpse, you will see the name appear in your target window. Then type /corpse to summon it.

How to drag multiple corpses:
Make a hotkey(s) with the following:
/corpse <name1>
/pause 2
/corpse <name2>
/pause 2

etc for as many corpses as you need. You will need to move slowly, but you will be able to summon multiple corpses along as one giant pile.

You can also loot a corpse by targeting the corpse and typing /loot 

Here are two corpse finding tips:

1. This tip requires a purchase so I don't know if it fits the theme, but it has saved me tons of trouble.

EVERYONE should have a Chipped Bone Rod in their bank, so when they get killed they can always find their corpse. Go to roughly where you were, target yourself, right click the rod, and run straight ahead until you see your corpse. A vendor in SH sells the rods and I think they're under 5pp.

2. Keep a Fire Beetle Eye in your backpack forever. It’s a required component for at least one of our spells, but more importantly, it makes your corpse light up so it’s easier to find in Misty or Gfay. 

People with only one EQ account can use alternate characters to drag their corpses.

My low-level cleric got himself killed deep within the Everfrost Mountains. As an experiment, he /consented my druid to drag his corpse before he camped. I was able to log on my druid and drag my cleric's corpse to the zoneline so he could rez himself.
