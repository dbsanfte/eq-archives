Young Druid, when you achieve your 14th season, get yourself to Lavastorm.  The way is easy for one such as ourselves:  Bind yourself regularly while traveling.  Then, before entering Nektulos Forest, SOW and Camouflage yourself, and don't stop running.  After arriving, Bind yourself to your new home.  New home it will be, for you will quickly grow to love the place.
 
The only common creatures that are aggressive are the Fire Imps and Fire Elementals.  They respond well to Expulse Summoned and for the 60 mana you spend, you can depend on a steady, although not guaranteed, 80+ points of damage.  At 16th season, I am rewarded with 90 points damage.  Other aggressive creatures are the goblins, green to me and practically immobile unless disturbed, and the uncommon spawn.  These last, I have read, are very rewarding for the difficulty involved in bringing them down.  I have some experience with one of them.  Imagine my surprise when I approached a winsome young woman wearing the blue pants and white shirt so typical of civilized areas.  She attacked me, both verbally and physically.  Run?  Hardly!  Even at my early 16 she conned blue, and I slew her, although with some difficulty, I must confess.  She had a name, 'though I can't now recall it.  I only remember that she dropped a Glowing Torch, armor class 7, and raising my charisma by 5 points!
 
The others there, the Fire Drakes which shall be your first quarry, the Rock Dervishes, Lava Basilisks, Lava Crawlers, and others, shall leave you unharmed.  'Tis best, though, to go always camouflaged, and cast first Shield of Thistle upon yourself before drawing your quarry.  Try not the way of "kiting" unless you have a taste for lava, and leave your fire based spells in your book, for these are worthless on the fire-borne inhabitants.  If you are accosted by aggressive creatures while subduing your quarry, judge the situation.  Discretion IS often the better part of valor.  With a steady head and SOW, the zone will be yours if you are not turned to stone too long or so low already in health that you cannot run with the speed SOW imparts. 
 
Aha, you seek not only adventure, but fortune.  Fear not.  The most common loot here are gemstones, words, and runes, all of lower value, it is true, but none less than a gold worth.  The most common drop, a bloodstone, is several gold, and when you are lucky enough to find a basilisk egg, congratulate yourself on 6 gold pieces work!  When you must sell (or buy some trinket lest the weight of copper and silver become too much for you) then seek out the gypsy camp in the West or the lone Gypsy seller at the Druid Ring.  But sell not these small, almost weightless things for the weightier mass of money, stack them and wait until your eventual return to civilized areas and banks.  However, part quickly with the blocks of ore, which weigh 15 stone, and happily receive your 3 gold 7 as a lesser encumbrance.
 
/ooc  I play mostly solo, and more than a bit on the dangerous side.  (In other words, I die a lot.)  But Lavastorm is the place to spend your latter teen levels with reasonable care.  I think the really neat drops carried by levels comparatively low for the value of loot are Varant's lure to increase use of an underutilized zone.  They are rare, but not unheard of.  Enjoy them while they last.  If you're going at 14, give yourself a padding of 2 bubbles to make sure a few deaths won't rob you of valuable 14th season spells, and it would be safer to start by grouping.  However, you'll soon have the confidence to go out solo or with a friend.  The lower level elementals and imps will drop with three Expulses and one Lightning, and when the Lava Crawlers become reachable, they are also damaged by Expulse.  The other mobs, though, are not, so Shield of Thistle, Swarm, Lightning and Hail will be your standbys.  Spend a few levels with the loot here, and by 19 your new spells will seem reasonably priced.
 
The world is ours at 14.  Not every inch of it, but enough for variety and experience.  Good hunting and watch your step:  Lava's hot.
 
Jeviz
Half Elf Druid of the 16th season
