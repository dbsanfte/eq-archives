At level 14 you can purchase befriend animals.  The 'charm' time is random and whatever you charm WILL come after you when its done.  The lower the CON  (risky blue, etc) the longer it stays charmed in general.

The most common tactic is to charm a blue GrifFAWN (not -on or -enne) and immediately have it attack another or a scythe beetle, etc.  The more you help out in combat the better off your pet will be.  Immediately attack another.  At some point they are going to turn on you so be ready with some burn.  I typically let my pet soak up the damage from killing something, then when it turns I kill it. 

The spell Feral Spirit at 19 is a pet buff.  While it makes your pet a killing machine, especially with a thorn shield, it will turn on you eventually so be ready!

Many players who have mastered the Charming technique say it gives better experience than kiting.

Pet Command List:

/pet attack (or /pet kill) - Pet will attack your current target
/pet guard me - Pet guards you from anything that attacks or that you attack
/pet guard here - Pet guards an area
/pet follow me - Pet follows you (not through zone boundaries)
/pet back off - Pet will cease fighting
/pet as you were - belay previous orders
/pet sit down - Duh
/pet stand up - Duh
/pet report health - give a percentage of remaining health
/pet who leader - tell who he is following

Nyrril sent in the following charming guide:

Befriend Animal can easily add as much xp or even more than Kiting
can.  The trick to effectively using this spell is not to get stars in your
eyes and go for befriending a Griffawn and attacking evil eyes, etc.  It's
important to note that charming stronger creatures seems to affect the charm
time, thus charming a white creature will not last near as long as charming
a blue creature.
Here are the tactics I've used to go from 14th to 17th in relatively
short time:  I charm a blue creature and then I immediately sick the creature
on another blue creature. I would then using Stinging Swarm and wade into
melee to help kill the creature quicker and keep my pet from taking too much
damage.  After this kill, immediately target another blue creature, send your
pet in, Stinging Swarm it.  Come in for a little bit of melee, but mainly use
ignites to help finish it off.  It is important to make sure that your pet
survives, but takes most of the damage.  After this battle (perhaps before
its even through) your pet should be ready to turn on you.  You should have
saved at least a bubble of mana for this. Immediately damage shield yourself
and you should be able to take it easily (its low on hp from fighting). 
Using these tactics you can take down 3 blue creatures within a few
minutes. Trust me, 3 blues gives more xp than one yellow and gives as much
as some red creatures.  Compared to Kiting, you will get almost as much xp
without spending near as much time.   In the long haul, your xp over time
will be much greater.
Unfortunately, these tactics don't work 100% of the time.  You'll
sometimes have your initial befriend animal resisted (I usually try at least
twice before giving up and blasting the creature). You'll also have your pet
turn on you too early in the 2nd fight and find yourself having to take on 2
relatively healthy blues or zoning.  Luckily, you will always have SoW up
and you will have no problems getting to a Zone.  It's important to make the
decision to Fight or Flee immediately and stick with it -- If you aren't
going to win the fight, don't waste the mana and hp seeing if you can kill
one of the creatures before fleeing. Its much better to just zone, have a
quick rest break, and get back to it.
