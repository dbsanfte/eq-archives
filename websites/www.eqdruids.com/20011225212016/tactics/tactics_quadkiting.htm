Hail Fellow Druids.

During my travels through Norrath, I have discovered a nice little way to get rapid experience. I have seen few others use this technique, and the ones that see me use it often wonder why they didn't think of it themselves. So for those of you unfamiliar with this method, I would like to share it with you.

At lvl 34, druids get this wonderful little spell called LIGHTNING STRIKE. It's an area effect spell that does 184 pts of damage, hitting up to 4 targets MAXIMUM at once. This is a wonderful time saver, as you have already gone through the first hell lvl at level 30, and at 35 you are on your second one, 3rd at 40, and the ever popular, "This lvl makes me want to quit the game", at 45.

The key to using this spell effectively is making sure that all of the targets are grouped fairly close together. This is rather simple to do if you remain patient and don't get to cocky. Here are some of the various ways i have experimented doing this....

1) Assuming the targets are side by side, harmony them and pull one with snare. Get him far enough away from the other so you can go back to the one still sitting there and then pull him with snare. Then run circles around them both until they are close together, then kite away.

2) Say you have a target at 9:00 and 3:00. Get within casting distance of both if possible. Target the first and pull with snare. Then turn and do the same with the second. As they advance on you, slowly walk backwards keeping them and yourself in a "V" type formation. With them at the tips of the "V" and you at the intersection where the lines meet. As they draw closer to you they will close the distance between themselves. Once they are close enough together, Kite away.

3) This is another good way. Especially for those who may not have enough mana to kite more than one mob efficiently. (Best used with the "V" formation example above) Once you have meditated to full, cast regenerate on yourself. Then med back up to full mana. Then cast your second damage shield on yourself. *always keep the self only version buff on*) then either med to full again, or pull if you are ready. Let both targets close in on you and hit you a couple of times. This has 2 advantages. a) Gets both targets side by side. b) Does a little bit of damage to each that may save you having to cast a last DD spell on them.

There are lots of other ways to do this. You should experiment and find whatever method works best for you. Also, I believe it goes without saying that this only works outdoors for the most room for kiting. Not to mention that Lightning Strike is an outdoor only spell.

I have used this on only a couple of different mobs. Gnolls outside of Splitpaw, Cyclops, Hill Giants, and various Guards. 

Some things to keep in mind using this method...

* Always keep the targets close together. IF they get separated during the fight, stand directly in the center of the 2 and slowly back away as the get closer. this will draw them back together. If you are kiting more than 2 and this happens, draw 2 of them together, the repeat the process above to get the 3rd back in the group.

* Lightning Strike takes a lot of mana to cast, and is not really mana efficient for the damage it does. For best mana \ damage efficiency you will need 3 or more targets.

* Before you get all gung ho and go trying to kill 4 mobs at once, try this out on a single target first. See how much mana it will take you to kill it, and how much you have left over. IF you have to med to much during the battle, it might be best to wait for your next lvl, or until you get another good WIS increasing item.

* Also keep in mind that just because you can kill 2 at a time, does not mean that you can take 3 or 4 effectively. Because of the low damage of the spell, it takes longer to kill, and you will most probably have to resnare each target during battle. (I usually do this at 1\2 mana just to be safe) You also have to factor in resists. If more than 1 target resists a large portion of the damage, or resists a blast completely, this could lead to problems when going to finish them off.

* This style of kiting is not very suitable for Tag Team kiting. It's efficiency is based on keeping the targets together. With someone helping you or healing you in battle, this may distract one of the targets and end up splitting them up. Forcing the one assisting you to keep right by your side for the possibly the rest of the battle. Causing him to get in the way of retargeting the mobs for you to resnare, etc....

* This spell also has a casting time of 4.5 seconds. So you need plenty of distance to cast it. I have found that running as soon as the casting meter disappears, until the spell icon resets for another cast, is enough time to get the distance you need.

* Because of the time needed to cast the spell, and the distance needed to cast it safely, it's a good idea to resnare each of the mobs you are kiting once you get to 1\2 mana during the fight.

The spell line up I primarily use is as follows.

Greater healing Regeneration Shield of brambles Lightning strike Combust (or Firestrike when you get it) Careless Lightning (keep them handy for finishing off the mobs if they don't all die at once.) Drones of Doom or Drifting death (if the last one standing is stunned you can DOT and med while he dies) Snare (gate is also another one to mem if this method is new or very tricky for you to do.

Update to Quad Kiting

Initial contact
---------------
Go out and find a mob snare it. Run to another mob that moves at the same speed (does not have to be same type of mob) and snare it. Do this until you have 4 mobs snared (preferablly in a fairly central location).

Grouping the mobs together
--------------------------
Now, you have 4 mobs snared. Run away from the mobs and either run in Y pattern or encircling the mobs until you have them bunched up tight. Then run a safe distance from them and cast your best area affect spell. But be reminded that the AoE spells have a very small range , so consider that the mobs need to be graphically touching.

The kill
--------
Get the mobs down and continue blasting until you are out of mana or they are all dead. If you have enough wisdom you can blast them completely, until they are dead. If you get them to go snare dumb (point they turn and stand still) , use your DD spell to finish the job.

Some points to remember
-----------------------
- They must run at the same speed snared. Multiple speeds = dead druid.

- Med a little early on in the kite if you think you will need it later on. It is easier to med a bit with 4 snared mobs than to be running and wishing you had enough mana to resnare or blast that last mob that resisted your AoE's.

- Don't worry about your own buffs. I normally only have my highest wolf form on. Saves down time when the kite is over you don't have to med, buff, med, kite. You only have to med to full and go get 'em.

- ALWAYS ALWAYS ALWAYS have gate memed. You sometimes will aggro more than 4 mobs and the way the game engine works you can only damage four at a time. So know when to get out. Quadding is most efficient if you are not losing exp because you were to hard headed to gate and tried to root that extra mob. Trust me I know and have died trying to save a good group of mobs.

- No Quadding before 34. This is our first AoE spell and you cant do it before. `nuff said.

- RAIN SPELLS ARE NOT AoE SPELLS!!! Rain spells do their damage in waves and the mob MUST be standing in the "rain" to be damaged. Therefore a snared/MOVING mob will be hard to get in the "rain waves", much less getting all 4 mobs into the wave in unison.

- Your health is not important as long as all mobs are snared. It is as easy to kite in wolf form with all mobs ensnared with full health as it is half health. BUT don't be over-confident. If it looks bad and you can't get them off of you gate. Again , if you lose an hour (s) worth of quadding to death you may has well have been grouping in a good group and been making steady, safe exp..

- Be considerate of other people. Yes you can solo. Yes, you are a well oiled machine. Yes you are an exp machine. BUT do not step on any other people/groups to quad. I mean, do not go to the snow cougar camp and grab your 4 mobs and run away if there are 75 other people there camping snow cougars. Most times there are other places to go, and after all , we are the masters of travel.
