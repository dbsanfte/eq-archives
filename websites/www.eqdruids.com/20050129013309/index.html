Developer's Update 11/05/04

Collision Update - 11/5/04

Grumbuk-Dev 11.05.04

EQ Programmer

Hello,

I'm happy to be able to let you know that more time has been spent recently solving some of the remaining collision bugs. For those wondering why this hasn't happened sooner, the main reason is that after the DX9 collision system became relatively stable we wanted to just leave it alone for a bit - partly because we wanted to avoid risking unexpected bugs during the Omens beta/release timeframe, but it was also (mostly) so we could become more certain about the nature of the remaining problems in the collision & movement system. Now that enough time has passed for the existing problems in the system to become fairly well identified and understood, I've been able to start working through them. Some turned out to be pretty easy to fix, some not so easy, but the good news is that there have been some fixes to the collision system.

Here are the things that I believe are fixed with a brief explanation of what was going on with them for the curious.

*** Please note that some of these changes haven't even hit Test yet but will be there soon, probably early next week. ***

------------------------------
These two are closely related:

* Getting out of water is much easier.
* Players standing on the lip of a cliff or ledge of some sort would not slide off of it; this had the side effect of the physics system getting confused and could cause a 20k fall bug to occur.

I haven't yet worked on the physics part of this problem (the 20k fall part; I intend to soon) but I did make changes that cause the collision sphere for the player to properly slide (deflect) off the edge of the surface they are not standing directly on or otherwise directly colliding with - this should make it near impossible to trigger the 20k fall bug. This problem was directly related to why players had a hard time getting out of water where the ledge to hop onto was a bit higher that the player's collision sphere; basically, the deflection of the sphere as you hit the edge of the land was always perpendicular to the surface of the triangle. This means, you hit the ledge and it just pushes you straight back instead of letting you hit the edge of the ledge and bounce up via deflection. I've tested all sizes of characters in halas, shadowhaven, ssra, and in all cases it is much easier to get out of the water. Shadowhaven has always been a little difficult to get out of but based on what I remember of the DX8 engine it's about the same now as it used to be. It's kind of an unusual case because it really is quite a tall ledge to use to get out of the water. I've asked about having something added to the water there to make it a lot easier to get out of but I'm not sure when/if that can be done.

------------------------------
* There were object instances in the game (ie one object that appears many times, one example of a problem object is a vase type object in drankcatacombsa) that would, when you walked over them, cause the collision system to freak out usually causing players to get tossed & stuck into the ceiling.

This was due to the original object being scaled so large from the original size of the exported object (from the 3d editor) that some of the math the collision system has to do was hitting a precision problem with side effects that aren't worth going into beyond saying it was just plain incorrect. The reproduction of the bug was 100%, the fix was pretty straightforward, and I am certain this is fixed. There have been sporadic reports about players getting tossed into ceilings and sticking there and I think this is what was causing it.

-------------------------------

These two are related.
* Mounts should no longer be dismissed when zoning
* Summoning mounts in certain places should no longer cause players to fall through the world.

These were both due to a pretty straightforward bug that solved all the cases of these problems happening. There was a variable that was being used for the floor elevation that was simply not being updated before the mount check was being done. Once this was changed I haven't seen or heard of these problems happening.

-------------------------------
* The bounding radius of small races has been tuned

There is a variable in the code that limits how short things are allowed to become with respect to collision & movement. This value was simply too large, and was preventing gnomes from entering small areas such as the huts in steamfont. I've tuned this down to where it needs to be and so far it appears small races can go where you would expect them to go. Click here for more info.

08/24/2004::EverQuest News
 Tip: Fix Random Video Lag:

A lot of people have noticed what appears to be random video lag since the DirectX 9 engine went live several months ago. Many players, even with very high end systems, are forced to log out and back in or even restart in some cases due to video lag that makes the game unplayable for them. Many others have been unable to "Dual Box" since that patch for the same reasons.

The problem that's causing this is a simple memory leak that Sony either can't find or doesn't care about. This is why most people notice the problem after they've zoned a few times. The better your
system, the longer you can go without noticing it, but eventually it hits everyone at some point.

Thankfully, there's a very simple workaround for this. Simply hold down ALT and hit ENTER at the same time. This is the toggle between windowed and full screen modes. It forces the game to re-allocate resources and re-draw the world and UI due to the screen changing sizes. You can do it twice to maintain whichever way you normally play, but this method will get rid of that "random" video lag that's been happening with the new engine.

Many people in my guild have used this simple workaround to great effect, and are able to play in
zones where they could not before, and are enjoying EverQuest again. If the lag is bugging you, give this a try. Heck, I don't even lag in PoK anymore after using this.
