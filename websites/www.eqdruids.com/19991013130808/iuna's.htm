Introduction

Kiting refers to a collection of battle techniques that allow druids, and some other classes, to kill creatures with magic while avoiding physical combat. Kiting can be a great way to solo and gain XP effectively.

Spells used in Kiting

Burn spells allow deal direct damage to a creature.

DOT spells deal damage to a creature over a set period of time.

Avoid mele spells allow the caster to avoid mele combat with the creature they are kiting.

What should you Kite?

Experience has shown me that the time to kite a tombstone is not signifigantly more than the time to kite a blue creature. However, killing a tombstone creature yields much more XP than killing a blue creature (about 4x as much?) Accordingly, the best creatures to kite are tombstones creatures. I usually will accept gambles, or even evens, if no tombstones are available. This does not apply after level 30, where it is very difficult to gain XP, and thus any kill is a good kill.

Creatures that are very high level will resist your spells. Only experimentation can help you discover what creatures will and will not resist your spells. A general rule of thumb is that a creature will not resist your DOT a lot if it is no more than five levels higher than you.

Creatures with spells, ranged attacks, and creatures that naturally run at SOW speed, are generally poor choices for kiting though they can be kited.

I'd like to update this guide with a level guide stating at what levels various creatures can be first effectively kited. A creature can be effectively kited if it will not resist spells, and it can be somewhat consistently killed in the kiting process. Please mail me (truffle@iname.com) if you can fill in information for this guide, or wish to suggest a new creature that is great for kiting.

Where should you Kite?

An ideal kiting zone is one with large, flat, open spaces. In an ideal kiting zone, you should be able to run backwards without worrying a giant or griffon will start chewing on you. South Karana is an excellent zone to kite in, and I'm sure there are others as well. Please mail me (truffle@iname.com) if you have suggestions on really good zones to kite in.

Good zones I've heard are South Karana, Kithcor Forest, North Karana, Oasis.

Basic Kiting: DOT Kiting

Basic DOT kiting is a very simple, very effective, form of kiting. It is accomplished as follows:

You are SOWed and have full mana.
Cast a Stinging Swarm spell on the creature. You can be facing away from the creature when you do this.
Run from the creature, using your SOW speed to keep out of combat range.
Recast your Swarm spell 60 seconds later if the creature is not dead. Repeat this process until the creature is dead.

Tip: If a creature is very weak, you can finish it off with bolts.

Tip: A clock is very useful for timing your kiting.

Tip: With SOW, you run at 'normal' speed when you run backwards. You can thus run forward for a bit, turn around, and run backwards to keep the creature in sight.

Tip: You can press Num Lock to run forward without having to hold down the forward key.

Tip: Make sure you know where the nearest zone exit is.

Basic Kiting: Snare/Burn Kiting

Since burn gives you less damage for your mana, snare/burn kiting will always require more mana to kill a creature. Why would you snare/burn then? Because snare/burn takes much less time to kill a creature. You can safely snare/burn if you know you have enough mana to kill a creature. This is in particular useful when clearing out blue creatures from a spawn.

Killing the creature quickly gives you more time to meditate. It's unclear whether total downtime is reduced by snare/burn kiting, but it is definitely much much less boring. To snare/burn:

You are SOWed and have full mana.
Cast snare on the creature.
Run back a bit and cast a DOT spell on the creature. You will be fighting long enough for the DOT spell to run its course.
Run back a bit and bolt the creature. Repeat this process until the creature is dead.

Tip: Some creature that do not resist DOT, will consistently resist bolts partially. If you find your bolt is doing half damage fairly often, you should probably stick to DOT kiting with this creature.

Advanced Kiting: Losing Extra Creatures

Sometimes you'll pick up an extra creature while you're kiting. You can get rid of them by:

Targetting them, this requires some running around and clicking, but isn't that hard.
Snaring them from a safe distance (about the same distance you can safely DOT).
Running directly away from them. Most will eventually lose interest, leaving you with your original creature. Watch out, if you run into them again they'll remember you and be pissed off!

Tip: Panic Animal can get rid of an extra animal. You can also use this as a way of kiting animals. DOT them. Panic them. Run after them and beat on them.

Advanced Kiting: Meditating while Kiting

Sometimes you'll be kiting and you'll run out of mana. In this situations you can try snare meditate. I don't recommend this unless you've got basicly full health. This kiting techniques could also perhaps be known as 'deathwish kiting'.

Snare Meditate

Snare Meditate is accomplished as follows:

Snare the creature. Note the time on your clock/watch.
Cast a DOT on the creature.
Track the creature, if you have tracking. This technique is much more effective when you can track.
Run away from the creature for thirty seconds.
Meditate until you notice the creature's health bar change. This is an early warning that it is about to wander up and smack you!
Get up, run away for a bit, snare the creature and start over.

Tip: Some creatures lose interest quickly and run off. If you've been waiting more than 45 seconds and the creature hasn't shown up, go find it.

Tip: Some creatures are hard to snare. If you're at full health, try running for 45 seconds, and then meditating until the creature beats on you. I've used this with avocets.

Tip: Avoid needing this technique by getting +wis and +mana jewelery. In particular +2 wis earings, +2 wis rings, +10 mana bracelets, and the +20 mana Skull of Jen'tra can be fairly easy and cheap to come by (your server milage may vary).

Advanced Kiting: Kiting after 30

After level 30 most creatures have too many hit points to be effectively kited. By teaming up with other players, you can still kite these creatures. These techniques work before level 30 as well.

Try kiting a Hill Giant, or other Giant, by:

Snaring him (this may take several times)
Kiting as normal with DOT
Have a tank friend run up and start attacking him. The giant should keep running after you even though your friend is beating on him.
Continue to snare the giant every minute or so, and cast those DOTs.

This technique may work with other high level creatures as well. You can substitute someone's pet for a tank.

I haven't discovered any other good high level kiting techniques yet, but I'll update the guide when I have.

Fleeing!

There is nothing wrong with fleeing a monster you're having touble handling. When you flee though, flee propertly.

If you're finding it hard to flee, you probably want to slide. Sliding is a techniqe for running faster than you're supposed to be able to. By running diagonally, you run faster than if you're running in a straight line. This is due to the way in which running is implemented in Everquest. To slide:

press num-lock to run forward
hold down Ctrl
hold down left or right.

Sliding will let you outrun a normal-speed creature even if you don't have a SOW. It will let you outrun a SOW-fast creature if you have a SOW. While sliding is really useful, the extra speed is too tiny to be used as a general purpose kiting technique (i.e. as a SOW replacement).

If your SOW is running out, don't panic! Run (or slide) forward until it wears off. Then stop and recast it. Be sure to wait a second so you don't interrupt your spell.

Spirit of the Cheetah lets you run at 2x the speed of a SOW for 45 seconds. It is very fast to cast, but it has a recovery time of several minutes. It can be used to run away to recast your SOW, or just get you out of a nasty situation.

When you zone a creature, be sure to announce you are zoning it. If the creature groups, try and make sure you keep it on you. Running away at SOW speed, leaving a creature in the dust, can result in that creature jumping on someone else. That won't make you any friends.

Kiting for Non Druids

I don't know a lot about this, but I've seen other classes kite. Here are some comments.

Team with a Druid: While most druids solo, if you can team up with a Druid you can kick serious butt. Druid + Necro = super DOT kiting. Druid + Wizard = super snare/burn kiting.

Necromancers: Use your darkness line of spells to slow the creature down. Use your blood line of spells to deal damage. Get SOWed to be safe. The major weakness of necros for kiting as I see it is when extra creatures join in. Get your level 34+ friends to help you farm a pair of journeyman's boots and kick some ass.

Wizards: You should probably just root-burn. Get a SOW so you can run away when root fails.

Bards: Thanks to Jens Anderson (eld@o21a.irrblosset.se) for this info. Bards can kite with chords or bellow. Kiting with chords is hard because it has a short range, but wisps can be kited this way (starting at level 8). To kite a wisp:

Hit it so it goes agro on you.
Run in small circles cycling Selo and Chords until it dies. Use healing song + selo if your life gets low.

At level 12 bards get bellow. Kiting creatures that are normal speed or less is good. To kite these creatures:

Use Selo so you have a bit of speed.
Bellow the creature, and run away, you'll get some distance from it with your Selo.
Eventually it dies.

Shamans: Shamans have SOW, and DOT. They lack snare though, and their DOT while superior seems to come later. They can probably try druidesque kiting, but I haven't seen many shamans kite. Shamans should wait to level 19 where they get their first good DOT, affliction.
