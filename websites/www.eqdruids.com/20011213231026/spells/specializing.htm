Specializing:

At Level 30 the druid gets to specialize in an art of magic.  You may pick any of the 5 magic area to specialize in.  Specializing only does one thing on a successful skill check - reduced post cast mana cost.  The highest skill level is 200.

What this means is if you don't have enough mana to cast a spell (say Evac: Lavastorm) then Specializing does not do anything for you.  However after a successful cast - the roll will determine if the cast takes less mana.

You have to seed 1 point in each skill to get them going.  The first skill that gets to 51 will go to 200...the rest stop at 50.  Make sure the skill you want hits 51 by casting or using some training points.

Alteration - this is very useful for a heavy grouping and backup healer druid...soloing is a little more difficult as nukes will cost full mana

Conjuration - at low levels this is a very poor choice - however by level 52 it is a good choice for the soloer as they will be stacking very high cost Dots

Evocation - this is an excellent choice for the soloer.  Ensnare and Nuke is still one of the best soloing tactics around.

Divination - Not a choice for druids.

Abjuration - a poor choice - even though high level buffs cost a lot - you will not be casting them much in combat except refreshing thorns

You can also switch specialization via a quest in the Temple of Ro.  Take a store bought ruby and 50 gold pieces to Ostorm and it will set your skills all to 49.  Then go train up to 51 in your preferred skill.  Right now you don't need the gold to do the reset.
