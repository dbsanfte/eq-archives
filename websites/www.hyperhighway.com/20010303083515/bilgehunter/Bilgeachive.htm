Bilge News 12/13/99 : I'd like feedback on this Rant - Vendors and EQ
I was thinking tonight about the extra gear I tend to pack around in EQ with me. I call it my "adventurers pack", and I refill it at the end of the night if I use any of the items:

Every single one of these items has been very useful to me while playing EQ. Nothing like uncharming a 46th level Ranger with 2 SSOY's and a Haste Sash while he is wacking on me. Now I figure most of you carry some items at all times such as these, and I'm interested in what they are. Drop me a line, I'd like to see what you carry around.

Now it wouldn't be right to have a nice little intro like that and not have a RANT to follow it so....

I'd like to see Verant put more useful items for sale on Vendors, I have tried all the potions out there, except the over priced GATE, and found most of them WORTHLESS. I have played several MUDs and spent to much time playing UO, and I have yet to see a CRPG have a working economy.

This is what happens: Player A finds something, if its good he either keeps it (which can lead to Hoarding of Items) or sells/trades it to another player. Good items do not go back to vendors. In EQ these items are found again and again, flooding the market with them and decreasing their value.

If the found item is basically worthless it is sold to a vendor, priced up by the vendor, and usually never goes back into the economy. Once bronze plate had value in trade or sale to others, now its given away or sold to vendors.

Players gather huge bank accounts of cash, selling these worthless items, and little of that cash goes back into the Economy.

IMHO Verant should revamp the items sold on vendors, giving us perhaps such options as:

A Weak Clarity Potion
A Better Healing Potion
A SOW Potion
More bags worth a damn, such as the Tinkers Bag
Stones with Charges of Certain Spells:
    such as a stone with 2 casts of Valor

Now I can hear the panic from Druids, Enchanters and Clerics already, "but those are our spells, whine, whine..." but to be honest I'd rather have a tank buy a rock somewhere then have me: sit down, mem Valor, stand, cast, sit, forget valor, mem my stun again, and lose the mana.

Just my 2 copper....

Bilge News 11/29/99 : The Cleric Sit In!
I tossed this idea around a while ago, and as great minds think alike, Norin Grimsbane of the Tribunal Server and Pirmin Nimblefoot of the Karana Server put this event together. I just learned of it. Here are the details:

Thank you all for coming.

You are here most likely because you either support our cause or are curious as to the basis of our cause.

We, the Clerics of all Norrath, feel that an injustice has occured that adversely affects us.  This injustice involves the recent additions into Everquest to replace Rubicite.  When Rubicite armor was removed from the game, all Rubicite wearers recieved replacement armor, that for the most part is obtained through various quests.  This decision to remove Rubicite and to add new and exciting quests to the game was and still is well-supported among most, if not all communities of Everquest.  The idea that a particular class can make itself unique just by the armor it wears adds an interesting aspect to the game of Everquest.  This would under normal circumstances make the game of Everquest even more enjoyable and playable than before.  Although, this has not entirely been the case.

 

When players began to investigate the new quests that were implemented into Everquest, one problem became apparent:  The Cleric-only quest armor was extremely hard to obtain even to the point that the armor became worthless and a waste of good time.  Clerics discovered that two of the items they needed for their quests appeared every seven hours on a tree stump and in a burned down cabin in the now dangerous Kithicor Forest (These two camps were aptly named StumpWatch '99 and CabinWatch '99).  As well the NPCs which carry the items the Clerics need to complete their quests spawn even more rarely.  One NPC spawns every seven hours, but yet has a chance that a place-holder will instead appear meaning that the NPC only has a slight chance of spawing every seven hours.  This continues also as one of the NPCs spawns (and rarely as well) in the near direct path that a band of NPC werewolf hunters wander; given that the NPC the Clerics seek is evil, the werewolf hunters tend to quickly kill the NPC leaving no chance that the Cleric may retrieve the item he is after.  One of the sought-after Cleric items appears on an NPC who happens to drop
two powerful items that many Druids wish to obtain; this results in Clerics competing with Druids for the NPC's loot.  The competition does not end there either, Clerics also share a rare spawn with the Shamans in the quests for the class-specific armors.  The list of difficulties that a Cleric most wade through to obtain thier armor goes on for quite some length. Also, the new Cleric quest armor has a lower armor class than the Rubicite is was supposed to replace.  But to present only the complaints, is to cast the cause in a negative light.

The new armor has been exciting to acquire as it provides a sense of uniqueness to one's character (and adds to the looks as well).  Many Clerics would be thrilled to seek out the NPCs they must find to complete their quests in order to obtain this new armor, if only it were feasible to do such a thing.  The essential problem lies in the fact that the armor is possible to obtain only through many mindless, boring, unenjoyable, and anti-social hours of camping a single spot in all of Everquest.  This leaves one seeking the armor full of despair and hopelessness, and might eventually result in an overall dislike to the game itself. 

The Clerics of Norrath wish to band together and show that they believe all of these problems can be remedied, and that the solutions do exist.  By staging a sit-in, we are opening the opportunity for our interest in this matter to be shown and to hopefully find a reasonable solution.  The Clerics across Norrath wish to see two events occur.  One, the Cleric-only armor quest should have the frequency of certain key NPCs increased as well as making the items more interesting to obtain, and two, the armor class on the armor should be increased to an appropriate level for a plate-wearing class (which Clerics are).  We, as the Clerics of Norrath, hope that all will participate in the Cleric Sit-In to show that they too believe Everquest should be fun and sociable.
