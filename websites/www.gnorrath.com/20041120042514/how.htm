How do I use Heroic Opportunities?

You, or a member of your party must start a heroic opportunity. You start a heroic opportunity by using your class skill (Fighter - Fighting Chance, Scout - Lucky Break, Mage - Arcane Augur, Priest - Divine Providence). Once a starter chain is begun, it can advance by using other skills/spells until you bring up the actuall heroic oportunity. The HO, once completed, will cast a beneficial spell for you or your party.

Special Note:

Heroic Opportunity effects use the level of the encounter engaged to determine their power. So, if used to fight greys the effects will be much lower than against NPCs equal level or higher.

Starter Chains

Starter chains are a sequence of icons that can trigger a heroic opportunity. When completed, heroic opportunities cast a spell effect that benefits the party.

Currently 15 different starter chains exist, one for each combination of archetype professions. The single profession starter chains are as follows:

Fighter: Fighting Chance (Shield Icon)
Scout: Lucky Break (Lock Icon)
Priest: Divine Providence (Druidic Stones Icon)
Mage: Arcane Augur (Tome Icon - looks more like a cube to me)

1.) Open a starter chain
A starter chain appears when you use your class heroic opportunity skill (listed above). When the chain is initiated, the icon of the spell or combart art that began it appears in the center of a circle with possible link icons around it that are blinking. In most cases, if one of the icons blinking is for your class, the skills you have that can advance the heroic opportunity also blink in your hotbar.

2.) Continue the chain
Use a spell or combat art that has one of the link icons associated with it. This responsibility is shared by the entire party. If anyone in the group uses a spell or combat art other than one with a matching icon, then the starter chain is lost and a new one must be started from scratch.

3.) Completing a Starter Chain
Repeat step two until a starter chain is complete. If completed, the starter chain initiates a heroic opportunity from its opportunity list. Currently each starter chain can choose one of three opportunities: common, uncommon, or rare.

4a.) Heroic Opportunity Completion
The party can complete a heroic opportunity by using spells and abilities with icons that match those in he opportunity wheel. Sometimes an opportunity wheel requires the opportunities to be completed in a certain order; other times it does not. If one of your abilities can advance the opportunity wheel, the icon for that spell or combat art will blink.

Using a spell or combat art that doesn't match the next icon in the wheel does not cancel the opportunity; however, it will not advance the opportunity wheel. Each opportunity has a time limit of 30 seconds; if it isn't completed in that time, the opportunity fails.

4b.) Heroic Opportunity Shift
Each opportunity has a special shift icon associated with it: the coin. Scouts may use combat arts with that symbol to shift to another heroic opportunity. If used, it gives you the opportunity to get the common, uncommon, or rare. Sometimes, it just comes back up with the same heroic opportunity you just had. They may do this only once and must use the ability before any others have been used to advance the opportunity wheel.
