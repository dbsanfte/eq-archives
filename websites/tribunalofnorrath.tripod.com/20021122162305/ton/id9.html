Tribunal Guild Policies

All existing members, new and prospective members, please take the time to read these policies. Please realize the policies we establish are always for the betterment and advancement of Tribunal as a whole. If at anytime we feel that any member is in violation of our rules or policies, and doesnt have an acceptable reason for it, it is within the rights of any council member to remove that member or place that member on probation. 

Removing Main Characters from Tribunal
-The loss of high-level main characters is an unfortunate occurrence for any guild. It would be ideal if anyone considering leaving ToN would contact the council and give the guild a chance to address whatever concerns the player has. In some cases changes in guild policy may result to address the issues, or the enforcement of existing policy may occur to solve the problem. In others, more awareness can be raised as to the needs of the individual player and the guild can band together and help that player achieve the goal(s) they have in mind.
-There are situations, however, where a player decides that leaving the guild is in his or her best interest no matter what. The guild always wishes this person well and hopes that there are no hard feelings. And of course, in some situations the council may decide that removing a member may be the only solution to on-going problems.
-The council has recently met to discuss such circumstances and to particularly consider the issue of a character dropping their main character out of ToN while leaving one or more lower alts in the guild.
-The following observations were made:
1. If ToN is not considered by the player to fulfill his/her expectations for the main character, why should they want to leave any alts here?
2. Will the alts of this player ultimately leave ToN when they get higher as well?
3. Is it healthy for the guild's future to allow people to use ToN as a stepping-stone to "uber" guilds?
4. Are other guild members neglected while time is spent helping remaining alts gain in level and equipment when they are likely to eventually leave the guild to join their main?
-The discussion that ensued touched on these topics and others. A new set of rules to address members leaving the guild has been developed to protect the integrity of the guild and insure its growth.
-They are:
A. Any member who chooses to leave the guild (or is disbanded by council) must also remove their alts from the guild.
B. Any non guild player who meets the eligibility requirements to join the guild may do so, even if they have higher characters in other guilds.
C. A member who leaves the guild on good terms and realizes they have chosen foolishly, may be offered an invitation to rejoin ToN if approved by the council. This offer is only made for one return. If the member leaves a second time they are no longer eligible for membership.
D. Members who have removed their main from guild prior to these changes may leave their alts in the guild on the premise that they may have chosen to keep their main in ToN had this rule applied at the time. However, if they chose to remove their highest alt at any time in the future they will be required to remove ALL their alts at that time.
-The council has given these situations a great deal of consideration and believes our decisions have been made in the guilds best interest. We all want to see the guild grow in strength, dedication, and size and having strict policies about such matters will ensure that all members take the quality of our guild seriously.
