Keeper of the Altar Defeated
Sakharra, Thu Oct 14, 2004 6:58 am
Let me preface this by saying that week long lockout timers absolutely suck.

Last week we finalized our strategy and killed the Oracle, we also made our first test run at the Keeper. As we ported out, we absolutely knew that we would complete Ikkinz 4 the very next time we zoned in. There were no doubts, there was little discussion, just a quiet sense of confidence and a fixation on the target.

Like kids waiting out the month of December in eager anticipation of the arrival of Santa and his bag of toys, we put little stars on our calenders and impatiently waited out the 7 days until we could zone in to Ikkinz 4 again. Our eagerness was pretty obvious, we broke all of our speed records on Time (5 hours 45 minutes from P1-Quarm), raced though Yxtta and slaughtered the Primals so fast that the entire raid is just a blur, the rest of our raids this week went with similar speed and efficency. It was as if the entire guild was just trying to get though the rest of the week as fast as possible so we could meet this goal.




The resulting goodies will be posted as soon as I can get the list typed up.
Alter Construct Down ! Keeper of the Altar reached !
Sakharra, Wed Oct 06, 2004 8:08 am
This week marked the return of our regular 5 day a week raiding schedule and we have hit Plane of Time and The Gates of Discord with a vengence. In addition to two clears of Time, we spent time in Ikkinz, Yxtta and hit a number of zones to start working on epic raid targets for our members.

We've been working Ikkinz 4 every time our lockout timer allows us, learning, developing strategies, revising strategies and watching the hard work begin to pay off. The add factory known as the Altar Construct was defeated by ES on Monday night allowing us our first attempt at the Keeper of the Altar whom keeping with the theme, spawns lots of adds. The first attempt was a good one, expect to see a glowing news report with all the gory details of his demise and our entry to Uqua very soon

It only took a couple days of grinding away at the new levels and AAs in Omens for a number of us to start to have serious issues with raiding withdrawl. The lure of fresh, unspoiled, content that we could try for the very first time was finally too much for us. Our scouts ran across something large and ugly named Bone Cracker and it sounded like just the thing to get our "fix" in before we returned to our treadmills for a few more days.

Newbrow squeaked out a few orders (literally squeaked, have you HEARD the gnome voice tells yet?) and we made our first attempt on a raid level OOW named, a few seconds later the score was OOW 29, ES 0. Our second attempt gave us the raiding fix we all needed.
