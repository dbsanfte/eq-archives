Etheios Raid Rules and Guildlines

I. Raid Party Formation:

A. Essential Classes: All raids typically need certain classes to succeed. These essential classes may include clerics, monks,
enchanters or others. Some planes raids need a "port-up" wizard for each group. Hence, the essential classes are selected first,
with priority based on membership, age, and experience. We will go outside the guild for these classes should the Raid Leader
determine such is necessary to ensure a successful raid (up to the maximum 25% guest rule for planar runs, unlimited for dragon
runs).

B. Loot-Specific Classes: Various numbers are chosen from each class for whom a large preponderance of class-specific armor
drops as follows:

1. Hate Raids:

Bard (imbrued)
Cleric (ethereal mist)
Enchanter (insidious)
Mage (apothic)
Rogue (woven shadow)
Shaman (runed etched)
Warrior (indicolite)


2. Fear Raids:

Necromancer (blighted)
Wizard (carmine)
All 4 Pure Casters (cryosilk)
Monk (shiverback)
Ranger (thorny vine)
Shadow Knight (umbral)
Paladin (valorium)
Druid (vermiculated)


3. Air Raids:

All Classes as the Raid Leader determines (quests)

4. Dragon Raids:

All Classes as the Raid Leader determines (various)

5. All Other Zone/Dungeon Raids:

All Classes as the Raid Leader determines (various)

C. Remaining Classes: The remainder are chosen either:

1. entirely randomly, OR

2. based on the following factors in no particular order:

a) Age
b) Class
c) Guild involvement
d) Prior raid involvement or non-involvement
e) Time of sign-up post

D. Minimum Ages:

1. Dragon Raids: 45 for tanks, 47 for casters, 40 for clerics, however, heavy preference will be given to Age in this type of raid to
bolster chances of success while keeping lag to a minimum. Maximum Age for Naggy and Vox is 52.

2. Planes Raids: The minimum age for plane runs is 47.

3. Other Raids: As determined by the Raid Leader.

II. Item Drop Looting
 (physically performed by XP Group Leader or his/her designated Group Looter)

A. Quest Items:

1. When a quest item drops, the people who can use the item to complete the quest get to roll on it.

a) The winner gets looting rights to that item but only for the purpose of completing their quest.

b) They cannot sell, trade or bequest the looting rights off.

c) The item is not theirs at this stage, it still belongs to the entire raid party.

d) If the high roller will not use the item for his/her quest then the looting rights are passed on to the next highest roller.

e) If none of the people who actually need the item for a quest will use it then the entire raid party gets to roll on it (a seperate roll
from the initial roll) and the winner of the roll can sell off the looting rights if so desired. At this point the quest item is effectively
owned by the winner and is no longer the property of the raiding party.

f) Note that raiders closest to completion of a given quest may be given priority on a quest item drop at the descretion of the Raid
Leader.

g) All quest gems that drop will be handed over to the guild gem mule. Gems will be distributed on a first-come first-serve basis,
the only requirement is that the asking member has the matching armor piece first.

2. Winners of Quest Items do not lose or forgo their right to random roll for a class-specific or non-class-specific loot item.

B. Gem Drops:

After the raid is paid for, the Raid Leader will include the rest of the acquired gems as loot. These gems will be randomed off at the
end of the raid , up to three at a time, to the raid members who did not get a loot item, quest or otherwise, on that raid.

C. Coinage Drops:

1. Coin collected by each Group Looter will be split amongst the Group at the end of the raid.

D. Class Specific Armor and Weapons:

1. Randoms: Randomed among the appropriate class who are on the raid, subject to the One-Per-Raider rule below.

2. Pre-Possession of No Drop Items: If the item is no drop and you already possess the item, you do not roll for it (obviously).

3. Default winners:

a) If you have not won an item (class-specific or otherwise), perhaps by choice or due to the scarcity of the remaining class items
you lack, and
b) If all others of your class have won one or more items (class-specific or otherwise) on the raid, and
c) Should a class-specific item you lack then drop, the item will default to you or be randomed between you and any other
class-specific raider in a similar situation.

4. Passing: Passing on random rolls is allowed.

E. Non-Class-Specific Loot Items:

1. The classes that can use the item get to roll for it, subject to the One-Per-Raider rule below and subject to the Raid Leader's
decision on whether certain classes can get greater benefit from the item than others.

For example, a Cloak of Flames is ALL ALL regarding race and class, yet obviously this item should be randomed amongst the melee
classes for it's Haste effect.

2. The Raid Leader will have the final say in who rolls for non-class-specific items.

F. Un-Useable and/or Un-Wanted Items:

1. Should a class specific, tradable item be dropped in the absence of any raiders of that particular class, or, should a
non-class-specific, tradable item drop that is un-wanted by all in attendance, the item will,

a) default to the Raid Leader as a "sellable item" to further defray the costs of the current or future raids, or,

b) be randomed off to some or all in attendance at the Raid Leader's descretion, or,

c) be saved to present to a guild member that can use the item at the Raid Leader's descretion

G. One Per Raider Rule: No more than one item, excluding quest items, can be won on a raid until, all raiders in attendance,

1. of your specific class have won an item in the case of class specific item drops, or,

2. of all classes have won an item of any non-quest type.

3. The rotation will begin anew for your class for class-specific items once Rule II.G.1. has been met, and for all classes for
non-class-specific items once Rule II.G.2. has been met.

H. Compensation:

1. All clerics (and other qualifying classes) will be compensated for reagents used during a raid of more than two groups. It will the
the raid leader's (or assistant) responsiblity to see that this occurs. It will be the cleric's responsiblity to account for monies spent
on reagents during the course of a raid.

III. General Raid Rules:

Time of Raid:

All participants and alternates MUST be at the raid entry point AT LEAST 30 Minutes before the scheduled raid time. Transport to
the raid site after the raid has begun will be the responsibility of the player. TP'ers will not be asked to leave a raid in progress to
pick up someone who is late.

Bind Point:

All participants must be bound either at the zone/port-in point or at the nearest city for non-binding classes. Remember – Greater
Fay is a ‘city zone’, so please bind at the Wizard Spires.

Stand By:

Anyone who is not at the raid entry point by 15 minutes before the scheduled raid time may be replaced by any alternate at the
sole discretion of the Raid Leader.

Minimum Time Commitment:

Do not attend if you cannot commit to be present for the entire raid, or for at least eight ( hours on weekends (Fri-Sun) or five (5)
hours on weekdays (Mon-Thur) for plane raids.

Summon Corpse:

Recommend all raiders except Monks possess one coffin (2 slot container) in their inventory. Alternative Suggestion –leave a corpse
with a coffin on it at the zone in point, or give one to a guild mule.

NO SPAM:

Do not use overuse emotes while on the raid.

Do not use the ooc or say channels (except as appropriate for raid communications only) or whichever channels the Raid Leader
designates.

If you feel the need to chit chat, do so in group only.

Use of the auction channel will be discouraged, auctioning items without prior permission from the raid leader will be grounds for
immediate removal from the raid.

Preparation:

Print and read the appropriate guides and maps for the raid you plan to attend, and make sure your questions are answered before
the raid. There isn’t time to ask them during the raid!

Communications:

Raid Leader: Do not pester the raid leader during a raid – funnel through your group leader as much as possible. Odds are the raid
leader is in tell hell while simultaneously trying to follow three channels and a lot of battle spam. Group Leaders may send direct tells
to the raid leader, but use sparingly and only for emergencies. Announce link deaths in OOC only.

Main Puller: Do not pester the Main Puller during a raid and never send the Main Puller a tell or any other message to pull a specific
mob because that mob drops something you'd like to have.

Main Assist: Do not pester the Main Assist during a raid.

Task Assignments:

The Raid Leader may assign certain tasks to individual raiders before or during the raid at his/her sole descrection.

Each Group Leader may assign certain tasks to individual raiders within his/her group before or during the raid at his/her sole
descrection, so long as it does not conflict with Raid Leader assignments.

Hot Key Assists:

Be sure to set up hot keys to assist the Main and Secondary tanks. Only switch to the Secondary assist if the Main assist is dead or
incapacitated and as the Raid Leader instructs.

Post-Raid Problem Resolution:

Please funnel any problems you may experience with a raid through your guild officers so we may discuss possible solutions.
