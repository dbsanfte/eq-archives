Fletching Guide

Hello, I am Quarthinos, the Peacekeeper's resident fletcher. This document is still mostly notes, and a lot of the information in the tables is based on memory. When I get done saving for my combine long sword, I'll be able to spend the money to confirm this information.

First of all, you need a fletching kit, which should cost about a platinum. Don't bother buying the books on arrow making or bow making, because I go over them below. I would also suggest you spend as many skill points as possible in your guild, rather than practicing. I started practicing the cheapest arrows until I was making them about 3/4 of the time, then I moved on to the next arrow. If you practice long enough, you will be told that it is a trivial task for one of your skill level. At that point, you definitately need to make a new arrow, because your skill will not be improved any more. I did not take notes on what skill level I was making each arrow at, so you'll have experiment. I know that currently (level 55), I can make hickory bows and all wood shaft arrows trivially. I can make simple elm bows trivially, but am 0/1 working with tools on an elm bowstaff.

The books give the following directions: To make arrows, you will need to buy a nock, a shaft, a point, and a fletch from a vendor. Put them in the kit, <combine> them and you'll either get an arrow, or nothing. Bows are a bit more complex. The simplest bows are simply a bowstaff and some twine/string. More complex bows will include a whittling knife or a planing tool, and REALLY complex bows get a cam (maybe several?) to make a compound bow. And I suppose you could use all four to get something that really kicks, but that will have to wait until I am MUCH higher in level.

It should be noted that vendors sell either arrow or bow parts, not both. In Surefall glade, you can find both an arrowmaker (at the archery range) and a bow maker (inside the ranger guildhouse). I haven't found either in Freeport. In Qeynos there is a person who sells finished items near the clock, but I haven't found anyone selling components. And if you happen to be travelling on Faydwer, an arrowmaker can be found outside the Ranger guild (Faydark's Champions), and a bowyer can be found outside Trueshot's bows.

Arrows

All the values under effect modify a mystical "empty" arrow that has 0 damage and 0 range. Thus, a Field Point Arrowhead, a Wooden Shaft, a Large Nock, and a Round Cut Fletch would give an arrow with 1 damage and 50 range. Such an arrow would be named Class 1 Wood Point Arrow (the nock in an arrow makes no difference to the name).

At skill level 93, I am still making hooked bone arrows for practice. I have not yet tried making bladed or steel shaft arrows, so those effects are guesses. I can make "flight arrows" (1 damage 150 range) for cheaper than a vendor will sell them to me, but cannot sell them to vendors for a profit. Because of this, I don't see any reason to make arrows other than for practice. However a Class 3 Steel Arrow (small nock, steel shaft, bladed arrow head, and shield cut fletch) might be able to kill a (small) monster in one shot from a good bow. Such an arrow would cost almost 2 platinum (unless I can get a smith to make me the parts?) for stats around 7 damage range 50.

I haven't bothered fiddling with the different string types since I discovered they decrease damage by more than 10% and give a 6% decrease in delay. Since more than one shot is questionable w/o huge ranges (but really good arrows and bows might provide this), I don't see the point. I have made one attempt at using a whittling knife on an elm bow, but did not meet with success. I have also tried using silk string several times, again with no success. I have been told that you cannot use any tools on hickory, and cannot use a plane on elm, but have not tested this.

The going price seems to be about 8 or 9 gold for a hickory bow, and 4 to 4.5 platinum for an elm bow. I have seen one bowyer selling an ashwood bow for 25 platinum, but have no idea if he sold it for that price.
