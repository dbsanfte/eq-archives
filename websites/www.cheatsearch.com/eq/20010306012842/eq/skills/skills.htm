Trade Skills

Trade Skills allow you to construct useful items from raw materials. The final products can help in the career/class of the character or provide a secondary source of income. Below is a list of all the trade skills offered and detailed information on how and why to choose that skill.

It takes a high level of skill in the field chosen in order to make items of great value with any reasonable chance of success.

Skill Items Required Description
Alchemy Medicine Bag Allows Shamans of 25th level and above to concoct magical potions.
Baking Oven Allows anyone to bake a wide variety of foods.
Brewing Still Allows anyone to produce alcoholic beverages and items for use by other trade skills.
Fletching Fletching Kit Allows anyone to construct bows and arrows.
Foraging None Allows Rangers, Druids, Iksar and Wood Elves to find food and water in the wilds.
Fishing Fishing pole and Bait Allows anyone to pull fish from larger bodies of water and rivers.
Jewel Craft Jewelry Kit Allows anyone to fashion magical jewelry from gems and enchanted metals. When combined with spells from a high level Enchanter, magical rings and neclasses can be created (both the Jeweler and the enchanter must be above 8th level)
Poison Mixing Venom Sacks Allows Rogues to create poison.
Pottery Pottery Wheel and Kiln Pottery allows anyone to craft clay into pottery. Some examples of end products are a Clay Jar, Ceramic Lining, Small Bowl, Mixing Bowl and Poison Vials.
Research Class-specific Tome Permits spell casters to research and create new spells from component pieces.
Smithing Forge Allows anyone to manufacture metal armor, weapons and utility items.
Tailoring Tailoring Kit Allows anyone to create leather items from hides and pelts. Items include backpacks, leather armor and silk armor.
Tinkering Toolkit Allows Gnomes to create mechanical items. For example: collapsible fishing pole, Gnomish Fireworks and spyglass.
