Debbie's Books Pens A Tale Of Success

Assistant Property Manager wasn't exactly the career of Debbie Goodrich's dreams. In fact, the long hours were inhibiting her from pursuing her real passion - opening her own home-based business.

In January 2001, after 4 years in property management, she bought a franchise license for a Create-A-Book dealership and launched Debbie's Books. Her business offers customers the chance to create personalized books. "The most popular book is the Baby's Create-A-Book," she says. Though she specializes in children's books, there are titles suitable for kids of all ages. Debbie got her business license, setup a bookkeeping system, got her startup inventory and began work on her Web site. "My goal was to be up and running by March 2001," she says.

Now, just a few months later, both Debbie's Books and her Web site, http://www.debbiesbooks.net, are taking off. She met her March deadline and now enjoys running her business. "I like the fact that I am my own boss. I decide what gets done and how. I enjoy meeting people and sharing information about my business because I absolutely love doing it!" She creates each unique book herself, "I can actually take my laptop and printer onsite and create books while my customers watch."

With marketing strategies limited only by her imagination, Debbie knew a Web site had to be part of her plan of attack. "The most important marketing tool to be developed in recent years has been the Internet. Having your own Web site is one of the most important tools you can use to build your own business. I wanted to be able to hand people my business cards and brochures with my Web site address on it. It makes you appear more professional and serious about your business."

Although Debbie had been using the Internet for years, she hadn't considered creating her own site until she started her business. Someone on a message board recommended BizLand. She began researching SiteDelux, BizLand's simple, template-based Web site building tool, and found out she wasn't going to have to learn HTML to create her Web site. "I was very impressed with SiteDelux," she says. "It is easy to learn to use and you can change (your site) very easily."

BizLand's community also helped ease the learning curve. "The people involved with the message boards offered a lot of advice and insight into what is involved with Web sites and online businesses. They told me many things I had no idea about. They really helped me a lot."

The prospects for Debbie's Books look great both online and off. Debbie recently joined her local Chamber of Commerce and is working towards contracts with some local hospitals and schools. She has also been running home book fairs. As for her online branch, "It generates a customer base like no other form of advertising," she says.
