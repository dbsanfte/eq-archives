MOBS YOU WILL ENCOUNTER

Amygdalan Knights
The Amygdalan Knight is a Shadowknight as the name implies. They come complete with useless (although annoying) pets. They drop Umbral Platemail armor (Shadowknight only). Their special skills are Harm Touch and Mana Drain. These can usually be seen guarding the temples of Fright, Dread, and Terror.

Amygdalan Warriors
These look just like the Amygdalan Knight without the ability to harm touch or make a pet. This means they are mana suckers because they are warriors.  They have a much higher magic resistance than most things in the zone. They are extremely common and you'll be fighting a lot of these. They drop a warrior only shield and whip. They are found wandering the zone or protecting the main temple of Cazic Thule.

Boogeymen
Looks like a huge Ghoul. They drop Blighted armor (Necromancer only). Their special skill is Ghoulroot.

Cazic Thule
Cazic is a God. Cazic is a vengeful God. Cazic does not like people. Cazic does not like you.  Cazic doesn't like ANYONE!  In general, he has several irritating quirks. First, he hits for OVER 400 and double attacks. Second, he uses gravity flux like there is no tomorrow.  Every time you are fluxed, it removes one buff (put JBoots in top buff slot and reactivate after every flux). Third, Cazic has pets. Not a typical type PET, but plural PETS, and they are fairly irritating, although nothing to lose sleep over.  His special skills are Gravity Flux, mean melee skills, and debuffing anything in his way.

Dracoliche
This looks like a huge Ashenbone Drake from Plane of Hate.  If you have never seen one of those, you have never seen anything that looks like a Dracoliche.  It looks like Vox died, decomposed, then returned to life with just its bones. This is Cazic Thule’s number one bodyguard. This bone dragon is a shadowknight with a pet. Attacking Dracoliche ALWAYS aggros Cazic himself, and throughout the battle with Draco, Cazic WILL be death-touching constantly, as well as sending in reinforcements.  He has a mean disease based DOT and dragon roar (area effect Fear).  Leave it to Verant to make a bone dragon, call it a liche, and NOT make it undead!  After killing Draco, everyone will have to log out, wait 3 minutes and log back in unless other specified by the raid leader to clear Cazic's Hate List!.  Draco spawns once a week (real time).  It's special skills are a DoT and dragon roar.

Dread, Fright, & Terror
Cazic Thule’s little buddies that have temples located around the zone. You know those UGLY Golems in CoM?  That is what these look like, only BIGGER and MEANER!  Fighting any one of these will also aggro Cazic.  Their special skill is usually Blind and DT & HT!  If one of these Killers yell your name, wave bye bye and... LOADING, PLEASE WAIT!

Fetid Fiends (Male)
Resembles a huge zombie. They drop Valorium armor (Paladin only). Their special skill is Disease.

Fetid Fiends (Female)
Resembles a huge female zombie. They drop a girdle that's quite good for Clerics. They have no special skills.

Frightfingers
A small reanimated hand. They drop Carmine armor (Wizard only). Their special skill is wizard spells, which they cast a lot of.

Glare Lords
A small version of an evil eye. Glare Lords are magicians. They come with mage pets and drop wands for Mages and Enchanters. Their special skill is an area effect debuff which is quite nasty. You could fight just one of these things and find ten buffs are gone by the end of the fight.

Gorgons
Gorgons resemble Harpies from Steamfont Mountains, but with snakes coming out of their heads. Gorgons drop Vermiculated armor (Druid only). Their special skill is a 15 second stun which can get pretty nasty if there's a couple of them on you.  Having a high magic resistance for these things is pretty essential.

Nightmares
A big black horsie!  BUT!  Squeaks like a rusty swing.  They drop a cloak that is not that great. Their special skill is Blind and have been known to Fear as well.

Phantasms
These look like spectres.  They drop nothing of great interest and have no special skills.

Phoboplasms
These resemble gelatinous cubes, like the ones in Skyshrine. They are located in and around the temple of Cazic Thule. These are loved by all because they can randomly drop any piece of planar armor in the game, including armor for classes such as rogues, bards, clerics, and warriors which is normally found in the Plane of Hate. There are conflicting reports as to whether they can truly drop ANY piece or just 1 piece from each class (i.e. Cleric Boots, Bard Bracer, Paladin Bracer, Warrior Gauntlets, etc.). Their special skill is an area effect debuff, but whereas the Glare Lords and Tentacle Tormentors debuff four buffs at a time, these only debuff 1 buff at a time.

Samhains
Happy Halloween!  A jack-o-lantern on a scarecrow. These drop a ranger only axe. They have no special skills.

Scarelings
These little things look like the imps from Sol B Efreeti area. They are rogues and backstab for 400+. These are VERY deadly and always the first target when one is in the camp. Another deadly tactic of the Scareling is to teleport anyone to a random location, (not too far away), potentially starting a massive train leading to a total wipe out of the raiding party. This is why they are given the highest priority. They are also very hard to target and see because of their size. They also run faster than SoW, making kiting them is pretty hard. Scarelings must die, and die fast. They drop a rogue-only rapier. Their special skill is Backstab and Teleport.

Shiverbacks
Looks like a big ape, hits hard and fast. They drop Shiverback armor (Monk only). They have no special skills.

Spinechiller Spiders
Spiders, ICK! They drop Cryosilk armor (pure casters). Their special skill is a relatively tame cold based DoT spell that hits for 300 in 30 ticks.

Tentacle Tormentors
Tormentors resemble their weaker cousins from Unrest and Najena. Chain stunning these guys is a great idea - it keeps them from using their special skills. They drop Thorny Vine armor (Ranger only) as well as a druid based belt. Their special skills are Lifetap, Throwback, and an area affect debuff. Like the Glare Lords, they're going to wipe out a lot of your buffs. Their knockback is quite nasty as well. If you're fighting them with a camp against a zone wall, fight them with your backs to the zone wall so that if you get knocked back, you don't go far and can return to the fight quickly.

Turmoil Toads
Resemble small Froglock ghouls that can be found in Lower Guk. Toads drop Turmoil Warts and a belt for Shamans. Their special skill is Fear. This is why you want to have SoW and Run OFF when attacking these guys. If they fear you and you start to run away from them quickly, you could run far enough to start aggroing other stuff.

Worry Wraiths
Giant will-o-wisp with a pet. They need to be dispelled.  They have a damage shield that can cause many people to go LD from the amount of "You have been burned" messages. They drop nothing of great interest and have no special skills.

THE MOST DANGEROUS ENEMY
The most fearsome of all of the enemies in Plane of Fear...

Someone who does NOT follow the rules and listen to the Raid Leader.  

This enemy is amongst you.  He could even be grouped with you.  He could be you.  This enemy is fearsome because of his ability to kill 50+ people within 3 minutes. His special skills are Train Making.  Beware Him THE MOST!
