Tips

 

Actions - Melee

  • To create a new melee skills button, simply right-click on the button once. A window will pop up showing you the list of skills you can choose from.

  • To use one of these melee skills, click on the button with the skill name.

  • To place one of these skills onto a hotbutton, click and hold on the button. When the button appears on the cursor, click on the hotbutton box that you want to place the skill in.

  • Some skills show up the list for Skills and for Melee, in those cases you may want create a button on the tab where you have the least skills.

  • Melee Skills:  In order to learn a new combat skill, you must obtain a skill tome. Some of these can be purchased from your guild master, others must be discovered. Once you receive a tome, follow these steps to learn your new ability:

    • Open your inventory by pressing [I] the key on your keyboard.

    • Click on the tome in your inventory so that it is placed on your mouse cursor.

    • Move your cursor with the tome still on it over to your guildmaster. Click on him/her.

    • Now click the [give] button near the bottom of the trade window.

    • You should now see a message in your chat window that says "Use your new skill wisely".

    • Press [ALT-C] on your keyboard to bring up the Combat Abilities window.

    • Click on the [S] located near the top right corner of the Combat Abilities window. This will open the Combat Skills window.

    • Your new skill should now be displayed in the window. Click on the name of the skill once, then look at the bottom of the window.  You should see a Make Hotkey button. Clicking on this will allow you to make a hotkey for your new skill.

    • Place the new hotkey in one of the empty hotkey slots of the Combat Abilities Window.

Actions - Skills

  • To create a new skills button, simply right-click on the button once. A window will pop up showing you the list of skills you can choose from.

  • To use one of these skills, click on the button with the skill name.

  • To place one of these skills onto a hotbutton, click and hold on the button. When the button appears on the cursor, click on the hotbutton box that you want to place the skill in.

  • Some skills show up the list for Skills and for Melee, in those cases you may want create a button on the tab where you have the least skills.

Adventure

The Wayfarers Brotherhood have many interesting adventures for you. You can find them in North Ro, South Ro, Everfrost Peaks, the Butcherblock Mountains and East Commons. To get an adventure, form a group and seek out the Wayfarers. The group leader must Right-click on the Adventure Recruiter or the Raid Recruiter to be given an adventure.  These adventures take place in dungeon "instances". This means that a copy of the dungeon is created just for your group, and no others can enter it. You will have a limited time to complete your task, so keep moving. Here are some other tips about the adventure window.

  • Only the group or raid leader can start an adventure for your group or raid.

  • Your group or raid must be complete before the group or raid leader accepts the adventure. Only characters in your group or raid when the adventure is requested will be able to enter the dungeon.

  • If you decline an adventure, you will have to wait a short while before another will become available.

  • The group or raid leader can choose a Normal or Hard risk for the adventure. If the group or raid leader chooses Hard the adventure will be more difficult but will also yield greater reward.

  • Once you accept an adventure, a waypoint represented by a blue line will appear on your compass to help guide you to the entrance of the dungeon when you are in the appropriate zone.

  • The Time to Enter shows you how long you have to locate and enter the dungeon portal. If you do not enter the portal before the timer expires, your adventure will end.

  • Once you enter, the Time to Complete shows you how long you have to reach your objective. If you do not complete your adventure by then, the adventure will close down.

  • If at any point you leave, complete, or fail your adventure you can still enter the dungeon and retrieve your corpse, as long as the dungeon is still open.

  • Any time that a dungeon instance shuts down, all PC corpses and any living PCs will be placed outside the dungeon at the portal used to enter the dungeon.

  • The View Stats button will show you your adventure statistics, and from the Stats window you can access the adventure Leaderboard.

  • Use the Looking For Group tool (/lfg) to find players in your level range to adventure with!

  • Take advantage of the /friend list after a good hunt, perhaps you can find those same people to group with at another time.

  • Once you "Accept" and adventure, no new members may be added to your group.  So make certain that you have all of your group members in the group before you visit the Wayfarers Brotherhood to get your adventure.

Adventure Leaderboard

  • Your rank on the selected leader board will always display at the bottom of the window.

  • Use the category dropdown box at the top to choose which leaderboard you want to see. You may see those that have the most wins over all or for each theme. You may also list those with the best win percentage (ratio of wins to attempts) over all or for each theme.

  • Choosing a new category listing refreshes the list.

  • There is a short delay between category changes and uses of the Refresh button.

  • You must have attempted at least 25 adventures to qualify for a win percentage ranking.

Adventure Merchant

  • Any item you can afford to purchase will be displayed in the window to the right. The list will also show items that cost up to 50% more than the points you have available. Such items will be greyed out because you cannot yet afford them.

  • You may wish to save your points. The more points you have to spend the more items you will see listed.

  • You can sort the item list by clicking on any of the headers (to sort by Price, just click on the blue Price title at the top of the column).

  • Some items can only be purchased if you have at some time earned enough points in a certain theme. Those will be noted in the Theme column. For example, once you have earned 50 points in Deepest Guk you will be forever allowed to purchase 50 point Guk items, as long as you currently have enough points for that purchase.

  • To purchase an item, just click on its name in the list window and click the Purchase button.

Adventure Stats

  • The first Rank column shows your rank among all the players on your server based only on your number of successes.

  • The second Rank column shows your rank among all the players on your server based on your success percentage (successes compared to failures).

  • The Total Points column shows how many points you have ever earned in the specific themes.

  • The Adventure Points Available listing at the bottom shows how many points you have not yet spent.

  • Refresh updates the list, but there is a delay between uses.

  • You must have attempted at least 25 adventures to qualify for a win percentage ranking.

Alternate Advancement

  • These abilities are only available to characters of level 51 and over (this also requires the purchase of The Shadows of Luclin or The Planes of Power).

  • To purchase Alternate Advancement (AA) abilities, your character will need to earn Alternate Advancement experience by setting the Exp. to AA slider to some number above 0. This indicates the amount of future earned experience that goes toward earning AA points.

Augmentation

  • Augmentation Items can be found in Lost Dungeon of Norrath zones and for sale from the Adventure Merchants.

  • The Augmentation Items can be used to improve items and equipment that show open slots. An item can have up to three slots available for Augmentation Items. The open slots are named Slot 1, Slot 2, Slot 3 which can be seen when you examine an item. Upon examination, each slot will show that it can accept a specific Augmentation Item type. The Augmentation Item shows which type it is when you examine it.

  • Augmentation Items are categorized by types and often carry restrictions for certain equipment slots (i.e. neck, waist, feet pieces) and slot types available on your item.

  • To apply an Augmentation Item to a slot on a piece of equipment, you will need to use an Augmentation Sealer, which can be found at each Wayfarers Brotherhood camp. Place the Augmentation Item and the compatible piece of equipment into the open slots of the Augmentation Sealer and click "Combine." Your new augmented item will be created!

  • If you wish to remove the Augmentation Item, you can buy solvents from one of the magi at each of the five Wayfarers Brotherhood camps. There is a solvent for each of the three slot types. For example, a Slot 1 Solvent will remove the Augmentation Item from Slot 1 of your equipment. Take your item with the Augmentation Item you wish to remove and the appropriate solvent and put them into the Augmentation Sealer. Click "Combine." The Augmentation Item will be removed and destroyed. Be careful not to misuse your Augmentation Items!

Augmentation Sealer

  • To apply an Augmentation Item to a slot on a piece of equipment, you will need to use the Augmentation Sealer. There is one at each Adventure camp. Place the Augmentation Item and the compatible piece of equipment into the open slots of the Augmentation Sealer and click "Combine." Your new augmented item will be created!

  • If you wish to remove the Augmentation Item, you can buy solvents at the Adventure Merchants, one for each of the three slot types available on an item. For example, a Slot 1 Solvent will remove the Augmentation Item from Slot 1 of your equipment. Take your item with the Augmentation Item you wish to remove and the appropriate solvent and put them into the Augmentation Sealer. Click "Combine." The Augmentation Item will be removed and destroyed. So, be careful not to misuse your Augmentation Items!

Bank

  • To see the name of an item, either in your inventory or in the bank, just point your mouse cursor at the item.

  • To see the names of all items in your bank, place your mouse cursor in the bank's inventory window and hold down the ALT key.

  • All banks in EverQuest are linked. So you can safely place your goods and cash in the bank in Qeynos and retrieve it all in Freeport when you arrive.

  • The banker will change your coins for you. If you take 20 copper pieces and drop them into the bank window where it indicates silver pieces are stored, the banker will automatically change your 20 copper into 2 silver.

  • Click on the "Change" button to convert all your coin, both in the bank and on your person, to the fewest possible coins.

  • Clicking on the banker with an item on your cursor is giving that item to the banker, not storing it in your bank. So make sure to open the bank window and place the items in the bank.

Bug

  • If you notice something that does not seem right, use /bug to report as many details as possible so that we may look at it. If you are in danger, use /petition as well for help (but please still use /bug afterwards to report the bug).

Consent

Someone has just given you consent to drag their corpse.

  • You can now move their corpse using the /corpse command. Just target the corpse and type /corpse and if you are close enough, the corpse will be dragged to you.

  • Use the Social. There is a Corpse social in your action window. Using this can make dragging corpses easier.

  • To give someone consent on you own corpse, type /consent {player name}.

  • Be careful who you consent. It's easy for someone to place your corpse in a hard to recover location.

  • Be polite when given consent. You are playing in a very social environment and if you do something mean to someone, like dragging their corpse to a bad spot, you can harm your reputation.

Consider

You have just tried to attack an NPC which is probably too difficult for you to defeat. You can take a close look at another character or NPC by typing /con with that character targeted or by right clicking on them.  The chat window then displays the NPC's attitude toward you and how difficult a battle you can expect. The /con message is red when you consider this NPC, so you should be very cautious.

 

Convenience

  • Hotbuttons are a handy way to quickly access abilities and skills.  To create a hotbutton, simply left-click and hold on a skill button, inventory slot, social or spell gem.  That item will appear on your cursor.  Just move it over to one of your open hotbutton spots and click to place it there.  Then you can just press the corresponding number key to activate that skill or spell.

  • It is a good idea to know your location before you die.  Noting landmarks is helpful.  But one of the best ways to do this it to try and get a /loc when you die.  This should make your corpse easier to find later.  You can even make /loc into a hotbutton so you can hit it quickly when needed.

  • Shift + 1, Shift + 2 etc... will switch between Hotbutton pages

  • You can drag any spell gem from your memorized spell list into your hotbank and then use the number keys 0-9 to cast that spell.

  • There are several ways to quickly talk to another character.  /ttell <text> - sends the text as a tell to the character targeted (this also works if you target a PCs corpse).  "r" - hitting the "r" key will Reply to the last person to send you a tell.

  • You can immediately stop the casting of a spell by hitting the "d" key (this makes you duck).  You should hit it twice, when you duck you stay in a ducked position until you hit "d" again.

  • If you own The Legacy of Ykesha, press backspace to bring up your map.  Use the map to navigate through unknown territory or find specific NPCs and landmarks.

  • If you think you may be able to get your corpse resurrected, take care not to remove all items from your corpse or it will disappear and can't be resurrected.

  • If you submitted a petition and have not had a response yet, do check to see if your petition is still waiting to be answered by /viewpetition   If it is no longer there, please resubmit your issue.   If you have a petition in the queue but you have resolved your issue before a GM or Guide has contacted you, please use /deletepetition so that the process will speed up for your fellow players.

  • When you create a character, be sure to note the name of the server you're on.  Characters only exist on one of the servers and to find that character again you will need to log in to the correct server.

  • To read your guild's Message of the Day, just type /guildmotd. If you are the leader or an officer, you can set the guild Message of the Day by typing /guildmotd <text>, where <text> is the message you want people to see when they log in.

  • The Bazaar can be a good place to meet other players and bargain for more potent equipment.  You'll need to own the Shadows of Luclin expansion in order to access the Bazaar.

  • You can join your favorite chat channels automatically when you log in by typing /autojoin [channel]. To remove an autojoined channel, simply delete the line from your eqclient.ini file.

  • Make sure to change your copper and silver into gold and platinum every time you use the bank.  Click on the money you wish to change, select the amount, and move the coin to the type you wish to make.  It will save you a great deal on weight.

Death

Unfortunately, you have just died. The good news is that if your character is level 9 or under, you have revived with all of your items and with no experience loss. This will not be the case after you reach 10th level. At that time you will need to recover your items from your corpse, which will be in the spot where you were killed. You will also lose a small amount of experience as a result of dying.

  • To help you find your corpse, if you have time, type /loc to get your location before you die.

  • To help you find your corpse, Bards, Necromancer and Shadowknights have spells that locate and even summon corpses. It's a good idea to offer a reward for such services, even if you have to get that reward off your corpse.

Display Options

  • The Far Clip Plane determines how far you can see. Obviously, the farther you can see, the more work your computer has to do to draw everything. Reducing the Far Clip Plane can help improve your framerate (how quickly things are drawn on your screen).

  • The Near Clip Plane only relates to spell particles. It determines how close to your viewpoint particles can be seen. Near means that particles can appear very close to the camera (your viewpoint). Farthest would mean that particles that might appear near your point of view will not be drawn. Pushing the Near Clip Plane out can improve your framerate, but you will see fewer spell effects.

Dueling

You have been challenged to a duel.

  • To decline the duel, type /decline.

  • To accept the duel, target your opponent and type /duel.

  • You should feel no obligation to duel with anyone.

  • Dueling shouldn't cause any loss of experience and does not allow any looting of items.

Dye

Dyeing is available to those that own The Legacy of Ykesha extension. If you do not own the extension you will not be able to save any of the dyes, but you will be able to preview them.

  • Dyes affect the location, not the armor piece. The dye will affect any armor piece in the dyed location, and will not stay with the armor piece.

  • You can preview the dye before selecting a final color. When you click the Dye button your view will change so that your character is displayed in the center of your screen.

  • You can change the colors as often as you want. They are not final until you click the Save Dye button.

  • You may need to close your inventory window ("i"), it may block your view of the dye preview.

  • You might want to jot down the colors of any existing dye before changing them, you may want to change it back to its old color later.

Etiquette

  • Be polite when asking other players for assistance, they will appreciate it and be more likely to help you.

  • Don't loot everything that your group kills.  Everyone in the group has earned a bit of the reward, so try to make certain that everyone gets a fair share.

  • You can use the /report {text} feature to send information to us.  /report captures the last ten lines visible in your chat window and send them to us.  The {text} parameter should be treated like a topic.  For example, if someone is verbally harassing you, scroll up your chat window so that you can see the offensive text and type /report <character name> and hit enter.  If you are reporting on bad behavior or a bug that you need help with, you should follow up the /report with a /petition {detailed text} so that we will be aware of your need.

  • Please remember to treat your fellow players with respect.  EverQuest is a social game, and the people you meet today may be your guild mates tomorrow.

  • Please do not petition for hints about the game.  If you need a hint, your best bet would be to nicely ask a fellow adventurer.

Expedition

The term "Expedition" refers to unique instances of zones that are created specifically for your group or raid. Once the expedition has been created, players can be added and removed at will. There are other options available for expeditions, as shown below.

 

Expedition Interface

The expedition interface contains specific information about the dynamic zone that is assigned to you, as follows:

  • Assigned Expedition: Shows the expedition assigned to you.

  • Leader: The name of your expedition leader.

  • Members: Lists all members that are assigned to the expedition.

  • Number of Players: Lists the number of players in the expedition.

  • Max Players: The maximum number of players that can take part in the expedition.

  • Quit Expedition: Removes you from the expedition. There will be a confirmation window for this action. You will have one minute until you are teleported out of an expedition zone and into the zone and area where the expedition was entered.

  • Outstanding Expedition Timers: The amount of time remaining until you can take part in the same expedition zone.

  • Leader Options: Contains options only available to the leader. See Leader Options below for more information.

Experience

You have gained experience, either from defeating an enemy or completing a quest. Congratulations.

  • To see your progress toward the next level, hit "i" to open your inventory. The progress bar under the NEXT LEVEL label shows your progress toward the next level.

  • If you earned experience from defeating a foe, you may want to loot the corpse. Right-click on the corpse to loot it.

Face Change

  • You may change your face as often as you'd like, but one of the most recognizable things about your character is his or her face. Changing it too often makes it harder for people to recognize you, especially if your gear changes a lot.

  • You may need to close your inventory window ("i"), it may block your preview of your character's face.

Faction

You have just received a faction message. The message tells you that a certain group (for example, The Coalition of Tradesfolk) has changed their opinion of you. If the message says that you have gained faction with that group, then they like you more than they did. If it says that you have lost faction with that group, then they like you less.

  • Complete quests and defeat foes to change your faction. Completing quests and killing NPCs are the most common methods for changing your faction, for better or worse.

  • Be careful who you anger. Losing too much faction with the wrong people may make it very difficult for you to enter a city. Some quest-giving NPCs may not offer quests to you if you are not well liked by his faction.

  • It is easier to lose faction than to gain it. Killing their enemies may make them like you, but not as much as they will dislike you for killing one of their own.

Friends List

  • You can easily see if your friends are online. The Who button will activate a /who all on everyone on your friends list. Or you can click on one of the names on the list and the Who button will check just that name.

  • Adding people to your Ignore list prevents you from seeing their text. So if they are trying to make peace with you, you may not know it. Be careful about who you choose to ignore.

 

Gameplay

  • Some spells take a while to become available to cast after you memorize them.  So if your spell gem is gray after you finish memorizing a spell (such as Divine Aura), it isn't broken, just wait a while and it will become available.

  • Remember that '/dopropertinting on' will correct some issues with the skin tinting on new player models, such as skin tinted the same color as your armor.  Type /dopropertinting and hit enter.  The next time you log in the command will take effect.  For some video cards this will not work, and may give you poor results.  If this happens, type '/dopropertinting off', exit EverQuest and then start EverQuest again.

  • Make an "assist" hotkey in groups to kill faster - things die faster if everyone is killing the same one!  To use /assist, target the person you want to help.  Then /assist will switch your target to whatever that person has targeted.  You may also want toggle whether your auto-attack turns on when you assist by using "/assist on" or "/assist off".

  • Try hailing different NPCs, some may have quests to undertake.  Even some of the NPCs that you've already talked to may have something new for you. Always follow their directions carefully!

  • Use the Sense Heading ability early and often.  Your compass will become more accurate as your Sense Heading skill improves.

  • The denizens of some areas come out only after dark.  Do not be caught unaware when an area that is tame during the day starts crawling with evil creatures at nightfall.

  • Check back with your guild trainers periodically and ensure you have learned the basics in new skills you may have acquired.  Some skills will not improve unless you train in that skill at least once.

  • Once your reach level 10 your items will remain on your corpse.  Corpses with items on them only remain in the world for 7 days, so you should make sure to plan to recover your items before 7 days have passed.

  • When purchasing an item, either from a player character or a non-player character, right-click and hold on the item and carefully check to make sure that your character's race, class and level can use the item.

  • NO RENT items will disappear when you log out (or are disconnected).  Some containers are no rent, and items inside the container will also disappear when the container disappears.  Check any items you are purchasing for a NO RENT flag by right-clicking and holding on the item.

  • You can easily prevent accidentally giving items to the wrong NPCs by checking the name in the trade window before clicking 'trade'.

  • When your character reaches 20th level you can give him or her a surname.  Just type /surname {name}, where {name} is the new last name for your character.  Please understand that your last name must meet the naming conventions for the game.

  • When using a bow make sure that you have arrows equipped in your ammo slot.  If you have a stock of the same type of arrows in a quiver it will draw from it and auto fill your ammo slot.

  • When using a quiver it will increase the speed of your archery because it is easier to draw from the bow.

  • If you aren't gaining health or mana at your normal rate, make sure that you have eaten and are not hungry or thirsty.  This will impair your ability to concentrate on the arcane and restore your mana pool.

  • Food and drinks are eaten automatically when your character gets hungry.  So all you have to do is make sure you have food and drink in your possession.  Alcohol is not a drink (not for sustenance, anyway), and will not prevent you from getting thirsty.

  • Fire Beetle eyes make excellent light sources!

  • Grouping with fellow players helps you to achieve levels faster and is more fun!

  • Remember to store excess coins in the bank.  That way if you die, they will remain safe and you will not be weighted down as much.

  • If you lose your corpse there are several classes that have the magical abilities to help locate it.  A Bard, Necromancer, or Shadowknight may be willing to help you if you ask politely.

  • When interacting with NPCs, make sure to read everything they have to say. You may miss something important if you do not pay close attention to them!

  • Your buffs will begin to fade after a certain amount of time, and will flash as they are about to fade away. Keep an eye on them and refresh them when necessary.

  • Some items have the magical ability to increase the power of your spells.  To see if an item improves your spells power, hold down right click on the item and look for "Focus Effect: "

  • When you cast a spell that is strengthened by an item you are wearing, the item will give off a soft glow.

  • Certain player made or vendor bought provisions bestow additional benefits, such as bonus stats or a longer duration.  In order for those food items to be in effect, they need to be the first food item in your inventory.

  • Certain creatures are resistant to some types of spells.  If your favorite spell does not appear to be working well, try something else from your repertoire.

  • You can use the sneak skill to get closer to enemies than normal before they see you, especially from behind.

  • You can start a conversation with many NPCs just by saying hi to them.  Click on them and press "h" to hail them.

  • Use the cartography tool to add labels for your favorite experience areas in mappable zones.

  • Some items have a recommended level.  When looking at an item with a recommended level the number in red is how the item performs for you, and the number in parenthesis is the stat for the item at or above the recommended level.

  • Some of the skills granted to certain races or classes don't improve at all, while other skills will not improve until you have reached a certain level.  For example if you have 100 swimming because you are an Iksar, your swimming will not improve before level 20.

  • Performance Tip

  • In areas of dense foliage, try lowering your clip plane if your system is slowing down.  Use SHIFT and - to pull the clip plane in and SHIFT and + to push it out.

  • If you are stuck in a pool or lake, look for the lowest ledge.  Then look straight up and press Space Bar to jump up and out.

Give

You have started to give something to an NPC by clicking on that NPC with an item on your cursor.

 

Group

You have been invited to join a group.

  • To accept the invitation, click the Follow button in the Group window (or press Ctrl-I).

  • To decline the invitation, click the Decline button in the group window (or press Crtl-D).

  • If you want to start your own group, just target someone (when you are not in a group yourself) and click Invite in the group window (or press Ctrl-I).

Group Adventure

  • You must be in a group of at least 3 members to get an adventure.

  • Your group members must all be of a similar level (within about 6 or 7 levels of each other).

Group Leadership

  • Only the leader of a group can gain group experience

  • Group leadership experience requires at least three members in your group.

  • The more members in your group, the greater the leadership experience that will be earned.

  • If you don't intend to give anything to the NPC, click Cancel.

  • If you are giving the NPC quest items, make certain that the items are not stacked. Hold down CTRL when picking up items out of your inventory to pick up one at a time.

  • Pets can use some items. Your pet might be able to benefit from a weapon or some armor.

Guild

Someone has just invited you to join a guild. Joining a guild can be a great experience. Being in a guild of like-minded players will make your game experience much more fun.

  • Make sure you know what guild you are joining. It's a good idea to have played with the guild members before accepting their invitation. And you should know what the guild likes to do, make sure that they have the same interests that you do.

  • To remove yourself from a guild, type /guildremove. Make sure you really want to leave the guild, because you will have to be invited back to rejoin.

Guild Management

  • To sort the guild member list, just click on the column header you wish to sort on.

  • Public Notes are visible to everyone in the guild. Only guild leaders and officers can create these notes.

  • Private Notes are only visible to you.

  • You can "dump" your guild information into a text file for later use. Pushing the Dump button creates a text file in your EQ directory, named after your guild.

Interactive Object

You have discovered an interactive object! These objects often appear as boxes, vases, crates, coffins, barrels, and other items, so keep your eyes open for objects you can target by clicking on them with your cursor.

  • Under the abilities tab in the Actions window, you will see that you have gained two new abilities. One is named "Open," and allows you to open interactive objects when you have it selected and it is unlocked. The other new ability is named "Inspect," which allows you to get more information on an interactive object when you have it selected.

  • The following is a list of the other ways to interact with these objects:

    • Melee - You can hit the chest in order to break it open. Beware though, some objects are trapped and may be destroyed if tampered with too much.

    • Sense Trap - If you are a class that has the Sense Trap ability, you can check to see if the chest you want to open is armed with a trap.

    • Disarm Trap - If you have the Disarm Trap ability, you can disarm a trap that guards an object you want to open.

    • Pick Lock - If you are a class that has the ability to pick locks, you can pick the locked objects you want to open.

    • Opening Objects with Spells - Magic using classes can seek out spells that will allow them to open interactive objects that are locked or trapped via magical means.

Interface

There are keyboard commands to open and close the interface windows.

  • ALT-Y = Player Window (character name, hit point, mana and stamina bars)

  • ALT-P = Party Window (group members and their health bars)

  • ALT-T = Target Window (target's name and health bar)

  • ALT-S = Spell Window (your spell gems)

  • ALT-B = Buff Window (spells that are active on your character)

  • ALT-H = Hot Button Window (banks of hot buttons)

  • ALT-C = Chat Window (chat text window)

  • ALT-M = Main Menu Window (abilities, socials and combat abilities)

  • ALT-F1 = Pet Window (pet commands and health bar)

  • You can use the mouse to look around! Just hold the right mouse button and move the mouse to look around you.

  • Holding alt and pressing a number between 1 and 8 will cause your character to cast the spell memorized in the corresponding spell gem.

Inventory Window

This screen will be your most commonly accessed window in the game.  It contains all the information about your character's current statistics and items.  Initially, this is the only place to see how well you're accumulating experience.  See the yellow bar underneath your name?  When that fills up, you've achieved the next level!  Here's a few things you can do here:

  • Click on the buttons at the bottom of the screen (Skills, Alternate Advancement, etc.), they give you access to more info about your character as well as ways to change the color of your armor, change your face, and more.

  • If you're wearing an item that has a clickable effect, you can click and hold your mouse button on the item slot, and create a "hotbutton" which can be dragged to your hot button bar for quick access to the effect.

  • To give or trade items with someone, click the item to place it on your cursor, then click on the player or NPC with whom you wish to trade.

  • To automatically equip an item, place the item into the blank square in the center of the inventory screen. The item will go into the proper equipment slot, if that slot is available, or into your inventory.

  • To view detailed information about an item, right-click and hold (or ALT-click) on the item and an information window will appear.

  • To read books and notes, simply right-click the book or note item icon.

  • To scribe a spell, open your spellbook, click the spell item to place it on your cursor, and click the empty spellbook slot into which you wish to scribe the spell. Spells may only be scribed once each.

Journal

  • To reduce the number of extraneous entries, you may want to turn journal logging off until you need it.

  • Remember to turn the Journal logging back on when you do need it.

  • If you forgot to turn it on and you haven't gone too far from the NPC, turn it on and go back and chat with the NPC again.

  • Turning off Journal logging will cause journal entries to no longer display. Don't worry, they are still there, and will reappear when you turn the logging back on.

  • To sort the list, just click on the header of the column you wish to sort on.

  • To find a specific entry, click on the Search button and enter the text you are looking for. This will filter the window to display only items with the text you specified.

  • Deleting an NPC deletes all of the entries associated with that NPC. So be certain that you want to delete everything that the NPC has told you before you delete it.

Keyboard Options

  • You can remap your keys to help configure the keyboard for your play style. Just find the key you wish to change on the list (you may need to change categories in the dropdown list above the key listing). Click on the key that you want to change in the Keypress column. Then hit the key or key combination that you would like to activate the command.

  • You may want to remap the Autoattack command. This command it set to the "q" key. It is not uncommon for people to accidentally hit this key when trying to type a message. To reduce the chance that you will accidentally attack your guild master, you may wish to remap this key.

  • The Reload Default resets all the keys to their default, so don't worry about making a mistake.

Leader Options

These are options that are only available to the expedition leader. The available options work on the player who is targeted by the leader and who appears in the "Player Targeted" field. This field is updated when a new player is targeted, or a name from the Members section is selected. The available options are:

  • Add Player: This will send an invitation to the targeted player to join the expedition. You must be below the maximum number of players allowed for the expedition and the player must be online. The player that is invited must accept the expedition in order to be added to it.

  • Remove Player: This will remove the targeted player from the expedition. As the leader, you may remove any player, online or offline, during the expedition. This action gives the player in the expedition zone one minute until he or she is teleported out of the expedition zone and into the zone and area where the expedition was entered.

  • Make Leader: This will make the targeted player the new Expedition leader. The player must be online at the time.

Leadership

Leadership experience works very similar to regular experiences, but it is a separate system and there are some key differences:

  • Leadership experience is earned by the leader of a group or raid when their group or raid gets a kill.

  • When gaining leadership experience, a group or raid leader will receive 20% less regular experience.

  • You can turn off leadership experience if you wish to earn regular experience at its standard rate.

  • As a character earns leadership experience, his leadership experience bar will fill up. Once the bar is full, the character will gain 1 raid or group leadership point.

  • You can use raid and group leadership points and spend them to learn leadership abilities.

  • The leader will not gain leadership experience if the NPC killed is not at least dark blue to the highest level member of the group or raid.

  • Raid leadership is separate from group leadership.

Leadership Abilities

  • A character can save up raid and group leadership points and spend them to learn leadership abilities.

  • A character can only save a certain number of points based on their level and the leadership point type.

  • The maximum number of points that a character can save increases as they level

  • You can see your character's current maximum in the Leadership window (L key)

  • Only the group leader's abilities will be available to the group.

  • Only the raid leader's abilities will be available to the raid.

  • Individual group leaders in a raid will be able to use their group leadership abilities (not their raid leadership abilities).

  • Some abilities are passive and do not need to be activated.

  • Other abilities can be activated by creating hotkeys for them in the Leadership Window. Some of them can also be activated using slash commands:

Level 10

Congratulations on achieving 10th level!

  • From now on, when you die you will need to recover your corpse. Until you reach 10th level you return to life with all of your items. From now on, your items will remain on your corpse, and you will need to recover them.

  • As soon as you reach 11th level, you will lose experience points when you die. Death takes a toll on you, and along with the need to recover your items, you will lose some experience with each death.

Looting

  • To loot items most efficiently, right-click on the item on the corpse and it will be placed in your inventory (or equipped if appropriate).

  • Coins on the corpse are taken automatically by the first person to loot the corpse.

  • Autosplitting (/autosplit) coins is often the fairest way to distribute coins, but talk to your group about this, some people may not want any coins.

  • Be polite about looting. Make sure that loot is shared with your group, don't take all the stuff. Also, don't loot corpses that may have been killed by other players without asking first.

Merchant

  • To see the name of an item, either in your inventory or in the merchant's inventory, just point your mouse cursor at the item.

  • To see the names of all items in the merchant's inventory, place your mouse cursor in the merchant's inventory window and hold down the ALT key.

  • To find out how much an item costs or how much the merchant will pay for an item, just left-click on the item. The merchant will tell you what his offer is.

  • To purchase or sell a stack of items, hold the SHIFT key when you click on the Buy or Sell button.

  • To purchase or sell a single item (of a stack of items), hold the CTRL key when you click on the Buy or Sell button.

Performance

  • For some machines, turning on your Texture Cache can reduce the time it takes to zone.  To activate it, add the following line to the eqclient.ini file - TextureCache=TRUE

  • For some machines, not loading the social animations can improve performance.  The trade-off is that you will not see any of the social animations, such as /cheer and /wave.  To activate it, set the following line to the eqclient.ini file - LoadSocialAnimations=FALSE

Quantity

This window gives you the ability to select a quantity.  In some cases, the quantity window can slow you down, so please note the following tips.

  • To choose only one item out of a stack, hold the CONTROL key down when you click on it.

  • To pick up the whole stack of items at the same time, hold the SHIFT key when you click on it.

Raid Adventure

  • You must be in a raid group of at least 18 members to get a raid adventure, up to a maximum of 36.

  • Your raid members must all be at least level 60, with a required level average of 63.

  • Each member of your raid must complete at least two adventures in the theme you wish to raid in.

  • Once you have successfully completed or failed a raid adventure, you will need to wait five real-life days to do the same raid adventure again. For example, if you take on the raid in Deepest Guk, when you fail or succeed on that raid, you will have to wait five days to try again.

Raid Leadership

  • Only the leader of a raid can gain raid experience.

  • Raid leadership experience requires at least eighteen members in your group or raid.

  • The more members in your raid, the greater the leadership experience that will be earned.

Safety

  • No peeking over the big hole in Paineel.  That really is a deep, dark hole, and if you fall in, you will most likely die.  And don't think that your levitation will save you...

  • When using levitation spells, make sure they are fresh before venturing over long distances at great heights, if it wears off before you finish you may be killed.

  • Use /charinfo to check your bind point before doing something risky.  Your bind point is the place in the world where you will appear after you die.

  • Soulbinders can bind your soul to most city locations, look around your town for the NPC with the title "Soulbinder".  Your bind point is the place in the world where you will appear after you die.

  • Always make certain that the name showing in the trade window is the name of the person you are trading with.  If you see another name, cancel the trade and start over.  This will prevent you from trading with pets, NPCs or the wrong player.

  • Only accept complete trades.  Never give something to someone that promises to bring you his or her part of the trade later.  Just wait until 'later' to make the complete trade.

  • Some NPCs can see through invisibility! Be sure to consider (/con) NPCs before assuming you can pass by them safely while invisible.  An NPC that can't see you will consider you in a neutral way (because he can't see you).

  • You always look hidden to yourself.  Only Rogues get a message telling them when they have successfully hidden.  So don't rely on an external camera view to determine if you can be seen.

  • Not everyone in every city likes you.  Be sure to consider (/con) guards before approaching them in unexplored sections of town.

  • When underwater, carefully watch your breath meter.  Don't let it expire or you will drown!  To catch a new breath, all you need to do is swim up to the surface (Page Up key).

  • Even though some monsters may look alike, be sure to read their name to tell the difference.  If they have a name, they are probably more difficult to defeat.

Security

  • Be mindful of dropping items to the ground as some NPCs, as well as other players, can pick them up.

  • Make sure to have sufficient inventory space to hold the items you are getting before clicking trade (or completing a quest).  Items that do not fit into your inventory will fall to the ground.

  • Remember that all trades are final.  Make sure to check what you are giving and receiving before accepting a trade.  You can see an item's properties by right-clicking on the icon in the trade window.  Make sure the name of the item and the information displayed matches the item you want.

  • Be careful not to leave items in world containers (forges, ovens, etc), as they may be gone when you return.

  • Remember that the term "No Rent" means that this item will disappear from your inventory 30 minutes after logging out or losing connection.

  • Take time to check the going rate on an item before purchasing from another player.

  • Never, ever give out your password to anyone.  SOE employees will never ask for your password.  Keep your account secure.

  • Never, ever give out your account name to anyone.  Don't allow your character name and account name to match.  Don't post to message boards using your account name.  Your account name is half of the security of your account.  Keep your account secure.

  • Remember that handing items to the banker is not the same as putting them in the bank!  Right click the banker to open up the bank interface.

  • Turn on your Destroy and Drop confirmation options, this will make it less likely to destroy or drop something important on accident.

Sitting

  • Sitting allows you to heal and regain mana faster than when standing.

  • Some monsters will think that you are more vulnerable when you are sitting, and may attack you because of it.

  • You may need to stand up (/stand) to take certain actions. You will need to stand in order to move or cast spells.

Skills

The skills window shows you the current level of all of your skills.  Note:  Some skills, such as Meditation, require that you train in that skill at least once before the skill will increase with practice.

 

Spellbook

  • To scribe a spell into your spellbook, click on a spell scroll in your inventory and then click on an open spell slot in the spellbook.

  • To memorize a spell for casting, click on a spell in the spell book and then click on an open spell gem in the spell bar.

  • To delete a spell from your spellbook, right-click on the spell and hit the "Delete" key.

  • To move a spell in the spellbook, right-click on the spell and then right-click on the destination (you can click on another spell and the two will switch locations)

  • To cast a spell, select your target (if needed) and click on the corresponding spell gem on the spell bar.

Storyline

The Storyline window displays information about the ongoing story of EverQuest, as well as historical tales of Norrath.

  • Any time there is a new story the window will open when you log in.

  • To prevent the Story window from opening when there is a new tale, uncheck the box on the bottom left of the window.

  • The stories are displayed in the order that they are added, so the newest ones are always on the top of the list.

Tradeskill Window

This window can be used to perform any tradeskill recipe you know with much fewer clicks than the old experimental interface.

  • To get a list of recipes, enter your search criteria (min and max trivial values and/or a partial name) and click Search. All recipes that you know that can be combined in this container and match your criteria will display in the list.

  • To open the container as a bag, click on the Experiment button (or use Ctrl Right Click to open the bag in the first place). This will give you access to the contents of the container, if you had placed anything in it before.

  • To combine a recipe, select a recipe after performing a search, determine whether you have all the components (they should all have a green background, red means you don't have it in your inventory), then click Combine. The results will appear on your cursor, which must be cleared before you can combine again.

  • Favorites: You can deem any recipe to be a "favorite" by selecting the recipe and clicking on Add underneath the Favorites label. Favorite recipes that match your container will be displayed immediately when you open the container, and you can re-show your favorites at any time using the Show button. Select a favorite recipe and click Remove to delete it from your favorites list.

  • Auto-inventory The Auto-Inventory button works just like the auto-inventory area in your inventory window.
    Use Ctrl Right Click When this button is un-pressed (the default), then right-clicking on a tradeskill container brings up this interface, and Ctrl Right clicking the tradeskill container brings up the old "bag" interface. Depressing the "Use Ctrl Right Click" button reverses these two.

Trading

You have opened the trade window by clicking on a player character with an item on your cursor.

  • Make certain that all the items in the trade window are what you expect them to be before clicking Trade. All trades are final, so point your mouse at (or right-click and hold) every item in both windows before clicking Trade.

  • Any change to the items in the Trade window resets the trade. If either person changes or adds an item to the trade after one person has clicked Trade, both will have to click Trade to complete the trade. This is for your protection. If you click Trade and find that you need to click it again, recheck all items in the windows to make certain that they are what you expect them to be.

  • All trades are final. If you make a trade and checked your items carefully, you will get exactly what you traded for, nothing more or less. Make sure you are getting a deal that you are happy with.

  • Make sure you have room for all incoming items. Some items are large and may not fit into your bags, or you may be gaining more items than you have room for. These items may fall to the ground when the trade is concluded if they will not fit into your inventory, and may be snatched up by other PCs or NPCs.

Trainer

  • Some skills require that you train the first point before you can improve them through use. So if use isn't showing improvement on a skill, you may want to head back to your trainer to learn the basics.

  • Some skills can't be learned until you attain the required level. The Rank column shows the needed to attain those skills.

  • You get 5 practice points every level, so you may want to visit your trainer after you hear the "ding" sound for more training.

Tribute Benefit

This window displays your list of currently active Tribute Benefits, as well as displaying your Tribute favor, the cost of your active benefits, and how long it will be before you will have to pay another maintenance cost.

  • To downgrade or deactivate a benefit, select the benefit in the list and click the downgrade button. Each click will lower the tier of the active benefit until the benefit is completely deactivated. Note that you cannot reactivate a benefit, except at your Tribute Master.

Tribute Master

This window displays your current Tribute status (favor accumulated, active benefit costs, etc.), as well as giving you the opportunity to activate new benefits and donate items and platinum for more favor.

  • To donate an item, click on the item in your inventory and click the Donate button.

  • To donate cash (platinum only), click on the platinum button above the Donate button, use the quantity selector to choose an amount, then click the Donate button.

  • To activate/upgrade a benefit, click the benefit in the Master list, then click the Upgrade button. Each click will activate the next tier of the benefit. Note that higher tiers generally have higher level requirements and favor maintenance costs.

  • To deactivate/downgrade a benefit, click the benefit in the Active list, then click the downgrade button. Each click will lower the active tier by 1 until the benefit is completely deactivated.

  • Career Favor is the total amount of Favor the character has ever gained.

  • Current Favor shows how much Favor is available to use.

  • Active Cost is the number of Favor points that will be expended at the next interval.

  • Level displayed is the minimum level needed to gain the benefit.

  • Cost listed is the number of favor points needed to maintain the benefit.

World Containers

You have opened a "world container" such as a forge or an oven.

  • Do not leave items in a world container. A world container can be accessed by anyone, so if you leave something in a loom it may not be there when you come back.

  • When working on recipes, make sure not to stack items. Some recipes will not work with items in a stack. Hold down CTRL when picking up items out of your inventory to pick up one at a time.
