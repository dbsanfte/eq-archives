PLANE OF FEAR MAP

ENTERING PLANE OF FEAR

PLEASE REMEMBER THAT THIS IS A SUGGESTION ON HOW TO ENTER FEAR.  THERE ARE SEVERAL PROVEN METHODS... Approximately negative 500, positive 2500, southwestern, desolate corner of Feerrott is a cave guarded by spectres. Upon entering the cave, you take a series of rights and eventually come to an open area with a portal you see above. This is the first staging area for the initial insertion into Plane of Fear.  DO NOT immediately walk through the portal until your recon team has confirmed you can enter with relative safety or you will find yourself on the receiving end of the first rez of the day.  

If you have gotten to Ferrott late as everyone else is getting ready to zone in to PoF, be sure to warn the awaiting parties inside the cave.  Due to the fact you may train a few spectres with you.  Not a good thing when no one is ready and this tends to upset people and get you off on a bad start!

Hopefully you are well organized and have formed your groups already, but if not, do it NOW.  And have constant communication at all times with your recon team.  If this is not meet, you will be in for corpse retrieval right after zoning into the Plane.

Death is imminent.

 

INSERTION & RECONNAISSANCE

To break in, use a team of 2 Shadowknights & 4 Necromancers &/or Monks.  Shadowknights enter first as a means of tanks, if they are aggroed on too heavily, they run them to the West Wall Camp and FEIGN DEATH.  The Necromancers & Monks follow in.  They immediately each face a different direction, North, South, West, & East & FEIGN DEATH.  This is the RECON team.  All 6 should be reporting what they see as they are feigning death to the remaining teams about to enter.

After everyone is in, corpses retrieved, rezzes preformed, buffs back on, you are ready to proceed to the North Wall Camp.  Cheer the group and move on to the North Wall Camp.   Insertion, recon, and getting camp set up will take about 3-6 hours, depending on how many experienced planes people you have.

Okay you have assembled your party (what ever the numbers may be, the more people the better but the suggestion is at least 18 people if you want to mark a spot as yours permanently, but you can pull it off with 12 people). 

If you have not died yet, congrats!

 

RULES TO REMEMBER (OR FORGET AND DIE)

Rule #1:  No one leaves the spot.  Always keep your pulls as far up to the group as possible to avoid runners.

Rule #2:  If you get feared or teleported.  Stand there like you were coming in for the first time and make sure you have not picked up anything else.  If you have aggroed mobs, die or gate out and the group will let you know when its safe to try to come back in.  DO NoT run throughout the zone, you may be without your corpse till your puller says it's okay for him to drag it back to you, which could be a long time.

Rule #3:  No arguing in /ooc about who isn't or is in your group at the time or what the loot order is. Most important thing at this point right now is killing about 7-9 mobs.  So, if you do die you can come back in safely.  Just keep your mind on the pulling machine and argue in your respective groups till you are safe and the area is cleared.  There is more time to have conversations later.

Rule #4:  DO NOT succor or evac unless you absolutely have to.  Better to take a few deaths than trying to reinsert all over again.  

Rule #5:  Stay close to each other while medding, fighting and waiting for your puller.  No need to aggro stuff.

Rule #6:  You will die.  Have that level 46 buffer fully intact!

Rule #7:  Praying does not help.

Rule #8: LISTEN TO THE RAID LEADER!

Plane of Fear Rules And Suggestions

PULLER

The puller should be someone that you trust.  One that is willing to die before bringing back too much stuff to the group.  And ALWAYS listen to your puller!  for he/she is the one who will die the most.

AUTO PULL:  Auto pull is when you out run something or that something aggroed didn't follow you back to your party for what ever reason and returns to it's spot.  Once this happens it transfers the aggro flag to everything around it.  So, when you get into range monsters just come at you. Normally, in ones or twos, depending on how deep in you are.

 

NORTH WALL CAMP

Ok, the Fire Wall is CLOSE!  The puller should be prepared to die and die often.  While at the North Wall, face South towards the firewall.  The idea is to work clockwise AROUND the Fire Wall.  The puller should start with the NW corner and then work westward while the group stays at the North Wall Camp.  Once the North Wall is cleared, the puller should work his way southward between the Fire Wall and the west wall.  Clear this area up to the Fetid Fiend House, but do not get to close to it YET!

Now the group should move to the the NW corner.

Remember, Death is always around the corner.

Everyone should be in the NW corner and the puller continues his clearing.  Pull the contents of the Fetid Fiend House.  There are 4 Fiends in the house.  They mez easily and have low magic resistance.  Clear the area a little bit South of the Fetid Fiend House, but not past the Gate. Directly East of the House is a bridge-like structure with Tentacle Tormentors on it. When the area between the house and the bridge is clear, pull the Tentacle Tormentors. 

WARNING!  Use EXTREME CAUTION when pulling around the bridge.  The mobs consider it a wall and will not pass under it.  Mobs will sometimes path back Eastward and aggro other mobs, including Dracoliche.  Accidentally pulling Dracholiche is a BAD THING! 

When the bridge is clear, the party then moves to the Fetid Fiend House.  Camp should be set up on the North side of the house, along the West wall, you'll be here for a while, so kick off your shoes and relax a little.

East of the Bridge is the Graveyard, which is where Dracoliche hangs out. Don't pull from past this Bridge unless: 

1.  Dracholiche is dead. 
2.  You are experienced with pulling this area with Dracholiche up. 
3.  You like to be Feared and slaughtered by a horde of high level mobs in an area where corpse retrieval is painful. 

One more fun fact... if Cazic is up... and he starts his sweet yell with (YOUR NAME)... it means he is pretty annoyed that you are in his plane.  He has decided you need to be dealt with "personally".  He will then start death touching (DT) everyone.  Camp out as soon as raid leader yells "CAMP!" and not before!  Cazic will DT every 30 seconds until he has taken care of business.  Stay out for 3 minutes.

MOBS YOU WILL ENCOUNTER

Amygdalan Knights
The Amygdalan Knight is a Shadowknight as the name implies. They come complete with useless (although annoying) pets. They drop Umbral Platemail armor (Shadowknight only). Their special skills are Harm Touch and Mana Drain. These can usually be seen guarding the temples of Fright, Dread, and Terror.

Amygdalan Warriors
These look just like the Amygdalan Knight without the ability to harm touch or make a pet. This means they are mana suckers because they are warriors.  They have a much higher magic resistance than most things in the zone. They are extremely common and you'll be fighting a lot of these. They drop a warrior only shield and whip. They are found wandering the zone or protecting the main temple of Cazic Thule.

Boogeymen
Looks like a huge Ghoul. They drop Blighted armor (Necromancer only). Their special skill is Ghoulroot.

Cazic Thule
Cazic is a God. Cazic is a vengeful God. Cazic does not like people. Cazic does not like you.  Cazic doesn't like ANYONE!  In general, he has several irritating quirks. First, he hits for OVER 400 and double attacks. Second, he uses gravity flux like there is no tomorrow.  Every time you are fluxed, it removes one buff (put JBoots in top buff slot and reactivate after every flux). Third, Cazic has pets. Not a typical type PET, but plural PETS, and they are fairly irritating, although nothing to lose sleep over.  His special skills are Gravity Flux, mean melee skills, and debuffing anything in his way.

Dracoliche
This looks like a huge Ashenbone Drake from Plane of Hate.  If you have never seen one of those, you have never seen anything that looks like a Dracoliche.  It looks like Vox died, decomposed, then returned to life with just its bones. This is Cazic Thules number one bodyguard. This bone dragon is a shadowknight with a pet. Attacking Dracoliche ALWAYS aggros Cazic himself, and throughout the battle with Draco, Cazic WILL be death-touching constantly, as well as sending in reinforcements.  He has a mean disease based DOT and dragon roar (area effect Fear).  Leave it to Verant to make a bone dragon, call it a liche, and NOT make it undead!  After killing Draco, everyone will have to log out, wait 3 minutes and log back in unless other specified by the raid leader to clear Cazic's Hate List!.  Draco spawns once a week (real time).  It's special skills are a DoT and dragon roar.

Dread, Fright, & Terror
Cazic Thules little buddies that have temples located around the zone. You know those UGLY Golems in CoM?  That is what these look like, only BIGGER and MEANER!  Fighting any one of these will also aggro Cazic.  Their special skill is usually Blind and DT & HT!  If one of these Killers yell your name, wave bye bye and... LOADING, PLEASE WAIT!

Fetid Fiends (Male)
Resembles a huge zombie. They drop Valorium armor (Paladin only). Their special skill is Disease.

Fetid Fiends (Female)
Resembles a huge female zombie. They drop a girdle that's quite good for Clerics. They have no special skills.

Frightfingers
A small reanimated hand. They drop Carmine armor (Wizard only). Their special skill is wizard spells, which they cast a lot of.

Glare Lords
A small version of an evil eye. Glare Lords are magicians. They come with mage pets and drop wands for Mages and Enchanters. Their special skill is an area effect debuff which is quite nasty. You could fight just one of these things and find ten buffs are gone by the end of the fight.

Gorgons
Gorgons resemble Harpies from Steamfont Mountains, but with snakes coming out of their heads. Gorgons drop Vermiculated armor (Druid only). Their special skill is a 15 second stun which can get pretty nasty if there's a couple of them on you.  Having a high magic resistance for these things is pretty essential.

Nightmares
A big black horsie!  BUT!  Squeaks like a rusty swing.  They drop a cloak that is not that great. Their special skill is Blind and have been known to Fear as well.

Phantasms
These look like spectres.  They drop nothing of great interest and have no special skills.

Phoboplasms
These resemble gelatinous cubes, like the ones in Skyshrine. They are located in and around the temple of Cazic Thule. These are loved by all because they can randomly drop any piece of planar armor in the game, including armor for classes such as rogues, bards, clerics, and warriors which is normally found in the Plane of Hate. There are conflicting reports as to whether they can truly drop ANY piece or just 1 piece from each class (i.e. Cleric Boots, Bard Bracer, Paladin Bracer, Warrior Gauntlets, etc.). Their special skill is an area effect debuff, but whereas the Glare Lords and Tentacle Tormentors debuff four buffs at a time, these only debuff 1 buff at a time.

Samhains
Happy Halloween!  A jack-o-lantern on a scarecrow. These drop a ranger only axe. They have no special skills.

Scarelings
These little things look like the imps from Sol B Efreeti area. They are rogues and backstab for 400+. These are VERY deadly and always the first target when one is in the camp. Another deadly tactic of the Scareling is to teleport anyone to a random location, (not too far away), potentially starting a massive train leading to a total wipe out of the raiding party. This is why they are given the highest priority. They are also very hard to target and see because of their size. They also run faster than SoW, making kiting them is pretty hard. Scarelings must die, and die fast. They drop a rogue-only rapier. Their special skill is Backstab and Teleport.

Shiverbacks
Looks like a big ape, hits hard and fast. They drop Shiverback armor (Monk only). They have no special skills.

Spinechiller Spiders
Spiders, ICK! They drop Cryosilk armor (pure casters). Their special skill is a relatively tame cold based DoT spell that hits for 300 in 30 ticks.

Tentacle Tormentors
Tormentors resemble their weaker cousins from Unrest and Najena. Chain stunning these guys is a great idea - it keeps them from using their special skills. They drop Thorny Vine armor (Ranger only) as well as a druid based belt. Their special skills are Lifetap, Throwback, and an area affect debuff. Like the Glare Lords, they're going to wipe out a lot of your buffs. Their knockback is quite nasty as well. If you're fighting them with a camp against a zone wall, fight them with your backs to the zone wall so that if you get knocked back, you don't go far and can return to the fight quickly.

Turmoil Toads
Resemble small Froglock ghouls that can be found in Lower Guk. Toads drop Turmoil Warts and a belt for Shamans. Their special skill is Fear. This is why you want to have SoW and Run OFF when attacking these guys. If they fear you and you start to run away from them quickly, you could run far enough to start aggroing other stuff.

Worry Wraiths
Giant will-o-wisp with a pet. They need to be dispelled.  They have a damage shield that can cause many people to go LD from the amount of "You have been burned" messages. They drop nothing of great interest and have no special skills.

THE MOST DANGEROUS ENEMY
The most fearsome of all of the enemies in Plane of Fear...

Someone who does NOT follow the rules and listen to the Raid Leader.  

This enemy is amongst you.  He could even be grouped with you.  He could be you.  This enemy is fearsome because of his ability to kill 50+ people within 3 minutes. His special skills are Train Making.  Beware Him THE MOST!
