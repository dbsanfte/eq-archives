Jewelry 101

LESSON 1

A Little History

The citizens of Norrath have developed a number of trade skills over the course of their history, as would any civilized world. Merchants on all continents buy and sell components to supply those who opt to learn these skills, be they apprentice or master. Some learn the skills to feed themselves, to earn money, or simply to provide themselves with items they would otherwise lack, due to their cost. Some of the trade skills widely practiced on Norrath are: fishing, tinkering, baking, tailoring, blacksmithing, jewelry, and pottery.

Beginning

Begin in your jewel craft as you would any other trade.  Go to your Guild master to acquire your starting skills, then practice, practice, practice.  Before attempting  your first piece of jewelry, invest at least 20 practice points into the skill.

Who Can Work This Trade

ANYONE!  It is suggested that an enchanter is best suited though.  For they are the only ones who get the "enchant" metal spells.

Enchant Spells

Enchanter's receive their enchant spells at various levels:

Enchant Adamantite - Blacksmithing Trade  - Can be bought in the Neriak Library - Level 49 spell

Enchant Brellium - Blacksmithing Trade  - Can be bought in the Ak'Anon Casters Guild - Level 49 spell

Enchant Clay - Pottery Trade - Can be bought in the Felwithe Casters Guild & Neriak Library - Level 8 spell

Enchant Electrum - Jewelry Trade - Can be bought in the Felwithe Casters Guild & Neriak Library - Level 16 spell

Enchant Gold - Jewelry Trade - Can be bought in the Felwithe Casters Guild & Neriak Library - Level 24 spell

Enchant Mithril - Blacksmithing Trade - Can be bought in the Felwithe Casters Guild - Level 49 spell

Enchant Platinum - Jewelry Trade - Can be bought in the Felwithe Casters Guild & Neriak Library - Level 34 spell

Enchant Silver - Jewelry Trade - Can be bought in the Felwithe Casters Guild & Neriak Library - Level 8 spell

Enchant Steel - Blacksmithing Trade - Can be bought in the Freeport Casters Guild & Qeynos Casters Guild - Level 49 spell

Enchant Velium - Jewelry Trade - Can be bought in Thurgadin from Nimren Stonecutter - Level 44 spell

Patience

Remember... You will fail and you will fail OFTEN!  Failed attempts can quickly drain your bank account as you get the "I am going to sit here until I make this %@$#!!" attitude.  After your skill has gone up and you are able to make a little more valuable jewelry, you will be able to sell your jewelry for a tidy little sum.  And you will start to make a little profit to help you make MORE jewelry!

Jewelry Needs

                   

Make all jewelry using the Jeweller's Kit that you purchase from a gem or jewelry merchant.  Like the kits for other trades, the Jewelry Kit, once opened, has slots for the components.  You may want to buy "Legacy of Jewelcraft", the jewelry "manual" merchants sell along with the kit, just for the background information if anything.  Once you have the kit, some metal and gems, you will be ready to begin.

LESSON 2

Getting Started

                   

You have your kit, metal and gems!  Get started!

Step 1

Enchant the metal.   You can right click your metal bar to make sure your metal has been enchanted.

Step 2

Place the metal bar and a gem in your Jeweller's Kit. 

Step 3

Pray. 

Step 4

Click 

Step 5

Depending on your skill level, intelligence, wisdom and LUCK, you hopefully just created your first jewelry item.  Or the components were just destroyed.  ACK!  GRR!  UGH!

Step 6

Repeat Steps 1 through 5. 

LESSON 3

Notes

Below a skill of 20, you run the risk of destroying more jewelry than you make, which can be very costly.  Begin with cheap gems (such as malachite and lapis lazuli) and silver bars, then work up to the more expensive gems.

More Notes

A good way to increase your skill:  Make large quantities of a simple jewelry items.  You will receive a message when you have attempted a jewelry item that is trivial to your skill.  Now move on to the next gem/metal combination.  You can sell the items back to the merchant.  In most cases you will lose a little copper or gold, but it is good practice.  Remember that the vendors will pay you less if the metal was not enchanted.

Even More Notes

Master silver jewelry first before moving on to electrum.  Then master electrum before moving on to gold and so forth.  This will be much more cost effective to your ever dwindling bank account within Norrath.
