HOW OUR GUILD WORKS

(A.K.A. GUILD BYLAWS)

 

 

 
Sacred Honor Operation:

(September 11, 2001)

Sacred Honor is constructed of a council, officers, and members. The guild is maintained by a fair balance of power. No ONE person or group of people have final say in any situation.

Sacred Honor's council makes the bylaws of the Sacred Honor guild. Council members constantly review the guild's bylaws in order to keep the code fair for all. Council members also make new positions available in the officer and council sections of the guild. They cannot, however, nominate or elect the new members to fill those positions. An individual may be removed from the council by majority vote by other council members, including the vote of the council member in question. Council members are allowed to hold positions as officers in Sacred Honor. A council member may willingly step down in his/her duties as an titled guild officer (banker, raid, liaison, etc.) That person will still remain an officer and council person with only the duties of a council member. An individual may also step down as a council member and remain an officer, keeping any duties he/she had as an officer. Council members are highly respected members of Sacred Honor and hold only the best interest of the guild in their heart. Their actions and commitment are rewarded only by the satisfaction of knowing honor, justice, and unity still prevail in the world of Norrath.

Officers effectively RUN the guild. They make sure the bylaws of the guild are followed, admit new members, schedule raids and participate in the everyday running of the guild. As new positions become available, officers nominate and elect new officers and new council members. There are some "titled" guild officers with duties involved such as: banker/treasurer, trade skills, external relations and so forth. Currently, the names of these offices are under revision. A list of what they are and their job descriptions will be available on the guild website. Officers are not required to take a title, but their opinions and actions are invaluable to the operation of the guild. Sacred Honor's officers take that extra step for the greater good of the guild. Officers give, knowing there will be no reward. Sacred Honor's officers contribute greatly to the guild's well being and are model members in our humble gatherings.

(September 12, 2001)

Sacred Honor's members are the heart and soul of this wonderful guild. Members have the authority to override any decision made by the guild's council or the its officers. A majority vote by guild members overturns any guild action. This action may range from the admittance of a new member, to the review of an established member, or even cause a review of the guild's code by the council. ANY action taken by the guild may be overturned by a majority vote from ALL of the guild's members. No action will be taken until the review or vote has been made final. Sacred Honor's members are expected to act with honor and uphold the guild's code. At times, this may be difficult and trying, but members of Sacred Honor are strong and willful, accomplishing much that others dare not even try. Carrying honor first, Sacred Honor's members hold pride in their journeys and their accomplishments. United forever, they will not falter, but succeed gloriously in any undertaking. Guild members are our greatest contributors and keep this humble guild on the straight and narrow.


-Council-
