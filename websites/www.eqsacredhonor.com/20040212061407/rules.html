Sacred Honor Guild Rules

(September 18, 2001)

Kill-Stealing (KSing) will not be tolerated. Once or twice may be attributed to a mistake or an accident and is part of the game, but repeated reports of KSing will cause a member to be placed under review.


(October 11, 2001)

Give of yourself expecting nothing in return. Treat ALL of Norrath with the respect and courtesy you would expect from them. You will often be surprised how your kindness and selfless acts come back to you in the future.

(October 11, 2001)

Be honest. Ninja looting (looting an item and running or logging) will be taken VERY seriously! If a member takes something that does not belong to them, it shames the character AS WELL as the guild. Lying and stealing eats away at the very fabric of what this guild stands for. Do NOT do it, ninja looting will NOT be tolerated. If you witness a member ninja looting, please report it to a guild officer.

(October 13, 2001)

Hold honor above ALL. Do what you feel is correct so that you will still feel good about your decision in the future.

United in mind and soul, Sacred Honor's members accomplish things unimaginable to others. Together, Sacred Honor's members and allies overcome ANY obstacle and have fun doing it!


(February 10, 2002)

IF a player QUITS the guild (with no notice) s/he will be required to submit a NEW application (requiring a MAJORITY vote by the Officers to be re-accepted) and ALSO must go through a new probationary period.
Further, if the player was an Officer or on the Council that player must be re-nominated and voted back into the position they abandoned/quit.

 

(September 1, 2003)

If you leave Sacred Honor with no notice, all characters belonging to you will be disbanded.  This will be done only after we verify that you were not hit by the "Disband Guild Tag Bug".
A notice of leaving the Guild MUST be emailed to an Officer OR posted on the Forums.  It can not be done in Guild Chat in game.  The emailed or posted leaving the Guild MUST be done prior to de-guilding.
If you have left Sacred Honor with no notice and want to reapplying to come back.  All members will vote on your application and if it is passed, your probation period will be a mandatory 90 days.

If you are going to be absent from game for more than 30 days, you will need to post, email an Officer with confirmation the email was received or speak to an Officer in game.  If you don't this is the procedure the Officers will follow:
30 or More Days: An email will go out to the absent person that he/she is being disbanded if the Guild does not hear back in 5 days due to we feel they have quit playing.  If the absent person that was de-guilded wishes to rejoin s/he will need to fill out the app again and then be on an extended probation as applies to the policy of leaving with no notice.

Sacred Honor's Raid Guide

  (basic guidelines)

1. During the raid, there's NO useless chatter during pulls. Someone not paying attention could cause the rest of us to get killed and destroy the raid.
PAY ATTENTION!

2. No extended periods of AFK. You have run to the little Norrath's Room? Go before hand or while we are taking a med break. Need to walk the dog? Walk the dog before hand. You need to go hang out with the wife/girlfriend? Put her to bed before hand. And holding up your group for your laundry just is not cool either.

3. You must pay attention to the raid organizer at absolutely all times and keep up with what the Raid Leader or Puller says. If you don't keep up, and you get left behind, you might:
a) die
b) start a train and kill us all
c) lose your corpse.
All 3 of these options are unacceptable.

4. Loot will be done AFTER the fight is over and it is SAFE, for EVERYONE who can use the item ONLY. It will be done by the group leader who's group took the kill, if there is more than one group, who will then call what the name of the item is. Those classes will roll with /random whatever Raid Leader says. The one who wins will loot their item.

5. Allies and all individuals listed as "Friends" have the same equal looting rights on all items. Guests will have no looting rights, unless stating so prior to the Raid.

6. There will be no bickering or arguing about anything, including loot. The raid leader's say is final.

7. There will be one person assigned as the puller. To make it as easy as possible, create a hotkey that says "/assist <First name of assist person>". Keep hitting that hotkey as soon as the puller says that a target has been aquired. Puller, make sure you have a hotkey made for INCOMING.

8. If you dont like the rules of a raid please do not come, because if you don't like the rules, but come anyway and then bicker about them, you will be asked to leave. If you feel we are too tight, strict, etc... about these rules, sorry, but we have found that it works well and everyone knows what is going on.

9. Trouble makers will be asked to leave the raid. Trouble makers consist of people that complain, whine, argue, loot without winning a lotto and/or just cause problems in general. We understand that people can have bad days, shoot for the raid day to not be one of those bad days!

10. There will be no talking while raid, group leaders or puller is giving instructions, idle chatter is spam and can be distracting to a raid, group leader, puller or another member. Distractions will kill.

11. Do not move from a camp until the puller and raid leader have announced it is safe to do so. Doing so can get us all wiped out. Which means... no exploring.

12. Everyone needs to have Magic Resistant Buff up at all times if we are raiding where there are casting mobs.

13. If someone tells you to consent them. Do it IMMEDIATELY.
It means they have a window of opportunity to drag your corpse somewhere safe, but they must do it quick or they too can die.
So, please read all messages.

14. Have a /loc hotkey made. Make sure you hit it as you are attempting not to die. This way you know exactly where you died.
If it is in an area that a corpse can be drug from, this will be USEFUL information!

15. Be kind to our clerics & enchanters, bring a few peridots or whatever gem they need, if you want them to cast these buffs on you, just a nice thing to do.

16. The Raid Leader is in charge of forming groups. He will assign the Group Leader and tell him/her who is in their group. Every group will be as balanced as possible. There may be change ups as the raid goes, so don't be alarmed if you see "RaidCoordinator shouts, YourName join BabbleAlong's group!

17. Upon entering the zone, put on your LFG tag!!! /lfg This will enable Raid Leader to see you NEED a group. Be patient, Raid Leader will have to shuffle a bit to get you grouped and may take a few minutes if there is more than one group on raid. Stay close to raid party to ensure you don't get killed while waiting to be grouped.

18. Have food & drink. Just a thought, don't want anyone starving to death!

19. Stay near your group members, a few good reasons here... Your healer can heal you easier, the buffers can buff easier & group buff spells have a moderate casting range. Not to mention... We really don't want you out wandering and dragging MOB's back with you!

20. The rules can change a little as we go, SOME are NOT set in stone and raid leader may switch things up as we go. All will be advised of changes.

21. Bind in a safe spot, ask if you don't know.

22. Give a pull or two of notice if you have to log.
