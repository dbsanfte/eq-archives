Support Your Local Bait Shop



    What once was a gathering place for fishermen of all types, is now a place to get your hair set and get your nails manicured. People of all ages came to the store to talk fishing, forget there troubles, and buy bait and tackle. Now we are forced to buy through catalogs or big chain stores, where you'll never get any information on current fishing conditions.
    Personally, I would rather stop in a local bait shop to buy my gear, and bait. Even if it costs a little more, the information you'll get on local fishing hot spot is priceless. Most of my knowledge and skills came from talking to the owners and customers that frequent these shops. Most people think that buying bulk is better, saving a penny is more practical, or buy through the mail is convenient. This is true in a lot of cases, like buying produce for canning, or stocking up on necessities, but for fishing equipment, buying it from a big chain store or catalog will limit the personal information you'll get on where and how to use the lure or product you just purchased.
   For those guys who order through a catalog because its cheaper, what will you do when you need live bait or a lure and there is no place to get it. Remember the mall opens at 10:00am, and mail order can take weeks to get, but your local bait shop is usually open at 6:00am and has what you need when you need it, like the glow spoon you lost the night before to a 25lb salmon.
I personally, have called bait shops and asked if they would stay open late, so I could come in for bait and tackle for the next day. I couldn't get out of work early enough, but they waited for me after closing time, try getting that service from a chain store.
    I've had some of the best fishing of my life because the shop owners unselfishly told me some of they’re favorite spots, and it didn't cost me a dollar to call a hot spot hotline. The owner of my local bait shop, which is now a salon, opened his store because he loves fishing and wanted to help people enjoy this great sport. The shop would still be open today, but lost business to catalogs and chain stores, forced they’re doors to close. My friends and I really miss this small bait shop. I feel obligated to help other small bait shops by purchasing equipment and bait, because this particular shop gave us so many good times and memories catching fish. One memory stands out clear, we walked into the shop to talk fishing and buy a few lures, and Keith (the owner) told us to grab the local fishing news paper that was available at his shop, and turn to page 6. Much to our surprise a picture of Jeff (@angler-info) and I holding 4 steelhead trout was on that page. He submitted our picture to the publication, not telling us, to surprise us because he knew it would make the paper and make us happy at the same time. We thanked him, and he just said "heck man, you guys did all the work".
    The point I’m trying to make is that no amount of money can replace the personal service you'll get from a small bait shop. They can show you the new high tech gear, teach a beginner how to string a reel and tie a knot, or explain certain techniques to help you boat more fish. In closing, if you want to keep the sport of fishing fun (fun = catching fish), please help support your local bait shops, just remember we're all in the same boat.

till next time...  Good Fishing.
