Here's a map my character (Ranger Efton) has made of the known islands of the Ocean of Tears. These maps were made using the /loc command and hence should be somewhat accurate.

This map is overlayed with a grid that is 1,000 units apart. The center point (0,0) is marked by solid lines. The blue line meandering through the map is the ship route and all the islands visible from the ship have been depicted. Two other islands that I discovered have also been depicted. Note that I have not been over every square foot of the Ocean and it's quite possible that there are other islands out there. Hopefully, though, this map will be useful to any who find themselves overboard and swimming alone in the ocean.

One interesting thing to note about the map is that the Freeport and Butcherblock exits seem to be backwards. Freeport exits to the east and Butcherblock exits to the west. This can be determined easily by riding the ship and using Sense Heading to determine the direction it's going.

Most islands were outlined from a rowboat for safety (level 7 ranger can't go up against some of the inhabitants). A few I explored on foot. Here are some specific notes:

Isle of the Sisterhood looks quite plain and boring, with the Sisterhood members patrolling around and nothing to hunt. There's a merchant in one of the huts where one can buy basic provisions. Bring a fishing pole and bait if you want some way to earn money and pass the boredom. There's also a small dwarf settlement on the back side of the island where one can buy armor. Overall it looks pretty innocuous except for one tiny detail: there's a small "switch" just outside the dwarf tent. Flipping it produces a grinding noise of some sort, but I couldn't find anything in the immediate vicinity that changed. I intend on re-visiting the island to try to determine what the switch does. Another interesting thing to note is occasionally a pirate will come along and try to get members of the Sisterhood to join his pirate band. He always gets his head handed to him.

Just west of the Isle of the Sisterhood is Boog Mudtoe Isle. Boog Mudtoe seems to be some sort of ogre, but he seemed friendly enough to me and didn't mind me traipsing over his land. On the island are the ruins of a small keep and inside that keep is a magic merchant selling what I assume are mid to high level scrolls (10plat to 12plat each).

South of there is the Isle of K'Arnon, which is populated mainly by Seafury Goblins and two humans: the Oracle of K'Arnon and the Guardian of K'Arnon. Talking to the Oracle reveals that he is both a seeker and provider of knowledge. I didn't explore the conversation further as I had much ground (or water, as the case may be) to cover.

The island I have marked as the Zachariah Reagh Isle is so named because I couldn't get any response from any of the inhabitants. Hence, I couldn't determine the purpose of the inhabitants. If someone managed to talk to them and describe to me what they are doing there, I can rename it.

Aviak Isle is one of the islands I discovered by accident as it is not visible from the ship at any point in it's path. It is populated by a tribe of Aviaks (think of humanoid parrots). They pretty much ignored me when I went walking about their island.

The other island not visible from the ship is Allizewsaur Isle. This island is named after its main inhabitant, the Allizewsaur. Imagine a big dinosaur in the shape of an upright alligator and you've got the picture. He conned red and angry so I did not set foot on the island. Return to Norrath home
