Adventures of Frostwolf...

by Frostwolf, Knight of the Tribunal a.k.a Burn

 
 










‘And so it was that the Prince of Hatred, known to mortal beings as Innoruk, was locked away in the demi-plane of Shadow for a millenium. The Six Hammers turned as one and spoke to the council of Powers that created them.

“And see how Justice is served upon a Greater Power, for all beings must bow before the power of Justice. As you have created us to imprison Innoruk for his crimes, we shall maintain our vigilance and be prepared to imprison all others who stray from the path of Justice with our Righteous Might.” And the Six Hammers turned and walked together, their glory merging into a single entity of Justice and Equality. The names of the Hammers have since been lost to time, and they are now called simply The Tribunal.’

- The Book of the North

This is a short account of the early adventures of the Northern Shaman, Frostwolf. He was not my first character, but he is my favorite and the only one that I’ve decided to keep for any protracted period of time so far. I just love towering over the other races…

Frostwolf appeared on December 30th for the first time, rising from nowhere in front of the gates of Halas, the Barbarian City in the Everfrost Peaks. I’ve come to love Halas, as do many of my kinsmen. There is a certain beauty amidst the peaks at dawn and dusk with the pink light of the horizon dancing across the pure landscape. One traveler once demanded to know the location of “something that passes for a city in this gods forsaken land.” He was met by a horde of angry barbarians, all leaping to the defense of their land’s honor. The sight of it warmed my heart.

I had been aware of my entry into Norrath no more than 4 seconds when a fellow Clansmen clad totally in leather armor and wielding a mace and lantern approached me. “Hail!” he said. “How fare your adventures?” The man’s name was Ethril, and he too was a servant of the Tribunal. Being of the 8th Rank, he immediately took me under his wing and showed me to the Shaman guild, the bank, the Everfrost Peaks, and later the magic and weapons shops. We exchanged a few pleasantries, I thanked him, and we parted ways.

If you are going to play a shaman, you’ll begin with the spells Light Healing (self explanatory) and Inner Fire. (minor enchantment that raises your hp, among other things) All spells use one of the spellcasting skills, abjuration, alteration, evocation, divination, and conjuration. The spells you begin with are (I think) abjuration and alteration, so place one skill point in each of those, and 3 in 1 handed blunt. If you’ve played spellcasters before, I think you’ll be surprised by the shaman’s hitting power.

Halas is separated from the Everfrost Peaks (hunting grounds) by a large, rippling crystal lake that is serviced by a small barge. While I like riding the barge and travelling in style as is befitting a servant of the Tribunal, it is also a quick and easy way to take swimming lessons. Just a couple of trips have taken my skill from Awful to Feeble.

I made my way across the lake and into the caverns that lead to the Everfrost Peaks. Once on the other side, I was greeted by twin tents and vendors who are there to supply rations to weary adventurers and also to take excess baggage off your hands. There are also two guards there who will soon become your best friends.

While in the lower levels, stay near the entry to the caverns, esp. at 1st level. You can take your club to the skulls of some Decaying Skeletons, gnoll pups, and ice goblin whelps, but stay away from anything bigger or groups of them. I especially like the skeletons. They’re weaklings, and sometimes they offer booty out of proportion to their difficulty in the form of a rusted weapon worth a gold piece or so. With a little persistence, you’ll be able to reach 2nd level in no time. I reached 3rd level in my first 6 hours of playing, if that long.

At 3rd, you can take on skeletons, white wolves, single goblin scouts, and maybe a polar bear cub, and groups of the above monsters. If you want to gamble, fight wooly spiders too. They vary largely in difficulty, and their friends always seem too glad to help. More than once have I found myself streaking toward the guards with a whole pack of spiders on my tail. But they are worth a fair amount of experience, all things considered.

About this time, I picked up a rusty mace from a fellow named Falaman. While much slower and heavier than your club, it does a fair amount of damage, up to about 10 points or so. (may not seem much to you plate heads out there, but that’s not bad for a spellcaster) I also purchased all of the 1st level spells available in Halas including the combat spell Burst of Flame and the enchantments Strengthen and Dextrous Aura. I never go to war without the latter two and Inner Fire active. Never.

By 5th level, I had attained a warhammer to replace my mace and some bits of armor. None of the monsters in the Peaks were a challenge anymore, and I was even taking on small armies of goblins myself. And at 5th you get access to a whole new slew of spells, including: the ass-saving Gate, (which zaps you to your starting point from anywhere in the world, but costs 70 mana and takes a while to cast) the nice offensive spell Frost Rift, and the equivalent of an adrenaline burst, Fleeting Fury. There are others, but those are the three I carry around with me.

I ran across Ethril again at about this time, and after a short gasp of amazement at how quickly Frostwolf’s power was growing (surely I was blessed!) he offered to show me to some of the other zones. We dropped by Blackburrow, the home of a fierce gnoll tribe, crossed into the Qeynos Hills, (oh, did ya’ll know that Qeynos spelled backward is SonyEQ?) and passed into Northern Qeynos where I glimpsed the great city itself. We parted ways for a short while and I ran around gathering snake scales (a component in Scaly Skin spell) and roleplayed amidst all the weakling humans I found there. I instilled the power of the Clans into their minds as I casually destroyed their foes, and earned their respect by rescuing those that had gotten themselves into trouble. It is in this way that I met Moira, a Wood Elf visiting the city. She was the only person I’d yet met in Qeynos with both a backbone and a brain and she surprised me, even moreso since she was in such a small and frail body. (Barbarians truly tower over the lesser humanoids… especially gnomes) We had a pleasant conversation concerning the blend of might and magic to create true power until she had to leave.

I was soon bored with the petty challenges that Qeynos’s monsters put up, so I soon travelled back home to Halas. One thing I see in Halas that makes me very fond of the place is the brotherhood between the Clansmen. If one passes by and sees or overhears another in need, they are most eager to jump to your aid. I have been saved many times by this kindness, and many times have I returned it. If you ever travel to Halas, please keep with the tradition.

All in all, playing a barbarian shaman has been, in Ethril’s words, “a very fulfilling experience.” I would like to thank noble Urgoll, cheerful Talcea, beautiful Moira, and wise Ethril for making my time in the Peaks a pleasant one. Oh, and Blain the Entertaining deserves honorable mention, just for being the “funny man” of the Peaks.

Until next time, may the Six Hammers keep you.

Frostwolf, Knight of the Tribunal
(Aka, Burn, Everworld Staff)
