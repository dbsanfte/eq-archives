And You Say There's No Role Playing In Norrath?

by Grymm Umbra

 
 
Some of you may remember parts of the story but for the benefit of the newer members of the site, a little history is in order. Once upon a time, long, long ago, in a land called EQ Haven lived two characters who grew to be mortal enemies. The two I speak of were known as Thorvard, the Paladin and Grymm Umbra, the Tier'dal Shadowknight. The story goes something like this.

Thorvard, a ranking member of the Brotherhood of the Fist, was a kind human with a warm heart who wanted only good for the world and used his strong sword arm to help that good come to pass. Umbra, on the other hand, couldn't care less about the good of the world and was interested only in wreaking vengeance on the heads of the human racists who had slain his wife and unborn daughter.

The way they became enemies was thus: a chronicler had come into the realm of the Haven. This chronicler began telling the stories of both Thorvard and Grim Umbra for all to read. (Well, for those who were capable of such an esoteric endeavor, anyway.) As their mutual tales took shape, these two powerful and diametrically opposed characters, began to discuss certain things. At first these discussions were rather genteel and mannerly, but soon the differing opinions of the ways of the world caused hot words to flare between the two of them. I seem to recall it completely breaking down just about the time the Chronicler told the part of the story wherein The Hand of Hatred, as Umbra later came to be known, tortured a captive by ripping the scalp off of his very head in order to gain information about a nefarious group known only as the Coven; this being the group who had commited the atrocities against his wife and daughter.

Many were the ones who commented on this part of the story, nearly all saying the Tier'dal was wrong for what he had done; and thus the community branded him as evil. Hardly any were so vocifierous in their condemnation as was the good and kind Thorvard. Many were the words passed between the paladin and shadowknight and in the end it was mutually decided that the only way to settle their differences was on the field of battle.

Much time passed as the two of them tended to their own business but neither ever forgot the other. Occasional words were passed between the two of them, and then one day as Grim Umbra was speaking to the one who had replaced him as the Shield Bearer in the Disciples of Shadow, word was given him that his noble adversary had died under mysterious circumstances. Frustrated and infuriated at having his victim snatched away from his grasp, the Tier'dal grew even more morose than ever. Eventually he was able to travel the lands of Norrath, and his vitriol was a bitter cup of poison for all around him to swallow.

Now you must understand this: some of the complaints this writer has heard about the lack of roleplaying are true. There are many who travel the lands (even in its diminished beta phase) who are not interested in roleplaying. There are many ways to avoid this sort of riff-raff. Two of the most effective are to immediately turn off your option to hear shouts and OOC broadcasts. For some reason, the ones who do not wish to roleplay would seem to enjoy the ability to ruin the experience for others and will incessantly yammer about with shouts and OOC's. I had heard much about the '/ignore' command and truth to tell thought I would be making much use of it, but the fact is, no one has bothered me in an up close and personal manner as of yet and setting your options to ignore all shouts and OOC's filters out all of the garbage in a very fine manner.

I am picky and choosy about who I will and will not group with in the game. I take into consideration the fact there are many differing visions of the proper way to roleplay and I accomodate as many as I can; however, I can guarantee I will never group with someone who approaches me with the words, "Sup man, can I group with u?"

As an aside, I remember the time I was attempting to gather a war party to attack the Camp of the Deathfists. There are certain times I will turn on my capability to hear shouts and this was one of them. I shouted out across all the land that I wished to gather a mighty party to attack the Deathfist Camp and asked if there were any courageous enough to join me in this endeavor. Of course, me being who I am, I had to throw in a denigrating remark about the courage and ability of humans. The most important thing for me when roleplaying is to stay true to your character. Not so much through 'thee's and thou's' but the actual personality of the character. Grymm Umbra is friendly to no one in particular and especially hates paladins, humans and high elves.

I digress from the point and will conclude this rather quickly. Eventually, I gathered enough warriors to make the attempt on the Deathfist Camp. It was a battle like I had never been in before. Many were the deathfists who were slain but the last was certainly the most powerful. His name was Brother Lun Tol and he was deadly. All of us were fighting him and each of us was nearly dead. We thought for sure we had him as he big bastard was fading fast. It was at that precise moment that two lions, whom we had apparently disturbed with our battle, decided to join the fight. You may think after defeating the vast majority of a camp of Deathfists that a couple of little lions would be no problem. You obviously haven't met the lions of the East Commonlands.

Anyway, to make a long story short, there were two fellows sitting on the slope of a mountain getting their mana back when I went running past with the two lions in hot pursuit. I won't soon forget the comment that was made. "There goes Grymm; after all that drama about the Deathfists, he gets whipped by a lion."

Lack of roleplaying? Perhaps...do I let it bother me? No. I search out the ones who, in my opinion, play well and I stick with them.

Meanwhile back at the story: There came a day when Umbra was hunting darkweed snakes in the East Commonlands with a group of fellow Tier'dal. As he stood on the ridge overlooking the land, he heard a voice from behind. The voice said, "Grymm? The Grymm from the realm of Everworld?"

I turned to see who was speaking to me (I must admit I was incredibly flattered that someone had recognized me) and came face to face with a lovely vision in red. A very beautiful high elf magician was speaking to me in a very refined and beautiful voice. "What is it, woman?", I replied in the surliest voice I could manage. After all I do have a reputation to uphold and it would have been awful out of character for me to be nice, especially considering she was an high elf.

This woman went on to say something to the effect, "Perhaps you have heard of me. I am Elenya, I am the one who has taken over for the slain Thorvard." (I never did learn the reason Thorvard was slain. Guess he was just no fun to play.)

Well, well, well, says I to myself. This looks as if it could shape up nicely (no pun intended). I replied to the high elf beauty, "Thorvard was my hated but noble enemy. If you have inherited his duties, then you have inherited his fueds as well, woman. I swore on the grave of my wife I would defeat him on the field of battle and now that honor falls to you. You look like no match for me in your slinky red robe; unless it would be in my bed chambers."

Now Elenya got it right off the bat, see? There was no need to tell her I was roleplaying, she fell right into it with me faster than quick can get ready. Her reply ran something along the lines of, "You foolish Tier'dal, you are nothing to me. On the field of battle I would slay you easily and you will never live to see me in your bedchambers, dog."

"Ah, woman, be truthful now. Admit to yourself as well as me. The very sight of me causes things to happen to you underneath that robe, doesn't it? Admit it, woman and perhaps I'll let you walk away from this confrontation with your head still attached to your shoulders."

"You are truly ungentlemanly, Master Braggart. I would never admit to such a damnable lie and you know it."

This confrontation went on like this for the better part of an hour, I'd say. There is one thing about my particular brand of roleplaying. It takes long and long to do it. You would be well advised to play in a very patient manner. Not all of us type quickly, and actually it takes a moment or two to prepare a decent statement. Eventually it was decided that we would participate in a duel. I challenged her and she accepted. Immediately I attacked with sword and shield. Things were not going well for me at all. Her magic was powerful indeed and I felt my skin burn, blister and crack open from the spells she cast. I was quickly weakening and thought I had made a grave mistake. I didn't wish to lose this battle in front of the crowd of onlookers who had gathered to watch this wonderfully crafted roleplaying and the action packed duel afterwards.

Just when I thought all was lost, I suddenly realized the spells were being cast much less frequently and she was beginning to rely on her dagger to do the majority of her damage. Well, Grymm Umbra is no idiot, regardless of what Elenya had said about him moments before. I realized with a start that her mana was depleted and she could cast no more spells. Now it was her dagger against my sword and shield. Even though I had less than one third of my life left on the meter and Elenya had over three quarters, I now had cause to be heartened. I had withstood the deadliest power she had to throw at me and survived.

Quickly I began moving out of her sight, forcing her to have to turn around in her attempts to attack me with her dagger. I did this because I knew she would get this message, "I cannot see Grymm. How am I supposed to pierce him?" It worked well and as I danced around her, inflicting blow after blow to her unprotected back, her vital fluids oozed quickly down the bloodgutter of my sword.

It was not long before all the world of Norrath was presented the message, "Grymm has defeated Elenya in a duel to the death."

Looking back at it, I must say she was a noble foe, for many are the messages like the one above that go on with the second sentence, "Elenya has fled like a cowardly dog." Of course, my pale skinned beauty would not disgrace herself in such a manner. She fought honorably and well. Had a few more of her spells not fizzled, it would have been me lying there dead in the dirt of the passage to the Neriak Forest.

Well, there you have it, my role-playing friends. This is a true story and it happened just like I said it did. Of course, it is written from my perspective and Elenya may not see it exactly as I do but I think she will not be offended by this tale. This was easily the pinnacle of my roleplaying in EverQuest and it won't easily be matched again.

So if you wish, you keep listening to the naysayers, those ones who say that roleplaying is dead in EQ....and perhaps was never alive at all. I say thee nay because this much is true, as long as Elenya and Grymm travel the land, you will find true characters played to the hilt.

Be well.
