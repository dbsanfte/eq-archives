Wisps of smoke rose lazily upward from the brightly burning fire. The fire danced, throwing shadows on the large hall filled with tables, chairs, and food. Pleasantly pungent smells came from behind a wooden door at one end of the hall. A burst of laughter broke the silence followed by a company of four. They entered the hall in a line, speaking and laughing as only old friends can.

"Come now my good mage, do you seriously expect me to believe that tale of yours?" The speaker walked into the light of the fire revealing a rather short stature.

The figure behind the halfling clapped him smartly on the shoulder. "You would be wise to listen to Elvin tales my short friend. You might even become a bit more proficient in your skill." The young elvin wizard gazed down at the halfling and smiled.

"The only thing I wish to become at the moment is full, my poor belly yearns for some honey backed meat." answered the halfling sitting himself behind the table.

The elf sat himself on the right of the halfling and beckoned the rest of the company forward. A gnome and an elf strode forward. Both wore long blue cloaks about their bodies with their hoods drawn low. The gnome was followed by a rather odd contraption forged of steel, and adorned with red glass. It walked just a step or two behind the gnome, its steel feet clanging upon the marble floor. As the gnome sat himself it stopped by his chair and fell silent. Meanwhile the blue-clad elf sat himself across from the rest bowing his head.

"My fair friends," begun the halfling with a sigh. "Seeing as how this is our last meal together why don't we spend it as true friends with no barriers."

The gnome glanced quickly at his blue clad companion, then at the other two. The mage tapped his staff upon the floor and rose. "I'm sorry brother but I'm afraid Tarn is not familiar with your customs, he is a bit shall we say, lacking in that area."

"Quite all right brother," answered the gnome. But made no move to remove his hood.

Growling the halfling stood and made his way towards the other side of the hall. His receding form was soon lost in the dim light of the hall. His companions watched as he left, then reassumed a discussion of magic started earlier that day.

As soon as Tarn was sure he was out of sight he bolted for a wooden door not twenty feet from where he stood. Throwing the door open he made his way forward not bothering to light a torch. Within seconds he was climbing stone steps in what seemed like a spiral staircase. Finally the steps came to a somewhat abrupt halt, and a large door loomed before him. Tentatively the halfling knocked upon it and stepped back, as a drop of fear leaked into his heart. He waited a few moments and knocked again, his only answer was an echo of the knock jumping from wall to wall. Swallowing hard Tarn gently pushed the door to no avail, locked.

A strange smile spread over his lips as he reached for something in his cloak. Quickly he drew out a set of lock pics making short work of the lock. Once again Tarn gently prodded the door, it swung open with a low hiss. Without a sound the halfling stepped inside. The darkness was too much even for him. It seemed to engulf everything, not even his own hands could be seen inches from his face. Cursing softly Tarn took out a bundle and opened it. A weak red light surrounded his hand emanating from a small stone.

Muttering, "This is it," to himself he began searching the room. Large tomes and scrolls were stacked everywhere. A seemingly endless sea of knowledge was all around him. Gold coins littered a stout wooden desk in a corner, but his gaze passed over them without halting. Countless minutes swam by until finally his eyes rested on an open chest. Tarn's brow was covered with light beads of sweat as he reached inside the chest and pulled out a scroll. With a nervous jerk he unrolled the scroll and looked it over.

Nodding to himself he quickly searched out a quill and with a trembling hand wrote on the scroll. A sigh of relief escaped Tarn as he replaced the scroll and made his way back down towards the hall of the feast.

The hall was now filled with people, dining, talking, laughing, and singing. A strange song was carried over the table towards where the halfling stood. All though he knew the tune, the words were more than just foreign to him. It sat heavily on his heart, forcing all thought from his mind. Tarn made his way slowly towards his seat. A strange daze seemed to have overtaken him, he had hardly noticed it when his companions hailed him.

"Tarn! We thought you would miss this lavish feast."

The halfling looked at the mage and forced himself to smile, "Me miss a feast? That indeed would be an anomaly my fiend." Without another word Tarn sat himself behind the table and looked around. All sorts of dishes and wines were spread on the large tables, but for some reason none attracted him this night.

The rest of the feast passed by as if enveloped in a thick mist. By the end Tarn could only recall one song that would not leave his mind.

Behind me stands the keep,
Before, there lies the sea.
Behind with home there is no deed,
But beyond exists a key.

Away from nightmares I will fly.
The waning stars shall show my way
Through mist and nightly sky.
But all will end in just one way.

Without the light,
I'll not succeed.
And loose my way for good.

There was more but Tarn could not seem to recall the rest. By the end of the night most of the hall had been emptied and even his companions had retired. Slowly Tarn made his way to his room and into his bed, where he feel asleep at once.

But the halfling was not fated to sleep a restful sleep. Strange images came to him, prodding him, waking him several times in one hour. Red eyes seemed to follow him in his dreams, freezing the halfling with fear. An all too familiar image of a dying tree returned haunting his very soul. Finally his sleep was broken by the gracious light of a candle, and a familiar voice.

"Wake friend, it seems that you are leaving after all. It seems the Elder had overlooked your name on the scroll last time."

The halfling opened his eyes and looked at the young elvin mage. The elf's face was drawn and tiered, lines of worry could be made out in the pale light of the candle.

"You look as though you've had a worse night than I," Tarn chuckled trying to lift his spirits.

The elf looked piercingly at the halfling, revealing to him that he knew of last night's deeds. "I am not sure if it was worse, but it was nonetheless filled with evil omens. But there is not time for idle talk, the stars are dying and we must go." With that the elf left the room.

The halfling stood shivering on the deck of the second ship. His sparse belongings had been stowed and now the departure was at hand. Tarn breathed the cold air with relief, he was about to be free of his torments, and pains. A new world awaited beyond the sea, and that was where his key was.
