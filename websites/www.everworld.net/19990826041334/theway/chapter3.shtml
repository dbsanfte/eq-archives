NIGHT OF PARTINGS
The long arms of the night tentatively withdrew out of the reach of the rising sun. The sky glowed with a red hue, melting perfectly into the horizon. Two lone ships were slowly making their way towards the same horizon.
"Land dead ahead!" The cry rang throughout the two ships. The final end of the journey was drawing ever closer. Out of the morning mist rose an isle. It's small beach dotted with trees grew clearer as the two boats made their way for it.
The night before, a short council was held, those who attended were the remaining Leaders. When the council came to an end word was spread that there will be a stop on an isle next to the mainland. None wondered about the decision and all looked forward to it. The last weeks had done nothing but lower the morale of the travelers, the loss of their friends lay heavily upon their hearts. Very few could go a day without recalling the storm and wondering if it wasn't a prediction of the now near future. But still there was one thought in all that occupied the ships, the Colleges must be founded, and the word must be spread.
Gently, the two ships came to rest as close to the beach as they dared. Small boats were once again lowered and filled. Back and forth they went until all but a mere handful that volunteered remained on the ships as watchers. Wasting no time small groups moved further up the island, and into the forest. Within minutes the air was filled with the singing of axes as trees were cut down and made into tinder. Other groups set about clearing land and forming a large circle on the edge of the forest.
The sun was just beginning its descent from the zenith when pleasant aromas spread over the isle. Tender meat brought all the way from home was quietly roasting on open fires. The cooks walked back and forth, dicing herbs, crushing leaves, and preparing the humble feast. The boats came for the last time, bearing not people but kegs of Elvin vine, and stout Dwarven beer.
Those who had nothing to do, or finished their tasks, walked about the isle relaxing their minds. Some were even catching up on their sleep in the shade of the lush forest. A sweet singing quietly rose out of the forest, at times growing louder, then dropping till it could hardly be heard. In the center of the forest a group of bards sat in a small circle tuning their instruments and singing together. If ever, tonight would be the night when their best talents would be called upon. Morale had to be raised before the mainland was reached.
Not far from the bards but far enough not to be seen, under the shade of the trees sat a halfling. With his back to a large oak he reclined with his eyes closed listening to the singing, and the occasional laughter. Just as the beginnings of sleep began over-taking him, footsteps sounded quite close by. The halfling opened his eyes and saw one of the bards striding towards him with a smile. Without standing, the halfling followed the bard with his gaze until the bard stopped before him. He then swiftly rose to his feet and bowed curtly, laughing, the bard returned the bow and sat before the halfling.
"Sit, sit, little friend." The bard motioned to the halfling and smiled once again. Nodding the halfling once again sat, reclining against the oak.
"I have spoken with the Leaders, and they tell me that you will be departing for the city of Rivervale alone."
The halfling smiled and nodded. "You have heard the truth Gelod, I will be heading there alone."
"I have come to personally wish you luck for the future. But I can not stay long, others await me just as you have waited. But know this Tarn, my songs will follow throughout your journeys. I know it's naught but words now, but I hope that in times of need my songs will come to your aid." With that Gelod stood, bowed, and strode away into the woods.

The day grew old and the shadows lengthened, fires were lit and everyone gathered. The night overtook the day quickly and without falter. One by one the stars returned floating above the isle. The moon was on its way to fullness, smoothly it cleared the treetops and rode high in the sky. Its pale light diminished by that of the giant fires of the adventurers.
Around the fires gathered the men and women of the secret island. Food and drink was served almost immediately. The meat still smoking from its day long cooking over the slow fires. The wine and beer flowed freely and none lacked either meat or drink. By the time the moon once again descended into the trees, most of the eating was over. Here and there some took a piece of their favorite food and chewed slowly, no longer out of hunger but for pure pleasure. The drinks still came freely, with everyone’s chalices kept full to their brims. Laughter broke out occasionally here and there, words rose above whispers as conversations were engaged.
Later all became aware of quiet music seeping into their conversations. It came from the group of bards now sitting together playing quietly. As the conversation died down the music picked up, louder and louder until it ruled the night. Just as the music reached its zenith Gelod rose and added his Elvin voice to the music. His voice blended flawlessly with the instruments, and those blended seamlessly with the sounds of the night.

Think, would it not be Sweet to live with me All alone, my child, my love?-- Sleep together, share All things in that fair Country you remind me of? Charming in the dawn There, the half-withdrawn Drenched, mysterious sun appears In the curdled skies, Treacherous as your eyes Shining behind your tears.

There, restraint and order bless Luxury and voluptuousness

We should have a room Never out of bloom Tables polished by the palm Of the vanished hours Should reflect rare flowers In that amber-scented calm; Ceilings richly wrought, Mirrors deep as thought, Walls with eastern splendor hung,-- All should speak apart To the homesick heart In its own dear native tongue, There restraint and order bless Luxury and voluptuousness

See, their voyage past, To their moorings fast, On the still canals sleep, These big ships; to bring You some trifling thing They have braved the furious deep, --Now the sun goes down, Tinting dyke and town, Field, canal, all things in sight, Hyacinth and gold; All that we behold Slumbers in its ruddy light.

ENTRANCE
When all came to an end, none got up or left. They merely slept where they were, until the bright rays of the morning sun tickled their faces to awake. A light wind was blowing playfully in the leaves of the forest, making them sound alive. A few gray clouds floated lazily across the sky changing shape as they went.
For the final time the council met, the travelers were separated and assigned to the proper ships. Each ship had its specific destinations in Norrath, and no exceptions would be made. After a somewhat sparse breakfast the druids thanked the forest for its gracious hosting and once more the ships went on. This time the voyage was not at all a lengthy one, within two hours the mainland was reached and without a word the two ships parted.
In silence each ship swam on, the completion of its task lay close at hand. Throughout the day the ships docked at many different ports, and once again without a word those that had to disembarked, and the rest went on in silence. And so it went till the skies once again dimmed with the fading day, and the last of the Founders set foot on their new home.
Apart they all stood, separated by many leagues, facing the dying sun. But grouped they all were in spirit and purpose. None will stand in the way of progress; the light has been lit and now the true test has begun.

They have braved the furious deep, --Now the sun goes down, Tinting dyke and town, Field, canal, all things in sight, Hyacinth and gold; All that we behold Slumbers in its ruddy light.
