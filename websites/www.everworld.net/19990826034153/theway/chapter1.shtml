Awakening
The morning fog quietly moved over the smooth waters towards the lone island, just as the sun made its presence known in the valley. Soft footfalls echoed in the silent morning, footfalls of the tired guards returning from their duty. Their hoods were pulled low and their torches held high as they marched across the narrow bridge and into the citadel. It had been a long night, cold, and perilous. Once again the Ice Giants were setting out of their northern dwellings and once again they were heading south. But for now the peace was unbroken and the first attack was turned away.

A bell rang sharply and clearly from somewhere in the citadel, in its wake the valley sprung to life. The still air churned and changed, it carried in it a sense of excitement and joy. Today was the day the Council of Leaders was gathering, the Elves from across the sea would speak today. All who dwelt in the citadel were encouraged to attend.

Council
The large hall filled quickly with the men and women of the Valley ,a quiet murmur was just beginning when the Leaders came into the hall. As one all voices stopped, and all eyes were focused upon the Leaders. Behind the Leaders came a group of six strangely clad elves. The Leaders took their seats at a large white marble table carved into the shape of a crescent moon. The strange elves were led to the center of the hall and given silk pillows to sit on. When all of the preparations were complete the Elder Leader rose to his feet holding his staff above him. He then hit the butt of the staff upon the marble table, a brilliant blue flame erupted from the opposite end of the staff.

"I Hold The Light." were his words, and the council began.

The Elders spoke little during the council, their attention was given to the six elves who spoke of the outside world. They told many stories, and sung many songs, some telling of great victories while others mourned great losses. A great deal had changed in the world since the day that all contact was severed between the hidden isle and the other lands. But above all one thing stood out in the minds of all that were present. The lands of Norrath had lost most of their ancient lore, without it they were slowly slipping towards darkness.

By the end of the day the council had ended and a decision had been made. The Elders would send out groups of men and women across the seas and back into Norrath. The groups were given the task of founding Colleges to bring back the old lore. All that were not evil of heart were to be welcomed and taught. No one was forced on the journey and all that wished to decline could. For the task would be difficult at best if not impossible at all, no one declined.

A New History
Winter fell upon the lone isle, howling winds tore at its walls. Great snow drifts covered all roads within it, and the sun was obscured for weeks on end. But those in the valley did not sit passively before their fires. From dawn till dusk sounds of hammers upon metal, and saws on wood filled their lives. In the face of winter great boats began taking shape within the harbor walls. Day after day the winds assaulted the workers, and time after time they lit fires and laughed at the winds. Again and again the guards returned tiered from the nightly clashes with the Giants.

Finally winter relented to spring, but the workers kept on with the outmost of speed. Masts were raised and oars shaped as time ran on. Until one day the hammer sat idle, the saw lay quiet, and the ships stood proudly upon the waters. The last preparations were made and the Council met for the final time. A great feast was planned that night, all were asked to attend.

The feast was the biggest in history of the isle. Great dishes cooked by master elves were presented (much to the delight of the halflings). Songs of courage and might were sung by the best of the bards, and a good time was had by all.

Next day before the soft sun had awakened, under the light of the waning stars. The boats were boarded, and final advice was given. At last when the last star had died the Elder came forth and once again called upon the blue fire. This time the adventurers to be, called out. "I Hold The Light!"

A new history has begun.
