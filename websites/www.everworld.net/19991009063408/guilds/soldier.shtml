SOLDIER

by Burn

 
 
Guild Name: SOLDIER
Interviewer Name: Dark Schneider and Phoebias
Position: 1st and 2nd Lords of Havoc
Members: 10, only the Elite
Address: http://www.eq-soldier.com


What brings together the members of SOLDIER?

SOLDIER strives to be the best of the best, and have an elitist attitude when accepting new members. (think Top Gun) Their ultimate goal is to control Norrath with an iron fist, balanced by the scales of justice. Members must want to take part in this and be dedicated to upholding law. Also, the total genocide of ogres and trolls.

How do you intend to accomplish these goals?

The Lords of SOLDIER wish to gather the mightiest PvP+ players and become the foremost gathering of PvP+ guys this side of Innoruk's home by destroying all rivals and taking their women. (female players beware)

What dictates who you do and do not attack?

They attack only those who pose a threat or are potential rivals for guild dominance. And ogres and trolls.

Do you have any in-game ambitions so far?

The Lords of Havoc I spoke to said that they really intend to solidify the guild before making any movements toward their grand plan of global conquest. Wise move, in my opinion.

What relations do you currently have with other guilds, if any?

SOLDIER has neither allies nor enemies yet, but it seems that they expect that to change in the future. But they do consider all ogre/troll guilds their enemies. (the Skorchers come to mind)

How exactly is the guild run?

Members of SOLDIER are divided into 4 classes, 3rd, 2nd, and 1st class, as well as the mighty Elite. A member's class will be decided based on level relative to others in the guild. Members of a higher class have command over lowers, but nothing like complete dominance. The absolute best are assigned to a Special Task Force.

The Lords of Havoc are titles only, and the guild is run more or less like a democracy, with the members' best interests in mind. Beyond certain rules, members are allowed free reign.

What requirements/restrictions are there to joining SOLDIER?

Prospective members must be willing to follow the Prime Directive, and be approved by the three Lords of Havoc. Beyond that, just no ogres or trolls.

Why such a hatred of the ogres and trolls?

They're ugly. And they stink.

What alignment (in the traditional AD&D sense) best describes SOLDIER?

Chaotic Neutral. (but with a sense of honor that often is not included in that scope)

Is the guild closely tied together, or are members allowed to more or less go their own path?

SOLDIERs are closely tied together, but are allowed to join other guilds as they wish so long as the two organizations are not opposed to one another. Any cowardly, dishonorable, or dastardly act by a member of SOLDIER will be dealt with harshly.

Would you give a final few words to our readers out there summarizing SOLDIER?

"In the end, our guild shall stand above all others and we will crush anyone who stands in our way… and take their women!"

Burn's thoughts on SOLDIER

After the conclusion of the interview, I was shocked by Phoebias's question of where the review would be posted and what the URL was. Needless to say, I chastised him and got promises from both that they had it bookmarked! Anyway… SOLDIER seems to be a pretty interesting concept. Imagine. A PvP guild run like a democracy, with no ill will toward anyone! And no "tolls!!" And a fierce hatred of ogres and trolls!!! They should get along well with most of the other guilds out there except for power freak guilds who might see them as a rival. (LoP comes to mind…) While I don't really understand the idea of a "PvP guild," it seems that enough people do to form a fair sized guild, even with their elitist attitude. You'll have to excuse the unfinished website, it's brand spanking new and Dark's trying to piece it together as he goes. Doesn't look bad though…
