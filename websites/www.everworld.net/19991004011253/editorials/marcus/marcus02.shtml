Alas, Dulen is Dead:

by Marcus Mcleode

 
 
When last we spoke, my half-elf warrior was going to try to make it from Qeynos to the Commonlands and Freeport. For this journey, I teamed up with a ranger. After taking a few minutes to ready myself with food and water, we set out. Then the world came to an end. Well, Qeynos did anyway. I took a two minute break and was back into Norrath. Unfortunately, I lost my would-be travel companion. After asking for her (I forgot her name, sorry) for a few minutes, I headed out on my own. Boy what a mistake that was. My warrior, being level seven, was ill-prepared for the lone journey. On my last trip around the Karana Plains, there were few monsters around, this time, the land was teaming with life. All of it deadly. Giant spiders, grizzly bears, and hill giants abounded. While I was engaged in a battle with a giant spider, I got a message from my female companion that she was also in the plains and would meet me at the nearest guard tower. After dispatching the spider, with some help by the way, I met her at the tower and we made our way east. A short time later, we met up with a grizzly. I being the warrior, took up the task of being the target of its assault. Alas, the bear proved too much for me and my companion. My friend and I fought bravely for five minutes only to succumb to the claws of this mighty grizzly. I awoke back in Qeynos with nothing to wear, and nothing to eat. I immediately made my way back to my corpse and my companion. After resting for a minute, we made our way east once more. This time, it was another giant spider. Once again, I took the brunt of the battle upon myself while allowing the ranger to cast her spells on myself and the beast. Alas, once again it was to no avail and I perished again. After finding my way to my corpse for the second time in thirty minutes, my friend decided that she was better off on her own, and I decided I was better off in Qeynos. We parted ways, and I bade her good luck on her journey. While on my way back to Qeynos, I happened upon a lioness. I tried my best to stay clear of this magnificent animal, but she attacked me anyway. I tried my best to defend myself. After a minute of never getting in even one hit, I decided it was time to run. With half of my life gone, I headed west and hopefully to Qeynos. When I crossed the zone boundary, I don’t think I had more than ten points of life left. This is when I decided that I had had enough of Dulen the warrior and his shortcomings. I got myself a real press account so that I could take screenshots to go along with these articles, and created Dulak (yes, I wanted something close to Dulen) another half-elf. This time though, I decided to go with the ranger class. Dulak started his life in Surefall Glades. This land is just north of the large town of Qeynos. Now, so that I wouldn’t be writing about the same thing all over again, I petitioned a GM to buff Dulak up to level ten. Yeah, it’s cheating, but who’s counting? I made my way immediately to the trainer in Surefall. I trained my little half-elf heart out. Hey, I had ten levels of training to make up. Right after that, I figured I’d spend a few hours in Blackburrow getting revenge on all the gnolls that had killed me in my former life as Dulen.

As it turns out, tenth level characters are just about perfect for Blackburrow Dungeon. Trains are still a problem, but I have noticed over the past few days more and more role-players are making their way to Qeynos. That adds a great deal to the atmosphere of EQ, but it makes the dungeon that much more crowded. At times, there were too many players in the dungeon, to the point where you had to fight over gnoll scraps. At other times, you spent the evenings running from a group of ten or twenty gnolls that have decided that you’re on their menu. With Dulak however, I quickly became bored with Qeynos and decided that it was time to join up with other staff members on the other side of Antonica. Once again, I ventured into the Karana Plains. This time, it was different though. Being a ranger, the local fauna left me to myself for the entire journey. I only had to deal with the few hill giants and such that will attack anything that gets in their way. I spent the better part of an hour or so just reaching the next city on the map, Highpass Hold. Upon reaching the area of Highpass, I found some beautiful carvings in the sides of the mountain pass I was in. (Picture One and Two) The city of Highpass is also a sight to see. And to get lost in. As I was wandering around the town, an orc attacked me. Screenshot Three, That’s right, right inside of this desolate town. It’s not all that common in Qeynos, so I was very surprised and not the lease prepared. I managed to get him half-way dead when I decided discretion was the better part of survival and ran. By chance, I found myself the one guard that was posted at the city entrance and he helped me to defeat this orc. Because I was able to weaken the orc substantially before the guard started in on it, and because I landed the final blow, the experience and loot were mine for the taking. Note that this was an extreme example of how the guards work. Not all of them are supreme warriors. In fact, some of them are the equivalent of the volunteer security police at parks and stadiums. Part-timers in a sense. It was in Highpass Hold that I met up with my two travel companions. Martyn and Iioliioliiol. Or something like that. Screenshot Four. Iiol (for short) was kind enough to guide Martyn and I out of Highpass and into Kithicok Woods, and from there, he told us the way to the West Commonlands. Once again, I thank you Iioliioliiol. I hope I finally got your name right. Martyn and I ran across Kithicok, through the West Commonlands, and finally into the East Commonlands. Without so much as a single fight. Without even coming close to any real threats as a matter of fact. Finally, we made into Freeport and found a kind magician to bind us there. After that long of a trip, I did not look forward to having to travel that route again. All together, the trip took me about two hours, maybe two and a half. Martyn headed on towards Faydwer and Kaladim while I stayed behind in hopes to link up with Grymm the Shadowknight Drow. While observing the local wildlife, I came across an orc camp for the first time. Deathfist camp. As I watched, I saw shamans casting on the soldiers of the group and decided that although individually, I could take one any of the soldiers, the shaman tipped the scales of power well into their favor. So, I continued on. As I walked, I came across a group of men taking on a Puma and decided to lend my blade. Being a ranger, this is a poor decision, but I was getting tired of just looking. The puma proved to be of no challenge whatsoever. After the brief battle was done, I spoke to one of the other warriors. As we conversed, another one broke into our conversation to warn us of the griffin behind us. I turned around to see a VERY large creature bearing down upon us. While I backed away, hoping that it had not, and would not, lock onto me as a target, it attacked my companion. In a heartbeat, it turned on me. I turned to run to the zone boundary which was just a few seconds away, but I was dead within five seconds. Qeynos was never like this. The high-level npc’s there only attacked if you initiated the battle. I did nothing to this griffen. Oh well, at least I made sure to be bound to Freeport before taking my walk. For the next couple of days, I walked around the East and West Commonlands alone. Although I had died a couple of times, and my level 11 Ranger had fallen to a level 10, I was still confident that I could make a life here if only I could find a good group to join. Alas, no group would have me as I was too high a level for them. I decided to head to Kaladim to enjoy the scenery there instead for a while. Another boat ride. and thirty minutes later, I found myself at the gates of Kaladim. After taking a stroll around the city, I wandered out into the countryside. It was here that I managed to gather up a party to take out some of the local goblin tribes. Baldaur and I took on the goblins while a cleric by the name of ?????? kept us healthy and took on one goblin at a time herself. The battles were magnificent to witness. The goblin wizards and shamans are by far the most difficult to defeat and require at least two people to handle him. At least, two level ten characters. A single level fifteen could probably manage just fine. Well, that’s where I left Norrath last night. After sharing the wealth with my cleric friend, I made my way to Kaladim and logged out for some sleep. After all, it was six o’clock in the morning here and I needed to get up sometime that day.
