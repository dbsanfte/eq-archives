The Riches of Norrath

by Zeras

 
 
Ah money. It really does make the world go round. Think about it. It buys you better food, better weapons and armor, and more spells. In turn, that lets you kill more things or tougher things. Getting money can be difficult. Usually you'll stick to killing monsters and selling the stuff, but you can do some other things. These are alternative ways to getting money, and if you're a little creative or entreprenual, you can grab some extra cash. There are a few groups, ones that can easily play extra trades, and those who have a tougher time. There are also odd class, shamans, that have to be more creative to earn stuff.

THE HIGH END GROUP:
BARD - RANGER - WIZARD - CLERIC - DRUID - ENCHANTER - ROUGE

THE LOW END GROUP:
MAGICIAN - NECROMANCER - PALADIN - SHADOW KNIGHT - MONK - WARRIOR

THE ODD GROUP:
SHAMAN

THE HIGH END

BARDS:
What can't you do would be a better question. Your songs, which can speeden, strengthen, heal, Revitalize, and hurt, can tremendously aid others and makes you hot party property. If you are a little extra risky, you can charge for a "round" of healing. Define whatever a round is, say a bar of the person's HP, and charge money. If you don't mind travelling all over the world through running, hire yourself out as a guide/guard and use the speed and vigor songs to aid your friends. You can probably easily think up others.

CLERIC:
Clerics, specilizing in healing, should not charge money. It makes you less likely to join a group and get experience. Generally, you can stand by fighting area resting spots, such as gates of a city or enterance/exit of a dungeon or fighting zone. Ask for donations though, many will be happy to help out a friendly cleric

DRUID:
You can do a few things. At low levels, provide tracking like Rangers can. Heal for extra cash donations, and scout using your outdoor skills. Later levels, you can make the large money with your wonderful group teleportation spells.

ENCHANTER:
You have a myrid of spells that can affect others, espcially warriors, in ways they would love. Charge a small fee for a strength spell, or a large fee for spells that double their hp.

RANGER:
Tracking and Foraging. Both of these skills are attractive to groups, and more so to people on their own with coin to spare. Sell both for recompense.

ROUGE:
Pickpocket. You don't need to help anyone do it, but you can make a insane amount of money at later levels (15+)

WIZARD:
A little tough, but you can do several things. Watch over a warrior or group until the monster is below ½ life. Once it is, destroy it with your spells. You can also stand watch in dangerous zones while the party or person kills the monster, and warn them if other things arrive. If your strong, you can even hold them off until the warrior is finished.



THE LOW END

MAGICIANS/NECROMANCERS:
You have a few attack spells in which to do the same as a wizard, and you have a wonderful ability to summon "tanks". Keep them weakened below the killer and the monster, and use it to occupy the monsters attention and give some aided damage. Make sure your pet doesn't do more than ½ of the monsters life, or you may find yourself out of the job.

MONK:
Your only real option is tank. Your abilties don't really help any other way.

PALADINS/SHADOW KNIGHTS:
You can tank somewhat, and you can also do a few other things. Lay hand for the paladin double your client's HP and harm touch cuts the monster's in half. Just don't kill it on accident

WARRIORS:
Tanks. Your best at it, so hire yourself out to do it.



THE ODD GROUP

SHAMANS:
You can do a enchanter, a magician and a cleric at once. Try all three.


OTHER TIPS:

1)You'll work better in a group that splits stuff, no matter your class. You get EXP and people are less adverse to it.

2)Start verrrry small. Lower than a copper until your level 10 or so, then start charging more for high end spells.

3)This is by no means a way of life. You need to get out there and grab some experience for yourself.

4)If your really a dedicated business man, pick up the trade skills. The armor and weapons you can sell and reform will give you a wonderful time.

That's about it. Experiment with it, and see what extra cash you can make. Keeping at it is the only way this pays off, and keep them levels climbing. It can only get better when you're powerful.

-Zeras-
