Hail and well met fellow travelers!

by Dain Ironhand

 
 
The seasons have turned many times since last we talked. Yes, it has been a long cold winter in Norrath ere we last laid eyes on each other, and indeed, much has happened. Here, sit by the fire, rest your weary bones and let it lighten the night. Spring should be here soon, but not just yet. What’s that you say? You cannot remember my name? No friend, there is no need to reproach yourself, the road has been long and hard. Let me refresh your memory. I am Dain of the Iron Hand or as many know me, Dain Ironhand. My tale is long, best told in small bites over a good mug of ale. But I forget my manners, you are famished as any eye can see, let me get you refreshment. Here we are friend, the best the house has to offer. The ale is Norrath Stout, although I prefer the spiced cider myself. The meat is as tender as a new born lamb, but mind you, it is hot! The potatoes are from the south, from where I am not sure, but the proprietor is especially proud of them. Ahh, that, yes, that is a little different. Go ahead and try it, you will find the taste exquisite! See, I told you friend, you will never partake of its’ equal, indeed it is my personal favorite. Go ahead have another pinch, you can work off the extra stones later. Yes, yes, my tale. Where was I now?

Well, I was born of good, hardy dwarven stock in Kaladim, on the isle of Faydwer. My early years were rather common, with no happenings of consequence. I apprenticed with Larok Slatebreaker, a smith of great repute I might add. What? Don’t be shy, indeed, you were famished. Let me order you another plate my friend. Now, where was I? Ahh, yes, to continue my tale…

Well friends, it is time to step out of character and into the here and now. In this column we will discuss the art of building and role playing a character in the land of Norrath. After some careful thought, I decided that the best way to do that would be to cover the different races of Norrath one at a time. In addition to the lore to be found within the game itself, we will include information from the many fantasy realms that have been created over the years. Let’s start off by talking about what role playing is. Fantasy literature is replete with stories of ordinary individuals who are thrust into extraordinary circumstances. The said individuals must brave perils beyond belief, overcome tremendous odds and slay untold monsters to succeed at their quests. To some extent, we role play every day. We might not have to brave perils everyday (of course if you drive the Southern California freeways, you might qualify) or slay monsters on a regular basis. We do have to face the everyday problems of life though, interact with others, make decisions, pay bills and the like. Who knows better how to role play your life than you? Who knows more about your likes and dislikes? What makes you happy and sad? You might be rolling your eyes in your head right now at this comparison but if you will think about it for a moment, you’ll see that I am right. To really flesh a character out you must give him all the emotions and feelings that you have. Not the exact ones that you have mind you, but similar ones that are all his own. He needs to become a living, breathing person in your mind. So what does it take to do this? The first step is thought. Yes that’s right, thought. You need to make him more than a few figures, a name and random statistics. Set up the “personal setting” so to speak. Immerse yourself in the character you want to create. Now I am not saying that you need to have a conversation with him at all hours of the day. Nor am I saying that you should assume the characteristics and idiosyncrasies of your creation. What I am saying is that you need to put some serious thought in him if you really want to role play all the way. The questions you need to ask yourself are many. Let’s list a few to get you started. Working on what we talked about above, let’s start with these. Is he good at making decisions? What are his likes and dislikes? What makes him happy and sad? Once you have those answered, we’ll add some more to the list.

What does he look like when he walks? Where did he come from and why is he here? What are the little things that make him different from the norm? Why does he have that limp? Is one leg shorter than the other? Was he born that way or is it a battle wound? If it is a battle wound, where was the battle? Who was he fighting? You might think that this is a lot, but not really. As you go along, you will find yourself answering questions with more questions. If you just follow your instincts and answer the everything that comes to mind, eventually you will have your character laid out in your mind. Then comes the fun part. I hope you would have made some notes up to this point of the creation process, but it is time to get serious about it now. Organize your thoughts and start writing them down. Once you get your general thoughts down in ink, take it one step further and start writing a history for him. This does not have to be real in-depth from point A to point B. It can start as a random thought here and there, working into paragraphs and eventually some short tales. Given the time and energy, there is no telling what you can do with it. Let’s go over the narrative at the beginning to give you some ideas of how far you could take this. Now mind you, Dain has been bouncing around in my head for a while, but is no where near complete. What was I trying to accomplish here? Well for starters, I hopefully gave you a time frame (evening), an idea of the season (“a long cold winter in Norrath… Spring should be here soon…”). Then I wanted to throw in some personal traits by telling you that he is fond of spiced cider (The Ale is Norrath Stout, although I prefer their spiced cider myself.”) and rich food (“never partake of its’ equal, indeed it is my personal favorite.”) I then went on and added some personal information such as, he was born in Kaladim, unexciting youth, good parents. He is also known as Dain of the Iron Hand. Think there might be a tale or two brewing there? For all intents and purposes he is or has been a Blacksmith (“I apprenticed with Larok Slatebreaker, a smith of great repute I might add.”) so he we have a profession. He is probably a little older than younger (“My tale is long, best told in small bites…”) and there we have his age. The word count for all of that comes in at three-hundred and change. It is really not a lot, but hey it is a start. But that is what you have to do, start! Once you take that first step, your on the way. It won’t be easy but it will be worth it in the end. A friend recently shared his story about his character with me. It is well written and without a doubt, you finish the tale with an excellent idea of who his character is. It reads more like a novella than a short story coming in at seventeen-thousand plus words! That might be more effort than most are willing to put out or have time for but who said you had to do that much? One paragraph is better than nothing! So give it some thought everyone, sit down tonight and start pondering what you want your character to be like. At the same time, send me your ideas on role playing the different races of Norrath. This is a group effort, and the more input I receive, the better these articles are going to be. We have an incredible world waiting for us, let’s create some characters that are worthy of it.
