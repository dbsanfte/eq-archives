First Impressions

by Lillith

 
 
I'm sitting alone on the docks in Rivervale, fishing to my little heart's content. There's a bit of splashing every time someone jumps in the water to kill the nasty piranhas, but it's quiet for the most part. Now, you have to understand something about being a human in Rivervale. You are tall, very tall. Not to mention you have double the charisma of the entire population. Not to say this is a bad thing, it does wonders for a girl's self-esteem and confidence.

I turn my face upwards into the setting sun, close my eyes and smile as a breeze blows through my hair. I feel a tug on my pole and I look down. My line breaks again -- It's about time I call it a day.

As I'm bending over my pack of fish and bait, trading my pole for my dagger when I feel glaring eyes on me. Not just in the way that someone is looking at you, but -staring- at you. I stand up and spin around to see 3 very small wide-eyed men, two Halflings and one Dwarf. I smile and greet them. They just snicker. I look over myself to make sure I don't have some fish bait draped across my cleavage or a fishhook stuck on my blouse. Nope, just me.

"So boys, what's so funny?"

No response, so I return to my fish, organizing and inspecting them for sale.

Kellimat pinches Lillith on the hiney.
I spin around in awe. They are all staring up at me, huge wicked grins on their faces.

I smile back tilting my head to one side. Still, they say nothing. I shake my head and laugh, returning to my fish.

I hear one halfling whisper, "Izn't she purty? Do yaz think she'd go wit us out 'n to da forest ta hunt?"

"We'd get more loot, that's for sure. She'd charm all the monsters!" said the dwarf.

I turn back around and look at them.

"If you wanted me to come hunting with you, all you needed to do was ask." I smiled and winked at them. After gathering my fishing pole and gear I wandered into the dock shop, ducking through the door. I greeted the vendor, selling off my extra fish and the sandals I drudged up from the bottom of the lake. I put the money into my pocket, ducking out the other door on my way to the bank.

After depositing my cash, pole and bait with the teller I turn to leave, but am blocked by my little entourage of admirers.

They look rather despondent and the smiles they'd sported earlier were gone. I stopped and sighed, rustling the halflings already untidy hair. " I'll go with you...." And with that they cheered and invited me into the group. I cast spells on their already strong bodies, giving them shielding and strength.

Now, this isn't necessarily how everyday goes, but you'd be surprised how often I get this sort of thing. Often times it's not nearly as up front and invasive, but I do get many ooh's and ahh's as I travel around the town and when I am hunting.

I could have taken offense to Kellimat's pinch, but I didn't. Unless I feel a person's intention is negative I will always give them the common courtesy, and benefit of the doubt, that anyone deserves.

Perhaps it's that not many people in Rivervale have seen a human enchanter. In fact, I believe I am the only one who calls Rivervale home, at least I have yet to see another of my type. But, I believe if I were a male, I wouldn't get near the amount of attention that I do.

From my very fist steps out in the streets of my birthplace (Freeport) the biggest influence I've noticed in the way people treat one another is their gender (with a close second to race). I can only assume the difference; I've seen how other males treat each other but since I am not a male myself, I really can't say for 100% sure what exactly it's like. But I can guess.

In Freeport there are a variety of races running around (and I'm sure that has some influence too) but race aside, since Freeport has an over abundance of Humans, my experience thus far has been interesting. When first discovering my gifts as an enchanter I made myself known in the west gate, casting strength spells on everyone passing by. Soon after slaying every rat, bat and snake in site, I made my to the East Commonlands.

This was where I first began to discover interaction with others. I made friends with a few other magic users. A few wizards and magicians... a paladin here and there, but most of the people I ran across were Monks and warriors.

I was being chased by a 6-pack of very mean and ravenous looking Kodiak bears. This is what is called a "train". My half-mauled delicate body was beginning to slow as I neared the Inn and guard post. Death at this point was inevitable. I turned to face my fate, and fell to the swift paw of a hungry bear.

I sat in the alcove, back at the west gate memorizing my spells and hummed a little tune in response to the low growl in my stomach. After I finished memorizing, I got up and began to run back to the site of my untimely demise to gather my things. Just before leaving West Freeport I stopped and noticed that someone had sent me a message. Someone out in the commonlands asked if I was looking for my corpse. I assured them, that it was something I had (unfortunately) NOT forgotten and I'd be back to the scene in just a moment.

Returning to my corpse, I sat for a while after casting strength spells on everyone, and meditated. I got that feeling again: someone was watching. I opened my eyes and smiled. The message that I'd received earlier to ask if I needed assistance was matched with a face; it was a husky looking Monk.

"Hello again." I said with a warm tone.

"Hello my dear, I see you've found your things. Anything else I can do for you?" He stood above me offering his hand. As he pulled me to my feet I felt his strong bristly hands wrapping around my delicate digits, almost crushing them. I thanked him and assured him that I was fine.

He stood close, perhaps too close. His leather armor was fashioned to fight tightly around his rippling chest. I could see his muscles straining against it. I smiled and looked into his dark brown eyes. He wrapped a hand around my waist and pulled me against him.

"You know, I could protect you out here... this is a scary place for such a pretty lady."

"Oh, thank you. It's difficult being an enchantress and adventuring out alone. I could really use your help, not to mention I could help you with my spells." And with that, we set of into the woods and raided a few Orc camps, showing them just who was boss.

Again, I could have taken offense, but I chose not to. I was simply using my charm to win a bodyguard, is that so wrong?

Another instance of interaction with others that really makes me smile, (and that drives my point home) is an evening I was out alone, trying my hardest to gain my latest level on my way to meet up with my sister Iolanthe.

I was kneeling over a spiderling corpse pulling some silk from its abdomen, when I overheard someone talking in a rather abrasive tone, and foreign words I had never heard anyone use before.

"Man that was kewl, did you know that n e 1 can make that patchwork shit now?" said one.

"DOOD, this rawkz! I think I'm gonna get some more skinz and make sum doller$!" The other replied.

I'd heard rumors of a strange hybrid of a few races called a "K3wL D0odZ" but until this day I hadn't ever really seen one in Norrath, let alone hear it speak in it's native tongue! I had to get a closer look for myself. As I crept up behind the two gentleman. They looked normal, nothing strange appearance wise, so I decided to announce myself.

"Hail gentleman. How is the hunting for you going this rather foggy and misty eve? I see you have just slain a wolf! I hear the pelts catch a pretty fair price in town."

There was a long pause. They looked at each other and looked at me. Perhaps the "K3wL D0odZ" didn't understand me. Was the tongue I was speaking too advanced for their feeble, underdeveloped brains? I attempted to solicit a response again.

"I say, do either of you strong handsome looking fellows know the direction I could head to find the goblin camps? I am meeting my sister there and have become a bit lost!"

At last one spoke. "Fair maiden, pleaze cum dis way, I will show u 2 the camps." I was astonished! They understood me! However the reaction was different than I had anticipated. I was hoping they might tell me a bit more about "K3wL D0odZ" but it appears my presence had made them nervous. Perhaps they can only be true "K3wL D0odZ" around each other.

All in all, my first few experiences with the inhabitants of Norrath have been positive. As I grow more familiar with Norrath I will make more friends and more enemies. So far, I've fallen madly in lust with an ever-so-handsome half elf bard that I met in Freeport, been propositioned by a monk, pinched by halflings, kissed by an ogre and hugged by many. They are all very nice and would oblige me regardless of my gender, but life is just grand when you are female. Stay tuned until next week when I tell you about the interesting time I had on the boat leaving Freeport and my ongoing affair with the handsome and daring Half-elf bard!
