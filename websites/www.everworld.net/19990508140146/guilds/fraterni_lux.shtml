Fraterni Lux

by Burn

 
 
Guild Name: Fraterni Lux
Interviewer Name: Thanquol
Position: Council Member
Members: 20, 6 initiates
Address: http://www.mindspring.com/~flwolf/fratlux/


What brings together the members of the Fraterni Lux?

I think the main reason would probably be the great friendships we share...as well as wanting to roleplay with people we know very well

The Fraterni is a very old and sizable organization. What is the guild's allure? What draws people to it?

I believe it is the way the guild as a whole carries itself. We try very hard to practice what we preach. Also the fact that the guild was able to stay together even through the toughest of times. We aren't going away anytime soon.

Is the Fraterni simply a group of like-minded friends, or does it serve a greater purpose?

OI believe it does serve other purposes. We hope it will be a model for others to follow as far as role-playing goes, and as far as in game, we hope to champion the cause of justice.

There is a fine line between justice and good. Do you think that your members will be able to differentiate between the two and not simply side with the good guys?

I believe we can indeed. We have no racial or class prejudices, showing that we are very open minded. Sometimes one must do things that are not necessarily good, but must be done in order to promote justice.

Do you have any in-game ambitions so far?

As far as ambitions go I would have to say having fun with friends, meeting new ones, while role-playing would be the greatest thing we could do. I can't lie though. It would indeed be nice to become a well-known guild because of what we may do in the game, but I don't feel that is what's really important.

What relations do you currently have with other guilds, if any?

Currently we are avoiding any alliances with any guild. In our honest opinion it would be impossible to truly know whom we would be allying with. We feel that actions speak louder than words and therefore have elected to waiting until the game's release before making any formal alliances.

Are there any that you look upon with a favorable eye, or with susspicion? Based on what?

There are no guilds I can name from the top of my head. In my own opinion though, you can honestly learn a lot from what a guild has on its web page. By this I do not mean how good it looks, because that really doesn't matter at all, but what they have to say. I myself love to see evil RP guilds. I believe FL will definitely shy away from guilds who have a "juvenile" outlook on things

How exactly is the guild run? Are the members obligated to follow the Council's decisions, or merely expected to

Well actually the guild elects a council. In fact, elections will be held again prior to the game's release. The council is basically formed to help organize and keep the guild informed and running, but all major decisions that the council may bring up are voted upon by all members. We feel that no one member, including council members, should be above another. We are a very democratic organization and feel everyone's opinions are valuable

You welcome all races and classes, yet I saw very few "evil" races/classes. Why is this do you think?

In all honesty I do not know. Currently I believe we only have 1 dark elf and another who is a necromancer. I assume it may have to do with the fact that most people who intend to play the so-called "evil" races/classes wish to play that way. Our beliefs allow any race or class as long as you follow the path of virtue and honor... maybe that scares "evil" guys away.

What alignment (in the traditional AD&D sense) best describes the Fraterni?

There are members who feel they are lawful, others chaotic, and some even neutral good, but I feel that Chaotic good best represents FL. We wish to be able to break new ground. Also sometimes you have to do what you have to do in order to get the job done. By this I picture Robin Hood. Yeah he stole money and goods but for a good cause.

Is the guild closely tied together, or are members allowed to more or less go their own path?

We feel that ones loyalty should be to FL alone. However adventuring with other friends and guilds is acceptable as long as they too follow the same general principles that we are founded on. We feel this should not be a problem considering they wanted to join in the first place. As far as having other characters that are not members of FL, one may do as he wishes.

Would you give a final few words to our readers out there summarizing the Fraterni?

We are more than a guild, I believe we are a family. We really do care about our members and their opinions, for we are only as strong as our weakest link. I know that sounds familiar but I really feel that is true. We don't seek power or fame. Instead we wish to be a role model for all. If we can obtain that objective then it would be the greatest reward of all. "Let honor not fail"

Burn's thoughts on the Fraterni

While it's always the interviewer's job to say something nice about the interviewee afterwards, this time it's sincere. The Fraterni seems to truly be set on being a good example of role-playing, and we need all of those that we can get. Their democratic spin on things is pretty unique, as most guilds go for either a despotic or a republican-type government with a council of officials. There are some base similarities that I noticed between the Fraterni and the Disciples (last Spotlight) such as the belief in total equality between people, and the willingness to do nasty things to achieve the greater good. If the two guilds could somehow overcome their few (but substantial) differences, they would make a powerful polarity force for RP-ing in Norrath. It is my expectation that, due to their sense of honor and devotion to justice, being a member of the Fraterni will become a mark of prestige in the future.
