The Final Night -
As Reported by Marcus McLeode

Well, it's done. After three years in development, they've done it!

The final hours of P4 were a marvel to behold. I spent the last hours on Tarew Marr in an attempt to help the denizens of Faydwer fend off the Tier'Dal invasion. Unfortunately, too many dark elves on the boat caused it to "sink". After several attempts, only a handful of dark elves made to Faydwer only to be turned back or killed within minutes. While they reportedly had an army 70 or 80 strong, the loyal denizens of Faydwer had two armies with 200-300 total warriors, clerics, and magic-users.

When it was obvious that there would be no great battle, everyone went their separate ways. Many elves went to Kelethin to party it up for a while. This is where I headed. We had GM's along with us to try and kill the local Priest of Discord, to no avail. Over the next hour and a half, we took to battling amongst ourselves in an all-out brawl. Several deaths occured, as did many acts of bravery and generosity.

And in the end? Just a simple message: "The server is not responding."

Thus ended an era. Looking back, we who have played and tested this game wish it all the luck in the world. But for this game to have a future, we the players must do our part in making this a great game starting Tuesday. Remember that we were all newbies at one time. And when someone who has never seen Norrath "in the flesh" asks for a little help, take the time to give them a push in the right direction. EverQuest will be a better game if we all do our share.

Attack On Crushbone -
As Reported by Pandorae

There we were in Orc Territory, Svanlaug nervously scouting out the orcs on the top of the hill, trying to determine if we had the brawn and the means to dispatch them. Celebairn and I rested and studied our spell books in preparation for upcoming battles. Bellows were heard all through Crushbone: "Come to the castle! We are taking over the castle." And while I sat still, scratching my head and wondering whether I should join the festivities, new screams and shouts began in a blur: "My God! He is Huge!" "We need help!"

At that point I stood and ran for the side of the hill, peering over the path to the Orish fortress. Soon I noticed the scampering wood elves, dwarves, high elves, gnomes, barbarians, and humans, heading to the entrance of Clan Crushbone. And Lo! There stood Crushbone and his minions!

Elves fought to their deaths, and the ground cluttered with corpses. Seeing such courage in my fellow elves, my party and I ran to aid. Crushbone, equipped with an axe that stood larger than myself, struck four to five blows to decimate a single elf, and I stood, watching the thirty-foot-tall beast in amazement. While in speechless awe at his enormity, two legionnaires piled atop me, and upon realizing the pain of the blows, I dodged out of the cave and back to Faydark.

When I returned to Crushbone, the Lord of the Orcs ran back and forth smiting my fellow adventurers to the ground. I took hold of my wits, and noticing an Orc Slaver headed for me, I focused my powers on defeating the Slaver. But as I fought, a strange feeling washed over me.

Suddenly I realized that I had lost all control of myself, and I was bending to the command of the Orc Lord. The slaver continued to beat me, and I was helpless to defending myself. Crushbone directed me back and forth, running aimlessly through the battle zone, stopping to try to hit my fellows, and friends turned on friends against their will. This little enchanter was charmed by the Lord of the Orcs! When she became useless to him, he turned on her and pounded her to the ground until she lay unbreathing.

When I had recovered and remembered all of my spells, and had rescued my worldly items from Clan Crushbone, I heard the cries of my fellows that the Orc Lord had left his domain and had been loosed into Faydark. Crushbone defied the elves and shouted his ownership of the forest. Rallos called the citizens to Arms. Word passed that the Lord had gone to Felwithe, then to Kelethin. And soon, he was back at the Crushbone entrance, with an army of elves following him.

Every one of us healed, casted, hit crushbone. Bodies dropped to the ground quickly and painfully. At least a hundred of us stood in the narrow entrance, wrecking our destructive forces on the monster.

Finally, the Lord of the Orcs began walking to the mouth of the cave, and turned around to deal some final blows. Suddenly, he threw up his arms in the air, and the earth rumbled as the huge beast fell to the ground. From the astonished silence arose the cheers of the allied armies. The Lord of the Orcs had fallen! Crushbone was no more!


Attack On Crushbone -
As Reported by Bretten

This is the story of the Great Battle of Crushbone. It was a time of darkness, where the Clan Crushbone Orcs roomed the lands as freely killing unwary travelers. The people were frightened of these beasts and so they never ever traveled to far into the woods in fear of crossing paths with one of them. One day, This day, The day of reckoning was at hand. The time for this fear to go away was now. Many a warrior has seen the outside of the Orc city Crushbone, but only a few have been in, and lived to tell the tale. This my friends is the story of when one army of brave warriors would not only go in Crushbone but they would...

As I was running threw the Forest of GreaterFaydark, I heard yells off in the distance. As I got closer I saw more and more people running toward this yell, this yell was coming from Crushbone. I thought to myself that this is it, the day when we take our freedom back from Crushbone. As I get inside the Cave of Crushbone I saw more and more people. This was an army, this army was huge, Moral was up, they were ready for battle and they were thirsty, thirsty for blood. I was informed that they had taken Castle Crushbone but the Orcs sent reinforcements and there was many a trapped soul inside the City. We all knew what we had to do, we had to storm the Gates of Crushbone and take victory. With clerics and paladins healing people, and enchanters and druids casting spells of strengthening we were ready. At the yell of one single soul everyone got up from their sit, and rushed into Crushbone. In side of Crushbone there is death and decay all around, the orcs had taken many lives and they were still taking them. We all rested up and what was going to happen next was one of the most momentous occasions ever.

People were reforming after dispersing of most of the Orcs in the area, and we all stood in one big mass of brute power. We had just received word that the battle was being fought at the castle. We got our forces ready and when the call was sent out, we all rushed to the castle in one big mass of death. Off in the distance we see the battle being fought, and for the moment it felt like we could do anything. With screams of death and battle crys we hit the wall of orcs. The orcs were being chopped down left in right, but with casualties of only a few the true battle awaited us. For inside Castle Crushbone awaited glory and victory. Most of the army waited outside the castle healing and preparing for the true battle. We heard crys of death, crys from our own men, we knew there was something the matter in the castle. When all of a sudden King Crushbone him self appears, with his royal guard, and elite. Everyone one got up from their short rest and rushed to Crushbone. Crushbone was a very strong enemy, he wouldn't lose his empire to just a small army. People were dyeing quickly, including me. Just before I die from a hit to the head I heard yells of retreat. The army failed to kill Crushbone.

I made my way back to Crushbone as quickly as I could. Waiting outside Crushbone was a very big group of people, 40-60 people maybe. We waited outside Crushbone for only a few minutes before the word was yelled to go in. This was the second wave to take over the castle. I made my way to my body safely.

Rushing back to the exit of Crushbone where 30-40 people waited there. They were dispersing of Orcs. When one warrior yells "CRUSHBONE IS IN THE FOREST!!!". This was overwhelming; everyone rushed back to the forest. Awaiting us was King Crushbone himself, but this Crushbone was no King, he was a God. King Crushbone had grown to over fifty feet tall. This did not intimidate anyone, with reinforcement coming from the east in Freeport we knew we could take him. Everyone in the forest came to slay the giant. Slay we did, Crushbone had fallen. We had won!!! But it wasn't over yet. The worst was to come.

We were informed that the war was now inside of Crushbone once again. But this war wasn't with the orcs; this battle was with Ambassador DVinn. Ambassador DVinn is a mean DarkElf with the strength of 100 men. The army would soon find out that this strength would be shown in full force. With the battle ragging on with DVinn, brave souls were being lost quickly. DVinn was stronger than Crushbone was, by the time he was to half-life we had losted 20 or so men and women including me.

I rushed back to Crushbone once again to find yet another group of people waiting inside the cave. I waited there for 3 or so minutes when once again the call of battle was yelled. We had all rushed in again, and there was more death and decay than one could imagine. I quickly retrieved my items and was back in the fight. It would take three waves of men and women to finally bring down DVinn. With DVinn and Crushbone killed the castle was for the taking.

It would take three waves of men and women to finally bring down DVinn. With DVinn and Crushbone killed the castle was for the taking.

The army had won, and this time we had one for good. The Forest was once again ours again, for the time being. With the hour dwindling down there were crys, not crys of death, but crys of cheer. The End of the World was at hand and we all wanted to get drunk. So drunk that we couldn't stay atop Kelethin. So at when the clock struck 12am in the year 3172 the end would come. Several Interesting Experiences
As Reported by Drakanius

Last night I did see many things. I traveled as far as the elf city of Felwithe, and all the way to Qeynos, and Halas. And all of these cities had many interesting sights. As I joined the Days of Darkeneing in Felwithe, I observed as the masses of Elven kind gathered in front of the gates to Felwithe, forming a plan to stop the Dark Elf invasion. Which unfortunately never happened, but I have a feeling it will resume as Norrath is once again inhabiated.
And hearing news that huge 50 foot Gnolls were attacking Everfrost, I switched to my Barbarian Shaman to see it for myself. And they were not lying. As I arrived at the battle scene, I found that the huge Gnoll had been slain, but I managed to get a screenshot of the huge corpse lying in the snow.
I then traveled to Qeynos, where rumors of a massive Gnoll invasion were spreading. I then found a huge battle raging on, against a very strong Gnoll. I missed out on this battle as well, arriving a second too late.
And as 12:00 PM was sneaking up, I traveled back to my hometown of Halas, to celebrate with my fellow Barbarians. As I arrived, I found that over half of them were far past drunk. I glanced at the clock, noticing it was 5 minutes past 12:00. So I decided to get in on the fun. I asked for a little liquor, and instantly various people were offering me Brandy, beer, and other different types of liquor. I drank up , and soon my vision was blurred, and my speech was slurred. Then came a broadcast that said the servers were coming down shortly, so I decided to take a final swim. I jumped in the lake, and slowly sank to the bottom, as the Phase 4 beta expired.
I would just like to say what a great job the Everquest team did in making the final night a huge success. I had a blast, and I am sure most everyone did. I just can't wait to see what goes on in final! See you all in Norrath!
