Night Attack
Lazy waves beat upon the hulls of the three ships making their way over the seas. Night had fallen and all was quiet besides for the rhythmic drumming of rain on the decks. For the fourth day in a row nor the rain nor the clouds relented their slow assault on the travelers. All eyes were stressed to the limit as the ships sailed onward. From the first ship a voice was carried on the winds, the watchman calling for the next shift.

Another hour passed as the stars moved in their predestined patterns. A new hour was just beginning when a scream for a halt rang through the leading ship. Within seconds all three ships were bristling with lanterns, all were on deck awaiting an explanation. Just as the lead ship was coming about a hellish wind rose from the sea itself, and beared down on the travelers. The leading ship was caught mid-turn and shoved sideways nearly tipping over. All crews sped to their jobs, and the sails were gathered as fast as mortal hands could manage. But the lead ship was still struggling, the winds ripped at the sails preventing them from being closed, half the crew was shortly thrown overboard.

"The ship is lost we must save the crew," was shouted from the second ship. Just then a wave hit all three ships carrying them with it. As the first wave died down another picked up the ships and once again bore them onward.

The captain of the second ship was ordering his crew to signal a turnabout when a young erudite rushed up to him. His hood was thrown back revealing a young face, the eyes were somewhat large but seep with knowledge. He waved his staff at the captain to get his attention. "Do not fight the storm! It has been summoned for a reason and if we fight it we only loose. The best we could afford to do is let it carry us where it deems."

"This ship is mine, and we are going to turn out of the storm! Unless you are wanting to...." The big man was interrupted by another voice.

"Why don't you be quiet and heed the wizard's words!" The voice belonged to halfling standing next to the erudite. His hair was tossed in all direction by the winds, but his eyes were burning into the captain's. A small blade glinted cooly in the light of the erudite's staff.

Behind the halfling appeared a slender elf in a blue robe, he quickly motioned for the captain to comply. Growling, the captain gave out the new orders which were quickly spread through out the three ships.

Turning, the halfling bowed his head and headed back down to his cabin struggling against the winds. After him followed the erudite holding his staff high for light. His body was bent into the wind as he hurried to get bellow deck.

Survivors

For the length of the night the ships had been carried by wave after wave into the deep darkness of the night. Finally the winds and waves vanished as suddenly as the had come. A strange calm settled over the sea and the ships. Not a soul could be seen moving on deck or bellow. The sun pushed its rays through the heavy layers of fog, illuminating the world. Still the silence remained unbroken.

A loud creaking rushed over the waters demanding to be noticed. Behind it came voices, or rather cries for help. As if waking from a heavy sleep the captains struggled above deck with their telescopes. What they saw chilled them down to their very beings. Ship upon ship was floating over the calm sea Each covered in green seaweed, and battered in every way imaginable. In the midsts of all this stood the lead ship. Its sides were already being over grown with the green sickness, as it gradually aged before their eyes. On its deck a few figures could be made out waving and yelling to the other two ships.

Minutes passed as the two surviving ships put together a force of small boats with crews. The boats made their way between the dead ships towards their dying comrade. Those that survived quickly boarded the boats with what supplies that could be saved. Slowly the boats headed back to the two ships.

Every crewman and passenger alike was fingering their weapons and looking around anxiously. The dead ships seemed to have moved closer in trying to block their escape. The oarsmen threw themselves into their work, moving the boats ever faster. Within an hour the boats were hauled back up to the ships and the survivors tended to. Very few words were spoken, and none at all were spoken about the disaster.

Before nightfall the leaders met to discuss their road from here on. One of the elves from Norrath explained that they were now in a legendary cemetery that moved with the tides. It never remained in one place for more than a day, and neither should they. It was quickly decided to leave at once. The course was re mapped, and to the dismay of all, they had been blown six days off-course.

A new night found the remaining ships once again heading for the lands of Norrath. All those on board got no sleep that night, anxiously awaiting another peril. None but the leaders could begin to guess at what summoned the storm upon them, and why. But they would relent their knowledge to none but themselves.

The halfling and erudite were outlined by the light of the stars standing in the stern of the ship.

"It seems as though your advice was right after all."

The erudite nodded without looking down at the halfling.

The halfling glanced up at the young wizard, "Let us hope that your advice keeps that quality."

Smiling the Erudite turned, and slowly made his way to his cabin. The halfling was left looking at the dark sea, and the shallow waves beating against the hull. Later, muttering a song under his breath he quietly returned to his cabin.
