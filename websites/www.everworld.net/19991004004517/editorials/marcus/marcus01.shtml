Where to begin...

by Marcus McLeode

 
 
Where to begin, with the world of Norrath, that is a big question. I guess I should start with my opinion of Everquest. EQ is a technological marvel. The graphics are utterly amazing. Screenshots simply cannot do it justice. Watching the sunset, with the clouds rolling along is almost as good as the real thing. And the storms! Although they come on quickly, storms in Norrath are a sight to see. Real raindrops fall from everywhere, with lightning and thunder crashing all around. The game engine itself is beyond amazement. I have played RP games such as Might and Magic 6, and let me tell you, Everquest puts it to shame. Better graphics, better history (which is still currently being worked on), and better sound by far. The storms I mentioned, I have jumped out of my chair when a storm starts with a large thunderclap and a bolt of lightning.

Unfortunately, Everquest currently holds very little for the role-player. From misuse of the OOC-type commands to discussing Clinton and Iraq while in-game, there is very little to be seen of role-playing. In the past week or so that I have been in the world of Everquest, the only game-run quest I have found was a storeowner that needed a favor. A quick slash and kill of a local pest. It took me about 3 minutes and I didn’t even get a reward afterwards. As a result, many players have taken it upon themselves to create their own mini-quests.

That being said, let’s all agree that this is the best game I have ever played. I have gone from twelve hours of gameplay a week, to almost twelve hours a day. And I work another 8. Hmmm, that only leaves me with four hours of sleep per day. ZZzzzZZzzZZ. Huh? Oh, sorry. This game is taking a toll on me. Thank the gods of Norrath that they shut down the world every now and then. Every day the server goes down for a short time. Whether it is to install a patch, or for general internet problems, or the occasional server crash, almost always the wait to log back on is short enough for me to take a bathroom/kitchen break.

Currently, I am working on a half-elf warrior. This is one of the quicker combinations to raise levels. With the warrior’s unlimited weapon choices, and his larger-than-average hit point allowance, I figured I could really manage to kill quite a few creatures. Boy was I wrong. The warrior may have more hit points but after you have 150 points, it seems to take forever to heal yourself back up. Even with skills to make the process faster.

Please note that I have not asked or received any help from a GM, or any other Sony employee while in the game. So, what I have is a very realistic account of what it is to start and play a character in Everquest. That said, over the past seven days, I have taken my half-elf Dulen from level one to level seven. Now, that’s not a level per day mind you. I had level three achieved in my first night. It took me two days to fill my requirements for level seven. I’m now looking at about three days for level 8, and so on.

On the subject of playing the game itself, let me tell you Dulen’s story. For the first few nights, I spent most of my time near the North gates of Qeynos. Here, there are snakes, rats, the occasional gnoll pup, and the even rarer adult gnoll. I quickly outgrew this area and headed further north to the Qeynos Hills. Here, the gnoll pups are still around, but now their older gnoll scout cousins are also abound. And the gnolls are a pack creature, if one sees another being attacked, he will join in to defeat the attacker.

After a night or two of this area, with it’s gnoll scouts and skeletons, I happened upon some really big kills. I won’t give out any names or detailed descriptions, don’t worry. Suffice it to say that each zone has its regular strength monsters then their super strength monsters. Each of these super monsters is designed to require teamwork between the local inhabitants. I personally have died several times because of a design bug with these creatures. Don’t worry, the creatures work the way they’re supposed to. It’s just a gripe of the other players that they don’t stand a chance against these creatures when attacked.

After I seemed to be able to handle Qeynos Hills, I ventured for the first time into BlackBurrow. The first time I went in, I survived for almost half an hour, racing around the twisting and turning tunnels. Never once did a gnoll even look at me funny. I figured that my low level caused them to ignore me. That is, until one that wasn’t busy found me and promptly chased me for five minutes through every tunnel in the burrow. I finally lost him when a barbarian began to whack him over the head with his sword. Alas, I got away from that one just in time to find 6 more in a single room. Needless to say, I died just outside of the room Upon my resurrection, I found myself back at the Northern gates of Qeynos. This is where I got lucky. It was only a five or ten minute run back to BlackBurrow and I was fortunate enough to not run into any aggressive creatures. I retraced my steps the best I could and got lucky. I found my corpse and looted it. This time, I got away from those lower areas without any more attacks. I managed to find a warrior who was on his way out and followed him to the exit (Thanks again Stranger!).

After my latest fiasco, I decided to join a group of adventurers both for the monetary incentive as well as the “Safety in Numbers” idea. We met at the entrance of BlackBurrow and figured everyone’s role in the oncoming battles. However, once inside, our ill-prepared and unorganized party quickly got split up. It wasn’t long after that that we started dying. Just a note here, when the group leader dies, the entire group is disbanded. A minor inconvenience to say the least. Once again I found myself at the gates to Qeynos. And once again I found myself racing through BlackBurrow trying to find my corpse. I got lucky this time though and I found it almost immediately. Again I looted my body and headed for the exit. By now, I had been through a portion of the dungeon enough times to remember my way out.

Later that night, I found a little time to go exploring further out into the world. I took a road that I had almost missed in the past 5 days. It led between two high mountain ranges and into the Karana Plains. I have never seen a more desolate land even in Real Life. The only break in the empty landscape of hills and plowed fields were the occasional homes I came across. After about fifteen minutes, I came across my first scarecrow. I studied him for a second and found him to be out of my league. Walking on, I came across a gnoll and thought, “My first kill in Karana!” Unfortunately, he too was out of my league. As I watched the gnoll, another humanoid came along and they started fighting. I watched from a short distance away thinking I could help the man if he needed it. Then I realized that it was in fact a battle between two NPC characters! Never before had I witnessed such a thing! The human killed the gnoll in a matter of seconds and it was then that I knew I was not ready for Karana and made a beeline back for Qeynos ASAP.

After a good nights sleep in Real Life, I headed back to BlackBurrow. After leading a few trains back to the surface, not that I wanted to, mind you, I managed to get up another level. Dulen is now at level seven. Tonight, I am on my way to meet another in the Commonlands. Wish me luck. I’ll keep you informed.
