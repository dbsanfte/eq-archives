All You Wanted To Know, But Were Afraid To Ask! - Phlashbak
    Guide Abashi tells all in this stunning story, of betrayal, deceit, and "Thumping".

    1. The PK Switch

    It's not going away. The only people who a PvP person will be able to fight will be other people who have chosen to be PvP or have chosen to duel. Gone are the days of being able to kill people who don't want to engage in it. There will be no "PK Days" where everyone is PvP for a few hours, or a day, or a week every 2 months. There will be no special advantages for those who choose to play PvP. There will be no limitations on levels, advancement, or weapons for those who chose to abstain from it. No one will *ever* be forced to engage in PvP against thier will. No one will ever be involuntarily switched PvP as a punishment for an attitude problem.

    If you wish to engage in PvP with other like-minded individuals, EverQuest will provide you a forum in which you can do that. However if the only way you will enjoy PvP is to kill people who want nothing to do with it, EverQuest will never provide that.

    2. PK vs. Anti-PK.

    Neither of these exist in EverQuest based on the modern definition. You are all PvP. PvP people will account for all races and classes in the game.

    3. Killing out-of-character

    Those PvP persons who chose to kill outside the racial and deity hatreds that exist in the land will find themselves without a home. Sorry, but that is how it is. Kill in-character and go home to find yourself a hero with your faction and associates. Kill out-of-character and go home to find a guard wishing to bury his longsword in your skull.

    4. One Shot Kills

    Gone are the one-shot-kills or the 20 second battles some might be used to. If you want to kill someone it's going to take time, and chances are that they will have to be just as willing to fight you as you are them when you meet. The level-limit (currently 4 levels) on PvP combat pretty much insures that no one will ever take anyone else out in one shot. If you are solo and the target decides soon enough in the fight that they want to get away rather than fight, count on them to be able to do so.

    5. Grouping PvPers

    I pity anyone who tries to solo a +PvP person be they magic, melee, or otherwise. As a solo PvP you will be USDA Prime for any group of PvPers that come your way. Not to mention your back will be unguarded when that Orc has got you down to 20% health, or when you are sitting on your backside regening health or mana. Healing and medding PvPers will be prime targets for other PvPs looking for an easy kill.

    6. Best Class for PvP

    Forget about it. There isn't one. Per #5, You are dead meat if you try to solo as a PvPer. You need a group, and like any group, PvP groups need to be well balanced as well. One on one is just too unpredictable. As a Wizard I will nuke you. As a Necromancer I will suck the life out of you while poisoning you from within. As a magician I'll nuke you and make you a playtoy for my pet. As an enchanter I'll just charm you and "introduce" you to a local Guard or wandering giant. As a cleric I'll thump you while healing myself. As a druid I'll nuke you while thumping on you and healing myself, same with the Shaman. As an SK I'll harmtouch you and suck the life out of you. As a paladin I'll lay hands on myself and heal myself proper as well. As a ranger I'll thump you with dual blades while you *try* to hit me and while I heal myself, and if things go bad I'll just disappear into the wilderness and sneak up on you later. As a rogue I will surprise you when you see how easy it is for me to circle behind you. As a bard you will find me swinging at you faster than anyone else. As a warrior or monk, I'll show you what it *really* means to be thumped. Did I miss any? Any questions?

    But wait! For only $3.95 you will also recieve some info on the 4 level pking difference

    The 4-level thing was almost unanimously agreed upon by the "notorios PK's" currently in the beta. It was cited as one thing that needed to go in to make PvP more viable. Quite frankly, much outside that 4 level limit, there is really no amount of skill and/or luck that can turn the tables against the stats.

    And we will also throw in this little untensil that tells you about RolePlaying and PlayerKilling, free of charge!

    I have heard the roleplay defense to massive unrestricted PvP before, I might actually have invented it. The truth is that though, yes, the PvP switch does hamper roleplay of racial hatred in that you cannot necessarily kill your enemy, it does the greater service of allowing all different playstyles to exist in the same world. EverQuest is a roleplaying game by definition, but that does not mean we can force people to roleplay any more than we want to force people to engage in PvP.
    Now, if we take your same scenario, and if you *both* wish to fight for roleplaying reasons, I suggest you use /duel. This tool has been provided for those who wish to engage in roleplayed PvP without the stigma caused by unrestricted full-time PvP.

    Well, if you didn't know what thumping was all about, now you do. I put up a player killing FAQ that will contain any and all info we find on player killing.

Guilds! - Phlashbak
    Ok, here is what you need to know

    In order to form a guild the following process must be followed:

    1. The leader of the guild must email Eqguilds@sonyinteractive.com. The header of the email must contain the NAME of the guild. In this email the leader will include a short Charter that includes the guild's role in Norrath. In addition, the leader needs to list at least 9 other characters that have agreed to form this guild.

    2. The leader and 9 other players need to email Eqguilds@sonyinteractive.com with the NAME of the guild as the header. The body of this email must include the name of the character that will be joining the guild and the ACCOUNT on which that character exists.

    3. Once the 10 prospective members (including the leader) have completed the above steps, the charter and name of the guild will be reviewed by 989. If both are approved, the leader of the guild will be notified of approval via email and told to approach a GM for formation of the guild. If 989 has any reservations about either the name or the charter, the guild leader will receive an email listing these concerns and be offered an opportunity to modify the charter or name as required.

    This is very important so you might want to spell check twice before you send. I am pretty sure there is limited guild space so hurry up!

'Dem RolePlayer Get All The Luck - Phlashbak
    A roleplaying organization might be able to convince 989 to host a complete roleplaying server.

    It seems EverQuest roleplayers may get their own server afterall. Norrath Roleplay, an on-line community of roleplayers, is preparing to make claim to one server in the name of roleplayers everywhere.

    While 989 Studios have made no official endorsement, the grass- roots movement continues to swell. With the recent premiere of their new website at www.norrathrp.com the group has seen their membership grow to well over 200 roleplayers, including many prominent guilds. "And that number climbs every day," according to Norrath Roleplay staffer, Sauleth.

    "At this rate, we'll have 400 people by Phase Four of the beta test," claims Jerris, another staffer. "And by the mass release, there should be well over 1,000 roleplayers involved." "We just want to make Norrath as immersive an environment as possible," says Kha’Dorak, another of the staffers. "Our definition of roleplaying is pretty liberal. We just want Norrath to smell like a High Fantasy world. We want to have fun."

    In addition to flooding one server with roleplayers, the group is taking several steps to foster a sense of community and immersiveness in the world of Norrath. Their efforts include player-run quests and several in-character taverns.

    I think that this is a good idea, and I think that it would be cool for the roleplayers to have there own place. I would REALLY be interested in having a PK sever as well. However, since player killers are stereotyped by everyone as immature, I doubt that 989 would give us the same treatment.
