GUILDLEADER
One member of Comrades of Norrath shall be known as Guildleader. The Guildleader has the role of mentor and guide. The Guildleader facilitates meetings, oversees voting, mediates disputes among members, and organizes official Guild events. This position is not permanent, but shall have a term of three (3) months. This term will automatically renew unless another officer seeks election. In such a case, an election will be held among all the members for a new Guildleader, who shall be an officer in the guild in good standing.
OFFICERS
Some members of the guild shall be known as Officers. The officers have the role of inviting recruits into temporary membership, of mediating disputes among members, organizing officail guld events and of assisting the Guildleader with the administrative tasks that ensure the efficient functioning of the guild. There shall be one officer per six members in good standing of the guild. Members in good standing who wish to become officers must first secure a nomination from the majority of the existing officers. New officers shall then be elected by majority vote of the members in good standing of the guild. The officers shall be not anonymous while in-game except in exceptional circumstances, and will identify themselves upon the request of guild members.
MEMBERS IN GOOD STANDING
Members of the guild who have completed the initiate's probationary period shall be considered members in good standing of the Comrades of Norrath.
RECRUITS
Those players seeking entrance to the Comrades of Norrath shall thoroughly familiarize themselves with the guild charter and website, and secure the recommendation of one (1) member in good standing, which shall be logged by the recommending member upon the public message board of Comrades of Norrath. Upon receipt of the recommendation, an officer of the guild will invite the player into the status of Recruit. A probationary period of approximately one (1) month shall then ensue. The new Recruit shall then post an introductory message upon the private message board. The initiate period begins from the date of this posting, and is a period of trial for the player, a time to be spent getting to know the members of the guild, understanding the guild's practices and policies, and deciding if the Comrades of Norrath is something the player wishes to become a part of. Recruits are free to resign their status at any time. At the conclusion of the probationary period, a vote shall be taken, guildwide. A two-thirds majority vote from the members in good standing is required for full membership in the guild. Should the Recruit fail to be accepted due to abstaining votes, the Recruit is to be given one (1) additional probationary period of one (1) month. If at the conclusion of that time the Recruit is not accepted as a full member, the Recruit is to be dismissed. Players whose main character has not achieved level fifteen shall not be considered for the status of Recruit. However, no character will be turned away due to race, religion, or alignment.

Code of Conduct
At all times, the members of Comrades of Norrath, from Guildleader to Recruit, will conduct themselves in a manner that will bring honor upon the guild. When other players encounter members of Comrades of Norrath, they must recognize the Comrades of Norrath members as people with whom they wish to associate and adventure.
Communication across the public channels of Everquest will at all times be mature and contribute to the play experience of the population of the server as a whole. The use of vulgar language in open text (i.e.: /OOC, /SHOUT, /AUCTION, or regular /SAY where a third party or parties could hear it) will not be tolerated.
Harassment of another player will not be tolerated. If a dispute arises between a guild member and another player, and it cannot be resolved by either party, then the guild member should /IGNORE the offending character and, if necessary, report the reason for the dispute to a Guide. A list of players who are well known for causing trouble, and who are better left alone, will be maintained by the guild on its site.
In general, Comrades of Norrath members will follow Verant's customer service policies and do nothing to interfere with the enjoyment of the game by others. In particular, the practice of "kill stealing" will not be tolerated. That is to say, the intentional taking of a kill from another player who clearly was fighting it first. Common sense and respect for others in a group situation must be used at all times. Under no conditions is it acceptable to play using tactics that are considered "dirty" or "unfair." Role playing an evil character is no excuse for this behavior.
General Policies
Loot Generosity
Is at the heart of the Comrades of Norrath. Whenever members of the guild earn loot together, the loot shall go to the member most in need of it. If more than one of the earning members has the same need, or if none of the earning members has need of the loot, then the loot shall go to the winner of a random lotto.
Guild Meetings
Monthly meetings shall be held over the /GUILDSAY channel on a day and time most conducive to maximum attendance. All members are encouraged to attend.
Recruitment
It shall be the responsibility of all members to recruit new members. The primary method of recruitment shall be to play the game forthrightly and with a sense of fun, thereby modeling for other players an example of a guild worth joining, a guild that they know will enhance their enjoyment of Everquest.
Sanctions
Disputes
Should a dispute arise among members of the guild, the parties involved shall bring their concerns before an officer as soon as possible. The officers and the Guildleader will hold council together at the earliest possible convenience to mediate the dispute. After carefully hearing both sides of the issue, their decision shall be final.
Violations of the Charter
Should it be determined by a council of the officers and the Guildleader that a violation of the charter of the Comrades of Norrath guild has occurred, the following shall apply:
I. Should the offending party be a recruit, he or she is to resign such status immediately. All further consideration of the recruit for full membership shall be terminated.
II. Should the offending party be a Member in Good Standing, then the officers and the Guildleader will hold council to determine a sanction appropriate to the offense committed. These sanctions may include, but are not limited to, probation, suspension, and dismissal from the guild.
