 2001

December 31st
 
~Alendora

 It just wouldn't be right to celebrate the New Year without a fond reminiscing of 2001. The world changed a lot over the course of the year.

January

Tunare had been the server of cooperation. Talisman, Krieger, Parthanon, and other 55+ players joined forces to kill the Kunark dragons. The larger guilds on the server had worked together to create the first cleric epic, rogue epic, and simultaneous first paladin and enchanter epics serverwide. January, however, heralded a new era for Tunare: guild autonomy.

Talisman underwent some changes. We decided to become a raiding guild and recruited some new folks like Southern, Malloci, and Falkahlan. We went through some lengthy debates about how loot was going to be done, and decided on a points system.

The other heavy topic was faction. The guild tried to weigh all of the advantages and disadvantages of Coldain, giant, and Claws of Veeshan factions, and in the end chose giant faction. Things can't be simple for Talisman, we had to pick the faction that had three sub factions (Kromzek, Kromrif, and Tormax) to worry about.

In January many members were still working on epics, and suddenly people were coming out of the woodwork to help each other. The guild was energized.

We took this energy to Wakening Lands and gave Wuoshi a try. Nobody knew what to expect. What we discovered was that the Wakening Lands portal guardian was about as scary as a 50 pound 10 year old declawed cat. We all vowed each other to secrecy and owned him every spawn for many weeks.

Ashana made us proud in the monk Best of the Best, beating Southern to become Ashana the Disciple.

February

With the momentum gained from our first Velious uberish mob slaying, we went on to have a busy February. We started out with Velketor the Sorceror, and took out the first Gorenaire, Klandicar, Zlandicar, and 425 Dain. The guild also hit its first speedbump: how to distribute Sleepers Tomb keys. Of course melee all wanted keys, but casters didn't want to spend points to go to a zone that didn't drop loot they were interested in. Once this was resolved, Talisman pursued Sleepers Tomb keys feverishly.

February also saw the Talisman team win the second Test of Tactics under the excellent leadership of Blitzberg the Strategist. The Test server finals were immensely fun, with Talisman finishing up in the final four.

March

In March, Verant decided they hated clerics. The Donal's breastplate underwent more nerfage, and the PoG BP went from having right click Celestial Elixir to right click Mark of Karn. Not only that, but Verant decided that clerics really shouldn't live through high end encounters, and made a Complete Heal during a dragon fight into the equivalent of shouting racial slurs in the middle of Compton.

Fumin had pushed for everyone to finish their Veeshan's Peak key quests, and we finally decided that we had enough keyed members to give VP a try. We tried Hoshkar 7 times over the course of 8 hours, and on the eigth try, we did something a little different and nailed him. Call us stubborn. In spite of the endless complaints in /gui about how incredibly boring VP is if you're not a puller, we cleared VP and paid the server's first visit to Phara Dar's lair.

That month Verant decided to revamp Fear and Hate, putting the best loot in the game on Cazic Thule. This sparked huge flame wars between the two large guilds on the server and the 'regular' Fear and Hate attendees who used a reservation system for the planes.

April

In April we killed Derakor the Vindicator and the Statue of Rallos Zek, and enjoyed only minor faction hits. This wouldn't last long, however, as later +CoV whiners would lead Verant to make the Statue faction hit so large that one raid could take you from amiable to KOS.

This month we also enjoyed our first golem killings in Sleepers Tomb, with our rangers Kaustran and Wine receiving the first two primals on Tunare.

Wine would go on to win the ranger Best of the Best, and Hamrok the druid Best of the Best.

May

May was a very quiet month for Talisman. The guild got together in Vegas for a wild weekend of debauchery. Otherwise, it was a sedate month full of ToV armor farming.

June

In June, the guild exploded. There were a lot of mobs in Velious who had enjoyed far too much time unmolested. We ate up Yelinak, Sontalak, Telkorenar, Gozzrem, Tormax, Dozekar (right before he got nerfed to be Sontalak's minime), and had the first 10th Ring War. As we finished up the month, we looked around at what we had not yet conquered, and set our sights on the Temple of Veeshan north wing and the newly upgraded loot.

Verant had !'d Nanzata, Tukaarak, Hraashna, and Ventani the Warder after the broken Sleeper script had been discovered. We were overjoyed to have them back, although we went after the new Hraashna with a sense of trepidation. We didn't complain overmuch when we found the Warders easier rather than harder after their "upgrade".

During this time we saw quite a few casters leave the game, as it became painfully clear that Verant would do nothing about the huge inequities between melee and casters, and the startling lack of caster loot in the high end game.

And, somewhere in there, Gorum won the Shadowknight Best of the Best, and came back from Test as Gorum the Revenant.

July

ToV north was the last great unexplored frontier for us, and we attacked it. We found out fairly quickly that Ikatiar and Eashen were pushovers.

Our monks then got frisky and decided to flop up to see the Plane of Mischief. We spent the weekend there exploring, but were back to ToV north again in short order to take out Jorlleag, Nevederia, Cekenar, Sevalak, and Zlexak.

In July, Verant did a really sad thing. They took away the cloth caps from the Warder loot table. Everyone cried big fat tears when we killed Tukaarak and there were 6 drops on him and *sob* none of our treasured cloth caps.

Surprising nobody, Ushio won the cleric Best of the Best, and donned his full suit of Ragefire armor to celebrate. When he came back from Test, he gave up his Templar title in honor of his in game wife.

August

In August we finished up what we had started and cleared the Temple of Veeshan north. We found that ToVn was what VP should have been. And the upgraded loot was magnificent.

Our guildleader Gendal won the bard Best of the Best in August, but only because of the purple boots.

September

September was not a memorable month. There were more forays into the Temple of Veeshan north following our agreement with Vae to trade off ToVn and everything else each week. The competition between our two guilds had been fierce at times, and the new agreement ushered in a new era of harmony.

Qheu became Tunare's Illusionist in Verant's unusual enchanter Best of the Best.

October

In October, the world changed. Dark Age of Camelot beta had been slowly stealing Talisman EQ members, and the full release saw about 25% of Talisman EQ change over to Camelot.

October also brought the Melee Patch to Everquest. Verant had analyzed the inequities in melee classes and delivered the long awaited class balancing. Rangers, monks, SKs, and paladins rejoiced. Warriors and rogues grumbled. 2h weapons stopped getting destroyed. Approval of Verant was at an all time high.

We had always heard stories from friends on servers like Ayonae Ro about the horrible competition between large guilds and how it would get so nasty that guilds would leap frog past each other to get to ToV north dragons first. With only two guilds vying for Tunare's uber mobs, we felt lucky.

Well, with an ever-growing server population, that can only last so long. The uber mobs are now shared between 5 or 6 guilds, three of which would be hunting in ToV north. It was inevitable that the 55+ population would swell, and the likeliest answer for the worsening ratio of uber mobs to guilds capable of killing uber mobs seemed to be the upcoming Luclin expansion. The only problem with that was the early rumors which indicated there was little to no high end uber mobs in the expansion.

It seemed that Velious had been the playground for uber guilds, and Verant wasn't going to be catering to them with this expansion.

November

November was another month full of farming the same old same old. A lot of people were feeling the strain of the constant farming of mobs and the lack of new challenges in the game. Talisman killed Ventani and released Kerafyrm, but afterwards the dreariness of more mob farming seemed even more oppressive. It seemed like Luclin would never arrive.

December

The serverwide Test of Tactics came the weekend before Luclin. This time Talisman only made it to the top 8, but then again, the best thing about ToT is not the competition.

Then, it came. The Luclin expansion. For many, it was about 4 months too late. Everquest didn't receive just a few more new zones. The changes were massive, not only in the game but also in the bare minimum pc requirements. Unless you already had top notch hardware, you had to shell out some money for RAM and a new video card. You had to have the right operating system. And even if you had all those things, you still experienced tremendous lag.

The new models were interesting, and hard to get used to. Suddenly, everyone looked different. After looking exactly the same for a few years, it was fun to be able to change your look at will. That is, if your pc would let you even turn on the new models. And, like DAoC, the women now sit in this sort of awkward-that-they-think-looks-graceful sideways leaning position. It looks STUPID. It makes me want to turn off the new models, except that would mean I can't see my horse, and that is an unacceptable solution. I'm sure all the guys out there have similar beefs with Verant, because after all, things like how you sit are among the most important things about this new expansion.

This expansion didn't bring another 10 levels like Kunark did. Instead, it brought alternative exp points, with new abilities that made everyone drool. The complaints about the stunning lack of high end uber mobs in Luclin were forgotten in the feverish pursuit of AA exp. Many found the joy of AE grouping in the Grey, just to have it nerfed a week later by Verant, along with a nerf that made it harder to get AA exp. In the end, though, I'm not sure it is the new abilities that drive people to do 12 hour exp grinds, or if it's all about the new titles. Vanity is a funny thing.

Verant patched and patched, and it was obvious that they had not understood just how quickly and efficiently high end groups could kill. But in their efforts to make it more difficult for the 5% of the server population to rip through their AA exp, they've forgotten the 20% of the server that has sufficient levels but sub optimal gear. I think they are stepping over that fine line where AA exp becomes so much of a torture that people would rather quit than face it.

In traditional MMORPG fashion, Luclin was released with lots of bugs and before some zones had been itemized. After the multitude of patches ("we'll keep patching every night until we fix the problems!"), Luclin seems to have stabilized. Verant turned it's eye on the Temple of Veeshan.

Inexplicably, the PoM entrance castle shrank to a barely visible rooftop in the revamped Temple of Veeshan. I don't know what this had to do with the proposed 'safe spot' clearing, but then I think logically and you can't apply logic to Everquest.

I'm actually rather glad to see the ToV north safe spot go away. Every guild approaches ToV clearing a different way, whether they start in the safe spot and Vulak pull all the dragons there, do a dungeon crawl, or some combination thereof. I'm sure that someone has already found a way to avoid the dungeon crawl, coth the entire raid in, and stare at the same walls for a few hours of dragon slaying. Enjoying a leisurely dungeon crawl is a luxury these days, one that guilds can hardly afford with the competition for spawns. I say this because I have never seen competition as a lessening of what uber phat lewt we get. To me, competition just means that everything fun to do is already done.

Luclin also brought the welcomest present of all for the die hard Talisman EQ'ers - the return of many of those who had departed for Camelot. The small group of Talisman who still play Camelot somehow managed to hold the second place in amassed Realm Points slot by having a ton of realm points (gained each time you kill a member of the enemy realm). I watch as a group of 8 Talisman take on a raiding party of 20 and kill them all with no casualties, and it's easy to see why they haven't come back.

Even the most hardened cynic can see that Verant has indeed tried to correct the problems in the game. It is in sharp contrast to Mythic's stunning lack of interest in fixing the problems that plague Camelot, especially in class and realm balancing. Verant still needs to address caster issues, but the pursuit of new AA abilities has casters preoccupied for the time being. However, there is one big problem looming on the horizon still. The burgeoning level 60 populations on all servers and the lack of high end raiding in Luclin is going to create a lot of animosity in Everquest. And a lot of boredom. The AA exp diversion can only last so long.
