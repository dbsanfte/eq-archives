Vanity

August 21st
 
~Alendora & Dain

Vanity is an evil thing. Anyone spend many planar hours working to get rid of their Enamelled Black BP? Then down the road you get the nifty Chestplate of Vindication from Derakor the Vindcator and it looks like...an EB BP. Anyone else know of someone in their guild who is determined to get a Warlord's BP rather than a Statue BP of Eradication only because they want to have a full set of matching armor? Why is it that only bards have come to terms with the idea that looking good is less important than being as well equipped as possible? Never mind that the rest of us cringe to look at them.

Monday night we killed Lendiniara the Keeper and our Shadowknight, Docvahn, was the lucky recipient of a BP of Superiority. So he goes and loots it, puts it on, and..."wooohooo Umbral!" Barber took off his Coldain gloves for his new Gloves of Stability and..."oh, same graphic. =(" Not as good as Cassai when she got all excited and took off her Thurgadin quest BP to try on her True Mithril BP. We all said, "try it on, we're waiting!" Cass replied, "er...it IS on." Let's not even get started on the Ancient Wurmhide Robe from ToV North (go Apprentice Robe!) or Kinadar's invisible Greaves of Distraction (pantsless ogres are very appealing). But no matter how boring or embarrassing, let's face it, it looks WAY better than PoG armor.

And then, there are those who could care less about appearances, who bravely wear even the most hideous and mismatched armor in the name of effectiveness. But I ask you, if you had to look at Ashana every day, wouldn't YOU start preaching the wonders of vanity?

~Alendora & Dain

We all know Verant in its infinite wisdom almost forgot to put caster loot in Velious. Well, Sleeper's Tomb was was widely championed by casters as the biggest 'fuck you' Verant gave them. And then Ushio got a primal brawlstick. And Hamrok. And Yikten. And then more primal went to Xcalabor, Edain, etc. Add to that our gnomes on Silent Star wattage and all of a sudden we've got an epidemic on our hands. They don't want to buff, or nuke, or heal...they want to melee. It's insidious.

It's a good thing that most of the guild was off camping, hiking, travelling, fishing, going to baseball games, the beach, musicals, and having barbeques, because the caster lust for melee is raging unabated. Ah for the good old days when casters knew their place and you didn't have to beg "Heal!" or "Nuke!"
