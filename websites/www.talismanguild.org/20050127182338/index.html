Unfinished Business 1/25/2005
Kaitlynn

Most people don't like to read so you are always supposed to open with a picture. Well I like to type, and there is nothing picture-worthy until later. So until I get to it, here is a picture of nekkid Portis flaunting her evil abs of steel.

We've been working on the Jelvan event in Anguish a lot lately, but until we beat it (VERY close!) let me catch you up on what's been happening. With all the time spent waiting on lock-out timers to expire, we have to find something for the guild to do, or bad things happen. The parents among you are well aware of the destructive power of unsupervised children. It's a suprisingly good analogy.

Talisman has a dark shadow across its past. It's something we don't speak of much, but it haunts us. That dark page from our past is Tacvi. It is the only zone since we formed that has truly beaten us. True, true, it was also the zone that drove away the top end of the guild hierarchy due to bugs and lack of itemization. It was the zone that was tuned (poorly) for level 70 and the gear and AAs that go with it - but went live in an expansion where the level stayed at 65. It was the zone that generated the need for the Guild Summit that created the relationship between SOE and some of the guilds to allow actual guild testing for upcoming raid content. Yes, with all those reasons for our failure to beat this zone, it still never sat well that we never finished it.

So we changed that.

On one night, as time-filler, we went in and not only tore up the mobs we had beaten when it was the high-end, but also were able to finally beat the mob that had halted our progress. Then we chose another night, and made Tacvi our raid target from start to finish. We had near-unprecedented numbers, and it was reflected in our progress. In one single night we were able to empty the zone right up until the final boss. By then, it was far too late, we were tired, and we had made our point.

Holiday Wrap-up 1/6/2005
Kaitlynn

In the days after Christmas, Talisman took on one of Blizzard's most famous villains and won. It was a fun battle that took us a few attempts to learn, but we defeated The Butcher! Errr I mean Warden Hanvar in Anguish.

Rafather picked up a Totem of the Chimera and both Tallie and Montecrizto picked up the thingies to make the new Retro-Look helmets. In a second win, Lobsang picked up a helmet thingy, Daydreamer got a Cloak of Regretful Transgressions, and Funeral got a Ring of the Beast.

Congrats to all!

A post-Warden shot as we had more time to look around.

Holiday Party

A little after the win in Anguish, we decided to retreat to a tropical island for a little holiday cheer. We had plans for singing, dancing, nude limbo, and general reverie. Massive amounts of alcohol were to be provided as a "training aid" in order to maximize alcohol tolerance for those few (lots) who never bothered to work on it.

A tropical island has its advantages for a holiday bash. Mainly no neighbors to annoy or sicken (nude limbo you recall!) But little did we realize that this island was the home to Trakanon! Or his bastard of an older brother - Ragefire, who as it turns out is not very social and complained loudly at the party.

So we soundly kicked his behind! Mind you that "soundly" involved 25 minutes of running around and around the island kiting the big decaying lizard by yours truly. All this while mobs and AE debuffs assaulted the raid, keeping 3/4ths of the raid out of commission either medding, rezzing, or facedown in the sand.

But after all was said and done, our own melee cleric Tallie gained the very first Cleric Epic 2.0 on our server! Congrats!

Emboldened by all the alcohol, we revisited our running wildly plan to gain another Epic 2.0 for Glori.

Recruiting

Talisman is always on the look-out for dedicated and talented people to keep our ranks full. We are a raiding guild and as such an applicant must have a high playtime availability, be adaptable to changing circumstances, and most of all be good in their class. We are in most need of Beastlords, Bards, Clerics, Druids, and Rogues, but exceptional candidates of any class are always welcome. For more information, please check out the Membership Information forum on this very website.

Harrier Grounded! 12/3/2004
Kaitlynn

Before and after other raids for the last few weeks, we have been making our way into Anguish with the full expectation of getting our heads handed to us by Keldovan the Harrier in the hopes of gleaning some information from the encounter. For a long time, very little we did seemed to make a difference. In the last couple of days however, tiny rays of hope began to shine through the gloomy clouds of despair. Wednesday night, things finally came together in one climatic moment when we knocked off Keldovan in Anguish!

We can really be proud of ourselves. Talisman has long been known as a guild that could accomplish tasks faster than a lot of guilds who have far more members. Our numbers have been relatively low of late due in a large part to the glut of really good games that are out, and some of our raids have suffered for it. The win over Keldovan really showed what skilled and dedicated players can accomplish once we set our minds to it - and stop whining over low turn-out. With the exception of the first day or two after gaining entry, not a single instance in Anguish has been filled to capacity. And yet still we were able to win this encounter (and others) and look ahead for more.

/rah rah OFF. Cene came away with the Infernal Staff of Fiery Fate, Rorek got the Choker of Imprisoned Visions, and Razoric got the Sorrowmourn Stone. Congrats to the loot winners and congrats to us as a whole for sticking it out night after night!

 

Earlier in the week we repeated last week's First Kill of Ture with a follow-up victory in a near-flawless win over the big doggie. The only two deaths we had were an applicant and ... Rafather.

 

Next, on Lifestyles of the Dark and Evil, we visit the sea-side castle of Anguish. This zone is the home to Keldovan the Harrier, a big dog named Ture, a host of really-nice-augment-dropping mobs, and probably more cool bosses that I haven't bothered to look up the names for.
