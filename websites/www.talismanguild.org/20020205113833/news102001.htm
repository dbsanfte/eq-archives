 Trick or Treat

October 28th 
~Alendora

Well we all logged on to the message that there were going to be GM events all weekend. We fondly remembered the Halloween GM events in 1999 when Hate opened where giant skeletons were running around newbie zones. The next year wasn't quite so exciting, Faydedar showed up in Lake of Ill Omen to slaughter noobs and we saw some GMs handing out little chocolate treats. So what, we wondered, are the GMs up to this year?

After putting the fear of cleric beatdown into Tulkas, I asked the guild if there was anything GMish going on.

Yes, that's right, the zone that randomly crashes three times an hour due to overcrowding was their choice for the GM event. Greater Faydark was down for over an hour, and when it came back up, there was nothing to be seen.

So we headed back over to ToVn and killed a few things. Abatoir was practicing his new disciplines.

Dwyer got a new weapon and couldn't resist:

And we moved on to Vulak. Add 'bad taste in movies' and 'fucked up priorities' to the list of things wrong with Daphni.

And then, there it is. The moment we've been waiting for. A server-wide announcement.

We were about as excited as schoolgirls with their first vibrator. We ran over to Fear and Bob impressed us all with his willingness to play along with the GM event.

We did AE Fear and cleared the zone pretty quickly. As always, everyone reminisced about the old days in Fear, when there were no clerics, no enchanters, everyone was wearing bronze, there was one Ykesha on the server, and it took months to finish a set of armor (read: uphill both ways). And, with excitement building, we took down the mudball. What would he have? Some kind of artifact? Something that no longer drops like the cryo robe?

Cazic dropped...cloak cloak pauldrons. So...would the GMs like to explain why Cazic issued his little challenge? Cause we didn't feel the true power of Fear at all. Really, the GMs don't know what everyone else in the world knows. When we come to the door and say "Trick or Treat!", it's not a multiple choice thing. /rude

 Raid leader disciplines

October 25th
 
~Alendora

Back in ToV again, the lustre has worn off a bit but it's still fun. Things like Jorlleag dropping three Helms of the Crawler kinda kills the thrill in the same way that finding out your date stuffs her bra does. The lack of real substance after all the work to get there is a bummer.

And Ragnrok sent me this truly amazing screenshot from the day we shot our little movie on how not to pull Mirinella:


You know, each raid leader has their own style, and doesn't vary much from it. It's boring always knowing what to expect. I think we should change styles like kung fu disciplines and keep everyone on their toes. You know, like in the movies, you start off with "Water flowing down a river" and then switch to "Song of hummingbird" to throw them off. Here are some sample disciplines:

The "rumbling volcano" discipline

This is the drill sergeant style. It is a good quick disc that requires high stamina to maintain. It is characterized by an impatience bordering on the psychotic, the belief that telling people they suck makes them into achievers, an affinity for CAPS and /shout, and the ability to flay a person verbally. Sample statement while in "rumbling volcano" discipline: "WTF. You people are pathetic! Do I need to come over to your house and hit your dispell hotkey for you?"

Once this discipline has been mastered, you will have an army jumping at your every command in the fear that you are going to blow.

The "warm bubbling brook" discipline

This is a combination of the cheerleader and den mother styles (I know that the combination of the two is an unsettling image for all you guys). This discipline requires an intense level of concentration and thus its duration is always limited. It is characterized by upbeat platitudes, unflinching optimism, and the belief that nobody ever does anything wrong...they just need to try a little harder. Sample statement while in "warm bubbling brook" discipline: "everyone did their best that try, this time we're going to OWN him!"

Mastery of this discipline will have you coaxing three groups into an assault on Vyemm with the unswerving faith that they will triumph.

The "sapling bending in the breeze" discipline

This is the laissez-faire style. It requires very little concentration and can last many hours. It is characterized by delegation and the ability to convey a sense that everything is under control with an absolute minimum of communication. The success of this discipline, however, is dependent on the performance of your front line men, making it very situational. Sample statement while in "sapling bending in the breeze" discipline: "Everyone get buffed, we have 6 dragons to kill. Ishalan is doing groups, Tulkas is going to pull, Edain is doing heal order, and Daphni's doing tank order and assigning assist. (5 hours later) Ok great job folks see you tomorrow!"

The proof that you have mastered this discipline will be that you never get tells during the raid but afterwards everyone says "great job leading!"

You can invent and name your own disciplines as needed. Like when the entire guild is whining about the pull taking forever and being bored. You can switch suddenly from the "warm bubbling brook" discipline to the "tiny insect in a storm" discipline, pull 20 mobs, and once everyone is good and alert or good and dead, switch to "rumbling volcano" discipline at the end for a good tongue lashing. The guild will be too busy cowering in fear and shock to ever be bored again.

Coding Issues

October 4th
 
~Alendora

 There are small coding issues and there are rather LARGE coding issues in Everquest. You remember how much people bitched when Verant introduced Gems? The sarcastic consensus was "This is a *great* use of developer time." However, some things do get fixed that really are more important.

When Talisman first killed the Progenitor (huge golem in Sleeper's Tomb) in his room, we found that we couldn't loot the warsword he was wielding because his center (loot point) was too high off the ground. After trying every conceivable method to loot (including troll totem poles) and receiving no help from our GM, we started an email campaign. Verant staff set up a test run with us to see the problem firsthand and the next patch included a change in the center point of mobs to allow even gigantic mobs to be looted. As a nice side effect, Gorum was also able to finally loot his corpse without levitate.

Wow, we all thought, Verant is concerned about our play environment! And responsive! There is hope.

Wednesday we gathered a bunch of Talisman and some friends from Forbidden Prophecy for a 10th Ring War. We had heard the rumors that the HUGE bugs in the 10th Ring War had been fixed. Yes, we're gullible.

Kukan did his hand in and nothing happened. So Blitzberg handed his ring in and the Seneschal shouted a bit, Narandi answered, and all the dorfs and giant spearmen spawned. And we waited for the first assault on Thurgadin.


And waited. And waited. And went afk to do the dishes while waiting. And waited some more. Nope, no first wave. Blitzberg petitioned and the request was escalated. The response from the GM? "I'm not familiar with that quest, you'll have to petition tomorrow when the senior GM is available."

So don't believe those rumors. As Qhue put it, "this is the third continuing year of beta testing of Everquest"

 Guild Identity: fubar

October 1st
 
~Alendora

 Every guild has its identity. They all fit into a neat little category:

- The mature, serious guild.

- The warm and fuzzy family-type guild.

- The uber conquer and destroy guild.

- The kick back, joking guild.

- The 'we're a guild of gamers' guild.

So, I wondered to myself, which is Talisman? The answer: schizophrenic.

How do I know this? I ask myself what would happen if some random level 58 wizard named Cylus Isurbich wanted to join Talisman.

The mature and serious members would vote 'no' because of "his stupid name."

The warm and fuzzy types would vote 'yes' because "he's such a nice guy!" and "he once helped me get an item when I was level 15!"

The uber conquer and destroy contingent would vote 'no' and say "put him up for vote again when he gets level 60 and DSL."

The kick back, joker types would vote 'yes' with the reason "this guy rocks bro!"

The 'we are more than EQ gamers' group would either abstain or not vote at all because they're off playing DaoC beta, AO, or taking a vacation until Luclin.

I don't know how anyone ever gets into Talisman. But I like my guild being schizophrenic. It makes guildchat all the more entertaining.
