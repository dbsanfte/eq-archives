Look! An Update! 5/15/2006
Kaitlynn

Sanguimanus the Redfang:

Ok, first thing's first. We finally beat that mangy bat Redfang! And as with so many of our first kills, we ended up with a really awful corpse pic to show off.

We began work learning the Redfang encounter just before Prophecy of Ro came out. Since that fight (and any fight involving those spinny bats) is annoying as hell, we dropped it from our target list as fast as we could so we could go frolic in the happy-fun greener pastures of PoR. Eventually guilt and the routine of killing PoR mobs got the better of us and we realized we needed to get back to the Demi Plane and continue where we left off. The amount of rust we had on encounters that we had so recently farmed was embarassing to say the least, but we got back into the swing of things quickly.

Eventually, after night upon night of intense (read: grueling) Redfang Appreciation Training, we finally beat his little furry ass down. Now it's on to Tier Two!


Prophecy of Ro :

With so many new mobs in the Prophecy of Ro expansion, there is no way to adequately list everything we have killed. Suffice to say, we've left a lot of bodies in our wake - besides rangers. And Redrover.

Some of my personal favorites are the big scary dragons that are just inexplicably standing around on aerial platforms. It's as if they are afraid of heights and won't go near the edges of their little areas.

Then there is the most terrifying of them all - giant shaman pets! OMG ... expansion after expansion of fighting dragons, gods, giant multi-armed trollops, and various tacvii-things, has lead us to big dogs. They don't fart fire, sprout extra heads, or explode into 9 Scrappy-Doo Annoying Puppies of Death, or anything cool. They just stand there and do the standard dog pawing animation.

Stay tuned for the next expansion when the most feared of all creatures makes it return to wreak terror on the masses - The_Fiendish_Moss_Snake. The Bixie_of_Terror and Orc_Pawn_of_Pain mini-bosses alone will be enough to give us nightmares I'm sure. I mean Beldon left the game after his shameful defeat by a bixie, so what does the future hold for the rest of us?

Daosheen in the Disco Ball of Doom is kind of a fun event too. Though SOE's idea of springing [censored] on you when the fight is almost over is just cruel the first time you encounter it.

Never letting a good model go to waste, here is a shot of our Porthio win from a while back (and a few times since).

Almost as annoying as the spinny bats are the hoardes of phlegm-ball tree-bush things that root you every 10 seconds in Theatre of Blood. Speaking of ToB, SOE has gone back to its roots (I'm so funny!) in making mobs drop class-specific items. Remember back in Fear when pullers would hear, "Pull some warrior mobs!" "No, Enchanter mobs!" and then they would train a ton of mobs to the raid and kill everyone? HAHAHAHAHA those were the days. Well now we can all revisit those rose-colored days of yore in ToB!

One final thing I want to mention in PoR is my admiration of the new Stronghold zone doors. Yeah, it sux to have to spend 2 minutes beating on a door just to have the privilege of zoning through. This is especially true if you are not a melee-type. It is funny though to listen to the casters in /guil crying about not being able to melee down the door. But the very best aspect of the new Widow-Maker doors? It's watching some dork blaze a trail of mobs right smack into the door and then realize he can't zone through. Mob after mob piles into the door and beats the holy living crap out of the training jerk while he screams for help. Then of course you die too from the train, but at least it was funny watching HIM die first!! :D

Ubiquitous Year-End Review 12/29/2005
Kaitlynn

Much like any year, 2005 was a year of highs and lows for those of us in Talisman. The highs included the typical things you expect in a MMORPG: new expansions, new mobs (and their first kills), new members (and their touching first TalisDeaths), and new loot. Some of the lows were the loss of some of our active members, continued dwindling numbers following the Summer Slump, and one very real loss to the Talisman family.

2005 in Review:

January saw both our very first Warden Hanvar kill in Anguish, and our very first (near) full-clearing of Tacvi. On the evening of January 17, we knocked off Pixtt Xxeric Kex, Zun Muram Kvxe Pirik, Zun`Muram Yihst Vor, Pixtt Kretv Krakxt, Zun'Muram Shaldn Boc, and Zun`Muram Mordl Delt all back-to-back. (Ed note: Thank god for copy-n-paste - that's a LOT of unpronounceable names!) I would like to add, once again, that SOE is still missing out on a great opportunity for more money, since demand remains high for the Plushy Tacvi Collection - most notably the Plushy Goat (Xxeric Kex).

January also saw our first defeat of the Jelvan event in Anguish, followed immediately by the 1st kill of the Arch Magus Vangl.

In March, we killed Vishimtar for the first time. Of course by then we'd already knocked off the rest of the Dragons of Norrath mobbies: Yar'Lir, Kessdona, Ju'rek, Rikkukin, Volkara, Shogurei, T'Shara, and a special appearance by Tirranun (though he left before we could kill him). We also tore a hole in the wall in Lavastorm exposing the Broodlands, and then promptly trampled the island into the sea, exposing T'Shara's lair.

In April we finally got the satisfaction we desired by getting our first kill of Tunat Muram Cuu Vauax in Tacvi. With the Anniversary celebration under way, we also took time to knock off the best loot-giving Fabled mobs until the celebration was over. I use my Inviso-BP every day, so am quite thankful. :)

In June, the Summer Slump officially took hold and we saw our numbers drop. Still, we were able to get our first kill of the revamped Vishimtar 2.0. We also goofed around with the Depth of Darkhollow pre-release fun by killing Ithiasor, though by then they had depopped the quest-giving npc so we never did find out what the quest gave hehe.

The Summer Slump drug on, Depths of Darkhollow was released, followed by the frenzied exploration that entailed. We also saw member losses due to EverQuest II and that other game (WoW, can't seem to think of the name at the moment) so things were pretty slow for several months.

By the end of October and heading into November the quest and flag progression reached critical mass and we saw our first (major) kills of the new expansion: Master Vule the Silent Tear and Sendaii, the Hive Queen. By then of course we had knocked off the rest of the progression mobs: Emperor Draygun, Matriarch Shyra, Bloodeye, and the Council of the Nine.

The late summer / early fall months were a tragic time for the US, and with members of our family spread all over the world, invariably some were in harm's way. Thankfully, even though we had a few members in the hurricane-devastated areas of Mississippi, Louisiana, and Texas, they and their families made it out safely. Hurricanes and natural disasters aren't the only bad things in the world however. Lozack lost his battle with cancer and passed away late in November (This deserves more than a simple note for the year, so more on this below).

At this point in the year, the Summer Slump gave way to the Thanksgiving holiday and then on to the Christmas/Hanukkah holidays and even onward to the new year. We've been in the Demi Plane for a long time now and still have not progressed past Zi-Thuuli. Our numbers are still very low for reasons that we can't pinpoint so as you can imagine, we will be pushing hard for recruiting as the new year approaches. Even so, with just a few days to go in the calendar year, we still generated over 70 pages of points records. So we've been busy - and we plan on staying that way!

Lozack:

As I briefly noted already, on November 21, 2005 one of our members passed away from Leukemia. Lozack was one of our Cleric Crew and showed a dedication that frankly, many of us lack. He'd be making no sense in chat and when asked, Oh he was hopped up on drugs after receiving some medical procedure. Or on occasion he would go on-and-off afk and when asked, well he is in the hospital and using a laptop so sometimes he had to put it down with little notice. The guy would play in almost any spare moment he had, when, I would venture to say, not many (if any) of us would.

Those are just a couple of observations I personally made. I won't lie and claim to have known him as well as I should, but his dedication was without question. To quote something his brother posted on our board, "He really loved 4 things in his life, his family, his cats, his ballteam and his EQ." Lozack is greatly missed.
