High Note 09/04/2006
Kaitlynn

I write this huddled behind the bank desk in the Guild Hall. Angry guildmates are all around. They eye me warily, some pass with a slight smile that is not reflected in their eyes. Others are more open in their disdain. "When are we getting an update?" they snipe at me.

"I'm working on it," I lie. Truth is I've just been out of it lately, barely managing the energy to log on at all. And in fact I gave in last weekend and *didn't* log on. So I've had nothing left to spend on what passes for creativity to work on an update. But they can't hear that. "It's coming, I swear."

We beat The Performer event last week for the first time. Let me restate that in case your eyes are glazing over reading this editorial:

We beat The Performer!

Our first win on this event came on August 21, after a LOT of practice. We had upwards of 35-40 people still alive at the end of the time. It was a truly stunning win all the way around. All the practice paid off, and we tore it up like we'd been farming it for weeks.

As fun as the first win was though ... I actually think the win a couple nights ago on the 29th was even MORE fun. Why? Because it was a nail-biter. Full raid, and we won with only 6 people alive at the end!!! Thank god we had a necro afk... err FD in there to rez a cleric lol. Truth be told, the raid was going down-hill - the mobs were just picking us off at the end. It was over. But the tables got turned and it was the MOBS who ran out of time. They didn't get us all killed in time lol. We Win! (again). :D

And from now on, I don't care how good we are doing on an event, I'll never "predict" again like I did in the last update. I predicted we were only a few days, a week tops from beating him. And that was JUNE! For unknown reasons we hit a HUGE slump with people not logging on. We actually had a few raid nights that turned into "Go farm some xp and AAs for the new expansion" since we didn't have enough people to do anything. So no more. I don't care if I do an update where we missed a new kill because someone tripped on a spent mod rod and cost us the win. I won't predict!!!

 

Snowtail:

Another of our First-Kills since last update was that big American Eskimo, Snowtail! Unfortunately, I wasn't there for it but they tell me it was exciting. /Gump So that's all I've got to say about that.

 

Mayong Mistmoore:

We've now got our sights set on the elusive Mayong Mistmoore. He spends his days practicing sword twirling and shouting ominous warnings at us through-out the zone. So we go to his room and beat on his minions, and taunt him by yelling "Hey, play Misty for me, muhahahaha." He just stands there, throwing his sword in the air, looking all spiffy in his cool armour, and pays us no heed. Until we piss him off enough. Then you get to hear some KICK-ASS music even if you have your music turned off. I have to hand it to SOE for this one, hearing that music when Mistmoore goes active is truly a "Holy Crap!" moment.

We're still refining the event, but we've turned him on a few times .... umm I mean, we've "gotten him active," and last night's attempts went VERY well. BUT I MAKE NO PREDICTIONS!!!

Update Updated! 06/11/2006
Kaitlynn

Another update so soon!!?!? OMG!

I had to wait until the month changed for this little addendum, otherwise the site might have crashed. It just isn't used to me posting more than about once a quarter! Anyway, this is more of an Update-update, so will be short and sweet.

Roley DeFarge:

With surprisingly little time spent practicing this event, we were able to beat Roley. Then again, it may have something to do with his name. I mean seriously, a vampire named Roley? I'm sure all the other vampires made fun of him, stole the dirt from his coffin while he was out, or hung bat sticky-strips near his windows. This fight is long enough that it has that air of unpredictable chaos (can chaos be predictable?) that makes it feel that it may be lost in a most gloriously horrific manner at any point ... but is otherwise pretty straightforward. :)

Seeing Roley and Adrianna dancing at the end was sweet though. And I think we can all agree that we'd do this fight without loot just to bring those two undead kids together. Aaaawwwww.

 

Tris Wallows III:

Well, no mention of Tris would be complete without questioning her name. Is this one that a Dev slipped by the Suits? Almost all of us did a double-take when her name came up on target. "Tri Swallows?" Errr "Tri Swallows ...3 !?!?!?!" Of course being the naive embodiment of purity and sweetness, I had to have someone explain the joke to me, but I digress.

The Wallows Encounter is pretty fun I think. Has those elements of timing, direction-following (a Talisman anti-trademark), and that moment where it better all come together or the event explodes. Fun!

 

Devlin Rochester:

Very shortly after my previous update, we beat Devlin. But beating him wasn't enough for me to de-lazify and do an update so had to wait until more. :)

Much like a lot of PoR, the Devlin fight requires that all of us pay attention to detail. No more Melee afk-fighting. No more Clerics watching TV pressing their heal buttons. No more Wizards standing in one place pressing their Big-Badda-Boom button then going afk for Rez. In short - this fight was a lot of trouble for us to learn. :D

But, like Bart reaching for the electrified cupcake, we are nothing if not persistant; in the end we prevailed. He's not so hard, but it does really test how you as a guild raid. It is far more about refining how the people BEHIND the keyboards raid, then it is about "clerics do this, melee do that," etc.

 

The Performer:

Our current target is The Performer. We are progressing very smoothly, and it wouldn't surprise me to have him beaten within a couple days of getting this update posted.

Yet another encounter where every single person has to pay super close attention. This expansion has tested our limits! :p

Look! An Update! 05/15/2006
Kaitlynn

Sanguimanus the Redfang:

Ok, first thing's first. We finally beat that mangy bat Redfang! And as with so many of our first kills, we ended up with a really awful corpse pic to show off.

We began work learning the Redfang encounter just before Prophecy of Ro came out. Since that fight (and any fight involving those spinny bats) is annoying as hell, we dropped it from our target list as fast as we could so we could go frolic in the happy-fun greener pastures of PoR. Eventually guilt and the routine of killing PoR mobs got the better of us and we realized we needed to get back to the Demi Plane and continue where we left off. The amount of rust we had on encounters that we had so recently farmed was embarassing to say the least, but we got back into the swing of things quickly.

Eventually, after night upon night of intense (read: grueling) Redfang Appreciation Training, we finally beat his little furry ass down. Now it's on to Tier Two!


Prophecy of Ro :

With so many new mobs in the Prophecy of Ro expansion, there is no way to adequately list everything we have killed. Suffice to say, we've left a lot of bodies in our wake - besides rangers. And Redrover.

Some of my personal favorites are the big scary dragons that are just inexplicably standing around on aerial platforms. It's as if they are afraid of heights and won't go near the edges of their little areas.

Then there is the most terrifying of them all - giant shaman pets! OMG ... expansion after expansion of fighting dragons, gods, giant multi-armed trollops, and various tacvii-things, has lead us to big dogs. They don't fart fire, sprout extra heads, or explode into 9 Scrappy-Doo Annoying Puppies of Death, or anything cool. They just stand there and do the standard dog pawing animation.

Stay tuned for the next expansion when the most feared of all creatures makes it return to wreak terror on the masses - The_Fiendish_Moss_Snake. The Bixie_of_Terror and Orc_Pawn_of_Pain mini-bosses alone will be enough to give us nightmares I'm sure. I mean Beldon left the game after his shameful defeat by a bixie, so what does the future hold for the rest of us?

Daosheen in the Disco Ball of Doom is kind of a fun event too. Though SOE's idea of springing [censored] on you when the fight is almost over is just cruel the first time you encounter it.

Never letting a good model go to waste, here is a shot of our Porthio win from a while back (and a few times since).

Almost as annoying as the spinny bats are the hoardes of phlegm-ball tree-bush things that root you every 10 seconds in Theatre of Blood. Speaking of ToB, SOE has gone back to its roots (I'm so funny!) in making mobs drop class-specific items. Remember back in Fear when pullers would hear, "Pull some warrior mobs!" "No, Enchanter mobs!" and then they would train a ton of mobs to the raid and kill everyone? HAHAHAHAHA those were the days. Well now we can all revisit those rose-colored days of yore in ToB!

One final thing I want to mention in PoR is my admiration of the new Stronghold zone doors. Yeah, it sux to have to spend 2 minutes beating on a door just to have the privilege of zoning through. This is especially true if you are not a melee-type. It is funny though to listen to the casters in /guil crying about not being able to melee down the door. But the very best aspect of the new Widow-Maker doors? It's watching some dork blaze a trail of mobs right smack into the door and then realize he can't zone through. Mob after mob piles into the door and beats the holy living crap out of the training jerk while he screams for help. Then of course you die too from the train, but at least it was funny watching HIM die first!! :D
