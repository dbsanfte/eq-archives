 Shadows of Laglin

December 9th
 
~Alendora

The question that everyone is asking this week is, "how do you like Luclin?" Of course you'll hear a lot of moaning and groaning. But the reality is: people we haven't seen in forever are back...and excited about the game again. Even those who have been level 60 forever and hate exp'ing are zealously grinding away at alt exp. You have to acknowledge a good hook when you see one. Especially one that gets you running to the store to buy geforce3's and RAM sticks without a second thought.

We spent Moon week exploring Luclin and running back to ToV for quite a few Vulaks while we got used to the new way of things. Ever see Where's Waldo? That's what it's like finding Gendal now. You have to wade through a mob of casters at every raid.

We did a little experimenting with the Luclin portals. So we can port straight to EJ or SF now, and we can port to Velious now without keys, but you have to wait 15+ minutes to go to Luclin? It's so boring that impersonating Nexus Scions and handing out diamonds would be waaaaay fun...except that you can sometimes get ported up even if you have no shard. Unless you're Abatoir.

The lag has been so bad that many have turned off the new models. Those that LIKE the new models and didn't already have them turned off, that is. Reading the chat window has become quite a challenge.

Upon logging in, Berryfyne: hot. Alendora: ick.

We visited The Deep and The Grey after another post-patch ToV session. The new zones really are breathtaking, and the new mobs are pretty cool as well. It has made exp groups really enjoyable again. As long as you don't die and have to spend 30 minutes getting back to where you were in Luclin.

We paid a visit to Akheva and wandered around setting off traps and having AE worm toast-a-thons. Qhue even tested out his new aggro spell.

By far the most fun, however, was Ssra's. We visited the zone again after they itemized it. The shissar are just too cool, the spiral portals are nifty, and the risk vs reward is pretty balanced. We picked up some new items and lots of level 60 spells. Qhue scribed Koadic's Endless Intellect and all us casters like him a LOT better now.

Any place with murals of snakes eating Iksar is an OK place in my book.

The scenery inside the temple just kept getting better and better. You haven't lived until you've been whipped by a 10' high snake.

Rallos Zek, Server of Warmth and Friendliness

December 9th
 
~Alendora

Rallos Zek was an interesting server to visit. When I say visit, we're not talking a brief stay. No, we're talking about two long days of sitting around waiting to fight. During that time the Rallos Zek folks made sure we all felt welcome.

I suppose my masculinity was being insulted? Ouch! Luckily RZ had a few good folks though.

Of course I could complain and whine and moan about how poorly run it was and how we waited around forever for two days and even got up early () or how everyone else had more Soulfires and more Ivandyrs Hoops, but honestly, the fighting isn't what makes ToT so awesome. It's getting to screw around with folks from other servers, like Azerinn from Brell's Silent Redemption, Lohengrin of the RZ team Ascending Dawn, Romell and Korwin from Legacy of Sorrow, and of course our old friends from ToT II, Triton and Zarach, Bayerithe, etc from Harmonium.

Well, that and messing with Gendal. Everyone who didn't have a surname chose Diegendaldie and we asked GM Sayah if she could DT Gendal for us just for fun. GMs need to learn to be a little more spontaneous and flexible.
