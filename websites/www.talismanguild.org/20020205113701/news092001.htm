The Artificial End

September 2nd
 
~Alendora

As we all know, Everquest is a game without an 'end'. No grand finale, no huge explosions, no "GAME OVER" is ever going to grace your monitor. It is the lack of an end that has kept everyone playing for over two years now.

Instead of a real end to the game, it appears that the clearing of the Temple of Veeshan is an artificial end, a signal that the pinnacle of EQ has been achieved.

A few people said, "as soon as Vyemm and Vulak are dead, I'm going on a vacation from EQ." Then, as soon as they were dead, more and more people were saying that they were taking long breaks, or they weren't going to play as much, or quitting altogether.

Verant waited too long between Velious and the upcoming Luclin expansion. Velious was the high end player's dream...lots of new uber mobs, tons of new uber gear, a virtual playground for the 50+ crowd. But at this point, it's 'been there, done that' for a lot of people, and Luclin promises to be geared towards the Lake of Ill Omen crowd.

People are crowing about how "Everquest is dying!" Of course, they have been saying that for at least a year now. Everquest is not dying, it will continue to have new subscribers, and even if boredom drives the 60 crowd from the game, there is still a crowd of 50s behind them waiting to view the marvels of the Sleeper's chamber or take their first steps into the Temple of Veeshan north wing.

Until then, we can all expect to see a few more artificial end game *extensions* too. Like decreasing the Plane of Mischief drops and hinting at undiscovered PoM quests and new things to be discovered in Sleeper's Tomb. Who needs new zones or expansions? Just add in a little change here and there and make existing mobs/quests harder. Yeah, that will keep us playing!

For now we will help Verant out by inventing our own challenges, like uber mob corpse placement and iron man raiding (we did it with 18!).

And speaking of challenges, have you ever played 'hide the snake'? It's one of my favorite games. I'm not talking some little puny thing, I'm talking one of the big ones. Take Koi'Doken for example, look at the length of that sucker:

And here, in one of the many mysteries of Everquest, almost the entire length and breadth of Koi'Doken is hidden. You try playing hide the snake and see if you can manage to sink it so deep only the hint of a head is showing!
