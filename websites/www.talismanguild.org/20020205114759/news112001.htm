Paladins: Expendable

November 28th
 
~Alendora

We are always so happy when it's ToV North week. Even more so now with the ongoing problem of everything except Velketor being dead by the time we log on after a patch. We did our usual dungeon crawl, with our usual amazing skill.

Now how many guilds ever get to see their raid leaders say "Book it to Vyemm lair"? Not that we mind, the best part about fighting in Vyemm's lair is that while the melee are busy we casters get to slide up and down the ramp leading down into the lair. But we take the encounter very seriously, I swear.

Ashana had been waiting for legs from Dagarn the Destroyer for an eternity. Well the unhuman patience (ours of course having to listen to Ash whine) paid off, and Ashana was rewarded with...the ugliest legs I've ever seen. Ashana was frightening before, now she's hideous. Our other lizard monk, Wynde, weighs looks over utility and makes the right call.

This Vulak, Bellelynda was the happy recipient of some Rowyl's Metal Armguards. Thank heaven they updated it from the ugly pink plate look to a nice druidy green. Our steaming sexpot Gneagle got some new Kelciferous arms and Dominatrix Santhia the first Mrylokar's dagger on Tunare. Luckily for Sandy, the dagger doubles as a shishkebab stick when a grill is handy.

Best of all, this trip we managed to kill Gendal *several* times. The guild is temporarily satiated. But even in the joy of the moment the guild didn't miss Canibal's dereliction of his proper duty (feeding clerics of course).

I have been noticing since The Melee Patch that there is NO paladin bashing any more. Verant has spoiled all our fun. So I have decided to work towards rectifying this. It's for their own good, they are starting to realize that they are really contributing to raids, and it's getting to their heads.

For example, when we were in PoG the other night with our twink army, we decided to give Ail the Elder a try. Back in the day he was the tree you avoided at all costs, so we had no idea what his AE was like. Well, as our piles of Frostweaver Axe-bearing twink corpses illustrate, we were a little unprepared for Ail's AE. Ail was at no life and the casters were nuking like mad and someone asked "are there any melee left alive?" The answer: "Abatoir." What does the raid leader say? "Abatoir tank that tree while the casters camp!" Now Abatoir I'm sure felt quite the stud, saving all the casters. But he missed the underlying message: paladins are expendable.

Then a few days later we're getting ready for Vyemm and we need to get him pulled. Who does the raid leader pick? Abreastoir of course. Next thing we see is Abs running down the halls of ToV, Vyemm chasing him, until Abs makes it to us and dies at our feet. Once again, you know he was patting himself on the back, thinking he's a hero, but what were we all thinking? Sucker.

It's time paladins face facts. You were the sacrificial lambs on Trakanon raids over a year ago and Patch or no Patch, you still are. In the end, everyone else >>> paladins. And, if you're Abs, paladins >>> Abatoir. Now shut up and go Lay Hands the main tank so that the clerics don't die and make yourself useful!

We have had a few people return from long absences from EQ. Dain Khaidu came to tovn and we all gushed about how much we had missed him. Until he logged.

Jeana wins the award for the most dedicated employee.

 Heathens, all of you

November 18th
 
~Alendora

Camelot's been a lot of fun lately, as you might expect when the guild goes on one of the many mass incursions into Albion and Hibernia. There's nothing more satisfying than walking over the piles of enemy corpses into your new Keep.

DAoC has also seen the return of some...er...ANCIENT Talisman members like Desslok, Aery, and Phobus (Kristian). Talisman has a high female to male ratio compared to the rest of the realm. You can run past 100 people and see only one female in Midgaard, it's very odd. For some reason the trend of guys making female characters just doesn't seem as popular in DAoC as it is in EQ...maybe it has something to do with the women not showing up in lingerie or a bathing suit when they die?

It's been rather quiet in EQ, mostly because everything is in a constant state of dead. We always used to read up about other servers and the heavy competition and be thankful there were only two guilds killing the high end mobs. Now we all sit around crying in our cups about the golden era when stuff used to live until American prime time. Everyone keeps saying "it's ok it's only two more weeks to Luclin", conveniently forgetting the lack of high level adventuring there (if reports are to be believed). The servers are overcrowded at the top end, people are bored with the same old same old, and the warm fuzzies from the Luclin release will probably last as long as a 13 year old's first orgasm.

We did have some more fun with another 10th Ring War though. As you can see from the picture below, Narandi watches as everyone attacks...nothing. I love the lag of 45 people holding a ring war with 65+ spectators (and the 20 or so people who chip in). =)

There has been this odd trend over the last month or two of people getting on corpses. We can't kill Narandi, Tormax, Dain, or any mob in a fairly public area for that matter, without some nitwit spamming the corpse and sometimes even succeeding in getting to loot it. Now why is it that we haven't seen this kind of behavior until recently?

Well, maybe it's because this is the realization of the beggar generation's legacy. You remember how long it took you to get enough money to buy spells, or even a moderate level weapon when you started out? Not the beggar generation. They hang out in Rivervale or other banks and send you those wonderful tells like...

These are beggars that are better equipped than my twinks. They LAUGH at the idea of wearing bronze. So what happens when at level two you are twinked to the level we were all at after the long months it took to hit 50? That's right, you get hordes of greedy little punk ass kiddies who think the best way to get a Bile Etched Obsidian Choker is to ninja loot it.

Now, I know with the anonymity that EQ provides, people do things they wouldn't normally do. But let me ask, would you do the following irl?

When in the men's bathroom taking a leak, do you stare at the guy at the next urinal?

Do you leave work before 5 pm without permission because you are "done with the task you were working on"?

Do you 'forget' to tip the hotty who just gave you a 'special' table dance?

Do you tell your girlfriend to meet you at work at 5:00 pm because you need a ride, then call her on her cell phone from home saying, "oh yeah, forgot to tell you, Mike gave me a ride"?

Whenever you get a ride from your girlfriend and she drops you off, do you leave your car door open and walk away?

When your friend brings his girlfriend to a party, do you spend the entire night hitting on her?

Ok then, so why is it in game...

When someone has killed ubermob01, you get on the corpse "just to see what he drops"?

You mem Ring: Misty and port out of Fear because all the gorgons are dead?

Someone comes from three zones away to give you a res or port and you don't offer pp or even say "thank you"?

You ask for a port from Great Divide to Greater Faydark and then say "oops, forgot to tell you I got a ride" when the wizzy gives you a tell from GD saying "um…/who all says you're in gfay?"

You get a port from a druid or wizard and disband from the group while he's still zoning?

You start hitting a mob someone else is on and try to KS them?

Lazy inconsiderate bastards! I swear 90% of the EQ population was raised in a barn.

And then, there is the haven of my guild. They are all kind, considerate, and truly caring...

So, you're looking for someone of the fairer sex to spend a lot of time within EQ, but your pickup lines aren't working? Well, news flash: we get enough cheesy pickup lines irl already thanks, we don't need them in game too.

So if you can't impress us with your flowery inanities, how do you get chicks? Well, if you're a mage, I have a foolproof method for you. It's called Monster Summoning. You should never, ever struggle with the choice of whether to go with mage epic uber pet or Monster Summoning pet...use the one that nets you the babes. Well, unless she's an evil bitch like Jeana and she likes killing small cute defenseless creatures.

Try this little guy in Chardok or Dragon Necropolis. All the chicks in your raiding party will pet him, hug him, and generally coo over him all raid long. Because "he's so CUTE!" And the great thing is, just like when you walk your dog in the park, some of that love and attention lavished on your pet will come your way too. If you learn to shrink your pets (smaller is cuter of course) and get them to play tricks, you have a guaranteed score on your hands.
