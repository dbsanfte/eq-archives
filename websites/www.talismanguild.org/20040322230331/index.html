The Passion for GoD! 3/3/2004
Beldon

Finally... New Content!! Talisman wrapped up our LDoN era with victorys over Valdoon, The Synarcana, and The Prison Break and jumped into the release of Gates of Discord, never to look back. The new content is extremely refreshing and is rejuvenating life back into the majority of the guild who were quickly losing interest, considering the only semi-entertaining raids were those in Plane of Time. We now routinely clear Plane of Time in one night, so that leaves 5 days to occupy ourselves with other content.

As is custom with Talisman, we immediately began to ride the bleeding edge of progression in GoD. The biggest challenge the first two weeks was our quest to find patience. As with any new expansion, things move fast and furious at the beginning, often seemingly leaving people behind. I feel we've done a great job in sticking together and helping everyone progress thru the early 6 trials. That opened up Kod'Taz for us. The exploration of all this new content proves to be exciting and extremely challenging. To date, several people have completed Ikkinz trials 1 thru 3. As a guild, we have defeated The Summoner Ring, as well as The Chambers of Glorification, The Chambers of Righteousness, and the Chambers of Transcendence. We progressed into Yxxta where we encountered some difficult critters as you see at the top of this page. We've spent 4 minutes in Uqua, The Ocean God Chantry were we were quickly destroyed. We eagerly await what lies ahead.

Talisman 2003 in Review!!! 1/12/2004
Beldon

With nothing all that new to report, I thought we'd go with a 2003 Year in Review style of update this time. 2003 marked a very busy year for Talisman. Lots of new content conquered and lots of changes in the interguild relationships on Tunare. Our goal going into 2003 was of course to reach the Plane of Time. To get there we'd first have to get thru the Elemental planes. We started the year out right with our first Rallos Zek kill in mid-January gaining us access to the Elemental Planes. We spent a few weeks farming elemental armor to begin gearing up for what lay ahead. In early February we took down our first Elemental god, Fennin Ro. He proved to be rather subdued compared to his cohorts in the other planes. We then spent 2 months farming the planes and refining the various encounters until we finally beat Xegony in late March. Again, 2 months passed before we took down Coirnav in late May. That left only one god standing between us and Plane of Time. A month later after many long nights, we took down Rathe to begin our summer with entry to the Plane of Time. After a relatively slow start due to the early summer attendance bug that seemed to hit the entire EQ community, we came back together in late July to begin our methodical destruction of one god after another in Time leading up to our first slaying of Quarm on September 4th.

LDoN of course came out in September as well, adding a new dimension to Norrath in the form of adventures which marked a new way to grind exp with the additional reward of Adventure Points. The raid content of LDoN proved to be demoralizing for the most part. Long raids with the high probability of zero reward at the end. We continue to work on mastering the various raids which prove quite challenging.

That covers most of our 2003 milestones. It's also important to note some interguild relationship changes that took place earlier in the year. Tunare has historically been a server infested with guilds racing for mobs, running over each other for content, emotional flare ups, flame threads on the boards, you name it. Early 2003 was no different. Our last race with DC ended on 2/22/03 when the race ended in a tie in Sol Ro's chamber in our final cockblock attempt into Plane of Fire, our precious farmland. The race grew heated. Wicked words were exchanged. Rogues from both guilds quickly snatched up the arrows the opposing guild's mages were dropping, it was flat out ugly. Both guilds quickly realized this had to stop. This marked the beginning of a time of tolerance between both guilds. Agreements were made to eliminate all racing by rotating weeks in the various planes. This agreement continued throughout the year up to and including our current Time rotation. Re-flagging agreements also began taking place among many of the guilds on the server to help the newer members of all guilds catch up to the rest on flags. This all resulted in what most consider a more enjoyable atmosphere to play in.

As we enter 2004, we eagerly anticipate the release of Gates of Discord and all the new AA's, some of which we hope will be useful.

As far as other stuff to report, we had an interesting Emp of Ssra encounter right before Christmas. Upon zoning into Ssra to do a late night Cursed kill, we quickly found the Emperor waiting for us at zone in. Somehow he had warped on Gale of Destruction when they wiped, so we chalked up our first emp kill at zone in as the rescue unfolded. We still have no clue how he got there, despite various allegations.
