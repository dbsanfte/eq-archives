Professions

There are fourteen player character classes available in EverQuest. When one grows and matures in the lands of Norrath, they have a wide range of available futures, depending on their race, aptitude with magic, innate intelligence, skill at arms, and overall potential.

Not all races can be all classes, and some items are restricted as well by both race and/or class.

  • Warrior A powerful adversary, this class is most skilled at the use of offensive weapons, various armor types, and parrying ability with shields. They have the highest "Hit Points" of any class, being a group that trains and thrives on armed combat.
  • Cleric the priests and Clerics of Norrath swear fealty to a patron Deity, and must pray for their abilities. They may heal the sick and injured, as well as smite foes by calling upon the wrath of their Deity.
  • Paladin this class is a hybrid of a Warrior and Priest, being a holy knight in the service of virtue and honor. In addition to their unique powers and abilities, they share some of the abilities of the Warrior and Priest.  They may use their "Lay Hands" ability once every 24 game hours.
  • Shadow Knight much like the Paladin is the guardian of all that is holy, the Shadow Knight stands as the antithesis, being a bastion of evil. They serve evil Deities, and are a combination of the Warrior and the Necromancer, and as such use their INT as the source of their mana pool.  Their "Harm Touch" ability can similarly be used only once every 24 game hours.
  • Ranger this outsdoorsman is a combination of the Warrior and the Druid. They excel at skills which deal with nature, such as Tracking, and are most at home on a trail in the forest or the like.  Like the other "hybrids" (Paladin & Shadow Knight), they gain spells at 9th level.
  • Druid this profession has dedicated itself to the preservation of nature, which can be drawn upon to protect and help the Druid in times of need. It has been theorized that their innate ties with the animals is such that no natural animal will ever attack a Druid unless first attacked by that Druid.
  • Monk ascetic practitioners of martial prowess, the monks stand to improve themselves without reliance on most armor and weapons. They see their bodies as natural weapons, and are fervently religious. Their gods notice this zeal and reward them and their skills.
  • Bard this musical class can hold its own in most battles, but their true skill lies in their music. They can weave magical effects by their charming tunes and ballads, and can keep audiences enthralled with their magical spells while they sing and play their chosen instrument.
  • Rogue this is the spy, the assassin, the pickpocket of Norrath. They can walk undetected in shadows, silently and efficiently killing from behind with precise blows. They are trained in various weapons, but must employ clandestine methods to battle a Warrior one-on-one. They are also skilled at entry into buildings, picking locks, and other forms of thievery.
  • Shaman this is the tribal medicine man among more "primitive" races and cultures, and employ their healing and offensive abilities in the protection of their tribe. They are much like a Cleric.
  • Necromancer this practitioner of dark magic can raise and command undead, rot an opponent's body, speak with the dead, and carry out other unspeakable acts.  They are shunned by all societies but for the malicious Dark Elves. 
  • Wizard this seeker of knowledge sees magic as the goal in and of itself, and uses magical force to damage foes. He can also instantly transport over long distances or merely view objects and scenes from great distances. Their lives are dedicated to the pursuit of arcane knowledge.
  • Magician the conjurers and summoners of Norrath, this class binds other beings to their will. The ability to summon minor items and food is also the province of the Magician. Even supernatural beings can be summoned and commanded by a powerful Magician. Like a Wizard, they know some offensive magical spells, but could never compete with a Wizard in that field.
  • Enchanter the ability to modify a weapon through magic, or the ability to beguile and charm a person is the province of the Enchanter. They have some directly damaging offensive spells as well, but not to the extent of the Wizard.
