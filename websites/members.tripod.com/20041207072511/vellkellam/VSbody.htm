The ink had only just begun to dry upon the decrepit and worm-eaten parchment when midnight blue fingers purposefully dripped the reddish molten wax upon the finished scroll, a hot musty smell gently rising into the stagnant air. The darkened hooded figure carefully pressed the signet ring of his left hand into the molten wax before gently rolling up the fragile parchment and lashing it closed with the string of fleshy sinews. The wax seal solidified within a few moments, leaving the unmistakable imprint of the author's insignia. Carefully, the midnight blue fingers, having seen many days, yet somehow unaged, placed the finished scroll into a sturdy holder crafted of the finest bone. The hooded figure approached the young dark elf who remained respectfully, fearfully silent in the corner. Handing the scroll box to the younger elf, the hooded figure nodded once - ever so slightly - and the adolescent hurredly scampered off, towards the equally darkened corridors of the Library of Neriak. His nights' work only just begun, the hooded one turned and sat down before his desk once more. The midnight blue fingers grasped the quill, sharpened fingernails glinting in the meager light cast about the small study by the solitary brazier. Ink, made of crushed bone and mixed with darkened blood, was used to coat the tip of the quill. The shadowy figure paused before beginning his writings once more... beneath the dark recesses of his hood a slight smirk came unto his thin lips... and his works recommenced...
                                                                                                 "The Vellkellam Scrolls"

This site is a collection of biographies, life-stories, tales of terror and heroism of the people of Norrath. Located mainly in the Library of Neriak, the Vellkellam Scrolls can be found, if one should care to look, about the continent of Antonica, possibly all of known Norrath. I, Vellkellam, have made no attempt to monopolize my knowledge gained in my many travels during my short life so far by serving only myself or my race alone. Indeed, I have taken painful measures to travel into lands where I am loathed and cursed to bring my works, through less scrupulous acquaintences, unto the more.. "unlightened" races of this world. And so, these scrolls are for the benefit of all, whatever that may be, that ye may know of heroes and so-called villians of past and present, of meager beggars and mighty warriors, of friends and of foes... And who am I, you ask, who should compile such works for the greater knowledge of my fellow person? I assure you, tis not soley for thy benefit, as dark elves such as I are not want to help outsiders without some nefarious reason, or so many think... Despite this, if ye should be so curious as to learn more about me, Vellkellam, the author of these scrolls, proceed here ...
