I thought about this one a little while given the present state of EQ and I am a little stumped.

If no outside buffs are allowed, period, then I would lean towards 6 Shaman. Mana regeneration is a non-issue. CC is a non-issue and healing really is nothing to worry about. The only control is damage output but a smart six-pack of Shaman will pull three to six and offtank (or selftank) while piling on the dots and such. They would be suboptimal except that mana regeneration is so damn good.

A nod would go to six Beastlords naturally and they might even be superior. Against summoning Mobs they are a far better choice. Still, although pet dps is far greater they are going to need to reclaim mana at some point, even with Paragon galore. It's hard to say though, with Bst pets they could actually melee some (except perhaps the slower) and this could well be the better group.

Outdoors, go with six Wizards or Druids. Sure, mutiple-person quadding would be painful to coordinate but it still is going to work pretty well. Mana regen (assuming no outside buffs) drops them down the pole a ways though.

Another call might be the old six Necros or Mages. Not interchangeable by any stretch, they still both boast amazing disposable pet dps and alternate skills that nest well together. Six Necros was formidable before DoTs stacked, they should be fairly frightening now.

Six Bards is an oddball one. If one could coordinate six Bards for outdoor swarming/charm kites it would frankly be hilarious to see. Even DoT kiting with six would be frightening. Indoors though I would need to think dps would suffer in the end.

Six Enchanters is curious. If charmable mobs are in sufficent supply (a rarity indeed) then they are insanely potent. Of course, so is a single enchanter though or a duo with a cleric and such. Kind of a wildcard I guess.

Six melee or hybrids is less fun. Sure six paladins could do well but my presupposition of no outside buffs really wrecks all melee. The only possible exception would be the briefly lived but entertaining six Warriors with Earthshakers and Rampage group. That one would make one hell of an impressive kill before disbanding (or crashing the zone as happened in my one experiment in LDoN using a single warrior).

Good fun game though, perhaps I'll think more on it.
