Item decay, to me, seems too much like the latter examples. Part of your playing time is spent trying to keep what you have; as opposed to spending more of that time moving forward.

There are too many other ways to build and stimulate a virtual economy without requiring players to waste time on a treadmill.

If time passes and you do nothing, and your character gets stronger relative to itself, you have been rewarded.

A game requires you to act to avoid a negative adjustment. <-- unfun

A game requires you to act to gain a postive adjustment. <-- fun

Q: What is it called when a game does not punish you and does not reward you when you do not act? A: A lack of mudflation, a n absence of unfun, an absence of fun; call it "peace."

EQII with item decay and repair will have more more-intensive unfun, and more less-intensive peace.
