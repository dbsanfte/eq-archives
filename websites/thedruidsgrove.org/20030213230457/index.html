5 New zones introduced with LoY 02/12/03 9:01 am
The Gulf of Gunthak is the only port on Broken Skull Rock. Every other place where the island meets the water is steep, rocky and exposed to the rough weather. That does not mean Gunthak is an entirely safe harbor, as the shore of the gulf is barren and rocky, and the cliffs beyond the bay are holed with caves. Most of these caves are shallow, but a few of them lead deep into the cliff and open into a rather expansive cavern system.

This is the gateway to the lair of the Broken Skull Clan....

Sounds interesting :) I love new zones and storylines.

Anyway, check out the LoY site for more info. It appears that the new zones will be Gulf of Gunthak, Dulak's Harbor, Torigan Mines, Crypt of Nadox and Hates Fury. The screenshots look great! Also, if these zones are without trees, my computer will do a little dance, I swear. The only problem I see is that these will most likely be lower level zones, which doesn't leave much for most players, but we'll see.

As a side note, it looks like druids and wizards are getting a new port to Stonebrunt. Less travel time = good!

2-4-3 Patch and other goodies 02/05/03 9:50 am
Once again, not a real exciting patch. The bugs associated with Spirit of Eagle/Flight of Eagles and Foliage Shield/Invis weren't fixed. No surprise there. You can view the entire patch message here. A few notable highlights are:

- Theft of Thought works properly now, it was draining all of the caster's mana, no matter who the target was.
- Damage over Time type Area of Effect spells will no longer do damage when player character resists the spell.
- Divine Barrier now does all of its healing in a single burst instead of as regeneration.

One thing they didn't mention in the patch, was that Affliction Haste IV items were changed so that they now only work with dots. A disappointment for many casters/priests that built their gear around this focus effect in order to extend spells like malo, slows, snares, etc. Here's Absor's statement on the matter, from The Shaman's Crucible.

This is a bug fix. I'm incredibly sorry that we didn't get this into the patch message. As you noticed, we had fixed this bug once, and somehow we got it back and fixed it again this patch.

Sorry seems to be the common theme these days.

Yesterday's patch definitely won't be one to go down in EQ history as a shining example of Our Finest Hour.....

Thanks very much for your patience, and we do apologize to those who were affected yesterday.

Anyway, enough of the bitterness over lame patches! Check out our Druid AA FAQ and add your input. It's a work in process which will hopefully be complete soon.

The Guild Management Tool, included in LoY, looks like it's going to be pretty nice. Some highlights that I'm particularly looking forward to are:

-Anyone who is in a guild may access this window to see the complete list of members of their guild. Each member's name, level, class and rank in the guild are displayed
-The Guild Management System also allows a guild leader to more easily manage guild membership. A guild leader will be able to Demote or Remove members of the guild by simply highlighting their name and clicking on the appropriate button. As a result of our recent work on the EverQuest guild system, the Demote and Remove functions used in this tool will even work if the guild member is off line.

This will be a great way to get rid of those lurking alts that you thought were long gone.
