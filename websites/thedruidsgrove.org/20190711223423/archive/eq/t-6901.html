03-05-2004, 09:16 PM
Casaron is a granule, looks a lot like Preen, except it isn't a pre-emergent, it's a "kill anything with non-woody growth near the ground". It essentially kills anything that sprouts or anything that isn't a bush/shrub/tree. It's great for things like gravel driveways (it's a growth hormone blocker), rosebeds or any other place where all you have is woody things.

It will run off if you put it on a slope, and kill whatever it happens to run off into. (Found that out the hard way)

Basically, if you don't want anything growing in that spot for, oh, 4 to 5 years or so, it's the stuff to use. The bed I had the Bishops Weed in happens to also have a HUGE hydrangea in it, so I don't care much about the area around it looking "bare".

Just FYI: if you're going for a viney/runner type propagating groundcover, the Preen will actually prevent it from spreading (unless the runners spread underground like ivy does, but ivy doesn't suppress weeds very well). Preen works as a root growth hormone inhibitor. If you ever see a weed growing in an area where the preen is down, you'll notice that it can be raked up, and it will have very very stunted roots.

So essentially, by putting the Preen down, any runner type plant will have any roots it might want to throw down inhibited by the Preen too ><

Aquila, where do you live? The ideal pruning time depends on your location. If you still have snow on the ground, or the threat of a killing frost still exists, don't prune them yet.

In Portland, the pruning season is valentine's day through st. paddys day. If you're in the great lakes region or the interior, it'll be later than that.

Burning bushes (I have one in my yard, they're so pretty in the fall) can be pruned pretty far back as long as you don't prune the 'trunk' type branches. The long thin branched can be taken back to 2-3 leaf nodules away from the main trunk (leaf nodules are those little bumps on the stems).

Kudzu is really awful. Unlike blackberries which are nearly impossible to kill, kudzu is just such a fast grower that once it gets started, you almost can't stop it. (runner type spreading like most ivys)) Good thing we imported it from another continent huh?
