Levels 1-4

"Newbie Zones": Greater Faydark. Misty Thicket. Qeynos Hills.

North Qeynos- The primary newbie zone for Qeynos. Rangers and Druids who would begin in Qeynos are instead started in Surefall Glades. the Qeynos newbie area is much better hunting for a new character than Qeynos Hills

Levels 5-9

Greater Faydark- Greater Faydark is a large zone with enough level variety to conintue hunting here for good experience and questing past the "newbie" levels of 1-4.
Misty Thicket- Much like Greater Faydark, Misty Thicket has a wide range of levels and is a good source of experience and questing beyond levels 1-4
Blackburrow- One of the lowest level dungeons in the game, the place where you will learn important aspects about EQ, such as the "Train". Zoning for safety. The import of your Snare spell. The basics of Corpse Recovery.
Crushbone- The Faydwer answer to Blackburrow. Instead of gnolls its orcs and if you hear "Dvinn to zone!" prepare to die
Butcherblock- The "newbie" zone for the dwarven race, it is contiguous with Greater Faydark and a nice change of scenery.
Qeynos Hills.
Qeynos Catacombs-Stick on the first level for now and becaureful where you wander. Only a few turns can move you from newbie mobs to bright red mobs
North Ro.

Levels 10-14

Paludal Caves- On Luclin, once you reach level 13 or 14 I would strongly suggest moving to Paludal Caves
Misty Thicket- Yes, still. Many players, even those non-halflings with the means to travel there, will level from 1 through 14 without leaving Rivervale and Misty Thicket. My personal feelings were that the experience started to slow down around level 12.
Blackburrow- Being level 10-14 won't help you much buahahaha
Crushbone- See Blackburrow above, double Buah
Western, Eastern, and Northern Karanas- Three different zones. Druids tend to spend alot of time here. Watch out for the lions, they don't care if you are a druid, you still taste good. Beware Hill Giants and Grifffawn/enne/ons
East Commonlands- Orc Camps. Learn the joys of grouping. Undead Ruins. Learn the joys of Adds. Lions and Bears and snakes of every size. Beware the Griffon
West Commonlands- More Orc camps. Derv Camps. All sorts of animals. Beware the Hill Giants.
Befallen- Another dungeon. Undead..lots of them. Locked doors requiring keys. Good fun time here
Runnyeye- A dungeon off of Misty Thicket, good place to learn additional engine-I-mean-dungeoneering skills
Oasis of Marr- The first of many zones which are considered the place to hunt for a given level span. As a result you will find a den of scum and villainy. People will KS you. People will train you. High level characters will unleash killer Spectres of death and mayhem to slaughter you and your companions and their companions and everyone in between. Sand Giants will squish you. Mummies will make you cry for Mommy and Lockjaw will pull a Captain Hook on you. All said and done, however, it is a great place in the early game to experience, learn your class in both a grouping and solo role, and meet the people who may end up being your best friends for the next 50 levels.
Lake Rathtear- One of my favorite places to level at this range were the little aviaks outside the Arena. Just the little ones!

Levels 15-19
Highpass Hold- Gnolls then Orcs. A very good place to learn your role in a group situation. Fairly good experience as well. Technically not a dungeon, it has all the inditia of a Dungeon. Many vendors and tradeskills are also available in this zone. This is also where you will most likely do your very first rare spawn camp for the Testament of Vaneer quest. It is also contiguous with High Keep which has a bank. A very nice zone.
Western, Eastern, and Northern Karanas- Remember I told you Druids spend alot of time here? While you are waiting for that spot to open up in High Pass you can solo a slew of mobs in East Karana. For a slight change of pace you can also play in West and North Karana.
Paludal Caves- Probably the best experience in the game for these levels. Once you hit 18 or so it starts to slow down, but its still good.
The Lake of Ill Omen- One of the most infamous zones in the game. It is a huge zone. It has a good Zone Experience Modifier since it is also a newbie zone for the Iksars. It has nice loot as well. You can level here from 15 to 30 if you desire and many people do. You will want to start out down the river from Firiona Vie zone and off to the left. If it /cons blue, kill it. If it /cons white, kill it. If it /cons yellow, kill it. If it cons /red, give it the ole college try...you have SoW. If you thought Oasis was bad, it was simply a training (pun intended) for the Lake. By the time you hit level 19, you will want to be hunting goblins in the ruins and at the goblin caves. You will also want to hunt around the Windmill and the undead house. Whatever you do..don't go in the water. Jaws doesn't hold a candle up to what a deepwater barracuda or bloodgill goblin will do to a level 20 character.
Runnyeye- Still a good place
Unrest- More trains..this time with undead. An oldie but goodie.
Netherbian Lair- I know little about this zone. It has fungus. It has Netherbians. It also tends to have 2 or 3 content groups happily pulling these critters and slaughtering them. It is in Luclin, so the experience can't be bad.
Mistmoore- People say its a great zone. Myself, I have never once gone to this zone and not been killed. Its a train station, but good experience with some decent low level drops. It is a good place to hunt up through level 30, even.
Najena- Not the best place, imo, but it gives you a chance to play with your anti-summoned nukes and is usually fairly empty. On the other hand its one of the few places Iron Oxide drops so you can often find a high level or two laying waste to every elemental in the zone.
Permafrost- Another goblin infested dungeon. Off of Everfrost the main entrance to this dungeon is guarded by Ice Giants. They don't see invisible though. There is also a secret entrance...

Levels 20-24
Lake of Ill Omen- Yes, this zone is still one of the most popular, if not the most popular zone to hunt for this level range. At 20 you can start taking on the weaker Sarnaks and continue hunting around the undead house. The various goblins are still good. If its blue kill it.
High Keep- Found in Highpass, this castle has various merchants and a bank in the basement. Further down in the basement you will find pickclaw goblins. Good hunting here.
Western, Eastern, and Northern Karanas- By this point North, East, and West may start drying up for experience. Fear not, there is still South Karana. The mobs are abundant and the rain is even heavier! Aside from various wandering mobs, like centaurs, there is the aviak tree which is excellent hunting until about 25. There is also a hermit I hear is good wholesome fun to slaughter. Warning, at these levels you will want to stay away from the Gnolls
Lake Rathetear- Now you can start heading back to Lake Rathe to take on the big aviaks which are scattered throughout the zone. You can also try out the Gnoll camp there.
Sol A- You know, three years of playing this game and for the life of me I don't know the proper name for Sol A. This was once one of the more popular dungeons for this level. Unfortunately the drops here are lackluster in today's game. The experience is still decent though and its another place to learn the roles a druid can play in a dungeon group environment.
Runnyeye- Still a nice place to hunt at this level
Upper Guk- Ah, the stepping stone to Lower Guk. Home of the dreaded Frogluks. Birthplace of the FROAAAK. Home of the vaunted Ghoulbane, Croc legs and arms, the Runed Bone Fork, and many others. Fun place to hunt.
Unrest- Unrest still isn't done training you. Get back there and play dodge the hag.
Warsliks Woods- I believe the Giant fort there is good experience at these levels.
Rathe Mountains- You can start killing here, just besure to /con before you kill. There are some very tough mobs in this zone at this level
Permafrost- Still good hunting here, you will have to move further in to find it though now.
Paludal Caverns- Kill the Recondite Camps here. Very nice XP with some decent loot along the lines of the Bandit Camps from the old world.

Levels 25-29
Lake of Ill Omen- Any sane person would be getting sick of this place by now...but it is still a very good place to hunt. The Sarnak fort and the Goblin Tunnels are the places to be.
South Karana- Still one of the most popular places to hunt at this level. You can still kill Avocets and Rooks over at the Aviak area. You can also start killing gnolls around Split Paw
Split Paw- A nice dungeon, if a little bit dangerous. At this level you should pretty much stick to the beginning areas
High Keep- Keep killing those Pickclaws. I'd still stay on the first floor of the mines, but if you are daring you can move a little bit deeper.
Rathe Mountains- Keep on hunting. Stay away from Hill Giants just yet, but the Giant Skeletons and the Cyclops are fair game
Upper Guk- Keep killing those frogs! Its tradition!
Sol A- Keep laying the smack down on those little red goblin butts
Unrest- At this stage you should be moving inside the house itself and down into the basement.
Dalnir- A dungeon off of Warslik Woods, this place, like most low level Kunark dungeons, is somewhat underconned. Keep to the 1st floor until it starts going green. The second and third floors are much harder than you would think. It is not an unregular occurance to see a group of mid to high 40's dead down there. Still of all the low level Kunark dungeons, its the only one where its really safe to hunt at the appropriate level (excepting Kurn's Tower which is an iksar newbie dungeon)
Kaesora- You technically could start hunting here at these levels. Don't do it. No matter who tells you it will be fun, adventerous, etc. You will die. You will have a hard time getting your corpses. You will be trying to find a friendly necromancer. You have been warned.
Frontier Mountains- The Giant Fort may be a little bit difficult, but maybe not. Give it a shot.
Warslik Woods- Supposedly good hunting here...I never tried it
Marus Seru- Good place to hunt. The only aggro mobs here are lightcrawlers and Zelniaks. Beware, though, Zelniaks have a very very large aggro range. All the mob types here are social to their own type and will assist good place for experience with a solid group as a result.
Netherbian Lair- Still a good place to kill I hear
Ocean of Tears- Kill the Dwarfs on Sister Island. Often.

Levels 30-34
Lake of Ill Omen- This place will finally start to peter out around these levels. You are basically limited to the Sarnak Fortress and Deep in the Goblin mines
Frontier Mountains- Giant Fort.
Firiona Vie- Drovlargs and the Giant Fort are good hunting for a solid group. Remember that wolf form is KoS in this zone
Dalnir- The top floor will be greening out. The second floor is good hunting, just becareful in the tunnels
Lower Guk- Ah...Lower Guk. The Original Uber Zone. You can start killing the live side entrance and the undead side entrance at these levels. Towards 34ish you can move to the Bedroom on the dead side with some skill.
Sol B(Nagafen's Lair)- The Counterpart to LGuk in the original game, over in Lavastorm. You can start killing the kobolds at these levels, but be careful.
Split Paw- You can start moving a bit deeper in, but be leary of opening the double doors.
Butcher Block- Ahh..now you can start killing the famed Dorf Wimmin of Butcher Block. Its a rite of passage.
Rathe Mountains- Ah...time go kill Hill Giants. If you can find any. There is also a spawn of two trolls at a tower on six minute timers. Good experience there for small groups.
Kedge Keep- One of the most underutilized zones in the game. Hunting the entrance with a small group or a duo is excellent experience though. Well it was back in 1999 anyways. Just bring plenty of fishscales.
High Keep- Personally I never hunted High Keep at these levels, but there are some deep spawns which are still good hunting
Butcher Block- Did I mention you can start killing dwarf women?
Kaesora- You will still die horribly. Do not come here. Stay Away. There are no Scooby Snacks worth it
Nurga- Technically you can start hunting here at these levels. Its horribly underconned here, however, and not worth the effort.
Droga- See Nurga. Plus, this place has a tendancy to be AE killed by level 60's working on faction for Chardok
Iceclad- The cougars around the gnome igloos are great experience. Just do not kill Stormfeather if he spawns. Yes he's blue con. Yes he's an easy kill. Yes he's an 18 hour spawn for a high level quest that can't hope to complete for another 20 levels or so. Don't be a dingleberry.
Crystal Caverns- Orcs to kill, dwarfs to sell to and bank with, great place to hunt.
Butcher Block- You really should consider killing those dwarf women. We won't let you into DUCK unless you have

Levels 35-39
Butcher Block- Those dwarf women are just getting juicier.
Lower Guk- Both live and dead sides are excellent at these levels. Go camp a Exe Axe of nostalgia's sake
Sol B- When you get sick of LGuk, you kill in Sol B =D
Splitpaw- You can start venturing deeper into this den of scum and villainy, be very careful though, it is very easy to get too many gnolls and swiftly be over your head
Ogguk- That's right, Ogguk. Get a decent group and kill the bouncers inside the Ogre city. Just invis past the two newbie guards to zone in.
Rathe Mountains- More hill giant killing to be had here.
Steamfont- Watchman Halv
Iceclad- Cougars should still be fairly nice killing.
Tower of Frozen Shadows- Yay, another dungeon. This one is keyed, which can make CR a real chore since the keys to each floor are not soulbound. At this stage, stick to the first level or two.
Eastern Wastes- Ulthorks and anything else that cons blue except for Giants. The Giants just take too long to kill to be worth it. Watch out for the eler snow griffons.
Crystal Caverns- Orcs to kill, dwarfs to sell to and bank with, great place to hunt.
Great Divide- Tizmaks are good experience. So are the Drakkel Dire Wolves. Giants are probably a bit too touch to mess with w/o a solid group and even then take too long to kill to be worth the bother. The same with the Wurms.
Dawnshroud- You can actually level here for quite some time. It really is a nice zone for that.
Marus Seru- This place is going to start greening out about now. You can still hunt here though if you choose.
Timorous Deep- The ogres at the island between Oasis and Overthere
Butcher Block- Do I really need to say it?
Dalnir- Second floor should be getting easier and turning into a nice little XP item gamp. I wouldn't go down to the 3rd floor quite yet though
Nurga and Droga- I still wouldn't bother hunting these dungeons, there is a reason they are always empty.
Overthere- Still good hunting for this range.

Levels 40-44
Butcher Block- Getting sick of those dwarf women yet?
Lower Guk- Froooaaak! Now you can start camping the Frenzied and Ghoul Lord with a decent group
Sol B- Window Room. King/Priest. Stay away from the bugs n Bats still.
Kedge Keep- You can hunt here. Most people don't, there just so many other, easier, places to hunt.
Firiona Vie- Drovlargs, Arachnids, Giants.
Burning Wood- The easier skeletons and the lower hornets. Hornets are considered summoned creatures.
City of Mist- Good hunting here, pull to zone.
Eastern Wastes- More Ulthorks, animals, and Ry'Gor orcs.
Dreadlands- Good XP to be found here for a chain pulling group.
Rathe Mtns- Hill Giants
Oasis, South Ro, North Ro- Sand Giants. They are ornerier than Hill Giants. You can also hunt the Spectres in the Oasis for some nice experience and the occasional scythe.
Stonebrunt- I never hunted here much except to farm pelts at level 60, I hear it is nice experience for the 40's though
Jaggedpine- Its supposed to be good hunting starting about this level up through your low to mid 50's.
The Hole- If you are careful you can hunt the entrance.
Splitpaw- You can start venturing even deeper...just keep lesser succor memmed and hot to trot
Dalnir- I'd stick with 2nd floor unless you need specific items from the 3rd. Be very careful on the 3rd floor
Iceclad Ocean-The dire wolves in this zone are good quadding, just beware of adds.
Twilight Sea- You can quad the shadows/aliens for good effect.

Levels 45-50
Lower Guk- The frogs are still viable targets at this level, in fact by level 45 there isn't anything in the zone you couldn't kill with a solid group. You can kite in here, just gotta clear alot of room for you to run around. Don't think of more than one mob, then you just develop problems.
Sol B- You can start killing Bats and Bugs now
The Hole- You can kill down until the drop off, past that I suggest you wait another 5-10 levels or go in raid force.
Everfrost Peaks- Ice Giants. Get experience while getting Hill Giant style plat. Just be aware that the three spawns are virutally perma-killed thanks to the Surefall Glade teleport
Ocean of Tears- Sea Homers! Or Seafury Cylclops as the gnomes like to call them. Good killing for a group or solo depending on level. At the very least you and your necro buddy can pull them Ancient Cyclops island and fear kite them. You'll need to do that for the Corrupted Cyclops anyways. There are also Spectres in this zone which are good killing up through about 47.
Timorous Deep- Spirocs. Lots and lots of Spirocs. They are the Dwarf Women of levels 45-50. They have their very own Aviak Tree on the same island you'll find the boats from Oasis to Overthere. You probably will want to start out root/doting or single Kiting them...eventually you will quad kite them. There are tons of birds here, so there is almost always enough to go around. You can also try for the Raptors on Raptor island...along with every other druid and wizard in the game.
Nurga/Droga- With skill, a good group, and careful hunting you can hunt these two zones now. I'm not sure its worth it however.
Dreadlands- The perfect levels for this zone. If it moves, kill it. Except Gorenaire...
Trakanon's Teeth- Nice killing here and you can run the forager/hunter cycles to get Sebilis keys while getting experience.
Lake of Ill Omen- You didn't think you were actually done with this zone did you? Bahahaha. Bloodgill Goblins are one of the fastest ways to level 45-50. The only problem is everyone and their mother knows about them.
Karnor's Castle- You will have a difficult time finding a group at 45, most likely, but it can be done. I personally hate this zone. Its a constant trainfest, the experience isn't that great, and there are not many places a normal group of the appropriate level can hunt. Still, its one of the most popular dungeons in the game.
Burning Wood- Giants and Wasps and Sarnaks and Undead. For effeciency, stay away from the wurms and the undead gorrillas. For good soloing fun, kill the wurms. There is something about soloing a mob with over 6khp for the first time that makes you tingly inside. Don't get lured into the Wurmslayer quest by the ease with which you can kill the Glyten, or whatever his name is, you will never actually find Azdalin, or whatever his name is, up.
Skyfire- I wouldn't suggest trying to solo here yet, but paired up with a good necro, or hunting in a decent group, you can kill the easier mobs found around the Burning Woods zone area.
Chardok- With the revamp it is supposed to be a better place to hunt at these levels. Be aware, however, that ketting sarnak faction is difficult enough without becoming max KoS here and there is a very nice series of quests found here for those who are allied with the Sarnaks here.
Howling Stones/Charasis- If you can find a solid group with keys, this place is quite the exciting challenge at these levels. I wouldn't venture past the west and north wings though. East and South are way beyond your abilities at this level, most likely.
City of Mist- Still a good place to hunt for experience, you can move in past the gate area and the Stable is good experience for quite a few levels.
Eastern Wastes- The Ry'gor fort is still good hunting. Anything else that is blue con is also fair game.
Emerald Jungle- Find a blue con or four and go to town.
Wakening Land- There is some good hunting here. It can also be quite dangerous. The roaming holgresh and various mercenaries can making things difficult. Beware of Wuoshi who spawns right at the teleport ring. Skyshrine- The Courtyard and surrounding areas are good hunting for a group at these levels.
Kael Drakkel- The giants near the Eastern Wastes zone area are good experience and good coldain faction. The giants right by the Wakening Lands zone are also good.
Great Divide- Giants and Shardwurms.
Dawnshroud Peaks- You can start in on the Sambata and Rockhoppers now, though they might be tough.
Mons Letalis- You can hunt here, but be careful. Things tend to be a bit undercon and it is easy to get adds. I wouldn't necessarily suggest soloing here.
Twilight Sea- I hear this is a good place to hunt at these levels.
Grimling Forest- I would imagine this place could be hunted at this level, I've never really noticed anyone doing so while running through, so I assume it probably isn't the best place to hunt.
Scarlet Desert- This looks like a very fun place to hunt at these levels. I've never personally had the opportunity to try it however.
Jaggedpine Forest- A new zone, there are a plethora of mobs to kill starting about this level all the up through level 60.

Levels 51-54
Timorous Deep- Raptors are supposed to be rocking at these levels. If you can get the camp. Spirocs are also still good experience.
Cobalt Scar- Othmir. Wyverns. Stay away from the Drakes until you are closer to 54 than 51. Yvolcarn and Azureake will ruin your day...stay away.
Karnor's Castle- Still a viable hunting area, if you don't mind the trains.
Sebilis- The successor to Lower Guk. More frogs froaking. It also is a good cash zone, between the named drops, gems, and spells. You could theoretically level from 51-60 in this zone, though trying to get a group here as a level 51 druid will prove frustrating. The experience is good though.
Howling Stones/Charasis- Keep killing with a solid group, as you get higher in level you can move deeper or hunt the same places with a less optimal group.
Sol B- Bats n Bugs still, plus some single group forrays to Efreeti.
Skyfire- You can start soloing here at these levels. I'd suggest pulling to the Burning Woods zone delta.
City of Mist- Kill the goos in the stable area and if you have a group with a rogue in it, you can go into the temple itself.
Velketor's Laboratory- Dexter's Lab this isn't. Revamped to make it Velious's answer to Lguk and Sebilis, there is good experience here and decent drops once you get off the main floor. It will be hard to get a group here at these levels, but not impossible. On the other hand its hard to find a group here at anytime, the zone is usually packed.
Siren's Grotto- Found off of Cobalt Scar, this place is decent experience with a couple decent items. Of more import, however, is the frequency of Velious BP gem drops here. Unfortunately, at these levels you may have difficulty killing the Sirens which drop them.
Wakening Land- Geonids, Sentient Armor, Giants.
Burning Wood- Giants and Wurms and the occasional Sarnak.
Chardok- Very nice experience at this level. You can charm most of the entrance area dogs with Call of Karana.
Skyshrine- Skyshrine wasn't my favorite place to level for this range, but it is a viable option.
Kael Drakkel- Again, not my favorite place to level at this range, but yet viable.
Dawnshroud- You will need to hunt the Sambata/Rockhopper cave almost exclusively at this level.
Maiden's Eye- Good experience here with a solid group. It can be tough at level 51/52 I would imagine though.
Mons Letalis- I would suspect you could still hunt here, but I couldn't tell you for certain.
The Grey- This zone has no air. It has lots of traps. Many trains. Tons of high level quadders. Evil Rockfiends. Enter at your own risk.
Tenebrous Mountains- I think you can still hunt here, again, I can't tell you for certain since it didn't exist when I was this range.
Acrylia Caverns- A solid group could probably start hunting here around level 53-54 I would imagine.
Jaggedpine Forest- I hear tell that gnoll quadding in the caves is good XP at this level range.
Scarlet Desert- Quad aliens. Stay away from the Sun Reavers and Revenants.

Levels 55-60
Cazic-Thule- The revamped Cazic-Thule is probably the best experience in the game with some very nice drops. Accordingly it is a challenging zone. Despite it being an ourdoor zone, it is not entirely Druid friendly. Many of the mobs are all but immune to our Fire based spells and harmony has been disabled. There is great reward here, there is also significant risk. Probably the best designed zone Verant has put out yet.
The Grey- A nice place to solo or duo at these levels, though it might behoove you to wait until you are closer to 60 than 55.
Ssraeshza Temple- This is the most important zone in the Luclin. Not only a raid zone, but also a single group zone. You will need to hunt the first two levels and the basement for key parts to get to the Emperor. It is a difficult zone with good XP. You will need a solid group and I wouldn't suggest coming here until level 58+.
Fungus Grove- This zone is mostly for Melee to solo or duo in. Many mobs are immune to snare/root. It is indoors. I only come here to hunt lucid shards or if I'm duoing with a melee. The caller cycles are decent XP and pretty fun, however.
Maiden's Eye- Anything and everything in zone can and must die. Just watch out for trains.
Ahkeva Ruins- Fairly easy. Nice XP. Start at the zone in and move deeper as you grow more confident. Just don't move down the north wing without a couple groups. They grey aliens can't be snared or rooted and some can't be mes'd.
The Deep- You can hunt with a single group pretty much up to the ancient mushrooms past the invis bridge. Oh, yes. The big bridge you see in the zone? Its false. Don't go there.
Sanctus Seru- You can kill most anything in the zone with a single group. I wouldn't go inside the various consilium until your group is mostly level 60's however. High hp mobs. Generally decent soloing for root dot at entrance or up in the towers around the edge of the zone. Quadding is a possibility in the towers but one should be an experienced kiter and have good gear.
Katta Castellum- The various guards are good hunting, expecially solo in your upper 50's.
Plane of Fear- At level 60 a single group can hunt experience for the thrill here. A novelty, to say the least.
Plane of Hate- See Plane of Fear.
Plane of Mischief- Fairly easy place to hunt compared to the other plains. If you can get there. Right now the only way into PoM is through NToV. Fairly easy to solo in, save for the fact mobs have massive hp now (used to be exp heaven). Root dot is your primary method of killing, but some things outside the castle can be quadded if you are good and have great gear. Primary focus will probably be on rats in the maze and mobs outside the castle. In either of these cases a good bind point inside the castle should allow you to gate and camp before the mobs path too you.
Skyshrine- You can start hunting further in and eventually head fairly far up with a single good group. This place is very MR though, so bring an enchanter or a tash stick.
Kael Drakkel- Everything can and must die here at these levels. Eventually, at level 60, you could kill everything short of the Statue/Idol/AoW or Tormax with a single group.
Icewell Keep- Commonly called Thurg B. Usually killed to gain Kromriff faction.
Great Divide- Kromriff Elites are blue cons all the way up through 60.
Velketor's Laboratory- Good all the way up to and through level 60.
Wakening Land- Once the Sentient Armors start greening out, you can still hunt Geonids.
Siren's Grotto- This is the optimal range for hunting in Siren's Grotto.
Western Wastes- Be careful hunting here if you are KoS to Cov. You will get many adds.
Dragon Necropolis- Good experience here. Chetari will hate you regardless, so you might as well kill them and leave the Paebala alone, unless you want to kill the Paebala.
Sebilis- Everything in this zone excepting Trakanon can be killed by a single group or less by the time you reach level 60.
Howling Stones/Charasis- The entire zone is your playpen by the time you reach 60. Drusilla is a wuss. Bring a monk for her room, however.
Chardok- Becareful as you go deep in this zone. Even a well equipped group of level 60's can have problems around Koro or the Herbalist and deeper.
Karnor's Castle- This zone becomes easy at level 60. Kill with impunity.
Skyfire- You can start quadding blue cons here if you get bored.
Sol B- Go kill the Fire Giants or the Efreeti area with a single group or less.
Permafrost- The pits beneath Vox's Lair are good.
