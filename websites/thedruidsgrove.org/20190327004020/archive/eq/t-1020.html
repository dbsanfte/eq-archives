Ok, maybe we can help out some of the people struggling in LDoN. But it'll take a willingness to try things that are a little different. Experiment and be ready to fail, but the greater reward is, when you learn something new you can increase your wins overall.

Let's keep this geared towards the folks who are struggling. Those of you multi-tanking 20 mobs and AE'ing can start your own guide! :p

<strong><span style="color:red;font-family:courier;font-size:x-large;">Pulling in LDoN</span></strong>

<em>Time to Engage Next Mob (TENM)</em> is probably the most important element in winning. Lets look at the math. Say you get a slaughter 60. You have 90 minutes to kill 60 beasties. If you picked a collection quest, you might actually need to kill more than 60, just depends on random factors. Ok, you must kill a mob ever 90 seconds. Sounds, easy! But in practice, if you aren't killing that mob in under 1 minute, you're probably going to lose. Even then, I've had groups that could kill fairly fast, but they <em>moved</em> onto the next mob so slowly that they lost.

If it takes you 1 minute to kill, 30 seconds TENM, then if you have one bobble, one small problem, one untimely death, you will lose the dungeon.

So, what is the answer? More DPS? Ok, that will help get that kill time down, but if your TENM is long, it might not be enough. The answer is to kill in less than one minute and make sure TENM is very, very short. Generally that means your pulling strategy needs to be sound and agressive and it means you can't have med breaks, cyber breaks, long bathroom breaks and so on.

<strong>Who Pulls?</strong>

The answer is anyone with the skill and guts! But there are some factors to determine who good choices are.

Tanks are probably the worst choice in most cases, unless you have 2 tanks in a group. They will make very large pulls which a pickup group just can't handle well (typically).

Bard: A fabulous choice. They can lull mobs and lull apparently lasts for 5+ minutes. Someone described their tactic as: Send in bard to lull everything, group runs into the room and fights the mobs where they stand, one at a time.

Enchanter: Another amazing choice. Enchanter method is usually: Pacify room, pull one with tash, run back to group. Mez it, root it (maybe). Go pull again while the group kills.

Cleric: But clerics with Pacification can pull incredibly well. Problem is they get hammered when the mob comes. However, if you pull with Mark of K(arn/ings) it has SO little aggro that anything can taunt it off you. I had a cleric pulling tonight, we had constant chain pulls, it was great. Rarely more than one in camp. And clerics have 2-3 get-out-of-jail cards in their invulnerability abilities in case a pull goes badly.

Ranger: Using a combination of sneak pulls and root pulls you can lower the size of the incoming. A really good ranger will follow up by snaring and root parking adds. Truly, a ranger can replace an enchanter if they're good. They should be going to pull the next before the mob in camp dies.

Wizards: Wizards make good pullers too. They're able to root park with snare, root and such. With their self-rune, they're fairly sturdy.

Paladins: Root pulls, but you have to be cautious. Don't just root the mob in the middle of the room. There might be ones on either side of the door you can't see. Try camera angles or use a rogue to tell you the layout or get you a target. You'll get big pulls, but with root, you can stagger them out some. Also, if you're able you can continue to root mobs and move out of the way. Also, don't necessarily pull with stun. The mob just sits there for 10 seconds drooling and you're losing time. Another paladin mentioned that stuns have a large "aggro splash" which might make for larger pulls than just running in and tapping something with your lance.

Druids: They can pull too, about like Wizards do. But they can be more fragile without the self-rune. Druids, like any class with snare and root, can make awesome crowd controllers.

Monks: If a monk is skillful at pulling, can minimize the size of pulls, won't obsess over single pulls, and will chain pull, then a monk is a great choice. Sometimes monks fall into the trap of feeling they have to split every pull and this can take an unnecessarily long time. You also need a fairly attentive tank to tag things off the monk. Here's a very good guide to LDoN written by a monk: pub147.ezboard.com/fmonkl...1214.topic (http://pub147.ezboard.com/fmonklybusiness43508frm2.showMessage?topicID=1214. topic) definitely worthwhile reading.

Duo Pullers: This can be slowish, so don't rely on it unless you absolutely have to. Enchanter or cleric pacifies and pulls. Someone else stands ready to root the mob before it kills the puller. Need someone with an attention span longer than a gnat's.

No One: Some dungeons it's just best to charge a corner closest to the doorway. Send in the tanks first, hug the walls, try not to aggro other mobs in the room.

No One: If you have enough tanks in the group, off tank! Count on your rangers, beastlords and others to do this. Beastlords make incredible off-tanks, because they can slow too and slow=massive aggro.

Everyone: Try to work it out so the tank isn't pulling and use chain pulls. There should almost always be a mob being <em>processed</em> to death.

<strong><span style="color:red;font-family:courier;font-size:x-large;">Mana Management</span></strong>

I've played 5 classes post 60 in EQ, so I'm not totally uninformed.

<strong>Got Mana?</strong>

Use it! If you're sitting close to FM and not contributing damage then you're not contributing! Hoarding mana for a disaster might lead to a disasterous loss.

<strong>Clerics</strong>... omg. I am a total backseat driver since a cleric is the one class I have played for all 4 of my years in EQ. I have opinions. :p First off, if you're without AA's, especially SCM and you're level 65, I mock you. Go get some AA's. Make SCM your first archetype choice. Saving mana is so important. After that, I recommend Divine Arbitration, that is such a butt-saver on large pulls and it gets you 0 aggro. Get ALL your spells, at least the ones sold on the vendors and LEARN how to use them. Look them up on <a href="http://lucy.fnord.net/" target="top">Lucy</a>. Groups need you, they depend on you. Don't suck. Go read <a href="http://gameglow.eqclerics.com" target="top">EQClerics</a> and learn how to be a good cleric.

Speaking of saving mana, use Mark of Kings/Karn! 75 mana to prevent what can add up to a thousands of hit points of damage or more taken by people duel weilding from damage shields. I had a cleric using a 1100 mana spell to heal up damage shield damage rather than spend 75 mana to prevent the damage in the first place. Guess what, she was oom constantly... no friggin' duh!

By all means, use Yaulp and hammer if you can spare the mana. Load up nukes. I'm pretty convinced that my groups where the cleric is attentive, knows WTF they're doing and are meleeing we do a hell of a lot better. I think cleric DPS on undead meleeing is pretty significant, but I haven't parsed it.

If you can't sit because of aggro, use Yaulp to regain mana.

<strong>Enchanters</strong> Another class I see going oom all the time in LDoN. It might be more mana effecient for you to do Pacify pulls and use your 50% slow (tepid deeds), keep your mana-sucking spells ready to drain casters. 50% slow is enough for any tank that isn't just horrible. Tepid deeds casts much faster and uses only 100 mana. If someone in your group can slow, bard, shammy, necro, beastlord, ask them to do it!

Come on don't be a wuss! Chain pull for your group! It might be easier on the mana than trying to mez lots of adds. Also, a lot of enchanters root their mezzed mobs so they don't bounce onto them when mez is broken.

Other enchanters recommend using Apathy if you must mez in the normal dungeons. It should work on all the mezzable mobs (excluding bosses). Sleep is more expensive.

Also, keep Guard of Druzzil or GRM up. I see people getting rooted, mezzed, and charmed that should have resisted it if you'd been keeping resist magic up. Shame on you!

<strong>Debuffs</strong> Consider skipping debuffs. Short fights really might not warrant the mana expenditure versus the occassional resisted nuke and insane aggro of some debuffs. If your group is killing slowly, might make sense <em>if</em> you can do it and manage your aggro. If you're grouped with a shammy, just ask them to use their unresistable debuff.

<span style="color:red;font-family:courier;font-size:x-large;">Damage</span>

Nuke, don't dot! In average groups (yes, I've timed the kills) it takes about 45 seconds to kill a mob. In good groups, it takes 30 seconds. In cooking groups it takes 15-20 seconds. In slow groups, it takes 1 minute or more. Most dots, especially the ones you cast from gear, just don't work fast enough to warrant casting. Only dot if you're lowish on mana or unless you have REALLY fast acting dots. Otherwise the mob is dead and you've done only a couple hundred damage.


<strong>No Ping-Pong</strong>
If you are nuking don't cause ping pong. If you want to ride the aggro envelope, go stand right next to the tank, shoulder to shoulder. You can be causing your group a lot of DPS loss if you make the mob bounce to you. While your meleers go chasing it all over the room. Your rogue is missing backstabs when you cause the mob to turn or run off, your meleer damage is reduced to 0 for N seconds. And besides, it makes me hate you. Try rains again.

Use root situationally. Most meleers aren't aware that you have rooted a mob (I am, because I use EQWatcher to alert me to the root message) if you root a mob it'll turn on the closest person causing the wrong person to tank, and possible loss of DPS from roguely sorts.

<span style="color:red;font-family:courier;font-size:x-large;">Misc</span>

<strong>Warriors</strong>, get some of those +aggro procs asap. Even if they're the cheapie ones, you can upgrade later. Don't taunt 3 times before breaking mez, that's just too slow. You're adding a good 18 seconds to the kill time for each mob.

For Pete's sake, use defensive or evasive when you have an enormous pull. Don't wait until you're at 5% hit points. You might survive, but the cleric will die trying to keep you alive.

<strong>Meleers</strong> Watch your positioning! Don't push things up walls where no one can touch them and the rogues can't get to their back. Don't push things into corners. If you're not the MT and you got aggro, get rid of it! Jolt, FD, evade, turn off auto-attack, whatever, just do it. You're going to screw up DPS and cause the healers to sink a ton of mana into you. Tanks, use sit aggro to move mobs if necessary.

<strong>Pet Classes</strong> Be ready to back off your pets when the positioning is so screwed up no one can hit the mob. There's nothing more annoying than everyone backing off the mob, except the pet... waiting for the mob to move.


<strong>Casters</strong> Bind outside the dungeon for faster recovery. Especially if you're in a marginal group and suspect things might go badly. (I really need to take my own advise :p ) At the very very worst, bind at the camp! If you have to run back from PoK, you suck.

<strong>Loot and Scoot</strong> Don't worry about resses until the adventure is over. Leave something on the corpse.

<strong>Buffing</strong> Do the buffing before you accept the mission, unless you have a really short duration buff. No reason not to do Virtue and most of the buffs early and be ready to zone in after accepting the mission. It's stupid to have to med up outside the dungeon for 5 minutes because you didn't cast a 2-hour long buff 10 minutes earlier.

Be in range for group buffs. Sheesh... I can't tell you how many times I've logged on my shammy said, in group, "Ok, come close, time for shammy buffs", cast the buffs and some doofus was chasing after a skunk or something.

<strong>Do the Math</strong>
If you have to kill 60 mobs to win you need to kill a mob every 1.5 minutes. Use alt-v to see how you're doing and adjust your technique if you're too slow. Try to finish with a good 20 minutes left over, so if you have a wipeout or other problem you have wiggle room.

<strong>Give Up</strong>
If you are on a slaughter 60 mission and during the first 20 minutes you haven't killed 15 mobs then it might be best to concede defeat and reform with a different group and not waste so much time on a loss.

<strong>Stick to Slaughter X</strong>

Unless you have people you trust to work together well, I would only do slaughter missions. There are too many things that can go wrong with the other missions and some of the simpletons that join pickup groups will do incredibly stupid things that will make you fail. They will anyway, but at least you're reducing the number of possible problems to a smaller set.

<strong>Beware of Assassinate if...</strong>
...you don't have anyone to pacify or any really solid crowd control. Here's why. In some of the missions the assassinate target can be surrounded by 4 or so mobs that are linked. In fact, tonight in a group that was easily winning the dungeon, we lost the entire adventure because we couldn't easily kill the adds. 4 adds and the boss was too much. In fact, we did manage to single out the boss, but it ended up Gating back at the end. We just didn't have the juice to be able to unload so it wouldn't gate. After that, we tried twice more to pull it, but the puller couldn't get it to work again and the group finally gave up. That particular adventure had an assassinate target of The FleshSomething, it was a big nasty enchanter spider. Other assassinate adventures can be quite simple.

<strong>Leave the Boxes and Barrels and People Lying on the Floor Alone!</strong>

You can come back and play with them. Actually though, it doesn't hurt for the MT to go beat up a person/thing lying on the floor IF they are full hit points. It's the low hit point ones that turn into something ugly usually. After the adventure is completed, you can play with the boxes and chests.

<strong>Maps</strong>

Well, some people don't like 'em, but if you want Teh Win, you'd probably better use 'em. Going the wrong way to reach a boss can pretty much guarantee you a loss with a less than uber group.

<strong>Eyeballs</strong>

You can use those ever so handy bloodshot eyeballs to figure out how to break the next room, acquire a target for someone to pacify, and to go scout for the boss.

<strong>Don't Follow The Rogue!</strong>

I've had this happen. I'm scouting ahead for traps or the boss and someone follows me into a room full of mobs! Doh!

<strong>Someone Snare!</strong>
Just like in good old Kunark dungeons, the mobs can SoW themselves inside, being exempt from the rules that govern us. Having a SoW mob run into a room of mobs is disasterous! Make sure someone is snaring! And that someone needs to pay attention to their resist messages. I make my resist messages a bright red so I don't miss them. I also use <a href="http://www.eqwatcher.com" target="top">EQWatcher</a> so when I do get a resist I hear a voice saying "resisted". That verbal prompt is oh, so useful!

An old trick...

A Mage/Druid or Mage/Wizard combo can pull <em>extremely</em> effectively if the Mage has tons of plat, and doesn't mind spending some extra mana.

1) Druid/Wizard: Snare 1 mob, start running for the group, but stay some distance away - don't let anyone aggro.
2) Druid/Wizard: Egress/Abscond (or whatever it is)
3) Mage: CoH the Druid/Wizard

When the Druid/Wiz has been sucked back to the group, you've got a nicely snared mob all alone, as the other have wandered back.

This method can often split very tough-to-split groupings quite easily. If you pick your targets (ie: middle guys) then you can often pull the regular way the next round, cuz they're far enough away from each other.

If you're close to the entrance you can do away with the CoH, just run back. Meanwhile a tank/hybrid can snatch that snared mob.

From my experience:

I'm a paladin so the tanking section is from that perspective.

<strong>Mission selection</strong>
If you are going with people you are unsure of, hold out for a slaughter mission to see how they do.

If you haven't got a slower of any kind, don't do an assassination.

I have had fairly good luck with collections of 28 or more. (100% success on 28+ collect.)

If you know who your with and you usually do well, go ahead and take a rescue.


<strong>Group makeup</strong>

I like to try to work with what I get. As you can imagine, it doesn't always work.

You need damage output, healing, someone to pull. Level 65 paladins can't pacify anything in the dungeons they will be going to. Use root (preferably greater immobilize or if your lucky or rich the tunare one).

<strong>General strategy</strong>
Use all your tricks. I haven't had to swap my normal spell around as much as I do in LDON since I was level 30 when Cazic Thule was for level 30s. I use remove curse, crusader's touch, pious might and my self haste quite often.

A new trick I started last night was to coordinate with the cleric (if we have one) on heals. I can dump a heal over time on a target and the cleric can land a direct heal. The result is more healing and no one wasting mana which is what usually happens when I try to help out on healing. I admit this was far easier since the player of the cleric was physically in the same room and I can just talk to her.

For the love of all that is holy, snare those mobs. In Takish there are a number of druid mobs and they sow (I guess they can cast it indoors where we can't) their friends. Those things take off like wildfire at 10% and if I fizzle my stuns twice in a row and it gets around a corner, your going to have 10 kinds of hell coming back toward you. Quite often I can stun the runner and we can kill it, but it only takes one mess up to put a dampner on an otherwise good run. If you play with me, you might get used to not seeing a mob run because I have a stun key and I'm on the ball. If you get a paladin that doesn't stun next time or just isn't as observant, your going to see what I mean about 10 kinds of hell.

For the tank, have AC and HP. I ride about 5k-6k hp. If we are lucky and have both a shaman and a cleric, I can get just above 6khp.

Help the slower out. Sometimes there are three mobs in camp or the current mob is resisting. Stunning the target is essentially like a very short duration slow. I have one button I press that cycles my stuns. If any of my stun gems are lit I can mash the button and one of them will go off. This helps in many situations. It stops casting (CH mobs are a real pain), it stops gating (another source of 10 hells), it stops runners, it grabs aggro, it slows the rate at which mobs hit.

Do whatever you can to contribute damage. If you have VOQ and Aura on and even with helping out with stuns and heals still riding at 100% mana much of the time, use that flame of light. Don't forget to put on any damage shields you can. I use the coldain ring 9. Buy potions, ask the mage or druid or ranger for a ds. Every little bit helps.

Don't hit the trees or people lying on the floor. Sure you can get a benefit but if you get a bad one it may make the difference between winning and losing.

Don't touch the interactive objects. When your done, you can but they still aren't worth it. Have DA, crusader's touch and remove curse memmed. Hit DA when the jack-ass of the group wants to fiddle with the chest and hopefully they will be done with their fun before it wears off. Then you can cure/remove and heal everyone.

OH, and don't overlook your healing wave. It will help when you get to that room of 5 mobs and one resists a lull or they otherwise all come running. With a KEI and Aura, you can cast quite a few of them.

/disc holy when you get to the boss in an assassination or at some point during the other missions. For a short time, you'll do a little more damage and every bit counts.

We had a group where the cleric was swinging the hammer and using the hammer pet. The shadowknight popped out his skeleton pet, the druid even used his little bear pet. Everything counts.

Avoid the Sunken Library on normal unless you know you can take 902 point hits. Right now its bugged in that the first earthen defender is hitting for 902 points on a normal difficulty.

Alright you don't have to avoid it, but know its coming and prepare.

<blockquote><strong><em>Quote:</em></strong><hr>Also, don't necessarily pull with stun. The mob just sits there for 10 seconds drooling and you're losing time.[/quote]

Well, a good paladin pulls with Cease (1 second duration) or Divine Stun (low aggro, 1 second duration). Follow up with a longer stun when the mob reaches camp to start establishing aggro.

On the other hand, if you're pulling 2 mobs at once (I try to get 2 and only 2 on each pull), stun one with a longer duration stun (FoA) and then switch targets and hit the other with Cease/Divine Stun. Run back to camp and hit the one following you with FoA. This helps to get aggro on both before they are in camp.

As mentioned, Pacify can't be used in 65th level dungeons, though it does work on some mobs in 60th level dungeons.

Our regular experience group varies from 62nd to 65th level and from about 20 AA's to 180 AA's. We always get 65th level dungeons and they are a challenge to complete. However, I'm starting to preface that with the fact that we still take assassinate and rescue missions and have NEVER gotten an exterminate mission; I think that has something to do with why we perceive successes to be harder to come by than many other people. We've had only 1 outright loss where we couldn't even finish during the extra 30 minutes but have had 4 losses where we finished within 5 minutes of the timer going off (one was a collect mission where we got our last item about 2 minutes after the timer, one was a rescue mission where we needed, oh, 45 more seconds to get the stupid dwarf back to the zone).

A couple of things we've been doing:

Your guide is pretty good. I’d like to make some comments and recommendations.

I am a 65 cleric, and I pull for about 2/3 of the groups I go with. If I am pulling, then my low DPS will not be a significant handicap to the party. Someone else, who would otherwise be pulling, can continue fighting, which is beneficial to the party. By single pulling most of the time, I can conserve CC mana, and even cut down on the mana I would otherwise spend on healing.

Pacification does eat a lot of mana, but I find I can generally pull and heal the group fairly well. The risky part is when a roamer is parked in a doorway, or you have to step into a room to target the mobs close to the door. Then, you sometimes get three pulls.

Unless I am stunned, three pulls are not a problem, DB and DA provide ample escape mechanism for me to bring the mobs to the group and dump agro. There are rare cases where I am stunned or my DB and DA are already used and I cannot clear agro. Then I rely on Divine Arbitration to save me. I can reliably tank one mob and can somewhat tank two for sufficient time for them to be mezzed, rooted, or slowed.

An important thing to know is how well your group can handle multiple pulls. If your party can rip through three mobs at a time, there is no real reason to pacify every mob in the room. Even if the group can only handle one at a time, generally 2-3 pacifies will break the room up so that every mob can be single pulled.

If I am low on mana, I sometimes direct pulls instead and med. “Pull right, then left, then middle when ready.” ---or--- “Pull at will.”

Some other suggestions:

Print off the maps. Nothing is more frustrating as hearing the mob is NW of you not knowing whether to take the east or the south exit from the room in order to get to him. With maps, you generally have a fair idea from the very beginning where the boss mob may spawn. You can start clearing towards him from the start.

Mission difficulty going from least to most is slaughter, assassinate, escort, collect. I don’t mind doing any of the first three, and in fact like variety in my missions. I resent losing a mission when you have killed 95% of the mobs in the dungeon, so I avoid collect. If your group has decent DPS and maps, then escort is no more difficult than assassinate. The advantage on assassinate is that if you run very low on time, you can pacify past some mobs without killing them.

If you have plenty of time, clear the mobs on the way to your objective. If you wipe, it is far safer not having mobs in the way.

Allow time for a wipe on the boss mob when doing assassinate missions. We had a group that started pulling his room with over 30 minutes to spare. We wiped twice and won the adventure with under one minute left.
Trackers are nice. Some of the loot off named is nice, even for reasonably equipped players (non planar). Trackers can tell you when a named is up. Maps can be used to locate generally where the mob is.

Don’t forget your mage when trying to find your target. They can send out an eye to locate them. Consider purchasing a few stalking probes if you are having difficulties location the boss mobs.

Sometimes rogues can scout, but traps add a risk factor for this type of scouting.

Some people consider chests to be a waste of time. I don’t. But they are definitely something to deal with after you have won the mission. Drops I have seen from chests include: 2 dex augments, 1 wis augment, 1 hp augment, 1 str augment, blue diamonds, black sapphires, and trinkets… lots of trinkets.

Get your trap spells when you can. You can always augury a chest with no risk of setting off the trap. It will tell you if it is in your domain to open. If it is, you can open it with no risk. If not, then someone else in your party may be able to open it with little or no risk. If no one can open it, you can decide whether to open it or not. Prior to doing that, ready your spells for recovery, 450 damage dots hurt a lot.

Whether it's worth casting Hand of Ro depends on two factors:

1. How many times you nuke each mob.
2. How much of your damage is resisted.

Let's take 3 cases: nuke each 3 times, nuke each twice, nuke each once.

<strong>Nuke 3 times</strong>
3 SF + 1 HoR = 1109 mana for 5200 damage or 4.69 damage/mana
3 SF = 953 mana for 5200 or 5.46 damage/mana

That means that as long as mobs tend to resist 14% or more of your damage without hand of ro and zero percent when you do cast hand of ro, then it's worth casting the debuff.

<strong>Nuke 2 times</strong>
2 SF + HoR = 791 mana for 2800 damage or 3.54 damage/mana
2 SF = 635 mana for 2800 damage or 4.41 damage/mana

If the mob resists more than 20% of your damage without HoR and never resists when you do cast it, then it's worth casting Hand of Ro.

<strong>Nuke 1 time</strong>
1 SF + 1 HoR = 474 mana for 1400 damage or 2.95 damage/mana
1 SF = 318 mana for 1400 damage or 4.4 damage/mana

If the mob resists more than 33% of your damage without debuffing and never resists when you do debuff, then it's worth it to cast Hand of Ro.

Now, I don't have any parse data to determine, on average, how often mobs in LDoN resist or how much they do resist (full or partial) when they do.

I do know that spending 800+ mana per fight is hard for me to maintain, even if I'm not healing. If you assume even 1 minute per fight, you're spending 80 mana per tick to maintain that rate (if it's less than one minute "per fight," then it is more mana per tick being spent).

Anyway, it should be clear that the more nukes you can sustain casting (both because the fights are long enough and you can maintain the mana needed to do it), the more beneficial Hand of Ro becomes. In my groups even with relatively low DPS, I tend to cast 2 Summer's Flame per mob and sometimes less if I need to heal as well. Just based on my subjective experience, I'm not losing 20% of my damage to resists without casting Hand of Ro - I'm losing MAYBE 10%, but I'd have to parse it (which I'll try to do).
