After PL'ing a lot, I've found what I believe are the absolute best spots. I did my PL'ing with a poorly equipped 54 druid with no AA's, but usually had KEI on. Also, the characters I pl'ed were also HEAVLY twinked melee's (IE, 400-500k in gear). Your mileage may vary.

*Also, keep in mind if you do it this way you should end up with max or close to max skills by level 20 - 25ish, and from there on out you should be able to max them every level. It may not seem that important, but the first time I did this was with a bard, and he hit a slump at level 35 when his weapons and defensive skills were like 90 - 100 when they should have been 170+.

1 - 4:
Make sure you start off with 5 points in whatever weapon skills you are doing. Any newbie zone will work, but Gfay is easily accesible with the POK book and has an abundance of mobs. I would recommend doing levels 1 - 4 without a DS, as you will get important skill ups. It may take you an extra 15 - 30 minutes than if you would have gone straight to Unrest w/ thorns, but I feel it's worth it 10 fold. Also, once you hit level 4, spend all the practice points you have availible to you in your weapon skill if you haven't maxed it.

4 - 13: Unrest. DS, Share wolf form, Str, Hp buff, Regen. You only need to hit each mob once for experience, so as soon as you've done a single point of non-DS damage, go find your next target. Your old mob(s) will catchup with you at some point and kill themselves. Please note that in wolf form you aren't KOS, which is very handy for the druid doing the PL'ing.

Once you hit level 13, I recommend doing this: Grab as many trash mobs as you can in the yard, and pull them into a corner without a DS on to raise your skills. I did defensive skill points 10 - 75ish here in about 20 minutes, and it was worth it. Note that when I say as many mobs as you can, that 15 is a minimum and 25+ is preferred.

13 - 24: Paludal. It's heaven. Same style PL'ing as Unrest, and hopefully by level 24 you're all caught up on skills and still making decent leveling time. I'd expect your total played at this point if all you've done is level and work skills to be 2 - 5 hours. If this is your first time PL'ing, expect a learning curve as you learn to effectively 2box/PL.

24 - 37: High Hold Keep. But don't waste your time on the goblins, go right for the gaurds. This is the best experience spot I've ever seen, for any level. It's ridiculous. There are gaurds all along the first, 2nd, and 3rd floors. You'll learn the lay out of them over time, and get it down to a cycle.

At 37 things open up. I always liked City of Mist because the undead don't run, but LGuk is a lot of funny and decent experience too. You can ride CoM to 45ish, and Guk all the way till 50+.

By level 45 you should know about where you wanna head too, and a lot of your choices will be dictated by the class of character you're PL'ing, their gear, and the level/gear/AA of the druid that's doing the PL'ing.

Hope this added something that hasn't already been said.

Here's an easy way to figure it out (and make organizing your spellbook obsolete):

There's a new feature for memming spells where you right-click on an empty spell gem, and it brings up a menu of the spells you know, organized by type. The sub-menus (where the actual spells are) also show the level at which the spell is acquired.

Level 49 and below spells are castable on all players, regardless of level. Once over 50, the spells have a level limit, roughly equal to 2/3s of the spell's level. So if you're PLing a lowbie, you can choose the spells that are 49 and below. For example, for "skin" buffs, that would be Skin like Diamond.

Hi,

There seems to be a lot of debate over how to PL casters efficiently.

After 20 hours played, my enchanter dinged level 40. At least 2 hours were spent in the bazaar gearing her up, and about 3-4 hours were spent leveling without the help of my druid (1-5) and practicing spell skills. So I made level 40 in roughly 14 hours, in my estimate and I think I could have done it faster if I had followed the tactics that I used from 20-30.

My main is a 65 druid, she is very well-equipped with plenty of well-spent AAs (well, in my opinion!). I just say this because the technique I use may not be viable for a lower level/naked or poorly equipped druid.

1-5: I did in Nektulos without any help. I imagine if you bring your druid to the newbie zone and buff with SLN, thorns and regen you could do this really quickly. The biggest drawback is the fact that thorns will KS your PLee and that damage takes out newbie mobs very quickly. But as a caster if you chain pull with a low level DD you would be fine.

5-20: Paldual Caverns (sp?) : Yes, it's crowded -- but you won't be hunting in the bandit camps and at least on Druzzil that's where most people seem to be hanging out. Buff the PLee AND the PLee's pet if they have one and fight your way in. Pull monsters with a spell or make sure you hit them for a pt of damage in melee. Grab *everything* but make sure you fight in deeper to get yellows. Since I was an enchanter I'd buff the pet, pull a monster and when the pet was engaged I'd already be moving on to pull/tank some more on my own.

The best hunting in here (IMO) are the phlarg/potog fiends. From 9/10 to 13/14 the phlarg fiends in the huge cavern with the lake are amazing. You can work on 2-4 at a time with a pet out. They're also fairly easy to train onto a pet.

14-20: I ran out onto the narrow walkways that surround the lake that the bandit camps are off of. There was nobody else up here, and there are two full caverns of Potog Fiends plus the numerous spawns on the walkways themselves. The way I did this was, once again, letting the pet and the enchanter tank. HOWEVER, the next time I try PLing a caster it may work better to just stand the caster in a safe corner and round up 3-4 Potogs at a time with the druid. Then, the caster would just have to fire off a low dmg DD on each one and sit back and enjoy the EXP. Your druid will have better thorns and be able to kill twice as fast as the lowbie. Once you hit 20, head to Unrest.

20-30 Unrest Basement: Unrest is deserted on my server. I buffed up my druid and my enchanter and set up shop in the basement. The corner right before the stairs at the end of the hall leading to the basement is nearly 100% safe. In 10 levels there I got maybe 3 roamers, and a fully buffed caster can tank a single with no problems. Leave the caster in the corner and go round up some mobs with the druid. If they won't jump you (they all jumped me at 65 so this shouldn't be a problem) just sit next to them and they will. The optimal amount of mobs on the druid at any given time is 3-4. My druid's health never dropped below 90%.

Just have the caster damage them (until 24 some may be too red so you have to step in and smack them) and enjoy the exp!

I left Unrest at 30, but I imagine you could go to 32-34 if you really wanted to. Last night I scouted out Kaesora, and it seems like a reasonable place to go from 30-40. Your only limiting factor is the fact that even a very well equipped druid is going to reach a point where they can't tank the mobs (they start hitting too hard). I believe the tactic that I described could be used to take a caster to 45, MAYBE 50 if you could find a steady supply of lower level mobs (low blues to the PLee).

30-40: Kaesora! I went all the way down to the church/Xalgoz's area. There are plenty of pulls and the experience was great. At this point the druid is having to toss heals on herself, which is disappointing but not completely unexpected. I walked out with level 40 under my belt in essentially three long evenings of playing plus the component I needed from Xalgoz for a key to Howling Stones. Xalgoz himself is a little high to be an effencient PLing mob, so I usually just root rotted him with the druid. Chances are your PLee won't be able to resist his spells, so be careful with him! The healers in Kaesora are also annoying but not enough to detract from the overall EXP.

Enjoy! From 40-50 I'm not sure how realistic it is to have the druid tank. The saving grace is that since I play an enchanter I can toss a clarity on her and MAYBE if she has enough mana to keep healing herself it will prove to be efficient but I just don't think so at this point. I think my tactics from here on out are going to be buff up my boyfriend's (Necro) pet and have the druid just babysit him and the enchanter. Probably not going to update this until we hit 50, and only then if we actually "powerlevel" to get there and I find a good place to do so. Next stop, City of Mist!

40-46: The EXP and ability to effectively PL has REALLY slowed down here. I stuck it out in Kaesora simply because with the zone exp modifier and the ease with which the druid can tank the mobs, it was just as quick as anything else out there. My only other suggestion would be to charm kill, but I still think Kaesora is faster.

Post 46: I'm now grouping the enchanter with the druid and charm killing in the planes. I'm guessing this is how I'm going to PL the rest of my levels. I hope this guide to the ever-tedious caster PLing helped a few of you out. If you start on a Friday night you can get a caster to 46 over the weekend. My only "hangups" were the occasional raid with my guild (as my druid) and the fact that I'm PLing TWO casters (my boyfriend's necro and my enchanter) and we also managed to run across the Ancient Cyclops, and by the time my enchanter had JBoots and I ported to SRo, it was up again. Lol. Yay! :) If anyone has any suggestions or questions, please let me know! :)

Edit: One thing I neglected to mention -- root the werebats and tentacle/dark terrors. Otherwise they will occasionally make a run for it. But most of the monsters (undead) will just stand there and kill themselves like the good fodder that they are! Happy hunting!

You can tell if you can group for xp with a lower character by using the 3x2 rule. Multiply the lower level character by 3 and the higher char by 2 and if the lower char number is equal or higher they will get xp.

For example:
lvl 20 Shaman (20x3=60)
lvl 30 Druid (30x2=60)
Shaman will get xp.

Here has been my recent powerleveling experiences given the current hotzones.

There is an assumption made that the class being pl'ed has some form of PB AE spell. If the AE is not for a few levels, then just low level regen and dmg shield the plee in PC or FoB til AE level.

Second assumption is that the druid has Reptile.

1. Kurns(2-14): Have the druid without any regen or DMG SHIELDS run around and gather as many skeletons as possible. The thing to watch out for is getting too far from the mobs, as kunark mobs will give up if you get too far. Find a corner and sit a few times to take damage. Then cast reptile, which should provide all the aggro you need. Have the plee cast the PBAE spell once or twice depending on resists. Only after the ae is cast, have the druid cast their most powerful dmg shields on themselves. Watch carnage ensue when the druid sits down.

Note: I said no regen so that your druid can take some damage for the reptile procs to heal the druid and build aggro.
Note 2: I said no damage shields on the druid because the mobs will die during the rounding up process.
Note 3: These mobs are skeletons, make sure the plee is further from the mobs than the pler or they will turn and splat.


2. Unrest(14-22/25): Park the plee at ZI. Have the druid minus regen and minus dmg shield run and pick up as much of the first floor and basement as possible. Run back to zi where there is an excellent corner. Reptile for aggro. Plee casts PBAE. Pler casts uber dmg shields. Carnage. Repeat with upper levels. Wait for respawns. This can be taken to 25 ish( which I did with my cleric ), but I gave up at 22 or 23 with my bard and headed to the next zone.


3. Lower Guk(22-46/49):
a)Have the druid, with regen this time, run from ZI to bedroom. From bedroom go around and round up all the bats, spiders, gargoyles, those 6 skeletons, ass/sup room and nearby frogs on that level. Return to bedroom, where there is a corner. Run the plee over and have them cast their stuff. Cast the uber dmg shields and watch the carnage.
b)Jump down with the druid to the safe hall area and get aggro on the few mobs prior to that hall. Run the plee down into the hall. Have the druid run from that area all the way back up towards ass/sup room picking up every single mob in the area. Having regen on during this time is fine, you can start leaving the big dmg shields up too while running around. Return to the safe hall, and setup shop in the first corner. Ensue carnage.
c)Round up every mob on the way to frenzy spawn. Run back to safe hall. Carnage.
d)Round up every mob on the way to ghoul lord. Run back for carnage time.

Note: Watch out for nameds with pets. They will aggro the plee unless you cast a nuke on them first.
Note 2: This is designed to pull all of lower guk undead in 4 pulls.
Note 3: Given the sheer number of mobs per pull, you WILL need reptile to survive. This can be done at 70 for sure, but back then when I did this on my necro I needed to break up the pulls into smaller batches.
Note 4: Have earthen roots ready, the final mob likes to run.
Note 5: I use lesson in this area around level 37( when most mobs get hit by the AE.) You MUST camp the plee between pulls. If you do this, then you should have double exp for about 3 rounds through...about 3x the time you normally would get.

The few classes I did this to took about 3-4 hours to get to 49. That is with using 1 lesson...and camping out the plee inbetween pulls while lesson is running.


4. Karnor's Castle(49-63): Setup shop in hand room, but make sure you run a timer so you know when the hands will respawn.
a)Pull the 4 hands and everything on the ramparts.
b)Pull the bodyguards/drolvarg warlord + 6 cells on the way.
c)Go down the stairs near hand room. Skeleton pulling time.
d)Go down the stairs near hand room Skeleton pulling time.
e)Go down the stairs near hand room Skeleton pulling time.

Note: I did say the same thing thrice for a reason, you need to break up the pulls down there. Try to pull in groups of 12 or so, unless you can handle more...or less.
Note 2: Just like in guk have earthen roots ready as some mobs when all their friends are dead will run.
Note 3: I took KC to 53 on the two most recent characters. The one I took to 63 was when KC was a HZ, but no matter what expect exp to slow down to a relatively obscenely slow amount compared to the first 4 hours.
