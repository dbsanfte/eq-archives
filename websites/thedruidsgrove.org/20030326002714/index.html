Archery and pathing changes coming soon 03/08/03 3:59 pm
Casters Realm has a new article regarding the state of Everquest. Here's a few highlights for your reading pleasure.

Archery & Line of Sight

“That’s one I definitely wanted a solid answer for before the next fan faire”, claimed Scott Hartsman.

“Here’s the deal with Archery. Ranged combat is currently very overpowered. Be it from a caster or an archer you could in some circumstances deliver enormous amounts of damage with almost no hate being added to you. This is being fixed very soon.

We do have a fix for the line of sight bug ready for the Test Server and the other little things archers want like auto-attack, but the game is going to change a little once the hate-list system is corrected and Rangers will need to be a lot more like Rogues in watching their damage output to control aggro. Auto attack will probably be situational at best once implemented if a Ranger wants to stay alive.”

That said the team is aware of the long term problems with archery and hopes to implement its ‘Archery Patch’ sometime in the next few months.

Is Caster Balancing ever going to be completed?

We feel the classes are fairly balanced now. We certainly aren’t saying everything is perfect, but we’re looking more long term and trying to work towards more permanent solutions with the role of classes and their abilities with the content we’re adding to the game. There are a number of things we expect to tweak and tune still, but no class is useless or unable to contribute in a variety of situations.”

From the test server:

PATHING UPGRADES ON TEST, MARCH 8, 2003

This is a test server patch mesage only. This does not apply to players on the live servers. Pathing Changes

Today we have introduced the first stage of an ongoing project to upgrade the pathing system for EverQuest.

Of course this means we would like you to keep your eyes open for NPCs doing bad things. NPCs will (should) move a bit differently, that is to be expected. If, for example, you are used to a specific NPC taking a round-about route to get from one place to another and he now takes a more direct route - that's a good thing.

Bad things would be:

An NPC that wants to get to you but can't Pets that can't follow you through a teleporter
Any NPC that takes a much longer path than it should to get to a player (like going away from the target for a bit then heading back)
Any time an NPC doubles back, walking past his destination and then going directly back to it Any significant 'warping' by NPCs
Any NPCs that defy gravity by walking a few feet above the ground
And any other really odd behavior by NPCs when moving.

We are aware of current issues with NPCs fleeing (either from Fear or trying to escape death). For now, there is no need to report problems with NPCs fleeing in odd ways. We're working on that one now.

It is very important that you note the location of the odd behavior, the name of the NPC, what it was doing (just patrolling around or chasing someone it was mad at) and the details of the oddity in your bug reports.

Spell research 02/25/03 10:46 pm
From the Dev Corner:

Research - an old skill given new life

With this patch we introduced the ability for intelligence casters (Enchanters, Magicians, Necromancers and Wizards) to create additional spell scrolls using their research skill. Many of the newly researchable spells have only been available from quests or killing NPCs in the past. The new research still requires components from risky locations or dangerous foes. Our goal is to increase the number of ways to earn rare spells, without reducing the challenge required.

This, however, creates a bit of an inequity between the intelligence casters and the priest classes (Clerics, Druids and Shaman) when it comes to acquiring this type of spell. While there is nothing inherently wrong with different classes earning their spells in different ways, we don't see a reason to give more options to one type of caster than another. In this case, we have taken the opportunity to give the research skill a little more value and at the same time negate any imbalance between classes.

Some time in the near future we plan to introduce recipes for the creation of certain spells for the priest classes. Intelligence casters can use their research skill to create these priest spell scrolls, and priest classes will benefit by having an alternate method for obtaining these spells. This helps out both priests and intelligence casters, and creates a new niche for research as a trade skill.

Alan

The patch from hell 02/24/03 10:12 am
Well, LoY was released today but with it comes nerfs. /sigh

On one hand you'll now be able to get unflagged people into flagged zones, IF you start the raid outside the zone and IF 85% of your raid is already flagged. However, if you go LD and are out of the raid for more than 3 minutes, kiss your butt goodbye and say hello to Plane of Tranquility. I hope you have a good connection and a fast computer.

Speaking of fast computers, horses were nerfed to those that have the graphics intensive luclin models off. Most people have the models off, especially on raids, becuase 1. they're ugly, 2. they reduce performance, 3. the start up slow down annoyance with horses, 4. problems with interrupting casting, etc. But no more. You will not be able to use horses now unless you have luclin models on. Thanks SoE for thinking about all your customers with this nerf. So long horse, it was nice while it lasted but I can't afford to upgrade my computer again just to use you.

Flagging events are now limited to 72 flags max.

The dialogue boxes for ressurrections and translocations will now only stay up for 5 minutes.

There were a lot of other changes, most not worth mentioning in the news, so you can read the rest here.
