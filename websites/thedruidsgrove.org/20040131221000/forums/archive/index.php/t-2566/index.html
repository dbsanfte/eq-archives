BricSummerthorne
It's sort of taken for granted that SCM1-3 are the first Archetype abilities we should get. Based on the comments of a Mage friend, I looked more closely at MC, and to my surprise found that <strong>MC can be a better initial purchase</strong>, depending on your gear.

For example, I have 2933 mana, and FT1. I usually play with only PoTC on. What's the better purchase?

Assume that I spend all that time on a horse. My effective mana regen is then 27.7 mana/tic. Add that to my mana pool, and I have 52,793 mana available in 3 hours. Using all this mana, my SCM savings are as follows:

SCM1 (2% ) 1,055
SCM2 (5% ) 2,640
SCM3 (10%) 5,279

In 3 hours (1800 tics), MC returns the following amount of mana:

MC1: 1,800
MC2: 3,600
MC3: 5,400

Note that at every level, MC is a better purchase for me. Note that SCM has two further disadvantages:

If I am not medding 100% of the time, my regen rate is less, and I save less.
If I am not OOM at the end of the 3 hours, I save less.

As a more realistic example, assume I am medding 3/4 of the time, and maintaining 3m. That's 21.3 per tic, and I have 39,213 total mana to spend. SCM breaks down like this:

SCM1 (2% ) 784 saved
SCM2 (5% ) 1,961 saved
SCM3 (10%) 3,921 saved

It's not even close.

Two final points. MC is time-based, and SCM is mana-based. For occasions when you use lots of mana in a short time span, SCM performs better than my example, since MC requires time to work to full effect. Also, as your manapool and regen increase, SCM performs better yet, since there is a larger amount of mana to base savings on.

A well-geared Druid who is usually raid-buffed would almost certainly get more bang out of SCM. In the long run, it's a worthwhile purchase for all of us. However, you need to look at YOUR gear, to decide which is better to buy first.

I went the SCM1, SCM2, SCM3 route, and I wish *I* had done a little more research.
Tudamorf
Your analysis is flawed because you're not considering the effect of specialization, which also affects the rate at which you can cast a spell. It's better to just analyze the amount of time it takes to recover the mana from casting a single spell.

Example: you cast your level 59 Wildfire, and you are specialized in Evocation. Your regular mana cost for the spell is 285 after Specialization (11% savings). Your mana cost after SCM3 is 253 (21% savings). Let's also take your 27.7 mana/tick figure.

Without SCM3, you can cast Wildfire once every 10.3 ticks on average.
With SCM3, you can cast Wildfire once every 9.1 ticks on average.
Without SCM3 but with MC3, you can cast Wildfire once every 9.3 ticks on average.

So SCM3 is slightly better than MC3, using your mana regen figure.

Now consider that very soon, you will be a level 65 druid, with, say, ornate gloves, around FT5, casting Summer's Flame. Your mana regen will be about 35 mana per tick. Without SCM3, you will be able to cast Summer's Flame about every 8.0 ticks. With SCM3, you will be able to cast Summer's Flame about every 7.0 ticks. Without SCM3 but with MC3, you will be able to cast Summer's Flame about every 7.4 ticks. SCM3 is a clear winner here.

Note that I'm not even considering in either of these scenarios that you will have mana regen (KEI, VoQ, Spiritual Purity, bard song, etc.) -- something you will likely have in a group, thereby skewing the results even further.

Given that, you'd be better off dumping your points into SCM3 and enjoying the gift that keeps on giving. You may also experience fewer fizzles with SCM3. Then, when you get the points, go for MC3 to round out your character.

<blockquote>Tatankawd says:<hr>Interestingly, someone mentioned SCF. That skill scales VERY nicely[/quote]Your post suggests another interesting question: whether SCF is a good alternative to MC or SCM for a nuke-happy, XP-frugal druid. SCF gives you the following average damage over time increase:

SCF1 gives an increase of 0.66% [0.33% per AA]
SCF2 gives an increase of 3.30% [0.66% per AA]
SCF3 gives an increase of 7.00% [0.62% per AA]

(Caclulation: I = 1+K*P, where I = increase, K = damage modifier, P = probability of critical.)

SCM also gives you a form of damage over time increase, because it allows you to nuke more with the same amount of mana.

SCM1 gives an increase of 2.0% [1.00% per AA]
SCM2 gives an increase of 5.3% [0.83% per AA]
SCM3 gives an increase of 11.1% [0.97% per AA]

(Calculation: 1/(1-K), where K = percentage mana savings.)

Now let's look at MC. Assume the lead poster's situation, 28 mana per tick.

MC1 gives an increase of 3.57% [1.79% per AA]
MC2 gives an increase of 7.14% [0.89% per AA]
MC3 gives an increase of 10.71% [0.60% per AA]

(Calculation: (28+K)/28, where K = added mana regen per tick.)

Notice that by considering only the percentage damage increase and not absolutes, a number of extraneous variables drop out (spell used, specialization, other mana savings items, level, etc.), making this analysis useful for druids in various stages of development. Also notice how SCM is the only skill that scales linearly per AA point, something SoE should look into fixing.

So in terms of bang for the AA buck for the lead poster, considering only the effect of average added nuke power:

1) MC1 is the clear winner with a staggering 1.79% damage per AA point.
2) SCM1 with 1.00%.
3) MC2 with 0.89%.
4) SCM2 with 0.83%.
5) SCM3 with 0.97%.

For 6th place, SCF2 is better than MC3, but since it has the last-place SCF1 as a prerequisite, we should consider them as a single unit: SCF1 + SCF2 = 0.55% per AA. So MC3 is the winner for 6th place. After finishing off SCM3 and MC3, the druid should move to SCF1, 2, and 3.

Another viewpoint is that SCM2 + SCM3 should go in third place and MC2 in fourth, because when considered together, SCM2 + SCM3 offer 0.91% increase per AA point, whereas MC2 only offers 0.89%. This would be logical for a druid who knows he is going to go for SCM3 soon anyway.
