    News: Bah, more stuff dead, guild conflicts, and the return of STNF, 19 July 2003

Well a few interesting things have happened since the last update. I personally hope that the drama can die down and guilds can communicate with each other. I really don't miss the SSRA spawn and mob fights I left on the Tunare server, and would really really rather not see them here. Our first tidbit for the update is a random Quiggle's tell I received. It was sufficiently twisted enough to warrant a mention.

If you cannot read it that well, it says "I want to bear Rob Thomas's Children". One question, WHO is Rob Thomas?

Fulmon, in a bout of jealousy at the utter pink gayness that is Quiggles running amok on the server, decided to follow the same fashion trend. Thank god he has gone back to colors not so loud recently. I think he's overdoing it just a bit with the phallic spear thing from the crotch area.

Somewhere in that fashion parade, we decided to give Aagnar our very first try. I think we got him to something like 40 percent. It was fun, and we'll get him 2nd try for sure. Tagnar spent most of the fight under Aagnar's skirts.

Among all this fun, we've been doing our standard Zek kills. Tallon and Vallon are in the habit now of just handing the loot over nicely. A couple of Saryn kills, another Behemoth kill, and a cheeseburger & fries to go with all the giant bladder busting serving of PoP. Here is a shot of Vallon, permanent scream on his face after seeing Herbal drop his skivvies and moon him. Herbal has dubbed it his "Moonstrike" nuke.

Last but not least, with all the bickering, flaming, fighting, and relative nasty behavior. I think I'm just going to go back to playing the best damn Role-Playing Game that has ever existed. Created around 1980, STNF was THE game of choice for us bastards who were burnt out on TSR's crappy AD&D platform. From now on, Devyn and Petachok will be played by my Tabbie-Cat Isis. I shall be elsewhere troubleshooting the Paranormal with copious amounts of firepower. The good old days loading 12-gauge shells with a mix of silver-shot and garlic, sigh.

- Devyn
