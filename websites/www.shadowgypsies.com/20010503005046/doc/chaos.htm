Chaos Storm



The beginning of the end started when a rampant evil overtook the world, and great evil beings arose who could not be put down. The evil infected the souls of many, and doubts began to grow even among the mighty Shadow Gypsies -- that even they would not be able to win the war. Several knights were corrupted, and fell apart from "The Way", some never to be seen again... and others seen again in evil form spreading the poison of evil throughout the rest of the population.

Kingdoms were rent asunder by the mighty forces the dark powers brought against them. The Kings, Dukes, Earls, and all the rest got together and determined that they had to work as one in order to rid the world of the evil menace. It was then that they called upon Lan, "Keeper" of "The Way," and leader of the Shadow Gypsies, to lead the forces of good in a great campaign against the forces of this new, but ancient, evil.

So Lan led the armies of the combined kingdoms against the undead, pushing them back towards the abandoned Castle Victoria, and it was then that the foe seemed to disappear altogether. It seemed as if the clouds rolled back and the sun shown once more. But it was only the eye of the storm...

Until that time, the leader of the undead armies had yet to show its face, or to make its presence known to the world of Meridian. Yet, even as the undead armies seemingly disappeared, a group of necromancers worked to resurrect their master, the Lich Queen, who still lay, seemingly asleep, in the bowels of the abandoned, and long-forgotten dungeons of Brax.

Unsatified with the armies sudden disappearance, and seeming retreat, Lan journeyed with his personal apprentice, Drakken, to the famed libraries of Castle Burgouise, where Princess Katriina dwelt. There, he studied tirelessly, as a man obsessed, for somehow he knew that something horrible was on the horizon. Long had his Mentor, Quanag, the old orc wizard warned him of 'a storm on the horizon,' and it seemed as if it was here. The Keeper knew his destiny was at hand.

As Lan bent over one an ancient tome he had dug up, on the third evening of his studies, he froze, barely breathing, as his eyes scanned again the passage he had just read. Drakken looked up, as Lan pushed the old tome aside, and seemed to cross reference something from another tome, and his face went white.

He read of an ancient ruler of a people long extinct, long before the gods had come to Meridian. Her name was of the ancient language, much of which remains undecipherable, but ended up sounding like "Ten'yuv'al."

As History would have it, the queen fell in love with a trabadour, named Tendrath, who died an untimely death. The queen then became obsessed with unlocking the secrets of death, so that she might be with him again. Yet evil twists the mind, and the body, and the queen unleashed an evil for which she was unprepared, dooming not only herself to a liche's existence, but also those of the kingdom were hideously deformed in such a way that, though the flesh decayed off their bones, they would not die.

One wizard, however, was able to resist the curse. Klavis Verge, fought the Lich Queen herself, casting a strong enchantment on her, causing her to slumber. Then, he sealed Brax it by magically creating an elevator that could only be opened by powerful magic from the outside, then vanished, never to be seen again. So it was, that the doomed city of Brax remained forever forgotten.

In a rush, Lan and Drakken tried to marshal the combined armies of the kingdoms again, but the nobles, with the exception of the rebel Jonas D'accor refused, thinking the battle won. So Lan, the Shadow Gypsies, a few of the other orders of knights, and those who followed Jonas D'accor, started their journey to the hidden city of Brax.

During the journey to Brax, one of the great Knights, already corrupted by the servants of the Lich, began to plot against the others. He willingly offered aid, but laughed to himself when a great Knight of any Order fell to the minions of evil. Three orders of strong knights were completely eliminated as repeated ambushes were repelled as they journeyed towards Brax, each more frenzied than the last, yet not one Shadow Gypsie fell to the sword.

Lan pushed the armies hard using the skills of a new apprentice wizard, Shanna, to strengthen them in their race against time, but as they crossed the threshold of the Brax, Lan knew they were already too late as the sounds of death heralded her awakening. The forces of good were halted in their tracks as they met the necromancers, and inhabitants of Brax head on, causing fully half of their number to fall on that one day, and were forced to fall back.

The fighting continued, and days turned to weeks, and weeks turned to a month, and their numbers dwindled. Now, several orders of knights were completely decimated, leaving only a few knights from each that were now without a master. Of those rebels that followed Jonas, a scant one hundred remained, and all those remaining were long since battle weary.

The Keeper fell into a foul mood as he began to have doubts where none were before. Never before had he dealt with such a strong foe...and never before had he felt the beginnings of defeat. So it was he called Drakken to him, instructing him to prepare for a final assault. If they were not successful this time, they never would be.

They again found themselves enthralled in fierce fighting as they pushed their way the dark passages, twisting corridors, and hordes of undead that were thrown in their way, eventually gaining entrance to a place they had not seen before, and had their first encounter with the queen herself. With a roar the remianing combantants thrown back by a force stronger than any had ever witnessed, many were killed instantly, including the remaining armies of the careless queen, and the others who were still alive ran in blind fear never to be seen again.  Lan, Drakken, and Shanna staggered to their feet to see the Lich coming toward them, raising her had for another spell. With a roar of enraged defiance, Lan released his anger as he ran towards the queen, his magical sword raised. The Lich released a fireball, knocking Lan on his back, charring his face.

Lan howled in pain an fury as he regained his feet and charged again. The Lich's face registered surprise a split second before Lan closed with her. The battle between the Lich and Lan raged back and forth, the lich blasting Lan with spells which were buffered by his magic armor, and Lan striking the Lich with his sword, causing wounds which seemed to heal as quickly as they appeared. Yet the battle began to tell on Lan, as his parries became more pronounced, and he began to weave on his feet. The Lich's face at last lighted with glee as she reached for Lan with her hand, prepared to suck out his very soul.

"No!" Drakken shouted, yet held immobile by the terrible spectacle before him. Drakkens exclamation, however, seemed to snap Shanna out of it, and she knew what she must do. With grim determination, She began to cast, releasing a bolt of purest electicity, stricking Lan dead before he hit the ground - and before the Lich could reach him.

"Noooooooo!" the queen howled. The Lich struck at Shanna with her hand, and Shanna instantly consumed, as the Lich turned toward the remaining Drakken.

Drakken jumped back as the Lich lashed out with a power that was unknown to this world, and yet another battle ensued, the Lich with a strange, evil light in here black eyes, and Drakken with a strength borne of desperation. On and on they fought, until both were weary. It was then that it happened...

The Lich and Drakken circled one another, neither attacking...or so Drakken thought. Yet while they Drakken began to feel weaker with every step, and less 'connected' to himself. The Lich smiled, as if receiving some kind of aphrodesiac, and promtly stumbled upon the rubble of bones. Seeing his change, Drakken leapt forward, his sword cleaving the Lich's head from her shoulders.

Instantly, the ground began to shake, and the very pillars of Brax began to groan as the world felt the death of the Lich. Drakken stood in astonishment, not believing the end had come with such a finality, yet....there was something wrong. Something terribly wrong....

Drakken's face contorted in pure terror as he felt himself slipping towards the warm embrace of death, and the cold realization that his body was no longer his, and his soul was quickly becoming displaced and set adrift. Perception faded....
