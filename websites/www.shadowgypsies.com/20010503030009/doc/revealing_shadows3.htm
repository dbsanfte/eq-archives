Revealing Shadows Vol. 3



Drakken's eyes were bloodshot, his shoulders drooped, his ringmail rent in many places from battles past, and a deep cut in his side gnawed at him, angrily reminding him of his short fight with the chasm minotaur a few days before...all for some lousy axe. Now, neriak's cool, humid air surrounded him, and the guards eyed him dubiously, as if waiting for an excuse to attack.

He had searched the dangerous crags of Highpass Hold, battled uselessly with Zhalfirin and Coumadin in the hot and humid air of Runneye Cave, Wandered aimlessly in the Beholder's Maze with Shanna, and even fought mindlessly in the reaches of East Karana with Elfmann, yet, he had found nothing but many more wounds, a list of scars that will join the others which will one day make good stories, and a bone deep exhaustion.

After arriving in Neriak - not even knowing why he went there - Drakken soon found himself in the library, an very rare occasion for Trollkind. He stood there, with the dust of the road still covering him, and his now marred and tired-looking halberd held loosly in a now thickly-calloused hand, looking upon the shelves upon shelves of books, while the nearby Tier'Dal librarian openly smirked at this "dumb Troll," playing at being interested in reading.

"Perhaps a book on Solusek Dungeon will wet your appetite." Drakken turn with amazing agility for a Troll, swinging his halberd in a vicious arc, causing the terrified Teir'Dal librarian to leap back just in time. Yelping an exclamation, the librarian scurried back to his desk, frantically fishing in one of its drawers before pulling out a small dagger.

"P-put th-that thing down!" he stammered, while pointing at the halberd with his empty hand. Drakken merely smiled and looked at the puny dagger the small elf held, then rumbled a quiet laugh.

"Drakk is sorry, elfie, but I will not part with my weapon. It has taken me through battles with evil eyes, Minotaurs, the curse dazed goblin warriors, tentical terrors, countless crocodiles, orcs, gnolls of all kinds....I would gladly give my soul to Inuruuk before I gave you my weapon." Drakk rumbled, his voice seeming to reverbate off of the library walls. The Teir'Dal stood still a moment, as if sizing up Drakken, then realized how futile he looked, with only a dagger, and the Troll in full armor, and a vicious looking halberd that seemed to glow with a soft, magical light. Sighing, he carefully lay the dagger on his desktop.

"I was told to expect you. My name is D'artagnan E'ltrover." D'artagnan chuckled "I must admit, when he told me to expect a Troll, I thought he had lost his mind, only once before had a Troll graced these halls, and even then, it was only because he was persueing a known murderer, not in quest of knowledge." Drakkens eyes brightened slightly.

"Who told you to expect me?" Drakken asked. The librarian's eyebrows arched in obvious surprise.

"Why, Dreyn of course!" He answered. At the look of bafflement on Drakken's face, D'artagnan continued, "He is a messenger of the gods, especially Inuruuk, and a necromancer of high power. He told me you quested to find some kind of "Way," or something like that, and then he pointed out this book for you to read." The Teir'Dal laid a finger on the book he had put down, in favor of his dagger. Drakken looked a moment at the book, it was old, its hard leather cover well worn with age, and the parchment within it old, creased, and dirty.

"How old is that book? drakk not wanna ruin it." Drakken asked, as he walked nearer to the book.

D'artagnan again smirked. "I have another copy of it that I have scribed. Why dont you take that one, and keep it? It is the original though, so be tender with it." Drakken grinned his thanks as he picked up the book and headed towards the nearest small table, and woefully undersized chairs to read it when the librarian cleared his throat, again capturing Drakken's attention.

"We do have chairs for...people of larger stature." he said, pointing towards the other end of the hall, which held one large table, complete with four large chairs. Drakken grinned again, as he walked over to the chairs of suitable size, sat in one, and began to read...

Solusek

Drakken lost track of time, as he was immediately immersed in the history of a dragon who once walked Norrath freely, then suddenly was reported to be imprisoned in the dungeon of Solusek. There were some vague references to the dragon being "punished," but for some reason, Drakken was not interested in that. No, the real interest came when he read of certain items having been lost through idiocy of travellers who thought themsleves strong enough to brave the dangers of the dungeon alone.
