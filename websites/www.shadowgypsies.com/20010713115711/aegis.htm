WANDERING MOBS

None of the mobs relevant to this camp wander at all. None of them. This information is only to quell any fears you may have from additional mobs interfering with spawn rates, PHs, etc. There are a number of wandering mobs around the Undead Camp where you will be doing your camp. From the Undead Cyclops (Paladin Quest) to the wandering Spirocs and the occasional lion/lioness, as far as I can tell they have no effect on the spawn or the camp. If they get close enough to the camp, wandering Treants will attack Putrid Skeletons, but wandering Elephants should not pose a problem either. Cyclops that frequent the area are a nuisance but can be avoided if you stay on the perimeter of the camp. From what I can tell, none of these mobs dying/wandering through the camp/etc. have any bearing on the timing or frequency of the Grimrot spawn. They seem to be independent of the pop, and despite rumours of an "SK Cycle" like the popularized "FM Cycle", there is no evidence to support this and I can only assume that it is not true.

 

 

THE PLACEHOLDER

The Placeholder (PH) for both of Lord Grimrot's spawn versions (skeletal and human) appears in the form of a Putrid Skeleton. The PH spawns directly underneath one of the upright huts that does not have a lantern on it. There are several huts without lanterns on them, but this one is almost right in the center of the camp. The real PH can be easily confused with another Putrid Skeleton that spawns and collapses right against one of the legs of this hut. Do not be fooled, the real PH is a Putrid Skeleton that does not fall. Also, the real PH always wields a rusty Broadsword, and when you attack it, instead of threatening you with some obscure Norrathian villain, the Putrid Skeleton simply says, "Die like the fool you are" when attacked. You get all the same faction from killing this Putrid Skeleton that you do the other similar mobs that spawn in the Undead Camp. Remember, there are eight or 10 Putrid Skeletons that spawn in the camp, but ONLY the one that STANDS DIRECTLY UNDER the center hut is the true PH.

 

 

 

 

CAMP CLEARING?

My first attempts at this camp were to clear the camp of ALL Skeletons and Putrid Skeletons. This, I thought, would help force the PH to spawn at the appropriate time. However, this is not necessary. The first time I allowed the entire camp to stay spawned, I was quite pleased to see the PH pop right on time. No clearing was necessary (though still recommended for faction) so it is NOT a necessity to clear camp to make Grimrot spawn. This changes the quest little, but now you can take comfort in knowing that if you are unable to keep the entire camp cleaned (from medding/healing etc) it is not a problem, don't worry about it.

 

 

TIMING IT

To finish my quest I had to endure seven (14) cycles of this PH. In that time I have narrowed the spawn time on the PH from 3hr10m --> 3hr25m. The average spawn time was 3h14m. The PH never spawns faster than 3h10m, and never takes longer than 3hr25m. That is fact.

 

This timer starts the second you KILL THE PH. Not as soon as it spawns, but as soon as you kill it. Once it's down, it doesn't seem to matter whether or not you even loot the corpse. The next PH (or Grimrot) will spawn ~3h14m after the PH dies. Since we discussed already "clearing the camp", you know that it doesn't matter whether or not the camp is clear and it seems the timer on the PH is completely independent of the camp being cleared, wandering mobs or seemingly any other variable. As I did, you are even afforded the luxury of logging off, resuming RL, then logging back on to await the next PH. The spawn rate is that static. Note: Logging off is not recommended, as anyone wandering by can begin camping Grimrot, unaware you have been for some time. Stealing a camp on Rallos has nasty results, I highly recommend doing this with friends or your guild to avoid losing the Grimrot Spawn.

 

 

GRIMROT SPAWN/DROPS

I was most fortunate with Grimrot's drops. The first version that spawned was the Skeletal version, I can only assume it was ~25th level as has been often reported; I was much too high to accurately con and can only guess according the the Harm Touch I received. The Skeletal Lord Grimrot summons a pet, has good Magic resistance and Harm Touches for ~250. When killed, he dropped the DECAYING HEART (magic, lore, nodrop). This is one component of the quest. He also dropped a Pestilence Scythe (lore). The Scythe was not nodrop.  This is a very important point; the scythe you must use for the quest drops from the Human version and IS MAGIC, LORE and NODROP. Please ensure that you simply destroy the droppable scythe, this will avoid confusion and perhaps even a mistake when handing the items in later.

 

 

When the Human version finally spawned, it was several PHs after.  He is dressed in full plate mail, and wields the Pestilence Scythe to great effect.  He may also summon a pet if he has time, but I attacked him as soon as he spawned so he was unable to buff or to summon an undead servant.  Note:  Please be careful, this version of Lord Grimrot is ~38th level and HARM TOUCHES. His HT amounted to ~380hp right off the bat. He is VERY Magic Resistant, ~100-120mr I would guess, and uses Sap Strengh and Lifetap as often as he swings; this prolongs the fight significantly.  Also, any of the Putrid Skeletons that are in the area will agro you.  This may be because of your close proximity to them, or it may be an effect that Lord Grimrot causes, I do not know this.  As I fought him, I had four Putrid Skeletons on me as well.  Be careful.  When the human Lord Grimrot fell, he dropped TWO SCYTHES. Both were named Pestilence Scythe, but ONE was nodrop, the other was the same kind dropped by his Skeletal version.  The nodrop scythe is the one used for the quest.  As with the Skeletal Lord Grimrot, I highly suggest deleting the other scythe, it has terrible stats and will only serve to confuse later.

 

Now that you have both these pieces, you are ready to hand them in and receive the Aegis of Life!  Oh... but wait...

 

 

FACTION

Just when you think your quest is over, think again.  This can be the most discouraging part of the quest.  More disappointing than waiting for the PHs, more disappointing than seeing yet another PH spawn instead of the coveted Lord Grimrot.  My cleric was a Dark Elf that worshipped Innoruuk.  As you can well imagine, the Priests of Life are not my favourite people, or vice versa.  Before I even began the camp for the items in SK, I visited Qeynos and carefully conned the priests.  So-and-so Scowls at you, ready to attack.  Damn.  Lots of faction to do.

 

As mentioned in the section "Clearing the Camp?" killing Putrid Skeletons (including the PH) give you small faction adjustments, as show below:

During the time that I spent camping Lord Grimrot, I killed enough Putrid Skeletons to get me into DUBIOUS faction with the Priests of Life.  (I only learned this after returning to Qeynos with both quest items, a pleasant surprise.)  To learn the details of the quest, you will need to speak with Camlend Serbold.

 

In order to raise my faction from Dubious, I had to seek out LASHUN NOVASHINE.  He is a Priest of the Temple of Life, and cons the same as any other priest... at this point, Dubious to me.  I had collected quite a few bone chips while camping Grimrot, and had them all with me when it came time to do faction work.  Hand Lashun Novashine four (4) bone chips at a time, (1 in each slot).  This will result in some xp (very little) and a faction adjustment.  He will also cast Minor Healing on you every time.  You'll get used to it.  In my work to get from Dubious faction until I handed in the quest items, I ended up giving Lashun Novashine ~32 STACKS of bone chips, yes, 4 at a time.  Tiring and ridiculous, but do you want the shield?  Thought so.

 

I had also heard about another way to raise faction with the Temple of Life in Qeynos, also with Lashun Novashine.  I heard that you can offer him cash donations and you will get +Temple of Life.  So, I found out that you can offer him 4gp at a time and get both +faction and a Cure Disease.  Annoying, but you'll get used to it.  Now, instead of buying stacks and stacks of bone chips ~5pp/stack in any newbie zone, it's much more economical to hand him cash.  (This is provided that the two quests offer the same faction adjustment).  I proceeded to make the Cash Donation quest my route to better faction.

 

With Dubious faction I did not receive any quest information.  When finally I reached WARMLY faction, I was elated.  FINALLY!  I ran to the Temple and spoke with Cameland.  He did not give me the quest details!!

I had heard that you needed incredible faction to do this quest, but come on!!!  So, back out to find Lashun again and continue the Cash Donation quest.  I continued to do it, over and over and over until I was more satisfied.  Note:  I had given Lashun Novashine ~32stacks of bone chips and ~2000gp (4 at a time) and was still only low-end Warmly.  Do not be discouraged, as long as you get the faction message, you're still getting faction, no matter how little.  I gave Lashun Novashine ANOTHER 1000gp (4 at a time) and was STILL WARMLY.  "This is ridiculous," I thought.  Back up to the Temple I went, hoping and praying.  I spoke with Cameland, and he GAVE ME THE DETAILS!!!

 

Finally!!!  Now, here is the tough part.  You've worked REALLY HARD to camp the items and then to raise your faction.  He just detailed what he wants you to get for him, and you already have it!!!  Should you give them to him right away and get your shield!?!?  What if you need more faction?  What if you give him the items and he destroys them!?!?  Is it worth it?  Are you willing to do some more faction work to ensure that doesn't happen?  I was.

 

Now that I was reasonably sure I would be rewarded when I turned in the items, I wanted to make absoloutely sure.  Back out I went, to find that rich dude (now, anyways) Lashun Novashine.  I brought with me another 1000gp and gave them to him 4 at a time.  BOOM.  --Lashun Novashine regards you as an ALLY--!!!  Finally!!! Best faction in game!!!  Off I went to the Temple.  Yep, Cameland regards you as an ALLY, too... here goes...
