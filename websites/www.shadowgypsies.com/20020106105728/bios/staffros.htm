All is dark, tonight in the world of Norrath.  From the region of Antonica to the realms of the planes of Fear and Hate, it seems like there is no stir as the dark forest of Nektulous sings with the night sounds of what lurks in the dark forest.  Nektulous, dark even when it is day.  Eons ago, it seems, when the Lavastorm Mountains erupted, the ash and brimstone from the volcanoes filled the air.  It seemed that nothing would ever grow in this jungle forest any longer; no animal life, no plant life, and certainly no intelligent life.  But the gods of Norrath are not to be taken so lightly and faithfully.  They do as they will, and care not what even their followers may think.  Not true gods are they, but just begotten remnants of whom once were powerful men and women of whom many follow and put their faith in.  These poor souls will never know that their faith is but a drop of rain in the Ocean of Tears.

 

So, after time went by, Nektulous did strive, and although the clouds of smoke and ash blocked the forest from receiving light from the sun, it thrived after all.  Plant and animal life alike live in this forest.  Shadow wolves, spiderlings,  moss snakes and more all roam the land of the dark forest.  These creatures could have wondered in from any of the out lying areas in which many animal kins of theirs live.  As well as these animals, creatures of the night, the undead; skeletons, mummies and zombies take a role of the essence of Norrath.  The strange thing about Nektulous is that it also spawned an intelligent species, and not just a species that can barely be considered intelligent, but a highly developed species of elves, with very high intelligence.  Unknown as to how this species of elves came to this land of Nektulous, many myths surround their existence.  If one of these elves were to be asked as to the beginnings of their existence, they would tell you they were created for Nektulous and Nektulous created for them.  These elves known for their ability to see in the dark (this ability is no doubt, an acquired ability developed over long periods of time due to the dark and shadowed nature of where they live), are consistently thought of as evil, the Dark Elves.  While most of the race of Dark Elves are evil and have mastered the arcane arts of necromancy and wizardry, some stray from the evilness of the rest of the pact and become well verse in the healing techniques of the cleric.

 

Burrowed in a mountain side, the city of Neriak exists.  An intricate series of tunnels and rooms make up this city with three city areas.  Included in these areas of the city are many guild halls for different classes of dark elves, a bank, many merchants, trainers, and guards.  Befriending the Dark Elves are the Ogres and the Trolls who have set up a camp of their own in one portion of the city.  In this city is where we find our character, a dark elf necromancer named Staffros Deneccros.  In the following pages you will find the journal and inner most thoughts of Staffros (Staff, as he is known by friends and members of his guild).  The world of Norrath has many treasures as it does creatures, monsters and beasts.  Take a journey to a mystical world filled with enchantment, magic, and horror.  Ride along with Staffros as he lives his own journey and grows to become a competent and successful user of the dark arts.

 

Dark and damp is the city of Neriak, lying amidst the dark forest, Nektulos.  Through mysticism and wonderment, yet unknown to all races, another soul appears.  Norrath, unlike other worlds where a being is born of a mother and a father, spins in an unlikely manner.  The races of Norrath, somehow just appear from seemingly nothingness.  Unknown for sure if it is the power of one omnipotent deity, or the combination of a tribunal of deities.  For some reason or another, this part of the history of Norrath is either lost or inexistent due to the fact there is no lineage to hand down from generation to generation.  So in confusion, beliefs are guided by a way of living each individual is born to.

 

In the midst of a Neriak, in the area known only as the Third Gate, yet this spectacle of mysticism takes place.  A new soul is born.  Rolls of thunder echo throughout Neriak, lightning and flashes that mezmorize the mind, none like Norrath has ever seen.  From old stories handed down, they tell of a being created by Inorruuk's own mold, with like abilities and that he would be "The One".  Could this new Dark Elf be "The One"?  Born; for the lack of a better word, creation may be more fitting a term, this day in Norrath rises a young dark elf.  Given the title of necromancer and the name Staffros.  Later, through Staffros’ journeys and experiences he will be able to choose a surname.  For some unknown reason, whether it be tradition or law, surnames may not be chosen until one reaches his 20th season of his life circle.

 

Born with a given intelligence and abilities, this young necromancer makes his presence known.  Created with a note in hand, to be delivered to a seasoned trainer, Staffros will begin his jouney.  Upon delivering this note, he receives instruction of how to conduct himself and experience the art of necromancy.  A training tunic place upon his back, to show his loyalty to the art which now becomes his religion.  Money, experience, and other desirable needs he is not born with, these tasks he must carry out himself.  Spells and conjuring he must learn.  He is necromancer, master of the dead, evil his domain.  Arcane in itself, necromancy is considered a threat to some, feared by most, but respected by all.  To some, necromancers only worship themselves and give homage only to hate and evil.  But there is something different about this necromancer, something that glows within him.  A light in a dark soul has sparked.  Not yet tapped, but burning still.  Over time, this light will either be extinguished or fueled, but this is decided by which path Staffros takes.

 

His journey ahead, will determine his abilities and his judgments.
