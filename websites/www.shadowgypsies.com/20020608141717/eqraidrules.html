General

  1. A Shadow Gypsy raids for these reasons:
  • A good bit of fun with his/her guildmates
  • Items so that he/she can advance himself in EverQuest
  • Faction
  • Experience is not the target when raiding and is an added bonus if you're lucky enough to get it. Enjoy the experience of taking out an uber target with your buddies and having the chance at some great loot.
  • It's all about FUN!!
    1. Reading and understanding the following rules is KEY!! These rules have been debated, scrutinized, and pondered by many. They seem fair. They are to be followed while attending raids. It is crucial to the success of our raids that these rules be followed. If at any time a member has an issue with the current rule set, that member should bring it up with an officer outside of a raid not during.
    1. Members of the Shadow Gypsies are expected to participate in raiding when they are able.  It is understood that due to RL conditions or geographical locations, some people will not be able to make posted raids.  All that is asked is that you participate when you can, be active in your guild, and communicate to the officers times that you are available for raiding.
    1. When arriving at the raid, it is your responsibility to let the "attendance monitor" know that you have arrived. The same is true for when you leave the raid. This should be told in /tell to the person listed as the "attendance monitor" in the post listing the raid details.
    1. BE ON TIME. Many of the places we will be going to will require you to be there when the entire party leaves. Most of us are on for a limited amount of time each day, and we need to make the best of it when we can. If you plan on attending a raid, plan to be there at least 15 minutes before the posted start time. If you know you're going to have a late start, please do your best to have your character camped at the raid start point the night before.
    1. ARROWHEADE'S RULE: Bitching is not allowed. If you don't like the rules or the raid, then leave without making a fool of yourself. Do not swamp the RL with /tell complaining about this and that. As stated before, we are there to have fun and get items. Sometimes things may not go the way you want them to. Guess what, it happens to us all. Suck it up, and let's have a good time everyone.

    Raid Communication

    1. GL (Group Leaders) will be the only persons to /tell the RL (Raid Leader), with the exception of the individual(s) assisting the RL.  If you feel the RL needs to be informed of something, go through your GL.
    1. /Shout is used only by the RL and pertinent raid info such as mezzes, snares, slows, and debuffs.  All other public channel use is to be kept in /say. /ooc is to be used by pullers and anyone that is separated from the rest of the raiding party.  If other people are in the zone, please keep in /guild.
    1. Link dead (LD):  If a member of the group goes LD, the GL will inform the RL and announce it in /ooc.  If it is the GL that goes LD, the group will elect a new GL (preferably pre-established before the raid, and a mature individual), this person will assume the role of GL and will inform the RL of the LD and /ooc the LD.

    Raid Looting

    1. GL's of the ex group only are allowed to loot.  GL's will loot all droppable items, /shout any pertinent and no drop loots.  (Words, runes, pages, and other suck drops need not be announced.)  Pertinent loots include, but not limited to: spells, no drop gear, weapons, gems, hi priced droppable, etc.  All droppable items will be /randomed and distributed at the close of the raid.  At the close of the raid, GL's will hand over all moneys, droppable loots, gems, weapons, etc. to the RL and those assisting him/her.  Moneys will be split between all clerics on the raid and enchanters IF the enchanters spent money for gems such as peridots for runes on the tanks.  Gems will be randomed between those who did not receive any loot on the raid.
    1. Loot randomed as per class usable.  Need before greed will be expected.  If you have something just as good or better, give your other brothers and sisters a chance to get equipped.  (Example: you are Necro and in Fear, Blighted Robe drops, but you have PoG Bonecaster robe or Thurg quested Warlock robe, abstain from the random for the robe, pass on uber loot to your guildies.)  Only one uberish/rare type loot per person per raid unless passed upon by all other usable classes.  Use common sense and compassion for fellow guildies. 
    1. Once a character wins a roll, s/he cannot roll again until all of the participants in the class have received an item.  If a person forgo's one roll to wait for something better that does not mean they get two chances to roll on the next item, etc.  They just forfeit their chance on that item only without any increased chances to win other rolls.
    1. Each class is encouraged to discuss amongst one another pre-raid any needs/wants for loots.  It is suggested that we try to keep these raids as organized as possible, this will eliminate greed and animosity, and add to a more quickly procession of the raid.  However it is not mandatory.
    1. Twinks have absolutely no loot rights until every raiding member of a race/class that can use the item has turned it down. Unneeded droppable items will not be given to twinks either. As stated before, they will be /randomed.

    Non-SG

    At any time, if outside help is needed for a raid (determined that SG alone can NOT do the raid without outside help), the efforts of the non-SG people will be compensated by a chance to /random any loots dropped.   

    Epic Exception:

    If it is an epic item, non-SG will NOT be allowed the chance to /random for the epic piece.  We should inform the non-SG persons that when they decide to do an epic mob, to let SG know and we will get as many peeps up to help them as possible. 

    If SG is able to complete a raid without outside help, and a non-SG want to come anyway because maybe they are bored, got nothing better do, etc. it will be expressed to them that this is an SG raid, and SG will come first for any loot.  If no SG on the raid wants a dropped item, then the non-SG may /random for the item.  (Bear in mind, this rule can be at the discretion of the RL with a discussion between any SG assisting the RL.) 

    Total wipe out situation. 

    For example:

    1. No clerics survived
    2. Necro/SK/Monk survived (whether by FD or other). 
    3. It is needed that a sacrifice be performed so that a Necro can rez a cleric to rez all other dead. 
    4. Mr. SK is FD, loses his agro by either /q or camping out.  Mr. SK may be asked or he may offer to be sacrificed so that a cleric may be converged (Necro rez) to rez all of his fallen brothers and sisters. 
    5. No other alternatives or easier way can be found. 
    6. Mr. SK consents to being sacrificed by Mr. Necro.  A portion of the money drops will be forfeited by clerics and/or chanters and given to Mr. SK for his selfless act AND/OR Mr. SK be given a dibs to have an un-challenged right on any loot that may drop on said raid. 
    7. Since there is no way of knowing what may drop, Mr. SK may choose at anytime during the raid up to the /random of droppable at close of the raid for his un-challenged right or (if it is decided that he may only choose one option of the two) he may decide to have the a portion of money's dropped. 
    8. IF Mr. SK chooses an item that drops during the raid, and it is determined that he may have only one option of either loot right or money drop, then Mr. SK loses the right to the money's and it will then again be distributed as stated above.  (Note: this is an example of something that is very possible on a raid such as a break in to PoF or PoH, or even deep inside a raid/dungeon area such as OS.

    Epic Raid
    Remember, the purpose for an epic item raid is for the recipient of the epic item.  Once an epic mob is killed, NO ONE will loot anything until the epic item recipient loots his/her epic piece.  The GL of the exp kill group may loot the money and announce the drops of the mob corpse.  Unless specified by the RL, the GL may not loot any items on the epic kill, droppable or not. 

    Raid Transportation (Ports and Translocate)

    If you need a port to the raid area, please be online an hour before the raid time so that we do not get too late a start.  If the raid starts early in the morning, it would be better to get to the zone the night before a raid, if it is an evening raid, please be on early to get to the raid if you are not already there.  It is important to be on time so we can start the raid on time and complete our missions.

    Porting classes are required to help non porting classes out when leaving a raid. If you are a druid or a wizard, it is your duty to make sure every single melee class member of the raid has a ride out!! If it is found out that a porting class member of the Gypsies gates out of a raid without offering the melee class members a ride, that member will find himself in jeopardy of losing membership within this guild.
