Revealing Shadows Vol. 2



Drakken's mood was foul as he threw open the door to his abode, causing it to smash against the door. He walked over to the spit over the fireplace, and rammed an orc thigh on it, put it over the already burning fire, and slammed the door, causing wall to shake from the force of it.

Dropping into the chair at his desk, his eyes slowly took in the contents of one of the letters, causing his already stormy countenance to become even more so. Sighing, he dropped the parchment and covered his face with large, green, and callused hands. When would the Grobb council get it through their thick heads? Within the paleskin ranks are those who are not our enemy! Why do they INSIST on warring for no reason?

Drakken jumped slightly at the knock on the door, then growled "Come in." The door swung back to reveal Orcaa, who noted Drakken's foul mood with a concerned look.

"Drakk? I's gotta talk to ya." Orcaa said, still hesitating at the door, not quite sure if he had come at a good time. Drakken waved him to a chair by his desk, signaling that it was ok. After all they had been through together, Drakk could still not understand Orcaa's hesitance.

"What's on your mind, old friend?" he asked. Orcaa fidgeted a moment before beginning.

"Drakk, its da others. Dey are beginnin' to wonder if the gods like us still. It's been a long time now, and a new "Way" has not been showed us." Orcaa looked even more nervous, and Drakken suddenly understood the reason for Orcaa's discomfort, for he was loyal to the end, and yet was somehow elected to talk to him about the delicate subject of "The Way." Drakken sighed in weariness.

"Orcaa, how long have you known me?" Drakken asked. Orcaa was silent a moment, as if thinking, then shrugged.

"I dunno. Been long tyme. Before we change into dis." he answered, spreading his muscular troll arms.

"In all that time, has I ever let you down?" Drakken asked. Orcaa thought again, then grinned mischievously.

"Yeah, ya didn't by me a beer last night." He answered, bearing his teeth in a beastlike grin. Drakken laughed, glad that Orcaa understood.

"Orcaa, Drakk is doing his best, and it WILL come...just takes time." Drakken answered. Orcaa nodded, seeming to be content with the answer, and stood and went to the door.Drakken stood to see him off, then closed the door.

"Seems like you are running out of time, my ugly friend." Drakken whirled at the sound of the new voice, and saw the Strange little dark elf again.

"Oh, you. Next time knock. Drakk not like unannounced visitors." Drakk said, as he crossed his arms. He did not like the way this strange dark elf made him feel so weak, just be being here. The little elf merely smiled.

"Well now, it's good to see you too." He said, jumping up from where he was seated on Drakken's desk. "Hmm...now where were we....Oh yeah! The books." he said, picking up where they last left off, as if hardly a day had passed, instead of a full cycle of the moon.

"You see, there is an interesting story...interesting indeed, for even I am interested in it. There is a man who walked two worlds, much like yourself, it seems. Hmm...hadn't thought of that...." the elf seemed to drift as he dwelt a moment on what appeared to be an immensely deep thought. Drakken roughly cleared his throat.

"Ah, sorry...lost me there for a moment, did you? Where was I...ah yes, the man who walked two worlds. His name was Jonas, though not the Jonas D'accor you knew on your previous world. He was a simple man, though properly smart. His passing was barely missed but by a few, and those few collected his letters, and what they could of his life, and compiled them into several volumes of books. These books are extremely rare, and, in truth, I do not know there specific locations. However, I do know that one is buried beneath the Karanas, guarded by a magic user of some sort, in a dungeon. Another may be found to the north, perhaps near where a Barbarian warrior protects its words - I would, perhaps, wander the tundra in hopes of finding the warrior. I KNEW that a third was hidden in Kaladim, but a Erudite, seeking some special knowledge of gating stole that, and some other nameless tome, and the last...being the fourth is known to be in the possession of a wood elf druid, whose curiosity will not allow him to easily give it up." the little elf finished. Drakken stood in shock at the geographical diversity of locations.

"Drakk cannot go to these places!" He shouted in indignation. "I will get killed by paleskins!" Drakken growled dangerously, wanting to crush the little elf, yet knowing better.

"Who says YOU have to go?" the little elf replied.

"Yeah right! Then how is Drakk supposed to know the where in those areas you tole' me where to go? How is Drakk supposed to know who are the names? How.."

"Careful, my ugly friend. Your anger is robbing you of your ability to speak clearly." the little elf interrupted. Drakken's face turned dark green as he sputtered angrily, then drove his fist down, hitting his desk and splitting it with a thunderous crack, while releasing a roar of frustration and anger.

"Tsk, tsk. I see you have not quite controlled that anger of yours." the little elf sighed. "It seems that you inherited that troll temper...ah...oh well. I see you are not ready to continue. Goodbye" And with that, the dark elf again vanished as before, leaving a livid Drakken behind to pick up the pieces of his broken desk...
