Revealing Shadows Vol. 5



"No!" Drakken roared, his fist hammering his desktop, splitting it down the center, causing Shanna to slightly jump, and kkal to snort derisively.

"You!" Drakken yelled, his face mottled with rage. "Go get that book, and don't come back until you have it!" Kkal nodded, then muttered some words, stepped into the shadows, and disappeared.

"You should send someone with him, Drakk" Shanna adviced. Drakken turned on Shanna.

"I know what I am doing this time, don't second guess me!" He said, as he sheathed his blackened sword and stormed out of his office, heading for the nearest bar to wash tonight's happenings in a flood of cheap ale.

Dark Omens

Kkal followed the trail of the thieves, not liking the direction the were headed. He had spent a half day persueing his prey, yet with every passing footstep, he knew with a dread certainty that they were traveling to the gate of fear itself...the plane of fear.

As night invaded in all its strength, causing the Feerrott to become even darker, Kkal hastened his pace, attempting to catch his quarry before they reached the gate...they were close, he knew. He smelled them. Then, passing to the right of a rather large tree, he froze a moment when he caught sight of them. One was clearly a necromancer, judging by his garb, while the other was...he couldn't tell. He...or it...was shrouded in a robe, and had its hood over his head, but there was some...power emenating from it that made Kkal uncomfotable, which was multiplied by the fact that they did not seem to be in a hurry...a trait not normal of a thief attempting to make a grand escape.

Kkal took a deep breath, centering himself as he drew is sword, his large frame barreling towards the prey. He quickly decided that the necromancer had to die first, which in turn would cause his pet to fall lifeless to the ground.

With a war cry that seemed to shake the very heavens, Kkal decended upon the unsuspecting couple, his invisibility wearing off due to lack of concentration. The necromancer's skeleton attacked, cackeling while its sword bounced harmlessly off Kka's armor, while Kkal's own blade sought out the necromancer, who had lept back while trying to stammer out a spell.

Kkal's blood boiled, and his vision was tinted with red as the necromancer's spell took hold, while kkal recovered from his previous blow by reversing his stroke, deftly impaling the dark elf. Kkal grinned evilly as the pet fell as if its strings were cut, then his vision blurred as he was thrown through the air as from a unseen blow, landing him gasping for breath, on his back.

'The other thief!' Kkal thought, as he raised to his feet, and looked about him. Kkal's eyes went wide in horror as he recognized what only could be merely an avatar of his god, Cazic himself. Yet, this avatar was taller, stronger than the others he had seen, and under one of his four arms was the book, nestled comfortably.

The avatar's arms lashed towards Kkal, striking with blinding speed, as Kkal lept back. The avatar's fist connected with Kkal's jaw, throwing his head back, and causing his blood to spray as Kkal staggered back losing his grip on his sword.

"You fool! You dare betray your god?" said a voice, seemingly unconnected to the avatar. "Leave now, or die!" the voice commanded. One of its hands made a curious gesture, towards Kkal, and instantly a fear gripped him...yet he was familiar with that fear, for he felt it often, while praying in the temple of Cazic himself.

The other ogres and trolls hunting in the area had long since left. Running away in a pure panic at the scene being played out before them. Kkal stood, tall and proud, his head erect and eyes glinting brightly. All his life, he knew this moment would come. With a roar that shook the very trees and earth of Feerrott shook as Kkal leapt toward the avatar. A great flame thundered as kkal unleashed a touch of harm upon the avatar. Flinching, the avatar covered his eyes, and Kkal tore the book away from the avatar, then focusing his eyes on the book, cast a spell which he knew was prohibited for him to know, causing the book to vanish.

Foreboding

Drakken slouched over his ale, sulking over his ill luck, when the book appeared in a bright flash of light, on the counter next to him, startling him. 

"What da..!" Drakken's face paled, and a look of fear briefly touched his visage.

"Shanna!" Drakken roared, grabbing the book , and running as fast as his legs could carry him towards his office.

"SHANNA!" Drakken roared louder still, causing his voice to echo off the rocky faces of Grobb. Shanna poked her head out of the general store.

"Get a druid here now! Kkal is in trouble!" Shanna's indigo face paled as she froze a moment, then her training took over, and she vanished in a flash of white light. Drakken threw open the door to his office, and frantically began to arm himself.

"Cierah!" Drakken yelled. 

"Yes?"Cierah walked in from the back room, where she had been studying, dressed comfortably in a silk gown.

"Get your medicine bag and gear, Kkal is in trouble." Cierah nodded, quickly slipping out the door towards her room where she stayed while in Grobb. A short time later, Shanna appeared, along with Kleintje, and Cierah arrived scant moments later.

"Take us to Feerrott, Klein." Drakk said, pulling his blackened sword.

Receding Shadows...

"You imbecelle!" The avatar roared, having recovered from his surprise. Kkal turned on the Avatar with renewed fury, his large fist connecting solidly with the avatar, who howled in surprise as he staggered backward. There was a brief flash of bright light then, and as Kkal's vision cleared, he saw that black weapons were held in each of the avatar's four arms. Suddenly aware of the gravity of the situation, Kkal's eyes darted from side to side, searching for his dropped sword, only to find it slightly behind the now armed avatar.

"You fool. You die now!" the Avatar roared. Kkal dove towards his sword as the avatar leapt towards him with stunning speed, one weapon slicing Kkal deep into his chest. With a roar of pain, Kkal landed hard, his hand clasping the hilt of his sword, yet he could not find the strength to raise it.

As the Avatar pulled the sword out of Kkal's chest, and brought up a sword for the killing blow, Kkal saw Drakken appear seemingly out of nowhere, roaring obscenities, crashing into the Avatar, causing both to go down in a heap.

The Avatar was first on his feet, screeching in hatred as he flew towards Drakken, who was still trying to regain his feet, and then Shanna was there, fire enveloping her in an aura of pure power. Her eye's seemed to glow as she threw a bolt of pure fire into the Avatar's face, causing him to drop one of his swords while he attempted to cover his face.

The Avatar muttered a quick spell, sending a bolt of his own streaking towards shanna, as she attempted to counter...then Cierah was there, grasping her mace in both hands as she brought it down squarely on the avatar's head, causing greenish blood to fly.

"You will all die!" the Avatar screamed, as he leapt away from cierah, and started running towards the gate to the plane of fear, only to be chased by a swift running Kleintje, his feet quickened by the spirit of the cheetah.

"Cierah, see to kkal." Drakken ordered, as he ran after Klein, and the departing Avatar. Cierah nodded, and went to work, chanting a quick spell as both she and kkal were enveloped in a blue aura, while Shanna stood guard.

The Door Of Death

Cierah's eyes opened, and she let a soft sigh escape her lips as she turned towards the awaiting Drakken, who had long since chased everyone out of Kkal's room, so that she could work. Kkal lay on his bead, his breathing shallow.

"I've done all I can." Cierah said. Her face was drawn, and there were dark circles under her eyes. "I...that weapon was not normal."

Drakken rested a hand on Cierah's shoulder and said,"You've done your best, that is all I ask, Cier. Go get some rest." Nodding gratefully, Cierah quietly left.

Drakken sighed and lowered himself into a nearby chair, staring at his long time friend. They had been through so much together...they had travelled worlds together...and now, some strange...freak threatened to take it all away.

"Don't die on me now, you young whipper-snapper." Drakken said, his voice gruff. "It all my fault. I should have never sent you."

"You know that's not true, Drakk." Drakk's eyes widened as he caught site of a ghostly form near the doorway. "It's not your fault. He knew what being a Shadow Gypsie required."

"But I should have sent Shanna with him, Master." said Drakken. The ghostly form of his teacher sighed, and shook his head.

"Perhaps, but you didn't." Lan answered. Drakken's face lowered in shame.

"That's right, I didn't. I was so arrogant, I did not heed Shanna's warning." Drakken answered.

"Listen, Drakken." Lan said. "You can sit here, and regret your decisions, or you can learn from them, but while you yet sit here and sulk, "The Way" is becoming ever more distant to you, and those new "Lost Souls" who are now with us. You are the only one who can do this, and it is time. Gather your precious few, and prepare them. Your journey is near." Lan's form faded from view, leaving Drakken to think in the dark...
