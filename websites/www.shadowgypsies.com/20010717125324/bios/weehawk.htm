Weehawk Pigeonslayer
Halfling Wanderer

     It was not every day that the Hobbits of Rivervale saw one brought into the World of Norrath wielding such power at a young age.

 

     The Pigeonslayer family had at one time been a vast and wealthy span of generations.  Over the years their fame and popularity had died down somewhat, however, due to corruption in the land and the seperation of the Pigeonslayer families in distant areas. 

 

     There came a day though when there was born to the family a young hobbit in Rivervale who, it seemed, would reclaim and reestablish the Pigeonslayer name.

 

     Born to a Cleric father and a Druid mother, they decided upon naming the youngster 'Weehawk', a type of name reserved only to those of great valor or vast deeds.  Immediately there was riducule; many there were that proclaimed Weehawk unworthy of his name.  His parents, however, did not care, and only smiled upon their son knowingly in love and praise.

 

     Observed playing one day some years later, elders of the city watched Weehawk incinerate fire beetles single handedly with magic that should have been beyond the reach for his age.  The matter was discussed, and within the child they saw the makings of a Druid.  And a powerful one at that.  He began his studies under his mother soon after.

 

     Weehawk was in Misty Thicket one day with his father.  He watched proudly as his father attacked and destroyed a camp of goblins.  His pride turned to horror when he saw the group of Dark Elven wizards materialize out of the shadows, and attack his father from behind as he walked casually back to his son.  His father's small flock of pet homing pigeons scattered in fear.  He screamed in terror, but it was too late; his father was slain before his very eyes, and the leader of the band wrested the shining metallic breastplate from his father's corpse as he watched.  The breastplate bore the Pigeonslayer insignia, and had been in the family for generations.

 

     Crying, Weehawk approached the band of killers and let loose a burst of fire that did nothing but wither the grass around the bandits' feet.  Their leader turned and laughed at Weehawk, even as he stuffed the Breastplate into a backpack.  "You are not worthy of our magics, small one.  Go home to you're mother's breast, and return one day to seek me out after you have grown a bit if you want revenge.  Hobbits.  Pah!"

 

     Laughing, the casters disappeared back into the shadows, leaving Weehawk to weep over his father's lifeless body.  His mother was crushed when she found out what had happened.  The life seemed to leave her and she could only sit sadly each day.

 

     The rage burned within him.

 

     One day, in one of his younger cycles, Weehawk was in the Vale, determined as ever under his practice, when a trio of ruffians swept by in pursuit of a young girl hobbit.  He saw first that they were intent on killing her, and in his minds eye he saw the cold face of his father's killer.  With a scream his threw out his hands, snaring one of the half-elven killers.

 

     "What's this!  A hobbit with an attitude!  Oh, I like it!" the bandit yelled, struggling as he forced himself towards Weehawk.  He pulled out a sword.  Weehawk's brow darkened, and he snarled, casting.  The ruffian paused, but it was too late.  Fiery magic ripped the half-elf's body to shreds, and he died screaming as his skin charred.  It was the first time Weehawk had ever killed anyone.

 

     The other two heard their fellow die and turned back.  "What the... kill that little runt!" one of them shouted.  Weehawk cast, and suddenly the shouter was snared as well.  The third member saw what was happening and panicked.  He fled the scene.  Weehawk approached the snared bandit, even as he tried in vain to get away, casting the whole time.  Spells knocked the half-elf back and forth, charring his skin, ripping his clothes, lighting his hair afire.  He died a pitiful, painful death.  A small group of stunned youngsters had gathered, gaping in awe and horror.

 

     The elders watched from the cave that lead to Rivervale.  Some watched with pride, others with troublesome looks.  Weehawk did not even bother to check his foes belongings; instead, he lowered his head and stomped past the elders into RV.  He had more training to do.

 

     Over the next course of years, Weehawk met many a friend; without a father, he was forced to grow up at a very young age, and though his mother still loved and watched after him somewhat, he did what he pleased.  It was not long before he had mastered the ability to change his shape into that of a wolf, and late into the night he would run with the packs, howling at the moon, an activity that many a hobit dreamed of doing but could or would never attain.

 

     He had a kind heart, but held a burning ember of fury there.  Young to be at the tavern, Weehawk still went, and questioned those travellers that he saw there.  One day he met an incredibly powerful human druid that was passing through.  This man's name was Mance.

 

     "Of course I know who you're talking about," Mance said, knocking back drink after drink.  He slapped Weehawk's shoulders companionably.  "But his name is best not talked about in here; he's hated everywhere and those that hear the name usually get upset and hold grudges towards the speaker."

    

     "Please," Weehawk said.  "I have to know.  He's the one who killed my father.  I am going to search him out, and kill him."

    

     Mance sighed.  "Buy me another drink, Wee-one, and I'll tell you."  But he smiled kindly when he said it.  When the drink came, Mance bent over slightly and whispered a name.  Weehawk's brows drew together.

    

     "But!" Mance said, holding up a finger.  "There is no way in Hell you will be able to fight him, or his followers, at you're level of power.  I would suggest you train for quite some time before undergoing that task!  Plus, you need good gear..." he knocked on his hard leather breastplate.  "It's what could mean life or death for you in a good battle."

    

     "Do you fight them alot?" Weehawk asked.

    

     "The Darkenbane Guild?  All the time," he smiled.  "That's where half of this fat loot comes from, you know."  Several of the bars' patrons scowled at Mance for his poor useage of terminology.  "Their a bunch of Wa-hoo's," Mance said, in a quieter tone.  He chuckled and slapped Weehawk on the back.  "Tell you what.  Tomorrow, I'll take you to the Guild of the Shadow Makers.  There, you will get the training you need.  I see something good in the making within you.  One day, like others, you could become a Shadow Gypsy.  The Elite!  And, if you choose, and proved yourself worthy, you could become one of the Marauder's...the Elite of the Elite!   What I like to call 'Leet'.  Heh heh.  Plus, you'll get to hang around me, which is probably great enough to..."  He trailed off into garbled mutterings as the alcohol finally set in.

    

     "Ma-raud-ers..."  Weehawk mouthed the word over and over.  His brow furrowed.  So, those that could find the guts to kill his father did this sort of thing, every day?  To anyone they could, whenever they pleased?  The cheapest, lowest, dirties kind of fighting there was...and from the sounds of it, this band of killers were some of the best at it.  He began to smile softly.  He would train, indeed.  He would become a viscious killer, as they were, and use their own tactics against them.  When they sat on the sun warmed rock to pracice their spells in peace, he would be behind them, creeping closer, a grin on his face.  When they were sitting around the end of the day fire, exchanging stories and drinking their drinks, they would feel the pain of a thousand stings as his swarms settled upon their unprepared bodies and ripped them assunder.  They would feel pain.  They would all feel his pain.  He would become a Marauder.  He would kill them...as many as he could kill, until he himself was left, lifeless, upon the ground.

 

     The next day, Weehawk became a Shadow Maker.  His mother roused herself from her sadness and actually smiled, tears of joy and pride in her eyes.  There, he met many others, and began travels of his own, which took him outside of Misty Thicket.

 

     Weehawk met his foes in a steadfast manner.  Many there were out there who knew druids, and knew their capabilities; but when the ruffians met this druid, who would not run, would not back down, would not give up, they were shocked and most times defeated, often in quite unpleasant manners, their last thoughts that of shame and confusion...and pain.  Many a time, Weehawk would hold his ground, battling until the end, refusing to give up, sometimes getting beaten to within an inch of his life.  Every piece of gear Weehawk sought, he set his mind to it.  It became his life quest.  Slowly, over the years, Weehawk became better and better equipped with finer and finer gear that put many a hobits' to shame.  He amassed wealth; money flowed into the Rivervale bank that made his mother's head shake in amazement.  He saw to it that his mother, though still sad and at a loss for her husband, was outfitted better than any family in the city.  In his travels, he contacted other families that bore his surname and donated to their causes with kindness and generosity.  And still he quested.  And of course, his power grew...and grew.  Creatures that used to pose troublesome threat to his elders he now strode through without fear.  He could destroy villages of orcs and goblins with the wave of his hand.  His fellow hobbits stared at him, often in awe, if not a bit fearful.  He searched for power like a thirsty human searches for water.  Those that stood in his way either gave to, or died.  His rage burned.

 

     On one quiet day, he sat alone in the Tavern and enjoyed a long conversation with two Shadow Gypsies that had just returned from battling a great dragon in a distant land.  After they had left, he sat quietly, mulling over his drink, when suddenly the door burst open and a huge...massive man strode in.  Behind this monster staggered his old friend, Mance.  Weehawk sat up, smiling.  Mance was no longer a 'Druid' to others.  He had amassed enough power to reach the status of 'Wanderer', a fabled rank and level of power amongst the Druids.  Even the Hobbits of Rivervale looked upon him in awe.  The massive, bearded fellow slammed the bar with his fist and shouted for drinks and food (none of that berries and roots crap), and though the patrons did not like him much, cleared a path for him readily and went about meeting his demands.

    

     Mance walked up, half drunk, and shook Weehawk's hand.  "How are you, buddy?  Man, you're looking good!  Where you been hiding?  Wanna buy me a drink?"

    

     They struck up conversation, and the large one turned out to be a Barbarian warrior from the North, named Warsyn.  Weehawk took a liking to the loudmouth right away, even though he kept trying to brush his pet pigeon, Toepecker, off the bar repeatedly.  He could eat over half his weight in food and drink almost twice that.  He was jovial and kindhearted, with a twinkle of mirth in his eye, but a quick sword arm and a heavy axe arm as well.  He and the pigeons finally called a truce and chose to simply eyeball each other.

 

     The bar was packed that night.  Later in the evening another of Mance's friends, a Ranger, named Gerlach, showed up, and they all sat about exchanging stories, drinking expensive ale.  On that night there was a large meeting in Rivervale of the Shadow Gypsies, and Weehawk was honored to meet their Leader.  The Shadow Makers were there as well.  When the time was announced to speak of the 'ridding of the land', as the SG/SM liked to call it, many looked about appreciatably and nodded in satisfaction at Weehawk's memory and recollection of names of those he had killed.  The list was quite long.  Mance snickered.  Inside, however, Weehawk burned with humiliation...for not a single one of these names...belonged to a 'Darkenbaner'. 

    

     The next day, a new crop of Gypsies was brought in, recruited from the Makers.  Weehawk was one of them, amongst Warsyn and Gerlach.  The following night was spent immersed in drink and fine women, with loud raucous music from the bards visiting from Qeynos.  Only the best for the SM/SG.

 

     The four friends stood atop a hill in the Karana's.  They maintained their normal pose, Mance and Gerlach poised thoughtfully to the sides, Warsyn towering behind them, practically bristling with weapons, Weehawk crouched in front, checking the ground for tracks.  There was a party of killers close by.  These were the worst kind of killers in the land.  What Mance simply called 'DB'. Mance said that he could smell them.  Gerlach claimed he could smell the ale they carried.  Warsyn just grunted and hefted his axe.  Weehawk straightened up, tense.  He had not encountered their Guild-kind yet, ever before.  This was one of the moments he had been searching for his whole life.  He fingered the tattered up piece of parchment he kept in one of his bags.  The names of his victims.

 

     They overtook the party mere hours later.  It was a group of casters, dark elves by the looks.  Unaccustomed to being chased for so long, they were weary from travel but very angry.  Some of their members were extremely powerful, and Weehawk gaped in awe as Mance exchanged knowing looks with them and attacked.  Those not as powerful became targets to Warsyn and Gerlach.  Feeling the rage of years past boiling over, Weehawk assumed the feared form of the wolf and attacked.  The battle raged.

 

     The magic burned from Weehawk's hands, fire reaching for the heaven's, but the spell fizzled and died, for the wizzard at his feet was already dead.  Weehawk's teeth were gritted, blood flowing from his mouth.  His fine garb was burned and tattered, his skin singed.  Everywhere his body ached.  He stared at the body at his feet.

 

     A massive frame hit the ground beside him.  Warsyn.  The great warrior sprawled out on his back, shouting his pleasure as he relaxed, allowing his weapons to slip from his bloodied hands.  "Ah, but that was fun."

 

     Nearby, Gerlach, his left leg slashed deeply but expertly bandaged, looking as torn up as the rest, leaned against his now cracked bow.  Used, finally, to the breaking point, it had served him well and slain many a foe.  He tore the cork from a wine bottle with his teeth and spit it out, then drank deeply.

 

     Weehawk stared at the corpse at his feet.

 

     Mance sauntered up, grinning, tying his money bag closed.  He eased himself down onto the grass beside them, clearly in pain also.  But they had given more than they had taken.  "Four down, many more to go," he said lightly.

 

     Weehawk stared at the corpse at his feet.  The spellcaster's face was frozen in shock and dismay.  Near one of his burned, outstretched hands, a backpack lay, burst open.  Jewelery and books lay jumbled on the ground.  Amongst them was a piece of armor.  A breastplate.  Softly, Weehawk spoke the name of the man he had just killed, the name of the man Mance had confided in him long ago.  Stepping over the corpse, he slowly picked up the breastplate, wiping the front of it with a piece of his tunic.  Scrolled into the front was a faint but still readable word: Pigeonslayer.  Carefully he placed it into his own backpack, and then sat with his friends amongst the corpses of those they had slain.

 

     "So you found that which you were looking for," Mance said, sharing some of the liquor Gerlach had recovered.  Weehawk nodded.  "You're game is complete, then.  You're revenge is done.  What will you do now?"

 

     Weehawk turned and eyed his companions.  Looking back, over the hills, he began to smile.  "I'm a Marauder.  What do you think I will do now?"

    

     "Keeel," Warsyn said, chuckling as he looked up into the sky from his rested position on his back.  He slugged Mance in the arm, demanding the bottle with an open, expectant hand.  Gerlach smiled.

 

     "I am Weehawk Pigeonslayer," Weehawk said.  "The Pigeons are my army."  His friends chuckled softly.  With the fighting over, the friendly birds were beginning to gather about the somber Hobbit once more.  "Where the killer's tread, there shall be no rest.  So says Mance Malhavoc, of Malhavoc's Marauder's."

    

     "Hear hear!" shouted Mance, and doused Weehawk with liquor.

 

 

     The years passed slowly, but to Weehawk, they seemed to fly by.  His power grew to immense proportions, to levels nearly as high as Mances'.  He attained the level of Wanderer easily when his time came, and assumed the shape of The Great Wolf with ease as well.  He watched his close friends grow and change in their ways as well, but through it all they remained Marauders.  He still saw his old friends from time to time, and travelled with many old friends of the Gypsies.  He encountered many of the killers along the roads and plains, and he killed them, almost with relish...and people grew to fear him.  Those that had wronged learned to look over their shoulder when they rested, or when they traveled down that dark path late at night.  Thought a joke at one time, the presence of pigeons now spooked many a person as they travelled their roads, or robbed their cities' banks, or overtook traveler's in far distant lands, because they all knew the story behind the quiet, cunning Halfling druid who kept the birds in his company.  Many had chosen to ignore this story, and had paid the price in their carelessness.

 

     Many thought it easy to thwart the Druid, to destroy them with powerful magic or with the blades of weapons.  But Weehawk quickly gained the reputation of the quiet killer, the one who cared not if he destroyed you in you're sleep, or released the binding magic on a creature as some random killer fought it, allowing the creature to charge in a slay the weakened caster.  One rumor that strayed back to the homelands was of a day when Weehawk met an old enemy on a cliff outcropping, who had stolen from their very homestead in Rivervale.

 

     Battered and bleeding, Weehawk knew as well as the cocky Mage before him that he could not 'outnuke' the Magician class as he so direly wished he could.  He finally called for peace to the Magician, and pledged his apology for attacking...whereas the Magician called Weehawk forth, to sit with him and break bread, perhaps, still unawares as to exactly who Weehawk was, or why he was really there...or what his true intentions were.  When close enough, Weehawk cast forth a powerful wind spell, and swept the Magician, screaming, over the edge, to where he fell thousands of feet and shattered on the rocks below.  Many were shocked to hear this rumor, but knowing the kindness that was in the Hobbit's heart, never chose to ask of the truth to the story. 

 

     Living to be quite old, Hobbits enjoy a lengthy, long life.  Weehawk, though young, became known amongst his closest allies as 'the ancient hobbit'.  To them, it appeared that Weehawk had somehow crammed three life spans of a hobbit into his one young, solitary life.  His mother, accepting in the end the loss of her husband, grew healthy and hearty once more, and re-married to a dashing little rogue who understood and accepted her for her loss and needs, and accepted and in a way was proud to be the 'father' of one Weehawk Pigeonslayer. 

 

     When Weehawk visits Rivervale, it is a day of festivity, and the elders come from far and wide to see the powerful little druid that they all remembered so well.  And the name of Pigeonslayer is once more becoming known throughout the land.  Respected by many, feared by many.  And a Shadow Gypsy he will always be...a Marauder.
