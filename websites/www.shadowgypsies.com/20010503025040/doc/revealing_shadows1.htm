Revealing Shadows Vol. 1



Drakken grumbled darkly at the reports on his desk. The armies of the dark races have again been mustered. Legions of young dark elves, trolls and ogres, attempting to storm towards Qeynos, for the glory of the dark gods. Such a useless conflict, it seemed. Used only relieve the mundanity of life.

"The gods were right. You are a strange troll." Drakken stood up, tipping his chair back, and reaching for a nearby weapon at the unexpected voice. Then he stopped in shock at the sight of a dark elf standing just inside the door, small even by dark elf standards.

"Who in Hades are you, little man. I do not like unwelcome visitors. Leave here before I decide your existence is useless." Drakken growled. The small elf smiled impudently.

"I think not." he said, as he made himself comfortable, "Unless, of course, you wish to never find that which you seek." Drakken stood motionless, in indecision, then finally sat down again.

"What could YOU possibly know about "The Way?" He asked. Something about this dark elf made Drakken glad he had not decided to fight him.

"Only that you must find a book, or books rather, for the information you seek. The gods look favorably on you and yours, though why, is beyond me." the little man said. Drakken raised an eyebrow in slight surprise.

"Just like that? Just find some books?" He asked, then chuckled. "When Lan had "The Way" revealed to him, he had to prove himself worthy of it. Why is that different now?" The little man laughed lightly.

"Oh, you will prove your worthiness, my ugly friend. For you see, Lan was not strong in body, yet to prove his worth, he had to do things that would require strength. You, on the other hand, are not strong in mind. Thus, you will have to do things which require that kind of strength." the little man answered. Drakken's countenance darkened, and his hands opened and closed, wishing for a weapon.

"Weak mind, am I? I who have learned under Lan, and who has proven himself worth of knighthood, then was given the honor of being Aegis, and now to lead those Shadow Gypsies which remain? Weak minded?" Drakken lashed out with a huge fist, and promptly was thrown back by a bright flash of light. The little man now stood, looking angry.

"I would NOT do that again, Drakken Soulblighter. I do not wish to be the ones to tell the gods I killed you because you could not keep yourself under control" He said, his voice deadly quiet. Drakken knew fear then. Not the fear of war, or of conflict, but the fear of being horribly outclassed in sheer power.

"Well then, I guess the rest of the information I have to give you will have to wait, since you are not ready yet. Your petty outburst of anger has shown me that!" he said. Drakken started to get a sick feeling in his stomach.

"Wait! I am ready!" Drakken exclaimed. The little man merely smiled, as he did when he first entered.

"I think not." He said, as he vanished.
