Thebouy Ge'Zaire
Halfling Druid

The wind that blew through the Thicket was cold and Thebouy tugged his cloak around himself for warmth.  He had been hiding now for three days and his stomach growled. He ate another root that he had managed to find amongst the fallen trees just outside the guards wall.  He heard the familiar chitter of the beetles as they scurried about the Thicket and the call of his friends the wolves.

It was three days since the Rivervale Beer Festival and three days since Bouy had blown his chances to be the Watchman his family had dreamed he would be.  For three years he had worked hard to build up his reputation and standing in the Vale and he was sure that after the Festival that Sheriff Roglio was going to name him as one of the new recruits. The Thicket Guard had been relaxed to allow as many people to enjoy the fun, The Sheriff had commanded no one to engage the Goblins in the hope that they would remain quiet during the festival.  After the first few hours of the festival, and many beers on, he had joined other hopefuls in the Thicket they had decided to prove their worth by collecting flowers for the Sheriff's Beautiful daughter Mylina.

 

Thebouy had bounded off into the Thicket to the spot he knew held the rarest and most beautiful roses, as he arrived at the spot he sensed that something was wrong.  Ahead he could hear the harsh grunts of Runnyeye Goblins. It looked like as the Watch had been relaxed, to allow as many hobbits as possible to make the Festivities, that a large Runnyeye war party had formed ready to attack the Vale. Just to his left Bouy noticed one of his fellow recruits, Gumble, sneaking through the bushes unaware that he was heading straight towards the Goblin horde.  Bouy did the only thing he could, springing out of the bush he was hiding in he shouted at the Goblins. "Here I am! Come and get me!" The Goblins turned towards him and their leader screamed for them to catch him before he could get to the Watchmen.  Bouy fled towards the Vale he was pursued by the snarls of the goblins and the occasional arrow.  He stopped for a moment just before a small clearing of trees and hid behind a tree, as the first Goblin reached him he readied himself.  As the Goblin rounded the tree Bouy stuck out his foot and tripped the green skin, who fell sprawling into a tree stump. Another Goblin bounded into the clearing and swung his rusted axe towards Bouy. Thebouy dived at the Goblin's legs, and it fell under his assault.  Thebouy realised that the Goblin was not moving, he stood gingerly, the Goblin lay with his axe buried deep into it's neck.  Looking around Bouy realised that the main body of the goblin host was headed his way.


He saw the entrance to the Vale and a few people outside, he shouted at the lounging Watchmen that the Goblins were coming.  The goblin host poured out of the Thicket towards Rivervale, the hobbits ran in fear towards the safety of the Vale.  The Watch though caught off guard were quick to respond and soon the battle to defend the Vale was going their way, as more Goblins fell under the Hobbits wraith.   Sheriff Roglio was surveying the damage to the homes outside the vale and the fallen hobbits, his face was full of anger. "Why did the Goblins attack us today who caused this?" The Sheriff bellowed, as he did Gumble came out of the Thicket "It was Thebouy he led the Goblins on the Vale, I tried to draw then away back towards the Thicket but Bouy kept shout for them to catch him!" Gumble stood up straight and tall.  "I managed to slay to of the greenskins trying to draw them away from the vale, I can show you the clearing!" The Sheriff walked over and patted Gumble's shoulder, "Your bravery will not go unrewarded, Recruit", Gumble beamed at Roglio.  At this moment Thebouy stumbled out of one of the decimated holes nearby.  Before Bouy could defend himself the Sheriff advanced upon him shouting.

 

"You are a disgrace to Hobbit kind Thebouy!  You directly disobeyed my commands and entered the Thicket and managed to stir up the Goblins to attack!"

 

Bouy tried desperately to get his side of the story across but it was too late, he had blown his chances of being in the watch, as the Sheriff would not hear what he had to say. Mournfully Thebouy wandered into the Thicket, he had no hope for his future now. So that was three days ago and now he sat under the cover of Thicket wondering what to do with his life.  Suddenly ahead of his a figure appeared ahead of him, taller than a hobbit and much more lean.  Bouy thought this might be one of the Wood Elves he had dreamed of meeting.  The Elf turned to him and smiled.

 

"Oerellius is my name small one. I was in the Thicket and saw what happened, it wasn't your fault young hobbit."  Thebouy managed to find a smile for the ranger.

 

"Looks like I have to leave the Vale now and follow a new Path, I just don't know what to do." Bouy stammered, Oerellius smiled and put his arm around Bouy's small shoulders.

 

"I have a possible answer to your problem, you could join my Friends the Shadow Makers.  They are young adventures who fight against the evil of Norrath.  If one day you become a veteran you can join the elite few who make up the Shadow Gypsies."  Thebouy stared in wonder he had see some of the great adventurers from the Gypsies in the Vale.

 

"Would you like to join me and my Band of friends we will be your family now?"  The Kindly Ranger said.  Bouy Nodded.
