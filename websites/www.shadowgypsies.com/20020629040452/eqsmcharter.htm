Recruiting Policy

Membership level requirement is 10.

Process

An interested member must post on the 'SM Membership Request' Forum. The request for membership should include level and class of the character. An Officer will then nominate him for membership on the Officer’s board. After some discussion and debate, the prospect will either be granted access or denied access. If denied, a reason will be posted on the original request thread.

New guild members – there will be a one week probationary period for prospective members. If the prospective member gets a minimum of three referrals from current members, the probationary period will be waived.

Member's Conduct

General

1. Treat everyone with respect and maturity. Act only as an adult.

2. Members will not deliberately train others. If a train is being pulled to zone, at least one of the group members must forewarn others of the train. (/ooc Train to zone)

3. Do not deliberately kill steal from other players.

4. Do not divulge information about Guild activities to non-members.

5. Always wear your guild-tag (unless in situations like raids to prevent lag).

6. Guild Members will respect and obey the advice of Guild Officers and Guild Leader at all times. There will be no arguing in guild talk once an order has been given. Any deviation from this rule is grounds for expulsion. If you disagree with recommendations provided by one Officer, you may contact that Officer, another Officer, or the guild Leader through /tells.

7. No cursing outside of guild chat channels. It's very immature and reflects badly upon Shadow Makers as a whole.

8. You are also expected to follow the rules set forth by Verant.

PKs

1. Intentionally healing, buffing, or helping PKs will be grounds for guild removal.

2. Members will not randomly PK an individual. (A random PKer is one who kills for pleasure or profit without just cause.)

3. It is a member’s responsibility to confirm if a player/guild is KOS through guild chat.

4. A member will not kill someone he/she is grouped with (unless it is self defense).

Guild Chat

1. Guild Chat is used as a method of communication. Continued swearing/cursing, complaining, attacking other members will not be tolerated. Language in the heat of a battle or death is understandable; however, continued daily complaining or swearing is grounds for possible guild removal or guild warnings.

2. Don’t ignore guild chat – one day you may need to summon help from the guild. Everyone sees that bright green type or what ever color you chose. You may not be able to respond at that moment, but you do know how to use the mouse and scroll back.

3. Hail your guild mates when they log on and hail the guild.

Guild Website and Relaying Information

1. Events, meetings, and raids are posted on the message boards in their appropriate categories. Members should regularly visit these forums as they are the best source of information on what's going on in SG.

2. We will also update the guild message of the day; however, due to time restraints, it is the member’s responsibility to check the forums for scheduled events.

3. Reading/Posting regularly on the forums is essential to being a key part of this guild. This is where you'll find out how to help your guild mates, post to have them help you, gain some knowledge along the way, and make new friends. Don't ignore communication's potential.

Help From the Guild

1. Part of the benefit in being a guild member is that you can receive help from other members.

2. The main help you will receive is in having access to experienced players that can advise you on almost anything you may need to know about EQ, and if our members don't know, they will probably be able to tell you where you can find out the information you need.

3. You will also benefit from regularly accessing the website to see what is going on and gaining EQ information, from attending guild events and from grouping regularly with other guild members.

4. While it is expected that guild members will help each other wherever possible, please bear in mind the following guidelines:

Items and Begging: Persistent begging is unacceptable. Item loot will follow the same rules posted under the loot section of the Raid Rules.

Power Leveling: If a twink is in the guild, it is not reasonable to expect higher level guild members to make themselves available regularly for power leveling (they are trying to level or may be doing quests or fighting in a group).

Teleports: A druid's or wizard's time is important just as yours is to you, and they also like to play the game, level, etc... Therefore, while they may be willing to teleport, please do not pester them as if they are a taxi service.

Resurrections: Just like the porters in the guild, a cleric's time is just as valuable to him/her. Our clerics are all more than willing to help a fellow SG out with a Rez. However, if you need to get a rez, it is your responsibility to work out a time that works for the both of you. Don't expect clerics to drop what they're doing immediately because you've waited until there's twenty minutes left on your corpse before a rez is no longer available. As well, it is your responsibility to help the cleric in any way you can by dragging corpses (when possible) and helping to arrange for ports.

Guild Expulsion

1. If a guild member's behavior breaks these guidelines and this is witnessed by a guild member, removal may be immediate.

2. Please note that, if you have more than one EQ character, these guidelines may apply to only or all characters who are members of this guild.

Process of Removal:

1. A thread started by an Officer on the Officer Message board calling for the removal of the member. Discussion of the removal will be presented in the thread with reasons why guild removal is necessary. The guild member may also receive a warning that additional actions would result in expulsion.

i. Members may be removed by any Guild Officer.

ii. Guild Officers may only be removed by the Guild Leader.

2. If you are removed by an Officer and you want to rejoin Shadow Makers, the Officers will vote on the character rejoining the guild.

3. If you remove yourself then want to come back, fine. Once is enough, twice is overkill, three times, don’t expect to be rejoined.

4. Members will have access to the webboards for one week after they are removed.

NOTE: It may be necessary for an Officer to remove a member for guild relationships while clarifying a misunderstanding. Once all facts have been presented, the member may be re-invited back into the guild.

Guild Alliance

Shadow Makers will provide protection, assistance on guild raids, and grouping buddies. Our allies will do the same. Please tell an Officer when you have a problem with an ally or an ally does not help you. Get the name of the person and the problem you are having. PLEASE do not do anything to jeopardize the guilds relationship with one of our allies. Things can always be worked out peacefully.

Guild Wars

1. If the guild should be involved in a guild war, all players are expected to play their part as far as they are able.

2. During a war, rules on player killing are waived with regard to members of the enemy guild only.

Officership

1. Promotion to Officer is based on the following:

i. Need of more officers in the guild for whatever reason.

ii. Guild participation (including but not limited to message boards, SG raids, ROS raids, fellow guild assistance)

iii. Maturity of the individual

iv. Adherence to the Guild Charter

2. Nominations require one Officer to nominate and another Officer to second.

3. Promotion and demotions require a majority number of Officer votes.

Officer Duties:

i. Recruit new members.

ii. Remove members who have violated the guild charter.

iii. Organize raids and participate in events

iv. Help maintain the KOS list.

v. Support guild members however needed.

Loss of Rank:

i. Violation of duties: Officers may be stripped of their rank by the Guild Leader immediately and without question if said Officer has violated the terms of their duties. In such a case, the Guild Leader is simply required to post notice on the member’s message board and that the Officer had been stripped of title with a recounting of the incident leading to the removal of title. A more elaborate post may also be made on the Officers’ message board.

ii. Justified Cause: The Guild Leader does not have the authority to strip an Officer of rank without cause. Cause is determined in the same way as cause for ‘Removal of Membership.’ If any Member feels an Officer should be stripped of their rank, a message must be posted, discussion held, and a vote taken requiring a strong majority of all Officers voting in the affirmative that an Officer should lose rank. A situation may occur where the Guild Leader may feel it is necessary to strip the rank of an Officer on the spot. If such a situation occurs, the Guild Leader must post an accounting of the situation with the reasons for stripping the title, at the earliest opportunity. A discussion and vote will then be held, and if the vote by the Officers does not confirm the Guild Leader's decision, that Officer shall have his rank restored.

iii. AWOL: Those who do not log on for 15 day consecutive days and posses a/an Officer position will be demoted on the 16th day to regular member status in the guild. Logging on simply saying hello is not acceptable. You must interact with the guild. Those who communicate their absence will be waived from these actions. YOU MUST BE AN ACTIVE PLAYER TO KEEP YOUR OFFICER POSITION!

Guild Fund

It is completely by choice of a member to donate to this fund or not. However, members that do donate and follow the guild charter do get recognized.

This fund is used to help members with coffins (when needed), trade skills, help people who cannot afford spells, etc...

If you choose to use the Guild Fund to help you with a Trade Skill you must train to MASTER level before using the guild fund for your next Trade Skill. (NEW MEMBERS CAN NOT USE THE FUND UNTIL THEY HAVE BEEN IN THE GUILD FOR 30 DAYS - DOES NOT APPLY TO ALTERNATE CHARACTERS.)
