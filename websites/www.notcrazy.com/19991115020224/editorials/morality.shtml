The guild that I belong to, the Noble Lions, are a good guild, active in Ultima Online (UO). Good in as much as we try to behave in a fashion that benefits the majority of players. Also good in that we are fairly active in opposing evil players and guilds.

Being good in UO actually isn't that hard. There are a few tough moral issues we have faced (the toughest was whether we allow guild members to play evil characters in addition to their good characters, we said no, not on the shard that we are active in). For most players, the basic test of morality is: do you kill other players without cause? Those who kill without cause are labeled PKers. Those who do not are called Non-PKers. In general this has been a reasonable dividing line and it has come to represent good vs. evil. However, the world of EverQuest (EQ) seems to offer a much more complicated moral landscape than UO. The key element that makes EQ more complex is the addition of true races.

In the real world, it is now a widely accepted fact that the supposed races of humanity (usually referred to by skin color such as white, black, yellow, and red) are in-fact nothing more than skin color. The degree of genetic variation between people of the same race is actually identical to the genetic variation between people of different races. In other words, genetically speaking, race as it is commonly understood today, has no basis in fact.

If we accept this genetic definition of race, then the idea that there should be NO discrimination against humans with different skin colors seems reasonable (to me at least). In a very real sense all humans are distant cousins, appearently descendants of a common ancestor from about 100,000 years ago.

What does this have to do with EverQuest (EQ)? In EQ, players are able to play a number of different races: Humans, Erudites, Barbarians, Wood Elves, High Elves, Half Elves, Dark Elves, Dwarves, Gnomes, Halflings, Ogres, and Trolls. I am assuming that a number of these races really are different races in a meaningful sense. The obvious exceptions are: Humans-Erudites-Barbarians; Dwarves-Gnomes and Wood Elves-Half Elves-High Elves-Dark Elves. In other words, it is not obvious that the Humans and Erudites are different races, nor is it clear that Dark Elves and High Elves are different races. For the purposes of this discussion I will assume that all Humans are of the same race, Dwarves and Gnomes are the same, and all Elves are of the same race.

This leaves 6 playable races in the game: Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Halflings, Ogres, and Trolls.

What are the moral implications of harming members of another race? This issue has come up most often in science fiction, where many writers have assumed that humans will come into contact with alien races. In the Star Trek galaxy and the Babylon 5 galaxy, the assumption has been that all life, especially thinking life, is morally at the same level. In effect the people who created these galaxies (Gene Rodenberry & Joe Michael Straczinky respectively) argue that different races all have the same rights, no race is superior to another, no race is inferior.

In other science fiction stories, notably in the novel Starship Troopers by Heinline, there was a real sense that some races, due to their behavior, occupied morally different planes. The bugs in Heinline's story were suitable for nuclear annihilation, the reason being it was either them or us. Heinline was neither the first nor the last science fiction writer to suggest this idea, I'm merely using his example because, with the recent movie, more people are likely to know what I'm talking about.

Where does EQ fit into this range of possible attitudes? I argue that in a world with limited resources (and almost by definition, all worlds have limited resources, exceptions being Ring Worlds or Dyson Spheres), conflict between races which compete directly for land, food, or goods is both reasonable and moral. The key here is that there are limited resources which multiple races require for survival.

War Between Ogres and Dwarves - A Hypthetical Example Let us assume that Ogres and Dwarves both eat the same food (sheep). Lets assume that they both like to live in the same places (mountains). Lets assume they both desire the same rare mineral (gold). Seeing as there are limited quantities of sheep, mountains, and gold, neither the Dwarves nor the Ogres are going to be happy about the inevitable competition between the two races. They have two options open to them: conflict or compromise. But what exactly do they have to compromise about? It is a zero sum game. Whatever one side gains, the other side loses. (I am assuming that no cooperative efforts on the part of the Dwarves and Ogres can result in increasing the quantity of mountainous terrain, gold, or sheep). Do the Dwarves and Ogres share a common language, culture, history, tradition, or relations that would lead them to consider compromise? Compromise is hard for humans, even when we share all these things. In the case of truly different races, compromise is highly unlikely.

What about morality? Why (for example) it is moral for the Dwarves to attempt to destroy the Ogres? This comes from the concept of natural law.

Natural Law - Survival
The bedrock principle of morality is based on survival. When you get to the bottom of things you find that everyone agrees that life is preferable to death. But all living things die. So survival must take on a deeper meaning. It is not just personal survival that is important, but the survival of one's family, and by extension, one's race. As a human, if I was forced to choose between the survival of ANY OTHER species and the human race, I would choose the human race. Even if another species somewhat limited the possible expansion of the human race, I would strongly consider elimination of the limiting species.

Returning to Ogres and Dwarves example: because they are in direct competition for the same resources and living space, the two races directly impinge upon each other. A war of annihilation between them is certainly reasonable. I argue that it is also moral because continued survival and expansion by one race directly threatens the continued survival and expansion of the other race.

I am aware of the fact that the Nazi's (and other would-be genocidal monsters) have used these same sorts of arguments. The problem is: they were wrong! Humans are all the same. There was no racial difference between the Germans and the German Jews. Nor is there any racial difference between the Tutsis and the Hutus in Rwanda. Nor is there any racial difference between the Bosnian Croats, the Bosniacs, and the Serbs. All humans are of one race.

Note that I am not saying genocide is the highest moral level. It is not the best possible option. In an ideal world, the Ogres and Dwarves would be able to live together in harmony by careful agreements, making sure neither race expanded at the cost of the other race. This would require a very high degree of communication and trust between the two races. Considering the wars that have erupted in human communities that share a common language, culture, skin-color, and generations of family ties, conflict between Ogres and Dwarves, who have none of these commonalities, seems inevitable. Should such wars be condemned as immoral? I do not think so. If true, is there any behavior that would be immoral? Sadly, nothing occures to me. I'm not sure that the word morality has any meaning in this context....

War is not necessary between all races All races need not fight wars of genocide. Take another hypothetical example: Trolls and Wood Elves. Assume that Trolls like to live in swamps, while Wood Elves only like to live in forests. Assume that Trolls only eat snails, frogs, and other swamp dwelling creatures while Elves prefer to eat deer, acorns, and fruits. Lastly, assume that Elves like nothing more than to play songs and make up poems, while Trolls prefer to lie in mud baths all day long, counting the number of flies they have eaten since breakfast. There is no reason why these two races should ever come into conflict (until the Elves decide they need to expand their favorite forest by draining the near-by marsh). Put simply: Trolls and Wood Elves are not in competition with each other over resources. Consequently they don't impinge on each other and so conflict between them is pointless and irrational.

In David Brin's science fiction, he has speculated on the existence of Hydrogen-based life. He further suggested that the Oxygen breathing races and Hydrogen breathing races could live in the same solar systems without bothering one another.

Historical Parallel
Archeology tells us that there was a time when both Humans and Neaderthals lived in the same places. Neaderthals are considered a different, though closely related species of Humans. Some recent genetic studies strongly suggest that the Neaderthals died out utterly. The assumption is, given our violent history, that humans fought a true race war of annihilation against the Neaderthals, and won. Speaking as a human, I cannot argue that it was immoral for us to have survived, even if it was at the expense of another species. Again, it wasn't the best thing that could have happened, but it was a lot better than the likely alternative (extinction for humans).

Parallels in Literature
The concept of race war is rather obviously at the heart of all Tolkien-inspired fantasy. In The Lord of the Rings, the Orcs, servants of Sauron, are irredeemable. It is a good thing to kill Orcs whenever possible and if all the Orcs were wiped out, none of the characters in the story would be unhappy. The situation was painted in quite stark terms, either the Orcs win, or the Free Peoples (Humans, Elves, Dwarves, & Hobbits) win. Painting the situation in this black/white fashion is typical of the hundreds of fantasy novels published since LotR.

Conclusion
Based on these examples, it is clear to me that a good, moral guild can choose an enemy race and declare their mission is the destruction of that race. The simple world of UO (Player Killers vs. Non-Player Killers) is completely obscured in the world of EQ. Two guilds with identical moral codes of good behavior can easily be total enemies to each other just because of their racial composition.

Some Minor Issues in EQ
Q: Why Don't Elves Fight with Humans?
A: The easy answer to this question is that Elves and Humans really are one race. The proof for this statement is the existence of Half Elves. The fact that humans and elves can interbreed is almost as unlikely as Human-Vulcan interbreeding (I refer to the oft stated fact that in the Star Trek universe, Mr. Spook is half-Vulcan, half-human). However, given the existence of Half Elves, it is easy to see why Humans and Elves are able to live together without engaging in wars of genocide. Survival for the Humans is, in effect, the same thing as survival for the Elves. These two races are not actually separate races and so there is no rational competition between them. Of course, you can say the same thing about humans, there is no rational explanation for wars of genocide between humans, but it happens with depressing regularity in the real world.

Q: Why Don't Humans Fight with Halflings
A: Now this I can't answer. Assuming that Halflings are not just pigmy humans but are a separate race, there is every reason to think that Humans and Halflings are in conflict with one another over the same resources.

Tolkien (who invented Hobbits, the obvious source for Halflings) invented a very strange world. The key oddity to Middle Earth is that it is a world with declining populations (with the exception of Orcs). The population of Hobbits might be expanding slightly, but the numbers of men, elves, and dwarves is declining and has been for several hundred years. This means that far from competing with the other races for the same pool of shrinking resources, the Men, Elves, Dwarves, and Hobbits must cooperate to destroy the Orcs, before the Orcs over-run the land.

Tolkien's world bears no relation to anything seen in human history. In the real world, the total human population has been always been gradually rising. Since the 1800s the Human population has skyrocketed. FYI: It is estimated that over the last 100,000 years, some 25-30 billion humans have lived. About one fifth of the total human population is alive today!

For the record, I venture to guess that Hobbits (and by extension, Halflings) are pigmy humans. In other words, they too are part of the large human super-race. If I'm correct, this leaves EQ with just 4 races: the Elf-Human-Halfling race, the Dwarves-Gnome race, the Ogres, and the Trolls.

I'm talking here about a game set in a virtual world with its own internal logic. This is a thought experiment based on my understanding of the logic of this virtual world.
