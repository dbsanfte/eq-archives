Make sure you touch something metal especially if you live in an area that gets really dry in the winter. One shock from the finger tip could take out your motherboard. I've seen it happen to many people back in my bestbuy service tech days.

You may also want to install any of the software that came with your old video card.

Hubby is a systems administrator and we always use a staic electricity wrist strap when we do anything inside our computers. But, if you don't have one of those, you can hold onto the side of case (metal parts). Not as safe, but safer than nothing. Also, always a good idea to not be standing on carpet (or have your computer sit directly on the carpet). Carpet can kick up a lot of static electricity that can run straight through your socks, and out your finger tips.

Static electricity can fry your computer and its components even when you don't feel a shock of any sort. Never, never handle the components extensively. Try to always grasp them by the edges, touching as little of the component as possible and avoid handling the gold strip (part that actually slides into the AGP slot).

I've seen brand new components fry from simple handling and I've seen entire systems fry (quite literally...sounded like fried eggs cooking) from pushing the power button and picking up static electricity from the capret at the same time.
