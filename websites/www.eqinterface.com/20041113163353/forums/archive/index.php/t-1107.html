I've checked out a few of the existing mods for text inclusive effect boxes. Definitely nifty, but the problem I had with it is the fact that it takes up quite a bit of space. (The current effect box is pretty compact, as you might have noticed).

I'm already using what I like to consider the maximum amount of space for information. I like to keep a wide horizontal as well as moderate vertical range of vision... it's often critical to know what is going on around me both solo and in groups. I'm semi-often a healer groups depend on, as well as crowd-control in certain situations using ensnare/root methods if no Enchanter/Bard is present or assisting if they become temporarily overwhelmed. Druid is my primary character, and a druid that sits back throwing around dots and an epic click here and there is... well... lazy. Uh.. back to the subject, sorry! ;)

I could increase my resolution to have more area to work with, but I prefer 1024 x 768. Due in part to performance issues, compatibility with not so state-of-the-art systems, and plain old visibility. At higher resolutions the extra space really becomes counter-productive, as it gets more difficult to read/see items clearly without enlarging them and thus eating the space you just created while simultaneously reducing framerate and response time.

Ok.... so, enough about what I don't like - and time for what I had been toying around with as an *alternative solution* (because I really do like the idea of being able to see a list of current effects, as well as for some other things).

Here's the idea...
Replacing another window with an effect list (possibly other info as well, resists or the sort)... an on demand pop-up list of what buffs-debuffs are currently effecting your character. That way you can hit a key, see what you have, and close it again - keeping your view at a maximum when you don't need such verbose information. I'm semi-sure it's possible to do, though I have not actually had time to test it.

What do you think? I'd been pondering it on and off for a while now, your post brought it back into focus (thanks Skarlett). I'll play with the interface a bit and see if it works, and how helpful it would actually, be as soon as I get some time.

There's always room for improvement. Discovering how, that is the real challenge.
~Tellgar

any way you could add an invite and disband for group maybe under the prim/sec hand slot?

Probably not. There's only so much space to work with, I like to keep things compact and the buttons really aren't needed. You can invite and disband groups/members by pressing CTRL+I (Invite/Join) and CTRL+D (Disband/Decline). I use those two key combinations for the group functions, so having a button for them just takes up space I'd rather use for something else.

It never hurts to ask though, just explaining why they don't exist right now - and probably won't in the future. I did reserve some slack space in the hotbutton window (you'll notice a small blank area under the mini inventory slots) for future use - maybe something to do with the charm slot eh? :D

As always suggestions are welcome, I'm always looking for ways to make the UI more compact without sacrificing function. I love having as much at a click of the cursor or a keystroke as I can, but when the UI totally surrounds the view of the game world or encroaches on the view to much, it makes me feel crowded and uncomfortable.

Thanks for all the compliments also. :)

-- Short note about the current status of the interface--

I'm fairly happy with the current state of the UI. It's compact enough to run at 1024x768 or more with ample field of view in both first person and external camera. Even those who are using it at 800x600 seem to be happy.

I've been contemplating the hotbutton window a bit - mainly the mini-inventory next to the actual hotbuttons. I use it (though not as much as I had)... and was possibly considering trimming it down to just the main weapon slots and the last two main inventory slots. This for reasons of reducing the amount of screen coverage used for information display. I'm not exactly sure though, as I do like having the inventory there for easy access without having to open another window. Any suggestions, comments, or input is welcome. (Do you use it, do you hate it, do you love it and wouldn't change it at all? etc.) For now I'm leaving it as is in the working version and fiddling with it on my spare time.

I've also been thinking of adding small numerical values next to the spell cast icons on the spell casting bar, and possibly the buff status window (while trying to keep them around their current size). I find myself having to manage my buffs more and more, particularly during raids. So many buffs, only 15 slots... my current method was to 'size' the buff window horizontally so that only the 15 allowed will fit. Thus I know when I am running out of room for buffs, and can easily cancel non-critical effects or organize them efficiently for encounters against debuffing mobs and the sort. Again, any input on this (good idea/bad idea) is welcome from those that currently use it).

That's it for now,
