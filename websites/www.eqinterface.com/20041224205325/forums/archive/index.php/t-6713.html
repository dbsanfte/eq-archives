Few notes on creating ui's:

I think the major part of a UI is the Functionality of it, then skinning it with something unique. Alot of mods I see become way too graphic-orientated thus lessens the use of it, hense clutter. Although there are a lot of mods that have a ton of graphics, yet the functionality was still intact. =)

Trying to find a good medium is difficult, thus I would suggest drawing one up 1st and re-vamping it on paper before you start one, it will save time in the long run. Try also to look at the mod as if you were a pure melee, and a pure caster. It should be user-friendly to all classes if you can. Another thing to try is .. never use or create a custom EQUI_Animations file (or Templates). This will cause your mod to break and add stress to the questions "so wheres the patch?". Anything you make that is Custom Code, just put that in the window you are working on.

As for the graphics, that is really up to you. There is no "place" where you get them, perhaps try drawing them if you are good at that sort of thing. Adobe can reallly make a stick-figure look like a beautiful object =). Its all a matter of "what do you want the skin to look like?" Then search the net for ideas of the objects you need. You could browse around the net for misc sites that have 256 x 256 backgrounds and such. There are plenty of sites out there with "free graphics".

Hope this helps and Good luck to you! Looking forward to see what you come out with =)
