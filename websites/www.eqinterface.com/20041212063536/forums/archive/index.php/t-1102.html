Okay - continue using your second method... just putting the new files in another folder, then allowing EQ to grab what it needs from the default.

But the problem is that it changed the EQUI.xml file. That's the file that tells EQ what to load. If you'll open that file that belongs with your custom UI up in Notepad (right click on it then choose open with, if it tries to open with Explorer first). Now scroll to the bottom of the file. There should be a list of stuff saying: <Include>...an xml file...</Include>

At the bottom of that list, right under the last <Include></Include>, add the line:
<Include>CompassWnd.xml</Include>

Then when you load, EQ will know it needs to load that window when you load your UI. And when you hit alt+D again, EQ won't freak out because it's looking for something that doesn't exist.

That should fix your problem. :)

~Lady Silver
