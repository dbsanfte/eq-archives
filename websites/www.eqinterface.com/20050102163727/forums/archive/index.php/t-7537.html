When you download a UI, the author could have zipped it in ones of two ways:

1st - all the files were just zipped

2nd - all the files were zipped under a directory

What this means is this: Here is your everquest directory:

\everquest\uifiles

In here is the default directory:

\everquest\uifiles\default

So if you made a directory called "quin" the contents of the \everquest\uifiles directory should contain

default
quin

Now, if your skin was zipped in the fist way and you unzipped the file into that directory, it would look like this:

\everquest\uifiles\quin

file1
file2
file3
file4
etc, etc, etc

However, if the files are zipped into a directory... it will look like this... pay attention to the \directory structure.

\everquest\uifiles\quin\quin

file1
file2
file3
file4
etc, etc, etc

This means all the files are now under another directory. So when you type /load quin in everquest, it looks in the \everquest\uifiles\quin directory and sees nothing because the files are acutally in the \everquest\uifiles\quin\quin diretory.

All you have to do is move the files from \everquest\uifiles\quin\quin to the \everquest\uifiles\quin directory.

Keep in mind i do not know your drive or folder names here and I am using examples. The \\ stuff may not match your system exactly.
