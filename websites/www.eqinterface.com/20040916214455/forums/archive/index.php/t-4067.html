Now that you've rounded out the pages a bit, try smoothing that sharp line in the middle. Think about a real book for a second- there's no thick line between the pages, the left page just curves down into the binding and the right one curves up and out right next to it. The division is part of the natural gradation of shadows as the pages curve down to meet each other. If you make the line itself very thin and light, like one pixel, in just a darker version of the color of the pages themselves, then just make it so the shadow on each page reaches down to touch the line...

also if you're trying to make it look real, have an idea of where the light on the pages is coming from. The book itself may be exactly symmetrical, but imagine a light source, say above the right edge- the left side of the left page might be a little darker, then the right side would be brighter because the light would be shining directly at it, the left side of the right page would be quite dark because of the curve of the pages, then the right side would be brightest of all.

It's beautiful btw, infinitely better than anything I could ever do.
