In about 6mo to early 05' All new Types of things are going to change ( New P5 coming out, ATX form factors will be the new BTX form factor, and even a new windows OS "Longhorn" in early 05). So if you can wait for the new Mobo's chips, ram, ect to come out then wait. Your system should do fine. Update ALL your hardware, and system drivers, then go buy a top shelf CPU, FSB, RAM, and GPU cooling system (a lot less then a full up grade) Call a friend who knows how to overclock w/ out killing things =) or even better spend a day learning how your self. you should be able to get close to a 3.0ghz w/ 3200 ram speed, also it will speed up the FSB to about 1ghz, and your GPU's speed too. In the end you'll spend WAY less time and money.

However, if you a rich student (is there such a thing?) I would start by upgrading your Mobo to a 875 or "Canterwood" board, don't go cheep on the mobo you really get what you pay for. Also the 5600 nvidia is a mid level card that should be more then enough (if you have $500 grab a ATI 9800) Another thing is since EQ is SOOO large a faster HD will help, Finding a Raid 0 SATA set up with 10,000rpm + and a 8mb cache will be a nice step up (and will still be in use with the new systems). Dual channel DDR is super fast and will feed your CPU all it wants and more look for the 4200 ddr2 ram (hard to find look on www.pricewatch.com) for uber status. If youhave a lame sound sound card .. throw it out the window, buy a SB Audigy plat pro. Make sure your Modem is hardware and not software run, Software modems use your CPU for help where as a Hardware Modem does not.

Make sure you Defrag weekly, and clean out all the programs, movies, mp3s, windows tools and anything else you don't use anymore. Also stop anything from booting up w/ windows that does not HAVE to be there.

With those overclocks, or updates, you will be able to play even EQ2 with every option and max make-me-cry-its-so-beautiful setting turned on.
