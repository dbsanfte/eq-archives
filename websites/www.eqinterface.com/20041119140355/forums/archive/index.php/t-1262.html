I posted some containers that fix this in an odd way, you might check out how I did my stay-put containers.

The container window puts the combine and done buttons where it wants them. It centers them in the original window-width and makes them just a few pixels below the last container slot. That's easily fixed by switching to AutoStretch. Go into the button code and remove the location and size sections. Add a line to make AutoStretch true and add lines for LeftAnchorOffset, TopAnchorOffset, etc. I recommend that you anchor them to the bottom of of the window, not the top, since each container is a different height.

The container window also automatically resizes itself. That's a little trickier, and explains why you get the gaps. For width, it makes the window just barely wide enough to hold all the content, and then adds 14. Autostretch items attached to the right side don't count, so you can actually have your buttons and some of your inventory slots flush with the right side of the window if you make them autostretch.

For height, it depends on a few factors. As near as I can tell it makes the window just barely tall enought to hold all the content then adds 32, then adds 4 plus the height of the done button. Then if there's a combine button it adds another 4 plus the height of the combine button. The maximum height, then, is (contents + both button heights + 40). The border and titlebar shift the window contents down a bit so you don't normally see 36 pixels of blank space at the bottom, but it's computed that way even if StyleTitleBar and StyleBorder are set to false. When the buttons are autostretch they count as zero height.

Autostretch buttons are re-drawn after the window is widened and heightened, so they can be made to fill those 4-pixel gaps you see.

For a simpler solution, you could make a new window type for the containers that has a custom background image. That background image could just happen to look like a dark grey background with two silver bars. That would fill the gaps in a purely cosmetic way.
