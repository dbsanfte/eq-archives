On a quick peak of your file and based on numerous other people I have helped from my site that had similar problems I am going to suggest a couple things to try first before I get into deeper aspects of coding issues. It may or may not be allowable to code a multi-frame animation on a button as I have never tried personally, but i am gonna go with intuition as a first shot.

First of all, i don't understand why, but everytime I made one of my TGA files exceed 256 pixels in either direction, it goes bananas. my images get shrunk and cut up all over the place and it took me weeks to get all the adjusted sizes in code just right so that my images appeared how and what size they were designed. When i broke my long images up into smaller ones and kept my TGA files under 256 pixels though everything was exactly ow it should. I suppose I could have found out why, but I never did knowing just not to do that. Your stone_button.tga file is defined as a 512 x 512 pixel tga. This is most likely not the culprit but it jumped out at me as a potential problem nonetheless.

Secondly are you familiar with alpha channels and image masks? Saved alongside the TGA files there is an alpha channel that blocks and allows portions of a image file to show through much like the same way a stencil bocks paint. This mask also allows you to control and preserve transparency like a gif image can. Though you didnt post your TGA file for me to check, this alpha channel is the most common cause of this problem in my experience with new UI modders when there are no code errors so to speak, but the graphics are missing. If you can post your tga file as well I will be happy to take a look at it for you. If this is not the case then we can move on to a little more thorough check but I would be willing to bet your problem lies here.
