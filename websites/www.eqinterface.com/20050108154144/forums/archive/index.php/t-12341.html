Yes, as is quite common now, this virus spoofs the sender address. It searches the hard drive for files that could contain email addresses - anything with these extensions: .htm, .sht, .php, .asp, .dbx, .tbb, .adb, .pl, .wab, and .txt.

It also randomly makes up names. This means you get a bunch more of these crap emails if you have a catchall domain, like guice said. It will take a domain and try common names like "bob" or "joe".

If it spoofs your address as the sender (i.e. your address was somewhere on an infected computer's hard drive) and the virus guesses a bad address (i.e. joe@domain.com is full or non-existant), the mail server will bounce "your" message back to you, just like it's supposed to. While people are saying that this may be the work of spammers or someone else trying to somehow profit, you getting error messages has no direct connection to spammers. Those error messages simply mean that an infected PC has your address stored somewhere on the hard drive. Note that it searches PHP files, which would include cached copies of forum pages like this one.
