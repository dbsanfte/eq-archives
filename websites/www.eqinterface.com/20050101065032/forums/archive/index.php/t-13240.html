I remember some time ago, as well as recently, here and there, of some having trouble with the Yes/No buttons on the little confirmation window (for no drop item looting, item dropping, AA/Adventure purchasing, etc). I believe I've found a fix. However, if you are uncomfortable editing INI files, I wouldn't recommend doing this.

You will need to open UI_Charname_server.ini (In my case, it's UI_Karaab_rodcet.ini). There will be a section in there labeled [ConfirmationDialogBox]. What I did, was add the following lines to the bottom of it (above [InventoryWindow] in my case, your mileage may vary)...

Width800x600=274
Height800x600=200
Width1280x1024=274
Height1280x1024=200
Width1024x768=274
Height1024x768=200

... after that was done, my ConfirmationDialogBox section looks like...

[ConfirmationDialogBox]
XPos1024x768=431
YPos1024x768=272
BGTint.red=255
BGTint.green=255
BGTint.blue=255
BGType=1
Fades=true
Delay=2000
Duration=500
Alpha=255
FadeToAlpha=255
Locked=false
XPos1280x1024=750
YPos1280x1024=568
Width800x600=274
Height800x600=200
Width1280x1024=274
Height1280x1024=200
Width1024x768=274
Height1024x768=200

... again, your mileage may vary, but so far, so good. My UI's confirmation box is smaller (about half the size) of the original, but this also worked if the yes/no buttons were up in the middle somewhere (which happened to me if I had more than 1 confirmation box open). I tested it with the default and it worked great.

The above is if the yes/no buttons are visible, but do not function. Now, as for the smaller confirmation box, where the Yes/No buttons are missing entirely, look in your UI_Charname_server.ini file for a [PopDialog0] (They go up from 0 numerically) line. If you delete the whole section, from [PopDialog#] to the next line inside brackets (signifying another window), it should fix the problem completely.

Disclaimer/Warning: Do realize, this involves editing files that are not friendly for the non-techie. If you perform any of the above operations, be sure to copy the file or save it somewhere else as backup FIRST, in case something bad happens. (Without looking like a blatant and shameless plug, my "Velious2" and "Quartz2" UIs here contain a smaller confirmation window within them and can be easily used on their own, but don't put them inside the default folder or they will be overwritten when you patch).
