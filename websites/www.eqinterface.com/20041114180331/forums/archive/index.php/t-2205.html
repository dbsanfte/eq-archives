Hi guys, im back ! :D

My location wizard MOD is finally released. Its still a Beta version, but fully operational. Contains over 700 locs all over the EQ world.

Ive seen many MODS with maps, but a map is worth crap when your lost, and especially when you tend to, like me, look for a zone running with a Benny Hill type of line of mobs on your arse

General continent tabs (norrath, kunark, velious, luclin) gives locs of all zone connections, dungeons entrances, wiz druid and nexus rings, gems vendors when they sell peridots, as well as docks when applicable. Also listed are popular outside dungeons like Giant forts, or mobs cities, etc. Zones like OOT or TD give locations for every island.

Cities tabs gives locs of all the zone connections, all the classes guilds (or class trainers for luclin when there is no actual guild), banks, docks when applicable, and gems vendors when they sell peridots.

Succor tab gives landing loc of succors in all the zones, dungeons (and planes) included, as well as a brief description of where you land.

It can be downloaded **EDIT** Beta 2 is out, check page 4 for link :D
Locs were obtained through eqmaps.com, eqatlas.com, and eqdruids.com mainly.

Dont be afraid of the mod size. Unlike maps MODS that use pictures, and despite of its 700+ locs, its very small (48kb), since all it contains is text and code.

It uses the EQUI_FeedbackWnd.xml file, and is toggled on by typing /F in game. That keyboard shortcut is hardcoded within the original window, and therefore cannot be changed.

You get out of the mod by pressing the Esc key.


/Enjoy !!

Ok. To change the label color from red to green. This is an example of what you have to look for :

<Label item ="Befallen">
<Font>4</Font>
<RelativePosition>true</RelativePosition>
<Location>
<X>80</X>
<Y>30</Y>
</Location>
<Size>
<CX>250</CX>
<CY>100</CY>
</Size>
<Text>Befallen</Text>
<AlignLeft>true</AlignLeft>
<TextColor>
<R>255</R>
<G>0</G>
<B>0</B>
</TextColor>
</Label>



Then you need to change the following to make labels green instead of red :

Change this :

<TextColor>
<R>255</R>
<G>0</G>
<B>0</B>
</TextColor>




Into this :


<TextColor>
<R>0</R>
<G>255</G>
<B>0</B>
</TextColor>




And im afraid you have to do that for each and every title...

If I get more feedback from pple that have a hard time seeing labels in red, i might as well change it for next version, we shall see.

Type /load <folder> 1 (DOn't forget the 1 or you'll have to redo all your window preferences)

After a few secs and what looks like a zone, you will have the new files loaded.

You'll need to load the mod for each character the first time you log them on.
