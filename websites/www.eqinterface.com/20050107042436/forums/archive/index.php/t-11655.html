SAN DIEGO, CA - December 17, 2003

Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming, today announced Gates of Discord(tm), a new expansion for the incredibly popular PC Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game (MMORPG), EverQuest®. This expansion will offer exploration of a newly discovered continent; a new class (the Berserker) and Alternate Advancement Abilities that will offer new and unique skills to master. Gates of Discord will be available February 2004 at retail outlets such as Electronics Boutique, Best Buy and Gamestop, and as a secure digital download from SOE. The retail version of Gates of Discord will also come with a unique in-box gift and special in-game magic item. By purchasing the retail version of Gates of Discord, players will be able to claim the in-game item.

"Players will face the greatest threat to Norrath and the chaos of a realm yet undiscovered. Norrathians will need to unite with the Wayfarers Brotherhood to oppose this mighty force," said Michael Lustenberger, director of product marketing, Sony Online Entertainment. "In preparation for one of the most epic adventures yet, the Gates of Discord will offer players alternate advancement abilities, leadership experience, the Berserker melee class and vast new areas and zones to explore."

The Gates of Discord directs players to the west side of Antonica where Nedaria's Landing is abuzz with activity in preparation for one of the greatest and most dangerous expeditions in the history of Norrath. Stories and events will lead players across an uncharted land twisted and scarred by an unknown force. New alternate advancements will challenge the most veteran of players while the new Berserker class brings inordinate amounts of strength and bravery.


About Gates of Discord

The rumors that began in The Ruins of Kunark® now hold true- a new continent has been discovered on the far side of Norrath. The Wayfarers Brotherhood will need to amass a brave and trustworthy army for an expedition of epic proportions. Join armies of adventurers on their quest to unearth long-hidden artifacts of power from temple ruins, secret catacombs, dank sewers and vast marshlands. Should you fail, Norrath, its inhabitants, and its gods may fall under control of the forces of Discord! Gates of Discord includes the following new features:

100 new Alternate Advancement Abilities offer a different method of character progression with new and unique skills to master
Gain leadership experience and unlocks many group and raid-enhancing tools
20 new zones, ten new instanced zones and ten new traditional zones including a huge ship city zone, complete with merchants, tradesman, and quest-givers, where you can gather your party and launch expeditions. The new combat zones are for characters level 50 and above
New Berserker class empowers you to ravage your enemies with two-handed axes or by hurling large objects
Discover the newly found continent of Taelosia, which holds the secrets of Norrath's future
20 all new NPC models make for hundreds of challenging encounters unlike anything seen before in EverQuest
New Tribute System enhances gameplay by allowing players to choose beneficial attributes, skills, or effects through offerings at their home cities
Dozens of new and exciting story-driven quests for the hardy adventurer
New tradeskill recipes to produce hundreds of new items
Unearth arcane spells, mighty weapons and other hidden treasures!

The new expansion will be downloadable via the EverQuest launch pad for $29.99. Additionally, retailers will be offering a boxed version that will carry a suggested retail price of $29.99, which includes a special in-box item and in-game item. For more information on Gates of Discord, please visit: Gates of Discord (http://www.gatesofdiscord.com)

The problem is, is that the game has limitations, and there's just so much lore you can integrate into it. The idea well is starting to dry, and it's visibly noticeable both in the story, and in the implementation of their ideas. I think that's partially because EQ1 is VERY slowly losing support from its parent company. They're shifting gears towards EQ2. I doubt they will ever actually dump EQ1, but I wouldn't expect development to continue much longer after EQ2 comes out.

It's a shame, really, because EQ1 has so much promise. Unfortunately, a lot of that promise involves they rewrite the game from scratch, rather than tack on messy code to an already messy base. They're trying, they really are, and I salute them for it. I just don't have much faith in the next expansion. I was not impressed by LDoN at all, and really haven't been impressed by anything in EQ for a very long time.

It's not the expansion itself, it's SOE's policy on its own product. SOE tends to ignore most bugs and such in favor of more content to cover it with. However, they release said content, and part of that content is broken as well. They fix some of it, leave some alone, add new broken things, add new working things, etc. Eventually, they end up with what is a big pile of some working, and some non-working quests, spells, skills, etc.

'Working', by the way, doesn't necessarily mean 'working as intended.' If everything worked 'as intended' Everquest would be even MORE imbalanced. Something that is broken does not necessarily mean it's bugged. Perhaps it's overpowered, or underpowered, etc etc. Atleast 20% of the content in EQ is broken in some regard, and SOE has made little effort to fix it up. It lacks the polished quality of a lot of other games in the same genre, even if it is a better game all around.

What I'm getting at, is that, I'd rather Sony worked on fixing old content, and taking a more active role in the balancing act, WHILE working on expansions. Revamp zones to give players something to chomp at, or add individual, new zones for free to show the players they really care (they actually do do this, but not for higher levels =/), while working on the bugs in the game. Keep content rolling out, but not in large expansion packs. Let it trickle down, so we get the most bang for our buck and get things fixed that deserved to be fixed years ago, as well as revisit things that may have been designed better.

You'll have more happy customers, content will still be coming out (albeit slowly), and you'll end up with a much more whole game that has a few less problems to worry about.

Speaking of content, I have suggestion for the PoP fandango: Change the VF system.

My suggested rules:

Twelve people must be flagged for XX zone and in group one (leader's group, thus, one must be the leader themself.) and two. As long as 36 people or more TOTAL are in the raid, flagged or not, and as long as both groups stay together (with, say, an hour leeway in the case of LDs and wipes. also, if another person that's flagged joins that group while the other is LD, it'll reset the timer), the entire raid will be able to zone in. This will take care of the backflagging problem for raiding guilds, and give some people who might not otherwise be able to see it, experience the content in those zones.

Here's the reasoning for those numbers, and the possible conflicts:

Firstly, for those in the elementals worrying about trains and people blowing spawns, and what not, it'll be hard to find twelve people willing to do it. Note, that it isn't impossible, but it is hard. This late in PoP where most high end guilds are mostly elemental flagged, it'll be easier to set up raids with a softer VF limit. Time is keyed, so there's no real worry there, and the content is psuedo-instanced, so blowing spawns and such is not a real problem.

I can't think of any real problem with a softer VF limit. It still keeps a bold line between newbie guilds and end-game guilds, but allows higher end guilds to function in PoP without worrying about backflagging so much, and if you keep the current rewards for the real flags, it's plenty of incentive to try to get as many flags as possible. The final stats on the charm are sweet =)
