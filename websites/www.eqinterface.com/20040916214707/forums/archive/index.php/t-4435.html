While I can understand what yer sayin, allow me to explain (as I do in the ReadMe file accompanying the UI) why I fell this isn't something that is normally an issue.

(EDIT: just realized that sounded like a brush off. Actually, I completely respect your opinion, I didn't mean it to saound as if I didn't. I just wanted to state that while I understand you feel the possibility of clicking the wrong spot, I personally feel that's not an issue. Again, didn't mean to make light of your concern.)

The effort was to reduce window size and condense the information as best possible. The Cast Window, by nature, was a bit large. I have seen several loverly spell gem mods, but again, they are all quite large. I decided to stagger the gems, then shrink the gems (they were at one point even tighter).

Now, since this UI started (back when we got the new UI), that window has not changed much from the design you currently see. I've played in many conditions since then (heavy lag, fast and furious exp groups, soloing, etc.) and have never clicked the wrong spell. Part of that is because I very rarely click my spells, I use the Alt-casting keys.

Since I was designing this as a ranger, I did think about this alot. I decided that there really wasn't very many spells we had to cast rapidly, and when we did, it was faster to use Alt-casting than to click the gem. With SNare, Jolt, and whatever two situational spells I need in the first 4 gems, my fingeras are already near those keys by default (hotkeys, plus my movement is re-mapped to be more typical of a FPS).

This is why I shrunk the window as much as I did. Honestly, once you are playing you will see you don't have many instances where you will click the wrong spell (unless they all have the same icon, and even then there's the tooltips).

You may want to keep an eye out for may more caster oriented version coming out in the near future. It will contain a different spellgem window.
