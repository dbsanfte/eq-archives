Haliken
11-29-2002, 03:05 AM
I'd be happy to explain it.

The coloring is actually done with gauges, 6 gauges per group member to be prescise.

I will explain how the coloring works for a single group member, then just realize that the same coding is then done 4 more times, once per other group member.

All 6 gauges are 20000 pixels wide and use A_WhitePixel for their fill graphic, they don't have background, endcap or linefill grphics, and have a gaugeYoffset of 0 and a labelYoffset of like -50.

The first gauge (layerwise, it's under the other 5) is colored bright blue (RGB 0, 0, 200) and is at X location 0. When a player is grossly low (less that 20 percent) on HP, or dead this is the gauge that you see.

The second gauge is colored slightly less bright blue (RGB 0, 0, 160) and is at X location -4000 (one fifth it's total length is off the left side of the group window). When a player has less than 40% and 20% or more HP this is the gauge you see.

The third gauge is colored blue (RGB 0, 0, 120) and is at X lcoation -8000 (2 fifths it's total length). When a player has less that 60% and 40% or more HP this is the gauge you see.

The fourth gauge is colored dark blue (RGB 0, 0, 80) and is at X location -12000. HP less than 80% and 60% or greater.

The fifth gauge is colored very dark blue (RGB 0, 0, 40) and is at X location -16000. HP less than 100% and 80% or greater.

The sixth gauge has to be placed carefully. It's what you see when a player is at 100% HP or is in another zone. It's colored black (RGB 0, 0, 0) and is at a location that is -20000 PLUS the width of the group window (in the case of my group window I think it's 141 pixels wide excluding borders) so my gauges are at something like -19850 but it might not be exactly that number, best way to test is color it white and adjust it in game 'til it touches the right edge then change coloring to black.

I hope that makes sense, it does to me, originally the window only had color coding of black if they were in the group, red if in another zone and purple if dead (and this only required one gauge, the one with an X location of 0). But while I was taking a shower I hit my head and saw spots (weird colored) and it made me think of adding color coding based on current HP status cause I hurt myself and colros changed. And I also simultaneously thought of how I'd do it =D

If you want to contain the color coding in a specific area (like a gem) just use an invisible single page tabbox with no border textures to constrict the area of the 20000 pixel wide gauges and put another textureless tabbox over it with a frame like a circle or something to keep it looking like a gem.
