It is designed for 1280x1024 resolution with a /viewport 0 0 1121
841 setting.

Pure melee classes will need to do some slight rearanging to fill in
the spot where the casting bar is.

I finally broke down and went through the huge PITA of making
custom borders to my UI to pull it all together.

I finally came up with some gauges I like for the group window,
they are based off some tking art, but I didn't use the full tking
tube becuase its to "buisy" looking for my taste.

I'm still looking for better spell gems for my casting bar. I'm also
gona replace the book button, the thing looks kinda orphaned
down there.

I havn't decided what I'm gona put in the area above the buff
window, prolly a timer mod or something, I'm still undecided.

The compass, target and timer boxes are movable.

The target box is made fromt he player window, so it gets the
flashing red box in it when attack is on. The timer is made from
the target window.

Download it here. (http://home.attbi.com/~mbeiser/Files/Caleal2beta.zip)

I included a .ini file in the zip. Rename it with your character name
and server number and copy it to your Everquest directory.
Backup your .ini file first.

http://home.attbi.com/~mbeiser/Images/Caleal2beta.JPG

Any feedback on it is welcome. =)

Sylphan
02-24-2003, 06:56 PM
Very impressive.

The 5 by 6 grid for the inventory is kinda nice by itself -- it's compact. But the way it splits into two tabs in the hotbutton window with that same layout is fantastic. That is just so slick... the best I've seen.

Putting all the stats in the selector window is clever too... I never would have thought of using it for anything more than a row of buttons.

In the target, player, and group windows you put the percentage numbers on top of the bars instead of off to the side. I really wish more designers would do that.

I'll also compliment you on getting your work out of the Animations file. It may be invisible to most, but that's significant.

The really large main chat window is also good. Too many designers would leave it about half that size... I wonder whether they actually play the game or just design pretty interfaces.

The only concern I have with what you've made is that the buff window takes up a whole lot of space. But on the other hand... what the hell else are you going to put on that edge of the screen? I suppose you could slide part of the hotbutton window up there... but that might not be so good for mousing.

I use my keyboard for just about everything, using the mouse only for targeting and for picking up items in inventory, merchant, and loot windows. So I arrange things a bit differently... for instance my buff window is smaller than your spell gems window, and I don't even keep the spell gems window on the screen, nor the selector window.

A timer window is an excellent idea for the upper right corner. Since you already have a timer on the target bar you really don't need a dial timer, but a slider-timer window for timing buffs would work very well.

What can I say... I like it. Although I'm not a mouse user, I will recommend this one to friends who are.
