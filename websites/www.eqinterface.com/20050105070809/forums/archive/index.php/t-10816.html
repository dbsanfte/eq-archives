Two most important things to look for:

Hard drive speed (you want 7200 RPM.. but there are almost NO laptops that offer it. At least get 5400 RPM. A lot of laptops come with 4500 RPM drives, and they will really kill performance)

Discrete Graphics - you want a real graphics chip, something from Nvidia or ATI with it's own memory (not shared system memory). I would probably recommend looking for the Geforce4 4200 Go. If you want DirectX 9 compatibility, which might not be a bad idea if you plan on keeping the laptop for several years, look for the ATI Mobility Radeon 9600. It's going to be hard to find any good graphics chipset... most manufacturer's don't offer very many choices (even the big companies) and typically don't give you very much info about what you're getting.

Other than that, you are looking for the same things you look for in a desktop. Fast CPU and front-side bus, Large and fast memory... You get the idea. Brand is not so important, But it is better to go with a big company, because laptops are hard to fix for yourself if they break. Look carefully at warranty. It sucks to have the motherboard fail a year after you get it and discover that you have to do major surgery on the thing... if you can find replacement parts.

You are going to pay through the nose for a laptop that plays EQ (if you want it to play EQ well), $2000 or more. Probably More.
