This is exactly the effect I was trying for when attempting to work the con color into my own UI. I was unable to come up with a version that was suitable to my tastes though. The problem I had seems to be a z-order one. I could not, no matter how I arranged the pieces, get the target color overlay to show up on top of the target HP gauge. The gauge always seems to want to show up on top.

It's still possible to achieve this look though, just not "perfectly." I was able to create a new gauge graphic with a semi-transparent alpha channel and then modify the mask on TargetBox.tga to be colored only where the target's health gauge is. Then I set the target gauge to this new graphic, removed its background and set the fill color to white. This worked, to a point. I was able to get the filled gauge to look exactly how I wanted but, as nwinn said, it looks funny as the target's health goes down.

I also tried making the portion of TargetBox.tga that's used look like a gauge background. This also worked to a point, but I couldn't get the unfilled and filled coloring to be different enough to be able to see how much of the gauge was filled at a glance.

All these problems, plus the fact that a white gauge (for even cons) looks kinda ugly (not to mention the fact that I overlay the target's name over their hp gauge to minimize space, and it's hard to find a color for the text that's visible on green, cyan, blue, white, yellow and red) made me drop the idea and just go for a nice outer glow effect around the gauge.

So in short, you can do this, but it's not gonna be perfect. /shrug
