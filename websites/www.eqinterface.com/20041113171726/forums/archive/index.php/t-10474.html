I don't know about the rest of you folks out there, but I'm too lazy to multi-box on more than one box. I'm also too cheap to buy a box that can do this effectively, so I have to run at bare-bones minimum graphics; this means the tiniest resolution possible.

Now I loooooove the original Vert mods and have followed them since the beginning, dedicatedly modding them every patch before Sokol does and even adding in a few new spiffy features that I personally prefer (t.king icons, my own custom inventory, and so on).

What's been frustrating me lately is that my low res window doesn't have a lot of mod options for the UI, especially my beloved Vert mod. So I finally reached my breaking point, and spent a good five hours reconfiguring some key windows to fit properly in my teeny screen. I've attached a picture of the preliminary end result below.

Some changes include:

- No more inventory access from the hotbutton window. I love this feature on my main, but it's not necessary for secondary and tertiary characters and it takes up lots of room. Off it goes!
- Ultra-small buff windows. I spent about 2 hours trying to make decent smallish buff windows, but in the end I just shamelessly ripped it off from Jaryn UI 3.3. They did it infinitely better than I anyhow, my apologies in advance.
- Added a cast bar to the player window over the XP bar. You don't need the XP bar in combat, and it cuts down on clutter when casting. Why not?
- Haxed together an oldish spell bar from a long-deceased Vert version, added the spellbook icon and removed a casting bar.
- Radically modified the inventory window. Changed to a more classic EQ layout, moved a few buttons around and removed the redundant stats that are visible in the player and pet windows.
- Some other stuff. Cleaned up some code and modified some files to add a colour scheme for my brother's shadowknight (Charcoal?!).

I don't want to actually release it unless I get the blessings of the original authors (both of the Vert mod and Jaryn). I really just want some feedback, mostly to see if people would be interested in something like this.

Constructive criticism is welcomed of course.

The HP move I actually considered once when I originally saw the first Vert mods.

The reason I didn't actually involves a bit of basic psychology though. Without going into too much detail, the brain processes analog input a fraction of a second faster than numerical. This is why healing classes shouldn't choose a UI that eliminates the HP or mana bars: The split second it takes to process the numbers is enough to let a person die, so it requires greater amounts of guesswork to preempt healing the target(s).

I found that if I moved the numerical HP display right next to the analog, I would try to read both at the same time (and it was to the detriment of my group, since my main and secondaries are a paladin and druid/ranger respectively). Having it on the opposite side of the window still allows it to be seen when I have time to calculate the best heal, but isn't so obtrusive as to inhibit split-second reactions.

Your mileage may vary. It's pretty trvial to change: <Location>
<X>76</X>
<Y>60</Y>
</Location>into: <Location>
<X>76</X>
<Y>-1</Y>
</Location>anyways if you prefer it that way. ;)
