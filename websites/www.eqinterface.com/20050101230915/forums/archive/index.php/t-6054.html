I'm pretty much a rookie, but I did figure out how to synchronize the refresh rates of my monitor and the game.

1. Computer: go to your control panel, then double click on display. Go to the tab 'settings', then click on 'advanced'. Then click on the tab 'monitor'. That will give you the refresh rate of your monitor, which you can either leave alone or reset. (watch the warning about setting it to too high of a refresh rate).

[alternately, go to your start menu help - and type in "refresh". That will take you to where you need to go as well.]

2. Everquest: go into your eq folder and find eqclient. Near the bottom (on mine, at least), it will tell you what your refresh rate is.

My eq refresh was faster than my monitor refresh, which for some reason was really slow. I changed my monitor refresh to be the same as my eq one, and when I logged on, I didn't have anymore bleedthrough.

wooot.

01-31-2003, 12:05 PM
Open eqclient.ini and look for "BitsPerPixel=".
Set it to either "16" or "32" depending on the color depth of your desktop.
Save and close.
Now start EQ and in-game under display choose video mode, check that resolution and refresh rate are the same as desktop settings, then click accept and then yes.
This should take care on the bleedthru and also you should be able to switch between windowed mode and normal as before patch.

Regards,
Feebb
