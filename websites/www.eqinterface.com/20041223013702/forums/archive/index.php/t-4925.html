The icon keyid can be parsed from the em_spells.txt file (name might be off slightly off. Otherwise visit http://lucy.fnord.net/index.html
Look up any spell and then look at the "RAW DATA"

For instance here is the link for Spirit of Wolf (http://lucy.fnord.net/spellraw.html?id=278&source=Live)

You'll see a bunch of attributes - for this you are interested "BookIcon" and "GemIcon".

Finally, you can right click the icon of the spell being displayed at the top and that will show how Lucy mapped it.

Someone enterprising could whip up a quick catalog for easier reference to how they are organized in the UI file.

Not quite complete but heres a good start.

I still dont understand how ALL the icons work or where they are used. My main experience has been a chanter. Anyway, if you're looking to create a new set of graphics, this catalog should help.
Fortunately, SOE took pity on us and made the SPELLS_EN.TXT available as an ASCII text file.

My recommendation is to import SPELLS_EN.TXT into a spreadsheet. Column 2 has the Spell names and column 132 has the SPELL ICON value being referred to in the attached JPG image.
