I designed my windows to be fairly small in 1024x768. Simply trimmed out all the fat from the default windows without resizing anything. I'm currently working on changing to 640x480. But I don't want all my windows to look huge in 640x480 so I'm reducing many fonts and changing many of the pictures (hotbuttons, inventory, bags, and such) from 40x40 pixels to 24x24 pixels. That means a whole lot of windows have to be completely redone.

I'm having real difficulty with the viewport solution. If I shrink it to 1024 x 560 for example I can't see what's at my feet. The bottom portion of the image is cut off. If I shrink it to 850 by 560 I have to think of something to do with a huge black bar along the right side of the screen. I like my controls along the bottom for the most part. Some things can be moved to the side, but chat windows really need to be wider than tall. And since my eyes are often on the chat windows, I like to have the other controls near them.

Currently the best compromise seems to be to push all the controls into triangles in the lower left and lower right of the screen. Most windows like to be squares, not triangles, but they can be fitted in around the chat windows in each corner.

I see that Kudane has done some typical things: keep the abilities window open to fill up space on the left, using the buffs window and full title bars to fill up space on the right, and sticking the player window way up in the top corner (not the most readable location). No I don't have a better way... that's what I'm whining about.
