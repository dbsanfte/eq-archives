Here's is a shot of my attempt at modding my UI. I needed something that gave me access to all the pertainent info right on my screen without having to open other windows. But of major importance was the necessity to have as much available clear space as possible. (I hate having to move something just to follow a mob when I'm medding.)

Some of the things I put in this UI were at the request of a paladin friend of mine who wanted certain things available for melee purposes.

One of the things you'll notice is that I do not have a 'Target' window. As things stand currently, VI didn't give us a means of adding new windows, and I had a need for a clean window for things like maps and trade skills and such. I took the gauge off the Target window, put it on my 'Actions' window under both the 'Combat' and the 'Skills' tabs, so you almost always have your target visible. This left me with a totally empty window that I could do with whatever I chose.

I left my map/trade skill window off the screenshot cuz I didn't want to step on anyone's toes or pinch any nerves. Besides, I didn't make the maps for it, and didn't want to steal anyone's credit. I did, however, incorporate full color maps from some of the popular EQ map sights into my UI.

Another thing you'll notice right away is the cool Spell icons, as well as the nifty spell book. Thanks go to T. King for his incredible talents and taste in graphic detail. I really like his work.

Take a look, and if you like what you see let me know. Copies of all files are available on request.

Ok guys, sorry I didn't get something new posted yesterday. What with the move and all, seems there was a bit of a bog down and for some reason my password wouldn't work. And it took a great deal of time to get it reset. However, here are the alterations I've made, plus a screenshot and even links to the files.

I've made 2 different Player info windows, one with the group info and one without. I would like to comment briefly on some of your suggestions if I may...

For starters, the level/class/deity info: I want to keep it there, because for some reason I feel that it belongs. Don't ask me why, I just think it would be lacking if I took it off.

Secondly, the stamina bar: I too am a caster, in fact I am a wizard. And I don't know about the rest of the wizards out there, but sometimes I find myself having to turn tail and run. And sometimes I am running a great distance, or Spirit of Wolf ran out at a bad time and my only means of evading my attacker is... the space bar. THat's right, I jump, as much and as often as I can to get away from whatever it is that wants to tear me a new one. And jumping gets me a step or two ahead. Thing is, when my stamina or 'fatigue' level drops too far, I can't jump anymore, and not only that, but I begin to slow down. So, I like having that little yellow bar there so I know when I need to stop jumping to let my fatigue level go back up again.

As for the casting and target windows: Where in the heck would I put them? See, the size of the gauges or bars is kinda a hard thing to work around. I tried making them smaller but they became hard to read, and the pixels began bleeding together. And with the size requirements of EQ interface windows (being a power of 2), well, I wanted to keep my image files within a certain size limit... 256x256. The edges of my Player window have already hit that size, and to add anymore, I'd have to make my file bigger... double the current size even. Now, that doesn't mean the actual window would be double the size, just the file. But that does mean slightly longer downloads, slightly more work, and I guess I'm just being lazy in that aspect.

I put the target window on the Actions window because it made it close to the attack/melee buttons, as well as the skills and abilities. For melee classes, if you move your mouse down to click on bash or taunt or kick, you have the target's health gauge right there above your mouse pointer. I know, many of you use Hotkeys for this so it really doesn't matter.

As for the casting gauge, here's what I did. I cast a spell that took a few seconds to cast (I'm a wizard, I have many of those), the quickly grabbed my casting gauge and moved it right over beneath my spells window. That way when I cast a spell, I glance up to where my spell gems are, and see my casting gauge. A thought I had is to attach the casting gauge to the spell gem window like I did the exp and AA bars.

Anyway, I'm running out of steam here, so I'll address some of the changes I DID make, and get to posting the pics and where to get it.

1: Added group health gauges to the Player Window. There are 2 different version available, one with and one without.

2: Added percents to both versions for Mana and HP. These percents are color coded so it should be obvious which is which. (Oh, and as for making a button that reports mana to the group... until VI gives us a way to create our own button functions, not bloody likely).

3: Adjusted the old group window so that everything fits on a horizontal plane, instead of vertically as it used to be. (I forgot to add F-key labels to each gauge so look for that in a couple of days, sorry)

4: Added the 'target' gauge to the 'Main' tab of the actions window. I originally had it only on the abilities and combat tabs, but it wasn't showing your target after you zoned because the 'main' tab defaults to being on top.

5: Adjusted the compass colors a bit so that they stand out a bit better (lightened the blues and reds a bit). Also added off-axis labels (SE, SW, NE, NW)

6: Added spell number labels to each of the spell gems in the spell casting window. Some people found it hard to remember that the evens went across the top and the odds down the side, so I added numbers to remind them. I also added a slightly different Spellbook button. The old red and gold thing was a bit ghastly so I took a snap of my spell book, shrunk it down considerably, and put it in there in an open and closed view.

I guess that's really about it. I've been reading over some of your suggestions and requests, and will likely be making some more alterations/special modifications in the near future. I am not opposed to making custom adjustments to my UI to meet individual needs, so keep sending me your comments. And Morniel... I'm working on that Hotkey window. I've just been trying to get some game time in lately. I finally hit 51 on my main character last night.

I found out why it wouldn't load when I added the target_hp item, the first entry for your group listing has an incorrect screenID:


<Gauge item = "PW_Gauge1">
<ScreenID>PW_TargetHP</ScreenID>
<Font>3</Font>
<TextColor>
<R>240</R>
<G>240</G>
<B>240</B>
</TextColor>
<RelativePosition>true</RelativePosition>
<Location>
<X>64</X>
<Y>80</Y>
</Location>
<Size>
<CX>108</CX>
<CY>24</CY>
</Size>
<GaugeOffsetY>16</GaugeOffsetY>
etc......
</Gauge>

The ScreenID should simply be Gauge1. Once I changed that, it loaded up my changes correctly. It was detecting 2 instances of TargetHP, even though the second occurance was actually Target_HP. It's wierd, but that's what was causing it.

10-18-2002, 06:35 PM
If you are going to be using my Player window as a basis for this mod, then yes, you will have to edit it in your favorite graphic editor (I use PSP7). My player window uses a fixed background image (the default UI uses a single 'fill' background for all of it's windows). My background image defines the shape and size of the window, then the XML simply supports that by setting the <Size> of the window equal to the actual size of the image.

If you plan to make a new player window based upon mine, you'll need to edit the background image to suit your needs (changing the size, shape, texture) then after you've made the graphic changes you will need to tell the XML what size the image is and what size to make the window.

I don't know what else to tell you... make sure you save your transparent layer after you make any changes to the image, and remember that all windows are square windows, even if your background makes them appear otherwise. Any part of the window that is transparent is only clear, not invisible... it's still a solid square window. So you won't be able to click through transparent areas. If you cover something with a window and think it will be ok because it's transparent.... think again.

Problem: When you load the latest release of my mod from my site (or from the recently added copy here on EQInterface), Everquest crashes to the desktop.

Cause: My mistake. I am terribly sorry for that. I was looking at the new Raid Window .xml file from the test server when I zipped this up. Apparently I left the file 'EQUI_RaidWindow.xml' in the folder. Seeing as this was the original raid window script before it went live, it would now be invalid since changes have been made since it was on the test server.

Solution: There are two easy ways to solve this problem.

1) The first and easiest way to solve this problem, is to open your UIfiles folder where you extracted this skin to. Then locate the file named 'EQUI_RaidWindow.xml' and delete it. This will force the UI schema to once again load the Raid Window from the default folder as it should have originally done.

2) The second is method is to download a fresh copy of this mod, either from my site, or from EQInterface.com's download section once it becomes available. I will speaking with Kudane about updating the file and possibly adding an addendum to it's description, referencing the error and fix.
