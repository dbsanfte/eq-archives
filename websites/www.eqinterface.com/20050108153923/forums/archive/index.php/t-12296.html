Things to consider if you are thinking about upgrading that video card. Again, these are just our experiences and opinions.
(Scroll to bottom for quick summary if you aren't the reading type.)

So my brother wanted a new video card. He went out, and a few hours later returned with a shiny new box. Curious, I went in to see what he had. To my suprise, he had decided on a Radeon 9800 Pro, we had both pretty much always stuck with Nvidia's cards as habit. Trying something new is always good though... isn't it?

The Radeon 9800

We unpacked it, took anti-static precautions and proceeded to pop that new monster into his machine. After about 15 minutes of figuring out the proper steps for the installation of the drivers in the correct order we had it all done (the driver software is... messy, in my opinion).

Thus began the nightmare installation from hell...

All looked good, everything ran, and it performed quite well... until after about a half hour. Suddenly the screen goes black, we hear window noises for critical stop and exclamation. The video returns but in 16 colors and tiny resolution. A little window informs us that the card has recovered from an error and advises we reboot before continuing - as if it wasn't obvious.

We reboot the machine, check DX9 and the drivers - all seems up to snuff. We run some 3D apps whilst monitoring the temperature of the card to rule out thermal causes. No problem there, runs quite cool (room temp to +15d above). Again we get the VPU recovery error... this continues for a while during the process of trouble shooting.

A summary: after extensive changes of drivers, bios settings, graphics settings, a new card (exchanged to rule out defective hardware), power supply units a brand spanking new install of windows again, more drivers, more bios tinkering, another computer - NOTHING stops the constant but random VPU errors the cards are spewing out.

We give up, at a loss for any explanation and decide to return the card for a Nvidia GeForceFX 5700 Ultra (it was pretty much the same performance of the FX5900 but cheaper).

The Nvidia GeforceFX 5700 Ultra

We return with the new card, praying there are no more issues (this is two days later). Pop the new card into the machine (wow, those FX5700s are heavy! Very beefy heatsinks on both sides). Install the NV 53.03 drivers in one step and 60 seconds, reboot, and are up and running.

Load up various 3D apps and games and everything runs without so much as a hiccup. In fact, we both notice that the Nvidia card is getting a MUCH better framerate than the Radeons were (despite all the documentation to the contrary - web reviews repeating again and again that the Radeon out performed the GeForce 5900 let alone the 5700). We wonder who the heck ATI payed off to write those reviews?!

Summary of Experience

ATI
A) Drivers are a pain, quite tedious.
B) Performance is decent (but nowhere near what all these web reviews would have us believe), gamma/color correct system is a total waste of effort.
C) Stability is absolutely horrible. A one legged tripod has more stability. Crashes were random but constant - load or no load on two different cards and two different systems.
D) Included software was out of date. Morrowind? C'mon - why include software that shows off not an iota of the cards capability? Not that I'd want to even play it anyway.
E) Heatsink and card construction seemed... somewhat flimsy and lacked design.

NVIDIA
A) Drivers are cake, one executable for any of the cards and sixty seconds later you are set to reboot and get going.
B) Performance was excellent! Graphics were smooth, quick and vivid. A quick adjustment through the color correction menu and gamma was dead on.
C) Stable as a concrete block, the card has never so much as flinched at even the heaviest of load (even has a temp warning system should you be the overclocker type).
D)Included software was recent, supplying a game that could show off a good range of the cards capability as well as having some decent reviews. (Splinter Cell)
E)Heatsink was solid and more than sufficient, though the fan seemed a bit small. It's fine for stock, overclockers might want to invest in a better one. Overall construction was very sturdy, nothing to complain about that I can see.

Thats the wrap up. I'd buy the GeForceFX 5700 Ultra again in the blink of an eye, where as I'd poke my own eye out and poor lemon juice in it rather than purchase the Radeon 9800 Pro again.

~Tellgar
