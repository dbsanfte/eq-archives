Are you running on WinXP? If so, I had this very same problem. The sides had a sizeable black space and made the game window smaller. I found, as well as my other machine, if your machines window background graphic setting (i.e., 1024x768) is different than EQ game window setting (i.e., 1280x1024) the computer will make cause the black lines on the sides appear. Once the computer settings match the EQ game settings, the lines disappear. And like I said, both my computers did this and once I made them both the same setting (i.e., 1280x1024) it disappeared. Give that a try, it might be the issue, if your running under XP. Good luck.

Oh and after you click print screen you have to open up a graphics program (i.e., Microsoft Paint - every Windows OS has this), just right click on the white background and select paste. This will paste your 'Print screen' you just did.

When you press 'Print screen' it actually has done something, but you just don't see it. It stores the image in a notepad format, but you have to do something with it. In this case, opening a graphics program - use Paint (Click Start - Accessories - Paint). Then when you have your paint program open, click on Edit and then click on Paste and that should paste your saved 'Print Screen' image. Once its finished pasting, click 'Save As' and type in a name. Its best to save the image as a .JPG format (use the pull down window to choose your file type and choose .jpg) in 'Save as Type'.

Hope this helps clear things up.
