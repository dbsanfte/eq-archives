Most of this is covered in the SIDL.doc.

1. Dont know - pretty big tho.

2. size is determined on the TabBox (which is a Frame/Control/Screenpiece). Pages dont have a defined size.

3. on the TabBox the TabBorderTemplate defines the border of the tab and any background images you want to apply. The individual pages have 3 elements for the tab:
TabText - Text to attach to the tab that opens this page
TabIcon - Icon to attach to the tab that opens this page. The icon is always drawn left-justified. If any text also exists, it will be drawn to the right of the icon.
TabIconActive - Icon to attach to the tab when page is active. The icon is always drawn left-justified. If any text also exists, it will be drawn to the right of the icon.

I believe the size of the Tabs is dynamic to fit the images and text, which would at least explain why these use left justifications.

4) Nope, you can make your parent window as big as you want and stick the TabBox (which is a frame/control/screenpiece)anywhere you want it.
