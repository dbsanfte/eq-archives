Are you aware that there's a rather annoying company by the name of Unisys, who has the copyright liscense for the LZH compression format that is used in .GIF graphics, and for the last couple years has been suing people for unliscensed use of their compression format? While they do focus more now on large buisnesses, they have freuqently in the past, charged people $50 grand per unliscensed use (each graphic made by a program that generated a .GIF file without their explicit permission). That is why I personally avioid .GIF files like the plague.

There is another animated graphic format in existence, however Microsoft's [worthless] web browser, Internet Explorer, still does not support the international network graphics formats that have been in existence for about a decade, while browsers that support these well-defined and thought out standards (like Mozilla/Netscape Navigator) do. The graphic format is .PNG, and the animated format of those graphics is .MNG. Those, as well as the .JPG formats are the only graphics I use (exclusively).

I'd just be careful about making a .GIF format unless you can be 100% certian that the program you are using to make the animations is legit.
