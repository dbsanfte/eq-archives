The spellgem slots in the Hotkeys window are just like the rest of them. There are 3 different types of buttons in the Hot Keys window, hotbuttons, inventory slots, and spell gems. You would moved or change them all virtually the same.

However, as with all hot buttons, don't expect your spell gems to just 'be there' when you load EQ. Just like the hot buttons and inventory slots, you have to drag a spell gem icon over to the hotkey bar and place it in a box, otherwise it will just appear as an empty box.

Cormanth chuckles softly.

What you are dealing with would be the SpellGemBackground and the SpellGemHolder. And just so you know, these are one of the handfuls of things that Sony has hardcoded into the XML Schema/Client (also the Buff Icon backgrounds and Spell Book Icon backgrounds, which both share the same graphic).

In truth, the only way to change the artistic look of the gems or their backgrounds is to change the contents of their respective files. You can make an entirely new window_pieces02.tga file, but it still has to have the same name. Everything else about the gems and icons you can change. You can change how they look in the file, and you can change their size and position in the xml. You just can't change which file they use, nor which animation is used.

Just one of the little nasties of trying to change what someone else has created. You can't always have it the way you want.
