(The following text is only my opinion and personal pondering, additional discussion and opinions are welcome.)

The Internet is proclaimed to be the the greatest innovation in communication this century. Certainly it has opened up new frontiers for business, commerce, education and personal communication or recreation. Yet also offering up the darker side of unregulated and freely accessible media and/or content consisting of anything the imagination can conjure up. Surprisingly, the latter does not alarm me much -- I support free access to whatever someone chooses to make available. It is my job to decide what is acceptable or what is not for myself and my children, and thus my responsibility to exercise caution and sound judgement on what is off-limits.

Now I'll move on to what really begins to worry me. Literary skills and the Internet or, shall I say, lack thereof. Every time I visit my favorite message boards I am astounded (even assaulted) by what seems to be a total disregard of literary skill and proper grammar. Indeed, in many cases, it seems to be better described as rampant illiteracy! Sometimes I find myself wondering about the overall 'health' of linguistic skill among the general population.

I certainly do not proclaim myself to be perfect when it comes to sentence structure, grammar, and even punctuation. A certain amount of 'slack' is to be expected in informal or recreational literature. However -- maintaining a certain amount of proper literary technique is something I (personally) try to accomplish. Is it foolhearty to expect the same from others? Facing the reality of the day, expecting something that would be considered close to normal/proper english in a game like Everquest or online Forums might be better compared to wishing for a utopian society!

"hi. cud i get a sum hlp plz?"; "can u hlp me w this tks"; "wat can u do to mabey teech me to make a inretface for eq", these are examples of what I receive both while playing Everquest and often private messages. I understand not everyone is great typist. I myself plot along at a decent 30 to 40 words a minute or usually less, even after having been working with computers since grade school (a priviledge as not many schools had computers available to students at that time). Often enough the text is even less understandable and contains so many instances of backward grammar, spelling errors and abbreviations that it becomes unrecognizable as the English language.

When I find myself stopping and wondering about the future of this colossal collection of literary cacophony we label the 'Internet', I do hope this trend of mangling the english language is just a small 'wobble' in the 'steps' of a society learning to communicate in a new and diverse means.

Regards,
Tellgar
