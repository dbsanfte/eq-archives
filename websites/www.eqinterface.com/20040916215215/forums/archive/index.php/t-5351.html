Originally posted by Kilorn
One question... is it possible to get a floating target window to work with your UI? Finding myself having to remember to look at the top of the Player window to find my target...

Sorry, no. See the lovely frame across the bottom? That's the target window. *grin*

Unfortunately, there are only so many "static" (always show on your screen) windows to work with to create the frame. The actions, hotbuttons, window selector, music player, group, target and pet windows are all part of the frame, in one place or another. Replacing any of them will end up with you not having a section of the frame. You can, however, replace the player window, if you wish, just make sure that it is a combined target/self/group mod.

Did you go into the Choices subfolder within the resolution folder and choose which PetInfo Window, Group Window and Player Window (combined Target/Player/Group) you want?

There are three choices for the PetInfo window:

PetInfo BlankSide means it will create the graphic in the bottom lefthand corner of the screen. This is used when you are using either the Group Healer or the Group - Plain file to create the bar across the middle.

PetInfo Healer has all of the pet control buttons, pet name and hp bar, PLUS has the additional F1-F6 Sphere Gauges for ease of targeting to heal self or group members.

PetInfo - Plain just has the pet control buttons, pet name and hp bar.

There are three choices for the Group window:

Group BlankSide means it will create the graphic in the bottom lefthand corner of the screen. This is used when you are using either the PetInfo Healer or the PetInfo - Plain file to create the bar across the middle.

Group Healer has the invite and disband buttons, PLUS has the additional F1-F6 Sphere Gauges for ease of targeting to heal self or group members.

Group - Plain just has the invite and disband buttons.

For the Player Window (combined Target/Player/Group), there are only two choices. PlayerWindow - ManaCasting or PlayerWindow - Stamina. The ManaCasting also has a mini stamina bar at the bottom of the Mana bar.

Pick one of each that is appropriate to your needs. Copy and move them up to the custom folder you have created. Right click on the name and delete the " - Description" part of it from the end of the file name ( - BlankSide, - Healer, or - Plain for Pet and Group, - ManaCasting or Stamina for Player).

/nod, that's what I thought the problem was. It'll behave much better in version 2, but in the mean time:

Okay first, you don't need to change anything in the animations file. Instead, what you need to do is to modify the hotbutton file.

Open EQUI_HotButtonWnd.xml using your text editor. Do a Search or Find function to find:

<SpellGemDrawTemplate>
<Holder>TK_SpellGemHolder</Holder>
<Background>A_SquareBtnNormal</Background>
<Highlight>TK_SpellGemHighlight</Highlight>
</SpellGemDrawTemplate>
</SpellGem>

You need to change it so that it is this instead:

<SpellGemDrawTemplate>
<Holder>A_SpellGemHolder</Holder>
<Background>A_SquareBtnNormal</Background>
<Highlight>A_SpellGemHighlight</Highlight>
</SpellGemDrawTemplate>

You will need to do that 10 times, one for each slot that you could be putting a spellgem hotkey.
