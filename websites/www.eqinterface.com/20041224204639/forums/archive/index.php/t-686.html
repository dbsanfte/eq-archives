First, you need a graphic editor, such as Photoshop, Paintshop Pro, or The GIMP.

The XML files (Animations to be specific) refer to the graphics by filename and by X,Y coordinates and size coordinates within the .tga files.

You must adhered to these coordinates unless you feel confident enough to change them within the Animations file, and all the other files that may call those pieces.

So, first you must examine the documentation (what there is), and read through the XML until you feel you understand it.

If you can't do that, then you must rely on others to make mods, and just install them according to the directions they give.

There's an Info tool in Photoshop. Window -> Info .. Shortcut 'I'

Use that to get your cords. You can also, if you know PS well enough, use the marque tool .. Sortcut 'M'.

Hit 'M', and if you have your window open, you can drag it over your graphic and it will tell you the size in the window. Use your cursor and place it at the far top left pixel and that will be the cordinates.

All this informaton is displayed in the Info window. It's pretty much self explanitory.
