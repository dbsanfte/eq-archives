03-04-2004, 04:43 AM
Ok, downloaded and started installing this tonight... haven't actually used it to fight with yet, so take my comments with a dragon sized grain of salt. ^^

I missed a help TXT file. Not that the UI was hard to set up, but just something to explain the different options.

Once I got in game, the very first thing that kind jumped out at me was that the right sidebar equipment list kinda blends into the background... you might wanna put some sorta solid neutral behind it so that it stands out better. The main viewport window is slightly larger, and overall I had a sense that everything was even better organized than in the original Cairenn UIs.

I like the new, smaller inventory... though I'd still like to see one that is just buttons and coin as an option. The redundancy, especially where I've placed my inventory in the bottom right of the viewport, of having two bag inventories just feels kinda odd still. Aside from that, the inventory is something that i'd been missing, neat, clean and small enough that it doesn't block the entire view when it pops up.

Unfortunately, cool looking as the new spell book looks, it was one of the first things that I replaced with a smaller book. This is mostly me, but it's also because I swap spells a lot in fights, and a small spell book really helps because you can see how far the MoB your sit agro is dragging toward you is. Yours is a great design, but it was just too big for my tastes.

One of the things I always had problems with your original UI was that the buff bars were in the main viewport, and I'd forever be trying to click on something through it and wondering why my target wasn't showing up. Kudos on moving the buffs to the top edge of the screen, where they are visible while still being outta the way. ^_^ Kinda miss the mp3 player, though... oh well, can't have it both ways, and I'd rather have the buff bar outta the way than an always visible mp3 player.

The spell gem bar is another of the nice, and neat little pieces, and fits in perfectly with the theme. Kept this one. ^^

I'm not sure if I like the new 'Target Dragon' yet... The fact that it shows con color is great, but it seemed kinda awkward in the middle of the screen like that, so I stacked it on top of the spell gem bar instead, along the bottom left corner of the screen. The pattern fits with the gem bar as well as it did with the group/self info bars, so another kudos there for excellent re-arrangement capability.

My main is a ranger, so I like the hybrid combat abilities bar, though as I mentioned above, I switched the position of the gem bar and the combat bar just because I was used to my spells on the left side.

I did run into trouble with the chat window bug... when I tried opening a new chat window, it appears at the very bottom of the screen, with the text input off the bottom edge... Might wanna look into that - along the same lines, is it a bug or by design that more than half of the windows have their 'close' option grayed out?

The Map window was the second window I outright replaced... it's just far, far too big, even in the full size viewport, much less one that's been made smaller.

I like the new self information bar, but I'd very much like to see a return of the healing spheres for the rest of the group in the little group box, as they came in very handy in my guild - the flashing red always drew my attention in enough time to retarget and heal while our cleric rattled off a C Heal - bear in mind that I haven't actually tried the UI out in a group, so if they're there when you're actually IN a group, my appologies in advance. ^^

The 'YES' and 'NO' buttons on the pet caster bar are a little vague... funny, once you realize what they are, but vague, and while not many people actually click on them, the extra time it takes to find the right button might for some people be time they don't have. Also, I wish that the pet get lost button were clearly marked and seperated from the rest like it was in the old UI... I can foresee panic clicking problems with it as is.

The bright, flashing 'ATTACK!' that appears when auto attack is on is a great idea, specially for a dumb ranger like me that some times forgets to turn it on. ^_^;;;

I do agree with what Tyndylla said... the cast time bar is too obscure to be made out till the spell is almost done casting... maybe place it to the right of the mana gauge, in what appears to be empty space between it and the spellbook icon? Just a thought.

Overall - I'll post a followup message when I actually get in and get some battle and group experience in on the UI, but it looks great so far and as solid as your other UIs. Most of my problems have stemmed from personal preferences and pet peeves, though the chat window bug and the cast time bar have the potential to cause problems in a raid setting where things are hectic and the wrong spell at the wrong time or a raid instruction lost to battle spam can cause serious destruction.

One random thought - perhaps a vertical version of the spell gem bar as a choice? Some people like to be able to see their Alt-Spell Labels.

In any case, it does look great overall, and very much up to the standard I've come to respect in your work. Congratulations on a job well done thus far.


***

Ok, now that I've gotten some group experience, I've come up with two things to add - first and foremost, the group HP percentage number is next to impossible to read with it inside the HP gauge, at least for me... It might be different for other people.

Second - I like the idea of the Invite/Disband buttons popping up only when there's an open invitation... except that the buttons didn't work for me when I tried clicking on it. Again, that might just be my own inept usage, but it's something worth noting.

Beyond that, another excellent UI. ^^
