Its good to know that I wasn't the only who suffered from this issue. If your running WinXP, I think I may have your fix. Simply change your computers Display Properties Screen resolution to 1280x1024 (right click on your computers background and select Properties). For some reason, if your running your normal computers window at 1024x768 or less, your computer will play games at that resolution regardless of what you have your games resolution setting at. As soon as I did this, my game encompasses full screen. Just another quirky thing of Windows. /shrug

I recently had to downgrade monitors from a 19" tube to a 15" flat pannel. The change forced me to go from 1600 x 1200 to 1280 x 1024. I started experiencing a similar problem.

Essentially, any time I put EQ into Windowed mode (usually by acidentally hitting the Windows Key) Full Screen Mode was simply a big black box filling the entire screen. Alt+Enter took me to a Windowed view and I could play, etc., but it was irritating.

I run EQ and my desktop in the same resolution and bit-depth (1280 x 1024, 32-bit) so the size of the window elements (title bar, frames, etc.) were just using up valuable space.

To work-around this problem, I downloaded and installed WinKey Killer (see WinKey Killer Homepage (http://home.plutonium.net/~bjackson)). I don't know what the other cause was, but you might try installing this, and changing the line in your eqclient.ini fileWindowedMode=TRUE to WindowedMode=FALSE
