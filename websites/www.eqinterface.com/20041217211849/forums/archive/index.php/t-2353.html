The higher your processor the better your graphics will be.
Nope! Sorry to break it to you but that's simply not true!
You won't see much of an improvement running EQ on a 1.5 GHz or 2.0 GHz processor while using the same graphics accelerator and same amount of RAM....to be honest, you won't see ANY improvement at all.
On the other hand, using a 1.5 GHz processor with a GeForce 2 and 256 MB of RAM going to either a GeForce 4 or 512 MB of RAM (or even both) you will see a HUGE improvement!!!
This is because the pure main processor calculations aren't really a big thing to your puter while calculating the huge graphics data of the meanwhile far outdated graphics engine EQ utilizes are. And the faster the GPU gets those through the faster your system will perform.
RAM is playing a rather big role on that one, too, because the graphics data is stored there to pass it through from the CPU to the GPU while the GPU's memory is still busy, and additionally the bus frequency your RAM data bus is running on is kinda important too, just as the speed of your mobo's AGP slot is...but that's all got nothing to do with the CPU but more with your motherboard.

On another note, I'm a convinced AMD user, since the Athlons serve you the same power a comparable Pentium does for roughly a little more than half the price (at least in germany). But that's personal preference I guess and a question of how slim your wallet is...

I personally would go with a Athlon XP 1700+ and invest what's left in a GeForce 4 Ti 4200 (or 4600 if you got enough left for that) and if there's STILL more than dust and cobwebs in your wallet invest in RAM till you got at least 512 MB (I wish I could upgrade to 1 GB of RAM but, well....you know, dust and cobwebs and such....)

This is just my opinion though, what do I know about 'puters...
