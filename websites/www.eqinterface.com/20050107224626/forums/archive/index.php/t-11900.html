Lets discuss the possibility of allowing multi class options...

You start as a warrior, work your way to at least level 20, any time after that you pick up your class charm from your guildmaster and turn it in to a new guild.

You are now a rogue. You are a level 20 warrior/level 1 rogue. You now retain the hp, Ac of the warrior, but have only the skills of a level 1 rogue. When you reach level 20 rogue you gain back all of the skills you built up as a level 20 warrior. From then on you may progress as far as you wish as a rogue, but you are frozen in time as the level 20 warrior. All AA skills must be in the 2nd and new profession. If you choose a 3 profession, it must be chosen at or above the level you switched the first time and you then lose all of the skills of the very first profession.

Restrict to all non-hybrid classes:

combos:

Warrior/cleric
warrior/rogue
warrior/wizard
warrior/mage
warrior/druid
and so on....

Well, what do you think? and yes I did steal the idea from rpg D&D.

I've been having similar thoughts... and thats also why I'm giving Horizons a try... so far it delivers very well with only a few annoyances...

the problem is, as always, the mighty "balancing" issue... but as an idea I'm all for it... if nothing else because it leaves the customization to the player to a greater extent...

AA abilities are all fine and dandy, but take 2 level 65 [insert class here] with equal amounts of AA points and you're bound to have 2 very similar characters in terms of skills and whatnot... admittedly, gear might differ and in the high end game gear gets sorta important (since skills and abilities are pretty much static regardless of gear)... but still... multiclassing would allow for example 2 warriors to be good at very different things while still being warriors... one might have, after lots of labour, aquired the Ensnare spell (THE most important spell in the game, if you ask me hehe) while the other has gained the ability to pacify mobs... or whatever... I know, I know.. rangers and pallies... hehe... work with me here...

it wouldn't surprise me if this sort of thing was implemented in EQ1 eventually... with the dawn of EQ2 they will need some pretty refreshing ideas to keep their customers from stampeding off to EQ2 (assuming it delivers so well that people are content to migrate and stay there)... in the end, new classes and AA's won't cut it, methinks...
