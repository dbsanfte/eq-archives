10-29-2002, 11:12 PM
Got to agree with both of the above.

Our guild, Solus, is first and foremost a Roleplaying guild - always in character unless in a private channel. More importantly, the founders designed an over-arching storyline fo Solus, a reason for being. That gives us a purpose, something to rely on and return to when the lure of XP and loot get to be too much. ;)

But we do that because we think it's FUN. We also get together with whomever's online and go PLAY. Most of us have multiple alts, some guilded some not, so we rely on a chat channel to keep the conversation going. We have members whose main is 60 and members whose main is under 20. Sometimes we're all over the map, sometimes we're all in the same place.

We have a lot of the same issues you mentioned, Jayzan - small numbers, different time zones... it happens. It's become a lot easier once we all decided to not get so worried about it... when we can play together, we do, when we can't, we don't. RL takes priority, things happen... all in all, we have a very low attrition rate, and the few that have gone still stay in touch daily.

I think a big part of our success (the way *we* define it anyway) is that we've decided to be close... we're all friends now outside the game as well, having a couple get-togethers a year. Much like ingame, not everyone can always come, but those that do have a great time hanging out.

In addition to all that, we've made 'alliances' with a few other guilds that have a similar outlook, whether it be RP or what. If there's not a guildie on, more than likely there's someone from one of these guilds doing SOMETHING we can join, or they come join us... or is just someone to chat with while soloing or doing tradeskills. That's gone a long way in keeping everyone involved and enjoying themselves.

Bottom line is, is everyone having fun when they ARE on? If so, awesome! If not, ask around and see what they think would be fun to do, and do it!
