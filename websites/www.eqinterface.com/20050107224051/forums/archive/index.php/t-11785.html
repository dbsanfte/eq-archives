Oh if only EQ worked like that!

The Sambata throwing boulders are, I imagine, gigantic, and yet, gnomes can wield them.

Besides, there ARE throwing axes and various other smaller tools. The best weapon to throw is one that is balanced. Throwing boulders and meteors is good and all, but it really makes absolutely no sense, in EQ, and IRL, which is why you rarely see any sort of 'large' items. Shissar fangs, shurikens, shattering hammers, etc, are small things.

I don't see a berserker as someone who would master throwing. I see a berserker who would run into the fray, go "RAAR", kill lots of people, and probably die doing it. However, for a berserker, they should incorporate a special "Boromir rule" (anyone who's seen/read the Two Towers knows exactly what I'm talking about).

When you go purple, you become invulnerable for the typical DA duration (18 seconds), and have perpetual Mighty Strike and some sort of positive damage modifier. This ability? Rage. Sounds a bit like the warrior rage, but warriors don't seem to be the type that would go berserk. They seem to be more like moral (or immoral, depending on the race), well-trained soldiers, not feral beasts unchecked by society's bounds. The DA effect would make the berserker invulnerable, but like DA, they wouldn't be able to be healed. In fact, they won't be able to be affected by ANY positive spell effect that would involve the alteration of HP or endurance, thus filling the berserker's role, while making him an effective DPS class.

The beserker's entire point, then, would be do deal INCREDIBLE burst damage, but die doing it at higher levels were resses aren't a problem. At lower levels, however, the berserker should fit a role similiarly to a monk, but a bit tougher, and simply use the Rage ability as a sort of emergency tool.

Berserkers should NOT start in town. They should start at the first newbie zone (Butcherblock, Commons, Innothule, etc). I imagine them to shun the cities, and not very well liked back home either (apprehensive to glower, depending on race).

Then again, what do I know?
