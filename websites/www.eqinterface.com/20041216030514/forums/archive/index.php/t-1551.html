This story isn't true. Stories like this, colloquially called "Lighthouse Tins," have circulated in the Navy for decades. Every time the story is told, there is a different ship (sometimes aircraft carriers, sometimes battleships -- the bigger the ship, the bigger the "fish story"). Every time the story is told, it takes place in a different area with different nationalities.

The biggest "noticeable" flaw is that neither radio operator used the international standard for communications and directing traffic.

One of these, so-called, "tins" stories even made it into an Issac Asimov book (_Asimov Laughs Again_, 1992) released before the 10/10/95 date that's given for its release from the US Naval Department.
