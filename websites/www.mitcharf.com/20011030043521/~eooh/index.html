Charter

Core

  • Goal: To make a name for ourselves as quality group members and upstanding players. People should see our guildtag and want us to group with them.
  • Principle: We should be respectful and friendly to other players, regardless of their level/guild/etc.
  • Principle: We should be adventurous and explore Norrath actively.

Elaboration

  • Our goal is the most important part of the core. Why? Because we're likely to be a small guild, especially to begin with. We won't be able to single-handedly hold many raids. But if we have a good reputation, then hopefully we'll have an easy time finding a place for ourselves in raids held by others. This won't happen right away, but it will happen, provided we conduct ourselves well. Of course, raids are a long-term goal. The guild has people of many levels (ranging from around 10 to post-50).
  • Our first core principle is to help us avoid the "pompous jerk" reputation that some of the more powerful guilds have gotten (justly or not). We want to avoid situations where a more powerful group steps on the toes of a less powerful group, just because they can. Our guild is going to rely on making allies, so this is important.
  • We'll be unlike most other guilds in that we won't hold recruiting events. We won't sit in Freeport and offer membership to the first 10 newbies to find us, or whatever. Membership in our guild will pretty much only come through us inviting someone.
  • We need to be selective in our invitations. Our most valuable asset will be our reputation as a guild. We lose that asset if we invite people who do not merit it. We don't want to be elitist (in the sense that we look down on people who aren't in the guild), but we need to be selective.
  • We should try to help out other guild members wherever possible, with teleports, rezzes, etc. This isn't an obligation, of course -- if you're a cleric and the primary healer for your group, it may not be feasible to go to another continent to rez a guild member. Unless the guild member is in dire trouble, they should try to use the services of a local cleric. Remember, our goal is to make a good name for our guild, and people won't look kindly on the group member that abandons them.
  • The guild does not exist for the purposes of twinking and powerleveling. They are not outlawed in certain circumstances, but if a potential guild member is looking for this in a guild, then they are the wrong place.
  • Power-leveling is worse than twinking. Even if the character to be power-leveled doesn't belong to a new player, it still can be detrimental. The only case when it is not harmful is if the player has already played a high-level character of that class. Just because you've got a level 51 druid doesn't mean you could play a level 51 rogue or wizard very effectively. It's important to learn your character as you go up in levels.

Membership

For a new member to join, they have to:
  • Have 3 sponsors, 1 of whom must be an officer. (Obviously the officer may not be of an appropriate level to group with the candidate -- they'll pretty much just talk to the person, make sure they understand what the guild is about, make sure they'll fit in with the guild, etc. At least 1 of the other 2 sponsors, and preferably both, will group with the candidate before giving their approval)
  • Have none of the current officers veto the membership (although only officers can veto, anyone in the guild is encouraged to make the case for a veto if they think it is appropriate)
  • Complete a task, decided upon by the current officers

Officership

To become an officer, a guild member must:
  • Be at least level 46
  • Have been in the guild at least 1 month
  • Have completed a task, decided upon by the current officers
  • Must have the support of two thirds of the current officers
