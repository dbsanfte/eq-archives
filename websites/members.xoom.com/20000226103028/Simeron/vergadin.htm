Vergadain

The Affluent One

Lesser God: God of Wealth, Luck, Merchants, Knowledge, Suspicion

Holy Symbol: Round Gold Coin of Dwarven make

History:

Vergadain is the dwarven god of luck and wealth. He is the trickster god, the schemer, and the rogue, and is greatly venerated by those who get through life by their wits. Clergy of Vergadain are normally on good terms with priests of Abbathor (at least the best terms of all the other dwarven gods) and have a similar set of powers although how you use them is different. While Abbathor worshippers strive to gain wealth through the darker side of life, Vergadain encourages his worshippers to live to the fullest extent of their means. Temples of Vergadain are lavishly decorated, ornate in opulance and always in search of new sources of wealth. Dwarven merchants, shop owners, business people and almost anyone in any trade where the buying and selling of items is done can be found in his followers. Vergadain stops short of endorsing thievery from dwarves (although other races are fair game), but his clergy and worshippers do their utmost to wind up on the top of every deal (including some ways that could be considered unethical).
