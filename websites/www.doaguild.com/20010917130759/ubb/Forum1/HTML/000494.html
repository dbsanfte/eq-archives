You can take this as you like it, but you are leaving out a HUGE piece of information about Chardok. THAT STATEMENT ends that raid, period. I had characters binded there, I rebinded where I was hunting, I'm sure anyone else with two neurons to rub together did the same. All the melee types caught rides out. Hours later when that group that beat us there wipes out, you call for another raid on Chardok. This raid is MUCH different. It's spur of the moment with ZERO chance for any preparation. I'm sorry, but the chance of raid deep into Chardok comming together at the last minute is slim at best. AND EVERYONE KNOWS it's darn unlikely to come together, so few people are going to spend 45 minutes working their way back out there. There is a monumental difference in heading out there and camping the night before or early in the morning before a planned raid and dropping whatever groups you've gotten into and running back there. Plus you can add in the spectre of the folks that got wiped out getting their courage screwed back up and going back in while we're all in transit.

The reason you see poor turnout on many of these raids is that those planning them just assume the raid fairy is gonna show up and wave it's magic wand and make the people you need show up. You have inexperienced raid leaders posting about a raid and just hoping that enough people will show up. That is a severe discourtsey to the people that do show for your raids when they wash out.

You have to speak to the key people you need and, as much as humanly possible, make sure they are going to be there. You may need to get to the site hours and hours early to try and make sure anyone else showing up knows you are going in. You may even have to make some contacts with people in other guilds so you can let them know when you are planning to raid.

Thre are some raids that fail because a given guild just wont support them, but the majority of the time the responsibility belongs to the raid organizer. The correct response to a failed raid is figure out what you did wrong, not wave your hands and walk out because your guild wont support you.
