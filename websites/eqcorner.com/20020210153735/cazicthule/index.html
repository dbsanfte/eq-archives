    Blacksnow Interactive Sues Mythic(DAoC)
    2/8/02, 14:33 PST | Stryker

    I posted this because of the nature of the case. This could have a ripple effect through all MMORPG communities.
    BLACKSNOW INTERACTIVE SUES MYTHIC (DAOC) IN FEDERAL COURT FOR MMORPG PLAYER’S RIGHTS

    Mythic Entertainment is named as the defendant in this case filed on Febuary 5th 11:50AM involving various anti-trust, copyright, and anti-competitive issues. BlackSnow Interactive (BSI) is a group of individuals that play, buy, and sell in various Massively Multiplayer Online Role Playing Games (MMORPG). Growing from only one person nearly two years ago, to seven full-time people, this group has successfully developed a market in which they are able to supply players with gaming currency, items, or characters at competitive prices. Mythic wants BSI to cease all sales immediately.

    After entering the DAoC market, shortly after its release, BSI created a website and began listing their sales on various auction sites, such as Ebay. BSI’s Director of Sales, Lee Caldwell, was quoted as saying, “What it comes down to is, does a MMORPG player have rights to his time, or does Mythic own that player’s time? It is unfair of Mythic to stop those who wish to sell their items, currency or even their own accounts, which were created with their own time. Mythic, in my opinion, and hopefully the court’s, does not have the copyright ownership to regulate what a player does with his or her own time or to determine how much that time is worth on the free market.“

    Caldwell goes on to say, “Mythic’s attempt to stifle competition in their own game makes it possible for only full-time gamers to succeed in the game and most MMORPG players can’t compete on that level. The person that plays just a few hours a week, can’t put in the time required to build their character or collect the items needed to join others in the online battles. No one has stood up to any of these software giants, until now.”

    If you would like to take a look at the actual court document, please visit www.camelotexchange.com

    Gamespy's Mr. Cynic Explains
    2/7/02, 11:03 PST | Kwill

    As you may recall, Gamespy's Mr. Cynic wrote a recent column which attacked EverQuest players for being "whiners" and looking for exploits whenever possible. I responded to his article here on PEQ. He has a new column, "Originality Doesn't Sell," where he defends his position and explains what he meant to say on page 2. He writes:
    The point of my editorial was neither the specifics of the Call of the Hero spell, nor its legitimate or exploitative function. My point was in regards to the presentation of questions/comments from the game's fans, which I thought was rude and totally nonconducive to any kind of rational exchange between developer and gamer. I still think that. Simply put: I, as a developer, would not bother listening to anything brought up in such a manner, legitimate or not.
    Since Absor posted the thread about Call of the Hero with all the player complaints on the Developer's Corner, a public message board, he must be pretty used to us!

    But there is something more to the equation, which Mr. Cynic probably would not know, not being an EQ player: the long term relationship between the Developers at Verant (now Sony Online Entertainment) and the customers playing the game. Those who have played the game for two-plus years remember the days when the Development Team Customer Service representative (who will go un-named for now) would be abusive to the players, and write untruths to get them to stop complaining. Yes, it's different now. But this history cannot be forgotten when you take into account how players talk to the Developers, and vice versa. There has been a lot of water under the bridge since Beta, and most players remember all of the ups and downs of the game's evolution, often documented through the Developer's Corner message boards on the Sony site. Every class and many encounters have undergone some serious changes (good and bad) in the name of "fairness."

    Development of a game as complex as EverQuest is not black and white, but rather a palette of colors, with nothing ever being simple. The balance of fair play and satisfying the player base which has advanced in levels and is testing the highest level content is not easy, and sometimes it is hard to tell what is an exploit and what is a clever way to play the game. Mr. Cynic should also remember that Absor and his team censor all the letters that come to them, and only publish the ones they feel are legitimate on the Developer's Corner. So they have ultimate control on what we see on that forum, and what we don't see--for every angry letter, perhaps there were 100 polite ones.

    So when the players stand up and shout and raise their arms, there may be a legitimate reason for doing so...and they may represent more of the polite players than Mr. Cynic would like to think.

    New GeForce 4 Card
    2/7/02, 7:41 PST | Kwill

    GameSpy reports:
    The leading graphics chip manufacturer has once again turned up the heat on the competition. NVIDIA has just announced their latest next-generation graphics chipset, the GeForce 4. The new chipset is going to come in two forms. For the high end power user (basically you who is reading this) is the GeForce 4 Ti 4600. And for the general consumer out there (everyone else) there is going to be the GeForce 4 MX 460. Successor to the GeForce 3, the GeForce 4 family of GPUs (Graphical Processing Units) contains a dedicated high-speed memory pipeline capable of calculating billions of operations per second. In addition, where current video cards rely on the CPU to handle several calculations, the GeForce 4 will now take over these functions which will allow the processor to concentrate more on game speed. The end result is incredibly realistic graphics - more so than what we've seen with the GeForce 3.

    Under the hood of the GeForce 4 is the nfiniteFX II engine. With built in support for dual vertex shaders, advanced pixel shader pipelines, 3D textures, shadow buffers and z-correct bump mapping, it is now possible to render incredibly complex objects at very fast framerates. Whereas before you had to cut back on your detail levels to maintain some level of performance - with the GeForce 4, gamers will be encouraged to crank up the detail level.

    Read more in the full article!

    Legends Server
    2/6/02, 9:09 PST | Kwill

    Registration for the Legends Server service is now in progress. The "Stormhammer" server should become active next week.

    There is a Legends Server FAQ, which answers some common questions about the new service. Here are a couple of questions and answers that I have heard a few people asking:

    2. What if I cancel my Legends service, what happens to my characters?

    A. Your character will keep any experience or equipment earned on the Legends server(s) and will be moved back to its original server.

    4. I already know that when I move a character to a Legends server I get to keep all my stuff, titles, faction… everything. But what if I cancel and go back to my old server? Will the unique items that I've discovered on the Legends server go with me?

    A. Absolutely. Anything you earn on a Legends server you get to keep.

    6. I see that you are only allowing 8,000 accounts to sign up for the Legends server. What happens if you get more than 8,000 people interested?

    A. More Legends servers will be brought up. Additionally, moves will be allowed between Legends servers once every six months at no additional charge.

    There are answers to other questions on the FAQ, so check it out if you are still unsure about whether the service will be right for you.
