Horror Stories from Neriak
by Torquerist Ancalagon of Imperia Discordia

Torquerist Ancalagon sat glumly at his little campfire somewhere in the icy waste of Everfrost.  Scrub tundra, and hills stretched as far as the eye could see.  "Blast" he thought, "If only I had a cooler temperament I would be back at the Enchanters Academy and enjoying the pleasures of Neriak, not to mention every curve of Mare X'ottl's luscious anatomy.  "Heh the little slut mistress is probably a whore at the Maidens Fancy dancing for stuffed shirt ambassadors by now.  I wonder if the priestesses ever restored the tongue of that loud mouthed pompous oaf of a professor back at the academy?"  The familiar smirk that had been missing from his dark features returned and he chuckled quietly to himself.

Returning south alone 2 weeks prior from a terrific battle at the stronghold of Permafrost the Dark Elf mercenary had the displeasure of running amok of the notorious McMarrin Brothers.  "What have we got here Lish?!" Bellowed the red haired Bandl to his Tawny haired brother.  Before his brother could reply Torquerist smiled sardonically and piped off, "I don't know, Halitosis?, a penchant for buggering goblin whelps?, the desire to breed with ones kin?"  The two huge warriors stopped dumfounded, mouths agape.  Recognizing his slim chances against the Burly Northmen Torquerist used this pause to give them the Teir-dal "Peace Sign" with a sarcastic wink and disappeared down the icy slope in a flurry of snowflakes.

The game of cat and mouse continued for days.  Even with all the trickery and guile bestowed on him by his dark race Torquerist came upon the realization that he could not elude the McMarrins forever in their homeland.  Not to mention with all his backtracking and false trail making he had become hopelessly lost.

The fire burned low as he reflected on his troubled youth.  Out of sheer stubbornness he reached for his food bag.  "Empty again!" he spat.  Three days since the last of his foodstuffs had been consumed.  Shivering he threw the empty bag into the fire, pulled his blanket over his head and moved closer to the fire.  He could feel the weakness of starvation seeping into his limbs.  "Tomorrow" he thought uncomfortably, "Tomorrow I will go and meet the McMarrin's and end this game."

Just at that moment he heard a shuffling and a soft whimper coming from the darkness beyond the other side of the fire.  "Hullo Sah?", squeaked a little voice as a brown haired shivering halfling came into the firelight.  "Hullo Sah, may I share your fire sah?"  "Certainly little one", said Torquerist from within his blanket.  "Come, take your ease from the cold night air."  Torquerist gently removed the blanket from his head, smiled warmly, and raised both palms in a gesture of peace as the quaking halfling turned to run.  "Pray!", he shouted earnestly.  "Do not flee little one.  I am no danger to you."  The halfling stopped at the edge of the firelight.  "I have not eaten in days and barely have the strength to stand let alone cause harm to your person even if I had a wish to do so."  The halfling relaxed a trifle.  Torquerist added mournfully, "I fear I shan't survive the morrow, please", he motioned the halfling to sit, "come sit and talk with me.  Let me not perish alone lost and hungry in this accursed place."  The halfling moved cautiously to the fire and sat down nervously.  "What is your name little one?"  "Ddddan", he shivered out.  "Tell me, Ddddan", he mimicked with a weak humorous smile, "How come you this place?  And have you any food or know the way to "Blackburrow Pass?"  Dan, bolstered by the compassion and lack of movement by his new acquaintance began relating a harrowing tale of how his merchant caravan had been ambushed by a tribe of mountain orcs in the area, and how he to his knowledge was the only survivor.  "Cursed Orcs they shall all drown in lakes of blood", muttered Torquerist vehemently.

"In answer to your question, yes I know the way to Blackburrow Pass", Dan offered cheerfully.  Dan rummaged with nimble fat little fingers in his pack and produced a worn piece of parchment and showed Torquerist a map of the region.  After some perusing of the map and some prodding from a slightly more animated Torquerist, Dan was able to pinpoint their approximate location and devise a route to Blackburrow.  "Very clever little one", said Torquerist with a smile.  "Now what do we have to sustain us on our journey?"  At this Dan sat back dejectedly and put his face in his hands.  "Sadly nothing", he exclaimed mournfully.  "I haven't eaten since the massacre two days ago.  Pity the journey is 5 days from here, we will both surely starve afore we reach Blackburrow", he blubbered into his hands.  "Have cheer little one.  At least we shall share eachothers company in these last few hours", Torquerist said warmly and patted Dan gently on the back.  Dan uncovered his face and grinned.  Chuckling with ironic humor Dan said, "Pity one of us doesn't care to eat Dark Elf or Halfling one of us might make it!"  Torquerist snickered at the jest.  "Oh that is a fine joke little one!" , he laughed.  Laughter became maniacal as he deftly rose and drew razor sharp steel.

The End
