Of Valor and Honor, Part III
by Mythrilmire

After what seemed like an eternity of walking through the streets of Freeport, the two guards escorting Mythrilmire halted and told Myth to wait outside as they entered an old building. Within a few minutes, one of the guards opened the door and summoned him inside. Mythrilmire warily entered the building and instantly the door slammed shut behind him. Realizing how dark it was as he could barely make out the outline of the guard which called him in; Myth started to become uneasy.

Mythrilmire felt a surge of strength in him that he had never felt before, then a blow to the head, which hardly affected him. Turning to his attacker, he realized that this guard was no escort at all. Before Myth could even decide what to do he felt his body go out of control. By no will of his own, he chanted a few words and flames engulfed the guard that had struck him. With a speed, he never knew he had, he turned swiftly and rooted the other and backed off, just to unleash another barrage of fire at him. In what seemed only seconds both guards were lying on the floor and appeared to be quite dead.

Disbelief overwhelmed Myth as he struggled to understand what had just happened. He wondered why the two guards had attacked him, but more so wondered how he was able to defeat them. He had a hard time fighting an Orc Centurion solo, lest two well armed and well trained guards. Just as he was about to make a run for the door and leave this wretched place, he felt himself being enveloped in a blue aura of light. Suddenly, he was not where he was moments earlier. He was standing in the middle of a large room with cobblestone walls and a large hearth set in the north wall. In the center of the room was a table with several tomes, which seemed to call him. Slowly, he stepped towards the table and peered into the larger of the tomes, which was open and centered on the table.

“Do not dig thyself deep into the unknown child, lest ye’ are ready to face the unknown”, an aged voice echoed through the room. Startled, Myth turned around to see who had spoken to him. Next to the hearth where moments ago was empty floor, stood an old decrepit looking man with a staff in one hand. Myth looked around frantically for an exit door. “Do not be frightened Mythrilmire”, the old man said convincingly. “Please excuse my fear old man, for I am running short on trust at the moment”, Myth blurted out. “How do you know my name? Who are you? What do you want from me?”

“Slow down lad, all these I shall answer, save your breath for an answer that I seek”, the man spoke calmly.

“What has become of your father, child?” “Allangazar!” exclaimed Myth. “Are you my father’s friend, the mighty wizard, Allangazar?” “You answer a question with a question, young one. I do hope your meditation skills are more seasoned than your social skills.” Quirked the man as he managed pulled something from his robes. “Yes, I am Allangazar child. I am a long time friend of your fathers. We attended the schools of magic together and were the closest of friends, until he met your mother of course.”

Mythrilmire, finally at ease sat down with his father’s friend and began explaining why he was here. He told him of how his father died at the hands of the CrushBone Orcs, defending a small caravan of travelers. How he had met his knight friend and traversed the Ocean of Tears to find him as his father instructed. He then told him of his incident with the guards that had tried to kill him. In return, Allangazar informed Mythrilmire that he felt his presence the moment he landed in Freeport, that he had charmed him from afar, and that was how he had bested the guards. “For you my child are the last in a lineage of wizards that is truly gifted and shall be protected by the gods, that is until they are done with you.”

After an eternity of shared dialogue, Myth still had an unanswered question. “What is the purpose of my being here and what is it that I am meant to accomplish?” Myth stated impatiently. With this question, Allangazar stood up from the stool he was sitting in and slowly walked towards the Hearth. “I can teach you more in the ways of the spells of the wizard, but thou destiny is thine own. Why you are here I cannot say, lest you discover for yourself the answers to your own destiny.” “Find the answers to where I lie and your question I shall answer.”

Mythrilmire turned around exhaled, “Why do you talk in riddles, can’t you just answer my question?” When he heard no response he turned towards the old man and found that he was again alone. Myth heard the voice within himself speak in the tongue of the old man. “I shall be with you Mythrilmire, do not give up. Think of your past, clear the way for your future and when you least expect it, I shall return. For now, I have other matters to tend. Make yourself at home and study the arts. Until then think with your heart and avoid the Freeport Guard.
