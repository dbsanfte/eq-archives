    Some Reminders
    12/6/01, 19:21 PST | Kwill

    Absor posted these hints and reminders for getting Luclin to run correctly:
    DirectX 8.1 is required to play EverQuest. I wanted to make that clear. Problems like "No 3D devices found" and many others can be fixed by upgrading to DX 8.1. Run DXDiag.exe and make sure the version says "DirectX 8.1 (4.08.01.0881)".

    For those using NVIDIA cards, the 23.11 drivers have proven to give much better performance in EverQuest. You can find a link to them here on our driver support page.

    Clipping Plane! Remember, you can pull in your clipping plane if you are having problems with your framerate.

    Choosing which models to load. Ester asked me to make sure that everyone understood that when we talk about going to the "options" to choose which models to display, we mean the options BEFORE you log in, not the options in game. This is done before you log in because it has to know what models to load before you log in.

    Another tip for the more advanced computer user – It might be a good idea to turn off your swap file, run a defrag and then turn your swap file back on. It's nice to have an unfragmented swap file. If you don't know how to do this, then don't worry about it.

    Oh! And, yes, there is a typo in the printed manual that comes with the game. I apologize to all of the budding young makeup artists out there for calling you Rouges rather than Rogues.


    Bazaar Feature
    12/6/01, 19:18 PST | Kwill

    A player asked Absor in the Developer's Corner about the promised player-merchant/bazaar feature. Absor had this to say:
    It was certainly on our desired feature list. And we are still working on adding it to the game.

    We just didn't have time to get all this code working properly for release. Because of the high potential for duplication of items or even item loss when dealing with this kind of code, we've decided it was best to hold on to this feature until it was done. We plan to get it into the game as soon as we can. We decided that delaying the ship of Luclin for this feature, as much as we wanted to get it in, would be a bad idea because players often plan time off around the release of a new expansion and we didn't want to change the date and upset those plans. And rushing a feature like this would lead, at the minimum, to a badly implemented feature. So we thought it was prudent to hold off until it was ready.

    Two-Faced?
    12/6/01, 15:52 PST | Kwill

    Absor addressed the problem of old faces and new ones, and changing back and forth:
    The old and new faces are linked. Each new face corresponds to an old face, ones that they were based on. So when your old characters are given their new models, they are given a face that was designed from their old face.

    This also means that when you change your new face, you also change your old one. If you really want the old face back you just have to change the new face back to the one that matches your old face. So if you think that there is a chance that you'll want to revert to the old models, take a screenshot of your new face when you first log in with that character and change back to that face if you decide to use the old models.

    But remember, in the near future we will be turning off the ability to change your face at will, so make your decision as early as you can.


    New Interface?
    12/6/01, 15:50 PST | Kwill

    Absor explains in the Developer's Corner why there is no interface:
    We decided to hold off on the new interface. It's going to be rolled out to all our customers, so it's not really a Luclin feature. Because we decided to make the new interface available to everyone, we had to make sure that it would work with all of the old commands. And since it wasn't a feature specifically for Luclin, we decided not to spend too much Luclin production time on its creation.

    We also wanted to take the time to let folks try the interface and give us feedback before it is finalized. We just couldn't do that under the time constraints of producing Shadows of Luclin.

    When we have a better idea when we'll be able to release the new interface, I'll let you know.

    The Darker Side of Gaming
    12/6/01, 11:41 PST | Kwill

    Wired Magazine's article, "The Quest to End Game Addiction" brings the often-discussed issue of game addiction into the spotlight again.

    Can games be addicting, like cigarettes, alcohol, or drugs? There is certainly no physical component, but the urge to escape into a different world can sometimes be overwhelming to the point of neglecting the rest of one's life.

    It's a left-handed compliment to Verant that they created a game that can exert this much power over someone's life, making it so immersible and irresistable. Whether or not you believe that you can be "addicted" to a computer game, it's a fact that many people make choices to spend most of their time playing online rather than doing other things, which can make loved ones and family unhappy (as well the players themselves). Food for thought!

    Today's Patch Message
    12/6/01, 11:27 PST | Kwill

    December 6th, 3:00 am

    ------------------------------ *Fixed Bugs*

    - Fixed a bug that was preventing characters from being bald (those that were allowed to).
    - Halved the speed of the new Skeleton walk and run animations because they were playing about twice as fast as they should have been.
    - Fixed a bug that was causing all of the facial features (hair, beard, etc...) to switch back to default in certain cases when you switched from one face to another (faces with special eyes, like the faces with eye patches and white eye).
    - Fixed a crash bug with /follow.
    - Found and Fixed the crash bug with soldungb and everfrost and other places.
    - Fixed transparency code with i810 display adapters
    - Fixed a problem introduced into the sound engine that causes framerate problems and sound stutter in some places.
    - Fixed a crash bug that had to do with dying from your familiar effect.
    - Research the group buff bug and fixed it.
    - Fixed a crash bug with root and gate.
    - Various item and content fixes.

    *Hot Items* (The items we're considering top priority)

    - Bard issues: We're working to correct the problems bards have been experiencing with their songs.
    - K6 Processors: We're working to fix, or provide a work-around for problems AMD K6 users are experiencing when entering some zones.
    - Armor layer tinting (e.g. tinting your armor without tinting your skin)

    We are reviewing the issues sent to us as they are sent in and will be doing daily patches as necessary to get everything working to your and our satisfaction as quickly as possible. Please remember that EverQuest users need to have DirectX 8.1 and the appropriate drivers for their video card. Visit our tech support center first if you are having issues: http://everquest.station.sony.com/support/luclin/index.jsp
