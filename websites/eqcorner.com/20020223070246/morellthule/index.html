    Legends Calendar System
    2/20/02, 8:25 PST | Kwill

    Will the calendar system on the legends server be enforced by the GMs, unlike player-based calendar systems on the regular servers? This issue is addressed in this thread on the Developer's Corner.

    The answer:

    In the past EverQuest Customer Service did not recognize player created web calendars for spawn contention resolution. The difficulty with multiple calendars, who hosted said calendars and a myriad of other issues made such enforcement not practicable. Given that the Legends service Calendar of Events is a single source, monitored and approved by EverQuest Customer Service, it will be a legitimate factor for the resolution of altercations. However, it will not be the only factor.
    Here are some of the mitigating factors:
  • The ability of the force present to tackle their scheduled target;
  • The current and past conduct of the players or guilds involved in the dispute as well as any record listed on the Calendar of Events;
  • While notifying others of your intent to tackle a raid zone is nice, if this activity is not open to others, we may not post it;
  • The constant monopolization of encounters will not be allowed.

    • Will this solve some of the calendar problems that other servers have encountered when they were purely player-enforced? Can the calendar be non-biased given it is being run by the GMs? Only time will tell...

    Old Quest Returns
    2/19/02, 19:02 PST | Kwill

    From the Station newsletter:
    Lost Quests - The Blued Steel Hammer

    The "Blued Steel Hammer" is a weapon of legendary repute, but whose existence has been relegated to myth. Most people believe it is no longer in the game.

    Those people are wrong!

    The "Blued Steel Hammer" was the reward for a very old and notorious quest that could not be completed for a while. Most players have forgotten about the hammer…

    However, the Blued Steel Hammer is out there. It is one of several items that no one has yet discovered. Or "rediscovered," in this case.

    "I implemented the Blued Steel Hammer quest a long time ago, before last E3, but no one has discovered it yet," says Jeremy Ellis, designer on EverQuest. "'Old school' players should recognize the name of the item and the quest it starts with, known as the 'Priestess of Najena' quest."

    So, start questing!


    Legends Launches Feb 22
    2/19/02, 18:58 PST | Kwill

    From the Station newsletter:
    The Legends Begin February 22, 2002!

    Off a small island between Erudin and Antonica, the mighty ship White Maelstrom and her crew met an untimely demise. What will follow as a consequence of that long-ago tragedy will rock the world of Norrath and test the mettle of every adventurer. No matter your experience, young or old, valorous or villainous, it will be up to you to help turn back a coming tide of darkness that could change the world irrevocably!

    Dateline Freeport: Like the ripples from a stone dropped in a glassy sea, an unhallowed call-to-arms spreads throughout Norrath from the newly risen Dorian Dulein, pirate captain of the Rotting Revenge who was slain in the naval battle with the White Maelstrom. In answer, the bones of dead sailors claw their way back to undead life and besiege the city of Freeport, their single-minded goal: Obey Dulein's irresistible summons…

    Dateline Oasis: The echoing call of Dulein crosses oceans of sea and sand alike, penetrating The Oasis, where it reaches the long-dead and dried ears of the sinister Captain Blackhand. Forced into undeath by Dulein's call, it is only through Blackhand's insurmountable will and boundless greed that he and his loyal sailors are able to resist Dulein's command and seek out their most prized treasure, buried long ago somewhere in the Oasis…

    Dateline Emerald Jungle: Scenting chaos in the air, the followers of the mighty Iksar general, Venril, rally in the depths of the Emerald Jungle with plans of their own…

    Stranded by his own evil arts on a small island after his defeat and death, Dulein calls his new home the Marauder's Mire, where he now rules as a tortured god. However, a brash young pirate seeking the legendary plunder of the lost island has recently disturbed Dulein's solitude. His anger has opened a permanent gateway to his Mire and unleashed a summons throughout the world so powerful that wherever a pirate has died in Norrath, their spirit would again find their corpse and rise to join Dulein in his new home, razing all that lay in their path.

    These server-wide events, plus many more, will culminate in the opening of a brand new zone unique to the Legends Server. Called the Marauder's Mire, this new land will challenge players of all levels, but they must first discover its location before they may begin seeking the untold pirate's treasures hidden there!

    The Legends events begin February 22, 2002. Get the full story from "Marauders of the Sea: A New History of Piracy in Norrath" and visit the Legends Service Homepage to make your mark on EverQuest!
