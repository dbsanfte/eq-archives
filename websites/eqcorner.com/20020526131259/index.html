Rich Waters on Caster Balance
5/25/02, 8:02 PST | Kwill

Rich Waters, EQ lead designer, posted this comment on Graffe's Wizard Compliation message board about the progress of caster balance:
Caster balancing isn't done, far from it in fact. We're very aware of it, and it's a priority of ours to finish. I understand that it's taking more time than players would like, and it's definately taking more time than I hoped to get it out to live servers.

I see a lot of posts saying how easy caster balance would be to do, or "here's one thing that would help us a lot and is quick to do!". Honestly, I wish that were true. I'd be very happy to have caster balance done and get on to the next improvements and additions to the game.

The fact is though, while small changes for each class are pretty obvious and easy to do, the bigger picture has to be maintained all the time. I've learned from the process so far that it's not good to release things bit by bit - the average player wants to know the big picture for each change as it happens. How does this change affect me? What do other classes get out of it? Will my class see more changes? Is caster balancing done now? I get these questions every day, and see them posted on community boards all over when any small change is made.

For example, we've got some plans to improve healing versatility for the priest classes. To do that correctly, all classes that can heal need to be looked at to see how they'll be impacted. Give one class a better heal and all the other healing classes look at how it affects them. The new heal spell may make perfect sense when looking at the class that got it, but any class that didn't get it has immediate concern about why they didn't also get it, how it makes them less wanted in a given situation, and how they now need something to make up for the other class getting a new heal. This means that before we can alter a single small part of healing, we need to have answers for players about the plans for healing across every class.

This gets more complex because no class has just one single ability. So, say (hypothetically) that we add a better heal to druids. Clerics are immediately concerned because druids are now healing better- what do clerics get? Wizards grow concerned because now not only can druids nuke pretty well, they also can heal even better than previously, making them a potentially better deal for a group or raid looking for members. No matter how small a change is, it causes ripples throughout the game, the player base, and all the community message boards. When it comes to class balancing, no change is really a small change.

So, on to the point - We're still working on caster balance, and intend to make the necessary improvements. We expect the additional changes to pets to be done soon, and then intend to focus on priests for a bit. We've been keeping up with the message boards and community feedback, so we've got a good handle on the concerns of various classes. Thanks for the continued interest and patience as we try to get this implemented in the best way possible.

Physical Enhancements
5/25/02, 7:55 PST | Kwill

A player wrote into the Developer's Corner to ask why the physical enhancement skill, which "adds an additional 2% to the Natural Durability, Combat Agility and Combat Stability archetypes" is only available to hybrids. Absor replied:
This ability came out of beta testing for Luclin.

Originally there were just the basic archetype abilities.

But there was some discussion among the testers about the usefulness of those abilities for hybrids. The logic went something like this.

Let’s assume that a theoretical hybrid was 75% as effective as a warrior in each of those melee aspects (the warrior being the base at 100%).

If we boost the warrior’s ability by 5% (of the 100) he gains 5%. If we boost the hypothetical hybrid by 5% (of the 75) he gains (gets out a calculator) 3.75%.

So the warrior with one level of the skill is 105% and the hybrid is 78.75%.

So rather than the hybrid being 25% less than the warrior, he’s 26.25% less…

As you add the levels of these bonuses up, you end up increasing the gap between the hypothetical hybrid’s melee skills and those of the warrior.

Now there was indeed much discussion about this, because if you look at it the other way, the hybrid is still 75% of the warrior (105 x .75 = 78.75). But in the end we decided to allow the hybrids to purchase a little more effectiveness.

That’s why it’s only available to the hybrids.

Caster Balance?
5/23/02, 18:16 PST | Kwill

A player wrote and asked Absor, what has happened with the caster balance and specifically the focus items that were promised? Absor replied,
Sorry about that. It’s no excuse, but the reality is that we have a lot going on here, E3 not the least of them. It’s one rather complicated balancing act.

But I assure you that the work on balancing continues, as does the work on the new interface and the bazaar. I’m not going to be able to give you any solid dates, but hopefully we’ll have more for you on all these items soon.

I have heard that the next steps in caster balance will be to finish up with pets, then probably move on to the priest classes for a bit.

Our desire is to do it properly, and for that we’ll just need more time. All I can do is ask for your understanding and sympathize with your frustration. We all want this to be done so we can move on to the next thing, and the team is working very hard to make that happen.

Player Event: NNDR Report
5/22/02, 13:51 PST | Kwill

Teeneeweenee Wombateer of the Swift Justice Guild of Antonious Bayle (chief guild event reporter and guild pinup)writes:
Hi all! just to let you know the first NDDR (Naken Drunken Dwarven Rally) was a small but successful event. Only five entrants in total, but all had a good laugh!

The event started at the Kaladim gate with the lineup being Arthur, Dregster, Gred, Racewinner and Bakenor. All were generously supplied with ale and at the appointed time, were told the position of the first checkpoint.

This was at moderator Dubii who was positioned just outside the wall of Butcherblock docks to the south. After hunting him down, (some returned with a puzzled look as I told them to head for the docks!) Wobbling back they were then told by Dubii to head for the zone entrance of Dagnor's Cauldron.

After reaching Veirnal at the Unrest zone, it was neck and neck with Dregster just ahead with Bakenor following behind. Gred had finally caught up and Arthur and Racewinner were already well around the cauldron.

The next port of call was the Crushbone entrance, a well known place for many a traveller! Lanas was stationed at this point and after the long trip there, Bakenor had pulled ahead, obviously a dwarf with a good sense of direction! After giving a hug to Lanas, they headed over to the Chessboard, back over in Butcherblock. Wodge was hidden well around the undead haunt but that didn't stop the runners finding him within short order!

Now the leaders were on the final home stretch! Sprinting back to the Kaladim entrance, Bakenor just pipped Dregster back with seconds to spare. After putting the others out their misery and calling them all back, the prizes of 50plat to Bakenor and 20plat to Dregster were awarded amid much cheering and congratulations.

EQ2 Interview
5/20/02, 15:01 PST | Kwill

GameSpy has an exclusive interview with Bill Trost, designer of EQ2.

From the interview:

EverQuest II is built upon 4+ years of experience creating, expanding, and maintaining a very successful fantasy MMORPG. The first generation of games did a pretty complete job of taking the text mud experience, adding graphics, and bringing it to the masses. I think we are going to see far more variety in content and deeper game play in this second generation of games in addition to mind-blowing graphical advances. Accessibility is also something that we are striving for with EverQuest II, and I think this will be a common goal with most of the successful games this time around. So, bottom line, I think we will see a wide variety of deep, yet accessible, persistent online games. It is an exciting time to be an online gamer.
