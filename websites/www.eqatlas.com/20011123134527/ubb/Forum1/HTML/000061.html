As I began my journey to the middle of the great sea between Faydwer and Antonica, I was determined to find this vile beast who has been the thorn in Hasten Bootstrutter's side for far too long. This Ancient Cyclops had been troubling my gnomish kin for long enough, and it was time, I decided, to prove my worth to Hasten.

Upon landing at an island with many beautiful female wood elves, I departed the boat and began my search. The very first island I came across had a lone Cyclops standing on the shore, a warning for any younger travelers to stay away. Being a master of the dead, experiencing forty four levels of growth, I decided to attempt to slay this monstrosity and make Norrath safer for everyone. After the smoke cleared, the Cyclops lay at my feet, crumbled into a heap. Although it pleased me greatly that I vanquished this (harder than most other) Cyclops, I was partially disappointed that I did not stumble across the infamous Ancient Cyclops.

After scouring the island for a few minutes and finding a merchant who did not like me very much in the center of the island, I returned to the beach where the Cyclops had just fallen. I viewed all of my surroundings and lo and behold, another vile Cyclops had appeared in the distance on the beach, where the previous one was standing. Again, I decided to battle it, and again, was victorious. To my amazement (ooc: not really :P) more and more Cyclops and other assorted vile creatures appeared where each previous had appeared exactly five and a half minutes after I defeated the previous creature. The Ancient Cyclops had been rumored to reside on this isle somewhere, so I hoped to entice him to show himself by killing all of his kinsmen who did.

After about two hours of waiting and killing the Ancient Cyclop's friends, the monstrosity showed himself. How lucky, I thought to myself, because many of my elder brethren have yet to lay eyes on him, or have looked for hours upon hours upon hours at a time. After a long and closely contested battle, I emerged victorious. I was so happy, I was visably shaken when I saw that message that the Ancient Cyclops was no more. However, I did not see the beast actually fall. I was on the beach while my trusty compatriot Jobobtik assisted my damage over time spells in felled the beast. I arose to find the corpse, to obtain the ring Hasten was seeking as evidence to my worthiness, but the body was nowhere to be found.

I frantically scoured the island for about five minutes, and began calling out for help from the surrounding area. After a couple minutes, a kindly wizard met me in the middle of the island, and that is when the horror of my situation finally sunk in. Behind him, I could see the corpse. As if to spite me, the Ancient Cyclops, with his last remaining health, climbed what must have been the largest tree on the island and gasped his last breath on the very top, out of my reach. I was devastated, for I could not reach his precious ring. Even with a levitation spell, neither the wizard nor I could reach it, and the ring was left to rot.

After taking a brief hiatus in Paineel gathering bone chips for my undead pets, I returned to the place that I had earlier seen the Ancient. I was again determined to taunt the Ancient Cyclops out of his hiding by destroying each and every one of his kinsmen that showed themselves. Hopefully, another Ancient Cyclops would even exist!! The Ancients must have not liked me killing one of their own and then having the audacity to return to their home, for after killing only three of the inferior Cyclops', another Ancient appeared, attempting to avenge his recently departed friend. This time, I called out to a nature loving druid friend I knew in the area for assistance, and we went about slaying the Ancient, keeping him on the beach this time. The ring was looted and my contribution to Hasten would be rewared.

I am now adventuring through Norrath with a gift from my gnome brother called Journeyman Boots which give me an uncannily quick running ability.

Every time I outrun a beast who has gained the upper hand in battle, I will remember my experiences with the vile Ancient Cyclops and how happy I was to stumble across a fast gnome in the middle of Rathe Mountains.
