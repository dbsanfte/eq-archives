This game is so incredibly deep that it isn't only about killing monsters.....

Are you a fun loving Halfling who likes adventure and seems to have a problem with things falling into your pockets (usually ones that belong to others)???

This game is not only about killing things...

Adventurer 		posted 10-17-2000 04:41 PM            
EQ is a true role playing game. Why? Because just like in real life, you play a role...and it goes on and on and on and on and on. Everquest does not have a fixed plot or a finite ending (like most games), and neither does real life.

Yes this is repetitive at times, just like real life. But unlike real life, EQ will let you achieve some of those fantasy dreams you normally cannot do with real life, like becoming a Hero/Heroine, becoming incredibly rich, and completing difficult quests and goals.

It takes persistence and some patience. At the lower levels, EQ seems like nothing more than a repetitive dungeon crawl game (like Diablo2). All you do at the lower levels is slash at bats and snakes... and you do this hundreds of times just to make enough money to buy any decent equipment. I am aware of this, since thats exactly what I did to go up in levels. And for anyone's information, I played the hard way... I earned everything! I was not twinked with ultra-powerful items when I was a low level character. I earned all my hard platinums. I never begged for it like some lamers do.

At the middle levels it's important to make friends. Making friends is the single most important skill in Everquest. Without friends and allies in this game, you are a loner.

If you are a loner type, then you have no reason to be playing a social role playing game like Everquest. Find a different game.

With friends, you belong to a group, to a community. You have people that watch your back, people you greet, respect, hunt, socialize and trade with on a regular basis. I have so many friends now that whenever I travel in remote regions, I can normally bump into a friend that will do me favors, like casting BUFFS or SOW on my character. They ask nothing in return, because of the friendship.

Let me point out again that Everquest is a role playing game. People here are for the love of role play. How intense is the role playing?

Well, consider the fact that an online "wedding ceremony" (between 2 enamored dark elf players, no less) was just done at the Xegony server. Thats how serious it gets sometimes.

Could EQ be better and even more "deep"? Of course. Some day I would LOVE to see players have the ability to own property, to ability to build and own their own dwellings (houses, castles, towers) just like in other games like Ultima Online.

EQ is a very deep and rich game as it is. Expansions and upgrades come out regularly, and it gets bigger, better and deeper all the time.

This is to second what chinaberry said about knowing how mobs affect you and your party.

I have to add that it's extremely important that you know the mobs before you engage them. Know the faction consequences.

Here is an example.

For many levels I never had problems with bears and wolves and lions. I didnt really bother them, they never bothered me.

Then one day I joined a group that enjoyed hunting these animals. To stay with the group, it was in the best interest for me to KILL these animals.

Strange. The animals are unintelligent and normally dont belong to any faction. In the end, my participation in hunting these wild animals has ended up having me as their regular enemy from then on. Any time I log into the game and see a lion, the lion charges me. Same with bears, same with wolves. They seem to keep a vendetta on you.

I used to walk past lions and bears peacefully. Nowadays the minute I walk near a bear or lion, a fight will ensue.

No longer can I walk in peace among the wild animals.

This is a warning for you newbie players before you do something that will affect you for the rest of your character life.
