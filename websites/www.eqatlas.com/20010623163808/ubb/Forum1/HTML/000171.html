I just see this as an unnecessary addition, that will be more trouble than it is worth in the long run (at least from my perspective). I do not see the GEMS feature actually breeding harmony between players, and THE ANGRY FEW would take death at the hands of Tetris addicts reason for a direct confrontation. Seriously from a role playing standpoint I want the person meditating to be trying to stay in tune with the environment by paying attention to the chat (and focusing his energy from the environment) rather than playing Tetris (or reading a book, vacuuming, or whatever)... While meditating, there are plenty of things to do, including using emotes to make your character come to life. Another reason that it bothers me is that from a Role-play standpoint we will see zone-wide arguments about deaths by this, as well as spam about scores and COMPETITION (like EQ doesn't already have enough of that)....

Even if I were to disable shout (which is useful for knowing about trains and communicating to the entire zone) as well as /ooc I would still be subjected to gems discussions. "Gems was made for those who solo or when your in the city selling items you got from Guk. or when your camping the AC need i say more. leave it to some players to complain. 2. Verant wants to enforce naming rules to preserve the "immersiveness", they then add a puzzle game to ruin it and then give players something to spam throughout chat <high scores and competition> and remove them from the environment as well.

GEMS is even worse of a detraction than bad names and reduces EQ to the level of a chat room, meaning that Verant has abandoned their "vision"... I think that Verant has finally destroyed the EQ environment completely (with GEMS), even in the light of the naming policy enforcement that they were trying to use to retain their integrity of the "World"... The world of Norrath I envisioned was for "playing your role" (ie RP) and I personally didn't want any reminder of the outside world, and the names (and rampant twinking) were a major detraction from that.

Currently, the majority of players do not adhere to their roles (i.e. stay in character), don't name themselves to suit the fantasy environment, or play the game as Verant once intended (RPG anyone?). This game was Verant's (temporary) solution to complaints about "downtime", that amazingly didn't require any of their resources, and rather than actually fix those problems they distract from it.
