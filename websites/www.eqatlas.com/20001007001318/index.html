Verant Goes Too Far...
Thursday, October 5th

Verant recently banned a player for posting, on EQ Vault's message boards, this story. I'm not sure if this info is accurate, however, and I think the actual post has been removed, and involves the death and rape of a fourteen-year-old DE boy. It's kind of irrelevant to me, actually, which story it was.

I'm honestly practically speechless that Verant would pull something like this. They are obviously exercising their right to ban anyone for any reason, but now I'm worried that I too could be banned for speaking out against the Company. I've removed my section describing what characters I play so they have a harder time tracking me down.

Verant has no moral right to ban this person. I'm sure that they think that they do. Free speech isn't simply a legal protection, it's something that people should inherently believe in, regardless of the law. Voltaire got that, but apparently the other big V does not.

Updates
Sunday, September 17th

Went through a week and a half of email today and updated some stuff. I also went through a bunch of the zones that I wrote descriptions on over a year ago to bring them up-to-date.

I also found out I was wrong about Halflings getting their own forge. They can sew clothing and bags, woopie. My advice to those who want to do trade skills in general is that unless you have a serious personal use for it (e.g. Rangers with fletching or Rogues with poison) or can make a ton of money off of it (e.g. Enchanters who do jewelry), then don't do it. You can just as easily spend all that time (and it's a *lot* of time) adventuring, where you will make enough money to buy the item you need and, in addition, make experience. I've also heard some people enjoy that stuff. The market for most of that stuff these days is diminished as well, because of the major twinking that goes on.

The DNS information still hasn't reached me in apparently distant LA, so the launch of the AO site is delayed until that happens...
