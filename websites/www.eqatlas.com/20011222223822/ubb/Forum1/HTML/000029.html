Other sites have recently posted something that Gordon said, I've included the relevant bits here:

As far as camping goes, about all we determined is that people differ widely in opinion on the issue. One of the most common suggestions is that we work actively to promote more rewards for playing the game in the preferred manner, and leave rewards as they currently exist for camping.

Unfortunately, doing that would require making playing the game in the preferred manner easier, safer, faster, or more profitable, which would in our opinion decrease the fun to be had from it. In our opinion, there is more fun to be had from playing the game in the preferred manner than by camping, and that fun should be the sufficient additional reward to get people to do it.

However it's obviously not, so we're left with reducing the reward for camping as an option. Of course, many made it clear in the threads they they prefer camping over doing anything else in the game. Hence our quandry.

He goes on to say that they're not going to make any changes. You can find the full post here.

I thought I'd discuss this. Reading through the above post carefully, I've determined that the true flaw at Verant is that their use of logic is basically non-existent. You're correct on that one, Gordon.

However, he goes on to state that the possible correction to that, making it "easier, safer, faster, or more profitable" would actually decrease our enjoyment of the game. Call me crazy, but if he made those changes, everyone I know in game wouldn't have been upset they didn't get into the AO beta (myself included). That's exactly what people want, and if Verant did that, it seems in line with their Vision where the preferred method was more fun.

However, Gordon goes on to state that they can't do that. ?? Which leaves them with nerfing, if you will, camping. ?? They don't want to do that because some people apparently actually enjoy camping (and cuz 50,000 accounts being cancelled in one day would really hurt the bottom line), so they're going to leave things the way they are.

In a nutshell, what Gordon is saying is this: Doing a dungeon, as per our Vision, turned out to not be as fun or rewarding as we planned. We cannot enhance this, because that would, trust us on this one, actually decrease your fun. The only option to make it more fun than camping would, in fact, be to make camping even less fun. Since we don't want you all to have less fun, you can plainly see how we are forced to make no changes.

On Ladel's comments:

I can see how what they said could be interpreted this way. My hangup is more with their argument, where they state that their intent was to make it more fun to do the game the preferred way, but for whatever reason this isn't fun to people. This could also be interpreted as saying that, although it is more fun, people still don't do it because it isn't as profitable as camping (in terms of whatever measure you use, exp, gold, etc.).

My problem, I guess, is more with the fact that they assume that they would have to make a huge change to the game in order to make the preferred method more profitable (or more fun) so that people would do it more. All I'm asking for is that they make it even possible to do things the preferred way.

One thing that stops this is the huge level variation in dungeons. For instance, in Blackburrow the people who get exp at the top get butchered at the bottom, and the bottom people walk through the top with impunity. Many of the Kunark dungeons are a lot better about this than the original ones.

The other problem, though, and in my opinion the biggest one, is the fact that the game, despite having a "preferred method" of doing the dungeon, is in fact designed for people to camp. Spawns and drops are static, so people looking for a particular item go to that room and set up shop. This means that even if you were to try to do the dungeon, you'd have vast areas of the "trash" to fight through, punctuated by rooms where the cool stuff spawns, but is now filled with some group.

This has led to something of a mini arms race where, because things were set up statically from the beginning, Verant had to come up with a way of making it a challenge for the people who might camp them. This process is where we got NPC's with infinite mana, who can run while casting, who seem to resist our spells with impunity, who have no damage caps, etc.

The side effect of this is that it is now mostly impossible to "do" a dungeon the way they intended because of static spawns and camping. In the perfect dungeon (e.g. Najena, which is do-able), basically your group would have to go from room-to-room breaking spawns all along the way, a process which we all know is the most dangerous step in any dungeon. Although this *is* fun, the simple fact is that you will probably die at one point doing this, simply because you are engaging in this high-risk behavior of perpetually fighting hordes of super-powerful monsters. What was fun a moment ago now becomes painful, because now you have to do corpse recovery in a dungeon in which, for balance purposes, many monsters can see through invis, invis drops with little warning, a mix of undead and regular and no stacking of appropriate spells makes it hard, doors are locked with keys on bodies, an unspaced spawn could refill the room within 30 seconds, making it a death trap while you loot your body, etc. Oh, and there are not many times that a high-level cleric or paladin is even in these lower zones to come exp rez you, mitigating that danger. Even in higher level zones they simply can't leave their group and come to you, nor can you safely drag to them.

The whole point is that you cannot do dungeons in all but a very few select ones (Dalnir, Kaesora, Najena, for instance). Even stating that it's fun but not as rewarding is ignoring the fact that it's simply not possible. I guess it technically is if you really don't mind dying and losing half a bubble of exp (i.e. 1-2 hours of time, not counting CR), but that doesn't cut it for me. A correction to make it "easier, safer, faster, or more profitable" might actually make this stuff possible. I also realize that it would make camping easier (cuz people would just "camp" the "doing" of a dungeon, sadly).

Now, I also know the counterargument to what I stated above, which Verant time and time again uses, and that is that people who are willing to take the higher associated risk and the penalties involved with that risk should, by virtue of their (bravery?) reap the benefits. This does make sense when seen from a certain angle, but let me propose a different possibility:

People bought this game to have fun. For some people, feeling like they've overcome a huge adversity is fun. For some people, finishing the game in one week or less is fun. For some people, simply the playing of it is fun. I count myself as one of the latter people, and obviously no one game will satisfy all three groups. However, I think there is a middle-ground to all of this. I, when I started this game, thought someday I'd fight a dragon or a god, especially if I put in enough energy and time. I personally feel the game would be just as much fun if, instead of taking 200 hours of play time (that's a time pulled out of a hat) to hit 50th, it instead took only 50-75 hours. This game, though, caters to the extreme one end of the spectrum where you must devote your life to hitting 50th in order to see some of the coolest parts of the world. I think everyone who buys the game should be able to do that, and the way it is now we can't, and Verant is okay with that, and that's what bugs me.

Muse:

Let me see if I can paraphrase your logic:

It's not possible to 'do' most dungeons in the game today for two reasons. First, because the range of levels from top to bottom is too high in most dungeons. Second, because most of the interesting encounters are camped, so you either have to pass up all the interesting stuff, or join the camp.

To answer the first point, I have to go back to my D&D days (which are longer ago than I care to admit). When we 'did' a dungeon in D&D, we would go in and fight our way down until we had more loot than we could carry, or (more likely) until we met something that kicked our butts. Then we'd limp back to town to sell stuff and rest. When we had recovered, we'd go back in, and hopefully make it farther the next time. When we were finally able to defeat the biggest nasties in the place, we'd usually leave and never look back.

I don't see why Everquest dungeons can't work the same way. There's no reason (except for the few one-way doors) that you have to be able to fight the whole dungeon in one sitting, or even one level.

To answer the second point, we have to go back to my original post. How do we stop people from camping? Either we nerf camping or we make not camping more rewarding.

The problem is, anything you do to make not-camping more rewarding will make camping more rewarding still. If the dungeon is set up as an increasingly difficult challenge, for each group there's always going to be a "sweet spot", where the level of challenge best matches their abilities. Unless you somehow limit the ability to profit from staying in that spot (read: nerf camping), it's always going to be advantageous to stay there.

Finally, I'm afraid I have to agree with you that Verant seems to have pretty much accepted that not everyone who plays will get to do everything in the game. That bugs me too, especially since I'm one of the people who will probably never get to do a lot of it. But, in Verant's defense (since I'm being their apologist), that's intentional too. Verant would prefer that nobody were able to do everything in the game. The only reason some people have is that their capacity for obsessive game playing exceeds Verant's ability to create new challenges. Is that the best way to design a game so that people have fun? I don't know, but it may be the only way to get the obsessive powergamers into the same world with the rest of us and still have a chance.
