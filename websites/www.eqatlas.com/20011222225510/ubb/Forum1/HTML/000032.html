This is the language event you’ve all been waiting for! Come share your knowledge with the people of Norrath!

WHERE: The Arena in Lake Rathetear (zone in from South Karana or Rathe Mountains TO Lake Rathe, then zone into The Arena)
WHEN: Saturday, September 2nd at 12:00n Eastern Standard Time

HOW TO TEACH A LANGUAGE

** 1** This is the way that I’ve found that works best for me. I would love to hear how other people teach languages as well. Note that you can only teach another person a language up to YOUR level of learning. If you are AVERAGE in a language, that is all the higher you can teach. Also, you will not see an increase in your skill until you zone out or log.

** 2 ** Make a hot-key called LANG or somesuch. On each of the lines, type the following:

/g type any text here to fill up complete line
/g I like to be silly and type dirty nursery rhymes
/g anything works, actually, it’s all your preference

The important part is to have the “/g” to that it all comes out in GROUP talk. Once you have your LANG hot-key set up, group with another person. I have found that if I group with multiple people and we all try to spam each other, I go LD. My personal choice would be to group with one other person and spam each other.

** 3 ** Change your language to the one you wish to teach. To do this, right click on the text area of your monitor (tan area where you see chats, auctions, shouts, etc.) and choose the proper language.

** 4 ** Turn OFF group chat so you don’t have to watch all the language spamming the screen. Press the LANG hot-key over and over. The person you are spamming should see messages that say something along the lines of “your language skill has increased.” Use the /tell feature to talk with the other person.

** 5 ** It would be very nice if people would learn how to set up their LANG hot-keys and how to teach a language BEFORE coming to this event. Ask your friends, read on the internet, post questions to this thread, etc. That way when we all arrive at The Arena we will all be set to get started.

Any further tips or advice is greatly appreciated!
