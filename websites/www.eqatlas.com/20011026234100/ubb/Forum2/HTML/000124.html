Lots of money is a very relative thing... at level 6, anytime I got more than copper off a mob, I thought it was nice. As a dwarf, you do have a few armor quests that are within, or at least close to, your ability. Talk to the npc merchant that you encounter just inside the warriors' guild in Kaladim (forget his name at the moment) and find out about the various scarab armor quests. You can easily do the scarab helm quest at your level, and with another level or two, will be able to complete the scarab boot and breastplate quests. This will give you three good pieces or armor for your level.

I would also work on the bone chip quest (speak to NPC's in the paladin guild hall)... for every 4 bone chips you turn in, you will receive pieces of patchwork armor, rusty weapons, and other miscellaneous items. The experience is very good for such a simple quest (brought a gnome warrior from 4 to 6 just by turning in about 36 bone chips, if I remember correctly), and the items can either be worn to round out your armor, or can be sold for money... ranging from a few copper for a torch, to several gold for armor pieces.

By the time you are finished with the scarab quests, you might be ready for the Ogre Heads quest; there are two ogres (Corflunk and Zarchoomi, by name) who wander out near the crossroads from time to time. If you take both of their heads and turn them in at the same time to the correct npc in the warriors guild, you will receive pieces of Bloodforge armor. Corflunk should be beatable solo by the time you are 8 or 9... Zarchoomi is a shaman, that you should be in the low teens before you try to tackle.

Hopefully this starts you on the way to the armor and wealth you want. If you want more specifics, take a look at the quests section on AllaKazaam's site (linked on the front page of Muse's site here).
