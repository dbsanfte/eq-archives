In response to Rimm:
Actually, the full post was regarding someone who was complaining about the lack of GM-run events for the lower levels, in the wake of the Halloween events which were (without exception) for higher level characters. My complaint was about how he referred to everything below 50th level as a "transitional" level, but I could also complain that they aren't making any new content for lower levels.

As an example, I'll review the changes that are significant that I can remember for the lower levels, since I've been playing this game since the second or third beta (I don't even remember). When the game started out, I remember Hook referring to the continent of Antonica as the "continent of random death" largely because of the hill giants and griffins around there (more on this later). The first big change was the addition of quests that actually did something in terms of experience, somewhere around August after the game came out. This was a big step, because now quests were actually useful to do. Sometime after that, the one lower-level "uber" quest was introduced for the Stein of Maggok. Very cool quest. Then, sometime this spring (after lots of testing) they introduced the quest armor quests for players in the high-20's to 30's in level. Twinking became more prevalent as people reached higher levels. Times were good. (Note that this is honestly about all I can remember as significant additions for lower levels, comment if I missed any).

But remember that all this time, things were also getting harder. Monsters were getting tweaked to overcome their inherently poor AI, and were given super powers. The ability to attack through walls, cast while running and never be interrupted or run out of mana. No damage caps on attacks. The list goes on and on. The "continent of random death" spread out, and I can't remember the last time a HG or griffin caught me flat-footed and got off a shot at me, because they are the least of my concerns now. Now I can't run through Kithicor at night, or Lesser Fay at any time. I can't hunt the mino caves in Steamfont without terror that the Hero will spawn. Splitpaw and Kerra Isle (Ridge, whatever you want to call it) are no longer playable below 20th or 30th. The newbie areas of Everfrost have 10th level skeletons roaming them now. Cazel roams Oasis for days at a time because nobody can kill him. The dragons have all been tweaked to be more "challenging" as well as the gods in the planes.

There are more but I'll stop. My point is that the plusses to the game for low levels have not been very many. Kunark was mostly a high-level addition, with a few exceptions (Lake of Ill Omen for people in their 20's, Kurn's Tower for teen-level necros, a couple of others for 30th level people). The minuses to the game for low-levels are many, and *mostly* because of this drive to improve the game for higher-level people. 50 levels and an ungodly number of played hours are not "transitional" when Verant themselves states that the majority of players will never get to kill a dragon or go to a plane, etc.

Like so many others, I registered just so I could toss in my two cents here, even if I am a week late. I found this whole discussion very refreshing and reassuring. It's good to know that other people are reacting the same way that I am.

1. I'm not so concerned about the amount of new content at the lower levels, but then I haven't run out of fresh content yet. What really troubles me is the way that the high level content is screwing up the lower level zones. For example, the addition of Cazel to Oasis is a disaster. Orc highway used to be a fun place to hunt, even though it was quite dangerous. SGs, specs, mummies, and "A" Orcs made the place treacherous. Now they toss in Cazel, who you can't even run away from. What was Verant thinking about? Why did they feel the need to drop Cazel into the middle of a well balanced, well designed zone? What will be the next zone to get some uber critter dropped into it just so the level 55 crowd can have some fun and show off their godlike powers in front of all of us peons?

2. I don't think that anyone really objects to some level of twinking. However, Verant chooses to ignore the impact that twinking has on the lower levels of the game, and the fact that a lot of higher level players are immature and irresponsible. It's bad enough that there are level 3 warriors with full cobalt armor and LoWs, but what really makes it bad is that so many of the uber twinks feel the need to *tell* everyone how twinked they are. Every twink Paladin in Unrest seems to feel the need to tell everyone "I've got Ghoulbane." It's bad enough to know that any newbie character that I create will not match up to the uber twinks. I don't need it rubbed in my face, and then hear Verant say "this is all okay."

3. All of this makes me wonder whether Verant is totally out of touch with its own game, or whether they are a lot smarter than it seems. No one is going to dispute that higher level characters deserve fresh content. But saying that the rest of us are just in a transitional phase? Are they serious? I agree with Muse 100% on this one.

4. Finally, I was pleased to see some of the comments about Lady Dae. Just about everything I have read by her has made me mad. I see two possibilities. First, she's off her rocker and really believes some of the incredibly condescending nonsense that she spouts. Second, she's toeing the corporate line and is doing the best job she can. For her sake, I hope it's # 2.
