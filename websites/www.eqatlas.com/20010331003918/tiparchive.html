Archive of Tips for Travelling and the Game

  1. A pet will always face the nearest monster when on Guard. Otherwise, it will face Northwest.
  2. Travelling on a canoe is easier than it looks. First off, when you jump into the boat, right-click on the bottom of the boat. You will get a message like "You have the helm captain." Then you control the boat. Right-click again to give up control of the boat and move freely. Beware that if you right click for any other reason you will likely free yourself from the boat and go running directly off the edge.
  3. Keep your ears peeled while meditating, you will be able to hear a giant or griffin approaching before they frenzy on you and, by then, it's too late...
  4. There are two ways to /consider monsters. The first is to target them and hit the 'c' button. The second is to right-click on them with the mouse. You can use the second method while fighting to keep your current target but see how something else /con's.
  5. When invisible, you are usually still visible to various monsters, like the undead, griffins, giants, etc. The way you can tell is to con them as you go. If they con anything other than indifferent, they can see you and might attack.
  6. Dropping items onto your picture in the inventory screen automatically distributes them into your inventory as if you had looted it.
  7. Holding the CTRL key while clicking on a stack of items selects only one item, essential for trade skills.
  8. Typing /who <name> (or just / <name>) will show you the info about a person, including race, class, name, and level. Use /who all <name> for people not in your zone. You can also substitute "friends" for <name> to see what your pals are up to, and you can use incomplete names, guild names, or class names if you're not picky.
  9. The clouds move from east to west, so if you watch the clouds movement, you know which way west is. Also, if you drop a sword on the ground, it's tip always points to true north.
