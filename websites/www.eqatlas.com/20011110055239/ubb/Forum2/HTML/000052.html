If you type /loc or /l , 3 sets of numbers are shown. The first number represents the North/South coordinate. The higher the number, the more North you are, the lower/negative, the more South you are. The second number is East/West UNLIKE the axis in math, the highet the number, the further WEST you are and the lower/negative the number, the more EAST you are. The third number is altitude. So if you were at 1500,-1500 and had to go to 1000,-1000 you'd have to walk South (you want to decrease from 1500 to 1000) and West (increase from -1500 to -1000). Hope this helps.
