Here's a repost from my main page about my initial assessment of Velious:

Also, I just wanted to comment now after having played in Velious for five days. I really think Verant has done a wonderful job with the new continent, this is what Everquest should have been when it came out. The whole continent is filled with quests, all the monsters drop cool stuff, the factions play a huge role, and when you're there you are really aware of a large purpose to it all.

There are two downsides to it, though. The first is that the continent is a high-level playground. It's a known fact that you will have to be in a large guild to do a lot of the quests here, much less even make it alive to many of the zones. I think this is bad, and I wish they had this much depth built in for lower level characters.

The other downside is that the high-level characters aren't moving along to their zones. Last night I figured I'd go check out Crystal Caverns or Tower of Frozen Shadow to see if I could get in a pickup group with my lvl 35 druid. I had similar experiences in both. I walk in, monsters are all blue to me, do a /who and there are about 30 people in each zone. Great! Wait a second... not one of these people is less than 45th level! The Tower did not have one living creature on the first level for me to even see, much less attack.

The point is, the game of EQ has gotten better, but it's still played by the same people. I applaud the higher-levels who have the balls to go fight and die in Velketor's or in Kael or in Cobalt Scar. That's where you're supposed to be. For the rest of you, realize that the cooler equipment that you get in the higher level areas will let you buy whatever you want from these dungeons with money to spare, and you might actually get some exp and others might actually enjoy those dungeons (the Tower, for instance, was built to be "done" by a mid-30's group). For you lower level people who are in Velious, start moving in and using these places, invoke the Fair Play rules and kick their asses out.

------------------
Muse

First, excuse my poor english as it's not my native language (i am from France).
I received SoV yesterday afternoon and didn't get any sleep yet (8am) as i spent the whole night on that new continent =). I play a level 55 mage, in a rather small guild (12 people 50+). I hope i will make you understand why you will find high levels in zones where most creatures are green..
Except for two people from quebec and two american people, i was the first in my guild to get SoV as it is distibuted a bit later in Europe and my guild is mainly european. After having installed the extension and run the patch, i logged on and found a GG to Ice Clad. I ventured in the outdoors, not knowing much of SoV as i tried to avoid all the spoiler sites so far. I found pretty fast the bridge to EW and then the frozen tower. All the mobs i met were green. I left the tower and was heading to EW when an old friend told me to come along with him to the frozen tower.. I went there, he had several keys and we made our way to the fifth level (wedding ceremony) and camped the mobs there. Exploring a new dungeon, even a green one, was really fun, but not very challenging indeed. After a few hours i got bored and when my friend logged i left and headed to EW, then to GD and Thurgadim. After some exploration i discovered the pit in the Icewell Keep. I made a pet, levitated down there and eventually found blue mobs =). I managed to gate after an avalanche golem double hit me for 186 (pet didn't follow me). I was really glad to have found a dungeon zone with some mos in the low 50s, so i started sending /oocs and tells to get a group or two down there. It was peak time, but in one hour i didn't find a single person willing to come.. There was noone in the pit at all either.. So i gave up, planning to come back some day with my guildmates when everyone has the expansion.
I left Thurgadim and a bit later i found the entrance to Velketor's Labyrinth. Again noone at all inside.. I cleared the first statues and some spiders, started climbing the slippery ramps, discovered that i had to cast see invis to see some of the spiders, eventually reached a place where two statues still green to me punched my pet in the low 100s, so i had to gate. I must say i loved that idea of a dangerous vertical dungeon where you can't cast levitate. But again, impossible to get a group up to hunt there (without my guildmates, at least). Impossible to find a single blue mob that i could take with just one or two people. Impossible to get any loot as the loot is quite probably much further inside.
So now i am going to head back to areas where i can 't get exp either but where i can at least explore and maybe find some items... (I ll try crystal caverns..).
The problem with the high level dungeons in SoV is that the risk factor, though very exciting, requires you to make rather long raids where people won't leave until the end of raid, and where you can't call replacements..
I guess you noticed that there are always much more people hunting in Karnor than in Howling stones, Chardok or Sebilis (though t he loot in these three other dungeons is much better, the mobs are not much harder, and for sebilis and howling stones the exp is mmuch better too). The reason for this is simple. Karnor is the kind of dungeon where you can zone in, find a group, stay one hour or two, and get a replacement when you have to leave. This could be possible in Sebilis too, but it's further from the druid and wiz ring, and also a key is required.. And this simply can't be done in Chardok unless zone hunting (not very interesting), and can't be done at all in Howling stones. In my opinion, hunting in Karnor is very boring, though. You get to know all the places up to warlord very fast. And it s static camping without much risk, very boring.. but many people prefer that because they can't stay logged for a long time and don't want to waste time organizing a raid, etc..
As far as i know (i haven't explored much maybe..) SoV seems to have no dungeon of that kind at all for the lvl 50+ (not even a sebilis like). So unless being in a very large guild, lvl 50s will very often log on to find themselves unable to make up a raid even with guildmates+extras, or they will log on to see that all their guildmates are already raiding some place and are far inside so you can't join them, or that the only available enchanter joined some other guild's raid hehe =). In such cases, some will log an alter, but that make you lose opportunities to get an invite to some raid; some will go to Karnor, but many people -that don't like karnor, will just go do some other stuff where they can hunt without a balanced group (that means greenie dungeons only..). And about the 'balls', i can tell you that soloing the church in kaesora when you're 51, for instance, requires some skill, involves much risk, and all in all is much more thrilling than camping the skel tower or the warlord with a C2 buffer-mezzer.
I'd appreciate to hear other people's opinion though, especially about the 50+ dungeons of SoV and what they think of them. So far i like them but i doubt i will be often hunting in them.

Ektarot, mage 55, Mithaniel Marr.

Well, I see it like this...

1) Everyone wants to see the new stuff... nobody wants to be left out, or to miss some really great loot off some relatively easy creature. (Iskar Berserker Club anyone?)

2) Most people are casual players during the week... signing on for only a few hours limits what areas you can hunt in.

3) If a high-level person goes to a high-level zone and sees nobody else there, then he's probably not going to hunt there himself... There are a scant few exceptions to this, but it's generally true. For example, if you went to the Plane of Fear and saw that nobody else was there, would you stay?

4) I don't know about others, but as a Cleric I spend about 1/4 of my time helping others at no benefit to myself. I'm reasonably sure that if you were to actually start wandering around the Tower while all those lvl 50+ people were there, you could easily find someone willing to help you out. In the end, what's the difference between getting help from a lvl 50+ person and forming a group in order to kill a monster? Oh yeah, lots more xp for you!

5) About 1/3 of the loot I've seen so far from Velious is barely farmable. The Velium weapons are barely better than Fine Steel and it isn't until you start fighting the lvl 45+ monsters (higher level orcs, giants in their city, named mobs) that you get the kind of stuff that could be farmed for real profit.

6) Some of those lvl 50+ people simply don't have lvl 30-40 characters. They have no other way to explore Velious. Others have their lvl 30-40 characters busy in other areas, trying to get the loot they know is there rather than risking getting "junk loot".

7) Remember, don't try forcing people out. Try asking for a shot, a turn or even the entire spawn area.

8) Understand that there will always be something that everyone wants. Some of that loot on the wedding party at the top of the Tower is pretty awesome... Just because you can get xp there doesn't mean that someone else wouldn't just prefer to get the item drops.
