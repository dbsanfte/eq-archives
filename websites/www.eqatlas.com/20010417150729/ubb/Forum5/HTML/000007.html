The latter way you mention is what I've seen. I've only been on two planes raids so far, but this is how they went for armor drops:
(Multiple guilds, btw)

Creature dies and drops an item usable only by class X.

If it completes a full set of planar armor for someone, they get it and are no longer in rotation for that type of armor. Usually they have to be wearing the full set to prove their need.

If it is a chest-piece, all roll who don't own it already.

If it is another piece, all who have not won any of that class type of armor rolls. When all have won a piece, all of class X are eligible again.

Anyone can pass on an item, if they don't want it. (To avoid taking drek pieces and then being ineligible later.) If everyone eligible for rolling passes, ALL of class X rolls and the winner gets the item, but it does not count as a loot, and does not affect their eligibility to roll on subsequent items.

Loot masters will call out the rolls. Most raids continue pulling even while determining loot winners. Sometimes a second piece drops before the first piece is rolled on. PAY ATTENTION to what roll is called. If a drek roll happens, and a good piece has also fallen, don't expect to get the good piece if you win the drek roll. You get the drek, and then must roll again for the good piece.

Here's the big rule for raids:
The raid is not for YOU, it is for everyone. When a piece of class armor drops, and you don't win the roll, be happy for the winner. Your turn will eventually come. There is no way you would get anything without the support of the full raid, so cheer all winners, and enjoy the raid. I've found some of the most intense and fun times in EQ on these raids.
