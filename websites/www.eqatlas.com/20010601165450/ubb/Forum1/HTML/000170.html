With my head hurting from reading the post in Muses thread I went to bed. I woke up with this idea.

Make the dungeons about the same lvl all the way through with interesting (read more powerful with better drop) stuff spawning randomly, not always in the same spot and then wandering the dungeon until killed. Also, make the high end items dropped random, not always the same item. They could be any one of a list of several items known to drop in that particular dungeon. People could not camp a spawn for an item because the mob may be encountered anywhere in the dungeon and it may not drop the exact item that they are looking for.

Items dropped randomly by the high end random spawns throughout the dungeon would be Lore but would NOT be No Drop. Make quest items (which would be NO Drop) found in the dungeon to be either laying in the back of a specific room or on a specific mob in a specific room, guarded by random monsters with random drops appropriate to the dungeon. Also, make the high end items unlootable by anyone who did not "GET EXPERIENCE" for the monster. Thus making the high levels go pick on something their own size. Then all the Farmers would be in the Karanas.

Use the Key idea similar to Najena so that to progress through the dungeon you or a member of your group, have to have gone to a certain area and acquired a key. The key to the next area would be dropped by a certain keyholder who would spawn randomly in one of the rooms in the area. (Keyholder to advance to area two spawns randomly in one of the rooms in area one, etc.) To get to the key you have to fight and kill whatever random monsters are in the room along with the keyholder. The key can only be looted by someone who "GOT EXPERIENCE" for killing the keyholder.

To make it more exciting and encouraging have a more powerful monster at the very end of the dungeon that protects a tresure hord of various items. These items CANNOT be looted as long as the guardian is alive and can only be looted by someone who "GOT EXPERIENCE" for killing the monster. These items would be high end No Drop items and each member of a group could only loot one of the items.

After the guardian dies, as each surviving member of the group loots an item, they are all ported to the entrance of the dungeon and their keys vanish. The keys BTW would be No Drop but not be No Rent, they would remain until the holder looted from the Guardian's hord at the bottom of the dungeon then the key would vanish.

The only way to play a dungeon of this design would be to "Do the Dungeon." What do you think? Not that Varient will ever creat such a dungeon.

This idea of unlootable unless you get experience may weed out some of the farmers, but it will also weed out any of the casual gamers who want the item, heard about it, then treked to get it. But now they are not allowed to have it simply because they are too high of a level?

I think the number of farmers is much smaller than the number of people who are just looking for one of the items, but to weed out the farmers we attack everyone.

Also, couldn't the farmers just get around this by finding the optimum level, and then just ensure that charcter doesn't advance by letting them get killed a couple of times,loosing EXP, etc. then going back to their farming?

However, I do like the idea of random wandering monsters with random drop loot. This is more traditional role playing.... you do not read very often of a group that sits wating for an enemy to continually reappear so they can battle.

However, how would you handle the trains caused by these wandering monsters cruising by and jumping you?
