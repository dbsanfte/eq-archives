Heilig
Adventurer 		posted 08-24-2000 01:37 AM               
The last bubble seems to go so slowly because the last two pixels stay white until you finally ding. It's part of the shading to make the bubbles look 3D. If you use a magnifying glass, you'll notice that the very top and bottom of the last two pixels do change color as you get closer, so they can be used to gauge your progress.

Hell levels are created because the level multiplier that's used to determine how much experience you need to get to the next level changes at level 30 and every 5 levels after that. At 30 it changes from 1.0 to 1.1, at 35 from 1.1 to 1.2, etc. That multiplier change means it takes a LOT more exp to get past those levels than it does the others, usually about 2.5 times as much. Not much you can do about it except just bash, slash, and cast your way through it. It does help if you hunt in an area that is well matched to your level (not hunting things 7 levels below you).

The experience required for each class / level / race combination is available at http://hackersquest.gomp.ch/html/xp/xpchart.html

It quite amply demonstrates the 'hell level' phenomena in real numbers. As for the 'hell+1' phenomena, where one loses twice as much exp in 31, 36, 41 or 46, this seems to be a result of how exp loss is tabulated, as a percentage of total exp. Since the previous levels required twice as much exp to progress through, the exp lost in those levels seems less, while the exp lost seems much larger in the levels immediately after. Basically, you have
more to lose.

My take on the Hell lvl issue...

Ok, there are (it seems) Micro hells up to 30.
These appear to be the levels prior to gaining new spells for casters. Non-casters..well..I am unsure of this.

30...the first Hell lvl. This took me all of 2 weeks to get thru. I did it in Warsliks by killing Brutes in the caves near Dalnirs.

Yes, we all know that Hell is 30, 35, 40, 45, 50-60.

The post-hells. The levels after you get thru a Hell level have one purpose.
Can you survive? You will lose far more EXP in the post-hell then in other lvls!
As one other poster said, between 3/4 and 1-1/4 bub per death. I found out the hard way in Mistmoor (man, you think i'd have learned to stay away from there by now...)

Why post-hells? Again, survival has to be the key. The logic seems to be 'Just because you made it to 31 (or whatever) doesn't mean you should rest on your laurels.'
The heavy penalty is a way of saying that you may not have leared survival skills enuff to go much further (just a thought).

Also, it could be a way of keeping players on their toes. After all, who WANTS to go back to the Hell lvl after they just left it?

Only good thing in Hell lvls (at least what I saw in 30) is that while it takes far more EXP to get thru them, you don't lose as much when you die. THAT happens post-hell.


So, if you JUST got out of a Hell (after 30 and before 50) make sure that you have at LEAST a 2 bubble buffer before you head out to more dangerous areas.

Just my take on the thread.

Alright folks, no more speculation and uneducated guesses on hells levels. Just go to the link provided at 2 different points in this thread and you'll know everything you want to know. The information was gathered using a utility called showeq. It is a packet sniffer that reads the data coming from the EQ servers and displays an easy to understand chart and maps or info such as exp a creature gives, how exp was split in a group, how much exp you have and how much you need to level (not a bar - an exact number) and what creatures exist in the zone as well as what they carrying. And just to head many of you off, it only runs on linux (no windows) and is absolutely NOT condoned by Verant. In fact, they will wipe your account if you are found to be using it. Now being that it is simply a sniffer which is completely non-invasive, and does not transmit any data, Verant can not find out you are using through any electronic snooping. However, if a warrior (or any other class with no form or means of tracking) is constantly running a bee line for the random rare spawn in a zone as soon as it pops, they could figure it out. So, everyone go read the page (I'll even put it here one more time) and let all your questions be answered.
