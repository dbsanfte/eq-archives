Ahem, actually I went in with my 29 Druid and had a whale of a time and picked up quite a fair bit of cash, but then a day or so later (when it had changed back) I went in with my 11 Warrior and equally had a whale of a time. The thing about the old Befallen is that as soon as you hit the second level it is essential to group, otherwise the trains will run you down. I was lucky enough to be in a pretty tight group and we managed to derail several nasty trains simply because we were well-balanced and worked together well. Our puller appeared to be a master of his art and never brought us more than 3 nasties at a time (a significant achievement in Befallen) and when we did see a train too big for us to deal with head-on we stood back, let it pass and chopped it up from behind. Now to my mind, the discipline instilled by Befallen once you get past the first locked door is invaluable to newer characters, it really is a school of hard knocks but it's the only school in town for characters of that level (characters who would find it a real challenge to reach dungeons like the Warrens or BlackBurrow). So much as I'd encourage Verant to keep adding newer and better areas for us to explore and die in, I'd counsel that they be careful not to disenfranchise the newer, lower level players just for the sake of pleasing the mid-level crowds.

Adventurer 		posted 07-12-2001 04:30 PM            
With regard to changes made to the EULA, they are as follows: (1.) A more emphatic statement regarding the prohibition against selling or auctioning EQ accounts, characters, coin, items, or copyrighted material has been included. (2.) A "Consent to Patching Process" section has been included which states that you (the user/account holder) agree to permit Verant/Sony to upload game file information from the EQ directory and to download game files to you.

With regard to Befallen, it has been reverted to its former status.

Additionally, regarding the EULA, Section 3 specifically states that Verant will notify account holders of amendments made to the EULA at the time you log in to EverQuest. They did not make any notification of the recent changes. I sent an email to Verant inquiring about the legality of a willful and knowing breach of contract on their part for NOT notifying users of amendments to the EULA, and whether or not their negligence voids the amended portions. (Legally, since they did not notify users of the changes to the EULA, the users are not required to be bound by them.) They have yet to respond.
