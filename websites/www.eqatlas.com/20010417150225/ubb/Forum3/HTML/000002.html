At level 34 stick to the Forest Gaint fortress in Frontier mountains, or group in The Overthere (there are usually groups already established in these 2 areas). There are always groups there, and the xp is good, not to mention the loot in this zone to be of the best. Droga and Nurga are great if you have a deep group, and they arent one-way, there are exits, you just cant exit where you came in from. Anywhere in kunark you will need a group ready with you to go there, for the most part. Droga's xp will start turning green at 36 or so and by 37 the endless day long pulls come to an end, so take advantage now. Other than that, SolB and Guk are great to mid-high 40's, but stick to the overthere and you can group or solo to near 50.

I'd get a group together and venture into Kaesora. That place is great...

Make sure you have a good 4 hours to explore this dungeon, and be sure to bring a cleric along.

From the entrance, there are two ways to go... a pit ahead, and a pit left. Head straight, and look down the pit. You'll see a hallway about 1/4 of the way down. Jump to that, Indiana Jones style, or levitate for those faint of heart

From there, head through the first room and drop into the pit in the second as you go straight. Basically, follow the straight path the entire way, and it will bring you back up to the top level again. You'll encounter prolly 6 - 9 mobs as you make your way back up, but make sure you know where it is before delving deeper, for CR is a pain there.

Good luck, and have fun! I love that place

For evil races, solo or partner in the North end of the Overthere at 22. I did - except Sarnaks, everything was non-aggro, non-grouping. It's a steady, if unexciting, diet of low-mid blues. Do be careful on Rhinos as they can hit pretty hard for the con (doouble 40s, ow!)

For larger groups, Warlsiks woods or Lake of Ill Omen are both good hunting (Sarnaks, Goblins, Forest Giants, Iksar bandits, undead, take your pick) Be aware that WW is a very dangerous zone - lots of mean, aggro, grouping wanderers & it's easy to get lost.
