Muse is right in issuing a call to arms.

*get's atop virtual soapbox*

First let me state, my spouse is a political lobbyist and in the course of being a lobbyist’s spouse I've had a lot of first hand experience with politicians and issues on the national, state and local level.

Gamers are sitting ducks for political grandstanding.

Until effective campaign finance reform is passed folks here's the way it really work. If an elected official or a political appointee is to stand up for your issue there had better be a record of a fat donation made to their campaign and political party. Otherwise it has to be a damned good photo op. for them to make a stand.

Correct me if I'm wrong but there's no political organization out there, that I'm aware of, representing Game players. No organization for a political candidate to solicit (read: shake down) for contributions means there's no representation on the issue.

I can assure you elected representatives will grandstand on any issue they feel has the most public exposure and the least amount of political risk. Game players, while a well organized group in their element, have no political voice or clout. Sure they could clog a politicians fax machine or an email server but I don't think I've ever seen it done, much less seen a game players lobbyist walk into a fundraiser with a fat check for a politician. Gamers are a no risk hit. Politicians can turn these types of media stories into a Mom against the Machines issue faster than you can frag a zombie.

Translation? An overly zealous, misinformed politician will use his Mom shaped red white and blue gun to frag you with apple pie ammunition for his own gain.

The games played today are a post Baby boomer phenomenon with the majority of electronic games being played by persons from age 34 on down. (Please note the use of the word majority. My father plays Everquest and he's over 60.) The laws of today are legislated by persons with a mean average age well beyond age 34. (Strom Thurmond skews that somewhat but not much). Do game players honestly expect these legislators to understand their culture?

The obvious solution is to organize an effective grassroots lobbying group to represent consumer issues from the gamers point of view. Does one exist? No. Should there be one? Yes. Otherwise we’ll have to wait till PhattLewter's, Blasterfragger's and KewlD00d's, have grown up and become legislators themselves before we see meaningful, thoughtful and informed debates on the issues. By that time though these issues will have been politicized and legislated into stone. It’s much harder to roll back laws once they are enacted. Someone needs to be providing policy makers with a clear and informed position from the players point of view.

In the meantime Game website owners and their users have a duty to issue a call to arms any time they see a threat to our Gaming culture and lifestyle. We live in the formative years of a new cultural entertainment era. Do we want Government pointing the direction that entertainment goes or do we want people doing it with the vote of a credit card?

*get's off his little soapboax*

Sincerely
