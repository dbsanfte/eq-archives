Tillene,

I have to disagree with you. A bard can solo with absolutely no down time, since bard dots are mana free and come in three flavors (fire, cold and magic based). Furthermore, with a sow potion, they can happily keep all three dots on the mob they are kiting.

For grouping, I break it up into four different aspects: partnering/broken trios, full groups outdoors, full groups indoors, and raids.

Partnering it is hard to beat a druid. The druid can root and snare and med, while the bard melts the mob away. This is brutaly efficient, pure non-stop pulling and killing.

For outdoor groups, pet classes rule, although I never object to a hard hitting rogue or monk. No one taunts, bard maintains aggro from a distance, kites while pets and hitters shred the mobs. Keep in mind, unless the monk scores a string of hits in a row, the mob will never even look at anyone but the bard.

Indoor groups can go many different ways, but I prefer melee heavy. A warrior, ranger (good damage dealer with nice utility spells), rogue, monk and shammy make a nice group for me to join. If the shammy is just slowing, that is all that it takes, there will not be enough damage taken to justify a full time healer.

Raids need clerics, warrior and rogues. Nothing else can really be substituted for them.

Keep in mind, everything listed above is from the perspective of a bard, what I find ideal for a group with a bard. This does not mean that there are not other combinations that are as effective or more effective, but just keep in mind, some of the wildest ideas work really well. Keep experimenting, because I doubt many would have thought that some of the combos listed above could even come close to being efficient.
