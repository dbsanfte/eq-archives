I was running around WC the other day when I fell into this tunnel entrance in the side of the cliff on the sandy side of WC. Not knowing what it was I ventured in and low and behold I had found Befallen. Now being a mighty dwarf Paladin of 9 seasons I figured this to be alittle over my head since was so far from a city. How wrong I was. The skeletons proved to be blues and whites with some decaying thrown in. This was the most fun I've had in many a week of adventuring. I was even able to defeat the Skeleton Llord with some help from a friendly cleric named, Care. Once I was able to defeat the lord I considred myself strong enough to defeat the Shadowknight who held the key to the lower reaches of the area. Got the key and just now am getting prepared to venture deeper. The undead fear spell the Paladins get at level 9 saved my butt many a times when I was starting to get over whelmed by the constant onslaught of Skeletons(not to mention the trains being brought up from the lower levels).

My point is, just because there is no city close by doesn't mean it is a HIGH level dungeon. Take a peek inside. You'll know after the first few mobs you encounter if it is to tough for your level.

Kitelar, Paldin on Povar.

I just went into Befallen with my 12 Necro. I've got lots of nice anti-undead spells, so I figured I could breeze the first floor and get into the second. Not so. First the named skelly on the first floor wastes my pet and brings me down to half a bubble of health before I killed him (he was a blue con). My second pet goes wandering off into oblivion thanks to the lousy pathing in Befallen. My third pet attacks and pursues a green con plague rat. I try to get it to back off, but no good. Then I try running, fall off a path and look behind me to see the rat, 3 skellies and a mummy. It was over fast. Don't think I'll be going back there any time soon. And I don't know I'll ever use my Pet in a dungeon again.
