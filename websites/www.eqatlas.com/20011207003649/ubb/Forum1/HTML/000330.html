I'm sure programs like this already exist, but if not and you are the type that likes to make your own maps (especially of new and interesting expansion zones) then feel free to use it.

Instead of reading this board at work during the 'not so fun' times, I decided to write a quick program with inspiration from EQAtlas. Its basically a map making program which I call EQMapMaker.

By typing some simple commands and recording /loc values you can turn your logfile into a map of your favorite zone.

here's an example of the start of solb:
--------------
http://www.revolverheld.com/vt/solb.gif

here's an example of the nexus in Luclin
--------------
http://www.revolverheld.com/vt/nexus.gif

Here's the program, distribute as you feel fit:
--------------
http://www.revolverheld.com/vt/eqmapmaker/EQMapMaker.zip


It makes maps based on /loc values in your logfile.

basically:

/say ::mapon
/loc
/loc
/loc
/loc
.
.
.
/say ::mapoff

feed your logfile to the program and it will spit out a map connecting those locs. A new line of points will be drawn everytime you do put /locs in between ::mapon and ::mapoff

(careful : is = /em so you need to do /say or /em ::mapon)

also you can do a /say ::mapnote sometext
and it will attach that text to the NEXT /loc you record in your logfile. So you can use this to log locations of mobs or other points of interest. This loc will not show up in your lines if its in between the ::mapon ::mapoff commands.

The actual program:

start it up and open up your logfile and it should draw the map. Afterwards you can use some hotkeys to manuver around the map such as: zooming in and out, scrolling around the map, turning on/off mapnotes, turning on/off the loc grid, reseting your positions, enabling depth representation, and changing your viewable depth. All these hotkeys are listed in Help->About

View->Options has some options that allow you to customize your mapviewing. Font stuff, rate of zoom / scroll / depth changing. # of guidelines showing, the range of depth you want to see. etc etc...

give it a try if you are bored at work like me, pop open the solb logfile it comes with. I'm not really a big mapmaker but some of you may be.
