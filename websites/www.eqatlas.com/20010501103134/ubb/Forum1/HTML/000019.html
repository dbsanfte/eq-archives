Just for reference: I publish a monthly journal on C++ programming. I print quantities of 1,600 or so. The journal is 16 pages. My cost at that quantity is about 90 cents per piece. This is two-color and 3 hole punched. Oh, and postage is about 26c US, 60c outside the US. And that's a Periodicals Postage rate (special permit).

Seems to me that the maps would have to be in color (not necessarily, though) That will make the costs prohibitive. I think the way to go, as you say, would be to get some advertisers to offset the costs.

Anyway, to answer your question, would I pay $2 for a "book" of maps? I dunno. Hard to say. Certainly it's cheap enough, but do the maps change that frequently? That would be one of factors, of course.
