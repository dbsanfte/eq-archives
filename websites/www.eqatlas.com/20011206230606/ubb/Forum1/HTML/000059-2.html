I think most classes can solo well at some point, and each has different strengths and weaknesses.

Best Group class?
hmm
Again it's hard to pin one down as being the best because with different situations different classes come out on top. In outside groups anything can work, and groups NEEDING any specific class except maybe a healer are probably not working together.

I'd say quite often the only class that is considered needed is a cleric for resurections. Once into the 40's undertakings that may result in multiple deaths will most likely not be undertaken without a cleric with a 49 rez.

Instead of a best class I'll make a list of which classes I think have primary group abilities and those with support abilities. Bear in mind that most classes have both primary and supporting abilities, though usually one is significantly better than the other. Primary abilities are things such as melee and nuking damage, hit points, armor class, and maybe healing.

Hmm. This is a nice topic. I do enjoy discussions like this. Anyway, off to the questions.

I think, with a poll, it would come out that druids and necromancers are the best soloing classes out there. Sure, every class can solo at some time or another, and there are many factors to contribute to it. But I think in the end, only necros truly pull the soloing aspect.

Look at it like this. Druids are the masters of mobility. They can SoW, and Port around. But the lonely Necromancer, a master of the evil arts, is what truly captures that image.

We have our pets, a very capable tank. (Not to mention there have been times that my pet is more capable than a hotshot fighter. Least for the most part, I have tight control on my pet. I can't /dumbplayer back off). We have a wide variety of Damage over Time spells, most of which stack upon each other with a variety of effects. We can Fear kite, which of course is how we will usually solo. Not to mention our DD, while not as damaging as a Magician's or Wizard's spells, has the benefit of healing us at the same while. Did I forgot to mention that we can also exchange our HP to Mana, making a cycle of getting more Mana while draining the HP back from the enemy? best of all, and this is the main best thing that I think pushes us over the druids for soloing.. we can summon our corpses. Yes, coffins are expensive, but it can make a dangerous solo CR nothing but a gesture of the hands.

For grouping, I can say Fighter and Cleric, because sooner or later every group -will- need one of those two. But, as in specifics, sure there are a lot of groups out there but I think I will once again stick to the Necromancer. I'll list why.

1) Extra Tank: Our pet can definitely change the tides of battle. Not only do they serve the purpose of being an extra hand in lashing at the enemy, but in dire circumstances, they can be left behind as a decoy to give the group time to zone or regroup or whatever. Remember, we can always bring back our pets.
2) Emergency Healing: We're not a Cleric, but if we must, we can try to aid. We can transfer our own life into a fellow group member. This is especially handy in battle. A cool and collective necromancer can have Allure of Death on themselves, transfering HP into Mana, then use a Shadow Compact to heal an ally, all the while Siphoning Life from an enemy to keep restoring it.
3) Manastones: While it's not a hefty amount, we can still sacrifice some of our mana and give it to other magic users in need. This is of course a quite handy ability. That fighter is dying. The cleric needs a bit more mana to get in that heal. Give him your own mana and see that fighter get a second chance of killing off that critter.
4) Crowd Control: We are in no way Enchanters. Ours is not the role to keep things in the pot. But if we have to, we can try. If you're in the Outdoors, and you pull something extra, why not try to Fear it? Or Screaming Terror it? Or if it's undead, even better. Numb it. Charm it.
5) Damage Dealers: In a group situation, some groups might not want us to use DoTs. At other times, they actually just might. With our stacked Damage over Time spells, we can and -will- do lots of damage to creatures. Needle away at their lives, and even every sliver of life can be important. And while our DDs aren't, as I have said before, as awe inspiring as a Wizard, when your group needs it, they are glad you just cast that Shock of Poison or the sort.

I'm sure that every class out there has benefits that they can bring to a group. If they didn't, then there wouldn't be so many varied groups out there. However, I will always stick to the idea that when it comes down to it, a Necro can be even better at a group than alone.

Well I decided to make a post on this topic even though what I'm about to say probably won't get through to most people.
First off I play a wizard and a paladin.
Wiz is 53, Pal is 26.
Ok who is the best solo'er, hard to say. Depends on the situation and the setting.
I'm going to make this about the high end game. Why? Simple at the low end game there are too many variances to make the difference and besides what good is it to solo young and not know how to group once you have to.
So this is all dependant on the 40+ game where post 50 things change drastically.
I'll get to groups in a bit. I'll tell you why the low end game is not worth talking about. It's simple, way to easy to twink and the difference between NPC's and PC's isn't much. At the high end game the NPC's have a lot of advantages like skills and hp/ac compared to PC's. Anybody can solo to 40 if they have the right gear. I know cause I have twinked my paladin in order to get him to raid level as fast as possible. I hunt with a monk and an chanter with my paladin. That's right I'm the healer, but guess what I don't ever have too. We are pulling constantly barely ever have to stop. Most the time we stop cause we can't find anything to kill. That's why it doesn't matter if you can solo at the low end. If you can't solo in the high end game you might as well group now so you know what to do in a group. If you don't know what you are doing you will get your group killed and that leaves a mark.
That mark is LFG.
Now back to soloing in the high end game. There are only a few classes that can do this. Tanks can't plain and simple, sure they can kill the light blues and greenies no problem. But the point of soloing is to get exp, or else you wouldn't bother. Sure there for loot as well but that's called farmig now soloing there's a difference. Classes that can solo: Wizards, Druid, Necros, Shaman, and Mages. Chanters and Clerics aren't very good at high lvl's for soloing, they just don't have the fire power. Out of the five that can solo I don't see many mages that do to any extent. Simple reason is they are completely dependant on their pet staying alive. Well just as tanks can't solo because the mobs hit way too hard the mage's pet can't. That means the pet dies fast and the mage is next. Don't get me wrong I've seen some mages soloing, but they were a couple levels higher than me and killing the same things I was. I'll get to Wizards in a bit.
So that's why mages aren't one of the best solo'ers. Next is Necro's, can't really say much about them I don't see too many solo at the high end either. I'm assuming for the most part the same reason mages don't. Once the pets gone they are looking for the door. On a side note though. These are outdoors zones only that I solo in. In dungeons, well for the most part the high end dungeons you can't solo to well in cause the adds are to easy to get and when that happens your dead, plain and simple. Unless you solo right at the zone line it's not wise to solo in a dungeon because if you die you then have to travel back down and get the body which puts right back in the same position you were in when you died. But if you are going to dungeon solo, I'd place my bets on either a mage or necro over myself.
Next is Shamans, this class is probably the most item dependant class when it comes to soloing. A shaman with his epic and Jbb bracer is with out a doubt the most efficient soloer. Reason being they are only useing mana for roots. They get mana free Dot and mana free nukes. Anybody that can root with these items can solo. But I still don't see too many shamans doing it, over grouping. They are a much need in groups for their support role. Also the pet is not that greatest. Not too many rely on it to save the day. If they dont' have their epic they still use it but after epic it's not worth it.
That leaves Druids and Wizards, well there is little difference in the classes at this point. Sure druids can dot things but we have more efficient nukes than them that cast faster. If you are going to solo one mob at a time I'll give it to the druid. I only solo one mob at a time if I can't find more. And that goes about the same with the druid. Post 50 not too many druids or wizards solo one mob at a time. Instead they start quad kiting. In a lot of the post that I read I didn't see anything about wizards. In some people said they didn't know enough about that class. One of the post it listed out every class as to whether it was a soloable class or group dependant. One thing though is he left out the wizard completely, I'm just wondering if he knows they even exist.
Those that don't know about the wizard class I really can't say why they don't. Might be because they never bother to see what they might be able to do in group so they are left to the side, or they think that wizards only have port spells, roots, and nukes. Other reason might be that wizards realize the benefit from quad kiting over grouping. Since level 38 when I started soloing HG's I only grouped for a few reasons. None being exp. They are: to group with friends, for raids, for loot, and for the fun dungeon crawl. I have gained exp doing this yes but it's not my most efficient way. When quadding I don't have to rely on any one and I get all the exp for killing 4 mobs at one time. Sure I have the downtime afterwards. But in that 10 min of medding I don't think a group could have brought in 24 mobs and killed them. That's how much they would have to bring in to equal the exp I got from one kite. It's the same way with druids. They would rather quad than sinle solo as well. So it depends on the situation as to who's the best solo class. But I would say it's between the wizard and the druid. As I said before I have seen mages soloing the same things I was, but remember I was pulling 4 at a time and killing them while the mage did one at a time. Well maybe the mage can solo 4 one a full bar of mana like I did with my quad kite. Well that's a time factor there. By the time the mage has 4 killed I probably already have half a bar mana medded back. After a couple hours that adds up. Now I know I'll get people telling me how we are completely dependant on our mana and that once it's gone we are done. That is true, but all pet classes have to rely on their pet like wizards and druids rely on their mana. No pet class can go toe to toe with a mob after their pet is dead. Just like we can't go toe to toe once our mana is gone. The only class that could go toe to toe with little mana is the shaman if he has this epic. But by the same token if I get a velks robe then I could do it without mana as well but it would suck. Way to time comsuming. The difference between druid and wiz in quad kiting is minimal. Sure their snares last a lot longer than ours(that's right for those of you that don't know we get a snare), but our snare is an AoE. Which means we round up the mobs first then snare them while the druid has to snare each one separate. It's a lot faster to group up full speed mobs than it is snared ones. Snared ones take to long. After getting them grouped wizards AE's are more efficient and higher damage. Which means the mobs die faster. And that means we are medding sooner than the druid.
Sure the druid gets sow and can heal himself if he's hit. But for one you should get hit, that's the point of kiting. To run away from the mob. 2, unless I get be down to a bloody pulp I can regen quite a bit of that health back. 3, we get a shield skin spell line. Simply put a damage absorber which will take the damage from being hit. So there you have it, Druids or Wizards are probably the best.

As far as groups are concerned, Bard or Chanter. Most will opt for the Chanter for haste and clarity, along with mez's. Yes clerics are needed but an enchanter makes a good killing group a fast killing group. And the faster your healing the less you have heal which means the less downtime.

Shut the hell up n00bies. Take it from a lvl 55 Mage WITH epic...

BEST SOLO - Necromancer

Plain and simple. There is no discussing this, the Necromancer is hands down the best soloer in EQ. Quad kiting?? PUH-LEASE!!! Let's see a Druid quad kite at later lvls. A Druid won't even be able to hit the mob later on. Also I can kite 6 or 7 blue mobs with my Necro. Ever hear of fear??? Necros can do ANYTHING a Druid does, but better. Better DoTs, Snare, FEAR and best of all, has a pet to kill mob while you sit and med. Can a Druid do that?? No. It uses all of his mana to kill mobs. Necros don't need to waste mana doing that. Snare mob, fear mob, sic pet, loot, rinse, repeat. A Necro can get jumped by many mobs, all he has to do is fear them and kill them one by one. Nuff said.

HOWEVER...

It can easily be discussed what soloing method is the best. I would personally say CHAIN CASTING PET. That's what puts Mages up there with the Necros. Chain casting pet is much much more mana efficient. Anything a Necro solos, a mage can solo with the chain casting pet techinique. However mages have lots of problem soloing 2+ mobs at once, that's their weakness. Get jumped by 2 mob and you're dead, unless you have Jboots/sow, then you can kite one while pet kills second.

Another really good soloing class is the Shaman. These guys can really cause some serious dmg. They have some of the best DoTs (if not the best) in the game, ok nukes, haste/slow spells AND pet. These guys are the masters of the Root/DoT/sit/med method. There is no arguing. Plus they have quite powerful pets which helps alot. Also their buffs are godly. Anything (or almost anything) a Necro/Mage can solo, a Shaman is not far behind. If the Necro/Mage can do it, they probably can too. And lets not forget they have debuffers (Malise series) which helps immensilly. A Shaman in my opinion is a Druid on steroids.

IMHO the best soloers are in this order...

1. Necro
2. Mage
3. Shaman
4. Shadow Knight
5. Druid
6. Enchanter
7. Bard

and so on...

To whoever said a Wizard can solo, well, all I have to say is seek mental help. There is NOTHING.. and let me say this again... there is NOTHING a Wizard can solo that all those classes can't. And they can't solo specially at higher lvls. They can't debuff and their spells are easily resisted.

BEST GROUP - Enchanter

Again, Enchanters are hands down THE best class to have in a group/raid. There will NEVER be a raid held if a few good Chanters aren't present. They're what will make the raid fail or be successfull. I donn't care how many tanks or clerics you have in a raid, but if a few chanters are present the raid WILL fail. Plain and simple. Nuff said.

Another very good group class is obviously the combo of Cleric/Warrior. If you have one but doesn't have the other, you'll never be at your full potential. A Warrior/Cleric combo is one to be feared. Believe me, you do not want to be up against this combo, SPECIALLY when there's 5-10 of these combos.

Yes, a Bard is a usefull class EARLY in the game (or up to lvl 45) in DL, but after that, when you start going to plane raids, there will be little use for a Bard. Need mana?? Chanter will do that. Need a heal?? Clerics will do that. Need haste/slow??? A Shaman will do that. Bards are jack of all trades. Ok at everything, good at nothing.

So the best group classes IMHO are...

1. Chanter
2. Clerics/Warriors
3. Shaman
4. Monk (FD pulling IS what your raid will be using. Learn to love it).
