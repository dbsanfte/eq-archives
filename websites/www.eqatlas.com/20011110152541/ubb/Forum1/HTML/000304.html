A big factor is what part of the world you start off playing in. My personal favorite is Butcherblock, as there are a few things in this region that help out in leveling fairly quickly.

Start off killing the usual critters that inhabit the newbie areas (in the case of BB, that's skellies, goblin whelps, rats, bats, spiders, scarabs and snakes)... the most important ones are the skeletons. First, they occasionally carry rusty weapons, which aren't much, but are an upgrade over your starting weapon. Second, and much more important for leveling, are the bone chips they drop. There is a quest in the paladin guild that is one of the best new character quests in the game... for every 4 bone chips you turn in, you get a good amount of experience, some coin, and either some light armor or a weapon. As for my definition of good amount of experience, I turned in about a stack and a half of bone chips, and went from level 4 to level 6.

From there, you have a few options... good grouping spots are the chessboard, the goblin camps, or the bandit camps. If you want to solo, there are several wandering monsters between the crossroads and the GFay zone (goblins of assorted types, giant scarabs, giant bats, skeletons) or you can head to the druid ring (4 single spawn skeletons that you can fight without anything else jumping into the fight). I personally loved using the druid ring skeletons from about 7 thru 9... they spawn fairly quickly, are out of each other's aggro range (meaning nice, safe 1 on 1 fights), and, due to the druid ring, there's quite often higher level characters nearby who will often help out with healing or buffs. And while the experience from it has slowed, the bone chip quest still will give you a little more experience to show for your work. The chessboard is very similiar, except that there's less chance of high level healing or buffing there, and two of the spawns are very close, making it impossible to fight just one without some spell assistance, such as a lull or mez spell. Also, if you're trying to make a little money along the way, the monsters at the chessboard are dead broke (unless they are carrying a rusty weapon).

At any rate, using these tips should get you out of your first 10 levels fairly quickly, while also getting you a bit of armor and money to boot.

Evry player out there prolly has a formula for getting through the levels, here's my two cp's;
Levels 1-5 BB hands down, the bone chip quest makes it easy, took me like two hours. GFay is also very newbie friendly, and it has the plus of being next to
Levels 5-10 The infamous orc lift and the surrounding area's, orc hill outside CB is nice for a grp 5-8, while thos a bit higher can venture into CB itself. The CB belt quest is pretty easy, great for faction with the dwarves, and gives you some nice exp and coin. CB itself is pretty nice, right through level 12, although some stay till 15.
Levels 12-20 Oasis. Great for groups, good exp, quick levels, and alot of people with buffs and such.
Level 20-30 South karana. At SplitPaw Spires. Always camped, always groups, although enchanters and clerics never have a problem getting an invite and it's open enough to satisfy any druid's or necro's want for kiting MOB's. Excellent loot, the aviaks carry gems, weapons and the occassional plat. Gnolls usually drop pp's and carry FS, as well as SplitPaw armor.
30 and beyond? Let you know when I figure it out. At 30, you should have enough money to buy spells, armor, etc.....alot of folks are simply giving stuff away ( esp if the same class as you) best advice? help each other, be selfless, accept dying and lost exp as part of the game. There's nothing quite as satisfying as twinking out a new woodelf ranger with SplitPaw armor and FS only to find out that person's main is a 50 warrior or wizzy, and that they are in the same dungeon as you are when you are being hammered by mobs and all seems hopeless.....
