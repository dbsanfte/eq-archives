Muse
Cartographer 		posted 08-29-2000 07:48 AM               
The stay left rule has quite a few problems. Since most people drop down to the left when they come in, I'll describe it briefly (since I hope to have the complete map up in a few days).

Dropping left puts you at the bottom of a pit. From there keep right through one big room and halfway down a hallway til you hit a 4-way intersection (not far at all). To the right is another pit, don't go down that. Across from that pit (to your left) is a short passage to an open room, and through that down a slight incline you'll reach a room with the floor all broken up. If you can make it to the broken floor room, you're halfway out.

Fall in the pit. There are two ways out, you want to keep going the way you came in (south). Passing through that tunnel will bring you to a room with a spiral stairs, go up. You then just follow that path all the way to the last room and drop off the small ledge there to be placed in the entry room again (this time in the "right" hall).

Once you hit that broken floor room, it's pretty easy. If you happened to fall "straight" from the entry room all the way to the bottom of that huge pit (I'm sorry!), then if you live, leaving this area involves staying to the left the whole way. You will eventually come out in the pit room, keep going and you'll be going out they way everyone else does.

And, of course, you could always gate.
