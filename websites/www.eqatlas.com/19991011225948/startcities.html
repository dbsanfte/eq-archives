Starting Cities in Norrath

The following guide was put together to hopefully give you an idea of which cities are good to start in, regardless of the consideration of race and class and religion. I will admit that I haven't started in all of these cities yet, and the ones I've never been in I will not even comment on. It's also important to notice that a great deal of the negatives were based on the beta and the development at that point. I would assume that these will be corrected by release <ahem>.

Ak'Anon

I haven't played here a great deal, but my accidental omission of this page the first day allowed me to gather quite a bit of information from emails about it... Thanks all!

Advantages

It is possible (in fact suggested) that you reach 4th or 5th level within the city itself. This also has one of the handiest tough critter zones on all of Faydwer, roughly 8th-10th level critters walking all over the place. Plus, minotaur caves and axes... Need I say more?

Disadvantages

City is somewhat confusing and slow to get around. Newbie mines are a maze and it isn't easy to run for help if caught and in trouble. I lost my first character by swimming up between a walkway and a building and getting wedged there, and being unable to escape. Don't do that. Have to leave your zone entirely to fight higher than 5th level critters.

Rating: 7

Erudin

My very first beta character was started in Erudin, and was quite successful, going on to become an 8th level magician named Kithuriel before I moved on to greener pastures...

Advantages

The newbie fighting area in front of Erudin is well-protected from the tougher zones by a river and a bridge, so you can't get lost. There are also people selling goods outside of the city who sell food and other items (although they may now be converted to artisan goods). This keeps you from having to zone to buy necessary items.

Disadvantages

If you want to travel outside the zone it is a 10 minute boat ride to Qeynos, plus all the zoning in between. Get used to begging to be bound. There is a lag in creatures to fight from 3rd to 4th level. The newbie area critters aren't abundant enough to make it worthwhile, and across the bridge there are just too many killer skeletons roaming around to make it worth all the times you'll die. Not many people play here, it can get lonely...

Rating: 7

Felwithe

The city of the High Elves...

Advantages

Any city on the island of Faydwer has access to the other cities and their zones, which means if it's too crowded here, go elsewhere. It also isn't far to recover your body in any of the other zones. By roaming to the other zones, you can easily find creatures your level to battle. There are also plenty of other adventurers to join with. And it's possible to find the pelts needed to make patchwork for most of the island.

Disadvantages

The newbie area is extremely crowded, due to Kelethin being nearby and these two races being very popular. During the beginning of Phase Four, it was unplayable here due to this. Greater Faydark itself is very confusing, especially after you wander off the paths. Getting lost in orc town is a problem. Access to Antonica is limited by the eternal boat ride from Kaladim to Freeport.

Rating: 6

Freeport

I haven't played much in this city. I disliked it, but I've received some feedback from some people who have played there a great deal more than I, and I add it here. Thanks Kevin and Ron!!

Advantages

Good newbie areas. Access to boat to Faydwer. Heading south to the Desert of Ro leads you to an area well-populated with 1st-5th level creatures. Easy access to food and drink, as well as the Commonlands nearby. Lots of good creatures to group against. Many quests and merchants in the area to keep it interesting. Also there are a good number of nearby zones for higher level characters to adventure in, including the croc killing grounds in the Oasis of Marr and Befallen, a tough dungeon for levels 7-27.

Disadvantages

Occasional wandering madmen in Northern Ro are aggressive and very tough, and guards won't save you from them.

Rating: 7

Grobb

The troll city attached to the swamps...

Advantages

There is a great deal of newbie material in Innothule Swamp and nearby Feerott. Of note, the frogloks are rich and often swathed in magical cloth armor. I've never had richer characters than my troll.

Disadvantages

You only have two zones to travel around in when starting out. You can really get stir crazy after a while and want to move on. Although there are zones nearby, the next 5th to 6th level zone is on the other side of the Feerott, which is a very long walk if you get killed. For that reason, many stay and kill frogloks for weeks, but that can get tiresome. No bear pelts for patchwork.

Rating: 7

Halas

My most fun beta character was a Shaman from Halas, who I acquired after some bug turned my 1st level Warrior into a 4th level Shaman. I didn't complain.

Advantages

This has one of the two best newbie areas for sheer number of critters and wealth attainable. Just kill everything in sight. Also has pelts available to make patchwork for yourself and your smaller neighbors to the south. Once you map out the zone, it's pretty easy to find your way around in and small enough to manage.

Disadvantages

You are *way* in the boondocks. If you don't like your barbarian friends, you had better get used to running through Blackburrow in the pitch black to retrieve your body. Doesn't sound like fun? Also, some of the "low level" quests involve some really dangerous areas.

Rating: 8

Kaladim

The dwarven mine turned city...

Advantages

Butcherblock Mountains have to be the best hunting grounds in terms of having creatures available for every level imaginable. I didn't leave there until 6th level, and then not because I had to. Easiest access to the boat to Freeport.

Disadvantages

City itself gets some taking used to walking around. Lots of instances of some random huge monster walking by and swatting everyone ("where did that undead pawn come from?"). Need to go elsewhere to get pelts.

Rating: 7

Kelethin

Tree city (makes me more scared of heights).

Advantages

Very easy access to the city (no zoning needed). Once you know your way around, easy to get to anywhere. Many small creatures to kill, with the orcs providing the occasional exp boost. Provides a great deal of goods if you know where to look.

Disadvantages

Tends to be a very busy area, along with Felwithe. Also, orcs tend to be overwhelming and group so much that even my high-level characters would occasionally die from their swarms. Makes my palms sweat cuz I'm scared of heights.

Rating: 6

Neriak

The dark elven city below...

Advantages

A world unto itself, you could adventure down here for weeks on end. Creatures are tough, but you advance quickly because of it.

Disadvantages

City itself is very large and confusing. There is the highest dweeb:character ratio of anyplace here playing, and the /shout'ing and /ooc'ing could make a ten year old weep in pain. Difficult to leave and find safe hunting grounds, because ogre and troll lands so far away. I found a lot of kill-stealers when I played.

Rating: 4

Oggok

The ogres smelly city...

Advantages

Very small city that is easy to get around in. Lots of good regions between here and by Grobb for newbies on up, and you can smash all day long. Not too many random wandering evil monsters.

Disadvantages

No bear pelts for patchwork. The best mid-level hunting around is probably frogloks, and they are almost two long zones away. Easy to get lost in this large zone. Most creatures are aggressive around here.

Rating: 6

Qeynos

The other human port city... In the beta, this was probably the best fleshed out city. It's also Muse's hometown.

Advantages

Very well fleshed out city. Really gives you the feel of being in a port town. Readily accessible to many different zones, including the starting zone for rangers and druids and Blackburrow. You could adventure for weeks and never leave the area. Easy access to a lot of other zones, really feels like a melting pot with barbarians, Erudites, humans and half-elves all around.

Disadvantages

Newbie area can sometimes get very crowded, and the only 2nd level critters (fire beetles and Klikniks) are groupers.

Rating: 9

Rivervale

This is the only city I have never once set foot in. Peter e-mailed me to give me his take on it, thank you Peter!!

Advantages

Great newbie ground with lots of fire-beatles and spiderlings everywhere. After that is easy cross a big wall to the harder creatures. Lots of pelts from wolves here. Also good fishing with a pond inside the town.

Disadvantages

Isolated except from Freeport. Confusing city with a lot of things looking the same.

Rating: 8
