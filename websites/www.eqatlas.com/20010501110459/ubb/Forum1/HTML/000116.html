Hear my story, and tale of horror, dear friends!

The second time that I had ever cruised on the ship from Faydwer to Freeport, this is what happened...

Ship makes brief stopover at an Island (Sisters Island?). A fellow passenger, an obviously stupid warrior-type with very low intelligence, jumps off ship at the Island, he then finds a RED very deadly NPC (near the docks) to pick a fight with and then trains that deadly NPC back onto the ship.

The rampaging RED NPC jumps into the ship. Ship starts moving. No player can ever cast spells on board a ship. Deadly RED NPC kills 2 low-level passengers on board the moving ship. He also kills the apparently stupid warrior passenger that trained him in the first place. Deadly NPC nearly kills my Paladin... my life was spared because the ship zoned from Ocean of Tears into Freeport which then immediately removes the NPC.

Is this a glitch in the game?

Why is this even allowed to happen????

One stupid action by a player ends up nearly wiping out the entire passenger crew of a moving ship. It is a known thing that ships are not meant for fighting. You cannot cast spells inside ship, so you can barely defend yourself inside ship.

I reported him to the GM on duty.

I've had something similar happen with a character of mine on the Sister's Island. I jumped ship to grab Kiola nuts and then ran back to the ship to catch it before it left. When he finally got low on hp's, I chased him all over the place, including below decks, all the while getting messages that he's too far away to hit, and I'm right next to him! Then, go cap it off, the boat zones to BB, and the game boots me. Back to Sister's Isle. I didn't know NPC's would follow you onto the ship, or I never would have dragged it there. Hope this helps everyone to avoid similar mistakes.
