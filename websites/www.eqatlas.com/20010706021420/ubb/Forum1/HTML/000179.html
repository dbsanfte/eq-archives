Plessy Tapenslap
Adventurer 		posted 06-29-2001 07:51 AM            
The server is in testing, it's team diety PvP, and I intend to have a character there when it goes live; however... Calling people to the mat for being rude, self-absorbed, or just plain mean is not "immature". I think the existence of servers where there are rules (just like laws in real life) that protect people from being abused and abased by those who chose to behave as if they are more important than anyone else is a good thing. It's not immature, it's called civilized society.

When a group of people gang rapes and then kills my friends wife, I'm not going to call him an "immature whiner" for wanting the state to take action on his and the rest of societies behalf by removing the creeps from the rest of the population. However, in a gaming arena such as EQ, where different servers can employ different rules, I like the idea of a server that allows the "law of the jungle" to prevail. It gives me a new experience for my money, and I hope it will remove some of the jack-asses I see scamming newbies in front of Freeport from the servers that are meant to have a less harsh experience (player interaction wise); and move them to a server where when I see something like this, I not only have the option of rigging out the newbie in new gear to replace the shield he just lost, but to kill the scammer, and then call in twenty or so friends (in anticipation of the coming posse following his CR) to make sure the guy isn't going to be collecting his body in the near future. The end of play nice rules on one server is a great idea, one that when coupled with one character per account, creates some interesting options.

For as many Ayn Rand wanna bes who cause grief and think that their narcissistic personality type betters the world; there are just as many that know how to play as well and level as fast, and who see the above player-types as social leaches rather than misunderstood adults who don't need the "Disneyland rules" of The Man hampering them.

If this server is a success, it will be because it draws in both of these types and allows them to settle disputes Wild West style rather than by /report. I don't see this as an "adult server" at all, but a fun one where the gloves can come off in a virtual world where no one really gets hurt.

As for your the first posters views on twinking or what-not, why do complaints of people about you manipulating the game bother you so much? Some people twink and power level (myself included) and some people don't (I'm also in this group). The character I've played over the last couple of months has fought hard for every small victory and every item he has. It's made the game interesting again for me, and given me some perspective on twinks I didn't have before. Manipulating the game does what exactly? It doesn't make me very connected or invested in a character or the story line. The twinked character just becomes another in a list of vanilla signatures we see at the end of messages on boards like this: e.g. 56 Pally, 47 Druid, 51 SK, etc... It is just creating "wealth" in a place where it means nothing in the real world (ebay aside).

Verant from day one has made an effort (many times misplaced or ignorantly) to put the story and the primary character above the desires of the power gamer. We all knew this when we signed up. Why now is there whining about Verant trying to follow through with what they told you they were shooting for in the first place? Why are there complaints when those who signed up thinking the was a good idea decide your SK is violating the spirit of the game by carrying a weapon with a DD proc at level 1?

The whiner is you. The game rules may be changed to disallow someone to level without suffering. So what? I may not be able to give a level 1 character uber gear and then have a friend come over and sit on my network and have him power level the guy with a level 53 druid to 25 in a day. Big deal. If I wanted a company that didn't care about story lines, twinking, and manipulating the game, I would just go buy anything made by Blizzard. I don't think that is a childish attitude, I think that paying money to someone for a game and then complaining endlessly that they won't let me do anything I want, whenever I want to do it regardless of how it affects others is.

Plessy Untwinked 30 SK
Vanilla everything else
