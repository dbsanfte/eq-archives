Here's a repost from the main page, feel free to comment:

Also, when I was home over the holidays, I played an interesting game called Pass the Nuts (get the laughs out of the way before going on). It's a game I played when I was about 8 years old, and it is kind of a mini-pinball game, where you have 4 animals and the objective is to have them kick this walnut up from one animal to the next, eventually to get it through a hole at the top, where it falls back to the bottom again. Hit the nut too hard and you hit the ledge above you, too soft and it won't make it to where it has to go. I spent days playing this when I was a kid, and I must have played it for 20 minutes after my little cousins got done with it until the bell sound every time you kicked it started to drive everyone around me crazy. Highly addictive.

Then it struck me how similar Pass the Nuts was to EQ. Highly addictive and yet highly frustrating at times. I used to try and get the nut all the way up without screwing up once when I was younger, and can remember crying when I failed (much like I cried at the end of Pitfall 2 when that damn poison frog whacked me on the final stairs, you know which one I'm talking about). I don't cry during EQ (not eight years old anymore), but my monitor has a little sign on it that says "don't punch me, I'm innocent" and that says it all. In Pass the Nuts, you continually strive to get higher up, and frequently fall down at least one level, sometimes all the way to the bottom. You can spend hours doing it, and even if you win, it just means you start over.

Anyway, there's more but if you don't get the analogy, more will just make you crazy as well. Maybe I had just eaten too much turkey...
