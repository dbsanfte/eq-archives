The server is in testing, it's team diety PvP, and I intend to have a character there when it goes live; however... Calling people to the mat for being rude, self-absorbed, or just plain mean is not "immature". I think the existence of servers where there are rules (just like laws in real life) that protect people from being abused and abased by those who chose to behave as if they are more important than anyone else is a good thing. It's not immature, it's called civilized society.

When a group of people gang rapes and then kills my friends wife, I'm not going to call him an "immature whiner" for wanting the state to take action on his and the rest of societies behalf by removing the creeps from the rest of the population. However, in a gaming arena such as EQ, where different servers can employ different rules, I like the idea of a server that allows the "law of the jungle" to prevail. It gives me a new experience for my money, and I hope it will remove some of the jack-asses I see scamming newbies in front of Freeport from the servers that are meant to have a less harsh experience (player interaction wise); and move them to a server where when I see something like this, I not only have the option of rigging out the newbie in new gear to replace the shield he just lost, but to kill the scammer, and then call in twenty or so friends (in anticipation of the coming posse following his CR) to make sure the guy isn't going to be collecting his body in the near future. The end of play nice rules on one server is a great idea, one that when coupled with one character per account, creates some interesting options.

For as many Ayn Rand wanna bes who cause grief and think that their narcissistic personality type betters the world; there are just as many that know how to play as well and level as fast, and who see the above player-types as social leaches rather than misunderstood adults who don't need the "Disneyland rules" of The Man hampering them.

If this server is a success, it will be because it draws in both of these types and allows them to settle disputes Wild West style rather than by /report. I don't see this as an "adult server" at all, but a fun one where the gloves can come off in a virtual world where no one really gets hurt.

As for your the first posters views on twinking or what-not, why do complaints of people about you manipulating the game bother you so much? Some people twink and power level (myself included) and some people don't (I'm also in this group). The character I've played over the last couple of months has fought hard for every small victory and every item he has. It's made the game interesting again for me, and given me some perspective on twinks I didn't have before. Manipulating the game does what exactly? It doesn't make me very connected or invested in a character or the story line. The twinked character just becomes another in a list of vanilla signatures we see at the end of messages on boards like this: e.g. 56 Pally, 47 Druid, 51 SK, etc... It is just creating "wealth" in a place where it means nothing in the real world (ebay aside).

Verant from day one has made an effort (many times misplaced or ignorantly) to put the story and the primary character above the desires of the power gamer. We all knew this when we signed up. Why now is there whining about Verant trying to follow through with what they told you they were shooting for in the first place? Why are there complaints when those who signed up thinking the was a good idea decide your SK is violating the spirit of the game by carrying a weapon with a DD proc at level 1?

The whiner is you. The game rules may be changed to disallow someone to level without suffering. So what? I may not be able to give a level 1 character uber gear and then have a friend come over and sit on my network and have him power level the guy with a level 53 druid to 25 in a day. Big deal. If I wanted a company that didn't care about story lines, twinking, and manipulating the game, I would just go buy anything made by Blizzard. I don't think that is a childish attitude, I think that paying money to someone for a game and then complaining endlessly that they won't let me do anything I want, whenever I want to do it regardless of how it affects others is.

Plessy Tapenslap

After reading my post, I believe that I too needed sleep before writing it. It was possibly a bit harsh, and I apologize. When I saw the adult server topic, my first thought wasn't sex, but some sort of age verifying system that is probably unfeasible, and as was pointed out, wouldn't exclude adults who behave like children anyway. What I saw of your idea in fact, was the fourteen year old power gamers dream of God-like status within a virtual world where he could behave like a tyrant, with little to no consequences. I still think this may end up being the case, but love playing on the server nonetheless. The one character per account rule means that people will live and die by their reputations, and if someone pushes the bounds too far, it will give being kos a very harsh new reality that will be harder to duck then the other pvp servers due to the 1 character restriction.

On to the twinking and what not. I really don't have a big problem with twinking to be honest. I just twinked a friend who bought the game yesterday. While I did twink him, I did not give him items for which there are no upgrades. Full plate, Bark Shield, Sparoli Defender/Messenger of the Queen etc. People twink for different reasons, and in this case, since the guy isn't into MMORPGs, killing rats with a rusty long sword just isn't going to be his idea of fun. I gave him gear that will allow him to not have to think about money or upgrading until he is level 30 or better, so he can just focus on the game. He bought the game to have another way to spend time with me, and I hope that over time he grows to enjoy the Everquest in it's own right.

I will not power level him however. My reasons are simple. First off, it would do my friend a disservice. He would not learn about what his character can do in different situations, and he would be ill prepared to group after I power leveled him to 30. I have run into enough bards that don't twist, and Wizards who don't assist, to unleash another one on my server. Twinking alone does not make a character a minor deity due to the skill system and damage caps, it only helps a bit. Power leveling however is a different deal. When someone who is twinked joins an equal level group, they pass on the benefit of the high level equipment to other people in the zone. Since twinks are less likely to need loot, and often bow out of the loot order, they help the group even more, and are the least likely to ninja loot.

When they walk into the zone with a high level healer, they take potential kills away from that same group. While doing this, they also "rub it in the face" of the people standing next to them at a camp (the Unrest wall comes to mind) who either are choosing to play legit, or do not have a level 58 cleric to level them. Watching someone who cons blue to you take out five yellows (one or two of which you would have liked to pull to your group) at the same time is a slap in the face to say the least. It is not fair to those sharing spawns near by or passing though wanting to camp the spot as it was designed to be fought in. It just takes advantage of a game design flaw to benefit one person who is exploiting it, at the expense many times of those who are not.

One other thing I want to bring up about twinking, is that I never twink a character with quest armor. I do this out of respect for those who have quested for every piece they have gotten. I know how much it must suck for a Bard who has just gotten his fourth piece of Lambent to walk proudly into his guild after many corpse runs, risks taken, and an empty bank account to get it, just to find someone on the other side of the door who cons green and has it all on. It's disrespectful, and undermines the achievement of the Bard who quested for his/her armor. Besides the armor being so noticeable, I choose Bards because I find that to be the class that twinks do the worst. Nothing amazes me more than a level 25 Bard in full Lambent who doesn't know how to assist or twist; and don't even get me started on the lame ass song selection.

The point of this whole rant is that on the twink and power leveling issue, please look at it from the perspective of someone who's primary character is the level of your twink. It's the equivalent of being in the working world for ten years, and finally getting to buy the new mid price car you want, and then being cut off on the way home from the car dealer by a teenager in a Lexis. The natural reaction is a bit of resentment, especially when the ideal our society (and this is true in Everquest as well) is that you work for what you get in life, and have an appreciation for what you have managed to achieve through that hard work. I bone chip leveled my friend to 6 so he wouldn't lose several thousand pp worth of gear, and let him set out to explore yesterday. Within minutes, I got a phone call from him asking how he should react to people calling him names who were high level and saw him in the gear. I explained to him that they had good reasons to dislike what I had done for him, as well as I had good reasons to do it. My advice to him was to ignore it, or if they engaged him in conversation, to respectfully tell them that he was new to the land of Norrath, but was lucky enough to have a patron who was willing to help ease his way into the world, but had now left him with this nice gear to explore for himself. He used this tact, and actually made a friend this way who showed him how to set up a couple of hot keys that I had not gotten to yet, and helped him find his first group. I will be playing a level 12 Shaman (who has long been on the shelf) with him soon who is using a kite shield and a FS spear with leather armor. We will make an odd couple.

That's my expanded, nicer version about how I feel in regards to power leveling and twinks. I think it's what people would tell you in a broader forum like message boards if they thought it through. Most people don't type very fast, and don't like to write, so it's easier to say "you suck", then "I disagree with you, and here's why". I think that having level restrictions on procs and damage caps takes care of the twinking issue aside from my observations on quest armor, although I would be ok with level restrictions on some of the higher end armor with high stat bonuses. I do think the various methods of power leveling are a huge exploit that I would like to see disappear. While I don't necessarily want people to suffer, I don't think I'm the only person who wants people to earn a last name and the ability to disarm an opponent. Having a high level character may enable one to share the wealth, and I think many people are ok with that, but it shouldn't take all of the effort out of having another. The "whining" that bugs me in the gaem is not the "you trained me/ks me/you are a twink a-hole" kind, because I don't see that as being a baby. Who I see as whiners are people following me around seeing that I have a Wurmslayer and an MM shield, and therefore, they are entitled to 10pp from me. EQ is not like real life where the guy begging you for a quarter may be bi-polar, and was sexually abused all through his childhood, never having a shot at anything more than a subsidized apartment and cheap booze; if you could manage to log on to the game, you can manage to make your own damn 10pp, but that's just my 2cp.

Plessy
Soon to be lvl 12 Shaman
