I've heard a few definitions for 'proc'. The most sensible one I've heard is that it is a shortening of 'process', as in the game will process the special effect of the weapon. As to what it means in game terms, some weapons have a special spell-like ability. These abilities do not function every time you swing the weapon; their function is related to your dexterity score. Most also have a minimum level requirement. When folks are asking when a weapon procs, they are asking what level do you need to be before the special ability begins to have a chance of working.

An example: the screaming mace is a relatively low level quest item weapon. It has a special ability of casting a spell similiar to the clerical spell 'yaulp', which gives a temporary strength buff. This special effect procs at level 10; a character of level 9 or less can use the weapon, but the special ability will not function. You will occasionally see a message 'Your will is insufficient to command this weapon' or something to that effect. Once you reach level 10, you will occasionally trigger the special effect strength buff.

As far as the effect of the velium shards proc, I am unsure. Velium weapons begin to proc at level 25, if I am not mistaken.

EDIT: velium shards effect is a small DD (something in the neighborhood of 15-25 points), provided the site I looked at was accurate.

What does this mean for you? It means you have a usable magic weapon (thereby you are capable of hitting monsters like willowisps that you need a magic weapon to hit) that will not proc for you for quite a few levels.
