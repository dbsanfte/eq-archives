Character History

My recollections of my childhood in Felwithe are like a bright globe shining with warmth and love. My father left to fight with Artillian Vincere when I was very young, so my memories of him are few. But the infrequent letters that we received from him spoke of his love for my mother, for his two children and for his beloved country, Faydwer. We read between the lines of his recounts of the fierce battles and saw the courage, honor and loyalty that shone there.

My mother scratched out a meager living, making her children rich with her love, if not with material things. My older brother was the light of my life; my hero, my joy, my protector. Even though there was seven years difference in our ages, he let me tag along at his side in all that he did. My memories of those golden days in Felwithe with him will forever fill my thoughts.

One day the letters from my father stopped coming. We heard after several agonizing weeks that he had been killed at Crushbone. My brother swore his vengeance on the Orcs and though he was only 16, vowed to join the fight. My mother begged him not to go, but I knew what he had to do. Early one morning, he awakened me to tell me goodbye. As he hugged me close, I wondered if I would ever see him again.

After he left, the days crept by painful and lonely. His letters reached us late or not at all. My mother’s health worsened and she was overcome with the fever that racked our village that winter. Without kin or money, a kind neighbor family took me in.

Feeling completely alone in this world, my one burning desire is to find my brother. It has been two years since I received his last letter. It said that he thought he would be sent to Kunark to scout the country in preparation for an invasion against the Iksarks. Although I am young, I want only to find him or his resting place. I am setting off in search of him. May Tunare bless my quest.
