Character History

My earliest memories are those of being left on my own in Qeynos yard with a few scraps of food and little more than a sharpened butter knife as a weapon.Clearly, I was to fend for myself.

I made my way, but grew angry and resentful as I questioned many things Why was I here and who was responsible for putting me here? Why did they abandon me? Or had they met an early demise? I had eschewed the gods and goddesses early on - they weren't offering any help nor answers. Even today, I hold them at arms length, though I have made peace with the Light Dieties and better understand their roles in the universe.

The true turning point in my life was meeting Juseanus near the Wind Spirit Song in Qeynos. He saw the anger I held and recognized that the path I would likely follow would be dark and tormented. He befriended me, and being the clever fellow he is, drew me into the League of Antonican Bards bit by bit. I had always enjoyed listening to him sing and play. Recognizing this, he and his mates at the Wind Spirit tutored me in music and on various instruments. In return, they asked me to run errands for them, such as running reports for their network of watchful eyes in Qeynos.

Jeseanus eventually encouraged me to venture out of Qeynos. "Musicians are a breed destined to tour," he had told me all the time I had known him. The stories of his days of travel made me eager to begin my own journeys.

I spent a few revolutions in the hills outside Qeynos, then a couple more in the western Karana plains. After that, my travels took me across Antonica, but I felt the need to learn more of my Elven heritage, so I sailed to Faydwer, spending five revolutions amongst the Wood Elves and High Elves who warmly accepted me. Still, the travel beetle had bitten me hard, and I needed to move on. Sailing back to Antonica, I grew familiar with Freeport, but I preferred to set my camp outside in the desertlands south of the city in lieu of renting a room within the city walls. Two revolutions later, we come to the area I presently call home - the isle of Kunark. I'd heard stories about it from the High Elves of Felwithe, but the adventurer in me wanted to experience it firsthand. For the past two - almost three - revolutions, I have been enamored with the lush lands and the still impressive ruins in the areas I have explored.

It seems so long ago before the disclipline of music shaped the wayward energies I had, and the companionship of the Qeynos bards instilled a faith in many of my fellow Noratthians and a desire aid in protecting the lands from the ever-present forces of evil.
