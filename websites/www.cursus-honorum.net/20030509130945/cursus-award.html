Awards earned in Cursus Honorum


There are many types of awards to be earned in Cursus Honorum. This is a list of possible awards and short explanations of what they represent.

Awards earned for exceptional personal actions include Valor, Nobility, Generosity, and Sacrifice. For each award in this catagory members will gain one political point (up to a maximum of 5 points.) To receive these awards your actions must be reported to the Regent and witnessed by another player. You may report yourself or be your own witness. Niether the reporter or the witness need to be guild members.
The Sword of Valor will be awarded to those who exhibit extreme courage or bravery in combat. Different symbols may be used depending on the class. For example, the mace for clerics or a scimitar for druids, but a weapon symbol is always used to denote valorous actions.
The Shield of Nobility is awarded to those who exhibit great acts of honor or nobility in a non-combat situation. For example, traveling across 10 zones without "sow" and possibly a boat ride to ressurect a guildmate.
The Shield of Generosity is for members who give to those who are less fortunate, but does not include twinking. The gift must be of signifacant value to the one giving and the one receiving.
The Shield of Sacrifice is given to guildmates who knowingly sacrifice themselves for the good of the group. Dying foolishly does not count. It is the knowledge of knowing you will not survive and then acting to save your group or even others around you that earns this award.

Many awards can be earned through guild participation. These include guild raids on the various dungeons and lairs in Norrath. You can also earn awards for participating in trade fairs or guild run contests.
This shield is earned for exceptional actions against the undead during guild raids. Their strongholds include Befallen, Unrest, Kaesora, and many others.
Members who prove themselves worthy against the goblins of Norrath during guild raids may earn themselves this award. Goblin lairs include Runny Eye and Permafrost.
This award is presented to those who surpress the Gnoll threat during guild raids. Gnoll lairs are Black Burrow and Splitpaw.
The Steel Castle Shield is awarded to members who participate in raids and sieges against the evil castles in Norrath. Examples include Crushbone, Mistmoore, and Karnor's Castle.
The Lion Shields are for members who help in guild events in the wilderness. The creatures being fought do not have a lair but occupy a region. For example, the many Dervish Cutthroat camps, bandits, or giants and griffons at higher levels.
This Copper Shield is awarded for actions against Aviaks during guild events. Their city lies in South Karana, but Aviaks are found in Lake Rathe as well.
The Polished Copper Shield is awarded for combat against the Sarnaks in Kunark. If the event is to siege to the Sarnak stronghold in the Lake of Ill Omen, then the castle shield would be approriate.
This Lustrous Copper Shield is given for guild raids against Froglocks and raids into Upper Guk.
This Lustrous Silver Shield is also given for raids against Froglocks, but is for guild events in Lower Guk, both live and dead sides.
These shield awards are for guild raids and events that are not otherwise covered by a previous award.

The High-Council can also give out awards to guild members. They can vote to give out awards that do not fall under other catagories. A maximum of 5 political points may be earned this way.
The Bronze Shield with Crossed Swords is awarded for repeated and consistant personal valor. No one needs to report or witness the individuals actions; over time the council will see for theselves who deserves such recognition.
The Silver Shield with Crossed Swords is awarded for consistantly grand honorable and noble actions. It is the highest honor the High-Council can bestow on you.
Castle awards represent land grants given to guild members. It can be earned by finishing a particularly difficult quest, participating in role-playing events, or any other reason the High-Council agrees to.
Rohwin's Vanguard is given to the most trusted members of Rohwin's house. They are true to Artillian's cause and have sworn fealty to Rohwin.
This missive represents personal grants given out by the Regent to exceptional members.
These dragons are awarded to those that slay the various dragons of Norrath. Which awards go with which dragons are not yet decided, and neither are the appropriate political points.

These are the awards available in Cursus Honorum. Suggestions for new awards or changes are welcome via E-mail. Additional shields, weapons, and other icons are welcome as well. Updates will be made to give more dungeons and activities their own award.
