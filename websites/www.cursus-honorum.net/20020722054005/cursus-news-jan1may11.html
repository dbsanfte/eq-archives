March 25, 2001

The High-Council have passed a motion to add three new rules.

1. Members must be of high rank (35+) to hold a seat on the High-Council. Since Benedain was promoted to High-Council prior to this rule he will maintain his position.

2. When a branch of power reaches eight or more members, then a second officer position will be opened. The two highest members of a branch of power will be officers and vouchers.

3. Low and mid ranking members may not accumulate more than ten political points. Points in excess of ten will be lost, but when level 35 is reached then points will be awarded normally again.

These rules have been added to add goals to strive for and to reward advancement in the game. They will take effect on Sunday, April 1st.

March 15, 2001

A minor rule addition has been made. A quorum was present for an impromtu vote and the following motion passed. The Regent my appoint Viceroys as required and/or members may make requests for certain members to become Viceroys. A Viceroy will be given officer status, but will not hold the same power as other officers. They will not be valid vouchers nor will they be listed as officers on the web-site. Their function is to make it easier to issue the invite command to newly approved recruits. This new rule has been added to the Hierarchy of Nobles and the Code of Nobility pages.

Rohwin

February 6, 2001

After cafeful consideration and consulting various people in Norrath, some rule changes have been made. Guild officers will only be those who have been promoted to the High-Council. Officer status is still permenant, so previous High-Council members will retain their officer status and are still eligable for vouching for new members. Also, all the founding members will be a part of Rohwin's Vanguard and will earn the extra political point as a bonus for helping to form the guild. The rules do not state this, but none of the founding members will be initiates. Everyone will become full members upon creation of the guild. Other minor revisions have been made, such as correcting spelling and grammer. Feel free to point out confusing or contradictory rules, as well as broken links or further grammer errors.

Rohwin
