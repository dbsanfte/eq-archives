Elyar's Kerra Isle Vacation

Elyar

Klennix
Greetings, good friends! Apologies for my absence, but a brief business trip to Erudin to meet with Al'Kabor regarding the Vah-Shir turned into a somewhat extended vacation upon tropical Kerra Isle. I was able to scribe some lovely images for you during my visit, I hope you enjoy them as much as I enjoyed my trip.

For those of you who are unaware, Kerra Isle is a small ridge situated off the west coast of Odus. Long ago, the Erudites fought a long and bitter war with the Kerran cat-people, culminating in the unleashing of magics so powerful, the Kerrans were nearly destroyed. The remains of this battle can be seen today in the twisted ruins known as The Hole.

Scholars have long wondered about the history of the Kerrans, but the Erudites have been tight lipped about their dishonorable war. No suprise, given their general attitude. Fortunately, the great Wizard Al'Kabor maintains some sanity, and agreed to see me to discuss some of his latest findings.

Ancient texts found by Al'Kabor in the ruins of the Iksar city of Kaesora suggest that the Kerran people were once known as the Vah-Shir, a race descended from cats. Legends state that when the Erudites eradicated the Vah-Shir, some of them escaped to Norrath's moon, Luclin. Several of the runes and texts found by Al'Kabor suggest that magical means exist to reach Luclin, hence my visit.

I left Al'Kabor's study and journeyed to Kerra Isle to speak with the Kerrans to try and learn of their history, but it became quickly obvious that the shamanistic cats no longer posessed the memory of their forebearers. Fortunately, the cats were kind to me, so I stayed some time and enjoyed the tropical climate.

Here on the left you can see the foreboding entrance to the island. The defenses are strong, as the Kerrans constantly fear continued violence from the Erudites. Whether war is again in their future, one cannot be sure, but they continue to harbor ill will against each other to this day. The sick tinge of the Toxxulia forest gives this whole area a feel of decay, but the tunnel leads to a bright and clear tropical paradise.

















On the right, a picture of Rrialli, a lovely woman who kept me well fed by cooking all manner of treats. The cat women in general were very friendly, and equipped me with fishing implements while teaching me how to catch the elusive Kerran Catfish, a truly delectable morsel found only in Kerra Bay, off the Abysmal Sea. This swimming was also wonderful, although there were a few piranha to contend with.























Here's a fine sunset as the Kerrans prepared for a Luau to celebrate my visit. Although they had never heard of me, they welcomed me with open arms. They were wary at first of my ties to Erudin, but after a few drinks and some tales of Erudite silliness, they warmed to me considerably. They seem relatively indifferent to most visitors, but are quick to trust those who show them kindness. I would not suggest entering Kerra Isle, however, if you are of Erudite blood.













Before I left, I was summoned to visit the resident priestess of Kerra. She was interested in the purpose of my visit, and we spoke at length. She was quite interested in my knowledge of Kerran history, and requested that I and my associates in Cursus Honorum endeavor to learn more of the Kerran saga, and make our assistance when we can. In return, she promised the goodwill of her people and all the catfish we can eat.











I hope you enjoyed the tale of my trip. It was a well needed vacation, and I shall look forward to returning to your service when I arrive in Freeport. Good journeys, my friends!

Sincerely,
Elyar Klennix, Wizard and Viceroy of Cursus Honorum
