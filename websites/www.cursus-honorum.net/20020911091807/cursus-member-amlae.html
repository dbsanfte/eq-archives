Character History

Again, the child Amlaith screamed. The Crushbone Clan had killed her mother in the initial attack. Her father stood with the guards, she didn't know if he was alive or not. The guards managed to hold off the orcs long enough for those that couldn't (or wouldn't) fight to make their escape. Amlaith took her younger brother's hand, and told him not to let go. Together they ran to the far end of the city, hoping to slip down and make their escape into the forest. Soon the panicked crowd, mixed of an equal number of wood elf residents and outlanders alike, surrounded them. No one saw the young girl and her even younger brother. Soon, someone ran into the boy, knocking him to the ground. In their panic, they either didn't notice, or didn't care.

"Amlae," he screamed, "Help." And she tried, but she couldn't pull him to his feet. The crowd kept shoving her away from him. Soon, the crowd was gone. Amlaith made her way to her brother's side. He was injured, but still breathing.

A figure stepped away from the battle. The girl had never seen a Dark Elf, but one stood before her now, and she shivered with fright. He came straight towards them. He kicked her brother away from her, and over the side of their precious city in the sky. "He was weak," the course voice, "But you, my little one, I sense strength in you already. Join me and I can show you what that strength is capable of."

"Never," Amlaith shouted, slowly moving towards the edge of the platform. Just before she jumped off, she shouted, "I'd rather die than join the likes of you."

She expected a thousand horrible fates. To have an arrow shot through her from the dark elf above, to painfully slam into the forrest floor and die. What she didn't expect was that for her fall to slow, and to land safely on her feet. The wolf before her changed shape into its natural Elven form. Amlaith had heard of these shape-shifter druids, but she'd never met one. Even so, instantly, she knew what it was. "Stay with my friend here. Salayas will protect you." She indicated the wolf by her side.

"Is he... Like you?" Amlaith asked.

"No, he's not a druid, just a wolf. But don't be frightened, he'll protect you."

The druid morphed back into a wolf form, and made her way back to the sky city to join in the fight, and Amlaith and her new companion slipped away into the forest. Amlaith would not return to the city, nor see another person, save for the occasional druid or ranger, until she was an adult. (Ok.. the stories A LOT longer, but you said brief, so I'm summing up a bunch to say she was raised in the wild and taught to be a druid by wolves, trees, and the occasional other druid)

Many years went by. Eventually, Amlaith came upon the wood-elf city of Kelethin again. One of the guards asked her for her name. She gave the only name she could remember. The one her brother used to call her, the one he shouted before his death. "Amlae"

Though the memories of that day had long since faded, the impact was still strong. She took a lift to the top of the city, and started walking around this foreign environment. Again, she was surrounded by people, shopping now instead of running. She became dizzy, disoriented, and very frightened. Soon, blackness came over her. When she awoke, she found herself in a cool building. Someone calmly spoke to her. "You're safe here. This is the home of the Kelethin's druids."

She stayed with them until they were sure that she was ok. Quickly, they saw in her the skills of one of their own. They taught her a few things she hadn't yet learned. They knew she was one of their own, though she would probably not often frequent the city. When she left, they told her she should come back as often as she wanted to learn new things. (Again, skipping LOTS for the sake of brevity)

More years passed. Very infrequently, Amlae, as everyone now new her, came to the city to learn a new spell, but for the most part, she stayed to the wilds of Norath, where she was far more at home. Her travels eventually took her to Luclin, where she met the Paladin Rowhin. A frenzied wolf had chased her up a tree. She was trying to figure out what was wrong with the she- wolf, but Rowhin killed the wolf, saving her life in the process. At first, Amlae was angry. He had killed the wolf before she had determined if it was necassary or not. She stormed off, leaving him confused.

Later, when her temper cooled, she went back to the tree, and tracked the paladin to his camp, apologizing for her anger.

That night, the two spoke of many things concerning the growing evil. He invited her to his fight, but she declined, explaining to him her problems with crowds, though not the origin of those problems. They went their seperate ways. Amlae continued to try and learn why the wolves of Luclin were so strangely afflicted. A few nights later, a sickly wind blew threw the trees, killing everything it touched. It whispered a name of evil that she couldn't quite understand. As the wind blew past her, she a cold deeper than death itself overcame her, bringing her to her knees as the breath was knocked from her. She didn't know what the message meant, and the only person she could think of that might was the paladin she had recently encountered.

After a few inquiries, she found him in the rathetear mountains, trying to keep the current frenzy of giants at bay. She joined him in his effort against this foe, much stronger than she was, keeping him healed for his battles. In the small breaks they had, she told him of the message. She also told him that she was ready to join his fight. He told her he knew of one that might be able to discern what the message meant, perhaps even tell what the name she couldn't understand was. She agreed to the meeting.
