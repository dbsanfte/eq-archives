Code of Conduct / Guidebook

Guild Class Definitions / Responsibilities

Guild Leadership
The guild leadership consists of the Guild Leader and the Guild First Officer (if one is appointed.)

The guild leadership is responsible for guiding the general direction of the guild and speaking on behalf of the guild when appropriate. The guild leadership also maintains final and ultimate responsibility for the guild and its members.

Guild Officership
The guild officership consists of all officers within the guild.

The guild officership is responsible for performing invitations to the guild, assisting the membership through education of the guild and the Code, and assisting the Guild Leadership in directing the guild.

Guild Officers are either Senior Officers or regular Officers. Certain Officers may have specific duties (such as Banker, Events Coordinator, etc.) Those duties will be reflected on the guild roster.

Guild Membership
The guild membership consists of all persons carrying the Advocacy of Shadows guild tag.

The guild membership is responsible for upholding the Code of Conduct and supporting fellow guild members.

Code of Conduct

Every member of the Advocacy of Shadows represents the guilds through their actions. With this understanding, a code of conduct has been established. The Code of Conduct is based on a set of common-sense rules and is the foundation upon which we operate.

(1) Members shall abide by the EverQuest "play nice" policy which includes such matters as kill stealing, fast "ninja" looting, creating trains, etc.
(2) Members shall refrain from communications that violate community standards (ex. extremely profane language)
(3) Members are expected to show an acceptance of persons that are different from themselves and shall not show acts of discriminatory bias. Further, members are encouraged to speak up against acts of discrimination or prejudice in any way.
(4) Members are encouraged to become involved in both our community, and communities abroad.
(5) Members are strongly encouraged to read and post on the AOS forums. This includes
posting their current levels as they progress, so that our roster may be as accurate as possible.

Violations of the Code of Conduct

Should a member perceive any violation of the Code of Conduct, or a violation of the spirit which our guild is founded upon, the member is encouraged to discuss their concern with the member involved and/or the guild leadership/officership.

When a possible violation is presented, the guild leadership will review the alleged violation. During this review, the guild leadership may request input from the officership and/or the membership

Should the review by the leadership find that the member was in violation of the Code of Conduct, actions may be taken against the member, include warning, temporary suspension, temporary deguilding, or permanent deguilding.

 

Requirements for Membership

It is preferred that all members entering the guild are of level 20 and above. The person being guilded must have the support of an officer and/or a member.

Exceptions to this requirement are as follows:
(1) real life friends and/or family members
(2) alternate accounts, when the primary account is guilded
(3) special circumstances, following a review by a member of the guild leadership.


Guild Communications

Communication within the guild occurs primarily through three venues, the webpage,
the forums, and in-game chat.

(1) Webpage
The webpage may be located either at www.webfavorite.com/pages/aos. The webpage has
links to our message board, screen shots, our roster and recruiting information.

(2) Forums
Members may connect to our forums either at www.webfavorite.com/pages/AOS/messagebd.html <http://www.webfavorite.com/pages/AOS/messagebd.html>. or <http://pub47.ezboard.com/badvocacyofshadows73523>.

The forums are an area for offline conversations, including general discussion, membership updates,
members only, guild events/quests, and officer discussion areas.

To create an ezBoard account and gain access to our members only areas, follow these
steps:
1. Go to the AOS Forums (either via the direct link or the webpage)
2. Click on the "Register Your Free Account" link.
3. Select a "Global Account" unless you have a specific reason for wanting only
a local account.
4. Enter in your personal information (note, this information is not accessible
by any guild member, including the AOS board administration staff.)
5. Post a message in the General Discussion forum, while logged in, requesting
access. If your username is not that of your EverQuest guilded character name, please
state your EverQuest guilded character name.
6.The AoS board administrator will review and grant the appropriate access, and
post a message stating that your access has been granted!

(3) In-Game Discussion
Communication in-game is done through /guildsay (or /gu).
