Red Fish - Another name is red snapper.  They are found in abundance throughout the tropics.  They have elongated bodies, large mouths, sharp canine teeth, and forked tails.  They are somewhat big and carnivores.  There are good fried and served with sauce on top.  The firm texture of the meat is also good for soup dishes.
Sanma (Pacific saury) - They are long, slim saltwater fish from the family Scomberesocidae.  They are small, about 14 inches long and are characterized by beaklike but weakly toothed jaws and a row of small finlets behind the dorsal and anal fins.  Sanma lives near the surface and commonly jump and skim above the water.  I also had this before, steamed with soy sauce and is very tasty.
Sardine - A common fish from the herring family.  They are small, silvery, elongated fishes with a short dorsal fin and no scales on the head.  They range in length from 6 to 12 inches and live in dense schools, migrating along the coast and feeding on plankton.  They are commercially fished for a variety of uses such as bait, drying, salting, smoking, canned, or fresh fish markets.  Pilchard is an European sardine found in the Mediterranean Sea and off the Atlantic coasts of Spain, Portugal, France, and Britain.

        
(left to right) - fresh salmon, Ikura (salmon roe), aramaki salmon, unisex salmon

Salmon - Salmon is a very common fish.  Some species live in the sea during adulthood and travel back to their childhood river stream during spawning season.  Personally, I do not like salmon due to their strong fishy taste.  They range from 3 lbs. up to 80 lbs. depending on the specie.  Salmon roe is used in Japan, usually in sushi.  Aramaki salmon is salted dried salmon that is given as gifts during new year in Japan.  An average aramaki salmon that weights about 2.3Kg can cost you  about $47.00.  Unisex salmon a very rare form of mutation that appears in 1-5 out of 10,000.  The sexual organ are not developed thus, the meat of the salmon is fatty and tender.  This type of fish comes exclusively from Hokkaido and is very expensive (ranges from $190 to $300 each).

        
(left to right) - sawara (Spanish mackerel), scorpion fish in the tank, scorpion fish illustration, sturgeon

Sawara (Spanish mackerel) - mackerel refers to a number of swift-moving, streamlined food and sport fishes found in temperate and tropical seas around the world, allied to tunas in the family Scombridae (order Perciformes). Mackerels are rounded and torpedo-shaped, with a slender, keeled tail base, a forked tail, and a row of small finlets behind the dorsal and anal fins. They are carnivorous fishes and feed on plankton, crustaceans, mollusks, fish eggs, and small fish. They congregate in schools and swim actively in the upper 25-30 fathoms of the water in the warmer months and then descend to as deep as 100 fathoms during the winter. They spawn during the spring and early summer along coastlines. Their eggs average 1 mm (0.04 inch) in diameter, are buoyant, and drift in the uppermost five fathoms of water. Mackerels are mostly caught by nets, rather than by angling.  Spanish mackerel, a species of mackerel, is an Indo-Pacific fish said to weigh up to 45 kg (100 pounds).
Scorpion fish - This fish looks more like a pet than a fish.  In fact, I believe a popular posionous pet fish,  lion fish belongs to the same family.  It is a bottom-living marine fish widely distributed in temperate and tropical waters. Sometimes also called rockfish, or stonefish, because they commonly live among rocks, scorpion fish are perchlike fish with large, spiny heads and strong, sometimes venomous, fin spines. The fin spines, with or without venom, can produce deep and painful wounds.
Sturgeon - This is an exotic and expensive fish.  Sturgeon is native to temperate waters of the Northern Hemisphere.  Most species live in the sea and ascend rivers (possibly once in several years) to spawn in spring or summer; a few others are confined to fresh water.  Only several species provide caviar from eggs.  Sturgeons are valued for their flesh, eggs, and swim bladder. Their flesh is sold fresh, pickled, or smoked. Caviar consists of salt for preservation and the eggs, which are stripped from ripe females who are subsequently released.  The better grades of caviar are classified malassol, Russian for "lightly salted." Fresh caviar must be stored at between 32º and 45º F (0º and 7º C) or it deteriorates rapidly; for better storage it is pasteurized. Most true caviar is produced in Russia and Iran, from fish taken from the Caspian and Black seas.

         
 (left to right) - tai (sea bream), school of yellowfin tuna, yellowtail meat

Tai (sea bream) - Porgies is another for this fish.  they are marine fishes of the family Sparidae (order Perciformes). Tai are typically high-backed, snapper- or grunt-like fishes. They have a single dorsal fin, and their small mouths, equipped with strong teeth, can handle a diet of fishes and hard-shelled invertebrates.
Tuna - Tuna is very  common and yet very important to commercial fisheries.  They are the most common fish used in sushi bars.  Toro (fatty tuna) is meat taken near the belly of a large tuna.  With high fat and oil content, it is suppose to be better than maguro, which is the regular cut.  Yellowtail or yellowfin tuna is a smaller species of tuna that has lighter colored/texture meat.  Tuna is very versatile and thus, used in many different ways.  bonito flakes are used often in Ironchef for soup stock.  It is made from dried bonito (known as wood fish in Asia) and shaved to look like wood shavings.  A good soup stock that I made contains 1 part bonito flakes and 1 part wakame seaweeds.  Very good in making meso soup.
