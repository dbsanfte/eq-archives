Shadow Knights Group Tactics

Gerst D'Harbazshan

Shadow Knights, like any class, are very valuable to a group if used effectively. This guide will help you earn your keep. Probably the most important thing to remember is that you are a tank, a meleer. Your job is to take dmg, protect the casters, and dish out some dmg too.

First, a SK must be good at taking dmg. This is more important than dishing it out at most times. In a group, you want the group to work towards its max potential. That cant be done if the wizzy or cleric in your group is getting nailed. Thus, you have to watch the health bars of your group members as much as you watch the fight. Taunting the mob is the tactic used to gain the attention of the mob. For a SK, taunting can be done by using taunt skill, spells, or harm touch. You want to taunt enough so the mob will gain your attention, but not too much so that it sticks to you. Spells are a better choice for a taunt than taunt skill since it will dmg the mob also. The taunt on the spell isnt too much either that the mob will stick to you. You have to communicate to your group members your actions so they all can act accordingly and so you can see the feedback. If you use taunt, say so. If you use a certain spell, like engulfing darkness, say so. Your group members, in turn, can do certain things at certain times, so each in turn taunts the mob effectively so dmg is spread out. Other tactics besides taunting is using the fear spell to make a mob run away or shadow step to get the mob off you.

Another job a SK has is scouting. SKs are very good as scouts due to the invis, invis to undead, and feign death spells a SK has. Cast invis on yourself and go scout the area. If you are caught and you got too many mobs, feign, and the mobs will leave you alone. Good hpts and ac are a plus too if attacked.

Shadow Knights are probably the second best pullers, behind monks, due to feign death spell and other spells used in conjunction. The feign death spell is has a slower recycle time than the monks and can be interrupted, but works just the same. On normal pulls, SKs can use engulfing darkness to engage the mob from a distance and slow the mob down. This will give you time to run back to your group before getting hit. Optionally, you can use a bow and arrow too. You can feign death if you get too many for your group to handle. If you want to break a room or pull one mob from a group of mobs, feign death can be used. SKs have an advantage in being able to spell the mob from a distance so SK can feign before getting whacked. Also, spells can be used to grab the last mob walking back to its spot. Monks have to normally run up to the mob to engage and to pull the last mob walking back. Other SKs spells help in pulling. Invisibility and invisibility to undead are useful to pull when you have to get into aggro range of mob and you dont want to be caught unawares. Shieldskin is also helpful. It protects you from 2 to 3 hits from the mob when pulling and prevents you from becoming stunned if hit. Lull undead can be cast on undead mobs to single out a mob.

Shadow Knights have some others useful talents. SKs can locate corpses if one of the group members lost his body. SKs also have some buffs spells, like endure and resist disease and cold. Other spells include water breathing, cancel magic, and shadow vortex. Sometimes the evil nature of the SK can help a group, since SKs can kill almost anything without it affecting most of the SKs factions in a bad way. The SK can kill a certain good NPC for example that holds a certain items that would give bad factions for someone else, but not the SK.

Hopefully this guide has help you in being a more affective SK in a group. Vendui.
