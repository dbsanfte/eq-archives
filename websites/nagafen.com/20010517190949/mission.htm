Our Mission

 

Posted on Everquester.com by Snaggle Tooth (KEG). 

Well, now that the dicussion is academic, allow me to address the exclusivity remarks.

Everquest is a game designed around "exclusive and inclusive groups". That is, to perform a task, you must select specific people from a group of people, include them in a party, then apply them to the situation. Use too few or too many and you spoil the task. This is what the game designers intended. Grouping and guild formation are a core game mechanic.

As a newbie, to survive in Everquest, you quickly learn that finding 5 other compatriots is much easier than working your way to the top alone. Now let's treat the "guild" as an individual for a second. As a "guild", Bozo decides that it is too difficult for him to work his way to the top alone. Therefore Bozo contacts his buddies, Schleppo and Tanko. He proposes they work together to achieve the things that are near impossible to do as an individual(guild). You see, Bozo trusts Schleppo and Tanko, as they don't tend to get everyone killed, unlike others such as Necro and Wizzo. Guilds coming together to achieve a task is simply a larger scale version of 2 or 3 newbies grouping together to take down dervish cutthroats.

No one is preventing other guilds from forming aliiances and doing the exact same thing. It is not our fault that we can organize. We should not be penalized or chastised for it.

I am sometimes criticized by my allies for making plans and maps available to the public. I have stated before, and firmly believe that everyone has the right to be able to do Dragons, Planes, and other large scale tasks. In the past, I have organized raids for both Dragons and Planes that were "open to one and all". These type of events are very dificult to manage and organize, draining huge amounts of energy and effort. That is why they are not the "norm", but rather the exception. It is much easier to accomplish these tasks with a known ally who you have come trust and rely upon.

This point completely disregards tactical considerations. From a tactical point of view, you want to accomplish the task efficiently as possible, with as little deaths as possible. You want loot to be predetermined to avoid conflict. You want a plan. You want a map. You want veteran participants. You want an efficient group structure that flows down from a central organizer. This means you bring along only people that you can trust to operate under these conditions. You bring your guild, and your experienced allies.

Our raids are successful largely because of group layout. We have the guild-specific organizers from each guild all in the same group. We have the groups diversified so that communication travels efficiently from the event-organizer to the guild-specific organizers to the individuals in other groups. Such communication is not possible in "non-Triad" events. The degree to which communication suffers in these non-Triad events can be measured in corpses.

I hope this provides some justification for the Triad's existence, and explanation on why people may be excluded from events for "no good reason".


Gud Bashing,

Snaggle Tooth

 

This is from one of the first posts on Nagafen.com by MFH Officer Sharak'Duom. We think very much reflects the spirit of the Triad.

The establishment of the Triad is an amazing step for the Rathe Community as a whole. It is an accomplishment that will always be remembered and will stand out among all of EQ for a very long time. The Triad puts the Rathe server and the guilds involved easily near the top of the spectrum in relation to accomplishments and ability.

I wish to commend everyone within the three guilds. Despite a few odd bumps in the road, we have easily held together. This is an alliance that hopefully will withstand the test of time. Given time, as well, it will grow even stronger and smoother as we perfect a number of the processes involved. We must realize that we are still in our infancy, with challenges to meet and overcome around every corner. The good will and understanding of the individuals within the three guilds will go a long way towards easily handling those challenges. Not to mention patience =)

Let me share with you my feelings on the Triad and what it is and stands for. I do not view the Triad as a means to an end (defeating dragons, gods, etc). I view it as more of the ability to reach out beyond our respective guilds and embrace others, those members in the guilds that make up the Triad. It is no coincidence at all that CIW, KEG and MFH have joined together, we are the three most accomplished and most capable guilds on the Rathe. We also know that each guild (CIW, KEG, MFH) consists of very fine people, each and every one.

Too long has a rift been in place between many individuals that had prevented this from happening, but hopefully that rift is steadily being pulled back together. We all know our histories as individuals and the history of the guilds involved with the Triad. Through working together, we have the opportunity and skill to accomplish anything we wish to. And through the goodwill of everyone involved, we have the chance to create a bond that will be quite timeless.

I have dedicated myself to the Triad and to doing whatever is necessary to further its growth. I am very proud of what we have accomplished and I am sure will be proud of whatever we accomplish in the time to come.

I want to thank everyone for their patience during the birth of the Triad and for the coming months. I am certain that with a little work, a little stress and a good bit of friendship, the Triad will become quite timeless.

Just had to say it =)

Sharak'Duom
MFH Officer
