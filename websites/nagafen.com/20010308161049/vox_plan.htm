The Vox Plan

Disclaimer: Other servers or groups using this plan, please credit the KEG website and provide a link to our website. Special thanks to Questor of Norrath, for the excellent map of Permafrost Keep.

  • You may die at least once during initial setup.
  • Be prepared to die about 4 times throughout the course of the evening. If this price is too high to pay, please notify me and you will be removed from the Vox raid.
  • Have a newbie character ready.
  • Know who your group leader is, so that you can send them tells as to when it is safe to log-in/zone/etc..
  • Be on time. If you are late, you will be replaced by someone on standby.

The Setup

  1. The meeting place will be at the King Camp spot marked on the Vox Map.
  2. During the formation of parties we will be clearing the King Camp spot as well as the Ice Giant Diplomat, and the two giants that spawn behind him.
  3. Once groups are formed, a warrior buffed with high magic resistance, and Spirit of The Wolf (SOW), will begin pulling all of the giants, Icy terrors, etc. between the King Camp and Vox's Lair. We will pull through two cycles of spawns to spread out the respawn rate.
  4. Once the giant respawn rate is under control, a Monk with SOW and magic resistance buffs and a warrior with SOW and magic resistance buffs will move up to the Monk FD spot (see Vox Map).
  5. The Warrior should back up a few paces from the Monk FD Spot in the direction of the King Camp and wait there.
  6. The Monk will proceed to Nagafen's lair, open the rock/door, and step inside. This should Agro all of the mobs within. This will usually "agro" Lady Vox as well.
  7. The monk will then race down to the ramp to the Monk FD spot and feign death.
  8. The warrior will wait until Vox is not in sight (if she was pulled), then move up to where the monk is feigned dead. He will allow whatever mobs are present (Hopefully the Priest of Nagafen, but not always) to "agro" on him, then pull them to the King Camp Spot.
  9. The parties waiting at the King Camp Spot will kill whatever mobs were pulled. During this battle the monk should remain Feigned Dead.
  10. All party members in the Monk's group and the Warrior's group should camp out. The monk should camp using the "/quit" command. The reason for this is that it is highly likely that Vox will be agro on the Monk and/or Warrior. And person who casts healing, buffs, etc, on the warrior on monk will inherit this agro. If the parties do not camp out, it is likely that Vox will appear at the diplomat doors (or if she has somehow taken damage) begin summoning party members to her lair.
  11. Steps 4-10 should be repeated until all of the mobs in Vox's lair have been pulled and killed. It is necessary to do these as quickly as possible.
  12. All of the parties will proceed to the Staging Spot (see Vox Map). They will have 5 minutes to buff before the charge on Vox.
  13. After all parties are prepared, the person staging the Vox raid should begin a 30 second countdown to charging Vox.
  14. Groups 1, 2, the Pets Group, and the Nuke Group should charge the lair.
  15. 20 seconds later Groups 3, 4, and any remaining groups (besides CR Group) should charge the lair.
  16. Vox must die on this initial charge. Unlike Nagafen, the assault cannot be done in waves. This is due to the fact that Vox heals herself much more quickly than Nagafen. It is also due to the fact that corpse recovery, while Vox is injured, is almost impossible.
  17. Once Vox is dead, the group leader of the killing group should loot the corpse. Once the Vox corpse is looted, all parties must immediately retreat to the the King Camp Spot to avoid respawn. There will not be time for mana recovery or resurrections within Vox's Lair. Corpses of slain comrades must be dragged back to the King Camp Spot for resurrection.
  18. Loot will be announced and distributed per the Loot List.

Corpse Recovery

  1. In the event of a failed raid, corpse recovery must wait until Vox has regained 95% of her health.
  2. Once Vox has regained this health she will begin running willy-nilly between her lair and the diplomat doors (just before the King Camp Spot). While Vox it going nutty, the CR group should make dashes into her lair to retrieve and drag corpses out.
  3. If there are any monks, necro's etc. that Vox is still aggressive on, they should feign death and remain so throughout the recovery. If a bard is in their group, they should disband to avoid Vox going Agro on the bard (due to Bardsong).
  4. The remainder of the Non-CR groups should camp out and log back in, to reset the dragon's agro.
  5. Once all corpses are recovered and resurrected, the raid can begin anew.

Things Not To Do On A Vox Raid

  • Never use any offensive AE (area effect) songs, weapons, or spells, during, before, or after the attack. There are numerous mobs that dwell beneath Vox's lair (level 50+ polar bears come to mind) that will be pulled if offensive AE is used.
  • Do not evac. Either camp out, or die and let the CR team take over. All evac spots are too far from Permafrost for the groups to come back together within a reasonable time frame. In the event of crash, your party's halfling can be used as a flotation device.
  • Do not wander in the area between the King Camp and Vox's lair. There are many pitfalls in this area that lead to underground caves chock-full of level 50 monsters ready and willing to eat you.
  • Do not fight Vox anywhere but in her lair. Fighting Vox in any other location will usually result in her corpse going "poof" (thus no loot).
  • Do not complain if you die multiple times, this aggravates others and lowers the morale of all groups. You knew what you were signing up for, so take it in-stride.

Notes

  • Dragon Fear spell is the biggest inhibitor to a successful dragon raid. Due to this, only level 50 players will be in the first 4 groups, and level 49+ players in the remaining groups. These players have the highest chance to resist the Dragon Fear based on their level, thus it is logical that they should be the ones in the raid.
