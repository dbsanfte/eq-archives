DISCLAIMER: Even if you follow all these guidelines we may not invite you for months, or even at all.

So YOU want to be TMO?

The next step is to convince US to want YOU.

First, there are 2 things you should know about TMO.

  1. You Will Respect Other Members.
    You may not like everyone in this guild, and they may not all like you. However, you will be expected to follow one basic principle in all that you do in EQ: your guild comes first. Be it arguments, grouping, exp, res, teleport, loot, calendar issues, guild fights, or spawn "rights"... your guild members must come first in your priorities. If you are in this for yourself, then you are better off soloing.
  2. You Must Contribute to TMO's Success.
    We constantly push the envelope of what has been accomplished on Fennin Ro. If there's a dragon we have not slain, a Plane we have not explored, or a dungeon we have not conquored we will be the first guild to do it. When a new zone opens, we will be the first guild to break it. In order to this you must level, become a more skilled player, and show up to the majority of our events. If you cannot do this then you cannot contribute to our success.

There are 2 basic factors about YOU that we take into consideration when voting on your application:

  1. Your Approach
    This is completely subjective, but there are some people who, no matter what their ability, just don't fit into The Mystical Order. When you are trying to prove yourself worthy of being a member of The Mystical Order, you have to realize that we are the best of the best on Fennin Ro, and we will not settle for less. Basically, try and be a human being. Think of going out and getting a new job, and how you would act in that situation; you would wear your best face, your best clothes, and you'd show off what you are good at and why the hell we should hire you. If you are going to come and hinder us, we don't need it. If you are going to screw around and not help, we don't need that either. If you're going to whine and cry and expect to get all the phat lewt, you're going to be in for a wakeup call. No one can tell you to be someone you are not, so be yourself, but don't expect to be accepted unless you have proven to us that you are worthy to have our tag over your head.
  2. What You Need to Make the Cut
    We are a high level guild. Many people in the guild believe we already have too many people, so we really have no reason to invite low level people into the guild. These days, anything under 55 is pushing it for new invites. Since most of our main characters are 55-60, it is harder for you to contribute to the guild if you are lower level than most of us. If you don't like to raid, TMO probably isn't for you. We go on a lot of raids, and this is something we do almost daily. If you have the patience, and the time, then by all means we'd be happy to have you along, as long as you can contribute.

As for equipment:

  • Think raid gear... shoot for 100+ resists in all categories (not necessarily all the time, just for specific raids). If we're going to kill Trakanon, we don't need people to have 0 poison resist gear on because you're going to end up dieing, and that doesn't help us at all.
  • Dungeon Keys. Don't even think of applying unless you have your Sebilis and Charsis (Howling Stones) keys.
  • We've recently made our first trip into Veeshan's Peak, and we plan on heading back plenty more times. If you don't know what Veeshan's Peak is, I don't think TMO is for you. If you know what Veeshan's Peak is, but you haven't done anything yet to get started on the quest, do so. We don't want to have to hold your hand through the game, and going to Veeshan's Peak is one of the most difficult things in the game. If you can't prove to us that you have the initiative to at least get a portion of your key done, then you'd bettter start cracking.
  • When and if we guild you, we don't want to guild your twink. You are joining TMO as yourself, and you are to contribute. Until you have your OS, HS, and VP key, you are level 58+ and you have all your necessary equipment, you should not be on your twink. Don't expect anyone to be very happy if we see your twink around more then you.
  • If you have approached joining The Mystical Order with the idea in mind that you will be getting great loot and then selling it, you have another thing coming. If and when you are rewarded with loot from a guild raid, that item still belongs to the guild. Usually, you will hold on to that item until you recieve something better, in which case we will most likely hand the item down to another member who does not have that item, and who needs it. If you need to make money, and you want to get your own items, then do it on your own.

When you're ready to apply, post on our message board about yourself, what you can do for TMO, and what you hope to get out of being in TMO. To even be considered you don't just need people to post support... you need MANY posts of support. 30+ different people is a good number to shoot for.

DO NOT constantly message officers for groups or ask officers to get people to post for your application. Our officers have enough to deal with and this would probably work against you.

These should not be considered hard and fast rules, but instead guidelines to help you decide if, when, and how to apply. Even successful TMO invites usually come after weeks of initiation. Get to know us, let us know you.

Do you have what it takes to become part of The Mystical Order?
