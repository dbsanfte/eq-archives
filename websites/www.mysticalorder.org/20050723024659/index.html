July 19,  2005    
   

Tormented No Longer - Opalopa the Court Jester

No bones about it, TMO had done it's share of slacking lately.  Ever since entering Anguish in March, we were able to make mince meat out of Ture, Warden Hanvar and Keldovan the Harrier, but at that point we were always unable to go any farther.  Was it because of our inept playing skill?  Was it because of the general suck that permeates across the guild?  Or hell, was it just extreme bad luck?

No my friends, the problem was none of these things.  For you see, there is a force in EQ that can dwarf skill, trump competance, and trivialize excellence.  What force is this you ask?  Simple, it's a matter of numbers.  The Tormentor event just simply wasn't designed around our raid force, so no matter how many times we'd flawlessly journey into Anguish, we'd never have what was necessary to continue.  In all those months, only twice were we able to make what was even more than a half hearted attempt.

THIS WOULD NOT STAND IN OUR WAY!  Using every mean at our disposal, the TMO Brain Trust spent several days debating the best way to fix this problem.  This proved to be a very intense period of time, as you can plainly see...

As we of course know now, it was Rarartiz who came up with the idea of taking a break from our brainstorming session to go to the zoo.  If not for this change of venue, we'd have never worked it out!  For you see, while standing outside the monkey cages watching them playing with their monkey toys and punching and pressing everything around them, someone (I can't remember who exactly, I was too busy engaging in a drooling competition at the time) realized that these monkeys were the ticket to advancing in EQ!  We hopped the fence, each of us grabbed a monkey, and ran like hell to the gates, laughing in delight as these primates flung feces back towards the zookeepers who were chasing us, as Rarartiz sang...

 

 

We now had 37 monkeys, 12 chimpanzees, 7 orangatangs and 1 gorilla at our disposal, and it was at this time the REAL work began.  The next two months were spent training these animals in the intricate art of EQ.  Many did not survive.  However, on July 18, 2005, those survivors of the group were ready.  It was time.

Gathering outside of Anguish on this day, we didn't have our normal force of four clerics.  Instead, what we had was a highly trained, highly skilled group of 42 primate clerics in addition to our normal 3!  This is what the average TMO cleric looked like that night: (Kuahara Pictured)

From that point on, it was a simple matter of exploiting the zone so we could fit our newly-buffed-up raid force of 108 into Anguish, and letting the monkeys go to work.  You've never seen an encounter absolutely DEMOLISHED until you've seen it exploited with twice the number of people it's intended for, and 1/2 the raid force controlled by trained monkeys.  Needless to say, on our first real day attempting the Tormentor Ring, Jelvan was saved, and the monkeys flung more feces in celebration. 

Since the encounter itself is as gay as any EQ encounter has ever been, there is of course no corpse to give a shot of.  However, if any other guild ever wants to attempt the Tormentor Ring the same way we did, feel free to use this diagram. 

Forging ahead, we confronted Arch Magus Vangl.  This gimp was such a pushover that 90% of our monkey clerics were actually on Nuking.  An easy kill for our uber brigade, and we meet a new friend!

Grats a bunch of people on loot that I don't have shots of either, but I assure you it's all highly uber stuff!  With any luck, we'll have OMM dead as soon as we train up a few more monkeys to play enchanters. 

Now, just to prove we're not all idiots, I leave you with some quotes.
