What is a MUD?

Imagine a text adventure game for your PC, great fun but after a while you either complete the game or you get bored of doing the same stuff over and over.  Now imagine that same game with thousands of other people also playing the game at the same time as you, that is basically what a MUD (Multi User Dungeon) is.
 
 
What is Discworld MUD?

Discworld MUD is one of many MUD's that can be played online, in my opinion it is the best by far.  It is taken from the books by the author Terry Pratchett about a strange world of swords and sorcery shaped like a disc which sits atop four elephants atop a giant turtle (sex unknown) by the name of Atuin which swims through space with no apparent aim.  The game and books differ from most fantasy books in the fact that there is a comedy element added to the whole thing.

How do I play Discworld MUD?

When you enter the MUD you will be asked to choose a class of character that you want to play.  The choices are as follows and time should be taken when selecting as once chosen you can't change without restarting your character:

Warrior:     The warrior relies on bags of health and weapon skills to vanquish his/her foes.

Wizard:      The wizard mostly relies on spells to get ahead in life, a good protection spell coupled with good offensive spells
                   can  make the wizard almost invincible.

Priest:        The priest is a mix between the warrior and the wizard with rituals depending on which god you follow.  Again a
                   priest  with a good protection ritual and a weapon to back it up with is a formidable foe.  Priests also have the
                   ability to bring back to life dead characters.

Thief:         Thieves rely on trickery and covert to overcome there foes.  They have the skills to thieve and can make vast
                   amounts of money by doing this.  When a thief becomes a PK (Player Killer) he can then also steal items from
                   other PK characters.  The thieves guild relies on a very tight guild structure, if you kill a thief for no reason then
                   you better watch your back as the guild heavies will soon pay you a visit for hassling one of their own.

Witch:        The witch class is for females only, altho a few males also play this class as female characters.  They rely on
                   strange spells (or sleight of hand tricks) called "headology" which can see them using broomsticks to fly all over
                   the disc to sending a swarm of killer bees to attack your character, they also have a great protection spell like the
                   wizards which floats a inanimate object around their bodies to deflect incoming melee attacks.

Assassin:   Assassins rely mainly on covert and weapon skills.  They are a tightly knit group and have very strict guild policies
                   which all assassins must follow.  This is the hardest guild to play as at a early stage of the characters life they have
                   to take the "Assassins Run", this is a test that all assassins must take to enable them to advance their skills more
                   from the guild, it also makes the character PK (he can be kill/be killed/steal/be stolen from by other PK's).  Also
                   once a Assassin has completed the run he gains a unique ability "Inhume".  As my character is a Assassin I will be
                   dealing with this character class in more detail.
