Creating your Ranger:

 

The Ranger is the most diverse of all the melee/casting hybrids.  The perfect combination of a Warrior, Rogue and Druid.  Primarily Melee, our attacks rely on speed and our ability to handle 2 weapons at the same time… combine that with our spells; Healing, Direct and Timed Attack.. SoW, Strength, Agility and Shielding and you have the best of all worlds… but there are disadvantages…

 

Unlike our Warrior brothers, the Ranger can not take a significant amount of damage.  Your primary defense will be to Slow your attacker, and use your Bow – here is where the Ranger excels, in a combat style made to suite you specifically… Your Bow is your Friend – never travel without it.

 

Your casting ability, on average, is half that of the Druids.  Yes, in advanced seasons, you may Heal and Buff – but you do not have the amount of mana to back it up, and you’ll find that you are quickly spent.

 

Pulling:  you can’t be beat !!  The Ranger’s Tracking is like no other classes.. range and accuracy, the ability to sort and filter.. you can find targets where most others would have to run all over the zone to find.  You are a valuable member of any team.

 

The following chart shows the differences in the available Ranger Races.

As you can see, the Wood Elf is the best, followed by Half Elf…

Humans, although they have an advantage in Strength & Stamina, do not make good Rangers.

* Recommended Areas to place your “Extra Points”

 

·          STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

·          STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

·          AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

·          DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows

·          WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

·          INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights

·          CHA: affects the  amount you pay for goods from NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you when selling

 

 

ARMOR:

 

Your Armor is all that stands between you and the Beast or Monster who is attacking… the better the armor, the less damage you’ll take per hit.  So, what do we at EQRangers recommend?  Well, that all depends on your resources.  We do not condone the practice known as “Twinking” – so, here is a list of armor which is obtainable at the appropriate levels:  When ever possible, Wood Elves should use “Small” Armor, it’s less weight.

 

Your New Ranger:  Right away, you are going to be fighting Spiderlings and Wolves – use this to your advantage and start working on your Tailoring Trade Skill.  Patchwork and Leathers, although are not the best, make a lot of difference compared to being naked.

 

·          Use one (1) Ruined Wolf Pelt + Pattern, to make each piece. (small – Wood Elf)

·          Use one (1) Ruined Cat Pelt + Pattern, to make each piece. (medium – Human, Half Elf)

 

Once you reach the point where you are making some cash, you may wish to start working on your Smithing Trade Skill and make yourself some Banded Armor – Many Rangers rely on Banded until their mid 20’s – and you can usually find someone to make it for you, if you don’t feel like spending the time to make it.. for approx 1plat per Armor Class.

 

Into your 20’s:  this is the time to start the Ranger’s  “Ivy Etched Armor” Quests, which are given out in the Haunted Woods of Kithicor.  This Armor can support your hunting needs throughout your 30’s.  Quests are listed below:

 

·          Etched Ivy Bracer – Magic – AC9, Dex+2, svPoison+3

·          Etched Ivy Gauntlets – Magic, Lore - AC11 Fire Resist: +7 Cold Resist: +7

·          Etched Ivy Boots – Magic, Lore – AC10 Sta: +5

·          Etched Ivy Helm – Magic, Lore – AC12 Wis: +5 Disease Resist: +5

·          Etched Ivy Tunic – Magic, Lore – AC19 Str: +3 Wis: +3 HP: +30

·          Etched Ivy Leggings – Magic, Lore – AC12 Agi: +5 Fire Resist: +5 Cold Resist: +5

·          Etched Ivy Sleeves – Magic, Lore – AC10 Str: +4

 

Please check those quests carefully – there are Gems which need to be purchased for each, and the “Runes of Ivy” are specific for each quest – but are Not Marked, so be careful not to mix them up.

 

Into your 40’s:  Now is the time to journey to the frozen lands of Velious and Hunt the RyGorr Orcs of Eastern Wastes – for they carry the RyGorr Armor.

 

·          Ry’Gorr Bracers:  AC9, Str+2 Cha+2 HP+5

·          Ry’Gorr Battle Mail:  AC18, Str+10 vsPoison+5 Mana+20

·          Ry’Gorr Coif:  AC13, Str+3 vsMagic+3 Mana+15

·          Ry’Gorr Collar:  AC9, Sta+4 vsMagic+4 Mana+10

·          Ry’Gorr Gloves:  AC12, Str+2 Cha+1 HP+5

·          Ry’Gorr Leggings:  AC13, Sta+2 Dex+2 svMagic+3 svCold+5 HP+5

·          Ry’Gorr Armguards:  AC11, Agi+3 svMagic+3 svCold+5 HP+10

 

As you can see here, the Etched Ivy Armor is Similar in Armor Class (+92 Ivy, +94 RyG), and you may decide to stay in your Ivy for a long time, and there’s nothing wrong with that…  but pay close attention to the Buffs which the RyGorr Armor provides and it will make a bit of difference.

 

Additional Wears:  Regarding the Ivy and RyGorr Armor(s), you will notice that a couple items are missing – Cloak, Belt and Mask – I recommend the following items, which can be acquired approx. level 30+

 

·          Cougar Skin Mask:  AC6, Agi+2 Dex+2 HP+10

·          Straw Spun Belt:  AC10

·          Dire Wolf Hide Cloak:  AC7, Str+5

 

The Straw Spun Belt can usually be found at Vendors for approx. 200p, or looted from Multiple “Mountain Giants” in Kunark.

The Mask and Cloak must be Quested for – The HQ CougarSkins and MQ Direwolf Fur may be found at the Gnome Igloos in Iceclad or Merchants of Thurgadim.

 

Weapons:

 

There are a Vast selection of Weapons for a Ranger to choose from, and honestly, it depends on your personal taste.  Over all.. the faster weapons suit the Ranger the best, as opposed to the slower – heavy hitting choices.

 

1 Handed Slashing is your primary Melee Choice. 

The Following Weapons are results of Quests, or may be found easily at Merchants or in Auctions (go to East Commonlands for auctions):

 

·          Velium Battle Axe:  28del, 8dam, effect:  Shards of Velium

o         Found on the Guards (47+) of Icewell Keep, or sold from Thurgadim Merchants

·          Short Sword of Ykesha:  24del, 8dam, effect: Ykesha

o         Found on the Ghoul Lord (47) in Lower Guk (1,000p)

·          Short Sword of Morin:  22del, 6dam, Str+3 Agi+3

o         Result of this Quest (28)

·          Fine Steel Weapons: various delays and damages

o         Purchased, or may drop from some 30+ Mobs

 

Archery is the combat skill that you are suited for the best.

We HIGHLY recommend that you work up your Fletching Skill, for you can make better arrows and bows than can be purchased, and it is a lot less expensive.

 

·          Dual Recurved Shaped Darkwood Bow:  41del, 19dam, 150range

·          The Raincaller:  45del, 20dam, 200range, Str+6 Dex+6

o         The next step from the Trueshot – see the info of this Quest.

·          The Wind Sabre:  22del, 13dam, 150range

o         Can you say Wow??

·          Priceless Velium Reinforced Bow:  33del, 35dam, Range is equivalent to your arrow

Where to Hunt:

 

This is a personal choice… a lot of people will give you different opinions, but I’ve found this to work:

 

·          Levels 01-10:  Misty Thicket, Antonica

o         Everything from Bixies to Goblins and Orcs

·          Levels 10-15+:  Eastern Plains of Karana, Antonica

o         Chasm Crawlers, Gorge Hounds, Lions, Bandits, etc…

·          Levels 15-20+:  Northern Plains of Karana, Antonica

o         Borer Beetles, Scythe Beetles, Highland Lions

·          Levels 20-30+:  Southern Plains of Karana, Antonica

o         Elephants, Gnolls, Aviaks

·          Levels 10-30:  Lake of Ill Omen, Kunark

o         Goblins, Skeletons, Sarnaks, Lions

·          Levels 15-45:  The Overthere, Kunark

o         Vast Variety of Animals & Sarnaks – beware of the patrolling Dragoons

·          Levels 35-45:  Ice Clad Ocean, Velious

o         Snow Cougars, Dervishes, Frost Giants

·          Levels 30-50:  Eastern Wastes, Velious

o         Snow Bunnies, Vast Variety of Animals, Giants, RyGorr Orcs.

·          Levels 35-60+:  Velketor’s Labyrinth

o         Blizzard Spiders, Crystal Gargoyles, Crystal Destroyers, etc…
