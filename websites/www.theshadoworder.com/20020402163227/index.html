Redefining "Ghetto" / Perplex
3/7/02

All I can say is, wow. TSO engaged Dozekar the Cursed tonight, with what you might call a very much ghetto raid configuration. Just under 40 people, only one shaman and one enchanter for debuff team, and with barely sufficient healing power.

Debuff and slow went off without a hitch, and all was going peachy till the lag monster the server had been plagued with all night reared it's ugly head again. With both shaman and chanter ending up kicking the bucket, the battle was doomed to fail once the slow wears off.

What happened then was a pure poetry. Slow wore off, and Doze started tearing through melee like toilet paper, quading 900 a pop with no delay. Duel, also known as The Brick Wall, got aggro and tanked the ugly Sontalak look-alike unslowed for good 30 seconds as 3 clerics chained CHs like mad, screaming at their Spell Casting time bars.

In the nick of time, the bard saves the day. Kanthis' bard slow lands, and situation is under control once again. Doze falls, defeated.

Truly one of the most memorable and wild battles to date.
