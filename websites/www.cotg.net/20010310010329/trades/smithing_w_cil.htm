 OK... all I can recommend is what NOT to do. I got to Master Smith by doing everything people later told me was dumb/way too expensive...
- Do NOT work on skewers until you have trivialed scalers and lanterns. Scalers are actually profitable on a success, selling back to the vendors. A failure will still give you a new item that you can sell for a partial recovery of cost. Lanterns are not profitable but are easier, however there are more items needed for them.
- Do NOT use armor for increasing your skill level. I have had many people tell me that armor actually has a penalty assessed against it in learning smithing. I have nothing to prove this but I do know my skill did not improve much when making armor but did when making other stuff.
- Do your smithing in Freeport - the stuff to make metal bits, and water are both available in North FP, and there are two forges there (there are also four other forges in FP, one in the Militia house, one at the Warrior guild out the back door, one in East FP at Ikthar's Armor, and one in the bad section of town, just beware Scraps, as the guards like him...). Metal bits are your life until you start making armor and weapons. Metal bits also enjoy failing even when they are 40 points past your trivial level so be prepared for that.
- Do NOT attempt smithing with both a WIS and INT below 80. Find items that will boost both of these. Go out of your way to do this. If you are tight on money concentrate on WIS items, if you are not go for INT items. STR also supposedly affects the success rate, boost that if possible, which leads to...
- Do NOT do smithing while overweight. Apparently this has an actual effect on the success rate. I don't know why, but I've seen this on a few web sites (which elude me now...)

Groflahs' Forge in North Freeport sells sharpening stones and small pieces of ore. Two small pieces of ore are used to make one metal bit.

Armor by Ikthar in East Freeport sells armor molds and sheets of metal. I don't remember her name, Istlyn or somesuch, but she is the vendor that has them.

Trader's Holiday in East FP - Vendor named Tislan sells most of the molds used to advance the skill, molds for things like spits, baking items, and, most importantly, scaler and skewer molds.

Blue building next to Trader's Holiday in East FP - Vendor in corner named Jillian Dayson sells the molds used to make weapons and containers. She also sells the books with recipies.

I think one of the armor recipies may be broken, I did not verify this tonight but I want to say one item has an incorrect listing for the number of sheets you use to make them. I'll look it up at some point soon.

Small tips... OK...
I don't know if sharpening stones are useful or not. I think I'd go with making metal bits as they can be used again to make other stuff and advance the skill further. Making metal bits... go to Groflahs', buy 80 small pieces of ore (they stack). go buy 80 waters. Go make metal bits. Take all the metal bits, and the 40+ waters you still have left, go to East FP and get as many scaler molds as you have metal bits. At least I *think* it's one to one on the metal bits (I'll look this up and update this again). Use the forge next to Ikthar's to make the scalers, on a success you get a container lid mold or some similar such, and a file. A failure just gives you the file (I don't know why, but hey, it's free!). In general, you want to stock up on items from North, make the items in North, then go to East. It saves you running around the city zoning excessively. I'd say if possible get 80 metal bits before heading to East FP, and bring water with you. I found that if you buy over and over from a vendor tha tthe vendor starts giving you a break on prices. It's a small cut but after a while it helps out a bit. If you do develop such a break, don't sell all of your items to that vendor; whie he will give you a better price, the next time you go buy stuff from him he will have taken back the price cuts. It's a balance thing I guess. (:
