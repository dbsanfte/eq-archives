Orioness' Fishing Guide

Fishing is the only "trade" that I am close to master at. I can't remember what my actual level is, but I do quite well at is. So here is my contribution.

As someone else said, buy 2 fishing poles, as you will get the occasional broken pole until you get your skill up. I haven't broken a pole in quite awhile, but I think I went through 3 to 4 broken ones. Poles cost a couple gold pieces. You should be able to buy them at any dock. In Butcherblock, the High Elf merchant close to the shore sells both pole and bait.

Early on, you will lose bait, but now that I am higher, I don't lose very often, and can count on catching something for each bit of bait.

*A money saving tip* Quite a few foragers will forage grubs and drop them. If you see something lying on the ground, with no one about, check it out - it might be droppped grubs. Before my foraging skill increased, that is mostly how I kept myself in grubs.

To keep my pole and grubs in, I have a 4 slot belt pouch. That gives me some place to keep the pole, a stack of grubs, and the products of labor. If you open the pouch and go full screen, you have some place to put your catch.


You will catch one of 4 things that I've seen so far:


- a fish

- fish scales

- a rusty dagger

- a sandal (looks like it's got algae on it - ewwww)



Each will consume one bit of bait when caught. I'm still half expecting to pull out an old tire someday

When you are starting out, sell the daggers and sandals back to the merchant for bait money. The fish you can either keep to eat, or sell. They bring a couple silver apiece, so you might be able to buy some food for cheaper, and make a profit by selling. The scales you can either sell or donate to a caster who has the Enduring Breath spell. I keep them, since I have the spell.

You have to be in just the right spot to fish - too far from the water and you will get a "trying to catch land-sharks, perhaps?" message. Too close, and you will get a "you must be standing on dry land to fish" message. Guess they've never seen the fishermen I grew up in Florida with I usually stand on the edge of a dock.

Remember to put your catch in the pouch before clicking the Fishing button again, as you can't fish with something in your hand. I usually practice my Sense Heading and Forage in conjunction with Fishing, and work out a decent clicking rhythm. A good time to fish is while waiting for the boat, or tell your friends to meet you at a dock next time your group up. You can make a small profit or feed yourself while waiting.

Believe it or not, I enjoy fishing. It seems like it would be boring, since you are just clicking a button. But it forces me to be still and watch the sunset for a bit, or watch the interesting conversations go by.

And 2 last things to remember:

1. Always remember to put your pole away when done. Nothing is worse than realizing you are whacking something blue to you with a bamboo pole!

2. Don't spill your beer while bringing in your line

Hope this helps someone out there.
