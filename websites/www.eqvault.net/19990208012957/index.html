Brad on Zone Density
Brad posted on alt.games.everquest newsgroup about the 2 uncompleted continents, and zone totals. Here's the story:

I heard that there might be two less Continents, but there are more total 'Adventure Zones' then they had when they were planning on the 5 Continents...

-Esoog

This is true. We are shipping with approximately 70 zones, as opposed to the (much) earlier plan of 40 or so. We decided to pack more zones into Odus, Antonica, and Faydwer, as opposed to spreading them out more.

So, bottom line, regardless of how many 'continents' we're shipping with, the game is a lot larger than we'd orginally planned.

-Brad McQuaid

Erudin Civil War Report
The Erudin Civil War took place today. The following is an account of it:
The Civil War that took place in Erudin today had a great turnout. At its peak over ninety people participated. The zone was filled to the brim of eager spectators and combatants. Due to the large nature of this event, the event became unorganized and the attack was delayed for a half-hour. People just stood around outside the city.

The war finally began with the crowd of spectators and warriors charging into the city and invading the Erudin City Library. The guards put up no resistance at all and the library quickly found itself under siege. People literally poured into the small door flooding all three stories of this massive building.

Spectators and warriors scrambled everywhere. The Library had three stories, each completely occupied. It became so crowded on the second and third floors that people began falling to their death to the ground below.

The guardians and keepers of the Library were quickly slain, as if lightning had struck them dead. Only one woman gave any resistance. Her name was Onissa. This fearless librarian killed more than any other NPC or PC. A few brave individuals gathered the strength up to try and best her in combat, but all were quickly slain. Eventually two fearless Barbarians engaged her in battle and defeated her.

Moments later the zone crashed, ending the war. The building had been completely conquered. The only NPCs left standing were the pets of Necromancers and other spellcasters.

    Thursday, February 4, 1999

    New Character Transfer Information
    The EverQuest patch server was updated with new information concerning transfering characters to Phase 4 servers. Here's the update:
    2/4/99 12:30pm
    -----------------
    In order to facilitate the testing of high level zones and items, the migration procedure into Phase 4 has been modified.
    - All characters from the 3.5 server will be automatically transferred to the Phase 4 Test server. No registration will be required.
    - Those players wishing to move all of their characters from the 3.0 Server to the Phase 4 Test server need to register using /feedback. Simply type the word RESTORE in your feedback while you are online with any of your characters, and ALL of your characters on the 3.0 server will be transferred to the Phase 4 Test server.
    - In order to use these characters in Phase Four you must set up your account using the exact account name that you have been using in Phase 3.0/3.5
    - All other servers in Phase 4 will start fresh. Initially, there will be no transfer of characters between servers in Phase 4.
    -Aurum @ 3:04 PM PT


    Brad On Name Changes
    Verant Interactive's Vice President, Brad McQuaid, made a post on the General Board clarifying the recent name change of RedEye Interactive. Here's the scoop:
