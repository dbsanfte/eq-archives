PC Fan has posted a new article entitled, "EverQuest: A Day In The Life Of A Ranger." As the name states, this article is about life as a Ranger. Here's a snippet:

Board Problems
Several people have reported problems with the message board loading up using Netscape, and I've managed to track down the problem. Because of the high traffic on the boards (900 posts a day on the general board and growing fast), we've been having to delete old posts every couple days. Unfortunately, Matt's WWWBoard script, which we're using a heavily modified version of, tends to corrupt HTML when doing this large of a purge. The end result is that the bottom HTML of our page, which closes all the table definitions, gets cut off, and Netscape apparently has trouble parsing this, although IE4 and IE5 beta manage to handle this just fine, since they seem to have a much better HTML parsing core. I have a solution that will fix this, but it's gonna require a complete board wipe for all boards and some HTML modification, so this might take a day or two to get done.
-Ilibard @ 8:52 AM PT

Concerning Release Date
Many people have pointed out that there is a major discrepancy on the release date for EverQuest, with the official EverQuest site still listing a January 26th date, while the 989 Studios site has it listed for March. At this time, 989 has no comment on this, but we'll bring you the official word on which is correct just as soon as we hear it.

I will say, however, based on my personal observations of the current state of the game, that I consider a January release to be unlikely. Too much zone tuning needs to be done, PvP isn't in place properly yet, the guild system isn't in at all, and quests need some more work. However, please keep in mind this is simply my own observations, and NOT to be taken as gospel on the matter.

This also means (if I'm correct) that phase 4 of the beta won't be happening as soon as people expect it to. It's looking like any sort of December start date is out of the question, so I'm guessing all you eager testers out there will just need to wait a bit longer. Hopefully we can do a bit to whet your appetite until then. :)

Level Advancement
This is taken from the alt.games.everquest feed, about people already being level 30 in beta.

Keep in mind, the open beta has been running since July. That's almost 5 months. It's quite likely the level 30 and up players have been around that long.

This is true. The highest level tester in the game (who naturally achieved that level) is level 35, and this person was in VERY early in the beta, and plays almost all day long. 6 or so months to level 35 is about right and all according to plan.

Spoilers, Policy, and Other Mumbo Jumbo
In the recent light of our release from the NDA, there has been a lot of discussion about our distinct "no spoiler" policy. We stick by that policy. The problem is that everyone has a different definition of a spoiler. In light of this, we have decided to clarify our policy.

We promise not EVER to post explicit secrets ANYWHERE on the site. By this we mean we will never post info on quests(where to find them, how to complete them), we will never post maps more elaborate than the ones on everquest.com, and we will never post steb by step directions on how to clear out certain dungeons, or how to find secret entrances. All that stuff takes a lot of the cooler mystery out of a game.

But since all people consider different things to be spoilers, we must make allowances for that. We promise that we will never post anything that we remotely believe could be considered a spoiler on the front page. However, the sub pages of our site will contain more info, and could be considered spoilers by some people. Therefore, beyond this front page, read at your own risk.

And finally, an apology. I posted some information here on the front page relating to my story, without realizing that it could be considered a spoiler. After it was brought to our attention, the post was deleted. This is exactly the type of post I promise will NOT come up in the future.

In the future, we hope to continue to provide the most thorough coverage of EverQuest without spoiling the game. On behalf of the EQVault staff, thanks for reading, and we hope to see you in Norrath!
