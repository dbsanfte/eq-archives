Expectations of Guild Members
by Rosha Cristi

As a former guild leader and a guild officer, I am going to give my opinion on what I expected from a guild member.  These are only my opinions and I cannot speak for the current guild leader or other officers.  I just wish to share my thoughts on the matter.

 First and foremost…I expected everyone to have fun. EverQuest is a game (granted, it is more than “just” a game, but it is a game nonetheless).  If you are NOT having fun, then you should log off!  If you continue not having fun, you should probably stop playing, as this is obviously not a game for you.  Likewise, if the guild is somehow making the game not fun, I expected to be notified.  Give me the chance to make it fun for you again.  Nothing irritated me more than to see people leave and be told there was something not right with the guild.  If something was causing you problems or distress, I expected to be informed!  You need to be as specific as possible because a blanket statement like “there are problems in the guild” does not do me any good at all.  That is like saying “we are having a party in Chicago…come join us” and leaving it at that.  Chicago is one big city so finding your party is going to be rather difficult!

 I believe a guild member should dedicate at the very least, 75% of his/her time to furthering the goals of the guild.  The other 25% can be spent doing whatever (I consider hunting for experience to be furthering guild goals by the way—levels are power).  When a guild event or raid is planned, I expected available members to lend their support.  When some spur of the moment raid pops up, I expect members to support the raid and do their utmost to make the raid happen.  I expected members to group with each other before choosing outsiders with whom to group.

 I expected members to put the good of the guild ahead of their own personal gain.  In a sense, making the guild stronger is making you stronger.  So what if you didn’t get item X…as long as someone else in the guild got item X, then it just increased your chances of the guild being able to obtain many more item X’s.

 I expected members to respect each other.  Think about what your gaming experience means to you and then transfer those feelings to your guild mate.  Do not do things to your guild mate that you would not like to have happen to you!  Do your best to answer questions your guild mates ask.  Everyone hates being ignored and the more people there are in a guild, the worse it feels asking some question and then being ignored.

 I expected members to act professionally.  They should know how to play their class and play it to the utmost of their abilities.  I don’t care if you have a personal disagreement with player X…you do your job and they will do their job and the entire guild reaps the benefit.  The last things the guild needs are members who refuse to attend events or join groups with some other member.  That WEAKENS the guild and that person should probably examine their expectations of what they want from a guild.

 I expected members to respect the decisions put forth by the guild leadership. This does not mean everyone must agree with the decisions or that they cannot voice their opinions about the decisions…by respect, I mean they realize the leadership is trying hard to make fair and objective decisions to further the guild as a whole.  If you disagree with a decision, please explain why in a rational manner.  Keep in mind, there may be things factored in the decision that YOU are unaware of at the time.  Likewise, by explaining in a rational manner, perhaps you will bring something to light that the leadership did not know about at the time. 

 I expected members to realize the guild leader and the guild officers are players as well.  We are also human beings…meaning we are not perfect!  There is no pay for being the guild leader or an officer.  There is certainly no glory.  The only gratification is knowing you are helping others accomplish things in the game that would be impossible to accomplish alone.  I have turned down loot or asked to be placed lower on a priority list in an effort to avoid any perception of favoritism or elitism.  I know many other officers who also do the same.  You practically have to force them to accept an item. 

 Wow, that looks like a lot of expectations for just being in a guild!  Why would anyone want to be in an organization that expects so much from him or her? It is simple.  Power.  The power to see areas of the game that one could not see alone.  The power to obtain items that are impossible to obtain solo (or even with a small group).  The camaraderie of being in an organization. 

 A Guild should give 100% back to its members.  Everything a guild does should be to further the progress of its members in some form or fashion.  A guild should be responsive to its membership and be willing to adapt, as the membership needs change.
