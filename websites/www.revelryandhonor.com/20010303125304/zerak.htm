I was born walking on both feet, perhaps quite a feet for the lesser races, you know, the ones strewn about hugging grass blades everywhere you travel.  I decided to try these feet out.  It took some time to realize that looking over balcony walls was forever going to be some trouble.  But as you all know, we stubborn Dwarves, especially with out intelligence attributes, will keep trying.  I never did find mom and pop...must have been dropped off in the hands of the Paladin class due to my extraordinary wisdom and physical abilities.  I gather it was pop who instructed me with his note to talk with the General, and thus I did.  Being alive 5 hours I became hungry and lost in Kaladim.  Fortunately I learned to kill for food, and later for better equipment.  Alas, I have been given various duties to clear out smaller evils just to have the permission to excel at doing bigger and better.  Before long I traveled far and wide with the advice of various Paladin Masters, learning new skills and places along the way.
 
    From time to time I venture back to Mistmoore Castle in search of my level 5 Paladin body, but with regret it never turns up.  As you know, we Dwarves will keep looking.  Soon I realized that I had a keen ability to take some serious damage that would cause several of the lesser races to collapse to their deaths...look around you.
 
    It became my second nature to kill, but did not offer much of a social life.   I decided that joining various groups and factions was much more pleasant, not that a dwarf need the help of course.  Soon I received an invitation along with a simple quest to join a guild (from a ranger of all things, go figure).  Saving the life of a near death friend was common practice, and thus I was accepted.  I found myself a member of a larger effort to rid the lands of Norrath of evil, not that the Paladin Master didn't already have enough for me to do.
 
    As I became more aware of the evils present in Norrath I was fortunate to have the master make it easier for me to assist them.  Of course this meant more bodies of the lesser evil races strewn all over.  I had no problem with it though.  I have come across many friends doing the same so we go at it together...simply for the conversation you know.  I continue to seek others willing to follow the same path.  The more the merrier.  Brell Serilis only know what is in store for me next.  But I have the faith of a paladin that the master will continue to ease those burdens with new skills, not that mom and pop would ever.
 
Your faithful paladin,
 
Zerak Travail of Prexus and Revelry and Honor.
