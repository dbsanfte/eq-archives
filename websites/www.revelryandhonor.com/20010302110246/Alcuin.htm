A Day in the Life of Alcuin Wizziefingers

The young Wizard raised his hands in the air and quietly muttered the words of power, "Madavenpad, Lenavenpad, Astelethon!" A crackle of energy began to form in his hands, and blue wisps flowed angrily up and down his arms. Alcuin grinned to himself and turned towards the leering Mummy. He threw his arms forward, sending the storm of energy hurtling towards the Mummy. Mid-flight, the blue wisps faded and died, leaving nothing in their wake except a quiet, fizzling sound.

"Another Fizzle!" Alcuin shouted, "DAMMIT!"

"I'll take care of it, Alcuin. Just meditate for a bit" said Kambei as she slashed the mummy with her long sword. "Try to relax", she added under her breath.

"Yes, we have it, young one", said the grizzled old Paladin, Troval Nobleknight. "Just keep back."

"The hell with that!" said Alcuin. He threw his spell book on the ground, pulled his dagger from his belt and waded into battle. The Mummy, sensing a vulnerable opponent, immediately turned its attention to the unprotected Wizard. It grasped Alcuin by the throat with one hand and slashed him right down the middle with its rusty short sword. Alcuin yelped in pain--two inches to the left and the would would have been fatal. Alcuin flailed away ineffectually with his small dagger.

Kambei struggled to get in a good swing with her sword, but with Alcuin between her and the mummy, she wasn't able to get into position to fight. Troval glanced at Kambei and said with his eyes, "Did we have to bring him, again?"

The Mummy attacked Alcuin again, this time with a deadly disease-touch. Alcuin's face went a deathly pale and the strength dropped out of his arms. The Mummy dropped him to the ground in a heap, and turned its attention back to Kambei.

"Heal me, please!" moaned Alcuin from the ground.

Reluctantly, Troval sheathed his sword and pulled out his spell book, leaving Kambei to dispatch the Mummy on her own. A few words of healing, and Alcuin was cured of the Mummy's disease and his wound was remarkably healed.

Kambei dispatched the Mummy with no further incident. Alcuin got up, brushed himself off, and did his best to look stoic. "That went well. Good job, guys!" He immediately began rummaging through the Mummy's corpse. "Oooh, a jade" he said, dropping the green stone in his pocket. "Did either of you need this sword?" he asked, indicating the rusty short sword lying next to the Mummy.

Troval turned to Kambei, "Could you talk to him?"

Kambei sighed, "I've tried. Tunare knows I've tried."
