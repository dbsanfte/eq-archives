Wizard Guide

The Magician

So...you've decided you want to be a Magician, eh?

The elder Dark Elf eyes you, arching one eyebrow as he sizes you up.

"I suppose that you will serve well as an apprentice. Allow me to tell you about our profession."

The magician class is a versatile class. With the ability to go for long stretches without going "home" for supplies due to the summoning of both food, drink, containers, and bandages...the ability to summon weapons, many of them enchanted in some fashion....the ability to summon stones that give varying levels of night vision....the ability to summon elementals from the outer planes to do their bidding...the ability to cause a world of pain to monsters via our direct damage spells....and the ability to debuff an opponent with the Malise line...Magicians are, in a word, utilitarian, though not so much as a shaman or druid.

All Magician Direct Damage spells are either Magic based (Shock of Blades/Spikes/Swords/Steel; Elemental Maelstrom, etc) or Fire based (Blaze, Bolt of Fire/Flame/Lava, Char, etc). You will find that the Fire based damage is considerably more powerful, but will be partially resisted more often than the Shock lines. At level 24, however, you are given the Malise spell, which lowers the targets Magic, Fire, Cold and Poison resistances by 20 each. This serves as an advantage for you, your pet, and your groupmates. You get upgrades to Malise at 44 (Malisement, removing 40 to the same resistances), 51 (Malosi, removing 60 from the same), 57 (Malosini, which does the same as Malosi, only casts faster and has no delay to recast), and 60 (Malo, only takes 30 points, but is unresistable...is usually followed by Malosini). I would suggest keeping both a Fire based DD spell and a Magic based DD spell memorized, depending on your situation. Your current Malise spell should always be memorized as well.

Pets...Magicians get 4 (5?) varieties of pets... Earth, Air, Water, Fire, and the Monster Summoning spells. Monster Summoning is currently most useful as a "toy" spell, since none of the monsters that you summon are as strong as your elemental pets. The Water and Air pets are my current favorites, although some Mages rave about the fire pets as well. Your Earth pet has the most hit points, and will consistently do more damage than another pet of the same level, and also roots your enemy. Your Water pet hits just slightly under the Earth pet, has a few less hit points, but has better Armor Class (AC), and casts an Ice based DD spell. Your water pet is also immune to poison and disease, and has a much quicker regeneration rate. Also, after level 50, your water pets have the ability to backstab your foe, if the pet is behind him. Your Air pets hit slightly less hard than the water pet, have a much higher AC, fewer hit points, and casts a stun spell every so often. Air pets also cast invisibility on themselves when not fighting. Fire pets have the least HP of any pet, hit just as hard as air pets, have the 2nd highest AC (only 2nd to air), and cast a damage shield on themselves that does more damage than the shield you can cast on them. You'll find that each pet has it's use, depending on your situation. For soloing, I suggest Earth or Fire, but Water for enemies that either poison (spiders) or disease (undead). Be sure to keep your pet buff (Burnout I/II/III/IV) on them at all times, as this makes them attack faster. Also, starting at level 24, your pets dual wield, but require weapons. Be sure to summon them daggers to unlock this ability. Only summon them Swords of Runes if you are fighting summoned beasties (imps, elementals). At level 39, you no longer need to summon them weapons, as they will dual wield on their own.

As for your summons...

Bandages are excellent for your lower levels. Healing yourself up to 50% of your total hit points can be a life saving ability, and doing it free of charge is even better. You will make friends with monks, rogues, and warriors, as they have no innate way to heal themselves (at least reliably). At higher levels, you will be using your bind wound skills to be a "combat medic" in certain situations, binding your allies' wounds as they fight. You can also keep Shamen alive as they use their "Cannibalize" spell to turn hit points into mana.

Rings of Levitation (Level 39) are invaluable for traveling. 4 AC and 2 charges of Levitate per ring, summon yourself a few when you log on.

Coldstones (29) and Heatstones (12) are invaluable if you are a Human or Erudite. Coldstones are also valuable for every magician that is not a Dark Elf, since they have innate Ultravision. You will find that almost everyone asks for Coldstones at higher levels, and you can make a night-blind newbie's day by giving them a heatstone as you pass through town.

Modulation Rods (44) convert 225 HP into 150 mana. You will find yourself summoning ModRods for clerics when you raid, so that they can heal more often.

I will not get into the higher level summon spells here. I will let you find out their uses on your own.

Vanamar Dz`Baernon, 53rd Dark Elf Elementalist, Lanys T`Vyl Server
