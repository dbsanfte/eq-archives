Acquiring Spells:

Certain classes, like Paladin's ability to Lay on Hand's or Shadowknights ability to Harm Touch, have one or more spell-like abilities.  These are skill that they gain with levels and not spells they have to locate on their own.  These skills do not require spell scrolls, scribing or meditating.  You can use them as often as the ability permits.  For most spells, however, you must obtain a spell scroll, record it in your spellbook and memorize it before you can cast it.

You can buys some spell scrolls from magic vendors, or if you have the ability to do research (such as Wizards and Enchanters) you can use items that drop in the game to combine and create some of your spell scrolls.  Most of the spells for players under the level of 50 are available on magic vendors (usually located in your class guild hall).  But once you reach higher levels, spell scrolls become more difficult to find. 

Spells that are not found on magic vendors, usually can be found on NPC's.  The scrolls can vary from zone to zone (expansion to expansion: Kunark mobs drop Kunark expansion spells, etc) and are usually easy to find.  Some are more rare and drop off a limited number of high level NPC's (such as Malo, Primal Avatar) and still others are no-drop and are considered "Ancient", which usually drop off extreemly difficult NPC's.

There are also other types of spells that you can use as Quest items.  These spells are listed by your Kunark class guild master if you locate them and say, "hail" and follow the [quest] key words.  If you locate one of these spells and  you already have it recorded, you can turn it in for a random reward of another spell that was listed by your class guild master.  In the Planes of Power zones, there are scribes that you can loot off certain NPC's that can be turned into any class guild master in the Planes of Knowledge for a random Planes of Power level (60+ level) spell.  You can turn this into any class guild master and he will give you a random spell that represents the class he/she belongs to.

Spell Types:

Offensive: Spells that are, or add punch to, combat attacks.  Examples are Shock of Frost, Burst of Flame and Enfeeblement.

Defensive:  Spells that boost armor ratings or give added protection or advantage during battle.  Examples are Courage, Skin Like Wood and Invisibility to Undead.

Healing:  Spells that restore health and stamina, like Minor Healing, Cure Disease, Endure Poison and Mend Bones.

Utility:  Spells that are neither offensive nor defensive.  Examples are Summon Food or B ind Affinity.  Neither of these spells is harmful or helpful in a combat situation but they have a specific purpose and provide some benefit, hence are utilities.

 

Spell Gem Colors:

Once you memorize a spell, the spell gem will be outlined or highlighted by a color.  That color indicates something about the spell that is memorized in the slot.

Yellow: Personal, affects the caster only.

Red: Single Target, affects a single target only.

Blue: Point Blank Area of Effect, spell affects an area centered on the caster but the caster will remain unaffected.

Green: Area of Effect, targets in an area centered on the target.

Orange: Area of Effect Rain, which means the spell affects the area around the target, usually in a continuing fashion.

Purple: Group, spell will hit the entire group of players.
