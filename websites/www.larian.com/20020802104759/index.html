Divinity Hands-On Preview at RPGVault on 02/08/2002 by lynn

RPGVault has posted up a Divine Divinity Hands-On Preview. The article describes their impressions of the recent press build, the backstory, gameplay, NPC and item interaction, combat, user interface, graphics, and more.

Here's a snippet:
NPC interaction is more developed than most action-oriented RPGs. Each one you meet seems to belong to a faction and has a disposition intimately related to your actions. These disposition ratings range from 0 (neutral), to 100 (admiring) and negative numbers (hostile). Each NPC may treat you differently according to your race, your actions and quests you have performed for them. Most start neutral and most monsters you meet will be as well. However, there are the exceptions and these will depend on your travels.

And their conclusion:
With close to 100 diverse skills for 6 distinct character types, thousands of monsters and hundreds of NPCs over some 20000 screens of real estate in a fully fleshed out fantasy world, Larian Studios' RPG is looking to make a big splash sometime soon in the next few months.

Divinity Preview at Gamespot.com on 02/08/2002 by lynn

Gamespot has posted up a preview of Divine Divinity (available in the shops in Germany today!) along with a couple of new screenshots.

Here's a snippet:
As we've seen, Divine Divinity has a much more involving set of quests that go beyond "kill this monster and/or bring back this item." In order to accomplish the game's hundreds of different quests, many of which are completely optional, you'll need to speak with lots of other characters, whose opinion of you depends on your actions and how you speak to them. The game will also have a great deal of full audio speech for most of the game's important characters--the spoken dialogue does a good job of highlighting the text dialogue that will appear onscreen, much like in Baldur's Gate II--though unlike the Baldur's Gate games, Divine Divinity will deliver all its characters' dialogues in full speech, rather than just a short snippet.

Judging by the other games that have been released already, you could say that 2002 has been the year of the epic role-playing game. And if Divine Divinity's great variety of character skills, items, magic spells, and quests is any indication, then Larian Studios' upcoming game is all set to follow suit.

Free e-book of the Divinity background story released on 02/07/2002 by lynn

Press release CDV Software Entertainment UK:

Whilst Divine Divinity – the hugely anticipated PC CD-ROM role-playing game from CDV Software Entertainment UK – is not due for release until September 2002, those desperate to immerse themselves in its detailed world and rich background story can do so immediately thanks to a new e-book which has just been released worldwide.

The Divine Divinity e-book – available at Eurocool.com – contains three chapters from a specially commissioned novella which chronicles the origins of the Lord of Chaos. It features the final communication from Zenfar Blutsporn, Chief Archivist of the Black Circle and last living member of the Legion of the Damned, whose reign of terror will dominate the game.

The e-book includes a host of characters prominent within the Divine Divinity World, making it an invaluable resource for those keen to tackle the full quest. Subtle hints and clues are hidden within its virtual pages, offering additional tips to those sharp enough to spot them.

“The feedback we’ve been getting from those that played the demo has been really encouraging, with consumers voting it one of their most anticipated releases of the year in its genre,” says Terry Malham, President, CDV Software Entertainment UK. “The world of Divine Divinity is extremely detailed, and we wanted to offer all players the chance to really immerse themselves in it. Whilst it’s not essential to read the entire 9000-word novella in order to complete the game, those that do will not only be treated to some of the finest fantasy writing around.”

Divine Divinity will be published by CDV Software Entertainment UK in September 2002, price £34.99

  • Character development system is highly open-ended
  • Player can choose to progress in either an action-oriented style or through the detailed quests and sub-quests
  • Huge gaming world of more than 20,000 screens
  • Screen resolutions of up to 1024x768
  • Unprecedented levels of graphic detail
  • Dynamic story telling system
  • Pick up and play style interface

** Demo Update **

It’s come to our attention that some people have problems starting up the demo of Divine Divinity after they’ve installed it. This has been isolated as a bug with the installer. To solve it, download the file divdf1.zip and unzip the executable that is in the zip file to the folder in which you installed the demo of Divine Divinity. Next run the executable instead of going through the menu, and the startup problem should be solved.
We apologize for the inconvenience.
