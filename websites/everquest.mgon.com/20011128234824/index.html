FGN Calls it Quits
Reported by: Mike   1 Comments | Add  

Joining a growing list of websites to fall victim to an online "recession", FGN today announced it will be dissolving. FGN has long been part of the industry and we are saddened to see them go.

"The site was an independent affiliate of IGN, and was published by UK company Interactive News Central. INC said the site had ceased to turn a profit, adding that no upturn appeared likely. The company continues to operate, supplying content to companies in Europe and the US."

"[FGN] was launched to inform people who love games, in easy-to-read chunks, what had happened in this market in the previous 24 hours. I think we did okay and I hope this site will fall into that 'Category of the Gone' that inspires warm recollection." says Colin Campbell, FGN founder.

    Eidos to publish Salt Lake 2002
    Reported by: Rogey   0 Comments | Add  

    Come next January you’ll be able to skate, ski and generally play in the snow and ice as the official Salt Lake City Winter Olympics game comes to the PC. The game is being developed by UK mob Attention to Detail and will be published by Eidos. In Salt Lake 2002 you’ll be able to compete in six different events, including men's snowboard giant slalom, alpine skiing downhill, ladies' alpine skiing slalom, men's two-man bobsleigh, women's freestyle skiing aerials, and men's ski jumping K120 individual.
