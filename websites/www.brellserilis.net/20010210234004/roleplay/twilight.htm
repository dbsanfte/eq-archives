The Walker of the Violet Twilight

Tell me your stories, barbarian -- you moved me somewhat, with your tales of drunken exhiliration in the face of peril. I find the lust for conquest equivalent to a lust for death: an emotion I find rather satisfying.

I have a tale or two of my own, since we sit here together, with this fire cooking morsels of rabbit meat that serve to nourish your still-living body. I am nourished by the very vitality that exudes from you. Please. Eat.

You follow the Tribunal, and I respect the heavy hand of Justise. Your gods have far more merit than that petulant monkey who seethes in his iron city of Hate. Surprised that I hold no love for the Father? I serve Nature: why would I?

Let me tell you of deeds performed behind the pallid walls of Felwithe, my hand gently soothing the forehead of a young priestess of Tunare while she sleeps in a cramped chamber in the Mother's Temple. My right hand, flushed crimson and burning upon her soft ivory forehead, her sun-spun hair, quieting her uneasy sleep with a haze of benighted dreams which both grant succour from a dreary life spent reading the Mother's tripe. I show her dreams of an end to her stagnating youth -- dreams that only serve to sedate her as they become a reality. She dies old, though not yet seven-teen winters have passed her by.

Let me show you nights in the stinking fens of Innothule, where a Knight of Innoruuk hunts down elvish paladins who seek to end his repulsive race. My right hand hidden in a shroud of shadows, I descend upon him in the moors while behind me, obscured by the rotting loam, beings that have turned away from the dawn, serve the twilight -- serve me -- by apprehending him, rendering him prone before me. He is of my race, and fiercely devoted to the Father. His hands glow like mine, sometimes, when the rage besets him and his god replies in raucous song to its fervour. They glow so now, and his touch burns the shadows from me through the intervention of Innoruuk, his liege. I brush my fingers against his razored features, and I smile softly. My shadows become manifest and are fed by his life, his faith and the brazen youth which drove them both. He staggers from the grip of my sevant's hands and falls to the peat, where I leave him. I say let the Paladins do their work in Innothule: it is the same as mine.

I serve no god, my friend. I hope to be as impartial as my master, to lay my hands randomly upon Norrath's creatures as he does, and without remorse.

Sometmes I find it difficult: after all, I am still mortal. I often find comfort in the company of those who I must inevitably touch: but it is most bittersweet.

My dear Ulver! Are you cold? You shiver so...

Your turn. Tell me a story. Perhaps that will warm your highland blood.

 

-- Story originally posted on Brell Serilis.net Roleplaying Forum by Humwawa.
