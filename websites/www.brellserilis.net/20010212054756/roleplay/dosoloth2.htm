The Story of Dosoloth (Part 2)

I could feel my heart's decay there in the blackness. I could feel it dying, what little part of it which had been revived from its umbral repose flaring as a shooting star and failing, failing into the ether, rotten and cold.

My fist tightened over the hilt of my blood red blade. My teeth ground together, my jaw tightened. My muscles tensed under the iron shell of my armour, prepared to strike the false creature down there in the solace of her secret lair.

Yet I could not bring my spirit to comply to my body's will.

I walked away.

In the deep woodlands of the Faydark I shivered under the wrath of the Father. His divine will assaulted my body in wave after wave of rile which sent my spirit screaming and tearing at the shackles that was my body. My soul begged for release from his rage. I could feel his shadowed visage floating before me, his cold breath tearing at me with hissing words describing how I would join the other heretics of his faith in a secret place upon his home plane. And I heard them there, as I quaked before the wrath of my god: their lamentations spoke of an eternity of desolation which no mortal could ever know. The scorn banshees sang abominable songs for me that night. I could bear it no longer. I took up my sword.

I could feel her heart beating. I followed its sweet ringing to the Koada`dal City, where I called forth my Father's will. I covered myself in inky darkness and entered the stronghold unseen, unknown, past shining steel-clad fairskin men who protected Tunare's City.

The aura of Tunare was smothering - I could hardly breathe through the scent of her, but still I tracked my lover's heart. I could feel it's rhythm slowing in slumber. Into the Temple of Tunare I followed her. Behind a door of purest oak I could hear her breathing softly, I could taste the smell of her jasmine-scented hair. I drew my blade slowly, savoring the hushed venomous hiss of its expanse leaving the scabbard. My gauntleted hands fell quietly to the latch.

I stopped once more. My heart rebelled against the shadow of the Father which followed me closely at my shoulder. His will and my love for this Koada'dal temptress clashed there at the threshhold to her chamber. And then he came.

There was another presence in her private chambers behind the oaken door. It tasted like the Father Himself somehow -- silent, covered in darkness. I felt countless deaths tied to this one being, but unlike my Lord its touch was not a hateful one. It was duty for this one - he was doing his duty even now.

I shook myself from the thrall of Innoruuk. This being was here to take her life! Soft whispers emanated from the room. I attempted to open the door. A flash of energy tore through me. I felt my Sularyn's heart burst with light, and fade away softly into the night. I had no need to perform the Prince of Hate's will that night: another had already.

I opened the door to see her young body bone white and cold in the scented spaces of her private room. Her window was open, the curtains lashing the night air. It had already left here, but his essence still pervaded my Sularyn's ashen body. Even as Koada'Dal, Sularyn sent my heart reeling in my chest. I could have loved her.

A voice spoke to me then. And I will not forget it. "See now how your failure to act has cost you your mate's life. You are my Knight, Dosoloth: your choice of servant holds no interest for me and had you complied to my wishes, she would serve you now in death. But your disobedience has lost you a prize most fair, and to the touch of a Heretic from the Lodge of the Dead. I will not forgive you this, child, not til the stars burn cold. Bring me the head of this Heretic and soothe your loss, and my displeasure. Go."

I now hunt him, I have tracked him here to these woods of Kithicor, half a world away. For the loss of my lover, and for the pleasure of my lord I will obey. I shall draw my sword across his throat, and cloud his eyes with death. Such is the Father's will.
