New Beginnings in an Endless Cycle (1/2)

Kaiyn Darious stood quietly behind Lokus, his ever vigilant eyes scanning the upper balconies of the temple of Solusek Ro, looking for the slightest hint of something out of the ordinary.

The words of initiation being pronounced to the new members were no more than a faint buzz in his ears, as were the antics of some of the younger women within the guild, joking back and forth like so many adolescents at play. Momentarily he wondered what he was doing there, how he had ended up as a body guard to the ruling family of a prestigious guild.

There was little profit to be gained from such things; certainly the risks by far outweighed the advantages. He was a spy, a soldier at best and a year before he would have laughed at himself for being in such a position. And yet he found himself filled with emotions alien to him. A strange feeling of affection for the unlikely gathering of people that stood before him. But such feelings would only bring him disharmony, and so he dutifully pushed them to a deep corner of his mind where they would remain until called upon.

Looking around at the assembled members of Evoluxa, he knew beyond any doubt that soon they would be plunged into war. He had experienced such situations before, a firm code of moral beliefs that threatened and often insulted those around it. Certainly such an organization would not remain unmolested for long. Softly he chuckled to himself, drawing a few looks from those around him. Only a short time ago he had infiltrated and eventually eliminated just such an organization, under the contract of the so called loving followers of Tunare.

He could still hear the helpless cries of the young mother as his men allowed her the final mercy, could still see the defeated and broken looks upon her father's and husband's faces as he himself decapitated the only son, a boy of twelve. Let none live, that was what he had been told, and he had been efficient in the completion of his task. There was no love lost between the followers of Tunare and the dark elven worshippers of Innoruk. They had wanted this family's destruction to set an example to all those who would dare attempt to take their unholy faiths beyond the protection of their own forest. They had paid him well indeed.

The retributive strike of the family's guild was disorganized without their leader and was easily countered. Soon every last member had been hunted down and placed securely within magical cages, to be transported back to the city of Felwithe where they would eventually be tried and convicted of crimes they had most likely not committed. None of these things mattered to Kaiyn. He was a soldier and he did his job well. His normal indifference and strict adherence to duty along with the pursuit of wealth had changed when he encountered Lokus and Evoluxa. He could relate to the man. Lokus's doctrine of hate and his excellent sense of tact and diplomacy attracted Kaiyn almost immediately. Perhaps he had found a cause worthy of defending.

He had yet to see. For the time being however he would complete his assigned duties as high protector of the Z'Asrithe family, even to the point of giving his own life to save theirs. After all, death was not such an unwelcome concept that he would have cause to fear it. The barest traces of a smile shadowed the corners of his mouth as the meeting was called to a close. Bowing formally to the new members, he welcomed them to the order of Evoluxa, along with the cheers and hollars of some of the more enthusiastic members. He had a few things of importance to do yet that evening, amongst those meeting with the son of a former friend, Xaviel Baen'blade.

Turning to Lokus he saluted him crisply, asked his permission to leave, and strode out of the dimly lit temple, emerging once more into the ash choked air of the Lavastorm Mountains....
