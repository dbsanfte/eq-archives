Tales of Aeani Fairblade (Chap 2)

The city of Qeynos, home of Paladins, Clerics, Monks, and Warriors, and it is one of the Human’s oldest cities founded by the infamous Antonius Bayle. It is here that Aeani considers a home and has been for as long as she can remember, but she cannot stop thinking of other places she can be at and see. Today, she continues her training as a Paladin, forging her loyalty to Rodcet Nife, city of Qeynos, and Antonius Bayle.

“Fairblade, you’re late again,” Camlend Serbold said. “Please, try not to disappoint me. You have been this temple’s best student but recently hesitating to show up on time. Now go grab yourself a sword and report to Astaed Wemor to continue with your training.” Aeani bows to him and walks over to grab a rusty sword from the small pile of weapons near the shop. There are other people training with Astaed, and he begins to explain to them the basics of melee combat. Aeani returns to them with the blade resting on her right shoulder.

“Gah-hahaha! These villans will be no match for me! Evil skeletons, fear my wrath,” one of the new Paladins said while swing his sword around. Aeani shakes her head while looking at the ground smiling. He holds out his hand towards her and says, “Hail, my name is Daelan, Daelan Darksky, but you can call me Dae.” Aeani just looks at him.

“Everyone, she has finally decided to join us. Let’s continue with our training!” Astaed glares at Aeani and then starts to explain about combat. Daelan still has his hand held out towards her, but Aeani simply lowers her head and rests her eyes. He tucks his hand away and turns his attention to Astaed Wemor.

Aeani begins to remember the dream she had this morning, “A land surrounded by lava and scorched hills. It has to be the Lavastorm Mountains I’ve read about, but I don’t know why I would want be there. Am I destined to perish by the blade of a Dark El…”

“Fairblade! Is there something troubling you that’s more important than training?” Astaed said while pointing his sword at her. Aeani looks at him. He reads her answer within her eyes, nods, and continues his explanation of basic combat. “Now that we’re all familiar of how to carry a sword, everyone grab yourself a partner and practice swinging your swords at each other. If you get injured, then come get me or a Cleric immediately.”

Aeani faces Daelan and holds out her hand, “Aeani Fairblade.”

Daelan looks at her hand and then at her eyes. He gladly holds her forearm while she holds his. “Well met, Aeani. Shall we get started?” Aeani nods, lets go of his arm, and holds out her sword, standing ready.

After what seems to only have been a few hours of practice and training, the sun begins to set. Everyone tosses their swords back onto the pile of weapons and exits the temple. While Aeani starts leaving the Temple of Life, Camlend moves infront of her to make her stop.

“Aeani, Astaed told me of your actions earlier today which revealed to me that you are having a problem. You were very distracted today, and I’m guessing it’s the reason why you’ve been late these past mornings. Please, tell me what’s troubling you.” Camlend looks down at Aeani, but she turns to her side and stares ahead of her, not at him.

She turns her head to glance up to Camlend’s eyes and says, “Have you ever had a glimpse of a fate that clearly reveals how you will perish?” Aeani looks ahead of her again. “I have.” He blinks in disbelief, and Aeani walks out into the hallway, leaving the temple.

At the end of the hallway stands Daelan, waiting for Aeani. Aeani pauses to look at him, but she continues to walk out the hallway. Daelan tries to grab her attention, “Hey, you were pretty good with that sword today!” Aeani keeps walking towards the north gate with Daelan following behind her. “Not too bad for a ‘fair blade.’”

Aeani stops and turns her head to look over her shoulder at Daelan, who’s still behind her, “What’s that supposed to mean?”

“Eh? Oh, I really didn’t mean anything by it. I’m just joking around! Lighten up, friend!” He chuckles to himself, but Aeani continues walking again. “Wait wait! I hope I didn’t upset you or anything. I’m just trying to cheer you up. How about I buy you a drink over at Crow’s tavern?”

Aeani smiles and says, “Daelan, I’m just going to relax for the night. Thanks for the offer and kindness, though. I’ll hold you up to that next time, hey?”

“Okay! I look forward to it, Aeani! Until next time!” He runs ahead of her to turn around to wave (almost tripping himself to the ground), then he continues running ahead of her and into the tavern.

Aeani visits a pool next to Crow’s Tavern that she likes to spend time next to for reading several books about other cities and continents. She hasn’t realized that Camlend has always noticed her reading here. For the rest of her night, she reads a book about Faydwer and wades her feet in the pool.

The next morning, she wakes up early and visits the Temple of Life, carrying the book she hasn’t finish reading yet. Camlend, Astaed, and the others are not here yet, so she sits next to the Pool of Jahnda and relaxes her feet in the water. She opens her book, lies down on her back, and begins to read. Aeani moves her book from her eyes and looks up to see Camlend standing next to her. She quickly stands up and puts away the book into her bag. Camlend remains standing while holding his hands behind his back.

“Sir Serbold, I did not know I was causing trouble with the Koalindl. I love the feeling of them swimming between my fight while I read.” Aeani lowers her head and closes her eyes, but Camlend places his left hand on her shoulder. “Aeani,” Camlend begins to say, “a thousand travel books do not make up for one real journey.” Aeani looks up and stares at him. Camlend smiles and hands her a newly crafted Longsword and kite shield. She looks at her new sword and shield in awe and shock. “They’re from Astaed Wemor, and he gives you his wishes and hopes to see you again. We all do.”

Aeani gives him a hug with tears in her eyes. Without any more words exchanged, she looks up to him, smiles, bows, and starts to walk away, but she turns around to look back at Camlend Serbold. He only smiles and nods at her. She nods back and leaves the temple. Running past the gate and out into the open field away from Qeynos, Aeani turns around and looks at the city walls.

“I will return to see this city again.”
