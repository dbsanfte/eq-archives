Featured Guild
"The Burning Shield"

A new feature to BrellSerilis.net, the Featured Guild section gives website viewers a closer look into the guilds on our server. This week, we sat down with Nedra Nightblade from the Burning Shield, and asked her a few questions about the guild, their philosphy and organization.

Q: BS.Net
Tell me a little about yourself, how long you have been involved in Everquest and what kind of character do you play?

A: Nedra
I have played Everquest since the release of the product. I play both a Rogue and a Cleric.

Q: BS.Net
What was your motivation to create a guild? What types of things do you look for in guilds in general (as a player) and now as a guildmaster?

A: Nedra
The concept of a group of players who help each other and be able to communicate with each other freely in the game excited me. I had been in two other guilds on the server that I originally started playing, and found that I really enjoyed that aspect of guilds. The inspiration came from the pitfalls I saw in those two guilds, I felt if done differently things could have been better. The things I did not like are the things I tried to change. I do not feel that a guild can consist of people with either just common interest or goals, I think this is only part of what is needed. The other aspect that is needed is friendship and respect, for without these two items I think that you cannot achieve harmony in a guild. I also felt that a HUGE guild could never have these qualities. I look for people I can trust and that I am friends with or feel I can become friends with, after having lead a guild for the past few months I still think that is what I look for. Friendship gets you through the times when you do not see eye to eye on issues and gets you over the hump so to say. Another important thing for me is beliefs, not in a broad aspect, but a common belief in what is right and what is wrong.

Q: BS.Net
Where did the name "The Burning Shield" originate?

A: Nedra
A very good friend of mine suggested it. We were trying to come up with a name and it sounded good. I know to some that is not a very good way to come up with a guild name but it did not matter to me what the name was, rather what the guild stood for and what the guild does.

Q: BS.Net
How many members are there in The Burning Shield. How many might you be looking to add to grow the guild to your ideal size? What are the approximent level ranges to your guild?

A: Nedra
There are about 15 to 20 members at the present time. I have never taken an actual count. We range in levels from 5 to 50.

Q: BS.Net
What is the political and organizational structure of your guild? Who are its officers and what do they do?

A: Nedra
I did not form this guild based on structure, it is a loose group of friends and always will be. We have 5 officers but to be quite honest most the members do not know who they are. We only have 2 rules and we all follow them so no real need has arisen to require an officer to do anything in particular.

Q: BS.Net
How often do you plan guild events? What kind of events are planned?

A: Nedra
We plan them less frequently than even I would like in part to a lack of a means to announce them. Recently we have started a message board and are currently working on a web site to allow us to plan things. Word of mouth is not a good means of communication so once a month is how often we have planned events. Currently we are planning a joint event with another guild to have some general fun in Everfrost.

Q: BS.Net
What is the theme of The Burning Shield?

A: Nedra
The theme of The Burning Shield is that good should be promoted in the lands of Norrath and that all players should help one another.

Q: BS.Net
What are the joining requirements of your guild (if any) and who would a potential member get in touch with if they wanted to join?

A: Nedra
Requirements for joining the guild are that you be of a "Good" race and a "Good" class, for example no necros or Dark Elves. A potential member would contact Taeolin, Mcbash, Etherial, or Nedra.

Q: BS.Net
If you were to sum up your guild in one catchy, concise "motto" what would it be?

A: Nedra
I cannot for the life of me come up with a motto to something that is as much a part of how I feel as if it were an object.

Q: BS.Net
How do you, as a guildmaster, promote unity within your guild?

A: Nedra
I try to promote helping one another by example. I try to help when people disagree by bringing the two sides of an issue out to the open and encouraging discussion on it. I propose a solution in guildsay to any problem or subject that needs addressed and encourage debate on it. Most of all I promote unity by being a leader who respects other members as having good ideas and valid statements, I am not a ruler or a dictator. I know that I am respected as the leader but never would I take advantage of that and always try earn that respect.

Thanks Nedra!!!
