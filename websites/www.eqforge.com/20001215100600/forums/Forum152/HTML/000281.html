It looks like the warrior should be the one pulling in your situation. Everyone knows the puller takes the brunt of the hits, particularly when you get hit from behind when you're running back to the group with a mob in tow.

Try this: use your mana for the tank's buffs and damage shield (this saves mana for the primary casters). let the warrior pull. if tracking is needed, lead the warrior to the mob and then run back and wait. when the mob comes back, wait a few seconds to make sure the warrior has his attention. then unleash the damage that only a ranger can do. let the warrior do their job. if you see the warrior is getting a beating, then you can taunt, (snare if you haven't already) and help take some of the tanking responsibility.

have you ever had a warrior thank you for taunting a mob off of them? it's a nice feeling.

A couple things:

First of all, a ranger does not attract attention as well as a warrior. Were you grouped with one? Warriors attack just as fast as us, and with higher skill caps they hit more often and do more damage. They are also continually taunting, while rangers do not do so except in emergencies. Unless you are absolutely stupid with your spells, a ranger in combat will NOT out-taunt a warrior.

Secondly, yes, we are MORE of a manasink than a warrior is...a warrior is supposed to be a better tank. So is a paladin or a shadowknight (although paladins don't taunt as well, with slower attacks and no offensive magic...SK's can outtaunt us easily with magic.) That is how we are designed. Our benefits lie elsewhere. That does not mean we are not viable, are a detriment to a party, or can not tank at all.

(Note that I think rangers DO need a slight defense increase, around 20 points, nothing major. That would make about a 5% damage-taken difference in combat.)

Lastly, guess what? Warriors, who are much better tanks than rangers, also get totally beaten to hell in Kunark. So does any class that gets close enough to melee. Welcome to Kunark, rangers. It's a tough place.

Any cleric that tries healing a ranger with Superior Heal and then complaining they're a mana sink needs a /bonk on the head--I've seen more than one handicap themselves this way. Yes, you're a serious mana sink if they're doing that, since you're paying the double penalty of needing more healing AND using a much less efficient spell to do it. It's a little effort (especially in a pickup group) for you and your cleric to get a feel for when you need them to start casting that complete heal, but it's WELL worth it.

I usually play a cleric on a second account when I play (lvl 44 cleric, lvl 52 ranger). The two of us can take a wurm easily by ourselves; if I melee it to death start to finish (I usually don't, since that's a waste of time and clerical mana), it takes 3 CH's, about 60% of that particular cleric's mana (lvl 44 192 WIS no +mana items)...that's a big chunk, certainly more than a warrior would need, but that's also worst-case scenario. Throw that warrior into the mix, and even if he NEVER taunted it off me, we'd kill the wurm in half the time and mana useage; or we could take a MUCH tougher mob for the same amount of healing mana spent.

Now, by the same token, if I tried that same fight with only superior heals (at 2100+ hp, each heal is less than a bubble and a half of my health bar), I'd die.

No, we can't tank mobs that hit for several hundred damage per swing...warriors can't even do that individually very well (if at all, on some mobs). However, on mobs that hit for 130ish, we can tank just fine. You won't ever be as mana-friendly to the clerics as that 3600hp warrior, but that's to be expected.
