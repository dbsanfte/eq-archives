It sounds like the primary idea behind continuing on to Thebes is that taking Egypt's capitol would reduce the chance of Memphis flipping back. Unfortunately this is not the case, because Egypt's new capitol will be Giza, which is just as close to Memphis as Thebes is. The only advantage to be gained there is that Thebes has over 100 culture and Giza has less than 100 (it has only expanded once).

Also note Memphis is currently 100% resistors. If we put MP there to quell resistance and it flips, we lose the units. If we get peace then resistance will fade quicker, which lowers the flip risk.

Building another all-Roman town near Memphis, dogpiling it with Roman workers, and building up culture might decrease the chance of a flip, but moving Egypt's capitol one tile further away is not likely to help much.

If we can find some way of keeping a city in the Memphis area, we can deny Horses from Egypt, which will give us an advantage once Chivalry is researched.

Here's an alternative plan. How about we pillage the road between Thebes and the horses (inside Egyptian territory) before getting peace. This way, if Memphis flips back they don't get the horses right away and we have a momentary advantage. If we get to knights before it gets connected, then we'll be closer to being ready to finish Cleo off.

Lest you think this last thought (finishing off Cleo later) is too bloodthirsty, there has been quite a lot of discussion in the GOTM forum lately about how one can enhance a spaceship victory by expanding to close the domination limit first. We'll have to take out one or more neighbors before going peaceful to make sure someone doesn't beat us in the race.

In the meantime after peace is obtained run down the population of Memphis by building workers and grow it back as Romans, or maybe even better yet pop rush a library to get culture as fast as possible.
