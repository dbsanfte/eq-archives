I hereby propose this as an amendment to our constitution, to help rectify the situation posed by mid-term vacancies and to allow expulsion from an office under certain conditions of prolonged absence. Article D is replaced entirely by this more detailed amendment.
D. Offices shall be filled by elections occurring at the end of
each calendar month and shall be filled for one-month terms.
1. In the event of a vacancy in an office,
the deputy for that office shall assume the powers of that office for the

remainder of the term. If there is no deputy or if the office was
newly created and had no office-holder, a mid-term election shall
be held if the vacancy occurs more than one week before the end of
the term. If the vacancy occurs less than one week before the
end of the term, the office may remain vacant until the beginning
of a new term.
2. In the event that an office has no confirmed candidates
when the elections begin, the new President shall appoint a
citizen to hold that office at the beginning of the term.
The appointee shall have to be confirmed in a poll of the
citizens before taking office.
3. An officer may be removed by Presidential decree if he/she
has not had any activity on the Democracy Game forum or has not
responded to an important request within seven days and has not
stated absence, or if an absence lasts for longer than two weeks.
The officer must have received at least 48 hours of notice by the
President before being expelled.

Jun 21, 2003, 06:39 PM
You are exactly right d8575. That is in fact the whole point behind having deputies. They are there to step in and act for the absent leader but they should not officially become the leader unless the original leader formally resigned or is impeached. We should not be so quick to try to replace leaders who are absent for short periods.

As for turn based terms and elections, the idea is to base terms on a fixed number of Civ 3 game turns. For example a term could be 60 game turns. There is a calculator that allows one to determine what game year it will be x number of turns from any other game year. The calculator allows a schedule to be constructed where in the beginning and ending years of each term are given as well as the game year at which elections should be held.

The reason I think turn based terms are superior to calender based terms is that if the president knows he or she has 60 turns to play he or she will be more likely to stop a chat if there is something important to discuss. Stopping the caht and allowing the citizens to make decisions in the forums is much better than a spot vote in the chat. I think that president's (especially near the end of the month) are reluctant to stop chat's since they lose their ability to play (and hence their power) at the end of the month.

I doubt we'd ever have a president who would play one turn at a time just to prolong time in office. The power of the presidency lies in the number of turns he or she can play and not the mere length of time in office.
