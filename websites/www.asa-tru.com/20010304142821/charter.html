I. Guild Description
ASA-TRU is a guild of High Honor. As a member of this guild, your loyalties are required to have this obligation. ASA-TRU has determined that the enjoyment of roaming the lands of Norrath be the Number One (#1) priority. We are here to build one of, if not, THE, Best guild in the lands. This guild provides friendship, loyalty and protection. In general, ASA-TRU is here for you, and all other members. To betray any one member is to betray the entire guild. If you are attacked by an outsider, you shall have the entire guild standing beside you. There is no tolerance for breaking of the rules stated below. There is, also, no tolerance for treachery or lack of self-discipline. Be brave and honorable, and the guild will flourish, as will all of the members who support it.

II. Malicious Conduct
A. Theft: An ASA-TRU member will not steal under any circumstances. If this is under suspicion, it will be turned over, and be viewed by, the Officers for investigation.

(back to top)

B. Deceptions and Falsehoods: An ASA-TRU Member will not promote lies, cheats, "Bug Usage" or insults, in the lands of Norrath. If this is under suspicion, it will be turned over, and be viewed by, the Officers for investigation.

(back to top)

C. Player-Killing: An ASA-TRU member WILL NOT attack another player, except in mutually consented duels.
1. If an ASA-TRU member becomes bored and decides to turn in their Tome of Discord, and become a Player-Killer, he/she will be immediately exiled and they will NEVER be welcome back into the Guild.
2. Intentionally training aggressive monsters or NPC's upon another player, no matter what the circumstances, is considered Player-Killing, and is a BANISHABLE offense, if you are caught doing it, you will be banished from the Guild. If there is a question as to whether or not you did so, intentionally or not, you will be questioned by the officers, as will all parties involved. If you admit to doing this, you will be asked to leave the Guild, of your own accord, or will be removed by one of the Officers.

(back to top)

D. Betrayal: Any ASA-TRU member who has been charged with betraying the Guild, attempt to subvert the Guild, or, in any way attempt to break down the moral structure or morale of the guild, etc., will, immediately, go before a review by the Officers. If found guilty of the said charges, they, and all other ASA-TRU characters they may have, are to be removed from the guild, at once. Anyone found to be supporting a person in the same fashion will be brought for review, as well.

(back to top)

E. Language: No ASA-TRU member will use language considered being obscene or vulgar, period. If you are caught using vulgarity, you will be given a warning, depending on the content or severity of your language. The Officer or Officers on line will document each subsequent offense, as they occur. Should it appear that there is a pattern to this behavior, you will be given a strike for each offense thereafter. Continued abuse will lead to a probationary period, where you will remain in the guild, but, be under constant scrutiny of the entire guild. You will be re-evaluated, upon the completion of the probationary period. The decision to allow you to remain in the guild will be decided at that time. (Note: If you group with people, and you know them well, what you do and say with them is up to you. If you offend one or more of them, and they report you, that is your fault.) *If the language you are thinking of using is questionable to you, the best recourse is to not use the language. The best policy is to just not use any language that could be considered to be an expletive...

(back to top)

F. Guild Dropping: On more than one occasion, members have left the guild, then, later, decided that they still wanted to be a member. Any member who leaves the guild has forfiet their right to membership from that time forward. If you worked at a company, quit and later decided that you wanted to go back to that company, the chances of your rehire are exceptionally small. The lack of faith, duty, trust, loyalty and other issues arise from such an action. ASA-TRU is a family. If you choose to leave that family, you must face the consequences. Think before you react. We all work hard at making a family atmosphere, remember that we all have good and bad days. If you are having a bad day, log off and think about it. What you add to the ASA-TRU family is as important as what you take out of it, should you leave. Talking of leaving the guild, frequently, is just as bad as actually leaving the guild. Talk of leaving the guild or game, on a consistent and frequent basis, is demoralizing and will be dealt with on a case by case basis.

III. Discipline
The breaking of every rule has its consequences. These ARE circumstances which will follow the associated breach of conduct.

A. Three Strike Rule: Each ASA-TRU member gets three strikes, that will gauge their status in the guild. Your behaviors and actions will dictate if you get a strike. Violation of any of the guild by-laws listed herein can prompt an Officer to give a member a strike. The severity of one's actions can bring about more severe sentence, such as two strikes for one action, or summary dismissal from the Guild. As certain offenses do not necessarily warrant a strike, a verbal warning may be issued. The officers will monitor strikes and after 3 strikes; a person will be put on probation for a period of two weeks. If, during that time, the person brings dishonor to him/herself or the guild, or breaks another by-law, they will be removed from the guild. After a period of one month of "good" behavior, each strike against an individual can be removed. Exceptional deeds can also prompt the removal of a strike, based on the decision of the Officers. You cannot build up good deeds and "bank" them to wipe out negative deeds, as they appear.

(back to top)

B. Initiates: Initiates can be deguilded at any time during their probationary period, for any reason, whatsoever. Asa-Tru is a guild of high honor, and one that is strict in its rules, therefore, we will attempt to weed out those that would tarnish the guild name, before they have a chance to. If you have a problem with this, then you may wish to consider whether Asa-Tru is the guild you wish to join.

V. The Guild

A. Membership: All members can and are encouraged to suggest people to apply to become members of ASA-TRU. All hopeful members must contact a Crusader or the Sentinel for interview/questioning. All applicants must be of level 15 or higher, must have read and understand our charter, rank structure, and know who the Guild Masters are. From there, all potential members must group with existing Asa-Tru members a minimum of 8 times for a duration of one hour, or more, each time. Once that is completed, the applicant must be sponsored by a minimum of two members. If the person is OK'd by the Crusader or the Sentinel, they will put that person up for a vote to the Officers. The recruitment team is in charge of making sure that all members are notified of the result of each voting session. For a person to become a member, they must have two or more members sponsor them for induction. NO candidate will be made a member until they have provided information to the Guild Scribe regarding any character that is to be Guilded. Information needed is: Email Address, Character Name, Character Race, Character Class, Character Level, ICQ # (if applicable), and your real first name (last name is optional).

(back to top)

B. Application Process: The application process to join Asa-tru has changed, there is a bit more to it, now. Please read this section CAREFULLY, especially if you are thinking about joining Asa-Tru.

  • 1. To even be considered for membership to Asa-tru, Applicants must be of 15th level or above. If you are not this level, your application will not even be considered.

  • 2. All applications to the guild must go through the Sentinel via the web site application. The Sentinel will do the initial evaluation of all prospective members. The Sentinel will post the name, class, race, and level of any applicant meeting with his approval in the Potential Members section of the Guild Message Boards.

  • 3. Any potential member MUST know who the guild masters are and have a basic understanding of the guild charter. If they don't, then they do not need to be in this guild. It is the Sentinel's job to determine if they meet this requirement. All Keeper's must also verify this requirement before they sponsor an Applicant.

  • 4. Once a person has been posted on the Potential Members board, that person must group with Asa-Tru members at least 5 times. Those who group with the applicant should then post their opinions of said applicant under that person's thread in the Potential Members board. The post should also include the date that the member grouped with the applicant. Any post that does not include a date of the group will be deleted. Only Warders and above can post on this board. After the Applicant has met the grouping requirement, the Sentinel will verify that all grouping reviews were favorable. If so, then the Sentinel will place the Applicant on the Sponsor board. The Applicant will then need 2 sponsors. These sponsors must be of the rank of Keeper of Truth or above. No Keeper may sponsor more than TWO applicants in a 30 day period. Once the Applicant has received 2 sponsorships, the matter may then move to a vote.

  • 5. Any officer may lead the vote. The vote will be done online in guildchat. There must be a minimum of 10 members of the rank of Warder and above online for the vote. The officer leading the vote must note the names of those voting and the vote that they made. This is to be posted under the Applicants name on the Sponsor board. The vote must be unanimous. Any applicant not receiving a unanimous "aye" vote will be declined membership into this guild.

  • 6. Assuming that the Applicant received a unanimous "aye" vote, the Sentinel will then post under the Sponsor board that the Applicant is approved for membership. The Sentinel or any Crusader may then induct the Applicant into the guild.

    C. Support: If a person is in need of a specific event, item, spell, level or anything else, they should ask the guild for support. The Guild is meant to function as one body. Higher-Level character members should take it upon themselves to help newer or lower level members, as appropriate. Lower level characters should not abuse this, nor ask for it too often. Try to find other members or people to group with that you feel good about hunting with. Those people may make good members in the future.
