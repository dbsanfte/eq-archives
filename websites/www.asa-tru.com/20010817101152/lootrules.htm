Loot Rules

Need before Greed applies ALWAYS. If you have something, if you wont use something, if you only need it to sell, if it isn’t an upgrade to an existing item, on the Character you brought, than DONT roll for it. Transgressions against THAT rule will result in heavy disciplinary action in one form or another, be it through Strikes, or through the loss of Lotto privileges for an amount of time determined at the time the transgression is found.

On multiclass loot, there are some items that are multiclass, but fit certain classes more than others. Or fit all classes that it CAN be used for equally, BUT... benefits the guild more as a whole in the hands of certain classes.

How to do this fairly? Much debate... Much discussion, compromise of sorts was come to after much discussion on this.

No one can argue that some items are better off going to some classes, and that some items are just more fair going to certain classes, even when other classes CAN use them. Solution?

BEFORE the raid, we research, what comes out of this zone?
All the items we can find on the web for that zone, even if we think there is little or no chance of that item dropping, we post the classes that will be allowed to roll on each item, and we check the board each day to make sure there were no questions as to why we did it like that, and if there were we answer them, if people have further question or comment on a particular item after we have answered their post, we make time to talk to them in game, hear them out, hear their reasoning, and actually consider their opinion. If we still feel the same way they get a heartfelt apology, but the decision stands. IF we change our decision, we post it, and it stays that way. The Hunters refuse to flip flop continually on items, if the privilege of having The Hunters hearing your agreements seems to be abused, this privilege will be revoked.

ALL lotto is performed by the person assigned to doing lotto’s, raid leader has final say. Everything is randomed on a number assigned basis, and rolled by the person assigned by the raid leader to do lotto's.

Note: Winning a Multiclass item WILL count as your loot until everyone from your class wins something.

The raid leader will determine ahead of time what is and isn’t "uber" loot and make that posted as well, and will also make the determination as to whether or not "uber" loot is separate from "normal loot"

In raids where the vault is paying for regents, we MUST remember to include ALL classes that will be using them for the raid, clerics, enchanters, Mages (call of hero has a regent). This will be handled by the treasurer and the Hunters.

In raids where the vault is NOT paying for the regents, we MUST remember to include those classes in the loot system for the cleric loots, even if its in a different capacity (such as providing runes)

As far as No Drop class only loot goes, this was a loooong debated issue, here's what we came up with. If you win something, you're out until all other people from your class have won something, there IS an exception to that.

There are certain, though not many, pieces of armor that most everyone would consider Junk for their class, a good example is the cleric bracers from Hate, which we've had to, in the past, INSIST *SOMEONE* *ANYONE* we don’t care WHO loot, just so it doesn’t rot, because NO ONE wanted them, especially if it was going to count as their loot for the raid.

No one wants to see something rot, and we came up with a dozen different scenarios as to how to handle this, and could find none that didn’t have some kind of bug to them. The eventual decision was that each class would discuss BEFORE the raid amongst themselves, what was and wasn’t to be considered Junk. They then tell the raid leader what was Junk, and he in turn informs the lotto leader. Thereafter, anyone from that class can roll on that item, for that raid, and it WON’T count as their loot. IF an item drops, and YOU consider it Junk, but the raid leader wasn’t informed that this was to be considered Junk loot, and anyone from your class disagrees with it being Junk loot even if no one else wants it, it counts as your loot. If an item defaults to you because everyone else has it, and you take it, and it wasn’t Junk, that is your loot, until everyone from your class has won something.

Kick-Backs…Something else long debated. Should a person who won something be able to, for the chance to lotto on something else, be able to give back an item he already won? And if so, should he lose the original item?

Here’s the deal folks…If you win something, and later on something else drops that you would like to be in on the roll for, you can Kick-Back the item you already won, for a chance at winning on the item that just dropped (note, this only applies with non-no-drop items). IF you win on the new lotto, you give back the item you had won originally. If you lose, you keep your original item. YOU MAY ONLY DO THIS ONCE PER RAID. Be sure it’s the item you want to do it on, you don’t get another chance.

Example1: Weesh wins the Boomstick of Buttkicking!
Later, the Uber-Boomstick of MUCH Buttkicking drops. Weesh wishes to roll on the Uber-Boomstick. He offers up his Boomstick, and rolls on the Uber-Boomstick, he loses, keeps his Boomstick, but cannot offer it up again to roll on something else that drops.

Example2: Weesh wins the Boomstick of Buttkicking!
Later, the Uber-Boomstick of MUCH Buttkicking drops. Weesh wishes to roll on the Uber-Boomstick. He offers up his Boomstick, and rolls on the Uber-Boomstick, he wins, gets the Uber-Boomstick, and then the Boomstick goes BACK to the lotto pool, and is rolled on by everyone who can and will use it. Weesh may NOT offer up the Uber-Boomstick to roll on something else.

These are the lotto rules as they stand now, they can and will be edited as necessary, any time they are changed we will be posting a reminder to re-read them.
