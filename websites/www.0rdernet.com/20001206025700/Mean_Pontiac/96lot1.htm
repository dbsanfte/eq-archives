click -> A maroon firebird parked on a rock. The base Firebird got an new engine for the 96' model year. The 3.8L 3800L Series II V-6 was now the standard rated at 200hp. This was as much or more than some of the 5.0L and 5.7L V-8s of the 80s and 90s.

click -> A green T-Top Formula. Some of the new options that could be had on a 96' model Firebird included a theft deterrent system, a new exterior color called Red-Orange Metallic, a new interior color called Taupe, and a power antenna. The W68 Sport Appearance Package was also offered on Firebirds and Firebird convertibles including a ground effects package, fog lamps, and a dual outlet exhaust system.

click -> A black T-Top Formula with the WS6 package installed. 1996 saw the reintroduction of the WS6 Performance & Handling Package which hadn't been available since the 1990 models. The new package was available on both Formulas and Trans Ams. Even more exciting about the advent of this new package was that the legendary Ram Air setup was also brought back as one of its features. A special dual-inlet hood was installed on cars providing forced air induction into the engine. The result was a horspower increase from 285hp to 305hp. This was the first time Pontiac fans had seen horsepower ratings over 300 since the early 73' SD455s which were rated at 310hp.

click -> The WS6 Trans Am decked out in red. Interestingly enough Trans Am sales dropped in 1996 even though the new Ram Air WS6 package was now available and horsepower ratings were at a new high. Over 12,000 units were sold in 1995 and that number dropped to just over 9,000. Substantial price increases due to these new packages was probably the biggest factor.

click -> A great pic of a WS6 Trans Am flying down the road. Part of the new WS6 package included several suspension upgrades including firmer springs, shocks, and bushings. Larger sway bars were also installed; 32mm up front and 19mm in the rear. WS6 cars alos got exclusive 17"x8" 5-spoke aluminum rims with P275/40ZR17 mounted on them.
