click -> 		This is a convertible Trans Am equipped with the Ram Air package. The car made an appearance at the 1997 Trans Am Nationals held in Dayton, Ohio. The pics were taken and submitted by Kenny G. The introduction of the ALL new 1998 Trans Am has proved to be an extremely exciting one. While the outward appearance may not please everyone, the new LS1 engine along with a host of other mechanical upgrades should.
          The new LS1 is an all aluminum block with cast iron cylinder liners and cast aluminum heads. The 90 degree 5.7L V-8 displaces 346 cubic inches and with a 3.90 bore and a 3.62 stroke the standard horsepower rating is now 305hp @ 5200 rpm with torque figures at 335 lbs.-ft  @ 4000rpm. Some other interesting additions to the new block are six bolt mains, hypereutectic pistons, powder metal connecting rods, a nodular iron crankshaft, a coil per cylinder ignition system, and a cast aluminum oil pan. There are a slew of other improvements and new technologies associated with this motor. I hope to provide an illustrated breakdown of the new engine soon. The Ram Air version will come in around 320hp.

click -> 		The new Formula with a Ram Air hood. Contrary to many fears the new Birds did not lose their signature Flip-Up headlights. They remained but with a new design. Each housing now holds two "mini-beam" headlights that are supposed to improve lighting during nighttime driving. Driving lights are also now standard equipment for Firebirds.
          Additionally heat extractor ducts are once again installed in the fenders to help with brake cooling. The braking system has also been redesigned. New rotors up front have increased in diameter from 10.7 inches to 11.9 and in the rear from 11.4 to 12 inches. Also installed in the front are aluminum dual-piston calipers and brake linings have been changed from semi-metallic to organic. The Anti-lock Brake System is now a solenoid based system manufactured by Bosch. An Electronic Brakeforce Distribution system has also been installed and the Firebird is the first American car to utilize this system. It replaces the standard hydraulic proportioning valves. Lastly the new Firebird also gets a revised traction control system that is operator selectable from the interior.
         
The new Formula carries a base price of $23,390. If you'd like to find out more information about these cars or are interested in buying one follow this link to go straight to the source.

click -> Another newcomer to the 98' section and O.J. Callahan is the owner. O.J. writes:

     "This car was purchased Valentine's Day 1998 to replace a '97 Formula that was Red-Orange Metallic. In my opinion, this is the most highly refined Firebird ever built. The changes from 1997 to 1998 are instantly recognizable when driving. The LS-1 is a much quieter and less violent engine than the LT-1 was. The larger caliper brakes and computer control system gives full pedal performance. Despite the less volatile nature of the LS-1, it is very easy to break the back end off the asphalt with an authoritative roar from the dual tail pipes. The new exhaust is much more pleasing in appearance and sound than what last year's dual chromes on each end were. Everywhere I go, my car attracts lots of attention. While the aftermarket has been flooded with all sorts of tricky aftermarket products for the LT-1, the LS-1 requires no such modifications. As a 305hp stock engine, I couldn't possibly want any more power than what I have now. Since I purchased this car, no one dares to race or even bother me. That wasn't the case with the LT-1. A Ford Mustang Cobra driver told me he wouldn't even try to take an LS-1. I have encountered and defeated C4 Corvette drivers who thought they could take an LS-1. It may not be packaged around an all fiberglass $40K body, but this is one awesome looking and performing machine that Pontiac has created. I don't see the popularity of the Camaro ever recovering to the lofty heights that the Firebird has achieved with the latest incarnation of the best valued sports car on the road today.
     What keeps the rubber on my wheels and the gas in my tank ? The simple knowledge that I am driving the third most powerful domestic car on the street today, behind the Viper and Corvette. And that's some pretty impressive company to keep, when you kept the price tag under $30,000."

O.J. Callahan
