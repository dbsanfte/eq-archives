             1970½ Super Pro Trans Am
                                           owner: Scott Rex
                            Scott's Web site: Pure Pontiac Racing

Engine: 71' 455c.i. bored .030, equipped with a studded 2 bolt bottom (ARP studs), a factory windage tray with a Warrior crank scraper, a RAIV oil pump BME aluminum rods, HO Racing lightweight wrist pins, TRW forged stock type replacement pistons with spiral locks, Total Seal gapless rings, a Lunati custom grind roller camshaft (grind#RRC-271-281) with .645 intake & exhaust lift (.690 actual measured lift) and .271 & .281 degree duration @ .050 and 114 centerline, Crower roller lifters with no restrictors, Manley custom length pushrods, and Sharp 1.5 roller rockers.
          The heads are ported 69 RAIV heads that include swirl polished and tuliped 2.19 and 1.77 valves, combustion chambers that have been opened to 82ccs for about 10.5:1 compression, Crower roller valve springs, an A.R.P. head stud kit and SuperDuty pushrod tubes that have been pressed in.
          The intake is a stock Warrior unit. An 850 Holley annular discharge carburetor sits on top. A Carter internally regulated "super race" pump with a Russel filter, no regulator and #8 line feeds the carb. A Mallory 140 pump and regulator feed 6 psi to both nitrous systems through #8 line. An HEI distributor with MSD 6A and 3 step ignition retard (removes 4 deg of ignition timing for 1st stage of nitrous and another 3 deg for the 2nd stage), an Accel Super Coil and a set of AC R44s plugs provide the spark.
          A 2-stage nitrous oxide system has also been installed. 1st stage provides an extra 150hp off the line and the 2nd stage, on a time delay relay, brings in another 100hp after 3 seconds. Lastly a set of hooker Super Competition headers vent the exhaust out of the engine. 

Drivetrain: A TCI "street fighter" turbo 400 with an ATI Treemaster 9" converter (stalls at 3,000rpm with the nitrous and 4,000rpm without it) is the tranny combo of choice. The original factory 12-bolt rear still sits beneath the car equipped with a Moroso brute strength posi unit, Moser axles, c-clip kit, and Zoom 3.90 rear gears. A set of Competition Eng. bolt-on ladder bars were also installed out back. The factory original leaf springs are still in place. M/T 29.5x10.5 slicks on Weld 15x8 Draglites provide the traction.

Body & Interior: Both are stock. Body weighs in at 3700lbs with driver

Best quarter mile time: 9.75 @134mph (This car is registered and inspected and is driven on the street regularly during spring and summer.)

               1979 Super Pro Trans Am           
                                            owner: Scott Rex
                           Scott's Web site: Pure Pontiac Racing

Engine: 1977 400c.i. 2 bolt main powerplant. Its built with a Crane cam spec'd at .508/.532 lift and .244 and .254 deg @ 0.050 and Rhoads lifters. The heads are 69 RAIII ported to flow 265cfm. The intake is another stock Warrior unit with a Holley 750 carburetor. For the street driving that this car sees a FlowMaster 4" exhaust system was also installed.

Drivetrain: The transmission is a Turbo 350 that came with the car. A 10" HO Racing unit has been installed that stalls at 3500rpm. Tire selection is a hefty 33x21.5" tire which is swapped with 33x14.5 Goodyear slicks at the track.

Body & Interior: The car has been back-halved and to fit the racing slicks. The interior is complete and unchanged. The car still has its power steering, power brakes, power windows, and power door locks.

Best quarter mile time: 10.60 @ 126mph (This car is registered and inspected and is driven on the street regularly in spring and summer.)

                1975 Super Pro Trans Am
                                          owner: Laura Brunson
                              

Engine: 428c.i. bored .030. Heres the internals: ARP main studs, Crankshaft Specialties crank (double knife edge, nitrided, & lightened by 12lbs), Eagle 3D steel rods, Competition Cams roller cam (.252/.262 @ .050 duration, .614/.604 lift), and Ross custom pistons.

Drivetrain: A 2-speed Powerglide tranny with a 1.96 1st gear and a 4.88 rear end get the power to the rear wheels.

Body & Interior: No info yet...

Best quarter mile time: 10.90 @ 123mph

                     1967 Comp Firebird
    (The 3rd Annual Tri-Power Sunday Comp Class Champion)
                                           owner: David Gilreath
                              

Engine: 455c.i. Pontiac bored 0.30 over (440cid) with Ross Pistons and a 428 crank. Heads are a set of ported Wenzlers with stainless valves and silicon guides. Engine compression is a stout 13:1. An MSD 7AL and a Unalite Distributor (Inferred module) provide the ignition. A 1050 Holley Dominator sits atop the block and provides the air/fuel mixture.     

Drivetrain: Tranny is a Coan Powerglide with a trans brake and air shifter. Rear is a Dana 60 with Richmond gears at a 5.13 ratio. Drivehsaft is 3" with a 1 1/8 universals. Underneath is a Ladder bar suspension along with a rear frame kit from JEGS. Tires used are 15x32x14s.

Body & Interior: Modifications include a 12 point rollcage with two shaft loops and rocker bars and an aftermarket steering wheel with a removeable wheel.

Best quarter mile time: 10.30s @ 135mph
