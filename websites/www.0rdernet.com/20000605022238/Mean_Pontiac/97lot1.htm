click -> 		This Ram Air Trans Am belongs to a good friend of mine, Rich Miller. Rich writes:

"When Car & Driver's first look at the '98 Trans Am hit the stands (actually my brother's mailbox) I headed out to find my dream car: a '97 WS6 T/A. Now everyone will have their own opinion about the restyled car, but I prefer the cleaner styling of the original 4th generation. The icing on the cake is Pontiac's clever reunion of Ram Air with Firebird. Five spoke 17" inch wheels and a WS6 badge complete the visual clues. I missed my '94 T/A and found out that my wife did too! With that stamp of approval I started what I thought would be a hopeless attempt to find a black 6-speed WS6 Trans Am after Pontiac had stopped taking orders for the '97 model year. As luck would have it a local dealer had a car on order exactly the way I would have optioned it. After a nail-biting wait in which the whereabouts of the car were unknown it finally arrived. All I can say is what a car! The power, handling, comfort, and conveniences are first class. I certainly don't want to start old versus new or corporate V-8 arguments, but nay sayers should test drive the latest Firebirds before condeming them. Am I selling my Judge? OF COURSE NOT!"

Rich previously owned a 1970 Trans Am (see the 70' Firebirds Lot#1) that he sold in order to pick up a more economical daily driver.

click -> 		A nice red convertible. The convertible model continued for the 97' models. You could still get either any of the models in a convertible as well as the 5.7L engine and the WS6 package. The wheels that came standard with the WS6 package were however unavailable. A different set of performance rims were installed in there place.

click -> A silver Firebird looking ready to drive. Standard 4-way seat adjusters became standard on all models for the 97' year and an electronic instrument panel with analog gauges was also added. Base Firebirds now received a 200hp 3.8L V-6.

click -> A red WS6 Trans Am. I think this is the best looking model of all the fourth gen cars except maybe.....the 98'.

click -> The 97' version Firehawk looks aggressive and ready to fly. The Firehawk contiued to be an available option for th 97' year model. As in the past it was only available on the Formula models. The Formula was built in stock trim by the factory and was then sent to Street Legal Performance (SLP) where the actaul Firehawk package was installed. Many changes have taken place with the Firehawk since its inception in1991. The price had become much lower and some equipment (like interior roll cage setups) had been changed removed or discontinued. However the end product was still as much of a performer as before and maintained its threat status with the other sports cars being offered on the market.
