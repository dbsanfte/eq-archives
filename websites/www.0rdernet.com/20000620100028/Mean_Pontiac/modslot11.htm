  Sealed Inside  $60     Model Store MPC Kit# 6427






















This is a large scale 1/16 kit built by MPC of the 1980 Special Edition Trans Am. It was part of the Trophy Series that was offered by MPC. The first unique part of this kit that you notice is the display stand thats included to display the model on after its finished. Its made out of plastic and molded in silver. Apparently you must paint it to make it look like the one on the box. Next most obvious thanks to the boxtop is the working headlights and tailights. I have seen VERY few kits that have offered functionality with the larger scale kits. This particular kit is just plain awesome. Its size contributes to its detail already but MPC went the distance to offer an outstanding kit. The engine is the 4.9L turbo V-8 that was available in 1980. The engine is very detailed down to the turbo unit, belts, hoses and even spark plug wires. The doors open and feature detailed and correct door panels and the interior is carpeted. The carpet provided is brown which will go along with the beige interior scheme and resembles something you might pick up from a arts and crafts store. It comes on one large square and must be cut to fit the interior. Its like a sticker and doesn't require glueing. It is used on both the floor board and the door panels.
The T-Tops are removable and come already off the car. They are made of thick plastic and very closely resemble smoked glass. VERY nice Tops. The chrome offered is what you'd expect with any kit with a lot more detail. The factory snowflakes, several engine pieces, engine turned dash cover and some suspensio parts are all chrome. The decal kit is incredible. It features everything needed for the Special Edition package. One oddity of this decal set is that the "Trans Am" decals provided for the fender and rear spoiler are broken into pieces. Instead of one long "Trans Am" decal you get "Tr", "an", "s", "Am" decals. Presumably this is to make easier on the builder to correctly place the decal without fear of tearing it. Just a guess. Besides the stripes, bird, and Trans Am decals you get a few really neat stickers not always provided in a regular kit. One decal is a large yellow square with the following text in red "Member of Midwest Firebird Club Do Not Touch". You also get several custom liscense plates along with an Iowa plate and a California plate and you get two "For Sale" signs in two different colors. The kit is dated 1986.
MPC Kit# 78-8200-201










This is a dealer promo offered in 1979 to buyers that bought a new Trans Am at the dealership. from 1976 to 1981 promos were offered as unbuilt kits. This kit is the starlight black version. The following is from the Promo section of the site:

Trans Am Coupe –Colors known to exist: Starlight Black and Francisco Red. This model marked the final year for the second-generation Firebird promotional models and "Promo Kit". With the exception of the new custom interior design this kit remained identical to the 1979 kit in all areas of detail. The decal sheet was very colorful this year and was printed in new colors. The "Trans Am" lettering and hood bird was red, yellow and orange. These colors on the black car made for a visually striking model. Since all 1980 Trans Ams came with either the 301 or the 305 5.0 litre motor the use of the T/A 6.6 shaker scoop decals would be inaccurate. The black cars still came with gold chrome plated parts and the red car had silver chrome plated parts. The box cover was the same as the 1979 kit however the information was changed to reflect the 1980 features. Value for an unbuilt factory sealed kit in mint condition is in the $25.00-$35.00 ranges.

  Factory sealed  $30     Model Store Revell Kit# 7314
This is a 1/25 scale kit of a 1980 Trans Am that looks to be outfitted for SCCA racing. "Firebird Racer" is the title on the box. Features include IMSA fenders, an opening hood, whale tail spoiler, custom interior with custom seats, custom Western Cyclone wheels, and the racing decals seen on the car on the box. Interestingly the words "Photo of painted and assembled prototype model" appear in the lower right hand corner. The kit is molded in white. Copyright date is 1980.
