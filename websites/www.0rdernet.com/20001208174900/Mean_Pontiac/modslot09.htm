Monogram Kit# 2611




This is a rather large 1/8 scale kit of a brown '79 Trans Am. I bought one of these a long time ago when they were new on the shelf and my wife and I built it together. Its a high quality, very detailed kit. I remember it was very easy to put together and had lots of features like decals for the dash, a highly detailed interior, highly detailed engine compartment and movable suspension.  I have learned through some research that a very limited number of these kits were ever sent out to distributors for some reason and as such they are veyr rare to come by and very collectable. I'd like to buy another one of these if anyone has one to sell.

  Factory sealed  $40     Model Store MPC Kit# 1-0735











The third kit in the WAR series differs from the first two in that it is a 1979 Trans Am. This is also an MPC kit and is 1/25 scale. The car can be built either custom or stock. The contents are molded in red. The T-Tops are not removable but, as with many kits, their is a large enough depression to cut them ou if you want them off the car. If you do choose to cut out the Tops you get another clean set on a tree presumably to replace the ones that will inevitably get damaged when they are cut out. This is the first kit I have seen to offer two sets of Tops. Like many real '79s of the day this kit can be highly customized. A roll cage is included for installation as well as chrome side pipes and a chrome CB antenna for the trunk.The kit comes with a lot of chrome. Besides the already mentioned pieces you get two sets of wheels, a set of snowflakes and a set of custom rims. Several engine pieces are chrome including the oil pan, which is an unusual addition as well as chrome exhaust pipes and a chrome shifter. Decals included are mainly what you see on the box. You get a yellow and black hood bird, 4 "Trans Am" decals, two sail panel birds, the multi-hued racing stripes seen on the box, two "Warlord" decals, two "400" decals and some Pennzoil and Accel decals. An interesting note is that the phrase at the bottom of the box reads," Photo of Painted Prototype Model". Also the "stock" version car depicted on the box is painted yellow which would seemingly make it very hard to see the hood bird. The box is dated 1978.

MPC Kit# 6323






10th Anniversary Firebird (does not say Trans Am on cover):
This model is a MPC brand 1/25th scale kit. The kit can be built two ways, either stock or race. The kit is dated 1988 and is molded in silver. The kit contains two sets of wheels, custom and the stock turbo version (both are chrome).  The T-tops from this car are not cut out but
there is a distinctive line on the outside of the roof where they should be.  The rear spoiler and wheel caps are separate parts from the body and must be glued on. For the race version of this kit, a full roll cage is included. Decals include all the decals on the box cover including the red pin stripes.  Note however that there are no "TRANS/AM" decals on the box cover.  There is however 4 full sets of the alphabet included from which you could cut the "TRANS/AM" name from (I think that was the intention as the letters are formatted in those type of letters).  There is also 2 T/A 6.6 decals included.  The deletion of the full Trans Am decals must have something to do with the SCCA regulations on the Trans Am name. Note: I have included a side shot of the box that shows the rear of the car to show there is no spoiler decal.
  Sealed Inside $18     Model Store MPC Kit# 6323


This kit is the exact same kit as above. To date I have seen the #6323 boxtop with the Firebird title and the Trans Am title. The same kit was released as 1-0741 but so far I have only seen it with the Trans Am title. One of the differences between these two kits is the box with the Trans Am title shows Trans Am decals but not a trunk spoiler decal while the boxtop with the Firebird title doesn't show either.

MPC Kit








This is a dealer promo offered in 1979 to buyers that bought a new Trans Am at the dealership. From 1975 to 1981 promos were offered as unbuilt kits. This kit is the starlight black version. The following is from the Promo section of the site:

Trans Am Coupe –Colors known to exist: Starlight Black and Platinum. It is basically the same promo kit as the 1978 model. New front and rear-end treatments and larger Fisher designed T-Tops highlighted the 1979 kit. The chassis, interior, wheels, tires and exhaust splitters remained the same as the 1978 kit. The decal sheet had gold Trans Am and T/A 6.6 lettering, gold Special Edition striping and black, gray and silver hood bird and sail panel bird and were used in both kits. This time the lettering is correct for a 1979 car. The black car had gold chrome plated parts and the platinum car came with silver chrome plated parts. The cover of the box highlighted a dark blue Trans Am with camel interior and T-Tops. Value for an unbuilt factory sealed kit in mint condition is in the $25.00 - $35.00 ranges.

  Open complete  $8     Model Store Revell Kit# 1387








This is a rather unusual kit but maybe a collectible one. It is a Revell model of a Trans Am customized by George Barris for John Travolta. The kit is a 79' Trans Am and is molded in silver. The decal set includes all the graphics you see on the car on the box. The engine in the kit is a 455 which is what was installed in the real car. The body is highly customized with flared IMSA style fenders per the Barris design. This kit drips of customization with custom wheels, a whale tail spoiler, side pipes, and a customized interior. On the roof Tops are provided for the pre-cut T-Tops but no chrome pieces are provided. This kit is dated 1979.
