click -> 		 This is Scott Holland's Trans Am. This brand newly remodeled bird comes with the 5.0L chevy 305 engine. Scott has warmed it over a little with some high performance equipment. A Crane compu cam, 9:5-1 casts pistons, crane 1:6 ratio rockers, Weiand action plus intake, a Holley 0-1850 600cfm 4bbl carburetor, Accel high output coil, and Accel street wires round out the mods. Exhaust is handled by a set of Hedmann Headers which feed a set of Hedmann Y-pipes which flow through custom 2½" pipes which feed a pair of Dyno Max mufflers and exits out a set of Megs 4" tips. The engine is bolted to a TH-350 tranny. A B&M transmission pack has been added along with a B&M trasnmission cooler. A new GM posi-trac 7 5/8 10 bolt disk brakes was also added along with new 3.23 Zoom gears. A 7/8" rear sway bar with polyurethane bushings was installed to improve handling. Finally a set of 15"x8" (rear) and 15"x7" (front) Outlaw II American Racing Rims were installed with a set of BF Goodrich tires.
     Most recently Scott has had the old TA painted Jet Black with Concept-90 paint. Also some under the hood gauges were added and the engine compartment was cleaned up and detailed. Scott also sent me some ¼ mile info:

Best ¼ mile time to date: 14.45 @ 96mph

click -> Chris Blandino's Firebird started out as a base model Firebird but is far from it now. It originally came with a factory 2.5L (151cu.in.) 4 cylinder engine known as the "Iron Duke". The engine was rated at a pitiful 92hp. Wondering why all the "import" 4-bangers made more horses than did his Firebird, Chris set out to make some changes. The factory throttle body was swapped with a Holley T.B.I. unit which had a larger throttle bore. The factory intake manifold was also traded for an Edelbrock T.B.I. intake. Hooker Super Competition headers replaced the factory exhaust manifolds, a Dynomax Super Turbo muffler was installed and an N.O.S. Nitrous kit was also added for that "little" extra. The engine was also rebuilt. The rebuild included a balanced motor and  align honed, shot peened heads, a Crane cam and vlave train components, and 4 custom pistons by Venolia. After all this the car was still only pulling low 10s on the bottle at the local 1/8 mile track. Very frustrating to say the least, but Chris continued to drive the car. About 40k miles later the engine decided it too was unhappy with the situation and dropped a valve. $10 and a scrap heap in a junkyard later and Chris had an opportunity to put a much better engine in the Poncho. An 83' Civic was bought to serve daily driver chores and Chris began building another engine for the bird. This time the block would be a V-8 (imagine that). Here's the current setup:
     A 383c.i. stroker, built with a Crane 272 grind cam, Speed Pro Hypereutectics Pistons, Edelbrock intake, Carter AFb 4bbl, 1.94/1.60 valves, and an Accel Hei unit. Exhaust starts with Hedmann Shorty headers and flows through Dynomax mufflers via 3" pipe. The N.O.S. kit was transferred from the old junk motor. A 700R4 automatic with TCI kit performs the shifting duties. Suspension Techniques sway bars were added along with 16" rims from an 86' TA. The nose from the 86' was also transferred over to Chris' car. The car was then repainted Porsche Guards Red.

click -> The 1982 year model saw the most radical changes of any year before. The third generation Trans Ams were born and the were a completely new design from models past. Gone were stationary headlights; replaced by the popular "flip-up" or "pop-up" headlights that are still in use on the current Trans Am model. This particular car proves a few things held over from the previous years. Although no longer a special edition the black and gold paint scheme continued.
