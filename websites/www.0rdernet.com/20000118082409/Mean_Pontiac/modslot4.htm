This '73 Trans Am model was made in Japan, manufactured by Doyusha, until it was discontinued sometime in late '97. Its a very large scale model at 1/12. I was able to buy one in Japan before they were discontinued. It was an expensive model costing $75 to $100 in U.S. dollars. Mint examples go for about $130 now. The model is highly detailed, molded well and comes with a good decal set so I think its worth the money. As you can see the hood, doors, and trunk all open and the front wheels will move back and forth when the steering wheel is turned.

ARII Kit# A571-1200
click ->















click ->
The kit is 1/24 scale and was made by ARII . Though a fairly basic kit, it is very nice in detail of what's there.  The body is molded in white and has separate doors that are hinged.  The hood is molded in and is non-opening and has a correct looking shaker molded as part of the hood. Although it is not stated on the box what year it is, the kit is obviously a 1974 due to the '70-'74 style rear window, and new for '74 bumpers.
The interior is molded in black and includes correct looking door panels, console seats and dash panel.  Unfortunately the dash face is not a chrome item. The Formula steering wheel is molded in black and looks correct other than the center cap. There are also some separate
front suspension parts, also molded in black. The chrome parts consist of a full set of honeycomb wheels, a wide set of chrome custom wheels (the rears being deep dish), head lamp bezels, grills, shifter handle, license plates, chrome for the side mirrors.
Instructions for this kit are in Japanese with brief instructions in English, with the title "Firebird Trans Am" with Trans Am as one word.There is is set of BF Goodrich T/A radials includes and the contacts for a small motor and a rear axle with gears attached but it appears that you must purchase the motor separately.
Decals for the kit include a hood bird in odd colors with the body of the bird being blue outlined in red, with the wing tips in yellow outlined in blue then red. There are a few sponsor decals too.  One oddity is that one set of the sponsor decals is Good Year Tires, but the kit comes with BF Goodrich tires.  There is also one decal about 1/4" X 1 1/2" that says FIREBIRD.
As a side note on the box cover under where it says Firebird Trans Am the Japanese writing says the same thing. This from my Father who can read Japanese. No date is available on this kit.

MPC Kit# 1-7615
click ->

click ->

click -> 		This is a 76' Formula model made by MPC, and the only model of a '76 Firebird I've seen to date. It is 1/25 scale and can be built in one of three ways. The entire kit (other than the chrome) is molded in white plastic. It includes a full roll cage. Chrome parts include a set of Rally IIs, a set of custom wheels, a set of sidepipes and some misc engine components. Decals included are the decals shown on the cover of the box (including the "Pontiac" on the quarters). The kit is dated 1975. I was just recently able to acquire one of these models still sealed in the factory plastic. This is another rare and collectable Firebird kit.
MPC Kit# 1-0777
click ->







click -> 		This is an MPC kit of the 1977 Special Edition Trans Am. It is 1/25 scale and dated 1976. The kit is molded in black. The usual compliment of chrome pieces are not included in this kit, instead all the pieces that are usually chrome are gold as they were on the actual Special Edition T/As. The decal set is extensive and detailed. All decals are in gold and include the complete bodystriping, the "Trans Am" badges, the 6.6 Liter decals for the shaker scoop and the hood bird and sail panel birds. The T-Tops have been pre-cut out of the body which is very nice if you like the "Tops-Off" look and no tops are provided. Interestingly the body provided with this kit has the front end already attached. The fender spoilers, however, are not attached and must be glued on. Overall this is an outstanding kit. Probably one of the better of versions of the "Bandit" Trans Am.
