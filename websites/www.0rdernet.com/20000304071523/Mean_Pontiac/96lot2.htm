click -> 		This fine looking dark green Trans Am belongs to Dekhyr Dragon. Dekhyr writes,

          "When I bought my 1996 Ram Air Trans Am, I had no idea of the attention my car would get. 

Once it got attention from a fast little Mustang on the highway. He wanted to race me in moderate traffic.  I revved my car's engine a little and shook my head.  I meant it to mean, "Race?  In this traffic?  You're an idiot."  but he probably took it to mean, "Race? Me against you?  Listen to this engine!   I'm not even going to bother!"  Besides, I'm not much into spanking cars, not even with my hand.

Speaking of racing, it also gets attention from the police.  I think cops love to pull over sexy cars.  Now I'm not the sort of person who breaks the speed limit by 50 mph.  I believe I drive at reasonable speeds.  But my opinion doesn't matter; I got caught twice for speeding.  After that I decided to get a radar detector, and I haven't had a ticket in the two years I've had it.  That, and I know all the speed traps along the New Jersey Turnpike.

Speaking of the New Jersey Turnpike, my hot Firebird gets plenty of attention from the gas jockeys along the NJT.  They would say things like, "So, how much did it cost?" (Answer: "$30,000"), "How do you like it?" (Answer: "It's a lot of fun to drive"), and "You should keep it in the garage instead of driving it out here!" (Answer: "It's a lot of fun to drive").

Finally, speaking about fun, my car gets plenty of attention from me. I love my car and I do my best to make it happy.  I wash it when I can, and keep it in the garage when I'm not driving it.  I give it all the servicing it needs and then some.  And I don't spank my car. Usually."

- --Dekhyr Dragon

click -> 		This silver WS6 equipped Trans Am belongs to Paul M. Olsen. Paul writes:

     "My WS6 is fully loaded with every option except a front license plate frame.  If it was available for the '96 model year it is on this car.  My car also has an interesting story behind how I got it (as it seems do many WS6's):

     I was working for International Speedway Corporation in Daytona Beach, Florida as their AIX/UNIX Systems Administrator.  At the time I had a '94 Jeep Cherokee.  I decided that I wanted something fun to drive.  I was told that Pontiac "GAVE" Trans Am's to the speedway executives for promo purposes.   One of the executives (the Risk Manager) had a '96 WS6 for his executive car.   I found that the car was coming up for sale and quickly put a claim on it. I also was told that the car was used on the Daytona International Speedway 2 times for demo rides, however it had a vibration at 115mph so it was not used again.  A friend suggested that the drive shaft may be causing the vibration.  After some searching I discovered that Pontiac has issued a TSB regarding out of balance drive shafts on some '96 Firebirds.  I had the local dealer in Daytona replace the drive shaft under warranty, using the TSB, with the 1LE aluminum drive shaft.  That solved the vibration problem. If I had waited any longer to get my hands on the WS6, Pontiac would have sent it to the local auto auction and there was nation wide interest in that specific car.

     Now I have always been a MOPAR man but MOPAR is not making anything in this price range that even comes close to the WS6!!  I  bought it in November of '96 and fell in love.  The car is a dream to drive.  However I never expected to get the attention that it gets.  I even had a guy ask me to pull over so that he could look at it.  In August of '97 I left International Speedway Corp. and drove the WS6 across the country to my home in California where I now reside."
