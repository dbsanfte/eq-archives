This awesome red Trans Am belongs to Daniel Norman of Sydney, Australia. Daniel writes:

    "Hi, my name is Daniel Norman. I live in Sydney Australia. I currently own a '75 Trans Am. I have only owned it for about 7 months but I am regularly trying to improve it. It has a 400 with a TH350 and a 3.08 posi rear. I have included some pics of it but I have changed it since the pics were taken, it now rides on a set of Turbo TA wheels which have been polished with the slots painted, I have also put a mild cam in the engine and swapped the Holley Street Dominator manifold for a stock 4bbl unit. The car picked up a very noticable amount of performance with the stock intake, the Street Dominator isnt very good for normal use. It also uses less gas with the stocker (I could only get 150 miles from a 80 liter tank before). The engine also got a fresh coat of paint while it was apart.I have also fitted a '76 rear bar, a '76 nose is also planned. Some interesting facts, the car is a '75 but has a fisher T roof, a previous owner has had this done. The steering wheel is on the right hand side of the car while the brakes are on the left, there is a shaft that runs under the dash and the pedal is relocated. BTW: Great page, one of the best I have seen."

Thanks Daniel! Be sure to check out the last two pics in the set. I've seen other TAs where the master cylinder was actually on the right side of the car as well as the steering wheel. This one has a little bit different setup as Daniel described. As a side note Fisher Tops were not available from the factory till '77. Hurst tops were introduced in '76 and replaced midyear '77. It is a very interesting and good looking modification. Too often aftermarket sets are installed that I believe just don't look as good.
