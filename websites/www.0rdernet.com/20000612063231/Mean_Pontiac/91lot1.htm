click -> 		This Firebird belongs to Joe Greco.

Current Modifications:
The 3.1L V-6 had to have the lower end rebuilt at 60,000 miles, so I had some work performed on the heads. They were milled .010, some bowl work was done, and a 3 angle valve job finished it up. Other additions included ADS Superchip, K&N air filter, air box cut open all around, Bosch Platinum plugs, Splitfire Dual wires, Koni double adjustable shocks (sport alternative), Polygraphite sway bar bushings and end links.

Future Mods planned:
Hedman Headers makes headers for the V-6, so I want to have a look at them, a complete exhaust system custom made for the car, a high performance coil, MSD box, and a custom chip made for my car and all of the mods. The time I have below is when I took the car to the track years ago with just the chip installed and the air filter removed.

Best Time:
17.20 @ 80 mph

click -> Here is the standard Formula with the same cool HiTech Turbo aluminum rims and powerplant options as years past. The 5.0L was standard and the 5.7L was optional. A required option for either of these powerplants was the GU6 Performance Enhancement Group which consisted of a dual converter exhaust system, 4-wheel disc brakes, an engine oil cooler, and a performance rear axle.

click -> This is most likely a promotional ad for the Formula.

click -> A red Trans Am. While still a third generation Firebird the 91' and 92' models got redesigned front and rear fascias. The spoiler treatment running along the bottom of the door was also new. The Trans Am and GTA also got a different rear spoiler than years past. The regular firebirds that were built during these two years had a very sleek aggressive look, but the Trans Am with the new rear spoiler was a VERY cool, mean looking ride. I'd still like to own one of these.

click -> Another Trans Am. This one was green. Coupled with the gold anodized aluminum rims and beige interior this was an extremely good looking car. 1991 was rather a landmark year for the Firebird although it was not celebrated by Pontiac nor did most people probably notice. For the first time since 1969 a Firebird could be ordered as a convertible. All models were available to be ordered with this including the Formula and the Trans Am. T-Tops also continued to be a successful option, although the 5.7L could not be ordered on any Firebird with the optional removable hatches.

click -> 		This beautiful dark green GTA is owned by Bob Morgan. The gold GTA emblems and accents go perfectly with the dark green paint. Add a beige leather interior and I think you have one of the best color combinations available for this year model. The bird has posted a best time of 14.37 in the 1/4 mile and harbors a host of modifications. A Stage II Hypertech chip was added to provide the control computer with performance programming, a TPIS air foil and TPIS adjustable fuel pressure regulator were also added. To improve airflow a K&N air filter and a FlowMaster muffler were installed. Shifting duties were also improved with a new B&M shift kit (setup as R/V not street/strip). Lastly a 4:10 rear end was installed to provide hard launches and quicker acceleration.
          A 383 stroker TPI is in the plans for a future modification to help push this bird to much lower ETs. Bob hopes to have this modification finished by spring/summer of this year.
