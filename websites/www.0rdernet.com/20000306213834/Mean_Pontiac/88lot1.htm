click -> This GTA pic looks like it may have been used in an ad. I found it on the net. GTA production in 88 totalled 11,214 and they would become the top Firebird model in the line-up. This new status meant all GTAs would receive the 5.7L V-8, automatic transmission, 16x8 in gold lace wheels, and WS6 suspension. These cars would also come loaded with almost every option available: A/C, rear defogger, power windows, power door locks, cruise control, tilt wheel, steering wheel radio controls, AM/FM cassette stereo with graphic equalizer, Pass Key antitheft system, articulated seats, 45-45 split folding rear seat, rear deck power pulldown, and either cloth or leather seats. 

click -> A blue Firebird. This pic was most likely used in an ad. Changes for the base Firebird in 88' included a new 120mph speedo, different exterior paint availability, 15x7 turbo or diamond spoke aluminum rims, and a new 5.0L 150hp TBI V-8 that replaced previously carburetored engines.

click -> A maroon formula with T-Tops. By 1987 the Formulas had taken on a much more aggresive look and presented a much cheaper alternative to the Trans Am without sacrificing performance. The same   5.0L TBI, 5.0L TPI or 5.7L that was installed in the Trans Ams could be had in the Formula and it was actually lighter weight than its big brother. Besides it has always been my opinion that the Formulas during these years got the coolest looking aluminum wheels of all the models. They were called Hi-Tech turbo wheels.  

click -> The third generation bodystyle had seen quite a lot of refinement by 1988 and the new models were starting to once again take on the aggressive look of a performance vehicle. The addition of the GTA option gave folks the opportunity to have a Trans Am with high dollar creature comforts seemingly improving the image and status of the TA.

click -> 		This awesome Twin-Turbo Trans Am is owned by Jeff Goodell. The engine in this beast was built from a 76' Chevy 4-bolt main block with a forged crank and TRW forged turbo pistons. Dual turbos were chosen and installed for packaging and exhaust plumbing reasons. A Turbo 400 tranny with an Art Carr 3200 stall converter were installed to handle the shifting duties and are mated to a 9" Ford axle with with Traction-Lok and 3.50 gears. The stall converter was recently installed and allows Jeff to launch the car at boost. The rear end gears are also new additions.
     With the newly installed equipment paid a visit to a local Dyno and got some impressive numbers. At 14psi boost 465hp was measured at the rear wheel along with an incredible 521ft-lbs of torque. Jeff believes flywheel ratings are close to 535hp and 600 ft-lbs of torque with a 15% loss in the driveline.  

Jeff also owns a 95' Ram Air Trans Am thats running in the 12s! You can see it at the 95' Firebirds Lot#2.
