MPC Kit# 1-0735


click -> The third kit in the WAR series differs from the first too in that it is a 1979 Trans Am. This too is an MPC kit and is a 1/25 scale kit. The car can be built either customized or stock. This may be a fairly rare and valuable kit among model car collectors. The contents are molded in red. The T-Tops are not removable but, as with many kits, their is a large enough depression to cut them ou if you want them off the car. If you do choose to cut out the Tops you get another clean set on a tree presumably to replace the ones that will inevitably get damaged when they are cut out. This is the first kit I have seen to offer two sets of Tops. Like many real '79s of the day this kit can be highly customized. A roll cage is included for installation as well as chrome side pipes and a chrome CB antenna for the trunk.The kit comes with a lot of chrome. Besides the already mentioned pieces you get two sets of wheels, a set of snowflakes and a set of custom rims. Several engine pieces are chrome including the oil pan, which is an unusual addition as well as chrome exhaust pipes and a chrome shifter. Decals included are mainly what you see on the box. You get a yellow and black hood bird, 4 "Trans Am" decals, two sail panel birds, the multi-hued racing stripes seen on the box, two "Warlord" decals, two "400" decals and some Pennzoil and Accel decals. An interesting note is that the phrase at the bottom of the box reads," Photo of Painted Prototype Model". Also the "stock" version car depicted on the box is painted yellow which would seemingly make it very hard to see the hood bird. The box is dated 1978.

Revell Kit# 7202
click ->







click -> 		This kit is made by Revell. Its molded in black and is modeled after a 79' - 81' Trans Am. It features IMSA style fenders, a 455 engine, real rubber tires, snap-in tinted windows and a full set of decals. The decals are all gold to match the black and feature a large hood bird, two sail panel birds, two 6.6L emblems for the shaker and three "Firebird" decals. The rims provided with this kit look like the aftermarket set depicted on the box. An unusual feature of this model is that the T-Tops have already been "cut-out" and a set of clear plastic "T-Tops" are provided along with the other windows. The body is based on the 79' Trans Ams customized by George Barris for John Travolta and the Fabian movie "Disco Fever", the only difference being the rear spoiler. The Barris cars received whale-tail spoilers. The information on the side of the box quotes 1/4 mile times in the 14 second range for the 455. The kit is dated 1980.
Revell Kit# 7116

click -> This is a 1/25 scale Revell model of a 79' - 81' Trans Am. It is highly customized and was part of a special series of models called "Street Machines.  The body is somewhat based on the 79' Trans Ams customized by George Barris for John Travolta and the Fabian movie "Disco Fever". The kit is dated 1985 and is molded in charcoal gray. Kit includes two sets of tires, stock and big and littles (bigs being slicks). There is only one set of wheels with them being a crosslace type wheel similar to the mid 80's GTA. The T-tops  have already been cut from the roof at the factory and includes
a set of very lightly tinted glass for the openings as well as for the front and rear windows. As for the body, it is molded as shown on the box cover with a wild set of flares and the large open hood scoop. Actually the body looks better than the box cover shows, however in my opinion the front fender flares are too square for the rounded body. The rear spoiler is a glue on item and would look best left in the box. There are no recesses molded in the body for mounting of the rear spoiler so deletion of this item would be easy.  Decals include pretty much what is shown on the box cover which are very tacky, being just a set of single color bold stripes without even so much as a pinstripe around them or even a color variation. Also included are two small Hot Rod license plate decals and one Hot Rod decal that is approx. 2" X 1".  This is by far is the cheapest (and were not talking price) Firebird model in my collection. There is a very small amount of parts included and I would consider this kit to be very suitable for beginning modelers. There is not even one chrome plated part in this kit.  Not even the wheels are chrome as the box cover would imply, nor are any of the engine parts. There is also no tail lamp red plastic or amber front turn signal lamps. Pretty bleak for what is supposed to be a street machine. I give this model two thumbs down!  As for the special magazine subscription offer, Hot Rod  was offering 12 issues for $9.97.

click -> This is a 1/25 ERTL 79' Trans Am model. This may be somewhat of a rare model as Kenny reports he has never seen another one like it. Interestingly it is not a typical model molded in plastic but rather it is a diecast, but what is odd is that only the body is metallic; the rest of the parts are molded plastic. It has separate doors that are hinged as is the hood and trunklid. The metal body is bare metal with no paint or primer, the plastic parts are molded in black with the exception of the parts that come chromed.    Wheels are chrome snowflakes. There is no date as to when this model was manufactured. The only decal included is a lone black and gold hood bird.

click -> 		10th Anniversary Firebird (does not say Trans Am on cover):
This model is a MPC brand 1/25th scale kit. The kit can be built two ways, either stock or race. The kit is dated 1988 and is molded in silver. The kit contains two sets of wheels, custom and the stock turbo version (both are chrome).  The T-tops from this car are not cut out but
the is a distinctive line on the outside of the roof where they should be.  The rear spoiler and wheel caps are separate parts from the body and must be glued on. For the race version of this kit, a full roll cage is included. Decals include all the decals on the box cover including the red pin stripes.  Note however that there are no "TRANS/AM" decals on the box cover.  There is however 4 full sets of the alphabet included from which you could cut the "TRANS/AM" name from (I think that was the intention as there in those type of letters).  There is also 2 T/A 6.6
decals included.  The deletion of the full Trans Am decals must have something to do with the SCCA regulations on the Trans Am name. Note: I have included a side shot of the box that shows the rear of the car to show how there is no spoiler decal.
