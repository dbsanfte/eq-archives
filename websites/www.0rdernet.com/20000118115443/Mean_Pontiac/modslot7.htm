MPC Kit# 6427


click -> This is a large scale 1/16 kit built by MPC of the 1980 Special Edition Trans Am. It was part of the Trophy Series that was offered by MPC. The first unique part of this kit that you notice is the display stand thats included to display the model on after its finished. Its made out of plastic and molded in silver. Apparently you must paint it to make it look like the one on the box. Next most obvious thanks to the boxtop is the working headlights and tailights. I have seen VERY few kits that have offered functionality with the larger scale kits. This particular kit is just plain awesome. Its size contributes to its detail already but MPC went the distance to offer an outstanding kit. The engine is the 4.9L turbo V-8 that was available in 1980. The engine is very detailed down to the turbo unit, belts, hoses and even spark plug wires. The doors open and feature detailed and correct door panels and the interior is carpeted. The carpet provided is brown which will go along with the beige interior scheme and resembles something you might pick up from a arts and crafts store. It comes on one large square and must be cut to fit the interior. Its like a sticker and doesn't require glueing. It is used on both the floor board and the door panels.
The T-Tops are removable and come already off the car. They are made of thick plastic and very closely resemble smoked glass. VERY nice Tops. The chrome offered is what you'd expect with any kit with a lot more detail. The factory snowflakes, several engine pieces, engine turned dash cover and some suspensio parts are all chrome. The decal kit is incredible. It features everything needed for the Special Edition package. One oddity of this decal set is that the "Trans Am" decals provided for the fender and rear spoiler are broken into pieces. Instead of one long "Trans Am" decal you get "Tr", "an", "s", "Am" decals. Presumably this is to make easier on the builder to correctly place the decal without fear of tearing it. Just a guess. Besides the stripes, bird, and Trans Am decals you get a few really neat stickers not always provided in a regular kit. One decal is a large yellow square with the following text in red "Member of Midwest Firebird Club Do Not Touch". You also get several custom liscense plates along with an Iowa plate and a California plate and you get two "For Sale" signs in two different colors. The kit is dated 1986.
