The 1992 Pontiac Firebird, Formula, Firehawk & Trans Am
Lot#1

click -> A red T-Top firebird. Little changed between the 91' and 92' model years. 92' would prove to be the last year for the third generation Firebirds. 15 additional option groups were added and horsepower ratings increased on almost all the available engines. The base Firebird V-6 was increased to 140hp, the 5.0L HO was upped to 230hp, and the popular optional 5.7L was massaged to 240hp.

click -> A white Firebird equipped with a bra and rear window louvers. The trend of putting louvers on the rear windows got its start back in 77' and 78' and has continued to be a popular modification. The convertible while pricey proved to be a successful option when it was reintroduced in 1991. Nearly 1,500 were manufactured in 91' and over 1,800 were produced in 92'. This option would be dropped in 93' but would be brought back again in 94' with the all new fourth generation Firebirds. I only wonder why they waited so long to offer it again.

click -> A nice pic of a cool Firehawk. Notice the air dam installed up underneath the car. This dam was put in place to force cool air up into the radiator to help with cooling. SLP continued to install their Firehawk packages onto factory built Formulas. For the second year the Firehawk was restricted to the Formula body and the color red.

click -> A Firehawk on display. I don't think theres any doubt in anyones mind this car was built to run.

click -> A yellow Trans Am with T-Tops. Supposedly this is a fairly rare color for any of the early 90s models. The standard wheel for the Trans Am and GTA continued to be the 16x8 diamond spoke aluminum rim. High performance P245/50ZR16 steel belted radial tires were mounted on the aluminum rims.

click -> A beautiful turqouise blue Trans Am. I love those gold anodized wire wheels.

click -> 		The 1992 year brought a new model to the Firebird lineup. A special kind of Formula could now be ordered and it was called a Firehawk. The Formula was constructed at the factory like normal but was then sent to Street Legal Performance (SLP) where the Firehawk package was added for a very pricey sum. The package included a lot of engine and suspension upgrades as well as a different tire and rim package and the Firehawk graphics.

click -> The rear view of the Firehawk. This car was another classic sleeper. Many peopel mistook it for an fancy Formula in the early years till the term "Firehawk" got around. I'm sure a lot of camaro and mustang owners got some surprises out some plain-looking Formulas.

click -> This car was a Firehawk concept car built by SLP during the early stages of development. The wheels on this particular car never saw production.

click -> 		A great shot of another Firehawk. This option would remain restricted to the Formula model until the new 98's debuted. Early Firehawks got some interior treatments along with all the upgrades in engine, suspension and braking. New shoulder harness seat belts and a full roll bar were standard upgrades. Aftermarket Brembo brakes were also an upgrade to provide better than factory braking for the increased horsepower provided by the warmed over LT1s.
