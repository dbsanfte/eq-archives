Coming soon!!! New '67 & '68 Firebird Kit
Updated: 22 October 2000

Snapshot of current '67 and '68 kits:
Few and far between is a relevant description when attempting to describe these kits. According to my current research:

AMT: Before they merged with ERTL, AMT released four kits modeled after the 1968 Firebird. Of these only one is 1/25 scale and that is the Famous and highly collectable "Tin Indian" Firebird. All others were 1/43 scale. All were retailed during the late '60s.

Revell: Before they merged with Monogram they offered three 1/32 scale '67 kits, and one 1/32 scale '68 kit. All were retailed during the late '60s.

MPC: Before they were bought out by ERTL, offered a very nice 1/25 scale kit of a '67 Firebird with the clear glass hood and a simliar 1/25 scale kit of the '68 Firebird. They also offered a highly customized '68 Firebird George Barris creation dubbed the "Super Teen Firebird" which was produced for a movie of the time.

Palmer Plastics: Offered one '68 Firebird kit. Scale is not advertised on the box but I believe it was close to 1/25, maybe 1/43. This company has long been defunct and the kit was a terrible one. You could count the number of pieces to assemble on your hands.

Summary: From the information above its plain to see that only one '67 kit (MPC) and two '68 kits (AMT & MPC) are out there that would interest the average builder. Unfortunately the same information conspires to make these kits very hard to find and extremely expensive to purchase because of their collectability.
The answer:
Over the last few months I have been working with a resin casting company and I am extremely happy to tell you that I will be offering a brand new 1/25 scale kit of the 1967 Firebird beginning in mid-December. For those of you that have never dealt with resin castings before, producing a new kit is a two step process:

1) A donor kit must be identified that can supplement the resin kit to provide all the necessary pieces to build a complete kit.

2) Research is performed to determine what pieces actually need to be "re-cast" in order to provide the desired end product. A test casting is manufactured and reviewed for technical accuracy and product quality. The kit is put into production.

What does all this mean to you? The resin kit is not a complete kit. Another kit, in this case AMT/ERTL's '69 Firebird 400 kit or AMT/ERTL's '69 Trans Am kit which is cheap and readily available, must be used to provide all the pieces that would not be different but are needed to complete the kit i.e. tires, windshield, engine, etc... This way the entire kit does not have to be recast saving lots of $$$. Currently this resin kit is projected to provide the following items unique to the '67:

1) Body
2) Front bumper assembly
3) Front grille
4) Rear bumper
5) Dash assembly
6) Steering wheel
7) Bucket seats
8) Base Interior

I have reviewed a test cast for technical accuracy and recommended modifications and I have to tell you it looks great!! The body is superb. All pieces exhibit a level of detail consistent with what you would find in a 1/25 scale kit today!!
The package...
The current projection is to begin selling the 1967 kit around mid-December. At this time only the hardtop will be offered but a convertible version of each is being considered. For delivery to the customer I plan to offer both the resin kit alone which will allow you to pursue a donor kit on your own and a "total package" kit that will include the resin kit and the donor kit packaged together and ready to assemble.
