How do I send you pictures of my Firebird ?

First let me tell you that I have a site standard limitation of five images per car. You do not have to submit five images of your car but you may submit up to that many. A good rule of thumb is to take 3 of the exterior and one each of the interior and the engine bay, but this is not a requirement. You can send me all five of the exterior if you wish or any other combination.
There are three ways that you can submit images to this site:

     1. Send me your Firebird photos via U.S. Mail at:
                          Jimmy Means
                          PSC 76 box 7258
                          APO AP 96319-7258
         I will only post five pictures of your car to the site but you may send me as many photos as you wish and I will pick the
         best five of the bunch. You also need to indicate if you wish to have the photos mailed back to you.

     2. A faster way is through e-mail if you can scan them or otherwise port them into your computer. There are several
         products on the market (i.e. the "Snappy") that will allow you to take "snapshots" from a VCR or camcorder and port
         them directly into your computer. Digital cameras work well too and are slowly becoming reasonably priced. Once
         you've got them in the computer you can send them to me via e-mail in any of the following formats: .JPG, .GIF,
         .BMP, .PCX, .PNG, .TGA, .TIF, and .WMF. The first three formats are the most desirable. Send them as
         attachments. There is no requirement for size or number of colors, however larger images are more desirable than
         smaller images. Get your images as close to full screen as possible without sacrificing image quality. A good size
         would be 800x600 in 16.7 million colors.

    3. Last and probably the quickest and easiest is to send me your images via FTP. This option is not yet available but will be
        in the coming weeks.

What kind of information would you like me to include about my Firebird ?

This is basically up to you. If you have already or are about to browse the site you can read the information included with the images and you should be able to get a feel for the kinds of information people are interested in reading. Ask yourself what kind of information would you like other Firebird fans to know about your car!? Heres a couple of suggestions to get you started, just remember you are not limited to answering just these questions:
           
        1. Engine/tranny - what kind of engine and transmission combo are you currently using? What factory high performance
            goodies have been installed on it? What aftermarket high performance parts have been installed? Has it been rebuilt
            either professionally or by yourself and if so what was done? Do you have a horsepower estimate?

        2. Interior - What factory options did your Firebird come with? Have you added any it didn't come with or deleted or
            modified any that that it did? What color is it? Is it the deluxe or standard? etc....

        3. Paint & Body - What color is your car? Is it the original factory color? What modifications have you made in these
            areas? Is the color a special order color from the factory?

        4. Misc - Do you have any 1/4 mile times for your car? Have you won any trophies or placed in any shows with your
            Firebird? Do you have an interesting story about how you found and bought your car? etc...

        5. Lastly if you have a homepage I will provide a link to it free of charge. If you would like to take advantage of this offer
            you need to provide me with the URL to your homepage and the name of it. I also need to know if you would like me
            to include your e-mail address on the site with your car. I don't post private information like e-mail adresses unless it is
            requested by the individual.

Why don't you offer free text only classifieds ?

When I decided to build this site I was struck with the idea of creating a For Sale Lot strictly for Firebirds and strictly image based. I decided this for several reasons. First there are already numerous sites available on the internet that allow free text ads and I found it boring and discouraging reading so many text ads and never having any idea what the car looked like. I think most people would like to see a picture of the car being sold along with the description. My goal is to have every Firebird that is For Sale on the internet with an available image to be displayed on this site where the people most interested in them can see them. Secondly I have seen a few sites that offered both types of ads and neither site had any with images to go with the text. That tells me that people will generally won't provide images if not required. Thus my For Sale Lot would wind up like the others: full of text only ads but no images. So instead I'm trying to bring you a quality top-notch classifieds section for a reasonable price. I hope you will agree with me...

How do I change my screen resolution to 800x600 at 24bpp True Color to view the site ?

          Before I discuss the procedures to change your resolution I would like to warn you that both your video card and your monitor must be capable of supporting a resolution of 800x600. If you are unsure about this then find out first before you make any changes. The problem that can occur is that your windows will start properly after you reboot but the screen will be too garbled to use and you will not be able to see the interface to change the resolution back to one that is useable. There is a workaround for this and I will post it here after some research. For windows 95 users, if you simply reboot in safe mode you will  be able to see the environment in a low resolution and will be able to change it back. This is not meant to scare you or dissuade you from changing your resolution I just don't want to cause problems for you.
          As far as exactly what you are changing I'm not going to go in-depth about it but to say that all you are changing is the video mode with which you view your software on your monitor. Standard resolutions are 640x480, 800x600, and 1024x768. Most modern monitors should be able to go up to 1024x768 which is not needed for this site (800x600 is a lower resolution than 1024x768 but higher than 640x480). I find a lot of people have their windows set to be viewed at 640x480 which is a low resolution usually because it is the default setting and either they don't know how to change it or don't know what the benefit will be. Basically the higher your resolution the smaller things appear on the monitor and the more space you have to view. If small icons and print bother you, you can usually set windows to use larger icons to compensate. So whats the difference you say? Well 800x600 is a higher resolution than 640x480 as I said so all of your graphics will be more highly detailed and colorful. If I have not satisfied your curiousity about this please feel free to send me an e-mail to ask any questions before you make any changes and I'll be glad to discuss it further with you.
          Now to tell you how to make the change I will discuss how to do it in Windows 95 since this should be the most common interface and then Windows 3.11 for those of you still using it:

          1. To change your screen resolution to 800x600 in Windows 95 follow these steps:
              
             I. Click the "start" button on your tool bar and select "settings" and the "control panel"....
            II. Double click the "display" icon...
           III. Click on the "settings" tab...
           IV. Under the heading "Desktop Area" move the slider from left to right till 800x600 is displayed...
            V. Next under the heading "Color Palette" click on the dropdown menu and select "True Color (24bit)"...
           VI. Click "ok" and if prompted reboot your computer.....  
          VII. Thats it!! You can change your video resolution to any combination you like this way, just be sure your video card
                 and monitor will support the resolution you choose.

          2. To change you screen resolution to 800x600 in Windows 3.11 is a bit harder:

            I. Find and double click your control panel icon....
           II. Double click on the "setup" icon....(note if your "setup" icon isn't located in your control panel window you will have
                to locate it to continue)
          III. You should see four settings one of which is your "display", click on the "File" menu and select something like
                "edit/change settings"
          IV. Once this has been selected the four settings that were just text before should now be contained in drop down
                menus, select the display drop down menu.....
           V. You will be face with an ominous list of choices, simply scroll down till you see something like 800x600 SVGA and
                click on it....
          VI. Click on "ok" to enter the new setting, you may be prompted to put one of your windows setup disks in your floppy
                drive, this is because the driver for the new resolution was not already installed and must be copied from the
                floppy, follow the instructions as they appear on the screen to install the new driver, once its finished you may be
                prompted to reboot your computer for the changes to take affect, do this and your finished!!!
         VII. You can change your video resolution to any combination you like this way just be sure your video card and monitor
                will support the resolution you choose. (Note if you wish to change your resolution back to its previous value you
                simply follow these same steps and select whatever resolution had appeared in the "display" category before you
                made the change. You should not be prompted to install drivers from a floppy because they are already installed
                from the previous setting.)

Again if you have any further questions please feel free to e-mail me with your questions and I'll do my best to answer them.
