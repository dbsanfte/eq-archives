click -> A 20th Anniversary Turbo Trans Am. Unlike Pontiacs first attempt in 1979 of placing a Turbo on a Firebird, the 20th Anniversary of the Trans Am brought us a car truly worthy of the occasion. The turbo was installed on a 3.8 Liter V-6 that was very comparable to the engine that was installed in the Grand Nationals a few years before. The attempt was an outstanding success and gave us the fastest Trans Am ever built. This car was the only one to ever serve pace care duty with absolutely no modifications. The 20th Anniversary cars would hold this title throughout the 90s till just a few years ago. As far as appearance the car received special badges on each fender and the front nose that proclaimed it as a 20th Anniversary. Click here to see one. These badges were trimmed in black with a gold background. With a very modest outward appearance and the lack of a charachteristic hood bulge these cars catch many an unwary driver by surprise.

click -> This beautiful burgundy GTA is owned by Tim Kohlmeyer. A 350c.i. L98 mill provides the power to motivate this bird. Factory performance goodies include a WS6 Performance Suspension, quick ratio steering (12.7:1), 4-wheel disc brakes, a JG-1 aluminum driveshaft, and a posi-trac rear with a 3.23 gear ratio. Tim has spent some time and money to have some modifications performed to improve its, already stout, performance.
     For better airflow numbers the heads have been milled and ported and a three-angle valve job was performed. The plenum has been ported, the runners were port-matched and a K&N air filter was installed. To take advantage of the new airflow a set of 24# ADS "Super Injectors" were also installed. A new computer and distributor were also added. Lastly a TB coolant bypass has been installed.
     Comforts are definitely not lacking on this Firebird despite its performance. Leather seats were a standard component on the GTA. It also has power steering, brakes, windows, locks, mirrors, seats, and even a power antenna and rear hatch. For convenience a set of stereo controls were installed in the steering wheel. Air conditioning and lumbar seats were also standard features.
     Tim's got several modifications planned despite its current condition. Such mods include an upgrade to Baer brakes, a high flow catalytic converter, ceramic coated headers, a shift kit & Corvette servo and synthetic fluids.
