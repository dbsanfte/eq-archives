This is an AMT kit of the 68' Firebird known as the "Tin Indian" raced by Bill Knafel. Recently T.D. Houser sent me the following information:

     "It's detail is immaculate ! Molded in high detail white plastic, it can be built in one of three set- ups: Race, HardTop Street, and Convertible street. I happen to believe that the model came out before the actual 1968 model Firebird because of subtle changes in body styling like Hide-a-way headlights and emblem placements. The model also happens to be a "400" car with the twin hood scoops and tach molded into the hood. The race mode also includes a set of hard plastic racing slicks molded to appear as if they were "wrinkling" from a hard launch. The true beauty of this kit lies in it’s convertible setup. The kit actually contains "Two Roofs". One hard Top and one immaculately detailed soft top in the up position. If the kit is built "Top Down", the rear tonneau cover is again immaculately detailed but also rather odd. It covers not only the roof well but the rear seat as well. All plastic "Brite Work" is superior in detail but has more of an aluminum look to it. The decal sheet hosts both Racing and "60’s Mod" motifs. I personally own more than 250 diecast metal and plastic autos and without a doubt this kit is the prize of my collection."

Pics courtesy of T.D. Houser

MPC Kit# 1-0822
click ->


click ->
This MPC 69' Firebird kit was the only kit ever offered that came with everything needed to build the ultra rare convertible Trans Am (only 8 made) sans the appropriate blue stripes. It came with the correct hood, air extractors, and rear spoiler. It also comes with a cool hood tach and realistic top boot. The modeler need only paint the body parts white (it comes molded in an ugly yellow) and apply a correct set of decals which can be found through hobby stores and suppliers. Decals included in the kit only include the sundancer decals shown on the cover and 2 North Carolina license plate decals. I have actually bought this model and built a 69' convertible Trans Am out of it. It came out really nice and looks exactly like a 69' TA convertible. I'll be posting a pic of it and my other Firebird models on this page soon.
This model usually sells for $50 - $125 depending on condition.
MPC Kit# 1-0781

click -> This MPC 69' Firebird model came with an interesting option. T-tops were never offered on any Firebird until 1976. This is an interesting look at what might have been. The body doesn't actually come the way it is depicted on the box cover (go figure). If you want the T-Tops you have to cut them out. All 69' MPC models had this option. Decals included are what's shown on the box top plus some sponsor decals (i.e. Cragar, Mr. Gasket etc...) and a small bird for the trunk lid that matches the one on the hood. This is yet another model that doesn't state that its a Trans Am but it does include all the necessary parts to build it as such (hood, air extractors, and rear spoiler). The kit is dated 1978.
