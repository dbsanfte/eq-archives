click -> 		This 74' Trans Am belongs to Crawford Smith. The car is painted Buccaneer Red and comes with a black custom cloth interior and a velour headliner. It also comes with the highly desirable 4-speed M-20 transmission. Even though the car was entirely original when bought and had only some rust around the rear window and lower rear quarter panles, Crawford decided to do some restoration work on it.

Here's an update from Crawford on the status of this restoration:

     I've had the chassis sent to a sandblasting shop where the complete underside, wheel wells, and trunk were sandblasted clean, and primed. I also took the hood, trunk lid, and front fenders to a stripping place where they will be dipped in a big tank and stripped, then dipped in a vat of stuff to neutralize any  rust, then primed.  I've ordered all new front and rear suspension parts, and will remove the front subframe, have it sandblasted and re-primed and painted chassis black before re-installing with the new parts. The body shop advised me that the rear quarters were a little more beat than we had thought, so a nice set of rust free California rear quarters were located and will be installed to complete the like new rear end. It was next to impossible to locate a set of 1974 Trans Am rear quarters. Seems like 1974 was a "one of a kind" year for the rear end. The car will pretty much sit out the winter at Martin Pontiac in Sheboygan, WI for the full restoration. I just dripped off a big truck load of new GM parts that they need to prime and paint, such as the new front valance, wheel well spoilers. New
weatherstripping and window felts are to compete the package also. I have also ordered a set of Corbeau front sport seats as the GM horsecollar seats just don't do it. For right now, I don't plan on having the T/A on the road in 1998, as there are a million and one fine details to attend to when putting it back together. I do plan on taking this T/A to many car shows and seeing how it does compared to the rest of the pack. Look for this T/A to be on the road in 1999.

                               
Crawford is Director of the Bandit Trans Am Club and Webmaster of the Bandit Trans Am Home Page. The club is dedicated to the 1976 - 1981 Special Edition Trans Am. If you own one or are a fan and haven't been by this site it is definitely a must see.

click -> 		This 79' Special Edition Trans Am is owned by Joseph Galate. Joe purchased the car in March of 1996 and recently decided to have some work done it. The plan is to have the engine compartment detailed and a ground up restoration performed on the suspension, brakes and fuel system. Following that will be a rebuild of the engine and a restoration of the interior.
     McBee CARS in Columbia, Missouri was chosen to handle the engine bay and undercarriage detailing. A complete engine bay restoration was performed along with the total replacement of the front and rear suspension. Polyurethane bushings and Koni shocks were installed during the process. All brake and fuel lines were also replaced with new stainless steel lines.
More info on the restoration of this car will be added as it becomes available. Until then you can go by Joseph's homepage entitled Jospeh Galate's 1979 S.E. Trans Am and browse even more pictures of his car and the restoration process.

click -> 		This Firebird belongs to Tom Griffith. Tom bought it for only $200. Tom is a member of The Trans Am Tribe and the vice-president of the club discovered it in San Francisco. It had been sitting behind a warehouse and was nearly covered with weeds. Tom had been looking for one to replace his previous 70' that had a bent frame. After the car was bought Tom paid $50 and had it towed home. The interior was completely trashed and both doors were missing. The car was without an engine although it did still have the transmission which was held up by a string!! The car had also had some aftermarket "pop-up" style roof hatches installed at sometime in its life. Tom cleaned out the car and washed it and began to put it back together.
          A set of classic Cragar five spoke rims were installed outfitted with a huge set of 15xN50 Mickey Thompson soft compund tires on the rear and G60s on the front. Next a black custom cloth interior was installed that had been removed from a 79' Trans Am. Also installed on the inside was a tilt steering wheel and a Kenwood stereo with twin equalizers powering a set of 10" sub-woofers and a pair of 6x9s. The sun dried dash and center console were dyed black to match the rest of the interior.
          Moving to the engine compartment Tom sanded it and repainted it and then installed a healthy 70' Pontiac 350 that had been in his last bird. The engine was painted black and a cool set of black Mickey Thompson valve covers were installed along with a pair of Hooker headers. The transmission is a turbo 400 and was kept. Lastly, in order to fit a Trans Am shaker scoop on the engine, Tom had to cut out a regular hood. A fiberglass L88 style scoop is in the plans for future modifications for the car. Photos of the finished product will be posted here when I get em'.

click -> 		Although its a little hard to tell the Firebird in the pics is a 1972 Formula undergoing a very thorough restoration. The owner is Will Baker. The story Will sent me was a little too long to include on this page so I have put the meat and potatoes of it here. To read the full story you can click here.

     "The car was originally a 400-4bbl (of course) with TH400 auto and 3.08 open rearend. Options included hideaway wipers, deluxe interior, AC, power brakes, power steering, deluxe exterior trim, rally guages, and not much else. It even had the plain-jane 14x7 wheels with "dog-dish" hubcaps. I hesitate to call what I'm doing to it a "restoration" as I'm veering significantly away from stock. The goal is to maintain a stock look, while really beefing up the performance. To that end I've had a set of 15x8 and 15x10 steel wheels fabricated that will retain those original hubcaps & trim rings. I upgraded to '79 TA swaybars (1-1/4" in front and 3/4" rear), '79 TA WS6 rear leaf springs, Vette Brakes & Products (VB&P) two-piece aluminum hubbed brake rotors (clamped by Performance Friction Carbon Metallic brake pads), VB&P upper tubular control arms, boxed stock lower control arms, HO Racing front springs cut 3/4 coil, PST "Polygraphite" control arm, swaybar, and body mount bushings, Global West tubular subframe connectors, and Global West "Del-a-Lum" rear leaf spring shackles & front spring eye mount bushings. Finishing off the suspension are Koni adjustable gas shocks at all four corners. 
     The engine & drivetrain consist of the original '72 400 block crank and rods, fully blueprinted and balanced, bored +.030, and 0" decked. The rods were weight matched, beam polished, shot-peened and honed for oversized lightweight (shh! Don't tell anyone) Big Block Chevy wristpins that were fitted into simlarly honed oversized, dished TRW forged pistons. The cam is a Comp Cams custom grind (232 @ .050 duration, .521 lift with 1.65 Harland Sharp roller rockers, 112 degree lobe separation, intake centerline at 111 degrees. Should produce performance similar to a RA-IV cam and I'm also using Rhoads lifters so that I can run the A/C). I'll probably change this cam to something a bit more mild in the next year depending on how it runs. All engine bolts are ARP, including the polished stainless ones on the outside of it. I also ground all the identifying markings off the Edelbrock intake manifold and painted it engine color. I purchased a Ram Air aircleaner and all the necessary pieces to convert the car to the factory Ram Air system. I'm also using a set of '70 #13 heads that I ported myself. They breath into HO Racing Tri-Y headers and a Flowmaster 2.5" mandrel bent exhaust system modified for a Dr. Gas X-style crossover. The carb is an 800 cfm Q-jet modified for my engine and fuel delivery will be handled by the stock 3/8" fuel lines and a Mallory Comp 140 electric pump, filter, & pressure regulator. The pressure regulator will be mounted where the stock fuel pump would go, allowing me to use the stainless-steel replacement stock carb inlet line and keep the "stock at first glance" theme going. Even the alternator is the original unit, but rebuilt with a 105 amp kit. Ignition will be handled by a custom-curved HEI and a Crane HI-6 capacitive discharge box with rev limiter.
The tranny is a TH400 out of a station wagon with the extra big clutch pack (5 composite, 5 steel discs). It's been converted to full manual operation (but retaining stock console shifter), is driven by a Coan 2800-3000 stall converter, and is cooled by a Derale tranny cooler with built in 500 CFM electric thermostatically controlled fan. The rearend consists of the original 10-bolt housing with new axles, an Eaton clutch-type posi unit and 3.23 (for now) GM gears. I may switch to 3.73 after I've gotten some long road trips I want to take out of the way - it all depends on the performance I can wring out of it with the 3.23s.
     The interior will be restored to stock, with the addition of a Kenwood 400 watt stereo system including 6-disc trunk mounted CD changer. No holes will be cut in the stock interior for speakers or anything else and it will all (except the tape deck and EQ) be hidden from view (unless you look in the trunk). I've covered all of the interior sheetmetal surfaces with Dynamat for extra sound-deadening and will install a baffle-board directly behind the rear seat to mount a 10-inch free-air subwoofer and also fully isolate the passenger compartment from the trunk. I'm also adding a factory 8K tach to the rally guage instrument cluster. "

     "Neat Page! Thanks for the information, as well as the inspiration. My name is Peter Abraham, and I live in Tucson, Arizona. I own a 1973 Pontiac Firebird Esprit. The car was originally equipped with a 350 CID Pontiac Engine, TH350 Trans, and 10 Bolt Rear. I bought the car when I was 16 back in 1985. I drove the car as my daily driver throughout High School, and College. I am finally in a position in life to build the car how I always wanted. I am in the thick of turning the car into a killer street machine. The hardware specs are as follows.

1) 1970 400 CID RAM AIR III Engine #12 Heads.
2) TH350 AutoMatic (My Dad is building this for me...He is a Hot Rodder from "Way Back")
3) 4.10 10 Bolt Posi.
4) Hughs 10 Inch Converter.

My goal is took construct a Solid 12 Second Street Car. The pictures were taken when I had the Car Stripped with Plastic Media. I am currently finishing my bodywork....Next I will remove the Sub-Frame for powder coating, and rebuilding....This Car is totally dissasembled! If it could come off it has! My goal is to Finish the car by the year 2000!"

Thanks for the Fun.
Peter Abraham
