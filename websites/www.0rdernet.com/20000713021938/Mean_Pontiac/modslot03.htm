1970

Monogram Kit# 2012




This is a 1/32 scale Monogram kit of a custom 1970 Trans Am. The kit is almost entirely molded in blue. Chrome accessories are limited to the mags, the front grill, rear bumper, tailights, shifter, and air cleaners. Their is no design for a shaker setup with this kit. Instead you get an engine topped by dual four barrels with seperate triangular chrome air cleaners that stick out of the hood. The decals include what you see on the box (notice the white "Trans Am" decal that is affixed to the door rather than the fender) and a liscense plate reading "Trans Am". The text on the side of the box claims the engine is a 455. The wheel spoilers and trunk lid spoiler are molded into the body. Being 1/32 the kit pieces are tiny and few. This one could be put together in just a few hours with your kids. The kit is dated 1983.
Monogram Kit# 2794




This is a Monogram kit for the 1970 Trans Am. Its the larger 1/24 scale. I have bought and built a couple of them. The model comes molded in white. Chrome pieces include the rear bumper, front grill, a set of Rally IIs, and the top end on the engine. Decals include a complete set of either blue or white stripes depending on whether you want to paint it white or Lucerne blue. Each set includes the large center stripe for the trunk roof and hood, the small bird on the nose, and "Trans Am" decals for the rear spoiler and each fender. A single Illinois license plate is also included. The kit is dated 1991. An interesting note: the box top features a short paragraph on the Trans Am and erroneously claims the car was powered by a 455. The best engine available in 1970 was the Ram Air IV 400. The 455 became the standard powerplant in 1971.

You may have also seen the kit of the 1968 Firebird Bill raced under the same name on Models Lot#1. 1972

MPC Kit# 1-7215-225






This is a 1/25 scale MPC kit of a 72' Formula (box identifies it as a Firebird). It can be built three ways, stock, drag, or custom. The kit includes a very unique set of "Royal" decals. The term "Royal" is famous with Pontiacs. It was the name of a famous dealership that had a reputation for modifying factory built Pontiacs with lots of high performance parts at the customers request for an added expense. The kit also includes a lot of extra parts that provide detail depending on which version you choose to build like a roll bar, slicks, custom headers etc... This is a very rare, collectable, and expensive kit.

1973

Doyusha Kit# DBS-7-9800






This '73 Trans Am model was made in Japan, manufactured by Doyusha, until it was discontinued sometime in late '97. Its a very large scale model at 1/12. I was able to buy one in Japan before they were discontinued. It was an expensive model costing $75 to $100 in U.S. dollars. Mint examples go for about $130 now. The model is highly detailed, molded well and comes with a good decal set so I think its worth the money. As you can see the hood, doors, and trunk all open and the front wheels will move back and forth when the steering wheel is turned.

MPC Kit# 1-7315-225




This is an MPC kit of a 73' Formula (box identifies it as a Firebird). This kit builds one of three way's, Buck Baker Grand Am oval track racer, street machine, or stock. This kit features racing side exhaust, authentic Buck Baker decals, big racing tires, a front spoiler, destroked 400c.i. engine (303c.i.) headlight, taillight, and license plate covers, open racing wheels or chrome mags, super detailed racing interior with full roll cage, racing bucket seat, steering wheel and protective netting, rear window straps, and a rear spoiler. The kit is dated 1973. Like the kit above this is also a very rare kit and very collectable.
