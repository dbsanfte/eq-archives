This 1969 Firebird resides somewhere in Finland. It has been sold recently and the current owner is unknown. The information provided below was the last info I received on this Firebird.

"Engine: 69" 400" +030 stock crank. TRW forged pistons, polished and shotpeened stock rods with ARP bolts. Compcams 280 Magnum Cam. Edelbrock Torker II intake and Holley 750 vac carb. Moroso Air cleaner and valve covers. Mallory Unilite distributor with Accel Super Coil. Coyote Headers.

Heads: 69" 72cc (milled 1.5mm), K-liners, 1,77/2,11 valves, Compcams Dual springs, Crane retainers, Harlan Sharp 1.65 roller rockers. 

Wheels are 16" with VR 225/50 tires (from 89 WS6 Trans/Am). Trans is TH350 with stock converter and rear is 3.08 10 bolt between 3.50-3.90."

This 1983 Trans Am belongs to Ross Meyers of Templestowe, Australia. The car was purchased in 1983 already converted to right hand drive from another Australian for $19,000 Australian (about $15,000 US). The previous owner had swapped the 305 for a 350 but kept the original cross-fire injection setup. He'd also added a new cam that proved to be to much for the car and caused it to barely idle during cold weather. Needless to say it didn't take long before Ross got tired of the car stalling so a new cam was selected and provided by a local cam company (Crow Cams). During the installation of the new cam he found out the heads had some cracks so new ones were bought and installed. A new Hypertech chip, a rear wing, stiffer suspension bushings, and host of new stereo equipment were also added. Ross has posted a best time of 15.47 @ 88mph.
