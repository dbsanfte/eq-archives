By decree of the members present at the formation of the guild known as Dragons of Kel`Dar we acknowledge our government herewith and declare ourselves unified with all the powers and judgment of formal assembly. Recognition of name shall be observed herewith as Dragons of Kel`Dar.


We of Dragons of Kel`Dar bind ourselves together in friendship and camaraderie for the purpose of adventure and fun. We stake our claim to the principles of honor and valor. We abide by the law set forth in the Writ of Dragons of Kel`Dar and honor our leaders with our duty. We shun spoilers of fun. We do not propose war with any other. We will defend our members and the honor of Dragons of Kel`Dar at all costs. We will uphold the virtues of the Guild's way in all things.


The Charge of Dragons of Kel`Dar describes and ordains the individual duties of each rank and office within the Guild of Dragons of Kel`Dar.


The Guild Leader

The Guild Leader is the highest-ranking position within the Guild. His/her duties are to lead Dragons of Kel`Dar with honor and valor, to oversee the actions of all members, to uphold the Writ of The Dragons of Kel`Dar, to regulate the guild's relations with foreign guilds, and to settle disputes within the ranks. The Leader may implement any actions they deem worthy. The Leader may perform promotion and induction ceremonies. Upon the advice of the Council of Justice of Dragons of Kel`Dar, the Leader may implement his/her recommendation for action against a member. The Leader has the right to veto any action of the other members with the exception of a decision by majority of the Council of Justice. The members of the guild agree to abide by the leader decisions or decree.

The guild leader is elected by popular vote of all members in the guild, with the exception of those in probation. Every (4) months, on the First of the month, a popular election shall be held to confirm or replace the Guild Leader.

The Council of Justice

The Council of Justice has 3 members with distinct roles but still acting as unity to make decisions concerning the guild. Councilors are elected by popular vote and confirmed or replaced, individually or completely, every 4 months, on the First day of the month. Councilors must be Officers before they can ascend to the Council of Justice.

The roles performed by the Council of Justice are recruitment, liaison with the Xegony Alliance and coordination of raids and events. In order to fulfill these roles, Councilors can request the assistance of volunteers (Officers or Members).

The Council of Justice or the Councilors individually hold the leadership of Dragons of Kel`Dar in the absence of the Guild Leader. Their decisions must always come from consensus, and their discussions are private unless they choose to make them public. Even by consensus, the Councilors cannot invite new members who have not been previously approved by the Guild Leader and/or by popular vote. The Council of Justice can veto a decision made by the Guild Leader if they reach unanimity in that decision.

The Councilors may rotate in their duties or perform more than one role.

A Councilor can be dismissed by the Guild Leader or the remaining Councilors in case of inappropriate conduct or lack of interest/dedication in performing his/her role as a councilor.

The Officers

It is the Officers duty to provide advice to the Council of Justice upon their request. This may include holding one or more voluntary duties, assisting councilors with duties, reporting on guild status, reporting on foreign guild activity, reporting on guild morale and welfare, reporting on guild activities and events, and any and all other needs as deemed necessary by the council. The Officers may perform ceremonies to invite new members if the councilors and Leader are unavailable.

The Members

The members of Dragons of Kel`Dar have voting rights to confirm/replace the Guild Leader and Council of Justice, as well as the removal or invitation of a guild member. They must go to the Council of Justice to request assistance or provide feedback on guild rules and structure.


Recruitment

All members are encouraged to recruit. Members should refer applicants to the recruitment officer. The officer will inform the interested party of our theme and what we represent and contact a Class Officer. A Class Officer is a high level member of that particular class who will evaluate the recruit in his/her knowledge and character. If a potential candidate has a continued interest in membership they should be supplied with a copy of our charter, review it and fill out the application. After evaluation of the Recruitment Officer and Class Officer, all members will be encouraged to vote. During the recruitment process, the recruit’s name and information will be made available so members can talk to him before casting their votes. At least one member of Dragons of Kel`Dar must support all applications with letters of recommendation, either at the beginning or at the end of the application process. The Council of Justice or the Guild Leader can veto the membership of a recruit approved by popular vote with a valid reason.

Should the recruit be elected to become a member, he will serve Dragons of Kel`Dar on a probationary period to be determined by the Leader or Council on a case by case basis but no less than 1 week. During this probationary period, the new member will be afforded every right and responsibility as that of any member of Dragons of Kel`Dar, except voting rights. An initiate can be removed from the guild during his/her probationary period for any reason and without advanced notice. Any items given to Dragons of Kel`Dar or its members will not be returned as they are considered guild property.

Our Moral Standards

The Members of all rank must conduct themselves with honor at all times. They must carry the name of Dragons of Kel`Dar with pride and power, spread the values of the guild and always serve his/her brothers and sisters in loyalty and friendship.

Marriages will be observed and respected by all of Dragons of Kel`Dar in and outside of our ranks. Marriages of a character between more than two persons will not be recognized. Marriages between a member and a non-member and between different races are allowed. Marriage does not require a formal ceremony. If persons wish to make known their marriage in order to have it acknowledged and recognized by Dragons of Kel`Dar, said persons should inform the Council of their status. All members will recognize divorce. It is the responsibility of the divorcees to inform the Council of their status if they wish to make known their separation.

Multiple Characters, Multiple Guilds

The presence of other characters in other guilds is strongly discouraged in Dragons of Kel`Dar. We believe your loyalty should lie with your family and confidential guild information may be jeopardized. If a member should decide to request membership in other guilds with a different character, he/she should contact the Council or the Leader immediately. Councilors and/or Leader will vote privately on allowing multiple guilds for that particular member.

Members with characters in other guilds will not be accepted into the Council of Justice but may hold officership in Dragons of Kel`Dar, at the discretion of Council and Leader.

Promotion of a Member

Officer status will be granted to members of Dragons of Kel`Dar by decision of the Council of Justice and/or the Guild Leader. Promotions are based on dedication and services to the guild, time in the guild, advancement of your character and relationship with the other guild members.

Guild Meetings

Dragons of Kel`Dar members are requested to join monthly guild meetings, usually held at the Lake Rathe Arena or East Commonlands Tunnel. During the meeting, the Guild Leader and Councilors will have an agenda of topics to be discussed, and will request participation of all members. Duels, off topic comments and unrelated emotes are considered inappropriate behavior and a member may be requested to leave the meeting.

The Councilors or Guild Leader may call additional meetings to address emergency issues.

The Council of Justice will meet with the Guild Leader bi-weekly in chat room. Officers and volunteers may be invited to attend some or part of these meetings.

In-Guild Trades

Trade and donations between guildmates is encouraged. However, charging for any spell casting that does not require a reagent, such as SoW, teleports, Resurrection, Buffs, is considered inappropriate conduct.

Dragons of Kel`Dar maintains a guild vault (mule characters) usually around the East Commonlands tunnel. Donations to the guild vault are appreciated but keep in mind the items you donate will become property of the guild and may not be available should you need them in the future. Guild members are welcome to borrow any item from the vault, provided they do not sell or trade the item and will return it to the vault as soon as they are able to buy/get a better item for themselves.

OOC Communication

By joining Dragons of Kel`Dar, one will commit to participation in all OCC communication via e-mail or message board, or any tools used by the guild during your membership. Ignorance of these rules, lotto/raid rules, recruits in evaluation or calendar of events is not acceptable.

Leaving Dragons of Kel`Dar


The Councilors are requested to submit their resignation to the Guild Leader, and the Guild Leader should submit his/her resignation to the Council of Justice either in person or via email. All members are requested to submit their resignations to the Council or Leader privately. After a brief interview, that member will be encouraged to share his/her decision with the whole guild.

Dragons of Kel`Dar is a family and a commitment. Should a member choose to leave and later decide to return, it will be up to the Guild Leader and/or Council of Justice to restore or not his membership. A probationary period may apply.

Members who leave the guild and return will not have membership restored more than twice. After that, he/she will be subject to the normal recruitment process. If this member chooses to leave once again, a future application for membership will not be accepted.

Officers and Councilors who leave the guild twice will also lose their officership.

Removing a Guild Member

Dragons of Kel`Dar membership may be terminated in case of inappropriate conduct or disrespected to the rules of this charter. Some cases where this may occur are (but not limited to): aggressive cursing or disrespect to any citizen of Norrath, proven Kill-Stealing or Ninja-looting, stealing from guild members or guild mules, providing other guilds or players with confidential guild information or any other conduct that could bring shame to our guildmates or stain our reputation.

Any member regardless of rank may recommend the removal of a guild member to Council or Leader. Detailed information and proof of inappropriate conduct will be required. Screenshots or game logs may be acceptable proof, as well as testimony of other parties.

The Council of Justice and/or Guild Leader may choose to remove a member immediately and without previous consideration if the guild reputation or welfare is at risk. Depending on the accusation, however, the member may be invited to a chat room interview with Councilors and/or Leader to provide explanation before a decision is made on his membership.
