Front Page news
Reposted for periodic re-reading.

The council and the officers have reviewed, discussed, and now present several new rules and guidelines for Dragons of Kel`Dar.  We expect these will make the guild a tighter family, with better communication, and fewer disputes.  Please accept and abide by them in the spirit in which they are intended.

-----------------------------------------------------------
Board reading is now Mandatory, too bad if you do not like mandatory rules.  Many of the guilds officers spend a LOT of time making things work for the rest of us.  If you cant take a few minutes a week to catch up on guild news, then you need to find another guild.

Mandatory topics will be posted in GUILD NEWS, THE ASSEMBLY, and FAMILY INN.  If you do not have a registered account on the guild boards (and on the ROSTER)within TWO WEEKS then your characters will be summarily de-guilded.

Epic fever and arguing about loot have gotten out of hand.  If you argue about loot or an epic drop on guild sends, you will be disciplined, up to and including loss of guild tag.  We expect everyone to act in a mature manner, and if loot and greed for an epic is more important than your guild status, so be it.

We STRONGLY Suggest that you examine your actions and motives.  And if you require help for your epic, that you follow these guidelines.

- Before you schedule an epic raid, you must have attended at least 3 guild epic raids.  
- You must wait 2 weeks before scheduling another epic raid after the guild helps you once.
- Before requesting an epic raid, you must have completed all steps you can do on your own or with a single group.
- Only one of your characters may request epic help. After that character has finished the quest, another may request help.
- You may gather regular groups to help with small epic encounters as much as people are willing to attend, but we suggest using the Assembly, not the calendar. Save the calendar for raids or guild events.
- Putting pressure on people to help with your epic encounters or whining on guild sends about lack of support for your quest may affect your membership with DoK.
-One request for help with a specific piece every two weeks (this means if you dont get your piece you may ask again, this will not penalize people with very hard quests).  Exceptions will be made for rare/difficult camps.
-We STRONGLY suggest that you lead your own "events" and not request others to lead them for you (exception are very hard zones, like Chardok, or POA, this does NOT include hate and Fear).
-Minimum level is the minimum level set by verrant for your quest, if you cannot complete it till 50, dont ask for major help till you achieve 50.
-Every request for help will indebt you to a similar service to the guild (lead a raid, do lotto capitan, help a lower level member with a CR or quest).



We also suggest that the guild utilize the services of our master Jewelers for making of resist gear.  We have several jewelers with a skill in excess of 200
Riffraffs (250)
Wendaar
Lillypaws (250)
Lyvja
Inari(197)
Guenhwyvar (~180)

These crafters can make all but the hardest Platinum, or Velium jewelry.  Only very difficult combines should be referred to Riffraffs or Lillypaws (except during designated JC hours).

We strongly encourage voluntary donations to our traders. That is not limited to Jewelcraft. Skills are expensive to improve, and an extra bar of metal, cash, gem, molds, anything you can contribute will definitely help the trader, they have spent a LOT of time and effort honing their skills, which you are getting the direct benefit of.

Recruitment Changes
Effective today (Sunday 16th), Dragons of Kel'Dar has a new set of rules for recruitment. Please see below.

Note that all recruits currently on the waiting list or being voted on will not have these rules applied to them.


1) Recruitment Freeze effective immediately. As usual, those recruits who are being voted or are in the waiting list may continue to be voted on, but no new applicants will be accepted at this time, saved exceptions approved by council and myself.

2) The current applicants and any future recruits we consider will still need 15 positive votes. Nothing changes about that.

3) Sponsors are from now on NECESSARY. (does not apply to current recruits). That means in order to even apply to DoK, recruits must have a DoK member sponsoring them.

4) Sponsors whose applicants behave in poor manner (probation OR removal) will not be allowed to sponsor another applicant for 2 months.

5) Sponsors who have 3 applicants behave in poor manner (probation) or 2 applicants removed from the guild will not be allowed to sponsor any more.

6) Recruitment officer will keep track of who sponsored each member in the guild.

7) Any guild member can only sponsor TWO applicants at a time. Once they are guilded (or rejected) they can sponsor more.

These rules were created to provide us with a better filter for considering membership and also to stop the flow of new recruits for a while until we get to know better those who have been recently guilded.

Sponsorship is not to be taken lightly. As you can see from the rules above, sponsors will be accountable for the problems their recruits cause. So think hard before sponsoring someone you hardly know - it may cause you a lot of headaches.
