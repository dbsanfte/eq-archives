The Enchanter's Many Forms
by Juliet Mindwalker
(download in .doc format)

The art of illusion is a highly technical subject, which would take many years of research for anyone other than a High Elf enchantress to fully understand all of its aspects so I will paraphrase it.  Illusion is the ability to take the shape of people or inanimate objects from your environment.  There are several reasons why one would want to change into something else or someone else.  The corrupt guards of Qeynos hate most if not all non-human races.  If I walk into town as my beautiful self, they scorn me and mock my very existence.  If though I change into one of their favorite ladies of the night, they admire me.  Some even go as far as liking me.  Also, when was the last time you visited Oggok as a Huuuman, as the horrid, non-intelligent Ogres call that average group of underlings from Freeport and Qeynos?  To summarize, the main reason for illusions is to become the ordinary and not the extraordinary, like me.  I have to change races for I am easily picked out of a crowd wherever I go.

The second reason for the wondrous art of illusion is the ability to morph into a common object of the environment.  Among the craft keepers this aspect is called a minor illusion.  This illusion is used to hide.   If someone is chasing you and you are not a fast runner, it is nice to be able to run into an inn and morph into the shape of a bed. Even if the attacker knew what you were, nine times out of ten he is going to leave you alone because he would look awful silly cursing and destroying a bed, which supposedly belongs to someone else.  The innkeeper would most likely have him thrown in prison for that. Actually, the innkeeper, who most likely is an ex-soldier, would probably kill the attacker without drawing a sweat.   Also, how many of you spell casters wish the dirty animals of the forest or wondering orcs would leave you in peace when you are trying to refill your mana supply?  Enchanters just need to cast minor illusion.  Most of the time this works.  However, I have met some foolish Highmen enchanters who talked to their party as a minor illusion.  Even the dumb goblins are not ignorant enough to believe you are a lamppost then.   Dang, men!  If you are not smart enough to figure this out then you ought to train in Felwithe with the ladies where we know better!  I am sorry if that just offended some of the lower life forms but it is the truth.  Sometimes even the truth hurts.

Now the primary reason for illusion, flirting.  Yes you read correctly. The High Elf women have for years been able to get any man to do what they want just by morphing into whatever that particular man finds attractive.  The hardheaded, hard-hitting barbarians from the tundra would not find a Gnome too appealing.  Nor would an Erudite find an, and excuse my Troll, inki very attractive.  If you want what they have you have to persuade them that you are giving them what they want.  So far, for you novice enchanters, I have seen more men persuaded by a scantly clad barbarian lass.   So, if you are looking for a fun time or if you want a gift, I would recommend that race.

One last note, some other races use the skill for playing tricks.  High Elves would never stoop the to level of using years of dedication for fun and teasing but I hear the Gnomes love to.  There are many books in their libraries on this subject so I recommend you look there for information.  I will say what I have observed other less mature enchanters doing for the purpose of this guide though.  I have seen enchanters lead a person into town to a certain location and loose their trailer.  Why or how you might ask.  Here is what I saw and scorned him for doing.  He would run around a corner and morph into a race so far from his previous race that the trailer would run right by without noticing.  This is utterly shameful and I refuse to go into detail.  It will be Christmas in the plane of hate before you catch one as noble as I doing that disgraceful stunt.

Enough comment on that twist to the exalted art of illusion.  I wish all enchanters well in their research and studies.  May someday you excel to the stature of the High Elves.  Tunare bless you and good journeys.
