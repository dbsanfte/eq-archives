I can appreciate your argument against abortion for the detrimental effects on society as opposed to the fetus itself. However, I disagree that our obligation to non-adult individuals (in this case, the fetus) can be so casually defined as what "matters" to that non-adult individual. Do we allow our children to eat chocolate cake for dinner? Do we allow them to ride bicycles without helmets or play tag in the street?

We allow adults (or should) to decide appropriate individual courses of action, but deny our children the freedom to make those decisions for the simple reason that a child's potential to make informed, adult judgments has not been fully realized. Selectively failing to honor what "matters" to a child is often the mark of a responsible adult. If that weren't true, the word "No" wouldn't be the most-used word in our house (my son is two).

And do we have the right to end the life of a suicidal individual, even though doing so may not "matter" to that individual?

Although a blob of human cells may not consciously object to the cessation of its life, every individual -- born or unborn, healthy or ill, retarded or sane -- has the right to develop to his or her full potential. The "right" to interrupt human growth should not exist at any arbitrary point on the human development continuum.

These points are made, of course, assuming its death doesn't matter to the referred-to blob of human cells. But what if it does matter to an *older* fetus (which are also routinely aborted)? While largely uneducated as to its cognitive abilities, I remain unconvinced that losing its life would not matter. Having carried a child, I find it hard to believe that although simple gestures such as poking or stroking my stomach elicited kicks and squirms and retaliatory pokes, the attempt to end its life would elicit no response at all. Survival is an instinct that even the fetus seems to possess.

Anyway, AB, I'm just passionate about your web site! It's one of my new favorites. You might check out Atheists for Life -- I don't have the URL on me, but can find it if you'd like. Also, PLAGAL [Pro-Life Alliance of Gays & Lesbians --I think is the name--] would make a great Alt Con link.

All my best, Stephanie Herman
