Hi AB,

Regardless of my usage of the word "matters" in my previous post, my point was actually that it doesn't *matter* what "matters" to the fetus. Kids make poor judgments and we, as adults, rarely make our decisions regarding their welfare taking into account what "matters" to them -- if we did, they'd eat ice cream cones and play video games all day.

But just so we're not talking apples and oranges, here: Is your argument, then, that abortion is not murder if performed before the nervous system is fully developed? It's been argued that the embryo isn't *really* alive until at least the membranes break down and separate parental chromosomes (18-24 hours), or until the activation of paternal DNA (2 days), or until implantation in the uterus (6 days). You say it's at least until the nervous system is formed. The problem with all of these defintions of when real human life begins is that they are simply arbitrary and capricious. The only unequivocal point at which to define life's inception is conception.

Yes, on the larger issue, regarding the negative societal consequences of abortion, we are in agreement, but I'm not sure I want to let our smaller, semantic arguments away like water under the bridge as our reasoning will help us both understand the abortion issue, as a whole, more precisely.

Yours, Stephanie Herman
