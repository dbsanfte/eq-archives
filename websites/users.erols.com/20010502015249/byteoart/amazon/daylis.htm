Daylis Summerbreeze

About Me

I was born into a very large Wood Elf family. We lived peacefully in the shadows of the towering trees of Greater Faydark. I lived a simple life with my brothers and sisters. We relied on the blessing of our great mother, Tunare. She blessed us with abundant food, and the power of Druid.

 

I remember many nights gathered around the ancient stone circle, listening to elder Druids tell fantastic stories. They spoke of far off lands, great heroes, and honorable quests. I longed to one day explore Tunare's great creation, and become a hero myself. As soon as I was able I joined the local guild, the soldiers of Tunare, and set about defending my homeland, and learning my craft.

Once I reached my second circle of power, the call of the wild was just too much for me to resist. I said my good byes, and embarked on my first journey. I ran though the Butcher Block Mountains, and found a boat headed for Freeport. I had never crossed the Ocean of tears before. I was so excited that I could hardly wait for the boat. The trip was long and agonizing, I was sick many times, but after an eternity it was over. I exited the boat as fast as I could. I found myself in a strange land. So many people, dirt, houses, bums, odd smells, and guards that looked at me apprehensively.

This was not what I had expected at all! Everything was so bizarre, no flowers, no animals, no smiles, and no beautiful trees. There were a few shriveled up dead trees, how awful! I ran from that city as fast as I could. Soon I was in the desert of Ro. Out of the pan and into the fire. Freeport was bad, but the desert was unbearable. It was a desolate land with nothing but sand and horrible creatures.

I prayed to Tunare for help, and a kind jackal came to my aid. He led me to a fishing village, where I met the most amazing people. Barbarians, they were so huge and strong! I had never seen such a thing before. A nice fisherman, that looked like a towering oak, explained to me which way I needed to go. I thanked him and went on my way. I found my way to the commons.

Finally a nice place: Trees! Bears! Wolves! I continued through Kithicor forest and finally to Rivervale. It was here that I would make my new home. I lived with the kind Tagglefoot family, and helped them with their farm. They continued my druid training, and taught me the ways of Karana.

Due to my previous training in the soldiers of Tunare, I decided to join the Rivervale Wall Squad, and defended the city from goblins, and orcs. I stayed here until the fourth circle. I felt very powerful now, and had to journey on to test my new strength. I decided to go explore the Karanas. The Karanas were the most wonderful place that I had ever been. It was so full of life and wide open. I ran and ran with the spirit of the wolf inside me.

I protected and defended travelers from the many giant bugs, griffawns, and those vile lions. I have been all over Norrath now, and met many peoples: From the home of those awesome Barbarians in the north, to the hideous Trolls in the south, the sinister Kerrans in the east, to the cute little Gnomes in the west. I am blessed that I now travel with the Amazonian sisters. I look forward to the many adventures in the future.

My Guild
I am the Educational Leader for the Amazonian Sisters, a guild exclusively for women. We are here to help and support all citizens of Norrath, but we feel that we women need to stick together. If you are interested in knowing more about our guild or joining, please check out the page.
