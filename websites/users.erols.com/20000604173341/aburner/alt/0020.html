Amazing! I am in almost total agreement with everything said here [minor exceptions to follow]. In your economics section I would suggest one other book: "America: What Went Wrong" whose author I cannot bring to mind at this instant - it grew from a newspaper series into a book & points out much of what is wrong by design with our current financial laws.

On abortion - I disagree. While I would agree that certain limits should be placed on abortion, I cannot and will not take an an anti-abortion stance. I am of the fundamental position that, as long as my actions are free of malicious intent or actual harm towards another, then I should be free to continue as such without interference from outside parties, and vice-versa. To wit: Tho abortion may be the wrong choice for my family, it is NOT our place to tell someone else that it's the wrong choice for them.

This stems from a prevelant "you-live-your-life, I'll-live-mine" theme on which just about all of my socio-political stances are based. Hey, you asked.

-Dan Dise
