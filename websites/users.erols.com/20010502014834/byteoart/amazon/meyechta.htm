Meyechta

About Me

As an orphan, one might think that I'd be down on life. Such was never the case, as I am a wood elf, and our little community was very close-knit. When my parents died, I was left to fend for myself.

Fending for myself proved easy, however. When I was still young, families would see me and pull me in for the night, feed me, give me their leftover clothes. When I started to grow older, those same families remembered my habit of humming to myself as I helped wash dishes, scrub the floors, and do a little needlework. I didn't cook. I am a lousy chef. They remembered my singing, and knew I had talent. In my early years of puberty I found myself whisked off to the bardic guild to be primed for a life of song, music, story, and wit.

Since then, I have travelled all over Norrath to gather stories and songs and share them with the world. I've found people I adore and people I would love to spend time with, but I've also learned that my years of being the bumble bee that samples from every house's hospitality, I'd find it hard to be the burrower that settles into one home, one husband, and one way of life. I must sample them all.

I've learned that even the bird must join a flock, and where in childhood I flew with the families of my community, now I soar with my guildsisters...the Amazons!

My Stories
My stories are what makes me unique. Though I sing a great deal and gather histories, as do the rest of my profession, I am known for my knack at weaving a web of fantasy for the weary traveller to rest by. One of my favorite memories involves sitting around Rivervale telling the story of the Wise Fools to those who gathered to listen. All work and no play makes for a very tuckered out fighter, hm?

My Guild
I am the Guild Leader for the Amazonian Sisters, a guild exclusively for women. We are here to help and support all citizens of Norrath, but we feel that we women need to stick together. If you are interested in knowing more about our guild or joining, please check out the page.

My Trades

In addition to being a student of life, I am also an aspiring fisherwoman and tailor.I have dabbled at drinking, and touched upon the fine art of begging, but that is all because of my past and profession, I'd say. I'm always on the lookout for silks...and am striving to learn how to make whips for my Sisters, to be used as a guild symbol for initiations.
