Where into this do you place the morality of killing a helpless human? I do agree with you that for a period of time, the baby is just a mass of cells. And during that time the baby has no way of knowing what is happening. But there does come a time when that unborn child *does* recognize what is happening. I don't know if you ever saw the video protraying the "silent scream" - an unborn baby being aborted... all caught on film for postarity.. and to be used as a scare tactic by the more fanatical. That video does, however, prove that this fetus is a human with senses. Is it therefore, morally acceptable to abort the fetus before it reaches awareness?

Not being too religious myself, I still have a hard time fitting morality into the issue of abortion. I do believe that women should have the choice (meaning that the government should not be permitted to make this blanket moral decision for America) that abortion should *not* be used as birthcontrol and that abortion is morally wrong.

One could argue: "What about an autistic woman who is raped and becomes pregnant?" The moral thing to do? I don't know. But I do know that killing is wrong. Whether the human killed is in the womb or 42 years old. Were I ever required to make that decision, I would begin on those personal convictions and make that decision for that situation.

On a tangent, it seems that society in general sees much more horror in the murder of a young child (toddler, elementary school age) than an abortion. I pose this question: If it is immoral for a mother to kill her 5 year old child, why is morally debateable for her to kill her unborn child?

Sharon Bean
