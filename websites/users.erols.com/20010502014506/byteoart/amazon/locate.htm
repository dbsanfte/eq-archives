Locations are an Adventurer's Best Friend
by Mabbena

Here is an article that I hope will address one of the game functions that is used a lot and one that many complaints are heard about. I have attempted to be as accurate as possible. I have written this article so that someone fresh to the game can follow it and hope you veterans will take the boring details in stride.

  2. Explaining how the Grid Reference works

Setting up the Location Command button
To speedily use the location command, place it into your hot keys. To do this, bring up your ‘windowed screen’ and click on the Socials button. Right click a social button that you are not using (write down the command so that you may be able to change it back later if you so desire). A dialogue box will appear, allowing you to change the title, text and colour of the button text. Left click the title of the button and type ‘loc’. Left click the first line of the text area and type ‘/loc’ (note it is important to have the slash present). Left click the done button. (Note that the other lines allow you to place further commands by left clicking in those empty lines.) Your newly titled button will appear in the social button list where it was previously. Left click and hold until the button duplicates itself, then drag-and-drop it into an empty space of the hot-key pad on the lower left of the screen. If all your hot buttons are being used, you can still access the loc command by typing /loc normally.

Explaining how the Grid Reference work s
Unlike modern mapping practices, Verant has used a ‘up the stairs and in the door’ reference. By this I mean the first number displayed is either Up or Down depending on whether it is positive or negative respectively. The second number displayed is either Left or Right depending on whether it is positive or negative.

Imagine a blank piece of paper with an X in the centre of the page. This X divides the page into four quadrants. The top left area is positive/positive. The top right is positive/negative. The bottom left is negative/positive while the bottom right area is negative/negative.

Example:

loc 1000.00, 1000.00 would place you in the top left area.

loc 1000, -1000 would place you in the top right area.

loc –1000.00, -1000.00 would place you in the bottom right area.

loc –1000, 1000 would place you in the bottom left area.

To further the excitement and discovery of mapping the coordinates, Verant has moved the centre X (displayed as 0.0, 0.0) on every map. So it is with Greater Faydark that the centre of the map is indeed 0.0, 0.0 and coincidentally is the rough centre of the tree city of Kelethin. While the Western Plains of Karana has the 0.0, 0.0 location on the extreme left side of the map. Verant also changes the size and shape of the zone maps between square and oblong. So far in my wanderings, Kithicor Woods is the smallest non-city zone, while the afore mentioned Western Plains of Karana is the largest (ignoring the Sea of Tears). Therefore wherever you are may be shown by these two numbers.

Why did Verant included the two decimal figure extention? It could only be for pin-pointing game design vectors for their design staff. Why are they allowing us to see this feature? Only Verant in their infinite wisdom know why and I am certainly not privy to their secrets.

How to use the Location Command to navigate in the game
Dakadin the Dwarven cleric tells Iludari the Elven druid that he is at loc 2456, -1827 and he wants her to meet him there as he has no clue as to how to read the locations. Currently Iludari is at loc 986, 657 and must figure out which way she is to travel in order to meet Dakadin. Looking at the two sets of numbers, she concentrates on the first number in each pair. The first number is the up/down number. Since Iludari’s number is lower than Dakadin’s, she knows her postion is below his (note: I do not mean her position is below 0.0, 0.0). Iludari then looks at the second number in each pair.  These are the left/right numbers. Positive numbers are left and negative numbers are right. Therefore she must travel right to get to Dakadin’s position. To do this she takes a few steps and uses the loc button again. This will give her a slight variation in numbers. (note: ignore decimal numbers) Example:

986, 657 present position

986, 668 after a few steps. This shows she traveled to the left instead of the right.

Now she can confidently turn around and head off to the right for a little bit. After a few yards she uses the loc button again. Now she is at loc 1057, 502. This is a good direction as the first number has risen indicating that she is traveling up and the second number has dropped indicating she is traveling to the right. She now begins to run while periodically using the loc command to keep track of where she is. Depending on your character you can see for about 400 units under ideal conditions. Therefore Iludari need only get near 2000, -1400 to have a chance of seeing Dakadin.

Tips to using the Location Command
When sitting with the magic book open, you cannot use the buttons 1 to 6 to make your hot keys function. However, you may use the mouse to click your hot keys. This will allow you to find your loc, sense direction or what have you.

When you initiate combat, use the loc command. This will capture your position incase of sudden death.

If it is obvious you will die, instead of running, use your loc command. This will ease the hassle of finding your body.

Sometimes I find it hard to see the numbers when I scroll back to locate my body. So I have started repeatedly hitting the loc command button so that a full page of loc numbers are displayed.

The location of my loc button is repeated for every set of six hot keys. That way no matter what set I am on, number 6 is always loc. To do this, click hold and drag from the social set of buttons for each set of hot keys.
