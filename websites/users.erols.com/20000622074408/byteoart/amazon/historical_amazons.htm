Real Amazon History
By Daylis

I just watched a show about Amazons on the history channel. It was very interesting.

It would seem that the ancient Greeks first wrote about the Amazons. The name "Amazon" comes from the Greek. It means "One breast." Apparently Amazons burned off the right breast, so they could shoot arrows better (that's some real commitment!).

The Greeks considered the Amazons to be the best warriors, and thought no normal Greek man could hope to beat one. Of course the Greek "heroes" could supposedly beat Amazons. In one story, a Greek hero (I forget which one - Herododis or something like that) killed the Amazonian Queen, then fell in love with the corpse. What can I say? The ancient Greeks were weird.

Then the show continued, and talked about Amazonian Celts, Native Americans, Africans, and even prehistoric Asian Amazonians.

The one thing they all had in common was that they were fierce, merciless warriors who defended their homes with great courage and skill.
