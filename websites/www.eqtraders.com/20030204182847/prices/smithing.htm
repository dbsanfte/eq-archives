Prices listed are using best charisma and faction. They are listed in platinum pieces in decimal form with no currency label: 9.524 would mean 9pp 5gp 2sp 4cp.

"Product" is the product made. Yields are given in parentheses when other than 1.

"Min Cost" is the price of all dropped items gathered yourself (farmed), and any subcombines performed yourself. The best example of a subcombine is the Coldain Velium Temper (hereafter CVT), which can be purchased in Thurgadin for $26.250, or can be multi-step brewed from vendor-sold components scattered over three continents (Velious, Luclin, old-world) for a total of $8.993. The brewed temper can be further reduced in cost by farming the research materials used in the four celestial essences, reducing overall cost of one CVT to a total of $2.693, which is nearly one tenth of the vendor cost. The cost of components that are themselves made as a tradeskill recipe assumes no failures making them; your mileage will vary.

"Max Cost" is the price of all dropped items purchased from vendors, and without possible cost saving subcombines. This is only used where options apply, and will provide a large variance in some items.

"Discrepancy" will note differences between Min Cost and Max Cost when they exist, usually noting farmed ingrediants and/or cost saving subcombines. Buying dropped items from players will obviously impact the prices greatly, usually to the costlier side. All indredient costs are listed after combines for your own comparisons. Cost of unconsumed tools such as smithy hammer or file is not accounted for, but will be listed among ingredients. NO DROP ingredients are not accounted for, as they must be gathered in the field.

Technical Exceptions: Clay and ore can be looted in Gorge of King Xorbb from Muddites. Bricks of ore drop from Rock Dervishes in Lavastorm. Numerous gems commonly stocked on merchants are also dropped widely by monsters everywhere. Even flasks of water drop from Desert Madmen in NRo, Oasis, and SRo. Discrepancies will not be given for clay, ore, water, or vendor stocked gems. Leave the desert moisture farming to Uncle Owen and Aunt Beru.
