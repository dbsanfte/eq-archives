#4: Fur-lined Coldain Prayer Shawl

You have survived baking...but are you feeling up to pottery? This is the quest that brought the infamous Thurgadin gate potions into Norrath, via the Vial of Velium Vapors which we deliver to Tanik to save his spying self from the giants. Many people, especially those who cannot gate, make the potion that you will need for this quest and use the one charge of Gate: Thurgadin as a last ditch escape when they're stuck somewhere, or as a quick port to avoid paying the ridiculous prices one often pays nowadays for a tp to Great Divide. Unfortunately, these things have also shrunk even further the already tiny market for gate potions, leaving many shamans wondering why they dumped all that plat into their medicine bags just to be replaced by a simple bit of pottery. Not that I'm bitter or anything, after about 10k pp spent getting from sow potions to gate potions...Ahem...anyway! On with the quest!

Detailed Instructions

The story line of this quest has you find Tanik once in order to get information and then again a second time in order to give him the Vial of Velium Vapors. Given the difficulty and danger involved to someone who is kos to giants, however, I recommend coming prepared the first time and doing all of your business with Tanik in one trip.

So, first you need your Vial of Velium Vapors. This means you need two small pieces of velium, a small block of velium, and three crystalline silks. If you are level 50+, you can get all of these along with some experience by hunting the spiders in Velketor's Labyrinth. If you are lower than that or don't want to fight for a group in Velks, or simply can't stand the music, head to Crystal Caverns. Kill orc miners for the velium. The small pieces of velium are the stackable kind, make sure to get two. The blocks are unstackable, but you only need one. You might get a block by auctioning, but the small pieces are worth far, far more than you want to pay for this quest because they are needed for Velious tailoring. You will probably get a block before you get your two pieces anyway, so just suck it up and kill some orcs, shouldn't take too terribly long. Then drop down to the bottom of the orc area and find the path over to the spider area. If you're not familiar with the zone, get someone to show you the way. You will get the crystalline silks very quickly off of the spiders. Don't bother auctioning for those either, they are also used in tailoring. The armor you can make with them isn't worth much of anything, but everyone who is a couple steps ahead of you in the shawl quest is using them to get their skill up for the sixth shawl. When you have these things together, go back to Thurgadin.

The Pottery Bit
For instructions on how to get your pottery up, see "What is the quickest way to raise Xxxxxx skill?". Most non-quest recipes require you to use a pottery wheel and kiln, both of which are found in cities. There are no portable pottery kits like tailoring kits or spits for baking. But pottery equipment is actually pretty easy to find, unlike the components that you need to buy to do the pottery. (There are a pottery wheel and kiln in both North and West Freeport, but you'll have to go to East Commons to find the nearest firing sheets. Go figure.) Pottery recipes, other than for this quest, always require a pattern bought from a vendor. One type of vendor has patterns for everything except for poison vials. The vendors that have clay and firing sheets have the poison vial patterns. The most basic recipes simply call for a pattern, the right size piece of clay, and a flask of water. This gives you an unfired version of what you need. Then you put the right quality of firing sheet into a kiln along with the unfired item and hit combine to get the usable item. The process of firing non-quest items is trivial at 17, but the velium vial for this quest has a much higher trivial. A few recipes need extra ingredients, like glass shards sold by the clay/firing sheet vendor, or metal bits made by a smith, or a pelt off of creatures. The pottery for this quest, however does not require a flask of water or a pattern.

Combine in a Pottery Wheel: 1 Small Brick of Velium + 3 Crystalline Silks + 2 Glass Shards = Unfired Velium Vial. (trivial unknown)
Combine in a Kiln: Unfired Velium Vial + High Quality Firing Sheet = Veluim Vial. (trivial 122)

Yes, they misspelled the word Velium in "Veluim Vial".

Go to Frundle Frenkler. She is located at approximately -350, 500. To get to her from the bank, head like you are going to the Temple of Lore. As you go down the ramp towards the creepy statue, on the left are three buildings. Frundle is in the middle building. She will not respond to a Hail, but when you ask her about velium vapors she gives you the Velium Purifier that you need.
Combine in Velium Purifier: 2 Small Pieces of Velium + Veluim Vial = Vial of Velium Vapors. (never fails)
Yes, this item has one charge of Gate: Thurgadin on it. Tanik wants it so that he can gate home, but you can also make it in order to gate yourself to Thurgadin, much to the dismay of alchemists.

Finding Tanik
Now we head on out to find Tanik. I did this by the old method of running up and down the ramp that leads down into Kael from East Wastes, and on the surface ground level along either side of the ramp. When you do this, eventually you'll hit the right spot and one of two things will happen. Either you will see a message saying "You stumble over a pile of snow exposing a Coldain!" and Tanik will appear lying on the ground, or you will get a message saying "You have been ambushed!" and the Kromrif Death Squad will appear and chop you into tiny bits. The Death Squad consists of two to six giants of approximately level 50. You might want to make sure you know the way from the ramp to the zone into Kael, and you definitely want to have jboots or sow, just in case. (Even if you are kos in Kael you can safely zone in as long as you don't take more than a few steps inside.)

There is a safer way to find Tanik. It is much more time-consuming, but if you are absolutely terrified at the prospect of encountering the Death Squad, it could be worth a try for ya. You will still run the risk of triggering the giants, but this is a way to know which you're getting before it is triggered. Basically, there are "invisible men" npcs scattered around the area. When you get close enough to one of them, they emote the message that you see and trigger the spawning of either Tanik or the Death Squad. These invisible npcs cannot be tracked or seen with any see invis spells and are not traps that rogues can sense or disarm. However...as any pet owner knows, when you tell your pet (other than enchanter pets who obey no one) to guard a spot "/pet guard here" they not only stay in that spot but they also turn to face the nearest non-pet NPC. See where this is going? Even a charmed snow bunny can find these things that none of the rest of us can detect. Either make a pet or charm a pet or bring someone with a pet and put the pet on guard at the top of the ramp. It will swing to face where the nearest trigger npc awaits. (Make sure that there aren't any other creatures closer to you than the entrance to Kael.)

Now, in order to find out whether you have a Tanik or a Death Squad trigger, make sure you have your options set to confirm before letting you drop anything, put an unimportant item (food, drink, bat wing, just nothing precious in case you make a mistake) on your cursor and click a few feet away in the direction the pet is pointing. If you get the confirm box to drop the item, hit No to keep holding it and take a couple steps in that direction. Bring the pet up and tell it to guard again. Check the direction the pet is pointing and try clicking again. If you get the confirm box, bring up the pet. Continue doing this until instead of getting the confirm box to drop the item, you get the message "You are too far away from..." message that you get if you try to give something to an NPC when you are too far from them. The goofy thing is that the Tanik trigger npc is named "You stumble" because that is the beginning of the message you get when you find him, and the giant trigger is named "You have" because that is the beginning of their message. So, you will either see "You are too far away from You have to trade" if you are approaching the giants or "You are too far away from You stumble to trade" if you are approaching Tanik. If you get the stumble message, then just walk forward to trigger him and give him your vial and collect your note and go back to Thurgadin. If you get the giant message...well, it seems that there can be more than one trap up at the same time. You might try moving your pet a ways away and see if they can detect a different trap. You could also try triggering the giants and running into Kael so that it resets and maybe you'll get Tanik.

Reports are that you might get really lucky finding Tanik or you might curse the day you heard that shrimp's name. I got two Taniks in a row right off when doing this for myself along with a friend doing his. Others have gotten 20 Death Squads before Tanik would show his face. When he does finally put in an appearance, be very careful not to go all the way through the quest text with him if you want to give him the Vial of Velium Vapors the first time you find him...he will despawn if you ask him "What are Velium Vapors?". You can either go through the conversation up to that point, or just give him the vial without saying a word, and he will give you the note you need.

Take the note back to Borannin in Thurgadin and give it to him along with your third shawl, and receive your fourth shawl! Hurray, you're making progress! At this point you're wearing a pretty respectable garment!
