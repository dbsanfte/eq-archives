Human Cultural Smithing

Please make sure to post corrections/updates to the smithing message board. Note that these recipes are ONLY to be tried by human smiths, and they MUST be combined using the proper cultural forge unless otherwise noted.

At the current time, incorrect recipes mixed in a cultural forge will NOT eat your items. Close the forge, open the forge, and your items still should be there. Failed skill checks will, of course, still eat your items, just like with any other trade skill.

Note that humans have TWO cultural forges, one in Freeport and one in Qeynos. They can make armor in both locations, but must use the proper forge with the proper recipe.

seafarers' ringmail

smithy hammer, sea temper, appropriate piece of leather armor, appropriate chainmail pattern, medium quality rings in the following quantitites:

  • Veils, neckguards, and bracelets - 1 set of MQ rings
  • coifs, mantles, skirts, sleeves, and gloves - 2 sets of MQ rings
  • tunics, capes, and leggings - 3 sets of MQ rings.

(note that the nearest known source for the chainmail patterns of the proper size are in Qeynos)

pieces are equal or slightly better than bronze in AC, and always lighter than bronze

trivial: >184 <=207 on all pieces but tunic and mantle; >=207 tunic and mantle
field plate

appropriate plate mold, smithy hammer, sea temper, leather padding, chain jointing, appropriate number of high quality folded sheets of metal:

  • visors, collars, and bracers - 1 folded sheet of HQ ore.
  • Helms, pauldrons, girdles, vambraces, gauntlets, boots- 2 folded sheets of HQ ore.
  • Breastplates, greaves, and cloaks - 3 folded sheets of HQ ore
  • Boots work ONLY for non-imbued version

Imbued version works with star rose quartz, sapphire, diamond, jade, plains pebble, opal, black sapphire

Imbued version reported trivial >190
