"Cultural" Recipes

Various cultures (races) have tradeskills that only members of their race can perform. These tradeskills generally require that combines to be made by a person of the proper race, using the proper cultural tradeskill container (racial forge, racial sewing kit, etc.). Note that illusions do not work to fool a cultural tradeskill container.

May 24: The new recipes from the May 8 tradeskill patch have all been merged onto their proper cultural tradeskill pages. In a couple of cases (humans and wood elves), this has caused the creation of new web pages. Additionally, we have merged the cultural smithing and the cultural tailoring listings into one master listing. Yes, I am aware that this means some of those nav buttons on the left don't match the shade/format of the other buttons, and we have no troll button yet. It will be fixed ... eventually. :) Sub-pages will also be a tad off on their nav buttons for a little while, as the cosmetic work is a bit low on the priority list.

 

  • Barbarians: chain, plate and a BST-wearable reinforced armor, plus weapons
  • Dark Elves: Armor plus a few weapons
  • Dwarves: Brellium Armor - can also be worn by gnomes and halflings
  • Erudites: cultural tailoring, with a small smithing component
  • Gnomes: have a variety of tinkered goodies, including some brand new cultural armor
  • Halflings: Tailored armor and weight reducing bags, with smithing component
  • High Elves: Smithed armor for a variety of classes, including casters
  • Humans: Smithed armor & weapons. Now split into recipes for the Freeport Forge and the Qeynos Forge ...new links!!
  • Iksar: Skyiron armor, weapons, shields (iksar only)
  • Ogres: Adamantite Armor - can also be worn by trolls
  • Trolls: finally have a little bit of cultural smithing, using other people's beat up old weapons and shields
  • Wood Elves: fletching, smithing, tailoring ... a little bit of this, a little bit of that
  • Half elves do not have ANY racial tradeskills planned at last check. Apparently, while they can wear much of what the various cultural smiths/tailors can make, half elves do not have a recognizeable culture of their own. (Do NOT flame me about this, please. I'm just reporting what we've heard from VI)
