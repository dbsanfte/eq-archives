Human Cultural Smithing - Freeport

Human cultural smithing is now broken into two pages - one for Freeport (below), one for Qeynos. Any human smith can make any of the items for either city, but they must use the proper cultural forge, in the proper city, for each recipe. Note that, as with all cultural tradeskills, cultural tradeskill containers are not fooled by illusions. May 8, 2002 new recipes are included on this page, and the new NPC text can be found at the bottom of the page.

Stats for these items can be found on the Freeport Human Armor/Weapon stats page

June 5 note: Yes, trivials changed for "old cultural" with this patch. No, we don't have a lot of detail yet, please post updates to the Smithing Forum

NPC text from May 8 patch:

You say, 'Hail, Issilyn Ristan'
Issilyn Ristan says ''Nichola! Well met! Come away from Sir Ranlon's forge one moment so that we may talk.' Issilyn steps away from the forge with a smile. 'We live in interesting times! Have you heard of Sir Ranlon's [armor]?''

You say, 'what armor'
Issilyn Ristan says ''Ah, it is impressive indeed. Already, he has several dozen orders from the naval merchant crews, and several from.. less honourable individuals. He has pioneered a [method] of creating unrusting, light and very durable armour - perfect for the sea!''

You say, 'what method'
Issilyn Ristan says ''With a smithy hammer, using the Freeport Traditional Forge, he uses a leather piece of armour as the base. With a little sea temper, a chainmail pattern, and some [infused rings], the armour is constructed. Isn't it a lovely colour?''

You say, 'what infused rings'
Issilyn Ristan says ''Velium rings are the basis of this armour, and with a smithy hammer, one strengthens them with a large brick of medium quality ore. However, the secret to this strengthening technique lies in the power of a [strange temper].''

You say, 'what strange temper'
Issilyn Ristan says ''Well may you ask. I wish that I could answer your question, Nichola, it baffles us too.' Issilyn looks around to ensure that there is no one within earshot. 'We buy it on the Freeport "black market", there is very little that cannot be bought in such a bustling city as this. I do not know from where you could acquire it.''
