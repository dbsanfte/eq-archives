Introduction to the Thurgadin Shawl Quests

Welcome to the wonderful world of the shawls! The most exciting, entertaining, and action-packed quests ever seen in the land of Norrath!

Weeell, not exactly....

Most people who have gotten very far with the shawl quest find themselves in a Love/Hate relationship. The thing about the shawls is that they require a LOT of work. Ok, here, take the amount of work that you are right now envisioning you will need to put into the whole thing, getting from nothing to the seventh shawl. Now, if you are a pessimist by nature, multiply that amount by ten. If you are an optimist by nature, multiply it by a hundred.

That is how much effort will go into just getting your tailoring where it needs to be to complete the sixth shawl.

You also need to get your baking, brewing, fletching, jewelcraft and pottery all up to similar levels. You need to get items off of creatures in Iceclad, East Wastes, Kael Drakkel, Cobalt Scar, Great Divide, Siren's Grotto, and Velketor's Labyrinth, among others. Some of these critters cannot be soloed by a single person of any level or class. Unless your trade skills are all already where they need to be, you WILL NOT have this shawl with only a week of work...and even if your skills are all up there, you'll need a lot, a lot lot lot of time and help to get it that fast. I think of this as the Epic Quest for tradeskillers. With all the time required to get my skills up and do some research and collect obscure stuff, I know I have easily spent as much time on this thing as I did on my shaman epic weapon...and while I have my sparkly spear, I am still wearing my fifth shawl since I can't get a success on the sixth.

My point is that you should not take this quest lightly. I cannot describe the woe in an experienced shawl quester's heart when they talk to someone who is collecting components for the fifth shawl even though their tailoring is at 30.

Verdandi sighs and fingers her fifth shawl.

Now, that said...I obviously love this quest and love trade skills or else I would not have taken the time to compile walkthroughs like this to help others through it. I just want you to realize with crystal clear understanding the magnitude of what you are undertaking before you make yourself and the rest of Norrath miserable.

The demands of the shawl are great. So are the rewards. And the headaches in between the two.

One note for the faction-conscious: this quest will require the deaths of quite a few giants and their wolves. If you are aligned with the giants and Coldain against the dragons, you will want to make sure you are willing to sacrifice some giant faction (or the time to repair it), or have friends willing to invest a LOT of time helping you collect things. In some cases you can buy what you need from others, but you'll still have to be extra careful if you want to maintain giant faction.

Still with me? Alright, let's get to work!

How to Use this Guide

I tried to make this as useful as possible to as wide an audience as possible, with an emphasis on the trade skills required. I know that some people just want the npc text and want to figure out the rest for themselves. Some people couldn't care less about the npcs and their plot and just want the freakin shawl already. So I have separated out the two sets of information, except for the page for the first two shawls since there is so little to say about them.

Suggestions for ways to improve are always welcome. Eventually I will add in more frills like complete stats and screenshots of the various shawls and items made in the process.

"What is the quickest way to raise Xxxxxx skill?"

There is a good guide to raising skills for the shawl quests at This Link. If you want someone to do the thinking for you try this first.

However, EQ Traders is a comprehensive site that tries to offer every conceivable tidbit of information that may be useful to a tradesperson. Therefore, if you have any specific questions or want to do the thinking for yourself (hurray!), then I suggest you take the following steps, either to find your particular answer or just to familiarize yourself with the site:

First, go to the EQ Traders Recipes page. Select the skill you would like to improve. Print that page. Then go to Supplies sorted by Skill and again select the skill you want to improve. Print that page.

To figure out how to raise your skill to the level you desire, look over both pages that you just printed. Specifically, look for recipes that require ingredients that you know you can find easily and/or that are listed on the supplies page as available somewhere within reason. For some skills like jewelcraft, fletching, or pottery, just about everything will be on vendors if you find a good city in which to work. For other skills you may have to do a bit of newbie vendor farming or auctioning or even rolling up of your sleeves and killing.

Once you get a feel for which recipes are feasible and which are not, look at where they become trivial and plan out an order to do them from easiest to hardest. It is possible to go from 0 to 130 on a single recipe that trivials at 130, if you wish. But it is probably faster and definitely less tedious if you use at least a few different recipes that trivial at gradually higher levels. Unless you are dealing with some really expensive stuff, like in jewelcraft, aim for things that are trivial 10-20 points of skill above where you are. This isn't always possible, but many folks report that this is roughly where you will improve the fastest.

If you want to give this trade skill stuff an honest try, look for a few recipes that sound fun (Gnome Kabobs!) and throw them in for the heck of it. When working up your skill, keep in mind that just because something is trivial does not mean that you are guaranteed to succeed. Depending on the value of your ingredients and the cost and trouble of increasing your skill, it may be worth it to get 10-20 points of skill beyond where the item trivials. Likewise, if a skill is very hard to raise and your quest ingredients are easier to get, you might be better off making several attempts at a skill below trivial than to try to get to trivial skill first.

If you still have problems or questions, make sure that you have read *everything* on the pages you already printed, and then go to Learn a Skill and select the relevant skill and read all of that. Question not yet answered? Try the FAQ. They are called frequently asked questions because many people ask them. If many people ask them then you might well be asking the same thing. Answer still not there? Ok now, go to the Message Boards and select your skill. Post your specific question. Depending on the time of day and week of your post, answers will appear within minutes or hours. When you have made it to the level you desire in your current trade skill, pat yourself on the back! You are that much closer to your shawl.

Do NOT under any circumstances go straight to the message boards without first checking all of the existing resources. Especially do NOT post "What's the quickest way to improve Xxxxxx skill?". There are at least twenty posts on that very same topic on the boards for every single skill, so if you insist you can go there and look for them and see what has been posted in response. All of the answers to those posts are based on people looking at the links I've given you above and regurgitating it for you. That level of helplessness is annoying. The EZ Board search engine is lousy at best, but it won't kill you to flip back through the pages of posts and skim the titles. You're not the first person to work on your skill, so chances are very slim that you have a question that has not yet been answered. Folks on the boards love helping with honest questions and problems and dilemmas, but do not appreciate being treated like personal researchers when the answer is just a few clicks away on the very same site. If you've looked and just can't find it, then by all means post. Sometimes it's right there and you just miss it, no sweat. But just make an attempt to look through what has been created here for you first, k?
