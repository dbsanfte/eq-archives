Janus Xanathis - human cleric

 

I guess I would need to begin this in the beginning. That is where most stories are supposed to start, correct?

I was born to pair of traveling human merchants, Brianne and Hoseph. They would take their small caravan of goods between Freeport, Rivervale and Qeynos all year round. I remember my father telling me that I was born on the path while they were trying to get a good batch of Jum-Jum Wine over to Qeynos. He said that my mother refused to allow him to stop to help her with the birth, as she was determined to have me, AND get the Jum-Jum Wine to the festival they Qeynos was having.

Their first love was being the traveling merchants that they were. Do not get me wrong; I was treated better then most children on this world. I always was fed and clothed. I had plenty of little gadgets and doohickeys from the gnomes to keep me occupied. How many people can honestly say that they have been across the breadth of Antonica more times when they were five, then most people their entire lives?

I learned much from them in the ways of buying and selling. I believe most would call it, haggling. I have even heard tales that my mother used me in her haggling when I was but a wee one. No… not to sell, but as a way to get the tough merchants to open up some. She was good at that kind of thing.

So how did I become the awesome force of undead slaying power that I am today? Well, that is a story that I have been able to piece together after the events surrounding it. The fall-from-grace of his Voidness has helped me put the final pieces together in the puzzle that was that section of my life. I was able to sit with him and ask him what I was, to be honest, scared to ask him while he was Lord of the Void. He answered me truthfully, and for that, I am indebted to him.


We were on the road between Rivervale and Freeport. I was 6 at the time. We had passed through Kithicor forest a few hours past (this was, of course, years before the events of Bloody Kithicor and it turning into the haven for undead that it is today). We always made sure to stop by at every inn along the roadway. The innkeepers were always on the lookout for that something unique that they could have at their inn, which no others would have. They would then use that as a tool to get passer-bys to stay there for the eve. We must have supplied the inns along that road to Freeport with over twenty different types of alcohol and foodstuffs over the years.

I also remember that my parents always liked to stray from the road, as it were, and go stop at a shop of a good friend of theirs. Jogre had always liked living a tad bit on the edge. He once dealt in rare, and sometimes, illegal items when he had his shop in Freeport. He always gave my parents as good of a deal as you could ask for, and he honestly was a good person. When he had found that the Paladin’s of Mithaniel Marr were going to start a formal investigation into his workings, he decided that he should leave for a while and let things cool off. He had heard of a dungeon that many an adventurer went to but were always grumbling about having to carry all of their goods quite a distance before they could do any selling.

It was this place out in West Commons that he set up shop, near the entrance to what I would later find out was that putrescent hole, Befallen. Back then; there was no inn or guard post along the road. Jogre had a perfect spot, or so he thought.

We had parked the wagon next to the building. Father was already inside speaking with Jogre. I could hear their deep voices booming out from inside. I was helping my mother to unpack the goods that we had brought to sell. One of the items had been given to us by one of the Clergy from the Temple of Rodcet Nife in Qeynos. He had told us to give it to Jogre. The Temple had heard of his shop, and it’s location to Befallen. They had sent with us an item that they hoped would be of use to someone that was intent on cleaning out the undead from there. The Clergyman had said that we were to give instructions to Jogre to have him display the item freely in his shop. When my mother asked what price should Jorge ask for it, the Clergyman had said that there was no price, the right person would simply use the item, at the correct time. Not one to argue with a Temple, my parents took the crafted box that held the item.

Well one thing I could tell you about the item, it was heavy with a big H. It only took one of the acolytes from the Temple to carry it, and place it on our wagon. But my mother was having a hard time simply dragging the box across the back of the wagon. I couldn’t even budge it.

Mother did manage to get the box to the back edge of the wagon. She called out to Father and Jogre to help her get it into the shop. The sun was starting to set, and my mother wanted to get back on the road as soon as possible.

Jogre and my father had just grabbed a hold of the crafted box when we heard a voice from behind us. It was what looked to be a man, in dark robes wielding a gnarled etched staff. He was standing in the shadows of the store, but we could see that he was alone. He asked if the proprietor of the shop was about, as he had some transactions that he wanted to do. Jogre told him that he was busy at the moment, and if he could wait, he would be glad to deal with him as soon as he was done.

The man said that he was there for the item in the box, and that he could not wait. My father turned and asked him why would he want the box. The man simply stated that his master Innoruuk had told him to retrieve the item, and so he would. He stepped forward out of the shadows a foot, and we could all see that he was not a man at all, but one of Innoruuk’s Children, a Dark Elf.

Father pulled his sword from its scabbard. He jumped between Mother and myself, and the Dark Elf. The Dark Elf simply laughed and began a magical incantation. My father lunged at the Dark Elf and had his sword deflected by some unseen force. Jogre had run back inside the store to gather his weapon, as well.

Mother pushed me back around the corner of the wagon to help shield me from what was going on. She sat me down on the ground beside the wheel and told me to not move. She then grabbed her sword from the front of the wagon, and began to move around to help my father. I crouched down behind the wheel trying to use it as cover, but I could still look under the wagon and mostly see what was going on.

I heard the Dark Elf finish his incantation as another of my father’s blows harmlessly bounced off the magical shield. He simply pointed at my father, and a blue-green bolt of energy shot out of his hand and struck my father in his chest. I heard my mother scream, and watched as my father’s body simply fell to the ground like a rag doll. I could look into my father’s eyes as he lay there on the ground and watched as the light faded from them. My world exploded before me, reality stopped to be.

The next few moments were a blur. Jogre came out wielding a two handed battle-axe. Mother held Father’s body for a moment, and then lunged at the Dark Elf as well. Blow after blow from both of them simply bounced of the Dark Elf. He said that they could not stop him from getting what was in the box, and began another incantation. Mother and Jogre stopped their attacks and backed a step away from the Dark Elf defensively. The Dark Elf stopped his incantation but nothing seemed to happen. Both Jogre and Mother looked around in confusion, but only the Dark Elf and myself were the ones that could see what the spell did. The Dark Elf began another incantation, this one he almost screamed out as he cast it.

I watched in absolute horror as the skeleton of my father ripped itself free from its skin and muscles. In fighting the Dark Elf, Jogre and Mother had positioned them between Father’s fallen body and the Dark Elf. With the loudness of the incantation the Dark Elf was casting, it was obvious that neither Jogre nor my mother could hear what was going on behind them. I watched as the bones of my father stood up, picked up its sword, and drove it through Jogre’s back.

Mother turned and screamed as she finally noticed what had happened. She backed away from my father’s skeleton. The Dark Elf finished his incantation and caused Jogre’s skeleton to rip free of its flesh casing before the body had even finished settling on the ground. It too picked up its weapon. Mother backed away from both skeletons in enough of a rush that she slammed into the wagon knocking herself unconscious.

 

 

Mother hit the wagon with such force that the crafted box managed to tip over and hit the ground. The Dark Elf commanded the skeleton of my father to pick her up. He then walked over to where the crafted box lay and knelt down. The box had busted open on the fall, and the Dark Elf reached in to grab the item. The Dark Elf stood up and pulled his hand back in pain as if something had burnt him. I could smell the acrid stench of burnt flesh as he turned to look at me.

“Boy, do as I say, and I shall not have your mother killed. Come over here.”

It was as if waves of fear oozed off of his body. I simply nodded and stood up. As I walked over towards him I looked to see what the item was. It was a hammer. Not like a hammer used to pound nails, or a war-hammer, but a metal almost brick shape topped hammer. It had golden symbols and worlds running over the head and the length of the half. The grip was woven with some sort of silken material.

“Do you see the Hammer, boy?”

I nodded.

“It seems that Rodcet Nife was trying to find a champion for his cause.”

I stammered out, “C-c-cause?”

“Yes, boy. His cause to wipe out all undead and the ones who control them, the Necromancers like myself. It seems the God of Life has a thing about bringing souls back from their final resting place. This Hammer was created by Him to be wielded by the One who would champion that cause. My God does not want this to happen. He has sent me to find the Hammer and destroy it.”

I looked up at the Necro, now knowing what he was. He rubbed my face with his hand. It felt as if all of the heat from my body was being sucked out where he touched me.

“You shall make such a nice sacrifice to Innoruuk, boy. I think I will keep your mother as a plaything.”

I looked over to my mother’s unconscious form. She was slumped over the shoulder of my father’s skeleton. At that moment I knew that even if it was my death, I had to do something.

The Necro pulled out a bag from under his cloak.

“Now boy, I want to you to pick up the hammer and put it into this bag. Then we shall go back into Befallen and start the sacrifice.”

Knowing that this was my last chance, I simply did what he said and grabbed the Hammer.

“By the Dark God Innoruuk! What is happening?”

A column of pure light shot down from the sky and slammed into me with great force as I grabbed the Hammer. The skeletons and the Necro were thrown back and off their feet as if hit by an explosive force. My limbs felt lighter as I stood up. I looked around and noticed that some sort of energy like a shell surrounded me. As I turned, I caught a glimpse of what it was in a mirror in the back of the wagon. It was of an adult male human wearing a set of plate armor adorned in the symbols of Rodcet Nife and one other. At that moment, I knew that it was me… that was my destiny, I knew the Hammer’s name and I knew what to do.

With no fear in my heart, and strength I had never felt, I turned towards the skeletons. With tears in my eyes, I pointed the Hammer of Deconstruction at the skeletons and spoke, “Sleep a final sleep weary Spirits of Hoseph and Jogre. May you know the piece in death that you may not have known in life. Sleep well in the Void.”

Twin arcs of white-red energy shot from the head of the Hammer and struck both skeletons full on. The bones shattered and fell to the ground as the explosive force from the bolts destroyed the arcane power holding them together. For a brief moment, I saw the spirits of my father and Jogre begin to float away. From my connection with the Hammer I could hear my father.

^^I am proud of you, my son. Go now and live your life as it is written. We shall meet again, someday.^^

I turned towards the Necro and spat pure hatred at him as I advanced.

“You vile, lump of putrescent scum. I shall send you back to your Dark God faster then you could imagine.”

The Necromancer simply took a few steps back and began casting the spell that I would later find out allows a spell caster to gate himself to a place of safety. It did not work for him today.

“My spell? It should have worked. Why did it not… By the Dark Gods, no!!!”

I turned to see what the Necro was looking at. Floating in the air beside me was, what I could best describe as a being of immense power. It seemed to be humanoid in shape, but I could not discern a face or hands due to the energy exuding from it.

^^You shall not escape your punishment this time, Devault. You have escaped my wrath long enough. You may be one of Innoruuk’s chosen. But your abuse of power continues to invade upon my realm, and that I shall not tolerate any longer.^^

“Oh please, Lord of the Void, do not…”

^^I shall not listen to your pedantic prattle, you are mine now.^^

And with but a thought, this being smote the Necro before my eyes. The Dark Elf known as Devault was simply no more.

^^Arise Janus, son of Hoseph, and by doing so, you are accepting the title of He who shall slay all undead.^^

I did not realize that I had been kneeling, but I was.

“I am simply a boy. How am I to do this task?”

Chuckling the being looked down upon me.

^^You shall age, young Janus, and you shall become the man that you see in time. Know this, once you step foot upon this path, you shall not be able to sway from it.^^

All I could do was look over at the prone form of my mother.

^^I shall make sure that she does not suffer, young Janus. Take her to the Temple of Rodcet Nife, and there you will find training, and your mother will be taken care off. This I swear.^^

Knowing this, I stood up.

“I, Janus Xanathis, pledge my soul to Rodcet Nife in the glory of His name and power. I shall live every waking moment to hunt down and destroy the scourge that is undeath upon this land. Smiting those that are walking that should be dead, and those that control them. So this I swear.”

Again, the being chuckled as he began to fade from sight.

^^And so you shall, young Janus… and so you shall.^^

As the being faded, the divine strength that I had been given faded with the armor of energy that had surrounded me. I was still able to wield the hammer, but no better then a young boy of six years of life could.

I remember things blurring at this point. I remember gathering my mother up and trying to make her as comfortable as possible in the back of the wagon. I remember getting to the nearest inn and the innkeeper taking us in and helping us. I remember finally getting to the steps of the Temple of Rodcet Nife. There were acolytes and Clergy and Paladins there to greet me. It was if they were expecting me. (Arlest’s Note: So Janus wasn’t always the smartest brick around. Come to think of it, he still isn’t.)

My mother never totally recovered from that evening. She was found most days simply sitting at a high window in one of the towers that the Temple owned and staring off into the distance. She hardly spoke, nor did she ever say anything about Father. She passed away in her sleep one year to the day after that evening. True to His word, she never had a moment of pain physically or emotionally.

After that, the hours bled into days, which bled into months, which bled into years. Many a night I had a dream of a grizzled man in the armor I saw around me that day. I now know whom the other symbol belongs to, the being that was there that evening, The ex-Lord of the Void. I remember thinking to myself on how I could not let my parents down, nor could I let Rodcet Nife or his Voidness down either.

I still remember the day I stepped from the north gates of Qeynos. Clothed in nothing but my simple garb and just some food and milk to tide me over, and my Hammer. It was my final test to see if I was ready. There was nothing more the Temple could teach me. The rest I needed to learn on my own.

With my first few steps out of the gate, I walked into my future embracing my destiny and the path that was laid out in front of me. Woe be it unto those that crossed my path and felt the power of the righteous and the glory that is.

This is who I am. This is my story.
