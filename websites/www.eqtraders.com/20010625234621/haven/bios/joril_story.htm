Wolf of Everfrost – The Beginning

Dawn broke upon the barren wastelands of Everfrost. Stunted trees dotted the landscape, twisted and bare. Everything is stark white as far as the eye can see, with an occasional spot of brown that betrayed the presence of dirt beneath the blanket of snow and ice. Wolves fought with each other for the feeding rights to a half-frozen corpse. A roar echoed through the hills and they quickly scampered away. A massive white bear arrived and settled the wolves' squabble. It glared menacingly at the wolves, daring them to dispute its claim.

A dark creature shuffled along quickly, dodging from shadow to shadow trying to avoid the light of the coming day. It paused to snuffle at the air, trying to locate the scent of its quarry. It grunted to itself and resumed its course, giving the bear a wide berth. The creature gripped the shaft of the shortened spear tightly, feeling it's unfamiliar length. Short, squat and bad tempered, considered by others of its kind as a sort of runt. Yet by human standards, it was quite powerful. Its long arms bulged with corded muscles. Its hands, strong enough to crush a man's skull, end in hard sharp nails. But most fearsome of all, was its mouthful of sharp teeth, made to rend flesh and its powerful jaw that could crack bone and suck out the sweet marrow within.

A trumpeting sound erupted from further ahead. The orc dropped into a defensive crouch, its eyes darted frantically back and forth, scanning the hills and gullies around him, looking for signs of an attack. Satisfied that nothing immediately threatened it, it moved forward slowly toward a small hill.

Below the hill lay a flat, open area. A hairy, four legged creature with immense tusks and of massive proportions was besieged by a hunting party of large humans. They were clad in furs, wielding an assortment of weapons. Each and every one wore a head covering made from the head of a great white bear, signifying they were from the nearby town of Halas. The orc's lips curled back in a snarl as he gazed upon these most hated of foes of his kind.

The hunters, bent on the task at hand, did not noticed the head peering at them from over the rise. They had spent two days tracking this great beast. The party had encircled the mammoth when it had stopped to forage. Working methodically as a group, they kept the beast constantly turning to defend itself. Every time it would turn, a pair of hunters would rush in from its rear, stabbing with spears or slashing at its legs with axes or great two-handed swords. The mammoth would wheel to defend itself, unwittingly placing its rear quarter to the pair it had just faced.

This continued for what seemed an eternity to the orc. The beast wheeled and the humans attacked. Finally, a hunter, larger than the rest, rushed in and with one mighty slash of its sword, it cut deep into the mammoth's neck and struck the deathblow. The others pulled back, knowing that it would only be a matter of time now. The hunter calmly wiped his blade on his cloak. A female of the party stepped to his side and gave him a quick kiss, then smiling, she turned to attend to those that had been injured by the mammoth.

Culbarth, as the great hunter was known to his people, watched Edrian at work. He smiled as he remembered the day they had first met. He always marveled at her skill as she mended the huntsmen's' wounds. Quietly speaking to each person, she bandaged wounds or murmured a prayer to the spirits to guide her hand if the wound was too serious for bindings. He shook himself from his reverie and turned to a young boy near him.

"Joril, you and Nroth, run back to Halas. Fetch more clansmen to help us carry the meat home." barked Culbarth.

"Aye, father," Joril replied. He and Nroth quickly gathered their packs and weapons. Joril stopped and said good bye to his mother Edrian. Then the pair ran off across the tundra in the direction of Halas.

Culbarth watched Joril as he dwindled in the distance. He felt concern for his son and Nroth. They had found signs that orcs were becoming more active than usual. He also felt a sense of foreboding. He hoped the two would be safe. Then he turned and surveyed the remaining party.

"Come. We must be quick about this," he said.

The rest of the party quickly set about to dressing the kill and quartering it.

The orc had gone unnoticed this whole time. It watched two scrawny humans run off. The wretched things go for help it mused to itself. The smell of blood hung heavy in the air and it began to whine from hunger. It began to drool at the thought of all that meat down there, enough to feed his clan for weeks. The little orc was so lost in its thoughts; it failed to notice the form creeping up behind it. Huge arms reached out for the orc and snatched him up roughly, a massive paw clamped over its mouth; the other arm pinned the little creature. It struggled and squirmed. A low threatening snarl sounded in its ear and it immediately went limp, soiling itself at the sound of that snarl.

The figure tossed the pitiful little orc down the hill in disgust. Recovering, it scampered to a boulder and sat rocking itself, quaking with fear and shame. The figure then turned and looked down on the humans. It was a head shorter than the hunters and bore necklaces of teeth and bone, clothed in the pelt of an arctic wolf. The shaman made a gesture and another dressed like it appeared by its side. More forms came from the shadows, two hulking mountaineers and four troopers.

Quietly, the orcs positioned themselves, and then the shaman began a guttural incantation. Flicks of green energy began to swirl from its hands and around its body. The other shaman began to utter another spell, surrounding itself with flickering blue flame, beginning to focus the energy at a hunter below.

The air around Androth began to glow green, stinking of ozone. He looked around in bewilderment for a moment, and then realization hit him like a hammer. His eyes grew wide and he yelled, "Orc Shaman!" He started reaching for his axe but he found he could not move his legs. It was as if he were rooted to the ground.

Culbarth reacted swiftly, snatching up the giant sword he had laid aside to skin the carcass. Edrian immediately began to cast a spell of her own, glistening shards of ice beginning to whirl about her body. Galron readied his halberd, preparing for an assault he knew would be coming.

The mountaineers and troopers ran down the hillside, howling in glee. One mountaineer's howl of glee turned to one of pain as Edrian directed the ice at him. Shards sliced open his skin, piercing through his hide armor, momentarily blinding him. The others continued to charge. A shaman loosed its spell on Galron, who suddenly felt very weary.

Culbarth knew there was no way they could stand and fight these things. He bellowed for them to fall back. Edrian began to cast another spell. She screamed in surprise and agony as her body was surrounded by flames, burning her skin and clothing. Her spell forgotten, she fell to the snow in an attempt to put out the fire.

Galron's eyelids drooped. "Noooooo!" he raged within his mind, willing himself to remain alert. As he reopened his eyes, two troopers hit him at the same time, pummeling him, knocking him senseless. He managed to strike a mortal blow to one of the troopers. A mighty blow to the side of his head crushed his skull and Galron fell limply to the ground.

Androth swung his axe back and forth. Tears of anger and frustration at his inability to move streamed down his face. An orc in its zealousness, stepped too close to him. With a satisfying thunk, he cleanly separated the orc's head from its body. As he twisted his torso to find another foe, a spear tip exploded out of his chest. He glanced down at it in surprise. "Well blimey" he thought, then he collapsed in a grotesque position. His legs still rooted upright in place, his torso bent in an awkward fashion.

The mountaineer that was the victim of Edrian's spell charged at her, howling with rage. He stumbled and fell, his left leg missing from mid-thigh. It bellowed in pain and fury, he rolled over in time to see Culbarth reverse his grip on his sword and plunge it straight down into his chest, sending him to Innoruuk's open arms.

Edrian climbed shakily to her feet. She was horrified by the fury of the orcs assault. She began to cast a spell of healing on her husband. Then called upon the spirits to lend him strength.

"Gate Edrian!" bellowed Culbarth.

"I will not leave ye" she screamed in reply.

"Gate damn ye! For Joril and Calamon and Sanu! And for all of Halas, GATE!" She knew he was right. She began to utter the words to open a portal to Halas.

Culbarth swung at a trooper and missed. He allowed the momentum to carry him around and he kicked out with his foot, catching the orc squarely in the chest and knocked it over. Then he swung his sword in a high overhead, cleaving another trooper almost in half. Culbarth was consumed with fury and desperation. He fought like a madman.

The two orc shaman on the hill began to utter more incantations. The air around Culbarth began to freeze, particles of frost formed into thin needles of ice. Whirling about him, blinding him. More frost formed into an ice spear and hurtled at him. The spear of ice buried itself deep into his chest and knocked him off of his feet.

Swirls of lavender surrounded Edrian as the portal began to take shape. Seeing this, the remaining mountaineer grabbed a warhammer from its belt and hurled it with a mighty heave at her. The portal finished forming and she stepped through it. Then the portal closed and vanished.

All was quiet for a moment. The little orc that had been near the boulder snuck forward and began to loot the corpses of the hunters. He howled in pain as the mountaineer vented his rage on the little scout, furious that his quarry had escaped him.

The two shaman looked at each other and began to grunt in their own language, turning from the carnage they had wrought. Finally, the remaining mountaineer, trooper and scout, collected the loot and what meat they could carry and marched off to their cave.

Teria O'Danos had a small vending stall near the gates of Halas. She sold various items, mainly food and the occasional 'treasures' of adventurers and hunters. The sound of thunder drew her attention to the docks.

"Wonder who's gating here" she thought to herself. A figure materialized before the docks, burned, battered and bloody.

"Edrian!" she gasped and ran towards her friend.

"Orc …war …party!" Edrian managed to croak. Guards and fellow clansmen converged on her. A warhammer suddenly materialized behind Edrian and struck her in the back of the head. She collapsed lifelessly to the ground. Shaman throughout the city grasped their heads and screamed in agony. Visions of orcs ripping apart the hunting party flashed through their minds.

Margyn McGennis, guild Second of the Shamans Guild, gasped "My mistress!" and then fainted.

Out on the tundra, Joril and Nroth ran on towards Halas, oblivious of the death and destruction behind them. Joril stumbled and fell to his knees. Nroth laughed and turned to look at his friend. His laughter abruptly died in his throat as he was assailed with the same images.

"Tunare have mercy!" gasped a horrified Nroth.

Tears streamed down Joril's face. "I must go back!" he said.

"Are ye daft?" replied Nroth.

"I must help him," cried Joril. He then turned to began stumbling back the way they had just come. Nroth tackled him and sat on his chest.

"Get off a me!" screamed Joril.

"NO! Think Joril. What can we do? Those things would surely kill us too. Who would look after Calamon and little Sanu?"

Joril went limp with sorrow. Nroth helped his friend to his feet and together the stumbled on towards Halas.

Quiet descended upon the plains. Wolves cautiously approached the mammoth carcass and the corpses. They squabbled with each other over feeding rights. A loud roar resounded from the hills. The wolves scattered as a giant white bear appeared and settled the argument for them. The bear glared at them threateningly, daring them to dispute its claim.

The cold, stark landscape grew dark as the day faded. Long shadows covered the ground and the grisly scene. Short stunted trees bore silent witness to what had transpired in this barren wasteland called Everfrost.

And somewhere, a young boy began on his path to bring vengeance to the orcs.
