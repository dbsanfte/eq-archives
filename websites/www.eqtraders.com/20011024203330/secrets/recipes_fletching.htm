Fletching Notes

Note that arrows of the same class will have different ranges based on the size of the nock used (the smaller the nock, the greater the range, when dealing with arrows of the same class). As you work your way from one class of arrow to the next, various fletches and vanes will further adjust the range and the damage of the arrow. At one time, I tried to make this list complete with EVERY possible arrow variation, but it's too unweildy. The basic wood point arrows are listed in their entirety, but many of the other classes will only have a few arrows listed as examples.

I include a link to Aoenla's Fletching Guide as phenomenal source of information - however, all the information is on a single page, it doesn't always load well, and it's so big as to make it almost impossible to print out the arrow charts without killing a small forest. Alternately, visit the Ranger's Glade and their updated pages.

Bows:

The different bow strings will affect both damage and delay. Bowstrings for player-made bows can be changed by combining the bow and the new string in your fletching kit (so far, no failure has been reported on string-swapping, even by a fletcher of 0 skill)

  • Rough hickory recurve: hickory staff and hemp, linen or silk string
  • Rough elm recurve: elm staff and hemp, linen or silk string
  • Carved elm recurve: elm staff, hemp/linen/silk string, whittling knife
