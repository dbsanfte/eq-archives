July 28, 3172; Surefall Glade

As I sit, fishing and foraging, I muse over the events of the last few days, feeling I've made some turning point in my life. What it will lead to, I do not know.

The Spiritwalkers have been wonderful to me, and I am now better armored and wielding a bow. I am slowly improving in it, though I've already had to run to buy more arrows, as I tend to lose them in the grass. It has spurred an interest in me to learn how to make arrows on my own, though I think I will wait a bit longer until I have more coin.

I learned how to hand in the teeth from my enemies, the gnolls, for a moonstone. Breda also taught me how to trade in the moonstone for something worth more coin. It makes me uneasy, handling alcohol even briefly like that, given what it did to my mother, but the more coin I have, the better equipped I can be to exterminate gnolls.

I am disheartened with my foraging. Try as I might, I've only managed to find two small fishing grubs so far. How am I to feed myself, much less my hunting partners, if I cannot improve? At least I am having moderate success in turning some of my hunting spoils into barely edible food.

Yesterday I did a good deed which reminded me of what it means to be a Spiritwalker. Though I yearned greatly to try out my archery skill, I heard a plaintive cry for help and heeded it, instead. A paladin of 5 trainings had decided to explore the bear caves in Surefall Glade, and wandered too close to a grizzly's den. The grizzly stood so firmly upon his corpse that he could not drag it away without re-angering the bear. After convincing him of the honor of any Spiritwalker, he consented to let me loot his corpse. I gave the grizzly a scritch behind his ears, rescued the fellow's gear, and when he tried to pay me, asked him to pass on a favor to one in need. Yet another convert, I pray.

This morn seemed to be the time for others to stun me with gifts. A ranger of only 2 trainings more than I tried to sell me arrows. When he learned that I was busy fishing to get any coin at all in the bank after paying Joril for the bow last night, he peered more closely at me. Something about what he saw must have moved him, for he ran off, returning 5 minutes later with a leather collar. He gifted me with it, saying I needed it more than he needed to sell it. I insisted on gifting him some fish for his breakfast, at which point he insisted on paying me 4 times their worth. Stubborn men!

I decided to run over to Qeynos to find some new adventurer to gift my cloth choker to, knowing how much every little bit of protection helps. Along the way, I saw an elder druid in wolf form, and bowed to show my respect before running off on my errand. A few minutes later, she found me in Qeynos Hills, and gifted me with a wolf form for a while.

Oh, the joy of feeling the wind in my fur, the freedom of feeling the ground fly beneath my paws. The exquisite joy of running and howling with the pack. I felt a sense of kinship, of family, that I have never known before. A heady feeling indeed for one who has no known family left in the world.

I know, the Spiritwalkers are my family now, but it's not quite the same. Sometimes I have to turn my face away to hide the tears when I see Unkle with his niece and nephews, the longing is so great. I will never know that feeling of blood kinship.

Even if my father is still alive out there, and I ever found him, I expect he'd be disappointed in his tomboy daughter. Why, twice in the past two days, I have been mistaken for a male. I know the close-cropped hair confuses them, but it's much easier to wander the wilds without worrying about it tangling, or leaving tell-tail strands behind for some gnoll tracker to find. Do I really look that masculine? Mayhaps it's just as well that I'm not likely to ever find him. I'd just disappoint him, like I did Mother.

August 12, 3172: Outside Blackburrow

I admit, I tried to push too hard, too fast. I can barely see to write through the tears, barely write due to the shaking of my hand.

I couldn't be satisfied with another training, some small success in making arrows, success in foraging, and surviving 3 gnoll watchers attacking me at the same time. I just had to try Blackburrow ... alone.

I nearly died as the memories that washed over me blinded me to the danger of an incoming horde of elite gnoll guards. I lived, but barely, dodging out of the way at the last moment, and picking off a scrawny gnoll from the pack. In the next 5 minutes, I killed another 2 small gnolls, almost died again, and saved two lives with well-placed arrows. Then I had to flee, like the coward I am.

Unkle didn't say so in so many words when he heard of the rout, but the thought was there "I told you so."

I am just too weak to brave that place yet.

However, until I can face that place, face the fears and the nightmares it regularly brings me, I cannot let go of the sight of my mother lying dead in that foul place. I must face it, so I can let go my deathgrip on those memories.

I must train more, harder, faster. I cannot let this weakness overwhelm me, or I am lost.

October 4, 3172

Much has happened since I last updated this journal. I have worked long, hard hours, striving to improve my skills. Striving to prove myself. Many days I have spent regardless of the weather, tracking and killing those vile Darkpaws. I've fletched enough arrows so that I feel I can make the simple class 1 wooden arrows in my sleep. I'm learning how to adjust for wind and movement of my target when I shoot my arrows. I'm even learning how to dodge some blows.

I've made progress on facing my Blackburrow nightmares as well, although I had quite a setback in there yesterday. I've been to that vile place several times since August, each time gaining a bit more confidence and wandering a bit deeper into the lair. At long last, I made my way to the vile pit where I had found my mother's body, and stood lost in memories until a gnoll came along and took offense to my presence.

So I killed him.

I found a group of hunters down there who wished me to join their party. Feeling I had been too much of a loner in my hunts, I agreed. All seemed well at first, as we slew anything that got in our path. Then other party members got impatient and cocky and started straying from the group, oft returning with several gnoll "friends". All was fine until two of them brought back a pack of gnolls each. There were at least 10 of them when I started counting, but by that time, most of my party was dead, and one had gated out, saying he had reached his training goal, and, oh, good luck with that train of gnolls. I tried so hard to protect the remaining party member, a shamaness of one less training than I. She fell, however, leaving me so surrounded with gnolls that I could not see a single landmark. They bashed against me, stunning me, spinning me in circles, punching and stabbing, until I could not tell which end was up, much less where I was. I tried to move, not caring what the direction was, hoping I could break free enough to find a landmark. I fell, taking a mere two of the beasts with me into death.

Our group returned separately to Blackburrow from our various bind points, and heedless of my request that we stick together, all ran off on their own to find their bodies. I know not which ones fell and which ones succeeded anymore, and I care not, so disgusted am I. Alone I went, seeking my falling place, knowing that I had few landmarks to work with other than that I'd been in a long hallway, made it through a door, and to a flat area before I died. A gnoll commander saw this lone unarmed ranger, and proved to me that while my kick might be rather powerful, I need much more training in hand to hand combat in order to do more than bruise him. Then he summoned friends, and I fell again.

I admitted defeat at this time, and, ashamed and afraid, asked the guild for aid. Joril came running to my aid.

Ah, Joril. What to say about that blue eyed barbarian ranger that will not make these pages singe from smoldering heat, or make me so distracted that I run headlong into a tree ... again.

He's been there every time I've begun to doubt in myself - keeping me company, teaching me how to fletch and how to shoot, making me bows (this last a lovely carven one with a bear head on it), celebrating my successes, and offering positive encouragement when I seem likely to dwell on my failures.

At first, I feared he thought of me as a pesky younger sister, and that he was just indulging me. I was quite disheartened by this thought, as he stirred very unsisterly feelings in me. I'd much rather be cousins ... kissing cousins. Lately, however, he seems afflicted with the same malady as I -- a distinct difficulty in concentrating on a given task when the other is around. I've lost track of how many arrows he's broken, how many times I've tripped over my own two feet. Let's not even think about what happened when he changed into that near-transparent linen tunic while he fletched. <several illegible scrawls follow and are crossed out, as if the writer was trying to write while not looking ... perhaps staring off into space?>

I know not where it will lead ... and I refuse to commit to parchment my hopes on this aspect of my life, for fear I will jinx everything.

December 6, 3172

He's been gone off-continent for several days now, and time's passage has slowed to a near-standstill. There's so much that I want to share with him, and his absence has made me realize how quickly he has become part of my life. Not just for rescuing me when I get in over my head, or teaching me fletching, but just for his smile, his sharing of my successes and defeats, his eyes.

Ouff, what a lovesick mooncalf I am!

My studies have progressed well, and I have finally learned how to work the most basic of magics, though they are still unpredictable. When I distance-spoke him and told him of my accomplishment, he congratulated me, but sounded wistful that he had no magics of his own. No magic indeed! It must be magic, how he makes me feel with just a look!

Perhaps I'll take up pottery for when the restlessness hits me. Goodness knows the clay is more forgiving than my arrows are. I've botched so many attempts at fletching lately that the merchant who sells me my components rubs his hands in glee when he sees me arrive, knowing he'll make a tidy profit.

February 9, 3173

At long last, Joril returned from his trip to Kelethin. I spent the better part of a day earlier this week, running around getting all the proper supplies, as well as attempting to buy or make a pie tin. All was in vain, though, as my welcome home dinner got fed to a troll instead. No sooner had Joril and I made arrangements to meet, then he was needed on the other side of the continent to help rescue Unkle. I am glad that Unkle had him by his side for that, and I don't begrudge him at all, but still, I was disappointed.

All was set right, though. We have finally had our dinner ... and a very long talk. I am walking on clouds now, methinks. I know not how his sister, his daughter, or his ex-love will feel about this, but one thing stands. I am his and he is mine.

Do not expect much else to be written in this journal for a long time. I have far to go before I can properly join him in the hunt, and I am determined to work hard to accomplish that goal.

My past may have been troubled, but my present and future, at least for now, look bright. I am at peace.

October 7, 3173

<the writing on the page is shaky, and appears blotched with rain ... or tears> My father was right under my nose all this time, and within the guild, no less. I can still hardly believe it. My father is alive ... and he hugged me.

I was in Halas, working on more quests, so that those suspicious merchants will stop looking at me askance when I saw him. Since he is a guildmate, I stopped to say hello, and to give him the gift of my meager buffing spells. Then I remembered that he is a potter, and asked if I could commission a muffin tin from him. I nearly dropped the tin when he handed it to me.

I knew that maker's mark.

Shakily, I questioned him. He had known an elven brewer named Kavanna, and he admitted that, upon reflection, he had dallied with her at one point. He seemed as stunned as I, as he had not known that a child had come of the dalliance. Mother had tried to lure him to stay, but being a footloose monk, he had been gone again before she knew she was carrying me.

She didn't want me, and looking back, I am amazed she even carried me to term. Mayhaps she still hoped she could find again that monk with the "wonderful hands" and trap him into marriage. I know not. I only know that as I grew from a toddler into a precocious young child, she resented me more and more.

But enough on that bitter woman.

We talked a bit more. He said I could call him father, or Etienne, as I have been used to calling him this past year within the guild. I like the thought of calling him father, of knowing at long last that he did not leave mother because of me, but that he had not known of me. While I get the feeling that he is not the paternal sort, just knowing he is out there is enough.

... and he hugged me. The first hug from a blood relative that I can ever remember in my entire life. Small wonder that I am shaking like a leaf.

July 15, 3174

Once again, it's been a while since I've written anything here. I don't even know where to start now, for fear I'll make the pages smolder, so I'll start with the more innocuous stuff.

Father and I have been getting along famously, and have had fun hunting together in Everfrost on several occasions. He's been away on personal business lately, so I've not had much chance to talk to him, much less to arrange "the talk" that he says he wants to have with Joril.

I've hunted with several guildmembers as I progress, and had a wonderful time. My skills progress rapidly, though not as rapidly as they used to.

At long last, I am of a skill where I can hunt with Jo. We've not gotten a lot of time together yet, but I've cherished what time we have been able to share. If all we did was play merchant for his bows and run through the hills, I'd still never tire of his company.

Rainbird embarassed me to no end last night when she calmly asked in the middle of a hunt if I'd bedded him yet. Sweet Karana, I thought I was going to start a brushfire right then and there in the plains from my blush. I spluttered a denial, but didn't have the courage to let her know that I'd never bedded anyone ... she's so worldly ... and I felt like a gangly drab buzzard next to her for a moment. However, Joril proved to me later last night just how appealing I am to him. Oh my poor fermented hormones. Talk with Father or no talk with Father, I have got to see what's under that man's kilt soon!

July 16, 3174

I can barely stop dancing long enough to write. This eve Father, Jo and I ended up together in Everfrost, trying to find Iceberg (who had just been most foully slain, it seems). Father finally asked Jo what his intentions were, and after reassuring father of his honorable intentions, he produced an engagement ring. I didn't even let him get the formal words out of his mouth before I said yes. He is the keeper of my heart, and we are proclaiming our happiness for all the world to witness. Father, wise man that he is, gave his blessing to the pairing, though he joked that he had to agree if he didn't want his newfound daughter to kill him. I'd not have become violent of course, but I would have eloped!

There is much to do and plan ... later. For now, I must try to sleep, with the knowledge of his love warming me more strongly than could even the best pile of sleeping furs...

April 13, 3175

I've been so busy with things, that, as usual, my journal is sadly out of date. Wedding plans progress well, and the completed gifts for Jo have been well hidden away. I've still got one more to finish, but I've got some wonderful aid helping me accomplish difficult tasks, and I feel confident it will be complete in time.

After a serious near-death experience and subsequent rescue by some nearby elders, Jovan (a guildmate) and I were able to attain some fabulous wedding gifts for Sanu and Aesop. It's taken time, but Sanu and I get along well now. I was still totally stunned when she asked me to be her maid of honor when whomever was supposed to do it was unavailable.

I've made many new friends, including a barbarian couple named Naera and Caernin, and Joril and I have had great fun adventuring with them.

Jo and I never get as much time as we'd like together, but we enjoy what we have to the fullest. It doesn't matter to me if we are hunting up a storm, or just running errands all over, as long as I can be with him. He completes me, heart and soul, in ways I didn't dream possible, though I must admit that his desire to do one certain act of physical completion until our wedding night has me more than a little frustrated. <rueful grin> Oh well, they say anticipation only heightens the pleasure, and with no practical experience of my own in that realm, I'll have to take the word of others on it.

I've passed the age where the elders will allow me to take a surname, however I have not assumed one yet. To those that query, I have but one answer. I will take my beloved's surname on our wedding day, and not a moment before.

Life is good indeed.

November 9, 3175

Dread news I scribe this day. Mayhaps later I will make a proper recounting of it, but for now, the bare details -- Morrighan is possessed by a daemon most foul, and has defeated several of us in battle this day. At the time when she vanquished me, we did not know of the daemon, but only that she was acting oddly. I ran from East Karana to Qeynos after she bragged of killing a merchant, worried about something in her tone. While she's always had a sense of the macabre, I was unused to such blatant viciousness that she expressed. I tried to reason with her, after delivering a gift to one of our new "refugees", a lass named Ellen, but there was no reasoning to be had, only madness and destruction. I fell most easily to her, as I was stunned with shock over the brutal attack of her and her giggling pet. Poor Ellen watched, horrified, as I struggled to defend myself from the glowing-eyed woman who is my sister-to-be.

After Mortekai was consulted a bit later, we found that he had entrusted a circlet to her, in which was bound a daemon, and after seeing her, he was convinced that the daemon had possession of her. It was a sad day indeed as Aleayha was the next to find and attempt to reason with that which used to be Morri, and fell. Gyro barely escaped, collapsing at our feet, while she continued with vicous taunts until Mortekai could stand it no longer. He leapt into the fray ... and was also defeated.

Later that eve she also stalked young Ellen, playing cat and mouse with her for a long while before striking the poor lass down before my very eyes ... and then disappearing.

I fear what this threat from within will do to the Spiritwalkers, and to the family that I am marrying into soon, and pray that we can find a way to free Morrighan from the daemon before her soul is lost.

March 9, 3176

Jo and I traveled to Faydwer for a few days, and while parts of it were good, and necessary, I can't say I'll be sorry to say goodbye to that continent. My elven cousins are very ... flighty.

While we were here, we made journeys into Crushbone. Not only did Unkle need Dvinn's heart for a ceremony he was working on, but Joril needed to face that foul den, and the memories it brought, head-on. It was not easy for him, revisiting the place of his capture and torture, and once I saw the slave pits where he had been imprisoned, I could understand why. I can't even begin to imagine how he survived it without totally shattering beyond hope of recovery. I went back later on my own, under cover of night, and managed to free a few of the slaves still held there. Once things have settled down, I hope to go back there and free more. Nobody, man nor beast, should have to suffer that way.

We left there with Dvinn's still-beating heart in Joril's possession. Gory, I know, but after seeing what I saw, and imagining what they did to my beloved when he was young, I could almost wish I'd sliced off a few trophies of my own ... including a lower part of that foul fiend's anatomy.

March 21, 3176

I almost let the daemon win today. Not with weapons or magic, but with words. It frightens me how close I came to true defeat, and only time will tell how these fresh wounds will heal on my already scarred soul ... and how similar wounds will heal on my beloved Joril.

The daemon possessing Morrighan taunted Joril until he did battle with it this day, and I was next to useless. No matter how hard I tried to pull the bony pet off Joril, so that he had a chance at touching Morrighan, I could not seem to land a single blow ... I could not guard my beloved's back, and he fell in front of my eyes.

I went berserk with rage and despair, maddened beyond all logical thought, and launched myself at the foul fiend. I finally dispatched the bony pet, and launched myself at Morrighan, only to find her slipping in and out of the shadows so much that I kept losing sight of her, and could not follow her trail. The ground was already too trampled and blood-soaked from her combat with Joril for me to find a clear trail. I bandaged my wounds hurriedly, while trying to find her, and was set upon by a second bony minion of hers. That too was dispatched, as she stood at a distance and lobbed spells at me, robbing me of breath, stealing my lifeforce. It felt like it was trying to tear my very spirit from me. In a blind rage I rained blows down upon the form of Morrighan, suddenly uncaring, unthinking that in destroying the form of my future sister-in-law, I might destroy her utterly, but free the daemon. I damaged it quite a bit, with a strength born of fury, but in the end, I too was struck down, to lie not far from my beloved Joril.

I cannot fully explain the deep despair I fell into, as I truly thought I had finally become secure in my own sense of self-worth. Somehow, though, all that crumpled in an instant.

Vicious taunts from the daemon rang in my head as I regained consciousness, preying on every single self-doubt I'd ever had. The words themselves are gone from my mind now, but the meaning behind them still burns painfully fresh. I was worthless, I was useless. I was unworthy of being anyone's wife, I was good for nothing other than being a scullery maid. Variations on the many hurtful things my mother had said to me, her unworthy daughter, when I was young. On and on, hitting raw nerves I thought had healed during my time with the Spiritwalkers, with Joril. They continued, almost beyond bearing -- I should crawl into a bottle like my mother did and give up and die.

Oh, giving up was such a tempting thought at that moment. I had failed, utterly. I had been vanquished a second time.

The daemon made a miscalculation, though, as it became more gloating and overconfident. It threatened to kill my father and make me stand by and watch ... just as I watched my mother die. I remember protesting that my mother had died all alone after leaving me behind, but to my own ears, my protests were hollow. I should have tried harder to stop her from going, even though I'd been such a wee lass at the time.

The daemon sensed my impending total emotional defeat. "Poor Ru, all alone."

Joril, who had been lost in his own pain and self-doubts, roused himself to my defense. I was not alone, I had him, and all the rest of the Spiritwalkers.

and that is when the daemon made a bad miscalculation, one that made us see where our strength against this thing lies. It verbally started attacking Joril. Bad idea.

As Joril looked ready to throw his beloved Langseax of the wolves in the ocean, I found the energy and the inspiration, from Karana knows where, to launch a counterattack. It had not studied us as much as I had thought. While my own worth was still in doubt, I knew full well Joril's worth, his strengths. "Have you not realized yet, foul beast, that adversity makes men like my father and Joril stronger? Joril carries Dvinn's still-beating heart as proof of what happens when you try to defeat them. They return, stronger, more powerful than before to exact vengeance."

I thank the gods for that inspiration, as I watched Joril re-arm himself with his beloved blade and stand straighter, launching himself into a full verbal offensive.

So close ... we had been so close to utter defeat, when all along, we should have remembered that together we are strong, and that adversity makes us stronger.

But has it? I still feel hollow inside, as if something vital, something precious, had been torn from me in that battle. So hollow ... so weak. I only pray that whatever inner damage was done, I can be strong enough to be at the final battle against this daemon ... though I doubt I can withstand another such battle, mayhaps I can buy enough time for the others to unite, and save Morri's soul, and the world, from this foul beast.

May 29, 3176

Joril and I were wed at long last two days ago. It was the happiest day of my life yet, while the day before that was one of the most frightening days I'd had since my mother died and I was a lost child in Blackburrow.

We finally cornered the possessed Morrighan in the Arena, and much was my surprise when mine was the final blow that struck her down. It was nothing, however to my surprise and fear when the demon rose up out of her corpse. A short-lived surprise, though, as it's first act was to strike me dead with a single spell.

I won't go into detail over what happened next (OOC: it's covered in "Little Ru Lost" in the Darkness Falls storyline), but suffice it to say that my soul was almost lost forever. The loving calls of the Spiritwalkers, and the surprising sacrifice made by the Avatar, Lord Spyke Pha'al, are all that saved me.

But that is behind us now, and the healing can begin.

There was much giddy celebration the next day at our wedding, and I had to contain my laughter when Bondli made a teasing comment. Joril and I were floating above the ground, dancing with each other, when the wee dwarf asked if he should warn the trees that Joril and I were in the area.

At that moment, I thought that our days of running bemusedly into trees as we were distracted by thoughts of the other were done. However, if anything, those incidents have gotten worse since the wedding night.

What that beloved barbarian ranger does to me is ...wonderfully indescribable ... and I am happy beyond all my imaginings.

November 20, 3176

Joril and I sailed back to Faydwer on a very belated honeymoon cruise this week, and my heart is quite heavy. While we obtained the water rings required for the crafting of my ivy etched gauntlets, the plight of the slaves in Crushbone eats at our hearts.

Joril is plagued with memories of when he was enslaved there, and I went into that foul pit of orcs alone one night on a rescue mission. I came back with a young lass who has torn at my heart-strings and shaken Joril badly. Whether she is truly D'Vinn's by-blow or not is still not determined, but the mere possibility was enough to initially harden Joril against her.

I had to prick him deeply with my words to get him to look past that point and see the battered lass for what she was -- an innocent child. The look on his face after the words left my mouth will stay painfully etched in my memories a long time, though thankfully he has forgiven me.

The lass been smuggled onto a boat leaving for Freeport, and Joril unbent enough to arrange safe-passage for her from Freeport to Qeynos. Such a meek little thing. I pray to Karana that she can grow up happy ... and free.

December 10, 3176

At long last, we have returned to the Antonican continent.

The rangers in Kithicor Forest have rewarded our hard efforts with a pair of ivy etched gauntlets of most cunning skill and magic. My only regret was that they would not fit Joril's larger wrists....

On the way to the forest, I found some very odd tracks in the dirt, and we followed them for a ways. Much to our surprise, a werewolf leapt out at us, claws extended. We'd heard horrible stories of attacks and maulings by these foul beasts, but it was no match for the pair of us.

And oh, what a pair we are. Our love seems to get stronger, our passion ever hotter and deeper, with every passing day. We've been through so many difficulties together, and they've brought us even closer together. Mmmmm, and how I do love to get close to that wonderfully wicked gentleman!

We find our thoughts turning often to the wee meek slave I rescued from Crushbone. Now that we're back on the right continent, we may have to do some searching, to ease our consciences by finding out how she is faring and ensuring that she has all she needs. The weather is getting colder, winter is just around the corner, and she was such a thin little thing!
