Begin your studies in jewelcraft as you would any other trade... study with your guildmaster to acquire your starting skills, then practice, practice, practice. Before you attempt your first jewelry item, invest at least 10 to 20 practice points into the skill. By doing this, you will still destroy components, but hopefully not as often.

Failed jewelry attempts can quickly drain a bank account as you develop an I'm-going-to-sit-here-and-do-this-until-the-<insert profanity of your choice>-thing-works attitude. The best way to handle this is to set aside a portion of your income to be devoted to jewelcraft and spend that and only that on components while you're learning. Later, when you are able to make valuable items that others actually want to buy, you can sell your wares and recoup your investment or even earn a tidy profit.

Make all jewelry using the Jewelry Kit that you purchase from a gem or jewelry merchant. Like the kits for other trades, the Jewelry Kit, once opened, has slots for the raw components what will hopefully produce an item. You may also want to buy Legacy of Jewelcraft, the jewelry "manual" merchants sell along with the kit, just for the interesting background information it contains. Once you have the kit and some raw components, you're ready to begin.

    

To make a jewelry item:

  1. Acquire a gem. (You can either find one or buy one from a merchant).

  2. Purchase a metal bar (or you can occasionally find them). If you want to make magic jewelry, find an Enchanter to enchant the metal for you. You can find out if the bar has indeed been enchanted by looking (right-clicking) at the item. An enchanted silver bar, for example, is labeled "Enchanted Silver Bar"; a non-magic bar simply says "Silver Bar." Otherwise, they are identical. Note that using an enchanted bar does not guarantee magic jewelry. See Things to Note for more information.

  3. Right-click the jewelry kit to open it.

  4. Place the bar and gem into the kit. 

    Note:  It is not necessary to fill up all four slots.

  1. Click . Depending on your skill and luck, you will either successfully make an item or destroy the components.

  

Things to Note

Top of Page

  • Below a skill of 20, you run the risk of destroying more jewelry than you make, which can be very costly. Begin with cheap gems (such as malachite and lapis lazuli) and silver bars, then work up to the more expensive gems.

  • A good way to increase your skill is to make large quantities of a simple jewelry item (for example, a silver malachite ring). You will receive a message when you have attempted a jewelry item that is not worthy of your skill. When this happens, move on to the next gem/metal combination. At this point, if you don’t have a player client, you can sell the items back to the merchant. In most cases you will lose a few coppers, but it’s good practice.

  • Try to master silver jewelry, then move on to electrum, and so on before you attempt to work with more costly metals (gold, platinum).

  • Merchants  (NPC) will pay you a little less than cost for non-magic jewelry. 
        
    You can usually sell magic jewelry for a very small profit. While this may save you a few platinum while practicing (assuming you are an enchanter and have the time and patience to enchant your practice bars), I would not recommend doing this as a money-making venture since the profit you make is not nearly enough to cover failures, especially with the more expensive jewelry.
