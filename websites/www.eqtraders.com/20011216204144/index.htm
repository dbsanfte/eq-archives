December 14, 2001
More information keeps trickling in, and I'm getting updates onto the site as my stamina allows. I've learned that if I don't reward myself with a bit of play after every hour or three of solid site work, I end up climbing the walls rapidly, so, at long last, Niami is level 60. {does the happy halfling dance}

For those of you who don't want to scour the message boards and recipe pages, trying to figure out what's new since Luclin launched, here's a brief update. If something sparks your interest, then I'd recommend you take a closer look at the message boards, and the temporary Luclin Tradeskills Recipes page. (I'm not going to move things off that temporary page, and onto their proper recipe pages until the dust settles more in a week or three - it's changing too much still to justify updating that many pages several times a day):

  • trade supplies: most of the zones are now recorded so that you can go to the Trade Supplies pages and look for what you need
  • alchemy: several new recipes, including clarity-type potions. Not for the faint of skill, or the weak of pocket
  • baking: owlbear and sonic wolf sandwiches and steaks, as well as a few quest recipes have been discovered
  • brewing: two new tanning agents for tailoring, plus a couple quest recipes
  • fletching: new bows, new arrows, both above the 200 trivial mark
  • pottery: Vah Shir bard quests - details are sketchy still (pardon the pun)
  • smithing: studs, boning, arrowheads, arrow shafts, plus a youngling quest armor, plus the start of details on other quest recipes
  • sonic wolf bane weapons: we're still translating the recipe into something workable, but it will require cooperation from multiple Vah Shir of varying skills and professions
  • tailoring: multiple types of armor, some with stats and trivials of 189 and above, as well as bags, threads, etc.
  • tinkering: + skill items for every tradeskill!

December 10, 2001
After much thought, we've decided to put up a PayPal button (it should be over there to the left, under the other nav buttons ...), so that folks who wish to help contribute to site expenses can do so. The majority of this money will go to the lovely folks at Refplace, who have been eating the expense of hosting this lovely community site for the past year. A smaller portion will go to the DenMother to cover expenses of software, time, and hair color to hide the extra grey hairs earned while playing mom to the site. ;) It should be set to accept credit cards as well as transfers from other PayPal accounts. Please whimper on the boards if you have problems with it.

December 9, 2001 - Tailoring "Bridge" Found!
Kudos to Irrani from the Innoruuk server for successful experimentation resulting in the discovery of two sets of tailoring recipes that bridge the gap between Wu's Fighting Armor (158) and the Velious tailoring (above 250 and expensive to fail!). Acrylia studded and acrylia reinforced armor seem to have trivials above 159 and below 250 (at least for the one and two boning pieces). The recipes are sitting on the Luclin Tradeskills Recipes page, which has been growing by leaps and bounds lately thanks to lots of contributions by all you lovely tradeskillers.

You'll also find a bit of brewing, a bit of baking, some smithing, new alchemy recipes, new tinkered tradeskill items, and so forth on that page. Once things stabilize, and I'm not making additions to the page a dozen times a day (literally), I'll split the recipes out into their proper sections in the recipes pages.

The trade supply locations pages have not yet been updated with all the findings from new zones. We've gotten as far as posting the logs for a lot of the merchant information on the message boards, but converting those logs into something useful takes time, and a lot more alertness than I have after the way this week has gone. It's almost midnight here, and I'm expecting another hour or so of testing and experimentation before I topple ...

Addendum (though technically its the 10th now, according to my clock): I almost forgot to mention that sabertooth hides and brute hides, for wood elf and halfling cultural tailoring, respectively, now stack. There is MUCH rejoicing! Next on my wishlist is making the holgresh furs (also used for tailoring) stack ... a girl can hope, can't she? And now for sleeeeeeeeeep.

December 4, 2001 - And So It Starts
It will be weeks before the dust settles around here, but I know folks are eager for information on the new tradeskill items, etc. For now, I'll be concentrating on getting information up, and will worry about splitting things onto to proper web pages and prettying things up later. The Luclin Tradeskills Recipes page will contain recipes as I can get them into place.

December 3, 2001 - Batten Down the Hatches!
Tomorrow marks the launch of Shadows of Luclin, and it's sure to cause a lot of chaos around here, as new things are discovered, verified, etc. Because of the huge amount of work this is likely to add to my plate during the holiday season, I'm going to create a temporary message board forum just for Luclin tradeskill information. Anyone with news on tradeskill vendors, new recipes, new forages, and other Luclin tradeskill information should post to that forum. It is only temporary, to be used as a clearinghouse for information that needs to make it onto the website. Please don't clutter it with chatter, speculation and so forth. Hopefully, this will preserve what little sanity the DenMother has left. ;)

Wheee! This past weekend, with only a few days to spare, the final piece of Velious tailoring for which stats were unknown (ice silk pantaloons) was made, and the site updated ... just in time for me to brace for impact when Luclin launches, and the possibility of new recipes.

Site news: I never expected the site to be still this popular for this long (over 1 million page hits to the site expected this month, not counting message board traffic) ... and neither did the web hosting provider who has graciously provided free web hosting this past year. It's costing him a decent amount of money, and me a decent amount of time, so I'll be looking into ways to fix that soon. I will NOT subject you folks to popup ads, or turn this into a pay-site. However, I may end up with some ads on the site, or a PayPal button to accept donations to help recoup some of the costs. We'll see how things go ...

November 17, 2001
We had a bit of a DNS issue, for those of you who had problems reaching the site yesterday. All should be well now. Trivials, stats and updates continue to trickle in, but don't expect the foraging pages to be fully updated for a while. It seems that in some zones, anything you can fish, you can forage now, and that's going to really enlarge my forage listing, or make me redesign it.

Other than that, I'm trying to conserve my "oomph" in preparation for the flood of new tradeskill stuff that's bound to come out with the release of Luclin ... just in time for holiday travel that will slow down site updates, of course. ;-)

November 11, 2001
Congratulations go out to Kallamindar Sunfury on Morrel-Thule for discovering the recipe for brellium chain armor! We've got stats for a few pieces of the imbued/enchanted (no "just" enchanted version). If you have stats on other pieces, please post on the message board thread with them.

November 8, 2001
Yesterday's patch changed fishing and foraging a tad, and as word comes in, the site will be updated with more information. So far, we've got verification that yew leaves and misty acorns can now be foraged, if rarely. Cinnamon sticks are also now live, and folks will be trying to pin down the trivial for Pixie Powdered Cinnesticks (it trivials somewhere above 174!). Still no word on a verified recipe for the brellium chain, though there's even a few gnomes who've decided to try and see if they can figure out a working recipe.

Other than that, there's not a lot going on tradeskill-wise, and I don't expect to see much more until Luclin.

October 26, 2001
The recipe for chocolate covered cherries was discovered a couple days back, and was placed on the baking recipes page. Tonight, we've learned that the cherries are, indeed, used in a quest, which rewards folks with a couple +5 WIS, -1 STA pouches of catnip! Meow! Still no luck on finding a working version for the brellium chain, however, despite being told that it followed the elven chain model for a recipe.

October 22, 2001
Several bits of info have been brought back from the Fan Faire of interest to tradeskillers. You'll find discussions of items of tradeskill interest here, here and here. This includes making cultural tailoring pelts stack, yew leaves possibly able to be foraged, mention that there IS a dwarven cultural chain, and that it has Bristlebane and Brell imbues (not quite what halfling clerics wanted to hear, but at least there actually is a Bristlebane armor imbue!), upcoming robes for erudites (and others), more tinkering, something to bridge the tailoring gap (chasm?), there is supposedly a working recipe for chocolate covered cherries in-game, and much, much more. Much, of course, will be waiting until after Luclin, but the tradeskill community hopes it won't be too long a wait after that launch before we start seeing some of this stuff! I've started a new message board thread for those willing and able to experiment with brellium chain.

At long last, someone has reported success with the creation of an ice silk robe. While, sadly, this doesn't have any fancy graphics texture attributed to it, it is one of the better tradeable robes out there, and kudos go to Qweeg of the Brell server for making this rare and difficult item. We still have no stats for the ice silk leggings, but a painful failure this past weekend by the DenMom at least verified that the recipe was correct. ;) We'll try again when more ice silk is available.

There's a new version of Verdandi's Thurgadin Shawl Quest guide in place. I don't know if we should cheer or just break down and cry that we've had word that there will be ten of these shawls one of these days, given the shawl-induced insanity we're seen. ;) New shawls, will, of course, not be in place until sometime after Luclin launches.

 

October 12, 2001
There have only been a few things to note in the last few weeks. First and foremost, brewers will be happy to note that, thanks to Laurefin on the message boards, we now have verified brewing recipes for Egg Nog and the more alcoholic version of it, Egg Nog XXX. A few more trivials have been narrowed down in other recipes around the site, but nothing too earth-shattering. The post-patch version of the city newspapers had little to note tradeskill-wise, other than a hint that bread might soon become readily available again.

On the ooc front, many already know that I'm almost never in EQ anymore. This site will continue to stay on top of up-to-date tradeskill information, but I will continue to be pretty much "retired from EQ" until further notice. Yes, in part this is due to DAoC launching (and the fun I had doing tradeskills over there in beta 4), but only in part. Perhaps once the visit by the House Ogre's mom is over, I'll post something to the message boards about my decision, but I decided it was time to make an "official" announcement. (I was all prepared to announce my retirement on September 11, well before DAoC beta was done, but events of that day made me decide to delay such an annoucement...)
