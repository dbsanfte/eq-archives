October 20, 2002 - Brace for Impact!
Those of you who have been with EQTC since the launch of Luclin (or before), know that things are about to get VERY busy around here. The site will load more slowly as thousands of eager folks try to get the most up-to-date information on the new recipes, vendors, etc. The boards will also be hit extremely hard. I, and the rest of the EQTC staff, will be sifting through tons of posts, trying to cobble together complete and accurate recipe listings and vendor listings, (as well as dealing with any of the curve balls that real life seems to like throwing at times like this).


What can the rest of you do to help as Planes of Power launches tomorrow? (Yes, we know most folks won't get their software until Tuesday ...)

  • Be patient - the site and boards will likely load more slowly, as you won't be the only one eager for information. Excited folks will likely be mis-posting anywhere and everywhere in their desire to be "first" to report in information, and tempers will get quickly frayed. It will take time for information to be processed from the boards and put on the site. Reloading 10 times in half an hour won't change that fact, and likely will make updates even slower, as we have to wade through that same slow loading time to read the information and push updates to the site.
  • Be complete and accurate - if you don't provide all the necessary information in your post, you can't expect the information on the site to be correct. You're also likely making more work for the staff as we respond to your post asking for clarification (vendor name, loc, exact inventory, etc., etc.)
  • Watch where you're posting - I'm setting up two temporary forums, much like we did for Luclin. One forum will be for general questions, comments, and conversation. A second forum is ONLY for recipes, trivials and vendor data. Any stray conversation in the recipes foum WILL be deleted. As with any "data only" sticky threads in the various forums, we will be actively enforcing this, as it speeds up the process of getting information from the message boards onto the main site.
  • Be patient. Yes, this bears repeating ... and repeating ... and repeating. If you're relatively new to the site, you may be surprised at what happens on the site and the boards in the next week or so. If you've been with EQTC for a year or more, you likely can guess how crazed it's about to be around here.

It's going to be a horrifically busy time for the site, but with the help of the community, it will hopefully be one of the smoother expansions with regards to the level of chaos it brings to the site.

For those who hadn't noticed, the Oct. 16 patch had a stealth "gift" for tradeskillers with regards to a new general AA ability. Those tradeskillers that are level 51 or higher, are allowed to spend 3 AA points to train in New Tanaan Crafting Mastery. This allows them to bring a second main tradeskill (baking, smithing, brewing, fletching, jewelcraft, pottery, tailoring) above the 200 cap. With the expenditure of more points, GM tradeskillers can bring others of these skills above the 200 mark as well.

In other news, the talented Verdandi has done some major updates to the quest page for the 8th Coldain Prayer shawl. Those of you working on this quest, or preparing to start on it may want to print out the newly-updated pages.

August 17, 2002 - A Word of Warning
It's come to my attention that one (or more) folks are sending out suspect file attachments and spoofing the sender id to niami@eqtraders.com. If you receive such an email, DO NOT download the file attachment. It is not from me. I have never, and will never send out email using that email address. Look VERY carefully at the email headers and return path, and it's likely that you can find the true source of the email. I recommend that if you're internet-savvy enough to find the real sender, that you forward the email to the mail abuse mailbox for that domain (such as abuse@earthlink.net), so that they are aware that someone is likely using their mail service to distribute viruses/trojan horse software using someone else's name. Addendum: it's been pointed out that it's also possible that someone has been hit with the KLEZ family of viruses, and that they are unaware that their machine is sending out additional infected files to/from various addy's on their hard drive. Regardless, always be very careful when downloading file attachments.

If any NPC is not responding to direct text do this. Clear your target window and say the quest text. For some reason they will respond when you do NOT have them targeted. So just make sure you don't have a target at all by pressing the escape key.

As an addendum to this, close your inventory window before you hail them, as this has been reported to be one issue with non-responsive NPCs.   • The initial writeup for the Protection of the Cabbage quest is up on the site, including the new earring reward. Nondruids who had been working on this quest should pay special note to the fact that the previous all/all earring is now druid-only.
  • Many new smithing recipes were put in today, as well as many new trivials. It WILL be mid-afternoon before even the first of these gets onto the site. It's even more important than ever that you READ before you post your questions and comments in the message boards, as we're going to be running a tight herd on the chaos caused by stampeding smiths. There are specific sticky topics set aside JUST for recipes and stats, and other threads set aside for questions.
  • Other quests, item stats, etc. have changed, and we'll get those updates on the site as able.
  • Rennet vendors are back in place.
  • Remember to watch your manners when posting on the message boards. A LOT of folks have been less-than-mannered lately, and have found themselves in the doghouse. The posting and conduct rules are there for a reason, and we are very actively enforcing them. This goes especially for those of you wanting to let off some steam. Even in the Primal Scream Forum, remember that the rules apply. Keep it family-rated, and remember NO personal attacks. We understand that when tradeskills change, folks tend to get a tad ... excited ... but this message board community really does NOT appreciate some of the foaming at the mouth we've seen lately. Such antics only distract us from making sure that data gets accurately reported, then recorded, onto the site.

June 5, Addendum 1:

The following is a post from Rich Waters, one of the Lead Designers for EQ:

If you are not a Druid and you've completed the PoTC quest, go and speak with Rebecca Cabbageleaf in Fungusgrove - She's more than happy to give you something you'll be able to use instead of Xanthe's Earring.

If you're one of the few people who did the quest twice and lost an earring due to it being made lore, petition in game and a GM will reimburse you with the nine ingredients needed to make another earring after verifying your loss.

If your Xanthe's Earring is out of charges, trade it in with Rebecca Cabbageleaf in Fungusgrove, and she'll give you one that has charges. When items that didn't have an effect are retroactively changed, it sometimes causes the "out of charges" bug.

To trade the earring in with Rebecca, give her your Xanthe's Earring and she'll give you an alternate kind of earring. Give her back the alternate earring, and she'll give you a fully charged Xanthe's earring back.

Sorry for any inconvenience this has caused you, this should solve any difficulties anyone's had with the earring.

Thanks,

- Rich

May 12, 2002 - Happy Mother's Day!

In a very hobbit sort of way (giving gifts on a special day instead of receiving them) this tired mother (of this community, plus 3 cats ;D) has a plethora of gifts for you all. Recipes, recipes everywhere! Yes, it's chaos everywhere, with tradesfolk running around, comparing notes, wanting to exchange their gifts for other gifts, and so on. It's going to take quite a while for things to settle down around here and for "DenMom" to clean up everything. In the meantime, you'll want to watch the message boards (as always), as well as bookmark the following: Main collection of May 8 recipes, wood elven cultural, other cultural. Yes, I really do mean to move each of the cultures to their own page for easier sorting out, and then later merge them with their other cultural recipes, but I keep getting sidetracked by new recipe updates. It'll get there.

I've been agonizing for over a day now what to mention in a front page update, as so much of it is newsworthy to varying parts of the tradeskill community, and I know, no matter what I highlight, someone will feel left out. Some posts late last night/early this morning decided me on what would be highlighted ...

Druids and tradeskillers from all across Norrath have been hard at work, deciphering the Liber Brassica Felix books, in search of the Protection of the Cabbage spell (a single-target PoTG). It's been a monumental undertaking, and I'd like to thank the EQTC community and the folks over at the Druid Grove for sharing information and ideas enough to get this sorted out quickly. The quest has been gargantuan, to say the least. And it's now over. The lovely Lady Xanthe Ravenwolf of Bertoxx exhaustedly reported in last night that she had completed the quest, and was kind enough to provide us with information on the reward (the spell and a nice earring), and a run-down of the quest. (I'll be working on webbing it after the caffeine kicks in.) We then received a visit from Rich Waters, one of the Lead Designers from EQ (I swear, he's got to be getting as little sleep as I am right now, from the looks of his posts on the boards!)

Rich said

Congratulations, Xanthe!

With the first completion of this quest, a new spell was brought into the world, and I'd like to thank the many druids and tradesfolk who participated in this event.

This quest is unusual in EverQuest, as it was intended to be done once and then change after it was solved. In the next patch, Xanthe will reap the rewards of finishing the quest first, and all druids will find the scroll much easier to attain now that the quest has been completed.

The quest will stay in game, for people who enjoy tradeskills and anyone who completes the full quest will find the final reward is quite suitable for the effort.

Thanks for playing,

Rich Waters
Lead Designer, EverQuest

In other words, folks, if you wait until the next patch, the spell will be easier to obtain. The quest will stay in game for those of us who enjoy playing with such stuff {curls protectively around her fishing pole and her Misty Thicket Picnic}, but for those who don't have a tradeskill junkie's patience with such quests, find enough patience to wait for the next patch. ;D

I'm back to practicing my dual-wielding skills - foraging for tufts of dire wolf fur on one machine while tackling site updates on the other ... have fun with all the new recipes, everyone!

~DenMom

 

May 8 , 2002 - The Patch Message

I won't repeat the entire patch message, but here are the parts that are relevant to tradeskills, along with some commentary:

  • Added over 1,000 new tradeskill recipes for all levels of skill, along with many recipe books. A few of the vendors of Norrath might be able to get you started on your way (of course, we'll be scrambling here to get the information online for all of you, but expect things to be a tad ... hectic ... for a while)
  • Added Mortar and Pestle to a Thurgadin merchant (this should make poisonmaking, dyemaking, pottery idols, and a few other things a bit easier for those who have chosen to live mostly in Velious)
  • Rubbing Alcohol is now available from a vendor in Thurgadin (happy shawl questing! No more running to Cabilis!)
  • Hit point bonuses on food items should be working (centi steaks and centi toe stew, anyone?)
  • Characters can no longer scribe a scroll that they can't cast due to deity restrictions (imbue gem spells were bugged for a while so anyone of the proper level/class could scribe them, now you should be able to only scribe the spell for your deity)

Your best bet for the first 24 hours is to keep an eye on the message boards for the new recipes. It's going to take some time to get these things webbed, especially with the server lag I'm already seeing this morning from eager folks at w*rk hitting the site, trying to see if anything's been found yet.
