- Travel -
Norrath is vast, huge! Crossing Antonica, the main continent, from north to south is a real expedition. The world is split into zones. You know you are zoning when your screen freezes and you get the Loading, please wait message. We’ll see later on how important zones are in combat.

A good sense of direction is necessary when you have to travel far. Wandering off the path can get you very, very, lost… There are no radars or maps and sense heading, the only skill that may help you in such circumstances, is long to learn and master. Don’t let the little compass on your screen fool you: its precision is based entirely on your sense heading skill. You may however purchase a real compass from merchants or tinkering gnomes. This might help you find your way. As always, relying on landmarks or on an experienced player is the best bet.

Another aid to find out where you are is Norrath own positioning system: the /loc command, which returns your location as a set of X Y Z coordinates. If you can type in a command before dying you’ll save yourself a lot of grief and trouble by noting your corpse’s exact location.

If running isn’t fast enough for you, you can try to have a nice Druid cast the legendary Spirit of Wolf (SoW) on you. There are other spells which may make you fly or even teleport you! Another form of travel, although frightfully expensive, is the purchase of a horse.

There are other useful spells for casters: Gate and Bind Affinty, the first allows you to teleport to your bind point (where you start off when you are killed). The second allows you to actually change bind points (another city or even anywhere else if cast on yourself), so if you are bound in Halas and are currently in Firiona Vie, it might save you a lot of trouble to bind yourself there.

To travel between continents, you have to go by boat. This is one of Norrath’s safest and most effective means of travel. All you have to do is head to a port city (Erudin, Firiona Vie, Freeport, Kaladim or Qeynos), head to the docks and climb aboard one of the vessels. Once at sea, there isn’t much to do… Don’t expect to memorize spells or practice fishing while the ship moves. Try meditating, chat with other players, grab a book… You’re there for a long time. Whatever you do don’t fall overboard: you’ll have to swim across vast expanses of water and face grave dangers before reaching a friendly shore. You don’t want to make a corpse run in the middle of the ocean…

Once you’ve safely reached your destination, just hop of the boat. Just make sure you’ve actually reached your destination (there are frequent stopovers on small islands along the way).

We hope you’ll enjoy travelling around Norrath and wish you a happy journey.
