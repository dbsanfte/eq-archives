- Your First Quest -
Remember the tattered note (the message you have to hand in to your Guild Master)? Go take a look in your inventory and right-click on it to see who it is destined to. You’ll usually find the recipient in your guild. Don’t worry, you usually start right next to, if not inside, your guild’s building. The great outdoors will have to wait…

Take this opportunity to get acquainted with your home town. Try to memorize its layout, take note of the major landmarks so you don’t get lost and waste time trying to find your way. Find the bank, the different merchants and the gate. You can also get to know your fellow citizens. Don’t hesitate to /Hail them all for some will give you quests to accomplish.

If there is in an NPC’s reply, hit the Enter key and answer including the [bracketed text] in your sentence.

Eg.
  • NPC : You sure seem like a kind soul to me. Are you a [follower of Tunare] ?
  • You : Yes I am a follower of Tunare.
  • NPC: Then you are welcome here. Maybe you can help me ? I am looking for some [rare plants] that grow only in Greater Faydark.
  • You: What kind of rare plants ?
  • NPC: If you could bring me a [Fire Blossom], an [Indigo Rose] and a [Crimson Lily], I should be very grateful !
  • You: Thank you.

Now all you have to do is go find the three flowers and bring them back to get your reward (experience, gold, faction...).Once you’ve found your Guild Master, hand him the tattered note : go into your inventory, left-click on the note and drop it on him. A “Trade” window will appear. Validate the trade to get your first experience points, your guild uniform (a gown or tunic) and 5 training points. Equip your tunic and right click your guild master to start training.

Once you’ve found your Guild Master, hand him the tattered note : go into your inventory, left-click on the note and drop it on him. A Trade window will appear. Validate the trade by clicking on the Give button to get your first experience points, your guild uniform (a gown or tunic) and 5 training points. Equip your tunic and right click your guild master to start training.

A new window will pop up with all the skills your master can teach you. The first few points should go into training the combat skill that corresponds to your starting weapon (blunt, one-hand slashing , piercing, or hand-to-hand for the monk) so that you may actually have a chance at surviving in the wild.

Now you can head for the city gates and start exploring Norrath. Don’t worry if at first you die: the worst that can happen until you reach level 10 is being sent back to your bind point with all your equipment. Once you reach level 10, however, you’re on your own…
