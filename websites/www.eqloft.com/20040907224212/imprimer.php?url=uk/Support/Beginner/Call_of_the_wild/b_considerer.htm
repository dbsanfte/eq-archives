- Choosing your adversaries -

Target an NPC and use the /con command (or type C) to consider NPCs or other players. You can also right-click on them.

The first part of the returning message indicates the character’s attitude towards you the social scale), the second how strong it is compared to you (the threat scale):

SOCIAL SCALE:
    +4, ally
    +3, warmly
    +2, kindly
    +1, amiably
    0, indifferently
    -1, apprehensively
    -2, dubiously
    -3, threateningly
    -4, ready to attack
THREAT SCALE
    Green, you could probably win this fight.
    Light blue, this creature could pose problems, you would probably defeat it.
    Blue, appears to be quite formidable.
    Black/White, looks like quite a gamble.
    Yellow, looks like it would wipe the floor with you!
    Red, what would you like your tombstone to say
Colours are there to help you decide between fighting and running as quickly as possible.

At first, even the weakest creatures will con Black/white or more to you. When you reach level 3 the same creatures will con Blue, then green at level 5…

As a rule, leave the green Mobs to lower level players – you won’t get experience, but they might. Focus on Blue and Black/white Mobs but keep an eye out for add-ons which can quickly change the balance of the battle.

Unless you have a special ability allowing you to have the upper hand over a certain class of monsters (the undead are especially vulnerable to the cleric’s spell, for example) avoid yellow monsters. You may also wish to stay away from tombstones (red mobs) – unless, of course, you are feeling suicidal.

Remember this system doesn’t take into account the creature’s special abilities or spells: at the same threat level, certain fight will be more dangerous than others. For example : bears make a lot of damage, snakes have poison, ghouls will root you…

Keeping all this in mind, gather all your courage and get ready to fight!
