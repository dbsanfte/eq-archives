- Death & Reincarnation -
Dying
Bad luck has struck again and you have :
    O been slain by an adversary
    O died from poisoning
    O fallen to your death from a great height
    O drowned in a wide expanse of water
    O all of the above
Don’t panic ! You will reappear in a few seconds at your bind point. If you are under level 10, you will even get all your gear back and start out as though nothing had happened. If you are over level 10, not only have you just lost some experience - and will lose experience every time you die (until you hit level 9 again), you also have to go loot your corpse to get back whatever you were carrying. This is called a Corpse Run (CR). Remember: your corpse will rot after a while taking everything with it, so don't waste any time.


Finding a good place to die
The best way not to lose your corpse once you reach level 10 is to know where you came to die. Get to know the area you are hunting in and take note of major landmarks: buildings, rivers, paths… Try to hunt near one of these landmarks and if you think you can’t face your opponent, don’t run all over the place like a half-wit… Choose a nice place to die and face death with courage.

Another good idea is to use the /loc command (or /l) just before you die and write down the coordinates of your corpse. You can then pinpoint its location.


Finding your corpse
There are several means of finding your corpse:
  • the necromancer Summon corpse spell, which brings back your corpse

  • the necromancer/Shadowknight spell Locate Corpse and the Bard song Lyssa’s Locating Lyric (which point you in the general direction of your corpse)

  • using the /loc command before death
If your corpse is inaccessible (at the bottom of a lake, stuck in a wall…) you can use the /corpse command which lets you drag it a short distance. You have to be quite close to it so if you die next to a big meany’s spawn site, try to find someone you absolutely trust to retrieve it for you.

To do so, type /consent (name) to authorize your friend to bring your corpse somewhere you can loot it safely. Take note however that the /consent command also allows him to /loot your corpse. So be careful…


Resurrection
What’s the point of having resurrection spells when you always resurrect after death? Take the Revive spell, for example, which teleports you straight to your corpse. Resuscitate, Resurrection and Reviviscence do the same but also reduce the amount of experience lost (up to 96% for Reviviscence).
