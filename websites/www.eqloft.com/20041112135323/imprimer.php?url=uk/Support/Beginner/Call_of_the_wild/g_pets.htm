- Pets -
Some classes can summon or charm pets to protect them. Necromancers can summon skeleton from level one (Shadowknights learn this spell at level 9), magicians can summon elementals, Beastlords summon warders, and shamans invoke a wolf spirit. They are totally devoted to their masters and can only disappear when killed or dispelled.
Enchanters, druids and shamans can charm certain creatures. They will protect their masters for as long as the spell holds but once it fades, they will turn on him mercilessly.
Summoning a pet
Some summonig spells require reagents (bone chips for necromancers, malachite for magicians…) and their cost in mana can be very high so be careful when you summon a pet: it’s usually better to enter combat with a full bar of mana and no pet than a half-full reserve of mana and a pet.

Since summon warder is a beastlord skill, it requires no mana to cast.

Using your pet
Pets obey to a number of commands. These commands have the same range as /say.

/pet attack
Tells your pet to attack the creature you have targeted

/pet report health
Asks a health report from your pet – useful to know to avoid finding yourself with no pet at a critical moment. It will also give you the list of its buffs.

/pet back off
Tells your pet to calm down and come back to your side.

/pet sit down
Your pet sits down and stays put unless you are attacked.

/pet follow me
Your pet will usually stay close to you, although they tend to “get lost” from time to time especially indoors and can sometimes even create trains.

/pet guard me
Your pet will attack anything that is aggressive towards you and gets too close, regardless of its level. Avoid using this command, especially in groups.

/pet guard here
Your pet will sit and effectively attack anything that gets too close to it while you can go off and do something else.

/pet as you were
Makes your pet “forget” all prior commands and return to the default “/pet follow me” mode

Pets and experience
Using a pet takes its toll on experience. However, if you didn’t have a pet you might not have been able to take on the creature. You can reduce the experience penalty by making the killing blow yourself (remember the /pet back off command ?). When grouped, it does its share of damage and doesn’t ask for loot.

Pets and weapons
Some pets may carry weapons to increase their damage output. To arm your pet just drop a weapon on it and it will equip it automatically.
