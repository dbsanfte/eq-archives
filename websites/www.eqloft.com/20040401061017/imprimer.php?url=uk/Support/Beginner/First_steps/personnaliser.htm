- The Interface -

The window selector on top of the screen controls all the other windows: If you place your cursor on one of its icons, the name of the window it operates and the keyboard shortcut to activate it will appear

Keyboard shortcuts
The interface uses a number of keyboard shortcuts : A to engage combat, C to consider characters, D to duck, H to Hail another character, I to open the inventory window, T to send a private message (or/tell) to another player, space bar to jump, and the - key on the numeric pad to make screenshots (stored in BMP format in your EQ directory).

You’ll soon find out that having A as the default button for attack can make life difficult and you’ll want to change this default command. To do so, bring up the Options menu and click on the Keyboard tab, then select Commands and modify the shortcuts as best suits you.

Abilities
Click on the Abilities tab in the Actions window : the Abilities bank appears with 6 empty slots. Right-click on one of them to access the list of skills available to your character. Left click on one of them and it will appear as a button in the slot you selected. Proceed accordingly with the others. To change the layout of the buttons, or add a new ability, right-click on the button you wish to remove and select a new skill, or left-click the button: it will stick to your cursor and all you have to do is “drop” it on your main screen like an item.

Once you are happy with your Abilities bank, proceed to the Combat bank. The same procedure applies. The only difference is that you may not modify the melee weapons buttons.

The art of conversation
You can know start interacting with the other denizens of Norrath. Click on the Socials tab. A twelve button bank appears. To modify it, right-click on a button. You can then rename the buttons and add up to five successive commands, phrases or emotes.

Eg. (Don’t try this at home)
Right click on the Hail button to bring up the macro menu:
  • Leave the first line as it is: /Hail

  • In the second line type: My name is Thingy

  • In the third line type: /bow

  • In the fourth line type: Can you spare a few plats?
Then click on the done button.

Here is an example of exchange that may occur when you click on the Hail button while targeting a player or NPC:
  • You say, 'Hail, Chanda Miller'

  • Chanda Miller says 'Um.. I do not know you. Please um.. leave me be.'

  • You say, 'My name is Thingy'

  • You bow before Chanda Miller.

  • You say, 'Can you spare a few plats?'
There are many pre-programmed emotes, some even have special animations.

Hot Keys
You can now customize your main interface by copying your shortcuts (combat, spells, skills and socials) onto the Hot Button window on the lower left hand corner of your screen. This window contains 10 banks with 10 slots each. To copy a shortcut in one of them, right-click on its icon until it sticks to your cursor (it may take a few seconds for a spell gem) and drop it in the slot you wish to assign it to.
Each of these slots is assigned a number from 1 (top left button) to 0 (lower right). You can activate any one of these shortcuts by typing its number on your keyboard (not the numpad). You can also access the banks directly by typing Shift + the number of the bank you wish to use or browse through them by clicking on the arrows.

Camera views
During gameplay, you can view your character from several different camera angles, zoom in and out, or pan up and down. You can also hide some or all of the interface. Here are the 5 camera views available : first person (default), overhead (rotating), rear (rotating), overhead (fixed), and rear (fixed). To cycle through the different camera views type the F9 key. Although you’ll mostly be using the default first-person view, getting used to the others is strongly advised.

The F10 key will toggle between the regular and full (no interface) views.

Bio
Go into an external view mode (hit the F9 key) and click on yourself. A new window appears with a blank area below it where you can type a brief character history or whatever you want. This will appear whenever someone examines your equipment.
