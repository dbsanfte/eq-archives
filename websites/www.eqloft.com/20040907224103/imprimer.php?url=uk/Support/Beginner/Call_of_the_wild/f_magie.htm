- Magic -
Whether you are a “pure” caster or a “hybrid” class, if you use magic, you might find this useful.


Mana
Unlike hit points, mana isn’t represented numerically. Only the blue bar gives you an idea of how much you have left. How much mana you have depends on your intelligence (for pure casters) and wisdom (for healers and hybrids)..


Buying spells
Most spells can be purchased in your home town. Start by looking for a spell merchant in your guild’s building. Right-click on the spells’ icons to inspect them and know the minimum requirements and restrictions that apply (level, class…). You may also find them in certain libraries. Some spells can only be found in certain cities, so you may have to ask other players to get them for you, or /auction to buy the ones you are missing. Some high level spells are rare drops from specific monsters or NPCs, or are earned through quests. You can also research certain spells.


Learning a spell
  • Open your spell book (ctrl + B)
  • Click on the scroll you wish to inscribe and drag onto a slot in your spell book
  • Once inscribed, you can change its position by right-clicking on it then right-clicking on a new slot. If you right-click on another spell icon, they will trade places.

Memorizing a spell
  • Open your spell book (ctrl + B)
  • Left-click on the spell’s icon. A grey gem appears on your cursor. Drop it on one of your spell bank slots.
  • If your spell bank is full, right click on a spell you can do without to “forget” it and drag the new spell to its location.
Casting a spell
  • Target a character, or yourself
  • Left-click on the gem of the spell you wish to cast. If you are successful the gem will fade, you have to wait a short while for the gem to reappear before casting again.
Your spell bank’s gems are assigned numbers (1-8); holding the ALT key will bring up the name of the spells and their number. Holding the ALT key and hitting the gem’s number will cast the spell.

Reagents
Some spells require reagents to be cast (notably pet summoning spells). Some of these reagents are very rare and expensive. They are entirely “consumed”, even when the spell fizzles. However, these spells are often more powerful or useful than others of the same level.
You can make some money by selling these reagents, most notably bone chips for necromancers who don’t wish to waste their time running after decaying skeletons.

Meditation
This skill is acquired at difernet levels according to your class. It allows you to regenerate your mana much more quickly. However, you are very vulnerable while meditating: most hostile creatures will attack you even if they /con green to you and would normally avoid you. So if you plan on leaving your character to meditate to go /afk for a while, make sure you are in a safe area, away from trains and wandering creatures.
