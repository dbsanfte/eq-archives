Finding people
Before starting to chat, you have to find someone to talk to… Type in the /who command (or /w) to bring up a list of all those present in your zone.

/who (class) or /who (race) will bring up a list restricted to those who fill your criteria : /who dwarf will return a list of all the dwarves currently present in your zone.

/who all (class) or /who all (race) broadens the search to the whole server.

If you meet another player and wish to know his class, level or race, type /who (name). So if you’re looking for a level 4-5 tank for your group and wish to invite Enora Quicksilver, type /who Enora and get the following message - “Enora Quicksilver, Druid 24, Half Elf” – you know you can keep on looking.

Sometimes you’ll just get “Enora”. Usually, that means that Enora is a high level player who deosn’t wish to be annoyed all the time with requests for SoWs or PPs. You can become anonymous too by typing the /anon command (or /a). If you type it again you’ll no longer be anonymous.

If you meet someone you like or play with friends, you can use the /friend (name) command to add them to your friend list. Repeating the command will remove them. Typing /friend will bring up your list of friends. /who friend those currently in your zone, and /who all friend all your friends currently online on the server.

Let’s say Enora saves your hide, heals you, gives you some advice and even hands you a nice item (these thing DO happen on Norrath). Full of gratitude and admiration, you /bow and /thank her profusely and add her to your /friend list (/friend Enora). Next time you log, type /who all friend : Enora is online and you even know what zone she’s in. Why not pop in to say hi?

Communicating
The default communication mode is /say mode in Common tongue: everyone within earshot will “hear” and understand what you say. You can modify this to speak in other languages – those who don’t understand the language you are using will only see garbled messages. You can also use other modes of communication:

/shout: your message will be heard by all players present in your zone.

/tell (name) : sends a private message to (name) wherever he may be.

/gsay or /g : this is a group /tell. All the members of your party will receive your message, wherever they may be.

/ooc (Out Of Character) : your message will be heard by all in the zone

A very useful command is /ignore (name) which allows you to stop receiving messages from annoying or irritating players.

Trading
You can buy and sell from merchants, but you can also trade with other players: type the /auction command followed by your message: WTB(Want To Buy), WTS (Want To Sell), WTT (Want to Trade)…

Once you meet someone who will engage a transaction, left-click on him or drag the item you wish to trade and drop it on him. An 8 slot trade window will appear. Make sure you both fill your end of the transaction then validate (hit the Trade button) if you agree with the terms.


Emoticons
You can use EQ’s pre-programmed emoticons (list here) or, create your own custom emoticons using the /em command (or just : followed by your message).

Eg: /em watches the Bixies go by…
The emoticon’s range is the same as the /say’s.
