- Grouping -
You’ll quickly realise that some classes can’t solo efficiently and that grouping, no matter what, is beneficial for all.

There are many reasons to group:

Security

In case of unexpected danger (additional creatures joining the fight, poisoning, blinding…), you can count on your group for assistance.

Complementarities

Casters are vulnerable in melee combat, tanks need buffs and healing… A well-balanced group sees its efficiency increased not only by its member’s ability in combat but by the sum of skills it encompasses.

Efficiency

If you’re lucky, you might vanquish a yellow foe by yourself at the cost of long periods of inactivity due to healing, or meditation. In a group, yo can take on several enemies in a row, or at the same time, rest for a while, and step back into the fray. You’ll get more experience, more quickly.

Risk management

An item you need or just want is dropped by a high level creature. Facing it by yourself might mean certain death or waiting several levels to fight it safely. Your best bet is still to ask the assistance of others.

Fun

It isn’t just about hack and slash, you get to meet new people, make friends, have fun!

How it works

Every group needs a leader : he takes the initative of creating the group and can invite or eject group members. To invite target a player and press the Invite button. To join a group press the Follow button. There can be a maximum number of 6 players in a group.

To retire from a group press the disband button or leave the game. If you wish to remove a player, target him and click remove – this is only available to the group leader. If the group leader retires without disbanding the group, all members have to press disband in order to create a new group.

Certain spells will only work on a group (group invisibility, teleport…).

Options

- /gsay (or /g) allows you to send a message to all your group members wherever they may be.

- /autosplit will automatically split the coin you loot among all the group members.

Strategy

Try to make a balanced group: 3 wizards might have great firepower, but are hopeless defensively. A good combination would be tank + healer + caster.

The tank will take the brunt of the attack and taunt and inflict enough damage to keep the creatures focused on him. Are considered tanks warriors and monks, as well as certain hybrid classes and, to a lesser extent rogues and bards.

While the tank keeps the monsters busy, the healer buffs him tries to keep him alive. If necessary he might even take on a few enemies for a while. Clerics make the best healers, followed closely by shamans and druids. Bards can also lend a hand from time to time.

Finally, the casters’ role is to nuke the enemy. They should stay back and monitor the fight, casting DD (Direct Damage) and DoT (Damage over Time) spells to help the tank. They should only go into melee combat when out of mana (oom) in extreme situations. Among casters, Wizards mete out the most damage, but enchanters, mages and necromancers have other interesting abilities. Healers and bards can sometimes take over the role of casters.

In larger groups you may have primary and secondary tanks and healers, multiple casters… Strategies may change entirely depending on the classes present and the enemies you face.
