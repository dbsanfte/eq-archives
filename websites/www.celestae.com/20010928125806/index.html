Monday July 9, 2001
Whoa! A Dark Age of Camelot Guild Beta!? By: Shastis @ 12:00PM PST 
Taken From CamelotHQ:

Guilds, or Player Associations as they are called in DAoC, will be introduced shortly. To test these systems, some guilds will be allowed to have their members join the beta test and this is causing quite a stir. In response to this issue, Dave Rickey has posted the following.

Folks, we're putting in Association functionality. What we're putting in goes *way* beyond what has been done to date, and we need people who are already organized in functioning as a guild to really test it to the fullest. Things like configurable ranks, and voting support, and the interactions between the Associations and the Realm points, and so on, and so on.

That's it, that's the secret plan. We have new functionality that needs a particular kind of testing, and we're trying to find the people who can test it most effectively.

I know how frustrating it can be to want to get into the beta for these games, and watching others get in when you don't. But you have to remember, the primary function of the beta program is to test the system, and we've got to do the best job of that we can.

 Sanya (Mythic's Internet Relations Manager) also posted the following concerning the guild questions and issues :

Also, don't be concerned about a lack of content. The guild beta is also known as beta 4, and we have a ton of stuff coming in right before beta 4 starts. Beta 4 will also add waves of individuals, in case I haven't repeated that enough.

Now, as for why player guilds have been selected as a target... some folks here have explained it very well already, but I will repeat. We have certain structures and concepts for the player guild system. We don't have much time to test them. Therefore, to fully test everything including the higher level goodies, we *MUST* have already organized groups come in ready to hit the ground running. People with a pre-existing structure of leadership, people already used to working together as a group. You can't throw a bunch of strangers together and magically get them to follow a leader and develop esprit de corps. Relationships within a player clan are built, not made, and we just don't have enough time to grow tight knit clans in beta. I fully expect that using the tools suggested *by players*, DAOC will become the home of hundreds of new player guilds, guilds with very different styles and hierarchies. That outcome can only be encouraged by having a fully tested structure in place to support those new clans when the game goes gold.
