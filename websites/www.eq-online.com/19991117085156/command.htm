In game commands

Command Function
/afk toggles the AFK (Away from Keyboard) option. When in this mode players will be given a visual alert when sending you a tell that you are afk.
/anon or /a (ON or OFF) Makes you Anonymous in /who and /who all, preventing other players from seeing your class, level, and current location.
/assist if you target a player who is engaged in combat, the /assist command will automatically target for you whoever that player is fighting at the moment -- this can be very useful in heavy combat. To be most effective, it is best to set this as a Social.
/auction chat channel used for buying/selling items. Seen zone wide.
/autosplit automatically splits any loot you get with the rest of your part. Each member must do this to split all loot.
/bow bows to the targeted individual.
/bug or /b takes you to a bug-reporting screen.
/cheer cheers to the targeted individual.
/chuckle Emote for chuckle.
/consent (player name) gives player permission to loot your corpse.
/consider or /con gives vital statistics of a targeted PC or NPC.
/corpse command used to drag your corpse away from danger.
/cry Emote for cry. Cries over targeted player.
/decline Declines an offer for a group.
/duel or /d challenges targeted PC to a duel - to accept, they type /d with you targeted as well. Duel is in effect until one "dies" (is knocked unconscious) or flees the zone.
/em (text) or : (text) emotes the text. Some emotes have animations associated with them, most do not.
/exit Instantly exits the game (Note: character will remain for a minute or two, and be linkdead. Still subject to attack and so forth.
/feedback takes you to the feedback/comments screen.
/filter turns the bad word filter off.
/follow Allows you to follow the targeted player. Useful if you dont know where you are going and need to follow someone. This command is not good to use if heavily lagged or near lava or deep bodies of water. You get this warning when activating it.
/friend (player name) adds or removes a ‘friend’ from your friends list. /friend (player name) will add the player to your list, and /friend (player name) again, with the same player, will remove him/her. /friend by itself will list the players in your friends list. A /who friend will let you know if anyone on your friends list is in the zone you are in, and a /who all friend will let you know if anyone on your friends list is on the server you are on.
/frown An emote for frown.
/gsay or /g text is seen by all in your group.
/gesture makes a rude gesture at targeted player.
/guildinvite An officer function to invite targeted player into the guild.
/guildpeace (guildname) Ends a guildwar Guildmaster only command.
/guildremove An officer function to exile a guildmember.
/guildsay A command to broadcast text to the guild.
/guildstatus Reports the name of the guild and rank of the targeted player.
/guildwar (guildname) Declares war on the specified guild Guildmaster only option.
/hug Emote to hug targeted individual.
/ignore (playername) Turns off ALL text of the specified player, even emotes. If typed again it takes them off your ignore list. You can have a max of 20 on this list.
/laugh Emote for laugh.
/loc or /l gives your coordinates in X and Y.
/loot loots targeted corpse, useful if your corpse is underwater or in lava.
/nod emote for nod.
/ooc chat channel for out of character text, zone wide.
/panic emote for screaming in panic.
/pet (command) a way of issuing pets a command, you can also target the pet and /say the command and they will respond.
/pet as you were commands your pet to enter a neutral mode. This command will stop the pet from pacing in dungeons.
/pet attack (target) tells the pet to attack the designated target.
/pet back off tells the pet to stop its attack.
/pet follow me tells the pet to follow you, but ends when you issue a different command.
/pet get lost Issues a command to have pet suicide.
/pet guard me tells the pet to attack anyone that attacks it's master, or that it's master attacks.
/pet guard here tells the pet to guard its current location.
/pet health report Pet will report there current hit points.
/pet sit down commands pet to sit down
/pet stand up commands pet to stand up
/pet target or hitting F1 twice targets your pet.
/petition a message sent to a GM, for assistance.
/played or /p gives date of birth and time played.
/poke emote for poke.
/quit see exit command, same thing.
/random (x) Generates a random number between 1 and x. This can be used to decide things randomly, for instance, who gets a recently acquired treasure. Simply have the players who want the item each guess a number between 1 and x, and then use the command to randomly generate a number. The player who guessed the number closest to the randomly generated number wins the treasure.
/reply or /r reply to the last tell, feature is mapable.
/rofl emote for roll on floor laughing.
/roleplay or /role turns roleplaying switch on.
/shout heard across entire zone.
/shownames toggles PC names on/off.
/shownpcnames toggles NPC names on/off.
/smile emote for smile.
/split # # # # splits a defined amount of money with your group: plat, gold, silver, & copper respectively...i.e. /split 4 3 2 0 splits 4 plat, 3 gold, 2 silver, and 0 copper with the rest of the group. All numbers must be present, use 0 for coins to not be split.
/stopsong stops the song you are playing if your a bard.
/surname Used at 20th level or higher to choose a surname (lastname).
/tell (playername) or /t sends a message to the specified player.
/time gives you the time of day in Norrath and Earth time.
/wave wave to targeted player.
/who and /who all this is a very broad command, basically the /who command just tells you who is in the zone. While the /who all command tells you who is in the game world. You can also use it to find specifics, for example you could type /who all cleric or /who all cleric 50. The first one would report how many clerics are in the world, the second would report how many are level 50. You can also do this by race or race/class, such as /who all barbarian rogue or /who all gnome necro. Abbreviations work fine most of the time. You can also do /who all friends to see a list of everyone who is on who is on your friends list. Or you can do a /who all GM to see any GM's on, if they are not in inviso mode =)
