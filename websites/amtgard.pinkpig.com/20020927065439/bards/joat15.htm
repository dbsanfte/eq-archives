Journal of a Traveler

This is the journal of Talies, a half-elven lad who joined the Bard's Guild on Bristlebane, and set out for numerous adventures. Although the most current page will be on the front, later pages are in order. To start at the beginning, click here.

April 15th, 3172 (Kelethin)

The trip home was uneventful enough. A couple of times I had to take a slight detour to avoid bandit camps and wandering orcs, but I soon found myself in the familiar City in the Trees.

The story I told Sylia was worthy of a song, and she said as much. As I related to her the amount and intensity of my combats she gave me some very good advice. "Buy a shield." I hadn't thought of that before, never having been that focused on combat before, but she insisted that a shield would have protected me much better than a sword would.

Since I was there, I went ahead and asked her about my progress - showing off my skill with the drum, and my mastery of the few spellsongs I knew. Sylia then gave me a rather funny look and asked me if I thought I thought I was ready for the next rank. I had to stop and think about that - was I really ready? Had I learned all I could at this rank? I finally decided that, yes, I had, and was rewarded with a huge, beaming smile, as she congratulated me on my new rank.

"You see, Talies, there comes a time when we must decide for ourselves that we are ready to move on. You have reached that point now - I feel you should be quite capable of deciding when you are ready to learn more. Self-confidence is a very important attribute for a Bard. You must believe in yourself before others will believe in you."

Having imparted that wisdom, she dismissed me, claiming time for other young Bards, and I retired to the back room to rest and perhaps pick up that song I often heard them singing back there.

"Hymn of Restoration" it's called, and it's written for stringed instruments, but for now, I think I'll keep it simple and just learn the words. The melody is complex, yet very well put-together. I find it quite relaxing, yet stimulating, a very strange combination, indeed. Truly an amazing song, and one that makes me thankful that I'm a Bard.

With this song, a shield, and some better armour, I think perhaps I can begin to adventure a little more fearlessly - tomorrow, I'll see where the winds take me!
