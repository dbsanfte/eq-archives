The History of the Fat Guys

As seen by Lord Gorlan of the Redlands

10 Dec, 1997
Like most good things it just happened. At the Double Wars in Sweden in 1994, myself and several other Americans working in Germany attended. Now if you have ever had a run in with the authenticity police, imagine an event with over 200 APs running around for a whole week. The Swedes play with gusto. Their encampments are the most period that I have seen anywhere. If you don't have a period tent, you have to set up in another area. Most of the Swedes had never seen or encountered the SCA American style. Many thought that they had invented it. But the main element that we noticed was that they were taking the game and themselves MUCH TOO SERIOUSLY.
Interject into this environment five middle-aged Americans, getting wide around the middle who had been playing the game forever. (I started playing back in 1974). We interjected some comic relief into the event. They didn't quite know what to think about us when we set up our plastic tents and Coleman stove. They did notice that we were having too much fun and as the week went by more and more of the folks were coming to our encampment.
About halfway through the event, they had a four man melee tournament. Each team had to come up with a name. As you can imagine, most of the Swedes took these serious, macho sounding, manly names, like the Red Death, or the Sharks. Well when asked what our name would be we chose "Four Fat Guys". On the bridge battles we devastated them. We came in second in the tourney. During the wars, if they needed a shield wall broken the cry was "Send in the Fat Guys!" The name stuck. For the rest of the event we were known as the "Fat Guys." By the end of the event we had come up the definition of living large. That's how it started. The only official status we have is the right to impose a beer tax at all feasts in the kingdom as long as we insure that the King will never go dry. We also host a "Beer Pit Tourney" at the Double Wars. Each entrant has to bring a beer to enter, Squires must bring two, and Knights, a six-pack. The winner of the tourney gets all the beer. We are known to ask the King quite publicly "Is this going to be a one, two, ... or six beer court?"

Explanations of the Fat Guy Songs

The Tale of Sir Axel

In Germany the word "Ausfart" means "EXIT" and is posted on all freeway exits. The word "Umlitung" means "Detour". Back in 1984 Sir Axel in court asked the visiting King of the East (Drachenwald was a principality back then) to go a quest to find the Holy Grail. He was convinced it was in the town of Ausfart. From there on the Ballad explains itself.

Obese

A Fat Guy tabard is green with a gold mug. The handle of the mug goes under the shield (to protect it). If the Fat Guy is a King or Prince (or Count, Earl, etc.) he can put foam on top of the mug. We've had a few.

Ballad of Blackleaf.

Baron Matthew Blackleaf - one the first four Fat Guys. He first Viceroy of the Crown Principality of Nordmark (Sweden) and will be King of Drachenwald this Jan. He didn't learn.

Cookie Wars

An event held every summer in Germany. It is always around some kind of cookie theme. This song was written the morning after as I gazed at the broken cookie crumbs and empty beer bottles. It wrote itself.

Three Drunken Fat Guys

Pretty much happened as the song went. In 1996 Michael of Aquitane (another of the original four and the one who came up with the name) was made a Queen's Companion being described at the personification chivalry and Grace in the Kingdom (You have to have met him to understand why half the kingdom was rolling on the floor). The King and Queen were trying hard to make us respectable. They didn't.

Thumper

One of the fat guys, Friedrich Karl von Wolfstein, fights with a huge two handed war hammer. In wars he likes to hide in tree and jump out and pound people with it. One of Michael of Aquitane's more notorious doings was to cheer on a friend in a crown tourney - shouting "Pound him into the ground like a tent peg into a grape". After his friend won and became king he augmented Michael's arms to a tent peg going through a grape.

Helmüt's Home Brew

One Fat Guy, Helmüt von Dornheim, makes cordials of really strange combinations.(He still hasn't gotten his Baloney Schnapps right yet). He has a warning label on the back of each bottle with the warning in the song.

Oh Knaeckebroed

This is a hard flat bread that has holes on one side and is flat on the back. One of the Double Wars in Sweden is the Knaeckebroed War. It is fought to determine which side of the bread will be buttered at all events for the next year. They get very passionate about the subject up there.

Count Garick's Tale of Woe
and
Where has all the Count's Beer Gone?

At last years Beer Pit Tourney, Count Garick was the winner. There was a stream running through the sites, and he built a small dam and put his winnings there. It rained that night and it all washed away into a lake (at least a case and half). He only recovered one beer.

Duke Elffin, Duchess Vanna

The first King and Queen, and now Duke and Duchess of Drachenwald. Back at our first crown tourney, Elffin had just arrived in Europe from Australia. He was knight at the time and got a waiver to allow him to fight in crown. He did not have a Lady, and asked the person he was staying with if she knew a suitable Lady he could fight for. They called up Vanna and she agreed to let him fight for her. None of them had ever seen him fight, and did not expect him to win. Vanna was not able to make it the tourney, and got a surprising phone call telling her that she was now Queen. During his first reign, he was constantly starting his sentences "In the West we..".

White Feathers/Blue Feathers

When I heard about a group of SCAdians wearing white feathers to show that did not want adult jokes or stories told around them, the song just wrote itself.

6 Beer Court In Drachenwald

Most scadians have at one time or another attended a "Court that would not end". At double wars last year , I had asked King Elffin "Is this going to be a 2, 3, or 4 beer court?. He replied, that it was going to be at least a Six. While it was a long court, I was only half way through my 4th beer by the time it ended. 6 Beer Court indeed! But it got me to thinking about a a Six Beer Court. Latter, when I heard "Little Honda" on the Radio, the chorus and Title fell into place. The awards and Honors listed are ones given out in Drachenwald.

Martin the Mercenary

If you look on the Fat Guys Web Page, there is a photo of Martin the Mercenary where his feet have left the ground while he delivers a blow.

Just Use an Axe and Mace

"Just Use an Axe and Mace" was inspired by a conversation at a fighting practice. In wars I prefer to fight with an Axe and a Mace. It's the funnest combination I've ever found.

The No-No, Go-Go Song

"The No-No, Go-Go song" came from a couple sources. At an Event last Jan, I set up a Fat Guy encampment at one side of the site. We had a large tent, portable fireplace, Beer table, Several cases of Beer, a case of single malt scotch, soft drinks. etc. We had lots of fun and good times. So did most of those who visited the camp. But one lady, took offense at my song "the Codpiece" and complained for weeks afterwards to many peers that it wasn't right since we had the only encampment at the site. Totally ignoring the fact that the feast hall was open all night. When this was finally brought to my attention a few weeks latter, my reaction was "So?". The common response from the peers was "What did you expect?, it was Fat Guy Camp." (I love living in Drachenwald). But it got me to thinking, what if I was pushing-for-a-peerage and had to "clean up my act" to please the peers. What could save me from such a respectable fate? At the Last Celtic Sheep and Garter Raids, the Fat Guy Wench and another lady pulled off drive by schmoozings with a cloven fruit at all the encampments. If that wouldn't reconvert somebody to Fun, I don't know what would.

"Get a Life" says the Baron

One of my few serious songs. A response to some silly local politics that have been dragging on for the last few years in my local group. It got so bad at times that I used to sign off my E-mails with "It's hard to live large when you live in Mayberry RFD". Leaving out specific names and incidents, the Barony got so disfunctional that it was put under a Vicker. We became a Barony again half a year ago and for the most part, things have been going well. But the "Courtiers" have started to surface again. This song is my answer to the "courtiers" as they try to drag me into their plots and schemes.

They Made Us Fight at Night

In Sweden, there is very little sun during the winter. At a few events this year they decided to try torch-light fighting. While I did not personally fight in these, I have been in enough tourneys and fighter practices where the final rounds were fought after the sun went down. The tales I have heard from those who did participate matched my experiences in night fighting.

Sir Prothal's Lament

Sir Prothal's Lament was the Idea of The Right Honorable Lord Goetz von der Krautlache. Sir Prothal has had a long standing feud with the combat archers. Last year at the Double Wars he went around and congratulated every fight who killed and archer, no matter what side they fought on. The references in the song about standing still for the Queen, came from one field battle where the then Crown Princess was chasing him all over the field shouting "Will you stand still?!" Sir Prothal is from Sweden and for some unexplained reason most of the Swedes are very fond of Mountain Dew. It is one of the preferred bribes for their mercenary fighters. They do not sell it there so it is a very precious item.
