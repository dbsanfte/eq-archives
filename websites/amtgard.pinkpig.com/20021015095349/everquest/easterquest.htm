What is an "Easter Egg?" No, we're not talking brightly-coloured poultry products. We're talking tidbits of humour that the programmers have hidden for their own egos or for our amusement. EverQuest, like many other popular computer programs, has it's share of Easter Eggs, and this site is an attempt to document many of them. (Last Updated January 6, 2002)

WARNING! There are MANY spoilers ahead! If you don't want your fun spoiled, then read no further!

Found an Easter Egg and want to share? Drop me a line! Talies the Wanderer. What is an Easter Egg? Well, it's not pictures of coordinates on walls or obvious visual bugs (like the recent spate of mip-mapping errors), or accidental mob-poses. These are not Easter Eggs (even if they are hilarious; I have a separate section for those). Neither is it random anagrams (yeah yeah, we know what Rucksif is an anagram of). Easter Eggs are pieces of code, artwork or other content deliberately "hidden" within the game, that (usually) has some sort of pop-culture significance, or other importance to the programmer(s). As you read through the site, it should be very obvious what will really qualify...

In Dagnor's Cauldron, there are two rats by the names of Flotsam and Jetsam - methinks they escaped a sinking ship somewhere... (Flotsam refers to any wreckage left in the sea after a shipwreck, while jetsam is any cargo deliberately thrown overboard and washed ashore or sunk.) Incidentally, these also happen to be the names of the two electric eels who harassed Ariel in Disney's "The Little Mermaid."
In another possible nod to Metallica (or perhaps just coincidence) Flotsam and Jetsam was also the name of a band - one of their former members (Jason Newsted) is now Metallica's bassist.
And another possibility, and the most intriguing to me, is the reference from JRR Tolkein's The Two Towers. "Flotsam and Jetsam" is the name of the chapter in which Saruman's tower, Orthanc, was destroyed by the Ents, who diverted an entire river, submerging the tower. Merry and Pippin took the names of Flotsam and Jetsam, from the idea of detritus washed up on the shore of the artificial lake. Since Dagnor's Cauldron also covers submerged ruins, this reference certainly has a lot of relevance...
