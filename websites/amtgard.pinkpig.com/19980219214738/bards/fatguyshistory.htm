The History of the Fat Guys

As seen by Lord Gorlan of the Redlands

10 Dec, 1997
Like most good things it just happened. At the Double Wars in Sweden in 1994, myself and several other Americans working in Germany attended. Now if you have ever had a run in with the authenticity police, imagine an event with over 200 APs running around for a whole week. The Swedes play with gusto. Their encampments are the most period that I have seen anywhere. If you don't have a period tent, you have to set up in another area. Most of the Swedes had never seen or encountered the SCA American style. Many thought that they had invented it. But the main element that we noticed was that they were taking the game and themselves MUCH TOO SERIOUSLY.
Interject into this environment five middle-aged Americans, getting wide around the middle who had been playing the game forever. (I started playing back in 1974). We interjected some comic relief into the event. They didn't quite know what to think about us when we set up our plastic tents and Coleman stove. They did notice that we were having too much fun and as the week went by more and more of the folks were coming to our encampment.
About halfway through the event, they had a four man melee tournament. Each team had to come up with a name. As you can imagine, most of the Swedes took these serious, macho sounding, manly names, like the Red Death, or the Sharks. Well when asked what our name would be we chose "Four Fat Guys". On the bridge battles we devistated them. We came in second in the tourney. During the wars, if they needed a shield wall broken the cry was "Send in the Fat Guys!" The name stuck. For the rest of the event we were known as the "Fat Guys." By the end of the event we had come up the definition of living large. Thats how it started. The only official status we have is the right to impose a beer tax at all feasts in the kingdom as long as we insure that the King will never go dry. We also host a "Beer Pit Tourney" at the Double Wars. Each entrant has to bring a beer to enter, Squires must bring two, and Knights, a sixpack. The winner of the tourney gets all the beer. We are known to ask the King quite publicly "Is this going to be a one, two, ... or six beer court?"

Explanations of the Fat Guy Songs

The Tale of Sir Axel

In Germany the word "Ausfart" means "EXIT" and is posted on all freeway exits. The word "Umlitung" means "Detour". Back in 1984 Sir Axel in court asked the visting King of the East (Drachenwald was a principality back then) to go a quest to find the Holy Grail. He was convinced it was in the town of Ausfart. From there on the Ballad explains itself.

Obese

A Fat Guy tabard is green with a gold mug. The handle of the mug goes under the shield (to protect it). If the Fat Guy is a King or Prince (or Count, Earl, etc.) he can put foam on top of the mug. We've had a few.

Ballad of Blackleaf.

Baron Matthew Blackleaf - one the first four Fat Guys. He first Viceroy of the Crown Principality of Nordmark (Sweden) and will be King of Drachenwald this Jan. He didn't learn.

Cookie Wars

An event held every summer in Germany. It is always around some kind of cookie theme. This song was written the morning after as I gazed at the broken cookie crumbs and empty beer bottles. It wrote itself.

Three Drunken Fat Guys

Pretty much happened as the song went. In 1996 Michael of Aquitane (another of the original four and the one who came up with the name) was made a Queen's Companion being described at the personification chivalry and Grace in the Kingdom (You have to have met him to understand why half the kingdom was rolling on the floor). The King and Queen were trying hard to make us respectable. They didn't.

Thumper

One of the fat guys, Friedrich Karl von Wolfstein, fights with a huge two handed war hammer. In wars he likes to hide in tree and jump out and pound people with it. One of Michael of Aquitane's more notorious doings was to cheer on a friend in a crown tourney - shouting "Pound him into the ground like a tent peg into a grape". After his friend won and became king he augmented Michael's arms to a tent peg going through a grape.

Helmüt's Home Brew

One Fat Guy, Helmüt von Dornheim, makes cordials of really strange combinations.(He still hasn't gotten his Baloney Schnapps right yet). He has a warning label on the back of each bottle with the warning in the song.

Oh Knaeckebroed

This is a hard flat bread that has holes on one side and is flat on the back. One of the Double Wars in Sweden is the Knaeckebroed War. It is fought to determine which side of the bread will be buttered at all events for the next year. They get very passionate about the subject up there.

Count Garick's Tale of Woe
and
Where has all the Count's Beer Gone?

At last years Beer Pit Tourney, Count Garick was the winner. There was a stream running through the sites, and he built a small dam and put his winnings there. It rained that night and it all washed away into a lake (at least a case and half). He only recovered one beer.

Duke Elffin, Duchess Vanna

The first King and Queen, and now Duke and Duchess of Drachenwald. Back at our first crown tourney, Elffin had just arrived in Europe from Australia. He was knight at the time and got a waiver to allow him to fight in crown. He did not have a Lady, and asked the person he was staying with if she knew a suitable Lady he could fight for. They called up Vanna and she agreed to let him fight for her. None of them had ever seen him fight, and did not expect him to win. Vanna was not able to make it the tourney, and got a suprising phone call telling her that she was now Queen. During his first reign, he was constantly starting his sentences "In the West we..".

White Feathers/Blue Feathers

When I heard about a group of SCAdians wearing white feathers to show that did not want adult jokes or stories told around them, the song just wrote itself.
