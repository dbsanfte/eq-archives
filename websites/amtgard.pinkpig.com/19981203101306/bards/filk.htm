The Happy Halfling's Book of Songs

This is a collection of various folk and filk songs, some traditional, some modern, some humourous, but all, I feel, appropriate to the kind of Bardics I tend to go to. Some of these come from the infamous "Black Book of Locksley," some are from the artist's themselves, and you may even find my name in there once or twice... The people who have kindly allowed me to display their work would also like a brief consideration from anyone performing it. Just be sure to give them credit when performing their works, and if you'd like to get in touch with any particular artist, drop me a line, and I'll do my best to help you out!

If there are any songs you feel I should include, or mistakes that need to be corrected, please send them to Snicker@pinkpig.com.

Most of the actual songbooks are left unorganized so that, if you want, you can just scan through them in a "random" order. The Fatguy's Songbook is courtesy of Lord Gorlan of the Redlands. The Bawdy Bits were collected by The Lady Aeruin ni Fial, and are presented here, because a certain online service censors the slightly risqué nature of these songs. Unfortunately, Aeruin's site no longer appears to be up. If you know what happened to her, please, drop me a line.

Well, we've grown to large for the old single-listing style of index, I've been forced to expand it out to several pages of listings, alphabetically. You can still flip through the individual books, if you want, or click on the first letter of the title of the song you'd like to find.
