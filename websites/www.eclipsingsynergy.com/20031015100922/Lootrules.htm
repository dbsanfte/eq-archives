Even though we only play the game for fun and never expect to win a ton of loot, upgrades in items help the guild do better things. There can not be any PUBLIC disagreements with how loot is allocated as it isn't beneficial and takes away from the person obtaining the item enjoying it to its capacity.

ANY LOOT OR ITEMS THAT WERE ALLOCATED TO YOU BY AN OFFICER OR THE GUILD LEADER ARE PROPERTY OF ECLIPSING SYNERGY AND WILL NOT BE SOLD!!!!

If you are the beneficiary of a droppable item that item may remain in your
possession until either you recieve an upgrade to that item or you choose to
leave our guild. If the item is no drop, of course you may enjoy that item for
your characters life 8). The reason behind this rule is we are a family, a
guild and we are not here to advance our personal fortunes. When a member
receives an upgrade that item that was awarded previously will be re-allocated
to another guild member.

Loot Allocation Method

Loot will be allocated by the officers of the guild and awarded to the member
that receives the most votes of the officers online. Online officers will cast
their votes in a private channel based on the following criteria:

1. Best suited class(es) for the item.
2. Online Attendence
3. Member contribution to the guild
4. Level Requirement(s) if any
5. Recent allocations to any one member or class
6. Current equipment of member (can the upgrade create another allocation)
7. Senority & Experience (Level & AA)

If there is a tie in the officer channel on allocation of the item, the Guild
Leader or Senior Officer will break the tie.

Loot will NOT be given to an alternative character unless no primary character
in attendence (or able to log in to loot) is in need of that item. Spells will
not be given to a member in which the level is not met. If a droppable item is
not needed by any primary character (present at the raid or otherwise), it will
go in the guild bank to sell for guild funds. Any member may purchase the item
from the guild bank for current market value of the item for an alternative
character. If the item is an parchment or a rune that can be turned in for a
random spell, an officer will loot that parchment or rune and turn in for
classes that provide guild assistance first.

Having said all that, these loot policies only apply to situations where more
than one group of guildees and the Guild Leader or an officer is present to make
a decision. If the Guild Leader or an officer is not present, the group or raid
force will make their own loot procedures but it is strongly suggested NBG (Need
Before Greed).

The equipment and items that a member attains themselves are property of that
member. No member will be required to turn in a FBBS they paid 1500pp for when
they get allocated a Cloak of Flames, for example. HOWEVER, if the guild member
wishes to donate the item to the guild for further allocation, this will be
taken in consideration during the officer vote.

KEYS, QUEST ITEMS

Keys (such as Sleeper Tomb keys), and quest items (such as the Temple of Veeshan
quest items) will be allocated in the follow manner:

1. Essential classes FIRST
2. Online Attendence
3. Member contribution to the guild
4. Level Requirement(s) if any
5. Quantity of parts attained to complete the quest
6. Senority & Experience (Level & AA)

Quest items that can be multi-quested will be looted by an officer that has the
requirements to multi-quest the item. Once the member allocated the right to
that item is ready to complete the quest, the officer will multi-quest that item
to them. Example, a Halls of Testing farming raid officers will loot all armor
molds, patterns, etc and will multi-quest for the members afterwards. Members
will ONLY be allowed rights to such an item if that member currently has the
faction needed to follow thru with the quest.

ONLY the Guild Leader or an officer is permitted to loot corpses on raids unless
authorized. Any member that attempts to loot, loots by accident, or ninja
loots items will be punished up to and may include guild banishment (ninja
looting or any infringement of SoE policies will also get reported to a GM and
pursued vigorously). It has happened many times, a member likes to loot to see
what is on the corpse, and instead of viewing the item.. it is now in their
inventory.

There may be times that an item is granted GUILD USAGE and will not be allocated
to a member. A good example of this would be our first one or two Blade of
Carnages off Avatar of War. Those weapons would remain in the possesion of the
Guild Leader or an officer and given to the current Main Tank for use in the
encounter until we have enuff of them to ensure each Main Tank will have one
equipped for use on raids. After the guild raid is completed, the weapon will
be returned for use on the next raid.

We are a team and all loot decisions will be made to further pursue our guild
goals, not the individual aspirations of members. Yes, the more mobs we kill,
the more loot that drops, and the more a member improves themselves they will
cain prestige. Just keep the guild in mind when loot decisions are made and
please NO WHINING!
