The Bard is an interesting and very unique class to play. More so than perhaps any other class, bards are very much group-oriented in nature. The bard gets a new song virtually every level. Examples of the bards abilities include the ability to slowly heal an entire group, to calming all creatures in the area except the one you wish to attack, to increasing the attack rate of every member of your party. A bard never has trouble finding a group wanting his/her services.

Bards don’t use mana for their songs; they can play any song indefinitely, which can be a huge advantage. However, as an offset to this, the bard songs are all meant to be effective over time. Different songs are most effective with a specific type of instrument, but most will still work even just by singing, which leaves the bard’s hands free to wield that sword.

Any type of armor is wearable by a bard, even plate mail. None of the races capable of being bards have a very impressive strength, so you may wish to consider putting some points into this, or trying to acquire some strength increasing magical items.

Half Elf: High agility and dexterity, although only average strength, still makes the half elf an excellent choice for a bard. Half elves retain infravision from their elven heritage, which is a nice asset.

Human: Humans have the highest base strength of the available races for a bard, which will be nice when it comes time for plate mail. Lack of infravision, and a lower agility and dexterity than either of the other choices tends to make most choose another option however.

Wood Elf: Wood elves have the lowest base strength of any choice, but is offset by a high agility and dexterity, plus infravision. A good choice for a bard.
