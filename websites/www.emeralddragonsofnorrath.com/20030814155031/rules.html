Communication within a guild is essential. We use it to keep in touch with one another, to ask for assistance, find groups or partners, ask questions, celebrate achievements and to chat, joke and share news of our lives. 
 
We believe in freedom of speech, we do however realize that we have many members of varying ages and beliefs, and as we do not wish to censor anyone we must respect one another. 

Guildmates are like family.  We always want to treat them with respect.  If you are invited to group/raid with another guild member, be sure you know all the details of such.  Also, if you do not have much time to play, be sure you let the group/raid know, so that they can decide if they need you.

If there is anything you see that needs to be improved, wether it be the guild web site, message board, rules, raids, etc, be sure to let us know.  You can e-mail us, send us a tell, or post it on the Message board.  We always appriciate feedback.  We cannot improve as a whole if no one speaks up when they see something that needs fixing.  One of my favorite sayings...'I can't fix it if I don't know it's broken.'
 
The general rating of guild chat should be kept to PG-13, mild language etc... slips happen at times, but abusive language and derogatory comments regarding race, age, sex, religion, and sexual orientation will not be tolerated in any form and will lead to either a warning or guild removal depending on the severity of the offense.
We do not however wish this to become an excuse for people to be language police. Most profane words will be filtered out using EQ's own filtering system. If you are offended by mild language you are encouraged to turn your filter on by typing /filter. 
 
Personal problems between members should not be aired in guild chat.Guild chat is also not an auction channel, if you have an item that you wish to offer to guild members before you try selling to the public feel free, but please do not spam everyone. We also have a space for selling on our message board, it's location is The Horde. 
 
If you have a question ask a few times, the people on might not know the answer and it should not be necessary to elicit an "I don't know" response from every guild member online. 
 
Same goes for a teleport, res etc... ask a few times. 
 
Family Matters 
If you have a problem with another guild member and can not resolve it yourselves in /Tells. Give a /Tell to an Elder or an Ancient, they will try to settle any disputes. The Venerable Fillie and/or Elders/Ancients are final. If no Elders/Ancients are on, take a step back, allow tempers to cool then discuss it. If nothing can be resolved without an officer drop it until one logs on. You may get angry and frustrated by an event but a word of warning, we have a general no return policy. If you in anger /guildremove yourself you will not be allowed back except with the express permission of the Venerable Fillie. Dramatic exits don't do anyone any good. If you have a problem/concern, talk to an Elder/Ancient or email Fillie directly. This is your family too, if you see something that you think needs fixing, help fix it. This includes problems with officers and/or the guild leader herself. We are all adult enough to handle constructive criticism, but also try to think of solutions as well. General complaining doesn't do any good without idea's to correct the problems you perceive. No guild is perfect but by working together we can improve ourselves. 
 
Power Leveling
 
If a guildmate offers to power level you that is fine. How you level your character is your business, but do not use guild chat to solicit someone to power level you and keep it out of guild chat if someone does. We do not want other guildmembers to think that it is their right to be power leveled. We are here to help one another true, but not by power leveling. 
 
Public Conduct 
The reputation of EDoN is extremely important. Most people who meet up with one or more of us have only good things to say. 
Some basic rules should be noted to avoid any confusion as to where we of EDoN stand. 
 
OOC, Shout, and Auction Channels: Use of public channels should adhere to EQ's policies. Profanity or obnoxious behavior should never be used in a public forum and never spam a zone. 

Grouping: The basic PnP should be in effect. While grouping if you must leave, try to find a replacement for yourself if possible and try to let your group know ahead of time if you must leave. Sometimes you have to log off immediately with barely a word, emergencies come up. Try not to make a habit out of it, Suddenly leaving a group looks bad on you and on us, and may hinder you obtaining a group in the future. 
 
Looting is usually decided on a per group basis, be sure everyone knows the looting rules. Ninja looting an item is grounds for immediate removal from the guild. 
 
There will be no intentional KSing or training by a Dragon. Improper conduct by one person can tarnish the entire guilds name. anyone found to be intentionally training or KSing will be given ONE warning. 
 
Accidental KSing and even false charges of KSing and Ninja looting does happen. Your word as a Dragon is generally good enough, if you are contacted by an officer do not take offense or assume we are accusing you. We need to hear your side of the story to help us defuse the situation if we are contacted. However an accusation of improper conduct by a Dragon will be taken seriously and investigated fully, if more charges are brought up and you are found guilty you will be removed from guild. 
 
Selling of items: Items made or found while hunting that you wish to sell or trade you of course are free to do so, if you choose to sell to a guild mate a price of less then market value would be appreciated, for improving a guild mates equipment helps to improve the guild itself, but it is not required. Selling to the public should be done honestly. Purposely deceiving someone on a sale/purchase or trade is not what Dragon's do. When people deal with us they should never have to question our integrity. 
 
Guild Tags: You guild tag should be visible at all times. If you must hide your class, etc. use the /roleplay switch. Which will keep your guild tag but hide your class/level/race/location. The exception to the rule would be if you are selling in the bazaar and are AFK for long periods of time.
