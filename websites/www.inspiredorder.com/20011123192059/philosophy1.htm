A Word on the Guild Philosophy

 

Inspired Order was founded because many of us wanted to belong to a guild where apathy and lack of motivation were absent. The underlying philosophy of Inspired Order can be described in two words: ACTIVE PARTICIPATION.

A healthy guild is a compact between all members and also the guild leadership. Members should be active participants in the life of their guild. This benefits themselves and benefits the rest of the guild. The guild leadership in turn works to create a positive playing environment, a functional family, coherent structure, and fun activities for all members to enjoy. Inspired Order membership is a privilege and not an entitlement. We are not a guild for loners or people who do not want to participate in the life of the guild. Those unwilling to uphold their end of the compact described above should probably look to join a different guild. This is true for prospective members and is also true for current members as well.

On the other hand, active participation is not about being a fanatic. Power gaming is welcomed, but is not our top priority. There is a place here for members advancing at different rates. We are, first and foremost, defined by our loyalty to the guild family and our commitment to being an active participant therein. All else comes second to this underlying philosophy.

So how does this work in practice? Here, we value FUNCTIONAL FAMILY above all else. For us, active participation is the key to this functionality. Active participation is qualitative rather than a quantitative. How frequently you play is less important than how you participate when you do play. Listed below are some suggestions about how members can be active participants in the guild:

  • When you log on for the first time each day announce yourself to the guild and say hello.

  • Say goodnight to the guild when you log off. Join in conversations that take place in guildchat.

  • Start your own conversations in guild chat.

  • Shout in guildchat when you ding a level.

  • If you have a question, use your guildchat to see if someone has an answer.

  • If someone asks a question in guildchat and you can help - answer them.

  • If these first few suggestions haven't been clear, here's a brief summary - USE YOUR GUILDCHAT - DON'T LURK. LOL

  • Check the website regularly. Things are frequently happening and the site is where you will learn about them.

  • CHECK THE MESSAGE BOARDS FREQUENTLY AND POST OFTEN. The conversations that take place there are integral in creating a larger sense of community within the guild. There is no excuse for members who haven't set up a board account.

  • Seek out others in the guild and group with them.

  • Perform a random act of kindness and surprise a younger guild member with your help.

  • Try to attend most of the guild events.

  • Propose and help organize guild events of your own.

  • Make it a priority to speak with and get acquainted with any guildmate you do not already know.

  • Do anything you can think of that makes the guild a better place for us all, but make sure you DO SOMETHING.
