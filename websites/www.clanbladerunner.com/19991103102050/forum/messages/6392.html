OK this is a WORD-to-WORD translation. The message of the song gets a bit "washed out", and the rhimes, well forget about the rhimes.. =)

: Apulanta: Anna mulle piiskaa

Fertilizer: Spank me

: Min‰ haluan sinut t‰nne

I want you (to come) here

: Miss‰ poliisi on yst‰v‰mme

Where the (cops) police is/are our friend(s)

: Ja ne sanoo sin‰ et ole yksin

And they tell you you're not alone

: Ja ne sanoo ja ne sanoo niin

And they say and say so

: Piiskaa hei beibi anna mulle

SPANK ME, hey baby (anna mulle=give me: so it's like give me spank or something=))

: Piiskaa hei beibi anna mulle

Spank me etc.

: Piiskaa hei beibi teeth‰n mulle niin

Spank me, will you do it

: Sit‰ samaa minun radio soittaa

"The same (tune) that my radio is "playing""

: Kuinka lopultakin el‰m‰ voittaa

How finally life wins ( i wonder what the fuck this means)

: Jane sanoo sin‰ olet kauhein

Jane (or maybe Ja ne) says that you are the prettiest/most beautiful

: Ja ne sanoo ja ne sanoo niin

And they say and they say (or maybe And they go on and go on)

: Piiskaa hei beibi anna mulle

Spank me etc.

: Piiskaa hei beibi anna mulle

Spank me etc.

Spank me etc.

: Ei oven takana sinua odota 1)
: Iloinen yst‰v‰ k‰dess‰‰n kukkia 2)

Umm.. 2)A happy friend with flowers in his/her hand 1)is NOT waiting behind the door

: Ei oven takana sinua odota
: L‰mmint‰ syli‰ ilman nauloja

This one is really tough.. Finnish is really tough. Basically this means that there isn't a person with a warm "welcoming hug" waiting
behind the door. But the "welcoming hug" also includes nails (nauloja). SO, I guess it means that altough the welcoming is friendly, the person that hugs you has nails in his/her arms. So it's like a double-edged sword (hahaha).
