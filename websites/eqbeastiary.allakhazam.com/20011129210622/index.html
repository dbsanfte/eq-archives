    08/31/2001 Judging from the number of Simcam virii I still recieve on a daily basis, most EQ players don't care about security. Well in this case please do. According to an article in the Seattle Times, Sony/Verant/etc.. has experienced a fairly serious security breach.

    From the article:

    Through that account, they gained access to the private accounts of Sony employees, a password-protected messaging board and "all other customer accounts."

    "Access to these accounts gives the superuser an individual's name, date of birth, work and home telephone numbers, Internet protocol address and other information," including home addresses, according to an affidavit filed with the warrant.

    Moreover, superuser status also "enables the user to have complete administrative controls over the game, including the suspension, banning and unbanning of accounts."

    I don't normally get all up in arms about scams and whatnot, I figure if you're silly enough to send your EQ account info to anyone, you deserve what you get. In this case it appears there is a chance that if the article isn't being overblown sensationalistic journalism, then any and all EQ accounts may have been comprimised. (last October mind you)

    Anyhow, now's the time to err on the side of caution and change your EQ password. (no, not the site password, the game password via the interface in-game). Hopefully, the publicity from this will make them rethink their policy of banning people whose account info is stolen. (or as Lum said, just ban Brad now ;)

    --Illia
