    08/17/2000 Phew! It's done. The comments engine which appears beneath most every page on this site has just been upgraded. It's a big change. It has a number of new features, which should make it easier to cut through the comments to the good ones.

  • You must login to be able to assign your name to the comments, though once you do, you have the option of posting under the anonymous name as well.
  • You may rate messages and filter messages based on the ratings they recieve.
  • As the messages that you post begin to gather ratings, their combined rating will give you a title (if their combined rating is a good one :). This should help you track the really good posters.
  • You may now reply in a threaded manner to comments.
  • If you really liked the old way of doing the comments, just set your preferences to "flat, always expand @ awful, oldest first, filter @ below average" and check the save box when you change your preferences - you won't be bothered again.

    Well, hope everyone likes it... please let me know if any bugs have crept in :)

    06/20/2000 Happy Summer Everyone! May we all not spend the whole thing glued to EQ :) I've made some updates to the FAQ, and more will be coming soon.

    One thing I didn't add yet is the answer to "Why was my post deleted?". Well, no posts are actually deleted, even the rude/completely off topic/trolls, etc. they're just "scored" lower. Posts are scored lower because they're a) hypothetical (ie: veeshan exists here...) b) answered questions c) plain rude or insulting to others d) redundant or made redundant by further posts or e) I hit the wrong button while moderating ;)

    Conversely a post may be scored up if it contains information which is particularily relevant to the Creature/Zone/Faction. This could be a particularily funny post, or an excellent tactic on how to assail the given creature.

    I tend to score more posts down than I do up.

    Regardless of how I (and the few other moderators) score posts, you have the ability to see as much or as little of the discussion as you like. At the top of the comments area you can select what score you want to filter out at (by default higher scores are always included). Browsing with a score of 0 will pick up more than browsing at a score of 2. A filer of 1 is default.

    --Illia
