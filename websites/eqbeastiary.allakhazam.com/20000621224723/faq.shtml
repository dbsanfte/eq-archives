    How do I submit images?
    User submissions are the lifeblood of this site. With a database of over 5,000 creatures and NPCs, it can be overwhelming for me to keep up with filling in all the details. Any screenshots of npcs, and beasts are welcomed and encouraged.

    To submit an image, send it as an email attachment to Illia. Images should be sent either as JPEG or BMP format. If you're sending a JPEG image, please save the image with a quality of (at least) 8/10. This allows the best possible image quality with reasonable image size. If you're sending bitmaps (.BMP files) please compress them with a program such as winzip first as a full screen BMP at 1024x768 is about 2.5Mbytes in size.

    Why didn't my image or creature addition get used?
    For the "Submit Creature" form, the data gets entered into a temporary table so that all data can be validated (and the juck removed). In some cases, your creature may have already been in the database under a different zone or location.

    How does the search work?
    All in all, the search is pretty simple. The search doesn't really do any 'thinking' on your behalf, it just looks at what you've typed and tries to find the smallest set of data which matches all your search terms.

    If you enter one word, the search will simple look in the fields: name, loot, quest, zone and faction and if your term is in any of those fields, the result gets displayed. Hence a search for "bone" returns barbed bone skeletons, skeletons who carry bone chips and all the creatures of Crushbone.

    If you enter two or more terms, the search will simply split them up on word boundaries, build the same query as above, and make sure that BOTH terms match. For example, a search for "bone chip" will return all the creatures who have the term bone in their search fields, AND also have the term chip in their search fields; thus returning only skeletons who drop bone chips.

    If you type a number into the search box, it only searches the level information for you (in addition to whatever else you type. Hence a search for "Froglok 30" will try and find all the creatures who match Froglok, and whose level is near 30. Near here means + or - one level on either side, to try and catch near miss monsters

    Hope that helps.

    Where did you collect all this?
    Much of the data was compiled from a spreadsheet available from www.k2.org (used with permission). This provided simply creature name, level information, and (sometimes) which zone it is found in. Much data has been user submitted, and where possible, replaces the info from K2. Also, the group that I hunt with has been very polite in allowing me to drag them to various places to gather info.
    Level info is great, but what about loot and what not?
    Many of the creatures in the database are in "placeholder" mode. Little to nothing is either known about them, or I simply haven't had time yet to fill in the loot/description/quest information. Eventually, I would like every MOB to have additional information.
