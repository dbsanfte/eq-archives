1/25/03 - Exodus Puts a Halt to Recruiting!

Exodus Fellowship is becoming very well known for it's kindness, kinship, and friendly atmosphere. The word has gotten out. This has provided us with a recent influx of new members. We have been blessed with some new and very good members, but many of us feel that we are growing too fast. In fact, some of us have not had a chance to get to know everyone yet. Therefore, we have decided to put a halt on recruiting temporarily. Let's take this time to get to know one another, and look inward for help and guidance, rather than outward. Reach a hand out to a fellow guildmate that you have not met before. Group with that quiet guy that you don't know yet. We are all here together and should help each other, and grow together.

There will be a FEW exceptions to the no-recruiting policy. Real life friends and family are still welcome, as are alts. Exodus has a few weak areas as far as what classes we have. Applicants that fill one of these weak areas MAY be considered. The High Council has placed Cobo and Ashmael in charge of deciding what exceptions are necessary. Please help them in their new roles.

Thanks everyone!

1/23/03 - Awards!

On Monday, January 13th, The Exodus Fellowship met in the EC tunnel and some announcements and awards were distributed (along with some fine ales!). Here is an account of what awards were bestowed:

The Medal of Contribution is given to those members who go above and beyond the call of duty when it comes to helping the guild, either in game, or out of the game. This medal goes to Niniane, Tacomir, Karmyn, Rivin, Ashmael, Evellyn, Boffle, and Chonn. Please congratulate these members and thank them for their support.

The Rescue Medallion is given to those members who are successful in retrieving corpses from very dangerous places. Karmyn has been awarded this Medallion for her work in Dalnir. Congratulations, Karmyn!

One of the most coveted awards is the Lemming Award. This is earned by dying repeatedly in less than necessary situations.

This esteemed award has been given to Gilgamesch for bravely binding in the ballroom in Mistmoore, and falling asleep at the keyboard during a wipeout. Grats, Gil, you earned it!

Strangely, and on a separate occasion, Chastisy tried to live up to the example set by Gil in Mistmoore, and bound herself in the ballroom. Later, without realizing she was still bound there, she gated. Several deaths later, grats on your award!

There was another incident in the Hole. Apparently Chastisy was leading Ashmael and Tanluin through Paineel. Well, she fell into the Hole. Like true Lemmings, Ashmael and Tanluin were right behind her. Grats guys!

The Good Conduct Medal is given to members who continually show selflessness and kindness to their fellow guildmates. These guildies are a blessing to have among our ranks. The Good Conduct Medal goes to Daliddar, Krugan, Vessago, and Karmyn. Your work is much appreciated guys. Keep it up!

Last, but not least, the guild's highest award was given out. The Medal of Honor. This award is not to be taken lightly, and is not given often. However, there has never been someone more deserving of this award. In fact, this person is LONG overdue for the Medal of Honor. He has continuously provided guidance, support, and his wisdom. Without his help, the guild would not even exist, and definately would not be the best guild on Povar. Please congratulate the "Father of Exodus Fellowship", Kamdar Entil'Zha on his newly awarded Medal of Honor.

To all the awards recipients, congratulations, and thank you!
