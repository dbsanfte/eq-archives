1. The raid you plan should be for your level or lower.

 

 2. Ensure attendance by advertising your raid (Guild MOTD, News section, Calendar and forum) and planning far enough in advance for word to get around.

 

 3. Plan only one raid at a time. This reduces confusion and allows others a chance to plan raids. You can plan multiple raids and announce them, however they should be far enough apart to allow others to squeeze in their needs.

4. Have clear goals and announce the goals to the raiding party so that everyone is on the same page.

5. Announce how to handle loot prior to raid. Need before greed, alpha, first loot first serve, or magic dice rolling.

6. Create chat channel with a name easy to spell or know (Example: /join Brewer {gnoll brewer raid in Blackburrow})

7. Choose group leaders who you know will conduct themselves well with you, or that you know personally

8. Establish "safe spots" when moving to the raid area. Healers need to med to keep their mana. Oh yea, no mana, no heal. Same with other casters.

9. Make sure everyone knows not to pass you unless running to next safe spot, and stand where people shouldn't pass.

10. Know the area very well before you get there. Study maps on eqatlas and print them out. If possible, scope out the area before the raid.

11. Explain politely how to use necessary things such as /assist and not to break mez to people who don't understand. Try  to be patient with people who are following the rules and trying.

12. You have the option to remove people from the raid who are  transgressing or going against your commands in the raid.

13. You are not required to wait for people who are late nor  send them guides. This choice is at your discretion.
