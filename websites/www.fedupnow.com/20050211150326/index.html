Creating Directories Using P2KTools
 

( Posted by: Rassilon on Tuesday, January 25, 2005 - 09:04 PM CST )

Follow these directions for creating an additional skin directory (XPBlue) for the Windows XP Mobile v4.0 Blue Scheme) via P2KTools v0.6.3.0:

1. Download the flex file zip required to make additional directories:

Netuddki_flex.zip

2. Extract all *.hs files to some directory.

3. Open Motorola PST (6.7_GENERAL)
- Go to File > Open > pFlex
- Browse to directory containing *.hs files, select XPBlue.hs and press the "Open" button.
- A dialog entitled "pFLEX" will open. Just hit the "Run" button.
(PST will suspend your phone, and the flex will be added.)

4. Success message will display. Close PST.

5. Open Bezol's P2KTools v0.6.3.0
- Change to P2K Mode (if necessary)
- Connect (press the "Connect" button.)
- Optional: Press the "Refresh" button (left side, below) to view phone's directory tree.
- Using the "Use upload path" field at the lower left, type "/a/mobile/skins/XPBlue/"
- Press "Upload" button (left, middle)
- (Browse dialog opens.) Go to your Windows XP Mobile Blue v4.0 "skins" folder and rename (if necessary) your skin files to:

XPBlue1.dat
XPBlue2.dat
XPBlue.dat
XPBlue.ski

When renaming is complete, select all skins files in directory and press the "Open" button.

- P2KTools will automatically create
the "XPBlue" folder under your phone's "/a/mobile/skins" directory and upload the skin files (all with file attribute 07000000; world readable/writeable)

6. (Optional) Bring your phone out of suspend mode, re-connect to P2KTools, and refresh the directory tree to verify that the XPBlue skin directory now exists and that the skin files (*.dat, .ski) exist within.

7. Bring your phone out of suspend mode (if necessary), go to Settings > Personalize > Skin

...and select XPBlue for your skin.

See the readme.txt included with your Windows XP Mobile Blue ZIP to understand how to upload the wallpaper, theme, custom startup/shutdown graphics and sounds, etc.

---

Use this same process to add other skins to your phone.

- Chris (Pagefusion)

How to bluejack with a V500, v551 & V600

Type: Phone
Make: Motorola
Model: V500, v551 & V600

Instructions:

1. Go to Phone Book
2. Select New Entry
3. Insert the message you want to send in Name, email address etc.
4. Save contact
5. The contact will now be highlighted in the phone book
6. Press the Info button (one with 3 lines on)
7. Scroll to Send and select Send
8. Choose Bluetooth
9. Select Look For Devices
10. Then select the device you want to bluejack
11. That's it!


 
 

Replacing Skins on The Motorola V3, e398, v551
 

( Posted by: Langly on Monday, December 20, 2004 - 07:01 PM CST )

This guide describes to change the skins on a Motorola Razr V3. It worked for me but I make no guarantees about your phone. If you follow this guide it is AT YOUR OWN RISK! Read the whole of this guide before starting to do any changes.

I believe that this process is the same (or very similar) for e398 and v551 phones and that skins are interchangeable between the e398, v551 and V3 but I have not tested this.

Firstly I assume that you have a working copy of p2kman and can connect correctly to your V3. There are numerous threads on how to set up this application on the Howard Forums (http://www.howardforums.com/forumdisplay.php?s=&forumid=38)

Method

Currently you cant add new skins you can only replace existing skins so you should make sure your phone is not using the skin you plan to replace. Decide which skin you are going to over write; in this example I will be over writing the Scarlet skin.

1.

Download the files for the skin you want to use and save them somewhere on your PC. There will be a .ski file and one or more .dat files e.g.
Newskin.ski
Newskin.dat
Newskin1.dat
2. Run p2kman and plug the USB cable into your V3. Once the phone is connected you should see the word connected in the bottom left corner.



3.

Now click the Update list button to get a tree in the left hand side of the window (patience, it takes a while):



4.

Expand the tree until you can see the skins folder and click on the folder of the skin you want to replace (e.g. Scarlet) which will show you the files for that skin in the right hand side of the window.



5.

Take a back up of the files in that folder, highlight them all and click the Phone >>> PC button. You will be prompted for a location to save the files. (Save them in a different location to your new skin files.)




You will need these files to return to the original skin. Note the name of those files, in the case of the scarlet skin they are:
Scarlet.ski
Scarlet.dat
Scarlet1.dat
Scarlet2.dat

You will need to remember the name portion, i.e. the part before the dot in the .ski file, in this case Scarlet. Remember the names are case sensitive.
6.

Next you will need to delete those files from your phone, in order to do this you will need to change the attributes of the files. The attributes will be listed in the right hand portion and should be 70004 for each file. The 4 says that it is a system file and cant be deleted.

So for each file in turn click on it to highlight it, type 70000 in the attributes box and click Change. You wont see the attributes change until you click the update list button again.



7.

Once the attributes are changed highlight all the files in the skin directory and click the Delete button. Click the Update list button and the files should have disappeared from the right hand side.



8.

Go to the folder on your PC where you saved the new skin files and rename all the files to match the skin you are replacing e.g.
Newskin.ski Scarlet.ski
Newskin.dat Scarlet.dat
Newskin1.dat Scarlet1.dat
Newskin2.dat Scarlet2.dat
Newskin3.dat Scarlet3.dat
etc.

Remember the names are case sensitive.
9.

Then with the skin directory still displayed in p2kman click the PC >>> Phone button and when prompted select all the new skin files and click Open.



The files will be copied to the phone. Click the Update list button and you should see the new files listed on the right hand side (it seems that you dont have to change the attributes back to 70004).



10.

You can now disconnect the USB cable and try out the skin.
11.

Some skin designers include graphics files with matching wallpaper and screensaver, for example the Black skin. If this is the case you simply need to use p2kman to copy them to the picture folder on the phone or, if you prefer, use mobile Phone Tools (as supplied with your V3). You can then use the standard phone set up menus to select the appropriate files.


  
 

Comprehensive MIDway Walkthrough/Guide
 

( Posted by: Langly on Saturday, December 18, 2004 - 07:17 PM CST )

Note: MIDway is a Windows application used to transfer Java games and apps to Motorola GSM phones using a serial or USB data cable.

- This walkthrough makes several assumptions.

1. That you have a Motorola phone with the Java App Loader enabled in your Java Settings Menu.
(If not, then you need to STOP and use PST, or P2KMAN + seem editing to enable it)
Note: SEEM 0032_0001 - Offset 43 - Bit 0 value=1 to enable, 0 to disable).

2. You have a USB data cable designed specifically for Motorola phones and have successfully used it to connect to Motorola Phone tools, p2kman or PST. In other words, the USB drivers should be installed and working properly. (Serial cable users: This walkthrough was written specifically for USB cable owners but the steps to use MIDway with your cable are nearly identical)

3. You should be running Windows XP or 2000, and know how to use it. While the steps for older versions of Windows are VERY similar, I intend to have screenshots below & I do not have screens for those operating systems, but the similarities should be close enough for you to find the necessary information.

4. You have the MIDway program downloaded and ready to execute. (Version 2.8 in this case)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Before beginning, your PC and phone should be powered on, and your data cable should be connected to your phone only, but
DO NOT , I REPEAT DO NOT connect the usb cable to the PC at this time. Only connect the larger end to the phone.

STEP 1:
On your phone press the Menu button, and navigate to Settings, then Java Settings, then highlight the Java App Loader option(should be at the top)
and select it. You should then see a screen that says Insert cable Now

Insert the other end of the USB cable into your pc now. After you do this, you should see a brief screen on the phone that says 'Jal link is active...',
then it should return back to the Java Settings Screen. If not, then you have USB driver issues that need to be resolved before proceeding.

STEP 2:
On your PC, open the control panel, then open the Phone and Modem options applet and click the Modems Tab at the top.

At this point your screen should look something like this:
.


IMPORTANT: Take note of the Com port number listed (COM 3 in this example). This information is also located in the device manager under
Motorola USB modem properties, Advanced tab, Advanced Port settings button.

Once you've made a note (mental or otherwise) of the com port number, close the control panel and the Phone & Modem options applet.

STEP 3:

At this point you should run the Midway program from wherever you have it stored on your particular pc.
Once opened, your screen should look like the one below:

.

Note: If you receive an error that states "Cannot open serial port! Would you like to change MIDway settings,"click 'YES' and skip ahead to STEP 5


STEP 4:

Click on File, then Settings as I did in the screen capture below.


STEP 5:

Once done that will bring you to the screen below:
At this point you should change the COM port to match what was listed earlier in the control panel.
Hopefully you remember it or wrote it down. In addition, the baud rate should be set to 115200 and the AT command
box should be left blank. Once you've confirmed these settings click the 'OK' button and move along to STEP 6:
.


STEP 6:

Click on the 'Open JAD' button on the program, circled in red below:


Browse to the location on your PC where you have your JAVA game or App stored. (MIDway is designed to only look for files
with an extension of .JAD, so if you only have files with a .JAR extention then you need a program such as jadmaker
or jadgen to make a JAD file in order for MIDway to recognize it )

Once you've highlighted the one you want to transfer to the phone, click on the Open button as shown below.


STEP 7:

Once you've done that, you will see the screen below, click on the 'Send JAD' button that's circled in red.


After you click this button, the screen on your PHONE should then change and show details on the game/app
you chose (Name, Size, Version & Vendor).
NOTE: If you receive a TIMED OUT error ,then verify your COM port is set correctly by returning to STEP 4

Assuming that the communications between the phone & pc are normal, your options at the bottom of the phone's screen
should be CANCEL or DOWNLD. Press Download and the transfer should begin.
This may take some time depending on the size of the game/app in question, but the phone should show a download progress meter.

Once the transfer is done you should see a screen that says "Download Complete", then another that says "installing.."

At this point, the game or app will either install successfully, fail or give an error message stating "Application installed but may have limited functionality"
In those cases the game should still run, but parts of game may not function, such as sound or the controls (This varies depending entirely on the game/app in question)

The phone should then prompt if you want to run the game/app or not.
If you DO, I recommend you close MIDway on the PC first, then disconnect the cable from phone & pc and enjoy your new game.

If you choose not to play the game and select 'NO,' you can then select another game or app to transfer by going back to step STEP 6
