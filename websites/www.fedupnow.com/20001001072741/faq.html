What are the system requirements for COX@Home?

COX@Home supports either:

  • Pentium equivalent with Windows 95/98 or NT 4.0 or higher with 16MB of RAM or higher.
  • Macintosh PowerPC with OS 7.6.1 or higher and 24MB of RAM or higher.

 

What is the COX@Home service?

The COX@Home is the Internet service that delivers the full power of the Net to your computer using a cable instead of a phone line. With COX@Home, you get the Internet up to 100 times faster than a 28.8 phone modem. And, since COX@Home uses cable, you don't have to bother with a phone line ever again. No dialing in, no busy signals, no unexpected disconnects, absolutely no hassles! COX@Home provides all the features your family needs to enjoy the Web, including: a customized browser, unlimited use for a low monthly fee, multiple email accounts, personal WebSpace, 24/7 technical support, search & navigation tools, and chat and newsgroup access. Not to mention a whole new dimension of revolutionary content you can't find anywhere else. Watch video clips on demand. Listen to CD-quality music while you surf. Pummel your rivals in the hottest online games -- all without the delays experienced on typical dial-up connections.

How fast is COX@Home?

@Home’s broadband technology offers speeds far greater than dial-up phone modems, ISDN and even ADSL. For example, a file that takes 9 minutes to download over a 28.8 phone Modem would take 2 minutes on ISDN, compared to 2 seconds on COX@Home. Actual speed experienced is dependent on several factors, including:

  • Your computer performance and configuration
  • Accessing non-cached or cached data
  • Performance characteristics of each component of the data network
  • File size
  • The number of users and overall network traffic and all users compliance with the @Home Network's Acceptable Use Policy
  • The part of the world the website you are accessing.
  • The time of day. (3 p.m. until 10 p.m. is the heaviest internet traffic)

 

How Much will the COX@Home service cost?

COX@Home's standard monthly service charge is $39.95 and $15.00 for the modem rental per month if you are not a COX Cable customer. If you have purchased a cable modem and are a COX Cable customer then the monthly service cost is $29.95.  Please call 1-504-304-7345 for more information.

What software is included with the COX@Home service?

COX@Home includes a customized version of either Netscape Communicator or Internet Explorer with the most popular tools on the Internet such as RealPlayer, Shockwave, and QuickTime. In addition, you also get the @Home Assistant and a Network Diagnostic tool for trouble-shooting.

What hardware is included in the COX@Home service?

COX@Home will help you get all the hardware you need to get up and running. This includes a cable modem and equipment to connect it to your computer or you may be able to purchase your own cable modem. Either way, we'll make sure you have everything you need to have a great COX@Home experience.  (PCMCIA cards for laptops are not provided by COX@Home)

How much will the COX@Home service cost?

COX@Home’s monthly service charge is $39.95 if you are not a COX Cable customer and $29.95 if you already are a COX Cable customer. This is for unlimited usage and 24/7 connection.

Are there additional installation or setup fees?

The installation fee is $150 and may be discounted at certain times.

If I don't subscribe to cable TV, can I still get the COX@Home service?

Yes. Depending on your home, apartment, and availability, a connection from the neighborhood cable system to the desired location within your home may be necessary. However, you are not obligated to subscribe to a cable television service to receive COX@Home.

Can I have more than one email address?

COX@Home supports up to 7 email addresses as part of the monthly fee.

What Web browsers does COX@Home support?

COX@Home currently supports a customized version of Netscape Communicator v4.6 and Internet Explorer v5.

Does the COX@Home service include chat and newsgroup options?

COX@Home offers access to all of your favorite chat rooms and newsgroups on the Web, plus @Home-only newsgroups where you can discuss ideas, opinions, and information about the service with fellow subscribers. @Home editors host regular chat events on issues, including the ever-popular News Trivia Challenge where participants compete to win cool prizes.

Does COX@Home offer a dial-up number for access when I am away from home?

COX@Home's Remote Access Services allow you to check your email and connect to the Internet when you're away from home. With COX@Home's Dial-Up Access service, you can use a telephone modem to access COX@Home and the Internet. You can read and send email, get the latest news, visit your favorite Web sites, chat rooms, and newsgroups -- everything you would do if you were at home. We provide toll-free coverage throughout North America (additional fee applies). COX@Home also offers a NetMail service which lets you easily check your COX@Home email from any computer with an Internet connection and an email client supporting the POP interface (such as Netscape, Internet Explorer, and Eudora do).

Can I watch TV and use COX@Home at the same time?

Yes. Neither television nor data signals are affected by simultaneous use.

How can I get cable from the TV in my living room to the PC in my bedroom?

One of the advantages of using COX@Home is that, in most cases, it uses the existing cable that is already in your home. This cable is capable of carrying both video signals and data signals at the same time. While it may only be necessary to install a splitter on the existing cable, due to the potential of introducing "noise" or interference into the cable system, a cable technician will perform the installation of the splitter and cable outlet in the secondary location. If user-installed splitters are already present, the technician will perform quality assurance tests before deeming the service operational, and, if necessary, will rewire the outlet to meet proper specifications.

Can I hook up more than one computer to my cable modem?

COX@Home offers Multiple Computer Access to each of its subscribers. You can hook up additional computers to COX@Home and have all computers online simultaneously. We provide up to three additional IP addresses so that you can have your own home network (additional fee applies).

Can I attach a server to your network?

No. The COX@Home service is for residential, casual use only and does not support or allow servers of any kind from the home.

Can I use any TCP/IP stack with the COX@Home service?

Yes. However, COX@Home currently supports only Windows 95, 98, NT 4.0 Workstation, and MacOS 7.6.1 with Open Transport v. 1.1 or greater TCP/IP stacks.

How will mutiple users impact the speeds of COX@Home?

COX@Home can operate up to a total of 27 Mb/s on a given sub network, with no noticeable impact with multiple users. After that point, as total bandwidth is allocated evenly among users, a degradation will be noticed. The network is engineered however, to always provide faster than ISDN speed.

Should the COX@Home business grow to the extent that there are more customers requesting service than the allocated bandwidth can accommodate, the cable operator will expand resources and open-up another channel for the service.

Will I be able to maintain speed during rush hour?

COX@Home is more than just a cable modem connection to the often bottlenecked Internet. Through the use of @Home's proprietary network technology, we can maintain high speed connections that are consistently as well as considerably faster than dial-up or ISDN offerings at highly competitive prices.

Who owns the @Home Network?

Investors in the company include Comcast Corporation, Cox Communications, Rogers Cablesystems Limited, Shaw Communications, Inc. and Tele-Communications, Inc. (TCI), and venture capital firm Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers . Its partnerships with Comcast, Cox,  InterMedia Partners, Marcus Cable, Rogers, Shaw and TCI provide distribution access to nearly 50% of all homes passed by cable in North America; additional affiliated cable operator agreements will further increase coverage. @Home Network's mission is to provide a high-speed, fully integrated multimedia service that revolutionizes the way people interact with information and each other at home, at work and at school. 

Are there privacy issues involved with hooking up to the COX@Home service?

Certain networking options under the Macintosh or Windows 95 operating systems, or third party network software such as Netware or LAN Manager may present privacy issues you need to consider. One such example is file and print sharing capability which, when enabled, is intended to allow two or more computers in a member's home to share files and printers. Such capabilities must be disabled before connecting to the COX@Home network to ensure that other COX@Home members, or anyone else on the Internet, cannot gain access to your files and folders.
