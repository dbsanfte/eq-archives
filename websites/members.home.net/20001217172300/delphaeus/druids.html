Swyft Wynd

Growing up in Kelethin is one of the greatest blessings that Karana can giveh. Each blade of grass, each drop of rain, every precious moment is filled with the magic and mystery that the good gods of Karana bring to Norrath. For me, the early years I spent in Greater Faydark were filled with wonder and adventure. Kelethin, the city of trees, is the home of my wood elven kin. Like mybrother Delphis, I was brought up in the Heartwood family. My parents are Druids of the woods and constantly traveling to far off places. Being a freind of the family, Heartwood agreed to watch after and help raise me while my parents were away, which ended up being most of the time.

Growing up in a family without my parents did not seem trange. Most elvenkind (of the woods) were a close family who worked together in a tightly knit community. What was odd, however, was the way Heartwood was bringing up my brother Delphis (I call him my brother since we grew up in the same family, but who his real parents are is a mystery). There were few Humans on all of Faydwer, not to mention in the wood elf city. The circumstances of this human boy being here were very unusual and some in Kelethin were a bit uncomfortable about it. But to me, Delphis was just another part of the family, and a fun big brother to have. Many adverntures did we have during our years together in Faydwer. The bond with the wolves that Delphis had was something I could understand, if only partially. As a Druid, I had a strong bond with wolves himself. Indeed, one of the most popular spells a Druid has is to impart a wolven blessing on his companions. But Humans grow up faster than wood elves, and it came to pass that Delphis left his home in Kelethin in search of his family.

Back at home it was becoming increasingly more clear what was expected of me. The Crushbone orcs were a constant menace, the brownie scouts made frequent raids, and the undead refused to stay in their earthly graves. The call to battle these evil forces was a loud one that I heard clearly. The way of the Druid seemed a natural one. Following the footsteps of my parents, and growing up in the Heartwood household, this choice was easy. It was a very fun path to follow too. The channeling of natural forces and using them to aid others was one of my favorit things to do, second only to blasting the enemies of Faydark with balls of flame.

But even with the hoardes of orcs, undead, and goblins, it seemed as though Greater Faydark was safe from immediate threat. Clan Crushbone had been conquered long ago and only served as training grounds for young wood elves and high elves anymore. The unholy estate of Unrest was filled with treasure seekers as well. The ghost of Unrest and the hags that guarded it were a very rare sight these days. Butcherblock was in full control of the stout Dwarves, and nobody really knows what happens in and around Ak'anon. So, when Delphis returned to Kelethin one day and spoke of the many adventures he'd had in Antonica, I asked to go back with him to Antonica.

"A large and dangerous place is the continent of Antonica." Delphis told me."You will need to use all of your Druidic ways to survive there, and it will be important for you to make good friends there as soon as possible. Once we get there my jouneys take me South to the evil lizard stronghold of Cazic-Thule, and that is a journey you cannot survive. I will leave you in the hands of my friends in Misty Thicket, the Halflings. Don't let their size fool you for they are the stoutest of heart and fierce warriors of the Wall." With that it was decided. Heartwood knew that I was ready to go forth and discover the deeper ways of Karana on my own. Before we left Faydark though, Delphis did a great deed and quested for the magical brass shield in Crushbone. Equipped with this mighty shield and my spell book I headed off to Antonica with my brother.
