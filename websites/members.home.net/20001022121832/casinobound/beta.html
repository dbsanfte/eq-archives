  February 1, 2000
When does Beta testing begin, and when can we start playing?

Brad McQuaid:Beta testing will be on a much smaller scale this time, in that we really only have zones and content to test and a few changes to gameplay (increased level limit etc.). Because of this, we don't need many testers, and the current plan is for beta testing to an invite only process, similar to the original EverQuest's Phase .5 and Phase 1. Beta testing will probably begin in early February.

  December 20, 1999
Ok here are some nice beta shots of the The Ruins of Kunark from the big boy toys.com

*update*Expected Release:April 24th, 2000

Alpha Movie(1838K / 28sec length)


Upon installing the expansion, you'll be faced with two options: 1.- Take your current character and hop on a boat leaving either Antonica or Faydwer, heading south of Faydwer to the new continent of Kunark. Or, 2.- Choose to create a new Iksar (lizardman--see the images and movie on the right) character, a native to the lands of Kunark. The choice of classes for the Iksar (a neutral/evil race) has not yet been finalized, but when we last asked Verant, they were planning towards offering Monks, Warriors, Rogues, Shadow Knights, and Shaman. An innate ability of most, if not all, Iksar will be a new tail attack skill--we're hoping for the Troll-like feature of health regeneration, too.
Surrounding their city and making up the continent, you'll find zones of all sorts--jungles, caves, crypts, dungeons, and.... of course... ruins. Again, these areas will vary in difficulty, providing players of all levels with a place to hunt for new treasure and battle new monsters like the Forest Giant and Giant Scorpion, Grey Dragons, and man-eating plants. Verant has managed to create this portion of Norrath with larger textures and three times the number of polygons used in the current EverQuest. Not only will the scenery be better in Kunark, the NPCs will have a far more detailed look to them.
Anyone who does not pick up the expansion pack will still be able to see the Iksar players that come to visit the existing lands of Norrath, and they'll be able to buy & trade the items that are brought back from Kunark, they just won't be able to travel to Kunark and play as an Iksar. Those same people may even be given a new skill..."You have become better at enduring ridicule by your peers! (2)"

The countdown to Kunark has begun; Verant says they're shooting for March, speculation has the game arriving on*update* April 24th, the one year anniversary of EverQuest's release. With the arrival of RoK, Norrath still won't be finished growing, new zones will continue to be added (via patch) and yet another new continent (read: another expansion pack) is planned. This brings new meaning to the EQ term "Life-Tap".
