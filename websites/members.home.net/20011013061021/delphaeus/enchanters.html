Enchanters

Never underestimate the power of the mindA

Delphic

Those inside the Storm's Retreat turned and looked in bewilderment as the light around the stout doors gradually grew brighter and brighter.

Swinging wide the doors and flooding the entire tavern with a shimmering coalescence of bluish-white light, Delphic serenely entered. Standing just a few steps past the doorway he looked around from face to face recognizing those he once called friends. Would they recognize him and remember all the wonderful times shared adventuring?

The enchantment of light faded as Delphic happily saw the face of Kaasa and walked over to the bar to greet her. "Kaasa, what you have given to the community of Norrath is a wonderful thing. No longer are we plagued by the incompetence of gods who know not what they do and inflict great harm upon their subjects. Truly, it is through your perseverance and hard work that I am able to reacquaint myself with so many good people, and to share my tale of sorrow and rebirth."

Kaasa filled a crystal goblet with fine Elven Wine and smiled as she handed it to Delphic. Sipping it slowly, he shared his story with all that would listen…

"Much faith did I have in Karana to guide me in all things, great and small. My typical day would be to rise up with the sun, swim in the great river of the Plains, hunt and track all day long, pass the evenings mastering the serene art of fletching around a cozy campfire, and eventually retiring under the awesome bows of great Treants. It was during one of these evenings of fletching and bowcrafting that I struck upon the notion that in order to complete my mastery of the art, I must craft the Enchanted Bow, Raincaller. Clearly, such a task would be demanding but I had no idea what was in store for me.

"The most logical choice for me to start my search was in Felwithe, at the Enchanter's hall. After braving the perilous boat ride through the Ocean of Tears yet again, I made my way to Kinool Goldsinger and pleaded my case. 'Master Kinool, I am a Ranger of Karana and seek the greatest prize, the Enchanted Bow of Rain', I stated. 'Is it within your powers to call upon great enchantments and transform my Trueshot Longbow into this greatest of bows?’

"Kinool looked at me long and hard with a gaze that made me wonder if he was reading my mind. Without a word to me, he began reciting the incantations and mystical ritual movements for the spell of Enchantment. I thought I saw a flicker of mischief in his eyes as the powerful magic swirled and weaved around the hall filling it with many shimmering colors. I stood there completely mesmerised as the colors shifted and fluxed around me. Although I could feel the magic starting to weave itself into my being, there wasn't anything I could do to stop it, and even if I could stop this, I'm not sure I wanted to.

"You see, in my days of being a Ranger of Karana a certain emptiness had grown within my soul and was threatening to consume me. I hadn’t recognized how powerful this feeling had become since it had grown so gradually over the last 15 of my 35 years. I now believe that Kinool recognized this void in my soul and in his own way tried to make me complete.

"With the most powerful of magic surrounding me, enthralling me, charming me, animating me, soothing me, radiating me, skewing me, adorning me, and permeating my soul, I was transformed from a Ranger of Karana to a creature of Enchantment. Kinool smiled serenely as he looked at me and said, ‘Delphic is your name. No longer will any such emptiness fill your soul.’"

Delphic gazed absently at his now empty goblet for a moment.

"There is much more to tell you of my recent adventures. And I also must warn you about the one" *cough* "small" *cough* "problem that Kinool did not foresee with my transformation, in all his mastery of magic. But for now freshen this goblet of wine will you please? The telling of such tales is thirsty work!"
