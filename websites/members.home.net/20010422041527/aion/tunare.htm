History of the Koada'Dal and Feir'Dal 

The Great Mother Tunare, Prexus, The Oceanlord, and Rallos Zek, The Warlord came to Norrath after Brell Serilis, who approached each of them, and after some time convinced them to meet as one to discuss the fate of the world.  Brell, proposed that they accept an alliance of sorts, to which all save Rallos Zek agreed, ignorant of the fact that Brell had already released some of his creations into the Underfoot of this new world. The planet that would be called Norrath was divvied up between these beings of power for the purpose of keeping the Veeshan in check. Each would create a race of beings to watch over Norrath and keep a vigilant eye on the schemes of Dragonkind. Brell claimed the bowels of the planet and created the Dwarves, stout and strong, deep beneath the mountains of Norrath. In the abysmal depths of the oceans Prexus left his children, the Kedge, hearty aquatic beings of great mental power and stamina. And on the surface of Norrath did Tunare create the Elves, creatures of limitless grace and beauty; The Koada'Dal, or High-Elves in the Common tounge, and the Feir'Dal, or Wood-Elves.

The Elves drew the unwanted attention of Innoruuk, Prince of Hate. In a decrepit tower overlooking the dark decaying alleys of the Plane over which he ruled, Innoruuk waited, stoking the fire of his Hate until it was a raging inferno. He cursed his fellow gods for not including him in their pact and vowed to make them regret such disrespect. From the halls of the Elves' fair city, Takish-Hiz, the Prince of Hate snatched away the first Elven King and Queen. In his realm of pain and anger he slowly tore them apart, physically and mentally, over the course of three hundred years. He then gathered the quivering remnants of these beings of light and rebuilt them into his own dark sadistic image, a twisted mockery of Tunare's noble children. In depositing the Teir'Dal, as Innoruuk's Dark Elven creations would come to be called, back into the Underfoot of Norrath, the seeds for The Prince of Hate's final revenge were sown.

Followers of Tunare believe that we are all Tunare's children. The children of Tunare seek to help Norrath, which they believe to be a living and breathing Being, to blossom and grow. Followers believe that the world of Norrath gave birth to Tunare and from her all life has sprung forth. Thus, by worshipping and protecting the land, followers are paying homage and respect to The Mother of Their Mother, who in turn protects and provides for them. Followers of Tunare will fight to the death to protect nature in all it's forms. Many Druids, Rangers, and a great many Elves follow the ways of Tunare.

During The Age of Blood, the empire of the Elves was the last of the old races to decline. The Elddar Forest spread across the entire southeastern quarter of Tunaria. Cities and villages built high into the trees housed thousands of Wood Elves, and the marble cities of the High Elves were built in the forest's clearings and meadows, their white towers and spires climbing out of the forest, higher than the tallest tree. But then came the last curse of the gods, as Solusek Ro, Lord of Flame, arched the spine of the serpent mountains, bringing heat from the burning sun to the ancient forest. The rivers ran dry, it rained less each year, and while the great elven druids fought long and hard, using their powerful magics to combat the change, they could only delay the inevitable. Slowly the forest gave way to desert, and eventually even great Takish-Hiz crumbled, and is know today as Befallen. The elves were forced to flee the continent of Tunaria, leaving much of their greatness behind.  The Elddar Forest stood once where the Northern and Southern Desert of Ro (named for Solusek, who destroyed the land) and the Oasis of Marr are now.  Tunaria was eventually renamed by the Humans to Antonica, after their first great leader, Antonius Bayle the First.

The fall of the Elves in the Age of Blood can be attributed to Solusek Ro and the Teir'Dal.

In The Lost Age, The High-Elves and Wood-Elves retreated to the far eastern regions of the continent of Faydwer, taking refuge in the Greater Faydark, beyond the Dwarven lands of The Butchblock Mountains.  The Wood-Elves built in the great trees of the forest, their new home, Kelethin, and the High-Elves established the great city of Felwithe, the City of Marble.  They live in peace, but should not be confused as the same race.

King Tearis Thex of the High-Elves, formed the secret order, The Silent watch, in the early days of Felwithe. King Tearis Thex had many cruel deeds to do (these deeds where never documented, nor reported). The regular Paladins were too righteous to carry out any of the necessary missions and they dared not bring in the Feir'Dal, because some of the operations were against Kelethin. King Tearis formed the Silent Watch; Koada'Dal, not of prim and proper breeding. They act as a secret guard and report only to him. They can be recognized by a distinct bracer reserved for the Silent Watch.

The Silent Watch went virtually unnoticed until recently, until in the Age of Tumoil, Princess Lenya of Felewith went missing. After much investigation she was discovered imprisoned by Carson MacCabe of Highhold by Tearon Bleanix. Tearon was eventually discovered by the Highhold guards just after the Princesse's escape and is rumored to walk the halls of Highhold Keep as the undead. Princess Lenya on the other hand never made it home. She was last reported dwelling near the shores of Lake Rathetear.

The reason for her initial kidnapping and imprisonment still goes unknown, but it is speculated that the Teir'Dal is behind this as a Dark Elf imposter of the Princess has been sighted in Highhold Keep, attacking all those looking for the real Princess Lenya.
