Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Hardd
Race: Barbarian Northman
Profession: Warrior
Likes:
Dislikes:
Quirks:

 I am the last of the Walker tribe. We are the greatest of the warriors and hunters of Norrath. My father was a great warrior, and his father, and his father also. My uncles were all great warriors. Walkers have fought honorably in all the great fights and hunts. Walkers have slain dragons, mammoths, and anyone who have come to war against us. This is in the lands of the north. Foreigners call it Everfrost, I have learned. We had no special name for our lands. We just lived there, following the mammoths.

 Gramps was in charge of the testing and training of great warriors. Even warriors from the wood elves and the trolls and the ogres would travel huge distances to beg for his training. I was a boy, and he had taken me out for the Final Test Of A Warrior, so that I would be a man. The rest of the clan was back at the camp site, preparing a feast to celebrate my feat. I completed the Test with pleasure. It was easy to me. We returned to our camp. As we approached the camp, we could hear the screams of children and the war cries of a few men and women warriors. I recognized my own mother's war cry. We ran to see. The noise had all stopped as we got in sight of the camp, except the noise of two ice giants stomping away in the other direction. I could see Father hacking at one of the giants, which he had already hurt terribly. But the other giant struck him from behind his peripheral vision, so he could not defend, and he was smashed to the ground with a broken neck. And there was something else, something about them that seemed magick, and like they were not alone. This magick was not from our own shamans of the Walkers, I knew what that felt like. This was very evil, and even I could tell it was very strong.

 One giant was moving very slowly, and we could see 'twas badly hurt.Gramps told me to run to our tent and see who could be saved. Then he ran after the giants. I will never forget the look in his eye.

 At our tent, father was dead. Still warm, of course. Mother was suffering and in unbelievable pain. It was obvious she would be dead soon. One of my little infant sisters was still breathing enough to cry, but she would be dead soon too. My other brothers and sisters were already dead. Mother told me again of her love for me, and father and my brothers and sisters. Then she had me hold her upright enough to deliver the mercy stroke to my sister, and then to herself. As is right for any honorable warrior. She was crying, but there is no shame in that for a woman.

 I was still very young then, so I will tell you that I was crying freely as I ran to the tents of my friends and cousins, things were the same all around the camp. I had to cut the mercy stroke on several of my relatives that day. I ran to catch Gramps and help him. Gramps was a legend of a warrior, and there are many tales of his greatness. He had more wives than anyone could remember any warrior ever having, because he was so great a warrior. Gramps could slay an ice giant alone. I was afraid though, because of that evil magick that was heavy around those giants that day.

 I caught up just as Gramps was laying a decapatitating stroke through a giant's neck with his zweihander. He used a roundhouse technique that he would normally have told me not to use, because it is too slow and telegraphs to the opponent. But Gramps was in the most perfect of berserker warrior rages I have ever seen, and the roundhouse was swifter than even a skilled jab. He cut the giant's carotid, and cut with such strength and skill, he severed it's huge head. Gramps was indeed a legendary warrior, and I am proud to be his grandson.

 The evil magick was not in the air now. The other giant was not nearby. Gramps had perfect control of his breathing, as he told me to ready my bow and follow him. Quick and quiet, we began running.

 We caught up to the second giant, though I don't know how. Everyone knows giants are too fast. I think our gods and the spirits of our dead families aided us somehow. Gramps was still in a perfect combat state. He pointed to a clever notch in the icy rocks, and told me to begin shooting when I heard his war cry. Quickly, I scrambled into position and counted my arrows. I had 120 arrows. I knew I was not nearly experienced enough to make any of my weapons count against a giant, but 'twas my combat assignment to try.

 Gramps ran up behind the giant, it all unaware. With his war cry ringing proudly from the surrounding ice walls, Gramps slashed a perfect hamstringing cut at this ignoble but mighty foe. It was lamed a bit, it would not be running away. Gramps began insulting and taunting this icy giant.

 They fought. Almost any of the giant's blows would have instantly slain Gramps if the giant could only get a direct hit. But Gramps was the greatest of master warriors, and always dodged to avoid all or most of the damage. His combat state was perfect, and you could see that his wounds were worse and worse but he wasn't letting the pain bother him. I finally forced my tears to quit. They were spoiling my aim. Gramps was my example of control of self. I began firing arrows at the giant's head more accurately, hoping to get an eye or other vulnerable spot. I watched, as Gramps showed how a great warrior can beat even another mighty warrior who is much bigger and stronger. But not without terrible cost to his own body.

 After a while, you could see the fear growing in the giant. I don't know how long they had been fighting, but I was almost out of arrows. Gramps was moving stiffly, and bleeding from many places, but the giant was bleeding even more. Gramps began speaking curses on the giant, and all the giant's kin. A steady stream of curses that the gods must surely be listening to. Spoken in rhythm with the cutting strokes of his black and blood-covered blade.

 The giant began to fight as one who is trying to get away, not one trying to win. Then, almost dead and gone from our world, it yelled something in its tongue that must have been a cry for help. Too late. Gramps again spun like lightning through a roundhouse and cut through its carotid and most of the way through its neck! But there was a sound I cannot describe, like in a huge thunderstorm maybe, and Gramps was thrown 30 feet backwards, barely clinging to his sword and shield now, hurt terribly. He was not too far below me now, down on the icy plain, as I looked down at him, then scanned everywhere for what had done this. The feeling of that evil magick from before was near.

 Gramps looked up and said my name quietly. Our eyes met, and I saw in his eyes the look I had already seen sometimes on opponents in sparring when it was clear they were bested and the fight about to be lost. He was not afraid, he showed no shame, embarrassment. Only a warrior's control, and somewhere behind it all, his harnessed rage and sadness. He hissed a command to me: "Run NOW, Hardd. NOW." Obedient from years of training, I began leaping down the rocks. I still don't know whether I was going to run as commanded, or stop and help Gramps. Most of the way down the ice and rocks, he spoke again to me "RUN AWAY! Make new sons and daughters,and keep the Walkers alive. You will avenge us all one day, but you must RUN."

 With a cold and clear vision that only a trained warrior in a crisis can have, I saw instantly he was right about everything. I ran.

 Behind me, I heard Gramps' war cry, and he laughed loudly as he was running towards whatever it was that had cast that fearsome spell. I could also hear the sound of that spell one last time. And then the spell was done, and Gramps' laughing was done. I think I also heard a scream, as from some night creature that had been struck deeply and had never felt pain before.

 After running a long time, I found the big city of Halas, and found our distant cousin, Mortekai. He showed me through this maze of buildings. All made of wood and stone! Sometimes two stories tall! He introduced me to the men who trained warriors in the city. Fighters to respect, but they did not impress me so much as several warriors of my own clan had. Mortekai gave me a new sword, and one of his warriors gave me some old patchworked armor he had replaced with metal armor. Weapons and armor were enough. I began the work of training myself to avenge our clan, and raise a new tribe of Walkers. I know why Gramps was laughing as he died. Because he knew that I would succeed in this mission. I will some day be a very great warrior, and I will avenge our tribe on the evil caster of spells who massacred us. Gramps was enjoying his last laugh over his foe.

 Gramps had travelled to many foreign places when he was younger, and even would go on trips sometimes while I was growing up. He always said he had learned a lot about combat, and studied many kinds of foes, while travelling. He had brought back special weapons and things that were powerful tools for a warrior. I knew I must travel too, like he did. And especially, must study how to defeat a powerful caster of spells.

 I worked at training a little more in Halas, but these city warriors could not teach that much. I went south in my fourth season as a warrior to find the way to the land of the elves, for people said they knew more about spellcasting than anyone.

 I went many places, and met a beautiful young wood elf named Jehanni. I had once glimpsed her when she came to visit her relative who my grandfather was training. But I lost her again, maybe forever. I have learned many things about fighting, and learned enough to know there is still much more to learn.

 One day, my sons and nephews and I will return to our home and once again camp and follow the mammoths and feast and train legendary warriors and hunters. And on the day we return, we will find that evil that slew our family and we will kill it and make its own family die. And then life will be good again.
