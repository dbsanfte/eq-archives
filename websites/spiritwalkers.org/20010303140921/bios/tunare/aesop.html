Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Aesop
Race: Erudite
Profession: Multi-Purpose Magician. Baker/Tailor/Smith/Brewer/etc. ("So help me, if I can't summon it, I'll MAKE it with my own two hands!")
Likes: New places and new things to explore. LONG battles against numerous enemies and hard-earned victories. Anything that can be traded, be it a new gizmo, or language, or useful information on a new place or skill.
Dislikes: Close-minded Elitism. Close-minded ignorance. Religious fanatics. Too long a lull between battles without SOME diversion.
Quirks: Itchy heels-- can never stay in one place for too long. Fond of long, solitary explorations to distant reaches-- often deep into very dangerous territory. Restless and always looking for something new.
Born Aesophaenis Thelemenes Ezerhauk; son of High Councillor Jandeel Ovidias Ezerhauk, about the only thing Aesop (as he prefers to be called) has in common with his Erudite brethren is an agnostic streak.

From an early age he was always a misfit among his peers, and even his rather impressive and natural aptitude for magic (not to mention having a high-ranking father) did little to spare him from frequent consternation over his oft-aired contrary opinions and restless nature.

His father served on the High Council and had little time or patience for him; his mother had died soon after he was born. As a result Aesop grew up a very independent child. Though an apt and eager pupil in his studies of magic, history, and the world in general, he had little patience in his more formal lessons pertaining to law, the Councils, and the political maneuverings thereof that are the lifeblood of Erudite society, often spending much of his time dreaming of the world beyond the sea and of discovering entire new worlds to explore.

Despite receiving mixed Letters from many a tutor (that is, evaluations that were frequently peppered with strong warnings and criticisms), it was his fascination in new worlds that brought high Letters in his studies of all things elemental. It was through his fascination of the elemental planes that he forged a bond with Tidaneus (or "Tidy" as he would playfully call him), the elder groundskeeper and tending squire for the Ezerhauk House who had always had a special fondness for the restless boy.

Aesop dreamed of being a wizard and travelling Norrath at will, and many a time would he pester Tidy (or any elder that he could) for information about Elemental Travel and the Planes, which was strictly forbidden before Trials. While his father did not approve of such close associations between his already oft-distracted son and the house help, it soon became clear that Tidaneaus was a grounding force and good teacher to young Aesop, and so their friendship was tolerated. This later proved to be a boon when Aesop underwent his Trials and emerged a Magician, for it was Tidaneus' support and encouragement that helped Aesop refocus his efforts constructively despite the loss of what was then his greatest dream.

Tragically, it was the death of Tidaneaus that ultimately drove Aesop to leave Erudin. It was brought to the attention of the High Council (quite possibly by a rival House) that Tidaneaus had close associations with and had on occasion helped a local group of Heretics (that is, Necromancers) -- an act punishable by death. In accordance with Erudite law, Tidaneaus was summarily sentenced to Purging (a rather brutal form of capital punishment).

Angered and outraged at his father's refusal to speak on behalf of Tidaneaus and powerless to act in his defense, Aesop risked life and limb to visit his old friend one last time, against his father's explicit orders, intending to free his old friend by any means necessary. Aesop succeeded in finding Tidaneaus, who, in the last words he would ever speak, told him that Aesop must leave Erudin at once, breaking all ties and titles with his family, for it was a matter of life and death and great import. Aesop, confused, asked why, whereupon Tidaneaus explained to him the secret he had helped keep hidden for all of Aesop's youth -- that his long-lost mother was not of Erudin, having originally come from Faydwer.

Tidaneaus told Aesop of a hidden place where he had stowed a small box containing a few items that he had hoped might someday help him find the other half of his legacy, as well as proof of his lineage. But it was with a heavy heart that he warned him the danger of this knowledge, for while Erudin was open to all who would come to learn of magic, Erudin society was strictly closed to other races, and family ties with other races were neither recognized nor tolorated, as it was felt they tainted the purity of Erud's original children.

In the hands of his father's enemies, information of Jandeel's deception (as he had claimed Aesop True-Born and none would question a Councillor without proof) threatened to fracture the High Council, possibly driving all of Erudin into civil war. Aesop was later able to find this evidence, but his conversation with Tidaneaus had not gone unheard by enemy ears, and, finding himself followed and eventually pursued by mercenaries from a rival house, he had no choice but to destroy all evidence of his past as quickly as he was able to find it.

He knew precious little, only that he must someday journey to a place called Felwithe. Bitter, disillusioned, and robbed of all he held sacred, Aesop left Erudin, took a new, simpler name, and spent many years drifting, hoping he might find a way to someday find his lost roots...
