Biography
 
Who is this Varalla chick and why isnt she raiding with
all her play time and 100% raid attendance anyway?

Well, I started playing back in 2001 drawn in for the
role play factor of EQ.
Roleplay has all but disapeared completly outside my
circle of friends and I started concentrating on the game
itself. Considering I have FAR more playtime than the
average player, I tried raiding for awhile but discovered
I didn't like it for a number of reasons.

1.) The family atmosphere was gone. Each and every
person was out to better themselves. The only reason
they banded together was to further their own ends.

2.) With massive numbers we were taking down targets
with relitive ease.
No real skill was required. It didn't dawn on me on just
how fake we were till a 4th string new tank to the guild
who was convinced mashing taunt built aggro proudly
proclaimed how good we were. We were not that good.
Its like a hunter shooting a tied up bear. There was no
satisfaction of accomplishment for me.

3.) I am a social person who likes to help people for the
warm fuzzy. I grouped outside my guild with others who
were sociable often and got to know many people and
knew on a personal level how skilled they were.
Once on an open RZ raid the best choice for VZ was a
high end casual SK who I knew personaly as extreamly
skilled and competant.
Another SK in a raiding guild demanded it should be him
because he was in a raiding guild. That was his sole
reason. Since then I have seen this elitism time and time
again from many raiders. Who on their own in a group
enviorenment... Simply suck and are incompetant.
Raiding guilds from my experiance have a lot of dead
weight players who use their guild as their crutch.

4.) I have discovered that taking on challenging content
with a single group to be FAR more satisfying in the
feeling of accomplishment department. One of my most
rememorable moments was in Dreadspire Keep.
As we explored deeper into the keep, we came upon a
freshly wiped group from a raiding guild called Frayed
Knot. My boyfriend being the nice guy he is ressed
them and we proceeded on our merry way. The mob
they wiped on flagged you for the door we were going
through. As we went through the door they were all
staring. Their tank said aloud
"How did you open the door!?You're in SoO!"
as if we were exploiting. (Our guild is a known casual
guild) My boyfriend Jyllian promptly replied "We umm
keyed ourselves last week some time"
It's moments like that that make my
day

5.) There comes a point in time where you out grow
your guild by a vast margin. There are no raids your
guild can do that will upgrade you or challenge your
power and experiance. Although I did not want to leave
my friends, I was compelled to look into other guilds
and see what they had to offer. It became pretty clear
pretty fast that I would be going from a BIG fish in a
small pond, to a SMALL fish in a big pond. Everyone
has thier faults. Ego is mine. I grew accustomed to
watch people part like the red sea for me as I strutted
to the front. I liked watching despair and
dissapointment turn to hope and enthuisaim if I logged
on late. When I went to stand between my guild and a
mob, morale was high. THIS is why I play EQ. Not only
to help others, but for my own selfish ego. I relish the
feeling of being needed and being THEE go to girl in
every situation.

So there was my dilema.
Stuck with a guild full of casual friends and unable to
progress. My solution? Become an Officer and spend
most of my time helping them progress. It makes me
feel good and as long as EQ keeps putting out
challenging single group content, I am happy.

There you go, theres the method to my madness
in a nutshell
