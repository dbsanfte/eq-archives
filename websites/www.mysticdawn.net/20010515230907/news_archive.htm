Saturday, Nov. 27, 1999

- A Monk exacts revenge!

Some news from Steele Talastarr:

Recently it had come to my attention that two of my Brother monks from the Silent Fist Order in Qeynos, Dareb and Shen, had come up missing. It was known that they had traveled to South Karana and the Brotherhood feared that they had run afoul of the evil gnoll Gahnex Drah. Indeed, as it turns out, their suspicions were correct. Last week, I journeyed in the hole of the gnolls known as Paw and after giving a number of lesser gnolls a sound thrashing, they revealed to me that Gahnex Drah had indeed slain both monks and had given Shen's head to High Shaman Grisok as a trophy and Dareb's head to High Shaman Phido.

Filled with a sense of urgency and the rage of revenge, I found the evil Gahnex and slew him, avenging my fellow monks' deaths. I then proceeded to find and slay the High Shaman Grisok, thus allowing me to collect Brother Shen's remains for a fitting burial back in Qeynos. I also desired to slay High Shaman Phido, but learned that he was indeed quite a powerful caster and a bit too much for me to challenge alone.


Therefore, I enlisted the aid of our sister Suzy, who joined me last night in a return visit to Paw. After much searching, we found his quarters but he was not present. Not to be denied, we set up our ambush for his return. (Interestingly, other gnolls clearly saw us there waiting for the High Shaman, but did nothing to stop us, treacherous beasts that they are.) After a short while, the High Shaman appeared and fell right into our trap. A battle ensued, a toe-to-toe slugfest between myself and the shaman. The battle was very close the entire way, but Suzy was able tip the scales in favor of good with some well timed heal spells upon me and a few well placed hammer blows of her own. After we defeated the Shaman, I retrieved poor Dareb's remains. Suzy and I remained in Paw a short time further, then parted ways for the evening. Alone now, I journeyed to Qeynos and delivered the remains of my brother monks and the head of their murderer to the Silent Fist Guild. In appreciation of my deeds, they presented me with the coveted Red Headband of their order.

Well done, and congrats on your accomplishments.  He has also provided a screen shot of himself, and a few of the others.

~Belrich the Druid
