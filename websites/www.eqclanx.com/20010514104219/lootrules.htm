Loot Rules

This text was written by Matok (edited for content by Rixma).
All leaders and officers of the Rathe Alliance should read and e-mail comments or suggestions to the Rathe Alliance.

Loot and Raid management.

Well its time to make sure that everyone understands how things have to run to keep everyone alive and well without any bickering and arguing during our upcoming raids.

For those of you who have been on large scale raids before that have been well run, this will be very obvious to you and make a lot of sense. For those that have not it will seem like a lot of sense but perhaps once in the raid it you may have to stop and think about some of it to keep any tempers under control.

So I will start off with asking a couple questions and answer them myself.

Why do we go on large-scale raids?

To have fun and excitement, and for loot.

If you thought exp was in there you are mistaken, once we hit planes don’t be surprised if you come back most of the time with less exp then what you went up with. For a raid to be fun low deaths are very helpful as dying is not a lot of fun, although they can be, so it is very important that everyone understand that we are going to get some good items for the people in our alliance.

Loot will drop on these raids and we will be posting which items drop there after and by whom so everyone understands how things have to work to keep things fair, and there is no mistaken looting. But if you don’t agree with the loot rules just look at it from the other side of the coin and see if you were the one rolling on an item would you want yourself rolling on it too.

Ok lets get to the looting rules: First rule is simple and yet we have seen it over and over cause problems, You will only be rolling for loot for the toon you bring on the raid, that’s it. If you have another toon who could really use an item, we're sorry but you don’t get to roll unless its NOT No drop and none of the classes who can use it that are at the raid want it, then anyone who wants it can try and roll on the item.

But before you roll on an item be aware that once you get an item your are done rolling until everyone in your class/race has got an item. So if you really really want a certain item that may or may not drop in the future you might not want to roll on a different item that you could win.

Where this can seem unfair is if we do a raid and there is only one of a class there and a lot of their class only items drop well its going to be a looting frenzy for them. Buy perhaps maybe that will be you someday also.

When a guild or an alliance start to do bigger things and start getting nice items there is bound to be some loot envy, and people will be jealous of others who win things and you will be looking at your inventory cursing how unfair it is (not that I have done this) when a good friend or great alliance mate just won an ass kicking item. At this point don’t get mad and stay how unfair it is or how many times you have shown up and you still have not gotten anything. We are just getting started and EVERYONE will be getting cool items in time, unfortunately we can not get everyone everything they want right off the bat. So don’t worry if you don’t get anything or go someplace that nothing you want drops at, we WILL be doing more raids and events so everyone will get theirs in due time.

Ok guess my self Q and A isn’t working out hehe, kinda got off track. Hehe

Once we are in the raid it is going to be very important that you listen and keep you group leader up to date on your mana buffs and over all readiness so he can report to the raid leader if his group is good to go or not.

If you have a pet it is very important that you keep a very close eye on it as they like to wander as we all know, and although it might be fun to fight six or seven FGs at once I’m sure we might not come off of that one unscathed.

NO WANDERING. If you have this sudden urge to go take a peak at a cool looking statue or see what’s around the next corner. DON’T. Stay with your group and move where the group or raid leader says.

Assisting: Before we start it will be made very clear who the Primary assist is, everyone will assist that person. And we will have a secondary assist so if the primary dies everyone will switch to the secondary. And just to be safe we will have a Third person just in case. In the confusion that is bound to happen everyone will have to make /assist name rather then just /assist.

Ack back to loot for a second. Lets say we kill Naggy in the future and of all the loot he drops he only drops two Cloak of Flames. The item is all/all. (For those who may not know, it’s haste item for the back.) Does because its all/all is everyone going to roll???? No. Only melee classes will roll on the item. The argument I was given was it’s not fair for the casters to do a raid like that and not get to roll on anything. Well the way I look at it is an item that you can not buy in game for any amount of PP and is very doubtful that you will be able to even trade for it, it would be only fair to let our melee classes have it.

However the shoe can go on the other foot. If a mob where to drop a crown of some sort and it had like +7str +7int 12ac and +75 mana all/all (made the item up off the top of my head) then it would be casters only rolling on it. Only way to keep it fair as far as I see it.

Where it comes down to each and everyone of us to be honest and fair with each other is if an item drops that you can use and you roll on it and win it for the purpose of selling or twinking another toon or a friend that is within your right to do so. As you got your one item, you do with it as you wish. We would ask though that we try and avoid this for now as a lot of peeps out there NEED items and if you have a great head piece then be fair and don’t roll on one that drops. Like we stated earlier we will all get what we need in due time
