What is The Sony Online Barrier?
 
Complaint:
“I am in a Family Guild but we never raid the big zones.  I want my Hate armor,  Velious Gear, and my Epic Weapon.”
The EQ Design Issue:
Big zones and Epics require participation from a lot of people --> requires getting people together --> generally requires schedules --> generally requires some sort of mandatory raid attendance (if not enough people attend, total party wipeouts will happen or raids get cancelled).
The Problem:
People in Family Guilds don’t like to be told what to do when they are in Norrath.  “Mandatory attendance to raids?  No way!”
“I am in a Raiding Guild, and although we get to do all the great zones, people are very rude to each other - there is no sense of camaraderie or respect.”
The focus for most Raiding Guilds is success - anything other than success is secondary.  There are time limitations that you have when chatting - success of these big raids requires everyone to be focused and on their toes - and the communication in Raiding Guilds is to make sure you are GTG. 
If Raiding Guilds need to communicate attack orders or group formation in a constant sea of raiding, the chatting and “warm fuzzies” becomes much less of a priority.  The focus to “get it right!” is often times taken as a personal attack.
