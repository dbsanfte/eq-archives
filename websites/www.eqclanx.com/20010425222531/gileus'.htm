"Uh oh," Gileus said, "this could be bad." He had just gotten done healing Rixma and she was at full health now. That was good. The rest of his party were no where to be found. That was bad. There were also three Pickclaw Goblins here, two warriors and a raider. That was also bad.

Gileus switched positions, trying to get a safe sitting place to meditate for more heals, when he found one member of his party. Lorkus, a hapless, none to bright warrior, was crumpled on the floor beside him, sword still gripped firmly in his hand. He looked like he was sleeping. If humans slept with their eyes open and scared, that is. He wasn’t sure.

As he tried to meditate, a warrior jumped from battering Rixma to beat on him a bit. As of yet, Gileus had been untouched and was feeling perfectly fine. He knew this was about to change. His blood would flow. He hoped not too much of it.

He noticed that Rixma was not faring to well either. Everyone knows that Rogues do their best work from behind, though Gileus, even a dumb Dwarf like me knows that.

He realized there was but one choice.

"Run, Rixma. Get out, I’ll be right behind you", he shouted.

"No way G", She said, "not leaving you to die. Not this time."

Gileus realized that Rixma was smiling. A wide, full toothed grin. Even as she was facing probable death, she was having a good time, loving the chaos. She was nuts. That’s why he loved her so.

"Damn it Rixi, leave. Run. We can’t win this one. Run to the guards, they will help."

"No way Baby G, this is too much fun."

Sighing, Gileus focused on the goblin in front of him. He had been doing a fair job of deflecting it’s blows till now, but the ugly creature was getting angry, and that mean harder, more accurate hits. He gripped his mace more tightly and began swinging for all he was worth, hoping to knock this damn goblin to the Plane of Fear and back. He landed a nice blow to the side of the goblin’s head, but the vile thing kept coming.

He remembers the first time he had encountered a goblin. It was outside of his hometown of Kaladim. How long ago that seemed. Almost like a dream.......

 

He stood outside the large wooden door, unsure of himself, wondering what he would find behind it. All of this because he had asked his mother to buy him a new pair of boots. "Gileus, my son, I think you are old enough that if you want something, you can buy it yourself", his mother had said. All of his pleading and begging got him nowhere. His mother had better things to spend their money on, and he was getting to old to depend on Ma and Dad for things.

So with head hung low and a scowl on his face, he walked out the door of his family hut. When he professed to his Ma that he had no money, she suggested he get a job. When he sais he had no idea where to get a job, she said to go see Wumpford, he always has work.

Wumpford. What a stupid name.

Taking his Ma’s directions, he headed out to see Wumpford. He soon found himself in a part of town he had never been in before. When he came to the market with his Ma or Dad, it was on the other side of town. This was uncharted territory and there were no parents to show him the way. He wondered if he had followed the directions correctly. Directions were something that Gileus did not do well, and he often got lost. More than once he had almost gotten totally lost going from the outhouse to his home during a dark night.

He was seriously thinking about not going knocking on the big wooden door and going home. Who needed new boots anyway? He would just stitch the ones he had until something better came along.

And he would have done just, if not for the door opening and a tall (tall meaning over 40 inches) graying Dwarf in front of him.

"Hello young Gileus", the old Dwarf said, "so good to finally see you here."

"I’m here to see Wumpford." Said Gileus, ready to run if this old guy tried anything funny.

"And seen him you have," said the old Dwarf, "now get inside before I close my door to you and you never see me again."

He said this firmly, but not without a gleam of good humor in his eyes. Eyes that were far younger than the rest of his face.

After a brief look around, Gileus decided, why not, Ma knows where I went. If anything happened, she would come looking for him.

Gileus stepped through the doorway into Wumpford’s cozy abode, unknowingly changing his life forever.

Gileus started out just doing basic chores for Wumpford. As he did this, Wumpford would train other young Dwarves in a way he had never seen before. They seemed to be learning some sort of magic. Watching the ice blue energy flow in small torrents from their hands, Gileus found himself both excited and quite interested. Perhaps, if he did a good enough job, Wumpford would teach him as well.

He wasn’t exactly sure what was going on there in the back room of Wumpfords place, but he could not seem to get enough of it. He would sit for hours after his job was done and watch the young Dwarves practice.

Wumpford, noticing the interest Gileus had, took him aside one day and spoke with him. He told him that he could train Gileus, but could not do it the same way he did with the other boys. The other boys’ training was being paid for by their parents, and they got exclusive treatment. If he wished to stay long after work, he may be able to squeeze some time in for Gileus.

Gileus eagerly accepted, promising to be Wumpfords best student.

Part of the deal was that Gileus would have to be a full time servant of Wumpford, which meant living in his home. This meant he would see less of his family. Not knowing exactly how to feel about this, he approached his Ma about it one day after coming home from Wumpfords.

To his surprise, his Ma was all for it.

"Gileus, my eldest son, it is time for you to move on." His Ma said as she stirred the stew that was to be the next night’s dinner.

"I feel that great things will become of you. You have never been the brightest, but you have certain gifts that I can’t even begin to understand. Perhaps Wumford does. If so, perhaps he can help you pu them to best use. In any case, it is time for you to do what you have to. Al I as is that you never forget you Father and me. We both love you very much. Please come visit from time to time."

With that, his Ma gave him a long, strong hug and told him to be on his way. Since he had been spending so much time at Wumpford’s anyway, most of his stuff was already there. All he had to do was move in officially, which he promptly did.

As days turned into weeks and weeks to months, Wumpford discovered that Gileus was very special indeed. The young, homely Dwarf seemed to have an innate ability for the arcane. Specifically, magic offered from the deity Brell Serillis. He seemed to pick up spells such as healing and endurance with little or no effort at all. Amazing.

It soon became apparent to Wumford that Gileus was the best student he had ever had. So good that very soon, he had taught Gileus all he could. Sadly, it was time to bid farewell to his best floor sweeper.

When told that he was to leave the comfort of Wumfords, Gileus became scared. What was to become of him now?

Wumpford saw the fear in his young disciple’s eyes. He tried to calm him.

"Gileus, I have taught you all I can. There is nothing more for you here. I think the best thing you can do for yourself is to go to the Butcher Block Mountians and continue your training. For much of it you may be on your own, but your talents will make you very popular among other adventurers. Soon, you will be more powerful than you can possibly imagien, but it will not be easy. I do however, have a couple gifts to start you off with."

With that, Wumpford grabbed for something on the table beside him. He handed Gileus a large club, some food and water, a couple of torn scrolls, and a thick, leather bound book.

"Now go young Gileus and make your name. The scrolls are spells that will aid you. Scribe them in your spell book, and they will be with you forever. Use them to the best of your ability. Your time here is done. Your potential is great, you are no longer welcome here as anything but a guest. Fare thee well young adventurer."

Barely had Gileus been able to comprehend what had happened when Wumpfords door was closing behind him. He had no idea what to do. He didn’t even know how to get to Butcher Block Mountains. For all he had known, that place was just fairy tails his Dad used to tell him about.

He knocked on the door to ask Wumpford for directions. No answer.

He knocked again, this time harder and longer. Still no answer. Finally Gileus understood. He was Finally on his own.

After asking a couple of strangers directions (one of them a tall person) he found his way to The Buthcher Block Mountains.

He stood there at the entrance to his hometown and looked upon the landscape before him. The scenery was breathtaking to say the least. Tall mountains and lush greenery for as far as the eyes could see. From here he could see a creature. It was unlike anything he had seen before. It looked vile and nasty. He soon found out that he had seen his first Goblin.

Before he forgot, he sat down between the two guards and began to scribe his spells in the book Wumpford had given him. One was a spell called Minor Healing. The other, Spiritual Armor.

He then took his first steps in to a new world and a new life.

He soon found out the way of the wild. If things got too tough for him, he would run to the guards, and they would help him. He fought little chicken like creatures, skeletons, skunks, and bats and of course, he killed as many Goblins as possible. He learned to hate the vile things. More than once they beat him badly, Gileus barely surviving by running and using his magic

Soon though, he felt he was outgoing the confines of Butcher Block. His learning and training seemed to be going slower and slower, although he had already made improvements on his fighting and spell casting skills. Unfortunately, he had no idea where to go next.

Occasionally he would fight with other adventurers of many races and skill levels, and these times he enjoyed greatly. On the whole however he hunted alone and practiced his spells. He got up enough money from selling looted goods to buy more spells, making him more formidable.

The time came when he felt he was through with Butcher Block, the monsters here just too easy to kill. He was ready to go back to Wumpfords place and ask him when he met a huge fellow name Henelan.

Henelan was a Barbarian and looked not only strong , but mean. Add to the fact he was three times the size of Gileus, and Gileus was ready to run to the Guards.

But Henelan was as kind as he was big. He helped Gileus by casting protection spells upon him, and gave him some armor. Henelan told Gileus he was a Shaman, which meant that he too could cast as well as fight well. After some talking, Henelan informed Gileus that he was considered a Cleric, because of his healing abilities.

Although they were complete opposites, They became close friends, Henelan often keeping an eye on Gileus while he hunted.

One day, Henelan suggested that Gileus go to Greater Faydark, where he could hunt ugly Orcs, and maybe even kill Emperor Crush himself.

Gileus confessed to not knowing how to get there, and Henelan agreed to show him the way, as long as Gileus would follow.

Foloowing in itself was a chore for Gileus. Being so short, he had trouble keeping track of Henelan over hills and around trees. Also, Gileus had been encountering the often-present Lag Monster, a beast that rarely cause harm but could be a major nuisance.

Through it all, Henelan finally brought Gileus to Kelethin, and bound him there, so he could gate back to the city without having to run back from Kaladim.

It was here that Gileus found the true joy in hunting with a party. First on Orc Hill then later in Crushbone, Gileus learned the ways of party hunting, splitting loot (more money than he had ever seen) and gaining experience at an incredible rate. Although the Orcs here were nasty, if you had a good group behind you, chances are you would live. And there were always the guards you could run to.

Gileus spent a good amount of time between Orc Hill and Crushbone, and in that time acquired more and more spells, better armor and weapons, and the knowledge that he could be an important part of any group. He was truly Enjoying his life. Plus, other adventurers were staring to know his name, asking him to group with them, wanting his healing abilities.

The feeling of being needed was overwhelming and welcome.

Eventually however, Gileus out grew Crushbone as well. He soon realized that his skills were no longer challenged in the places. He needed a new place to hunt.

He had kept in touch with Henelan throughout everything and he told Gileus he should go to Lavastorm, a hot , muggy place that would be good for his training. So off Gileus went, hopping a boat to Freeport and running to Lavastorm.

This was not a particularly fun time for Gileus, as although he learned much, he mostly fought alone, some thing he increasingly disliked to do. He had become accustomed to group hunting, and he now started to think of himself as a helaer firts and a fighter second. Lavastorm was a lonely, dangerous place, the only redeeming quality being the amount of money he made. He was becoming quite the wealthy Cleric.

He soon found his way to a place called Oasis, a dry place on the outskirts of South Ro, where there was a whole different breed of orcs. Also there were Spectres, and the occasional Sand Giant that would ruin anybody’s day. And Gileus though Henelan was tall!!

It was in Oasis that Gileus’ life would take another major turn. It was there in Oasis that Gileus met a beautiful Wood Elf named Rixma.

Rixma seemed to just appear to him. He joined a group of hunters along the shores of Oasis, said his hellos, sat down and began to meditate. When he got up, fully prepared to begin healing, standing in front of him was a stunning Wood Elf. He had noticed her when he first joined, which was odd because she was the group leader. She had to do the inviting.

Without thinking, he began talking to her, saying anything, just to be close to her. Little did he realize how silly he looked with the horns on his helmet sticking out like some sort of strange antennae. Had he realized this, it is doubtful he would have cared.

Not only was Rixma beautiful, but she was pleasant as well. Not like many of those High Elves, who seemed to look down on him, noses in the air. And on top of everything, she was as deadly as she was pretty. With the use of just two daggers, Gileus had seen her take orc after orc to their knees with archer like precision. He was spellbound.

But before he knew it, it was time to camp for the night. The party was breaking up. He promised to group with her again. He had to.

He did group with her again a few more times, and Gileus knew he was falling in love with her. This, he told himself, was not good. Besides the fact that she would never go out with a Dwarf, but she was due to be married to Gress, who was either a Half-Elf or Wood elf himself. How could he compete with that? He himself was ugly and short, and he knew he had the charisma of a bad cold. It seemed he was destined to have his little heart broken.

Still, Rixma had taken a liking to Gileus. He seemed to heal her first, and always made sure she was safe and damnit if he didn’t look cute in that horned hat. He was fun to have around too, always making her laugh when no one else could. He was a likeable little fellow. And a good cleric, she thought.

They had quite a friendship going. They started grouping together frequently.

Then quiet unexpectedly, Gileus took off to hunt in South Karana. One of his friends, Tanven, had offered to take him there, but he had to leave now. Oasis had already grown tired to Gileus, so he decided to go with Tanven. He could not, however, find Rixma to tell her where he was going. As he left Oasis, he feared he would never see her again. He was frowning the whole trip to South Karana.

He wasn’t in South Karana for more than a day, fighting giant bird creatures known as Aviaks, when he heard a voice behind him.

"Hey G, funny running into you here. How’s my favorite little Viking?"

It was Rixma. Somehow she had found him here. What was a Viking? Who cared, Rixma was here that was all that mattered.

He was so happy he lept at Rixma and gave her legs a strong hug. He had never been so happy.

Rixma went on to explain that she had wondered where he went. When she didn’t see him in Oasis anymore, she decided to come to South Karana and hunt. The loot was good and she would learn a lot, she had been told. So here she was. And she happened to see him. She was very happy to see him as well.

From that moment on, whenever you saw Gileus hunting, there was a good chance Rixma was with him. And vice versa. They became almost inseparable, looking out for each other, helping each other. Through all this, Gileus’ feelings remained strong for Rixma, but he realized they would never be more than friends. He knew Rixma loved him dearly, but only in friendship. He was happy with that. He vowed to himself to do anything to protect her, even if it meant he himself dying.

The two of them fought many tough, good battles in South Karana, both against the Avaiks, and those filthy dog creatures, the SplitPaw Gnolls. They weren’t even housetrained. Their lair was digusting.

Before too long, Gileus and Rixma found themselves fighting Hill Giants in the Rathe Mountains. By now, Gileus had upgraded his armor and weapon and Rixma became more deadly with every passing day. She also developed a strong lust for the battle, for chaos. She craved action and always stuck the fight out till the end. This particular trait meant that Gileus stuck around till the end, not wanting his love to perish. He had seen much bloodshed and many great fights because of this. He had also taken his share of bad beatings, often needing more powerful Clerics to heal both he and Rixma.

It was in these Rathe Mountains that Rixma approached the idea of Gileus joining her guild. It really wasn’t her guild, just the guild she was part of, but in a very real sense, it was her guild. The guild belonged to all of it’s members.

She went on to explain that a Cleric by the name of Larton had started the guild, called the Ancient Order of Guardians. Every Guardian had to abide by a certain code of ethics, and they had to carry themselves with honor and dignity. According to Rixma, this was the best guild in all of Norrath and she would be happy to have Gileus as a member.

Gileus thought about this. He didn’t know if he was honorable or dignified, but he liked the idea about joining a guild. He agreed to try and get in.

Rixma brought him back to Oasis to meet with two officers of the guild, Another beautiful Wood Elf named Abbra , and a noble Paladin named Lonedor. After answering a couple questions and swearing his allegiance to the Guardians, he was sworn in, with a short probationary period. He was given a Rusty Dagger by Larton, leader of the guild. He was told when his probation was up, he would turn in the Rusty Dagger and get a Tarnished Dagger, the symbol of membership for the Ancient Order of Guardians.

Gileus cherished his Rusty Dagger, and when it was replaced with a Tarnished Dagger, he was overcome with both pride and joy.

A strange thing began to happen to Gileus. Before he joined the Guardians, he was just content to wander around and live for himself, and maybe Rixma and hunt and kill and loot for his own personal gains. Now he seemed to have more of a purpose. He seemed to care greatly for his fellow Guardians. He realized that he would gladly lay down his life to save any one else’s in the guild.

He approached Larton about creating a Guild Fund, and was soon appointed Minister of Finance. He was given a Medal of Merit for his contributions to the Ancient Order of Guardians.

Gileus was stunned. He had never had a title before. He had never gotten a medal before. He realized that his fellow Guardians cared for him in the same way he cared for them. This truly was the best guild in all of Norrath. He loved his new family.

Rixma was also overjoyed at Gileus’ success. She was amazed at his assertiveness, and even as she made Officer Status in the guild, she continued to praise him.

Gileus also performed the wedding services for Gress and Rixma. He did this with mixed emotions, as he was glad Rixma was happy, but wished he was in Gress’ spot. Some things were just not meant to be.

So here he was, Gileus Thunderhead, a young Dwarf who once swept floors for copper pieces, and now he was a respected Cleric and a prominent member of the Ancient Order of Guardians. It seems as if Wumpfords best student has made a name for himself. He could hardly believe it himself.......

 

All of this flashed through the mind of Gileus as he was trying to smack this Pickclaw Goblin in to ground Goblin chuck steak. He was bleeding from a gash on his arm and a cut above his left eye was flowing pretty good. The goblin looked worse than he did, though, and he felt he would win this fight, but what about Rixma?

He glanced over at her, and she was battered, but she was winning. She had felled one of her foes and she had the other on the run, perfect for a Rogue. Soon that one would be dead too.

After the final goblin lay dying in a growing pool of it’s own blood, Rixma came and sat facing Gileus and smiled. He smiled back.

"Let me med up crazy lady and we’ll get our party back together, and we’ll do this all over again."

"Of course Baby G," she said, grinning fiercely,"would I have it any other way?"

He sat there, meditating and thinking. He thought about the members of his guild, Rixma , Larton, Sensy, Elgor. He thought about Abbra and Lonedor and Alexedia and Jaston and Manus and Rizzo and Xanon.

He thought about his friends outside the guild, Henelan and Tanven and Wulbyan and Druus and Drugan.

 

What a wonderful bunch of people I know, he thought, what an amazing family I have become part of.

He thought of his Tarnished Dagger and smiled. He would have to stop back and see Ma and Dad and Wumpford soon. He wondered what they would say. He wondered what adventures would be next....

He wondered............
