Loot Rules

Here is a summary of the loot rules as agreed upon by the Rathe Alliance Council.  The council is still discussing many of these issues, and the loot rules may change. E-mail comments or suggestions to the Rathe Alliance.  NEW LOOT RULES IN YELLOW (as of 9-11-01)

Need Before Greed - On most loot, anyone who can use a dropped item is allowed to roll for it. The number of points must be declared before hand (Check Our Point System for more info on points).  Where this can seem unfair is if we do a raid and there is only one of a class there and a lot of their class only items drop - well its going to be a looting frenzy for them. But perhaps maybe that will be you someday also.  

  • How does Need Before Greed work? - Lets say we kill Naggy in the future and of all the loot he drops he only drops two Cloak of Flames. The item is all/all. (For those who may not know, it’s haste item for the back.) Does because its all/all is everyone going to roll???? No. Only melee classes will roll on the item. The argument I was given was it’s not fair for the casters to do a raid like that and not get to roll on anything. Well the way I look at it is an item that you can not buy in game for any amount of PP and is very doubtful that you will be able to even trade for it, it would be only fair to let our melee classes have it.  However the shoe can go on the other foot. If a mob where to drop a crown of some sort and it had like +7str +7int 12ac and +75 mana all/all (made the item up off the top of my head) then it would be casters only rolling on it. Only way to keep it fair as far as I see it.
  • Some of the invites (guests) get to roll for these items.  Which guests?  The guests that are invited to raids to help because we do not have the classes or levels to do raids ourselves AND are approved of by the RAID LEADER.  Other people may be invited to our raids, but if they have not been approved by raid leader as NECESSARY to the success of the raid, they will not be allowed to roll for these items.
Uber Loot - Uber loot (ie Robes, Breastplates, etc) will be rolled on by the toons who are on the Uber Loot List for the raid. Each guild attending is asked to produce a list of people, one from each class, for their guild concerning those toons that in the opinion of their guild leaders, are deserving of Uber Loot. These people will be given the opportunity to roll on Uber loot, no one else (please see how Clan X decides on who within Clan X gets put on the Uber Loot List).  Invites may not roll for these items.  The uber item is only rolled on by the people that will use it who are on the uber loot lists of the guilds.
  • Example, a warrior bp drops. Only the 3 warriors on the lists will roll for this (only one warrior per guild is allowed on the list). However, if a bp drops that can be used by all the melee types, then all the melee types on the lists can roll on this--assuming they are going to use it.
  • Points CANNOT be used when rolling on uber loot items.
  • The list of eligible recipients will include one person per class per guild.  Each guild gets an uber drop, and then the cycle starts again, regardless of the raid we are on (i.e. continues from raid to raid.).
Epic Loots - The priority for epic loot goes to those within the alliance that are furthest along in their quest AND are of adequate level to obtain their epic pieces.  If there are questions about this during raid time, those alliance members that contribute more to guild success will have priority on epic drops.  Guests are not eligible for epic loots unless there is no one else there needing the items.
   

Points - On all alliance raids, a point system will be in place.
How are points gained? For every hour that a character is on the raid, they will be given one (1) point.

  • How are points used? When an item comes about that you wish to roll upon (must meet looting requirements) you will declare, BEFORE rolling, how many points you wish to use. Points will add to the value of your roll.
    Ie. Just say i declare that i want to add 6 points to my roll. Then, i roll a 62. The value of my roll becomes 68.

    If you use points and win the roll, those points are gone from your total. If you use points and loose, you get those points back.
Each Raid will have a Raid Leader and a Loot Leader.  The Loot Leader is responsible for the following duties:
  • When items drop, finding out what classes can use items and calling for random rolling during raid.
  • Calling out who gets to roll on the "Uber Loot" or who is eligible for epic drops.
  • Keeping track of the points that each person on raid has to use toward their rolls.
  • During times of intense fighting when random rolling is problematic, ensuring that someone loots items before they rot.
  • Making all decisions regarding loot during raid time, with the purpose of being fair and consistent from raid to raid.
Additions -
  • Once you have won an item on the raid, you may not roll on any other items for the rest of that raid, until everyone else has gotten an item. However, if you win an item and are on the uber loot list, you will be given the opportunity to roll on the uber loot, if any drops, but not any other loot.
  • If a class is needed to allow a raid to happen and a person volunteers to play that class to allow the raid to occur, they have the option of rolling for items for their other class that they would have played had they not been forced to play their needed role--however they can only roll for one character per raid not both.
    • Example, player1 has a high level necro and a high level cleric. Player1 wants to play his necro so as to get a chance for some necro items dropped on the raid; however, no clerics show up--forcing player1 to play his cleric so the raid can occur. Player1 should then have the option, if he so desires, of rolling for necro items INSTEAD of cleric items but NOT both. Player1 will make a choice at the beginning of the raid to roll for 1 or the other and this will be effective throughout the raid unless an item drops that no one else can use--in other words, player1 can not roll on an cleric item one time and then a necro item another time during the same raid.
