Epic Quest Leader
The Epic Leader has three main responsibilities to the guild.  They are, Epic Information Specialist, Epic Loot Administrator, and Epic Raid Scheduler.

  • The Epic Leader is responsible to place all information of each class epic in an organized fashion on the guild web page.  This not only involved links to different pages but also the splitting up of each individual section of every epic so that guild members can follow along their progress and that of fellow guild members.
  • The Epic Leader is also responsible to determine loot order for any epic loot which may drop.  This decision is based on the person's involvement in Epic Quest raids and his own progress in his Epic.
  • The third thing that the Epic Quest Leader is in charge of is to help guild members organize and schedule their epic raids.  This involves the scheduling of the 2 Epic mobs each month, work with the Raid Leader to get as much information to him/her as possible, and as well to help members get better organized when raiding with smaller groups for smaller epic pieces.

The main job for the Epic Quest Leader is to be the main contact for all of Clan X for any and all epic needs.

Recruiting Officer
The Recruiting Officer's job description is based on four main tasks:  Recruiting New Applicants, Guiding New Applicants in their Application Process, being the Central Focal Point for Officers and Sponsors to comment on applicants, and making the Final Decision on Guilding new recruits.

  • Recruiting New Applicants:  Along with the Recruiting Team, the Recruiting Officers will aid in promoting Clan X and recruiting new members in order to enhance and strengthen the guild.
  • Guide for New Recruits:  Along with the Recruiting Team, the Recruiting Officer will aid the new applicant with questions and guide them along the process of completing the requirements to become a full fledged member of Clan X.  The Recruiting Officer will also keep track of probationary periods and be responsible for keeping tabs on the newly admitted Clan X'ers behavior.
  • Central Focal Person:  The Recruiting Officer will act as the go to person for all inquiries, interview results and sponsor votes concerning all new applicants.  This will allow the Recruiting Officer to keep tabs on where the applicant is in their recruiting process and what the outcome of every step has been.  The Recruiting Officer will be responsible for providing a final report summary of the applicants efforts to the officers for comment before the applicant is guilded or rejected.
  • Final Decision Maker: If for some reason there is some controversy or confusion over an applicant, the Recruiting Officer will have the final say in whether or not the applicant is guilded or not.  The Recruiting Officer can in no way overrule a majority officer vote.  It is up to the Recruiting Officer to give the final OK before any officer shall guild the applicant so that completion of the recruiting process is ensured.

The Titans
I would like to take the time to invite all of you “End Gamers” to something a bit different. Something I hope will help satisfy some of the woes in our guilds and in EQ in general.  A hybrid of sorts, for those of us that would like to retain our “family” guild structure and at the same time achieve some of the benefits that Uber guilds enjoy.

I present to you The Titans. A weekly group for dedicated players that want a bit more out of EQ life that might not be possible without some added structure and goals. Although I envision a club type setting there WILL be goals and expectations on top of the fun that we all seem to have mastered having in our current guilds.

I would like to enlist a group of driven people that would like to make a real effort in obtaining these goals:

  • XP XP XP! No BS here, this is our main function, to start with anyway. Everything will fall into place after we have a nice solid group of 57+ toons.
  • Zone and Mob XP. Who couldn’t use a little more info on what they are fighting? Well let’s learn them inside and out.
  • Lootz. Not only is it nice to upgrade your gear, but also who doesn’t have a friend that could really use an upgrade or two. <This would be really an Alliance Guild Fund> More on that later.
  • Leadership. Maybe you don’t want to be a Leader, but maybe you have what it takes and just don’t get the chance in everyday guild raids.
  • Stronger Guilds. If we could accomplish these goals, think of what our guilds could become.

I foresee one day/evening per week being set aside to from a Titan Group; I would expect this to be lengthy camps and a little more militant. What I mean to say is there is time enough for fun and BSing around. This would be a bit more serious and a bit more uptight. Although there might be the argument, “Well that will take all the fun out of it, It’s a game after all.” The people you hear saying that are probably not the people I’m looking at for this Group. I also see much less flexibility for things such as Lateness and inability to follow direction. I understand this might sound unreasonable, but you must understand the people in this group would be DEDICATED! I’m not willing to baby-sit for a few stragglers that cause those people down time waiting or CRing. Different zones, Different camps, same goals. The idea here is to become a TEAM and to Roxor some places that we have either failed or been too weak to take out this far.

The Titans group is still being formed, as well as the Chain of Command of The Titans, and how we schedule our events.  Please check back here for more.
