Raid Guidelines

General guidelines on how to run a planes raid (these are not listed in order of importance as all these are important).  These guideslines are being updated constantly - please email comments or suggestion to the Clan X Officers. 

Well its time to make sure that everyone understands how things have to run to keep everyone alive and well without any bickering and arguing during our upcoming raids.

For those of you who have been on large scale raids before that have been well run, this will be very obvious to you and make a lot of sense. For those that have not it will seem like a lot of sense but perhaps once in the raid it you may have to stop and think about some of it to keep any tempers under control.

So I will start off with asking a couple questions and answer them myself.

Why do we go on large-scale raids?

To have fun and excitement, and for loot.

If you thought exp was in there you are mistaken, once we hit planes don’t be surprised if you come back most of the time with less exp then what you went up with. For a raid to be fun low deaths are very helpful as dying is not a lot of fun, although they can be, so it is very important that everyone understand that we are going to get some good items for the people in our alliance.

Loot will drop on these raids and we will be posting which items drop there after and by whom so everyone understands how things have to work to keep things fair, and there is no mistaken looting. But if you don’t agree with the loot rules just look at it from the other side of the coin and see if you were the one rolling on an item would you want yourself rolling on it too.

When a guild or an alliance start to do bigger things and start getting nice items there is bound to be some loot envy, and people will be jealous of others who win things and you will be looking at your inventory cursing how unfair it is (not that I have done this) when a good friend or great alliance mate just won an ass kicking item. At this point don’t get mad and stay how unfair it is or how many times you have shown up and you still have not gotten anything. We are just getting started and EVERYONE will be getting cool items in time, unfortunately we can not get everyone everything they want right off the bat. So don’t worry if you don’t get anything or go someplace that nothing you want drops at, we WILL be doing more raids and events so everyone will get theirs in due time.

Once we are in the raid it is going to be very important that you listen and keep you group leader up to date on your mana buffs and over all readiness so he can report to the raid leader if his group is good to go or not.

  • Always know who the main and secondary assist are and what assist your group will be using. Often everyone will be using the main assist with the secondary assist as a backup however, there are times when the raid will be handling 2-3 mobs at once to lessen the loads on the chanters. If this is the case make sure you are using the correct assist and if you are unsure of your assist ask the group leader.  In the confusion that is bound to happen everyone will have to make /assist name rather then just /assist.

  • When the pull comes into camp assist then engage the mob and re assist a few seconds later incase the main assist has changed. Attacking a mob that a chanter is trying to mez will not make you a favorite of the enchanter.

  • If a mob attacks you from the pull don't fight back. STAY on 
    target. The enchanters will be doing crowd control and will get the mob off you. If you don't see that happening let the group know so the crowd controller in the group can take care of the mob.

  • Never use shout on a raid that is reserved for the raid leader and the person in charge of groups. Any communication up to the raid leader that needs to be done should be done by your group leader.

  • Keep your group informed about your ready status. Let the group know when you need buffs are low on mana etc.

  • Only group leaders check kills and let the correct people know what loot if any is there.

  • Never loot anything unless you are told to.

  • Cast on mobs from melee range this is more important on large boss mobs than the smaller pulls but as a general rule cast almost in melee range.

  • NO WANDERING. If you have this sudden urge to go take a peak at a cool looking statue or see what’s around the next corner. DON’T. Stay with your group and move where the group or raid leader says.  No sightseeing during the raid. Stay with the group at all times. If you want to go sight seeing wait till the raid is over and the zone is clear.

  • Wait till a mob hits 75% health before you dot or nuke. Nothing will get a raid killed faster than someone who dots a mob right off only to have targets change and one pissed of doted mob that the chanters cant mez.

  • No pets until you are told its ok. If the raid leader says no pets 
    then no pets and no argument.

  • If you have a pet up keep a close eye on him. The second you don't see him or cant him off a mob kill him. If your group leader or raid leader tells you to kill your pet then kill it no questions.

  • Expect to wait. Wait on groups to be formed. Wait on the 
    insertion. Wait on mobs to be pulled. Wait to get a rez.  Nothing goes quickly on a planes raid until everyone has a good deal of experience in that plane.

  • If you need to leave the raid for any reason let your group leader know.

  • Don't expect to get much loot if any. The loot drops on the planes have been upped from what I understand but that is no guarantee you will not see 4 of the same pieces drop. I have been on fear raids to see 3 pairs of blighted boots drop and that is it. It takes time to get good loot on average expect to go to 8+ plane raids to get a decent set of gear if there are more than 4 of your class on the raid. It takes time don't get impatient.
