Clan X was born as a group effort from citizens of Norrath who wanted a guild in which they could fight alongside one another, gather great loot, be able to level at a reasonable pace, and above all, HAVE FUN!

The following list is the basic outline of how the guild exists in Norrath, with a few important rules for conduct within Clan X. (Please note that all of this information is very different for Wood Elf Rogues. Their charter can be found at www.Igetlosteverytenminutes.com )
       

  • This guild was created with the basic premise that every member has an equal say in how our guild operates. Members will elect the Officers. Officers will elect the Guild Leader.  

  • The Officers work FOR the Membership and the Guild Leader works for the Officers.   

  • Every member of Clan X has a say-so in how the guild is run. All major changes will be voted on in open forum. Every guild member has a vote.  

  • All members shall act in a mature way to reflect the true integrity of Clan X. This applies to all forms of communication, whether in guild chat, group chat, or external EQ forms of communication like e-mail and message boards. Remember that you show off that guild tag wherever you go in Norrath! No Clan X member shall use foul, belligerent or disrespectful language in guild chat. Profanity and name-calling will not be tolerated.

  • Always offer support to fellow members of Clan X whenever one is encountered in transit.

  • All alternate characters shall be known to members of Clan X. 

  • No Clan X member should ever put a citizen of Norrath ahead of a fellow Clan mate.     

  • Anon shall not be used by any Clan X member. Rather use the “Role-playing” switch. 

  • No Clan X member shall kill steal or train any individual, group and or guild. Any such member will be considered for removal from the guild. 

  • Clan X currently recruits all classes and all races. In addition, any of your alternate characters can be recruited, regardless of level, once you have a primary character in the guild. Keep a look out for other recruits even if you are not an officer - if you think they are valuable, then most likely the guild will feel the same way.

  • Members will do everything in their power to assist the Guild Leader move towards the goals set forth for the Guild Leader. 

  • Members will go to the OFFICERS with a problem and NOT the Guild Leader. If the Officers cannot solve it THEY will go to the Guild Leader.

  • A majority vote of 75% of members that vote is required to pass and/or amend any voting issue.  

  • We are using a point based, blind bid loot system.  You can view the loot rules with this link.  Loot rules    If it is decided that NBG or  random bidding is better for a certain raid, we will adapt one of the above methods to be decided before raid begins.

  • Officers shall make all attempts to make themselves available when real life permits. There are specific responsibilities and behaviors that an officer must display. Please see the responsibilities of an officer.

  • Members are responsible for their own travel plans and resurrections. Our Druids, Wizards and Clerics are NOT at your beck and call and want to have fun as well. If one us IS free to help you, fine. If not, make other arrangements.

  • Members are to adhere to the rules set forth here. If there are problems you will receive a warning. Then another warning for repeated infractions. 3 strikes and you are gone. Although this may sound harsh, it is for the good of the guild.

  • Finally. Get Involved in guild matters. Take some responsibility. Help out. This is your guild and if we all work together we will become strong and victorious.

Upon your entry into Clan X we guilded the person behind the character. If for any reason it is found out that you are no longer playing the character, or you are no longer the owner, or that character falls into other hands, that character and any guilded alternate characters will be disbanded.


"If, during your tenure with Clan X, it is viewed that your behavior is of a greedy, whining, complaining, demanding, overbearing, or unhelpful nature, you shall be Guild Removed."

Guild Leader

  • Lonedor is the Leader. PERIOD.

  • Lonedor answers to the Officers.

  • Officers have the ability to keep Lonedor in that position or vote her out.

  • Lonedor has the same vote as any Officer and votes with the Officers on any and all issues.

  • Lonedor does NOT have any Veto Power.

  • Lonedor is the final step and final word on any conflict the Officers cannot handle.

Officers

  • Officers answer to Lonedor and work for the Membership.

  • Officers are elected and removed by the Membership.

  • Officers are responsible to vote and shape minor changes in the guild as well as major changes before going to the Guild Membership.
