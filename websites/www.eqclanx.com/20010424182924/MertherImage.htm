"I answer the heroic question "Death, where is thy sting?" with "It is here in my heart and mind and memories."

"You fool!" screamed the Dark Elf, enraged by the sight of his cowering son. "How pathetic can you possibly be? How difficult is it to simply hold a dagger, and attack!?" The boy, trembling, was unable to answer. Tears and blood covered his waning, dark blue face. His father had been teaching him the ways of the rogue. The boy’s father expected him to excel in the art of combat. Today the boy began his training.

The Tier’dal train as if it were real combat, so, Tuval, the boy’s father, attacked his son as he would a Fier’dal or a human. Being a respected Rake within the Hall of Ebon Mask, having a son with less than amazing abilities was an insult. "You would dare shed tears for mere pain?" In an uncontrolled fit of rage, Tuval strikes the boy, sending him flying across the room. Despite his heritage as a rogue, Tuval had impressive strength which matched his larger than normal size. "I will show you true pain"

"The boy has had enough, the day is over. You will continue tomorrow." Came an unannounced voice. Turning to identify this new presence, Tuval found himself confronted by his wife, Lana. Standing calmly, her white hair hanging loosely on her strong shoulders. Lana’s red eyes stared defiantly into those of her husband.

Annoyed at her presence, Tuval drew his daggers and lunged at her. She was prepared, however and easily evaded his attack. Drawing her own daggers, a twin pair of delicately crafted Tier’dal blades, she tensed her stomach and legs and prepared to defend herself.

"You would dare interfere with the boy’s training? Perhaps the leader was right in exiling you." He launched a barrage that was met by and equally effective set of parries.

"You have grown slow and week Tuval." Taunted Lana. "You should not stay here and waste time with this excuse for a son." The two had trained together for decades and each knew the other almost as well as they knew themselves. "Why waste your time with this boy? Norrath is ripe for the picking, yet you stay here, growing slow and weak."

With that, she launched her body at his, only to find he had already stepped aside. She landed hard on the floor, but almost immediately began to get up. But Tuval was quick to act. Before she was on her feet, one of Tuval’s daggers had already found its way to her side. She fell, and doubled over in pain. Tuval turned her onto her stomach, then looked over to his trembling son.

"She does not cry." He said. "Yet she deserves death." Lana had allied herself with many people she had met throughout her life, people whom the Tier’dal were forbidden to even interact with, unless it be to do battle.

He returned his gaze to that of his wife, and whispered "You, woman, will never be my equal. I may be a fool, but I am not your fool." Raising his dagger above her body, he prepared to thrust it into her spine.

"May Innoruuk have mercy on your soul" She whispered with her final breaths. Closing her eyes, accepting her fate, she waited. And then, Tuval ended her life.

He stood up and faced his son. "This is what your life will be. No one will help you. No one will care. All you will feel will be the unending hatred of Innoruuk. That is how you will live." He removed the two blades from his wife’s dead body. "Or you will not live at all." He said, looking at his wife’s body. She was now as lifeless as the dagger that had killed her; as lifeless as her own dagger, only a few paces away from her; as lifeless as her son feared he would be. Tuval then walked away, slowly and unwavering, leaving his shivering son huddled in the corner, crying.
