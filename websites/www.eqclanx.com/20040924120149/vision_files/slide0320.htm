What exactly is “family” in EQ?
The Sandbox Metaphor
“Family” is a matter of perception.  Us kids in the family used to play in the sandbox (newbies in the newbie zones).  But as we have gotten older (leveled), some of us like to go down to the beach and ride the big waves instead of building the sand castle (trying out new zones which just might mean a demand for raiding instead of grouping).  Are we less of a family for doing that?  Absolutely not. 
The Tea Cup Metaphor
Little Jenny needs to know that Tommy and the big boys may go off and ride the Sky Coaster without her and that they really do like to ride the Sky Coaster.  So if Jenny and her friends are okay with riding the Tea Cups, that’s cool and they can meet her for lunch occasionally.
Family does NOT mean NO RAIDING
Being part of a family guild is an attitude that you bring – if you want it, you drive it.  If you want the family feel of non-raiding, then set up non-raiding events from single dungeon crawl groups to scavenger hunts to brawls in the arena.  But raids do NOT make a guild a NON-FAMILY guild – it just means they decided to try something new and exciting once in awhile.
Families grow together as a natural part of life.  And as a result, change will happen.  Instead of dolls and legos, you will have bikes, sleepovers, and dating.  But that family is still a family.  Family guilds in EQ can maintain their family and togetherness, they just have to learn to grow together, and learn that each individual is at a different place in that growth cycle.  Respect the differences, find a way to give each individual what they are looking for, and modify the guild around the needs of its members.
