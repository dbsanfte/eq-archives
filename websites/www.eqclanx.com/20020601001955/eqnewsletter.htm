Absor's Update Hello again! Welcome to another edition of the Harpy's Head Herald. EverQuest is all about adventure. Sometimes we take the adventure off the computer screen and into real life. If you've ever wondered what goes on at the Fan Faire, or been curious about how your fellow players act in real life, well we've got something for you. We have a short video taken at the Dallas Fan Faire, so check it out to get a glimpse at the fun of these events. But we haven't neglected the in-game adventure either. Legends has opened up with its first server, Stormhammer, and the tales of adventure from the folks playing there are, well, legendary; take a look at some of their stories at the Legends site (just click on the Tales of Adventure button). Those of you that have been playing longer in EverQuest might remember that we took a very in-depth look at the way melee combat worked, and at how each of the melee classes compared to one another. It's a time consuming effort, and a complicated one to implement properly.We'll be keeping you up to date on our progress, and you on the Developer's Corner. You can read the first update about this in the Caster Balance Update thread. May your adventures be exciting, in-game and out. -Alan

EverQuest: State of the Game - What Can You Expect? EverQuest has seen a great increase in new players since the release of The Shadows of Luclin. Given the sudden arrival of so many new adventurers into the world of Norrath, we've taken time to enrich the EverQuest experience at lower levels. At the same time, we're in the process of making a number of changes aimed at improving the game for players of all levels. What can you expect as a player? We are detailing the upcoming changes in a series of "State of the Game" letters, which you can read on the EverQuest Live site. Here are a few highlights from the first two letters: For those new to EverQuest, as well as our mid-level players:
  • You are no longer required to stare at your spellbook while meditating.
  • The rate at which people gain experience up to level ten has been accelerated.
  • Characters level nine and under will appear at their bind point with all of their equipment intact when they die.
  • The commonly known "hell-levels" at 30, 35, 40, and 45 have been largely done away with. Further, the "post-hell-level" experience loss in levels 31, 36, 41, and 46 has been removed.
More changes are on the way, so stay tuned to www.EverQuestlive.com for further details.

.   Third Anniversary Celebration March 16, 2002 marks the third anniversary of the launch of EverQuest. During these past three years, much has happened in the game. Three expansion packs have been launched with great success. Additional free zones and planes became available for download. The EverQuest Legends Service opened. Five Fan Faire events have been held throughout the country with many more still to come, and EverQuest's graphics engine received a complete overhaul in the Shadows of Luclin expansion. EQ's third anniversary celebrations will run through the weekend of March 16th with special events on all servers. Here's just a taste of what is happening in EQ:
  • Fishing contests
  • Archery contest
  • Royal Rumble (PvP fighting in the Arena)
  • Miss Antonica Pageant (Talent contest and question and answer session)
  • Level 1 Drunken Race (Racers will be PvP flagged to make it more challenging)
  • Invasion Quests
  • Roleplaying interaction quests
  • AND MUCH MORE!
Rumor has it that some gods will even be making appearances as well!

  Guide to Earning Shar Vahl Citizenship for New Vah Shir Players Welcome to the City of Shar Vahl. This city is the home of the descendants of Vah Kerrath, King of the ascended Vah Shir tribes. King Raja Kerrath, the grandson of King Vah Kerrath, currently rules the city. Each citizen is expected to pay homage to King Kerrath and maintain their obligations to both their community and chosen order. Becoming a Shar Vahl Citizen The process of becoming a citizen of Shar Vahl starts with the Guildmaster of the order that you have decided to pursue. After turning in your guild summons to the person listed on the note, you will be asked to deliver a note to Registrar Bindarah. Her office can be found in the building complex just across from the South Gate bridge that leads to Shadeweaver Thicket… Continue to the step-by-step guide. The Professional Orders of the Vah Shir Upon becoming a citizen, each member of the community is expected to choose a professional route. They will be required to follow this route for the duration of their time asa citizen of Shar Vahl. Life as a Vah Shir has been very difficult since the time of the Ascension. The darkness of Luclin has caused decades of conflict. Due to this, each profession is expected to fulfill many roles. After completing the formal process of becoming a citizen of Shar Vahl, a young Vah Shir can choose from five professional orders. These orders are the Dar Khura, Khala Dun, Khati Sha, Jharin, Taruun… Learn about the five professional orders of the Vah Shir.
