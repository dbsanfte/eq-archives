Lonedor’s Early Years

Chapter 1 – Lonedor is named

Here begins the tale of Lonedor. Lonedor lived in a world known as Norrath. Norrath is a world with several known continents peopled by a multitude of races including Barbarians, several different races of Elves, Erudites, Gnomes, Halflings, Humans, Ogres, Trolls, Dwarves and Lizardmen. For the most part, these races stay among themselves in their own areas of Norrath. However there were also many brave adventurers from all races that can be found through out Norrath.

Lonedor was raised on the continent of Antonica in a small coastal town called Freeport. Freeport is located midway between the north and south ends of Antonica on the Eastern seaboard. To the south of Freeport lays the Desert of Ro. The Desert of Ro is a dry and dangerous place. The further south one goes the more dangerous and inhospitable the area becomes. Populations dwindle and monsters run rampant in the southern most reaches of Ro. The Desert of Ro has many pinnacles, spires, and obelisks poking through the sands of Ro. Learned scholars believed that there is an ancient massive civilization buried beneath the desert. The ancient civilization seemed to have been a Mecca for powerful magic users. There was a platform in the northern part of the desert of Ro that was imbued with a sense of magical power. Wizards and other magic- users still continued to use it as a teleportation site. These spires and obelisks were all over Antonica. Even in the Commonlands. Here was a tunnel to some catacombs known as Befallen. Befallen seems to add credence to the scholars theory that there is an ancient city buried under the desert. The magical energy in Befallen is enormous and necromancers and undead roam the corridors and halls of this ancient underground ruined city. It is filled with the stench of stale moldy air and death and decay. Many of the monsters are diseased by rabies and other nasty plagues.

To the west of Freeport are the Commonlands, which is a grassy savanna teaming with natural and unnatural wildlife. There are bears, lions, wolves, griffins and the undead. To the north of Freeport is the Nektulos Forest. This is a region that Lonedor has not ventured to yet.

Freeport is mostly a human city with most of the normal attractions found in cities of its size. There are merchants, shops, a theatre, an arena and a city guard. Most guilds are represented in Freeport. They range from Paladins, Clerics, Warriors and Magic-users. There are forges, bakeries and breweries. And of course, there are docks since Freeport is a coastal city.

Lonedor did not know anything about where he was born or who his parents were. He was left alone at the door of the paladins as an infant. The paladins took him in and raised him as their own. Since they didn’t know anything about this infant left on their doorstep they called him Lonedor because he was left a "lone" at the "door", hence, Lonedor. It is a little known fact that paladins do have a very good since of humor, although maybe not the wittiest, as shown by the naming of their new ward.

Lonedor was normal human baby. The only distinguishing feature about him was a mysterious mark on his left breast slightly above his heart. The paladins could not tell if it was a tattoo, a birthmark, or something less mundane. Upon occasion the paladins would swear the mark glowed slightly iridescent.

Chapter 2 - Life as a Squire

Lonedor spent his youth becoming very familiar with physical labor while serving as a squire to Sir Theo. As a young boy, Lonedor was a squire to Sir Theo. Lonedor received much of his training from Sir Theo and continues to practice with him upon his returns to Freeport. Lonedor was not the best pupil Sir Theo had. Lonedor was left-handed by nature and so he had to work hard to overcome this flaw because there is no such thing as a left-handed swordsman. Although many warriors fight with a weapon in each hand the paladins did not see this as a noble and chivalrous way to fight. A true noble warrior should give his opponent at least an equal opportunity to be able to win in battle because the virtuous warrior would win because he was fighting the noble fight and his god would protect him.

Lonedor began practicing his fighting skills just outside the West gate of Freeport. Lonedor was given a rusty one-handed sword from Sir Theo to begin his training. Lonedor practiced his skills by killing rats, bats and snakes. Upon occasion a Deathfist Pawn would catch Lonedor straying too far from the West gate and give Lonedor a real beating. Fortunately for Lonedor, the ever-vigilant guards were still close enough to save Lonedor’s life on several occasions. Lonedor still has many of the scars from his encounters with the Deathfist Pawns. It was these encounters that made Lonedor want to be more than a warrior. He wanted to be able to help all his friends and even people he didn’t know by being able to do more than swing a sword. So as Lonedor grew he trained with the Clerics at the Temple of Marr in the ways of healing.

Lonedor remembers the warm summer days and the smell of the sea mixed with that of the war-horses as he cleaned their stalls, rubbed them down and brushed their manes and tails. The war-horses were large powerful animals that the paladins adorned in armor similar to their own plate mail. The paladins used the war-horses in their pursuit to cleanse Norrath of all evil. The war-horses were invaluable in battling larger creatures such as griffins and giants. The war-horses were more than weapons to be wielded by the paladins; they were partners with their riders. Each horse and rider functioned as one powerful massive weapon.

Along with Lonedor’s duties of tending to the war-horses he also polished the armor and did some minor sewing to mend leather armor and backpacks. These sewing skills were something he continued to practice so he could be self-sufficient while traveling.

During the winters at Freeport when travel was limited due to snow and other bad weather, Lonedor worked at one of the local forges in the Northern part of the city under the tutelage of a master dwarven smith. While working the forge and swing the hammer, Lonedor began to develop a very strong and hard body.

Another aspect of Lonedor’s training and education was handled by the clerics at the Temple of Marr. The clerics were wise masters at the art of healing and lived by a code of helping all that were in need. Many of the paladins and clerics were followers of Mithaniel Marr. Followers of Mithaniel Marr believe that valor is what separates civilized beings from beasts. His followers live by a strict moral code that prizes truth, honor, and charity. They are champions of the downtrodden and the most noble of warriors. His followers strive to rid Norrath of all things dark and evil, often sacrificing themselves in this never-ending crusade. They will not rest until the day when all of Norrath is cleansed in Mithaniel’s light. They take themselves and their duty very seriously, and have little patience for mischief and mayhem. Many citizens of Norrath have said that paladins have a "holier than thou" attitude which is not true. As mentioned earlier they have a wonderful sense of humor but they also take their duty to the citizens for Norrath extremely serious.

The clerics were also very well read and had amassed an amazing amount of knowledge in all areas including economics, politics, geography, mathematics, science and magic. The brotherhood of clerics at the Temple of Marr taught Lonedor much, an instilled in him the virtue and honor of helping others. Unfortunately for Lonedor, the brotherhood of clerics at the Temple of Marr had also taken vows of celibacy and they taught Lonedor about the sins of carnal pleasure. The paladins however took no such vows and many had wives. There were some paladins that were so into their crusade that they did not have the time for such diversions. Due to this mixed education in philosophy, Lonedor continues to struggle mentally and emotionally with the virtues of sexual pleasure. Physically Lonedor has never partaken in the delights of knowing a woman but in his own mind he was a master at pleasuring women. Lonedor found all women beautiful and fascinating. Unfortunately every time he met a woman his mind would stray to their physical beauty accompanied by some kind of sexual fantasy. Due to his training as a swordsman and the duty to his code he would never let his mind stray when he was actually engaged in a battle.

Chapter 3 – Lonedor meets friends

Lonedor was a shy, quiet lad while growing up. Most of the time he practiced his skills by himself and didn’t have time to practice with the other children due to his duties as a squire. However as Lonedor got stronger in both the healing arts and swordsmanship he began to venture further from Freeport and traveled into the northern area of the Desert of Ro. There his skills have been truly tested. Lonedor started to rely on others and group with other noble people who had a desire to eradicate evil from the land. Lonedor began to seek out alliances with people of different races and skills. There were magic-users, bards, druids, drawves, elves, Halflings, rogues and shamans. Most of the people were also adventurers honing their skills and wiping out evil where they found it.

Lonedor was in the desert one day, not to far from a local inn, walking near some palm trees, when he happened upon a lovely young female High-elf. Young is a relative term when describing elves. No matter how old elves are when measured by years they still maintain a youthful appearance. This particular elf had long flaxen hair and was dressed in a flowing sea-green robe made out of the most delicate and wispy silk fabric that clung deliciously to the shape of her body, at least as seen by Lonedor. In her hand she carried a delicately carved ornate oaken staff. Lonedor was well aware that the staff was much more than a decorated walking stick. It could be wielded as a lethal weapon. Lonedor had seen others of her class slay extremely dangerous beasts with similar staffs. Lonedor came to learn this lovely elf’s name was Marissa. During that day Lonedor helped Marissa hone her skills, as a magic- user on some of the minor beasts of the desert, by acting as her guardian in case her spells failed or the beasts became more that she could handle. As Lonedor visited with Marissa he learned she was a member of a guild. They talked briefly about the guild, but it was not until later when one of Marissa’s friends came along that Lonedor learned much more about guilds and Marissa’s guild in particular. Marissa’s friend was a powerful Barbarian Shaman named Elgor. Elgor was an officer in the guild. Lonedor learned from Elgor that the guild was called "The Ancient Order of Guardians." Elgor explained the purpose of the guild and the Code of Ethics the members lived by. Their mission was to protect and serve the citizens of Norrath by showing courage, charity and educating anyone in need. Lonedor himself had spent many days helping people just like Marissa and was already living by that code.

Lonedor was very impressed by this mission and was of a like mind to the two members he had met so far. Elgor invited Lonedor to join the guild and Lonedor gladly accepted the offer. Lonedor was soon promoted to officer status within the Ancient Order of Guardians, only Mithaniel Marr knows why, but he saw it as a great honor and accepted the position. As a badge of being a member within the guild, Elgor gave Lonedor a tarnished dagger and told him that it would act as his compass. It was not a compass in the physical sense where it would point North, but it was a compass to remind Lonedor of the proper path to follow on his spiritual journey in helping others.

Lonedor is very non-political by nature and is sometimes oblivious to most issues that are not part of the code he is living. However as in any loving family there can still be disputes that arise and so there was within the Ancient Order of Guardians. But in this particular instance there seemed to be something sinister that was acting upon one of the guildmembers. Lonedor had no idea of what kind of force could make a person act that way but Lonedor knew very little in the ways of enchantment.

Chapter 4 – Lonedor thinks of women

At one of the guild meetings in the Northern Desert of Ro by the Ocean, Lonedor was introduced to a human female Paladin. Human female paladins are fairly rare and Lonedor found that to be exotic. Lonedor felt a special bond with all Paladins; especially human Paladins from his hometown. Her name was Scarlitt and she was a long legged dark haired beauty. Her long dark hair reminded him of the pleasure he got from brushing the tails and manes of the warhorses. She was adorned chain mail and wielded a long, powerful 2 handed sword. Scarlitt had a powerful athletic build and yet very shapely female’s body. Female warriors and paladins looked stunning in their finely crafted chain mail and breastplates. The dwarven smiths were superb artisans. They crafted the breastplates with a hint of nipples and the full, well-cupped breasts, tapered ribcage and washboard stomach. Lonedor was well versed in the buckles and trapping of paladin armor and knew he could have it all off of Scarlitt in less than 2 minutes if she was willing. Lonedor was torn between his chivalrous nature as a gentleman and his male desire when he looked upon Scarlitt. On the one hand he wanted to be the prefect gentleman and on the other hand his male desire wanted to take her like the barbarians or lizardmen would or even like the druids in their wolf-form. Lonedor had to mentally laugh at himself as his mind came back from his fantasy about Scarlitt. Here he was a fine righteous upstanding paladin having sexual fantasies about a fellow adventurer and guildmember. But as long as he was thinking about physical pleasure, he could also mentally picture the shapely druids in their leather tunics, or magic-users in their fine, lightweight silk robes, or even the huge, rotund framed ogre women could be strangely appealing. As Lonedor got to know Scarlitt he learned she was a very flirtatious and gregarious young woman. Lonedor, being a true gentleman, often wondered if she got the name, Scarlitt, because that is the color she made the young men’s checks turn with her flirting. Scarlitt would later become one of Lonedor’s favorite hunting partners.

Chapter 5 – Lonedor receives help

As Lonedor ponders his early years, there is one figure that stands out more so than anyone else that has aided him as he was growing. The figure is that of a diminutive dwarf cleric named Guldarn. Guldarn was always nearby when Lonedor needed anything. He gave Lonedor platinum pieces, better armor, better weapons, guidance, advice and magically fortified him every time they met. There seemed to be a bond between Guldarn and Lonedor but Lonedor did not know why.

Lonedor also received many gifts from very skilled paladins he only met in passing. As these paladins upgraded their own armor and weapons they would pass their extra equipment down to other struggling paladins.

Chapter 6 – Lonedor picks a surname and learns of his parents

As is the custom among many of the citizens of Norrath, when a person comes of age in experience and skill, they select their own surname. The time was quickly approaching for Lonedor to select his surname. As it so happens, Guldarn shows up unexpectedly and informs Lonedor that he has something he must tell him about his parents now that he is of age.

Guldarn explained that he knew Lonedor’s mother and father and that Lonedor’s mother’s name was Adora and his father’s name was Herradon. Adora and Herradon were also followers of Mithaniel Marr. While doing the will of Mithaniel Marr, Adora came upon a group of noble warriors being badly beaten by some vile evil forces of Norrath. Adora was a cleric and was able to save the group of warriors, which included Herradon. It wasn’t long after that Adora and Herradon fell in love and were married. The two were inseparable and fought together in many battles against the forces of darkness.

Eventually Herradon and Adora conceived a child. Since Adora was with child, the two of them did not travel as far from home as was normal. On one stormy night there was a knock at their door and out in the storm stood two elves, one male and one female, also with child. Their names were Ailwon and Liana. They said they were running from an evil wizard by the name of Gylurec and that they need a place to hide for just a couple of days. Doing what any follower of Mithaniel Marr would have done, Herradon and Adora concealed the elves for a couple of days and listened to the evil story of Gylurec. It wasn’t until weeks later that Gylurec would discover the trail of Ailwon and Liana but some how he did. After returning home from a short visit to the neighbors Herradon opened the door to their cottage and found Gylurec standing inside. Herradon recognized Gylurec by the description given him by Ailwon and being the quick thinking warrior Herradon was he slammed to cottage door, drew his sword and leapt at Gylurec. Tragically Gylurec was an extremely powerful wizard and hit Herradon with a ball of fire before he could reach him. But the weight and momentum of Herradon’s leap propelled his charred body toward the wizard and the wizard was pierced by Herradon’s sword. It was not a mortal blow but it was enough for the wizard to exit in a flash of light.

Adora was distraught by her beloved husband’s death but knew she also need to take care of her unborn child. Adora set about to devise a plan. She had read a spell in one of the ancient tomes in the Temple of Marr that would cast a permanent rune of protection upon a living being. The spell had not been performed in centuries because the caster always died. She enlisted the aid of Guldarn, who protested fiercely, but eventually bowed to Adora’s wishes. Guldarn collected all the needed spell components and set up a room for the ritual. Adora eventually gave birth to the child known as Lonedor and upon his birth Adora cast the spell. The spell required all of her physical and mental energy to cast the rune of protection upon Lonedor. Adora was successful at casting the spell where upon she died and Lonedor was forever marked. Adora had succeeded at both tasks she had planned for. She protected her child as she left one realm and she was able to be re-united with her beloved husband in the next.

It was Adora’s wish that Guldarn leave the baby with the paladins so he could know both the ways of a warrior, his father’s ways, and that of a cleric, his mother’s ways. Guldarn would secretly watch over Lonedor as he grew until Lonedor came of age and would learn of his mother and father’s deaths.

After hearing this story Lonedor wept but he was proud that he could follow in both his mother and father’s footsteps. After much thought and reflection Lonedor chose his last name. He chose Blade to symbolize his father the warrior and Wise to symbolize his mother the cleric. Hence from this point on he was known as Lonedor Bladewise.

Here begins the story of Lonedor Bladewise, the middle years, subtitled;

Lonedor takes a wife or

Lonedor take vows of the priesthood or

Lonedor goes insane.
