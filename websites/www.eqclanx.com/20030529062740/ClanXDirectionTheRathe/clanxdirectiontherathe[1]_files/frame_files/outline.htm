  The City of Mist story
When Clan X first started going to City of Mist, we did everything wrong.  If we were lucky, we might have killed the first Black Reaver during the raid, seldom would we find a second one.  Our trains were record breaking, our CRs lasted for hours.  But the guild was relentless, and would not stop trying for the brass ring (in this case, Jade Reavers).  We didnt know where to stand, so we fell in the water and died as the mobs pounded on us.  And lets not forget Rixmas favorite Blonde Blunder when she attacked Rak Ashirr and wiped out the alliance.

Today, Clan X OWNS this zone.  We no longer need four groups of people to take out all 6 Black Reavers - we can do it easily with two, sometimes one.  And we can pull all 6 Black Reavers and have to wait till the next spawn cycle starts.  Not only that, so many people in Clan X could LEAD this raid - why?  Because you have gone to so many of them that you know instinctively what to do - where to stand, when to attack, when to back off, when to pull more mobs.  And after over 100 Black Reaver kills, Verrant has finally decided to reward us with Jade Reavers.

City of Mist is a SUCCESS STORY for Clan X.  This is what you are all capable of.

Our Needs
  March 2, 2002
1.  Lets focus on our guild relations
Lets make sure we are having fun again in Norrath, and that our guild has high guild morale.  People should be able to do what they want while in Norrath - no mandatory raiding.
Restrict Official Raiding - too may raids scheduled - some are burnt out on raiding.  Max official raids scheduled monthly are 2 Planes Raids, 2 Final Epic Mobs, 4 Velious Faction/Quests, no more than 2 raids per day scheduled on the calendar, impromptu raids encouraged.
YOUR 2 Days a week - No official raids will be scheduled on these two days - members are encouraged to do whatever it is they like best.
Focus on Leveling our Members - people want experience - lets give them that.
Officers to attend scheduled raids (if they can - since we are reducing official raids)
Chain of Command - bring issues to your officers - dont take all issues to Rixma.  Rixmas role is more like the CEO of a company dealing with strategic planning, hence the guild leader, BUT she is equal in power to the rest of the officers.  Members know who the officers are!
Problem members will be addressed immediately and put on probation for causing conflicts.
