General guidelines on how to run a planes raid
(these are not listed in order of importance as all these are important)

  • Always know who the main and secondary assist are and what assist your group will be using. Often everyone will be using the main assist with the secondary assist as a backup however, there are times when the raid will be handling 2-3 mobs at once to lessen the loads on the chanters. If this is the case make sure you are using the correct assist and if you are unsure of your assist ask the group leader.

  • When the pull comes into camp assist then engage the mob and re assist a few seconds later incase the main assist has changed. Attacking a mob that a chanter is trying to mez will not make you a favorite of the enchanter.

  • If a mob attacks you from the pull don't fight back. STAY on 
    target. The enchanters will be doing crowd control and will get the mob off you. If you don't see that happening let the group know so the crowd controller in the group can take care of the mob.

  • Never use shout on a raid that is reserved for the raid leader and the person in charge of groups. Any communication up to the raid leader that needs to be done should be done by your group leader.

  • Keep your group informed about your ready status. Let the group know when you need buffs are low on mana etc.

  • Only group leaders check kills and let the correct people know what loot if any is there.

  • Never loot anything unless you are told to.

  • Cast on mobs from melee range this is more important on large boss mobs than the smaller pulls but as a general rule cast almost in melee range.

  • No sightseeing during the raid. Stay with the group at all times. If you want to go sight seeing wait till the raid is over and the zone is clear.

  • Wait till a mob hits 75% health before you dot or nuke. Nothing will get a raid killed faster than someone who dots a mob right off only to have targets change and one pissed of doted mob that the chanters cant mez.

  • No pets until you are told its ok. If the raid leader says no pets 
    then no pets and no argument.

  • If you have a pet up keep a close eye on him. The second you don't see him or cant him off a mob kill him. If your group leader or raid leader tells you to kill your pet then kill it no questions.

  • Expect to wait. Wait on groups to be formed. Wait on the 
    insertion. Wait on mobs to be pulled. Wait to get a rez.      Nothing goes quickly on a planes raid until everyone has a good deal of experience in that plane.

  • If you need to leave the raid for any reason let your group leader know.

  • Don't expect to get much loot if any. The loot drops on the planes have been upped from what I understand but that is no guarantee you will not see 4 of the same pieces drop. I have been on fear raids to see 3 pairs of blighted boots drop and that is it. It takes time to get good loot on average expect to go to 8+ plane raids to get a decent set of gear if there are more than 4 of your class on the raid. It takes time don't get impatient.

I would also like to talk about loot rules as well.

  • On a raid there are two classifications of people who are considered for loot. There are primary people who are part of the guild(s) on the raid. There are then secondary people who are invites and there to help the raid guild(s). Typically we are on a raid to get loot for our guild(s). Anything considered desired loot is for the guild(s) doing the raid and its members. The guild members are given first choice on any and all loot that drops on the raid. If no guild member can use the loot then it is offered to the invites. At no time is an invite give loot or the option for loot over a guild member. I think this just makes sense we are there to outfit and help ourselves first.
    Random for loots is fine to assign loot order on the planes but there are some pieces of loot that are so rare or prized that the are never done by random. This is things like plane robes and breast plates (esp warrior breastplates which people wait months to get) weapon drops off of gods etc. These items are most often rewarded to members based on seniority or guild involvement due to the rarity and stats of the items. I would like to see some system worked out for such loot that we can all agree on.  Random amongst guild members for most loot is fine but if you have a better item do not random. In the early days many people will want or need these items and if you random on an item that will either sit in the bank or you will sell for phat plat then you are no help to the guild. Many members will need items to help make themselves better. We are not going on raids to help fatten someone's bank account.
