The Making of a Guardian

Through the eyes of Jaston
Chapter 1

It wasn’t all that long ago, now that I think about it. Mother and I out in the fields, we used to help farm for the Druids at their guild. Mother had always wanted me to be a Druid, but magic just wasn’t my thing.

I had often wandered off to Misty Thicket, and watched proud Warriors fighting against all sorts of creatures. I one day decided to join them. For me this was a proud day, as was it for my mother. She was so proud, she had bought me a brand new suit, made from raw hide. I had asked her where she got the money, she just smiled.

Our family had never been rich, so we knew how to pinch a copper to go the furthest. I wore the raw hide in honor of her for a few seasons of training. It was about my 4th or 5th season when I met my mentor. He was far stronger than I, on a visit home was, he would be cutting through Runny Eye to get to the Karans.

I was in the midst of a battle that I was almost positive I could win. The goblin had laughed when I hit it with my arrow, but after a few swings of my blade, he began to be weary.

The creature had run as fast as it could, but not fast enough, and I dealt the finishing blow. Unfortunately he had yelled for help loud enough to get the attention of Motoo. He began to attack me, I tried to defend myself, but it was no use. I ran as fast as I could, but the guards were to far away, I yelled and yelled, and finally, I couldn’t here the creature behind me.

I turned around to see a Halfling Warrior, the likes of I had never seen before. He wore full crafted armor, and wielded a gigantic Executioners Axe.

"Are you ok son?" He asked me.

"I- I think so" Was all I could squeak out. I was in total awe. He then bandaged me enough to walk me back to Rivervale.

"My name is Jaston, I have to thank you for helping me back there, please come to dinner at my house tonight. Mother always loves company."

"Thank you son, and please call me Poppen"

We ate heartily, well, for us it was heartily, Poppen seemed to be hungry afterwards, but he didn’t say anything about it.

Poppen later went for a walk through Rivervale while Mother cleaned the kitchen.

"If you don’t mind son, what happened to your Father?" Poppen asked, as we neared our guild.

"He, um, well, ya see, the thing is I never knew my father. Mother says he went out explorin’ one day and never came back."

"Oh, I’m sorry. Here, I know you are a good kid, so here, I want you to have some of my old equipment. They used to be my armor."

Poppen went to the bank teller, and returned to me with a Bronze Breastplate and legs, and a langseax. I was so happy. I didn’t know what to say, I could hardly manage a thank you.

From that day I have looked up to Poppen as the father I had never had, he has guided me through everything I have done, and has helped me in so many ways I couldn’t begin to thank him.

When Poppen went to the Karans, I was left alone with Mother. It was one night in late September that I first got the vision. It was of Brell, he was beckoning me. I told Mother, she was taken aback by what I said, but she quickly excused it as childish imagination.

This ‘imagination’ didn’t do away though. I kept getting more and more vivid Visions. Then one day, out of nowhere, I felt my body get gated away to somewhere new. I was told later t was the Plain of Underfoot, but for the time, I was too scared to care.

I came to my scenes in front of a massive throne, atop it sat Brell himself.

"JASTON!" He boomed. "My son! You have come of age, and it is beyond the time to speak to you the truth of your existence." He stopped for a moment, had his servants gather me food, drink, and a soft place to sit.

"You my son are special" He began again, "Unlike most others whose existence upon Norrath won’t amount to anything, you my son are destined for greatness. You are one of my chosen sons, my blood flows through your veins, and will give you the strength to overcome all one day." This was all unreal, I couldn’t grasp what he was saying, but somehow I knew it to be true.

"Your brother, he is the only other like you. He is a Paladin, from Kaladim. He is a Dwarf by the name of Caviat. You should visit him, together you will be unbeatable!" Again he rested, he obviously wasn’t used to talking for long periods of time.

"Finally, I have one last deed for you to accomplish. Go to the Eastern Commonlands, and find a High Elf named Larton. Ask him for a compass, when he offers you one, ask him again. He will this time show you a rusted dagger. Take this. You will then be considered one of the Ancient Order of Guardians. Only with their help, will you ever be able to reach your full potential."

With that all went black again, and I awoke back on my bed at home, Mother was standing over me.

"I must go to East Commons" was all I said. She seemed to understand.

The Making of a Guardian

Through the eyes of Jaston
Chapter 2

The Commonlands, oh how I despised them. Full of vile creatures and corrupt merchants, each fighting for the best price on everything. It has always seemed so senseless to me. I guess I’m just old-fashioned thinking that the greatest item is the one you get yourself.

None the less I was on a journey from Brell, I had to find Larton. He was very good at keeping himself hidden in a crowd. The type you don’t know is there until he shows himself to you.

Fortunately that is exactly what he did. I was about to venture to the desert to look harder, but he approached me from almost out of nowhere.

"Little one, I know this will sound odd, but I have seen you before. Not in this world though, in another, in a dream almost." He had said. I almost jumped out of my skin. I was nothing more than a country boy, the thought of appearing in a total stranger’s dream was almost too strange.

But none the less, I was on a mission from my God. I had to see if he was the right one, the one that Brell had directed me to.

"Sir, you wouldn’t, umm, wouldn’t happen to have a compass I could have do you?"

"Why of course, here, you will find none better in the land" He said, just as Brell had told me, he showed me a normal compass.

"Ummm, no sir, I meant a ‘compass’, do you have a ‘compass’?" He seemed to understand then, because he walked me to a dark corner, gave me a rusty dagger and told me to hold it close to my heart at all times.

That I have done to this day. Larton and I have met on several occasions since, as have I met many other guardians. One of the greatest ever was Cnyyen. He was a fine paladin. He saved my life many a time, and I have not the abilities to begin to thank him.

He and I first went to see Caviat. We traveled for hours on a boat to cross to Kaladim. Almost as if Caviat was expecting me, he got up from the docks. We embraced as brothers, and talked long into the night. Cnyyen joined the merriment, and I must say, that night has lead me to become the alcoholic I am today.

The morning came, and as we had decided the previous night, Caviat and I would return to the Commonlands and meet Larton, and Cnyyen would return to Kelethin. The plan was to have Caviat join us and become unbelievably strong. But it was not to be………

On the boat home, I experienced my second inter-plain travel. Caviat and I were transported to the Plain of Hate. Cazic Thule himself spoke to us, he spoke of the way of fear, of why he was far more powerful than Brell. He was desperate to have Caviat and I join him, that he offered us all of the land upon Norrath for our loyalty. I refused, as did Caviat, but he was not as fortunate as I to escape. Cazic grabbed a hold of Caviat and transported him to a plain he only knows of. I was far more fortunate, as Brell transported me to the Plain of Underfoot before Cazic could grasp me.

Time went on, I continued to run errands for Brell. Cnyyen has come and gone, his memory still lies ingrained in my mind for eternity. As does that of Caviat. I wondered what Cazic had done to him. I imagined he had killed him, but I wasn’t 100% sure.

This is when the worst news of my life arrived. Poppen approached me one day, he seemed in a panic. He told me that we would be Teleporting to Misty immediately. He told me my mother had a incurable sickness, and she didn’t have that much time left.

Poppen and I arrived at my home to see Mother laying in bed. Large blisters could be seen all over her skin. I knew it was an act of a higher power, none had ever seen this plague before, and there seemed no way to cure it. Cazic knew he couldn’t attack me directly without alerting Brell.

I stayed by Mother’s bed for 2 weeks, then one day, I woke, and, well, she didn’t.

That day was the worst imaginable, for no more than an hour after I found Mother dead, I heard a bitter laugh. Upon searching the house, I couldn’t find the source, but it seemed to be following me. Then in a flash of light, a creature appeared before me. I was a hideous lizard creature I had seen before. These Iksar were some of the vilest creatures on the planet.

This one was a necromancer, as I began to unsheathe my axe, it hissed in a unforgettable voice, "Hello Brother"

It was Caviat.

Chapter 3:  The Tables Turn

It was almost instinct as of now. My axe was quickly in my hands, and I charged him headlong. How could I kill Caviat? My brother? No! He isn’t! My mind was racing, but it seemed to be right to attack him, though even now I can’t see why. Probably had to do with Brell and Cazic. Damn him till the day I die. DAMN YOU CAZIC THULE!

My attack seemed worthless, for Caviat merle sidestepped my swings. He began to cast a spell, and for a moment my heart stopped. I realized I was not wearing my armor, for that had been mother’s only rule. "No armor in the house, this is a place of peace, not of war." She had lectured me about that many times. But now I was doing battle with my ‘brother’ here. She would be ashamed. The spell hit me hard, and I felt my life force begin to drain, but only momentarily. As quickly as the drain was felt, it vanished, and I felt a warm glow around.

"Be safe child, for this road is a hard one." The voice of Brell rang in my ear, as an armor began to form on my body. This miracle was incredible. The armor was the normal I had always worn, but it seemed to be different, almost mystical.

Caviat seemed unimpressed though, and continued his offensive. His spells had no effect on me though, but he continued to cast, using what seemed to be an unlimited mana supply. He would cast, I would slash, but neither of us seemed able of finishing the job.

The air around us was filled with mystic powers as Brell and Cazic tried to give their pawns the advantage. Eventually I could not even tell where I was, I could only feel the magic, and my axe. The world around me seemed to be nothing but magic.

I have heard tales that on this day, Earth Elementals rose from the grounds in Freeport, because of gigantic earthquakes. Or that hatred was at an incredible level, that even the best of friends would turn on each other at a moment’s notice.

The powers of hatred and of the Earth were so concentrated wherever we were, I am sure today that we were no longer in Norrath. But still the battle raged. Until, until I saw an opening. Caviat had left his guard down on his left side. With a swing of blinding speed I brought my axe into his cloak.

I stood back in my house, in my farming breaches and old shirt. My axe trembled in my hands. On the floor was a crumpled robe. Caviat was nowhere to be seen.

I needed to get out. The house suddenly felt as if it was collapsing upon me. I ran outside into the Druid’s field, through down my axe, and began to weep.

"Sir, are you ok?" The sweetest voice I had ever heard said to me. I looked up to see what now defines the meaning of beauty for me.

"May Brell take me now, you are the most gorgeous creature I have ever laid eyes upon" I said. Though the second the words were out of my mouth I vomited. She blushed and giggled, she must have thought me to be drunk, and oh I wish I had been. I wish it had all just been a drunken hallucination.

She lead me into a small chamber of the Druid guild, the candles there made her hair sparkle like diamonds. She gave me some liquid, it made me cough, and she gave me a look of disapproval.

"Well, if it’s not liquor, what is it? Have you been feeling ok … umm, what is your name anyway?" She asked. I had the urge to tell her everything about Caviat, and Brell. But in her presence I could barley manage a "Jaston, miss"

She giggled again and began to go to work. She mumbled many times about magics and things she had never seen before, but never said anything to me about it. She never really did any healing, just tried to find out what was wrong.

After an hour of these futile attempts, she let me go free, with a wink. I asked her name before I left, she blushed a bit and told me it was Tarratree. Oh what a name, I thought I was a float for the next week just watching her at work while I was in the fields. I had not looked at my axe since the encounter, and had no urge to pick it up again.

Then one day, it was almost unimaginable, but I was watching Tarratree, and she took a small rusty dagger out of her blouse and looked it over. A Guardian? But how? I needed to ask her about it, for if she was a Guardian, she could help me with Cazic.

I finally got up the nerve and asked her to the Fool’s Gold Tavern in the middle of town. There we drank ourselves silly and regaled each other with stories from our various adventures.

As we were sobering up, the sun was rising over the hills, and I asked her "Do you have a compass?"

She looked at me in astonishment, and brought out her rusty dagger, then for a moment looked as if she would use it against me, until I revealed mine. Then, un-expectantly, she leaned over and kissed me. Wow, my first kiss, it was incredible. Oh how I wished to save that moment forever. But that was impossible, for work was to start in an hour, and I had no rest that night.

Weeks past, and Tarratree and I continued our dates, and under her request, I once again picked up my axe and began practicing. Then one day it all happened. EVERYTHING! Tarratree told me she was ready to see the world, and that I should come with her, to protect her, and to be hers forever. It was a dream come true! I accepted eager to see the world once again, but more so to be with Tarratree forever. But it was a tragic day as well. It was the day Brell decided to revisit me. He shouted at me for hours about Tarratree, that she was un-pure because she did not worship the Underfoot. He told me that if I left with her, he would not be responsible for the consequences, and warned that it would be fortunate if either of us lived for another year.

I did not heed this warning, passing it up as divine jealousy. To this day I wish I had stayed home, but I didn’t. We had made our way to the Northern Desert of Ro, we were on our way to the Oasis of Mar, but a familiar laugh caught my ear, as Tarratree’s. Caviat appeared before the both of us, and before I knew what was happening, I was surrounded in hate. It encompassed me, as I tried to fight it, it just continued to consume me.

"Jaston NOOOO!" Was what brought me back. Tarratree was kneeling weeping over me. I could see the black magics of the Plain of Hate around us. Cazic Thule stood above us, Caviat at his foot.

"Give it up Jaston, you are nothing without Brell, and you chose her over him!" Caviat’s shrill voice cut into me like a knife.

"You will never have me, I will sheath my blade upon myself before I give my self to you!" I screamed. Cazic Thule seemed not to care for what I was saying as he began to cast a spell.

"Jaston, I will love you for eternity. You chose me over your god, forever be safe my love." Tarratree whispered into my ear as she threw herself into the way of Cazic Thule’s magic. It was the most tragic thing I had faced losing her, but at the time I still had a job to do. How can I defeat a God alone? Tarratree, please don’t be gone, please. I began to run, away from them all, but my feet were stuck to the ground by some powerful magic.

"Hmmm, nobody wants you to die, do they Jaston? Your mother had to be plagued before she would tell me where you were. But in the end she had Poppen deliver that message." Caviat hissed. "I only wish I had been there to see her die, to actually find out how well my plague worked."

"Caviat, I swear, as I yet live, you shall never feel another breath in your lungs." I felt a sudden surge of power. My feet sprung forward toward Caviat, catching him completely off guard, as I gave him a fatal blow to the chest. He fell at my feat dead. All was normal suddenly. I was back in the desert, with two corpses at my feet. One of Caviat, the other of Tarratree.

From that moment I swore never to touch a blade again, and hide myself far away from the rest of the world. I began to live in caves on Faydwer, and would only come out to gather food. I wanted not to get close to anyone, for the fear that they might be hurt.

On one such trip, I found a girl that seemed to be in distress. She claimed she was ok, but I knew better. I trailed her for a few miles, she was trying to escape someone. It was obvious. I had no idea whom from, but I knew she would not be all right if they found her.

"I know you are there little one, please, if you wish to protect me, stand by me, and not away" She introduces herself as Rixma. She said that if I was to help her I would need to arm myself. The only weapon I had was my rusty dagger. I pulled it out, and to my surprise she took it from me, and said she had once had a compass. She told him how she had been a Guardian, and I listened, she was ok for a Wood Elf. Damn Treefolk.

She told me about Clan X, and how they had helped her. She said she knew I must have been running from demons to be this far from anywhere. They were understanding, and asked nothing more than she offered them.

From that day forward I joined Clan X. Though I may still have the heart of a Guardian, X has been far greater a help for me. I have been able to talk with them, to see what I needed to see. I have come to many conclusions, and realize now that this is wear I belong, for now.

---present day----

Jaston gets up from his rock "That was the story oh gracious Lord, what will you have me do now?"

"Nothing my son, my TRUE son" The voice rang out, the voice of Cazic Thule.
