Pillar 2:  Recruiting
The degree of sophistication in our guild recruiting system and the amount of effort that we put into getting the best members possible.

Recruiting Team (Lonedor, Alexedia, Takel, Senzii, Dloreh)
 The Recruiting Officer's job description is based on four main tasks:  Recruiting New Applicants, Guiding New Applicants in their Application Process, being the Central Focal Point for Officers and Sponsors to comment on applicants, and making the Final Decision on Guilding new recruits

  • Recruiting New Applicants:  Along with the Recruiting Team, the Recruiting Officers will aid in promoting Clan X and recruiting new members in order to enhance and strengthen the guild.

  • Guide for New Recruits:  Along with the Recruiting Team, the Recruiting Officer will aid the new applicant with questions and guide them along the process of completing the requirements to become a full fledged member of Clan X.  The Recruiting Officer will also keep track of probationary periods and be responsible for keeping tabs on the newly admitted Clan X'ers behavior.
  • Central Focal Person:  The Recruiting Officer will act as the go to person for all inquiries, interview results and sponsor votes concerning all new applicants.  This will allow the Recruiting Officer to keep tabs on where the applicant is in their recruiting process and what the outcome of every step has been.  The Recruiting Officer will be responsible for providing a final report summary of the applicants efforts to the officers for comment before the applicant is guilded or rejected.
  • Final Decision Maker: If for some reason there is some controversy or confusion over an applicant, the Recruiting Officer will have the final say in whether or not the applicant is guilded or not.  The Recruiting Officer can in no way overrule a majority officer vote.  It is up to the Recruiting Officer to give the final OK before any officer shall guild the applicant so that completion of the recruiting process is ensured. 

The Recruiting Liason will be in charge of actively seeking out potential new recruits and interacting and reporting feedback on existing Clan X recruits to aid the recruiting team in their efforts.

Pillar 3:  Quests & Epics
The degree of sophistication in our Epic Quest tracking system and the amount of effort we put into obtaining Epic Quest weapons and other advanced quest items for our members.

 
Quest Leader (Selc, Delissandra, Himeo, Denniss)
 The Quest and Epic team focuses on organizing encounters and information for a variety of quests. They create guides and compile information for members to use when researching steps of their quest/epic. They also ensure that member driven raids are properly organized and advertised via the motd and message board. The team will be available to assist in running raids if a member is unfamiliar with a zone or mob, however participation from the member requesting a raid will be required. Finally, the team is a point of contact for members who need help with a quest/epic step by providing assistance or directing them to a source of information to answer their need.
  • Create walkthroughs for all class epics and rewarding quests for our current guild progression. These guides include information on location of mob spawns, placeholders, faction, maps of a zone, and any general tips to help members plan their quest.
  • The Q&E team will be available to assist in running a raid if a member is unfamiliar with a zone / mob. However we strongly encourage members to run their own raids for the rewarding experience of completing a difficult step. It is important for a member to be well versed in what will be required for a step of their quest/epic when requesting a raid.
  • When a member has a question regarding a step in a quest/epic the Q&E team will direct them to a guide. If none is available, the team will assist in researching the quest and contact the member with an outside source of information (class forums, Allakhazam, web sites).

 

Pillar 4:  Loot
The degree of sophistication in our guild looting rules.

 
 Loot Team: (Chrometalon, Sirelk, Paith)
 The Loot team and its leader are responsible for gathering input regarding loot and defining loot rules or modifications to those rules based on GUILD input. The loot team keeps the rules up to date and are responsible for acting as mediators and consultants on loot issues that arise during raids. The loot team also handles guild MQ'able items and the updating of point totals and loot bids after raids are completed. If after reading the Loot Rules you have any questions please direct them to Chrometalon.

 

Pillar 5:  Family
The amount of effort we put into maintaining a family atmosphere in our guild.

 
 Family Team: (Uphoria, Takel, Heallary, Steinlin, Tossen, Beejay)
 What is a "Family" in the world of Norrath?  A group, united as players in our goals to achieve the most from EverQuest and still be civil, caring, and close knit. To be part of Clan X an individual player is part of a "Family".  We join Clan X not to be part of a small elite group, but to be part of a large elite, motivated, and mostly harmonious family.  The Family Team is responsible for monitoring the overall guild atmosphere in the following areas:
  • Player Development - Education vs. Handholding
    • Education:  Clan X is a work in progress.  Our members need to be brought along throughout the journey of Clan X.  Our guild is more about the journey than the end-game destination.  Our members oftentimes need to be helped, grouped with, taught the ropes, encouraged to lead, and motivated to become the absolute best players and people that they can be. 
      Examples of a Proactive Member who is learning:
      • On the message board and in guild chat ... "I have researched my epic, scheduled my final epic raid for 2 weeks from today which has been added to the calendar, have asked Vortigern to lead the raid and he said yes, and Uphoria has offered to form groups for me.  Uphoria said she would also show me how to form groups quickly and effectively so that I can do it on my own for guild events.  I need about 3 groups of level 52+, and a chanter would be great.  I also made sure that a 55 mage would be there for CoHing.  Can the guild assist me on my final epic kill?"
      • In /guild ...  "I am starting a group in ME, a shaman is on the way, there are 4 spots left available in the group.  Would anyone like to join us?  We plan on getting some good exp for the next 3 hours or so."
    • Handholding:  Clan X is not about driving your toon to success FOR you.  You drive your leveling, your own quests, your own epics, your own progress for getting keys and loot and flags for PoP. Clan X members must have a willingness to learn and the drive to get what they want themselves.  We recognize effort and do not reward laziness.
      Examples of a Non-Proactive Member who is using:
      • In /guild ... "I don't know what I need to do for my epic, but can you lead a raid today for it?  Where is Ixiblat Fer at anyway?"
      • In /guild ...  "I have been looking for a group for over an hour, is anyone listening?  Are you guys ignoring me?  57 rogue LFG!" 
  • Individual Attention  - RL > EQ
    • Are we treating people as individuals behind the keyboard?  Or are have they become a statistic, simply part of a "necessary" class within the guild?  Are their individual needs (as long as they are taking responsibility for their own progress) being heard within the guild?
    • We have to realize we are all HUMAN behind the toon. We are not all young, we are not all old, not all experienced, nor are we all a bunch of idiots.  Are real life situations being given priority to events within Norrath?
  • Perception is Truth
    • How does the guild view itself?  Do we care for one another?  Do we laugh and fight together?  Do we deliver what we promise?  Are the majority of us satisfied with our progress?  Do we deal with conflicts in a positive manner and then get back to enjoying our time as friends and fellow adventurers? Are we a primarily unified force striving along a focused path to victory?  Is there a "ONENESS" to Clan X?
  • Courtesy
    • Are we being courteous to the lowest member of our family, or being brave enough to step up and speak your mind to the highest member of our family?  Are our officers respecting the views of all our members regardless of rank?  As a family it is on ALL of us to make ALL of us feel welcome.
    • Are we taking care of our newer people and making them feel part of the family?  We have shy folks and folks who have barely ever raided, are we coaching them?  
  • Everyone is a Specialist
    • Every person has gifts.  Every person is an asset in one form or another.  Clan X recognizes that all people in the guild have something to contribute towards its success, and are welcoming of the input and feedback from every single person.  In Clan X WE all have a voice - it is a unique way to be - it is the FAMILY way.  The Clan X method of communication is a welcoming environment where each person can contribute in a positive, enthusiastic, and non-abusive manner
  • Accountability
    • If members do not believe in the Clan X Vision or do not take responsibility for their own development, or if they cause strife within the family, they should not be a part of the family. The hardest thing in any guild is kicking folks out, but sometimes it is necessary. Families do it, it is called discipline, and to be a healthy family Clan X must have discipline. People will play how they want, but if how they play interrupts the life of the family as a whole, they need to be warned, warned again, and then removed.

Communications Officer: (Senzii, Crystessia)
The main role of the communications officer is to make sure that the voice of all the guild members are heard. No matter what the issue, the communications officer will make sure that your questions are answered and that your solutions to problems are listed and voiced to the right team.

On the same token, the communications officer is the main voice for the guild members to the other officers. This means that all inquiries and solutions must be brought up with him directly and not any other officer. The communications officer will ensure that the right people are involved in resolving any issue which a guild member may bring to the table.

Here are points on job description and guild member responsibilities in light of this new position. Please read carefully:

  • The Communications Officer will only get involved after a formal suggestions form has been filled and submitted. That form can be found here: Suggestion Box. 
  • No other officer should be contacted directly about official suggestions or complaints. The Communications Officer will ensure that the proper team ( family, recruitment, loot, end game, officer core) will get in touch with you as soon as they possibly can. This is being done to ensure that the right people are on the job to solve any issues.
  • We encourage all of our guild members to voice their opinions, but in cases of problems being brought to light, the job of the Communications Officer is to ensure that solutions are presented before passing the issue along to the appropriate team. If the person submitting the problem cannot find a solution, then the Communications Officer will attempt to work on a solution with the member so that the process will go through.
  • The Communications Officer will help promote ideas brought along by members.
  • The Communications Officer is also in charge of viewing and evaluating all of the forms handed in on the web.
  • Tells about complaints or a push to solve problems right then and there in game are frowned upon unless it is an urgent matter. We encourage the use of the Forum and Personal Messages to communicate with not only the Communications Officer, but to all officers about sensitive topics (such as loot distribution, raiding, recruitment, etc...). It is a proven fact that things said at the heat of the moment might not reflect your true intentions. We encourage that you step out of the game and put your ideas online when it gets to heated discussions.

 

Pillar 6:  Raiding
The degree to which our guild pursues the end-game experience and the complexity of the zones our guild is capable of mastering.

 
End Game Team: (Rixma, Aoyama, Calamarr, Denniss, Ekondo, Gravelor, Nylarra, Selc, Yoddle)
The End Game Leader and his/her team is responsible for the majority of the raiding aspects that concern Clan X. Their team looks for new challenges that the guild is capable of success with at any point in time and takes into consideration the direction in which the members of Clan X wish to pursue. Along with finding new things, the End Game Team is also constantly looking to aid in sharpening our overall raiding skills which can involve the following: assemblage, groupings, gear needed, levels needed, classes needed, zone knowledge, amount of raids, and strategy.

The following are taken into consideration when focusing on our end-game abilities and goals:

  • Leveling:
    • Is leveling a large enough priority in the guild right now?
    • Are guild members actively grouping?
    • Do we have the levels needed to do the things we want?
    • Are the members knowledgeable of the AA's enough to choose the ones that will aid the most?
  • Gear:
    • Are we getting Armor that has high AC/HP/Resits/MANA?
    • Are we getting Weaponry that have good DMG/DLY with stats?
    • Are we getting items with Improved Damage/Mana Conservation Focuses?
    • Do our melee have good haste items that also offer other stats?
    • Are our members completing their spellbooks and are we helping them do so?
  • Strategy:
    • Do we have Solid Raid Leaders?
    • Do we have Knowledgeable/Fast Decision Making Members?
    • Do we know the target Mob/Zone we are raiding?
  • New Challenges:
    • Are we Finding New Encounters that we will have a high success with?
    • Are we finding Encounters that will offer us a high reward loot wise?
    • Are we finding Encounters that pose a fun challenge/atmosphere for the guild?
  • Scheduling:
    • Are we Scheduling Raids when we will have a good turn out?
    • Raids must be Scheduled when we have highest opportunity to do the raid (staying away from weekends and other prime times for certain zones).
    • Are the Raids Scheduled in Moderation?
    • Are we able to offer the same raid at multiple times for those that can't make the original?
  • Direction:
    • Are we keeping everyone busy enough without overload?
    • Do the things we do hold meaning or are we just out for loot?
    • Are we doing the things that members want?
    • Are the officers happy with where we are going?
