Responsibilities of Clan X Officers

An officer should not be one just because he has been in the guild the longest or is of a high level. An officer is one who shows the ability of leadership. (LDRSHIP)

Loyalty: Bear true faith and allegiance to your guild, fellow
              guildmates, and honorable citizens of Norrath.

Duty: Fulfill your obligations.

Respct: Treat people as they should be treated.

Selfless Serive: Put the welfare of your Guild, and your Guildmates
                        before your own.

Honor: Live up to you your own expectations and values

Integrity: Do what's right, legally and morally

Personal Courage: Face Fear, Danger, and Adversity

An officer is there to solve problems and to lead guildmates. They may not be of higher level or status of fellow guildmates, but are there to help and resolve conflicts and bring betterment to the guild. An officer is also a representative of the guild, how they act shows how the guild acts. Their actions and they themselves represent the guild as a whole.  They should have and use the knowledge, judgment, and natural leadership required to be an officer.  Officers must have the courage to take responsibility for not only their own actions, but for the actions of the guild as a whole. This spirit of leading by example breeds honor and respect within Clan X and throughout Norrath.

Officers have more responsibility. Officers are putting their reputation on the line when they guild someone, when they make a decision on a problem in the guild, and pretty much every time they interact with other players.

Officers are required to meet once every two months to review issues pertaining to the guild (this is usually done via phone conference).  The general agenda of these officer meetings is based on the guild vision, the guild timeline, and our respective duties as officers.  Problems and successes are also discussed at these meetings.

An officer must be there during game time to answer questions of Clan X members and to be readily available when there are guild issues to deal with. This DOES NOT mean that it is an officer's responsibility to find you a rez if you die. However, officers are responsible for maintaining order in Norrath if there are conflicts emerging in relation to the guild or its members. Officers must know when and when not to step into any given situation. In addition, officers must be able to discern the difference between people who will improve and be a credit to Clan X and those who will cause negative controversy. They should be compassionate and firm as the situation dictates.

Part of the responsibility of an officer is to be there for the guild and in Norrath available to guild members in need. That means significant PRESENCE IN NORRATH required to be an officer. If real life issues infringe on this, officers should let the clan know so that the load can be carried by others.

A trial period of 3 months within Clan X must be met before a member can become an officer. The purpose of this is to ensure that the recruit fits in with the guild family before they are excepted to perform as an officer within the guild.

Finally, officers should be able to have fun with Clan X and enjoy what the guild has to offer, as well as the people in it! Remember that officers are here to play and have fun as well!

How do members become officers?

Clan X encourages all of its members to pursue becoming an officer if they wish to take on the responsibilities of an officer.  

  1. A member can request to become an officer, or they can be nominated by those in the guild.  Who suggests he should be put into consideration to be an officer - whether guild leader, officers, or members - shouldn't matter.  Requests and nominations should be sent formally in e-mail to the current officers.  
  2. All the officers should get together to discuss the nominations and requests.  The officer-to-be should be considered for that position because they show the traits needed to become an officer and are willing and able to fill those large shoes.
  3. If the officers agree that this person would be a benefit to the guild and gets the majority from the officers, the guild will be notified that this member is being looked at to become an officer - the members name will be posted on the Pending Officer list.  Then the guild will vote on it.  Why?  Since an officer represents the ENTIRE guild, everyone who is being represented by the individual should have a fair say - for this reason, the members of Clan X are responsible for voting in their leaders.   
  4. Those that are not considered ready for the position of officer will receive an Action Plan to enhance their abilities to perform this very important role.  The Action Plan for each member is created to ensure that each officer upholds the goals and integrity of Clan X, and is designed to help that member become the best officer that they can be.
