Plane of Mischief
5/9/01, 11:55 PST | Kwill

Can the Plane of Mischief actually be broken? Or is the idea that it is broken, and therefore fun? You may recall some months ago, a monk was temporarily suspended from play as an exploiter because he was killing npcs with one hitpoint, among other things, in an attempt to get their items and solve the puzzle in this plane. He convinced a guide to come up to the plane with him, and the guide did see that there was a problem with the plane, clearing the monk of any wrongdoing. Soon after, the PoM was revamped, including making many items in the Plane no-drop.

Here are some tidbits for thought as Absor updates players on this most frustrating of Plane, specifically that it seems that when you do solve a quest no-drop items will often be unusable by the class that gets it:

A player asks Absor, Was just wondering, since most items are kind of vague in figuring out what combos make what in PoM. There will be items that, upon first getting, will go to the wrong class. Are you aware of how the quests/items work there and does this seem correct to you?

Absor responded in April:
Yup, we're very aware of how the quests work. I announced those changes the second I heard them so that you would have as much warning as possible.

Since then we've looked into other, more interesting ways of fixing things that we deemed wrong with this area (I'm not going into details, I love the fact that this zone is complicated, and I'm not spoiling things). I'm not sure how, exactly, we're going to make these changes yet. And it might very well include making many of these items No-Drop, as I mentioned. But I think that you'll see that it works out well in the end, and making those items No-Drop shouldn't actually be a problem for those that are playing in the zone. We're adding levels of convolution to the zone.

I can't explain it further, but if the items do become No-Drop, we are aware of the concerns that folks have brought up, and we know that it's nearly impossible to tell what's what up there. We're not going to make it so that those that do the experimentation will get the least reward. As to how that will be accomplished, you'll have to trust us.
In an attempt to pin the whole thing down, another player posed this direct question: "Please, simply announce the following if it's true: The Plane of Mischief is fully itemized; all quests are working; and all mobs are dropping what they should be dropping at the appropriate rates."

Absor answered:
The Plane of Mischief will probably never be 'finished'.

With this zone in particular, we plan to make changes, additions and generally screwy updates whenever the time is available and the urge hits us. This zone is really always going to be in flux. It is the home of the god of goofiness himself. Stability should never be part of his realm.


Finally, a player suggested recently that Verant make the no-drop items tradeable only in the PoM, which would solve a problem of making an item you can't use. Absor had this to say about that:
Sure, it's possible to do that.

But we've come up with another solution. I'm not going to outline it, because, well, it's complicated and you'll have to figure it out on your own. But, basically, there is a way to use the no-drop items to get other items that will get you other stuff that you can keep, or use to get other stuff they suits your class better…

Or something like that.

Basically, the items will be no-drop, but you'll be able to get something that you can use if you do more convoluted things within the zone.
So the Plane of Mishchief remains something of a "practical joke," if you will...but there is a reward at the end if you can just keep your sense of humor and not lose your thinking cap! Good luck to those players who keep working on this most unique zone in the game.

No, You Can't Move
5/9/01, 9:11 PST | Kwill

Absor reminds people about the official EQ policy on moving characters from one server to another:
We regularly receive requests from customers that want their characters moved from one server to another, or from one account to another. Unfortunately, due to the EverQuest EULA, the number of requests that we receive, the desire to maintain stable server communities and economies, and other problems associated with character moves, we can not move characters across servers or accounts.

It is important for the integrity of the server that items from a server remain on that server. We also feel that it is important that characters that have built up a reputation, good or bad, have to live with the consequences of their decisions, and are not allowed to retreat from that reputation by changing servers.
The exception still remains for mass moves to a new server, during the allotted time period for those moves.


Cleric Epic Quest Stands
5/9/01, 9:04 PST | Kwill

After an involved discussion on the Sony Developers Corner about the "bottleneck" with Zordak Ragefire in Nagafen's lair for the cleric epic weapon quest, Absor had this comment:
...Today we had a Tuning meeting that was mostly about this particular issue.

First, we can't be changing epic quests all the time to make them fit the current game. Just like any other content, these quests will get easier as time passes and players get higher level, better items and all that.

The bottleneck here is caused almost exclusively by the massive volume of Clerics and their guilds and friends and friends of friends helping them and urging (or insisting) that the get this item. The only solution to that would be to nerf the item, make it less desirable so that fewer people will be insisting that Clerics have this item. We've decided not to do that.

There are no other changes that we can make to this quest that would not simply be making the quest harder or more tedious. We've learned from past experience that making the quest harder would probably not reduce the bottleneck. Making more bottlenecks earlier in the quest would be considered a nerf, but is the only solution to reducing the bottleneck at Ragefire. While this is a perfectly viable solution, adjusting these quests all the time isn't something we want to do, since the difficulty of these quests will change as players and characters advance (constantly), and we're not planning on playing catchup all the time this way.

So, the answer is, no. We are not going to change the Cleric epic quest. The problem doesn't really lie in the quest (except perhaps that it is too easy), it lies in the desirability of the item and the vast number of people trying to get it (or help others get it).
