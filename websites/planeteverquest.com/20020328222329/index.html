Just A Reminder
3/28/02, 12:52 PST | Stryker

Well guys, I just wanted to give you the all-known warning about viruses in email. Lately, I have been receieving mail with viruses attached. Of course, I havn't opened these emails, and neither should you. Luckily, Gamespy filters incoming email and alerts me when there is a virus attached.

However, your email service might not offer this utility, and therefore you should watch out! If you get an email from someone you don't know, or even someone you know, check the attachment. Use a virus scanner, or just common sense. NEVER download a file with an extension of .exe, .vbs, and many more, too many to list.

A common way people send viruses, is to tell an interesting story in the body of the message, and then tell you to finish the story, open the enclosed attachment. DON'T DO THAT! Most of the time, that attachment is a virus. (I got this example from a friend of mine who got a virus this way. Luckily, I was at his house that day, and wiped the virus from his computer before it got too bad.)

I hope you read this, it can really save your computer some day.

New EQ Server
3/26/02, 17:23 PST | Stryker

There is a new server opening up in Amsterdam soon, named Kane Bayle. It will be the second server located outside of the United States. Here's what The EverQuest Team had to say:

EQ Lore
3/26/02, 11:45 PST | Kwill

Absor tackles the issue of EQ lore and "differing points of view" when a player questions some apparent inconsistencies in the Planes of Power storyline on the Developer's Corner. Here is part of his explanation:
It's important to understand that lore is written by folks with a viewpoint. The Dark Elves don't view history the same way that the High Elves do. Until recently very few Norrathians believed that wild tale that Ogres accepted as truth, that one about them once being like gods in the far distant past. In fact, I can think of a few interesting tid-bits of information that Norrathians take for granted as true that are not as true as they think…

But I can't talk about that.

The EverQuest world is very large, well defined, and has a ton of lore. But when history is written by individuals with agendas there can be a conflict in that lore. Such things are not wrong, or even bad, for a game world. Quite the opposite is true, especially in a shared game space like EverQuest. The idea that Dark Elves and High Elves can argue all day about the truth of the origins of life, or that Gnolls believe themselves to be children of Brell - something that a Dwarf would disagree with using the blade of an axe, all add to the depth of the world. The truth in such matters is dependent on the character speaking.

Has Absor has been doing some reading about realitivism and revisionist history? Perhaps. Bottom line is, Absor says the lore of Norrath has been the foundation of everything they have done, but sometimes they have to fix the lore to make the game play better.

EQ Article on MSNBC
3/26/02, 9:55 PST | Kwill

Just when you thought Ebay'd accounts were a thing of the past, they again make headlines as a good way to make money...as evidenced by MSNBC's article, Big Bucks for a Bunch of Bits.

If people will buy characters, why not just invent a game where they have to buy everything with real dollars? Project Entropia is going to try just that:

...A Swedish company called MindArk is hoping to cash in on the obsession [of buying virtual items].

It is developing Project Entropia, a Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game, or MMORPG, that will require players to plunk down real cash for game items: vases for the virtual coffee table, mining equipment, weapons.

Jan Welter, MindArk’s general manager, said that just like in the real world, the cost of items will depend on their rarity and durability. A simple weapon may cost $5, but a one-of-a-kind magical object could fetch thousands.

The company hopes to create a three-dimensional sci-fi world that lures hardcore gamers and neophytes interested in a second, electronic life.

Well, it could work...but although people do buy and sell the time it takes to create a mid-level to upper level character, I don't know if the concept of buying everything would go over well with the gaming community.

Meanwhile, I hope we don't have to remind readers that buying an account from someone in EverQuest means you have no recourse when something goes wrong, as SOE will not deal with any account that does not belong to the original owner--and if it comes to their attention that your account has been given to someone else, you can be banned.

Magician Focus Items Nerfed
3/22/02, 6:12 PST | Stryker

All you magician's out there should've noticed, the focus items have been nerfed. It was unintentionally left out of the patch, and the EQ team recognized this. They wrote a whole letter on the Developer's Corner last night. I will post a few exerpts from it, since it is such a long message.

With all the excitement and concern that focus items have brought up lately, we wanted to take a minute and talk about them. For those of you unfamiliar with them - focus items are armor, items, or weapons that enhance an aspect of spell casting for the character that wears them. Focus items can have a wide variety of effects, including things like increased damage or healing, lowered cast times, decreased reagent use, increased spell range, and decreased resist rates.

...

We understand that adjusting things after they have come into the game causes people some concern. As a rule, we try to not make changes to existing items when possible and to recognize the impact this may have on players when we do make changes. We will always try to notify our customers of changes of this type when we can, unless we want to keep something a mystery. Discovery is an important part of the game. This change, however, wasn't something we were trying to keep hidden, and it wasn't something we could have kept hidden had we tried to. We apologize for failing to mention these changes in the patch message.

...

Here are the changes that we introduced in last night's patch:

- Jedah's Choker items - This item gives a chance of not using a reagent when you cast a spell that normally would use a reagent. The items were modified so they no longer effect Enchant Metal spells, or Imbue Gem spells. The goal of the item was to save reagents used for summoning pets, casting buffs, or using Translocate fragments, not to give people a chance at a free platinum bar every time they enchanted one.

- Naki's Ring items - This item decreases spell cast time when worn. Initially, it worked on any spell. As of last night's patch, it works on spells between 4 and 9.5 seconds casting time. We are currently working on a feature to allow us to exclude certain spells from working with a focus item, and when we get that in we will alter this item to not have a max casting time limit for spells it works with.

This item was altered because the decrease in casting time for Complete Heal proved to be too powerful, and was potentially unbalancing to the game. Complete heal is already an incredibly powerful tool for the Cleric, and was not in need of improvement. The 4 second minimum time was added to prevent some very fast casting spells from becoming even faster. We'll continue to evaluate fast casting spells and may improve this focus item in the future if it's needed.

- Gallenite's Bracelet items - This item increases the amount of damage done by direct damage spells. This item was changed to not give additional damage on Wizard's Bane spells. Bane spells are already very efficient and are not in need of further improvement.
