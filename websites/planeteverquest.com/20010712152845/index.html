Patch Message
7/10/01, 9:13 PST | Aracnakat

** Bug Fixes **

- The "NPC unerringly sticks on pet" AI bug implemented in the last patch has been fixed. This resolves a bug that occurred a short while ago that would cause pets to be the focus for most attacks by NPCs.

- Fixed the animation problem where an animation played on the upper body would get stuck. This could be seen by starting to cast a spell and then walking forward while it was playing the casting animation.

- Fixed a bug that wouldn't allow a player to target an invisible PC or NPC with the F7 or F8 keys even if they had See Invisible up.

- Stacking issues with 'change form' type spells have been corrected. Lich and Clarity will no longer stack, nor will Spirit of Wolf and Wolf Form.

- The bug with screenshots that some people experienced when using 32-bit color mode should be fixed.
** Item and Vendor Adjustments **

- Changed the forged Antonian Javelins to be stackable.

- Gnome Skin Sleeves are now droppable and rentable.

- Spiderling and Spider Silks are now stackable

- Spider Legs are now stackable.

- Tarnished weapons worth slightly more to the vendors than the rusty ones.

- Reduced the weight of the monk only Black Pantherskin armor.

- All Sheets of metal and Folded Sheets of metal are now stackable.

- Rangers will now be able to wear Girdle of Reflection

- There is now a vendor in Felwithe that sells the Enchant Ores series of enchanter spells.
** Zone and Quest Changes **

- The Crazed Ghouls should be less of a problem in Unrest.

- The Monk red headband quest should now be available again.

- We've added a few more Yew Leaf spawns.

- Reevaluated the values of the various fish fillets. All fish vendors should return to normal, but they won't be paying as well for certain fishy items. This resolves a problem that was created with the increased value of player made food items all over Norrath. Some of food items were made too valuable, and merchants were complaining to their union leaders...

Character Transfer Service Announced
7/6/01, 7:50 PST | Kwill

Verant has introduced the opportunity to transfer characters from accounts and from server to server:
Due to the many requests to transfer characters from account to account, or server to server, we have implemented a value added service and have enacted a new policy to meet these requests. If you desire to transfer a character or characters from your primary account to your secondary account, or wish to transfer to a different server during non server split opportunities, the following service is offered. This is a value added service which requires a fee that can be billed to your credit card.

This service to move your character(s) will be offered temporarily. This service may end at any time without prior notification.
One stipulation of the transfer opportunity is worth noting:
When moving characters between accounts, billing information must be identical on both accounts and you must be the original owner of both accounts. ...attempting to transfer characters from another person's account or to or from an account purchased by or for you or given to you where you are not the original registered user of that account, the characters will not be moved, your request will not be processed or approved and your account(s) will be subject to review and appropriate disciplinary action taken.
The basic fee to move one character is $50. Moved characters are stripped of all equipment and cash. To read all the conditions of this offer and to find the request form, click here to go to the Sony site.

Cleric Gladiator Battle
7/5/01, 8:13 PST | Kwill

Is your favorite cleric really as good as you think they are? Find out when they are assigned a GM Gladiator to fight for them in this unique Best of the Best Competition! The contest is for clerics level 54 and up. Here are the rules:
Each cleric will have a Gladiator assigned to them as a teammate. The object for the Cleric is to kill the opposing Cleric or the opposing Cleric's Gladiator. The Clerics may engage the opposing Gladiator with spells, but may not melee the Gladiator at any time. The Cleric may engage the opposing Cleric with spells or melee anytime after the fight command is issued.

Each Gladiator will have identical equipment, stats and skills, and will be played by a representative of the Events Staff.

Immediately before the buffing session the Gladiators will stand in the center of the combat area. They will use auto attack when the fight command is issued by the Events Staff and continue to do so until dispatched by the opposing Gladiator or Cleric. The Gladiator will not move, unless spells cast on them or combat forces them out of combat range. If the Gladiator is forced out of combat range they will immediately move forward to return to combat range.

The following spells may not be cast on the opposing cleric's Gladiator at any time: any form of Fear, Blind, Death Pact, Divine Intervention or Unswerving Hammer of Faith.

There is no entry fee for this event and the only prizes will be titles awarded to the winners. Title for winning at the server level will be the level 51 title for Cleric "the Vicar". The title for the 3 runners up on Test Server Finals will be the level 56 title "the Templar". The grand champion of the Test Server Finals shall have the level 60 title given to him on his own server "the High Priest".
The dates are July 9th through July 26th on Live Servers, times will be posted at least 48 hours in advance. The final is on the Test Server July 28 at Noon. All possible servers will be represented.

Check out all the rules at the Events board on Sony's Harpys Head Tavern.

No New Armor Color
7/5/01, 7:59 PST | Kwill

Some players have been writing to Absor on the Sony Boards and asking for changes in tint or color to armor so it looks as good as the stats say it is. Absor is very clear on this:
We're not going to revamp every piece of armor in the game to meet the desires of a few people just so we can upset other people that liked the way they looked already. In a few rare cases we've seen some changes that seem to make sense. But we are not going to go back and add this graphic to that sword or that color to your favorite piece of armor…

We certainly understand the desire to have armor that looks the way you want it to look. If you want a different look, there are plenty of other armors or items to use that look different. And no matter what we change to please one person or group, someone else isn't going to like the change. It's not worth our time to annoy one group to please another.
So there you have it--if you have really l337 armor that looks like it came off a level 30 mob, you just have to bask in your secret uberness...or get different armor if you want to look as good as you feel.
