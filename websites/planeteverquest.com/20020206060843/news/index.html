Changes to the EverQuest patcher
2/1/02, 14:42 PST | Stryker

Straight from Absor:

On Monday the 4th we'll be making a change to the way our Live and Test patching works.
A little while ago we combined our Test patcher with our Live patcher. The intent was to remove the confusion that having two patchers can cause and to encourage more people to play on Test.

Well, we're going back to the old way. We are going to split the Test patcher and the Live patcher back into two separate programs.

This means that in order to access the Test Server you will need to run TestEverQuest.exe, which you will find in your EverQuest directory. To play on the Live Servers just start EverQuest as you always have.

There are a few advantages to separating the two patchers. Splitting them up makes it a little easier, as a player, to be sure that you are headed to the right server when you double click the icon. It's also a little more intuitive for those that want to run the game in two directories.

Using two directories for EverQuest does use up a lot of hard drive space. However, for those with enough space that play regularly on both Test and on Live servers, this is a safer and faster way to work. Using the same directory means that each time you change servers (Live to Test or Test to Live) you will have to patch. The data on the Test Server is very rarely the same as that on Live servers.

And for us internally it makes it easier to test new patchers. It gives us one more step for testing. Now we can push a patcher to the Test Server and try it out there after internal testing and before it goes to the Live servers.

In any case, we've decided to move back to the old way. Starting on Monday, February 4th, 2002 you will need to run TestEverQuest.exe to patch and play on the Test server, and Everquest.exe to patch and play on the Live servers.

* Patch Message Addendum *

The command line argument "/test" should no longer be used to play on the Test Server. Anyone using this should remove this argument from their shortcut. Please manually select "TestServer" from the Advanced Options window to use the Test Server. On Monday, February 04, TestEverQuest.exe will once again be used to patch and play on the Test Server.

- The EverQuest Team


EverQuest Legends: Something New
2/1/02, 14:39 PST | Kwill

From the Sony team, a new way to experience EQ...at a price. A new server, with new features, is being launched next week. Sign up is Monday, February 4, and is limited to 8,000 people. The cost is $39.95 a month, which includes your regular EQ subscription. From the website:
Make your mark on EverQuest! Sony Online Entertainment is proud to offer a unique and rewarding opportunity for EverQuest players: the EverQuest Legends Service. Are you ready for a new, more dynamic EverQuest experience? Are you prepared to take on exciting new challenges, discover unknown treasures, become a hero of Legendary proportions? To find out more, take our guided tour of all the new features we're preparing for EverQuest Legends, then reserve your spot today!

Personal Character Pages
Part of the Legends web site, each player character will have a personal homepage featuring all the character's statistics and items. This character page will be updated daily by the EverQuest Legends server, so you have more time to compare and share your accomplishments with your friends and even the entire EverQuest Community. Also included is a personal journal in which you'll be able to record achievements and victories in the world of Norrath, open quests, accomplishments, future wants, current friends and enemies, and share your adventures with others. You control what is and isn't noted here, so be creative.

Dynamic Server
Everquest Legends is an exclusive server, with a limited population of players able to participate in the new, more dynamic world. Our dedicated team of Customer Service & Development staff will not only be providing in-game support to our Legends players, but also running new quests and adventures for players of all levels to participate in. ...In addition, be the first to take part in special Historical Quests, where you'll be able to interact with key personalities in the world of Norrath and participate in truly momentous events.

Legendary Items
In EverQuest Legends, truly unique and awesome new equipment will be introduced prior to their release on our other servers. ... Some of these Legendary items will even be named after the first player who discovers them.

Calendar Events
A web-based calendar will be kept up to date by the Legends Team, and will let you know who, when, where, and what is going on server-wide. You'll be able to organize and list your own events for other Legends players to see, or search through the listings to find other exciting, player-run events to participate in.

Interactive Maps
New dynamic maps help chart the travels of your character with step by step tracking of your progress through the world of Norrath and beyond. Quickly find which zone your character is bound in, and which zones you have or haven't visited. As these maps progress, more detail and dynamic features will be added to assist your exploration of Norrath.

Tales of Adventure
Ever wanted to take a stab at chronicling your adventure? The Tales of Adventure will feature the best stories submitted by Legends players, prominently displayed for the whole world to marvel in your glorious deeds! A lasting legacy and homage to your characters' adventures, we'll be posting everything from the short story to the grand epic in the Tales of Adventure.

Guild Halls
Membership to the Legends Service will provide you a one stop website for all of your Guild needs. This new service includes a guild calendar, message board, member roster, and pages to post guild news, the guild history, and more! Everything you need to manage your Guild and its activities. Setting up that next Plane raid? Or perhaps just organizing a Guild wedding, the Guild Hall will give you the flexibility and tools that you deserve.

Rules and Regulations
Once the new Legends server is live, the EverQuest Legends service will be available to you from the moment you complete the sign-up process! You may immediately log in, create a new character and begin a journey to forge your own Legend! You will also have access to the many features from the new EverQuest Legends website!

This first open signup for the Legends Service is capped at 8,000 subscribers. Given the current size of the EverQuest game world and our experience with subscriber to online player ratios, we feel that this per server number will lend itself to a healthy community, with an average online player count in the 2,300-2,400 range. Our target is a game environment that neither suffers from an empty, under-crowded feel where grouping is difficult, nor a cramped, overrun setting. Should our best guesswork not hold true, we have hardware standing by to ensure a great gaming experience.

As a value-added service to the EverQuest Legends service, you may choose to move up to eight (8) of your existing characters on a single account, with their items, from any existing server to an EverQuest Legends server. (Customers who wish to move characters from more than one existing account may accomplish this by using the character move service to consolidate their characters to one account and then upgrade that account, or by upgrading each account individually.)

See all the details, rules and regulations on the official EQ Legends website.

More on Ring of Fire
1/31/02, 12:23 PST | Kwill

Steve Burke, lead designer, wrote more about the Ring of Fire encounter in the Acrylia Caverns, after players wrote in to the Developer's Corner about its new level of difficulty:
The level of the mobs at this stage does make them very difficult to manage. This was intentional. I have personally participated as a player in the event both before and after the bug fix. It is still very possible for a good force of players to get to the fifth boss of the encounter, and as players become more powerful through alternate advancement abilities and equipment they may indeed make it farther.

As for smaller raid forces, there are some rewards for making it to the fourth boss that veteran players would be happy with.

The encounter is now as difficult as it was designed to be after the fourth boss. The treasure available past that point is commensurate with the difficulty, and the ability for players to obtain it very frequently makes it necessary to require a solid force of players.

If time permits, I may place a few waves in between these stages to make the progression more granular. This would have the side effect of taking longer to get to the toughest encounters.

As always, I am open to ideas. Feel free to email me any well thought out suggestions.

Thanks!

Steve Burke
Lead Designer, EverQuest Content
Sony Online Entertainment, Inc
sburke@soe.sony.com

Absor adds, "And since Steve is brave enough to offer up his email address, I urge you to please keep your comments concise and clear. And if you're not sure that your comment is something for Steve specifically, please send it to the Dev Corner and I'll see that the right person gets it. Steve has plenty of stuff to do as it is."


Are We Ungrateful Morons?
1/31/02, 11:25 PST | Kwill

Seems that Christopher Buecheler is of the opinion that EverQuest players are "spoiled, ungrateful morons," and explains why in his article on Gamespy, "Game Players Are Spoiled."

He tried EQ way back in 1999, and dismissed it as "nothing more than a glorified IRC channel with ugly textures and misshapen models." Despite this, he read my article on the magician comments to Absor ("Absor Addresses Magician Concerns," January 30), and latched on to the complaint that Call of the Hero spell does not work in all zones. Doesn't matter that he has no idea what Call of the Hero is or how it's used, he's apparently just disgusted that players would complain about it, because Gordon (Abashi) said he didn't want people skipping content. That's reason enough to call players whiners in Mr. Buecheler's book.

Now those that play high level mages, or play with them in guild, know that mages have gotten the short end of the stick for a while now, in terms of spells and usefulness. Call of the Hero became a lot like clarity, or sow, or ports--it actually gave the mage a role in a group that was important. Skipping content just means that players don't have to fight their way through a zone to join a group at the other side--and probably they are going to the other side to fight something. I think EQ players over level 50 understand the mage complaints and they probably understand why Gordon said what he did. It's a point of contention that CoH doesn't work in every zone, and therefore its harder in those zones to get people moved around.

Now Mr. Buecheler doesn't play, so he doesn't know all of this background, but he feels that he can use this rather technical example to demonstrate why EQ players are "retarded chimpanzees," and asserts "just because something is possible, does not mean you have the right, or the reason, to do it." Just because you read something, doesn't mean you have the right or the reason to write about it, sir.

There is a difference between being a "resident cynic," as Mr. Buecheler likes to style himself, and writing out of sheer ignorance. It's much more difficult to be a cynic and actually criticize games or gamers when you understand a game, than to take the easy way out and criticize something you don't understand to look clever.

Cleric Epic Update
1/30/02, 9:30 PST | Kwill

The cleric epic quest is supposed to work like this:
First, the 'merchant' version of Zordak will now appear at a different location in SoldungB. He will appear once each day (except after a server comes down, then he will spawn in one to three days). The appearance of this NPC will no longer require the absence of Lord Nagafen.

The cleric still turns in his Shimmering Pearl to Zordak. But now the merchant Zordak will return a Swirling Pearl to the Cleric and disappear.

At that point the Cleric will need to head out and look for him. A killable version of Zordak will appear independent of the pearl turn-in every few days (except after a server goes down, then he will appear a little faster). Killing Zordak will transform him into dragon form, and the Cleric (with help…) must kill him immediately.

Absor confirms:
The second Zordak is indeed broken. We'll have him fixed as soon as we can.
This may be fixed now, there was a patch last night that brought down Skyfire zones...

For more discussion on the technical details of the cleric epic quest, Absor answers a number of questions on this thread on the Developer's Corner.


Ring of Fire in Acrylia Caverns Changed
1/30/02, 9:28 PST | Kwill

From Steve Burke, Lead Designer, as posted on the Developer's Corner:
I was cruising the boards and read some responses to the recent changes to one of the events in Acrylia, thought I'd take a minute and dispel a rumor. I hope the author of this post doesn't mind me using it.

Did RoF last night and man did they nerf it! From 4th boss till 5th boss the skele's that pop all do AE complete heal. We had about 40 peeps there and got owned twice. Never even made it to the 5th boss.

After the fourth boss the mobs that spawned were level 2, this was a result of a bug, and fixing it brought the mobs up to the appropriate level. There is a chance that a healer will spawn, but there are none that can cast AE heals.

I apologize for the bug having slipped past us.
Thanks for playing,

Steve Burke
Lead Designer, EverQuest Content
