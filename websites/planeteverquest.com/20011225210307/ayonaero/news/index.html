An EverQuest Christmas Poem
12/24/01, 13:49 PST | Kwill

Christmas in Norrath!
(Camping Santa)
by Hedronius, posted to the Sony boards

Christmas in Norrath would differ by far,
Than singing of Wisemen that follow a star.
When Santa arrived his whip making a crack,
Some ranger would shout, "I've got him on track!"

Then quickly shouts would cross the land,
Let every guild come lend a hand!
We'll mez him, stun, ensnare and dot:
Unleash wizards with fires hot!

The battle with Santa should be a breeze,
Wait! What was that... An AOE Sneeze?
But still it should be over quick.
Then the reindeer began to kick!

DASHER! DANCER! PRANCER and VIXEN!
COMET! CUPID! DONDER and BLITZEN!
They took out the first line of tanks with their hate,
Who knew they could quad for 648!

Despite heavy losses the battle did turn,
One by one Santa's reindeer did fall down and burn.
Then we circled round Santa content with ourselves,
But those sounds from behind us?
Egads! Swarms of elves!

Each carried a cane that cast peppermint shards,
And we found that had muted most all of the bards!
Pets were dispatched to deal with the elves.
Those that could lifetap restored themselves.

We finally focused on saint Nick.
Clerics!
Complete Heals!
Cast them quick!

The battle raged
This would be a tale!
Santa Death Touched a GM!
We all went pale...

A battle so grand it took all we could muster,
and finally Santa lost all his luster.
He stumbled and turned then fell into a heap,
Then all gathered round to see what loot we would reap!

One knelt down slowly and we saw him glower.
No weapons, no armor, no items of power,
Just a scroll wrapped in ribbon?
We were all feeling sour.

So we untied the ribbon and unrolled the scroll.
We read it aloud so that all there could hear.
"HAPPY CHRISTMAS TO ALL, AND TO ALL A GOOD-NIGHT."
And we thought killing Santa was not really bright!

Patch Message Dec 20
12/20/01, 9:22 PST | Kwill

* Bug Fixes *

- Fixed a bug with sleeves not showing properly when wearing robes

* Item Changes *

- Aqualung charges should now work properly

* Trade Skill Changes *

- Regular, medium quality, high quality, mithril, brellium and adamantite rings, as well as their enchanted versions are now stackable.
- Added a few trade skill vendors to Ak'Anon
- Added ore, clay, pottery sketch, baking, and brewing vendors to Felwithe to round out the tradeskill supplies available in Felwithe and Kelethin.
- Crafted fishing flies, special fishing poles and special baits now add a fishing skill bonus.
- Made all Bait and flies equipable in the Ammo slot.
- Added clay, ore and brewing supply vendors to Halas.
- Added several trade skill vendors to Oggok.

* Localization Information

Localization on the EverQuest login/chat server has been fixed so that users can once again select French and German. Japanese and Korean have also been added.

To obtain the text files which enable a language, go to the Advanced Options screen in the patcher and click on the appropriate language box before clicking on "Download".

* Soulbinders *

A new group has appeared in Norrath (and on Luclin as well). Rumor has it that these 'Soulbinders' have dedicated themselves to research on the power and essence of our inner being. Some call them fanatics, as they claim that knowledge of the spirit that dwells within will lead them to an understanding of the whole of existence. Citizens often treat them with the same respect and distance that they treat the Priests of Discord. However, these Soulbinders have dedicated themselves to public service, and for them that service falls along their lines of specialty...

The next time you find yourself far away from home, you might seek the services of a Soulbinder.

/ooc: We've added these "Soulbinder" NPCs to all cities that player characters can start in, as well as Shadowhaven and Iceclad at the request of players. It is our hope that these new NPCs will help players by reducing the time it takes to find a bind from another player character.

Luclin Update
12/20/01, 8:01 PST | Kwill

Absor reports on what has occurred so far in this fourth update:

I wanted to pop in with another update to cover a few issues that I'm seeing posted around the net.

Some people ask why it is that our patch messages include a lot of content changes, and suggest that our time would be better spent fixing bugs than changing or adding content at this point.

It is important to understand that the people working on content are not the same people that work on programming. We are not taking any of our resources away from fixing programming bugs to work on content.

I also wanted to reassure you that we are working with NVIDIA and ATI to resolve the issues that are affecting a few of our customers that are using those cards. We don’t have any firm news for you on this right now, but we are working on it.

Also note that I've reversed the order of the list. The list of fixed items is getting long, and really it's the list of things we are still working on that seems the most urgent. So I've moved the fixed issues to the bottom.

---

We're still working on some issues and we've fixed others. Keep in mind that we have a bunch of people working on things, so most of these items are on the top of someone's list.

While some of these issues only affect a tiny portion of our customers, we want to give a full and complete rundown of issues we are aware of. We will be keeping you updated as things change. Also, check the patch messages for more information.

Stuff in the works, some of these will be fixed with the next patch (in no particular order, most are priority one for the person working on them):

  • Reducing loading and zoning times
  • Crashes occurring for some people when logging in
  • Some characters being 'stuck' if game crashed while playing that character
  • Choppy, buggy, faint or flat sounds for some sound configurations.
  • Some systems have mouse pointers which are being lost or duplicated
  • Characters changing to wolf form look tiny
  • Some spells (mostly 'bolt' spells) not displaying effects
  • Faces that sometimes don't remain as selected
  • New Bard Area of Effect songs are acting strangely
  • New Necromancer group debuff spells are not working properly
  • There is a very short pause for some players when other players enter a zone
  • A few players are being disconnected at random
  • Slow framerate on some fast computers*

    • *Every bug has its story. This one begins as an incompatibility between EverQuest, AMD processors and Umbra (a technology that, ironically, speeds up framerates). There is a known problem in the Umbra engine that was causing the crashes that some AMD users were originally seeing in Shadowhaven. In our desire to make sure that both Intel and AMD users could enter the game, we introduced another condition that unfortunately slowed down framerates in certain circumstances. We have one person working on nothing but this at the moment and he's made significant progress. We expect this problem to go away soon.

    For the list of fixed items, please refer to Absor's post at the Developer's Corner.

    Tech Tip
    Pauses with Win 98/ME ONLY: A possible solution for those with a fair amount of RAM (technically, anyone with 128 megs or more…) who are seeing pauses while the hard drive runs a bit, might benefit from this simple change (it's also easy to undo). It might also improve load and zone times.

    Add the following line to the [386Enh] section of the SYSTEM.INI file: ConservativeSwapfileUsage=1

    For best results, change only this setting (don't change a bunch of other stuff at the same time) and see how it performs. And if it isn't better or seems worse you can just erase that line from your System.ini file. I should tell you that this change might make EverQuest better and it might also cost you some performance with your system overall, though this does not seem to be the case for most people. Keep that in mind if you decide to use this tip.

    Thank you all for your patience. We are working hard to get these issues straightened out as soon as possible. --Absor
