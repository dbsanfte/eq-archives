    Verant's New Stolen Account Policy
    6/11/01, 8:24 PST | Kwill

    As you can see, Verant has changed their policy toward compromised accounts. Please be sure to read item number two before you report that your account has been stolen or compromised!
    As the population of Norrath increases, so too do issues regarding stolen or compromised accounts, and/or accounts that have been transferred from one player to another in violation of the User Agreement. These issues - and the time it takes to investigate and attempt to resolve them - hugely detract from the time that SOE's customer service staff have to devote to providing quality customer service to issues that impact upon the gameplay experience. Accordingly, SOE must remind all players that:

    (1) Account security -- whether it be password protection, running virus checks, disabling file sharing, or any other element of making sure accounts are not stolen or compromised -- is the sole responsibility of the account owner.

    (2)Any and all stolen and/or compromised accounts that are reported to SOE will be banned, and no items will be reimbursed. Any player found to be involved in an account theft will have any and all of his/her accounts banned.

    - The EverQuest Customer Service Team

    Conquest Disbanded for Exploits
    6/7/01, 7:11 PST | Kwill

    Earlier this week, the guild Conquest on the Lanys T'Vyl server was involved in a high level dragon encounter in Sleepers Tomb, and was accused by the GM of using exploits during their encounters with the warders. As a result, 28 of their members were either banned or suspended by Verant, and the guild disbanded.

    Conquest explains it this way on their website:
    We were fighting the 4th warder (Ventani) and had it down to 60% or so when Kerafyrm [the Sleeper] spawned, and wiped us out. Kerafyrm then killed the 4th warder. [this triggered the script that began a series of events that would cause massive destruction in the world of Norrath, affecting dragons and other monsters all over the world; Verant shut the script down before it could proceed any further].

    Roughly 15 minutes laster, GM Valdelmar comes in and had this to say: "Everyone that is in this zone, has a corpse in this zone, or was involved in the invasion of this zone is receiving a warning for exploitation," giving the reason that "We witnessed your clerics below the bridge keeping the warders agro by complete heal so that the tanks could safely kill. That's exploitation."
    Verant believes, and presents evidence in a letter from Braid McQuaid posted on Casters Realm that the guild indeed knew what they were doing in this high level dragon encounter was an exploit of world geometry and other aspects of the game engine. Verant quotes logs from their guild chat that seem to show they were bragging about this exploit, such as "we're useing [sic] massive amounts of exploits and u send a gm down here?"

    Conquest had this reaction on their website:
    These lines are totally taken out of context. They were NEVER in reference to the exploit you are describing.... These guildchat lines are in reference to ...the SAME tactic EVERY guild who killed yelinak used... The statements were made IN JEST, because useing this border-line exploit is about as illegal as jaywalking. Verant watches people use these tactics EVERY DAY and doesnt even care - not to mention this isnt what we have been charged with doing. And to top it all off, we never even came CLOSE to killing the monster. The mob was down to 65% at most when we lost (yes we LOST the fight badly - VI decided it was wise to spawn kerafyrm anyways to kill the last few of us off - thus triggering their entire script).
    Verant stands firm on their policy that "You will not exploit any bug in EverQuest and you will not communicate the existence of any such exploitable bug (bugs that grant the user unnatural or unintended benefits in game), either directly or through public posting, to any other user of EverQuest. You will promptly report any such bug via the in-game "/bug" command or via the eqtesting@verant.com email address."

    Verant further goes on to say:
    SOE regrets having to take these measures, but SOE is committed to protecting the health and viability of the game and of the EverQuest community. Wed also like to correct a few related misconceptions regarding this event: (i) Conquest did not complete the encounter SOE staff inadvertently spawned the Sleeper during its efforts to stop the cheating, (ii) the human figures in some screenshots are a result of the screenshot taker not patching, and (iii) the zone is complete and working -- once SOE has patched fixes to these exploits we encourage players to wake the Sleeper.
    The guild of course does not agree with Verant's actions, and asserts that Verant should not have spawned Kerafyrm in an attempt to stop their gameplay. Verant believes the tactics Conquest used resulted in an "unnatural or unintended benefit" during their encounter. Conquest believes that the tactics they were using were the most efficient to bring the dragon down.

    The line between exploitation and coming up with new tactics for high level encounters seems to be a thin one. The high level mobs take very creative thinking, not just huge amounts of player force. Readers might recall an article I wrote some months ago concerning the Plane of Mischief, where a player was warned for using exploits in that plane in the course of trying to solve the puzzle there. Verant has the final say on what is an exploit and what is a fair encounter with what seems to be an unbeatable foe. It is their judgement in the end that determines whether players will stay in good standing or not.

    Are there two sides to this story? Undoubtedly. Will the GMs tell you when they feel you are using an exploit before they come down with the wrath of the gods and take extreme measures? Probably not, if it is an epic encounter in a new zone. It is wise to remember that Verant is logging your tells, your guild chat, and your playing. As with any disagreement, the just result of a conflict is often to make both parties unhappy.

    Lets hear what you have to say about this in our Discussion Forum.

    ForumPlanet is an Internet community for the millions of game fans that visit the GameSpy Network each day. ForumPlanet's highly interactive model of user-generated discussion and community focuses around every game-related interest under the sun -- game reviews and previews, new games on the horizon, game mods and user-created content, and much more.     Patch Info
    6/5/01, 8:01 PST | Kwill

    This note about the patch was on the Developer's Corner:
    When we patch tomorrow (6-5-01), we will be introducing code on Rallos Zek server that will result in faction hits similar to those on the team servers.

    Killing a character in any of their races home town will cause you to take a faction hit. This counts for all home cities. So, if you kill a Human in any of the city zones of Qeynos (or Freeport, or Surefall Glade), you will suffer a faction hit, even if that Human was born in Freeport. All of Greater Faydark is the starting city for Wood Elves. There will also be faction hits for killing High Elves and Half-Elves in Greater Faydark. Killing an Erudite in either Paineel or Erudin will cause a faction hit.

    Also, for those of you trying to start or complete quests for King Tormax, you need to know that we've made a change. You will now need to be in good standing with him in order to do his quests. In game terms, this means you will need to be at least amiable.
