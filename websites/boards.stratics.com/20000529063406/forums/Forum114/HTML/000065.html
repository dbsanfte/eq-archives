<-----On the left frame scroll down to the scribe profession, it should answer a few of your questions.

As far as getting blanks, yes, you purchase them from the npc vendors.

To get the cost of scribing, add up what you paid for the blank scroll and the reagents necessary for a particular spell. To inscribe heal you would need a scroll, garlic, ginseng, and spidersilk. Since the scroll normally runs 6gp (have seen them for 5gp) and each of the reagents are 3 gp each, the material cost is 15 gp for one scroll. Time spent is another factor. If you read the article "The Scribe" on the left it will answer this question as well as the others including copying books. Hope this helps.

I cant emphasise this enough
TRIAL and ERROR

try scribing the 1st level scrolls. do this 1-5 times. 1 of 3 things can happen

1. You fail 1 or more times. You have not yet mastered this circle, more pratcie and skil gain is required
2. You succede 4-5 times WITHOUT skill gain, it seems you MAY have mastered this circle, try the next one up and go thru the above formula
3. You sucede at least once with skill gain. You still have to work on this circle as you hae not got the best results from it... Keep working through the formula until you can advance to the next circle

The old UO adage of working on your hardest item to gain skill is crap. You should always work on 1 item(in this case CIRCLE) above your skill level. Like Smithing, try Ring 1st, then chain and then plate working like Circle levels too... This way you expend the LEAST amount of regs or reosurces and gain the maximum skill you can

Quick point
You can inscribe POISON and 2nd circle pretty well, you still get good skill gain but not as many failiures. Jumping ahead of yourself and working on recalls COULD work out to be a good next step, and better money... DONT
even if you gain 1 out of every 5 as a SUCCESS and get 0.2 skill from it, think on
if you are getting skill from a MANAGEABLE item, dont move on... This is GOOD skill @ low cost to you, jumping ahead is only going to cost you more resources and less skill gain

Sure, flying is a GREAT thing, and that RUNNING lark sounds like something you could get into, but since you have only managed to get the hang of walking about, do you think you should try to RUN or FLY before you CAN

The long and the short of this rant is simple. Work on LOW level skillgain until you gain no more, then take 1 step further... trust me it is better and quicker in the long run(and cheaper)
