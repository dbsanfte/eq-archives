Revised Version:

In UO2 there needs to be a powerful and different race that takes an extreme amount of time to master. This race would be a dragon race. There would be 5 stages of development, hatchling, welp, sprat, drake, and dragon.

A player would start as a hatchling. The hatchling could only use its claws or bite. It could not even fly. A starting player of any other race could kill a starting hatchling with little effort. The job of a hatchling is to learn how to fight with its claws and teeth. Once the hatchling has mastered (say around 80 skill) fighting he would become a welp.

At the stage of welp the player would learn to breathe fire. This would be a very hard skill to learn since it requires perfection. After that skill is perfected the player would become a sprat.

At the status of sprat the player would being to learn the art of flight. Since the player was still too weak to fly it could only glide. Gliding would need to be worked on before the player would learn to fly. Once the skill is mastered then the player would become a drake.

As a drake the player would actually being to fly. They would have to work on flapping their wings in order to get off the ground. At the beginning it would be only vertical movement. As the player got better then vertical and horizontal movement would be included and the player would fly (more about flying is farther down the string of posts).

Once flying is mastered then the player becomes a dragon. As a dragon the player would learn magic. Just like any other mage it would take a very long time to master that skill. Dragons would require certain reagents to learn each spell. Once learned he never forgets it.

Dragons would come in a wide varitey of colors to represent its habitat. Dragons could not enter houses since they are too big nor towns since guards would kill them.

Dragons would search out gold since it would take a certain amount of gold for a dragon to survive in addition to eating.

Flying aspect of Dragoning (if thats a word :P):

A hatchling would only being to learn to fly upon reaching 3/4 of the way to the status of drake. The "newbie" at flying would only beable to make a hop into the air and in the forward direction depending on the strength of that hatchling.

As the hatchling began to improve his hoping he would being to learn to glide on the wind for short distances. Meaning he would have to hop off something really high to glide long distances. His ability in gliding would be determined by his gliding ability (which was at one time his hoping ability). The hatchling would have to perfect the ability of gliding in order to become a drake.

At the status of drake flying begins. Flying would be flapping the wings hard enough to pull the drake off the ground not gliding through the air (just needed to make the clairification). The drake would begin unable to lift off very far or for very long, but as the drake got stronger he would beable to lift strait up and sort of hover over the ground at a height determined by the strength and flying ability. The duration he could remain in the air would be based on his stamina to continue to flap his wings.

Now comes the fun part, flying in directions.

The drake would have to run in the direction he wanted to fly in, although he would not have to remain in that direction for the remainder of the flight. He would have to run a distance required by his flying ability and strength inorder to lift off the ground. Once off the ground the height at which the drake could soar would be determined by his strength and flying ability. Flapping of the wings would only be required to get off the ground and to keep the height at which the drake is flying. The rest is all gliding. Once the drake is out of stamina to continue to flap he begins to slowly glide back to the ground. A certain amount of flying ability would be required to advance in levels. A dragon would be expected to have perfected his flying technique.

Now for some combat rules and flying:

Since running and flapping requires stamina any damage taken while either running or flapping would inflict a loss of stamina. The loss is determined by the amount of damage taken. If a drake is running away from an attacker and attempting to take off and gets hit by the attacker he looses some stamina. This loss can make him run farther to take off and/or reduces the amount of time the drake has in the air. Thus if the drake was taking heavy damage it would be very unlikely he would get away.

If a drake or dragon is hit in the wing while in flight (accuracy depending on height of drake or dragon and skill of attacker) he comes crashing into the ground taking damage for anything he hits on the way down (including the ground). The severity of the hit by the object on the wing is determined by what hit the wing and how much damage it did (its easy to fly with a small hole in the wing from an arrow but not one from an axe. :P) The more severe the hit the faster the dragon or drake would crash to the ground. If the dragon or drake was flying at a high altitute and was hit hard enough the impact on the ground or some object in the way could be leathal.

I believe that concludes the aspect of flying.

I do not agree with the dragon idea at all. It deviates from the play style of Ultima.
You stated that, as limitations for the Dragon's power, it wouldn't be able to go into houses and I would imagine town as well (If not, then that does screw with Ultimas past)
I understand that you said Dragons would be weak as hatchlings and perhaps equal with humans as older drakes. But of course the eventual goal for any character, Dragon or otherwise, is to develop into a stronger version of the stats and skills you select. (Roleplaying is apart from this, the weakest characters can still roleplay as such).
So what we have is an ultimately stronger player than other races, which cannot enter town or houses, and exists by attacking and consuming things (read: players) for meat and their treasure. Although they could hang out in dungeons and attack other monsters, this is implausible with the gains that could be earned from turning on their human counterparts instead. Dragons are PK specific.
A major argument for people against playerkilling is that it disrupts roleplaying because other than crazed murder, PKs have no motive besides their own greed and desire to cause havok for others who would just as soon enjoy the world around them than run from it all the time. But if Dragons were to roleplay their characters naturally, they would be malicious, evil, intelligent creatures that desire only wealth and complete dominance of the other races. I myself admittedly am a PK in Ultima, but even I (in my clouded view of other players feelings, as has been told to me by other anti-pks often) can see the imbalance dragon players would impose on the game world.
Also, Dragons, being mystical creatures, should be more rare than they are in even UO1. I can't stand the fact that you can tame a dragon (even though its cool to have it fight with you in battle). Dragons, at least as a creature definition in Ultima, are superintelligent, oftentimes surpassing the mental prowess of human scholars. With the addition of a Dragon race for players to control, their presence would be felt and seen too much. Oversaturation of a otherwise very cool creature would occur.
In UO2, dragons should be played by GM's and (hopefully) a much more sophisticated AI than in UO1.
