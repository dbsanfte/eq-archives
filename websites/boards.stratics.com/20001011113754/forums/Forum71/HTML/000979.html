After reading the posts on various boards about new types of mountain rats (Lugan-rats, grommie-rats, Dillo-rats, etc..) I truly expected horrors upon horros to befall my mage. I was sure glad I was in town, and not by the gollem graveyard where all those things are plentiful. Then I logged in and gingerly set foot upon this patched world...

I was not run down by anything in this world with one exeption. I do not mind the changes made with the patch to much. I even like faster lugans, it makes things a little spicery. I also notice my treasure is a lot better then it was before which I do like. I actually have found 3 wands in the space of one day. Up until this point I have NEVER found a wand. But its not all good.

Although I think the armadillos were just fine the way they were, I can deal with them still, but there is one creature which is completly insane. Even mountain rats are tame compared to this once not so threating creature... That is the Ash Grommie, or grommies in general. I do not know if all grommies are this fast, but this one is insane. Here is what I saw and experienced.

Walking up to the golem graveyard area, I noticed lots of dead people around the porthole, and on the mountain sides around the porthole. Some were comming back to get thier corpses, and they were high level too. Maybe 21 - 25 from the few I looked at. This area isnt that hard, its really not. There were not any monsters around, but one... An ash grommie near the porthole. I didn't think much of it, but I ran into the porthole.. no big deal. I looked around and there were lots of golems around and no one in here, so I decided to wisely(???) leave. Upon exiting I learned of the true horror, the ash grommie near the exit. I ran away as fast as I could. Not only could it keep up, but it could keep up and attack without effort. My mage has a run of 135 and I was only at 90% of my weight and this grommie was on my tail. He zapped me ONCE for 40 pts of lightning damage. Fortunally I have 48 HP. I ran up the mountain, and down the other side and this thing would not lay off. Mountain rats give up on me uselly after two swings. This bugger had chased much farther then any citter I have ever seen. Some fool attacked it, and it broke off me. He was not close to me, on the edge of radar. Before I could tell what happend the grommie seemingly folded space, waxed the guy, and then went back to its holding sport near the porthole.

I am really upset about this. Grommies have gone from the slowest monsters to the fastest (that I have encounterd). Its insane. I do agree they were to slow, OR maybe they should be that way considering how much they will smite you if they get close. I think they should be the old armadillo speed would be good enough for them. But other then this one small, but big problem, I do not have problems with this patch at all.

Heh,,i agree..all you hear is mages complaining about the patch and how they are so weakened, the fact is they should never have been so powerful to begin with. This should have been taken care of in beta, but better late than never. Just take a look around, the vast majority of characters who are high level, 25+ are mostly mages and archers, this shows how unbalanced the game is. The few non-mages who are so high level are people who play 17+ hours a day.

This wont be the last balancing of the game and everyone is going to be unhappy about changes in their character at some point, it's unfortunate that these issues of balance weren't taken care of during beta, but the fact is that Turbine and MS rushed AC out the door in order to get a jump on holiday sales, and thus the changes are going to be coming in the form of patches over the next several months. Just accept this, and get to learning how your character has been changed and figure out how to use hte changes to your best advantage, or find another character to play, and if you are still complaining, then perhaps AC just isnt the game for you.
