Okay, although I have a 45 necro, im going to throw all that aside for a little while to talk about something else, baking.

This trade skill is currently in the game purely for fun. You can sell the items you make for up to and including 1gp profit per item. This is by far not good enough to make this trade skill even worth the effort. Sure it makes some cool sounding foods, but thats about it right now.

My suggestion is this: Make some sort of enhancement to the baked / cooked foods. Some possible suggestions of mine are:

- Food lasts longer
The bakes food would last a certain amount of time longer in your inventory (be eaten slower) than the currently store bought food.

- Healing Benefits
Some forms of baked goods could provide a healing or mana regen benefit... now im not talking chloroplast or clarity here, just something to almost make it worth while.

- Stat boost
Give some baked foods a temporary stat buff, be it 1, or 5 even to a certain stat, or multiple stats, even resists.. something at all would be great. Make this last until the next piece of food needs to be eaten, or for 5 minutes after it is eaten.. anything would be great.

I enjoy cooking a great deal, its fun to do when you're sick of camping rediculous spawns and overcrowded zones. I believe that if all, or any of these mentioned boosts to the trade skill would make it have a practical application in the land of Norrath, and make our trade *almost* sought after

I think this is a very reasonable idea, and could be easily implemented if thought out.

Baking is useful! Useful for making friends, roleplaying, and having fun.

IMHO baking is about the one skill that Verant got right from the outset. Nobody has any delusions of being able to make a lot of cash from baking, almost everyone who does it does it simply for fun. Ingredients are inexpensive and on the whole relatively easy to obtain.

Baking itself is quite easy to master, given a few pp and a few hours. Not everyone bothers to master it because to the k00l d3wds it's useless. If some baked goods suddenly became useful in a exp-gaining sense then I'm not sure I'd like what I think would happen to baking.

Those of us who bake do so because:

- we can give food to friends they've never seen before
- we accept that it's a sink for our extra silver and copper
- we accept that it doesn't do anything gameplay-useful like helping you gain experience
- it gives us a handle on roleplaying if we so desire
- it's just fun

I have mixed feelings on the issue. I'd like it if there were some logistic reason for having baked food. The only reason at this point is because it's lighter than any storebought food (I'm still pretty sure the storebought muffins weigh 0.4 each.) But when you can buy muffins for 5cp each, no one has much incentive to to spend more to even make baked goods themselves, much less buy them from a baker.

On the other hand, it's kind of nice to have acheived the level I have in Baking, and know that I'm part of a pretty small group of Craft...well, um, freaks, really. If there were a k3wl reason to have baked goods, and "everyone" started doing it, it would detract from my enjoyment of having the skill I do in such an obscure craft.

If there were to be a very low-level bonus applied to a character that ate a certain kind of food, I think it should last until the next time your character ate. And of course, the other thing is that if different foods did different things, you'd have to make some choices about what you wanted, because otherwise you'd carry nothing but food! So I don't think it would be very heavily used, even if it were ever implemented.

I also have to say that I have serious doubts that Verant would ever allow a craft to be that...well, useful, really. Come on — a craft skill that can make an item that would enhance a character...but is consumable, so that there is a persistent demand for such items, rather than an eventual market saturation? It makes too much sense, as compared to the established way of operating the crafts and the "economy."

Of course, I think it's do-able, with some careful thought put into it. But again, looking at the track record for craft skills, that's going against the grain.

In the end, I have to say that if you're doing Baking (or Brewing, or Pottery) for any reason other than fun, you're probably wasting your time and letting yourself in for too much frustration.
