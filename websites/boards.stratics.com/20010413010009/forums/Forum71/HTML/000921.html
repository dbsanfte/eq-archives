I also have been reading a lot of the posts here concerning the effectiveness of weapons, apparent advantages of unarmed combat over weapon use, unfair advantages for mages and archers, etc. While I agree with most of the posts (I have a blademaster as my main character, but I also have an unarmed expert who is pretty fun to play), I think I would be amiss if I didn't point out one thing: roleplaying.

Believe me, I am not pointing fingers at anyone, but shouldn't we all be in AC to roleplay? Isn't that the intent? Don't get me wrong, I want a powerful character because he is more fun to play, but I use swords on Naamor because he is a long-time character of mine (a fighter from D&D way back in the early 80's who then became a Paladin in AD&D, and has had various incarnations since then ).

Even if swords were totally bunk in AC, he would still wield a sword because THAT is Naamor. I am not saying that perhaps some adjustments aren't needed, but I am a little disappointed that the majority of the griping seems to be based on power in the game. When I was a budding RPG'er I will admit... my goals were usually power-based: I wanted a stronger character people would like at and say "wow". As I matured, I found that the true fun of RPG is BEING the character! For those of you who have not experienced the true thrill of serious roleplaying, let me tell you... it ROCKS!

For example: when I developed Naamor all those years ago, I decided he would not trust magic. To this day, Naamor has been involved in several games (including Diablo and soon to be Diablo 2) and in several of these, magic is quite powerful. I could easily make him a stronger character by implementing some magic skills to augment his combat, but that would be ooc.

My bottom line as I close this tirade is this: if you are constantly obsessed with making your character the "best" or "most powerful" (I have seen TONS of posts on here about this is better than this, or what skills make the best this, etc.) then you are not enjoying roleplaying to its fullest. Develop a character in your mind, give him/her a personality, background, etc... then build him/her to match your template. And above all, stick to it. If you character is crass and rude, stick to your guns and play it out. You will get a lot more out of the game, and those around you will as well.

Respectfully,

I guess I can see both sides.

I am a roleplayer, and my primary character is a highly modified Blademaster. I spend most of my time in AC playing him, though if I wanted to powergame, two of my other characters would probably be a better choice...One is an Unarmed Combat specialist and the other is an Archer. But I want to roleplay a Samurai, so I play a Sho Blademaster.

At the same time, I do have a problem with Unarmed Combat vs Sword...Before you start flaming, I do NOT think they should nerf Unarmed Combat. It does tend to be very effective, but my biggest problem is that it is so much cheaper than sword in terms of skill points. You can become specialized in Unarmed Combat for 12 points less than it would cost to specialize in Sword. Why is that?

If we accept, for the sake of arguement, that the skills all work fine the way they are...what is the reason for having Sword cost so much more than Unarmed?

In terms of game balance, I don't see it. Those 12 points are a big deal. It will take the Blademaster 16 levels to aquire the same set of skills that the Unarmed Combat character started with, and by that point the Unarmed character has another 12 points they've spent on new skills.

If it is for the sake of realism - that Unarmed Combat is easier to learn than Sword, then I don't agree with that, either. I've studied both, and it is much simpler to inflict damage when you have a weapon. That's why people developed weapons to begin with.

I am totally against nerfing Unarmed...I just think it should cost more, or Sword should cost less...

There are two issues at stake here -- and the more they get talked about as one, the less likely it is that anyone will be satisfied with anyone else's point.

The first debate is over which is more powerful -- unarmed combat or sword. This is an in-game question that, to some extent, can only be answered individually over time. Many say the two balance out in later levels, and I'm inclined to agree at this point. Some also say it has to do with weapon choice and the availability of powerful weapons to counter an imbalance that occurs during game play. Others say don't worry about it -- just enjoy the roleplaying aspect of playing who you created and don't sweat the power unless you are a powergamer. It would seem then, that most people think that Unarmed does not need to be nerfed and that Swords do not need to be beefed up. Whatever one's answer to this question is -- it doesn't seem to be too much of a problem.

The second debate still is unanswered. I'm not sure it can be answered by any of us who aren't involved in the game creation. Perhaps we are all missing something. This is, by the way, the quesiton I'm most interested in -- and the one that often gets side-stepped in favor of discussing balance in the game. Read Joe Napalm's first post in this string (or ones in other strings from him and myself). They'll explain it nicely. So...what's the question?

Why is sword so much more expensive to learn than Unarmed Combat?

We can't even decide which of the two is more effective, but the Turbinites obviously think sword is the better one if they buy into the idea that you should pay more for better quality. We who are playing the game don't seem to know which is better -- if we did there wouldn't be multiple strings on the same subject. There is room for debate, and yet one skill is so much more expensive to get.

This is interesting to me, but I'm not sure anyone can give us an answer except perhaps the folks over at Turbine...

Actually - It's a lot harder to dodge the sword, believe me...I've taken enough bamboo to the noggin to know that.

The reason is this - someone with a sword has significantly more reach than with an empty hand, and contrary to popular belief swords do not weigh all that much. If it is properly balanced, you can swing a sword almost as fast as just your fist - at the hilt...due to length, however, the tip of the blade is moving much faster than you could ever punch. There is also a lot more you have to get out of the way of. To dodge a punch, move your head out of the way and they miss. To dodge a sword, move your head out of the way and lose an arm or a leg.

Of course, Real Life combat is much too complex to simulate in AC - I would be more than happy just to have balance. Make Unarmed more powerful than War Magic if you want - just make it cost more skill points.
