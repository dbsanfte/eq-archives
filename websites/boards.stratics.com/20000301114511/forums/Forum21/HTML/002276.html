The answer to this one is ... it depends.

At mid levels, you are usually going to do more damage with a big 2 hander than dual weild. Conversely you will generally taunt better with dual weild.

At high levels, you'll want to switch to duael weild. Due to the way verant's combat algorythm works, dual weilding low delay weapons will outdamage a 2 hander against anything except a green or low blue. This is true even though a raw damage/delay comparison doesn't show it.

The reason is that at high levels, your modal (not max, modal e.g. most common) weapon damage is :

2 X DMG + (lvl-25)/3

Not that the additive component (lvl-25)/3 which is not a function of DMG. This skews everything in favor of a low delay weapon.

Example:

I can hit 2 times with an exe axe or 6 times with my yaks (this is a little unfair to the yaks; more like 6.5 hits per 100 time units is reasonable).

Exe axe modal damage : 2 X 25 + 8 = 58

Two swings so total damage : 116

Yak modal damage : 2 X 8 + 8 = 24

Six swings so total damage : 144

This discounts the weapon procs, etc.

The fact is that at high levels dual weild outdamages and outtaunts a 2 hander, even if the raw damage/delay numbers don't seem to support such an assertion.
I actually think this was something that Verant never realize when they cooked up their combat damage rules, but its probably too late to change it now.

At the moment at the highest levels 1h seems to have an advantage. THere seem to be 2 factors contributing to this. One is the the frequncy that hits for the Magic Number come up with. On low damage weapons the MN is reletivly closer to max damage so that each of these hits is more efficient. Also there are fewer hit above the MN than below it. the second effect that adds to the effectiveness of 1h weapons, especially, fast weapons (which often have lower damage) is the minimum hit you get with the primary hand. Scoring a lot of minimum hits of 8 with a Ykesha is a lot more efficent than socring a lot of 9 with a Mith 2 Hander.
