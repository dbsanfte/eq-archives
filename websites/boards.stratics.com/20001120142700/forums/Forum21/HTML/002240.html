I'm looking at a berserk skill more as an option to a warrior in a group who happens to not be tanking. Yes, the warrior could berserk, which would possibly increase his damage output and could effectively cause the mob to switch to him...at which time he would probably want to start thinking levelheadedly and come out of berserk to better evade his opponent's attacks. Hmmm....in response to your original question:

Not sure about class balance but I would love to get another little button to push above lvl 35.

Rationale of Crits (EQ RL?): it would seem that crippling hit literally means that the warrior has tagged the MOB so hard, in the right spot, that he/she has deprived it of the ability to move (it would seem that a blow like this in RL would result in catastrophic damage as well as lack of mobility...think sword through the heart).

This would mean that crippling blows would actually be a sub-set of critical hits (crits are all melee blows that hit a substantial weak spot of an opponent causing it harm).

What is the rationale for it kicking in when we are beserk? Not sure based on this rarionale of what crips are.

What is the rationale for it being controllable? None at lower levels (unless by sheer luck). Possibly explainable at higher levels by increased experience & expertise. I kinda lean towards non-controllable.

Is this really mutually exclusive from SK HT?
Absolutely not. The HT is a manifestation of the SKs relation with their evil Mojo & their God. The crippling hit should be based upon the warriors experience & expertise with hand to hand weaponary.

Whoops logic problem! Shouldn't Monks get this skill to (provided the rationale I stated for crips/crits makes sense)? Yes!!
They have as much knowledge of melee fighting as we do. It would seem to follow that they would get crits as well (yikes good bye class balance). Heck their additional speed at higher levels probably already shows their specialized training (so maybe there is a push back rationale on this).

So without thinking of class vs class arguments I advocate:

- Crips: non-controllable as they are now. Although I do want to see another little button! This reflects that they are sub-set of crits.

- A probability, that rises with lvl, that a crit hit will instead be a crippling blow. This makes sense (its skill & knowledge that tell you where to hit critter to hurt it badly).

- The dam on crippling blow should be a pct higher than the corresponding crit it would have caused. No excuses.

- Beserk should be disconnected from crit/cripling functionality & that instead stay focused on stat increases/decreases

Ok best for last:

One wild & crazy idea.

A "Find Weakness Skill".

This would involve a button click & then require the warrior to stand still, say nothing, hear nothing, initiate no attack and focus on a particular MOB.

After a given period of time, the button recycles & you get a sucess or failure message. Success (lvl based) means you get an offensive advantage of some type (MOB has lowered AC etc.). Prob need to make the skill unusable when invis (lose invis), on a rooted/snared critter bfor playability. Sure there are lots of holes in this idea *BUT* its an idea. If not for warriors how about another melee class (Rogue guys?). Think skill would start at 40 or so.
