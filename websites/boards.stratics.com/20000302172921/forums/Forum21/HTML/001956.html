Tanks can do multitaunt quite effectively. That is, engage one target, quickly change targets to hit a non-engaged mob when taunt refreshes, taunt it, and go back to the primary target. It takes a bit of dexterity and getting used to the horrible targetting system, but very effective.

AE's also do not have high taunt built in, and a damage shield is not necessary. Hate seems to build by them simply beating on the tank, or perhaps by the tank beating on their friend. Either way, with my wizard, I could drop an AE into a pack of mobs if I let the tanks beat on one to about 60% health. I could do it earlier but that would mean I would occasionally get stuff on me.

Tip for wizards using AEs: Have shieldskin up (do this anyways, steelskin is VERY nice and quite cheap, your cleric will love you) and be sure to communicate with your party before and after you cast. Perhaps macro a "/g Casting AE" "/g AE cast, nothing on me" and "/g AE cast, pissed something off" so your tanks know what's going on.

Tip for warriors in an AE using group: Spread those taunts around. Don't just taunt the main target, hit the others too. This is useful in any multitarget situation as it keeps them off your cleric if he has to start dropping the heals.

As far as spreading out damage and taunts go, there's an extremely fine line to be walking in a multi-monster situation. Monsters fight at 100% effectiveness until they run or die, so spreading out the damage more than what is required to easily re-taunt is counter productive.

If the group has multiple tanks (with taunt, specifically) it's a good idea to discuss beforehand who be the primary tank. The group should come up with a plan for dealing with monsters that aggro on casters or clerics so that the group doesn't fall into disarray when this happens.

In my opinion it's in everyone's best interest to have the person with the highest actual Taunt skill be the primary re-taunter when stuff attacks your cleric or casters.

On the topic of the original post, I would like to see an area-effect taunt for warriors. Popping this off a couple times during combat (pre-heal) would help greatly in getting stuff off your cleric when they heal. Same goes for AoE damage spells.
