You get XP in two chunks. The first is when you do damage to it the first time. The second is when the monster dies. Both are proportional to how much damage you do. IE: your killing a monster worth 1k xp. Its almost dead, like 2hp left and a someone else runs up and whacks it, killing it. They will get some, but almost no xp from the kill since they did so little damage overall. Xp is always divvied out fairly from killing a monster.
People who run up and help you, well this is the norm and get used to it. If your from EQ, get used to it. The xp system is differnt here and you do not have the kill stealing problem (where you either get all or none of the xp) that exists in everquest.

I try not to do everything that everyone has said . . . but sometimes I can't help it.

I walk around a corner and I see someone engaged in a battle with some big nasty critter, and I immediately jump in to help. I don't think -- "Hey! Experience Points!" or "Hey! An Easy Kill!" or even "Hey! Loot!" I'm just helping.

Unfortunately, I realize that when I do that there is a good chance I'm annoying whoever it is that I'm helping. But, I'm also a roleplayer. If I walked around a corner and saw someone being attacked by a rabid dog in RL, I would probably help in some way -- even if the person looked alright.

But, that's other difficulty, you can't tell from a quick glance whether the character is healthy or not. You have to stop and examine them and sometimes I'm too ready to jump in and help instead of standing around looking at them.

I feel worse if I *don't* help than if I do . . . I've seen people die while I stopped and tried to examine the situation and that was decidedly unpleasant because I so easily could have helped.

All of that said, I know a lot of people aren't jumping in for the same reasons I am and I also realize that I shouldn't jump in because people don't know why I'm doing it and might take it the wrong way.

So, I guess I'm really not sure what my point is. I think I've been concerned about this issue because rarely do I know what to do when I encounter someone else in battle and I want to stay in character while trying to avoid angering other players . . .

Its a good question. Since I'm originally from E.Q. I must say that I thought this was very bad form when I first saw it. However, now I have changed my tune. It a simply all those things mentioned above, A helping hand, A motive for role-players, and a good way to take on more powerful monsters. Since the exp is split based on the amount of damage you do (as has been mentioned) there is no real "kill stealing" in the EQ way of thinking.

One example of this is the Beach of Eastham. Lots of monsters that are many times more powerful then the people fighting them. These roving bands of characters virtually thrive on this type of combat. There are no complaints of "it was mine go find your own" but many requests for help. A littering of corpses is evidence that this type of fighting is a necessity. Eastham is truly a place of cooperation.

This is just one place this practice exists, several roads between specific cities, the wilds around certain dungeons, and in these dungeons themselves. See Qalaba'r Beach for a similiar situation in miniature (even the temple can be considered but this is extreme). Or even the Towers scattered across the lands. There are lots of these areas, and if this is not your style of play. Then there are many areas that are untouched by players. There are some dungeons that are empty. No players whatsoever. Even more dungeons are used sparingly, housing only one or two characters. Stay away from places that are popular and well known. Go exploring. Stay off the beaten path.

In my mind A.C. is a portrayed as frontier land, a small population of "humans" that must subdue and make safe the land of Dereth. All the while gaining riches and glory. It is a combined effort and the spirit of cooperation that will make this happen.

Since this is the second go around on this topic, and it has been a while since the first, I'll give my opinion once again.

It seems there is a rigid division in this topic. Some people look at it as kill stealing. Don't touch MY monsters, and I won't touch yours. And the other side is, Hey there's someone that might benefit from my help. I'd hate to see them die, so I'll give them a hand.

Now how can you tell which school of thought someone is? How can you take the time to judge the situation? The answer in my eyes, is for those that like to actually help people, go ahead and help. If they don't want your help, they'll certainly let you know. And you must be prepared for the occassional rude person that is utterly offended at your offer to help. On the other hand, if you're of the leave me alone, this is MY loot, MY exp, MY creature, then please be polite when someone offers to help. Let them know you'd rather be solo, and be on your way. IF they continue to follow you around taking your kills, remind them once more. If the problem persists, then you are free to take whatever action you feel is necessary. You can move on to another area to avoid conflict, or you can bring on a conflict by trying to get the kill stealer killed. Or you can try to contact the powers that be. Report him if he's harrassing you, or send a tell to his monarch describing the inappropriate actions of your kill stealer. I think this second option can often be more effective. There's nothing more satisfying than seeing a monarch step in and threaten his follow for poor behavior.
