I don't really see a problem with them. Weigh them out...

Pros:
Gives people a reason to leave the towns. The less people in the towns, the better.
Many newbies will be afraid to just "head out into nowhere." Now they at least have somewhere to go. They can say to their friends "Hey, lets go try to get to that dot to the east."

Cons:
Destinations might become crowded now that they are obvious to everyone. This makes the assumption that your average player can survive at point of interest and that the treasure/xp is worth staying for.
Instead of discovering places by scouring the countryside, more people will be inclined only to hop between points of interest.

Doesn't seem so bad to me when viewed that way. You know they are't going to put the *best* places on the map. It would be a waste. People need to be rewarded for their discoveries, not rewarded for simply surviving the run. I don't think all you powergamers have a lot to worry about. But everyone else and the newbies that are sick of crowded towns have much to gain.
