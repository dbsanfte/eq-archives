To answer Silvan's question, yes there are some skills and attributes that are better than others for certain character types, but the variety is amazing. I have not seen any "archetypes" become so prominent to become the majority by any means. It would be very rare indeed to find someone with the same or even similar stats and skills. Everyone seems to have their own preferences, and AC has a lot of customization. I give my advise to newbies on what worked for me, but even then, they tinker a little and put more into something than I did and less in another. There is certainly not a correct way to make a character, there are just so many good combinations...

As for tank mages, they would be laughable in AC. Anyone who tries to be a fighter and a mage is going to be much less of a fighter and much less of a mage than their single-minded counterparts. While this hybrid can be somewhat effective simply because you can fight with the aid of spells, but tends to not be as powerful as the "pure" fighters or mages. AC accomplishes this with its attribute and skill system. To be a good fighter you need to specialize in you weapon of choice, melee defense, and maybe healing, or at least train them. To be a good mage you need to specialize or train in your school(s) of choice and maybe mana conversion. You simply cannot have both...ever! You can only specialize in the beginning so you can never get specialized in everything. Even training in all of those skills would take a lot of skill pts, and then you would be mediocre in everything. Then there's the attributes. A mage's attributes are focus and self, a fighter's are strength, endurance, coordination, and quickness. These are obviously opposites. To do both you would have to split your exp, again making you just ok if not poor at everything.
