Also speaking as an enchantress and a warrior here are some tips if you are fighting with a smart enchater.

1 as lead tank target a creature FAST so other can assist you as soon as the fight starts. When running back from a pull have auto attack already on. When you move to the second creature, engage fighting as fast as you can. As lead tank dont stop to cast a spell, just attack. Sometimes mez breaks during your cast time and you know what happens.

2 dont break from that critter and change mid fight NO MATTER what else pops up. A smart enchanter watches for new creatures.

3 alert the healer of the party to watch the enchanters life closely until things are under control. This is the red alert time as the enchanter will be casting on critters who have yet taken any damage.

4 the half health rule. In large pulls if the enchanters life goes to half there is usually a problem. Try using taunt without actually attacking. Odds are against you agroing the monster off the enchanter (high agro spells) but taunt may work and attacking will only wake up the monster if it is succesfully mezzed. in this case see #5

5 have the cleric cast a HIGH heal spell on the enchanter. this usually pulls the creature on the cleric/shaman/druid. Now the enchanter can cast.

6 if something gets on the cleric make sure the DO NOT attack it. just say something (if the enchanter doesnt see it for some reason) and they will mez.

7 npc pets. If you are about to attack a npc caster with a pet, get the pet first (as long as both are mezzed). attacking the caster usually wakes the pet for some reason. It will make a direct line for the enchanter.

8 dots. if someone is using these, it cannot be mezzed. so make sure you finish it off.

9 i have been told that if a creature is TAUNTED three times before a mez is broken it will not go after the enchanter. cant say though.

10 if you see the messages X has been mesmorized several times in a row, the enchanter has fallen back on area mez. Usually duiring a large overpull. Time to watch out. Several may resist and if they do try to grab one if several go after the enchanter. Also note you have about 15 seconds before they all wake up again. They may or may not head for the enchanter. Time to watch him/her.

I agree with the above posts. I would also like to add that if the enchanter does get in trouble, usually the easiest fix for the situation is for the healer to toss a big heal on the enchanter drawing the mobs to himself so the enchanter can mez them.

It's amazing what a group can take on as long as the cleric and enchanter can keep each other alive. Also, make sure your first target is the most dangerous mob. That reduces the chance that a resist will result in a dead enchanter before anything can be done about it.

One other thing: a good enchanter is the best addition to any group over 20th level, period, no arguments. In a discussion with a 50th level warrior, she mentioned how clerics were essential to take on the big hitting mobs like LDCs (Lava Duct Crawler), as Complete Heal was so much more efficient. I didn't argue the point at the time but felt differently.

Last night my 40-45 pickup group in SolB (2 Warriors, Bard, Ranger, Enchanter, Druid) successfully took on LDCs individually and 2 at a time. Yes, only 1 druid to heal and no casters to take'm down fast. Between charms, stuns, slows, strength debuffs and the corresponding buffs on ourselves no one even went below half health or 20% mana. The one-dimensional aspect of most clerics would have just slowed us down.

On topic, a good tactic for when the enchanter has a group of mobs mezzed and you don't want the one you hit first to run at the enchanter is as follows: Taunt, wait for recycle and Taunt again, then attack. 2 taunts often outweigh the enchanter's hatelist from the mez, 3 always seem to if you have the time to wait that long - on the last mob usually. Takes a little training of your group members to wait for you, but worth it. Time is on your side with a good enchanter in the group.
