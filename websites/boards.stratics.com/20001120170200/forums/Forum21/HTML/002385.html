First things first: This isn't an anti-twinking rant. I used to be really irritated at twinking, but I got over it once I realized that there's a fundamental difference in the point of view between twinkers and non-twinkers. The difference lies in the answer to the following question:

Does the player earn the phat lewt? Or does the character earn it?

The key to understanding why the issue of twinking will never be resolved, and the key to my getting over my irritation of twinkers, was the realization that there is no wrong answer to this question. Once I understood that, I stopped being bothered by twinks and twinkers except in those instances where I was competing for gear for myself with 50th level casters who wanted the gear for their twinks. (I *do* still believe that a person's primary character should be given preference for equipment over the twinks of others.)

So again, this isn't an anti-twink rant. There is no wrong answer to the question I pose above. Thus, there is no rant.

What I seek instead is to further my understanding.

I know a lot of people who twink so that they can sail through the first 10-15 levels. Even though I don't twink myself, I can understand this motivation. These are people who twink a couple Langseax, a SBH, and maybe a full set of banded or a visual set of bronze.

What I don't understand are those who MEGA twink. Those folks who start off a newbie warrior with the best possible equipment their 50th level primary characters can get. I recall not too long ago some fellow posting that he'd started his warrior off with the absolute best non-plane armor he could get, 2 diamondine earrings, a Mith 2Hander (or maybe it was a Skarlon), 2 Yaks, FBSS, TBB, and so forth, and so on. His Level 1 Warrior was decked out in gear the listing of which looked a great deal like my wish list for the next 11 levels.

And as I read down his list of items that he'd twinked over, I found myself thinking "Jeez, what does this guy have to look forward to?" I mean, for me, the journey is at least as much fun as the destination. Having two EBWs is neat. GETTING two EBWs was even neater. I felt a real sense of pride, elation and accomplishment when I got my first EBW, and I was equally thrilled when I got my second. If someone had just handed me two EBWs (assuming that they were tradable items) at 1st level, it wouldn't have been as fun or as exciting as it was to get 'em myself.

So I'm curious: For those of you who megatwink full suits of crafted, Mith 2handers, etc etc... What's in it for you? What do you have to look forward to over the next 50 levels? Where do you derive the majority of your enjoyment from the game?

None of these questions are meant to be facetious or sarcastic, btw. I really am interested in hearing from some of the folks that really twink up their characters.

I think many of the people that mega-twink aren't starting a new character because they want to start over. Rather, I think they start a new character because, having gotten to 50th, they look around at the people they're grouping with and think, "Hey, I'd like to try that."

And when they say they want to try that, they mean they want to be that class at *50th* level... not levels 1-49. So, in the interests of getting there as quickly as possible they load up their new character with everything the can find, and get them to 50th as fast as possible.

Personally, I don't like this in the least. First of all, I find it belittles the effort those of us that are working on our *first* character are doing, particularly when someone throws together a twink that's identical to us. I made my Wood Elf Warrior way back in April when there were only 2 others on the server that I knew of. 8 months later, I swear every 50th level out there had made one, outfitted them in better gear than I had, and powerlevelled them.

Secondly, I'm sure everyone's aware (at least on the older servers) of how bad the begging problem has become. You can't stick your head in Greater Faydark anymore without getting spammed by /tells asking for money and equipment. While I despise begging, I think a lot of it has to do with new players who are working on their first character having to group with the twinks of 50th levels. It has to be disheartening when you're trying to throw together the money to buy a piece of bronze plate, and the warrior next to you is dual wielding Ykeshas.
