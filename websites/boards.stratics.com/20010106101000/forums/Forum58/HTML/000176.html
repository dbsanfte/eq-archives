Suggestions welcome.

From what I've seen, here goes:

Find an excellent pottery location.

Excellent means "has both sketches and clay vendors."

Good Races: Halas is your best bet.
Evil Races: Neriak is your best bet.

There are lots of other places as well, such as Erudin and Rivervale having the Clay/Poison Sketches vendor and Kelethin having the Sketches vendor...I'm pretty sure most cities have one or the other.

I know for sure that Halas does have both (trained there myself) and am pretty sure Neriak does too.

(For exemplary purposes we will start with the basis that you are based in Halas.)

0: Don't train a point into it. It's unnecessary. You can do it, but learning a trade skill is a lot like learning a language. You don't need the point to start learning. Just sit and work (or get spammed.)

What you DO need to do is put on all INT, WIS, and CHA items you have. They'll help.

1: Pick up 20 Flasks of water, 20 Large Jar Sketches, 20 (Medium) Blocks of Clay. (If you're VERY short on cash, I think small and medium jars are even easier than large ones and you get more successes at low skill.)

In Halas: Teria O'Danos (by the docks), Skoni (right behind Teria), and Dok's wife Merle (in Dok's Cigars)

2: Go to the pottery wheel. (Halas: exit Dok's Cigars, turn left, and enter "The Golden Torque" to your right. The wheel is inside.) Combine the clay, sketches and water, 1 each. If you're cheap, save any successes at this point. If you have cash to throw around skip step 3.

3: If you're cheap go back to your clay vendor and pick up a quality firing sheet for every jar you got right. Fire them in the kiln. (Halas: the back door of the Golden Torque, outside there you can't miss it.) Use one sheet per unfired jar. Sell the containers off. Since they can't be placed in bags at this point ('cuz they ARE bags) you may have to make a few at a time and sell them off. Having a friend or a large percentage of low-levels in your guild who all need cheap bank storage helps.

4: Lather, rinse, repeat until you have 41 skill in pottery. Took my 60-int Dwarf Rogue about 3 trips.

5: Change the recipes to that of a medium bowl: medium bowl sketch and the appropriate size of clay. This might require buying the book until someone reminds me what it is. (Or I check my book when I log in next.)

6: If you have a high wisdom (I'd say high 80's) and low bank account, go ahead and fire the bowls. You will lose RL-time doing this though.

7: When you reach approximately 120 skill (really 122) stop doing medium bowls. It's time to move to poison vials.

Any addendum post below.

--Benanov

Don't discount combining clay for starting out from zero skill. No, you don't get anything for it, but you just carry around the clay (no sketches). 3 small + flask of water makes one (medium) block, three mediums + flask make one large. One large + flask makes a (medium) block, one medium + flask makes a small. Don't bother with the large-- just do smalls to medium and back again, since it doesn't take long to get your skill up and everything you keep you can then use again to build more skill-- no sketches or firing sheets.

Unless you're SUPER-CHEAP, I don't recommend even bothering to fire most of the stuff you're making for skill gains-- it's faster to just destroy it and take the loss, since firing trivs out pretty early, plus the firing sheets cost and time is lost. With about 12pp, I went from 36 to making vials (122?) in about two hours.

But the recommended sketches progression looks good.

Medium Jar to skill level 25. You'll get very near 1:1 for combine:skill-up on these.

Medium Bowl to 122. Don't bother firing, even ditching the unfired bowls doesn't lose you that much cash. And if yer strapped for cash, what are you doing learning a trade skill? =)

Large Bowl to 148.

Vials to 163 or wherever the current cap is.
