I agree that Saum's descriptions of what Rangers get in relation to other classes ignores many of the advantages Warriors and other hybrids have over them and the disadvantages they have, so I'll skip that and address the question asked.

Berzerker Frenzy is really only useful soloing, since it allows Warriors a better shot at winning close fights or fleeing when critical. In a group, it's the HEAL ME!!! alarm.

Almost every suggestion that's popped in begs for a new hotkey, some special ability that we can invoke on demand. As nice as these ideas are, it seems pretty clear that on-demand attack skills (aside from autoattack and Kick/Bash) don't fit Verant's concept of what a Warrior should be able to do. Being the most basic fighter class, I find it unlikely that new abilities would be added to the class that would be on par with those that hybrids and other melees use.

The rationale for this is because every other melee class gives up equipment selection, hit points, skill caps, and/or experience points to gain their special abilities. Being that one of our greatest advantages is the variety of equipment available to us, Verant has been implementing Warrior "upgrades" in the form of new weapons and armor that only we or a select few classes can use.

Things I'd like to see (which fit along these lines) include boots which proc like weapons when used with Kick or a new SotSU-type shield which is an off-hand only weapon with a good dmg/delay, AC bonus and a stun proc. Aside from that, I'm of the opinion that Warriors don't need any more hotkey skills.

First off, I think Rangers add a lot to a party. Even if just for Snare. Some parties may not need this, since Druids, Necros and SKs have spells that the same or equivalent, however not all parties will have an SK (and the darkness line is resisted more anyway) and I would certainly prefer that my Druid or Necro was able to concentrate on other tasks than keeping the creatures snared. If find that a Ranger in a group with me is able to control runners excellently, does appreciable melee damage and can taunt stray mobs off the casters if necessary. I was in a group recently with a Cleric and an Enchanter, both of whom can use spells to Root and Stun, we were in a situation where we could not allow a runner to get more than 10 feet or we would get stomped. This was going badly until we got a ranger and from then on it was pretty much plain sailing (barring one nasty incident where snare failed 6 times in a row).

Secondly, I think the reason that people often suggest button operated abilities as new options for warriors is that those give us more tactical decisions during combat. As most here will be able to tell you there is actually a lot of decisions to be made during combat for a warrior (what creatures to target, do I need to pull that thing off the enchanter, should I call an evac now) but a lot of these only get developed at higher levels, when they become more important and when the warrior has the experience to know their job better. A lot of the time the warriors role can seem to be 'press autoattack, put something heavy on the kick button and go make some coffee'. Compared to all the decisions that other classes have to make (more notably the casters) this can seem boring. Giving us more decisions of the 'do I use the blender of death special now or should I wait' nature makes it seem that the warrior has more decisions and is therefore more interesting.
