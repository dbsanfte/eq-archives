Depends on what room you're trying to break.

Breaking the kitchen/servants quarters and the garden is easy. Invis yourselves and run to the closest safe spot (for the kitchen) or to the fountain room by the garden (which isn't *safe*, per se, but there's only 3 MOBs there).

Breaking the courtyard and the castle entranceway is easy if you have a druid or a ranger to cast harmony.

Breaking the ballroom and/or the conference room is tricky because if you aggro one room, there's a very good chance that you'll aggro the other. And there's no good spot to get to where you can cast harmony without aggroing the MOBs. I've never been in a group that's succesfully broken the ballroom or the conference room, and after several deaths I finally stopped trying.

My knowledge of the upstairs rooms is limited. I'll have to get back to you on those.

Go through the back of the castle, through the secret door.

Clear the jail (if you want) then go up the stairs (through the hidden door). Make a left when you get to the top.

First door you come to opens into a room with 2 spawns. (This is typically called the torture chamger.)

Camp here for now. Only one other door in the room, so when ready have someone pull the gargoyles (should be able to get them one at a time) which are right down the hall back to the TC.

Right across from the gargoyles is what looks like a dead end but is actually a secret door. Behind it is the jacuzzi.

There are 4 spawns in the jacuzzi, one of which is either the Mistmoore Guard (who will not attack you unless you sit down) OR Xicotl (40th level troll shadowknight). You can clear out the jacuzzi by using proximity to pull (just edge slowly toward the jacuzzi until something yells at you or starts to cast).

Lasna Cheroon spawns in the tub (right in the water), so you can camp her (sit in the TC and just pull works well). Prince Cherista spawns past the master bedroom, which is the room through the door from the Jacuzzi. She has NEVER dropped anything for us.

If you don't want to camp here, go down the stairs from the gargoyle spawn. Open the door and you're at another gargoyle spawn (2 gargs and occasionally a roamer). Clear them, open the other door and go down the stairs. Make a right at the bottom and you'll come to ANOTHER gargoyle spawn. 2 gargoyles here and rarely a roamer.

Clear those and now you're ready to head into the more dangerous area. 8-)

If you take the hallway out of this gargoyle spawn it appears to dead end, but it is a secret door that opens onto the piano room. This is a bit dangerous and you MIGHT get a big pull. Invisibility seems to work inconsistently for scouting also.

The piano room as 3 to 4 spawns in it (usually 3, but sometimes a gargoyle sits in the middle of the room). Right outside (in the garden) there are a LOT of spawns (around 8 or 9). When you pull (by proximity) from the piano room, you might get some of the spawns from outside (especially the gargoyles that sit at the door outside).

Kill off whatever you get until you clear the piano room. Now you can either clear the garden and go for the cloaked dhampyre or you can move on to the ballroom and/or maid, butler, etc.

If you do clear the garden and camp there, you can pull from the piano room, the gargoyle spawns, the alcove right passed the piano room (across from the secret door you came thru) and the ballroom.

If you pull by proximity and you're 40+, you should only get one or two at a time (the gypsies are actually pretty low level and you have to walk up close to get them to come after you ;-).

So, camp in the garden and pull to there, You can get the ballroom clear pretty quickly (and maybe get Mynthi Davissi, werewolf talons, etc.).

If you decide to move on after that, go through the double doors in the back of the ballroom. You'll come to a open room with 4 gargoyles sort of recessed up close to the ceiling. Pull those back to the ballroom and kill them.

There's a door to the left, which leads to the conference room. There are usually negotiators in there, so be ready to get charmed. You could also just move past this room and they don't tend to aggro (just keep your distance from the door). We've never gotten any special loot from the conference room, so I would just skip it.

Now if you follow the hallway around to the right you pass another door (this time on your right). This is the kitched and it contains to ancille cooks (shadowknights, around 35th level) and one other mob.

Bypass the kitchen and go to the end of the hall and you come to the butler room and a short hallway. The short hallway leads to the maid room.

There are usually 2 mobs, one right outside each of the rooms. We usually invis our way into the butler room, kill everything there (stuff outside doesn't seem to aggro), then kill the stuff in the hall, then pull the maid room, then pull the kitchen.

Whew.

Okay, Romidar just told you the best way to break MM =P. Mine sucks, has a higher chance of death, and requires big cojones.

But damn it's fun
__________
There are 2 keys to Mistmoore.

Key 1) Invis is a *must* have.

Key 2) Any runners and you're dead.

In MM you can *not* afford to chase anything. Those critters can *NOT* run. stopping runners take priority over everything in MM. As far as I can tell, MM is the second most trainy zone in the whole game. I've broken in here serveral times, and I have to admit that invis and run to the camp spot is the best way to do things. It's not as satisfying, but you'll live longer.

But if you really want to crack this baby open, you need a *good* group that's very used to working together, and an enchanter will make this *much* easier.

I'm assuming you're going to kick in the doors and try and clear the castle (this is a *lot* of fun, but really deadly). Work your way through the valley, and up to the platform. there are about 5-6 mobs + pets (lots of casters usually). This is a pretty tough fight and sets the tone for the rest of the evening. clear the platform, then med up a bit, but don't delay to long. You need to move in fast before the plat respawns. Past the door is a weak mob, but weak mobs are more deadly than a red con in MM. Snare/root/stun him fast, because he may bolt on you. Once you drop him, move to the left and wait in that passage. You'll see the main foyer/hall in front of you, and this is where it will get nasty. There's about 5 mobs (including the gargs) that you'l have to take here. do *NOT* let anything make it past the stairs or you're all dead.

Actually, forget it. Don't try to break MM unless you *really* have a kick ass group, and even then it's mainly just don't let anything run.

One thing I want to warn you about are screamers. Occasionally in the mix will be a greenie, and this is pure evil on Verant's part. If that greenie sees you it'll scream at you, then bolt straight back into the castle. It's totally insane how a winning battle can turn into a bloodbath in seconds.

I don't recommend anyone try to break MM. but if you do, feel proud because it's something few people actually can do. Half the fun is figuring it out on your own =).

If you want goodies, invis and go camp. If you want guts and glory, kick the doors down and hope you're good enough

I've been spending a good bit of time in the castle since about 40th level. Our group usually consisted of:

Paladin
Paladin
Ranger
Ranger
Shaman
Wizard

with me always being the highest level. The experience there in the upper 30's/low 40's is WONDERFUL, especially since so few people go there (constant pulls).

As others have said, you do NOT want a runner here or you'll have a dozen freaking casters on you, and you'll die fast. (One nice thing about the method I described is that you can escape easily by running back through the torture chamber, out the door, leap off the bridge and you're by the zone.)

A group of 6 of us has held the entire second floor down to the 3rd gargoyle spawn. That's 30+ mobs every 20 minutes. If you've got a wizard, this is doable, as they can take the dark offerer's, etc. (casters) down in seconds.

Only reason I go there now is to try for the hooded black cloak, but that dhampyre is RARE (and the cloak is his rare spawn, damn it). Now 3 of us from 40 to 47 can handle the fight down to the piano room & garden, and usually managed to pull from the ballroom (the place is usually so empty that you can camp Lasna, the dhampyre and Mynthi all at once).

We fought our way from the rear entrance to the front of the castle once, then took a bunch of pictures of our group standing at the front gate - it was FUN.

I hated Mistmoore with a passion when I was in the 30's because we always tried the pit/courtyard/graveyard. Trains were deadly and those annoying roamers.... yuck. Once you go in the castle though, especially on the second floor, your fate is in your own hands and depends on your skill.

I was usually the puller and I'd string 3 things out behind me (whoops, bad pull from bedroom; root #1, run a bit, root #2, take #3 to the TC) when the pathing made for a bad pull (this happens a LOT in the bedroom behind the jacuzzi - you'll get stuff from the "waiting" room and throne room sometimes).

We go back there some with a friend who is 40th level. Most of us get no experience, but the people around 40th clean up. And, it's worth a shot at that sweet cloak!
