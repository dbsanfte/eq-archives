I posted the same message on the Haste vs AC topic (was a little off topic ).

Well there is a level damage bonus who influences the average hit - only depending on your level and the weapon you use. Str bonus is a different thing.

As you noticed you often hit for the same value (for me it is 47 with the mithrill 2 hander) ... this value is = (2* base_dam) + lvl_dam

From level 1 to level 30 your lvl_dam is 1 ... but it increases with your level: at 42 I have a 5 lvl_dam and my friend level 50 as 7 or 8 I don't remember exactly.

This is shown when you perform a crit:
You perform a critical hit!(124)
You slash a Mob for 129 damage.

If you dual wield fast weapons, this lvl_dam applies to each blow (even if you may still perform a 1 dam hit, you are more likely to do the 2 * base + lvl_dam)

Added to this there is a strength bonus appearing around level 20 wich is relative to your weapon:
I often hit for near 80 with the M2H wich means a 80-47=33 str bonus, but I never saw a 21+33=54 damage with the Ykesha unless it is a critical hit.

I have not the haste sash yet so I'll wait before trying to test it with parsers. I am very happy with the Mithrill 2 hander for now.

Not too sure about this lvl-dam bonus. Fighting a tough mob I get plenty of low hits, single digit. But that may be because of the difference in level between us.

Completely unscientific testing, but I swapped my YK RF and FBSS with a guildmates Mith 2H (no TBB), while we were fighting froggies in LGuk. There was one level differnce between us and we kept it this way for about an hour.

My overall impression of Mith 2H was that it is very fast, but after using a Exe axe for so long, damage seemed low. By this I mean Mith with 21 base dam, did not seem to be doing anywhere near Exe axe with 25 base dam. In fact I was surprised a lot of the hits were on average only 10 points or so better than a Runed Falchion with 11 base dam.

Also there was still the problem that missing or a low hit with a 2H is always more noticable and affects your taunting ability more than with fast dual wield. My friend using my set up could certainly keep the mob on him a lot more than I could. After an hour I was getting so jealous of his constant stream of decent hits that I said "Thanks for letting me try Mith, can I have my stuff back please!"

As I say this was all done by 'feel', no parser or anything, but I came away much less impressed with Mith 2H and he came away very interested in switching to dual wield. Of course this may have been different if I had been able to use an all out str set up with Mith, but I am not even convinced of that at this stage.
