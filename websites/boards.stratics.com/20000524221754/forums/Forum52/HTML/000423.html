I really think that if you try to make a guild that determines prices, you're asking for endless arguments. A fair price to one person might not be fair to another; one reason is that people have different skill levels. So it might cost 3 times the materials for a younger tailor to make an item than it would for a master in the trade. When I was younger and wasting more HQ bear pelts, I charged 100pp per backpack. As I got better, I now charge 80pp. Sometimes as a favor I'll do it dirt cheap for people. And then figure in the differing prices throughout older/newer servers, etc., and the fact that some people want to be capitalist traders and some are more financially socialist... it's gonna be a hassle, believe me.

A general trade guild though, is a good idea, I think. A guild of various trade folk who, by the tag at the end of their name, are known to people to be tradesfolk. You can approach the person about an item you need, and know that even if they don't turn out to be a tailor, they must know a few, and then that person will use guildchat to say 'hey, tailors in the freeport area, i've got a Wu's Gauntlets customer by name of So-and-so for you.' It would work well as a network. If I were making such a guild though, I'd keep a pretty laissez-faire attitude about pricing policies, and focus more on people being generally fair with one another and helping each other out with info and referrals.

I'd thought of doing such a thing myself, but haven't wanted to put the time into it. Good luck...
