It was a dark, rainy day in the Oasis of Marr. I'd just recovered from a fight with a Crocodile, and was inspecting my Langseax of the Wolves, making sure I hadn't nicked my precious little baby. I, being of the 12th Blade, was insulted by an Inkie wizard of the 12th spell. He claimed he could defeat me in a duel of honor. Being a dwarf, I gladly accepted.

Everyone came to watch. He was collecting energy, while I was saying "Get up, you fool! Let's get this over with!" He simply ignored me, shielded himself, meditated a bit more, and said he was ready. Upon my acceptance of the duel, he immediately rooted me. Scum! He was playing cheap. A blast of fire surrounded my body and combusted around it, searing off my leather tunic (now everyone could see my buff upper-body). The magical vines around my feet broke, and I charged into the wizard as he was chanting a spell. Boom! My Blackened Iron Boots gave him a hard, swift kick into the balls, obviously interrupting his casting. I prepared to hack large pieces out of his frail body. He was at about half life when he rooted me, and another fireball singed my beard, while yet another nearly knocked me unconcious. I was down to half life. The magical vines around my feet once again broke, and his balls once again met my boots. I hacked away at him while he ran. He managed to Shadowstep, and teleported a short distance away. I searched for him, around the dock, at the gypsie camp, and at the rock pillar in the water. I'd assumed he was meditating, regaining lost energy. I was hurt, so I sat down and began to recollect on his weak points. Suddenly out of the blue, sickles of ice were jutted into my back. I pulled them out, and saw the wizard chanting another spell. I threw a shuriken into his balls, thus interrupting his casting. He was less than a quarter life. This time he would be mine. Again my faithful Langseax of the Wolves hacked into his body, my Blackened Iron Boots repeatedly kicking his balls. At least, he was unconcious and lying there incapacitated. He said, in a half-alive voice, "It appears...you...may...have....won...."

"I guess I have" I said. I raised my Langseax of the Wolves, and chopped him in half. That was the end of the inkie wizard, who doubted the power of the Blade, and put his confidence into his feeble Magik.

Well duels with wizards are usually very interesting. With the changes in PvP where wizards spell only do 66% damage on you most wizards should barely have enough manna to kill an equal level warrior. This is if none of their spells are resisted or if they don't waste a lot of manna trying to root you. The point is that if you resist a couple of spells (I love resisting the all or nothing spells like force shock) then the wizard will need to med sometime during the battle.

The points above about having a bow is also very very useful. One of the best PvP bows IMO in the Carved Elm Silk Bow. The damage is not great, but it has a delay of 39 which makes it the fastest bow in the game. The main use of a bow in my experience has been to shoot arrows at casters to try and interrupt them when you are rooted. Here the prime concern is firing quickly not doing tons of damage.

Also another tip is to dual weild some of the fastest weapons that you can find. Unlike NPCs, PC casters are almost always interrupted when you hit them. In order to really interrupt casters I like to use two weapons with a mismatched delay. For example a langseax and maybe a Barbed Leather Whip. These weapons won't swing at the same time so you will have a more constant output of swings and combined with kick or slam you will have a really good chance to interrupt.
