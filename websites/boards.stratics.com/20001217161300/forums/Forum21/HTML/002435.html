With the servers the way they are (crowded) I find the best places to go are thoose generally considered the worst. Avoid like the plague Oasis, S. Karana, Unrest, High Keep, High hold pass, and any other highly recommended zones. I have bad Mistmoore experiences to, but some like it.

That being said, Solusek's Eye is by far the best place from about 22 to 30. I hear the cringing now, but trust me. There are never more than 12 people in the zone, and half are high levels camping items. The biggest problem is finding a group, but even a 22ish warrior can solo youngs if you pull them to the "rug room" by the Sol B zone one at a time (a trick, but possible). This is not an efficient strategy to say the least, but its a decent way of killing time until a partner shows up (any sort of a healer, even a dranger and you're set) and you can kill stuff in earnest. After 25 or so you can move on to camping the foreman and predator for good "named" loot and decent exp. My level 22 ranger (in a good group) made level 23 in less than 4 hours there, and had about 150 plat on him from selling fine steel weapons. Oh did I mention that any weapon you see, unless on a young goblin, is fine steel?

Of course there are drawbacks, the biggest being the lava. However, 90% of the zone has no lava in it. NEVER FIGHT NEAR LAVA. As a corollary, never pull to the Lavastorm entrance. ACtually its best to get to know the zone before you plan on killing stuff there (always a wise most). Fortunately, if you're a dwarf you're not going to be attacked by the goblins there, at least not at first. Go in and look around. Stay FAR away from any lava. Find the "safe zones" where nothin spawns and the zone boundaries to Sol B. If you're not a dwarf, make a level 1 dwarf and scout the place out.

Other choices, if Sol A isnb't to your taste (and perhaps more appropriate if below level 22) are Runnyeye (a party of 3-4 20s can get decent exp and loot breaking and holding the warlord room) which has the advantage of a nearby bind point, Najena - tentacle terrors are not a bad choice, the flag room in permafrost (dangerous but fun), maybe mammoths in Everfrost? I've heard good things about post Rubicite Cazic too, but haven't been there myself. Upper Guk is another one I haven't been to myself, but I hear isn't too crowded.

Basically get off the beaten path. Go places that no one goes to cause they "suck". A crappy zone with just the 6 members of your group in it is WAY better than a great zone with 80 people.

I hope no one reads this message
