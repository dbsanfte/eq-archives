Does anyone else have a problem with the upcoming looting "fix"?

I know that some people were shouting to the Heavens about being looted, but personally I found the looting system to be a good thing.

First, it served the purpose of making death an unpleasant experience...something to be avoided at all costs. Sure, there is still the vitae penalty. Big deal. I won't die often enough for vitae to have much of an impact. Then again, if I don't have to worry about losing my prized possessions, then I just might be significantly less cautious. What have I got to lose?

The second - and most important - aspect of looting was community. Sure, there were people who would steal from the dead. But at the same time there were plenty of people, myself included, who would go out of their way to see to it that people got their stuff back. I've made many friends in Dereth this way, and I know several others have as well. Roleplaying in AC is possible, but this is going to shut down yet another mechanism in the game that provided an oppourtunity. What good does it do me to play an honorable character if there are no moral decisions to be made?

Maybe a lot of people are thinking that the end of looting is good for everyone save the looters...but for me the game is going to be far less enjoyable when I am rendered incapable of performing good deeds.

I dislike looters as much as the next person...it is too bad that in a little while we will be no better than they.
