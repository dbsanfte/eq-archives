I like the idea of letting a virtual world evolve to the dictates of its residents. In other words, to let a social order develop to meet the "needs" of its residents.

The PKer mob thing is fun, I'm sure (if you're into that), but I think any player-run government that forms should be established by some sort of compact--an agreement of purpose or services. I think I saw on one of the community news announcements that there was some sort of governmental organization that purported to provide "safe passage" services from one city to a next. I like this idea. Perhaps we could have more organizations like this. This organization--though not strictly governmental in its nature--does serve as a sort of "patroling" or "policing" function. Perhaps if any solid player-run governments decide to have a go at it, they could offer policing/protection as one of their core services.

Another idea would be more merchant-based. Now, I don't really support the growth of player-merchants as a means-to-an-ends in virtual worlds. In fact, I think it's a corrupting influence (see http://boards.stratics.com/forums/Forum71/HTML/001226.html for a more thorough descriptionof my views on materialism in online worlds). But if such a thing is inevitable, then I could see the merchant class becoming the driving force for some sort of regulatory agency (e.g., a merchant guild). Maybe a government could grow out of that sort of agency.

Basically, I think that player-run governments are a good thing--as long as they exist to meet a need and aren't simply established for the sake of having a government.

Why _should_ there be a government?

The people of Dereth all come from Monarchies on Ispar, and they've come to Dereth as indiviuals. Even the factions of the various cultures are in total disarray, with all sense of order and chain of command lost in the transition.

The only organizations that exist are those that have been carved out on this side of the Portals.

While it is true that RL monarchies would need to be able to control territory to provide for their subjects, this _is_ a fantasy game. There are certain points you need to take into consideration. Land control isn't neccessary because no one in Dereth needs to _eat_...if you don't need food, you don't need land, or the peasant classes.

On top of that, with the exception of Darktide the only threat is that of the monsters. Instead of the normal requirements of food, water, and shelter, the only need of the people is strength.

As anyone who has ever been in an effective Monarchy...or even Fellowship...knows, the strength of a group far exceeds that of the individual. Therefore the Monarchies in Dereth are based on a power structure, for the most part...people organizing to be stronger as a whole rather than facing the hazards of the land alone.

Of course, I have to disagree with the statement that Monarchies are nothing more than status symbols and organized "twinking". For the above reasons, they make a lot of sense, but more importantly they create and group of like minded people who can trust one another, with a ranking system to give the unit more cohesion and direction. A formal Alliegance protects the members from the few nasty things that people on Dereth can do to one another - stealing, harrassing, and generally being a jerk.

If you are in an Alliegance, you have a group of people who you can trust in battle, in trade, and to aid you without having an angle. Not all Alliegances work that way, but the ones that _aren't_ just for status and twinking tend to.

I guess I'd have to say that Dereth does have governments. Many little ones at the moment. My guess is that some of them will eventually become virtual nations, possibly consisting of large segments of an entire World population. There are already Monarchs with several hundred followers, out of a few thousand players on each world.

As far as law enforcement goes, such a massive Alliegance could indeed deal with disruptive people. No, you can't be killed or imprisioned. But you can be exiled and ostracised. It would be a rather Twilight Zone-ish punishment to be squelched by the majority of a World. How long would someone continue to play a characer in a MMORPG when suddenly you are a ghost? No one can hear you, no one seems to see you, and they just walk right through you. Even if they have the psychological make-up to shrug off that sort of thing, the end result is the same...you remove the offender from society.
