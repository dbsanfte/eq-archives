Brad, since you obviously follow these threads, let me address you directly.

First, I understand and respect that game balance is a very detailed and touchy process. I am a developer as well and I know one of the things you never, ever do is tip your hand as to what the new product will or won't do before you know if it'll "work" or not. However, the rumor is that 55 is it, and it is prevalent enough to be plausible.

I must say I find this to be a mistake if true. Seems to me the strategy of the company would be to keep your huge base interested far into the future. The older servers are filling up with 40+s now and lots of the lower level chars are higher ups' second or third characters. I am two bubbs from 49 and already wondering what there will be to do when i get my last circle of spells. Throwing a few new higher level zones at us now and then is very nice and appreciated, believe me (way to go on Kithi, by the way ) but this only puts a band aid on the problem really. The new continent won't help us out a great deal either since by definition most of the zones will be geared toward newbies or mid-level chars. The EQ community is growing too big for its skin in a sense, and rather than stretch the old skin 10% (i.e. five more levels) we need room to grow beyond, or at least have something to look forward to. I would love to see the level limit shoved out to 80 or so and once again be a "mid-level" player with months and months of playing and discovery to look forward to. If you have to make new planes with insanely high-level mobs to accomodate us eventually, so be it. If players could eventually solo dragons or gods in Fear or Hate, so what. Make bigger and more powerful dragons and gods. Make loot so incredibly cool that level 60's consider POF stuff or Nagafen stuff "newbie" loot. I believe the quickest way to kill off a system is to limit its potential for growth. Having only five more levels to look forward to (with maybe one more spell rank at 54 or whatever) will keep us occupied for a couple of months, but I guarantee you that as hard as it will be to say goodbye to EQ, a lot of us will go to greener pastures.

Again, I'm not trying to presume what your decision is or will be. For all I know you haven't decided yourselves yet. I'm just trying to echo what a lot of us have been privately saying within the game as we sit around wondering what's new that we can do

Even if the level limit is raised, I'm not sure it is necessary other than to appease those who've made 50 already and bring them back to playing their characters instead of their alts. I can't exactly pretend to be Brad, but I believe I understand his mindset. At high levels, the game switches away from group oriented to a larger scale of things--Guild Plane excursions, Nagafen raids, etc.

Reading up on your history of Norrath, you will remember that the Combine Empire was destroyed when the people at the time grew too knowledgeable and powerful, and the Dragons became jealous and destroyed them all. As servers develop more and more high level players, I forsee a lot more GM events helping migrate Norrath into some really cool directions. For example, and it may not be soon, but The Ice Continent of Velious was where Veeshan laid her Eggs. We can assume Nagafen and Vox are maybe just down here to keep an eye on things. How would you handle a whole continent full of Dragons? It's not about levels..it's about groups of people...large large groups like way back on the MUD days when people would gather for a weekend all the high levels and go someplace really rough. Imagine rough places that are 10 x 20 x times worse than plane of Fear. As servers develop more high levels, this is probably the direction the game will go, isntead of raising the level limits.

There is nothing wrong with reaching the peak of character development as far as level. It isn't "Game Over" when you ding 50th. In fact, on most MUDs, the game more or less started at 50th. I can't wait to hit 50th on EQ, because I'll no longer feel pressure or that I'm "wasting time" when I'm not getting exp. I'll be able to "just play" instead of the endless levelquest.
