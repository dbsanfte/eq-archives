Nope, no need to kick yourself. I haven't checked lately, but I probably have 60 or so practice points gathering dust right now. You got pretty good use out of yours. Trade skills are about all you can use them for that will do you any good. Yeah, it only cost coppers, but you saved a bunch by giving yourself a head start. When I first started, I put most of mine into sense heading, which was stupid. Basically, you get all the training points you will ever need by second level. Put 1 into kick, 1 into bash, 1 into taunt, and save 4 for parry, ripost, dodge, and disarm. the other 3 you can do with as you please. But seriously, bind wounds is a good one...I find it hard to gain any skill in it. I will start to bind, and be full health before I've done it once because the cleric healed me. But it is very useful at lower levels when you actually do solo.


As for the 2H blunt, get yourself a fine steel great staff and use it whenever you can. It has a delay of 36, which will give you more skill checks, and should raise your skill up more quickly. I use FS daggers to raise piercing skill because they are so fast. Once I have the skill maxxed, I move to the better weapons.

Hope this helps

So I'm wondering if there's something I should be saving these practice points for? Am I going to be kicking myself at level 35 because I wasted a bunch of practice points on 2H blunt?

Highly doubtful. Heck, half the time I forget about them entirely. As Darodar said, you only need a few points, namely enough to learn the following skills:

Taunt
Bash
Dodge
Dual Wield
Double Attack
Parry
Riposte
Disarm

These are all the skills which require the investment of one point to activate. So that's 8 points.

How you spend the rest is entirely up to you. Personally, I put 10 into Dual Wield and 10 into Double attack. I think I put 15 into Riposte. The rest I spent on trade skills and learning languages.
