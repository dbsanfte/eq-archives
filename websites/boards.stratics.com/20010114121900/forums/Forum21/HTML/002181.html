The comment about Rangers being over-powered compared to other tanks wasn't meant to suggest that Rangers are over-powered in melee.

It was meant to suggest that the Ranger utility spells are 10x better than the utility spells that the other hybrids get and that Ranger tanking ability prior to the recent patch was way too close to SK and Paladin to justify the difference in the effectiveness of their spells.

For reference these are the spells that Rangers get that any class that can't cast them would give eyeteeth for:

Snare (the best slowing device against runners in the game, Rangers get this at L9)

Camouflage and later Superior (outdoors only but functions just fine until it wears off indoors allowing infiltration options that are surpassed only by ShadowKnights)

Feet Like Cat (best low level stat buff in the game, eventually adds up to 18 to AGI and makes magic numbers a LOT easier to reach)

Enduring Breath (allows underwater adventuring, Sk's get something similar late in the game)

Harmony (best outdoor lull type spell and AoE makes soloing outdoors much easier)

Bind Sight (want to scout a dungeon by remote control? You can get pretty deep in before you run out of mobs to bind to)

Shield of Barbs (excellent damage shield even at later levels, cast this on the hittee and the hitter drops hp much faster)

Stinging Swarm (300 pts of DoT is nothing to sneer at as an additional damage source on a melee character, particularly when you pull with it)

Strength of Earth (another decent low level buff can really help on STR)

Levitate (oh, we can run on water now can we)

Spirit of the Wolf (Yah, they get the best spell in the game also) Of course they're about to get...

Wolf Form (so SoW becomes less of a bonus).

This is a disgusting assortment of utility spells for a primarily melee character to get. The fact that for a period of time they were arguably the second best tank also was just ludicrous.

That's what I meant about Rangers being over-powered compared to other melee classes. Verant had melded a reasonable strong tank with most of the really good Druid utility spells and in the process created an extraordinary Frankenstein monster from the perspective of other melee classes.

Don't even get me started on what Rangers look like from the perspective of a Rogue.

Tavish and Temil, relax and go to your separate corners.

This debate has been hashed and rehashed for months now, and we're all sick of it.

Simple fact: Ranger, Shadowknight, and Paladin spell lists are roughly equal in value. Period. They all have their strengths and weaknesses.

Simple fact: Pre patch, a Ranger had just as many hitpoints as a same race paladin or shadowknight, and could bring their own armor class extremely close to a shadowknight's or paladin's. This despite the fact that they cannot wear plate.

Simple fact: A ranger can outdamage a shadowknight or paladin by a good margin using the same weapons warriors use to outdamage us. Dual Ykeshas and a FBSS.

Given that rangers had equavelent spell lists to other hybrids, equavelent hitpoints, nearly equavelent armor class, and much higher damage output, many questioned why anyone would play a shadowknight or paladin.

Verant took a look at this and realized that, because many of the highest armor class items in the game (Azure sleeves, Skull Shaped Barbutes, Crested Spaulders, etc) can be worn by rangers as well as the plate wearing classes, their vision of Rangers as being the higher damage/lower defense hybrid was not entirly correct. Rangers were the higher damage/same defense hybrid.

They corrected this by improving Paladins and Shadowknights. Now rangers do not tank as well as paladins and SKs, but do more damage. This gives people a reason to choose a paladin or shadowknight over a ranger again (they want to tank more than to do damage).

That's the end of that story.

Now, as for spells:

Both rangers and shadowknights have the same problems with their damage spells. In both cases, the time required to cast the spell will often cause them to loose enough swings in combat that they would have been better off not casting in the first place.

Think about a Shadowknight casting Heat Blood during battle. Heat blood does 160 damage over 60 seconds. 16 damage per "tick". Given that most combat on a single mob is completed within 30 seconds (or less depending on how many casters you have in your group), you're looking at a total of possibly 40-80 damage done by this spell. That's one solid hit by my sword. I loose anywhere from 2-6 swings during the casting of Heat Blood. (Depending on timing, haste effect, and double attacks).

People who say that Shadowknights can outdamage rangers are looking at stats and paper too much and are not playing the game. Shadowknight damage spells are less effective than swinging our weapon. Ranger damage spells are less effective than swinging their weapon. Rangers, through dual wield, are able to use the best weapons in the game (Short Sword of Ykesha) to the best of it's potential. (Pallies and SKs can use them, but can't dual wield them). This point is conceded by Verant and is beyond debate. Now, the debate over whether Paladins and Shadowknights are better tanks has been settled by Verant and is also beyond debate.

Rangers do more damage. Paladins/Shadowknights tank better. Warrior do more damage AND tank better than all 3. Rangers, paladins, and shadowknights have roughly balanced spell lists, warrior have no spells. All 4 classes suck compared to casting classes, but hey, whatever, I can stand in and tanks one of those friggin ninja monk mummies up in Plane of Hate (quad attacking for 158 and flying kicking for more...). Those robes go down in about 3 combat rounds. ;-)
