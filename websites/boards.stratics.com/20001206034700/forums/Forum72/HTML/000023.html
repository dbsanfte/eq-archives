Hmm, Well the reason I put Sho on the continent with the others is for the following reasons:
Sho History Vol. 1
"The most ancient legends tell how the Sho warred with the Dragon and were driven to the edge of the sea. There, the family of Shou-Jen established a kingdom and named it after the Dragon (Ryujii) to placate the Elder. However, rival families instead founded their own kingdoms upon islands named after the Firebird (Iiwah) and the Unicorn (Chiran-tou)."

What the above quote tells me is that the Sho were driven to the edge of the continent they live on (which I believe is the same that the other heritage groups live on - see below) and inhabited two nearby islands.

Sho History Vol. 1
"Meanwhile, trade has begun in earnest with the Gharu'ndim and the Milantos, the two nearest kingdoms, and the Sho are growing increasingly aware of the vast lands beyond their borders."

This tells me that since the Gharu'n and Milantos are neighbors on the same continent, sharing a mountainous border to the east of Gharu'n. And that if the Sho are approximately equidistance from both and they were pushed to the edge of a continent by their god. And finally for the Sho to look across borders means a land connection instead of looking across the sea which would indicate an ocean like you propose. With that information it is not a far leap to perhaps see that the Sho are on the same continent as my map projects.

Now as for size. Note I drew no scale on the map. But in my mind I see the map drawn to be the size of the Terran Continent of Eurasia with a bit of Northern Africa thrown in, I see it as very large. But you must realize to even show it here requires certain "sizing" but I appreciate thy concern.
And last but not least, one must realize that by looking at ancient medieval maps of the world scale was a matter of faith and politics, not geographical accuracy.
I am sure as the "powers that be" reveal more information about cities and other features of Ispar, the map shall undergo changes.

M'lord Ashe your vision and mine are very similar, but I wish to address all your points so that we can come together for a good map.

First the reason the Roulean are West of the Gharu'n instead is the simple fact that Milantos is to the East already(1). And since the Gharu'n took almost over half of the land once controlled by the Empire of Roulea(2) they had to be neighbors. But since the south was occupied by the trackless Naqut Dessert(3), that left only the west or north. Since The great Poet Yasif was to sail to Milantos to avoid the mountains to the east(4) I placed the port city Mawwuz to the north and thus the position of the Ironsea. So this leaves West for Roulea.

As for increasing the distance between Viamont and Roulea, aye very doable, but let me explain my reasons of making them so close. The key reason is the idea that the Ironsea was very much like the Mediterranean Sea of Earth(at least in my mind). There is proof that the Rouleans were the first to establish trade around the Ironsea and thus establish a common time measurement and calendar as well as common trading tounge which all heritage groups use(5). With that and knowledge that the first naval vessels developed were probably small and had limited range, I figured the distance to reach the various other countries would be small. But heck we can stretch that distance out.

Now you mentioned about making Gharu'n "Africa", I tried to do that but since we know that for the Sho the Gharu'n and the Milantos are the closest neighbors (6) I figured the landmass would be without many boundaries be them mountains or water, just distance. So I basically vanished the "Indian Ocean" and thus linked the deserts of Africa and Arabia with the plains of China, skipping the massive mountains of Tibet and Pakistan.

Which brings me to the following quandry. Where are the Silverans and the Souia-Vey?
My idea is to make the Souia-Vey kind of like the mongul horde that roams the plains between the Sho and the other heritage groups. But the Silverans?
Hmmm how about this idea. Make Gharu'n much like Saudi Arabia and the Rouleans ruling the Mediterranean coast of Africa. The Silveran would rule the southern tip of Africa with trackless jungles seperating the Rouleans and the Gharu'n from the Silverans.
Hmm let me redraw the map and post it with those ideas.
