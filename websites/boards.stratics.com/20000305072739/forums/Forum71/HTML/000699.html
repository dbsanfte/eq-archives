I imagine they cut down on the loot so that money would not be so easy to accumulate. However, if I decide to take on a creature with a sword and I get damaged (arguably more) from that sword, I expect to rip that sword from the creature's filty claws when I put it to its deserved death!

It stinks of shooters, doesn't it? You kill a bad guy, and he's carrying some ammo but no weapons...hmmm...

You would think that they would have just implemented some simple economic fixes...if the market is being flooded with weapons, make it so they aren't worth much unless they are exceptional in some way. Sure, it would also make common weapons more affordable...but no one ever buys them anyway. The less common items would still be just as expensive.

At the same time, why not just tweak encumbrace so that it is more realistic? Weapons might not be very heavy, but it would be next to impossible to fight effectively with a half-dozen spears, bows, axes and swords strapped on. They already take this into account by making spears and axes more encumbering than a short sword, for instance...why not just crank it up another notch and make it more realistic while helping fix the money surplus?

Either way fixes they problem - either the weapons are worth less, or you can't carry as many...the end result is the same.

The fact that they chose to implement such a silly fix bothers me...Are they under so much schedule pressure that this was the best solution they could think of? I know they have milestones to meet and pebble-counters to satisfy, but the quality of the game is measured by the little nuances like this as much as it is by the flashy features.

I agree with most of you; the way the loot is set up now is unrealistic and difficult.

I personally liked the loot the way it was before, where 90% of the time you got the weapon from what you killed.

Many argue that it is too easy to accumulate wealth that way, but there is a purpose to having extra pyreals.

Mages need new components. Archers need new arrows. Warriors need new healing kits.

And if you have enough pyreals, potential vassals are always asking for a little hand out.

Good stuff ain't cheap, and saving ALL the pyreals you get comes in handy. While it was a bit easier with nearly always getting the weapon off a creatures body, the pyreals didn't go to waste. Unless you had a SoLL, you were always on the lookout for a better blade, or unless you were wearing green mire, you were always looking for better armor. And when you couldn't ID that item, and had the spare pyreals, you bought it and tested it out.

Microsoft broke one of the most imporant game developers rules:

If it ain't broken, don't fix it.

Perhaps I only see things like this because I am always strapped for cash lately, but I know of many others who feel the same way.
