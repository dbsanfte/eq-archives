Regarding Isobel's thoughtful insight on Russian torture in the Middle Ages, I recalled two tortures from Charles Dickens "A Tale of Two Cities" that were actually used in the 1700's.

Draw and Quater: A man is stretched with each hand and foot tied to a horse and pulled in seperate directions. Then he is slowly cut open and hot coals are placed in his intestinal tract. Finally, all his limbs are hacked off and he bleeds to death. Fun, no?

The other unpleasentness happened to a poor peasant farmboy who passed 50-60 feet from a dirty procession of monks and failed to bow. He was tied up and holes were drilled into his arms and legs straight through the bone. Molten lead was then poured into each hole. He died of exhaustion. Screamed himself to death.

Amazing what you can learn in English II.
