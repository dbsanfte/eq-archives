That's what I did. Using the formula (DW skill + level)/600 gives you about 41 to 42 percent chance to dual wield. Or, a multiplier of .41 or .42. But you also have a chance to DA based on the formula (DA skill + level)/500, which at fifty is 50% or a .5 multiplier.

So, when you do succesfully dual wield, you will automatically get 1 attack and have a 50% (at level 50) of getting a second attack. So, if you add 1 and .5 (your DA multiplier) and multiply that by the modal damage for that weapon(ignoring the level bonus) that will give you the total damage you do with that weapon (provided you DWW all the time). However you do not DW all the time, so you must multiply this result by your DW multiplier. Then just divide this by the weapons delay to get the total damage per delay (or divide it by 10 as well to get the damage per second).

Now, you have an estimate of the total damage per delay (or per sec) that your off hand will do. Now, if you add that to the total damage pe delay (or per sec) of your primary hand weapon, you will have your overall total damage per delay (or per sec).

Also, could someone please let me know how to post the spreadsheet I made (all I really need is to post a link) and I will put it up here for everyoe to use. Also, a few notes on it.

1) it only factors DA into the offhand weapons damage when that the DW skill is 150 or higher.

2) I used 1's and 2's to indicate whether a weapon is the primary (or 2hander) or the secondary, respectively.

3) it calculates the level damage bonus before the damage, and assumes the bonus is zero if the persons level is 25 or lower or if the weapon is in the offhand. It also rounds to the nearest integer value.

4) the modal damage is calculated the same for all weapons. It simply multiplies the weapons damage by 2 and adds the level bonus to this result. This works because, the damage bonus uses a series of ifs to determine if a bonus should indeed be calculated, otherwise it's just 0.

5) then to calculate the average damage done each time the weapon cycles, again using ifs, if the hand column equals 1 (for that weapon), it multiplies the modal damage by (1 + the DA chance (which is .5 at 50)). If the hand column (for that weapon) equals 2 (or any other number than 1 really) the it checks to see if the DW skill is 150 or higher. If it isn't the damage is calcculated by multiplying the modal damage by the chance to DW (about .41 at 50). However, if the DW skill is 150 or higher, the damage is calculated by multipying the modal damage by the chance to DW and then multipying that by (1 + the chance to dual wield).

6) finally the average damage per delay is calculated by dividing the average damage per weapon cycle by the delay of the weapon. Also, this column can be easily modified to divide by the (delay * 10) to find the average damage per second.
