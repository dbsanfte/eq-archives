I guess you could call it roleplaying...

I have musicianship with none of the three bardic skills, and I am a begger. So, prior to begging I play my tamborine each time, and while the music is still going I "beg". To me this is more bard-oriented than regular begging.

It would be cool to have a modification to the game so your fame:karma wouldn't go down (and possibly be slightly favorably affected) if you play for your gold. The "grovel" message would be replaced by "From whom do you seek a donation?". Your music would have to actually be going at the time, and it would have to have high enough musician skill so as not to offend your customer.

Beggar/Bard == Musician

I decided to put the proposal in a more detailed post (crossposted to Bard and Beggar boards).

Basically, a musician should be able to earn some money with his talent. UO currently has almost all of the elements necessary to allow this. It just needs to tie together the beggar and musicianship skills.

Currently, a succesful beg can get about 10% of the gold being carried by an npc (up to 10 gp). Multiple begs per npc are allowed, and I've seen reports where up to 400gp can be obtained from a single noble or seeker of adventure. (That's persistence -- 40 beg attempts minimum!) There is a slight karma drop due to begging, but it will not make your karma go negative.

Also currently, musicianship in itself is not useful except as a base skill for entice, peace-making, and provoke.

My proposal merges these two skills into an honorable profession, musician. If the musician plays his instrument, and then begs while the music is going, then the beg attempt will not be "groveling", but instead be viewed as a request for a donation. There could be 10 extra seconds after music stops to allow for lag and players with mobility limitations.

Success of the donation request would be based on the players skill in begging. The quantity donated would be based on his skill as a musician. Only one donation per npc per UO day would be allowed. The npc could say something like "I'm sorry, I've already spent my extra gold for today" if he's already donated to a musician. Regular begging would still be allowed from the npc, however.

The amount donated would be up to 50% of the npc's gold. Simply take music skill and divide by two to determine what percentage of the npc's gold would be donated.

There could be a slight karma increase from using this talent. It would not be enough to get a char beyond the first tier of positive karma. But it would be high enough to overcome the negative effect that will occasionally happen when he begs when he meant to play.
