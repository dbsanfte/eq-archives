I decided that I would like to play a gnome warrior.

Welcome to the Gemchoppers. There's a ton of us around here. In fact, I think we outnumber everybody but the Barbs. (Me, Alewuf, Keryath, Caper, and others whose names I can't recall at the moment.)


I know they probably have the worst stats for it

You'll be pleased to know that stats don't mean a great deal in combat. Odd, but true. Welcome to Everquest.


Could those of you who have played a gnome warrior give me some advice placing my stat points?

If I had it all to do over again, I'd put 15 points into STR (to avoid encumberance) and 10 points into STA (for extra HP). As it is, I put 15 into STR and 10 into (get this) CHA.

Just make sure all your stats are over 75 and you'll be fine.

Of course now that I said that I'll probably get a dozen posts of "Wittle gnomies caint be warriors!

If you'd started posting here back in August, you would have indeed seen many posts of that nature. These days, however, the consensus seems to be that (A) stats don't mean much; and therefore (B) any race can be a good warrior.


So I need help on stat points, religion, and where to go once my Gnome has been created.

If you're a role player, pick the god that suits you. However, if you want the religion that gives you the biggest in-game advantage, be Agnostic. Agnostic Gnomes can go anywhere but Grobb and Oggok.

As for where to go, there are plenty of XP and loot opportunities on Faydwer. The Nybright sisters are a good source of bronze weapons during your teen levels, and there are several bandit camps throughout Faydwer.

I put all my bonus points into strength, just so that I could brag. This is a remarkable conversation stopper, viz:

Caper: "Hi! I'm Caper, the strongest Gnome in Norrath! What are you?"

Other: "I'm...I'm...I'm taller than you are."

I can't say I've regretted it. With a 95 strength I'm still often encumbered. As I go up in levels and get some good Strength items I'll probably not notice so much.

The real power of warriors is in our hit points, so sometimes I wish I'd added points to Stamina. Also, I do find myself getting stunned a lot. Doesn't Stamina help prevent that? Or am I creating rumors?

We Gnomes have some other advantages. We can't Slam like the big boys, but we get to wear small armor which weighs less. We get to max out all our weapons skills because we're so smart.

Also, being this small, it's much easier to hide. Thi is an important consideration if you're on a PvP server like I am. At the bank in Kelethin I'll turn off my guild tag, walk behind the counter and sit down to do my banking. No one sees me.
