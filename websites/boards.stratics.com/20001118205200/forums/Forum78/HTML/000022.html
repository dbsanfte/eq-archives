Your basic components are readily available in all starter towns. That includes just about every herb/regeant/powder. The more advanced (and more expensive) components such as higher level scarabs and tapers are found in non-starter towns. I've never heard of components being 'sold out' in the game. I haven't much noticed, but I'm quite sure the prices on components are set. Anywhere from 6 pyreal for the simplest herbs to 40-100 or so for tapers and on up to 1800 for the pyreal scarab (Not to worry, you won't be using that for a LONG time).

As far as component consumption, the higher level you get, the better you get at casting lower level spells and therefore burn less components. For instance, when you're reliably casting level 3 spells you're chance of burning components during a level 1 spell is farily rare. Conversely, casting a higher level spell (say level four) will burn components more easily. By far, the component most readily burned is the taper (unfortunately). The least burned seems to be the scarab.

By the time you get to casting level 3 spells readily you should probably be carrying 50 of the most often used component (onyx for war mages). Probably 20-30 would suffice for the spells you use regularly and 10-15 of those you scarcely use. Also, the better you get at casting level 1 spells, the fewer number of scarabs you'll need to carry (since you'll obviously be burning less).

I've found tapers and regeants to be the most burdensome component type. The others seem to weigh significantly less. With a little planning, you should not have to worry about running out of components. (Although I'm always running out of tapers, especially during spell research)

As for cash flow and components, the most I've spent on components at any one time was 4,000 pyreal, but expect to spend an average of 1,000 - 2,000 per gaming session. That's nothing when you can find a ring or bracelet worth 1,000 - 1,500 pyreal; usually once per outing if you're hunting in the right areas.

Obviously the higher level you get, the more you'll be spending thanks to the scarab and taper prices.
