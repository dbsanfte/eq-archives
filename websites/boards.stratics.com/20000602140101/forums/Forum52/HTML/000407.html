*Patchwork Leather*- BARD, CLERIC, DRUID, MONK, PALADIN, RANGER, ROGUE, SHADOW KNIGHT, SHAMAN, & WARRIOR

Ruined Pelt, Pattern and a Small Tailoring Kit to combine them in.

Wolf Pelts make Small Armor-(Dwarves, Wood Elves, High Elves, Dark Elves, Gnomes, Halflings)
Cat Pelts make Medium Armor-(Barbarians, All Elves, Erudites, Humans)
Bear Pelts make Large Armor-(Barbarians, Ogres, Trolls)

*Raw Silk*- WIZARD, MAGICIAN, ENCHANTER, NECROMANCER, and MONK

2 Spider Silks (NOT Spiderling) = 1 Silk Swatch

Items that use 1 silk swatch and a pattern:
Mask-AC 2
Headband (Cap)-AC 3
Collar (Gorget)-AC 2
Mantle (Shoulderpads)-AC 2
Cloak-AC 3
Sleeves-AC 3
Gloves-AC 3
Sandals (Boots)-AC 3
Sash (Belt)-AC 2

Items that use 2 silk swatches and a pattern:
Robe (Tunic)-AC 6
Leggings (Pants)-AC 4

*Cured Silk*- MONK ONLY
to make Heady Kiolas: combine 2 Kiola Saps, 1 Water Flask, and 1 Empty Bottle in a brewing barrel.
SS= Silk Swatch(es) HK= Heady Kiola(s) P=pattern
Mask- AC 2 (1 ss + 1 hk + p)
Headband- AC ? recipe unknown
Collar- AC ? recipe unknown
Mantle- AC 3 (2 ss + 2 HK + P)
Cloak- AC 4 (2 ss + 2 HK + p)
Sleeves- AC 4 (2 ss + 2 hk + P)
Gloves- AC 4 (2 ss + 2 HK + P)
Sandals- AC 4 (1 ss + 2 HK + P)
Sash- AC ? recipe unknown
Gi(tunic)- AC 8 (3 ss + 2 HK + P)
Leggings- AC 5 (2 ss + 3 hk + P)

*Studded Leather*- (upgrade of Patchwork Leather)
For Studded Leather you will need Metal Studs from Blacksmithing. To Make Studs, combine the following in a Forge: a File, a Water Flask, 1 Metal Bit, 1 Metal Bit, 1 Metal Bit (or put three metal bits in seperate slots of the forge)

Piece(s) that use(s) one Stud: Masks
2 Studs: Gorgets and Bracers
3 Studs: Caps, Shoulderpads, and Sleeves
4 Studs: Cloaks, Gloves, Pants, and Boots
5 Studs: Tunics

*Reinforced Leather*
(I forget how to make Steel Boning)
Pieces that need 1 Steel Boning: Mask, Gorget, & Bracers
2 Steel Boning: Cap, Shoulderpads, Cloak, Sleeves, & Gloves
3 Steel Boning: Boots & Pants
4 Steel Boning: Tunics

That's all the recipes for tailoring that I know of for now, good luck!

bob the naked has the best and most comprehensive tailoring guide I've ever read, though there is always more out there. The following is his guide cut and pasted from the everlore site.

I have edited a couple items with updated info. you will see a * where I fave made changes and a (note where I have added information

-Dorr

The first thing you need to know is how your wisdom and intelligence effect your skill. Intelligence effects how fast you learn your tailoring skill. So, keep in mind that with a high intelligence your going to learn tailoring faster and also spend less money. Wisdom effects how often you succeed when combining items. So, with a higher wisdom your going to fail less times when making an item.

When first starting tailoring, I wouldn’t put any training points in it to begin with. Just start at 0 because its easy to raise your skill, just spend your practice points somewhere else.

Trivial Levels

Silk Swatches - 15
Patchwork Armor – 26
Raw Silk Armor – 36
Studded Armor – 82 (not sure)
Cured Silk Armor – 82 (not sure)
Wu’s Gloves – 88*
Backpacks – 88*
Reinforced – 108 (Maxed) (not sure)

Just in case you don’t know, the trivial level is the level at which making an item will not raise your skill in tailoring. Even though something is trivial to you, you still will fail at making it at about 25 percent of the time. This can vary depending on your wisdom.

Size
The pelt you use determines the size of the armor.
Wolf Pelt = Small
Cat Pelt = Medium
Bear Pelt = Large

Recipes
All components are put in the sowing kit, then you hit combine and depending on your skill you get something new or you fail and get nothing. If you’re a good race you can get a large sewing kit at the South Ro Inn.

Silk Swatches = 2 Spider Silk (not spiderling)(don’t stack them either)

Patchwork = Ruined Pelt (Wolf, Cat or Bear depending on size) + Pattern

Raw Silk Mask = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern (Raw Silk fits all sizes)
Raw Silk Headband = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Collar = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Mantle = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Cloak = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Sleeves = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Gloves = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Sandals = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Sash = 1 Silk Swatch + Pattern
Raw Silk Tunic = 2 Silk Swatches + Pattern
Raw Silk Leggings = 2 Silk Swatches + Pattern

Cured Silk Mask = 1 Silk Swatch + 1 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Headband = 1 Silk Swatch + 3 Heady Kiola + Cap* Pattern
Cured Silk Collar = 1 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Mantle = 2 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Cloak = 2 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Sleeves = 2 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Gloves = 2 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Sandals = 1 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Pattern
Cured Silk Sash = 1 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Belt* Pattern
Cured Silk Gi = 3 Silk Swatch + 2 Heady Kiola + Tunic* Pattern
Cured Silk Leggings = 2 Silk Swatch + 3 Heady Kiola + Pattern

Wu’s Fighting Gauntlets
1 Silk Swatch + 4 Heady Kiola + Pattern + Greater lightstone + Vial of Viscous Mana
Viscous Mana = 1 Pearl + 1 Poison Vial +Thicken Mana Spell

Shadow Silk
1 Silk Swatch + Shadow Wolf Pelt + Scroll Gather Shadows + pattern

Inky Shadow Silk
1 Silk Swatch + Shadow Wolf Pelt + 2 Scroll Gather Shadows + pattern

Add extra shadow wolf pelts for large sizes

Backpacks = 1 High Quality Bear Skin + Pattern
Quivers = 1 High Quality Bear Skin + Pattern

Studded Belt = Medium Quality Pelt + 2 studs + Pattern
Studded Boots = Medium Quality Pelt + 4 studs + Pattern
Studded Bracer = Medium Quality Pelt + 2 studs + Pattern
Studded Cap = Medium Quality Pelt + 3 studs + Pattern
Studded Cloak = Medium Quality Pelt + 4 studs + Pattern
Studded Gloves = Medium Quality Pelt + 4 studs + Pattern
Studded Gorget = Medium Quality Pelt + 2 studs + Pattern
Studded Mask = Medium Quality Pelt + 1 stud + Pattern
Studded Pants = Medium Quality Pelt + 4 studs + Pattern
Studded Shoulderpad = Medium Quality Pelt + 3 studs + Pattern
Studded Sleeves = Medium Quality Pelt + 3 studs + Pattern
Studded Tunic = Medium Quality Pelt + 5 studs + Pattern
(note: some studded items appear to be broken, to see which read some of the other postings - Dorr)

Reinforced Leather Boots = High Quality Pelt + 3 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Bracers = High Quality Pelt + 1 Steel Bore + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Cap = High Quality Pelt + 2 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Cloak = High Quality Pelt + 2 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Gloves = High Quality Pelt + 2 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Gorget = High Quality Pelt + 1 Steel Bore + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Mask = High Quality Pelt + 1 Steel Bore + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Pants = High Quality Pelt + 3 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Shoulderpads = High Quality Pelt + 2 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Sleeves = High Quality Pelt + 2 Steel Bores + Pattern
Reinforced Leather Tunic = High Quality Pelt + 4 Steel Bores + Pattern

Brewing for tailors

Trivial Levels
Short Beer – 31
Heady Kiola – 46

Recipes
Short Beer = Barley + Hops + Malt + Cask
Heady Kiola = 2 Kiola Sap + Water + Bottle
Some Advice
I started tailoring when I was level 15, so this may be harder for you if you are lower level.

The best place to get your skill from 0 – 26 is in Everfrost Peaks. There is a place called 3 igloos. It’s a cross roads where polar bear cubs and white wolves go through all the time. Cubs and wolves have about 36 hit points and they almost always yield a ruined pelt. Also, the vendors at the ramp to
Halas, in this same zone, sell patterns. I went from 0 –26 in about 90 minutes.

The best place to get silk is in the east karanas. There is an abundance of crag and carrion spiders over here. Carrion spiders are level 12 or so, and crags are 18-20. I get a max of 4 silk from carrion spiders (sometimes you get none) and 8 from crag spiders, but you need a group for them. Also, check the vendors here for spider silk people have sold. I once bought 45 spider silk for 2 gp each from vendors.
(note: actually, the very best place to get silks is in upper Guk from heart spiders. Quick spawn and lots of silks. - Dorr)
