Hello, its an enchanter perusing the druid board, and i noticed alot of things on charming strangely enough. I am very adept at charming now, having used it to solo from 24 to 33 very easily in Unrest (not that you druids can, but are werebats considered animals?)
Ok, charisma helps the chance that a charm sticks, and is believed to lengthen its duration. While charming, i have 195 charisma (helps ALOT)

The higher charm spell has less of a resist rate, and has a higher time cap. The lower level spells have a cap of around a minute or two i believe, while the higher ones can last much much longer. Because of this, if you use a charmed pet in a fight, kill it at the end so it does not break in the middle of the next fight.

Always charm blues or greens. Whites wont last long, and yellow or reds is suicide, since the charm line has a large taunt (apparently they dont like being puppets) and will be difficult to pull off of you. And since you druids dont have mez or ae stuns, i guess it will be even harder.

Thats about it... charm is a good spell if used the right way. It can either be a life saver, or something that if unlucky, makes you die a WHOLE lot faster (hate those 2 second charms)
