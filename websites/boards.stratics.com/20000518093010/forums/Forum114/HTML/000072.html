Anywhere from 3k up seems reasonable. I'm currently selling for 5.5k but then it's a new shard and they're still moving at that price. I expect that I'll be charging my usual 3.5k before long.

Pricing always seems to be a big question in all of the professions. IMHO it always depends on how often you're willing to restock your vendor and how quickly your goods are moving. If they're selling at 4k when you check you're vendor then fine, if they're not consider lowering your prices.
The location of the shop and how well know it is always makes a big difference.

Something I like to do with full books is to take trade-ins. If you happen to be around when someone is purchasing they are often happy to swap their book for a full one at a reduced price. Saves on having to put in all that extra time and resources on the lower level scrolls and the customer feels like they're getting a good deal. People will usually pass over their spellbook for you to look at (amazingly) then once you agree a price you just reprice one on your vendor. I've traded 5 in the last week and both the customer and I have come away feeling we've got a good deal. I suspose people are willing to hand over their book because my vendor is obviously selling eighth circle scrolls - I doubt that many would be willing to do it if there are only spellbooks for sale and no scrolls - the obvious empty book for sale.

One last thing on earning trust selling books -- I always label mine as "A Full Spellbook (use item id to check)" - hopefully I can help educate people not to buy empty books at full book prices. The usual price for a full spellbook using item id is around 2,300gp.
