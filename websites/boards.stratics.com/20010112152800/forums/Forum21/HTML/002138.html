Our Bind Wound caps at 100 skill. This translates to 25 hp per bandage. At what point does it become pointless to lug bandages around? Tonight Mrol used 18 bandages on one recovery. How many slots can one really devote to bandages? Eventually you just run out of room to carry these things around, and that becomes the limiting factor on the skill's utility. Eventually the warriors just wait around hurt until some healer class performs a mana dump to fix him up.

I'd like to see the Warrior's Bind Wound skill cap raised to make the skill inventory space effective. Group will benifit as the healer types will blow less mana healing their tanks.

I always carry at least 40 bandages with me. The 2 slots these take up in one of my backpacks are inconsequential. If i run out of space because of too much loot right before i go sell, i just destroy a stack of bandages to make room for fs or whatever.

I never know when i have to use the bandages. Often, i can go weeks without using one, but when i need to, boy, am i glad i have them. The most common times i use the bandages are after huge fights which leave all the healers oom, and players with dangerously low health. That or when i fight a train at zone and have to zone due to low health. I quickly (relatively) bandage up and zone back in to help my groupmates finish the mobs that are left. Although it hasn't happened in a while, bandages are also extremely helpful if you're at low health and are dotted or poisoned. Then, they can save your life.

I usually never leave home without a full back pack of bandages. Bandages are not really cheap but they are very useful in alot of situations.

For example, when grouping with mojoo users that can convert their HPs into Mana, you can bandage them while they they convert their HPs. This greatly reduces downtime.

Using this tactic with a shaman in a group, the shaman can fully buff up the whole party, convert HPs while I bandage. Toss a regen on themselves and we will be ready to fight again within a few minutes FoM and fully buffed.
