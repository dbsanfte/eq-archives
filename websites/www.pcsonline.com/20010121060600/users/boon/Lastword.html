Trying to find the words...
Ok, now that he can't hear me, let me try to tell you what I have learned from this experience. There really is no way to describe what the birth of a child, especially your own child is like. Everyone told me how great it was, but words just can't describe it, I was completely unprepared for the experience and the emotions. 

I can say this, and maybe it will help folks to if not understand the experience for themselves, it will at least illustrate one of my feelings about it. 

Everyone in my life that I love or have loved, people, animals, souls took some time before I loved them. Time to get to know them, time to develop a relationship and time to trust and gain trust. Even though I am an Aquarian, and prone to love quickly, unconditionally and forever, nothing compares to the instaneous, unconditional and infinate love that I feel for my son. Jamie says the same thing. Maybe this is the one thing I can say that might illustrate a bit of the feelings I have. I have heard of some parents that say they didn't feel this right away, and I can't imagine it.

The delivery itself was sureal. Even though it was just 4 of us in there, I felt others. Maybe past loved ones, or loved ones yet to be, but we weren't alone. Days before the delivery I felt someone put a reassuring hand on my spiritual shoulder late one night as I sat contemplating the upcoming event. As I think back about the delivery, it brings images of sailing through a storm, and working so hard to keep everything in control and to stay on course that I was blind to much of what was going on. I don't care how much science, medicine and technology invade and assist our lives, I know there is magic, and everyday I grow more aware of it and the patterns it draws in our lives. I see it in my son, in my wife, in the trees, animals and stones. I hope that everyone gets to know this, and finds a similar path to follow that reveals the patterns of nature.

Bright Blessings to each of you.

Boon
