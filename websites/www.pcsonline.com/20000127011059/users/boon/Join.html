How Do I Become a Member?

First things first

  • We are a mature guild and any kids we have had in the guild have usually left unhappy because we roleplay and don't typically powergame.
  • We often have guild events for fun where no one gains exp. If this bothers you, then there are other guilds for ye.
  • We are a group of friends and as such, if you are not recommended by a current member, you have very little chance of getting into the guild.
  • We are a neutral guild, seeking Balance in the world. You cannot have Light without Dark so we embrace good and evil races.
  • All members are expected to be a social participant from time to time. We all have times where we like to be alone, but we love to have folks get involved.
  • We discourage "twinking" of lower level members with items, but will aid you to get those items yourself when you are a high enough level. We do hold events where "hand-me-down" items can be won or earned by lower level members, but these will not be extreme items. (This does not limit twinking your own, secondary characters, so long as it is role-played in some way: child support, helping a sibling/lover/cousin, repaying old debts, righting past wrongs).
Failing any of these means that this is not the guild for you.
 

Our Charter

  • We seek Balance!
    • This means that it is okay to kill those things considered "Good" if it helps to balance out those things that are "Evil." For balance in nature, there must be both. So far we have seen an overpopulation of Evil in our world and find little, if any need to control the population of "Good" creatures.
    • The killing of animals, even Bears and Wolves is ok in moderation as their populations growth has resulted in the lack of grazing animals in our world. This imbalance occurred before my birth, and the birth of my father.
  • It is wrong to kill any guards to the point that there is no protection of the youngsters. The killing of guards or NPCs of your own race and alignment is considered odd, but if it assists in Balance, is acceptable.
  • Dropping whatever you are doing to help others is almost always the right thing to do, if doing so does not endanger others. This is not just for guild members, but everyone, provided they are respectful, polite and it aids Balance.
  • We discourage the killing of good aligned NPCs even though sometimes we understand that balance is served by diminishing the powers of "Good."
  • The fair Treants deserve our respect and protection, (Grizzleknot however is an abomination). We are after all, The Keepers of Oak.
 If you are still interested then here are your steps.

1. Your first task is to leave a biography on our message board of your character. Not your stats and equipment, if you feel inclined to do that, then again, this is not the guild for you. Instead tell us where you (your character) were born and how you were raised. Tells us a bit about the things that occurred in your life that shaped you into the personality you are today.

2. You must perform an assigned task or quest given by your Guild Officer. Read on for details.

Here is how the guild is set up:

We have Officers assigned to each race. He or She will be your leader and first contact in the guild. These officers are assigned the task of first determining if you are a good candidate for The Keepers of Oak and then of assigning your introductory Quest. If your officer is not available another will serve in their place.

The screening is done by doing a quick survey to see if anyone in the Guild or in the Druid Council has reports of your lack of honor or maturity. Yes we are particular about maturity. We are a fun guild but expect folks to know how to behave and treat others. Constantly failing to use vowels (U R, plz, pplz, and such) is impolite and not acceptable. People in Norrath aren't "Dudes, Dewds, Biatch, bro, cool, kewl" or so forth. Use of these terms is sign of a drunkard and a knave. We aren't hard-core role-players, but we expect courtesy, treat people as people, not NPCs.

The quest assigned to you will be race and level appropriate and someone from the guild will probably go with you to get to know you better. These quests can be anything from gathering High Quality Lion Pelts to acquiring the Borxx Cornea Lens. Whatever is acquired in the quest becomes guild property and will be used for guild members, probably even yourself.

There will be a period after completion of the quest where you may be observed after which, the officers will vote to accept you or not. We wish you luck. You can check our members page to see the Officer for each race.
