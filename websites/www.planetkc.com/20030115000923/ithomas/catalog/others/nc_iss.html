"Unreleased LP from a few years back capturing Campbell in pre-New Age mode, carting his tiny violin around his tiny country. Consists of two home-made tracks where strings rattle, radios hum and casios twinkle, plus a live track from the Freek Records festival in London, which is relentless full-on engine roar complete with audience whoops of encouragement and cries to desist. Gritty precursor to his new job trying to conduct the Vibracathedral Orchestra."  --Freedom From

"Neil Campbell once made the mistake of stating plainly and clearly, not that I was paying attention, something about his desire to be nice. For all the flack he's received, you'd think the guy confessed to eating his own barbecued fecal matter on the weekends. It is a fairly radical lifestyle Campbell's espousing -- flushing the toilet at other people's houses, telling the truth when necessary, pinching one's partner often enough to be able to refrain from throwing punches -- especially for someone who relies on fanzines and backwater e-list sarcasts to pay the rent. Recorded over a one-week stretch in July 1996, Campbell's Itinerant String Section CD contains more than enough Gloria: the jack-in-the-box in permanent limbo between versus of "Pop Goes the Weasel" of the Wellingborough recording; Dylan Nyoukis' introduction to the London piece (the Scots slang "woo" erupting as the Thai friend ants started to kick in), with its near opaque rows of feedback and lawnmower engines set against striations getting burned across the backside of Campbell's tin violin, just like the one Johnny Winter used to play; and high-contrast cover art made from inkpad prints of Diz Willis' flanks (may they rest in peace)." -Seymour Glass, Bananafish 14, Fall 2000, p. 156
