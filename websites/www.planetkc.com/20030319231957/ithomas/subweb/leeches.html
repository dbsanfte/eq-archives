Leeches (Class Hirudinea)

Key Features:

- flat underside
- circular, sucking mouth
- size range: 1/2" - 4" when extended (see text)

Leeches are usually small and flat.  They tend to cling to smooth stones with their circular "sucker".  Leeches generally have the appearance of being segmented, with the lines running perpendicular to the length of their body.  They move by extending and contracting their tough muscular bodies, so they appear quite long.  Do not confuse these with the flat soft-bodied planaria; leeches are your basic, run of the mill, parasites.  Approach them with extreme caution.
