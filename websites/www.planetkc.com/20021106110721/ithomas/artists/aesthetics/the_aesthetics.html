BIO INFORMATION
It was the winter of 1995 that The Aesthetics began.  Matthew Middleton, J-mz Robinson [bass] and Russle Coveney [drums] were the first incarnation.  At first the band was a practise room project, but as the chemistry proved, things were to be taken a little more seriously.  The band gelled like a petrie-dish and the first few recordings were designed.  These in part contributed to the now classic 'College Rock' cassette - a series of practise room outings and live parties, and what parties they were. Romance prevailed.  Goth/industrialist J-mz brought a savage yet beautiful flavour to the band - perhaps never to be repeated.  But of course, each Bass player has his/her effect.  As the bassist filtered away a new line-up emerged.  'Twas the Shaun Jury - local noise merchant and career student.  Shaun brought professionalism to the band and a sharp edge to the sound.  From this line up many songs from the 'My Right to Riches' album germinated.  Russel was expelled for growing a beard and Stefan Neville - world-famous folkster/cartoonist joined on the tin cans.  What a line up.  By this time it was 1997.  Romance flowered.   Hearts were broken.  Classic shows ensued.  Palmerston North - unforgettable.  My Right to Riches was released in 1998.  Shaun and Stefan migrated to Auckland city and the Aesthetics was left in financial/philosophical tatters.  Eventually the OFF! generation line-up came together.  And this line-up is how it remains...  Anticipate 'OFF!' like a shot in the arm or a bungee jump. Cherio!!!!!! - Matt Middleton March 21, 2001
