"Respectively, the string and percussion sections from acclaimed free sound Orchestra step out alone. See the "Lino Hi" CD on Giardia for the full tilt sound, but catch intimate glimpses of the various factions at play here. Queasy scanty shanty string swing on one side - disorientating bell drum shaker rumble on the other. Will redefine "primitive" for a whole new set of folk, and "folk" for a whole new set of primitives. Edition of 300, second pressing." --Freedom From

"The Vibracathedral String Band/The Vibracathedral Drum Troupe- is a new project from Neil Campbell who, aside from being a founding dad of The A Band (on Siltbreeze), was also a brief Smell & Quim'ster, is a present Sunroof'er and a major factory hen in the cassette underground. The untitled "String band" side (both cuts avoided the christening) is a massed cello gathering (celli?) and bowed miscellany in search of "the perfect note" and ... they've found it! And in doing so, destroying that age old beatnik joke. So, if John Coltrane's 'Ascension' needed acoustic scaffolding before the breather apparatus/plumbing tsunami ripped the globe apart, then make believe this is that thought made flesh. The ear-rimming beauty could also be seen as a complimentary bro/sis to the terrific John Clyde-Evans LP & 7" (Fisheye and Giardia respectively)." - pTolemic Terrascope, Fall 2000
