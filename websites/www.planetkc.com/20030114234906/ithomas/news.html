10.05.02 | October News
M/tel HQ has been a buzz with some very heavy action recently, as we’re busy putting the finishing touches on our latest release…

TRIGHPLANE TERRAFORMS NO.1 RELEASED:
We're overwhelmingly ecstatic to announce that our long awaited, 3-way split compilation CD featuring The Magic Carpathians, Six Organs of Admittance and Vibracathedral Orchestra, will finally see the light of day this month.  Trighplane Terraforms No.1 will first make a special, pre-release debut at the Terrastock V music festival, running through the weekend of October 11th-13th at the Axis club in Boston, Mass.  Thanks to our friend and co-conspirator Nemo, you'll be able to pick up copies of the CD from the Time-Lag Records merchandise table.

Following the festival, October 15th will mark the "official" release date for Trighplane Terraforms No.1, at which time, the CD will be available directly from M/tel mail order, and from most of the usual places in the coming weeks, too.  Due to the already swelling demand and limited number of this release, we are not taking pre-orders; hopefully giving everyone a fair crack at getting a copy.

6.18.02 | June Updates & News...

 TRIGHPLANE TERRAFORMS:
Trighplane Terraforms, our much anticipated 3-way split compilation CD featuring The Magic Carpathians, Six Organs of Admittance and Vibracathedral Orchestra, is well underway and we're working extra hard to have it out before Summer's end.   We're also very happy to announce the involvement of our friend Nemo Bidstrup, who has joined on to design the artwork/sleeves for Trighplane Terraforms.   You may recognize Nemo from the gorgeously hand crafted LP/CD jackets he has created for his own label Time-Lag Records (Charalambides, Drona Parva, Matt Valentine, Songs from the Entoptic Garden) and trust us, his work here is just as remarkable.   Yep, this one continues to amaze and surprise even us!  Stay tuned.  Visit the Trighplane Terraforms info page here.

STRAPHANGER 7":
M/Tel will have a new 7" out real soon, put together with our North Dakota friends Straphanger.  This limited edition single boasts two exclusive tracks ('The Victory Lap (Goddamn the Circumstance)' b/w 'Glad') of the 'straps uniquely noisome brand of anvil pounding punk rock.  This single was originally scheduled as a tour support release for the 'straps happening-as-of now Midwest tour, however we just couldn't keep those guys away from the Econoline long enough...no matter, we dig it anyway!  Sadly, this single will mark the trio's last outing before drummer Ike Turner (also of Imipolex G fame and heir apparent to the 'socks of Bonham') ships off to teach in Norway.   An official release date is right around the corner kids, so hold tight.  In the meantime, you can jump over to our mail order catalog and check out a few samples from the 'straps recently released album The Grain Belt.

MAIL ORDER:
M/Tel Mail Order is keeping fairly busy these days and we've got a batch of new items in for your listening enjoyment, all accessible now via our on-line catalog.   Recent arrivals and restocks include: Ashtray Navigations 'Tristes Tropiques' CD [Betley Welcomes/Blackbean & Placenta/Rhizome], Ego Summit 'The Room Isn't Big Enough' LP [Old Age/No Age], Guided By Voices 'Universal Truths and Cycles' LP/CD [Matador], Screamers 'In a Better World' DBL CD [Extravertigo], U-Sound Archives, Vol. One DBL CD [Parallelism], Vibracathedral Orchestra 'Lino-Hi' CD [Giardia] and Lexicon Devil 'The Fast Times and Short Life of Darby Crash and The Germs' Book [Feral House].  >>>

TERRIFYING EXPERIENCE NEWS:
Mitch Mitchell and The Terrifying Experience are currently holed up in an undisclosed Ohio basement, writing and recording songs for their next album.  TEX guitarist Duane Hart has formed a new band called Gypsy Witch, who will have a track featured on the upcoming Columbus music CD Cringe.com/pilation Volume #3.  Axle Rose phones Gypsy Witch front man and asks for his lungs back.  TEX bassist Geoff Ortlip has opened a vintage guitar shop in Columbus, OH.

THE AESTHETICS NEWS:
Matt Middleton & The Aesthetics have re-formed under the band's classic My Right to Riches line-up.  They've already played a handful of live gigs around  New Zealand and are rumored to be recording once again as well.  Thurston Moore promptly soils his britches over the news (...it's OK buddy, we did too!).
