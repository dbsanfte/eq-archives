Title: Lexicon Devil - The Fast Times and Short Life of Darby Crash and The Germs
Author(s): Brendan Mullen, with Don Bolles and Adam Parfrey
Format: Book/312 pgs.

"LEXICON DEVIL, an oral history that probes the brains of over 100 characters, and contains 140 never-before-seen photos, goes beyond early punk rock into secret regions of suicide, mind control, suppressed sexuality, corrosive humor, self-abuse, and addictions to rock celebrity, drugs and cults. Thieves, surfers, skinheads, nymphettes, chickenhawks, skateboarders, Scientologists, boho artists, under-age sado-masoschistic punk kids, and TV stars confront and hustle each other in a late '70s L.A. history that reads like bowdlerized chapters of Hollywood Babylon that have finally come to light."

"Rock & roll, death, urine, slime, blood and pus [were] reborn on the Sunset Strip in the punk summer of '77.  Darby Crash was the Christopher Columbus of horror, grotesquery, and bad behavior - of course he needs his story told.  I couldn't believe the book - it brought me back in a time machine to my own brush with madness and insanity." -- Kim Fowley

"This book scared the shit out of me. It's amazing, it's great ... I can't believe how good it is." -- Pat Smear, Germs guitarist, and later a player for Nirvana and the Foo Fighters

"Forget about London and New York and fashion and politics.  The Germs wrote and played the best punk rock songs of all time." -- Flea, Red Hot Chili Peppers

140 photos and illustrations.  Includes a comprehensive appendix section featuring: Cast of Characters, 'Lyrics of the Germs' Recorded & Unrecorded Output' (by Darby Crash), Discography, and a chronology of the Germs' Gigs and Key Events!
