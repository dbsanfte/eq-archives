How to play a necromancer in a high level dungeon group

Hi there.

Throughtout this web I have constantly repeated that the necro s usual way of hunting is the outdoors soloing Darkness-Fear combo. Indeed, this is what we do the best, and no class (not even mages or druids) can match us necros.

Most likely, this is exactly what you have been doing for the past 50 levels. Zones like Oasis, Lake of ill Omen, South Karanas, Overthere or DreadLands have been witness of your excellencies as an experienced soloer. Besides, you have leveled up fairly easily and fast and you have found little or no need to get into any group so far. Unfortunatelly, those days have come to an end  : (

As I mentioned in the level 50 to 60 section, you can still find some spots to solo post 50, specially at your early 50s. But this doesnt mean that its the right choice. Exp bar wont move as it used to, and we all know that outdoors loot always sucked. This is why i advice you to change your way of thinking and start thinking about the other part of tha game : the group hunting.

In my opinion, to hunt in group is not as rewarding as to solo. The feeling of beating the crap out of an even conning mob by yourself is only comparable to the feeling you get when emerging victorious in a duel to death. When you are in a group, the satisfaction comes when you work as a perfect killing machine with no downtimes and no deaths rather than the personal pride on your own powers. The necro is not the leader of the group. Enchanters, clerics or tanks are. Therefore, dont expect to awe your team mates or even yourself with your devastating DoTs and pet. Actually, a necro s duty in a group is quite "dark", working always behind the shadows and doing the dirty job  : )

Ok now, as I have mentioned several times, necromancers have one of the widest range of possibilities among all the casters. We can perform many things, yet no one is our speciallity (apart of soloing, of course) : we can inflict damage, but nor as fast as mages or wizzies; we can do some crowd control, but not as efficiently as enchanters; we can do assist healing, but not like druids or shammies. So, what the fuck can I do in a group ??

Everything is going to depend on what group are you in, which classes your group has got and which are missing. As i said before, since the necro is not the group s showman, he has got the posibility (and the obligation) to adjust his role to the group s needs.

Now, Im gonna decribe some of the different alternatives you might face while getting into a group, and what should you be doing if you are in such : 1. Mana battery, 2. Healer, 3. Crowd controller, 4. Outdoors grouping and 5. In the perfect group.

 

 

1. Mana Battery role :

Ok now, lets say you got into a group with too much of nuke power and lacking of a proper tank. The biggest concern of the group will be to kill the mob fast enough because the tank wont handle to much mambo fight without burning down the cleric s mana. For example, such a group could formed by :

1. Cleric

2. Tank

3. Enchanter

4. Two mages / Mage-Wizard

5. Shamman / Druid

6. And yourself

Most likely, the group will finish the mob pretty quickly because of its high and fast damage hability. So, in order to avoid long downtimes, we will become the rest of the other casters s mana battery  : ) 

Basically, the two mages / two wizards / mage & wizard will cast the hell away alternatively one each (to not overtaunt the mob) while the Tank and the pets try to keep up with the damage taken. In this case, the nukers will burn lots of mana fast, and you have to supply them back with that mana or prepare for long medding downtimes. Also, you might have to dump some mana on the cleric, but since combats will be fast (not many complete healing performed) and there is -or should be- a shamman/druid healing assisting, cleric s mana should be ok.

Well, this wont be a very passionating role, but a necro gotta do what a necro gotta do :

Get regrowth / chloroplast from the shamman / druid in order to lessen  lich s effect. Cast lich or demilich on you, and sit your butt down till nukers or cleric say "getting low on mana" where you will kindly dump on him your refreshing mana (Covetous subvesion spell -check spells list if needed-), just to afterwards sit down again to med up. And thats it, not big deal, is it? I know, i know, this sucks. But hey ! what can I do, this group is not a beauty either  : )

Again, this kind of group wouldnt be feasible in places where mobs hit hard like hell (see sebilis or Howling Stones), but could be ok in lighter places like the areas close to the zone out in Karnor Castle or the Hole, where the monsters are not that tough.

What about your pet ? Well, this time he will have to take some damage too, so send him in straight away with the taunt on. Anyway, keep and eye on his health and be ready to type /pet no taunt and /pet back off when his health is getting under 40%. Of course, augmentation must be up for its HP regeneration factor.

2. Healer role :

Ok. Now lets turn the situation all around. Lets say that this group has some very powerful tanks and thats is the main damage source and lets top that up assuming there is no cleric on the party, only a Shaman and you as healers. Ok then, lets play doctors and nurses  : ) -I loved that game hehe-  The group would more less look like :

1. Shaman

2. Enchanter

3. Warrior

4. Monk / Paladin / SK 

5. Any other non healing class

6. And yourself

That couple of high level and greatly equiped tanks could be the main damage source of the group. On top of that, they could be backed up by a ranger / rogue as well (slot number 5 open). With so many melee fighters to heal, the shamy should have somebody to give him a hand. 

Mem up both pacts (lvl 54 one which is 500 Hp transfer in 24 secs and lvl 44 one which is 300 hp). Also mem the highest LifeTap DoT u got (Bond or Vexinia) and Deflux / Touch of Night too. The plan is as follows :

The warrior single pulls a mob and the other melees jump on it like war dogs. The shamy should land slow ASAP (spell torgors insects) which will make the mob to increse its punching dalay in about 50%. Ok now, watch the main tanks health bar. When it drops 1 bub and a half (meaning he still has 3.5 HP bubs left), cast on him both your 54 and 44 pact spells ( check spells list if needed ) and Vex Mordana or Bond of Death on the mob (if you dont have yet Vex Mordana, I recomend you to use Bond + one Deflux the offset the 2 pacts HP drain). Now sit down with lich and med that mana bub up you have just burnt. Watch how those 800 hp you have just pumped on the tank heal him up slowly. Rinse and repeat.

Your comitment is to keep one tank alive at once. If you see that the assist melee is getting hit, heal him in stead of the main tank. BUT if you see that both of em are getting the beat (lets say the puller brought few mobs), focus yourself on the main tank and leave the other melee to the shamy (you two will have this agreed at the beginning of the grouping session). This way, you wont dump too many HPs which would be risky and the Shaman wont burn too much mana.

The 54 Pact - 44 Pact - LifeTap Dot technique is based in three very things : 

  • To have the mobs always slowed by the shaman. Otherwise, you wont be able to keep up with the mob s damage output, they just hit to hard and fast. 

  • Ask the shaman to regrowth you, so you can lich 24 / 7 not wasting your valuable HPs which can better be used in healing the tank.

  • Single pulls. Two mobs hitting the tank will be too much for your health tranfer spells. So, either you camp an easy spot where the tank can pick up singles, or you have an experienced chanter to do the crowd controll.

What about your pet ? Well, we have already enough tanks in this group, so have your pet s taunt in off. This way, he wont get beaten up too much so you wont have to worry about his health. Augmentation always up in order to increase the damage rate.

3. Crowd controller role :

This is, in my opinion, the most fun role you can take while grouping. People seem to not understand or believe that necros can indeed do some mass control. Our best weapons will be Screaming Terror -ST- and Paralizing Earth -PE- ( check spells list if needed ).

The group would be formed by a balanced team without enchanter.

Whereas you might be able to keep under control up to two mobs at once, please be aware that a necro CANT handle a 3 or 4 mobs train. Why? because our mez spell ( Screaming Terror) has a max duration of 18 secs, taking 2.6 secs to be cast off and a long 6.5 secs recasting time. This means that you HAVE to be recasting ST on 1 mob constantly. As maximum, you can be alternatively non stop ST 2 mobs, but if any of them resists even once, you will NOT have time to recast it before the other one brakes free. Lets do the maths : 2.6 + 6.5 + 2.6 = 11.7 secs since you first cast ST on the first mob till you ST the second one. Since ST has a max duration of 18 secs, we are ok so far. But lets imagine the mob have resisted ST and you have got to wait 6.5 + 2.6 secs to recast it. That comes up to : 11.7 + 6.5 + 2.6 = 20.8 secs, which is more than 18 secs. So, by the time you are ready to reapply ST on the mob that initially resisted ST, the other one has been freed of ST s mez effect, hence jumping on you right away.

So how can we safely control 2 mobs? We will have to use a ST - PE - Pet combination. Im going to describe what should you do in case you party is camping a room and the puller brings along three mobs on his tail :

Ok, wait till the tank and the rest of the group stick to one very mob (/assist tank applies here). Then ST another one and send pet to the third one (with the pet s taunt on). Try to tell your group to step aside out of those two mobs s melee reach (couple of meters will be enough) pulling the creature they are fighting along with them to a corner of the room. Reapply ST on the first mob and PE it straight away. The second one must be hitting your pet hard (prolly pet s health is 50% by now). Ok, here it comes the tricky part : ST that second mob and right afterwards hit the "/pet back off " hotkey. If you get him to back off without hitting the monster (and therefore braking the ST), then you ll be doing ok. Now PE that second mob while he is STed and park the pet aside to recover HPs (or send him to the mob the tank is fighting against).

If the group has done what you told them (to drive their mob and themselves out of the other two s -the ones you are dealing with- melee reach), now you have 2 mobs rooted, your pet parked aside, and the group finishing off their prey. Your only duty now is to recast PE every 20 secs on each of those two creatures. If the room is pretty narrow and they cant get away from the mob s reach, keep them STed alternatively recasting it on each of them. But ALWAYS have them PEed because if ST fails they will jump on you, and then your pet on them, and that ll be the end of the group.

So basically, PE the mobs you want to control and chain cast ST on them, renewing PE every 30 secs more less for sefaty porpouses. Both PE and ST burn little mana, so dont worry about overcasting it.

Of course, if you are doing the chanter s job, you WONT be able to cast even one single DoT on the mob the group is with because of the ST s short duration. Anyway, the crowd control duty is the MOST important role in a group, along with the cleric s one.

What about your pet ? If you see that your pet is actually difficulting your job because he keeps jumping on STed mobs, do NOT hesitate to type /pet get lost. An uncontrolled pet might easily kill the whole group !!  Anyway, the pet is not that important for a crowd controller (how many high level enchanters do you see with their pets hanging around?). So, if you are able to keep your pet under control, let him be. If you dont, sacrifice him without a second thought.

 Tip : If you are hunting in dungeons with lots of undead like Karnor or Howling Stones, it might be more proffitable for you to not have any pet summoned in order to charm an undead creature. This will ease you mass control duties. Taking the same example as exposed above, lets say the puller brings a three mobs train, and one of those is undead. Well, just charm it and sick it to another one while the group deals with the third one. Recast undead charm every 20 secs and watch how they anul each other  : )  Of course, you have previously PE them both before the charming in case the charm gets resisted. I assure you will get nice praises from your group mates since its a very espectacular way to reduce a train down.

Tip 2 : If any of the mobs is a caster, PE wont stop him from casting away his DDs on you (and then having your pet on him). If this is the case, PE him, destroy pet, and keep him STed at EVERY moment. Actually, the best thing you can do is to tell your group to ALWAYS kill first the casters, leaving the non casters under your good mezzing skills.

Tip 3 : If the room is large enough (ie. Cheff room in Sebilis), you might want to park a mob aside using Paralizing Earth. PE lasts over 1 minute, giving the group some time to deal with the rest of the mobs.

Also, I would like to warn you to not venture deep inside any dungeon without a propper enchanter. In spite of the fact that you do can mass control, ST has a too short duration to be feasible in nasty spots. So chose an easy room to camp and you ll do just fine.

4. Outdoors grouping :

If you are grouping in a high level outdoor zone ( Skyfire, for example), the best service you can do to your group is to play like if you were soloing :

If there is no druid to snare the mob, use Dooming Darkness rather than Cascading (less mana expensive and lasts a bit more. Check spells list if needed). Then send the per in and cast Fear. DoT him once (with the quickest DoT you got -envenomed bolt-) and let the pets and tanks and nuker kill it off. Just like if you were soloing.

The point of grouping in SkyFire is the non stop fast pulling group situations. The Darkness / Fear combo will help to save mana to the cleric and hence reducing sensibly medding up downtimes.

What about my pet ? just like if you were soloing. Send it after snared has landed and always with the taunt on in case Darkness gets resisted.

5. In the perfect group :

Well, first of all I gotta acknoledge that I wouldnt include a necro in my ideal group. In a perfect balanced group, each of the members should be specialized in one area, and since we necros arent the masters of ANY group needs....

Anyway, lets say you get into an execellent, balanced group. This would more less be as follows :

1. Cleric

2. Enchanter

3. Warrior

4. Shamman

5. Monk / Rogue / Ranger

6. And yourself

In a group like this, every basic need is covered : tanking (warrior), mass control (chanter), healing (cleric and shammy), buffs (cleric, shammy and chanter), damage output (warrior and the rogue, monk or ranger) and pets (shammy and yourself). This are, in my opinion, the areas that every nice group has to fullfil. Prolly you have noticed that there is no nuking performed in this group (no wiz, mage, or druid) but unfortunatelly post 50 game is broken in my opinion and there is no need of firepower when it comes to exp grouping. Grab a second melee in stead. 

As you can see, there is little room for you to work because there is anyway somebody else who can do it better than you. So, which should be my duty in such a perfect killing machine?

Well, you will be the "jack", helping them all in order to cut downtimes : 

You will act as a heal assistant doing the pact-bond-pact combo when you see that the cleric has already performed couple of mana bitting healings.

You will help to inflict damage casting Splurt at the very beginning of the battle (if the mobs lasts than long like for example in Sebilis) or Envenomed bolt when mob s health has been beaten down to 80%. Do not cast two DoTs for the overtaunting business.

You will dump mana on the cleric or enchanter if they get low on mana. I wouldnt dump mana on the shammy since they can canibalize themsleves and since the mana dumping rate sucks (you lose 3 and they gain 1 - well, bit less if you are specialized in alteration-).

You will have PE-ST ( hehe sounds like "pest" ) combo ready in case a mayor train falls upon your group and the chanter cant handle it. Also, you should be ready to use it in case any mob is hitting the chanter or the cleric hard and they cant brake free of their grip. Always have in mind that in a group, you GOTTA protect the cleric and enchanter on top of anyone else !! Anyway, dont ST any mob unless its 100% necessary because afterwards the chanter wont be able to mez it (mez DOESNT overwrite ST).

Basically, your dutty is to assist them all cutting down medding times : healing assist, mana dumping, crowd control assist when necesary, DoT once, snaring issue if there is no druid or ranger -use Dooming in stead of Cascading-, pet work, etc

As you can see, your role in this kind of groups may not be very crucial, and definitely wont be a must, but it will help in order to achieve a smoother hunting evening, with short downtimes and good exp for the group.

I told you : whereas necros are the outdoors soloing generals, we are only flat soldiers when it comes to group in dungeons. But still we can be usuful if the group is smart enough to appreciate our help.

What about your pet ? Well, it will be treated as another damage source, so have his taunt in off possition, and send him into the combat ONLY once the chanter has mezzed all the mobs and the tank has focused his atention on one very mob for sure ( /assist tank command is needed as a hotkey).
