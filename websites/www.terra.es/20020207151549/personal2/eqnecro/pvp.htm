Player vs Player

Well, in my humble opinion, this is the most fun part of the game. Being able to size up your skills and powers with your same is an amazing experience, and you won´t find that exciment ( and nervousness) anywhere else in Norrath but when dueling. Also, you´ll make mistakes that you wouldn´t otherwise make in a mob fighting, and in a PvP, those mistakes mean most likely death.

First of all, these PvP guide has been dessign on blue servers dueling enviorment rather than in actual PvP servers. In those ones, rules are compleatly different and some other tactics could apply.

The most important thing in duels is to keep in mind who are you about to fight against. Every class needs a different combat strategy, and you gotta adapt yourself (due to your wide spells range) to the enemy´s class fighting way. Each class has got some weakpoints, and you must be smart enough to exploit them to the end.

In the equipment section I stated that your main equipment issue would be to reach 200 Intelligence. Well, that´s not 100% accurate when talking about dueling a spells caster class. Then, magic resistance bonus items should be what you´re looking for. Heat and cold items too. Poison and Disease are important while dueling a Shaman or another necro.

Of course, no need to say that you should cast everysingle buff and shielding spell you´ve got in your spell book on you. Also arm and buff your pet up, and med up both mana and HP till full.

In spite of the fact that your pet is one of your main weapons in any monster-hunting, they won´t be so crucial when dueling. Remember that your adversary is no brainless mob, and therefore he won´t waste his time and HP dealing with your pet but you !!  So don´t rely too much on your pet and just consider him as one of your DoTs. And don´t worry if the guy fights him and kills him. Believe me, that would be the best that could happen : a moron wasting his HP or mana against your pet. So, don´t heal the pet and don´t recast  if he dies either unless you are full mana and you are hidden somewhere -pet summoning casting lasts over 10 seconds-.

I have tested all out DoT lines in order to determinate exactly how much resist you need to be inmune to our DoTs. Please note that this is considering both fighters equal in level. The bigger is the level gap between both, the tougher to resist the spells :

  • Heat blood line : Fire based. Lure (you need over 250 FR to resist it).
  • Lifetaps and lifetaps DoTs : Magic based. Lure (you need over 250 MR to resist it).
  • Dissease line : Scourage, Assystole, Heart Flutter, Plague, Cessation of Cor, etc. Dissease based. At equal level, most of the times resisted with as little as 70 DR.
  • Poison line : Poison based. At equal level, most of the time resisted with as little as 70 PR.
  • Splurt : Magic based. Lure (you need over 250 MR to resist it).
  • Ignite bones : Fire based. At equal level, most of the times resisted with as little as 70 FR.
  • Chill bones : Cold based. At equal level, most of the times resisted with as little as 70 CR.
  • Darkness line : Magic based. At equal level, most of the times resisted with as little as 70 MR.
  • Fear : Magic based. This spell depends mainly on the level (very tough to stick to guys higher lvl than yourself), but considering equal level it is very easy to resist with as little as 70 MR.
  • ST and Root : Magic based. This spell depends mainly on the level (very tough to stick to guys higher lvl than yourself), but considering equal level it is very easy to resist with as little as 60 MR. In the high levels they are even less realiable, regardless of your oponent's MR.

It is very important that before accepting the duel you try to lurk close to the guy and take a quick look to his gear. Try to guess roughly his resist figures (is he wearing blue diamond and diamond gear? is he wearing a specific item with high resist boost? etc). Now, chose your DoTs depending on his gear since necros got a wide range of possibilities. If you see that he is wearing a hell of a bunch of resist gear, then your only weapons should be lifetaps and pet. If you see that his resist figures are crap, always chose dissease and poison lines because they can't be dispelled.

 

General tips as a necro dueler:

Tip : As a necro, you need plenty of space to perform your powers, no matter if hunting or dueling, so only accept duels in open-field non-KoS zones. You´ve been warned. Main reason for this is because Shadow Step  is one of our mayor PvP spells -specially against melee classes-, and it´s only useful outdoors.

Tip 2 : Before starting a duel, when you cast your buffs, don´t cast the best ones at the beginning. Cast two or three shit ones (like grim aura, deadeye, etc.) and then cast your valuable buffs (shieldskins, mayorshielding, spirit armour,etc.). Once done this, cancel the shit ones (click on their symbols). Hence you´ll have few empty slots up at the top of your screen. What for? Because in those empty slots where be placed your adversary´s nasty permanent spells (DoTs, root, blind, snare, etc) and since Cancel Magic spell dispells one by one the spells from the top to the bottom, you won´t need to cancel all your lovely buffs in order to get rid of his DoTs. This is very important, it can safe your life when dueling against casters !!

Tip 3 : Against classes that have cancel magic spell, and specially at high levels when you can not start the duel with ST (above lvl 40-45 ST starts getting resisted very oftenly), I would recommend you to buff up your oponent before landing your lure DoTs with 2 fast buffs -Impart Strengh and Spirit Armour are perfect-. Why? Because if your oponent is smart, he will clean up the first two buff slots, being able to dispell your DoTs afterwards. If, alternatively, you refill those two slots with some silly buffs, the DoTs will placed at the bottom of his buff window, so he won't be able to dispell them unless he fully dispells himself. This is usefull only if you expect a long duel, like for example against druids and shamans.

Also, try to focus on poison and disease based DoTs because those cannot be dispelled (only class that can is a shaman or also if you have shaman made potions). Unfortunatelly, poison and disease DoTs are easily resisted :(

Tip 4 : Beware of tricky players when dueling within zones with ponds, lakes, rivers or seaside. Why? Because there is a bug (or I believe it to be a bug) about not being able to cast on players underwater if you are outside, and viceversa. And this applies even if only your feet are in the water. With this, they would step outside the water, cast the hell on you and step back in afterwards when it´s your turn to cast away. No damage will be taken. If you notice any adversary playing this game, shout it out loud and make him feel ashame of himself.

Tip 5 : Mostly, you ll see that I advice to open up the fights with Screaming Terror -check spells list- hoping to mezmerize your oponent for a short lenght of time and therefore gaining few seconds to load DoTs on him. Well, this is only possible under two circunstances :

  • Your oponent is blue conning or even white conning, never higher.
  • Your oponent s magic resistance aint very high.
  • Neither of you are over level 40. Over lvl 40 the chances of Screaming Terror, RooT, Fear, Stun etc are senssibly reduced.

Tip 6 : Against pet classes, you never fight the pet. Never load DoTs and waste mana on them. Just root them aside or fear them away, but do not try to kill them. If the master dies, so the pet does, so keep focus'd on the master.

Tip 7 : Remember that ST is mezmerize spell, meaning that it will brake off as soon as the target is inflicted any kind of damage. So, when you start up with ST, load first the longest casting DoT without initial DD damage (for example the heat blood series). Once landed, you got 6 seconds to land a second fast casting DoT (DoT's damage starts to deal damage away the tick after it lands, and 1 tick in EQ is 6 seconds). So, in those 6 seconds after landing the first DoT, you land your second DoT. By the time you land it, ST should be about to brake off due to the first's DoT damage tick. Having done this, the duel really starts having your oponent double dotted, and that might give you an edge.

Tip 8 : Once you have landed ST, you might want to Root the guy (specially if he is a melee) and load up not only 2 DoTs, but all of them.

Tip 9 : There is this lovely item called Circlet of Shadows (right click instant invisivility, drops in Temple of Droga). When you invis yourself in PvP, you will get removed from your adversary's target window unless he has got a see invis buff on or wearing a see invis effect item. This is specially usefull against casters, because you can cast on him, shadow step and invis up, not allowing him to cast back on you. They will have to re click on you, otherwise they will recieve the mesage "you need to have a target in order to cast this spell". This is extremly useful against a wizard or another necro in a toe-to-toe nuking battle once your pet has been killed (invis poofs it) : Lifetap - CoS (he loses you as target and his casting drops down, he needs to re click on you wasting time) - lifetap - CoS - etc. By the way, this item has been nerfed lately, it is no longer instant but rather got a 5 secs casting time which makes it rather useless  : (

Tip 10 : Never waste your time using our fire, dissease and poison debuffer (Scent spell line). It is useless indeed. It is poison based but it gets MORE resisted than the poison DoTs itself LMAO. So, if you cannot land the dissease/poison DoT itself, you won't land the debuffer either. Utterly and completly useless.

Tip 11 : Shadow Step is your main weapon againts casters. Please note that SS cannot positionate you at any higher Z axis figure than your original point. This means that, in order to teleport you farther away, you should run to a high spot (top of a hill for example) before casting SS.

Now, I´ll list different ways to face a duel againts different classes. If you´re worth your salt mate, you should learn them all up by heart!!!

PvP:

How to duel a Warrior-Rogue-Monk, a Paladin, a Shadow Knight, a Druid, a Ranger, a Wizard, a Mage, an Enchanter, a Shaman, a Cleric, a Bard and a Necromancer.

 

 

1. Dueling a Warrior-Rogue-Monk:

These are mellee classes, specially the warrior. But, at any rate, they all need to be within mellee distance to beat your butt. They actually lack of any other fighting strategy apart from the slash, hack, kick, bite and spit. And that´s your main advantage : They need to be close to you. Thanks God we´ve got a lovely lv 8 spell called Shadow Step (check spells list for more info) which is a random teleportion within a 300 feet radius circle. Its main adventage is that is 1 second-long casting spell, so it´s virtually instantaneous. Besides, it´s almost mana free to cast. This spell will be the key to succees against mellee classes. Watch out cause this spell can easily drive you out of the casting distance, so you might have to walk back into it : )

Don´t accept the duel being any close to the adversary. Get plenty of space between you two. If the guy doesn´t give you that space, cast Shadow step immediately after accepting the duel. With this, you´ll get that gap needed and also the guy will be confused for few secs trying to spot you out. Now cast quickly the lvl 24 spell Screaming terror (check spells list for more info). Its a fast 2.5 secs casting spell, so you´ll be able to cast it before the guy reaches up to you. It´s a brief, short version of the enchanter´s spell mesmerize. It makes the target to stand still for 10-15 secs without any control of himself (he won´t cast, move or attack). In those 10-15 seconds, your job is to cast your best two DoTs on him (read Tip 7 above please). Afterwards, send pet on him and run away. Just run in circles untill the DoTs wear off. Some other necros would try to Fear the guy away, but in my own experience Fear gets resisted much more often when dueling than in normal combats, I am not sure why. Once the DoTs effect has worn off, wait for him to get close to you and cast once more Shadow Step. Again, he´ll be desorientated and you will have gained a safetly gap between the two of you. And now start all over again: Screaming terror followed by two DoTs, and run away  : )

Doing all this written above, I have won duels without being hit even once.

There´s another variety of this very same strategy. Whenever you know in advance you´re gonna duel some lad, just bind yourself at any hidden spot not very far away from where the duel will take place (within the zone, of course). Best idea could be to get bound inside any small hut, or behind a large rock, or on the other side of that hill, or whatever spot you could think of. After having cast two DoTs and sent pet on him, he will run towards you like an enraged bull. Just shadow Step and you´ll now have time enough to cast the 4 secs long casting lvl 4 Gate spell (check spells list for more info). Hehehe I love this : Your adversary will be desperatly looking for you while you´re sitting down listening to the chart hits on the radio and medding mana up. On top of that, DoTs are doing their job. If for some reason you got beaten up, and therefore your shieldskin series spell (check spell list for farther info about this series) is off now, recast it. Once DoTs wear off, show up, spot the guy, re-ST, re-DoT, re-Gate, re-listen to the chart hits. Believe me, this could really piss the guy off, but hey! this is a duel to death ain´t it? Fuck the honour. You are a smart ass and therefore you win!! If, by fluke, the guy finds your lair, Shadow Step and run the hell far away (with the lad chasing you several yards behind). Say you get to the other corner of the zone. Just shadow step again and gate back. Hehehe. Too lovely to be real, isn´t it? There´s one main drawback : the dude will probably /petition you for being too annoying  :  )

If during the duel, he gets on you slashing you ass hard, Shadow Step is your best defence, or even have harmshield ready to cast if you don´t know what to do. This´ll give you few secs to cool down and think clearer. Just remember, keep him full DoTed and away from you, and you´ll win. Don´t cast DD spells or lifetaps (this only if really needed) since tank classes have loads of HP and you´ll need your whole mana bar in order to cast DoTs (more mana efficient than DDs). Do not forget to have always the dark pact series up, because tanks have got loads of HP and you´ll burn most of your mana on them.

And what about the rogue? He´ll always try to wear his camouflage on (invis effect) and snake up on your back to stick his tiny dick into your butt ( high damage backstab attack). So, first of all, have Dead eye or DMF spell cast on (sees thru invis. Check spells list for more info), and don´t worry too much about them circling and spinning around you looking for your back, you can still cast while turning yourself in circles, or just Shadow Step away and Screaming Terror afterwards. It´s very important not to let them hit you, ´cause they grease their weapons with poison, and it will deal good damage to you. Also, don´t root them and step back thinking your safe. They are skillful with bows and throwing knives too. So, Shadow Step away and cast ST from the distance.

Tip 1 : Beware of warriors. Most of them wear a MR bonus gear, boosting it up to 100 or ever higher !!  If you notice than your Screaming Terror spell keeps being resisted, quit casting it. In these cases, gain some meters with Shadow Step and DoT him, and keep shadow stepping out of reach till DoTs wear off to renew them. I know you might look like a chicken, but when Screaming Terror becames useless, Shadow Step turns out as our best option. Most of the times, a necro is a kitter in duels.

Tip 2 : Whenever he would hit you and hence having wasted your shieldskin (check spells list for more info) damage absorving effect, Shadow Step away and renew it straight away. No matter if you spend few components during one battle. These guys hit hard and may easily brake your casting concentration bashing you with their shields. To have your shieldskin spell up will give you few secs to clear up your mind and cast the lifesaver Shadow Step spell.

 

Spells to have memorized when dueling against a Warrior-Rogue: lifetap spell (rather a quick casting one), your two most powerfull DoTs, Screaming Terror, highest shieldskin spell you got , Harmshield, Shadow Step and Gate (optional).

 

How to fight a high level pure melee once fear, Screaming Terror and root wont work?  

Pure and plain kitting : Start duel, Shadow Step away getting few seconds, land your highest Heat Blood series DoT (it's hardly ever resisted), if the foe has spotted you out Shadow Step again gaining few more seconds, land Splurt, and now just run in wide circles (SoW potions or J.Boots are a must) while the DoTs and the pet do their job. Renew DoTs as needed and land your highest lifetap DoT for healing issues if you got it. If, for some reasson, you are cought, stuck or something and the melee is beating you down hit HarmShield, relax for a second and run to open fields. As soon as HS wears off, shadow step the hell away, lifetap and resume the kitting. 

Some high level melees got pumice stones, which are a very rare (I think Verant took them out of the game) item with no other stat but right click cancel magic. Of course, they will use them to remove your DoTs off. Just keep Shadow Step - DoT'ing them with a mana cheap DoT and with Lich series spell on. At the end of the day, how many pumice stones are they willing to burn??

Watch for high level rogues and monks !! A post 50 rogue/monk with nice gear will only need 10 seconds of melee to rip you thru. Top that off with nasty poisoned daggers (rogues only) and you will realize how dangerous a high lvl melee is. You really must not mess up with the kitting. As i said, he will only need few secs to kill you (their damage output is just amazing). Shadow Step, run with SoW/jboots, Shadow Step again, etc.

Monks heal up too : High lvl monks can heal up themselves with their ability Mend. This ability can be used once every 5 mins and it does heal a bunch of hp. Therefor, you better try to kill the monk fast or else you will run out of mana if you let him mend few times. In my opinion, you should try to land as many DoTs as you can on him: Shadow Step - DoT 1 - Shadow Step - DoT 2 - Shadow Step - DoT 3 - Shadow Step - nuke him down. 

back to PvP selection

 

 

2. Dueling a Paladin:

Well, well. This will be undoubtly tougher tank to fight against because they can cast damn good PvP spells : blind, healing, root, Fear undead, shielding buffs, cancel magic, etc. Besides they can stun you from the distance and they´ve got an special hability : Lay on Hands, which is a once-per-hour-only massive healing. I´m telling you, not a nice dude to mess with.

The DoT-Gate technique won´t work out since they´ll dispell your DoTs with cancel magic. Also you can´t DD them down ´cause with so many HPs and healings and shieldings and stuff you won´t have mana enough. Also, forget about your pet since they´ll defiantely fear it away or blind it (pet walks around like a head-chopped chicken unable to make his blows good). On top of that, they can stun you, then root, then walk into mellee distance and slash you to a shapeless bloody pulp on the floor. 

Ok now, stop it, don´t bray around thinking you ain´t got any change against this adversary. It´s indeed an awful dude, but let´s think something up. The only way to beat them I can think of is to not let them use their defensive spells (healing, cancel magic). So I´m affraid we´ll have to be quite agressive this time. Let´s just hope he hasn´t got a high Magic Resistance bonus gear.

Start off, as usual, the battle with lvl 24 Screaming Terror spell (check spells list for more info). Cast your 2 most poweful DoTs, forget about minor ones. Now, in order to have him away of Cancelling our DoTs, cast fear and lets hope it holds on him. Ok now, if it does stick to him, you´ve got about 10 secs to deal large amount of damage :  cast Shock of Poison (lvl 24 spell, check spells list for more info) the hell away few times. Then, stand still and reapply Fear even if it hasnt woren off. And again DD him few times, recast Fear, and again burn all your mana on him. I know this is not the way a necro should play since our DD are not very mana efficient, but there´s no other way to fight this guy. He has got just too many HP (considering several healings, cancel magic and Lay on Hands). Actually, if you let him use his healing skills, it´s like if you had to take down three times his whole HP bar, and you just haven´t got that much of mana.

In the other hand, let´s say he got free of our Screaming Terror and Fear spells and he´s on us. Like any other tank class, he needs to mellee in order to win. So, once more, if he has rooted or blind us, Shadow Step will be our best deffence (shadow step macro'd in a hot key of course).

Let´s say you have done a good job and you eventually inflict lots of damage to him, but, in the last moment he resorts to Lay on Hands and gets his HPs bar back to full. Of course, after such a combat you´ll be low on mana. Well, don´t worry, use the previous explained tacnic to Gate to a hidden spot (see how to duel a warrior-monk-rogue section above). Then med up HP and mana, and of course let him do the same. Then, let´s start the combat again, only difference is that now he won´t be able to use LoH because it can be used only once every 70 minutes.

The main issue while fighting paladins would be to have them under the effects of Fear so they can´t use Cancel Magic nor healing spells, and meanwhile let DoTs run thru them and blast them down very quickly. Another tip is to have all your spells macroed in hot-buttons, so if they happen to blind us, we´ll know which keyboard to hit for the beloved Shadow Step and Gate spells.

Tip 1: Try to dispell them because they got some nice self HP buffs.

 

Spells to have memorized when dueling against a Paladin : Lifetap spell, Shock of poison (if you don´t have this one, the highest lifetap you´ve got), your 2 most powerfull DoTs, Screamimg Terror, Fear, Shadow Step and Gate.

 

How to fight a high level paladin once fear, Screaming Terror and root wont work?  

Exactly the same as you fight a ranger : Cancel Magic him few times to dispell his buffs, Shadow Step, try to land some dissease/poison DoTs (tough to land with decent resist gear), and deflux away while Liching up all the time. Shadow Step - Deflux combo all the way. Also, have cancell magic ready to dispell your own pet in case the Pally fear/roots him (which he will). To keep your pet dispelled is very important because since poison/dissease DoTs are tough to land and the rest of them are dispellable, you need your pet as damage source or you will be out of mana soon.

The only thing you have to remember is to have a high Magic Resistance. You know, if a root, blind or stun does actually land of you, it would mean Game Over. Your larger mana pool (Lich rules) should eventually make a difference.

 

back to PvP selection

 

 

3. Dueling a Shadow Knight:

Shadow knights are and hybrid class, a cross between a necro and a warrior. They usually have our same spells, just 2 circles later (at lvl 29 they get most of our lvl 20 spells including pet). This makes them, in my opinion, absolutly useless casters. Only thing that can irritate you is the Fear spell, but do not worry much about their pet (yours will destroy his very quickly) or attacking spells (they will fight you with Heat blood when you´ve got boil blood, or even venom of the snake. Anyway, have Cancel Magic ready against his minor DoTs) or even their Shadow Step ( we are no mellee class, so no need for us to get toe to toe with them). So, this leads us to a mellee class with fear, cancel magic, lifetaps and Harm Touch.

The main problem with Shadow Knights is the fucking Harm Touch. It does indeed get nasty. Just for your information, a lvl 60 SK's Harm Touch deals 1000 hp instant damage, and if they decide to proc an offensive discipline, it climbs up to 1500 hp !! As you can see, one of those shots will painfully halve your health. 

So, what would a smart SK do? he would fear your pet away, mess around with you for a while in melee, maybe some minor DoTs, maybe some lifetaps here and there, etc. And whenever you let your health bellow half... BANG!!... a freaking Harm "torpedo" Touch on your butt killing you right in the spot. Trust me on this one: You never let your health drop under 60% against a SK because that is exactlly what he will be waiting for.

IMO, the best way to beat a SK is kitting them with Shadow Step and with fast casting lifetaps. Dispell your self which ever minor DoTs they land on you (mainly the darkness and heat DoTs), and try to land a poison/dissease DoT yourself. Your pet will tear his apart very fast so he will also have to waste few secs fearing it away. Also, you must keep cancell magic ready to dispell your pet in case the SK fears him (which he will) because you will need him as damage source or else you will run out of mana eventually.

Remember, the main point is to keep your life always over 60% due to Harm Touch, so it might be a good idea to lifetap dot him too. Even if he dispells himself, you still get healed up. 

My main concern while dueling a SK pre 49 (Lich and Bond of Death are 49 spells and they plain rule) is that you might run out of mana before the SK bites the dust. Lets not forget that a SK has got a bunch of HP and he self heals himself with lifetaps too. It's pretty much the same than fighting a Pally, despite SK are rather more offensive and Pally more deffensive. Of course, you will have your highest Lich line spell up all the time. As I said, you will indeed need extra mana for this fight.

Tip 1 : Unexperienced SKs, for some reasson, always tend to fight you in your very speciallity : fear kite. They always start the duel with darkness, followed by fear. As long as you have a high magic resistance figures you will be safe and the SK will burn his mana becoming nothing more than a warrior who hits softer and with less HPs.

Tip 2 : Harm Touch can be resisted with high Magic Resist even tho I'm not possitive about the certain figure needed. If you can bump your MR over 100, dont hesitaste it. It could save you againts a SK.

 

Spells to have memorized when dueling against a Shadow Knight : highest lifetap you got, 2 DoTs, Screaming Terror, Cancel Magic, Harmshield, Shadow Step and Gate.

 

How to fight a high level SK once fear, Screaming Terror and root wont work?  

He will probably STR unbuff you in order to overweight snare you, hence Shadow Step will be needed since you cannot run fast; deflux away while Liching up all the time (you will need the extra mana). Also, have cancell magic ready to dispell Fear from your own pet for extra damage. Very much alike of Paladins, SKs can wear out your mana pool so try to have your pet to deal some damage too. And never ever let your health unmonitored, or else the Harm Touch will sort you out.

back to PvP selection

 

 

4. Dueling a Druid :

This class' most important adventage is speed (they have SoW and super SoW), and believe me, that´s exactly what they´ll try to perform. They´ll snare your pet (slow him down) and will run away willing you to follow them, hence having your ass far away from your pet-bodyguard. Or, alternatively, they´ll snare you down and run away with your pet on them to kill him easily away from his master. Do not play the druid´s game, don´t chase them. But if he does get to SoW himself away with your pet on his back, just dispell snare with Cancel Magic and run in the oposite direction !!. Have your pet in the target screen (hit F1 button twice) and whenever the pet dies, sit down, memorice pet spell and recast a new one. Cast your highest HP-to-mana transfer spell ( check the spells list for mor info), sit down, re-memorize your previous spell and wait till full mana. By the time he finds you, he´ll go breathless watching a brand new pet, a full mana adversary and his own mana bar a nice chunk down wasted against your former pet !!

Of course, an experienced druid wont do that; he will just agro your pet by snaring you, run with SoW out of your reach (to the other corner of the zone), root him there, and run back to you. With this, when his root brakes free, your pet will be so far away that he will be out of agro range and he will just peacefully walk around :(

Tip : Have a hot key "/pet back off" macro'd in case he snares you and your pet follows him away from your dispelling range. Always call pet off instantly, dont let him drive your pet away.

Keys against a druid: don´t chase him around, don´t let him get away your casting area with SoW spell.

Druids are excellent damage dealers themselves: They got very good lure (never gets resisted) magic based DoT and nasty fire based nukes. So, you should try to improve your FR, but specially you should have cancel magic ready (remember to always leave your first 2 buff slots empty) in order to dispell their DoT. It is very mana expensive DoT and druids dont have mana regen like us, thus he will freak out when his most powerful DoT goes wasted with your mana cheap cancell magic.

Tip 1: You should try to dispell the druid few times because they got excellent buffs : HP regen, SoW, poison/dissease resist buffs (these might hurt you), HP buff, damage shields, etc, etc. So head my advice and dispell him few times at the begining of the fight.

Tip 2: Don't start lifetapping him like crazy because their heals are more mana efficient than your lifetaps (remember that lifetaps deal only 2/3 dmg in PvP but his heals act for full amount). In a necro lifetapping vs. druid healing battle, you will be OOM before him.

Ok, how do we start then? As I said, you should better cancel magic him with few shots to remove his buffs. Also call your pet off if he is pulling him away from you as above mentioned. Once you have dispelled his buffs, dispell your pet (he will 100% sure have rooted or snared him) and yourself too if you got any of his shit on.

From now on, it will all depend on mana managment. As opossed as against other classes, druids must be dueled from a deffence point of view!!  Why? Because of their extremly wide options : they can dot, they can nuke, they can heal, they can run around with SoW, they can even melee you down!! As I said before, it will depend on mana managment. Your main goal is to exhaust their mana while you have always your highest Lich spell up.

So, once he is been dispelled. Stand still and watch what he does. He will probably root/snare your pet. If does so, cancell magic that root/snare, hence the pet will jump of him again. All in all, a fight againts a druid (and againts a shaman too) is a test of endurance, a long term race won by whoever has asministrated his mana the better. Just dont let him confuse you running around and pulling you pet off far away from you and you will win. Cancel Magic is a big asset againts druids too, keep it in mind.

If so, none of his nasty spells will land on you even after being debuffed (which he most likely will) since all of enchanter's spells are magic based. So, you know, switch your gear to MR bonus items and you wont have any problem. 

In case you do not have that MR equipment set, try the following advices, despite enchanter will probably win :

Before accepting a duel with an enchanter, you better know him as a honourable adversary. I mean, enchanters with charm spell can really do evil PvP duels. I´ve seen enchanters charming the other guy as a pet, casting levitate on themselves, and walk you to the middle of any lake for a ´swim´. Of course, they didn´t cast levitate on you so you drown. Or even worse, to a lava pit, where you won´t ever be able to recover your toasted corpse back !! Or what about having you to jump off a cliff? Also, they can charm your butt and have it to punch that bulky grimmy-looking red conning guard. All these ways of dueling are extremly evil since you´ll definately lose exp, and since no server-wide message will be typed proclaming the enchanter of any duel. If you ever are target of any of these shits, petition it straight away. Do not let it go. If many people report this unfair duels, Verant will undoubtly nerfed down Charm spell in duels (they have already done so in PvP servers, pretty much like our Fear spell), and that´s the way it should be.

Ok now, let´s consider the enchanter is a fair player. Still they are the toughest PvP class in game in my opinion. They´ve got mesmerize, stun, manatap spells (same as our lifetap spells, but mana tapping), powerfull DD spells, Fear, blind, Nullify Magic, pet, Charm, buffs, Magic Resistance debuffer, etc.

First of all, it´s most important when dueling to enchanters to have a high Magic Resistance bonus gear because of their Magic Resistance debuffer Tashani spell, and hence you won´t resist any of their spells. Also, it would be usefull if you Cancel Magic them because they buff themselves with Magic Resist buff (not good because our ST and Fear spells are magic based) and Rune (damage absorb spell, like our shieldskin spells).

Let me explain how enchanters duel. Most of the enchanters follow the same pattern while openning up duels : they stun you, they mezmerice your pet and then they mezmerice you afterwards, they slow your pet (he will hit much slowlier), they mana sieve you (spell that "steals" your mana), they remezmerice you and your pet, they keep lowering your mana, they eventually summon a pet, buff him up with haste and they send it to you finishing you off with few nukes (dont understimate enchanter's nukes, they are not bad at all).

We have only one very way to win enchanters in duels : Fear spell. I know I have told you that is far too often resisted, but believe me, no other way around against this fellows. Now, the first seconds of the duel will determine the winner, no doubt about that. It´s important to know the different ways enchanters open up the duel : a) with Tashani debuffer. b) casting the 2.5 secs long casting mesmerize spell on you. c) stun spell (takes only 1 secs to be cast away and stuns you for fews secs).

a) If they start with tashani spell, and therefore drop your Magic Resistance to floor, they´ll cast right afterwards mesmerize. This is the best turn things could take, because that second they waste casting Tashani, is your only chance to hold your Scream Terror on them. ST lasts only about 15 secs, so cast the highest Fear spell you have to prolong their lack of control on themselves. Now, cast your 2 highest DoTs (the quickest to perform their total damage. Lvl 34 spell Venom of the snake is perfect bacause it cannot be dispelled) and chain cast Shock of Poison till you kill him. Trick is to finish them off as quickly as you can. Enchanters are THE masters of control, so if you let them think, you´re a dead necro.

b) If they open up with mesmerize spell, then it´s all about whose spell lands first, either his mesmerize or your ST. Or who resists the best (that´s why a high magic resistance gear is so crucial against casters, specially against enchanters). Pray you are quicker, or you´ll be in deep shit. They´ll probably suck your mana while you´re mesmerized, and they´ll be ready to stun you as soon as mesmerize wears off, hence gaining some more seconds and starting all over again. When you´re mesmerize, keep hitting the Shadow Step button, and again pray for it to be a long distance one.

c) If they start with stun spell and you dont resist it, I´d say you have already lost the battle mate. Once you´re mesmerized, they will suck all your mana and then you´re out of it !! Only solution I could think of is to cast Shadow Step the first and prey God you are faster casting AND it drives you far enough.

I know it really sucks, but whereas soloers none can match a necro, when it comes to dueling I´d state that enchanters are the one to beat, at least pre 50 since at low-mid levels is very difficult to find Magic Resist gear. Post 50 is much easier to beat a chanter because your resists will be higher.

So, how to open up a battle against an experienced enchanter? If I was one of them, I´d definately go for the last of the duels opening up strategies shown above. This leaves as with 1 very only solution : Shadow Step as our first spell, expecting it to take us the hell out of the casting distance. If it does, aproach to the battle area step by step, and between steps hit the screaming terror button. Once you have landed it on him, you´ve got few seconds to cast Fear till it holds, and then just run closer, DoT him (spoison and dissease of course) twice and nuke him down with shock of poison or your highest lifetap spell.

Believe me fellow necromancers, these guys are tough. I consider myself a fairly good and experienced dueler, and I have lost several battles against enchanters (in the pre 50 levels most of them)

Tip: Before dueling an enchanter , cast on yourself your lvl 1 disease spell (check spells list for more info), AND your best Dark Pact series spell. This small amount of damage that you´ll take eack tick (each 6 secs), will brake the mesmerize free, so it´s really worthy to deal with that minor damage in order to gain control of yourself once more after being mesmerized for only 6 secs. Guys, this can save your life against an enchanter.

Tip 2 : Apart from all shown above, the best way to beat an enchanter is to have high magic resistance. At least 120 or so. All the chanter s spells are based on magic : stun, charm, nukes, etc. So if you have a high Magic Resist you ll almost be inmune to any of his spells.

Tip 3 : Alway leave the first few buff slots open to dispell his magic debuffer Tashani spell and his magic based DoTs. Also try to keep dispelling your pet (much alike when dueling a wizard) forcing them to spend mana in roots and mezmerices, and eating up few blows in the process.

Tip 4 : Cancel Magic is a must in order to keep dispelling his Magic Resist and Clarity buffs and his Rune spell (damage absorb). Actually, this Rune spell is the only way enchanters got to mitigate damage.

Due to this, if you are about to duel an experienced shaman, dont even bother to mem your DoTs because you will burn your whole mana bar wihtout making a dent on his health bar.

Also, shamans got our very same poison and disease DoTs (some say that necromancer class "borrowed" them from shamans), and some powerful cold based nukes too. Since poison and disease are easier to resist that cold, I would advice you to try to raise up your PR and DR when fighting a shamy. Check out the chart above listed and you will see that with as little as 70 PR-DR you should almost be inmune to his posison and dissease DoTs considering equal lvl. But beware!! Shaman's malise spell series decrease all resistance a nice deal and it is lure (very tough to resist), so you will definitelly need more that 70 RD-PR. Anyway, if he casts malise on you, cancel it off with cancel magic right away (remember to leave first few buff slots open). If you are in your pre 50 lvls and therefor you dont have a healthy wallet try to gather up few cheapo items with DR-PR boost like stein of moggok (DR+25), green silken drape (PR+15), etc, or if you are in your post 50s you should already have a full set of blue diamond jewelery set.

Since you cannot use your DoTs to beat him, and since you will run oom before killing a shammy with lifetaps (always remember that in PvP heals are more mana efficient than lifetaps), you will have to rely on your pet as damage dealing source. The shaman will of course root it aside and Cancel Magic will become your best friend. Also you will have to take care of his pet if he summons one.

Ok, the duel starts!! Send your pet against his pet. ST the shaman and Fear him right away. If those spells get resisted he probably have buffed himself with resist magic. If this happens you might be in trouble because shamans are the king of buffs, and if he isn't stupid I bet he would first buff himself with a bunch of silly buffs (increase STR, increase AGI, etc) and then the good buffs afterwards. This means it would take you ages to get to the useful buffs and thats not good. My advice if he resists ST and fear couple of times is not to try anymore and do the following:

  • Fear shaman's pet.
  • /pet back off.
  • /pet attack shaman.
  • Chain non stop cast cancel magic on your pet dispelling the shaman's root on him.
  • Renew fear on shaman's pet as needed.
  • Don't let the shaman heal himself. They also have their own form of mana regen via cannibalize spell, so don't expect him to run OOM before you. As long as you keep your pet on him you will be ok.

Ok, let say he doesn't resist your ST / fear combo. Send pet against his pet, ST the shaman, fear the shaman, fear shaman's pet, /pet back off, /pet attack shaman and non stop chain re apply fear on the shaman every few seconds. Don't do anything else but fear the shaman, run along with him for few secs, stop and renew fear, rinse and repeat. As long as fear sticks on him, your pet will sort him out.

 

pells to have memorized when dueling against a Shaman : Lifetap, lifetap DoT (self heal), Fear, Screaming Terror, Shadow Step, your highest pet and Cancel Magic.

High level shamans are indeed one of the toughest adversaries for a necro. They have problems against high magic resistant melees because they can't Shadow Step, and if the tank has got J Boots, their SoW becomes almost useless.

Once this has happend, they root your pet one last time and nuke you down to death with the mana free bracer's nuke. 

Basically, in my opinion, the only way to beat a high lvl shaman is to have awesome saves vs poison, disease and cold. And I really mean awesome. Poison and dissease are easy to beef over 100 each, so no problem there, but the bloody bracer's nuke is another story. Even with 150 CR you will not resist it. Sorry, but thats the cold truth (hehe speaking about cold). Anyway, the main resists you should look at are PR and DR undoubtly.

But lets say you do have enough Sv cold, poison and disease gear to resist his spells. Do not bother no cast any of your DoTs on him, he will dispell em all. Your main weapon will be your pet, so save up  your mana for Anul magic on your pet once the shamy has landed paralizing earth on him or even to recast pet if he dies. Be smart, don't burn your mana and do what I said few lines above: fear his pet off, let your own pet deal the damage for you, and have Cancel Magic handy to dispell your pet or you (he might click his epic on you, which effect is our splurt DoT with just another cheesy name).

The shamy will try to cast his highest malosi (debuffer) spell on you. At high levels thats -45 to all saves !! Of course, you'll have your first few slots on the buffs screen empty to dispell whatever he lands on you promptly.

I dont know, but even if you get to resist his spells and his bracer's nuke, I'd still say the shaman is one of the toughest adversary a necro could face. It will all depend on whether you can keep your pet on the shaman chain Cancel Magic'ing it.

Not as hard as enchanters are, but still a cleric can be a tough fellow. Don't burn your mana, play with your pet and everything will be all right.

Tip 1: Try to wear all your magic resist gear. If you resist his stuns/roots/nukes he will run out of offensive manners. This can be cruicial because stun is a real pain in the very ass.

Tip 2: Clerics can almost double their HPs with their buffs, not to mention their resist figures. It is very very adviceable to cast 3 or 4 times cancel magic on him at the beginning of the fight.

Tip 3: Don't understimate cleric's nukes, especially if your MR gear sucks. Don't let your health drop bellow half or else he could nuke you down in couple of shots.

11. Dueling a Ranger :

Rangers are another tricky and missunderstood class. People tend to see them just as weak melees with some cheesy spells, but thats far from truth. Rangers' damage output is superior to warriors' and they can easily nullify your pet with root and snare. Also they can cancel your magic and heat DoTs and do some self healing.

Pre 50 they are not very tough since they don't have disciplines (special one-per-hour attacks) and they will probably not resist poison/dissease DoTs. Also, fear and root might land: piece of cake.

  • Load two DoTs (dissease and poison, because he will dispell off any other), shadow step and run away with SOW -sow potions are a must in duels-. Notice that I said run away. Don't stay hidden cuz he'll get to you within seconds due to their tracking and archery skills. The ranger will root your pet and chase after you.
  • Eventually, they'll stop chasing you to heal themselves (DoTs are nastily harmful, arent they?). When you notice that he is far away healing up, cast call of bones, lich or whatever you got and sit down to med. Also, if your HP bar isnt full, tap any low lvl mob around.
  • When he has finished healing himself, he will track you and everything with start again : DoT away, Shadow Step, and run away.
  • Thanks god his mana pool is FAR shorter than yours, so he will eventually run OOM -no more heals- and then he is yours.

BTW, always have Cancel Magic ready because he will root/snare your pet, and he can also snare you (very low resisting rate).

 

How to fight a high level ranger once Fear, Screaming Terror and Root wont work?

Here is where real problems begins. Why? very simple: fucking true shot discipline. If they haven't wasted it, they will activate it and drop a bejesus rain of arrows on your ass. What kind of damage are we talking about here? hehe well, extreme damage indeed. For the records, the highest dmg critic score with true shot disc I have ever seen was 1400 hp. Not bad uh?  : )  They don't even need to chase you around. They would root your pet, activate the disc, and nuke you down from distance.

If you notice that he has proc'd it up (and believe your health bar will notice it), Shadow Step the hell away and run (SoW potions are a must) to the other very corner of the zone. The disc lasts for 2 minutes, so basically your only way of survival is to keep yourself out of arrow range. Shadow Step and SoW potions will be your best friends. Remember not to stand still once you have lost sight of him, he will track you down. Just keep moving around clocking true shot discipline till it wears off.

Once the disc is down, follow the same tips stated above for pre 50 rangers. If you see that he is resisting disease and poison DoTs, go shadowstep + deflux in stead.

BTW, always have Cancel Magic ready because he will root/snare your pet, and he can also snare you (very low resisting rate)

 

Spells to have memorized when dueling against a ranger : lifetap (the highest you have),  2 DoTs (disease/poison), Fear, Screaming Terror, Shadow Step, Cancel Magic.

12. Dueling a Necromancer:

Well, well, well. So you´re about to fight your brother-in-believes, aren´t you? I can´t really give you any advice when dueling a nec because your adversary will already know all your tricks. Only thing I´ll tell you is to open up with Screaming Terror. Not all necros use this lovely spell while dueling. This is because they don´t know how to duel with their own character. Land ST on him, ST or fear his pet, call your pet back with /pet back off, load one DoT on the other necro, send pet to your adversary with /pet attack, and chain cast the highest lifetap you´ve got on your spell book. 

Most of the times, while dueling another necro, pets nullify each other and everything turns out a lifetapping battle between the masters. If you have DoTed him in advance, that extra damage will lift you as the winner. If in the middle of the battle you see that you are loosing while lifetapping, cast a Harmshield, wait for him to cast few times on you (burning valuable mana) and cancel the invulnerability (left click on the symbol) just right afterwards his spell has harmlessly landed on you, and straight away lifetap him back.

Not really much else to say. You know how a necro works, just figure out the way to anull him. But you´ll see by yourself how everything will eventually turn into a non-stop lifetapping each other. In this chaos, if you have ST and DoTed him before, you shouldn´t lose this duel mate.

If, in the other hand, he has cast nasty DoTs on you and there´s no way you can keep on with the battle, Shadow Step away, Cancel Magic the magic based DoTs. Then Lifetap some low level zone inhabitants to regen some HP back and med with Lich (or whatever version you got) till he finds you out.

 

How to fight a high level necro once Fear, Screaming Terror and Root wont work?  

High level necro duels mainly depend on lifetaps and pet control. 

At the beginning of the fight both of you will try to anul each other's pet with ST or fear (ST is better IMO; for some reason fear wears off just too quickly). So, before accepting the duel, have his pet targeted and start off ST'ing it. He has probably done the same to yours, so you have to really fast double hit F1 button (targets your pet) and cast anul magic on him. Your pet will jump on your adversary who will ST it once again (probably eating up some beating in the proccess). Don't hesitaste one second and re-anull magic your pet and re-apply ST on the other guy's pet. The idea is chain dispelling ST (or fear or root or whatever) off your pet and renewing ST on his pet every 2 dispells. Doing this he will little by little eat up some damage from your pet and you will keep his pet off of your butt. Once his health is, let's say, half down, re-ST one last time his pet and chain cast your highest lifetap (touch of night better than deflux in a one to one lifetapping battle) on him. 

Tip 1 : It is extremly important that you keep your DR and PR over 75 because if he lands an Envenomed bolt or Cessation of Corr on you it's over, believe me.

Tip 2 : Once the one to one lifetap battle has started, if you see that he has got the casting edge, fire up HarmShield and let his lifetap land unharmlessly on you, click the HS icon off and cast lifetap right away. If you time up this secuence, you will gain the casting edge on him.

Tip 3 : lvl 60 necros will banish your pet away with banishment of shadows spell (check spells list for more info). You should do the same.

Tip 4 : If you have lost your pet and he still got his up, charm it and dispell it. This will poof it away (it's an EQ pvp code bug).

Tip 5 : keep cancel magic ready to dispell any of his DoTs on you, and if he lifetap DoTs you, cancel magic yourself first and him afterwards. Never let a DoT running thru your blood because it will kill you.

Tip 6 : do not burn your mana on heat or magic DoTs. He will dispell them and you will unecesary burn valuable mana.

Tip 7 : If you see that he ain't got enough disease or poison resist (gotta have a quick look to his equipment first) don't hesitaste to use Cessation of Corr or Envenomed blood (check spells list for more info). Those DoTs are PvP killers. If you think he might have enough DR/PR, don't bother and don't waste your time. Necros duels are just too fast to waste even one second.

Just remember : hold his pet ST'd and keep your pet dispelled as long as you can and his health will drop step by step. It is always a lifesaver to have more HP than him when lifetap battle starts.

 

Spells to have memorized when dueling against a Necromancer: Lifetap (last version you got), Lifetap DoT, DoT, Screaming Terror, Cancel Magic, Harmshield and Shadow Step.
