We, the undersigned, free-born and fellow travelers through the worlds of Norrath, joining together in our mutual need, seeking to promote the forces of good wherever it is possible, and fighting the forces of evil wherever they be found, do hereby institute and ordain this Great Charter for the Lords of the Ters'Tai.

Article I: Legislative Powers

The Lords of the Ters'Tai shall be governed by its Council of Lords, to be composed of such Lords who shall be in office at the date of this ratification, and their heirs and successors as designated herein.

  1. The Council of Lords shall have the following powers and duties:

    • To enact policy for the admission of new members into the guild, and to provide for their testing and advancement.
    • To enact policy and rules concerning the conduct of guild members, while engaging in guild activities and when not.
    • To enact policy and rules concerning guild property, both in coin and items.
    • To enact policy and rules for the raising monies, in the form of coin of the realm, for the support of guild activities.
    • To ratify such actions of the High Lord as are required by this Charter, or are requested by the High Lord.
    • To act as the court of final appeal in matters of discipline.

  2. The Council of Lords shall initially be composed of eight Lords, and one High Lord. The High Lord shall not vote in council except in the case of a tie.

  3. The Council of Lords shall meet in regular session, as determined by the Council, not less than one time every two calendar weeks, and in such special sessions as determined by the High Lord.

  4. The High Lord may appoint and designate a member of the Council of Lords to preside in his absence, in such case, said council member shall have his or her regular vote, but shall not cast a vote in the case of a tie.

  5. The Council of Lords shall operate by a simple majority of those Lords present in meeting, excepting those items which are required otherwise by this Charter.

  6. The Council of Lords may expand or contract its number, when in the opinion of the Council it is so necessary. Any such addition or subtraction shall be made by a vote of three-fourths of the members of the Council, and no member of the Council shall lose their seat due to the contracting of the Council.

  7. The Council of Lords shall determine the qualifications for its members, and the means of selecting same.

Article II: Executive Powers

The Guild shall be governed by its High Lord, who shall be, de facto a member of the Council of Lords. The High Lord shall have the following powers and responsibilities:

  1. The High Lord shall preside at all meetings of the Council of Lords, and of the Guild, excepting that he may designate any Lord of the Council to deputize for him at any meeting of the Council, or at any ceremony of the guild.

  2. The High Lord, and/or his appointed agents thereof, shall enforce the actions of the Council.

  3. The High Lord shall supervise the daily activities of the Guild, and shall make such decisions as are necessary to the operation of the Guild.

  4. The High Lord, or his designated agents, shall conduct all relations with other guilds and entity, and the High Lord or his designated agents shall be the lone spokespersons for the guild.
    The High Lord may enter into alliances or treaties with other guilds and entities, but shall submit same for confirmation by the Council of Lords. The Council of Lords shall approve same only by a vote of at least two-thirds of the members of the Council.

  5. The High Lord shall, from time to time, appoint such magistrates or other persons as are necessary to hear matters coming before the guild. A permanent appointment shall be confirmed by the Council of Lords, by a vote of two-thirds of the members thereof. A temporary appointment may be, but need not be confirmed by the Council of Lords.

  6. The High Lord shall have such other duties and responsibilites as are requested and required of him by the Council, and which are not inconsistent with this Charter.

Article III: Judicial Function

From time to time it becomes necessary that the guild settle differences, and to discipline its members. Disputes shall be first mediated, and then if necessary, formal procedures invoked to resolve the matter.

  1. Mediation will be the preferred method of solving disputes within the guild, and without. Mediation shall be conducted by any member of the guild so designated by the High Lord. If the High Lord is not available, any Lord of the Council may appoiont either himself or another member of the guild to mediate. The mediator shall bring the two parties together, and, shall in an impartial way attempt to negotiate an end to the dispute that allows both parties to retain their honor and dignity. If the mediator is unsuccessful, the mediator shall refer the matter to a Judicial Lord, for decision.

  2. Judicial proceedings shall be conducted by a member of the Council of Lords so designated by the High Lord. In the absence of the High Lord, any member of the Council of Lords may be called upon to appoint himself, or appoint another member of the Council to sit as a Judicial Lord.

  3. The High Lord may, at his option, designate a permanent Judicial Lord, who shall be confirmed by the Council of Lords, and who shall supervise all matters of discipline and mediation. The Judicial Lord shall in all other ways be a member of the Council of Lords.

  4. Any appointment of a permanent Judicial Lord shall be confirmed by the Council, by a two-thirds vote of the same, and they shall serve during their good behaviour.

Article IV: Amendment

This Charter may be amended by a three-fourths vote of the Council of Lords, and the subsequent ratification by the membership of the guild. This ratification shall be made by a two-thirds vote of the membership of the guild, within such time period as determined by the Council. Amendments to this Charter shall be numbered, and shall be inserted after the concluding article of this Charter.

Article V: Ratification

This Charter shall become effective upon its ratification by the councils of the Lords of the Ters'Tai, and the Council of the Guild of Viators.
