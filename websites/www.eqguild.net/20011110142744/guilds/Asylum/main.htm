Welcome to the halls of the Arcane Asylum.

     The Arcane Asylum was founded by Surien Stormsong and Melechor with the goal of bringing unity to all classes and races.  Through our experiences as a guild, we hope to create a peaceful forum for teaching and learning how to work together in battle to form a cohesive and effective fighting force.  Specifically--combine the power of the casters and the might of the warrior classes--and watch the bad guys fall!

     An often heard disagreement concerns the place and function of each class working in a group; in other words, what should the caster be doing and what should the warrior be doing?  What spells should or shouldn't a caster cast at a certain time?  At what point should a cleric or healer stop trying to heal people and join in the fight?  When should a warrior pull a mob?  These questions are endlessly debated in groups, adding undue stress to the battlefield.

     Surien laments, "I have been in groups where I wish I could have sent a blast right up my group members' rear ends and ditched them.  I have also been in groups where everyone seemed to work so well together it was like magic in itself.  We become a powerful fighting force when there is a unity of minds.  I want this guild to learn and to teach ways of accomplishing this unity, this magic."

With these lofty goals in mind, here are but a few of the Arcane Asylum's ideals:

Guild Conduct:    

Arcane Asylum is NOT a warring guild.  Guild wars cause too much hostility both within the guild and among the various guilds in EverQuest: This goes against our goals.  Members of this guild are expected to act in a kindly and helpful manner whenever possible; however, not to the expense of themselves or the guild.  Defending the honor of the guild against hostile outsiders is more than expected, it is a necessity, barring the declaration of guild wars.

     Arcane Asylum does not believe in a free-ride for ourselves or our guild members.  Charity is freely given when members feel moved to do so -- we don't give handouts.  Our guild members grow through experience, not through begging.

Character Development: 

It is the hopes of this guild that we become self-sufficient, not only as efficient casters and warriors, but also as tailors, blacksmiths and the like.  To be well-rounded characters, we must develop skills which set us apart from the others.
