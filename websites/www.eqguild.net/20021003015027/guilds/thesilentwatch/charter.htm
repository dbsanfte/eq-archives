I. The Silent Watch

The Silent Watch is oldest European guild in Karana server. We use english chat and we have members from all around the globe. Our raiding times most of the time are from 6pm GMT/1pm EST to 1am GMT/8pm EST. We are currently allied with Skyshrine (CoV faction) and Thurgadin. We are part of the Karana Guild Central (KGC)

II.Behavior

A member of TSW is a representative of the guild as a whole. Remember to always be civil with those you come into contact with, and show as much respect as you would expect back. Disagreements between members should be handled in tells, and outbursts in /gu can lead to a warning from an officer or further action deemed appropriate by the Council.

III. What TSW expects from you

    Weekly raids will be split up into several categories. For large target raids, everyone online is expected to turn up. Exceptions can only be made if you have
    contacted an officer prior to the raid. Events which are hastily planned on the day are not however compulsory attendance.

    Raid days to xp days are generally in a 3 to 4 ratio depending on rotation targets and random spawns. For large target random spawns, attendance is required if you are online. Even if you are only online for a few hours, your help is valued. Attendance is noted in regards to loot.

IV. TSW Goal

The main aim of TSW is to go forward and experience all the challenges within Norrath and beat them, while still having fun along the way.

V. Use of /gu

We keep guildchat open minded (our /gu is rated PG +18 most of the time), but it is not a channel for general moaning or about loot. During guild events, /gu can be used for whatever purpose, but the raid channel must be kept clear when its game time. Disputes between members should be carried out in tells and not in guildchat. Outbursts in /gu will not be tolerated.

VI. Loot - TBP

TBP is TSW's point based allocation system for loot. The necessary points are described below:

1. Every guild raid attended by a member will be said to be worth 1 point. A guild raid is defined as a coordinated effort by 3+ groups of people (18 or more).

2. The points are stored in a seperate forum on the guild messageboard. Members can view their total points in this forum, and post for corrections where they think appropriate. It is the members responsibility to know how many points they have.

3. Every item dropped on a guild raid will be assigned a point value, the majority of which will be pre-defined in the database, and also kept track of in the TBP forum. Members can then view this list as they wish. Members are also encouraged to check up on the stats of items they are interested in, in advance.

4. To be in for a lotto you must have points equal or higher than the item cost. If noone with enough points want the item a member that want it can roll for it and go negative points. No member with 0 or less points can roll for any items. If noone still want the item we either bank the item if MQable or not no drop or lower the point cost for no drop items.

5. Maximum points allowed to roll with on any item will be 40, even if the member have more than this.

6. If a member wins a 10 point item, the database will be updated and the member will have 10 points less than prior to gaining the item. This takes effect immidiatly.

7. Every member is only allowed to win 1 (one) item in any single raid or any single day. The only exception to this are tertiary items which drop on the way to a Target, which when announced will be handled seperatly from the Target's drops.

8. Drops are defined in 3 categories. General items are handled by straight point based rolls with the above requirements in effect. Quest and Epic items will cost points, but their allocation is decided by GL/Officers generally in advance. ST keys are seperate again and currently slated for 12 people who will form the core group for ST. The keys will be lottoed between those 12, if they have the points till all have keys. Then, the keys will become General items to the point of anyone can roll with the required points, as long as they are lvl60 and have been in TSW for 6 months.

9. Loot officers call out the item with stats and how many points the item is worth. The members that can use it and want it then tell the officer how many points he have. The officer can check if its right by checking the database. The officer will then calculate the max roll and let the members roll. The item will be announced 3 times so that every member sees it. When rolls are completed, an officer will check the winners roll does coincide with the number on the webpage. Where a member has given their points incorrectly, they will be deducted upto 10 points.

10. Members not participating in the current event or are VERY late are not allowed to roll on the items.

11. Quest and Epic encounters that require 3+ groups will cost points to initiate. Contact an officer to discuss this.

12. Maximum of the roll will be calculated like this: Random X*10 to (Y*10)+100, where X is your points and Y is the points from the member with the highest points participating in the roll.

Example:

We kill CT and brain of CT drops, it costs 10 points. Roydal, Jayk, Darkivy and Rezam are the only members that wants the item.

Roydal got 45 points
Rezam got 20 points
Darkivy got 10 points
Jayk got 5 points

Darkform doesnt have enough points and are not allowed to roll for the item.

Max roll is determined from Roydals points (45) because he have most, but no more than 40 are allowed to roll with. The 3 remaining will then roll:

Roydal: /random 400 500
Rezam: /random 200 500
Darkivy: /random 100 500

Everyone can win this roll, but Roydal have been to most events and got the most points so he have the biggest chance to win the item. Roydal is unlucky though and rezam win the item. So in the database after the raid we will have the following points, included the point from participation in the event:

Roydal 46 points
Rezam 11 points
Darkivy 11 points
Jayk 6 points

13. Prospective members with a probationary tag do not earn any points nor can roll on an item, unless it is going to rott. Once a probationary member becomes a full member, they can start earning points and rolling on items.

14. In the interests of equipping MTs to be the taunt machines the guild needs, they will be given first refusal on certain items - in cases where this will occur, it will be announced before hand.

VII. Membership

To apply for membership in The Silent Watch, you need the following.

Have a sponsor within the guild
Minimum level to join TSW is 58
Have sufficient resist gear +100 in all resists unbuffed
Seb Key

Application Process

It is wise not to be guilded while you apply for membership in TSW. If you meet the above requirements then you can post in 'Applying to join' forum and your application will be moved to the private section of our message board for consideration from our members. Prior to this an interview with the recruiting officer and class leader will be arranged. In the period of one month you need to gather 15 positive votes, we strongly suggest to group with TSW members so that will get to know you and you know them. Also you can contact your sponsor to find information about upcoming events and raids. If you have any negative votes during that period we will look into the situation and extend the evaluation period if needed. If your application is rejected you can re-apply after the period of two months.

Probation and Loot

Once you are invited you will have a probationary status for two weeks. GL and Officers will be able to evaluate your overall contribution to the guild. During this period as a probationary member you are not entitled to roll on any item that drops on a guild raid. It is a time to prove instead that you want to be a part of the guild. The goal of your probationary month is to asses whether you are a helpful member of not - if you do not help others, no one will help you, after all. At the end of this one month probationary status you will be considered a Full Member or rejected.

Special cases may apply and an applicant will be considered without having to go through the full application process, this is up to Council.

Alternative Characters

When your main character is guilded you are allowed a maximum of 2 alternative characters guilded. However, remember that we guilded your main for a specific reason, we don’t need your alts. For those with dual accounts, you still have one main only, and loot is allocated as such.

VIII. Guild Removing

If you guild remove from TSW for whatever reason the following will happen:

i. First remove will result in a 24hour ‘cooling off’ period before re-invite to the guild.

ii. Second remove will result in another 48hour ‘cooling off’ period, and the case brought to the Council’s attention.

iii. Third remove will require you to re-apply from scratch for membership after a month.

IX. Quests and Epics

Probationary members are not entitled to ANY guild help with quests or epics until their first month is up and they recieve full member status. All requests for help must be made to an officer, and will be schedualed when possible.

However, certain contested spawns (eg. Scout Charisa) are viable cases where a full member may ask for help in /gu. Probationary members may ask for no help at all in regards to quests or epics.

The GL still has the right to make decisions in absence of the Council as appropriate.

Problems related to guild or its members should be addressed to the appropriate person. Contacting an officer is the first port of call, who will in turn relate to a Council member and finally GL if it cannot be resolved.

XI. Officers

The appointed officers within the guild should be treated with the respect due their ' position - respect their decisions. All officers within the guild have a specific role. Problems can this way be addressed to the appropriate person and by-pass the GL and Council where possible.

For instance, if a member has a problem, perhaps with a decision or another member, go straight to one of the internal relations officers and express your concern and offer a solution, that way the problem can be nipped in the bud early, if there is one.
