Welcome to the home of the Greycat Tribe.

We are currently aspiring to create a new guild on the Luclin server. Once we have a quorum we will then make the guild official.

Our Charter

We are dedicated to the exploration of Luclin and Norrath. As a result the tribe will migrate once every two weeks or so to new territory. We are a tribe of nomads with a proud Vah Shir history although we accept members from any race and class. We help one another and our friends. We ask that all members work on tradeskills and languages as well as their class skills. We are highly honorable as we must depend on each other for our very existance. Most of all, we want to have fun and build community spirit. We are democratic, we vote on where to move to and Guild leadership and policy.

History of the Greycat Tribe

The Greycat Tribe has a history reaching back beyond the first days of Vah Shir civilization. At one time the Greycat's held the throne of the Vah Shir empire but the royal line was slain by the ancestors of the current king. Stories were told among the tribe of treachery by the usurper but the younger generations having worked with the king through the time of troubles when the Vah Shir were exiled from Norrath have formed a grudging respect for him. They have even begun to question the validity of the stories and seek to know if our royal line was itself corrupt and lost in decadance before they lost the throne.

The Greycats have always been nomads and are skilled in trades, languages, and the art of making profit from transporting the hard to find supplies and items that they encounter on their travels. The Greycats are proud, never begging for anything, and always seeking to find some way to pay back any kindness shown to us and also seeking ways to do a kindness for others, especially if it doesn't cost us any of the money we need to see us through our wandering lifestyle. We maintain a list of both friends who have done us good so that all might know to aid them and of all who have done harm to one of our members so that they might be placed in coventry. We are all ambassadors for our tribe and all should be glad to see the Greycats travels bringing them together with new friends.

The Greycats are interested in different cultures and peoples. We are concerned generally with the history of Norrath and Luclin and their races. We are passionate about Vah Shir history and are always seeking to learn more about the history of the old kingdom and the circumstances of the disaster which exiled us from Norrath. We are looking for the truth of the stories of the usurpation and if the King's ancestors did behave treacherously then our blood will demand vengence and we will move to slay the king. To aid us in this we are devoted students of language.

Guild Policy

1. No begging, we are too proud to do so. Use of the Beg skill is OK however since it can be used to taunt and doesn't annoy other players.
2. With the exception of the founding members we need to start the guild, no one can join the guild until they are 10th level or above. Lower levels can still be affiliated with us, helped by us and migrate with us. The reason for this is that lower levels are considered Cubs and as such would be kept isolated and protected by the tribe until they have the skills and maturity to deal with outsiders.
3. We are democratic, we can vote on Guild leadership, Guild Policy and Migration Destinations. The current leader is only doing the job because it was his idea for the guild and has never run a guild before so please be patient with him.
4. Officers are required to check on the members they recruit to see that they are doing what the guild needs them to do and that they are getting what they need from the guild.
5. Anyone can be made an officer to help in recruiting since we'll need people who are on at different times and those people who choose to stay in one place and not migrate would also be useful for recuiting. Officers with fancy titles will be created as special rewards for outstanding Guild activities.
6. Priority is given to Corpse finding and retrieval since that is a task we all must face and can often be a difficult challenge.
7. We are a roleplaying guild so please try to remain in character as much as possible. Also it is fun to think up a history and lineage of your character and to create tribal lore events and history that we can add to enrich the guild.
8. Every member must take on one apprentice at some point, they can take on more if they want but one is required. The apprentice should be about 10 levels below the teacher and should be helped for about five levels. Help can include guarding their backs while they fight, grouping, buffing, healing, etc. Help does not have to include giving gifts if you have no surplus.
9. We migrate approximately every two weeks at an agreed upon time or in shifts if we get enough people who play at different times. If someone takes the time to bind us all then they will be rewarded by everyone chipping in a PP or two so that they can buy new spells since it would be a pretty boring process for them and unproductive. Group, bind, bind, bind, bind, meditate, degroup, group again, bind, bind, bind, etc.
10. Among the tribe, when asking some tradesperson to make you something you will supply the raw materials needed plus a bit more so that they can practice their skill and have something to sell. Tradeskills are very important to nomads so everyone needs to develop at least one tradeskill.
11. Language skills are also very important to nomads. Everyone should train a point in any new languages they see offered. We will try and hold language fairs where everyone spouts gibberish at one another in their chosen language until the language skills improve.
12. We are strict adherants to the play nice policy. Annoying other players is strictly verboten. If we choose to camp a site we'll be nice and share with anyone doing the same thing. We will also do our bit to be nice to other players regardless of guild. If we encounter annoying players we'll put them on a list here so everyone can add them to their /ignore and place them in coventry.
13. Guild punishment, which I hope will never ever be necessary, will include probation for 2 weeks, being placed in coventry, fines, and banishment. The Greycats themselves will be called to sit in councel to determine what will happen to any given offender.
