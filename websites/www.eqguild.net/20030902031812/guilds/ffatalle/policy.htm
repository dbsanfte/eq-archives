Our Guild Policies

General
All members of FF will act with courtesy and respect to other players, GM and the Rules and Customs of Norrath. Intentional ks'ing, ninja looting, bad mouthing FF and/or it's members in public forums will not be tolerated. Begging for money, items and/or pl'ing from guild members is frowned upon (this is in ALL guilds, not just FF). Members have been known to offer these things as they see fit or as they have time.

New Members
In order to become a member of Femmes Fatalle candidate must:
-be at least level 10 (no minimum level for alts of established members)
-introduce yourself on our guild Message Board
-join us on one of our weekly xp group nights
-have the endorsement of a current member
-Unknown players will be admitted on a trail basis to ensure that they are a "good fit"

Governance
FF has a guild leader (required by EQ) and a Circle of Elders.   The size of the Circle of Elders will be dependant on the size of the guild.
The role of Guild Leader shall be rotated among the Circle of Elders approximately every 3 months.
The classes shall be divided into Rings for easier management of guild governace, each Ring shall have a leader who may or may not be a Senior Officer.  
The Rings shall be as follows:
Blue Ring: Bards, Beastlords, Clerics, Shamans
Green Ring: Druids, Rangers
Red Ring: Monks, Paladins, Rogues, Shadow Knights, Warriors
White Ring: Enchanters, Magicians, Necromancers, Wizards

Officers
General
-Show themselves to be helpful, knowlegdable, mature and have a "good head on their shoulders."
-Be online regularly.
-Must attend guild meetings and functions whenever possible.
-All officers have the authority to invite new members.  Only the guild leader may make guild officers.
-Promotion of the Guild through recruiting, functions and setting an example of courtesy and honor for all.
-In keeping with the flavor of Femmes Fatalle, only female characters may be Elders, Senior Officers and preferrably Ring Leaders.

Circle of Elders
-Minimum level of 40
-Elders are responsible for all final policy decisions, final dispute decisions, and approval of all officers.
-Will serve the guild as leader as name comes up in alphabetical rotation. Each term of service shall be for no more than 4 consecutive months.

Senior Officers
-Minimum level of 35
-Senior Officers are responsible for help in resolving disputes, input on all policy decisions.

Ring Leaders
-Minimum level of 35
-May or may not be a Senior Officer
-Ring Leaders are responsible for resolving disputes, when possible, input on policy decisions, promotion of activities and advancement of thier Ring through experience groups, quests, raids and work on epics.

Junior Officers
-Shall also include Quests Officer, Raid Leaders and any other positions the Circle of Elders deems necessary.
-Minimum Level 35
-Specific responsibilites shall be determined by the nature of the officers position.

Quests Officer
Promotion and help members with quests.  This officer is not required to do research on requested quests, This shall be the responisibility of the requestee.  Though Quest Officer should be knowledgable of a variety of quests in Norrath.

Raid Leader
To schedule, organize and in most cases lead guild raids.  If a particular raid is requested by a member then that member (or members) should work with the raid leader on research of the zone before the scheduled raid day.

Resolving Disputes
If a member has a problem that is guild related (i.e. a policy, problem with another guild member, etc), member should first talk with their Ring Leader.   If the member does not feel that thier problem has been resolved or if the Ring Leader feels they can not resolve the dispute to the satisfaction of the member, then the dispute should be taken next to a Senior Officer.  If this still does not provide results, then apply to a member of the Circle of Elders.

Disciplinary Actions/Removal from Guild
Should a problem arise with a member of FF that is deemed harmful to FF, it's members, or other players, said problem shall be presented to the Circle of Elders for action. The Elders may elect to place the "problem" member on probation for a specific length of time (usually 2-4 weeks). Said member will be notified of the Circle of Elders decision and reasons why such action was taken. During this probation period any continuing problems with said member will be considered grounds for guild removal. All information concerning this action will be kept confidential among the Elders, Officers, and any members involved.

Only the Guild Leader or a member of the Circle of Elders may remove a member from the guild and only with a unanimous vote by the Circle of Elders.
