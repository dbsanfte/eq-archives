Introduction: Preventing Stagnation with a Common Union


Multiple guilds have fallen from simple stagnation. It might have been due to a lack of initiative or simply not knowing where to go once they reached a certain point in development. Keeping guild activities and member advancement moving is critical to avoid stagnation and withering of guilds.

A Common Union is made to ward off stagnation, allow people to know they are accomplishing something, and keep everyone actively involved with their guild. As an added bonus, a guild participating in a Common Union is able to focus and holding their values while still helping its members improve their characters.

In a CU, 2 guilds work together achieving reasonable, short term goals. The goals can be anything within the guilds' ability to accomplish together. While 1 guild is trying to achieve its goal, the other guild will help and have to wait until it is finished until anyone will work on their next goal. This drives both guilds to do their best helping their partner guild with its own goal. The less help that is received, the longer it will be until the helping guild will receive help with their goal. Both guilds must work their hardest for the CU to work for themselves and in addition for their partner.
