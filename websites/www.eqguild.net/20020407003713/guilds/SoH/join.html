The Shield of Honor is an Invitation Only guild. What that means is that, while anyone is free to ask for admission, the process is not a quick one--we do not discourage requests, but we are very up front in saying that we are striving for quality of members instead of quantitiy. We have no desire to be the biggest guild in existence, only the best.

With that in mind, there are some qualifications that potential members are required to meet. Those currently agreed on, or under discussion by the Guild council, are as follows:

Language
Firiona Vie is a Role-Playing server. As such, we require that our members refrain from using symbolic English--meaning the substitution of symbols for words, as in "4" for "For" or "U" for "You." There is no requirement at this time for speaking Archaic English (thee, thou, thusly, etc.) although it is certainly acceptable; we only ask that if such language is used, that the user know the proper manner of using it. In short, normal American English is acceptable, contractions and all, but no slang or offensive phrasing is allowed.

Behavior
The theme of our guild is stated clearly in the title: Honor. By saying "Shield of Honor" we are not just promoting a catchy name, but a manner of behaving--honor in all things, both to your guildmates and also to the fellow travelers you meet in the course of the game. Members of this guild are required to act honorably in all ways and at all times. Exactly what is meant by "honor" and "acting honorably" is discussed elsewhere on this site.

Dues
While there is not a formal dues system, the guild does have a Treasurer and a general fund. Since that fund is intended to help the membership in its entirety, the entirety of the membership is intended to contribute to it. Contributions can range from old equipment that you no longer need to items that you have looted but cannot use. Cash is also acceptable, but it is realized that in the game--as in real life--there is rarely such a thing as "extra cash." To make up for this, any donated items that are not claimed will be sold for cash after a set period of time, with that cash going to the general fund. One such donation, large or small, must be made every 2 weeks, nor can extra weeks be bought in advance--2 items cannot be donated simultaneously to cover a full month, for example.

Registration
In order to keep things running as smoothly as possible, the guild makes use of several means of communication and organization. One of these is the guild discussion group, hosted by Yahoo; the group discussion page, including subscription and posting instructions, can be found here. Another involves the many services offered by Guildmagic.com, where members are required to register characters and keep the information updated; their front page can be reached here. Each character is also required to have a profile posted on this site in the Members section; these profiles generally consist of a photo (screenshot or other) of the character plus a brief background--family, friends, likes and dislikes, etc.

Please note that these are not suggestions, they are requirements; the invitation to join will not be extended until they are met.

Attitude
Perhaps the most important factor of all, as well as the hardest to judge, is your attitude. Part of the initiation process is a long period of time during which you will be required to spend time hunting with members of the guild, individually or in groups. The purpose behind this is two-fold: first, so that the guild can get a feel for what you are like, what your attitudes and behaviors are, and whether or not you will fit in as a member; second, so that you can do the same--get a feel for the guild, what our attitudes and behaviors are like, and whether your desires and goals can fit into ours. While the time for this is required, the span is variable; it will never be less than a week of game-time, with the member assessing you being responsible for keeping track of time spent.


If this seems like a lot of work and a lot of bother to you, guess what--you are right. The invitation process is intentionally long, drawn-out, and filled with details that only someone who really wants to be a member will put up with...becaue that is the sort of person we want, and that is the sort of guild we are building. If all of this seems to be too much, you are in the wrong place; there are plenty ofd casual guild in existence, and one of them is for you. If, on the other hand, you are serious about your gaming and in your desire to acheive the fullest, richest, and most entertaining experience possible...then we are pleased to meet you, and more than willing to give you the chance.

Llanwe na Finn
Senior Master, Order of the Staff
