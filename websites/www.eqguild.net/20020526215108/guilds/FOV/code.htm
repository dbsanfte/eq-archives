Code of Conduct

 

Above all - HAVE FUN! The ultimate purpose of the Fellowship is to enhance the enjoyment of EverQuest for those of us who play it...whether this be by helping someone out with a heal or a buff, to achieve an item they have been questing for or simply to be there to talk to.

All new members will need to read the Fellowship's Charter and agree to these rules before they are accepted into the Fellowship. They will then be considered on probation for their first month, during which time the guild can get to know them and visa versa. It is also asked that new members sign up on our message board and make a habit of checking the board at least twice a week regarding current guild events. All members are asked to register their characters and alts at guildmagic.com so they may be listed on our roster. All alts are welcome to join (after new members have finished their probationary period) but will not be invited into the guild by an officer until they have registered at guildmagic.

We will only accept applications from characters that have achieved the minimum level of 6. We feel it is necessary for a new player to become familiar with the basic mechanics of the game and the structure of the world before attempting to join into a guild. However, if someone is an experienced player, having another character(s) above level 6, and wishes to enter our guild with a new character, we will accept their application.

Members shall conduct themselves in a mature manner, rude and antisocial behavior does not befit being in The Fellowship. Playing an evil character does not give you the right to be mean and abusive. The use of vulgar or foul language in open text (ie: /OOC, /SHOUT, /AUCTION, or regular /SAY's where a third party/parties could hear it) will not be tolerated, and repeated offences could be grounds for dismissal from the guild.

Harassment of another player will not be tolerated. If a dispute arises between a guild member and another character, for whatever reason, and it can not be resolved by either party, then the guild member should then /Ignore the offending character and, if necessary, report the reason for the dispute to a GM. Under no circumstances should the arguement be taken to /OOC or /SHOUT channels.

The practice known as "kill stealing" will also not be tolerated. That is to say, the intentional taking of a kill from someone else who was fighting it first. This is an ambiguous area, and common sense and respect for others in a group situation must be used. When you arrive in a new zone you wish to camp in, ask for a camp check. Then if upon reaching the camp that you presume empty only to find a camp, send a tell to ask if they are camping as they may have missed the afore mentioned camp check request. If you are the victim of kill stealing, do not use /OOC or /SHOUT to deal with the problem, send a tell to the person and let them know you are camping that area. If they continue to Ksteal, petition a GM. Please remember that wandering creatures are uncampable and you are only entitled to it if it aggros you first or you lay the first blow.

Under no conditions is it acceptable to play using tactics that are considered "dirty" or "unfair". This includes such practises as 'ninja looting', buying low/selling high, and/or 'loot hogging'. This is not helping anyone but that person, not to mention it takes advantage of other players. As should go without saying, your actions reflect on the guild as a whole, and therefore, you should act in a manner that won't adversely affect our image.

Given that this a Role Playing Game, having the roleplaying tag on is warmly accepted, but under NO circumstances should the /Anonymous tag be used as this takes your guild tag off. If you are not proud of the guild you belong in then you shouldn't have the tag at all and simply type /guildremove while having yourself targeted.

Welcome to The Fellowship of Vazaelle, Seeya in there!
