Arcane Asylum Rules Of Conduct

The Fourteen Commandments

 

1.            Everyone in the guild should be supportive of one another. This increases the guild's overall experience, strength and morale.

 

2.         When members disagree with one another, PLEASE keep it civil and between individuals.  Please keep the disagreement in Tells and out of Guild Chat.  If the dispute cannot be resolved, contact the Guild Leader or any officer who is online.  DO NOT bring other individuals into the dispute.

 

3.         All new members have a probationary period. The officer who invites a prospective member is that character's Sponsor.  The sponsor is responsible for the prospective member's character conduct.  The officer must also report information such as class and race to the guild secretary and/or the guild web master.

 

4.         All guild members should know who the guild officers are.  If you do not know the guild officers: ask.

 

5.         When asking another guild member for items (especially lore and hard to get ones) it is bad form to turn around and sell those items.  This can lead to distrust in the guild.  Any member caught doing so will be warned and possibly removed from the Arcane Asylum.

 

6.         The exploitation of bugs in the game is against the terms of service (TOS) agreement with Verant.com and will not be tolerated.  Use of bugs in the game is grounds for immediate banishment from the guild.  Notification to all other guilds of the transgression and the incident WILL be reported.

 

7.            Arcane Asylum members can and are encouraged to assist other guild members in helping fulfill quests.  Other non-member players may be given regular hints, otherwise how will they learn the game?  This doesn't mean we are to give each other details on the quests or complete the quests for each other, only that we will steer our members in the right direction.  Upper level guild members will not camp an item for you.  If you want a hard to get LORE/QUEST item you must be of sufficient level to help participate in the questing for the item.

 

8.            Arcane Asylum DOES NOT twink its members; however, some members might be willing to pass unused equipment on to those who can use it.

 

9.            Begging for cash or items is not allowed in Arcane Asylum.  Begging is any repeated asking or pleading that becomes annoying or distracting to other members.  Anyone found doing so will be warned and reminded that constant begging will result in removal from the guild.

 

10.       Power leveling is not a standard practice in Arcane Asylum and will not be treated as such.  No guild member is to harass or pester any other guild for power leveling.  However, if a guild member offers to power level another member, as long as both parties agree, the guild as a whole will not look down on this practice.

 

11.       No Ninja Style Looting.  This type of looting (i.e. running in when the corpse opens up and looting) is looked down upon in Arcane Asylum.  Most people will not mind letting others loot a corpse or two; however, be sure to ask.  This will alleviate confusion and unintended hard feeling between guild members and other guilds.

 

12.            Auctioning items in Guild chat is an acceptable practice providing the person auctioning the item is not spamming the guild with their auction request.  Please limit auctioning in guild chat to once per hour (real time).

 

13.       Rude or obnoxious behavior of any kind is unacceptable in Arcane Asylum.  Every member is considered a guild ambassador in the EQ world and should act and behave as such.  This includes actions in guild chat and in the general game itself.

 

14.       While playing in a group, all members should remember that they are responsible for whether their character lives or dies.  In other words, if your character is in trouble, dont expect others to save you or to take the blame if you didnt try to escape soon enough.
