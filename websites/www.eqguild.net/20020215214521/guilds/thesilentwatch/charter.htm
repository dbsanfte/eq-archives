I. The Silent Watch

The Silent Watch is oldest European guild in Karana server. We use english chat and we have members from all around the globe. Our raiding times most of the time are from 5pm GMT/12pm EST to 1am GMT/8pm EST. We are currently allied with Skyshrine (CoV faction) and Thurgadin. We are part of the Karana Guild Central (KGC)

II.Behavior

Be civil to all members of TSW and all other people you come into contact with, remember that your actions always represent the guild as a whole. We are stronlgy against Kill Stealing and training (this does not include our pullers pulling in guild raids).

III. What TSW expects from you

    Turn up for raids, not all, not compulsary, but some (eg epic) you must attend if your online. It would be appreciated if you could attend as many as possible while you are online. While on a raid, listen to the raid leader and pullers, and follow their instructions.

IV. TSW Goal

Have fun. We are not an uber guild not planning to become one, we do what we do to have fun and we really enjoy big challenges.

IV. Have Patience

If you require help with an epic mob or help getting an item, think that we have over 100 people in this guild, the majority of which are 50+ so some patience is going to have to be given as not just you will want something done. The more people you help, the more chance you have of those people helping you in return. Never give up on something and always have faith, if something turns bad during a raid learn from it and have patience. Some of the raids might be level restricted depending of the nature and the difficulty of them (eg +55 for ToV), we all have our chances in the future.

V. Use of guildchat

We keep guildchat open minded (our /gu is rated PG +18 most of the time), but it is not a channel for moaning about loot. During guild events, /gu should be kept to a minimum. Disputes between members should be carried out in tells and not in guildchat.

VI. Loot

The majority of loot on a guild raid will be given out in an 'award' fashion. TSW has a dedicated Loot Master who keeps track of everything given out on guild raids, along with notes on attendance, to work out who should be awarded what items.

In the instance of a item which will not be used by a main present, it is lotto'ed for whatever purpose - in some cases, items are held to be sold to fund the guild bank.

In the case where you receive an upgrade to a non no drop item, it would be appreciated, but not expected, for you to hand down your previous item.

General etiquette on raids:

  • Don’t spam /gu for stats, they will be announced
  • Don’t madly try and check a mobs corpse - group leaders only are responsible for reporting items and looting non no drop loot or cash items.
  • Have patience, and your time will eventually come.

This system is built on trust, and has worked well for a long time now. If you do have a problem with a decision made on a raid, take it up with the loot master at the end of the raid, not during it. Note, that for new members joining the guild still within their probation month, they will generally not be considered for loot over a primary who needs the item

VII. Membership

To apply for membership in The Silent Watch, you need the following.

  • Have a sponsor within the guild
  • Minimum level to join TSW is 55
  • Have sufficient resist gear +80 in DR/PR/CR/FR/MR (unbuffed)
  • Seb Key

It is wise not be guilded while you apply for membersip in TSW. If you have the above requirements then you can post in 'Applying to join' forum and your application will be moved to the private section of our message board for consideration from our members. Prior to this an interview with the recruting officer and class leader will be arranged. In the period of one month you need to gather 15 positive votes, we strongly suggest to group with TSW members so that will get to know you and you know them. Also you can contact your sponsor to find information about upcoming events and raids.  If you have any negative votes during that period we will look into the situation and extend the evaluation period if needed. If your application is rejected you can re-apply after the period of two months.

Once you are invited you will have a probationary status of one month. GL and Officers will be able to evaluate your overall contribution to the guild. During this period priority on loot will be given to full members (eg. ToV BP drops,  cleric and  warrior (prob. satus) need the BP, the BP will be awarded to the cleric). This also applies when it comes to epic items/raids, full TSW members will have priority over a spawn or an epic item as long as they want the specific item/spawn (eg. RF spawns, X cleric and Y cleric (prob. status) need it to finish their epics, X cleric is unable to camp RF, Y cleric will get all the help he/she needs to complete the epic). At the end of this one month probationary status you will be considered a Full Member or rejected.

Special cases may apply and an applicant will be considered without having to go through the full application process, this is up to GL and officers.
