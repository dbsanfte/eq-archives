What does it mean to role-play?

Role-playing is building a character around a personality you create. That personality may be anything your imagination allows. Your character may or may not be anything like you in RL.

Role-playing is a form of acting, without a script. You play your character as you would a part in a play. And just like a character in a play, role-playing characters have motivations, backgrounds and reasons for behaving and acting. Many role-players will often write a story about their character to assist them with this process.

Role-playing is more than clicking on stats and gaining gear to outfit your character. It's becoming a fictional personality.

Role-playing is more than text chat banter too, it's thinking like your character and acting on it in game.

Role-playing is asking yourself, "What would my character do?" rather than doing what you would do.

Role-playing is a way of thinking. It's not a matter of how well you speak like you're in a Shakespearian play. You don't have to be an expert, have years of table-top D&D behind you, or even be a good typist. Think like your character and the rest will come.

Role-playing is looking at your co-worker or buddy down the street, who plays the same nights you do, and not seeing the guy behind the keyboard, but rather the character persona he created.

Role-playing is removing yourself from the real world for short while and submerging yourself in a fictional world. Think of how characters in classic fantasy books or movies behave. Would King Arthur ask Mordrid to group? Would Merlin ask Morgana for a Resurrection? Would the Fellowship in Lord of the Rings raid LothLorien for uber loot? Would Frodo ask Elrond to "twink" him? Would Harry Potter ask for "Crack"? Would Conan the Barbarian say "Dude, u kicked ass!"? Think about it.

For those that choose to role-play, it adds depth to a game that would otherwise be kill, loot, sell, buy, rinse and repeat. And there are plenty of games like Quake and Diablo out there to cover that kind of play.
