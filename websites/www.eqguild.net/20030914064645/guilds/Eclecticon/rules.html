LONG VERSION

1. Membership Requirements: Anyone interested in joining must be at least level 20(level requirement may be lowered during special recruiting sessions as determined by the guildleader). Player must be of a good and fair demeanor. An officer shouldn't have to travel long distances out of their way to go invite someone, a meeting place and time may be decided upon. There are no level restrictions for an alt of a guild member interested in joining, but they must have the name of their guild character at their disposal at the time of membership request (Proof by logging in on their guild character and responding may be required). Level requirement rules can be overridden by the guildleader or a majority guild vote.

2. Member Conduct: Guild success is dependant upon a cooperative effort and requires active, respectful and honorable participation by all members. Each person is responsible for upholding The guild’s good reputation and name wherever he/she adventures. Kill stealing, camp poaching, ninja looting, or any other kind of unethical behavior will NOT be tolerated. If you are accused of any of these types of behavior an officer will contact you for your version of what occured. The officer may send a incident report to the guildleader.If the officer or guildleader determines to his/her own satisfaction that you were involved in any of these unsavory acts, you may be removed from the guild (a warning will be issued for first occurance). Be mindful of language and content in guildchat and public forums (i.e. shout's, ooc's, and say's). No abusive language or behavior will be tolerated in any way. Your guild tag should be visible at all times. We do understand the need for being anonymous at times. To be anonymous yet still display the guild tag type in "/roleplay". Keep your member information up to date. Check the web guildsite, update character information. and post regularly so that you and fellow members are knowledgeable with event schedules, general information, issues, etc. What you know is important to others. Share what you know by offering advice on magic, battle tactics, trade skills, quests, and so on. Aiding other players in game like giving directions,escorting people, other forms of behavior can only benefit oneself positively. Members are not required to give money or anything else to beggars, if they do so, it is their own choice. You are encouraged to converse and group with your fellow members.

3. Guild Chat: - includes shout's, ooc's, and say's. It is fine to speak in other languges as long as you do not spam guild chat with them, although if you are asked to speak in common, please do so. It is fine to joke around as long as the other person and the rest of the guild knows that it is indeed a joke. Foul languge and spamming will not be tolerated. Refrain from broadcasting arguments,keep it in tells. If a solution cannot be reached, contact an officer or the guildleader to be the mediator. Open criticism of an individual member, arguing and bickering, and saying things for the sole purpose of stirring up trouble in guild say will not be tolerated. If you have a problem with a particular person then take it up with that person on a one-on-one basis. If you do not feel comfortable doing this, ask an officer to mediate for you. Using guild chat as a medium to perpetuate an argument is frowned upon. Take it to tells or a chat room. An officer may give requests if he/she believes that the discussion has passed the point of what everyone needs to hear. If you do not take it to tells, at that point you are subject to disciplinary action up to and including removal.

4. Requests for - General Assistance or Information: If a request for assistance or information has been asked within the guild chat, please do not ignore these requests; assist IF you can, if you cant at least reply. Do not assume someone else will.

5. Requests for - Revives or Resurrections: A request may only be made twice with no less than 5 minutes between requests per death. Clerics will not be responsible for corpse retrievals. A location of the resurrection must be decided upon prior to revival with consent from all parties involved. Clerics are not to be expected to leave a group or other activity they may be involved in to perform a "rez", nor is it the clerics responsibility to revive you every time you die. The final decision is the clerics.

6. Requests for - Teleports: State your location and destination when asking for port. When needing a port, please try to be at the druid ring, wizard spires, or other agreed upon designated place prior to pick-up. Requests for teleports may only be made twice with no less than 5 minutes between requests. Druids and or Wizards are not expected to leave a group or activity they may be involved in to teleport someone. Final say is that of the Wizard or Druid.

7. Requests for - large guild assistance for quests: Should be posted on the community or in the forums so that people may respond to their availability. We cannot guarantee assistance in these quests, but we will try.

8. Requests for - Power-leveling: Planned power-leveling may be posted for lower level characters as an event such as a raid or guild party. Power-leveling should not be begged for, but if it is offered up by a higher level, it is a decision of the higher level.

9. Guild Events and Raids: Anyone can post the intent to have a guild event or raid. The post should be on the guildsite at least 3 days ahead of time and contact an oficer so that they may put it on the guildmotd (there are exceptions with spontaneous events). If a raid or event is scheduled and you are tardy do not expect to always be able to join once it is under way. Some raids may involve moving through dangerous areas that would make it very difficult or impossible to join the raid or for someone to leave to get you. Be considerate of those that were on time. Don’t whine, complain, or criticize their decision to begin the raid. It is not their fault that you were tardy or that it was not a convenient time for you. When raids are scheduled all guild members are encouraged to attend. Not every raid will have any tangible benefit for you, but every raid we do as a team will only serve to strengthen our bond (and our levels). Plan ahead if a raid or event is scheduled. Anticipate the need for a teleport and arrange it with a member or outside party ahead of time if you cannot be available when or if the event has ports scheduled. Anyone can plan an event or raid. Finally, all raids should have a definite anticipated beginning and ending time. Both of these times should be reasonable and adhered too as much as possible.

10. Leaving or banishment from the Guild: Exceptions to these guidelines are only permissible via an e-mailed appeal to the leader. Re-applying back to the guild must be done with the leader via e-mail or tell. If you have left the guild under good terms you may request re-invite from an officer in tells. If you are instructed by an officer to contact the leader, do not side step that officer to find one that will, this will not be tolerated. If an officer denies you access back into the guild and you feel unfairly treated you must bring this up with the leader. If you were an officer, officership shall not be granted at time of re-invite or may never be an option. If you are removed from the guild, all other guild characters played by that you will be removed as well.

11. Officer Conduct: An officer must conduct himself/herself in a manner respectful their rank. As one of the guild governing ranks an officer must act accordingly, following and upholding the conduct and codes of the guild. An officer is expected to make every attempt to attend scheduled council meetings. In the event of a dispute in the guild, you are the voice of reason within. Advise argueing parties within guild chat to take the dispute to tells. If called upon as mediator in a situation, remember to listen to both sides of the story and use your best judgment. If you have deemed a member unsavory, this member needs to be brought to the attention of the leader via e-mail or tell. Respond (not react) to situations with good humor, dignity, and diplomacy.
