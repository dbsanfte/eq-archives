Guild Charter
The purpose of Eternal Faith is to provide a positive, encouraging community of support to its members.  Eternal Faith is a network of friends working together to assist each other in developing their characters to their fullest potential.
 
Guild Rules
New minimum level of 30 for all new invites (alternates of any guild members will be admitted at any level).
Members of Eternal Faith shall not go Anonymous.  This shows lack of pride in our fine guild.  If one wishes class and level to remain secret, then /roleplay can accomplish this goal while still displaying pride in our guild.
All new invites will be asked to log off game and read the website thoroughly.  Upon returning, potential invites will be asked questions about the site/guild by either the RO/SO/Leader and/or their two sponsors.
The Raid Coordinator will organize all EF raids.  Raids will be done according to the order in which they have been posted on the website.  Only one request pending per person/account please.
Meetings for officers will be held each Sunday at 6 PM Central(4PM Pacific).
The website for the Eternal Faith homepage will be posted at the end of each MOTD.
Begging from guild members is not acceptable.
We want everyone to have fun on the game, but comments of an "adult" nature should be made in a respectful fashion.
Members of Eternal Faith do not conduct arguments in guildchat.  If there is a difference of oppinion, it should be discussed in tells.
Members of Eternal Faith do not condone disrespectful remarks to anyone in Norrath, especially GMs.
Members of Eternal Faith die gracefully, without crying or finger-pointing.  Asking for help is always acceptable.
Members of Eternal Faith do not KS.  Kill Stealing is simply not tolerated.  In case of a camped spawn, Eternal Faith members respect the camp and do not intrude until the camp is relinquished.  If there is a dispute about a certain spawn, we try to reason it out, or petition a GM to come to a civil decision.
Power leveling is a privilage, not a right.  Members may extend their hand to power-level, but it should not be expected.
Be nice.  Above all, be respectful, kind and always be willing to lend a hand in the name of Eternal Faith.
Any member not following Eternal Faith guidelines may be removed.
Only the leader of Eternal Faith can select and remove officers after consulting with Senior Officers.  Only the leader of Eternal Faith can remove a member of the guild after consulting with Senior Officers.
Senior Officers help set guild policy and assist the leader of Eternal Faith.  They also assist the Recruiting Officers in finding new recruits.
Recruiting Officers have the honor of recruiting new members who are worthy to wear the Eternal Faith flag.  The Head Recruiting Officer will futher assist the Senior Officers and Leader in setting guild policy.  The Head Recruiting Officer will also replace a Senior Officer if an SO no longer holds thier position.
All alternate characters of guild officers will also be made officers for the purposes of making guild invites easier to handle.
Two existing members of Eternal Faith must sponsor a new invite by meeting and spending time with the potential invite.
