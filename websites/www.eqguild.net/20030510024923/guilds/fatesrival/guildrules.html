So, you are now a member of Fate's Rival. Welcome to the family!

 

Here are a few guidelines to show you what we expect from our brethren. By the way, Grimhold Lionsheart is the Fearless Leader of Fate's, and as such, he has the last word on all issues.

 

Generally, we expect all members to abide by the Play Nice Policy and Rules of Conduct set forth by the people at Everquest, show their guildtag at all times (use /roleplay instead of /anon), and act Honorably and Fairly in all situations. Never forget that everything you do while wearing a Fate's Rival tag reflects on the guild as a whole.

 

1) Try to Be Polite and Courteous to Others. Play Nice.

 

Some suggestions:

 

  • Group with fellow guildies! - Whenever possible. This strengthens our guild by promoting unity, teamwork and harmony.

 

  • Know the loot rules - before joining a new group. If you don’t like the rules, don’t join that group. If you are the group leader, see that party members know the loot rules and stick with them in a fair and consistent manner.

 

  • Be considerate - If you know you will be logging, give the group some notice so they can look for a replacement (exception for emergency situations). If you are the group leader, start looking for a replacement immediately, especially if your groups’ safety will be an issue.

 

  • Do not leave during a corpse recovery - unless you are certain you can be of no further assistance and you are not needed for the groups’ safety. If you are needed, but absolutely must go, try to get a replacement before you go. (Exception for Personal Emergencies)

 

  • Be understanding - of the needs of others (i.e. Sleep, Food, etc). Release them from obligation to assist on lengthy CR’s if they cannot assist further. Pressuring them into staying when they cannot aid or speed up the process is pointless and inconsiderate.

 

  • Be Prepared - for raids and events you agree to attend. Gather your equipment and supplies ahead of time.

 

2) Don't Beg!

 

Please PLEASE, don't repeatedly ask for Items, Teleports, Rez's or Power-Leveling in guildchat. Asking once is fine; asking over and over is annoying. Asking for help obtaining an item is ok too, but if no one is available, use the website to schedule an event to get the help you need. No one is here to baby-sit you!

 

3) Try to keep it Clean.

 

Try not to use profanity or vulgar language in public channels (/ooc, /shout, /guildchat, /em, /say). Same goes for jokes or insults that are of a sexual, racial or religious nature. Some people just don't like it, try to respect that and know your audience.

 

4) Do NOT Ninja Loot, Ever. Not only is it a bannable offense (and you WILL get caught), it's just wrong.

 

Ninja loot: To take an item from a mob that was not yours to loot.

 

This includes:

·        Looting a no drop item without the consent of those who helped down the mob.

·        Looting an item on a random you did not win to keep for yourself without the consent of the party.

·        Looting a mob that you did not kill without consulting the party who killed the mob.

 

5) Do NOT Kill-Steal

 

          KS’ing a.k.a Kill-Stealing - The killing of an NPC for any reason that is already fighting or pursuing another player or group without that group's specific permission. This is against the Play Nice Policy, and not very nice. Just don’t do it mmkay?

 

Consequences

 

Warning - The person will be spoken to by an officer and advised that repeated behavior of this kind is not acceptable and may lead to deguilding if they don't knock it off.

 

Removal of Guildtag - Depending on the situation the person may be asked to remove his guildtag or have his guildtag removed by an officer or the Guild Leader.

 

The Following will be Cause for Automatic Removal from the Guild

 

  • Ninja looting or Kill-Stealing
  • Repeated personal attacks
  • Personal RL threats against someone
  • Attempting to cheat members out of cash or items

 

We are reasonable people. Members have the right to have their say and tell their side of the story. However, at least two officers and the Guild Leader should be involved, along with any other parties affected. Should a deguilding happen, a short explanation would be posted in the Forums.
