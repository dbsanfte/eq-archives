Preventing Destruction

In addition to preventing stagnation, a Common Union will help a guild increase in power. There is a problem that can arise from the somewhat dissimilar goals of maintaining caring, high quality membership and advancing in power. People tend to not be satisfied even when they have achieved their original goals. They want more. This requires growth in guild size and gear, an escalation that would probably never end and can lead to a guild's destruction. This can be avoided.

By implementing an upper limit on what the guild will ever reasonably do, unchecked escalation (and excessive greed) is avoided. This limit could be comfortably finishing all epics without help from other guilds and anything else that's agreed on. Once this limit is decided, the guild will need to start setting other goals that are within the same difficulty as the set limit. Some goals could be getting a group of people their epics before getting anyone else's. It could also be outfitting everyone with a particular armor or finishing a certain quest. This avoids stagnation while still allowing for accomplishments, and most importantly the guild will avoid destruction by desire and greed.

Rest and Relief

If the raid organizers of a guild need time off to avoid burn out, they can have their guild concentrate fully on CU goals. This will give them a break from stressful duties while still allowing their guild to be active and productive. The CU leadership can decide on activities that will require more daily time by members to finish. It can also involve activities that might normally be raid activities that others can lead.

Family Guilds

Family guilds have an extra benefit by participating in a CU. They will be able to concentrate more on maintaining a family feel while still allowing their members to achieve more and acquire better gear. Their raiding ability will be ensured and they will know help will always be available.
