Joining and Leaving

1. To join the guild, you must be an iksar monk. 
2. We are currently accepting members between levels 3 and 20. This will change as current members progress through the levels. This is not a hard and fast rule, so if you are beyond the current level cap and wish to join, ask an officer. Obviously, if you advance beyond the current level cap while in the guild, that is not a problem.
3. We may be initiating a starting package of equipment to give to new members if they need it.
4. If you decide to leave the guild for any reason, we would appreciate it if you told an officer that you were leaving and why. Also, the return of any items that you borrowed from the guild bank is required. If you borrowed anything from another guild member, settle the matter with them.
 
General Conduct
1. DO NOT BEG. It is not appreciated by anyone, and we do not want to listen to spam from beggars. You may certainly ask for help, but realize that other people may be busy at the moment, and if they cannot help, do not annoy them.
2. Do not kill steal, purposely train, or otherwise violate the "play-nice policy" instituted by Verant, for any reason. This is not excused by saying that you were acting in the name of evil or roleplaying an evil character. There are other, more accepted ways of demostrating your alignment.
3. Help your fellow guild members.  This is to clarify the other side of the do no beg rule.  Just because somebody is asking for help does not mean they are begging.  While you are not obligated to assist every member with every request, anything you can do is for the better, and will be appreciated by all members of the guild.
 
Guild Banks:  Use and Abuse
1.  There will eventually be guild banks located throughout Norrath for the convenience of guild members.  These will provide ATM service where iksar are generally not welcome (i.e. Everywhere).  They will also be used to hold items that members wish to donate to the guild, as well as supplies for trade skills.  
2.  As mentioned above, if you have borrowed an item or money from the guild bank, and plan to leave the guild, you must return everything that you have borrowed.
3.  If you simply want to deposit money in a guild bank, rather than using one for an ATM, we will write down the amount deposited, and you will be able to withdraw it from any other bank... between the hours of 9 and 5 (j/k).  On the other hand, if you have not deposited money, do not attempt to rip us off.  It is not needed, as you are allowed to take loans from the guild bank, provided you have an actual reason other than "I like money."
4.  Feel free to donate money and items to the guild bank, as that is the only way it will remain supplied.  The leader and officers are in no way solely responsible for this task.
5.  If you wish to remove the items and money that you donated to the bank when you are leaving the guild, then you may.  Once again, we will be keeping track of donations.  If somebody else has borrowed the items, we will ask them to return them to you.
6.  Obviously you should never sell anything that you have borrowed from the guild bank.
Trade Skills
1.  Trade Skill Supplies -  There will be a list on the members page of which members have generously volunteered to use their trade skills for the good of the Legion.  They will be known as our Guild Artisans.  These people will be donating their time, and most likely their money, to help better equip the rest of us.  Show them consideration by donating any trade skill supplies that you come across to the guild bank, rather than selling them for personal profit.
2.  If one of our Artisans gives you something for free, such as a set of cured silk armor, never, under any circumstances, sell it.  It is not a nice thing to do, and if you no longer need the items given to you, it is expected that you will donate them to the guild bank.
