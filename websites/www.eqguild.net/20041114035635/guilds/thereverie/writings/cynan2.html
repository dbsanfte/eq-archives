THE HANDKERCHIEF


He staggers through the desert
Nothin' in his pockets
But a worn out wallet
and four lousy bucks.

Buzzards circle above his head
waiting for his invitation.
He chuckles when he thinks
about where he's been.

Not a lot of difference
where he's been and where he's going.
Seems noone cares about the beginning
or the end of a tramp.

He continues walking. The hot sand
scorching through his soleless shoes
while he wipes away the sweat
with a filthy handkerchief.

His parched body won't allow the tears
that should fall as he looks at the silk initial.
One last sorrowful sigh and he falls to his knees.
The heat greets his soul and escorts it away.

The swirling wind tears the handkerchief
from his deadman's grip
and carries it to the heart of the desert.

No, noone cares about the beginning
or the end
of a tramp.
