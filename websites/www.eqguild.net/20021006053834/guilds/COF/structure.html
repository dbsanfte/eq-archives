CoF Guild Structure
 The structure of CoF is based more on a Tribal Pattern than a rigid Feudal Hierarchy.  This shows up in three areas:   

  1. There are a large number of  Officers who all share the responsibility of running the guild smoothly.  

  2. Depending on what is happening, leadership can pass from Officer to Officer (ie. a War Leader will take full charge during a raid).  

  3. While an Officer is highly respected and their voice is always listened to, they have the same amount of say of what happens in the guild as any of the regular members (this includes the Leader).  We are a voting guild and every vote counts!

Membership Guidelines
 A main concern of all members was to keep our guild A Circle of Friends. Therefore, a few things were put forth:

  1. Instead of massive message board posts, etc., ALL Guild Members should be recruiting from the players they meet and play with.  

  2. We ask that some time is spent with each new possible member before they are invited to ensure the likely-hood of a good fit with our guild.  An officer may have an additional  /tell conversation with a possible new member for the same purposes.

  3. Once invited, the player is given the title of Initiate to last for approx. 1 month.  This gives THEM time to get used to the guild and its workings and US time to be sure this is a person everyone is comfortable playing and chatting with.

  4. The name of the Initiate will then be put forth as part of a Voting Ballot and ALL members get a vote final induction into the guild when the player will become a regular Member.  

This sounds a bit formal, but it is only to ensure everyone will have fun in our guild.  We have an inclusive charter and do not foresee many folks (if any) being turned away.  In case it is needed, however, we wanted to have these guide-lines in place. 


Voting 
This is how we thought it should work:

  1. Voting on Raids, Guild Issues and New Members will take place via email.  An email will be sent to you if your name is on the Guild Master Email List.  If not contact Flax to let her know how else she can contact you . 

  2. Each issue will get a yes, maybe, or no vote.  A blank vote slot would mean you have no opinion on an issue or have not had contact with a particular Initiate/New Member.  This allows for additional commentary and perhaps a later re-vote. 

  3. The Guild Leader or the Voting Officer will then tally the votes and announce the results.  

We felt that this procedure would ensure votes were private and fair.
