Although Roger Wilco has served us gamers for quite some time now, I'm becoming a little irritated with the shortcomings of the program.  For example have you ever been on a Roger server with about 10 people and there are 3 or 4 groups in different places doing different things?   This seems to happen quite frequently during the normal Norrathian day.

In addition to this, as a host for the Guild Roger servers, I've seen the base station program reboot my file servers on a nightly basis.  This appears to you as if you've just been kicked from the server and you have to log out and reconnect to the server to fix the problem.  Not something you want to happen in the middle of a heated battle!

In an effort to eliminate these problems I've been on a search for another program that will serve our needs while providing us with a comfortable voice chat environment.  These are the specifications that I was looking for:

  • Ability to host servers on dedicated file servers (has to run as an NT Service).
  • Sound quality as good or better than Roger Wilco.
  • Multiple channel support.
  • Ability to switch between channels WHILE running Everquest..
  • Support for slower speed connections.
  • Functionality from behind a NAT server, proxy, or firewall.
  • Push-to-talk support.  I hate voice-activated stuff.
  • Free download from the Internet.
  • RELIABILITY!

I think I've found this in Teamsound.  I would like to invite you to help me put this program through the rigorous testing that will determine whether the Noblehearts can use it.  Follow the links below to download the program and join me in the testing of this program.

Click here to download the sample Hotkey file that I'm using.  After you download this file, go to the Command and Control section of of the program and press the Import button.  Specify the location of where you saved the downloaded file.  You will then have the commands that I have been playing with setup in your program.  There are more things that you can do with hotkeys than I'm showing here, but we will have to experiment with them to see how they work.  The commands that you can use in-game from this file are listed below:

  • CTRL 1 - Switch to Group1
  • CTRL 2 - Switch to Group2
  • CTRL 3 - Switch to Group3
  • CTRL 4 - Switch to Group4
  • CTRL ~ - Switch to Root (this is the default place you drop into when you start the program)

There are other commands to allow you to broadcast to specific channels or listen in on specific channels.  More experimentation is needed.

Primitive races used hands to communicate ideas, thoughts and emotions by hand gestures, or by carving images and letters into the sides of cavern walls. 

As humans evolved and found it necessary to hunt in larger packs to defeat larger beast, a new way of communication formed. It started as low grunts and sharp cries that emitted while battling these rather large beast. Soon the noises became symbolic of excruciating pain or triumphant victory, and the difference between them became the foundation of our modern day tongue.  

We learned that it was nearly impossible to fight and communicate with our hands while in the heat of battled. And by using the grunts and cries that could be heard we didn't miss vital warnings from one another. We truly became a superior species over the Norrathian domain. 

No longer do we watch our comrades  be mercilessly slain in the hunts of such great beast and new terrors that await us in the killing fields. Ye cries of pain and joy now have the power of sound to fill the air, rejoice at our ability to save one another and share in the glory of victory!

I say lay down your keyboard and arcane hand gestures that often go unsaid and be heard! Join us in the evolution of grunts and cries, join us for we have evolved to the higher ground of knowledge and wisdom.
