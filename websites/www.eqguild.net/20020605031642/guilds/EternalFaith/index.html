Guild Charter
The purpose of Eternal Faith is to provide a positive, encouraging community of support to its members.  Eternal Faith is a network of friends working together to assist each other in
developing their characters to their fullest potential.
 
 
Guild Structure
Largand Daxamite is the leader of Eternal Faith.
The Officer Council will assist the leader of Eternal Faith in setting guild policy.  They also assist the guild by identifying potential recruits, leading raids, performing research for the guild and any other duties requested by the leader.
Only the leader of Eternal Faith can select and remove officers after consulting with Officer Council.
Only the leader of Eternal Faith can remove a member of the guild after consulting with Council.
All alternate characters of guild officers will also be made officers for the purposes of making guild invites easier to handle.
The website for the Eternal Faith homepage will be posted at the end of each MOTD.   (www.qibix.com)
 
General Rules
Members of Eternal Faith shall not go Anonymous.  This shows lack of pride in our fine guild.  If one wishes class and level to remain secret, then /roleplay can accomplish this goal while still displaying pride in our guild.
Members of Eternal Faith do not condone disrespectful remarks to anyone in Norrath, especially GMs.
Members of Eternal Faith do not conduct arguments in guildchat.  If there is a difference of opinion, it should be discussed in tells.
We want everyone to have fun on the game, but comments of an "adult" nature should be made in a respectful fashion.  Profanity is unacceptable at any time.
Begging from, or by, guild members is not acceptable.
Members of Eternal Faith die gracefully, without crying or finger-pointing.  Asking for help is always acceptable.
Members of Eternal Faith do not KS.  Kill Stealing is simply not tolerated.  In case of a camped spawn, Eternal Faith members respect the camp and do not intrude until the camp is relinquished.  If there is a dispute about a certain spawn, we try to reason it out, or petition a GM to come to a civil decision.
Power leveling is a privilege, not a right.  Members may extend their hand to power-level, but it should not be expected.  Be nice.
Above all, be respectful, kind and always be willing to lend a hand in the name of Eternal Faith.
Any member not following Eternal Faith guidelines may be removed.
 
Recruiting Rules
Minimum level of 30 for all new invites (alternates of any guild members will be admitted at any level).
All members have the honor of recruiting new members who are worthy to bear the Eternal Faith flag.
All new recruits must post a message on the guild forum in the “I Want In” section to begin the recruitment process. This message should contain their character information and why they would like to be considered for membership in Eternal Faith.
There will be a one-month probationary period for all new recruits.
It is the responsibility of recruits to seek out members of the guild to group with and to attend guild raids so that we can get to know you. During this process, the recruit must find two members who are willing to be his/her sponsors.
Eternal Faith members are encouraged to seek out recruits and get to know them. The more participation that we have in the recruitment process, the better the chance we have of being sure that the recruits fit in with Eternal Faith. All guild members are encouraged to comment in the “Rate the Recruits” section of the guild forum.
Eternal Faith does not accept recruits who are still in another guild, nor should Eternal Faith members fly the Eternal Faith tag while going through another guild’s recruitment process.
 
Raid Rules
All Guild members who wish to participate in Eternal Faith raids MUST find the Required Reading post in the Raids section of the forum and post that they have read it before they will be allowed to raid with the guild.  This is for YOUR safety!
The Raid Coordinators will organize most EF raids.  Raids will be done generally in the order in which they have been posted on the website.  Your raid will happen sooner if you do as much research as possible before posting a raid request.  A thoroughly researched request will get scheduled before one that requires additional research by the coordinators.
Only one raid request pending per person/account please.
Members are free to research and lead their own raids.  The more members of Eternal Faith that are capable of leading raids, the stronger we are as a guild.
Guildies should post 24 hours (minimum) before a raid to attend.  If you do not, or if you are late, you won't be able to attend.  If you post ahead of time that you will be late, arrangements can be made, if it's safe, to have you attend.  This makes it fair to those that are on time and take the time to post on the site.
 
Loot Rules
Eternal Faith follows a "Need before Greed" (NBG) policy.  If you can use it on the character you have on the raid, class-specific, you may roll on it.  One win per person until all on raid have something.
If no one needs a class specific item, then an open roll will take place.  The open roll will also occur after all guildies have won one item.  You may trade up (obviously not for no-drop).
Cash and cash loot (including low plat gems) will be given to the guild bank for needed raid items (planes stones, peridots, coffins, etc.).  High plat gems will be rolled upon.
When raiding for epic pieces, the members of the class for that epic will decide amongst themselves which guild member will be allowed to loot that epic piece.
For the purposes of loot distribution, any one on an Eternal Faith raid will be treated as if they are a member of Eternal Faith.
The above policy is for RAIDS ONLY, not experience groups. Experience groups, even groups that are all guildies, have autonomy to choose their own loot policy.
 
If you are here to check out Eternal Faith, we hope you like what you see. Above all, remember that Everquest is a game and HAVE FUN!!
