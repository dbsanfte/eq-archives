Guild Commands

/roleplay - use this command instead of anon to hide your level and location but show your guild name

/who beneficium decem all - lists who's online in the guild in all zones

/guildstatus - will tell you what guild someone is and if they are an officer (Must be in same zone)

/guildinvite - Officers may use this to recruit a new member (Must be in the same zone)

/guildsay or /gu - Use this to speak to guild members. Can also access this by left clicking anywhere on the text screen.

Guild Etiquette

A guild is ALWAYS a team, and the officers are there to serve you, however sometimes distance is a factor. Keep in mind that when requesting an officer to recruit someone you found for the guild, it may take some time for them to reach you. An officer in the area should make every effort to get there, but do not expect an officer 10 zones away to come running! The same rule stands when requesting help finding a corpse, a guide to a new zone, etc.

Another issue is mana and time. Our guild casters are happy to heal, sow, cure, buff, breeze, teleport, etc. etc. for guild members, HOWEVER it is unfair to ask a healer to heal you so you don't have to waste time sitting, and THEY end up having to med twenty minutes while you run off to get hurt again! Also keep in mind that the casters are not strictly there cast spells on everyone that asks, they would like to be off fighting, leveling and etc. as well!

If you DO need to ask for a heal, SIT DOWN in the meantime! Sitting greatly increases your heal time, thus hopefully saving the caster a little mana!

Also be aware that sometimes casters are busy! The wizard you just begged to nuke the croc you can't quite kill may be trying to get a Drybones off his own butt! Or the enchanter you are begging for a breeze from may have 14 tells requesting the same thing!

To those of you that ARE casters, you should make yourselves as available as you can to heal, sow, cure, buff, breeze, teleport, etc. etc. to fellow guild members, as long as it is reasonable and not an interference with your own game enjoyment! Don't be afraid to tell a persistant member (or non-member for that matter) that you aren't there to be their personal mana slave!

Group Commands

/gsay or /groupsay - Use this to speak to only your group members. Can also access this by right clicking anywhere on the text screen.

/assist - this command will automatically make your character attack whatever your target is attacking. Ex: You are in a group and an Orc Priest jumps your healer, Tweehugger. Target treehugger and type /assist to rush to her aid! This is a good command to have on a hotbutton if you group frequently. To quickly target the right player, simply click on their name in the box on your upper right.

/autosplit - can also be accessed in your socials bank, this command automatically splits any money a group member gains while looting equally among all group members.

Group Etiquette

There are a few topics here to cover for a good and organized grouping experience. Like the guild etiquette section, much of this pertains to our wonderful casters, however all group players have their considerations. I'll try to cover it all, so bear with me. If I leave you out my apologies!

First let's talk about grouping in general. The best thing to do when looking, whether you just want a partner, or a full group, is to /shout. Include your level, class, and what sort of hunting you would like to do, if you have something specific in mind. Use "LFG" (looking for group)

ex: Tweehugger shouts, lvl 20 druid looking for croc partner

Tweehugger shouts, lvl 14 ranger LFG to hunt gnolls

or just

Tweehugger shouts, lvl 56 pally LFG

Ask in /guildsay as well, there are over 30 members, chances are you can find someone around your level and close enough to group with!

Some suggestions when grouping would be to seek others close in level (I usually only group with those within two levels, higher or lower than me). This is important so that experience is distributed fairly equally. Also look for players of "like mind", whether you are the fun loving sort, serious minded or whatever. Also before joining an already established group, or when starting a new one, take the time to discuss what you wish to do and how you wish to do it, addressing what you would like to hunt, how you are splitting loot/money, and who is doing what (who heals, who nukes, who tanks/pulls, etc.) Look for a grouping that has at least one healer, one nuke and one tank/puller. An enchanter is good to have, and it never hurts to have a second healer for back-up. An idea for what to look for in a group would be a healer (Druid, Shaman), a second healer for backup and buffing that can nuke and fight when not healing, a nuke (Wiz), an enchanter, one tank (Warrior, Paladin, Monk) to pull, and a second tank for backup. A tank with taunt is great to have, keeping the casters from getting attacked is priority! Of course there are many variations on this, and take into consideration WHAT you are fighting. You may want two nukes instead of two tanks. There's no rule either saying you have to have 6. However, keep in mind the quickest death is the best death!

Second let's tackle looting. Looting in "free-for-all" style is never fair and just bad manners. The casters of the group usually stand back some from the fighting. So in a "free-for-all" group they keep the others alive and do the nuking but are never close enough to get any loot. Sound fair? I thought not. :)

The best solution I have seen so far is Alpha-looting. Looting in alphabetical order, with one player keeping track of turns. Determine ahead of time how it will be handled when a corpse has no loot on it, whether you will continue in order or the person that missed the loot gets to go again. Another method is dice rolling, but that doesn't distribute the loot fairly like alpha does.

As far as money goes, ALL group members should either have autosplit on or off. It's not fair for one or two to share the money while others do not. I personally suggest having it always on, then one does not have to worry about forgetting (see Commands, above, for instructions for turning autosplit on) and I think a good group shares loot and money. Having autosplit on while soloing will have NO effect on your take, there is noone to share with!

The next topic is casters and mana, healers and nukes in particular. Sometimes the group gets a bit too excited and starts pulling too fast, forgetting that the casters need to med. This is NOT a good idea! A healer doesn't do much good when they are "OOM" (out of mana) and the tank is getting his butt kicked! Nukes can't do a thing either if they don't have enough mana to cast anything. If you do not heed this advice you may find out the hard way how important it is. Nothing like pulling that Wooley Mammoth you've been dying to kill, having it get you down to 12 health, yelling for help and hearing your healer or nuke yell back, "sorry, I TOLD you I was OOM!!!"

And casters, it is YOUR responsibility to tell your party you are OOM. Keep in mind that at times the text if flying quickly by, don't be afraid to yell it (in groupsay) repeatedly, best way is to type OOM in caps.

Ok, let's talk about someone besides the casters now. How about our faithful tanks? Tanks have a tough job too, not only do they have to find good targets, take all the hits, and stay alive, but they have to keep the casters from being attacked as well! Healer's need to give the primary tank their priority and attention, always watch his/her health bar! If he dies that monsters going to look for something else to eat! Keeping your tank in one peice will keep your own butt safe! Keep the tank buffed as well, especially SOW, if available. It will make his pulling so much easier. Let him know if you are not getting exp off his targets.

All in all, the most important thing to remember about grouping is that you are a TEAM! Act like one! Always help each other, and always pay attention to your primary job. A healer who is using all their mana nuking is not doing a good job, neither is a tank that isn't keeping monsters off the healer!
