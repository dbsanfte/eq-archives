Note From Grudger.

I stood looking upon the great expanse of the lands beyond the gates of mighty Kaladim. My sword and cloth tunic gave me a small measure of confidence. These were intangible things that I knew would not betray me. Over the nearest hills, I could hear the sounds of the wild, caught glimpses of things both dangerous and wonderful. Untamed lands beyond what I knew as my home lay before me. What was I doing here? Where was I to go? Uncertainty began to wrap me in it's warm, constricting embrace. Then, it was broken. "Which way do you want to go Grudger? I say we follow the trail left, where Gaiayn showed us. You remember don't you, the Cross-Roads? Let's race!"

And that, as they say, was that!

All of us have felt the excitement of starting their first character. For those of us that had friends who played EQ before us, we received one warning over and over, "You will die a lot!” For those of us that learned the basics through reading the EQ books, we received one warning over and over, "You will die a lot!” For many of us, this was a frightening thing. We were told that our "corpse" would rot and disappear, taking all of our precious items along with it and to "never lose your body!" We were cautious, we ran to the guards like lost children in a Supermarket. Everything seamed so BIG! When we looted a slain foe and discovered that “there is a rusty longsword on this skeleton!” It was as good as an Epic Item to a level 60 character…we didn’t care…we were having FUN! I try and keep this feeling alive in my game play, and believe it is why so many people find the game as entrancing as it is...

Do you?
