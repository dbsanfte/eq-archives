Our Charter
 

What's a charter?

If its anything like the United Nations Rules of Engagement, then no, this is nothing like that. And no offence to anyone who has served with the UN either.

We DO like to have fun and its even better when there are other people to goof around with too. And that is the main rule we follow. Please be advised, that a lot of us are crusty old farts (read: over the age of 26) and while in the guild, if you are not prepared to be chippy or sarcastic, you may feel overwhelmed.

Respect yourself, your fellow guildies and your fellow players in the game. That's all we really ask for.

Kill lots of mobs, get lots of levels and loot. Repeat as necessary.

Well, on to the basic rules for potential recruits.

1) You must provide an email address so you can be inundated with loads of email spam on a daily basis. This may change if/when there is a large number of active guildies, or when we get the message board fixed.
2) You must display the guild name at all times. Just use /role instead of /anon. Be proud to be Tarnished. Well, its not really a rule, but we would prefer you show your colors.
3) There are no level caps at the moment. But don't join us thinking you are going to be automatically twinked. You need to learn your class and learn it well. Our experience has shown us that a single group made up of people that know their roles, is a lot more effective than three pickup groups.
4) All new members are subject to a minimum of a 2 week probation subject to the officer's approval. During this time you can be booted with no explanation necessary. Usually, we will give an explanation, but the officers reserve the right.
5) No alts will be allowed until your main is at least level 30. This shows us that you you have the bare minimum of experience to make a good showing for yourself.
6) DO NOT BE AN IDIOT IN OR OUT OF THE GAME. In this kind of setting, a person's rep is everything, doubly so for your guild. This guild was made up of close friends, and we'd all like to leave the game as friends. If someone accuses you of anything, or if you need help, your guild will back you up. But if the fault is yours, and especially if we find out afterwards, you will find yourself with no support. Conduct yourself in a civil fashion, you represent the Guild when you are playing.
7) Until such time that the original group of Heroes quits the game, or a majority officer decision is made, all officers will be inner circle people, or alts of the same. We've known each other the longest, and we trust each other the most. But never fear, you can eventually work your way up into a position of trust. Remember, trust is always earned, never given for free.

General rules on guild conduct

1) Don't beg. Whether its for assistance, items, cash, buffs or anything else I may have missed. Ask once or twice. If there's no response, assume its because we are busy fighting or otherwise. We'll answer or help when we can.
2) Loot rules. Main characters get the goodies first. Usually because we are the ones risking ourselves to get it. Our focus is to equip our high level mains first, so they can continue getting stuff for the guild later. If mains don't need the equipment, then it goes to the alts. Please respect this rule.
3) As we mentioned earlier, we like to kid around, and make wise-ass remarks to each other, IN GUILD ONLY. This does not give you the right to be an ass to anyone in or outside the guild. Feel free to join in the fun, but if an officer tells you to shut it, then do so. If you want to argue with someone, take it to tells. Don't announce it for the entire guild or zone to hear. And while on the topic of arguing
4) If there is a conflict, refer things to an officer. That's part of our job, dealing with these issues. The game can get frustrating, but that's no reason to take it out on fellow guildies or friends. Stay cool.

Now that everything has been explained, if you're interested in joining, send one of us a tell and we'll be more than happy to speak with you!
