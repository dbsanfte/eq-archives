Tapestry of Heroes Charter
Following is the Guild Charter for Tapestry of Heroes:
 
* All members of Tapestry of Heroes MUST follow this charter or face removal from Tapestry of Heroes.

* All these rules are the backbone of our Guild.  Those who do not agree with these rules have no place in Tapestry of Heroes.
 

General Rules
The Golden Rule
1)  All members must uphold the reputation of the Guild at all times.  This includes helping people if it does not endanger you.  It also includes not harming anyone in any way.  We are a good Guild and will not have ANYONE ruining our reputation.

2)  All Meetings are mandatory.  If you have a valid reason for not being able to make it (i.e. on your deathbed, work, hottest date ever, etc.), please let Lore Master or Guild Leader know at least two (2) days ahead of time, or within (2) days after missing.

3)  All members are expected to not use the /anon flag, as this removes the Guild tag from behind your name.  Wear the Tapestry of Heroes flag proudly.  If you wish to hide your level, class and race, use the /roleplay command instead.

4)  Your alts, friends and family are all welcome in Tapestry of Heroes provided they (you) agree with all of this charter.

5)  You must attend at least one (1) raid a month, unless a valid reason is given to the Battle Master in advance. (see number 2)

6) Respect your fellow Guild members by not spamming the Guild chat with things that you do not want them to see, or they would not want to see.  Use /tell, /group, or /say to keep the Guild chat from being cluttered with local issues or "off color" talk.  We have younger members that don't need to hear about things you wouldn't tell them in front of their mothers.

7)  Guild crafters will be compensated for their materials.  Their time is donated.  If the crafter wishes to donate the materials as well, that is up to him/her.

8)  Respect will be given to all other members of this guild at all times.  There will be no harassment or badmouthing fellow Guild members.  If you feel there is a problem, bring it up with the Chaplain.

9)  Respect will be given to all players of Everquest at all times.  Harassing, training, or otherwise interrupting the  fun game play of others will be treated harshly.  We are a friendly Guild and will not tolerate such behavior.

10)  Members of Tapestry of Heroes will follow all of the current "Play Nice" policies of  Sony Online Entertainment.  There will be no exceptions.
 

New Recruit Rules
1)  New members must submit their information (on the website) to the Lore Master within 10 days of joining the guild.  This will be posted on the website so all can see the person behind the character.

2)  New members must sign up for and use the forum for communication offline.  You must sign up within 10 days of joining the guild.  This forum is linked on the main page .  Please use the forum responsibly.

3)  New members must group at least two (2) times with current members of the guild for one (1) hour minimum, preferably within 30 days of joining the guild.  This is so we can get to know you better.

4)  New members must attend at least (1) guild raid/event, again preferably within 30 days of joining.  The reason is fairly obvious.

5) Current recruiting rules for Officers will be posted on the forum under the Officers section.  Please keep up to date on the current policies.
 

Grievances
A grievance is defined as such:
"Any issue against a Guild member, including violations of the Charter in any form."

Grievances will be handled in the following manner:

1) First contact will be the Chaplain except for when the grievance is against the Chaplain.  Then the Guild Leader is the first contact.

2)  Contact to Chaplain (or Guild Leader) will be in the form of tells, email or the online form.  No other format will be accepted.

3) Chaplain (or Guild Leader) will then investigate the grievance as early as possible.  If the Chaplain is unable to investigate in a timely manner, then the Chaplain may ask an Officer, Admiral or the Guild Leader to investigate for him/her.

4) Chaplain will then report his/her findings to the Guild Leader, Enforcer and Lore Master.  If Guild Leader is unavailable, and immediate attention is needed, Chaplain may report the findings to the Admirals .  This does not remove the requirement to inform the Guild Leader, Enforcer and Lore Master.

5)  Guild Leader or Admirals will then determine the appropriate action to take on the grievance.


Officials

Guild members should understand the official positions and know their duties.  See here for explanation.

1)  All Guild Official's authority is to be respected at all times. They are the cornerstone upon which this Guild rests.  They are your peers and your Leaders.  Treat them with the deserved respect.  (no need to bow and scrape)

2)  All Officials are accountable to the Guild Leader.  If you feel that an Official is abusing his/her privilege, take it up with either the Chaplain (first contact, unless the Chaplain is the problem) or the Guild Leader (only if the complaint is against the Chaplain).  Use tells or email to start the grievance process.

3)  The Guild Leader is the absolute power in the guild.  Any problems with him, and chances are, you do not belong in this guild.  We live and die by our Guild Leader.  However, if you feel that there is a problem with the Guild Leader, take it up with an Admiral in tells or email, to start a grievance.
 

Violations
1)  All violations of this charter will be brought before the Officials for review.  That is part of their job.

2)  If any member is found to have violated the charter two (2) times, they will be removed.

3)  If any member is found to have violated any of the rules found here in a severe manner , they will be removed immediately.

4)  Any Official violating the charter will be suspended from their position immediately, and proper action will be taken.  Punishment will fit the crime.

5)  Any punishment will be final.  All punishments are voted upon by the Admirals and Guild Leader, and as such are not reviewable. All warnings will last a period of one month for members, and two months for officials.
 

All of these policies are subject to change.  All changes will be noted in the news page.  New members must know this Charter.  Ignorance is no excuse for violating any of these policies, and will not be accepted as a valid reason.
