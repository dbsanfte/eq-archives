Evidence: Support for Success


The idea for the Common Union was the result of Dekurgen Heyokah giving his view from experience of why some guilds fail and a famous Psychology experiment. The player had seen more than 1 guild fall due to stagnation. The Psychology experiment showed how strong bonds could be formed between even enemies and success achieved when people worked together cooperatively.

Muzafer Sherif and his colleagues set up situations that required 2 groups of summer camp participants (who previously became enemies due to competition) to work together to be successful. They learned to work effectively in a cooperative effort, and later became close friends. The groups' cooperation allowed them to accomplish what they needed to do, and ended the harmful conflict that existed before due to competition.

The Common Union takes this farther and has additional factors for success of the effort. The CU has short term goals that constantly give everyone satisfaction of accomplishment and shows them they are always making a difference and progressing. The final, long term goal gives a reason for the short term goals beyond simply getting better gear etc. The limit on progression ensures that greed will be kept in check. In general, the CU keeps both guilds healthy and active so they can continue to aid their members with their personal goals and their allies' with theirs.
