Ryshon Hunsti finished by saying, "I see, take this note then to Kanthuk Ogrebane. I've been told he can be found fishing not far from the dwarven city." Without another word, he stuffed the note into Pallidmortis’ hand and vanished without a trace. After his many journies, Pallidmortis decided this was worth more investigation, so he pulled out his compass he had forgotten he carried, and started his travels to the lands of the dwarves…the Butcherblock Mountains.

Pallidmortis was in a friendly mood, so he decided to entertain this crazy dwarf and listen to his tales. "Who is Amstaf Trunolis?" Pallidmortis asked. Kanthuk ogrebane started explaining, "Amstaf Trunolis was a good man. I've followed into [battle] many times. We've seen many of our friends die by the sword of others." Thinking this nut was talking of a Paladin as if they were chosen of the Gods he said with a bit of sarcasim, "What courage?" Kanthuk Ogrebane not noticing it replied, "I'll never forget what he did for me that day. He gave me the courage to continue. No one's ever done anything like that for me. I vowed that day that I'd follow him into any battle. I'd be there alongside him, no matter what. As time went on we came across an elven woman named [Ambreen]." Pallidmortis having had his fair share of elvish women asked, "Who is Ambreen?" Kanthuk Ogrebane told Pallidmortis, "Ambreen was younger than he and I. She knew of the powers of nature. She was able to control the rain which fell from the heavens and would call upon the spirits to aide her. It was obvious that Amstaf took a liking to Ambreen. She ended up coming with is on out crusades. The last I saw of Ambreen was when we went to the snow covered lands of Everfrost. We were wander the lands when a freak [snowstorm] hit us. We were seperated from one another for quite some time." Thinking this whole tale was one, Pallidmortis said, "What snowstorm?" Kanthuk Ogrebane explained, "I don't know where it came from, but it was so sudden that it caught us all off guard. The snow quickly became unbearable. I tried to move to higher ground, but was unable to. I was stuck within the snowy confines. I didn't know what to do [next]." Pallidmortis continued, "What happened next?" Kanthuk Ogrebane told him, "As I laid there my body began to slowly freeze. I knew death was near and accepted the inevitable. Suddenly I felt something clutching at my cloak. I was being dragged upwards out of the snow. A hand pressed against my chest and began to glow. I felt energy pass into my body. Do you know [how] he had saved me?" Pallidmortis rolled his eyes and said, "How did he save you?" Kanthuk Ogrebane says, 'He saved my life by laying his hands upon my body. Kanthuk Ogrebane sounding half alive as it was told Pallidmortis, "We ended up taking refuge within a deserted cabin not to far from our location. We knew that we didn't have much time left to bring life back into her body. However, the storm didn't pass through and we knew we couldn't venture to far without the fear of freezing. I don't know what happened that night. I don't even know if what I dreamt was real, but before I awoke I saw a [strange image]." It was the blade of a sword, and etched into the blade was the words, Blade of Nobility. Pallidmortis gasped in astonishment, the Human in the Karanas was telling the truth! More investigation was now warranted, so with a renewed vigor, Pallidmortis broke camp and headed for Dagnor’s Cauldron.

These ghouls seemed bent on tasting the warm blood that coursed through the veins of Pallidmortis, as if it could give them their long forgotten lives back. After many hard fought battles, Pallidmortis trust his hand into the now cold flesh of a crazed ghoul and wretched forth its heart. Somehow, the blade in his bag seemed to be drawn to the heart, so Pallidmortis placed it in his bag for further investigation. Now tired and weary, Pallidmortis decided to head to Neriak and take in some elvish wine and women. After a days rest, Pallidmortis set out on his journey to discover the truth behind this Amstaf.
