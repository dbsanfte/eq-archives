About Us

The Guardians of Truth are one of the many guilds of Everquest, but we'd like to think that we are one of the best. Why is that? Because we are an interesting and very diverse group and we form a cohesive family, always helping each other out. We can be found on the Morell-Thule server, and we include members from almost every race and class possible. Our members and officers are found on every continent of Norrath. Because we are a relatively young guild, without high level restrictions, we are growing rapidly. Our members range from the very low levels to characters in their lower 50's. To find out more about our philosophy and rules, read on. If you are interested in joining, contact one of our officers in Norrath or send us an email and we'll look you up.

Our Creed
Our philosophy is relatively simple: we believe in helping others. We realize that the world of Norrath is a wonderful place, but is not without considerable challenges and dangers. Everyone in Everquest has had a fairy godmother or a knight in shining armor swoop in and help them out at some time or other. Helping each other is what makes roleplaying great. Unlike many other guilds, we do not have a high level limit for members to join. Anyone level 8 or over is welcome. This is because we realize that often, low level characters need the most help, and we would like to be there to help them out. If you agree with our philosophy, please consider joining our family.
Guild Rules/Behaviour Requirements
1. All new members must have attained at least level 8 to be considered for membership in the guild.
2. All new members (and old members who haven't done so yet) must register with our forum and with our member list on guild magic. Guild officers will record the name of each new recruit and will enforce this policy.
3. Using Anonymous is not allowed. Anonymous is considered hiding your guild for ill behaviour and hides your existence from the guild roster as well. Please use roleplay if you want to go anonymous.
4. Do not beg for Money, Equipment, Power Leveling, or ask to be twinked. Power Leveling, begging, and rude behavior are not allowed or approved by Guardians of Truth, and will result in intervention by the officers (i.e. you WILL be kicked out). There is nothing wrong with making reasonable requests (a TP, CR or Rezz) but if no one can, then accept they can't at that time. If no one responds to your request do not beg or spam the guild chat channel.
5. If someone in the guild gives you an item or items, DO NOT sell them. If you are no longer going to use them, offer them to a low level character in the guild at no charge.
6. Respect the guild members, we are all here to enjoy our time on the server. I'm sure we would all love to help you with every request, but if the guild is busy, stop asking for the day.
7. Be polite and sociable with your guild mates. If a guild mate makes a reasonable request, and you can help them, then do so.
8. Do not try to rip off your guild, If you get an item that is worth 300-500p offer it up to the guild for 300p or maybe even 200p.
9. If you are looking to accomplish something you can't do yourself, please go to the forum and post a request or talk to an officer in-game. The guild will try to organize some help for you.
10. Any in-game problems or disputes will be decided by officers. Every officer has the power to remove players from the guild and will do so without hesitation for breaking the rules. All officers sit on a council that meets once a month to decide new guild policies.
11. All officers will anounce their arrival on line, as has been standard for some time already.
12. Officers will hold a meeting at the end of each month, on the last Sunday 12.00 PST on the chat, to discuss any and all Guild related issues.
And finally and most importantly,
13. Have fun, it's just a game. Remember that and don't stress.
Guild Raid Rules

1. All raids must be announced at least one week in advance to give people enough time to sign up and coordinate plans. Raids will be announced on forum, on website and in forum calendar.
2. Everyone shall sign up to raid ahead of time if you want to go. If you do not hear about the raid in time to sign up, you MUST be at raid location on time.
3. If you're late, you are responsible for getting there on your own. If a spot is available, you can have it. Don't expect a spot to be ready for you.
4. Everyone must be at raid at least 15 min ahead of time. If you have a long trip or TP, please make it before hand and be ready to be on time. Raid leaders are encouraged to set up a meeting spot near a TP point.
5. The host of the raid makes the groups, and his/her decision is final.
6. Groups are assigned objectives (camps, etc... ) by the host.
7. All groups have to coordinate. Group leaders have to stay in contact with the raid organizer at all times and groups have to go where organizer tells them to. In many cases, we are in dangerous places where there is no room for error. The guild channel will be used by group leaders and raid leader to communicate. OOC or shout will be used to communicate between group. Group talk will be used within a group.
8. All members of a group have to obey and follow the group leader, who is in contact with the organizer, in order to support other groups properly.
9. Raid organizers are strongly encouraged to have a meeting with all group leaders before the raid to outline strategy and assign tasks.
10. Any member who does not abide by the final decisions of the host will be kicked out of the raid with no regrets.
11. You must notify raid leader of any particular loot or quest items that you need prior to raid so plans can be made. If an object is found during the raid that was not asked for, all participants of raid will be given a chance to claim it, based on greatest need. If two or more people want an item, it will be rolled for. The raid leader's decision is final and will be based on need before greed.
