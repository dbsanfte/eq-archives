A Young Monk’s Dream

 

 

 

            Shinsengo, or “Shin” as his friends call him, was an orphan raised by the Freeport Monks.  Rumor has it that his Father died before he was born, and that when his mother grew ill, she had entrusted the infant to a wize old Monk named Kenetsu.  Shin’s early years training with the monks were happy ones.  Their way of life was calm, peacefull, and without the everyday worries that come with owning many material possessions.  Though they were powerful fighters, the monks never went out of their way to start any major conflicts.  Their combat training missions consisted mostly of keeping the Wild Animals of The Desert and Commonlands under control.  By fighting Bears, Wolves, and Giant Beetles, the monks were helping the people while training and learning the ways of nature.  Little did Shin know that it would be one of these missions that would change his life forever. 

It was a cool October evening when it happened.  A party of ten monks, including Shin and the aging Kenetsu had left the safety of Freeport to help the people of the Commonlands rid themselves of a hungry pack of lions.  It would be an easy mission.  A party of ten monks was overkill.  Most of the monks could solo multiple lions at a time by now.  It seemed that the leader of the group, Kroten, just wanted to make a spectacle of the monk guild’s power.  After an hour or two of walking, the monks arrived at a small cottage.  The lions had been seen here often and a young ranger had tracked the pack to this area.  They were all sure that they would encounter the lions that night.  All of the monks gathered and overcrowded the small cottage.  The owners of the cottage, a poor family of five, made the monks as comfortable as they could.  Blankets, a full dinner, and even some fine ale put the monks into a relaxed mood.  Kenetsu made a comment to Shin about the monks growing soft and lazy.  There was a small argument between Kenetsu and Kroten resulting in Kenetsu taking Shin outside with him to camp there and be on the lookout for the lions.  After a few hours of listening to Kenetsu talk about his youth, and what it was to be honorable and just, the young Shin fell into a deep sleep.  He didn’t see why he had to be stuck with the boring, long winded mentor.  But he had to admit, Kenetsu was a great man, and was well liked and respected.  Althought the youth would never admitt it, he loved his foster father deeply. 

It was probably around 2 AM when Shin awoke to the sound of more arguing between Kroten and Kenetsu.  “Great!” Shinsengo thought.  “I’ll probably be sleeping in the lions den with twice the lecturing after this one!!”  Shin soon learned that they weren’t arguing about being lazy.  Apparently the 5 year old son of the poor farmer had dissappeared.  He had last been seen by some of the monks heading in the direction of the dreaded Orc Camp and the monks had been too drunk and/or lazy to do anything.  “How was I to know?” Kroten was saying.  “I figured the kid couldn’t sleep and was going off to play a little.”  What Kroten didn’t mention was the fact that the kid had been angered by the monks who were stealing what little food they had.  He had gathered up some supplies, and his favorite wooden sword, and taken to the woods so he could take care of the lions himself.  “Damn You!!” Kenetsu Shouted.  “Someone send a message to the ranger staying in the inn down the road!  We need to track that kid down before something terrible happens!”  One of the monks immediately ran off in the direction of the inn.  Kroten had failed and Kenetsu was taking over.  “I won’t waste your skills by sending you stumbling blindly through the commonlands brothers, but I can’t sit here and wait for the ranger.” Kenetsu said.  “Shin and I will go up ahead and see if we can find him.”  Normally Kenetsu wouldn’t want to take Shin on a mission like this, but he knew that Shin wouldn’t stay behind.  Shin was learning to sneak around danger quite stealthily anyways.  Kenetsu and Shin took to the woods with very little equipment to slow them down.  “Now do you see what I was talking about?” Kenetsu was saying as they jogged through the woods.  “I hope you don’t forget this.” 

The two had been wandering the woods for over an hour when Shin started to lose hope.  Maybe the ranger had already found him.  They could head back to the cottage and check in.  It was then that Kenetsu caught the light of the Orc camp fires..  The two crept closer to get a better look.  There, in the fire light, a group of Orc Legionaries were grouped, engaged in some kind of game.  Looking to the center of the group, Shin saw the game, and began shaking with fear.  The giant orcs were surrounding and tormenting the courageous young boy of five.  The young boy was crying and trying desperately to defend himself with his pitiful wooden sword.  The site made Shin sick to his stomach, and was near tears himself seeing the young boy and his pitiful sword.  Picturing the boys horrible death made him want to die himself.  Shin was brought back to his senses by a low moan of anger to his left.  Kenetsu dropped his pouch and turned to Shinsengo.  “No matter what happens, don’t go down there unless I say to!” he whispered harshly to Shin, his face like death.  It was all Shin could do to nod, still seized with fear from his hiding place in the hills. 

Kenetsu darted forward.  He moved with inhuman speed, glowing with a magical Ki.  Shin never thought that this elderly monk still had so much strength in him.  This was the power that the monks had always told him about, but he had never witnessed.  Kenetsu swept down upon them so fast that they never saw it coming.  He lept high into the air and slammed the leader with a solid flying kick to the chest.  The leader flew back, crashing through the fire.  The orc finally rolled to a stop,  laying face down in the dirt, dead.  The other orcs, recovering from their innitial shock, rushed forward in a furious rage.  Kenetsu dodged the axe of the first attacker, and caught him with a four hit combo that left him unconsious.  Just as the third Orc reached him, Konetsu met him with a furious Dragon Punch, ending the orcs life before he hit the ground.  Kenetsu landed, and turned to face the fourth Orc.  “Get that boy out of here!!” Kenetsu yelled as he grappled with the Orc.  Shin snapped to his senses and ran towards the boy.  He grabbed the weeping child by the collar and began dragging him back up the hill.  Though Shinsengo was only 11 years old himself, he was still incredibly strong.  Kenetsu swept the Orc to the ground and prepared to land the finishing blow.  Just as he prepared to strike the Orc, Kenetsu felt a blade of cold steel slide through him from the back.  Shinsengo watched as Kenetsu fell to his knees and was slaughtered by the remaining Orcs. 

Shin couldn’t believe what he was seeing.  Kenetsu had raised him lovingly as his own son.  How could he be killed by an ordinary Orc Legionarie?  Shin got his answer.  Through the corner of his eye, he caught sight of a dark elf, smiling to himself in the shadows.  The Orc was glowing with a dark aura.  The Dark Elf had obviously empowered the Orc with his own dark magic.  Shinsengo rushed forward.  He knew he was as good as dead, but he was going to die fighting.  He was not even ½ way there when a magical arrow flew over his head.  It implanted itself deep into the Orcs chest.  The monks, aided by the ranger, swept down on the camp.  They killed all the Orcs swiftly, and knocked over the tents and bunks looking for any more that may be around.  When Shin looked back for the Dark Elf, he was gone.  The monks probably wouldn’t even believe him if he told them what he had seen.  Shin stumbled into the camp, overcome with shock and grief.  Kroten was standing over Kenetsu’s body.  “Poor Guy..”  Kroten said.  “But it was his own damn fault.”  Shin couldn’t take it anymore.  He flew at Kroten, landing a roundkick to the monk’s midsection.  The monk doubled over, and Shin pounded him, splitting his lip, and knocking him back.  Kroten recovered and threw a swift Eagle Strike, bringing Shin to his knees and making him choke.  Kroten lept forward to strike again, but the other monks pulled him back.  The ranger put the tip of his sword to Kroten’s throat.  “Leave him alone, or I’ll lay you next to Konetsu!” The ranger threatened.  When they turned back to look for Shinsengo, he was already gone.  He fled through the woods back to Freeport.  But he wouldn’t go back to the monks.  He had seen and heard enough of them to last a lifetime..

It was the night that Kenetsu died that Shinsengo had his vision.  His foster father’s death had given him a new drive and commitment to make a difference in the world, and not  just sit around like the monks of his guild.  He dreamed of one day riding at the head of a Legion of true warriors.  This Legion would be dedicated to each other, and work as a unit.  They would grow in power together, and they would be feared and respected thoughout all of Norrath.  And thus begins the story of The Dread Legions…
