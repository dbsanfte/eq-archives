    You're balanced (at least till the next nerf) -- May 2, 2000 11:52
    Submitted by Sensei

      Lot of interesting stuff today, so let's get to it. First, a couple of Abashi-ism's, starting with comments from yesterday on Iksar versus human monks:

        Previously, with only one class capable of becoming a monk, balancing the monk class on the basis of race was not difficult. However, the inclusion of the Iksar, with many different racial attributes, has made this a bit harder.

        Infravision and regen make life much easier for the young Iksar, which leaves us with a need to validate humans as a monk class. In addition, monks are the only race that get an AC bonus based upon their race. This leaves them already getting one just by virtue of being a monk, and now another because they are also an Iksar.

        In this case, we decided that in addition to including the expected XP and faction issues, we've also made it so that Iksar monks will not be as "good" at *being* monks as humans. It's not by a large extreme, but human monks will always be better at doing what monks do: dealing damage.

      Secondly, a long one on class-balance and the WhinePlay Forum itself:

        I just got done taking a look at the first page of this forum. As I'm sure many know, each page holds 40 threads. Of the 40 threads at the time that I'm writing this:

        13 are complaining about Rogues
        2 about Rangers
        2 about Paladins
        1 about Wizards
        1 about Magicians
        1 about Druids
        1 about Enchanters
        1 about Necros

        That's over half of the posts on the first page that basically consist of "My class isn't balanced, isn't powerful enough, isn't desired in groups, can't do something some other class can." or something along those lines. The other 18 posts are perhaps issues that we'd like to answer, but people constantly bumping the class-envy posts keeps pushing them down. That isn't fair to the people who have issues that we can and are willing to address or do something about.

        I'm very sorry if we haven't been able to give everyone the answer that they are looking for in regards to their class, but being as clear as possible, notwithstanding current planned and announced enhancements, we consider all classes to be essentially well-balanced at this time. This means that we feel that there is a compelling reason to play each class beyond the desire to roleplay. It does not mean that every class is equivalent in offensive power, desirability to a group, or has some unique special skill or ability, just that there are tangible reasons someone might choose to play your class.

        I don't wish to get involved in the unending recursive arguments that surround class-envy posts any longer (e.g. Explain how we are balanced, what is the compelling reason to play us, etc). This is all meant to be part of the discovery process in the game. Part of the process where everyone finds out what character class suits them. We can’t tell you what you should like about your class, and if there is no other way to convince to you like your class other than to start handing out enhancements, then we may be at an impasse.

        If you wish to keep discussing these issues on our boards, it’s perfectly fine. We’ll read them and we may respond to some issues raised from time to time providing that the content is respectful and the answer would be of benefit to players as a whole, but we won’t feel compelled to answer class-envy posts regardless how many people comment in them. We’ll close the threads when they hit between 300-400 posts, and if the number of threads reach spam proportions (like the 13 rogue posts on the first page), we’ll start closing them before that.

        It is not at all our intention to suppress anyone’s ability to discuss these issues, but we want to be sure that people with issues that we can address, and that we’d like to address, have priority on the boards.

        Thank you for understanding, and I do intend to follow up on this thread today if content allows.

      Notice what class isn't on that bitch-list. Imagine that.

      Lastly from the news front, seems the equipment load tables in several Kunark zones were hosed the first week, which explains some of the ungodly equipment found. This has been changed and you should notice a severe change in load rates going forward.

      The items keep pouring in, and yes the union typing monkeys and I are still working on the quest stuff mentioned yesterday, so stay tuned. Today's humor link is 'dis one (Dojo props to Wolvinsbane from Mith-Marr for that one).

    Your new name will be the 'fry chef of damage' -- April 29, 2000 13:54
    Submitted by Sensei

      Ghitming from Bristlebane managed to get a monkly response outta Abashi late in the week:

        Seems that the skill cap increases for our primary skills (HTH , 1HB, Offense, Defense, etc)have run into a hard cap of 252. I know this must be a programming issue, but it has had the effect of weakening us relative to our melee brethren (ie, Warrior offense increase from 200 to 240 - Monk offense increase from 240 to 252.)

        Not really. We've accounted for any data-type mandated caps in code. Though you may stop skilling due to reaching 252, you'll still get better as you level. The gap may close a little bit, but not by any great extent.

        Please keep in mind that a classes power relative to another class changes as they level up. It's not something that is fixed in stone.

        Is it still Verant's vision that Monks be the "kings of melee?"

        We're beginning to dislike the "King of..." and "Master of.." titles that have been floating around. It gives classes the impression that all classes should be the master of "something", where "something" must be something that "matters". The only real important thing here is that some classes must be better and worse at some things than other classes. One class need not be the master of something. Maybe instead they are second-best at a few things.

        In any case, monks will still do more melee damage in a toe-to-toe fight than the others.
