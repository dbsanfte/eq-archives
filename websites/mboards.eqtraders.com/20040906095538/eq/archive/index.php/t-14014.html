Well, not necessarily. I know that it looks like that on the surface but here's the idea...

Erollisi Marr Field Plate cloaks go for 762 Favor
Erollisi Marr Field Plate cloaks sell for 200pp

Last night I bought 4 Tae Ew Bloods for 500pp each in the Bazaar, made 4 shields. So I basically spent 700pp-ish on an item I made that can be turned in for 4k favor. That's a hefty return.

So in your example, I could sell 4 Erollisi Marr Field Plate cloaks to a vendor and get 800pp, take that 800pp, buy parts for shield and turn in shield to get 4k favor. Had I justed turned 4 Cloaks in, I would have only gotten 3048 favor.

Ok, I know that doesn't take failures into account, so this isn't a perfect example but I think you get the idea. It's not important that you get more favor than how much it sells for, the ratio is the key. Try to find an item that is abundantly available to you that has the best plat to favor ratio and use that as your key.

700pp to make a tae ew shield gets me 4kish favor, about 1:5.5 ratio. So when deciding whether to sell an item for x plat or y favor, y must be 5.5 times more than x or else I'm selling it to reinvest.

Again, not a pure example because of the failures but I'm still trying to find the "magic" item with the "magic" ratio.
