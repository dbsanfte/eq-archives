Sylphan
There's all sorts of possibilities:

1. Drop table was changed, item can't drop. I know several examples of items that dropped for a very short time, and then never did again. If nobody got one that week (or didn't save it), it'd be counted.

2. Drop table was always bugged, item can't drop.

3. Norent items. There are many, and they would of course not show up in a search of the database during or after a patch.

4. Items removed from inventories. Sometimes it's an item that gets fixed by having a GM swap it out, other times there's a bug that just deletes a particular item from everyone's inventory (yes I know of examples).

5. Broken recipes (and broken component recipes and recipes where one item doesn't drop).

6. Broken quests (and subquests).

7. Subquest parts. People doing a quest usualy don't stop halfway through, and in fact usually move on to the next step of the quest in a few minutes or less. Any intermediate items might not exist in any banks even though the quest gets completed every day. This also includes very rare quest parts -- anyone who gets one is likely to go finish the quest right away.

8. Artifacts that were never given out, or were given out the first year and those folks since deleted their characters and quit EQ.

9. Items nobody wants -- some things that are worth 0 copper, or are useless except to sell, or are for quests nobody cares about, will simply not be kept. Such an item could be well-known and 'found' but nobody saved one.

10. Tradeskill items nobody makes. Almost all recipes get made at least once... but there might be some particular combination of meat and sauce, or one particular type of dry fly, or one particular 5-dose potion that got skipped or got made once and was since deleted.

Without knowing more, I just can't make myself believe that about ten percent of all items 'have never been discovered by players'.
