Oh dear - I've seen worse but not often.

Used to see some corkers in my old companies procurement dept. UK Ministry of Defence had a habit of making mistakes over product specs especially steel specs. You see steel specs start BSS (British Steel Specification) while specs for all other items start BS (British Standard), lose one S and some really daft things happen.

Like the time we were told to make something out of stainless steel but when our engineers looked up the spec it detailed the procedure for measuring tar content in cigarettes.

Or the time we got an order for what according to the spec was supposed to be 30,000 WW2 mule mufflers. Considering the company I worked for made submarines there were a lots of heads being scratched as to why the Ministry wanted to have any mules on a submarine never mind 30,000 of them. Although they did agree that if you had 30,000 mules on a submarine you would want them to be fitted with mufflers to avoid them damaging the floor plates and a large stock of corks might also be a good idea.
