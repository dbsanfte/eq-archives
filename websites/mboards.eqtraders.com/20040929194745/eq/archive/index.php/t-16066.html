Koru
How about letting rogues refine items for other trades? The chance of poison being allowed to other classes is almost nil, I think most of us know this. Especially when combined with the limits of the current EQ engine. So instead lets ask ourselves, what would give the skill marketable products that could be sold to non-rogues? Rogues take glands and such things and refine the slightly poisonous parts to become quite potent. It's not too big a stretch to think a class that regularly works with quicksilver and lead can find other applications for their skills.

One such solution would be to let rogues refine some materials to make other usable materials. Now traditionally you could say refining metals should go to either smithing (which already has upgrading metal into bigger pieces) or alchemy. However EQ alchemists deal only in plants. They're really herbalists more than alchemists. Rogues are the ones who traditionally use the non-smithable metallic substances in the game. There's already prescedence for letting rogues make some of the cultural components such as drops of mercury for dwarf temper. The skill limit is even fairly formidable on that, needs a skill of 200+ to be able to do it. How about the same for morning dew for example? There's been some quests that need rogue poisons made as well. A good call and that could be continued in future quests.

How about letting rogues make components for new recipies? By including cross-skill components you increase trade and interaction between the various classes, in many cases it adds a bit of rarity as well. Say you have Breastplate of the Zodiac. To make it would require a special mold of the rhino (pottery), cheeta leather padding (tailoring), sheets of elephant's metal (smithing), oiled chain joints of the fish (JC+brewing), and chameleon temper (brewing) as well as 3 essences. One essence of the bear (alchemy made). One essence of the eagle (imbued gem) and one essence of the snake (poison made). Or a new weapons recipe, do the recipe bare and get an ok weapon, however put in a poisonous rogue-made contraption and get the same weapon but with a minor proc.

Just naming an example of how the chores of making an item can be logically split up. As it is EQ recipies are very bare, very basic in both what they take to create and what they yield. Some require the occasional imbued gem, but beyond that most components are completely logical and mundane (in a fantasy sort of way). The problem with make poison as a trade skill isn't that poisons suck, it's that they have no value to anyone outside of rogues. Your market in that case is only a fraction of what it could be, that's not even counting how many of the poisons are no drop. The solution would probably not be to give everyone poisons as it would upset balance and still with poisons the way they are not make them terribly useful. But instead to give the skill a use outside of only making pure straight-up poisons for use against mobs. The first step in that has already been taken in allowing refining of poison ingredients for other trades.
