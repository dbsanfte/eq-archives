I don't know how many of you had the honor to meet the fellow who played Meerc, Kolya, Scre, Gnoc and others. He was a serious tradeskiller on Mith Marr server.

He was a shy, most often quiet (though with an off-the-wall sense of humor) fellow who was always available to help with tradeskills, gating, or to share his wealth of EQ knowledge.

Monday, April 21 ... he fainted from low blood sugar (he was diabetic), and fell in a position that did not permit him to breathe. By the time he was discovered, it was far too late.

His funeral is tomorrow, and I'm ... probably not going to be on EQ for a few days. I shall miss him terribly. It's gonna take me a while to feel like playing again, though I may log in and wander about aimlessly visiting places he and I used to frequent.

Thank you for your patience with me while I mourn him. Losing him really hurts.

Rest in peace, dear one.

I thank you all for your kind condolences.

It has been decided that I shall inherit his Everquest account. SOE was informed ... they're not thrilled, but not forbidding it either. I have the full support of the family in this.

I know he had promised that his characters would be available to aid others in tradeskills, etc, and that some of them held items in storage for others. (Yes, I know what and who - so don't get any bright ideas!)

Whenever I am able to access his characters without crying so much, I will begin to fulfill my promises to help them become epic and max out certain tradeskills ... in his memory.

Too many of his dreams were buried with him, his own story incomplete. I have no power to complete all of them. I can, however, continue his legacy through his own EverQuest characters.

If you hear me speaking of my "legacy account" or "legacy characters," that's when I'm talking about his. Even though I "own" his account now, I still feel that I am only a steward of his creations ... I feel they will always properly belong to him. I'm only caring for them in his absence.

Whenever the day comes that Kolya, Meerc, Scre, Gnoc, and the others again appear in Norrath ... please understand that I will be the hand that guides them. I must learn their classes, and I will do my best to reflect his own style for them as they go forward.

I realize that it is unlikely I can ever play them as well as he would have done. I don't have his incredible knowledge and understanding of the ways that EverQuest works. Those of you who knew him well may be disappointed that his characters cannot be exactly the same. I can only do my best, and I crave your tolerance for my shortcomings.

Thank you.
