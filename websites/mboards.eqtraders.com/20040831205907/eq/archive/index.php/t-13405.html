That was concise LOL

No finished pottery items besides ceramic linings stack as far as I know.

Nothing sells back to a vendor for anything close to what you spend on it.

Golden idols of tunare eventually sell as people need more solstice earring (and star ruby steins will a bit too). Faithstones/Spiritstones can sell but mostly on a custom basis and so are hard to skillup on - planar pottery items sell well but don't expect to find a lot of the materials needed to make them and expect to fail rather frequently.

Check the trivial list for pottery in the recipe section. Stuff to make are crucibles of escape, some of the different bands, old style idols and the star ruby steins and golden idols. Except for the last two the others make nice gifts but never really sell well, ok, except the crucibles for the people really good at marketing.
If high end pottery items were used for some skill path high end smithing or tailoring combine stuggling potters would be set for life. I share your pain.

Silverfish
There's one option that everyone is skipping, probably because you asked for over 215 combines, but if you're at 204 right now, try doing unfired planar steins. They're 11 plat for store-bought stuff, and 6 more if you buy your tainted planar essences from vendors (more if you get them in the Bazaar). That's cheaper than most other items and doesn't require you to run down a friendly enchanter or find imbuers. They'll get you to 216, and you can fire them into steins that sell (the peridot ones sell for the Beginner's Stealth Manual quest, and others will move, although not fast at all). After that, drill steins and sell them, or drill white ceramic bands and throw them away, depending on your patience level and bankroll. After 222, go to non-golden idols, with side trips into crucibles as you can get or farm heavy clay.

Good luck with your throwing!
