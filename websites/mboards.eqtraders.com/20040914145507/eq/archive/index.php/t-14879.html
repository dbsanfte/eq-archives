Jylarien
Don't forget you need your brewing up for the Jum Jum beer. Believe it is trivial at 165, so close to or above that to avoid having to run back and forth to vendors to pick up supplies to cover botches.

Just a couple of pointers on MTPs: If you are doing them and can't forage (me being a cleric I can't do much of anything effectively for tradeskills) make sure to keep an eye out in the bazaar for good prices on veges and fruit. Veges you will need at least 20 for a 200 picnic run, fruit isn't nearly as much... 2 if I remember right. Brownie parts are easy to get, just go to lfay and farm the ones that aren't brownie guards and you'll collect parts rather quickly. Oh, and you need eggs for dough. Spend a few hours in Lavastorm and kill basalisks there for them. 1 egg will last you for over 1000 combines so you don't need a ridiculous amount of them.

All in all aside from farming and buying stuff in the bazaar it takes me about 35-40 minutes to prep for an MTP run and about 10 minutes to do all the combines. Even at 225 skill I can still get about 220-250 MTPs out of a run (with geerlocks) and immediately /ooc them in the bazaar for 3pp to make a quick 600-800pp and clear out my inventory for the next run.

At this point I'd say it's mostly personal choice. If you spend a good bit of time in GoD, well then use those recipes eh? Otherwise, MTP's would be my recommendation.

My 31 druid is my 250 baker.

He did MTP's from 191 to 250 before the new UI, with 255 WIS. Took him 1965 combines total.

Basically 200 combines per day with a couple extra 200 runs thrown in. Took me a bit more than a week total.

One brownie part, one snake egg, 3 fruit, 10 veggies per 100 combine run. Everything else can be bought in Shadowhaven. :)

With the new UI, I can crank out a 200 combine run of MTP's in about 45 minutes... Assuming I have those few items that can't be merchant bought in advance. :)
