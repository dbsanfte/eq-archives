There is also a 2-spawn cave closer to the bandit camps. I don't know the loc, but here is how to get to it.

Enter Paludal Caverns from Shadowhaven. Go through the bandit camp, then go to your right and follow the path up the ledge. Eventually you will see a stone 'bridge' that goes over the chasm. Take the bridge and head left. Very soon you will see a tunnel on your right, take it. Take the next right and you will enter a short dead-end cave with two owlbear cub spawns.

My experience on Wailing Substances has been around 1 in 4 drop rate.

What I generally do is invite a friendly wizard or druid along to my Hollowshade farming sessions. Have them mem a PBAoE (point blank area of effect) nuke, which unlike the targeted nukes can affect an unlimited number of targets.

First we set the war so that the wolves or bears have taken over the south.

Then I go aggro about 30 of the owlbears (or wolves). I start by putting on my best defensive buffs and Spirit of Eagle. Getting every last mob on the island mad at me is a bit of a challenge.

Once I have enough mobs, I drag them over to the druid, who then casts the AoE nuke.

Boom! 30 dead mobs. (One nuke from a 65 druid generally kills the whole bunch, although occasionally a second nuke is needed to finish off the few stragglers.) Then the time consuming part begins - looting up and destroying all the junk. :)

By the time the looting is finished, the islands will have respawned and I can go get another batch.

I generally get about, oh I'd say 60 wailing substances per hour this way.

Virgin poster but i really heavily farmed this so here's my advice.

1)There's 3 factions of armies, they generally attack only whoever is anti-clockwise of their base, i can't remember them attacking in a clockwise direction.

2) Only the South Island camp thing will drop wailing or shrieking substances.

3) To instigate an attack you kill the 'bosses' and 'defenders' at a certain camp then wait, whoever is clockwise of here should attack soon. Sometimes tho, like 1 in 5, the camp you've forgotten about may attack the camp you're trying to make take over the one you're at. If this happens rush over and kill their attackers.

4) When they attack the one you're in just make sure they win, and weeee, the island will go how you want it.

5) Now you're probably farming, but you still can't slack off. Whoever has south island will probably have another camp too, this means they could be likely to attack the remaining camp, if they do this and win the scripted war thing starts and you gotta reset it all after you kill whoever wins the war.


Wizard spell - Winds of Gelid ( lvl 60? ) will wipe out anything you pull. With a puller and 1 wiz you can destroy the entire south island population in a couple of pulls ( don't just get the village stuff, get the turtle shepherds and what not too), and you get a *lot* of substances - be it wolf or owl. Respawn time is 7 minutes or so, i believe, so basically you're going to be killing 40+ substance dropping mobs every 7 minutes which means after an hour or so you generally have at least a bag of substances if not two.

I don't recommend doing this alone, not because it's difficult but because 1 other person of the appropiate class will triple your yield. Also,it shouldn't be hard to find either a wizard friend or someone to pull for you if you plan to do the AEing because it's always fun having huge trains and obliterating them in seconds. If you ask a wizard if he wants to join an AE group he'll bite your hand off, once he realises it's green mobs he may lose some enthusiasm but the carnage usually leashes them for a fair while =)

The war will NEVER run to completion without player intervention. When the side with 2 camps attacks the side with 1 camp they ALWAYS fail. When the side with 1 camp attacks the side with 2 camps they ALWAYS win. Unless you intervene and either kill the defenders or attackers of the side you want to lose, they will just swap camps around randomly and endlessly.

Incidently, this is frequently how people leave the war when they exit the zone. When you come in if the war hasn't been initiated. Kill the grimling boss and bears will attack next always. Kill the grimling defenders and they are eliminated. After that just ignore the war. You need both bears and wolves at south camp. Left without intervention they will just swap camps around endlessly.

Sometimes you'll have bears in south, sometimes wolves, but it doesn't matter as you need large quantities of both.

Occasionally someone will alter this static state to make owlbears win. This is because the owlbear boss drops a range slot FT1 item, and many casters can solo the whole process.

If that happens, the war will reset when they kill the owlbear boss in the vah shir camp and the war will reset. Simply kill the grimling boss again and continue as before.

Leaving it in this state (grimlings up and bears/wolves endlessly fighting) is considered the polite thing to do.
