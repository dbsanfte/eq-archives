Eleena Transient
Krazick, I think you misunderstand what these do:

Increase Mana Pool by 100

All it will do is allow you to have 100 extra mana (this is the viscous one). Currently, any spell or item that adds to mana pool ONLY adds to the cap, not your current. Good way to see it is when you loot your corpse after a death. I may be fm naked, but with all my gear, I'm only 30-40% because my current is still 1200ish but my pool is up to 4700.

To give an example, my cleric had a mana pool of about 4700. Say she went and healed the group and stuff, dropping down to 50% (2350). She drinks a viscous potion. For 2 hours, she now caps at 4800. She 'still' only has 2350, and will regen at the exact same rate.

Only use I can see for something like this is to hand to the clerics in a ch rotation so they can last a bit longer. Staring the fight with 5200 vs 4700 (thats at a cost of 4 rubies, so 500pp) would let me go an extra round or 2 of heals.

Now, if they made them +1 mana / tick to +5 / tick instead of +100 to +500 to pool, Then they'd be useful (and likely over powering).
