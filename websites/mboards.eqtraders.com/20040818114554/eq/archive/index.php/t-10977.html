bearcaller
Planar steins are actually far cheaper than 30.550 if you are looking strictly at increasing your skill because it is the unfired stein that only uses essence, clay, sketch and water that trivs at 216. This brings the total cost down to right around 18pp, which isn't too shabby for a post 200 triv combine.

If you have a decent JC skill I would recommend doing the opal encrusted steins after the planar steins (or even mixed in should you run out of essence in PoK) as I noticed an excellent skill up rate on them. I went from 214 to 222 on opal steins in around 13 stacks of combines at wis 255. The opal steins only cost 1pp more but have a sellback of 3pp and change as opposed to the planar steins.

Getting to 220 is relatively cheap and easy even if you don't have a chanter. Post 222 is where you either pay through the nose for vials and star rubies, or PL a chanter to 29:mad:

As for getting from 148 to 199 there are 2 ways you can go about it.

Casserole dishes are a much more skill up friendly route now that the ceramic linings stack. So if you don't mind making up stacks of the linings it can be very easy to load up on sketches JPF and then sit in front of a wheel for a couple hours.

If you prefer to save your wrists the trouble you can go the lined/sealed poison vial route to 188 and then do the casserole dishes for only the last 11 points. This is also a very cheap route since the lined/sealed vials sell back for over a plat each.
