The ratio does not change (unless they fixed the recommended level damage bug) regardless of level.

In the off hand, a 5 11 will always be a 5 11.

In the main hand, a 5 11 will be a 5 11 always. However, you'll do more damage than the simple ratio would suggest due to the damage bonus. The damage bonus does not affect ratio, however, it only affects how much ratio plays a part in determining relative weapon power. 5 11 or 10 22 . . . in the hands of a level 20 toon, both are the same. In the hands of a 65 toon, the 5 11 will outdamage because the damage bonus will be applied twice as often (statistically same hit rate, just twice the hits on the 11 del weapon than the 22 del weapon). However, a 16 20 should outdamage a 5 11 even at 65, because the increase in per hit power is so much greater than the extra amount of damage bonus applied.

casala
Just to add a little bit of clarification.

Every weapon with an innate proc has a procratemod field associated with it.

For example:
Copper Hammer of Striking
procratemod = 0
http://lucy.allakhazam.com/itemraw.html?id=62186

Hammer of ****ation (lvl 63 summoned cleric hammer)
procratemod = 530
http://lucy.allakhazam.com/itemraw.html?id=29365

As you can see, the hammer of ****ation procs a lot more than the copper hammer of striking. Ignoring stats and AA bonuses, you would expect (as based on the values) that the CHoS would proc on average once every 30 seconds. Similarly, you would expect the Hammer of ****ation to proc on average 5.3 times every 30 seconds.

The procrate can be modified by a number of factors (dex, AAs, etc) but in general each weapon has the procrate hardcoded.
