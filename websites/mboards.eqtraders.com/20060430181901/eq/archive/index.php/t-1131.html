It occurs to me that there are multiple classes of "wealthy" people on EQ. For the sake of argument, I'll arbitrarily divide them into three groups by level:

<b>Poor newbie</b> -- characters who are under level 20 or so and rarely have cash to flaunt. Most spare cash goes to buy equipment upgrades. Hence, they'll farm rather than buy most components.

<b>Mid-range character</b> -- characters about levels 20-45. They have enough cash to make a serious stab at tradeskills while still maintaining their equipment. These guys will buy some cheaper components, or maybe the odd hard-to-farm piece.

<b>Ultra-rich uber-char</b> -- maybe levels 45-65. These are people for whom anything under 1K is chump change, because they can earn that much in less than an hour. They often have bundles of cash in the bank, with little other than tradeskills to spend it on. They will happily pay bazaar rates for components to save themselves the tedium of killing weenies like spiderlings and cats for hours on end.

(As I said, the levels associated are arbitrary. You may agree or disagree, but the point is the three levels of wealth.)

So, to someone in the "Poor newbie" class, paying 20-40pp for a padding is absurd. He can farm the components and gain exp doing it! For a mid-range character like me, buying cheaper components is a decent deal, since farming without exp is frankly boring. (e.g., I won't buy padding at the bazaar, but I will buy low-priced pelts and silks and make my own.)

For the uber-chars, however, money is no longer an issue. Paying 40pp per padding is nothing. 5 stacks would end up costing 4000pp, which is not too much for them to blow on practice. Heck, they can sell one of their spare, no-longer-needed weapons for 10-20K! Hence, the focus shifts more on convenience and time-saving, rather than cost-efficiency.

It is to this extravagant market that the padding vendors (disclosure: this includes me) caters. I am very happy to find my paddings selling briskly at 35pp each, and my bone chips for 20pp per stack, etc.
