"take what you can get" approach seems to be good in the short term but doesn't really pay off in the long run. This is especially true of acrylia studded and reinforced. You can't control the size of acrylia bricks your getting or the quality of pelt you will get. Yes you can file down the acrylia and I think scale down the quality of the pelt and it will get you more combines initially but once studded trivs at 188 you won't be able to do them anymore for skill ups. You can combine up the acrylia but it isn't very efficient and you definitely can't increase the quality of the pelts.

I'd say stock pile the pelts and make the acrylia bricks into boning so it stacks and just save em till 188 skill. Then pull out your piles and do combines for skill. In the short term your skill will go up slower but in the long run you will be using your resources more effectively and end up with less camping of parts that you can't use for skill ups anymore.

I'm not a rich tailor. I couldn't afford to throw away money on making Wyvern masks. So, I mainly did Halfling Cultural (still couldn't sell them, but at least it didn't cost me too much). I banked the small pieces of velium and wyvern/tigeraptor hides I got.
