The skill-up process isn't very well understood. About the only things we're certain of are that the higher your skill is, the more difficult it is to raise your skill, and that certain stats (Int, Wis, and possibly a tertiary stat - none for tailoring) help you to raise your skill level faster.
As far as we know, making different items with the same triv level won't affect your ability to raise skill, but that's merely an educated guess.

I have noticed that sometimes making 'harder' or should I just say items that require more components, within the same trivial level, sometimes raises the skill faster. But, they may just be a fluke.

Anyway, say you're making tunics, and you get skill up every 8-12 that's every 32-48 studs for 1 skill up. Say you're making masks where you get a skill up every 25-35 combines, which is 25-35 studs.

Tunics = less hides and more studs
Masks = less studs and more hides

Skill ups - about the same, but numbers wise, more chances of a skill up (including the coveted 2 in a row) with the masks.

So, I'd look at it like this, is it easier for you to get hides or studs? Therein lies your choice, but for me, I often select to use the least number of components possible for as many combines as possible.
