OR, a very effective way I've discovered thanks to a cleric friend...

Go to the old world, and bring a Complete Heal able cleric with a Manastone

Have the enchanter enchant the ore till they are OOM. Use the Manastone to clicky their way back to FM while the cleric heals when they get low.

Give Manastone back to cleric when FM. Cleric then fills his/her mana to full using the manastone while the enchanter is enchanting bricks.

Rinse, repeat. Soon you'll have more ore then you know what to do with in a VERY short time.
Tenasi
12-06-2002, 02:06 PM
Another advantage of the manastone is that using it, even once, resets the spell-reset-timer-gem for the enchant metal series. I was enchanting tons of steel VERY quickly when just 3-popping the manastone in between enchants (tested it with a single 'hit', as well, worked the same way...3 just seemed to get me a good amount of mana back). Also, don't forget that druids/shamans can *almost* CH now-a-days as well...not to mention the regen adding mana regen to the equation.

You used to be able to do the same sort of thing with mages pre-modrod2 nerf...cleric + mage = infinite mana fast. I used that combo once before the nerf in the Bazaar to make tons of imbued/enchanted mana vials/gems.

Of course, that requires that you have an enchanter you somewhat trust. Above all, what I'd suggest is tracking down any friends you have that are of the level you need to enchant your ore and try and trouble them for a little of their time. Also, I suggest if they are rarely available to create a ore mule near your forge...take them there...and wring them dry. Fill the mule so you don't need to go 'WTB: 49+ enchanter to enchant XXXX' whenever you get an order/urge to smith.

Good luck.
