I think a large part of the problem Sony has with Alchemy is potions can be used by everyone. A potion can be used by a level 1 Rogue or a level 65 Wizard and anything in between. This may not sound like a bad thing, but it limits Sony into just going down the middle of the road. For example, to a level 65 Cleric a potion that heals 500hp in 5 ticks isn't that powerful, but it's godly to a level 10 Warrior.

This is part of the reason there are such weak potions with such high costs. They can't make good potions for a level 50 player without making it cost so much to ensure people won't use these to powerlevel a level 10 one. Potions were meant to be situational things, not something people could use to powerlevel their twinks.

So, I really think Sony needs to revamp Alchemy and add potions that can only be cast by certain classes and can only be cast by certain levels. It might also be neat to add some racial potions as well.

Mostly, having potions being useable by everyone is really not such a big deal.

First, out of the 64 or so (not counting "old" alchemy, which is universally terrible) potion recipes, only about 10 of them are actually useful to any degree.

Rundown:

Sow: Not a problem, ever timed the duration of this potion at level 1? Bring a stopwatch that measures down to thousandths of a second. Plus, this is first available in non-potion form at level 9, so it can hardly be considered a twinking nightmare.

IVU: Just like the spell, the random duration is a severe limiting factor. Plus, not all that many undead about that people might want to avoid, certainly a twinking/PL issue.

Kilva's Blistering Flesh/Skin of Flame: Innately level capped. Skin of flame does 31 points at level 60, but only like 6 at level 1. Duration is also shorter, non-issue.

Shrink/Grow: Presents no balance issues of any kind, at any level.

Stinging Wort: Ah, now we get into a potion with some room for abuse. 300 instant heal...that's pretty spiffy for a newbie, and isn't (AFAIK) level restricted. But, it requires a drop that comes from only 3 mobs spawns in the whole game, and the people camping them, in the interests of saving weight, destroy them. Sometimes you can get lucky and find toes inside Perma, but that is OBSCENELY rare. The 3 everfrost giants are the only real source.

Field of Bone/Innothule/Everfrost gates: Extremely expensive, no real balance issues I can think of. The 30 second cast time makes them completely useless as an emergency item, and PoK being open to level 1 characters means that there can't be a problem in VI's mind about ease of transport.

All the other potions on the list are either flat out useless (stat potions), of exceedingly meager use (levitate --- still requires the drinker to carry bat wings!, serpent sight, cancel magic), or far too expensive for any possible use you could have (all of the luclin potions except Grow).
