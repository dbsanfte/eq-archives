Alchemy would be the precursor to chemistry, but both baking and brewing are also chemical processes. Brewing would actually be most closely related to chemical engineering.

Now to make diamonds or other gems that is the realm of material science - smithing is for metallic materials and well pottery is the closest thing for working with inorganic non-metallic crystalline materials and non-crystalline glasses. So pottery should get to make blue diamonds.

Now until recently you would need to use a kiln that could generate huge pressures to cause the phase transformation from carbon sources like graphite or even charcoal like in the comic books. But now with a little bit of plasma enhanced chemical vapor deposition you can grow your own in an evacuated kiln with an RF generator. Wooooo.

Tinkering is much more like mechanical engineering.

Poison-making would be organic chemistry.

Jewelcraft would be the artistic end of metallurgy for the most part although the edges would be a bit blurry.

Fletching and Tailoring are industries that would use attributes of the other skills with some specialized knowledge tossed in.
