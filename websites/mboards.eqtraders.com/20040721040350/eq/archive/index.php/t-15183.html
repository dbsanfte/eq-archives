Annalina Romanov
With the seven standard tradeskills, you cannot use Training Points to advance the skill higher than 20. If your rating in a tradeskill is 21 or higher, you're going to need to do "skill-ups" like the rest of us.

Trust me . . . I would have had maxed out Blacksmithing a looooooooong time ago if I could use Training Points to get up there.

-Annalina
mewkus
Regarding tailoring, I wouldn't bother putting training points into it till you're done with making silk swatches. Those are NoFail combines and will get your skill up to 15 and you're gonna need a bunch of them, so you might as well get skill ups on them while you can. If you don't want to waste your training points, then I probably wouldn't bother spending them on tradeskills. The cost to skill your way up to 20 (which is the highest you can use your points to get to as Annalina said) is so minimal that it doesn't make it worth it to spend the points. I'd recommend saving those training points for one of your other skills where you can spend it to top them off instead of just starting them off like with tradeskills. Later in life it can get risky to try to build up your defensive skill.
