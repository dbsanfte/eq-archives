tawnos
I used to hunt there until the prices dropped too far to make them worthwhile to farm. I did them by myself boxing a 65 chanter and a 65 druid. It took me probably two and a half hours or so to get one combine worth for the ring (2 eyes + stone). I couldn't quite clear all of the beetles by myself even using harmony to bypass all the worms. With a full group you could probably clear nearly all of the worms and beetles, but doing so would get you more stones than you would need.

The beetles are in the mid 50's and double for about 300. Almost all of them can be ae stunned (with normal ae stuns, not the ldon one) which makes controlling them pretty easy for an enchanter. The spawn rate was relatively slow. As for group structure, just about anything should work so long as at least one person can pacify/lull/harmony. There is one nasty spot where you can get chain aggro through a wall but its best to just learn that spot and avoid it by going around it rather than trying to cc it. The worms and beetles have fairly low magres and provide decent damage as pets.

rugenpayne
If you get the maps from maps.eq-toolbox.com, and are standing at the entrance to the caves looking at the first two geonids:

Turn right, turn right again, turn right almost immediately into a dead end.

This dead end has 3 spawn of beetles/locusts, with a beetle/locust just outside, and one just before the turn in to the dead end tunnel.

I set up camp there as myself (65 cleric) and my husband (65 beastlord) and was able to clear the bugs as well as a fair number of surrounding worms each round. The eyes are rare, make no mistake. One day of 6 some hours down there got me 2. Another day, I got 1 from each of the 3 beetles in the room, and then no more dropped again til almost 5 hours later.

The middle spawn in the room of beetles is the PH for a named. You will see "a dread locust" or an "envergerated beetle". These are the PH. The named is a "diamond beetle" and drops a ranger bow and the Justice Cape.

Either way, I was mostly PLing a tradeskill mule/assistant for myself to level 49 down there, or I would never have spent the time there. Very boring at level 65, and not the best xp, and those eyes are very very rare.

Just some info for ya.

I'll add a few details to get info straight. Lower level beetles/larvae/gralloks are 51 - 55. Higher level ones are 53 - 57. nameds are 59 and summon unlike the rest. Since all the chanter AE stuns except Color Cloud cap at 55 you could only AE the lower level spawns safely. Drops on the Cloudy Eyes are about 1 in 20 from the lower level beetles and 1 in 15 from the higher level beetles. The Alkaline Etched Stones(which drop off Larvae) arn't a big Issue, I have many more of them than I need and I've been focusing on beetles. With 243 JC ( + Geerlok) and JCM3 I'm 7 and 7 on Justice rings(also I've failed only 1 PoP JC combine out of 20 or 30 and that was durring the period in which JCM was suposedly broken).

I Regularly charm solo this and/or duo it with my druid friend We're both 65 (when I first started soloing it I had maybe 70 AA?).

On Povar I've sold 4 of the 7 rings for 15k each and the other 3 I gave to friends. Some of the Drops off nameds I've seen have been Justice Cape/True Bow of Balance/Replendent Scalemail Boots.

The camp is soloable as an enchanter and probly a druid, not sure on other classes. With a full group and good DPS you could quite easily clear out all the beetles(charm soloing I can clear out a good deal of the beetles). Its a good camp for making money with JC or farming gear for friends. I don't know why but people realy drool over 20 wis on a ring /shrug I'd rather have more HP/Mana.
