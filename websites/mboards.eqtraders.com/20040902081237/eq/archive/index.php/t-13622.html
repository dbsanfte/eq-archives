Hmm...

Think there are a few websites dedicated to "matching" motherboards to what you want to do. Motherboard-dot-org or somesuch. (google search)

Generally I'm pretty sure if you check out a website with a name similar to PriceLine (love to Watch those comercials, so cheesy) you can find Motherboards that meet your needs.

(category search P4 motherboards in your clock range, then do a ctrl-f search for DDR)

The PC numbers on RAM are data bus speeds. Back in the day PC100 and PC133 were screaming fast. Now PC-a-billion seems to be the trend. The websites of the vendors SHOULD have a tech spec sheet for the motherboards they are selling. If not run SCREAMING in the other direction and look for a different vendor. From the tech sheet you can tell what CPUs and RAMs and ports (AGPx2 vs. AGPx4) are supported.

If you are a "tech dummy" perhaps it's not a REAL good idea to replace a motherboard yourself. Here's a couple of quick questions to answer...

1) ESD is what?

2) ESD is good / bad ?

3) You should put the CD-DVD drive and hard drive on the same IDE cable right?

4) The floppy drive cable is different from the IDE cable how? Which plug of the cable should go to the A: drive, the close one or the far one?

If you do NOT know the answer to all four of those questions COLD, and think me an idiot for asking them... DO NOT replace your own motherboard.
