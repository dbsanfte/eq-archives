Silverfish
I have a secret for avoiding the woeful destruction of collapsed containers.

Step 1: Open the container, so you can see that it's empty (or only contains stuff you want to destroy).

Step 2: pick up the container, which will force it to close. That way, you know you picked up the right one (I've destroyed about half a dozen glaze mortars because I picked up the wrong bowl).

Step 3: open another bag, and put it in the bag. If it goes in, you just saved yourself a bunch of platinum. If not, you can comfortably click destroy.

Since I got into this habit, I've never had a problem losing a collapsed container.

DeegerStormcrow
When creating a new toon there is a (fairly) easy way to set up his windows/hotkeys to match another toon. The easist way to do this is to open a "command" prompt:

- Make sure you are not logged into a toon (being on the char select screen is OK.)

- Click "Start" then "Run..."

- Enter "command" for Win 98/ME, "cmd" for 2K/XP. Hit enter.

- Go to your EQ directory (usually "c:\program files\everquest") by typing 'cd "\program files\everquest" '. Note that the double-quotes are necessary around the folder name. Hit enter.

- Get a list of all the files for the original toon. For example, to get a list of all of Deeger's files I would type "dir *deeger*" and hit enter.

- Copy each of the files and use the new toons name. For example, I would type "copy UI_Deeger_.ini UI_Mynewtoon_.ini" and press enter. (You could do "copy *deeger* *Mynewtoon*" but depending on your names it might not work.)

When you are done the new toon will have the same setup as the original one.

Lots of good stuff, here. Now, to add some more.

Your bazaar mule should have in slot 1 and 2 normal food and water. This is to prevent eatting your player made goods. Nothing like having a hobgoblin supply (750pp) go poof. Do not mention this to your buyers. I have gotten some repeat business because they accidently ate it.

Calculate your platinum farming average. (I use a conservative 1k/hour) If farming a tradeskill items yields you 20 items in an hour, but sell in bazaar for 25pp a piece, it is more cost efficient to buy them instead of farm them yourself. This also keeps the younger tradeskiller types employed.

Charisma gear is your friend. Get your charisma to at least 104. The purchase could pay itself off with one night of tradeskilling.

Be wary of matching prices in bazaar blindly. I pull down 2k on a good night buying from players and reselling to venders in bazaar because they got below the magic number. (Example, buying a stack of diamonds at 150pp each - sell to vender for 190pp, and pulling in 800pp.)

Look at vender sell back for optimal profit. For example, large bowls... The finished product sells for 3sp 2cp, which is lower then the cost of the firing sheet. In this specific case, it is more cost efficient to destroy the item then it is to finish it.

Keep one enchanted velium bit in the bank. This will prevent you from having to click destroy. It does not prevent skillups what so ever.

Most skill up items are not worth reselling in bazaar (Acrylia Studded Masks for example). The market is flooded with hundreds of them, since everyone else is making them as well. Use them for tribute, or just sell them to a vender.

The tribute master can raise your Int or Wis by 12 additional points. This can help with tradeskilling significantly.

Fishing grubs sell to venders for 3 cp each. That can add up. (and this coming from a 65 character)

The general consensus seems to be that no rent bags are eeeevil. I certainly agree they are something to be used with extreme caution, however, no one has mentioned the cases where you actually WANT to trash stuff.

If you're skilling up on something that's only going to sell to a vendor for 1 silver, doesnt stack, and you're making hundreds of them, replacing most of your normal bags with no rents means you can just destroy them when they are full, summon some more, and continue - instead of having to go sell all those things indivdually to a merchant (who may or may not be nearby), for a whopping total of like 2 p...

Also works well for .. well not sure what to call it these days as it's not quite merchant mining anymore, but - there are certain merchants I try to keep somewhat cleared out by buying up stuff on them and moving it to my designated dumping ground merchant... Of course, you don't want to destroy stuff from the Tranquility merchants, for example, as most you can sell back for very close to what it cost, and sometimes that's a couple hundred plat per item. You're mainly just wanting them moved elsewhere... But if you want to clear a newbie merchant or even a higher zone merchant who happens to have alot of junk on him at the time, you can just position your bags so that stuff goes into a nice freebie no rent bag and trash it. You may lose like 5 plat in stuff *gasp*, but if you do this sort of thing extensively, its usually worth it. I often make 4 or 5 trips back and forth to my dumping merchant in Neriak when I'm cleaning up PoT merchants. Needless to say, if you can't port that's alot of running around, so being able to destory the basically worthless bits in one swell foop is kinda handy. :)

And, nawh, I'm not generally someone who goes around trashing stuff all the time. Heck, I don't think I even started getting rid of silver and copper until level 35 or something, even when I could hardly move! I'll never trash something worth more than say... 3 plat just to get it out of the way. (Thus for me, the lower level words and runes are fair game, but not the higher level ones, for example). Just decide what limit works for you.

Anyway, just my random thoughts on no rent bags from the other perspective. :)

If you are having a hard time looting mobs like in LDON you can create a hotkey like this:
/target a
/pause 5
/loot

/target playername
/pause 2
