I believe the difference between your skill and the trivial of the item definitely affects skill ups. I get skill-ups most often between 10-20 points under the trivial. This is much more visible at higher difficulties because skilling up is much easier at lower levels. When GM'ing jc I found that doing combines 10-15 skills above my level, instead of doing the next one above my level, would raise my skill faster and give me slightly more failures. I also found on Wu's that the closer I got to trivial, the less my skill would go up. When I switched to a higher level piece, my skill ups would come faster for a little bit.

The data suggest that int/wis does help, however, for an INDIVIDUAL, the sample size is too small to draw conclusions for the above situation.

In smithing, it took me 347 combines to go from 199-200 skill. With NO changes in gear, I then went from 200-205 in 40 or so combines.

RNG= RANDOM number generator.

A person with MAXXED stats, may still end up averaging 25 combines per skillup, while someone with a 200 wis, might end up with an average of 12.

However, you are MORE LIKELY to get a skillup with the stat maxxed.
