I just finished an extensive revision of the Economical Smith based on feedback I'd received. Here is a list of most of the changes I did between versions 3 and 4:

The following corrections and clarifications were added between Editions 3 and 4:

- Fixed omissions of Brell Serilis and Bertoxxulous in the Imbue Gems table
- Added Shamans as able to imbue diamonds for Mithraniel Marr (frogloks)
- Added Clerics as able to imbue amber for Cazic-Thule (erudites)
- Clarified section on Smithing Races to make it better understood that cultural races have more skilling options, which is what makes them preferable.
- Clarified description of medium quality ore vendors to point out that smiths should not buy ore from three specific vendors with no forge nearby.
- Clarified and added information about morning dew foraging/looting
- Added metal rings to the cost of making shadowscream.
- Changed layout of shadowscream section to call out swirling shadows, wailing substance, and shrieking substance into their own sections.
- Added hilt mold to the list of costs for making mistletoe cutting sickles (already included in the sum totals)
- Corrected the name of Qeynos human cultural plate to full plate.
- Corrected the name of the Velium Smithing Hammer.
- Corrected the spelling of Master Barkhem’s name.
- Corrected costs for Ornate Gold Coif.
- Fixed a few minor grammatical errors.
- Added information on deluxe toolboxes as another alternative for containers.
- Removed Grobb from supply location lists (our sympathies to the trolls. Naughty frogs!).
- Added Gukta to appropriate supply location lists.
- Added information about needed Everquest® expansions for skilling.
- Added rusty weapon supply vendors under Tarnished Weapons Conversion section.
- Updated section about the Hollowshade War to reflect changes in a recent patch.

New sections added:

- Added section, "Tips for Moving Ore", that gives a variety of tips on moving large quantities of ore, including the two-account “ore fetcher” method for those who cannot gate.
- Added "Richeldys’ Tips for the Super Cheap Smith". These are some tips I have gotten based on my experiences running a new smith on the Stromm newbie server.
- Added a new section about Forges, explaining the types of forges and their uses.
- Added Vale Sewing Kits as a halfling-only alternative for skilling to 162.
- Added an explanation of why shears are not a good skilling alternative to ornate chain.

It's all at the same location as before:

It won't apply to very many people, but there is another low cost skill route to go 220-227. Breastplate Cleanings.

This requires that you have killed Bertoxxulous and received the appropriate flags, so most players cannot benefit from this technique. For those who can, its easier to skill up on than it sounds (for gating classes).

Prepare a large amount of Acid Wash (3 CE + Jar of Acid, Brewed). I found 60 Acid runs were adequate. Prepare a hotkey for the quest prompt ("I am well versed in the art of smithing"). Ride the elevator to the top of the tower. Click the button to send the elevator back down (this is best done at off-hours when no one else will use the elevator). Bind at Aid Grimel. Cast Levitation (or whatever means you have to float). Hit the hotkey, drop the (Lore) Filthy BP into your inventory. Levi down the shaft (pointing down and running a spiral around the shaft wall goes fastest). Ramp up the outer wall of the library so you can fly over the inner city retaining wall and down to the main smithing building (the one with 3 or so forges - its closest). Perform your combine. Dispose of the Cleaned BP, if successful (and desired - otherwise, you'll just get duplicate Lore messages - but I didn't know if those affected skill rate). Gate. Repeat the above steps, beginning with hitting the hotkey, until you hit 227 or run out of Acid Wash.

With 280 str/280 wis, it took me 120 or so combines over 2 sessions to run 220-227. Time consuming? Yes ... but not nearly so time consuming as farming Shadowscream Steel sploof.
