Just wanted to add that this one is confirmed (http://www.snopes.com/history/american/curse.htm) as being true from Snopes.

If you look at the facts though, it's stretches a little. I always interpretted this to mean that they were elected for the first time in the years divisable by 20, but that is not so.

William McKinley was elected for the first time in 1896, but then re-elected in 1900. Franklin Roosevelt was first elected in 1932 and reelected in 1936, 1940 and 1944.

Also, this also fails to point out that two presidents didn't die in the year divisable by 20, but in their second term. Lincoln was elected in 1860 and again in 1864 and died in 1865. Roosevelt died during his 4th term which started in 1944.

So:

1840: William Henry Harrison - Elected in 1840, died in that term.
1860: Abraham Lincoln - Originally elected in 1860, didn't die until his second term in 1864.
1880: James A. Garfield - Elected in 1880, died in that term.
1900: William McKinley - Originally elected in 1896, didn't died until his second term in 1900.
1920: Warren G. Harding - Elected in 1920, died in that term.
1940: Franklin D. Roosevelt - Originally elected in 1932, didn't die until his 4th term in 1944.
1960: John F. Kennedy - Elected in 1960, died in that term.
1980: Ronald Reagan - Elected in 1980, assassination attempt took place in that term.

My thoughts are that Reagan was saved only because of our technology. Had he been president in even 1960, he likely would've died. Contrastly, the other presidents might not have died had they had the technology back then.

However, if this "curse" is actually true, from reading the facts Bush won't have broken the curse until he is out of office - either in 2005 or 2009.

Snopes does confirm it's true, but after reading the facts, it just seems like the facts were streched to fit the curse. When you consider that one president is either elected or re-elected in a year divisible by 20 every 5 terms, it isn't that huge of a coincidence that these presidents who died in office were in office in a year that was divisable by 20.
