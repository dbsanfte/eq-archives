Erm, that's not price fixing.

Price fixing is when a group of sellers decide on what the selling price will be and make sure that everyone sells at that price.

When a single seller finds out 'people will pay X' for an item, and sees someone selling it for 'X-Y' PP, it's good business to buy from that other person and sell for the 'Y' profit.

There's nothing even remotely unethical about it, and it's stunningly common in a situation where there is a standard amount of a single good hitting the market (velium can only be farmed so quickly) and a demand for that good.

Now, sending you something of a childish tell wasn't the best plan ever (good plan, make fun of your customer), but your lecture was incorrect, at least as you describe it. If you don't think the price that HE is reselling at is correct, then farm it yourself. But it seems both you and others keep paying it...

Footsore
A little more of my observations as a "professional" NPC vendor miner:

I'm pretty sure "cornering the market" doesn't work. I buy my wares almost exclusively from NPC vendors (though I'm not above buying something from trader that is grossly under priced at the current market - it just doesn't happen often).

There was a time when I would try to match other traders when they boosted their price. My experience in doing that, however, shows that sales slow down and eventually stop when the price is too high. Yes, one or two might sell at the “too high” price, but not often - certainly not often enough to justify the price. Most people seem to exercise their consumer rights and just won’t buy if the price gets too high.

The “acceptable” price does change over time – sometimes higher, sometimes lower – for BDs. When no one is skilling up on BD items, I’ve seen the “acceptable” price drop briefly to 300. Probably lower, but I just stopped mining when they wouldn’t sell at 300. I'm not wasting my valuable inventory slots on something that has a mark-up that low. Not when I can sell other stuff consistently at 500% mark-up.

I’ve never seen them sell fast at higher than 500 (on Zeb). When I’m pricing a regular supply at lower prices, the person(s) trying to corner the market inevitably ends up dropping their prices. (I've even had one of these folks ask me if I wanted to buy their entire stock at half what they were asking in trader - was still too high though.)

The laws of supply and demand are obviously at work, but I’m still sure that demand is the dominant force. Don’t buy at the higher prices and they WILL come down.

Kaysha Soulsinger
I'm not sure if that's the cause. In the first year of PoP, there was a huge influx of Blue Diamonds and Diamonds, as they dropped plenty in the lower Planes. The initial rush was easily soaked up by a parched crowd, thirsty for them. As they got quenched, more and more remained on the market and prices fell.

With LDoN, many people shifted their hunting grounds to areas that didn't only provide exp, but also fun. Furthermore, in that year a pretty large population also moved from the lower level Planes into the higher ones, only rushing their alts through the lower level ones. I remember that Plane of Nightmare had a population of 40+ around the clock a year ago. Last few times I went there recently, there was hardly anyone. The same counts for other Planes. And I don't have the impression that Blue Diamonds drop as much in the higher Planes, they are replaced by other money drops.

A third aspect might be that people complained that Blue Diamonds were too common and that SOE nerfed the overall droprate of those gems - but I doubt they would tell us, if they did this.

As a result of the well drying up, the amount for sale drops and thereby the price goes up. This is clearly visible on Lanys.
