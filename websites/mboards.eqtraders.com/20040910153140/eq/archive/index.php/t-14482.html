The issue being that if they change the outcome of the combine it will by default change the existing items.

My understanding is that the items that exist in EQ are in a Database of sorts, where your character has X Y and Z items (These values match database entries) so every time you log in your character it goes out and looks at the DB and presents you with the items you should have. So let us say that the Pale Velium Inlaid Cestus is "Fixed" so that the stats are what the Velium Inlaid Cestus actually has. This would alter the DB entry for both of these items and thus when you log in and your characters equiptment is pulled up you get the "Fixed" version of the Cestus... There have only been one or two times that I can remember where a "Fix" has resulted in a "pre-nerf" item... I highly doubt that they would do that here as it would require additional entries into the Database.

Grimwood
There are basically two kinds of nerf:

The "ethical" nerf IMHO is to leave existing items unchanged under the "pre-nerf" label; subsequent items have the new, lower stats. There's a track record for this, like the classic pre-nerf Circlet of Shadow (175K lowest bazaar price today on Fennin Ro) and its post-nerf version Circlet of Shadows (2.5K in Fennin bazaar today).

The "retro-nerf" is the unethical one, where an item's stats are changed after the person has already gotten it. Poster children for this approach are some of the Thurgadin quest armor pieces.... Plate Bracer and Plate Boots-- Effects: Abundant Food/Drink ! One assumes that the original effects were massively unbalancing to game play, or that a designer was just in a bad mood that day and didn't mind shafting whoever was overly trusting. This "change everything" approach hurts everybody and leads to a lot of dissatisfaction, judging by our bazaar OOCs and bazaar chat channel.

There aren't a whole lot of these Cestuses in existence, and like Maddoc said, it's not like the stats are earthshaking. For those of us who made them in good faith and those who bought them in good faith, let's hope the designer types choose the imho "ethical" approach and do the pre-nerf/post-nerf thing with the Cestus. Don't change the existing items (maybe a dozen or two on Fennin at the moment, my best guess), do change items made with the recipe subsequent to the patch. Maybe change the name a tiny bit on the post-patch items, and everybody is happy.

In fact the "ahh wow that's neat!" factor for us Tradeskillers is the one great thing about this expansion, and I've got hopes that it'll continue that way :-). (Otherwise, I'm sure you've seen, this expansion is getting at best "mixed" reviews, but that's a topic for other boards ;-).)
