Krazick
Having my Pottery at 216... and bought up 47 Cloudy Mana Vials, and worked my trivial up to 218, just this past weekend... at least the Green Ceramic Band is in the correct target of 222.

These have been nailed, without question for a long time (unlike other recipe trivials). Take them as correct.

But I strongly suggest that instead of making 500 Mana Vials in one sitting... make 20, or 40 or 100. Go for the lowest Trivial one first. And work up. Make more as needed.

And please note... with all of the Ceramic Bands that:

A. Players have rarely paid much for them. Their benefit is very limited. Don't make 100, stick them on a mule and hope to sell to recoup losses. You will be sadly disappointed.
B. NPC Vendors will buy both the Fired AND the Unfired versions. And oddly enough, they pay more for the Unfired Versions.
C. The Bands themselves do NOT stack... you will fill up available slots very quickly.

Krazick
I don't have any hard numbers for you...

The general feeling about firing items and selling them to the vendor is that

The cost of the Firing Sheets (based upon # needed, and the Quality of the sheet) is far MORE than the amount the vendor will pay for the fired item.

Even if the unfired item costs you nothing, you would still lose money on the deal by firing them. Hence the sale of the unfired item to the vendor..

Does that make econmic sense? NO
Does it make sense from an EQ Economy persepective? Not to the Player... But to Sony, they don't want money be generated via a single player interacting with NPCs.

Sorry.

But if you find that you can buy HQ Firing Sheets for say "1 Plat"... buy an unfired red ceramic band for 2 Plat... fire it... and resell the fired red ceramic band back to the vendor for 5 Plat... have at it.

I just don't think you will find that scenario.
