Really, I do think taht if you're looking for low, medium or high quality cat, bear or wolf pelts head to Jaggedpine and the bandit camp located in the south east corner of the zone. There's a camp of about 18 to 20 poachers there that respawn about every five minutes or so. When ever I'm on a 'make padded leather for my sweetie's smith' run I go there and hang out for a while.

At lvl 55 the poachers are light blue, kind of ok for AA exp (thanks to the amazingly low respawn time) and are very rarely camped. Even when they are camped the poachers are, at least on Bertox, broken into 3 sub camps and I haven't seen more than two groups there. Usually though the poachers are empty. After a full days hunt (roughly 5 hours for me) I usually have enough leather to make 3 stacks of leather, over 200 plat, sometimes some items, a boat load of runes and stuff like that. Best thing about the poachers is that they never drop ruined pelts. :D

Then it's off to Lfay to pick on the Brownies and the Spiderlings in the Brownie outpost for more exp, brownie bits, and the Spiderlings can drop up to 6 silks per kill.

Having farmed primarily in the Old World for the pelts for my skill-ups so far, I can say the the kejek leopards and leopardesses in the Southern reaches of Stonebrunt are easy to hunt, spawn like drops of rain, and never drop ruined: only LQ, MQ, and the rare HQ. Fairly good drop rate, 2-3 per, and at high level, very little should bother you in that part of the zone. If it's HQ you're after (but you're not), the Karanas are a much better choice (where I farmed for my reinforced and my quivers). As for the silks, I'll throw Misty Thicket into the mix if you can get there safely. The spiderlings and large wood spiders both drop spiderling silks, so you increase your chances of stumbling across a mob that drops what you need.

Hope that helps,
