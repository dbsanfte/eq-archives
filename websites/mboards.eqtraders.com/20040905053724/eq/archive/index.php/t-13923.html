I'll tackle some of your questions;

1. A lot. Alchemy doesn't reach all the way to 250 skill as it is level-capped. You can earn 5 skill points per level, start at level 24. That means that at level 65, you can reach the skill rank of 200. Alchemy is not a cheap skill, so expect to use up a great deal of coin in raising it.

2. Most of the things you need to make potions can be purchased from Alchemy vendors. The complete list (http://www.eqtraders.com/sources/source_trade_page.php?st=Alchemy&menustr=110020000000) is too long to post here, so I just linked it for you.

3. Alchemy allows you to have potions to fill gaps in your abilities, buff yourself, and do other things you couldn't without the potions. The benefits take a little imagination to see, but are certainly there. Blood of the Wolf potions grant SoW. There are potions to add to your resists, potions to add to your stats, potions to transport you, and even potions to do snare.

If you're going to do Alchemy, take it slowly, work on it bit by bit, and it will be smooth sailing. It is both expensive and frustrating to try to do it quickly.
