Tudamorf
Solstice robes are the fastest option to 250 if you have a Tunare priest handy to imbue the emeralds, a 250 jewelcrafter (preferably a JCM3 enchanter too), and some seed money. The only non-purchased item is Spider Silk, which you can easily buy in the Bazaar, so you can do this without farming. Once you get going, you can do a stack per 15-20 minutes, probably faster now with the new UI, not to mention less wrist fatigue. Your successes will also sell back to mitigate your losses. I did the robes to 250 and I have never regretted it.

Othmir fur, in my experience, is very rare in the Bazaar and when I do see it it's very expensive (more expensive than solstice robe components). It's more of a cheaper farming route, but at level 65 you won't get much XP from it and you will be spending hours getting the furs for just one stack of combines. You will also need vials of clear mana. And, you end up with a useless product.

Cultural robes are the worst in my opinion. Not only do you have to contend with a rare forage/loot, but you have to spend ages farming vendors for the research components. Definitely the slowest option of the three, and expensive if you're buying the components. Do this only if you need a break from whatever other method you're using.

Chenier
Both otter and solstice robes are going to require additional classes to help you; otter caps need mana vials (chanter), robes need imbued emeralds (tunare druid or cleric) and high jewelry combine (which I would suggest you go to a chanter with JCM3 because they'll fail less).

But other than that, do whichever doesn't drive you nuts. Going the otter route, you're definitely going to have to farm and it will be slower. However, it will be a HECK of a lot cheaper. Going fast and depending on your cash vs. supply of silks in the bazaar vs. ability to get a Tunare priest, solstice robes may be a better route.

Personally, I did both, but probably more solstice robes because 1) I had the money, 2) I am a tunare priest, 3) spider silk supply was good (see also 1), and 4) I had a chanter friend who had JCM3.
