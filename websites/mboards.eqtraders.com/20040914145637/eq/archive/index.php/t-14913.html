Lothay
When I was a young enchanter, skilling up and making the skill pay for itself, I looked at what items would have value to specific classes.

As a result +HP and +Mana items were my biggest sellers. Followed rapidly by +Cha, +Wis, and +Int items.

Look over the list of things you can make, then decide which to market. Go through zones in which people are leveling up. Lake of Ill Omen, Paludal, etc. and offer them for sale. You'll get enough takers to be worth your time.

KiTA,

Another thought: try going to Shadeweaver's Thicket and killing lesser shades (who are also level 8). The shadeling silk and swirlling shadows can be sold to tailors and blacksmiths for great deal of money. You'll be gaining exp and making lots of plat at the same time. A full stack of swirling shadows can sell for ~500pp, which should be a sizeable chunk of money to put toward your skill.

My bazaar mule always has a stack of enchante clay, silver, electrum, and gold, as well as a stack of vials of viscous mana. If you put your enchanted silver and clay bars up in the bazaar, you will get AT LEAST twice what you paid for the items from the vendor, you will most likely get a lot more, but server prices vary.

The enchanted bars have made me quite a nice sum of money, and still do. They are a great way to fund your skill.

As for items that sell, I've had the best luck with the star rose quartz items (int), jasper (wis), and jade (hp/ac).
