Silverfish
There are a number of ways you can get some serious fun out of things if you're into it.

As Verdandi wrote, farming faction is a good way to pass the time in pursuit of a goal. Getting to the point where you can walk to the bank and do your business without being molested by the locals, in every town and city in the game (including places like Runnyeye and Kael Drakkal) will keep you juggling for a long, long time.

One very curious hobby I've taken to is underwater exploring. Get yourself an EB item and check out the undersurface. If you've never gone to Veksar or Kedge Keep, or the Plane of Water, it's an experience not to be missed. Some folks hate swimming, especially when they fight, but I found it to be quite an interesting challenge.

One extremely odd and possibly dangerous exploring option that I fell into (literally) is exploring under the world. As a cleric, you can fall through the world and cast divine aura (and hope to your diety that you don't fizzle it) and the landing is nonlethal. Then you can tool about under the world, and gate out afterwards. Don't take anything with you that you can't afford to lose, since you could die anyway, but it's a weird thing to view a zone from the bottom up.

Another possibility for exploring is high climbing. This isn't your run-of-the-mill idea of climbing a zone wall, but finding extreme heights to explore. To give yourself an idea of how it works relatively safely, get a lev and then go to the Combine fortress in the Dreadlands. The Kunark portal in the Nexus will take you right near where you need to go, if you can't get a port. Approach one of the huge spires from the back (the outside part from the fortress grounds) and run up the spire. When you get to the top and over the crown of it, pan down, and you'll see the fortress far, far below you. It gave me vertigo the first time. Another place, inspired by a picture I once saw in a .sig, is the statue of Firiona Vie in the elven fortress in the zone of the same name (you did the faction walk described earlier, yes? If not, this can be one of your practice areas). Get up on her head, and the view is spectacular. Get levitation if you're worried about falling, or don't if you like scaring yourself. I only recently started trying to get up on Karnor's Castle, so I don't yet know if it can be done, but if it can I'll let you know.

I hope some of these ideas will relieve you ennui.
