Well, that's it! It's not a button, it's a link. LINK, darn you all! Why back in the day before anything stacked and you had to walk across Everfrost, uphill, both ways, just to buy a brick of ore, calling a link a button would have gotten you banned from the game, and furthermore...

Sorry, but I couldn't resist. Being one of the relative newcomers, I understand where you're coming from. I never tradeskilled, or for that matter even played, before the Planes of Power expansion came out. To me pelts and sheet metal always stacked, you could buy just about everything you need without going more than two zones away, and the concept of faction work to buy materials is still alien to me.

The problem I see is two sided. On one side, there are people who have played since the olden days, who did a lot of stuff the hard way because there wasn't an easy way. They've been bombed by newbies asking questions for ages, and having been on the "master" side of the "OK, how I make metal bits" discussion, I can see where it begins to wear after a while, especially since there are SO MANY people who just want it all handed to them that the good people with simple questions to get started get the ill will.

On the other side, there are lots of people who, for whatever reason, didn't get into tradeskills until recently. They ask a starter question, and wonder why so many oldsters bark at them. They can't see why they should feel badly about having a tool or feature just because there are those who skilled up without them. There are a lot of oldsters who will make a new tradskiller feel inferior without even meaning to, because they "only got into it for the Aid Grimel earring" or "just do it for the money" or any of a million other reasons that are just as valid as doing it for the love of crafting.

The solution to this clash in the middle is obvious, but like real life, there will always be people willing to do it politely, and people willing to do it rudely, and the attitude that crops up when someone does it rudely unfortunately takes time to fade back down. On the whole, this place is one of the friendliest, most helpful and most informative sites I've ever experienced. So if you see attitude flying around a discussion, be patient. Those who really do need the help will get it eventually, and the rudesters will eventually tire and leave.
