zumavt
I did mostly acrylia studded armor post Wus occasionally getting multiple skillups per stack offset a few times with no skillups for a stack. On average I got better around 1.5 skillups per stack though one should plan for the worst and not get disappointed.

I took a slightly different approach than most. I made only WIS gear that seems to sell better than other pieces: legs, arms, tunics and cloaks. While this took much longer to buy that much acrylia, I found that I could sell the pieces at break even prices if I didn't pay more than 50pp for bricks. While it took me 5 months to get to 188, I pretty much broke even when you add the profit and a couple of skillups on fleeting quivers. After a stack of combines, it would take a week or two to sell most pieces during which time I'd be buying superb rockhopper and acrylia for the next run.

Concerning those dismal skillup rate droughts, try exiting the game and restarting. This may only be possible or worthwhile when you have numerous stacks of combines but it has worked for me numerous times to reset the RNG. For example, last weekend I had saved 5 stacks of smithing combines to get from 196 to 200. After getting no skillups with the first two stacks, I logged out and back in and then got 4 stillups in 16 combines. All I was hoping for was to get the 4 skillups with the last 3 stacks but hit a lucky streak with the RNG. This is one reason why I am still sitting on 5 stacks of wyvern and velium studs, it would crush my morale to get very few skillups so I am saving for one or two restarts if the skillups don't occur in the first stack of two. While this doesn't guarantee a better series of skillups, it may provide for better than average results if the RNG is behaving favorable when I finally get started.
