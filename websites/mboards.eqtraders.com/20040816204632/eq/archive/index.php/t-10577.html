Here's a little way around to get some more components (which I did NOT try yet, only speculating, but Im pretty sure it would work)

Get your foraging mule (druid or ranger) to 20th level so he can start LDoN adventures;

Get 3 other players to log mules between levels 20 to 28 (or 27, whatever the level limit in LDoN for groups is), so you get a party of 4 members (the minimum number of players in a group for adventures).

Request an adventure at "Adventure Recruiter", then move in the dungeon. At this point, your friends can log out their mules. Since they did not enter the dungeon, they are not getting a failure - only your foraging mule will get a fail, after spending 1h30 minutes at entrance foraging, PLUS the 30minutes for semi-success. After 2hours of foraging with a skill of ~105, you should have at least enough components for a 10-dose invis.

If you are lucky enough to be an iksar, just let your group know at the end of any adventure, that you will stay in the dungeon during the extra 30min for gathering alchemy components.

Also, at the beginning of your mission, ask rangers/druids/bards to save you the components they might get. By offering to pay them for these, you should get some stacks rather quickly if they maxed out their skills; after all, even if you offer 100pp a stack, it is still in range with our cheapest herbs.

On a side note, there is no reason why we should be whining about a new option for getting our herbs. It would be bad if they changed ALL of the older recipes to require foraged items, but being allowed to get free components via mules/foraging groupmates seems to me like a great thing for us, and should help to regulate the market a bit... unlike those SoW potions that every twink/alt/mule/vendorizer/etc. are selling, taking the price down so much that its barely even worth making them.

SOE did a great job on LDoN for once... AT LEAST, give em some credit on that one. (just for the hell of it, compare LoY to LDoN...ugh.)
