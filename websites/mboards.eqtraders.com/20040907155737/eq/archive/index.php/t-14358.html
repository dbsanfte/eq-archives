Percentage = (skill - (trivial*.75)) + 51.5

That's the basic formula used. So at 252 (max skill) you have --

% = (252 - (335*.75))+51.5

% = 52.25

Now that is only for final combine though, it doesn't take into account the trivial combines along the way that can make use lose ingredients. You might fail several of the combines before you even get to the final combine.

I've always quoted people 40% success rate when they ask, because it can be very streaky. I've had days of making 4 out of 5. And days of making 1 out of 5.

Leewei
If the formula posted in the success rates thread is any indication, the odds of success work out pretty much as follows:

Skill modified by Geerlok - 199 = expected success rate.

If this number is lower than 5, the 5% minimum success rate rule applies.

For those with some math background, the proof follows:

Success = Skill - 0.75 * trivial + 51.5 (round up)

Success = Skill - 0.75 * 335 + 51.5 (round up)

Success = Skill - 251.25 + 51.5 (round up)

Success = Skill - 199

Since all cultural blue diamond recipes appear to have a trivial of 335, this formula predicts a maximum success rate of 53%. It also shows how overwhelmingly useful a Geerlok is in the low 200s when attempting this type of combine.
Reph
According to a write up here somewhere from dev's remarks, high trivial combines have a built-in failure rate that the trivial level doesn't take into account.
i.e. an item may have a triv of 335 and, according to the figures, therefore have a success rate of c.53% but in actuality may only be around 40%.

This is noticable on PoP pottery combines that have a 295 triv vs. PoP smithing combines that have a 335 triv. The success rate for the pottery vs. the smithing doesn't seem to match the supposed trivials at all.
