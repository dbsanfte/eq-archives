Best Anime Ever: Armitage the Third (not the Movie, not the second movie... the OVA series).

Then comes Utena, then Dual, then Excel Saga, then Air Master, then Rose of Versailles, then Tokyo Underground, then Scrapped Princess, then Key: The Metal Idol (might have to move that one up a bit), then Ranma 1/2...

Armitage the Third (OVA series) is available on DVD from Pioneer. It's the Polymatrix (first movie) story, but much more in-depth and a billion times better voice-acting in the Dub.

Dual: Parallel Trouble Adventure is also available on DVD from Pioneer. If you've heard of Evangelion... it's a little like that (kids piloting giant robots), only generally cheerful and funny. Think Tenshi Muyo crossed with Evangelion.

Revolutionary Girl Utena, Movie and TV series, are available on DVD from Software Sculptors. The Movie is the same basic story idea, just told in a very different (almost surrealistic at times) way. It helps to watch the TV series first, btw. ;)

Key: The Metal Idol is available on DVD from Viz... but it's been more than two years now, so many "video stores" probably don't have it. The story is slow-paced at times, and there's a lot of exposition (characters talking to advance the plot/explain the story)... but I love it. Especially the music.

Excel Saga is available on DVD from ADV. Extremely over-the top comedy that is rediculously fast-paced. The American voice actress for Excel strained her voice doing the dub, simply because the character is so manic. The inside jokes (there are lots of those, too) can be easy to miss if you're not a long-time Anime fan... But Excel Saga is still funny, even then.

Air Master, Rose of Versailles, Tokyo Underground and Scrapped Princess are not available in the US. Rose of Versailles is actually fairly old, having been made in the early 80's, I think. The other three are recent series that are very common on the Fan-Sub circut. I think Tokyo Underground is licensed for release in the US (but probably not in production yet), but I know the others are not.

For more information about the Fan-Sub circut, I suggest www.animesuki.com BTW, having a broadband internet connection is best if you're going to get into downloading Anime. :bookworm:
