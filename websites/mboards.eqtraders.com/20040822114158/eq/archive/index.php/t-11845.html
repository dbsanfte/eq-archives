Calabar
In the midst of exploring this new zone I was invited on a couple of adventures with some friends, so I didn’t get to explore it as much as I would have liked. But the zone is very similar to Jaggedpine Forest, even down to being able to forage plant shoots. The mobs are much lower level, but there are griffons and anaconda. Has anyone found them to drop meat? I only had time to kill two or three of each. I didn’t see any meat drop, but that was a very small sample. If they drop meat this zone will make baking much easier to grand master.

Just in case someone might be interested, I’ll add the zone description I posted on my guild’s web page:

Well, I visited the new zone last night. The graphics are really nice, with an ocean beach, two waterfalls and lots of trees. There are bears, wolves, panthers, griffons, turtles and frogs. I also found schools of salt-water fish swimming together in the crystal clear water. All the animals were very green to me at 62, but someone in OOC said that everything was blue at level 34. For the moment the only way to get there is to run through Jaggedpine Forest, which is a much higher level zone. But there is a wayfarer’s camp near the water and I expect that at some point we’ll be able to access the zone via the adventure camps.

This will be a nice addition for mid level characters. The zone is very reminiscent of Jaggedpine Forest, including the ability to forage plant shoots, but with lower level mobs. It will make a very good hunting area for many characters. I found some of the animals to be aggressive, such as the wolves and bears, but others, such as the turtles, frogs and ancient anaconda, completely ignored me.

This is also a great zone for farming pelts. I looted several varieties of bear and wolf pelts, including high quality bear pelts, while I was there. I’m hopeful that griffon and anaconda meat will also drop, but I didn’t see any from the few creatures I killed. I did a little fishing, but thunder salmon were the only zone specific fish I found. There are a few things you can make from the salmon, but nothing extraordinary.

From what I’ve read this new zone is simply a staging area for a new expansion connected to the wayfarers. Still, it’s useful in and of itself. It’s a good zone for level appropriate characters, it provides a new source for some trade skill items and it’s graphically appealing. All in all not a bad deal for a zone added during a patch.

Woo Hoo!! I’m glad to hear that the griffons drop meat. Perhaps the ancient anaconda do as well. I only killed about three griffons and two anaconda with no meat drops. I’m happy to see I was just having a bit of bad luck.

Grenoble, I wouldn’t worry about faction hits. There are only two zones that I’m aware of that give faction hits for killing animals. Don’t kill the bears in Surefall Glade or you’ll tick off all the locals. For some reason the bears in Surefall don’t like Vah Shir shaman. I had one aggro on me once when heading to Jaggedpine to buy cheese when I was about 30th level. I knew better than to kill it so I headed towards the zone line, with several of the locals hot on my tail, quite literally. And killing the bears, wolves or cats in Jaggedpine will lower your faction with the locals in that zone. The griffons, wisps and anaconda are faction free so you can kill them to your heart’s content. But these are the only zones where you have to be careful. You can kill bears and wolves for days on end in places like East Commons and North Karana without taking any faction hits. The only reason not to kill these critters when playing a druid or ranger is for role-playing reasons. And personally I feel that it’s the role of druids and rangers to keep the population of these animals from becoming too large as well as from becoming endangered.

While in this new zone I killed griffons, cats, wolves, bears, snakes and turtles. None of them gave a faction message. So it seems this zone is perfectly safe to hunt in. And if the griffons are indeed dropping meat I expect this will be a heavily hunted zone indeed. Unfortunately, I bet those who can gain experience from the kills will have to compete with high level characters who need the hides for tailoring/smithing and the meat for baking.
