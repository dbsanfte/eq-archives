Updated to here.

A couple of editorial decisions I'm making (hey, if you don't like it, maintain your own list :D ):

1) In order to maintain some vestige of sanity, I'm setting cutoff levels for some tradeskills. Generally, this will depend on how many high-level artisans there are in a given craft. For example, for Jewelcrafting, where there are already 20+ people listed with 250 skill, I'm not inclined to include a submission at 180. I do plan to include anyone who actually has a (unmodified) skill level of OVER 200; if you've committed enough to a skill that you've elected to have it be your single skill over 200 (or to spend 3 AA's on it, should you be of a level to do so), I will include you, no matter how many others are already on that list. In general, if your submission compares reasonably well with the ones already on the list for that skill, it'll go in, but if you're significantly lower, probably not (except in the rarer skills, like tinkering, though for tinkering there's a much better list at the Tinkerer's Compendium...)

2) Regardless of the above, in those skills where race matters (cultural smithing, tailoring), I'll list at least the top couple submissions for any given race (for the appropriate races), regardless of skill level. I don't think this should be a problem, as I think we've already got at least a couple for each appropriate skill combination, except, it appears, troll smiths. (I may have missed one somewhere, but I think that's it...)
