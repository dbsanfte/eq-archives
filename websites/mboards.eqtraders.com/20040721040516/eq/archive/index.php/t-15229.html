Aquila Swiftspirit
I'm guessing they were trying to balance overall numbers of class level AA skills available during the first Luclin period, and overall utility.

For class AA, Enchanters get

Mass Group Buff
Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery
Dire Charm
Gather Mana
Jewel Craft Mastery
Permanent Illusion
Quick Buff

MGB and Gather Mana are really useful for chanters, but situational; DC is useful in specific zones (and was probably more powerful in Luclin than it has been since LDoN or GoD came out). So, the devs gave them something that seemed at least moderately useful.

Rangers, on the other hand, get:

Ambidexterity
Body and Mind Rejuvenation
Physical Enhancement
Double Riposte
Mass Group Buff
Innate Camouflage
Archery Mastery
Endless Quiver

Ok, MGB doesnt' strike me as a really useful ranger skill, but Innate Camo, Archery Mastery, and Endless Quiver are GREAT. I doubt many rangers would choose to lose any of these or the melee based AA in favor of Fletching Mastery.

But I'm willing to bet that a LOT of Enchanters would be willing to lose Jewel Craft mastery for something really useful for a skill useful to the "kill mob X" aspect of gameplay.

That said, I DO think it would be neat if other classes got something like Jewelcraft mastery. Perhaps a GoD style AA which allows anyone to gain a little more success chance at 1 chosen skill (and that anyone would, of course, include enchanters; the devs would have to decide if they could add to Jewelcraft or not).
