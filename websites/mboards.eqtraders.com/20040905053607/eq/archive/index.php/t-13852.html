I went to Abysmal Sea and Natimbi today and busted out the ole fishing pole. I noticed that the tuna and crab I caught sold to vendors for a few platinum each, depending on the weight. So I went to Abysmal to get a good test, and here's what I found out:

1. You won't catch fresh fish. At least I didn't, with a skill of 183. The only things I caught were tuna and crab of various weight, and the occasional dagger and sandal.

2. If you carry a mixing bowl, you can combine one tuna or crab, by itself, and convert it into 1 lb. pieces so that all will stack. When you sell to vendors, the price evens out exactly as it would if you didn't convert them.

3. With 3 stacks of bait, I made 270 platinum. Not crazy money, but it's significantly better than any LDON runs that I do, and far better than any other fishing spot I've ever heard of.

If you forage while you fish, I'd recommend going to Natimbi since the stuff you forage there is new and mostly used for tradeskills, whereas in Abysmal the only things you forage are the standard fruit, water, veggies, etc.

Hope this helps.

Froni Thunderpaws
You make crab meat by "combining" in a mixing bowl (or any of the other fished and foraged meats in GoD so far). Place a single X weight something meat in a bowl with nothing else and hit combine and you will wind up with X 1lb something meats (and learned the recipe to convert X weight something meat -so a 10 lb tuna makes 10 piece stack of 1 lb tuna meat). Now take each of those individually and do the same thing to get something meat without the weight on it and you will be ready for the baking combines (so 1 lb tuna meat is now tuna meat). I think the conversions take a baking skill of 16.

Look at the baking recipes on main site and the GoD baking threads for details.
etitameh
combine any tuna or crab (whatever number of lbs it is) alone in a mixing bowl to get that number of 1lb pieces. example: 7lb saltwater tuna in mixing bowl --> 7 x 1lb saltwater tuna. all 1lb tuna stacks together, all 1lb crab stacks together.

i believe this also works for the foraged/dropped hynid and cragbeast meats, but i haven't gotten my hands on either of those. ;)

1 and 2lb. tuna/crabs have never had a tribute value anyway - just look at lucy. This is to stop people from increasing the value by chopping it down. The pp worth was lb x 0.4762, and thus is not affected by moving from 15lb to 1lb - you end up with exactly the same pp. The tribute was half of the lbs rounded up however, and is an integer unlike, for example, 15lb selling for 7.143pp.

So:
8lb. = 8 / 2 = 4 tribute.
7lb. = 7 / 2 = 3.5 rounded up to 4 tribute.

If you broke them down to 1lb, and could donate 1lb:
1lb. = 1 / 2 = 0.5 rounded up to 1 tribute (lowest integer possible)
8 x 1lb. = 8 tribute. Double what you would have got if you just handed in the 8lb by itself.

This further proves that the sale/donate price of crab/tuna was planned carefully, and wasn't just a mistake or oversight left in the game that had to be corrected (*cough* nerfed) to "the way it was intended".

It's still my impression that tuna/crab etc were created to supplement tribute, since it was released in the same expansion, is one of the very few stackable/fishable items that will donate for tribute, and it just makes perfect sense that you can sit around, relax, chat, wait for friends etc, whilst preparing for your tribute-buffed adventuring later.
