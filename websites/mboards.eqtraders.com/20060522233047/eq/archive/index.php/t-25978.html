for a long time people had the mantra that skill-ups were about 1 in 20 or 5%.The reason for this was that prior to the changes to the skillup formula, you really did have a 5% chance to skill up from 190 skill onwards.

When the changes to the skillup formula went in, the skillup curve was made much smoother. From skill 175 to 268 (I think, I'd have to look it up to be sure), you had an easier chance to skill up than before. From skill 269 to 300, it was harder, with the final skillup from 299 to 300 being approximately twice as hard to get as before.

I understood that 3 factors are relevant when looking for a skill-up:

Your actual skill compared to the Trivial for the item being made.
How high your primary attribute is (I.e INT/WIS etc).
How lucky you are.
That's absolutely correct. Your chance to skill up is approximately doubled when you succeed a combine, compared to when you fail. So by working as close to your skill level as you can, you maximize your chance to succeed. Your primary attribute is your main determinant in your chance to skill up, so you want to get it as high as possible. Wunshi and other cap-lifting spells are a great way to boost that.

And of course, everyone here knows the impact of luck. Good streaks and bad streaks usually make for memorable posts on the Primal Scream forum. =)
