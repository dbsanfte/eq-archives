Well... since you asked...

I'd thank the /pet (petitioner) for their input and tell them I'd look into it.

I'd pull up your account records and see if you had been previously warned about naming violations or had this name previously noted as an exception by a Guide or GM.

Then I'd window out (old days, check on second computer) to see if that spelling were indeed a copyright violation.

Presuming:

1) No prior naming problems
2) No prior permission
3) It is the copyrighted spelling

Then I'd contact you, politely, and tell you about the problem. If you claimed it was your real life name I'd say that it's a Bad-Idea(tm) to use your real life name in an online environment because of the rise of identity theft. Then I'd explain that you would have to request a name change from a GM.

(In the old days I'd tell you to /petition during Server-GM L's office hours, or if that was not possible to email the Server Senior Guides and make an appointment with one of them. Now Sr's don't have power to change first names and there are no "Server-GMs" ... sigh)

Then I'd ask if you wanted me to suggest some possible names. Then when you were ready to get re-named I'd bump the petition up to red and go look for a GM to resolve the problem.

OF COURSE if you happened to be level 35 or higher the point is moot, I'd thank the /pet, note it in my log and delete the petition. (No first name changes for 35+ characters unless they violate naming policy item 1 or 2. Period. Ever. ... Unless they pay the fee for it.) :rolleyes:
