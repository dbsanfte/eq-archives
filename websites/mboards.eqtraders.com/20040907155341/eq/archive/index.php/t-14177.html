Reflan
When there's something I need a vast amount of, like celestial essences, I sometimes buy out the little guys first, even if they charge a bit more, unless it's quite a bit more.

If I buy out the guy with hundreds(and yes, I've gone through hundreds VERY quickly on skill-up runs), more often than not the next thing I know he is racing me to the remaining sellers in the bazaar to buy them all out and double the price, hoping I will now just be a hapless target for his greed.

Plus if people see their smaller amounts aren't enticing people to buy, they may just not want to make 400 more at a time when they can't seven sell 60. They may leave the market for a while, or forever. I want to encourage them to stay. So I buy out some of the lower priced guys, but also some of the people who charge a little more just to keep them in the market so prices don't lock up without any competition. The guys with monster numbers of items will be there tomorrow; tomorrow if the little guys are left, I buy out more of their stuff so they're motivated to keep the competition strong.

I almost never buy the guy with tons of stuff out unless he's pricing his stuff at a real bargain, because these guys usually have or quickly develop the ability to control the market. Buying them out almost always results in them buying everyone else out they can find and then raiding the price dramatically. Both the big seller and the little seller, all at their various prices, has to see that there's money from everybody but also a threat from competitors everywhere.

Same thing with spider silk and spiderling silk, which I've used to turn into swatches for my own skill ups and leather padding for sale. I usually leave at least one of the cheapest sellers with his wares intact even if I'm doing an out and out blitzkreig and buying up almost the whole bazaar's worth of a particular item. I don't want new sellers thinking there is nothing in the market so they can name their own price, or old hands thinking that either. I want them to conform to the low price they already see on somebody else.
