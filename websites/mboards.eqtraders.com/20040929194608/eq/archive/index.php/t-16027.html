Delores Mulva
I saw where you posted this as a bug, Absor, but perhaps it could be converted into a feature? Folks have been a little peeved that, until you've done many, many combines, you get a better return on conversion simply by handing the items to the conversion NPC. This dissatisfaction led to people asking for a higher drop rate on tradeskill stuff, which has been implemented.

But what if, instead of a high drop rate, there was a high conversion rate instead like there is now? Since the minimum yield is two, a player is rewarded for doing the conversion quest immediately. Because each conversion gives one more item than before, the effective drop rate is boosted by between 33 and 50 percent. That should be sufficient, combined with the old drop rates, that tradeskillers can find what they need without servers being flooded with 16/20 one-handed weapons.

Based on the number of such weapons I see in the Bazaar on my home server (Quellious), I believe the new drop rate is too generous and there is a serious risk of massive mudflation. The guilds doing Kod'Taz and above have no need for (almost) any of the products made with GoD tradeskills, but do have a severe need for cash because of the Qvic armour quests (10500 pp per piece). Since GoD, for all intents and purposes, doesn't have any droppables that could be sold like in PoP (spells), all the guilds can sell is tradeskill weapons. Since the raid zones are instanced, and the tradeskill drop tables are common to all Kod'Taz+ mobs, every guild with Kod'Taz access can be farming components for these weapons at the same time. The result is what I've seen on my server: multiple guild bots, each with up to 10 16/20 weapons of each type (1HS, 1HB, piercing, HTH), trying to undercut one another to sell out before the demand dries up. Last time I checked, one of the 16/20 piercers was selling for the same as a 31% haste belt. Having a dagger which, in the hands of a rogue, will likely out-DPS any piercer from Luclin back be priced the same as a common haste belt tells me there's a vast oversupply of daggers.

Oh, as for my original post: I've done the refinements post-patch, and the recipes have been learned again. Thank you very much for the prompt response. :)
