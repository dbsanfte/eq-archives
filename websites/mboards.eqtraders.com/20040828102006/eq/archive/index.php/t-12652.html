I was sitting in the bazaar in the middle of a skillup run on patty melts, privately chuckling at the cyclical conversation of price whining, reseller bashing, and desperate pleas for help when a lovely frog toon stepped in front of my trader and started to purchase some of my patty melts. I thanked him, as is only polite, because I happened to be sitting ther at the computer and we got to talking about tradeskilling and being new and how to keep loving the game without burning out. My main toon is a tailor. His toon is much higher than mine. Possibly much higher than mine will ever be, who knows. He asked me to drop out of trader for a moment and much to my surprise when I did a substantial sum of plat popped up in a trade window.

"For your tailoring. Because you love this."

How do you thank someone for this? My god. I felt awkward turning it down and awkward taking it. I took it and promised him and myself that I would do the same. I do it now when I can, if it's buffing a newbie or healing them while they're getting pounded on by one of the Vengeful Trio. Or perhaps handing over that polar bear skin that I have no use for at all, but which might help them with the newbie quest. I've never given plat though. But now I have a debt to repay.

He probably doesn't even read this site or forum. I won't name his toon. But I wanted him to know I'm still thankful. I do love this. Thanks, sir. You make the game a better place.

Lothay
Long ago there was a small group of people, and they all wore a single guild tag. They were generally just people playing the game, but they had one small difference. They liked to help.

And so a tradition was born. These people assist others when they can, mounting expeditions to recover corpses, logging in alts to rescue someone imperiled, or just ready to thrown in and help finish a quest or do some buffing for fun.

And these acts are inspired by the genuine interest in helping people. Sounds corny, or perhaps contrived, but it is true.

The kindness of strangers starts with you and if you're kind to those around you, others will be kind in turn. I won't share any particular stories, I'll leave those for others, but I'm glad to be part of that group of people, and I'm happy to see others follow in their footsteps.
