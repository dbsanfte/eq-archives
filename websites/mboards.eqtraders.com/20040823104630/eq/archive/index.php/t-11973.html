Sylphan
It's amazing how many foraged things sell for 30 pp and up. There are several good forages on every continent, but you do need to have some idea what's best.

All the temper ingredients from the LDoN zones, also Sedge Branches.

Plant Shoots from Jaggedpine, although bakers pay more for Griffon Meat and Anaconda Meat than for Plant Shoots. Justice Fruit is always in demand from PoJ. Tea Leaves from the Karanas. Tufts of fur from Velious zones sell by the hundreds to Halfling tailors. Planar Branches from Plane of Growth are probably the most expensive forage of all. Yew Leaves from Wakening Land are also pretty expensive.

Vegetables are more in demand than the other common forages, but still only sell for one or two plat each. Eggs of all types sell for a few plat for bakers to make dough. But Dragon eggs and Drake eggs from Skyshrine or ToV are for tailors. Mushrooms foraged on Luclin also sell well, as do Cinnamon Sticks from Greater Faydark.

Morning Dew/Sylvan Berries is at the top of the list for smithing/tailoring... but also the orcs in Lesser Faydark are only around level ten or so, the pixies around level 20, the brownies under 40 I think. They all drop berries, you can hunt them while foraging... and pixie dust and brownie parts also sell well in bazaar.

Sylvan Berries aren't the only good tailoring drop... all the cultural tannin forages sell for around 50 pp each. You could try in Dreadlands or Firiona Vie (the barley also drops from creatures, not just forage). I found an especially good forage rate in Field of Bone, but there's nothing there to kill for drops.

There's more, but I figure that's enough to keep you busy for a long long time.

Hrmm, lets see. Gonna make a list of plat making forages and zones that I can think of...

LDoN - Prolly the best you can do all around. Exp, usually good
cash in and of itself, points, and 100pp+ forages. There
are tons of forages that are used in tradeskills in these
dungeons! Smithing, Brewing, Baking, Alchemy. Almost
everything you forage is used for something.

WL- Foraged Yew Leaves, kill Tigeraptors/Panthers/Black Panthers
for thier hides, and also haze panther eyes for tinkering $$$

WW- Forage Drake/Dragon Eggs and Tufs of fur, kill Wolves and
Mammoths for pelts.

Warslik Woods- Forage Oak Bark, kill goblins at the camp for
Shrunken Goblin SKull Earring and other goodies.

Lfey- Forage Morning Dew/Slyvan berries, kill brownies for parts,
and pixies for dust

EK- Forage Tea Leaves/Karana stone thing, kill cats and spiders
for silk/pelts

CS- No forages worth anything, but kill wyverns/drakes/othmir for
pelts, and fish cobalt cod while ya med *othmirs and named
drake/wyverns are bad faction*

PoJ- Forage Justice Fruit, Bread Crumbs and Cockroaches, can kill
random junk for giggles and maybe a lil exp

PoS- fish for Thunder Salmon

Luclin- many zones have mushroom forages, and lots of stuff to
kill while you do it

PoG- Planar Oak forage

Jaggedpine- Forage Plant shoots, kill griffons/anaconda's for meat

Gfey- Forage Morning Dew/Cinamon Sticks, Kill pixies for dust,
Brownies and bandits for different meats.

Thats all I can think of off the top of my head atm, I will post anything else that comes to mind later. A couple other tradeskill style money options for you too though....

Twilight Sea- Kill elementals by the droves for tradeskill stuff
(pottery and smithing)

PoK- Forage Habenero Peppers, do combines of Celestial
Essences, buffs for donation, basic smithing combines
(ie med qual folded sheets, metal bits, rings etc.. though this
might be taking a chance)

Also- If you can manage it, sell the other componants for known
skill recipes along with the foraged item. (ie: Thunder
Salmon fished in PoS, and Bread Crumbs foraged in PoJ....
Mushrooms foraged in Luclin, with premade dark chocolate
cream) That way, you are one stop shopping, and people
will always return to you first, cause you are know for
having everything they need.

Last one- Low quality rockhopper hides (hides can be scaled
down, but dont ever scale down Superb or Flawless...a
tailor would die =P) from anywhere there are
rockhoppers, and shadeling silk from outside Shar Val.
Make leather padding for smiths, it sells well, and they
go through it by the pack full doing most pre LDoN
combines


Ok, Im spent. Thats all I got for you, but its some good ways to make money and help fellow tradeskillers as well. Hope you like some of my ideas.
