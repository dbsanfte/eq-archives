bearcaller
If your computer has on board video and you haven't noticed any change by adding an agp or pci video card there is a possibility that you are still using the onboard video. Most boards that have onboard video require you to set a specific jumper to change your active video card to an agp or pci slot. Check with your board manufacturers website for where and how to change this jumper; and if your board is a little out of date then just google it and you should have an answer.

Also be aware that memory is not necessarily the most important factor when it comes to a video card. A ti4200 with 64mb of memory is a huge difference from a mx400 with the same amount of memory. Check into not just the memory of your vid card but also the clock speed and benchmarks. Also, if your card isn't using DDR memory you need to get one that does--the difference is huge.
