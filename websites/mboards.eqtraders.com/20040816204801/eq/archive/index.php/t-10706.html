Hello!

Are you a research component packrat? If so, this might help you out. I created a spreadsheet that can be used to keep track of what spells and how many of that spell you can make with the resources you have available. All you have to do is an inventory of which components you have and how many, and it will tell you what you can make.

I'm uploading it here for your use. I play two characters with my boyfriend who also plays two. We make a great team and typically, unless friends wish to join us, all the loot goes to our cause =) I hate selling useful things like components or tradeskill items because I'm a big fan of tradeskills. Therefore, I found the task of sorting through all those 16 bags of components to figure out what we could make intimidating. I found creating this spreadsheet more interesting so I'll never have to think that hard again ;)

I hope it proves to be a useful tool for at least some of you out there! Please post any questions or comments about the spreadsheet on this thread, whether it be complaints or compliments. Most definitely, mention anything inaccuracies you might have noticed.

Thanks ahead for the feedback! Good luck fellow artisans!

For those who don't wish to mess with Excel:
*Download the sheet (making sure it will not overwrite yours. Hopefully you renamed it ;P).
*Open up old and new.
*Highlight cells B3:B173 in your old "Amount" column in the "Components" sheet, copy.
*Select B3 in the "Components" sheet of the new file and paste.
*Save new.
*Close old and delete old.
*Eat a cookie or do a little dance to the wonders of copy and paste.
*Don't forget to rename new to something happier. Bob, for example.

For those who just like to mess:
*Unprotect the "Components" and "Spells" sheets. (Tools --> Protection --> Unprotect Sheet)
*In cell C20 of the "Components" sheet, add "blessing of aegolism (C60)" before KEI if you want to be consistent.
*In cell E30 of the "Spells" sheet, change 'Left' to 'Right'.
*Be a safe and reprotect the "Components" and "Spell" sheets.

I too have created a spreadsheet for high level research, but it does not actually contain inventory columns. Mine is strictly like this:

Column 1: every ingredient (sometimes duplicated because some classes use it for more than 1 spell)
Columns 2-8: each class.
The 'meat' of the spreadsheet says what each ingredient is for in this format: "53, Scent of Terris" which is in the necro column.

On top of each column I put pull down arrows; you can pull down the class arrow to the spell you want, and see what ingredients you need from column A (the ingredients column);
OR you can pull down the arrow in the ingredients column to see what spell(s) it is used for.

I also created separate tabs just for each class, incase I gave this to someone who didn't know how to use pull down arrows.

Sounds like some of the references might either not be loaded on your computer or are just not selecting as it should be for some reason.

Open the spreadsheet.
Hit tools->macro->Visual basic editor (or hit alt+f11)
