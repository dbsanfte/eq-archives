You may be half right...

First, it's not unethical, it's capitalism and free market trade. The market price is determined by supply and demand. I someone will buy my picnics at 20pp each, they are satisfied, and so am I. Making a profit, no matter how big that profit, isn't unethical if both the seller and is willing to sell and the buyer is willing to buy. It's a contract. Both are willing participants, and it is worth the price to the buyer. If the buyer doesn't like the price, he or she can skill up baking or do without.

Which brings me to the second point. It may be greedy, but I don't think so. We've worked on tradeskills, and worked a lot. Say what you want about tradeskills being too easy now, etc., but the fact of the matter is it takes a lot of time to GM a tradeskill, even if the clicks are now fewer. They want picnics? They can pay me for my time and energy.
