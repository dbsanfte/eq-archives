Welcome to my guide to the best sites for the upcoming Bioware version of Neverwinter Nights, scheduled for release at the end of June 2002.  Yes, you can pre-order the game. The game is now in beta; click here for the press release.  In addition, the beta toolset has been released for all you builders and dungeon masters:  see Beta Toolset in the third chart.  

          This site consists of: the main resource chart, a chart of other useful sites, and finally a chart for builders and dungeon masters.  If a slot is blank in the first chart below, either the site at the top of the column does not offer that information or it is not so good, in my humble opinion.  Warning:  Given that the beta toolset has just recently been released, a large amount of information about the final version of the game is just becoming available.  Not all the links here may have the latest info (though presumably they will soon); probably the forums are your best bet at the moment for up-to-the-minute information.  

          If you have suggestions--or if you find broken links--shoot me an e-mail!  (And if you're looking for my EverQuest site, click here, if you are looking for my Dark Age of Camelot site, click here, and if you're looking for my Ultima Online site, click here.)  

          Neverwinter Nights (NWN) will be different in that the software manufacturer--Bioware--will NOT be hosting the game worlds.  Bioware will simply sell the software, and various volunteer groups will provide the servers and worlds for game play.  (One advantage is that there will be no monthly charge.)  Different servers will have somewhat different game rules.  Not sure where to begin in understanding the game?  You many want to go to the first chart below and start with Guides & FAQ, then move on to Classes.  To see what the game will look like, visit Images in the second chart.

BioWare The software manufacturer for Neverwinter Nights.  Among other things, this revised site offers forums, downloads and links, and special sections for players, dungeon masters and world builders.

Character Creation See Gladimir Player Information for info on spells, ability information, character guides, skills, feats and more.  Bioware offers an extensive character creation guide.  FatherMatt's recommendation for newbies:  first pick the class you want to be, then pick a race that fits well with that class.  Also see Bioware's What method will be used to determine my character's core statistics.  
Deities Each world may have its own deities, but for the Forgotten Realms gods and goddesses see 
     an excellent chart in The Daggerford's Campaign's Gods, 
     The Sorcerer's Place Forgotten Realms, 
     Deities of the Realms by Don Riggs, and
     Deities by the Kitaran Sea Campaign.  
Elvish Dictionary Many thanks to the Grey Company for making available this Elvish Dictionary!
Guild Software Guild-Builder and Guild Boss offer software to manage a guild; there is a fee for use.  Player Vault is free, though it is much more basic.

Litigation This is history now, but I include it for those who are curious.  For info about the BioWare and InterPlay lawsuit click here.  The outcome:  Bioware and InterPlay settled; Infogrames will publish NWN.  
Neverwinter Nights "The Official Neverwinter Nights website."  This now points to the Bioware site; it's not clear if this will be resurrected as a separate site or not.  
Neverwinter Nights Consortium One of the "Campaign Worlds" with roots in the original Neverwinter Nights.
          "The first goal of the Neverwinter Consortium is to create the closest possible facsimile of the original America Online-hosted Neverwinter Nights game, which was closed by AOL at the height of its popularity in 1997."  
         Offers FAQ, maps, a board, news, store and more.  For other campaign worlds, see Servers & Worlds in the first chart.  
Order the Software You can pre-order BioWare's Neverwinter Nights software through the Neverwinter Nights Consortium store.  By doing so, a small amount of the purchase money will go to the Consortium, a non-profit 
Original Neverwinter Nights If you want to see archives and information on the original (AOL) Neverwinter Nights (circa 1994-97), check this site!  "This is the unofficial Classic Neverwinter Nights Archive. There is nothing of the kind on the web, and I felt it was important to display what Neverwinter was before the making of the Interplay version of the game."
Servants of Mystery One of the original (AOL) Neverwinter guilds still in existence today...and my guild.  The famed "fireballing midget" graphic gives some idea of the primitive graphics available at that time for online games.  The guild will be located on the Neverwinter Nights Consortium servers.  For other Servants of Mystery sites, see the Servants of Mystery Links Page.  For other guilds, see Guilds in the first chart.  
Site Search NWN Stratics has a search engine for its site in the right-hand corner; see the Neverwinter Nexus Knowledge Base and the Neverwinter Vault Knowledge database as well.

For Builders and Dungeon Masters:

Beta Toolset      See Neverwinter Vault's Toolset Beta Page where you can download the beta toolset and view a toolset review and intro; there are additional download sites at 3-D Gamers.  
     Wydraz offers a beginner tutorial.  
     Bioware is hosting toolset forums, Whoop offers beta toolset FAQ, there is a tip or two on learning scripting by Pat Chan of Bioware, and Richard Conner has prepared a Scripting Reference PDF.  
     In addition, Neverwinter Vault provides a Toolset Tip of the Day and a Module database where folks can share their modules if they choose.  
Forums See BioWare's NWN Dungeon Master's Realm, Neverwinter Haven's Dungeon Master Forum, NWN Stratic's Master's Hall, NWN Vault's Dungeon Master Board, and Planet Neverwinter's Dungeon Masters Den.  Also see News below.  
Links Go to Neverwinter Vault, then scroll down and click "Resource Links" under "DM Resources".  (I can't link there directly because this uses a JavaScript tie to their database.)
Mapping The Clan ROM offers information and downloads of the NWN map editor and the NWN map editor message board.  
Planning Worlds and Adventures      Gamespy offers many adventure and dungeon creation guides; scroll down to "General Articles".  
     Neverwinter Vault offers Dungeon Master Articles, module creation articles and module tutorials.
     Also see NWN Stratic's Dungeon Master Strategy. 
     There is also the Roleplaying Tips Newsletter site.  
Roleplaying Check out RolePlayingMaster, although the site takes forever to download.  
Scripting Start with GameBanshee's Neverwinter Scripting for background information. 
Then see NeverWinter Nexus and click on NWScript for some FAQ about NWscript.  
Neverwinter Stratics has a Scripting Forum.
Finally, Neverwinter Vault offers a Scripting University, a scripting course, a scripting forum, and a commands database.
