Welcome to my guide to the best sites for the upcoming Bioware version of Neverwinter Nights, scheduled for release in early 2002.  If a slot is blank in the first chart below, either the site at the top of the column does not offer that information or it is not so good, in my humble opinion.  If you have suggestions--or if you find broken links--shoot me an e-mail!  (And if you're looking for my EverQuest site, click here, and if you're looking for my Ultima Online site, click here.)  
          For info and rumors about the BioWare/InterPlay lawsuit click here.   The latest:  Bioware and InterPlay have settled; Infogrames will publish NWN!  (Last updated 1/25/02.)
          Also see the new resource chart at the bottom For Dungeon Masters!
          Neverwinter Nights (NWN) will be different in that the software manufacturer--Bioware--will NOT be hosting the game worlds.  Bioware will simply sell the software, and various volunteer groups will provide the servers and worlds for game play.  (One advantage is that there will be no monthly charge.)  Different servers will have somewhat different game rules.   Neverwinter.net will list the servers and allow players to sort and select them.  And, yes, you can pre-order the software at several sites. 
            Not sure where to begin in understanding the game?  You many want to go to the first chart below and start with Guides & FAQ, then move on to Classes.  To see what the game will look like, visit Images in the second chart.

Neverwinter.net No, it's not open yet, but it looks as if it will be key.  
          "Neverwinter.net is a dedicated matching service that is being integrated directly into the Neverwinter Nights game. It will be a central gathering place for the game’s online community and will allow you chat, plan, and adventure with fellow players, Dungeon Masters, and module creators. As with other matching services of this sort, Neverwinter.net will list of currently active servers and allow you to sort them according to a number of important parameters such as game type and ping. Launching new servers and joining existing ones will be a quick and painless ordeal, even for new users."
Neverwinter Nights "The Official Neverwinter Nights website."  This now points to the Bioware site; it's not clear if this will be resurrected as a separate site or not.  
Neverwinter Nights Consortium One of the "Campaign Worlds" with roots in the original Neverwinter Nights.
          "The first goal of the Neverwinter Consortium is to create the closest possible facsimile of the original America Online-hosted Neverwinter Nights game, which was closed by AOL at the height of its popularity in 1997."  
         Offers FAQ, maps, a board, news and more.  For other campaign worlds, see Servers & Worlds in the first chart.  
Order the Software You can pre-order Bioware's Neverwinter Nights software from Amazon.com, Dragon and EB Games among others.  
Original Neverwinter Nights If you want to see archives and information on the original (AOL) Neverwinter Nights (circa 1994-97), check this site!  "This is the unofficial Classic Neverwinter Nights Archive. There is nothing of the kind on the web, and I felt it was important to display what Neverwinter was before the making of the Interplay version of the game."

For Dungeon Masters:

Tools--Start Here! Begin with the BioWare forum on "What tools can we currently use to create NWN Dungeons?" and "Something Every DM should be doing".  The bottom line:  there are preparatory things a Dungeon Master can be doing--but because the NWN toolset has not been released yet (and apparently won't be until the game is close to being released), no actual NWN modules can be built. 
     For basics on tiles and modules, see the NeverWinter Nexus offerings on Areas, Tiles and Modules.  (Yes, NWN is tile-based, as opposed to using 3D geometry.)
     Check out RolePlayingMaster, although the site takes forever to download.  
     The Clan ROM offers information and downloads of the NWN map editor and the NWN map editor message board.  
     Head here for Dungeon Crafter.  See also Tyger's Guide to Dungeon Crafter and Hints and Tips for Dungeon Crafter, as well as Qball's RPG site, where you can pick up Dungeon Crafter PSD and the Dungeon Drafter simulation kit.  Also see Tilesets below.  
     Also see Modeling and Scripting below.
