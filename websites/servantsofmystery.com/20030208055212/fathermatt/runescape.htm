Other Useful Sites
 If a category has * after it, there are additional sites listed for that category in the chart above.
Archery Runescape Help has a page regarding archery tactics and an article on archery, and Runescape Tips has an archery page.  
Boards* Runescape Planet provides a Message Board.  Also see the Runescape Hall boards. 
Capes & Dyes Info on capes and dyes is available from Gunn Associates' capes page. 
Clans Runescape Tips has two pages on clans (aka guilds):  clans1 and clans2.  
Events Runescape Help tries to list upcoming events.  Also see Runescape Planet's Events page.  
Experience Charts This experience meter at the Gunn Associates site shows the experience needed for each level.  Runescape Tips provides a monster database showing the level of each opponent and how much experience it provides.  Runescape Help offers a similar experience chart.  
Firemaking Gunn Associates explains firemaking and woodcutting.  
Guild Software Guild-Builder and Guild Boss offer software to manage a guild; there is a fee for use.  Player Vault is free, though it is much more basic.  
Herblaw See Runescapetip's Herblaw page and the Runescape Planet page on Herblaw.  
High Scores The official site provides high scores.  Runescape Help offers the high scores lists along with a search function.  
Imps Imps may be found in Lumbridge, Varrock and Karamja Volcano.  Runescape Tips offers a  map of where the imps spawn.  And if you don't know why you should hunt imps, see Quests above.  =)
Locations* RS Guild provides an explanation of certain guild buildings that can only be entered when you reach a high enough level for that skill.  Also see the Runescapetips Shops page.  
Magic* Runeworld has a list of rune locations (scroll down).
Manual The official site provides an online manual.  
Map Dots As Runescape Help explains, on the map available in your game window, "You are the big white square in the middle. Yellow squares are computer characters and black ones are stairs or ladders. Blue ones are scenery and red ones are objects that are lying around. White ones are other human players and green ones are your friends."
Maps* Rune News provides wonderful land maps and mine maps, and Alien Chaos has FULL map of Runescape that includes the Wilderness, islands etc.--all with the usual icons.  
Money-making  Runescape Help offers a page on how to make money.  One thing to do is buy silk in Al Karid (purchased for 3 gold each) and sell them in the clothing shop in Varrock (for 16 gold each).  Be sure to wait a few seconds between each transaction when selling so the price rises back to 16 gold.  The round trip from Varrock (including selling) takes about 20 minutes, and it's common to return with 20 or so silks for a net profit of 260 gold.  
Monsters The Order of the Spiff provides Monster Analysis and Monster Map pages.  
Newbie Tips See FatherMatt's own Tips for Newbies.  
News Runescape Tips has a news page, the Player's Guide to Runescape offers news, and Gunn Associates provides news as well.  There is also news--as one might expect--at Rune News.  
Official Site The official Runescape site offers downloads, Customer Support, the Online Manual, the World Map and more.  
Prayer*      Prayer increases by burying bones and simply using prayer. Prayer can be recharged at any church's altar.  One way to increase prayer is by going to an altar, running ALL prayers that you can simultaneously, then clicking on the altar to recharge.  
     By picking up then burying enough bones, you raise your prayer level.  One place to pick up bones is in Edgeville in and around the two buildings closest to the bridge.  Perhaps an even better place is the house and cow pasture area just north of Lumbridge.  Also see the Prayers section in the first chart.  
     And a tip of the helm to Gunn Associates for the following:  "Free normal bones can be picked at ghost town (nothing guarding). Big bones can be picked up at around level 20 wilderness on the east side, but guarded by level 31 armed skeletons (beware).  Normal bones give you 3.75 prayer experience when buried (15 prayer exp for each 4 bones). And big bones give you 12.5 prayer exp when buried (25 prayer exp for each 2 big bones)."
Prices* Runescape Tips offers an interesting page on the impact of selling on merchant item prices.  
Quests* Runescape Reference offers an extensive guide to quests.  Rune News provides a walk-through of the dragon quest.  Alien Chaos has quest walk-throughs.  
Scams For some of the ongoing scams, see Zonia88's Scam Guard, My Runescape Site's scams, and scams by Runescape Help
Skill Advancement Runescape Tips has a page explaining how skills advance, with links to various guides. Runescape Help also provides an article on raising skills.  Runescape Planet has a number of skills guides in the left-hand column.  
Skill Calculators Runescape Tips offers a variety of skill calculators.  Runescape Help offers a variety of calculators to help you determine what you will have to do reach a specific target in skills like mining etc. 
Staffs (aka Staves) Runescape Help has an article on the various magic staffs. 
Strength Potions Runescape Tips provides a page on making strength potions. 
Thieving See Runescapetips' Thieving Guide and Runescape Planet's Thieving page.  
Trade Certificates Runescape Tips has a page dedicated to trade certificates. 
Trading Runescape Help offers a forum for trades.  
Updates See Runescape Planet's Game Updates.  
Utilities Rune News has a page with utilities for downloading.  The Mad Onion calculator is available here.  
Wilderness Gunn Associates provides an article on adventuring in the Wilderness.  
Wizard Items      For runes, see the rune shop in Varrock, or defeat dark wizards in the "Stonehenge" circle just southeast of the south Varrock gate or at the Wizard's Tower.
     For staffs, see the staff shop in Varrock.
     For hats, visit the magic shop in Port Sarim. (Blue hats are better than black.)
     For robes, check the general stores or defeat wizards in the "Stonehenge" circle just southeast of the south Varrock gate or at the Wizard's Tower. (Blue robes are better than black.)
     Actually, a good place to search for blue hats and robes is in the Wizard Tower and on the ground a bit northwest of the crossroads just north of Port Sarin.  It seems a lot of players fight mages there and simply leave the hats and robes on the ground.  
     For black capes, defeat the Highwaymen.  Highwaymen can be found only around the crossroads just north of Port Sarim. They always drop a black cape. Blue capes can only be purchased in the Guardians Guild building; entrance requires 32 points of questing experience.  (Blue capes are better.)  Red capes have no armor effect.  
     Gloves and boots can be purchased at general stores.  Alternatively, you can kill cows to get cowhides, visit the tannery in Al Karid to have it made leather, then use a needle and thread.  (If you start in Lumbridge, take the bridge to the east and then take the road that runs north, you'll find cows in an enclosure on the right-hand side.)  Al Karid has a crafting shop that sells needles and thread.
