1. Statement of Purpose

The Servants of Mystery shall have the following purposes:
* To promote role playing among both Guild members and non-Guild members, regardless of alignment.
* To instruct the Good (and good-hearted neutrals) in the use, range, affected area and duration of the spells.
* To aid fellow Guild members, the weak, and the Good in battle.
* To educate the weak and the new in the ways of their Realm, regardless of whether they are Guild members.
* To aid fellow Guild members in all of their endeavors in their Realm.
* To share weapons, armor, magical items, maps, knowledge and money with fellow Guild members.
* To promote fellowship within the Guild.
* To recruit new members.
* To train and max all members.

2. Proposed Activities

The proposed activities for the Servants of Mystery shall be as follows:
*Participation in events sponsored by their Realms.
*Guild meetings at least once a month, and more often as needed.
* Weekly Guild outings to build experience points, collect items for use by Guild members, to aid the weak and the Good, and to recruit.
*Individual training sessions to build experience points will be arranged as requested. Members requesting training will post their requests in the Guild message board. Trainers will then contact the member by e-mail to arrange mutually satisfactory times.
* Player vs. Player Combat (PvP) training sessions will be held whenever requested by a member, and will be posted by the Lord Captain so that others may participate.
* Special Guild events as appropriate.

3. Background

a. Description of Mystra

Mystra is the goddess of magic, the most powerful deity in the Forgotten Realms. She is the goddess of the spells themselves, the magical energy that makes all spell casting possible. Known also as the Lady of Mysteries, she taught the first spell casters and enabled many of the races to use magic. Any powerful spell caster is said to be "touched by the Goddess." The Servants of Mystery ("SOM") follow the teachings of Mystra, and use their magic to protect, guide and enlighten the good characters of their Realms.

b. Official Colors

SOM's colors are black, dark blue, blue, light blue and white. Each member must wear a customary item of the appropriate color for his or her rank within the Guild in Realms where this is possible.

c. Official Symbol

The official symbol of Mystra is a red mist flowing from the center of a circle of eight stars, with each star having five points

d. Official Salutations

The official blessings of the Guild is "May Mystra guide thy magics."

4. Membership Requirements

a. Alignment and Class

All individuals of good alignment are welcome. Characters of neutral alignment will be considered on a case-by-case basis. Evils need not apply. SOM accepts all classes, but prefers that each member have some spell-casting ability

b. Stand on Player vs. Player Combat (PvP)

Each member is free to decide whether he/she will participate in PvP, but the Guild encourages PvP training for purposes of self-defense and combating evil. More specifically, PvP is approved in the following situations at each member's discretion:
* If he or she has been unjustly attacked.
* To aid in the protection or training of those unschooled in the use of magic.
* If he or she is directly combating the forces of evil at work in the Realm.

5. Admission to the Guild

a. Application

Applications for membership in a Temple of the Servants of Mystery are to be e-mailed to the Guild Minister and Assistant Guild Minister of that Temple. Prospective members are requested to also post a general request for membership on a Guild message board. In addition, prospective members are asked to post on a Guild message board an initial role-playing message that discusses the applicant's character, how he/she learned of the Guild, any adventures with Guild members, and his/her interest in joining the Guild. Any member who has knowledge of the prospective member shall post recommendations and comments.

b. Selection

Approval of new members will be made by the High Lord/Lady, or in his/his absence by the Lord/Lady Regent, upon submission of an approved application by the Temple Minister of the Temple to which the prospective member applies. Decisions are to be made within 1 week. If both are unavailable, approval of new member may be made by any two members of the Templarate of the Temple in question. All new members will be announced on the Guild message board and greeted by e-mail.

c. Guild Oath

Players that are accepted for apprenticeship must take an oath at a Guild meeting, while surrounded by eight burning candles, to perform all the duties of a Guild member. The Council shall establish the wording of the oath as it deems best.

6. Roles, Duties and Promotion of Members

a. Duties of All Members

All members of the Guild are required to do the following:
* know, uphold and further the Official Purposes of the Guild;
* aid fellow Guild members, the weak and the Good in battle;
* promote role-playing among all in their Realm;
* aid fellow Guild members in all of their endeavors in their Realm;
* share weapons, armor, magical items, maps, knowledge and money with fellow Guild members;
* recruit newcomers by providing them with information, spare weapons and armor, and telling them of Mystra and how to contact the Guild;
* attend the Guild meetings; and
* abide by the Guild Charter.


b. Role of Apprentices

Apprentice of Mystery is the ranking for all new members to the Guild. This is an observation period where the members of SOM evaluate the prospective new member. Apprentices must identify their rank according to the customs of their Temple. For example, Britannian Apprentices must wear a white sash, while Athasians must instead add their rank to their profile, the following being an acceptable profile statement: SOM Somebody wears the white sash of an Apprentice of Mystery. The duty of each Apprentice is to become familiar with the ways of their Realm.

c. Role of Disciples

Disciple is the ranking for all members who have served out a sutiable apprenticeship and are worthy of recognition as true followers of Mystra. The special duty of each Disciple is to maximize their abilities in service to the Goddess. Disciples must identify their rank according to the customs of their Temple. For example, Britannian apprentices must wear a light blue sash, while Athasians must replace their Apprentice profile statement with an appropriate statement displaying their new rank.

d. Role of Lords and Ladies

Lords and Ladies of the Guild are responsible for the training and protection of Apprentices and Disciples, and for upholding the beliefs and purposes of the Guild. They must identify their rank according to the customs of their Temple. For example, Britannian Lords and Ladies must wear a medium blue sash as a sign of their loyalty to Mystra and the Guild, while Athasians shall replace their Disciple profile statement with an appropriate statement signifying their new rank.

7. Promotions

a. Promotion to Disciple

Upon serving a minimum of one month from the date of admission, an Apprentice may apply for promotion to Disciple by posting a request on the Guild message board and e-mailing the request to the High Lord/Lady and Lord/Lady Regent. Promotion will be based not only on the member's length of service, but also on whether the Apprentice has participated in the Guild and has generally upheld the beliefs and furthered the purposes of the Guild.

b. Promotion to Lord or Lady

Upon serving a minimum of three months in rank, a Disciple may apply for promotion to Lord or Lady by posting a request on the Guild message board and e-mailing the request to the High Lord/Lady and Lord/Lady Regent. Promotions are based on the player's amount of activity within the Guild, including consideration of the following:
* Does the player post regularly on the Guild message board?
* Does the player participate in guild events?
* Has the player upheld SOM beliefs and purposes?
* Has the player completed any specific quests that have been assigned?
* Has the player acted bravely in battle?
* Has the player shown leadership?
No one item will be decisive; overall participation in the Guild will be the determining factor.

c. Making of Promotions

Promotions will be made by the High Lord/Lady, or in his/her absence by the Lord/Lady Regent. Decisions are to be made within 1 week. If both are unavailable, promotions may be made by any two Guild Council members. Promotions are effective instantly, but each promotion will be honored by ceremony at the next Guild meeting. All promotions will be announced on the Guild message board.

d. Experienced New Members

At the discretion of the High Lord/Lady or Lord/Lady Regent experienced players may be admitted to the Guild at the level appropriate for their experience. Decisions are to be made within 1 week. If both are unavailable, admittance of the player may be made by any two Guild Council members.

e. Ceremonies and Oaths

The Council shall proscribe such ceremonies and oaths as it deems fit for promotions to Disciple, for promotions to Lord/Lady and for other appropriate occasions.

8. The Orders of the Servants of Mystery
a. Purpose

The Orders are the highest honors the Guild bestows upon its members. Each character class, with the exception of thief, has an Order to which accomplished Servants of Mystery may belong. Rank within the Orders is based on achievements in the Realms. The main purpose of the orders is to foster role-playing and allow character development in accordance with published materials regarding the Servants of Mystery. The Orders of the Servants of Mystery are:

Clerics: The Temple of Mystra
Fighters: The Blue Gauntlet
Mages: The Many-Starred Cloak
Paladins: Knights of the Mystic Fire
Rangers: The Order of the Shooting Star

There is also an Order for those who lend artistic talents to the Guild. The Starry Quill is the Order to which these Bards of the Realms belong.

b. Membership and Discipline

Any member who is fully maxxed and interested in Order membership may apply. Applications to the Orders shall be e-mailed to the First Officer of that Order, and, for record keeping, to the Guild Council. The First Officer shall determine admittance to the Orders. Each who is admitted shall change his/her surname to reflect the Order and their own rank within it. The Orders may also have their own color schemes for character icons, although all members must wear the sash of rank bestowed upon them by the Guild to signify their devotion to Our Lady of Mysteries. Members may choose to belong to more than one Order if their classes and talents permit.
If needed, the First Officer may issue such discipline as he/she sees fit, including expulsion from the Order (although not from SOM). Grievances ensuing from disciplinary actions shall be heard by the Guild Council, and their vote shall hold over any actions by Officers of the Orders.

c. Chain of Command

In terms of the chain of command for the Orders, the High Lord/Lady shall serve as Commander in Chief, and each First Officer as the equivalent of an Executive Officer. First Officers are appointed by the High Lord/Lady, and should preferably be experienced, senior members of the appropriate class. The First Officers may appoint assistants as deemed necessary to the functioning of the Order, and shall determine their hierarchy within the Order.

d. Duties

Each member is expected to maintain an active presence in their Realm. This does not mean daily presence, simply a pattern of activity that shows the member is participating in the Guild. In addition, it is the duty of each Order member to role-play in a manner appropriate to the honorable status the Guild has granted them.

e. Status

The Orders are the primary method of advancement beyond the milestone of maximum levels. As such, all Servants of Mystery who have attained maximum level are encouraged to seek membership in the Order or Orders appropriate to their class or talents. Rank within the Orders does not confer authority over those of lower status within the Guild, but it does command respect for the accomplisments made in its attainment.

 f. Rules

The First Officers shall establish such rules for the Orders--including ranks and insignia--as he/she shall deem fit, subject to the approval of the Council. This will include requirements for advancement within the Order, such as specific quests or missions that must be accomplished for a given rank.

9. The Guardians of Mystery

a. Purpose

The Guardians of Mystery constitute the military arm of SOM. In war--which may be declared only by the Council--the Guardians are the Guild's primary forces, and are required to fight for SOM and its honor. (Other members of SOM have the option of fighting or not.) In addition, the Guardians may fight in response to individual challenges posted on the Guild message board or otherwise made. The Guardians are charged, however, to avoid doing or saying anything that plunges the Guild as a whole into war. The Lord Captain shall lead the Guardians. The symbol for the Guardians shall be a flaming screwdriver.

b. Membership and Discipline

Any member who is fully maxxed (dualled and maxxed in both classes) and interested in PvP may apply for membership with the Guardians. Applications to the Guardians shall be e-mailed to the Lord Captain, Lieutenant Captain and, for record keeping, to the Scribe and Assistant Scribe. The Lord Captain or the Lieutenant Captain shall determine admittance to the Guardians. Each who is admitted shall change his/her surname to "Guardian of Mystery." If needed, the Lord Captain may issue such discipline as he/she sees fit, including expulsion from the Guardians (although not from SOM).

c. Chain of Command

In terms of the chain of command for the Guardians, the High Lord/Lady shall serve as Commander in Chief, and the Lord Captain as the equivalent of the Chair of the Joint Chiefs of Staff. The Lieutenant Captain shall assist the Lord Captain.

d. Duties

Each Guardian is expected to practice and become knowledgeable in the arts of PvP. In addition, it is the duty of each Guardian to inform other SOM of the ways of PvP when appropriate.

e. Status

Guardians are those who are particularly interested in PvP. As PvP is not the primary focus of the Guild, Guardians have no greater status or privileges than any other Lord or Lady of the Guild.

f. Rules

The Lord Captain shall establish such rules for the Guardians--including ranks and insignia--as he/she shall deem fit, subject to the approval of the Council.

 a. Regular Meetings

Regular meetings are to be held at least once a month, and may be called by the High Lord/Lady, the Lord/Lady Regent or any two Council members. The High Lord/Lady or the Lord/Lady Regent will post on the Guild message board a notice of the time of the meeting at least 72 hours in advance (except in case of emergency meetings), with seven days' notice preferred. The posting will be accompanied by an agenda for the meeting. Agenda items will be set initially by the person(s) calling the meeting. Subsequent agenda items--excluding the election or removal of Council members--may be added only by the High Lord/Lady or Lord/Lady Regent, although they must be posted at least 24 hours prior to the meeting. In addition, the Guild Scribe will e-mail each member the notice and agenda for each meeting as far in advance as practicable.

b. Emergency Meetings

In the event of an emergency, a Guild meeting may be called by at least three members of the Guild Council (also known as the "High Council"), who will set the agenda, e-mail to each member and post on the Guild message board the notice of meeting and the agenda at least 24 hours prior to the meeting. Council positions may not be filled or confirmed at emergency meetings, nor may Council members be removed at emergency meetings.

c. Conduct of Meetings

Guild Meetings will be conducted by the High Lord/Lady or, in the High Lord/Lady's absence, by first the Lord/Lady Regent, then the Guild Chancellor, then the Guild Scribe, then the Guild Ambassador. Should none of these persons be available, one of the Temple Ministers shall conduct the meeting. The person conducting the Guild meeting will determine who is allowed to speak next. Members may request to speak by typing a "!" or "::raises hand::" or some similar short request. However, members are allowed to express agreement with statement by the briefest of comments, such as "agreed" or "I agree with _______________." For longer statements, or to express disagreement, members must obtain permission to speak. With long messages, a member will signal that he/she is finished by typing "/e" (for "end").

d. Voting

All members--Apprentices, Disciples and Lords/Ladies--may vote at Guild meetings. Voting shall be done by typing "aye" or "nay" (or "yes" or "no") unless other arrangements are announced.

11. The Guild and Advisory Councils

a. Guild Officers and Their Duties

The Guild Council of the Servants of Mystery will consist of a) the High Lord/Lady; b) the Lord/Lady Regent; c) the Temple (or Guild) Ministers of each Temple; d) the Guild Chancellor; e) the Guild Scribe; f) the Guild Ambassador; g) the chosen assistants of each of these major officers; and j) such other positions as the Guild Council or the Guild members choose to elect. In addition to the duties described below, the Guild Council members shall have such duties as specified throughout this Charter.

i. The High Lord/Lady is the highest position in the Guild. His/her duties shall include approving applicants for membership, promoting Apprentices to Disciples, promoting Disciples to Lords and Ladies, calling Guild meetings, setting a standing monthly Guild meeting, posting a notice and agenda for each Guild meeting, conducting Guild meetings, calling and conducting all Council meetings, and posting minutes of Guild meetings. The High Lord/Lady is also the primary representative of the Guild to the staffs of the game worlds in which the Servants of Mystery operate. The High Lord/Lady may delegate this duty to the Temple Minister(s) of the game world(s) as needed to ensure contact between the game operators and the Guild Council.

ii. The Lord/Lady Regent is to assist the High Lord, and to fulfill the High Lord's duties when the High Lord is unavailable or requests help.

g. Temple Officers

i. Each Temple shall have elected administrative officers as described in this section. These officers are: Temple Minister, Temple Scribe, Temple Chancellor, Temple Captain, Master/Mystress of Apprentices, and Temple Ambassador. Additional offices may be created by the Temple as the membership sees fit . New offices are created when approved by a simple majority of the votes cast by Temple members at a regularly scheduled Temple meeting.
ii. Each of these officers may appoint assistants as they see fit, to aid them in the performance of their duties.
iii. The Temple Ministers serve as the chief operating officers of individual Temples of the Servants of Mystery. They are responsible for approval of applications from prospective members and their submission to the Guild Council prior to admission by the High Lord, and for the well-being of their Temples within the Realm in which they exist. Temple Ministers are the highest ranking officers within a Temple of the Servants of Mystery and are second only to the High Lord in rank within the Guild as a whole. While their duties encompass all duties listed herein, they are expected to delegate duties to Temple officers according to the guidelines set forth in this Charter.
iv. The Temple Scribe is responsible for maintaining a current history of SOM in their game world, maintaining maps and help files for members, e-mailing Guild information to prospective members, e-mailing new member welcome packs, maintaining a current Temple roster, maintaining a current roster of Temple allies, e-mailing each member a notice and agenda for each Temple meeting, posting the names of all new Apprentices and all promotions, maintaining minutes of Temple and Council meetings.

v. The Assistant Temple Scribe is to assist the Temple Scribe and to fulfill the Temple cribe's duties when the Temple Scribe is unavailable or requests help.

vi. The Master/Mystress of Apprentices is responsible for the safety and well-being of all members during their time as Apprentices and as Disciples. He/she ensures that Apprentices and Disciples are trained until they achieve high levels of advancement. The Master/Mystress of Apprentices has the duty of requesting Guild members to provide such training.

vii. The Assistant Master/Mystress of Apprentices is to assist the Master/Mystress of Apprentices and to fulfill the duties of the Master/Mystress of Apprentices when the Master/Mystress of Apprentices is unavailable or requests help.

viii. The Temple Chancellor shall:
* sign up leaders to conduct standing outings and other Guild events, including recruiting outings, quests, treasure-collection outings and PvP training;
* publicize Guild outings and events in the SOM message board;
* publicize recruiting events in the Message areas of the game world in which their Temple is located;
* act as backup to the Temple Scribe in sending Guild information to potential recruits;
* encourage members to post on the Guild message board by, among other ways, asking questions to or information from the Guild members as a whole;

ix. The Assistant Temple Chancellor is to assist the Temple Chancellor and to fulfill the Temple Chancellor's duties when the Temple Chancellor is unavailable or requests help.

x. The Temple Captain is responsible for PvP training for their Temple. He/she shall arrange, conduct and post regular PvP training sessions. The Temple Captain is also responsible for gathering--and preserving security concerning--information and manuals on PvP. He/she shall also act as the Sergeant-At-Arms for all Temple meetings, maintaining order and decorum as necessary.

xi. The Temple Lieutenant is to assist the Temple Captain and to fulfill the Temple Captain's duties when the Temple Captain is unavailable or requests help.

xii. The members may elect other officers, who shall also be Council members, at regularly scheduled Temple meetings.

b. The Advisory Council

The Advisory Council shall consist of those individuals chosen by the Guild to fulfill special roleplaying functions. Examples of such functions include Court Astrologer, Court Jester, and other functions of similar roleplaying utility to the Guild. Each Temple shall have its own Advisory Council. These offices carry no vote on the Temple or Guild Councils, rather they are special titles bestowed by the Guild on individuals who carry out important symbolic roleplaying functions for the benefit of the Guild.

c. Hierarchy of Authority

The hierarchy of authority shall be High Lord/Lady, Lord/Lady Regent, Temple Ministers, Guild Chancellor, Guild Scribe, Guild Ambassador, Assistant Guild Chancellor, Assistant Guild Scribe, and Assistant Guild Ambassador.

d. Duties of and Actions by the Guild Council

i. Each officer is required to provide the Scribe with his/her real name, work telephone number, home telephone number, address and a description of appropriate times to call.
ii. Decisions as to alliances and going to war shall be made by a majority vote of the Council.
iii. Decisions regarding discipline shall be made by a majority vote of the Council.
iv. Decisions as to the removal of a Council member may be made by a majority vote of the Council.
v. Amendments of this Charter may be made by a majority vote of the Council, but may be overridden by a majority vote at any regular Guild meeting. In all actions, the Council need not have a formal meeting. Rather action of the Council will be considered taken if a majority of the then-current Council members approves the action via e-mail.

Templarates (Temple Councils)

Each Temple of the Servants of Msytery shall be administered by a Templarate composed of the Temple Minister, the Assistant Temple Minister, the Temple Chancellor, the Temple Captain, the Master/Mystress of Apprentices, the Temple Scribe, and any other functional officers the Temple should decide to elect. The heirarchy of authority and duties of each Temple officer shall be in the order listed. The authority of Temple Council officers holds only over the members of their Temple, not over the Guild as a whole, except for Temple Ministers, who are also members of the Guild Council and who are the equivalent in rank to the Lord/Lady Regent. Nonetheless, each Servant of Mystery is expected to show Temple officers the respect due them as officers of the Servants of Mystery.

12. Elections, Term of Office, Removals and Vacancies

a. Assistant Officers

Each Assistant Officer shall be chosen by the Primary Officer for that department, and shall serve at the Primary Officer's pleasure. The Primary Officers are the High Lord/Lady, Temple Ministers, Master/Mystress of Apprentices, Lord/Lady Captain, Lord/Lady Scribe and Lord/Lady Chancellor.

b. Elections

Elections of Primary Officers may only be held at regular Guild meetings and must be posted and e-mailed as an agenda item no less than 72 hours before the meeting. Elections for all Primary Officers will be held in January of each year. Elections for vacant or temporarily filled Primary Officer positions will be held at the next regularly scheduled Guild meeting. If multiple members are running for a position and if no one candidate receives a majority of the votes then present, a run-off ballot will be held.

c. Term of Office

The term of each Primary Officer will be for a one year, although each will hold office beyond that point until a successor is appointed or elected.

d. Removal

Primary Officers may only be involuntarily removed upon the recommendation of the High Lord/Lady or any two Council members and upon the majority vote of majority of either i) the Council members or ii) the Guild members present at a regularly scheduled meeting. However, any Council decision in this regard may be overridden by a majority of the Guild members present at a regularly scheduled meeting. Notification must be posted and e-mailed to all members and to the Primary Officer to be removed at least 72 hours in advance.

e. Vacancies

Vacancies occur when a Primary Officer resigns or is removed from office. Primary Guild Officer positions may be temporarily filled by appointment by the High Lord/Lady or, in the absence of the High Lord/Lady, by first the Lord/Lady Regent or, in his/her absence, by the Lord/Lady Chancellor or, in his/her absence by a majority of the then-current Guild Council members. Elections for the positions so temporarily filled will then be held as needed but not more than one month from the date of departure of the Primary Officer in question. The same process also applies to Primary Temple Officers, in which case the Temple Minister may temporarily appoint a replacement. Should the Temple Minister be unavailable, the Assistant Temple Minister, the Temple Chancellor, or a majority of the then-current Temple Council members, in that order, may appoint a temporary replacement. Elections for the positions so temporarily filled will then be held as needed but not more than one month from the date of departure of the Primary Officer in question.

13. Discipline
Failure to comply with the Guild Charter or Purposes, failure to maintain an active presence in a Realm in which the Servants of Mystery operate, or violations of the Terms of Service ("TOS") of the Realm in which a character exists will result in a Council meeting to decide the fate of the member responsible. Prior to the meeting the miscreant will be e-mailed a notice by the High Lord/Lady and invited to e-mail a defense in response. Punishment will vary from counseling on appropriate behavior to exile or expulsion from the Guild, depending on the severity of the member's actions or inaction.

Return to Top of Page

14. Revisions to the Charter

Revisions to the Charter may be made by a) the Council or b) a majority of the members present at any regular Guild Meeting, so long as those revisions are part of the agenda. However, any Council decision in this regard may be overridden by a majority of the Guild members present at a regularly scheduled meeting. The Guild Master will notify the staff of each Realm in which the Servants of Mystery operate of all changes to the Charter.
