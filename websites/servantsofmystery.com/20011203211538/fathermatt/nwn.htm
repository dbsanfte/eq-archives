Welcome to my guide to the best sites for the upcoming Bioware version of Neverwinter Nights, scheduled for release in early 2002.  If a slot is blank in the first chart below, either the site at the top of the column does not offer that information or it is not so good, in my humble opinion.  If you have suggestions--or if you find broken links--shoot me an e-mail!  (And if you're looking for my EverQuest site, click here.)  
          Neverwinter Nights (NWN) will be different in that the software manufacturer--Bioware--will NOT be hosting the game worlds.  Bioware will simply sell the software, and various volunteer groups will provide the servers and worlds for game play.  (One advantage is that there will be no monthly charge.)  Different servers will have somewhat different game rules.  Continuity will be provided by two sites that are described in the second chart below:  Neverwinter.net will list the servers and allow players to sort and select them, and Neverwinter Nexus will provide community activities for all players.  And, yes, you can pre-order the software at several sites. 
            Not sure where to begin in understanding the game?  You many want to go to the first chart below and start with Guides & FAQ, then move on to Classes.  To see what the game will look like, visit Images in the second chart.

Other Useful Sites:

BioWare The software manufacturer for Neverwinter Nights.  Offers news, FAQ, boards, artwork, characters, desktop background downloads and links.
Campaign Worlds See the Online Worlds by NWNights.info, the Worlds links by Neverwinter Stratics, Neverwinter Sites' Worlds, World Reviews by NWN Focus, and the links by A Land Far Away (scroll down to "Other NWN Campaign Worlds").  Also see "Servers & Worlds" in the first chart above.
Combat For specific issues regarding combat, see Player Information by NeverWinter Nexus; hit <ctrl>-f, search on "combat" and scroll down from there to see the different headings.  Also see Combat in the first chart above.  
Deities Each world may have its own deities, but for the Forgotten Realms gods and goddesses see an excellent chart in The Daggerford's Campaign's Gods, The Sorcerer's Place Forgotten Realms,  Deities of the Realms by Don Riggs, and Deities by the Kitaran Sea Campaign.  
Images Want to see what the game looks like?  See Planet Neverwinter's screenshots and movies,  Neverwinter Stratics' image gallery (check the "Gallery Areas" list on the right) and movies, Neverwinter Haven's screenshots, the Neverwinter Nexus screenshots (scroll down), and Neverwinter Nights'  Image Gallery.  
Interface Neverwinter Nexus provides a nifty set of topics on the game Interface, including discussions of movement, inventory, party portraits, communications, targeting and more.  
Links A Land Far Away offers nice links.  NWNights.info offers a good links page as well.  Also see Topsites for Neverwinter, which ranks sites by visitors' votes on an ongoing basis.  For other pages of links, see "Links" in the first chart above.  
Neverwinter.net No, it's not open yet, but it looks as if it will be key.  
          "Neverwinter.net is a dedicated matching service that is being integrated directly into the Neverwinter Nights game. It will be a central gathering place for the game’s online community and will allow you chat, plan, and adventure with fellow players, Dungeon Masters, and module creators. As with other matching services of this sort, Neverwinter.net will list of currently active servers and allow you to sort them according to a number of important parameters such as game type and ping. Launching new servers and joining existing ones will be a quick and painless ordeal, even for new users."
          For more info see Bioware's Neverwinter.net FAQ.  
Neverwinter Nexus "The Neverwinter Nexus...is an unofficial, volunteer based, inclusive community for the advancement and encouragement of all aspects of participating in the Neverwinter Nights experience.  ...  [I]t is open to all.  More specifically, it is a collection of activities each focused on different aspects of the NwN experience."   
          For the index of currently active Nexus activities, visit the Index.  The site also offers boards, a calendar, news, FAQ, Domains (worlds) and a Knowledge Base, among other items.  
Neverwinter Nights "The Official Neverwinter Nights website."  Features the following, among other items:  what's new, downloads, links.
Neverwinter Nights Consortium One of the "Campaign Worlds"--and the one I personally am involved with.  
          "The first goal of the Neverwinter Consortium is to create the closest possible facsimile of the original America Online-hosted Neverwinter Nights game, which was closed by AOL at the height of its popularity in 1997."  
         Offers FAQ, maps, a board, news and more.  
Order the Software You can pre-order Bioware's Neverwinter Nights software from Amazon.com, Dragon and EB Games among others.  
Player Information Neverwinter Nexus offers extensive Player Information:  a set of topics concerning player characters, including but not limited to rolling for attributes, points, reputation, leveling, resting, skills, combat and spells.  
Servants of Mystery One of the original (AOL) Neverwinter guilds still in existence today...and my guild.  The famed "fireballing midget" graphic gives some idea of the primitive graphics available at that time for online games.  But fear not:  our esteemed Guild Master Snake is leader of the Neverwinter Nights Consortium described above.  
Site Search NWN Stratics has a search engine for its site in the upper left-hand corner; most sites do not.  
Spells Go to Player Information by NeverWinter Nexus; hit <ctrl>-f, search on "spells" and scroll down from there to see the different headings.  Also see Spells in the first chart above.  
System Req's See Neverwinter Garrison's System Requirements, or Planet Neverwinter's Requirements.
