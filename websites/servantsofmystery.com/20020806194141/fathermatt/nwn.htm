Welcome to my guide to the best sites for the BioWare version of Neverwinter Nights, released in North America on June 19, 2002.  (The Mac version will be out in September.) There is a wonderful trailer created by a Korean guild, Asgard (though it does take time to download); if you're thinking of getting the game, check it out!  For a quick view of  what the game looks like, visit Images in the second chart.  Among other places, you can order the game through a worthy entity, the Neverwinter Consortium.  

          This site consists of the main resource chart, a chart of other useful sites, and finally a chart for builders and dungeon masters.  If a slot is blank in the first chart below, either the site at the top of the column does not offer that information or it is not so good, in my humble opinion. There is information on character creation and hints and tips in the second chart, as well as guides to the Official Campaign.  If you have suggestions--or if you find broken links--shoot me an e-mail!  (If you're looking for my EverQuest site, click here; if you are looking for my Dark Age of Camelot site, click here; and if you're looking for my Ultima Online site, click here.)  

          Neverwinter Nights (NWN) is different in that the software manufacturer--BioWare--is NOT hosting the multiplayer game worlds.  BioWare simply sells the software, and various volunteer groups provide the worlds for multiplayer game play.  (One advantage:  no monthly charge.)  Different servers can have somewhat different game rules.  Not sure where to begin in understanding the game?  You may want to go to the first chart below and start with Guides & FAQ, then move on to Classes.  

This site is best viewed with Internet Explorer and a screen setting of 800 x 600 pixels or higher.  If you still can't get the first chart on one screen, try setting View/Text Size set to "Smallest".
One way to search this site  is to hold down the CTRL key and press the "f" key.

Level Changes Changing levels is useful if you want to play in a group and have similar levels, or if you are a builder and want to test modules.  If you want to raise or lower levels for a character, go into the multiplayer mode and select the server that says "Advanced Training".  Talk to the dragon and you can raise or lower your level to whatever you want.  Be sure to save your character!  Warning:  this is a very popular place, and the lag is bad.  Alternatively, download the The Halls of Advanced Training module.  As always, you can only use such a character on the Local multiplayer servers or solo play.

Progression Tables Planet Neverwinter provides some interesting Progression Tables that summarize:  the effect of  increasing levels on base saves and attacks; the effect of ability levels on bonus spells; the effect of increasing levels on skill maximums, feats and abilities; and the effect of increasing levels on the number of spells casters have available.  
Roleplaying The basic aspect of roleplaying is to determine what sort of personality your character has and then make your conversation match.  Using Middle English is NOT required.  Neverwinter Vault provides a guide on Role-Playing, though much of it is directed at Dungeon Masters. 
      Still, if ye wish to learnst how to speak in the older tongue--and for more detail on roleplaying--head to "What is Roleplaying?" by the Chesapeake Daily News and scrolleth down to the section titled "A Guide to Conversational Britannian".  (Yes, I know this was done for UO but it works well for other games  too.)

Scripting (tutorials, FAQ,  reference  and forums)      Before designing your own script, be sure to check the lists of already created scripts in the Scripts section below--using them can save an immense amount of time.  
     Utilities: 
L BrainFrameNWN "allows you to construct scripts graphically using a flowchart-like display. Actions and decision points are represented as flowchart nodes connected by arrows, and the flowcharts can be nested, making complex behaviors easier to script."
L Neverwinter Rush has a beta version of its Easy Script utility up, with the final version due to be released August 5, 2002.  "Who has time to read tutorials, edit other user made scripts, read how strings work, and what to do with variables? With Easy Script you just select what you what your object to do, and type in the object's name! No scripting knowledge is needed!"  
L Though it's technically a script rather than a utility, Palor's NWN Spawn System lets builders easily spawn encounters and items with many options available.

For Builders."  Also see Gimpy's Guide to Making Quests  by Sir Gimpy.  BioWare provides multiple scripting tutorials.  See GameBanshee's Neverwinter Scripting for background information and a tip or two on learning scripting by Pat Chan of BioWare.  Neverwinter Vault has a multipart scripting tutorial in cartoon format for non-tech types; it also offers a scripting course and links to scripting tutorials.  Lyoness has a Scripting Knowledgebase with tutorials on specific scripting situations.  Yoran provides an entire Neverwinter Nights How-To Scripting site.  Neverwinter Source-rer also offers scripting tutorials.  BioWare has a forum listing Scripting FAQ & Tutorials.
