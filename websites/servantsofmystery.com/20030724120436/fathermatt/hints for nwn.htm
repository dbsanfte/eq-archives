FatherMatt's Hints and Tips

1.  Lighting Loot and Doors.  By holding down the TAB key, all items you can interact with will be light up.  This includes loot chests and doors (not to mention players, NPC's, animal companions etc.)  

2.  Movement and Camera Angles.  Despite what Bim says in the Bioware Prelude, the keys to control movement work in all three modes: 
          w and s for forward and back,
          a and d for turn left and turn right, 
          q and e for step (strafe) left and step (strafe) right.  
In addition to clicking where you want to go (including on doors), you can also move by holding down the left mouse button and moving the cursor to where you want to go. 
     I recommend using the "Chase" mode, since the screen rotates when your character moves.  (Sometimes you may need to take a step to start the camera following you.)  In this mode you can use 
          the GRAY up and down arrows to zoom in and out, 
          the GRAY left and right arrows to rotate the camera, 
          and the PgUp and PgDn keys to change the camera angle.  
You can also rotate the camera by moving your mouse pointer against the edge of the screen on the left or right.  Using the PgUp key to get a direct top-down view is useful for looting if you have an animal companion, familiar or henchman who tends to get in the way. 

3.  Quickbar.   In addition to dragging items from you inventory and memorized-spell list, you can add items to the Quickbar by right-clicking on a free space on the bar and using the radial menu to add an ability or spell.  (Don't forget the Quickbars accessed by holding down the CTRL or SHFT keys.) Also, if you use two weapons or a weapon and a shield, you can drag both onto the same slot in your Quickbar; that way when you click the slot, both are equipped at once.  (This is useful in, for example, switching back and forth between a crossbow and a mace/shield combination.)

4.  Speech.  Hit the "Enter" key to place your cursor in the box when you want to send a message.  Click on "Shout" to change the style of speech--to "Talk" or "Party", for example.  (Note that "Shout" goes module-wide.)  Use the "v" key to pull up lists of expressions that will actually be spoken to other players.  
        In multi-player worlds, to see who is in the game, click the two-sword icon under your portrait.  To send a private message ("tell"), use the form /tell "Lord Nasher" Hello, where you put the recipient's name in the quotes and replace Hello with your message.  To send a tell to someone who has already spoken, click on their character icon in the chat window in the lower left.  If someone is in your party, you can also double-click their portrait in the group to send a tell.  (To add someone to your group, right click on them, then click the South icon on the radial menu.)  

5.  Maps.  If you are playing the Official Campaign module provided by Bioware, you can buy maps for each of the four districts in the Southeast corner of the Peninsula District from Mizzenmast Mercantile.  (You will have to fight your way to get there.)

6.  Windows Key.  You can use your Windows key to reach the Windows desktop while playing.

7.  Light.  Some will consider this cheating, but you can lighten your screen so that torches etc. are not needed.  Go to the Options menu under your character portrait (the button with the two wavy lines), then hit Video Options and move the Gamma Slider to the right.

8.  NPC Companions.  If you have trouble with summoned creatures, animal companions, familiars or henchmen running off and starting battles when you don't want them to, right click on them and select the "guard me" option.  It's not 100% but it helps. 

9.  Chests.  Casters who have offensive spells (even clerics, who receive a few offensive spells) can use them to open chests!  This is useful when the chest will not open or when trying to avoid a trap on the chest.

10.  ESC Key.  Hitting the ESC key will clear any radial menus from the screen.
