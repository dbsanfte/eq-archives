Tips for Dungeon Masters

I won't describe all the DM client features here, though do read about them before starting as a DM.  Planet Neverwinter's DM Client page provides a brief description of the features, and BioWare's Introduction to the Dungeon Master Client provides more in-depth descriptions and is well worth reading.  Also, my comments here are more directed to persistent worlds rather than a particular campaign or quest, though many apply to the latter as well.  

I also have not described instructions for the DM's Helper here (scroll down on that page for features), since this is only available if a builder adds it to the module.  To see if it is available, in the Creator click Item/Custom/Special/Custom1/DM's Helper.  Create the wand and put in it your Quick Bar.  To use it, click on the wand, then click on yourself, players, NPC's, monsters, objects and the ground (to change the weather etc.).  

Lastly, remember that you can go back up a level on the radial menus by clicking the center icon (if there is one), and you can remove a radial menu by hitting the "ESC" key.

0.  Practice.  Note that anyone can practice using the DM client:  you don't have to be given the DM password for an online module.  Start Neverwinter; click on DM Client (instead of Play); click on Multiplayer, then on OK at the login screen.  Now you have two choices.  You can click on New Internet/LAN Game and then load the beginning of a chapter from the Official Campaign.  Alternatively you can click on Load Internet/LAN Game and load either your saved games from the Official Campaign or another module that you have downloaded.  (Neverwinter Vault has a short tutorial on how to add modules to your game so that you can play them; for links of places to download modules, see my Modules section.)  Create your DM character and then play.   

1.  Login Account.  I recommend that you set up a new login account--separate from your player account--on the Player Login screen using the Create New Account button. (You can use the same password for both accounts but you will have to switch the name depending on whether you log in as a player or a DM.)  The reason for a separate name is that when you first enter the game your login name is shown.  While I certainly hope to establish a reputation as a good DM, I do not want players knowing my player identity when I DM.  In other words, I want my DM identity to be completely separate from my player identities.  

2.  General Approach.  As the DM guides suggest, the DM's purpose is not to make matters impossible--or too easy--for the players, but to make them interesting.  Also, while at times it is necessary to talk to the players, I recommend that this be kept toward the minimum needed.  First, you'll find yourself too busy to talk much; second, in my view a DM who talks too much interferes with the players' enjoyment of the game.  Roleplaying as a DM is recommended, however.  I play a goddess as a DM, but you might also cast yourself as a spirit advisor, a ghost, a guide from another plane etc. 

3.  Moving Yourself.  As you know from the feature descriptions, you can jump to whatever building etc. that you want by using the Chooser.  (Click Chooser, select the building, then click the GoTo icon in the Chooser menu.) You can jump to wherever a player is by right-clicking on that player's portrait and then clicking the Northeast icon.  If you pull up the map, you can go to any point by double-clicking there.  If you want to teleport over a wall, right-click the spot you want to jump to then click the Northwest (Jump to Point) icon.  

4.  Moving Others.  If you want to bring ALL players to you, right-click on the main screen and click on the South (6:00) icon.  If you want to move a single player, you'll need to take control of that player with full DM powers (right-click him/her, click on the South (6:00) "Take Control" icon, then on the East (3:00) "Take Control Full Powers" icon); after that, move as you would as a DM.  To release, right-click on the player, then click the South (6:00) icon to unpossess.  (You can create and practice on an NPC.)  

5.  Difficulty Slider.  Remember, the Difficulty Slider affects the whole module, not just those players in your immediate vicinity.  If you are going to increase it, you may want to first make sure that no one is in combat, and then tell everyone that the difficulty is being increased.  (You can roleplay this, of course:  "Beware!  An evil spell has just been cast over the area that grants opponents more strength.")

6.  Healing and Resurrection.  If you introduce a monster that may be too tough--or if you have newbies involved or the players are in an area where they can't rest--you'll probably want to heal.  One way to heal is to right-click on the player portrait, then click on the East  (3:00) icon.  The heal power also has been set up in the Quick Bar for the F4 key.  Of course, dying is part of the game and you often have to let players die, particularly when they do something foolish.  
        To resurrect, all you need to do is use the Heal function on the corpse.  

7.  Adding Creatures.  As you know, you can--and should--add creatures to your DM Quick Bar, as this gives you a way of quickly adding creatures to the area.  Note that there will be few if any custom creatures unless the builder has added them, so start with the standard ones.  Unfortunately, the names of the creatures are not shown on the Quick Bar!  As a result, be sure to keep track of them using the traditional AD&D approach:  pen and paper!  
        Often you'll find that a creature you want to introduce has a level too low or too high for the players you are working with.  To add or subtract levels from a creature, right-click the creature, click the Northwest icon and click the Southwest  icon; then click the Southeast icon to increase its levels or the South icon to decrease them.  An alternative, if you have a creature of too low a level, is to put it on your Quick Bar and create several at once.  One bugbear may not be enough, but three may be; one stirge may be laughable, but 15 may get some attention.  
        You can also talk through a creature by possessing it.  (Right-click the creature, click on the South (6:00) "Take Control" icon, then on the East (3:00) "Take Control Full Powers" icon.)  Of course, roleplay the creature:  it's always fun to have your dragon say something like "You are puny, mortals, but I am hungry and you will tide me over until the next meal!"  (To release, right-click on the creature, then click the South (6:00) icon.)  
        You can give a creature weapons, shields and armor, but the process is cumbersome.  Use the creature and use the mouse pointer to create the item "on" the creature--and the item will wind up in the creature's inventory.  You can then take possession of the creature, open its inventory and equip the item.  Note that items you give a creature will not drop when it dies, but vanish instead.  
        To kill a creature, right-click on it, then click on the Southeast icon.  

8.  Rewards.  If the loot drops do not seem sufficient, you can use the Creator function to create additional items.   (Again, try the standard items first, rather than the custom ones.)  Of course, you can also drag and drop items to your Quick Bar for repeated use.  (Write down what each one is!)  While you can also give gold or items to a creature, unfortunately they will not drop when the creature dies.  One way to deal with this is to keep some gems (or other items) on you Quick Bar, and create them on the spot where a creature dies; if you do this immediately after the creature dies, it will look to the players as if the creature dropped the item.  (Gold does not work well as a reward, since you can only drop it one piece at a time.)  Keep the rewards appropriate, of course, rather than giving too much.   

9.  Spells.  Don't try to cast spells from the radial menu you get by clicking yourself or the main screen:  only some spells are available and targeting is not possible.  Instead, add spells you want to use to your Quick Bar as follows:  right-click on an empty slot in you Quick Bar, click the Northwest icon, then the Northeast icon, then the South icon; click the class you want, the spell level you want and the spell you want--and it will appear on your Quick Bar.  Finally, if you take control of a spell-caster, the spell-caster's spells will automatically appear on your Quick Bar; you can also access the spells through the spells button under the portrait.  

10.  Crashes and Levels.  I have been involved in crashes where players lost levels they had gained since the last game save.  To add levels to a player, right-click the player, click the Northwest icon, click the Southwest icon, then click the Southeast icon.  Of course, this should only be done after crashes.  

11.  PvP.  I realize there's an overpowering urge to intervene, but it's not fair (unless all concerned approve it in advance).  PvP is part of the game, and a DM should not take sides.  The one exception I might make is if high-level evil players are ganging up on newbies.  Other than that, stay out of it.  

12.  Retrieval from Limbo.  To retrieve a creature or player you've placed in limbo, open the Chooser, scroll down to the bottom, click on Limbo, click on Creatures, then click on the individual creature or player.  Click on Jump, then click in the main window where you want the creature or player to materialize.  

13.  Communication.  Note that if you are working with a group, you will not be able to hear anything they say in Party mode.  You likely will want to let them know this.  If you need to talk to an NPC, right-click on the NPC, click the Northwest icon, click the next Northwest icon, then click the top icon.  There is no need to become visible.  

14.  Summary of Useful Commands:

Movement Move to player Right-click player portrait, click Northeast icon
  Move to spot on map Double-click point where you want to go
  Move to a building Click Chooser, select building, click GoTo icon above
  Move across a wall Right-click the target spot, click Northwest (Jump to Point) icon
  Move one player (take control) Right-click player, click South icon, then East icon--and move as a DM would
  Release control of player Right-click player, click South icon
  Move all players to you Right-click main screen, click South icon
Magic Heal/Resurrect Right-click player or portrait, then click East icon
  Add spells to Quick Bar Right-click empty Quick Bar slot, click Northwest icon, then Northeast icon, then South icon; click the class, spell level and spell
Creature Control Add/subtract levels to creature Right-click the creature, click Northwest icon,  click Southwest icon.  Then click either Southeast icon or South icon.
  Kill creature Right-click on creature, click on Southeast icon
  Retrieve from Limbo Click Creator, scroll to Limbo at the bottom, click Creatures, click the individual, click Jump, click in main window.
Keyboard Commands Go back up a level on radial menu Click the center icon (if any)
  Remove radial menu from screen Hit the "ESC" key.  
NPC's Talk to an NPC Right-click NPC, click Northwest icon, click next Northwest icon,  click North (top) icon.

Good luck!
