Thursday, September 05, 2002

WWII Online

It's been scarcely a week since the last map reset (Axis victory) and we're already looking at another. This time, however, it's the Allies that have swept the enemy aside. What does this mean in terms of the future? It means that preconceived notions of how players behave from one engagement to another are best left behind. WWII Online has proven not only to be a great game but also one that brings out the devious tactician in all of us. Although the learning curve has raised a few eyebrows, most of those who play the game regularly are now very familiar with the game and able to spend more time on the strategic elements of war. Battles are now planned out ahead of time by players who sit high atop the chain of command. Air strikes are coordinated with ground forces. Radio communication protocols are established. Objectives are clearly defined and moral at an all-time high on both sides. If you haven't experienced this game or quit shortly after it's rather dismal release, you should really consider checking it out again.

In Greece, play an computer game, go to jail

The Greek government has banned all electronic games across the country, including those that run on home computers, on Game
Boy-style portable consoles, and on mobile phones.

The Greek government introduced the law in an attempt to prevent illegal gambling. According to a report in the Greek newspaper Kathimerini, Greek police will be responsible for catching offenders, who will face fines of 5,000 to 75,000 euros (about $4,980 to $74,650) and imprisonment of one to 12 months. "The blanket ban was decided in February after the government admitted it was incapable of distinguishing innocuous video games from illegal gambling machines," the report said.

Tuesday, September 03, 2002

Housing Phase 3 - Thrusday September 5th

The activation of Housing’s Phase 3 is now scheduled to begin on Thursday, September 5th, 2002 CDT. Notable points regarding the implementation of Phase 3 include:

  • The last day of global auto-refreshing will be Wednesday, September 4th,
    2002 CDT.
  • The first day that all non-Primary Houses will begin needing to be
    manually refreshed by their players is Thursday, September 5th, 2002 CDT.
    Primary Houses will, of course, continue to auto-refresh themselves from now
    on.
  • Houses owned by inactive accounts will no longer be able to be claimed by
    co-owners/friends.
Also, the auto-updating of houses owned by single-house owning accounts to Primary House status that was recently announced has occurred. However, owners of such houses will not be affected by the 7-day house update timer as originally anticipated.
