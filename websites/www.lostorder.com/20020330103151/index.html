Excellent Editorial on the Adrenaline Vault
There is an excellent editorial on gamer rights and selling in game items for real life money posted on the Adrenaline Vault. Take a look at it for yourself here: http://www.avault.com/articles/getarticle.asp?name=mmogsell


The columnist makes a good point, when it comes to in game items, are you selling 1's and 0's or are you selling your time? And if its your time, does the game company really have a right to tell you what you can and can not do with it? I for one am not a big fan of selling in game items for real life money, but he makes an excelent point, that the time you spend in game is yours, and what you do with that time (and if you sell it to someone else) is your buisiness.


But why shouldn’t a person be allowed to sell the products of his or her time? It happens all around us every day. Two elements determine the price of any product: the cost of the materials and the charge for the labor required to create it. When viewed in this fashion, outlawing the sale of non-illegal products of a person's time does not make sense. Mythic wants you to believe they own your character and all of his or her items simply because your avatar exists on their servers. As a player who cherishes free-form gameplay, I’m not keen on the notion that someone is laying legal claim to what I do with my time. I purchased Camelot from Mythic and have put considerable time into my character; if I wish to earn a profit, they should not seek to stop me. Mythic cannot claim ownership of my time.

Read the whole column, it's only three pages long and well worth the time.

News from our EQ Chapter: The Quest
Posted to our CORE list by Corwin:
It was a Quest indeed.

After gathering ourselves in the Ocean of Tears, Mrald, Beltaine, and I contemplated this riddle that we had before us. Beltaine consulted books of arcane lore. I sought the wisdom of the creatures of the wood. Mrald sought... well Mrald was hell bent on the death and destruction of his foes! From our collective scrutiny of maps and lore, we surmised that we should travel to the land of Kunark, to a place called the Field of Bone, and to the dungeon that resides there, Kaesora.

We could not do it alone; it was as simple as that. We needed help in some way, and there was none in the Lost Order who were readily available to embark on this journey with us. It was quite possible our quest would be over before it even began, but then I received word from a friend, Moirra of The Keepers of the Elements, and Mrald, persuasive as he can be at times, convinced her that this quest held merit, and she would join us.

Warrior, Magician, Druid, and Enchanter.

We reached Kunark. From the Dreadlands we met with Moirra, and from there we traveled to the Emerald Jungle, then the Field of Bone, and finally Kaesora at last.

Moirra and I had been to this place before, but Mrald and Beltaine knew not the twists and turns of the place, so we moved cautiously through. Many creatures set upon us, but were easily dispatched. We passed many other adventurers, all in combat, until we reached an ancient Library. It was here we sought forgotten knowledge of what was needed to complete this Quest.

"The first item I seek takes its name from a flat wide river valley made of bone, it will remove the rancor from your veins."

These were the words given to us, puzzle pieces needing to be unraveled. The answer, we hoped, lay within this place.

Upon entering the courtyard, we were ambushed. Skeletal warders and minions of Xalgoz set upon us, determined to keep us from the Library. We battled, we were injured, but we would win this fight. Victory was assured.

Until-

Another party, less competent than ours, ran past, injured and fatigued, fleeing a battle with many of the denizens from within the Library. These creatures, now confronted with the four of us, turned and attacked, adding to our melee.

What force of evil can stand against the might of Akalabeth? None, I say, or very few at least. And even if there were foes that could face us, these were not the ones. Triumphant, if somewhat drained, we entered the Library of Kaesora.

The sounds of the undead filled our ears; we knew this place held many guardians. Spectral Guardians, Tome Keepers, and Tortured Librarians surrounded us. A fierce battle ensued. Many battles followed, and though we did nurse our wounds we managed to stay victorious throughout. Many items did we collect, a magical backpack, several pieces of armor, and also the coveted Spirit Tome, a prize indeed. But nowhere did we find the answer to our riddle, not in any volumes of lost knowledge, nor from any of the inhabitants of Kaesora. Somewhat discouraged, we left the Library, to find answers elsewhere in that underground place.

We searched, we battled, and not an answer was found. I could see in the eyes of my companions the reflection of my own inner thoughts. It was all for nothing. The answer is not here. We have failed. In the end, it was decided to leave that place, and search elsewhere.

Making our way through winding hallways and convoluted corridors, we searched for a way out. Turning a corner, our defenses and spirits lowered, we stumbled upon a nest of Strathbone spiders. But not just any spiders, Frenzied Strathbones! Battle! And what a battle it was... Mrald, blade drawn, fought valiantly, fighting on even though he was surrounded and wounded badly. Beltaine practiced his Art, calling for fire, raining flame down on our foes. Lady Moirra used her beauty and her magic, mesmerizing our attackers. All the while I fought to keep us alive... using the powers of nature to heal our wounds.

It was the hardest battle we had waged. The frenzied spiders were determined that we would not leave. The first spider went down, and largest and most powerful of the nest, and with that death, the fighting spirit of these spiders vanished, and the remaining ones were destroyed.

That's when we knew.

It was there, in our minds, that which was hidden from us. Strathbone spiders. Antivenom. It was so clear now, as if we knew all along, and we had come to this place specifically for this purpose. Strathbone antivenom! That was what we sought, the answer to our riddle, the end of our Quest! We gathered two vials of the serum, and stored them carefully. With our quest completed, there was no need to stay any longer. After a few moments of meditation, I summoned a portal, and we were off.

We said our goodbyes to Moirra, thanking her for her company and aid. All that was left was to journey to Felwithe, the city of the Elves, and find the Quest Giver. His Riddle solved, we would give his Answer.

But that would be another day.

Asheron's Call News: LOA Participates in Another Guilds Meeting

Our guild was invited to attend another guilds meeting on this past Sunday evening. El-Sayed ibn Aly and Edgecrusher attended as (un?)official representitives of LOA. The purprose of our invitation was just to socialize and make friends with other good, mature, like-minded AC players. Their Monarch, Fastallia, is well known and liked on our world of Dereth. Her guild is called the Family of Valor. Their motto is to put others needs above their own so they will spend a lot of time/effort helping others out.

Anyway, in a nutshell, the meeting went well. FOV had about 15 members attend and we all walked out to the "sacred sands" around Al-Arqus and sat around in a circle to discuss things. LOA was introducted, Edgecrusher and I said a few words, and we got to chat with some of their members. FOV now considers LOA official "Family Friends" and will list us as such on their website. Unfortunatly, a little way into the meeting it started to rain and get a little chilly (hey, at night in the desest can be chilly!). Since Edgecrusher was recovering from a cold we deceided it would be best to retire early and get him inside one of Al-Arqus's warm and cozy buildings and perhaps have a spot of warm cider ale.

Please, if you see any of Fastallia's vassals on, say hi from LOA. I expect they will do likewise. In the future perhaps we will build even stronger relations with them and share in questing/hunting and friendship. They are a good group of people.

Ever Quest News: Big Changes Ahead!

There is a big list of things changing with the 3rd Aniversary of the game, and the complete list can be found at: Sony Forums

A brief overview:
o No level restriction(35th before) for meditating without the spellbook in your face now.
o Faster experience up to 10th level along with recovering following deaths having full equipment.
o Increased experience in Dungeons, prior to 30th level.
o Increased level range on Resurrection, Summon Corpse, and Succor/Evac line of spells.
o No more 'hell levels' prior to 46th.
o New classes for Gnomes and Halflings.
o Player-created chat channels and a change to the way people zone.
o And various spell modifications to make them more effective.

Asheron's Call News: Character Purge is comming!

Letter to the Players March 14, 2002
On Wednesday, March 27th, we will be taking the worlds offline from 8 AM to 11 AM PST to do a database purge.
What this means is that any account that has not had an active subscription since March 21, 2001 (either AC Classic or Asheron's Call Dark Majesty), will have their characters wiped from the database.
If you have an account that has not been active (i.e., no subscription on the account) since then, and you would like to preserve your characters, you will need to renew your account on or before 11:59 PM PST on March 26th, 2002.
Any account that has had an active subscription, regardless for how long, since March 21, 2001, will not be wiped.
--The AC Team

If you have had an AC account which has not been active for over a year, and you do not want it wiped, go to the zone and reup your account today. I expect that many active characters are going to have to find new patrons in the very near future. Please contact me if you need help getting back in the allegiance tree after the purge.
