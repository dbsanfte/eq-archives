Friends?
A writing by Santiago, written long, long ago...

The two of them talked until well into the evening while, outside, the storm raged on without abatement. Its howl was heard in the crashing of thunder, and its breath lashed against the shutters of the tavern windows. But try as it might, the storm could not penetrate the tiny haven of warmth and comfort. The sturdy walls of wood and plaster kept the chaos from entering, and storm could only voice its fury and frustration to the indifferent night.

Ye say he was a cruel man? asked Santiago.

Lacey nodded. An yetthar was tenderness, too. Bneath all the rudeness an roughness, thar was a heart. It was his heart that called out to me, an made me look past the rest. She sighed. Were willin to look past so much, fer the sake o love.

Aye, that we are, milady.

Lacey stared quietly at Santiago for a moment, then reached into her boot and drew out a long, ornate dagger. Ye ever heard of a Blood Oath, Santiago?

Santiago frowned and shook his head. Naw, cant say as I have. Some kind o drink, maybe?

She solemnly shook her head. Its an oath that two pirates share with each other. Its a promise, an a vow somethin that no pirate ever breaks.

What do ye promise, milady?

Ye promise never to run away from a battle less yer partner runs away too. It means layin down yer life, if need be. It means A faraway look came into her eyes. It means, no Raider ever dies alone, she murmured.

Santiago had begun to drink from his mug, but he nearly choked on his ale when he saw Lacey draw the blade across her own palm. Milady, what do ye think yer doin?! He grabbed her hand and fished around in his pockets for a handkerchief.

Now ye have to cut yer hand, too, she said. Our mingled blood sets a seal on the promise. I dont leave ye, ye dont leave me. Thats the Oath.

Milady, I aint cuttin me hand open without a darnd good reason! He finally found the bit of cloth, and quickly wrapped it around the wound on her hand.

Laceys eyes began to well with tears, but Santiago was too busy with the cloth to notice. Ye dont want Her voice faltered, and she bit her lip. Ye dont want to take the Oath with me?

It was then that Santiago detected the sadness in her voice, and looked up at her.

Its all right, she said, turning away from him. Ye dont have to. Its just that II thought ye might A tear began to slide down her cheek.

Oh, milady, said Santiago, as gently as he could. He reached over to her, and brushed away her tears as gently as he could with his rough fingers. It aint that at all. He reached for her hand and clasped it between his own. Its just that I dont believe in such an oath.

She looked at him with an expression of bewilderment.

Lets say we was bein chased by the kings men, he said. Lets say it were a fight we couldnt win. If thar was a chance I could hold em off long enough fer ye to get away, then Id take that chance, milady. Id trade in me own freedom so that ye could get away, an maybe come back to rescue me.

I would not leave ye, she said.

Then wed both wind up rottin away in prison, milady. What good would that do?

Wed be together, she said quietly.

Santiago smiled. Ah, yer a stubborn lass, I can see as much as that. Maybe even as much as meself. He gently pushed her dagger away. I wont be slicin up me hands, milady. Not fer some oath I dont believe in. But I will hope that we can be friends, you an me an that if we do wind up goin toe to toe with Death itself, then well both come out still on our feet. What do ye say, milady? Friends?

Lacey stared at her blade for a moment, then at Santiagos outstretched hand. This was not what she had expected. With one breath, he rejected her loyalty and with the next, he asked for her friendship? It was ridiculous; he was ridiculous. She ought to have slashed his hand anyway, and then his throat, just for good measure. But when she looked in his eyes, she saw them staring back at her with undisguised sincerity. There was no malice in those eyes, no cruelty. Only a mirthful sparkle that made her want to smile. And it had been a long time since anything or anyone had given her a reason to smile.

Slowly, she reached down, sheathed the dagger and surrendered her hand to his. Friends, aye, she said with a tentative smile. Its a start, anyway.

Shadowbane Release Date

Since it affects a good portion of the guild whom are valiantly testing Shadowbane, Ubisoft today unveiled the new website for said title and announced a March 25th release date. Here is the press release:
UBI SOFT ANNOUNCES SHADOWBANE SHIP DATE AND LAUNCHES NEW WEB SITES

Chronicle of Strife Unveiled

San Francisco - January 09, 2003 - Ubi Soft Entertainment, one of the top 10 video game publishers in the world, today launched two new web sites for Shadowbane, the highly anticipated massively multi-player online (MMO) game. Shadowbane is being developed by Austin, Texas-based Wolfpack Studios, and offers a ground breaking approach to MMO gaming that allows players to permanently change the history, politics and landscape of the game.

The company has unveiled the Chronicle of Strife, an extensive community site for Shadowbane players at http://shadowbane.ubi.com. Besides daily announcements and updates, the Chronicle of Strife features in-depth details of Shadowbane races, classes and disciplines, interviews with the developers and an encyclopedia containing the lore of Aerynth. The site also features a newspaper-styled College of Heralds in which community members report on the evolving politics, religion, war, commerce and other events occurring in the live game world. Ubi Soft has also re-launched Shadowbane.com with a new look and updated information about the game.

Since its launch last year, the Shadowbane beta test has attracted tens of thousands of players. The company is targeting a March 25th ship date for Shadowbane and plans to continue to expand its beta test over the coming months.

Shadowbane has been called "The Next Great Online RPG" by Computer Gaming World. The game offers online gamers an unparalleled challenge that combines role-playing and strategy. Shadowbane is the first MMO to offer a Dynamic World Model, in which players' actions and decisions permanently change the history, politics and landscape of the game. For the very first time in an MMO, players will be able to determine how the game evolves by building and managing cities, organizing armies, and laying siege to other player-owned kingdoms.
