Another Shadowrun game in the works?

By: Freejack @ 17:18

Shadowrun will reappear next gen as launch title for the next Microsoft system. The co-creator of the Shadowrun universe Jordan Weisman (and founder of FASA) is working closely with Bungie to make the game. The game is a reworked version of the failed "Project Phoenix" as it literally rose from the grave in its re-birthed form of Shadowrun after being canned by MS to focus on Halo 2 development. A skeleton crew second team (comprised mostly of the old Oni team) is doing the work for now. Several FASA employees have visited Bungie's studios recently while work begins on FASA's next title also slated for the next MS console code named Xenon.

Alas, still no PC version. *sigh* Article came from here.

LOA Announces "AVATAR" Guild Portal

By: Brimstone @ 21:28

LOA Announces "AVATAR" Guild Portal:
www.lostorder.com

A few days ago, the Lost Order of Akalabeth moved their homegrown guild port, AVATAR, into production.

AVATAR was designed by Brimstone and programmed by Freejack, both Founders of the LOA. For years, the LOA has struggled to have an all-in-one application to meet the needs of their members and guests. After testing several apps, none of which measured up to the task, Freejack decided to program the brains behind AVATAR.

AVATAR offers news, roster, forums, dynamic page creation and management, shared stories (Tales) and a guild "historical timeline" feature. Full members of LOA have been provided with access to post and utilize all of these features as they wish.

We hope that you can join us in using AVATAR and provide any feedback that you may have. During phase 2 of our development cycle, we hope to implement a downloadable version of AVATAR that other guilds and communities can use, free of charge. Additionally, AVATAR will eventully have the LOA's own application process built into the portal.

To benefit from the future downloadable release of AVATAR, guilds must be using the current full version of vBulletin forums (www.vbulletin.com and www.jelsoft.com). vBulletin is the foundation of our guild portal.

For questions and comments about AVATAR, please visit this forum.

Thank you for your time and thank you for being an important part of our growing community.

FFXI Wins Best Online Game!

By: Shancy @ 11:47

LOS ANGELES, Calif., December 3, 2003 – Square Enix U.S.A., Inc. ("Square Enix U.S.A."), the publisher of Square Enix products in North America, announced that FINAL FANTASY XI won the accolade of "Best Online Game" at Spike TV's first annual Video Game Awards. The winners were announced last night at the awards ceremony held at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas, Nevada.

FINAL FANTASY XI was selected for the interactive entertainment industry's top online game honor by gaming industry experts, the network's editorial board and public opinion via Spike TV's website Spike TV. FINAL FANTASY XI for Windows, released on October 28, 2003, was a finalist in two categories, "Best Online Game" and "Best Fantasy Game." The game is the first online title in the world-renowned FINAL FANTASY series, which to date has sold over 45 million units worldwide. The first cross-platform, cross-continent massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG), FINAL FANTASY XI allows gamers to connect to the same exact world regardless of whether they are playing on a personal computer (PC) or PlayStation®2 computer entertainment system and whether they are playing in North America or Japan. FINAL FANTASY XI for the PlayStation 2 is scheduled for release in March 2004.

The first annual Spike TV "Video Game Awards" (VGAs) premieres with a two-hour fete on Thursday, December 4 (9:00-11:00 PM, ET/PT). Hosted by actor/comedian David Spade celebrates the many accomplishments of the past year in video "gamedom" while also looking to the future of games in 2004 and beyond. Awards in 16 wide-ranging categories were presented during Spike's "VGAs" which was held at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas. For all Spike "VGA" results, log on to Spike TV.
