the more fundamental reason is that role-play is entirely a function of players, not code. Role-play is about people connecting with each other, not with bytes of code or icons. It is about turning what might otherwise be isolated imagination into a shared experience. There are no rules or systems that truly make that more meaningful: it is the personal interaction that makes it significant.

The "perfect" MMORPG?

By: Brimstone @ 09:30

From the mmorpg.com site, Jeremy Starley explains HIS vision of the perfect MMORPG:

What I want to know: where is the "ultimate" MMORPG? I have read about the new games on the horizon, and they seem to all have one thing in common: They focus on a few key features, and leave out brilliant concepts that have already made it in to modern games. That means that in order for the players to get all the features they enjoy in a game, they would have to play more than one MMORPG, if not many MMORPGs. I do not know about you, but I struggle with playing one at a time. What we need is for one bright young developer to sit down and take the best concepts from all games, and put them in to one game. No one will ever agree on what features are the most important in an MMORPG, but if one game had all the features, then it would make sense that that one game would have a great majority of the market.


It is a good read and I agree with everything he has written. I think you will, too.

July 17, 2005

Mythic Puts Imperator on Hold

By: Freejack @ 18:52

From the press release:

"This was the most difficult decision that we have ever had to make," said Mark Jacobs, CEO and president of Mythic Entertainment. "However, our tremendous success with 'Dark Age of Camelot' set the standard for Mythic of releasing nothing less than triple-A games, and 'Imperator' was simply not meeting that standard."


I know how tough a decision this can be for a studio and I will be one of the first in line to say that sometimes, canceling or postponing a title is far better than releasing it before it's ready, but somehow, this sounds like a bullet in the back the back of the head, to me. And not just to Imperator, but possibly to Mythic.

The best Mythic has going for them, as they so triumphantly declare, is a game that is now three or four years old. The other title they're putting resources on is a project they bought up that was also canceled because it "wasn't ready". I really think that, at this stage, if Mythic can't get a new title to market in the next 6 to 12 months, they're going to lose enough subscriptions and mind-share in their target markets that they may not be able to recover.

Thoughts?

July 9, 2005

RYL: Path of the Emperor

By: Elkmountain @ 16:23

Last week, I had been browsing the new games that were coming out at www.gamespot.com and I ran across the RYL: Path of the Emperor

I was intrigued since it listed alot of things like Player vs Player, Guild vs Guild and Questing. Yes, I know it does not sound revolutionary, but Questing was one thing that was missing from Shadowbane and Guild Wars really does not offer Seige Warfare or REAL Guild vs Guild battles.

The monthly fee is $12.95 but free online play is supposed to be offered if you fill out the monthly surveys they send you. I have not gotten a survey yet so I can not totally confirm it.

When i get to Best Buy, I find out an intersting tid-bit. There is a $1,000,000 tournament that starts on 1 July 2005 and ends 25 April 2006. Every month the characters compete for one of 12 slots in the next round. At the end of the month all characters are wiped (not the name) from the Tournament Server. This is to allow a fresh start each month. The winners are based upon Fame points (points gained in killing another character and I think Seige warfare as well)

Races: Human and Ak'kan

Human Classes: Assassin, Enchanter, Priest, Defender, Archer, Sorcerer, Cleric, Warrior

Ak'kan Classes: Attacker, Templar, Shadow Officiator, Rune Officiator, Life Officiator, Gunner

Nationalities: Kartefant, Merkhadian, God's Pirates

Kartefant is Human only
Merkhadian is Ak'kan only
God's Pirates is open to both races

As of right now, i am not sure how the nationalities interact as I am only lvl 14 as of this writing. I am sure it has to do with Seige warfare in some manner.

Other Comments:

Graphics: The graphics seem a bit dated. They seem very similar to Shadowbane graphics. The graphic style may be on purpose as they look more realistic in styling.

Movement: There are 2 types of movement. Keyboard and Mouse. These two movement types are easily switched betweeen by useing the ESC key.

Keyboard: This is more similar to FPS movement than that found in World of Warcraft or Everquest. What I mean is that the mouse controls the camera and direction of movement while W moves forward and S moves backward. Strafing is controlled by A and D. To use the mouse as a pointer you must click and hold the Shift key. This is the mode I use most.

Mouse: This type of movement is the point and click type. Its good for moving through crowds.

Combat: I am not sure how to explain how it works but here it goes. The left mouse button is the attack button. The right click is the button use to activate special skills. To continuously attack click and hold the left mouse button.

Misc: There are lots of things that I still need to learn and I will try to post more information as I find it out. The game seems a bit complex at the beginning since there are lots of different classes and the nation interaction that is to follow.

Feel free to ask me any questions, hopefully I will be able to answer them.

Here is a link to a Screen Shot


UPDATE: There is no crafting in game.

The armor and weapons do get Graded (as in A, B, C, D) there are also variations within each grade which are indicated by the "+" mark. After reaching a certain level you can upgrade and combine armors and weapons to improve them.

July 8, 2005

Hands-On: The Lord of the Rings: Shadows of Angmar

By: Brimstone @ 11:01

Wanted to share another write-up regarding LOTR with everyone. I thought this quote regarding combat ("Conjunctioning") was interesting.

Combat is always an important part of a game, and LOTRO is anything but lacking. Taking a page from a few other titles recently, the game has a feature allowing for combos involving multiple party members. Called "conjunctioning", combo attacks allow for a deeper combat experience; enemies will often require teamwork. Conjunctioning also adds a great deal of damage in the form of a bonus, so it's pretty much never a bad idea.

Turbine has developed a very intuitive display for character and party status. There is no text onscreen in normal play. Instead, players' two health stats are described in a two-tone bar along the bottom of the screen. The first (and largest) portion of the bar displays your character's Endurance, which regenerates automatically. The left-most tip is another shade, however; it shows the character's Health, which must be healed magically or through resting in a camp or town.
