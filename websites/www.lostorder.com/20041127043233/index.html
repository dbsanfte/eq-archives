Atriarch Developer Interview

By: Freejack @ 11:16

FileFront interviews Atriarch's lead designer, Serafina Pechan asking questions about World Fusion (the name of their company), how they have so far survived the six years of development, potential pitfalls, and:

Q: What would you say Atriarch successfully does better than other MMORPGs?

A: Two things... uh... make that three

* Player Construction
* Player Influence
* Epic Alien Culture and Atmosphere

1. Player Construction... We have a unique player-building system that is only found in Atriarch! Players are given building blocks and can piece their own style of building together... not just a pretty structure, but a functional building! For example, if 100 different players build a power plant to supply energy, then there will be 100 different types of power plants, not 100 cookie cutter buildings. The make-up of each power plant can be different as long as the structural and functional requirements are met. My guess is that other games haven't done it yet, because they haven't figured out how to have so much variety without killing bandwidth... hehe... we have ;-)

2. Player Influence... What a player does in the world matters! We believe that when players have the opportunity to make a difference beyond their own individual player-character, then that is when we truly move from being a "game" to being a "world"! When the world responds to your character's actions in the world, that's cool. Knowing your character can make a lasting influence to the culture, story and politics, no matter how small or large, that's compelling! I want to be in a world like that, so I'm making it!

3. Epic Alien Culture and Atmosphere... This ain't no human game! There are a lot of really fun games out there. Yet, few present new storylines and cultural genres. We want to transport people someplace new and stimulating and weird and believable! November 17, 2004

California Guitar Trio pays a visit to LOA Member

By: Brimstone @ 10:20

As reported by Fryn, here.

As most of you know, my husband and former LOA member Andy has brain cancer and has been blind and bedridden for almost a year now, having lost almost every ability he used to have- to run, to teach, to play in his bands... even saying yes and no can be difficult now.

One of Andy's favorite bands is the California Guitar Trio, a band formed out of Robert Fripp's League of Crafty Guitarists. Excellent, excellent musicians Andy got me hooked on before we were married. Well, I keep up with their tour schedule and releases through their website, and a few months ago saw that they were playing in Lexington and then Birmingham with a day in between. I thought maybe, just maybe, one of them could come visit Andy and play a bit if they happened through Louisville or better yet got a gig here.

So I e-mailed them and asked if they would consider it. They wrote back right away to say that they would be glad to play for Andy, and they did- all of them. The whole band came and played a private concert for my husband today. Andy was as thrilled as I have seen him in months. He was smiling, nearly crying - he can do so very little any more, but thanks to these guys, today was different. If Andy can't go to the concert, well by God this time the concert came to Andy. These guys were all so gracious, so humble, so unassuming, I could hardly believe it.

I just got back from the wonderful concert they played to a full house. They dedicated their last song to "their friend Andy, who wasn't able to be here tonight..." I'd held it together for (nearly) the whole show, until then.

There are so many things that make me think the human race is going straight down the toilet- people getting impatient over parking places; cussing out store clerks for missing buttons off their Tommy jackets - stuff that makes you want to just be a hermit. But then there are these guys, who without any convincing, question, or promise of reward, said and honestly WERE glad to come and play, to bring joy to a few people at a time. For us- Andy, me, our son, and for Andy's parents, this is a story that will last our lifetimes. I just had to share.

If anyone wants to know any more about this band, e-mail me and/or visit their site at www.cgtrio.com - I owe them every plug I can give them, for their talent and for their compassion.

Peace,
Jenni aka Fryn

We recently received a response from CGT:

11/17/04 - Thank you for your kind email messages. We had no idea the impact of our visit to Andy's home. When I saw Andy smile while we were playing our guitars at his bedside, I knew we were doing a good thing.

Very best wishes from the California Guitar Trio to Andy, Jenni, their families, friends and all their friends at lostorder.com - Paul Richards

News From Camelot

By: Namoric @ 15:49

As of today's Pendragon patch, the Classic Camelot game engine will no longer be supported for the Pendragon Server. Classic users who wish to play on Pendragon will need to upgrade to the Shrouded Isles engine to continue to update and play on the test server.

Classic Camelot users will still be able to play on live servers using the Classic Camelot game engine until the launch of the Dark Age of Camelot: Catacombs expansion. At launch of the expansion, the Classic Camelot game engine will no longer be supported. Classic Camelot users will not be able to patch or play on any server with the Classic Camelot engine. Classic users who wish to continue to play Dark Age of Camelot must upgrade to the Shrouded Isles engine.

For users who do not wish to get the Shrouded Isles retail expansion at this time, the Shrouded Isles engine (with no Shrouded Isles content) is available for free download from Fileplanet. Please see http://www.camelotherald.com for a link to the full game download. Please note that you will not need the 7-day trial CD key for the game installation since you already have an active Dark Age of Camelot account.

Jump to Lightspeed puts the Star Wars in SWG

By: Ganelon @ 10:21

I've had the game for 2 weeks and I have to say the JtL expansion has recreated the entire gameplay thrust of SWG. It really has exceeded my expectations for what this game could be, and I'll be the first to admit I had burned out of SWG with a bad taste left in my mouth for the experience.

Let me start off by saying 'Forget the Ground Game'. Ground combat is still terrible and is not expected to change until the beginning of next year. SOE has laid out it's plans for essensially remaking the ground combat in SWG, up to and including a full respec of your combat characters once it is released. As far as I'm concerned, unless it is twitch based ala Planetside, the best it will ever be is balanced but uncompelling.

As for JtL... it is entirely twitch based. If you've played Freelancer or XvT (or any of the Xwing games), you get the idea of what the space combat is like. JtL is very much an action space combat sim, and a damn good one at that. All the elements of throttle, shield and hit location are part of this experience. As you advnace in your pilot skills, you gain abilities to enhance your fighter combat and ship management.

Now, i recall reading some impressions of the beta, with comments that it was too hard etc.. etc... I've also read some disgruntled comments that it is 'more of the same grind'.

First, in response to the accusation that it is too 'hard', my only comment is 'RTFM'. Really. I've had NO problems getting thru my newbie mission or my following teir 1 and 2 missions. Does that mean I don't die? Hell no. If I bite off more than I can chew, then try and run with 5 fighters tearing up my 6, then I'm dead meat. No big deal, cuz I respwn at the space station and can immediatly repair the damage. Combat can be challenging or a cake walk... it depends on how ballsy you want to play.

Second, this game is NOTHING like the SWG grind. Let's face it... advancement in any game is going to require doing stuff over and over until you get your next 'bump' in level/skill/power. Call that a grind, if you will. What makes or breaks that experience is the quality of gameplay for that experience, and JtL is a blast. The missions are right out of Xwing v Tie fighter. Each mission is multipart and is as diverse as the Xwing series... included are rescue, escort, patrol and kill missions(and a few others)... each following a story arc much in the same way as CoH missions do.

And then there is ship design. Ah... this is where JtL shines well above any previous space combat sim. The level in which you can tweak your ship is excellent. When you buy a ship chassis (from a player Shipwright), you have an empty shell that must be filled with ship componants. Each chassis can only hold so much mass, and each comonant uses up x mass. Componant types are:

Reactors: These detirmine your total energy output. Other componants drain on that output. The better the reactor, the more powerful the componants.

Engines: A good engine will provide both speed and manuverability. A great engine can turn a slow and ponderous heavy fighter into a fast killing juggernaught.

Shields: Front and back energy protection. Shields regenerate back over time. Good shields regen faster and provide more protection.

Armor: Armor is the hull protection under the shields. Once that goes, it's your componants that are going to take damage. Good armor can take more damage. Always put your best armor on your 6.

Weapons: All kinds of different weapons. Launchers, laser, disrupters and Ion cannons. Are you wanting to take out shield fast or go for maximum damage? Each has advantages and disavantages. The better the weapon, the faster the kill.

Capaciters: These govern the recharge rate and stregth of your energy weapons. The better the cap, the faster you recharge after fulling unloading on your target.

Boosters: Need to get out of an area quick? Boosters can help you get out of a bad fight. Boosters are your friend.

Countermessures: Explosive chaff and holo copies can confuse incoming missles. Always a good thing to have.

Droids: flight computers and droids can also be added to enhance your effectiveness. They take over shield management and can increase you componant efficiency dramatically.


Each of these componants also have ratings for hitpoints and natural armor, and all the attributes for each componant can be improved based on the skill of the shipwright who crafted them.

You may also loot componants from your kills and swap them out on your ships. A skilled shipwright can even improve these looted componants, which are pretty useful to begin with.

You can also customize the textures and colors. This, with the ability to customize the ships abilities, focus and name, personalize the ship to the pilot's style of play.

So, in summary... SWG has become a viable game with the addition of the Space game. SWG has great roleplay and community building tools. And though the crafting system is a serious grind, it is also very rewarding and is fundamental to the player economy. With the JtL expansion, the combat is exciting and very true to the Star Wars expereince that we all wanted over a year ago when the ground game was released. For players that are looking for both a ground combat and space combat game, I'd wait and see what will happen with the ground combat revamp.

October 28, 2004

City of Villians Testing Plans

By: Freejack @ 17:13

NCSoft has released the following information regarding their testing plans for City of Villians, the much anticipated expansion to City of Heroes:

AUSTIN, Texas, Oct. 28, 2004 - NCsoft® Corporation (KSE: 036570.KS), the world's leading developer and publisher of online computer games today announced preliminary beta testing plans for the highly anticipated City of Villains massively multiplayer online computer game. The plan will reward long time players of City of Heroes with beta testing slots for City of Villains, slated for release next year.

Created by Cryptic Studios, City of Villains is the sequel to the hugely successful and critically acclaimed City of Heroes, which launched in April of this year.

The program will give priority to those who have been subscribed to City of Heroes the longest. A percentage of slots will be reserved for City of Heroes' veterans, players who have subscribed to City of Heroes for at least 12 months. Those players will automatically earn a slot as a Villains beta tester. Players with at least six months of time in City of Heroes will receive the next level of priority consideration for the beta testing program. Higher character level is not necessarily an advantage in the selection process.

"We are announcing the plans today due to the overwhelming amount of requests we've received from our subscribers," said Jack Emmert, lead designer for City of Heroes. "It makes sense for us to offer this incentive to our most dedicated players. Who else would be more capable of assisting us in the beta test than the folks most familiar with the way City of Heroes works?"

Emmert added, "We'll be announcing more details at a later date, but the bottom line is that the longer you've subscribed to the game, the better your chances will be of getting a first-look at City of Villains."

City of Villains will provide players with a chance to play the "bad guy" in the comic-book inspired world created by Cryptic Studios. Take on the role of super-powered villains and create your own character using thousands of power and costume options. Then battle heroes and become a true menace to society while plotting your way to world domination.
