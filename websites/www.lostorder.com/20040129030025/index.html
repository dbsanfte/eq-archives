January 18, 2004

Withered Aegis Attacks!!!

By: Gileean Crystalthorn @ 18:33

For the first time the inhabitants of Istaria, on any server, living in the Shard of Dawn beared witness to what we had long heard of from Artifact Entertainment, creators of Horizons, and their AI system. Many of us had longed to see it in action.

On Friday evening we learned all to well what it was like. The scary thing is...it was just a small taste of whats to come.

Last night while many of the inhabitants of the lands of Istaria upon a shard of "Dawn" milled about making armors, spells, weapons, building home or whatever the case may have been..or perhaps out exploring the wilds...a very real threat emerged.

Amidst the winds came a cry from the scouts out in the expanse. The enemy marches upon Mahagra!!! Within a matter of moments LOA and Obsidian order rallied to the cry to defend our very own city for the first time, but we were not alone. Many others from the lands came to our aid and once the army of the Withered Aegis marched into town, they quickly jumped into battle. The fight was fast and furious with few casualties but somehow we emerged victorious, defending our city.
After assessing the toll of the battle and surveying what damages there were, a small band of defenders stood guard while sending out more scouts to look for possible other armies. Lo and behold reports began trickling in about a large army marching towards Kirasanct and off we went to protect the city of Fiends. Once arriving we met the hoards in a fierce battle choaked with undead, Golems, and creatures that defied explanation. We, the Gifted ones, were an army unto ourselves roughly 60 strong at the time. We nearly lost the battle several times. Often sending runners to come back with reinforcements. In the end we once again prevailed....barely. But before we could even breathe a sigh of relief more reports came in of other armies marching upon the Dragon city of Dralk and the Satyr mines near Kirasanct.

We knew we would have to split our forces and pray for more reinforcements on both fronts; sadly they did not come. We did manage to secure the Satyr mines but that security was short lived when we were ambushed from above and flanked...soon the Withered Aegis had cut us off from the only way out and systematically slaughterd the entire army we had massed. Our corpses littered the frozen lake outside the mines..not a soul survived.

Meanwhile, the army we sent to Dralk was barely hanging on, not winning nor loosing. Soon we had come to the realization we had to pull back from Dralk and return to the Satyr mines. The Mines were the most critical point of protection. Why ye ask? We, the inhabitants, have learned of the plight of the lost race of the Satyr and work to free them from their subjugation to the Withered Aegis... The mines are the rumored prisons of the race. The goal being that we must rebuild the mine shafts to enter into their depths.

After nearly 4 hours of this war we learned once again that the Withered Aegis again marched towards Mahagra. This time they rallied thier own troops en masse picking up any creature of the land to join their intent to destroy our beloved city. Sadly we did not have the forces to withstand the onslaught that marched toward us. We stood our ground none the less and prayed to what ever gods we all worshipped. By some miracle a small group learned of our stand and came to our aid... We nearly lost the battle with our very own Zoo and one cleric left standing in the end.

We had hoped and prayed this would be the end, unfortunatly we remembered the mines and did our best to rally the troops to take them back. Eventually we had the force and marched towards the mines to eradicate the WA from the area. When we arrived we were happy to see another army of the Gifted had jumped to the cause and was near to finishing them off.

In the end many went to thier slumbers for the eve, but I myself stayed and roamed listening for word of any other attacks... My patience was rewarded to learn the WA once again marched on to Dralk and made haste to the home of the Dragons.

Eight hours later I went to my slumber with the War still raging against Dralk... A Mexican Standoff is what it seemed when I departed. I can only hope we, the Gifted Ones, had not falterred and lost the city of Dralk.

January 17, 2004

IGDA to Meet at the Offices of Big Huge Games

By: Freejack @ 18:35

This news item is primarily directed toward those living in the Maryland/Virginia/DC and possibly Delaware/New Jersey area. The local IGDA (International Game Developer Association) chapter is going to be meeting at the officies of Big Huge Games, who are the makers of the much acclaimed game: Rise of Nations. It looks like the Jersey chapter will also be meeting there, so it'll be an interesting little inter-chapter...thing.

The Big Huge office is in Timonium Maryland, which is basically Baltimore, I think. It'll be from 14:00 - 17:00 on Saturday, January 24th. Don't be confused by the name of the organization, you don't have to be in the games industry, in fact, most members aren't. Usually meetings are focused on some particular topic, but often we end up talking about game design or other development issues, mainly from the perspective of people who haven't really done this stuff before. In the past we've had people from Bethesda Softworks and Mythic at the meetings and things are generally just real friendly.

If you might be interested in going, please leave a comment here. I think it would be nice to go with or meet some LOA (or even friends of the guild) there and maybe get some dinner afterward in the area. Please let me know if you are interested.

You can find more information about the chapter and the meetings at the IGDA DC and Vicinity page.

January 2, 2004

Media Strikes Again

By: Freejack @ 14:30

I found reference to this article over at Penny Arcade. The article from the New York Post is trying to explain why Take-Two's stock is so terrible. It does this by talking in some detail about GTA: Vice City. After describing some of the actions a player can take in the game, the author goes on to describe how he believes these actions compare to real life stories:

"This is 10,000 times worse than the worst thing anybody thinks Michael Jackson ever did to a little boy - or than any lie the feds think Martha Stewart ever told them, or any line in any song that Bruce Springsteen ever sang that rankled a cop in the Meadowlands."

The author also brings up the case of the two teenagers in Tennessee that killed a passing motorist and game "GTA" as the motivation. All I can think is that some people have a really poor grasp on reality. I won't deny that GTA is an excessively violent game, but it's also a GAME. In the game you deal cocaine, you steal cars, you kill people and do unspeakables with prostitutes... but it's a game. The action in the game does not translate to direct action on people in the real world. When I kill someone in the game, someone in the real world is not dying as a direct result of my actions.

By comparison, if/when Jackson molested a child, a child was actually molested. In the real world. The things Martha Stewert did actually happened and people were hurt (financially) because of it. Springsteen actually said those things. See where I'm going with this? Some things happen in the real world... in reality. Some things happen in virtual space. Fiction. Game. There is a difference.

I feel like I'm explaining simple addition to rocket scientists and everyone just looks at me with a "You have GOT to be kidding me!" expression. Yes, it really is as obvious as it seems. Yet, the media can't comprehend the painfully obvious and persists in demonizing a form of entertainment that really is innocent. Someone who has a poor grip on the distinction between reality and a game could be mislead just as easily by watching Friends or Sex and the City as they could GTA.

Reality. Get with it or get out. PLEASE.
