May 21, 2005

Richard Garriott, still making excellent games...

By: Brimstone @ 22:05

LOA owes a lot to Richard Garriott and his games. We were inspired by him, created our guild name to honor him and have enjoyed the games he created immensely over the years. From the early Ultima series, to Ultima Online, then City of Heroes and now, Guild Wars.

In 2001, I was at the E3 expo in Los Angeles as a game reporter working for Ianstorm.com. This opportunity included a press conference that we (Ianstorm) helped arrange and manage for the Destination Games (Richard) and NCSoft partnership announcement. It was incredible. We hung out with the Garriott brothers, got autographs, talked a little about LOA (I couldn't help myself) and received from free games and gear. Amazing.

Anyway, I remember being confused and unsure of his decision to partner with NCSoft, a company I *never* heard of. It seemed odd... it seemed desperate. Well, after playing City of Heroes (and loving it), along with beta testing Guild Wars (a very good game), then reading the following press release, he has again confirmed his brilliance in my opinion.

Anyway, I just wanted to bring the press release to the attention of everyone who visits our guild site. Enjoy!

May 17, 2005

Let the Wars Begin! PS3 Introduced

By: LostintheWood @ 08:22

Sony Unveils New PlayStation 3 Consoles
Sony officials said the PS3's new processor technology called Cell will offer high performance for movie-like realism in games, high-definition movies and other features.

"The PS3 is truly is a system to be placed in the center of the living room," said Ken Kutaragi, creator of the original PlayStation console as well as the more recent PlayStation Portable handheld.

Prices or a specific lineup of games that will be available were not were not provided, though Sony showed off numerous demonstrations of games in development.

The PS3 will use the Blu-ray disc format, capable of holding 50 gigabytes of information, which is about six times the capacity of existing DVDs, as well as slots for memory cards, a detachable hard drive and Bluetooth wireless for up to seven wireless controllers.
