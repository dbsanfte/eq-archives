WEEKLY UPDATE: 12/27/00 - Site rearrangement

Nothing pissed me off today except my own website. Those of you who have been with me since the Geoshitties site know that the "design" hasn't changed much since inception. I had a few things in mind for this website - text only, and a place to rant. Colors were chosen on the highly scientific principle of "ooh pretty." The text-only thing came out of my desire to make the site load fast, that and when I started I didn't have the faintest idea how to upload my own graphics. For that matter, I didn't have the faintest idea of what HTML commands existed. I still don't, really. Y'all should see my desk. I am the Queen of Post It Notes, with little square reminders of how to make the copyright symbol, how to change the pixel size, and so on.

But anyway. I also figured, when I started, that I'd do maybe 150 hits a week, and then fade into obscurity around about November. I thought I'd run out of stuff to bitch about around October, hence the obscurity by November. When my roommate set me up this fancy site on his domain towards the end of September, I told him to put in a five digit hit counter. My old counter clicked over each and every time the main page was loaded (including when someone hit the "back" button). This counter registers one hit per user per day, assuming the user leaves his browser open between views. I assumed my hits would drop drastically - a six digit counter would just depress me.

So, here I am, nearly January, over the 100K mark, and the list of rants was getting so long as to be unwieldy. Hence the archived rants, still available for your consumption but not in the great big list anymore. And, um, gosh, /em blush, thanks for reading.

WEEKLY UPDATE: 12/20/00 - The money of it all

Got an interesting letter from a reader, who went through the math of running a game company, and drew the following conclusion. Anje wrote:

"The reason Sony formed SOE and brought Verant under full corporate control (think they could have if Verant was REALLY making money?) is because the Strategic Planners at Sony have decided that MMOG is a major, if not THE major, form of entertainment 10 years from now. If there is nothing else the Japanese are good at, it's the willingness to sacrifice near term revenue for long term success. Some would replace "success" with "domination". Just ask Detroit. Whatever is good about Detroit's automobiles today is the direct result of the Japanese auto industry's crushing domination of the world marketplace for 2 decades.

"In short, Sony likes Verant for the pedigree value it will have a decade from now. And all those other companies wanting a slice of the pie? Well, there's lots of money to be made for a few new players, but when AO, DAoC, SB, Sovereign, and Star Wars all join the list of MMOG choices within a year or two, there just won't be enough money to go around."

An interesting point I tend to agree with. How many of us are willing to send ten bucks a month to more than one game? How many of us are willing to pay more than that price benchmark? Something to ponder anyway.

Alert readers also notified me of a new slap in the face in Velious. There's a apparently a really neato ring you can quest for. The first step to making this thing is to sew together pelts and make a no drop blanket. The third step involves smithing a no drop dagger. You see the no drop? Instead of setting up a quest where all the tailors and smiths could finally feel useful, Verant created a situation where EVERYONE needs to be a smith or a tailor. No word on whether or not the quest can be chained like the Jboots quest (one person turns in the ring, and the person who wants the boots turns in the rest). Good planning! Thanks! Mutter.

WEEKLY UPDATE: 12/6/00 - Velious

So, Velious was released. I got a bird's eye view, no pun intended, when my two roommates loaded it up to play at the South Pole of our favorite virtual world. No maps, no cheaters, no quest spoilers, a brand new continent. They ran their little ogres and trolls over this brave new world, and it was so damn pretty. This isn't a review, so I'm not going into details, but I did notice one thing that was so damn funny, I had to share. At about 8 PM EST, the guys charged over a bridge shaped like a dragon skeleton (can I say wow here? WOW it was cool looking) and into a new zone. Right behind them was a mage, level 57. The first place the boys arrived at was a little orc camp. The 57 mage stopped there to kill an orc, and the guys ran on. It was a strike force, just checking out the scenery, whacking a monster, moving on to see more. Seven hours later, the guys were charging back to the zone with the druid ring, and passed the little orc hut again. The mage was STILL THERE. A brand new world to explore, quests to discover, and this dude picked the first camp he came to and didn't budge. Join me, brethren, in a big shout out - LAMER!!! Your mind is its own prison, dumbass.

Sure, on first glance the expansion has issues - your faction gets better and worse with the stuff you kill, but it doesn't tell you WHAT faction gets better and worse. A number of folks are still crashing to their desktops when they try to gate to the new world (gate, the self-only spell, not the teleport). But for crying out loud, last night was a blast in the Qrack. People helping each other, giving advice, discovering traps and quests, making random pickup groups strictly to explore… so why were people playing camp the safe spot in a game that wasn't yet twelve hours old? Mutter.
