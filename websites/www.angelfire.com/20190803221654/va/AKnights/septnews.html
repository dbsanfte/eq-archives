A story about the Blackburrow event

Gnolls, Gnolls and more Gnolls!
A story relating the actions of our Knights in Blackburrow

The evening started like an ordinary evening in the wonderful world of Norrath. The stars were shining and a mild wind was ruffling my hair. A good night for hunting it seemed.

After taking a deep breath and relaxing a bit, I heard some of our brothers and sisters talking through our mind link. The discussion seemed mostly to center around a named Dungeon called Blackburrow, home of the gnolls. Well did I remember that dreadful place from my younger days for I had hunted there in the past and still had occasional nightmares about it! After listening for a while to the mind link discussion, I understood that my evening plans had suddenly became clear. People in Blackburrow were in need of us Arcane Knights. Thus did I start on my journey to the Qeynos region.

While I was making my journey over the rolling plains of Karanas, the knights party was slowly getting organized. Our sister Crystala went to hunt some bats to get their wings in order to provide us with the required components for making the mighty magic of levitation. Other knights also started making their way to Blackburrow, coming from all corners of the world. On my way to that much awaited gathering, I stopped at Qeynos to get some extra food and some more spell components. By that time some of our brothers and sisters had already reached the gathering place, the entrance of the Blackburrow dungeon.

As soon as I entered Blackburrow, I was stricken speechless by one of the most impressive sight: 10 of our brethren were gathered in the Dungeon. Quickly it was decided the best way to help the good people of Norrath would be to watch the bridge area. Some of our brothers and sisters were thus dispatched to that location while I stayed near the entrance to help people and to watch for any groups of gnolls that get passed our brothers attention at the bridge. Let me tell you how proud I was of our knights since my post was more or less boring as almost no gnolls ever made it alive to the entrance. Some of our brothers magicians also provided free summoned items to all in need. Seeing as the bridge area was under our control, some brothers went patrolling deeper in the dungeon to see if any needed help. People could now safely hunt the dungeon knowing that we were there if they were in need. Of course, as we Arcane Knights have knowledge of magic, that part of our heritage was put to good use. People came to see us so we could provide them with healing and empower them with our mighty character enhancing magics.

All in all it was a glorious evening for us Arcane Knights. People enjoyed our presence and some even inquired as to our guild purpose looking for apprenticeship. The tales of courage and honor of that night are too numerous to relate here in this letter. Since our mind link cannot let us share more than words I could not see yet through our brothers eyes to witness all the mighty deeds of that guild event. Maybe we should try to search for some mighty magic that would let us all share our actions by seeing through each other eyes. I will look into this. I cannot remember all the names of our brothers and sisters that were present that night, but I must thank them all for participating. I must also make special mention of our brother Khala efforts to join us; efforts that ended in an untimely death I am sorry to say. The gods have been clement and have let him reincarnate so he could continue to better the people of Norrath lives and help us accumulate more arcane knowledge. I am very proud of you and to be part of such a noble order. Hail the Arcane Knights!

Brother Balduk SwiftMind.,

Raid on Kerra Isle

To further illustrate how much fun staying around the same area could be, I would like to share about and adventure that Apolyion, Charisa, and I had last night.

That Darn Cat
By: Serro Deimos

Dawn had fallen upon Toxxulia forest. The sounds of the forest filled the crisp air of the woods, as life began to return to the forest after the cold night. Three Arcane Knights sat at the dock on the western shore of the forest. They had just killed the one that the Gatecallers call "the heretic", and were discussing the next adventure they should embark on. They all remembered and discussed their days on a small island, located a few minutes off the shore where they sat. It was called Kerra, and was home to many cat people. The three knights decided to leave for the island immediately, and take revenge on the cats for the way that had been treated by them in their youth. I, Serro, was one of the knights.

When we finally got there, we found that nothing had changed, and everything appeared as it did so long ago. It was very quite and little was heard accept for the pounding of waves upon the shore, the light breeze that swept through the small village, and the meows of hundreds of cats. As we walked in we notice cats on all sides of us, who all glared at us, as if to dare us to go any further. In our younger days, these cats would have jumped on us, but now they knew that such a decision would be a very unhealthy one. We continued across the brown bridge and continued our journey around Kerra. After a short distance, the trail we were on suddenly opened up. To our left, we could see a small, run down dock. To the right, was a building, that's use was unknown. But there, straight ahead, as far as the eye could see, was an enormous army of cats. We slowly walked through the crowd. The hatred that they had for us was apparent in their eyes, but none dared to make a move. Our eyes moved back in forth quickly searching for the slightest hint of movement. Then it happened. I do not know who attacked first. It may have been the cats, it may have been us, but that spark triggered the entire army to converge on us. At first, we felt that the battle would be hopeless, but soon found out that we were stronger than we were in the days of our youth, and the big, bad cats of Kerra had become kittens. With pets summoned and spells blazing, we began to terminate every cat in site. The death squeals of cats echoed across the island, as Apolyion said, "I have been out of mana for the last six kills!" After several minutes of utter chaos, and may furballs, things began to slow down. Needless to say, there were dead cats everywhere. From shore to shore of Kerra, the island was littered with the remains of cats who had fell prey to a very dangerous party of Arcane Knights.

There was one casually suffered when Apolyion's pet got a little out our hand and decided to make a train, which wound up running over top of me. Fortunately I was bound to the island. It was indeed a VERY good time, and we would like to take the guild there sometime to reek even more havoc than we did last night. Below are some screenshots I took of the "Kitty Killing". =)
