Merry Meet Everyone! and Hello OHH im feeling halloweeny this month, no candy to give out unless your on the Legends server but anyhow, I would like to welcome you to issue 11 of the enchanter letter. Like all the letters before, I would like to remind you, it is most effective as a focal point to post your own ideas and discuss them, but anyhow onto the trick or treatin!


With our Eyes looking to the future and our two promised expansions to come (via eqlive interview with Alan Vancouvering) we wonder what to expect. As usual I expect to see something new that will add to the power or prestige of classes that will be inaccesable w/o said expansion (pop and levels, ldon and augments, ykesha and spells, etc).

Levels and AA's -
Theres been alot of buzz about adding more levels, and your usual lashback about being put to the hamster wheel again just to stay viable, I personally enjoy leveling and gaining access to new abilities (im also more inclined to 10 more levels vs 5) Then again I have a suprise further down in the letter that I promised to talk about in serverwide channels. More AAs are always an option, as this allows us to further advance our characters but does limit that amount of growth. I expect a combination of the two.

New Zones -
Theres ALOT of creative talent chained up at SOE just gnawing at their bits trying to create. I suspect that one of the two promised expansions will have a large story arc and greatly expand the EQ world, perhaps 20-40 new zones (of the non instanced type). Be it Luggalds and their continent across the oceans or the uncovering of the Greater Underdark with more dark elves and accessing brells domain and the darkness beyond odus, who knows, im sure not privy to this... yet <Muhahaha>

New Zones are good, just a few key things have to be remembered:

1) ZEM - it must be worthwhile xp wise to go there, but not so much so that all old zones become pointless

2) Treasure - people want plat, they want items for themselves to upgrade, and they want stuff for their alts

3) Access - People really are lazy and spoiled with PoK, and unwilling to go through too many zones to access this new content

4) ***Content - This one is my personal fav, but not really as important to power gamers, but each zone should have a unique flavor to it... My first time ever in griegs end, It was late at night, I had the lights out, I had my speakers turned all the way up... and it was CREEPY! You could hear that girl whispering, the light flashes, etc...

Instanced Zones -
LDON has created a great area for people to learn group skills while at the same time xping, advancing their character, and lining their bank accounts (all the while working towards nicer gear). This is great, and I expect to see new themes in the works, but perhaps something other then a dungeon in the strictest sense of tunnels and underground.

I think I was on the rogue webby where someone spoke about this but we shall combine it and talk about..... <dum dum de dummmm!>

Multistep Instancing -
Currently we are only scratching on the most basic functions of the ldon engine. Imagine a set of "Campaign recruiters" each with their own story arc. Each arc would consist of 6 - 10 LDON type adventures (approx 1-2 hours to complete each one) and a player could only have 2 active story arcs at once.

Ex. Blah Blah soandso has broken into the family crypt and we need you to investigate

adventure 1: go down into a instanced Mistmoore dungeon and slay some baddies, only to have a small cut scene where mysterious dude escapes but his buddy leaves a valuable clue you return to access...

adventure 2: campaign recruiter needs you to visit soandso sage to translate this valuable clue, and as you enter his tower, your group is AMBUSHED! upon victory you move on to adv 3...

adventure 10: After discovering that the sage you have been working for is a ebil demon worshipping a fire demon, you travel to his lair to go defeat him, get phat loots, and a really nifty augment or something for each of you.

The individual story arcs could be randomized ie, it slaps together scenes 1, 2, 4, 16 with middle scenes 1 and 6, with final scenes 2 and 10, whips out some names and you have instant campaign with a real storyline. It may take a week or two for a casual group to complete a story arc, but it gives them something to do and is great for people who like to adventure together.

Character Customization - (ELITE CLASSES)

What makes enchanter_01 different from enchanter_02... well you have gear, and dyed armor, different faces, and races, and hmmn... maybe specialization...

As long as were adding more levels, why not make it count (note this is somewhat similar to advancement trees in both eq2 and swg). Add 5 more levels to each base class that would cover the basic abilities of each class. Then setup a series of quests to allow access to the ELITE classes. In the case of the enchanter...

ex1.
Mezmere: gains an innate resist adjustment to all detrimental CC spells (root, mez, stuns), a few AAs that concentrate on crowd control, and say the crem de la crem of a mana balance AA

or our chanter could become

Artisian: gains a base percent increase to all crafting skills (+5 perc per level for success), gains access to tinkering and alchemy, summoned animations gain power significantly, and access to new recipe combines that no one else can do

Slap in a few abilities and enhancements during the 5 hard levels before the elite class, and set a AA req (say 250) to access the elite professions: enhanced stat illusions, phantasmal killer AA, and a few new spells.

Then just go back and do this for each of the classes, balance them against eachother first then balance them against the other classes, and I suggest using hybrids as a baseline; everyone should be able to solo to an extent and everyone should DEFINATELY bring something to a group and a RAID... ie chanters should stack etc..

Class Balance - YUCKY!
I've harped on this in many places, but alot of good ideas can be found HERE Im particuarly fond of the use of the stamina bar being reworked into a spell bar for melees.

Ancient Spells -
Alot of people would like to see these, so they'd have something to work for, but how to implement them? I saw to interesting viewpoints on implementation...

1) Spells that are VERY powerful and grant hereto unknown abilities but not only do they drop rarely in time, they require a component that must be farmed in time-a (only guilds who have slain quarm could use these)

2) Moderate enhancements on current spell lines that dont really add anything new, but perhaps extend spells (such as a CoD with 14 min duration) or have resist adjustment (Bliss with -50 resist) less mana or even a new more effecient nuke

Religion! - (whats the point?)
This has come up several times, and to be perfectly fair, the only places it really makes a difference anymore is old world content, but its defaintely a charged subject. Some have suggested completely revamping the system, maybe making a quest to allow agnostics to choose a religion, or ringwar style events involving religion/race. My personal favorite is adding in diety specific augments via quest (similar to the token quests that i did and was disappointed to find out it was cleric/shd/shm only...) so type 7 and type 8 augments available through hard quests, and something a bit less for everyone.

ex1.
Inny clerics send you on a series of ldon adventures to collect a rare drop component which you return to them and create an augment that is a 450DD poisen proc with +200 hate and 30hp/mana.

ex2.
Zek clerics send you on a series of collection quests which finally reward you in a type 8 augment that gives 75 hps and 5 to all resists and a 30 sec cast click of berserk str.

ex3.
An agnostic sends you on a series of quests to prove yourself and upon completion you get a 50hp/50mana 5 to all resists and 3hp/mana pertick regen.

Basically make it worthwhile to do each one.... and of course the augments should be lore (note in this scenario the non-agnostic peeps could get 2 augments total)

Hirelings! - (what??)
An interesting concept would being able to hire some dps pets (albeit they would be hired adventurers) maybe implemented as paying 1k-5k for a charged item that would summon a pet, or several pets (swarm)

ex1.
I know im going into a hard dungeon and were a bit short on dps, so i pay 5k and buy a 3 charge token that summons a 9k hp human warrior who deals out 130 dps and follows pet commands, or i can use this when i go solo, his duration is 60 mins or maybe 120 if i paid a bit more.

ex2.
Gonna be doing a large raid mob, i get a token that summons a wizard mage duo that chain cast and melee some perhaps even summon their own pets (have a set dps rate for each and 6k hps or so)

And Finally we shall end with the enchanter to do list, some things I havent really gone into depth here, but would like to discuss with you all nonetheless:

Enchanter Animations vs other pet casters (especially pet focus items).... why dont we have these?
Charm and LDON, why are so much of the dungeons flagged immune?
Direcharm, worthless in LDON? Please throw in some direcharm bait..
Recipes and Tradeskills, useful stuff worth making and selling W R U?
Boggle are you broke? Maybe its time to make this a clicky like wizzie pants!
Enchanter Animation models...suck.. revamp please
Illusion doppleganger.. we miss you.... please go live
Werewolf model vs the new werewolves.. omg we want! (dont forget this in new graphics engine)

Questions, comments, DISCUSS!

Posted: Fri Oct 17, 2003 3:41 am    Post subject: Future expansions and character development
Character customization has so much potential and is something I really think that could keep EQ running for a very long time to come.

The problem with classes today are that they are too one-dimensional. Two enchanters with 300 AXP points - one completely decked in Time gear and one fresh out of ToV - still have the exact same chance of mezzing, charming, rooting etc. any given mob. Their haste/atk buffs, clarity line and benefits from illusions are likewise completely identical.

The only thing that seperates those two enchanters is around 3000 HPs and Mana + some focus benefits (albeit it ofcourse makes a significant performance difference in raid situations).

Looking at future expansions and character development it would be fantastic to see 5 Elite levels (going by Nans' idea) which required the character to make important decisions for the road ahead.

Imagine if you had to make a specialization choice at lvl 65 which would follow you through to lvl 70 and open up for advanced skills and spells within that branch. 4 Arch Sages offering you a choice of apprenticeship between Alteration, Divination, Abjuration and Conjuration.

As an apprentice you are to complete quests/missions ranging from solo adventures to mini type raids to advance through the Elite levels. As you develop your character your current spell line within that specialization will gain new benefits and completely new spells will be offered by your mentor. Such Elite specializations could materialize as:

Alteration

1st Elite Lvl (lvl 66):

+5% bonus on mana gain from the clarity line
+5% bonus to charm duration + resist modifier
+5% bonus to the Haste & ATK values on the haste line

5th Elite lvl (lvl70)

+20% bonus on mana gain from the clarity line
+20% bonus to charm duration + resist modifier
+20% bonus to the Haste & ATK values on the haste line

New spells / abilities could include single target major burst haste/atk (100% Haste / 100 ATK for 2 mins), a clarity line/ability akin to the cleric AA ability that evens out the groups mana etc etc etc.

Divination

1st Elite Lvl (lvl 66):

+5% bonus on stun resists and range
Minor racial bonuses to illusions

5th Elite lvl (lvl70)

+20% bonus on stun resists and range
Major racial bonuses to illusions
Upgrade to Project Illusion (new benefits are mad targetable...)

New spells could include completely new illusions with major/specific benefits (illusion vampire - immunity to charm etc), single target stun upgrades to Whirl till you hurl etc.

Abjuration

1st Elite Lvl (lvl 66):

Upgrade to the Shield of Maelin line
Upgrade to Rune line (cast time and absorbation)
Upgrade to the Bulvark line

5th Elite lvl (lvl70)

Shield of Maelin made targetable and/or stackable with the Focus line.
20% bonus on cast time, mana use and absorbation on Rune line
Targetable Arcane Rune etc.

New abilities to include stuff like group rune spells, group Umbra with improved AC and stacking, group Bulvark etc.

_____________

For other pure caster classes an Elite study in the Elements (wizards/magicians) would probably make more sense than pure Specialization (Evocation all the way...)

Pure melee would have to choose a road of defensive vs. offensive vs. stealth etc. A warrior for instance could further specialize as a commander/general raid leader type giving defensive/offensive benefits to an entire raid (major balancing needed here - but hey it's a nice idea).

I could go on and on...

The creation of these Elite / Specialization levels would be a major pita to balance individually. Obviously you would have to make the benefits of going down the road of Abjuration as an enchanter - a major boost as opposed to choosing the standard Alt or Conj.

In the end I love the perspective of 5 enchanters (or any other class for that matter) working side by side, each contributing within their specialized field.

Phew - long post. If you made it this far you must surely be bored at work

I'd love to see every item have a required level. I'd love to see raids capped for people as low as 30. That way, they can add some content for those levels and the items will be the best they can get...at that level. Likewise, the older content won't be instantly outdated by the next round of mudflation. There'd be much more incentive to go get armor for your level if you couldn't buy some recommended level item that drops like candy in a high level zone that has better stats for you than anything you can get yourself...and you don't have to replace it. And being able to get it themselves would encourage people to spend more time in dungeons, rather than living in outdoor zones.

Unfortunately, EQ was never designed with expansion in mind. And even when you think SOE has learned a lesson, an expansion or two later they show they haven't and do something as stupid as giving all the loot in LDoN recommended levels only. Works well for my twinks, but it shouldn't have been done that way.
