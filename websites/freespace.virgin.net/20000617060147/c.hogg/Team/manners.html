The Assist command- some usefull tips

I write to reiterate the importance of the /Assist command. Any player that does not have an /Assist hotkey is doing their group a great disservice as well as possibly putting everyone at great risk.

As an Enchanter, it is my job to control trains and multiple MoBs. There is little more frustrating for me than to successfully Enthrall a MoB only to see someone whack it and bring it right back into the fray prematurely. This usually happens because the player cannot "see" the puller in the ongoing melee in order to assist them.

The cure for that is the F1 through F6 keys. It is surprising to me, especially at higher levels, how many players do not know how these work. Simply put, each Function Key (i.e. F1 through F6) corresponds to the members of yoru group. You are always F1. The next person on your group list is F2 and so on down the list. By hitting the Function Key you bring that person up in your "Target Window" then you need only hit your /Assist hotkey and you are instantly assisting that person.

Healers take note here too please. Too often I have heard " I tried to heal you but couldn't see you ". That is because in the heat of battle with six members, a gang of MoBs, spell effects going off everywhere, it is very difficult to try and use ones pointing device to "pick out" the target.

There are lag issues involved here as well. The Hotkeys DO NOT suffer from lag like your mouse does. Hit the F# key and your Heal Hotkey. Boom...no long walk for your buddy, he feels better and so do you :) Casters, if you use a pet, by hitting F1 twice your pet is then targeted. Unfortunately by hitting F2 twice it doesn't target your buddys's pet ( perhaps a letter to Verant is in order ;))

I hope this will help others keep from making those long drafty walks :) Good hunting all.

Submitted by Mez- Enchanter on Innoruuk presently of the 31st level.

Loot splitting Options

 

Alfa Looting

In its simplest form this basically means looting in Alphabetical order, but even within this simple loot system there are variations.

a) You can loot items and cash without auto split on - each person just taking the luck of the draw. This is the simplest and the easiest method to keep track of. The only draw back to this is some juvenile always ends up whining that they aren't getting much.

b) Alfa loot with autosplit on- this way everyone will get a share of the cash, but items are still luck of the draw- this can be varied for example if the prey are know to drop bronze or something else more valuable than the usual rusty weapon, by having a sub-alfa loot list- meaning it might be 'C's turn to loot but 'A's turn to get the good item. A pain in the ass to keep track of this one so I prefer not to use it as I hate keeping lists:)

c) You can Loot as above (without auto on) but have a set cash value limit to loot up to- this means that once you have looted up to that value (cash and items), your turn is over- this is more relative at higher levels and does demand a certain amount of trust within the group. As one that I have only come across recently-and am using more and more, it works well and limits the amount of small change you end up carrying.

Auto Split

Fairly self explanatory if you go to the options you can set this to on or off- on will mean that any cash loot is automatically split between the party However! Be aware it splits coins evenly, not amounts. Thus if a party of three loots a corpse with 1 pp, 1 gp and 1 sp, the looter will get a platinum, another person will get a gold and another person will get a silver. Big difference! -But this will also balance out in the end if you are Alfa looting, but if you want to be totally finickerty and completely egalitarian,(and totally trust your party) have one person go to the bank and start at zero loot, loot every monster and split the money evenly with the party at the end of the day. To be really frugal, make your treasurer the person with the highest charisma and local faction and have him sell any extra items before the final accounting.(Lifted from Alkahzars page-((Submitted by Llyndeyr Splitter))

Loot now split later

This is simple but needs a lot of trust- one person (usually the person with the most containers and most weight allowance) loots everything -- money and cash and sells it all and splits the profits between the group at the end of the hunt. Or here is another tip for maximizing profits while hunting with a group: This will take a lot of trust, so if you do not know your party members well do not do it. 1. Get a lot of backpacks, sacks or chests and distribute them to all party members. 2. Have one person loot the corpse until he is full, then the next person loots, and so on. 3. Do not go sell until everyone is full. 4. Have the person with the highest charisma sell all the items at a shop that will give him/her the best prices (most likely his/her home city). 5. Then split the gold between all members as the party sees fit (we always do an even split). 6. Now if there is an item that a person wants the party can have them buy it and split the money between the group or the group can give it to the person. This works very well with a group that hunts very often together. We have employed this tactic to our advantage very often but everyone needs to remember that people get dropped from the server. They will come back with the stuff or your share of the gold(if they are honorable). (Submitted by Brambo the Barbarian)

Random dice throw.

This involves typing the command ......../random 100...... which will give you a random dice roll - the person who rolls closest to 100 - wins.

If a group is hunting an area where 'good' items are drooped - i.e. special weapons or artifact's- then to be fair,if a group assisted in the hunt then the group should have a fair chance of getting that item- regardless of if they already own one or not. Let me explain how we have worked it in the past . During a hunt any items rolled for are open to every player even if that player already owns one, they can still roll the dice. However should the same item come up for a roll again, whoever already won it-- can not roll again for that item. You could change this slightly to those who can use the item only roll- or to only those who do not own the item roll- but unless you are all really good friends someone will get miffed and feel missed out, because after all- a lot of the time we are talking a lot of money!

Basic common Sense

Root Root ROOT!

OK I know its real basic stuff - but its amazing how many groups we have hunted with who forget. Root or snare save lives- no exaggeration, if one of your victims should get away- a lot of the time they run off and bring some very very nasty friends with them- and by the time you have realize your in trouble ( we all know how hectic a big group fight can get) it is often too late. So either designate a person to cast snare or root or just make sure its a habit anyone who has the spell gets into. Some people I know hate the root spell and much prefer snare, the reason being a rooted creature will try and fight to the death, however if its snared it will just try and run away. At the ends of the day I don't care if its rooted or snared as long as it doesn't go get friends! If one does get away - and you know a "Train" is coming and that you are going to have to run, for goodness sake shout to warn the whole area- we've all been run over by trains - its not nice- even worse when its appeared out of nowhere with no warning, make a hot button just in case.

Assist the Puller

Use the assist command! I didn't discover this till we started hunting dungeons in larger groups, what this basically means is the person who pulls the monsters for the group usually arrives being hit by more than one- the quickest way is to kill that one creature first - to ensure you are all hitting the same thing make an assist button - target the puller and use the assist key which will make you target the same monster. This is also essential if you are lucky enough to have an enchanter in the group who can mesmerize any creature in the crowd that isn't being hit, its very satisfying to get it right and just pick them off one at a time:)

Keep 'em off the Casters.

Remember your casters are the killers the rest are just tanks! I'm stating the obvious again- sorry, but its hard to appreciate how important casters are while you are happily bashing away admiring your double attack for 44 each hit with a BIBs (hehehe sorry had to add that , I love my bibs) what you forget sometimes is you wizard friend- quietly standing at the back is often hitting for a lot more per cast- but is very vulnerable, so always watch the casters health and casters-- if you get one on you-- TELL the group, because it can be missed. Also a word about healers- a lot of mobs have an unhealthy hatred of anyone who heals- so all of a sudden your cleric or druid can suddenly find themselves with a very unwanted fan club. Where ever possible try and save healing till after the battle, this is not always an option and don't let someone die cos your worried about being mobbed( I really admire healers- everyone expects you to save them and its a huge burden) but just be aware - and again if you get mobbed TELL the group.

Well at the end of the day its your conscience, cos the game design is such no one can see what you've looted, and to be honest I've only ever once come across someone in the group who I had not teamed with before- who I was 99% certain lifted a very valuable drop and wouldn't own up. My philosophy is as its hard to prove I wont accuse - but I sure as hell wont hunt with them again, and oddly enough they never did ask to join us again - which is unusual:) The bottom line is honesty or greed or selfishness is inbuilt, and if you feel or know someone ripped the group off-- don't join with them again- calling in a GM or name calling solves nothing, people are good and bad in real life and in the game- and I am here to enjoy myself not to waste time bitching:)

Miscelanious group tips

Try and pull to and fight near a zone edge. (In buisy dungeons this is dangerous and not relevant as a lot of people rest there- just use common sense.)

If you can - and in some areas like Runny Eye for example - get the puller SOWN it is essential.

Have the puller designated as safty person- if he/she screams RUN! or ZONE! on the way back- just do it- less downtime and far preferable ot corpse retrieval.

Have the strongest player or the player who knows the area best pull - preferably with a missile weap of some sort, or - in the case of camps- if you have a caster with harmony or lull - get them to pull.

Always try and warn other players in or at the zone of trains.

Better to pull too small than too big.

If possible wipe out enemy casters first.

Do not be selfish with buffs- buff everyone:)

Better to spend 10 mins discussing team tactics before starting than to spend 20 mins waiting for everyone to get thier body back.

If you have to zone cos health is low (healers, casters and pullers usually) but the others are doing fine- get back in asap to pick up some experience as the team finish them off:)

Game driven rules

Well as far as I am aware there are no guidelines printed anywhere(for goodness sake if you know of any let us know) But the rule of thumb seems to be for best results group within a 5 level spread- and as far as there being an Experience bonus for group fighting rumours are rife- so far the best we have come up with is that its 10 % per extra person in the group, another source said you get 25 % for a full group bonus. It may even change as you level, so take your pick.

 

Info Rooted out By Eldarian

Following a short converstation with a GM after a bit of prodding he revealed 2 things of interest:

Xp awards are level based ( no suprise there ). But also your class goes someway to determining how much XP you gain from each kill. I was unable to get a class breakdown later, being told that such information is not available to the players.

But as general observations I have found that if you have a group with a wide level range ( I once hunted at 13 with a level 19 partner and we both ended up with a bubble of XP) it is better for the group as a whole to hunt a wide range of monsters.

For instance a goblin miner will be green to the level 28 wizard, but will be blue to the level 24 cleric. While the wizard will get no xp from the miner, the cleric would get more than if the miner was blue to the wizard. This is one reason why people like to either be the highest level or close to the highest level in a group but as long as the group takes the time to see to everyones needs ( which IMO a good group should) then no one will feel left out and everyone will still have fun.

So, unless your in some sort of personal race to get level 50 in the shortest time having fun is the whole point.

Dealing with a Team Death

(thanks to Eldarian for thinking of and writing about this subject)

No one Ive met who plays enjoys dying. When you are soling Dying is just a matter of mourning your XP loss and going off to recover your body. In a group death takes on other dimensions. First of all the person who died isnt going to happy despite what they may be saying (Unless the death was heroic in some way). You then have the time to wait for the person to return to where you were hunting.

During this time you have to deceide if you are going to carry on fighting or wait till the person/people return. First thing you should do is ask if the dead group member needs help in returing to his/her body. Its no fun running across 6 zones without a weapon, and nothing is worse than dying on your way to recover a corpse. Casters will most likely be bound just outside the zone but may still need help in the actual recovery. You should also ask periodically if the person is ok as they travel and be prepared to go out to assist if they get lost or something else happens.

You may lose a hunting spot but people appreciate the effort and making a long term friend is worth far more than any XP or loot you may lose because of time lost hunting.

The group should then keep an eye on the person who just died to see that they dont die again right away, even if you have to zone early or flee a fight you may have been able to win. This is especially true if the group is close to level or has just leveled and needs to secure it. There really is no way to avoid death. But the group can limit the impact it has on their hunting companions. A few pointers:

1. Pulling is dangerous - If the puller dies and someone else is able to pull then switch pullers. If thats not possible then reward the puller in some other way: Extra loot, and lots of compliments.

2. Casters are fragile - A caster may have 500 hps but will be getting hit more often and for greater damage than a melee class. Keep the casters alive and remember you both may have 2 bubbles of health but yours will last a lot longer if you have armour.Just because they are bound outside and your 5 zones away dont treat them as sacrificial lambs.

3. Fight near zone for a while if you just took a beating. Its a good way to get everyone back into the routine without expossing the group to un needed danger.

4. If someone dies many times in one night and your a regular group consider moving to a new spot or helping them to regain the lost xp by assisting them to solo for a while. They only have to do 51 % damage to get the credit and with a healer and the rest of the group helping that lost xp comes back fast.

5. Dont dwell on death. Easy to say if your not the one who just lost a bubble of Xp but if you dwell on it you will more than likely die again as you try to gain it back by taking extra risks.

Ideas for a group fight

(Recieved by email) -Keep em comming!

When in a fight like to have a tank on front and back of the mob. Have the casters all on one side and that way can watch the reaction of the mob to caster spells on screen rather than watch hp bars and game text.

Have the pull set up with back to casters, assisting tanks come in behind. This way always have someone on the targets back. If a mob from a multi pull leaves to attack a caster you can see it leave to engage and pursue before it actually starts its attack on your caster. This is usually your nuke or healer and responding is the job of 1 of the non pulling tanks. The puller does the pull and future group target selection.

This sort of setup allows for good coverage of the battle area for wanderers as well. And as always if your group has an enchanter their job is to deal with these outbreaks and mes the aggressive on caster mob.

Personally only like zone edge when new to the zone, find that you have to deal with others mistakes too often there. It is useful in terms of finding out how things con and fight in a zone. Your safety is your group and knowledge of the area, and the best things are deep inside anyways.

Agree with the 10 minutes of discussion first in a group, like to do a "My job is..." when grouping with strangers, clears the air about what might come. Like to see casters, especially if 2 the same in group discuss what they will be doing. Casters then are able to maximize the available 8 slots for spells and give the party a better response. 2 shamans with the same spells and all you get is twice the mana, 2 shamans with 16 spells up for combat... nuff said there.

Usually my group casters are shamans, druids and clerics. With this sort of group the healing load can be split such that mob aggro due to healing is small.

When hunting HG's the tanks need alot of healin throughout the fight, by taking turns the tanks can take turns with the giant. Down to 2 bubs switch off and back out, the other tank(s) take up the slack and the injured tank is healed up, maybe throwin in a few arrows, and then back in.

Another good setup

(sent in by Arran)

Hear is my great pulling tactic it works well with 3 melee class and 3 casters, although you can do this with any group, it works brilliant out in the open as well

In this tactic you have 2 rows, the first row is your melee class, anyone who can tank basically, i prefer shadowknight in this case, failing that a good warrior with a bow or any melee character with a ranged wepon.

The 2nd row can comprise of you spell casters, anyone with offensive magic, failing that more melee types if the situation warrents it. The reserve consists of the puller and a cleric, other healer will do, or even better a cleric and a other healer type like druid or shaman works well hear.

Most consistem spawns are of around 3 monsters so this is what i based the example on, each of the group pairs into 2 people who take one monster instead of all attacking one, this reduces death and downtime and is a fairly good tactic.

1 The puller attracts the mobs attention, prefrably from range so the melee can target there oponent before it hits you. The puller runs back and in the middle of the melee and positions himself at the back of the group where his parnter is, hopefully he will get 1 mob on him, and the two of them can kill it anyway they like.

2 The 2 melee character cast/throw/shoot at one of the monster following the puller, and when they hit it from range the monster breaks off and runs straight to the new assailant, this is great with shadowknights as they can make use of there spells and pull at the same time and they have the melee ability to stand there ground in combat. This is when there partner (any class, at least 1 caster is prefered though) helps them out and the two of them take there foe.

3 Now you should have roughley 3 monsters with 2 people on them, as soon as one of the monster drops help the group that is fairing the wose and soon they will all be dead. This requires that people almost stand where they are and you can see the whole fight, you know what is going on and it is easy to get out of sticky situation.

The great thing about this tactic is the death rate, it is very low and have great downtime, meaning you are ready for the spawn when it comes.

The cleric does not have to constantly heal one person so the healer is not mobbed by more than one NPC.

The casters should have a pause between there blasts so that they dont agro the mob, they then have some mana left for emergencys and can play a more active role in the fight, even i daresay melee!

The melee class are figting and none of them is getting extreemly beat up, wich makes them easyer to heal and there downtime is the same for each, wich reduces the chances of a tank getting picked off while low on health.

Downtime is lower, mana recovery is better, the cleric doesnt wast all his/her healing on one person no one has the chance to get mobed, tanks are not picked off ater battles and most importantly group is happy.

What I also love about this is that groups are so much easyer to build, you dont need compatable class, just get a warrior and hybrid, healer and caster and fill in the rest with what is available, this group is effective with small spawns but for mobs greater than 4 you will have to be even more clever and mes ones on the way, but it is still the same tactic.
