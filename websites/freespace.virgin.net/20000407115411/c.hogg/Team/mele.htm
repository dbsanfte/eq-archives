Tanking- the Basics

Well guys - as you know we are the tanks, but do not equate being a tank with giving damage, (except maybe monks who are better at giving damage than taking it- more your damage dealing - fast light tank) because most casters can out damage us every time. In the group setting our basic job is to keep the little monsters occupied while the casters do their stuff, be it healing us, mesmerizing the mob or blasting it. That is not to deny that we can each of us do some awesome damage ourselves but the higher you get the more you appreciate the importance of keeping a caster free to do his job.

So in the team setting there are general rules that apply to most of the melee classes and then some specifics to each class. And again I make no apologies for stating the obvious.

General guidelines.

Always target the toughest monster in the pull (or the caster if there is one)and preferably make and use an assist button to assist who ever is pulling for the group.

If a monster breaks off and heads for a caster switch targets to that monster and do all you can to get the monster off ( your healer will be particularly vulnerable to this happening)

If the monster your hitting has a damage shield - and you have a caster in the group with dispel magic- tell the group so they know to try and cast it to remove the shield.

If you have an enchanter in the group who has 'Mesmerize' make sure you use assist to make sure all melee types are hitting the dame creature.

If you have a rogue in the group, pull targets away from walls/doors/lava so that the Rogue can maneuver behind it. There is 90 degree angle behind the mob where backstab works.

If you are down to 2 bubs of health, but the other tanks look OK, -- Step back, stop taunting- and heal if you can or just wait it out-no shame in common sense, and heroes are nice but better alive than dead:) And at a guess this is the one we find the hardest to do- but it makes sense:)

If the command to run in given by puller or team leader, it is often in the midst of chaos- and while I'd like to say the sensible thing is just to leg it - (UK slang for run :o) ) - our 2 bubs of health will last a lot longer than our casters 2 bubbles so try and be last- but be sensible, if all looks hopeless go- you can help in other ways with corpse retrieval later.

As a member of the melee class or tank- you will at some point be asked to be the puller for the team - so here are some basic pulling tips:)

Paladin

As a paladin, by definition we are sworn to protect those around us, in the group situation we are basically tanks, but our cleric abilities can be of huge use before and after a fight.

If you have a shaman or druid in the group their buffs are more powerful but its always worth casting your buffs on people in the absence of any better- ( I found out recently that 'center' is not only a buff but can also be used to heal if multiple casts are made- its slightly less manna intensive than light healing.)

If in a group fighting the undead and you have invis Vs undead- after a battle - cast it on your casters so they can med in peace.

In the absence of a cleric, druid or shaman you will most likely end up being group healer, but stress to the group that your heal is not really an in battle spell, more of a after battle spell, having said that you do have lay hands to use but it usually is an emergency cast and don't let you team rely on that miracle:)

(I recently reached level 30 and find that my heal is somewhat more usefull- and have found myself acting as in battle healer on occasion, but prefer not to<g> also my shields are getting better and do stack with some druids buffs, so I do tend to buff the other tanks and if I have a good supply of reagents casters and pets)

If you do have a healer in the group and you do have lay hands available to use- tell the group so the healer knows to heal others before you if things get nasty, because you can save yourself in an emergency.

Make sure you magic abilities are up as high as cap allows- spell practice is a bore but can save time and lives later.

Bard

Bards are meant for groups. They fill in so many roles and add so much to a group. To be effective as a bard in a group, a bard must not only know how to play his class well, but he/she needs to know how all the classes should be played. Many bard abilities emulate abilities from other classes and almost always in a lesser degree. Never try to fill in a special role if there is a specialist available.

The best way a bard can help his group is to stay alive. Until a bard learns good song control he will almost always be the first to die. Don't play songs if they are not needed. Make sure your puller always gives warning before bringing a mob. As soon as he says, "inc" you should stop any songs being played. If it's a multi mob pull you have to know as soon as you start playing partywide songs you will pull every unattended mob onto you. Learn to use single target songs. Brusco's Boastful Bellow at level 12 is the first of these. Others include Selo's Consonant Chain, Crission's Pixie Strike, and Fufil's Curtailing Chant. Once you think things are manageable switch to party wide songs.

Learn to be a puller. Bard's have many tools at their disposal to make them some of the best pullers. You can scout invisibly, can break camps with our lull song, and can even arrive unscathed by snaring the pull with our chains song. At level 32 (manna song) people don't like bards leaving the group , they like to keep you there. But at that point bards still make excellent initial pullers to break up camps using Kellin's Lugubrious Lament.

Learn how your songs interact with other classes abilities. All enchanter speed up songs are faster than bard speed buffs. Almost all spell stat buffs will stack with bard stat buffs, but the song must be started after the spell has been cast. The same goes for SoW. It will not stick if Selo's is playing, so wait till all party members are SoWed before playing Selos.

Learn how to use Shauri's Sonorus Clouding (invisibility). It is the quickest way to get a party deep into a dungeon. Its quicker than individual casting of invisibility and see invisibility and since the song is refreshed every 3 seconds it is a lot more reliable. Inform your party members that they have to stay in range for the effect. If in a high train area always keep this song memed, if you can get it up fast enough your party can just sit back and watch the show as all the other groups head for the zone.

Protect your casters and healers. After level 27 and obtaining Solon's Song of the Sirens (charm) this should always be the role of the bard. Charm is one of the best ways to pull a mob off a caster or healer in 3 seconds flat. (Due to a bug with charm it is sometimes necessary to use the /pet back off command) Once the mob breaks it's charm though it will be stuck on you, but this buys time for other measures to be taken, such as enchanter mes, or just plain old tank taunting. You are better able to take a few hits than your caster or healer.

Remember one of your biggest roles is to minimize downtime. Make sure you tell your tanks to trade off damage if they can. Your healing song is useless if you finish a fight and one tank is down to one bubble of life and everyone else is full.

Make sure you know your roles in any particular group. Is there a druid or ranger present for snares or will you have to use chains? Is there an enchanter there or will you have to handle crowd control.? Communication is very important to success as a bard.

Lastly, know how to twist songs. If you only know how to keep one song going at a time you are not being the best bard you can for your group. If you don 't know how learn it.

Ranger

(thanks to Eldarian again for this)

A rangers role in a group is primarily that of tank , even though rangers don't have the hps of the other tanks we do dish out a lot of damage. Rangers are also the best equipped class for pulling MOBs to the group.

The versatility of our spells make us unmatched as pullers in the outdoors. Another role we fill nicely is that of preventing runners, snare everything your fighting even if it doesn't hold right away because someone has rooted the MOB.

Tracking will allow you to see what's around before blindly walking into danger. In the outdoors you can pull what your group is killing as fast as everyone can meditate and heal.

In a pinch you can turn the tide of battle with a some well placed spells but most of your manna will probably be used for healing after the battle. This will cut down your groups downtime and let the clerics keep their manna for combat healing, something that we really cant do at any level.

Leave buffing to those better suited for it. Unless no one else in your group has access to those buffs. One that springs to mind is Feet like cat, unless you have a shaman in the group you should cast this on all the tanks.

Rogue

Thanks toTurlo Lomon on Mithaniel Marr for this info

1. The Rogue will position himself behind the target for backstabbing. Once the fight has begun, avoid moving around as this makes it much harder for Rogue to keep in backstab position. All group members should stay on the same side of the target so Rogue can maneuver behind.

2. Pull targets away from walls/doors/lava so that the Rogue can manuever behind it.

3. Keep in mind Rogues have about the same HP as a Shaman.

4. Taunt as much as possible to keep target off the Rogue (see #3).

5. You don't have to remind the Rogue to use backstab, if you don't see them doing it it's because the backstab keeps missing or the mob turns on them just before they attempt it.

6. Poisons are too expensive/time consuming to be used except in the most rare occasions. Even if attempted it is a one-time random effect.

7. Rogues CAN now walk invis, but it is VERY slow (Imagine walking with half bar of health).

8. If you're a druid, cleric, caster, etc... and something is beating you down, DON'T RUN AROUND! Makes it much harder for the rogue to backstab (taunt) it off you. 9. Rogues shouldn't be pullers if there are tanks in a group. If a rogue pulls makes it much harder to have things taunted off him.

10. If a mob runs let the rogue have room to backstab, don't need the whole group chasing or wasting mana on it (unless its for snare or root).

 

Basic things for a Rogue to Know

Learn to circle strafe. Practice it until you can do it with as few 'you can't see your target' messages as possible.

Know the spell effect for stun (red dots) and when you see it circle around, backstab and return to your position. When a NPC mob begins casting circle around and backstab and return to your position.

If there is a chance in hell that that mob is going to turn it's back on you save that backstab, if not use it as an extra pierce.

Nobody taunts off you like another rogue, the most effective groups I'm in feature two rogues oppisite each other or three rogues in a triangle.

You are a group only class, on the rare occasions you can solo you are not nearly as effective as you could be at that moment in a group.

Always be aware of what's happening to the casters. If they can't cast your most likely to die. Make sure that they are always opposite you and if a mob breaks off unto them it's your job to get it off.

Eldarian's guide to Pulling

Rule 1 : Don't pull anything that you cant make it back to your group with. For example even if your group can handle 10 goblin elites there is no point pulling them if your never going to make it back or you are in too poor shape to fight by the time you get back. Also be aware that unless they are pulled off you the who lot you pulled will be beating on you.

Rule 2 : Know the area. Unfortunately knowledge is most often paid for in blood. Know what spawns where and it will make you a better puller. One easy example is knowing that the bar room and the knight room in unrest are linked. Knowledge of where to pull from makes you look good and cuts down on deaths.

Rule 3 : Always take out the trash. If your pulling from deeper then you need to clear any green trash out of the way and know how long it takes to come back again. The last thing you want is to come back with a good pull and have 10 greens tag along. Even greens hurt in numbers.

Rule 4 : Know when to run. If you do pull a huge train that you know your group cant handle yell train and run for the zone. Don't take the train anywhere near your group and don't yell for help if you know you cant win.

Rule 5 : Take charge. Your the one taking the risks here so do things on your terms. If the group doesn't like it leave, you will mostly likely see them getting their corpses half hour later. If something isn't working change it, find a good method and you are well on your way to being a really top puller.
