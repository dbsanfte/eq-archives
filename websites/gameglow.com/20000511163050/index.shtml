Quake III: Team Arena
Posted Wednesday, May 10, 2000 - 22:16
by GG-Xynon

E3 will bring many new games and new fans tommorrow. id software will bring their newest addition, Quake III: Team Arena. This mission pack for Quake III will introduce new cooperative gameplay, such as harvester. In the harvester mode players must return skulls while being in the middle of fire.

Only id could take Quake III Arena to the next level by developing a mission pack that melds their legendary gameplay with new advanced team play features," -Mitch Lasky, executive vice president of Activision Studios.

Many new weapons are included. The nailgun, chaingun, and a proximity-mine launcher are lined up for this mission pack. As the name states, Quake III: Team Arena will zero in on team play. Predesigned logos, skins, models, and features are just a little bit of what id has in store for E3!



PlayStation2 Launch Oct. 26
Posted Wednesday, May 10, 2000 - 19:17
by GG-Xynon

On Wednesday, Sony Computer Entertainment America Inc. stated that they would launch PlayStation2 in the North America with a shipment of 1 million units. After the inital shipment, Sony says, 2 million units will be shiped through March 31, 2001. As it was released earlier this year in Japan, it brags super-fast graphics and realistic 3-D images.

The overwhelming success of the product launch proves that the market is ready for PlayStation2, as we chart a path toward the future of networked digital entertainment," said Ken Kutaragi, the company's president and CEO.

A controller, multiport and 8MB memory card will be available for $34 extra, and software titles will be $49. Over 800 software titles from the PlayStation platform will be available for this new system.

XBox @ E3
Posted Tuesday, May 9, 2000 - 21:35
by Merlin

"The gamers have spoken! In an unusual move, Microsoft asked the gaming public at large how big of a splash -- if any at all -- the company should make with their future-generation video-game console at the Electronic Expo (E3) in Los Angeles this year. With well over a year before release of the console, Microsoft had not planned to attend this year's E3 with great fanfare. With the show a scant few days away, gamers overwhelming voted for Microsoft to bring Xbox to E3 -- and to do so in a big way."

"The Xbox poll ran over this past weekend on gaming news sites and Xbox fan sites alike. With the votes counted, the outcome was clear: an overwhelming 86% of fans demanded that Microsoft go whole hog and bring the Xbox to E3. Honoring the fans' request, Microsoft made emergency preparations and delivered the Xbox to the Los Angeles Convention Center this morning."

Looks like Vidguy's poll was a big sucess!

"Turns out Microsoft got a ton of votes from all the "Xbox at E3" polls that have been running all over the Web, so it looks like we'll be doing it up this week after all! Thief II: The Metal Age Review
Posted Saturday, April 29, 2000 - 13:38
by GT-Ogre

Today editor Ogre reviews Thief II: The Metal Age. This game is the sequel to one of the best games ever, Thief: The Dark Project. Looking Glass Studios has made yet another master piece. Below is some of the review:

"Thief's gaming engine, the Dark Engine, lacked many modern lighting and 3D effects. Basically it would just draw and texture the shapes. There were no sky boxes like what you see in Quake, Unreal, and Half Life. Instead the sky textures would not move at all. Thief II: The Metal Age has fixed this though. Now skies look more real and believable. Not only are skies better to look at, but so are the actors. In the original game actors such as the guards looked blocky when close up. This was because the textures and skins were not detailed enough due to the limitations of the graphics engine. All of this has been fixed which will make Thief II way better than Thief. You may say but what is so big about having nice graphics when the game play is not the graphics? Well the graphics add to the realism of the game."

Doulber Review
Posted Friday, April 28, 2000 - 17:57
by GT-Ogre

Today we take a look at Doulber. Doulber is a new puzzle game by a small game development company named Phelios. Editor Ogre says it is one of the best games puzzle games he has ever played. Below is some of his review:

"The basic idea of Doulber is to go collect a certain amount of diamonds in a set amount of time for each level. Yes that task may sound easy at first, but soon you realize as the levels get bigger that it is difficult. There are also many enemies, or baddies as I like to call them, to get in your way. Actually there are four types of baddies that try to stop you. These include the darker, bouncer, guardian, and flyer. Each of which has there own attack styles. Also some other obstacles you might find on the levels are blocks and stone walls. However these obstacles can be taken out with bombs that you can pick up while playing. Speaking of levels, there are over 50 levels under 5 differently themed worlds. Now that I have talked some about the game play, lets now talk about the graphics and sound."

Microsoft Fanstock 2000
Posted Thursday, April 20, 2000 - 21:08
by Merlin

In what I hope will be an annual event, Microsoft rolled out the red carpet for a select list of webmasters from around the world. Microsoft Fanstock 2000 (April 17-18th) treated 23 attendees to a rare inside look to the MS Games division, and showed what they have in store for the online gaming community. AoE2.com was included in that list, and GameGlow.com was represented as well because Merlin is in charge of both the fan site and the hosting network. Personally, not only was it a real privilege to be included in this event, I also had one heluva great time.

I met some old friends face to face for the first time, and made new friends by the dozen. Some giants in the gaming industry were on hand, and some very exciting current projects were previewed. Today we will give you an overview of what happened, and in the coming days much more will be written about each of the games we previewed and all the great people we met, and some pictures will be posted once all the shots come in from everyone who had a camera.

Microsoft Fanstock 2000 was basically 2 evenings and one very full day, crammed with lots of seminars and round table discussions, and surrounded by special events and side trips that were like icing on the cake. I think as a group we made as positive an impression on Microsoft and their gaming allies as they did on us, so you other webmasters reading this could be on next year's list!

Read Merlin's Travel Journal for the personal view of what a great time we had. After getting what sleep I could on the plane, and after I got home, I am still a bit wound up about the whole trip. I had a truly excellent time, and wish to thank Microsoft, Arbuthnot, and Ensemble for everything they did to make this a memorable time for me. Next will come some coverage on the games we were shown during Microsoft Fanstock 2000. Stay tuned over the coming days for more!
