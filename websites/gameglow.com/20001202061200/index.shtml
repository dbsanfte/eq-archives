GG AMCC Interview
Posted Wednesday, November 22, 2000
by Merlin

Derek at Interviews@GameGlow.com just posted an Interview Marcel Neamtu of AMC Creation on their RTS with role playing elements, No Name War. For starters the title features three sides and randomly generated maps. Here's a taste:

GameGlow: There seems to be a heavy influence from the Total Annihilation series at least in terms of graphics. What is your take on that statement and what are some of your other design influences?

AMCC: Thanks for the opportunity to explain this. Our lead graphic artist played TA for the first time one year later after he had been working on NNW graphics (!), by that time the graphic style, as it can be seen now, was already set up. When NNW is playable, the player will see much more. It could be the 'feeling', partly similar to TA's (it happens), but no influence (neither from other games).

Link: Marcel Neamtu with AMC Creation Interview

GameGlow Reviews Heavy Metal: FAKK 2
Posted Wednesday, November 8, 2000
by Xynon

Guest writer Rajiv Ashrafi has taken a look at the the action hit, Heavy Metal FAKK 2, and compiled a complete review here at Game Glow. This reviews includes his thoughts on the game, graphics, game controls, and more. To read the full review and see how it scored, click here. Here is a clip straight from the review:

The game attempts to combine the best elements of several genres: It looks like a third-person action game, begins like a role-playing game, plays like a platform game, and uses both first-person shooter conventions and fighting-game elements in its combat system. That sounds cool enough and for players who didn't like Tomb Raider or Quake, might want to get this game! Since FAKK2 is a third person action-adventure game it does include its fair share of mind melting mayhem, the action is balanced nicely by story elements, puzzles and exploration.

Thomas Friedman Interview
Posted Tuesday, November 7, 2000
by Merlin

Derek at Interviews.GameGlow.com just posted an interview with Thomas Friedmann, Co-Founder and Managing Director of Funatics on Cultures. Cultures was called "the rural version of The Sims," by PC Gamer. We asked about the roles of trading, management, fighting, and relationships, artwork, objectives, interface, and more. Check out the Thomas Friedman Interview. Here's a quick taste:

GG : To someone who has never seen or played the game how would you describe the way it plays?

Thomas Friedmann: "It's similar to real life. Imagine a worker being always hungry, sleeping under bridges and being very lonely and isolated. He'll be very unmotivated and so a quite lousy and unproductive worker. That's obvious.

But if he has enough to eat, has a nice home with beautiful furniture and has a nice wife, than you can imagine he'll be very glad, motivated and will work very well. The player's task now is first to do his best that each character in Cultures is feeling well and is well equipped (with tools, shoes etc.). Than they work fine and the player can concentrate more on the growth of the village. Getting babies, explore the area, find new jobs etc. etc."
