Hitboxes

First hitboxes. For those that aren't familiar with the phrase, a Hitbox is the area that you are shooting at to hit your opponent. The visual clue for the hitbox is the model, but in a lot of games the model and the hitbox don't match up perfectly.

This was a huge problem in Counter-Strike. After testing, I realized that this is a huge problem in NS too.

The Marine hitboxes are fairly standard. In fact they're almost perfect. If you see a piece of a marine you can shoot it (at least with Light Armor, I forgot to test Heavy Armor).

The Alien hitboxes are fucked up....big.

First, it appears that the hitbox for the aliens is the exact same size no matter what alien you are (only exception seems to be the fade...I have to retest this though).

Skulk — This is probably the most noticable problem (cause most people are skulks). The image on the right shows a model of the skulk, the red box is the hitbox. If you shoot a skulk from the side in the head your bullet will magically pass right through him.


Gorge — Same deal with the skulk. Don't shoot a Gorge from the side in the head.


Lerk — Same as above. The tips of his wings protrude from the hitbox, so they aren't good targets.


Fade — This one seems to be the only normal looking hitbox. But I might be wrong, I'll need to test it again.


Onos — This one is the most screwed up of all the hitboxes. Just look at it. One of the largest parts of his body is completely out of the hitbox (head), and so are his hindquarters.


Summary for Hitboxes — If you're Alien, you won't have a problem. If you're Marine, shoot for center-mass of your target...you might actually hit it.

Notes (yay!)

  1. The rough calculation for L1 is L0 * 1.1, L2 is 1.2, L3 is 1.3. That's rough. Seems the game rounds strangely at times.
  2. Knife — Two things about the knife, first it's damage doesn't appear to go up with the Weapon Upgrades. Second, the knife seems to be the only Marine weapon with a Knockback effect (it'll move your target around if possible). Most physical alien attacks have a Knockback.
  3. Pistol — The damage wasn't really consistent at Level 0. It either did 18 or 20, but most often 20.
  4. Shotgun — The damage listed is assuming you are pointblank. Basically at Level 0, it's 16 points of damage/flechete with 10 flechetes/shot. This weapon is one of the best weapons for killing onos and fades..if you can get close. Also, this weapon is worthless at more then about 15 feet. Also the Reload time was for all 10 shots. You can reload a shot at a time if you need (great for chasing an alien).
  5. Grenade Launcher — This thing will do AE damage. It will hurt the shooter.
  6. Mines — We all know that Fades can detonate any mines with Acid Rocket, killing any marines nearby. Gorges can shoot the laser mines to detonate them. But when an alien walks over the mine, if it detonates it will damage the marine that place it, if he is nearby. Also, Onos (is that Oni?) are basically immune to ground laid mines. This is because their hitbox is too far off the ground to trigger the mine. Skulks Leaping through a mine field are also basically immune. It'll detonate a laser mine, but won't really damage the Skulk.

Notes

  1. Leap — Wow. I never realized what it did until I really tested it. Basically it's a mode of travel, but if it hits a Marine it'll damage them...the damage will go up (really fast) if you can stay on the marine. A single leap has enough damage to kill any light marine. It'll take about 4 leaps to kill a Heavy (and it's less efficient then just biting em outa their armor). If your leap touches the ground or leaves the marine, it'll stop doing damage. This is easiest to use if you can land on top of a marine...they will die before they can move much. Also, it does have a cap of damage, about 160 or so I think. That means against a Heavy Armor marine you'll only do about 80 damage to his armor. That's why it takes so much to kill them. (I'll explain armor below.)
  2. Heal — I'm not sure how many of you realize that Healing has a range. In fact healing has a large range. See the image on the right? That marine is being "healed" from that far away. Click on the image for the full-size view. That image was taken in the Eclipse Command hive.
  3. Babblers — Babblers have two damages, one is 20 for their bites, but anytime they die (from either wearing out, or being shot), they'll explode like Xenocide and do 40 damage.
  4. Spores — The sporecloud lasts for 10 seconds. During that time any marine in the cloud will take ~9 damage/second. I need to double check, but I believe that it bypasses the armor reduction.
  5. Acid Rocket — Unlike the Marine weapons and most've the Alien weapons, the projectile for the acid rocket doesn't spawn on the outside of the shooters hitbox. It spawns maybe 2-3 feet ahead of you. The picture on the right shows the range from the shooter that a marine has to be to hit them. This also means that any marine within that range has nothing to fear from the Acid Rocket. This "feature" allows Fades to sit on the other side of "solid" objects and shoot through them. This means glass windows, grates, doors, and sometimes very very thin walls. Joy.

Marine Armor

If you read the information about Marine Armor in the patch messages and the manual, it's a bit misleading. They'll state stuff like the Heavy Armor absorbs 95% damage. Which is kinda true.

The way Marine Armor works is that it has an absorption amount which the armor will absorb (taking damage), then it erases that amount of damage from the rest of the incoming shot as well, then let's the rest through. In case that doesn't make sense, let me demonstrate:

An Acid Rocket will do 80 points of damage to a Marine without Armor. If the marine has 100 health and 50 armor (default) then the Acid rocket will only do 56 points of Health Damage, and 12 points of Armor damage. Leaving the Marine screwed at 44 Health, 38 Armor.

Math: A - (B*A) - (B*A) = Damage to Health Where A is the damage the weapon should've done, and B is the Armor Absorption (the first A*B represents the damage the Armor takes, the second represents that damage that poofs).

So a level 0 armored Marine that takes one Onos Claw (horn) will be left at 16 health and 32 armor.

Here's a table that lists Armor Absorption, and the "effectiveness":

Armor Level Absorp. Effect
0 15% 30%
1 20% 40%
2 25% 50%
3 30% 60%
Heavy Armor 47.5% 95%

Just a note before I forget. If the damage absorbed is something like 4.7, it'll round down, so it'll really only help for 4 damage.

Alien Armor

Please read the above Marine Armor section. They function very similiarly. I need to do some more testing though because with 1.04 they changed how they did Carapace math.

Level 0 alien armor is just like Level 0 marine armor, 15% Absorption, giving you a 30% effectiveness.
