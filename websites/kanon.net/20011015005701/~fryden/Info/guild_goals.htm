Our Methods
  1. The first and most powerful method is Respect. Respect the people around you and what they are doing. After all, they are paying just as much as you are to play. Respect means exactly what you think it means: the Golden Rule. If you come across  role-players, try to role-play your character a little. If you stumble upon a private group chatting about football games, don't yell at them for being out of character - unless they are shouting across the whole zone. Don't kill newbie monsters just because you are bored. Don't just give away the game's secrets. And so on.
  1. The second method is Charity. OoL members above the 10th level are required to go out of their way to help a newbie at least once a week. Helping players who are completely new to the game (as opposed to simply low-level characters) is strongly preferred. The reasoning behind this is not only so that new players can begin having fun sooner, but also to make the OoL stronger.

    The single best method of recruitment is to behave in a way that other players want to behave. By going out of your way to make sure some new player is having fun, you will demonstrate what kind of guild we are, and all of the people who are the kind of people that would go out of their way for a stranger will want to join our guild... and those are exactly the kind of people we want.

    "Help" doesn't mean dumping money and equipment on them (though that doesn't hurt), it also means talking to them to help them learn some of the basics of the game and how to get around. A sympathetic ear, a few tips, directions to a good beginner's hunting ground, and the kind of basic equipment that all the experienced players get for their low-level characters will make an indelible impression.

  1. The third method is Justice. It would be a mistake to assume that the PvP switch will fix everything. There are still many, many ways an anti-social player can wreak havoc upon other players. It would also be wrong to assume that there is nothing we can do about such a player. Specific tactics will be discussed elsewhere, but the basic strategy is a long-term approach. EverQuest is a communal world, and exclusion from the community is always the ultimate punishment. This applies to players who are PvP as well. 
  1. The last method is Truth. In the world of Norrath, words are more powerful than they are in other worlds. A member of the OoL must be careful with the words they use. Lying, in particular, is a dangerous and dishonorable act that poisons the very air we all breath. A member of the OoL should never commit any act that he or she would not willingly disclose to any other member of the OoL, or even the public at large.
On Evil
The OoL distinguishes between two kinds of evil: the anti-social and the social.

Anti-social players are the sort of vile creature that should be exterminated from Norrath, and the OoL will co-operate with any social organization (including evil guilds) to that end.  The other kind of evil character is better viewed as an unfortunate accident of nature. Dark Elves, Trolls, Ogres, Shadow-Knights, Necromancers, etc., are the result of karmic imbalance operating on helpless individuals.

Sadly, the best known cure for these afflicted individuals is to return them to the Well of Souls and hope that their next re-incarnation is not flawed. Other individuals (such as Rogues) can often be turned from the path of evil even in this lifetime, by combining appropriate rewards and punishments. Strangely, it seems that showing kindness and honor to even the worst Dark Elven monster increases the chances that its next reincarnation will be good. Or perhaps not so strange... the path of Good is hard but fruitful. To that end, we wage a war of extermination against evil, but an honorable war.
