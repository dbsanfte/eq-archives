These members are the lifeblood of the Order of Light.

Adjucator
These are the ordinary members of the OoL. Aside from behaving in an acceptable manner, their duties are light:
  • above 10th level, to help one new player a week.
  • They are also expected to enforce the Judgments of the OoL, to the extent of their capabilities.
  • They are also expected to vote on those few matters that come before the guild.
  • Adjucators are inducted by an Officer of the guild, after having convinced the Officer that they are worthy. Potential Adjucators should refer to the Joining Page.
  • Adjucators are removed (ejected from the guild) by Officers for misconduct or other offenses. They may also request to be released from the OoL at any time. The Officer that inducted a member may remove them at will; other Officers should seek a judgment from the Inquisitors before removing a member.
Justiciar
It is known that some individuals have surrendered the protection of the Gods and made a compact with the Priests of Discord. These individuals are outside of the normal laws. However, the OoL expects its precepts to be met even there. To that end, the Justiciars also surrender their protection, so that they might bring law to the lawless. Their other duties are largely unaffected; they may also hold an office. The Justiciars are responsible for enforcing the Hunt. Justiciars are self-appointed: by entering the compact with the Priests of Discord. In all other regards, they are the same as Adjucators.
Officers
Offices in the OoL are positions of responsibility. This means they entail work, but not power or privileges. Any Officer may choose to resign and join the ordinary ranks at any time.
Subaltern
The Subaltern's duty are the ordinary members of the OoL. They assist and administer the ordinary members, settle disputes, organize parties to carry out the Judgments, and recruit new members. They are generally responsible for the members they induct, and will be expected to repair their own mistakes (thus, each Officer is encouraged to determine their own criteria for inducting members). Before removing a member inducted by another Officer, however, they should seek a judgment from the Inquisitors.  Subalterns are appointed and de-appointed by the Captain, as he or she deems necessary.
Inquisitor
The Inquisitors substantiate charges brought against individuals. To be named Inquisitor is a singular honor, because it requires an unquestionable integrity. Inquisitors must be able to distinguish between the true and the false, between the emotional and the factual. When an individual is charged with a crime, the Inquisitors are charged with confirming it. To fail to confirm a crime is to possibly allow a criminal to go free; but to falsely confirm a crime is to destroy the record, the integrity, the honor of the entire OoL.

Inquisitors, of course, make no distinction between investigating a member of the public and a member of the OoL.  Inquisitors also recommend a punishment for the crime they have investigated. The Captain may suggest an alternate punishment. The punishment is confirmed by a vote of all Inquisitors and the Captain (each voter may also cast a "Vote for Acquittal"; if acquittals achieve the majority, no punishment is confirmed). Once confirmed, any member of the OoL may carry out the punishment. Inquisitors may also act as a Subaltern, although they are not required to.

Inquisitors must be nominated by the Captain, and must be confirmed by vote by at least 3/4's majority of the OoL. Inquisitors can only be removed from their position as the result of a Judgment.
Secretary
The Secretary maintains the web pages, and conducts and records elections. The Secretary may appoint Scribes to perform specific functions of the Secretary's post. The Secretary may also act as a Subaltern, although not required to. The Secretary is self-nominated, and elected by majority. The Secretary must stand for election at least once every six months.  The Secretary can only be removed from the position as the result of a Judgment (or losing an election).
Captain
The Captain is responsible for leadership. Since Subalterns are the recruiters for the guild, the Captain's choice of Subalterns will determine the character of the guild. The Captain appoints and de-appoints Subalterns, nominates Inquisitors, suggests and votes on punishments, and calls for elections. He or she may call for a Captain or Secretarial election at any time, provided that it has been at least one month since the last election for that post, and not more than six months. The Captain calls for an Inquisitorial election by nominating a member to that office.

The Captain must stand for election at least once every six months. By accepting the office of Captain, an individual explicitly agrees to be bound by these by-laws in all particulars. Failure to do so is grounds for automatic removal.  The Captain is elected by a majority vote. The Captain can only be removed from the position as the result of a Judgment (or losing an election).

The Captain or any Inquisitor may suggest a modification (including eliminations and additions) of the by-laws.  The Captain and all of the Inquisitors must approve the change by a 3/4s majority. If they do so, then the change is submitted to a general election of all the members, where it must again achieve a 3/4s majority for final approval.
