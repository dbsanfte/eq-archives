Modifying the By-Laws
 The Captain or any Inquisitor may suggest a modification (including eliminations and additions) of the by-laws.  The Captain and all of the Inquisitors must approve the change by a 3/4s majority. If they do so, then the change is submitted to a general election of all the members, where it must again achieve a 3/4s majority for final approval.
Glossary
Voting
  1. The Secretary will conduct official elections. Generally, this will involve each member recording his or her vote with the Secretary. This may be by voice, by email, or via web page, at the Secretary's discretion. Votes involving more than nine members may not be conducted by voice.
  2. Elections that result in a tie are discounted, and the election must be held again.
  3. A quorum is 1/2 (one-half) of the eligible electorate. Thus, for any election to count, 1 out of 2 of the members eligible to vote in that election must record a vote. Each member must be given a reasonable amount of time to register a vote. If, after that time, insufficient members have voted, the Secretary may eliminate non-voting members from the official voting role until a quorum is obtained. Membership in the guild is not affected in any way by this process. However, if a member misses three elections in a row, he or she may be permanently removed from the guild by the Secretary on the grounds of de facto retirement. Such members may re-join the guild in the ordinary fashion.
  4. To participate in an election, a member must be inducted and have filled out the web page Join form before the election is actually begun. If a member leaves during an election, their vote will not be counted. Alternate characters cannot vote.

Judgment
A judgment is a punishment confirmed by the Captain and Inquisitors. The Secretary will see that all current judgments are displayed on the Judgment page. Two special kinds of judgments, War and the Hunt, are discussed below.

War
A guild war can only be instituted as the result of a judgment. Thus, to wage war, an Inquisitor must investigate a complaint and return a recommended punishment of War, which must be confirmed by vote of the Captain and and Inquisitors. Once declared, a War may only be terminated by the Captain. Any rules of engagement (such as excluding certain individuals on either side, limiting the war to a particular zone or zones, etc.) are the exclusive domain of the Captain, and may be changed at any time.
A war necessarily involves all members of the OoL. Because of this, the Captain will naturally exercise extreme discretion in declaring War, and will make every reasonable attempt to exempt those members that do not wish to participate.

The Hunt
The Hunt is a punishment of extreme prejudice. It only applies to PvP characters, and thus can only be carried out by the Justiciars. Hunting a character means systematically tracking down and killing that character until experience loss reduces them to fifth level. A Hunt can only be initiated as the result of a judgment; once initiated, it may only be disbanded as the result of a judgment. It is understood that the Captain and Inquisitors will consult the Justiciars before deciding any matter concerning a Hunt.
