Druid from 1-50 :

Choosing a race:

When choosing to be a Druid, keep in mind of what this means to your fellow players - you are not only someone to group with, but someone who will get a /tell every 5-7 minutes for a SoW, a Teleport (taxi service basically), or some extra food :)

First thing you have to look at when picking a Druid is the Wisdom - the Druid is the only class that has trouble getting to the maximum (200) Wisdom later in the game. Now since your mana is determined by your Wisdom, you want it to be as high as you can make it. Keep in mind that your Wisdom can actually go to 255, but after 200, you only increast 5% or so of the stat booster - so if you hit 200, start looking for ways to increase another stat (like Agility). 

The one Druid I would never choose by looking at the stats is the Half Elf - he has the lowest amount of WIS. Now the Human is only 5 below the Halfling and Wood Elf, but keep in mind that he also can not see at night - big problem when your trying to powerlevel (a full day in a game of EQ is one hour, so for at least 25 mins of each hour, you will not be able to see things around you - I don't care how big of torch you have) :) This leaves us with the Wood Elf and Halfling...which both have perfect stats for a Druid, and you can choose either and do just fine. I recommend the Halfling of course due to the fact that he gets sneak as an extra ability - where the Wood Elf's extra ability is starting at forage skill 50 (which is nice for newbie levels, but if you level quickly, you will be able to get forage up quickly as well by using it as a Halfling). Now for the ladies out there (no, I am not talking to all you EQ transvestites who play women on EQ), I know that the look of the character can be just as important (if not more) then the stats (it is for my girlfriend anyway - she would only play a wood elf or high elf), so stick to the Wood Elf if you wanna stay attractive :) With your extra 30 skill points, max out your Wisdom (25) and put the rest in Agility (a small increase in AC).


Levels 1-4

So if you are following the guide so far, you have created either a Halfling or Wood Elf now. Doesn't matter if you are in Greater Faydark or Misty Thicket - you have one mission here, and that is to go out and fight, and fight like a dog! Before I send you out however, let me give you the first level of spells and what each does:

Name of Spell Mana Effect Type Details
Flame Lick 7 Fire Based Single Target Direct Damage Evo Inflicts 5 points of fire based damage on the target
Snare 15 Target: Reduce movement rate (41% - 55%) Alt When a target is damaged with a spell, there is no additional chance for snare to break as there is with root.
Minor Healing 10 Single Target Instant Health Replenishment Alt Heals target for 10hp
Skin like Wood 10 Single Target AC, HP buff Abj Adds 3 to AC, and 10 HP (+1hp per level)
Burst of Flame 7 Single Target Damage over Time Evo Does10 damage, reduces AC by 4
Lull Animal 10 Lower Animal's Agro range Alt Great for pulling one animal or other mob when nearan animal to Lull
Panic Animal 10 NPC Animal Fear Alt This will make an animal run away from you in fear (it will come back to fight after time)
Endure Fire 20 Adds 20 to SV Fire Abj Great spell for increasing your SV Fire, but wont be needed until later levels of course
Dance of the Fireflies 10 Conjures up a Ball of Light Con Great if you don't already have a lightstone, but will probably not need if you have night vision
Sense Animals 5 Point you towards the nearest animal Div Not sure you will ever use this spell, some like it to find a near animal as a newb to find a kill.

If you are just starting off on this character and low on cash, stay focused on buying the first 5 spells in this list, the others are really not important for now. I would start with either Flame Lick or Burst of Flame so you have some sort of damage spell, then work your way into snare, healing and the AC buff. All of these spells are found on NPC vendors (except Sense Animal and Panic Animal, those you need to buy in either Surefall, Rivervale or Kelethin) - the rest are at any other Druid Guild. This next part is very simple...in whatever town you start in, run out near the town and just start fighting - swing your club, use your spells, do whatever it takes to just keep killing. After a few fights, heal up and grab some mana, then do it again...NEVER RUN AWAY. There will never be a need to run in the other direction when working towards level 5. Death has no penalty at these levels other then having to go grab your body (and then only if you actually had something worth picking up). So just do what I do, run around, kill those rats, bats, snakes, pawns, etc until you are near low health - the more you kill, the quicker you level, the quicker you get a new batch of spells! Once you hit 3-4, start moving out a little further to find harder things to fight, even if they are even to you. If you are then able to have all of the 5 spells I mentioned, killing mobs that are even to you should be very simple :)
