Must be aware of EQ system requirements: P 166 or greater, 3-D Card, and 32 MB's of Ram

All members must download the ICQ program so that we will be able to get in touch with you.

Although mIRC is not required, many guild meetings are held in it, so it would be beneficial if you also download it.

All applicants must go through several steps in KP's joining procedure in order to become a member.

All members must have †KP†, -KP-, +KP+, KP, of KP, or of the KP ( only one ) at the end of their names.

All members are expected to be active and play frequently with the guild

Only allowed to be in one guild, self explanatory

While Playing EQ rules

Although there is no rule on which way you set your pk switch, all members are still expected to uphold a certain ideal of virtue and common sense in the way you control their vengeance...

Explanation of the rule above: don't go around looting, stealing, and pking. There is times when there is exceptions to these, but on most occasions, each person should be treated with what they deserve

Encourage all members to Roleplay with their character in EQ

Treat all members with respect, same thing applies to people you meet in the game

Guild colors and or clothing will be based upon on what the guild votes, depends on what guild options EQ has. (i.e. all of us have the symbol of a griffon on our chests, etc )

Alliances

All allies should be treated as if they were a member of KP themself, therefore the same rules apply... As for making an alliance, the guild leaders will do most of the talking and all the members can vote on whether or not we want a particular guild as an ally. Any problems with a current allies, whether it is a member of their guild doing something disagreeble or whatever, make sure that guild is aware of it ( both guilds ) so any problems can be resolved.

There is no set punishment for breaking guild rules, granted many rules may have exceptions, just remember think before you act. Each case is handled by itself and all members' input will be used as a deciding factor in the event a wrong doing of some sorts occurs.
