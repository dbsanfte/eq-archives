Chicken Thighs Wrapped in Bacon


4       Each          Chicken Thighs, bone in
8       Strips        Lean Bacon
4       Tablespoons   Lemon Juice
4       Teaspoons     Lawry's® Season Salt
1 1/2   Cups          All-Purpose Flour
2       Teaspoons     Salt
2       Teaspoons     Black Pepper
1       Teaspoon      Poultry Seasoning
1       Each          Paper Bag



When I went shopping for the ingredients for Ken's Chicken, I saw a package of Chicken Thighs and decided to try substituting the thighs for the breasts. As I started to prepare the thighs, I noticed a package of bacon in my refrigerator and decided to wrap the thighs in bacon. Boy did it turn out good and the dark meat of the chicken is less expensive.


Directions:

Pour 1 tablespoon of the Lemon Juice over each chicken thigh and then sprinkle 1 Teaspoon of the Season Salt on each one.

Put the All-Purpose Flour, Salt, Black Pepper and Poultry Seasoning in the paper bag. Put the chicken thighs, one at a time, in the bag and shake well, this will cover the thighs with the ingredients. Wrap each thigh with two strips of bacon, one strip at a time, and secure them with toothpicks. Place the thighs on a plate, cover it with aluminum foil and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preheat the oven to 450 degrees.

Place a rack inside a pan that has four sides, as there will be a lot of grease run-off. Put the chicken on the rack, skin side down, put them in the oven and immediately turn the oven down to 425 degrees. Bake at this temperature for 25 minutes. Turn the breasts over, and turn the oven down to 375 degrees and continue to cook for an additional 20 minutes.
