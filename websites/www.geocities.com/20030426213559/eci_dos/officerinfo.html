Officer Requirements 
  • The applicant for Officer must have been a member of the guild for at least 6 months or at the Guild Leader's discretion.
  • The Guild MUST have a need for an Officer position.  In other words, there has to be a open Officer position available.  There will be no persons promoted to Officer simply because a person wants to become one.
  • The applicant for Officer must be recommended by at least 3 Members.
  • Officers are expected to know a good deal about the game in order to assist new guild members.

Officer Duties
  • Officer's MUST abide by Guild rules.  (Guild Rules)
  • Officer's MUST have ALL his/her alternate Character's as members in the DOS.
  • Officer's must keep in contact with  the Guild Leader via email.
  • Officer's must have an ICQ number or a valid e-mail address for Officer meetings.
  • Officer's must host raids.
  • Officer's must settle/handle any inner guild disputes between regular guild members.
  • Officer's are expected to handle any dispute in private /tell not in guildsay.
  • Officer's must warn the members disputing in guildsay not to use guildsay to have a disagreement, to use private /tell.
  • Officer's are expected to give each member 2 warnings per offence.  The third warning warrants the Officer kicking the member out of the Guild.  Officer's are to notify the Guild Leader when ever giving a warning to a guild member and what the warning was about.
  • Officer's must seek help from the Guild Leader or other Officer's if they are having difficulty handling or deciding a dispute between Guild members.
  • Officer's must email the Guild Leader to inform him of what has conspired between members that lead to the decision of that person or person's termination as a guild member.
  • If Officer's disagree it must be done in /tell.  If a favorable conclusion cannot be reached, then each must email or discuss it with the Guild Leader, who will serve as a mediator and make a final decision, if one cannot be reached.
  • After an "invite" of a recruit, Officer's are to inform the new member or new alternate character that a Application is to be completed if that new guild member wants his/her character info and stats posted on the Guild Roster.  Without a completed Application the character's info and stats will not be posted.
  • Once notified that a recruit is ready for an "invite," Officer's are expected to track down that recruit to complete his/her's indoctrination  into the Guild.
  • Officer's are required to inform new members or recruits of the DOS web site address.  The site offers information about our guild that the new member may find useful.
