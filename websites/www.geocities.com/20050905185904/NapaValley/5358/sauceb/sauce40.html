Dave's Salsa



6           Medium        Tomatoes, quartered, 
                          seeded and divided
4 to 10     Medium        Jalapeno Peppers, seeded
1-32 oz     Can           Stewed Tomatoes, drained
1           Large         White Onion, chopped
3-4 oz      Cans          Tomato Sauce
1-14.5 oz   Can	Ro-Tel®   Tomatoes with Chilies, drained
3           Tablespoons   Garlic, Minced
2           Tablespoons   Lemon Juice
1/2         Cup	Apple     Cider Vinegar
To Taste                  Salt
To Taste                  Black Pepper
6           Pint Size     Mason Jars, with lids



A coworker gave me the basics for this recipe. A few things to note: A Yellow Onion will make the Salsa sweeter; by adding more Jalapeno Peppers and leaving the seeds and white pulp in them, the 'heat' of the Salsa will increase; also by using the Extra Hot Ro-Tel® Tomatoes you can increase the 'heat' of the Salsa. And finally, for a 'Thick and Chunky' Salsa, hand dice the Tomatoes and Onions while using a food processor will allow you to prepare a finer texture Salsa.



Directions:

In a large pot, combined the Tomato Sauce, Ro-Tel Tomatoes, Garlic, Lemon Juice, Apple Cider Vinegar, Salt and Black Pepper.


In a food processor, and one vegetable at a time, coarsely chop the Stewed Tomatoes and then half of the regular Tomatoes, adding them to the mixture.


In a food processor, and once again one vegetable at a time, finely chop the Onions, the remaining regular Tomatoes, and the Jalapeno Peppers and add them to the pot.


In a food processor, finely chop half of the quartered Tomatoes and add them to the pot. Coarsely chop the remaining Tomatoes and add them to the pot. Stir the mixture thoroughly and then put it into the Mason Jars.


Thoroughly stir the mixture, and then fill the Mason Jars with the Salsa.


Note: The Mason Jars should be sterilized according to the manufacturer's directions.
