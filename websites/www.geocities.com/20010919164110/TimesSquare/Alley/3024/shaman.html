THE SHAMAN




After many months of playing a wizard, I decided to create a barbarian Shaman character. I chose the barbarian race because I generally like to play characters with good alignment. This would not have been possible with an Ogre or Troll. It is such a wonderful class to play, that I now find myself playing my Shaman regularly at the expense of my wizard. The Shaman's extraordinary ability to buff, coupled with healing and tanking capabilities make it a very sociable class. Because he/she is so useful in a group setting, the Shaman seldom has trouble finding people to group with. When soloing is required, the Shaman has sufficient melee combat skills to successfully gain experience without having to die as often as the mage classes. The Shaman also has a varied and effective repertoire of spells to work with, as well as the ability to wear armor up to chainmail and wield blunt one and two handed weapons.

 

To date, there are only three Shaman guilds in Norrath. They are located in Halas, Oggok, and Grobb, being the cities of Barbarians, Ogres and Trolls respectively. The scarsity of Shaman quilds require that the Shaman make periodic trips home to acquire exclusively Shaman spells. For this reason, I recommend that, wherever possible, these spells be purchased in advance of level and deposited in the bank. This approach makes them accessible at a later time without requiring an arduous trip home. Since other spells are available at other class guilds, the Shaman has an easier time of purchasing them at any time.

The most important attributes for the Shaman are stamina (for hit points), wisdom (for mana) and charisma (for spellcasting). Shaman should train in the magick disciplines of Abjuration, Alteration, Channeling (Level 4), Conjuration, Divination, Evocation and Meditation (Level 8). They may train in Dodging at Level 15, Alchemy at Level 25 and may begin to specialize in the magick disciplines at Level 30. Spell research is not available to the Shaman class. At the present time, new spells beyond Level 24 are gained at Levels 29, 34, 39, 44 and 49. For a complete listing of Shaman spells beyond Level 24, I recommend that you visit The Shaman's Crucible web site. There you will find a complete listing of Shaman spells from Level 1 through 49, including magic discipline required, mana cost, spell duration, and effect descriptions. Since the site does not identify which classes share spells with the Shaman, I felt it would be useful to provide information on them. The benefit is twofold. First you know where you may purchase them and secondly, it helps you coordinate spellcasting with other classes that may be in your group at a given time.
