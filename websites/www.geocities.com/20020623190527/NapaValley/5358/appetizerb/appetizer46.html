Texas Size Nachos -2



6           Each        Flour Burrito Size
                        Tortillas, quartered
2           Lbs.        Lean Ground Beef
1/4         Cup         Chopped Onion
1-15 oz     Can         Spicy or Zesty Chili
                        Beans, undrained
1-1.25 oz   Package     Taco Seasoning Mix
1/2         Cup         Water
1-15 oz     Can         Refried Beans
4 1/2       Cups        Shredded Cheddar Cheese
1           Jar         Salsa
1 - 8 oz    Container   Sour Cream
                        Vegetable Oil for frying



Well, as usual, I can't leave well enough alone. This is a combination of the original Texas Size Nachos and the Taco Biscuit Cups. The meat mixture in the Taco Biscuit Cups is fantastic and the size of the nacho chips makes them easy to prepare, and eat.


Directions:

In a large skillet, brown the Ground Meat over medium high heat. When some grease starts to appear, add the onions. When the meat is cooked, drain off the excess grease.

In a mixing bowl, mash the Chili Beans using a Pastry Blender. Add the Beans, Taco Seasoning and Water to the meat mixture and mix well. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes or until the meat mixture is slightly thickened, stirring occasionally.

In an electric skillet, heat approximately 2 inch of Vegetable Oil to 350-degrees. Fry the Tortillas Wedges, a few at a time, in the oil until they are lightly browned and crispy. Drain the wedges on a paper towel.

Place the wedges in a single layer on a baking sheet. Top each wedge with a heaping tablespoon of the Refried Beans, then 2 teaspoons of the Salsa, and finally 3 tablespoons of the Cheddar Cheese.

Broil the wedges 4 to 6 inches from the heat until the cheese is melted, approximately 1 to 2 minutes: be extremely careful that the chips do not burn. Top each wedge with a dollop of Sour cream and serve warm.
