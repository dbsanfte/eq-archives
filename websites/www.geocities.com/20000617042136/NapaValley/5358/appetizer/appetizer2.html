Shrimp Toast




1     Pound         Precooked Salad Shrimp, 
                        Chopped Coarsely
2     Ounces        Pork Fat, Chopped Finely
1/4   Cup            Coarsely Chopped Bamboo Shouts
1/2   Cup            Coarsely Chopped Water Chestnuts
1/4   Teaspoon    Ground White Pepper
1/4   Teaspoon    Grated Fresh Ginger (Optional)
1/2   Teaspoon    Salt
1/2   Teaspoon    Sesame Seed Oil
1     Tablespoon  Finely Chopped Green Onions, White part ONLY!
1     Raw             Egg
1     Tablespoon  Dry Sherry (Optional)
1     Tablespoon  Cornstarch
1     Loaf            White Bread



This is one of the appetizers that was served in my Mom and Dad's Restaurant, the Kon-Tiki. I was the Second Cook and as a result I was responsible for working the Deep Fat Fryers. I have cooked a ton of these appetizers. They are excellent and the pre-cooked appetizers can be frozen and kept for at least 30 days.




Directions:

Place all the ingredients into a bow and mix well, by hand. I mean you must use your hands to get a complete binding of all the ingredient. Keep stir, wiping your hands off periodically, until the ingredients form a smooth, firm stuffing paste.

Spread the mixture onto a piece of white bread that has had the crust removed and layer them on a tray, separating layers with Wax Paper. For best results, refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours.

Pre heat your Deep Fat Fryer, or bring oil in a skittle to approximately 350 Degrees. Place the bread with the shrimp mixture in the oil, mixture side down to start. When they start to turn golden brown, flip the shrimp toast over and continue cooking until they have completely turned a Golden brown. Remove from the oil, place them on a paper towel to absorb any excise oil. Cut the shrimp toast from corner to corner forming triangles. Serve on a platter with Cocktail Sauce.
