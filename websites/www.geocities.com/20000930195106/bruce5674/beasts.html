Butcherblock Bestiary

The Butcherblock Mountains is full of a variety of wildlife, ranging from insects all the way to magic-using undead! This section details some of the enemies and creatures you can encounter, as well as loot, special powers, etc.

Dwarven Bandit:

These guys are the sworn enemies of the Miner's Guild 628. They are weak foes, but don't confuse them with the named bandits at the bandit camps, some of whom can reach up to level 14! These are often found running on the road between the Greater Faydark and the Crossroads. Loot consists of a few silver and rusty daggers, as well as Dwarf or Gnome meat (yuck). When you off one of these guys, your faction with Miner's Guild 628 and Stormguard goes up, but it drops with Butcherblock Bandits. Also, be advised that these guys can sometimes backstab and will aid eachother.

 

Lowland Basilisk

These are moderate level foes. They can cast an almost uninterruptable spell called Stone Breath, which paralyzes the victim for about 10 seconds. They can hit for up to 20 and are fairly fast runners. Loot is sometimes a Basilisk Tongue (sells for a couple gold with good fac), or a chunk of meat. These guys are found on the rolling terrain between the Crossroads and the Greater Faydark. No faction adjustment for killing.

 

Emeral Drake:

These are inoffensive dragonspawn that roam the same area as the Basilisks. They have a weak form of fire breath, and usually drop coins when killed. No faction adjustment is associated with them. I once had one drop one of the cheaper gems, but I believe it's a very rare drop.

Giant Bat

A much larger cousin of the small bats found outside Kaladim and in the Greater Faydark, these creatures are also much harder to kill. They hit harder too. Sometimes they'll drop up to 4 bat wings or pieces of bat fur. These roam the same area as the drakes and basilisks. They're good exp for the level though. No faction adjustment.

 

 

Giant Scarabs:

Another good expirience monster, the giant scarab doesn't carry much loot, but that which it does is good money. It carries the cracked carapace, egg sac, and pristine carapace (extremely rare drop) No faction adjustment

Goblins (Regular):

There's several subtypes of these roaming around. The weakest are the whelps, which are level 1. They sometimes drop containers, which are great loot for the level. The next step up is the regular gobling. these sometimes drop containers, rusty weapons, and cloth armor, as well as goblin war beads. They you have the warriors, who almost always drop beads. The most powerfull form of regular goblin is the Wizard. These guys hit hard with spells up to 8th level ability. They often drop cloth armor. Killing these guys raises your standing with Stormguard, Stormreapers, and Guardians of the Vale. It also drops faction with Clan Runny Eye.

Goblin (Aqua):

Bigger, meaner, bluer, and nastier than the regular goblins, these beasts often appear near the port. They can outswim almost anything. Their mages are just as powerful as a regular goblin mage, but the average aqua is much stronger than the average goblin. Their loot is similar to that of a regular goblin, with one exception. There is a rare spawn named aqua called Glubbsink. It is rumored that he has the Riptide Spear, a piercing weapon capable of casting Enduring Breath.

 

Krag Elders and Krag Chicks:

These are members of the local Aviak tribe. The Elders can reach level 12, but the chicks are around level 4 or 5. The Elders carry money up to several gold, as well as the odd rusty weapon. The Chicks I've seen are all weaponless, but hit hard for their level. From the adults you get Aviak Talons, and from the chicks you get Chick Talons. Killing these raises faction with Stormguard and lowers it with Krag.

 

 

 

Large Snakes:

The name says it all: it's large, and it's a snake. All you get off these guys is large snake skins, fangs, and the occasional egg.

Large spiders:

My personal favorite monster, these guys can be gold mines. They carry up to 2 spider silks, worth up to 2 gold each, AND sometimes spider venom glands, wich can be worth up to 4 gold. Even at the 18th circle, I still kill them if one wanders by. Be careful though, because the have weak poison, plus they aid each other.

Skeletons:

There's several varieties of these malnourished fellows in the Butcherblocks. There's your level 1, inoffensive decaying skeleton, the KOS-all, aggressive regular Skeleton (fixed spawn point), the timid dwarf skeleton, and the weak decaying dwarf skeleton. The regular skels sometimes carry gold and cloth armor, but the other types seem to be limited to bone chips and rusty weapons. No faction adjustment on any type.

 

Large Skunks:

The smell says it all. PO one of these guys and you'll get hit by Skunk Spray, which lowers your charisma by about 30 points. The glands themselves are often useful and can be sold for a couple of gold each, if you don't mind your pack smelling like a skunk for a while.

 

Chessboard Undead:

The undisputed king monsters of the Butcherblocks, these powerful undead that spawn at the Chessboard range from about level 8 (pawns) to level 14 (the king). All types but the pawn and rook have some form of magic power. Knights act as Shadowknights, but Bishops and the possibly the King cast as Necromancers. The only loot of any consequence (besides rusty weapons) is the rare Black Iron Crown, a very valuable piece of armor. No faction hits are associated with these, but EVERYONE is KOS, plus they feel no remorse about ganging up on one person. They will also attack any level, as I've had level 8 pawns attack my level 19 warrior.

 

There's a plethora of other creatures wandering the Butcherblocks. Don't believe for a minute that this list is anywhere near complete. I will be adding new creatures as time permits. There's rumors of ogres and orcs also being in this area, as well as a few other types of creatures.
