Donna's Pork Chop Casserole


4           Lean	Pork Chops
1           Medium	Yellow Onion
1           Medium	Green Bell Pepper
1           Cup         Uncooked Rice
1 1/2       Teaspoons   Salt
Dash	                Black Pepper
1 - 16 oz   Can         Diced Tomatoes
1           Cup         Water



A very Special Lady made this for me, the very first meal she ever cooked for me. She caught me sneaking into the kitchen for many, many tastes. Should you look up the definition of "Georgia Peach" in your 'Funk and Wagnels', you will see her picture next to it. My friends, this one is a 'keeper' and worth all the effort to make. This is one of the few recipes that I have not, and will not change. And a new rice recipe has be 'born' from it. Honey, I love you.!!!!!



Directions:

Brown both sides of the Pork Chops in a hot skillet. Remove them from the skillet and place them in a 9" x 9" casserole dish.

Cut Yellow Onion into 4 slices. Place 1 slice on each pork chop.

Cut Green Bell Pepper into 4 rings, removing the seeds and the white pulp. Place one ring on top of the onions. Sprinkle with rice, salt and the black pepper.

Mix the Diced Tomatoes with the water and add to the casserole dish. Cover the casserole dish tightly with aluminum foil and bake at 325 degrees for 1 1/2 hours. If the casserole is too moist, remove the aluminum foil for the last 20 minutes.

4 Servings.
