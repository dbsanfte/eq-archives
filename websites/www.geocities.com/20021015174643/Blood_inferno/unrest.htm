Tactics

Pulling Monsters

Monsters in this zone do not pull like most monsters do. If you do not stay within attacking range of the monster, then the monster will take an alternate route, trying to follow you. This increases the chance of your single pull gathering up friends. It also increases the likelihood of another group of stealing your pull. So, pullers be warned, trains are easy to start in this zone.

Pullers

The person pulling monsters for a group should have their buffs cast so that the very first buff slot is empty. By doing this, any root spell cast by any of the undead will appear in the first slot. This in turn will allow someone in the group to cast Cancel Magic to remove the root, without removing any of the other buffs. The range of the Cancel Magic spell is about 200 Feet, which almost reaches anyplace in the building.

Spells

Spells can be a good thing and a bad thing. Area of Effect Spells are definitely a no, no. Area of Effect Spells, of any power, will almost always pull monsters from the floor above and the floor below. And, when this happens, the monsters will find you and thank you in person for casting that spell. Moreover, on their way to find you, they will gather a welcoming committee to make sure you know what you did. So, please memorize Direct Damage spells, only.  I have seen too many calm battles go bad because someone used an Area of Effect Spell.

Spells that will cause monsters to run are a bad thing. Running monsters in a small zone like this will tend to bring more monsters with them when they come back. So, please no spells that will cause the monsters to run away from the group. On the other hand, Snare and Root work well, and should be used on anything that is attacked.

A very valuable spell in this zone is going to be Cancel Magic. If you do have this spell, then Memorize it. It can make the difference between life and death for the puller or group mates. A person should be designated a Debuffer, and they will be responsible for casting cancel magic on people that are rooted and NEED to be unrooted.

Healers

Due to the fast pace and amount of damage dealt, healers are a premium here. Each group should have a primary healer, and their job is simply to sit back meditate and heal.

Paladins should keep a reserve of half mana for healing. If the primary healer is Out Of Mana, and people are getting low on health, then you should step back and cast some healing spells.

Lay of Hands is a very valuable commodity. It should only be used as a last resort when all healers are Out Of Mana, and someone is less than a bubble of life.

Area of Focus

Primary

The rooms that are marked by the numbers 2 and 6, on Map 2, are going to be the rooms that we will try to take.  These rooms are the Barroom and the Tower Room. Several guides recommend having one group in each room to prevent monsters from coming from the other room.

Also, these rooms are slightly off the beaten path, and are not as prone to trains as most of the other places in the zone. If things do get a little out of hand, there is a quick exit out of each room, simply by jumping out the windows.

Secondary

If the Tower and Barroom are camped, then we should try to establish ourselves in a room on the Second or Third floor. Other rooms to try and camp and pull are the Reanimated Hand Room, The Basement and the Ghoul Magi Room.

Loot

These are the valuable loot items. There are two no drops that are marked by a zero value.

There are a number of words, armor pieces, and bronze weapons dropped here. So, to keep things fair, looting should be done in Alphabetical Order. Each person will loot until they have reached 3 Platinum in money and items. Words will count as 1 Plat. Armor pieces will count as 3 Plat. And, Bronze weapons will count as 3 Plat.

All items below will be rolled for by everyone there that is part of the Attack.

Monsters

This is what I believe to be an accurate accounting of the monsters and their levels. If you find anything in error, please submit a change. If you have any suggestions or recommendations, let me know.

One thing to note about this zone and the monsters is the way they follow you when you try to pull them. Most of the monsters will follow you for a short distance when you initially hit them for a pull. If you do not stay within striking distance of the monster, the monster will take a different path. By allowing the monster to take a different path, it gives him the chance to bring his friends. This is how a lot of trains get started.

Maps

Map1

Map 1 Legend

  1. Cave with Goblin Spelunker. Reached by swimming underwater. Typically spawns with Werebat, Skeletal Monk or Festering Hag.
  2. Gazebo where various low level MOBs spawn.

Map 2

Map 2 Legend

  1. Back Room where Reclusive Ghoul Magi spawns.

  2. Barroom where Undead Barkeep and Zombie of the Unrest Noble spawn

  3. Reanimated Hand Room.

  4. Tower Room.

  5. Tower Room.

  6. Tower where Undead Knight spawns.

  7. Main Basement Room where Werebats and Festering Hags spawn.

  8. Lava Pits and Trap, Walk through the lava here.

  9. Dwarf Room, where Garanel Rucksforth and Terrors spawn.

Also Noteworthy

  1. Kritchoff spawns in a closet on the Second Floor.

  2. Khrixs Abomination spawns when Kritchoff is given a Fire Beetle Eye.
