Neat Tradeskills and Hobbies


One of the primary goals of this site was to provite entertainment options until 'the next big thing'; with Velious, as with Kunark before it, this boredom has subsided somewhat for the time being. We have new areas to explore, quests to solve, and terrific items to get. Fortunately, Velious also gave us some new features, upgrades and much-needed fixes to the tradeskill aspect of the game. In an effort to mesh this roleplaying-based end of the game with the quirky focus of my site, I proudly give you my current project: an exploration of some of the very cool things now available in tradeskills. This category explores some of the neater things to do within the topic of tradeskills and hobbies, so not all will be listed nor elaborated upon. For anyone interested in tradeskills in general, or wanting a follow-up site to this page, two places to start researching are EQ Traders or Allakhazam.



Fishing and Tackle-Making: Did you know there are more than 20
types of zone-specific fish in the game? Not to mention some non-
icthyoid fishable items such as seaweed. And there are even quests implemented that force you to make use of this skill (such as a totem
quest from Cobalt Scars that requires said seaweed, and a quest in
Cabilis that requires a specific catfish). A great list of what can be caught during your free time in various zones can be found on EQ Traders. But the really neat thing you might want to try your hand at is tackle creation. Using components that are easily gatherable in any low-level zone, you can make some fairly amazing varieties of specialized bait. Now, to my knowledge, this bait does not currently have any advantages over vendor-purchased bait, but the uniqueness alone is worth the effort. To get started, visit brother island in Ocean of Tears (first stop on the boat from Butcherblock) and pick up a tacklebox kit and some standard lure components. Then, even with zero tailoring skill, you can kill spiderlings and combine two webs in a sewing kit to make each silk thread needed for the lures. After that, simply visit zone vendors or whack a few low-level mobs (rats, pixies, bats, etc.) to get your primary lure attachments. A fun list to check out the various types of bait that can be made, and recipes for each, can be found on Allakhazam. Take a day off from stressful powerlevelling to make bait and go fishing, and see how enjoyable this universal skill can be!

           Baking: Looking for something a little more useful? Then the new
           features of the baking tradeskill might make it the one for you. When
           the game first came out, baking was solely a role-player's skill; you
           could make many types of foods, and they DID have the benefit of
           often weighing less than vendor-purchased food items (a nice advantage for those of the monk class), but beyond that it was merely something for those with a bit of time on their hands. However, recently baking was upgraded to include several recipes for foods that, when eaten, give temporary stat or resist bonuses to the player. Granted, most of these foods are made from either racial meats or meats that are more difficult to procure (Velious wurms, wyverns, dragons), but for those looking for a unique and useful skill for all those formerly 'useless' looted meats, this definitely fills the bill. How about plus-3 to your strength and stamina stats? Try some pickled giant! Prefer boosting your resistance to poison and disease? Then munch a few Cobalt cupcakes! And now that players are able to right-click food and drink items to specifically consume them when they're ready, these stats are controllable by the player. Don't take a bite out of that hard-to-get dragon steak until you are READY for a plus-4 boost to all your stats. As you can see by the list given on EQ Traders Baking Recipes, there are many combinations available, depending on what stats or resistances a player is looking for at any given time. A tip for anyone considering starting baking: the easiest and fastest way to do it is to set yourself up in East Commons at the merchant near the West Commons zone who sells batwings. Then buy stacks of wings from her, and stacks of frosting (from a vendor near the auction tunnel) to make Batwing Crunchies until skill level of 42. After that, Fish Rolls made from stacks of batwings and stacks of fish (buyable at the barbarian village in West Commons) will quickly get you to over 100 and in prime position to make these specialized munchies we're talking about. So start saving those meats you loot, and check out some interesting and handy cuisine-making!

Tinkering: Like alchemy and poison-making, tinkering in EQ is limi-
ted to select players who chose a specific race or class; gnomes are
the only players in the game with this particular racial ability. Tinker-
ing is the production of useful mechanically-oriented items, and all gnomes start out with a skill of 50 in the trade. This means that upon creation, they're able to produce such things as collapsible fishing poles, compasses, and fireworks at a skill level of 'trivial'. To gain increases in the skill, however, players must advance in experience to level 16. At the 16th season, the real fun begins! Wanting to check into tinkering more, I made a gnome warrior and levelled her up high enough to begin tinkering in earnest, because there were two things that caught my eye:

     Undiscovered Recipes: The game is quite possibly full of them. It all
     started out with Gimblestan, the father of thinkering. His diaries on
     the subject are the basis for the skill, and provide amusinging stories
     that relate various recipes. However, it appears that several volumes
     are missing from the collection, and many players theorize that there
     are recipes yet to be discovered. In fact, two of the currently known
     tinkered recipes (powered gloves and, I believe, the catapult) are not
     listed in any of these books and were instead discovered by enter-
     prising players. Given the tremendous quantity of tinkering components
     available, including a variety for which the current purpose is not
     known (small and large power sources, force and frost modules, and
     numbered gizmos and sprockets, to name a few), I feel there are yet
     more such inventions to be discovered by players with the motivation
     and patience to try a hand at mechanical genius; for instance, because
     many of the known items replace intell-caster spells (invisibility, glimpse,
     underwater breathing), I feel there is a flying/levition device awaiting
     discovery, and I'm currently experimenting with component combinations
     to that end. For a list of components and their known uses (or unknown
     usage status), visit The Tinkering Compendium's Ingredients and
     Gnomish Curios pages.

     Watchman's Armor: Imagine you could have all the features and procs
     of some of the higher-level armor, without having to wait 50 levels for
     it, or trying repeatedly to get in on raids where the armor is dropped.
     Sound too good to be true? Well if you have begun your interest in
     tinkering, and made a gnome warrior or cleric to experiment with, then
     level 20 is all you need to proc some of the most cool armor in-game,
     tinkered Watchman's armor. The silvery, gem-inlaid pieces of this set
     of gear are not easy to make (most have a trivial level of well over 200),
     but consider the effects on them, if you have some time on your hands:
          Helm: See invisible
          Breastplate: Invisibility
          Greaves: Sow (tests show it's faster than jboots, even at level 20)
          Vambraces: Stackable 40% haste buff
          Gauntlets: +30 strength buff
          Bracer: Summons 'wrench' (no-rent weapon)
          Boots: +30 dexterity buff.
     Now, those crafty gnomes made two concessions for those who would
     attempt this armor: the pieces must be equipped to proc them, and the
     buffs are self-only.  So if you are not at least a level 20 gnome warrior or
     cleric, you will never be one of the chosen few using this fabulous
     armor. However, twinking to 20 is a small price to pay for the enjoyment,
     so consider giving it a try! I've already crafted the greaves, bracer and
     vambraces for my snazzy young gnome; screenshots, stats and related
     text are found here. A final note: Don't really have the time to reroll and
     twink, but want to get in on the tinkering action? Level to 16, cap skill,
     and make the stuff. On my server and many others, it currently sells for
     20-25,000pp per piece of armor. Whoa, pad that bank account! Find out
     more at The Tinkering Compendium's Recipes section or Ak'Anon Bar
     messageboard.
