Red Beans and Rice


1     Pound       Red Beans, Soaked for 
                  approximately 4 hours
1     Medium      Yellow Onion, peeled and 
                  chopped
1     Bunch       Green Onions, chopped
7     Cloves      Garlic, peeled and chopped 
                  fine
1/2   Cup         Parsley, chopped
1     Rib         Celery, chopped
1/2   Cup         Catsup
1     Medium      Sweet Green Bell Pepper, seeded 
                  and chopped
1     Tablespoon  Worcestershire Sauce
2     Teaspoons   Louisiana Hot Sauce
2     Whole       Bay Leaves
To Taste          Salt
To Taste          Black Pepper
1/2   Pound       Smoked Sausage, cut into bite size pieces

4     Cups        Cooked Rice



I am one of Jeff Smith's (The Frugal Gourmet) biggest fans and have watch him for years. I own several of his cook books and this recipe came from one of them, although I have modified it to meet the tastes of my family.



Directions:

Drain the beans and put them in a 6-quart heavy pot and add 3 quarts of fresh water. Cover and simmer for 1 to 2 hours, or until the beans are tender. Watch that the water does not boil down too far and add more water, as necessary, to keep the beans covered in water at all times.

When the beans are tender, add all the other ingredients except the Cooked Rice, to the pot and cover with water, if needed. Simmer partially covered for 1 1/2 hours or until the liquid has thickened.

Serve over the cooked rice.
