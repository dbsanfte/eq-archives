A SYNOPSIS OF THE UO REPUTATION SYSTEM








The UO reputation system has taken many months to develop and will generate substantial changes to how the game is played. Due to its impact, I will try to highlight the most important aspects of it. I strongly urge that you read all relevant documentation at the Origin Site "owo.com".

The reputation system in its entirety relies on two major factors. These are Karma and Fame. While karma keeps tabs on the level and degree of your alignment with good or evil, fame represents the level and degree that you are known for your deeds,(good or bad), in the world of Britannia.

Karma may be raised by philanthropic and/or heroic actions such as charitable contributions to the downtrodden (beggars), or killing evil creatures and players, etc. Conversely, it will be lowered by killing good creatures and players, casting beneficial spells on evil creatures, PCs and NPCs, stealing or provoking creatures to attack innocent players. The game does not show the numerical value of karma.

As with karma, fame may be raised by philanthropic and heroic actions. Unlike karma, evil and despicable acts will also increase fame. Your actions, regardless of alignment, make you better known throughout the Britannian world. Your fame will be increased by giving away gold to NPCs, killing creatures and players with higher fame than yours, completing escort quests, etc. Fame will be lowered by 10% when you die. It also has a chance of decreasing by 1% every four hours of gameplay. In other words, fame has to be continuously maintained.

A character's level in fame and karma manifests itself by the title assigned to the him/her. Example: Siegurd of Yew begins UO as a neutral newbie. His paperdoll will show his name as "Siegurd of Yew." As he progresses within the game he falls on hard times. Through his actions he loses a little karma and gains a little fame in losing that karma. His paperdoll now reads "The Disreputable Siegurd of Yew," and so forth.

Another important aspect of the reputation system is the colors you see as you move the cursor over other players, NPCs, monsters, etc. For this reason, they are provided below:

Blue - Innocent

Grey - Aggressor or Criminal

Red - Murderer

Green - Fellow Guild Member

Orange - Rival Guild Member


TERMS DEFINED

Aggressor - A player who directly attacks or attempts to steal from another player. He/she becomes an aggressor to the intended victim and the victim can retaliate without penalty.The aggressor flag generally lasts approximately two minutes which can be extended if you damage another player.

Criminal - A player who exhibits antisocial behavior and who has committed crimes in Britannia, including looting bodies.The criminal flag will generally last for 2 minutes. However, if you stole from a blue player, you remain a criminal for life, (until you die).

Murderer - A player who has killed five or more innocent people. Damaging an innocent player who dies also gives him/her the ability to report you. Too many reports can flag you as a murderer. Murderers will be pardoned for one victim each eight gameplay hours. However, if another murder is committed during the eight hours, the time period towards pardon is reset. Guards kill murderers on sight, a bounty is placed on their heads, vendors will charge prohibitive prices to them and dying as one will result in skill loss commensurate with number of kills.

Innocent - A player who has not transgressed or acted against anyone. An innocent will be shown as blue when the cursor is over him/her.

According to Origin, a player may attack anyone who is not blue with no reputation loss. However, the penalties for attacking blue are stiff indeed. Because the notoriety system is so complex, I again urge that you read the complete documentation at the Origin site.
