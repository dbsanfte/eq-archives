  • Me - Hi, my name is Jan Stephan Lundquist. People call me Steve since it's usually too much trouble to teach them how to pronounce my name correctly (and it's difficult to get it right without a Swedish accent). I've tried to make this page easy to navigate. Each paragraph begins with a link to another page that details that subject more fully. I hope you enjoy looking at my page as much as I did creating it. As always, I accept constructive feedback on any page I've designed.

  • Family - I'm married to a wonderful woman named Brooke. We have a small daughter named Megan. Brooke and Megan rate as the two most amazing women I know. I love them both dearly.

  • The Air Force - I am an Air Force Major stationed at Offutt AFB, in Omaha, Nebraska. I fly the C-21A (military version of the LearJet 35). All I can say is that I love to fly and hope to do that for the rest of my career!

  • Swords - I have an all consuming interest in collecting swords, armour, and more gaming materials. Since this is really expensive, I decided to make a company that sells these things at a bettter price than some. I called it Bright Blades (it's the name of my elven ranger's adventuring company, read on for more on that). Unfortunately I had to shut this company down due to all my other personal commitments and the demands of my "real" job (again, read on). Now I send people to Arms of Valor. One of my first swords was a Starfire sword that I use in live steel demonstrations with my friend Jason. It's all beat up now, but still servicable. I also have the largest Web Ring on the net dedicated to Swords. Click HERE to get the scoop on joining this ring.

  • AD&D - I have more hobbies than I'd care to name, but here goes anyways. I've always been an avid AD&D player. I started playing in 1979 when I was 13 years old, and have tried to stay active ever since then. My favorite campaign is the Forgotten Realms setting. I really like the third edition rules. My favorite character right now is an elven ranger named Larian Le'Quella.

  • Dark Age of Camelot - Okay, it seems that there are a lot of REALLY COOL games out there. This game will seriously compete with EverQuest, at least they won my patronage! (Verant could learn a few things about Customer Service from Mythic!). I am trying to build a good resource here. I think between the links and my guild page (Armyn ab Treanid), I have something good for you.

  • The S.C.A. - I am also into the Society for Creative Anachronism (SCA). This is where we dress up in actual armour and fight with rattan weapons. It's a lot of fun, and a good way to get aggressions out.

  • Highlander - A collectible card game that I used to play. The company that created it, TCG, killed the game (and still owe me $1000!), but These Folks have revived the game a bit.

  • My Opinions - I just thought that the Web would be a great place to post my opinions on several subjects. I'm no Dennis Miller, but I think my "ranting and raving" says a lot of really good and important stuff. Of course feel free to disagree with me! (I know, I know, I still haven't posted anything good here yet! Give me a while to put it all together.)

  • al Brindisi - I am currently deployed to Italy on a NATO assignment, and found this place. It is probably the singularly most amazing place I have ever been! I have a wonderful family, I fly airplanes for a living, and love my life, but there are some things about what Federico does that I envy. That is high praise indeed!

  • Another hobby of mine is homebrewing. I've always enjoyed a good brew. Along with a good brew, I like to light up a good cigar. I also like to try new foods, and to go along with the food, good wine. It's a miracle that I'm not 300 pounds with the things I like to eat. My wife will be posting some of my favorite recepies if you're into that. Some other hobbies include rugby, camping, flying, computer games (the bloodier the better!), and science fiction.

Well, that's a little bit about me. I'll update this page whenever I have a chance, or when something new in my life pops up.
