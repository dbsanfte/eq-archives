Roleplaying a Dwarf

Dwarves can be a fairly easy character to roleplay, depending upon your tastes. One must remember to fit one's character to his or her class. For example, you aren't going to see a paladin running around murdering people, looting, and sacrificing babies. Here's a few general personas and guidelines that I've found to be good for an interesting roleplaying experience:

General Guidelines: Dwarves tend to be stubborn people, rooted much more in reality than most other races. They can seem to be biased against other races or classes, magic users in particular. They often find other races to be a bit vexing to deal with (Elves because of their tendency to dawdle and think too much, Humans because they make rash decisions and change their minds too often, and Halflings because of their propensity toward practical jokes and stealing, etc.). Dwarves who have traveled much, however, are often far less extreme in their biases, having seen and heard much more than their kinsmen. Dwarves of all types are comfortable in the presence of Gnomes, who share their affinity for the earth. They are still somewhat afraid of the magic-users though. Dwarves (with the notable exception of Rogues) tend to hate practical jokes or horseplay, instead preferring an orderly society. Dwarves also tend to be afraid of the sea or other large bodies of water, and suffer from seasickness much more than other races. Perhaps this is because they consider the sea to be the ultimate symbol of chaotic change

Paladin: You're the knight-in-shining-armor type. Never hesitating to help other good players, you're always the first one into combat against evil creatures and the last one out. You are absolutely fearless in the face of danger. You are also a healer; use your power to help the sick and injured. You will also gladly sacrifice yourself if it means your friends will escape. Your sense of honor and fair play is unmatched, and you never run from a battle, no matter the odds. When dueling, you refuse all help, determined to stand or fall on your own. You also do not like magic-users at all, considering their arcane powers to be unholy. You also tend to be suspicious of rangers and druids, although their nature-based powers are more palatable. Many other races and classes see you as intolerant, which is quite true.

Alternate persona: You are a sad and dour individual, who's faith is wavering in the face of unending struggles against evil. You are becoming more and more convinced that you are fighting a losing battle. You struggle onward more out of a sense of inertia than out of faith. Your loyalty to your friends is still strong however, and you will fight to save them. Perhaps you will lose your faith, or maybe your adventures will lead to a reaffirmation.

Alternate persona: You are the reluctant (but devout) warrior, joining the ranks of the Paladins more to help others and save souls than to do battle or vanquish evil. Some consider you a coward, but others see you as you truly are. You're more interested in converting followers of other religons than fighting, and are more comfortable in the company of Clerics than your fellow Paladins. You will try to avoid combat, instead preferring negotiation, but when forced to fight, you do so with terrifying (and suprising) skill and viciousness.

Alternate Persona: You are a Crusader, traveling afar to protect the weak and innocent, and to destroy the evil and vile races that threaten all creation. You enjoy the company of others, and are more tolerant than other Dwarves (due to long years on the road), but where evil is concerned you are ruthless. Since you are far from your church and often fighting evil, you tend to collect more wealth than is usual for a Dwarven Paladin, but you spend sparingly, giving most of your assets to the Clerics of Underfoot whenever you are home.

Warrior: Your bloodlust for the enemies of the Dwarves is unending. You feel no remorse for killing an orc or ogre, even children. You are, in short, a killing machine and sociopath, completly detached from your actions and how they affect others. Many people, especially Paladins and Clerics, see you as being as bad as the enemies you fight. Magic-users distrust you, and the feeling is more than mutual. Your only loyalty is to your race and home, your only joy is butchering your enemies.

Alternate Persona: You are a mercenary, a sell-sword of the highest order. You will fight for anyone if the pay is good and the job agreeable. You try to keep your interests and the interests of your employer seperate, but it is difficult sometimes. You won't work against other Dwarves unless there is a VERY good reason, as you do have a sense of honor. You have also learned quite a bit of blacksmithing, as you have often not had the money to pay for repair of your weapons.

Alternate Persona: You are a young, hot-blooded member of the Stormguard, who sees being a warrior as his path to fame and prominence. You never back down from a challenge, even what you're grossly outmatched, due to your impossibly large ego. You tend to get in almost as many fights with your allies as you do with your enemies. You think you're the deadliest warrior and most skilled swordsman the world has ever seen, and are a poor loser when proven wrong. Plunging headlong into a fight, without any thought or consideration of tactics, is your hallmark manuver.

Alternate Persona: You are the grizzled veteran of many battles. The life of an adventurer has not been easy, but it has been rewarding, gifting you with a wealth of knowledge and expirience. You are not quick to take offense, but will gladly fight if the reason is good enough. You do not look for trouble or try to prove yourself a deadly warrior. You have an air of quiet, reliable confidence and skill about you, supported by years of experience. In battle, you use your wits and intelligence, considering them as much of a weapon as your blade. You go for magic users first in any battle. You try to steer your younger compatriots out of trouble, but believe that experience is the best teacher, and will not force them to do anything, unless is has dire consequences. While you still fear magic users, you also respect them, due to a lifetime of seeing their power.

Rogue: You're the merry Rogue. You are a seasoned traveler, and prefer to make your living by your wits rather than by your labor. You're charming, witty, and clever. You won't steal from those who are poor or in more sorry shape than you, but the wealthy had better keep a watch on their purse strings. You have no problem with taking young, inexperienced adventurers under your wing, but many others look upon this with trepidation. You are also much more at ease with other races and classes. You are more fascinated by magic than afraid of it, and so find magic users to be agreeable company. The inverse is not true, however, as you tend to want a "closer look" at spellbooks and valuable magical components. You also do not suffer from seasickness as much as most other dwarves, finding your fellows discomfiture to be somewhat amusing. You're also a skilled gambler, and enjoy games of chance, especially when you can swing the odds in your favor.

Alternate Persona: You are an assassin: cold, efficient, and deadly. You feel no compunction about stabbing your target in the back with a poisoned weapon. You are a lurker in the shadows, a predator of the night. You hire yourself out to the highest bidder, the only thing of importance to you being to get the job done, regardless of it's nature. You have no remorse, conscience, or ethics to speak of.

Alternate Persona: You are an outcast from Dwarven society. You were once a member of the Miner's Guild 628, but you couldn't resist the temptation to prey on your fellow Dwarves. Any other members of 628 are sworn to kill you on sight, so you tend not to go near Kaladim or the Butcherblocks. You instead roam the world, making a meager existence by preying on others. Anyone, regardless of who they are, is a target.

Cleric: You are a devoted follower of Brell Serilis, Lord of Underfoot. Every action you take is devoted to furthering His glory. You often try to convert followers of other religons, often to their annoyance. Other churches sometimes fear you for your fervor, and may take action against you. You are sworn to heal the injured and the sick, regardless of their religon. The only people who you will not aid are evil races, shadowknights, and necromancers. You wage unending war against the followers of Veeshan, Bertoxxulous, Cazic-Thule, and Innoruuk. You sometimes find companionship with followers of Rodcet Nife, as your oaths and beliefs are very similar. In battle tending to your injured comrades is your primary concern, and you view slaughter of the wounded or innocents to be crimes of the highest order.

Alternate Persona: You feel nothing but disgust for your fellow Clerics. You joined the church as a path to wealth and power. You are more devoted to enriching yourself than to helping others. While you hide your true motives well, others are beginning to suspect that something is amiss, and will take action against you should they find out your true aims. You have contacts among the dark races and classes, and will not heistate to use them to remove obstacles from your path. You are vindictive towards those who thwart you, and find cruel vengenace to be particularly satisfying.

Alternate Persona: You are a missionary, traveling afar to spread the good word of Brell. You do not hate or distrust followers of other Deitys, but instead pity them, for you know it is only through devoting yourself to Brell that one can find true salvation. You travel often, with other adventurers as a defensive retinue, as you truly abhor violence. You will only willingly fight those you regard as evil and unsavable, or who wish to harm the innocent. You offer healing, food, and water to any who need them, and live off the generosity of others. Most of the treasure and wealth you collect is thithed to the Clerics of Underfoot.

If you have a suggestion for a persona, please send me an e-mail with your name, character(s)/server(s), the class the persona is for, and a description of the persona. I will try to add it as soon as possible, and will give you due credit.
