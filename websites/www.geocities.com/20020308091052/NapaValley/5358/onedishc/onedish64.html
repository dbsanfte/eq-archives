Tyler's Chicken Sandwich


4         Each    Boneless/Skinless Chicken Breasts
2         Large   Eggs, beaten
2         Cups    Progresso® Italian Bread Crumbs
1-14 oz   Jar     Ragu® Spaghetti Sauce
2         Cups    Shredded Mozzarella Cheese
6         Each    Hoagie Buns
                  Lettuce
                  Sliced Tomatoes
                  Vegetable Oil


While scanning the Internet for new recipes I read one and this just popped into my mind. I tried it for Tyler, the chicken lover, and he gave me two thumbs up. So it's a keeper!!



Directions:

Place the Chicken Breasts, one at a time, between two pieces of Wax Paper and, using a meat mallet, or a rolling pin as I do, flatten the chicken until the entire breast is approximately 1/4-inch thick. Cut the breasts in half and place them on a plate, cover them with aluminum foil and put then in the refrigerator for 10 to 15 minutes or until they are chilled. (Note: Chicken that has been refrigerated and is cold is much easier to work with than chicken at room temperature.)

Put the eggs into a bowl and beat then. Put the breadcrumbs in another bowl. Dip the chicken breasts in the eggs, coating both sides and then press them in the breadcrumbs. Place the breasts on a plate, again covering them with aluminum foil and then place them back in the refrigerator and let them chill one last time for 10 to 15 minutes. (This will allow the breadcrumbs to adhere better to the breasts)

In an electric skillet, place enough Vegetable Oil so that it is between 1/4-inch and 1/2-inch deep. Heat the oil to 350-degrees.

Place the breaded chicken breasts in the hot oil and cook 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and the chicken is completely cooked. Drain the breasts on paper towels to absorb the excess oil.

In a saucepan, heat the Spaghetti Sauce.

Cut the Hoagie Buns in half, making sure not to cut them completely through. Spread some of the Spaghetti Sauce on the bottom half of the bun and then put two chicken breasts on top. Spread the Shredded Mozzarella cheese on the top half of the bun. Bake in a pre-heated 350-degree oven until the cheese is melted.

Put the lettuce and tomato on top of the chicken, close the sandwich and cut it in half, on a diagonal.
