Dave's Beer Batter/Breaded Chicken



4   Each   Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breasts
1   Cup	   All Purpose Flour
1   Cup    Beer
2   Cups   Italian Bread Crumbs



Once I figured out how to bread chicken, without the breading following off, trying a batter with the breading was just a natural progression.


Directions:

In a large bowl, combine the Flour and Beer. Mix thoroughly until there are no lumps. Put the batter in the refrigerator for approximately 20 minutes.

Place the Chicken Breasts, one at a time, between two pieces of Wax Paper and, using a meat mallet, or a rolling pin as I do, flatten the chicken until the entire breast is approximately 1/4-inch thick. Place the breasts on a plate, cover them with aluminum foil and put then in the refrigerator for 10 to 15 minutes or until they are chilled. (Note: Chicken that has been refrigerated and is cold is much easier to work with than chicken at room temperature.)

Put the breadcrumbs in a bowl and remove the beer batter from the refrigerator. Dip the chicken breasts in the beer batter and then press them in the breadcrumbs. Place the breasts back in the refrigerator and let them chill one last time.

In a skillet, place enough Vegetable Oil so that it is between 1/4-inch and 1/2-inch deep. Over medium heat, heat the oil to approximately 350-degrees.

Place the breaded chicken breasts in the hot oil and cook 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and the chicken is completely cooked. Drain the breasts on paper towels to absorb the excess oil.

Place a breast on each roll and garnish with the Sliced Tomato, Lettuce, and Mayonnaise.
