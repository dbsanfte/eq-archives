Nagafen Raid Overview

Some Intro

Expect the raid to take about an hour from the time it starts. However, a Nagafen raid is usually preceded by a Fire Giant raid. You should read the overview on that to get some idea of how you'll get to the Nagafen portion. Once the Fire Giants are cleared, there is nothing left to do before Nagafen. The respawn rate on Fire Giants is sufficiently low that they won't be a factor if things take a bit, unlike the Ice Giants on a Vox raid. Also, the Fire Giant raid will leave you right at the staging area for a Nagafen raid.

The Nagafen raid essentially consists of a buffing circle, then a camp out to the chat room. When the command is given, everyone logs back in and the Charge! command is given.

Who Should Go

I believe 49+ folks. Lower levels can make a contribution, sometimes a significant one. However, they have lower resistances and a harder time hitting with weapons or sticking spells on the 55th level beastie.

Configuring Yourself

Resistances are of primary importance in tackling Nagafen. They are more important than your AC. If you are tempted to keep that great AC item instead of the great Fire Resist item, consider that Nagafen will only be clawing/biting you every once in a while; he will be hitting you with his AE fire attack continually.

Three types of spell effects will be hitting you: a fire attack, a fear, and a cancel magic. This means that you need both Fire Resistance and Magic Resistance. The former is the most important, but don't build it to the exclusion of MR (a FR of 200 won't help that much if you spend the entire battle running from the fear). Try to get both above 100 after buffing, with Fire Resistance significantly above 100.

There are a lot of items for raising resistances; you can browse one of the various databases, such as EQStratics, for them. For example, right now for Nagafen I use:

  • Head - Froglok Crown, +20 Fire
  • Neck - White Gold Necklace, +7 Fire/Magic
  • Face - Gold/Diamond Mask, +10 Fire +3 Magic
  • Ears - Earrings of Fire Reflection, +5 Fire each
  • Shoulders - Bloodstained Mantle, +10 MR
  • Wrists - Platinum/Emerald Bracelets, +10 Fire each
  • Fingers - Gold/Emerald Rings, +6 Fire each
  • Range Slot - Runed Bone Fork, +10 Fire/Magic
Turn off Run if you are a tank. This will prevent you from going too far when feared; you can then Run back. Casters have Run on; you may have a chance of running out of the room if Nagafen summons you.

Casters should bind back beyond the last turn before the door to Nagafen. Mages and Necros should duel to leave a corpse with pet reagents.

Minimize spam and lag by turning down the settings on your machine:

  • Particle Density: LOW
  • Level of Detail: OFF
  • My Misses: OFF
  • Others Hits: OFF
  • Others Misses: OFF
  • Attacker Missing Me: OFF
  • PC Spells: OFF
  • NPC Spells: OFF for tanks (casters may want to know when to run)
  • Bard Songs: OFF

Buffing Circle

The purpose of this is to provide the desired buffs to the participants in the desired order. You want the least-important buffs stripped by Nagafen first.

Everyone must remove all buffs before entering the circle--even if you already have a buff you know you are going to receive. This prevents buffs from running out prematurely, buffs not taking because you had too many active, etc.

Note about the following lists: the spell listed is the minimum spell to be cast in a series. If you have buffers of the appropriate level, and enough bards to keep them full of mana, then you can increase the level of buffs used:

Once you have completed the buff circle, immediately camp out to the Brell-Nagafen chat room. Once everyone is buffed and has medded back their mana, the order will be given to log back in. Log in immediately and drink your potion (if you have one). Pet classes summon pets and have one of the druids cast Resist Magic on it. Med up...the charge will be very shortly.

The Charge

Tank1 and Tank2 and the pets charge upon the command. Get all the way across the bridge, if possible, so that you fight him in the room. After 10 seconds (to minimize traffic jam and lag problems), Tank3 charges. When Nagafen gets to about 50%-60% health, the raid leader will give the command to the nuker group to open up.

Tanks - Dish out as much damage as possible. Don't fight defensively. Text can lag significantly. So, if you find yourself getting hit by Nagafen a couple times in a row, or if you get to 50% health, back out immediately for a heal. If your cleric is dead or has called OOM, run back to the buff group for a heal. Make a hot key that says "/shout XXXX coming back for heal" and hit it so they know you're coming and not just feared.

Bards - Don't fight. Take out your instruments and keep the group's Fire and Magic resistances up. This is the biggest contribution you can make.

Clerics - Heal. Heal some more.

Wizards - Hang back at the turn until it's time for the nuke groups. Don't call attention to yourself by casting. When the time comes, run in and chain cast Ice Comet. You must be in melee range to cast!

Mages - Stay out of range of the AE spell. Send in your Fire pet. Resummon as needed.

Necromancers - Stay out of range of the AE spell. Send in your pet. Resummon as needed.

Enchanters - Stay out of range of the AE spell. /assist off Nagafen and Rune IV the tanks.

Druids/Shamen in Tank Group - You are healers. Stay out of range of the AE spell if you can. Heal your folks, including the cleric.

Druids/Shamen in Buff Group - Hang back at the first turn and heal any tanks who come running back for heals. Heal clerics who need it. If the fight is going very well, when the time for nuking comes, shaman run up and cast your ice-based spells. You must be in melee range to cast on Nagafen.
