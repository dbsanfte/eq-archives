Apple Dumplings


4-6	Large	        Granny Smith® Apples, 
                        peeled and cored
2	Tablespoons	Sugar, Per Apple
To Taste		Cinnamon	
Regular Pie crust for a Double Crust Pie			

	Syrup Mixture

2	Cups	        Sugar
1	Cup	        Water
2	Tablespoons	Cinnamon
2	Tablespoons	Butter or Margarine



When I was much younger, my Grandmother would make these and I loved them. I did not have the opportunity to get the recipe from her, so when I saw in on the Internet, I immediately copied it. I hope these bring back fond memories for you as much as they did me.


Directions:

Roll out the dough, cutting it into four or six squares. Place a the Peeled, Cored Apple in the center of the pastry squares and sprinkle with the Sugar and Cinnamon, filling the core. Seal the pastry around the apple and place, seam-side down, in an 11" x 17" baking dish.

Prepare the syrup by putting all the ingredients in a pot, mixing and cooking over low heat until the sugar is completely dissolved. Pour the syrup over the prepared apples in the baking dish.

Pre-heat the oven to 350 degrees and bake the apples for 1 hour, basting occasionally with the syrup. Serve warm with a little vanilla ice cream.
