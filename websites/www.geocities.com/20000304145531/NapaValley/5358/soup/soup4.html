Cream of Vegetable Soup



1       Tablespoon   Unsalted Margarine
1       Large        Carrot, peeled and 
                     sliced thin.
1       Large        Potato, sliced thin
3       Cloves       Garlic, crushed
1                    Bay Leaf
1/4     Teaspoon     Dried Thyme, crumbled
2 ½     Cups         Low-Sodium Chicken 
                     Broth
2       Cups         Low Fat (2% milk fat) Milk
1/8     Teaspoon     Salt
1/8     Teaspoon     Black Pepper



This recipe was emailed to me my one of my "Grandaughters" from the Bourbon Street Chat Room. You may know her as "Pyrogirl" or "Andrea". But to me, she is My Grandaughter, and a Special One at that!!!


Directions:

In a medium-size heavy saucepan over low heat, melt the Margarine. Add the Carrot, Onion, Potato, Garlic, Leaf and Thyme, tossing to coat the vegetables with the margarine. Stir in 1/2 a cup of the Chicken Broth, cover, and cook for 15 minutes or until almost all the liquid hasevaporated.

Raise the heat to moderate, stir in the remaining 2 cups of Chicken Broth and the Milk, and cook, uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are very tender and the flavors well blended--about 30 minutes.

Cool the mixture slightly and strain it, reserving both the vegetables and the liquid. Discard the Bay Leaf. Place the strained vegetables and 1 cup of the broth in a food processor or electric blender and puree by whirling for 1 minute. Return the puree and reserved liquid to the saucepan. Add the Salt Pepper and bring to serving temperature over moderate heat--about 3 minutes.
