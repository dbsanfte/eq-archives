Shrimp Creole


4     Teaspoons    Oil or Butter
1     Large        Green Pepper, chopped
2     Sticks       Celery, chopped		
2     Medium       Yellow Onions, chopped
2     Cloves       Garlic, crushed
2     14 oz cans   Diced Tomatoes, reserving 
                   4 tablespoons of the juice
2     Each	   Bay Leaves
1     Tablespoon   Chives, chopped
1     Tablespoon   Dried Parsley
3     Medium       Green Onions, chopped
1     Teaspoon     Cayenne Pepper
1     Teaspoon     Louisiana Hot Sauce
1     Teaspoon     Worcestershire Sauce
1     Teaspoon     Salt
1     Teaspoon     Fresh Black Pepper
1/2   Teaspoon     Thyme
2     Tablespoons  Cornstarch or Flour
4     Tablespoons  Reserved Diced Tomato Juice
2     Pounds       Shrimp, uncooked



The exposure to Cajun food has been on of the many wonderful things that I have experienced since I moved to Texas. I have searched and searched for a good Shrimp Creole recipe and finally had to just create my own.



Directions:

Place the Oil, or Butter, in a large saucepan and add the Onions, Celery and Green Pepper. Cook a few minutes over medium heat until the vegetables are tender, the add the Garlic. Turn the heat down to low and let this simmer for approximately 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Add the Diced Tomatoes and the Juice, reserving 4 tablespoons, the Bay Leaves, Cayenne Pepper, Salt, Black Pepper, Chives, Green Onions, Parsley and Thyme and bring to a boil. Turn down the heat to low and allow this mixture to simmer for about 5 minutes, uncovered.

Mix the reserved tomato liquid with the cornstarch. Add this mixture to the saucepan and bring it to a boil, stirring constantly until it thickens. (If the mixture isn't thick enough, combined 2 tablespoons of water with two more tablespoons of cornstarch, mix thoroughly and then add it to the pot.)

Add the shrimp and cover the pan. Simmer over gentle heat for about 20 minutes, or until the shrimp curl and look pink and opaque.

Remove the bay leaves before serving. Spoon the Creole over cooked rice.

Variation: If using cooked, shelled shrimp, cook the tomato sauce mixture for about 15 minutes and then add the cooked shrimp to heat through.
