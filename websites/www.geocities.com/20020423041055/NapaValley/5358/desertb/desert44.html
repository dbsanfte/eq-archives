Cherry Truffle Squares


1-18 oz.   Package       Pillsbury® Refrigerated 
                         Sugar Cookies

           Filling

1/3        Cup           Semisweet Chocolate Chips
1/4        Cup           Butter or Margarine
1/4        Cup           Unsweetened Cocoa
3          Tablespoons   Light Corn Syrup
1          Tablespoon    Milk
2          Cups          Powdered Sugar
1-10 oz.   Jar           Maraschino Cherries,
                         drained and chopped

           Topping

1          Cup           White Vanilla Chips
2          Tablespoons   Crisco® Shortening

           Garnish, if desired

           Chocolate Shavings or Curls



I found this recipe on the Internet and all though I don't like to change a recipe the first time I make it, this one got changed by accident. I did not chop the Cherries and I increased the Chocolate Chips to 1 full cup. Hey, it was early Sunday morning and my eyes and brain weren't working well together.


Directions:

Cut the cookie dough in half, crosswise and then cut each section in half, lengthwise. With floured fingers, press the dough into the bottom of an ungreased 13" x 9" pan to form the base. Bake in a pre-heated 350-degree oven for 12 to 16 minutes or until lightly golden brown. Remove from the oven and allow it to cool for 45 minutes or until completely cooled.

In a medium microwave-safe bowl, combine the Chocolate Chips and the Butter. Microwave on HIGH for 1 to 2 minutes or until melted and smooth, stirring every 30 seconds. Add Cocoa, Corn Syrup and Milk and blend well. Add the Powdered Sugar and mix until smooth. Press this mixture over the cooled base. Top with the cherries, gently pressing them into the filling.

In a small microwave-safe bowl, combined the Vanilla Chips and the Shortening. Microwave on HIGH for 1 to 2 minutes or until melted and smooth, again stirring every 30 seconds. Spoon and spread this mixture over the filling. Refrigerate for 20 minutes or until set. Cut into squares and, if desired, garnish with the Chocolate Shavings.
