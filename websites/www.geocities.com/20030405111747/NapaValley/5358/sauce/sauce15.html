Jalapeno Corn Bread



"Why the cookbook?" That is a question I get from a lot of friends and family. Here is a prime example of exactly why. One weekend, I had some friends over for Chili dinner and to watch the Dallas Cowboys play. They brought the corn bread and when I asked for the recipe, cause it was wonderful, this is what I was told.


2	Bags        Corn Meal (who knows what size, just 2)
1	Hand Full   Finely Diced Yellow Onions (who's hands)
Some	            Baking Soda (she just knew when it was 
		    enough by looking at it)
Leftover	    Sour Cream (just what ever was leftover 
                    in the refrigerator)
To Taste	    Finely Diced Jalapeno Peppers.
2	Hand Fulls  Shredded Cheddar Cheese (again, 
                    who's hands)



Well I finally pinned them down, and here is the actual recipe. They were close, I guess.

1-6oz   Package      Martha White® Yellow Cornbread Mix
1	Large        Egg, beaten
1/2	Cup          Milk
1	Cup          Shredded Cheddar Cheese
1/3	Cup          Finely Diced Yellow Onion
1	Tablespoon   Finely Diced Jalapeno Peppers (seeded)
		    (Pickled Jalapeno Peppers can be 
                     substituted)
1/4	Cup          Sour Cream



Directions:

Combined all the ingredients in a large mixing bowl and mix thoroughly.

Spray an 6-cup muffin pan with Pam® Vegetable Spray. Fill the muffin cups 2/3 full. Bake in a 375-degree oven for 18 to 20 minutes or until golden brown.
