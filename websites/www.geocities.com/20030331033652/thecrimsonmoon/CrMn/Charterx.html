Charter for Crimson Moon



A Guild is more then a collection of friends. It is more then a support group in case someone needs
help. A Guild must have a purpose beyond these functions in order to provide cohesiveness, tolerance,
and entertainment for it's members where none would ordinarily exist. To that end, this Charter is
written to define what makes Crimson Moon unique as a Guild worthy of the loyalty and participation
of it's members.

Guiding Principle: The most fun we have while playing is in groups
Guiding Principle: Groups are best when each person is really good at their job.

Mission: To be the best group of Hunters on Seventh Hammer so that we have the most fun while we
play.

We accomplish this mission by doing the following things:

A. Wherever and whenever possible, grouping with members of our own Guild to assist each other in
the in the acquisition of items, money, and experience.

B. Teaching and discussing the roles of each character and character class.

C. Collecting and announcing information about Zones, Mobs and Items.

D. Developing and practicing methods for teamwork in combat situations.

All of this is possible only if each member agrees to be guided by the following principles:

1. Respect: I respect the worth and opinions of all other members.

2. Compassion: I help my fellow Guildmates before helping myself.

3. Esprit de Corps: I group with other members of Crimson Moon, teaching them what I know, and
learning what they have to teach.

4. Mirth: I remember that this is a game, and its object is to have fun. I strive to help make it fun for all.

5. Study: I constantly look for ways to improve and expand my function in the group.


Doing these things will result in a Guild we can be proud of. One that will be strong, well respected,
and fun to play in. What better reason to have a Guild charter, and what better reason to be a part of
Crimson Moon!

One of the most important tools used to reach our goals is the message board.
This is our tool for communicating outside of the game. Raids, news, views and more
can be shared on the message board. It is because the message board that we
require
every member to visit and post on the boards at a minimum of 1 time a week, though
more is greatly encouraged. Doing this will only benefit you.


Rules

There are always issues that will arise when more than one person joins together for a
common cause. It is for this reason that we must have some rules. These rules are, by no
means, the end all to all situations that come up. They are, though, a good guide for us to
follow so that we do not spoil the gaming experience of our fellow guild members and
players.

There will be absolutely NO begging for items On or Off guild channel. Make your own way, if you
need help obtaining an item arrange it with friends/guildies to help you. (This is a game, not everyone
is going to be interested in camping you an item all the time. Be reasonable with requests and most
importantly, if you get help getting things you have to be willing to assist others obtaining items also.)
Repeated,(substantiated) complaints by guild members of begging incidents will result in termination
of guild status.

Fighting between members in guild chat will not be tolerated. If you have a problem with another guild
member, try to work it out with each other in tells. If that does not work, then please bring an officer in
to help resolve the manner. If an officer is not available, find a senior member of the guild or try to put
the dispute on hold until you can get help from an officer. Bringing that kind of stuff into guild chat only
spoils the game for others and might be a dangerous distraction to those who might be preoccupied.
Anyone abusing this will be dealt with. It is a matter of courtesy to your fellow players who are on to
have a good time. Please try to respect one another

Basically, what you put into the guild is what you will get out.
