Purpose

Avenging Storm's primary focus is to have fun and, as such, we strive to build an environment of camaraderie and companionship among our members. We strive also to aid those in need of our help. We are a benevolent guild that works for the good of all Norrath.

Guild Structure

Avenging Storm is lead by Kindoz Truart, and the officers roster can be found here. Kindoz acts as both the guild leader and mediator and has the final say-so on all activities, policies, etc. The duties of an officer include but are not limited to:

  • Interviews and invitation of new members upon approval
  • Mediation of disputes between members
  • The planning of guild events
  • Collection of monthly dues
  • Attendance of officer meetings whenever possible
  • Resolution of disputes involving other guilds
  • Voting on possible guild policy changes
  • Providing general direction and focus

Guild officers are voted upon by the guild, and all should do their best to exemplify the ideals of Avenging Storm as they, like other members, are our representatives to Norrath at large.

Code of Conduct

Once you become a member of Avenging Storm, your actions reflect on all other members of the guild. Please remember to act accordingly and with respect for other players as a good reputation will encourage players with positive attitudes to seek membership.

Be respectful.

It is understood that disputes will arise between people. You must, however, remember to handle yourself in a manner befitting a member of Avenging Storm. Try to resolve the disagreement so that both parties are satisfied. If you are unsure of what you should do in a particular situation, feel free to contact an officer for guidance.

If a situation arises which directly involves another guild (not merely a member of another guild), contact a guild officer immediately. One of the duties of an officer is to help resolve intra-guild disputes.

If you have a dispute with another guild member, please try to resolve it in tells. Do not air grievances against another guild member in guild chat, regardless of whether or not that person is online at the time. If you are unable to resolve the dispute, contact a guild officer for help.

Be helpful.

This does not mean you must donate fine plate armor to newbies outside of Qeynos. What this does mean is that, if you encounter someone in need of assistance and you can provide this assistance without serious risk or inconvenience to yourself or another guild member, you should do your best to help him or her.

Be generous.

Spells: Members are strongly encouraged to provide whatever magical assistance they can to other guild members for free. The exceptions to this rule are teleports (you may choose to charge a reduced rate or teleport for free) or spells which require costly components (in which case you may charge a guild member the cost of said component).

Trade skills: Whenever possible, items should be produced for guild members at cost. This does not mean you must absorb the cost of failures, but please use discretion when determining how much to charge a guild member for a particular item.

Buying/selling: Selling items that you do not need to guild members that could find a use for them is strongly encouraged. Always charge fair prices to guild members, and you are encouraged to offer a "guild discount" whenever possible. The better equipped a member is, the better chance he or she has of growing in experience so that he or she may then help newer members when the opportunity presents itself.

Be responsible.

Guild dues: Guild dues are completly donations and optional donations can be given to any guild officer (who will keep track of the money given to him or her each month). Giving donations are very important as it allows us to aid members and put better guild events. There are plans in progress to expand the scope of what dues may be used for.

Guild meetings: Are basically when the guild feels one is needs as one per month is attemped. The place for the most part will vary but mostly just be in guild chat. In meeting everyone is encouraged to partake. We love to hear from everyone.

Be proud.

Anonymity: We ask that guild members do not enter anonymous status as we are proud of all of our members and hope, in turn, that all members are proud to be a member of the guild. You may choose to use roleplay status, esp. if you do not wish to be contacted for class-specific services (ex. teleportation, resurrection, corpse location, etc.).

Guild inquiries: If you are asked by another character about Avenging Storm, please share with them the positive experiences you have had with us. Make them aware of the new member guidelines and encourage them to visit the guild web site for more information. Never talk about Avenging Storm with a character from another guild with the intent of convincing them to change guilds unless that person has expressed an interest in changing.

New Members

Avenging Storm is interested in growing and providing a sense of family and friendship to new members who would seek to uphold our ideals and fulfill our purpose. We do, however, have several guidelines for new members which are strictly enforced:

  1. Level 14 or Above
    Applicants must have attained level 14 or above to be considered for admission. This is to ensure that guild members are experienced enough in gameplay to be productive and capable of participating fully. Alternate characters up to 3 are allowed to be any level from 1 to 60. Having 5 to 8 characters in the guild all your character must be at least level 14 so you can have 8 in the guild but all at least 14.
  2. Good Alignment
    Avenging Storm is a good guild and, as such, members are required to be of good alignment. This does not mean we will not accept members from an evil race (Dark Elf, Iksar, Ogre, or Troll) or of an evil class (Necromancer, Rogue, or Shadowknight), but applicants of these races and/or classes will be examined more closely than applicants of a race or class which is conventionally not evil. Avenging Storm does not, under any circumstances, admit player killers. This philosophy goes against the ideals and purposes of our guild.
  3. Active Characters
    Any character seeking admission must be the primary or secondary character of any player. We do not allow more than four characters per player as we prefer our members to be as active as possible. Secondary characters seeking admission are exempt only from the level requirement, which there is one for alternates. All other requirements must be fulfilled.
  4. Officer Interviews
    Applicants will first be referred to a Guild Officer who will conduct a preliminary interview to insure that he or she is suitable to be admitted to the guild. Please be prepared to discuss your reasons for wanting to join and what you are able to bring to the guild as a member. Upon completion of this interview they will fill out an application on our web site.
  5. Guild Application
    Characters seeking admission to Avenging Storm must fill out a guild application for review by guild leader, Kindoz Truart. A copy-and-pasteable version of the application is available here for those whose computers are not configured to make use of html forms. Please note that the contents of your application (with the exception of your sponsors and the reason you wish to join) will be posted as your member profile if you are admitted to the guild. Real life friends of current guild members and alternate characters need only to fill out an application and include an indication of such.
  6. Guild Leader Interview
    If all of the above criteria are met satisfactorily, the applicant will then be interviewed by Guild Leader Kindoz. If Kindoz is away for an announced, extended period of time and will not be available to conduct the guild leader interview, another officer may serve in his place.
  7. Exceptions
    All people wishing to be members of Avenging Storm must complete the above process, with the exception of those people invited "on-the-spot" by Guild Leader Kindoz.

Once a new member has been admitted to the guild, he or she will enter a 2-week trial-period, during which time he or she will be closely observed to ensure that he or she is fulfilling the role of an Avenging Storm member. You will be notified of when this trial-period has ended.

Guild Events

Because we strive to promote camaraderie among members, guild events are an important part of Avenging Storm. Attempts will be made to insure that at least two guild events will be held each month and that as many people are able to participate as possible. Teleportation and binds will be provided whenever possible and will be free of cost. Events will be announced via guild chat and the main guild page so please check often! Suggestions for events are always welcome and should be submitted to Kindoz or any guild officer.

Guild Dismissal

The following actions warrant immediate dismissal from Avenging Storm (please note that you will be given the chance to defend yourself in any of the following instances):

Kill stealing
This is the intentional attack of another party/character's MOB with the intent of taking the experience or loot and/or trying to take a camp that is already occupied. This does not include defending yourself when you are attacked by a MOB someone else is camping. Please use your own discretion in an instance such as this.

Ninja looting
This is excessive looting while in a party or looting a no-drop item before it has been rolled upon. Please ask about your group's loot rules as soon as you join and strive to uphold them as well as you can.

Training
This is the dumping of MOBs on another party or player regardless of whether or not that person/persons die. The exception to this is when you shout a warning that such a train is coming and that all should beware.

The contents of this charter are subject to change. It is each guild member's responsibility to be aware of the contents of the guild charter at all times.
