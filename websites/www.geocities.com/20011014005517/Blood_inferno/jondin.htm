Jondin Sottovoce - Bard
    

     I was born in the tree tops of Kelethin among the wood elves of the area.  My father was born and raised in Freeport, being a Cleric he was brought up in the Temple of Mithaniel Marr in the northern part of the city.  My mother was a very quite woman not belonging to any class within the tree tops she made cloths and some leather armor for the young ones there.  She often sold these hand crafted items to the merchants all over the city.  My parents met while my father was stationed within Felwith, he rushed to Kelethin one day when the Orcs of Crushbone mounted an offense against the wood elves of Kelethin.  He saved my mother from certain death with the last of his healing manna.  He was soon killed afterward, only to be resurrected by a young cleric shortly after he fell.  I was born shortly after there 6 month wedding anniversary, with both the characteristics of Human, and Elf.  I was accepted only because of who my father was, but that was enough for me.  I was young and unsure of what I wanted to do, I was just happy with playing in the tree tops with my friends, never thinking of leaving the area.  On my tenth birthday my father announced to my mother and I that he was being called back to Freeport across the dangerous Ocean of Tears. 

     We began our journey to Freeport, it was a long dangerous trip, luckily we had an uneventful trip and arrived in Freeport 2 days after the trip began.  I learned quickly that Freeport was no place for a child to play.  West, and East Freeport was off limits to me, as that was where the Militia was housed.  I wasn't allowed to leave North Freeport, or go many places within the area.  I mostly stayed home but mother would make me sow, and help with the things she made.  I would also have to accompany her to the market where the Coalition of Trade Folks met to sell their things.  I was teased a lot, I was not as welcome there as I was in Kelethin, I was beat up by a lot of the kids, and the adults shunned me for being a "half breed".  It was hard to get along, I knew I didn't want to make garments my entire life (a woman's job) so I set out to become a cleric like my father.  I was certain I would make it because of who my father was, just because of who my father was.  No matter how hard I tried I could not keep up with my studies, I could not learn as fast as the other students and after several months of trying to keep up I was asked to leave the temple.  My grades had fallen so far behind I could not keep my enrollment.  I wasn't failing, but I wasn't what the other kids where either, they learned so fast, and there mediation skill was something I could only dream about.  I was frustrated but determined to find my spot in this city.  I walked over to the Hall of Truth still in the area my father allowed me to travel, and enrolled.  The teachings where centered around fighting rather than the art of casting.  I was sure this was where I belonged, I knew I would do well here.  After a few weeks I began to fall behind like before.  I spoke with my parents about it and they assured me I could do it.  When we began our meditation skills within the Hall I was well beyond the other students who where just learning.  Unfortunately they closed the gap quickly, and with there combined skills of fighting and casting I was unable to keep up, and once again asked to drop out.  I bowed my head and began to look for another area to excel in.  I stayed at home for the next year practicing what I learned between the paladin guild and the cleric guild, I combined both sets of skills to become stronger.  I was still not as advanced as the other in each of the classes but I though if I learned from all classes I could defeat any foe I came across.  I of course kept this to myself, passing from one class to the other until I was eventually asked to leave  as each began the more advanced studies.  My parents unaware, of my intentions or my studies continued to push me to become something worth while in the city.  I learned blacksmithing skills until my techniques in it where advanced enough to do most anything that was asked of me.

     A month after I flunked out of the Enchanters school I heard of a competition that was being sponsored by  the Warriors of West Freeport.  I began my work immediately, training day in and day out, until I was able to duel wield with the best of the students.  I though to myself this was my chance to show my parents and everyone that had looked down upon me I was someone.  The day of the competition was at hand, I was one of the first to fight.  My melee skills not as strong as my competitor I used the skills I learned from my monk teaches before they removed my toboggan and saw I was less than human.  Not able to hit as hard as most I relied on my agility and won the first match.  My parent where proud and for once I felt like I was something special.  I fought the same way for the next few matches each match getting harder and harder until I was down to the semi finals.  By this time the day had lost most of its sun light and the stadium was packed.  I was called on to fight against a young boy whom I had never met.  My parents said they had also moved from Kelethin.  I looked at him as he took off his cloak, I looked into his unsteady eyes, and was suprised when he removed his head piece to see pointed ears.  He was half elf too, with much the same characteristics as I.  I stepped into the arena as they announced our names.  The crowed didn't seem to pleased with either of us being in this arena and having made it this far, we where not human, even worse we where half breeds and didn't really belong to any race. 

     I was the first to be introduced "Jondin" the introduction rang in my ears as silence followed within the crowd.  Why did they not cheer for me? Did I not work as hard as the humans that where there, even the wood elf maiden that was there was treated with respect and kindness.  "Necklare" the burly warrior yelled as he introduced my opponent.  The fight began and I soon found myself eating dirt as his strength was greater than mine.  I could not rely simply on my agility, a gift I was granted by the gods from being half human, and half elf.  This Warrior had much of the same skill I had, he was faster, stronger, and I soon found myself bleeding into the ground.  The fight was over in a matter of minutes, I could not hold him off, I could not get within his defenses.  It wasn't really a fight with him, he slipped end of his sword across my temple and I passed out.  I found out later that night he did not attend the warriors school there in Freeport but his brother had trained him.  I was also happy to hear he was the one who won the grand prize.

     That night I sat in the darkness crying, I failed myself, and my family again.  I did not belong in this place, I wanted to go home, but I knew I nothing would change there because my father would still be in Freeport, he was the only reason I was accepted in Kelethin.  I ran out of the house, my destination was east Freeport, I was going to sneak abort the boat and head back to Kelethin.  Wanted in Kelethin or not, it was my home, and that was where I would spend my days.  On the way to the docks I meet a group of boys talking.  One of the finalists of the tournament was there, boasting of his deeds.  I though to myself that should have been me there in the center of them, what stories I could tell.  As I walked passed them in a hurry to get out of the Western part of the city he stopped me.  I did not want to stop but knew if I didn't I would be though a coward.  I stood up straight and turned to face him.  He stepped out in front of his friends, and began to mock me and my family.  The other kids just stood behind him and laughed.  I tried to ignore it, and turn away, but he grabbed me and threw me to the ground, continuing with his mockery.  He had no weapon and I knew my skills in hand to hand combat where far superior to his after being taught by my monk trained for so many weeks.  I attacked him, he was a slow warrior, strong and steady in his attacks but slow non the less.  I continued to connect blow after blow, until his face begin to bleed, I knew he was nothing without his weapons.  The more I hit him the more angry I got, I began to scream, almost to the point of chanting with each blow I dealt.  One of the boys threw him a knife and he came at me slicing my right arm with the blade.  I fell to the ground stunned as he kicked me in the ribs.  I fought for the strength to stand up straight.  I tried to sneak off, but they saw me again.  The young warrior began to charge me, I tilted my head back and let loose a bellow, the young warrior stopped in his tracks and began to shake, as every bone in his body was struck by a force.  The boy fell to the ground, barley alive blood flowing from his ears only.  I was shocked, I stood there and stared, unsure of what I had just done.  The other boys scattered into the shadows as there friend laid there bleeding.  I ran toward the docks hopping for a short wait on the boat.  After entering the Eastern part of the city I saw the half elf warrior that had beaten me earlier in the day staggering in the streets, I began to run to him, but a large man atop a giant horse came charging by, and in one movement swooped the young boy atop his ride and kept going.  If I would have blinked I wouldn't have seen it at all. 

     I continued my journey back to Kelethin.  It was a long trip, but once out of Freeport I was content to what ever happened next.  When I arrived in Kelethin, news had already arrived of my defeat within the city across the ocean.  It was very difficult moving around the city, the Warriors and ranges knew of my defeat, and the scholars of each class knew of my failures.  One evening while speaking to a beautiful druid atop the Kelethin lifts the Bard guild master heard of my tail of what happened to the young warrior in the streets of Freeport.  She took me in, explained to me what had happened, and how I used my voice as a tool.  She trained me how to use my voice along with all the other skills I already learned.  The spells I learned of minor healing in Freeport, she showed me how to heal while humming a soft Hymn.  She showed me how to defend myself and those around me while chanting a battle tune.  I continued my training within the Kelethin Bard guild.  I have since then told many stories to people around Norrath of my travels, and of what happened that night in Freeport.  I have spread stories of the corrupt Freeport Militia, and of the dragons that plague this great land.  I have yet to learn, or see all there is, but I will continue traveling these lands, helping those that I can, and joining forces with those in need.  I have yet to find my place in this land, but I know what class I belong too.  I am a Bard, a voice that can be both seductive, or bitter to those around me.  I can make you love me with one tone, and hate me with another.  Many say a tongue can be a two edged sword?  I agree, but knowing when to use it as a sword to strike with, and a honeycomb to seduce with, is a talent only a Bard can know.
