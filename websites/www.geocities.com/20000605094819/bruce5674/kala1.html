Finding Your Way Around

Kaladim can be a confusing place to those unused to traveling underground. The city is located in a huge complex of caves located beneath Stormhammer Mountain. The city's entrance is very hard to miss, as the statue of King Kazon Stormhammer looms above passers-by. Our entrance, called Butcherblock Gate, is a fine example of Dwarven stonework. You'll find no better masons or blacksmiths in all Norrath than the Dwarves! Note the many guards as well. No enemy has ever succeded in entering the city proper, not even the great Wyrm Mordeethan, slain by Kul the Great during the last of the Dragon Wars.

After passing through the gate,the traveler is presented with two passages, one leading to the left, and one leading to the right. Let's examine the left hand passage first. Passing through the tunnel, you will enter one of the larger caverns in the city. You will see the path continue on between an armory shop and Pub Kal, one of Norrath's finer drinking establishments. You can find a wide array of beverages here, and Dindaek Darkfoam will be more than happy to buy loot from you. Traveling onward, we come to a large, white marble structure. This is the Arena of Kaladim. Our Warriors, such as myself, obtain our training here. Be sure to greet Hogunk! If you are one who enjoys the song of the blade, you will find much of interest at the adjacent building, Stormguard Hall.

Stormguard Hall is the largest of Kaladim's Guilds. It is also the oldest, as Dwarves are warriors, first and foremost. You can often find me here if I'm in town, which is rare these days. Rounding the pool in front of the Hall (it's deep, so be careful), you will see the figure of Vacto Mounel. He is a retired Warrior with a unique talent, of which he will tell you if yor speak to him. Takeing the hall to the right, you will often see one of our members looking out through the window. He may have some work for an aspiring Warrior. Continue on down the passage until you reach the Feast Hall. There is a merchant here who often carries piece of patchwork armor, for the Warrior neophyte. Entering the passage at the end of the Feast Hall, you will see that it extend away from you in both directions. At one end, you will find Canloe Nusback, who trains our newer recruits. At the other, you will find Byzar Bloodforge. Don't be put off by his gruff manner, he means no real harm. His daughter was recently left at the altar by a cad. Perhaps he will want some help....

After exiting the Warrior guild the way you came in (there's an exit to the Arena too), you will see Redfist Metal Shop across the courtyard. You can purchase many fine weapons and shields here, if you have the platinum. Continue down the path between Redfist and the Arena. You will come to an are with the Tanned Assets Shop (fine leather goods, leather armor), and Irontoe's Eats. Be sure to say hello to Tumpy, the proprietor. His Tumpy Tonics are a drink unrivaled in the land, far better than that dog-water called Blackburrow Stout. Now head down the path that runs next to the Arena. You will see a building off to your left, as well as a path entering a tunnel. Follow the tunnel to reach North Kaladim, home of the Cleric, Rogue and Paladin Guilds. Turning to the right, you will see a path that connects to the path you used when entering this cavern. Go back up that path to reach Butcherblock Gate. Taking the right path this time, you will see three buildings as you exit the tunnel. The first is another leather goods shop. Across the bridge, you'll find Gurtha's Wear and an Potion Shop. Continuing on down the path, you'll come to Stormhammer Hold, the residence of King Kazon Stormhammer. Be sure to show proper respect towards the King, as the number of guards here rivals the number at the Gates.

The first thing you see upon entering North Kaladim are the famed Dwarven Mines. Here we extract valuable metals and gems from the earth. The gems are exported (primarily to Felwithe), but the valuable metals, such as gold, platinum, and silver, are purified and sold at Trantor Everhott's Forge. The base metals, such as iron, tin, and copper, are made into weapons. We Dwarves have a great affinity for the axe (which tends to make druids and treants leery of us) and the sword. Next to the mines is the the Ratsbone Assay and Treasury Office (RATO). This building contains the bank and those working here are responsible for keeping track of the earth'sbounty we remove. Oft found standing next to the RATO are the heads of the Local 628, the Rogue/Miner's Guild. Be sure to talk to Jeet about his vermin problem. He's known for giving unique items to those who help him in some fashion.

From where your standing, you can see two passages: one lies on the path between the Mine Cap and the RATO, and the other between the RATO and the cavern wall. We'll examine the second tunnel first. Heading down this passage takes you to Underfoot Cathedral and Underfoot Hall, the Cleric and Paladin Guild, repectivly. As you enter the cavern, be sure to greet Doramafi Ratsbone, who sells supplies needed by adventurers. Underfoot Cathedral is the large building bewfore you, and is dedicated to the worship of the Lord of the Caverns, Brell Serilis. He is the father of the Gnomes as well as the Dwarves. Much to our anger, the blasphemous Gnolls of Split Paw and the Runny Eye Goblins also claim he is their creator. Why would the Lord of the Dwarves create two of their sworn enemies? As you gaze upon all that is holy to the Dwarves, be sure to take time to notice the fine dwarven architecture and engravings. If you feel compelled to bow and pray to our Lord, feel free to do so. Behind Underfoot Cathedral is aset of stairs leading up to the entrance to Underfoot Hall. This is the home of the Paladin Guild. The awesome structure housing the guild is constructed of pure white marble. It almost seems to gleam with a holy light. As you walk down the long hall to the guild, be sure to consider the immense amount of work that went into the creation of this structure. You can't be the best miners and stoneworkers in all Norrath without having something to show for it!

After returning to the RATO and the Mine cap, you can go down the other passage. This leads to a small cluster of shops, not the least of which is Trantor Everhott's Forge, considered by all to be the home of the finest weaponsmiths on all Norrath. We Dwarves can not claim to know the first thing magic use, but no one can match a Dwarf when it comes to creating a fine blade with a keen edge. Across the way from Everhott's is the tunnel to Greybloom Farms, producer of some of the most succulent mushrooms you could ever taste. Truffles are a specialty. The other passage leads back to South Kaladim, emerging near Stormhammer Keep.
