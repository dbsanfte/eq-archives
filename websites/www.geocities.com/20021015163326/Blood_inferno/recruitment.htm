Blood Inferno Recruitment Policy

     New members must be sponsored by four different BI guild members who have been with the guild for 60 days or longer.

     New members must be a minimum of level 40 to join the guild.

     In order to join the guild, a proposed new member must first have two grouping sessions with existing BI members (of 60 days or longer membership). The grouping sessions must be 4+ hours each minimum, or can be spread out in smaller 1 hour increments across more grouping sessions. Grouping means fighting MOBs or completing quests -- it does not include festivals, fairs, or just "hanging out."

     The sponsors of a new member are responsible for instructing him/her about guild policies and "mentoring." All new members must be informed about the proper use of guild chat and guild resources. Also, the sponsors are to relay the person's character information to the recruitment officer and inform the new member about the Onelist and Web site.

     Prior to an officer admitting a new member, he/she must be screened by the recruitment officers. Any officer can make the invitation, but recruitment officers will make final approval on who to admit. Their decision can be over-ridden by a 2/3 vote of the guild officers should there be disagreement in their decision.

     New members will be on a one month probation when they are admitted. During this time, their behavior and actions will be reviewed to see if it truly embodies the ideas of honor and family that the guild represents. The recruitment officers can revoke membership of any new member during that probationary period should any investigation discover actions contrary to guild policies (for example, KSing).

     During the month probation, any current guild member may protest the proposed member's membership.  This is to help eliminate admitting a member that has had a prior conflict with a current member.  An officer will then investigate the protest and determine if it merits revoking the probationary member's membership.

     All new members, prior to admittance, will be asked if they've been informed of guild policies and accept the said policies.  Each recruited member should know the guild web page address and have their name submitted to the onelist.

     New recruitment into the guild will be limited to 5 new members per month. The recruitment officers can be petitioned to review this limit should it prove overly restrictive.
