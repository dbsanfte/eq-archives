Twice Baked Potato's


5 or 6  Each       Baking Potato's
8       Ounces     Sour Cream
8       Ounces     Soft Butter
8       Ounces     Shredded Cheddar Cheese
1       Can        4 1/2 Ounces of 
                   Pre-Cooked Ham Chunks, 
                   diced
1/4     Cup        Finely Chopped Chives 
To Taste           Salt
To Taste           Black Pepper

2       Ounces     Shredded Cheddar Cheese for Topping



My Mom and Dad use to own the Kon-Tiki Restaurant in Pittsburgh,Pa. and they "employed" me as a cook while I was in college. Their Chef prepared a twice baked potato and that is were I got the idea for this recipe. A 4 1/2 ounce can of either shrimp or chicken can be substituted for the ham, it just depends on what main dish you are serving.



Directions:

Wash the Potato's and let them dry. Once they are dry, use a fork to poke holes in the potato's (this will prevent them from exploding). Rub some vegetable oil on the outside of each potato and bake at 350 degrees for 1 and 1/2 hours.

Remove the potato's from the oven and allow them to cool for about 5 minutes. Cut the Potato's in half, lengthwise and spoon out the insides of the Potato's into a large mixing bowl. Add the Sour Cream, Butter, Chives, Dices Ham, Shredded Cheddar Cheese, Salt and Pepper and mix thoroughly with a fork. When the mixture is smooth, but not the consistence of mashed potatoes, spoon the potato mixture back into the potato shells, heaping them slightly. Put the remaining Shredded Cheddar Cheese on top the Potato's and and place them on a cookie sheet. Return the Potato's to the oven for approximately 10 minutes or until the cheese has melted.

These make great after-school snacks as they can be re-heated in a Microwave oven on High for approximately 1 1/2 minutes.
