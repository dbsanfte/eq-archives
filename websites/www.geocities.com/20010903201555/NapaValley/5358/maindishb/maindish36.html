French Toast Sandwiches


4     Slices        Bread
2     Large         Eggs
1/4   Cup           Skim Milk
1/2   Teaspoon      Vanilla Extract
2     Tablespoons   Philadelphia® Soft 
                    Spread Cream Cheese
2     Tablespoons   Strawberry Preserves
2     Teaspoons     Powdered Sugar
1/4   Cup           Maple Syrup
                    Cooking Oil Spray



I found this recipe in a brochure that was mailed to my home by one of the local hospitals. There is another one for Carrot-Oatmeal Muffins that I haven't tried yet, but if it turns out good, it will be the next recipe I put on my home page. See, you just never know where a good recipe will come from.


Directions:

In a bowl large enough for flat bread, beat the Eggs, Milk and Vanilla Extract.

Spread the Cream Cheese on two slices of the bread and the Strawberry Preserve on the other two pieces of bread and put them together to form two sandwiches. Put the sandwiches, one at a time, in the Egg/Milk/Extract mixture, carefully turning so that both sides of the sandwich absorb the liquid.

Lightly coat a nonstick skillet with the Cooking Oil Spray and heat to medium hot. Cook the sandwiches until golden brown, turning once. It should take 2 to 3 minutes per side. Place the cooked sandwiches on a serving plate and lightly dust with the Powdered Sugar. Serve with the Maple Syrup.
