Autumn Apple Cake


	             Cake

2        Cups        Sugar
1 1/3    Cups        Vegetable Oil
2        Large       Eggs, well beaten
1        Large       Banana, peeled and mashed
1        Teaspoon    Vanilla Extract
3        Cups        Plain Flour, shifted
1        Teaspoon    Baking Soda
1/2      Teaspoon    Baking Powder
1/2      Teaspoon    Salt
3        Cups        Apples, peeled and finely chopped
1/2      Cups        Pecans, finely chopped

	             Frosting

1        Stick       Margarine, softened
1-8 oz   Container   Philadelphia® Cream Cheese
1 1/2    Cups        Confectioner's Sugar
1        Teaspoon    Vanilla Extract
1        Cup         Pecans, finely chopped



This is one of my Mother-In-Laws recipes. My stepdaughter made it before I could and I am sorry that it took me so long to get around to it. I am not a big desert eater but this cake is simply wonderful. If you don't prepare any of my other deserts, make sure you try this one.


Directions:

Cake

In a large bowl combine the Sugar and the Vegetable oil. Stir in the Eggs, Banana and Vanilla Extract. When the mixture is well incorporated, stir in the Flour, Baking Soda, Baking Powder and Salt. Mix thoroughly and then stir in the Apples and Pecans.

Pour this mixture into three greased and floured layer cake pans. Bake in a pre-heated 350-degree oven for 30 to 40 minutes. Allow the cakes to cook completely before frosting them.

Frosting

Cream the Margarine and Cream Cheese together in a large mixing bowl. Mix in the Confectioner's Sugar and Vanilla Extract. Stir in the Pecans. Assemble the three cakes on top of each other, placing a layer of frosting between cakes and then frost the outside of the cake. Store in the refrigerator.
