Mexican Cheesecake



16          Ounces      Softened Cream Cheese
2            Cups         Monterey Jack Sheese, Shredded
24          Ounces      Sour Cream
3            Large        Eggs
1            Cup          Salsa, Mild or HOT
4            Ounces     Canned Diced Green Chili Peppers, drained 
1            Cup          Guacamole
1            Medium    Tomato, seeded and finely chopped
Tortilla Chips



This is still another recipe that I obtained from some ones Home Page and I did not remember to copy down the address of that page.....Duhhhhh!!!!



Directions:

Combine the Cream Cheese and Monterey Jack cheese in a bowl and beat until light and fluffy. Beat in 8 ounces of the Sour Cream and add the 3 Eggs all at once and continue to beat at a low speed until just combined. Stir in the Salsa and Chili Peppers and pour into a 9" Spring-Form-Pan.

Set the pan on a baking sheet and place in the center of a preheated 350 degree oven. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes or until the center is almost set. Immediately spread the remaining Sour Cream over the top being carefull as the the top of the cheesecake will be somewhat delicate at this point.

Cool on a wire rack. Cover and refrigerate for 3 to 24 hours. Before service, remove the sides of the spring pan and garnish with guacamole, in a ring, and top with the chopped tomato. Sever with Tortilla chips. *Note: This should serve about 20 people*
