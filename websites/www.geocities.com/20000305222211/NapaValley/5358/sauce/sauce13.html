Turkey Bread Stuffing



1	Cup	      Butter, stick is preferred
4	Cups	      Celery, diced
1	Cup	      Yellow Onion, diced
16	Cups	      Square Sandwich Bread, cubed
1	Can	      Campbell's® Cream of Chicken Soup
1	Teaspoon      Black Pepper
1 1/2   Tablespoons   Poultry Seasoning



This is my Mom's recipe. It is one of those that has been pasted on from generation to generation. Since I cook the Thanksgiving dinner, this is the stuffing that I prepare. You can make patties out of any left over stuffing and fry them in butter in a skillet. They are wonderful with breakfast the next morning. (Note: The size of the bread cubes varies, depending upon your personal preference. They have 'prepackaged' stuffing in the grocery stores and you might want to look at them to get an idea of how large/small you want to make your bread cubes.)



Directions:

Melt the Butter in a large skillet. Add the Onion and Celery and cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the onions are translucent.

Put the Bread cubes in a very large pan, I use the roasting pan that I am going to use to cook the Turkey, and let the them get hard. Sprinkle the Poultry Seasoning and Black Pepper over the bread cubes. Add the Cream of Chicken Soup and stir until the bread and soup are mixed thoroughly. Add the cooked onion and celery and again mix thoroughly.

This mixture is then put in both the front and rear cavities of the Turkey, after they have been washed and then salted and peppered.

The remaining stuffing can then be either put in a large casserole dish and covered with aluminum foil, or simply wrapped in aluminum foil. The stuffing is then place in the oven, with the Turkey, for the last 45 minutes.

Serve in either the casserole dish or put the stuffing in a large bowl.

P.S. I cover my stuffing with a good turkey gravy…..hmmm hurry up Thanksgiving.
