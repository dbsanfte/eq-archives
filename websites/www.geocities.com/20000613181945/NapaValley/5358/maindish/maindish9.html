Mesquite Grilled Salmon



4   Each          Sliced Salmon Filets 2" thick
4   Ounces        Butter
2   Tablespoons   Lemon Juice
    To Taste      Salt and Pepper



This recipe was given to me my Connie and Jerry, who have become good friends via the Interntet. Connie claims that Jerry is extremely happy now because he is "Published". LOL!!


Directions:

Slice salmon filet into 2 inch portions. Melt 4 oz. of butter and add 2 tablespoons of lemon juice. Salt and pepper salmon to taste.

Prepare fire in charcoal grill. Start charcoal and let it burn until ash covered. Place mesquite chunks on fire, do not soak the chunks, place them on the charcoal dry.

Baste the salmon with the lemon and butter mixture and place the salmon on the grill, meat side down. Let the mesquite flame up to the meat. If it becomes too hot, move to the side until flames go down then place it back over the mesquite. Cook until salmon is cooked about half way, then turn and place skin side down. Cook the salmon until the skin is hard, and the salmon flakes easily. Then baste again with the lemon butter and serve hot.
