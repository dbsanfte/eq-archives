Parsley Potatoes



3	Large        Idaho Potatoes, cubed
1	Tablespoon   Dried Parsley
4 to 5	Pats         Stick Butter



This is an old time family recipe and I am not sure exactly who created it, but it is one of my favorites. But then again, I love potatoes. Note that the ingredients do not call for cheese, but I really enjoy some shredded Cheddar Cheese sprinkled over them just prior to serving.


Directions:


Peel and cube the Potatoes, keeping them in a bowl of water until you are ready to cook them. This will keep them from getting brown.

Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the cubed potatoes, making sure that they are completely covered with water. Sprinkle the Dried Parsley over the surface of the water and then cover the pot with a lid.

Allow the potatoes to cook for fifteen minutes and then check to see if they are fully cooked by inserting a fork in a piece of potato. If the potato slides off the fork, they are done, if not, allow them to cook for an additional 5 minutes and then check them again, using the same technique.

Drain the cooked potatoes and then put them in a large serving bowl. Place the pats of butter on top of them and serve. *Note: This is where I love sprinkling the shredded Cheddar Cheese on top of the potatoes*
