Level 8:

Making Level 8 May seem like a big step, But your increase is not that big as when you hit 4 or when you hit 12. Don't get me wrong a level 11 Necromancer will die against a Level 12 rather quickly. The Spells your receive In this Level are not Really Destructive or crippling (exp. Ward Undead but that's undead only). I Like to call this the Utility Level because most of your spells are just that.

Here are some Examples In order of Importance.

Bone Walk: A Big Upgrade in pets, Just as big as your first pet to your second.

Gather Shadows: Your Personal Cloaking device, This spell will allow you to travel through east commons with no trouble from the meddling guards. This is a must have when you decide your ready to befallen to avoid the trains.

Ward Undead: Also a must have for Befallen. This spell averages 38-40 Hp of damage to Undead Creatures. This spell alone will make you a strong force against the Undead.

Mend Bones: This spell is a heal spell for your pet. Just as useful as healing is to a cleric. This will heal your "Tank":)

Lesser Shielding: An Upgrade to your shielding line of spells. This spell is down near the bottom because if your doing stuff right your not the one taking the damage.

Dark Empathy: Allows you to heal anyone, Takes your hp's to heal though. A good spell to have anyway. Because you can heal a party member and drain back your Hp's from your target.

 

Vampiric Embrace and Impart Strength are basically useless. For Vampiric Embrace to work you can't use a weapon: P. A Spell you want to get when you get the Money is Shadow Step this works well for an escape spell.

 

Time to leave the Forest.

 

At level 8 your going to have to leave the forest to get good experience. I recommend going to East Commons and attack the many Creatures. All Creatures in Commons have Varying Power levels so make sure you /con and take on the "evens" if Soloing with your pet.

**Orc Centurions, Orc Oracles, Dark Weed Snakes, Some Young Kodak's, Lions, Lioness, Pumas. Are Some Examples. **

Level 8-10:

Lions/Lioness's/Puma's: Puma's are found on the sands on the far end of east commons. They give good experience. You really don't make much money on them. Humans hunt them for there pelts to make patchwork armor, We are restricted to cloth. Lioness's are usually /con the same as pumas. Lions are the most difficult of the cats in East Commons.

Dark Weed Snakes: These snake Pack a good punch but you should be ok with your pet tanking. They give great experience, and carry Giant Snake Fangs (weapons).

Orc Oracles: Magic Using Orcs cast shaman spells, kill these first, as they tend to heal the Centurion's. They also cast Drowsy, which slows your attack rate.

Orc Centurion: One on one these are easy to kill and you can make some good money. They usually attack in packs though and can be deadly. They usually are at the Orc Camps that are scattered about but you can find them walking alone.

Level 10-12:

Young Kodak's: You should be able to take down some of the weaker Young Kodak's. I recommend hunting these in-groups though as they are tough and very social (love to gang up on you).

Asps: The Strongest of the snakes in East Commons. Attack With Caution as there poison is strong. They give Great Experience and Giant Snake Fangs (weapons). One must consider the risks: P

Around Level 10 or so you may Consider Heading to Befallen in West Commonlands. To get they're just cross east commons. (On the other side of east commons away from Neriak)  Then Follow the Desert Line until you zone into West Commonlands. Continue to Huge the Lest side of the zone until you see a Cave in the Mountain and a green glow coming out of it. This is befallen.

Befallen Tips:

Alot of money and experience can be gained in here but also a quick death.

Keep Invisible to undead on all the time and watch for trains.

Consider grouping until your comfortable by yourself (w/pet).

Ward Undead is your Friend Does Alot of damage too undead.

Try and stick by the Zone out of Befallen if you get into trouble you can leave.

 

I'm Leveling slowly is that Normal?

 

For the most part yes. Your sharing Experience with your pet so make sure your attacking at least "Even Fight" to you. You may even Consider taking on some cats by yourself (without your pet) until you get your next level. 

General Tips For Level's 8-12.

 

Money: As Always, Save your money. Your spells only get more expensive and you won't start making more then enough until 20 or so.

Pet Weapons: You can give your pet any weapon you feel like. Your pet is much stronger and will not die unless faced with overwhelming odds (2 Kodak's will shred your pet). Try not to zone too much or you lose your pet and his weapon.

Sacrificial Pets: This still works of course, usually this happens in befallen. Its been known to happen in commons when faced with multi enemy's

PvP: This is a good level to start thinking about attacking those Light Dwellers. I wouldn't recommend it if your not on the PvP server, because its bugged on the other servers. (Non-PvP can't Heal PvP and vice versa.)

Skeletons: It seems that EQ put in the game that if you kill too many of these that your faction goes down with your guild. I never see a message for the faction hit. If this is true you may want to skip befallen.
