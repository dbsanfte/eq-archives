WINNERS OF
BLISTERED SOLES

FIRST PLACE

"Yesterday, Today & Tomorrow"  

by Quiotey

Yesterday,
I stood in fields of wild flowers and listened to animals play;
Orange blossoms filled the air with sweetness each and every day,
And my nearest neighbor was miles away.

Today,
I watch and wonder with visions of the past.
Meadows are filled with blocks of homes; I'm afraid it will not last.
My neighbors are closing in on me all to fast.

Tomorrow,
Cement and asphalt lie where once stood trees.
Space is gone; air is rank; there's barely even a breeze,
And now I can hear my neighbors sneeze.

What is civilization if all of God's things are spent?
Where is the logic when we use up every last red cent?
Living with thy neighbor -- that's not what was meant.




SECOND PLACE

"The Prisoner"  

by Wayfarer

For days on end the time just crawls
And to live seems but an endless quest
Of racing rats and climbing walls.
From early man till the end of things,
From summer through the end of spring,
We plod along just hoping for
A kind word or an open door.

But the world, it seems, is apathetic
To our woes and our tribulation.
Better t'would be to hide inside,
Walls of stone and our foolish pride.

And there are those that choose that route,
Never to seek that which they crave.
They hide inside and look through the glass,
As the parade of life by their windows pass.

But just when it seems that all hope is lost
And living isn't worth the cost,
The prisoner vows for one more try,
To kiss the land and touch the sky.

And so with trembling knees they go,
Expecting nothing but a slamming door,
And yet with every timid step,
Fear slowly loses its mighty grip,
And the seeds of hope forth proudly spring
And loneliness slowly lessens its sting.

They're back again with heads held high,
And truly they CAN touch the sky,
When all is lost and seems to end,
The thing to do is to try again.
