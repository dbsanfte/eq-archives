Cheese Dip in Rye Bread



2/8oz Packages  Soft Philadelphia Creme Cheese®
8-10  Ounces    Soft Mild Cheddar Cheese Spread
1/2   Cup       Beer
2     Loaves    Uncut, Rye Bread



I am not exactly sure who gave me this recipe although I do know I obtained it while I was working for a steel company in Gadsden, Alabama. Whenever I was invited to a tailgate party prior to a Florida State football game, I was told that I was bring this dip. I didn't really mind because it is my favorite appetizer and it is very easy to make.



Directions:

The Philadelphia Cream Cheese® and the Soft Mild Cheddar Cheese Spread should be allowed to come to room temperature. (Note: Regular brick style Cheddar Cheese CAN NOT be substituted for the Soft Cheddar Cheese Spread)

Combined the Philadelphia Cream Cheese®, Soft Cheddar Cheese Spread and Beer in a Large mixing bowl. Using an electric hand mixer, blend these ingredients until they are smooth and creamy.

Cut the top off of ONE of the loaves of Rye Bread and hollow out the inside of this loaf leaving at least a 1/2 inch on the walls and the bottom. Keep this bread for dippers. Pour the smooth and creamy cheese mixture into this loaf of rye bread, cover it with the top of the loaf and refrigerate for at least one hour prior to serving.

Cut up the other loaf of Rye Bread, and the bread that was removed from the inside of the first loaf, into bite size pieces and use them for dippers
