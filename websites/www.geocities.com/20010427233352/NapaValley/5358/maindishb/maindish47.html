Baked Stuffed Flounder


1/2     Cup           Chopped Celery
1/2     Cup           Chopped Green Pepper
8       Tablespoons   Butter
1       Clove         Garlic, minced
4       Teaspoons     Lemon Juice
1/2     Cup           Chopped Green Onions, with tops
1       Cup           Bread Crumbs

1/2     Lb            Salad Shrimp (200-300 Count)
1       Large         Egg, slightly beaten
4       Medium        Flounder Fillets
1/2     Lb            Lump Crab meat
2       Tablespoons   Chopped Parsley
1 1/2   Cups          Shredded Monterey Jack Cheese.
To Taste              Salt
To Taste              Black Pepper
To Taste              Cayenne Pepper



Still one more recipe that I found while surfing on the Internet. I use to work for a Shrimp Company that made a commercial product that was similar to this. I have changed this recipe, slightly, to more closely resemble that product.


Directions:

Sauté the Celery, Onions, Green Pepper and Garlic in melted butter over low heat until the onions are clear/transparent. Remove this mixture from the head and add the Bread Crumbs, Shrimp, Crab Meat, Parsley, Monterey Jack Cheese and Egg. Mix thoroughly and then season with Salt, Black Pepper and Cayenne Pepper.

Laying the Flounder skin side down and cut the fillet lengthwise and crosswise. Loosening the meat from the skin, forming a pocket for stuffing. Brush the inside of the pocket with melted butter and fresh lemon juice. Lightly salt and pepper the inside of the pocket.

Stuff the pocket with the above mixture and then brush the top of the flounder with melted butter and fresh lemon juice. Again, lightly salt and pepper the top of the fillet.

Place in a pan with enough water to cover the bottom of the pan. Broil approximately 3 inches from the heat, until the fish flakes easily with a fork (approximately 15-18 minutes). Baste the fillets every few minutes with the liquid in the pan, until the fish is done.
