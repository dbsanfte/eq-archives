Rules and Regulations

Membership Rules

  1. All members must be in roleplay mode at all times
  2. All members must submit a character background
  3. All members must belong to an EQRPA Guild
  4. All members must not engage in twinking and power leveling
  5. All members are expected to wear their gear - NO NAKED HUNTING!
  6. All members must follow the Rules of Engagement
  7. All members must follow the Looting Rights
  8. All members must follow Channel Etiquette
  9. All members must give destroyed items over to the Chroniclers of Norrath
  10. All members must be able to recognize EQRPA guilds
  11. All members must respect the rights of others to play their own way
  12. All members must follow the spirit or the rules and not exploit loopholes or technicalities contained within

 Rules of Engagement

  1. A member can issue a duel only if established by the character background or world setting (with background taking precedence).
  2. A member cannot con before issuing a duel, only judge based upon the hp bar and outward appearance.
  3. A member must immediately accept duels, but taking time to verify by guild status if required.
  4. A member who flees a duel by zoning is considered to have successfully evaded the attacker, and may issue duels at any time to any player.
  5. An attacker may not reissue a duel to someone who successfully them for thirty minutes, unless they actively pursue.
  6. An attacker who actively pursues an evader and remains in the same zone(s) is considered to have succeeded in pursuit, and may issue a duel after ten minutes.
  7. A member who is defeated in a duel is considered to be seriously injured, they may not issue ANY duels for a period of one hour and their attackers cannot issue them a duel for the same period.
  8. A member that is defeated must also follow the procedures in Looting Rights.

 

Looting Rights

  1. The nearest member player must loot the defeated player, but is required to return all coins and the item taken promptly.
  2. In the case of multiple attackers where who should get looting rights is not agreed upon, each attacker generates a number from 1 to 1000 and the highest number gets Looting Rights.
  3. The defeated player is then required to give a full list of all items (excluding no drop items), containers, and coin on the character at the time of death to the looting attacker.
  4. The looting attacker than may choose either one non-stackable item OR one stack of stackable items OR up to one hundred coins as loot, with no compensation if the defeated player cannot provide a full twenty stacked items or one hundred coins.
  5. The attacker is expected to base his choice of looting on character motivations.
  6. Should the looting attacker choose a container and the looser will no longer be able to carry all items, reasonable arrangements must be made to allow the looser to store the items or replace the container first.
  7. Should other inconveniences arise, the attacker and looser are expected to make reasonable arrangements for the granting of Looting Rights.
  8. All time delays for resolving looting rights do not count towards the one hour between duel time, only after the looting is resolved does the hour begin.
  9. While resolving looting rights, the involved parties are not obligated to engage in required roleplaying activities but must inform others that they are resolving a previous duel.

Channel Etiquette

  1. Say and Shout are in-character channels
  2. Tell, groupsay, guildsay, and ooc are out-of-character channels
  3. Yell is not a channel, but a special say used to call for help - and thus is considered to be in-character.
  4. Auction is a special channel used to seek or sell specific items, inquire about the statistics of items being sought or sold, or for making bids and offers on items being sought or sold.
