Texas Size Nachos


6        Each        Flour Burrito Size
                     Tortillas, quartered
24       Slices      Deli Ham or Chicken
4 1/2    Cups        Shredded Cheddar Cheese
1        Cup         Salsa
1-8 oz   Container   Sour Cream
To Taste             Garlic Salt
                     Vegetable Oil for frying


In staying with the 'stereotype' that everything is 'BIGGER' in Texas, this recipe will allow you to make nachos a dinner rather than an appetizer.





Directions:

In an electric skillet, heat approximately 2 inch of Vegetable Oil to 350-degrees. Fry the Tortillas Wedges, a few at a time, in the oil until they are lightly browned and crispy. Drain the wedges on a paper towel.

Place the wedges in a single layer on a baking sheet. Top each wedge with either a slice of Ham or Chicken, then 3 tablespoons of the Cheddar Cheese and finally 2 teaspoons of the Salsa. Sprinkle the wedges with Garlic Salt.

Note: Broil the wedges 4 to 6 inches from the heat until the cheese is melted, approximately 2 minutes. Top each wedge with a dollop of Sour cream and serve warm.
