Fried Rice


1     Cup           Chopped uncooked meat 
                    (chicken, pork, shrimp, etc.)
2     Tablespoons   Vegetable Oil
1     Medium        Egg
1     Teaspoon      Water
3     Each          Green Onions, chopped (reserve one 
                    tablespoon of the green tops for garnishment)
1/2   Cup           Frozen Field Peas, thawed
1     Medium        Clove of Garlic, minced
1     Teaspoon      Ginger (optional)
4     Tablespoons   Chinese Oyster Sauce
4     Tablespoons   Soy Sauce
1     Tablespoon    Sesame Oil
3     Cups          Cooked Rice



Fried Rice is one of my favorite things to eat. We use to serve it in my parent's restaurant but I could not remember the exact recipe. After surfing the Internet for a long period of time, I finally found one that started to refresh this old memory.

Directions:


In a small bowl, combined the Egg and the teaspoon of Water, whisking it with a fork until it is thoroughly combined.

In a large pan, or wok, stir-fry the Meat in the Vegetable Oil over medium high heat stirring constantly. Once the meat is cooked, add the Chopped Green Onions, Field Peas, Minced Garlic, Ginger, Oyster Sauce, Soy Sauce and the Sesame Oil to the pan and cook for one more minute, stirring constantly.

Add the Cooked Rice to the pan and incorporate it into the mixture. Once the rice is completely coated with oyster and soy sauce, it should turn brownish in color. Once this happens, add the beaten egg and sir the mixture until the egg is thoroughly cooked.

Turn the heat down to simmer, cover the pan and allow it to cook an additional 5 minutes, stirring periodically to prevent it from burning.

Remove the Fried Rice from the pan, garnish with the reserved Chopped Green Onion Tops and serve immediately.

NOTE: Additional types of vegetables, such as broccoli, snow peas, etc can be used in this dish. Let your imagination and tastes determine what vegetables to use. Simply add them to the pan at the same time you put in the Green Onions.
