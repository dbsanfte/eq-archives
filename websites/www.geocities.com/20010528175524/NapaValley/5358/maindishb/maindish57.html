Miner's Chicken


4        Each     Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breasts
4 to 8   Slices   Baked/Pressed Honey Ham
4        Slices   Provolone Cheese
To Taste          Salt
To Taste          Black Pepper



I had this dish at the Baby Doe's® restaurant in Dallas, Texas and it was awesome. It was covered with a mushroom sauce, something I do not have a recipe for YET!

For what I call a "Lazy Man's Chicken Cordon Bleu", bread the chicken with Italian Bread Crumbs and then follow the cooking instructions except pan fry the chicken first, in vegetable oil, but only until the breading starts to brown, turning once.


Directions:

Place the Chicken Breasts, one at a time, between two pieces of Wax Paper and, using a meat mallet, or a rolling pin as I do, flatten the chicken until the entire breast is approximately 1/4-inch thick. Place the breasts on a plate, cover them with aluminum foil and put then in the refrigerator for 10 to 15 minutes or until they are chilled. (Note: Chicken that has been refrigerated and is cold is much easier to work with than chicken at room temperature.)

Remove the chicken from the refrigerator and Salt and Pepper them, both sides. Place them on a wire rack then place the wire rack on a cookie sheet and bake them a 350-degree oven for 20 to 25 minutes or until the chick breasts are almost completely cooked. Place the Ham Slices on top of the chicken and then the Provolone Cheese on top of the Ham. Return them to the oven for an additional 5 minutes or until the cheese has melted.
