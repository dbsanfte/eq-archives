Steak Diane


1 1/2   Lb.           Sirloin Steak, 1/2 inch thick
1/2     Teaspoon      Dry Mustard
1/4     Teaspoon      Salt
1/8     Teaspoon      Black Pepper
1       Tablespoon    Chives, chopped
1       Tablespoon    Lemon Juice
1       Teaspoon      Worcestershire Sauce
3       Tablespoons   Cognac, heated
2       Tablespoons   Vegetable Oil



As a younger man, I had a special restaurant that I would take a 'first date' to. It was build on the side of Mt. Washington, in Pittsburgh, Pa and overlooked the city. At night it was a very beautiful and impressive site. This is the meal I would order and the showmanship that went with the presentation was just as impressive as the view. *See, I was a sneaky-little-devil as a young man, not that I have changed that much…..LOL*


Directions:

Mix the Dry Mustard, Salt, and Black Pepper together. Using a paper towel, wipe the steak to remove and excess moisture. Sprinkle the Mustard mixture over one side of the steak and rub it in thoroughly.

Preheat the Oil to a temperature of approximately 375-degrees. Brown the steak in the hot oil, about 2 minutes on each side. Sprinkle with the Chives, Lemon Juice and Worcestershire Sauce. At the table side, and BE CAREFUL ON THIS PART, pour the heated Cognac over the steak and "IGNITE" it. When the flame goes out, transfer the steak to a serving plate and spoon the pan juices over the meat.
