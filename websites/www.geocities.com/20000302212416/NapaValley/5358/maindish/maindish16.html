Traditional Chicken Cordon Bleu



4   Each          Boneless, Skinless Chicken 
                  Breasts
3   Large         Eggs
2   Cups          Plain Bread Crumbs
4   Slices        Baked/Pressed Ham
4   Slices        Swiss Cheese
2   Tablespoons   Vegetable Oil
		  Toothpicks



This is on recipe that appears in just about every cookbook and mine is no exception. I am not exactly sure where I got this recipe, but the not on the handling of the chicken breasts is my little "touch" to help make it easier to prepare.


Directions:

Place the Chicken Breasts, one at a time, between two pieces of Wax Paper and, using a meat mallet, or a rolling pin as I do, flatten the chicken until the entire breast is approximately 1/4-inch thick. Place the breasts on a plate, cover them with aluminum foil and put then in the refrigerator for 10 to 15 minutes or until they are chilled. (Note: Chicken that has been refrigerated and is cold is much easier to work with than chicken at room temperature

Remove the chicken from the refrigerator and place a slice of Ham and a slice of Swiss Cheese on each breast and roll them up tightly, and then secure the end with toothpicks. Return them to the refrigerator for another few minutes until they are once again cold.

Put the Eggs in a bowl and beat them and put the BreadCrumbs in another bowl. Dip the stuffed chicken breasts in the egg wash and then roll them in the breadcrumbs. Place the stuffed breasts back in the refrigerator and let them chill one last time.

Put the Vegetable Oil in a hot skillet and lightly brown all the sides of the stuffed chicken breasts. Then place them in a casserole dish and bake in a 350-degree oven for 20 to 30 minutes or until the chick breasts are completely cooked. Remove the toothpicks prior to serving.
