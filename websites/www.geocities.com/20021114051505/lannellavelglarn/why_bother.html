Why has this subject become a near obsession for me?

Why does the Highway matter at all?

 

Valid questions, I would say. 

1)  The joy of discovery.

2)  If I'm right, it could aid the ease of travel for Evils everywhere.

3)  It could help ease the burden of transportation on those 'Porters who resent the demands put on them;  and put out of business those who are little more than "port-whores", bleeding income from land-bound travellers.

3a) (ooc) As a casual player, VI is making Norrath too darn big.  I shouldn't have to spend 2 hours crossing it, or give up 2 hours income just to get from one end to the other.  For someone to get from Paineel to, say, Dreadlands could easily take 2 hours on foot (An example solved by the Nexus).  I know too many porters who hate being asked and are forced to go /anon, and others who charge ridiculous amounts.  Melees are already unable to solo at higher levels and must surrender their incomes to those who can all to often.  (me: waaah)

4)  I'm not fond of magicks and would rather that I have the control over their use.  I would prefer to port myself to 'near' my destination than accept the magicks to have someone else get me 'to' the target.

5)  (ooc) A welcome distraction to 'Evercamp' or 'Everquake', the perpetual mind-numbing hack'n'slash in pursuit of ph4t L3wtZ.

 

I realize that this entire pursuit may be just one great, big dead end.  But, in the end, it's just a game and I'm trying to have some fun with it.
