Blooming Onion



1   Large          Vidalia Onion or Walla Walla
1   Large          Egg, beaten
2   Tablespoons    Flour
1   Cup	Bread      Crumbs

	Dip

1/2   Cup          Mayonnaise
1/2   Cup          Sour Cream
1/2   Cup          Thousand Island Dressing
2     Tablespoons  Grated Horseradish



This is one the "Outback Steakhouse" best and well know appetizers. I found in on the Internet, along with several other 'famous' recipes which I will be posting at a later date.


Directions:

Select a well-rounded onion. Peel off the outer skin, leaving the root intact, but cutting it so it will stand up. Using a small, sharp knife, divide the onion into four sections by making 2 cuts crosswise, beginning at the top and cutting toward the root, stopping about 1/2 inch from the bottom. Make sure you don't cut all the way through the onion. Cut each section twice.

Putting a fork in the root of the onion, place the onion in a bowl of enough boiling water to cover it completely and let it stand for 5 minutes. The sections, or "pedals" will begin to open.

Remove the onion from the hot water and immediately immerse it into ice cold water. This will also assist in opening the pedals. Drain the onion well by turning it upside down and placing it on a paper towel.

Put the flour into a paper bag, add the onion and shake gently to coat the onion with the flour. Roll the coated onion in the beaten egg until it is completely covered. Put the Bread Crumbs in a paper bag, add the onion and shake gently to coat.

Refrigerate for 1 hour before deep frying it in hot oil until it is Golden Brown, approximately 3 to 5 minutes. The cooked onion may be kept for a time in a warm oven. Serve with the Dip.
