Guild Policy, Pledge Requirements, & Rules
                     
Guild Policy
We should be proud of our Guild, and what we stand for, therefore, we ask that Guild Members refrain from using /anonymous.  Should, for some reason, being /anonymous is necessary, please revert back to /roleplay when the situation has passed.

If you are fortunate enough to recieve a "twink" item from a fellow guildmate, you should expect to pass the item on to another Guild Member when you no longer have need for it (rather than sell it).  This is not to say that you are expected to give our hard earned items away just because there is someone in the guild who wants one, but if you have received an item in kind, return the favor, and pass the item on.  It should also be understood that others in the Guild are not there to "twink" your low level characters.

If you should see a fellow guildmate behaving in such a manner that does not befit the Honorable nature of our guild, please report it to an Officer, if they cannot solve the problem then it will be reported to the Guild Leader.  Your name will not be disclosed, and the offending member will be politely asked to stop.  Should the problem persist, however, the offending party will be asked to leave the Guild.  If the player will not willfully remove him/herself, the matter will be discussed amongest the Guild Leader and Officers and the player will be removed.

The Greater Druids are not a powerleveling guild.  We try to help all in guild make new friends, but we will not powerlevel you.  We try to help equip our people so that they may do well and get stronger as they go, but a level 14 has no need for GEBS so please do not consistantly beg for someone to go camp a very hard item to twink you.

Guild chat is there to assist us in finding friends, ask for advice, and help sell or trade some item a guildmate may have or need, say hello, and coordinate events.  Please do not spam guild chat.
