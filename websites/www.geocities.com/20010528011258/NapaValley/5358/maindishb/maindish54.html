Taco Biscuit Cups


2           Lbs.      Lean Ground Beef
1/2         Cup       Chopped Onion
1-1.25 oz   Package   Taco Seasoning Mix
1/2         Cup       Water
1-15 oz     Can       Spicy or Zesty Chili Beans, undrained
1           Can       *1 Lb. 1.3 oz.* Pillsbury® Grands!® 
                      Refrigerated Golden Corn Biscuits
1           Cup       Shredded Mexican Cheese Blend
1/2         Cup       Shredded Lettuce
1/2         Cup       Chopped Tomato
1/2         Cup       Sour Cream
1/2         Cup       Old El Paso® Salsa
                      PAM® Nonstick Cooking Spray



I found this recipe on Pillsbury's home page and I must confess, the directions were a little difficult to follow when I tried to make the Biscuit Cups. Hopefully I can clarify them so that you don't make the same mistake that I made. I also modified the Chili Bean preparation.



Pastry Blender



Directions:

In a large skillet, brown the Ground Meat over medium high heat. When some grease starts to appear, add the onions. When the meat is cooked, drain off the excess grease.

In a mixing bowl, mash the Chili Beans using a Pastry Blender*pictured above*. Add the Beans, Taco Seasoning and Water to the meat mixture and mix well. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes or until the meat mixture is slightly thickened, stirring occasionally.

Place 8 - 6-ounce custard cups upside down on a cookie sheet and lightly spray the entire cup with the PAM® nonstick cooking spray. Separate the Corn Biscuits into 8 biscuits. Roll or pat out each biscuit to form a 4 1/2" round. Place the rounds over the sprayed custard cups; press to fit over the bottom and part way down the sides of the cups.

Bake the biscuits in a pre-heated 375-degree oven for 14 to 16 minutes or until deep golden brown. Remove the cookie sheet from the oven and carefully remove the biscuits from the cups. Place the biscuit cups on the cookie sheet, right side up and spoon the meat mixture evenly into each cup. Top the meat mixture with the Mexican Cheese Blend, return them to the oven and bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted.

Top with the Lettuce, Tomato, Sour Cream and Salsa just prior to serving.
