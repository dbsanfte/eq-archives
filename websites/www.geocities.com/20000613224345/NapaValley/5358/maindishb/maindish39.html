Chicken Breasts Stuffed with Shrimp and Crabmeat


6        Each          Boneless/Skinless 
                       Chicken Breasts
1/2      Lb.           Medium (31-35) Shrimp
1/2      Lb.           Crabmeat
1        Stick         Butter
1-3 oz   Can           Sliced Mushrooms
1/2      Cup           Chopped Green Onion Tops
4        Tablespoons   Instant Flour
1/2      Cup           Minced Parsley
1/2      Cup           Chicken Broth
1/2      Cup           Milk
1/2      Cup           Champagne
1/2      Cup           Bread Crumbs
1 1/2    Cups          Chopped Swiss Cheese
To Taste               Salt
To Taste               Black Pepper



Let's hear it for the Cajun's and their wonderful sense of cooking. This is yet another recipe from Steve's copy of the Cajun Cookbook.


Directions:

Pound the Chicken Breasts to tenderize them as well as achieve an even thickness.

Boil the Shrimp until they turn pink. Cool and chop into small pieces. Remove any shell from the Crabmeat.

In a large frying pan, melt the Butter over medium heat. Add the Onion Tops, Mushrooms and sauté for a few minutes. Add the Flour and cook until the mixture begins to bubble. Little by little, stir in the Chicken Broth, Milk and Champagne until the sauce has thickened. Season with Salt and Black Pepper and remove the mixture from the heat..

Put the crabmeat and chopped shrimp in a bowl and mix together. Add the Breadcrumbs, Parsley and half cup of the cooked sauce to this mixture. Spread this mixture over the chicken breasts and then roll them up, securing them with a toothpick. Place them with the seam side on top in a greased casserole dish.

Pour the remaining sauce over the breasts and then dot them with the chopped Swiss Cheese. Bake in a pre-heated 400-degree oven for 30 minutes.
