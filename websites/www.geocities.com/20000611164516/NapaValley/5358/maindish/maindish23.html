Chicken Breasts in Mornay Sauce


2          Each         Boneless, Skinless 
                        Chicken Breasts
1          Tablespoon   Butter
1          Tablespoon	All Purpose Flour
1          Cup          Milk
1/4        Cup          Shredded Swiss Cheese
2          Tablespoons	Grated Parmesan Cheese
1 - 4 oz   Can/Jar	Mushrooms, diced
1/4        Teaspoon	Salt
           Dash         Black Pepper
	                Paprika to garnish



Whenever we have a party at the office, birthday, anniversary, etc, Diana would always request, actually insist, that I bring the Cheese Dip in Rye Bread (in the Appetizer section). When the last party was being arranged, I told her I would only bring that appetizer if she was willing to give me one of her best recipes. She agreed and this is the one she gave me. My Friends, this is a definite "keeper". Thanks Diana.


Directions:

Cook the Chicken Breasts in boiling, salted water for approximate 20 minutes, or until they are tender and completely cooked. Remove the breasts from the water and place them on a serving dish, keeping the warm.

Melt the Better in a saucepan over low heat. Add the Flour to the melted butter and stir until the mixture is smooth. Continue to cook for 1 additional minute, stirring constantly. Increase the heat to medium and gradually add the milk to this mixture, again stirring constantly.

Once this mixture thickens and bubbles, add the Shredded Swiss Cheese, Mushrooms, Salt and Pepper. Continue to cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the cheese is melted. Spoon this sauce over the cooked chicken breasts. Sprinkle with Paprika and serve.
