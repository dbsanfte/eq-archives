Butter Flake Biscuits



1/2	Cup	      Evaporated Milk
1/4	Cup	      Hot Water
1	Large	      Egg, beaten
3	Tablespoons   Sugar
1 1/2	Teaspoons     Salt
1	Package	      Yeast
1/4	Cup	      Luke Warm Water
3 1/4	Cups	      Shifted Flour
1/4	Cup	      Melted Butter



This is yet another recipe that is on a 3"x 5" note card, which has everything imaginable spilled on it. And the directions are extremely difficult to follow.



Directions:

Put the Evaporate Milk, Egg and Salt in a large mixing bowl. Dissolve the Sugar in the Hot Water and then add it to the mixing bowl.

Dissolve the Yeast in the Luke Warm water and then add it to the mixing bowl.

Finally, add the Flour to the bowl and mix thoroughly. Roll out the mixture so that it is approximately 1/4" thick. Liberally butter the top of the dough and then cut it into 5 strips, making sure to keep the buttered side up. Crosscut each strip, then form each strip into an 'N' shape and set them in a muffin tin, again butter side up. Cover the muffin tin with a damp dish towel and let the dough rise for 1 and 1/2 hours then bake in a 425-degree oven for 12 minutes.
