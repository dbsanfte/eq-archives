Mexican Manicotti


1         Lb.         Lean Ground Beef
1-16 oz   Can         Refried Beans
2         Teaspoons   Chili Powder
1 1/2     Teaspoons   Dried Oregano
1-8 oz    Package     Manicotti Shells
2-20 oz   Jar         Picante Sauce
1-16 oz   Container   Sour Cream, divided
1         Cup         Shredded Cheddar Cheese
2         Cups        Shredded Monterey Jack or
                      Mexican-Style Cheese
1/4       Cup         Sliced Green Onions *Optional*
1/3       Cup         Sliced Ripe Olives *Optional*


I found this recipe in a cooking magazine brought to the office by a co-worker. As I started to type the recipe, which I had planned to prepare that night, I noticed that the directions called for the shells to be stuffed with the uncooked meat, refried beans and oregano and refrigerated over night. Well it became 'scramble time' and I had to figure out another way to prepare it. This recipe is the results of the scramble.



Directions:

Cook the Manicotti Shells according to the package directions, drain and allow them to cool so that they can be handled.

In a skillet over medium heat, brown the Ground Beef and then drain off any excess grease.

In a medium soup pot, cook the Refried Beans until they are heated. Reduce the heat and stir in the Chili Powder, Oregano, Cheddar Cheese and 1/4 cup of the Sour Cream. When the cheese has melted, remove the beans from the heat and stir in the cooked ground beef. Allow this mixture to cool and then stuff the cooked shells, using a long Ice Teaspoon.

Place the stuffed shells in a 13" x 9" greased baking dish. Pour the Picante Sauce over the top of the shells.

Bake in a pre-heated 350-degree oven for 20 minutes and then spread the Sour Cream over the top of the shells. Sprinkle the Monterey Jack Cheese, Green Onions and Olives over the top of the sour cream. Bake for an additional 5 to 10 minutes or until the cheese has melted.
