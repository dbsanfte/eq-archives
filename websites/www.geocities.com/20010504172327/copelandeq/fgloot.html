Summary Version of Rules

This is the quick-and-dirty perspective on the rules. More detail is found below.

A - You select an item that you want. If this item drops, you will be in the roll for it. The only restriction is that it must be an item you can equip. If the item does not drop, you will be in the rolls for unclaimed items at the end of the raid.

B - In general, you can only be in one item roll, not counting research components.

C - Items are intended for your use, not trade/sale/twink.

Example #1

Six folks on raid: Ann (enchanter), Bob (paladin), Charlie (paladin), Diane (shadow knight), Evelyn (druid), Frank (mage). Ann chooses crown; Bob chooses torque; Charlie and Diane choose sword; Evelyn and Frank choose mask. Torque, sword and crystal drop, plus a BCP from run in.

Bob gets torque automatically because he's the only one who selected it.

Charlie and Diane roll for sword. Diane wins and gets sword.

Frank gets crystal. No one selected it, but he's the only one who can use.

Ann and Evelyn roll for BCP. Bob, Diane and Frank are out of roll because they got something. Charlie is out of roll because he already had a roll on an item.

Detailed Rules

Here is a more detailed description of the rules, covering some of the special circumstances.

1 - All items are for the personal use by the characters on the raid.

In other words, they are not for trading or twinking...unless we find that there is absolutely no one on the raid who can use it. Of course, if down the road you get a much better item, you can trade or sell the one you got on the raid. But there's an honor system in place that you actually intend to use the item.

2 - Before the raid starts, each participant is allowed to select one item that they want. If this item drops in the raid, they will be in the roll for that item without consideration of whether they are the "best class" for that item.

The only restriction on this is Rule #1.

3 - Each participant is allowed to participate in, at most, one roll for an item, except as noted below.

For example, let's say you're a Paladin and choose the Polished Mithril Torque as your selected item. It drops, and you roll and lose. Then the Mithril Greaves drop, and no one has selected them. You will be ineligible for the roll for it (except under circumstances noted below) because you have already had a chance at an item.

4 - If an item drops with no one signed up for it, the item will be held until the end of the raid and randomed among those who can use it and have not yet participated in an item roll. Certain items have "Preferred classes" who will get priority on these rolls. If an individual would be eligible to roll for more than one item at the end of the raid, they will be asked to choose which item they wish to roll for.

The only exceptions to holding items to the end are the Polished Mithril Torque and Razing Sword of Skarlon since they are NO DROP.

For example, Rokyl's Channeling Crystal drops, but no one has selected it. All Wizards, Necromancers, Mages and Enchanters...who have not rolled on another item...will be in the random.

5 - In the case of the Torque and the Sword, any participant who can use the item, and whose selected item is still to come, may elect to change their selected item to the dropped item. If no one changes their mind, then the item will be randomed among those who can use it and the winner will not be excluded from rolls on their selected item, but is excluded from rolls under #4 or #6.

Ok, this one is a little tricky. First preference is given to those who don't yet know if their selected item will drop. In the case of the Torque, this means anyone who has selected an item dropped by Skarlon or Rokyl. In the case of the Sword, this means anyone who has selected an item dropped by Rokyl. These folks have first choice of changing their mind.

If no one elected to change their mind, then the item is randomed among those who can use it. If their selected item is still to come (e.g., it's the Torque we're concerned with, and they chose the Sword as their first choice), they can still roll on their selected item. This is one circumstance in which folks can get into two rolls.

In any case, they are excluded from other rolls on "unwanted" items.

6 - If, somehow, an item slides through all of this without being awarded to anyone, it will be randomed among all those who have not participated in a roll, without regard to usability or preferred classes. If no one is in that category, then it is randomed among all who did not get an item.

There is no desire to let items rot.

7 - Research components are randomed among members of the appropriate class who need them. This distribution does not count for purposes of Rule #3.

An important point here. Rule #1 applies here, as well. This means that you can roll on Necro words if you're a Necro and need them for the spell. However, you do not get priority on your research components as trade items, or to make additional copies of the spell for sale/trade. In that event, they will be treated as "regular loot" for randoming.

NB: I'm still cogitating on this rule. Quite frankly, I need to know more about the value of the research components that can be found on FG raids; those in the plane can be worth a small fortune. A couple of changes that might be made to this rule: a) spell components might be upgraded to count as do regular items in Rule #6; b) restrictions may be imposed on rolling for a spell component just to build skill (as opposed to getting the spell). I'll announce if I've changed this rule at raid time.

Example #2

Folks on the raid: Ann (enchanter), Bob (paladin), Charlie (paladin), Diane (shadow knight), Evelyn (druid), Frank (mage). Everyone selects the mask as their choice except Bob, who chooses the greaves. Crown, sword and mask drop, plus a BCP from run in.

The crown is held until the end of the raid under Rule #4.

Under Rule #5 Charlie and Diane are given the opportunity to change their minds about wanting the mask and taking the sword. Bob does not get this opportunity because his item is known not be dropping. Charlie changes his mind and gets sword.

Ann, Diane, Evelyn and Frank roll for the mask; Frank wins.

Bob (the only one not to get a roll) is eligible for both crown and BCP; he must make select which one to roll for. He chooses the crown and gets it because he is only one rolling.

Ann, Diane and Evelyn roll for the BCP since they did not get anything.

Item Matrix

The following table is a matrix of classes against items.

Items marked with 'No' are not usable by the class in question.

Items marked with 'Yes' are usable by the class in question.

Those items marked with 'Pref' are usable by the class in question, and that class is considered a "preferred class" for rolling on the item in the event that no one indicated they wanted the item. For an item to have preferred classes, I have to feel that it is overwhelmingly more important to one set of classes over others. Most items do not have preferred classes.

These are my subjective assessments and reflect who I think "should" have an item. Feel free to argue with me outside of the raid...but these decisions are not open for discussion during the raid.
