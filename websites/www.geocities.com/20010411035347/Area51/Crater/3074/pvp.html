Strat's

Let it be known that as a Necromancer you WILL be at a disadvantage in terms of PvP for a long time (and against some classes forever). Here is a list of classes and how you rank against them.

*Note these match-ups are based on reasonably equal levels and PvP skill*

Wizards: Their lack of healing and buffing spells, is killer for them.

Tips against Wizards:

1. Always keep your pet attacking the wizard if possible give him a fast hitting weapon to disrupt those killer spells of theirs. (Most piercing weapons are good for this, faster the better)

2. Melee that wizard, you may not be very effective but the more you interrupt the better.

2. Drain, Drain, and Drain. Without the ability to heal the more you drain there Hp's and add to yours the better, after they get off a strong burn, you will be glad your draining.

Magician: These battles tend to be long and bloody as this classes is closest to your own.

1. If possible get the first good hit in, a damage over time is best as it takes their life and disrupts their spells when they take damage.

2. Keep your pet on the magi, Always go for the caster first then deal with the pet.

3. Attacking with your pet is a toss up because you want to disrupt their spells but yours will be disrupted also.

4. In most cases these battles go to the Necromancer because of the Drain Hp Spells.

Druid's, Enchanters: These are combined because there handled the same way.

1. Pet attacks first followed by a good damage over time spell.

2. Melee these all the time, they're weakening and damage potential is high.

3. Drain often (as usual)

For the Bards:: Your pet should have a Weapon With Medium Speed and Damage. One handed blunt or one handed slashing.

1. These Musical Misfits should be targeted with your pet first and depending on the song that is going will tell you how to handle them.

2. If you see that he is gaining HP's back, then he has the healing song going. Damage over time will slow this down (IE. gain some, lose some) Then Drain spells to lower the HP's More. You may consider doing Poison and Clinging darknes (or more powerful of the type, Slowing down and damage over time x 2)

3. Any other type of song should be approached like this, Try your best to stay as far away as you can. Start the attack with a good Damage over time and a Disease (Disease will stop the natural healing). After this its Drain City. I wouldn't recommend trying to Melee the Folk Singer, If you really must then try hitting from the back (or your pet in the back with you in the front) with your staff.

**Word of Caution the next ones are more difficult for your class**

Clerics and Shaman's: These are also together for the same reason.

1. Start the attack with your pet attacking with a large weapon but fairly fast, One handed blunt or sword weapons.

2. Cast Cancel magic if you have it first (remove some of those buffs).

3. Cast Root or other slowing spells. You must keep away from melee range.

4. Drain baby Drain.

**The rest are pure Melee classes. Extreme Caution when fighting these**

The next few tips will cover Rangers, Rogues, Paladins, Shadow Knights and Warriors and the Monk.

1. Root, Root, Root this spell is you biggest advantage should pet your first attack. If you don't have root use your best slowing spell)

2. Send in your pet with the biggest weapon you can find. (2 handed swords work well), Your pet will most likely die in battle and the more they Damage/Distract the better.

3. Damage over time and area effect spells should be casted following the root spell (or other slowing spell).

4. Run Backwards FACING your target, Stop to cast only, then continue running. Try to lose dodge in and out of trees/hills to keep him from getting into melee range.

5. You WILL lose if they get in Melee range so don't let them.
