Crawfish Etouffee


2        Cups          Fresh Crawfish Tails, 
                       Cooked, Peeled and 
                       Cleaned.                                                 
4        Tablespoons   Flour
4        Tablespoons   Butter
1        Cup           Finely Chopped Yellow 
                       Onions
1        Cup           Finely Chopped Green 
                       Onions
1/2      Cup           Finely Chopped Celery
1        Teaspoon      Finely Chopped Garlic
2        Medium        Seeded and Chopped Sweet Green Pepper
1        Cup           Chopped Parsley
1-16oz   Can           Tomatoes, Drained and Chopped
1        Cup           Chicken Broth
1        Tablespoon    Worcestershire Sauce
1/4      Teaspoon      Cayenne Pepper
1        Teaspoon      Salt
To Taste               Louisiana Hot Sauce

4-6      Cups          Cooked White Rice



This recipe comes straight from the Frugal Gourmet Cooks American cook book by Jeff Smith. Although I have made a few changes to it.



Directions:

If you buy the pre-cooked Crawfish Tail meat, you will need about 1 pound of meat. If you are going to boil live crawfish or use pre-cooked shell on crawfish, you need about 3 pounds. Yes, it takes about that much to get one pound of tail meat.

In a heavy 5 to 6 quart soup pot, put the Flour and Butter and prepare a roux and toast it gently until light brown. Add the Yellow Onions, Green Onions, Celery, Garlic, Green Bell Pepper and Parsley and cook over medium heat until the vegetables are soft.

Add the Tomatoes to the pot, stirring carefully so that the mixture begins to thicken. Next add the Chicken Broth and all the seasonings and stir well.

Reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes and then stir in the Cooked Crawfish meat.

Server over the hot rice.
