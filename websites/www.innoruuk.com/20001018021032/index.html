Do to an entry fee, Akkirus and I will be speaking with Ifurita and Aradune early Monday to determine the prizes for the event. High-Risk High-Reward was asked for, we will do what we can.

Tallon Zek (4pm) and Nameless (6pm) events are tomorrow in the arena. A full scheduel will be posted tomorrow night for the rest of the servers.

Good luck to everyone involved! See you on the battlefield.

Best of the Best - Test of Tactics
5:15PM EST - Cymric
Verant Interactive Presents

The Test of Tactics – Best of the Best Event

The Test of Tactics was the first official event held by Verant Interactive, being held over a year ago on Xegony. The event is designed as a 6 vs 6 event, with minimal amounts of rules involved. Due to the massive time cost on the event, it was decided upon to move to smaller events that would take less time to run and place a smaller burden on the server staff.

With the incredible demand that the players have shown for the events, we are going to bring back the Test of Tactics, and do a Best of the Best for all servers to find the greatest 6-man team. We have modified the rules slightly so that we don’t have 200 teams per server enter, and it will be able to be run quickly and efficiently, while still trying to keep the original spirit of the event alive.

The 6-man team has now become a 7-man team. The "Captain" of the team must be a level 58 or higher. Two "Elite" team members need to be 55 or higher. The final 4 of the team make up 3 "Support" team members of 49th level plus, and a "Substitute/Coach" position that can be of any level. This does allow for a 7-man team of all 60th level players.

Only 6 will fight at one time, and the 7th can be substituted before the match. Once the team has been chosen, those 6 must fight. The 7th during the match is restricted to observation or coaching.

Rules are fairly simple. No starting buffs, no starting pets, no use of fear, no use of charm, and no use of knock back spells. Once the match starts, you may buff, summon pets, use activatable items, cast spells, engage with melee, hide and sneak, disarm opponents, use uber-quest weapons, do whatever is needed to assure the victory of your team.

The "Ring" for this match is the raised platform of the arena. The only characters allowed in the center arena is the 12 players involved, and the Lead Judge of the event. All players involved must remain in the tiles at all times. Leaving the tiles for any reason will result in a warning, then disqualification of the player involved.

Security for this event will be extreme. Any non-combatant who even stands up on the raised platform will be removed from the arena and banished from the event. No spell casting, no bard song, no use of shout will be tolerated at anytime for those watching the event. Event Staff can and will remove any player that causes even the slightest disruption of this massive event.

Before the claims of uber-guild, uber-item, uber-whatever start, be advised that of the 3 Test of Tactics that have been held, the team that has practiced the most have won these events, even over higher levels, better equipped, and longer playing characters. How well you understand the basics of 6 vs 6 player vs player combat, and getting a well balanced team will determine how well your group does in the event.

The ToT-BotB will start October 16th and run through October 28th on Live Servers. The final event will be a 2-day event on Test Server, October 28th and 29th.

Prizes for this incredible event are still undecided, as well as the question of an entry fee. This will be discussed and made final in the next week.

Good luck to everyone participating, I shall see you on the battlefields!

Lady Daegarmo
Online Events Coordinator

Murphy's Laws of Everquest
5:14PM EST - Cymric
From www.monkly-business.com:
Murphy's Laws of Everquest

- Everything is camped.
- Your corpse will always end up halfway inside a mountain, nowhere in sight of the roads, or under a tree.
- The skelton in front of you is there to distract you from the kodiak approaching behind you.
- If your attack is going really well, you are in an ambush.
- If the MOBs are within range, so are you.
- The tougher MOBs invariably attack on two occasions--when they're ready and when you're not.
- One MOB is never enough, but two can be entirely too many.
- The MOB is not retreating; it's just going to get its friends.
- The MOBs you never encounter when armed and buffed always appear during your corpse run.
- The MOB you've been camping for hours always spawns 18 seconds after you leave.
- Teamwork is essential; it gives the MOBs other people to attack.
- Don't ever be the first, don't ever be the last, and don't ever volunteer to do anything.
- For every action, there is an equal and opposite criticism.
- The greatest danger to your group is a Paladin with a map and a plan.
- When the going gets tough, the tank goes linkdead.
- A group without healers or bandages is not a group for long.
- Your buffs will fade when you need them the most.
- Whenever you have plenty of mana, you never miss.
- Whenever you are low on mana, every spell fizzles.
- When in doubt, gate.
- There is not a single bathroom in all of Norrath.
- The Armor Class of a piece of armor is inversely proportional as to how good it looks on you.
- The item that you want the most drops the rarest.
- The item that you want the most is out of your price range.
- The item that you want the most was sold at an auction while you were offline.
- The item that you want the most was picked up by some moron who didn't know the definition of "No Drop".
- The item you'll need tomorrow is No Rent.
- The item you accidentally drop will be the most valuable / powerful / important item you carry.
- If you can afford it, it's not for sale.
- If you want two of them, it's a Lore item.
- If you want to sell it, it's a No Drop item.
- The High Quality Bear Skin drops every 28 days, the same schedule as PMS. Coincidence?
- Even greens kill.
- Backstab means never having to say you're sorry.
- A bow without arrows seems awfully heavy all of a sudden.
- Merchants are perfectly capable of kicking tail and taking names.
- Trains have the right of way.
- If it's stupid but it works, it isn't stupid.
- The guards are never there when you need them.
- Misuse of a combine button in a hurry = highly colorful language.
- The time it takes to upload the newest patch is directly proportional to your eagerness to log on.
- No two people have ever agreed on a single, precise definition of a twink.
- Never forget that your newbie weapon was crafted by the lowest bidder.
- A roleplayer drinks to increase his alcohol tolerance; an idiot does it in the Kelethin treetops.
- Having a tenth of a bubble of health left is Nature's way of telling you to slow down.
- Gnome wizards are proof positive that size does not matter.
- The greatest mystery in Norrath is how NPCs figure out what religion you are just by looking at you.
- Every shaman on Norrath at one point in his or her life has done the pig joke when someone asked for a SOW.
- Only twelve people in all of Norrath are truly happy with their last names.
- The last bubble takes forever to fill.
- It is somehow discouraging to realize that Qeynos is "SonyEQ" spelled backwards.

My First Trip to the Plane of Fear
11:41AM EST - Kadafi Oakbane
Last night, the Justifiers had planned to go to the Plane of Fear, hopefully for plenty of good times and good loot. They were also nice enough to allow myself and another member of the Knights Hospitaller to participate. I arrived early, in my desire to complete my first raid on Cazic-Thule's ethereal lair, to find a lone dwarf of many years, Agate the Scaled Claw, systematically killing Cyndreela, punishing her for her misguided devotion to the twisted god.

He told me of certain changes that had recently taken place on the home turf of Cazic-Thule. Apparently, the god had become weary of being devastated week after week as bands of adventurers skillfully crawled across his plane. In fact, the previous three attempts at entering the Plane of Fear had been thwarted by Cazic-Thule and his minions!

This gave me great pause. Did I want to risk multiple deaths for no reward whatsoever? Did I think the Justifiers could succeed where so many others had failed? As I pondered these questions, Alek, Guild Master of the Justifiers appeared. He strode up to the portal confidently, and invited me to his group.

I told him the disturbing news I had heard from Agate, and they talked with each other about the changes that had happened with the Plane of Fear. Agate informed Alek that the Legions of Darkness were interested in lending a hand if it was necessary.

Slowly, the room outside the portal to Fear began to swell with Justifiers. Eventually there were 30 of them in the room! Truly, this was an awesome fighting force to send against Cazic-Thule. I steeled myself for the impending break in. Alek gave the signal, "GO!"

I entered the Plane, pointed Northwest as I was ordered, and began taking careful note of where I was, should I be struck down in my mad dash to our predetermined gathering point, near the northwest corner of the zone. By some miracle of Tunare, I was left unmolested during my run across the zone. I camped.

When the 'all clear' signal was given, I returned to Fear, and joined with those that were alive. Truly, I had not seen so many fallen gathered in one place since my early days in the bedroom of Lower Guk. Dutifully, the clerics breathed life into their fallen comrades. I did what I could to help those who had been revived deal with their sickness, so the clerics could spend their prayers on more pressing concerns.

Cazic-Thule would not let us gather our strength without challenge, however. He sent some of his minions to disrupt our preparations, but they were cut down quickly. This gave rise to my heart. The Justifiers were well organized, dealing with threats rationally and coolly, with never a moment of panic detected.

The entire trip across the Plane was that way. When Cazic-Thule began his rampages of Death-Touching, people quickly hid themselves from his sight. When Dracoliche came, we put him down, at great cost to our ranks, but I never detected a hint of fear, other than the magical sort that beast instilled. As the evening waned, some had to leave, to attend to other matters.

We had not forgotten the generous offer of the Legion of Darkness, however. Soon, our ranks had refilled to 20, then 25. We were ready. Cazic-Thule's time on his plane grew short. We dealt with Fright, Dread, and Terror in short order. We gathered near Cazic-Thule's cave. A decision had to be made. Someone would have to take the first Death Touch. I was not brave enough to volunteer, but Stickboy, a brave Lizard from the southern continent, stepped forward.

The slaying of Cazic-Thule was quick, with only two lost during the battle. It was over so quickly, I could not believe it. He was dead. He had touched my soul once that evening, sending me careening to Trakanon's Teeth, and that was all the mark he left on me. I was elated. Not only had I been to a plane for the first time, but I had participated in the slaying of a god. I had served Tunare's will.

I would like to thank the Justifiers for taking me with them and making this experience much less painful than I had thought it would be. I would like to thank the Legion of Darkness for providing the manpower, firepower, and expertise we needed to destroy Cazic-Thule so quickly. I would also like to thank Tunare for seeing me through so many seasons of life, that I might serve her in this way.

Thanks Ristorette!
