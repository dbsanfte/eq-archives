Guild Leadership

·        Government of the guild will be performed through a Council of guild members.

·        The number of initial council members was established at five (5). Current Council consists of six (6) members.

·        The current six Council members will determine the addition of other Council members in the event that the growth of the guild requires additional representation on the Council.

·        The council currently consists of:

  • Kumasho Rongushotto

  • Bareldi Notebender

  • Rebekkah Du’Charme

  • Comb Ustion

  • Jantiz Deathbringer

  • Ketch Stormbiter

 Advisors

  • Didayne.

  •  Melbasset Andjam

·        Higher level druids and wizards will serve as acting officers for ease of teleporting around Norrath to invite new members. Acting officers have no Council powers, only the ability to invite new members. Acting officers must go through the same procedure for inducting new members as all other guild members. They may not arbitrarily invite new members.

·        The position and title of “Guild Leader” is for technical purposes only in order to have established and formed the guild and in order to technically establish guild officers according to Verant.

·        Guild issues must be brought to the attention of a current guild officer in order for any, if necessary, action to be taken.

·        The guild officer to which the action is brought to will have the responsibility of promptly posting the issue in the Council section of the guild’s web site. NOTE: This section of the web site will be accessible only by the guild’s Council members. All issues will be handled confidentially.

·        Guild Council will have five (5) days from the initial posting to comment and decide on a course of action, if necessary.

·        Council decisions will be made based on “majority rules”.  For example, if the guild currently has five officers, the majority is three of these officers.

·        If the situation warrants and all guild officers are not available to comment within the five-day period on the issue at hand, the majority will be based on those officers that have commented.

·        After a Council decision, Council members have the obligation to elect a Council member to communicate the decision results to the Guild family.

·        This Council member will have the responsibility of posting the Council’s decision to the guild web site for full guild viewing.

·        This Council member will also have the responsibility of communicating any decision explanations to guild members in the event one is requested. 

·        Guild members are entitled to an explanation of Council’s actions when they request one.

Guild Recruitment

·        Invitations to become members of the guild will be based on the recommendation of a current guild member(s).

·        The sponsoring guild member will have been required to have grouped or played with the potential candidate to determine their compatibility and fit with the guild.

·        The sponsoring guild member will have the responsibility of posting the potential candidate name in the Prospective Members area of the guild web site in order to place the candidate up for review.  In addition, it would be beneficial to consistently remind guild members while on-line to review the Prospective Members area during the three-day review period.

·        Current guild members are encouraged to post their acceptance or objections on the recruitment of the potential candidate.

·        The review period is three days from the day of the post from the sponsoring guild member.  If the sponsoring guild member posts on a weekend, the three day period begins on the Monday following the post (only because current members normally do not read the web site on weekends….too busy playing!!).

·        After the three-day period, the comment period is closed.

·        Silence is Acceptance!  If there were no objections or comments at all on the guild web site, it will be assumed that the potential candidate is acceptable.

·        If there were no objections, it is the responsibility of the sponsoring guild member to arrange a time/place for the actual invite to take place between the candidate and a current officer.

·        It is also the responsibility of the sponsoring guild member to ensure that the new recruit is aware of the guild web site and has read the material herein.

·        Secondary or alternate characters of current guild members will be invited automatically into the guild without a review process since the primary character is already a current guild member.

·        All races and classes are welcome into this guild.

·        Former guild members are welcome to rejoin the guild at any time provided that the former guild member has not, during his/her time away, developed into a character that violates our guild guidelines.

Code of Conduct & General Guidelines

·        Guild members are encouraged to play fairly and represent the guild in all actions honorably.

·        This guild is about the quality of its members, that each member play honorably and fairly and respect other players accordingly.

·        Guild members are encouraged to group with other players outside the guild in order to further expose the presence of the guild.

·        Guild members should have fun but not at the expense or belittlement of others.

·        Guild members are asked to help fellow guild members where and when possible and if feasible.

·        If a guild member has an inventory item that he/she wishes to sell or dispose of, guild members are encouraged to offer it to other guild members first (but not required) prior to offering the item on open auction.

·        However, guild members are encouraged to fairly compensate the “selling” guild member for their costs in a trade-skill product or a reasonable value for other items.

·        Certain races and classes will have inherent abilities/skills available to them.  Such guild members are encouraged to assist other guild members as much as possible without causing detriment to themselves.    However, other guild members will not demand or expect any such services just because there is a guild member that can provide that service.

·        Guild members are encouraged to respect other guild members’ game time.

·        Given the large level spread of the guild, high-level guild members will be able to offer great assistance to lower level players. However, please remember that our higher level players also have things on their own agendas. If you ask for assistance and a guild member can help, great! If you ask for assistance from a guild member and he/she says that they are tied up, accept that as fact and try to work out your issue on your own, or seek the assistance of another guild member. It is rare that everyone is tied up at once, and though you may not get the assistance you originally requested, someone should be able to take care of you.

·        Attendance to a guild event is not mandatory.

·        Ninja looting will not be tolerated.

·        Kill stealing will not be tolerated.

·        Intentional vendor killing will not be tolerated regardless of the vendor’s race.  Other players need the vendors to buy and sell.

·        Intentional training onto someone or another group of players will not be tolerated.

·        Squabbling with other guild members in the guild chat channel will not be tolerated. If you have an issue with another guild member, work it out in /tells. If you can not resolve it together, contact a Council member via /tells. 

Leaving the Guild

·        Guild members can leave the guild at any time with no questions asked.

·        Council officers that will be leaving the guild are asked to contact another Council member prior to their departure so that the process can be set in motion to elect a replacement member to Council.

Violation of Guild Guidelines

·        Transgressions by a current guild member need to be reported to a Council member.

·        Based on the severity of the transgression, a warning will be issued to the guild member that he/she has violated a basic guild guideline.

·        A single report from a player outside of the guild or from another guild member may or may not warrant a warning from the Council to a “transgressing” guild member, depending on the situation.  The Council shall make every effort to isolate incidents that are intended to be vindictive from those incidents that are true violations of guild guidelines

·        Frequent reports on violations of guild guidelines from different players within different zones will result in a warning from the Council to the “transgressing” guild member.

·        Council will follow the assumption that an individual with a history of “misunderstandings” will have created the evidence against him/herself.

·        Consistent warnings (three in total) to a guild members will be cause for a decision by the Council for possible probation within or ejection from the guild.

Council holds the right to review any severe violations on a case-by-case basis in order to determine if the consistent warnings should be waived and immediate ejection from the guild is warranted.
