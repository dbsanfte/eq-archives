INSTALLATION

Before installing EverQuest, make sure that your PC specifications meet or exceed the requirements listed.

Insert the EverQuest game CD into your CD-ROM drive. If your CD-ROM drive has the AutoPlay function enabled, the installation will begin automatically. If AutoPlay is not enabled, Open the "My Computer" icon on your desk top, double-click on the CD-ROM icon, then double-left-click on "setup.exe". The installation process will then commence.

  1. Choose an installation size. The "full" installation will install all of the required game files, plus the intro movie, whereas the "typical" installation will only install the required game files. To view the intro movie with a Typical install, the EverQuest CD will need to be inserted into the CD-ROM drive. (Note: The movie is not available on the Phase IV BETA CD).

    2. Typical requires 500MB* of free disk space.

    Full requires 600MB* of free disk space.

  2. Select a directory in which you would like to install EverQuest to. The default directory is C:/Program Files/EverQuest. If you would like to install the files into a different directory, change the path and proceed. If the directory does not exist, select a directory name(Example: C:/Program Files/Eqrules), then proceed.

  3. Installing the DirectX 6 drivers.

    4. During the installation of EverQuest, and option to install DirectX 6 to your hard drive will be given. If you already have DirectX 6 installed, select "No" when prompted. If you are unsure that DirectX 6 is installed on your machine, you may check by running the "DXSETUP.exe", which is located in the Program Files/DirectX directory. To proceed with the installation of the DirectX 6 drivers, select "Yes" to proceed, then follow the on-screen instructions.

  4. After completing the installation, you will be prompted to restart your computer, select "Yes" and allow your system to reboot.

Version 1.1   3/15/99

This document contains information to troubleshoot and solve problems that you may encounter while playing EverQuest. If you are unable to locate troubleshooting information regarding a problem that you are having, click here for contact information.

Please Note: This is a living document and will change often as we discover solutions to various problems and post them. Come back and check this often if you are experiencing problems as we will keep it as up to date as possible.

Sections

I. System Requirements
II. Technical Issues
III. Video Card Problems
IV. Sound Problems
V. Connectivity

I. System Requirements

Windows® 95 or Windows® 98

Pentium 166 (or greater) (PII 200+ suggested) (Note older Model Cyrix
processors WILL NOT WORK with EverQuest.)

32 MB RAM (64 MB or higher suggested)

Direct3D or Glide compliant card w/ 4MB RAM

28.8k bps Internet connection

Sound Blaster compatible soundcard

Microsoft compatible mouse

8x CD-ROM drive

Anyone who ever worked with a programer or marketer knows exactly what "minimum requirements" mean. For those who don't then you should expect to have a poor preformance and experiance if your at the minimum. Minimum is great if you only want the most basic and flaky experiance or your a poweruser who can tweek things to work.

II. General Technical Issues

If you are running EverQuest for the first time, or are encountering sound and video related problems, please make sure that you are using the most current drivers available on the hardware manufacturer's web-site. In addition, we STRONGLY advise you to go check out the Video Card FAQ.

Make sure that DirectX 6.0 or newer is installed on the system when running EverQuest. DirectX 6.0 is included on the EverQuest CD.

Problem: When attempting to login to the World Server or while playing, the game crashes to Windows.

Solution: Make sure that you have at least 100 megabytes of free space on the drive that the EverQuest directory is located on. In addition, you will need to have Windows managing your virtual memory memory AND that you have at least 200 meg free on the drive Windows resides (usually Drive C).

Problem: A Windows Dialog box comes up that says illegal operation error when clicking on the Play EverQuest button on the Server Selection screen.

Solution: There are a few possibilities relating to this error. The most likely is that you are using an older model Cyrix processor. These processors don't work with EverQuest. To confirm this, we recommend calling our Tech Support. Click here for our contact information.

Problem: Every time you patch it downloads the file eqgame.ex_.

Solution: This most likely means you have the CIH virus on your system. It is extremely important that you rid your system of this Virus for a lot of reasons (EverQuest being one of them). We recommend going to http://www.avp.com and downloading the Anti Viral Toolkit Pro. This will find and clean your system. Please note that this Virus DID NOT come with EverQuest. It is most likely gotten by downloading programs over the Net.

Problem: When running the patcher a message comes up that says "DNS Error".

Solution: This usually means you aren't correctly connected to the Internet. Please make sure you are connected to your Internet provider and try again. If you need help, please call our Tech Support. Click here for our contact information.

III. Video card problems

Problem: When running EverQuest with a Voodoo 1 based chipset in Glide mode, item icons, on-screen overlays, and the chat has a pink border.

Solution: This problem is solved with the latest drivers from 3dfx. You can either go to http://www.3dfx.com and get the drivers there, or go to the Video Card FAQ section and you can get the link to the correct driver there.

Problem: When running EverQuest with an ATI Rage 2, Rage 2+, or Rage Pro based chipset, pink borders will appear around the item icons, on-screen overlays, and chat.

Solution: This means you don't have the latest drivers. Please go to the Video Card FAQ section.

Problem: While attempting to run EverQuest with a Voodoo based chipset, the game crashes at the "EverQuest" loading screen and an error window with the message, "VXD error, expecting 1.4, found 1.X".

Solution: You are not running the most recent drivers for your video card. Please perform the following procedure; Click on the "Start" button located on your Windows taskbar. Next, highlight "Find", then select "Files or Folders…". Then type "Glide.*" in the "Named" field and click the "Find Now" button. After the search for the "Glide.*" file is complete, delete all of the "Glide.*" files from the list. Once the Glide files have been deleted, you will then need to install the most recent drivers from the card manufacturer's web-site.

Problem: When using a card with a Banshee based chipset, you are unable to run EverQuest in Glide.

Solution: Currently Glide mode in EverQuest does not work with the Banshee Chipset, so you won't even see it is an option. The reason for this is a known problem in Glide 3 that 3dfx is working on. They have just released new drivers that appear to fix the problem. After we have completed testing of these drivers we will turn Glide mode back on for the Banshee.

Problem: When using the ATI Rage Fury many graphical problems occur

Solution: Go to the Video Card FAQ section and download the latest drivers right from our site. These drivers ARE NEWER then the ones on the ATI site. Once these go live on the ATI site we will take the file down from our site and put in a link to theirs.

Problem: The game is locking up after clicking on Play EverQuest on the server selection screen on the loading screen with the Elf Woman on it.

Solution: Many errors occur at this point, almost all of them are related to not having the most current drivers for your card. Lots of problems occur with Riva TNT cards in this area if you don't have the latest drivers. Make sure you have the ones we have tested EverQuest with by going to the Video Card FAQ section.

Problem: Your Voodoo2 locks up when switching between resolutions.

Solution: Most likely you DO have the latest drivers. The ones that are up on the 3dfx site (and most of the card vendors) actually have a known bug in Glide 3.0 - To solve this, go to the older drivers in the Video Card FAQ section. 3dfx is working on this problem, so hopefully the next release of the V2 drivers will fix this problem.

Problem: EverQuest is very dark and the Gamma Sliders don't seem to work.

Solution: Currently the Gamma Correction only works in Glide mode. It will be fixed very shortly in D3D. If you are using D3D mode, use your cards video control center to make the game brighter.

Problem: EverQuest seems to run very slowly on your machine.

Solution: EverQuest is a very high end application. We recommend you increase your ram first. Next is to make sure to have a 3d card that is modern by today's standards. A Voodoo2, a Riva TNT or something along those lines. We also recommend turning off particle effects (on the Display Options menu in game). We also strongly recommend turning Sound Realism down to the lowest setting as this will also help with framerate.

IV. Sound

Problem: I don't get any music in the game.

Solution: As of March 16th, EverQuest only supports Sound Font Capable sound cards for music. These cards are the Sound Blaster Awe32, Sound Blaster Awe64, Sound Blaster 128PCI and the SB Live. We plan VERY SHORTLY to turn music on all sound cards.

Problem: I don't get any sound in the game -

Solution: Make sure you have the latest drivers for your soundcard. To get the one's we've currently tested please go to the Sound Card FAQ section. If you still don't get sound please contact our Technical Support. Click here for our contact information.

Problem: I don't get music on my SB Live

Solution: Go to your AWE control Panel and increase the SoundFont cache setting to 3 Meg or more. If you need help with this please click here for our contact information.

V. Connectivity

Problem: I can't seem to stay connected for more than about 15 minutes. It then gives me the "You have been disconnected" Screen.

Solution: Unfortunately this is most likely the symptom of a bad connection to the game. If this happens consistently we would recommend contacting your ISP. In most cases the answer you are going to get is "everything is fine"…which in most cases isn't true. You may want to evaluate other ISP's.

Problem: When attempting to run "EverQuest.exe", the following error occurs; "ERROR Host not found, no DNS entry for host"

Solution: Make sure that you are properly connected through your ISP(Internet Service Provider). If you have verified that you are "on-line", and you are still receiving the error, contact your ISP.

Problem: Every time I do a "Ping" or a TraceRoute to the EverQuest servers I get a "Request Timed Out".

Solution: We have blocked ICMP traffic at our ISP, so you won't be able to do Ping's or Traceroutes to us. You can Traceroute to the last hop along the way to our servers to check for any type of bottlenecks. This will most likely show you any problems anyway.

Problem: My network runs Firewall/Proxy software, what "ports" need to be opened by my Network Administrator?

Solution: Let your Network Administrator (or whoever set up the network) know the following information:

Open TCP connections to/from patch.everquest.com, port 7000.
Open UDP to all incoming and outgoing traffic on all ports over 1024.
