What Enchanters Do Best, and other txt
by Azeraphel of Solusek Ro
posted 5/20/99 9:13:48 PM


I posed the question to GZ that has been posed before; what is it that makes enchanters valuable? What makes
us worthwhile? And he actually gave an answer that makes a bit of sense.

Me:
I guess my real question is, what is it an enchanter is meant to be to a party? Why would a party want an
enchanter, as opposed to any other class? Wizards burn much better, mages have much better pets, necros
have much better DoT, clerics and shamans have much better buffs. We seem to be the "do just about
everything, but poorly" class.

GZ:
Best speed up attacks (warriors will love you), slow down monster attacks, whirl, mesmerize, AOE mesmerize,
Charm, lull, soothe, etc.

So, it seems to be all about combat pace and crowd control. If you think about it, it's a fairly valuable function. I
don't think it's what we all figured the class would be about, but it's a decent answer.

We do have the only haste/slow combination, to my knowledge. This tactic doesn't become available until level
16, however. That's a long, long ways to go for the newbie enchanter, though... a class that doesn't come into its
own until level 16 is a tough class indeed.

He also talked about resist rates. All enchanter combat spells fall under the all-or-nothing 65% success
category, with a few exceptions. (Mem Blur) Damage spells generally have an 80% chance of success. Necro
DoT falls under the 80% category as well, while ours does not. Tashan, according to GZ, lowers that resist rate
by about 15%; later installments of the spell do even more.

Effectively, on a Tashaned creature, we have the same success rates on all of our spells as other casters do on
their DD. This does mean, however, that Tashan is not really optional, if we want to be effective.

Anyway, GZ has more or less made a believer out of me. I'm sticking with the class, problematic though it is.

Though, if he gimps another one of our spells, I'm driving to california with a baseball bat.
