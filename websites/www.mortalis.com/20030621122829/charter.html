Our general motto is to be fair to everyone around us; to try and help each other out as much as possible and to be able to do the things that not every one is able to do. This guild is about accomplishments, teamwork, pride in each other, and efficiency. Our focus is to be the betterment of the guild, never the individual. In order to do this we all need to follow some sort of guideline to keep things running smooth. The following define the basic rules of the guild, how to handle problems and our loot rules.
Expectations

Mortalis is a guild of hard work and honour. We base our efforts on team-work, energy and a sound and historic knowledge of Everquest; we strive to be the best we can be. However we also believe that we should at all times be as fair as reasonably possible with those around us in Everquest, guild or non-guild. We respect that people play this game during their free time. It is a game, but a game in which many people invest a huge amount of time and skill. Playing within a high level guild requires the basic understanding that people are busy. Players have a long history and play for experience in their own way to compensate for the rogiurs of combat in new zones. They also conduct themselves professionally: if you require the services of a certain class i.e. for res, CR or for transport you request it politely. People will get to you when they are able.
Remember that people playing during their free time is just that, their time to do what they want to do! NO ONE is forced to do any thing they don't want to do.
If some one tells you "No, I am busy" then you need to find another option and leave them alone. DO NOT harass people please!
Even though you will never be forced to do things, it is highly encouraged that you help out your friends and guildmates whenever possible.
  I will be honest with everyone right now - If you need special attention, if you need a group the second you log in to the game, if you expect everyone to hear every thing you say - then you are in the WRONG guild! This is a big guild, not every one is going to know each other right away. YOU as members need to make yourselves noticed and make the effort to help each other out. Showing up to our raids is the best way to interact with each other and meet friends. YOU need to make the effort to READ the message boards to keep yourself informed of guild events and raids.
Respect to Others Guild Chat

Guildsay is NOT your personal auction block. Keep the auctions or trades of items or the pricing of items OUT of guildsay. If you need help with this type of thing, please ask some one you feel comfortable with and use /tells.
If there is ever an argument, it is to be taken to /tells immediately! Dragging an argument into guildsay is unacceptable!
Use of racial, derogatory remarks or verbal abuse of a person with the intent to hurt is completely unacceptable and can result in immediate guild removal.
While it is important to keep a sense of humour, sometimes people do get carried away. Please be considerate towards those who may be sensitive to what you say and to those who just like to have quiet guild chat.
Please keep foul language to a minimum.
Don't spam guildsay with requests for a resurrection or a teleport. If you need this type of service then look for some one near your area and ask them using /tells.
Whining in guild chat is annoying, don't do it.
  Complaints

If an issue comes up where you have a loot, camp or grouping problem that you can not deal with on your own, then please contact your class leader, an officer or myself for assistance. However, we are NOT babysitters! Try to resolve things on your own if possible! NEVER argue with people using /shout or /ooc. Take it to /tells please! Don't be afraid to talk to some one if you have a problem, please talk to us, that is what we are here for.
  Day to Day Grouping

It is YOUR responsibility to find or make groups. Many times guild-only groups are not possible. Some times there simply is not room for more people in certain areas. Don't take these types of things personally. There are tons of options for people to get experience, sometimes it's just not the place you might wish.
You need to take the initiative and form a group if you can't find a group yourself. Any 4 people in this guild can kill almost any blue mob in the game. You have to take what you can get and grab whoever may be asking for groups at the zone you are in. If you are going to limit yourself to guild-only groups with enchanters and clerics every time, then you are just going to frustrate yourself.
  Help with Quests and FA Items

YOU need to know what to do and how to handle your quest! It is YOUR responsibility to gather the information you need so that every one helping you fully understands what is going on. Know as much as you can before every one is gathered so that there is as little delay as possible.
Plan ahead - Have a time and date as to when you need help and where to meet. Arrange your own transportation for those who are coming to help as well.
Post a message on the board so that people see that you need help on a certain day.
Contact the people you need help from in game and plan ahead with those people. Obviously certain classes are needed for certain things.
Be PATIENT and reasonable as to when and what you need. You can't expect people to drop what they are doing and come help you at a moment's notice. You also can't expect to post a message on the message board and expect everyone to read it and be there. YOU need to plan ahead, YOU need to ask the people that you need there, YOU need to organize it.
  Raids

Guild sponsored raids are those designated before hand by officers. It will generally include ST, NToV dragons, SoV mobs, VP, SoL bosses etc.
EVERY ONE IS EXPECTED TO BE AT A PRE-PLANNED RAID IF YOU ARE ONLINE! It is one thing if you have Real Life things to do, or if you can't be on for an extended period of time. It is the people that are camping experience and 'dodging' raids that are the problem. The reason you are in this guild is to raid the hard stuff and that means that EVERY ONE is needed. I have been told that "I need experience" or "There is no loot there for me" or "That is such a boring raid, I'd rather go to sleep". This kind of attitude will not be tolerated, if you can't be a team player then it is time for you to find another team.
IF YOU ARE LATE, YOU ARE EXPECTED TO MAKE AN EFFORT TO GET TO WHERE WE ARE! Logging in 30 minutes after we started is no excuse for you to not try to get to where we are! The least you can do is try to help out. Also try to park your character in an area that is convenient for transports or even better, place your character at the meeting point itself so you are ready to go. Please be prepared prior to an event with any supplies needed as well, such as tiny coffins, peridots, food and water, pearls, stones for Hate or Sky etc. (pearls & peridots are supplied via guildfund)
NON-GUILD WILL BE TREATED FAIRLY ON RAIDS! Of course we will not go out of our way to invite non-guild people, but if they happen to be in an area we are raiding, we WILL respect them and treat them fairly. People that feel we should shut others out just because they don't have our guild tag can /guildremove right now.
  MANDATORY - Means that if you are online you are expected to be at the event that the guild is at. It does not mean that you call in sick to work or give up RL things to be at the raid!
  NON-MANDATORY - Means that you are NOT forced to be at the event, but it would be nice to have every one there helping.
