Journeyman Boot Quest Guide

Step 1(optional) - Track and talk to Hasten Bootstrutter in the Rathe Mountains and he will give you the quest.
Step 2 - Head to South Ro or Ocean of Tears and kill an Ancient Cyclops to retrieve a ring of the ancients.
Step 3 - Kill a Shadowed Man to retrieve a shadowed rapier.
Step 4 - Return to the Rathe Mountains and give the gold and the items to Hasten to receive your Journeyman Boots.

Step 1 (Optional) – Find the Gnome

     Target and hail Hasten Bootstrutter.  This sounds easier then it is for Hasten is one fast little gnome.  He will easily outrun a player with a 50th level Spirit of the Wolf.  He does have a static route that he travels so please refer to the included map.  I have never seen him initiate combat with another player, but that doesn’t mean he can’t so becareful.  Also I have been told, but never it, he MAY occasionally get stuck under a rock or other obstruction, when this happens you wont be able to target him nor talk to him.  If this happens you can try to petition a GM or get a player to pull him out.

Ring of the Ancients (NO DROP)

Spawn Methods:

There are several rumors as to how to make the Ancient Cyclops spawn in South Ro.  I will start with the most intriguing and one that has been done by several people I know.  Using this method, one individual has been able to make the Ancient Cyclops spawn within 15 minutes from his arrival in the zone, while killing three mobs: 1) a mob named  “crypt mummy” 2) A random spawn of Sand Giant and or a Mummy of various types and finally 3) the Ancient Cyclops.  Although this seems to be a very rare case, this method of killing the place holder, its spawn and then the resulting SG or mummy spawn does seem to lead to the Ancient Cyclops spawn much quicker than annihilating the entire zone.  Let start with this “3 Spawn Point Method”

Spawn Point Method

First in the series is a spawn at a half-buried temple near the sand and grass line of the zone.  The loc is ENTER HERE.  Inside this temple, in the center of the floor, spawns one of the following: a ghoul, dry bones skeleton and the important one “crypt mummy”.  According to several player reports the Ancient Cyclops spawn seems to be linked to the “crypt mummy” spawn. 

1)         The spawn rate for this temple mob is 26 mins.  After killing the “crypt mummy” a Sand Giant and or one of the various kinds of mummies seems spawns within the zone.  This spawn appears to help and or is directly linked to the spawning of the Ancient Cyclops.
2)         The second spawn is the Sand Giant and or Various Mummy spawns mentioned earlier.  The following types of Mummies spawn within the zone: A crypt mummy, lesser mummy and mummy. Search and destroy these four types of spawn within the zone.  Although these don’t seem to be a placeholders for the “crypt mummy”, it does seems to assist in the spawning of the AC.
3)         The third spawn, should you be as fortunate as player mentioned earlier, would be the Ancient Cyclops.  All statistical information on the Ancient Cyclops can be found in this guide.  Upon the death of the Ancient Cyclops it will yield the “Ring of the Ancients”.  LOOT IT! 

While this method seems to have the ability to reduce camping time, it DOES NOT mean you will be in and out of South Ro in 20 minutes.  I also know of several cases where this method took between 3 and 6 hrs to yield the Ancient Cyclops.   This does seem to be below the average spawn time for the Ancient Cyclops, which is often 6 to 12, hrs of straight camping for players.  Remember this does in no way mean this is the only way to make the Ancient Cyclops spawn or the best.  Only the Verant Coders know that information.  This is a player-derived technique that does seem to encourage the Ancient Cyclops spawn. 

Kill Em All Method

Although, I will mention it in this guide, it is of course the most obvious and most blunt of the methods I will talk, about you can always resort to the “Kill Em All and Let God Sort Em Out” method.  Although this can produce the Ancient Cyclops, I hope you are saying to yourself, as I once did, “there has to be a better way.”   This Question is what prompted me to begin my many, many hours of information hunting on this subject.  Killing everything in the zone is not only time consuming and attention demanding, you will also begin to thank the Gods of Norrath for the /ignore command.  This zone is home to many 15th to 25th level players and all of who seem to know how to use the repeat last tell feature.  They will begin to use it on you at will, when they find out you are the one raping the zone of all the viable targets.  Many, aren’t aware that this ISN’T the only zone in Norrath that spawns 20th to 25th level mobs and will let you know how much they don’t like what you are doing.  Explaining the circumstances, which have forced you to become Jboot crazy and are currently causing you to annihilate the entire zone, does not seem to appease them.  I recommend either camping during the off hours of EverQuest and (2 to 5 am PST) or choosing an alternate course of action.   

Personal Comments:

If South Ro is your choice of camping locations I would combine with most recent spawn rumor (3 spawn point) with a limited “kill em all” approach.  I recommend hitting the spawn points outlined and thing depending on your level, time and resources killing Mummies and Sand Giants.  Many times there is already a Sand Giant group in the zone, which has its pros and cons.  The nice part is you won’t have to hunt and kill all the Sand Giants that spawn.  However, if the Ancient Cyclops spawns, you will have more competition.

Step 4 – Your Just Rewards

Level: 40th

Type: Melee

*Notes*

No real reason to kill him, No Item loot, No coin loot, No faction adjustments

Travel Path: See included map(Rathe Mtns)

Faction:  Beta Neutral

**Since the recent zone changes I have never found Hasten to be despawned.  In the past there were issues regarding his spawn cycle.

After you are lucky enough to get both the “Ring of the Ancients” and “Shadowed Rapier”, the next step is to return to Hasten and give him the items.  With the items you need to give 3250 gold pieces.  This can be a problem for some characters and we will discuss several ways to solve it.

First the gold weighs 81 stones.  So if you have a Strength buff that can provide you with a STR higher than your normal weight plus 81 stones, you are all set.  If you cant get a buff from a kind player, if you are a good race you will most likely need two buffs to make the run to Rathe Mountains from freeport/Qeynos/Rivervale, then here are some other options.

If you are a caster and can bind yourself to any area you can….. Bind yourself to a safe spot near the Ferrott zone line and that is also in Hasten’s run path within the zone.  Refer to the Rathe Zone map  (I recommend Loc ENTER HERE).  Get a friend, of sufficient level to be able to kill you with you playing the role of a punching bag, to meet you at this loc.  While your friend is running there, you are running to a bank.  Freeport if you are good and Oggok if you are evil and will be allowed in the city.  Once your friend is there, gate back.  Put one trash item in your main Inventory screen.  Have your friend kill you in a duel.  After you recovery from the embarrassment of a duel death.  Go over to your corpse and loot ONLY the money and the ONE trash item.  

1) Now you have a choice, if Hasten is up and in the zone, you can also loot the ring and the rapier from your corpse.  You should be able to run around the zone and find Hasten with your items waiting for you at your corpse.  This is somewhat more dangerous than the next method but faster.  
2) This next method picks up just after you looted the one trash item and your money from your corpse.  Next, give your friend a good reason to want to kill you.  You will need to die in another duel.  After recovery from the duel, go over to your original corpse holding all your gear and loot it.  Now you are fully equipped and you have a personal bank on the ground… i.e. your second corpse.  DON’T LOOT IT!   You want to drag your corpse in the opposite direction of Hasten’s run path.  Shouldn’t take long to run into Hasten along this path.  However, he is short and fast and if you don’t have track or don’t have a tracker with you, I would recommend waiting at loc  ENTER HERE

Regardless of the method you use for transporting the gold, once you find Hasten give him the Ring of the Ancients, Shadowed Rapier and 3250 gold pieces and he will return the Journeyman Boots to you.
