Non EQ news (BG) - Sacrates
I don't believe it, some non EQ news. Looks like Greg Peterson was over on the Boards and had a bit to say on the release etc of Baldur's Gate. Check it.

1) Scratched CDs. They suck. If you get a scratched CD, return your product for a replacement. Of course, if you can play the game with the scratched CD, it is your choice. The CD holder we used with Baldur's Gate we have also used with other games and haven't had many problems previously. For future multi-disk games, we will be doing something else. Some people have made some good suggestions for alternatives, like shipping CDs in 3-ring binder plastic sheets with the manual in a binder, etc. (similar to Falcon 4.0).

2) Multiplayer/Gamespy that doesn't work. We are looking at ALL of the support issues related to the game. Our first priority is to resolve hardware conflicts / driver issues that are preventing people from installing the game. Then we are looking at in-game issues that are causing problems for some systems. Next, we are looking at multiplayer issues. Finally, we are looking at in-game bugs and design recommendations. This is not to say that NO ONE is working on multiplayer until we get driver conflicts resolved, just that these are viewed to be the priority issues. Multiplayer and Gamespy work on many systems by the way, just not on all.

3) Crashes are NOT the same for everyone. There has not been a SINGLE duplicatable (is that a word?) crash bug found yet in this game. Most people are installing and playing the game without problems. Our major area of problems so far has been that Baldur's Gate uses some aspects of DirectX that no other game has used before. It is absolutely imperative that you have the proper drivers and that your hardware supports DirectX. We are working through the issues on a system by system basis. As we get more information, we are constantly updating our troubleshooting guide in the Customer Service Database. While on chat last night, I personally helped 'fix' two systems that people were having problems with - neither of which had anything to do with the game.

Please read the troubleshooting guide in the CS Database if you are one of the minority of people that are having trouble. In priority of order check to see:

a) That you have a video and sound card that is fully DirectX compatible.

b) That you have the most current drivers for the card.

c) That you are not running excessive TSRs on your system (Terminate and Stay Resident programs). These are things like Norton Anti-Virus and Memory Manager programs. If you hit Ctrl-Alt-Delete once in Windows, and you see anything other than "Explorer" and "Systray" you are running other TSRs that may cause problems.

d) Finally, if you HAD been running the game fine, but suddenly had a quirky crash or something, check to make sure your disk cache hasn't gotten corrupted (especially if you have a fragmented disk drive or didn't have much extra space on your drive). Delete the cache file and when you restart the game, the cache will be reloaded from the CD's.

If you have done ALL of the above, and are still having problems, contact our customer support at the links above, and we will get to you!

Alright, this must be official EQ day at Altered Worlds. I have a feeling news is going to really start kicking in on EQ soon so sit back and relax because we'll be there to cover it. Here's Brad talking about how good the Beta testers are doing right now.

Let me clarify a few things.

The majority of testers are doing just fine, and we are deluged with feedback and bug reports. From my perspective, the beta is going even better than we'd hoped, and we are very pleased.

There are all sorts of people in phase 3, and this is according to plan. While .5, 1, and 2 were more carefully screened, with phase 3 we were purposely more lenient. We want 'exploiters' in the game, to help find all sorts of problems. They are quite welcome.
The people we don't want are those who exploit and both don't report it and continue doing it.

They're in the game, but there aren't that many, and we are banning them as quickly as we can (and publically, for that matter -- we broadcast bannings in-game, not to be mean spirited, but to make it clear that we are serious about the matter).

We have the spine, trust me. I've a mailbox full of emails from those banned and all their friends, guildmates, etc., begging to be let back in the game. I rarely respond.

Important Notice: All EQ Beta testers - Sacrates
Mahrin Skel of EQ Vault fame made a post last night that I thought deserved to be slapped up here. This is something EXTREMELY important in regards to Beta testing EQ. I just got my copy late last night so can't really comment on it yet, but he does a good job right here. Read on.......

Okay, it has become blatantly obvious that all too many testers are engaging in rampant exploitation. To give you some idea of what may be exploitation:

1) If a mob gives way more loot and/or XP than others of the same level, and you keep killing it over and over, it's exploitation. To give you an idea of what "too good" is, normal 30th level mobs should not be dropping more than 3 to 5 platinum worth of loot, and no single mob should move your XP bar more than a few pixels. This also goes for any quests, if an 8th level character can complete in a few minutes a quest that pays 5+ platinum it is a bug.

2) If there is a way to kill a mob where it cannot possibly fight back (stuck in walls/behind doors, it keeps running in circles/back and forth, it is in the water and you're on a bridge, etc.), it's exploitation.

3) If you can cast defensive spells in a certain order and they "stack", when they wouldn't if cast in another order, it is exploitation. Defensive spells that are different levels in the same series should *not* stack, if they do it is a bug, and the same goes for many "Enhancement" buffs. If they have similar names, similar effects, and come at different levels, they probably shouldn't stack.

4) Anything that just seems "Too easy" *may* be exploitation. Confirm it, report it with /bug, and move on. Just as a benchmark, *no* solo character (casters with pets are not solo for this definition) should be able to consistently beat anything higher than a "blue" mob, and you should not be able to beat *any* yellow or higher mob singlehanded regardless of method. This isn't my definition, it is 989's. Any combination of tactics that can allow you to do so anyway is imbalanced, and needs to be reported.

Everyone please remember that this is BETA. You're supposed to be helping to balance the game. Exploitation leads the Dev Team to mistaken conclusions about the current state of balance, they believe that certain classes may be much more powerful than they really are. This can have *disastrous* results for the real balance when the exploits are fixed.

Saying "If it isn't supposed to work that way they shouldn't have it in there" is no excuse. It's a Beta, we all know there are bugs, we're not supposed to exploit them, simply report them so they can be fixed. If it is a easily duplicated and imbalancing bug, or if you see it being heavily abused, you may want to /petition it as *well* as /bug it. Remember, the goal is to make a better game, not have the "best" character in Beta. They're all getting wiped eventually, anyway.
