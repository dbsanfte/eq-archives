Unknown:Quest for the Golden Dragon Interview
By Madbard
With: Kevin Ross

Role Playing has become a buzzword in the Computer gaming arena lately and everyone seems to have titles in the pipe. Ross Systems is looking to add some of it's own Magic to the mix and possibly continue this trend. Our boy Madbard recently had a little discussion with Kevin Ross of Ross Systems and this is what came of the conversation. Step up to bat Unknown: Quest for the Golden Dragon.

MadBard: First off could you tell us a little about Ross Systems and the team of people who are working with you on Unknown The Quest For The Golden Dragon?

KR: Ross Systems is a small consulting firm in upstate New York. We specialize in network designs and custom programming. Our Team consists of a wide range of people ranging from programmers to artist. We have Kaid Software, which is working exclusively on the Unknown in producing
the models for the game. They have just come aboard in Late September.With both companies working on the Unknown we have 5 people working on it full time and 13 working on it in a part time bases.

MadBard: What is your background in CRPGs and RPGs in general?

KR: Being a Role Player for over 10 years and a CRPG player for over 8 years I have a wide range and fascination on these type of games. From the early days of the Texas Instruments with Fairy Tale to the beginning of the Bard tales to Might and Magic I play them all. I am an avid D&D and Warhammer player also. I enjoy the imagination required to build a world
for others to play.

MadBard: The first thing people will notice when the read about Unknown is the immense amount of detail that you are putting into the game. You easily win the Altered Worlds "longest FAQ" award. Details such as weather, appropriate clothing, party creation, swimming, eating, sleeping,
alignment, aging, player spell creation, banking, bank robbing, siege weapons and getting drunk are just a few of the things addressed in Unknown. Why did you decide to take on a project of such scope?

KR: The reason I took on such a project was the disappointment of other RPG games. I want to be thrown into a world that I can interact with and have it interact with me. RPG games always seem, to fall short in one aspect or another. If the graphics are great the plot is weak. You would either get skills you never would use, or plots that meant really nothing in the game. We wanted to create a game also that we did not get
just our input but received ideas and views from others. We think this is one of our biggest advantages we listen to what other people have to say, we can not do this all by ourselves. We are not RPG gods, so it is nice to bounce ideas off other RPG players.

MadBard: Tell us a little about magic in the game. How does magic work in Unknown and what different types of magic are there?

KR: Magic is done with Spell Points (Mana). Each of the casting classes, Mystic, Mage, Woodsman and Priest start off with x spell points in the beginning. Each spell they cast cost x of these points. For different type of magic is quite extensive. Besides breaking it up by class we have also broken it up by alignment. So evil Mages and Good mages might have a few of the same spells but they also have different ones. We also have advance classes that they can achieve that open up a whole new list of spells. We also have 20 unique Unknown spells in the game.

MadBard: Are you still planning to allow players to create their own spells?

KR: Yes, this feature is written in but not fully tested. Combining spells together or talking to an NPC Sage who will create the spell for you will do it.

MadBard: Now Unknown will be both a single and multiplayer game. Is the multiplayer version going to be different then the single player version?

KR: Yes the major difference between the multi-player and the single player is the multi-player is actually larger and has more plots. We also move the locations of all the dungeons and cities so someone who plays the single player can not enter the multi-player game and have an advantage.

MadBard: You also say that you plan on having a big game servers like battle-net. How much are you going to charge players for their use?

KR: Right now we are trying to see if we can charge no monthly cost for the use of the game servers. We feel that charging a person to buy the game then play it is not fair.

MadBard: Can a player transfer a character from the single player game into the multiplayer version?

KR: No, the single player characters are non-transferable. Since this would cause an imbalance in the game. Also some items in the single player are not in the multi-player.

MadBard: Will the online multiplayer game world change and grow?

KR: For sure the Quest for the Golden Dragon is just the first chapter of a very large book. The world will expand with new lands, monsters, races and classes.

MadBard: How is communication handle in multiplayer? Can I talk to anyone anytime like in most large CRPGs?

KR: Communication is handled as on screen commands only. You may talk, whisper and shout but it affects the characters only in the area that you are in. No mass communications exists at this time. Right now we are discussing on the message forums how we should handle this, by either displaying over a persons head or in message boxes. Since names are not
revealed in the game it adds a twist to already a complex task. Mass communication is not allowed because it is not RPG like. Being able to talk to a friend on the other-side of the world is not real. They did not have cell phones or telephones back then. You can hire a messenger to deliver a message to the person, which can be sometimes instant.

MadBard: Can people own buildings in Unknown?

KR: Yes, you may own a house; guild and certain shops can be bought in the Unknown.

MadBard: Talk a little on player guilds in the multiplayer game.

KR: Player guilds are something we are working on with the guilds. We currently have given 15 guilds the ability to have us design a guild to his/her specifications. This means if they want a castle for a guild, or an underground cavern or even a house we gave them the choice. We aren taking these 15 guilds and the other 15 we designed and are making these the guildhalls for the game servers. Guildhalls can be bought, freed from evil if they occupied, or your daring taken over by attacking one
already occupied by players. The choice will be the players.

MadBard: How do you address player killing in the online version? And how does your jail system work?

KR: Player killing is a fact of life so we had to approach it from a fair viewpoint. We have a level 3 system. Your level can not be any more then 3 levels below the person you wish to kill. This includes parties also. An entire party's level can not be 3 levels below the person or persons you wish to kill. This rule is voided though if the person attacks you first. Our jail system works by actual putting a person in jail until his/ her time is up. They can be rescued if you have friends daring enough to try it. The times can range to a few minutes to a few days. This is real life, if you do the crime you will do the time. Since names, levels health points and spell points are not displayed when touching a person it will be harder for a pker to figure who he/she is attacking. Are the pkrs willing to swing at someone wearing no armor and is a 25th level and they do not know it. We have over 15 rules in the game to combat it. We came up with a fair system that allows it but does place limits on it.

MadBard: What types of transportation will there be in Unknown?

KR: Right now we have walking, running, riding a horse and soon flight. Ships are used for water travel.

MadBard: Just how big is the game world?

KR: The world is hard to compare to games on the market. We currently feel we are about 25% smaller then UO world. We have 7 Major cities, 14 smaller villages over 50+ dungeons, caves, keeps, castles complex sewer systems to explore in the game. Not counting mountains, deserts and
forest to run around in.

MadBard: What perspective is Unknown played in?

KR: The Unknown can be played in 1st or 3rd perspective. 3rd perspective can only be used outside open areas and right now it done from 6 pre-determined views. We are working on a tracking camera that will allow the player to keep 3rd view on all the time. But will never reveal his/her back. None of us have eyes behind our heads.

MadBard: What engine are you using to make Unknown?

KR: Due to NDA agreements we can not go into our engine. It is a joint adventure with another company.

MadBard: Are you planning on having 3Dfx support?

KR: For sure, along with D3D support.

MadBard: Now a big part of any RPG is character creation. How is this handled in Unknown? And how many races and classes will you have?

KR: Characters are created upon entering the game. You must sign up first with a name of the character and a password. This is not immediate the name of your character has to be approved by us and you are sent back a reply stating that name has been added. You then proceed to the character creation, you roll for basic attributes, and then you are asked what race you wish to be. We then allow you to pick your class based on the attributes you have. After this you are given your base skills, and are allowed to pick 2 additional skills. Then we give you
skill points that can be used to adjust your character as you see fit. By increasing or decreasing skills or attributes or spell points. We currently have 14 races listed with 2 still not revealed we have 15 classes I with 3 of them still not revealed.

MadBard: About how many different skills will be available to learn in the game?

KR: Currently 54 skills exist in the game.

MadBard: And lastly about how much of Unknown is complete right now?

KR: Currently the game is over the 50% mark. We would be closer tocompletion but has you might already know we are converting the entiregame to 16bit. This is taken time and some re-coding. Along with addingcolored lighting, smoke affects, glow and other visual affects to thegame.
