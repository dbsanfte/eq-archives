EQ and Norrathian Language - Sacrates
Abashi was again back on the boards keeping us all informed and enlightened in the ways of Everquest. This time he talks a bit on good and evil in Everquest. Seems everyone is being philisophical today.

Norrath is a completely new world with its own vast history and virtually limitless diversity. The common phrase "Good kills evil, evil kills everyone" has no place in Norrath, as the concepts of good and evil are in the eyes of the beholder. We use the terms good and evil in their generic sense from the standpoint of a good human. However, in Norrath, dark elves do not beleive they are evil, they are oppressed and fighting back. Ogres and Trolls don't believe they are evil, they just know that the humans, dwarves, and elfkin try to kill them on sight, and also make a mighty fine stew.

Human Paladins certainly believe the Dark elves, ogres, and trolls are evil, but those races likewise think the Human Paladins are evil.

I realize this concept may be difficult for some people to understand, as most books are told from the standpoint of someone who is "good". I have also read many books told from the standpoint of someone "evil" who thought all those who we think of as "good" were evil. Both types of books can be very convincing.

But back on the concept of Norrath, Norrath is a land where it is taboo to kill your own. One can certainly take that path if you like, as a Dark elf who slaughters their dark bretheren, but don't expect to be welcomed back into the bousom of Neriak if you do. Though the possibilities are limitless, do not expect that by becoming a random killer you will end up liked anywhere. In fact, should you wish to play the role of an indiscriminant, expect to be treated like one by all you meet.

I hope I've clarified the Norrathian situation to you. I'm sure if you think about it long enough, you will see that this format allows for infinite possibilities. You can play that human Paladin gone bad, or that Dark Elf who is really a magically altered High elf. You can play that Ogre who thinks dwarves are soft and cuddly, and you can play that halfling who for some reason just cannot stand wood elves.

Beta 4 and PvP info - Sacrates
Here's a bit of info from Abashi regarding what is going to happen when we go from Phase 3.5 to 4. Also some news on the PvP server also.

Current testers will have the opportunity to register one character for restoration prior to the start of phase 4. This will allow them to retain their levels and receive a small reimbursement in the way of gold for the continued testing of mid and higher level zones.

However, they will not be able to get their characters restored on the "PvP Only" server in Phase4.

Additionally, though the ability to move a character between servers will be in for final, and possibly the middle of phase4, no one will ever be able to move a character TO a "PvP Only" server. Whether they will be able to move it FROM (retaining PvP setting) is still being decided.

The reason they will not be allowed to move TO a PvP server is out of a matter of fairness. One of the issues stated by PvP server advocates is that they didn't like the idea that someone would be able to build a character up and then go PvP. This philosophy is being embraced for the P4 PvP server, and will be kept into final should a PvP server be financially viable.

If you wish to adventure on a PvP server, you must build the character there starting at level 1.

CGO on Odium - Sacrates
CGO has been on the ball lately. This time they are posting a Preview of the highly anticipated Odium formerly named Gorky 17. (Thank god for the name change)

The game is a strong mixture of role-playing (RPG) and certain tactical-style "strategy" games; RPGers familiar with Fallout or strategy gamers who've played X-COM or Jagged Alliance should all feel comfortable with the basic structure of the game. On the RPG side you've got the story and character elements. Aside from Sullivan himself you may add up to three other characters to your party as you move through the city. Despite the physical danger posed by over 40 different, err, "entities" (we don't want to give too much away, but we can say that it sounds like Sullivan will leave the aliens to the X Files crew), all is not combat. Character development will play a big part in the game, as will character/NPC interactions. To add to the tension, that which you would normally fight may instead be an unlikely ally, while those you assume to be friends could turn out to be your deadliest enemies. Expect to encounter many flip-flops like this in the game; Monolith won't go into specifics but it sounds like you won't be able to figure out the game's ending until you actually reach it.
