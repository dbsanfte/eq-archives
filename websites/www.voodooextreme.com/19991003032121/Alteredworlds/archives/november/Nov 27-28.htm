Feist and RTK - Sacrates
How can you not like a game with this kind of support behind it? Ray Feist was on the RTK message boards again spewing out tips and other fun stuff on Return to Krondor. He also had this to say on why him and Sinjin are there.

Folks,

Just wanted to take a moment to post a short message on why Sinjin (when he gets back from vacation) and I are here.

While I'll try to offer hints now and again and offer suggestions about problems, that's not our reason for being here. If you have a serious problem with the game, head over to Sierra's Web site and talk to Tech Support there, or use one of the other methods as outlined in your game materials.

As for hints, there are plenty of folks around here who are about to finish the game and will be happy to share hints with newcomers.

What we're here to do is to talk about the game, or more pointedly, listen.

Return to Krondor, like Betrayal at Krondor before it, is one of a series of computer entertainments we're developing. We want to "cherry pick" the best features from both games as we consider what we're doing next.

We're taking note of the things you like and the things you dislike, and we will try to answer questions where we can about how decisions got made; realize, though, that this game in particular went through a very weird evolution as it moved from one design team to another.

But, whatever you have to say, good or bad, we'll listen. We may not always agree with your point of view, but we'll certainly listen.

Here he is again on the games length.

As I said in an earlier question, if you're fast, and go straight from point to point, and get lucky so you don't have to re-fight too many battles, you can do it in 20 hours. 30 hours and up is the norm. 50 hours isn't unreasonable if you really explore and linger places. Gamespot on UO T2A - Sacrates
Ooof, Gamespot go's and does the bitch slapping to UO The Second Age in their Review. Personally I think it's the shit at least for what it is, and that's a damn fine addition to UO.

The Second Age is a no-brainer for current UO players. At only $6.95 to upgrade, it's hard to resist the idea of an expansion pack that provides more room to breathe and more creatures to fight. Newcomers to the UO universe should be a bit more wary, however, especially with other games like EverQuest just around the corner. The Second Age adds some nice new features and fixes a few things, but it introduces a whole slew of its own problems. Does this mean that we won't play the game? Of course not. We'll still log on every day to bash monsters, gather gold, and build upon our reputations. That's just part of the love-hate charm of this addictive and frustrating game. But we had honestly hoped for more from The Second Age and, after all this time, from the state of Ultima Online in general.

Revenant Preview - Sacrates
Just noticed over at Billy's Love shack (VE) that Gamelink have posted a spiffy ass preview of Revenant you want to cuddle up to. This game looks pretty damn good so far so be sure to scope it out.

Combat includes 4 basic modes. We have weapon based combat, hand to hand combat, bow combat, and stealth mode. Weapon mode features three basic attacks: a thrust, a swing, and a chop. As you gain experience you can learn variations off of these base attacks. These variations will eventually lead to chain attacks that link moves together in a multiple hit attack combination. In certain situations, an interactive move can be performed. Interactive moves include fatalities, throws, and power moves. These are not reserved for the player alone however. Expect monsters to pick you up by the head and throw you, punch you in the face and send you flying head over heels; even pick you up and powerbomb you. Did we just say pro-wrestling moves in an RPG? You're damn right we did. Hand to hand mode works much like weapon mode with the 3 basic attacks being punch, thrust kick, and jumping sidekick. Variations can be learned like the uppercuts and spin kicks. Interactives and combinations are also included for some serious martial arts combat.
