Vampire Bites - Sacrates
Ouch. Excuse the pun on the Subject. Anyway Robert Huebner was on the Vampire boards and passing out an extra bit of goodies in the game.

Yeah, I would agree with you, Sandman, that the character modeling in Jedi was one of the aspects we could have spent more time on. Its an area that we've really focused on for this new engine. In Jedi, each character body part was a single "mesh" of polygons, so it was difficult to make joints like knees and shoulders to look natural. The system was much better for doing 'droids than humans. Our lead artist on Vampire, Maarten Kraaijvanger, has really pushed us to do better this time. To that end, we support what are called "soft-skinned" models, where there can be on single mesh of polygons that is stretched over the entire skeleton, so gaps are a thing of the past. We also support weighted-vertex skinning, which means that as the bones move, the skin stretches over the joint in a more natural way.

Of course the polygon count has gone way up too.. In Jedi I think Kyle had around 350 polygons at his highest setting, in this game we've got characters that are well over 1000 polygons. We're using skeleton animations, of course, so you can expect lots of different animations and unlimited interpolation of frames, so the animations are all very fluid and smooth. We also switched from 3DStudio to Alias/Maya for modeling, which seems to be a more powerful tool, overall. And of course those annoying animation glitches with the joints going the wrong way sometimes as in Jedi are history, since the animations are stored in a different format now that prevents that sort of error.

There should be a release soon of some downloadable screenshots at high resolution so you can really take a close look at the character renders and see for yourself. The combination of the better engine, animation, the incredible textures, and accurate 32-bit RGB lighting really make them seem realistic in their environments.

EQ Patch - Sacrates
Just keeping the EQ news flowing this morning. Here's the latest patch message straight from the game.

We have found and fixed two major bugs with spell casting and the newly implemented "/corpse" command. These fixes should account for many of the crashes or disconnects that players were encountering. A number of other bugs have been fixed with this patch and are listed below;

- 1)Bows were drawing onto the user interface overlays and eventually leading to crashing.
- 2)The newly implemented "/corpse" command was causing players to disconnect when used.
- 3)The Mesmerize effect for Bards and Enchanters has been fixed, and should be working as intended.
- 4)Exiting from the character selection screen, selecting a server, then clicking on the "Play EverQuest" button would result in an Illegal Operation.
- 5)If a character died and his or her starting city was down, it would result in the user crashing. Now, instead of crashing, the user will be returned to the character selection screen until the starting city comes back on-line.
- 6)Range weapons that were equipped would not appear on the character model until the weapon was activated.
- 7)When commanding pets, text of pets coordinates would spam the user's chat bar.
- 8)After jumping into water with a torch equipped, then unequiping and re-equiping it, the torch would then activate while under water.
- 9)When using the "Eye of Zomm" spell, the eye model would only face in one direction.
- 10)While fishing, the player would receive a message that the pole has broken, however, the pole was not actually damaged.
- 11)If the player purchases multiple scrolls of the same spell, only one scroll would appear in the character's inventory.
- 12)Upon consenting another character loot your corpse, the consenting player would receive the message, "You are given permission to loot corpse".
- 13)While riding the ships, no ocean sound could be heard.
- 14)Display mode settings would not be saved when exiting and re-entering the game.
- 15)Text overlapping would occur while the spell book was open and the options menu was up.
- 16)Players were able to activate the hot key functions while meditating.
- 17)When a player was anonymous, and the option menu was up, the character was in fact not anonymous.
- 18)With the Autosplit function enabled, party members would receive money when a fellow party member looted his or her corpse.
- 19)Various grammatical changes were made to the race and class descriptions at the character select screen.
- 20)Placing multiple "stackable" items in the trade screen would result in an incorrect number of items on the receivers screen.

- The EverQuest Team

EQ Bug - Sacrates
Looks like a new bug popped up two nights ago with the latest patch kids. Here's a little somthing to slap it on the ass with.

Yesterday's patch seems to have re-introduced the "Black Screen" bug for some Voodoo2 card users. It you are getting an apparent lockup with a black screen after selecting a server and clicking the "Play EverQuest" button, you need to delete the "eqopt16.opt" and "eqOptions1.opt" files from your EQ directory. As a temporary workaround, if you are having this problem you need to return to 640x480 mode before logging out of EQ, otherwise you will need to delete those files again.

If you have this problem, please send email with information on your system configuration (especially your Video Card model) and stating you had a "Black Screen" problem to eqmail@verant.com, and please attach your dbg.txt file from your EQ directory.
