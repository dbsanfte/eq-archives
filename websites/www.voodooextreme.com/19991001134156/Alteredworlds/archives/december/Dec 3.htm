More Elysium goods - Sacrates
Continuing it's RPG coverage of the day OGR has slapped up a Preview of Elysium that you should defenitely check in on. Ah, another new RPG to add to the list of upcoming gems for us to cover at AW.

We took a brief look at the infancy of Elysium in mid-1998, and although some things are certain to change when it decides what it wants to be when it grows up (it is not due until the end of 1999), it certainly is an intriguing design from the get-go. So what is Elysium, anyway? Cutter calls it "a world beyond dreams," a concept that he fully differentiates from "a dream world." So we are supposed to accept this world as real-but when does reality become fantasy? In our dreams. Elysium is an alternate universe that is reachable only when dreaming or during moments when your brain is in a particular, creative state. One of the ideas with which they're toying is that Elysium has been the inspiration for some of history's greatest thinkers, artists, and writers. These innovators have had adventures in Elysium, but they forget about them when they wake up. With this in mind, don't be surprised if you encounter characters such as Mona Lisa in the game, or come upon a background from a Rembrandt painting. In the first episode, for example, the characters find a stone circle and assume that it is Stonehenge.

Fallout 2 patch info - Sacrates
As if this game wasn't getting enough rave reviews. At least the guys are patching that buggy bastard. Somebody should have kicked them in the Scooby Doo for releasing the game too early.

We continue to work on the patch - we're having a hard time fixing the Lo Pan/Dragon fight bug where people Save in the middle of the fight. The engine really doesn't handle Saving the game in staged fights like that. But we are working on a fix.

I've gotten a lot of people thinking that I wrote Myst in my last update as to what Fallout 3 would look like. The example I was using for what Fallout 3 might be is MYTH - The Fallen Lords by Bungie, not Myst - the five year old adventure game. Also, I was not saying that it would look exactly like Myth, but it was just an example of an engine that was 3D and isometric.

Now for the UK version of the game, we will be releasing a patch for that version at or around the same time as the US version. There might be a slight delay because we have to send the version to the UK to have it hooked up with the copy protection that was only put on the European and UK builds. French and German patch versions will also be coming out close to the US version, same reason for the delay with them.

We are still working on the character import utility. Just wanted to mention that, because when I don't I get 20 E-mails from people thinking we're not doing it. :)

Lastly thanks again for everybody's support, good wishes, and help with sending in Save Games,

-Feargus (fallout2@interplay.com)

PS. And as a last last note. Black Isle just wants to say "Big Ups to Monolith for Shogo". We know where the love is. :)

Darkstone cometh - Sacrates
Delphine Software is currently working on an upcoming RPG by the name of Darkstone which looks very cool. Plus a damn cool sounding name also. So if your blow up doll hasn't been providing you with enough loving lately this would be a good idea to check it out. CGO has kicked up a Preview of it.

The game is fully 3D, and supports most 3D accelerator cards via Direct3D; it sports a viewing system that is fully player-controlled for both orientation and zoom and is somewhat reminiscent of Dungeon Keeper. Beautifully detailed characters are done in a whimsical animé style, and there are eight of them to choose among, male and female from four castes: Warrior, Cleric, Rogue, and of course a Magic-User-one that looks disturbingly like Little Bo Peep. Also like Diablo, each new game uses a quest generator to randomly place treasure and quests (of which tens of thousands are possible) with over 30 levels per quest, and over 80 types of monsters, including killer bees.

System Shock daily briefs - Sacrates
Ken Levine was hitting the boards again. After playing the Thief demo I can honestly start looking forward to System Shock. Looking Glass is defenitely someone to watch. First we have a talk on Cut scenes etc.

We're firm believers in consistency, but our need for flashbacks, extended exposition and general depth of storyline requrired bigger guns in the cutscene department than halflife. The System Shock 2 story is quite large.

The cutscenes are a combination of strictly in game, in engine, in engine rendered to avi and enhanced and a few, brief, 3D studio type sequences.

Here he is again talking about an object next to the gun in a recent screenshot.

That would be a vial of a chemical compound. Chemical compounds have a very cool role in the game. Which I will reveal very soon. Think XCOM...

Ah, a little engine goodness.

The Shock version of the engine is strictly a superset of the Thief engine. We have 16 bit color, colored lighting, larger spaces available via some cool lightmap tricks, etc. The AIs naturally can't see people in shadows (or more to the point, you can make them sensitive to that as much as you want. That said, Shock 2 is less a game about stealth and more a game about going into combat with the right tools on the battlefield of your choosing. What kind of ammo is the foe sensitive to? What condition is my weapon in? Should I use my powered armor that could run out juice in the middle of the battle or my less stoked (but more reliable) gear? Should I push my psi powers on this guy and risk overloading and blacking out? Shock is a game of choices.

However, the shadows work for us too, so you'll definitely find some of that...

BG daily briefs - Sacrates
As usual those wacky Bioware bastards were on the boards again spreading all kinds of medevil love. First we have Greg Peterson talking about Multiplayer support and what we'll be using.

We are finalizing all of the details on how exactly the game matching will work and what services we will offer, but we will have a service set up with Gamespy and are in negotiation with a couple of other organizations.

The Gamespy logo is on the box.

Next we have more Greg talking about pricing the game etc.

There are two ways to make up for high development costs:

1) Charge more per game.
2) Sell more games.

We are taking the second track.

Without going into all of the details behind managing development budgets, we have a pretty good handle on what we are spending and when, and for what features. The development of this game has taken a long time, however it is not 'over' budget. We will actually be making a slightly LOWER unit profit margin on Baldur's Gate, because of the added materials in the product build (big map, 5 CD's, etc). However we will more than make it up on volume.

I don't think anyone is worried about the profitability of this game without having to charge extra for it.

Ray talks about interesting things and the meaning of life.

Want to hear something interesting?

If you select guard/protect and click on a group of hostile creatures, your party members will actually do this! The reason this is interesting is that they will proceed to attack not only the hostiles ('cause that's what their scripts say), but also each other ('cause you've just told them to guard/protect the enemies).

I was going to enter this as a bug, but then I sat back and thought about it. It's actually not a bug, really. It's actually what you'd expect them to do if you gave them conflicting order!

Hmm. Verrrrry interesting.

Even more Drakan goodness - Sacrates
High on my charts of games to look forward to is Drakan. This bad boy does everything but brush your teeth. PC.IGN have kicked up a nice Interview with one of the Blokes working on Drakan. Speaking of which we have a bit of goodness on it's way along these lines also. Check back later.

IGN: In Drakan, the character can go indoors, run along the ground or fly high up in the air. Is this all handled within the same engine? If so, how is it being done?
Patmore: Yes, the entire game is rendered using the same engine. Our tools allow us to essentially edit "layers" of geometry. By manipulating this geometry we can create incredibly organic indoor and outdoor environments. Some of the latter levels of the game are incredibly fantastic. Massive spiraling mountains, lava waterfalls, floating islands are just a few of the environments that the tools allow us to create. The ability to edit layers allows us to create geometry that is impossible in traditional landscape engines.
