Dragonflight goodness - Sacrates
Here's a piece I neglected to throw up when I saw it a few weeks ago. Anyway, was just over at Desslock's who also missed it and slapped that bastard up. So I thought i'd jump on the train too since this isn't a bad looking game at all. We also have an Interview with these guys in our Interview section so stop by and check it. Also stop by and check their Site out.

We are constantly playing with the player character statistics and get the world to recognise you have them. This is going to need some fine tuning right up to the last minute. For instance, you have a charisma stat, this can change depending on the way you play through the game. We have configured the dialogue so you have multiple responses when talking to the characters, so if you begin to take a hostile route when you talk to the NPC's, your charisma rating will fall and you will become very unpopular around Pern. If you are polite and helpful then your charisma setting will go up and people will adhere to you. A good example is that near the beginning of the game, you meet a woman who has lost her son in some dark caves. If you rescue her little boy, you will be rewarded, your character rating will be enhanced and the people of Pern will begin to hear about you and you begin to build a good reputation.

You can also search many of the bookcases, these reveal skins, objects, many things that will help you in your quest. You can spend a great deal of time reading skins, I'm going to include as much information as I possibly can, as you're all aware, this amounts to a great deal. Its not essential to read this to complete the game but it gives you the added option to research Pern if you want to, especially if you are not familiar with Anne's world.

Fallout Save game - Sacrates
Feargus Erquhart slipped up this little sausage on Save game problems and what they are doing to fix them over on the Boards.

Well, I can see that the fact that Save Games are incompatible in the patch, has not made BIS anymore friends, so I am looking for a compromise. A user E-mailed me with an idea last night that we are currently looking into. We are seeing if we can create a utility that will take your character from an old Save Game and start him at the beginning of the patched game. He wouldn't have his items or reputations (these can potentially screw up scripts), but would have all his other stats. We are also thinking of giving the character an Extra $500 or $1000 in cash per level he has to make buying stuff easy.

We'll try to get that done this weekend as well, so it can release with the patch early next week.

System Shock Blurbette - Sacrates
Ken Levine was back in action Starsky and Hutch Style on the SS Boards. Had a few words to say on the Interface. Check it.

Today, myself Xemu and Jon (the lead programmer and project leader, respectively) had a meeting on this exact issue (as I've mentioned before, we've been refining the interface over the last few weeks). There are actually _3_ ways to reload a weapon.

1) through the inventory

2) through the ammo box in the lower right hand corner (it's really not small, probably over an inch square at least)

3) through (remappable) hot keys

Our philosophy is to give people redundant ways of doing things. And then let them customize it. No one, except the user, can make a perfect interface. We want to provide the tools to make even a rich game like Shock eminently playable, even to the novice.

Gamespot UK on Silver - Sacrates
Gamespot UK slipped up a little paragraph on Silver which we haven't seen or heard from in quite a while. Read up on it right here. (VN)

Here's a game that disappeared off the edge of the earth, or so it seemed. In development for several years, the last time we saw it was 1996 and then all went quiet ­ until a month ago. Then it suddenly reappeared as a Final Fantasy VII-style action-adventure with cutesy cartoon-style characters and a swords and sorcery story line. And the one big difference between it and Squaresoft's famous offering is that the combat in Silver is real-time as opposed to turn-based.

Silver is an evil emperor and he decides to round up all women of child-bearing age. Your task, as the main character David, is to find out just what he is up to and, eventually, stop him.

The game is beautiful to behold with around 350 individual scenes all featuring stylish backdrops, while some of the indoor views have a weird 3D top-down view that allows David to climb stairs and move about all on the one screen.

There are 52 characters and character types to interact with and while some will help you with useful bits of information or some strong-arm support, there are many of the emperor's cronies to battle with. Control is by mouse ­ you point where you want to go and left-click to go there, double-clicking to go there faster. Right-clicking brings up a comprehensive cyclical menu giving you access to your inventory, spells, weapons and so on.

Real-time combat is simple to control to and happens relatively slowly, so you're not floundering around aimlessly hoping to hit your enemy. It is mouse-activated ­ hold down the Ctrl key then press the left mouse button and swing forward to thrust, swish left and right to slash in those directions or pull back to do a nifty reverse turn thrust. Right-clicking while holding Ctrl raises your shield. Picking up items is easy using a mouse drag and select system for multiple objects.

It's been a while in the making, but we like the look of Silver around these parts.
