BG Multiplayer bitchin - Sacrates
Believe me, I'm one of the bitchers in this case. I don't particularly like or enjoy the dialogue screens popping up every time one of your companions Pimps on one of the locals. Anyway James Ohlen was on the Developers Board to dish out some sauce in this department. And he does a good job of getting his point across.

There have been many complaints about the game pausing during dialogue or stores. I would like to explain our reasons for designing the game this way, and then hopefully convince you that it ain't that bad.

Dungeons and Dragons has always been a party based game, and as such involves co-operation and characters adventuring together. During an adventure every party member should know what is going on and work together at possible solutions to their problems. We felt that in Baldur's Gate, co-operation would be impossible if each player did not know the story of the game. Therefore we felt it important that every player had a chance to see what each NPC had to say. Was it a bad decision? We won't know until we get feedback from YOU the game players (the ultimate authority on design decisions).

We can't change this feature in multi-player as it is an integral component to the game. TO change the way dialogue works would be to make a new game. This is not to say we aren't looking into multi-player dialogue - it will be something that is discussed for BG2. For those of you who have voiced concerns, know that this 'problem' becomes less prevalent later on in the game. You start in Candlekeep, where there are lots of people to talk to. In the wilderness there are very few people to talk to (and lots of monsters to kill), in dungeons there are few people to talk to. Only in the cities will the dialogue window pop up regularly. Trust me, once past Candlekeep it won't be as noticeable.

Multi-player can be fun, but remember that the game is a party based game, and as such should be played together. Try to stay in a group and it will be much more satisfying.

My turn damnit. I completely understand where he's coming from. But the fact is does it make the game fun in Multiplayer? That is the point of the matter. In my experience it made it tedious and repetetive since we already heard this same spew before in single player and we inherantly want something a little different while playing Multiplayer don't we? At least when I played Diablo I know I did. Excuse that rant....just me talking out my ass again.

EQ biscuits - Sacrates
Sorry, no big Beta gravy to add on top of those biscuits but hell, it's EQ news all the same and we all love the smell of that. With that said, Thorim was over on the boards doing what he does as an EQ Guide...spilling the beans. First, he bitch slaps the kids that are playing the EQ Beta right now and not "Testing" it. From what I hear, Sony was a little "loose" (Almost Lewinsky loose too) with who they chose for Beta 3.

Please try to understand something. The game is currently in *BETA*.

The anti-twinking rules that are currently in effect are only in effect for beta, so that the data being collected by the dev team isn't skewed by someone advancing faster than they should because of higher level help.

Now, as to your specific question. There is no way that a low level character could provide a service to a high level character that would justify a legitimate reward of an item that is beyond the ability of the lower level to get on his/her own.

For beta only, 'gifts' (which is what this really is) are not legal.

Remember, we are supposed to be testing the game, not just playing it for our own amusement.

By the way, for those not in the know, "Twinking" is the act of higher level characters giving away free items to Improve lower level Characters stats. (i.e. Diablo etc)

U:A Joy - Sacrates
Sith was at it again and slapped up some posts by Yavn on the boards. It's good to see the guys aren't wasting any time getting back to work. First he talks about the games Launcher and if it will be similar to Ultima's of the past.

We have not finished the launcher, although we will start working on it in January. We will pass on more information soon.

Here he is again talking about what the staff will be like for Christams. Sounds pretty tame to me. I would get my pet Monkey collection and a few strippers and see what happens myself.

Good question. Peace on Earth? No more hunger? All the worlds problems solved? Actually, I'd would be happy if the fans keep supporting us, and staying excited about Ultima Ascension. ( a new dual Pentium 450 would also be nice).

On killable characters.

Right now the plan is to allow everyone to be killed. Yes, Lord British can be killed, but not by normal means....

This is subject to change as we see what effect it has on the balance of the game.

Next he jumps on if there will be monkeys or any types of no monsters in the game.

Yes, there are animals in the game, although the player will not be able to ride them. As for how many, I don't want to spoil anything, but there are several different kinds!

A bit on the compass.

The compass sits in the lower left corner of the screen. It can be turned on, or off. Presently it can not be moved.

The camera can be pitch locked.

Placing the cursor over an object brings up a tool tip, so the name of objects can be found easily.

On the hot topic of the camera etc.

The player can lock the pitch of the camera. We do not have any plans for pre-assigned cameras, although that is a good idea.

How will Reagents be handled.

Reagents can be found or bought. Some will be more common then others, and much more difficult to find. As with previous Ultimas, the reagents needed for spells are related to the functions of the spell.

Clarifying his statement he made on Gamecenter.

Stonegate Tower is made up of 5 floors. What I should have said, was that each FLOOR teaches a different skill. All of the floors are on the same map, and movement is seemless between the floors.

Why things have been so quiet lately.

Thanks for all the support of all the fans, and everyone involved with this board. We are indeed taking time off for the holidays, and will be fully refreshed, and ready to get back to work in January. I hope everyone has a safe and wonderful holiday.

UO T2A Review - Sacrates
Here's another Review for you to read by the fire, or by the heater etc. Hell I just get the Intern to keep me warm. Anyway, looks like everyone is willing to dish out better ratings for this game now. It's about time I say since AW wouldn't even be here if it weren't for this game!

For the most part, The Second Age is the same game I played last year, with some minor modifications. The graphics haven't changed, and the only major addition to the interface is a chat function, based on the ichat system. If you have a character online, you can log on to any one of a number of chat "conferences," which hold up to 300 players each. Most of these are password-protected channels, where guild members or groups of friends talk, but occasionally there is  a conference that allows people in without a password. None of the conferences are particularly lively, though; the chat window isn't well-integrated into the game window, so you have to be in a safe spot if you want to stop and talk.

RTK Review - Sacrates

Boot up Return to Krondor and you can expect to give up sleep, food, and meaningful interactions in the real world for at least a few days (the game is so compelling that you tend to devour it in as few bites as possible, like a great thick novel). Return is a stunning return to form for the Krondor series, and bodes spectacularly well for any future installments in the on-going tale.
