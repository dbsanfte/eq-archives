BG Yummies - Sacrates
It's always good to hear from the BG team lately as they are this.................close to going Gold. Here's some good stuff on Expansions for Baldur's Gate. The wait is almost over

First, I know you are all waiting for news on BG - we will post as soon as we have something concrete to say. We don't want to post every few minutes with stuff like "Feargus is scratching his head - ohmygod!". You will see the news here first.

Second, I wanted to keep this post talking about BG development... so I though I'd start a thread talking about the expansion(s). At this point we have one in the works and have a LOT of suggestions for the development. I would like to pose this question for discussion...

Would people be interested in 2 expansion packs (as add-ons to the current game) BEFORE we came out with an additional free-standing BG game? IF so, how might you structure the progression of the games - how would you handle experience points, levels, etc? Do people like the xp caps or think it is too artificial?

Giants Preview - Sacrates
Just got word from Tim Williams the Creative Director over at Planet Moon Studios that Next Gen just slapped up a Giants Special that you may want to see. He's been breaking me down for a few weeks now and I have finally submitted. This game looks like all the Biz and has a ton of innovation. Not to mention it's got quite a few RPG Elements. Here's a small biscuit.

Giants looks set to include many different gameplay features in an attempt to break the tired molds of traditional gaming genres, as Creative Director Tim Williams explained: "The action will certainly be fast and furious, but as the game progresses the player will notice many levels of complexity that will start to build up in parallel. For example, if you play as the Meccaryns you'll first find yourself having to rescue your four teammates, then later in the game you'll actually have to set up a base and capture some creatures to work for you. You'll be able to sneak into enemy camps and kidnap creatures that will work for you, giving yourself an edge over your opponent. On top of this your Mecc teammates will be growing more and more intelligent, which will directly affect their skill in battle. As you can see, although this is most definitely an action based game there are elements of both RPGs and strategy that add spice to it and retain its longevity."

Dues Ex info - Sacrates
Bob White was on the boards and had a bit to say on Camera angles etc. Bob hasn't been releasing too much info lately. It's good to see him back in action.

I remember some of those games you mentioned and the one that sticks in my mind most is Ecstatica. Great concept but the camera switching was detrimental to the game play. Nothing like having the camera switch angles in the middle of combat so you cannot see your opponent,or maybe you only see his back and you cannot see much else. Our game utilizes the camera positioning changes during conversations or perhaps in some scripted sequences. They are used to convey a view of the scene normally not observed. Perhaps it is to see an assassin moving into position behind you while you rtalk to the mild mannered reporter. Other times it is used just as a mechanism to show the player character from an outside perspective while he is discussing matters with an NPC. The player will still be making the conversation choices, we are just going to be showing the scene from a slightly different angle. There will be no loss of game play as in Ecstatica and you will get to see what your character looks like too.

U:A and MP3's - Sacrates
Audball was on the boards again with ever more sound information. This guy is like GI Ho with all this sound news. He just doesn't stop. Far be it from me to complain though...read on.

You are correct in that MP3 is an excellent compression scheme and has very good sound quality. However, DurhamDragon is also correct in that using MP3 would have a severe performance penalty. To use MP3, you'd either have to (1) decompress on-the-fly, which would drastically impact framerate and other game functions, or (2) decompress at level-load time, store the audio in memory, and playback the uncompressed data, a technique which would require large amounts of memory. In our case, that memory is better used by object/terrain meshes and/or data caches so you can see more on the screen at once, get better perfomance, smoother gameplay, etc...

KQ Review - Sacrates
Looks like Adventure Central smacked up their own Review of Kings Quest themselves. Obviously it doesn't them warm in the Nuggets like using Whips and other sundry accessories does. Stop by and check it...until then hit this blurb.

Here's one area Mask excelled in. The music was beautiful, varied, and always fit the mood. All the sound effects, and there were a lot of them, were very well done and didn't intrude on gameplay. As for the voice acting, although there wasn't as many characters in the game as previous King's Quest's, the ones that were there, were, for the most part, done well.
