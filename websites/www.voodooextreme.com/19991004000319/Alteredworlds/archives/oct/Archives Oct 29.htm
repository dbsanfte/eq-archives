Hmm...If I didn't know better, I'd say the Interplay was going through some rough times. Noticed a couple of bits over on VN on the Lead Programmer of Fallout taking his new company, Troika Games, over to Sierra. Here's some cut & paste goodness from Sierra's press release.

TIM CAIN SIGNS WITH SIERRA STUDIOS

Sierra Studios Announces Publishing Deal with Troika Games
Tim Cain's New Role-Playing Game to be Published in Exclusive Game Agreement

BELLEVUE, WA - October 29, 1998 - Sierra Studios announced today a publishing deal with Fallout creator Tim Cain's new game company, Troika Games LLC. The first yet to be named title is scheduled for release in the third quarter of the year 2000 and is a role-playing game from the core team who brought the gaming world the award-winning RPG Fallout, published by Interplay in 1997. This marks the first game from this stellar team as a company and solidifies the re-branding of Sierra Studios and its commitment to publishing high quality games for the millennium.

"Tim Cain, Leonard Boyarsky and Jason Anderson made a name for themselves with their work on Fallout last year," said Scott Lynch, senior vice president of Sierra Studios. "Their team has a combined 20 years in the games business and we're excited to be working with them on their next project. We are confident Troika Games will produce another award-winning game and bring RPGs a new brand of quality in the genre."

"Over the course of six months I looked at a lot of different publishers, I went with Sierra Studios for one reason - trust. I trust them and their experience at handling big ticket games," said Tim Cain. "This is the RPG I've been waiting 15 years to make - it's got everything I want in an RPG a cool story, a large variety of characters you can create, lots of paths through the game, great artwork and an awesome engine that really supports the plot."

Troika Games LLC was founded in April 1998 by Timothy Cain, Leonard Boyarsky, and Jason Anderson, the creators of Fallout - the 1997 winner of OGR's Game of the Year, and winner of RPG of the Year from PC Gamer, Computer Gaming World, GameSpot, Adrenaline Vault, Games Domain, Gamesmania among others. The goal of Troika Games is to develop the highest-quality games available for the home computer. Troika Games is currently developing a new RPG for Sierra Studios due for release in the summer of the year 2000.

More with Lord British - Bowmeister
Yessiree...Desslock has slapped up part two of his bigger than Monica Lewinsky's ass interview with Richard Garriott, aka Lord British. This installment focuses on the decision to switch the format of the Ultima series into the wonderful world of 3D.

What people first need to understand is that from day one – even prior to the Ultima series – since back in 1974 when I was in high school making simple paper-tape games that I'd just call "D&D 1", "D&D 2", etc., every one of the games that I've written has had the same goal: to create a completely believable, virtual world and a compelling reason to be there. I think every game I've done through the years has gotten me closer to that goal, in the sense that characters have gotten more realistic, world physics has gotten more realistic, graphic detail has become more realistic. I also think I've done a pretty good job at making the worlds compelling places to be, since they've had epic stories that made the gamer the hero and given that hero a reason and purpose. It's still my dream, my goal, to create a completely believable, realistic world.

Now the viewpoint is a very important part of achieving that goal. Ideally my games would literally take gamers to that realistic world, where they wouldn't see, or hear, or feel, or smell, or touch the difference from reality. Of course that's a pipe dream that's decades off, but people shouldn't be so concerned when they see that we've gone from 2D graphics to 3D graphics. Earlier Ultimas had both 2D and 3D portions in them...

Vampire: The Masquerade - Sacrates
We haven't really been talking about this game much lately. Partly due to lack of info, but it looks like the boys are ready to come out of their coffins and speak. There are also a few Jedi Knight team members tossed into the fray in Nihilistic, the company making the game. Here's a rip from a post made by one of the developers on their boards that was posted over at Jediknight.net. Vampire is a new RPG using an entirely new 3D Engine to do it's dirty work. Check it:

I'd love to be able to give a full feature list, but we're not quite ready for that yet, because things are still in development and we wouldn't want to announce a feature and then not deliver it to you.

What I can say (I hope!) is that we are developing a brand new engine that is specifically tuned for both hardware acceleration and an RPG 3rd person perspective. One of the biggest trade-offs made on Jedi was to done to support a wider range of hardware than Quake2 (which also came out after Jedi Knight). With this engine, we are focusing on hardware accelerators which gives us a lot more freedom when choosing features.

Our primary focus with this engine is creating a highly moody and realistic environment, so we are spending the most time working on features involving lighting, shadows, fog, etc. We also have some really incredible 3D artists working on models, so we've spent a lot of time in the engine creating a character rendering system that does them justice.

We can't say for certain what will be out on the shelves at the time we ship this game, but our feeling is that this engine will go toe-to-toe with the feature sets of other engines at that time, and hopefully exceed them in the areas we're focusing on.

Sorry for being vague, but when we have nailed things down we'll let you know all the cool features we've got planned.

Seth on Automapping etc: The Avatar will have a journal that keeps track of important information, events, and maps. Although some people think this makes the game too easy, others like the idea of knowing what has happened, and what quests are still unfinished. I find this especially helpful for people that stop playing, and want to go back, but not replay the game to that point.

The auto-mapping is being worked on now, so I won't comment on how it works until it's finished.

Seth on the Backpack: The Avatar has only 1 backpack, although we have not finalized the number of slots in it. The world will have different sized containers.

Seth on Virtues: There will be consequence to not following the virtues. This will effect how people react to you, and a few of the other game systems. On the flip side, doing virtuous things will help the player. This is about I can say without giving away the plot.

Seth on Money and buying selling: For selling items to be important, money has to be important throughout the game. We are working hard to keep money a desirable throughout the game, and not something that is meaningless halfway through the game.

No more wading through conversations to buy or sell items, merchants will just buy and sell.

We have not talked about buying and selling multiple items in a single transaction, although it's a good idea. I'll let you know if we decide to implement this.

Bill on the Camera: Durham's right--the camera won't let you see through walls or into rooms you're not supposed to; it stops when it's blocked by walls & objects.

Heretic II is very close to the camera control in Ascension.
