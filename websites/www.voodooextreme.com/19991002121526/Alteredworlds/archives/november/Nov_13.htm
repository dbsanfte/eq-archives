BG Goodies - Sacrates
Looks like the guys over at Bioware are in the homestretch. You can almost sense the impending release now. A lot of discussion going on about Preordering etc. Here is Greg covering all the bases tonight with a few tasty blurbs. First he touches on the replayability of the game.

Though I have never played through the game all the way to the end, I have started three different parties and played for a while with each. My first character was a Chaotic Good Fighter, second was a Lawful Good (obviously) Paladin, third was a Neutral Good Mage.

All three times I have had different experiences. (The Paladin was strongest right out the gates... the Mage might be strongest long-term)

Oh, and I haven't even BEGUN to mess with races yet... my Fighter was a half-elf, and that was about it...

A bit more on Preorders.

With the exception that I have received 249 emails in the last 4 hours, I am happy to be here... it will just take me a while to get through all the feedback.

The other thing - we are going to take care of people, so I am not worried about some of these short-term issues. The important thing is to focus on the longer term and make sure that we get the best possible pre-order system up and running. I want to hear about ALL problems so we can fix them. Once we fix them, I expect to receive TWICE as many emails from fans saying 'thank you - you guys exceeded my expectations!'

(Ok... don't rain on my fantasies...)

Luke also jumps in and talks about Ressurection penalties in the game.

We read and post often. Best way to contact us? Exactly how you did.

Now for the part you don't want to hear :)

The decision to allow for resurrection with no penalty was made some time ago, and will not be changed at this point. Whether it was in at some point or not is irrelevant, and the "ease" of implementation is also not a factor at this point. Anything changed effects something else somewhere, and we cannot risk altering a feature we do not have time to re-test.

As for why it was done in the first place, it is a game balance issue. Players that choose to re-load are not "lesser" players. The fact of the matter is that if you turn a corner on the first level of a dungeon and two kobolds get lucky critical hits on your mage, he might die. There is no DM to make a special dispensation because it was the first 10 seconds of the adventure; the computer makes you dead. If it is your main PC, game over and you re-load, but if it was an NPC, you have a choice. Re-load and paste those worthless creatures, or continue and raise your character later. If you re-load, you are breaking no rules. The game lets you do it, and you lose a few moments of your time. You may not be playing "hardcore," but there is no rule saying you cannot do it. In major battles almost everyone will be doing it (yes, even you). It's the small encounters where the defeat was not so marginal that it becomes a sticky issue. Sure, if your entire party got pasted by that major villain you might continue on and swallow the loss because you squeaked by and gained enough treasure to cover your butt, but if you lost a member to a silly mistake or a lucky shot by creatures you normally (by your estimation, anyway:) should dominate, the choice gets a little wishy washy.

If the choice to continue involves a later walk to a temple and (relatively) few coins, you may choose to go on despite the loss and the difficulty of packing your fallen friend's gear. If however, it also entails the permanent lowering of a stat, it's very tempting to just re-load. That is a very serious penalty for a mediocre death. There is no glory or heroism in this tale; you got wasted by a kobold. Surely you shouldn't be expected to suffer such an indignity :)

It's purely a game play issue, and it maintains the spirit of AD&D, in that death is not necessarily the end for your character. It is this spirit that we want to foster in the people who are playing Baldur's Gate, but have never played AD&D before.

That's the reasoning, and some of you aren't going to agree. That doesn't make you wrong, but house-rules will stand.

Here's a bit on Scrolling in the game.

The screen is free scrolling. The characters are NOT restricted to being on screen at all times. Each person controls their own view. You can scroll over to the other side of the map if you wish, but it won't effect the view of any other players. All of the characters do NOT have to be on screen.

Example: You are scrolling along controlling your character. Player 2 is not limited to seeing only what you do. He scrolls HIS view wherever he wishes, and can move his characters wherever he wishes within the 8/8. If he moves his screen over to where your character is he can see what you do, but it does not effect what YOU see or do.

Speaking of which, the Milestone went well, and Scott and I put together a map which is quite monstrous, yet turned out nice and we had several matches of grand scale earlier this week on it. On the backburner until yesterday, I'm returning to my crusade to make the single player game what it should be, thus I'll be spending most of my time working on those maps, and communicating with the other team members who have those levels on their plates.

On the home front, I've tried to snag an hour or so a day to play Fallout 2, which is an extremely addictive game, and is one of the first games to actually present a mature adult theme and content, without attempting to use that aspect as a "shocker" to sell more copies... imagine a Mad Max film, with characters who act and speak in manners you would fully expect in a post-apocalyptic world, complete with crude dialog, violence, prostitution, slavery... all the darker sides of man, and you have the Fallout world. Hats off to Black Isle, you guys have my vote for one of the best RPG developers out there.

More on BG Pre-Orders - Bowmeister
Continuing the ongoing effort to confuse the hell out of everyone, Greg Peterson was on the Backroom message board further explaining Baldur's Gate pre-orders. Speaking of which, I really have to pop over to Interplay's site and order this baby.

First of all let me say THANK YOU to everyone who is posting on this board or sending me emails. Our order fulfillment company is new, and we are QUICKLY working through issues with them. For everyone who sends me a note, there are probably ten who don't know HOW to send me a note, so you are really helping us improve our systems.

Second of all, we are going to resolve all issues. So if you are having problems, please be patient, we will get to you. If your issue is not being resolved to your satisfaction, drop me an email and I'LL make sure you are taken care of.

Third, just so people know, the response to our current offer has been overwhelming. Within the last two days, pre-orders on Baldur's Gate have TRIPLED. We have far and away broken every pre-order record at Interplay. People at our order entry company are pulling their hair out. (I told them about the interest in the game... but I don't think people took me seriously) We will be sorting things out today and over the weekend.

Here are the biggest issues currently with the pre-order program (in no priority):

1) Buggy order entry. I have gotten a couple of examples of issues with order entry - i.e. errors, not enough address lines, etc. This seems to affect the minority of people, but we are scrubbing the entry forms and cleaning things up. This should be resolved today.

2) Charging credit cards. This is confusing even me. Apparently credit cards are not being CHARGED, they are just having credit on them 'reserved'. It is as if you have bought the product, but won't have to pay anything on your bill until after the product actually ships. I think this is confusing and raises a multitude of issues. We will work on this today. I want to make this go away.

3) Confusing order confirmation. The order confirmation states "your credit card has been billed", etc, etc. Also, you get a strange email that states the product is on 'back-order'. Again, not the way I'd like it to work, but apparently there are system issues on this one. We are trying to immediately implement a program that states "if you are pre-ordering a product, you will receive a back-order notice. This just means that your product order has been received, and an order is in queue waiting for inventory to arrive"

4) Shipping charges. This is a new one. I can't change what Fedex or UPS charges for shipping. I WILL, however, check into rumors that orders have been changed without people's permission. This one sounds bizarre to me. More research to be done.

Again, thank you all for your help. I am aiming for 100% satisfaction with our pre-order process, so many changes are on the way. Just so you know, our Director of Direct Sales is working really hard to get everything worked out. She gets paid based on how many happy direct sales customers we have each year, so she is very appreciative of all of the people who have pre-ordered Baldur's Gate. (Oh, and she has only been here a couple of months, so she is 'cleaning up' old systems and processes that did not work well)
