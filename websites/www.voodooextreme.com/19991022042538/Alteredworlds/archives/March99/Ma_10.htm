Here comes Dragon Hoard - Sacrates
Blue Fang Games is working on something called Dragon Hoard that sounds phenominal if it can be pulled off. Although it doesn't have a Publisher yet, I suspect with this idea it may soon have one. Check out this little First Look over at GA Source Lorien and the other kids dished up.

Dragon Hoard combines a real-time strategy game with role-playing and action game elements, to create a game experience for the player that is both role-playing game and god-game, where the god-like being is the player-dragon. The concept works on a number of levels, and has a strong precedent in the classic game Sid Meier's Pirates. In the game Pirates, the player's task is to become a great pirate, performing all of the acts for which pirates were universally known: fighting warships and sword duels, amassing great treasure, and becoming famous. Dragon Hoard will advance this concept by applying the latest technology advances to this hybrid game experience to create a rich gaming experience for the player.

EQ Events trouble - Sacrates
Looks like the early ending to Everquest this Friday has put a crimp in a few EQ chimps plans for big events. Here's Brad on the subject.

I know a lot of you out there were planning some ending events for the weekend, and are probably a bit disappointed at the fact that we're closing things down friday night. Please understand that this is simply due to logistics -- we have only so much time to transition internally from phase 4 to launch, and we need that weekend. So I apologize if this has interfered with plans, but I do hope these events can be rescheduled. We definitely didn't do this arbitrarily, and we are keeping phase 4 alive for as long as possible.

thanks for understanding,

-Brad

EQ Preview #2,193 - Sacrates
Gamesdomain have poked up their own Preview of this incredibly hyped game. Lately i've given up on my regular character now in an attempt to get the feel of the rest of the games classes and races to see what I want to be most. Only 2 days left of Beta testing so i'm really trying to figure out what I want to be in final. Here's a bite.

Another point in favour of EQ (in this case especially over M: 59) is the huge, enormous world of Norrath. Locations stretch from the icy wastes of Halas in the North, through the steepled cities of men to the stinking swamps of the Trolls in the South, and that's just one continent, there are two other sot explore so you'd better get those hiking boots on. Sony promises new islands and adventures will be added in the time to come, which should make the EQ experience even more varied. What is interesting, however, is that there are no in-game maps available. This discourages long journeys because it would be so easy to get lost. Pity, and somewhat strange, really.

U:A Bite - Sacrates
Audball was around the Ultima Ascension boards and had a bit to say about A3D Implementation. All I know is my SB Live will be screaming when I get this game with full EAX support.

Currently, A3D support is not on our development schedule. Supporting the A3D 2.0 feature you desire (WaveTracing) would simply require too much time to implement good and full support. (There are modeller, editor, and game data issues, which taken together just don't fit in our timeline - and please, no rants/flames on how much better it sounds or how easy it is to implement; we've already covered this topic previously).

As for EAX support, I'm not sure that any of the Vortex 2 based cards out there have implemented it. I've read from various Web sources that EAX is *not* implemented, but I'm no official source for that answer...

WoT Weekly - Sacrates
Doh, I can't believe I missed this one. Anyway, here's your latest dose of WoT Development Update from Glen.

Development Update
I spent most of today reviewing stuff, so I lost a boatload of time.  I still have tons of work to do and no day left, so I'm afraid that there isn't going to be a whole lot of content.  So I'll get to what I've got.

Last week was Robert's turn to hang out here and work on decorations and angreal effects.  He liked the area so much   that he went ahead and bought a house.  Sometime reasonably soon, I expect the whole Wisnewski clan to arrive, stuff packed high in their Beverly Hillbillies Truck (although that's only my personal mental image).  It's going to be great working with Robert on-site permanently. 

With Robert gone, this week we're featuring Steve Bodnar (our animator) as our guest contributor.  He's here to examine the current state of the game and make whatever animation tweaks he can with the feedback from the team.  He's set up in the level designers' cave and is installing all of his tools and work-in-progress as I type.   Next week, watch for Paul and Julie.  The local hotel is loving us right now.

The level designers have put a lot of spit and polish on a lot of levels.  Scott has opened up "The Approach to the FoF", James has added a bunch to "The Approach to SL", Warren has put a lot of stuff into "The Portal Stone World", and Tim has spread his time throughout "The Vault", "The Streets of SL", and "The Inner Sanctum."  Although it's clear that there's some work left on each, they've all come a long way, and they're looking mighty fine.

Joel has cranked out some serious-looking Trolloc skins, both saving memory and increasing the nastiness factor for each of these bad boys.   Paul has focused on more UI pieces, stuff which Jim should be implementing shortly (if he can do it before his baby arrives; congratulations--before the fact!).  Julie and Marc have been finishing up our store of normal, detail, and macro textures.  We're about to critical mass with our textures.  One more, even wafer thin, could make the game explode (or at least our disk budget).

The tech team has been focused on performance optimization and have made some great strides, especially in reducing the memory footprint, which was the biggest offender.   We had a review today to look at the new levels, but most of these optimizations didn't get into the build.  I'm looking forward to tomorrow's test where we'll see the difference between the two days.  From what I gather, it's significant.

In addition to all of the internal stuff, I've received a bunch of shipments from our outside contributors.  Robert Berry delivered the last of his songs for the soundtrack, and it rocks.  Everyone who has heard his stuff, composed with Leif Sorbye, has been blown away.  I sincerely hope that GT decides to release a WoT soundtrack CD.  Hell, I'm already driving around with the CD in my car. 

Also, I just got a bunch of new cutscenes, one of which has Andy's music, sound effects, and dialog track added in.  The cutscenes are really taking shape.   It'll be wonderful to slap them into the mission structure to see them help propel the plot along.  Also, the other studio has delivered some great intro elements.   It's going to be very cool--much more extensive than I originally had planned.  

And, finally, the artifact sound effects flowing in from our new sound studio are working their way into the build, and making a world of difference.  As you know from Unreal's recent patch to the old effects, sounds that have weight make the difference between a weapon that merely fires to one that lets you feel like you're really affecting the world.

That's all I have time for if I'm going to get any sleep tonight at all.  Thanks for dropping by.

Until next time!
