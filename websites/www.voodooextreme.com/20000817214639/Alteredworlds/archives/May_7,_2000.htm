Vampire : Redemption  - Ash

I got Vampire : Redemption the other day and I must say that I am thoroughly impressed. Nihilistic sure did a great job with it. The game has many facets of Ash-worthy qualities. First is are the graphics. I have to admit now that my GeForce makes everything look pretty good, but this game really makes good use of my card. The detail is amazing. I am wondering if this is a high polygon game? Well, the engine is as impressive to me as Unreal Tournament, or Nocturne. 

Second, the multiplayer has some real potential. That is if you get a good story teller. 

But the final toping on the cake is set of editing tools that are going to be coming out for it. Already there are two possible mods I have caught wind of from scanning the Vampire message boards. 

As far as RPG content goes, it's good. But yet it could be better. You are somewhat restricted for 95% of the game as a Vampire. True, you have cool powers, but there is already coded into the game the ability to be a Vampire Hunter. Mainly this feature is intended for multiplayer, but I would have liked the option to choose if I wanted to be "Embraced" or not. 

I shouldn't complain though, this game is great. It's going to set another gaming standard almost like Thief: The Dark Project did. 

It's incredibly immersive, fun, deep in history, complex yet simple, and has a lot of possibility for "non-linear" gameplay. However, right now out-of-the-box it's main usefulness is a single-player linear mode. Later on when we see the editing tools released we will probably see some cool mods for this game come out.

Highly Anticipated Action-RPG Set to Hit Stores Nationwide

SANTA MONICA, CA - May 30, 2000 - The wait is over for RPG fans that have been clamoring to get their hands on Activision, Inc.'s (Nasdaq: ATVI) Vampire: The Masquerade-Redemption. The title, which boasts a rich storyline, amazing graphics and the first-ever digital tabletop storyteller option, has code released and is expected to be available the week of June 12 for a suggested retail price of $49.95.

Vampire: The Masquerade-Redemption was developed by Nihilistic Software and is based on the well-known White Wolf role-playing system. In the game, the player survives as a Vampire over a span of 800 years. It features four fully 3D gothic cities set both in the dark ages and modern times, a party-based, role-playing system, a complex storyline and a faithful online re-creation of the popular paper-based version. The title is rated "M" by the ESRB.

Romero's Next Game  - Certis

Once esteemed designer turned public whip bitch John Romero slipped a little nibblet on what he will be working on next. 

"Daikatana used principles from Quake as a baseline," said Romero behind closed doors at E3 last week, "and so my next title will use elements from Daikatana as a baseline for its content. It's an evolutionary process, as each game takes elements from its predecessors but adds to and improves upon them."

He continued, "My next game will have side-kicks, and the characters will have statistics and abilities. Daikatana mirrors games that I really like such as Chrono Trigger and Final Fantasy VII, in that you control up to three characters at once. You choose to control each of these from a number of characters, each of who have individual stats. I also derived the use of stats in Daikatana from the Rune system in Quake."

This may or may not be a good thing depending on how Daikatana turns out. With reviewers not receiving advance copies before it hits shelves and a shoddy demo released to the public its not looking good. There will be plenty of biased reviews in the coming weeks, I'll wait and see for myself.

Ultima Worlds Online:Origin - Certis

Ultima Online 2 has a new name! Read all about it and look below for my comments:

Last week, it was announced that Ultima Online 2 was a working title, and that we felt our game would be better represented with a different name. After much discussion and furrowing of brows, we have come to a decision and are happy to share with you the news that Ultima Online 2 will now be known as Ultima Worlds Online: Origin.

What's that you say? Isn't Origin the name of the company? Yes it is. Won't that be confusing? We don't think so. By the time this game ships, we won't be putting the Origin (the company) logo on the box. This will be our first game going out under the new EA Games logo. We expect this to be a game with a lifecycle spanning for many, many years, so the Origin name can live on.

So now, you ask, why Origin? As we searched for a name, we wanted something that:

-relates to the fiction (this world is the result of a cataclysm, and players will be entering the world of Origin near its beginning)

-feels accessible and familiar to casual and experienced gamers alike

-maintains the branding of Ultima Online and Origin, keeping our extensive experience in MMORPG's on the forefront

For the time being, the URL for Ultima Worlds Online: Origin will remain www.uo2.com. Keep an eye out on this site later in the week for an exciting video made from both in-game and rendered work, as well as several in-game screenshots, rendered creature art, and concept art!!

EA's death grip on Origin is readily apparent now. Being swallowed up and having the game listed under "EA Games" Origin has to do what they can to retain some identity. A few years from now Origin will cease to exist entirely and the golden age (for them anyway) will truly be over. Lets hope the change of name will herald a new attitude and maybe even a great product.
