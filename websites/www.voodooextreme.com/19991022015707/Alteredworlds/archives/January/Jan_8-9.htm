EQ Guild and Beta news - Sacrates
Lot of news on the EQ front. First I stayed in last night just to play the mofo and the game had been down for a few hours. It went back up with the latest patch and info on Guilds you may be interested in.

In order to form a guild the following process must be followed:

  1. The leader of the guild must email Eqguilds@sonyinteractive.com. The header of the email must contain the NAME of the guild. In this email the leader will include a short Charter that includes the guild's role in Norrath. In addition, the leader needs to list at least 9 other characters that have agreed to form this guild.
  2. The leader and 9 other players need to email Eqguilds@sonyinteractive.com with the NAME of the guild as the header. The body of this email must include the name of the character that will be joining the guild and the ACCOUNT on which that character exists.
  3. Once the 10 prospective members (including the leader) have completed the above steps, the charter and name of the guild will be reviewed by 989. If both are approved, the leader of the guild will be notified of approval via email and told to approach a GM for formation of the guild. If 989 has any reservations about either the name or the charter, the guild leader will receive an email listing these concerns and be offered an opportunity to modify the charter or name as required.

A Bit O' BG - Sacrates
Looks like the guys at Bioware aren't going to stop hitting the boards any time soon. Ray was over on the boards dishing out more of the sauce on this gem. There are a lot of mixed Reviews on this game, but I personally am hip to it even though I haven't even considered playing it since I got EQ. Credit go's out the BG Chronicles (For all things BG)  for grabbing the goods.

On the bastard sword: We decided early on that there wasn't an intuitive User Interface to switch [between one-handed and two-handed style] so the bastard sword is purely one- handed.

On how to report multiplayer problems: Do you mean the buttons (in order): "Multiplayer" "Protocol" "TCP/IP" "Done" "Join Game", type IP address, "Done" ... and then the game crashes? Do you just get to press "Multiplayer", it asks you to kindly create a new character before joining in, you hit "Connect" and the game crashes? What type of network card are you running? Are you sure you are running the right drivers for the card? What other "net" games are you talking about? Give me a list of the games that you've used successfully -- in particular, I'm looking for one that uses DirectPlay.

On assertion error D:\Dev\Chitin\ChTlkTbl.cpp at line number 353: What are you doing when this happens? Is a character attempting to talk to you? Are you just trying to start up the game when you get this error. I've seen this error on startup when the previous version of the game hasn't gone away correctly (we acquire an exclusive lock on the talk table to ensure that only one copy is running on a machine). Is that the problem? You may want to try rebooting the machine to get rid of the Baldur's Gate that is hanging around.

On C++ runtime error: If you are experiencing a C++ runtime error during the Gorion cut scene, try turning off the Environmental Audio support (under Options/Sound from within the game). If the problem still persists, please respond to this thread. I'd like to know what hardware you're running and where you are in the game (be verbose). I'll try to respond sometime this weekend if anyone is still having problems.

On number of custom portraits: It certainly wasn't intentional. It appears that if you have more than 255 portraits this causes an error. We never thought to try more than that number.

On dart specialists: A dart thrower specialized in it is quite nasty - great for disrupting spells actually. This is in fact the reason that darts are quick weapons and not in your quiver - else they'd be too powerful.

On why a drink and its actual price may not correspond at the Friendly Arm Inn: I'd like to say "Maybe you drank too much beer," but this is quite probably a bug. Albeit a minor one.

You can't continue playing after you finish the main plot in Chapter 7, however: Use the "final save" - that's what it's for. Finish anything you want to finish then come back when you're ready.

On the patch: Am reading all these posts. Most of the suggestions are great for the sequel but are not feasible for the expansion. But that's too global a statement to apply to all of them. Basically with regards to a patch: we'll be reviewing all the bugs that have been turned in to customer support and will try and work on a patch over the next few weeks - exact time depends on how many bugs need to be patched [and Q&A of the patch].

EQ Comments - Sacrates
Well, I will officially dish out a look at it soon but feel I need to give it a few more hours to do it justice. A few things that I haven't seen many other sites touch on is the colors in the game. The changes from night and day are absolutely gorgeous due to the fact that everythings color changes in proportion to how much the sky changes. You'll find yourself in combat with a Skeleton and notice that it's color changes depending on what the sky looks like. Very cool affect. Also, the character animation is one of the nicest touches I have seen. Player Characters look exactly like you've seen in the shots, but the monster animations are superb! RTK Review - Sacrates
The latest in the long line of Return to Krondor reviews has been slapped up. This time by the news chimps over at GamePen. Stop by and tell the Sacrates sent you for a good swift kick in the ass.

Most scenes in RtK are colorfully rendered with excellent artwork. Characters are somewhat blocky in appearance but have a wealth of colorful textures for clothing and items. 3D models of the characters accurately reflect the armor they are wearing and their weapons. Dues Ex update - Sacrates
Looks like The Ion Storm daily informant made an update on the progress of this interesting looking RPG. Stop by and read up on it or just read it below.

Boy, a lot of what I've been doing recently sounds deadly dull and not particularly game-related:

Stuff like going through all the missions, gathering up data from the mission designers and seeing what objects and characters we need to make our world come alive and seem real. Prioritizing all those thousands of pieces of artwork so artists know what to work on first and second and third and so on. Translating that information into a spreadsheet with checkboxes so we can track work as it's completed. Seeing if we can cut anything to make it more likely we'll ship before the end of the millennium. That kind of thing. Yawn.

On a more fun, game-related note, I've done a bit of designer map checking, to make sure the look and feel is what we want and that gameplay will be up to snuff. I've been working with programmer, Al Yarusso, to fine-tune how our computer monitors are going to work and how the last details of our interface are going to look. Right now, I'm going through every NPC in the game, making sure we know enough about their behavior in all situations -- combat and otherwise -- and what weapons they carry and so on. We already know their roles in the game pretty well but I need to make sure Scott Martin, the AI god, knows how each NPC should behave at all times. I've been talking a lot with Christian Divine, the conversation writer, about how we're going to get across the murky conspiratorial stuff that's the heart of the game -- man, does he have a big job ahead of him! I've done a fair amount of PR stuff -- magazine interviews and the like -- which is kinda fun, even if it does distract from the work of making a game...

The toughest thing I've had to do recently was evaluate each of our missions -- now that the design is fleshed out -- and see if we'd overdesigned anywhere. If you've ever worked on a game you know how seductive and dangerous the kitchen sink design approach can be. And, surprise, surprise, we had some stuff in the game that just didn't fit anymore. So, to ensure quality and ease the inevitable schedule crunch to come, I made some mission cuts. I remember when I was writing for a living, someone very wise told me always to edit out the thing (the word, the paragraph, the chapter, whatever) that you love the most because that thing you think is so cool, will just draw attention to itself and stick out like a sore thumb. I cut the missions that stuck out. We lost a couple of cool locations but shored up some other ones pretty dramatically. And the game is much stronger for the tightening and increased focus. No fooling -- no one'll ever miss the cut missions (which is why I cut them, of course...).

I guess I've taken the holidays and the beginning of a new year to kind of post-mortem the state of the project and make sure we have a solid plan for moving ahead. I set out some new, clearer goals for the team (which I think I'll just keep under my hat until we've met 'em!).

We're at the point where we have all the tools necessary to create a great game and a lot of the assets we need. We know what we have to do. Now, it's just a question of staying focused and concentrating on the job at hand (and not getting sucked any further into Half-Life, Thief: The Dark Project, Zelda 64, Southpark, Baldur's Gate and all the other terrific games that have distracted us over the last couple of months!). If we do that, Deus Ex will rock.

And if anyone reading this hasn't played Half-Life, stop reading this NOW and go do it! I haven't enjoyed a game as much in years

EQ goodies - Sacrates
Another quick update until I get back to work in Norrath. Abashi made a brief appearance on the boards today with a few goodies you may be interested in. This first one is on Shadows in the game.

But yes, you are correct, 3D objects do not cast shadows on the environment. Shadows add tons of processing time that could be better used to render frames. Each shadow must be calculated in relationship to the lightsource, distorted, then cast with tranperancy.

In order to make the hardware requirements necessary, the game is shadow free.

Here is a great one on the availability of Artifacts in the game. This sounds very cool to me. When I get one of those rare items I want it to be extremely rare and not like Godly plate in Diablo.

A note on Artifacts.

There generally won't be artifacts "available" for anyone. Artifacts are unique items (only one in the world) and will be distributed on an as-earned basis.

So, to answer your question, if you are playing a monk, and you are deserving of an artifact, there will indeed be an artifact for you.

But no one should expect to ever earn an artifact, they will be *very* rare.

BIG EQ NEWS! - Sacrates
Here's the biggy many of you EQ jumkies (Like myself)  have been waiting for. Late last night while playing EQ Brad (Aradune) Hit the beta and made a broadcast on the status of EQ etc. Here's the goods.

We are finally able to announce some dates! Most of the announcements will go out tomorrow, but I had the chance to post them here tonight, and I know people have been waiting a long time, so here goes:

EverQuest will be released on March 16, 1999.

Phase 4 testing will begin early-mid February (I'll hopefully have a more precise date soon, but it should be some time between the 1st and 15th).

We will be sending out an email to all registered beta testers (yes, the one we had hoped to send out in early December) explaining how and where to sign up for Phase 4 next week. This will include pricing.

So there you have it. So far the beta test has been a huge success, and we appreciate both those who have participated, and patience of those who could not, who have been waiting.

Phase 4 is around the corner, and the release of the game around the next. It's a very exciting time for us, but at the same time there's still a lot of work to be done, and I and the team will deliver the game you all have been waiting for shortly.

Thanks for your continued interest and faith in the game and the development team behind it.
