Roberta Williams Interview - Sacrates
The Queen of sting herself was Interviewed by none other than Game-Interviews.com. It's always good to read the munchies from her as she is one of my personal favorite game designers. And not only because she's a she.

I knew back in 1994/95 when I was finishing up "Phantasmagoria" that my next game would be 3d. I also knew that KQ8 was going to be my next game. Going from 2d to 3d is a natural progression. Since I've been working on adventure games (early 80's), I have wanted to create true 3d adventure games...the only problem was that until very recently, computers haven't been powerful enough to create 3d worlds which were both responsive and beautiful. Since adventure games are, at their core, interactive stories, detail and beauty of a world is very important, and computers just weren't capable of creating detailed, beautiful 3d worlds until the last year or so. Now they are...and I'm very glad that adventure games will now be able to 'come into their' own in 3d from now on. Once you have experienced an adventure game in 3d-it's very difficult to go back!

Do severe wounds cause continued bleeding?

Only if the player is already unconcious.

Yes, there are DOT (Damage over Time) spells that do this. It's actually a clock time, rather than number of rounds. Poisions are like this.

By the way, his first question is totally true. I like the fact that you can actually get knocked into a bad wet dream before your impending death. Makes it that much more dramatic with you sitting there staring on in horror as a few Orcs get medevil on your unconcious ass. I've actually been able to escape this too...where someone would come along and help right after I got knocked out, so I got up and ran like a one legged Halfling. Anyway, here's a bit on Quests.

Quests provide a nice diversion. If you are in the area of Freeport, Rivervale, Erudin, and espicially Qeynos, there are a lot of quests. For instantance, in Qeynos, virtually every named NPC has or is part of a quest. Some quests require further killing, some require travel, and others escorting. There is even a spy mission or two.

Trade skills will also provide a nice diversion, as you can't exactly run around killing while working on your skills and selling your wares. Well, i suppose you can, but at least its something else.

And hey! If all else fails you could try socializing :)

Even more on quests etc.

As a powergamer, one thing you will want to keep in mind is that the best items in the game will come through questing. I hear powergamers like toys :)

As for the quests, steps are being taken now to make sure that people don't feel like they are playing "Guess the magic keyword" like in UO. They are already far more indepth, but some people still find it hard to figure out what to ask the NPCs. One thing to keep in mind is that you must speak in complete properly punctuated sentences. One thing you do is actually read what they say, the thing you ask must be in thier text.

For instance, if you say hello to an NPC and they start complaining about the Pirates of Katharg, you might try asking "Who are the Pirates of Katharg?". Just saying "Pirates of Katharg?" will usually not work.

Just remember the 5 W's and H. Who, what, when, where, why, and how.

Who are the Pirates of Katharg?
What do the Pirates of Katharg want from you?
When will the Pirates of Katharg attack?
Where can I find the Katharg of Gunthak?
Why are the Pirates of Katharg killing your people?
How do I defeat the Pirates of Katharg?

Here's another important little feature you'll notice about EQ when you first start playing.

No, you cannot see how many hitpoints, but you can see a health bar. The health bar purposefully lags behind the hit to show impact to simulate the amount of time that a large hit might take to show damage. For instance, if you hit a creature with 100 HP's for 50, it might take 2 or 3 seconds for the bar to start moving, and another 2 or 3 seconds for it to get down to 50%. It really makes your heart leap up into your throat the first time you take on that new and dangerous looking critter and hit it 3 times for all you are worth before you see it's healthbar move :)

Here's an even more interesting bit on racial balances in the game.

No dual wield. Also, dwarves aren't as liked by the other races as say, a human. Not to mention, due to the racial advantages of being a dwarf (e.g. infravision, sense heading bonus, Strength and Stam), you can expect a sizable experience penalty for playing one, and your agility is lower.

However, one thing I would like ot stress is that STATS are not the be all and end all. Stats are the LAST modifier used to determine the stregth of a hit, the amount of mana, etc. First is skill, followed by level (which are closely intertwined).

Then take into account that people will gravitite to magic items that improve thier stats as a character. For instance, dwarves will not be as interested in items that raise strength as those that raise agility. Humans will be more interested in strength.

That's the purpose of balance. A wood elf warrior is no worse a warrior than an ogre warrior. Even at 50th level, I'd expect naked fight between the two, tactics not withstanding, to go even. Sure, the ogre is stronger by far, but the elf is much more agile (harder to hit) and dexterous (has better control of thier blade) than the ogre. It all balances out.

-Abashi, Everquest Guide
