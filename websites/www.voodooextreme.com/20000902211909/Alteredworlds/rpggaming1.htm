the future of computer RPG gaming  

by  Ash

I was sitting around feeling sorry for myself that I had no games to play ....  so I decided to make better use of my time than to just mope about it.  This is not really an article, just maybe a short rant about some of the really cool crap that is coming out for the PC and is going to put you in gaming nirvana. 

A lot of my views are "probably" biased and are strictly my opinion. I am not a computer genius nor do I believe I ever will be. However, I am an avid gamer who is obsessed about computer games to the point where my wife gets jealous of the time I give them.  I am probably asking for it, but do correct me if what I say is just not accurate. I really want to hear what you, the reader, thinks. However, if I hurt someone's feelings and they claim that I am biased -  ***cough **Romero***cough*** - then that is just a matter of my opinion. And one man's opinion is his own. 

 

Lately there has been some really cool technology released to the public.  The Gigahertz CPU ( that’s 1000 mhz ) reared its head this year, the very cool Voodoo5 and GeForce 2 also will arrive very shortly - we should see those cards in a software stores in about a month - and we are finally seeing DSL and cable modems become more common. However, the big question is: "Where are the games that make use of all this cool technology?!?".

Well basically they are all in development. Well, you know what??? I want my games NOW!!!. I am so bored that I wrote this article as a sort of self-help step to get my mind off things, and maybe lift my spirits.

I’m going to focus on two things here, Internet Technology  and Graphic Cards.

 

Internet Technology

Right now one of the current goals for all online game developers is to immerse the player into their world, and a lot of that has to do with detail and eye-candy. The more internet bandwidth you have, the more detail you can put in. Currently Everquest can function with a 28.8K modem, although 56K is recommended. Now, if the minimum connection speed was that of a 56K modem, and a DSL/Cable modem was what was recommended … then the level of detail would be able to go up.

My big gripe with EQ is how hard it is to find a unique look for your character. That problem in part is from the low graphic card and internet connection requirements that the game has. EQ is made so that almost every computer can play it.

Think of it this way. If Verant made a remarkable post tomorrow that said they would now allow everyone to have "custom" faces and armor in EQ, then the requirements for the game would shoot up. To give you an example on how it would require more internet bandwidth think of the following scenario. You have a tavern full of about 50 players, each with a custom skin. Before you play the game, all of those textures are pre-loaded into your computer as an automated update. If you have a DSL or Cable Modem … no problem. If you have a 56K, well, that may take awhile to get the whole update. 

Currently, Asheron's Call has a very charming way of dealing with server congestion: "Portal Storms". In all seriousness, I think they stink. Whenever you have people in your zone or area, the main server has to send you info to your computer telling it what they are doing.  If the player Bendor moves toward the tavern, then it has to send you info about that and what he looks like doing that. Now if you have 40+ people roaming around in a tavern, and each of them is doing a separate task …. then you have trouble. The server has to tell you what each and every one of them are doing and what they look like.

You may think that your 56K is just fine, but in reality it is just holding back a good game from being "THE-BOMB". Internet speed is really important, and as new internet technology becomes available and existing technology improves, we the gamers can only benefit.

 

Graphic Cards

This has to be absolutely one of my favorite topics.

3 – 4 Years Ago: I remember 3 years ago when the Voodoo2 came out. I thought it was the bomb. If you had a V2, you where awesome. And if you had two Voodoo2’s in SLI (SLI basically means you put two of those cards in your computer and they worked in unison) then you were a pagan-god. Amazing as it may seem, I believe you can run Everquest on a Voodoo2 yet, though not the current expansion pack.

Then came along the TNT1, with full support for DirectX and OpenGL and no mini-GL nonsense with the TNT, just pure gaming power. The TNT held its ground against the V2 even in SLI mode.

1 – 2 Years Ago: It seems like it was almost last week when we had the TNT2/ Voodoo3 wars. Actually, there wasn’t any war. TNT2 beat V3 hands down. V3 is a great card, just not the best.

NOW : Ok, just a minute, I have to take a deep breath for this one. You have to remember before you read this, I am really only drawing from what I have read in previews and other articles. I do not have a degree in quantum physics nor am I anything of a brainiac. I simply am a power gamer on the lookout for my next gaming rig.

GeForce: This baby is really sweet. It was the first to include incredible support for T&L (Transform and Lighting) and is able to handle higher polygon counts better. Trust me when I say that these two things are gonna make gaming smoother and look better. Everquest may look great to some of you, but it could look a whole lot better. The polygon count for the characters is very low, which makes them look somewhat primitive compared to the models in Quake3. High polygon counts make a game look impressive and add a lot of cool detail. It would require a LOT of polygons to get the effect of the "mysterious" Ultima Online 2 screenshot, but as you can see polygons are really cool. Current video cards wouldn’t be able to handle that, but I have no doubt that in a year and a half video cards will be able to without breaking a sweat.

Next Month: Has the debuts of two very cool and competitive cards: the GeForce2 and Voodoo5.

I wouldn’t mind having either of these cards. Both have incredible speed, and they support a feature called FSAA. Basically, FSAA takes any jagged edges that you may see and smoothes them out.

In 6 To 7 Months: Will be the debuts of the NV20 (Basically GeForce 3), and the incredible Voodoo5 6000. These are both out of this world. I have seen a screenshot of the V5 6000, and am pondering whether it is real or not. The V5 6000 has 128mb of onboard ram. Little is known about the NV20, but you can be assured that it will probably be able to push over 1200 megapixels per second. The GeForce could do 480 mps and the GeForce 2 is said to be able to do 800 mps.

So what does this mean to you? Well, it means incredible eye-candy. And I have always been a sucker for eye-candy. Take a look at some of the screenshot for the upcoming Morrowwind http://www.elderscrolls.com/ScreenShot1.htm . Be sure to look at all 4 and keep in mind that these are "in-game" screenshots. These are what it’s going to look like. My first impressions where good ones to say the least. The amount of polygons that Morrowwind will be pushing is unbelievable. That is just a sample of how detailed future computer online games will be. If you are one of the lucky who get a Playstation 2, just remember that in a year everything for the computer will look twice as good. You may still be thinking "so why is Ash so worked up?" Well, think of it, a game with no graphical limitations.

Think of an Everquest with these added features and tell me if this doesn’t excite you:

  1. Instead of maybe 20 different faces for each race , how about 200 or even maybe 400. A game so detailed that you have to shave you face. You could grow a beard one month or sideburns the next.

  2. Instead of the same old choices of armor at the armor smith, you could choose from over a hundred styles of armor for a really cool and unique look. Right now Everquest can be played by people who have a TNT1 and a Pentuim2 233 mhz. To do this, Verant had to limit the amount of choices you have for different armor types so those TNT1’ers can play. Imagine a full city of warriors, wizards, and elves …. all of them unique looking with their stoic armor and cool robe designs.

  1. Wouldn’t it be nice if a city was really the size of a city? Hey don’t get me wrong, I think EQ's Qeynos is pretty cool looking, but my hometown is bigger than that. It would be nice if the city had all those little details that real cities do. Imagine being able to buy a house. Example: You live on elm street, you have a dog, and a maid who comes and cleans every once and awhile. You also decorate your home, you buy furniture, upgrade the door lock, have a trophy room, and maybe paint the walls.

The ultimate thrill for me would be to buy a castle with my fellow guilds-men and live there. That would be cool, a stable, training hall, kitchen, bar, meeting room, and living quarters where you could put your stuff.

Now are these some cool ideas, or what? All of these new video cards and fast Gigahertz computers are going to make these things possible. And when that happens, I will be in gaming heaven.
