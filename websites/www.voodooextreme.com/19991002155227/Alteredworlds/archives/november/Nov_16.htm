These chips have really caught me by surprise. I was expecting another marketing stunt ala two-headed chimp, but it seems like this is going to be Something Good. 3DFX is determined to remain the leader with this single chip solution that is touted to be more powerful than the Voodoo 2 SLI. From what I have been able to gather from preliminary benchmarks, this seem to be the case, although we still have quite some time for hardware and driver maturation to show increases in speed.

Speed is not the only thing to consider in this chip, however. Some of the things that are definitely not something to scoff at are:

Dual 32-bit texture rendering architecture
True Multi-texturing: two textures per pixel per clock
Single Pass, Single Cycle Bump mapping
Single Pass, Single Cycle trilinear mip-mapping
Per-pixel atmospheric fog with programmable fog-zones

The chip will also support DVD playback at 30 frames per second with no dropped frames

More Diablo II DD - Bowmeister
The guys over at Gamespot have slapped up part two of their Designer Diaries' interview with Diablo II's Max Schaefer. There's more insight to the dark and dirty underground of designing game graphics, as well as mention of 3DFX in Diablo II.

GameSpot: So how does 3Dfx work into your art design?

Max: The beauty of our particular use of 3Dfx is that it is entirely on the programming end. We [the art department] actually did nothing new to have the 3Dfx implementation. In other words, we didn't have to redo any artwork. We didn't have to format it any differently. [The 3D-enhanced graphics] just works within our given construct and [Diablo II] takes advantage of the 3Dfx chips that are there. One of the reasons [for this] is that we don't want to get into a situation where we are making a game for the 3Dfx at the expense of people who don't have a 3Dfx card.

Elves in ME - Sacrates
It's a Monday and I am getting about as much RPG news as I am getting Screenshots or Beta slots from Brad. However I am getting a morsel here and there, and just happened to wonder over to the ME boards and noticed Janus Anderson posted a bit on Elves in Middle Earth.

We've proposed a few systems to deal with overpopulation of elves in ME. According to the statistics we've gathered from the registration at our website, around half of the players want to play Elves initially. This, of course, is quite contrary to the works of Tolkien.

One proposed solution is to have an initial contest of sorts where people send in a brief essay describing the background and history of their Elf in order to secure the ability to play an Elven character. In this way, we would start a small seed population of strong RPers who would hopefully play in character. After the initial seed population has been selected, they would be given the power to make other players 'Elf-friends'. After a player had accrued enough 'Elf-friend' tags, he/she would also be able to create Elven characters. We would, of course, limit the number of friendships awarded based on time - Elves could maybe only award one player a month.

Unfortunately, this system is fairly complicated and would contribute to customer support costs. We may just award the ability to create an Elf randomly, or use a less complicated system.

One other possible solution is to make the elves advance much slower but have them ultimately become more powerful than the other races. Since elves live much longer than the other races, they're not in a rush to learn anything quickly since they have forever to do it. In this way, casual players would be more likely to play a non-Elf race, and the hardcore players who have lots of time to invest would play Elves.

More U:A Bits - Bowmeister
Yep...Sith is back with more juicy tidbits off the U:A Discussion boards. I don't know if you've been over to the boards, but finding info on them is like trying to read a novel in Hebrew while riding a tilt-a-whirl. Ah well, here's a bunch of goodness from the man himself, Richard Garriott...first, on multiplayer in U:A...

In the future a Multiplayer party adventure would be a cool thing! But in this release of Ultima IX ascension, no.

Then he responds to a bunch of questions that have been kicking around...

Wow, some good stuff in here! Let see, how can I respond to some of these?...

Most mentioned was the party, I hear the wish, but we cannot meet thy desires. Nor do I think it would be the right thing to do at this time. We'll see.

Next most asked seemed to be audio. We do have excellent surround audio. World noises and creature sounds are 3D positioned around you and even enclosed spaces echo correctly. Is is cool. I'm sure you'll like it.

Their was a question posed about does immersive equal fun. To which I say, no. Immersive is a tool to make the experience feel as real as possible. I want to believe it is really happening. If you just want to play a game, their are plenty of those out too. Immersive does not preclude fun factor, but does present certain challenges. That's our job.

Replay... Boy I hear that one from my bosses every day. But I do not have a great answer. We are creating a well crafted experience, we do not have a plan for add ins, downloads, mission disks, level editors or post winning levels at this time. After we ship, we'll rethink this again, but right now, we just want to finish this solo player masterpiece. We think that's enough of a challenge.

And finally on why there is no option to play a female Avatar...

Let me lay this debate to rest. As the perpetrator of said discrimination or strategy, let me tell you which it is. Neither! It's purely practicality. I would much rather give the option and have the whole game configure itself based on gender preference and even your gender preference for a mate. Early on in U9 that's the way we had it. However, once we decided that we wanted to have pre-rendered movie segments. Then we had to decide to either never show the Avatar, render two of ever scene that had the Avatar, allow only male Avatars or even if you were a female in the game, the flics showed males. The never show didn't make for the artistic style we wanted, the double render was more than we could afford, so we have chosen male only Avatars. The fact that only a few % of Ultima players are female (known by UO account registrations) did help us decide on this path. But we will always provide female options when feasible.

So, please don't interpret the answer in too deep a fashion, we are just trying to get a game out.

Cynthe from the developers team also stopped by with this to say on transparency...

Yes, the water will be transparent in the waterfalls, oceans, etc. A few revisions are being made to the transparency system, but we will certainly post some water screenshots soon! You should see some of the gorgeous waterfall areas our artists have created...:)
