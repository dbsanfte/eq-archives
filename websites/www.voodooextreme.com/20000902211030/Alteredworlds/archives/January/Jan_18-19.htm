Of course, all would not be right and good with the world if there weren't the weekly Wheel of Time update by Glen. I have no idea what's going on with the servers so don't ask me, all I can do is hope they get the Mofo back up sometime within the Millenium!

To be honest, I just spent way too much time writing up the promised Counter Contest column.  If I'm going to get any work done this evening, I can't write a whole lot for the Development Update.   I'll just hit the highlights.

Mark has been heading up the tech team's effort to integrate the game with the Unreal 220 build in the anticipation that 221 is just around the corner. Every attempt spits out a few bugs, which are quickly squashed, and most of the major issues have been resolved.  We should have a stable, up-to-date WoT build any time now.

Joel Walden showed up on our doorstep today with his wife in tow, after a harrowing trip across the country.  He's here and ready to dive into his new job painting textures and skins. We're very glad to have him here and can't wait to see him start putting his touch on this game.   Welcome!

Progress is occurring on every front--AI, animation, modeling, effects, texture mapping, UI, etc.--but I'll have to go into it all next week.

One more thing: I saw that in this month's PC Accelerator print mag--on stands now--The Wheel of Time managed to snag a prize for one of the Best Games of 1999, earning their "Gorgeous Design" award.  Make sure to check it out! 

Until next time!

Abashi was over on the boards slapping the readers around like a Pimp on his Ho. New sounds went in last night and Beta 3.5 begins sometime today which requires another fat ass download. Thank god for a cable modem. Here's a mini interview.

Do severe wounds cause continued bleeding?

Only if the player is already unconcious.

:Are there spells that damage an opponent for X number of rounds instead of all at once?

Yes, there are DOT (Damage over Time) spells that do this. It's actually a clock time, rather than number of rounds. Poisions are like this.

Keep in mind that there will be magic weapons in game that can cast spells from "every once in a while" to "every hit". Also, there is the wonders of a poisioned weapon.

:Im sure its WAY too late to add this if there isnt anything already, but I was just wondering if there was.

Yep, it's there :)

By the way, his first question is totally true. I like the fact that you can actually get knocked into a bad wet dream before your impending death. Makes it that much more dramatic with you sitting there staring on in horror as a few Orcs get medevil on your unconcious ass. I've actually been able to escape this too...where someone would come along and help right after I got knocked out, so I got up and ran like a one legged Halfling. Anyway, here's a bit on Quests.

Quests provide a nice diversion. If you are in the area of Freeport, Rivervale, Erudin, and espicially Qeynos, there are a lot of quests. For instantance, in Qeynos, virtually every named NPC has or is part of a quest. Some quests require further killing, some require travel, and others escorting. There is even a spy mission or two.

Trade skills will also provide a nice diversion, as you can't exactly run around killing while working on your skills and selling your wares. Well, i suppose you can, but at least its something else.

And hey! If all else fails you could try socializing :)

Even more on quests etc.

As a powergamer, one thing you will want to keep in mind is that the best items in the game will come through questing. I hear powergamers like toys :)

As for the quests, steps are being taken now to make sure that people don't feel like they are playing "Guess the magic keyword" like in UO. They are already far more indepth, but some people still find it hard to figure out what to ask the NPCs. One thing to keep in mind is that you must speak in complete properly punctuated sentences. One thing you do is actually read what they say, the thing you ask must be in thier text.

For instance, if you say hello to an NPC and they start complaining about the Pirates of Katharg, you might try asking "Who are the Pirates of Katharg?". Just saying "Pirates of Katharg?" will usually not work.

Just remember the 5 W's and H. Who, what, when, where, why, and how.

Who are the Pirates of Katharg?
What do the Pirates of Katharg want from you?
When will the Pirates of Katharg attack?
Where can I find the Katharg of Gunthak?
Why are the Pirates of Katharg killing your people?
How do I defeat the Pirates of Katharg?

Here's another important little feature you'll notice about EQ when you first start playing.

No, you cannot see how many hitpoints, but you can see a health bar. The health bar purposefully lags behind the hit to show impact to simulate the amount of time that a large hit might take to show damage. For instance, if you hit a creature with 100 HP's for 50, it might take 2 or 3 seconds for the bar to start moving, and another 2 or 3 seconds for it to get down to 50%. It really makes your heart leap up into your throat the first time you take on that new and dangerous looking critter and hit it 3 times for all you are worth before you see it's healthbar move :)

Here's an even more interesting bit on racial balances in the game.

No dual wield. Also, dwarves aren't as liked by the other races as say, a human. Not to mention, due to the racial advantages of being a dwarf (e.g. infravision, sense heading bonus, Strength and Stam), you can expect a sizable experience penalty for playing one, and your agility is lower.

However, one thing I would like ot stress is that STATS are not the be all and end all. Stats are the LAST modifier used to determine the stregth of a hit, the amount of mana, etc. First is skill, followed by level (which are closely intertwined).

Then take into account that people will gravitite to magic items that improve thier stats as a character. For instance, dwarves will not be as interested in items that raise strength as those that raise agility. Humans will be more interested in strength.

That's the purpose of balance. A wood elf warrior is no worse a warrior than an ogre warrior. Even at 50th level, I'd expect naked fight between the two, tactics not withstanding, to go even. Sure, the ogre is stronger by far, but the elf is much more agile (harder to hit) and dexterous (has better control of thier blade) than the ogre. It all balances out.

-Abashi, Everquest Guide

Avaris Lives! - Sacrates
Looks like Avaris from Bifrost is holding on for dear life. Just got word that they are indeed still looking for a publisher to cover the overhead. We had an Interview with Donald a while back but due to the problems they were experiencing were unable to post it. Here's the blurb.

Yes, we are still alive! I'm sure most of you have been wondering :-)

Well, I suspected a very luke-warm reception from publishers and that's exactly what happened. In the last few months I've been in contact with lots of people and have been given the same line by all of them: small, relatively unknown development teams will not be advanced all that much money. Because of Avaris' AI, it is still going to require an awful lot of development time putting it above publisher's budgets.

I do not plan on simply rolling over and playing dead, though :-) Initially I thought about creating an adventure game set in the Troll Wars, about 40 years before the current story takes place. Because it would have been an adventure game and because it would have featured only a partial implementation of the AI, it would have been a lot less expensive to make. I don't feel that this would be the right track to take, though. What I'd like to try now is to concentrate on finishing the design and implementation of the AI. I then hope to try to license it to a larger developer for use in an RPG or adventure game. That would give us enough capital to bring Avaris itself to market.

I'd like to thank you all for all of your help and suggestions up until now. If you are still interested in keeping up with the game, stay subscribed to the list or check the web page periodically - ya, I know it's looking REALLY corporate right now, but you can jump directly to the Avaris section (which is still being redone) with this URL: http://www.BifrostCreations.com/avaris

The Avaris pages are really barren right now, but there are some details of the AI engine posted. If you're interested, head on over and check it out.

Take care for now,

Donald

Spector on RPG's - Sacrates
GamasUtra have gone off the deep end and poked up one hell of a big look at RPG's for Developers by Warren Spector. This thing is so indepth it leaves the genre laying naked and exposed like a monkey in a lab experiment. It's quite an interesting read for developers and otherwise. Here's a biscuit.

My intent with this article is to lay out the abundant variety of choices available to would-be and practicing RPG designers. Only by analyzing the tools that we all use in the creation of our games, discussing the ways in which these tools have been and can be used, and identifying the ramifications of those uses, can we take this genre forward.

Fundamentally, this article is an attempt to identify what it's going to take to be a player in the RPG category in the years to come. I hope to identify the critical elements a game must have to compete and dominate. However, before looking at what it will take to best the RPG competition in 1999 and beyond, it seems reasonable to look (briefly) at where roleplaying is today and how we got here.
