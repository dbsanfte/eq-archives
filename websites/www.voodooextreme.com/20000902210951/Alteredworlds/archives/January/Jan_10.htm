Sunday January 10, 1999

Norrath...the final frontier - Sacrates
Here is a bit of an update on my continuing adventures in EQ after my marathon this morning of another 5 hours. First of all I have switched from a Wizard to a Cleric a few days ago and have built myself up to level 4. Today was also a milestone for me in that it was my first opportunity to venture out of the Greater Faydark into the Steamfront Mountains. So far my whole experience has been in the dark and gloomy forests of the Elves, but today I ventured out into the light of day and noticed one thing....it's bright! After looking at Trees, trees, and more trees with a whole lot of fog tossed in, this was an amazing experience. Not to mention the most beautifull sunset I have seen on a PC. I also noticed some very cool, yet tough new monsters. Let me emphasize that the character animations are simply amazing and must be seen. Screenshots don't do it justice. I don't know how to emphasize this even more, this is no UO here kids! The funny thing is that I haven't had a chance to play this with any sound yet! I have been playing in absolute silence since I got it. They aren't putting in the new sound system until Beta 4.

Altered Take - Sacrates
There is a lot of talk running around right now about what people like more, single player RPG's or massive multiplayers? I would love to be "old school" on this issue and say that I like Single player more but I can't. As Baldur's gate proves, it's got the massive size and return to the old days of RPGing, yet is still limited to what can be done. Don't get me wrong, I love BG and the single player RPG experience BUT, more personal opinion leads to massive multiplayer. Why with all of those bugs and all those kids running around spamming your screen with trash talk would I like this type of game? Because in this case it actually does promote role playing the way it was meant...personal interaction. I don't care how advanced our AI's get over the next few years there is nothing that can beat the depth of a human mind and it's unpredictability. Take Billy and I for example...we could boggle the mind of even the most intelligent Psychiatrist. Anyway, I think you'll understand as soon as you get to play Everquest for the first time. It has all the potential and even more than UO had but this time it capitalizes on it. With that said, it has been a 3 day marathon for me with this game so far and it doesn't look to end any time soon. I'll see you guys soon in a whole new world.

EQ Morsels - Sacrates
It is always good to get these little morsels on the boards. Abashi was back to it answering a few questions. This first one is on how long Everquest will last and how much is there to see.

How about a world so full of depth that realisticly no one will ever experience the full diversity EQ has to offer?

So you spend 60 days p-time building up a monk to the highest states that a character can reach? What then you ask? You still haven't fought and killed every creature in the game, you probably still haven't seen nor survived through to the bottom of a number of dungeons. By then, Kunark and Velious will probably be up in which case you definatly haven't.

You haven't even seen the majority of magic items in the game, much less worn/wielded them. You haven't gone on even 10% of the quests, as many will be race, class, and deity based.

By the time you reach 50th level there will be 20x more stuff you haven't done than the stuff you have.

Here he is again talking about the scarcity of Artifacts in the game.

There will indeed be Artifacts of Great Power (funny, that's what they are called) and they will have a history behind them. But don't ever expect to own one, as they will be exceedingly few and far between. I shudder to the the odds of one person ever having one, let alone more than one.

U:A goods - Sacrates
Sith was back to work after a brief hiatus with a bunch of good morsels from the programmers on the boards. We're getting close to this jewel of a game. First is a bit on where the hell the Christmas Present they promised is.

We are working on something.. unfortunately it got delayed (due to no one person, but you may blame me if you like ;)... I'm hoping to have something new and exciting within a couple of weeks to share. :)

Here's a bit on Digital vs Midi sounds.

Completely depends on the source material and how much time our Audio Engineers have. If the source audio was composed with MIDI and recorded into digital, then it wouldn't be too huge a deal to have a choice. However, I was under the impression that the music is being recorded using live performances, which would make it difficult to provide MIDI tracks... Don't hold me to that though - I'm not the authority on the source material.

There is a clipping plane for the terrain (which includes mountains), but it is more distant than the clipping plane for buildings and other objects, and separately adjustable.

ME and the Spawn - Sacrates
Here is a popular question with these types of games...the all important spawn. It's also been a while since we got any news for Middle Earth so this is always good to see. This first one is on whether the spawn will handle like The Realm's.

We may use the spawning dungeon system in some isolated sub-dungeon areas. For instance, Moria is a HUGE place that is filled with many mini-dungeons. Some of those mini-dungeons may spawn. In the event that they do and you logout, the spawn would be held. You would reappear in the same spawned dungeon if you log back in. Expect the use of spawning dungeons to be rare, if at all.

And here's a small bit on becoming an Elf and looking like one too.

Once you get the ability to play an Elf, you will need to create a new elven character. That elven character will look like an elf, with specific facial features and clothing options.

Darkstone cometh - Sacrates
Now here is a game that is starting to make me all warm and fuzzy without the use of Interns or exotic drugs. Gamecenter is dishing out the goods on this upcoming biscuit with this Preview. Graphically it looks like it could surely give Diablo 2 a run for it's mega millions.

With sci-fi action/adventure and racing firmly under its belt, Delphine is now trying its hand at fantasy role-playing in Dark Stone. Based on our early look, it certainly appears up to the challenge. Prepare to lead two of your own characters on a Herculean quest to collect seven magic crystals, which empower a mystical orb, and finally conquer one very scary dragon, Drakk. In the process, you'll journey through 32 daunting dungeons created by the game's Quest Generator, which assures no two games are ever alike.
