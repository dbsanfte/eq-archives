Dr Ray on BG - Sacrates
About time Ray made an update to the page. I tell ya those Crack habits are a bitch. Anyway, he updated the official page with this whole bunch of Baldur's Gate lovin. Enjoy.

We continue to work 14-16 hour days, seven days a week, both here at BioWare and down at Black Isle, trying to finish the game. What we're aiming this week is a FC, a final candidate. This isn't necessarily THE final candidate, rather it is a version of the game with all known bugs resolved that Black Isle and BioWare quality assurance can review in detail, and let us know what bugs they then find in the FC so we can fix these. We've still got work ahead of us, but we're finally starting to see the end of this three year tunnel now...

We reached a new milestone on the game over the weekend - we were down to 56 active bugs (out of some 6000 total dealt with to date). That number didn't stick unfortunately - it went up to just over 100 active bugs after we added in the bugs that were found at BioWare over the weekend. But, all bugs were assigned and fixed Monday and again we await the next bug report to see where things are at. The good thing about all of the bugs is that a lot of them are becoming harder and harder to actually see as bugs unless you know the game very well - that is, you wouldn't know that someone's dialogue is not working completely since it makes sense as it stands. This is why it's great having people playing the game who really know the way it's supposed to be, and the AD&D rules - they are catching a lot of things that are increasingly more and more subtle.

We're doing the multiplayer long distance checklist right now - making sure that we catch any areas where the multiplayer game functions differently from the single player game is a top priority - since we anticipate that a lot of people will be playing the game in multiplayer mode, or exporting characters back and forth between single and multiplayer games. The multiplayer games are increasingly more and more solid of late. But we continue to test the multiplayer checklist with each and every build.

Everyone here is pretty tired, but no one's complaining. Morale is quite high, and people are glad to see the game becoming more and more fun. It's a delight to play each new version of the game and see the bugs that had been on the bug list last time, now working...

We're now in what is, I'm told, the most difficult part of any project - the final finishing kick. The team at BioWare and Black Isle are all busting a hump to make sure that we're 100% happy with what goes out the door - everyone's doing a great job, and I feel very proud to be on the BG team.

Ok, new game...same mouth. Bob really puts a hurtin to those bionic implants of his. This guy posts like it's nobodies bidness. Anyway he was on the boards dishing out more Dues Ex sauce that you'll want to cuddle up to right here. First bit is on NPC's etc.

NPC's in our game will have activities above and beyond interfacing with you. Lots of times you will stumble onto them at different areas in the game as they go about their routines. In fact you might even be asked to "tail" some one at one point.

Actually we thought just a card with the interface layout would have been enough...

Just kidding ;) We will do our best to provide you with the best documentation we can. And then on to the Weapons oh my.

Also there will be the "secret" weapons that the goverment always keeps under wraps.

Weapons can be gained in several ways:
1. Issued by the "agency"
2. Black market arms dealers
3. Theft or found in locations
4. Pried from the cold dead hands of enemies.

Some weapons will be comon place (i.e. 9mm pistol) but others will be difficult to get. The arms dealer has access to weapons other than what can be found on the street and the agency has a few different ones too.

BG shipping news - Sacrates
Greg was on the boards spitting out goodness left and right for us tonight. One in particularly that winked my fancy was this one on shipping and preordering through them. Makes me go....hmmmm.

This is a new program entirely. We are changing the way we do things in order to deliver better service. We have hired a new fulfillment center. We are processing our shipments differently.

On Fallout2 we had problems because we were in the midst of switching companies. Everyone who got a late copy of the game also got an apology letter and a free Fallout2 t-shirt.

So I'll take your bet... and I'll raise you a copy of "Volo's Guide to the Sword Coast" ;)

Greg punched the bucket even further with this post.

I myself have been burned by many pre-orders, so I know what you are talking about. Here is what is going on...

Previously, we did not prioritize the pre-order product AS STRICTLY. Typically it would take about 2-3 days to produce all the product for the initial sell-in. We would produce the product and then start shipping it out. In some cases, other shipments went out before the pre-order product. Then the pre-order product would get to our fulfillment center (taking several days) and our fulfillment center would take a few days to start sending out orders, and then if you shipped it two day, it would take a few days, etc, etc.

On the other hand, EB has been doing a fantastic job getting product on their shelves very quickly. For Fallout2 they actually OVERNIGHTED product from our production facility to their warehouse and had people fulfilling store orders the same night so that stores had it on Saturday when the product was produced on Wednesday. I think they did a great job!

So what we have done is shaken up our distribution system to get the pre-order process moving along much more quickly. We will send the pre-order product out first, probably before the rest of the first production run is even finished. Then we will use faster shipping to get it to the fulfillment center more quickly. Finally, we have the fulfillment center staffing up to guarantee a one-day turnaround on orders.

Note that we are not trying to BEAT EB to the market. I wish every retailer was as fast. But not everyone lives near an EB. Plus, some people like to know that the product is going to show up at their door without having to go out and search for it. Imagine if you knew an EB had the product and you had to wait for a weekend to get there and they had sold out?

Eventually we want to get to a state of the art order fulfillment system where you can check, in real time, the status of your order. The second that sucker ships, you will receive a confirmation email stating that it is on the road.

So it sounds like you are one of the folks that lives close to an EB, has a copy reserved with them, and will hop into a car the second the title arrives (kinda like me with Railroad Tycoon II this weekend). Also, doesn't sound like the Volo's Guide offer holds much appeal for you. Go with EB. Most likely you will save some money as well.

My note was more to make sure people understood that we had changed our system (and will continue to improve it) versus make it sound like the end-all be-all solution. Whatever works best for everyone from a price/convenience/features standpoint, we want to make sure we have a good program to support...

RTK Preview - Sacrates
Those delightfull lads over at VN slipped up a Preview of Return to Krondor. This is another one of those games that doesn't seem to be getting the press that it deserves. This thing looks like the deal.

Combat in Return to Krondor is turn-based using the Initiative attribute. Unlike its predecessor, however, it does not take place on a separate screen. "When you're in combat, you really get into it," says PyroTechnix Studio Manager Sinjin Bain. "Even though we maintained the turn-based structure which everyone who has seen it really likes, after you choose your tactic for the round, the action is carried out in real-time for that turn." Ranged attacks using bows and arrows require line of sight.

The combat experience is also enhanced by many of the game's 25,000 frames of motion capture, and spells make effective use of the True3D engine's real-time lighting capabilities. According to Bain, "The bottom line is we've added cutting edge action and graphics to a classic turn-based RPG system. The feedback has been very positive."

Important notice on BG - Sacrates
Greg Peterson was in the Backroom and slapped this info up on Preordering Baldur's Gate directly through them. Of course at the end he states that BG WILL be released Fall 98. If this game is released any time in November I swear I will post news standing on my head for an entire day.

I'm going to post this here because we won't have all the fancy buttons and stuff up on the home page until tomorrow.

Here is our pre-order program. Note that this affects you if you already placed a pre-order for Baldur's Gate with Interplay - you will get this for free.

First a statement about delivery prioritization:

All our pre-orders take top priority for order fulfillment. With Baldur's Gate, the day we produce the game we will aggregate our total number of preorders, add on a safety factor for additional sales and pull that many units off the production line BEFORE ANY OTHER PRODUCT FOR ANY OTHER PURPOSE. (Yes, even before the product for the Marketing group at Black Isle ;) ) This 'priority' product will be expressed shipped to our fulfillment center (Digital River in Minneapolis) where it will be reshipped (with one day turnaround) to you depending upon your shipping instructions. Our target is to get you the product no later than it shows up in the first store in your area. In many cases you will receive it BEFORE it is available elsewhere.

Now our special offer:

All Interplay pre-orders will be accompanied by a free copy of "Volo's Guide to the Sword Coast". This is an official game accessory for the Forgotten Realms campaign setting for the game of Advanced Dungeons and Dragons. "Volo's Guide to the Sword Coast" covers much of the area that is found in Baldur's Gate, and the content is consistent -- locations that are described in one are found in the other. The book is an oversized paperback with color cover and 240 pages (with many illustrations and maps for you non-reading types). It is also out of print - we have obtained all remaining copies to use in this program.

If this sounds like something you might be interested in, check out our pre-order site at www.interplay-store.com. We don't have a mention of the program at the store yet, but don't worry ALL ORDERS WILL INCLUDE VOLO'S GUIDE. We will have official banners and book art up on the Baldur's Gate home page and the Interplay sales site in the next couple of days.

Oh... and Baldur's Gate will be shipping Fall '98.
