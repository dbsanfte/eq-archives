Revenant revelations - Sacrates
Gatta love it when we get some more goodies from this game. Ben Cooley was on the boards and had some happy word to spread. First one is on Multiplayer Cheating.

We've thought quite a bit about multiplayer cheating. Here's how we're getting around most of the problems...

1. The setup screen for multiplayer mode allows the host of the session to view all the players stats and equipment. The host can edit the players characters in the session (the original character will not be changed though) by changing stats, adding or subtracting equipment, etc. The host can also grant viewing and editing rights to other members of the session. This allows a multiplayer game to be started with all the players being aware of who they are playing with, and how powerful they are. (Players always know if the host can, and has, edited their character .)

2. Combat results and random rolls are validated between the players. If a result isn't verified as 'fair', it is reversed.

3. Game items have unique id's which must match up between players for a multiplayer game to work correctly. This makes it difficult for players to just 'add' items on their system which don't exist on the other systems.

Note that it is still possible for the host to 'hack' the game EXE and cheat, it's just harder to do, and their are many ways that the clients can detect cheating.

How about a Beta guys?

As soon as we can.. we always promise, but there's always more to do....

What's left to be done damnit? I want the game!

Getting closer now. There are only a few functional systems left to hammer out. Multiplayer still isn't complete, there's a bit to rework and finish on the interface (load and save screens aren't in yet!) and then there are the bugs. But the maps, characters, script, and most of the rest of the resources are finished, the voice acting is finished except for retakes, and the animations are done. Anyhow.. not to long.

Yeah so is Motion Capture in the game?

We began the project by attempting to use motion capture for EVERYTHING, but quickly found that there were problems when applying the motion capture to combat and cyclical (walking/running) animations. Instead, we did most of the 'acting' animations with motion capture (a decent amount of animation), and the lion share of combat and interactive moves with Character Studio. This required a much larger animation staff, but it finally worked out the way we wanted it to.

For motion capture, we used a 12 sensor Ascension MotionStar EM based motion capture system (that we bought), combined with an incredibly useful motion editing system called Kaydara Filmbox. Without the Filmbox software, all the motion capture stuff would be garbage. With it, it was still difficult, but workable.

The main problem with motion capture is the fitting of the character with the actor. When you design the character for the actor, this is not a major problem, but when you are trying to motion capture for an 'Ogrok' (Revenant's Orc), etc., you've got some major problems. Filmbox allows you to remap motion fairly easily, but there are still problems with the animation that need to be edited. It really depends on what the motion is, and where it's used, as to wether it's worth it to capture or hand animate.

BG Expansion update - Sacrates
Straight from the official BG Expansion page comes this bit of an update on it's progress into your hands. Hell, I don't even think i'll be able to buy the damn thing at this rate the way i'm playing EQ.

Things have been pretty crazy with trying to get everything finished up for Tales of the Sword Coast. We've just recently managed to finalize all of the marketing materials such as the box, manual, etc, etc. But more importantly, QA is moving towards its final phase, with mostly little annoying bugs left, rather than major "cool, this breaks the entire game" type bugs (although we still have a couple of those too...).

One of the biggest things that has come up recently is that the expansion was a bit too hard. Some of our best QA testers were going into Durlag's tower with their max'd party and...well...getting utterly destroyed. It was like taking a group of first graders to beat up a football team... actually, make that a football team dressed in full plate riding miniature red dragons. Anyhow, we've gone through and figured out how to make TSC balanced as well as more rewarding. The next rev should have all these changes in it -- and if we need to make more after that, we will.

Other than balancing and fixing bugs, the last major step is audio. We were in the studio in Burbank on 2/26 doing all the new VO for TSC. The takes have been chosen and mastered and the new VO got FTP'd to Bioware for implementation this morning. Also, the music is being cut to the movies -- which have already gotten SFX -- and being cut for the narration scenes. The new SFX are pretty much done and are currently being mastered.

Basically, things seem to be coming together pretty well, as noted by the fact that Ben and I were discussing the readme this morning -- a sure indication we're getting close on this one.

Abashi takes leave - Sacrates
Senior Guide Abashi has left this message about him having to leave EQ for a while and resign his post. Too bad since he gave me more news to cover than a chimp on steroids. Here's the bit.

Greetings all,

That vicious demon we refer to as "Real Life" saw fit to drag me away from my comfortable abode in the warm city of Tucson and plunged me face first into the cold wretched wasteland of Chicago. For the next 4 months I'll be in a horrendously small hotel room, forced to watch my cholesterol sky-rocket due to restaurants and fast food. I've done this for the last two weeks, it's pretty bad, but the worst part of this is that I can no longer fulfill my duties within EverQuest. It was with a heavy heart that today I resigned my post as a Senior Guide in EverQuest. My last duty as one of those who handles public relations is to let the EverQuest community know of my departure.

Though I will not be around to enjoy the game in the first few months of release, it is my hope that all of you will give it the chance it so richly deserves. EverQuest has already proved itself to be a concept nearly beyond comprehension in diversity and depth. Here, a week before release, we are prepared to see that concept laid out in all its glory for the whole world to see. No other MMORPG, either in development, or on "the radar" offers an immersion factor and sheer level of playability as high as this game. It truly is a sight to see. Please remember that it is not EverQuest's job to take "fun" and hand it to you on a silver platter. EverQuest's job, as is the job of all other MMORPGs, is to provide you with a diverse atmosphere where you can choose from a buffet of things that are "fun" and to combine them in the proportions that YOU consider to be fun.

I'm not going to jerk anyone's chain and say that there won't be problems from time to time, but they will be few and far between. The Verant staff has proved to me time and time again that they care about this game to the level that a mechanic cares about their car or an artist cares about their canvas. I've spoken with many members of the development team, both on the phone, in game, and through email, and each one of them views this game as their personal measurement of worth. They will not stand idly by and let anything "ruin the game", whether it be the occasional bug or the obnoxious player.

The Guide Program and the Verant Customer Service Staff will continue to work in a swift, caring, and unbiased manner to provide an atmosphere as universally beneficial to all of the desired playstyles. This covers everything from answering your questions, dealing with those who would alienate the other customers, and designing and implementing serious and well-thought-out GM run quests across all the worlds simultaneously. As to the Guides, I've never seen a group of volunteers work so hard for something such as this before. Both they and I feel that we have a stake of ownership in the game. Some of you might think this is strange coming from a bunch of people who used to be just fans, but I assure you that it will go a long way towards making the game a success.

Remember that beyond the graphics, the gameplay, and the customer service staff, one thing, and one thing alone has the capacity to make or break the game: The players. It is the players that will see the world before them and decide whether this game will be a success. It is the players that test the resolve of the development team and customer service staff, and it is the players that ultimately decide how long the game will last. I have great confidence that the majority of players will come to see this game as the work of art that is obvious to me. Please don't disappoint me, please take everything that the development team has given you and go out and make the best of it.

Take care,

Abashi Returning in July

Brad on EQ Duping - Sacrates
This is a VERY rare occurance and it sounds like most of them are squashed anyway. This in no way was anything like the UO fiasco at least. Brad had a bit to say on the subject on the boards.

I've read a few messages here and there by people claiming that there are still dupe bugs in EQ. Now, we've had VERY few dupe bugs, and we've fixed them FAST (we take them VERY seriously). Yet here are these posts (written by people who won't even leave their email address so I can ask them for more info).

So here's the deal. Email me how to dupe in EQ. bmcquaid@verant.com

If we can reproduce the dupe bug, I will PERSONALLY award you a title in-game after launch for your service towards making this a great game.

We are doing everything in our power to make EQ as bug-free as possible, and, like I said, we take all claims of duping very seriously. If there are really some of these bugs left, and you care enough to post on this board or another (or even if you don't), then email me. bmcquaid@verant.com

-Brad McQuaid
Producer, EverQuest

1st/3rd person stats-based RPG for 1 to 4 persons. Set in the 1920's Gothic Horror world of H.P. Lovecraft, players are investigators who must battle sinister plots, crazed cultists, and other-dimensional fiends to find out secrets so terrifying that it may cost them their sanity. For the first time ever, computer gamers will experience the true human interaction and creativity of the original paper Dungeons & Dragons type of games in a CD-based, online, interactive world. Built on the Shattered Light point-and-click dungeon management system (DMS), Dungeon Lord will LOOK like Diablo/Ultima Online (with 3D rendered graphics), but will PLAY like pen & paper RPGs (like Dungeons & Dragons).

Mother of a BG Guide - Sacrates
Desslock over at Gamespot has really stroked (Not that way) the keyboard for this bit. He's made one hell of a large Guide for BG that you'll defenitely want to see if your stuck. Or in my case not even a quarter of the way through. (Ugh EQ) 

This guide contains a complete walk-through for each location in the game, detailing the quests you can find in that area, the monsters and enemies you'll encounter there, and where all of the local treasure can be found. Did someone say quests? Each area walk-through also contains a comprehensive chart that will not only let you know where and how you can obtain each of the more than 100 quests in the game, but also where and how to solve them, and the reward you'll receive for completing the tasks. Finally, if you're just stuck in a particular section of the game and are wondering what to do next, this guide also contains a mini walk-through for all seven chapters in the game, which, when combined with exhaustive strategies detailed in the rest of the guide, will enable you to decisively rout your enemies.
