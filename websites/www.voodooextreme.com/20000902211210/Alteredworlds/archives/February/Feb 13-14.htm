Middle Earth munchies - Sarcrates
You know what they say, a little ME news a day will keep the Crack dealer away. Stephen Nichols, who may indeed be called a form of Crack dealer if your "really" into ME news, posted some goodies.

I have stated this many times already... once more won't hurt. Our goal is to make a fun game based on the world of Middle-earth. Our goal is not to make a ultra-realistic simulation of Middle-earth. In some cases the rules of "reality" are broken to accomodate our genre of game. The global chat system is one such case. It is a necessary feature of these kinds of games that the public repeatedly request. I could continue to rant about how this is a game, and that people in real life have certain expectations for this type of game.... but I won't. Feel free to turn your global chat off and never use it. If global chat is going to make you not want to buy the game then I'm sure you'll find another game that suits you.

Next is one day and night cycles etc.

There will indeed by day and night in Middle-earth. Sadly, the scale of the map forces us to have somewhat unrealistic day/night cycles. We are not setting up the day/night cycles based on the sacle of the map... to do so would have a full cycle every few minutes. Our current plans have the full cycle occuring every hour or so. We will balance this during beta to get the most comfortable timing.

Here's some goodies on Magic.

Here's the bottom line: Magic will exist in Middle-earth, just not in the way it exists in other D&D style games. Tolkien has written a few tidbits describing the nature of magic in Middle-earth. Refer to letter number 155 in the book "The Letters of Tolkien" for more specific information. This letter states that "magic" is not attainable by Men, but the Elves and Istari use it (albeit sparingly). He specifically says that "magic" is not learned through lore or spells, but it is an innate ability. This entirely precludes the notion that men, hobbits or dwarves can cast magic spells. Now, Middle-earth does have an interesting flip side to this. The idea of scientific "magic" is worhty of note. There are many items that were crafted by non-magical races that had what appeared to be true magic (epecially to the unlearned). This opens the possibilty of many other kinds of lore based magic. This kind of "magic" will be the most popular. Those in the know will be able to craft all kinds of devices that perform different "magical" effects. Of course, that obviates the need for raw materials and the like (which the innate magic races don't have to use).

In any event, true magic will be used very sparingly and will be quite difficult to master.

Hre's one for you "Questionable people out there.

No, there is no [animal] breeding.

Etc.

When you enter a building, you will no longer see what is going on outside of the building. It will be treated like a separate map altogether.

EQ Phase 4 Status - Sacrates
Here's Smedly, John that is, back with the goods on the current status of Phase 4.

Here's a brief update of where we are at:

1) Chat/ Login Server - We've basically got it stabalized. Early next week you WILL see the front end go directly to the Server Selection Screen, and there will be a chat option on that page. We had intended this all along, but for now, we needed to load test it AND make sure that we are seeing the problems that are occuring with people trying to get in.

2) Problems with people who can't get into the chat server - We are seeing an isolated problem with some people who get a Black Screen upon logging in. We are looing into it, and we have some ideas.

3) Problems with people locking up on the Loading Screen (Elf woman with the scenes behind her). - This sometimes occurs when a person get's kicked out of a zone because it crashes, or other reasons. We are debugging this problem.

4) Problems with people locking at the character selection screen - this is a pretty rare problem. A couple of notes - If you are running a Monster Fusion - MAKE SURE you have the latest drivers. The older drivers have this problem, the current ones don't. If you don't have this card, make sure you have the latest drivers for your card. We are still looking closely at this problem.

5) Sound Problems - We are aware of a sound crash bug - If this happens to you often, disable your sound card for the time being, but we should have this fixed really soon. This should be a pretty uncommon problem.

6) Server Stability - We are working on several bugs in this area. We've fixed many already.

Overall, we are seeing things at a relatively stable place, with a few exceptions. The login/chat server problems we had early on were expected since we didn't have the load on them to test before. We are fixing the bugs as rapidly as we can and we will continue to do so.

One last thing I want to mention is the restoration of characters. This is progressing as FAST as it possibly can. We have decided that resources are also needed to deal with tech support issues, so things are going a little slower then we would like. Please be patient.
John Smedley

EQ and Video probs - Sacrates
Wondering why your copy of EQ Phase 4 has been slapping you around harder than a constipated Ogre? Check this post from Thorim.

Many many people are asking questions about video problems.. Here are a few known problems and solutions for them.

If you are running a Voodoo 2 card and have crashed when switching to 800x600, and now crash when entering the game, delete all of your .opt and .opt1 files out of your Everquest directory. These files are your 'options' which contain your screen setting, keyboard layouts, hotkeys, etc. Deleting them will kick you back to the default 640x480 resolution and allow you to get back in the game.

For any 3dfx chipset that is crashing while running Glide drivers. Try switching to D3D drivers (DirectX) instead of Glide to see if you can get in that way.

For ALL video cards, check your driver versions against the *tested* drivers listed on the EQ web site (http://everquest.station.sony.com/beta_video.html).

For TNT cards that are crashing after server selection (or character selection) try uninstalling and reinstalling DirectX, this has fixed the crash for several people that I know of.

Hopefully this will help a few people while Verant is working on resolving the larger crash bugs.

NGO on Odium - Sacrates
Can't hold a good game down I say. Next gen slipped up the spew on this game coming from Monolith and Topware Interactive.

Adrian Chmielarz, of Polish developer Metropolis, explained: "Odium is one-third RPG, one-third strategy and one third-adventure. I don't think there's another game like this on the market. Let's say it's a mix of FFVII, Vandal Hearts, Fallout and X-Com. A new genre is being created and I think we'll see more hybrids like this in the future."

Chmielarz continued by voicing the development team's intentions for the title: "We're trying to do what Diablo did for action-RPGs. We took the best part from each genre. From adventure games, we took exploration and interaction with characters and environment, which not only puts the player into an active, role-playing part, but also enables him/her to actually influence story events. From RPGs we used the character development factor, and simplified the player's statistics, making every bit of data really vital and the data values (ie the value of accuracy) easily understandable and clearly visible during play. From the strategy aspect, we implemented battles which really make the player use his thinking cap just to stay alive and make progress."

Keep your eyes out for Odium for PC from Monolith sometime this Spring.

Global Chat in ME - Sacrates
Personally I love this shit in EQ. Hell, even though the opening chat server has been acting like a sexually deprived Nymphomaniac lately I still like that too. Here's what Steve Nichols has to say on that in Middle Earth.

This point has been debated many times already. The simple fact is that we can not stop people from using an external chat program while playing Middle-earth. We can try to do so, like EQ, but all that would do is remove the use of such programs from the hands of the normal players. Anyone with some basic programming background could modify our client to allow them to use a program like ICQ. Then what we will have is a bunch of people subverting the controls that we have in the game (you know those "hacker" types, they're always sharing their hacks). This would mean that a select few people really would have an advantage over other players. For that one reason alone, we should embrace the idea of global chat.

The argument of realism when it comes to global chat does not hold water with me. It certainly is not realistic, but that does not mean it is not a viable feature that belongs in this game. Just the fact that the entire game is populated with phantom players (players who appear and vanish at any time and have no realistic life-cycle) breaks the entire realism paradigm. The fact that you are playing with other people from around the world obviates the need for a instant messaging system. Without global chat, it would be quite annoying to keep in contact with your on-line friends. Even if you put that aside, there's still a reason for global chat. Guilds! Imagine a guild that has 500 members. How could they all get together for a guild meeting? The current technical limitations makes meeting in-game impossible (or at least unplayable). A perfect solution is an in-game chat forum for guilds that allows large, moderated, group conversations. Of course, you might think that having pre-made virtual meeting halls would be a good solution... but not really. Imagine the huge crowding issue as all 500 guild members make their way to the meeting hall. It would take hours just to get everyone into the meeting hall... not to mention the nasty lag caused by so many. Even if you put that aside, you still have the issue of new-player help. What's more friendly than having a group of 30 people in a global chat channel waiting to answer your every question about the game? It's a great way to meet people without the annoying "I'm too busy to chat." that you'll get from the standard adventurer in town. Global chat has many uses, not only to cover technological limits, but as an ease of use feature.

Global chat is not only a nice feature for players, but it is a necessary crutch to support our broken leg of technology. If we had the technology of the 24th century and could make Middle-earth holodecks then all would be perfect. Sadly, that's not the case.

One final perspective. If you really don't think that global chat is a feature that lots of players would want (if not the majority), then why did UO add it to their major product upgrade? It's not a difficult feature to have... it's one of the easiest. They left it out of the original product because of their determination to stick to ultra-realism. It's my theory that plenty of players complained about the lack of such a feature and it got added to keep the customers happy. The bottom line is that Global Chat will not ruin the game for anyone who does not use it. If you leave it turned off, you'll never be bothered by it. That is a valid compromise in my opinion.

steve
