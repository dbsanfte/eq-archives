Wizardry 8 goodies - Sacrates
Niel Yates has been missing from the Wizardry boards for a while. Hmm, those Crack habits are hard to break, I bet the rehab took away his Donnie and Marie records. Anyway, he was on the boards all cleaned up and had a few words to say. First bit is on overall development.

Couldn't have said it better! Wiz8 will be by far the best Wizardry yet.

It never ceases to amaze me - the graphics are phenomenal - reminds me of Unreal and Half-Life, but blend-in a great storyline & plot. The release may be 'far away', but that can only really mean one thing - it's a big title with an intricate storyline and complex levels - a huge programming undertaking, let alone designing/animating/level-building. I don't like to bash other people's work, but Heretic II is, in my opinion, not that great. I think by the time Wiz8 releases, most people should have the hardware to enjoy it. Take that as a subtle hint :-)

The next bit is on what programming language they are using on Wizardry.

MS Visual C++ (Part of Visual Studio 97).

RTK Review - Bowmeister
Go Lorien! Gamer's Alliance just threw up a review of the spiffy new 3D RPG Return to Krondor. It's a good review, with some fantastic screenshots. Kinda shoots down parts of the game though. Ah well, I'll give you the AW scoop on the game as soon as it hits stores here in Canada.

The story keeps you interested, even if it is fairly linear. Basically, as Jimmy the Hand you and your party are out to find a lost and ancient artifact known as the Tear of the Gods (that belongs to the Ishapian Church). Along the way you’ll run into a hulk of a man named Bear, who is also desperately seeking the Tear for an evil magician name Sidi. Your job is to grab the Tear before Bear and his pirate friends do. Along the way you’ll run into a large cast of characters with a wide range of personalities. This plot is very run-of-the-mill to me and the only thing that makes it enjoyable is the variety shown in the citizens of Krondor.

BG Thoughts - Sacrates
Looks like we are getting the whole spectrum of thoughts today. First Thief, then EQ and now Baldur's Gate. No complaint coming from me. Greg posted a ton of good stuff today including beta testers thoughts on the recent beta.

Well, I asked the beta testers to respond with their initial impressions of the game, and I only got 4 reponses (between day before Thanksgiving and now). Of course, they SHOULD all be playing 12 hours per day (hehe) so they may not even be checking emails. We just got a weekend update from Ray which we will be posting on the homepage as soon as the web team gets in... but here are the verbatim responses from the 4 beta-testers (in no order)

#1: THIS GAME ROCKS!!!! The interface is very easy to use. The character creation is a great, streamlined version of the p&p game. I've spent many hours making and remaking characters and totally loved doing it. I haven't found many bugs yet. Most of the glitches I've run into I have not been able to reproduce. The only one that has been consistant is that female Gnomes appear onscreen with male bodies complete with beard. ;^) I'm saving alot just in case, but I do that with any game. The gameplay is also very intuitive and easy to learn. The initial town with it's green garbed tutors helped alot. My biggest surprise so far has been how funny this game is. Many of the npc's have great lines. And the characters you can add to your party are hilarious. They have so much personality and style. Also, the fact that characters of opposing alignment will bicker and even fight one another is a terrific feature, too. So far, it's everything I've expected (after following the development for almost a year) and more. I'd place Baldur's Gate in my top three CRPG's of all time, and if you take away the nostalgic value of Wizardry 7 and Betrayal at Krondor, this would win. Did I mention this game ROCKS?! ;^)

#2: Greg, here is my initial reaction: AWESOME! The game is gorgeous, plain and simple. The music is beautiful and sets the mood perfectly. Game play is very intuitive and every button has roll-over help. Even without the manual most things are easy to figure out. I have not come across many bugs, mainly issues with my video card, so the game is also very stable. All in all, I am very impressed. It is definitely one of the best CRPG's I have ever played. Its actually much better than I expected. I am not a great fan of AD&D, but this is a great implementation of the rules.

#3: It's like Pringles. Once you start, you can't stop. :) One thing that surprised me is the speed of the game. I have an old Matrox Millenium One vidcard so I am unable to make use of the accelerated features of the game. However, the game still runs at a very playable rate. I can't wait to get a REAL video card and see what this puppy is like. I was also delighted when we (the beta testers) were told to submit bugs as well as SUGGESTIONS. It really shows that you guys care about our opinions. The interface is intuitive enough that no manual is necessary, yet complete enough so that I can do everything I would want to in the game. Even if I get a manual, I doubt I'll be reading it. The look and feel of the game is wonderful. The characters in the game are so lively that I never feel like I'm the only player in the game world. I even had a an internal squabble between party members once which really surprised me. It's that type of attention to detail and realism that help to immerse me into the game. The bugs I'm running into are not that bad. Most of the problems I was having were due to my video card (matrox millenium I). Very few of the bugs I've run into are technical things. Most of them have to do with characters not doing the right thing, such as saying they will take me somewhere and then not going.

#4: My initial impression is that the game is fantastic. I am amazed by the sheer beauty of the game. I also am impressed my the quick load times. I love the music. It is very easy to become quickly immersed in the game. I, also am very happy to see how easy it is to move around to the game. I, unfortunately, have found some bugs, which I hope get ironed out. I have been looking foward to this game for a long time, and I want the public perception to be that it is the best game of the year and the best RPG of all time. This is selfish on my part because I want there to be no problem in producing future sequels. Although, the game can be played without a manual (obviously, because I'm doing it), one would have been very helpful. Things such as character creation and alignment are fun to read, but not necessary to the play of the game. I have some D&D stuff anyway. However, a manuel is really needed to discuss fighting options, scripting, pausing the game to give orders, and using items, casting spells etc. I'm still not sure that I am doing things the optimum way.

FYI... none of the testers is close to finishing the game. If I hear anything from our other testers I will send out an update. I may recheck with them by the end of the week to see how things are going...

Thief thoughts - Sacrates
VN has posted one of their readers thoughts on Thief: The Dark project demo recently released. He does a good job on this mini review. Even though it's a fat download, it's well worth it in the end. For the rest of this head on over to VN and tell them Sacrates sent ya for your big swift kick in the ass.

I finished the training, and began the 1 mission available in the game. There is a 1-page description of the mission, with a recommended method to complete it. Your mission is to steal a valuable sceptre from a noble's mansion. Then, you are brought to a screen showing what items you have available for the mission, and what items are available for purchase. I am guessing that in the full game, you will have to steal money in order to buy these tools. Once you have picked your equipment, and gone over your objectives, the game loads. I won't describe the entire mission and spoil it for others, but I will tell you that getting involved in a melee struggle is almost always a bad idea, as you are a thief and not much of a fighter. The focus is more on hiding in the shadows until a guard walks by you, and then clubbing him in the head. You can douse the torches with water, making it easier to hide, clean up blood stains if you have to cut somebody, even pick up bodies and drop them in places where they are less likely to be noticed.
