ORIGIN/SYSTRAN ANNOUNCE LANGUAGE TRANSLATOR FOR ULTIMA ONLINE:THE SECOND AGE

SYSTRAN and ORIGIN Systems Combine to Introduce Translation Technology to Ultima Online Players

On-The-Fly Translator is First of Its Kind in Computer Gaming - Breaks Communication Barriers in Virtual Fantasy World of Britannia

SAN DIEGO, CA., November 9, 1998 -- SYSTRAN Software, Inc. announces that it has entered into a licensing arrangement with ORIGIN Systems(tm), and is breaking new ground in the software entertainment industry with the introduction of a unique on-the-fly translation program in ORIGIN's just released online only game, Ultima(tm) Online: The Second Age. The Second Age was developed at ORIGIN's Austin, Texas studios. ORIGIN(tm) is a wholly owned subsidiary of Electronic Arts(tm) (Nasdaq: ERTS).

SYSTRAN's translation technology, which comes installed with The Second Age, can automatically translate many different natural languages, effectively eliminating language barriers in worldwide online gaming. It takes into account the grammatical structure of each language and uses rules to transfer the grammatical structure of the source language (text to be translated) into the target language (translated text).

Four language pairs are included in The Second Age / SYSTRAN translator: English to German, German to English, English to Japanese and Japanese to English. Other language pairs are supported by the program and will be available for download soon from The Second Age web site (http://www.owo.com/t2a).

"This partnership proves that the need for translation runs deep and is becoming more and more important as millions of people use the web for communication," said Laurent Pontegnier, SYSTRAN's director of web business development. "SYSTRAN's translation engine can successfully integrate into many different applications and uses."

"We're extremely pleased to have the SYSTRAN system in Ultima Online: The Second Age," said Jeff Anderson, Ultima Online (UO) executive producer. "The Ultima Online community spans the globe with UO players in each of the world's time zones. With the help of the SYSTRAN translator, we can start reaching beyond language barriers and bring that community even closer together."

Worlds of Fantasy still alive - Bowmeister
Lorien is the man...seems that the rumors of Games Galore stopping development on their 3D RPG Worlds of Fantasy were a bit premature (I hear there's a cream you can get for that though). Here's the scoop from Jason Bernard, Lead Games Designer.

I would like to clarify the recent announcement of the "cancellation" of Worlds of Fantasy. Worlds of Fantasy has not been cancelled. We have suspended active development of the game for a short period of time. What this means is that we aren't writing any new code for the time being. The reason for this is that it became apparent to us that our current development path was not going to meet with success. We are pursuing some very promising opportunities to get us back on track.

As I have stated before, Worlds of Fantasy is a great role playing system. The paper and pen version of it has been played many times and was very much enjoyed. I am very confident that a computer version of Worlds of Fantasy will be equally as much fun.

I apologise if my original message on the official Worlds of Fantasy page was somewhat misleading. Also, I have reopened our bulletin board where I welcome your questions and comments.

Tag-team action on RTK boards - Sacrates
Raymond E Feist was back on the Return to Krondor boards and Sinjin came along for the ride too. It's really good to see the Author getting into this game and seem so knowledgable about it at the same time. Anyway here is Mr Feist talking about Puzzles and traps oh my.

There are no word-lock chests. I never liked the concept much and found them very intrusive to the "literary" sense of the game.

But there are puzzles. There are no puzzles that you can't find an alternative solution for, i.e. the thing behind the puzzle is also located somewhere else, or you can get to it through another set of rooms, or if you kill enough things, it opens anyway, etc.

Chests are all trapped/locked, and part of the deal is that as James gets better at picking locks/disarming traps, they get easier to open. You have a choice of either using a "twitch" reflex (you have to click on a swinging pointer as it comes between two points) or you can use a dice roll. For those who are "twitched-challenged" you're going to want to build up James's theif skills a lot, else carry a LOT of healing potion.

And here is Sinjin coming in with the killing blow to top off what Mr Feist said.

What Ray was referring to was an alternative method to lockpicking, it is not the primary method. The lock picking is a skill based system for the player and presents an opportunity for the player to learn the trap system over time, building a history. For those who, as Ray says, challenged in this area we do have an alternative method that is based on a success percentage based on successful previous experience, not a pure "dice roll."
