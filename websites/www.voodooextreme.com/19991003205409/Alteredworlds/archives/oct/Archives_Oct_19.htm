More BG Gravy - Sacrates
Scott Greig (Who else?) was spreading his scent over on the Developers corner again. That's quite a scent he's built up there too?! He's a goddamn Skunk if you ask me. Read on.

On The Story Holding Up: From all accounts, it held up pretty good. There were a few spots where the player was sort of left dangling, but that's why we playtest this thing from beginning to end. James and Co. are busy adding more clues and hints and moving important characters so that they are easier to find. We also have a couple of people, who haven't read anything about the story, working their way through the game. They will be giving us the final feedback on the story cohesion.

What Ben and Ross are up to: You might be wondering what Ben and Ross are doing now that they have finished playing through BG? They are playing through again multiplayer!!

On Sound Effects: We are finally getting around to the last data entry tasks. Don is currently doing a sweep through all of the areas and is placing the ambient sounds.

Wow, what a difference this makes. It literally makes the areas come alive. I have honestly have never seen (or should that be heard) a game that has quite this many sounds in it. There are over a hundred sounds just for the spell effects alone, and each major type of monster has it's own walking sounds. If you hear a deep, slow thumping sound, run.

Trust me on this. I think it's going to top off around 1400 distinct sounds, not including the voice over stuff.

One thing to note if you have 32 megs or more of RAM, then you will get longer, more detailed versions on the ambient sounds. It has been been a constant balancing act between the need for a 16 meg version and the sound team's desire for a richer sound bed. Even with the sound compression techniques that we use, we could only fit so much into memory. The solution: we made two versions of the really long sounds. If you have 16 megs, then you will get a good ambient background with lots of localized sounds. If you have 32 megs, you will get truely amazing ambient sounds with extra localized sounds like music outside the taverns and echoing water sounds in the underground lake chamber. Oops, there is no underground lake, honest. :)

Sooooooo, I would recommend that if you only have 16 megs of ram, you should consider upgrading for the sound effects alone.

ME Beef - Sacrates
Here is a little Middle Earth Biscuit without gravy that you'll want to skinny up to. It was posted by Stephen Nichols and Jay Esparza. I noticed this from the guys full of marshemellowy goodness at VN. Check it.

on ME's current status: While I can't comment on specifics, I can say that it is still relatively early. We have the majority of the framework for the game built and are nearing the point where the real meat can be added (you know, combat systems and the like). As you know, our planned beta is this time next year, and we won't ship the product until it's been in beta at least six months.

on camera views: ...you are able to control the camera as well so that you can get a good point of view in all cases.

on interacting with others: We have two major goals here... to create a world as close to Tolkien's works as possible and to make that world fun and easy to use. Like it or not, global chat serves the second purpose nicely. I agree with the realism argument. Global chat is not realistic. However, you don't have to use it if you don't want to. We're also providing an in-game mail system that allows you to send mail messages instantly to any other player on your server. That's not realistic either, but it's a necessary feature in my opinion. Sure, people can just use ICQ and their standard internet mail. But if players have to rely on outside sources to accomplish things in our game, we've failed on ease of use. If you don't want to partake in global chat, don't. It won't affect your play in any way.

System Shock juice - Sacrates
Rob Fermier was over on Usenet once again spewing out more System Shock goodness. Keep it comin Rob.

on the difficulty getting started in System Shock 1: Glad to hear that you enjoyed it. You are dead on the money that it limited its own success by being rather impenetrable to the casual user. I like to think we've learned our lesson and that Shock 2 will have an easier learning curve withough losing any of the powerful expression of the interface.

on the interface: Currently leaning is only handled through the keyboard, and while you will certainly be able to remap what keys control it, there aren't any plans for having leaning on the mouse. The same holds for crouching. A lean / crouch analog mode is an interesting one, we'll have to toss it around the team. As for the interface proper, mouselook is definitely supported, and is a major part of the default interface mode.

However, there is no need for circle-strafing and similar shooter techniques; it's there because we on the team found it a great way to wander around and really take in the "3d ness" of an area. The vibe of combat is definitely paced like the original -- deciding what to use on what monster, juggling ammo types and energy is a vital part of the game. Of course, in System Shock 2 you have even more axes to tweak around if you want --weapons settings, psi powers, and cyber implants all are part of the picture as well!

Although we haven't finalized all the parameters of what will be customizable to what, those of you who prefer the original System Shock interface should be able to customize back pretty closely to that. The one big thing that we aren't intending to support is the different movement cursors, and moving based on clicking on the screen. Movement is primarily handled through keyboard (defaulting to the WADX cluster), and we may support a "move forward" type interface in mouselook as well (no decision has been made yet).

on the official site: The "official" Shock 2 website should be going up sometime next week, barring unforseen problems. If you want a little tidbit in the meantime to tide you over, check out www.irrational-games.com, which has some new content on it's Shock 2 page.

Fallout 2 lovin - Sacrates
Was just over at VN and found some happy smack in the form of a Fallout 2 devleopment update. Feargus Erquhart was on the boards spittin out this goodness. Read on.

Sorry about not having an update for the past few days. I've been sick and we've been trying to get the game done. Also, our internet provider got their pipe to the internet cut in half by a Back-Ho (however that is spelled) yesterday morning.

Anyway, a quick update is that all of the quests are in and working. We are going through all the end game stuff to make sure that is working and doing some last minute tweaking.

We just got the audio for the new intro movie ending that we decided to do about Thursday night. The Intro movie was cool, but we just felt that the last part of it wasn't in line with the rest of it - so it was changed. One of the Stonekeep 2 artists jumped in and helped fix it Thursday night. It went off to the audio guy for sound effects on Friday, the sound effects came back (through a different internet provider) on Saturday and our Audio Director worked on the movie and got the soundtrack done at 2:49pm today.

Other than that we are balancing and making sure the car shows up where it is supposed to. There is a big problem with the car because it has to keep inventory and change maps - something the engine doesn't like to do except for the player and partymembers. So in a moment of genius we made the trunk a partymember - but that cause a crapload of problems.

We've fixed some crash problems with some of the party members, and the game is VERY stable right now.

We also modified some of the to-hit stuff in the past week, so you'll all have to improve your Small Guns a little more than the 120 to get all the Eye and Head criticals you got in Fallout 1.

And the programming team in a fit of hyper-programming fixed about 30 medium/major bugs in the past few days. They are pretty much working on little stuff right now.

All and all the team is really kicking ass, and getting a lot of stuff done. The game is looking really good and we've polished off most of the bugs. We don't have the bug count of Baldur's Gate, but of the over 3700 bugs found on the bug list we should have less than 50 still in the game.

That's about it, any questions just ask
