Deus Ex impressions  - Certis   07/03/00

I've been meaning to whip up some impressions of Deus Ex for a while now but every spare moment of my time has been spent playing the game instead. Deus Ex is similar to System Shock 2 in the sense that you can put skill points onto different stats and decide how you want to play the game. You can be the tank who rushes in guns blazing (provided you have enough skill with various weaponry). You can play a more thief-like character picking locks, disabling security systems and skulking in the shadows. I go for a mixed approach depending on the situations and how I feel they should be dealt with. Therein lies the beauty of Deus Ex, there is almost always at least two different ways to handle any given situation. 

 

For example: you see a locked door. You can either find the key, hack a security system and see if there is an open switch there,  blow down the door with a rocket launcher or pick the lock. This design is not restricted to just doors, it applies to almost anything. You can sneak around enemies, snipe them, shock prod them or just blow them apart with a shotgun. This is no linear Half-Life where you kill kill kill and follow a set path.  

 

The plot is nothing too fancy but it is enough to keep you interested as you move through the levels. I won't give anything away but it centers around various conspiracy theories in the future. 

 

Before I talk about the graphics, let me lay out my specs for you: 

 

Celeron 500mhz

32 meg Diamond Viper TNT2

SBLive sound card
8 meg Voodoo 2

128 megs RAM 

 

Deus Ex uses the Unreal engine with good results. Certainly not great but good enough to pull you into the world. There are some very open areas, reflective floors, particle effects and anything you would expect to come with the Unreal engine. I initially tried to play Deus with my TNT2 card and it ran like a monkey studying a math problem. Until they patch this game up to Unreal Tournament D3D speed you should use 3dfx or don't play the game. With my Voodoo 2 I can play at 800X600 with details maxed out and get a smooth frame rate. With my TNT2 I had all the features down to nothing at 800 X 600 and still got a horrible frame rate. 

 

If you like a plot-based thinking man's shooter I highly recommend Deus Ex. It is a rarity for me to stay interested enough to finish a game at all these days. After about 20 hours of play time I'm still loving Deus Ex and will probably want more by the time I'm done. Congratulations to Harvey and the rest of the gang over at Ion Storm, you've made one hell of a game!
