BG mini reviews - Sacrates
Greg Peterson can't hold himself back from posting all these good comments on the recent Baldur's Gate beta test they had in CA. Here are a few more tasty morsels for you to read up on.

I have only heard back from two other beta-testers... I am going to resend my note this week and hope to get a better response rate. Here are two more comments...

#1 Just a few words on the game. I thought the game was well developed and the bugs, not too bad. I’m only on chapter 2 right now cause I was gone for the holidays. I have sent 2 pages of bugs and suggestions already. If you would like to have a copy of those pages let me know. Also I think the game is very fun.

#2 Well, I must say I was expecting a lot, with all the hype, and the Interplay and the BioWare team really impressed me beyond my expectations. Believe me, this isn't vaporware (I don't think vaporware takes up 2.5GB of my HD). The interface is very user-friendly (we didn't receive any sort of guide or overview manual so all of us had to figure everything out ourselves, and trust me, it wasn't difficult at all). I love how the full information on spells and items is just a right-click over the icon, which also includes a history of the item, if it has one (usually most of the magical weapons and armor do). The NPCs are well-written and I was surprised that the characters squabbled or complimented each other the way they did, which sometimes lead them to attacking or thanking each other. The artwork, and not just the background art, is wonderfully done. As for bugs, not many. Most of my bug reports have been for mispelling that I caught and sometimes tweaky grammar, very few game ruining and pretty much only one crashed the game on me (and it happens rarely). I think I enjoy single-player much more then I do multiplayer... I find multiplayer a tad slow for my liking. I thought that since AD&D is pretty much turn-based and BG is real-time, that the whole game would be really strange to play, but I found that it was quite well done. Haven't been getting much sleep at all lately, which stinks since I work early in the morning at my other job. And yes, I have to agree with what one of the other testers have already stated, it was great to hear that you wanted our suggestions too, even though we pretty much aren't involved in the project at all. It just shows that you guys care and want to release a quality product with BG. It's an honest pleasure to "be a part of the team", even if it's just for a little while, to do what we can to help make BG great. Many thank yous for the opportunity and kudos go to everyone involved with the project.

ME newsbrief - Sacrates
It's always happy time in Sacrates land when more ME goodness comes along. There is a lot of controversy right now over the permanent death system in ME. Personally I think it's the shit, but many others don't. Here is a post on killing other players under this situation.

Allow me to clarify the exact circumstances of when you can kill anoher player:

1) If a player is a murderer you can attack and kill them without ramifications. This is, of course, assuming that you are in a geographic area where the player is branded a killer.

2) If a player attacks you, you can kill them in self defense without ramifications whether they are a murderer or not.

3) You can only take the life of an unconscious body that is not guarded. I should have made this more clear before. Our combat system will allow you to guard both conscious and unconscious people. If someone is guarding an unconscious person then there is a pretty good chance that you can not kill that person. By pretty good, I mean that it is still possible. You can only guard against so many players, making it quite possible to be overrun by superior numbers.

Now, I'm very much aware of the pack mentality of PKers. A well run pack of PKers may well be able to kill some players before being dealt with. I'm counting on this to be a pretty rare event. With the time that has to be invested in your character before you can even partake in murder combined with the potentially dire consequences for such actions I'm confident that most KewlD00ds will find the enjoyment lost. They key to this system working is making the benefits of player-killing miniscule in comparison to playing the game as it's meant. Our current approach handles that goal.

Here's another interesting post on why they opted to go with the Isometric view rather than the 1st person.

The reason we chose to develop the engine to disallow a free floating camera is a matter of performance. Simply put, we can take lots of shortcuts in our engine if we avoid the evils of a free floating camera. These shortcuts allow us to have much higher polygon counts than our competitors. Higher polygon counts mean more realistic looking artwork. Not only that, but since our world is partitioned into to large regions it would be quite bizarre in a Tomb Raider like view when you came across the region boundaries. Speed is of the essence. We need to have large amounts of trees actually crowding the screen in many places. Do do that we need to take lots of shortcuts. ;)

Dues Ex goods - Sacrates
VUP has the goods on the upcoming RPG Dues Ex that you'll want to see. A nice fat interview with (Bionic) Bob White, formerly with the U:A development team.

6. Rumour has it that the attention to detail present in Deus Ex is taken to new extremes, with the Hong Kong levels proudly touting rats and litter, all enhancing the 'atmosphere', to what extent will such perfectionism affect the player, and what other delightful little touches will be present?

Once again, the idea is to immerse the player into as real an environment as possible so that things they would expect to happen in the real world, will happen here. If you throw a chair through a window in our game it will probably break the glass and if it falls to the street below, the chair might break and maybe it won't (Better chance of breaking the higher up you are). If you shoot into a crowd, the people will scatter and run, just like they would in the real world. There are many more examples, but that is enough for now.

Looking Glass' Thief: The Dark Project Sneaks Into Stores

Highly Anticipated Game Delivers Stealthy New Concepts in Gameplay

Cambridge, MA, December 1, 1998 - Looking Glass Studios, a leading entertainment software developer, announced today that its first-person 3D action/adventure game Thief: The Dark Project has shipped to retailers. Published by Eidos Interactive, Thief breaks new ground in gameplay, favoring cunning and deception over an itchy trigger finger. Thief is now available for a suggested retail price of $49.95.

"We believe that gamers will be looking for something fresh this Holiday season and the stealth-based gameplay of Thief: The Dark Project offers exciting new challenges in the first-person action/adventure genre," said Paul Neurath, managing director of Looking Glass Studios. "Now that Thief is shipping, gamers can experience for themselves the thrills and suspense of playing a master thief. Of course, we would encourage players to buy the game, not steal it."

Thief: The Dark Project is a first-person 3D action/adventure game where players assume the role of a master thief relying on stealth and cunning. Utilizing the revolutionary new Dark Engine, Thief: The Dark Project transports players into a mysterious and immersive techno-fantasy world of intrigue, deception and combat.

In Thief: The Dark Project, players embark on a series of adventures that are woven into a compelling story of seduction, betrayal, and ultimate revenge. In order to survive, gamers must use sly, evasive maneuvers to progress through a world where the shadows are your only allies, trust is not an option, and confrontation usually results in death.

Noticed over on the best host site in the world, VoodooExtreme (ya ya, I know...just trying to butter up Billy...ugh, bad metaphor :)) that Gamespot UK has posted a cool little Baldur's Gate package, complete with an interview with Dr. Ray! No screenshots though :(. Check it out here.

Gregory Zeschuk, Ray Muzyka and Augustine Yip met at medical college, where they were developing educational software for the medical faculty at the University of Alberta. It was there that they discovered a mutual love of games, and began working on a project together. In 1995 they met up with a team of programmers and started work on Shattered Steel, which was published by Interplay.

Now they are working on the ultra-ambitious Baldur's Gate, which is looking more special with each passing day. Amazingly, Doctors Zeschuk and Muzyka still practise medicine two days a week, and spend the rest of the week at BioWare supervising the development of the game.

Feargus on Fallout - Bowmeister
Oops...missed this one yesterday :). Feargus was on the official Fallout2 site again, trying to put our fears of another patch of death to rest.

Well I hope everyone had a great Thanksgiving - a bunch of us actually took a whole day off. I haven't whined in an update for a while, so I need to give Desslock something to edit out. :)

Anyway we continue to fix things for the patch like disappearing Power Armor that is being worn by Party Members when they go up levels, and what seems to be the last problems with the Car that shows up on the Raiders Map. There are other things that I have gotten from people that we are fixing as well.

Our QA department continues to test the game, and we are fixing things as they find them. They are all a little gun shy right now about missing things, so they are being very very careful.

We should have the Final Patch through QA sometime between 12/4 and 12/11. That final date is in case something horrible happens, but at this point I don't think it will have to get that long. We are still working on the utility to bring your character over from a Save Game. That will be ready the same time as the Final Patch.

I have gotten a lot of E-mail from people worried that Fallout 3 will be a First Person Shooter (FPS) because of the statement that it is going to use a 3D engine. So everyone knows, it will not be a FPS, but it is possible that it will use a 3D engine - think Myth. Having said that, Fallout 3 has not been launched as an official project yet - we are just throwing ideas around.

Finally, thanks for all the E-mail form people and for the guys up on ICQ on Saturday that helped with a few bugs.

-Feargus (furquhart@interplay.com)
