U:A and sound - Sacrates
Audball is the man to talk to at Origin when it comes down to sound in U:A. He was just on the Message Boards and had a few goodies that get me and Bowmeister excited since we both have the SB Live beast with EAX. Check it.

At this stage in development, it would NOT be easy to add full WaveTracing support to *this* game. (Consider editor changes, in-game changes, database changes, potential modeller plugins/exporters to facilitate surface tagging, etc.). Simply passing a set of geometry to the Vortex2 processor is probably indeed simple, but that alone does not give you full immersion. Geometry itself isn't enough; surface properties must also be taken into account, and I see that as the bigger hurdle. (There are obviously other issues, such as how our world geometry is divided / organized / loaded, and how that affects downloading to the audio processor, which affects framerate, etc., etc.)

The decision to implement EAX was made before the A3D 2.0 SDK was out of beta, and our production schedule reflects this. EAX may not provide as mathematically "correct" a representation as WaveTracing, but it can be tailored to give good effects (we'll have to wait and see how EAX 2.0 does things). If we have time to revisit the issue before we ship, who knows, maybe we'll put support in.

As always, "Nothing's final 'til it ships."

ME newsbrief - Sacrates
Stephen Nichols was on the Middle Earth message boards and talked a bit about having Mule characters and how many characters per server you'll be able to get. Scope this out.

We will support four characters per account in Middle-earth. This means that you can have four characters per server. We will most probably place some kind of restrictions about switching between characters on the same server. We won't be able to eliminate it, but we can put some kind of time restriction on it. I'm currently thinking that we should limit how quickly you can go back to a character you have already played within a certain time frame. Simply allowing one character per server is a bit overkill. There are too many families that like to play these games together. While together does not mean simultaneously in this instance, it is still an important concept. Having three brothers who play at separate times but are working toward the same goals is a good thing. We definately need to keep an eye on people who like to abuse the multiple characters, but we also need to cater to families that like to play "together".

U:A Camera - Sacrates
Have you been wondering about how the Camera angles will be in Ultima Ascension? Many others have and Lady Moi was there to dish out the Sauce on the deal.

The camera's pitch can be locked, or zoomed in and out, but the Avatar will alays face forward and moving the mouse left and right will cause the surrounding to rotate. The Avatar will have some head movement and body movement as you move the camera, but for all intents and purposes, saying he is facing forward during normal movement is pretty accurate.

Even more BG - Sacrates
Ahem, even more BG bits are flooding in faster than the Skipper can eat a full Cow. Noticed over at BG Chronicles that Luke was Interviewed by The Game Gurus. Check this little nasty copy and paste that was up.

We are definitely looking forward to BG2. We'll be tightening up the basic classes and fixing the odd thing here and there. There may be a few additions to the class choices, but the basics will still come first. I really want to push for more class-based plots that evolve the player character. Things like acquiring followers, attaining status and property, and generally doing something for each class, based on the changes AD&D characters undergo around 10th level and up. They really need a separate game to get the proper treatment. Freaky-powerful creatures will be great too. It is called "Dungeons and DRAGONS" after all, and when we do them, we'll do them right. There are things creepier than the big ol' lizards too, but we'll keep them for a surprise.

Chat Log available - Sacrates
Yup, you can grab that Mofo for the Baldur's Gate chat the other night right here. Also Luke was on the boards and had a few more things to say on questions etc. First one is on Kits.

Possibly, though the basic classes will alway take priority. There's a lot we can add to higher level classes. Kits will be a consideration though.

Next he jumps on Editors.

Editors are not in the cards for BG. We've heard the demand though, and will be looking into the area soon enough. We don't do anything halfway and everyday it gets more "solid". There is a difference between playable and bugfree. Keep your shorts on, it'll come and it'll be the quality we demand.

And finally on the Expansions and rasing the XP Cap.

The expansions will raise the cap, though probably not more than a level's worth. One more level from 7 to 8 or 8 to 9 is more than all the experience in the game already. [The current cap of 89,000 XP can be reached] after about 140-160 hours of play.

Bundle of BG Joy - Sacrates
Alright now that the Pussy, er...Cat is out of the Bag on the release date, we're getting a few extra goodies coming our way from the message boards etc. First one is Don talking about the Release Date.

Ok, we know all of you are anxious and chomping at the bit for BG to be released. Believe me we understand this as we are all game players. We have worked on this game for a long time now and want it out just as much as the rest of you. If we had believed this game would not come out this fall, I think the decision would have been been made to officially push the release date to next year. It's not anyone's intention to string along the public. We are real close to shipping, for the last six months we have felt close. As more time passes the closer you feel. Even I feel this way, and I am known as one of the pessimistic people in the office. When asked what how long something is going to take my estimats are usually double everyone elses. Unfortunatly, it's real hard to get a bead on development time , especially when it involves fixing bugs. With the regular development cycle at least you know roughly where you are by how much is not done. But when bug testing, the amount of new bugs increases for a while over time as more are found. It's real hard to magically pull a date out of a hat when you don't know the future. If I was in charge of choosing and posting a release date and I had said October, then November 15, then December 1, etc., a lot more harm would be done then good. Just think how many upset posts we have seen about the changes in release dates the game has already had. Please bear with us just a little longer, we really do appreciate your support, in fact some us are floored. This is the first game I have worked on and I am still not used to it. We do notice the messages posted whether negative or positive, even though we don't have time to reply to them all. Yes, this message I am posting may be 'same old, same old' but the only real news I think any of us want to give you is that the game has went Gold.

We don't want to release the game with known bugs but its also naive to think that the game will ship without any unknown bugs. So, of course, if we have to we are prepared to patch the game. We sure hope we can prevent that though. I understand what you mean by overkill though, but what is overkill and what is not is very subjective. But we are not releasing a mission critical piece of software here. If we were, we would be doing another 2 years of testing. It's real hard, on one hand you hear people that wish the game was released in beta form and others that complain if they find one minor bug.

Then a bit on a few people not liking the chat. WTF? Hell, people they had a F'ing chat for gods sake! It never ceases to amaze me how incredibly immature the gaming community can be sometimes.

I really shouldn't be posting to this as I don't want to be involved a flame war. I wasn't able to join the chat, but I am surprised that people think it was a joke and just a media event put on by people who don't care. Those involved from Bioware and Interplay were taking time out of their busy schedules to try to help answer your questions. No one ever said the chat was about the release date, it was a last opportunity for people to ask questions to the developers. If the game had been sent to the manufacturer tonight, we would have told you so, anything else is just speculation. We are trying to develop the game that you want to play and we would want to play. Yes, there are actually software companies in the world that care about their products. Most developers don't have an active presence on the internet because it is too time consuming and when it leads to flaming and unconstructive feedback it becomes counterproductive. They end up wasting their time defending themselves and not doing their jobs and getting the products you want done. This is why I am submitting only one post on this issue. I probably shouldn't post at all because someone will take it the wrong way and hate me for it. Clamming up would be the easy and safe thing to do. I am just hoping that some of you who feel burnt will have a change of heart.

Not exactly enlightening news for those waiting to get on Phase 4 of the beta. I suspect they won't start it until around Early January with the March release date sounding very possible now. This is only a guess from my part, but when have my guesses been wrong? If i'm wrong on this one i'll gladly make all updates standing on my head for an entire day Without the help of an Intern!

We having been conducting the third phase of beta testing for about a month now. So far everything is running smoothly and on-track. However, we have not decided whether or not phase 4 will be beginning before or after Christmas. The programming team has been working diligently, and we would like to have phase 3 as bug-free as possible before phase 4 begins. Our goal is to please our testers, and ultimately the consumers after the game is released. We appriciate everyone's enthusiasm for EverQuest, and will keep you informed on the starting date for Phase 4 testing.
