More BG Bits - Bowmeister
And still more Baldur's Gate lovin flowing over on the Developer's Corner message board. If you are like 90% of the role playing population, and pre-ordered BG, you'll want to check out this update from Greg Peterson...

Let me first say that this is what is GOING to happen... it will take about a week for Digital River to get all of this in the system correctly, but this is what is GOING to take place.

1) For all Baldur's Gate pre-orders, you will have the option to decide if you want the Volo's Guide or not.

2) We originally designed the program assuming that the game and the Volo's Guide would weigh under two pounds. As we have gotten closer to finishing the game we have found out that the game and guide together weigh MORE than that... and that adding the guide to certain international orders would push shipping costs up (in some cases by as much as $60). Therefore we will be unable to offer the Volo's Guide with FREE SHIPPING to every place in the world.

3) All Volo's Guides are Free... we are not charging anything for the guide itself.

4) Shipping will be charged based on final destination and shipping instructions.

4) To cover EXTRA shipping costs for the guides, we will offer up to $5 credit to cover any additional costs driven by the weight of the Volo's Guide. What this means:

Domestic UPS > normally $2 more - so we cover ALL shipping for Volo's Guide.
Domestic FedEx > normally $5 more - so we cover ALL shipping for Volo's Guide.
Canada UPS > normally $5 more - so we cover ALL shipping on Volo's Guide.
These three options cover in excess of 90% of all of the pre-orders.
All other orders are FedEx and the upcharge will be MORE than $5... you will need to check your shipping and decide if you still want the Guide... for example, the upcharge for Alaska and Hawaii would be $15... less the $5 credit would mean that you would still have to pay $10 extra shipping for the guide. You may decide to opt out of the program (or not).

5) Before we ship, we will have ALL orders corrected in the system. We will assume that ALL domestic orders and Canadian UPS orders will want the Volo's guide (since the guide is free and there are no added shipping charges). We will check the online site to confirm Volo's Guides in all other orders. I will let people know when all of the orders are corrected so you can check your order status.

6) You can cancel/change your order at any time (up until ship date) without penalty. We will give everyone in the WORLD notification when the product has silvered... that will be your notice that you have about 5 working days before your order becomes final.

In terms of the priority shipping stuff... just to confirm: the first production units off our production line will be express shipped to Digital River, who will pack all orders and ship all orders out within 24 hours. With the fastest shipping options, we are targeting to deliver product about the same time that it first shows up in the first, fastest stores.

All of this information will be copied to our web site and the Interplay store, as soon as people come back from Thanksgiving vacation.

BG Bits - Bowmeister
Another day, another flood of info on the Developer's Corner message boards. First and foremost is Greg Peterson's response to the wingnut that freaked out saying the .wav files and other info on Baldur's Gate Chronicles was going to ruin the game for him.

Rather than commenting on the BG Chronicles issue specifically, I just want to comment on pre-release infobits and the challenges.

We want to keep people up-dated on the development of the game. We want people to get a feel for how cool this game is going to be. It is impossible to do that without releasing SOME materials to the public. Arguably, this post could have been about screen shots, or equipment icons, or character portraits, or ANYTHING that is going to be in the final game because they all do, at some level, reveal material that would otherwise be a 'surprise' the first day you open the box. It is a constant challenge for us, and one that we take very seriously.

We try NOT to release major spoilers. This includes in the game manual, with our fan sites, with the commercial online sites, and with magazines. Even the Strategy Guide does NOT include everything in the game. Once you play the game, you will see how very little we have actually revealed. We want people to play the game and to 'discover' it first-hand.

So we have a problem - to some people, a sound file comparison is a betrayal of confidence, while to others it is an interesting update in game development, while to others it is not all that interesting (sorry to say I fall into that last group...). So we try to balance ALWAYS the needs of different groups. (And no, I did NOT give Silverdawn the sound info).

I hope that people understand that we are not just randomly tossing stuff out for public consumption. If you are cruising a fan site, or happen to be checking out a preview, and you see that something is available that you think might spoil your game experience, just avoid it. But at least rest easy that if we have released the information, we have done so only after we have evaluated it and felt confident that knowing the information will NOT diminish your ultimate game experience.

Then Luke showed up with some info on combat and the auto-pause feature...

The auto-pause at the end of a character's round only occurs when that character is actively in combat (trying to attack, and thus operating within the 6-second rounds. The rounds are not universally tracked from game beginning or anything, they activate on an individual basis for characters actively in range of hostiles). If you are not engaged in combat, you are not in a round and walk freely (though the screen automatically centers on the character that causes the auto-pause (in single player), so trying to do something else while other characters are in auto-pause combat would be a big headache. You don't want to be disregarding combat like this anyway).

I myself only have the auto-pause activate when a weapon becomes unusable (breaks, out of ammo). Not having the "pause on sight of hostile" means that I am surprised occasionally, but it's more fun that way (more akin to true AD&D. I also play with the character's green "circles" turned off. It just looks better). This is definitely not to say I do not pause (I do, VERY often), but certainly not every round for every player. That gets tedious very very fast. It is an approximation of turn-based by customer demand, but it is not meant to be an exact equivalent. BG just doesn't operate that way, but IMHO even the most ardent turn-based fan will like being able to take their turn whenever they wish, and not having to sit through multiple-enemy maneuvers is really nice as well.

I want to address something else said in this thread as well. Combat is NOT scripted such that the scripts do everything for you. They can do quite a lot, but if you do not take control of the characters yourself in all but the smallest encounters you are in big trouble. The scripts are great emergency measures, but they will not play the game for you.

Take that mage and direct his spells exactly where you wish, position your fighters as blocking frontlines, and sneak your thieves around the perimeter. The scripts will tell your wizard to cast, your fighter to attack, and your thief to backstab, but nowhere near as effectively as if they are actively directed and specific actions are chosen.

And finally a little bit from Ben (woohoo he's alive!) on thieves...

The base scrore is based off your DEX and racial bonuses. THEN you get points that you can spend wherever you want. You get points everytime you go up levels as per AD&D rules. So yes you can specialize in stealth and pick locks (my favorite two :))

Looks like someone was on the EQ Boards dissing on EQ. Although if that were me, I would have taken this guys jewels and chewed on them until he screamed mercy for attacking EQ in such a way. Has anyone tried Electroshock therapy to the Testicles before? Ok here's my thoughts on Everquest. i am open to any discussions. here goes.

Everquest is nowhere near a new UO. It is a glorified diablo/descent to undermountain/AD&D. Unlike in UO, you have no chance to earn a living in EQ. what does this mean? No profession skills. Just adventuring!

While the focus is indeed on adventuring and questing, there are many trade skills as well.

Sure, it will be popular for...oh...3 months. But after that time people will get bored after they have super characters that can kill all the monsters with two hits and have their pk switch off so the only thing they CAN do is kill those monsters.

The game is designed to take at least 1 year to max out a character, but that said, you can change to +pvp at any time.

Second major point: Lag. If you think UO has lag, wait till you get your copy of EQ. UO: cartoonish graphics with thousands of users...major lag. EQ: Quake II'ish graphics with the same number of users...you get the picture.

3d graphics do NOT mean more lag -- there's no more information required to be sent across the net with a 3d game than a 2d.

and my last point: guilds. ok i know this seems odd, but it isn't. With the 1000 player max on any server, it will become increasingly harder to establish a guild. You'll have members getting a "Server is full" message right before a guild war or something

Our target capacity is between 1000 and 1500, but who ever said there would be 'server is full' messages? Not me.

that's my thoughts on the matter. Please post yours.

There is a very detailed FAQ on www.everquest.com, yet I still see lots of misinformation around :(

-Brad

RTK quick look - Sacrates
Well now, here is something that gets my Willy Wonka workin in overdrive. John Hoffman had a few words to make on the release of RTK. Basically this is Joe Schmoe giving his heartfelt. But he does it fairly eloquently, and I am looking forward to this beasty myself. For the full meaty goodness head over to VN and read the rest.

Well, I've only been playing for a couple of hours, but I do have some strong impressions.

This game has all the RPG tappings, stats, skills, exploration, and a great story. However, it feels a bit like an adventure game at this point. I find myself waving my mouse back and forth looking for the hot spot that I can interact with. Many doors, and objects are just gloss without the ability to interact with them at all. Not even a this door is locked style message. Your character also moves from screen to screen, kinda like the older kings quest games. There are however other characters moving about that you can sometimes interact with.

The instruction book tries to sell this as a series of camara views like in a movie. I don't buy it. If the game moved like Redguard I would be ecstatic. Anyway, think movement is like kings quest with no screen scrolling. You walk near the edge of the screen or closer to a side of the street and the screen switches to a different view sometimes closer, sometime from above, sometimes from behind and sometimes from the front. It makes it very easy to get confused as to which way you were going.

Official WoT update - Sacrates
This one's coming in a little late. Too many late night drug parties and strippers for those guys over at Legend. Glen finally got around to making his weekly Development update so be sure to check this silly bastard out.

Welcome back.  I'm not planning to write a whole lot today, but I do want to give you something to tide you over.  Just yesterday, I received a shiny gold CD of the new music by Robert Berry and Leif Sorbye.   The pieces cover some of the darker environments such as Shadar Logoth and the Ways, but I thought I'd share one with you based on the White Tower.  Remember that the Berry/Sorbye team is producing the in-game music (with possible additional music from Eric Herberling), while Andy Frazier is producing the cut scene score.  Grab yourself an MP3 player and listen to this 4 meg beauty (copyright Legend Entertainment blah blah blah).  I don't have to tell you that Robert and Leif are doing an excellent job; I think the music speaks for itself.

Everything is running in high gear, and people are doing great work.   We've almost put together all of the necessary Aes Sedai primitive architecture required for the upcoming milestone; now all they need is more textures, and the artists (Paul, Julie, and Marc) are cranking those out too.  James is making some wonderfully curved exteriors, while being beautiful, are still speedy.  Scott has started on the Ways world map.  Now that Aaron has Machin Shin running, I can tell you that the experience is going to be absolutely terrifying.  I've seen Machin Shin coming for me now.  All that remains are the sounds.  In multiplayer, this dynamic is going to be extremely cool (picture using Swap Places right before being consumed by the Black Wind; get the idea?).  Warren's hard at work on the Portal Stone world level, along with the primitives he's built.   The design is very cool--I can't wait to see it realized.  Tim has created a wonderful Shadar Logoth map and is working on the sequel as we speak.

Speaking of Shadar Logoth, Aaron now has Mashadar up and running (or flowing).   I'm going to be intentionally vague about how he works, because he's extremely cool and I want there to be something left for the release of the game that you don't know about.  He has also continued to bang out the artifacts and has most of them working up to spec.

With Kathleen, I'm trying to wrestle the cut scenes to the ground.  We went through hours of voice auditions today, but we have most of the parts assigned.  One of the nice things about making games for a while is building up a stable of talented voice actors, and the contacts to meet new ones.  Everyone we have slated for this game is really skilled.    One of the reasons I'm in this business is to have people more talented than I bring my vision to life; having these actors and actresses read my lines makes me remember that.  An interesting note: normally games usually have more male roles than female.  Since this game was based on Robert Jordan's books, it's chock full of strong female parts including, of course, the main character.   One session of voice recording starts tomorrow with more to come later.

Mark is tackling engine-level features and fixes, along with some fundamental UI work.   Jim is integrating his newly working traps into the build.  Ryan continues in his ongoing crusade to defeat the AI beast, while Mike F. is integrating all of Steve B.'s new animations.   Our next milestone calls for working grunts and captains, so again, these guys are working hard.

That's got to be all for now.   I've lost the day to auditions, so I've got to catch up.

Until next time!
