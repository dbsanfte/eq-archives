Brad on character customization - Sacrates
Just how detailed can you get with your character in Everquest? Sadly you can't braid your barbarian's butt hair or pierce your big breasted female Wood Elve's nipples but you sure can do a lot of other fun things.

I'm not sure everyone is understanding the issue of cusomization in EQ. So let me clarify:

EverQuest has 12 races X 2 sexes X between 4 & 8 texture sets X virtually unlimited tinting of serveral of the texture sets. And on top of that approximately 8 faces per race/sex.

And on top of that, the characters are divided up such that you could have a crimson breastplate, leather boots, a chainmail coif, etc.

From this perspective, the characters are very customizable.

But here's a key difference that some players are perhaps not seeing in that just about everyone is a newbie: it's what you wear that makes you look different. If you want a crimson breastplate, you're going to have to buy one or quest for one. You don't just arbitrarily set yourself to any color. This way you can have real pride when you appear different, and when a higher level player strolls through the streets of Qeynos, people KNOW he's formidible.

An example would be when I visit Norrath as Aradune -- I'm garbed like a high level warrior (actually, paladin, even though I'm a ranger :). I have emerald plate on and am wielding a fiery avenger 2 handed sword. I definitely stand out, and people constantly ask me 'where did you get that armor?' To which I typically respond that armor such as I wear is found in the depths of dungeons.

Now if I could just head down to the tailor at level 3 and buy some plate and color it green... well, that really cheapens the RP experience from our perspective. Perhaps some of you will disagree, but this was a design decision we made for EverQuest long ago (you'll see similar examples from me in chat logs and interviews probably dating over a year ago).

Now I will say that not every item changes appearance, and that we are limited to some degree in terms of how many textures will fit in the texture memory of widespread 3D cards. But still, when you do the math and calculate the combinations, it's pretty unprecedented.

You lost in EQ? - Sacrates
Have you been getting lost in Everquest while being chased by a few blood hungry savages? Breath easy young one for thou hast cometh to the right place. Check this blurb from EQVault.

If you're like me, the EQ /loc numbers are a little confusing. X,Y,Z is easy to figure, but what about heading and how does that all relate to sense heading skill? Well, I sat down last night (playing a troll no less) and decided to figure it out. Here's what I found out. The EQ coordinate system uses a standard X,Y,Z axis. If you've ever plotted anything on a graph you know how that works. The problem is, EQ doesn't use a standard 0-360 degree heading. For some reason they use a 0-512 degree heading (maybe that's just a different system, I don't know). Having 512 degrees means that the standard degrees translate like this.

0 and 360 = 0 and 512 (they're the same point)
90 = 128
180 = 256
270 = 384

To further complicate things, as you look at this graph system plotted, the direction North is not "up", at 128 degrees. Nope, North is "right" at 0/512 degrees. Here's a list of the 8 main directions, their corresponding heading, and what moving in that direction will do to your X and Y /loc numbers.(keep in mind since you can't get exactly on a certain heading there will be minor variation.

North = 0/512 degrees heading, increase X axis/no change in Y axis.
Northwest = 64 degrees, increase both X and Y axes.
West = 128 degrees, increase y axis/ no change in X axis.
Southwest = 192 degrees heading, decrease X/increase Y.
South = 256 degrees heading, decrease X/no change in Y.
Southeast = 320 degrees heading, decrease both X and Y axes.
East = 384 degrees heading, decrease Y/ no change in X.
Northeast = 448 degrees heading, increase X/ decrease Y.

uh...ok got it.......so that's X=349 degrees and Z 485 into H 284. No prob.

ME Yak - Sacrates
Stephen Nichols hit the boards to give out a description of how closely they were going to stay to the original Middle Earth writings. Here's the biscuit.

There has been quite a bit of excitement on the board lately regarding whether or not our interpretation of Middle-earth for massively multi-player use is correct or not. Most of this conflict revolves around the inclusion of a global chat system within the game. I've stated my reasoning for the inclusion of such a system in the game and have no intention of wavering. I truly believe that global chat serves a very useful purpose in ANY massively multi-player game and should not be excluded. While I agree with you on many of the issues at hand (i.e. global chat, map scale, animal breeding, etc.) I must make what I consider to be the right decisions (given our development schedule) for the viability of this game. I fully agree with and appreciate the fact that a global chat system may have a negative impact on some players, but that does not negate the usefulness of it for many. Yes, it can give the players who use it an unfair advantage over those who don't. There is very little that can be done to eliminate the use of external chat programs and what CAN be done is easily (and undetectibly) reversed by an intelligent hacker. I think on the point of global chat we will have to agree to disagree. While there have been very few vocal supporters for global chat on this board, that does not mean that they don't exist. As it sits right now Middle-earth is barely known by the hard-core gamers let alone the casual market. Most input that we get off of the board is generally from hard-core RPG players with lots of Tolkien knowledge... that's great, but it's not representative of the overall gaming population. The Lord of the Rings sold millions upon millions of copies. Do you really think that each and every one of those readers is a hard-core role-player with a huge reperitore of Tolkien knowledge? No, I think not. If they were, then there would be no argument as to what features are included in the game. Now, our overall goal is to make a great game based on the world of Middle-earth. That means that our locations, races, RPG system, magic system and stories are based wholly on the writing of Tolkien. We are very much aware and sensitive to the need of keeping the game as close to the writings as possible. There are, however, times where this close relationship is weakened to accomodate ease of use features. Global chat is one such example. My job is to make sure that this game is as close to the works as possible so long as that closeness does not make the game unfun. Sometimes these goals conflict and in those times I have to use my best judgement in selecting what makes the cut. There are simply not enough hours in the day for me to debate every issue on this board. To me, this board is a helpful resource that gives me valuable insight into the minds of our hard-core players. My occasional responses are due to time limitations, not desire for debate. Ultimately, it seems to me that some of you believe that if I disagree with you then the game is worthless to you. To that I have no rebuttal except that there are a great many games of this kind in the works and you are sure to find something that strikes your fancy. I don't say that in an arrogant tone, but in a realistic one. I can not please everyone... I won't even try. I will, however, make a great game that will give lots of people enjoyment... both hard-core Tolkien fans and casual fans alike. After all that's what's important; making a fun game that will entertain loads of people (hard-core Tolkien fans and casual players alike).

Everquest: The update - Sacrates
Here's a little juiciness that all you phase 4 testers should have recieved in the mail last night.

Dear EverQuest Phase 4 Testers,

We wanted to give you an update as to what's going on with the P4 Test:

1) First and foremost, we had a problem with our registration server - THIS IS NOW FIXED. If you haven't been able to register yet, please try again now. We've attached your account key to the bottom of this email in case you lost it, or for some reason you didn't get your original.

2) Chat Server problems - We've gotten rid of all the big problems we had early on with the chat server. We're sorry about this happening, but we needed the load to find out some of these problems.

3) No CD's? - We're just in the process of sending out another 100 or so CD's. Out of almost 25k Phase 4 testers, less than 300 people didn't get their CD's. Most of these people are in Canada, although there are some elsewhere. We believe those people will eventually get their CD's, but we wanted to make sure that everyone get's their CD's.

4) Server Stability - We had a couple of big problems, but as of now the servers have really stabalized. Over the weekend they were crashing quite a bit due to one particular bug. That's been fixed, so life should be good again!!

5) Lockups at the Character Select Screen - We think we've fixed this problem, and the fix should be up on the Test Server later this evening. Please give it a try up there by running TestEverQuest.exe. If that works we will publish to all the servers shortly thereafter.

6) Getting kicked back to chat when logging in - We believe this problem has been fixed in the version that is up right now.

7) Random lockups after playing for a short period of time - We think this is sound related, and are still tracking this down.

8) Banshee Drivers - The version of Glide 3.x that is up on the 3dfx website for the Banshee has some MAJOR problems. 3dfx has been working with us directly on this, and has solved the problem. They have given us a special "EverQuest Banshee Driver" that is really a test version of some drivers that will be released publicly very shortly. They were kind enough to provide us with those drivers. You can ONLY get these drivers at www.everquest.com under the Phase 4 video card faq section. These drivers should be posted either tonight, or tomorrow. This should allow Banshee users to play under Glide. If you DON'T get these drivers, and still use the old ones, it is likely that you will simply lock up. So PLEASE download them once they are available.

Very shortly we will be putting the music back into the game. We've been doing a lot of work with the various sound card companies to try and make sure everything is solid before releasing that code to you folks. It should be up on the test server in the next day or so.

In addition to all of the above items, the Dev Team is hard at work on all of the in-game bugs. We had to concentrate on the main problems with getting into the game first, and getting the game back to a stable code-base.

We appreciate your patience with us on these issues, as we have been under a tremendous amount of pressure to make these changes as fast as we can, while still testing things out before we just put up a patch.

The one fundamental truth why EQ is great! - Sacrates
Warning: Sacrates spew ahead! Here it is the true philosophy why Everquest is such a good game. It ultimately forces players to be social. People love to say they hate to be forced into doing something but in this case it works out so well. For as long as I have been playing I have seen this in EQ. Kids that act like jerks become known as kill stealers or just spammers then nobody will team with them and they don't level up anymore because in higher levels it's suicide to solo or just takes much longer to level up. I mean yes, EQ makes UO look shamefull in every way other than this, but this one reason is why EQ is such a good game in the long run. (Oh and let's not forget those skimpy little outfits the girls wear too!) Here's Dave Rickey on the situation.

About 10th level or so, you learn that you *must* get along with the people around you, and that Norrath is a big world but a very small town. People who do antisocial things have a harder time finding groups and getting help when they need it.

I've seen this in action several times, Phase 4 may not last long enough for it to have full effect. Remember who the jerks are, tell others about them, and trust to Karma. Eventually they start new characters and either you leave them way behind, or they start to behave.

He just described it perfectly. I have already seen this in action in Phase 4 too. So all you anti social nutcases out there who just started Phase 4 better read up.
