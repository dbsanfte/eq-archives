EQ and PKillers - Sacrates
Even though the server is acting like a Ho right now, i'm gonna slap up this very important post from Abashi to amuse and abuse you. Been wondering about the fate of the dreaded PK switch? (You can't possibly understand how much I love this thing!!!!) Also they have some shots from Monday Madness last night tht looks like a convention of clones over at CoN.

1. The PK Switch

It's not going away. The only people who a PvP person will be able to fight will be other people who have chosen to be PvP or have chosen to duel. Gone are the days of being able to kill people who don't want to engage in it. There will be no "PK Days" where everyone is PvP for a few hours, or a day, or a week every 2 months. There will be no special advantages for those who choose to play PvP. There will be no limitations on levels, advancement, or weapons for those who chose to abstain from it. No one will *ever* be forced to engage in PvP against thier will. No one will ever be involuntarily switched PvP as a punishment for an attitude problem.

If you wish to engage in PvP with other like-minded individuals, EverQuest will provide you a forum in which you can do that. However if the only way you will enjoy PvP is to kill people who want nothing to do with it, EverQuest will never provide that.

Sacrates suddenly wants to give Abashi some manly loving for that!! Bravo 989!!! Bravo!! Ok on with the rest of the post.

2. PK vs. Anti-PK.

Neither of these exist in EverQuest based on the modern definition. You are all PvP. PvP people will account for all races and classes in the game.

3. Killing out-of-character

Those PvP persons who chose to kill outside the racial and deity hatreds that exist in the land will find themselves without a home. Sorry, but that is how it is. Kill in-character and go home to find yourself a hero with your faction and associates. Kill out-of-character and go home to find a guard wishing to bury his longsword in your skull.

4. One Shot Kills

Gone are the one-shot-kills or the 20 second battles some might be used to. If you want to kill someone it's going to take time, and chances are that they will have to be just as willing to fight you as you are them when you meet. The level-limit (currently 4 levels) on PvP combat pretty much insures that no one will ever take anyone else out in one shot. If you are solo and the target decides soon enough in the fight that they want to get away rather than fight, count on them to be able to do so.

5. Grouping PvPers

I pity anyone who tries to solo a +PvP person be they magic, melee, or otherwise. As a solo PvP you will be USDA Prime for any group of PvPers that come your way. Not to mention your back will be unguarded when that Orc has got you down to 20% health, or when you are sitting on your backside regening health or mana. Healing and medding PvPers will be prime targets for other PvPs looking for an easy kill.

6. Best Class for PvP

Forget about it. There isn't one. Per #5, You are dead meat if you try to solo as a PvPer. You need a group, and like any group, PvP groups need to be well balanced as well. One on one is just too unpredictable. As a Wizard I will nuke you. As a Necromancer I will suck the life out of you while poisoning you from within. As a magician I'll nuke you and make you a playtoy for my pet. As an enchanter I'll just charm you and "introduce" you to a local Guard or wandering giant. As a cleric I'll thump you while healing myself. As a druid I'll nuke you while thumping on you and healing myself, same with the Shaman. As an SK I'll harmtouch you and suck the life out of you. As a paladin I'll lay hands on myself and heal myself proper as well. As a ranger I'll thump you with dual blades while you *try* to hit me and while I heal myself, and if things go bad I'll just disappear into the wilderness and sneak up on you later. As a rogue I will surprise you when you see how easy it is for me to circle behind you. As a bard you will find me swinging at you faster than anyone else. As a warrior or monk, I'll show you what it *really* means to be thumped. Did I miss any?
Any questions?

Also, here's a bit on the 4 level difference you need to PKill.

The 4-level thing was almost unanimously agreed upon by the "notorios PK's" currently in the beta. It was cited as one thing that needed to go in to make PvP more viable. Quite frankly, much outside that 4 level limit, there is really no amount of skill and/or luck that can turn the tables against the stats.

Role Playing and Player killing.

I have heard the roleplay defense to massive unrestricted PvP before, I might actually have invented it. The truth is that though, yes, the PvP switch does hamper roleplay of racial hatred in that you cannot necessarily kill your enemy, it does the greater service of allowing all different playstyles to exist in the same world.

EverQuest is a roleplaying game by definition, but that does not mean we can force people to roleplay any more than we want to force people to engage in PvP.

Now, if we take your same scenario, and if you *both* wish to fight for roleplaying reasons, I suggest you use /duel.

This tool has been provided for those who wish to engage in roleplayed PvP without the stigma caused by unrestricted full-time PvP.

Drakan goods - Sacrates
Now here is a game that's got me warmer than seeing a toothless, leather-clad Granny all buttered up with Ben Gay riding her Moped down the street. Extreme Games have tossed up an Interview with Duncan of Surreal software for you to dig on.

Chris O'Brien: What are some of the advantages and disadvantages of the Surreal Engine?

Duncan: I can't think of many disadvantages except maybe that it is hardware only. One of my favorite things about it after having come from bsp engine stuff like Half-life is that it is all real-time. This feature is very cool for a designer because you don't have to wait for things to compile. All the geometry and even the lighting is calculated in-game. What I see in the editor is also the same as what I get in the game, complete with lighting and even any events I may want to have going on.

Chris O'Brien: Could you list a few features of the Surreal Engine briefly explaining each one?

Duncan: The landscape geometry can be layered...it's not a height map, it's as many layers of mesh we need to get whatever shapes we are looking for...it's kind of hard to explain even in person how it works or the interface for creating it...but it allows us to create natural looking environments that can look very strange and entertaining...a mountain that at the top turns into a spiraling piece of landscape that supports an island at the top which has rivers spilling over the edge down to the mountain is actually pretty easy. The later levels use the power of the engine a lot more than the earlier ones.
