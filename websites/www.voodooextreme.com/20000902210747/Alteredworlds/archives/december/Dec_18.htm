Westwood gets hungry - Sacrates
Looks like Westwood has grabbed onto two RPG scooby snacks to munch on. After Virgin took a dive whatever happened to Swords and Sorcery etc? Well read on ya manchkin.

Westwood Studios is now the proud publisher of two of the titles orphaned by the demise of Virgin Interactive Entertainment. In August, 1998, Electronic Arts purchased Virgin from Spelling Entertainment for $122.5 million in cash. While Westwood (a wholly-owned subsidiary of Virgin) still survives the deal, Virgin Interactive was dissolved and its products set afloat. Two of them were ultimately picked up by Westwood. Swords and Sorcery, by developers John Hight and D.W. Bradley (creator of Wizardry), and Nox, another role-playing game that has gained the moniker "a thinking man's Diablo," will now bear the Westwood label.

"We were already looking at Nox," said Westwood director of public relations, Aaron Cohen, "but it was only under Westwood's umbrella for a month before the EA thing happened." Even though Westwood already has an upcoming RPG, Lands of Lore III, Cohen is confident that these fledgling RPGs will be excellent companions and worthy of the Westwood name. Look for Swords and Sorcery in Summer 1999, and Nox in the Fall.

Big Fat EQ message - Sacrates
I'm starting to like Ryan Palacio This guy is really pimping out the messages on the Boards lately. Now let's see if he can keep up with the BG boys. Here is a rather large biscuit in response to an Economy question.

The concepts and measures that have been implemented to cope with the situations that arise
from players bequeathing items to lower level players does _NOT_ affect the player who
plays without outside help. The system is in fact designed to discourage bequeathal yet
acknowledging the fact that it will happen. Many phase 3 testers were not granted anything by
others and yet have accelerated FASTER than phase 2 and phase 1 testers. Why? Because
the economy is based around the concept that items will find their way to lower level players
eventually. It may not be "given", but rather sold/traded/auctioned/bartered etc... So in essence,
you are indeed reaping the rewards of those before you, just at a cost to yourself instead of free

Let me give an example:

Everyone is even level - therefore everyone is competing for the same items and NPCs. Rare
items hold an extremely high value due to high demand and low supply. There is a stagnation
in the economy due to lack of player-to-player trading and lack of desire to do so.

Levels are homogenously distributed over a range of 30 levels or so... Level 30 PCs require a
larger sum of capital to improve their equipment due to rarity, difficulty of acquisition, and/or
cost. Therefore it is feasible for the level 30 to collect items which he/she does not need for
personal use (said PC may find a weapon that is not as good as his/her current weapon) and
therefore see a means of generating cash. The merchants are very cut-throatish and tend to
drive a hard bargain - therefore it is in this players interest to find a lower level PC who would
desire said item and pay well for it (read "better than merchant price"). This generates cash for
the upper level player while also placing the items into the hands of the lower level player.

Scenarios such as the above are and will be common for the 20-30 player saving up for plate
armor (remember the quote about platemail and leer jets?) and therefore affect the lower level
community. This is by no means a bad thing as it saves the lower level player time and cash.
A semi-symbiotic relation begins to form. The lower levels provide the higher ups with cash,
and in exchange, the higher ups provide improved weaponry at discount prices.

Should you wish to play solo and be relatively anti-social and refuse interaction with others all
together (not suggesting that you are, just making a point about extremes), shops will provide
you with equivalents for most items at significantly higher-than-street-price cost.

The system by no means handicaps the "pure" player (as you put it).

If you group with others, explore the world, interact with the environment, complete quests and
slay foul beasts, you will be more than self sufficient and quickly find yourself in the position of the higher ups - looking to generate cash while keeping your eye on whatever golden ring you
have set for yourself - as a paladin, most likely plate and a very big sword =)
