Revenant Preview - Sacrates
Just noticed over at Billy's Love shack (VE) that Gamelink have posted a spiffy ass preview of Revenant you want to cuddle up to. This game looks pretty damn good so far so be sure to scope it out.

Combat includes 4 basic modes. We have weapon based combat, hand to hand combat, bow combat, and stealth mode. Weapon mode features three basic attacks: a thrust, a swing, and a chop. As you gain experience you can learn variations off of these base attacks. These variations will eventually lead to chain attacks that link moves together in a multiple hit attack combination. In certain situations, an interactive move can be performed. Interactive moves include fatalities, throws, and power moves. These are not reserved for the player alone however. Expect monsters to pick you up by the head and throw you, punch you in the face and send you flying head over heels; even pick you up and powerbomb you. Did we just say pro-wrestling moves in an RPG? You're damn right we did. Hand to hand mode works much like weapon mode with the 3 basic attacks being punch, thrust kick, and jumping sidekick. Variations can be learned like the uppercuts and spin kicks. Interactives and combinations are also included for some serious martial arts combat.

BG Bits - Bowmeister
K...holidays over...everyone pop a tums and were back in business. Ray was making the rounds on the Baldur's Gate Developer's Corner and had some info on how things are going. On a side note, has anyone in Canada gone to the pre-order site for Baldur's Gate? It might be just me, but isn't 70 some odd dollars american a bit steep for a game!?!? I mean, that's like $110 canadian!!!! Ah well, it's friday morning, had to bitch about something :).

Small update: things are going ok. There are a few remaining bugs being worked on in the multiplayer code - we want to make sure that people don't get arbitrarily dropped out of the game because of a strange problem with packet loss that has appeared in the past few days (that would be bad :) - and a few remaining bugs in some of the more complex single player scripts - we want to make sure that these are really solid. We're also still awaiting a few final sound tracks for some of the movies, which are supposed to be here early next week (after which we prepare our next release candidate).

If anyone's interested, I just finished the game for the second time (the first person in the world to have finished the entire game twice, start to finish). This time I tried to do everything - visited every area, tried to explore as many dialogue options as possible. It took me 189 game days (a game day is about 2 hours real time, but this figure also includes time spent resting, which takes a third of a day each time you rest) to see everything. This took about 160-170 hours of actual game play (last time I spent maybe 100 hours, since I did fewer side quests). I reached the XP cap of 89,000 XP about 140 hours into the game, while in chapter 6. It wasn't very easy to reach - you have to do a LOT of the subquests to reach it.

I'm pretty comfortable saying (from personal experience) that I think the game is really good now.

Now I start the game again (well, at least part of it - doubt if I will be able to finish it a third time before we release it) Yee-haw!

Revenant Preview - Sacrates
The boys and girls over at Games Domain have kicked up a spicey little Preview of Revenant. The game that is more colorfull than a Partridge Family rerun but still looks to kick much monkey ass! Check it.

The combat system features four modes, depending on your preferences, and also how you are equipped: weapons, hand-to-hand, bow, and stealth. Weapons mode will allow a player to execute moves such as thrust, swing, and chop. The hand-to-hand moves feature punch, thrust kick, and jumping sidekick. As experience is gained during the course of the game, your character will be allowed to combine various weapons and hand-to-hand moves to obtain fearsome combination attacks. Bow mode is self-explanatory, and stealth mode allows you to fight like a thief, utilising surprise attacks from within shadows.

Farmegeddon? - Bowmeister
Much as it's nice to see some quests and special events in Ultima Online, I think there is a programmer over there that needs to lay off the crack pipe :). Just kidding. Saw this up over on the Ultima Online site, under the Updates FYI section.

"et congregavit illos in locum qui vocatur ultimatum Fermagedon"

"and they were gathered into the place which in Ultima is called Farmageddon"

It was prophesied long ago, and perserved in ancient grimoires, that a terrible war would be waged in a distant and transient land. The prophecy speaks of beasts, consumed by the spirits of demons, that will rise up against each other, and leave a trail of destruction so deep and wide that none shall be left unscathed; the massacre shall be total.

The scholars have interpreted that for a few short days, the war cycle will begin and end over and over again, like waves that ceaselessly beat upon the sand. All souls who dare will be afforded the opportunity to participate in this dangerous dance, as the cycle will begin anew with great frequency.

All who choose to gather into this place shall go by way of the five great cities and then their fates will be known, as is detailed in the following rhyme:

Those who go by way of Yew shall be seen as swine.
And those from out of Trinsic come, now become bovine.
From the place known as Minoc, all creatures live as Cervidae.
And only in Britain do they stay as the pilgrim Hominidae.
But to complete this tragic tale, and to make the waters more murky,
Those lost souls who of Vesper come shall be seen as the turkey.

This great war of ensuing bestial possession is known as FARMAGEDDON and all who gather there will be swept up in the red river of beast blood that will flow freely beginning tomorrow and lasting until the month is out. Be forewarned.

Woohoo! patch #2 - Bowmeister
Well you gotta admit, when Interplay finally gets the ball rolling, it takes off doing mach 10. Feargus was on the official site to announce a new patch for Fallout 2 (v1.02...yah I know, that's the same number as the last one, Who ever said programmers can count :)).

Located in the Files section and at this link, is a new version of the Fallout 2 BETA 1.02 Patch. It is still a BETA patch though, so it has not finished going through our QA department.

It should be a lot more stable then the last one, and fix a bunch of little things.

We are a little concerned about Save Games between this and the previous BETA patch, only if the Save Game you are trying to load is corrupted. We aren't 100% able to determine if a Save Game is corrupted when it is being loaded. So don't try to open a Saved Game that was previously corrupted. I know that sounds a little lame, but I wanted everyone to have that information.
