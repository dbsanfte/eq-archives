EQ Goods - Sacrates
And Abashi returns to the boards dishing out more of that lubed lovin he always sauces up. First he talks a bit on character balance in PvP.

Keep in mind that it is not the goal of the dev team to make the classes equal in PvP. They will try, but if comes a choice between balancing something in PvE while leaving it horribly weak in PvP, they will balance for PvE.

However, I haven't exactly seen this happen yet. There will be no best PvP class. Below is an example of what I think they are aiming for in PvP. The comparisons are not necessarily representative of what is in game currently.

For instance, perhaps, at level 50:

A warrior might:
Outclass Bard
Go even with a Druid
Fear a Wizard

A Bard might:
Outclass a Monk
Go even with a Shaman
Fear a Ranger

A Druid might:
Outclass a Necromancer
Go even with a Warrior
And fear a Shadowknight

An Enchanter might:
Outclass a Wizard
Go even with a Cleric
Fear a warrior

Etc....(again, this is all pulled out of thin air)

The point being, I think they are aiming to make sure that there is no one class that beats everything in PvP. Tactics, who attacks first, and knowledge of the game will go a long way towards determining PvP battles.

I know enough about the tactics in game that I'm pretty sure if handed any class I could beat any other class if I attack first on the terrain of my choice in the zone of my choice. I'm relativly certain that I would not lose a battle under those circumstances against an equally leveled opponent.

Of course, this is all just my opinion...

ME Updates - Sacrates
And the band plays on in the ongoing development of Middle Earth. This has got a lot of people in a frenzy and I feel for the devlopers of this game because of the love people have over this stuff. I pity the fools who decide to go and do a Star Trek Massive Multiplayer game. With that said here's some goodness for ya. First Stephen Nichols talking about the Economy.

I am not in favor of a totally PC driven economy, it really is not practical. However, there will be advantages to being a PC craftsman (or shopping from one). PC craftsmen will be the main source for specialized items in the world. You will never go to a NPC shop to buy mithril chain armor for instance. While you can stop by a NPC shop and buy some items of average to good quality, you'll get a better price from a PC craftsman or vendor (and probably better quality too).

It's a noble idea to believe that players can run their own economy, but it simply is not true of the masses. Oh no, there's the "M" word again. Certainly there will be many players who run shops and create goods for sale (we have an extensive system for this), but the majority of players will NOT do that. Look at the two extremes on the board that we have already... on one hand we've got the super hard-core RPG players who want ultra-realism. On the other hand we've got Eugene (and the hundreds of thousands of players in between). Those two sets of players do not mix in a player-run system of any kind. We need fail-safe systems to deal with the volatile nature of these polar opposite players. Don't get me wrong, we are making the game to support player extended economy, but not a totally player-driven economy.

And the all important weapons balancing act.

Just having different weapon skills does not add a whole lot to the game unless there are obvious advantages and disadvantages to each weapon. Each weapon should be balanced. By that I mean that each weapon needs to have a valid use in combat. If you use a warhammer, you're gonna be slow and easy to hit after a missed strike. Likewise, if you use a dagger, you'll be much faster and be able to hit many times. Beyond that simple concept there is the issue of weapon vs. armor balancing. If you are wearing chain armor against someone carrying a spear, you've got pretty good protection. If your opponent is wielding a warhammer, you'd be at a disadvantage if struck. Our goal as the designers is to create a use for every weapon so that warriors can't just pick the "best" one. There can be no "best" one in general, just the best one for the job.

And some various spewage for ya.

Why do people say that UO has failed?! That game is consistently bringing in at least $1,000,000 a month and is not slowing down as far as our information shows. That's a damn good success so far as I can tell.

What's with this elite attitude? Did you know that the "casual player" outnumbers the hard-core player many times over? If you doubt this, why did Deer Hunter do so well?! While we're not making Middle-earth/Deer Hunter, we must make the game palatable to a broad range of people. For us to make money, we must focus on an acceptable middle ground... not on one relatively small group of players. While we can not please everyone, we'll please as many as we can.

For you to even suggest that we should not be here to make some money is just insanity. That is exactly why we are here. My family's propsperity rests solely on my ability to create games that make money for this company. We are not in business just to make games for free. As if. This is America, yes?! lol Just insanity! Money IS the reason WHY we are making MMORPGs. If it was'nt we would not be here at all. (Nor would anyone else)

Our goal is to provide a Middle-earth based sandbox, if you will. With lots of things to do and try, but with no particular order or story IMPOSED on any players. But we are also filling the void that all other MMORPGS have fallen victim to. That would be the lame quests and absence of system-driven gameplay that so many players require to have purpose. Our goal is to provide both of the aspects of gameplay to our customers so that each player's style of play is accomodated. And it is indeed a Herculean task.

Proof is in the pudding - Sacrates
Quite a few people have been wondering just how important the Party is in EQ. Let me give you this short story so you get a good idea of just how important it is. This is the story of Tefe, a Cleric with a Holy Hammer big enough to make even Monica Lewinsky blush. First I shout across the zone looking for other adventurers around my level and get 3 responses. I head on over to Crush Bone to do a little Orc bashing. The others are already there and waiting, since I got lost 3 times on the way there damnit. We gather ourselves and let 3 others join in the fun. So there we are standing at the Entrance to Crush Bone, a kick ass group of level 8 to 11 Characters. Feeling incredibly good about ourselves we head into the zone ready for anything except a naked Ogre. In that case we would run like the wind. We head straight over to the big Orc Camp to the West and find ourselves in Combat with 2 Orc Slavers and a Legionarre. This is when all hell breaks loose on screen. I am watching the health of all 6 members of our party while bashing the skull of an Orc myself. Of course this is the perfect time to scream my favorite War chant "Bend over and take it like an Orc!". Feeling much better after releasing my scream I start healing our Barbarian since he is taking the brunt of the heat. The incredible thing is that there is so much killing going on you just sit there watching "You have gained party experience" the whole time. By the end of a good session of that You can easily gain half an experience bar. If I were to have done that Solo I wouldn't have even gained a bubble in that amount of time. This is the true joy of playing Everquest. It's an incredibly social experience. Unlike UO which was an incredibly Anti-social experience. Hope that gives a fair explanation on that situation.

Witchboy Update - Sacrates
As always, Harvey "Witchboy" Smith dishes out his own skewed perspective of games, gaming and that strange game he's working on called Deus Ex. Here's the bite straight from the monkeys mouth.

the last few months have been good to gamers, at least according to my tastes. half-life, thief, baldur's gate & the s.m.a.c demo have all been good enough to give me a stiffy.

but of course i must have more... something new. so now, looking to the far horizon of the coming year, there are several games that have me getting really excited.

system shock 2--they just released a series of new shots that i think look awesome. i can just *feel* the game-play radiating off of these images. every location seems to tell some story or imply some interesting interaction-possibility. friends at looking glass & irrational games say that shock2 is really shaping up. some members of the thief team have moved over to work with the shock2 team (i believe). both groups are made up of some of the people who were responsible for underworld, system shock, terra nova and thief. if that doesn't get you wildly excited, you probably should not be viewing the content on this page. go here for the new shock2 shots. (also, saam runs a really excellent site called Through the Looking Glass. it's another great place to get info on LG games.) TTLG.com

anachronox--yes, i know i work for ION, but i work at ION *Austin*, so i rarely get out to the dallas location, the mighty tower that it is. (here with warren's group, we work in a little bank strip plaza... from which we have nearly been evicted several times for reasons i'd rather not divulge.) anyway, i just went to the ION web site and looked over all the new screen shots for the game. so far, from the demos i have seen, the game itself seems like a really interesting blend of strategy and role-playing. so i was already hyped for the game. but now, after seeing the latest work they have done, i am floored. it just looks amazing--revolutionary is the word that comes to mind. like jack nicholson said in _as good as it gets_, "you make me want to be a better designer..." really. looking at those shots is like a kick in the ass--those guys rock. & they are going to raise the bar. so this game is another title i am eagerly awaiting.

diablo 2--baldur's gate is cool & it consumed a lot of the last few weekends. but as far as stat based/character advancement/kill monsters/collect treasure/ gain levels games go, diablo is the best. (btw--as you probably know, i am personally more inclined to the other side of the RPG-- plot, character, setting; this is more what Deus Ex is all about. so don't be thinking our game is going to be some sort of hack & slash fest.) the new diablo 2 shots seem to show that blizzard has repeated much of the same goodness that made diablo so damned addictive. plus they've added a bunch more. something like 6 character types, new monsters, spells, etc. and the ease of their multiplayer access is nothing short of 'the best in the world.' i can't wait to play D2--it's all the stat based game-play of a game like baldur's gate, plus it's faster, leaner and with fewer annoyances. note to blizzard: you should avoid areas that force instant repeated reloads, features that take control of the player's character away & NPC interactions that suck everyone in the adventuring party into someone else's conversation. (imho, the 3 worst aspects of BG.)
