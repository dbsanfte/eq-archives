Official WoT update - Sacrates
Yup, it's that time of the week kids. Glen slapped up this weeks dose of WoTTy goodness. WoT fans have got to be in a state of bliss right now with the new novel and game in progress. Here ya go.

Making games is fun. So is poking out your eyes with a hot spike. You just have to be in the right mood...

I'm afraid the official WoT bag of treats is empty this Halloween. All you get is a tasty tidbit of text, and as weary as I am, I wouldn't be surprised if it really tasted like sandpaper. I'm just tired and irritable because I got little sleep this weekend, feverishly writing the scripts for the cutscenes. Given that they have to be done ASAP, as we need to lay down the voices for the animation, I'm spending every free moment on them. I like them, though. If we really can pull off what I've scripted, we'll be including a freakin' movie with your game! The recent release of The Path of Daggers (Jordan's latest addition to the Wheel of Time series of books) has helped put me in that 'WoT' dialog frame of mind.

Kathleen Bober, a long-time friend of Legend, artist, and resident voice-and-video talent coordinator, has arrived to help me out. It's been a while since I've worked with her (voice recording on Death Gate was the last go-around), but it's always a pleasure and I'm glad for the opportunity. And, again, we're extremely close to nailing down the animation studio responsible for bringing it all together. Watch this space.

We're steaming ahead for the milestone on the tenth. It includes a wonderful multi-player map, consisting of the world map that I've been showing off the past two weeks, and three citadel variants that rock. In addition, the level designers are producing new maps like crazy. Our first single-player map from James is at alpha, and more are well on their way. I've seen some wonderful single-player work from our mystery mapper, whom I'll now introduce to you: Tim Jervis. He comes to us fresh from a stint on Heretic 2 and his style is meshing very well with what we've already achieved. Scott finished optimizing and enhancing the Whitecloak primitives. You thought they were beautiful before; now, they're absolutely awesome. Warren is producing a couple of variants that look fantastic. Soon, he's moving onto a Portal Stone world. Cool.

New software architecture is being implemented throughout the entire game. Jim has moved from pathing to traps, Aaron is getting the artifacts in place, and the Ryan and Mike show are hard at work on the upcoming milestone requirements (player models, animations, and grunt AI). It's pretty cool seeing the grunts running around, suddenly smart about their surroundings and each other; before they tended to exhibit a behavior we affectionately termed 'Cluster Fun' (only it wasn't quite 'fun').

The texture artists have finally left Shadar Logoth in favor of the Aes Sedai primitives. In the meantime, they've produced some incredible world/skybox textures, and Paul rediscovered some older textures he painted ages ago (remember that door I showed you?). The level designers, most of whom joined the team long after those textures were created, ripped into them like starving dogs. Evidently, they're very happy with the discovery (not that they didn't have over 1,100 textures to work with before this). Paul has also started generating the long-anticipated architectural sketches and reference material for the White Tower. It shouldn't be long until we actually see finished primitives and variants using them.

Is your tongue dry yet? Ah, I long for the days of old where I stayed up until the wee hours writing counter contest columns and viewer mail. I hope you don't mind if I keep the creative writing assignments focused on the game. I think, in the long run, you'll like it better that way. Now I'm off to figure out how to get every female character to cross her arms under her breasts.

Until next time!

BG Backroom bites - Sacrates
Why on earth did they name this the backroom? I swear it sounds like one of those dark areas in AOL that questionable people visit. Next thing you know they'll have the BG Bathhouse and the BG Bungalo. Anyway Greg Peterson was bitch slappin BG style in the backroom today. Here's a few tasty biscuits for you.

Interplay and money not being a good mix: But trust the press release...

The issues:

1) A couple of major titles have slipped.

2) Big returns and mark-downs from products that did not perform well in the market.

The facts:

1) Sales are up 50% versus same quarter last year

2) Big development/investment dollars have gone out the door and have not yet yielded returns.

I leave it up to you to do your own analysis of whether the money that has been spent on Baldur's Gate, or Messiah, or Giants, or Earthworm Jim 3D, etc, etc, will yield appropriate returns.

The press report basically said that even a company the size of Interplay has only so much cash to invest. It also stated that Brian Fargo is willing to put up $5 million of his own cash in order to secure a line of credit to get Interplay through this 'lean' period. (A personal vote of confidence in Interplay).

So when I say it has not impacted Baldur's Gate, I mean that Baldur's Gate is one of the products that will be critical to successfully launch in order for us to get a nice healthy return on all of the dollars that have been invested in it to date (several years of development, marketing, etc, etc). In my PERSONAL opinion, it does not make sense to pull support right before a product is getting ready to be launched.

Baldur's Gate is getting more than its fair share of attention and support - if you ask Bioware, it is probably getting too MUCH attention and support ;)

Dues Ex Interview - Sacrates
AGN 3D just posted news that the Warzone kicked up an Interview with the guys working on Dues Ex. Other than that not much happening in the lands of online RPG news just yet. Sit back, put in some Tito Jackson and read this Interview until the news kicks in.

WarZone: Okay, that all sounds good, but what about the player character? How customizable will the player character be (looks, traits, etc.)?

Steve: Very. There is a lot of variation in the skills you choose, the augmentations you acquire and the inventory you carry around. Since you are not a pack mule and you cannot carry everything at once, you have to make some critical decisions about what to pick up. Plus conversations reflect your attitude. By the end of the game, the character will be highly customized by the player’s choice.

Witchboy: We want to give the player as much freedom as possible. Our systems depend on the player making choices and living with the outcome. Also, we have multiple versions of the player-character for different suits and environments. We’re toying with how to effectively do multiple races and both genders. No promises, but we’re very much into the concept of user creativty and differentiation of experience.

Fallout 2 coverage - Sacrates
Continuing with our Allout Fallout 2 Hint and Tip coverage from the Message Boards we have a few more dug up from our boys Chris and David. Again, for those of you who don't want their game spoiled please do not read this post. Don't come to me and start bitch slapping because you read these, if you do i'll get Billy's Mom to come over and put a hurting on you.

Getting the wrench: There's a few places to get a wrench.
1) Travelling Merchant
2) Sneak around some of the New Reno shop areas or visit the garage in New Reno.
3) Complete the quest for the little boy, Curtis in the Vault City courtyard, then listen to what he says after you complete the quest.

Sleeping with the Bishop's Daughter: 1. Mordino only wants you to hurt the Corsican Bros if they don't pay the tribute. Since they will as soon as you tell them who you are and what you're there for, you don't need to hurt them.

2. If you sleep with Mr. Bishop's wife or daughter, he will attack you as soon as he sees you walking out of her room. He gets kind of ticked off about things like that.

Rescuing Vic from Slavers: You have to help him repair his radio then buy him... OR just kill off all the slavers. Not to hard if you're 6th level or above with a good weapon.

If you don't know how to repair the radio talk to Vic. If you can't talk to Vic, get permission from Metzger.

Where's Myron?: Myron is in a place called the "Stables" outside of New Reno. You will have to talk to someone in town who knows where that is before it will show up on the map.

Maybe the town greeter knows...
