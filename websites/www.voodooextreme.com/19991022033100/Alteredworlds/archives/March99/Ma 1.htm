WoTTy update - Sacrates
Glen has returned from his trip to Italy with this great weekly update on WoT. This time he slaps everything in there with a few screenshots and development goodies. I'll give you the link since the post itself is huge. Here's a bite though.

Anyway, I'm still digging myself from the virtual pile of email and general mile-long list of tasks that built up in my absence.  I must say, though, that people here made good use of the time.  All of the level designers spent their time focused on one or two levels.  James completely revamped the 'Approach to Shadar Logoth', and it's looking really fine--definitely a great showcase level, and as it's the first mission, that's appropriate.  Warren kicked ass, adding material to Tim's extensive map the 'Bowels', Tim himself has been working on 'The Streets' and 'The Inner Sanctum'--optimizing, polishing, and adding architecture required by the cut scenes--and Scott's made some wonderful progress on the 'Approach to the Fortress of the Forsaken' and 'Saving the White Tower' with Julie's new textures.  Everything's looking really sweet.

It was only a matter of time: Aaron slapped decals into the build.  Now blood sticks on surfaces, explosions result in scorch marks, and Balefire really leaves its mark.  He also worked in some cool new artifact graphics, and we combined them with some wonderful new sound effects.   These really have the weight and edge that I've been looking (listening?) for.    Fireball (among others) really feels like a big deal now.

Here is a tidbit more about the races ...

Humans
The great generalists amont the four player races. Humans might not be the strongest, fastest, or smartest characters, but they also don't tend to have any particular weaknesses. Their pioneering nature has led them to spread their settlements throughout the land, much to the chagrin of other races.

Elves
Elves have developed their culture around values of aesthetic refinement. While they are not the most physically powerful of people, Elves do tend towards higher intelligence and have great hand/eye coordination. Elves usually live in their own communities built in harmony with woodland settings.

Dwarves
Dwarves keep to themselves in mountain settlements. The generations spent perfecting their mining and stoneworking crafts have given members of the Dwarven race naturally higher levels of strength and endurance. They also tend to have weaker eyesight and to move at a slower, more deliberate pace.

Goblins
Making a relatively more recent appearance in Erathia, the Goblins have quickly established themselves and control vast regions of the wild hinterlands. They are a warrior race possessed of great strength and speed, but as such have not spent a great deal of energy developing either their intellectual potential, nor their sense of civility. In other words, they tend to rub people the wrong way.
