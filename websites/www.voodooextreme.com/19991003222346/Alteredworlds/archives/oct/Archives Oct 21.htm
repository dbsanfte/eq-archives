More U:A goodness - Bowmeister
Hmm...looks like Sith has been busy today:) There's some more new info on Ultima:Ascension up, as well as some choice info on Ultima Online 2 (no, not the 2nd age expansion pack, a whole new shebang). First a couple of posts from Bill Randolph on the new discussion boards. The first is on the performance of the demo at E3...

Yes, our performance at E3 was sub-optimal. But it was for reasons beyond just frame rate. If you stood in one place & looked around, the frame rate typically (on a P300) was around 25 - 30 FPS. But, as you'd move through the world, the frame rate would drop to as low as 10. Now, 10 FPS isn't completely unplayable, although it's unpleasant. What really hurt in that version of the engine was the changes in speed--going from fast to slow. Also, there were portions of the world that would cause the engine to pause for at least 1/2 second while a new section of the world loaded, and thathurt most of all. Notice I said "that version of the engine"--we're well aware of our deficiencies at E3 and are attacking those portions of the code with gusto right now... Our engine isn't level-based. It loads the world in, in chunks, while you move around, so you can circumscribe the entire world continuously. But, this introduces interesting performance issues, which we're working hard to address!

Second is the awesome news that Ultima Online 2 will be using an engine much like U:A...

UO2 won't actually be using the Ascension engine per se; but they are shooting for a similar look & feel. Ascension's demands on the CPU are high. UO2 will have to take different approaches in the areas of collision & poly count to maintain good performance with such an increase in # of characters on-screen & moving about the world. Their set of problems are so different from ours, that they're developing a parallel set of code; but we do share a fair amount of tools.

Finally another little blurb from the one and only Lord British on the possibility of adding extra features via patches after U:A ships...

Though time is always a factor in any endeavor, we think we have sufficient time to get in all that is needed. That being said... Our basic free form 3D world approach, which allows us to build Britannia the way we want, is making shadows hard if not impossible, for now. Doing a patch of new monsters, terrain, and events could be fun and worthwhile though. We'll have to see when we get there.

Middle Earth news - Sacrates
Stephen Nichols was over on the boards spewing more Middle Earth goodness our way. This is gonna be one tasty game, I am surprised they talk so much about it so soon.

on armour: We are planning quite a few different kinds of armor. And while there are no planned "blanket" restrictions on armor being worn, you'll find it difficult to maneuver if you are too encumbered.

on communicating in the game:If you are unconscious or engaged in combat, the global chat system will be disabled (if you are dead, you're dead). Sadly, there is nothing that we can do to stop people from changing between applications unless we make the game entirely unfriendly to Windows. And even if we did that, I'm not certain that we could stop task switching from happening. So, the simple fact is that people will chat whether you want them to or not. Personally, I have no problem with global chat. It's a fine feature that enhances the game for many. There's a few other points that I need to mention: 1) Middle-earth is a HUGE game. With 10,000 users in a world at the same time it is not feasible to have truely "global" chat. Modems everywhere would gag and choke if we allowed even 1,000 people to be in a single chat channel. So, the persistant chat channels will be based on geographic location (i.e. Shire Chat, Moria Chat, etc). Of course there will be guild chat channels and other miscellaneous private forums. 2) Global Chat will exist whether we provide it, or Middle-earth players set up IRC or ICQ forums. I feel that the benefits outweigh the problems. If you don't want to use global chat, don't use it. You'll have more on-screen playing area if you choose not to use it and can live forever oblivious to its presence if you like.

Dues Ex biscuits - Sacrates
3Dnews posted some interesting stuff found on the message boards about Dues Ex. Speaking of Dues Ex we have some goodness coming your way quite soon on it. Keep your eyes peeled. (RpgNews)

Music:Music is vital for creating atmosphere and Deus Ex is (better be) a very atmospheric game. We have a particular style in mind that will be cool and unique (no driving, techno stuff here!). Suffice it to say we want the player to be creeped out and paranoid every moment of the game and music will go a long way to creating that kind of mood. Chris Norden (lead programmer dude) has some stuff up his sleeve, technology-wise. Hate to be cryptic but I don't want to get into specific code, styles or uses or anything right now. Ask again later. Just know that music will play an important part in Deus Ex.

Gameplay:Regardless of whether each and every obstacle hallows for multiple solutions--which is, as warren said, The Goal(tm)--one thing we really want to support is multiple, cusotmized approaches based on play-style. in other words, if you like sneaking, then you should be able to sneak, spy and steal your way through Deus Ex. we don't want to get to the last 20% of the game and suddenly force the stealthy player to become 'rocket man' or whatever. as much as i loved Fallout (a great game), i was angry near the end when i could not continue sneaking along and instead had to start slugging it out more. i want Deus Ex to do a better job of allowing players to express themselves through the game. your character, your actions, your choices.

Gorky 17 info - Sacrates
Those fluffy Snuffleufugus kind of guys over at VN have just smacked up some good info on the upcoming Gorky 17 (Uh, this name had to come from late night experimental drug sessions?) that you'll want to read up on. Here is a small piece and for the rest of this beast head on over to VN.

Gorky 17 is a mix of adventure and strategy-RPG. Remember "Final Fantasy Tactics" and "Vandal Hearts"? In those console hits the main focus was on the battles, with the plot shown non-interactively in-between. Now add fantasy-full adventures and plot-twists, explorations and much, much more! That's Gorky 17! For all you PC fanatics out there, here's the description in a nutshell: it's similar to "XCOM", but with a much simpler user interface (doing for strategy what "Diablo" did for RPGs), plus implemented adventures (so it's not only a matter of having the right strategy, but also using the right objects, getting information through conversations, interacting with other characters, etc.). In short, this is state of the art graphics with 3D characters and a "living" environment.

Infinite Worlds - Sacrates
Sean Palmer was over on their General message board laying some good news eggs our way.

on framerate: That first room is a doozy... probably the slowest room in the game.

On my P2 266 MMX AGP running SGI software rendering, underlying card is a RIVA TNT AGP but I'm not using that driver due to driver problems..

I'm getting 6 fps in that same spot using Medium detail

Methinks this will improve for next release due to a bug I think I found.. I used to get almost 20 fps in software rendering in most rooms, 40 tops!!

on excessive darkness: Yes we will be doing something about it, but I'm kinda of the opinion that some people are getting the game even darker than we set it to be... abnormally dark.. it's curious..

Please, anyone who thinks the game is "unplayably dark", send me a screenshot of the dungeon in the Main Entrance, as soon as you step into the dungeon, without moving. Right by the doors to the city, looking north. Send me those screenshots and I'll compare them to what it's supposed to look like and we'll see if there's some other problem with lighting that I don't know about yet.

on the ability to see players in the 3D view: I don't think it's that difficult. Controlling player avatars is just like controlling the current monsters... and we can use many of the same models. I have high hopes of getting player avatars and their companions/party members showing up in the 3d view before we're done.

Now of course that may not happen due to any number of reasons... and if anyone asks... I didn't say this... I was never here...

And Mike Larossi was talking about what they expect in terms of what the game does Mass Market.

Unfortunately today's market does not allow technologically inferior products to thrive. It's true that the hardcore gamer can spot the good games from the bad regardless of graphics but to the average gamer they enter a store look at a box and make a decision. Alot of game sales are spontaneous bought on a whim sales and before we went to 3D Infinite Worlds was not fit for a mass commercial release. Mordor 1 is a prime example. It was made and sold and didn't make dick for sales. Alot of you bought M1 but it really didn't make any money at all and thats pretty sad. I myself buy games I know I will enjoy. I used to buy everygame that came out just so I could say I had it. Nowadays I might buy 1 game a month. Why? Because 95% of the games out there suck. Either they are all graphics and no gameplay or they are just plain boring. The gaming industry needs a wake up call. With the advancement of computer technology alot of companies are too busy thinking of ways to take advantage of all this new hardware and are forgetting how to make good games.

If we knew we could get mass sales with primitive graphics we'd have stuck with them but I know for a fact that most of the gamers out there who are big on graphics would take one look at what we had and passed it right up. The hardcore market can't generate the sales to make a game successful.

We are doing the best we can to make use of the 3d we incorporated. First it was just the view that was 3D and then people asked for monsters so we gave you monsters in 3D. The combat now has something to do with the 3D because of the attack range. By the time we are done we hope to have a superior product from what M2 was to what Infinite Worlds now is.

Some of you may feel betrayed by us buying into this trend of fancy graphics and selling out to the man. I understand where you are coming from when you say it but if you think about it you'll understand why it was neccessary. We have about 8-9 people working on IW right now and if we can't make a game that will sell we'll all be job hunting when we finish.
