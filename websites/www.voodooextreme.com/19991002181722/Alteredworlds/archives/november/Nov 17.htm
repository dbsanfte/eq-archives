RPG's history and future - Sacrates
Not only did CGO slip up the EQ beta preview but it also posted this article by Harvey "Witchboy" Smith called "Computer Role Playing Games and their fans". Good reading so head on over and let him dish some up for you.

There has been much talk lately about the future of computer role-playing games. Some say that they are in the middle of a popularity resurgence, while others cry that CRPG's in the "classic" sense are dead. Yet the argument is not necessarily a binary thing-there are some people that feel the classic CRPG has simply been scattered finely throughout computer gaming and that it has evolved away from its roots. I think proponents of the latter opinion have a good point. You could make the argument that the RPG has been one of the most influential forces on computer games of all types, having permeated every other genre to such a degree that it is no longer even noticeable. Think about all the adventure games and action games that have RPG-like inventory systems. Think about the origin of the fictional settings for games like Master of Magic, Myth or Warcraft. Think about all the terminology that has crept into other non-RPG games-even "character" itself is used in a fashion that more in common with paper RPGs than with fiction in the context of computer games. It's not "a character," it's "your character."

More on ME and Elves - Sacrates
The discussion on Elves and Middle Earth rages on. Janus Anderson was back on the boards adding a bit more to the Elf conversation. This bit was posted in response to an Interview that was done with Steve. The Bold is the response.

We’ve been tossing around quite a few ideas regarding limiting elves in Middle-earth. After quite a bit of discussion we’ve decided that an enforced waiting period, along with some in-game goals to complete would suffice to limit the influx of new elves. A new player has the choice of making a character of any race (except elf) when he first begins playing. To create an elf character, the player must have been active in the game for a certain number of hours AND achieved a prerequisite level of elven lore with an existing human, dwarf or hobbit character. While this does not enforce a population cap, I feel that it will help to foster an elven community that has knowledge of the game and hopefully role-playing personality.

Oops! You got me... I had forgotten to mention this proposed solution in my other message.

Steve and I are constantly discussing and modifying designs, so almost every aspect of the game is subject to design changes from time to time. We rarely freeze any design decisions until we actually implement them since we are constantly coming up with better ideas for systems. You are right though, the solution that Steve related is at least as likely as the ones from my previous message.
