Everquest: Noting the concerns
by Sacrates

Beautifull naked woman: Oh Sacrates, it's so big......

Sacrates: Yes I know, it's been given to me by my parents.

BNW: You have such skill and how were you able to.......

Sacrates: Practice makes perfect.

As they both look at the stick figure painting of the woman that he had just completed. (What else were you thinking Perv?) He stands up, stretches and heads over to his computer to start up a session of Everquest as she lays seductively at his feet.

BNW: So what's with your addiction to this game?

Sacrates: Every Gamer dies, but not every gamer truly lives.......

That in a capsule is why Everquest is so great. You will never be able to experience all this game has to offer. The world is huge for you to explore, there are so many items for you to acquire unique and rare, and most important, creatures to kill and people to meet. Now that I have become literate and knowledgable in the ways of Everquest let me take you on a journey through this unique world. I will enlighten you to it's greatness and expose you to it's "few" flaws.
I had a brief chat with Brad Mcquaid, the head honcho over at the newly named RedEye Interactive on a few issues that have been bothering me lately. I came out feeling confident most of these issues will be resolved by the final release and will not reflect the end product. These are simply a few noticable flaws that stuck out to me and a few other beta testers. With that said, let me hit on a few recent issues before I jump in the sack with Brad.
As a few of you know who have been keeping in touch with EQ lately, Phase 3.5 of the beta has been released for download to all current Beta testers of 3.0. After downloading this fat mofo I quickly start it up and jump right in. First thing I notice is the world is a much brighter place now. Brighter in the fact that the textures have been incredibly improved upon. There is now detail and characteristics to items and the environment. You can see Dirt on the giant monolith structures outside of Felwithe was my first impression. The grass, instead of looking blurry now had more defenition. Everything looked much cleaner including Player character models.
Many of the bugs to this point had been fixed. I also noticed new paths throughout the Faydark areas making travel much easier so you didn't get lost quite as much. From what I hear, there were quite a few changes made to the discerning eye. Overall it was an impressive jump from one phase to the next and I greatly look forward to seeing how Phase 4 looks. I can honestly say this game will put you in a whole new world. For those of you looking for the Halfling Pimp Class, i'm sorry but they haven't added that yet.
After playing this game for hours on end, where food and water are annoyances and all you want to do is play, I have noticed a few flaws. Believe me, these are incredibly minor glitches, some regarding gameplay and others not. First and foremost is one you've probably heard if you have been visiting any of the message boards. Spawn Camping has become quite in vogue in EQ right now, where players would run from one camp to another waiting for spawns. The problem with this is that you would have 6 players waiting in line for 3 creatures to spawn. I swear one time I thought I saw Mickey Mouse walk by. It felt like you were waiting in a line at Disney.

Brad: We are doing two things to address this issue.    First, we're not done putting items and treasure and such in, so by release there will be many more 'spawn' points, which should spread the treasure seekers out quite a bit more.  Secondly, we are experimenting with some anti-camping measures, such that items only appear on an NPC every so often, as opposed to a % chance.  What this means is that once you've received an item of note from an NPC's corpse, you're not going to see it again for some time.  The group might as well move on to another spot.   We hope this will get people moving around more.

This was my greatest worry to this point in Everquest. Beyond this point everything else was minor annoyances. My next concern was regarding Quests and how they would be handled and integrated into Everquest. Obviously we aren't going to be searching down Crack dealing Orcs here but I was wondering how they would be handled. I made an example to Brad that if you were to return to your Guild Master after having leveled up and the Guild Master may say something like:

"thou art a budding adventurer to our guild, twould be wise of you to venture further into the world into the lands of Dwarves and kill the Goblins that have been plaguing their lands and mayhaps it would also be wise to employee the help of other adventuers in your cause. Return to me or others of our order when  you feel you have helped cleanse the lands further evil"

Thus giving your character and yourself some purpose in the world other than simply getting lost and wondering where to go next rather than getting stuck sitting around spawn camps. After all this is EverQUEST.

Brad: I really think we can address a lot of that by just putting in more and more quests, some of which working the way you've described with guildmasters and all.  Quests are being entered into the world daily, and will continue to be up to and beyond release (which is something great online games can do).

Something else that I really didn't notice until I reached about 8th level is that once you do get into higher levels of play Health takes a while to regenerate. Literally you could have a few woman over for an orgy all the while waiting for your Health to regenerate. "Hold on girls, let me see......ah ok, only halfway there, we've got plenty of time". 

Brad: We will be experimenting with different healing rates based on level.  That said, it's still very key to be grouping with healers of some sort (shaman, cleric, bard) at higher levels.

Ok so he's got a point there. My last minor problem was in the Interface. To navigate Everquest particular in closed and confined areas you have to use the directional keys to move forward etc. Well, when your in the heat of battle it kind of looks like you are going into Epileptic conculsions on your keyboard trying to contort your body just enough to hit that key on the other end of the keyboard. I swear I think I would rather have an alien rectal probe than doing that at times.

Brad: Have you tried the mouselook functionality?   I think it needs some tweaking here and there, but overall is very usable.

Although with that said, it does become easier down the road and less cumbersome. Particularly learning the Auto-run feature which to most EQ players is a gift from the gods. Remember those times in Diablo or UO where you had to stop and type just to get a message to your friends? In EQ you set Auto-run and off you go, your legs doing all the work while you type. That is an incredibly cool feature and makes the Interface seem just a tad bit less clunky.
So there you've got it. The only real problems that "I" saw in the game. This game is much more than simply a game, it's an experience. To find yourself running maniacly through the forest screaming at the top of your lungs, er Keyboard, while being chased by a few blood hungry Orcs wanting to have you and your Halfling buddy for lunch is exhilarating to say the least. To be standing in front of Kaladim looking at the Giant Dwarven statue in front of their city is awe-inspiring at best. To find yourself driving down the street in your car and spotting someone that looks vaguely like an Ogre and wanting to drive them over is disconcerting by far. The best way for me to cap this off is to let Brad do the job with his thoughts about the game.......

It's definitely a labor of love :)
