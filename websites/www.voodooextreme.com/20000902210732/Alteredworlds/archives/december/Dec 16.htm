UO first year Interview - Sacrates
Those boys and girls over at VN have kicked up an Interview with Rich Vogel about UO, it's first year and what's to come. Personally I think UO has come a long way since it began.They have done a slap up job keeping that beast up and running.

Jonric: What do you feel went well during the year? Were there any pleasant surprises? Did anything not go as well as you would have liked? Were there any unpleasant surprises?

Rich Vogel: That the crafting skills and home ownership were so well received by our players was a pleasant surprise. We feel strongly that ownership and identity are vitally important traits in an online game and we think UO did a good job supporting those characteristics. On the other hand, we had to learn simulations are not always "fun" in a gaming environment. There is, of course, a balance. You can design nice systems that give a game elements of simulation but are still fun for the player.

With the economic and ecology systems, we found we had too much simulation. At any given time, we have a lot of players in the game, and as these players hunted creatures in the old system, they would often force many areas to become extinct because the ecology couldn't support the traffic. This frustrated players since they could not always get the raw materials they needed. We also found that players wanted NPC shopkeepers to buy items from them at reasonable prices even if the item is not in demand. Although that's not realistic, it's frustrating if you are unable to get money for excess equipment. But these examples are all a matter of course, and part of the challenge and excitement of learning how to develop a successful model for online gaming. The only truly unpleasant experience was the lawsuit. The team felt that it was unjustified and that frustration really lowered morale. It was like working with this dark cloud hanging over our heads..

Looks like Jason and the other Halfling Gimps over at PC Fan have dished up even more EQ lovin for us non-beta playing chimps. This time they go into High level playing in the world. Here's a blurb.

In the world of EverQuest lies a continent of massive proportions. Its name is Antonica and it's the largest landmass one can explore in all of Norrath. Chris and I have been gallivanting around Antonica's countryside for quite some time now, and we know we've only begun to scratch the surface.

U:A explosion - Sacrates
You want U:A news? Well, here is a literal explosion of posts that happened over on the message boards lately. Hold onto your butts for this big boy. First we have Trellek on game physics. Thanks to Sith's U:A love page.

Moving a box out from under a stack of boxes was one of the reasons we decided to implement a physics system that allows objects to rotate. Objects in Ultima 8, in contrast, could only slide around, since the art was done as 2D sprites and the collision system only worked with cubes.

Although Ascension has a rudimentary physics system, it isn't the focus of the game and doesn't have all the features of a physics-based game. Barrels are really polygonal rather than round, so they don't roll all that well. Pivots aren't designed into the system as currently implemented. However, objects do react and rebound when they hit, and float or sink in water depending on their weight and volume.

Here's some from Jaccl on the monster AI.

Several of us are currently working on honing creature AI. As far as magic creatures are concerned, there are a variety, some of them are smart, some of them are not. The smart creatures will use their resources in an intelligent fashion. There are definitely 'mages' in the game thou not all of them are the classical humanoid type. :)

And Yavn on where the heck are Britain's Rivers.

You want to see a shot of a river? We will try a picture of a river in the next set of screens we release.

Ask and you shall receive!

We are working on something for the fans, and will let you know the moment it's ready...

A bit on the Codex.

Yes, the Avatar will need to follow the 8 virtues. Yes, the Codex will play an important part in the story. And yes, the 2 lenses are also going to be important. I don't want to give away too much more, but I hope that answers some of your questions.

Here's some on Clothing options in the game also.

Sorry to disappoint, but we currently have no plans for capes. We do have over 10 different sets of armor for the Avatar, so he will have lots of cool things to wear.

An update on the Development team and how things go.

Right now as we approach the holidays, things are going very well. As in any development cycle there are many ups and downs. The design is really coming together, and we are going through all the systems to make sure they are perfect.

I truly believe that this is the best team in Ultima history, and when we finish, you will not be disappointed. Keep up the questions, and we will do our best to answer them.

Demo Developments.

The topic of a demo is something we talk about all the time. What it will be, and when it will be out, I can't answer at this time. I know that's not what you want to hear, but until we have the "Right" demo (People got the wrong impression from the demo we showed at E3), we will keep working on it. We will let you know as soon as we have the answers.

Talking about NPC Plot holes etc.

We are going to do our best to keep plot holes / NPC holes out of the game. We have some experts on the problem (Craig Bowlin, Chuck Zoch) who have worked with conversation systems on many games before. We have already started going through the story, and each of the designers is taking extra time to wipe out problems.

Some juiciness on combat.

Not sure I really want to touch this one... Yes, you will need to use both the mouse and keyboard for combat, although we believe that what we have come up with works very well, and is not arcade-like. IronFury was correct about real time vs. turn based combat. We have spoken about this quite a bit, and I have no new information to pass on. When we finish combat, I'll give an update, and take your questions.

Accesability to newbies.

We have made an extra effort to give new players, and those who have missed an Ultima or 2, all the history of the world. There will be no need to have any past Ultima experience, although the people who have played Ultimas before will see a lot of old friends, and be privy to in jokes.

A bit on Release parties.

I'm sure when Ascension ships we will have one HUGE party...The fans will have to wait for us to recover before we make any patches!!

Classes in EQ - Sacrates
I missed this blurb yesterday from Mahrin Skel over at EQ Vault in their EQ Guidebook. He go's over the advantages and disadvantages of certain classes in Norath. Head on over for this fairly informative read.

Warrior:

989 says that "Warriors are the masters of armed combat, in all its many forms. They are at home on the battlefield, and are trained to take punishment as much as dole it out, so their Hit Points are the highest of any class."

I say that Warriors are just about the easiest class to play. It's straightforward, you walk up to something and start hitting it until either it falls down or you have to run away. If you play with a spellcaster in your group (highly recommended you have a magi and a clerical caster), you use Taunt and try to keep all of the mobs focused on you. You get a wide selection of toys, can wield almost any weapon and wear nearly any piece of armor.

Warriors are built to dish out and take punishent in melee battles, and they do it well. However, if you don't have a Cleric along, you spend *outrageous* amounts of time sitting on your tail between fights, regaining your health. If you don't have a Magi caster along, you wind up chasing down "runners", praying that they die before they reach reinforcements.
