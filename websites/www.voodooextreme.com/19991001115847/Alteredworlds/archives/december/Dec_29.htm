BG goodness - Sacrates
About time someone started posting again on the Baldur's Gate boards. Here are a few tasty posts to keep you guys hyped who haven't gotten the game yet, and also for those that do have it. First one is on Killing important NPC's and what will happen in an Expansion.

If a character is killable in BG (as far as I know, all are) they will NOT be able to play a critical role in the expansion unless we have all options considered.

(For example, if someone kills Thalantyr, but we have built into the expansion that he gets ressed or something and becomes a mortal enemy of yours - it would be easy enough to work out)

Problems from Game to Expansion. The big word on killing Drizzt is "IF".

Let's try this again...

If you kill Drizzt, he MAY not be in your expansion game.

Not saying he WILL be, of course, but we are working to tie the expansion game VERY closely with what happens in the original game.

If some of your NPC's die in Baldur's Gate, don't expect to see them suddenly alive and happy in Tales of the Sword Coast...

What's the name of the damn expansion pack and beyond that?

It is called "Tales of the Sword Coast"

We are still working out the details for additional BG products. There appears to be interest in an additional expansion, but we don't know if this is going to happen or not. Nothing has been decided...

Here's a bit on Paladins.

Couple of things...

We were not able to implement paladins EXACTLY the way they are in the handbook, so we made allowances. First of all, both Protection from Evil and Detect Evil skills that paladins have in BG are MUCH more powerful than either of these skills in the player's handbook - in BG this is balanced by their not being able to use them constantly (though their abilities increase with levels).

To offset this, as well as a few other things (the lack of a warhorse, for example), we gave paladins the ability to specialize in weapons (we're talking about a bonus to hit and damage, as well as an extra half attack per round).

Paladins are NOT a weak class in BG. At higher levels, I think paladins are stronger in BG than in the handbook...

How is the game selling and when do we get more?

All should have it by this weekend...assuming that we don't sell out again ;)

We are shipping more product this week, now that everyone is back from Christmas and our production and shipping facilities are back in full swing.

How close to the rule book are they?

However, I would assume YES unless came up with a REALLY good reason to change these (which Wizards of the Coast would have to approve). Note were are trying to do the best job of bringing AD&D to the computer world, NOT reinventing AD&D (or coming out with 3rd edition rules or something). If it is in the rules, assume it will be in the expansions unless told otherwise.

Finally a bit on Ranger penalties.

There is a "too hit" penalty added for every creature between you and your target.

There is no point blank bonus but this is a melee range penalty. You try shooting someone with a bow when they are close enough to grab it.

EQ and Spawn camping - Sacrates
I was getting a bit fiesty today wondering what and if anything were being done on a problem I am greatly worried about. So I took my EQ lovin Chimply ass over to the boards and slapped up a message to the guides. Without further ado mine first and Thorim's in Bold.

I have quite a few friends currently in Beta 3 at the moment (I am not) and all of them are ecstatic about EQ. However, many of them have been griping about one thing particularly. Camping at Spawn locations. This could get quite ugly if nothing is done about it. Already I am hearing many arguments and players screaming at each other on this matter. "That was my creature" or "this is my spawn".
I hear the spawns are at pre-set locations in the world? Once B4 starts or the consumer version is released do you have plans on randomizing spawn locations? I hear some sections of the game have been camped barren from players sitting on Spawn locations. This, to me, is possibly the most alarming thing I have heard yet about the game.

Some fixed spawns have this 'camping' problem, and various solutions are being discussed.

I have confidence that this will be solved before final, but it is a concern at the moment, yes.

Be assured that it is a known issue, 989 has been very good about listening to us (the testers) and coming up with solutions based on our input.

BG Tech tips - Sacrates
Have you been one of the few taking it up the rear with Technical probs? Has Baldur's Gate been getting medevil with your back end and you don't like it? Well, it's time to fight back. Mathew Bryne was on the developers board with this goodness for the BG butt buddies out there. Enjoy.

This is our preliminary troubleshooting guide for users who may be experiencing random crashes. We are doing our best to try and compile everything into one document. As additional information is gathered, including specific information relating to specific hardware, it will be added.

Random Crashes/Problems

1. Verify that you have DirectX6.0 installed. The newest version of Microsofts DirectX can always be found at www.microsoft.com/directx. DirectX 6.0 was also included on the Baldurs Gate CD-ROM.

2. Verify that all of your current drivers are DirectX certified. Information on this can be found at www.interplay.com/support/directx/

3. If applicable, compare the driver version you are using with another users posted and working driver information. This is available at www.interplay.com/support/bgate/hardware.html. These are driver sets sent in by customers experiencing no problems. If yours does not match, contact your manufacturer for the specific version.

4. Shutdown all programs running in the background. To verify you have no remaining TSRs, use ‘CTRL-ALT-DELETE’ to bring up your task manager. Disable or shutdown any utility programs that might be causing a conflict.

5. From the readme.txt file - Some video cards may experience graphics glitches while playing the game. These problems are caused by DirectX Hardware Calls to your video card that are not functioning. You can enable a software render for these calls if you have the most recent drivers for your video card and are still experiencing them. Using the software render will fix the graphic glitches, but will result in a performance hit. IF YOU ARE EXPERIENCING PROBLEMS WITH THE GRAPHICAL DISPLAY OF BALDUR'S GATE, PLEASE MAKE SURE YOU HAVE THE LATEST VERSION OF YOUR VIDEO DRIVERS. DRIVER UPDATES ARE AVAILABLE AT MOST (if not all) HARDWARE MANUFACTURERS AND CHIPSET MANUFACTURERS SITES ON THE INTERNET. If you have the most recent drivers for your video card, and you are still experiencing problems, you can enable software renders for these calls. The options are available in the options menu under 'Graphics'. The following list describes the known graphics glitches and which call should be disabled to fix them.

Green boxes surrounding options –Disable Software Transparent BLT
Animations appearing reversed – Disable Software Mirror BLT
Glitches with the Fog-of-War – Disable Software Mirror BLT
Vertical Lines down the main game screen – Software Standard BLT
Mouse trailer is leaving graphic problems – Software Standard BLT

6. Try disabling or enabling Direct3D or DirectDraw. These options are available by running DXTOOL. (Start/Find/Files or Folders/DXTOOL.EXE)

7. Try disabling EAX or Environmental Audio Sound. This option is available on the Sound screen of the games main ‘Option’ Selection.

8. Verify that your in-game resolution matches your system resolution. (i.e., 16bit for both.)

9. Verify that you have enough available hard drive space. The games cache requires a minimum of 80 meg available. Please take into account that the windows swap file will also require its own space.

This information will also be placed in the troubleshooting section of the Baldurs Gate support page, and will be reposted periodically.

Return to Reviewing Krondor - Sacrates
Right after I said I wouldn't post a few thousand Reviews of the same title I go and post this Review. Don't start jumping on good ole Sacrates like the Skipper and a full course meal Yet. OGR has a tendency to write up some good Reviews and this is another good example to that rule. With that said, they do the deed to Raymond E Fiest's Return to Krondor and come to the same conclusions we did in our own Review....it's pretty tasty although somewhat short.

Does Return to Krondor live up to the grand expectations? You bet your enchanted broadsword it does. It isn't perfect, but it's very engaging; if you didn't know about the rocky development road this game has taken, you'd never guess from the final product. Raymond E. Feist's world of Midkemia is back, along with the dashing Squire James, plenty of open-ended exploration, and strong narrative structures worthy of a novel. There are also some new twists, most noticeably the excellent 3D graphics (card supported but not required) and a clever trap-disabling system used for accessing chests. The real star of a good Krondor game, however, is the story, and this edition serves up plenty of it, piping-hot.

QFG Reviewed - Sacrates
Ah just another Quest for Glory Review coming your way. It seems everyone has the same opinion of it that I have seen. Gamespot has done the Reviewing in this case and have come to the same conclusion as the rest. Blah. Here's a biscuit for ya.

I really enjoyed my first ten hours of Quest for Glory V, but as the game wore on, I became more frustrated by the pace of the game and the pixel-hunting instances of gameplay. A better interface, a quest log that showed you what you had to do, and better handling of combat would have alleviated some of my concerns. I did finish the game, and I enjoyed it overall, but the ending was definitely disappointing. As the final farewell to Hero, this game should have at least included an epilogue of some sort that let you know what happened to Hero and all his companions. Quest for Glory V has a few quirks and problems that will try your patience, but series fans will want to play this game to see old friends and follow Hero's last adventure.

EQ bit - Sacrates
Thorim made a brief visit to the boards again and had a bit to say about Pets in EQ and their uses. I wouldn't mind having a pet big breasted Nymph? I could train her to do all sorts of things! Hell, I could even write a book called "1,001 ways to enjoy your big breasted Nymph".

A pet when used properly, will allow a magician/necro to advance much faster than the same character without a pet.

Yes you share experience, but you share experience for rougly 3-4x the number of kills when properly employed, which gives you roughly 1.5-2x the experience you would get running without the pet.

Its all in how you use it.
