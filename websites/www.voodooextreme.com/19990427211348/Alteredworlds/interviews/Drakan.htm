Drakan: The Interview
By the Sultan of Smack: Sacrates
With: Stuart Denman

There are times in a gamers life that come close to religious experiences. That moment when you feel almost total immersion into a games environment. The moment when even a good Blow up Doll will loose it's luster. I have seen the light and it's name is Drakan. Haven't you ever wondered after reading all those Fantasy novels what it would be like to ride on the back of a Dragon? Let us take you on a ride into the world of Drakan.

Sacrates: Alright, let's hit the basics first. Who the heck are you and what the heck are you doing? (position on the team etc.)

Stu: Most of the work I've done has been game and graphics programming on my own and with Surreal for almost 14 years.  After graduating with a Computer Engineering degree (specializing in computer graphics) at the University of Washington, I started Surreal with three other founders.  My official label of the month is Vice President and Technical Director, but my main duties are as Lead Programmer on Drakan.

Sacrates: Just the name Drakan gets me all warm and fuzzy without the use of Alcoholic beverages or mind altering drugs. How do you think this game will stand up in todays 3D market? (For those of us that don't know about it)

Stu: You can do some pretty crazy moves when flying Arokh that areguaranteed to give you a good head-rush.  We've made every aspect of Drakan, from the graphics to the sound, to the gameplay a unique and mind-blowing experience.  Drakan is not a copycat game, you can't really compare it as a whole to other games, so it will easily rock the 3D competition.

Sacrates: Alright let's start slapping the Engine around a little bit.
Starting with the basics first. Do you guys expect to support Voodoo 3 and will the game utilize any of it's newer features? I assume it will already be running the V2 card on the mouse wheel at full throttle.

Stu: We're definitely supporting all the high-end cards with bountifulgraphic elegance like high-polygon counts and over 10 MB of high-resolution true-color textures per level.  Of course, the engine can scale down these figures for lower-end machines so you'll still be able to play great with a Voodoo 1 or Riva128.  The engine also support lots of jucy multi-texturing effects like colored shadow/light-maps and detail textures.  Voodoo3 and TNT do this in a single-pass.

Sacrates: Will there be Bump mapping and any other of the pretty V2/V3 features added?

Stu: Bump mapping will hopefully be supported for at least the characters. It's a time issue, so we're hoping to fit it in.  Detail texturing and other single-pass multitexturing effects are already looking amazing!

Sacrates: I got to play a bit of this beast at E3 earlier this year and
although crashed your machines twice enjoyed doing it immensely. (playing the game that is ;)) I noticed the play mechanics were kind of awkward at the time. Using the keyboard etc, will this game support all kinds of various input devices such as Keyboards, Joysticks, Mice, etc etc.

Stu: Yes, those devices will be supported.  Our controls are almost exactly like Quake, with the addition of 'up' and 'down' keys for Arokh, which double as jump and crouch when controlling Rynn.  Other movement keys are strafe left-right, forward-back, and walk (sneak).  All controls are customizable and can be assigned to joystick/mouse buttons instead of keys. Surreal is big on controls, if a game is inconsistent and hard to control, then it is usually not very fun to play!

Sacrates: Can we expect any kinds of Interesting weather effects in the game such as Rain, Snow, strong winds etc. Will there be any kind of turbulance that the dragon will hit while flying in the air?

Stu: Yes!  We have wind, rain with fractal lightning striking the ground in 3D (right in front of you sometimes), and snow that looks incredibly realistic!  Flying through the snow with Arokh looks amazing!

Sacrates: I noticed the great looking waterfalls in the game while flying around. Will the Drakan engine support rippling water, possibly adding waves also?

Stu: The tropical islands have lots of wave effects in the oceans.  For rivers and streams, I've created a procedural water effect that creates waves and interference patterns in the water.  Water also creates caustic reflections on the walls and ceilings of caves, which is very cool.

Sacrates: What kind of environmental effects can we expect other than rain etc. Will there be birds (Other than dragons) flying in the skies or simple animals on the ground to add to the atmoshphere?

Stu: Yes, Tim has created a flocking AI that we use for birds, fish, bats, and bees, which are mostly ambient.  We also have yak-like creatures that the locals (in Drakan) keep.  They run away if you try to hurt them. Scavengers aren't really ambient because they attack you, but they cruise around and search for dead things to eat.

Sacrates: How many types of environments will there be in the game? So far I have only noticed a few types, specifically the mountanous areas etc.

Stu: Yeah, there have only been screenshots of the mountain areas, but the game has mountains, snowy mountains, mines, tropical islands, underground river, volcanic, and one other world that we aren't revealing yet.

Sacrates: Will there be day and night effects in the game and what kind of lighting will you be using in the engine? Strobe lights, lens flares, shadowing etc?

Stu: Yes, lighting will change depending on the time of day and weather (fog, also).  We have dynamic lights with varying qualities (like caustic reflections for cave water).  We also have other goodies like lens flares, full-screen lighting effects, and dynamic shadows that crawl along surfaces and actually follow the light sources!  Our real-time character lighting is the best we've seen in any game to date, all seamless on our single-skin characters.

Sacrates: Obviously a popular question. What will the system requirements be for Drakan and what kind of Frame rates can we expect?

Stu: Minimum requirements are a Pentium 166 with at least a Voodoo 1 or Riva128.  Frame rate on this low-end machine is 15-20+, but the recommended spec is a Pentium 233 with Voodoo 2 or TNT where we're getting 30-40 FPS with shadows and all multi-texturing effects turned on.

Sacrates: Will the Interface be fairly customizable? And will we be able to control all of our video settings and features?

Stu: Of course.  Any command in the game can be bound to any key or mouse/joystick button.  Tons of video and graphics settings can be tweaked to get optimal look and performance on whatever machine you're running.

Sacrates: A very popular question at the moment especially after the release of Half Life which did things with sound that have never been done so well before. Can we expect full 3D sound support in Drakan and what standard will you support? Both A3D and EAX? Personally I use EAX so when you send me an evaluation/demo copy of Drakan (hint hint.....) make sure you have it in there for me. :)

Stu: Yes, Drakan supports full 3D Sound with Doppler effects and support for both Creative Labs EAX and Aureal cards. At COMDEX, Creative Labs was using Drakan to show off EAX. When Rynn walks into a cave, for example, the sounds of her footsteps, water dripping, etc., all take on an eerie echo effect.

Sacrates: Do you guys plan on having an open sort of Beta test for Drakan's Multiplayer or will it be closed?

Stu: It's a closed beta, sorry. But there will be a public demo closer to when it is released.

Sacrates: What type of combat can we expect to see? Traditional sword fighting on the ground or dropping Dragon dung from the sky? (Hey could be affective for something with very strong sense of smell like my girlfriend for example)

Stu: Our sword fighting combat is more fast paced action sword-fighting.  We use Quake-style controls, so there's a lot of dodging, strafing, and rolling.  We focus more on movement than having hundreds of attacks.  We have around 10 different sword attacks, then lots of evasive moves that can create more attacks like crouching, crouching rolls, flips, flip and reverse (all in every direction).   We also have several different bow and arrow attacks.  Oh, and don't forget spells, including earthquake, lightning, ring of fire, tornado, and a few other surprises.

Sacrates: Can we expect to hear environmental music at all? Will the music change according to what is happening? I noticed one thing while at the Drakan booth at E3...you guys had a killer high fantasy track kicking in there. Will it be that type of music?

Stu: The game will use DirectMusic and has a powerful fantasy style to it, but the music is mostly being used to add mood and to enhance the gameplay experience.  In general, we want the player to feel like they are truly within the world of Drakan by letting them hear ambient sounds like birds, rivers, and distant creatures.

Sacrates: How open will the game be? It seemed like I could fly anywhere when I played a bit of it. Will it be like Trespasser with large open areas but on a fairly linear path. Or will it be totally open to exploration?

Stu: There are large open areas with different paths, but the paths where you can go are pretty distinct.  Most levels have a map, so it's hard to get lost.  Each area has a lot to explore before you have to open up the next area.  For example, the islands levels are pretty open for exploration, but the story leads you to want to fly to particular islands.

Sacrates: Will there be different types of Dragons in the game and will we be able to customize them before playing in Multiplayer? Color, weight, speed, etc etc.

Stu: Drakan has several different dragon skins for multiplayer, including Arokh, a Skeletal Dragon, and others.  These hulking dragons also show up in the single-player game.  You won't be able to customize their speeds, as that would be cheating, and nobody likes a cheater.  You can pick up all kinds of killer dragon-breath weapons, however, which relies more on skill and sheer determination!  

Sacrates: How will this game appeal to many RPG fans since it seems more on an adventure action path? Will there be inventory and NPC interaction etc?

Stu: The NPC interactions are done through in-game cut-scenes using the 3D engine.  We have a full Diablo-style interface with all kinds of weapons and items to collect.  Weapons have damage, durability, magic effects, and other statistics.  Items are used for quests and spell casting, which should be another thing RPG fans love!

Sacrates: That will about cap it off. One last thing, about how far are you into development at the moment percentage wise? When can we expect to seeDrakan on shelves. (Don't say when it's done please!)

Stu: We're about 80% done, and we're looking at an April '99 worldwide release!
