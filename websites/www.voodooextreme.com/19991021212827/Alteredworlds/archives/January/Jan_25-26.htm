Bit O' BG - Sacrates
Rob Bartel hit the boards and had a bit of a response to one of it's readers. Looks like he got a little finger happy so expect it to be fairly long.

Mr. NoName, your point regarding the Phase Spiders is an important one and valid. To my knowledge, this specific issue is not addressed in the upcoming patch (though I am admittedly out of loop, as patch issues are being dealt with by others higher up the design chain).

The issue of when a creature 'sees' you and when they don't is more complex than it first appears, however. The final battle, for instance, would lose a lot of significance if the party could simply sneak in and obliterate the end boss (sorry, no spoilers here, people) before he could get a word in edgewise about you, about himself, about the nature of the past you have inherited. The city of Baldur's Gate would be a meaningless maze if Scar did not approach you upon entering and lend you at least a small degree of guidance. There are countless similar examples. For reasons such as these, we made the decision we did regarding the initiation of dialogue with hidden characters.

Unfortunately, that meant treating hidden PCs in a very specific way in the code. While my grasp of programming is severely limited, it is my understanding that reactions to hidden PCs were divided into two basic categories: combat actions (which are specifically dealt with under the AD&D rules) and dialogue actions (which, being a lot more amorphous, we treated as a default setting of sorts). As you have seen for yourself, the 'phase' action of the Phase Spiders (and a number of other things that have come up in discussion of this string) was never transferred from its classification as default action to that of combat action. This is, indeed, regrettable.

As for using the girdle of femininity / masculinity to get past the assassin, what a wonderful idea and I, for one, am kicking myself for not having had the forethought to accomodate such creative problem-solving into that specific subplot (although, would Khalid and Jaheira still have recognized you?). On the other hand, there is simply no way to program each and every character in the game to have separate dialogue for you being of a different gender (or having taken the form of a wolf, a bear, a squirrel, whatever) than they expected. As it was, the text dump for the game was roughly 450,000 words (the equivalent of two or three good-sized novels!), all of which had to be translated into a variety of different languages. There simply was not time, space, or money for such an undertaking.

That in mind, those of us in the design department have put some serious thought into how to improve the issue of when creatures 'see' you. Hopefully you will be getting a taste of that when the proposed sequel is released (and no, don't ask me when or even if -- I haven't a clue and won't for quite some time... =) Some of our thoughts have involved reversing the default settings in the code and allowing you to walk past most dialogue encounters undisturbed. We would then give specific override scripts to those carrying plot critical dialogue. In this way, we hope to make the unfortunate bending of the rules the exception, rather than the norm. If that is the case, however, we do intend to make some further design decisions to compensate for this new strength of Stealth (no more going into stealth mode in the middle of bright sunlight when standing in front of a neutral character who you have every intention of backstabbing, for instance. *wink*).

So, until then, enjoy the game and remember that, in all your armor and with the sweat of countless adventures clinging to your unwashed back, are you at all surprised that the people and spiders know you're about? ;)

All the best and I hope this clears at least a few things up

Odium under fire - Sacrates
Those wacky cats over at The Firing Squad have gone off the deepend and poked up a bit of Preview lovin on Odium. Now here is a game with some potential. Believe me, these guys better have lofty standards after EQ and BG.

Of course, what's a game without combat? As far as we can tell, exploration and combat in Odium are seamlessly mixed in the game play, just as it is in Baldur's Gate or Diablo. You'll not be whisked away into a different interface in order to fight monsters as you would in say, Final Fantasy 7. Those kinds of interruptions can give a game that "choppy" feeling, which the developers do well to avoid in Odium. With the seamless transitions from exploration to combat and back, players can focus more on plot development. This kind of commitment to the story is also evident in how Odium handles important transitions in the game. When you run into a "boss" monster, or trigger some other key part of the game, the camera angle will change or zoom in to give you a different view of the environment. It also helps inform you that you have in fact reached an important part and you should pay closer attention.

Daily EQ love - Sacrates
Abashi was back and in force today. He had the fingers primed and ready to respond to all the inquiring minds on the boards. Unless of course he has Voice Recognition software. (The Bastard) Anyway, here is a bit on Monks and dual wielding.

Yes, monks get dual wield at first level. This is so they can swing both fists. They can also wield 1hB and 2hB weapons. After about 10th level though, they do best hand to hand. If you don't work on your hand-to-hand skill prior to that, you'll be one sorry monk.

Here he is on PvP Spells affects and NPC.

Spell damage will not be altered based on the PvP setting of the target or caster. It functions identically whether against monster or person. PvP battles in EverQuest will be primarily against people who want to fight each other, or, a balanced group against some lone wandering PvPer.

Yes, group balance is important in PvP as well. Keep in mind though, it will be easier to kill a player of a given level than to kill an NPC of a given level. The NPCs have way more hitpoints and hit harder.

3rd Party Proggies in Everquest.

Regardless the type of video card you use, you will not be able to alt-tab out or alt-enter into a window with EverQuest. It is the first step against limiting these third party programs from emerging.

There are other things in place to futher limit this, as well as things to detect usage of such a program and let the staff know.

On the upcoming player wipe. Personally I wouldn't mind seeing a clean slate going into P4.

Testers will be allowed to carry one character from P3.x to P4. The character carried over will depend on the server you register your character on when registrations open.

There will most likely be what we call an economic wipe. Meaning that you'll get to take one character, but all money/items will be gone save a small reimbursement.

System Shock 2 First Look - Sacrates
Well, Looks like Sharky Extreme just slapped up their First Look at System Shock 2. Here's another game that's got everyone's Underwear bunched up like only a good Bull Ride could do. Stop by and check it.

First things first, System Shock's engine now looks about as trendy as an 80s Escada suit complete with shoulder pads. Yes it's really aged that much, we're sorry to say. In an effort to bring the game bang up to date, Looking Glass will be using a modified version of the Dark engine, which was actually the 3D engine used for Thief: The Dark Project (which was plum). The engine can render in full 16-bit color (no 32-bit has been mentioned as yet) and will take full advantage of the current crop of 3D graphics accelerator cards (no surprises there then). A full array of special effects will be ever-present including, colored lighting, variable translucency objects and (everyone's favorite) particle effects for explosions. Basically the game should look nothing short of drop dead gorgeous.

ME and Quests - Sacrates
Stephen Nichols was on the boards dishing out even more info on how quests etc will be handled in Middle Earth. By the way kids, don't even start expecting this title any time soon. We're talking late 99 for a possible Beta and then early to mid 2000 for full release.

You will not gain money in Middle-earth through quests alone, but through trading with others and participating in the economy in any way that suits you. Middle-earth will definately be quest based however. While you can increase your skills through practice, you will not gain any new abilities based on those skills without finishing quests. If you want to learn how to make a new piece of armor, you will need the appropriate skill level AND be taught how to craft the item from a NPC. We want to provide both a free form environment for the hard-core players AND a more structured goal oriented environment for the casual player.

It will also not be possible to be 100 times more powerful than a newbie. We are being very careful in considering all gameplay elements to keep the standard super-high-level-killing-machine syndrome out of Middle-earth.
