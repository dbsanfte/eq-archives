BG Tips - Sacrates
Yeah I know it, Baldur's Gate is not exactly a romp through the proverbial Frey. This is no Diablo that's for sure. Actually when you come right down to it, Baldur's Gate is pretty damn tough. With those words of wisdom I am going to toss a few tips your way if your having any probs. Just a few goodies for ya.
1. Learn to live on the Space Bar and pause the game in combat. This is absolutely essential as you can't handle combat like Diablo and just jump in. Otherwise your gonna get an ass kicking like you've never gotten before. Not to mention reloading the game every few minutes!
2. Make sure you have ranged fighters early on. Grab yourself a Ranger or anyone who is fairly proficient in a bow. Imomen can handle herself pretty well with the bow so use her.
3. Pimp on as many locals as possible and grab a few side quests for extra Cash flow. Basically talk to anyone who has a name other than commoner.
4. Improve your Rep early. Do anything you can to improve your reputation with locals! This is an absolute must. Or at least have a high Charisma leader! I noticed the game is completely different when you play with a character of low charisma, everyone treats you like mud.
5. Find as much combat early on in the game to Improve your level as it takes a loooong time to level up. Not kidding here...it took at least 12 hours of playing time just to get to 2nd level with my Fighter/Mage.

BG Goods - Sacrates
Jst noticed a few more posts on the boards by the BG gods of programming. BTW I just got to the Cemetary in one of the Towns and noticed that all the Tombstones have sayings on them with all the programmers listed! Very cool! Ray had his very own Tomb himself. First Luke jumps on a bit about a possible Sequal. Hey, we all know it's coming damnit but it's always good to hear in advance.

We pay attention to all suggestions on the boards, and we will likley be looking at additional classes for a potential sequel. As I have said before though, the basic classes will come first. There are a lot of perks and changes that AD&D characters go through around the higher levels, and we want to make sure we draw from them.

I'd love a few kits myself though :)

Here is Don talking a bit on Multiplayer. This might be an acquired taste for some people but basically it's just Single player with a friend or two or three.

Multiplayer works just like single player except that instead of you controlling a party member your friend does. In its simpliest form think of it as a party member with a very intelligent AI. In reality it can add alot to the game and make it more interesting, especially when you work together.

As far as having 5 people in your party the beginning. Very quickly, you can have 5 NPC's in your party so there really is only a slight difference.

When you meet NPC's you have the option of kicking an existing member out and replacing. One of the players will then assume control of this person.

If you decide to play the game solo for awhile, you can go right ahead, now you control all 5 players. Once your friends come back they can resume control.

You are playing with the same party members whether you are controlling all 5 members or they are all controlled individually so there is no such thing as your character being lvl 4 and your friend having a lvl 1. The whole party comes with you regardless of how many players are controlling the game.

It's all about options. If you want to give your players full control you can, if you don't want to give them buy and sell priveleges you can do that to.

On the mission pack. Wow! Already 50% done!!

Don't worry, we're hard at work at the mission pack (already 50% done). The mission pack is going to rock. The art is superior to the first game, and we're trying to do some new things with the A.I. (among many other things).

Want to Print out your character for all the other chimps to see?

Yes, you can take a screenshot of your character sheet and print it out. Thanks for correcting my earlier response. Perhaps in BG2 we'll have an in game method of printing your character sheet.

Even more EQ - Sacrates
Continuing with our daily serving of EQ News fresh off the grill, we have a few posts made by none other than the squid miester himself Brad Mcquaid and his henchmen in crime Guide Abashi. First we have Brad on why you don't see numbers and stats.

The reason we don't post numbers and percentages and such is because we change them in beta. That's what beta is for, to adjust and tune.

Next Abashi jumps into the proverbial Frey with this bit on mapping.

There will be no mapping system or radar in EQ, hence, I suggest you spend time each level practicing your "Sense Direction" skill.

There is currently a command that pulls your xyz-coord in the current zone, but it will be removed for final.

Here he is again talking about level loss after dying. I totally dig this idea myself. I want Death to hurt like a Mofo.

Death can cause a level loss, but will never cause multiple level loss. It has less bite from levels 4-24, with a setback of a few hours to a day or two of gameplay at higher levels depending on your playstyle. Death bites :) Also, it's not uncommon for someone to die several more times while trying to retrieve a corpse. This *really* hurts.

Cleric will have some resurrection ability, some with the capability to reduce the death bite, but never completely and will be a very expensive spell to cast.

There is also the potential for a completely unrecoverable corpse. It doesn't happen often, but when it does it hurts.

So where are we at in Beta phases damnit?

Yes, it is still phase three, and last night it became evident that some more people have been invited quite recently.
