MOE Preview - Sacrates
Was over at Billy's place and noticed that Riva 3D just smacked up a Preview of Mask of Eternity. It's good to see the King's Quest series really evolving this way. You know the crazy thing is that nobody is having a tissy fit over this? Yet when U:A was announced to have the new perspective everyone's underwear got bunched up and the bitch slappin started. My personal opinion is that all things should evolve and the Ultima series should not be an exception. It's time to move forward instead of staying with tried and true 2D engines. Yeah they are tried and true, but this industry always has been and always will thrive on evolution. After seeing both of these games at E3, I can honestly say they made the right decisions.

The strength of this game presents itself in many aspects: character and camera control, 3D engine and effects, voicing, story, and attention to detail. Character control is accomplished by both the keyboard (movement, actions, etc.) and mouse (picking up or examining objects, interacting with the environment, camera movement). Conner can walk, run, swim, jump, climb, push, throw, shoot and fight, using little more than the direction arrows on your keyboard and the mouse. Other keys are occassionally used (for example, the END key toggles between running and walking, the INSERT key is used for jumping, etc.), but overall, the controls are kept simple to maximize your enjoyment of the game. Simply walk up to an object, and if it can be moved, Connor will bend down and push it. If you go into deep enough water, you'll swim. This control makes it very easy to start playing without having to memorize a bunch of key combinations. Battle is also straightforward. Pick a weapon, point at your foe and click, with the SHIFT key allowing alternate attack moves. Moving the mouse with the right mouse button moves the camera, and allows you to zoom in and out, pan, and look up and down.

Mother of BG - Sacrates
Whew, Scott was seriously hitting the Crack pipe pretty hard lately because he hit the BG Development board harder than a bottle of JD. Grab a cold one, sit back and enjoy the show.

On movement and random encounters: Not really. We automatically pick the shorted route. Because of the terrain type waiting, this almost always involves road travel and therefore the least nasty of the random encoutners. The only time that you wouldn't want to do this is if you are looking for nasty random encounters. If this is true, then you should just travel between far away wilderness areas.

Time for last second adjustments (Not those kind): You generally get enough time to set your party in a formation, before the monsters arrive, although you won't want to spend too much time on the inventory page.

On Random Encounter locations: The random encounter areas are quite small. Some are nice and open, but some require you fight your way past the monsters. You can thank James for those :)
You drop into one several different random ecounter areas. You will have to pick up any items before you leave, because scavengers will find your stuff and take it. Honest, not because we don't save changes to random encounter areas, it was scavengers :) . I'm not sure if you can rest before leaving. This was disallowed at one point, but that may have been changed after game testing. (Oh no! I used to know how everything worked. It's just too big, too big .... Aarrrggh!!!)

On graphic adjustments: There is no remove tree top option. Since the graphics are all unique, this would have required us to double the number of CD's. We do have a dither creature and object option. When this is on, all objects are dithered by obstructing objects instead of being completely obscurred.

By the way, this what the folks at SirTech have done in the JA2 demo. We didn't know that they were doing it, and I doubt that they knew that we were. I guess, simular problems, simular solutions.

On Character graphics: You can change your character's portrait, sounds, and colors at any time. We weren't going to let you do this, but the play testers said it should be allowed.

When you dual class you get the character animation appriate that would be used if you were the multiclassed. This is because there is no armor animations for mages etc.

You are never penalized for plot related events.

Combat etc: Terrian definitely effects missile combat. Since you have to dodge trees and boulders, it does take longer to reach the archer and therefore the archer gets more shots in. As far as trees and other obstructions, an archer will either move or wait until he has a line of sight to the target before aiming and firing. Dodging around trees and buildings, does hamper an archer's ability to hit you. Of course while you busy dodging, the rest of the party is being slaughtered. Ah, decisions, desisions.

On Dead and Raising: We have removed the constition hit from raise dead or resurrection. This is because none of the play testers were using raise dead or resurrection. When asked why, they said the constition penalty made it not worth while. They just reloaded every time.

Revenant update - Sacrates
Looks like the Official Revenant page made an update on how things are groovin in development world. Sounds like this hooker is coming along nicely? Head on over and check the site out or scope out this blurb.

3D Characters
In this weeks news update we're going to discuss one of Revenant's most important features, the 3-D characters. As you may know by now, Revenant features 3-D characters instead of the sprite based characters that are common to tile based games. 3-D characters offer many crucial advantages over sprites. Two of the most important advantages to 3-D characters are lighting effects and animations.

Lighting Effects:
One of Revenant's visual features is a colored lighting system that rivals those seen in games like Quake 2 and Unreal. These lights are more than just eye candy however. As your character walks through dim caverns he is concealed in darkness. Walking into a pool of torchlight will illuminate your character with an orange-red glow. You can use this method to conceal yourself in the shadows while avoiding the lit areas. This is a major part of Revenant's stealth mode, but that's another news story for another day. For now just imagine walking across a low bridge over a glowing green swamp, your character bathed in a translucent green light. Imagine a cool blue color lighting your character as they step into a moonlit glade in the forests. All the 3-D characters in Revenant from the players to the monsters to the NPC's will all be effected by the colored lighting. From a visual standpoint it's damn impressive, from a gameplay standpoint we have two words for you... Kick Ass.

Animations:
Choosing 3-D characters over sprite based characters gave us a huge amount of flexibility with our character animations. Our main character alone features literally hundreds of animations ranging from weapon swings, spin kicks, picking up objects, talking, walking, running, sneaking, throwing,.... Well the list goes on and on.

The NPC's also benefit from being 3-D characters. You won't find any static characters "just standing there". Walk into the "Drunken Whore Tavern" and you'll find the barkeep Rubold wiping down the bar, pouring mugs of ale for the patrons, and walking around keeping the place tidy. The bar patrons will be drinking mugs of ale, playing darts, falling over drunk, arm wrestling, etc. You'll even see Misthaven's resident "woman of the night" Kylie, dancing suggestively on a table at the center of the bar.

Then of course, are the monsters. Not only can we give our monsters a multitude of attacks, blocks, and interactive power moves (not to mention the spell and projectile attacks) but we wanted to do something more. Too many games have the enemies standing around waiting for the player to arrive. You won't find much of this in Revenant. Coming upon our monsters you will see them sleeping against walls, feeding on hunks of meat, fighting each other, patrolling back and forth across crucial areas, talking to each other, and much much more. This type of flexibility would simply not be possible with sprite based characters.
