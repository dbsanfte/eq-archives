UO Housing Patch!!! - Bowmeister
Wicked...this has been far too long in the coming. Origin's development team for Ultima Online has posted the first public draft of the proposed changes in the upcoming housing patch. From the looks of it, it's a winner, addressing most (if not all) of the issues that have been plaguing UO from a tent/vendor/house standpoint. Check the whole thing out here, and make sure you send feedback in - this one looks like a winner.

That brings us to vendors. We'd like to foster the use of shops, rather than roadside vendors. Shops are a more social experience, can provide more fictional atmosphere, and can be coupled with the lockdown feature to allow larger inventories for merchants. So we propose to not permit vendors outside of buildings anymore. Instead, the capability to have more than one vendor per building would be added. We would also add the ability for the owner of the building to allow vendors placed by others as well, so that players can create mini-malls or marketplaces which house multiple businesses. However, in order to ensure that vendors are used as shops, and not as banks, we will remove the ability to lock the front door of shops.

In other words, the building will be designated as "public." This will allow those merchants who value the location of their current vendors to replace their tent with a building in the same location, and create a nice shop. Additional features, such as adding the ability to customize the shop sign to use the graphic for whatever shop type the owner designates, and for automatic advertising on bulletin boards, allows the shop to function better.

BG Bits - Bowmeister
With info pouring out faster than a tourist in Tijuana running for the can, here's the lastest tidbits off the Baldur's Gate Developer's Corner. First, from Luke on Dual classing and experience points...

When you dual class it freezes your first class at the minimum experience for it's current level.

So, if you play a fighter to 89000(level 7) and you dual class, you get set to the minimum for level 7 (64000). That means you could go and gain 25000 XP all over again, reaching level five as a mage in your second class.

Your first class would be forever out of your reach though, as you need to get one level higher in your new class for the old one to reactivate, and the total XP cap would not allow that(you would need 90000XP to reach level 8 as a mage. this would be 154000 total between the two classes).

Next, Luke looks at inter-party relations between "good" and "evil" chars, as well as what happens when you start screaming for help...

What happens when my kindly and forthright paladin whomps the crap out of my stupid hafling thief (Gumrot) for picking pockets on an old lady? Does my paladin become an enemy and trigger the rest of my party's NPC scripts to attack him? (I'm sure Gumrot would approve)

If you have a paladin and a thief in the same party, the paladin will not automatically attack the thief for stealing (unless it's multiplayer and it's another PC :). If your thief is caught stealing too often, your party rep will drop. When this happens, the paladin will complain (as will other good-aligned characters). If it drops too low, good characters may leave. Some may attack you as well (because you are now evil).

There are a number of reasons for your party to turn on other members, but the instance is rare and simple stealing won't trigger it.

In any case, if a party member turns on another, the other party members stay out of it unless you direct them to help one side or another. It's your call as leader.

In multiplayer, my hafling thief (Gumrot) wanders off screen into a trap, and some gnolls to boot, and yells for help. Can the other group members hear my pitiful cry? (even if the response is only laughter.)

The other PC members can "hear" your cry in the chat window. They can also see everything you can if they move their screen to your character. The screen is not permanently centered over any one character, it is free-scrolling because you might have control of NPC's or need to see off the edge of the screen.

NPC's will not "hear" your cry automatically if they are out of visual range. You can run them over quick manually though. Once in range of hostiles they will attack (if you have assigned scripts) or you can control them as you will. I prefer the latter.

And finally, Luke clears up some of the latest info on spell casting...

It won't tell you the target is too far away. If the target is outside of the range of the spell, the character will move until he is within range, and then cast the spell. If you don't like the direction he is going, you can stop him any time before the spell is cast and you won't lose it.

As I said, if he has begun the casting process it will be lost if you interrupt him.

Anarchy Online! - Bowmeister
I haven't really heard anything about this one, but hey, if it's good enough for the chimps at VN, it's good enough for me. Anarchy Online is currently in development by a Norwegian company, Funcom. So WTF is Anarchy Online? Head over to VN for the full scoop, but it looks like another Massively Multiplayer online RPG, this time based in the far future.

It is 30,000 years into the future. Large corporations run the universe. They venture into space and acquire planets for their exploitation. The largest and most influential of these corporations is the Omni-Tek Corporation. On the planet where Anarchy Online takes place, the head of the planetary board is Phillip Ross. The reason Omni-Tek is the most powerful is due to their discovering and patent of nanotechnology, the power to rearrange matter on a molecular level.

The planet in question, Rubi-Ka (Red and dry), is run by Omni-Tek and is their key resource of Notum, a biological building block derived from the breastplate of insects, known to be the key element of the creation of the minute Nano machines. The key resource of Notum is actually a new element that has been discovered. It contains 4x as many atomic binding points that carbon making it very stable for nano-machine creation.

The planet is in opposition. There is a story defined conflict between previous workers of Omni-Tek, now living in cities out in the wasteland as rebels, and Omni-Tek. I will not get into the nature of the conflict here.
