ME bit - Sacrates
It's been a while, but Stephen Nichols was back on the all new Middle Earth Dev Board and had a few sparkly things to say on permanent death in ME.

Permanent death relies on a few design ideas that still need to be realized:

1) The player power curve needs to be realistic. We can't have single players that can take out 10 opponents without a scratch.

2) There needs to be an enforced waiting period before you gain the ability to permanently kill another player. We're currently thinking about 1 month's worth of play time for the character before enabling the murdering option.

3) To kill a player, you must have disabled ALL opponents. After ALL opponents are disabled (or have fled), you can take out the unconscious folks.

4) The civilized game world must react negatively
when you murder someone. Shopkeepers should call guards, sherrifs should want your head. Of course, if you leave the area in which you have murdered, you'll be out of harms way. Guards should be believeable... not teleporting instant killers.

5) Pulling the plug during a battle should have NO effect. The AI system will take over such characters and allow them to continue fighting. Of course, if you log back in while the battle is in progress, you should be able to resume. This should allow your buddies to guard your AI controlled body in the event of a network failure.

All of these options should help to make permanent death feasible.

BG bites - Sacrates
The boys were at it again on the boards. No rest for them that's for sure. Anyway, they did a little tag team action today. Let's hit the first one by Ray who was responding to a possible December 5th release date.

Says who? (we're hoping it would be sooner than that, but we still want to fix every last little bug first, so this damn game will be perfect :)

Here's Luke on why there is no Waterdeep.

All the reasons stated by the regulars on the board, and more.

Waterdeep is a fan favorite to be sure, and lots of people would love to take a walk through it's streets. Unfortunatley, those streets are exceptionally well known.

Here's a little insight into what we must consider when choosing a location.

All TSR liscensed products must fit the setting and the events that are currently occuring in the "world." Continuity is vital, and any events we create in an actual TSR world must not conflict or damage plots "currently" occuring in other products.

Basically, the more "interesting" a current setting, the less likely we would go there. If there are plots and intrigues already on the go, any events we construct must not effect them. You COULD go to waterdeep, but you couldn't do anything of consequence while there. The Sword Coast and Baldur's Gate are both still very much a frontier, so we can shake the tree a little harder there than elsewhere.

As well, the more things that are detailed currently, the more we have to try and impliment as acurately as possible. This does take away from what we can create ourselves.

Besides...Waterdeep would have to be the WHOLE game to do it right. It's freaky huge.
