KQ Brief with the woman - Sacrates
VN have kicked up a bit of talk with the creator and visionary behind the Kings Quest series, Roberta Williams. Always good to read cool stuff like this since I have enjoyed Kings Quest since I was in diapers. Ok not that long, and some may argue that I am still in diapers which is not true, I usually opt for the more colorfull Underoos.

TruthSword: The King's Quest series has always consisted of pure adventure games, so why did you decide to introduce RPG and action elements into Mask of Eternity?

Roberta Williams: This is still an adventure game; the core of an adventure game is an interactive story along with exploration and puzzle-solving. This game has all of that. What it has is extra stuff on top of the 'normal' adventure game stuff; it has some action and simple RPG elements to it to enhance the adventure game experience. The new gamers of today seem to have little patience for more 'old-fashioned' adventure games, and it seemed that they needed to be spiced up a little bit. I think we succeeded admirably with that task with KQ: Mask of Eternity.

BG is badass - Sacrates
At least according to 3D Gaming.net it is. They review this game concluding that it makes Diablo look worse than a pile of drying  Goblin dung. I must agree with them in this and look forward to playing it again some day. Maybe never the way i'm playing EQ.

Then, finally, after choosing all this (don't get me wrong, I love choosing stuff like that), you get into the game. You start off in the city of Candlekeep, your hometown. You have been raised since birth (you don't know who your real parents are) by a mage named Gorion. Gorion has trained and educated you, and you think of him as a father (he probably did shag your mom, but I haven't found that out yet). In this town, you run some errands for people to get gold (find a sword, get antidote, standard stuff really) when suddenly you get attacked by assassins. You quickly finish them off and run to find Gorion to help explain why so many people want to kill you. He tells you that you have to prepare to leave the city, so you do.

Official EQ News - Sacrates
As if the very word of the EQ Messiah himself (Brad Mcquaid) wasn't enough to wet your appetite. Then take a gander at this Press Release and feel comforted that you'll be seeing the official retail version of Everquest will be in your hands soon.

FOSTER CITY, Calif.--(ENTERTAINMENT WIRE)--Jan. 13, 1999-- 989 Studios announced today that its highly anticipated PC title EverQuest[tm], a massive online role-playing game, will be available Mar. 16, 1999.

Already heralded by game reviewers as an extraordinary evolution in online role-playing games, EverQuest features a multitude of adventures and quests and is ``the dream role-playing game,'' according to PC Gamer.

``EverQuest will be a defining title in the online role-playing game genre because of its unique free roaming environments and graphically immersive story line that progresses according to the player's individual decisions,'' said Jeffrey Fox, vice president, marketing, 989 Studios. ``EverQuest is the most detailed and visually
stunning role-playing game ever developed.''

EverQuest is being launched with a substantial marketing campaign that includes broad-based print and online advertising, retail-specific promotions and in-store merchandising. Retail pre-sell promotions will begin in February 1999.

989 Studios, a Sony Computer Entertainment Group Company, is a wholly-owned division of Sony Computer Entertainment America Inc. and is the third-largest PlayStation(R) software publisher in North America.

Headquartered in Foster City, with additional development studios in San Diego and Santa Monica, 989 Studios develops software for the PlayStation game console, personal computer and online markets. 989 Studios is a recognized leader in the videogame industry, with top franchise titles such as NFL GameDay[tm], Twisted Metal(R), Jet Moto[tm] and Cool Boarders(R).

Unknown update - Sacrates
Kevin Ross made an update on Unknown: Quest for the Golden Dragon recently in their message forums. Stop by and read up on this tasty looking game.

It is just as easy for me to post what we have been up to in the programing side so let me drop some notes here:

1) We removed all words related to religion and replaced it with the word deity

2) We added a new feature that you can now display your name if you want to everyone. You have 3 choices. A) Display no name B) Display your real Character Name and C) Display the alternate name. C can only be changed by two classes during game play. That is the Thief and the assassin. It will have to be a successful skill check to have it changed.

3) We are working on spell affects. Since we have particle and storm affects now in the game, we wanted to make sure when a spell caster casts it is seen. We are getting those affects down now.

4) Weather has now been fully introduced. Rain, snow, the thunder the lighting is now done

5) The user interface is coming to a close. We will be releasing Screen shots to UKC showing how we have the interface. It is easy to use and understand. We hope to release shots this week, next week at the latest.

6) The 16bit conversion is finally coming to an end. We lost 39 levels during the conversion process. And are re-doing them as fast as we can.

7) Kaid and Ross Systems are working on the final set of Icons and Buttons on the game. Since we converted to 16bit we are now converting all the buttons and icons to 16bit also. We are also changing some of them.

8) The player manual is being touched up; screen shots are being taken for it. We plan to have a few users edit it for us.

9) We have everyone confirmation number ready. Once we post the alpha date 2 weeks prior to the release you will receive your confirmation and NDA. You have 2 weeks, to have the NDA back at us. The faster you get the NDA back to us the faster you will get your CD. People asked could we rush them. We are toying with that.

10) We are discussing communication now in depth in the game. We see the potential problem of abuse in the game. We have also seen the lag it does generate and plan to place a post today on this message board on how we would like to set it up. I suggest you read the post and give us your views. This is going to be a debate people.

11) We are adding latency and FPS that are removable to the game as we speak. We will make it in option that you can removed or display

12) Last Kaid is working on the monsters and we are animating them as fast as we can. We will have between 50-60 monsters in the alpha done. We will be introducing monsters during the alpha, until we get all 111 monsters in the game. Yes we will have 111 different monsters in the game. Not counting some of these 111 monsters will be the same model but with different weapons, swords, spears and shield.

That is it for now!!!

EQ Bones - Sacrates
Well, here is something unusual lately? Slim pickings for Everquest news. At least Abashi hit the Development Board and tossed up a few that piqued my interest. His first one is probably the most controversial issue in the Beta at the moment.

Any information floating around on how the transition from phase 3 to phase 4 will be handled is rumor and supposition. This information has not been released to the dev team by anyone.

In previous phases people were allowed to take one character from one phase to the next, that was how it was done in those phases but does not necessarily have any bearing on P4.

I have heard arguements saying things like "you don't want to have 1500 level 1 players running around a server". Since there are currently 4000+ people in beta, and phase 4 will consist of between 10x and 20x that number, the arguement is moot. And for release, that will be the case anyway.

The only valid arguement for keeping a character from P3 to P4 is that 989 still needs to have people testing the mid and high level zones. A complete wipe from P3 to P4 will hamper this effort.

This is the only arguement being weighed by the dev team, though a decision is still pending.

And of course always a little something on lag etc. Let me put it this way, the Network code in EQ is simply beautifull! As good as, and "maybe" better than even the great Tribes at the moment. Even though i'm on Cable Modem I have been getting 0% packet loss to and from the server! On my regular modem (Which I confess to not using much anymore for obvious reasons) I still got a damn fair ping. Read on you Euro chimps.

I spoke to a guy playing from the UK today. He was playing with a 350 ping and 5% p-loss. Those are fantastic stats. EQ Screams with those stats.

Here's his feeling on Guilds and Halls.

This stuff really hasn't been worked out in its entirety yet. I personally expect the number of guildhalls to be controlled via the economy, with them becoming more and more expensive as the "ideal number" of guildhalls is approached.

But that's just my feeling, I've no data to support that.
