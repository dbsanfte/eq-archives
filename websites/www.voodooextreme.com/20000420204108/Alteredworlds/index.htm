Anarchy Rules! - Certis
Anarchy alone is sad, anarchy together is victory! So the crazy man down the street tells me. Gamelinks has posted an interview with Funcom about their upcoming MMPOG, Anarchy Online. Definitely worth a look if you have not heard much of this one yet. Snippy snippy:

GAMELINKS.NET: The futuristic setting is somewhat of a departure from other massively multiplayer games that have been thus far produced (and that are currently being worked on). What were some of the deciding factors that made the company delve into such a different environment?

FUNCOM : Of the 70 or so massively multiplayer games in development as we speak only a handful of them are science fiction. It makes us very proud to be the first science fiction MMORPG on the market. The single most important reason to why we chose to go for sci-fi rather than fantasy was because we are die hard fans (of sci-fi). With a future setting we could now gather inspiration from so many different great sci-fi novels, movies and games in the past that gave us so much enjoyment.

Dungeon Siege interview - Certis
Dungeon Siege Vault has posted an interview with level designer Sarah Boulian. I got a kick out of how she got her start in the industry. Have a look:

What is your professional background, and how did you get into the games industry?

I have a degree in Biology from Colby College in Waterville, Maine. Most of my college jobs were involved with science - I was a lab technician/intern at a pharmaceutical company, was an animal husbandry intern at the Mystic Marinelife Aquarium in Mystic, CT, and a lab TA [Teaching Assistant, not Total Annihilation 8-) - Ed.] for a number of biology courses at my college. I was actually on track to go for an MD/PhD in Immunology when I started playing UO. It become apparently obvious that making games was way cooler than wearing a lab coat for the rest of my life, so I contacted GPG for an interview during a trip to Seattle. A month later I moved west to learn design and help out around the office. And now I get to make levels for a living!

Origin, Bow Your Head In Shame! - Certis
If you have not been following the sickening fiasco that is Origin lately follow this link to Desslock's and read up. Since they have released UO: Renaissance, effectively a box without a game in it, the community has been crying for blood. Well, here is Origin's futile attempt at redeeming themselves:

The Ultima Online: Renaissance CD was released to retail stores in North America on Wednesday. Unfortunately, last minute hardware and software issues arose which forced us to withhold the final Renaissance publish until we were certain we could provide a smooth, quality transition to all of our customers . This was a difficult decision to make, and we offer our apologies to both our veteran player community, and to anyone who purchased the Renaissance CD as their first venture into the Ultima Online community.

In addition to our apologies, we wish to offer two options for our new customers.

  • Any customers who purchase the Ultima Online: Renaissance CD and registers for a new account prior to the release of Renaissance to all of our shards, will receive an additional 30 days of playtime free with their registration. Thus, these new accounts will receive a total of 60 days free of Ultima Online: Renaissance.
  • Alternately, those customers who have purchased the Ultima Online: Renaissance CD may return it to us for a full refund by mailing the game and receipt of purchase to:
UO Refund
C/o Origin Systems Inc.
5918 West Courtyard Dr.
Austin, TX 78730

Both of these offers will be valid until Renaissance has been published to all of our 21 shards worldwide. We will provide information on specific offer dates as they become available.

Once again, we wish to apologize for any inconvenience this situation has caused members of the Ultima Online community, both new and veteran. We are very excited about the features included with the Ultima Online: Renaissance publish and look forward to introducing them to our community as soon as possible.

Gordon Walton
VP Online Services
Origin Systems

Ohhh an offer to play an incomplete game two years in the making for an extra month free! Do they think they are doing us a favor? With companies like Verant interactive producing guaranteed hit games like Everquest 2 and Star Wars Online I won't be surprised to see Origin go down in flames. Can't say I'll miss them.  

Baldur's Gate II Site Updated - Certis
The official Baulders Gate II site has been updated with a progress report, saddle up:

Well, it's been a couple of weeks since we've posted an update here so let's get you guys and gals up to speed. Right now I am waiting for the newest rev to finish coming across. It promises to have a bunch of fixes and the Q.A. team is really looking forward to it. The last rev we got actually cut half of the active bugs off of the buglist so that made the folks on our end very happy. Kudos to BioWare for that one.

At Black Isle we have been working on casting the voice over characters. We are happy to say that we were able to cast the same actors for all of the returning characters. This may not sound like much but for the sake of continuity we were ecstatic about it. BioWare has been plugging away at getting the final content into the game so that we can lock down code and really hammer away at bug fixes. Things are coming along very nicely on both ends and real progress has been made to complete BG 2. Sorry it has been so long since I've written to you but rest assured, everything is going very well.

Doug Avery
Assistant Producer
Black Isle Studios

Baldur's Gate II makes me think very dirty thoughts. I actually just finished the first one a few weeks ago. The real question is, do I wait for BG II or do I start on Planescape Torment? Not a bad dilemma for an RPG gamer to be in!
