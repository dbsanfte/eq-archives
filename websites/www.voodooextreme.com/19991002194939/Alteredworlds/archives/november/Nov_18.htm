This first one is on the controversial subject of Gate and Teleportation spells.

There is no gate spell to get anyone anywhere. There are, as I've always said, teleport spells that are very limited, the more powerful of which are only available to higher level wizards and druids.

The spell 'gate' is given to most mages at lvl 10, and allows them (and only them) to return to their home point, which is very restricted as to where it can be.-Brad

There were definite critera used when selecting phase 3 testers, however they were different than those used for previous phases.

Are we becoming less picky as time goes on? Yes and no.

A lot (but not all) of phase 3 is about stress testing our beta server, about filling it up, optimizing, debugging, etc.

We've been tuning the gameplay and balancing since phase .5, and we continue to do so (everyone in the previous phases is still in phase 3).

Also, keep in mind that at this point we need people with different backgrounds, some more technical than others, some more experienced than others, to test the game. An example: is the interface intuitive for a non-hard core RPG player? How does the game play on lower end machines? etc. etc.

We have a lot to learn from beta, which is why we're doing beta tests. And each phase has its purposes, and so far so good :) Phase .5, 1, and 2 were a great success.-

Destiny talk - Sacrates
The guys over at VN had a little chat with Terence Tan who is the lead developer behind Destiny an upcoming RPG that looks tasty. Here's a blurb to tide you over.

Arreouw: Your website indicates that Destiny was designed as a multi-player game - will it be for unlimited players, or for a fixed number playing quests together as a party? Will there be one or more servers to handle the players? Will there be continuity among the servers, or will they be separated, as are the shards in Ultima Online? How do you plan to handle lag?

Terence Tan: Destiny was orginally designed as an online-ONLY multi-player game. We spent about 6 months developing a prototype of this system, basing on developing a 'story driven' multi-player game system. I felt that what Ultima left out was the fact that people who play wanted to be heroes, and live stories. The system had Windows NT has the server, and used DCOM/COM to do our fault-tolerant and distribution. The client ran on Windows 95.

We finished our prototype, and were planning on building the 'real' version of the game but unfortunately as fate would have it, the Asian economic crisis struck. Beggars can't be choosers, so I talked to our investors into converting much of our researched technology into a product that we could sell off-the-shelf. So it will also have a single-player mode.

BG Bits... - Bowmeister
Couple of updated from the man - Dr. Ray...dammit just send the bug ridden beast off for duplication anyways...:). Just keep releasing patches before it hits stores...:). Here's some thoughts on BG multiplayer from Ray...

Let me ask you this:

Would you play pen and paper AD&D with someone who told you that she or he had all 18's as their rolls? "Just got lucky," or some such excuse. And a pet pseudodragon to boot. Yeah, right.

The same applies to multiplayer sessions. Play with people you know, and if you don't know them, run your own server and control what they are allowed to import. If you think they're cheating and don't like it, boot them out.

We realized early on that only a human DM could provide the control that was required to prevent pkilling and cheaters from proliferating. They'll still be out there - and that's ok, because they'll be in uncontrolled games where everyone knows that the sky's the limit and you can do whatever you wish. If you don't want this, we provide an alternative - play with people you know or run your own server.

ray

And on playing the game/bug testing...and some Fallout 2 love :)...

Wow, haven't been here for a while, and look what I wandered into...maybe I should have pretended I didn't see this and just go back to playing BG some more (finding bugs is FUN :)

But for your first question: I haven't yet played Fallout2, nor has anyone else here at BioWare - so I can't comment on the quality level - I haven't seen the game yet. I WILL be playing it as the #1 pick on my "games to play" list soon as we ship BG, and I'll let you know what I think once I have played it, all the way through.

I am playing BG for the second time through right now. I'll be either the first or second person in the BW office to finish it twice, and this time I'm doing EVERYTHING. I'm devoting every waking hour to playing the game and finding bugs - 14-16 hours a day, seven days a week (for nearly two months now. Ugh. I've played chapters 1-3 about 50 times now...) So far, I've spent about 120 hours in this particular full-game (all chapters, all subplots) session, and I estimate that I have another 40 hours or more left still before I have done everything. I've talked to every single person I saw, entered every single house, walked over every inch of terrain I could walk to, and tried to finish every single subplot that I know about. I've reported over a thousand bugs personally on the project so far, and I can honestly say that this pass, all of the ones I found last time are fixed. I and others here and Interplay's QA department are still entering more, but they are increasingly of the type that only people "in the know" - i.e. who know the interface and story intimately - can really call bugs. We still want the game to be perfect, the kind of game that we'd buy ourselves, so that's why we're still fixing these bugs. I know that the people (Chris Parker, Greg P and others) who are working on BG at Black Isle feel the same way that the folks at BioWare do - we've been working together a LONG time.

Trust me, we've invested over one tenth of our lives on this project (I'm 30, and most of the other team members are 25-30), so we don't want to let it go until we think it's ready. The game is HUGE, so there are certainly bugs that will be missed, but we want to keep these to minimum - everyone's doing their very best, really.

Now back to finding bugs...fun, fun!

respectfully,

----------------
dr ray muzyka
