Westwood Studios Brings Lands of Lore III to Neutral Ground

First Public Showing of Latest Installment in Hit Role Playing Game Series at New York City's Neutral Ground Gaming Club

LAS VEGAS, NV, December 4, 1998 - From the far reaches of Gladstone to the streets of New York City, Westwood Studios today announced that the first public showing of the highly anticipated role playing game (RPG) Lands of Lore III, will take place on Tuesday, December 8 at 7:30 PM at Manhattan's Neutral Ground gaming club. The event is free and open to the public. Neutral Ground is located at 122 West 26th Street (between 6th and 7th Avenues) on the fourth floor.

Lands of Lore III continues the rich heritage of the epic role playing game series with a new adventure, new characters, and the immersive gameplay that fans have come to expect from Westwood Studios. Lore III features classic RPG elements like professions, guild quests, skills, familiars and an expansive spell system. However, the game is more accessible than ever thanks to a streamlined, easy-to-use interface, detailed auto-mapping, and a self-annotating journal that is virtually a manual that builds itself as you play. The epic story leads you through huge six words, each one with its own challenges and surprises. Lands of Lore 3 will be released early next year.

Neutral Ground, open seven days a week and located right in the heart of Manhattan, is a 4000 square foot space dedicated to the serious gamer. For $7 a day or $35 a month, gamers can play from noon until late in the evening a wide assortment of games -- card games, role playing games, chess, board games, as well as darts, pinball, and video games. Every day Neutral Ground features an intensive daily schedule of events that include several type of RPG tournaments for a variety of great prizes. There are also sign ups for leagues, classes, RPG campaigns, exclusive premiere parties for new games, exhibits of original artwork from upcoming cards games, and much more.

Westwood Studios is a prominent publisher of best-selling PC, and next-generation console games. Formed in 1985, Westwood Studios has released several popular titles including: Dune II(tm), the Lands of Lore(tm) series, the Kyrandia(tm) adventure trilogy, Monopoly(r) for the Internet, the Games People Play(tm) series, Blade Runner(tm) and the Command & Conquer(r) product line. Westwood is a wholly owned subsidiary of Electronic Arts(tm).

Electronic Arts, headquartered in Redwood City, California, is the world's leading interactive entertainment software company. Founded in 1982, Electronic Arts posted revenues of $909 million for fiscal 1998. The company develops, publishes and distributes software worldwide for personal computers and advanced entertainment systems such as the PlayStationr and the Nintendo 64(r). More information about EA's products and full text of press releases can be found on the Internet at http://www.ea.com.

QFG 5 update - Sacrates
You want Gold? Well, looks like the guys working on Quest for Glory V are giving it to you. They recently updated their page with this goodness that confirms that it did in fact go Gold.

After nearly two years in development, the fifth installment of the Quest for Glory series was completed tonight. The Dragon Fire team worked 12-hour shifts through the American Thanksgiving Holiday to meet the deadline. The gold master disc left Yosemite Entertainment’s Oakhurst, California studio today for rush replication.

Boxed copies of Quest for Glory V will travel via air-express courier to retail centers across North America. The game is expected to arrive in stores in early December. International versions will follow in the new year. Further international release news will be posted on this site when available.

Dragon Fire, the most detailed series installment to date, completes an epic saga in the eyes of designer Lori Cole. Dragon Fire’s detailed 3D characters, scrolling background panoramas, and a critically acclaimed score have been described as "nothing short of dramatic."

Members of the gaming press, treated to advance versions of Quest for Glory V, are praising the game’s combination of the "best attributes of adventuring and roleplaying." (CNET Gamecenter) Chris Harding of Adrenaline Vault raves, "For the last couple of weeks I've been playing with a beta of Sierra's Quest for Glory V: Dragon Fire, and they've been two of the most delightful gaming weeks in recent memory."

EQ Preview - Sacrates
Yup, the Everquest joy just keeps on coming in. All I see are incredibly positive Reviews which is a scary thought considering the amount of time I invested in UO and that got very lackluster Previews/Reviews etc. So when Beta 4 of EQ starts and you don't see Sacrates around you'll know exactly where he is....in his elusive search for big breasted Nymphs at play under a waterfall somewhere in Norath! I know they are hidden there somewhere damnit and it is my mission in life to find them. Er, anyway check out the latest Preview by Gameznet.

There is something about this game that grabs a hold of you and won't let go. I have had countless marathon sections where my work has been put on a backburner while I attempt to make that next level, or to get that next magic item. It is hard to liken this game to anything previously released, though it bears most similarity to Meridian 59 and to Ultima Online and will always be compared to their ilk. In real world terms this game is the gamers version of crack. You cannot pry yourself from the keyboard until you are up way past your bedtime. Then you go to sleep only to dream of the game. This is the RPG game that role playing fans have been waiting for.

Thief Demo Review - Sacrates
Welcome to the brave new world of FPS gaming. Half life and Thief have come along and nearly redefined the experience in two fell swoops. Thief is extremely interesting in that it is a combination of various genre's including RPG. And we all know Half life kicks everything elses collective asses. With that said 3DGN have kicked up a review of the Demo for all to see until we start seeing full version reviews.

The atmosphere, in accordance with Gameplay, is nothing short of superb. When starting off, you can hear the drunken guard moaning and speaking jibberish while the other guard trades pleasantries with you - they won't attack unless you un-sheath your sword or arm an arrow. Once you get into the compound you can overhear guards talking, one explaining the whole concept of guarding the compound to the other. You can also here them call for backup if they hear you in a dark corner, or yell "Ahh! They got him!" if they spot a fallen comrade.

Overall, I would say Thief is an above average game. If the demo is any indication (damn I say that a lot) then you can expect that I'd be first in line to pick up my copy - regardless that if I wait a few days I could probably get it through my editor for review. While the game may have a few flaws such as long load times or somewhat choppy graphics, they don't seriously hinder the gameplay in any way. I said it once and I'll say it again, I have seen the future of First-Person Shooter games in Thief: The Dark Project.

[Stormtroopers]How helpful has Microsoft, 3Dfx, Intel, etc. been so far with providing development support and equipment? Foresee any problems with Microsoft?

[Albert] Most of the development support we've needed has come from Epic, and more specifically, Tim Sweeney, who has been very helpful in answering our Unreal questions.
