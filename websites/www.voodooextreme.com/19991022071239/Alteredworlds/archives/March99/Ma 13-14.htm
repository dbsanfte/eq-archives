Everquest Release goodies - Sacrates (The Dwarven stud)
Ok kids, in just a few days you'll have a brand new copy of Everquest in your hands. You'll be coming back from your local software store grasping a copy of EQ to your chest like a straight man holds his soap in Prison. (Very tightly!) Smedly, John that is had these good words to say on some issues before then to get you up to date.

Greetings Citizens of Norrath

The time is finally upon us. The lands will be open at 9:00am PST on Tuesday March 16th.

During the first week, you can expect a very short downtime (less than an hour) during off-peak hours for network maintainence. We will be posting scheduled downtimes on our website (http://www.everquest.com) beginning sometime on Monday March 15th. You will also be informed ahead of time on this site regarding any patches that will be upcoming. We anticipate heavily using the Test Server to do our testing and moving those patches to the Live Servers only after we feel the new version has been throurghly tested. The Test Server is used completely for testing purposes. This means the server AND different zones on the server will come up and down at our discretion. There will be no GM or Guide support on the test server.

In addition, sometime on March 15th, you will find that the website has changed and a much more robust Technical Support section will be added to address various issues. Please check this site out before you log in if possible so that you have the most up to date drivers for all of your hardware. If you need any assistance, don't hesitate to give us a call... you will find all of our tech support contact information on our website as well. We are here to serve you as our valued customers. Again, the best thing you can do is to check out our website and check to make sure your drivers are current.

Many people have asked what they should do with their Beta versions of EverQuest. You should delete this prior to installing EverQuest. There will be some older files there that you don't want.

Here's a couple of other bits of information in no particular order:

- Beta Testers - When you register, you can use your existing Station Account for registration purposes.
- Pwipe? - Yes, there has been a pwipe.
- Patching for release - When you log in, there will be a patch. It isn't very large.
- Billing - I would urge anyone who has any questions to check out the Billing FAQ on the website (sometime March 15th).
- Are Credit Cards Needed for the game? - Currently Yes (Visa, Mastercard or American Express)- Debit Cards will work as well. Are we going to have more options? We are working on it and looking into different possibilities. Clearly it's in our best interests to make the game available to as many people as possible, so rest assured this is important to us.
- Is Billing Secure? - We use SSL just like websites that do credit card transactions. We also dropped the requirement for Mother's Maiden Name (although people didn't realize all this really was was a "keyword" to help identify the person).

We are excited beyond belief at the support that EverQuest has recieved, and we intend to deliver the best gaming experience anyone has recieved.

We'll see you in Norrath!!!

M&M VII Preview - Sacrates
PC Games have dished up their usual mini Preview, this time it's Might and Magic VII that gets the treatment. Stop by and taste the goodness.

So here we are in early 1999, with Might and Magic VII just around the corner, and this time, the nice stuff is in. At the top of the list stands graphical enhancements for hardware-based acceleration (D3D). The landscape (particularly the dungeons) gains perspective and depth from the lighting effects and shading, though it entails a slight loss to color—most noticeable in the many monsters. In compensation, the spells and monsters look better and truly appear to move in the 3D space. Turn-based combat has been changed to support movement. You can now use a portion or all of a given turn to walk around that jutting wall or slip out from between two sets of attackers. Some monster types, too, will attack one another now. You may come upon a battle in progress, and if neither side hates you, choosing to support one or the other could garner a reward or a new quest. You’ll also discover that you can lead monster groups into nearby towns where they’ll attack the citizens (though not, alas, set fire to the buildings) and be attacked in turn by the local militia. True, you lose the experience points gained by killing the critters yourself, but when you’re inexperienced and wimpy, there may be no other choice. Besides, you still get access to the treasure chests monsters conveniently leave out in the field.

Vampire Update - Sacrates
Ray Gresko, The project leader on Vampire has made a stop by the message boards to lay down some smack on this game. This looks like a possible winner here.

Finally have some time to post-

Just wanted to make this crystal-clear. We LOVE this license, and are stepping up to a very big challenge doing it justice in CRPG form.

Things that should allay some fears:
- We are thoroughly knowledgeable about the license. I've personally read every rulebook and supplement made for V:tM. The only V:tM books I haven't read are some of the novels (although I'm working on it, just finished the new Toreador Clan Novel - excellent work by Steve Wieck!). So if something from the rules doesn't make it into the game, it's not because we're not aware of it. It would be due to a design or focus constraint, which are tough to make but crucial in cohesive game development.

- Due to our fortunate relationship with WW, we also get copies of every new rulebook as it's released (just got Cainite Heresy). We're dedicated to making Redemption be as accurate and up to date with V:tM rules/backgrounds as possible.

- White Wolf, as it should with it's 'baby', has been involved in reviewing our work and making sure everything fits within the WOD (this was especially true with the storyline, settings and characters). It has been incredible to discuss various Vampire nuances with Justin Achilli and the many others involved in the creation of Vampire. It is immediately obvious why the license is so truly unique and innovative.

- Where possible, we've included characters, locations, and events detailed in the V:tM books. Expect some suprises here...

- Several of us has worked with licenses before (Top Gun, Star Trek, Star Wars, etc), and understand the importance of capturing the 'essence' of what makes the license so appealing. The other aspect involves pulling the player 'into' the world, allowing them the joy of actually making choices and 'role-playing' within a universe that previously only existed on a movie screen, on paper, or in their own imaginations. This all aside, Vampire still offers a huge challenge as it's depth, richness and nuance is very different from concepts we have worked with before.

- As far as the non-Vampire players getting lost, keep in mind that the typical Vampire mythos can offer a slight advantage. It gives us the opportunity to get players that might find something 'typically vampiric' interesting, then we get to expand upon or utterly shatter their conceptions by exposing the WOD to them. Most people know what a typical Vampire is. It's not like we're making a game about some alien race of Frobnoids that are fighting a war against the gelatinous Zibfrids. It's utilizing a horror element that they have some base familiarity with, and it occurs in locations they've heard of and understand. The roleplaying involved deals with familiar mature subjects such as horror, love, betrayal, greed, etc. And since the single player game follows a mortal through his embrace and discoveries in the WOD, we have the ability to slowly peel back and expose the inner workings of the Kindred in digestible and interesting portions.

I'd relate it to our work with Jedi Knight. We didn't have to start off by explaining what a Jedi was, what a droid was, etc. It was assumed that everyone playing had seen or was familiar with Star Wars. That offered us an incredible advantage of being able to move forward with higher concepts like story and character development, and the new offering we presented above and beyond the movie settings.

Don't worry, we'll have the masses speaking the lexicon in no time! :[
