Anyway go read this little piece if you thought Warren Spector's was any good yesterday, this one is great. Here's a sneak peak.

The plot lines in most RPG’s are basically the same old drivel, over and over again. I call it the standard RPG plot. I think you will find it familiar: You start the game as the great hero. (You know this because you are told so in the instructions.) You job is to kill the big evil guy. (Also, from the instructions.) You pillage and plunder everyone and everything to become strong for your final battle. (Of course the supposed bad guy is doing no evil right now, but you sure are.) When ready at last, you kill the “bad guy” who has been sitting there peacefully waiting.

Well, after 3 games of my own that retold this story, combined with all that introspection, I was ready for something more. I felt that role-playing would be even stronger in a more realistic setting. I felt that people are basically good, not merely for fear of some divine being, but because it was logical. Thus was born the modern Ultima design. I was really worried before we released Ultima IV: Quest of the Avatar, that people might think I’d gone off the philosophical deep end, but instead, it was my first best selling game.

Ultimas since then have had a deep sense of ethics, which I carefully separate from morals. By my definition, Ethics are logically based, while Morals are religion based. Thus, if you object to pre-marital sex because it is against God’s laws, that’s Morality; if you oppose it because of the risk of unwanted pregnancies, that’s an Ethic. I do not subscribe to any Morals, yet believe I am a very Ethical person.

DarkViper: Will there be other Multiplayer Modes?

Stephen Patt.: There will be two different types of Drakan Multi-player in the box: Deathmatch and something tentatively called "Queen of the Dragon". Deathmatch play actually has two variations: Ground combat only (i.e. no dragon) with sword-to-sword combat only, and aerial combat which includes ground combat, but truthfully we don't expect anyone to hop off their dragon for sword-to-sword play when there are other dragons flapping around! The second mode, "Queen of the Dragon" will have up to 8 people on the ground and only one dragon.  Everyone will be fighting sword-to-sword, while trying to find the Dragon's soul crystal.  Whoever finds the soul crystal can then approach the dragon without getting instantly toasted, mount the dragon, and rain fire down on all of the other players, raking up the kills!

Spector speaks on Role Playing - Sacrates
Another morsel I noticed while perusing VN was that Warren Spector did a piece for Game Developer magazine on Role Playing in CRPG's. Turns out to be a fairly good read and he has some good ideas to throw in the grind mill. Damn good reading.

"6,753 unique skills!" "827 errand boy missions!" and "A world so big you won’t want to explore it all!" Give it a rest. This is shallow. It’s silly. It betrays our geeky roots in paper gaming (a
medium with only a dangerous, super-ficial relation to electronic gaming). Role-playing isn’t about statistics or exploring randomly generated worlds of crate-filled buildings. It isn’t about ran-dom
quests and combat encounters
every sixteen steps. It isn’t even about +37 Swords of Instant Critical Hits that do Double Damage From Behind! Role-playing is about giving players the free-dom to act as they see fit, within the framework of a story we provide. Role-playing is about characters
developing in unique and meaningful ways as a result of player choices. It’s about trying new behaviors in a safe set-ting before we try them in the real
world. In the space I have here, I can’t tell you how we make a game that allows us to do all that. But let’s start by identifying problems, and by looking at character, setting, and story, and how we usually approach them.

Diablo 2 Multiplayer - Sacrates
Just over at VN who noticed that the boys at The Unofficial Diablo 2 page have skanked up something off of their very own message boards about Diablo 2 in Multiplayer. Particularly cheating etc. This will be one hot topic as we see Diablo 2's development progress.

Hi Elly and others,

Well, this issue sure has stirred up a lot of controversy! I'd like to defuse that a bit if I can. As the game is not complete and we haven't made final decisions about a lot of things, there is a considerable amount of info that I'm not at liberty to give at this time. However, I can tell you the following:

* There will be cheat-free, B.net-only characters. You will not be able to level up offline as we must control all of the character information internally. The cheating is by far our biggest complaint from our customers, and we'd like to accomodate them with this arrangement.

* THERE WILL BE OTHER MULTIPLAYER OPTIONS!!! We understand the situation our customers overseas face. It is not practical to log onto the internet every time you play, due to the costs, etc. So, we will include provisions for you to play multiplayer without the characters stored on our side, and without necessarily logging onto B.net.

However, these characters and the above B.net-only characters will be incompatible, and cannot play in the same game, as this would introduce possible hacks into the cheat-free world. So, in effect, there will be multiplayer much like it exists now (with many extra options as well) AND a pristine, cheat-free B.net environment for those who are able and eager to do so.

I hope this clears it up a little; we are concerned and sensitive to the differing needs of our customers. Diablo II will hopefully be an improvement in EVERY way to the original.

Additionally, the fear of "losing" your character due to a disconnect are unfounded. Your character data will be frequently updated and backed up on our server network.

Thanks for your continued enthusiasm!

More Luke on Experience etc.

When you dual class it freezes your first class at the minimum experience for it's current level.
So, if you play a fighter to 89000(level 7) and you dual class, you get set to the minimum for level 7 (64000). That means you could go and gain 25000 XP all over again, reaching level five as a mage in your second class.
Your first class would be forever out of your reach though, as you need to get one level higher in your new class for the old one to reactivate, and the total XP cap would not allow that(you would need 90000XP to reach level 8 as a mage. this would be 154000 total between the two classes).

UO Housing Patch!!! - Bowmeister
Wicked...this has been far too long in the coming. Origin's development team for Ultima Online has posted the first public draft of the proposed changes in the upcoming housing patch. From the looks of it, it's a winner, addressing most (if not all) of the issues that have been plaguing UO from a tent/vendor/house standpoint. Check the whole thing out here, and make sure you send feedback in - this one looks like a winner.

That brings us to vendors. We'd like to foster the use of shops, rather than roadside vendors. Shops are a more social experience, can provide more fictional atmosphere, and can be coupled with the lockdown feature to allow larger inventories for merchants. So we propose to not permit vendors outside of buildings anymore. Instead, the capability to have more than one vendor per building would be added. We would also add the ability for the owner of the building to allow vendors placed by others as well, so that players can create mini-malls or marketplaces which house multiple businesses. However, in order to ensure that vendors are used as shops, and not as banks, we will remove the ability to lock the front door of shops.

In other words, the building will be designated as "public." This will allow those merchants who value the location of their current vendors to replace their tent with a building in the same location, and create a nice shop. Additional features, such as adding the ability to customize the shop sign to use the graphic for whatever shop type the owner designates, and for automatic advertising on bulletin boards, allows the shop to function better.
