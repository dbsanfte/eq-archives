BG (Expansion) Bits - Bowmeister
Noticed this over on the one and only Baldur's Gate Chronicles. Looks like Dr. Ray Muzyka has been making the rounds of the news groups again - still no comfirmation on the one legged halfing sightings though. Here's the goods...

On where you start the expansion from: You can load any saved game and continue the expansion. i.e. the final save is made just before the final battle.

On why he hasn't been as vocal as before in the newsgroup: I'm still here. I'm a little busy (somehow I expected to be less so, but if anything it's worse now than before BG was released...go figure), and more than a little tired the past month, so I haven't been posting as much, but I AM reading a lot of messages, and passing along the suggestions to the BG2 and BG mission pack teams. I wish I had more time to post here, but I only am able to get to the newsgroup about once per week - my emails have skyrocketed and I spend nearly every evening trying to catch up...

On the reason for the XP cap: It is there for game balance, not only in the expansions but in the main game.

On your ability to reach the cap: I agree, it is quite possible to reach it. I did finally reach it, for example, and I did about 95%+ of the subquests. But I dropped a few characters and added others in the latter chapters, and hence I ended up in my main session of the game with a couple of characters who weren't maxed out. I still think that most people will be less than the XP cap when they finish (since most won't do all subquests), but certainly if you do nearly everything then you will reach the XP cap.

On why they capped experience points instead of levels: We actually added a fair number of sidequests to help flesh out the world - these probably made reaching the XP cap more likely, in retrospect. However, to add new monsters and spells and items befitting higher level characters wasn't feasible for BG - we wanted to ensure that we made a fairly balanced game for levels 1-7 or so characters. Our DM's felt that characters with equivalent amounts of XP would be roughly equal in terms of power, hence the decision to use an XP cap rather than level cap

On an individual's dislike of the cap: I apologize for this - certainly we did our best to make a good game, and if the XP cap is limiting your enjoyment then I am sorry.

On the XP cap just being a tool to make more money selling expansions instead of including it all in the original game: No, it wasn't intended as such. If it was I would have no problem in saying so. I do hope that people buy the expansion but I would not be happy if I felt that their purchase was contrived in any way - i.e. if they didn't feel that the expansion would be fun to play for the new areas and quests that we have designed.

The expansion adds new areas to the world to explore, new quests, higher level spells, items, and monsters. We're trying to make it fun and enjoyable to play. It increases the XP cap to 151,000.

We've decided that if you use a hack program to increase the XP your characters will get, then we will import them "as is" into the expansion and the sequels...hence, if they have more than 89,000 XP, they will keep this in the XP. We don't officially support this - again, because the game is designed like a pen and paper module for level 6-7 characters at the end (XP 89,000) - but if people want to do this, we won't try to stop it. Again, I am sorry if you don't like this decision, but we did and do have good reasons behind it. We try to make the game balanced and fun. I'm very proud of what my team put together, and stand behind the decisions that were made, although I freely admit that there are flaws that can (and are) be(ing) pointed out here. We ARE listening and will do our best to improve our future products.

If you don't like the decisions made in the design of Baldur's Gate, feel free to blame me; as the producer I am ultimately responsible for any flaws, and I respectfully apologize in advance. I've read most of the messages in this thread and will be discussing the issue with the team again to see what ideas we can come up with beyond the above (allowing higher XP's to be imported as such). I'll let you know what we decide, if it is different than what I have discussed above.
