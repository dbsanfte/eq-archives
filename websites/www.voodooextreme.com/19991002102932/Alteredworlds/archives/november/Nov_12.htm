BG bites
Couple of BG biscuits I found lying around that needed to be posted. Greg talks a bit about the release parties everywhere.

Haha...

No, not trying to 'dupe' anyone or anything... just trying to do what fans asked us to...

Many of you have not been on the Fallout2 boards or were fortunate enough (or unfortunate enough) to make it to the Fallout2 launch party. It was a LOT of fun, both for us, and for the fans that made it.

However, the overall feedback we got from Fallout2 fans around the world was that they didn't like the fact that they had to fly out to California to be at the party. People were asking for 'satellite' parties around the country (and in Canada, Europe, Australia, Japan)

So this is the closest thing we could come up with. We can't be EVERYWHERE at the same time, but we thought that some stores and fans would enjoy getting together for the release of the game - you know, meet people in your community that you might want to game with, that sort of thing.

So no, it is not some evil conspiracy to corrupt the souls of American youths... and NO we are certainly NOT delaying anything on the game for this. We just though it would be a cool thing... Whatzamatter? Don't want a free mousepad?

And then we've got this bit on the Final Release candidate. Basically this is about it, the other posts were small.

We have to walk a fine line between total secrecy and total disclosure (because we are now a public company). We cannot make 'forward-looking' statements that people could construe to mean one thing, and end up meaning something else.

Now we all know that a Final Candidate is a good thing. However, we also know (or should know) that there is a big difference between a Final Candidate and a shrink-wrapped box sitting on a retail shelf. The Final Candidate can have all sorts of issues that need to be resolved, etc. Personally, I would love to be able to let people know as we move through every step in the process... my concern is that, on the off-chance we hit a bump in the road, people will start screaming "you SOB's said you had the Final Candidate done in early November!!" (Of course, these are usually the people who know the LEAST about the entertainment software industry, but at that point it really doesn't matter)

Note that this is the single biggest reason why most game companies have stopped talking to their fans during the development process.

OGR on Fallout 2 - Sacrates
Those wacky bastards over at OGR just slapped up their Fallout 2 Review. Basically they say what everyone else says so eloquently: Fallout, but bigger. Head on over and check it out.

This is still a must-have for fans of combat-heavy RPGs. Fallout 2 is built around the time-honored motto of this sort of game: Travel to fascinating places. Meet interesting people. Kill them. Take their stuff. Repeat as necessary. It executes this formula exceedingly well. It's unfortunate that the game as shipped is marred by bugs and problems, but it's still great fun. It will be even better once Black Isle Studios and Interplay run it through quality assurance one more time. So grab your Pip Boy, smack a magazine into that FAL, and get ready to rock the wasteland. Just be sure you're prepared for some bumps in the desert as you push your Highwayman towards the sunset.

Daily U:A Dose - Bowmeister
As always, Sith over at Sith Dragon's U:A page is dishing out the goods on Ultima:Ascension. More of the Avatar lovin goodness we've come to expect, along with the first mention of pre-ordering U:A. First is a bit from Yos on NPC AI...

We use both AI and scripting to create creature and NPC behavior in our game. Most creatures and NPC's have specific AI based on certain needs or percived threats (i.e. mice will go after cheese and monsters will automatically attack creatures that are unlike themselves), but we have a very powerful scripting system that allows us to give specific characters and creatures specific behaviors and actions. So, when you play Ascension, you will encounter creatures that are operating on basic motivations and you will encounter tightly scripted scenarios where creatures have unique actions.

Then Seth on items in hand, specifically torches...

Our present plan is to have shields and torches shown in the Avatar's hands. We are still working on this, so exactly how this will work is not known. Unlit torches will act the same as lit torches.

Using items (other then weapons) as weapons, is something we are discussing.

and U:A Pre-orders :)...

There seems to be a lot of interest in doing a preorder. This is more of a Sales & Marketing issue, but we will be sure to let them know we think it's a great idea. We should have more info on this in the next month or so.

and finally on combat...

Combat is something that is being worked on as we speak. I don't want to get into it until we are finished. I will say that combat is in real time, although it will be more strategic, then arcade. We are working hard to make combat exciting for all types of players.

Check back in a month or so, and we will have more details on combat.

BG here and there's - Sacrates
Of course what would the BG boards be without the developers? Greg Peterson and Don Whatshisname :) were one the boards posting some fruitfull thoughts. First is about release parties.

About now people should be asking themselves the question: "The game is almost done, the boxes and stuff are done, the manual and command card is almost done... what could those Marketing folks be doing with their time?"

The answer: "Making cool Baldur's Gate premiums"

Along the lines of the famous saying "the devil finds work for idle hands" the marketing team has been busy making cool stuff for loyal fans...

Baldur's Gate t-shirts? We got em...
Baldur's Gate mouse pads? Say no more...
Baldur's Gate map posters? How many hundred do you need...
Baldur's Gate big boxes & coming soon boxes? Done...
6' tall big bad evil villain posters? We're off to the presses...

So how could you get your hands on any of this stuff? Well... it's not as easy as it sounds. We are putting together Baldur's Gate retail 'launch party' kits so that people all over the country (and Canada) can have their own mini BG launch parties. HOWEVER, these parties HAVE to be affiliated with a retail store. We will be sending out the specifics of the program to stores shortly, but if you have friends that work in/for a computer store, and they want to host a launch party, send me an email and we will send them a party kit with a PILE of goodies to hand out at the party.

More details coming soon... but if you know anyone at a store, get them to fired up and get ready to help us launch the best RPG of all time!

Next one is on guessing the actual release date. Basically what he is trying to say here is to not expect it before December. :)

on release date estimates: Release dates are always estimates. They aren't there to mislead the public. Its when a developer thinks the game will be done. Things can affect this estimate, whether its new features added or something taking longer than expected.

Why do we feel the need to advertise release dates? There is a lot of pressure to give a release date, just look at this message board. Very few companies can truly say, "It's done when its done."

If no one knows the game is coming out, no one is going to know when it hits the shelves and no one will buy it.

Here's one on violence etc.

Yes there are a few changes for the low violence versions, but they aren't of the swap "blue oil" for "blood" variety. Mainly things like replacement artwork.

ROM Review - Bowmeister
I really gotta check out the demo of this puppy, since everyone and their pet goblin is talking about it. The guys over at Computer Games Online have tossed up a review of Monolith Production's Rages of Mages. It's a good little review, although it doesn't seem to cut the game down for out of date graphics like many of the others I've seen.

With the reemergence of the role-playing game (RPG) and the continued popularity of real-time strategy (RTS), it's not surprising that a game has merged those two gameplay elements. However, unlike the recent strategy and 3D action hybrids, games blending RPG and RTS feel integrated and whole, as both game types lend themselves to thoughtful play.

While unlikely to satisfy hardcore fans of either genre, Rage of Mages is a fairly high-quality synergy of the two genres; in fact, it's an almost perfect split down the middle. Take the stats, character building, treasure hoarding, and fantasy setting most RPGs and combine it with the style of combat and interface of most RTS games, and you'll know what you're in for.
