General Lotto Rules

 

These rules apply to any items that drop that aren't covered by the DKP system.

Background
There are two different philosophies when it comes to doing lottos, and both are equally valid.  The first is that everyone present when an item drop is equally entitled to it, as each person there put forth the same time and risk.  This is one way of doing lottos, and it is completely valid.  This is not, however, the way lottos are done in Afterlife.

The other way of doing lottos is to give each item to the person that can best use it.  This is the most efficient use of resources, results in the fewest "bad lottos," and this is the system we use.

General Application
Designated character Time spent camping Can vs. Will use. The general philosophy is that the character that can best use the item gets the item.

When armor drops, the people that can use the item can enter in for it, and no others.  If one person can benefit more from it than the other people present, then that is the person that should get it.

For example, say a weapon drops that warriors can use, and there are two warriors present.  If one warrior has a weapon of nearly the same power, and another warrior does not, the second warrior should get the item.  The item is of more use to him, and giving him the item is a better use of its power than giving it to the first warrior, to whom it may be an insignificant upgrade, or soon become bank trash.

Multiple characters/one player
Any character under level 46 is considered a "twink", any character level 46 or above is a real character.  Real characters always come before twinks.

If a player with more than one real character is camping in an area that has items that more than one of his characters can use, he must specify before an item drops which character he will enter the lotto as, otherwise the currently played character is assumed.

This means that if a player with a 46+ warrior and cleric is camping something with an Afterlife group with his warrior, and a cleric item drops, the warrior/cleric can't enter, because he didn't specify he was there as his cleric beforehand.  If, however, the player says beforehand that he is there as his cleric, he can enter for any cleric items, but not for any warrior items.

If there are no players present with designated real characters that can use an item, then the item is lotto'd amongst any non-designated characters that can use he item.

For example, say a cleric item drops and the player above with a warrior and a cleric is present as his warrior.  If no other warriors are present, then he can enter the lotto as his cleric.

If there are no real characters, designated or not, that can use the item (use in this case means would actually use it, i.e. if the item would be sold, even if it can be equipped, it won't be used, and thus a player can't enter in for it to the exclusion of others after all As with everything, there are exceptions to this rule. Say a player Because we are not a random collection of people, we can distribute armor in a fair manner, or at least far more fair than a random groups can.  To this end we will strive to distribute armor based purely on time invested.  It is up to all those of a given class to pay attention, and to know when someone else present has more time invested, and to react accordingly by deferring to the older, in terms of time invested, player.

In those cases where it can't be determined who has spent the most time, the item will be lotto'd amongst those with equivalent times.

It is up to every player to notice and remember the time spent by others of the same class.

Time invested, in every place mentioned above, is time invested per piece of armor gained.  If one player has spent more time, but has proportionally more armor, then the two players are equivalent.  If two have spent equal time and one has 2 more pieces of armor, the more armored player would stay out of any lottos until both players are equal in armor obtained.

Note that this isn't total armor the player is wearing, this is total armor obtained in the same time invested.  When comparing two players it's quite likely that one had armor before the other started going to planes, in which case the time invested counts from that point on, ignoring original armor.

Completing the Set
In any case where the time per armor piece is equivalent, and one person has more armor than another, the lesser equipped person should stay out.  This is necessary because as a player gets more armor, the chances of finding the remaining pieces becomes smaller and smaller.   Note that this is only true when the time invested is equivalent or near equivalent.

Multiple Characters
If a player has more than one character that gets armor in a plane, they may win items for either character.  The same rules apply as above, with armor for both characters counting.

 

 

Plane Armor

These rules apply to non-god/dragon items of any kind, but primarily to planar armor.

Background
If everyone played equally, each item found would be lotto'd and given to someone until everyone present has one item, then any items dropped thereafter would be given in the same order as before. The lotto exists only to determine the initial order, when all those present have the same amount of time invested.

Unfortunately, not everyone plays equally.  Since in random groupings there is no way of knowing who has spent the most time, each new group starts over from scratch doing random lottos for each item. Since people come and go, sometimes every item is lotto'd across everyone present, even including those that won earlier, since the group composition is now different.  Afterlife can do better than this.

Theory and Practice

Because we are not a random collection of people, we can distribute armor in a fair manner, or at least far more fair than a random groups can.  To this end we will strive to distribute armor based purely on time invested.  It is up to all those of a given class to pay attention, and to know when someone else present has more time invested, and to react accordingly by deferring to the older, in terms of time invested, player.

In those cases where it can't be determined who has spent the most time, the item will be lotto'd amongst those with equivalent times.

It is up to every player to notice and remember the time spent by others of the same class.

Time invested, in every place mentioned above, is time invested per piece of armor gained.  If one player has spent more time, but has proportionally more armor, then the two players are equivalent.  If two have spent equal time and one has 2 more pieces of armor, the more armored player would stay out of any lottos until both players are equal in armor obtained.

Note that this isn't total armor the player is wearing, this is total armor obtained in the same time invested.  When comparing two players it's quite likely that one had armor before the other started going to planes, in which case the time invested counts from that point on, ignoring original armor.

 

Completing the Set

Misc

There are certain unwritten rules we've been following which need to be written down to prevent any mix-ups from happening. There are certain exceptions, consult an officer if in question.

Haste Items
If you already have a Haste item, for example a Runed Bolster Belt, and want to go in for a Cloak of Flames, if you win the cloak you must be willing to turn over the lesser item. Haste items are highly sought after and we want everyone who wishes to have one to have a shot at one.
