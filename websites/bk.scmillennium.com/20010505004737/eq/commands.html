This is a listing of all the commands in EverQuest the game and the EQ chat rooms.
EverQuest Commands
Chat Screen Commands

Names in red in the player list are 989 staff.
Names proceeded by 989 are official Tech Support staff.
Names in blue are official non-staff guides.

#goto - go to whatever channel that player is in.
#ignore - lists all the players who are ignoring you.
#ignore handle - start ignoring a player.
#make - make your own chat channel.
#myip - reports your ip number.
#pagehelp - pages all staff on the system, if you require help.
#priv - Find out your privelege status level.
#report - report bad language, spam.
#channel allow Allow player to access channel.
#channel kick - Bar player from channel.
#squelch - same as #ignore.
#unignore - stops ignoring a player.
#whereis - find out what channel a player is in.

In-Game Commands

/anon or /a - Makes you Anonymous in /who and /who all, preventing other players from seeing your class, level, and current location.

/assist - If you target a player who is engaged in combat the /assist command will automatically target for you whoever the player is fighting at the moment.

/autosplit - Automatically splits any coins you get witht he rest of your party. All members must have this on for it to work properly.

/bug or /b - Takes you to a bug-reporting screen.

/consent or /c - Gives the player permission to loot your corpse.

/consider or /con - Gives vital statistics of a target PC or NPC.

/duel or /d - Challenges targeted PC to a duel. To accept they type /d with you targeted as well. Duel is in effect until one of you dies or leaves the zone.

/em or : - Emotes the text. Some have animations with them, most do not. Some emoticons are /wave, /cry, /nod, /panic, /bow, /cheer, and /rofl.

/feedback - Takes you to the comments/feedback screen.

/gsay or /g - Text is seen by all in your group.

/ignore - Turns off all text from that player.

/loc - Gives the location in numeric coordinates of the current target. If nothing is targeted it gives your coordinates.

/ooc -

/pet - Gives orders to a charmed or summoned pet. Commands for pets are:
/pet guard here
/pet guard me
/pet follow me
/pet attack
/pet back off
/pet as you were
I don't think I need to explain those :).

/petition - Used to request assistance for a GM. The more detailed the text is the quicker the assistance can be rendered. (This is usually as effective as hitting your computer with a large hammer).

/played - Gives you the birthdate and hours played with your character.

/shout - text is seen across zone

/shownames (on or off) - Turns on or off visible names above PCs and NPCS. Red names are PvP(pks).

/split # # # # - Splits a defined amount of money with your group. Pp, gp, sp, and cp respectively.

/tell - Text is seen by named player anywhere on the server.

/time - Gives you the time of day in Norrath.

/who - Lists all players in your zone.
B/who corpse - Lists all the corpses a player has in the current zone.

/who all - Lists all players in the world. Note:/who and /who all are also usable with masks to look for certain players or classes online. Ex.: /who wiz all - searches for wizards. /who ae all - searches for characters whose names begin with ae.

Guild Commands

/guildinvite - Invite a person to join the guild. If this person is to be an officer, include the argument (as in Oscar) for the rank. Usable by the Guild Leader and Officers, but only the Guild Leader can make officers. You would also use this function to promote a member to an officer.

/guildremove - Remove a member from the guild. Only the Guild Leader may remove Officers. You may use this command on yourself to leave the guild.

/guilddelete - Disband your entire guild. Only the Guild Leader may use this command.

/guildstatus - Will return information on whether the named person is a member, an Officer, or the Guild Leader.

/guildleader - Transfer guild leadership to

/guildsay - Sends a message to all guild members

/guildwar - Initiates a guild war against the guild of the Guild Leader which appears in the field. sets the duration in real days for the guild war. This defaults to one real day if left blank. The opposing Guild Leader must send the same command with the of the enemy Guild Leader to fully initiate the conflict.

/guildpeace - Ends a guild war before the duration has expired. is the name of the opposing Guild Leader. Both sides must send this command to end the war prematurely.
