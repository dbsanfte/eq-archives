 Ranger Profile

Important Attributes: Strength, Stamina and Agility have all been honed by years of living in the wild while a Ranger's Wisdom has been sharpened by the necessity of survival.

Races: Human, Half Elf, Wood Elf

Specific Skills: Rangers learn a number of skills related to woodcraft and survival, including Tracking, Forage (level 3), Sneak (lvl 10), and Hide (lvl 25). Rangers also pick up some of the Warrior's skills, including Taunt, Dodge (level 8), Bind Wound (lvl 15), Dual Wield (lvl 15), Parry (lvl 18), and Double Attack (lvl 20). Rangers also have their own brand of magic, which they begin learning at level 9. Rangers will learn magic related skills to fuel their spells: Abjure (level 9), Alter (lvl 9), Conjure (lvl 9), Divinate (lvl 9), Evoke (lvl 9), and Meditate (lvl 14).

Spell Listing:
 Level 9:

  • Skin Like Wood - Add AC and MAX HP
  • Glimpse - Brief Telescope sight
  • Minor Healing - Healing
  • Endure Fire - Adds to fire resistance
  • Flame Lick - AC on Target and some damage
  • Lull Animal - Lower animal reactions
  • Snare - Lower movement rate of target
Level 15:
  • Burst of Fire - Direct damage
  • Cure Poison - self explanatory
  • Camouflage - Invisible
  • Grasping roots - Root w/damage
  • Invoke Lightning - Area Effect Direct Damage
  • Feet Like Cat - +Agi
  • Thistlecoat - Personal Armor
Level 22
  • Light Healing - Healing
  • Enduring Breath - Water breathing
  • Ignite - Direct Damage
  • Ward Summoned - Direct Damage to summoned creatures
  • Harmony - Calms animals outdoors
  • Skin Like Rock - Add AC and max HP
  • Bind Sight - See thru target and fatigue loss
Redcloak's Thoughts: The Ranger is a mix of the warrior and the druid. They are closely in tune with the wilderness, and their bowmanship is unmatched. I haven't played much as a Ranger so that's all I can say at this time.
