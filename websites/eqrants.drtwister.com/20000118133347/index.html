Calling it quits...

In case you hadn't noticed, I have exactly been updating the site very frequently lately. There is a reason for that. I wanted to give myself a week to take a break and come up with some new stuff to write about. I don't play anymore and I have actually canceled my account and removed the game from my hard drive. I don't think it is fair to you guys to have me comment on the game when I can't do it from a players perspective. This site started as a place for me to bitch about things that I felt were wrong within the game. In the end I found that camping for hours on end just to get one damn item wasn't fun. Fighting to get a kill in an overcrowded zone wasn't fun. A lot of things about this game stopped being fun. So this is it, the final post on EQ rants. I will leave the site here for as long as Dr. TwisTer let's it stay. Feel free to use the message board still. I will stop by every now and again and see what you have to say. I hope to move on to some bigger and better things. I have some ideas for other sites that I want to start, possibly right here on the Dr. TwisTer Network, possibly on my own completely. Just keep an eye open, I am sure you will see me resurface somewhere. Until then.....enjoy!

Wednesday, January 5th 

The Good, the Bad and the Ugly

I usually don't update this late at night, but I didn't get a chance to today because every one of the network printers where I work went out. Talk about some pissed off people! But that is a rant all unto itself. Today I want to talk about a game. It is a game that shouldn't have been released when it was. It is a game that I played through to the finish somehow, fighting off horrible game play bugs with a flaming sword. It was a game that was supposed to bring a satisfying end to an era, but instead disappointed fans all over. In case you haven't guessed by now, that game is Ultima 9: Ascension. I'll probably give some spoilers in my little mini-review so if you don't want to know much about the game, I would suggest careful reading.

The Good: 

Ultima 9 probably has the best graphics of any RPG ever, the cities are all unique and have their own certain flair. The city of Yew is completely up in trees and the Buc's Dens docks are pretty cool. Also the world is pretty much all interactive. You can pick up almost anything you see and move it or manipulate it somehow. You can definitely tell there was a lot of work put in to making the world as lifelike and unique as possible. There is a ton of voice acting in the game also, which I always dig. It wasn't done too horribly either! The Avatar did talk a bit like Sylvester Stallone and looked a bit like Woody Harrelson, but they are both pretty cool so I didn't mind. There are also some return visits from some old Ultima friends, and some are not exactly the way you would remember them!

The Bad:

The good part wasn't all that long and didn't cover too much. That is because I pretty much covered all the good points that the game has. On with the bad. The pretty graphics come at an unbelievable price. Game play is so slow that sometimes Ultima 9 feels unplayable. Walking into a dungeon results in some decent frame rates, if you have a good computer. But when you walk out of a dungeon you feel as though you are walking straight into a 486 with 8 megs of ram. The game slows to a crawl and the hard drive starts thrashing like a drunk white guy at a heavy metal concert (Metallica Rocks!!!). Turning the graphics options down didn't seem to improve performance much at all. There are ways to adjust things in the .ini file so that you get a more playable game, but the damn game should have been playable right out of the box! There is just no excuse on Origin's part to have released a game that felt so un-optimized. 

Bugs? We got yer bugs right here. Do you like bugs that cause games to not realize what you had already accomplished and won't let you progress any further? You know, showstopper bugs? Then U9 is for you! Do you like bugs that cause you to constantly crash to the desktop at the most inopportune times? Then, once again, U9 is for you! The bugs in this game are too numerous to mention. At one point I neared the end of the game and accomplished a fairly large goal. Well after that Raven (the Avatars love interest) is supposed to offer to take you to the Isle of the Avatar. Well for some reason she didn't and you are left to sail there yourself. Not a big deal I suppose, I am the Avatar after all! I can sail the mighty seas!. Well once you get there the Isle has a barrier around it to prevent you from going there too early. Because the game failed to realize that I had accomplished the needed goal to make it to the island, it wouldn't let me on the island (after scanning many message boards looking for a solution, I found that a lot of other people had the very same problem...it's just sad). Rather then wait for another bug filled patch from Origin I had to use a 'Fly' cheat (think of the noclip option in the Quake games) to get on the island. There are various other bugs that are a lot like that one too. I have also fallen through the world on many occasions. It is actually rather humorous to find yourself floating through a black void...until you realize that you haven't saved the game recently and have to do a bunch of stuff over.

The Ugly:

The ugly is just the game in general. There are so few redeeming qualities about it that writing about the good actually requires digging for almost anything. The ending left me feeling unfulfilled to say the least. Rather then let us feel  like the Avatar ascends to something bigger they just (BIG SPOILER HERE) kill him off and leave the player to imagine that he went on to something greater. It was hardly a way to bring such a series of games to a close. I am going to give the game 2 out of 5 Whatevers® (everyone picks Stars or something, so the standard around here is going to be Whatevers®) and that is being generous.

Agree with me? Disagree with me? I am interested to hear the thoughts of some of my readers that might have played Ultima 9: Depression.
