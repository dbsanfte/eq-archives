Days of Future Past...

This is a quick page I've put up for anyone who happens to stumble across it. I came across a couple old BBS Listing Archives from back in the glory days of the BBS. Back when the Internet was in its infancy the BBS reigned supreme.

Back in my day my platform of choice was the Atari 800 and later the Atari ST and the Intel 386, the later 2 systems of which I ran a small struggling BBS as a hobby on and off around the years of 1990-1992. I was on FidoNet with a group from Ben Lomond, we had our own little user group, life was good and simple.

I recently acquired several old Atari Systems which took me to search for BBS programs I might be able to setup and run over the Net.

I stumbled across the name Pyrzqxgl in my search. Don't ask me how, I honestly can't remember how I did it (did not directly enter it), and following the link brought on one of the worst flashbacks of my earlier BBS days that I've ever had.

Pyrzqxgl was/is? a BBS run in Santa Cruz County by a system operator named Tachyon. You can read all about it on her pages. Pyrzqxgl was a tree based (I think) message system renowned for having some of the most interesting people as well as creative people you've ever had the pleasure of not meeting IRL, although spend a year or so reading and eventually you might meet some of them IRL. I did on a couple occasions as I recall, but I never got too close, something I'm kicking myself for not being more outgoing in my younger days.

I went under the name FastBlaster, god knows where I came up with that one. I think it was my tendency to be a Video Game Addict (an addiction I'm still not cured of to this day) and my stubbornness to stick with something no matter how silly..

Now a days I tend to favor Android8675 (Sing it with me...867-5309) as an alias. I was using Android9, but I found it hard to make accounts under that alias and 8675 is a number no one tends to pick up on, and it's easy as hell to remember. Maybe too easy.
