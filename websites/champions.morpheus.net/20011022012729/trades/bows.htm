Tips

I noticed that Issa put a few tips about how to purchase and make trade items so I made up a few little notes of my own to help everyone out too.

1) Get a crude stien and as much +cha stuff as you can get, the boost of say 20 cha makes my arrow parts drop ALOT, for example field poiint tips cost me 7 copper, with my buying stuff on the cost 5. This may not seem like alot of difference, but when you go to buy a Darkwood staff at 275pp and add the +20 cha and it drops to 230-240 it makes a BIG difference.

2) As Issa Stated, Get as much + Int items as you can gather up too.

a) higher Int means more skill increases

b) higher wisdom means less failures

Also look around before you start for a high level enchanter, he may be able to buff your Int, wis and Cha! which will equal ALOT of cash saved!

3) If you are a class that can "sneak" Use it when needed! if anything cons indifferent or worse then hit sneak, and con them then. I am still apprehensive in my home town to the merchants using sneak I get much better prices. This will also work on merchants whom you are not KOS with but also will not talk to you, you can sell to them. Make sure you are behind them when you sneak though.

4) Believe it or not, this works! Go do something that you can easily increase skill in other than your trade. IE: Swimming. Skill hits are Random, just like doing the /random 100 stuff, they come in spurts. So get a bunch of parts, put on your +int and +wis stuff, get your buffs from an enchanter, and jump into the pool, every time you get a skill hit in swimming (or whatever it is) then hit combine on your kit. Now swimming obviously wont work for smithing, but anything you can carry with you will, Fletching, JC, whatever. kind of an Exploit I suppose but not really.

5) As Issa said again, BUY IN BULK! Don't put on all your +cha equipment to just buy 20 pieces of stuff, Buy ALOT! otherwise you'll spend DAYS buying fletching and selling.

6) Don't give up! I spent 1500pp on a single skill hit in fletching.... From 175-176. Once I got it, I zipped through to 185 with about 200pp so it all evens out.

Ard, you may wanna put this under a general trades section or something, thought it may help out some people.
