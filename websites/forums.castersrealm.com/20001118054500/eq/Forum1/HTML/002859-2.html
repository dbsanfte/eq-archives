I'm quite shocked at the number of responses involving melee classes. I can't imagine any melee class being efficient in a tandem, with the exception of rogues with a partner casting fear.

Any other melee is just too inefficient regardless of complete healing. Any combo that actually has to depend on each other to survive is also inherently weaker. If things go awry and the two get separated, then both will likely die.

The strongest tandems will be able to solo well, and also have compatible abilities to make them even more efficient. Combinations of shamans, druids, necros, mages, and enchanters will always beat a duo involving a melee class. Wizards have too low of a damage/mana ratio compared to the DoTs and pets of other classes. Bards enter the picture at 46th level as a superior solo/tandem class.

Note that this refers to the point when the classes get their defining abilities. Let's face it, soloing to 15th in any class isn't that tough. So Countess, while the cleric/warrior combo works, it'll NEVER be efficient enough to be the "best" at making xp. I'm not even sure if the manastone and j-boots would be enough to put this combo on par with the others listed above.

If you need proof, I'm sure someone could arrange a demonstration in the Burning Woods, Dreadlands, Rathe Mountains, or any other common soloist zone.

Let me chime in here with Shaman/Monk, at around level 30.

Monks strength? Massive damage.
Monks weakness? Downtime and low tanking ability.

Shaman can heal and haste (and otherwise buff AC HP, etc.) on Monk.
Shaman can debuff (slow) the MoB.

Shaman: Emergency crowd control, SoW.
Monk: FD in an emergency, SoW on.

Shaman can melee or not as mana availability dictates.

Quite low risk.

Result: Tanking ability of monk increased, monk damage output gets nutty, and downtime eliminated due to heals/regen.

This works really well against non-caster mobs -- pull blue giants all day long with really no downtime at all.

For me, (the shaman) this has been my best XP gaining combo.
