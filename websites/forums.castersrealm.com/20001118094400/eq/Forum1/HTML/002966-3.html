Actually twinking an int caster with 200 int doesn't do much good. That's because although they have more mana in their full tank than the person with 120 int, once they have used it they only get mana back at the rate at which they meditate it back, and you can't change that with any item. A twinky wizard in Bonecaster Robe and other gear with 200 int will regen mana back at exactly the same rate as a same-level non-twinked wizard. In the long run over a several hour camp, the fact that the twinky wizard had more mana for the first 10 minutes starts to dwindle into statistical insignificance.

What helps int twinkers is the ability to add lots of AC and HP stuff, reducing the likelihood that they will accidentally die when crap gets outta hand. When the group is running for the zone chances are the non-twink will die before the twink caster dies.

As for melee classes, they are truly the ones who benefit the most from twinking. My lvl 14 paladin twink (who is only about half twinked, he still has 4 pieces of bronze armor on for example) can kill multiple blue orcs at the same time (two to three) and end the battle having taken less than a bubble or two of dmg. And then he can simply heal himself (albeit with a zillion casts of the puny lvl 9 pally heal) and do it again. Since melee types don't use a consumable resource such as mana to do dmg, twinking a melee type with a weapon capable of achieving the level-based dmg cap will have a huge effect, and equipping that twink with so much AC that the mobs miss him three out of four swings really tips the balance. If my pally were twinked with *all* of the things I am working on getting him, no blue mob (except casters) would even do more than scratch him. No caster can be so twinked.

And personally my twinky paladin only likes grouping with other twinks. I have helped my share of people in this game. I will continue to help more people, and I help people a lot as is, but when I log in for 30 minutes to play my twink (if I had more time I would log in with Parm) I am just out to have some fun, not give private EQ lessons to some true newbie. And I am not saying that *all* untwinked players are true newbies, nor am I saying that I am better than newbies. It is a value judgement, where I feel it is more valuable for my twink to level as fast as he can during that particular 20 to 30 minutes than it is for my twink to group with some untwinked player who will, in a very real way, simply drag down the combined effectiveness of that particular group. I don't dislike these people, I just don't choose at that particular time to group with them. It's not personal. Parm groups would complete and total strangers all the time. That's the way an enchanter fights, and I enjoy it, but when I am in grouping mode for several hours that's one thing, when I am in twink-fighting mode for 20 or 30 minutes that is completely another thing. Don't be offended by this.

And to those who claim that a twink never learns the proper skills for their class, frankly this is bullshiit. The skills that are required for any class often change as the player levels. What were appropriate tactics and skills at lvls 1 - 20 are often just going to get you killed at lvl 30. Keep in mind that twinks are only really well beyond their intended element in the low levels. By the time that warrior whom you twinked with full Crafted hits lvl 30, normal warriors would actually be expected to have at least a couple if not most pieces of Crafted already, so you are much closer to what you "should be" by then. By the time you hit the 40s all that gear that you uber-twinked your character with are now standard equipment, and you have no real advantage over those who reached the 40s untwinked. So, as you gain in level you are forced by the sheer fact that EQ requires you to spend so much time and kill so many creatures, to learn the skills that your class needs.

Anybody who has a diverse playing experience (ie: not sitting in the same one spot killing the same mobs over and over for 10 levels) will have learned just as much if not more by the time they hit lvl 30 to 35 as someone who was untwinked and reached those lvls "the hard way".

So get off your high-horses. Twinking is merely the way people with often 50+ played days avoid some of the tedium of the low levels. When my pally groups with another twink I really like it, because we advance fast and both of us know what we are doing. I have yet to really run into another twink who is clueless.

I even know a former member of my guild who sold his druid when he was like lvl 24. That druid (under new ownership) is now in the low 50s, and trust me the guy behind that keyboard knows his class well, and knows how to play it. It may have taken him several levels to really figure stuff out in the mid 20s, but it didn't really take that long and he took off and excelled rapidly from then on.

Unless you are powerleveled, I don't care how highly twinked you are, by the time you reach the mid-levels it will still take you many hours each lvl, and you will be grouping with a lot of non-twinks, making your progress as a group just slightly faster than it would be if you were not twinked.

Let me clarify my position a little.

My main character is lvl 44. He groups with all sorts of people all the time. Talking about twinks and non-twinks at this lvl is a bit silly.

I have a few secondary characters. My current pet secondary character is Parmighty, a lvl 15 dwarven paladin. I only play this paladin when I want to play EQ and I don't have enough time to find a group for Parm. Parm pretty much has to group to play.

I want to level up my twinky Paladin as fast as I can. To that end, I don't want to have to give private EQ lessons to some true new player who doesn't know what is going on. To play efficiently (ie: in a worthwhile manner) during, say, the 30 minutes that I have at any one time to play Parmighty, I have to either solo, or else play with people who already know what is going on. In addition, playing with people who have newbie equipment just drags down the rate at which I can lvl this twink. It is quite simple. If my paladin groups with a warrior in patchwork armor and a fine steel long sword, one of two things will happen. Either he will hit Taunt constantly and get himself completely thrashed, or else I will have the mob on me 100% of the time due to my superior weaponry, and he will just stand there not really contributing much dmg to the group dmg output, but will still suck up experience, in which case I would have been better off soloing in the first place.

I can imagine that at some point in the future I will be willing to devote more time to playing Parmighty, and he will have reached a lvl where it will become necessary to group in medium to large groups and do dungeons and such in the conventional style, and at that time I will quite likely group with plenty of non-twinks.

In addition, since even first-time players gain equipment and such as they level, at some point the distinction between twinks and non-twinks starts to blur as the non-twinks twink themselves. Of course a lvl 30 paladin with Deepwater armor will still stand out, but it is not unreasonable at that point for a good, enthused paladin to have earned a complete or partially complete suit of the Armor of Ro, or Crafted for warriors, or whatever. And grouping with first-time players in Crafted or Ro or whatever is not the same at lvl 30 as grouping with someone in tattered or leather or mostly banded or whatever at lvl 15. My cleric friend Roechek had a full suit of the cleric quest armor that he gained by himself and in cooperation with other like-leveled clerics by his low 30s for example, and that is his first character.

I don't hate non-twinks. My paladin has in fact grouped with non-twinks before. He will likely do it again. However, as a general practice (notice I didn't say as a general rule) I will tend to either solo or else group with players who are twinked *and* are experienced players, out of purely practical considerations. If I group with a twinked player who turns out to be clueless, then I will disband and go play somewhere else.
