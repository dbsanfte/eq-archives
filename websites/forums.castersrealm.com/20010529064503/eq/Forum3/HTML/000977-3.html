In my playing time which is less than a month I have noticed the following:

Every starting city guilds have quests to aid the new characters to level up to 8-12. Then it stops, no more guild specific quests. You are now on your own to gain levels and set goals which in my opinion sucks for the casual player. It is already hard leveling solo(casual players have a hard time grouping), now once they "hear" about a good xp area or an item to improve their characters stats they go and it is overcamped by classes that cant even use the item and are way too high for the spawn itself. If you ask the players there why are they camping the spot the replies are usually "it is for guild memebers" or "we are aiding a much lower level character get this" or "the xp/loot is so awsome here....."

Realistically there is no way to stop overcamping by serious players. Verant could impose xp/loot penalties based on time at spawn, but they wont.

If Verant was serious about attempting to cater to its players and give a surrealism to the game it could, but they wont.

Verant could easily make class specific items no-drop and/or code items so only that class(es) could pick it up, but they wont.

Verant could also keep specific(only that guild. example: only an erudin mage could get xp from completing an erudin mage quest and no one else) guild quests going all the way up to the current max level 60. Offer special items(no drop/class specific) as well as xp. That would keep the high level characters away from farming low level spawns. It would also give the casual player goals that they can realistically achieve, but Verant wont do this.

Verant could also use the one thing that I havent seen used in gameplay so far. The deities. That is an option I think that has vast opportunities to increase gameplay in every aspect. From character creation, to gameplay, to quests, to certain items, etc.., but they wont use it.

The reason I see Verant not changing or improving any major aspect is that it fears it would lose its serious players.

Think about it some person has spent a god awful amount of time getting his/her character up to level 50-60. Now lets say Verant comes out and makes a(or several) major change(s) to the game(quests, items, deity aids, etc). This level 50-60 may lose some of its items, unable to obtain some items, deity choice starts hurting the characters development(the past comes back to haunt it etc). What do you think this person would do? Verant fears this person would quit and go on to something else, sure there are those who would shrug it off and continue or restart, but how many?

I am sure there are alot of game improvement ideas floating around the game design arena in Verant, but nothing major to improve gameplay/surrealism for the casual player will ever happen.

There is usually a GM runned auction too for some unique items that goes into 1000s of plats. The stats on these items should be unbelivable. What these festivals does is it bleeds money out of the game. Many people will hoard their money when the festival comes around. This, I think is a good system to help the casual gamer get things they otherwise cannot attain due to time constrain. What may also happen is that power-gamers will go hunting for the best money generating mobs and free up some of the camps for the quest items that you wanted.
