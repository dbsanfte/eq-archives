I think that the issue of the casual player is more serious than simply whether or not certain items are attainable. It's a double whammy in the truest sense of the term: Because you cannot play the game in 5-10 hour blocks, you are unable to obtain a substantial number of the desirable items in the game... but couple that with the fact that you will be "level-lapped" by everybody you know so quickly that there will likely be nobody around to assist you with these quests, and suddenly 75% of the desirable items in the game are well beyond your reach.

I don't know that I can think of a "quick solution" to help smooth out the disparity between people who have 50 hours a week to play and those who can only spare 10 - but it seems like ther has to be a solution that's equitable.

I truly love the adventure and comeraderie of everquest, but it's very upsetting to know that I really just can't play the game unless I have a block of 3 hours to dedicate to it. This might be less true for melee classes, but the wizard class (and any caster class without a pet) is all but crippled wihtout a group - so there's no way to even begin making any progress in the game in under an hour.

Isn't there a creative solution somewhere? Could there be "solo zones" where grouping is not permitted or experience is only given for one hour of gameplay? Couldn't they create quests that still take similar amounts of effort to the existing quests, but could be accomplished in more bite-sized pieces? Puzzles of intelligence and magic rather than the current "run all over the globe and kill stuff" bounties that pass for quests?

Ultimately, yes - it's just a game. But more and more, it's a game that frustrates me because it could be so much more with a little help and a few cherries thrown in for freshness now and again.
