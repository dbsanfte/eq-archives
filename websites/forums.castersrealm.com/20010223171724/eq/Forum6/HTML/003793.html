There are valid reasons for at least adding the /pet no attack command.

The margin for error between 55-60 is razor thin. One broken mez means you all die as a party even if you were doing great up until that point. Your pets act like wet tissue paper as far as how long they can delay a mobs before dieing at this level.

And while your enchanter can stun and mes a mob your pet is engaged with it's still not assured he'll get it the mob on the first attempt while you back off and move your pet to a safe distance.

You can midigate pet control concerns and limit thier impact by smart play but can't eliminate them.

-not sending a pet until everything is mezed
-not sitting when pulls come in
-move pet and yourself as far from the multiple mobs as possible so loose swings don't agro your pet.
-keeping a tight vigil on your pet
-know the bad pathing areas and how your pet will behave around them
-don't let your pet chase mobs
....

The list goes on but the final conclusion is that regardless of what you do your pet will engage a mob eventually that you didn't intend it to. And this is the danger of pets. And from a damage per evening standpoint why not get the tank that does more damage and is smart damage absorption.

Think about that before you dismiss pet control as a non issue.
