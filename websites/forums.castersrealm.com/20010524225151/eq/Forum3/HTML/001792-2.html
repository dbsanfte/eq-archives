quote:
In a lvl 45 group doing a dungeon or whatever, given the choice between taking a paladin, a shadowknight, or a ranger, all same lvl, all well equipped, I will have to think hard about the pally or the shadowknight to decide which of them joins the group first, but the ranger definitely joins last of the three.

This is natural, the ranger is not intended to be a primary tank, the ranger is a secondary tank. In a group, you take a primary tank (warrior, pally, sk) and you take a secondary tank (ranger, rogue, monk). One takes damage well, but does not deal it out, the other deals huge damage but does not take it well. I think the whole idea of rangers comparing themselves to warriors is ludicrous, and I think Verant is stupid to listen. To compare apples to apples, rangers should have been compared to rogues and monks from the start. If rangers are a great deal less survivable than are the other seconday tanks, then they should have been fixed. If they take damage and have similar hitpoints to the other offensive classes, then they should have been left as is.

My impression now is that rangers are looking to get the best of both worlds. They want to do the damage of a monk, while being able to tank like a warrior. I think it's sad how Verant continually caves to every single complaint by a class that decides to be overly vocal. Indeed, let's look at what's been done for rangers since Kunark came out: They got jolt, an aggro lowering spell . . . they got "combat archery" and can do double damage with their bows now . . . they go their mana pool more or less doubled . . . and now, they're getting an increase in their defensive skills to caps that are approaching warriors.

Now I don't necessarily begrudge rangers their new buff, I don't play a ranger, I can't really say. I do believe, however, that there was a problem with the definition of a ranger in this whole issue. Rangers should have been compared to the other secondary tanks, rather than being compared to primary tanks, a problem I still see being displayed in this thread.
