EQGirl2000,

Interesting name for a married man, by the way...I'm sorry your wife got so upset that she broke down and cried when her character's name was changed. I truly don't like any person to be emotionally hurt. Even so, I have to ask two questions:

1. Did either of you ever read the EverQuest rules? The naming policy is fairly clear. I cut and pasted the policy below:

quote:
Naming Policy
Character Naming Policy

Character names in EverQuest should reflect the genre of the game. Original, high-fantasy names are desired. These guidelines apply both to first names and to surnames, and also to the combination of first name and surname. (For example, Luke and Skywalker are acceptable names, but Luke Skywalker is not.)

Right here, your wife encounters the first problem. ManaGirl Beachbum is not a high-fantasy name nor in the genre Verant is trying to produce.

quote:
The following types of names are unacceptable and are listed in order from the worst to the least offensive:

1. Vile, profane, rude, or racist names including common swear words, anatomical references, racial slurs, and homonyms of these words.
2. Combinations of words that produce an offensive result (e.g. Hugeaz, TugMcgroin).
3. Names of religious, occult, or significant historic origin (e.g. Jesus, Allah, Satan, Stalin).
4. Copyrighted or trademarked names of products, characters, services, or concepts (e.g. Drizzt, Marlboro, Sony).
5. Non-fantasy names from popular media (e.g. Rambo, Darthvader).
6. Common words and phrases that would not be found in the place and time setting of the game (e.g. Switchblade, Phaser, Toaster, Cannabis, Sloegin).
7. Proper names from EverQuest (e.g. Rathe, Karana). This also includes any name of a significant EverQuest NPC (e.g. Dorn, Trumpy, Karn).
8. The names of senior Customer Support Representatives or employees of Verant Interactive or Sony Online Entertainment (e.g. Aradune, Solist, Rhystan)
9. Names chosen with the intent or possessed with the effect of harming the reputation of a player or Customer Service Representative.
10. Names containing titles within them, such as, but not limited to: The, Lord, Lady, Master, King, Knight, Sir, Father (e.g. Sirtallon, Lordeagle, Mothermaggy).
11. Names that contain sentences, phrases, or more than two words. (Ikillorcs, Ontop, and Petcarbob, Diediedie) Descriptive compound words are allowed, especially in surnames (e.g. Treehugger, Giantslayer)
12. Popular and easily recognized names from existing media (e.g. Merlin, Gandalf, Belgerath, Tanis).
13. Names where the combination of the first and last name form a phrase or violate a previous rule (e.g. Crymea River, Bran Muffin, Story Thyme)

What probably happened is that someone /petioned the name as inappropriate to the genre. Once that happens, the GM has to evaluate it. If it appears to be a clear violation, they have to change it. For violations of #1 and #2, they change it immediately without even telling the player first. For all others, they contact the player, let them know it needs to be changed, and ask them to choose something else. It would have been nice if the GM would have given your wife 24 hours to choose a new name (that seems a reasonable amount of time), and the few minutes seems pretty hasty and insensitive. However, based on it being changed to "Named Matemp", it's obvious that it IS a temporary name and that she can still choose another.

The other question I wanted to ask is whether your wife is having some other issues that need attention? If a character name change could reduce her to tears, perhaps it's merely a symptom of other important factors in her life. As a loving husband, you can help her find out what those issues are and how to best address them.

Good luck!
