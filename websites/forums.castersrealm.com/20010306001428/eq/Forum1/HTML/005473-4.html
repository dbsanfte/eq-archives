Robmc,

“What I want to know is, what method of study did you use to figure these things out about this particular spawn.”

I hypothesized this was a timed spawn, as with other type of spawns in EQ. I knew the various placeholders traveled to the center of the ruins, I identified this characteristic with my main “toon”. During the day, the PH will obviously be out of place (giant rat, large rat, etc). During the nighttime, it would be the only standing undead at the center of the ruins, if the ruins are left unmolested by PC’s and NPC’s.

From my observations of the northern Qeynos Hills region at nighttime, I realized that when a putrid skeleton spawned away from the ruins out in the field it would always walk to the center of the ruins, a placeholder was identified!

I made a hotkey for “/time” and kept it on my main hotkey page, and hit it at the exact moment that I killed the putrid skeleton. Eventually, another PH (dread corpse, decaying skeleton, skeleton, putrid skeleton, Varsoon, or Pyzjn) would spawn, and walk to the ruins. When I would kill it, I would hit my hotkey and note the EARTH time. After awhile, I was able to deduce that the pop time was around 12 minutes. Doing this for several weeks, I became proficient at identifying the current PH at pop time (12 minutes), I would just clear tracking of all undead 3-4 minutes prior to pop time, made for spotting the new PH quite easy.

One day while doing this, guess who showed up on track? Yep... Pyzjn. But, guess what else showed up? Correct again... her PET. Pyzjn’s first casting is not her pet, it is a self-buff spell called “Grim Aura”. Then she walks about 15 feet and casts her pet. Now I knew the pop time was not 12 minutes, it was less!

A week went by and I had whittled the pop time to exactly 11 minutes 43 seconds! After that, it was easy to identify ALL of the placeholders, day and night. And, all of my other times (pop time after decay time and the “suicide” pop time) fell neatly into place.


“But, you say there are 24 spawn locations, how did you happen to locate each of these.”

Whenever Tovax Vmar makes the trek to the northern Qeynos Hills region, just follow him. He drops the note exactly at Pyzjn spawn points. This neat little fact was uncovered by me witnessing various placeholder spawns and taking note of all “locs”.


“Was this done with the old tracking system, or the new tracking system?”

Both.
