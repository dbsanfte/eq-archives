Play what you like. If you enjoy playing a wood-elf, it seems to me that it's the best choice for you. It's your character, and unless you delete it, you'll be using it for awhile.

I'm happy with a Halfling Druid. I was told they made 'better' druids, but I chose a halfling just to try something different.

Misty Thicket IS an awesome place to be a Druid. I don't know if there's any xp modifier, but I'd expect you to level quickly there just from the huge amount of things to fight! (And a lot of the REALLY dangerous ones at low levels won't aggro on a Druid.) There are three camps of goblins, all easy to break with Harmony. There's a camp of orcs for later levels. There are bats of all sizes, giant wasps, scarabs, skeletons... a little bit of everything. And with a bunch of (sometimes overly-)protective guards. (I'd rather be KS'd once or twice and have them save my life, than die.

Regardless of what race you choose, try to work your way over to Rivervale. The run through the Commonlands isn't too dangerous, and Kithicor isn't too bad as long as the sun is up. Just make sure you like your character... and how they look in leather armor.

It sounds to me like Charisma DOES matter, if you're going to be using charm at all. The way I understand it, your charisma has an effect on an animal's resistance to your charm. Since the animal gets a resist every so-often, having a low charisma (as we halflings are wont to) drastically reduces the effectiveness of our Charm line of spells. (It makes it easier for the animal to resist, and to break charm.)

Personally, I'm quite happy with my Halfling. He's been fun to play. In the end that's what really matters.

Here's what it comes down to.

Wood elves have better wisdom and charisma, and have the benefit of wearing small or medium items.

Halflings are better on other stats, and level faster, and can wear some neato halfling-only gear.

Frankly, I wish I could charm better, but a good enchanter and some items (like the Leatherfoot Skullcap!) can fix that fairly easily, and I'm happy being a peck.

When you get right down to it, it's a matter of personal preference.

I know people who play human and half-elf druids simply because of the challenge, though it's generally accepted that both races are at a serious disadvantage compared to the rest of us.
