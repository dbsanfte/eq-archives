Brad,

Lets pretend, for a moment, that Verant did remove the experience penalty on Hybrid classes.

The Big Question: Would that be enough to get anyone to change classes and start playing a Ranger, SK or Paladin?

My feeling is that it would not! People would not care. The hybrid classes are still not balanced enough to get people to play them even if they did remove the experience penalty.

Fact: Experience Penalty’s in no way effects actual in-game balance.

Balance is simply defined as two sides being equal. Verant, for the most part, try’s very hard to maintain this balance. BUT! What’s being overlooked is WHERE the balance is taking place. It needs to take place IN the game itself and in the abilities and hindrances of the classes themselves. Not in the mechanics of the classes! Experience Penalties are nothing more than game mechanics and NOT in-game abilities and hindrances.

This is precisely why it would not matter if Verant took the experience penalties away from hybrid classes. It would still not balance the classes within the actual game itself. Sure the mechanics might be balanced but that is not where the player’s care about the balance!

Question for Brad: How is it possible for experience penalty’s to create in-game balance?

Question for Brad: Do the other classes become balanced with the rangers, paladins and shadow knights because they do not have the experience penalty?

If Verant wants to balance the classes, I think that’s fine and well. I would point out, however, that using “experience loss” as a means to attempt to “fake” balance is a cop-out and only displays the lack of ability to create balance by means that require some amount of creativity and thought.

Brad! Verant! Anyone! Please impress us with some “real” class balance solutions and stop with the lazy, quick solutions that have no bearing, in any shape or form, on the actual game-play itself!

Addicted none-the-less,
Colorblinder
