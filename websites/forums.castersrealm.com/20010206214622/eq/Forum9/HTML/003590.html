I switched from Lvl 52 Wizard, to an Enchanter when I moved Servers. The Enchanter is, in most situations, I'd say about 100 times more powerful than the Wizard. Perhaps that figure seems strong, but there is just no comparison to me.

With regards to "nerfing" the Enchanter, I can think of 2 things I would take away from the Enchanter right now (although Verant would never do this anyway). The Tepid/Shiftless Deeds slow line, and the Wisdom buff line. This would leave the slow line the sole domain of the Shaman. I would also give the wisdom buff line to the Shaman, as I think it would make a certain sense story-line wise that Shamans get Wisdom buffs (I was amazed they never did). Keep Int buffs with the Enchanter, and replace the wis/int buffs with just INT buff versions.

I understand Verant's idea about having classes share spell lines, but considering how absolutely key the enchanter is to any group just because of clarity and CC, the fact that they can slow and buff wisdom is just so much added gravy - you never need a shaman as most of their stat buffs have dubious gameplay value anyhow. If the shamans were the only class with the slow line and wisdom buffs it would add at least some more reasons to want to have this class in your group, while I don't think it decreases the value of the enchanter. Anyhow, this is all vapor discussion really.

What would I like to see the Enchanter get? Targetable or group versions of some of their illusions - especially the race ones. This just makes so much sense that the Enchanter should be able to alter others appearances as well as their own, I can't understand why they never implemented this already. A spell, like an illusion but able to be added "on top" so to speak and doesn't actually show up as a spell icon that changes the enchanters gender (with also a targetable version). Kind of silly i know, but what the heck. Of course, you must be grouped to cast any of these illusions - we wouldn't want evil inkie enchanters turning high-elves into dark-elves inside their own town would we? ...oh, wait, maybe we would actually.

I think every class should get some uber spells at level 60 that are the absolute height of their defining class abilities. I know the Enchanter gets a nicely expensive fixed-duration charm - not sure what level mobs this works on exactly. But I thought of another - lvl 60 spell called...something like Mimic/Impersonate/Doppleganger (whatever sounds cool) - it's a very special illusion that turns the enchanter into a copy of their "target". You could even turn into Innorruuk if you wanted for example. It would give you factions LIKE who you copy, BUT - Gods and special mobs can see through this illusion generally, especially whoever you have copied (if a special mob), while their lackeys may not.

I could probably think of a dozen new spells, or some new spell lines that could enhance the enchanter, not that they really need it. But it's fun thinking of ways to improve or change the classes. I think it's a huge shame that the EQ dev team wants to close making any changes to the classes in EQ and to me that signifies the death of EQ. The game is already static enough, to openly say your not going to make any more changes should indicate the death knell folks. Start looking for the next big thing
