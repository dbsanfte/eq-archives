Your pet, charmed or summoned, is acquiring kill credit as it damages the creature. When the creature dies, kill credit is determined.
If your pet gets the kill, then it takes half the experience and your group splits the rest.

If your pet was charmed and goes wild before the creature dies, then it keeps that kill to itself, counts as an NPC kill, and the body goes poof. I had that happen to Gullerback once. (It was a funny fight, 53 shaman, 49 paladin and me, 50 enchanter trying to kill Gullerback. Everyone was OOM, so the paladin was running in big circles, with Gullerback chasing him and my pet chasing Gullerback while the shaman and I medded. I had to recharm about five times.)

1. mez/tash/charm pet.
2. send it in.
3. tash/root target.
4. med and monitor health
5. step in and blur/nuke the target so that

6. pet guard here
7. cancel magic type spell on pet.
8. mez/root. then dd the ex-pet to death.

You will often get 100% of the experience on the first target. This depends on if the blur hit or not. You will always get 100% of the experience on the ex-pet. This means you are getting 150-200% of the possible experience for two critters.
