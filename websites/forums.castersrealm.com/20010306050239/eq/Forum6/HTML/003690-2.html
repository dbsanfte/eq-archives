People seem to be missing some of the
tactical benefits of these new spells.
Pet jboot speed, have any of you ever
forward kited or circle kited with your
pets? With him having jboot speed you
can turn wider circles and not get hit
as much. I use the 53 air pet to kite
blue mobs in BW. Let loose a lava bolt
on a mob, send in pet and back up, every
time the air pet stuns the mob, let loose
another lava bolt to keep it agro on you
while the pet whacks it from behind. Very
effecient way to kill mobs solo. I used
to do this to solo avocets in SK at level
28 except I had to get sow for me and my
pet first from someone else. Now you can
do it all by yourself. If you have jboots
and this spell you will rule outside.

Summon pet spell, I consider this a
traveling spell. I would have liked a 3
second cast time but 5 is still channelable.
You port into DL, summon pet at spire/ring
arm and buff it, tell it to guard here and
then head out on your way to Karnors or
wherever else you were going to. A mob
aggroes on you and you get a lead on it
and summon your pet or channel out the pet
while it is beating you. Consider it a
quick cast pet. You summon one at zone in
to Sebilis and start with your group down to
whatever camp you are headed to. Your party
gets aggroed on the way, and boom you have
a fully armed and buffed pet in 5 seconds.
Sounds good to me.

The rain spell fix still doesn't make them
appealing to me because of their abominable
resist rates. I would try them on giant
flagged mobs where the pet didn't count
anyway and would never get more than
two waves in without resists. Better to
just DD in my opinion, because with resists
being what they are on the rains, dd is more
effecient.

The fear resist is nice but comes a bit late
I think. Would have been nice at 49 so mages
could be of some use on Voxx/Nagy raids.

Okay, here's my general impression, for whatever that's worth:

a) New level 59 spell, Valiant Companion, makes pet immune to fear (own pet only) - the level is, as others noted too high. 49 would seem to be a more appropriate level.

b) New level 39 spell, Summon Companion, if pet not noticed by any hostile npcs, pet returns to you instantly. - Occasionally useful, but most use earthen boots to control pets quite effectively. If the spell cleared aggro, or even had a chance to clear aggro, we would all cheer and bow down before the wisdom of Verant.

c) Rain spells no longer count pets against total number of entities they can damage (all rain spells)- good fix. Upper level rain spells with insane mana efficiency become even better.

d) Expedience for Mages at level 29. Target casters pet, J Boot speed buff for pet only. - Ummm, I became a mage because I don't like to kite, but maybe that's just me. Could be handy in emergencies.

Well, I'm glad Verant has its thinking cap on, now all they need to do is give us a line of hip summons.

My response to those of you who seem to want mana regen, I would rather have more things to do with my mana than have a way to get it back faster. As it is, we're the least mana dependent pure caster. Instead of giving us mana regen, give us useful summons that allow us to invest our mana in conjured items for later use. That's more in-line with the mage's focus on conjuration anyway.
