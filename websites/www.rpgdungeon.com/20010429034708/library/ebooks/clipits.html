Ye Olde Role Playing Games
Think it not a child's game for I, a Hero of The Goblin
Wars, do play such Roles even in these present days. Several
diverse companions and I gather at a safe retreat to enact
the noble--and some not so--lives of times gone into mythic dust
or even of times not yet born. Our assembled group spans some
generations yet we are equals upon the playing field..
As a young squire I found great enjoyment in novels of fantasy
and magic and I read them still, almost to the very exclusion of all
else. When I reached an age of young manhood, the fates brought
together a group of seasoned and yearling Mages, Clerics, Dwarves
and Fighters within the Dungeon of Something-or-Other where the
uniqueness and challenge of Role Playing became known to me.
With the passage of time 'Coffee Table' RPGs became ye olde BBS
RPGs and MUDs; computer games got better, and now online Web play.
Now it seems I've come full circle and am sitting 'round that coffee table
once again! We enter helpless, and we exit helpless, eh?
