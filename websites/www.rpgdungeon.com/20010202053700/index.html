The Dragon Slayers
Without the word Dragon, the dragonslayers would simply be murderers. They killed themselves as surely as they did the dragons.

Ode to the forgotten kind,
What they hunted, they would find.
Sworn in by the sacred creeds,
beloved and hated for their deeds.
With their swords shining with their might,
They would seek and destory the children of the Night.
Once so proud, so strong, and so reverred,
they would hunt what The others feared.
They would win, as they always did,
Winning what was given at highest bid.
You were once so strong,
Once your power righted the wrong,
But now your kind is all but gone,
damned by your own haste.
You were depended on dragons,
Your kind locked with theirs,
And like every chain, there is a weak link,
Ohh, if only you had stopped to think.
Now the dragons are all gone.
There, you've done it, you've won.
All that is left of their history,
Is a long and tangled mystery.
So now the dragons silently abide,
In the tales of poets much like I.
Away from their hunters,
They rest in peace,
In their heaven among the stars.
by Amber Silver
