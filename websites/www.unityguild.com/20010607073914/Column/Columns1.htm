US Foreign Policy
Fayvren @ 5:06 PM
This entire situation pretty much brings to light the problems with American Foreign policy in the last 50 years. In the past 50 years the US has found itself extended and involved in just about every nation and cause in the world. This involvement is a direct fall out of what happened in world war II when the US had an isolationist policy towards the rest of the world. We learned a difficult lesson in regards to being isolationist and appeasing other nations in their demands.

What resulted was a swing to the other extreme. An attempt to get our hands involved in just about everything and trying to control the direction the world progressed up to the smallest detail. In the Middle East, in Asia, in East Europe, in Africa and just about everywhere in the world outside of Western Europe and North America, the US government has slowly attempted to direct the fate of the world. Our response to Communism was nothing more than a fear of a repeat of events in World War II (different ideology of course).

All our efforts to force human rights, civil rights and a relatively high handedness in regards to looking at other nations has done absolutely nothing in those nations. Nothing the US has done in trying to FORCE a nation to change has created one ounce of change. Instead it is our friendly interaction with nations that indirectly have created change. Being trading partners with China and being a part of economic prosperity has lead to far greater "progress" in China than any sanctions could have ever done.

At this juncture and in light of the worlds response to most of our actions whether good intentioned or not... I think its time that we give thought to our foreign policy. Everytime something occurs in another nation that disagrees with our own conception of what is "right" we feel a need to take action, even if it does not involve us. Taking action only makes us seem like the overbearing enemy and creates animosity. The US needs to take a step back and involve itself in those events that direclty effect us or those events which our aid,action is requested by the foreign parties.

If we were on friendlier terms with China this entire waste of time would never have occurred. Instead it was an us against them scenario created because the US has often felt a need to step in on the internal business of other nations.

Nations need time to progress on their own, no amount of pushing by foreign powers will speed it up, and in most cases simply cause a backlash against progress.

Modern Art
Fayvren @ 1:50 AM
"From the moment that art ceases to be food that feeds the best minds, the artist can use his talents to perform all the tricks of the intellectual charlatan. Most people can today no longer expect to receive consolation and exaltation from art.

"The 'refined,' the rich, the professional 'do-nothings', the distillers of quintessence desire only the peculiar, the sensational, the eccentric, the scandalous in today's art. I myself, since the advent of Cubism, have fed these fellows what they wanted and satisfied these critics with all the ridiculous ideas that have passed through my mind.

"The less they understood them, the more they admired me. Through amusing myself with all these absurd farces, I became celebrated, and very rapidly. For a painter, celebrity means sales and consequent affluence. Today, as you know, I am celebrated, I am rich.

"But when I am alone, I do not have the effrontery to consider myself an artist at all, not in the grand old meaning of the word: Giotto, Titian, Rembrandt, Goya were great painters. I am only a public clown--a mountebank.

"I have understood my time and have exploited the imbecility, the vanity, the greed of my contemporaries. It is a bitter confession, this confession of mine, more painful than it may seem. But at least and at last it does have the merit of being honest."

- Picasso
