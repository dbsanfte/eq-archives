Review: George Foreman Grill
War @ 6:05 AM
Name: George Foreman Lean Mean Fat Reducing Grilling Machine
Price: Starting at $19.99 for the one person grill
Where can you buy it: Almost anywhere

If you're lazy about cooking like I am, but you love to eat, this is the ultimate appliance for you. To put it simply, this is one motherfucking fast grill. It grills everything from beef, chicken, fish, lamb, whales, pigeons, and even your cat in under 5 minutes. (depending on thickness.)

Pros:
Heats up fast (in under a minute).
Cook meat evenly and fast (usually under 5 minutes).
Slanted design drips fat into an easy to clean tray.
Non-stick surface is easier to clean than regular grills.

Cons:
Won't burn away fat that you already have without causing serious bodily damage.

Conclusion:
This grill is totally worth the 20 bucks you can get it for at Amazon or whereever you can find it. The greatest thing about it is that it's FAST. If you want a hamburger, but you don't want to heat up a grill or cook it on your stove which usually takes a long time, you can use the George Foreman grill and have it cooked in less than 5 minutes. I've cooked hamburgers, steaks, and chicken so far, and it's all been good (I haven't tried fish. I'm not a big fan of fish unless it's sushi.) And I'm usually in a rush, but with the grill it only takes me 15 minutes to cook, eat, and clean up.

In addition, the model i got was the single person grill with the bun warmer. The bun warmer is nice when making hamburgers or sandwiches, but that's about it.

What I don't like about it:
It doesn't have an on/off switch. Nor an automatic shut-off if it reaches a critical temperature. If left unattended and plugged in, it can be a potential hazard.

Recommendation:
Get it, it's great. But be careful when you use it.
