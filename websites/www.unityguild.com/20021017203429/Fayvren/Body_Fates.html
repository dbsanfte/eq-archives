CHAPTER 1, Part i

The lean youth leaned upon the old oak tree. An unremarkable young man of light figure topped by untamed short black hair, few would notice his presence in a crowd. Standing upon the grassy knoll, the world he knew was sprawled accross the quaint countryside below. His eyes glazed over at the thought of his long time home here in the Vale. The smell of grass, freshly moistened by the morning dew, brought a flood of memories of the years gone by. Thoughts of childhood games, evenings spent with friends and youthful adventures floated into mind. Nostalgia crept in as his mind raced on its own imagining the many hours his younger self had spent in the very same spot. As he let his weight fall upon the tree, he smiled at the thought of this oak tree, a familiar if not silent friend of his past.

"FAY!" a familiar cry came, breaking through this momentary reflection. "Come down already, we cant very well have a farewell feast without the person we're saying goodbye to, now can we?" Turning, the young man saw the comforting figure of his old friend Sam. If there was anyone in the Vale that he could trust, Sam was that person. With a quiet nod, Jonthys or "Fay" as he was called by those closest to him, began walking towards his own goodbye.

 

The decision had been made seven days ago. Months earlier the spellweavers had come calling, their presence quite unusual this far out into the countryside. In the empire of Nyrwind the spellweavers were masters of their surroundings, often discovering early in their life an ability for the extraordinary. Members were far and few between, individually picked up from their lives and trained to manipulate the worlds natural forces. Certainly none had ever previously arisen from this small village, and in all likelihood another would not for ages to come. That he was chosen, Fay realized, was a gift of unlimited opportunity, a chance that few in the world would ever see. Yet, as the days turned into weeks and soon into month, the choice still stood before him and he was reluctant to choose his fate. Often his thoughts found themselves resting upon his mother Shylah, who with his father's passing two winters past, would be left alone should he leave.

Spellweavers were mentored and trained at the Academy, whose location was remote even to the capital cities, and much further from the Vale. Training took the years of dedication from which there was no leave. The thought of the miles and years it would take from him and the world of his youth made the decision difficult. The moments he would lose and the events he would miss haunted him over the weeks, but eventually he found his resolve. Assuring Fay that he would have no need to worry over her wellfare, Shylah counselled her son. "You cannot allow yourself to sacrifice this occasion on my behalf. Your thoughtfulness while considerate is misplaced, your future is at stake. If there is any greatness in you then this is the chance for you to find it, and perhaps in the end you might bring wonderful things to our world." It was thus that the fate of the young man came to be.
