Quest for the Eastern Sea: A Barbarians Journey from Halas to Freeport

- By Tormod

This story is guaranteed to be true to in-game detail.  However, as a whole it is only based in truth as I have compiled some of my more memorable experiences and best screenshots to give you an rough overview of the life of a Barbarian Warrior from 1st to 16th level and linked them here as one story.

This story starts off where all stories should, at the beginning.  The beginning happens to be the Barbarian city of Halas.

I come into the world of Norrath a naked Son of Halas.  Note to soft folk: Only you soft folk call us "barbarian."  As we know this is a term that means "uncouth wild person" we except it.  For it is truth.   However, we do not take your term for us as our own. 

I am born with the ability to slam and stun my enemies, to endure cold, and greater strength and stamina than all but the evil Troll and dark Ogre.  I choose to be a warrior as my father before me.  After killing many polar bear cubs, goblin welps, wooly spiders, decaying skeletons, gnoll pups, and learning the confusing maze of glacial crevasses that is Everfrost I make 10th level.

Soon after I have saved enough platinum to buy a chain helm.  Although it impresses many that I meet I still am not nearly tough enough to take on a wooly mammoth.

After running in fright from the thundering, stomping feet of the mammoth I find myself lost.  For some time I was wandering around in the icy wastes of Everfrost and I saw the entrance to a castle lit by huge torches.  I peek inside the castle and get my first and last living look at an ice giant.   I died to bring that shot to you so you better look at it.  That is the closest you will ever come to an ice giant unless you are willing to die.  There were two hits...he hit me and I hit the ground.

After realizing that the wastes of Everfrost could be too perilous even for a warrior of my stature I decide to find the famous dungeon of Black Burrow.  At first I am disbelieving that such a peaceful scene could be the home of vicious beasts.  However, if you look carefully you will see the gnoll watcher that is silently calling his mongrel friends to come slaughter my while I soak in the false serenity.

Five minutes later the gnolls have taken over!  They are formed in a battle formation that is as powerful as its name entails.  A gnoll TRAIN!!  If you love life, don't be in the same area as a train or you will soon die.  I thought that I would be able to run away from this death machine.

Just after that screen shot was taken a turned to run only to be buried in gnolls that had snuck up on me from behind.  I am fairly certain that everyone in that shot died soon thereafter.

After learning how to survive in a dungeon (hint: learn how to swim) I found that battling the gnolls of Black Burrow was what I had been born to do.  Many, many gnolls died to my axe's blade before I returned to Halas for what would be the last time in many moons.

While I was in Halas I heard from a friend that there was much more to the world than ice, snow and gnolls.  He warned me it would be dangerous, but I decided to journey to the easternmost city of Freeport and on the way I would see the world of the soft folk.

My first sight was the beauty of a Karana sunrise.  I was in awe of that event and would have stopped to take it in.  However, our party had spotted several Hill Giants in the area.

After running east for many miles through the plains of the Karanas (where many of my party died to Hill Giant attacks on our camp) we arrived at Highhold Keep.   There we were ambushed by a family of gnolls called Mucktail.  They were bad news and nearly finished off the party.  Fortunately for us, we were saved by a group of High Elven mages.  The smell of fried gnoll still reminds me of Highhold Keep.

On the other side of Highhold Keep is Kithicor Forest.  There we decided to rest for what we thought would be a night or so.  It turned out that we stayed for many moons as we found the people of Riverdale charming and the minotaurs of the maze very challenging.

By the time I left my fine, fur footed friends of Riverdale I was 15th level and ready to face the world.  I headed east again through the sands, forests, and hills of the Commons.  In the Commons I did battle and fled from a werewolf.  Those creatures of the night are vicious and have the ability to infect you with the disease of Lycanthropy.   If you are infected you will become a werewolf.  Not a nice thought.

After several other adventures in the Commons, I finally arrived at Freeport and I had made enough platinum to buy a couple more pieces of chainmail and beers for my comrades in arms in a local tavern.   Getting drunk after a good battle is second nature.

Freeport is a very large city and it took me many days to understand its alleys and streets.  Once I began to feel comfortable, I got the yearning to head east once again.

After waiting much of the night for the ship that would give us passage on the Ocean of Tears, my travel companions were ready to do battle with anything.

A note to the gnomes: The first of your kind that I met wore a very large metal helm.  It took me by surprise to see what looked like a walking, talking helmet addressing my boot.  For a minute I thought I was enthralled by some eastern magik.   Soon, Zurditen convinced me he was not a helmet, but a cleric of the gnomish race.   I still call him Helmet.  He still talks to my boot.

After finally booking passage on a fine ship, we settled in for the long voyage.  As soon as I had taken refuge in the ships hold, we docked on an island.   My fast moving, new found, gnomish friends decided that there were pirates on the island and forced me to accompany them to rid the island of the sea brigands.  After such a long wait for passage out of Freeport, I had second thoughts as I saw the ship leave for the eastern horizon.

However, the island proved interesting as we found pirates to battle.   We also found strange green skinned "aqua goblins" and an even stranger island race of giants called Gornit that could swim faster than most fish.

Best of all I found a secret Stone Ring.  My people consider these places holy ground.  There I took a moment to bless my sword to the service of my kinsman and the Clan MacLear.

There ends the story.  For you have caught up to me.  Now let's see if I can avoid those Gornits and make it back to the ship alive...
