Learning the New Weapons - Nosferum (6-27-03)
    No doubt, it will take some time to acclimate to these new weapons. Brother Wolfdor, in particular, is still prone to mace his enemies with his rifle. Now, before I continue, let us learn a few words.

Pistol - Held in a single hand. Point the hole away.
Carbine - Held usually in both hands. Again, point the hole away.
Rifle - Always held in both hands and, again, point the hole away.
Vibro- - A mechanical type of standard weapon that increases it's damage.

    Do not fret. There are more normal weapons in this land, though their design is strange and sometimes awkward. As can be expected, there are staves. There are swords, but you'll note that some are of a strange design. As well, there are two-handed axes and so forth. This essay will focus, mainly, on the newer seemingly magical weapons.

    These new weapons are called "guns" by the locals, and they are very dangerous. As noted before, make sure to point the hole away from yourself and toward the enemy. This is where the Gnomish fire spits out. As you can see, the Bothan on the left is pointing the hole at the enemy, as these boys in white were nice enough to demonstrate. Pay attention to the arrow. The three types are the first three words we learned, and they all have a range of 65 meters.

    Pistols are small enough to be wielded in one hand. It takes no real strength to use any of these new weapons, but it does take a steady hand. Literally, you just point it and squeeze the triggering device placed just where your first finger would go. It is the least accurate of the three types at a long range, but it does rather well up close, within spear-throwing range. The training masters in the city teach that the primary role is to sap your foe's health. Those who choose to specialize in it even learn ways to use it as a blunt instrument.

    Carbines are a hybrid of the Pistol and the Rifle. They are actually built much like the Rifles, but are shorter. Because of this length, it is awkward to use in defense against a foe who uses melee. You will get hit harder by non-ranged enemies. Those who are good with this weapon focus mainly on keeping the enemy from moving around, and saps their stamina.

    Rifles are the longest of the three weapons, and requite two hands. They are the most accurate at their maximum range, and are a poor choice up close not only because of their inaccuracy, but because they are almost useless to parry. You will certainly take a lot of damage if hit with a melee weapon or an unarmed foe. I highly recommend either switching to a Pistol or some sort of brawler weapon when they get close.

    There are forces in this land similar to our former home, but a little more lenient. Rather than completely preventing you from using weapons well beyond your capacity you simply won't know how to use it effectively. The best weapons really only should be used by those of sufficient training.
