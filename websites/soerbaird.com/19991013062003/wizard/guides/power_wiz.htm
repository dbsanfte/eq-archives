Caster Power Gaming Guide
(On Faydwer and Eastern Antonica)		 by Quae of Veeshan

Introduction

Let me start by saying that I start all my caster characters on Faydwer for one reason, easy money and experience. I have tried wizards on Odus and Antonica but I find that leveling is slow and tedious.

First off if you are a high elf or wood elf caster type (I define casters as mana wielding non-hybrid classes) don't worry about this little beginning, skip to the first section.

For gnomes, I suggest an immediate departure from Steamfont. Why you ask? Because Steamfont in my opinion is one of the worst experience gaining areas for a defenseless caster. You can find the zone to Lesser Faydark by following the road you encounter while leaving Ak'Anon. Once in Lesser Faydark follow the right wall until you happen upon a second zone entrance. Zone through and you shall find yourself in Greater Faydark! Your journey is now over.

For dwarves, try to make your way to Greater Faydark as soon as possible, because although Butcherblock is for your level, you will find it extremely tedious finding creatures your level that are close by guards in case something happens. When you leave Kaladim you shall also see a road. Head left on the road until you encounter the fabled crossroads. Turn left again and follow the road to the Greater Faydark zone entrance. For Humans that start in Freeport, gain a level or 2 and then take the boat to Butcherblock. Follow the road until you hit the crossroads (don't get discouraged it's a long walk) and turn left. Follow the road into Greater Faydark.

Levels 1 to 4

At level 1 try to stay near Kelethin or Felwithe. I leveled my gnome wizard near Felwithe because Wood Elf guards are very slow. Try to attack Decaying Skeletons, Bats, and Giant Wasp drones at levels 1 and 2. But be careful--giant wasp drones tend to swarm, and if you're not careful you'll end up with 4 wasps all attacking you at once.

Also at level 2 take the occasional orc pawn or pixie trickster but /con them first.

At level 3 I went to Kelethin and hovered between the "orc lift" and orc camp, picking off stray pawns. If you cannot afford your spells when you hit level 4, I suggest doing the bard mail delivery quest that requires you to take mail from Felwithe to Kelethin.

Levels 4 to 8

At this point the paths diverge. You can choose to stay in Greater Faydark, or move off to Butcherblock.

Greater Faydark

At level 4 I partied with maybe 1 or 2 friends my level and fought on the "orc hill" in the orc camp because I needed money for my spells and centurions drop some of the best money for their level.

At level 6 or so I went to the bandit camp which is located roughly at negative 1100 negative 400. 3 bandits spawn but they swarm, so try to get a party. The spawn rate is a bit slower, but the loot in money is good and the experience is also acceptable.

Butcherblock

From Greater Faydark, the Butcherblock zone is found by following the road left from Felwithe.

At level 4 stay near the "2 houses" which are found by following the road that starts at the zone. Suggested creatures at level 4 are large snakes, and giant skunks. As you become higher level move further down the path. The only problems with this area are A) there is almost no loot whatsoever and B) many higher level creatures such as goblin wizards roam around the road.

Also at level 7, maybe level 6, you can go to the skeleton spawn points, the tower and the ruins. This is some of the fastest experience you will get but the loot consists of cloth and bones.

On a final note if you are on the road and you see a NPC ogre, run like the wind. It is either Corflunk the ogre warrior (roughly level 10) or Zarchoomi his shaman wife (roughly level 11).

Levels 8 to 12

At this point (level 8) you should start heading into Clan Crushbone in Greater Faydark. Stay near the entrance and the "trainer hill". Orc centurions drop good loot, 1 or 2 gold per kill, plus whatever weapons they are carrying.

If you're a bit adventurous, you can go to the slaver pits which can be found by following the left wall at Crushbone entrance, crossing the bridge, and going over the craggy rocks. Once again the most you will take centurions and the occasional /conned blue slaver.

At level 9 you can head into the entrance and the very beginning of the castle. Partying is highly recommended here because all sorts of orcs will attack you that are not your level.

At level 11 venture into the Throne room of the castle and stay near a window, dropping out of it if the orcs are killing you. Also beware of the 3 NPCs that spawn in the tower (room behind the throne): Ambassador DVinn, Emperor Crush, and Lord Darish. Any one of those three will kill you so fast, you'll think you are level 2.

Although I have not tried, I hear that Steamfont skeletons are also good for experience, but as a caster (who is not tweaked) money is essential for new spells. While in Steamfont head towards the windmills and then follow wall until you enter what the players call the dragon's skeleton.

[Tollo's Note: Steamfont skeletons near the dragon skeleton or near the 'gears' are an excellent source of experience, and solo'able by a wizard at levels 10 and 11. Just Root/Nuke until dead, but be ready to run. Having a Shadowstep/Gate combo is essential here. Also, be cautions about attacking these skeletons; the plague rats will assist them. Also, beware the Minotaur Hero.]

At level 12 there is a place called the Chessboard in Butcherblock. This is great exp, but no loot. Kill undead pawns, but not much else.

Kadath of Cazic-Thule adds this tip about Crushbone:

  • Gather as many belts/pads as you can. Get yourself bound at the orc lift. When you're done for the day auction that your buying MORE pads and belts and buy all you can, then put all your belts and pads in the Kelethin bank. I had two small boxes and a couple of small bags, it worked great.

    Every three days or so, make the run to Kaladim. Go to the bank and get out all your pads and belts, then turn them in. If you've gotten any skeleton bones on the way there, turn them in to the paladin in the back. You get TONS of exp and can sell all the loot you get in exchange for both for a pretty penny.

    Once you're done, talk to the postman and get a letter for Kelethin, then make the run to the butcherblock docks and get a second letter for delivery to Kelethin. Then, gate yourself back to the orc lift, make the quick run to the priest lift and turn the two letters in. Voila! Instant exp!

    Now go back to Crushbone and loot and buy as many pads and belts as you can, rinse lather and repeat! You've got to get the pads and belts off your kills to REALLY make Crushbone worthwhile. I've gotten at least three bubbles of EXP just from belts. Don't forget to deliver the mail, cause with your gate it's instant exp.

Levels 12 and beyond...

Although under extreme debate, at level 13 I went to the Oasis of Marr. While in Butcherblock go to the docks and board the next ship. Do not get off at any of the docks in the Ocean of Tears. You will zone eventually into East Freeport, and you will get off there. Leave Freeport via the South Gate and head straight until you zone into the Desert of North Ro. Keep heading straight until you hit the beach. Facing the ocean turn right and head down the beach until you zone into the Oasis.

Now the Oasis is a pretty nasty place- Sand Giants (level 30s) and Dry Bone Skeletons (level 20s) roam free. But at level 13 if you party you can kill some caiman and possibly a crocodile.

At level 15 start soloing caiman for fast experience.

At level 16 you can take crocodiles solo or fight orc warriors. Keep in mind, there are no guards here, so always look for other creatures that might be trained on you if you attack your target.

At level 17 the land of Faydwer is yours. You can head back to Crushbone and kill emissaries (level 15) or party with people in Unrest (found by going through Butcherblock to Dagnor's Cauldron to Unrest). Most dungeons give good loot while regular spawn sites give good exp.

On a final note:

Remember, although I find power gaming extremely helpful, I always take an hour or 2 to have some leisure time. I drink beer, hold contests, work on my trade skill, and make fun of Halflings. But that's just me!

 

-- Quae, Noble Gnomish Wizard and Tinkering prodigy of Norrath (on Veeshan server)
