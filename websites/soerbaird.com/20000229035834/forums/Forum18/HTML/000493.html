I was looking at a post up here a while back that suggested some price on a mammoth cloak... can't remember exactly what it was, but I seem to remember the price being under 500. Someone else replied asking what server the first poster was on. A good question, I think: I saw 2 folks selling mammoth cloaks on Cazic Thule last night (the first ones I've ever seen for sale), and both folks wanted over 2500 plat for it. One of them got it right after he auctioned.

This leads me to wonder: has anyone done a general price comparison on semi-rare to rare items on different servers? By my experience (and of course, this is just my experience), I have had to learn to cover my cod-piece protectively before asking a price on various auctions on the Cazic-Thule server. It seems that folks always want at least a little more--often a lot more--than I have been led to believe an item is worth from my readings online and whatnot. It is only the rare person who will engage in even a little haggling (somehow haggling seems like a natural thing to engage in in a place like Norrath), and I know at least one person who has gotten fabulously rich on the Cazic-Thule server (rich by my poor, non-mammoth cloak-having estimation) by hunting down semi-rare to rare items and hawking them at the highest prices the market will bear.

So is Cazic-Thule an acception to the rule? I feel a little foolish, because I've made a practice of giving away items I can't use to friends who can use them(goodbye, mino rib cage... we hardly knew ye), and a worse practice of selling smithed items at damn close to cost just for the thrill of watching somebody go from full patchwork to full banded--MY banded--before my eyes. Is this just a function of being level 27 now? Will I have to become a hard-ball throwing, no-deal giving coot to scrape up the money for the odd dufrenite? If so, is it because CT has a higher... uh... cost of living than most other servers? Or is this every server?

Just wondering.
Beryde Mann of Cazic-Thule
