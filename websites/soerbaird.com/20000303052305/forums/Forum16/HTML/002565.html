How rare are shamans?

This is hard to answer because a lot of shamans go anon. I routinely do a /who shaman, and /who druid, and it is not unusual to see myself as the only non-anon shaman, with 5 or 6 non-anon druids. I would say the overall ratio of druids to shamans is something like 4:1. I’m not sure how rare they are compared to other casting classes.

Are they useful (can they put the smack down)?

A shaman can do a lot of damage to a creature. Our DoTs are second only to those of the necromancer, and we’ve got decent DD. Our DD spells do nice damage, but are very inefficient, and take a long time to cast. My level 24 DD spell does 137 points of damage, and maxes out at 150 damage. Shamans get a new DD spell every 10 levels (every level that ends in a “4” gets a new DD spell). You will get an upgrade to either poison, or disease DoTs almost every spell level. Also, the shaman has some very nice Debuff spells. Including spells to debuff magic resistance, and attack speed. I’ve played a lot of classes, and (with the exception of the 19 to 24 stretch), I’ve never been disappointed with my shamans offensive power.

The shaman also gets the best line of buff spells in the game. You will be able to buff EVERY stat except int, and wis. You also get a decent AC buff, and (after level 34), a nice HP buff. The AC and HP buffs that the cleric (and maybe the druid, not sure how much the “Skin Like” series helps) are better though. The shaman also gets attack speed buffs starting at level 29, and regeneration starting at level 24.

largest advantages and disadvantages

Advantages:

1) SoW at an early level. If you’ve been playing a druid, you’ve become used to having SoW. It will be difficult to live without it after having it for so long. Once you hit level 9 with a shaman, you won’t have that problem.

2) Pet at level 34. This really separates the druid from the shaman. The shaman gets a decent pet at level 34, and every spell level thereafter. Not as strong as the necro, or mage pets, but still a nice addition. With all of the shaman buffs, (and debuffs), this makes the shaman one of the BEST solo classes at high levels. Plus, the pet looks just like a druid in wolf form. You’ve got to love that shamans get druids as pets .

3) Second best DoT spells in the game. The shaman poison DoTs are great. At high levels (level 49), you can do more damage than ice comet. Also, once a DoT sticks, it will always do full damage (assuming the MoB does not die early  ). So, you don’t have to worry about resists once you get it to stick. They also seem to stick a LOT easier than DD spells.

4) The best buffer in the game. No one can beat the shaman at buffing. We may not have the best AC/HP buffs, but we more than make up for it with our stat buffs. Tanks will love you.

5) You will be a great debuffer. Reducing attack speed, and magic resistance is great. Tanks will be hit less, and spell casters will do more damage.

6) It is easier to group as a shaman. Druids have a reputation for being solo players. They also have a reputation for being jerks. Now, they probably don’t deserve this reputation, but if you play a druid, you have to live with it. As a shaman, I can almost always find a group when I want one.

Disadvantages:

1) No teleports. If you’ve been playing a druid, you probably got used to jumping all over the world whenever you wanted to. Get ready to start paying for that service or running if you play a shaman.

2) Low wisdom. Shamans have lower wisdom, and less mana than any other casting class. The cannibalize spell at level 24 helps with this, but be ready to be low on mana much of the time.

3) 19 to 24 hell levels. This is not THAT big a deal, but the level 19 spells are pretty week compared to the other shaman spell levels. That makes these five levels pretty tough for most shamans.

4) No snare. You have become used to having Snare. Either to kite, or to slow down runners. Although the shaman gets root, it is no substitute for snare. You will just have to learn to live without it. Of course, this is true for ALL other casting classes (except maybe the necro who gets the darkness spell that also slows the MOB).

5) Week disease line of spells. The shaman gets two lines of DoT spells. Poison, and Disease. Currently, the disease line is pretty week. It does nice damage, but it takes two minuets to do it. Thus, it is useless in a group setting. They are still good for kiting, but not much else. Verant has said they are going to do something about this, but don’t hold your breath.

6) Alchemy is broken. This is probably not a big deal to you, but if you are starting a shaman so you can be the ONLY class that can make potions, don’t choose the shaman. Again, this may be fixed some day, and it is not that big a deal for most shaman, as they are not interested in trade skills.

Overall, I’ve had a GREAT time with my shaman, and I highly recommend the class. I’ve tried a lot of the other classes, and the shaman is now the only character I play. We don’t have quite the same versatility as the druid, but we can still do a LOT of different things. I say go with the shaman, on The Nameless, I think you will find that you won’t even want to play that druid any more (I never played my druid after starting a shaman).

Samor
