I used both Affliction and Infectious Cloud quite a bit between levels 19 and 22 while kiting Crag Spiders in East Karana. The combination is far more effective than Affliction by itself because they stack as you point out.

However, Poison Storm is not a DOT in the classic sense. It is a AE DD spell that just happens to "hit" three times. How is that different? Poison Storm does 60 to each mob in its AE on each of 3 consecutive ticks, so that makes it sound like a DOT. The difference is that the mob gets a chance to resist each tick, so it rarely does the full 180 to any given mob.

Since it is also significantly more expensive than Envenomed Breath, you're not gaining an advantage by using it unless you need to do damage to several mobs at once.

The 2 problems with AE spells soloing are:

1. When soloing you usually try to fight 1 MOB at a time, so regular DOTs/DD are more efficient.

2. You can get hit by your own AE, with no tank to keep the MOB placed away from you, so you have to hope root holds for the spell duration.

In a group fighting multiple MOBs these spells are great. Even with just 2 MOBs, Poison Storm can do 360 damage for almost a 3:1 dam/mana ratio. I'd be leary of using them in multi-level areas of a dungeon though, as you will hit and agro MOBs on the floors above and below you.
