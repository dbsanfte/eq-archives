Since it has been awhile that a new guide has been written I thought I should start one consolidating tricks, advice and things to know that I have heard to make EQ more enjoyable.

Tricks; Things that work that probably were not intended but are not exploits.

Large sewing kits make light backpacks (0.4 vs 3.0). Also many quests start by giving you a container that that you can use while you are too poor to buy a normal container.

Summoned food will stay after you camp if you place it in a container and place that container in the bank.

Jumping while running is faster than plain running. Using a forward arrow key combined with a turn arrow and controlling your direction with mouse look (right mouse button) is evan faster and does not tire you out like jumping.

Fast way to increase faction is to either do a low amount of damage to something that is fighting someone else, or heal one of the players that is fighting the mob. Do this in heavily occupied zones.

Silk swatches stack and sell for the same price as two spider webs.

Advice; Free for what its worth.

Skills to learn; fishing and bind wounds. Fishing can make you money (4cp for bait into 2sp for fresh fish) and makes for cheep food. Once bind is maxed out at 100 it can heal about 20 points per bandage.

Allways stay about 2 bubbles of health. Less results in reductions to your abilities and running speed.

Learn how to use the user defined social buttons. Most popular are Train (/shout Train to xxx zone !!!), Yell (/yell) [this is better than shout for help because it tells other nearby players where you are], Welcome (You are welcome, good hunting), and Role playing. Use your imagination for the last one but I'm going to try [/say I'm here to pump you up, /rude] which shows that you have multiple lines to combine into one button.

Observe the paths that monsters take and pull them off it. Especially in the newby zone I see people fighting one mob when one of its brethren comes along a joins in the fight. But if you pull the first one off a bit the second will walk on by.

For what its worth, the most up to date EQ manual is on your computer.

Shaman specific tips.

Sprit of the cheetah resets whenever you zone.

Save those bat wings and fish scales from your early adventures. You will want some later for levitation and enduring breath.

Whenever you get a new set of spells, there will be many that are not worth it until a level or two later (mostly damage and debuffs) due to cost and monster resists.

Myths; common knowledge that may or not be true.

More memory will shorten the time it takes you to zone. This may be true up to a certain point but afterwards 64mb does not seem to have an appreciable effect on my system.

Buffs are not worth the mana. Mine seem to be appreciated and I notice when they fade on me in the middle of a fight.

Shaman's melee ability stops at XX. I have heard values ranging from late teens to the thirties, but at 23 I can still can take a blue on by myself. This seems heavily affected by equipment. It does not compare to a warriors ability to take on many mobs at once but shaman are more fun anyway.

Its not memory that you all are seeing that slows your zone load it is the seek times on the hard drive. Faster hard drive = Faster write to the RAM = Faster Zone load. Another factor is the speed of the processor at this point as it has work to do while all of the textures and things in the Zone load to RAM. The reason you see faster loads with more RAM is more than likely attributible to the quality of the ram and or the quantity makes for les swapping IE: if you are on an older machine with regular SIMMS you will be sower than a new one with DIMMS as the clock speeds on the ram are different 66 mhz compared to 100 mhz usually.

However just about all of the time in Zone loading is your hard drive... try this defrag the drive on a regular basis for those in a windows based environement, you will notice a huge differene the first tiem you do this if it isnt your regular routine... this is because the data gets "spread" around everytime you use it and it takes longer to find.... we are talking miliseconds here but they do add up
