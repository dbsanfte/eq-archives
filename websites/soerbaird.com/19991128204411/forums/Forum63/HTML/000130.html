Bearing in mind that an apprentice fletcher I know with a skill of 58 managed to make a Trueshot a couple of days ago anything is possible. (He's now in Kelethin infirmary being treated for shock - he also has a guard on his door to stop him being lynched by all the Master Fletchers I know).

Having said that it is possible to mess up even trivial bows occasionally, as witnessed when I attempted to make a carved elm for a warrior yesterday (of course you can't tell him that!), but I successfully made a carved ash at skill 90 and have made at least 4 or 5 shaped ash bows since achieving Mastery.

And of course that 'big fat Master' counts for something - when some warrior wanders up to you and says 'Hey d00d - Ur a ranger? - Make me a bow' you can say 'Sir, I am a Master of my craft and I do not make bows for those who show no manners nor respect for the art' ....or something like that :-)

Edit: Re Scout's follow up post.
I disagree that ash bows are not profitable. I have made and sold several shaped ash bows for 55 - 60pp (prices are guild / non-guild) and these are the best bows you can buy for less than 100pp (retail). Ash bows are a good mid level compromise bow between speed and damage.
