I personally use this zone to develop all of my characters when they first begin. This zone is easily hunted from seasons 1 to 15. You can either start by gaining a few quick levels in Greater Faydark, or head right over to Steamfont. It's not a dangerous trip and you can see the directions by following the maps on this site.

As a level 1, stick to the rats, kobold runts and decaying gnome skeletons. There are three distinct areas to find them. One is in front of the windmills heading from Ak Anon. You'll see a large valley to the right of the road. That valley and its surrounding areas should provide you with near unlimited prey. This area is usually populated with more players due to the fact that you are in the middle of two guards.

As an alternative, there are dragon bones straight out from the city. You can hunt around there and be close to a guard if you get in trouble. Be warned however, there are 4 skeleton spawn points around the bones and if you get too close, they will eat you for breakfast, cackling the whole while.

The third site is usually far less busy and has a lot more creatures in my opinion. The main drawback is there is no guard close should you choose to run. This site can be found by heading left when you exit the city, or if you are coming from the lesser faydark, it will be on your right.

Things to note while fighting decaying gnome skeletons, rats and kobold runts. Kobold runts and the decaying skeletons will attack on sight for any race I have seen so far at the lower levels. So be warned as you are fighting and keep an eye out on your surroundings as multiple enemies at this level can be the death of you.

As far as loot, the decaying skels have often been known to carry around various rusted weapons and coin. The kobold runts are also nice because they can carry various sizes of bags and boxes and the occasional rusty dagger, as well as coin. Rats drop various portions of their anatomy, but you can sell all of it for some copper at least and most of the items are stackable except for the ears and fur.

Important note here.. DO NOT go to the city to bind or sell. There is a clockwork jeweler that often sits at the entrance of Ak Anon that will kill any follower of Tunare.. and as you know, most of us Rangers are. You can sell at the vendors at the mill if you are fighting there, the huts up by the city if you are fighting by the dragon bones (you'll have to duck to enter the little houses) or if you are fighting out by the newbie kobold camp, there is a vendor that sits beside the teleport ruins.

Continue killing these baddies until you are level 3. Once you hit 3, you can go after some tougher enemies. These include Runaway Clockworks, Diseased Rats and Spiderlings. I personally never fight the diseased rats. They have little if any loot and it is VERY annoying to have to wait around for your disease to wear off. Go after the spiderlings; they have good loot in the form of spiderling silk and are pretty good experience for a couple of levels. Make sure you /con them though as they vary from level 3 to 4. At level 3, you would be hard pressed to beat a level 4 spiderling. Also note that the spiderlings and their mechanical cousins will gang up on you if they see you beating on friends. If you want to try the runaway clockworks, they are the same levels as spiderlings (3 to 4) and they drop coin as loot and the occasional black box fragment. (worthless) The thing I love about fighting the clockworks is that "ting" when you hit them :)

Once you hit level 4, the fun starts. You can continue to solo, but you'll have a harder time doing it as there are less roaming mobs that will con blue to you. Your best bet is to hit the kobold camp, which is to the left of the city proper, with a small group of level 4's and 5's or so. Three kobolds spawn in that camp and another spawns nearby on the hillside. If you want you can solo the one on the hill, but it is more time consuming. The kobolds at the camp range from level 4 to 6, so you'll get some good exp there. Occasionally a kobold shaman will spawn, but he isn't much tougher than the others with the exception of some minor spell-casting abilities. Like their younger siblings, the kobolds drop various containers and pretty decent coin for this level. I have also seen them drop semi precious stones (malachite and jasper.)

There is another kobold camp in this zone, but it is patrolled by harder creatures and there are more that spawn (4 spawn at camp and one on hill.) If you have a larger group and can follow the map (I edited it to show you level areas) on this site, you might do well there as it is lessed camped. You can reach level 7 with these techniques so far, although you'll find 6 to 7 is tough by staying at camp.

Once I hit level 6, I like to take my party up to the windmills (or you can hit the area where the other kobold camp is.) At the windmills, you can take kobold scouts, pumas and rogue clockworks. These enemies range from level 5 to about 8. The kobold scouts are a little tougher than the kobolds and drop the same kind of loot. The rogue clockworks are pretty good exp and they drop blackboxes (can be used to affect faction.) Be wary when fighting the rogue clockworks as they often bring rogue cleaners with them. These rat-like beasts don't hit too hard, but they are annoying and can group quickly. I have seen the cleaners rarely drop backpacks (don't ask me where they put them.) The pumas are by far the hardest at this level, yet yield a greater experience reward. They hit pretty hard and fast for their level, but the most dangerous fact is that you can not outrun them. They often yield ruined cat pelts which you can work on your tailoring with.

After battling these monsters, you'll become more familar with the zone and you'll wonder where to go now. By now you should be level 8 to 9. From here you have a couple of different options depending on you modus operandi. My personal path leads me to start taking on skeletons and young ebony drakes. These enemies can be found in two main places in the zone and there are a couple of notable skele spawn points. The skeletons hit hard and have lots of hit points, but they often drop pieces of gold as loot along with various cloth armor. The fire drakes have mediocre loot and they hit pretty hard because of their fire based breath attack (if you are level 9, you can help somewhat with endure fire.) You can hit the roaming skels at the mills, but that often attracts the attention of annoying and deadly giant plague rats. Make sure when you hit level 8 and 9 that you train to get spells and dodge.

You can try camping the dragon skeleton, but this brings up two problems. One is that area is often camped. Two is that there are three skel points at the tail end of the dragon skel and any two adjacent skels are linked. My favorite place in the whole zone is to go out by the "cogs" and fight the skeletons out there. You'll need a good group of 3 to 4 people around level 9 to do this. A cleric will really help as they can heal and have ward undead. The nice thing about this skele spawn area is that there are 5 spawn points there. They are widely spread out and none of them are linked. You won't have to worry too much about roaming enemies either. IMPORTANT NOTE: Keep an eye on tracking if you decide to hunt here as this is where the Minotaur hero spawns randomly. He is a very aggressive level 45+ that will tear the zone's occupants (even guards) to shreds when he gets on a rampage. You can easily make a few plat in money per hour and hunt this area until level 12 or 13.

Once you hit level 12 or 13, you can either move into the minotaur caves (check out our guide to Steamfont mino hunting,) or stay out in the fields outside of the mino cave entrance. Inside the mino caves you will find a few wandering minotaur slavers ranging from level 12 to 15. Occasionaly they will carry the much-coveted minotaur battle axe that is a favorite weapon around these levels. Be careful when fighting minos though as they often group up and cause havoc in numbers.

You can venture outside the caves to find giant Rogue Clockworks, Earth Elementals, Harpies and Lesser Ebony Drakes. These monsters all range from levels 12 to 15 and usually offer pretty good experience. However, most of them drop little if any loot, yet the Earth elementals can drop precious stones. Keep in mind that the Lesser Drakes and the Harpies have fire based attacks which can affect your entire group. The clockworks and elementals tend to group up as well, so be careful.

Once you hit level 15, you are essentially done with this zone. You can find an occasional blue enemy, but they yield little if any exp. The only reason I see people stay is to maybe gather minotaur axes for sale in other areas.

That concludes my Ranger's guide to Steamfont. I hope this serves to help the aspiring young rangers in the area.
