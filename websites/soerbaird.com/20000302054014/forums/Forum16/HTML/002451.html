Well this started off as a rant, but now I'm just sad.

I don't think I'll get to the magical level 34. I'm losing faith in Everquest everyday. I left EQ for over a month about a month back...got refreshed, had some friends catch up to me in levels and then jumped back in and had fun again. Mostly because I had fun playing with my friends.

Now even my friends are starting to quit.

I don't blame Verant so much. They've made a great game. The best game ever in my opinion. Never thought I'd get so addicted to a game as I did with Quake. Once EQ came around, I haven't played Quake since. But Everquest is quickly taking a turn for the worse. There just isn't any excitement anymore. Now the servers are more crowded then ever, and every good place to fight is camped. The whole world is mapped. Ever quest is catalogued. Skip these steps to finish it faster. Do it 50 times.

And is there any true newbies joining us in Norrath? It seems that all newbies start in full Bronze (at least) these days.

Now we're agruing over taking those last remaining truly fun zones (Paw), and making it just another crowded, camping zone. It's probably for the best, more people would be using the zone, but it is still sad.

I'm just getting so tired of-- Run to this zone, overcrowded, run to this zone...overcrowded, fighting over ever spawn...killstealers (and they don't mean to, but everyone blasts anything that moves anymore). I've had too many nights of running around the world, and logging with nothing to show for it but 4 hours of looking for a place to camp.

In hopes of making EQ fun again, some friends and I have all created new characters on Tallon Zek (Racewar Server). Gnomes...We just wanted to run around as a pack attacking elves and such. I've always been under the impression that the Racewar servers would be more of a role-playing server (I enjoy roleplaying). What is the first thing I see? Some level 41 Dark Elf (named Nath), power leveling off of the Gnome guards. In a bit of role playing I attempt to lifetap the guy. Now I know lifetap does four points of damage, and there is no way in hell my spell would hit him for damage (because of the level rules). What is his response? "I'm level 41 you ****ing moron!". He then proceeds to request a duel (I'm level 1!), I accept and he kills me with one high level spell.

And this is exactly what I fear EQ has become. Just a bunch of power levelers (and I power level too sometimes), repeatedly killing the same mob over and over. The excitement is gone.

My hope...is that, at some point Verant starts over new and begins to make an Everquest 2. I'll be the first to pay 50 dollars. Because when the world is new, and you don't know where that path leads, or what creatures are on the other side of that zone...this game CAN be good. AWESOME really.

So please Verant, stop wasting your time on Everquest, and move onto Everquest 2. Don't allow people to move over characters, build all new levels, new monsters.

It is my opinion that adding new zones and re-tuning zones/dungeons is not the answer. We need to start over.

I'll tell you, a lot of times I feel the same way.

I started my shaman in March. The swamp of my birth scared the crap out of me. And during my newbie days, no basher killed ANYTHING. They just sat there scratching while newbie after newbie zoomed past to get to the town zone. Innothule had an average of 10 people in it during peak hours... no one wanted to play a troll.

Once I hit level 9 and sported my new speed spell, I decided to make the fated run up to Neriak. I had no printed map, only the one in the box that I was given. And so, following that sketchy route I travelled North. South Ro scared me.. orcs chased after me. But I was able to hit Oasis... which had an average of five (!!) people in it. Did I head up the coast in the safest way? Heck no, I plowed straight through the middle! I swam the lake right by the spectres... and then there was the breakneck run to the zone. I made it though, and got to North Ro. Here I did stick to the zone line, stopping at the Derv camp to throw newbie buffs at the few people there. It was an uneventful trip to the tunnel.

Once in East commonlands, though, I kept hearing shouts for SoW, so I decided to stay at the tunnel and advertise my "mojo". To this day I'm credited as being one of the first shaman to do that on my server in ol' EC. I made countless friends from doing that... and EC was a dangerous place for a newbie troll with bad faction. The magic still existed back then.

Now, I play to spend time with the friends I've met, help out others when I can, and occasionally level. I'm only level 24 to this day, when some people have three level 50s on their account.. but you know, I think I had more fun than those people. And there's still magic in the game.. when I get in a shouting match with an evil troll, or tell young humans the story of why the Freeport guards don't kill me.. just moments like that.

But the original wondrous magic the game held is gone... too many people, and too many morons.
