The song loadout varies a lot depending on the party. With a few tanks in the group, put those weapons away, and increase the tanks' attack speed (and resistances, when attacking casters). And I'll second the recommendation for Psalm of Warmth-- every mob in the mid to high 20's attacks a LOT-- use that against 'em! :-)

If you're in a scary dungeon and you're not the highest-level party member, for heaven's sake save the healing for BETWEEN fights. I was ALWAYS the first one dead in the party in dungeons, because even though I did *NO* damage, all the mobs that weren't aggroed on a hitter said "Hey, that lil' punk is HEALING them all!" and came and beat me to a pulp. So instead, help the tanks wreck things more quickly, and THEN heal them up after the carnage is done.

For soloing, once you get fear(26), use it with Selo's Chain and abandon the Psalm of Warmth. You won't be getting hit much anyway. :-)

But the Charm song (27), my fellow bards, makes it a whole different game. I can solo two yellow-con melee-type mobs at once, and kill them FASTER than killing one of them alone. Let a mob do the dirty work for you, and then reward its hard work by bellow-kiting their last few pixels of health away.

There are a few important tricks to learn to ensure that you get exp and loot from BOTH of your victims, but that belongs in a thread of its own...

Fellowship Associate 		posted 10-18-1999 21:24         
There are 2 or 3 ways to kill a mob and get *no* exp or loot. I detail some methods of avoiding those ways, in another thread about the lv27 song specifically.

Anyway, the easiest way to get 2 mobs to start fighting each other is to just choose mobs that aggro on you, and simply walk by them. (Griffawns in EK at first, then move on to giant skels in Rathe Mtns. Just a couple examples; there's plenty stuff to choose from.)

The pet will, by default, defend you, so pick one and keep it charmed and the rest is automagic. For the NON-aggro mobs, though, you need to do a couple extra steps.

Bind yourself a whole bank of pet-command hotkeys. Competent necros have had these since level, what, 6?, but it's a whole new thing for bards. Read some necro guides for various advice.

Basically, once the charm sticks, you target the "target" mob and hit the button you'd created to type "/pet attack" for you. And off goes your pet. Quickly retarget your pet, because you DON'T want to keep switching which one your "pet" is (see other thread), and you'll have to recharm soon.

Then, just keep it charmed and watch them fight, simple as pie. They'll both chase you when the charm wears off, but recharm your pet and they'll continue killing each other.

Bards can cast WHILE RUNNING AWAY. For all our disadvantages and all our stupid songs that make every mob for miles around aggro on US, that one fact is an incredibly huge advantage over all the other caster and hybrid classes. USE YOUR ADVANTAGE!

And for heaven's sake, stay outdoors. I got SO sick of getting killed in dungeons, even in a decent party. But outdoors, I am king of the world, baby.
