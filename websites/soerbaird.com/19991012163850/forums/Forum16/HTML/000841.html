1. We lost the spell Charm

Getting a spell at 34 that only works on 20th and lower MOBs was of very little practical and tactical use. Shrink does come in handy in a number of places though.

2. Poison nerfs. (If you can provided specifics then include them here)

NerfS? Poison spells were made better, faster acting (~40 seconds compared to ~2 minutes). The disease line of spells, which did an insane amount of damage over an insane amount of time were indeed overpowered. Only real use for these were for kiting or as inefficient regeneration debuffs. Now they work in a 2 minute time span, and do reasonable damage. Although Plague should be tweaked some way, either more damage or less mana cost - simply because Envenomed Bolt does the same functional damage for 20 more mana in a lot less time.

3. DOT Nerfes

I now do more damage with my DoTs because I'm almost always in a group. When I solo, I'm an in-the-mobs-face kind of person, so the 'nerf' did nothing to me. I still solo at 34th level, and I still meele at 34th level, so it can be done. Don't expect to be killing yellows and whites this way, but you can easily handle low-mid blues.

4. Pets Nerfed.

GZ stated that it was a data error that the pets were doing so much damage. While it is true that they should have researched this more during beta, they never did extensive testing of the higher-level spells. They have to take the blame for this, but on the other hand, pets were doing more damage than they intended, so they corrected it.

5. Wall of Fire scribed as "Rage" then moved to a new L49--> lost one spell on L34.

Wall of Fire was a level 49 spell, moved to level 34, changed to rage, and then moved back to level 49. I believe they stated that wall of fire never worked in the way they wanted it to, so they removed it and gave us Rage, which was an upgrade to the Frenzy/Fury line of spells. Didn't you find it odd that Rage was a more powerful version of Fury, yet both were the same level?

6. Useless Alchamy class specific skill.

No argument here. Currently bugged beyond belief, but we've been told (time and time again) "we're working on it". With that said, if they make changes and make it viable and useful from months upon months of testing and tweaking internally, I'll be happy. If it turns out poorly, then I'll be miffed.
