This is just a note to any and all young rangers out there, use snare, use often. It wasn't until about 15th lvl that I started using it and found out what I was missing out on.

Use it to get two-three shots (or more) from you bow into a mob before melee.

Use it to guard against runners (I was 15th and chasing mobs down, looking like a silly ranger)

Use it especially in a group. When my group enters combat, I don't jump in. I snare each and every one of the mobs first (unless they frequently resist). I then notify my group that all are snared, or if some managed to resist. This prevents a caster from rooting your snares. If for no other reason, my snaring has made me a very valuable member of my group.

On the topic of using Snare multiple times during a fight, don't bother. I snare at about halfway through the fight and then forget about the MOB running. Snare lasts wonderfully long - if you get the Snare to stick in the first place, so to speak.

Now this is coming from a ranger who's used to working with talented bards and enchanters who ensure that the group concentrates on one MOB at a time in situations that would normally be chaotic. My unique job as ranger is to get that Snare in, along with as much damage as I can, on the one MOB that we are all working over.

Your puller targets the most dangerous MOB (healers and casters are top of the list), pulls, and all attackers in your group /assist him. The bards and enchanters work to mesmerize or lullaby the others. So long as nobody whacks one of the sleepers, you're in great shape.

If you're thinking, cast a damage shield on your bards and enchanters so that when a bunch of soothed MOBs wake up and go aggro on them, they dole out damage to everything that hits them until they are put back to sleep. Bards and enchanters are gutsy people. They stand there, hoping that their song or spell will take effect again...
