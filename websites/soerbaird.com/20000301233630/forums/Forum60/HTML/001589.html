The best way to use harmony is to walk up to a group of mobs, say 3 in a camp, until you can see their name tags showing. This keeps you out of their range but allows you to see which mobs you actually caught in the cast.

Cast it on 2 of the mobs, (It is an AE effect so you actually only need to cast it once) some people debate on this, but I find it more effective to make sure you have actually gotten both mobs with the spell, the second is kind of a back up.

Target the Mob you want to pull and snare it. I use snare mostly because the DD spells seem to aggro the others more often than not. Cast snare immediately after you harmony because you dont want him walking slowly by the others when harmony breaks.

Go back, cast harmony on one mob, pull, kill and got for the 3rd.

The other reason to use snare to pull is that, if harmony didn't take hold on one of the other mobs and you need to retreat at least you only have one hitting you in the back. Or, and this can be fun, run far enough from the first mob that you can snare the second, then dual kite.

As far as Harmony use goes,

The mob you "harmonize", becomes the center of the Harmony radius, which in turn,harmonies any MOBS within that radius.

You'll have to get used to the actual radius affected and learn basically what will be harmonized according to which one you cast it on. Never cast on the one you want to pull, rather, Cast on the ones you want to stay put. Pick the best target thats harmony radius would lull the most targets you dont want following your pulled MOB.

Pull your Mob

Test it out on stuff that you can handle should more stuff come on the pulls. Make sure to test on things that still agro though. No sense harmonizing stuff that wont come anyway
