Azzigoth's Quick Guide to Improving Wizard Skills

As a wizard your spells are your weapons. Just as a warrior you must be proficient in their use.

I make it a point to keep my skill levels maxed in 3 of the 4 schools of magical skills. Your skill max is determined by your level. The formula is simple:

Skill Max = (lvl x 5) + 5

For example, for a lvl 12 wizard, Skill Max = (12x5) + 5 = 65.

Improving magic-based skills
Alteration (needed for Root, Enstill, etc.)

Memorize Fade and Shadow Step spells. On a full bar of mana, alternate between casting these two spells. By using the two spells alternating you avoid waiting for it to recover.

I RECOMMEND you do this in a safe zone. It will take some time, but with a high Alteration skill, those Roots should be a bit more successful.
Evocation (needed for damage spells.) These will normally go up on their own through use, since your primary attack power as a wizard is through Evocation-based spells.
Abjuration (defensive spells)

I normally memorize and repeatedly cast Minor Shielding on myself. Even if your currently using a higher level shielding spell, the low cost of 10 mana for Minor Shielding should allow you to cast it a lot.

Another method is to mem and cast O'Kiel's Raditation on your party tanks a lot. At 15 mana it costs more, but can be used as you play.
Divination (invisibility)

Divination has minimal use initially, but inevitably you'll need it for invisibility. Memorize Sense Magic or Sphere of Light (level 1), Glimpse or See Invisible (level 4), or Sense Summoned (level 8)or and cast repeatedly.

On PVP servers, this is less of an issue since you get See Invisible at level 4, and you use it often.
Conjuration

Not very useful yet. My skill in conjuration is very low, and I have not found an effective way to increase it. This is another overlooked skill that you'll regret not practicing once your damage spells at level 16 require Conjuration to cast.

The first conjuration spell a wizard gets is Eye of Zomm at level 8.

 

Improving other skills
Swimming

Do it alot.
Sense Heading

Click it A LOT. Boat rides are a good time, and also while your Meditating. It's always good to have your bearings.

Teremarr of Freeport adds this tip:
"If you use the keyboard for moving and turning, there is a really fast way to fire off Sense Heading often. The User Defined buttons can also be bound to keys. More to the point, they can be bound to the same keys as your movement is. So, for example: If you use "a" to turn left and "d" to turn right, then go into options-keyboard and bind two user defined buttons to those keys, and drop sense heading into each button. This worked great for increasing my skill in sense heading fast! "
Meditation

You should find that this increases pretty readily on your own, since you use it constantly after 4th level.

1HBlunt, 2HBlunt , Piercing
(melee skills)

Once you reach a high level your need for these will disappear. Although many wizards consider it a waste of their time to melee, I think it is very important. It will show your group members that you're not afraid to get dirty and bash monsters with them.

I normally wade into the melee when the monster is down to its last 1/4 of health. It is an opportunity to increase your melee, offense and defense skills.

Once you get your basic skills up to the max, as you level it will not be as difficult to raise them a few points.

Many wizards I know neglect their alteration and divination skills. Investing the time to raise them and keep them maxed is important.
