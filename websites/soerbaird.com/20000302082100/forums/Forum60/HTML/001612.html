I had a blast at Paws last weekend...=) Glad they finally beefed up the joint and it was fun to watch some of my clueless level 50 friends who couldn't even get past the bridge. Remember, this place is simply the same as Blackburrow but beefed up and you have to fight in a SMART way and not simply try to fight your way in or you will be living in trains.. This is not a place to zone fight. You have to clear out sections at a time and avoid fighting along main corridors since you will aggro everything. Best thing to do is camo up, jump down near where Rex used to be and fight from the inside-out. Ironically, the safest area in this place is the secret area where Zixx (the DE necro) used to spawn. And all the former named rooms are safer to fight in than out in the open corridors. Just becareful of the former Gnoll Necro room ( the guy who used to have some crappy dagger, forgot his name) since there are about 4mobs in the alcove that used to be empty.

The water now has a TON of snakes in there with nasty poison so try to avoid fighting them if at all possible and for those of you in the know, the good ole safety rock is no longer safe...=)

Items appear to drop randomly by class and there is no named spawns per say but a LOT of items do drop although we were dissappointed in the low level items themselves. A 3-20 ( I do like the stun effect though) should be at least 5-20 in a dungeon such as this and what is the deal with the PAW with 5 charges of dances with fireflies...=P

Anyway, Paws is very challenging and fun but like any place, you must learn the place in order to succeed. I was in a Group of 3 and 6 during my stints there and we had no trouble at all.

Balerius
Fellowship Veteran 		posted 12-13-1999 15:55            
So far the new Paw is interesting. Went in several times this past weekend with low to mid 30 groups.

Unfortunately, neither I nor anyone else in the groups I was in had much prior experience in the old Paw. So I'm not sure what the "best" way to fight here is. Since Nobunga obviously does have such experience... I would take his advice. Now if I only knew what half of his location names referred to

Our groups made it as far as the room across from the bridge that is just past the double doors. We setup camp there and pulled from the bridge area and from the rooms just beyond (deeper) our campsite. Never saw anything higher than blue to me (35). Gnoll types we saw (spelling is all wrong):

Lethe Gnolls: Normally green (to 35)
Tesche Gnolls: Normally blue
Niche Gnolls: Blue or Green (healers)
Roche Gnolls: Blue (casters: charm and a whirlwind spell and have green/blue pets)

Certain there are more gnoll types..but barring a train from deeper, those are the ones you'll see up to the point we went.

Saw quite a few people pulling gnolls towards zone. Didn't care much for it. After initial spawns are broken the way to do this is to camp a room. Beware though that there are a number of gnolls patrolling main passages. Pick your camp wisely.

Double doors. Through them is a decent camp site. But the first to open the doors will immediately get 3-4 Tesche/Nicsche Gnolls in his/her face. Be ready. The next camp spot for us was in the room just beyond the bridge. Easy to manage with a group of mid 30s. All in all, once set in the room, we had all of the blue gnolls we could want... just limited by heal/mana replenishment time requirements.

Loot: This seems to be pretty random. Off of Tesche/Rosche gnolls we got the Splitpaw Lariat, Splitpaw Gloves, Splitpaw belt and Gnoll eye. No distinguishing features on the gnolls that dropped them (other than the gnolls equipped with the Lariat). More than half of the gnolls carry a FS weapon of some type. cash loot is minimal with most dropping only a few silver or gold (tho some may drop 1 plat or so).

So that's what I've seen. Admittedly not much since I haven't ventured too deep. From the sound of things... deeper may not mean higher level Mobs. We'll see.
