One of the more exciting things about playing a ranger is that in order to have any kind of effectiveness solo or desireability in a group you must use all your tools. I'd like to throw in the word underpowered here. The point being that you must think rather than just act. Keep in mind this comes from a lvl 35 ranger, so I don't know everything, not by a long shot.

Solo: Snare is your friend. A Bow is your friend. Dot's and DD are your friends. Basically, the drill is pretty simple. Snare the enemy first. If you have a dot, use it. It is your most efficient damage dealer. If you want to take an enemy down quickly, throw on your DD spells next, they are very time efficient, but do not burn all your mana. Archery is your least efficient method of dealing damage, but you will need to use it to soten your foe up further. Make sure you have your buffs and damage shields up before moving to melee. In other words, you will be doing a low speed form of kiting with snare. Melee can be used to take off the last bit of health.

Groups: Your primary responsibility is absorbing damage so the caster can nuke it. You will keep the enemy off the caster by kicking, taunting, and using spells that make enemies mad. You will always be responsible for snaring the target. In fact at later levels this will become your primary responsibility as you cease to deal significant damage and can no longer absorb significant damage in comparison to other tanks. Dots make wonderful snares. Casting DD spells in melee is generally frowned upon because you will be burning valuable snare mana and your melee will be more powerful, and/or whatever DD you can muster will be insignificant in comparison to what the casters are dealing. Archery is a good way to get yourself kicked out of a group. Pulling will likely be your job. Resign yourself to being a puller and snarer.

PvP: First note is that playing a melee class on an actual pvp server, or playing a melee class that is PvP is stupid. If you lose one piece of ruby, you are looking at 30 + hours straight camping to replace it. In duels the strategy is similar to soloing. Expect to lose badly to casters if you don't pull off the solo tactic perfectly. The only real note in addition to solo strategy is that you will not want to stand off from a caster. Melee will interrupt their spells, and will be required to have a shot at all. Comparable level casters can kill me with 3 spells. You cannot let them cast.

That more or less sums it up.

Always cast flame lick on a mob in their last bubble of health. If both me and a mob are down really low in a close fight I will snare DOT and run, pop a few arrows and they will succumb.

Never let yourself be caught with less than a bubble of mana in combat. Save that last bit for unexpected trains or bad luck runs.

When fighting casters save your kick and time it to coincide with their spells. You will interrupt a lot more often.

When in a group always take the rear of a MOB. Tanks always seem to choose the front. Surrounding a MOB causes them much confusion you will find and I seem to hit easier.

Always stop a second before firing a bow. Even when kiting. You will get critical hits more often.

When opting for the better part of valor or just plain old running for your life you will sometimes find the zone within arms reach. JUMP! you clear more distance jumping covering more ground quickly. Just remember your stamina drops so this is a last resort tactic.

When you can, run backwards to a zone fighting as you leave. You will take less damage in the long run I have found.

Death is inevitable. Don't panic. Find a nice spot to die. Find a land mark nice and level spot away from walls. Someplace away from MOBS so you can drag your corpse back out.

You cannot cast CAMO in a dungeon. But, you can cast it before going in. A good thing to know for corpse retrieval or general exploring to learn a new dungeon.

Don't ever forget glimpse. I always take a look at tough MOBS up close to see if they have that rare item I want. If they are empty handed I offer my turn to the next guy or group in line being the NICE guy hehe.

Use a good cheap arrows not the absolute cheapest. Field tip, bone shaft, parabolic fletch, small nock. Still a cheap arrow to use and way better damage and range.

Next time you get a couple good bronze weapons of your favorite type and a good cheap tunic like snakeskin. Put them in a backpack along with a normal lightstone, food, water, beetle eye, and 2 cheap dervish rings, etc. This is your emergency pack for that extra tough corpse retrieval. And in celebration of your corpse reunion give the pack to a deserving young adventurer.

Master alcohol tolerance when you get bored. This is a suprise tactic I will say no more. I learned this from an Ogre hehehe.

Practice all weapon types always. You never know what new weapons may turn up.

Always pay generous donations for services like bind and SOW. You make friends who will often buff you without asking. Most just want to know that their services are appreciated.

More later I don't want to write a guide here hehe.

Someone already clarified this for me, reguarding archery while in a group. If you melee you can deal quite respectable damage for a melee type. Your stacked damage shields actually make a ranger a wonderful primary tank if you can get your AC up, because when they do hit you they will be reciving significant damage in return, and since one of the damage shield lines we get is self only, it is more efficient to have them beat on you.

The main reasoning behind not archering under normal group situations is simply that your damage dealt will go way down. Archery simply is not that efficient at dealing damage.

I probably should elaborate that there is a certain group situation in which archery is particularly useful. Get together a couple rangers and a few casters and go hill giant hunting. The objective there is to avoid standing toe to toe with them at all costs. Casters will do the bulk of the damagae, as always, but the bow combined with a snare and a swarm ensures they will stay mad at you and will chase you, giving your casters plenty of time to stand still and nuke the hell out of the MOB. This is actually quite a lot of fun to do.

DD frowned on in groups? By whom? Heh, it actually depends on what spell you're casting. Careless Lightning IS effective enough for us to use in group combat; it's about the only spell we get that's more effective than straight melee for the amount of time spent casting vs. the same amount of time meleeing (unless you've got two very high-end weapons like Ykeshas).

At level 40, I can comfortably cast maybe 5 Careless Lightnings and still have mana for snaring. Since this spell is seldom resisted, that's 400-500 damage I can deal out in about 14-15 seconds...nothing much compared to a caster, but still generally superior to melee--it helps substantially when you've got several mobs on the group at once, and the fight's hairy, and you need to cut down enemy numbers quickly. It's also very handy when your healers are OOM, and that mob will NOT get off that almost-dead party member (usually the mage that would be nuking it--if they could get off a spell, that is...)

One more thing; while soloing, if you're going to use magic to pull, use Lightning, to pull, not Swarm...with DoT nerfing, you want the mob to move around as little as possible while the spell's in effect. Swarm is also easier to get off while being hit; cast it after the mob's toe-to-toe with you.
