As you all know one of the feilds in which we rangers accel at is pulling for a large group. Ever wonder how to pull 1 out of those 3 or 4 blue mobs standing together in a zone? Well here are a few pointers on the art of Aggro Range pulling.

First as you know all mobs have a bubble or circular radius of aggro range. The range of this "aggro zone" is dependent on the mob type and/or your lvl and faction (lvl and faction have yet to be confirmed). If you get on the outermost edge of a mob's aggro range you can easily pull that mob away from the pack.

First off rember to get good at this you NEED to experiment. You'll have to inch up on spawn points from diffrent angles for diffrent groupings of Mobs in diffrent zones. The whole theroy behind aggro pulling is to get on the outermost edge of the aggro bubble of a mob and wait till he see's you and runs after you.. For example take Mobs A,B,C lets say they are Greater Kobolds in SolB you see them in the following arangment.

A

C

B

if your not careful you can spring all 3 at once however imagine 3 circles around these mobs (size dependent on the above listed factors)
*
* *
* A *@
* *@ @
+ *@ C @
+ + @ @
+ B + @
+ +
+

As you can see there is certain area's of overlap in the circles. The trick is to approach the mob's in a fashion that you could pull them one at a time without aggroing the others so In this case you would first approach B...

*
* *
* A *@
* *@ @
+ *@ C @
+ + @ @
+ B + @
+ +
+

^
|

and inch closer to him slowly pausing each time to see if it will aggro you.. if it does you have to retrace you steps in the same path you came just turning and hauling ass could run you across the other mob's and put all your efforts at naught. Repeat this with the other mobs in the area pulling them as necessary.

In some cases mobs seem to be "linked" or certian mobs will automatically trigger other mobs in a certain range around them to pull with them. This is usually the case with "Named" monsters. Here the trick is to figure out the outermost range of the largest Aggro feild and pull everything else you can before even getting close to the big guy.

I know there are a LOT of sceptics of this technique however I know it works, and have refined it to an art in the areas I used to frequent regularly. I've found this technique works best for pulling BLUE and the occasional WHITE mob and works less frequently with the YELLOW and RED mobs. It is also good for spacing a large spawn of blues that would hurt you bad if they came all at once (Ex the anticilles in mistmoore at the end of the cave)

Try it out a few times on spawns you know you can handle if you mispull to your group. You will quickly figure out which spawns it works on what mobs have what ranges and how to easily clean out named monster spawns. By first clearing out their gaurds to get them solo.

As far as linking MOBs goes, they very much are. I've been in Highpass Keep for a while and the goblins are linked together. Good example is the lookout room. If you hit one, they both will come at you. The aggro overlap is such that I don't think you can "inch" up to one without the other triggering. If you hit one with an arrow, you've got them both. There are two ways around this:

1) Mesmerize (enchanter spell) one and it stands there looking stupid until it is either attacked or the spell finally wears off. It will be aggro when that happens.

2) Harmony (usually affects them both) and draw one with an arrow. As long as no one gets in aggro range of the other it'll stand there forever.

Below is a long discourse that I made a while ago to my guild's mailing list about my understanding of the various agro radii.

Hope it is helpful.

--Q

As far as I can tell, mobs have three radius that determine their actions:

1. The basic aggresion radius.

This is the radius around a mob in which it will activate if *you* get too close to it. Experimentation has shown that the level difference between the puller and the mob affects the odds (per unit time) that it will go aggressive.

2. The "help" aggresion radius.

This radius is what causes mobs near to one that you pull to come as well. It has some interesting characteristics as well. Let's say I am staring into a room with three mobs. If I pull one, they will all come if they are within range. Everyone knows that.

Here are some interesting things I have learned though:

- Mobs that are green (even if they are agro green) will NOT answer the "help" call of its friends. That means, if I am facing a room full of green critters, I can pull just the one that I want. This information should be obvious to anyone over level 35, but apparently it is not. I have seen many 40+ players in Soldunga clearing entire rooms just to try and make one rare spawn.

- Mobs that answer a "help" call do not appear to generate one of their own for some period of time. This is one of the more useful pieces of information I've been able to glean. The best way to illustrate this point is by example. There is a spot in Solusek A that sure looked like it had the potential to deliver a new rare spawn. I asked our resident gnome area expect, whether he had ever cleared the area. His answer was that attacking the gnomebot in question caused an large mess of yuck to respond to the "help" call and rush in. Thinking that undesireable, we hit upon the idea of engaging another mob well out of everythings "help" radius and then nudging it slowly closer to the mob we wanted to pull. Sure enough, after a bit of playing with it, we were able to use one mob to lure another to use without pulling everything else.

Even still, we weren't sure that it was working correctly, so we tried another experiment. We moved to maximum archery range (at least, maximum that we could in the confines of Soldunga) and lured the mob we wanted directly. Sure enough, his call for "help" brought a bunch of junk with him.

It is my belief that this technique can be used to pull single non-green mobs that meet the following criteria:

a) The mob you want is placed in such a way that there is some radius X where it will respond to a "help" call, but the mobs you do not want won't (we will call their radius Z).

b) There be sufficient LOS distance to make it possible to generate a mob "help" call with a radius Y, where Z > Y > X. (Yay algebra).

Phew. Got that?

Ok, the last radius that we've found is the "heal and buff" radius.

3. Each caster mob that has heal or buff spells has a third radius that determines when they will either heal or buff a nearby mob. This radius is generally much larger than the "agro" or "help" radius and in and of itself does not trigger aggresiveness. A few notes on this:

- I believe that the non-aggresion part of this radius is why healer type mobs never seem to run out of mana. If they did, it would be very easy to pull a weak mob into heal range and just keep meleeing it and letting the caster heal it until the caster was drained of mana and therefore became easy pickings.

- It is possible to run non-healing casters out of mana (though they regen mana VERY fast compared to PCs). This makes me wonder if one couldn't bring a weak mob into buffing range and melee forever while continually canceling any buffs that the caster provided (fireshield, etc). This *might* have the effect of slowly putting the caster mob into a LOM or OOM state, if the buff was large enough that it would eat mana faster than the regen rate. Worth a try sometime.

In any event, I hope this information wasn't completely obvious to everyone that has managed to stick with it this long.
