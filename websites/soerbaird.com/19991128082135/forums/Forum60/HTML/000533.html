I'll freely admit that I'm not a high-level ranger who's seen it all. But I still have an opinion, and I am still a Ranger who enjoys her path in life.

And one thing that saddens me these days is to see post after post of Rangers whining and complaining that this doesn't work, or that broke, or how low-powered Rangers are, and I'm getting really tired of it.

I *enjoy* being a Ranger. I like being able to track my prey through the plains and the forests, and I'm getting quite good at rooting an opponent, riddling it with arrows, and repeating once or twice more before closing and tearing it apart with melee. I enjoy helping out those in trouble in my forests, giving them advice, and helping them to survive.

If you don't like playing a ranger, stop whining about it and try something else. Be a halfling warrior, or dwarven paladin, but go out and *enjoy* EverQuest like you did when you were just starting out.

I read this forum because I want to see the ideas that others have in becoming more effective rangers, or hearing of quests undertaken or great successes in the field.

Maybe I'm unusual in that I want to see the good things here, rather than the bad. Perhaps I'm out of line, believing in the enjoyment of our craft. So be it. I am a Ranger, protector of the forests, and denizen of the land. And I *like* it.

Djell, I think most of us here really love being a ranger, too. I know that I, too, really loved tracking prey in the plains and the forests, because that's where rangers are in their element.

Unfortunately, as we approach level 30, we find ourselves increasingly forced out of our element into dungeons. Although I've done my share of contributing to the discussions on how to "fix" the ranger class, I really don't think we're broken, either. It's just that if dungeons are all that's left for higher level players, I am beginning to see how the ranger class might need to have adapt to that environment. In outdoor zones, we get to take advantage of all of our skills, whereas in dungeons, we're just another tank, and a bit weaker one at that. We made a trade off for being a hybrid, but then find that many of our advantages are useless in dungeons, like some of our better spells. That was fine with me, as long as I could take a break from the dungeon rat race every now and then, and return to the relaxing peacefulness of tracking prey in the plains and the forests, but those days are coming to an end for me.

As I posted on the Wish List Board, ideally, I think we need more outdoor zones, like Butcherblock and the Karanas (as opposed to Crushbone, CT, and Mistmoore), but for higher levels. That's where rangers belong and in those types of environments, we're perfect just as we are. Protectors of the Forest? Of course, so surely you can understand how some of us might feel when there's no more forest to protect.
