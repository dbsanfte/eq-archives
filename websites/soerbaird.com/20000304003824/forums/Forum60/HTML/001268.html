First thought was that for 500pp they might have thought you were selling a Stein of Moggok rather than a Crude Stein... but rereading it then it appears that he probably was just trying to get you out of the zone.

I'm not an expert on contract law but reneging on a deal once it has been struck (even if somebody offers you a better price) is dishonorable as it amounts to 'breach of contract'... you have agreed to provide goods to somebody at an agreed price and the buyer has agreed to pay that price to you.

I don't think it would be a good idea to try that trick at somewhere like Christies - I'm not sure what happens if you win a bid on an item and then can't pay the asking price, but I have a feeling you would not be welcome there ever again.

The same thing applies here - the auction is based on a level of trust on both sides and if a player is acting in an untrustworthy manner then it is good of Rhadgast to make other players aware... if you read this thread are you going to take this Rheo character seriously if he offers you the sale of the century? I think (hope!) not.
