I have a lvl 36 mage, and at later levels the difference between wizard and mage is quite large in my opion. Ok, first off let me say that soloing with a mage is alot easier than a wizard at low lvls, but at around lvl 25, a
wizard with sow or jboots seems to be more effective. This is manly because at these lvls the mob is going to ignore your pet all together and go straight for you, and since mages have no "root" spell at all this can be a problem. Also, wizards generaly can cast faster than a mage can, this adds up quick, but remember when a wizard is out of mana he is S.O.L. where as mage generally has a pet still. Also, at higher lvls wizards are generally more welcomed into groups than a mage for a couple reasons. Wizards have transport spells and evac, always useful in a dungeon. Mages can summon stuff, but at higher levels it is fairly useless crap when compared to what most people have already. Also, many groups are pet shy in dungeons, and if a mage can't summon a pet, he is reduced to a second rate caster. Though I like playing my mage, it can be very fustrating for me at times. If you really want to play a INT. caster and want a challenge consider an enchanter (probably one of the most power classes in the game "IF" played very well). Oh, on a rambling side note. One of the most fun things that I have done with my mage is to walk in a low lvl dungeon and let my pet massacre every thing in sight, it a good way to blow off steam. (lets see a wizard do that)
