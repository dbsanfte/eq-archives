I hate to admit the following story - pretty dumb result for a street-wise Ranger - but I got to thinking that if some of these things can happen to me they can happen to you and I would really hate for any other Ranger to have a problem getting their bow.

Getting the Rain Caller bow is tedious at best. If you don't have a Trueshot and make your own for the quest it is even more so. I started it the other day and got some of my guildmates to help me get the hair from the Mermaid. No problem we loaded up with scales and went to camp her. We got hair for three of us that were Rangers in about 3 hours and also got some nice experience. Then we gated to Cazic Thule to visit the Gorillas. We arrived and ran over to the TR and waited. Five minutes later two spawn and we joined battle. I looted one then ran over to loot the other. Doh, I looked in my inventory and realized that I hadn't an empty slot from filling up on Swordfish bills and one of the gorilla hairs had poofed because it was no drop! So we had to wait for an additional spawn cycle. Then everyone camped in my group camped, but I had some time so I ran to Freeport and bought the tonics from the gnome in West FP. Then I got a TP to GFD and then ran over Aknon to turn them in. I arrived there and finally found the NPC when to my horror I realized that the tonics are consumables and I had consumed two of them by placing them in the first empty slot in my back pack. ARGGGGG....back to Freeport for more and no one to do TPs! So I take the boat grrrrr. Got more tonics and took boat back to BB then ran back to Aknon. I got the toupee this time and then back to BB for Boat back to Freeport. Repair Toupee then look for Jyle. He isn't hard to find when you know where to find him. My misfortune was I didn't have a clue and it took me awhile to find him. Seems he is a "roamer" and had been out of his spot the first time I looked in the room that he hangs out in, if I hadn't offered a reward for locating him it would of probably taken me longer. So I got the heart and then jumped on a boat and went back to Aknon....finally I get the wing! Wonderful I say to myself, now all I have to do is turn this in and get my Rain Caller!!!! Well, the princess is REALLY on a weird spawn/despawn cycle. I sat and waited for her for over 2 hours. The guys I camped her with said she only turned up like every 5 or 6 hours and only stuck around for a little while then poofed, so beware. Anyways, I finally had all the things I needed so I ran over to Felwithe. Now it is 2 am and I am really tired and want to get some sleep. I find the Enchanter and offer him the Terant Heart, the 3000 gp, the fairy wing and my Trueshot bow. Cool...but there is a snag! He says he cannot give me the Rain Caller because I have not completed the Quest. I need to provide him with the Fairy Dust and a Trueshot bow! ACK!!!! I look in my inventory and see a small bag and a bow and I think hmmm....guess you can only give him one thing at a time and he "bounced" these things back to me. So I give him the bow again and he tells me "Thanks Andros for the Rain Caller bow" .... I don't need to tell you how I felt! Of course I immediately petition this then check to see if there are any GMs on...yeah right. Needless to say I felt really stupid for not checking the bow before I gave it back but it looks exactly the same as the trueshot and I had no reason to suspect it wasn't as he told me he couldn't give me one. So be warned - there is some kind of bug with this quest and PLEASE don't let this or any of the other things I fouled up happen to you. Finally, wish me luck as I once again set out to redo the quest as I suspect the GMs will give me very little assistance.

Andros Silvermist
Rain Callerless and Sad Ranger of Fennin Ro

I think what happened here was this: I've noticed before that if you give a questmaster your items all at once, he will pretend he got them all individually, one right after the other.

For instance, both times I did the Ivy Bracers, I put the ring, rune, and gem in the windows, then gave them to gandarin. I got a couple of 'are you forgetting something' messages, then the 'here is your bracer' message.

This is something people should keep in mind when giving multiple items for quests.

NPCs keep track of items only for quests (not who gave them) and after all of the items are given the giver of the last item will get the reward. The 4 slots for giving items to the NPC also act as near instantaneous but separate trades so giving all of the items at once will work in any case that I know of.

On the aspect of giving back the item though I would have asked a GM to check the body of the NPC for the item as pretty good proof that you did in fact give the item to the NPC (only "no drop" items will poof from a corpse so far as I know if this would have been done - GMs probably also have better means to check the body for items though).
