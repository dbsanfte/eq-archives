Traveling alone has always been my true love. I suppose that is why I became a member of Faydarks Champions. I love to wander through the land, exploring every hill and valley until I know the land like the back of my hand.

I was exploring in the Commons when I came upon a pass in the hills. I followed this pass and it led me to a forest. The dense trees blocked out the very light of the sun and an strange chill went down my spine. I immediately knew this place was dangerous and yet I felt so at home. The place drew me in and I began searching for signs of its inhabitants.

Following the tracks of a human led me to a rangers station. I entered the station and started a conversion with Morin Shadowbane. I learned then that the forest I had discovered was named after the great wood elf ranger, Kithicor. Kithicor was not always the dark place that it is now. Some darkness has entered the woods and now threatens the very existence of the forest. It consumes the forest and Kithicor himself. Morin desperately tries to hold ground against the darkness but the rangers are losing and are in dire need of assistance. Morin told me that if I was up to the task that they were in great need of weapons and information.

He told me that the rangers here required the legendary Ebony Blade. I had heard of this blade and knew that it was to be found in lands that I, being young and inexperienced, would be unable to obtain. But information, I was good at gathering information. Morin went on to explained that there was a dark messenger, one that lived beyond death, that carried information to and from the darkness that consumed Kithicor and the undead camps in Karana. He went on to explain that if I were able to apprehend this messenger and bring back any information that he might be carrying, that I could have his sword as my reward.

I promised Morin that I would do my best to do this for him, for my love of the forest, and for my devotion to my brother rangers. I left him then and started upon my long journey. I traveled throughout the land asking all that I encounter if they had any news of this messenger. I journeyed long and hard and everyone I asked had the same story. There is no messenger, he does not exists.

I traveled throughout the lands of Highpass; journeyed into the Karanas; visited the wee little folk of Rivervale and still no messenger. I fought goblins in Misty Thicket, gnolls in East Karana, and the Orcs that block the pass but everywhere it was the same. No one had seen him and most had never even heard of him. Tired and frustrated I turned back to Kithicor. I had failed and my head hung low as I returned to Morin to tell him my sad tale.

As I approached Morins cabin to deliver the news of my failure, I encounter a beautiful wood elf. I could tell by her dark green armor that she was a ranger of many seasons. Her beautiful smile and graceful presence brought joy to an otherwise sorrowful day. I ventured into a conversation, intrigued by her beauty, but in actuality, avoiding my unpleasant task.

Tansia was her name and we sat about idly chatting. Eventually I confessed to her the purpose of my travels and of my ultimate failure. She smiled and for an instant my heart sank as I thought she was thinking me the fool. "I have good news for you brother ranger", she said. "Recently I was traveling back from Qeynos when I caught sight of this messenger. I know for a fact that you can find him if you travel to the undead camps of West Karana."

The journey to West Karana was not that far and if I hurried I could reach it by nightfall. Invigorated by her words, I thanked her and quickly left. It was a long hot journey; full of perils along the way; but as the sun began to set, I came to West Karana.

Donning my best camouflage, I left the path and headed south towards the undead camps. There were many signs of things that walked without life. Some were old and some were not, but the tracks foretold of an evil presence.

It was not long before I came upon some fresh tracks and my pulse quickened as I raced to encounter with my prey. Would this be him? Would I finally see the object of all my trials?

In all my journeys I have encounter many of the living dead. Many have I fought and few have I feared. Call me young, call me foolish but I was never prepared for what was about to come. Foolhardily, I raced toward an unknown foe and it was not long before I caught sight of it. At first I thought that it was like many of the other living dead and quickly engaged it in battle. But this was no ordinary ghoul, he stood half a head taller than any that I had seen and he delivered blows that cracked my ribs when he struck. The fight had barely started but I knew the fool I was.

He delivered blow after blow and it would not be long before the buzzards would be cleaning the meat from my bones if I did not turn to run. I swung one last time and turned to run only to find that my legs had not the strength to move. My foe was greater than I and there was not to be done. I turned back to my foe and valiantly fought, knowing full well that it would be to my death and not his.

He swung quick and hard and the obvious end was near. I said my final prayers as I swung my sword and axe for I knew that soon I would face Tunare. The world went black and next thing that I was to see would be in another world. And when I opened my eyes, the billowy white clouds and blue sky was all that I could see. That is, until he leaned over me to apply another bandage.

I could tell by his painted face that this was not the heaven I had prepared for. He stood now and towered over me. He was as tall as the trees, or so it seemed to me. The muscles of his arms fought against the constricting bands of his armor and I knew him to be a barbarian of the frozen north. He looked to be a great warrior. His armor was torn in places and he bled from many others. He had recently fought a great battle and he was the obvious victor. A wolf sat by his side and he spoke to it as if it understood him. It seems that he had seen the battle and come to my aid. He fought off the messenger just as I fell and he nursed me back to health.

Sitting up I glanced around to see the messengers corpse laying near our camp. My new found friend informed me that he had searched it and that it had upon it some note that he had no use for; that I was welcome to it if I wanted. I rested a little longer to regain my strength. I rose then took the note from our common foe. Sitting by the fire I slowly unrolled it to see what it might be.

My heart leapt as I came to realize that this was the information that Morin required. I rested a little longer with the barbarian and his wolf. I thanked for him all his help and asked if there was anything that I could do for him. "A few rations if you have them to spare" was all he said. We said our good-byes and started my journey back to Kithicor.

Nowadays I am more careful of the battles that I engage. At night, I sit by the fire and as I sharpen Morins blade, I dream of Tansia and wonder if ever I shall see her again.

Cirmie
Conclave of Champions
Karana
