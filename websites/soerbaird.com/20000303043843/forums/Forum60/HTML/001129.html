On Kithicor, camo, and wielding a boomerang that uses 1H Slashing instead of Throwing as a skill....

This is a new thread that covers related topics from several other threads.

First off, Kithicor. At first I was ticked about the zone change. I even /petitioned about it and had a discussion with a guide. I don't remember the name of that guide, but if you're reading this, I appologize for ranting. I think the change in Kithicor is a great idea, dispite the fact that I will finally be able to hunt there in about 2002. I agree whole heartedly with Saldrin's reference to fantasy literature. IMO this only serves to add to the attraction of living out your own novel through EQ. All in all, this has become one of my favorite zones.

Training in Kithicor: This is no big deal. I've done it since the zone change. Sure, it's a little more tricky, but the risk is still preferable to running all the way to QRG or taking that LOOOOONG boat ride to Kelethin. The complaining based on this point just proves my theory that when you give someone something that they're used to not having, then take it away or reduce it after a few days or weeks, they always feel like they are worse off than they were before they knew it even existed.

Travelling through Kithicor: I have made the trip dozens of times since the zone change without incident. I just run high on the north wall and everything leaves me alone. RARELY you have to watch where you're going and take a slight detour, but if you keep your eyes open, it's no big deal day or night.

Camo in Kithicor: I tried using camo for the first several trips. I never got attacked, but the few things I conned showed ready to attack, so camo does NOT work on the undead there. However, nothing that I conned ever attacked me..........

SSoM: This sword really IS a 25ish or less weapon. Case in point: Langseax. The ONLY difference between a Langseax and the SSoM (apart from who can use each) is that the SSoM has a whopping 1 point faster delay, and has the +3 STR and +3 AGI stats. Two 19s can easily do the Langseax quest. Heck, two 17s can probably do it. I've done it several times with the help of a shaman. The stat bonuses are NICE on the SSoM, but I hardly think they are worth 15 levels above the warrior's benefit of a Langseax. Plus, you can dual wield Langseaxes while the SSoM is LORE.

Finally, the "big banana": Call me crazy, but I actually LIKE the new look of the SSoM. Currently my favorite weapons look is a ranger dual wielding a SSoM and some sort of Scimitar (although I use a Screaming Mace with my SSoM currently). One curves in, the other curves out. It's a DEADLY looking combination. Imagine yourself holding both and taking a double handed, diagonal downward swing with your fists touching. You have more power on the swing, a dual razorblade from hilt to curve, and seperated tips for multiple styles of wounds in the same location on your target. Almost impossible to heal without leaving a NASTY scar if it doesn't kill first.
