You've got a tough decision to make, and I really do not envy you in this. I already noted once today that there are people in those high level guilds that are truly "bad apples" as you put it.

90% of guilds do not actively police their members for aberrant behavior. The other 10% are truly difficult to be an officer in. You spend a lot of time taking in complaints, and listening to people who have a beef with a member. Then you have to decide how to handle the situation.

So, your problem is now that you're in a guild with a lot of people who are pleasant, and a few that are really not. Those few have made your guild's reputation suffer. What do you do? Hmm. Usually you just have to have a bad rep . . . and PERSONALLY combat it, by being very nice. But say you don't want to . . . well, you can actively try and talk to the people with the unpleasant stigma, and see what can be done . . . but usually there is a reason they're disliked. More often than not, they really are selfish bastards.

(I know you're not in a guild now, I was generally illustrating.)

So, honestly, you might want to take a very careful look at those guilds that ARE out there. See which ones are the best . . . few disruptive members, playing time near yours, etc. But no matter what, don't jump into the next guild headed to Fear/Hate . . . take some time to look around.

There are other advantages to being in a guild besides getting into PoF.

One of our younger members was screwed by a guy in CB today who decided to keep a dragoon dirk for himself instead of sharing with the group.

Well, when I heard about this I immediately talked to an officer of this guy's guild and told him what happened. I can tell you, as a guild officer I want to maintain good relations with other guilds and uphold the honor of our clan, so I take their complaints very seriously if I get them. This officer felt the same way I am sure, and the matter was dealt with.

Guilds help control poor conduct on any server, especially if they are well led. If you want the protection and backing of a powerful system of allies, do some guild shopping very carefully and join one that has a very good reputation, strong leadership, and a fairly rigorous admission policy (in my opinion). If some guy you've never met invites you into his guild you can bet they've done the same thing with lots of others, and you'll get a lot of idiots to play with.

Guilds should help you play the game, not take away from its enjoyment.

if you're in a guild and someone wants to join, try and find out through them (and check with others) if they were in another guild. i've heard of people getting kicked out of guilds for their actions and then joining another guild the same day. don't let it be your guild they join. it is exactly like the guy above said, you don't wanna be in a guild that doesn't know you before you enter, because that means they don't know the other people either.

so while it may seem like a pain to have to "prove" yourself to people, and haughty of them to judge you, the best part about being in a guild is that you are going to have closer contact to people that have similar attitudes and in order to maintain that environment a guild has to determine the attitude of every applicant...even you.

it is very interesting being on my side of things, where i joined a guild way back in the days of dog-eat-dog diablo battle.net where not being in a guild was a pain and it was a constant struggle to keep the bad element out. we carried the guild over to many other games, and now EQ is one of them. you wanna talk about one strict gun-shy set of people who are very careful about evaluating new members, try coming from a game where 90% of the people cheat and they inflict themselves on the remaining few.

It all became about how some people resented the higher level people in the guild who wouldn't help them camp items beyond thier level.

If we get one item that a bunch of people need, we'll continue to work at it until everyone gets what they need. Camping like that gets boring at times, but if you're with a group of people who all agree with you that the sword Painbringer looks like a minor rip off of the sword used by Voltron, it's a lot more entertaining.
