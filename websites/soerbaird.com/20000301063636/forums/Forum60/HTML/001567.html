That depends VERY much on the setting of shadow men you're facing. Harmony works so and so, they have very high magic resistance.

1.If there's a necromancer in among them you may be feared and find yourself fleeing while beeing pounded on by 4 or so very angry men. Recognized by the book in the hand and the little brown bonepile beside them.
2. Clerics pose a problem, they do have a very good heal and it takes a long time to kill them or any other shadow man while there's a cleric alive among the enemies. Recognized by the ice cream cone in their hand.
3. Warriors should at level 33 be ignored until they are the only ones remaining. You won't lose a bubble meleeing them.
4. Kill order priority is a tough question. Even though the necros fear you and hurts you bad I'd go with killing the cleric first, then necro. Leave the warriors for dessert.

To give you some insight, when I was level 31 and my necromancer friend was 30 we gladly went out thinking that we would easily break and solo a shadow man group. They were after all low blues. First try was in Nektulos, I found myself feared during the course of the battle, we had 2 necros and one warrior on us. I didn't ONCE make it back to the spot where we decided to go at it. After a couple of Shocks of Poison and a constant picking of the little brown skeletons I had one bubble of health when I came out of a fear. Decided to bolt for the zone. One of the very angry neriak guards beat the snot out of me while the shadow men took care of my friend. We made one more trie and killed 5 shadow men all in all but it was painfully apparent that there was no way we could hold the spawn.

Next try was in Western Commons with various friends, we died there too :P Only with a couple of friends around 30 you're safe(r). The setting of 2 necros, 2 clerics and 2 warriors is never easy.

Now to put this lengthy post to an end, I was looking for information on shadow men after the first disaster and couldn't find any. So I told my tale. And about your question, I honestly only have an educated guess, I think it will be possible to solo any setting as a ranger somewhere around 37 to 38, at 39 I'm positive. You should resist most every spell by mid 30's.
