Here's my first experience with kedge. I'm level 33 and went in with a 39 wizzie, 39 cleric, and 36 shaman. We didn't have much time but wanted to see what was around (we'll go back later). We just cleared the first part of the area where the swordfish spawn. Not too many problems, and we chopped up 4 or 5 of them when we encountered a different kind of swordfish, impailing or impailer I think. This conned red to our level 39 I believe and we got it down to half health before we decided to zone out. It was hitting me for damage in the 90s sometimes. If the rest of our party had made it, I think we could have taken it. If the golden mermaid is in a room near the zone as I've read, then it may be possible for a group in the high 30's to take her. Most of the swordfish at the zone entrance were blue to me at 33 (except that impailer one). It may be possible, after clearing those swordfish, to pull the golden mermaid separately. I did not get a chance to look around much, maybe more tonight. It's a pretty cool zone but you definitely need a good mix of classes in the group. Bard, wizzie, cleric, tank. Two bards are good too because one can sing stamina song while other sings the breath song if necessary. We have an advantage having enduring breath, that's for sure. If you need fish scales, go to Rivervale and kill trout, hehe.

Hail,
I went to Kedge last night on a scouting mission to see what it was like beyond the zone line.
We had a full group of mid 40's, with evryone assigned a secondary role.
Ranger-Enduring Breath
Palidan-Invigor
Wizard-Evac
Cleric-Resist Magic
Enchanter-AoE Stun
Warrior-(well, he did what he always does and took the damage)
We found the following in the first few rooms
Lancer Swordfish-35th level
Shimmering Sailfin-35th level (caster, the size of a large trout)
Piercer Swordfish-37th level
Impaler Swordfish-39th level
Stiletto Toothed Pirana-44th level
Saw several other species that were indifferent.
Phinigel Atropos (50+ I am told), the zone Boss is so close to the zone you can hear him casting as soon as you enter.
It was a stressfull evening to say the least.
We made some ok xp and a little loot, but learned a lot and we were glad we were a good team and could depend on one another.
Our visit ended when we were fortunate to encounter the rare and very impressive Cauldronbubble a 50th level Hammerhead that hits for double 130's and can block the entire hallway.
Gonna have to guess that he is the one who drops the ranger only Hammerhead helm, Stats are Ac 13 +7 Str/Sta and weight 4.0We have our guild tinkerers hard at work making Aqualungs and Rebreathers for future trips and plan to return soon.
