Even though it seems that Verant is reducing its "tuning" of more Shaman spells on the test server, the wife and I have decided to leave Norrath for good. She played a shaman as her primary character as well, and was equally displeased with the events as of late.

Most of you will probably not care, some of you may care but continue to play, some of you may stop playing for a while, some of you may quit all together. NOT because of my actions, but because you too are disgusted with the attitude displayed by GZ towards PAYING CUSTOMERS. For the wife and I, this was simply the proverbial "straw", as was it the BIGGEST factor in our final decision to leave the game.

This was NOT a decision that was reached lightly. The wife and I started discussing the possiblity after the first "tuning". We decided to grin and bare it, but this latest round was just too much. Especially when we were witness to GZ's own response to some of the issues we thought to be important. I have never been so offended as I was when reading Welans post/reply from GZ. We, again, discussed it last night and agreed to sleep on it. We woke this morning with the same sentiments and decided to pull the plug.

Both of our characters were deleted this morning. The only thing that remains is my account with two lvl 1 characters with our equipment in backpacks. I'll be giving all of it to a friend so it doesn't go to waste. Don't worry....he's also a master smith and is 10 levels HIGHER than Valgoth was, so all of this stuff he could get easily himself. Just felt compelled to address that since I know someone here was bound to accuse me of twinking.

I guess, at least according to GZ, I must be one of those "dramatic" folks. Some may view my actions as childish. Some may understand. Some may flame. I'm ok with all of the above. All I ask is that you understand one thing....

This WAS more than just a game to me. In all respects, Valgoth was my alter ego, the fantasy character I always wanted to be. Through him I was able to escape much of real lifes sorrow and tragedies. I started playing EQ the day before my dad passed away unexpectedly. 2 weeks later I was laid off from my job due to a reduction in workforce. The next 2 1/2 months, EQ became my LIFE (outside of emailing resumes). I was able to get away from some of the pain in my real life and enjoy an alternate reality for a while. I made some new friends, got to visit new places, experience new things. For all intents and purposes, I WAS living in an alternate reality. It was therapeutic to some degree.

And they say EQ isn't a drug...BULL!

A little more background:

I originally started playing a human paladin named Kurry Ahkin. Had even composed an entire story about his past, and how he met his battle bride Kyrah (my wifes first character ). We both played these characters to lvl 15 and then decided we had made some mistakes during character creation by distributing the extra attribute points unwisely. We started reseaching other race/class combinations, and ultimately decided on the Barbarian Shaman. We understood that we would have to rough it during the early levels, but would be compensated at higher levels (starting around 19 or so). At least that was the impression we were given from all of the spell data we had accumulated/reviewed, as well as talking to other players. So...off we went to start ALL OVER AGAIN.

[time passes]

When I heard that "BoOoOoOoNGgG!" at level 16, I felt a sense of great accomplishment. EVERYTHING up to that point was merely getting back to where I was when I stopped playing Kurry. Reaching lvl 16 was PASSING that point and achieving a major goal. It felt good. Just before hitting 19 is when Verant made the first round of sweeping changes to our spells. It was disheartening, but we continued to play.

Unfortunately, can't do it this time. It's just not worth the stress and hassle. I give up and throw in the towel. Back to real life for a while I suppose. It'll do me some good.

Perhaps the last few paragraphs may help some of you understand exactly WHY I was so passionate about what I saw happening to the Shaman class. Maybe, maybe not. Either way, I want to express my gratitude to many of you for being my friend here and in game. Also for being a voice of reason in some of my earlier rantings. Many of you (Arkand, Nora, Mung, Max, and many others as well helped bring things into perspective for me on more than one occasion. You will all be missed. Even those who caused me to buy stock in Fruit of the Loom Asbestos Undies....it was fun and challenging debating with you, but it's time for me to depart.

Farewell all. Be well.

Valgoth has LEFT the building!


------------------
E.J. James, formerly
Valgoth SoulReaver. May
he rest in peace.
