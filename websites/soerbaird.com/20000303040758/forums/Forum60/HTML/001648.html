Yes, I agree that you should continue to pursue the matter. I get the feeling sometimes that because we are gamers, playing a game, we are not treated with the same respect as we would be treated if we were buying a pair of shoes, for instance.

Call back, try to speak with someone else. Try to be really friendly about it. As someone who has worked in customer service I can tell you this - customers who come in who bitch at me don't get as good service as those who come in with a smile and kind words and a legitimate problem. I really don't want to help the first type of person - I want to get rid of them. The second type of person I take a liking to, and really really want to help them, and I'll go out of my way to do so.

Explain to the customer service person that you are somewhat frustrated because you don't understand what the problem is. Tell them the facts without accusing them of sloppy service or whatever...the agent you talk to can't do anything about his or her coworkers or company screw ups =).

Put it this way - its not that Verant has some stupid policy that you are arguing about. Everyone in that company, I am SURE of it, would recognize that your problem needs to be fixed. Sounds like this is simply a matter of miscommunication, and you'll just need to be patient, but insistent, as they fix your problem.

Call them back. If a regular agent can not fix the problem, try talking to a supervisor again. If you get the same one as before, say to him, "Look, I was frustrated and didn't mean to be rude. I am sure we can fix this problem. What do you need to ensure that I can get up and running again?" I don't know any manager who wouldn't go out of his way to help someone who approached him in this manner.

If the fellow is still unhelpful and/or your problem is not fixed to your satisfaction, then ask to speak to a superior. Keep going up the chain of command until your problem is fixed. And I'd say if you have to go too far to get this problem fixed, I don't think it would be too bold to ask for some sort of compensation for all the hassle. Say, a free month? Hey, I wrote McDonalds corporation a letter once when I got horrid service at one of their restaurants and they sent me 5 McDonalds dollars. Maybe Verant will be good to you too.

I work hard at providing quality friendly service for people, but really, much of how good my service is depends on their attitude towards me. Just keep that in mind as you pursue the matter. I am sure they will sort it out for you if you are polite and firm.

Hope this helps. Let us know how it goes!
