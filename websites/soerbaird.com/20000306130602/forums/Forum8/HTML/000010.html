Yes, my Sister in Song, it breaks my heart too, that Verant will not allow us beautiful women of Norrath to show our joy of being a Bard by dancing as the men do.

I would never play an other class than a bard. Below is a repeat of what I said in The Concert Hall, but for those of you who did not read it, here is my thoughts on our class.

We are the grout that makes up the mosaic of a group. We fill in the cracks, even the group out.

If the group is all casters, we play the tank. If the group is all warriors and paladins, we equip our instruments and perform our three-song symphonies. If we are all alone in our adventuring, we can take care of ourselves. If we have a full, powerfull group, we can really kick ass! If we have a small group, we can play that wonderful Lullabye and let our friends pick off that train one, by one. Our primary role to play is to enhance, and balence our groups.

Also, aside from serious battle, we have the best lifestyle to play in all of Norrath. Bards are lovers, romancers, dreamers. You can gaze into someones eyes, sigh, and sing them a song, and they will remember your name. We do not have to wear the mismatched armor that other classes must to be as powerful as their purses will allow. We are expected to be as beautiful as the music that we make. Don't like that Christmas tree ornament look of Rubicite? Well, tell all of your friends that as a Bard, you have an immage to maintain, and wear that sexy Blackened Iron Tunic. You can play your Hymn and out-regen that Rubicite aura anyway.

Not everyone can play a Bard. Being a Bard is not for those who want to be heros; it's not for those who want power; it's not for those who want riches. Being a Bard is for those who love being alive and want to share that love with the Land.

May you never miss a note, true Bards of Norrath.

Well-said! And I especially agree with the part about being able to tailor your appearance.

A shaman buddy of mine looks like he rolled around in fruit salad, with all his WID-boost gear on.

Me, I wasn't really interested in the game until I heard there was a bard class. I don't think running around as a paladin saying "safe travels, good fellow" to everyone would be very entertaining. But as a bard, you get to be the silly jester. Write bad poems, shout innuendoes, poke fun of people, help people out when they need it without being OBLIGATED by your character class to do so, and mostly just be an entertainer.

I dumped ALL my character points into charisma, for no good reason other than that I wanted to be the prettiest, funniest, fruitiest bard in all of Norrath.

And I go to great lengths to make sure I'm dressed to a T. By my early 20's, I was decked out in "phase 1": blackened iron tunic, leather pants, blackened iron gloves, cloth cap, and a pair of whips. It's kind of an "S&M biker dude" look.

I refuse to wear any helmet that appears on my persona; why would I cover up my gorgeous hair? A stone circlet is AC 6 and doesn't show up as anything.

I've since upgraded to a Charred Guardian Breastplate (black platemail; gives humans a great six-pack stomach look) and blackened iron pants. My shopping list includes Azure sleeves (AC12 but invisible, for the complete bare-arm look) and Dark Mail Gauntlets.

Sure, I could find cheaper stuff with better stats. So what? This is half the fun of the game!

I've tried charming the greenest of green Dark Elf guards. I could NEVER get it to stick. And I charm orange-con stuff all the time. (high yellow / low red =).

So either it's dark-elf specific and I jumped to conclusions, or else it's a recent change... (shrug)

My advice for charming if you're having trouble:
- hit F9 for external view so you don't have to walk backwards. (this is also a great help in kiting)
- use a flute for it; makes the charm last much longer than a sung charm.
- get your charisma up there; that also affects charm effectiveness.

To Mayne/Bayne-- suggestions for alternate helms:
Blackened Iron Crown (looks like chainmail but darker; doesn't cover face like platemail, and has +5 str and +5 ALL resistances, from Runnyeye I think. ~180pp)
Stone Circlet (invisible, 6 ac, 2 wt, nothin fancy, from Najena dungeon. ~6pp)
Crown of the Froglok King (invisible, with incredible stats, from Lower Guk. price unknown, nobody ever sells them)
