OK, a friend and I were discussing this. Between us we have been masters of every trade skill in Norath save tinkering (another worthless trade skill that needs adjustment) and Alchemy. Here is basically what conclusion we came to:

Basically, potions are known for their effects on the drinker of the potion. So we don't have to worry about getting necro or wizard effects (and very little magician effects) into a bottle. I don't think that we have to worry about cleric's either as theirs is based on faith and the only religion in a bottle I've ever heard of was a reference to alcoholism. Druids I do not want to discuss in this message so I'm skipping them. That leaves Enchanters. This is where there is actually good methods already in place for this to happen. Enchanters can produce viles of mana. I would figure getting pieces from them and different herbs on our side it wouldn't be out of the realm of reason for us to put some enchanters effects in bottle form. I am thinking of potions of healing, and maybe mana restoration. These could be used in combat, but would be like fishing skill. You can attempt it in combat, but you are running the risk of losing your bait. In this case your bait would be the mana potion that you paid 20 plat for. I would like to see bottle illusion forms of some types. Considering most of the NPCs can see your religion just by looking at you, this isn't going to be much more than a novelty, but could solve some of the problems with people not being able to fit in some places. This wouldn't ruin the enchanters quests as for most of those they have to cast their raise faction spells to make them work.

I don't think that there should be a place where potions replace that class in a group. That means that potions will have to not be inexpensive, or the effects would be considered emergency use.

I think that a bind potion would be great if it allowed non-casters to bind anywhere. If it doesn't, it is worthless. A gate potion is a must and would be a big seller to non casters or the half-casters.

Basically this is a shaman only tradeskill. We are the ones that have to push the combine button. But, this doesn't mean that this skill can't be combined with items from other casters or other trade skills.

Muktuk Wolfsbreath
Fellowship Associate 		posted 12-12-1999 06:49            
In no particular order, my thinking on alchemy.

I don't think that potions should be stackable; if you want to be a travelling potion merchant, you can carry stacks of the components and make potions as you sell them. This will limit the possibility of warriors using backpacks as a substitute for spellcasting. If there's a possibility shaman could abuse this as well, perhaps make sitting a requirement for mixing potions as it is for applying poison.

Come to think of it, poison (despite people who call it useless) has some characteristics that are good for alchemy. There is a mixture of consumeable, bought components (vial sketches, suspension) and looted components (poison sacs, etc), corresponding to alchemy's herbs and looted components. This removes money from the economy, but doesn't allow someone who bought 10,000 plat on eBay to have an unlimited supply of poison/potions. Basic potions of limited usefulness (like that spirit of snake potion) might be made only with money, or with money and a simple component. The emergency use, kickass potions (Gate, if it could be made by shaman) would have much, much rarer looted components.

I'm afraid I don't know who suggested the idea of giving shaman some sort of Herb Lore skill which would allow them to forage for herbs. I love this idea! My version would have different herbs requiring different levels of Herb Lore mastery to find (thus putting a level limit on the potions you could make... hm, maybe that means foraged herbs would be no drop - they have to be fresh?). I'd also like some or all foraged herbs to be location specific: you could only forage Graveyard Moss in the Mistmoore zone, f'rinstance. Again, makes time by the shaman a requirement for some potions, not infinite cash. And I like the idea of having to range all over the face of the continent to find that one grotto where that one type of mushroom grows (ooh... seasonal foraging? =) )

Blah blah blah. What it comes down to is, I very much want alchemy to have a RANGE of difficulty and usefulness. I might like to pass out ultravision potions to my blind brethren, or bottle a couple of the buffs I can already cast for mana-saving purposes. These shouldn't be a big deal to make. I'd like to see other, more difficult potions: I can't cast damage shields, for instance, but sometimes a damage shield is *really* necessary; so this potion would take some time and effort to locate a component. And then there are the kickass, I'm-going-to-die-and-lose-my-corpse-in-Hate Gate potions which require a month of hunting and questing to make and are sold to someone for 1200 pp (worth every copper). I think that managing the rarity of component drops/foraging should allow for this type of range.
