What is kiting?

Well there isn't an official definition from verant or anything and you will probably get a different answer from most everyone you ask.


The most common definition is to use sow/jboots etc to make you run faster than the mob. What you then do is run out ahead of them and then use the distance to allow you to deal dmg to them before they get to you. This can be a dd spell, a dot spell, even a bow or throwing weapon anything that you can deal dmg with at range. Most commonly it seems to be done with dots. They are efficient but need time to work and are all or nothing resist (ie you successfully poison them they are going to take the full dmg over time). These types of tactic are commonly assosiated with druids and shaman since they can sow themselves and both have descent dots. Necros often do it too since they have a great variety of good dots but they need someone else to sow them, jboot or to use their darkness line of spells to slow the mob down but that is highly resisted since it is a dot too.

A more general definition would be establishing a speed differential between you and a mob and using that to deal dmg without taking any yourself. This definition includes slowing or compleatly stoping the mob in addition to speeding yourself up.

Verant has recently been trying things out on the test server to trying and control kiting. They claim to be going after the ability of some classes to kill a yellow or red mob with little or no risk by kiting and therefore lvling faster. They claim that kiting blues will be unaffected but that remains to be seen when they actually impliment something.

As far as when you can kite basically you can start kiting if you want as soon as you can have a speed differential between you and a mob and con deal dmg at range. Some mobs are slow and can be kited without any special spells. Most however will require that you can sow your self or slow them down. You get sow at 9 and could start kiting then. Most people don't kite at that point since our melee is still desent and our spell aren't that good yet. One us might be to soften up a mob before a fight by running it aroud poisoned for a little while before you finish it melee.
