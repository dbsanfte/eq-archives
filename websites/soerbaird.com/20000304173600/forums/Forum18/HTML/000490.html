Be careful when you go to get the the mammouth rib bone. It's too big to fit in any containers, so you have to have an open slot in your base inventory. Apparently it tries to drop on the ground as if you had a full inventory, but since it's a no-drop item, it gets destroyed by the game.

This has happened to me and another of my guild, watch it!

The comments I made haven't been posted here yet (so this message may appear before my previous one?).

Anyways if my comments do pop up I take back some of what I said about the level 19 troll shaman attempting to buy the looting rights to the rib bones.

I did manage to finally get the bone so here's what I learned:

I spent almost the entire day trying to get the rib bone (it became an obsession). Out of that entire time, as far as I could tell, only three rib bones were dropped by mammoths (someone also spotted the glacier bear early on and, I'm guessing, it was killed.)

If your doing this quest solo the lowest level I would reccomend would be 29 (I was 29 at the time but by the end of the day was 30). The mammoths you attack are blue at that level but hit hard and tend to do double attacks (talking 40-50 damage each attack). A lot of the battles I fought ended up with me very close to dieing but I managed to survive (even one where a second mammoth joined in mid-way through the battle).

There are three types of mammoths that spawn in the area around Permafrost. Mammoth Calfs, Wooly Mammoth Calfs, and Mammoths (the big guys).

The mammoths have very odd spawning times. Most of the time you won't find a single one around for 15-20 minutes. Most times you might find just one in the zone, maybe two if your lucky. Then, and I don't know when this is, there is a massive spawn of mammoths throughout the area. Previous posts have mentioned this phenomenon and I saw it as well. All of a sudden there were like 15 or so mammoths roaming around the area.

It was during this period someone killed one with a rib on it and offered to have looting rights. I offered to pay 20 plat to loot it (at this point I had been hunting 4-5 hours) and attempted to find the location where the group that killed it was. Since I didn't know the area (and have no skill using the /location function) it took me awhile to get there. Just as I could see where the party with the mammoth was a non-shaman ran by, looted the corpse, and ran off. (I think the other group that killed it was more upset than I was)

It wasn't till 3 hours later did I kill a mammoth that dropped the rib bone. Fighting almost the entire day I probably killed between 15-20 mammoths (figure I was there many hours so you can see how crummy the spawn for the mammoths is).

I highly reccomend offering to buy the looting rights for this paticular quest as early as you can. It's just silly that the drop rate for the rib is so rare (on a critter that is pretty rare too). This quest is not like the Bull Elephant one (that was a cake walk compared to this one).

What I found worked well on mammoths were to disease first, root, poison, then at this point you can either use walking sleep and go in and melee, or meditate and wait for the poison to do it's work and cast again when needed.

For the harder hitting mammoths you may find the later tactic helpful. In the few battles where I ended up with low mana and health I resorted to that tactic and won.

Most of the mammoths seem to spawn near the Permafrost entrance. I ended up killing most of the stuff around there to see if that would help spawn mammoths or not but really couldn't tell (it gave me something to do at least).

As the other posts have mentioned make sure to leave at least one slot open before looting the rib. The mammoth tusks can't fit into a regular backpack so you could fill up those slots easily. I ended up giving the extra tusks I had to wandering orcs (get a nice "Graa" response). The orcs will drop them as well when you kill them so people were finding them on the orcs to their surpise. I /ooced I was placing my extra mammoth tusks on various critters around Permafrost. I don't know if that increased people to kill the various wandering creatures around Permafrost (to get those mammoths to spawn) but it may be a tactic you might try.

Well that's it. Good luck to anyone who is mad enough to try to get the rib bone on their own without paying for looting rights.
