Okay, I've never seen any of this written down, so I'll do it.

Spirit of Cheetah is a great way to save yourself a lot of running time. But you need to know some tricks for using it...

SoC has a long recovery time. 8 minutes or thereabouts, though I've never timed it. It's self-only, and you run about 4x normal speed, 2xWolf. It lasts 48sec or so.

Now, yours truly hates beggin for teleports because he hates being begged for SoW. I've got only one TPer in the guild at the moment, so I hate putting him out for just me. That means I walk. A LOT. And, so, I learned how to use SoC to speed the journey.

#1 SoC can refresh immediately after you zone. Basically, cross the zone line and you'll find SoC ready to go. BUT this can fail IF SoC has been unready for a long period. Meaning: if you memorized SoC 4 minutes ago, it may not be immediately ready when you cross the zone; however, if SoC was memorized, or recently cast (2min) then it will likely (95%+) be ready on teh other side of the zone. Zoning includes gate, evac, or other wizzie/druid TP's.

So, if you memmed SoC too early, stop at the zone line, remove it from memory and re-memorize it. You'll save yourself a lot of time with that little trick.

#2 SoC is a great escape plan, so you want it ready in case an HG spies you.


For optimal use, you want one SoC/zone. No zone takes 8 minutes to cross, so SoC never refreshes, even in WKarana.

So, cast SoC just before you cross the zone! That means you are SoC'ed on the other side AND SoC is immediately recastable for escape OR the next zone line!

For a trip from Halas to Freeport, the zone list is:
Halas, Efrost, BB, QH, WK, NK, EK, HHP, KF, WC, EC, WF

Assuming SoC is memorized at the Halas/Efrost zone, you get 10 castings, saving you 8 minutes on the 45 minute trip. NOT a waste of mana or time.

Casting SoC before you zone also can get you out of some nasty situations quickly. Someone trains a Bouncer to a zone edge in Feerott (heh, I'm a Cazic junkie now, after gaining 26-31 in there), and you're gone before the second attack.

Anyway, I hope I save some of you a little time. Happy Cheetahing...

Casting cheetah right before the zone line is great, but i hate having to come to a dead stop for a couple seconds to recast sow each time. And you're also losing that sow/soc mana every single zone, forcing you to kill time medding eventually.

So i usually do a double cast of cheetah in every OTHER zone. SoC right before you zone, SoC again when it runs out, hopefully make it to the next zone... where you cast sow again. Then SoW through that zone till the edge, and repeat.

Travelling this way with SoC actually reminds me how cool jboots would be - you would never have to stop to cast SoW.

Actually a great way to have full mana and health at your destination is to use regen and canniblize too. Start your journey by casting regen on yourself, then cannibilize back the mana you spent. Fairly soon you have regenned back that health while simply running. When near full health again (and possibly stopped to recast a sow), hit cannibilize a few more times, and head off running again. Using this trick I can take off running from somewhere with only half my mana, and get there with full health and full mana. This sure beats arriving somewhere with essentially the same mana you left with.
