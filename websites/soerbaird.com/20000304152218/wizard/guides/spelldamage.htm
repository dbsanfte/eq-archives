Understanding Damage Done By Wizard Spells

There are two factors for wizard spells that are key in understanding which spells to buy/use for a given situation: area of damage and type of damage.

Where the damage happens

For all the wizard spells, there are three types of damage you can do:

Direct Damage

One target, one damage. Plain and simple, this is point and shoot wizardry. These spells tend to be the most efficient when fighting a single opponent, or when you want to cause the most damage the fastest.

Direct Damage spells have two methods of reaching their target: Shock and Bolt.

  • Shock: (Shock of Frost, Shock of Fire, etc) Shock spells damage whatever is targeted, regardless of how many obstacles are between you or the target, or which way you are facing.
  • Bolt: (Ice Bolt, Fire Bolt, etc): These spells hurl a missle at the target. If anything gets between the you and the target, the missile is blocked, doing no damage to the target. Bolt spells tend to do slightly more damage than Shock spells, and have longer range. However, they are best used in wide open spaces when you are absolutely sure you will have a clear shot at your target.

Radius Damage

The damage of these spells radiates out a short distance from the caster. If you're being attacked at close range by multiple targets, this spell is great for spreading out the damage. When fighting three or more targets, you'll often find that you'll do more cumulative damge with a Radius Damage spell than with a Direct Damage spell of the same level.

Rained Damage

These spells create an area of Damage Over Time (DOT) at the target--esentially a cloud of damage-causing flashy lights. Say you want to destroy three goblins at their tents. Well, with a Rained Damage spell, target the center one and boom all three take damage, ususlly in three pulses. At least until they run out from under the damage cloud. Then they run at you. Then you take damage. You can target yourself for a Rained Damage spell; however, be warned that YOU will take damage from your own Rained Damage spells.

Note: Radius and Rained damage spells often have a maximum number of targets. Some only affect 3 targets, while others affect an unlimited number of targets. Also, when cast indoors, there affect has a shorter span, usually one round instead of three.

Types of Damage

Not only do damage spells come in different shapes, they come in different flavors! Wizard damage spells come in several elemental forms:

Cold Attack. Ice and frost spells; these are obviously most effective against fire-based creatures, or creatures with a low cold resistance. Probably not a good idea to attac an Ice Boned skeleton solely with these spells, as they will resist them more frequently.

Fire Attack. Flame and fire spells; most effective against cold-based creatures. Fire spells work with decreased potency when it is raining.

Lightning attack. Lightning bolt, among others, does electrical damage to the target. Electrical spells work with increased potency when it is raining.
