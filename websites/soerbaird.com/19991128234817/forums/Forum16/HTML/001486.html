Modoc continues to preach that there is not a single "right" answer here. Our choices have increased! This is wonderful news.

Tis true that if you play a most-efficient-casting style shaman, yee are best off with +wis gear.

The only other obvious choice short of POF equip was to suit up in rubicite. This was the only option for those shamans that liked a bit of hack and slash to balance out their spell casting diet.

Now there is a third choice, or from Modoc's view the "high AC" option has been slightly improved. A diligent shaman may elect to wander about Norrath and collect up Totem armor which has the AC of rubicite, less (ahem) gaudy looks, and assorted dex/char/con/etc ability bonuses which should give it a slight advantage over plain rubicite.

Wonderful news. Casting shamans will experience a flood of modest +wis items on the market as some shamans move to Totem armor. Those (including modoc) who never dreamed of sitting and camping long enough to get Ruby can now imagine gathering a nice set of somewhat combat oriented Shaman armor.

The range of options for roleplaying a shaman has increased! Modoc suggest a joyous cheer around the campfire
