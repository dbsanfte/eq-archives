A while back, it was trivial for casters to kill the hermit (level 23) by staying out of his reach while he was "stuck" in his house. Doing so was considered a bug exploit and people got warnings from GM's for doing so. As long as you allow the poor stupid hermit to come out the non-opening door of his house to fight you (so he at least has a fighting chance), GM's don't care if you kill him.

In a fairly recent patch, all mobs were given teleport capability. This little piece of lazy programming makes it impossible to exploit the hermit, because if you don't lure him out his "door", he'll teleport right on top of you.

So go ahead and kill him without fear of GM warnings. The quest items are junk and yield a couple copper pieces and a random song (level 1-6), so you'll get better exp AND loot just by sitting there killing the hermit repeatedly. (3.5-minute spawn)

Not to mention he's a smelly tree-hugger who steals from bards, has a stash of Fine Steel weapons, and basically deserves to die many times.
