Get used to it. This happens alot, and can get embarrassing while hunting. Consider this scenario:

You are the puller for your group in South Karanas. You go out and grab a likely victim, then track back to your group. Only tracking leads you to a spot on the other side of the map.

or

You are in a group hunting in South Karanas. One of your group members runs off to sell/buy something to another pc, then gets lost. You tell them to stay still, and you will track to them and lead them back. You get lost as well as them.

This has happened a few times to me. It only seems to get me when tracking group members. Now if I have to track back to a group when I get turned around and lost, I track at least 3 group members as I'm running to make sure they all agree on which direction the group is.

Not surprisingly, but I have had similar problems as well. One thing that works for me, and I used this technique while hunting Thistle, is to have a party member stand still and make that your pull location. I.E. while in Lfay looking for said brownie, a druid and I were tracking while our paladin companion stood at the pixie tower serving as our tracking "anchor". Once I tagged Thistle, both myself and the druid tracked the pally bringing us both to him.

Note: I have noticed that in tracking a party member, you will be brought back to the location where you/they split off, i.e. if you're at the NK bridge in SK and members head off in different directions, you'll track back to the bridge. So, in using the aforementioned technique, get to your pull point first, then go hunting.

Hope this is of some help.
