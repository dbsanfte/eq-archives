I suspect you're not giving Verant enough credit, actually. The change to Kith was great . . . well, HALF great. The increase in danger was good. But as noted, too much. What should have happened was obvious to us all now, but at the time, they had no idea what would happen when they did this.

They figured it'd be more exciting to travel maybe, but mostly they just wanted a place for more of the ever-increasing 30+ folks to hunt. Well, unfortunately they forgot that the critters would be all over . . . sure Kith is a big zone. Sure there are only x undead. Unfortunately it's a bit dangerous even with only x undead . . . as they wander uncontrollably. *grin* Hard to keep a bad corpse down I suppose.

So Kith is not that dangerous. However, compared to how dangerous it was before (i.e. 300% safe) it's a deathtrap. People freak out, Verant goes "oh crap" and it gets blown out of proportion by people who think thay know how to make design decisions better than Brad and Co. *shrug* I can make changes to EQ, but then it wouldn't be EQ. You can say: "that was dumb" but it doesn't mean if things were up to you that you would not make a mistake someone else would find "boneheaded" . . .

If Kithicor despawns at day (even every two days) then Verant has a) listened, and b) made a change. I cannot count the number of companies out there that would not admit to a mistake by changing it. It's a good thing they listen. It's a better thing that they take the initiative to remedy a problem that they've got.

As much as you want to be unhappy, you're unhappy because you play the game . . . and if you don't want to play it, don't. But we're all here because we enjoy it, even with all the "evil" that Verant inflicts upon us. So instead of being up in arms about how Verant sucks for making the wrong decision (supposedly) why not try and be glad that they bother to make changes at all.
