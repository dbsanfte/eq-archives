i read a lot of the posts in the guild thread and many of the complaints regard twinking. As someone who was once vehemently opposed to twinking, i thought i'd drop a hook and see what other people think of this practice.

I have a non-twinked, primary character (lvl 28 shaman), a heavily twinked lvl 22 enchanter and a soon-to-be heavily twinked lvl 14 warrior.

I no longer despise twinking. at least not all aspects of it. i belong to a guild on prexus with my shaman and refuse items which are beyond my level. my feeling is that a primary character should be developed through patience, skill and some luck (be it good or bad).

However, on occasions i need a break from Aemon for various reasons and so i play one of my two alts.

I use my warrior to group with friends who have blown by me in levels and are now hunting in the planes. We created these characters so we could still group together and we only play if another is available. So i twink up with the best gear i can get and use Aemon to quest for stuff like crafted.

My enchanter is on Povar and I created it with the explicit purpose of getting to 50 as fast as possible. I originally started playing EQ on release weekend so i could group with my roommate, Ranolin. Unfortunately, fanatic devotion doesn't begin to describe his attitude towards the game, so he quickly out-paced me. So hopefully, in the near future, i'll get there and head in to pound on Innoruuk with him.

So what i want to know is: who out there has a twink and why, or why are you morally opposed to twinking?

Sore - there are many ways to twink. A trusted friend is a good one. You can also leave bags on the ground and relog, while praying no one picks it up in the meantime. My wife has an account, so I just log her in, port her wherever I need her with my wizard, and twink away.

At first, I was very very against twinking. That was before I got sick of my first character and wanted to start over. It is nice to be able to go for xp only, and not worry so much about loot. Everytime I start a new character, I twink stuff over. The lower levels are just monotonous, and I HATE camping for items. Why shouldn't I be able to reuse them on a new char?

Another reason I twink is for my wife's character. We only get to play one or two hours a day together, so why should I waste her time camping items? That's no fun. I give her any good equipment I can acquire with my high level chars when she is not playing.

Another way you can twink is to die on purpose. Leave what items you want on your corpse and then go drown yourself in town. Or attack a guard. Then type "/consent other_character" and relog with the newbie, and loot your first character's corpse.

I used to be against twinking, but I'm not anymore. It's just a game and if someone wants a 1st level warrior in full crafted, then whatever. It just them having fun.

However I still don't twink myself. I've got several characters and all are untwinked. Yes, I still enjoy killing spiderlings. Why? I play the game for the social aspects. It is fun to kill stuff in a group with others. It doesn't really matter what you are killing. The group dynamics are pretty much the same. Although finding players who really want to group at very low levels is sometimes tough.

For example, my necromancer is a riot. She's 5 level still, I think. Last time a played her, quietly killing skeletons with my pet (pets are cool), there where these two TOTAL SCRUBS just starting out in EQ. They were dying like every 5 minutes. I got tired of listening to "I keep dying, what is wrong?" and "Where's my corpse?" and "HElp!@!"

So I group with them and help them out. These two guys didn't even know to look at their health bars and rest when they were low. They were dying because they'd attack stuff when the were under half health! So I taught the the basics. Where it was safe to sit and rest. WHEN to rest! How to con stuff. How to find their way around. It was cool and fun.

I don't need to twink to do interact with others, so I don't twink.

My 2 cp.
