A Manual on The Skill of Fletching.

By Folletto L’Arciere <Arma Viresque>

Arrows - Beginner - Intermediate - Advanced - Pricing/Summary

Arrow Making

Beginning Arrow Making:

STEP 1: You start to train in fletching by first purchasing a fletching kit and putting at least one practice point into the skill of fletching at your guild. One hint that I have heard of is that if your a level 20 character and you put one practice point into fletching, you will be made a level 20 fletcher. If you a level 10 character and you put one point into fletching you will be a level 10 fletcher. Optional things you can buy are the books on fletching and bowery that the merchants have. I have never been completely sure if these are needed or not. So buy them at your own descretion.

And one other thing about using practice points, eventually you will reach a level 20 or 21 and the guildmaster will tell you that the remainder of your training must be done in the field, in other words, get out the fletching kit and fletch.

The new flecthing kit has 8 slots while the older kit had 4. They only weigh .4 so I use them instead of backpacks. BUT, be careful not to hit the combine button, as you will combine everything in the backpack and will lose it all.

STEP 2: Purchase 20 bundles of each of the 4 parts needed for making arrows from a vendor that sells arrow parts. Start with the cheapest: Field points, Wooden Shafts, Round Fletches and Large Nocks. These will cost about 5 to 6 copper for a bundle, and a bundle produces 5 arrows if you're successful. So your first attempts will cost 4 X 5 or 6 copper, equaling 20 to 24 copper for 5 arrows. The only thing that will cause your cost of materials to go up or down is your personal CHR value and possibly how you’re greeted by the vendor. I have discovered that any level of CHR over 105 is wasted as you get no cheaper price with higher CHR, although I only tried CHR 115 and saw no increase. If you discover that a higher level of CHR gets you a cheaper price, please let me know. Later when you make arrows and sell them back to the merchants a high level of CHR will also get you the best price from the vendor.

STEP 3: Now place one, and only one (Ed Note: You can select one part by holding down your CONTROL key when you click,) of each of the 4 parts into the fletching kit and press the combine button. As you fletch you will have two different things happen. The first is that you will either succeed and make 5 arrows or you will get a message saying that you lacked sufficient skill to make that item. If you get this message do not stop. Just keep trying. You will see this message a lot, but keep making arrows. The other thing that might happen is that you might receive an increase in your level at fletching. It may seem funny but you can fail at making a bundle of arrows and still get an increase in your level at fletching.

Each player has a certain level of INT and WIS. These values were determined at the time that you created your character. Each of these values affects your training in the following way. A higher WIS gives you a better chance of succeeding at making arrows instead of getting that message, "You lack the required skill….". A higher value of INT gives you a higher chance of getting an increase in your level at fletching. These attributes can be increased with equipment that has INT or WIS enhancements, like a Savants cap. Or, you can purchase INT and WIS increasing jewelery.

STEP 4: Continue making arrows with these cheapest parts until you see the message that these arrows are now TRIVIAL.

With these early arrows you can either use them or sell them to other archers. These early arrows will have a damage value of 1 and a range of 50. They are better than store bought arrows and can be sold for at least 1 silver per arrow, if not more. However, selling arrows can take a considerable amount of time and you may just prefer to use them to increase your archery level. If you are a class that cannot use a Bow, what the heck are you doing fletching. I know you can, but I ask, WHY?? A good archer is a better salesman of bows and arrows than a non-archer.
