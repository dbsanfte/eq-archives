Arrow Making

Intermediate Fletching:

Now that the Field Point, Wood Shaft, Round Fletch and Large Nock arrows are trivial, start making the same arrows but use the Medium Nocks instead of the Large Nocks. Throughout your fletching training, only one of the four components will be other than the 4 cheapest that you used for your first arrows. NEVER use TWO components that are more expensive than the first four that you used, while TRAINING.

From the Medium Nock you should proceed through the following list of items one at a time progressing to the next and the next with each message that the current arrow is trivial. The full list is as follows:

  1. Field Point, Wood Shaft, Round Fletch, Large Nock. These items make a 1 damage, 50 range arrow.
  2. Medium Nock gives the arrow +25 range
  3. Parabolic Fletch gives the arrow +50 range
  4. Small Nock gives the arrow +50 range
  5. Bone shaft gives the arrow +1 damage
  6. Shield Fletch gives the arrow -50 range and +1 damage
  7. Hooked Point gives the arrow +1 damage
  8. Wood VANE gives the arrow -25 range and +1 damage
  9. Ceramic Shaft gives the arrow +2 damage
  10. Bone Vane gives the arrow +25 range and +1 damage
  11. Ceramic Vane gives the arrow +2 damage
  12. Steel Shaft gives the arrow +3 damage
  13. Bladed Point gives the arrow +2 damage

While your making arrow number 11, you should reach level 200 in fletching and I know of no one that has gone beyond level 200, as that is the supposed skill level cap for fletching. There is no level cap based on what level your character is as with other skills like 1HS or 2HB. So, a level 1 character could fletch until he became a level 200 fletcher. Hard to do with out cash, but feasible.
