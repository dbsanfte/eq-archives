Hail one and all, and welcome to the Annals of the Soerbaird.

We, The Soerbaird, are dedicated as honored bards to preserve the history and persona of the land. Our calling is high and true, and our purpose is to make legends live in their valorous splendor, and not die as unknowns.

Through ballads, stories, and history, we will present to you the land of Norrath and its wonders. Through concerts and events, we will create a quality setting, worthy of the nobility of the realm. Through our work, we will record the oustanding feats and journeys of the inhabitants of our land.

We will produce, procure, and proclaim ballads of heroes.
We will record and recount history of the land.
We will tell to all who will hear.

We are the Wind of Faydark;
We are the Breath of Antonica;
We are the Song of Norrath.
We are The Soerbaird.

Divisions of The Soerbaird

The Soerbaird is divided into three branches:

  • The Academy - Bards-in-training who serve basic duties before they arise to the rank of Filidh.
  • The Filidh - The core of the membership who carry out the business of barding.
  • The Council - The guild leaders who administrate the guild and all its branches.

The Academy

The Academy is the training and proving grounds for any bard desiring to wear the title of Soerbaird. Bards must complete all the requirements of the Academy to graduate into the Filidh.

Students of the Academy are expected to act as full-Soerbaird in every way: they attend guild activities, sing around the land, compose works, and try to record the land's people as best as possible. Students do not wear the title of The Soebaird until they have graduated from the Academy.

As students complete requirements of the Academy, they inform the leadership, and once all requirements are completed, they notify the Ollave of the Academy Filidh, and their membership is taken before the Council for consideration.

Once the council has voted in the affirmative, the student is then inducted into the guild and granted the title of Soerbaird. At that time, the student is expected to declare to a branch of the Filidh.

 

The Filidh

The Filidh comprises the main body of the Soerbaird. Here, bards from across Antonica and Faydwer combine their talents and resources to spread the history and culture of the land to its peoples.

The Filidh has four branches:

Balladry

Bards who are a member of the Balladry Filidh compose ballads and songs. The Balladry Filidh is also responsible for organizing concerts around the land.

Responsibilities:

  • Write ballads
  • Maintain Iona Hall
  • Perform in concerts
  • Perform on request at events
  • Enlist bards to sing at scheduled concerts

History

The History Filidh is concerned with documenting the history of the lands and its events. They will seek to chronicle an accurate history of Norrath in a prose, rather than verse, format.

Responsibilities:

  • Write history (in the form of reports in Caim Hall)
  • Edit and verify submitted historical account
  • Maintiain Caim Hall
  • Research history for parts of Norrath

Heraldry

The Heraldry Filidh discovers and announces events around Norrath. More than just newsbearers, Soerbaird heralds will serve as diplomatic ambassadors to other guilds, and will handle sensitive cross-guild relations, ready to mediate disputes as needed.

Responsibilities:

  • Make details of concerts known in-game by shouting across zones (or organizing that kind of activity)
  • Create and maintain a list of guilds and guild leaders
  • Make social contact with all the guilds on the list
  • Maintain contact with guilds, and become a source of newsgathering from them
  • Report any news of upcoming events to the appropriate sources in Balladry and History

Academy

The Academy Filidh concerns itself primarily with new students--their admission, graduation, and integration into the Soerbaird.

Responsibilities:

  • Process applications to the Soerbaird Academy
  • Accept new students
  • Monitor new students completion of requirements
  • Submit students who have completed requirements to the council for graduation approval
  • Train new students in barding
  • Be sure students are equipped to carry out their duties as a bard
  • Keep students informed and integrated in guild affairs and events
  • Run the AoC (Academy of Combat) to train bards how to defend themselves, and fight effectively

The Council

The Soerbaird council oversees and administrates the guild and all its branches.

  • The Pencerdd. The Pencerdd acts as the head of The Soerbaird, presiding over the Council.
  • The Ollave. The Ollave are master bards who hold Council seats. Each Ollave also heads a branch of the Filidh.
  • The Poswardd. The Poswardd are the leaders of the Soerbaird on other districts (servers) of Norrath, or in other worlds. They are appointed by the Pencerdd and Ollave, and have access to the Council forum. While able to discuss guild issues, they do not have a vote on council matters; just a voice.

Associate Members

The Soerbaird allows bards affiliated with other guilds or groups to become an associate member of the guild. Associate members are expected to complete all the requirements of the Academy before they are considered a full associate.

Associate members are allowed access to the in-guild discussion and planning, given the opportunity to perform in Soerbaird concerts, and (if part of the History Filidh) are given the ability to scribe history to Caim Hall.

Associate members (obviously) do not wear the title of Soerbaird next to their name in Norrath. Associates also may never serve on the Council.

 

Non-bards affiliated with the Soerbaird

The Soerbaird is a guild for dedicated bards, who have chosen barding as their occupation in Norrath. While this may seem strict, it is what we have chosen as a standard of quality for what we seek to accomplish in the land.

We do, however, welcome others as friends, and love to hear song and verse from those who have chosen other professions--even allowing good friends to perform in a Soerbaird concert now and then. Feel free to drop by our Iona Hall and share your songs and tales with us, or find us in one of the many taverns in Norrath.
