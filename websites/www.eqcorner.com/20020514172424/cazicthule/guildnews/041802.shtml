Cazic-Thule Guild News

April 12-April 18, 2002
Compiled by Kwill on 04/18/02

Note: unless news is emailed to me, I check guild websites on cazicthule.com for news, and post recent news I find there.

If you would like to have news of what your guild is doing around Norrath posted here, please email kwill@planeteverquest.com. And, if for some bizarre reason you would not like your guild featured in this report, let me know that too!

News from Guilds Around Cazic Thule:

General News:

Nightbladers and Schism has been added to the guild listing this week. Order of Oblivion has a new guild leader, and they have moved their website to another location. Their link has been updated.

Juggs and bard Trak
Posted by Ztalten On Apr 14 2002
Started off the evening stopping by Oasis to take down the big giant Cazel to get Licifed his spoon for enchanter epic. Tried to hit the swamp of no hope to get Devraider's frog for epic, but unfortunately it was killed right as we all zoned in... we'll get him next time bud. We heard that Trakanon was due to spawn sometime that evening, so we all headed to sebilis to get some spells from Juggs while we waited for him to spawn. Turned out to be an incredible supply of spells - almost 100 of them. Unfortunately real Trakanon didn't spawn for us, but the Undead Bard did and after spending a bit of time getting Corus ready to do the trigger we moved to the lair and took him down with a little help from Project Eon. BIG HUGE GRATS to Corus on finishing BARD EPIC. You've been working on that for quite some time now and it makes us all very happy to see that it's finally finished. After finishing in sebilis meff noticed that the scout was up and untriggered so a few of us went and took down the captain to get him a Talisman of Benevolence (7ac 15wis 55mana 38hp 12mr 12cr)

Grats to the following people on the spells they recieved: Gabbin on Wake of Karana and Girdle of Karana; Lowkii on Quivering Veil of Xarn; Thooven on Words of Restoration; Bahgoon on Words of Restoration, Mythika on Entrapping Roots; Licifed on Umbra and Forelorn Deeds; Kendera on Bedlam and Torment of Agli; Meff on Upheaval; Draconal on Lagarn's Lamentation; Agilent on Visions of Grandure

We also picked up a Blood Ember Helm for Samn (21ac effect: deadeye), a Hardened Clay Bracer for the bank (please post if any MAINS would be interested in this bracer: 5ac 5str 5sta 5agi 5dex 1wt), a Talopmuj Robe for Draconal (14ac 5str 5wis 10int 50mana effect: haste), and a Manarobe for Ztalten. Grats to everyone on a very nice raid with some good drops for everyone.

ToV
Posted by Ztalten On Apr 12 2002
CoG plowed through the Halls of Testing in Temple of Veeshan this evening with a really nice turnout of 45 people at one point. Grats to the following on the nice drops:

Katalia on chain helm, Blasko on chain boots, Agilent on silk bracer, Licifed on silk boots, Bromm on plate gaunts, Bregga on plate arms, Mancinia on plate bracer, Bahgoon on plate bracer, Naezariit on plate boots, and Liinc on leather legs!

After zoning out of ToV a few of us decided it would be fun to kill Tantor the big elephant... Since we were rather unsatisfied from getting nothing off his corpse but coin, we decided to give Esorpa of the Ring a try. After a little bug where she fell off the world and then gated on us... we managed to kill her with a little help from a charmed mamoth with just the 10 of us. No spells this time, but we got a book for the Wreath of Nature (5ac 10int 7cha 20mr 40mana) to be MQ'd for a Tunare following Enchanter.

APR 16, 2002

APR 12-14, 2002

Monday, April 15th, 2002
My Advice For Those Who Die, Declare The Pennies On Your Eyes...
They said we couldn't do it. Thund was outa town, the assling cannon was not firing all barrels, and the AoW was up. The word was out that there was just no way we could handle it. It caused us great concern. There was a firestorm of strategy posts flying across our web board today to determine what we could do with weapon #1 on the fritz. Ta tell you honestly, I was afraid to log in.

I don't think I was alone in this regard. As soon as I logged in, I was deluged with "Please, hold me!" /tells from Chadd....way more than usual. What were we gonna do? Thank heavens for our newest initiate Herugrim. Dorf stepped up to the plate and swung for the fences, strategy wise. He came up with our newest ELITE strategy when he said "Why don't we just try NOT dying?".

Long story short, his plan worked like GANGBUSTERS! Sure, we had like 4 people who screwed up and couldn't follow the plan, but every day is sort of a work in progress, right? So here is the madatory pic we get to post for beasting this bad boy....Hats off to the MT and healer core of SS & DA, the finest folks around!

Thursday the 11th, 2002
When first you don’t succeed… throw a gnome…
Using our new army of assling rangers, we were able to spot the Burrower Parasite up for once since his loot had been upgraded. Don't get me wrong, the loot is fantastic, but the fight itself is outstanding requiring everyone to be a well-oiled killing machine.

Our first attempt, we breezed through the waves of wurms with long breaks between each. We were rocking along on Mr. Hanky the Xmas Wurm but with about 40% left we lost a few tanks to pain and suffering and too many others got chewed up in the ensuing chaos. We pushed it down to 20% but that was as far as we could get so we pulled out for regrouping.

The next attempt was beautful. We decided that the heal bug was not going to be an excuse on why we failed so we adjusted our strat a bit. We lost a few here and there on lag enrages during the wurm waves, but when it came to Mr. Hanky we smoked him carrying most of the fight on non-disced warriors. Loot was two rings, a cap and a ranged item. Not bad, but where's the fricking tunics!

We haven't been doing much else lately as the spring weather has taken a toll on numbers. We have managed to kill a few odds and ends and had a great fight on the AoW. Considering the lag and this cursed heal bug, I think the below picture speaks for itself. Shame he got away with his loot.
