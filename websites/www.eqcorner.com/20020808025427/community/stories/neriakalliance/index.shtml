The Neriak Alliance
by Jack Holt

Chapter 1 - Emissaries

The forests of Faydark are relatively safe, but danger can spring suddenly from the quiet, damp, darkness. And so it did. Niffun Tuttle woke to a cry for help. He had been napping near what is commonly called the Orc Lift of Kelethin, and fortunately so. For a pair of the Orcs of Crushbone pursued there a wounded Ranger.

The Orcs didn't see the young Halfling as he had his kind's natural ability to hide almost without trying. He was also a Druid, so he matched the wilderness setting. The Elven Ranger didn't see him either. His hope was to reach the Wood Elf guard at the lift.

The Orcs, with their red eyes, extended fangs, and pale blue-green skin were beating the Ranger with their bare fists. That was enough. Orc's fists are clubs made for beating. Niffun could see that the Ranger was not going to make it.

Within his mind the runes of the Ancients stirred. He cast first one, then another healing spell on the Ranger, whose body was consequently surrounded by a faint blue glow.

Immediately one of the Orcs responded, pouncing on the Druid. The Ranger, with renewed strength, squared against the other.

Niffun was not in any real danger, but he was worried about the Ranger. He fended the Orc's blow and immediately called forth his most powerful option. Thunder sounded and lightning struck the Orc. Scarcely had the sound faded when the sky spoke again at his request, and the Orc fell dead.

The Ranger had no intention of running at this point. His pride had been hurt, and his life nearly lost. He traded upper body blows with the Orc, and then kicked him viciously.

The Orc was deterred not at all. For every wound he suffered he dealt two to the Ranger.

The Druid summoned, and hundreds, perhaps thousands of bees came seemingly from nowhere to swarm the Orc. Still, the Ranger was not safe. Niffun raised his Bloodforge Hammer with both hands and charged to his side. Blow by blow they beat the Orc until his resolve broke.

It turned to run, but the Druid was prepared and snared him with a spell. Now the Ranger was safe as he methodically moved to take revenge on the Orc's life.

"Well met, my Druid friend, and thank you for saving my life," he said after catching his breath.

"I'm sure Maesyn Trueshot would not approve of your being in this area at your age and experience," Niffun said.

"You know my master?"

"I do. In fact, I am here to see him, but I will not mention this." Niffun healed the relieved youngster, and then dismissed him with a simple command to "Run along." "Thank you, my lord," the Ranger said, bowing. Then he moved on toward the lift.

Niffun considered following, but decided it was still too early. Besides, he had used a good portion of his source of magical power, his mana. Only through quiet meditation could he build it back up again.

Beyond that, there was much to meditate about. Foremost on his mind was the cause for his return to the Wood Elf City of Kelethin. He pulled the message from his pocket and read it again. "Urgent you come at once. Maesyn Trueshot." There was no explanation, or polite beginnings like, "How are you. Or, I am fine." There was no news about the Elves that populated the city in the trees, or the men and Dwarves who often visited it. And most uncharacteristically of Trueshot, no stories about recent feats performed by his Rangers.

The command itself was insulting and flattering at the same time. Niffun knew Trueshot, but they were not particularly close or friendly. Trueshot was not his master. Niffun's allegiance was first to the master of the Druids, and second to the band known as Lucid Chimera. On the other hand, this was Maesyn Trueshot, head of the Rangers of Kelethin, a member of the Elder's Council, and ambassador to Felwithe the City of the High Elves.

No matter-the urgency itself was compelling.

Niffun had been far away when he received the message. In fact, he had been on the continent of Kunark campaigning against evil creatures known as Sarnaks. For most, the journey back would have involved several days travel over land and sea, but as a Druid Niffun had transport spells that allowed him to make the trip overnight.

A subtle, gradual change in the shade of the forest's darkness indicated to Niffun that a new day was beginning. He broke camp and followed the path the young Ranger had taken toward the lift.


Dragoon Dvinn, a Dark Elf of the city Neriak, stood stiffly at attention in front of a tribunal's bench. The three judges had already found him guilty, but instead of sentencing him they had instructed him to remain and then filed out of the room. Strangely, the guards who had escorted him into the Judgment Hall had followed the judges leaving him alone.

In the silence of the hall he pondered his crime--failure to successfully complete an impossible mission. The whole matter had been strange from the beginning. First, he had suddenly been reassigned from commanding a cohort to leading a small special missions unit. Then he had been instructed to attack and destroy a Halfling village in Nektulos Forest. Halflings were pitifully weak creatures, but twelve could not take out so many. His objections were dismissed, and he was curtly told to do his duty. He attacked the village, and even managed to set it on fire, but predictably the majority of the Halflings rallied and drove him and his unit away. Upon returning to camp he had been arrested and escorted back to Neriak for trail.

In Neriak things went from strange to frightening. He was charged with dereliction of duty, treason, and desertion. Witnesses he had never seen before testified he had failed to plan the raid, or press the Halflings in battle, and that in the end he had ran away leaving his unit behind to die. He wanted desperately to raise objections, but as a defendant in the court of the Dark Elves he was not allowed to speak.

Someone had staged this--probably some jealous officer in the army who had the political connections to pull it off. He cursed himself for his lack of political allies. He had always thought he could avoid the political mire and just be a soldier. Now he could see that he had been a fool.

The door behind him slowly opened. The hairs on the back of his neck stood up and a lump rose from deep within and shot to his throat. He closed his eyes and fought desperately to control raw, unfamiliar emotions while maintaining his military bearing. He knew the common fear of battle and loved it, but this was deep, dark and dreadful.

"You may turn."

Dvinn executed a perfect military about face. When he saw the black robed figure he immediately dropped to one knee and bowed his head. The robe bore the symbol of the Imperial Senate.

"I am Senator Exnir. Your choice is simple. Serve me, or die. Decide now!"

Dvinn's mind raced. He had heard of Exnir, but he did not know his role in the Empire. He cursed himself again for his political ignorance.

"Delay is not an option Dvinn. Speak now or I shall turn you over to the Shadow Men for their cruel rituals."

Dvinn hesitated no longerl. "I am your servant my lord."

"Good. You are hereby promoted to the status of Ambassador. Your first post will be to represent the Empire to the Crushbone Orcs."

"Ambassador? Orcs!" Dvinn shouted. "They are a senseless mob. They can barely speak. They need someone to herd them, not negotiate with them. They eat their own dead, and kill their wounded. They have no honor, no military bearing. They… They…" He shook his head in exasperation.

The Senator, ignoring the outburst, took advantage of the hesitation in Dvinn's objections and continued his instructions. "We have formed an alliance with them for the purpose of destroying the Wood Elf City of Kelethin. The plans are complete. You will relay the final information concerning the timing of the attack to King Crush. Once our troops arrive you will assume ultimate responsibility for the assault on the City from Crushbone."

"Senator, like any other loyal member of the Empire I want to see Kelethin fall, but the Orcs of Crushbone are no match for the Elves of Kelethin. Besides, the High Elves in Felwithe, the Dwarves in Kaladim, and maybe even the Gnomes in Ak'Anon would come out of their grubby holes and all you would accomplish would be the destruction of the Orcs."

Dvinn shook his head and searched it for additional objections, and then it hit him. "Wait a minute. What did you say about our troops? Empire troops in Faydark? How?"

"That, Ambassador, is my problem." He turned and shouted, "Guards!" Then, turning back to a bewildered Dvinn, he said, "You will see no one but these guards until your departure for Crushbone. You will speak of this to no one except King Crush of the Crushbone Orcs. Accomplish this mission, and you will be great. Perhaps you will be greater than I am. Fail, and you will die."


Welcome little Niffun," Maesyn Trueshot said with a wink.

Niffun grimaced, but bowed politely. He then moved past the door into a well-lit room that was completely empty except for the Ranger Guild Master.

"Still sensitive after all these years? You must learn to accept yourself for who and what you are."

"Perhaps, Master Trueshot, Elves should learn to curb their inclination to poke fun at others."

"Alas, we cannot change what we are, and would not try to. But you, you are trying very hard from what I hear. They tell me you always pick the largest, most dangerous foe in battle, and that at night you leave the company and hunt alone in the dark. Niffun, recklessness in battle will not add an inch to your stature."

"Is this the urgent matter you called me home to discuss?"

"No. No it is not. But hear me Niffun Tuttle. Others depend on you. Recklessness will lead you to an early end if you do not find peace with yourself. Acceptance--that is the way of life. Bitterness only brings sorrow."

"Yes my Lord," Niffun said. "Now, what is so urgent?"

Trueshot reached inside a pouch and pulled out an oblong object wrapped in a cloth. "Do you know what this is?"

He sat it on the floor and unfolded it. It was a dagger, or more properly a dirk. Niffun picked it up to examine the symbols on the handle and the blade. They were Teir'Dal. "This is a Dark Elf Dragoon dirk. It is the symbol of the rank, and it has magical properties. It is only lawfully used in two circumstances--as a last resort, or when challenged for rank."

"Challenged for rank?"

"Yes. Lower ranks can challenge a Dragoon for his rank. He can only use his dirk in the death match, and if he is defeated the challenger takes the dirk and becomes a Dragoon."

"You have studied your lore well Niffun."

"Thank you Master Trueshot. How did you come to possess this?"

Instead of answering, the Ranger pulled a scroll out of the same pouch the dagger had been in. He unrolled it on the floor and allowed Niffun to study it.

After a brief examination he said, "Lesser Faydark."

"Right again, Niffun. As you know, Greater Faydark is, relatively speaking, a safe forest. Lesser Faydark is exactly the opposite. Orcs, Giant Spiders, Shadow Men, and even Brownies are thick there. Even so, careful travelers could make it through to Steamfont, or other destinations."

"Could?" Niffun queried. "I take it that they cannot any longer."

Trueshot nodded his head solemnly. "I set up a Ranger station here," he pointed to the center of the forest, "and sent in a small company to try and find out what was going on. Except for the fact that the Orcs have tripled in number, things appear normal everywhere except in the eastern portion of the forest." The Ranger drove the dirk into that portion of the map pinning it to the floor.

"A well armed group can still make it through the northeastern section, but the Orcs are thick and much more aggressive than normal. On the other hand, every individual, team, or group that I have sent into the southeastern area has failed to return except one. He made it back, but died before he could tell us what he found. The only clue was the dirk. It was in his back."

"You want me to check it out," Niffun concluded.

"Yes. You have skills and spells my Rangers do not have. You can shape change and blend in. You're an expert at working alone, and you know Teir'Dal lore better than anyone in Kelethin. They are probably just bandits. The Empire wouldn't dare set up an operation this close to us. I need to know how many of them are there, where they are located, and how we can eradicate them."

Niffun studied the map for a long time in silence. He was familiar with Lesser Faydark, but the map indicated the presence of several new Orc camps, and he wanted to be able to locate the new Ranger Station just in case he needed it. Once these were in his memory he said, "I can be on the edge of Lesser Faydark by evening. Give me three days to thoroughly scout the area, and another to return."

"Good," Maesyn Trueshot said, nodding his head.
