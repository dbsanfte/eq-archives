Story of Ro
by Gromak

Forward to story: Upon behalf of Gromak, son of Gruumsh, I am sending you the story that he asked to be retold.  He insisted that our methods of "writing" do no justice to the tale as such things have had an oral tradition and are passed on thus.  Therefore I am to warn you that the tale is lacking in emotions and drama, as was retold by Marda, and then him.    I can only retell the tale here, in writing, and hope that my clumsy skills can do justice to Ro's tale.  I thank you for your patience and time.

"Do you know why the sand giants look so desperate, Gromak?" Marda asked me.

I had just finished retelling my journeys to the Desert of Ro and the strange and dangerous creatures that I had encountered. The sand giants possibly being one of the more lethal experiences, though with some regard did I recount these beings. I did question Marda about their lost countenance as they always seemed to be pursuing something lost in the sand.

"As I have instructed you before, the giants were once of our kin. But because of the Great War, the Gods sundered any connection they once held with us."

I nodded with familiarity.

"During the Age of Elders, the giants were quite possibly one of the greatest races-next to ours of course. Their civilization flourished and they were in possession of a beautiful culture. The only trait they lacked was the one of conquest. A terrible tragedy as their might could have aided us.

"A fatal flaw in their race as the aftermath of the Great War sought to punish all of Ralios Zek’s creations. The giants, being one of them. Scattered throughout Norrath, they no longer retain any culture nor unity and with some cases, have been cursed to pursue the opposite. The sand giants are one such example. Listen to the tale of Ro, Gromak."

The sand giants have always lived in the Desert of Ro, but the Desert has not always been the barren land it is. During that time, the Desert of Ro was called the Lands of Kosar, named after the first giant that built their city within. The Lands of Kosar resembled ours in its lush forests and life. A land quite the antithesis of what it is now.

With the ending of the Great War, the Gods met out all punishment. Ro, was one of the giants that lived in Kosar. She quickly foresaw what would happen to her people and at the least sought to save those in Kosar

She stood up to the Gods and asked that they be spared. All of Kosar heard.

No answer was given.

She shouted out that their race was of no fault. All of Antonica heard.

No answer was given.

She cried that this justice was unfit and unfair. All of Norran heard.

No answer was given.

Finally with disgust and weeping, Ro turned away from the Gods and muttered that the Gods lacked intelligence.

With this, one god answered. Bristlebane Trickster. Or as the others call him, Bristlebane Fizzlethorpe, King of Thieves.

The trickster took some personal offense to being accused of lack of wit and he approached Ro and made this challenge to her.

"Ro, your race like all of Ralios Zek’s will be conquered. But I will bequeath to you the possibility of redemption. Solve the enigma I present to you and the Lands of Kosar will be spared."

Ro immediately agreed not knowing that the Trickster sought only that, to trick her. For the Gods are petty.

"Ro, I present to you this gem. It is of great value and comes from my personal cache. Somewhere within your Kingdom of Kosar, shall I hide this gem and you are to discover its whereabouts."

With this, the Trickster whistled off like a zephyr. Ro then embarked upon her quest.

She searched high and low when she soon discovered that the deep forests made her search difficult.

"Oh, but the plentiful trees make my search so hard."

With this, the Trickster disguised as Termite, came to Ro.

"Ro, I have heard your pleas and I can help you."

"How can you aid me, Termite?"

"I can eat away all the trees so that they will not hinder your search."

With this, Ro agreed and Termite proceeded to eat away all the trees. Ro then continued with her search.

After a while, Ro came across all the rivers and lakes and found herself wading through their waters.

"Oh, but the deep waters make my search so hard."

With this, the Trickster disguised as Beaver, came to Ro.

"Ro, I have heard your pleas and I can help you."

"How can you aid me, Beaver?"

"I can dam the rivers up so that their waters never reach here."

With this, Ro agreed and Beaver dammed all the rivers so that they stopped flowing into Kosar, and all the lakes dried up. Ro then continued with her search.

After a while, night came and Ro found it difficult to search in the dark.

"Oh, but the dark makes my search so hard."

With this, the Trickster disguised as Eagle, came to Ro.

"Ro, I have heard your pleas and I can help you."

"How can you help me, Eagle?"

"I can chase the clouds away so that the sun will always shine down upon here, even at night."

With this, Ro agreed and Eagle flew up, and flapped his mighty wings and the clouds blew away. Without clouds, the Lands of Kosar always felt the presence of the sun. Ro then continued with her search.

After a while, Ro found nothing and looked up and around and discovered what she had done to her beautiful lands. Without the forests, the rivers and lakes, and the clouds, Kosar had turned into a desert.

Ro then cried out that Bristlebane had tricked her, in which he responded,

"Ro, I have only lead you to your fate-something that cannot be changed. But I will uphold my part of the deal. If you find my gem, then restore the Lands of Kosar I will."

"And since then, Ro’s people have wandered and searched everyday, seeking Ro’s Lost Gem."
