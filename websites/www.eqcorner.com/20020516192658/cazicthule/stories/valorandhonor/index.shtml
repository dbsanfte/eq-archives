Of Valor and Honor, Part I
by Mythrilmire

The sun was once again giving way to the darkness that fell upon Norrath, providing the denizen’s of the night the cloak they needed to wreak havoc upon unwary travelers that had the misfortune of crossing their path.

Mythrilmire was but an intermediate wizard and was seeking the first sign of refuge so as not to fall to a creature of the night. The past few months had earned him a few new spells, which provided him with the means to dispatch lesser creatures, but without any warriors he could not chance an encounter at night in this unfamiliar territory. Luckily he had obtained plenty of provisions from his hometown of Felwithe before he started his journey to Kaladim. Believing that he had underestimated the travel time, he decided to take cover in an inn that he came upon.

During his stay at the Inn, he inquired from other adventurers he met as to the distance to Kaladim and what beasts inhabited these parts. One of the beings he met here was a dark elf, which darted off without so much as an acknowledgement of his presence. Another seemed not interested in conversing with a lowly wizard and he was about to give up to retire for the night when he met a Knight by the name of Sestin. It just so happened that this knight was from the Knights of the Round Table and he immediately knew he would find the answers he sought.

Growing up in Felwithe, Mythrilmire was sheltered from the outside world by his parents and was taught the ways of a wizard and to seek the path of enlightenment and order. In his teachings he learned of the Knights of the Round Table and how they fought the forces of evil in Norrath. They were one of just a handful of groups that could be trusted.

Sestin seemed happy to oblige him and informed Mythrilmire that the roads to Kaladim were full of dangers, particularly the Griffins and was a risky journey for a lone wizard of his stature. "Where do your journeys take you to, Mythrilmire", asked Sestin. Mythrilmire explained that his father had instructed him to journey to Freeport after his death to seek out Allangazar who would complete his training and look out for his best interests. "You’re in luck, for I too journey to Freeport", explained Sestin. He was going to meet up with the rest of his guild to partake on a quest and would be honored to provide an escort for the humble wizard.

Sunlight pierced through the window of the inn, waking Mythrilmire to a new day of adventure, but as he looked over to the bed where Sestin had settled in, he noticed it empty. Thinking he had been fooled, he prepared to make the journey alone by preparing a few spells and left the inn. "Gonna sleep all day, wizard?" he heard as he stepped outside. There was Sestin conversing with some other warriors apparently passing the time until the wizard arose from his deep slumber.

They had not been on the road for more than an hour or so, when Sestin grabbed the wizard by the arm and pushed him back behind a tree. "Griffin!" Sestin murmured, "We don’t want any part of that, take a good look and do not forget it." After a few minutes had passed there was no sign of the Griffin so they continued down the path, past another inn and again back to more uninhabited forest. Up ahead a squatty figure came from out of nowhere and stood in front of them, seemingly blocking their progress.

It turned out that this was just an Orc pawn and Mythrilmire had easily dealt with stray pawns near Felwithe. Mythrilmire, eager to display his latest arsenal of spells, rushed ahead and began chanting magical words not understood by Sestin. Bolts of flame shot out of his hands and struck the Orc pawn, putting him down with a single blow. As Mythrilmire turned to gloat to Sestin, he was astonished to see the Knight lunging in his direction sword drawn and a bit of vengeance in his eye. The valiant knights’ sword buried itself deep inside of the belly of an Orc Centurion that was coming to the aid of the pawn and was almost upon Mythrilmire.

When Mythrilmire finally realized what had happened, he had to sit down and meditate to clear the fear from his veins, before he could thank Sestin for saving his life. Sestin explained to him that in these areas as well as others in Norrath, you must be aware no matter how small thy foe. Most creatures with even the smallest amount of intellect, including orcs rarely travel alone and often will use a weaker member to draw you to a more elite member or an entire clan.

After scouring the bodies for any loot, they continued their journey, which was mostly uneventful except for a spiderling or two. Once they arrived at the Kaladim port, they restocked their supplies, had a few drinks and did a little fishing while they awaited the ship to take them to Freeport.
