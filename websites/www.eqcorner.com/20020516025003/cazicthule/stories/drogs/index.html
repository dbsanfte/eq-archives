Drog's Demise
by Drog Trollsbane

The rain was coming down harder than normal. In the distance the low rumble of thunder and the flash of lighting changed the sky into a opera of gloom. Once again I sat outside the Inn staring blankly into the darkness.

"Blasted lamp!", I grumbled but of course it was through fault of my own and lack of funds that I decided to buy the cheap thing. Slowly I began to pull myself up as my wounds began to feel better. I saw a few fellow travelers seemingly dcking in and out of view between the flash of the lightning and I began to wonder if I should ask someone to join me in my travels. But then my warriors side grabbed my ego and ran away with.

"I'll need no help this day. I shall cleave a path of bone and blood the likes which has not been seen!", I hollered. A few of the passerbys looked at me oddly but I bade them no mind. I begun to strap my helmet under my chin like I had done on countless other journeys though this time a cold chill ran down my spine.Of course i payed this no mind as well.

Immediatly I began to set out for the undead ruins, a favorite hunting spot of mine, to swiftly deal my punishment. "blast!", I yell as I dive back to the safety of the Inn and more to the point the guard outside. "Griffon headed to the west along the road!", I yell to give warning to all the other travelers out this dreary night.I have seen first hand the deadly swiftness these beast have dealt death and I wish this fate not even on my most dreaded enemies.

After a short time I once again head out for the ruins keeping my eyes fixed to the sky in case the griffon had smelt my presence. Thankfully, it had apparantly found some other quarry elsewhere and has left this area none the worse for wear.

The rain has begun to stop and light has once again begun to crack the deadly blackness as I begin at a gallop toward my unsuspecting targets of my swords edge. I think to myself how i love to hear the sound of my blade as it breaks bone and cleaves skin and I let my mind wander for a second. Soon my mind is pulled back to me as the sound of a puma is heard from the hill. Its unmistakeable growl is deafening and satisfying both.

"Just as well", I mutter between breathes as the thought of bashing skeletons to bits has to take a backseat to new thought of my sword piercing the flesh of this wild beast. A few seconds later I am upon the beast before it can even turn to face me. What seems likes mere seconds later, although surely a bit longer than that, the massive cat has fallen before me and its blood coats my blade like a sheathe. I pause a minute just to admire my work when I heard the screams of terror coming from the ruins. Once again I am in a full gallop and headed in the directions of which I am sure to find adventurers being overwhelmed by undead. A grin begins to form on my face as I crest the hill as I begin to think of the skeletal hordes which shall taste justice.

But soon I find the bodies of three adventurers and another barely alive and slowly retreating from what seems to be a large gust of air! I stare blankly for a moment in disbelief of what i am seeing, arms form from the air and swat repeatedly at the doomed warrior. As he collapses I see the look of sorrow in his eyes which at the time I mistook as a emotion directed at his own demise but later find out that it was diect squarely on me as i began to charge this monstronsity.

The battle was swift and fierce and as my sword slid from my hand and my body fell heavily to the ground I could still only think of the look in the young warriors eyes.It is a look that sticks in my mind to this day. It was pity. Pity for a warrior with boundless courage and an ego to match.
