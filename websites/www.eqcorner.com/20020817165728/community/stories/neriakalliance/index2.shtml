The Neriak Alliance
by Jack Holt

Chapter 2 - The Mithril Ring

Kelethin destroyed!" Dvinn thought to himself. "And I will be one who does it." A brief smile crossed his face. "But Orcs," he sighed visibly and rolled his eyes. "They are not troops, they are chaotic mobs. Will there be Neriak troops as I've been promised? How many will there be?" He looked down at the Ambassador's robe and sash that had been given him. "Political allies... But I don't like being coerced and manipulated." And so his thoughts came in disjointed, rapid succession for several minutes.

They were interrupted by a knock at the door. He rose and opened it. A guard greeted him and instructed him to follow him. Another one fell in behind them. They led him to a large courtyard of stone where he was given a sealed scroll by a robed figure whose face was hid. "King Crush cannot read. Give this to the Orc Oracle, or the King's Emissary. One of them will read it to the king."

Dvinn took the scroll and bowed.

"Now, Ambassador, I'm going to blindfold you. Under no circumstances are you to remove it. Understood?"

"I understand," Dvinn said.

It was not a blindfold. It was a deep, black cloth bag. Dvinn listened carefully for a clue of what was going on around him. Several persons came into the courtyard. At least one was in chains. Two people came close to him. He heard the sound of cloth moving on either side of him, and sensed something large and round go over his head. Then he heard someone fall. The sound of chains told him it was the prisoner.

Dvinn was a soldier. He knew the smell of blood and the sound of death. He started to reach for the hood, but the voice that had spoken to him earlier reassured him. "You are in no danger Ambassador as long as you do not remove the blindfold."

Then the two near him began to chant. A spell, but what spell? Several long seconds ticked by, and then Dvinn's attention was drawn to a humming sound around his waist. The temperature dropped suddenly, and he could hear the sounds of a forest. Hands were on his shoulders, and a new voice reminded him not to remove the hood. They guided him on a short journey, and then finally allowed him to see his surroundings.

It was indeed a forest--a very dark one. It was also a military camp. On either side of him were several rows of uniformed Teir'Dal--Neriak Regulars. A Dragoon bowed before him. "Sir, I have an escort prepared to take you to Crushbone."

"Where am I?" Dvinn inquired.

"You are in Lesser Faydark, and these are the troops which will destroy the Elves of Faydark."

Dvinn surveyed what appeared to be two companies of Regulars. This just might not be an impossible mission after all.


Within minutes of entering Lesser Faydark Niffun had made contact with the enemy. He spent the first day dodging Orcs as he drove deeper into the forest. On the second day he saw his first Teir'Dal. Then he spent six days scouting instead of the three he had predicted during his conversation with Maesyn Trueshot.

The Dark Elves in Lesser Faydark were not bandits as Trueshot had predicted. The Empire had a small army in Faydark. The majority of them were Neriak Regulars. The rest were elite members of the Empire's darkest kind--Dragoons, Necromancers, Shadow Warriors, and Wizards. And a single addition was made to the force every four hours.

For a full day Niffun studied the ritual by which this was accomplished. It always began with the execution of a prisoner. After the execution, two Neriak Wizards would emerge from a tent in the middle of a small clearing with one on each side of a ring of mithril that was about five feet in diameter. Of course it was not ordinary mithril. It was some kind of magical teleportation device. After the wizards stood still and chanted for a few seconds the ring would hum and grow bright, and then someone would appear in the middle of the ring.

Niffun had also seen it work in reverse, presumably sending a messenger to Neriak. Where else could it be linked?

As interested in the device as he was, Niffun had another problem that was more pressing at the moment. It had to do with the prisoners that were there for use in the teleportation ritual. The Teir'Dal had several of them tied to trees at one end of the clearing. Most of them were Elves, but there were a couple of Gnomes, an Orc and a Dwarf as well.

At the moment, the Dwarf was tied up to Niffun! The Druid, who was in tree form, had moved behind the prisoners and shape changed with the intention of saving as many as he could once the opportunity presented itself. Before he could do so two Dark Elves appeared and bound the Dwarf to him.

Neither the Dwarf nor the Neriaks had any idea he was there, but that would have to change soon. Niffun had to get that ring, teleport out with as many prisoners as he could, and get back to Kelethin to warn the city of the danger.

Releasing the prisoners would be easy enough. A pair of guards appeared every fifteen minutes to check their bindings, and then continued to patrol the area. The interval provided just the window of opportunity he needed. However, once he changed shapes the prisoners would be able to see him. How could he keep them, especially the Orc, hidden and quiet? And just how would he retrieve the ring? Normally he could depend upon his natural Halfling abilities to sneak and hide, and his Druid camouflage spell. These worked well against Teir'Dal Regulars, but he didn't expect for the Wizards in the tent with the teleportation ring to be that easy.

 "Ouch! Don't kick me."

It's hard to say who was most astonished. The Dwarf was trying to get his feet free and in the effort had kicked backwards with his heels. The Druid had been deep in thought and had responded without thinking. A Dark Elf Regular had been walking nearby carrying firewood.

All three recovered from the surprise at the same time. Niffun released the shape spell in his mind, but the transformation back to his normal form would take a few seconds. Until that was completed the Dwarf was still tied up, so the Dark Elf had the edge.

He dropped his firewood, reached for his weapon, and sounded the alarm. "Treant! Treant!"

Apparently he had heard of the creatures, but never seen one or he would not have made the mistake. He swung at the "Treant," but it shrunk under his swing and the Dwarf took the blow. Now the "Treant" was a Halfling and the Teir'Dal was surprised again.

The Dwarf had been injured, but not critically so. He grabbed the soldier's weapon and butted him with his shoulder. The gawking Dark Elf was knocked backwards and his weapon came free in the Dwarf's hand. Niffun was himself again, and silenced the downed soldier by crushing his skull. He turned to face the Dwarf. He could hear the sound of running feet, but they were a few seconds away. The Dwarf embraced him, but Niffun pushed him away. "There is no time for that! Stand close to me!"

The prisoner's cried out as one for help, but there was not enough time to help them. Niffun closed his eyes and selected the proper rune in his mind--the Circle Teleport Spell for Butcherblok. The spell required certain words and hand movements, so he began them.

From the center of the clearing he heard two chants begin. The wizards were preparing to cast spells! He had a head start, but he was casting a long spell. What spells were they choosing? Would they be long or short?
