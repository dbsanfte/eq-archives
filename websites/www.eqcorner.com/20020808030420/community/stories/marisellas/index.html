Marisella's Fall of Fate
by Cassiopeia

Marisella was running around and happened to pass a hole in a wall she'd never seen and exlaimed, "Whoa. What is this? I thought I knew this whole area. Let's investigate".

As she entered the dark hole, a ominous feeling overwhelmed her but she decided to ignore it. It was a short corridor to an open area with plagued rats and skeletons a plenty. From what she could see, none of these creatures attacked her and she proceeded to investigate and looked around doors and crevaces. Nothing really interesting for the experienced druid except... There was this little area with what seemed to be a well.

Always the curious one, Marisella decided to take a look but it was a little hard to see what was down below and she leant over a little further. "Aaaahhhhh", she screamed as she lent too far and went head first over into the well.

A lot of bruises and a sprained leg later, she sat at the bottom of the well contemplating the foolishness of it all. As she got up and looked around, she saw several more skeletons and even a ghoul. They didn't attack so she was becoming more and more confident that this area she had never been in, was not a dangerous one. So when all her magic protection wore of, she didn't even bother to call on her gods for a new coat of armor.

Deeper and deeper she went, all the whilst not having a clue on how to get out of this place with all the twists and turns she had made. All of a sudden there was the sound of crackling fire. Marisella carefully looked around the corner and saw a dark elf casting spells on his skeleton pet.

"Oh, how I hate necro's, especially dark elves", Marisella muttered. "I wonder what he's doing.", she thought. Apparently the necromancer was protecting some area with his boney friend. Marisella appraised the situation and her hate got the better of her and she decided to attack the Necromancer. "Die, you worshipper of death! For Kelethin!"

SWOOOSH, a orange ball of fire combusted the dark one as his pet leaped forward to attack Marisella. "Hah, you're no match for me, you incompetent romancer of the undead!! Let me how you the power of the good and the living!", she shouted.

Another burst of fire hit the immitation elf square on the shoulder. That was apparently enough for him as he turned and ran away. "Haha", laughed Marisella, "Leaving your poor pet to fight your battles for you huh? How typical. No spine!

Well then, you boney excuse for a skeleton, let me help you to where you came from", said Marisella as she once more summoned up the powers to unleash a great ball of fire. The skeleton took it well and , unlike his master, fought a bitter fight.

Marisella, already bruised from her fall down the well and without magical protection actually got more hurt than the had anticipated. Once more, the monster was hit with the fire from above and finally it lay still. "Aaahhh", sighed Marisella. "I'm glad that is over. That puny skeleton had a little back bone after all", she quips.

Quite pleased with her prowess, the druid sat down to replenish her depleted stamina and especially her mana. But, she had forgotten about the Dark elf hurt but no doubt being healed elsewhere. Suddenly she felt a sharp pain on her arm. She broke out of her concentration and stared into the face of a fully healed,eager-to-go and very angry blue face. "Yahoooo", she yelped as she jumped up and was about ready to let loose another ball of fire as she became aware of several skeletons approaching.

Out of the corner of her left eye, she saw a ghoul was just about to join in the fight too and from the right, a mummy appeared. As mighty as she was, Marisella realised that this was fast becoming a perilous situation and decided to make a run for it.

"Oh for the speed of wolves", she thought remembering that the wolves spirits didn't have the power to penetrate the walls of the caverns she was in. Up and down she ran, frantically trying to look for something familiar. It seemed that there were now nearly 5 or 6 undead monsters after her and the necromancer had at last gathered his wits and started calling on his evil gods to hurt the poor druid even more than the undeads could. "Look, look", shouted Marisella. "A door! And another one. I'm going to make it". Then, as she tried to open the door, she realized with a sickening feeling, that she had no key. "Aaaah, it is locked!! Is there any one here that has a key to this door?" she shouted to no one in particular.

She turned around and found her way blocked by monsters now numbering at least a dozen. Bravely and valliantly, she fought the monsters and managed to better one, then another, a third. However there were too many. She dodged and twisted and turned trying to avoid the punches and the blows of the rusty swords of the mob ganging up on here while at the same time trying to send all of them to their eternal grave where they should've stayed in the first place.

Then, out of nowhere, someone called, "Here, are you in trouble? I have a key that'll unlock that door. I'm running as fast as I can but i'm a dwarf and only have short legs!" "Oooh yes please", cried Marisella desperately, "Please hurry because I can't hold them of much longer! Hurry, Hurry!"

"I'm nearly there", cheered the dwarf. Just then, a green cloud appeared from the hands of the evil necro and surrounded poor Marisella. She staggered and fell on her knees. Several blows hit her hard on the shoulder and another hit her right above the ear. Blackness engulfed her as the last thing she heard was the creaking sound of an opening door.
