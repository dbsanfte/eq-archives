Wheel of Fortune
by Wranglir of the Protectorate

Wranglir watched halfheartedly as the "wheel of fortune" spun round and round.

"No more bets! Shouted the dealer as the wheel slowed to a stop. "The lucky number is 12! No winners on 12!"

Wranglir shot the dealer a nasty look causing the man to take a step back away from the table. With a snicker, the ranger picked up his small pile of coins and walked to the flap and out of the gambling tent. He strolled rather aimlessly around the crowded stalls of the Nashkell Carnival. He was feeling restless and bored and just a bit tired of "civilization". The recent adventures around Baldur’s Gate left the ranger battered and mentally drained.

"Well…", mused the ranger "at least we got out alive. And it will be nice to travel north and see how things are in Neverwinter after all this time…"

Wranglir sat down with his back to a large oak tree and let his thoughts drift back to the last few years. And what a journey it had been. Whisked away from his beloved Neverwinter by godlike forces that did not understand, even to this day. Teleported into the Ethereal plane where he drifted, pulled away from his world against his will. Then, suddenly appearing again in a strange, new world. Athas it was called. A harsh desert world, ravaged by uncontrolled magic. This was his home for many months.

But this home was short lived, once again he found himself tossed into the void. His mind was filled with incomprehensible visions. Brief glimpses of a world that looked familiar yet foreign. Somewhere deep in his mind he could sense that perhaps it was his home that he saw. World after world he found himself in, only to be plucked away again by an irresistible force. The world of Britannia became a short-lived home. He found himself for a short time back on Faerun, tossed into the dungeon known only as the Undermountain. Later he was forced into battling against magicians of world crushing power in the world known as Shandalar. But as always, he was whisked from this world as well, and tossed back into the void and then deposited once again onto the lands known as Faerun, to do battle with but another evil, this one to be found in the Sword Coast city of Baldur’s Gate.

"What is it the Fates plan for me this time" Wranglir wondered from under the shade of the oak tree. "Am I to stay here on this world of my birth? Or am I once again to be…"

As if in answer to his thoughts, the world around him became suddenly gray and formless. The scenic carnival being replaced with the swirling near-nothingness of the Ethereal plane. This time though, he spent almost no time in this void before he felt the now familiar pull that told him he was entering yet another world. Images of vast plains, deep oceans, towering mountains, frozen tundra, all raced by him. Then he was falling, as if from a great height. He saw the land rushing up to meet him and then felt the hard slam and icy jolt as he was plunged into chilly water. Stunned and barely conscious, he swam the short distance to the shore and pulled himself from the water and passed out upon the land.

Upon awakening a short time later, he sat up to gather his thoughts. He found himself on the shore of a small pond, in the midst of tall trees, as tall as any he had ever seen. He crawled over to drink from the pond only to come to a startled stop as he glimpsed himself reflected in the water. It was his face that stared back at him incredulously. Yet was it?

"My ears!" Shouted the ranger," my eyes! I have the look of….an elf!"

"Aye, a stinkin woodelf!" came a shout from behind him. "You stinkin’ woodelves have ruined Faydark and all of Norrath! We, the orcs of Crushbone will destroy you all!"

Instinctively Wranglir reached for the sword, his momentary anxiety gone when he felt it’s familiar hilt.

"Norrath you call this world? Well let me teach you the manners that I bring from my world!" The ranger cried as he rushed to meet the charging orcs."

"Another new "home"" grunted the ranger, "and as always, the same "friendly" inhabitants to meet me…..!"

As with Wranglirs life to be continued…
