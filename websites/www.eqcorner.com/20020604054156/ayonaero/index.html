    Pet Changes Posted by Rich Waters
    6/3/02, 9:55 PST | Kwill

    In what appears to be a legitimate post, here are some interesting changes to pets as part of the caster balance project, posted by EQ Lead Designer Rich Waters on the Magician's Tower message boards:
    I wanted to take a minute to let you know about some pet upgrades that we've just completed. The changes are fairly significant, and we think they address a lot of concerns that have been brought up by players.

    Here are the changes we've made -

    Pets will not attack mesmerized creatures
    All pets now think of mesmerized creatures as being dead. Pets will not break mez, will not attack mesmerized creatures, and will forget any hate they had towards a mesmerized creature.

    In short, if a monster gets mesmerized, your pet will forget about it and stop attacking it. This is good for all pet classes, and especially handy for Enchanters, who can now trust their pets to not break every mez as soon as it lands.

    Pets are neutral to monsters
    Monsters will not attack your pet first. All monsters see your pet as a neutral creature, and will leave it alone unless your pet attacks something. This means if your pet wanders off, it won't bring a train of monsters back with it.

    Keep in mind, if your pet chases a fleeing monster, other monsters may still attack it. Your pet is only treated as neutral while it is not acting in a hostile manner.

    Pets no longer take experience in most situations
    In the past, pets would take a full share of experience if they did the most damage to a creature. We've changed this rule as follows -

    * Pets take ZERO experience from a creature, unless no player does damage to that creature.

    * Pets take 75% of the experience from a creature - if no player does damage to that creature.

    This means that your pet takes no experience from you or your group unless your pet kills a monster with no help from players. As long as you or your groupmates do damage to a monster, your pet will take ZERO experience.

    Due to the high potential power of dire charmed pets, they will continue to take a share of the experience. In the worst case, dire charmed pets will take a full share of experience - this is the same as it's always been.

    The amount of experience taken by a dire charmed pet scales based on how much damage the pet does. If the dire charmed pet is doing the majority of the damage, it's experience share gets larger. If you or your group are doing reasonable damage to a monster, a dire charmed pet will not take much experience. The more damage you do, the less experience a dire charmed pet will take. In most situations, dire charmed pets will take less experience than they did previously. As with all pets, if a dire charmed pet does the majority of damage, and no player does any damage, the dire charmed pet will take 75% of the experience from the kill. Overall, these changes are a significant boost to pet classes. The dev team has been hard at work on finding and implementing the right solutions, and we hope you'll enjoy these changes. Thanks for your patience as caster balancing continues, and thanks for playing.

    The Rumors of EQs Demise
    5/30/02, 20:30 PST | Kwill

    In what is probably a vain attempt to squash a rumor, Absor posted this to the Developer's Corner:
    I’m not really sure where this rumor started, but there is talk about town that we announced something called Exodus at E3 or that someone heard from someone’s brother’s roommate’s third cousin…

    The rumor is that we’ll be making one expansion after Planes of Power and then folding up shop.

    It’s not true. Not even a little bit true.

    Sure, we’re known for our interesting expansion names (and acronyms), but this isn’t one of them.

    We do not plan to stop working on EverQuest until you folks decide it’s time for us to stop. It’s your participation in the endeavor that will keep us going. For as long as you want to play our game, we’ll continue to do our best to entertain you.

    Somehow, I doubt this post will stop people from talking about EQ's demise, considering that many folks believe that EQ2 will ultimately crush "EQ Classic" as I like to call it now...but they keep insisting that it won't, so maybe the pessimists are just plain wrong and EQ and EQ2 will live happily side by side. We shall see!


    Second Account Free for 30 Days
    5/30/02, 20:25 PST | Kwill

    If you subscribe to the Harpy's Head Tavern email newsletter, you have probably already seen the email with your free account key for a second account. If not, here is the word from Sony:
    Sony Online Entertainment is currently in the process of sending out an email to its EverQuest subscriber base who have opted to receive special emailing from us. Within this email is an account key for EverQuest and an invitation to try EverQuest free for a 30 day trial.
    After a month, after you have leveled up a character you can't let go of *now*, you will have to pay the regular subscription fee, of course. But personally speaking, it's rather nice to have two accounts for various reasons--you can log on your cleric on the other account for that pesky corpse recovery without logging off your dead enchanter, for instance!
