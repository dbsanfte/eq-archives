EQIC Update Archives

3-18-2000
As you already know (by having to choose what version you wanted to see when you came to the site), we now have two versions of the site. The plain version will probably be the most preferred version for Netscape and AOL users, whereas "The Works"  version is most suitable for Internet Explorer users. Whichever version you prefer is fine with us, we just wanted to give you a choice. Be sure to bookmark the home page for each version so that you won't have to make the choice each time you visit. If you ever want to jump to the other version, you'll see that we've also provided links to both versions under the "Home" navigation menu. If you have any feedback you'd like to give us about the plain version, contact us at eqic@fullbyte.com or post your thoughts in the EQIC Community Forums.

3-12-2000
First the update...

We've added 3 new pages to the site, the first is Emotes which gives you a list of all the in-game emotes your character can do; second is the EQIC Patch Archives which lists the majority of patch messages we've gotten since August 1999; and third is the EverQuest Products page where you can find links to purchase the software (if you're not yet playing) and links to player's guides that you can also purchase. We've also got some smaller additions to the site, a couple more class specific news links, and a new entry in the combat section (a link to spawn guides).

Now the announcement...

First, we are about 65% complete on the "plain" version of the site for those people whose browsers cannot handle the more specialized coding features of the current version. Also, we've received some email challenging our ability to know which browsers you are using without conducting a poll of our visitors. Let me assure you that not only do we know which browsers you use, we also know what version it is, what screen resolution you use, what browser plug ins you have installed on your machine and what operating system you are using when you visit the site. All of this information is available through CGI scripts which are embedded onto each of our pages. The CGI script captures the information about your browser and then we are able to view that data as statistics. If you'd like to see an abbreviated example of CGI scripts in action, click on this link. The site explains the data captured and then gives you a demonstration when you click on the button in the middle of the page. So as you can see, no polls are needed to obtain this statistical information about our visitors' browsers.

3-1-2000
Since our site recreation/update on 2-21-00, we have received several emails from people regarding their inability to see the site correctly (as it was programmed to be seen). Unfortunately, this is happening to those visitors who are using Netscape as their browser (AOL people especially). Because Netscape does not fully support the latest in web programming and design (HTML 4.0, Cascading Style Sheets, DHTML and even some JavaScript) their experience on this site will be less than optimal, and in some cases horrendous.

Because 80% of our visitors use Microsoft Internet Explorer version 4.0 or higher (which DOES support these conventions), we will not be reprogramming the newly developed site to accommodate the smaller minority of Netscape users. As a compromise, we will create a separate set of technically plain pages for Netscape users, which will use an older version of HTML and will not contain any Cascading Style Sheets, DHTML or JavaScripts. We will then create a brand new home page where you can choose which version of EQIC you'd like to view, based on  your browser type.

Because this will involve a duplication of the website for the Netscape and AOL people, this will NOT happen overnight. However, please understand that we are working on it and will implement the change as soon as we can. If you have any comments or concerns about the planned changes, you can contact us at eqic@fullbyte.com or by posting in the EQIC Community Forums. As always, thanks for your support.
