Laws of the Guild
As a guild we would never presume to incorporate a terribly strict code of eithics on our members. Prosecution is rare within our ranks partly due to our policy of not discriminating by level, race or class, but moreso because our members do not need guidance; we are a family and as a family we try to not impose restrictions or tell members how to act. However there is generally a set of base guildlines which, though not forceably upheld, are generally common etiquette.

1) Above all else, before any other rule, remember you are a God. As a God you are part of our family, no better and no worse than any other God. All Gods are equal, we have no hierarchy, we are a family, we are united and we are here to have a good time. Remember to respect your fellow gods and remember to always carry yourself in a manner befitting a God.

2) As a God, please do not forget those not as privledged as yourself and do not be so arrogant as to refuse to take 5 minutes out of your day to help a fellow player in need (within reason)

3) We do not discriminate by race, sex, level, class, religion or membership into other Guilds. Anyone may become a God and anyone will be welcomed the same, be they a level 40 Cleric or a level 1 Warrior.

4) When you are hunting remember you are a God, and as a God you have an obligation to fight with both honor and valor. Part of that is remaining fair, and we do not encourage kill-stealing, unfairly looting or leaving your party members behind. Remember, as a God the world watches you and your actions will inspire how the world reacts to us.

5) We have yet to have a complaint lodged against any member so far, which is a fact we take great pride in. We our in the world of Norrath to have fun and part of that is offering the same curtiousy and respect to those you meet.
