Tuesday May 14th, 2002

Development continues on my test map, and I'm learning great new things every day! I finally figured out how stitching works! WOOH! What is stitching you ask? Well, considering it would be a very BAD idea to amke large worlds as one HUGE map, GPG came up with a technique called stitching. First, a breakdown of how maps work. Let's say you have a main area with a dungeon and a cave. The main area, the dungeon, and the cave, are all their own seperate "region". A map is divided into regions, and are stitched together at certain points. Ever notice how the game gets smoother when you go into a dungeon? Less to draw right? WRONG. Less level to load is what it is. Right at the entrance would be where the stitch takes place. This allows for seamless integration. GENIOUS! I love the flexability this editor has, and even though I had problems with getting it working initially, I think I've figured out most of the quirks. The only disadvantage I have right now is not being able to actually PLAY my map, due to the lack of the 1.09 beta patch. I'm hoping GPG releases it before Friday, so I can do some in-game testing. I will post my map in the MAPS section when I feel it is at a playable level. Expect a beta release within a week or so.

Until then, happy Dungeoneering, and enjoy this fine game!
