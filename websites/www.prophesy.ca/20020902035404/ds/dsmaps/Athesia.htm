Back Story

The Land of Athesia has seen it's fare share of war and desctruction. From the rise and fall of the
Dark Armies who invaded from the seas to the west a thousand years ago, to the interracial war
known as The Purification. 40 years after the purification, each of the races, Humans, Elves, Dwarves,
Droog, and Krug, have found a tenative balance among each other. The Humans, being the most
prosperous of the races, Claimed the southern continent. Dispersed goblin camps were spared any
further destruction, but at a cost. The Goblins were given a swampy area of land to the Southeast,
now known as the Goblin Moors. The Dwarves, now on good terms with the Humans, reside within
their mountain fortress of Cairnwotlen in the center of the Mithril Mountains to the south. As for the
Droog, they took the eastern desert known as the Burning Sands, as it is their preferred climate and is close to the shoreline.
The Droog still hold tenantive trade routes with the Humans and Dwarves, but it is tenative at best for sure.
Secluded within Qwithnesti, the ancient Elves took the northern part of the continent, including the
Northern Forest and Northvale. As the Elves distrust all other races, they swore that any tresspassers
would be killed upon sight. To the Southeast of Northvale, the Krug have calimed the vast swamps
known as Blight Mire, setting up a primitive city within the ancient ruins of Tenesia upon the Bay of Blight.

All was well despite the few skirmishes that arose at times, but now a new threat has arose from the
depths of the vast caves and tunnels under the Mithril Mountains.

Long ago, an elven Magus known as Lord Soth began to experiment with the ancient and dark powers of
Necromancy, effectively being able to control the forces of life and death itself. The Elven High Council saw
this as a dessacration of life, and banished Lord Soth into the Underworld. Taking his followers with him,
Lord Soth traversed the twisting, endless caverns and passages of the Underworld until he found a huge cavern
with an underground hotspring, and vast groves of strange underground plant and animal life. The area was rich
and was the perfect place to start a new civilization. After 1000 years, the Dark City known as Drak Vilerran was
flourishing, and a new race of Elves, the Dark Elves, were growing in numbers and power. Now, Lord Soth has
decided it is the time to reclaim the surface world which was denied to him by his Kin. Entrusting his most adept students with the command of their own Undead Armies, the Dark Elven Necromancers forged a dark pact, and became known as The Six Deaths, Dromael, Eithos, Anasia, Trevail, Habrina, and Soth. With their Undead Legions, coupled with the witless Krug with whom a deal was struck, the Six Deaths have begun their assault upon the land of Athesia, and are using the powers of terror and darkness to bring suffering and terror upon those
of the surface world. It is truly the stuff of nightmares come to life...

You are a former merchant from Arnheim, who fled from their due to perilous circumstances and moved to the secluded
snowy valley where the Town of Frostwind lies. The Town Guard has issued a lockdown, the Necromancer Legions are
beginning their invasion, and preperations for war are underway. Are you the one who will rise to the occasion and seek
out Lord Soth and destroy the leaders of the Necromancer Legions? Then enter The Land of Athesia, and prepare for an
epic adventure that will thrill and haunt you for ages to come.

Prelude

Moving to Frostdale was a good idea. Life in Akrain was getting a bit too "dangerous" for
any person's liking. Even though it meant leaving all your connections and friends behind, at least
your Aunt Tessie and Uncle Garek lived there. It wasn't like you had a choice in the matter, the
Royal Guard of Akrain were looking for you, and even went so far as to offer a 1,000 gold pieces
reward to anyone who turned you in. Sneaking past the guards late one night, you ascended the city
walls, and began your trek to freedom. The journey to Frostdale was not without peril, however.
You almost died on several occasions, and were forced to take up a job as a caravan guard in
Cairnwotlin for the Dwarves. The pay was good, but those Dwarves never left you alone for a second.
It was like they were picking on you. But how could you blame them, what with all the tension between
the races of Athesia. 3 months after your escape from Akrain, you arrived from the Crystal Caverns to
the sight of the Frostdale Tower of Mystecism looming in your vision. The smell of wood fires was faint
in the gentle, but cold winds as snow whipped across your face. "What a barren place" you thought to yourself,
but the dismal weather did not hide the sounds of merrymaking that wafted from the Inn past the Tower.
Opening the door, you fought with the wind to prevent the door from being blown off it's hinges. You turned
around, and traversed the rose carpet leading to the stairs up to the bar. Who else was the innkeeper
and bartender of the Frostdale Pub & Inn but your very own Uncle Garek!

"Garek! I can't believe it! I haven't seen you in a long time old man, how are you?" you exclaimed.

"Wha...who are you? ... wait a minute, OF COURSE! How are ye doin youngin? You've grown!
Here, take a seat by the fire and I'll get you a cup of mulled wine to warm your blood! Tessie!
Tessie, you won't believe who's here!" Bellowed your Uncle Garek

The sounds and smells of the Inn were warm and comfortable, washing away the hardships of your
long journey. All thoughts of your former life in Akrain slowly faded away. At last, you could find some peace in the turmoil that plagues the land as of late.

Two years pass by, your Uncle Garek being a rather wealthy man in Frostdale, gives you your own
house beside the Inn. It's small, but it's yours and you love it more than anything. Working the
bar late nights, keeping the fire going and cooking meals for patrons, and maintaining the suites upstairs, you manage to not
only earn your keep, but extra money on the side. The local blacksmith has taken a liking to you,
and hopes to teach you the secrets of metalwork.

But peace can only stay for so long. Reports of invasion have begun to surface from the Southern reaches
of Akrain and the towns to the East and West . Legions of Undead are stalking the lands, laying
waste to towns and farms, killing the innocent in what looks like coordinated attacks, planned by the
force controlling the undead hordes. Nobody knows for sure, but the local Magus has reason to believe it is the
work of Necromancers.

Dark Elven Necromancers.

Not only that, but the usually peacefull Krug have taken alliance with the Undead Legions, and swarm
from Blight Bog and the ruined city of Har Ganeth, looting and pillaging caravans and secluded
homes. The Captain of the Town Guard of Frostdale has issued a lockdown, preventing anyone
entering or leaving Frostdale without his explicit permission.

Feeling helpless in the middle of all this, you seek the advise of your Uncle Garek, who through
the days past has come to rely upon you as his most trusted of Kin.

"What should I do, Uncle? I feel as though I must do something, not stand idly by and watch as
the world goes to the Abyss! I don't know who or what is responsable, but if anyone can find out,
somehow I just KNOW it's me! I know it sounds crazy, but I must!" you exclaim.

"I understand, oh how I understand. You are growing, and so you crave adventure and a purpose.
I was like that too, when I was your age. I travelled to far lands in search of fame and fortune, but after
what seemed an eternity my travels were for naught. And so I came to find myself here, a lone merchant
with barely the shirt on his back, a mule, and a rickety old wagon. I stayed here and worked the Inn,
and it was passed to me when the last owner died of pneumonia. Oh, he was a great man, kind and
honorable. I still feel like I didn't accomplish something in my journeys though. So perhaps, it is YOU
who must." said Garek.

And so, you prepare for the long journey to the ruins of Tenesia and beyond, hoping you won't die and knowing
that at the end of all this, you will have accomplished something great. Any and all you meet will
know that you are the one to change the course of history on Athesia. These are indeed dark times,
and you are the light that shines through it.
