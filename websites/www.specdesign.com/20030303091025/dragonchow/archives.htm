It is intended to be a comprehensive resource for Dragonchow members by:

Jesse the Wurm
Last night I encountered the most intelligent AI I have yet seen in the game. While fighting wurms in GD with Sarenia and Cabbz, we were pulling them out of their cave, and killing them with no problem. Then we pulled "Jesse" (my name for him not Verant's). The most impressive wurm--no the most impressive mob--I have ever fought. He seemed to be a typical Ancient Shardwurm. I pulled him and he blasted me over and over with nasty spells cutting my health down. I was almost dead when Cabbz (a shaman) healed me. I then turned and began massively nuking. I delivered several powerful blasts and brought him down to where he had no visible health. Suddenly he heals himself. I had never seen a wurm do that before. In a few seconds he was at full health. Again I nuked, and again he healed just before we could deliver the killing blow. Running out of mana I nuked him again until he was near death, then I watched as Jesse cast another spell. The first spell failed, (never saw a mob fail at a spell before either), then he cast a second spell of the same kind...and vanished. Now in the past wurms have displayed a nasty habit of poofing; that is to say they teleport for no good reason to the mountainside above the cave entrance. No spell causes them to do this and it seems more like a bug than a natural defense. It is only an inconvenience however, as you simply chase them up there and finish the job, but this wurm never appeared above the cave like the others. He was simply no where to be seen. I still had him in my target window and was concerned he would heal himself again. Sure enough his health surged up. Damn! I sat down and tried to med, hard to do when you are in such a fowl mood. And as I sat there staring at the target window a sudden realization stole over me. The wurm hadn't poofed. I know from experience that you can't accurately tell changes in the health of a mob unless you are near it. So the wurm couldn't be up on the mountain. Jesse had to be close by. PORTRAIT TAKING
For those of you wishing to send in pictures of your character, here is a helpful tip: Try standing near a wall, then change to camera view and rotate the camera until it hits the wall. This will push the camera close to your character so you can take a nice close-up. Then rotate yourself for the right angle, and hit the minus key on your keypad. Zip it, (with Winzip), and attach it to your email. For those of you with graphics programs the image should be 193 by 355 pixels at 72 dpi RGB and saved as a quality 4 jpg.
