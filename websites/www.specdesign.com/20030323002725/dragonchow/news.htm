Jesse the Wurm

Last night I encountered the most intelligent AI I have yet seen in the game. While fighting wurms in GD with Sarenia and Cabbz, we were pulling them out of their cave, and killing them with no problem. Then we pulled "Jesse" (my name for him not Verant's). The most impressive wurm--no the most impressive mob--I have ever fought. He seemed to be a typical Ancient Shardwurm. I pulled him and he blasted me over and over with nasty spells cutting my health down. I was almost dead when Cabbz (a shaman) healed me. I then turned and began massively nuking. I delivered several powerful blasts and brought him down to where he had no visible health. Suddenly he heals himself. I had never seen a wurm do that before. In a few seconds he was at full health. Again I nuked, and again he healed just before we could deliver the killing blow. Running out of mana I nuked him again until he was near death, then I watched as Jesse cast another spell. The first spell failed, (never saw a mob fail at a spell before either), then he cast a second spell of the same kind...and vanished.

Now in the past wurms have displayed a nasty habit of poofing; that is to say they teleport for no good reason to the mountainside above the cave entrance. No spell causes them to do this and it seems more like a bug than a natural defense. It is only an inconvenience however, as you simply chase them up there and finish the job, but this wurm never appeared above the cave like the others. He was simply no where to be seen. I still had him in my target window and was concerned he would heal himself again. Sure enough his health surged up. Damn! I sat down and tried to med, hard to do when you are in such a fowl mood. And as I sat there staring at the target window a sudden realization stole over me. The wurm hadn't poofed.

I know from experience that you can't accurately tell changes in the health of a mob unless you are near it. So the wurm couldn't be up on the mountain. Jesse had to be close by. That's when it hit me--Jesse hadn't teleported he had gone invisible! To test this theroy I stood up and fired off my Staff of Temperate Flux--this is a clickable debuffing item. Sure enough the staff took effect. The bloody wurm was standing somewhere right next to us medding and healing! I have to say I was impressed.

I cast a nuke spell at the invisible creature and it did full damage. I began to cast a second when I went link dead. Shortly afterwards Sarenia went LD too. When we returned Sarenia discovered she was standing naked in Gfay--she was dead. The wurm had found her LD and killed her. I was starting to think Jesse could cast Linkdead spells too. I had to gate to port Sarenia back from her bind spot, and I am disappointed to tell you that we never did manage to find or kill that wurm. Yet it is interesting to know that Jesse is still out there, somewhere. One day we hope to find him and finish our dispute one way or another.

(btw the name "Jesse" is in deference to the infamous bear from Northern Exposer)

Eldeberon

Mrpain's Goonie Adventure

From the diary of Mrpain:

It was a normal day in the Overthere. Occasional rain, fog, and Goons roaming the zone. Accompanied by Swiftpain, Drasus, Seung, Scalezz and Drukinpinoch (With Lindsay and Kinney watching over us) - I was hunting Sarnaks at the ruins. Making pretty short work of these atrocities, we cleared out the wandering wildlife to pass the time.

It was about then that we heard the call "Goons are out!" and begain discussing the possibility of facing one. It was then decided by the group that we would actively seek out one of these intolerant beggars and make an example out of him. Dragoon T'Vem was the closest, so Drasus walked up to him and got his attention. T'Vem had little clue what was waiting for him just over the next rise.

Once he crested the hill, he saw Scalezz. The necromancer was quick to deal damage to T'Vem through the use of his lifetapping spells. This enraged T'Vem and he proceeded to chase Scalezz around the field with blood in his eyes. While the rest of us attempted to catch T'Vem from behind, it proved very difficult to follow his movements. Occasionally hitting him from behind, and calling "STOP!" to Scalezz, we finally got his attention and Swiftpain, Drukinpinoch, and myself proceded to beat him up. The pets added in and the battle raged.

As the Shamans worked furiously to keep us healthy, Swiftpain and I split his attention to lessen the urgency of their spells. Soon T'Vem was looking a bit ragged and torn. All the while, Scalezz continued to drain his life. We fought him with renewed ferocity when the shamans begain calling out "Out of Mana!". Fortunately, Lindsay was nearby and was casting heals herself.

Seconds later, standing over the body of Dragoon T'Vem, someone mentioned that the Goon Death Squad doesn't take too kindly to one of their own being taken down. We decided that discretion was the better part of valor and looted quickly as we headed out of the area. Soon the bulk of us (the ones without Feign Death) were in the Frontier Mountains. When we were sure that they had forgotten about our battle with T'Vem, we met back at the ruins again and continued hunting.

Now with the effort involved with killing one of these Dragoons, one would think they would have something worth looting. Truth is, this military midget left behind a Long Sword and a bad taste in the air -- what the softskins call 'smell'. Other then the bragging rights to having faced down a Dragoon, there wasn't much yield from our struggle.

Despite the lack of good treasure, I found great enjoyment in ending his reign of terror. Shouts of relief came up accross the zone as he fell, and the fame of Dragonchow was spread once again. Now to move on to his boss - General D'Veers!

PORTRAIT TAKING
For those of you wishing to send in pictures of your character, here is a helpful tip: Try standing near a wall, then change to camera view and rotate the camera until it hits the wall. This will push the camera close to your character so you can take a nice close-up. Then rotate yourself for the right angle, and hit the minus key on your keypad. Zip it, (with Winzip), and attach it to your email. For those of you with graphics programs the image should be 193 by 355 pixels at 72 dpi RGB and saved as a quality 4 jpg.
