ABOUT THIS SITE...

It is intended to be a comprehensive resource for Dragonchow members by:

1. Providing in one place, (the Library), direct links to popular game resources (such as "Item Search"), that eliminates the need to pass through multiple pages.

2. A Member's List that is easier to read (although less current than the present Roll Call)

3. A site for players to post in-depth biographies of their characters.

It is also intended as an educational tool for new recruits by:

1. Providing a guild history (that can be updated as events warrant it).

2. Character bios that will provide  new members with a better understanding of their guildmates.

3. A more easily found guild description.

Congradulations to the Newlyweds

By Haghoula Gravestone

The wedding of two guild members, Lyzzerd and Minimoretocome, both Iksars, was a very strange event. In the first place, the location for the ceremony was The Arena, a zone off Lake Rathetear whose sole function (well, aside from weddings) is to serve as a Player vs Player site. Due to the late arrival of some guests, the early arrivals, including Haghoula, passed the time by attacking one another and getting drunk.

Once the ceremony finally began, there was some additional trouble. One of the guests, Aend, went link dead, so his character kept attacking people (especially Haghoula, who earlier had cast Vampiric Curse on him, a DoT). She had to repeatedly root him, but finally some audience members got tired of his attacks and simpy slaughtered him--right there in front of the bride and groom. Then the bride went link dead, creating a general round of laughter and many jokes.

What was most disturbing was the end of the ceremony. Instead of "you may kiss the bride," it was "KILL THE GNOME!" Fortunately, this was not a reference to yours truly, but another of Haghoula's race (Brodda). The guests, bride and groom began to pummel the Gnome, beating him senseless and leaving him dead. Cake and refreshments followed.

The drunken guests finished off the night by slaughtering one another. Haghoula, drunk but not stupid, decided it was time to leave.

Epic News!

Have you been struggling to obtain items for your epic? Would you like help, but are wondering if you should bother the guild? Have you not even looked at your epic because you think it will be too hard to even do the simple things?

Now there's hope.

Klothe, your smarter-then-the-average-bear shaman, and keeper of the DC Roll Call, would like to welcome you to Epic Quest, a new guild program geared toward helping members learn about, and advance in, their epics. On the chat board you will find a new DC Only header called simply "Epics". This category (which is accessed by using the same password as any of the DC Only categories), has been placed here for each of you to post your epic progress and what you need help with. It also contains links to sites holding information concerning various epics. This list of links has been nicely organized by class for your convenience.

The intention here is that this information will then be used to organize weekly raids, mini-raids, or small groups adventures (as the need may be) to maintain the ongoing epic advancement of those Chow who wish to pursue their epics. Klothe is overseeing this program and once people begin to sign up, he can be expected to post the time and place for the first Epic Advancement Day (EAD).

It is our hope that everyone interested will post in the new thread, and that the EAD will become a regular weekly event that everyone will enjoy participating in not simply for the promise of epic advancement but for the chance to take a break from exp grinding, or tradeskill drudgery, to group with new guild faces, and friends.

For more information of this subject please visit the "Epic Hopping" thread.

Eldeberon

EQ for the Playstation2 is set 500 years in Norrath's Past

EverQuest Online Adventures takes place during the Age of Adventure; a time set approximately five centuries before the original EverQuest for the PC. When the city of Freeport was a new coalition of merchants and the elves have yet to abandon their ancient homeland completely. To the west, Antonius Bayle the Second has recently been crowned ruler of Qeynos. In the eastern lands, the once great Eldarr Forest has all but disappeared, its trees withering away as the once rich ground beneath it turns to desert sands. To the far north, on the frigid plains of Everfrost, many of the nomadic barbarian tribes have put aside their differences and banded together for survival. The city of Halas stands as a new concept to these proud warriors, opening communication and trade with other races for the first time in barbarian history. Fortunately the barbarians have found strong support in their endeavors, especially from the dwarven mining city of Moradhim nestled in the northern mountains.
