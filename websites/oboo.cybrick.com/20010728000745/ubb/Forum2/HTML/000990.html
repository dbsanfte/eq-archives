Ok, I’m posting this as a help to all our members who are just getting into the raid groove. Basically, this is to help everyone know the standards of performance expected of them, the reasons behind some rules, and usual practices. While these may not be standards that everyone practise, there are standards that I would like the Bears to practise, at all times. You chance of getting invited to raids increase the more professional you are.

General:

1) Punctuality. There is no greater stress for a raid leader than to have people sending tells 5 minutes before the allotted raid time asking for ports or directions. Therefore, if you sign up for a raid, be at the designated meeting spot early. Most raids now post a gathering time 30 mins before the actual raid slot. If you can’t make this time, PARK your character at the spot the night before. Raid leaders reserve the right to replace you with someone on standby or who is present at the starting spot. I plan to be VERY strict about this for future raids I hold.
2) When on a raid, only leaders are to use /shout. Everyone else must confine themselves to /gsay, /say, or /ooc. This is to keep the shout channel for important directions and announcements.
3) Pay attention. Early on, proper leaders will call main assist, main haster, main claritier, main shammy buffer early. Know who they are, and if possible, hotkey the /tell to them.
4) Make every effort to garner information about where you’re going. Print a few maps, read up on them, find out what your best role on the raid is.
5) Unless something is outright stupid, don’t stress out the raid leader any further by complaining or bitching or questioning instructions. If you are not happy, finish the raid and then don’t follow that particular raid leader again. Do NOT leave in the middle of a raid you signed up for. Not only is that completely unprofessional, you could be screwing the whole raid for 35 other people.

Assisting:

On a raid, always hotkey an /assist (main assist) button and always use it when assist is called. NEVER attack before the main assist calls it. If he doesn’t call it and he dies, /shrug his problem. Often there are reasons why an assist call is not made early. Ok, I’d like to explain a little bit about why /assist is important. On a raid, esp planar raids, having multiple adds in camp can wipe out your raid. The only thing going for you is chanter crowd control. However, for the magically-challenged, enchanter Tash, mez and charm lines are HUGE aggro. So the main assist’s job is to ensure that once he breaks mez, he can keep the beast on him, thus attacking quickly, or standing in front of the mob seemingly doing nothing – in reality he is taunting. Now, the reason why assist is called – if you randomly press F8 or target a mob, attack and pick the wrong mob to attack, YOU ARE KILLING YOUR CHANTER. And this not only pisses chanters off, it pissed leaders off too. So ALWAYS use an assist hotkey, and NEVER attack until assist is called. One caveat. Members of the pull group will know who and what is being pulled in groupsay, so more often than not you may see other members of the pull team attacking. While yes, sometimes it is because they want to swing early, more often than not it is because:
a) they are trying to keep the mobs distracted until a mez can go in – they know exactly when to stop, as mez calls are often made in group
b) they know exactly the target is
c) the main assist is assisting THEM (esp the puller). Last week’s CoM, I received comments about Expendable attacking before assist was called. Reason for this is – Expendable was the one who peeled several mobs into camp in the first place. True, some mobs he prolly just wanted to swing at, but at all times he knew which was the target.

Now, this is important discipline I want to drum into everyone. This is an unshakeable rule. I’ve seen too many raids screwed because people simply cannot understand NOT to attack before assist is called. At all times, maintain calm and control your excitement. That mob that drops your uber loot could also be the one causing you to go through 4 hours of CR. Last week’s raid didn’t have a chanter, and hardly needed Lirah to mez either. But it was still discipline I wanted EVERYONE, including myself, to follow.

Camp Calls:
When the raid leader, AND ONLY THE RAID LEADERS, call camp, this mean you MUST ensure your grp cleric, or raid clerics camp before you do. The reason for this is, the leader sees the possibility of a raid wipeout and to ensure that CR is as short as possible, a cleric must be camped and cleared of aggro. If you have to throw yourself at the mob and nuke it’s ass until you die, you have to. However, it sometimes happens that some panicky idiot will yell for cleric to camp. ONLY listen to raid leaders. Once clerics are camped, then you can camp. Exceptions to this rule, are on planar break-ins. Everyone is to run to the camp spot and immediately camp, to avoid mobs which may have aggroed you, following into camp and killing everyone else.

Dying
If you die on a raid, or while moving, ALWAYS get a loc, and know who to consent. Then you wait. If you have to wait 3 hours while they get to your corpse, so be it. People aren’t leaving you there to rot for the fun of it. And if you constantly send tells to the CR or leader of “where’s my corpse”, “am I getting rezzed”, etc. you’re just making their jobs much harder. Another rule, keep AFKs while waiting for rez to a minimum. Oftentimes, the raid will try to get you rezzed so they can move, and not being there to click the yes button not only slows down the raid, it also puts everyone else at risk.

Loot
Group leaders (or designated members of group) are to handle loots. Leaders must call out the loot, even if it’s just a rune on the corpse. Honesty is of paramount importance, and in cases where people try to ninja, they have been banned server-wide from raids. Gems and money items normally go to clerics for Symbol expenses. Now I’ve had people bitch to me about clerics not using that much at all, or scamming off the cost of gems. Well, this balances out for those times when clerics come to help on raids and get no reimbursement at all from poor drops. The leaders will usually ask the clerics how much they spent, and reimburse that amount. Again, honesty is the key.

On Pulls
There is nothing ruder on a planar raid than for you to ask the puller to pull more mobs that drop your armor type. Don’t even JOKE about it. The puller is there doing his job, according to a system he knows. It’s considered very bad manners to do so.

Experience, not XP
Don’t expect xp in a raid. If you get any, that’s a bonus, not your due. You are there to see a dragon go down or for a shot at winning some nice gear, not getting your next blue pixel.

Ask if you don’t know
When in doubt, always better to ask than to do something wrong and get the raid wiped. If you want to know if it’s ok to cast a pet, ask. Usually, pathing problems cause having pets around to suck, so rarely are they used on Hate and Fear raids. Sometimes, pets are only used during certain fights, then killed after. Another example, necro dots are not allowed on certain mobs, because if the mob dies to it, the corpse poofs. So ALWAYS ask when in doubt. Better to look like a noob, than be one.

Resist Gear
Once you hit your over 45’s, start invest in your resist gear. They’ll be critical for raids and even normal use later on. Stop using your money on your twinks first. Give your main a higher survivability chance on raids first. Look up the best pieces for your needs, plan out your chars’ equip. Balance out the stat items with the resist items.


Ok, so far, this is all I can think of right now. Sorry if the tone is strong, but well, raiding is serious business. And if we wanna seriously enjoy more raids, we gotta all learn. If I’ve missed anything here, I hope our experienced raiders will help add on more information.

I’m also making this mandatory reading. Every member must read these, and post a reply that they read, understand and agree. Questions or clarifications, hell, even objections are welcome for discussion. Feel free to post that I’m full of it, and why you disagree. But at least you read it. Let me make this clear. If your name is NOT posted under this forum, you will not be allowed onto any of my raids. I want EVERYONE to read this.

Okies, enuf of the hardass tone. I’ll leave the rest of the grumpiness to Two. But seriously, please, read and follow. It’s important not just to you and your reputation on the server, but to our attempts to become a more successful raiding guild as well.

Ari

Thanks guys. Please remind everyone else to read. Now, as for chanter guidelines... hmmm... well, I'll post what I see, although they will suck - my chanter is only lvl 16. :P Bear with me.

1) There are usually multiple chanters on a raid, and the raid leader (if they know their job) will designate one chanter for clarity, one chanter for haste, and one chanter for mez (usually the chanter in pull group). What usually works is that the chanter in pull grp will hotkey a tell to the other 2 chanters, saying that they are mezzing a mob. This saves mana and avoids conflicting spells, like stun or root hitting a mezzed mob.

2) If on a raid, your mez keeps getting broken and NOT by the main assist, feel free to tell the group, the raid leader and the raid in general. Cuss and swear. I've had raids where the chanters threaten to gate out if their mezzes are broken again (after like 10 times) and no one contradicts them. Arrogant? maybe. But no one likes dying multiple times through someone else's stupidity.

3) Do only the role designated to you. Not sure how Tash is usually assigned - I think cahters work it our among themselves - but don't be like a chanter I know who will mez, run around stunning mob, casting and nuking, keep getting low and eating cleric mana, then dying and eating more cleric mana. While you CAN do a lot in a raid, stick to your assigned role, but be prepared to back up the other chanters, i.e. mezzer keeps getting resisted or interrupted.

4) AoE stun is a lovely thing.

Not sure what else I can add here, maybe Tanaman can drop us a few lines should he visit the boards.
