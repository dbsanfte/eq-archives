heya Lil.

Application procedures are built to keep players out of the guild, that is thier purpose. Even more proper, the application process is created to allow the players that the guild is searching for to get into it. It alone serves to keep the current AD members happy with those who are guilded with them.

There have been talks of adding a probation period on players who gain entrance into the ad guild. Yet, a probation period won't make AD an easier guild to gain entrance into as the probation period itself will stack onto our already existing application procedures. The desired effect from implementing an additional layer is kinda simple. There are alot of players who push themselves to get into AD, to find that drive later down the road disolve. Putting in place a probation period for a specific amount of time, will allow AD to not feel so guilty about removing a player whom has become a member if the need arises. A probation period wouldn't have any impact on our current process, except to extend it during the transition a player makes when becoming a new member to a seasoned one.

AD needs to do everything it can in order to preserve the guild itself, and the reasons for its existance. Our officers are motivated by the members of the guild to allow players into AD who match with the guild, but to restrict players whom aren't ad with heart and spirit.

Its impossible to get into AD if a player is looking to join ad for a few very easily spotted traits which the guild gets to witness over the time an applicant expresses interest in the guild, and begins the application process:
If a player wants in AD to get ph@t l3wtz we can see this and deny
if a player is not concerned over making or holding friendships with the current ad members we can see this and deny
If a player is immature, we can see this and deny
If a player is looking to join an 'uber' guild for simple raids and possible items, Ad can see this and deny.

There are dozens of other qualities we look for in our members. And, while process's seem harsh to most, and many don't make it into the guild, its only fair to our members to provide them the best guild atmosphere we can.

The players that do get into ad, show that they have an enjoyment with the current members, work well with the current balance of the guild, enjoy raiding, show loyalty, show that they are willing to play the long-game, and show commitment to the guild. Thats all the process really is looking for, and its created to put up obstacles to those lacking in those traits.

AD's a guild that has lasted the test of time. Over the time ive lead AD, ive seen 1 in-guild argument that got out of hand. 99% of the time, the guild channels, and players on raids are extremely pleasant to be around. The guild itself makes me want to log in and play the game just to be around these guys. And, many do everything they can to protect AD, as well as to watch out for each other.

Those who have the qualities the guild is searching for, effortlessly seem to slip right into the guild and become a part of it which defines the core and ad as a whole.

Hope this explains why our process is there. I've heard many on the server say its impossible to get into ad. Its the rumor lately that floats around, and, undoubtably many don't apply due to those rumors. I've seen players that would effortlessly slip into AD even say 'i didnt apply to AD cause i didnt think id get in'. While its impossible for most, its natural for the ones which the guild is built to entertain. Id also like to state, that if one doesn't apply, they will never know if they can get in or not without at least tryin.

I've seen alot that i thought would run through the applicant process with ease not even try because of the rumors of it being 'impossible'. AD is not easy to get into. Most guilds on RN will invite players into thier guild after a 3 day initiation period. AD's process's last for a few weeks, to lasting several months. AD wants to make the best possible judgement they can on each player who expresses interest. And, the things the guild looks for, simply takes time.

This all has lead to a guild which has not had any real internal strife, has lasted on RN since creation, and a guild who's core members join and stay in-guild. Our base is tremendously large, while remaining enjoyable.

AD has lasted the test of time, which is not an easy feat for any guild. We're not going anywhere, and we all seem very content with the guild itself. It's worth it imho to give it a shot if this kinda guild is of interest.

We're strong, close, confident loyal and giving, while maintaining a large raid oriented guild and an enjoyable atmosphere. Thats what we're looking for in those that apply to the guild. Such things take a long time for an applicant to show thier true self. After a players real self is shown to the AD guild, the guild then votes.
