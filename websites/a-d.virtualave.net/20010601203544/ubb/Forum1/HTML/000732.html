There is no fixed upper limit on our guild's size. We've never said we're gonna stop at 50 or 100 or 1000. There is a practical limit on our guild size however. When there seems to be too many new faces, and when people don't know one another, then our guild is too large. When we have separate factions in our guild, split by timezone or playing times (ie, EK), then our guild is too large.

We want a tight and close guild where people know one another. Having too man people makes that difficult, if not impossible. Members who do not play when most of the rest of the guild is playing makes that difficult. Members who chose not to raid or grind with us during the time that they are online make that difficult. Members who log in infrequently if at all make that difficult. Members who other members dislike or feel uncomfortable around make that difficult.

This is why our application process is the way it is. If you want into AD, show us that you play at around the same times we play, want to do things with us rather than just wear our tag over your head as you get your own pickup groups, are online consistently and often (more than a couple days a week, but not necessarily every day.), and have a good personality and are a very likable person.

We do have a very high rejection rate, but in all my time with AD, I can only really remember 4 or 5 NO votes coming out. Typically applicants just don't make an effort to group or raid with us. They don't hustle and try to keep informed of what is going on. Many of them simply slip into the same playing habits they've always had. Shout for a group, look for opportunities on their own and stopped looking for opportunities in the guild. We're looking for people who will look to what the guild is doing first. It's a change in playing style from what a great many of our applicants are used to, and many just drift out of our notice, never to return.

AD is only hard to get into because it requires effort to break out of your normal daily routine and change the way you play the game. It also requires that your personality fit with the rest of the guild in a non-abrasive way. Of these two requirements, far, far, far more people have come up short on the first than on the second.
