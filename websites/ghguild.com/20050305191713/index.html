Grey Hawke in EverQuest1 has finally won! YAY! WINNAR WINNAR! Grey Hawke seems to be the only really viable guild on Karana these days, and things are progressing nicely. To all the guilds who couldn’t hold it together : we are enjoying your ex-members just fine, and they are all fitting into the guild magnificently, thank you.

However, the intention of this post wasn’t to talk about how nice it is to have such a solid guild after the introduction of two major competing MMOs, or call out idiots (you know who you are… your names rhyme with Vagina-gistica and Vagina-ilicien) who assume that Mr. Smartypants is a PR guy for ANYONE much less Grey Hawke. Although I have to tell you… anyone who reads this front page and assumes they will see anything other than opinionated ranting is a flaming idiot. I mean seriously… get a fucking sense of humor once in awhile or go hide in your shell, play with your stamp collection, and let the big people talk. This is a rant page you retards, not a “please join Grey Hawke because we will make promises of high elf reach-arounds with every two items looted, just to get you here in our guild” page. Grey Hawke relies on its long-standing reputation of excellence, not on what a lunatic writes on the front page, but some of you humorless morons obviously can’t understand that.

For further information please call 1 (900) IMADUMBASS. Your call is important to us, please stay on the line.

ANYWAY…

Rather than hide and pretend we don’t exist, like some horribly disfigured stepchild shuttled away in an attic and only allowed to moan when company isn’t over for dinner, I am here to tell you… Grey Hawke is alive and well in EverQuest 2. There, I said it. The cat is out of the bag. The pigeon has flown the coop. The party has left my pants. We are a multi-game guild.
 

So how is life in EQ2, you ask? Good, thanks! Grey Hawke has roughly 140 people in the guild at the moment, and we generally seem to enjoy the game well enough. But like any game where there are a large number of people gathered together in a rough society, there are assweasels guaranteed to rear their pimply heads, and issues that are going to occur. Below, I have outlined what I feel are the biggest issues currently in EQ2.

First, instanced zones on player number caps. On paper it works wonders, but in reality there are some issues. For example, to complete the Ghoulbane quest, there is a rare named mob (The Creator) that spawns deeper in Ruins of Varsoon that is PERMA-CAMPED. And fine, with multiple instancing, it wouldn’t SEEM to be a problem. However, the people cap on this zone is excessive and causes nothing but problems and heartache. 72 people in that zone means EVERY spawn area is camped, and usually there are 2-3 groups LINED UP ON TOP OF EACH OTHER waiting on the named to pop.

Here’s an idea! Make the cap in zones where you need named SUPER-RARE mobs to spawn a smaller instance spawn number. Maybe 50, tops. Or make the named spawn a clicky event… or put it in a mini-zone off of the main zone like you have in Stormhold. Right now, this is extremely reminiscent of camping Jboots in Najena, and it frankly pisses people off more than anything. There is nothing like sharing a piece of floorspace the size of a nipple with four groups all planning to pounce on the next spawn to make you scream and beat on your keyboard in frustration and agony. That, or just have the named spawn more frequently. As it is, I forsee that camp and others like it turning into rage-inducing shitfests that cause player grief, loss of subscribers, and GM headaches. It CAN be fixed, but right now it’s a pain in the ass.

Next, nothing pisses me off more than people who feel they can just wander up to the resources you are harvesting and help themselves. If enemy encounters can be locked, why aren’t harvesting encounters locked too? Just put a timer on it… no activity for 30 seconds means it unlocks the harvestable resource again. But shitty little kids who rudely take your resource when they didn’t spend 30 minutes clearing all the 4 million fish spawns to properly pop new resources make me want to scream and throw blunt objects at them and the nearest SOE employee.
 

Finally, with any new game I expect to see people being idiots. Here are some common household idiots and the things they do to annoy others:

1) Character Names. Come on… does SOE even HAVE a name filter? I have seen more names that don’t even come CLOSE to meeting your naming conventions here than I ever did in EQ1. Hawty Hotpants may be stupid but funny, but names I have seen (but will not post here because they don’t DESERVE to have their shitty lame names posted in a public area) are unacceptable. And so far I have seen an absolute lack of GM intervention on this issue.

2) Griefers. Several times now I have run into griefers of a new EQ2 ilk. What they do, since they cannot train mobs in EQ2, is simply stand in the middle of your group. Typically “evil” classes seem to do this more, and based on previous experiences, the typical griefer shithead assfucker is usually a teenager who feels they are “roleplaying” an evil class even though they wouldn’t know roleplaying if they started shitting d20’s tomorrow. So imagine a large group of people standing in the MIDDLE of your group, waiting to steal your mob, or blocking your view, spamming you with emotes the entire time just because they can.

Of course my dream resolution for griefers would be a couple rounds from a silenced MP5 in the back of the skull, but I'm sure there are other solutions I haven’t thought of yet. Hopefully with more blood.

3) Ebayers. Yep. Shit is ALREADY out there for sale. There are already people who 6-box characters as fast as possible and sell them on Ebay or other sites that care more about making a few dollars from the sales of stolen data (stolen because SOE owns the stuff they are selling, NOT the sellers) than being ethical.

And for those of you that own “sweat shops” where you hire people from shitty 3rd world countries so you can sell the accounts, or you 6-boxers that do nothing but play a game to make money, here is a big happy FUCK YOU from people who just want to play a game for entertainment. Thanks for making fun into something that feels cheap. I hope you get hit by a bus full of diseased rapists. You ruin the experience for people who just want to have fun by perma-camping things and selling them off. You ruin the sense of community that COULD be such an integral part of MMO games just by existing, so fuck you and die. HINT TO GMS: When all 6 people in a group go LD simultaneously several times in a row… they MIGHT be a sweatshop.

SOE needs to hire a group of people that just go out and fuck up Ebayers like this. Break their fucking knees and let people know that we don’t want this kind of shit in a new game. It’s NEW…start fresh and stop this now or it will only get worse.

4) People who rip other people off. Look, no one faults a person for making a few extra gold pieces. I have a merchant I set up some nights as well to sell off Adepts that no one in my guild can use, so I understand it. But making crude items and selling them (DAMAGED, usually… I can’t believe someone would sell an item at 40% damage…pay the 8 copper for repairs before you sell it, fucker) for the same price as pristine versions to people who don’t know any better. Or you other people who SPAM THE FUCKING ZONE 40,000,000 TIMES that you want to buy an item (paying big!! PST!) and then turn around and sell it at 5x the cost immediately after a purchase RIGHT IN FRONT OF THE PERSON YOU JUST RIPPED OFF.

The absolute lack of ethics I see from some people just astounds me. A few nights ago, I saw a guy trying to sell off the FREE STATUE he got with the game until I made it quite clear in the zone that the item he was selling is free. I had two people who thanked me afterwards because the asshead was trying to tell them it was a rare and valuable quest item. Another guy I saw buying a quest item for 5 gold then selling it at 30 gold, and I quickly let the zone (all instances) know exactly what he was doing. I think if more people LOUDLY expressed what these ripoff artists are doing, or GMs came in and capped them in the back of the head with the GM version of an MP5, we would see less of it. A few hangings in a public square is a SURE deterrent from this type of behavior.
