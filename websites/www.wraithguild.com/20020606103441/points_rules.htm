Rules

What is Points system?

'Points System' is a system shamelessly cloned from BotS. These points are awarded to each individual as they attend a guild raid. The current Points of each member reflects his or her priority for loot. Essentially, the eligibility of a guild member for an item based on raid attendance is now quantified by your Points standing. Officers can make discretionary decisions where following the Points system does not make sense, including awarding loot to twinks, or 1 member bidding for someone not present there. In most cases the person with the highest points wins the item and loses a Points amount that reflects the value of the item.

What is the benefit of Points?

Points allows for an unbiased comparison between guild members when decisions about loot are to be made based on attendance and recent items that have been awarded. Eligibility is determined by the highest current Points among those interested in the item.

What negative effect will the Points system have?

The implementation of Points will never properly reflect past contributions over the 2 years of the existence of the guild. As such, it is a tool to help award loot, but ultimately the decision lies with Wraith/Reviv officers. As time progresses, Points will be more reliable, and become the dominant method of loot distribution.

Because each item costs Points points, members cannot equip their secondary characters at the expense of the guild without it affecting their Points standing. If an item drops that a member is not willing to pay for, it will rot. The ramifications are three-fold: items that would have helped a member will rot, members will hoard Points points for items they want, and members will think more carefully about showing interest in items.

How are raid values determined?

The incoming Points points and the outgoing Points points must be equal to control inflation. However, the effort involved in an encounter is not always reflected in the loot. Dain will always drop a head, but his other loots are random, and so is the number of attendees. One Dain encounter could easily be worth quite a bit more than the next if a straight value of (total item Points / # attendees) was used to determine that raid value.

Instead, an average value is used, and values are adjusted accordingly for the difficulty of an encounter and the available needed drops. Encounters such as Klandicar and Sontalak are assured to drop no loot, so they cannot be worth Points. However, Points is still awarded for such encounters, and failed raids, because it reflects the effort of members.

How are item values determined?

An item value is set to properly reflect the amount of active raiding required to earn that item. There are a few guidelines used to determine this value:
  • Whether or not the item can be traded.
  • The classes that can use the item.
  • Usefulness of an item, including its stats and versatility.
  • Rarity of an item.

What if I arrive late ? 

Everyone present at the time of the kill will get full points. In some cases (PoF clearing, Chardok clearing to royals, Clearing defenders before dragons in NToV, etc) pre-clearings will have separate points. If someone is repeatedly showing up just before a mob is killed, action might be taken against that person.

What if I play 2 PCs or someone else's character out of necessity?

Points will be awarded to the person NOT the character. So it doesn't matter which char you are playing (for instance someone else's character due to necessity) or playing 2 PCs etc.

What about spell drops and drops to complete quests for keyed dungeons?

All Spells and Keys will be distributed based on officer award method. No points will be spent for these items.

Are there any special bonuses to points?

First time kills of non-epic mobs results in a one-time double point bonus.

What about failed Raids?

Failed raids will get half the value of the mob.

What about recruits?

As of now recruits do not get any points for attending raids. This might change in future

What about costs for items not in the database?

New items dropping, which aren't in database, will be given points on the fly, and this value might be later retroactively changed (though if it's increased, it wont be increased by much) on further evaluation.

How will points be handled for Reviviscence people ?

All Reviviscence members will get the same points for attending raids as Wraith people and can bid on the same items. Reviviscence members also need to track attendance on any raid (such as spur of the moment epic raids, with small numbers) and inform Alzedo or Desi about it. 

Who informs the other guild about raids ?

Everyone must remain on the raid channel all the time. It is moderated and will not increase spam. It's only used during raids or to announce any future raids.
