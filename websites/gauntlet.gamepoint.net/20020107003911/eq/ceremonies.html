The Ceremonies

Learn these ceremonies by heart. Not knowing them is to disrespect the whole guild. Lessons in these ceremonies can be taken, contact the Ceremony Master.

The ceremonies will always, unless stated otherwise, have the following setup:

The higher ranks have their position close to the Bishop and his Holy Circle. The Holy Circle are the 5 highest in rank of the Bishop's Canton. Other lesser members of the Bishop's Canton will take seat in the place reserved for their order. The lesser ranks of the different Orders take seat the fartest away from the Bishop.

When the Bishop has arrived he holds up his hand and all will be quiet. The Knight, Advisor, Priestess and Sage will then ask the Bishop for blessings. He will give them and their followers the blessings. After that the ceremony or meeting will commence as named by the Ceremony Master.

Rules that are common for almost all ceremonies are:
- No weapons and no helms or hats are allowed to be wielded/worn when the Bishop arrives. Showing weapons or helms is a direct offense to the Bishop. (PS: Staffs are not considered weapons and may therefor be wielded.)
- Lesser ranked members must also remove armor that is better then leather armor (so get yourself a leather outfit for the ceremonies; to come bare naked is not allowed either!). Showing full plate armor as a lesser ranked member during a ceremony is a direct offense to the higher ranks. Robes may be worn at all times.
- Every member stands up when the Bishop makes his entry.
- No talking or by other means making noises is allowed after the Bishop has raised his hand. Casting spells is not allowed either (not even for making food or water).
- After the blessings have been received everyone may sit down.
- Everyone must sit down when the Bishops sits down or when their direct leader/officer sits down.
- No one will take leave before asking their direct superior.
- After the Bishop has left it is allowed to leave on your own.
- When the Bishop or other council member calls for a council meeting the lower ranked members must take leave in an orderly manner.
- Lower ranked members don't talk during ceremonies, they talk via their Leader. They only speak when called forward by the Crystal Gauntlet and to speak.
- High ranked members positioned right behind their leader may enter open discussion with their leader when started by their leader. They also stop talking when their leader tells them to stop.
- The members of the Crystal Gauntlet should appoint replacements for them if they have urgent or important matters at hand.
- Try using 3rd-person view with all ceremonies, so you won't be stepping on someone's toes!
