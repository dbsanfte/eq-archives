In the wastelands of the North, the strong and powerful mercilessly cut down the weak and young that the frozen tundra has left barely alive.  The eternal winter is a battle from which only the strong of heart and body may survive.  The souls of those who perish in youth and those inexperienced in the harsh demands of such a master have cried out for a protector.  For time unknown, this spirit of need has attempted to forge one able to guide those able to become strong, but unfounded in the experience necessary to survive an unforgiving master.

     No-one knows how these souls hammered the spirits of those still living, bending them to grant unexpected mercy, saving a youth and putting them upon the path to strength and wisdom.  For such acts of mercy are swiftly punished in the barren lands. And yet, within our own lifetime, such has happened. A soul was born, one who knew that such acts of mercy are rewarded manyfold, in this lifetime and the next.

     This guide began as nothing more than a humble, adventurous youth.  But his spirit grew strong and his compassion grew as he saw injustice carried out by the strong upon the weak and defenseless.  He became justice in a land ruled by strength alone. Wandering the lands of Norrath,  he saw that the twisted ways of darkness pervaded every land, every kingdom. And his spirit grew to protect these foreign lands as well.  Knowing that one man could not undo the injustice of an entire land, a deep desire filled him to guide others to the meaning of justice.  Unbeknownst to him, whispers and rumors called him the Frost Spirit for wherever he traveled he sought not glory but only to show others the spirit of justice that dwells in all beings.

     In time some began to be drawn to him, not to worship or serve him, but to learn of the Justice the tortured souls of the Frozen North had forged into the spirit of this man.  He became the thread, weaving a tapestry from others lives.  He did not weave the patterns of their lives, but each individual together to form a whole that is greater than any one of their lives could be. Known for guarding the ways of the Spirits of Frost, forgoing old allegiances to clan and country, these protectors of justice became known as The Frostborn.

     Knowing that he could not be present in all places at all times, yet aware that those sharing his calling were scattered across the face of Norrath, Falladar, the Frost Spirit, formed a council of three.  From among his followers, three felt the spirits call to rise to the need of the now many Frostborn Protectors.  One felt the need to become the Voice of the Frostborn and was known as the Frost Lord, the two others as the Frost Knights.  This triumvirate follows the spirit of justice, embodied in the Frost Spirit.  They guide those who feel the call of Justice in their veins and decry all ties to Kings and Clans.  These are the Frostborn Guards, the carriers and executors of justice in all the lands of Norrath.
