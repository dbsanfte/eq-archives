THINGS TO DO DAYS BEFORE RAID


Single biggest delay to raiding Vox: people not arriving on time. If there were 30+ of the right people ready to raid, the whole thing could be over in an hour or two. Permafrost is in a remote location, so I recommend doing the following, the day before the raid.

  • If you are a melee, get transport, and get a bind in Halas. This is especially important for pullers.
  • Set up hotkeys for Disciplines if you have them.
  • If you have programmed any of your hotkeys to include non-critical comments get rid of them to cut down on spam & the associated lag (e.g. "/say I am assisting %T"). Information that is critical to battle (e.g. Heal, Lay-on-Hands, Tash, Slow) are fine.
  • Get your MR/CR gear ready in backpacks so that it is ready to equip quickly.
  • Check with friends if they would like to attend raid. Lining up some backups can make the difference in going ahead versus rescheduling a raid.
  • Kill Ice Giants. 3 are located outside Permafrost and others are inside the zone. All yield decent experience and loot even in the 50s.
  • Get CR bracers. The Ice Goblin King has "Symbols of Loyalty to Vox" bracers AC7 CR7. They aren't lore, so opportunists often stock up on them and sell to others before the fight.
  • Get CR leggings. Elite Goblin Guards occasionally have AC8 CR5 legs.
  • If you don't have one, get a weight reduction backpack off Goblin Archeologist.
  • Stay in the area and build factions. Kill Black Burrow gnolls for druid & Qeynos factions. Collect teeth, and turn in 4 at a time to female warrior near PvP area in Halas for barbarian factions.

STEP 3: Pulling / Killing

 

As soon as all buffs are finished, it is time to move to the pull / staging area.  It looks like this:

 

7.       PROCEED TO THE CASTER BIND SPOT AND ALL CASTERS BIND. DO NOT PASS ME IF YOU DON’T KNOW WHERE IT IS. AFTER YOU HAVE BOUND YOURSELF HAVE A SEAT.

 

We will all proceed to the caster bind point.  If you wanders past me you put the raid in jeopardy.  All casters must bind here.  THIS IS WHERE ALL CASTERS WILL STAY UNTIL THEY ARE CALLED.  In this spot, Casters completely avoid Vox's AE and can heal close to the wall at the side by the pull spot and possibly even through the wall.

 

8.       GROUPS 1 & 2 PROCEED TO PULL SPOT AND WAIT FOR VOX TO COME TO SPOT.  WAIT AT THE PULL SPOT DO NOT CHARGE HER OR YOU WON’T BE IN HEAL RANGE. SHE IS ABOUT TO BE PULLED.

 

Quick note on this pull spot - I have learned that this is the best spot to kill her in. If for some reason the GMs changed her pathing we can kill her in the lair as well.  This pull spot is an old tactic that was recently rediscovered on Bristlebane- credit goes to Toki on rediscovering this pull spot.  /bow.  All the melee's groups will go around the corner to the pull spot and hug the wall in the corner area.  When Vox is pulled STAY PUT and DO NOT charge her when they see her.  When she is pulled she has a "hesitation point" (marked on map) where she stops to cast a nuke on her puller.  It is VERY important that the melee's do not engage her at this spot- If the melees move too far up the ramp the clerics cannot heal them and all hell breaks loose.  LET HER COME TO THE PULL SPOT.

 

Note to Casters: If you die load up their best Fire-based nuke or heal spell and re-enter the fight.  Your corpse will be rezzed. 

 

Note to MAIN PULLER on pulling Vox - It doesn't matter who pulls her.  Make sure that you know the ramps well.   To pull her- the MAIN PULLER just steps into her lair.  If she aggro's great- run back to pull spot.  If she doesn't, fire an arrow at her and run back to pull spot.  She will take about 15-20 seconds to come to the pull spot because of her pathing.  Its also a good idea have the main groups move to the side of the Pull Spot because the nuke that she will cast while in her hesitation spot could affect the whole raid force and its best to avoid it. After the first AE strafe up to the pull spot where Vox will walk up to you to be attacked.  

STEP 3a: Killing Vox

 

This is the breakdown:  Class Roles (expected/advanced)  Raid Timeline.

 

IN GENERAL

You know your class and may know better techniques.  This is an ever evolving section that can be changed but please tell me after the raid when I am a normal person again.

  • Warrior, Ranger, Rogue, Monk:  Stay at pull spot, hit attack.  If you are a large race, start slamming at 40%.  Use Shout for Heal if you are at 2 bulbs and your cleric is not getting making the cut.  Do not run away from Vox thinking you will live! She will just summon you and youll die anyways get in as many blows as you can down to your last tick.
  • Advanced: If you have a discipline .. um use it when your supposed to.
  • Paladin:  Stay at pull spot, start chain stunning at 40%. Don’t be afraid to use complete heal to save the highest level tank or even the Bard who is keeping all alive.  You just might save the whole raid if you get it off.
  • Bard:  Do NOT Melee or her.  Her Ripostes will kill you. Just play your Resist songs or other usefule songs the entire time, keep your tanks alive.   Try to stand under her back legs so that you avoid any AE.
  • Advanced: Play Guardian Rythems and Elemental Rhythems with a percussion instrument which will fill the ONE!! open slot everyone has made by clicking off the first crap buff.  When the dragon tries to debuff it will be hitting the first buff slot but it will keep coming back.  Remind your tanks to open that spot for you when you start singing.  Try also singing Cantan of soothing rather than Chorus of Clarity since it is health and persistent mana regen
  • Wizard:  Load your two best fire-based DD spells and watch for the nuke call (50%)
  • Druid:  Land a Ro's (first one to land, shout it out), then blast away with your best fire-based nuke (50%)
  • Advanced: Use your mana for heals only if a cleric is down and a group is without a cleric. But make sure you still throw in a nuke or two or else she wont go down.
  • Mage:  As soon as melee's engage, load your pet and send it in.  Afterwards nuke with best fire-based nuke (50%)
  • Advanced: Make some mod rods for the the clerics in the group… a few rods here or there can really help save a raid.
  • Shaman:  Backup heals for your group.  One shaman will be designated Malo Make sure you shout it out that she is Malo’ed when its called for…Hotkey this if you are the main Shaman(80%)
  • Advanced: You must also make a hotkey “Assist Lady” and throw quick heals to the melees in groups 1 and 2 in the beginning to help keep all melees alive. By using the hotkey you see who her current target is.
  • Cleric:  Keep your group alive- quick heals are more efficient than CH's.  Your goal is to keep their health as high as possible waiting to cast a complete heal on person while the rest of your group takes a hit is just plain stupid.  Quick heals and practice clicking!  No Complete Heal or Celestial.
  • Advanced: If you are in group 3 or 4 there is no reason why you can throw a heal to the melees in group 1 and 2.  Make an Assist Lady Hotkey so that you know who her target is and throw a quick heal to help keep the entire raid force alive.
  • Enchanter:  You are the most important part of this execution.  ONE enchanter will be designated to tash (80%).  At 90% ALL Enchanters will start casting their level 34 Mana Sieve spell, START to FINISH.  This is the only spell you will cast- it is unresistable and does not generate aggro as much as the higher and  resistable Theft of Thought.
  • Advanced: After Vox is at 70 send your pet in for some action.   Start casting Runes on Vox’s main target when by Assitsing Vox and seeing who is in need.

ONE NOTE ABOUT BLASTING TO ALL CASTERS!!:  You must be within MELEE RANGE for their spells to land.  That means hide until you are called and then come around the corner blazin'.  If I didn’t say it before try standing under her back legs or anywhere else you can avoid her AE.

Bonus Section- A Rogue's Guide to the Bear Pit Corpse Recovery

 

By Nimmbull

 

Pssst!  This part is for the kickass rogues only.  It's inevitable- every Vox raid I have been on (probably over 40), someone happens to fall in the Pits.  Bear Pit recoveries are sometimes a bit difficult (especially if there is full lair and hallway spawn).  Don't shy away from it- this is what you were made for.  Once you get the hang of it, it actually becomes quite easy.  In the map provided above, find the trap marked as number 1.  Make sure you are hidden and follow that ramp down until you plunge into the bear pit.  When you get down there, you will see bears.  Don't worry, they don't see you.  Here's a shot of the room directly under the Number 1 trap (and a couple rogues doing their jobs):  

 

This is where most of the corpses will probably be (unless a caster came down and started flailing wildly).  If you don't immediately see the corpse(s) you're looking for, take a look around this area.  Nothing here sees you, yet.

After you've located the corpses and have consent you can start dragging.  There are two efficient ways of dragging multiple corpses- either by using a hotkey (/target ______'s corpse , /corpse , /target _______'s corpse , /corpse) or by simply walking a bit, clicking bodies, and dragging them with you manually.  I used to prefer the hotkey, but it's too buggy for me and have since just done it manually.

Getting out

Here is a map of the way out.  The X is where you start from after falling from Pit #1:  

 

Ok, to get out it's actually fairly easy at first.  From this room, turn away from the 2-bear spawn and start forward, following the right wall.  It will keep bending to the right and up.  Continue to follow the right wall until it opens up and you see a ladder off to the left.  Go up the ladder.  You'll see another ladder.  Go up that ladder as well.  In front of you will be a ramp heading straight up and to the right.  STOP midway up this ramp.  At this time you will hear what every thrill-seeking rogues loves to hear- the snapping of tentacle terrors.  They WILL see you.  They are positioned across from each other and are impossible to just sneak by without SOW (if you have sow and only one corpse, run straight through the middle of them and they won't aggro most of the time).

 

Let's assume you have multiple corpses and no SOW.  Lead the bodies up past the terrors as much as possible.  They will both see you and knock you back down the ramp.  Press on!  Shortly beyond them is the ledge that drops down directly into Vox's lair.  Before you jump off that ledge make sure all of your corpses are near the top of it beyond the Terrors.  If they aren't you will have to head back down, they will be out of range to drag.  Once you are in the lair, the terrors will NOT follow you- their pathing is bugged and does not allow them to follow you into the lair.  The rest of the raid will be there to res.

 

And remember, anyone suggesting a necro to summon corpses or using potions is insulting all of us.  Make sure to flip em off for me.  =)

If you are ballsy enough to do a Bear Pit CR with a FULL lair spawn (including Vox), then you have probably done a lot of CR before and don't need me to tell you how to get out.  Just be aware that there are 2 tricky terrors on the way back to the King Room and that ALL of the lair spawns see you.   Have fun 8-) you crazy rogues.
