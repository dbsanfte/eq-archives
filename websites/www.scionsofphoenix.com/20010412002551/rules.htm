Guild Rules and Policies - Scions of Phoenix  

 Chapter 1: Guild Structure
Prime Officers

1. These are the 5 elected officers: Guild Leader,Melee Prime,Hybrid Prime,Wisdom Prime,Intel Prime.
2. These 5 positions will be up for election every 3 months.
3. The leader and 4 prime officers set the general direction and priorities for the guild.
4. These 5 elect the “Staff officers” to assist in running the guild.
5. Each Prime officer, if he chooses to do so, may appoint a Class Leader for the classes in his section that can help him manage that class.

The Powers of the Guild Leader and his role in the guild

The role of the GL is to give direction to the guild.
To watch for it's members as well as for the officers.
The guild Leader is in charge of the Recruitment and he can delegate it to an officer but he keeps the last word on recruitment issues.
In the event of an Officer's Vote the GL has the last Word and may overrule the votation.

Staff Officers - (these positions are also voting officers)

  • Tactical_Officer:
    		•  This position leads raids sometimes but primarily sets the priorities for raids that are in the best interest of the guild as a whole. This person acts as a consultant to other members who are leading raids.
  • Treasurer:
    		•  This position handles the guild funds and dropped items from guild raids that either haven’t been given out yet or are excess and will be trade material later. This person will also deal with people outside the guild on the excess trade items.
  • Public_Relations:
    		•  The PR person is responsible for dealing with external issues between our guild & other guilds or individuals.
  • Internal_Affairs:
    		•  The IA person handles internal problems between members. This is the person that mediates between guild members that might have a problem with another member or with another officer. That means there are 9 voting officers that will work for you to help us build and grow strong.

    These officers will communicate with each other and the guild and work together.
    Any officer that's absent for 3 weeks or more or is unable to perform their duties will be replaced.
    We need active players for these positions.

    Class Leaders

    The Prime Officers may choose a class leader, if they deem it necessary, to help them manage a class.
    The Class Leaders may interview recruits, help the prime officer keep track of class attendance at raids, keep an up to date list of equipment for that class, as well as resist gear needs.
    Class Leaders may also advise the prime officer on loot order, but does not make decisions regarding loot.
     Chapter 2: Member Guidelines
    Communication Rules:

    1. Do not get into any negative vocal exchanges with people in and out of game in any form. This includes /guildsay, /ooc, /say, /shout, and messages boards.
    2. Do not use swearing or sexual words in public places (refer to #1).
    3. Do not respond to any threads on any other message boards or other message boards that contain flames about us.
    4. Do not give any information related to the guild such as strategies and scheduled events to any non-guildies.

    Guild Tag:

    1. Before de-guilding yourself, talk to an officer first. Do not /guildremove yourself without guild leader/officers’ permission.
    2. Do not let people outside Scions of Phoenix play your characters with the guild tag. Please /guildremove yourself before letting your friends who aren’t in Scions play your character (refer to #1).

    General:

    1. Respect your guild mates, especially your guild leader and officers.
    2. If you have problem with any guild mates and can’t solve it out in /tell, please let officers know so they can help. Again, do not get into vocal exchange with guild mates in /guildsay. (refer to #1).
    3. Remember and follow the rules.
    4. “Scions of Phoenix” is your only AND primary guild.
    5. You cannot give out information related to Scions of Phoenix to anyone outside of Scions of Phoenix.. This includes information such as guild event time, place and loot.

    1. Level Requirement based on Class and Needs.
       Cleric: lvl56+, moderate desireability
       Druid: No open positions
       Shaman: lvl55+, low desireability
       Bard: lvl57+, High desireability
       Warrior: lvl57+, Low desireability
       Rogue: lvl55+, Moderate desireability
       Monk: lvl57+, Low desireability
       Ranger: lvl58+, Low desireability
       Paladin: lvl59+, Very low desireability
       ShadowKnight: lvl57+, Low desireability
       Wizard: No open Positions
       Magician: lvl60,Low Desireability
       Enchanter: lvl56+, Low Desireability
       Necromancer: lvl57+, Moderate Desireability
    2. We will only accept recruits for classes that we need. Ask the guild leader or an officer in game about which classes have open positions for recruitment.
    3. Applicant must have a Strong desire to engage the high end of the game
    4. Applicant must have Self-sufficiency and Willingness to place the needs of the guild over the needs of the self
    5. Applicant must have a Reasonably predictable play schedule
    6. Applicant must have Good reputation on the Ayonae-Ro
    7. Applicant must have a Minimum of 100 unbuffed resist in Fire, Cold, Poison, and Magic.
    8. Applicant must be guildless, and not applying to any other guilds on server.
    9. Applicant must have a sebilis key, charasis key, and both Kunark and Velious expansion.
    10. Applicant must agree with all Scions of Phoenix rules.
    11. Applicant must not have any conflict with guild members of Scions of Phoenix in the past.
    12. Applicant must know how to get to ALL druid and wizard portals in Norrath, Kunark, and Velious.
    13. Applicant must know how to get to PoF portal in Ferrot and other common zones, such as Sebilis, Charasis, Karnor’s Castle, Kael Drakkal, Siren's Grotto and Thurgadin.
    14. Applicant must know how to play their class
    15. On rare occasions these officers can vote and by a majority override this class requirement to recruit exceptional people.
    16. A guild member is allowed to sponsor up to 2 recruits at a time until the people they sponsor become full guild members.
    17. Once you sponsor someone who is of a class we need and level 55+ that person is invited to the guild as a trial member for 2 weeks while we get to know them. At the end of the 2 week interim period any “full member” has a chance to vote "No". Any member voting "No" will meet with the officers and they will decide whether the recruit really shouldn't become a member or if it's just a question of a personality conflict or minor problem.
    18. If a recruit gets past this stage they're on another 2 weeks probation period. During this 4 week total the recruit has no loot rights on guild raids unless an item is going to rot. This recruit also cannot invite other outside people to guild raids. At the end of the 4 weeks if there are no major problems or additional "No" votes the recruit becomes a full guild member.
    19. If during the trial/probation period the officers decide the guild member is not good for the guild or is found to create problems for the guild, It will be guildremoved instantly.
     Chapter 4: Loot Distribution(from Guild Raids)
    These are the basic loot rules that we must follow.

    1. Loot goes to whom it will benefit most.
    2. Loot is primarily based on Raid Attendance and Guild Participation.
    3. No loot is randomed.
    4. All droppable loot is property of the guild. So you may get asked to return something at any time.

    How this works:
  		•  Prime officers stay up to date on current needs of their classes. They decide who benefits most from an item.
  		• No Drop Loot is assigned to those present adhering to rules 1,2 and 3.
  		• If no officers are present, class leaders determine distribution.
  		• If no officers or class leaders are present, the decision is made based on the consensus.

    Droppable loot:
  		• During a raid, the cleric in the group may loot mobs for the platinum.
  		• All dropped gems and cash items go to the raid leader or an officer to be turned into treasury.
  		• If the group that got the kill doesn't have a cleric, or if the cleric does not wish to loot, the group leader may loot mobs.
  		• Distribution of droppable loot will be determined by the same factors as no drop loot, only it will not be given out on the spot, in the case that the person it will benefit most is not present. Also, it will keep us focused on the task at hand, and not worried about who is getting what loot.
  		• In the case that the raid leader is not an officer, he will pass the loot to the treasury only so that it may be distributed accordingly.
     Chapter 5: Guild Events
  		• Guild Events are announced raids involving 2 or more groups that have the goal of getting epic quest items or spells, quest gems, or equipment for the guild as a whole. This is not the same as a group or two whose purpose is to grind for experience or two groups that decide for example to go to Seb & work D1 & D2.
  		• Anyone (full members only) can announce, lead and organize a guild raid as long as it doesn't overlap with another event already setup.
  		• It's preferred that you post it on the Events board a day or two ahead of time AND announce it several times on guild chat. Please check the Events board to avoid conflict with someone else's guild raid. To ensure the success of your guild raid coordinate it with the Tactical Officer and try to keep it in line with the goals of the guild. If you need advice organizing your raid feel free to discuss it with the Tactical Officer.
  		• Please do not just make these raids to push for "your epic quest". At this point we're concentrating on epic quests for the clerics and enchanters, spells, quest gems, better equipment to make the guild stronger as a whole unit. That doesn't mean we won't ever do your quest, it just means we're pushing to get the entire guild stronger FIRST.
  		• Every time you assist to a guild event you will be awarded a "Raid Assistance" point.

    During official guild raids we expect everyone to follow these rules:
    1. Be on time at the designated time and place that is set for the raid. Either camp your character at the designated place the night before or get on early to make sure you get a ride. If you are not there at the start time, you will not be ensured a spot in a group. If you know that you will be late, speak with the raid leader to make arrangements. If you do not make arrangements to arrive late you will not attend. No Whining Allowed.
    2. Have any equipment or resist gear that is needed on you at the appointed time. We don't want to hear people asking to borrow gear or have jewelry made for them at the last minute. It's your responsibility to be prepared ahead of time. NO GEAR - NO GO!!
    3. It is highly recommended that everyone have several small coffins on them. They stack & weigh next to nothing & there's no excuse not to have them. We have enough DE's in the guild to get these coffins ahead of time for people - so ask your favorite inky to pick up a few for you. Tanks should have a gate potion or two on them for raids such as Hate. Don't just expect a wizard or druid to park for days in a plane until you can log on.
    4. No one invites other people from outside the guild along unless you clear it with the Raid Leader first. New members on probation aren't allowed to invite guests on guild raids.
    5. If a situation arises where there's a problem during a raid with another guild or individuals outside the guild let the Public Relations person or the officers deal with it.
    6. We expect everyone to conduct in a decent manner and not inflame a situation by shouts or OOC talk or tells to the individuals we're having a conflict with.
    7. All members must respect whatever decisions or compromises the PR officer or other officers come up with. If you have a problem with that discuss it AFTER the raid please. These situations are usually difficult at best and not made easier with people yelling at each other.
    8. Once a raid has started the organizers will make groups and keep you informed on tactics & important orders. We expect everyone to STFU and listen. We realize sometimes people are zoning or miss their group list so these orders will be repeated. At this point please control jokes, horsing around, repeated emotes. This helps not only the organizers but also everyone else who's trying to pay attention. Feel free to relax and mess around before or after a raid but once we're in NFA mode please try to control yourselves.
    9. The Raid Leader or the person assigned to make the groups will list the group leader first in a grouping list. Please do not argue with them about whom you're grouped with. We realize some people have slow computers or poor connections and the Raid Leader will try to make sure these people aren't made group leaders. Each group leader is responsible for letting the Raid Leader know the status of their group - when they're all together, mana status, rez status, LD status, lost corpses, etc. - any important information will be relayed to the Raid Leader by EACH group leader. If the group leader goes LD on a raid the remaining members will regroup ASAP and that new group leader will relay their status to the Raid Leader. This means that NO OTHER PERSON except the group leaders will be giving information, talking on channel or sending tells to the Raid Leader.
    10. No Dueling without permission from guild leader or raid leader.
    11. Do not question guild leader or raid leader’s decisions during guild events. If you have questions, suggestions, or comments about any decisions, or people, please contact officers or guild leader ‘after’ guild events
    12. Members must attend to guild events if you are online during guild events’ periods and your assistance is requested. If you have more than 1 suitable character for the guild event, consult your guild leader or raid leader about which char you should bring to guild events. Remember that every member is needed at guild events. If you are not needed in the guild, you shouldn’t be in the guild in the first place.
    13. Do not wander/gate/evacuate/camp/zone/leave camp without guild leader or raid leader’s permission. This includes running to get puller’s corpse and/or pulling. This also includes leaving early in guild events to play twinks somewhere else (refer to rule#12).
    14. Do not give out commands in guild events in any way. Only guild leader and raid leader can make such decision. Only authorized puller can issue a moving command. Again, if guild leader and raid leader are AFK, officers will be in charge.
    15. All rare gems, normal gems, treasures looted during guild event period should go to guild banker. If guild banker is not around, please hand them to officers or guild leader at the end of the raid.
    16. If you acquire an item from a guild event, you must turn in your old item of the same type (e.g: Crystaline Short Sword vs Lamentation Blade), unless your old item is still useful to you at some point (e.g: Bone caster robe vs Haste robe). This ONLY applies to items awarded to you from the guild, not items you got on your own time.
    17. Nobody loots ANYTHING without officer's/Raid leader's permission even if they think it will rot.
     Chapter 7: Member Status
  • FULL Member
    		•  The status of full member entitles the member to be eligible for loot. In order to keep this status the member has "score" 2 "Raid Attendance" points per week.
    Not doing so for 2 weeks (less than 2 points per weeks during 2 consecutive weeks) will demote him to Regular status
  • Regular Member
    		•  The status of Regular member entitles the member to be eligible for loot, in line after FULL members. full members that don't play regularly enough to get the 2 raid attendance points per week during 2 weeks in a row retain this status until they reach the 2 points per week again.
  • Probation Member
    		•  The Probation status is for Members that have passed the "trial period". .
    These members aren't eligible for loot.
    In order to become a Full member, the probation member has to have 2 consecutive weeks with 2 raid points on each.
  • Honorary Member
    		•  These are long time members of the guild that don't have the minimun level to assist raids but are given the same rights as a Full member (been long enough in the guild to earn it).
    Nobody can attain the "Honorary" member title from now on.
  • Inactive Member
    		•  The Inactive status is for Members that haven't been online for quite a while or quit playing EQ but pop every now and then to say hi to us =).
     Chapter 8: Notes
