Ezra sipped at the fine red wine. She was ever cautious of the eyes on her from around the table and never took a sip unless someone was watching. She drank tiny amounts, more concerned with the way she displayed the “kissing” of the glass than the flavor of the wine. (Though she did think it was an excellent choice) With this particular drink, she seemed to have finally snared Edwrin Obsidian’s focus causing him to stumble slightly over his words.

“Well Dark Lord.” Ezra finally said, putting the glass back on the table at the exact 1 O’clock position it was supposed to be presented. Aware that a serving man was close enough to see, she placed her fork and knife side by side diagonally across the plate signifying that she was finished and the servant should remove the plate. This had been second nature to her as she had grown up as a member of the Noble class in Neriak. Though she knew not all people were so well trained in proper etiquette it annoyed her when the servant hadn’t bothered to remove the plate and chose to instead ogle her. She broke custom and shot the servant a wicked look. He didn’t notice.

However, Sarkam did notice and whether he knew proper etiquette or not had at least figured out what Ezra had wanted. Having given the servant a few seconds to respond, Sarkam stood from his seat and strode menacingly to the man. In a swift, almost graceful motion, Sarkam removed the plate from before Ezra and simultaneously grabbed the offending servant by the collar, never breaking stride. Once out side the dining room a muffled yelp came from the man as the sound of a porcelain plate shattered over his head. From the sound of the commotion, Ezra suspected the powerful warrior had beaten the man within an inch of his life.

Ezra smiled at Edwrin. The dark bard, leader of the Brothers of Obsidian, was not attempting to hide his rage at the insult to his guest. Edwrin picked up Ezra’s unspoken acceptance of the apology Sarkam had extended with his actions, and calmed himself slightly. Ezra suspected that Sarkam left the servant alive for only one reason. She wondered if Edwrin would like her to heal the man for him before she left.

“Forgive me, Dark Angel, please, go on.” He said to her, regaining his bardic charm.

Ezra would not insult the man further by pretending the she had not been offended, she simply nodded, telling the man she had accepted the retribution as satisfactory compensation. “I have told you that I am not so convinced about your god, Obsidian, and will continue to follow the ways of my people and my father, Innoruuk. The Children of Darkness are about filling this world with the dark powers and dreams of the gods of the greater races,” Ezra paused, throwing a soft, innocent smile at Edwrin. “I mean, the races of Trolls, Ogres and the Teir’Dal. Your heart, dear Edwrin beats of the same desires as mine and we share a common enemy. The Children of Darkness is proud to be the first to show our dedication to our alliance with you and support the Brothers completely.”

Edwrin stood and bowed slightly to the Teir’Dal woman. Then strode purposefully to where Ezra was sitting. He offered his arm and she accepted it. “Thank you Dark Angel. Eltherion’s army is large and powerful. He has many allies as well. He has laid out a strong threat to bring his storm down upon all who oppose him. Our way is true and I am certain of victory. However, casualties will be numerous.” He said as he led her down the hall to the ballroom for the evening’s entertainment.

Ezra smiled warmly at Edwrin and snuggled closer to his body. “Then I shall simply have to remember to keep my umbrella with me.”
