Chapter 1

Hail all, and well met!  I am Valmor, Ranger of Surefall Glade and I have a tale or two to tell.  Kick your feet up by the campfire, gaze up at the stars and share in a mug of this fine ale!   For all concerned, I'd best start at the beginning of beginnings...

I was born and raised in the area surrounding Surefall Glade, in the West Karana plains, not far from the city of Qeynos.  The half-elven product of a Human father and an Wood Elven mother, I would spend nearly every free moment roaming the countryside - not that I had a great deal of spare time. My family worked on a small farm, producing wheat shipped to the nearby cities for baking, and most of the my early days were spent laboring away, helping to keep my family fed and clothed.

In my early teens the land began to call me; I knew that the farming life was not for me.  I began to train in secret, fashioning a makeshift wooden sword from instruments found around the farm.  I travelled slowly, examining the earth around me, learning how to follow the tracks of the local animals, as well as which plants and herbs were safe to eat.  Before long I could forage for food and water, and I began to think that it would be possible to live off the land like the Rangers that I had looked up to for so long.

At sixteen years of age, my destiny was chosen for me.  I woke in the night to horrible screams and cries, and as my eyes adjusted to the darkness I spied a horrible dog-faced creature walking towards me, sneering.  It spoke in the common tongue with a raspy, hissing voice.  "You damned half-elves have spent too long on Blackburrow land!  Now you will all die!"  It followed this comment with horrible howl and bark, and lunged at me.  In a flash I had whipped my wooden sword out from under my cot and scored a lucky hit on the surprised creature, taking out its eye!  It ran out of the house, yipping, screeching and clutching its face, and I scurried about in the darkness trying to find my family.

I could see outside through the crude windows of the house, and in the moonlight I saw the pack of gnolls heading off into the distance.  I realized then that the gnoll that was in my room was much smaller than all the others; luckily for me, it must have just been a small pup.

Running into my parent's room, I stopped in horror as I saw their bodies twisted in horrible, unnatural positions; they had been ruthlessly slaughtered in their beds.  Lighting a lamp with trembling hands, I saw them bathed in their own blood with their blankets soaked in the deep red fluid. A quick look across the hall told me much of the same; my brother and sister had been murdered as well.  I shot outside and saw others outside their homes, cradling dead and wounded family members - it seems that the gnolls had come in quite a force.  All told, more than fifteen people had died and countless others were wounded before the monsters had been driven off.  I went inside to see my family again, and saw that my father still grasped a bloodied, rusty short sword that had belonged to my uncle.  It had done him little good, and I carefully removed the blade from his hand.

The next days were spent grieving over my loved ones, and I buried them in the woods nearby the farm.   I did not trust cemetaries, as they recently seemed to be running grounds for groups of the walking dead.  The forest was as good as any resting place I could think of.  I made up my mind - I would train as a Ranger in Surefall Glade, if they would have me, and I would exact my vengence upon the horrid, murderous gnolls of the Burrow.
