Drachenreiter charter


ear Traveler, Dear adventurer, Dear Friend....

Drachenreiter is a guild which was established out of the idea of having friends throughout the realms of Norrath who want to have fun, enjoy the game and look for like-minded people to conquer the monsters in our beloved land.

As life is not steady and storms are followed by sunshine this guild went to all the good sides of life, but also had to face the dark sides of Norrath.

As we don't have anything to hide, beside the secrets of our friendship, it is written for all of you who are willing to learn more about Drachenreiter - The guild and the members behind it.
If you are interested in joining us please read the following carefully. Here we stated what we are up to, our goals are and what is expected of our applicants and full members.

Happy reading!

ode of conduct

Quicklinks:
loot policy - members - raiding - guildlife - communications - application rules

Drachenreiter is a friendship-based guild. If you are looking for an 'uber'-raidingguild, we are probably not going to fit that need for you right now.While we certainly are after large targets, and are willing to try and die finding out tactics, we are not in any kind of race or competition with anyone.

Although we are a German-founded guild, our raid chat is English, simply because we have members from various countries like Holland, Denmark, the USA and Austria. Some understanding of German might help to cope with guildchat and jokes, though, but it is by no means required.

Important note:

We are member of Karana Guild Central (KGC).

Therefore, all rules of the KGC will apply to our members, and we will follow all the KGC-policies regarding scheduling and rotations.
Do not intentionally killsteal from anyone, intentionally train anyone, or scam anyone in trades.
Verification of such instances will be grounds for immediate removal from the guild.

oot policy

Our loot policy is based on a kind of "Need before Greed" system and not using any point-system.
During a raid loots will be distributed by the officers, considering:

-Is the item an upgrade for the player, the last time player got an item, proof of willingness to attend raids, and the overall impact on the strength of the guild.-

Therefore, if your main goal in the game is to acquire the best of items for your character in the fastest possible time, we are probably not going to fit that need for you.

aiding

We intend to become able to attack and win against the larger targets all over Norrath including PoP (future goals: Tier 1 & 2 Gods - like Bertox,
Saryn, Mithaniel Marr, Zeks, etc... and LDoN raids.

However, our tactics will not consist of throwing characters at the target until it goes down. Instead we will focus on using skill, strategy and unity, aka working as a team to accomplish our goal(s).

Normal raiding time is between 7pm to 11pm (CET, GMT +1), but we do not want to focus our guild exclusively on high end raiding. There's always room for fun, quests and general sillyness besides our raids. We are running raids three times a week.

Personal items and class components:
This means our clerics have Peridots, our mages have Pearls, and all other classes have enough food, water, arrows and components to be self-sufficient during raids. While we can provide for the costs of components AFTER the raid (via the message board) we expect you to come fully prepared to a guild event every time.

uild-life

While it is not required, we want to encourage all of our members and applicants to group and hunt with guildmates in order to get to know eachother. This will also familiarize everyone with their guildmates strengths and weaknesses, and allow us to learn to work as a strong, cohesive fighting unit. Listening to, and the following of issued raid orders is mandatory. If you have problems staying at your keyboard or with working as a team you will cause problems for your fellow guildmates, and are perhaps better off in another setup.

We expect you to help all your guildfriends in all matters (quests, tactics, leveling, guidance, porting, ress'ing, etc, etc).

However, don't expect to get a group at login immediately, just because you wear a guild tag. Other guildies might be in a solo camp, or after a specific target, or doing something balanced. Don't whine and log an alt, but try to make/find your own group and do some public relations for our guild, or just let the guild know you are available for whenever a spot opens.

Your alternate characters will also carry the Drachenreiter tag, to stay in contact with the guild. We do not tolerate multiple guilding.
We understand the need to play alts from time to time, which is why we don't require 100% of your time on your primary character, but having ties and/or responsebilities with other guilds than Drachenreiter with alternate characters is not permitted.

Be proud of your Guildtag and show it, use /roleplaying and not /anonymous.

Being a member of Drachenreiter, we would like you to:

-To think about what you can do for your guild and not what your guild can do for you.

-Help to improve the guild and the reputation of Drachenreiter.

-Before you sell stuff in the bazaar try to keep in mind if there are any guild members who might need this item. This especially goes for resist gear and spells.

Note: Some droppable item(s) you will receive during a raid will remain guild property, and handed down to other members as soon as you get a better item, to make us stronger as guild. If this is the case, it will be stated before distribution.

ommunication-structure

The main communication form in Drachenreiter is the Ezboard. All memebers will have access to our private board, and for all others our open board is located here.
The main reason for this is privacy, not to hide something, but the prevent the guild from minor or major damage due to any conflicts that may arise. We urge you to use our board for any problems, questions or irregulations. If no one knows about a problem, nothing can be fixed....
You should read the message board and update the informations about your character regulary.

Also, all major and minor guildbelongings are distributed on this ez-board and therefore it is mandatory to have access to this board.

If you can not log on this board you are asked to inform any officer to help you to get instant access.

eneral application rules

Due to the amount of applicants we are attracting, we had to think about new standards in recruting.
As we have reached a very powerfull raidforce by now, which makes us capeable of killing new targets, we only want to add new members if they fit to us in matter of social skills as well as in experience and gear. As you all can imagine it is sometimes very hard to keep the balance between having fun and meeting friends as well as improving the guild.

So, in general we made new standards for applicants, where they can add value a to the guild immediately, rather than we have to put effort in them to raise them up to the level of our coremembers. For rl friends/ family exeptions can be made, on a case by case basis.

The standard requirements for applicants are now:

LvL: 65
AA: 35
Equipment: ToV/Ornate equal
Flags: Trials, BoT

The officers will judge all applicants prior to getting tagged, so you must post a Magelo profile with your application.

- To apply for membership the applicant has to contact any officer, and/or leave a message on our ezboard. Knowing a full member that can speak up for you sure helps.

New members will also be on probation for a one month period, during this time they should be grouping with guild members, and attend as many scheduled guild raids as possible. You should play your Main charcter during this time.
Your alternative character will not be invited during your probation period..
Please note that the probationary character will be the Main character in our guild.

All members and applicants should own the following Everquest-expansions:
Kunark
Velious
Luclin
Planes of Power
Lost Dungeons of Norrath.
The expansion Legacy of the Ykesha is not required.

Map knowledge

A basic knowledge of the zones we are raiding in is required. Since the 'net is full of maps, tips, message boards and information we expect our members to be able to move swiftly to any given location on Norrath and the Planes, should an opportunity arise for a needed kill/mob/quest item, etc. While we can't expect everyone to have a full knowledge about every single zone in Everquest we do want our guildies to be able to reach spots of interest in a reasonable time, or at least the knowledge of where to be for pick-ups and directions.
