Character Idealization

Before you venture into the magical world of Everquest, take some time to think about what you plan to do in the game. If you have no idea about what Everquest is before you play, then you can check out this site for information on the different classes and races. It is very important to have a good idea of what kind of character you want before you actually create one. If you want to be a fighter, then you will probably want to go with the Barbarian, Ogre, or Troll race, who combine brute force with skilled use of melee weapons. Fighters are great as front lines men that break the ice of a war, and protect the mages in the back. However, many people will soon become dissappointed of constantly hitting things with weapons, as oppose to using the eye-popping variety spells that make casters so powerful. Mages are known to be the most powerful characters in Everquest when they reach the potential of using devastating spells (level 20 or so). The races that are most suited for being casters are High Elves, Dark Elves, Gnomes, Erudites, and Humans.

When I used to play Ultima Online, I started out with a swordsman because someone told me it was easy to become one. I also liked the idea of being a ruthless warrior that could slice people's heads off with a slash from a viking sword. By the time I reached Master Swordsman (90 out of 100 skill) I was about to throw my Ultima cd out the window. Then I realized how much more fun it would be to become a mage since there are so many different powerful spells to keep me busy.

In Everquest, warriors are more the instant gratification types of characters because they are so much easier to develop than casters. Casters take longer to develop, but when they reach their peak, they are the most powerful, most respected, and most envied players in the game. So when choosing what fighting role to take, keep in mind that warriors are powerful in the beginning due to their large hit points, but good casters are generally much more interesting to play.

While most characters will want to either become warriors or magic users, many will want to strike a balance between the two by choosing to become either Paladins, Shadowkights, or Rangers. These characters are especially good because they provide the melee power needed in the newbie stages with strong spells when they reach higher levels. This does not mean that a Dark Elf Shadowknight at level 20 is necessarily better than a Dark Elf Mage at level 20 simply because pure Mages have much more powerful spells than warrior/mages. To find out more about classes and races, refer to the classes or races sections in this site.

Faces

When you are creating your character, you have the choice of a variety of faces. Do not take this option for granted because your facial expression will have a great deal of influence on how you are viewed by others. Also, other characters will remember you more if you have a face different from most others. So when you are thinking of which face you want on your character, think also about what kind of role you want your character to play (evil, good, decietful etc.).
