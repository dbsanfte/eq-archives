Guilds
Benefits of Joining or Creating a Player Guild
- You recieve a last name, which distinguishes you from other players not in your guild. It is an honor to have a last name since only players in guilds have them.
- You are able to participate in guild wars without loss of experience points upon death. Anyone in a guild will tell you that guild wars are the most fun you can have on Everquest.
- Once you join a guild, you get a chance to meet and hang out with EQ players in the same guild.
- Overall, guilds are great because you become more involved in the Everquest community, and you assume a distinct role depending on the charter of your guild. This guild role helps you enjoy a more fulfilliing level of role-playing.
 
How to Create a Guild
1. Write a Charter for your guild about the purpose of your guild in the land of Norrath.
2. Get ten players that agree with charter and would want to be in guild.
3. Work out symantics of guild. Who is Guildmaster? How are the ranks determined? Rules of Guild? Recruiting Procedures? Guildmember responsibilities?
3. Then, email Verant at eqguilds@989studios.com that you want to make a guild. In the subject put the name of your guild, and in the body of the email put the charter and the name of the ten or so players that will be joining your guild.
4. After the Guildmaster sends this to Verant, each player that wants to join the guild needs to send an email to the same address. Put your guild's name in the subject, and in the body of the email put the character's name that will be joining the guild and the character's account information.
5. When this is done, Verant will review your guild proposal and if approved will email the Guildmaster to tell him to see a GM next time he is online.
