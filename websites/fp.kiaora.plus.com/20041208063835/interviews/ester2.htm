Ester the Tester Interview page 2

Everquest and the message boards

Q Has having your own testing message board on the Everquest site helped you much in your role?

A The testing message board has opened up a line of communication between the players and my team, along with the dev team, that I feel was needed. As we do not handle balance issues but only bugs it allows us to find the problems and fix them without as much discussion.

Q How do you feel you are received by the audience there? Posters often seemed ready to be suspicious of comments by Gordon Wrinn when he held the position of lead punch bag, do you find they are relatively ready to believe you?

A I am allowed to be a little more blunt. I am not customer service, so I can send them over to Absor. I am not trying to make them accept a decision, I am telling them if something is broken or not. Not broken is not open to argument. Whether or not it is a good idea may be, but that is out of my responsibility. Some people think that I am rude or abrupt but they are the minority. I feel that most people appreciate the bluntness.

Q Do you actually have time to play Everquest for your own enjoyment? If so what class/race is your main?

A I don't play as much as I used to, but my Human Monk is my main. I also have a Barbarian Rogue, a wood elf Druid and a little baby Ogre SK.

 

Testing... testing..123

Astonishingly Verant apparently have a tester/programmer ratio for Everquest of "about 1.5 to 1", and here I am struggling along with a ratio to 1 to 7 or so.... Ester describes testing as her "first love".

Q As a tester myself, I *shudder* at the thought of testing something as vast and complex as Everquest. We both know 100% testing is impossible, so how do you prioritize what bugs reports get looked at first, and indeed when verified, what sort of priority they have to get fixed?

A Well our first priority is new content. Things going into the game correctly avoids new bugs. Then we read the /bugs from test server first and then from the rest of the servers. We pay close attention to the PvP servers also. I go through the QA board, one of my team goes through the eqtesting emails and another goes through the General Discussion boards. The GM's bring us things and we rely heavily on the Guide's reports of bugs.

As for fixing them, that is the dev team's decision. I can make recommendations only.

Q Is all your testing done "in game"?

A Our testing is done on Test Server and occasionally on Live Servers. We also have some internal only servers where we can test things like the PvP code. We try to stay off of the live servers whenever possible, but occasionally we have to check things there also.

Q In your interview with Lum the mad I believe you commented that testing fishing was probably the most tedious thing to test, as you can only stand there without moving what other things are particularly nasty to test?

A Foraging isn't fun as you have to stay in the same zone. Testing trade skills can be tedious as we go through all the recipes. I think the worst is testing the boats.

Q What are the most FUN things to test?

A New zones, looking at them for the first time is always exciting. Doing raids with our beta testers is also something we enjoy doing.
 

If EQ women readers are remotely like me you will probably now be buzzing with additional questions (I know I am!) such are the drawbacks of trying to interview someone several thousand miles away! A hearty thank you from all at EQ Women for talking to us Ester :)
