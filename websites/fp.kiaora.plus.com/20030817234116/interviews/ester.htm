Ester the Tester

An interview with Ester Sauter for EQ Women.

This is the first in a series of interviews with a variety of "EQ Women".

 

Great was my jubilation at hearing that Ester "the Tester" Sauter had agreed to an interview for EQ Women. She is of course the most prominent woman at Verant as far as Everquest players are concerned. As it happens I am in the same profession as Ester so my interest is particularly keen.

Who is this Ester person anyway?

Ester (who is 28) started in the testing field working on Novell's NetWare. She joined Verant in August 1999 as GM. It is perhaps not know to many (until now) that Ester was GM Camii of Povar and then Innoruuk. After that she transferred to testing, working "a little" on Sovereign but her "main focus has always been Everquest". Here is a little more about Ester in her own words:

"I went to college at Brigham Young University but did not graduate due to health reasons. I live here in San Diego with my two mini dachshunds Mutt and Jeff who are the loves of my life, and my cat Bannshee. I am the oldest of two, and my younger brother works here at Verant as a programmer. I grew up in New Hampshire, lived there until moving to Utah for college. I was in Utah for 8 years before accepting the job here in San Diego."

Q Are you a gamer generally? What sort of computer games other than EQ do you enjoy?

A I am not hard core, but I do game. Right now I am playing Mech Warrior. I enjoy the sim type games and my most recent ones were Pharoe and Zeus. I like AOE [Age of Empires], we used to play here at work multiplayer.

Q Do you enjoy science fiction or fantasy books? If so could you name a couple of favourite examples and what you particularly liked about them?

A I absolutely adore science fiction and fantasy books. Picking favorites is difficult. If I can pick authors. For fantasy it is Tolkien, Eddings, Salvator, I like Jordan and McCaffrey. Card, Brooks, Asprin, Science Fiction, E. E Doc Smith, Azimov and Heinlein are by far my favorites. Zahn and Stackpole are my favorite Star Wars authors. Though I also enjoyed the one by Salvator. These are the books that were my bedtime stories as a child. I love the fantasy type futuristic books as they take me somewhere else, escapism I guess. The worlds that these and other authors create are rich in detail and I am always eager to find out 'what happens next'.

Q Do you find any difficulties arising from simply being a women in a male dominated industry?

A Not really. I grew up with Computers, my father working on them my whole life. I am used to being in the minority. I feel no discrimination against me being a woman. If nothing else when I travel with the company I get my own room!

Q Are there many other women working at Verant? Have any of them got technical jobs like your own?

A There are not very many women working here, but there are a few. We have quite a few women GM's, at least one in Tech support, a few on the EverQuest development team, and I am in testing. Other game teams have a few here and there are many women in the marketing, sales, Human Resources and Legal departments. We have women programmers, editors, many women artists. The Art Director for EverQuest is Rosie Crosgrove. Our head of Human Resources is Tina Cruz.
