Deus Ex

First person shooter? Adventure? Strategy? RPG? Somehow Deus Ex manages to combine some aspects of all these genres into a very playable and addictive hybrid (with no xp penalties!). I played through the entire thing in a week and a bit, so if I have a complaint it must be simply that it wasn't long enough!! Like Everquest, I loved it for it's immersiveness. I was engaged enough to really care about the storyline, the characters, and to be genuinely terrified when creeping around corners straight into enemy bots...

The Story

You are code-named JC Denton, agent for UNATCO, an anti-terrorist organisation, set in a not so distant corporate future in a world afflicted by a man made virus. The introductory movie is really quite mystifying until you have completed the game, at which point you realise you recognise every character in it, and the locations. There is a strong plot, with engaging characters, and it has to be that element that pulled me so strongly through the whole game - I had to know what might happen next! Actually this brings me to one of the interestingly non-linear aspects of the game - during cut-scenes you often have a choice of things to say. Some of these end up having serious consequences later on. Tip: tell Jaime to stay at UNATCO!(I didn't!).

I don't want to give away much about the plot obviously, but you start out fresh from the academy, a kind of cyborg with abilities enhanced using nano-technology. Working for UNATCO you undertake several missions to foil the plans of the NSF terrorist organisation, which is trying to steal barrel of Ambrosia, the only known cure for the plague ravaging the world. Your brother Paul Denton, already an agent, is apparently working on different missions with the same objective. As the story unfolds you will find yourself in locations as diverse as New York, Hong Kong, Paris, and a whole slew of military and medical research bases, including a rather challenging under-sea base. Corrupt corporations, terrorist organisations, governments, the triads, the Illuminati and even UNATCO itself may fall under suspicion as the story unfolds...

Gameplay

The game is divided into missions with primary and secondary goals. You are given goals by other characters, sometimes mid way through a mission, so it is very important to speak to everyone! Completing goals, and exploring thoroughly, results in the allocation of skill point bonuses, which is where we see the role playing aspects starting to show their face! The skills can be distributed as you see fit, each of which starts out untrained and can be raised to mastery. I would definitely advise getting that computer skill up, as it saves much hassle and enables you to do fun things like control enemy gun turrets (oh dear I am blood thirsty! 8P). In addition to skill points, you can develop you characters augmentations. Each augmentation slot has two possible alternatives, and they can be upgraded for increased effect. In your "leg" slot for example, you have the choice of a speed enhancement or run silent. Using those augmentations takes "bio-energy" however, and you will need to recharge...

Each mission always has more than one way of being completed, though the choices are more limited for some missions than others. The ability to sneak around means that you do not have to behave like a bloodthirsty lunatic throughout the entire game, and you are also able to incapacitate many of the enemy without actually killing them.(Note: someone really should tell all those evil megalomaniacs to secure their ventilation systems a bit better...)

Complaints?

Yes, two of them!

  1. You can't play a woman. Well what's new /sigh.

  2. It was not long enough! I was completely absorbed by the storyline and game, and was sooo disappointed when I completed it. The compulsion to play simply vanished.

Other drawbacks include the fact that the game runs best on glide cards, so it can sometimes get a bit choppy on other types. I've tried both though and actually played most using a geforce card which was quite bearable. It also seems to feel very, well, grey! I think this is called atmosphere. Atmosphere it certainly has in wads, but I do wish someone would come up with a dark dismal future that was not quite so dark and dismal.

Verdict

I liked it. I liked it ALOT. Great fun, absorbing, addictive, excellent gameplay. Just wish you could choose to be a woman, bah!
