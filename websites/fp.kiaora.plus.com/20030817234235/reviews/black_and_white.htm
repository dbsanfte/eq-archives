Black and White

It's about time we had another game review here at EQ Women I thought, and there is simply no contest about what it should be... Black and White from Lionhead is simply the strangest and most absorbing game I have played since errr... Deus Ex I guess :)

Need a break from EQ and have a penchant for both strategy/sim and roleplaying? It's an interesting mix from the creator of Populous, and is the only "god game" I have enjoyed since Civilisation II (yes I have alpha centauri and Civ III, but it was just more of the same). Basically, you play a new God whose actions will determine whether you are seen as good or evil, or something in-between, and at the same time you must wrest control of the belief in the hearts of villagers under the influence of rival Gods. You also have a creature who you can teach to perform miracles, impress villagers and fight other creatures - and it too starts out a blank canvas, capable of good and evil deeds, playfulness and greed, kindness and aggression. There is a simple system of punishment and reward, plus you can encourage it to be interested in particular things...

Black and white has a pretty steep learning curve, and even moving around feels pretty strange at first, as you click and grab your way around the beautifully rendered landscape with a disembodied hand, but you do get the hang of it! You have to tend to your villagers desires enough for them to keep believing in you, either by means of miracles or by actually directly helping with things like farming and forestry. You can also expand your villages with a building program when you have enough resources, though I have to say that one annoying feature was that simply finding enough room on the landscape for necessary buildings like graveyards and workshops could be quite hard at times.

But it's not just a beat the other god and win game (though you can play like that by choosing skirmish games). The main game has a strong plot with many sub-quests for you to perform, often introduced by your good and evil consciences... There is a particularly amusing one in the first land involving some villagers who want to sail off in a boat; they reminded me somewhat of the pirate gnomes in Vellious, though it has to be said that the humans of Black and White look remarkably like halflings! The only annoyance here is that if you get the urge to start a new god, the only way I have found of doing so is to start a new full game, and as the first 30 minutes or so is heavily tutorial guided this is rather a pain...

Among the many novel features of the game, one that stands out for me is the closest you get to a shortcut to casting a miracle, which is by using what they have called gestures. The idea is that you have to draw a spiral shape in mid air to indicate that you want to cast a miracle, then trace out the shape of the spell - a sort of W shape for the water miracle for example, or the S shape shown in the screenshot here for the food miracle. It's a real test of your mouse control I can tell you! Once you get the hang of it I think it adds very nicely to the atmosphere of the game...

Like EQ, Black and White is very time-consuming, so if you are looking for a quick filler before your guild raid this would not be your first choice! It does have the advantage of a pause button however... It is also very challenging - but not too challenging in my opinion, a nicely balanced and multi-layered experience of indeterminate genre. I also liked the humorous aspects rife in the game - I love it when your creature dances with the natives (the tiger break-dances with them!), and the dancing cow still seems funny but maybe that's just me :)

I can't really end the review without pointing out what must be obvious from the screenshots: the game is graphically superb, and not too clunky either! A feast for the eyes on top of everything else! Ok I'll stop raving about it now.............

Kiaora
