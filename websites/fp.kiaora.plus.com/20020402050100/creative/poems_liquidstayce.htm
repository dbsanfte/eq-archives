Poetry by Stacy.

Waltzing Bones


I am the iksar warrior
crouching in the dirt
watching the wind pull the dust sheets away
revealing
two naked skeletons amongst
the creeping scorpions
in the Field of Bone

paste joints creaking out tired symphonies
tired symphonies of strange music
mandolin and lyre strong with tongues
grey, dust coated they dance
twining themselves
anxious hands
confused muscles
eyes
and teeth
tied together with ageless curiosity
they sought, found
uncomprehended
melting of flesh and body
Melding spirit
Soul
the copper taste of lust
just like blood
just like rust
the flaking corrosion
that never quite eats away
the heart

-Muggwamp, The Iksar Warrior

I Gather These Bones



I Phase into my darkness
as a pulse of light
blinking closed

I weave into matter
and it is beautiful
this robe of nightime burial
My bones and roots and shadows
are intimate
with the wet smell of Norrathian fossils
beneath us

I dig into the dark
and fertile soil
searching for my body
I gather these bones
shaping them gently
beneath my fresh skin
molding myself around them
healing the pieces together

I make a sculpture
with these glowing white shapes
of light

My time has come
to offer
from the quiet of the Earth's womb
a new life and another battle

-Velrgirl, The Necro

The Dream


In the Dream:
Love enters our hearts
Lust invades our innocence
Our bodies tangle together
In a knot of youthful ecstasy

Awaken by my Ranger sister for duty:
The dream dances into my consciousness
Teasing my sense of reality
He is gone again
Chilling my soul

Four Seasons Later:
Others pass between us
A flickering facade?
Love fades to friendship
While I secretly yearn for old warmth

Liquidstayce Arborvitae, High Priest

The First Seal, Nameless
