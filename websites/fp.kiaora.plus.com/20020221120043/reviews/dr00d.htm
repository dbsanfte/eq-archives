Balancing act continues at Verant – An end to the Dr00d?

Verant today announced new adjustments to be made affecting class balance. Readers made remember the “anti-camp code” which was rumoured to have been introduced last year at certain spawn points which had the effect of lengthening spawn times when a player was within a certain distance of a spawn point. Most players believed this to be a myth, but this was indeed implemented at certain spawns such as Emperor Crush and Ambassador Dvinn in Crushbone. The latest changes are refinements based on this feature, which have been introduced to help balance the spread of class and race in game and encourage grouping. In effect the anti-camp code will have more or less effect depending on what class and race the players are and how many of them there are.

A spokesperson from Verant, who wished to remain nameless, said, “basically people were fed up with kill-stealing farmers, so we decided to do something about all those nuke happy wood elf druids”. A table describing the increase in spawn time based on class and race when solo appears below.

As you can see from the table the hardest hit would be woodelf druids and dark elf necromancers, followed closely by dark elf magicians. The class/race combinations standing to gain the most are Erudite Paladins and Shadowknights. If grouped the adjustments are cumulative but halved for every group member, so a group of 6 erudite paladins would see a large number of spawns! It will be interesting to see how preferred group make-up will evolve in the wake of these changes.
