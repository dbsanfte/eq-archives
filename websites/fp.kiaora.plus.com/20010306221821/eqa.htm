EQA (EverQuest Anonymous)

     Lots of people have heard of Alcoholics Anonymous, but not too many have heard of EverQuest Anonymous.  It’s a new addiction that's catching on rapidly.  It is a new experience that was first released in March of 1999.  Since then it has undergone several upgrades to give the addict an even bigger high.  It affects mostly males, but women have also been known to suffer the effects.  It has no age limits, but targets those in their 20's with credit cards.  Unfortunately the craving can hit day or night, at work or in sleep.  It can sometimes be satisfied by sampling for a few hours, but the craving begins again as soon as they stop.

     EverQuest addicts can be considered dangerous, especially new addicts.  They search out new victims and have a very successful strategy.  They often begin by mentioning a few positive things, how dynamic it is, how much better it is then anything else on the market.  Conversation grows until the new victim wants to see what its like.  Sometimes the new victim watches the addict, the braver ones jump right in and try it themselves.  For the new addict the rush is not immediate.  It develops slowly as the addict continually points out new highs.  The new victim of course also wants to experience these highs and comes back for more.  The first few times are free.  The addict usually supports the new victim in the experimentation stage.  Shortly the new victim turns into an addict himself or herself and because the craving has grown so strong they start spending their own money to feed the craving.  Because the new addicts have not yet suffered from any of the negative side effects they often begin the recruitment process and it cycles again.

For longer-term addicts they have often experienced one of many of the negative effects of the addiction.  These can include isolation for long periods of time, neglecting basic human needs (food, sleep, hygiene), and deteriorating relationships.  When you consider that some alcoholics binge drink for several hours this seems like a lengthy time, except for EQ addicts.  According to a pole done by EverQuest Stratics over 50% of addicts have had a binge last 7-18 hours.  In other words, most EQ addicts are spending lengthy periods of time feeding their addiction.

EverQuest Anonymous offers some tips for those addicts who are suffering many of the negative affects. 

1)        Join the EverQuest sub-culture.  Since you’re reading this article on the Blood of the Twilight website I would imagine if you are taking part in the EQ sub-culture to some degree.  The wonderful thing about this addiction is that there is lots of others out there just like you.

2)        Recruit a partner.  For this I recommend someone you’re interested in sharing long periods of time together and someone you may end up sexually involved with.  This way your relationship can grow and prosper as you show each other new highs of your addiction.

3)        Position yourself.  Where do you like to spend most of your time?  If the answer is in the bathroom, then feed your craving there.

4)        Get rid of anyone who refuses to try.  Believe it or not there are those who refuse to experiment.  Probably wiser for them.  An addict should surround themselves with other addicts.  This way while not high they can be discussing the next hit, without boring others.

    EverQuest Anonymous does not encourage addicts to quit.  It has been proven that the withdrawal symptoms are too severe.  They can include severe headaches and grouchiness, blurred vision, inability to adapt in a healthy atmosphere and increased likelihood of other substance abuse (Quake, Diablo II).

So for those of you out there who think they have an EQ problem the truth is, you don’t have a problem, but anyone hassling you about it does.  The best thing for them is recruitment, get them addicted too.  Knowledge will be the key to them leaving you alone.
