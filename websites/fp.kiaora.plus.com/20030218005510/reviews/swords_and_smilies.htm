Swords, Sorcery and Smileys :0

I spotted this statistic recently in OnMagazine, "According to the IDSA, 43% of all gamers in America are women. Studies show that among online gamers the percentage is even higher: 50.4%.". That seems amazingly high compared to the sorts of numbers people used to bandy about with Everquest, and I certainly always assumed women made up less than a third of the players in EQ. In support of the statistic is another story that highlighted women as being far more likely to use smileys and emotes in emails and test messages. Whatever the reality, it seems certain that figures like that will put women firmly into the eye of game designers : perhaps at last there is an opportunity for women's interests in gaming to be heard.

...With respect to age, 70% of the most frequent PC gamers are age 18 and over, and a remarkable 40% of them are over age 35; console gamers skew slightly younger, with 43% of the most frequent players under 18, and 57% over 18; only 21% of the most frequent console gamers are over 35, significantly less than is the case with the PC. Thirty-nine percent of the most frequent users of the PC for games are female, and 61% of the most frequent PC gamers are male.  For consoles, 74% of the most frequent users are male, and 26% are female...In addition, 53% of those who purchase PC games and 46% of console game buyers are female

So what do we want most? I intend to start running some polls (particularly in the members sections) to gauge genuine feeling about various issues concerning gaming, and the results will be collated on this page.

So what are the assumptions to examine? Certain issues seem obvious:

 - the ability to choose how your avatar looks (i.e. not just being able to play a woman, but being able to choose how revealingly they are dressed etc)

-The social dimension is more important to women (a sweeping assumption and it will be interesting to see if any polls refute that).

Others perhaps less obvious:

- Though absorbing games are fun, games that squeeze your essential time with children or family are not so good.

- We seem to prefer more rounded games than simple first person shooters (though we can enjoy those too!), and fantasy settings are more popular in general than science fiction.

Is there more? well this article is to be completed by YOU, please add to the message board thread below with ideas and vote in the first of the polls...

 

  • First Poll Results : Do you badly need that choice to play a woman? Basically everyone answered yes (and that they would play a female if they had that choice) with the exception of one male high elf wizard with an identity crisis.

  • Second Poll results: How important is control over your attire in game?
    I would like full control over character clothing appearance.  61.5%   
    I want the choice to wear revealing or non revealing armor!  13.8%   
    I really do not mind how my character is dressed.  4.6%   
    The male characters should be made more nekkid to match!  20.0%   

Well it looks as if we do care about what we look like, is anyone astonished?
