Thief of Time
Terry Pratchett

Thief of time is the latest in Terry Pratchett's Discworld series, featuring one or two familiar characters (Death, Susan, Quoth the raven, Igor, among others), and a host of new ones, including 2 saffron clad monks (one with a broom), Time, lots of auditors and a rather insane Clockmaker called Jeremy.

The plot.

Jeremy is approached by a mysterious woman called Myria Lejean with a commission to build a clock that will measure the tick of the universe. Unfortunately, what Jeremy doesn't realise is that once started, this clock will stop time for the universe as a whole. It is up to Death's granddaughter Susan (a schoolteacher of special talents) and two History monks (one with special talents of his own) to find a way of thwarting this evil plan, that originates with the life hating auditors.

But is it any good?

I gobbled the book up in about 2 1/2 days: it's funny, there's actually a good plot with an exciting climax, and there are those little Pratchett gems strewn throughout, such as a yodelling stick insect in the Garden of Five Surprises, which also contains bonsai mountains (only Pratchett could come up with bonsai mountains for heaven's sake), and the fifth horseman of the apocalypse, who left before they got famous and now runs a milkround in Ankh-Morpork. My favourite cameo has to be Qu, a wonderfully silly saffron clad version of Q from the James Bond movies, so that's where my excerpt is coming from!
