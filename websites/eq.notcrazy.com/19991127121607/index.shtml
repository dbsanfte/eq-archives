Brad on spawn times and Kithicor
Thursday, November 25 22:38:21 PST -- Catalyst

Brad "I know where my towel is at!" McQuaid made two posts on the news group alt.games.everquest today. One about the changes to Kithicor, and the other about the tweaks they have made to zones, and spawning times, as well as what some of the EQ team is working on now. Brad's remarks are in Bold:

And I have to agree with the people who are complaining about it, the change is nonsense.

I went through a few times immediately after the change, actually, and didn't see ANYTHING, but I believe it was because the zone was actually crowded with high level folks at the time (I never did a who to find out, I'm just assuming). However, this time, to play it safe, I decided to head through in broad daylight. Didn't matter. Almost through, and two zombie troopers and a dreaded wolf assault me. Little 20th level me.

I BARELY made it to the zone alive, and I nearly died from the troopers DOT spells on the other side.

Bad design decision for several reasons:

Kithicor is a funnel. The way to get more people into it was NOT by making a bunch of uber mobs for people to hunt, but placing interesting quests and locales in it (ala the tiger quest). It's a funnel for practically EVERY halfling that wants to go ANYWHERE (as the Gorge was a death trap and a half) other than Misty Thicket, as well as mid level folks (like myself) who want to go from High Hold to Freeport, or mid levels who want to go from Freeport to Qeynos (or High Hold). It's a transit point, and until recently, a very well used one.

The MOBs in their currently make the Hill Giants and Griffins of the East Commonlands (which I never had a problem with, personally) seem like a cakewalk...because you could see them. In Kithicor, due to the mist, by the time you see a MOB, you're in it's aggro range.

I heard someone mention that a Guide had spoken with them about it and had agreed that it was overdone, and that something was being done about it...here's hoping that's true.

We're going to make it only dangerous like that at night.

What is the deal with this?

Everfrost, formerly the best place to hit level 18 now runs out of mobs at level 14. This appears to be a clear example of Verant nerfing spawn rates to prolong the time taken to level. This is so shortsighted as to be flabberghasting.

Hey, McQuaid, the HORSE HAS BOLTED. There is no point shutting the stable door now, all the Powerlevellers have hit 50 and either given up already or started a new character (and in the case of the latter why nerf their level progression when they demonstrate they will play on but with a different class).

The only people that this is *really* hurting are new players. Wouldn't you *hate* to be starting your first character today with nerfed spawns and no twinking gear to make up for that (by hitting harder mobs)

We haven't touched spawn rates there, and we are not across the board messing with spawn rates -- there's no reason to. I can only speculate that perhaps there are more players in the region now, killing more things, making it appear there is less to kill.

The only areas that come to mind that we changed recently were Kithicor and the area outside of Paw, and Paw itself. The team is working on populating and tuning the Plane of Sky now, not engaging in a secret conspiracy to reduce spawning rates.

In fact, I can't recall since launch any negative change to the general spawning rate of a zone. Sure, we've lowered the spawning rate of specific NPCs (usually named ones, ones involved with quests and such), but not to an entire zone.

There is also, btw, no general conspiracy to slow the powergamers down. When they or anyone else find an aspect of the game that allows progression at a significantly higher rate than was planned, then yes we definitely try to address that. When they or anyone else finds a method of defeating a region or NPC or set of NPCs that is contrary to how difficult that process was intended to be, yes we definitely try to address that. But, contrary to what some believe, we are not 'terrified' at the general rate at which powergamers (or anyone else) progress in EQ and we rarely if ever make sweeping, global changes to the game to slow them down, thereby negatively and unfairly penalizing the average or casual gamer.

Lowering the spawn rates of entire zones would be a sweeping change. Addressing specific tactics and/or aspects of the game clearly not balanced with the rest, like cherry picking NPCs with impunity, kiting, unresistable lifetaps, etc., are changes that unfortunately are sometimes necessary to keep advancement rates fairly constant across the board. Balance is the key word here.

The game as a whole has only become easier since release. Positive changes we have made, for example halving the experience loss death penalty, combined with the increased knowledge players have, the help other players get from higher level players, and the deflation that has naturally occurred in the economy which makes more powerful items more obtainable to lower level players, only make the game easier.

In summary, we are very happy with the general rate at which players are advancing (most players are around levels 20-25, right where we expected them to be) and only when we see a specific aspect of advancing (amidst the myriad of ways and places you can advance in this game) that is out of sync with the majority do we take action.

-Brad
