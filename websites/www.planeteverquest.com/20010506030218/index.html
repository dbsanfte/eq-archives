/Assist
5/4/01, 9:01 PST | Kwill

Absor addressed the /assist command on the Developer's Corner, with some helpful hints for macros.

For a fight Absor suggests this sequence:
/target Caladon
/g assisting %t
/assist
/g with %t, going to make %t rue the day!

You need the %t in the /g parts of the macro to replace the target's name. The only place you shouldn't need it would be with the /assist part.

The only reason that I can think that this might not work would be if there is a lag time between the time you target Caladon and the time he actually becomes your target. If the rest of your macro runs before you've achieved Caladon as your target, then it would fail.

You might try adding a /pause 10 after /target Caladon. Pause uses increments of one tenth of a second, so a /pause 10 will have the macro pause for 1 second, that should be long enough to acquire the target.

Macros verge on programming, and I'm not a programmer. There are a lot of tips in the supplemental manual on your EverQuest directory (eqmanual_suppliment.doc).
For healing targets, you can use this sequence:
Loegan wrote:

I use /assist %t in raid situations to heal the current target of the mob we are fighting.

For instance, if we are fighting Velketor the Sorcerer I target him first, hit assist and it targets the tank he is hitting. Cast heal, hit assist again which puts me back on Velk, hit it again to target the tank he's hitting, repeat.

With this method clerics/druids/shaman can heal non-group members quickly during raids.

And that will still work. Just take the %t out of your macro. You don't need it, and it won't work in there anymore.
Alan
A player asked, "Is it possible to re-add the zone-wide assist range backs into game?" Absor answers:
Unfortunately that long range on /assist was being abused. It was used by people to skip content and barriers. Assist will probably never go back to that range again.

I can check into the current range and see if it can be extended slightly, perhaps just a bit beyond spell range, to help out those with bad connections so that they can attain a target at about the time it comes into range. But that would be the largest increase I would expect in the range of this feature.

Keep in mind, this feature was only added to improve a character's ability to target things, not as an addition to the power of transportation spells. We want to retain its value as an aid to targeting.

Roleplaying
5/4/01, 8:43 PST | Kwill

The Roleplaying Thread on the EQ Developer's Corner is still going strong, with Absor putting in his two cents about how he role plays....for instance, he buffs guards (even though it doesn't actually buff them) in the role of an evil character.

It takes some strength of character to roleplay continually in EverQuest, and it is rare to see a pure roleplayer anywhere. It just doesn't work to say a dark elf will never group with a paladin, for instance, unless you want to spend all your time sitting on the wall roleplaying and not getting experience!

Let's give credit to those who do try to inject some color into the game and do a little role playing, or even just develop a personality with their character, although that may not be the "role" of an evil or good race or class. It certainly makes the game a lot more fun to hear an ogre or a troll talk a little differently than a high elf, or group with someone who has a sense of theatre about the whole world of Norrath.

Will we see a role-playing server? That is still up in the air. Certainly it has gotten as much support from players as many of the changes that have been put in the game lately. I know I, for one, would miss the few roleplayers I encounter if they all left to go to their own utopia where no one /shouted out for all of South Karana to hear, "Wow, have you seen what's on TV tonight?!"

Wizards to be Masters of Transport
5/4/01, 8:17 PST | Kwill

Absor explained in the Developer's Corner in the EQ Forums why wizards have more transporation spells than druids:
Regardless of past perceptions or texts or class descriptions, Wizards are becoming masters of teleportation. They will probably never get foot speed enhancement spells, that sort of travel will always be the real of the Druid, Bard and Shaman.

The reasoning behind this is pretty simple, really.

Druids have few weaknesses as a class. In fact, with the recent addition to their high level healing abilities, Druids are a very well-rounded and balanced class (though this is not the result of any final balance decision about the class, and things might be changed). Their ability to transport people is pretty darn good as it is. Not only can a Druid pop you across the ocean, he can speed up your running and get you to the final destination pretty quickly.

Wizards right now have two specialties, damage and teleportation. They should be the masters of arcane magic and ripping holes in the fabric of space. Making them the masters of teleportation not only makes a lot of sense, it also adds a dimension to the class.

Yes, we are moving toward making Wizards the master of arcane transportation. This seems like the right direction to take the class.

From the EQ Developers Corner
5/4/01, 7:43 PST | Kwill

Absor had a few comments about spells:

Draught of Jiva and Voltaic Draught the Same?
These spells are not the same. Draught of Jiva has a stun effect. Absor says "The higher level spell is not a better spell in all cases. It does not obsolete the previous spell."

Translocate Box:
After half the players said they wanted it, and then half said they didn't (what if you fell asleep on a raid and didn't click your box?), Absor says, "Translocate has had a confirmation box added. Going forward all translocate spells will have a confirmation box...I would imagine that it is just as unlikely that someone will fall asleep and need to be sent home as it is that someone will take advantage of their ability to send someone home against their will. For now, we're going to stick with the box. But I'll bring this up at the next tuning meeting and see if we can use some other method to make this work."

Teleport to Bind Spot Spell?
No way says Absor: "A spell like this will probably never be created in Norrath. There are far too many ways that such a spell could be abused. We're certainly not set against the convenience that this spell might add. But there are far too many ways to use a spell like that to trivialize zone content and the challenges that are presented in the game world."

Read all the comments in the Forums on the official EQ site.

Scam Alerts
5/3/01, 7:56 PST | Kwill

Absor reports two scams that have arisen in connection with the news of the Luclin Expansion:
I'm sorry to report that there is yet another jerk out there trying to steal your passwords.

Someone has created a site claiming to be taking applications to beta test Luclin. This is a fraud. Please, under NO circumstances enter your login name or password into any web site like this. We will NOT be doing any such thing, and any announcements about entering any beta will come from our web site, not some site hosted by another service provider.

I'm obviously not going to give out the URL for this site. But they've managed to steal our art and layout, so you'll know it when you see it. Avoid this site.

I'm sorry that people are trying to ruin your enjoyment of our product. And I urge you to be extra careful whenever you put any personal information out on the web.
And the second one:
Someone is asking people to download and run a file (perhaps called eqtest103.exe) in order to participate in testing the new expansion.

This is a scam. Do NOT download and run any files like this.

Most likely this person is trying to steal your password, or perhaps do something even more devastating to your computer. Please, don't download or run files from people that you don't know. If something sounds too good to be true, it usually is. We won't be sneaking around passing out files like this.
As always, be careful, folks. The best cons play on your trust.

GameSpy Article On EQ
5/2/01, 15:34 PST | Delrachnid

GameSpy posted up a recent editorial on the state of EQ written by a veteran player. The author touches on many points which you may find familiar. Here is a snippet of what he had to say:
It's a disturbing, but nonetheless trend, that the way EverQuest was intended to be played is slowly dying. Some may argue that EQ was not meant to be played in any specific way, but the majority of people I've either spoken to, or seen voice their opinions, would say that EverQuest is a role-playing game and thus should be played as such. Each time I log myself on to EverQuest and play my fantasy character, in Verant's computer-generated fantasy world, along with thousands of other fantasy characters, I see something that is just not right. I see before my very eyes that a percentage of players just don't seem to embrace the role-playing or the community "togetherness."
