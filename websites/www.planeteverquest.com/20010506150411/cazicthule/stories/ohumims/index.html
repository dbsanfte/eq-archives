Ohumim's Revenge
by Norad, Guildmistress, Disciples of Innoruuk

I glanced casually around at the mystical missives that bombarded me as I worked, the Disciples had grown quickly, perhaps too quickly, ahh well the Father’s hatred is never sated. One missive captured my attention, Ohumim, a rather cunning Ogre with whom I had worked on many occasions. Apparently an incompetent Ogress had given him leave to take possession of her equipment, excellent. I marveled at how Ohumim was able to connive so many people, I had always preferred the straightforward approach to such things, duplicity was always the method of choice for those too weak to take what they wanted. However, one could never argue with Ohumim’s results, seventy platinum into coffers at the cost of a moment’s duplicity was nothing to scoff at.

I did not give much more thought to the missive, inundated as I was by requests from the rest of the Disciples for this, that, or the other thing. Grobb and Oggok are so lacking in the niceties of a truly civilized culture (not to mention the general lack of Ogre hospitality) that I am forced to Neriak simply to make sure that the Disciples remain well equipped. On my return journey to Grobb from one such journey to Neriak I was with Ohumim, selling our wares (it irks me so that I must stoop to mercantile avenues to generate revenue for us) when the Ogress (I believe Zugtess was her name) began an attempt to appeal to my merciful nature (something that thankfully surfaces only once in a great while) and spurn Ohumim from the Disciples. I asked her why I would expunge so profitable a member from our inner council, to which she answered, theft and duplicity.

At this point I began to go into a lengthy diatribe concerning the nature of Innoruuk and dark elven gods in general, but thought better of it considering the simplistic nature of the person I was dealing with. However, it did not surprise me when moments later, a human cleric spoke up on the behalf of this ignorant simpleton. A human! I was disgusted by the simple sound of her dialog! Obviously the Disciples count a small number of the "good" races amongst their numbers, but they are thralls, and treated as such, they know their place! Anyhow, this backwards individual insisted upon deliberating with me at length on the nature of fairness, fair play, and honor. After educating this individual on why the father had brought me into the world I bid her adieu and continued upon my pleasantly liberating and thoroughly evil way.

As you can imagine, after the annoying conversations I was forced to endure I was interested in learning the particulars of Ohumim’s trickery. It seems that Ohumim had adventured with these individuals briefly while waiting for a few of the Disciples to run errands, buy supplies, etceteras. While adventuring with this group Ohumim became annoyed by the Ogress’ excessive use of mana resources for offensive magics, while receiving none of the magical empowerment and healing for which Shamans are known, a slight he would never have had to endure with one of his own. As Ohumim came closer and closer to death in successive encounters instigated by the same over eager Ogress he began to contemplate ways in which to take revenge for the wonton ways in which the Ogress had threatened his life. Finally the Ogress began a fight she had no chance of winning and Ohumim, choosing revenge over heroism and honor (like any good Disciple when a non-brother is in danger) let her die. The amusing part of the entire exchange occurred when the Ogress told Ohumim that she did not know how to get to the Oasis of Marr from her home in Oggok.

Now keep in mind that Ohumim and I had been making the trip between Neriak and Oggok since before we had half the capability of the Ogress, hence Ohumim became even more disgusted with an individual he considered worthless already. It was then that a disturbance in the world threatened to leave the Ogress’ belongings to the whims of the gods and Ohumim decided to take his chance at complete revenge. Now, the chance was slim that the Ogress would let Ohumim recover her equipment, why should she? Her purported closest comrade and adventuring partner was in the same group with them, but Ohumim felt he had to make the attempt. Much to Ohumim’s surprise the Ogress consented with almost no resistance to allowing an individual she had known for barely an hour to take possession of her belongings! Of course at this point his revenge was complete, the equipment quickly exchanged for cash and quietly added to the guild coffers. I still marvel at the blind luck of the entire exchange that Ohumim should stumble across an individual so easily duped into an obviously detrimental course of action! As you can imagine I was proud almost to the point of tears the duplicity of the entire exchange easily rivaled the time Aahzmandius swindled the gnome, but that is another story.

-Norad, Loyal dog to the Father
