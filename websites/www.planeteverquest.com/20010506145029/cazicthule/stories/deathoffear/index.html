The Death of Fear
by Norad, Disciples of Innoruuk

I wiped the moisture from my brow, to detached from it to actually be pleased that it was blood on my hands, twas not the blood of my enemies anyhow, it was the bizarre blood rain indigenous to the home of Cazic Thule, it was blood tainted with fear. We had been forced to ground several times by the mad god and his minions, yet now here we stood, watching his mad pacing from a safe position, his feared Kinghts lay butchered amongst the lush vegetation, his vaunted avatars broken in their temples. He was a gargantuan beast, of a size and grandeur one would expect from a god, ogres were dwarfed by his height, four arms sprouted from his spine covered body, covered in bulging muscle and toughened sinew, ending in massive, wicked looking claws. Truly this was one of the most fearsome opponents we had ever faced.

Clug barked orders to the troops, organizing them for our final assault on the lord of the realm, his orders were received and obeyed with swift precision. The clerics and shamans wove powerful protective magics around all of us, although should Cazic Thule single one of us out for death we knew that no amount of protective magic would stay his wrath. Finally all was in readiness, Clug tromped into the bushes and returned moments later with dark haired wood elf whose hands had been bound behind his back. I know not exactly what Clug said to this elf, I believe his name was Zildjin, but whatever it was the elf simply nodded and cast a forlorn look upon the mad god in the distance. The elf had the bonds at his wrists cut and Clug pushed him forward with a rough hand, turning to our gathered brothers and sisters to address us as he forced the doomed elf forward.

"Dis one be da furst ta die to da will of Cazic Thule!" he shouted, his words followed by the roar of the gathered forces, "Den we attack!!"

There was a palpable tension in the air as we watched the dark haired elf run across the field towards the meandering god, then almost without warning Cazic Thule roared, "ZILDJIN!". His voice was like a thunderclap, and to one sensitive to magical energies such as I it felt as though a shockwave had rippled through my mind. The corpse of the dead wood elf was trampled into the blood soaked grass as our forces surged forward in their desperate rush to engage the fearsome opponent, I think I would have smiled at the poor bastard's demise had I not been distracted by the more immediate threat at hand.

Our warriors struck zealously at the god of fear, but they were as gnats to a horse, flung wantonly about by Cazic Thule's flailing arms, yet still they returned to the fray over and over to strike desperately at our enemy. Shamans and magicians brought poison, disease, rained sheets of white hot metal down upon him, our wizards and druids called lightning from the skies, and yet still he rampaged, paying no heed to an assault of magical energy that would have already slain a lesser foe. Again the lord of fear bellowed, "JEKHAR!", and the black ascetic who had been leading our expedition was struck dead. I glanced nervously to Vamael, he was calm, the blood rain blending with his armor, making it glisten in the twilight, I was steadied by his composure and moved forward and continued to call down the lightning that did not seem to affect the monster in the slightest. Obviously the masses of warriors hacking madly at the god were beginning to affect him for I saw him stagger, the first sign of weakness, our forces were further emboldened by this sign of impending victory, attacking with renewed zeal. Cazic Thule was not one to shuffle from the immortal coil lightly though, again he roared, "ZITH!". Zith's body shivered briefly, I watched in horror as his eyes rolled over white and his final breath left his body, and then it was over, Zith's broken body joined the litter of other corpses that lay scattered across the bloodstained landscape.

Then it came, almost without warning, the lord of fear staggered again, this time an almost drunken, slewing stagger that scattered warriors from around his feet like so many ants. His body fell with a thundering crash, our forces scattered from the path of the falling behemoth, I fear some of the halflings may have been caught beneath the crushing bulk, ahh, such is the curse of their stunted legs, I tried not to laugh too hard. A bloodthirsty roar erupted from the survivors, Balamor stepped forward to the corpse with his blade and carved the heart from our fallen foe, we feasted well upon the heart of fear that night, and rejoiced at what was obviously the first of many gods who would succumb to our power.
