<span class="headline">John Smedley Promoted</span><br>
<span class="byline">11/20/02, 10:26 PST | <a href="mailto:kwill@planeteq.com">Kwill</a></span><br><br>
<span class="newsitem">Congratulations to John Smeldley, who has been promoted to President of Sony Online Entertainment:<blockquote>SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT APPOINTS JOHN SMEDLEY TO PRESIDENT<p>

-Company Announces Promotion of Additional Key Executives-<p>

SAN DIEGO, CA - November 18, 2002 - In an effort to further strengthen its organization in the online games market, Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE) today announced the promotion of John Smedley from senior vice president and chief operating officer to president, effective immediately. In this capacity, Mr. Smedley is responsible for all of SOE's operations and functions. Mr. Smedley will continue to report directly to Yair Landau, vice chairman, Sony Pictures Entertainment. As chief operating officer, Mr. Smedley has been instrumental in the continued growth of the company's flagship product, EverQuest® which currently has more than 430,000 subscribers, as well as expanding SOE's presence onto new devices including the PlayStation®2 computer entertainment system, the Pocket PC and wireless handsets. <p>

"With the continued success of products like EverQuest and the anticipation of top title launches on the horizon, we are confident that this senior staff will drive the company's continued growth," said Yair Landau, vice chairman, Sony Pictures Entertainment. "The SOE team has done a phenomenal job creating interesting and dynamic online worlds and have played an important role in the emergence of massively multiplayer online gaming. We look forward to more of the same groundbreaking results from the newly structured management team."<p>

To further fill out the executive management staff, the role of chief operating officer will be assumed by the current chief technology officer, Russell Shanks. Mr. Shanks assumes responsibility for SOE's international expansion strategies, product development studios, and oversees all software games including EverQuest® II, EverQuest® Online Adventures™ for the PlayStation®2 and PlanetSide™, the first massively multiplayer online first person action game for the PC.<p>

In addition, John Needham has been promoted to chief financial officer. With more than 10 years of financial management experience, Mr. Needham will be responsible for overseeing all of the company's accounting and financial operations.<p>

Finally, Adam Joffe, currently vice president of information technology, assumes the role of chief technology officer. In this role, Mr. Joffe is responsible for overseeing the entire IT network and gaming infrastructure which hosts as many as 100,000 simultaneous EverQuest fans. Mr. Joffe will also be responsible for overseeing the rollout of an expanded online network to host such anticipated titles as Star Wars Galaxies and PlanetSide.<blockquote>So now you know who to write if you get really mad about something!

<span class="headline">Today's Patch Message</span><br>
<span class="byline">11/19/02, 13:54 PST | <a href="mailto:Stryker@planeteq.com">Stryker</a></span><br><br>
<span class="newsitem">Today's patch was a fairly large one, mostly just fixing a few minor issues with PoP. The new music window has been added also, see <a href="http://www.planeteq.com/#PQN399882" target="_top">Kwill's post</a> for more information on it.<p<blockquote> 

November 19, 2002 3:00 am<br>
------------------------------<p> 


** Spells **<p>

- Affliction Efficiency should now work on Damage over Time spells of level 61 and greater.<br>
- Imbue Disease should be scribeable by Shaman at level 63. <br>
- Corrected the range on some Planes of Power group spells so they will affect the group, not just the caster.<br>
- Corrected the animation for Katta's Song of Sword Dancing. <br>
- Corrected the animation for Spirit of Eagle. <br>
- Increased the affect radius of Radiant Cure so that it will affect group members.<br>
- Changed the method of obtaining the Planes of Power 'imbue' spells. Speak with the fellow that gave them to you before for more information.<br>
- Fixed a bug where casting Levant while on a horse could cause crashes.<br> 
- Fixed a problem that was preventing pet owners from controlling their regular pets if they had a Wake the Dead pet in use.<br>
- Fixed a bug that caused characters to summon corpses with similar names rather than the intended corpse.<br>
- Fixed a bug with Unswerving Hammer of Faith and Flaming Sword of Xuzl that prevented them from disappearing when they should.<br> 
-Temporarily removed the Scarecrow Change Form component from Nights Dark Terror to correct a faction issue. The Scarecrow form change will be reinstated in the near future.<br>
- Fixed the spell gem colors for some Area of Effect spells.<br>
- Corrected more issues with spell turn-ins. You should no longer get vendor sold spells, and all the spells you get should be at the appropriate level.<p> 


** Items ** <p>

- Removed the level casting restriction from Necrotic Divining Device.<br>
- Fungus Covered Scale Tunic is now Magic. <br>
- Removed the "Pending Lore" tag from the Shralok Pack. There is no current plan to make this into a lore item. <p>


** Alternate Advancement ** <p>

- Corrected the description for Host of Elements. <p>


** Tradeskills ** <p>

- Fixed the invisible forge in Felwithe.<br>
- Added Collapsing Fletching, and Collapsing Mortar and Pestle. Added recipe books for them. <br>
- Added Vah Shir Spirit Stone recipe. Check existing recipe note for information. <br>
- Added a few forges to Crystal Caverns in Frostone. <br>
- Added the Ignore Trivial Loot flag to several temper components for the Firiona Vie server. <br>
- Removed the Lore tag from the crafted planar steins. <Br>
- Added a level limit and recommended level to some Planes of Power jewel crafted items. <br>
- Saltwater Seaweed is now tradable. <br>
- Paladin and Shadowknight gnomes should now be able to gain skill above 200 in tinkering.<br>
- Attempting to create planar sheets of metal will no longer destroy the Tanaan Smithing Hammer. <br>

<br>
<span class="headline">Developer's Transcript from CR</span><br>
<span class="byline">11/15/02, 8:56 PST | <a href="mailto:kwill@planeteq.com">Kwill</a></span><br><br>
<span class="newsitem"><a href="http://eq.crgaming.com/" target="_top">Casters Realm</a> has released the first of the transcripted sessions from November's fan faire.  <a href="http://eq.crgaming.com/viewarticle.asp?Article=4899" target="_top">The Producers and Developer's Panel Discussion</a> answers many player questions about gameplay and the PoP expansion.<p>My prize for the the best eyebrow raising answer is this one:<blockquote>"Will Epics be upgraded at least? They aren’t that useful now."<p>

Some will always be useful. Please keep in mind, though, that these are Kunark items. You might want to look at them in that perspective, as we’re now three expansions later. The fact that some of them are still as useful as they are means they were probably overpowered initially.</blockquote>Got that, folks?<p>Other questions and answers of interest:<blockquote>Q. Can we delete our keys yet for keys on our keyring?<p>

A. It is okay to delete them once you get a notification that the key has been made permanent, by using the key.<p>

Q. With the change to Monks have they achieved the result you wanted?<p> 

A: At this point we don’t think that we have achieved what we wanted with Monks. Right now it seems to be having too much of an impact on Monks who aren’t the top 5%. We’ll be going back and looking into it some more.<p>

We were really happy with the projected results our internal testing had for the changes, but that just didn’t pan out when it went onto the live servers.<p> 

Monks won’t be going back to the uber-tanks they were, but there will be more changes made to try to more accurately reflect our intent.<p>Q.Where do warriors stand in the grand scheme of things? 
<p>
Most warriors tell us they are pretty happy with their advancement and the inherent advancements they get from 60 to 65 that they don’t have to spend any AA points on to acquire, We’re pretty happy with how warriors are right now. <p>

A warrior isn’t measured on DPS alone, but they can take so much punishment and have always been designed to get hit points above and beyond any other class. They are really a solid class for both offense and defense where other melee do sway towards more offense, or give up some of both for magical abilities.<p>Q. What role do Paladins play now?<p> 

Paladins are designed to not only melee but to have fast, fast heals. Paladins are excellent in situations where a character in your group is going to die RIGHT NOW, and that’s one of the roles we’ve tried to carve out for Paladins.. We are trying to create niches for classes and Paladins are there as a Warrior of sorts who can take damage but have spells more suited to a Battle Cleric with their group heals, fast cast heals, lay on hands, stuns, etc.<p>

Q. What role do Shadow Knights play now? <p>

Shadow Knights are excellent secondary tanks. They have better damage output than a Paladin and their agro control spells are very useful as well.<p> 

It’s hard to sum up a class in this short a time, everyone plays them differently as well. We’re generally pretty happy with class balance as it sits right now and the roles the classes play. However, class balance is a never ending task, and we’ll continually evaluate how the classes stack up against one another as well as against the environment in the future.</blockquote>There are a lot more questions and answers of interest, so be sure to check out the whole article.
