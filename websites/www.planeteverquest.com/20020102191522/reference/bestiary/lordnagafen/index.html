Dragons 101 - Defeating Lord Nagafen



This is a brief outline of how to take on Lord Nagafen. These strategies have been contributed by several adventurers on the Cazic-Thule Server. The information below is what worked for them, this will not necessarily work by the book for you .. play with these strategies and develop your own. This is only a guideline to use if you’ve never killed the beast before:
  1. Be Mentally Prepared: Badaf gives some words of wisdom here, he says “Get started early and be prepared to stay late. And also be ready to die a few times with no exp res.” – Make sure that you are ready for this. Plan this raid only if you have the time and don’t mind dying a few times over.

  2. Defense: Vaziel Oloth’Colbauth says that everyone going on the raid needs to get both their Magic Resist and Fire Resists as close to 120 or above as possible. Assassin adds a list of items which are suitable for raising Fire Resist and suggest getting these items if at all possible:

    • 2 rings (+20)
    • 2 bracers (+12)
    • 1 helm (+20)
    • Total = +62 fire resist

    Badaf states that anyone over level 49 need only concentrate on Fire Resistance items as Nagafen’s fear spell is much less effective on anyone of that level.

  3. Selecting Your Brave Adventurers: Assassin gives the following outline for the members of this raid including classes and levels (Badaf reminds everyone that you can’t be over level 52 to kill Lord Nagafen!):

    • Melees of level 45+ are useful. Anything below that for a melee and they should be cut off for lag reasons etc.
    • Clerics and bards are very useful no matter what the levels pretty much so any that are level 40+ or so are useful.
    • Nuking classes should be of 49+ since nukes are highly resisted by Nagafen.


  4. Setting Up Groups: Assassin gives us great insights on setting groups up:

    • Bards are great for Dragon runs because of their resist songs. Group 1 bard per "super group" if you happen to have any bards along (I'll describe a super group later on).
    • There should be 1 cleric per group or if you have an abundance of clerics then put 2 in the "super groups".
    • Now try to make super groups. Take your 3 highest level tanks that can do the most damage (Warriors, Monks, Rangers, whatever level 52 tanks that have good equip). Add the best cleric, and a bard to complete the group with room for 1 more assistant healer. This can be a druid, shaman, or another cleric if there are extras. This group should be able to last the whole fight or at least a very long time.
    • Two healers per group. Main healer should be a cleric with either a druid, shaman, or cleric for backup. A paladin can also be used as an assistant healer if need be. The main reason for 2 healers per group is because the cleric will mainly be casting group heals to counteract the area effect nukes from Nagafen while the back up healer can heal any tanks that may be taking melee hits from Nagafen.
    • Nuking groups. These are the big finishers to the fight. Melees do the initial damage and take the brawn of the damage, but once Nagafen hits about half health or so, then the Ice casting nukers come and drop their load on him.


  5. Setting Up – Getting Ready – Buffs: Both Vaziel Oloth'Colbauth and Assassin speak about debuffing prior to buffing:

    • Once all FG's are cleared then begin debuffing everyone for buff preparation.
    • Have rangers or any non-cleric, non-enchanter, and non-shaman cast 4-5 absolutely worthless buffs on everyone to help against Nagafen's area effect dispel. For example Ultravision, Underwater breathing, DMF, feet like cat, poison resist, disease resist, cold resist, etc. are perfect junk buffs against Nagafen.
    • Once everyone has been junk buffed, it is time for real buffs.
      1. First off get Shield of Words (AoF will do as well) from your cleric.
      2. Now go get Stamina and Talisman from a shaman.
      3. Now the cleric should have enough mana to cast
      4. Resolution/Heroism and symbol of naltron/marzin on everyone.
      5. Once this is done cast magic resist followed by fire resist on everyone. This should bring clerics to low mana.
      6. Now the tanks should get haste from the enchanters.
    • Everyone with mana should be medding and getting ready.
    • Furbur Funguseater says that casters should bind by the stairs leading up to the doorway to Nagafen. You should also have a character there with a bunch of copper to give to the naked casters so their corpses don't poof.


  6. Ready Aim Attack: Manorien, Assassin and Vaziel all agree that the “waves” system of fighting Nagafen works the best. Below are some of their various strategies:
  7. Manorien’s Tactics:
  8. In your first wave you want to send in some of the debuff team, Enchanters and shaman. These can take the place of a tank and secondary healer respectively in the groups he listed. Also you might want to look at resist potions that Shaman can make if you are short on gear. Due to lag and not being able to see a dragons health till you are up on it, i suggest you send two waves of 3 if you have it, or the initial three groups then the rest. Have the first wave go, then follow with the second wave 15 seconds or so after. Don't forget to count the time you have from running from the claw room to the dragons room. Fight him in the back of his lair if possible, count out your buffs before hand, don't go over 15. Rune your tanks, and casters if you have mana. Don't forget damage shields, very important.
  9. Mages should use firepets (duh) and Enc should go in with illusion drybone (adds fire resist). Resists are more important than anything else. His AOEs are what kill you, not the relatively minor melee damage he does. Also, if you don’t have someone meleeing him, he regens at an insane rate, so when the tanks die, chances are the casters are soon to follow. My experience is that if they die, they don’t have time to run up and nuke at a rate where you are gaining damage. Good luck on your raids. Assassin's model holds true for most large type mobs, but there are nuances to each. Time and pressure overcome all things.
  • Assassin’s Tactics:
    • Charges on Nagafen should be made in waves, otherwise many people will go LD most likely. First charge should be of the 3 best tank groups. Second charge should be of the remaining tank groups. Final Charge should be made by all nukers when Nagafen is at approximately half health.
    • If my memory serves me correctly, all nukers have to be within melee range in order to hit Nagafen with a nuke.
    • All casters should bind themselves within Sol B at the buff area. Any wizard/nuker that dies during the battle can mem an ice nuke and run back in for a few more chances at taking the lizard out. This will also save some time on CR if all goes bad.
  • Vaziel’s Tactics:
    • Run in waves (because of the way VI handled a lot of PCs in one area you will LD the majority with an all out attack.) before the junk buffs wear off. 3 waves of 2 groups per wave usually works. You can either call waves by shout or just time the duration of each wave. Repeat till Nagafen dies, important note, if all tanks are dead, the run is over, do CR.

  • Corpse Retrieval: If you have killed Lord Nagafen, then you can skip this part, but if you weren’t so fortunate, here’s what Assassin suggests:
    • Coffins are unnecessary
    • Have 1 person agro Nagafen by walking a few steps into his lair. As soon as Nagafen agroes on this person, have then run out of the lair and sit on the steps facing his door.
    • Now that Nagafen is agro on this 1 person, everyone that has camped out and come back can now safely go into the lair and drag their corpses out without the beast eating them alive.
    • *This has been crossed out as recent information states that this might be considered an exploit. I would suggest using Coffins to retrieve corpses.


  • Reap the Rewards: If after all of this you were fortunate enough to slay the mighty Lord Nagafen, below is a list of items which might be on his corpse (list courtesy of Vaziel):
    • Bladestopper
    • Blight, Hammer of the Scourge
    • Cloak of Flames
    • Gauntlets of Fiery Might
    • Gold Plated Koshigatana
    • Heirophant's Crook
    • Orb of Tishan
    • Prayers of Life
    • Red Dragon Scale
    • Red Dragon Tooth (no longer drops I believe)
    • Selo's Drums of the March
    • Treasure Hunter's Satchel

    Many thanks to Assassin, Badaf, Furbur Funguseater, Manorien & Vaziel Oloth'Colbauth – all of the Cazic-Thule Server.
