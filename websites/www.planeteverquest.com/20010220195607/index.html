Geeks, Games & Sporting Events
2/17/01, 13:16 PST | Aracnakat

Fargo brings to light an interesting topic over at 3DActionPlanet. I for one am very interested in this. While EverQuest isn't the main focus of this topic, gamers in general are .. check it out.
The thread of the week on the 3DActionPlanet Forums has got to be This Discussion About Getting Computer Games Accepted In the Mainstream. It started with this post from Perros:

Despite efforts by many groups, computer gaming is a long way from being accepted as a sport. Infact it is still not wide spread enough for gamers to escape the stereotype of geeks who lock themselves up and have zero social skills and very low hygene.

I am part of a wide circle of friends, only a couple of which actually use there computer for anything apart from Word Processing, I have a goodlooking girlfriend which I didnt meet at a LAN party, I shower every morning, go out drinking every weekend. And I dont wear glasses. But I still get counted by many people as a exception.

What can we do to shed this stereotype?! How many of you actually fit the image of a geek? -Perros

The discussion has wandered all the way from geek profiling to Chess as a pro sport and back again. Talk! I think part of the reason that gamers have a difficulty getting their hobby accepted into the mainstream is the fact that we adopt and latch on to unintelligible phrases and then scream them at the top of our lungs at LAN parties. ALL YOUR BASE ARE BELONG TO US!! Of course I wouldn't have it any other way.

Plane of Mischief Still Puzzling
2/17/01, 7:00 PST | Kwill

The Plane of Mischief's secrets have still not been revealed. Despite the difficulty getting past the level 60 dragon to get there, players have managed to enter the plane and collect the playing cards that drop there. Other than the cards, no loot has dropped from this plane.

The puzzle of those cards has still not been solved. Both Abashi and Esther the Tester say the cards are key, but will not reveal any other details. Verant has indeed stumped players that have so far managed to solve most other game quests in record time.

The card game probably has something to do with the game "Kings Court," mentioned as a favorite game of the god Bristlebane.

The most outlandish theories are being considered, since the results have been disappointing so far. Some folks are just convinced it isn't working as intended...who knows. Good luck to all who are trying to solve this puzzle!

LA Times Interviews Brad McQuaid
2/15/01, 21:34 PST | Aracnakat

The LA Times has an interview with Brad McQuaid of Verant. The interview talks about the appeal of EverQuest, design challenges and MMOGs in general -- among other things.. Here's a clip:
Q: What makes "EverQuest" so compelling?

A: "EverQuest" has three primary areas of focus: character development, community building and an immersive world. Players build up their characters over time, acquiring experience, knowledge and possessions. And because of the social and interdependent nature of the game, players also develop relationships with other people. Both of these aspects create an ownership that compels players to keep playing over the long term, all the while exploring and experiencing a very immersive 3-D world.

More Changes on Test
2/15/01, 8:04 PST | Kwill

Casters Realm is reporting new enhancements to the test server:
Tracking:

Two new commands for tracking have been found: /trackfilter Options: Red/Yellow/White/Blue/Green. e.g: /trackfilter red removes red cons from the tracking list. Doing it again puts them back on.

/tracksort Options: Normal/Distance/Con

The tracking window has also been made so that it can be moved.

Spell Casting:

Alt Key casting has received an upgrade on the test server. When you press the alt key to quick cast spells, the name of the spell now hovers next to the spell gem for easier identification.
