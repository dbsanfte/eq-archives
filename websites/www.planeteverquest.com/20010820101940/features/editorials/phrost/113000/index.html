EQ & Prep-H
By Phrost (11/30/00)

You know, the other day I was at the grocery store to buy some Q-Tips and soda, meandering up and down the aisles casually without any serious thoughts going through the ol' noggin for a change.

As I passed by the medicine section, the label for a box of Preperation-H screamed out at me for some reason. This was weird because I've never had a hemmorrhoid. Heck, I'm not even sure what one is. I'd always thought it was some condition on your ass like acne or something, only painful.

Now normally when I realize my own ignorance in a subject, I go a bit out of my way to research it for future use, just in case it comes up in a conversation somewhere. But the subject being what it was, for some reason I didn't see myself firing up the browser and doing a search for information on a condition that irritates one's rectum.

This is when EQ wandered into my head like a drunk puppy, a clumsy, stumbling thought that defied the normally logical progression of things that go through my mind when I'm in a grocery store.

Or did it? For a few seconds I stood there, in front of the Preperation-H with a disturbed look on my face as I tried to pin down exactly why my brain would leap from rectal discomfort to EverQuest. I hadn't played EQ for at least a few days, much less thought about it until now, and to be reminded of it by an anal ointment seemed strange...



...until I made the connection between the two. You see, like hemorrhoids would probably do, EQ makes my ass itch. It's not really a physical itching sensation I feel, but more of a psychosomatic, quasi-spiritual itching of the ass on my inner child.

Then I started to wonder if there were a product for such an itch, a sort of "Preperation-H for the Soul" kind of thing. It turned out, as it always seems to do, that the answer was right in front of my face. It wasn't the drugs on the medicine aisle, nor the smell of the old man behind me waiting akwardly for me to leave so he could grab a tube of the stuff for himself unnoticed.

No friends, it was the air that I was breathing. It didn't smell like old pizza, empty cans of soda, and stale bong water. There was no grating sound of a hard drive's platters spinning in the background, no faint hum of a monitor slowly microwaving my brain.

Real life, it turns out, is the best salve for the "mild discomfort and irritation" caused by this game.

At least...

...that's what Dr. Phrack perscribes.

Next week, we'll discuss that "not so fresh feeling".
