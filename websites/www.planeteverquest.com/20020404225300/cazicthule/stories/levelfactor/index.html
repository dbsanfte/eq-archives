The Level Factor
by Rane Westwind of Divine Alliance

We all love Everquest, we all spend too much time playing it and we all can't wait for that level when we get the new set of spells, or the new combat technique, or last name, etc. This is good since it keeps us motivated and maintains a level of competition between players. But with this are also some inherently negative qualities.

EverQuest markets itself as promoting inter-personal relations with the "Grouping" feature. And it works! Groups are fantastic..sharing the exp and greatly increasing the chances of survival by blending the combined talents of differing races and classes. However, the "level" factor has the ability to tear this down completely and sever people from each other.

Perhaps you were in a guild from a previous game (or games) and continued it here with your good friends you know and trust Perhaps you made some good friends and formed a guild for the first time. Either way, it is people you enjoy gaming with, and hunting with in the dungeons. Now, we all lead different lives outside of Norrath--some of us may be on summer break from school, some may already be out of school and working for a living. The focus here is the fact that different people can devote different amounts of time to gaming. This becomes an issue with levels in the game. The people that cannot play as often soon find themselves falling behind in levels and before they know it, are unable to hunt with their friends.

The game is structured so in order to further your level, you must stick with people of your approximate level [thankfully the delta increases as levels do]. If you fall out of range, you’re left in the dust. You can’t ask people to wait for you because leveling takes a long time, and people want to further their own level to reach those new spells or skill—or to be able to camp that new MOB that carries the armor they’ve wanted because now they are able to fight it—and thus, leveling themselves even more in the process. This can create a certain stress level: feeling that you need to "keep up" with everyone else so you do not fall too far behind them and lose them for good.

So while EQ aims to bring people together, it in essence, has the power to separate them. I came to EQ from Ultima Online like many others, where there were no levels. If you started falling behind in skill, it was not a problem, you could still hunt with your friends. Now however, that’s just not possible. Communication is not restricted, as /guildsay maintains an easy forum to stay in touch with everyone no matter where they are in Norrath. And of course, as a guild grows, you pick up more people of varying levels and make new friends, so overall it’s not that big of a deal. I’m not arguing against levels at all, since they also carry many good points. These are just the musings of a Ranger who just can’t help thinking how the "level" structure of EQ was able to separate him from some of the best online friends he had.

What do you think?
