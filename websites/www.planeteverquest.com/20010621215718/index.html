Minneapolis Fan Faire
6/18/01, 21:45 PST | Aracnakat

Diggler McFeely of the Cazic-Thule server attended the Fan Faire this weekend in Minneapolis and was nice enough to share the event with everyone in the PEQ Forums.
Well, it was an interesting day. Although, somewhat surreal at times (i.e.: During the "Live Quest," and seeing some people dressed "in character." - Only saw 3 of those though, out of about 1,000.)

There are many wonderful people that play this game, and only interacting with them in-game, doesn't even come near to showing you just how wonderful. Aside from a few people that I noticed with the off-to-side snide remarks, the general attitude was festive.

Some notable points I heard while popping in and out of the various rooms where panel discussions were happening:

GM's & Guides
- Iksar models can now be chosen when you create your guide character. This was actually announced at the San Diego Fan Faire, but it's still something that I (and a fair number of others, for that matter,) didn't know.

- The "real" reason Rogues cannot be Guides/GM's. Apparently, there have been incidents in the past, where some Rogue GM's and Guides took it upon themselves to "acquire" certain GM-type items from some of the various Cshome NPC vendors, and distribute them to their RL friends. They were caught (of course,) and it was decided that having a Rogue character as a GM/Guide was not a good idea.

Tradeskills
- Did you know? The Fleeting Quiver is indeed the best quiver in the game (yep, this came straight from the Dude of Tradeskills.) Someone asked the Dude if it would be possible to make it have 8 slots, instead of the present 6. The Dude thought about it for approximately 3 seconds, at which time the answer, "no" was given.

- Tradeskills in general are having a lot improvements being made to them in the coming months. No details were given, aside from Tinkering, that is (yes, The Dude mentioned that Tinkering is going to have some major stuff done to it. He made reference to Mechanized Lockpicks being much more expensive than regular Lockpicks, but offering nothing more as far as usage or functionality. He used that as an example of one thing that needs to be improved.)

- It was mentioned by a player that Power Gloves (a tinkered item) are no better than Crafted, and that it seemed like a waste for that to be the case. The dude agreed, and made a note of it in his notebook for possible future enhancement.

- Someone asked what the chances were of being able to stack item in piles of more than 20 (say, 40 or 100+.) The Dude sincerely thought about for approximately 5-10 seconds, before he settled on (paraphrasing here,) "I doubt it can be done at this time."

- Another question was asked about being able to equip quivers in the ammo slot. This managed to garner the most thought from The Dude, before he answered (paraphrasing again,) "I doubt it can be done without introducing dupe issues into the game, or corrupting the character... which means you wouldn't even be able to log in, and that would be bad."

Magic & Spells
- To sum up this area:

ShadowKnights are fine the way they are. Necros could use some enhancement to make them more enjoyable, from the necro's view, in group situations. However, any forthcoming changes to necros, in that area, will not happen until after Luclin has been released.

EQ Community
- I didn't spend much time in this room. But, in the time I was there, the following was mentioned:

- Classes are pretty balanced the way Verant would like them to be, for the most part. (This kind of contradicts what another person has posted in their own summary.*)

- They are looking at possibly making a change to Complete Heal to make it more effective for Paladins to use. No further detail was given.

Armor & Weapons
- I only spent about 5 minutes here, as time was running out. However:

- Someone asked if there was the possibility of having, for example, spike armor with a damage shield effect. The A&W Guy liked the idea, and said that pre-Luclin, it's not something that would be possible to do, but with the new enhancements in Luclin, it should be feasible. (NOTE: There was some confusion during the asking of that particular question. As a result, I was given the impression that the A&W Guy only heard "Spiked Armor" and went on that, without understanding that the asker was also wondering about a DS effect as well.)

The Luclin Demo
- It was rather sweet-looking. The demo was run with an nVidia GeForce3 card (I gathered that much from the PNY Advertisement sitting on top of the monitor.) I asked the guy handling the mouse and keyboard what the specs were (of the system that was currently running the demo, since it ran so smoothly,) thinking that my P3 866 might be up to snuff if I slap a GeForce3 in it. But, he seemed confused by the question, as his answer was, "Well, the card on the top of the monitor gives you a pretty good idea." Heh, a smartass reply from a Verant employee... who would've thought, eh? I guess he didn't understand the difference between "PC Specs" and "Video Card." /sigh.

- I noticed the hands on the Luclin models. The fists actually look like fists, rather than square looking boxes.

- Also, armor and items, when placed in inventory slots, will be rendered over the surface of the model itself, rather than being meshed into the model. View it as this: Rather than having your shirt painted on your body, it's worn over top of it. On that note, one of the male human models was wearing some type of armor, with the coolest-looking sword I've ever seen. Details of the sword included wear marks on the blade (static, I'm sure,) as well as a detailed hilt (with green jewels, if I remember correctly.)

- The Demo Guy mentioned that with Luclin, level 60 characters will have the opportunity to advance their characters further, but not by level. He mentioned the ability of getting new titles for your level 60 character (once your xp bar is filled) as one of the ways they can "advance."

I'd explain more about the demo, but it just wouldn't do it justice. (The ol' "picture says a thousand words" adage.)

That's about it as far as the Panel Q&A Sessions and Luclin Demo are concerned.

Keith Parkinson was there, selling his wares. As well as the creator of /gu Comics. Both very nice people, and really easy to talk to. I'm not sure if any of you are aware, but Keith Parkinson also did the cover of several Terry Goodkind and David Eddings books, and has already finished the cover of the next Goodkind book, (which is apparently due out by Xmas this year.)

Also, Verant is looking at holding the next Fan Faire somewhere in Europe later this year.
Have anything to add? Please post your comments under the topic which Diggler started.

Wizards New Update
6/15/01, 4:16 PST | Aline of Povar

Lawrence Poe finally announced the Group Enhancement for Wizards and here it is:

Hey guys, as I get ready to head out for the Fan Faire I wanted to sit down and write up this post I've been putting off all week. A couple quick notes.

The "problem" with rain spell pulses always being resisted if they will be the killing blow isn't actually a problem....It's an intentional design feature. The problem was much like the old DOT corpse poof buf. If the Rain killed the NPC and the server was unsure where the damage came from the corpse would poof, so these spells were coded in this manner to prevent that from happening. I'm working with the coders to come up with a better solution. We're all pretty busy right now so unfortunately I can't give you a time frame on when a better solution will be implemented.

Kunark Evac spells: these will now work anywhere in the world. You won't have to be in a Kunark dungeon to use them. This change will apply to both Wizards and Druids evac spells.

Druid Spell Egress: this spell will now work indoors. It didn't make sense for the group version to work indoors (a mistake on our part) and not have the self version work indoors.

Shroud of Death: I added Illusion Skeleton to shroud of death on test server..... I removed it the next day I just didn't get to update spells on test. I forgot that in skeleton form trolls and ogres would lose slam, sorry for the scare. I'll see if I can think up some other way to give SKs skeleton form.

Stacking issues: I continue to address these as time permits. We're working on some changes that will allow me to fix them much easier. More stacking issues should be resolved in the coming months. High level players should be pleased to know that I'm 99.99999% certain that Call of Earth and Speed of the Shissar will no longer conflict after the next patch.

New Translocates: as many of you know there are new translocate spells up on test. I'm not going to comment on these other than to say you can expect that cool cat Al'Kabor to be out and about researching again.

Now on to the "Burning issue". I know the Wizard community has been *patiently* awaiting the enhancement we have been working on The coding for the new spell effect has been finished and it is going through extensive internal testing. It has also been on Test Server for a week, but I doubt you would have seen it in a parser – cause I'm sneaky like that. Before I go into details on the new spells there's a few things I'd like to say. Honestly I feel that wizards are balanced fine from a power standpoint, to that end you will not be receiving an upgrade to your damage output. Where I do feel wizards need help is group utility.

As I'm sure you can understand coming up with a highly desirable utility type spell for an EverQuest wizard is not an easy task. I've spent countless hours pondering a solution, I've read countless message board forums and emails looking at suggestions from you guys. Finally a solution was found! I'm not naive enough to believe that everyone will be happy about the new enhancement but this is an idea I've seen tossed around on a number of forums and I think not only does it fit in well with wizards being the masters of arcane transportation, I think it will make them very desirable in the 50 to 60 levels where they seem to be struggling for usefulness.

The new spells will give wizards the ability to prevent NPCs from gating. There will be 3 versions of the spells starting at level 51. They will each have a different resistance type. One of the problems with duration based effects is stacking and negating the need for multiple characters of the same class. I am designing these spells with that in mind. If you want to 100% ensure an NPC does not gate you will need more than one wizard. I understand this may upset some people but the need for multiple wizards to "Anchor" an NPC won't really happen until you fight the more epic mobs with higher hit point numbers. For single group encounters one wizard with these spells should, in most situations, be enough to get the job done. My plan is to get these spells implemented and ready to go before the next patch.

Lastly I would like to send a huge thank you to all the players who have reported stacking exploits to me through Email, IRC, and ICQ over the past couple of months. The feedback is greatly appreciated.

Take care all!
/Lakers secret handshake

- Lawrence Poe

To see the original message, head over the the Developers Corner.
