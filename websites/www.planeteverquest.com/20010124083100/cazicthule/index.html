    Game Updates Suspended
    1/23/01, 8:56 PST | Kwill

    Absor reports on the official Sony boards that they will not be making any changes to the game for some time:
    For at least a few weeks we will be doing some internal work that will prevent us from making any updates to the game unless they are critical (and there are no such things that we know of at this time).

    We will still be taking in reports about bugs and other suggestions for change (such as the Class Talk stuff), but we won’t be making any changes to the game. And the two-week timeframe is only a guess at the moment, it could go longer.

    While this means there will be no changes to the game during this time, we will still be maintaining the servers, the physical hardware, and all other aspects of the game. We are just not changing any aspects of the game software itself.
    Brad (Aradune) added this clarification:
    I just wanted to add a bit more detail to what Absor's posted.

    Basically, we're going through and spending a focussed 2 weeks to evaluate, document, and plan, getting us ready for the remainder of this year. I can't be too specific yet, because it's too early to make major announcements, but this would include finishing up design for future free zones, planning out engine mods to keep EQ current, as well as anything major EQ related to be released in the next year or so.

    So, bottom line, we're planning and documenting as opposed to implementing during this period.

    What that really means for you guys, essentially, is you won't see a patch for 2-3 weeks unless an emergency arises. That's all.

    Auctions and eBay
    1/22/01, 11:02 PST | Korelin

    An update on Delrachnid's story back on Friday about the demise of Everquest items on eBay. Ebay has less then 50 items total, and doing a search for the unique server names, nothing came up. In comparison, a week ago or so I searched for "cazic" and found over 3 pages worth of items. Now, it appears a lot of these people are going over to Yahoo! Auctions as well as a few other mixed auction sites. However, it's only a matter of time before Verant gets them to remove their items. Imagine that, Warriors knowing what taunt is for, and clerics not asking what "Complete Heal" does. So it looks like the end is near for EQ items being sold for real-life money, right?

    Maybe, maybe not...

    While browsing some sites for more info, I stumbled upon this from EQVault, involving a law suit against Verant for their actions regarding eBay. The site, Gravityspot.com, a web development firm in Seattle, has sent this letter out to Everquest players:
    Notice of intent to file a class action suit against Sony Computer Entertainment, Verant Interactive, eBay and others.

    If you believe that any one of the above-named parties have infringed on your right to freely sell your time, please add your name to the growing list of those who wish to stop these unconstitutional actions.

    The suit will seek unspecified damages and will seek to enjoin Sony Computer Entertainment, eBay and Verant Interactive from interfering with these activities in the future.

    If you believe that your rights were violated and would like to be a party to this action then click here
    It looks like this isn't a dead issue after all...

    Missing Zones - Brad's Take
    1/22/01, 10:56 PST | Korelin

    On responding to an issue brought up earlier, Brad responded on the "missing zones" in the game. Here's what he had to say:
    Once upon a time (early 1996, to be more precise), Steve Clover and I drew the world of Norrath on a white board in the old Sony Interactive Studios building. We added rough names, including most of the starting cities I think (Steve is really good with fantasy names, and he also came up with the name 'EverQuest', btw).

    Then, a few months later, Bill Trost came along, and he and Kevin Burns began to fill in a lot more detail... mountain ranges, lakes, oceans, and other regions, as well as dungeons and such.

    They did this for all five continents, and after working on the map for a while, they started to flesh out the points of interest, writing up documents describing them.

    Some areas we meant to include in the game, and some areas were meant as context and background, similar to maps included in many fantasy novels, depicting areas the protagonist may or may not one day travel to, but implying a vast world and a deep history.

    And, as actual development of the game progressed and we learned what could and could not be done, and how long it took to make a zone and all of the content associated with a zone, we narrowed things down a bit and came up with a list of primary areas of Norrath we wanted to cover.

    And then, a little later, we decided, after experimenting with zone density, that it would probably be better to include less continents with more zones, as opposed to more continents with less zones. So we axed Kunark and Velious, hoping one day we'd get to do those too, but realizing it would have to be after launch.

    The good news, of course, is that slowly but surely, due to the continued success of the game, we've been able to fill in other Norrathean points of interest; for example, the Hole, Paineel, and the Temple of Solusek Ro, and over the next year, the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains.

    Ebay Going...Going...Gone
    1/18/01, 11:46 PST | Kwill

    It appears that Sony OnLine Entertainment has asked Ebay to disallow the sale of items and characters from EverQuest on their auction site. No official statement has been given to the press, but Everlore had this quote from PR Manager of Sony Online Entertainment, Christy Fritts:
    ...due to continuing and severe player complaints we have asked Ebay to refrain from supporting auctions of EverQuest merchandise and characters for the following reasons:

    Potential for our players to be defrauded, it poses an unfair advantage to a select few and finally, it breaks the End User License Agreement.
    I checked Ebay as of 2:00pm Central time on January 18, and there were still over 1500 items under the search category "Everquest". It would still allow me to list an item under the category games:everquest (at least I got to the screen that would allow me to do that), and there was no mention of items from EverQuest as being prohibited items on Ebay's official list of things you can't sell there. I cannot say with any certainty how Ebay or Sony OnLine Entertainment will enforce this policy of stopping auctions on Ebay. However, it is clear that Sony has asked Ebay to stop allowing these auctions, and players should be aware that there may be consequences for continuing to sell (or buy) items for money on auction sites.
