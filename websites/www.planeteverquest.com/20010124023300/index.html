Game Updates Suspended
1/23/01, 8:56 PST | Kwill

Absor reports on the official Sony boards that they will not be making any changes to the game for some time:
For at least a few weeks we will be doing some internal work that will prevent us from making any updates to the game unless they are critical (and there are no such things that we know of at this time).

We will still be taking in reports about bugs and other suggestions for change (such as the Class Talk stuff), but we won’t be making any changes to the game. And the two-week timeframe is only a guess at the moment, it could go longer.

While this means there will be no changes to the game during this time, we will still be maintaining the servers, the physical hardware, and all other aspects of the game. We are just not changing any aspects of the game software itself.
Brad (Aradune) added this clarification:
I just wanted to add a bit more detail to what Absor's posted.

Basically, we're going through and spending a focussed 2 weeks to evaluate, document, and plan, getting us ready for the remainder of this year. I can't be too specific yet, because it's too early to make major announcements, but this would include finishing up design for future free zones, planning out engine mods to keep EQ current, as well as anything major EQ related to be released in the next year or so.

So, bottom line, we're planning and documenting as opposed to implementing during this period.

What that really means for you guys, essentially, is you won't see a patch for 2-3 weeks unless an emergency arises. That's all.

Missing Zones - Brad's Take
1/22/01, 10:56 PST | Korelin

On responding to an issue brought up earlier, Brad responded on the "missing zones" in the game. Here's what he had to say:
Once upon a time (early 1996, to be more precise), Steve Clover and I drew the world of Norrath on a white board in the old Sony Interactive Studios building. We added rough names, including most of the starting cities I think (Steve is really good with fantasy names, and he also came up with the name 'EverQuest', btw).

Then, a few months later, Bill Trost came along, and he and Kevin Burns began to fill in a lot more detail... mountain ranges, lakes, oceans, and other regions, as well as dungeons and such.

They did this for all five continents, and after working on the map for a while, they started to flesh out the points of interest, writing up documents describing them.

Some areas we meant to include in the game, and some areas were meant as context and background, similar to maps included in many fantasy novels, depicting areas the protagonist may or may not one day travel to, but implying a vast world and a deep history.

And, as actual development of the game progressed and we learned what could and could not be done, and how long it took to make a zone and all of the content associated with a zone, we narrowed things down a bit and came up with a list of primary areas of Norrath we wanted to cover.

And then, a little later, we decided, after experimenting with zone density, that it would probably be better to include less continents with more zones, as opposed to more continents with less zones. So we axed Kunark and Velious, hoping one day we'd get to do those too, but realizing it would have to be after launch.

The good news, of course, is that slowly but surely, due to the continued success of the game, we've been able to fill in other Norrathean points of interest; for example, the Hole, Paineel, and the Temple of Solusek Ro, and over the next year, the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains.


Quad Kiting
1/22/01, 10:47 PST | Korelin

Absor had this to say concerning "quad kiting":
First, I feel the need to make comments about this whole quad kiting thing.

All I was saying was that I, as a player and a person, don’t particularly like it as a tactic. As a Verant employee, my intention is to bring that up in a tuning meeting (as my opinion, not as something that the players want changed). So, please, can we stop all the threads about how bad my idea is? I’ve got the message already, I know that many of you don’t want anything done to reduce quad kiting. I’ve received several e-mails explaining how it is a valid and balanced (from a risk/reward perspective) tactic. I will bring that with me to the meeting when I mention quad kiting, have no fear. And that meeting isn’t going to be next week, so don’t panic…

I also wanted to talk about a trend that I have been seeing lately. It has probably always been there and I’ve just been slow to see it. But I have been reading more and more posts wherein people are attacking other people. And it bothers me.

I have absolutely no problem with people engaging in spirited and even aggressive arguments over ideas or gameplay or any of that. I encourage it.

But I do not want to see one customer attacking another. I have the responsibility to try to keep this place a forum for talking about EverQuest. For Verant this is one of our main sources for discovering new ideas, areas for improvement, and the general sense of things from our customers. When discussions break down into personal attacks, all that functionality is lost, and bad feelings are generated. Those bad feelings ultimately harm EverQuest and its community.

I would really like to see a dramatic reduction in personal attacks. I hate having to be the bad guy here, but I will if it becomes necessary.

Calling other customers ‘idiots’ is not acceptable here (or any other form of personal attack). And, if I start seeing an excess of personal attacks on Monday or over the weekend, we will be issuing warnings.

I don’t want anyone to feel that we are trying to control topics, or control what you say. But we are willing to control the way you say it. Basically, be civil to each other.

Or else…

As a final note, I want to get through all of the classes with a ‘talk’ thread for each before February 9th (or thereabouts). This will be… well, a mess. I will be trying to start a new one every other day, starting on Monday (looking at a calendar, I may have to do more than three a week…).

This will take up a lot of my posting time, I suspect. I think it’s a good thing for the game, though, so I’m going to do it.

Don’t be expecting instant results on these issues, though. There are always tons of things going on here. And we really want to look at all of these issues as a whole, so I want to get them all done before we dig deeply into them. You might see some of the easy issues resolved right away, and others might not be addressed for a while (and by ‘addressed’ I mean either changed or checked off as ‘ok as is’).

I’ll do what I can to keep you updated on all that.

GameSpot Interviews Gordon Wrinn
1/19/01, 14:07 PST | Delrachnid

GameSpot conducted an interview to delve into the mind of Gordon Wrinn. After sorting through the thoughts of insta-killing players named "Mastah Indahouse" and "Swysse Cheeze" they were able to gather information on what he thought about the recent changes made in the latest patch, the huge impact it will have, and further modifications to the game forthcoming. The full interview can be found on the GameSpot site here, but here's an small sample to get you going:

GameSpot: EverQuest has been criticized for being extremely inaccessible to players who don't make a serious effort to familiarize themselves with the game. Regardless of whether you agree, do you see this update as a step to help alleviate this criticism?

Gordon Wrinn: I don't think that "inaccessible" is the right term here. EverQuest is a game, and all games require that players familiarize themselves with the game in order to excel or advance. That "effort" is, in fact, gameplay itself.
