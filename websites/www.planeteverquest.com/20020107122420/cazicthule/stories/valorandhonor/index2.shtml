Of Valor and Honor, Part II
by Mythrilmire

The winds were blowing in from the East, carrying upon them a somewhat salty, yet fresh smell, which was not familiar to Mythrilmire. The only fishing he had done in the past was in fresh water, within the security of Felwithe Keep. The largest fish he could ever recall catching was a mere seven pounds. In the course of the past few hours waiting for the boat to Freeport, Mythrilmire had pulled in a couple which easily weighed eleven pounds. He could get used to this way of life quite easily.

After what seemed an eternity, Mythrilmire was distracted from his thoughts by the ringing of bells and the shouts, "Ship Ahoy" from the end of the docks. Looking up to see what the commotion was about, he saw Sestin looking down upon him and motioning to him that it was time to depart. He reeled in his line and stored away his pole, jumped to his feet and took a deep breath as he began to head down the dock towards the ship.

Approaching the ship, he could not help but notice the massive masts waving in the wind. This vessel was larger than he had imagined, with a length of at least eighty-foot and a width of approximately thirty-foot. As he boarded the ship, the thought crossed his mind as to whether or not he was making a wise decision; after all, save this one knight he was completely alone.

Once the ship set sail and he began to lose sight of land, any thoughts of turning back were unfounded, unless he wanted to swim. He felt a tap on the shoulder and turned to see Sestin with a look of concern in his eyes. "Mythrilmire, how do you fare?" Sestin asked. "Just thinking of my home and parents", he replied. "Sestin, just call me Myth, Most people find it less exhausting on the tongue". "Ok, Myth", Sestin said with an approving tone, "Myth it is"

When they entered the Ocean of Tears, Sestin suggested to Myth that now would be a good time for him to take cover in the hold and work on his meditation and Sense heading skills. "Besides this journey being lengthy and uninspiring, many an adventurer has traversed miles of beast infested lands, just to fall overboard and become food for the sharks." Sestin said with a gleam in his eye.

Mythrilmire spent most of his time meditating during the trip, and would not know that they were upon Freeport were it not for the echo from the deck "Land Ho!" Myth gathered up his equipment and headed for the deck. As he made his way to the deck, he instantly noted how much brighter things seemed to be here than they were in Faydark.

The Freeport docks were bustling with scores of fishermen and people that seemed to be doing nothing more than enjoying late afternoon ale or awaiting the ship to take them to unknown destinations. They made their way through the Harbor Masters shop, stopping to replenish their provisions of food & drink and headed towards Freeport.

As they entered Freeport, Sestin turned to Myth and spoke almost apologetically, "Aye my friend, This be as far as I can take thee. I must travel a great distance in hostile lands to meet with my brethren and cannot guarantee mine own safety, lest yours."

Myth replied, "You need not apologize Sir Knight, for I shall remain in Freeport until I can locate a Master Wizard by the name of Allangazar. You have done me a great justice by bringing me this far, which I undoubtedly would not have made it here without your help." Sestin looked at Myth with a bit a pleasantry in his eyes, something Myth had not seen before and said, "Myth, you are truly an honorable soul, which I am certain will one day be a powerful wizard. When that day comes, I may seek your assistance." Myth smiled and said, "Would be mine honor, Sestin. If ever that day should arrive, just send word and I shall be at thy service." With that Sestin smiled, turned away and started heading into the maze of city walls.

In no time at all, Myth was standing alone in an unfamiliar city, which made him nervous, yet somewhat excited of what was ahead of him. Not knowing where to find this wizard by the name of Allangazar, that his father had spoke so highly of, he decided he would find an inn and get some much needed rest. Mythrilmire caught the attention of a guard and inquired as to where he may find an inn. "Are ye’ new to Freeport, eh?" questioned the guard. Myth sounded back, "Yes, I have just traveled to your magnificent city from Faydark." Seemingly unimpressed by the wizard’s attempt at a compliment, the guard said harshly, "Well, just see that you do not cause any trouble in Freeport, Wizard. We do not look too highly upon newcomers, especially those that are magically inspired by the devil. Therefore, state your business here wizard!"

Perplexed by the sudden hostilities of this guard and noting that two more guards were coming to assist their comrade, Myth decided he should be just as hasty with his answer.

"My name is Mythrilmire, I come from the city of Felwithe, sent here by my father. I was instructed to seek out a master wizard by the name of Allangazar, who would look out for me." Before Myth could even say another word to assure the guard he meant no harm, the guard bowed before him. "Please forgive my brashness, Mythrilmire, For we have had much trouble with newcomers of late. I am Captain Burdock, I will have my two best soldiers escort you to Allangazar." Myth responded with gratitude, "Apology accepted, I do understand your animosity. One can not be too careful these days."

By now, there were two guards standing to either side of Myth and after receiving instructions from their Captain, summoned Mythrilmire to follow. "Finally, I shall meet my fathers friend", Myth thought to himself.
