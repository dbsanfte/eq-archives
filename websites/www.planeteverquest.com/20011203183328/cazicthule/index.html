    EQ Patcher Help
    12/2/01, 12:47 PST | Kwill

    For information on the new patcher, the EQ Team has set up a page to familiarize players with how it works. Here is an introduction from the page:
    There are two things that are vitally important about the new EverQuest Patcher. One, is its functionality. Not only do you have the ability to customize the patcher for your personal preferences, this patcher takes advantage of differential patching technology. Say for instance that we created an addendum to a certain file. Let's use EverQuest.exe as an example. In the past, we would have downloaded to you a brand new EverQuest.exe file. Now, with differential patching, we are only downloading the appended part of the EverQuest.exe file thus shortening the download process.

    And the second thing vitally important, this new patcher is much better looking than the previous one. As an added feature in the near future, the EverQuest Patcher will allow users to create their own skins or download some special skins that we create to customize the look of their patcher.

    This page has been created to help everyone get accustomed to the new patcher. Below, you will find some information on the new options and how they work with each other.


    CT Server Makes Round 2 in the ToT3
    12/2/01, 12:12 PST | Kwill

    The team from Cazic Thule made it to Round Two in the finals for the Test of Tactics 3 on the test server this weekend, which will move them closer to the final goal of total victory! Caleal described the event this way on the CTC Forums:
    After logging in and getting copied to the RZ server, dealing with multiple zone crashes as 700+ people are expected to move to the same 2 zones, getting our guild tagged, and speculating about how much of the team will actually show up, we had a fun day standing around in South Karana mostly afk.

    Matches started off about an hour and a half late due to the zone crash problems, etc, the scedule went down hill from there. Arena 1 crashed several times during early matches, so they kept having to restart, FUN. Things were further delayed by teams with all soulfires ending in 18-18 ties after 20 min, causing tiebreaker matches. One gay ass match between 2 teams that had 18 soulfires each required 3 tiebreaker matches before the events team decided to make it sudden death(if tied at the 20 min mark, first kill wins). Things moved along a bit faster after that, but there were still a couple of matches that lingered a while. So, we spent the day chatting and scouting out the competition. I tracked down old friends from Tanarus and early EQ beta. We putzed around with some duels, etc.

    Finally, fully 6 hours after our scheduled fight time, it was time for our first round match. We didn't have to fight in any pre-matches because we had to fight more than one team to get there in the first place. Thanks EoD/TR for fielding a 2nd team, saved us from having to participate in the lagfest pre matches. Apparently RZ server hardware wasn't as robust as they thought, so 1000ish extra players caused some problems. Basicly there were about 600 fewer people logged in when our match came up, so lag and arena zone crashing wasn't a problem by then.

    Our opponent was the team from the Prophecy guild on the Bertox server. They had a lot of good gear, but imo, they didn't have the greatest class balance. It was a fun fight with a lot of close calls for some people and a lot of nice saves by our healers and others, but we had a better strategy and picked our targets better, so the final score was 16-1. At least I believe that was the final.

    Tommorow we face the team from the Quellious server, they look fairly strong, but they don't have the best class balance for this type of fight, so we are very optimistic. =)

    If we win in round 2, our round 3 opponent is gonna be FUN! Possible oponents for us in round 3 are the team from the Afterlife guild on Mithaniel Marr and the Pandimonium guild team from Tallon Zek. The Mystical Order from Fennen Ro also has a very strong team in that bracket and could pull an upset over Afterlife. Considering that Blood of the Spider and Fires of Heaven were eliminated early in round 1 while it was still a lagfest, the winner of our round 3 fight will probably be the favorite to win the final. IMO, the fight between Afterlife and Pandimonium in that bracket is the one to watch for. I know our team is looking forward to having fun with one of the 2 if we make it to round 3. For now though, we are concentrating on our round 2 fight and will worry about the round 3 one when its time. =)

    Good luck to all of you, and congratulations for a job well done!

    Trouble Logging on Yesterday?
    11/30/01, 9:22 PST | Kwill

    The word from the the official EQ site:

    On November 29 some EverQuest players were experiencing problems logging into the game. These players were being kicked back to desktop after selecting "Play On Line."

    To fix this problem, go into your EverQuest directory and delete bmpwad6.s3d. By doing this, the problem should be solved.

    However, if this fix does not work and you recently edited your eqclient.ini file to help us test out the DirectX 8 DLL, then you can go back to your eqclient.ini in the EverQuest directory and change "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx8.DLL" to "GraphicsDLL=EQGfx_Dx7.DLL".

    Comments on the New Graphics
    11/27/01, 8:15 PST | Kwill

    Absor had a number of answers to questions about the new graphics on a thread on the Developer's Corner:

    Turning on and off graphics
    You will be able to turn the graphics on or off separately for each of your computers.

    You will also be able to change your choices. You won't be locked into any graphics restrictions you make now because of your current system. So if, for example, you turn off a few of the new race models because your system doesn't have enough RAM to run them all, you can turn them back on later. This will all be available through .ini file settings as well as through a setup option. But you'll only be able to make these changes out of game.

    Horses
    In order to see your character riding a horse, you will have to have both horses and your new character models turned on. If you decide to not display some of the new models you will not see any player characters of that race type on horseback. The old models just can't ride horses.

    T&L Hardware
    We're considering a T&L card a required feature for anyone who doesn't have a P3 or Athlon processor (or better). If you have a 16mb non-T&L card, you can get away with it as long as you have a P3 or Athlon.

    That said, we're listing T&L as a requirement for framerate concerns more than anything else. Luclin features some pretty beefy poly-counts and the more processing you can offload to the card the better. However there's nothing that will kick you out of the game and say "You must have a T&L card to play EverQuest".

    128 Megs of Ram?
    You'll have to turn off models until your system runs at an acceptable framerate (and load time). What acceptable is will really be up to you. But with only 128 megs of RAM, I can tell you that you most likely will not find your performance to be at all acceptable with all the models turned on. Though, as always, all systems are different and personal preferences vary. So you can turn the models on regardless of your RAM, but without the minimum requirements we really don't think you'll find the performance acceptable.

    3DFX Voodoo Video Cards
    If you get the latest drivers for your card, the card should be able to run Shadows of Luclin. However, with some of the older Voodoo cards you will probably find the performance less than satisfactory. Again, that's really up to the preferences of the user.

    Windows 95 Not Supported
    We won't officially be supporting Windows 95 anymore. Heck, Microsoft isn't even supporting it anymore…

    The main reason for this is because Windows 95 won't support our efforts to localize the game. Once we complete our localization work, people using Windows 95 won't have the code needed to translate the text. This will probably lead to all sorts of badness, not the least of which would be that Win 95 users might not even be able to see any chat at all.
