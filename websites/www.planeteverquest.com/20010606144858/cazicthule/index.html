    Patch Info
    6/5/01, 8:01 PST | Kwill

    This note about the patch was on the Developer's Corner:
    When we patch tomorrow (6-5-01), we will be introducing code on Rallos Zek server that will result in faction hits similar to those on the team servers.

    Killing a character in any of their races home town will cause you to take a faction hit. This counts for all home cities. So, if you kill a Human in any of the city zones of Qeynos (or Freeport, or Surefall Glade), you will suffer a faction hit, even if that Human was born in Freeport. All of Greater Faydark is the starting city for Wood Elves. There will also be faction hits for killing High Elves and Half-Elves in Greater Faydark. Killing an Erudite in either Paineel or Erudin will cause a faction hit.

    Also, for those of you trying to start or complete quests for King Tormax, you need to know that we've made a change. You will now need to be in good standing with him in order to do his quests. In game terms, this means you will need to be at least amiable.

    Playing CDs While Running EQ
    6/4/01, 12:30 PST | Kwill

    It is possible to play a CD with your computer while playing Everquest, although according to Absor, "the command isn't very user-friendly right now."

    The mcicontrol command is used in-game to get a CD to play. Here are instructions from the Sony technical help board:
    Until this command is further developed, you can do the following.
    1. Put a CD in your cdplayer before entering EQ.
    2. Once in game, type /mcicontrol open cdaudio (enter)
    3. Type /mcicontrol play cdaudio (enter)

    From the Developers Corner
    6/4/01, 12:17 PST | Kwill

    Absor recently addressed these issues on the Sony Developer's Corner message boards:

    Imbued Idols
    These are in the game, but currently not equipable. Absor says probably not in the next patch "for technical reasons that he can't get into." Hmm, could something interesting be happening?

    Shadow Knight Spell Issues

    Speed of Shissar Doesn't Stack: This spell, just like Call of Earth, is built into the spell data base in a particular way that causes it not to stack with other SK spells. Lawrence Poe, spell developer, writes "I'd like to be able to tell you 'I'll move some stuff around and throw it up on test to see if it works'; unfortunately I can't fix it right now. I've looked over the problems with speed of the Shissar a few dozen times. All I can really tell you is that a number of stacking complications we currently have should be rectified in the coming months."

    Feign Death:
    FD does not give a caster a message that it has worked, a player noted, which makes it hard to use in combat. Lawrence Poe replies, "Having played a high level Shadow Knight, I agree there are times when I will not notice my spell going off. However when I get into situations where I think I'm going to need to FD/DP I just pop into 3rd person mode so I can see my character."

    DeadMan Floating Does not Stack with Deadeye:
    Lawrence Poe will look into this matter.

    Discussion on Agnostic versus Choosing a Diety
    This discussion has gotten quite involved. Check out this thread for opinions from both players and Absor.

    Server Economies
    This thread has been around for a while...players are still submitting ideas about how to improve the economy.

    Rangers and Cinder Snare
    A player wanted to know if there would be any high level less resistable snare spells for their class. Lawrence Poe responds: "We gave Rangers Cinder Jolt to address their ability to survive encounters. A lot of high level encounters are designed to make snaring impossible/very hard. It is very doubtful we would put in a less resistible snare."

    Patch Addendum
    6/1/01, 14:42 PST | Aracnakat

    This was from a small patch last night:

    This evening we put in another patch to correct some issues that arose with the patch yesterday. The changes implemented include some that weren't announced previously:

    - Resist-debuffs will do 1.5 times their normal value for PvP encounters. In other words, if the spell did -60MR in PvP before, it does -90MR in PvP now. PvE (combat versus NPCs) remains unaffected.
    - Faction-loss for PvP kills on Vallon and Tallon now only occur against factions within the hometown of the person killed. In other words, if you kill an Erudite in Erudin, you will only lose faction with the factions in Erudin, not the factions in Paineel.
    - Turning in the Tomb of Order and Discord will have no effect on Vallon and Tallon.
    - On all PvP servers, only the person who gets the killing-blow in PvP combat will have looting rights. We would prefer to implement some sort of "majority damage" logic however this should solve most of the issues associated with uninvolved players "ninja-looting" the loser in PvP encounters.

    The New Runnyeye
    5/31/01, 11:47 PST | Kwill

    Absor answers player questions about how the old spawns are affected in the new Runnyeye zone after the patch in this thread on the EQ Developers Corner. There is some good information there, so be sure to check it out.

    I also wanted to address something that I've been seeing posted on a few sites. Some folks are upset that we announced the change to Runnyeye, blaming the influx of high level characters into the zone on the fact that we mentioned it in the patch message.

The only difference, I suspect, is that now the flood of curious high level characters will happen all at once and then, hopefully, trail off to something like normal.
