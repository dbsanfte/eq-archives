Diaries of a Traveling Bard
by Hailaria, Bard of 9 Songs

Message One: Beginnings, and new found friends:

For those that know me not, I am a wood elf bard and I call Kelethin home. I was finding myself in a rut in Kelethin, and decided to travel abroad, to write songs, and live a more exciting life. This is my journal of travels and tales, and the songs that they inspired.

In preparation for my trip, I took leave from my guildmaster to travel abroad. It was with happy heart that she wished me well, and bid me carry a message to Freeport. This was a wonderful thing for me, as I had not decided where my feet would carry me, and well it is easy to get somewhere if you know where you are headed. Carrying the package, and with lute in hand I headed out, singing a merry song. I choose to follow the path to the right, as I felt that it was the direction of the setting sun, and likely the direction a boat for hire for Freeport.

I had traveled a days journey when I beheld a wondrous sight, a town of High Towers, built up from the ground. I had heard tales of Felwithe, city of the High Elves, but had never before laid eyes upon it. I was taken aback. Much in need of refreshment, and a new string for my lute, as the playing and practice had taken its toll, I set out to find a tavern and mercantile. I asked the guards at the gate for assistance, but they were very stoic and simple pointed me onward. In my haste to head onward, I nearly tripped over the figure of a High Elf in glowing armour. "Hail, and humblest apologies", I said to the noble man before me. "Hail, and well met" he replied. "where is such a lovely elf as thee going in such haste" he replied. "My name is Hailaria, and I am hunting for provisions and lute strings noble sir" I replied blushing slightly at the compliment. He introduced himself as Moozh, and it was apparent to me that from his armor he could be nothing, if not a knight. He offered to show me around town, and we were soon on our way. He showed me all the best of Felwithe. He took me to the travelers lodge and secured a room for me there, for which I was much appreciative, as I hadn't the coins to cover the cost myself, and the thought of a warm bed was comforting. After purchasing my provisions and strings, I stowed my gear in my room, and headed out with Moozh for an afternoon swim in the beautiful river. We dined late, at the shoreside café, and took a leisurely time making our way back to the tavern. I was much entranced by such a noble escort, and thought he would surely wish to accompany me bedside for the evening. After many songs, and a few too many ale, I awoke to find I had been carried to my bed, and the noble gentleman was outside the room, having kept my honor in tact. I greeted Moozh with a cheery good morning. He smiled, and mentioned that I must be naïve to the world, and that not many a man would have kept my virtue as he had. I told him of my plans to travel, and he mentioned he was heading on to Freeport as well, and that it might be best to travel in pair. I whole heartily agreed, finding myself entirely curious about this strange High Elf fellow that had resisted my bed, and determined to make him mine.
