An Unhappy Ranger's Tale
9/25/01, 9:18 PST | Kwill

Ferkyl, Ranger of the Veeshan Guard guild on Cazic Thule server, lost his epic weapon last week. How could this occur? According to Lillianan, who posted on this topic on the Cazic Thule Forums his story went like this:
A few days ago Ferkl of Veeshan Guard was hunting in Kael. He died and was looting his body, then looted his lammie in the wrong hand, so when he looted his first epic sword it poofed. How this happened is why I'm posting. A ranger's first epic sword is offhand. So if the ranger loots in the wrong order the primary weapon goes to offhand and then epic goes to the ranger's bags...unless those bags are full.
If the bags are full, a no-drop item disappears, rather than dropping to the ground. His epic was gone.

Ferkyl petitioned, but was told this was a feature of the game, and his epic weapon could not be replaced. The server GM explained in an email to Ferkyl, copied to the CTC Forum thread, that

...Inventory management is the responsibility of the player. While I understand you do have the drop confirmation set as I explained in game this does not include when looting or trading. If you do not have inventory space of the appropriate size and are using the quick loot key "right clicking the mouse" then you will 'drop' whatever you are trying to loot....if it is no drop it will try to drop but because no drop items can not be dropped they will poof....
She goes on to inform him that she is sympathetic to his plight, but cannot replace the weapon. In a subsequent email, she clarifies further:
... No Drop items that are mistakenly dropped are coded to disappear and there is nothing I can do to change this fact. It is not a bug but part of the code of the game. ...
At the present time, Ferkyl will not be getting his epic replaced, although he continues to contact customer service about the issue. Verant has made it clear that they do not help with epics at all, either during the process or after it is obtained, which may be one complication to this issue.

When right clicking to loot a corpse, it is important to remember that if you don't have room in your inventory, or the wrong size container, no drop items will poof. That is a "feature" of the game. Whether this "feature" will be changed in the future is unclear. Until it is changed--if it ever is--players should be aware of this condition, and take care that they don't lose valuable items inadvertently when quickly looting a corpse. That goes for NPC corpses as well--so before looting that one of a kind no drop item, make sure you have room for it!

On a more general note, there are many incidents of items dropping/poofing when being combined, looted, having the wrong inventory slots filled up, having the wrong size bag, or in a trade situation. It is clear from the GM's response to Ferkyl and other situations of this kind that items will not be replaced for what the GMs would consider a player's error. Word to the wise: check before you right-click loot and make sure when you are trading or questing that you have slots open or the correct size containers to receive items.

Player sympathies have gone out to Ferkyl, who lost something he worked so hard for. It will be interesting to see in the coming months if this game feature is modified in any way. Verant did recode the trade skill kits so players now get a message about things that cannot be combined, rather than losing items stored in the kits when hitting combine by accident. Hopefully they will also take a look at the "dropping no-drop" problem.


Patch Message 9/25
9/25/01, 9:10 PST | Kwill

** Spell Changes **

- We have removed the changes to bard song stacking that were made with the recent emergency patch. We will be reviewing those changes for possible update in a future patch.

** Item Changes **

- The Shaman Epic effect has been returned to its previous effect (and it should conflict with fewer Damage over Time spells, as was the intention for the change made last patch).

** Quest Changes **

- Ivory Sky Diamond is now dropping as it should.

- Fixed an error in a Stonebrunt quest where an NPC was handing out the wrong reward.

** Miscellaneous **

- Added some code that might fix a bug with female hair and Erudite hoods that caused them to fail to appear sometimes when a helmet was removed. This would cause some characters to look as though the back of their head was missing at times. Editors note: hehe!

- Mouselook will now work a little differently when in the first two third-person camera modes (overhead and chase). They will work similarly to first-person mouselook (i.e. your character's heading will change when you move the mouse left or right, the left and right movement keys will cause your player to strafe, and the camera angle will pitch up or down when you move the mouse forward or back). These changes should make those first two third-person camera modes more useful.

Interesting SoL spell changes on Test
9/21/01, 9:22 PST | Infernal

Some very interesting things on Test, and I believe the changes to the Enchanter class will be very important for aggro management where Mobs are unmezzable, uncharmable, or otherwise extremely resistant -- in short, game-altering. They are named "soandso Visage". See my comments about them at the end of this post.

Luclin-Specific

  • Summon Horse Obviously, this summons a horse. May be a clickable effect on expendable/charged/unlimited item(s)
  • The Nexus A self-only teleport spell to "Nexus" zone. May be a clickable effect on expendable/charged/unlimited item(s)
Necromancer
  • Dead Men Floating (Nec 49) Added. It is an exact duplicate of Dead Man floating but GROUP-based, with a marginally longer casting time and costing 375 mana. A bargain for 3 or more group members.
Wizard
  • The New Wizard "Familiar" Spells (Wiz) Durations modified from 0+3 (skill based, an hour-ish) have been changed to 0+3600, which is 6 hours. This is an aberration from the normal time settings and may represent a hack, in which case duration could be anything Verant wants.
Enchanter
  • Enchantment of Striking, Enchantment of Luminance, Enchantment of Savagery, Enchantment of Grace, Enchantment of Force, Enchantment of Tormenting -- all removed in favor of the following new spells:
  • Intellectual Advancement (Enc 12) Added. Not quite sure what effect 112 is at this time, parameter is 3.
  • Intellectual Superiority (Enc 20) Added. As above, but parameter of 6.
  • Haunting Visage (Enc 29) Added. I believe that "Effect 114" is an Agro MULTIPLIER. With a parameter of 110, the player with this "buff" up will do 110% Agro (or 10% more than usual) to any MoB they attack.
  • Calming Visage (Enc 39) Added. Again effect 114, but parameter is 90. This should cause a character's agro to be reduced by 10%. In other words, keeping this buff on the clerics should keep them off the top of a MOB's agro list -- and out of harm's way.
  • Beguiling Visage (Enc 54) Added. Effect 114, Parameter 50 but SELF ONLY. Also lasts only 54 SECONDS. However, this spell cast immediately before Tashing or debuffing an uber-mob would certainly be worth while. Maybe the Dragon-Tashing Enchanter isn't on a suicide mission anymore :)
  • Horrifying Visage (Enc 56) Added. Effect 114, Parameter 130. Targettable. Lasts only 54 Seconds. Since this spell can be targetted, imagine the effect of casting this on your Main Tank as he engages his discipline on an uber-mob: His damage output is not only good, and warrior epic's proc'ing for extra agro, but this UNRESISTABLE buff increases that tank's agro by an *extra* 30%.
  • Glamorous Visage (Enc 58) Added. Effect 114 (Agro Multiplier), Parameter 70. Targettable. Lasts only 54 Seconds. This could be used on a wizard before he begins the finishing Nukes, etc.
  • Illusion: Imp (Enc 49) Changed -- Resist Fire component was increased from +15 to +30 Sv. Fire
  • Tricksters Augmentation (Enc 52) Level requirement for the spell reduced from Enc 56 to Enc 52.
  • Trickster's Torment which is invoked by Trickster's Augmentation (Above). The Taunt Factor has been reduced from +200 Agro to +100 Agro on each successful proc of this spell.
BeastLord
  • Beastlord pet healing spells were given new names
  • BeastLord pet buff spells were given new names
  • BeastLord Pet duration changed from 0+3 to 0+3600 (same as Wizard Familiar changes). They may now be permanent, or 6-hour duration. I suspect a code hack that makes 0+3600 a special number.

Soandso Visage
I believe that the new soandso-Visage spells (see the Enchanter section) are going to be an extremely important part of the agro-management end game, especially since Mez is utterly useless in epic encounters, many mobs are uncharmable, all high level mobs are un-stunnable. Here are a few preliminary thoughts:

  • There are two possibilities on how the Visage spells work. First, they could affect all agro a player currently has and continues to accrue. I doubt that this is the case. Alternatively, the spell could affect agro that is gained while the spell is up.
  • If the Visage spells affect existing agro, they could be used for direct agro control. You could cast an agro-reducer on a player to reduce their agro and possibly move them out of harm's way. Likewise you could cast it on a non-agro person and they could become the prime agro player.
  • If the Visage spells do NOT affect existing agro, as I believe likely, then the spells must be cast in advance of agro generation (or, at least, used with the understanding that they'll only affect upcoming agro effects). This means you'd need to coordinate with the main tank for a 'signal' that they're about to charge or engage an agro-discipline.
  • These spells can be used to passively balance unbalanced groups. Give 110% agro to the Paladin/SK/War and 90% to the Ranger/Rogue/Monk, and they won't need to resort to time-wasting (and therefore, damage-output-reducing) Jolts, Stuns, Evades, etc.
  • As I said earlier, a 50% self-only agro modifier is a great life-saver when the time comes to Tash/Slow/Cripple that bad mobbie. Also, you can afford to do more agro-causing things, or do them earlier.
  • Are the spells going to be group-only? If not, I know I can see some abuses coming. If a group annoys an enchanter, all he'd need to do is cast 110% agro on the Cleric, 90% on the tank(s), and watch the group get slaughtered. Thankfully, most enchanters are not anti-social :) Another resolution would be to make these spells "click to deactivate" by the recipient.
  • These spells should affect ALL types of agro. Nukes, DoT, Melee, Proc's, Stuns, etc. That makes it useful not only for tank agro management, but for casters too -- a Necro with a 90% agro multiplier up can afford to lay more DoTs on a mob before having to Feign or take a dirt-nap.
  • Most buffs can be cast on pets. If so, I sense a resurgence in Necro rogue-class pets...
  • If this spell affects agro, does it affect kill credit percentages? Is it possible to "kill-steal" by buffing your own group and debuffing others?


Melee Changes on Test
9/21/01, 7:31 PST | Kwill

The following changes were posted on the test server:
** Melee changes **

We're wrapping up an extensive look at the way that melee works in EverQuest. These changes are appearing now on Test (in fact, most of them are working as of right now). We wanted to make you aware of these changes in some detail so that you can give us the feedback that we need on them. Please try these changes out and comment on results that you've seen.

We will be beginning our final analysis on casters in the very near future.

* Melee Tables *

Melee combat is based, at least in part, on tables that determines how each class functions. Up until 50th level melee and hybrid classes are on the same table, with the exception being monks who are on a separate table (one that has them doing more damage). At 50th level warriors, rogues and rangers were all moved to the monk table along with the monk, who did not receive a new separate table.

The problem here is that post 50th level the disparity between these two tables, combined with lower combat abilities for the hybrids as well as a more restricted selection of weapons acted as a double (almost triple) penalty, making them less effective in combat than was really intended.

Therefore, all melee and hybrid classes will be basing their combat off the melee table once they reach 51st level. This should increase the combat effectiveness of hybrids noticeably.

Monks, however, are intended to be somewhat better at dealing out damage in combat than warriors. To slightly improve their combat prowess they will be working from an improved combat table once they reach 55th level and again at 60th level. This will improve the combat ability of monks somewhat, essentially placing them a half a step ahead of everyone else in toe-to-toe combat in the same way that they were prior to 50th level. The rogue will still maintain a margin of superiority when you consider their ability to backstab.

* Two-Handed Weapons *

The effectiveness of these weapons falls slightly below our desire for them. This tends to make those that rely on them most often (shadowknights and paladins) also fall slightly behind our desires for their damage output, at least at levels beyond 50. We'd also like to see a broader range of weapons used by higher level warriors, and it would be nice to see two-handers as a more viable option.

Therefore, we will be increasing the minimum damage of these weapons for all classes once they reach 50th level. There will be an increase in the minimum damage of two-handed weapons based on the wielder's level and delay of the weapon. The higher level the character and the larger the delay, the bigger the bonus. Obviously we're not going to give out exact numbers, but this should result in a solid increase in damage output when using two-handed weapons.

We also believe that there has been a bit of disparity between the very high-end one-handed weapons and their counterpart two-handed weapons. The ultra-high end one-handed weapons are comparatively better than their two-handed counterparts. We will, on a case by case basis, be improving many of the high-end two-handed weapons. Because this is essentially a fix for an oversight on our parts, we will be making these changes retroactive (meaning you won't have to trade the items in or hunt for new ones, existing weapons will just change).

* Rangers *

We've also noticed that rangers, while intended to be hit harder in combat than other melee/hybrid classes, are getting hit harder than we planned. Again, this only happens in the 50+ level range. So we will be increasing the defense cap for rangers again slightly to 210 at 60th level. Exactly how these points will be distributed is uncertain, but probably just one point per level after 50. We will also be giving rangers triple attack. Note: This change was reversed later, as the team felt that rangers did not need triple attack as a necessary upgrade.

* Monks *

Monks will be getting triple attack. This along with the improvement of their combat tables should place them about where we want them, combat-wise.

* Bards *

Bards are indeed intended to have the least ability to deal damage physically of all the classes we're discussing. However, with their lack of double attack they didn't meet even our intentions for their damage output. We will, therefore, be raising the offense and one-handed weapon caps for bards over 50th level to match that of warriors. This should place their ability to do damage by means of melee within our intended range without making them more effective than the knights at any given level, since double attack is a potent melee skill that bards do not get.

Obviously we want to see how this all pans out when applied to the game. Feel free to send your comments on the results you see to us through the Developer's Corner on our web site, or contact our staff if you see them on the Test Server.

It is very important that you understand that these are changes that we are testing. These changes (and any others made on the Test Server) may not be moved to Live Servers. After all, that's the reason we're testing them.

- The EverQuest Team

Absor summarized the changes this way: "After all of these changes, hybrids will not be able to deal out the damage of a pure melee class, with the possible exception of rangers, who might be able to get close. The warrior, monk and rogue will still out damage hybrids, and by a noticeable margin. That difference is in part what hybrids lose for the power of their spells."

There is much more to this discussion on the Sony Developer's Corner, including Absor's responses to player suggestions and concerns about the proposed changes.

Trivial Loot in SoL
9/21/01, 7:29 PST | Kwill

Brad McQuaid posted this letter recently to the Developer's Corner:
Hello all,

I wanted to update you all on our current thoughts regarding the Trivial Loot Code as it relates to Shadows of Luclin zones.

As per my last couple of 'Ask Brads', we've made both an effort to explain what the Trivial Loot Code does and why we were considering it for all Luclin zones. I'd also mentioned, however, that if most players appeared to be against its inclusion we'd not use it.

After talking with players, reading emails and feedback, and then very recently running a poll, it is indeed apparent that the majority of our playerbase does not feel that the Loot Code's advantages outweigh its restrictions.

In fact, after polling over 200,000 players, most want the looting system left alone, almost as many want us to explore a different solution for bottom feeding and rampant farming, and a minority were in favor of the Trivial Loot Code as it exists today in the Warrens and Stonebrunt Mountains (the remainder either didn't have an opinion or were unfamiliar with TLC).

Well, so be it.

We will head back to our drawing boards and keep brainstorming on this issue, but at this current time we do NOT intend on implementing TLC for the zones in our upcoming expansion, the Shadows of Luclin.

I do want to thank everyone for all the feedback and discussion regarding this issue -- there was a lot of debate surrounding this issue, and we've done our best to pay attention.

-Brad McQuaid
Chief Creative Officer & Vice President
Sony Online Entertainment/Verant Interactive
