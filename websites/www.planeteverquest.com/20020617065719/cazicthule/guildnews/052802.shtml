In Memorial...

Posted by: Druss on Monday, May 27th, 2002 @ 11:53 AM

"...that from these honored dead we take increased devotion to that cause for which they gave the last full measure of devotion, that we here highly resolve that these dead shall not have died in vain, that this nation under God, ...and that government of the people by the people and for the people shall not perish from the earth." --Abraham Lincoln

No EQ update today. No sarcasm and silliness. Although this is a game-related site, today it is important that we remember those who paid the ultimate price for the freedoms we enjoy today. The freedom to even sit back and play silly games like Everquest, the freedom to enjoy BBQ's and picnics with friends on a day off from work. And maybe even freedoms more weighty and serious than these...freedoms we often lose sight of, only because those in our generation haven't had those freedom's threatened, nor had to pay the same heavy price asked of those who have gone on before us to preserve those freedoms. So for a moment, lay aside your thoughts of this game, and remember those who gave the "last full measure of devotion".

Roleplaying...Verant Style!

Posted by: Druss on Tuesday, May 21st, 2002 @ 10:36 PM

Most of us "old folks" hooked up with EQ when it came out because of our love of Roleplaying games. Many of us, myself included, skipped the UO/Meridian/The Realm/Etc Mud scene, so EQ was our first experience "Roleplaying" in a persistant online world.

Most of "us" loved fantasy environments, and most of us had background playing either single player RPGs, or tabletop gaming. We never imagined "I_337 D00dz", Ninja looters, Phat Lewt Whorz, Intentional trainers, Kill/camp stealers, or any number of ills unique to online gaming. I know a lot of us expected some roleplaying, in the way players would communicate with each other if nothing else. I still go a little bug-eyed when I see some guy shout "Need SoW at orc3 PST!!!" or some other inane crap like that. Heroic names like Mysogynist Wifebeater and Labia Majora (Yes, both were actual characters on the CT server, god save us) do little to maintain the illusion of an immersive world. Even the encounters seemed poised to trash the roleplaying aspect of the game - remember the only way for a Paladin to get Ghoulbane (which in the old days was the ONLY "holy" sword available) was to kill a Froglock paladin...a good aligned creature waging war against the undead, someone who should have been an ally to the PC. Or Conversely....a Troll SK seeking the best weapon available to him (Soul Leach), had to kill his God to get the weapon. Yeah, GG Verant.

Time passed, Kunark came and went, with Verant's first attempt at having Faction actually mean something beyond not being able to go into certain towns. We have the disaster of Venril Sathir faction. Evil races who wanted to actually be welcome in their "evil" city were forbidden from hunting in most of the high level dungeons. Gee...so they can hunt only in OS, or they can choose not to be welcome on any city on the new continent.

Then came Velious, where we found, for once...an actual benefit to faction. Pick a side, ally with it, kill its enemies, and be rewarded. It worked beautifully for a while.

Why the nostalgia trip? Well...it comes from witnessing the Dain runs the past week (we offed him twice). Now, keep in mind, half the casters of the guild are madly working on their 8th shawl, and a good quarter of the guild are working on their coldain rings. And yet...here we are, Killing the Dain.

I can almost hear the Dain asking "WTF D00d?!?! Didn't you just save this town?" as he gets hit by the damage shield on my ring.

Add to this the comedy of 60% of the raid logging as the Dain hits 30% health, to avoid the faction hit. And yes, I'm one of them - I've grown pretty fond of those little DA scrolls he hands out.

Anyway..I guess the point of the rant (Other than bumping the pictures of Taraddar in drag further down the page so that maybe I'll be able to start receiving Focus and other shamanly buffs at raids again), is to admit something most of you probably realized long ago. EQ has about as much in common with roleplaying as a game of Quake.

In other news....Dain died twice, as I mentioned before, as did the Creator, Glyphed, Exiled, Yelinak, Tormax, and I forget what else.

Another update later this week - I'll be roleplaying the part of a Zergling until then.
