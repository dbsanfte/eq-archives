Info from the VI Developer's Corner
6/25/01, 12:36 PST | Kwill

Here is the latest from Absor and friends:

Why Can't I be a Monster?
A player asked, why don't enchanters get a monster illusion? Absor answers that it's about faction and body type, because if you looked like a monster, then the other monsters wouldn't kill you. Isn't that the same as invisibility, another player suggested? Absor adds:
..an illusion is different than invisibility. There is no 'see invisibility' for illusions. The places where we've purposefully staged NPCs that you just can't sneak by would be compromised by such spells. NPCs treat different body types differently, and an illusion essentially gives you a body type....

Nobody denies that it's an interesting idea. It's just not something we're planning to do.
Read more on this thread.

Changes to Spell Interruptions
On test, Absor reports, they have changed it so when you duck to interrupt a spell (you all know to do this, right?) it is interrupted immediately. This was to stop an exploit. Absor says they plan to leave it this way for now, but it may change as of the next patch. A bard asks, "Will this interrupt songs as well?" Absor responded:
When you are casting a spell or using an item that casts a spell or a song, you will no longer be able to sit down. You will still be able to sit while singing. In fact, you will now be able to start singing while already seated.

Ducking will immediately interrupt the casting of any spell or the use of any item. It shouldn't affect songs at all.
Remember, this is only on test. Read the whole thread here.

Changes to Essence Emeralds?
Absor responded to a player's request about the use of Essence Emeralds:
We've already discussed Essence Emeralds, actually. We don't have any plans to change the way they work or the spells that use them. We want those spells to be rarely used and difficult to gather components for. We want them to be cast rarely and with good reason. We think the spells and the Essence Emeralds are right where we want them.
Here is the complete discussion.

Shadowbound Spell Overwrites Skin Like Nature?
Lawrence Poe replies:
This issue should be fixed next patch, as well as many other problems with Skin Like Nature/Natureskin being overwritten by DOTs like Splurt and Asystole. They will now stack instead of being overwritten.
Read the question and answer here.

Reporting Mana
Absor has this to say:
What players have been asking for (for those of you just tuning in) is the ability to use a %m in macros that would report their percentage of remaining mana. We've said that we don't intend to do that. The reason is basically two-fold. The primary reason is that people living in the world of Norrath don't use phrases like "Hail, %t. I am ready for the next pull, as my mana is at %m." The secondary is, well, we like the vague nature of the mana bar. We don't show the numbers, and that leaves us disinclined to show percentages either.
For more discussion on this topic, Absor directs you to this thread (which rambles).

There is always lots more in the Developer's Corner, so check out the EQ official forums!
