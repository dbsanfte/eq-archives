How the Scars of Velious Ended My Life
by Whakk De'Ogre - Greenblud Ogre uf Cazic Thule

6:00am Awake to Alarm, today is VELIOUS!!! YES!!!

8:00am Make it in to work, boy I wish had taken today off...

8:25am Can't wait any longer... dowload the last page of the 12 days of Velious for later reading

11:00am Break for an early lunch, spend 45 minutes walking around downtown, unable to find a computer store that sells games

4:00pm Claim I skipped lunch (well, I didn't eat), leave work early

4:15pm Begin reading the 12 Days of Velious downloaded from Sony's website while on the train; Gnomes, Dwarves, and Giants, everything to get the saliva flowing...

5:30pm Tear out of parking lot in excitement; almost clip a Transit Bus diving for the freeway on-ramp...

5:47pm Pull into CompUSA, dash inside

5:55pm After briefly attempting to explain the concept of an on-line role-playing game to a pimply 15 year old I'm informed they don't have it. It says so in their computer system.

6:05 pm On way to next store; call Girlfriend, let her know I won't be seeing her to-... well, for a few days

6:12pm Call friend on cell phone; yell at them for not buying me my crack-fix, they hang up mumbling something about being afk in the middle of an Ice Cave, but not before mentioning EBX (a computer games store)

6:12pm Cut across 2 lanes of traffic, changing direction to EBX

6:32pm 16 year old kid informs me that Scars of Velious has been sold out for 3 hours. I mumble, "You've got to be kidding me." He hears me, "Well, what did you expect, something like 300,000 people play it every night" I yell, "YEAH?!?!? Well OBVIOUSLY I realize that better then the IDIOT who does your bulk ordering!" Kid looks stunned at a customer who isn't cowed by his EBX golf-shirt, I knock over a display case for "ALICE" on the way out... evidently there ARE other computer video games.

6:48pm Call another friend (attempting to keep sane) while on my way to Best Buy. They can't talk now, they're hunting snow orcs. I curse their ancestors. They laugh at me.

7:32pm Stop at Wallmart (it's a shot) while on my way to Best Buy. The slack-jaw behind the counter offers "Big Game Hunter" instead of Velious. Mocking my world. I grab him by the collar and shake him. He still doesn't have the game. I run out the door immediately. "Best Buy... Best Buy... Best Buy..." I am now mumbling to myself (and maybe drooling a bit)

7:49pm Call a second EBX. They're sold out too. A man who claimed to have bought the last 7 copies at the first EBX bought their last 12 copies. They deny that any of the employees bought Velious for personal use. I offer $50 for a copy. A slight hesitation in their voice causes me to raise my offer to $80. They still claim they have no copies. They protest that I'm tying up their phone line when my offer hits $120 and hang up.

7:57 Call girlfriend again. Propose the "Sony" theory. Sony is attempting to create civil chaos in America by stratifying society through denying benefits to the priviledged few. I cite the Sony PSII as well as Scars of Velious before I realize I'm talking to a newly dead line...

8:12 Run through the aisles at Best Buy. Bump into a large warty man who was squating and fantasizing to the back of "Tomb Raider Gold Edition" sending him face first into the shelves. Dodging (most) other customers I begin to yell "Velious!" hoping some Avatar of Mithaniel Marr will answer me and lead me to the icy continent. An out-of-uniform employee stops me and asks if I need something. With no knowledge of the depth of crack-addiction, he casually refers me to one of the blue shirted pre-teens that looks remarkably like the guy from EBX. The fourth grader tells me it's not on the shelves (as if a blind man would play video games). I agree with him that that's true unless the product is invisible, which sends him into a moment of confusion, feeble mind attempting to measure the possibility that an invisible computer game called "The Scars of Velious" actually IS out on the shelves. I brush him aside and interupt a manager helping folks with a washer in the next section. He, perhaps noticing the unstable glint in my eyes, agrees to check to see if they came in and haven't been stocked yet (at that point, the best thing he could have done for his future children).

No Velious. My eyes bulge. He stares at me dubiously. With the truth beginning to dawn, I scream and fall to my knees sobbing, grasping at a display of Kid Rock CDs for support. Job was never so oppressed. People awkwardly shuffle around the wreck on the floor that was once an EQ player. The manager leaves for a problem at the front register. Two employees snicker at me from down the aisle - they have no idea.

8:42 I stop at the liqour store for a 12pack after buying the gun at the pawn shop. I seem to worring that I didn't have enough PP on me before realizing that American money would be accpeted in the shop.

8:54 Open the first beer while loggin on to EQ

9:03 First guild-mate mentions Velious.

9:12pm Finish the 6th beer. Begin to write "How the Scars of Velious Ruined my Life"

9:27pm Finish guzzling the 7th beer. Load the gun.

9:28pm Get the 42nd tell from a friend saying basically, "OMG you've got to see this!", right as I look out at the landscape just outside the North Wall at Karnors Castle for the umpteenth time and realize it still hasn't changed.
