Upgrade to Direct X 8
11/3/01, 0:00 PST | Kwill

Absor posted this to the Developer's Corner page :

In the next month or so, prior to the release of EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin, we will be moving to DirectX 8. This will be true not just for those purchasing Shadows of Luclin, but for everyone playing EverQuest.

In fact, some time soon, prior to the new interface rollout, we will be giving everyone the DirectX 8 version of the graphics engine dll. This will give players the option of using the new dll to help us find any hardware incompatibilities prior to the time we move to DirectX 8 game-wide. We'll be testing as many configurations as we possibly can in-house, but with the huge number of possible hardware configurations we can't test them all. The plan here is to nail the problems down now and have as many of you updated to DirectX8 as soon as we can before we make the switchover.

Why are we doing this? Well, you'll need it to use the new interface and we need it for all of the good things it lets us do for the game, primarily improving framerates (yes, even in the old world and even if you don't buy Shadows of Luclin). For a good idea of the new features we'll be able to implement with DirectX 8, check out our latest In Development (the EVERQUEST TECH DOCUMENT at the bottom).

Upgrading to DirectX 8 is pretty simple. Here's what you do:

1) Download DirectX 8.0a. Store it in a directory so you can find it when you need it in a few minutes, but don't install it yet. You can find them on Microsoft's site. Make certain to get the driver that is appropriate for your operating system.

2) Locate the most recent drivers for you video card and download them into the same folder (this makes them easier to find). Don't install these yet either. Do this even if you have the latest drivers installed. If they have a text file with installation instructions, you might want to get that too (or print it out).

3) Locate the most recent drivers for your sound card and download them into the same folder. Again, don't install these yet, and go ahead and download them even if you have the latest drivers installed. You might also want to get any installation instructions for the drivers. You're going to need to reinstall them shortly, so it's best to have them on hand.

4) Set your video adapter to the Standard Display Adapter (VGA)

The steps required to do this will probably vary for each of you a little bit. But here are the steps I followed to change this on my computer (keep in mind that I am using Win 98 and the procedure will be different if you're using Win 2k or XP):
a) Click Start, then Settings, then Control Panel.
b) Double Click on Display
c) Click on the Settings Tab. Then click on the Advanced Button.
d) Click on the Adapter Tab, then choose Change.
e) Click Next.
f) Select the radial button (the little circle) next to the option for Display a list of all drivers and click Next.
g) Select the button next to the option for Show all hardware.
h) Scroll to the top of the list and select (standard display types). Then from the window on the right choose standard display adapter (VGA) and click Next. i) It will ask you if you really, really want to use this adapter. Choose yes.

5) Now install DirectX 8.0a by finding it in your folder and double clicking it. Follow the instructions for installation.

6) NOW you need to reinstall your video drivers. Use the new ones that you saved in that folder. Follow the instructions for installation given by the manufacturer.

7) Install the latest sound drivers for your sound card, also following the instructions given by the manufacturer.

That should be it. You may want to reboot after this if the last driver installation didn't already force you to do it, just to make sure.

NOTE: It is important to reinstall the latest video and sound drivers after you install DX 8.

Yes, if you read that carefully you noticed that I said something about a new interface. We will be releasing a new and impressively customizable interface to all EverQuest players with the release of Shadows of Luclin. I don't have a lot of details for you about the new interface at this time. I can tell you that we think you'll be very impressed with it. The main feature of this new interface will be the customizability that it gives the players. We're still building it, so I can't really elaborate, but there is no doubt in my mind that you'll be impressed, or in the least intrigued.

We wanted to let you know about this as soon as we could to give you as much time as possible to make the upgrade. Please consider updating to DirectX 8.0a as soon as possible. EverQuest runs fine with DirectX 8 now, so you shouldn't see any problems running EverQuest after the update.

We will be double-checking our audio and video support page to make certain that it is up to date. If you need help with audio or video problems, including finding a driver for your cards and identifying your cards, check out our Audio and Video Support Page


Test of Tactics Timing
11/3/01, 0:00 PST | Kwill

Lady Daegarmo responded to a player's question about the timing of events:
In every event that we run, we always have issues with the start time. Remember, while a 4pm pst sounds early to us West Coast people, on East Coast that is already 7pm est. If the event runs 5 or 6 hours as some of them can if its a huge event such as Test of Tactics, it could be a very late night indeed for someone.

Also please remember that EverQuest is a world wide game, and our European players, Australian players, and Asian players also need to be taken into consideration as well for some of the events.

With the ending of the "Best of the Best" first round series, as well as what could be the Final Test of Tactics, I am looking to design shorter duration, higher intensity events that can actually be scheduled by the guilds that are participating in them, at the time of that guild's choice. While this is a "Hopeful" in the future, it is something that I am planning on making happen.

Feel free to join me in discussion on events on the Harpy's Head Tavern Message Boards, right here at www.everquest.com, where I do answer questions daily on events, as well as responding to ideas.

Exceptions to the /Movelog
11/1/01, 9:38 PST | Kwill

The move to the Antonious Bayle European server is on, but there are some exceptions to who can move there, according to this update from Absor on the Developer's Corner:
Characters from special rules servers will not be moved to the new server, nor will characters be moved from Test. This includes the Zek servers as well as Firiona Vie. The rules for play on those servers are different than on the standard servers, and we want to preserve that uniqueness. Also, gaining levels and equipment is going to be either easier or harder on those special rules servers, so to maintain a level playing field we don't move characters across rulesets.

IMPORTANT! Loot any corpses you might have before the move date! While we will be moving all equipment and banked items and cash and all that, we won't be moving corpses. So if you leave something on a corpse you will lose it.

Absor also reminds peopple about names:
If two characters are moved to the server with the same name, one of them will randomly have an X appended to their name. You can then ask a GM to change your name if you don't really want that X appended. Character names must be unique, so someone has to lose their name. That's one of the unfortunate necessities of moving characters.
