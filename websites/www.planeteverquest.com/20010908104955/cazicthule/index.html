    OMG Horses!!
    9/5/01, 12:29 PST | Kwill

    This just in....horses will be a part of the Shadows of Luclin Expansion. This is great news for your horse-crazy reporter! Here is the press release:

    SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT INTRODUCES HORSES INTO THE WORLD OF EVERQUEST

    -EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin To Offer Massive Gameplay Enhancements

    SAN DIEGO, CA – September 5th, 2001 – Sony Online Entertainment, a worldwide leader in multiplayer online gaming announced today that mounted horses will be added into the world of Norrath when EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin ships to retailers worldwide this December. Players will be able to purchase a horse of their own in designated locations and will be able to summon their horse with the aid of a magical whistle. Players will be able to traverse greater distances in shorter periods of time and even battle from their mounts.

    With the addition of all new player models, numerous additional monsters, and an enhanced game engine, EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin offers EverQuest subscribers an incredible amount of benefits with no additional monthly fee. Set against sweeping moonscapes, Luclin provides a new look and feel for EverQuest players and an entirely new world to be explored. Players can begin anew on Luclin or find their way to Norrath’s moon on their own. How ever players get there, a new adventure awaits.

    "We are committed to expanding the world of Norrath, creating new adventures and unexplored territories for EverQuest fans around the world," said Jeff Butler, Producer, Sony Online Entertainment. "We've always prided ourselves on giving great value with all of our expansions packs but Luclin goes even further than previous installments. This is going to be an incredible journey for all EverQuest fans."

    EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin ships this December. For more information on EverQuest and EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin, please visit http://everquest.station.sony.com/luclin

    Spell Changes on Test
    9/5/01, 10:07 PST | Infernal

    General Changes:
    • Aura of Soandso Petals (PoM Click Effects) Are now all Self-Only. Black Petals was previously still targettable.
    • Journey: Fay, Tox, North, and Combine are no longer Targetted Teleports -- instead, they are an Self-Radiating AE Rain. This implies a 4-target limit, does not necessarily require group membership, and has been upped to a 30-second Casting Time. Draw your own conclusions, I have no idea what the purpose of this would be.
    Magician Changes:
    • Transon's Elemental Infusion (Mag 52) now buffs the Mage's pet with 580 HPs for 275 Mana, upgraded from 556 HPs for 250 Mana.
    • Rod of Mystical Transvergance (Mag 56) Mana cost increased to 400 Mana. The clickable effect Mystical Transvergance is unchanged, causing 300 HP damage and adding 220 Mana.
    • Transon's Terrible Torment (Mag 58), a 950 Nuke saved vs. Cold for 350 Mana, was replaced with Transons Phantasmal Protection (Mag 58), a +5 HP/tick Regen, +3 Mana/tick regen, +24 AC buff.
    Wizard Changes:
    • Garrisons Mighty Mana Shock (Wiz 20) ADDED. A fast-casting, -148 Mana Sieve DD, costing 90 mana -- mana sieving rate will be approximately 28 Mana/second, comparing poorly against Mana Sieve which has a sustained Mana drain rate of 33.6 Mana/sec. Also unfortunately, this spell has a resistance check versus Magic.
    • Familiar (Wiz 54) was fixed: It incorrectly buffed Resistance vs. Fire twice, for +20 each. Now buffs +20 Fire and +20 Magic in these same slots, as was likely intended the first time.
    Paladin Changes:
    • Instrument of Nife (Pal 30) ADDED. Imbues your weapon with the Proc Condemnation of Life, a Magic-based 243 DD vs. Undead. (Starts at 223 damage at lvl 30)
    • Wave of Life (Pal 49) - Group Heal 110 HP for 225 mana, was REPLACED with Valor of Marr (Pal 52), a 120% self-only Haste buff. Does not stack with ANY of These Spells, so it's fairly useless unless you're soloing or have no haste-ing class.
    • Divine Fortitude (Pal 52), a +100 HP self-only buff, was REPLACED with Thunder of Karana (Pal 49), a Targetted 1-second Stun and 36-second Spellcasting SILENCE Spell. Very Nice!
    • Words of Conviction (Pal 54) Now does 108 DD, in addition to the existing 10-second stun component.
    • Wave of Divinity RENAMED to Nife's Touch (Pal 58), otherwise unchanged.
    • Worbolution (Pal 60), a watered down Aegolism was REPLACED with Crusaders Protection (Pal 60), a +200-HP Group Buff that should stack with anything OTHER than These Spells.
    Ranger Changes:
    • Tangling Weeds (Rgr 9, Dru 1) ADDED, currently an exact duplicate of Snare. I would expect some changes shortly to differentiate it from the existing spell.
    BeastLord Changes:
    • BeastLord Pet Buff 2 (BL 22) Base effect parameter improved from 16 to 22. This may set the pet's Level.
    • The Rest of the BeastLord Pet Buff series was unchanged.

    Account Security Policies

    (1) Account security -- whether it be password protection, running virus checks, disabling file sharing, or any other element of making sure accounts are not stolen or compromised -- is the sole responsibility of the account owner.

    Trivial Loot and Quests
    9/3/01, 9:53 PST | Kwill

    Absor gives a lengthy response in the Sony Developer's Corner to a player's question on a quest in Stonebrunt Mountains that requires a character be a certain level to start the quest, but a higher level to complete it. He says, in part:
    Quests are not always designed to be completed all at one time. Many quests are designed for the character to grow into, so that a character might begin a quest at a certain level that he can't complete until he reaches a higher level. A lot of people see quests as something to be done all at one time, and that's just not the case.

    So it might indeed be true that some quests in areas that use the Trivial Loot Code can't be completed by folks that didn't discover the beginnings of the quest in that zone when they were of a level to do so.
    This policy is an important one to understand, as the Shadows of Luclin expansion will be using trivial loot code in every zone. For Absor's explanation of Verant's policy on trivial loot and quests, read this thread on the Sony Developer's Corner.


    Server Population Numbers
    9/3/01, 9:33 PST | Kwill

    /wave goodbye to seeing server population numbers in the near future on the login screen, as well as doing a /who all in game. This will also affect seeing these numbers on fan sites.

    Reasons given by SOE staff for this change include the benefit of reducing server loads and the increased competion in the gaming market. John Smedley, COO, had this to say about it:
    Due to the increasing amount of competition in the online gaming space we feel that it's not in our best interests as a company to provide our existing and future competitors with valuable information on our userbase. We have always had a very open stance on the issue of what our numbers are specifically, but as we grow in other markets around the world this information is simply not something our competition needs to know.
    Absor assures that this should have little impact on players, as the servers can still be sorted by population to see which are the most or least populated at any given time.

    Server numbers may not be missed much, but not being able to perform a /who all may certainly impact a number of players, notably guild leaders and raid leaders. Read the entire commentary on the Sony Developer's Corner.

    Hacker Incident
    9/3/01, 9:07 PST | Kwill

    A Seattle teen has been charged with hacking into the EverQuest computer systems one year ago, including Brad McQuaid's home systems, as reported by Mike Carter in The Seattle Times. FBI agents seized ten computers and computer gear from the teen's home last week. Apparently the teen was in league with another man from Alabama.

    After breaking into the system, the hackers downloaded a file with blueprints for EQ2. Was this file actually Shadows of Luclin, we have to wonder?

    Last October, the Seattle Times reports, these individuals hacked into the Sony computers and obtained control of a superuser account, which allowed them to access other people's accounts as well as change their own.

    At this point, no one knew what they had done and they had free rein to pillage at will. However, it seems it's no fun to do something bad without bragging about it. The teen sent an email to a Sony executive, attaching the stolen files, so it would be clear that the computers had been hacked. This led inevitably to the teen and his accomplice being caught by the FBI.

    Sony Online Entertainment had this response to the incident on the everquest.com site:
    We have seen the article in the Seattle Times regarding an incident almost a year ago in which someone apparently got a hold of one or more superuser accounts for a short period of time. We want to assure our customers that at no time was any consumer billing information compromised. No one should be worried that their credit card information got out... IT DID NOT. In addition, some time ago we added additional layers of security in an effort to prevent this from happening again. Beyond this, it is inappropriate for us to comment in light of the ongoing investigation.
