<a name="PQN430063"></a>
<br>
<span class="headline">What's it Like to be a Guide?</span><br>
<span class="byline">6/16/03, 3:48 PST | <a href="mailto:muskie@planeteq.com">Muskie</a></span><br><br>
<span class="newsitem">Yesterday I managed to interview a Guide after a wedding in the Plane of Knowledge between Ruuj and Naiyani. (I forgot the spelling of your name, Sorry. if you read this, please email me at <a href="mailto:muskie@planeteq.com" target="_top">muskie@planeteq.com</a> so I can fix it.) A high elf married an ogre... And yes, she was drunk. :P Anyway.
Upon flashing my PEQ Norrathian Press Pass (tm) I managed to secure an interview with Miednight the Pouncing Bard, a member of the Everquest Guide program. Mind you, the interview is a little rough, as he was working on petitions, and I was working on my smithing skills at the time. (I hit 210! yay!)<BR><BR>

<b>Muskie: How easy was it to be accepted into the guide program, and what was your training like?</b><BR><BR>

Guide Miednight: Hmm.. I'm not too sure of the acceptance rules that they use, but a body of SOE reviews your app and either accepts you or not. Time length can vary. For myself it was pretty rapid..maybe a few weeks after I submitted my app. As to my training....=)<p>I had one the best trainers that has ever guided and trained: Xukuth of the Dragon. She is without a doubt an exceptional trainer. She made sure I knew if I made a mistake and corrected me on it. Stern, hard but fair and caring. I put in long hours to make sure I knew the policies and she was a constant friend and stern mentor through it all. Still remains to be to this day.<p>Please note though that these are my own opinions and other guides will have other experiences =)<BR><BR>

<b>Muskie: Is it true that Guides get their own spell lines?</b><BR><BR>

Guide Miednight: hmmm...how to answer that =) No we don't but to find out what we do get, you'll need to become a guide and discover the mysteries yourself first hand ;)<BR><BR>

<b>Muskie: One thing I do know is that you get your own zone called the Guides Home... could you elaborate on what the zone is like? At least tell us what kind of terrain it is, etc?</b><BR><BR>

Guide Miednight: Aye that I cannot do =) Our home is a sanctuary and is one of our most highly guarded secrets. But I can tell you it is a beautiful zone, SOE's Development team did a great job for us =)<BR><BR>

<b>Muskie: What about your experiences as a player? How do you play EQ when you're not answering questions, marrying characters, etc?</b><BR><BR>

Guide Miednight: I can only answer that as a Guide who has no petitions to work on....We are encouraged to walk among the citizens, explore areas that are new to us, seek out new things and new experiences, meet new people and help as best we may. Personally I hop from zone to zone meeting people, watching and learning =). We have the ability to do quests for players, marry them, and basically promote the good will of the policies of SOE and the server.<p>'A good example was the wedding tonight. [It was]unplanned and [I] got the request a few minutes beforehand. Knowing how much it would mean to the bride and groom, I made the room and came to preside =). Anything we can do within limits to help further the experience that is EverQuest, we try and do =)<BR><BR>

<b>Muskie: So you don't go out and bash up some goblins if you feel like it in your free time?'-grin-'</b><BR><BR>
Guide Miednight: No we don't =). Goblings, giants, etc...all the mobs of Norrath, Luclin and the Planes have a right to exist as the players do. From time to time they will complain about each other and we must mediate between them. A good example is the other night..I had to mediate a dispute between the goblins and orcs in Misty Thicket! Nasty thing but it was finally resolved with an exchange of weapons and warbeads, plus safe passage through Runny Eye and Crushbone. Doesn't happen often but it does happen! =)<BR><BR>

<b>(Note: Misty thicket had actually gone down for maintenance earlier in the week.)</b><BR><BR>

Guide Miednight: Being a Guide and a protector doesn't just mean for players. It also means for all life on Norrath, Luclin and the Planes =)<BR><BR>
<b>Muskie: Being OOC for a moment, is the ability to roleplay well as you do a requirement of being a guide?</b><BR><BR>
Guide Miednight: No but they do ask us to Roleplay as much as we can. Some of us are decent at it, some are better. For me, I just be me...furry and pounceful. lol<p>
<b>Muskie: Do you have any other Experiences in being a guide that you would like to share?</b><BR><BR>
Guide Miednight: Hmm other experiences....Oh I've had many in the hours i've logged just over the last week =) I guess what I would say is that for everyone to play fair, and know that Guides have play characters too. We have been in their shoes and probably will be again after our guide shift ends =). We understand their frustrations and their anger. Plus their joy and their sorrows. When they petition we try to get to it as fast as we can, sometimes it is quick, others it is not but we do the best we can... So to be patient with us and try to not take their frustrations out on us. We are here to help and cussing and yelling at us does not help...plus it is against policy :P. Also I think if every player can learn the commands that govern their petitions..the /viewpetition and /deletepetition it would be helpful. Also to learn to use /report for issues as it only gives us more proof to assist them =)<p>Beyond that to enjoy themselves and to have fun. If you see a Guide, say hi, be nice and smile. You might just make our day as normally all we deal with are the issues and problems that happen in EQ =)<BR><BR>
<b>Muskie: Ok, last question, probably the biggest of all...</b><BR>
Guide Miednight: sure =)<BR><BR>
<b>Muskie: What do you, overall, think of EverQuest itself?</b><BR><BR>
Guide Miednight: Hmmm ;P ... This has to be one of the biggest and best online games there is. I've played most of them out there and I come back to here for a few reasons...the people..good and bad, the people are what make this game! Sure we have bugs but each online game does. The ability to grow in a community here is probably one of the best features of this game.<p>The changing and additions to the game itself. Playing below 50 is an experience in and of itself but when you get over that lvl ... it's like a whole new game =) I love it hehe<BR><BR>
<b>Muskie: Well, thanks for your time, it's been great talking to you.</b><BR><BR>
Guide Miednight: Any time. If you ever have any problems, questions, feedback...please let us know =) that is what we are here for =)<BR> 
<BR>
It's also "Hug a Guide" week in EQ ( Read <a href="http://www.gucomics.com" target="_top">GU Comics</a> For more info), so, if you see a guide out there on your server, be sre to hug Him/Her, Ok?</span><br><br>
</td>

<br>
<span class="headline">New Expansion: Lost Dungeons</span><br>
<span class="byline">6/13/03, 19:06 PST | <a href="mailto:kwill@planeteq.com">Kwill</a></span><br><br>
<span class="newsitem"><a href="http://www.gamespy.com/interviews/june03/eqldonpc/" target="_top">GameSpy</a> has an exclusive interview with the SOE's Shawn Lord about the new expansion, Lost Dungeons of Norrath!  This is a very unique expansion, focusing on dungeon crawls:<blockquote>The lands of Norrath are still expanding. Faithful followers of Sony Online Entertainment's incredibly popular MMORPG -- EverQuest -- are about to get the chance to open another expansion pack for their beloved game. Lost Dungeons of Norrath is different, however, than the previous four expansions. Focusing firmly on single group dungeon crawls, LDoN brings some cool new technologies to the table. GameSpy recently got to speak with Lead Designer, Shawn Lord, about LDoN. Read on to get the exclusive scoop on what you and your EQ-happy friends can expect to find to later this fall. <p>

 

GameSpy: What's the idea behind LDoN?<br>
Shawn Lord: The Lost Dungeons of Norrath is designed to bring back the thrill of the dungeon crawl. There's a certain sense of danger that comes along with being a small group of heroes traveling into the depths of a dark cavern seeking adventure. LDoN is built for single groups of characters that want to push their way through a dungeon from beginning to end. There won't be any "camping" of spawns. To further your adventure you'll have to forge ahead and see what lies in wait for you in the gloom around the next bend.<p>
GameSpy: Please explain how the new "Dynamic Dungeon System" technology works.<br>
Shawn Lord: Your group can approach certain NPCs who will ask them to perform a task or explore a location. If the party leader accepts the adventure, the party will be given what is, essentially, a key to the dungeon. All members of the group will be able to enter that dungeon to face the dangers there, but other player characters will not. These dungeons will be created on the server specifically for their use, and will only exist as long as the characters are having their adventure.</blockquote>Read much more about the upcoming expansion in Bryn William's <a href="http://www.gamespy.com/interviews/june03/eqldonpc/" target="_top">article.</a>
</span><br><br>

That's the bad news. The worse news is that we will have to recreate all of the characters on the server from backups that are perhaps more than 24 hours old. This may make some of your characters appear to be missing. They are not missing. All you need to do is delete one or more of the visible characters in order to see the ones that are not shown.<p>
