The Council of Arcana was formed for a multitude of reasons, both ambitious and practical. Ordained through necessity and volition, the Council was founded in 5 August 3172 in the High City of Erudin, on the Continent of Odus. The ultimate purpose for the Council of Arcana is to uphold the Sacred Six Virtues of Law, Order, Justice, Honor, Truth and Comradery. Highly structured and disciplined, the Council only grants membership through invitation.

Under the leadership of Kaelon Xerxentis, the Arch Chancellor of the Council and a member of the High Council of Erudin, the Council of Arcana has sought to harness and regulate the power of Norrath and to enrich the meaning of life, consistent with its beliefs. The purpose of the Council's existence is described in its greatest document, the Principles of the Council. In its function, the Council is governed by two major documents, also listed below.

Regularly Updated Documents

An Introduction to the Council
This document is a brief, clearly written introduction to the Council of Arcana, its community and expectations, benefits and responsibilities of membership.

The Prime Charter
Detailing its organization, structure, purpose and power, the Prime Charter is the sacred text created by the Founders of the Council of Arcana. It explains the relationship between the Arch Chancellor, the High Council and the general membership.

Principles of the Council
The Purpose for the Council of Arcana's existence, and ultimately, the driving ideology behind its formation was approved by the High Council by a unanimous vote calling for direction and meaning in the Council's actions and objectives.

The Code of Honour
The Council of Arcana and all of its members swear to uphold the Six Sacred Virtues, and as such, pledge to live by, in and through an honor code of conduct and behavior, setting themselves as examples by taking the higher road of civility and gentility.

Out of Date Documents

Ranks
Complete listing and comprehensive annotation of all titles, posts and positions in the Council of Arcana. A must-read for the aspiring officer.

Membership Reform Act of 3184
The MRA serves as the central text and legislative document governing membership admissions, the mentorship program and uniform membership policies for the Council of Arcana. It is available as a reference.

Quaestorial Commission
Frequently asked questions about the Council of Arcana are answered.
