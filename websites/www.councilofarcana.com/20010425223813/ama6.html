TITLE VI: REVOCATION, SUSPENSION, AND EXPULSION

Art. 50: Members of the Council of Arcana are given expectations of their association in the Council of Arcana, and if these expectations, principles, or facets of confidence or trust are violated or otherwise misappropriated, then said abrogations are to be approached and properly disciplined.

Art. 51: Methods of discipline are to be consistent with the values and virtues of the Prime Charter, intended to correct individual behavior and conduct, as well as to set examples for the collective as a whole.

Art. 52: The High Council shall be the exclusive legitimate body for arbitration, adjudication and the dispensation of member discipline, and any officer or member seeking to restore the integrity of the guild and its alignment with its virtues by disciplining another member, should approach the High Council and petition for its intervention.

Art. 53: If the High Council finds that a guild officer has been repeatedly idle or negligent in his or her duties, it may judge to perform a revocation of said officer's rank and status, demoting him or her to no further than the rank of Councilor.

Art. 54: If the High Council finds that a guild member has been repeatedly inactive to the extent that his or her integration into the guild or appreciation of its values has been compromised, it may judge to perform a revocation of said member's rank and return him or her to a new Service Period, during which time he or she assumes the rank of Junior Councilor and undergoes the same procedure as specified under the articles of Title IV.

Art. 55: The High Council shall, through its adjudication and arbitration, have the option of issuing a suspension, wherein a member is deprived of his or her membership, rank, rights, privileges, status, and otherwise association with the Council of Arcana.

Art. 56: The High Council may determine the length of a suspension, not to exceed ninety (90) days.

Art. 57: The High Council may assign the conditions for re-integration of the member after suspension, after if met, the suspended member may be re-integrated with the rank of Junior Councilor, and the expectations and duties thereof.

Art. 58: The High Council shall, through its adjudication and arbitration, have the option of issuing an expulsion, wherein a member is deprived of his or her membership and association with the Council of Arcana for a period no less than ninety (90) days, and at its extreme, permanent status for perpetuity.

Art. 59: Expelled members may petition the Arch Chancellor for a Writ of Dispensation to re-apply for membership, either standard or special, after ninety (90) days.

Art. 60: All members of the Council of Arcana have the right to appeal to the High Council for review or investigation of any individual, matter or affair.

Art. 61: The findings and judgments of the High Council may be appealed to the final review of the Arch Chancellor, pursuant to the Prime Charter.

Art. 62: The Senior Council and its members are to have an exclusively consultative and advisory role in the affairs of the High Council's role as adjudicator and arbiter in the Council of Arcana.

Art. 63: Members of the Council of Arcana, regardless of rank, have the option to engage in an act of self-revocation, otherwise known as resignation, wherein said member will surrender the rights and privileges of membership and disassociate himself or herself from the Council of Arcana.

Art. 64: Members seeking to resign honorably will inform the Arch Chancellor and the High Council of the intent to resign and remain associated and in reputable service with the Council of Arcana for a period as requested by the High Council, not to exceed fourteen (14) days.

Art. 65: Members who resign honorably shall have the option to re-apply for membership after ninety (90) days, without any special writ or authorization, and shall be considered a friend of the Council of Arcana if the High Council deems it appropriate.

Art. 66: Members who resign dishonorably, that is, without the provisions of service in courtesy, honor and respect as aforementioned, shall not be permitted to re-apply for membership until one hundred and eighty (180) days have passed, and shall be considered a foe of the Council of Arcana if the High Council deems it appropriate.

Art. 67: Members who resign shall have the privilege to appeal for Clemency, as specified in Title VII.

Art. 68: The High Council, with the approval of the Arch Chancellor, may at any time override, suspend or implement alternate rules for discipline as specified within these articles, so long as these rules are consistent with the values of the Prime Charter.
