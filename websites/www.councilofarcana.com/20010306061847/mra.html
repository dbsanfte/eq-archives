The Membership Reform Act of 3184 was passed by a Call of Acclamation of the General Assembly and the High Council in order to establish a uniform membership policy and trial-membership mentorship education period for all members. It includes detailed information in rather legalistic terms, but is available herein for reference.

MEMBERSHIP REFORM ACT OF 3184
Co-Sponsored by
Kaelon Xerxentis, Arch Chancellor
Luciano Manente, High Councilor

WHEREAS the Council of Arcana is an institution and organization of like-minded individuals, supported and committed to our Sacred Virtues, the Principles of the Council, a Code of Honour and constituted through its Prime Charter, and;

WHEREAS the Council of Arcana, as a guild of individuals, constituted exclusively of self-selected members through the consensus of the existing membership, should have a membership admissions and education policy consistent with its goals, objectives and the very same aforementioned principles to which it holds true, and;

WHEREAS the current, undocumented and informal practices and policies provide for an often-confusing, inconsistent and contradictory method for the admission of members, and the approval of junior members to full-membership status, and;

WHEREAS the High Council, as the Council of Arcana's final arbiter, and the Committee on Membership, as the High Council's advisory committee on membership affairs for the guild, both should collaborate to provide final clarity and permanent resolution on this issue, therefore;

BE IT RESOLVED that the "Membership Reform Act of 3184", herein contained, be fully endorsed by the Council of Arcana's membership, and approved to be enacted immediately retroactive to 24 April 2000, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Council of Arcana AMEND the Prime Charter as follows:

(1) Article II, Proclamation 7 to read "For the first period of membership, new members shall be titled 'Junior Councilor', and be required to study under a mentor. Junior Councilors will be nominally in the guild, but shall not be confirmed a full member of the guild until satisfactory completion of this study."

(2) Article II, Proclamation 8 to read "Once Junior Councilors have demonstrated to full satisfaction their appreciation for arcana to the acceptance of an officer of the guild, and supported by the mentor, shall be granted full membership by said officer, with approval from the High Council exclusively, and thereby promoted to the rank of 'Councilor'."

(3) Article V, Proclamation 28 to read "In the case of chapters or committees being designated by the High Council for functional purposes, an individual may be appointed by the High Council, not a member of the High Council, but with guild officer authorities, to act as a 'Senior Councilor', with the authority to appoint and dismiss new Councilors within the locality and jurisdiction so specified without the overseeing and immediate deliberative review of the High Council, but will be required to submit an advisory and periodic report for final affirmation."

(4) Article V, Proclamation 29 to read "The post of 'Senior Councilor', either in General or of Review over a specific committee, may be revoked by full consensus of the High Council, or be broad authorization from the Arch Chancellor."

(5) Article V, Proclamation 30 to read "In the formation of a new committee for the Council of Arcana, a committee mission statement must be prepared with specification of the local chapter/committee headquarters and the full name of its Senior Councilor, for the approval by the High Council.", and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in recognition of necessary clarity that does not belong in the Prime Charter, but through the legislative documentation which forms the basis for action moreso than principle policy, the Council of Arcana AMEND the High By-Laws as follows:

(1) The High By-Laws, in whole, are to be repealed, and the Arch Chancellor is directed to re-write them, and submit them through affirmation and declaration of the new Membership Policies contained herein, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the natural process for an unaffiliated, first-time applicant to petition for membership in the Council of Arcana is as follows:

(1) A veteran ("Councilor", or higher) member of the Council of Arcana must announce over guildchat that s/he is supporting someone for membership in the Council. With no objection received, s/he may direct the interested applicant to fill out and complete the application form found on the Council's web site at www.councilofarcana.com.

(2) The Board of Admissions will review the application and through consensus - in whatever way the High Council or the Arch Chancellor judges appropriate, validate the consensus through a vote, so that both the the majority of discussants and peers of the Councilor are satisfied with the applicant's petition to join, with no opposition from any member of the High Council.

(3) After a period of no less than four (4) days, but not to exceed ten (10) days, if the High Council or Arch Chancellor are dissatisfied, the application is to be rejected and dismissed, the petition to be declined. Otherwise, if accepted, the High Council is to publicly and personally direct the invitation of the new member as a 'Junior Councilor' of the Council of Arcana.

(4) Should the candidate for membership be of Level 40 or higher, an alternate application method should apply, in that the sponsor must obtain the support of three (3) High Councilors within the Plane of Norrath, direct the applicant to submit a Class 2 Application form at the Council's web site, and then call upon the guild to unanimously accept the admittance of the new member. With no objections and the above pre-requisites met, the new candidate is immediately inducted into the Council of Arcana as a 'Junior Councilor'.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Council of Arcana adopt the following procedural policies for repeated-applicants for petitions to join the Council:

(1) If the applicant has been declined on a previous application, a period of thirty (30) days must pass before s/he may reapply or have his/her petition re-examined by the Board of Admissions.

(2) If the applicant was expelled from the guild or resigned from the guild, a period of ninety (90) days must pass before s/he may reapply or have his/her petition re-examined by the Board of Admissions.

(3) If the applicant has been expelled or has resigned more than once in the past, s/he may not reapply under any circumstances and will not be permitted to reassociate with the Council of Arcana, unless by a Writ of Authority from the Arch Chancellor or a consensus of the High Council, in which case, the previous provisions apply for re-application.

(4) All applicants that re-petition for membership must be sponsored, each time, by a different and unique veteran member of the Council or Arcana, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in the case that a member of the Council of Arcana departs or resigns from the guild under conditions of duress, through acts or judgments of passion, and/or under personal haste or error, that said member have a fifteen (15) day period in which he or she may be reconstituted into the Council of Arcana by any member of the High Council or agent of the Arch Chancellory, provided that at the time of the reconstitution within the said period, there be no objections by any members of the guild present, both as to the re-constitution and/or readmission, as well as to the fact that the initial departure is qualified under the above conditional criteria, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Council of Arcana designate newly admitted members as 'Junior Councilor', with all privileges and benefits derived from membership, EXCEPT that s/he may not vote in any elections, referenda of the Council, or sponsor or mentor any person for membership, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that all Junior Councilors be immediately enrolled in a Mentorship Program, wherein the newly admitted Junior Councilor must seek out a "Mentor", that this Mentor be only of the rank of 'Councilor' and not a sponsor of said Junior Councilor during the petition/application process, and that the Mentor guide and supervise the Junior Councilor's transition into the Council of Arcana for a period of new less than ten (10) continual hours of adventure and instruction, and that the Mentor guide and supervise the Junior Councilor's transition into the Council of Arcana for a period of new less than fifteen (15) days, nor any more than thirty (30) days, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Mentor is assigned the specific functions and duties in the obligatory role in supervising the transition of the new Junior Councilor, and that the relationship between Mentor, the Council, and the Junior Councilor be as follows:

(1) Mentors are to group with Junior Councilors more than once, and for a minimum of ten (10) hours. (Amendments One, Two)

(2) Mentors are to meet with a Junior Councilor informally to discuss the Council of Arcana at least once.

(3) Junior Councilors will be required to submit an essay detailing their experience during the Mentorship Program.

(4) Per Internal Affairs' Recommendation on Junior Councilor Inductions, no member of the Council of Arcana may power-level or give any items, currency or material assistance to a Junior Councilor, until fully confirmed a full-member and Councilor by the High Council.

(5) All mentors are to be active members of the Council of Arcana in good standing, and approved after the fact by the High Council.

(6) All mentors are to be offerred access to an overseer from the High Council, that can offer assistance during the mentorship process in the education of a Junior Councilor.

(7) Mentors are prohibited from passing judgment on a Junior Councilor.

(8) Mentors may not discuss his or her personal opinions on the performance during the Mentorship Program with a Junior Councilor.

(9) The High Council retains the exclusive right to promote, dismiss or retain Junior Councilors in the mentorship program.

(10) The Mentor is to serve as a neutral observer, and a guiding resource for the Junior Councilor, reporting observations to the High Council in a final report.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that within the specified period of conclusion for Membership, the Mentor must fill out a Mentorship Form at the Council of Arcana detailing the completion of activities and education that verses the Junior Councilor through adventures and exercises in (1) Law, (2) Order, (3) Justice, (4) Truth, (5) Honor, and (6) Comradery, that the Mentor be the creator of such activities, discussions or adventures which satisfy these requirements of the Mentorship program, and that this report from the Mentor be required for any considerations for conclusion of the Mentorship period, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the High Council be responsible for reviewing and, through the discretion of the Arch Chancellor, find consensus in accepting or rejecting the Mentorship period, wherein if accepted and confirmed by the High Council -- the Junior Councilor is to be promoted to Councilor in a monthly ceremony and derive full membership benefits, and if declined, the Junior Councilor is to be either (a) dismissed from the guild, or (b) assigned to a new Mentor to re-engage in new mentorship period, and;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that should the High Council decline a second Mentorship period, that the Junior Councilor be immediately dismissed from the guild, permitted to re-apply for petition for membership (and thus repeat the process) under new conditions for membership forty-five (45) days after dismissal, and;

BE IT FINALLY RESOLVED that the Council of Arcana's High Council reserves final opportunity to judge, review, observe and overturn any ruling of any of its committees through weekly review and weekly response of said committee reports, and that the Arch Chancellor, as the protector of the guild and defender of the Principles upon which the Council is founded, may singularly direct action contrary to the recommendation issued by a Committee thereby preserving the Sacred Virtues, upholding the Principles, demonstrating the Code of Honour and defending the Prime Charter to its ultimate determination for the community of family and friends that shall always remain the Council of Arcana.

Proposed on this Day, the 26th of April, in the Mundane Year of 2000.
Signed:
Arch Chancellor Kaelon Xerxentis
High Councilor Luciano Manente
Proposed Jointly to the Committee on Membership
(later, the Board of Admissions) and the High Council
