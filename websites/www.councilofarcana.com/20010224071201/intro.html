An Introduction to the Council of Arcana
Updated 20 February 2001

What is the Council of Arcana?
The Council of Arcana is an association of players on the Quellious Server of the popular massively-multiplayer online role playing game, EverQuest. While some guilds are formed for the specialized focus of experience-gathering or treasure-hunting, the Council of Arcana exists to promote a community based upon trust, respect and friendship. Thus, one can consider the Council of Arcana a true family of kind and caring players.

What are the pre-requisites for membership?
Membership in the Council of Arcana is through invitation only. Potential members should be grouping and adventuring with current Council of Arcana members -- when the time is right, you will be invited and sponsored for membership. We always welcome new faces over our message forums and encourage you to introduce yourself and be a part of our popular web site.

What is expected of members?
Members are expected to follow a set of principles and guidelines that govern conduct and attitudes, as well as to conduct themselves in accordance with our Code of Honor. Members should be kind, courteous, respectful and supportive of one another, and should keep in mind that the Council of Arcana is a family environment that is inappropriate for profane or adult-themed discussions over guildchat.

What should members expect of the Council of Arcana?
Members of the Council of Arcana should expect to be part of one of the richest and oldest guilds on the Quellious Server, with a detailed history and reputation for excellence that is spotless. Members will find themselves welcomed and supported by their fellow members, and can rely upon their officers for guidance and counsel on all affairs -- likewise, members should take advantage of these resources by regularly being a part of the community and contributing to the collective what they derive. Formally, the Council of Arcana holds between two and four formal raids and guild-wide adventures per month - geared at satisfying the breadth of adventure for the average level of guildmembers. Informally, however, there are dozens of concurrent groupings and raids going on throughout the week - and members are encouraged to lead their own activities and involve their companions.

What should members not expect of the Council?
Members should not expect the Council of Arcana to be a port/res/power-leveling/twinking service. While members are usually more than happy to help, please note that most members are usually involved in activities of their own and cannot drop everything at a moment's notice to assist members with matters of a relatively low priority. That being said, the Council does ensure that members requiring immediate assistance (e.g., a non-recoverable, about-to-expire corpse requires rescuing) do benefit from the loving and supportive community. And, more often than not, members can find one another to help with resurrections or teleports. Nonetheless, members should not expect this to be a given. Furthermore, members should not expect that being in the Council of Arcana will give them instantaneous group access. If no groups are available - there are usually good reasons for this, and we encourage you to start up your own group or adventure and involve others in your activities.

Who leads the Council of Arcana?
The Council of Arcana is structured as outlined in the timeless Prime Charter, wherein sovereign power is vested in a collective of guild elders that are both experienced and active participants in the Council of Arcana family. The membership elects its advocates, Senior Councilors, who serve to convey their ideas to the higher echelons of leadership and support them in their pursuits. The High Council consists of both founders of the Council of Arcana as well as former Senior Councilors who are promoted through internal vote. Finally, the High Council is led by the First Speaker - a rotating position that all High Councilors share - and the Council leadership is presided by the Chancellory. Description of Flow-Chart
The "Line of Constituency" refers to the democratic line of accountability and responsibility of each participant in the leadership of the Council of Arcana. The general membership elects Senior Councilors, which in turn are selected to make up the High Council, and finally, the High Council elects the Chancellory. The "Line of Authority" describes the type of power that each tier of authority wields -- the Chancellory sets the agenda, which in turn the High Council translates into policy initiatives, and the Senior Council and membership, in turn, openly enforce and follow. Finally, the "Sphere of Activity" describes the nature of interaction between tiers. Councilors and Senior Councilors "consult", or exchange ideas and brainstorm. Senior Councilors and High Councilors translate these ideas into solutions and legislative results. Finally, the High Council and the Chancellory act in unison to carry out the executive decisions of the guild.

This sounds pretty complicated. How much does this play into the Council?
While the Council of Arcana is formalistic and adheres to codes, rules and procedures that are honored in time and practice, these elements are ritualistic and secondary when it comes to the pursuit of fun and friendship in our family. The organization becomes necessary for the leadership of the guild and more often than not, the Council of Arcana's organization and hierarchy has been a source of strength and continuity for our guild's existence.
