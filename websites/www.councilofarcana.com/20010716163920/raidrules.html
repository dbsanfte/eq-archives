COMMON SENSE RAID RULES

Kaelon Xerxentis, Lanenya Silverfyre
Last Updated 13 May 2001

The Council of Arcana is a higher level guild, and our members raid. A raid is not an experience group - a raid is a multiple group operation wherein everyone operates as a team in attaining an objective larger than any one individual. Raids are laborious and fun. They can also be laborious and hellish - so, prepare for them and use your common sense, and you may just survive any experience having at least grown closer to your guildmates amidst adversity. This seven-point plan is simple, direct, and uses common sense.

1. Prepare for the Raid

  • BE INFORMED. Learn about the zones that we intend to raid. Print out maps and have handy information. Study zone guides and details in advance before the raid. If you are not doing this, then you're simply expected to being held by the hand - and we know that you're capable of so much more. Whatever you do - don't sit in the dark. Get zone maps and info from our resource links. Know where the raid location is. Be at the raid location BEFORE the intended start time.

  • ARRIVE PROPERLY PROVISIONED. Make sure that you have at least 40 food, 40 drink, and sufficient regents for your personal spells and enhancements.

  • SETUP YOUR CHAT FILTER. Use /serverfilter on command to filter battle spam by reducing lag. Turn off all unimportant battle messages so that you can more easily read instructions being delivered by the raid leadership. Make sure that your chat screen is legible and easily maintained so that you know what is being said and done at all times.

  • DO NOT COMPLAIN ABOUT WHERE WE RAID. Plans will change. Sometimes, the primary raiding location or activity will not be available and we will switch to backup plans. Be flexible. It's hard to plan a raid and even harder to change plans midstream. This is a time to follow directions, not clutter up guildchat with suggestions and complaints.

 

2. Arrival and Time Commitments

  • ARRIVE ON TIME FOR THE RAID. This is critical to ensuring that the raid gets off on time. Other players are dedicating their real life time to making it on time. If you don't arrive on time, you are showing disrespect to your fellow guildmates who put forth the effort to be there in a prompt and timely fashion. Arrange your own ports and travel ahead of time, and only bother the raid leader or guild members if you cannot find transportation. Ideally, arrive early.

  • COMMIT TO THE HOURS. Raids will have starting times and locations posted in advance on the message boards. Please be at that location at or before that time. Arriving late may count against you for looting purposes. Attendance for weekly events, also posted in advance, is as mandatory as it gets. If you haven't posted otherwise, you are expected to arrive on time and be in full attendance. You are expected to be present as long as reasonable for completion of the raid. Time estimates are usually commonplace and common sense. Don't commit to the raid unless if you can be there for the entire duration. Real Life matters and emergencies are always understandable - but if you are online, we are counting on your support and presence.

 

3. Chat

  • Please remain silent. The difficulty of the raid is indirectly proportionate to the amount of public chatter allowed. The harder the raid, the less talking there should be. Raids are not intended to be a social gathering - they are competitive and complex multi-group operations. Practice the discipline of silence, and you shall go far. Save discussions for after the raid, preferrably on the message boards.

    • /ooc and /shout channels are reserved for raid leadership and raid instructions only. When you see colored text, that means an important set of instructions have been relayed. Keep guildchat clear for this purpose as well, if necessary.

    • Guildchat should be used only for specific questions to clarify instructions.

    • Off-topic, non-raid, and goofing around discussions should remain in private tells and groupsay.

 

4. Pay Attention

  • GENERAL. You are responsible for everything that is said. You must be paying attention to the raid instructions and events around you. If you are not paying attention, you become a liability to your companions and you jeopardize the success of the raid. Practice common sense and don't be afraid to ask questions of fellow group members if you're unsure of what is going on.

  • MOB PLACEMENT. If mobs are being pushed into walls or other three-dimensional objects -- back up! This is just common sense; no one should have to be told time and time again to back off from a mob that has been mispositioned in combat. Mobs must be engagable during combat, and lootable when dead. Pet owners should use /pet back off to ensure that the mob is not engaged when mobs are misplaced. Exercise reason.

  • OBEDIENCE. If the raid leader says something, do it.

  • BE TEAM ATTENTIVE. Look around you, and assist others as you see they require assisting. Teamwork is the foundation upon which success is built - and fun is shared best together.

 

5. Basic Class Instructions

  • GENERAL: Make sure that you create an /assist hotkey. Always know what creature is being engaged by pressing it periodically, refreshing your target. Do not attack until you have acquired a target by way of /assist. Always, please, follow the directions of the raid leadership.

  • CASTERS: Do not chain-cast, and use tactics and caution when casting spells. Use common sense, and do not get agro'd by spacing out your attacks and assisting the main agro team.

  • CLERICS: Please restrict your activities to healing and buffing only, unless otherwise instructed.

  • TANKS: Set your assist hotkeys to the main assist. Do not hit anything until you have obtained a target via assist. Continually upgrade your latest target by pressing the assist hotkey several times before, during, and after each encounter.

  • BUFFING: Buffers are asked to set timers so that when buffs begin to fade, they can rebuff everyone for the specific fading buff in question. This will eliminate the constant requests for buffs under ordinary, non-combative circumstances.

 

6. Morale

  • GENERAL. It is crucial that raiders not do anything to lower morale or weaken the unified spirit and resolve of the participants, regardless of the adverse conditions being experienced. If morale is lowered, raids will become more difficult to complete and less enjoyable. Keep the tone positive, encouraging and supportive of one another. Specific ways to do this are as follows:

    • Cease all whining. Whining about loot or experience is completely inappropriate. Most participants in a single, short-term raid will most likely leave with no material rewards or experience gains. Save comments regarding the raid's events for the message boards - there should be no negative discussion at all.

    • Do not complain. In general, if you have a problem with how something is being handled - do not complain publicly about it. Instead, send a supportive suggestion to the raid leader. If you want to comment on how something is being done, save it for the message boards. Be supportive of the raid leadership, and be a resource to all of your guildmates.

    • Do not send tells to any of the pullers. They require silence and your complete support in order to concentrate. They are the heartbeat upon which the rhythm of the raid is played out.

    • Be supportive. Mistakes will happen, and when they do - always be there to encourage and cheer up fellow guildmates. You can use constructive criticism to help others improve on their tactics only after the raid has concluded.

 

7. Miscellaneous

  • TELLS TO RAID LEADERS: Raid leaders are very busy. They are always on top of everything and need to have total concentration. Please do not send them tells unless if it is absolutely critical and imperative.

  • AFK: Going away from keyboard (afk) should only be done when it is important. It should be the exception rather than the rule. If several others are already AFK, you should remain on until others have returned. Always be sure to inform your group leader and group mates whenever you go AFK.

  • CORPSE RECOVERIES: If corpse recoveries have gone under way, you should remain in attendance to support your fellow guildmates. They need your comforting, understanding and encouraging. Please do not leave a raid until all corpses are recovered. You should only depart if a real life circumstance has itnervened.
