TITLE II: STANDARD ADMISSIONS PROCEDURES

Art. 11: Potential candidates for membership will first adventure with a wide range and diverse group of Council of Arcana members for an extended period of time prior to seeking the sponsorship of another member, ranked Councilor or higher.

Art. 12: The Councilor that sponsors a candidate for membership shall assume the role of "Sponsor", of his or her own volition in accordance with the pursuit of the type of member sought for the guild, and the said sponsor shall be charged with the duty and responsibility of introducing the candidate to other guildmembers, providing the candidate with opportunities to meet and adventure with other members within his or her leveling range, while also introducing the member to the rules, procedures, activities, and general concepts behind the guild itself.

Art. 13: After both the candidate and the sponsor agree to move forward, an application for membership may be completed and submitted for consideration, debate, discussion and approval of the Board of Admissions, a forum limited in access to only members ranked "Councilor" or higher.

Art. 14: In order for a candidate to be accepted into the Council of Arcana, he or she must receive the support as voiced within the Board of Admissions, or provisionally in another oral or written form verifiable to an officer of the guild, of at least five (5) percent of the guild membership.

Art. 15: If there are any objections or oppositions to the candidate's entry into the Council of Arcana, it shall require an additional five (5) percent of the guild membership to override said concerns, in the same fashion as in the aforementioned article, this process being repeated until all opposition is quelled.

Art. 16: If the candidate fails to receive the pre-requisite support in the Board of Admissions for acceptance into the guild within a period not to exceed fourteen (14) days, then said applicant's petition for membership shall be rejected, and an officer of the guild shall inform the candidate of such a status, referring him or her to these articles for further guidance.

Art. 17: A first-time rejected candidate must wait a period of thirty (30) days before submitting a second application, during which time, he or she should re-evaluate objectives, goals, activities, priorities, and exposure to the Council of Arcana, if the intent for the candidate is to re-apply, and should seek the counsel of his or her sponsor.

Art. 18: Candidates for membership do not enjoy the rights or protections of the Prime Charter until they have been accepted in the aforementioned fashion, and properly inducted into the guild.

Art. 19: Candidates that have had their applications accepted shall be inducted into the Council of Arcana with the rank of "Junior Councilor", and/or "Initiate", by an officer of the Council of Arcana, preferrably in the presence of a neutral witness, so that the Rite of Induction may be conducted, during which time the candidate must swear the Oath of Induction by publicly reaffirming said commitments.

Art. 20: The Rite of Induction shall have an Oath of Induction, wherein the candidate is informed that the Board of Admissions of the Council of Arcana has approved his or her application, and asked if he or she accepts the judgment and invitation of his peers; this being accepted, the Oath of Induction is recited, compelling the member to swear fealty to the Council of Arcana's family, elders, and patrimonial patriarchy, as well as to adhere and exemplify the virtues, principles and codes of the guild.

Art. 21: Only upon public confirmation of the Oath of Induction shall an officer then conclude the Rite of Induction by seeking any final objections from the general assembly over guildchat; with no objections being found, the officer shall then induct the new member with the rank of Junior Councilor.
