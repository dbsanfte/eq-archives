TITLE IX: EXCEPTIONS

Art. 89: The High Council of the Council of Arcana may, with a two-thirds superior majority vote, override the AMA, either in part or in whole, for any reason declared or undeclared.

Art. 90: The Arch Chancellor may issue an Edict suspending, limiting, overriding, invalidating, or abridging any section or sections of the AMA, either in part or in whole, for any reason declared or undeclared.

Art. 91: It is the duty of the Arch Chancellor to immediately suspend the Admissions and Membership Act, and all of its activities, if it is found to be in violation or conflict with any other overriding policy, practice or procedure, and to resume its course only if and when the conflict has been resolved in the standard and appropriate fashion.

Art. 92: The High Council may outline other reasons or conditions wherein it is appropriate and required to suspend the AMA.

Art. 93: The Admissions and Membership Act may be amended by a simple majority vote of the quorum of the High Council, and is subject to the Arch Chancellor's veto or popular relegation to other bodies, including but not limited to the Senior Council and the General Assembly, as are all legislative documents separate from the Prime Charter.

Art. 94: When there is perceived an inconsistent or conflicting application of the AMA on any member in good standing of the Council of Arcana, any member may petition for a review by the High Council or the Arch Chancellor, thereby suspending final judgment on the inappropriate application until a resolution is deemed concluded.
