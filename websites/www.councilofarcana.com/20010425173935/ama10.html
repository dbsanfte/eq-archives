TITLE X: THE PRIMACY OF THE PRIME CHARTER

Art. 95: The Prime Charter of the Council of Arcana shall remain the foremost document governing all actions and activities of the Council of Arcana, its members, leadership, and integral policies, procedures and laws, irrespective to the individual merits of the actions being pursued in an otherwise infringing act.

Art. 96: The AMA should govern the standard and natural course of quotidian admissions and membership in the Council of Arcana, and should not require re-interpretation if on a standard or natural basis.

Art. 97: The Prime Charter should govern the special courses not explicitly derived or explained from the scenarios outlined by the Admissions and Membership Act, unless if otherwise amended in future sections or titles of these articles.

Art. 98: The AMA may not create new institutions or sub-organs of the Council of Arcana not previously specified in some fashion by the Prime Charter, nor should the AMA's power be invoked in order for any member, officer, or official to do such.

Art. 99: The Admissions and Membership Act may be suspended temporarily in the fashion prescribed by the previous Title.

Art. 100: Only a unanimous vote of the High Council, or a singular decree of the Arch Chancellor, may revoke the Admissions and Membership Act, either in part or entirely, during which time an interim set of flexible principles may be applied to govern the duties and activities prescribed in the revoked portions of the AMA, until a suitable replacement or amendment may be implemented.
