As Promulgated on 5 August 1999
Last Amended on 28 April 2001

PREAMBLE

The Founders of the Council of Arcana, scholars and adventurers tested in own right, proclaim that the articles herein form the constitution and governing charter of this guild, having been proposed, discussed, amended and ratified by all originating members. May the Prime Charter serve as our governing principles and embodying beliefs which compel us to pursue our identity and ultimately the destiny of all life.

ARTICLE I: THE HIGH LAW

Proclamation 1 - Be it proclaimed that the guild formed be known as 'The Council of Arcana', with or without article of distinction.

Proclamation 2 - The Council of Arcana shall be resolved to, forever in its perpetual purpose, uphold the virtues of benevolence to the extent of comradery, honour, truth, law, order and justice, and in pursuit of these six dictates, seek mastery over all that is arcane to thereby save the world of Norrath from forces which emperil it.

Proclamation 3 - The Council of Arcana, with reverence to all who follow our Second Proclamation, shall form its center of guild operations in the City Offices of Erudin Palace, High City of Erudin, Odus; with all reunions meriting personal appearance occuring within said chambers.

Proclamation 4 - In pursuit of our Second Proclamation, the Council shall authorize the partition of land, authority and power amongst certain members to propagate membership throughout Norrath and thereby allow it to grow and flourish.

Proclamation 5 - To establish our firm virtues of benevolence, the Council shall deliberate on all issues of arcane or magical purpose, and reflect upon such applications throughout the world, resolving if necessitated for any such direct interventions.

ARTICLE II: INITIATION AND DISMISSAL

Proclamation 6 - All members of the Council of Arcana shall, prior to their initiation as such a member, be offered sponsorship by a fellow member, or be endorsed and condoned by an officer of the guild.

Proclamation 7 - For the first period of membership, new members shall be titled 'Junior Councilor', and be required to study under a mentor. Junior Councilors will be nominally in the guild, but shall not be confirmed a full member of the guild until completion of this study.

Proclamation 8 - Once Junior Councilors have demonstrated satisfactorally their appreciation for arcana to the satisfaction of an officer of the guild, and supported by the mentor, shall be granted full membership by said officer, and thereby promoted to the rank of 'Councilor'.

Proclamation 9 - An Induction Ceremony or Ritual, approved by consensus or directive, shall be performed periodically to ceremonially welcome new Councilors into the Council of Arcana.

Proclamation 10 - Should a Councilor disobey, demerit, discredit, disdain or deceive the guild, any of its members, its officers, or its leader, shall be reviewed by the officers in session and, if so judged, stripped of title and authority by an officer, and dismissed from the Council.

ARTICLE III: THE HIGH COUNCIL

Proclamation 11 - The Council of Arcana and all its members ('Councilors') shall be overseen and directed by the High Council, consisting of 'High Councilors' who act as officers of the guild.

Proclamation 12 - High Councilors will have the authority to initiate and dismiss members, either alone or when in collective session as the High Council.

Proclamation 13 - The High Council will convene regularly to appraise members of the status of the guild, the welfare of the Council, and the developments in pursuit of its purpose.

Proclamation 14 - The High Council may singularly amend the Prime Charter by superior majority vote numbering no less than two-thirds of all officers present.

Proclamation 15 - The High Council may singularly remove any officer of the guild, for whatever purpose, by superior majority vote numbering no less than two-thirds of all officers present.

Proclamation 16 - The High Council may conduct business only on the provision of a quorum of all its members being present; the absence of the quorum will render any activities of the High Council thereby invalid until the quorum re-established.

Proclamation 17 - The Founders of the Council of Arcana shall be High Councilors for all perpetuity.

Proclamation 18 - Any member of the High Council may resign his or her commission as a High Councilor by submitting, in writing, to the guild leader a statement of such resignation or abdication of authority, citing reasons for such action and the date of effectiveness.

Proclamation 19 - The High Council may uniquely pass decrees, proclamations, edicts or directives by simple majority vote consisting of no less than one-half and one of its members.

ARTICLE IV: THE ARCH CHANCELLOR

Proclamation 20 - The Council of Arcana, all its members ('Councilors'), and its officers ('High Councilors'), as well as the High Council, shall be presided and led by an Arch Chancellor, acting as the guildmaster and guild leader.

Proclamation 21 - The Arch Chancellor shall preside over all sessions of the High Council, as well as all formal guild-wide reunions or functions of the Council of Arcana.

Proclamation 22 - The Arch Chancellor may override any decision of the High Council by a veto, unless if the High Council acts to override such a veto with three-fourths of the vote.

Proclamation 23 - The Arch Chancellor may call for any amendments of the Prime Charter proposed in the High Council to be approved by guild-wide plebiscite, thus requiring three-fourths vote of all members.

Proclamation 24 - The Arch Chancellor will execute the promotion and demotion of High Councilors, as officers of the guild, by whatever fashion he or she so deems fit for the Council of Arcana.

Proclamation 25 - The Arch Chancellor may singularly call for the adjournment, recess, order, dispersion or dissolution of the Council of Arcana.

Proclamation 26 - The governance of the Council of Arcana is intended to be open-ended, deliberative, facilitating and democratic; to that end, the office of Arch Chancellor and the leadership of the guild should be of equal concern to all members of the High Council.

ARTICLE V: ADMINISTRATION

Proclamation 27 - The Council of Arcana is empowered and directed to expand its membership by any and all means conducive to its beliefs and principles, with provision that membership can only be granted in the aforementioned fashion.

Proclamation 28 - In the case of chapters or committees being designated by the High Council for functional purposes, an individual may be appointed by the High Council, not a member of the High Council, but with guild officer authorities, to act as a 'Senior Councilor', with the authority to appoint and dismiss new Councilors within the locality and jurisdiction so specified without the overseeing and immediate deliberative review of the High Council, but will be required to submit a periodic report.

Proclamation 29 - The post of 'Senior Councilor', either in General or of Review over a specific committee, may be revoked by full consensus of the High Council, or be broad authorization from the Arch Chancellor.

Proclamation 30 - In the formation of a new committee for the Council of Arcana, a committee mission statement must be prepared with specification of the local chapter headquarters and the full name of its Senior Councilor.

ARTICLE V: APPEALS AND REVIEWS

Proclamation 31 - All members of the guild ('Councilors') have the right to petition for appeal and/or review of any matter concerning interest to the Council of Arcana, and such appeals and reviews should be handled by a full session of the High Council.

Proclamation 32 - Whereas the High Council cannot refuse an appeal, the Arch Chancellor, acting on behalf of the High Council, may reject any such appeal, or override the High Council's decisions of appeal or review in a fashion consistent with authority on veto.

Proclamation 33 - In an instant where attempts to communicate an officiating act for appointing new members (promoting a 'Junior Councilor' to a 'Councilor'), but not for dismissing, the current Senior Councilor or High Councilor present may issue an act on singular authority for the proxy of such an appointment, only overriden if deemed necessary by the High Council in session or the Arch Chancellor.

Proclamation 34 - No member of the guild may be deprived of association without explicit review by the High Council, and any Senior Councilor or High Councilor revealed of such an act will be found in violation of the Prime Charter, and liable for contempt of the Council, punishable by ejection from the guild.

ARTICLE VI: AMENDMENTS

Proclamation 35 - The Arch Chancellor will be empowered to recognize any officer of the Council of Arcana as his immediate Vice Chancellor, or under any title designated, will serve under him in the leadership of the guild in all aspects and representation of the Arch Chancellor from within and abroad, and under conditions of verifyable permanent incapacity, the Vice Chancellor may assume the Arch Chancellory in the temporary interim, providing for the High Council to elect a new Arch Chancellor from among its own ranks. (7 May 2000, Amended 8 October 2000)

Proclamation 36 - The Arch Chancellor will be empowered to recognize a member of the High Council has the First Speaker of the High Council, who shall in turn serve for a single month in equal rotation with fellow High Councilors in the presidency of the High Council and the direction and supervisory management of all High Council meetings and agendas. (7 May 2000)

Proclamation 37 - The Arch Chancellor will be empowered to recognize any member of the Council as an Adjutant of the Chancellory, thereby removing said member from all accountability, responsibility and duty, yet empowering said member with the authority of a procedural, non-policy officer, personal advisor and counselor to the Arch Chancellor, and solely accountable to and representative of his person and no one else. (18 May 2000)

Proclamation 38 - The High Council shall be empowered in standard process to initiate a period of reorganization, which shall either temporarily or permanently repeal any and all outstanding legislation, procedures, articles, or rules, while retaining the simplified structure presented within the Prime Charter, for the purposes of, but not limited to, membership mobilization and coordination of efforts. (28 April 2001)

Hereby and Hence Declared and Proclaimed,
Indissoluble for all Perpetuity,
The Council of Arcana Founders
