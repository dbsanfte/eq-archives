Ranks of the Council

The Council of Arcana is, in the words of the Founders, a democratic union and open community that is exclusively and selectively founded. In the tradition of upholding the Council's hallmark of selectivity and defending the principles of family and community, each guild member has a specific role, office and purpose in fulfilling the dreams and high ambitions for the future. The Prime Charter governs most political titles and posts, but their duties come from the common laws of convention and tradition.

Definitions
A few definitions should be made clear about the types of power that are expressed in the Council of Arcana's political structure.

Edicts are declarations and assertions made by the Arch Chancellor, that instantly become law and cannot be challenged.

Proclamations are similar to edicts, but are expressed by several other officers of the Council and may be overridden by a two-thirds vote of the High Council.

Acts are pieces of legislation considered for implementation by the High Council, and when passed, they become binding documents that govern over the Council of Arcana's policies and procedures.

Veto can be evoked by to overrule an act by the High Council that passes with a simple majority, but can be overridden with a two-thirds affirmative vote. Acts that override vetoes become by definition 'proclamations'.

Recommendations are suggestions for explicit courses of action to be taken by the High Council that are issued by the committees or commissions at-large.

Office of the Arch Chancellory
The Office of the Arch Chancellory is the executive authority of the Council of Arcana. Those who serve within the Chancellory are firm defenders of decisive action.

Arch Chancellor
The Guild Leader and the formal head of the Council of Arcana, the Arch Chancellor's primary responsibility is to uphold and enforce the Prime Charter, defend the Principles of the Council and maintain policy-driven Codes of Honour and Conduct. The Arch Chancellor's authority is supported by the advice of the High Council, yet the Arch Chancellor has the privilege to reign and rule through the power of Edict and Proclamation, and may veto acts of the High Council.

Vice Chancellor
Proclamation 35 of the Prime Charter enables the Arch Chancellor to recognize a member of the High Council as his Vice Chancellor. The post of Vice Chancellor is purely ceremonial, but often advises the Arch Chancellor on affairs. When the Arch Chancellor is absent, the Vice Chancellor can speak on behalf of the Arch Chancellor. If incapacitated, the Vice Chancellor may assume the post of Arch Chancellor with the unanimous backing of the High Council until a new guild leader may be properly selected.

Adjutants of the Chancellory
While this position carries neither organic (i.e., Prime Charter) recognition nor actual power, the Arch Chancellor may recognize any member of the Council as his personal advisor and empower an Adjutant of the Chancellory to be an officer and assist the Office of the Arch Chancellory with administrative or bureaucratic affairs.

The High Council
The official policy-making and legislative body that leads and directs the Council of Arcana is the High Council presided by the Arch Chancellor. Its members are recognized as the leaders and elders of the guild, and jointly, the High Council can act to pass proclamations by supreme majority (two-thirds) vote, and acts by majority vote.

First Speaker
All members of the High Council take turns, per Proclamation 36 of the Prime Charter, serving as co-chair of the High Council, for one month each. The First Speaker has the authority to veto an act of the High Council, set the agenda for all High Council meetings and issue proclamations on behalf of the High Council. The First Speaker jointly presides with the Arch Chancellor over meetings of the High Council.

High Councilors
Each member of the High Council is titled 'High Councilor', and has the privilege to speak before the High Council, summon others before the High Council, and call special sessions of the High Council for any purpose. Their privilege is eternal and they are sanctioned as the highest officers of the guild with the authority to vote and call for vetoes on any issue.

The Guardian Council
A purely advisory organ, the Guardian Council consists of the highest level per class guild members, regardless of rank. Jointly, the Guardian Council discusses class issues and class-specific quests and programming events. It is overseen directly by the High Council.

Guardian Councilor
The standard title given to someone that serves on the Guardian Council is ironically enough, 'Guardian Councilor'. Members of the Guardian Council gain privileged access to the Guardian Council chambers and may singularly send messages to the High Council seeking a response in the form of recommendations.

The Senior Council
While not an organized unit of the guild, per se, the Senior Council consists of all other officers that have procedural and administrative duties (such as guilding and de-guilding members or hosting events), but no policy-making authority or responsibilities.

Senior Councilors General
Officers that have no specific policy-making function or responsibility, but that are examples of excellence and dedicated commitment to the Council's principles and virtues, are titled 'Senior Councilor General', and enjoy full official rank and privilege.

Senior Councilors of Review
Officers responsible for overseeing the functions and day-to-day operations of committees or commissions (see below) are titled 'Senior Councilor of Review'.

The Committee System
At the heart of the Council of Arcana's operations, where ideas are born and formulated, rests the Committee System - consisting of permanent standing committees and temporary, purpose-driven commissions. Each committee is led by a Senior Councilor of Review. Any member of the Council can serve on any and all committees, but should dedicate himself/herself to but a single one principally. All committees may submit weekly recommendations to the High Council requesting specific and immediate action.

Committee on Finance
Responsible for fiscal maintenance and proposing fundraisers, as well as suggesting allocations of the treasury, the Committee on Finance can ask the High Council to issue acts of a monetary nature.

Committee on Guild Events
The Committee on Guild Events oversees weekly guild-raids, community events and other guild gatherings. Participants should feel free to propose and organize their own events and receive public recognition for them.

Committee on Internal Affairs
This committee oversees faction standings, internal policies and procedures, and may suggest any such alterations to the High Council in the form of a recommendation. The Internal Affairs Committee may also ask the High Council for specific judicial action in conflict resolution against a member.

Committee on Membership
The Committee on Membership oversees the discussions and public votes on all membership applications, recommends mentoring program assignments and is actively involved in the growth and population of the guild. (Cf. Membership Reform Act of 3184.)

Committee on Newbie Events
Intended to be a sort of public relations committee (nb: the Council of Arcana does not typically engage in public alliances with other guilds or external entities and this is a privilege reserved exclusively to the High Council), the Newbie Events Committee plans events and activities for younger Councilors and non-aligned, lower-level adventurers of Norrath.

General Assembly
Members of the Council of Arcana that are not assigned to committees or are undergoing mentoring through trial membership, are grouped into what is commonly called the General Assembly. The Assembly's members have the privilege to participate in other guild events and committees and derive benefits of membership.

Councilor
All members of the Council of Arcana, whether assigned to committees or not, enjoy the title of 'Councilor'. Councilors have the explicit right to vote in committees, be mentors in the mentoring program, sponsor others for membership and ascend to higher positions in the guild.

Junior Councilor
Newly admitted members of the guild receive recognition as Junior Councilor, and enjoy all benefits of the guild membership except voting, being mentors, sponsoring others for membership or voting in committees. Junior Councilors are assigned a Councilor to be their mentor during trial membership. (Cf. Mentorship Program.)
