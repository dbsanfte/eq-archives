TITLE IV: THE SERVICE PERIOD

Art. 31: When a candidate has been inducted into the Council of Arcana with the rank of "Junior Councilor" or "Initiate", a mandatory "Service Period" commences, during which time the Junior Councilor has provisional membership and abridged rights under the Prime Charter, consisting of access to Council of Arcana events, membership and activities for the duration of the Service Period.

Art. 32: The Service Period shall consist of no more than thirty (30) days after induction, after which time if the Junior Councilor has not completed it satisfactorally, such a candidate be rejected from the guild, operating as if his or her application had been rejected by the Board of Admissions.

Art. 33: The Service Period shall be administered by at least one (1) officer of the Council of Arcana, whose duty it shall be to supervise the process and progress of Junior Councilors under the Service Period, as well as coordinate with the sponsors of said new members to provide Junior Councilors with the confidence and services prerequisite to completion of the Service Period.

Art. 34: In order to complete the Service Period, a Junior Councilor must individually plan and organize, although not necessarily lead, a social event AND an exploratory adventure with defined goals and objectives, or another activity as approved by the officer responsible for supervising the Service Period; these two events shall constitute "the service activity".

Art. 35: The service activity integral to the Service Period must have the attendance of at least ten (10) percent of the guild membership ranked "Councilor" or above, in that it benefits all participants equally, and be held so that it is accessible to a clear majority of the guild, being planned so that the activity is commensurate to the level of the Junior Councilor planning it.

Art. 36: The Service Period shall not be considered complete until, in addition to the planning of an activity, the Junior Councilor performs any and all tasks asked and/or required of him by either the supervising officer, the Board of Admissions, or the leadership of the Council of Arcana.

Art. 37: Junior Councilors in their Service Periods may not benefit from commerce, gifts or excessive aid from other members of the Council of Arcana, and acts of 'twinking' or guilding of alternate characters are to be strictly prohibited.

Art. 38: The Board of Admissions shall have the authority to unilaterally waive the Service Period requirement, if it is deemed that the spirit of the Service Period has been met by the Junior Councilor in question.

Art. 39: The High Council may set forth an amended set of conditions or activities for a Junior Councilor to complete in satisfactory completion of the Service Period, which may or may not override the previous pre-requisite activities contained herein.

Art. 40: Junior Councilors that satisfactorally complete the Service Period shall be promoted to the rank of "Councilor", during which time, said member shall enjoy the following privileges and rights previously denied: (1) full protections and rights under the Prime Charter, (2) the right to vote in all Council of Arcana deliberations accessible to the General Assembly, (3) the right to sponsor candidates for membership in the Council of Arcana, (4) the right to sit in deliberation on the Board of Admissions, (5) the right to run for an office if it is called for election, or to be appointed, (5) the right to advance through the Council of Arcana hierarchy, (6) the right to be recognized through merit, and (7) any and all other rights and privileges so granted to a full member of the Council of Arcana in that the service period has concluded and the provisional membership has become temporal.
