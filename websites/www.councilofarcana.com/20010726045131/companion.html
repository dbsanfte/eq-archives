A COMPANION TO THE COUNCIL OF ARCANA
    Last Updated: 1 May 2001

Welcome to the Council of Arcana! The Companion to the Council of Arcana is a concise, comprehensive, and complete guide to membership in the Council of Arcana - one of the oldest, most respected, selective and prestigious guilds on the Quellious Server. If you have any questions about anything that you have read here, please don't hesitate in visiting our active message boards and posting your questions or comments there.

BASICS

The Council of Arcana was founded on August 5, 1999, by a group of nine adventurers that are today mysteriously remembered as simply the "Founders". The Founders convened on the gates to Kerra Isle in Toxxulia Forest and proclaimed a guild to be founded upon the principles of Law, Order, Justice, Truth, Honor and Comradery. These principles, called the "Sacred Six Virtues", have been the guiding elements for all policy decisions of the Council. The resulting guild has been an association of players believing in the strength of chivalry and nobility in the power of community. Over the years, the Council of Arcana identity has evolved from a small collection of players into an elite league of family members dedicated to the same august crusade.

Today, the Council of Arcana is a leader on the Quellious Server. Councilors can be found in all of the most competitive dungeons and adventure hot-spots, spreading the same Sacred Six Virtues that were promulgated by the Founders years ago. As one of the largest guilds on the Quellious Server, the Council of Arcana also boasts one of the most comprehensive and easy-to-use web sites. Its guild web site, www.CouncilofArcana.com, is updated automatically with all of the latest information and has numerous interactive areas. Highly selective and extremely competitive, the Council of Arcana offers the advantages of a large and skilled guild of extremely capable and gifted players, with the comforts of a small and tightly-knit family.

GUILD STRUCTURE

The Founders set forth an ancient document, the Prime Charter, to serve as the organic law and constitution for the Council of Arcana. In the Prime Charter, readers will find a short but clear piece of constitutional rules that set forth the basic organization and fundamental process for administration in the Council of Arcana. However, because most members of the Council of Arcana are busily playing, politics has taken a back seat to the sensibilities of gaming - yet, the organization of the guild is imbedded into the psyche and role playing persistent in the guild.

The Guild is led by an Arch Chancellor, who serves as the chief executive officer of the Council of Arcana. Arch Chancellor Kaelon Xerxentis has been the only guild leader that the Council of Arcana has ever known. Wed to Allanna Xerxentis, who serves as Arch Chancelloress, the two take the definitive actions in the name of the guild and are mostly responsible for the administration of the guild itself. The High Council consists of High Councilors, the first tier of guild officers. These officers are responsible for jointly deciding policy. Senior Councilors, who do not sit on the High Council, have officer privileges, but serve as role models and community advocates and collaborate with High Councilors in dispensing with justice. The typical member is known simply as a Councilor, denoting the firm sense of equality and fraternity that all members in the guild enjoy. New members that have just joined serve as a Junior Councilor with abridged rights for an interim period.

Senior Councilors are elected by a vote of the membership - candidates are nominated by the High Council, and again confirmed by the High Council. The High Council can promote a Senior Councilor into the High Council when a seat opens up. For all intents and purposes, the High Council sets the political tone for the style and structure of the guild. Since April of 2001, the Council of Arcana has operated under a "Common Sense Leadership" agenda, which has prioritized decisive executive authority over structure and process. Councilors enjoy all privileges of membership, including voting, running for office, and participating in all guild activities and deliberations.

JOINING THE COUNCIL

Membership in the Council of Arcana is through invitation only. Given this, however, players can still actively pursue to join the Council of Arcana in the most rational ways. Interested players will adventure frequently with other council members, be of the proper level range for the mean level of the Council of Arcana's activities, and subscribe to our beliefs and ideals. Speak to an officer and make sure that they are aware of your interest. Councilors will discuss in private prospective members and, if approved, candidates will be invited as provisional members for thirty (30) days, as a Junior Councilor. Upon invitation, Junior Councilors should do the following: (1) add themselves to the Council Ledger, following the prompts and instructions for adding your primary character, (2) post on the common public forum on our message boards and request access to the private, member-only forums; and (3) begin meeting with other members and adventuring with them actively.

Junior Councilors have abridged rights - they may not vote, run for office, or deliberate in assembly matters. However, Junior Councilors have access to all of the activities and adventures that all other members enjoy, and thus, the distinction is often informal. Nonetheless, at the end of the thirty-day "Interim Period", Junior Councilors will be brought up before a vote of the officers. Junior Councilors that have been active, dedicated, and exemplary in their membership will be promoted to the rank of Councilor and given full benefits and permanent membership. Those Junior Councilors that have been inactive, failed to contribute meaningfully or positively to guild activities, or committed some grave transgression, will be expelled from the guild at this point.

GUILD CHAT

Members have access to the guild's communication infrastructure, including the private forums over the message boards, and guildchat - that green guild text. To use guildchat, members just type /gu followed by the text they wish to transmit to all other guild members. There are rules for guildchat, however, and all members are expected to abide by them. Profanity, sexual and/or racial slurs, and flooding the guildchat are unacceptable actions, keeping in mind that we have members of all ages and descents in our guild. Guild chat is also an inappropriate forum for airing grievances or complaining - and those types of discussions should be taken to the private forums. Members are not to ask repeatedly or excessively for services - such as teleportation, resurrection, or market prices on items. All members should remain considerate of the fact that guildchat is a public medium, and all guild members see them regardless of the activity in which they are engaged.

ETIQUETTE

All guild members should strive to be helpful, friendly and courteous. Their public actions reflect upon the guild as a whole. Complaints lodged against a member of the Council of Arcana are taken very seriously, and fully investigated, in order to preserve the integrity of the community.

OBEDIENCE

Councilors are expected to be respectful to one another and recognize that the bond of family unites everyone. Guild officers are to be respected and obeyed; orders from officers and guildleaders should be followed. While dissent is cherished and welcome on the message forums, disobedience is not an option. The Council of Arcana is a democratic society but it is hierarchically structured and unified under its principles. Spirited debate is encouraged, but personal insults and ad hominem attacks are strictly prohibited.

POWER LEVELING

We believe that members are more capable and skilled players if they level naturally and without unfair advantages. Power Leveling a "Primary Character" is not recommended. If you are not of a group level to receive an item, then it is not intended for your use. If you do "power-level", then we ask you to use discretion and keep it out of guildchat. Members should never be expected to assist in power-leveling or twinking.

KILL STEALING, ETC.

The Council of Arcana is firmly against kill-stealing, which is defined as the killing of a creature after it has been engaged by a party or individual. We recognize spawns as legitimate camps, and those who arrive on a "first come, first serve" basis have a legitimate right to the produced spawns. Ninja-looting, defined as the looting of an item from a corpse that you did not explicitly kill without the original victor's permission, is completely unacceptable. Individuals should earn the items that they obtain, wear and use. Finally, intentional training - or, causing a train for the purpose of hindering the efforts and/or endangering other players - is strictly prohibited. Cases of verifiable kill-stealing, ninja-looting, or intentional training may result in a summary expulsion of the transgressing member from the guild.

LOOT RULES

Greed and selfishness have no place in the Council of Arcana. On formal raids or expeditions, the Council uses loot rules as a way to retain a certain degree of fairness in the distribution of treasure and items. Members who have items that are better than or equal in value to an item dropped may not roll for that item, unless if it is required for a special quest or purpose. Only those who can wear/use an item should roll on an item. Members should rotate the treasure, so that everyone of eligible rolling class has an item before a member rolls for a second item. On unofficial raids, or every-day grouping experiences with fellow members or nonaligned players, members should exercise common sense, selflessness, generosity and love.

COMMERCE

We encourage members to share their trade skills with one another, and when selling items to fellow guild members, sell them at or below the average market value. If an item was given to you by another guildmate and you no longer need it, we ask that you pass it along to another guildmate who could benefit from its use. Guild common items that are held in the guild vault, such as resist gear, may be used by anyone in the guild if returned promptly and courteously after its use.

Common sense and practical etiquette are to be used when engaging in trade or commerce in the public market. Members are not to engage in commerce with the intention to manipulate the market value, deceive traders, or maliciously or selfishly engage in any act that unfairly deprives someone else of property or money.

FRIENDS AND FOES

The Council of Arcana is an active member of the Quellious Server Community, and is a recognized leader with an unblemished reputation for excellence. In our adventures, we have made alliances and formed partnerships with other individuals and groups. Likewise, we have also encountered those who oppose our way of life and have been a danger to themselves and those around them. We have presented a factions section on our web site, listing persons on a scale using the standard EverQuest factions. Members are expected to treat friends with the same respect and courtesy that you would a fellow member, while remaining sensitive and distanced from foes who oppose or have endangered the guild community as a whole. The Council of Arcana's official policy on guildwars is that they are to be only waged in a friendly capacity with friends as a means to practice large-group dynamics and tactical combat.

CREDENTIALS

Member Credentials - or the status of a member in the guild - are regularly being updated. All members are expected to keep their primary character attributes updated in the Council Ledger, while also participating reasonably over our message boards. Members are expected to contribute to official guild raids and events if they do not have prior commitments. Members are encouraged to help one another out and render their assistance and services to fellow guild members in need, keeping in mind that there shall be a time when all are in need and require the aid of their comrades.

Members who repeatedly or intentionally fail to contribute to the guild beyond reasonable circumstances, fail to update their profiles on the Council Ledger, do not remain up-to-date with Council Events and Activities, or become absent, lose their good standing and are considered "inactive". Inactive members may be removed from the guild when officers undertake a periodic review of all members. Members seeking to be absent for an extended period of time need only inform their fellow guildmembers of upcoming absences, so that we not consider members to be inactive.

Members who commit a violation of guild rules or policies, or have committed a transgression against a fellow guild member, shall be considered "in opposition" to the Council of Arcana. Members "in opposition" will be approached by officers and disciplined and rehabilitated back into the community to become a member in good standing once more. Repeated flagrant, intentional, or unresolved abuses of guild rules or policies will result in expulsion from the guild. Singular acts of a malicious nature against the guild community or a fellow guild member are punishable by prompt expulsion from the Council of Arcana. Expelled members have the option to appeal their expulsion to the High Council, but unless otherwise informed, decisions of guildremovals are final. Members who are expelled - for failing the Interim Period, becoming inactive, or entering into opposition - may be re-invited to the guild at a later date.

COMMUNITY

The Council of Arcana is not a typical guild - it has often been a case study in contradictions and exceptions to the rule. The Council is a large guild, but emphasizes intimacy and closeness amongst its members. The Council engages in competitive expeditions that result in tremendous victory and earning of treasure, but is a selfless and generous guild by nature. In the end, the Council of Arcana has believed strongly that the value of the community is enriched by the unique talents, skills and personality traits that each individual contributes to it. The pursuit for community has been the foundation for creating a civil society founded upon respect and operating using common sense. It remains this enduring spirit that has defined generations of council members, creating a legacy that shall forever be remembered. Welcome to the tapestry of adventure and to the ultimate experience in community building - Welcome to the Council of Arcana.
