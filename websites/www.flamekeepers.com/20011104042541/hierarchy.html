FlameKeepers of Norrath
Hierarchy

First and foremost, the Council of Elders believes in open channels of communication. For that reason, there is no "chain of command". Any member may approach an Advisor or Elder with problems, ideas, requests, or just to say "hello". If ye feel ye have not been heard, do not hesitate to contact another Elder or Advisor.

Our family is a team and we will strive to work together in all avenues. As members move through the posts, the Council of Elders expects increased interaction within all areas - from guild functions (planning, hosting, volunteering) to recruitment and everything in between.

FlameKeepers of Norrath is based upon an unusual system where posts are attained by quest/deeds. There are only 6 posts, outside of the Council of Elders. Below is the tree.

Council of Elders

a. HighKeeper-Guild Leader - Maintain site, communication avenues (boards, emails, news)

b. Sage - Helps members with information relative to game/guild.

c. ArchPriest/ess - Ambassador and Master/Mistress of Ceremonies

d. QuestMaster - Oversees all members and seekers on quest.

Members can attain Elder status but be forewarned - the road is long and arduous though worth the trip. Active CoE members reserve the right to decide when an Advisor will be invited to join the Elders Council. An Advisor will only be invited if the Council of Elders has unanimously agreed to do so.

Advisor & Advisor with Quest Title (Officer Status)

Advisor(Officer Status) is attained in slightly different manner than other posts. Only those members who have reached Protector or Protector Titled will be considered for officer positions, as needed. This is the only post where a quest will be designed by the CoE and the chosen member must complete it - as given - to attain officer status. All other posts, the member must create his/her own quest and obtain CoE approval to undertake it.

Officers are members who have dedicated much time and energy (and must be willing to continue to do so!) into the guild and have shown excellent leadership traits throughout their time. Officers must be able and willing to assist members in all areas needed with efficiency, objectivity, and good spirits.

**Scribe** FKoN Newsletter Editor - This post is NOT obtainable by quest. If a Keeper is interested in this, they must submit a letter to the CoE requesting consideration. Any Keeper (except Seekers or Apprentices) may apply for this. It is not a "post" like the others but will attach to your designation (ex: Giggen, Initiate-Scribe). For further information, please contact the CoE.

Protector & Protector with Quest Title - Advisors' aides(assist as needed in all areas with Advisors - events, raids, meetings, recruits), Honor Guard of FKoN (meeting guards).

Knight & Knight with Quest Title - QuestMaster Assistants - aid in over-seeing questing members and seekers.

Cavalier & Cavalier with Quest Title - Assist Initiates as needed, familiarize them with guild on continual basis.

Initiate - Continue in the activities as described in Apprentice post.

Apprentice (30 Day Probationary Tag) - Apprentices should use the 30 days to get a feel for the guild - meet folks, post on boards, visit website for news and events.

Seekers - A Seeker's main responsibility is to undertake a role-play quest for admittance.

Questing

In order to "step-up" a post within FKoN, all members must do the following:

1. Submit a written, in-character request to all members of the Council stating the post you are questing for. Remember - you may only move one step at a time. You may, however, submit two part quests to attain new posts.

2. If you are questing for your titled post, you MUST choose a special "title" which only you will hold within that post. Example: Cavalier and then name your special title for that post. Example: Cavalier Protector. CAVALIER PROTECTOR AND CAVALIER WILL BE THE SAME RANK - one is not 'higher' than the other.

3. You MUST decide upon a quest or swear an oath to perform some deed that you feel adequately represents the post/title which you have chosen to attain. The Elders will decide whether that oath of deed/quest is adequate (based on the post you are applying for) and then will inform you of their approval within 3-5 days of your submission. We will also offer suggestions if we do not feel that your proposal is adequate. You may accept, reject, or counteroffer these suggestions without fear of consequence until such a time when both parties (Member and Council) are agreed.

4. You will then perform what you have sworn to do, in order to obtain the post and title that you have chosen, with due proof of your work. Most likely, this will be the QuestMaster or QM Aides, who may "visit" you while you are doing so (and, in addition, you may choose to have witnesses provide testimonials.).

5. The CoE will then convene and decide, unanimously, whether you have earned the "step-up" according to the actual performance of the oath of deed/quest that you have sworn to perform.

You may only step up one post at a time, but you may do so in a way that you see fit, as long as it is approved and proven. The Council of Elders also holds the right to deny your attainment of a post (if you have not performed what you have sworn to do.).

Awards

We, the Council of Elders, will also bestow special awards upon Keepers who have earned them, awards that are not given within the hierarchy, but are based upon the following:

Role-play (In Game) - Shield of Flames (icon coming soon)

Recruitment - Silver Star (icon coming soon)

Participation - Gold Leaf (icon coming soon)

Role-play(Other) - Sword of Excellence

Merit/Spirit - Eternal Burning Torch

Other awards may be created as the need arises. All awards are for guild members only unless special circumstances warrant a bestowal to a non-guild member. All members can submit nominations for awards.
