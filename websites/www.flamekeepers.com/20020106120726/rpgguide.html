What is Role-playing?

Ta play "in character" consistently requires both intelligence an' imagination, not ta mention humor an' patience. Think o' role-playin' (RPGing) as actin' fer that is what it comes down ta. An' if'n ye think o' it in this light, how can ye be offended when a dark elf cleric decides he willnae help ye just because ye are a High Elf. One may argue that the player has more control over his character than does an actor in a movie. Truly? Think o' the relative roles in Norrath o' the Tier'dal an' the Fier'dal. Consider their histories, as laid out by Verant. In the world o' Everquest, Verant is the director an' we are the actors on the Internet stage.

How do ye role-play? In general consider yer character type. Male or female? Gnome, Elf, or other? What class have ye chosen ta grow in? Dig into the history o' yer race an' class. Draw from historical mythos an' other fantasy classics. Read J. R.R. Tolkien's "Lord of the Rings" for a great look at racial interaction - specifically between Legolas the elf an' Gimli the dwarf.

Outside tha strictures o' tha game, it's perfectly possible ta give yer character a personality quirk or two that make him or her into a person rather than a "cardboard" cutout. Try bein' an astrophobe who turns an' runs fer tha nearest inn whenever it rains. Or develop a hatred fer tha color orange and refuse ta have anything ta do with anyone who wears bronze. The choices an' options are limitless.

Developin' a character can take time. If ye are new ta tha idea, model yer character on yerself. Can't stand spiders? Make yer character an arachnophobe. As ye play yer character, ye'll begin ta develop a personality. When ye gain experience in RPGin', ye can decide what ye want yer character ta be like while yer settin' him or her up. "I'm going to be a half-elf bard, based in Qeynos, who is determined to learn every language in the game." Again, yer choices are limitless.

RPGin' in Everquest, at its simplest, is avoidin' references ta real life except when absolutely needed. Limit them ta /ooc (out of character) even then. The so-called "doodspeak" is anathema ta all RPG'ers an' should be avoided at all times. Sprinkling yer speech with "thee's" an' "thou's" is a nice touch if done properly an' not carried ta extremes. Ditto fer Old English verb forms (dost, shalt). References ta "zoning" or "trains" should be restricted ta /shout an' /tell. Use the /auction channel fer buying an' selling only.

Now obviously, one needs ta hit a balance betwixt RPGin' an' keepin' the game playable. It's a lot easier ta type "TRAIN TO ZONE" than a "A vasty number of most foul gnolls are chasing me out of Blackburrow!" Especially when runnin' fer one's life. I would also suggest turning on the /role-play flag.

On tha subject o' speech, swearin' is a two-edge sword. It can be argued that it is "in character" fer a warrior ta spew four-letter words every other sentence. But swearin' just ta swear is offensive ta many an' doesnae add anything ta tha game. Try emotin' something like "Selkyee calls Taylah every filthy name in the book." Or "Benecleod spits out a stream of invective." I personally think that continual foul language is a sign o' immaturity, and I tend ta avoid such players.

Tha races in EQ are apparently divided into two groups, good an' evil. High Elves, Dwarves, Gnomes, Halflings, Wood Elves, Erudites, Barbarian, and Half-Elves are good. Trolls, Ogres, and Dark Elves are evil. There is some inherent bias towards good in this game, it is much easier fer good characters ta get around.

RPGin' takes a liberal amount o' imagination. Anyone can go out an' hack things or toss spells. How do ye make yerself stand out? Again this is where the developin' personality comes into play...Just won a big duel?? Emote cutting off yer opponent's ear an' stringin' it on a cord. Brag about 'another for the necklace'.

Spend time learnin' trade skills an' hawkin' yer goods rather than simply levelin'. Learn languages an' use them. Offer ta teach them fer cash or trade. Run mini-quests fer newbie characters. Treat others o' yer race with respect an' dignity (an' sometimes discount on goods an' services), an' be a little aloof with others till ye get ta know them. Now this does not apply if yer grouped with them. In that case, pitch in headfirst an' do yer best fer tha group!

Clerics, Shamans, ShadowKnights and Paladins have a spiritual side ta flesh out. Chargin' into battle shoutin'"Mithaniel deliver us!" is kinda cool. Help out a newbie who's in trouble an' give them a lecture on yer glorious deity. Think about tha nature o' yer god, Veeshan is a dragon so when attacking say something like "Feel Veeshan's claws, foul beast!" If ye are in a group an' someone dies, emote chantin' a prayer fer tha dead over them.

In short, RPGin' is doing lots o' little things that may not get ye levels, but will add an entire new dimension ta yer game. Godspeed an' May Brell watch over ye, traveler!

Adapted by Taylah Triskelion, Guildleader o' FKoN.
