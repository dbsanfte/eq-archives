Jewel Crafting 101

Go to your class guild master and get your jewel craft skill up to 21pts (you will be level 4ish, but don't forget to put one point into meditation at L4. It really sucks going all the way through L4 without meditation (believe me, I know). At this time, GET A TON OF MONEY, about 500 to 750pp. Jewel craft has no ceiling per level, so you "could" be a L4 grandmaster in jewel craft with 200 pts. Having 500 to 750pp you should be able to get your skill up to 120 ish, only if your Int (raises skill quickly) is high enough and your Wis (reduces failure) is comparable. You will notice less monetary loss and maybe even a small profit if your Charisma is high enough.

A couple other helpful profit ideas...A: sell to the vender of your race, and B: Get your charisma to 100. There is a Silk Tunic that can be picked up from a clothing vender in Qeynos for a few plat. This nice little evening shirt adds 5 Cha, get this and equip it whenever buying and selling.

Jewel Crafting 102

When making jewelry, you will need a few items. A Jewel kit, the jewel crafters book, a gem and a bar of metal. You do not need to enchant the bars of metal to acquire skill, you will not grow faster and it will sell for the same amount to the NPC venders, so save your time. When making an item, be sure these actions have been done...Jewel bag in 'inventory slot', Book in 'inventory slot', Gems in 'inventory slot', Bars of metal in 'inventory slot' ***NOT IN BAGS***. Place a single gem and a single bar of metal into the jewel bag then click the combine button. If your skill is high enough, you will be rewarded with an item of some kind. Know however, that even though your skill may be high enough to craft the item in question, there is always a chance of failure - destroying both the gem and the metal.

You cannot choose what you want to make, the game will automatically generate the item. Rings, Necklaces, Bracelets, Earrings and Masks are all available for the jewel crafter's skill. The item generated will vary depending on the metal you use. The only 'real' pattern that I have seen, is whatever item is made with silver, will also be made with plat, and the same pattern occurs with electrum and gold. Listed below is the items created with the combination of gems and metal bars. You will notice four slots in your jewel bag but you will only ever use two. I heard a rumor that it was a small bag made into a combine item and Verant was too lazy to take out the other two slots. This is your Jewel craft table in its purest form. The info herein, resides the triviality, the end product of the gem and metal combination, and a rough estimate of what to charge your clients. As you create items you become better at your craft. You will notice after you make an item so many times out of a particular gem and metal, it will become remarkably easy for you to combine them, almost trivial. For example, when you pump all your points into jewel crafting and get up to 21, you will gain skill by making Cat's Eye Agate necklaces (cats eye and silver) but will gain no skill from Malachite Rings (malachite and silver). Jewel Crafting 301

To create magic items, you will require an enchanted bar of metal. It has come to my attention; there is a mob, Queen Klicnik, who spawns quite regularly near the entrance to Qeynos. Unfortunately, she spawns very close to the entrance, and if you're not quick, the guards will get her first. Mainly because she makes a b-line for them. Why am I telling you about the Queen of all fire beetles? She loots a "Giant Fire Beetle Eye" (sometime two) which, for those jewel crafters who can't cast enchant metal spells, this little baby will offer a small, temporary solution for you. Place it into your melee, and use it. You will glow, and lights will swirl. When you check your inventory, it will be plus one enchanted bar of silver. IT DOES NOT ENCHANT A BAR OF SILVER. It summons a bar of enchanted silver. Please let me know, if you find anything that will summon enchanted electrum, gold or even...*drum roll* enchanted platinum.

For those of you jewel crafters who are enchanters, you might have a question along the lines of.... WHERE IN ALL OF NORRATH ARE MY ENCHANT METAL SPELLS? Well, rest assured they are out there, and unless you're a dark elf or reading this info, you will have a hard time finding them. They are sold in the Neriak library. There are a few ways you can get your spells without being slain by the evil residence within the dark city.

A: Make a dark elf (mule character) and twink him a few pp. Then use him to buy the enchant (silver, electrum, gold and platinum) spells. Finally twink the scrolls back to your enchanter.

B: Go through the town while invisible, get to the library then find the vendor and buy the spells. After your done buying you will have reappeared, so don't forget to re-invis after the transaction has concluded, when done, get the hell outta there.

C: As an enchanter, at L12, you can illusion yourself into a Dark Elf, but if you worship any other god besides the dark prince, you will be slain anyhow (You might avoid slaugher if you are AGNOSTIC). So I recommend invis and the dark elf illusion.

D: Pay someone to do it for you. I don't recommend this particular path, but if you must, you must.

Jewel Crafting 302

Money...the real reason we got into this whole outfit. How to sell, where to sell and who to sell to.

It is pointless to say you will rarely find anyone who wants an unenchanted item, so only make unenchanted items when you are practicing your skill up to a skill of 150. Then, enchant all the gold you practice with until your 200. When you make an item out of enchanted metal, and it's on the border of your skill, I don't recommend taking the risk of destroying your materials. Instead make "trivial" enchanted items. Sell your Enchanted items at retail. When you get your reputation up and gain a few regular customers, lower the cost a little, by about 10%. Your crafted items should fly off your shelves...allowing money to come flying into those empty shelves, making you a seriously stinking rich EQ junkie.... err, lots o' plat. Auction items once every three minutes or so. When auctioneers broadcast more than that, they become rather irritating and listeners may go as far as to toggle their auction filter. If you have not sold anything in a 20-minute time period, consider moving over two zones and continue there.

***REMEMBER***DO NOT MAKE THESE ITEMS UNLESS YOUR TRIVIAL IN THEM***REMEMBER***. I hear there's a good amount of gems which can be looted off certain mobs.

- Dervishes in Oasis (or anywhere else they spawn) *Jade*
- Kerrans on Kerra Isle in Toxxulia Forrest *Cats Eye Agate*
- And a few gems can be found off a few mobs in LavaStorm (Bloodstones) and various other zones.
- A big one is, the L40 Paladin in Ocean of Tears, when killed, will almost always loot a *Star Ruby* which sells for at least 75pp by itself, not even as a jeweled item.
