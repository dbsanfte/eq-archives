Stella Awards!
January 14, 2002
Submitted by Freka Hailstorm

In 1994, a New Mexico jury awarded $2.9 million U.S. in damages to 81-year-old Stella Liebeck who suffered third-degree burns to her legs, groin and buttocks after spilling a cup of McDonald's coffee on herself.

This case inspired an annual award - The "Stella" Award -for the most frivolous lawsuit in the U.S. The ones listed below are clear candidates.

1. January 2000: Kathleen Robertson of Austin Texas was awarded $780,000 by a jury of her peers after breaking her ankle tripping over a toddler who was running amuck inside a furniture store. The owners of the store were understandably surprised at the verdict, considering the misbehaving little rodent was Ms. Robertson's son.

2. June 1998: 19 year old Carl Truman of Los Angeles won $74,000 and medical expenses when his neighbor ran over his hand with a Honda Accord.  Mr. Truman apparently didn't notice there was someone at the wheel of the car, when he was trying to steal his neighbor's hubcaps.

3. October 1998: Terrence Dickson of Bristol, Pennsylvania was leaving a house he had just finished robbing by way of the garage. He was not able to get the garage door to go up, because the automatic door opener was malfunctioning. He couldn't re-enter the house because the door connecting the house and garage locked when he pulled it shut. The family was on vacation. Mr. Dickson found himself locked in the garage for eight days.  He subsisted on a case of Pepsi he found, and a large bag of dry dog food.  Mr. Dickson sued the homeowner's insurance claiming the situation caused him undue mental anguish. The jury agreed to the tune of half a million dollars.

4. October 1999: Jerry Williams of Little Rock, Arkansas was awarded $14,500 and medical expenses after being bitten on the buttocks by his next door neighbor's beagle. The beagle was on a chain in its owner's fenced-in yard, as was Mr. Williams. The award was less than sought because the jury felt the dog may have been provoked by Mr. Williams who, at the time, was shooting it repeatedly with a pellet gun.

5. May 2000: A Philadelphia restaurant was ordered to pay Amber Carson of Lancaster, Pennsylvania $113,500 after she slipped on soft drink and broke her coccyx. The beverage was on the floor because Ms. Carson threw it at her boyfriend 30 seconds earlier during an argument.

6. December 1997: Kara Walton of Claymont, Delaware successfully sued the owner of a night club in a neighboring city when she fell from the bathroom window to the floor and knocked out her two front teeth. This occurred while Ms. Walton was trying to sneak through the window in the ladies room to avoid paying the $3.50 cover charge. She was awarded $12,000 and dental expenses.

7. And just so you know that cooler heads do occasionally prevail:  Kenmore Inc., the makers of Dorothy Johnson's microwave, were found not liable for the death of Mrs. Johnson's poodle after she gave it a bath and attempted to dry it by putting the poor creature in her microwave for, "just a few minutes, on low," The case was quickly dismissed.

DNA match may be clue to Nimh killers
Judge Pangea Panthea will rule on test validity
December 17, 2001
Submitted by Freka Hailstorm

DNA evidence is not just for murder and rape prosecutions anymore.

Midgard Game wardens have been using animal DNA samples since 1099 to link poachers with the wood rats, frosty colts and other animals they kill. They say it’s an affordable and reliable way to charge and convict illegal hunters.

Now, wildlife officers locally are using DNA to link three Gna Faste trolls accused of killing a white tail wood rat out of season. They go to trial in January.

It could be the first such case in Southeast Midgard. Prosecutors don’t recall any other cases involving animal DNA in Southeast Midgard’s 20th Judicial Circuit that includes Gna Faste, Galplan, Askheim, Nalliten and the Greater Myrkwood area.

“This is my first trial dealing with DNA. I never thought I’d be doing this for some stupid rat,” said Assistant Troll Attorney Gugur Kleenteeth. He declined further comment about the details of the ongoing case.

There were no eye witnesses linking the Gna Faste trolls to the dead rat found on private property near the werewolf fort by Midgard wildlife officers this past Monday.

Wildlife officers were on patrol in Skona Ravine when they saw the trolls at the circle of stones at 1:50 a.m.

There appeared to be fresh blood on their cloaks and weapons. There were also clumps of what officers thought was wood rat hair on the troll’s armor.

Officers questioned the trolls about the blood and hair and the trolls said they had been riding in nearby woods on private property and had their pets with them.

They said they didn’t see any wood rat named Nimh and didn’t know how the blood and hair got on their armor.

Officers searched the woods, but saw nothing.

Later that afternoon, the property owner reported finding a freshly killed Nimh near the northwest section of Skona Ravine. Wildlife officers took samples of the blood from Nimh and from the troll’s armor and clothes.

The samples taken from the trolls matched samples of the wood rat’s blood, according to DNA tests conducted at a University of Midgard lab. Hair samples taken from the armor also match the wood rat hair, the tests show.

Officers charged Fenris Kneesmasher II, 31 and Yoyo Bigdaddy, 25, both of Gna Faste, and Kill U-dead, 20, of Askheim, with killing Nimh out of season and two counts of trespassing.

They face a maximum of a year in jail and 3,000gp in fines if convicted on misdemeanor charges.

The Midgard Game and Fresh Water Fish Commission has used DNA evidence in illegal hunting cases in other parts of the realm for two years, said Sentry Karl Gat.

Gat, who is in charge of the agency’s southeast operations. “It’s just like a detective using DNA evidence in a murder case,” he said.

Before DNA evidence, labs could only verify that blood samples were from a particular species, such as wood rat. The tests could not identify a specific animal, Gat said.

The University of Midgard lab developed the DNA testing technique for animals three to four years ago, Gat said.

The University of Midgard’s genetics lab compiled DNA data from samples of white tail wood rat and other rat like animals received from the wildlife commission, students and private hunting preserves, said Sydney, lab director.

DNA is the major component of chromosomes - the structures that transmit heredity characteristics.

“Who and what you are is determined by your DNA,” Sydney said.

By collecting the different DNA samples, Sydney said she could tell if the blood came from a white tail wood rat or a rugged dwarven pony.

With that information, Sydney said she can narrow the test farther to determine if a blood sample matches the blood of an individual wood rat.

These tests became important when the sale of meat from rugged dwarven pony and other previously protected hoofed animals became illegal a few years ago, Sydney said. The sale of rugged dwarven pony meat remained illegal and the tests were able to show what kind of animal the meat came from, she said.

The wildlife commission first used DNA evidence to win a conviction in an illegal wood rat hunting case in Fort Atla last spring.

In that case, a Gotar kobold pleaded guilty to possessing freshly killed wolf nipper meat out of season and possession of untagged Jybralla meat.

DNA tests by the University of Midgard lab showed the meat was from a freshly killed wolf nipper that lived in the area. The man was ordered to pay 1,661gp in fines and court costs.

“A lot of DNA has gone over the dam since then,” said Capt. Flymm Flamm of the wildlife commission who headed that first investigation.

In another famous case, several restaurant owners on Midgard’s East Coast were convicted in this winter for illegally selling Njessi parts - she is a protected species - as raw “fish” for sushi, Flamm said.

Wildlife officers got Njessi parts from sports fishertrolls who caught the sea monster legally. They took samples for DNA testing.

The undercover officers then sold the “fish” to the restaurants. Other undercover officers posed as customers and bought the “fish”, which they were told was sushi made from other types of “fish”.

Lab DNA tests later verified that the Njessi parts sold to the restaurants was the same the restaurants turned around and sold as sushi, Flamm said.

“We take prosecuting such cases seriously,” Gat said.

The cases involve people stealing game from legitimate sportstrolls who pay the license fees and hunt game and fish legally in season, he said.

DNA testing is not only effective, but it is relatively inexpensive, Gat said.

The university lab charges 35sp a sample to the wildlife commission because the lab and the commission are Midgard agencies, said Sydney. She said the lab handles about 20 cases a year.

The cost of testing the four samples in the Gan Faste case was 140sp.

The charge to private defense attorneys is 70gp a sample, Sydney said.

The national Fish and Wildlife Forensics Laboratory in Jordheim has kept DNA data for white tail wood rat and other animals, such as black mauler, for the past 10 years, said Valekyri Stormrider, acting lab director.

The lab pioneered the research.

It conducts hundreds of DNA tests a year in federal game violations cases and for all Midgard cities and outposts, Stormrider said. The accuracy of the tests has been well established in all courts, she said.

In a famous case involving the killing of a badger in East Svealand in the early summer, federal wildlife officers used the lab’s DNA tests to track down and charge the hunter, Stormrider said.

A book, “Odin’s Fury - The Story of Wildlife Detectives,” was written about the case.

Defense attorneys are challenging the DNA evidence in the Gna Faste case, saying they doubt the tests are reliable.

The evidence should be thrown out because DNA tests on Nimh have not been generally accepted in the scientific community, said attorney Jira Hammerfist, who represents U-dead and Bigdaddy.

“I don’t doubt that the science has come far enough, but I’m questioning whether they have the data established so that these tests are accurate,” Hammerfist said.

Gna Faste area Judge Pangea Panthea will decide on the day of trial whether to throw out the evidence.

Even if the DNA evidence is allowed in the Gna Faste case, that doesn’t mean the trolls are guilty, said attorney Shiver Blackfang, who represents Kneesmasher.

An important point is that an arrow was found in the wood rat’s skull, Blackfang said. “The defendants didn’t have any bows. They didn’t have any arrows,” she said.

A wood rat can travel for miles after being shot, Blackfang said. A hunter could have shot Nimh and the animal got away only to die later.

The wood rat did have several spider bites, which is consistent with the trolls’ story, Blackfang said.

The trolls said they were moss maiden hunting when their pets found the wood rat’s carcass and started chewing on it.

The spiders got wood rat blood on them and the trolls got the blood on them when they retrieved the spiders, Blackfang said. “I don’t see how they can prove the wood rat was alive when our clients found it,” she said.

Assistant Troll Attorney Gugur Kleenteeth was quick to point out however that trolls don’t have pets. He said he speaks with experience as he, a troll, always wanted to be a hunter but was not allowed so he became an attorney instead. “I’ll kill you in the courtroom now!”

October 30, 2001 
Submitted by Torfi Egilson

Hearken unto me, my friends, for this is truly a momentous day for all of us.  It is with great pleasure that I announce that on the evening of October 29, the Sisters Hailstorm did, in the great hall of Jordheim, secure for our use the Great Emblem of Renown!  The lovely Freka can be seen modeling a cloak emblazoned with the colors, ivory and burgundy, and symbol of Renown, a rope knotted into an infinity pattern.  Tied together forever: once Renown, always Renown.

Emblazoning may be added to shields and cloaks by Karis the Guild Emblemeer in Jordheim's great hall for all members of the guild who have attained the 20th level or who have exceeded 400 skill in any craft skill.  Karis charges 5gp per item for her services.

And in a totally unrelated but no less historic event, Horel Hammerstorm, Thane of Thor, did on the 29th day of October earn Renown's first Realm Points.  Horel participated in a daring raid into Hibernian territory and while there caved in the head of a fierce but unfortunate Lurikeen with his hammer.  It is with great sadness, however, that I must report that Horel himself fell in the engagement.  

Defending the Relic
October 24, 2001 
Submitted by Torfi Egilson

Reports are trickling in - reports, I feel obligated to add, almost exclusively made by Kareena Skycastle - that one of Midgard's mighty and holy relics was saved from a recent invasion by none other than Renown's own Kareena Skycastle!  

According to eyewitnesses, Madam Skycastle was ogling Thor's Hammer at the very moment that a ferocious horde of termites set their sights on turning the handle of that proud symbol of Midgard's dominance into a high-rise apartment.  Kareena was, at first, totally unaware of the threat that lurked mere feet from the hammer, busy as she was trying to figure out a way to get it under her cloak without anyone noticing.  But then she felt an itching sensation on her leg, looked down, and noticed that the termite horde was passing around and, as it were, over her on their way to Thor's Hammer.  


Kareena ogles Thor's hammer

It is important to note that at this point Kareena's own recounting of the story begins to differ from the reports of the eyewitnesses to the action.  According to Kareena, she quite calmly and deliberately called out to the guards, "I say, there seems to be a swarm of termites heading for that mightiest of all mighty relics, the great Hammer of Thor.  Why don't you chaps be good little boys and help me deal with the situation?"  After which she gently lowered herself to the ground and proceeded to "steamroll" (ed. - her word, not BSN's) the termites until they were all quite thoroughly smashed into a puddle.

Eyewitnesses relate a slightly different version of events.  They say that when Kareena noticed the soft-bodied, wingless, blind and creamy white termites crawling around her feet and even up her legs, she shrieked in terror and hollered to the guards to "get off your lazy asses and help me!"  She then began to slap frantically at her legs in a futile attempt to kill the termites that were climbing on her.  Undeterred, the termites continued their ascent until some had even gotten so far as to climb above the collar of her tunic and into her hair, at which point the gallant Madam Skycastle passed out from fright and collapsed into a heap on top of the termite colony - quite thoroughly smashing the critters into a puddle.

As for which description of events is accurate, this reporter will make no judgment.  After all, in the immortal words of Sancho Panza, "Whether the stone hits the pitcher, or the pitcher hits the stone, it's going to be bad for the pitcher."  Feel free to form your own opinions as to the validity of this story, but please don't bother me with them.  I really don't care.

Oh, I almost forgot.  Madam Skycastle was kind enough to include a photographic record of her travels/travails.  View it here or in the pictures section.
