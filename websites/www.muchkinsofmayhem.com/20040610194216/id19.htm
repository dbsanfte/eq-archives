Part II

We had come to a sliced channel among the wind swept scrub dunes. The cut went against the wind, and we could be more or less comfortable for the night.

Bedazzled began unloading the Tanks, casting aside the packs haphazardously this way and that. Thwump shuffled off in search of fuel and whatever morsels might cross his path.

"Lords of Velious, man," Masticate grumbled. "You could be a bit more careful with that plate... we had to take on the entire West Commons outpost for that gear."

"No, it was the whole outpost and the Freeport reinforcements..." Pogrom chimed in. "There had to be at least 20 more slags from the garrison that came up on us near the end."

"Those were Orc Pawns, you tard, and there were two," the first countered. "Stop being an idiot."

Even for Barbarians, the two brothers were overly argumentative. I knew that any simple conversation could escalate into a riot if they were not calmed down quickly.

"No matter, Bedazzled apologizes," I shot a suggested look toward the Chanty.

"Yeah, sure... many apologies for scuffing your treasured plate. I'll be sure to give it all a good buffing tonight while you're asleep." He winked in my direction to share the joke, of which I returned a scolding grimace. Now was not the time to get out of control.

"See, he apologized... now, lets set up the tents and get a fire pit ready for Thwump."

"Someone talk about Thwump? I got food..."

Our Beastlord flung himself over the channels edge and landed in the middle of our camp. One hand cradled a stack of grey, moss-eaten firewood to his chest. His other grasped a scraggled ankle of a scrubby Kobold.

"We eat dog tonight," Thwump said proudly. "I seen him crawling already, so I smushed doggie's head. Good stew these doggies. Good stew."

Looking around at the other members of the party, I could see that I wasn't the only one repulsed. Up till now, we had been dining on bear meat, the occaisional moss snake... even a giant spider now and then. But here before us was the corpse of a semi-intelligent being.

"You think we can eat that?" Masticate asked. "I mean, it is a kind of dog I suppose..."

"Um, I think it's OK, I mean, it's not like an Orc or anything..." Pogrom said. "I've eaten dire wolf before. Probably the same thing here."

Thwump chuckled. "It no problem. Eaten doggie many times. Tastes like... griffawn. Like wussy griffawn."

"Personally, I think I'll pass on this barbeque, I can just munch away on my dried snake skin. Kobold is just too weird for me..." I said.

"Myself as well," Bedazzled chimed in. "I'll just have some of the wizards snake."

"More for Thwump, that good. Thwump happy," our TimberTroll said. "Doggie make strong."

He pitched the firewood into the pit, and I flared off a low level burst of flame to get it going. In no time at all, we had ourselves a comfortable camp...
