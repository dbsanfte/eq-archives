I. DEFINITION

The Circle of Virtue is a guild on the Rathe server. It is a guild dedicated to the path of light, and helping those good creatures of Norrath who are beset by evil.

Within the Circle, guild members are treated as family, supporting each other above all else.

II. INFRASTRUCTURE

The guild is composed of three parts: Guild Leader, Council of Officers and The Circle.

GUILD LEADER - A member of the guild who has the rank of Guild Leader. The Guild Leader has the responsibility of guarding and overseeing the guild as a whole, and making certain that the reputation of the guild remains untarnished. The Guild Leader will be available to anyone in the guild for assistance in resolving disagreements, and may call upon other Officers in closing any issue. All decisions of the Guild Leader are binding, so long as they do not conflict with the rules set forth in the Charter. The Guild Leader will decide all issues not directly covered in the charter.

COUNCIL OF OFFICERS – The Council of Officers consists of the Elders, Baronies, Justicary and Duchies. Each Officer shall have a specific responsibility within the guild.

Elder – An Officer of the guild who once held the rank of Guild Leader and has stepped down.

Barony (Baron/Baroness) – An Officer of the guild who handles affairs and problems regarding events outside the guild, and assists the Guild Leader in governing the guild.

Justicary (Justicar) – An Officer of the guild who resolves disagreements between guild members, and assists the Guild Leader in governing the guild. The Justicary consists of two Justicars and an Inquisitor. The Inquisitor is head of the Justicary.

Duchy (Duke/Duchess) – An Officer of the guild who aid the Guild Leader, Barony and Justicary in resolving disagreements as they arise.

THE CIRCLE – The Circle consists of all guild members who are not Officers. There are four ranks within The Circle: Page, Squire, Knight and Captain. Advancement in rank within the Circle can be achieved by completing a Quest given to you by an Officer.

Page – The first rank of the Circle, and the rank to which all new members are assigned.

Squire – The second rank of the Circle, and one that is attained by the completion of a Quest given by an Officer.

Knight – The third rank of the Circle. This rank is achieved by completing a more challenging Quest given by an Officer.

Captain – The fourth rank of the Circle. This rank is achieved by completing a difficult Quest given by an Officer, along with great service to the guild.

III. INVITATION POLICY

For admittance into the guild, you must be a minimum of level 15 and have three Sponsors, one of whom must be an Officer. All three Sponsors need to have been in the guild at least one month.

One Sponsor must accompany the person petitioning for admittance into the guild for one real-time hour within Norrath. The behavior of the Petitioner during these times will determine whether they are recommended for admittance into the guild.

Part of the admittance process is to go to the guild website, read the Charter, have some knowledge of who the Officers are, and post a message on the Message Board. Once you have been admitted, you will need to post your character information using the Member Information Update Form. The Officer who invited you will introduce you to the guild and answer any questions you have about the guild.

IV. CODE OF CONDUCT/PRINCIPLES

We are as a family, and all members shall treat each other with respect, regardless of rank, level or length of time in the guild.

Absolutely no Kill Stealing, Ninja Looting, blatant rudeness, or vulgarity (to anyone, not just guild members) will be permitted in a guild member. Kill Stealing is defined as killing an NPC or Creature in combat with (or being camped by) another player for your own gain. Ninja Looting is defined as looting an item that multiple people in your group may want before a decision has been made as to who gets the item.

Guild members are encouraged to group together, to request assistance from the guild and assist each other in achieving goals and equipment. Requesting items from higher-level members in the guild is discouraged.

Keeping your character in a continuous Anonymous state is discouraged. Not only does it not allow your fellow guild members to identify compatible levels with whom to group by typing /who all "circle of virtue", it implies that you do not wish to be bothered by requests for assistance. This mind-set does not suggest the definition of virtue that the Circle wishes to represent.

Roll playing is also encouraged, as it gives your character some depth, and gives other players more information about how to react to your character (you could be afraid of ghosts, have a certain code of honor, or have some other personality quirk.) Some people have developed character histories which are posted on the website. Roll playing is not required, though.

V. GUILD MEETINGS

Guild Meetings are held on the First and Third Sundays of each month at 5:00pm PST (Pacific Standard Time) at the Wizard Spires in North Karana. If you cannot be there, please try to participate over guild chat.

Meeting attendance is not always mandatory, but strongly encouraged so that you know what issues and events are taking place within the guild. If you have any announcements or questions that you would like to be addressed in the guild meeting, tell the Officer running the meeting in a private message before the end of the meeting.

Always feel free to make comments or suggestions during guild meetings, but please keep chatter, spell casting, fighting and other types of spamming to a minimum during meetings so that they can be as short as possible.

This charter is subject to change as we grow.
