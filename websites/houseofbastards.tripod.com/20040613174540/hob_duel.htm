WILDRIDE (25) def. NIKAR (24) - Dagnor's Cauldron, Dec. 20, 2000
  Only a few weeks ago Nikar and Wildride sparred each other in Greater Faydark just to hone their fighting skills and pass the time. It was then that Wildride received his first experience in fighting a Magician of the skill and cunning of a true HoB member. With two full levels separating them (Wildride the senior) Nikar proceeded to display his mastery of the earthen elements. Just before Wildride took his last breath, Nikar showed mercy on his foe and allowed him to escape. Although this was not an official duel for the HoB crown and both combatants held their most leathal skills at bay, it was nevertheless a wakeup call for the current champion that there were members of the HoB clan that were growing strong and that the crown would not be held long without a serious challenge.

Last night Nikar formally challenged Wildride for the crown. Having worked long and hard over the past few weeks, Nikar closed the gap forbetween the two to only one level and with a new complement of pets to command, he felt he was ready the task. Immediatly after the HoB raid on the Estate of Unrest, Nikar, Wildride and Ziff (ever the referee and guild photographer) proceeded to the high ridge overlooking the lake in Dagnor's Cauldron and prepared for battle. Nikar summoned the elements to form some earthen abomination. He then equiped the pet with two Gnoll Hide Larriats and proceeded to layer magic shields on them both. Wildride doned what little magic shields he had and they were ready. Both faced each other and wished each other good luck, for they both felt confident that their opponent would need it for them to emerge victorious. Ziff counted down and the battle was on.

Immediatly Wildride rooted Nikar's pet and went after the little Gnome with steel swinging. Wildride was able to land a few significant blows before the earthen abomination broke loose from Wildride's magic and began to root Wildride instead. With some distance between them Nikar then prepared his magic. Wave after wave of fire and magic hit Wildride while he was helpless to defend himself, rooted securly to the scarred stone that was the ledge of Dagnor's Cauldron. Then as Wildride was down to his last bits of health and he saw Nikar's hands again glowing with the force of magic that had so devastated him so far, Wildride called upon his god, Rochet Nife, to deliver a healing hand to his faithful follower. Rochet Nife did not fail him and restored Wildride to fighting condition. Still rooted, as he would be for the majority of the battle, Wildride was being whipped by Nikar's the duel wielding earthen pet but also began to fire arrows at Nikar from a distance. Although not causing as much damage as would his sword, the constant barrage of arrows kept Nikar from concentrating on his next spell casting. With Nikar's pet slowly killing Wildride and Wildride switching between swings at the pet and arrows at Nikar, both saw the battle at a turning pont. Wildride was close to killing the pet and Nikar realized that without the pet, he would surely die. So Nikar dove into the melee to assist his pet and hoped to slay his foe before the pet met its end. Wildride then had to decide whether he should hit Nikar or finish off the pet and break free of its incessant rooting. Choosing to turn his attention to the little Gnome stabbing his backside, Nikar took some tremendous damage from Wildride's blade. When Nikar backed off, Wildride turned back to the pet. With the pet, Nikar and Wildride down to their last few breaths, the break that Wildride was looking for came. Wildride's rooted feet came free and Nikar, slowed from his failing health, was unable to doge the last few deadly blows from Wildrie's Ghoulbane and Mistmoore Shield. Nikar fell to the ground and the earthen pet turned back to dust.

As close a duel as we have had to date and one that demonstrated that the HoB members truly are masters of their professions.

WILDRIDE (23) def. ZIFF (22) - Lake of Ill Omen, Nov. 1, 2000
  Wildrides account:

The trash talking before this latest duel for the crown of the HoB was fierce. Ziff claiming certain victory and the imminent surprises that Wildride would experience. Wildride describing the method of decapitation that would be employed to help Ziff with his day-after-Halloween costume (the headless horseman).

Wildride and Ziff met in an empty clearing not far from the goblin mines that had just recently been claimed the rightful property of the HoB. Each buffed themselves and wished good luck to the other before the battle began. Wildride scored the first blow with an arrow to the midsection of Ziff but his magical armor deflected its damage. Then Ziff cast a nasty long spell on Wild that rendered him incapable of action. While Wild stood enthralled, Ziff prepared his surprise. He wandered deep into the Goblin infested mines and charmed a Goblin warlord to fight at his side. When Wildride regained his faculties, he was faced not with the expected feeble enchanter pet, but a full fledged Goblin Warlord salivating to rip Wildride apart. What trickery is this? Had Ziff made a secret pact with the goblins of the Lake of Ill Omen against the HoB?, thought Wildride. The Goblin attacked Wildride and inflicted considerable damage before Wild was able to use his new root spell. Once the goblins feet were firmly rooted to the ground, Wild approached Ziff and began to give him a beating suitable of a enemy of the HoB. When the goblin broke the root spell Wild again turned his attention to the goblin, rooted him and then back to Ziff. But this time Ziff stayed close to his new vile protector so that any attack on Ziff would be followed closely by a counter attack by the goblin. Then Wild had an idea. He waited until Ziff was a fair distance from the goblin pet and then rooted Ziff as well making him more easily attacked. About that time Wild discovered that Ziff had not turned his back on the HoB but had only charmed the one goblin. The goblin protector shook off the charm spell and turned viciously on Ziff. Now Wildride had an ally in the duel and watched as the once pet turned to foe and brought goblin friends to help. The Warlords made quick work of Ziff and even after relenting in the duel, Wildride was unable to taunt the goblins off of Ziff in time. Ziff died at their hands and lost some experience in the process.

As strange of a duel as he had ever had, Wildride remained champion of the HoB.

One reason why Norrath is not like the real world. In the real world they say keep your friends close and your enemies even closer. Ziff now knows this does not work too well in Norrath.

Ziff's retort:

Ziff brought out a Goblin Warrior, I'm not foolish enough to bring a Warlord. The Warrior was 22nd level. The Goblin Warrior never turned on me for more than 2-3 blows. As my trusted pet, he was of little consequence and quickly corrected his wrongful ways when striking me.

The Warlord was an entirely different matter. He wandered in and began POUNDING me just after Wildride had rooted my trusty pet again. This became a SERIOUS problem very fast. Wildride was very noble in his attempts to save me, but alas, the Warlord was too powerful.

Wildride, I will tell you. The battle was only 1/2 over when we received outside interference. I had revealed but a small portion of my new surprises. The tables were about to turn most horribly for you when the Warlord arrived and if it were anyone of less noble spirit I would suspect unfair play.

Be warned, I will be back. My bag of tricks is deep and as you learned last time you were defeated, as an enchanter I will wear you down, slowly, surely and horribly till you face your ultimate doom. You will have time to savor the fear of death.

In the meantime I will say it was a very interesting fight, well fought by a worthy adversary who deserves the crown on his head. For now...

more from Wildride:

I look forward to another test from my worthy opponent. It was not a fair test of skills and though I will retain the crown and wear it proudly, I will only receive full satisfaction for the duel when I can wrest your last breath with my own hands. --- Wildride


WILDRIDE (23) def. LUIFANG (23) - Lake of Ill Omen, Oct. 19, 2000
  Another duel for the crown and glory of the House of Bastards was witnessed in Lake of Ill Omen last night. The longtime comrades Luifang and Wildride stood in a shallow valley sizing each other up. Though Wild and Lui had fought together against common foes recently, it had been the cold caverns of Permafrost in the far north many seasons ago that they last tested their skill and courage against each other. In that battle Wildride was fortunate to retain his title. Since that last meeting much has transpired and this time Wildride was standing before Luifang, as a challenger instead of the defending champion.

Both of the 23rd level, this battle would prove to truly be a test of skill and cunning for both combatants. A crowd assembled around, and the low murmuring of the onlookers' speculations and wagers could be heard as the first strike of steel against flesh and fists against body broke through the din. Luifang started the fight with a furious barrage of flying kicks, and punches. The speed at which she moved and hit shocked Wildride and before he was aware he had been reduced to half his health. Trying to regain his composure he ran from the battle unsure of his ability to defeat Luifang who had obviously grown in strength and skill since their last meeting. Sufficiently far from the battle where none of the assembled crowd could view, Wildride took a deep breath, better prepared himself for the now expected fury and rushed back into the battle where Luifang was still waiting. This time Wildride was able to inflict some significant damage to Luifang and surprised Luifang with his new abilities to affix Lui's deadly feet to the ground. While rooted, Wild took his available moments to heal himself enough for the next wave. Eventually Lui's superior capacity to inflict damage brought Wildride to the brink of defeat where he called on his immortal master Rochet Nife to help him summon the will to continue. Rochet Nife smiled down on Wild and revived his loyal follower. Reinvigorated, Wild slung blade and magic against the still furious fists and kicks of Luifang till the final blow was dealt by Wildride's magical harpoon. Luifang lay limp on the ground as the crowd cheered and the champion's belt was awarded to the new HoB Champion.

ZIFF (20) def. WILDRIDE (20) - Dagnor's Cauldron, Aug 13, 2000
  Ziff, even without his spiffy new colorshift spell, wore down the seasoned champion with a barrage pets, and hurling whirls. Utilizing every trick in the book including walking on water far out of the reach of Wild's Deepwater Harpoon and magic arrows and magically entwining Wild's legs so as to make him completely immobile.

Even with the Ziff's pranks, Wild was able to stay in the fight for the longest duel to date, slaying pet after pet and getting a few good licks in on the sly one himself. But in the end, Wild was slowly being worn down without being able to inflict enough damage on anybody but Ziff's pets. In the exhaustion of battle, Wild mistakenly summoned the will of Rochet Nife to heal Ziff's Jabner, no doubt another trick, and once Wild came to his senses, realized the battle had been lost and that the only chance to escape alive was to make a sprint for the zone. With a refreshed Jabner and an Undertow Skeleton joining the chase, Wild reached the zone with only the slightest life remaining.

WILDRIDE def. XANTHALUS - Beholder's Maze, July 26, 2000

XANTHALUS def. WILDRIDE - Innothule, June 28, 2000
  Wildride and Xanthalus faced off outside of Guk at 8:00 CST. With the swamp gas rising a battle for the HoB championship ensued. The wizards spells caused the earth to tremble, Wildride's new harpoon cut deep. The clanging of steel on steel could be heard throughout the swamp and Wildride was seen to be getting weaker. His knees began to buckle under the heavy blows of magic and so he called upon his gods to assist him. He conjured a wondrous healing spell, and in his pain and confusion laid his hands upon his enemy. Wildride was doomed and cried "yield!" before Xanthalus. Xanthalus was crowned champion of HoB

WILDRIDE (16) def. ZIFF (15) - Innothule, May 30, 2000
  At a brief impromptu gathering in Guk Tuesday, Wildride and Ziff decided to duke it out. The result was Wildride handing Ziff a sound trouncing. At one point Ziff had Wildride reduced to the point where he almost couldn't even swing his sword, but alas, Ziff managed his mana poorly and found himself soon facing a reinvigorated and extremely angry foe, with no mana and no where to hide. After being reduced to less than a bubble of health, Ziff humbly surrendered.

WILDRIDE (16) def. FAAN (15) - Oasis, May 29, 2000
  Without either having any prior dueling experience, nor premeditated strategies, Wildride and Faan decided to see what it was all about...

On Monday was held the battle royal between Faan the Cleric and Wild the Paladin. With many advantages, including one level higher, Wild had little problem reducing Faan's health to such low level it sent him running for the zone screaming in terror. However during the battle it should be noted that Wild was stricken with Fear several times and lost control of his senses.

And thus, the unofficial beginning of the Dueling Bastards.
