RECOUNT

by Ziff

First and foremost, I want to tell everyone that although the night went badly, I still logged off proud to be a member of this guild and of the fighting force known as HoB. We were faced with a lot of bad luck last night. Massive lag for many players, Link Deaths, pulls gone awry, bad mob combinations and just plain unlucky "rolls" lead to an uphill battle for the entire night. Many people commented "doesn't this seem harder than last week?" and indeed it was. But as a team we stood our ground. We forged ahead. We stood back to back, neck deep in gnolls, blood and chaos and no one flinched. No one backed down. I saw tanks standing, swinging and taking blows in combats with less than 20% health knowing full well their healer was either dead or unable to assist them. I saw healers chain casting onto tanks to keep them alive knowing that the Gnolls would soon turn on them in pure hatred and pound them to bloody ruins. I saw casters wade into combat and make valiant attempts to interrupt or damage an enemy even though they knew that enemy would soon turn on their practically naked body to pummel abuse. I saw a team that stayed focused, even when the chips were down. To a man no one flinched, no one lost sight of what their role was. We stood, fought and died as a team. The end may not have been pretty, but it made me no less proud.

HoB rocks. Pure and simple.

The story...

For a solid week various members had harried the gnolls. They invaded their dank hole and chopped down their brethren. They sneaked into their lair and slaughtered their minions shouting insults and warnings. After 6 days of such abuse they mounted a miniature assault and, with a crew of merely 6 members, ripped the teeth from the gnoll brotherhood and tossed them on the floor like used gum. HoB had devastated the gnolls the previous week and they were keeping them on their furry heels all week with a constant battering of assaults.

Now, 8 days after the first highly touted raid, HoB was back in full force. Excitement spread through the gathering throng as they prepared their assault. The goal was again to cut a deep wound into the gnoll lair and attempt to take out the gnoll leaders to a bloody grave. The tale of their exploits had spread through the land and they were ready to meet the challenge.

10 members began the assault. HoB worked their way to the feared "double doors" with ease and precision. Gnolls fell with nary a scratch being dealt to the team of heroes. The assault on the double doors proved a bit troublesome, but HoB again prevailed and charged deeper into the lair.

Soon they set up a defensive camp in the ante room. Here they were joined by their old friend Awzz. They cleared the ante room and Luifang began bringing gnolls from farther down the hallway. But it was soon becoming clear that this night was different. The gnolls were somehow stronger. With each fight HoB became weaker. HoB stayed focused, stayed solid but their enemy was bringing them closer and closer to death with each combat.

Then the gnolls struck. While mired in a combat with 2 large gnolls 2 more charged the room. Chaos ensued. Spells flew, blades rang out against blades but the gnolls had a target of their own. In moments they had taken down both druids and reduced the HoB force to a very weakened state. The surprise attack by the gnolls had proven a deadly one and hushed whispers could be heard from the ranks.

HoB regrouped and shook off the devastating attack. With renewed strength they pushed on. Soon they were back at the stone bridge with the waterfall. The assaults from the gnolls kept coming, fast and furious. It was a constant struggle for HoB to stay on top but they fought bravely, tooth and nail. They butchered their way across the bridge and charged the landing and set up a barricaded camp. They would make their stand here. It had taken them almost 1 1/2 hours to get here and they would likely not try to go any deeper. They all knew they were in as far as they could go.

Luifang began retrieving gnolls from the depths attempting to reach some of the named leaders. Suddenly, a pull went very wrong. Luifang could not shake the gnolls from her and as she entered the group they remained focused on killing her. Her reputation had spread through the dogs ranks and these ones were bound for revenge. The team fell upon the gnolls but not before she was destroyed by powerful magic's. Soon after the gnolls again turned on Lorsua and as a focused attack forces, cut him in two and left his bloody corpse to rot on the stone. Panic could be felt welling in the throats of HoB. But, strong to their name they buckled down and went to work. With anger and precision they assaulted the enemy and soon, bloodied, battered and nearly spent of life and magic they stood victorious.

Luifang and Lorsua returned to the group. HoB decided to reorganize a bit in hopes of having a better balance to handle the gnolls. They cleaned their weapons and donned protective magic's and soon were fully prepared for the next assault.

What they did not know is that the gnolls had set a deadly trap for the team.

Luifang was assaulted by a Nich Val Gnoll, of about 37 seasons and two Nisch Mal Gnolls. One of the deadliest killers in the entire lair backed with two of the most powerful clerics. The trap had been sprung.

HoB met the trap with the force and teamwork that has made them legends. They turned their backs on the deadly Val and attacked the healers knowing that if they were not killed, they would never prevail. The deviousness of the trap then began to reveal itself. The Val ran freely through the ranks of HoB like an assassin. He dealt blow after blow to various members, crippling them and confusing them. The clerics focused on healing each other and, without an effective means for interrupting them they would soon go from nearly dead to full health. HoB brought the clerics to deaths door 7 times each before the battle would be done. Each time the clerics healed themselves back to life while the Val ran amuck in the ranks. This trap proved deadly for the group. The Val slaughtered Luifang and Faan in short order. Then it went to work on the druids and tanks, sharing damage destruction amongst all of them. The mounting tide had to be turned. If the clerics could be killed HoB could still prevail. But, seeing all three of his warriors at below 50% health, Ziff called for a retreat. Too late. The gnolls cut down the HoB crew like wheat, barking howls of revenge. Zoritsa and Ziff barely escaped with their lives. The rest of the crew was slaughtered where they stood.

HoB had been smashed by a devious gnoll trap. The most difficult and deadly one they had faced to date.

Outside in the tunnel they regathered. With the help of a friendly cleric they were resurrected from the dead and, seeing as it was late, decided to call it a night. Discouraged and defeated they packed their treasure and prepared a cold camp for the night.

Loot for the night was 12 plat 5 gold each. We have a bunch of tunics, belts, masks and other goodies to sell. When we do, we will split the cash for those as well. I think we also have enough scrolls for 2 or more of the quests to be completed. This will be a nice reward for 2 evenings of minimal treasure.

Expereince was about 1 bubble for those that only died once and got resurected. Those that had a more difficult night did not fare as well. On the good side, Ziff and Wildride did level.

Again, the night ended on a bad note, but we still stood strong. We will come back to Paw after we all gain a level or 2 and we will finish this assault and go even deeper. The gnolls have not heard the last of us!
