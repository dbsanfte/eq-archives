Ziff:
Howls, growling and barking echoed up from the depths. This place known as Split Paw Lair stank. It stank with the fetid smell of blood, death and dog. HoB was no stranger to the rank beasts within this hole. HoB had fought many a gnoll before in the depths of Blackburrow. That lair was where HoB first hunted and began honing their skills and tactics. Over a year later they were anxious to demonstrate all they had learned to a more seasoned more viscous clan of gnolls.

At the top of the hole, 12 veterans of the depths of Norrath assembled. Protective magic's were cast, armor was donned, tactics were discussed and lots of chest thumping and shield bashing could be heard in answer to the growls and howling. Excited, and pumped full of adrenaline HoB charged the hallways like a wave of death.

That wave nearly crashed upon the rocky shores of the gnoll stronghold. Their first enemies, a pair of semi seasoned gnolls, reduced the party to less than 1/3 mana and posed some serious problems to the group as a whole. But HoB would not be stopped. They quickly reorganized, made small adjustments in tactics and began anew. HoB's honed skills and flexibility proved to be the bane of many a gnoll that night.

With a revised approach Luifang began delving the depths of the lair, pulling gnolls out in groups of two and three. HoB fell upon their prey with a reckless precision. In less than 1/2 of an hour Luifang had cleared a pathway deep into the heart of the gnoll tunnels and HoB charged across the broken and butchered corpses to make a stand deep in their lair, leaving behind a large group of veteran adventurers, some more than twice HoB's level, cowering in the doorway HoB had charged through.

Whispers could be heard from the veterans. "Madness. They are simply crazy. They are all going to die."

HoB would have none of it. They established a foothold in a small ante room and Luifang began harrying gnolls from deeper in the lair. The gnolls were getting stronger, trickier and more deadly. HoB simply buckled down and attacked with organized patience, wearing down the warriors till they fell to gleaming blades and spells and harassing the casters till they could no longer concentrate on their magic's. In less than 30 minutes Luifang again announced that a pathway had been cleared to a landing overlooking a cavernous lake.

HoB stormed the landing and set up shop. Luifang again brought gnolls from still deeper in the lair. These proved to be an even greater challenge, but one HoB met with a mad blood lusting fever. A fever that would not be quenched by these dogs.

As the group was preparing to move even deeper within the caverns, Calaran experienced a strange magical calling and was suddenly whisked away. HoB at first moved deeper into the caverns but then, deciding they were too far from where Calaran would return, moved back. Gladly, Calaran returned shortly afterwards and so HoB moved to the other side of the cavern and cleared an area for battle. This was as deep as they would go, nearly 3/4 of the way into the lair of the gnolls HoB had fought with bravery and intelligence. Not a single member had fallen victim the gnolls magic's, claws or blades. They would make their stand here and Luifang would attempt to draw out Rosch Val L'Vlor. A gnoll who carried a tome of great power and magic.

For more than an hour Luifang combed the depths of the Split Paw clan searching for this gnoll. She brought gnolls out who were casters, rogues, clerics, necromancers, enchanters, shaman and more. Each one fell to the brutally organized teamwork of HoB. She even brought several of their leaders, Mal and Val gnolls. Some proved to be great adversaries, taking 5 minutes or more to kill. Others fell so fast they barely had time to wine. At several points HoB was overwhelmed with gnolls and things looked bleak. But, the group stood strong and each time came out bloodied, battered and victorious. The corpses piled higher and higher and still no sign of Rosch Val L'Vlor.

The evening began to come to a close. The named gnoll had not shown his ugly face and HoB was ready to leave the lair of Split Paw. Like an organized swat team Luifang led a retreat from the depths of Split Paw that was never hurried, never dangerous and always precise. In less than 15 minutes the entire raiding party emerged into the morning light, battered, bloody, and tired, but most of all victorious. The gnolls had been dealt a savage blow. HoB would be back in a week to deal them another.

First and foremost - INCREDIBLE JOB EVERYONE! I mean that. HoB has been together for more than a year now. We have run numerous raids in varying levels of success. The loot from last night was nothing to get too excited about, but the teamwork was top notch. The communication was excellent, everyone stuck to their roles and kept their heads. We had a few mistakes here and there but we banded together and recovered from them like a solid team. Special "hats off" to the Kodman, Galadriel and Dicerman. This was their first time working with us and they did a top notch job, even in critical roles like healing and snaring. I can honestly say that this was our most dangerous raid EVER and yet I felt like we were in more control of the situation the entire night than we have been on most other raids. I hope everyone had as much fun as I did.

Experience for the night was very solid. First - no deaths. Guadzilla leveled (congrats) the rest of us got between 1 1/2 and 2 1/2 bubbles of experience. The new tactic of attacking multiple mobs seemed to spread the experience out a little more evenly without so much work and distraction.
