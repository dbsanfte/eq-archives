SOLSEK'S EYE
Mar. 8, 2001


RAIDING PARTIES
Faan - cleric (27), Nekko - shadow knight (28), Zoritsa - enchanter (29), Wildride - paladin (28)

Others: Ssaul - monk (25)

RECOUNT
by Wildride

Great night all! I think we had one death in each group but overall I think everyone received net positive experience for the night.

Group 1 was unable to locate the King but laid waste to his court all night. We also met some very nice people, including a Lvl 25 Iksar Monk named SSaul. He was a solid addition to our team and if you ever see him looking for a group I would recommend inviting.

We also explored some uncharted areas in Sol A, found a strange force field that kept us from advancing down one hallway and finally let Faan take a lava bath (phew and did he need one!)

Loot was not spectacular for Group 1 (about 30pp per person) [ed. note: though no special items, the loot was spectacular -- best total per/char ever!] but we had fun, adventured like it was meant to be done, meet nice people along the way and charted new territory for HoB.


RAIDING PARTIES
Garall - paladin (29), Gorron - shadow knight (28), Guadzilla - shaman (29), Lanorolen - bard (27), Ziff - enchanter (25/26)

RECOUNT
by Ziff

Nothing stinks like sulfur, except maybe sulfur mixed with the fetid stench of an age old goblin lair. That is a smell so foul it is nearly unbearable to even the staunchest of men. Yet back into that very stench HoB crawled again. One last time into the hot reeking hole of Solusek eye One last time to rip the putrid heart from the goblins of Norrath.

At the entrance they assembled as two groups. As a unit they plunged into the rank depths like a hot dagger leaving a wake of dead like Hansle and Grettle's bread crumbs. HoB struck so fast and hard they did not even bother to loot the dead.

In ten minutes they were crouched in a small room they had dubbed the gatehouse. As the teams organized Wildride began dragging goblins from the depths. This proved to be a bit of a mistake. A band of goblins stormed the small room and became attracted to Guadzilla horrid smell. In typical weird goblin fashion they showed appreciation for his rank smell by slashing and clawing him to death.

After collecting themselves, recovering Zilla's corpse and adding some new members they split into two teams. Team one charged off in the direction of the king. Team two headed to the bar.

In a slight fit of madness team two alternated pullers between Gorron and Ziff. For almost two hours team two ruled their half of the dungeon. They wandered to new territory with little worry leaving corpses and stone slick with the blood of their foes.

As they carved their way to new territory, Ziff found a place on the map that was oddly blank. There was a door on the faded map he carried but beyond that door was only a skull and crossbones. Lacking any fear, (and some would say brains) the team sought out the door and charged through. On the other side they met two Solusek goblins, some of the most powerful goblins in Solusek eye. Like their brethren they were dispatched quickly. Team two took a short rest and charged through the next door. There they met three Solusek goblins. This was a nasty battle but again team two proved victorious. Now standing in a huge entryway containing two massive wooden doors HoB prepared for its next battle. After a brief rest they threw open the doors to see a giant staircase leading down to a massive chamber. Through the smoke and haze could be seen this outline of two Solusek goblins. Ziff gave a shout and cast a spell to make one more vulnerable to Ziff's magic's. As the two angry goblins charged up the staircase the side door burst open and three more goblins charged the room. To make matters worse, all of these were Solusek goblins. One was even a wizard and another was a shaman. Team two was in trouble.

A panicked organization took place. Ziff worked desperately to control the beasts. Slowly the tide was turning in their favor but not before Lan was cut down. As the haze cleared team two stood gasping for breath badly wounded and fully depleted of mana.

They chopped their way back to the barroom, dragging Lan's bloody corpse behind. The plan was to make a stand in the barroom until Lan could return. The goblins had other plans.

In minutes team two was up to their necks in the weaker monsters. They fought desperately but the goblins were numerous. When the battle was done team two stood battered, bloody, and spent, but victorious. Sadly, during the combat they had once again taken a liking to Guadzilla's stench and rewarded him with a gruesome death.

Team two collected the corpses of their possessions and headed home. To celebrate a solemn and sobering victory.
