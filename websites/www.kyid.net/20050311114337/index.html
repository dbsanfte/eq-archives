Ok, I ran with my new runners and socks today. At first (perhaps the first couple mins) it was a tiny bit uncomfortable across the outside edges of my feet. I am very flat-footed when I walk or run, and these new runners have very strong motion control support so my arch is held up when I run and my feet don’t pronate as much as a consequence. Turkey Chili

March 10th, 2005 at 06:50

This recipe is good in all phases of the South Beach Diet.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 lb. (454g) ground turkey
  • 2 medium onions; chopped as finely as desired
  • 1 yellow bell pepper; chopped as finley as desired
  • 1green bell pepper; chopped as finely as desired
  • 2-3cloves of garlic; finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp. chili powder
  • 1 tsp. ground cumin
  • 1 can diced tomatos
  • 1 can kidney beans, or black beans
  • 1 can tomato sauce

Directions:

  1. Put olive oil, turkey, bell peppers, onions, and garlic in a pot and cook until vegetables soften and turkey is no longer pink.
  2. Add remaining ingredients and simmer in pot, stirring occassionally for 30 minutes to 1 hour
  3. Serve and enjoy!

Interval Changeup

March 9th, 2005 at 08:41

Lower quad update: “Still sore! More to follow below!”

Well, when I got to the Wellness Institute this morning I decided to try changing my interval timings and see how I felt.

Since this past Sunday I have been doing a 30 minute workout consisting of 5 intervals of 4/1 walk/run with a 5 minute walk at the end. Today I decided to try a harder workout, so I changed my intervals from 4/1 to 3/2 walk/run with a 5 minute walk at the end. The first three intervals were not too bad. By the third I was starting to feel my breathing get more laboured and it was much harder to get my breathing back to normal in time for the fourth interval. The fourth and fifth intervals were hard. The fifth was killer and I really wanted to stop running after one minute. But, I pushed myself to finish, “Come on, it’s the last one! Suck it up!”.

Prior to the workout I stretched both my calves and my quads, and I think it really helped. My quads are sore, but my gut feeling is that they would feel far worse if I didn’t stretch considering the interval change I made today.

I will try this same interval timing until next Wednesday, and see how I feel from that point about switching to a 2/3 walk/run interval. Though, based on this morning, I may need to stay at 3/2 for a while.

No Pain, No Gain?

March 7th, 2005 at 07:51

To say that my legs are extremely sore would be an understatement. I think I need to change my pre-workout strategy. I have only been doing calf stretches up until this point, but I think I really need to do fairly complete leg stetches from now on. The pain is in the same general area it has been since day 1 - the quad above the knee. However, today the pain feels more localized to the part of the quad on the inside of both legs (think inner thigh, but near the knee).

I couldn’t run very fast at all today. I did keep with the 30 minutes on the track that I started yesterday. My left leg was/is more sore than my right. I don’t think I will switch to a 3/1 walk/run interval this week. I will stay with 4/1 until I adjust to the 30 minutes in workout length, and the soreness in my legs subsides. I think I will buy some Tiger Balm (beware - cheesy flash site) to help with the muscle pain if I can find some. I used to use it years ago when I studied Kung Fu when I lived in Toronto.

So my plans for modifying my workout look something like this:

  • shop for an exercise mat
  • research some leg stretches
  • perform leg stretches shortly after getting up, but before heading to gym
  • incorporate a limited number of crunches (15-25?) to start developing abdominal strength (perhaps 3-4 times per week)
  • adjust as required

Oh, and I will put off starting a late afternoon workout until my legs aren’t so sore all the time. Though, I would like to add one in the future.

Ok, time for breakfast and the off to work I go.

My Mom, Connie, and Carla

March 5th, 2005 at 09:40

I was talking to my mom yesterday (early) evening trying to talk her into coming over for dinner tonight (Saturday). I decided to tell her about the Cheesy Frittata I made from the South Beach Diet book. It is the best frittata I have ever tasted. So she invited herself over last night for dinner instead of tonight, the condition being I had to make the Cheesy Frittata for her. So, over she came, and I got to making the frittata.

I put on the TV to entertain her while I chopped vegetables, etc, and what was on one of the Movie Central channels? Connie and Carla. I had a feeling this was a movie my mother would like (I had seen parts of it before on Movie Central), so we watched that while I prepped supper. It was like our own version of Dinner and a Movie.

The Dinner

My mom had a couple glasses of wine, I drank Carb Options Raspberry Ice Tea, and we both enjoyed the Cheesy Frittata. Next time I won’t add any diced (seeded) tomato. It added too much moisture. For desert I made Ricotta Mocha from the South Beach Diet book. I think I need to add a bit more Splenda the next time I make it. It wasn’t bad at all, it just wasn’t as sweet as I expected.

The Movie

This movie rates a 6/10.

I’m not sure that G would find this movie amusing, but I certainly did. Connie and Carla reminded me (slightly) of some of the comedies that were filmed in the 50s and 60s. My mother certainly thought it was funny, as did I. It was campy, and enjoyable. I say - rent it.
