Robots

This movie rates a 7/10.

Even in a world populated entirely by mechanical beings Rodney Copperbottom (McGregor) is considered a genius inventor. Rodney dreams of two things, making the world a better place and meeting his idol, the master inventor Bigweld (Brooks). On his journey he encounters Cappy (Halle Berry), a beautiful executive `bot with whom Rodney is instantly smitten, the nefarious corporate tyrant Ratchet (Kinnear) who locks horns with Rodney, and a group of misfit `bots known as the Rusties, led by Fender (Williams) and Piper Pinwheeler (Bynes).

Robots is a fun movie to watch. The audience when I was at the theatre was very young (a lot of parents with kids), but there is enough humour at the adult level (I mean in terms of cultural references that children probably wouldn’t get) in the film to keep the parents entertained as well. The quality of the animation was impressive, and I think not having to worry about things like animating realistic looking human skin and hair in the film allows the audience to appreciate the complexity of the visuals even more.

I would say go see it - especially if you have a child, sibling, cousin, etc who is young that you can take with you. They will really enjoy the movie as well.
