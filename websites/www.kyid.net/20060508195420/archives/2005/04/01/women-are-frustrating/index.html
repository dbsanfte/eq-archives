Women Are Frustrating

Women are frustrating.

I am a member of several online dating services, one of them being Lavalife. On my Lavalife “hotlist” (basically a personal list of people you are interested in keeping bookmarked), there is one woman in particular that I have had a desire to write to and meet for quite a while now.

I had, a while back, either sent her a brief message, or a smile to indicate my interest, but received nary a reply. That was long before I started working out, and losing weight. I now am feeling much more confident about myself, and I decided to give it another shot by emailing her a fairly long email that was more detailed than my onsite profile.

Now, before I go any further, you need to understand that this woman has been on my hotlist for a few months at minimum. I occassionally re-read her profile to see if she changes the text, and I know for sure she is rotating the main picture she uses for her profile. She is fairly active on Lavalife - I would guess logging in at minimum several times a week.

The email I sent her outlined more details of who I am, and gave some insights into my values as a person. I also included this blog address and invited her to read it passively to get to know me a bit better with no pressure at all on her.

She actually responded kindly. She basically said she admired my lifestyle changes, and that I should be proud of them (and I am). But then she said something that I didn’t quite know how to interpret:

This is part of what she said in reply:

I’m not really actively looking to meet people right now. I just kind of threw a few profiles out there to see what was up. And I have about 1 minute of tolerance for Lavalife before it starts to eat away at my soul, so I dont respond to many messages.

See, my issue with this is that I know she is active on Lavalife. She changes her pictures on a semi-frequent basis. If not interested in meeting people, why keep a profile? Or, at the very least indicate in your profile you aren’t interested in meeting people at the moment. I get the feeling she just wasn’t interested, but was trying to let me down easy. I would rather have the truth than misdirection.

So, I decided to reply once more. I started my reply with a preamble saying that I wouldn’t send her any more emails after this one unless she responded to it in a positive fashion (i.e. if she responded with “piss off” I would assume that was bad). I then gave her a few more tidbits of information about me and my values, and ended the email by inviting her to email me and ask for my cell number to call me so I could take her to lunch on Saturday or Sunday.

She has opened the email, but alas, I have not received a response.

I find the process of trying to meet someone online to be a massively frustrating one. I think the women on these dating sites (especially attractive ones) must be deluged by asshole guys looking for one-night stands and such. As a consequence the women seem to be very guarded about the idea of meeting in real life after only a few exchanges of emails through the Lavalife system. I find it to be an strange thing because if two people meet in real life (not in some virtual dating system), there wouldn’t usually be a massive stigma if they were interested in each other, and one asked the other out to coffee, or lunch, etc.

Another thing that is frustrating for me personally, is that I have very high standards for the type of woman I want to date, and I feel no need to compromise those standards in order to get more dates that I really wouldn’t be interested in long-term. My friends and family have at times in the past called me on this and suggested I lower my expectations. I however maintain that I shouldn’t have to, and if the price of keeping the bar set where it is means that I am single longer, then so be it.

My pickiness about those women I am interested in certainly also proves to be a barrier in the online dating world. I rarely see profiles that interest me. I like to see the woman has a visible picture on profile, that she has taken the time to write a profile, and that she has at least a decent grasp of the english language and how to use it. Demonstrating attributes such as wit and intelligence score points, and being a woman I find attractive is important as well.

Oh well, in the end I am better for trying to talk her into a date, than having never tried at all, and that is the spin I will have to take from this.
