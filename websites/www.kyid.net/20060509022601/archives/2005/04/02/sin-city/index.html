Sin City Rocks! »

Sin City

This movie rates an 8.5/10.

Walk down the right back alley in Sin City and you can find anything.

In 1991 Frank Miller started a graphic novel serial that shook the comic world. The Sin City graphic novels introduced a visceral world where the good guys are often the bad guys, and the anti-hero rules the story. The series was hailed for its unique visual presentation, and is admired by many comic fans as one of the best serials to ever be released.

A quote taken from IMDB:

After a poor Hollywood experience in the early-’90s, Frank Miller refused to relinquish the movie rights to any of his comic works, “Sin City” in particular. Robert Rodriguez, a longtime fan of the comic, filmed his own “audition” for the director’s spot in secret. The footage, shot in early 2004, featured Josh Hartnett and Marley Shelton acting out the “Sin City” short-story “The Customer is Always Right”. He presented the finished footage to Miller with the proclamation: “If you like this, this will be the opening to the movie. If not, you’ll have your own short film to show your friends.” Miller approved of the footage and the film was underway. Rodriguez also screened the footage for each of the actors he wanted to cast in the film - all of whom are reported to have been instantly amazed.

Flash forward to 2005 and the release of Sin City (the movie). Frank Miller has had a few movie adaptations of his work done in the past, and purportedly has been very disappointed in the past with the screen realizations of his visions. Robert Rodriguez, the director hired for Sin City wanted to make sure the movie would live up to Frank’s vision, and Frank was so involved in the movie project that he is receiving a shared director’s credit with Robert. Quentin Tarantino receives a guest director credit in the film, but you can feel his taught edgy influence all over the film. Indeed, I am sure some will draw comparisons to Quentin’s mega hit, Pulp Fiction. And there are a couple of commonalities between the two films. Both are a collection of short stories where characters of the short stories cross paths with each other while living out their story-lines, and both are also visual treats to watch, though in very different ways.

The realization of the world in which Sin City exists is, in my opinion, one of the best realizations of a comic book to ever be released on the Silver Screen. The film stays true to the black and white stylings of Frank Miller’s original work, with colours exploding out of the dark imagery to reinforce certain images in the viewer’s mind. You will find that the colour red pops out a lot, which is completely in tune with the amount of blood shed in this film. This is not a film to ever allow a child to see. The violence is extreme, and people lose their heads and body appendages in some extreme and gruesome ways.

A visual, visceral and imaginative tour-de-force, Sin City is truly a must-see movie if you can stomach the violent frentic unrelenting pace that it sets out.

Go see this movie to appreciate what an excellent adaptation of a comic book should look like in a movie theatre.
