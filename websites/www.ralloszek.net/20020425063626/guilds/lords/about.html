Purpose:

The Lords of Law was created in response to the large number of random acts of violence (PKing) observed in various parts of Norrath (mainly in the Steamfont, Lesser Faydark and Greater Faydark zones). The purpose of the Lords of Law is to reduce the occurrence of such acts, both by providing protection for new or struggling players and by 'persuading' confirmed 'random' PKers to reform. Since EverQuest is largely a role-playing game, it is hoped that the purpose of the Lords of Law can be fulfilled in a manner that is both fun for Lords of Law (LoL) members and/or in a manner that is consistent with good role-playing. However, LoL members should be prepared to sacrifice both fun and good role-playing for short periods and to do what is necessary to rid Norrath of the most pernicious PKers.

Before proceeding with guidelines on how to achieve the purpose set forth for the Lords of Law, let's set forth the definitions of some terms so that we are speaking a common language with the same meanings for words,

Character--A single character played in EQ, Never assume that all the characters played by a given person will behave the same. The crimes of one character do not spill over to other characters played by the same person. Nor do they spill over to the alignment, gender, race, class, faction or guild to which that character may belong.

Random PKer: This is a character who kills other characters in the game with no apparent reason. Examples, characters who haunt the beginning spawn sites to PK newbies, characters who hang around monster camps and when the player(s) are weak, PK them. Characters who declare the nearest race to 'enemies' just to have a justification for PKing someone. We will also sometimes refer to a random PKer as simply a PKer. It must be understood though, that PKer means random PKer and does not refer to a non-random PKer.

Non-random PKer: This is a character who PKs other characters but for reason. This should include all members of Lords of Law. It also includes most members of Darkenbane who PK members of Light races but only under very controlled circumstances, circumstances which are consistent with good role-playing. Feuds between characters is another example. ANY Guild war PKing should be considered non-random.

Good role-playing. this is a hard one to define. I put it here mainly to point out that the rules that most people accept for good role-playing were defined for a game system where character death was just that, the (likely) permanent death of that character. EQ is different. To be an effective member of Lords of Law you need to understand and work with those differences.

Hunt. Systematically seeking out one or more known random PKers and killing them repeatedly. This is one of the primary duties of a member of LoL. Hunting, as defined above, should NEVER be carried out except against known random PKers.

Guild war. This the EQ system of formalized warfare between two guilds. During a Guild war, the two guilds involved cannot change their rosters---add or remove members.

Guild hunt. This is a hunt, as defined above, applied to any and all members of a certain guild. Only the Lords of Law guild leader may declare a guild hunt.

Guidelines:

1. Identifying random PKers

Lords of Law maintains a list of known random PKers. It is the duty of LoL members to see to it that this list updated with new characters who are clearly random PKers and also to have removed characters that have reformed. When submitting someone to the list of random PKers, NEVER submit a name given to you by one character. In most cases you should witness the random PKer at least attempting this crime once yourself before submitting the characters name, preferably either more than once or against more than one other character. ALWAYS be prepared to admit a mistake and ask for that characters removal from the list if it is discovered that the 'random PKing' was really something else---retaliation for another PKing, feud, guild war, etc.

2. Hunting

Recall that hunting is something we only do against random PKers, never for any other reason. When hunting a random PKer(s) the goal should be to make on-line life so miserable for that character that the character reforms or the player playing that character finds it more rewarding to play a different character or do something else entirely. To further that goal, he re is very little within the game that is discouraged or considered out-of-bounds for L0L members 'hunting'. PK the random PKer frequently and often. Take loot him, most valuable items first. Take their experience if you can. If you can't take it directly seek the help of those who can (whether they belong ot LoL or not). Loot taken from during hunts should either go to the guild treasury or to non Lords of Law participants in the hunt. (We do not want the APPEARANCE of hunting for our own personal (profit.) If you are told that a certain item used to belong to a victim of the PKer, return it to its original owner. There are some restrictions however. Do NOT 'camp' the PKers body. It is certainly understandable for you to still be in the vicinity of his body upon his return if you are still battling other characters in the area. If his return is very rapid (his respawn point is nearby and he comes back very quickly), it is reasonable for you to still be in the area. But if he is killed a second time and there is no more fighting going on, LEAVE! Reports of 'camping' a PKers body, can get a member removed from Lords of Law.

During the course of a hunt you will frequently be questioned or attacked by characters near the character you are hunting. NEVER retaliate against these characters--although you may defend yourself without explanation ONCE. DO explain your actions to these characters. Explain why you are hunting the character you attacked, explain that you are a member of Lords of Law ant that it is your DUTY to hunt this character. If they doubt your claims of the villainy of the character you were hunting, try to provide them with a reference (another character, preferably outside LoL, who can verify this character's villainy). If these other characters continue to attack you after the explanation has been offered, you are then free to retaliate. However, do not immediately submit their names as random PKers. See the notes below on Guild hunt for info on what to do about them. Lords of Law will observe "non-PK" zones, such as Unrest. This is somewhat counter to the overall purpose but is in keeping with our 'law-keeping' role.

3. Guildhunt

Under certain conditions Lords of Law may wind up hunting an entire guild. This should only happen when either the guild engages in random PKing as a matter of policy OR a majority of the guild members are random PKers and the remainder of the guild defends those members when we hunt them. A guild hunt may only be declared by the guild leader of Lords of Law or, in his absence, his appointed replacement. Prior to declaring a guild hunt the Lords of Law guild leader should consult with the guild leader of the target guild and explain why the guild hunt is being declared and how the guild hunt can be ended. There are three ways a guild hunt can end.

i) The leader of the opposing guild announces that the random PKing practices of his guild are no longer supported by the guild. Any future acts of random PKing by members of his guild will either result in the members removal or at least will result in that member not being defended by his guild when LoL hunts him for his crimes. ii) It is no longer true that a majority of the members of the guild are random PKers. Whether this is achieved by the guild removing PKers from its roster or not, is not relevant.

iii) The Lords of Law guild leader can declare an end to a guild hunt when he believes that its continuation is not in the best interests of LoL. Hopefully, this would be under circumstances such as the guild has become so small and powerless, that they no longer pose a serious threat. But this could happen if a bigger threat arose, some change in EQ rules required it or some dispute within LoL made this desirable.

iiii) Guild hunt always applies to a guild, never the members. That is, once a player leaves a guild that we have targeted for guild hunt, they have a 'clean slate' and must earn the title of 'random Pker' anew in order for us to hunt them. Members of a guild that is being hunted should have this explained to them individually as well as to their guild leader before the guild hunt is declared by the LoL guild leader. Invite members of a guild being hunted to leave that guild frequently and often.

4. Guild war

This is a formal EQ war with another guild. This can be great fun for both sides with lots of opportunity for role-playing, etc. Guild wars are for guilds whom we, Lords of Law, respect. So this is not something we would do instead of a guild hunt. In fact, since Guild war precludes changing the guild rosters (characters entering or leaving a guild), guildwars should not be undertaken while a guild hunt is in progress.

Guild wars are NOT guild hunts! One should always treat the members of the other guild in guilder with chivalry and honor. Do NOT hunt them, Outside of large organized battles, kill them only once. If you loot them at all, take only money. Do not deliberately take their experience.

Summary:

Do pursue known random PKers relentlessly and without mercy.

Do grant mercy to those PKers who have reformed, unless you catch them again PKing randomly.

Do aid new players or others being troubled by PKers.

Do give away loot taken from hunting random PKers, either to their victims, to people aiding you in hunting them or to the guild treasury.

Do NOT camp bodies. Period.

Do NOT easily condemn someone as a random PKer. Get your facts straight first. What we plan to do to PKers IS NOT NICE, so be certain before you bring a character's name to the guild as a random PKer.

Do NOT name a character a random PKer just because they PKed you, even repeatedly. This could simply be 'role-playing' on their part. A single victim, even if it is you, is not enough to brand a character 'random PKer'. This one deserves an example. Cadric was PKed by Temporalis. She took his sash, which is a VERY bad thing to do to a monk---deep insult and all that. That act, by itself, is not enough to brand Temporalis as a 'random PKer'. That is not to say that Cadric could not or should not pursue his own, personal, revenge. He certainly should. But that act alone does not warrant the full wrath of Lords of Law.

Do give due honor and respect to the non-random PKers we find, whether in a guild or not.

Do NOT drag LoL into personal feuds and disputes.

Do tell non-LoL members of the reasons for hunting a particular 'random PKer' or guild.

Do tell prospective guild members about both our purpose and the guidelines above. In fact, new members should probably be required to read these guidelines and be prepared to answer questions about them on-line before being invited into the guild.

Do tell the guild leader or nearest available officer if you see another LoL member acting in violation of these guidelines.
