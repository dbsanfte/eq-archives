ENJOY
Welcome to the guide for Blacksmithing! One of the very profitable trades now in the game. The ultimate goal for most smithers to make is banded armor, very good and cheap armor for low level players. So this guide will focus on banded and how you can get high enough to make it.

STARTING OFF (SKILL 1-20)
First off, I suggest when starting off smithing you try and do it with a character that has really high INT or can do charisma buffs (Like enchanters and shamans) - high INT to learn skills faster, and charisma to get cheaper prices when buying and selling to the store. Enchanters would be the best for both INT and charisma reasons. Now back to raising your skill, you can either make metal bits (And you will need ALOT of them to raise your skill past 20) or sharpen rusty weapons, they must be sharp weapons though, not staves or maces. The easy way though, is to go to your guild and put 20 points into smithing, however you can’t train past that. Anyway to make metal bits you need to get two pieces of ore, separated, and NON-SUMMONED water, store bought water, its very important you don’t try to use summoned water.

PAST THE EASY STUFF (SKILL 21-40)
Once metal bits and sharpening weapons becomes trivial, its time to go to start the tedious phase and move on to more advanced stuff. NOTE: You will still need to make metal bits which is a bummer because it takes a long time and you don’t advance in skill while making them. At 21 you should start making scalers to about 40 when they become trivial. They require 1 flask of water, 1 metal bit, and 1 scaler mold that cost about 6 silver I believe. If you have high INT you should quickly raise your skill. Soon enough you should be at the point where scalers are trivial for you to make, then its time to move on to more costly and less successful skewers.

LAST STAGE BEFORE BANDED (SKILL 41-60)
To 60 you need to make skewers, you CAN make banded at 50 although I suggest you DONT TRY because you will more then likely fail in trying to do so. This is a very tedious process raising the skill now because making metal bits can take a long time. You will fail at making skewers, a lot, and it costs about 3gp per attempt, on the plus side you will really start making money soon with banded. To make skewers you need 1 metal bit, 1 non-summoned water, and a skewer mold. You can sell the skewers back to the store for a small amount and it will, in no way, be a profit. However skewers can be sold to gnomes who tinker as it is used to make some items. Just keep making skewers and bits and after a while you should finally get to 60 and can finally make some banded!

BANDED ARMOR BEGINNING (SKILL 61-80)
Through 60 I suggest you churn out banded gorgets and bracers, banded requires metal sheets, certain amounts for certain pieces, the lower items use 1, which cost about 1plat, those are gorgets, bracers, and boots. I suggest you make banded in that order, too. To make banded, you need the mold for that piece, a metal sheet (depending on the piece as well, for these 3 pieces and this skill you will need one). Also, the molds vary in size, there are medium, large, and small sizes. So I suggest you take orders for banded armor unless you have a lot of money and can make them in mass and for all sizes. Once you are up to about 80 you should move onto harder pieces.

ADVANCED SMITHING (SKILL 81-100)
Now you can move on to higher items, these will use 2 sheets as opposed to 1, the same formula though with the mold and 1 flask of water. Once again I suggest you make it in this order as your skill rises, Mask, sleeves, helm, mantle, and belts. The great thing about smithing is that it doesn't cost much as opposed to how much you can sell it for. Just keep churning out the armor and sell it off to players, there is always a demand for banded armor.

MASTER SMITHING (SKILL 100+)
Now you should be a master smith, however you are not guaranteed you will always make armor, there is always a chance of failure. Now you should make, in this order, leggings, tunics, and cloaks. All of these will require 3 sheets of metal with the same formula as before. The great thing about these items is you can sell the tunics and cloaks for an easy 20pp when they only cost half that, big profit! Now for the good part, Verant is supposed to implement field plate, which you can sell for 100pp+ an item! And high level players will just beg you to take their money, so like I said earlier, smithing is one of the most profitable skills implemented and will only get better, while the early stages are extremely tedious tremendous profits are your reward for hanging in there. That's it for the smithing guide, good luck!
