STEP 1 – STICK IN THERE, SMALL PRICE TO PAY
So you just bought EverQuest and are anxiously waiting to play this great game. Hold your breath, it will take a while before you can even start! If you haven’t installed it yet do that now. One of the first things I would do is run through the tutorial by going to the start menu then programs/EverQuest/tutorial, because it shows off the basics and you get used to movement in the game. This takes about 15 minutes, and then you are ready to try out the ongoing role-playing masterpiece that is EverQuest.

Now it’s time to launch EverQuest itself, which means you get to download all of the latest patches. This is very frustrating if you don’t have a fast connection, but hang in there! After what seems an eternity of waiting, you'll finally get to the main screen. Read through the legal disclaimer and hit "I Agree" to continue on. You’ll be shown 3 company logos and then the animated video that introduces this never-ending world. After this is done you’ll be taken to the main screen with selections as follows:

Connect
Account
Credits
Options
Exit

Now you’ll want to hit "Account" and then "New Account", which brings up the account registration screen. Fill out the form, hit next, and continue until you see "Sony Station Account". Simply type in a user id and password for your sony station account. This does not effect your name in EQ or your chat handle, so put in (for example) your first initial and your last name. This way you’re sure that your account name is unique. Click on finish and you’ll be taken to a new screen asking for your station name (user id) and password.

Now when you enter your username/pw a box will pop up asking you to subscribe. Hit "Now", fill out all the billing info, and then hit "Finish". Your account usually takes a few minutes to become active.

 

STEP 2 – LET THE ADDICTION BEGIN
This is where the game first gets you hooked; I’m talking about the many variations of characters at your disposal. You can either choose a character right off the spot or do a bit of research. I tend to research before spending several days on a character only to find out I don’t like it. Check out our website, read the stats, skills, and whatever else you find intriguing about the race/class of your choice. Note that the evil races, Dark Elf, Troll, and Ogre, will be killed on sight in the non-evil cities, so be careful!

Now that you have your character picked out it’s time to create it. Launch EverQuest and hit connect. Enter your station name and password, and then you’ll see a huge list of servers to play on. Sometimes if you’ve just downloaded a patch there will be a patch message or a voting question. If so, read the patch message and/or answer the voting question. Now, back to the servers. At this time all of the servers except a few are the same. Rallos Zek is a PvP server, which I’ll explain later, and Tallon Zek that is soon to be the first Race Wars server, but the rest are standard servers. Most people buy the game because they have friends who play it, so find out the server they play on and join in! Click on the server, then hit "Play EverQuest" which is located on the bottom right.

After a few seconds of waiting you’ll be on your character screen. Hit create character and a screen allowing you to pick your race/sex will pop up. Pick the race and class that you decided on. In the middle is a 3d view of what your character will look like after you enter the world. You can change his face, or leave it, your choice. Now on the right it shows your character’s attributes, including strength, agility, dexterity, etc. The class and race you chose decides which attribues you can edit. On the very top right it will show you how many points you can distribute among your character’s attributes. You can only add points to values in green, so add them wisely! The very last thing to do is to create your character name, bottom middle. Most of the time if you have a common handle you go by, the name will be taken. If you’re really bad on coming up with a nick, or aren’t too creative, then hit the random name generator until you see a name that suits you. Then hit next.

Now you’ll be shown a list several deities that depends on the race/class that you chose. Choose one that suits your needs (if you even have a choice). Once that’s done hit next. Now you’ll see a map of the world, with the starting cities. Most of the time you won’t have a choice where to start, but, for example, humans can either start in Qeynos or Freeport. Hit next, and you’ll be shown a screen telling you the difficulty level of your class/race/deity selection, and then you’re done. You’ll be brought back to the character screen and be able to see your character as an option. Click on your character, and hit enter world!

 

STEP 3 – NO GOING BACK NOW
You enter an unknown world, scared and lost and wondering what to do next. Don’t worry, that’s what this is all about! There are several ways you can start the game, and several things that seem important that you do not have to do. I’m going to walk through the basic steps which many players prefer to take.

First off, it is always a good idea to change your options around a bit. You can do this by clicking on "Options" on the top left. First change your attack key (using the "keyboard" option), because you may accidentally attack a friendly npc by pressing the default attack character (a). Other keys changes are up to you, just make sure to change keys to whatever configuration is easiest. You may also want to change your display and adjust your gamma so things are bit brighter. This choice varies on your race because some have better night vision then others. Explore the other options as you see fit.

Next I would like to talk about hot buttons which, in my opinion, make it much easier to play. They’re really easy to set up, and very effective. On the bottom left of your screen are 6 boxes and the number "1" with 2 arrows pointing opposite directions. If you click the arrow right of 1, it will change to 2, and 6 new boxes will pop up. You can go all the way to 6, with 6 different hot buttons in each section, making a grand total of 36 configurable hot buttons. Hot buttons are used to give quick access to actions and commands that are normally accessed through the menus at the bottom right of the screen. To make a hot button hold left click on the command button (the "sit" button for example) until it pops up and then let go. Move the button over an empty hot button slot and left click. You can now use that action by pressing the number key that corresponds with its slot. You can also change between hot button sets by holding shift and pressing 1 through 6. You can configure it any way you want, but I’m going to show you how I have it configured. My hot buttons for selection set 1 are: Personal, Sit, Walk, Camp, Invite, and Disband. Selection set 2 I use for combat keys; this varies according to your class. If you’re a warrior-based class, and have skills like Bash, Kick, or Slam, then you’ll want to configure those. On the right menu, click on the option for combat. It’ll list (depending on your class) Melee Attack, Range Attack, and four empty boxes. Now right click on one of the empty boxes. You will have a selection of items that you can choose, including Bind Wound or Kick, depending on your class. Pick one of those, and then move on to the next box and pick a different skill. It’s a good idea to make these hot buttons so you can easily use them while fighting. Hot buttons may seem a bit complicated at first, but it is really easy to get the hang of it.

Most people play in the transparent mode, where the world view fills the screen. This is my favorite type of playing style as well. Just hit f10 to get there. Then use f10 2 more times to get back. Really simple, and very effective.

 

STEP 4 – LAST STEP BEFORE FIGHTING
You’re probably wondering when you’ll actually be able to play the game, and the answer is now! If you click on "Personal" you’ll notice some food and water in your inventory as well as a weapon, a book, and a tattered note. Go ahead and equip your weapon by left clicking on it and dragging it over your character on the top right of your screen. Don’t worry about stopping to eat or drink, that will happen automatically. Now, lets take care of that tattered note.

Press and hold the right mouse button on the note. A window will pop up displaying the contents of the note and who you should take the note to. This person is your guild master. This is the best time to search for your guild hall in your starting town, but more then likely you will get lost. If you get frustrated type "/shout " and then your problem; this lets everyone in town hear what you say and more then likely someone will come to your assistance. Once you have found your guild master, go to your inventory, left click on that note, and give it to him/her. You’ll gain some experience as well as an item. Equip that item and train some skills. Right click the npc and train in the skills you’ll most likely use according to your class. Then click done, and we’re ready to deal with that book.

"To PK or not to PK, that is the question." PK (People Killer) separates you from the common masses, giving you the ability to kill other pker’s. This is a huge choice to make, and most people decide not to pk. This is also my choice; I tend to like to live a bit longer. If you do decide to be a pk, find the priest of discord in your city and hand him that book. If you do not decide to PK, then left click on the book, drag it to "destroy", and click. Yes, you want to destroy it, so of course press yes. Farewell book! Next up, fighting monsters.

The last thing to do before actually going to fight (if you’re a magic user) is to memorize your spells. Open up your spell book by pressing the button on the middle left of the menu. 2 scrolls will be in your inventory. Left click on one of those, drag it to one of the open boxes in your spell book, and left click again. You will begin to scribe the spell, and the gauge below it will tell you how close you are to being done. Once that is done, left click on the spell in the book and drag it to the spells section on the left. Drop it there by left clicking on one of the bubbles, and it will begin to be memorized. Do this to all your spells, and you’re ready to go.

 

STEP 5 - HOOKED
We’re at the end of this guide, and you’re ready to fend for your own. If you’re a magic user that has the ability to cast "buffs" (anything that makes you stronger by magic) then do that now. Usually there is an area around your city, if not in your city, with all sorts of weak creatures to fight. One thing you want to make sure to do is "con" the creature by selecting it and pressing "c". Make sure the con text is white or blue before you attempt to fight it. Also note that creatures of the same type aren't necessarily of the same strength! One giant rat may fall easily to your attack but it's neighbor may slay you handily, so be sure to con every creature before you attack it. Approach the creature and hit your attack key or cast your damage spell on it, and hope that you beat it. If you die, don’t worry; in your first 4 levels you don’t lose any experience. One thing you want to make sure of, and I can’t stress this enough, is that you always loot your own corpse! Always, if you die, go find your corpse and loot it. I also suggest staying near your city so you can run to the guards. They will kill any creature that comes near them, so running to the guards for help is always a good idea in insecure situations.

Once you’ve fought some creatures and your inventory is full, you will want to go sell your loot. Go to your city’s merchants (they’re all around) and sell the merchandise you have acquired through your slaying spree. Sell by right clicking on a merchant and then clicking on the item you wish to sell. The merchant will tell you the amount they’ll give you for the item. If you’re satisfied then press "sell". Buying works in the same way; you click on their items and then press "purchase".

Now you’re ready to go back out and fight for the glory of your class and race. Once you reach level 5 you may want to consider grouping up. Some people keep playing by themselves until they reach a higher level, but this choice is completely up to you.

From now on you’re on your own. I hope I’ve helped you get setup the best possible way. I’ll be adding more info to this guide in the near future to help you out. Until then, good luck, and bring pride upon your name!
