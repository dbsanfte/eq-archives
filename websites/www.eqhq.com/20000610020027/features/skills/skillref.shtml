A - D

Abjuration
One of the several skills related to the art of magic, abjuration deals with defensive magic, for example a spell that enables the character to take less damage from extreme cold.

Alchemy
Allows Shamans to concoct potions like Charming Deceit.
Side Tip - Requires: Medicine Bag

Alcohol Tolerance
The ability to handle alcohol.

Alteration
Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, alteration deals with changing things, whether it be illusion or an actual physical change. An example would be a spell that teleports something, or that enables a character to move faster, or forces him or her to move more slowly.

Apply Poison
A rogue only skill, if successfull it applys poison to the enemy.

Archery
In addition to the skills dealing with melee combat, range weapons are also prevalent in the world. The skill of archery deals with a characters efficacy in using bows, crossbows, and other similar devices.

Backstabbing
The Rogue has in his repertoire many unique skills, including the ability to strike at his opponent from behind, often dealing a great deal of damage in one blow.

Baking
A very nice trait to have, expecially for the people are tired of looking to buy food in new towns or just hate paying that 1 silver, 3 copper for a lof of bread.
Side Tip - Requires: Oven

Beg
Ability to try and beg for money from Vendors... most I've seen is 1 copper. It can also get you killed, so don't pester them to much.

Bind Wound
Allows you the ability to heal you or a fallen comrade who's health has gone under 50%. You can only be healed to 50%, so it's a waste of bandages past that point.
Side Tip - Requires: Bandages

Bash
Certain races and classes have the ability to knock an opponent down, thereby both disorienting him, lowering his defenses, and also interrupting the process of spellcasting.

Block
Negating an attack by deflecting the blow with one's body is a skill unique to the Monk, and blocking is one of several skills available to that class.

Brass Instruments
A unique skill to a Bard. Some of there spells can only be casted through this instrument.

Brewing
The art of brewing alcohol to sell or to get your companions drunk. Either way a fine skill to learn!
Side Tip - Requires: Still

Channeling
One of the several skills related to the art of magic, channeling allows the caster to decrease the amount of time necessary to cast a spell, regardless of its nature.

Common Tongue
Ability to speak and understand the Common Tongue.

Conjuration
Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, conjuration brings something from elsewhere into the world, whether they be elemental creatures or powerful clouds of energy.

Dark Speech
Ability to understand the evil languages in Norrath, including the special tongue of Dark Elf, Ogre, and Troll.

Defense
How to avoid getting damaged during combat.

Disarm
This skill allows the combatant to dislodge a wielded weapon from his opponents hands, knocking it to the ground, and forcing him, at least for the moment, to fight hand-to-hand.

Disarm Traps
One of the reasons why you always want a rogue in your party, to disarm these traps!

Divination
Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, spells of divination allow the caster to both detect things previously concealed, as well as to conceal things previously visible.

Dodge
One of the better ways to negate damage is to completely avoid the blow itself, and the skill of dodging allows the combatant to do just that.

Double Attack
A very nice skill usually attained at higher levels for warriors

Dragon Punch
Special Monk attack.

Dual Wield
Certain classes will be able to wield a weapon effectively in both hands, thereby increasing the number of attacks and thus the amount of damage dealt in a given amount of time.

E - I

Eagle Strike
Special Monk attack.

Elder Elvish
Ability to speak and comprehend the language of Elder Elvish.

Evocation
Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, evocation deals with things that go boom. Fireballs, force spells, etc. all fall under the auspices of this offensive skill.

Feign Death
A monk skill that allows a user to do what it says, feign there own death.

Fishing
The ability to catch fish and other objects such as daggers, and raggedy shoes. Not a good trait to make a lot of money in, but you can catch your food while your waiting for that boat that always seems to be late.
Side Tip - Requires: Fishing Pole and Bait

Fletching
The ability to craft fine bows and arrows, a great trait to learn and prosperous at that..
Side Tip - Requires: Fletching Kit

Flying Kick
Special Monk attack.

Foraging
Being at home in the wilderness has many benefits, including the ability to forage food. This skill has been known to save quite a few trips back to town when the supplies have inadvertently run out mid-quest.

Hand-to-Hand
Although this skill can be mastered by only the Monks, most every other class can at least throw the occasional punch. Besides, one never knows when one might be relieved of one's weapon, both during combat and at other times.

Hide
The ability to camoflauge yourself to an area... one drawback, you're not sure if it worked or not.

Instill Doubt
A rogue skill that comes in handy when fighting unsespecting prey. It brings fear to the enemy causing them to run

J - R

Jewel Craft
Great skill for someone that it's planning to make money without the usual combat. Look for this skill when you plan on wedding your beloved woman..
Side Tip - Requires: Jewelry Kit

Kick
Being able to dish out a bit more damage during combat is attractive to many of the warrior classes in the game, and kicking one's opponent in unpleasant places in between sword swings does just that.

Make Poison
A skill unique to the Rogue class... allows you to, you guessed it, make poison!

Meditate
Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, meditation allows the caster to memorize spells more quickly.

Mend Wounds
A Monk ability to heal one's self. If not performed properly this skill can actually cause damage to the target.

Offense
The overall ability to inflict damage upon an enemy during combat.

Old Erudian
Ability to speak and comprehend Old Erudian.

One-Hand Blunt
Ability to use a blunt object, a cracked staff for example,  that only requires one hand.

One-Hand Slashing
Ability to hit others with one-handed weapons, such as long swords and mino axes.

Parry
An ability to deflect blows from an enemy with your weapon.

Percussion
The ability of the Bard to use the drums, a few of their songs can only be played through this device.

Pick Lock
Oftentimes finding that precise key is just too time consuming or expensive, and the lock picking abilities of the Rogue class come in very handy.

Pick Pocket
PvP+ may attempt to pick the pocket of NPCs or PvP+ PCs. PvP- is limited to NPCs, but be careful, if caught this can lead to certain death.

Piercing
Ability to use piercing weapons, such as spears and daggers. Rogues love it because of backstabs.

Pottery
Care to make clay containers? Here's the skill you need to know.
Side Tip - Requires: Clay, Pottery Wheel and Kiln

Research
Permits the magic using classes to assemble spells from component pieces. Spell Research is a trade skill, similar in theory to tailoring and cooking. Specific items are inserted in to a container, combining to produce a new item.

Riposte
Special attack.

Round Kick
Special Monk attack.

S - Z

Safe Fall
Monks, being typically unencumbered by heavy armor and weapons, often work on their ability to fall great distances, yet still land on their feet, incurring minimal if any damage.

Sense Direction
Although compasses are known to exist, they are also typically rare and expensive. Certain races and classes, however, have the innate ability to determine their heading and direction. Then again, they are also often times wrong.

Singing
Bards (one can at least hope) have great singing voices. And the better they are at this art, the more effective their songs, which are themselves often magical in nature.

Slam
An ability that allows an user to slam there oppenents. Usually preferred by a larger class like a Barbarian, Troll or Ogre.

Smithing
A very popular skill that allows one to create items included the popular banded armor found on so many people. Good for making money, even though starting off you'll be spending a lot of money.
Side Tip - Requires: Ore and Forge

Sneak
Ability to sneak up on other players and NPCs unnoticed, a skill commonly used by a rogue. When used, walking pace is slowed to half of normal speed.

Specialization When a magic user attains a high enough level (usually quite high) this skill becomes available. It allows the user to "specialize" in the spellcasting skill of his or her choice. The added talent in the selected area allows the magic user to cast faster and with less mana usage.

Steal
Another art known only to the more shady of individuals, this Rogue-specific skill allows the miscreant to pilfer items from both players and NPCs. Their intended victim, however, will at times detect this activity, and will rarely appreciate the attempt.

Stringed Instruments
Certain Bard songs require certain instruments, and the better the minstrel is at playing Stringed Instruments, the more effective he or she will be at playing those songs.

Swimming
Many areas in the world of Norrath are covered by water, and one's ability to maneuver in that environment is directly related to one's skill level in swimming.

Taunt
A nice skill to learn, since it's a life saver to fellow comrades. It "taunts" the enemy so much that it gets furious with you, and it in turn attacks you instead of your comrade.

Throwing
Ability to use throwing weapons, like throwing daggers.

Tiger Claw
Special Monk attack.

Tinkering
Allows Gnomes to make mechanical items.
Side Tip - Requires: Toolbox

Thieves Cant
Secret thieves' language performed by using body language.

Track
Ability of the user to sense the presence of others in the area. Great skill for anyone looking for a particular creature.

Two-Handed Blunt
Ability to use two-handed blunt weapons, such as Great staffs..

Two-Handed Slashing Ability to use two-handed slashing weapons, including the awe inspiring executioners axe..

Wind Instruments
Certain Bard songs require certain instruments, and the better the minstrel is at playing Wind Instruments, the more effective he or she will be at playing those songs.
