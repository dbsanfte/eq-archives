Question for Gordon
A million dollar question? Aye, read on:

This question is for Abashi for $1,000,000 ...
If YOU were an average joe with an EQ account would YOU find this weekends outages acceptable ??

If my "acceptable" you mean that I wouldn't be upset, of course it wasn't "acceptable". However it is understandable. When things break we have to fix them even when it's really inconvenient. Something that went in with the patch was causing severe problems by increasing the amount of data that was being sent back and forth between the server and the client. This wasn't really apparent with the standard loads that we see on the test server. As the number of people on increased for the weekend, we realized that we had a serious problem. Leaving the bug in would have made the game unplayable for nearly the entire weekend for 80% of the subscribers. A few relatively short outages appeared to be the better course of action.

We apologize to everyone that was inconvenienced by the problem. We don't expect you all to "accept" these problems without getting upset, but we do ask that you understand. We don't take servers down for the heck of it and giggle as we see the "DIE VERANT" posts build up in the newsgroups and on the boards.

-Gordon

Group Damage
Abashi on group damage:

Kills are now awarded to the most *hits* no matter what damage that hit does. So an Ice Comet with 1100 damage get the same credit as a punch for 1.

That's not true. I'm not willing to rule out a possible bug in the code, as I have heard some strangeness such as a lone warrior stealing a kill from a group of six people, but it's generally working correctly. The kill goes to the group that did the most damage, which means that all group members' damage is added together. If anyone can come up with a way to reproduce the problems that I've heard off and on, our test team would definitely be interested.

-Gordon

Corpse Dragging
Abashi posted this:

Since you did not detail the "trust" issue for dragging, I can only assume that you thought that people with drag consent would take the corpse into the lava, some unreachable place, etc. While there is this risk, this is miniscule in risk and effect compared to having all of your items stolen.

Indeed. That was the basis of my comment.

Having your corpse dragged off to the lava pits is not catastrophic...a necro with summon corpse, or a high level friend or acquaintance to drag it out, are obvious alternatives. All that is lost is a relatively small amount of time...a few hours, at most.

These same alternatives can be employed without a change to the way /consent works.

I'd trust someone to /drag a lot sooner than I'd trust them to /consent. I'm willing to bet that the majority of players would agree (all you have to do is ask them). In fact, I'm willing to bet that many would rather have /drag rather than /consent....

I'm sure a lot of people would like to see this, however this change is much more difficult to make than it might seem. Given that the amount that would be gained is relatively small, we can't justify pulling programmers off of other more-pressing issues at this time.

That's not to say that it's totally off of the "would be nice" list, just that we have no plans to work on this in the foreseeable future.

-Gordon
