For those who don't know, I've retired from EQ, I being, Hideo...  I told the guild last week, but never really told anyone else.  Sorry friends outside the guild I never spoke to.  Been thinking about it awhile actually, I took a break over the winter, came back.  Game just isn't fun for me anymore, tedious if you ask me.  When a game becomes tedious every little flaw becomes more apparent and eventually every little thing, from resists to spells to minor bugs would make me want to quit.

For example, a green con froglok bok knight resisted my cascading darkness spell 3 times, that spell I got at 59th lvl, 300 mana per cast.  So I use my Blood Ember Gloves which cast engulfing darkness, and it wasn't resisted...  I submitted a /feedback and /bug with colorful expletives, so much that it elicited a response via email from Verant warning me on my language.  Great customer service...

Anyways, I still lurk around the various forums and wish my friends best of luck in game, and in life.  If you want you can email me at eqstew@hotmail.com keep in touch and junk.

On other stuff, Shadowbane looks very interesting although still in the beta phase.  These damn new games always seem to lose their publishers half way through development  Apparently, Wolf Pack the guys making shadowbane lost theirs... and people are beginning to call the game Progressbane... /shrug.  If they want to compete with EQ they better create something better, instead of an EQ clone with fancier graphics, and different names for the same classes.

Shadowbane will be mostly pvp though, and I know alot of people don't like that at all.  It looks like micro managing allied guilds is a very important factor in this game.  One major guild, will have a number of sub guilds to accomplish major goals, such as acquiring territory.  You can even take over cities, form a kingdom if you hold a number of cities for a period of time, even an empire.  From the F.A.Q. on their page it looks like they don't think one single guild will rise up and basically control everything...  um helloooo mcfly *knocks on head* take a look at EQ.

Horizons is another, although it looks like development is even slower then Shadowbane.  People seem to think Dark Age of Camelot will be released sooner.  Of those three, I think Shadowbane looks the most promising.  Whether or not they actually get released remains to be seen, that or if I want to get addicted to another online game /sigh.

Whichever game comes out next I hope they provide better customer service then Everquest.  If thats the case, they'll be getting my 10 bucks a month.
