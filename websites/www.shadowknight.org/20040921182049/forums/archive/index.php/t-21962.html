Skoggel
As I understand it, resist adjusts work like taking that much off of your resists. So a -100 spell that checks MR would sorta work against you as if you had 100 less MR.

This means if you had 100 MR, you'd save as if you had 0, and 0 to a resist means you're almost assuredly going to be hit by it, and hit hard.

I have tested with myself as the caster when I was testing for enchanter BotB, so let me make it clear that this is by no means necessarily accurate being a) long outdated and b) could fall under some different rules due to it being cast by a PC rather than NPC. However, those are just possibilities and not necessarily a real difference. So there's your disclaimer.

The rule of thumb I've found was 80ish would be about right to resist spells. CONSIDER ALSO that naturally, since I was casting spells on myself, that it was saving vs a white con spell... most ubermobs are considerably higher in level and thus you'd need more of a resist to balance that out. How much is beyond me.

In practice, you get all the resists you can, and worrying about it only comes into play in the case of a) running out of buff slots - maybe clicking off that talisman of the tribunal for something else is a good idea or b) resist gear, which in all likelyhood won't make a really statistically significant difference anyway unless you have a whole lot of resist gear.

Regardless, if you can wind up with something like positive 250-300 or so (just a guess from what feels right, don't take it as science) after resist adjust is taken in, you should be safe from the spell all or nearly all the time against standard boss mobs. It's a sliding scale from there on in. If you're fighting blues, maybe even being net 100 is enough.
