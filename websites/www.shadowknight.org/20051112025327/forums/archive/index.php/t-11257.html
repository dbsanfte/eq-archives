Originally posted by Method
Make Offer is about negotiating the price and selling the item, for cheaper than its worth. Not about ripping people off.
I think that, for a small number of people, that is how 'make an offer' is used. And that is how it should be used, as a way to start off a negotiation.

But what I found was that for all but a very few, 'make an offer' was said in the hopes that the offer was more than their intended price. If you lowball the price, they'll say they want to sell for XXXpp. Note that they wouldn't tell you to bugger off if you came in really low, nor would they start haggling if you were close. They'd immediately give you their exact price. In fact, I always felt that they would rarely 'start high' in the hopes of negotiating towards their intended sell price. They'd just tell you their intended price up front! This made it easier to get a good deal, because even if they wouldn't budge a lot on the price, you'd wind up paying less than they had expected to get.

There was one other particular benefit to the 'make offer' people. If you ran into one that was really willing to use it as a tool towards negotiating a price, you usually knew you had a smart trader on your hands. And they were always the most fun to deal with, since you could trade more than just items for pp, you could work out deals involving multiple items. Which was great for moving 'inventory' that didn't seem to be selling.
