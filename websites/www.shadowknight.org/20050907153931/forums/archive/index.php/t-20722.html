I think the main issue you're going to have with a three man LDoN team is that you're lacking in DPS. I've seen some rumors that mobs have fewer hit points when the dungeon spawns with a smaller group, but as yet they're sitting in my "unconfirmed" pile.

You could probably xp in those dungeons, but winning a slaughter or high collect might be a bit rough, as you're going to move much more slowly. On average, with a competent full group, I'm averaging around 45 minutes per adventure doing normal difficulty level 65 collects and slaughters. We almost always avoid assasinations and rescues, and typically try to avoid slaughters unless there is no other choice (slaughters take longer).

On an average night, I can finish two to three adventures before I log, after group formation time is added in. Scale the group back to three, bump up the time to completion to closer to 70 minutes (probably longer) and personally I'm looking at being able to finish one, maybe two if I stay on over my usual limit. If you don't play 5 nights a week, that's a snail's pace. Hell, if you do play 5 nights a week, that's a snail's pace.

Of course, forming the core of a group with those three is a great idea. I have a friend that I play with pretty constantly (65 Shaman), and we form the core of any group we put together around us. This tends to work fairly well, as we know each other well, know our play styles and our capabilities. I'm sitting at 101 successes and 13 failures right now, and the majority of the failures were times when he wasn't in the group. If you play up from the lower levels together (presuming you're all playing around the same amount of time), you'll find that you know what the other two are going to do without communicating, which is essential in tight/hairy situations.

3 man teams are possible, but DPS is definitely going to be a concern. I duo LDONs all the time with a fellow two boxer, and that works out well... normally we can finish adventures in about an hour. If at all possible, I'd try to get a fourth toon involved, preferably DPS.

I normally use SK, Shaman, Cleric, and Ranger all level 65.

SK tanks, shaman is utility and ghetto CC, Cleric is healer and DPSs with Yalp loaded, and ranger is dps.

Shaman is not good DPS at all during an LDON, but useful for buffs / debuffs. I could see it being worthwhile to swap out the shaman with an enchanter... if you can handle charmed mobs correctly, an enchanter can be good DPS.

The ranger could really be interchanged for any DPS with decent offtanking ability... A well equipped rogue can handle offtanking in LDON. The beastlord idea is a good one as well, as their pets make good offtanks/dps, and they can slow.

Cleric could technically be swapped out with a druid... If your equipped well enough the druid could DPS and heal. This MIGHT allow you to drop the 4th spot down to three.

Enough thinking outloud... Maybe a good three person group would be SK, BL, and Druid? I definitely reccomend adding a fourth toon if possible. I didn't catch if you would be three boxing or not.

As a side note, if you are doing this for experience, I'd have to warn against it... There are many better places to get experience other then LDON. The only good experience I ever received in LDON was when I was the lowest level member of the group. In these situations I'd get almost half a level whilst mid level.
