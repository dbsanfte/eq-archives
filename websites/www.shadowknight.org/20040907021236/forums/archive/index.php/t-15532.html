I think that most of the advantage in leveling the ogre over the SK is related to 2 things. 1) ogres have slightly lower exp penalty, and 2) the better armor of Fulvous gives an advantage.

For most of us that can't afford to buy full Fulvous armor for a twink SK (my SK is my main and has had to earn and buy almost everything on him himself, and he's still decked out pretty nice), you'll find that almost every class of SK is going to be approximately as easy to level with the exception of the xp penalty, which is a killer at higher levels (my cleric that I 2-box with is catching up with and will soon pass my iksar SK at the current rate).

At lower levels, the regen doesn't make a difference, but when you get to higher levels, it can be a huge difference. At level 51 is when regen first gets a boost. When non-regen classes are getting 2 points standing and 5 points sitting, trolls and iksar are getting 6 points standing and 12 sitting. At 56 we get another nice boost, and then finally at 59 we get another smaller boost so that while other races are doing 3 points standing and 6 points sitting, we are doing *12* points *standing*, and *18* points sitting. That means that if you are 59th level and you use Deathly Temptation because you managed to die and lost your KEI but don't want to break camp for it, you are only dropping 4 HP per tick standing to gain 6 points of mana. The other major advantage is that when you feign, you regen at your standing rate. Once in a while I go running into somewhere that I really shouldn't be going if I were very smart. I'll go charging in to check and see if Vindi is up, even though I'm completely KoS and all the see invis giants are up. (yeah, I like to have fun). I charge in, get whacked within an inch of my life and feign, and sometimes I get stuck somewhere under a see invis giant with 10% health. If you are a bit crazy like me and find yourself in a nasty spot like that, where one or two hits can kill you at 10%, then regen at 12 points per tick is enough that you arent sitting there forever trying to regain it.

No, at the pre-51 levels, regen doesn't really make a difference that anyone really cares about, but if you are planning on going all the way with it, the regen is awesome and in my opinion well worth the exp penalty.

One thing that no one has brought up yet though is the fattie crowded hall death factor. My first SK was an ogre, and although I enjoyed the higher stats and being unstunnable from the front (don't think anyone brought that up either), I died a number of times in Sol B, Lower Guk, or other dungeons where people would be blocking the hall or sitting or feigned outside a dungeon preventing you from getting in. One day outside Sol B, I gave up after 15 min of someone blocking me and being AFK and I had to go into Sol A and take the long way into Sol B. Many times as I was trying to get out of a dungeon and escape a train I would be blocked by people and die when feign was interrupted or there were tons of casters preventing a good feign. Trolls are the same size as ogres and have the same problems with size. In my opinion, this can be a very significant factor in choosing a race.

in the end game, it's not really going to matter which you choose between troll and iksar. the 40 or so points you gain in AC probably isn't noticable in a fight, and the extra stats you have as a troll probably won't be noticable in a fight either. If you have regen, that isn't going to be noticable in a fight, but when you need to feign and regen or use one of our scaled down lich spells it can be a blessing in the post 50 levels.

To sum up. As others have said, play what you think will be the most fun and what you can get into. I love playing iksar and although many like trolls, I couldn't play one. Ogres were fun to role play with, Iksar I have a hard time really playing the role, but I love the race and how they look. I think that the number one thing to look at when choosing a race is what race you think is the coolest, and the second thing to look at is if you want regen and nasty exp loss for troll and iksar, or higher HP and unstunnability from front from ogre and almost as nasty exp loss, and the third thing to look at is how much the large size issue is going to bother you.
