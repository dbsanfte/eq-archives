That logic always astounds me. It comes up every time someone asks the good ole "ID vs LR" question...

Avoidance doesn't make you avoid X% of attacks, and shielding doesn't make you take less damage on X% of attacks... Avoidance adds a number to your avoidance AC, shielding removes a percentage of the mob's Damage Bonus, lowering its max damage and DPS. (For the record: In the case of the AA's, they both reduce damage intake by a given [equal] percentage.)

Brael
I've always felt that avoiding a hit completely was better then being hit for less.

SK's avoid the least % of attacks of all melee classes (going to count stun as avoidence there since well, you do avoid the mob attacking, if you don't count stuns though Pal's equal us in stucktacular avoidence). While the same is true for any tank it's most true for us: You're going to be hit... alot. Would you rather get hit for quad 1500's and be missed 4/10 swings or make those hits not hurt and get hit for quad 1000's and be missed 3/10 swings? Not to mention that mitigation is less streaky than avoidence and taking more steady damage is much much much more important than taking less total damage. The more predictable your damage intake is the better you can be healed, taking a constant 500 dps is much better than taking 300-700 dps per mob. You have the same average dps intake either way but you're a better tank with more mitigation over avoidence.

Bottom line, take smaller hits not less. You'll do 1000x better. The few rare crazy high db low di mobs there are in the game (which are all raid mobs) you can just tank with an avoidence class.

Brael
You're not looking at the big picture. Let's take a 600/75 1 groupable named mob (675 min 2100 max) which if I remember right is pretty close to what Jhiru was in his original form. With high AC and atk debuffs you'll be taking about a DI6 as your average hit so 1050 average hit. Since it's not unreasonable to expect 10% shielding on a tank (any Qvic+ SK should be hitting 15% without trying if they've got gear, 10% is quite doable on a Time SK). You'll reduce that average hit to 990, this is a 5% decrease in damage intake which on it's own is good. What happens during a spike though since afterall it's not being 1 rounded that kills a tank (usually), it's a string of bad rounds that kills a tank. Shielding will reduce the impact of that worst case scenario because you simply can't be nailed as hard. If a mob has a low DB (Quarm, some GoD stuff, etc...) you're not going to get as much of an impact out of shielding. The more AC you have the more shieldings going to be helping you too since it's reducing that damage that you can't mitigate, which as the mobs average DI gets lower and lower makes up more and more of your damage intake.

Like everything in EQ its all incremental upgrades, 1% shielding isn't going to turn you into the worlds best tank but its a worthwhile increase when you consider the AC/HP the item adds to you as well.
