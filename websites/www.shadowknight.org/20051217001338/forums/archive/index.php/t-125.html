This is the guide that my guild goes by when hunting the AC, its all common sense stuff really, but I hope it helps..

~HERE LIES THE SECRETS TO THE DESERT OF SOUTHERN RO~

The first thing when hunting the Ancient Cyclops is to forget all the rumours and tales you have heard as to how to make him spawn. He is NOT triggered, he does NOT spawn only at night, and he is NOT linked to the mobs in the sunken tower (actually part of an Erudite cleric helm quest). Anyone who tells you otherwise has trouble with the concept of coincidence.

The Ancient Cyclops follows the normal EQ “named” procedure of being a rare spawn with a placeholder. What has caused such confusion is that he has roughly six spawn points, many placeholders and has only a 1% chance of spawning. However if you know his placeholders and concentrate on them, it greatly improves your chances.

The easiest way to categorise the mobs in Sro is as “commons” and “rares”. Many of the mobs that seem common in Nro/Oasis are in fact quite rare in Sro and these make up the majority of the placeholders.
The best tactic is therefore to know what your looking for and not waste time with anything else.


The most efficient way to hunt the Ancient Cyclops is with a team of five people. One member sets up on the hill by the gypsie camp, each of the others picks a corner, NE,NW,SE,SW…….only cover the sand area, the Cyclops never spawns on the grassland. Each member is responsible for scouring their area for “rares” and once killed call it in grp chat. Should one of the larger mobs, or the Cyclops himself spawn it is easy enough to call “AC in NE corner” and have everyone get to him quickly.


Be Warned: The Ancient Cyclops is a free for all mob and following Verants rules you cannot claim him. The first person (capable of killing him) that lands a blow has the right to the mob. Basically if you put in all the work to make him spawn, you need to be quick and organised to get to him first…people in zone WILL try to KS you. Five pairs of eyes and a few Harm Touches should give you the advantage. However, should you lose the Cyclops to someone else, do not engage in a /shout, /ooc slanging match. The GM’s will not help and all you will do is make yourself and your firends look foolish. Better to congratulate them on “finally getting their j-boots”…….most KS’rs are high level and will either get laughed at for not having j-boots already, or slated for farming it instead of giving it to those who need.

~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~

Rare Spawns of Southern Ro.

A Crypt Mummy.
Dune Tarantula.
An Asp.
A Giant Rattlesnake.
A Madman.
An Orc Raider (3 spawn).
A Dervish Cuthroat (single derv that wanders the desert, not fixed at camp).
A Sand Mummy.
A Young Ronin.
“Scrounge”. Named Jackal.
“Erg Bluntbruiser”. Named Orc.
Sandgiant Hussam.
Sand Giant.
Terrorantula.
Ancient Cyclops.


Concentrate on finding and killing the above. However avoid killing the desert madmen and desert tarantulas, there is a theory that they reset spawn timers and I don’t want to spend hours in Sro to verify this.

Final word of warning, the three things that will be against you are:

The idiots who will spam /shout about how to get the AC to spawn because their brother’s friend’s cousin once met a man at a bus stop who was given the secret by a talking dog….so it must be true and everyone else is wrong.

The KS’rs…...most are druids and so used to reading track lists that they’ve forgotten how to actually look for something with their own eyes……be quick and alert, most druids will get lost from one side of the zone to the other anyway.

The mind numbing pain of targeting snakes and other mobs amongst endless sand dunes. You have about 2 hours before the boredom sets in, another three before it becomes painfull, and then you just don’t care if it pops on your head. You need to stay reasonably alert and the boredom is your worst enemy.
