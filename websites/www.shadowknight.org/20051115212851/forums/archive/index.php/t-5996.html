At the moment, I see no reason to use 1H over 2H anywhere, unless you just don't have a good 2H.

I have absolutely no problem holding aggro with a 2H weapon and it does far more damage than 1H, even with bash. The extra AC and HP from 1H and shield is negated by the fact that one extra riposte (and you're going to have far more than one extra) will do more damage than you're going to gain in HP.

I parsed my Black Rock Maul of Crushing (23/23) and Shield of Fury combo up against my Primal Velium War Lance (45/44). I kept the Avatar proc up with both weapons, only recording fights where I had Avatar up the whole fight.

1H + shield bash came out to about 40-45dps, with bash accounting for 2.5-5dps of that. 2H came out to around 55-60dps with slam accounting for about 1-1.5dps of that.

Now, that said, this is at level 60 where you have the maximum 2H bonus. The results may vary slightly when dealing with 1H vs. 2H below level 50.

Also, if you compare difficulty of obtaining pre-NToV 1H and 2H weapons, the Lance of Thunder (52/46) or Shovel of the Harvest (50/43) will far outdamage the Eye of Rigtorn (25/25) or Black Rock Maul of Crushing (23/23).

Aye I agree kraz, a 2h always dominates a 1h in the end game. I am not familiar with mid ranged levels any more since i have been 60 for so long. Hasted with a facesmasher (48/42 with dmg bonus of 34, 25ac, 25str, 15sta, 100hp I deal about 65-80dps not including procs, lifetaps, or dots being used (and they did account for some of the time used) while my black rock maul of crushing (23/23) only deals about 40-45dps with the same specs. The reason i give the other stats is to show that even 2h's have ac built into them (some do rather) and they usually give a ton of hp. Tanking is actually better if you use an awesome 2h compared to an awesome 1h. The amount of damage dealt by them actually increases your aggro when compared to 1h. If you are a large race or have the aa ability to bash you can still bash while using a 2h. Since slam stuns just like bash does its still advantageous to use a 2h over a 1h in fear kiting if you are a large race. Another important thing to note is that when we cast spells we cant attack. With a 2h you have a longer delay between swings and can often cast an entire spell before the weapon is ready to swing again-therefore not losing any of your dps. With a 1h you lose 2-4 swings worth of damage when casting a spell-with a 2h you lose almost none. Casting spells does not slow down your weapon's delay and if you cast a spell thats 5 secs long then your 42 delay weapon will have popped and be waiting for the spell to go through upon which you will hit the mob right when the spell lands. Another thing to understand is a 2her's ability to cut through ac. For instance my 1h will hit for average of about 80 in sebilis, my 2h will hit for average of 200. When i go raid ssra nameds my average hit with a 1h is about 35-40 (almost half) and my 2h is still about 200. This puts 2h about twice as powerful from 1hers compared to what they were beforehand. Almost always a 2h is better than a 1h in the end game.
