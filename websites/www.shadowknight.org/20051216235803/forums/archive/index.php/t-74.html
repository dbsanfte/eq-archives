We had a decent thread going at the old site that included a couple of folks posting their /hitsmode configurations. I thought we might start something up here for people who haven’t taken the time to learn it or are looking for ideas on how to set up their boxes.

I have been searching for good /hitsmode information lately and found a nice guide at steelwarrior.org. I printed it off and sat down at The Outpost and figured out what’s what. I was pretty happy with the results.

First off, here’s the guide. It was posted by Kaladornus, so hats off.

For whatever reason, the board here wouldn’t show it as it was posted over there, so I’ve had to touch it up a bit. It was the < > I am certain.


/hitsmode consists of 9 values. me|my|other, m2 - m5, X, Y, lines, font, head, bg and width


me/my/other = you hitting something, you getting hit, and others' hits.

-This gives 3 "temporary" boxes where battle spam shows up instead of in the
chat window. If you don’t want one of the boxes, simply leave out the me, my or other line.



m2/m3/m4/m5 = level of detail given in the new hits boxes.

- m2 = Highest level of detail. "You punch a large bat for 2 points of
damage."

- m3 = Second highest level of detail. "Punch a large bat for 2"

- m4 = You get the following text for a 2 point of damage punch, "2". Just
the number.

- m5 = This can ONLY be used in reporting YOUR attacks. "Punch 2".

It works the same way for me, my, and other:

"You punch a large bat for 2 points of damage."
"Punch a large bat for 2"
"2"
"Punch 2"

"A large rat bites YOU for 1 point of damage"
"A large rat bites for 1"
"1"

"Guard Droolas slashes an orc centurion for 124 points of damage"
"Guard Droolas slashes an orc centurion for 124"
"124"



X and Y

Remember coordinates from math class? Well think way back. X=0 and Y=0
[origin = (0,0)] is the top left hand corner of your screen. Add to x and
the text box moves to the right. Add to y and the text box moves down.


lines

This is a value between 1 and 10. How many lines of text do you want to
appear in the new hits box? choose wisely


font

Font size. Yay! 0 to 3 I think...3 being the biggest...I THINK!

head

Displays a heading on the top of the hit box indicating which it is: you
hitting, getting hit, or others' hits. 0 and 1 turn it on or off.


bg

Toggles transparent polygons. Either the box will have no background or a
tinted background. 0 and 1 turn it on or off.


width

The width of the box. I dont think this affects anything other than the
width of the shaded area. It doesn't make text skip down to the next line or
anything.

Since this guide was written, Verant has added the ability to make the new windows persistent rather than them fading away when you aren't in combat. Because I don't know what Verant calls it, I'm calling it... tada! Persistance. You can toggle it by placing a 1 at the very end of each line. Adding the 1 makes the windows persistent. Leaving it empty will allow them to fade.

So, when I went about deciding how to set up my /hitsmode, I decided that I wanted to keep as much as possible in the lower quarter of the screen.

I play in 1024 resolution. This won’t really work in any other resolution, so you’ll have to fiddle with it on your own.

Me/my/others - I wanted to see my hits, my being hit and others hits

M2-M5 - I wanted names and dmg type for all three windows

X/Y - I wanted the three new windows to sit right on top of my chat window. Since my chat window wasn’t going to be as congested, I decided to shrink it down by a few lines.

Lines - I wanted to have at least 5 lines of text in my combat windows

Font - I wanted a readable font in the new windows. I was still not happy with the results, but couldn’t find a way to make the font larger.

Head - I did not want a header at the top of each window. It takes about 1 second to figure out which is which and once you know it, you know it forever. The headers are really silly if you ask me.

BG I wanted a background to help me see the information. Some people don’t like it. It’s the default /transparant polygon set up that you can toggle in game for your other windows.

width – I wanted to have My hits and My hits taken to be a smaller window. The reason being is that both of these windows will have only one name. That of the NPC hitting you/being hit by you. The Others window is the one where you’ll see “An orc punches Ikikikikibobob for 3 points of damage” I wanted more room for the longer lines that would come with a two name text line, so I made it bigger than the first two.

Persistance – Since the guide above was written, you can now add a 1 at the very end of the line to make the windows stay up all the time. I haven’t decided for certain if I want them or not. For now, they’re always up. It is definitely a help when you are setting this up to have the 1 at the end of the line because it helps you see what you’re doing.

So, what I came up with was

/hitsmode my m3 143 595 6 3 0 1 195 1
/hitsmode me m3 342 595 6 3 0 1 195 1
/hitsmode other m3 541 595 6 3 0 1 217 1

You can put it on a social hotkey if you like and then swap it around with other configurations while you are in game.

Now, I had to fit my other windows around that. I like things to be orderly, so I tried to find a set up that would keep everything in the lower portion of the screen. I had a little leftover room, so I built a little buffer around my hp box just for grins.
