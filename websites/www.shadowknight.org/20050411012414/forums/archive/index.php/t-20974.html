Nemya
Occasionally I use this technique to pull a mob from the crowd. I have used it a lot in Veksar, to break up the froggie camps in back, and in BoT.

I send the pet in and then spam him to back off, till the mob is far enough away from the other mobs. Then get a spear off on the mob. Spear spells, will usually draw the attention off the pet to you, even if they are resisted. If for some reason, the pet gets 2 mobs, FD, and let pet die.

In BoT its essential to get the aggro off the pet or you will loose, the pet. If you do loose the pet, hopefully the mob is far enough from the others, not to aggro them.

Once you get back to camp, try to park the pet, if you have that aa. He should not get aggro, but if you let him fight the mob that was pulled, he might and since he dont' have a lot of hps, will die.

Klaaz
You don't actually have to FD, thoguh it can be incredibly useful. Mages have the ability to pet pull just as well as SK's with the exception that they cannot feign if things go wrong. As long as the pet is alive, you are fine without feign, but if the pet dies you get the aggro. If this happens outside the aggro range of its friends you are fine, but if not they will all come after you as soon as your pet dies.

If you want to play it safe, you can just feign before sending in the pet. If you want to speed things up, just wait to feign if the whole room comes out to play.

Elkar
The problem with that is that you're snare will agro surrounding mobs, so you will have to stay FD until the others reset, by which time your pet will probably get eaten.

Pet pulling works because the mobs around don't agro a green con. YOU DO NOT HAVE TO FD TO PET PULL. You do, however, have to be out of assist range, if not FD'd, which can be fairly large. To make sure, I creep into the range that my pet will actually attack from (if you are too far away and give a pet attack command, the pet will ignore until in range)
