Vost
I have a 57 SK, but my main is a level 65 necromancer currently with 92aa.

With my necromancer, I solo camps in halls of honor almost as efficiently as some full groups do. I also solo some mobs that I have watched multiple groups wipe on, like Black Reavers in City of Mist. As someone else said, a necromancer is pretty much a self-sufficient class if played well. A large % of teh EQ population does not realize how useful necromancers can be in LDoN or other groups untill they get a good one in a group with them. We add some very good DPS on mobs that have large amount of HP, we also can be very good pullers, and have a variety of utility spells that can be helpful if not lifesaving in different situations.(like the ability to FD on a bad pull and rez the cleric back in on LDoN hard missions)

With a shadow knight it is possible to solo by fear kiting and meleeing. However a shadow knight does not have the mana regeneration to sustain this for more than 1 or *maybe* 2 kills before going OOM. A shadow knight fear kiting *might* be able to kill a given mob faster than a lone necro of same level could but the shadow knight is likely going to be sitting on his butt for teh next 10 minutes getting mana back to do another, while the necro keeps on killing. For groups though the shadow knight is far more in demand.

Just a word of caution though, some levels for a necro are a down right pain in the arse, the epic is almost as tough as the SK epic, some of the higher level spells rae extremely rare and very expensive when you can find them, and despite being very useful in groups the majority of the peopl that play EQ don't realize it. The worst part I think though is that after soloing almost exclusively from 1-65+93aas soloing gets down-right boring. The whole reason I made my shadow knight is that it is easier to get groups.

Just some food-for thought before you make your decision. If you do decide to go with a necro the best place for information will be http://necro.eqclasses.com
