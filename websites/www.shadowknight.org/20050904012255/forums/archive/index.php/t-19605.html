It's been discussed before, but mostly embedded in other posts. Damage Shields, so powerful in the early to mid-levels (especially when powerlevelling), diminishes in power as we level and the mobs we fight hit harder (and so the dmg shield does a smaller percentage of damage relatively) and mobs are usually slowed.

The majority of people I might talk to nowadays consider DS's to be useless or at least of dubious value at 65. I've always been a fan of damage shields in 4 years of playing EQ but perhaps I need to take another look at it.

Please keep in mind though, that I do not play in the high end of EQ and so I generally do not tank 1100+ hitters and such. On the other hand, I tank 99% of the time -- currently doing normal adventures pretty much exclusively. And JUST recently, we started doing normal adventures without a slower or crowd control (rather than doing other stuff till a shm/enc/bl/bard got online).

Okay, enough preamble. I generally have 48 pts of DS on (10 from Cloak of Luclin, 8 from 9th ring, and 40 from druid DS) at most times. And, that takes up a few buff slots. CoL I would keep up regardless of whether I liked DS's or not; Shield of the 8th I would keep up because it's not just an 8 pt DS -- it's also an insta-clicky for anti-dispell purposes; and so the lion's share of the DS comes from druid DS. If no druid/mage is in group, I even go to the trouble of loading up Banshee Aura, which then takes up a spell slot (due to it's short duration) in addition to a buff slot, that gives me 30 pts of DS.

Question: is it worth it? I think it is but it seems not many agree. I'd always preferred a DS over agi/str/dex buffs, etc. I'm looking at this now again because of the proposed GoD Intense Hatred AA line. If it truly does give us, at level 5, after 45 aa points, 30 points of innate DS; that means the majority of the time I will have a 78 pt DS with druid present or 60 pt DS self buffed. Or, if I choose to go with no DS because buff slots are tight, I will still have a 48 pt DS (CoL-10, Shield of 8th-8, innate-30).

Before anyone thinks I am completely whacko for wanting to blow 45 aa points on Intense Hatred (not to mention spend 30 on this right off the bat and first), I should mention that by the time GoD comes out, I will have ~500 aa points plus 30 banked.

Thanks in advance for input and discussion.

OK at lvl 65 my understanding is that we do something in the ball park of 100 DPS melee. I am using 100 even though I have heard 88-140 so I could be 40% low or 12% high.

With 100 DPS each % haste is 1 DPS.

If the mob averages 1 hit per tick ave (I am totally guessing here but again using easy number) then each 6pts DS=1% haste. It is very easy in a group to get to 30pts DS and at 65 it is possible to get up in the 100-120 range.

Realize that if my numbers above are right anyone who tells you that 120 DS is not very important is also telling you that the difference between 3k SCHW with 21% haste and 41% haste valued at 100k is small.

Even the difference between 36% and 41% items in bazaar are telling. So if you think you would rather have 41% haste over 36% haste then you probably want to have your ~30 pts DS up.

NOTE I am lvl 55 so I may be off on the numbers above for lvl 65 but if you 65 you should be able to tell if I off my a factor of 2 or not.

Also, the more mana my cleric saves, the more she's gonna be nuking, which is also better damage than a DS will do on your average slowed fight.

Anyway, I'm rambling, I think there may be a point in there somewhere, good luck finding it. :D
