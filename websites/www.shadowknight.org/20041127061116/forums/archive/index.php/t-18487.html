BrikBludgaard
-Endurance pool for hybrids is lower than melee pools, but not so miniscule as to be worthless.
-Hybrids get one "opportunity" ability to use endurance with, while melee have many.
(for instance, Shadowknight opportunity should be a melee damage or perhaps a mini-harmtouch.. paladin opportunity should be in line with their protective nature, and perhaps the ranger one is a "point-blank-shot".. beastlord should be a savage swipe fo soime sort)
-Hybrids disciplines are not only brought to the endurance pool for cost and recast, but they are retuned to actually be worth using. (Knight disciplines especially)


Considerations:
-Warriors will undoubtedly be doing more damage overall due to the newabilties. Shadowknights currently outdamage Warriors by about 10% overall sustainable due to spells... if the warriors close that gap, what advantage do Shadowknights hold? Especially since warriors are also being given "utility" and "taunt" with their opportunity attacks.
-Looks like warriors are getting a 5% bonus to mitigation off the top. Not a bad plan really... but perhaps the knights should get the 5% and the warriors 10%?
-Shields STILL need a looksee... warriors and knights should have more use for shields than they currently hold... many ideas for improving them have been discussed... pick one?


Now don't get me wrong, I am happy about the possible changes... as melee in general has needed a looksee for awhile.
However, the last time such a "balancing" was done, the spellcasters not only lost ground, but became useless except for buff pure melee, and the hybrids got double whammied into "log your knight so you can play this warrior" status.

Here's hopin...
