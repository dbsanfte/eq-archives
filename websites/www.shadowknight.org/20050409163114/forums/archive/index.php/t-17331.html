Air pet: Casts stun. Got good avoidance and mitigation, decent hp.

Water pet: Casts a cold DD. 54+ water pets backstab. Mages LOVE tanks that know this and cares enough to let the pet backstab. If a mage is using this pet, try being consistent in where you position mobs and which direction you are facing so the mage can park his pet where it will have access to the mob's back. Epic pet: Casts a 143 DD + stun, and procs 70ish DD - alot. In LDoN and other non-PoP zones this is the best dps pet. Due to the low level it attracts adds easily, and it can't really land anything on mobs in PoP. Nothing like the epic to mow down greenies tho...

Ulreik Fellreaver
I play a mage as well as an SK, and I can tell you without a doubt that the earth pet is not only useful for soloing.....marginally....its utility in a group with an SK is way sub-optimal.

Post lvl 49, Earth is handy for soloing, but air is often better.

Post lvl 49, Air is best for grouping, Water a close second.

In a group situation where you just want the maximum DPS no matter what, and there is no danger at all, and no chance the healer mana will ever get strained, then water wins.

In a group situation where conserving healer mana might be an issue, or if an add may need to be pet tanked until a mezzer gets it under control (to keep it from sprinting to the healer on the first heal), the air pet wins. Notice that this describes LDoN adventures almost to a "t".

Air and Earth do nearly identical DPS. Water does more, but needs to be positioned by the tank to the rear of the mob--most SK's are experts at this from their own experience with Jibober.

In general, the smart mages use air, and sometimes water in groups, and air, or earth to solo with (depending on situation). If you (an SK) are grouped with a mage who insists on earth pet, boot him.
