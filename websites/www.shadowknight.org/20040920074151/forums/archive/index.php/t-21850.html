i'll bring this up only because it was asked. i'm sure no one here really cares or will even agree with me, but i'm not one to just bail on a thread, even if i know what i say is going to be ignored or rejected.

i think the problem with class balance in general and sk's specificlly stem from a much deeper rooted problem then GoD or perceived 'class role' changes.
the problem is that, since the velious expansion, they've been systematiclly changing the focus of the game.
during the original expansion and through kunark, there was a pattern to gear. you had AC gear, you had HP gear, you had resist gear, and you had stat gear.
while of course there were many overlaps, usually you didn't get more then 2 or 3 of these on any single piece of gear. this held true up to mid-level velious.

take, for example, the 'top end' 1h weapons from original release through mid-level velious that were tradable.
the best 1h in the original expansion was 8/24, 75DD proc
the best 1h in kunark was 9/19 6str/sta 20hp
the best 1h in velious was 12/22, 7ac, 60DD neg15ac proc
luclin didn't really have any tradable 1h weapons that were much of an upgrade, they were just easier to get... luclin was when the 'raid oriented' idea that kicked off in upper-level velious really hit most of an expansion's worth of content and it changed the rules.
now that's just the tradable stuff. if you look at the top end raid content... from kunark to velious you jump from lammy and a couple VP weapons that were barely upgrades, to BoC's and the katana from ToV.
in the course of one expansion, there's this HUGE jump in equipment, where suddenly things are flooded with multi-stats, having everything you could need on one piece of equipment. compare for a moment the kunark class armor to the velious class armor, for example.

when luclin came out, they were still struggling with how far to raise the bar. as many of you will i'm sure recall, when velious came out we were told NOTHING would ever have more then 100hp on it, nor would any rogue dagger EVER have more then 15 damage.
when luclin was released, it's obvious that the bulk of the content was meant to be skipped by guilds who were at the top of velious. unlike kunark, where even the top end guilds had a lot to work with content wise (due mostly to the level cap increase and variety of new zones/equipment) and velious (which was difficult enough when it came out that even top end kunark people had a lot to work with in the lower areas) with luclin, the 'next level' of gear from ToV was limited to very few zones... like the deep, acrylia, ssra, VT (as far as a guild raid context is concerned, obviously other zones had some upgrades for certain people, but GE and AR didn't have a lot to offer a guild raid wise due to low number of named)

now... this is where the problems started. since the content was no longer progressing on an equipment-based scheme (since the equipment upgrades were becoming less and less of an increase over what was in the last expansion until ssra and vt) the developers seemed to up and switch gears.
before luclin, the content had been primarily tuned around the classes as they were, and you could just progress through it naturally with the equipment available in the new expansion (minus a few tweaks here and there that have been needed throughout... like druid/shm heal upgrades and ranger tanking)

with luclin though, the gear didn't progress like previous expansions. items were comparatively small upgrades when replacing previous expansion items (for example, BoC vs BF, or PaS vs sword off ssra and BBB)
however, the content (mob difficulty) still increased on a comparative level, becoming a hell of a lot harder then the last expansion's top end, but there was no gear to compensate for it.
this is when the developer's started tweaking classes. things like AA's and the beginnings of the massive class balance changes started tuning classes so they could handle the content already available, instead of the content being tuned to the classes already in existence. (consider that from top end kunark to top end velious, hp upgrades on a bp went from about 50 to 100... double the previous amount! and from velious to luclin, it went from 100 to 125, a merely 1/4th total upgrade)

since luclin, the classes have been systematiclly retuned every expansion to fit the content. it happened massively in PoP, the primary reason being mid-level PoP wasn't an upgrade over high-end luclin, and high end PoP was too difficult for the equipment that was available.
at this point, the classes started being upgraded so they could handle the content that had already been made. damage and mitigation tables got shifted, skill caps were raised, new aa's were put in. the classes were being tuned to the content that already existed, instead of content being made for what was already in the game.

this just got worse the further into PoP we went. as items got better and better, blowing away the previous expansion's gear, the mobs got insanely hard compared to the previous expansion, furthering the need for classes to be retuned in order to meet the content.

this progressive issue is now reaching a head in GoD. granting even the admission that GoD and OoW were supposed to be released close together and GoD content was meant to be done at level 70, the content is a lot harder then the classes/levels it supposedly made for.
and now, we see classes being retuned to match the content. wars got their upgrades... so did monks and rangers. them being retuned threw them out of balance with sk's and paladins, and now chances are good that we'll be retuned as well. then another expansion will come out, and my guess would be healers will get retuned to fit the content, or DPS classes.

it's a systemic problem. the constant trend of making content far and beyond the capabilities of the classes current in the game, and then retuning the classes so they fit the content, is going to continually throw the classes out of whack in relation to each other and require constant and nonstop tweaking of the classes.

thusly, i feel the problem we are now faced with is a content one. they're making mobs and scripts that are too difficult for the classes in the game, then beefing up the classes to meet the content. if this continues, it will never end... class balance will never be possible because classes will constantly be getting changed in relation to each other (as happened with warriors in relation to knights)

the issue at hand is, in the end, the same: knights can't handle content that wars can.
the root of the problem though, in my opinion, is the content... not the classes. when the issues about vxed/tipt first started coming out, i initially thought the problem was the content was too hard. same with ikk and beyond and the more serious problems going on there. i've never felt that sk's were lacking... simply that the content was made beyond what is possible.

the conclusion?
retuning content is the answer, not retuning classes. something may have to be done due to the fact the last war upgrade was, i admit, a creator of a tanking gap that is an issue and needs to be fixed. but i simply do not feel knights need a class revamp. the content is the real problem, and the systematic process of tuning that has been going on for the last 3 years
