Well first primals only drop off 7 named mobs in the zone that are on 1 week spawn times. MoTG, Prog, FA, 4 ancient dragons. Each name drops up to 2 primals, i think the entrance named (motg + prog) can also jew you and drop crappy caster books instead of primals rarely. The 4 ancient dragons drop 2 primals + 2 loots or so i think.

If the rest of your guild is as well geared as you and as skilled I imagine you could handle MoTG and Prog. MoTG is a vindi type fight with add's that spawn and run to assist the MoTG until he is killed or so. What i remember of this encounter you have MT war tank MOTG ch chain on him and have 2 off tanks tank adds and burn the add's down, multiple add's spawn im thinking it like 6 of them will spawn before you kill MoTG, i used to do this encounter with 18 members mostly 65 but mostly wearing bazaar gear. He's the first named and is like a good 1 min run to but to clear the mobs around him takes like a good 30mins i think. Wiped on him our first attempt i think.

Prog is behind MoTG, supposedly he is supposed to come with lots of little adds but all the times i did him a guild monk was able to single pull him. He's a vindi type fight never wiped on him. War mt tanks cheal rot does their job everyone else dps.

Now the other 5 mobs are in deep inside ST as such most guilds park a CoH mule their to coh the raid to them i think supposedly it takes like 2 hours or so to clear to the coh spot if you dont use a coh mule. As a small guild i dont imagine you guys having a coh mule for this so suggest you just stick to the first 2 named. Though most of the real primal farming comes from the dragons.

FA = identical to Prog fight cheal rot+mt+dps easy.

4 ancient dragons each have different Aoe's what i remember of them they were worse then Lendi but not as bad as Sonti. Excluding the 4th dragon who unresistable AOE slowed. Though due to the Aoe i dont think doing them with less then 18 is a good idea.

Resists were really high in ST i remember, like dragons i think most were DR/PR immune and i think also had high MR FR and CR to the point that non lures weren't very useful. I think the golems and garg's (fa,prog, motg and trash) had just high resists in general i remember casting AuraoHate on guildies rather then on the guards due to the resist rates for instance. Anyways hope this helps i honestly have some of the fondest raiding memories in ST.

If you really want to do MoTG with just 6 there is a lot easier way than dealing with the adds and him at the same time.

MoTG has 8 untargetable mobs that come alive during the fight of MoTG. Once he is pulled, every 30 seconds 2 will charge the raid, zone wide agro, that kind of thing. So MoTG, who doens't have zone wide agro, were to be kited for 30 seconds, and then the kiter happens to FD and MoTG goes back to his spawn point, 2 of his protectors are gone. Don't use darkness or dmg to pull for obvious reasons. After he is back at spawn point, go back to group and help kill the protectors. You will have to kite MoTG for longer times each time since each set of mobs is on the 30 second timer, so a min for the next set, then kite for 1.5 min for next set, then 2 min finally. It might be longer than 1.5 and 2, but i usually zone out while running around in circles. MoTG himself is resistant and when you finally pull him, keep dark/fd till it lands and then kite him around in circles till all the debuffs are in. We used to do him frequently with myself, and a war/wiz/chanter/shaman/cleric.

The way prog is pulled single is he has like 10 mini mobs that are right in front of him. Just agro the front mobs, and pull them down to the big room and have. another person pull Prog once the little ones are clear.e usually move into the room up the left ramp and opposite of progenitor. We added in a bard and another cleric to kill prog as he puts out more dmg. can't remember if he's not slowable is the reason or what. Only killed him once since we did the majority of the farming with just 2 people 3 boxing.

I would suggest only doing these guys as taking your casters through an entire ST raid will get you no casters at your next raid.

I would start with the MoTG and Progenitor if I were you. Once I could clear those easily then I would ponder how to get to the other mobs.

Master of the Guard (MoTG) use the FD method described. You raid has to be able to handle two mobs at once anyway, since the first doorway has two guards behind it. These aggro pretty much as you open the door (my suggestion stay FD if you form raid inside, some moron will aggro them). I had him double aggro me one 1 pull and he loosed 4 guards. If so, just flop down till it all resets.

Progenitor is an easier fight. His guards can be pacified. Then pull him and walk him in the little halls outside his room.

From that point, we had a necro + monk + SoS rogue pull a CoH mage (who was then necro rezzed) to above the Final Arbitor (FA). This was tricky and the necro died twice doing it. However, once there you then have 5 named mobs with just a couple of wanderers to clear.

FA is the same fight as the Progenitor.

The Ancients are straightforward fights. You can use the gate area right behind the FA to park them so that casters can avoid the AE. Read up on their AEs, some of them are quite nasty.

If you are just interested in Prismatics, I would just do the MoTG and Prog. The ancients drop a couple of interesting pieces (my back slot is an ST drop). One thing. Caster loot in ST sucks. They will be POed after a while about this and not want to go there.

We used to do this with 14 folks. The ancients were touchy fights with this number. The others not bad. As for a ton of HPs, these are not close to say VT mobs.
