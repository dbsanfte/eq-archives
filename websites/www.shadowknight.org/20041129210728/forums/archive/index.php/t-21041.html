THE MONKS
SoE developers and staff. First, let me thank you for taking the time to read this. There are a great deal of thoughts and concepts across the boards and in the past it has been mutually helpful to condense the thoughts into one document. In the light of past success, we again come to the table.

Quite a few of the players of the Monk class, while dedicated to the character felt that the Monk class has lost its distinction, and further, lost its role in EQ. In the past, monks were able to do a number of pure melee tasks. At present, many of us feel un-needed, looking for a niche to fill.

A must have, for group desirability for a pure melee is some form of utility. Formerly, monk’s utility manifested in beinga high end dps, a more effective puller than other classes and the ability to serve as a light tank. Neither of the latter situations definitively applies in the current game, bringing the monks only definition to be higher end melee damage. Improvements to all roles are necessary for monks to regain the utility and desirability they once had. In the current game, the monk suffers a doubled penalty of weight restriction and mitigation limit, further set against harder content and itemization.

1) Avoidance. (ed. blah blah theory to increase avoidance numbers - don't want to post this)
As an additional aid to our avoidance, defensive skill caps should be raised to the maximum, to reflect a monk’s mastery of melee combat.

2) Run speed.
Movement Increase % bonus = 3*(24-weight)

3) Rip-Riposte: This is a Dev generated idea that has a lot of merit, allowing a monk to effectively be immune from riposte attacks by automatically riposting the mob when riposted.

4) Turning: This latent skill would give the monk the ability to directly turn a mobs attack onto itself upon a rip-riposte.

5) Range attack. Itemization needs to be looked at, or alternatively, monks need an inherent ability to do decent damage under such conditions with available weapons

6) AC cap. It has been shown through substantial parsing that monks receive little to no benefit past 1340 ac. This cap needs to be eliminated.

7) More melee related endurance based abilities should be developed for the monk

8) Again, reflecting a monk’s mastery of melee combat, increasing the monk's special attack skill levels to max would add a small amount of base damage to the class.

ROGUES:

1. Endgame Rogues usually (if not always) cap their worn attack. Make more worn attack items droppable or available at the non-raiding level. I'd also propose a 'Vengeance' AA for AA-based ATK (maybe VengXX at max level)

2. Increase bonuses to minimum damage.

3. Raise the backstab skill cap.

4. Increase the quality of lower-tiered or droppable daggers.

5. Expand Rogue usefulness in non-damage roles. Shroud of Stealth and Escape were huge steps towards this. Abilities like 'Tendon Slice' (snare) or 'Sap' (lull) or some form of stun would make us all the more useful to groups

Summary
Problems:
1) Tankability: needs boost in GoD post Ikkinz/KT
2) sustained DPS is weak including spell damage
3) Hate needs boost
4) taps/self-procs are out of touch with expansions
5) SK utility needs boost

Solutions:
1) High-end tankability
- Add a Pal/SK upgrade to Harmshield/Divine Aura at level 65 that reduces incoming damage spikes somehow and lasts a reasonable amount of time, which you can cast through
- Mini-defensive (drops in GoD somewhere post KT/Ikkinz), 30% mitigation, reduces dps, decreases cast-haste. 3mins duration, 15 mins recycle OR no recycle, no duration - always useable as long as endurance
- mini-evasive, same as defensive in regards to duration and recycle, also reduces dps and cast haste
- Dodge disc, Dodge +10000%, reduces mob resists and AC, Duration 2-3 ticks, 4050 recast time
- up innate mitigation: can be AA or for all knights (akin to 5% warriors got, but not as high)
- mitigation recourse: spell which would allow you to "tap" mitigation from mobs

2) DPS
- Examine SK/Pal skillcaps to adjust for a minor melee DPS advance... Yeah, it can be an AA... allow it up to 235 or 240 for offense and weaponskills (Paladins should stay at 80% of a warrior, Shadowknights should be at 110% of a warrior casting spells and non-tanking)
- A resist modifier is needed on spears, they're low dps and equal to or less (depending on the mob/zone) in mana ratio than the damage only portion of lifetaps.

3) Hate/Aggro
- Our taunt spells could use a boost, nothing major as this isn't considered a serious balance issue but a slight upgrade is warranted nonetheless so our aggro can at least compare to Warriors again at the high end, maybe an aggro dot component on Terror lines and a much higher initial hate plus faster cast on the 1.5 cast time ones. Plus an adjustment to the aggro modifiers on Knights to be equal to the one Warriors/Monks (and others?) have of 1.4 instead of .82 when it comes to melee aggro. Another thing, BoC's shouldn't provide more aggro over time than Knight 1h weapons from Time, thats an itemization issue other than a class one though.
- Add a fear/53 effect to Terror line. (OR change terror line to become a beneficial spell that creates a fastcast, 18sec RUNE/1-shot high-end damage shield

4) Taps/Heals
- Self healing upgrades to be where they should be in comparison to other classes heals
- With GoD every class capable of healing themselves except SK's and Necros recieved a significant upgrade to compensate for the much much much higher DPS the new mobs would deal. Due to the nature of lifetaps they're at their most balanced on moderate DPS mobs. On low damage mobs they're too powerful and on high damage mobs they don't do enough. The only way under the current system to upgrade SK healing is to upgrade our DPS as well which I'm judging from our AA's and spells recieved in GoD is not intended. In order to bring our healing up to par we would have to gain literally hundreds of DPS in Lifetap upgrades which obviously isn't going to happen, what I propose for SK's/Nec's is to add for lack of a better term "efficency modifiers" to level 61+ lifetaps perhaps 15% to Level 61-63 taps and 30% to Level 64-65 taps. What this would do is take the damage dealt from the tap and multiply that by the modifier to determine the healing amount. To take my Level 65 lifetap (Touch of Innoruuk) for example it does 470 damage and I have 35% magic focus for it plus SCF3, lets say I roll 28% for the focus mod and get a critical nuke on this cast as well, it would do 1072 tap, under my suggestion it would do 1072 heal and 1394 heal, if the heal were to crit also (mind you this is rare, only 0.7% of all lifetaps do this with max AA's) on that roll it would have done 2788 heal. At minimum value with no focus it would be 470 damage 611 heal. This has the benefit of allowing Lifetaps to remain viable for both tapping classes in GoD and beyond. Also, seperate from this issue when we offtank or even tank the damage our Lifetaps do is not much of a concern for us and we cast them for heals mainly, when you consider the dps of everyone else hitting the mob this gain of damage suddenly ends up being VERY minor while the heal remains weak because it does some low damage.
- perhaps look at a way of allowing us to get some other self only heals as well on longer recycles (30 sec or something) that do no damage.
- make heal component from taps/self-buffs 1.X times base damage as the base. perhaps via a new spell or AAs

5) Utility
- Aura of Hate: The recycle/duration on this should be the same, this will help with lower end desireability. 8 tick duration/recycle would work best; AE effect and moving to bard buff box for Aura of Hate; make it stack with FA AND ferocity
- AE effect and moving to bard buff box for Aura of Darkness
- AE effect and variable mana cost for Zevfeer's Bite. By variable I mean make Zev's require a minimum of 120 mana or so (it's 200 currently), then make it's recourse AE, then make an additional recourse where an additional amount of mana is taken from the SK for each person the effect hit. If you're in a group of 6 people it would be 120 base, 100 for 5 people (besides yourself), so 200 total like now. If you're in a raid of 72 people and it hit everyone it would be 120 base, plus 71 additional people besides yourself or 1540 mana. Hefty yes but it keeps it from becoming unbalanced from insane mana ratios on the spell and you wouldn't usually be hitting that many people.
- our pets are too low level to proc lifetaps on mobs meaning damage shields shred them, they lack the hp to survive a single ae or riposte; The ability to trigger /shield from your pet; A level and hp increase for pets, leave overall DPS the same
- More AC debuffs for us to hit mobs with, perhaps add a debuff component to Aura of Pain or upgrade the AC debuff on another AC tap line of spells, these have the potential to add ALOT of indirect DPS from having an SK along.

Feel free to add to this etc. I think this has it all covered?
