I would advise getting the Royal frosted boots. If you have some friends, you guys can do the 2 sentries at the entry to Icewell. Those boots and gauntlets (very nice too) drop pretty regularly. The sentries are lvl 44, but fight more like a 47. Bring lots of melee. One group of 35-39 with a cleric can do one sentry.

When we did it, we had quite a few casters, and didn't know just how resistant those buggers are. Get damage shields and lots of healing. Also use Fire or poison based spells. You CAN do it at your level, but you'd probably need 2 groups to work the pair of them over.

You CAN split them but when they start to flee, it will probably make it to the other one (Even at my level, snares rarely stick). So you'll have to expect to do both of them in succession. The spawn time on them is (was) about 15-20 mins.

At low levels, I wouldn't suggest setting camp past the entry wall. If you are low/mid 40s, the best place to set up is on the ice to the right of the bridge (against the wall about midway between the entry ramparts and the keep itself). As an SK, you can aggro mobs from inside the fort, and go halfway down the keep bridge then jump onto the ice and FD. The mobs will path to either end of the bridge to get down to the ice. So you can get an FD off. Beware the sentinels though. Man they are tough as hell. I believe the higher leveled ones can summon.

The nice thing, is that those entry sentry dwarves drop alot of components and Velious spells. As well as alot of sellable weaponry. The loot actually is very good, and at your level, very manageable.

I can't tell you how satisfied I was to get my own pair. Even though it's assumed you scavenged them (which I don't really have a problem with personally). Still, I've had these boots for over 10 levels now, and the list of stuff I can replace it with in the near future is pretty thin. The resists on em are actually quite good for the slot too. Mine will probably stay in my resist pack for some time.

EDIT: You can also "pay" your way by offering to sell the items a raiding group pops for them. I tried that with a group doing sentinels/named once as a very young SK.
