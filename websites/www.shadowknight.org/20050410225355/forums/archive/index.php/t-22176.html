Groff Gorespiller
Your role depends on the make up of your raid, plain and simple.

Is everyone your level? Does everyone have the same gear levels? Do you have more or fewer knights than warriors? Does the encounter require special tactics (off tanking, special aggro concerns, need for kiting, need for higher mitigation/defensive disciplines, need for fearless)?

You're thinking in a group mentality, which is understandable, considering what I'll presume your normal game experience has been like up to now. However, this doesn't work in raids. You don't have a normal group; you have several groups working together, and some jobs you can double (or quadruple) up on, some you can't. Your raid only needs one Main Assist. Presuming that because you wear plate armor and you usually fill that role for your group isn't doing what's best for the raid, especially if there are special circumstances around which the raid strategy revolves.

In short, you don't decide what your raid role is, and neither do we. We know what we're capable of, but if you're going to be going on raids you need to learn early that the raid leader should be the one calling the shots, good or bad.

For Spiritseer Nadox:

You're going to either be a MT, or an Off-tank. His room is occupied by about 8-10 other troll mobs that for your level are going to have a ton of hp's and hit fairly hard. Once they're slowed, they're cake. Re-pop in the room is pretty fast, though, so you're going to need more than yourself handling them. I would suggest 3-4 off tanks dispatching them (this is based upon a guild with your similar levels).

For Nadox: Hits about 400's. For a lvl 50's guild, you're going to want some clerics on him. I can't remember his dot, but I believe it's MA only (person on top of the hate list). He'd be a pretty good test for a 50's guild, actually.

You don't need coffins for Nadox. Invis works everywhere, except in the undead area, and IVU works there. If you're going in through the Luggald Temple in Gunthak, you're almost to Nadox's room. If you've got a caster with improved invis, they can CR the whole damn raid. There's sort of a safe hall at the zone-in of Nadox from the temple.

One of the reasons I love this class so much is our able to serve many functions. We are much more than just tanks.

I serve as main puller, but often I assist our monk on difficult splits by tagging or pulling when he dies. If I am assisting, I also do reports on the status in camp for the monk.

Keeps the raid from dying as we work our way to the big guy (aggro again is the reason)

I serve as off tank or tank mez. Works great, since we get aggro so fast.

I serve as summoner. There are quite a few raid situations in which people need summoned as part of the raid.

I serve as kiter. Some raids require a class that can take more damage to kite for crowd control. Also helpful on a wipe to train the mobs away from the camp for faster recovery.

I serve as a spotter for break ins or after wipes. If I am not in another vital role that kills me easily I can fd at camp and let people know when things are clear.

I serve as extra damage. With the low resist life taps, fear resistance, feign death and finally a Harm Touch I can often be the last alive on a raid to put in that vital kill shot. Then I go kite adds or spot again.
