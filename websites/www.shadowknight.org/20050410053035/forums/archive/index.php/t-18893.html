Any number of people of pet/people can be hitting a mobs back, as long as the mobs hit box can fit that many people in the back arc of the mob. For xp groups hiting the back should be easy peasy.

Basically what I do for positioning.

Pull past pets so they attack back, most rogues are adpeted enough to get to the back of the mob themselves. I try not to corner pin a mob (IE having a mob back to a corner) unless I absolutely cannot get it out of there.

If there is a ranger in the group I ask if the have archery AAs. If they do, I try and position the mob away from walls, casue walled mobs cause arrows to poof. Generally this can be hard if there are 3+ pets (so much push and im so lazy =P).

In LDoN for instance, I hit the puller's assist with festering as soon as it's safe and back up enough, "pulling" the mob to me, to where I still have enough room at my back to allow for the eventual push. Basically "drag" the mob to me with agro and the pets all end up nicely positioned behind. If there isn't room for that, once I've got the mob and everyone's on it, just dragging it back a bit will get everyone else behind. (people love to send the pets in the instant they see the mob whether or not they've seen you cast yet)

Yes, BL pets are the worst, as well as their owners. Push is a big peeve of mine so I have no problem asking a BL to counter his pet's push by positioning himself opposite it. Helps a bit.


Once everyone's engaged, slight circlestrafe adjustments can help. If the mob's being heavily pushed into a corner or about to be walled a good pet owner will back his pet off if it's the blockage to a reposition. Unfortunately many pet owners don't pay attention to the signs of a tank trying to yank the mob out of a corner or off the wall.

When all is said and done, I do what's necessary to keep the backstabbing pets or PC's where they need to be in order to maximize DPS. Everyone else is secondary. In a stationary camp (not a dungeon crawl) I try to ensure the mob's engaged as close to the same spot each time, so the backstabbing pet owners can position their pet to be sent in properly.

Sometimes there's nothing you can do, people won't listen, and you just suck it up, grit your teeth, and do what you can.
