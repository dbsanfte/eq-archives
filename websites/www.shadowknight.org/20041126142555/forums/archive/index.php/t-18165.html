Just as an FYI, I remember when I hit lvl 15, I'd imagined that I would be Mr. Super Necro Melee; fear kiting with the pet while whacking on it's back. Rinse Repeat, rake in that xp!

Well... from what I recall, it didn't quite work out that way. I'd start off with Clinging Darkness and try to land a snare, then switch over to casting Fear. By the time I landed a Fear, CD would be wearing off (duration is pretty short), so I'd have to recast that, plus the snare component is very low at this level... so Jabober and the mob are generally out of melee range while you are casting (well, unless you are getting stunned and interrupted), especially if you hadn't had a chance to refresh CD yet. Combine that with Jabober's low level and the pathing changes for Fear, you may suddenly have an add(s).

So at the end of the fight, you are OOM, low health (unless you lifetapped for a whopping 18 hp per cast), pet usually dead (so there goes some more mana) and realize you could probably have melee'd a couple mobs in the time it took you to fear kite.

Heheh, don't get me wrong, fear kiting is great -- later on. It just didn't seem worthwhile at lvl 15 from what I remember.

At 15 forget about your weapon skills. They aren't a concern. Just level. Go to Padual Caverns and get to 25 in a couple days. At 15 come in from shadeweavers thicket side lots to kill there avoid fiends unless you have someone with you to cure disease or have natural regen. At 17/18 get in a bandit group from the Shadow Haven side and you will level to 25 in no time at all.

Up to 25/30 you can level quite fast and keeping all weapon skills up it hard. 40+ you level so much slower that maintaining (and gaining skills from 0) is so easy there really isn't a point to doing it earlier. I play a troll so skills go up about the slowest of any race and tanking 40+ the skill increases come fast.

So pick your best weapon go out there and level. Once you hit mid levels you can worry about swapping weapons out but honestly I never worked on a weapon skill till I got a new weapon I wanted to use. At higher levels especially when fear kiting, it may reduce damage a bit for a while but you skill up pretty fast. I recently skilled up from 200 to 235 in about 2 hours solo.

There really is no reason to raise a weapon skill, simply to raise it. IF you get a great weapon for that skill, then work on it.

At 65, my 2hb was in the 30s. I got a great 2hb weapon and started working on my skills. The higher level you are, the easier it will be to have high dex. As a result, I went from the 30's to max in about 7 hours of play time in normal groups.
