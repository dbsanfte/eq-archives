Rueeliar
Not quite about their low stuns. It's about the fact that three paladins can essentially prevent most level 65 or under mobs from dealing damage at all unless they are entirely immune to stuns unless they are monstrously resistant, and that a single paladin can keep a mob stunned by chaining two 4s stuns and a 10s stun.

Cast first 4s stun. 2.2 seconds later, spell gems refresh and second 4s stun is cast, landing approximately 3.5s after the first (1s casting time and latency delay). 2.2s later, gems refresh and cast the 10s stun. 2s casting time, assume 4.5s total since last stun was cast. We're now up to 8s since first stun was cast, and 4s left until first 4s stun refreshes, with 6s left on the mob being stunned from 10s stun. That means that by the time the 10s stun wears off, both 4s stuns will be up, and the mob will have had .5s of time unstunned (one combat round plus Bash/Kick). Assuming zero resists, the mob will get virtually no opportunity to deal damage at all. Realistically, it is fair to assume at least a fifty percent resist rate, which still leaves the mob's active time reduced dramatically.

That a better explanation?

Edit: That is theoretical ideal, unlikely to happen. HoS and/or Tash will help it along alot, though. In addition, it is the benefit of mitigating at exactly the same time as building aggro, while our spells that are considered to mitigate (taps) are low aggro, thus aggro must be built first before we can try to mitigate, leaving us at base mitigation when aggro needs to be solidified the most.
