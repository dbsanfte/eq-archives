I'm playing a troll SK and a halfling cleric on two computers, both always grouped. Halflings used to level the fastest and troll among the slowest. I got them both to level 52. The cleric has 9 AAs and the SK has 8 AAs. The SK was started when the cleric was already level 20. There does not seem to be much difference in xp at low levels (yeah, level 50 is still low level for me). Maybe I'll see a bigger difference in their leveling in the next phase of the leveling game.

As a final thought, don't base your choice of race on the amount of xp needed. What really matters is that you can play a race that you'll enjoy for a long time. I've played both a human SK and now a troll SK. If today I was given the opportunity to change my race I'd probably go back to a human SK. Yes I'd lose the regen and the ability to 2-hand bash. But I'd have an easier time moving around. And I would have the bonus, yes, that's right, the bonus of not having an innate vision modifier.

Most of the game is lit and in those few cases where it's pitch dark you can use deadeye or the other vision spell for infravision or ultravision. But most of the time you are fine and you can much better appreciate the graphics of the game. No, not spell effects but the sky, lands, cities, etc. Make a human and a dark elf shadow knight and go to Neriak and see the difference for yourself.

Situationally yes....when you pull and you are going to cast turning forces an updateof your your position with the server. So if you are running stop and turn you'll never get interrupted due to the server mistakenly thinking you are moving. My shaman always turns (usually 45 degrees oneway the turn back and 45 degrees the other) when casting even if he wasn't running. It seems he gets interrupted less but THAT part may be a placebo, I've seen no proof on it. However turning quickly after running then casting DOES help prevent interrupts
