The trouble with life taps is, whenever "equivalence" is attempted by the developers, it seems that something that does x damage is equivalent to something that does x/2 damage and liftaps for x/2.

Healing and damage output are not one to one conversions. They are apples and oranges.

The value of a point of healing factors in a)average mob damage and b)average pc hp.

The value of a point of damage factors in a)hp of the mob being faught, and b)average damage being done in other methods by the pc.

So there's no reason to link the recourse tap of a lifetap to the damage inflicted. I'd like to see spells which did a lot of damage and a little lifetap, as well as little damage and lots of lifetap. This 50/50 crap has gotten stale.


Alternatively, we COULD make them one to one conversions via coding: "any lifetap healing which brings the lifetapper over max hp is instead inflicted on the target as damage."
