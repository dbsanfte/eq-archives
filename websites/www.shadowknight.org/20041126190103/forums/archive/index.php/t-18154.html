I was going to originally post this in a couple more days, but I have been having trouble convincing most potential testers to assist me with this so in the hopes of sparking some interest I am posting this early even though it means everything isn't 100% accurate.

All tests done on Tarmok Beetle Harvesters in Fungus Grove.

Using some basic info found here (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=3789&perpage=15&display=&pagenumber=1) I decided to figure out SK melee aggro generation. If you don't feel like reading the thread I just linked what it shows is that each swing of a weapon (not to be confused with a round) provides a set amount of aggro regardless of the amount of damage done or as is often the case, not done. The rest of the information however is more or less irrelevant for us other than the formula used to determine hate/swing. Also, just for the sake of comparison I used the same mobs/spells in my testing however any could be used.

To start with I verified the initial testing with Terror spells.
The first thing to do was to confirm that Terrors do or do not work at range.
Test 1: Cast Terror of Darkness once, SK #2 stands 150 range away and casts Terror of Thule, takes 2 casts to pull aggro off me.
Test 2: Cast Terror of Darkness twice, SK #2 stands 150 range away and casts Terror of Thule, takes 2 casts to pull aggro off me.
Test 3: Disarm mob to initially get on the hate list and cast Terror of Darkness x2, SK #2 stands 150 range away and casts Terror of Thule, takes 2 casts to pull aggro off me.
Test 4: Cast Terror of Thule once, SK #2 stands 150 range away and casts Terror of Darkness, takes 2 casts to pull aggro off me.
Test 5: Disarm mob to initially get on the hate list and cast Terror of Thule, SK #2 stands 150 range away casts Terror of Darkness, takes 5 casts to pull aggro off me.
Test 6: Cast Terror of Thule twice, SK #2 stands 150 range away and casts Terror of Thule twice. Mob stays on me, run mob closer to other SK than to me and mob turns to the other SK. Repeat placing me as the closer SK and aggro back to me.

Conclusions: Terrors have a hate cap on them when used as your first method of aggro on a mob, in addition to this mobs's have a hate cap on their initial aggro which most likely increases as the mobs level increases. Higher level Terrors hit this hate cap when casted on lower level mobs, but lower level Terrors do not. Terrors work exactly as they're supposed to at range and are not coded to give low amounts of hate when outside of melee range.

Using the formula of Initial Aggro+Subsequent Aggro=Swings*X*Hate per spell, with X equaling the % of the spell being cast (in this case, % of 510) and substituting Initial Aggro for the value Y we have the following formulas for the three weapons.

FS Staff: Y+510=5610X and Y+1530=14790X
Sword: Y+510=4590X and Y+1530=11730X
Enchanted Staff: Y+510=4590X and Y+1530=12240X

Solving these we find that for the value for the FS Great Staff is .11, the Sword is .14, and the Enchanted Staff is .13. However, there is actually a range here with the values being .105-.1149, .135-.1449, and .125-.1349. This gives the weapons the following hate ranges

Fine Steel Great Staff (9/36 39 dmg bonus) 54-59 range
Sullied Two-Handed Sword (9/48 49 dmg bonus) 69-74 range
Enchanted Tier'Dal Staff (15/42 42 bonus) 64-69 range

Finding a formula from this I determined there the aggro formula is Dmg+Dmg Bonus*Mod, this mod is somewhere between 1.22 and 1.29, more weapons need tested in order to narrow this formula down further.

Hate and aggro are two separate things. The "Hate list" is a set of numbers that each mob has about each player. A successfull taunt brings the taunter to the highest number on this list, plus one.

An aggro check takes a number of things into consideration, of which the hate list is ONLY ONE OF MANY. Perhaps the most important one, but not the only one. Ok, the test has been performed, and my findings are this...

- I believe that aggro generation has nothing to do with the "delay" of the weapon. It has to do with the dam/delay ratio, and maybe a bit more (see later)
- Damage does not have to be dealt to generate aggro. Larath stunned a willowisp to get initial aggro, and after a set amount of swings with non-magic weapons (no damage to the mob), I was able to pull aggro off him.
- I now believe that there is a "hidden aggro bonus" of sorts, but see below...

It is apparent that weapon aggro is "predestined" to an extent before the fight starts. The actual damage that the weapon puts out in any given fight has nothing to do with the aggro that weapon generates. (of course, specific amounts of aggro are still generated from weapon procs.) It's almost as if SOE held the dam/delay ratio up on a pedistal and decided that no matter what the weapon hits for on any given swing (even on a miss), there will be XX aggro generated (based on that ratio).

ie; I am attacking with a rusty weapon, with a crap dam/delay ratio. Each swing (hit or miss) against a mob will generate 3 aggro points, let's say. Now, I will get the fine steel version of that weapon, which has a better dam/delay ratio. Each swing of that (again, hit or miss) will generate 5 aggro points. The damage the weapon does on each swing is insignificant. So, if a weapon hit for exactly 100 damage each time for 10 swings, it would have generated the same aggro had it missed every time.

That is a quite a revelation for me, since I was brought up believing that damage = aggro. I was told that over and over by warriors in uber guilds that I looked up to.

Here's the test results, with analysis. In each of these cases, I had Chelynn (druid) snare the mob twice and pull it to her, then let it beat on her while I attacked and tried to pull aggro. These mobs were the guards in WC. I had a lightstone in my offhand, and absolutely no buffs of any kind, save a "swift arms 1" tribute buff, which increases my worn weapon haste slightly. I was also wearing a Cloak of flames (36% haste). Every weapon I equipped in my pri hand had a damage bonus of 13, and no procs went off during the testing. The mob was kept equidistant from Chelynn and myself, thereby eliminating "proximity aggro". Keep in mind that the reason I sometimes get more swings in over time is due to double/triple attack &/or flurries going off. So in summary, my findings show that higher dam/delay ratios (with the damage bonus being added in) yield higher aggro generation per swing. I am more than welcome to go out anytime, with anyone, and re-perform these tests to verify these results.

Let's look at the results Vtull's buddy on the SK boards came up with a sec:

- a dagger (3/20) pulled aggro in 48 swings
- a blade (20/39) pulled aggro in 22 swings
- a hammer (14/24) pulled aggro in 28 swings

All this makes since, except for the blade. The hammer has a better dam/del ratio than the blade, and should have pulled aggro in fewer hits (I can gaurantee you it pulled aggro faster if you count elapsed time). Now, in the case of my shank, it pulled aggro just as fast or even slightly faster than my GBoS, so MAYBE there is a hidden aggro bonus there based on the dam value of the weapon, in addition to the aggro generation based on the weapon's dam/del ratio. What I mean, in easy to understand terms, is:

A 15/XX 1HS weapon will create slightly more aggro per swing than a 13/XX weapon. This aggro "adjustment" is added in before the dam/del ratio is calculated for the "base" aggro. So, we look at a 15/20 weapon. We award, say, 6 points of aggro per swing because it has a damage value of 15, then we add in 12 more points of aggro due to it's dam/del ratio of 0.750. Finally, we add in 3 points of aggro for the damage bonus. So, total, it had a "hidden aggro bonus" of 21, meaning it generates 21 points of aggro per swing, hit or miss. Now let's look at a 12/24 weapon. Let's say this one gets 4 points of aggro due to the damage value of 12, then we add in another 8 points of aggro due to the dam/del ratio of 0.500, and then the 3 points of aggro for the damage bonus. So we are left with an aggro generation of 15 points per swing for a 12/24 weapon. This WOULD explain why the shank generated slightly more aggro per swing than the GBoS, even though it had a slightly worse dam/del ratio. (Again, the numbers I picked for aggro values are arbitrary.)

Going back to the SK's findings, let's look closer at the blade and the hammer, to try to determine in a RL situation, which one would hold aggro better. Assume we are tanking mob "X" and are using the hammer. It will take, according to him, 28 swings to pull aggro off two terris casts. The hammer, with no haste buffs or haste items, will swing once every 2.4 seconds. So, in order to swing 28 times, it will take 67.2 seconds to pull aggro. Now, let's look at the blade. The blade will swing once every 3.9 seconds, and takes 22 swings to pull aggro. That would take 85.8 seconds. This means, that even though it takes the hammer MORE swings, just due to the fact that it is swinging faster, it will pull aggro faster and hold it better than the blade.

This is why the SK's findings are not worth memorizing blindly, if you are trying to find a weapon that is better than another at holding aggro. He neglected to include the time factor. However, it is nice to see that through his findings, there may be that other bonus to aggro based on higher damage values for weapons. Would be worth looking more into.
