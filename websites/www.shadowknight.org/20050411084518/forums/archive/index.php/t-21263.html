Gash
Get Twinge of Pain 1, which is available for Tribute, which is Affliction Haste (33%). When they pull that golem, spam Clinging Darkness. With the first Clinging Darkness (whether it sticks or not), you'll have comparable agro to the chanter, and the mob will go for whoever's closest so stand a bit closer to the mob than the chanter, or ask him/her to stay behind you. Immediately following, cast Festering; the 2 snares at the beginning of the fight will ensure way more agro than your previous methods. Using this method, you can tell your chanter to slow as soon as it's within range of him/her. This in turn (having the mob slowed at the very beginning) will help with the healing problem.

If you're getting a lot of resists on Festering, an alternative is to open with 2 casts of Clinging, then Aura of Darkness, (meanwhile your chanter tashes, or shaman malos), then Festering.

Then, do whatever you like for spells. Spears if you like, or a Touch of Volatis when you hit 62, but forget about Voice or Terrors, they're not worth it.

I haven't looked up those weapon/shield details, but the bottom line is: You're the tank. Staying alive is your top priority.

Hope that helps.

I two box those golems quite a lot with my sk/shaman. The reason you're losing aggro is that you are not tagging with either Clinging Darkness or Festering Darkness before the tash and slow attempt.

If both are coming from the chanter, then you really need to hit them with FD or CD before they reach the camp, otherwise, they're gunna pound the slower.

I know with my shammy, FD will out aggro malos and slow, which means he never gets hit. When a buddy chanter is along, he tash'es, and shammy slows...they never get hit either.

FD, CD are your aggro weapons, far over and above taunt and terrors. Hell, with either of those, there's rarely a need to use taunt unless you have a a group member that is doing incredible damage. Hell, when I pull, I just tag em with that, that way I have time to malos and slow before they get to the camp spot or within the first round or two of melee.

save the spear for later.
Keevel
The first and obvious part of the question about holding agro has been answered well. I use the spamming Clinging Darkness on incoming and it hold agro great. The only time I ever have hit taunt is when I ding to bring it up to max for that level otherwise I have no use for it and I see taunt as a bad thing because other people abuse it too much.

Think of taunt this way (even though it's oversimplified). A sucessful taunt will bring your hate equal to the level of the person with the highest previous hate +1. So yes you have agro now but by a slim margain, and if the person who used to be on top of the list is still doing something to piss off the mob you are not going to have agro for long. This makes taunt unreliable and inefficient. It's better to spam clinging on pull to get as much hate worked up as possible. That way your group have plenty of hate room to cast and beat up on the guy without you losing agro.

Spears are not efficient for agro. Terrors don't generate enough agro to be reliable and are proximity sensitive, so you have to be right on the mob to get the full effect. (think of terrors like casting on onld world dragons, you have to be in melee range to land or in this case to get full effectiveness out of it) Even then the terror line isn't the most reliable.

The other issue that I see is you dropping fast and dying often. This may be an issue of you not having as good of gear as you think. We will need a Magelo to determine that and the sucess rate of advice you get from here will improve if that can be looked at.

Dakkin
I'd suggest:

Hit the mob with festering at max range while it's inbound then follow it instantly with fear or clinging darkness.

If it's too close to you when you first catch sight of it, or unsnareable, use clinging then immediately follow it with fear.

Keep casting clinging at intervals through the fight.

When trying to prise mobs off aggroed casters, run up close to them, hit taunt and immediately follow with clinging.

Bottom line, if the slower sees the snared mob crawling toward you while you're spamming aggro spells on it before it arrives in camp, if he's remotely competent it'll be slowed before it reaches you. (Or you can ask him to do it.) Not having to take unslowed hits compensates for a lot of gear.
