A: They can let the warrior die/suck up cleric mana waiting the 8-10 seconds needed to establish enough aggro to tash/malo and then slow, praying for him to proc.

B: They can slow on inc, pray for the 50/50 success rate of taunt on blue mobs, and if it fails, the slower dies/sucks up cleric mana. Let's not even think about slow resists right now.

C: Boot the warrior, find a knight. This shouldn't be the preferred option. Any one of the three plate tanks should be able to stand at the front of a mob in a exp grind group and perform adequately, and have thier own unique advantages.

We are seeing the other side of mudflation here: to make things a challenge for the folks who are coming in with the last wave of gear, mobs must hit harder and faster. Beginning in Valor/Storms, mobs start to toe the line where the innate durability warriors have over knights is completely overshadowed by knight snap aggro.
