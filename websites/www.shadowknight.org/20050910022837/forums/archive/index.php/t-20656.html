Shadowrage Demonbound’s Guide to the Shadowknight

Shadow Knight’s are the second most powerful Tanks in the Game, and the DPS masters of the Plate classes. They employ a wide array of tactics to slaughter the masses. They are the hybrid of a warrior / necromancer, and as such are at the forefront of the battle. A wise Shadow Knight will be aware of his surroundings at all times. He is often chose to be either puller or tank, and is aware if the feces hits the fan he may need to resort to desperate measures. A well-placed spear of Disease, D.O.T, or Harm Touch may be just what the party needs. However, if party members start dying you may need to Feign Death quick. Always be able to accurately assess the situation.

First, we will start with character creation. When I first created Shadowrage I had several races to choose. At the high end of the game race does not matter, but in the lower levels (1- 50), it most certainly will. Gnomes level the fastest; although in my honest opinion as well as many others are the laughing stocks of the Shadow Knight community. Let us be honest, how fearsome can these cute little creatures be (LOL)? Dark Elf’s are second for leveling speed, and have the second highest INT score behind Erudite’s. They also start with the ability to hide to start out with, which comes in very handy. Humans are next, although they are blind as a bat, this is easily overcome and should matter none. I laugh at the people who think this matters. There are all too many really good items out there for humans to see just as well as the other races. Not to mention the spells we get at the higher levels that negate this disability. Humans also have decent starting statistics. Erudites are next, although they should stick with spell casting and leave the Shadow Knight business to us. This is just my opinion, if however my words do not sway you, feel proud that you have the highest starting INT of all the races. Ogres take an EXP hit although they start with ungodly strength and can not be stunned from the front. They are dumb as a rock though, and lets face it if you wanted to be a hack then you should have chosen a warrior. Ogres, in my honest opinion, never get the ability to cast as well as the other races, and until the higher levels face this problem continuously. Trolls and Iksar level the absolute slowest. Beware, if you choose either one be prepared to spend twice the time leveling as your other Shadow Knight counterparts. They do get regen though (useful when Feign Death’d, lying on the ground, you will actually be able to recover HP’s). Also at the higher levels, through AA’s, you can also add more regeneration power. You will no doubt be thankfull for this. Also one more note, Iksar have the ability to obtain the alternate EPIC “Greenmist, the Unholy Kukri of Rile” far easier than the other races, and can skip the long painstaking faction work involved to enter the city of Cabalis. With that being said Race truly only matters in your starting life, I recommend choosing the race you think looks cool and has a history you enjoy.

Choosing where to place your starting statistics should be easy. Put all of them into Stamina. This is the hardest stat to raise, and because you are going to need every ounce of HP’s that you can muster, I recommend putting them all into this. All of the other stats are easy to raise either through equipment or buffs. You will be spending your life in the forefront of the battle though, and Stamina will have a great impact on you survival. You have been warned.

Harm Touch(HT). Throughout my life as a Shadow Knight I have found limited resources containing how I should employ this spectacular ability and when. I have therefore several tips that may help anyone who is at that place where I once was. Harm Touch is an ability granted to the Shadow Knight from our Dark God. If I am soloing I keep Harm Touch reserved and ready. If, the enemy is all too hard (and is spankin my bottom like I was a redheaded stepchild) I will HT. Then often more than not they think twice before messing with me. Now in a group it becomes tricky. I will use HT every chance I can, if I get in one of those great groups where we are killing yellow and red mobs one after the other. I simply cast it just to help that much more. If however, I am grouped in an area that is dangerous for my level, or I am unsure of the abilities of the people I am playing with, I save it. I cannot count the times people have complimented how handy that skill must be to have, simply because I needed it to save the group from getting into trouble. More often than not though I look at it like this, If I have to rely on HT to save my behind that bad, then I must assess the situation to make sure that it don’t get too out of hand for the group. All in all, it is a great ability to have, but if you do not use it you might as well not have it. Let others see what you came to be a Shadow Knight for, and what you are.

PETS. Always cast a pet (unless, you are in a dungeon where if your pet wanders too far he may just bring back the entire dungeon) your pet is always useful to you. The Shadow Knight pet is only meant to provide that little bit of extra damage. Give him a weapon and a shield though, and his damage output increases extremely. I always carry several weapons and shields on me to give to my pet. Initially, I cast my pet, give him a weapon, and tell it to no taunt. Second, make sure that you instruct it to guard whatever place you have designated as camp. This is because our pet dies easily, even though he may put out decent damage. Therefore, if you have your pet guarding a particular spot, you have more of a choice as to what he attacks and when. Your pet will hit for maximum damage more often than not if you place him behind the enemy you are fighting also. This also, is accomplished very easily if you have already had your pet guarding. For the added damage he gives, you simply cannot afford not to cast him. Not to mention he will gladly sacrifice his life for yours in a pinch, while you make your strategic escape. =)

LVL’S 1-9. First, you must see the Shadow Knight guild master. This is easy since upon creation you start several feet from him. Secondly, we must earn our armor. Unless you already had good armor given to you by a friend, you will need to start questing for your armor. Every young Shadow Knight should make this quest his or her first goal, this is because despite what others may tell you it really is not difficult. NOTE: The calcified bones needed are rather rare. While you hunt, make sure you frequently let other players in the zone know you are looking for them. This will make finding them that much easier. I found that the skeleton camp by the East Commonlands zone had dropped them more often than any other place. If you get into trouble you can run back to the bridge (where several guards are just for your safety) or just zone out. If that was not convenient enough, there is a merchant by the bridge also. You could very easily stay here until all the bones are gathered. Another good place is by the Lavastorm zone line. To the left as you are about to enter the zone there is an area where you may find skele’s that drop calcified bones also. Watch out for this area though, as you will quickly be overrun by mobs. Make sure you hunt here in a group, before you take on this place solo. At level nine, you will have enough cash to buy your spells, and if you saved bone chips as I did, you will have enough bone chips to last you for 10-20 levels.

LVL’S 10-15. By now you probably have either all or most of you armor, and are ready for more. Head to the Planes of Knowledge (This can be done through the book in Nectulos Forest), and once there you will need only two buffs. I say this because with these two buffs alone you can level very fast. The first buff is Temperance(given by clerics), and often can be given free if you explain that you are a newbie. It does however sell for around 9 platinum usually due to the peridot that is consumed upon spellcasting. For the second buff, Regeneration, you will have to find a druid. Simply shout this, and more often than not, you will find one quickly. Regeneration wares off quicker than Temperance (which lasts for about 2 hours), so you may have to come back more than once. Now that you are buffed, head to the Field of Bone by using the Cabalis Stone. Once you are there start making your way to Kurn’s Tower. This zone yields awesome experience simply because of the added experience bonus. You can easily stay here until level 15 or 16. After that experience will start to slow down. Here you will have access to undead, an area in which we shine, as well as easy and quick access to the PoK.

LVL’S 16-25. Now that you have skyrocketed in levels you will need a good place to go next. Start making your way to the Nexus, by using the Nexus stone in the PoK, then head to the Shadow Haven zone. Once you are in Shadow Haven use your map to find the Paludal Caverns Zone. This will be your new home. You will find this is the best experience and loot in the game for this level. Everyone knows it too. The average drop here will easily sell for one to three Plat. on the average. Make sure you don’t try this zone solo though, your best bet is find a group and watch the levels fly by. There is however a mushroom in the passage as you first enter which is easily solo’d. Also beware as you look for a group here to watch for trains that players will pull by occasionally.

LVL’S 26-35. Now you may be ready to start your Darkforge Armor quests. Darkforge armor is definitely worth earning. Head to Cazic-Thule and start the quest. I will not go into detail about doing the armor here. If you find that you need information head to one of the numerous sites on the internet. After you have your armor and you need somewhere to hunt head to Overthere through the stone in PoK. Here you will have no trouble finding a group. Another good place is Marus Seru. First, go to the Nexus Zone and head to Netherbian Lair. Once in Netherbian Lair, head straight down the passage and make the first right at the Giant Pillar. You will run right into the zone. You will get decent experience, though you will not get rich to say the very least.
