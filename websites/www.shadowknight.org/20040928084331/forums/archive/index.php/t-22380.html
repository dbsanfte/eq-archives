Sounds like your system was overclocked all right...what happened was your bus speed was prolly tweaked (Pentium 4 rig, I'm willing to bet), and that bus speed barely worked, but passed your friend's testing due to the colder temps from that time of year....so now once its jsut a bit warmer...POOF.

Overclocking is a very hard thing to work with, due to the millins of variables, it's almost more art than science. Sure you can say "jsut do x and see if it works", but to get any decent results, you have to put in tons of research and careful part selsection.

Just find out what your CPU is, and make sure in the BIOS, it's either set to suto-detect cpu and bus speed, or force it to whatever teh proper ratings are.

Really, there is no point in overclocking anymore, since hardware is now so cheap. i remeber back when a good CPU could easily cost you a grand, and motherboards were about 400 and up, back then I did overclock, and was damn good at it. OCing was more of a thing about how costly hardware was back then, and finding a way to make it still reasonably usuable just that much longer.

Quick way to make sure that it is not overclocked:

Open your BIOS (usually the delete key when you are on the very first screen that does the memory check and drive display)

There is usually a 'Set to Fail Safe defaults' or 'Set to Optomize defaults' select one of those save and exit.

If you are having problems still, you may be screwed. Post as much information about your system as possible.

Is the CPU an AMD or Pentium? What speed?
What motherboard do you have?
How much and what kind of RAM?
Vid and audio card?
