Ashrai
Personal opinion here: I never have my self buffs memmed- just drop and mem 'em when they need refreshing. You get used to knowing when MC is going to drop and there's plenty of time to drop and mem real fast as you head out to pull or whatever. Use a miscellaneous slot so if you don't have time to switch it out before the fight you're not doing without something you need.

Festering is a mana hog if you're doing it more than once. CD should be fine- MC makes up for it easy.

Also- leave the nuking to the wizzies if it's draining your mana pool. I'd rather use the mana for a dot and be procc'ing or regenning back my mana while the dot ticks. I'd have BoH up instead of one of those spears for sure. I always grouped with as high a DPS makeup as possible so instead of 1 spear I'd rather be giving ATK to my rogue for instance.

Also, you're 63- have AoP up if you're tanking, I never step up to the plate without that tap.

If you don't have Aura yet, use Torrent of Pain. More AC helps you, less AC on them also helps the party. Throw down the cash and get Aura, it's very much worth it.

I only use Torrent of Fatigue when my str and sta aren't maxed from shammy/BL buffs. Load up Torrent of Hate instead. It's good for a nondamaging caster pulling tool and all melee classes will get a boost.

I leave up MC all the time when someone else is pulling and we always have a mob in camp. Especially in LDoN.

I'm not a big FD advocate unless I'm pulling. Most of the time I put Bond of Death in it's place for more healing power.

Astrakan
Spears. I use them occasionally, provided:

1.) I am not tanking.
2.) I only get resisted 1/3 times on average.

With those provisos, I think it works out that spears do more damage.

Ie, ToI is 350Mana 470damage.
ToV is ~250Mana 350damage.
SoD is ~250Mana 660damage.

(I can't remember my numbers precisely, sorry.)

Regardless, with those general numbers I think it works out
that it is pretty much a wash with damage if you are resisted 1/3 times, and saves mana by several 100.

Of course, it is rare to find stuff that is not resistant to disease anymore, so it really is only situational at 65.
