Okay, maybe there is another way to look at the roleplaying aspect of this. Who in the entertainment world (fiction) would be a shadow knight? I am not talking about actual spells and abilities, but whom do you think would portray the "ESSENCE" of being Shadow Knight?

I nominate the villian from the movie THE CROW, whose name escapes me at the moment.

Reasons: He is evil through and through with little regard to human life, but loyal to those that he befriends...as long as they don't cross him. He seeks power, control and wealth, but is not running around just killing anyone. If you stand in his way - well then, hey, you are fair game. He is not a coward - sending others to do his bidding. He just send others to do his fighting when the adversary is beneath him.

In EQ - I think a Shadow Knight would be an underworld figure. He would befriend, even help good races, in an effort to convince them that he is actually quite the nice fellow. Behind that friendly smile is a heart of ice however. By underworld, I mean that to the general public, he is a pillar of the community, nice to dogs, kisses babies, and donates to the church. But under the shroud of secrecy, a cloak of darkness - he works his evil ways. He is the chess master - and all else - even his closest allies are just pawns.

He would group in EQ with anyone, as they too are just pawns to further his wealth. He would be friendly, cordial even to them. Hatred does not mean stupidity. Hatred is not cutting off your nose to spite your face. It is working with others and twisting them to your own evil designs.

A Paladin would see someone in trouble and run to their aid. A Shadowknight would see someone in trouble and decide if that person was of any use to them. If not, he would sit and watch him die, just because watching him die would be well - for lack of better words - WAY COOL!

A Shadow Knight knows that he cannot make it in the world alone, but forming a bevy of Lackies would help his cause.

Morgana La Fay would be a Shadow Knight. Creating an entire army just to further her evil designs. Even to the point of mothering a child from her own enemy. Her desire for wealth, revenge and power would be all-encompassing. REVENGE is surpreme here.

In EQ Terms - no death to the shadow knight would be left unpunished. If a young SK accidently took on a guard, and died, (a blunder we have all done) a SK would not be stupid enough to kill the guard herself. She would however, hire someone to do the deed, and stand by and watch her revenge be extracted.

In EQ Terms - you have to live by Terms of Service. You can't train things to zone, etc. But a Shadow Knight would never do that. That is petty and small. Being Evil does not mean you are an idiot. Being evil means you know how to manipulate, and control others. They are, after all, only worthy as boot-lickers.

That to me is the goal of my Shadow Knight.

The way I see it is not unlike in "The Paladin of the Night" by Weis and Hickman. We, all evils, are a society of our own in which we thrive in happy communion. Our goal? To please our god and in the process, ourselves.

All outsiders are not to be trusted unless our god tells us otherwise. We defend our own to the death. Shadow Knights are the Hand of the god on earth, and we live by a code. We enforce his will, but do not betray our own. We spawn hate and fear where we tread, which makes our god stronger.

Oooh, roleplaying..

Although I'm too chatty to be *too* focused on roleplaying, I always envisioned Hrendra as more of a bitter loner than anything else.

To me, it's more a matter of what one values. Hrendra values strength over weakness; she admires strength, independence, and order. She is contemptuous of the weak, the sniveling, and those who cannot defend themselves *or* lack the impetus to try. Who are those that deserve to live? Those who earn it--and that garners her respect.

She's loyal to those who have proven their strength in battle with her; regardless of philosophical beliefs, they've earned their existence, she feels.

Yet, at the same time, one of my favorite moments was when I was helping a friend (hey, Taelnia!) with her Soulfiend Lance quest, as much as I could, in Stonebrunt. The spirit of Ridossan the Unliving manifested, and ran towards the Kejekan city--only to run into myself and a handful of my guildmates there, a motley collection of rangers and druids. Well, Ridossan apparently didn't want vengeance on the Kejekans as much as he wanted to punish my guildmates, and proceeded to drop one of them like a fly and chase the others to the zone line.

I'd warned them to clear out of the area..and they didn't..and there was no way Hrendra was going to interfere with the decision of one of her Shadow Knight idols. :) Instead, she merely watched them die...

My guildmates understood, and we all had a good laugh about it afterwards, but at that moment, I was possibly closer to being evil than I'd ever been :)

Paineel isn't based on chaotic "kill everything and be an ass"..to me, it's based on law and communal desire to explore knowledge at any cost--and over the centuries, it's become a subtle current throughout the society that those who are unwilling to embrace the darker side are fooling themselves, and are indeed lesser..

and weak.
