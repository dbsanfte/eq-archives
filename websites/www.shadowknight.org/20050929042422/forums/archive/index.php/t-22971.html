Only thing with the /autofire command is that it does NOT auto-face the ranger in the direction of the mob, which means you have to kite in his ~180 degree point of view. the 3rd-party programs (I assume) do auto-face the ranger bot giving you more freedom of movement, as long as you don't run the mob right past the ranger:

(Inside rng point of view)
Hit by non-melee
Hit by non-melee
Target is too close to use a ranged weapon
Target is too close to use a ranged weapon
Target is too close to use a ranged weapon
(Outside PoV)
Cannot see target

Basically since the rng isn't firing while the mob is inside bow range, he won't turn and if the mob then leaves his PoV you'll lose dps until you get him back in PoV.
Hope that helps some.
