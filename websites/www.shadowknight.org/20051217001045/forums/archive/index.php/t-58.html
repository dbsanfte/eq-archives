Fire Giants in SOLB- These take forever to kill, and even then its still a close fight (you need to toe to toe most of them) and Skarlon is just too hard hitting to solo. The rest are all doable if you have a LOT of mana and are very careful. The Wizards are easy because they waste time nuking (chuckle).

Amy Knights (and other mobs) in Fear- Fear kitable and an outdoor zone means easy solo, provided your mana holds out. This is a case where the crappy pathing in Fear works to your advantage. I did this while my guild was CRing from a bad Temple pull.

Horses in POG- Getting bored waiting for a CR after an attempt on Ali went south, I fear kited one of the white horses for amusement. This was extremely easy (I dont think POG mobs have as many HP as Fear mobs on average)

Krups in Sebilis- If you pull with Dispell to knock down their haste and SoW, these guys are doable (but not the most efficient solo target), especially the wizards. Basically, Dot the snot out of it and then chain tap while your DS, DoTs, and mele wittle em down. If you screw up and lose health too fast, fall back on Leechtouch and HT to pull your ass out of the fire.

For the Record, Yeldamra can be soloed in WW and it was done by an SK on test server. Hes level 52, has no AE, Snareable, Fearable, and has the weakest mele and HP of any WW dragon. He actually drops some ok stuff too. If your mana pool can hold out and keep him feared, hes a pushover, provided you dont get any adds.

PS- My gear, while good, is not what I would term "uber" for a level 60 SK (Full SS, Soul Defiler/Relkos, COF) and I would expect the real big guns to be able to solo harder mobs at less risk, especially with the advent of focus items and AA points. The lynchpin to soloing is manapool, however. IF you run out of mana before the mob dies, then you lose.

Ive seen plenty of SKs with superior gear to myself who suck at soloing. There are a lot of subtleties to even fear kiting that escape a lot of people, due to laziness, inexperience, or general incompetance. Good example is do you wait for Cascading Darkness to wear off before recasting or do you have it timed so you resnare just as its about to run out? Do you study the pathing and move your mob to a safe location to kite it? The seasoned soloer takes these for granted but a lot of up and commers to the class don't consider these things.

But I will agree soloing a mob toe to toe is a lot more gear dependant than skill dependant.
