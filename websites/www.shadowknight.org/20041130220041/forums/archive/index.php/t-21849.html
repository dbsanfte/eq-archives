At the suggestion of others, I'll go with something more concise for now. Still a work in progress, though I'm getting close to finished with this stuff... suggestions/comments/etc. welcome. As always, please cross-link any re-posts.


There are currently two tanking gaps between the three tank classes. One gap between Shadowknights and Paladins, and another between Warriors and Knights in general. These issues need to be addressed, but cannot be treated entirely independently from one another.

- Link the paladin Force of XXX line of stuns so that only one can be used at a time.

- Replace the paladin Quellious' Word line (10.0 sec stuns) with a 0.0 sec stun with a high resist mod and low cast/recast time and a moderate mana drain. (Spells would fill paladin role of interrupting casters.)

- Add a “Recourse Box” – a buff window specifically for recourse effects.

- Give shadowknights a 25% accuracy debuff with high aggro. 18 sec duration, 12 sec recast, 1 sec cast, -55 resist check, equivalent restrictions to paladin stuns.

Alternatively, give shadowknights a 0.0 second stun with a 25% avoidance recourse. 24 second duration, 20 second recast, 1 second cast, -55 resist check. It should have a max level paralleling the equivalent paladin Force of … stun and a minimum level low enough to not be a hindrance, but high enough to prevent use on Eye of Zomm and random low-level mobs.

- Give knights two disciplines to return them to their offtanking role.

--- Melee immunity discipline: shield block (thus shield must be equipped), 18 second duration, 30 minute reuse.

--- DI-mitigation discipline: 30-35% DI-mitigation, 72 second duration, 4.5 minute reuse, shield required.



Paladin stuns are at the root of a lot of the current class balance problems for the tank classes. They are extremely powerful against blue trash mobs and the like, are entirely useless against immune mobs, and address only part of the problem with others (incoming DPS vs. being one-rounded). The above changes would reduce paladin reliance on stuns and make their impact more consistent. Currently, the impact of stuns varies greatly from mob to mob depending on level, immunity, resistance, etc. Paladins would be compensated for the lost stun power with the much-needed tanking disciplines.

Shadowknights would be brought into line with paladins through the avoidance buff/accuracy debuff, which would effectively parallel paladin stuns. These abilities, together with the rapid-fire mitigation discipline, would cement knights in their traditional offtanking roles while having little impact on the traditional warrior role of raid main tanking. All three tank classes would be viable for single group content.
