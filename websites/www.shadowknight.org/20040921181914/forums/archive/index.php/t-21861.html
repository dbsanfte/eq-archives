Backflagging is a priority, however we have not decided on how best to impliment a system. So it currently stands at an "in discussion" phase.


Make raid flags available in three ways:

1) The normal way - beating the event, etc.

2) Be a current member of a guild that has X members flagged (possibly a %...) and has completed the flag event X times.

3) Receive the flag via "naturalization," so to speak. Have the flag via membership to a guild for X time and spend X time in the flagged zone and receive the individual flag.

This would remove the backflagging strains on guilds, but without completely trivializing the importance of flag mobs. You wouldn't just kill the mob once and have your guild tag flagged and never worry about it, like some have suggested - you'd still have to do the event a few times. If a guild died off, they wouldn't have continual collective access to flag zones for new members. Members who leave a guild would not loose access to zones they've gained access to, either by actually beating the event or by simply having been associated with the flag long enough.
