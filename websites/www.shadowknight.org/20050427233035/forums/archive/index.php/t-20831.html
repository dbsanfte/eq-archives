Inferna
Do the newbie armor quests. Save your silks and sell them for money for gear.

Its a lot easier for new players to get geared up these days then it was when I started.

The one bad thing is that their is very few real newbies around to group with. Really there is not a lot of low levels in the game now so it is harder to find groups. Lucky that sk's can solo a lot during lower levels.

Go for it.

At the same time you might want to consider a class that has less gear dependence. Any class that tanks is highly dependent on gear but many casters can function very well with basic gear.

Dunno about the gnomes though, they don't look like SK's to me but play what you like.
Keevel
Race is fine - all races can be fun just play

Save silks AND pelts for selling in bazaar. The low Quality pelts sell quick for padding and HQ pelts still sell nice to tailors too.

There is alot better gear out there than what you were used to, yes alot of the uber stuff will be well out of your price range for a long time, but alot of very serviceable stuff is quite cheap.

Save some money and some player made armor (gothic, sandblasted, frosted, etc.) will service you well into your 40's and generally go for just a couple hundred per piece. Yes that sounds like alot now, but you'll get it quicker than you think. Until then newbie quest armor will serve you nicely.
Chayde
I recently changed servers. (Well not really, just started some newbies on another server) It was weird starting out with nothing, again. But like Keevel said, pelts and silks, they are a newbies best friend. In one day I got enough pelts and silks to make about 600 plat, to gear my new character. On most servers silks go between 5 and 15 plat. That is about 100 a stack if you go for the quick sell.

low medium and high quality pelts go for about 10pp each. Also bone chips sell very quickly (not for much, but a stack can get you about 10pp) Zombie skins go for 3-5 plat each. Just level like any newbie, and save the stuff you collected, by the end of the night you can have a few hundered plat. (If you are willing to sit and sell in the bazzar for a few hours that is.)
