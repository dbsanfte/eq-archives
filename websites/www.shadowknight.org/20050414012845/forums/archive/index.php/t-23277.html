Trying to remember, it's been like 3 years here so i'll do what I can.

Info about the main burrowerito: Doubles? for 1100, not that high of attack -- nothing like AOW, and actually my guild when we were doing this event (and when I was in it) killed burrower way before AoW. The main PITA of this event besides the adds is THE HIT BOX ON THE BURROWER IS DASDUIOASUDIOSAD RIDICULOUS. Seriously. The worst part about it is the hitbox. It constantly moves around from push, and the hitbox is the size of a firebeetle even though the mob itself is huge. Hitpoints were not bad actually though no estimate.

The adds though, I can help you there. We never killed them. We tried, and it was so much of a pain we said screw it. At the time I was a monk, and here is what I did - I kited. Everyone goes back to where the burrower spawns (the cave to the left as you head in the room) and the monk runs in circles gathering the adds until they have a train of 381023813203 shitlings behind. The monk (or monks because I can guarentee the first will get tired of it and kill themselves) does this for approx 45 minutes, while you sit on your duff and wait. Then, the big fawk pops, your warrior or SK or druid gets agro, and you kill.

In short, don't waste everyones time on the adds, you don't need to kill them. Just keep them occupied. IIRC there are 2 sets of 3 waves, so 6 total, but like I said don't quote me, it's been a long long time.

We kept our casters and healers up on the walls, away from the center, the waves seem to agro those in the middle first, though i cant say for certain on that.


The key is knowing how many mobs spawn per wave, I dont remember off the top of my head. We had our knights warriors and rangers tank the spawns, each one had assigned healer, and some DPS. As they started to die off we'd just start moving from one to another.

Keep the tanks up and have enough to handle each spawn. For us it was mainly a fight for the tanks. We never failed using that strat.

Its been a while since I did this event, but.....

We always FDed a monk at the center of the event and ran the raid down the tunnel behind the untargetable burrower. We had FD pullers bring the first 3 waves there and we AEed them.

For the non-mezzable waves, we put a ring of knights around 1/per mob and assigned them a healer. The raid assisted one of the knights and then followed him around to kill the rest of the wave.

For the 2 larger non-mezzables, we had 2 warriors tank them and raid assisted the knight again and burned one and then the other.

For the final mob, we warrior tanked him and try to hold him relatively still.

There are 3 waves of 12, 3 waves of 6, 1 wave of 2 and then final beast, as I recall.

We just freeforall tanked the first 6 waves (anyone can tank the first 3 waves, any hybrids+ can tank next three, and then we had 2 warriors tank each of the 2 spawns with one add tanking while raid killed other one and then finished the add tanks worm.
