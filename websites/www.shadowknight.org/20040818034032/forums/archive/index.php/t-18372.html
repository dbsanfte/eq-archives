Here is the problem with the new SK diety specific Human Newbie quests......

The no drop quest items required simply do not drop often enough. I know it may sound like a whine, but let me explain.

I have run both types of Human SK (Freeport and Queynos), working entirely on the newbie quests only. I kill only the mobs that drop quest related items. I buy the other items as needed.

Here is the kicker. The Trivial Loot Code causes a major issue. Because the items drop so rarely, an average SK Newbie will be at level 12 before he (or she) can get enough of the no-drop items to put their armor together. At that level, almost every mob that COULD drop the quest items will show as green to you, and the TLC will kick in, resulting in NO No-drop items being given.

It's a catch 22, you have to kill them to get the drops, but by killing them (and not being lucky enough in your drops) you get too high, to fast and can no longer get the damn things you wanted in the first place.

DISCLAIMER: YES, I know the new quests are better than nothing and that all you "oldtimer" SK's would love to go back to the days where Newbies got nothing. But that isn't happening, and if EQ is going to have a Newbie Quest, it should be balanced with the other races/classes.

I also know that many would say "Screw the Quest" and get Banded or some other easy to buy items. Fact is, the newbie armors look cooler than heck, and are decent statisticly for your average non-tweaked newbie. Those two factors alone make me want them, and want them badly.....

Could we not pressue EQ to raise the drop rate just a bit.....

ZarrosLivinglight
The TLC is a very good idea that was very poorly implemented. The intent is to make it impossible for people to farm green mobs for valuable loot/gear (aka farming) as said activities can prevent the use of said content for the lower level characters it was intended for. The problem was and is that many quest drops are subject to the TLC, and that means either finding ways around it, or knowing about head of time so you can plan your hunting, or hiring lower level adventurers to do your dirty work for you. :cool:

Implemented properly, quest drops would be flagged as exempt from the TLC, making it possible to do them when it would normally make sense to do so or not having to worry about rare drops causing you to outlevel the mobs and being unable to get them. It could have been used on the high-PP vendor items in the aqua gobbie camps in BB to prevent higher level people from farming them endlessly, and thus remained a nice place for lower level characters to get some decent money for a little while. Once they outlevelled it, the high-PP items would go "trivial" and stop dropping for them. Instead, the entire camp was nerfed, hurting everyone, including the newbies it was designed to help.

Personally I think the quests should be reworked ala the froglok progressive quest, where you get character flags as you proceed, and that if your character has a flag that says "he's doing quest_01" that the mob intended to drop the quest item should drop it. Doing the turn in, or combine, should turn said flag back off, or set it to "quest step completed". That and a TLC that ignores quest drops, would work quite nicely.
