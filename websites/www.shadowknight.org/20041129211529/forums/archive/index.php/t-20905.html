Warning: this is another thread where someone complains about SKs being the worst of the tanks for no good reason.

THE PROBLEM:

We are clearly the worst mitigators of the 3 tanking classes, but what is our strength? Dealing damage? No one is going to invite us into a group because of that unless what they're fighting trivial content. Basically, for a comparably equipped/AA'd warrior, pally, and SK:

1. Warrior is the best tank where maximum mitigation is essential, especially defensive.

2. Pally is the best tank for exp mobs because he has aggrolock and the 2nd best mitigation in the game. He also makes the best offtank in raids for the same reasons, in addition to his ability to heal others, radiant cure, and BSS.

3. SKs are the best tanks at.... trivial encounters where survivability isn't a factor and where the extra DPS is nice? Useful in VERY few situations where there are no monks to FD split or necros to summon corpse.

It seems kinda unbalanced that we're never the *preferred* class for anything. Well, other than tanking trivial encounters, which is probably one of the most boring aspect of EQ next to skillups. In any challenging content with a risk of dying, you'd rather have a pally over an SK.

True, not all classes have to be preferred at something. Druids get by just fine but only because they have INCREDIBLE utility, second only to bards. Beastlords have less utility than druids but by far more utility than SKs and also output better damage. SKs, as they are now, are probably one of the least desirable class for a group or raid. Who's more undesirable? Monks? The 2nd shaman?

The problem is that all other hybrids (BL, pally, ranger) are built from parent classes that help their groups and raids (all priests in fact). SKs, on the other hand, are built from a class that's reknown for soloing. If you remove our ability to solo, where the hell does that leave us?

POSSIBLE SOLUTION: SHORT DURATION SLOW

Provide us with a quick casting, low resistance, very short duration slow. Something like a 50% disease slow that lasts 2-3 ticks with a recast time of 3 minutes. This would be consistent with the fact that our spells are harmful, yet increase our mitigation and provide true group and raid utility.

In raids, this slow would carve out our niche as the best pre-slowers. In groups, this slow could also be used as emergency CC on an add until someone could root or mez it. It's not game breaking nor does it displace any other class.

The idea would be that its quick casting and has a good resist check. Bards cannot reliably preslow, even in LDoNs hards, because their slows check off MR and doesn't have a great resist adjust. Besides, you don't always have a bard or beastlord in group.

Any worthwhile raid mob is going to have high MR and you're gonna need to tash and malo on it before you can land any debuffs. If an SK can reliably preslow on incoming, it gives the slowers that much time to debuff without any fear of pulling aggro. After slowing, you can immediately harmshield to drop the aggro generated by it. As it is now, most slowers wait a few sconds after someone starts tanking to slow due to aggro concerns (even with a knight MT).

The beauty of a 50% slow for 3 ticks is that it wouldn't displace any of the slower classes. Shaman, beastlord, enchanters slows are all higher than this, so ultimately you will want to have their slow on a mob. The advantage here would be that it is quick casting, and that we can absorb the punishment we'll take by aggroing early. I guarantee you that a mob isn't going to be slowed for at least the first 10 seconds its engaged, and this is where our slow could shine.

50% slow, lasting for 18 seconds, every 3 minutes would basically mean that we would keep a mob slowed 10%. Assuming the mob doesn't mitigate (although most do nowadays), this would be a minimum 5% increase in mitigation for us. Its not game breaking, but nice to have.

The additional bonus is that we don't have to be main tanks for this to work. We would effectively mitigate 5% for whoever was taking, a la pally stuns and heals. This slow could also be a viable method of CCing, by slowing an add and giving others time to root or mez it.

Anyway, Keevel has a valid point about us obtaining a new line of spells. Given the state of GoD spells, I don't think Sony is either creative enough, or has any desire, to introduce new abilities to a class. In terms of it being a Pandora's Box, however, it definitely doesn't have to be that way. It's true for any class that subsequent designers may screw them by nerfing/inappropriately scaling abilities. When this happens, you can bitch to Sony about it then. However, as things stand, I'd be willing to risk a lot of our spell lines (with the exception of darkness and zevfeer's) if we could get an ability like this slow.

The problem with building off our current abilities is the fact that we're the hybrid of a soloing DPS class. First, you can't combine a group dependent class with one that solos because then it won't complement the other group members. Second, you can't combine a tank and DPS if it wants to do either decently. Suggestions I see are always about increasing SK damage, but you can't really do this without threatening the lower end DPS classes, namely rangers, beastlords, monks*, and perhaps wizards in high uptime groups. Having the worst tanking ability of the tanks and the worst DPS of the DPS classes is an unenviable position to be in.

I'm not saying this slow is the only way to get class balance or anything, but it would address some of the concerns I have about our class with minimal reprecussion to other classes.
