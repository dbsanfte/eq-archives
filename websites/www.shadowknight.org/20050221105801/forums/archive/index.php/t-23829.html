Think of it as a game:

You're desire/objective is to get free stuff.
Their desire/objective is to make you pay for said stuff.

They do what they can, you do what you can. Both sides take risks. Lawsuits like this are a huge investment on the industries part, and the artist contributing nothing to the effort will still have their songs/movies/games not on the program. If the lawsuit fails, the company/association is out a LOT of money, and a hole has opened up.

The user takes the risks of viruses, and getting caught. Those involved in the distribution take greater risks.



Now, sometimes the theory is right. I downloaded Boondock Saints awhile back, loved the movie. Eventually bought it.

However, many of my friends who are into mental, particularly fantasy metal, have heard of, like, and have some Nocturnal Rites. Great band... but I'm one of ... two people I know to ever buy their music.

It's an issue that isn't going to die, and the fight will go back and forth as technology increases. Legal loopholes get found. Consider the risk vs reward, and be prepared to get a new program. I remember when Napster was shut down, everyone freaked. Then Morpheus came along... then Kazaa, then ((and still)) Soulseek, and Bit Torrent. It's not over for either side.
