While I've never dueld a "high end" shaman, I have dueled a shaman several times that was several levels higher than me. Fights vs. shamans are usually either really fast and the sham loses, or they're not so fast, and the sham will end up winning. They can simply outlast almost any class because they can cann and heal themselves so well.

Here's what I found worked well:

Bash, a lot, and bash from behind. When he starts to cast a spell, get behind him, and bash him. Save your bashes for when they start to cast a spell. PCs and NPCs can't dodge/parry/block/riposte when attacked from behind, so being behind the sham when you need to bash him will prevent him from dodging.

Put your pet on him, not his pet vs your pet, and try to get his pet on you, then ignore his pet and focus on beating him down.

Dispelling him is a good idea, if possible.

Eyepatch of plunder is nice to have. 20% more haste, and another quick buff he would have to dispell before he gets to your good ones... Not that we really have any.

Good spells to use would be Touch of Innoruuk (-500), Touch of Volatis(-200), Bond of Death (-200), Blood of Hate (-100) if his resists aren't great, and maybe even try using Drain Soul (-200) because it refreshes quickly and you could cast it while waiting for the big taps to refresh.

Using a jousting weapon might be a good idea, to maximize dmg in between lifetap casts.

Just chaining lifetaps on a shaman, and whatever melee you do to him, it seems like you could easily get them down to that critical 74% pretty easily. It's not like they have a whole lot of HP.
