I really like the idea of posting suggestions, in hopes they are read, so here I go. While I disagree with a few suggestions made, I won't point them out, as I do not wish to tear down anyones post / idea. The one idea I would like to see, somewhat mentioned earlier in this thread, is a slow / haste tap. Here is what I would propose, if it were up to me:

LvL: ?
Effect: slows a mob for 3 minutes, by 20%
self haste for 3 minutes, by 20%
Cost: 100 mana?

It would only be a soloing tool, of course, and the duration, imo, should be short, as if I had this, I would slow every mob I fought anyhow.

I don't believe this is an unachievable wish, because compared to most melee, only SKs and Warriors have to rely on a subset of items providing haste. My reasoning is the that:

(1) epics are easy nowadays ( comparatively ), for most classes
( SKs, Necros, and Mages are still difficult ) which mean that
Monks, Rogues, and Rangers have a fairly easy to achieve
1% haste item.
(2) clerics, while having a spell haste buff, do not have melee
haste buffs, yet Paladins have a 27% self haste spell ( see
Valor of Marr on lucy's site ).
(3) Comparing to other hybrid types, Beastlords inherit the
ability to slow & haste like Shamans, yet we do not have
the slow inherited from Necros.
(4) Rangers also have a 50% slow proc on the harder-to-get
epic, and a couple of more items to provide haste ( Plane of
Sky cloak for instance ).

I surely don't think this would unbalance us any, as it is minimal spell haste, and not much of a slow. The only reason I added the slow part in my idea is we have a line of different "tap" spells, so it sort of "fits". With this spell, we still could not slow mobs enough to replace other slowers, in say Plane of Storms, and anyone else's haste would be better than ours. This would simply be a soloing tool that I feel is missing, and would be useful. Also, if it make minimal to no difference in a fight at these values, then it certainly fits in with a lot of the spells in my spell book! hehe

Either way, I think it would add to game play, not dramatically though, and not enough to upset other classes or unbalance us.
