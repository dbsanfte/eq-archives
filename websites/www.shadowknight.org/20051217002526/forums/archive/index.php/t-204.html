From a Non-SK point of view:

I play a Chanter, he's level 57... and in all those levels the people that I trust the MOST to break a mez properly are SKs past level 9 (when we get spells) and level 60 paladins. Others are adept at breaking mezzes, but there's NOBODY who can control aggro like a Shadow Knight.

I've been in groups where the SK saved my butt because of aggro control and only aggro control. That (IMHO) is what we do best, there's nobody better.

I've seen SK's out-pull monks, and in some cases were preferred over monks because of their ability to snare/split and invis pull past other mobs. Granted, we use mana to do all of these magical things... but if you're grouped w/ a Chanter/Bard you'll never be LOM (never had a SK in my group say... OOM someone else pull for a bit).

*****

From the SK point of View:

I USED to play a Chanter. Now I'm a Shadow Knight. I enjoy the versatility of being able to solo, and I love the challenge of being in a group w/ a Rogue who is several levels higher than me... and have him say (after about 3 hours of playing)... 'Nice work Ghob... I never got hit once!' (aggro control...).

FD is icing on the cake. Yeah, I don't do as much damage as a monk at my level, but can a monk split-pull mobs that just won't come apart? Can a monk stop a mob from running back to it's other friends? I can bash / slam / bludgeon / slash and hammer mobs and if I get into trouble SNARE run a bit and FD (if I even take the time to do that...). I can bank anywhere I want to (w/ FD and CoS) and never get hit. And most importantly... I can grunt and make funny quips about my overly rotund ass and big ass feet (still looking for boots btw...).

At 60, a Shadow Knight (without any AA abilities) has not 1 but 2 FDs (making that better than a monk) perhaps not as reliable... but we have the HP to stand there and take a beating while trying to cast the next one. Yes, we do have less HP and AC than a Warrior... but our job isn't to be the main tank (we can of course fill in... and VERY nicely when necessary... but it isn't our main role).

*****

In terms of finding a group, that is up to you as the individual... not as the class. Sure, some groups would rather take a chanter over a bard... or a warrior over a SK... or even a Monk over a SK. BUT, if you've proven yourself... you won't have any problems finding a group. Learn the class... ask questions (as I seem to ask daily)... and absorb what those more experienced can teach you. And that doesn't necessarily have to be about being a Shadow Knight. (It could be... don't pull that one, pull the left one first... etc etc...).

All in all (this post seems to have gone on long enough), I really enjoy playing Ghob, he's in the thick of things, I can control the tempo as to how quick my group makes XP, and from playing a chanter... I'm aware of things around me (like when the cleric gets jumped for healing someone).

Just my .02... but I'm not looking back...

"[M]y question is at high levels are we viable or not? Can we get groups? Can we split effectively (at lease equal to monks)? Will people want us to join them or get a warr or pallie instead?"

The SK is a viable class, relative to the other classes, for grouping for experience (alt or normal) up to and including level 60. Most posters have already provided ample reasons above.

The SK is not viable, relative to the other melee classes, for high end raid situations. If your goal is to raid and contribute effectively at the highest levels of the game, you would be wise to choose another class. Your SK skills alone will not be necessary or important enough to merit your inclusion on a raid. You will lack the hp and damage mitigation skill to tank most mobs, FD cast time will cripple your pulling ability relative to monks, your spells will not augment your tanking relative to other hybrids, your HT will not adequately damage mobs, your alt skills (at best) allow only negligible improvements in performance relative to other melee classes.

This is not to say that you will not merit inclusion on other grounds (raid knowledge, specialist roll in certain minor situations, etc) or that the class is not enjoyable (for the most part); it means that, at level 60, you will have fallen behind the curve in relation to other melee classes in raid crucial areas.

Verant shows no sign of either acknowledging this, or moving to fix it, so you should be aware of our predicament before you invest time leveling up.

Just my 2cp
