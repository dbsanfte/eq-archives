If you are asking what stats are most important in general.
I say STA > INT > STR > DEX > AGI

STA = Hit points
INT = Mana
STR = Attack Rating (How much you hit for)
DEX = Increase weapon proc
AGI = minor increase in AC (Armor Class)

STA returns less HP after reaching 255, you start out with a decent amount being a Troll.
INT will need to be raised because you are a Troll.
STR you are probably ok with being a Troll.
DEX raising it is nice, but not a "need"
AGI just make sure it's over 75, like DEX it's nice to raise, but not a "need"

Raising trade skills faster you will probably want to get INT items. This being trade skills as in "making items"
Groff Gorespiller
Tradeskill stats are as follows:

Baking, Brewing, Tailoring, Jewelcrafting, Alchemy, Fishing, Pottery, Tinkering, Poison Making, Fletching --- Use higher of either Intelligence or Wisdom for skillups.

Blacksmithing -- Uses higher of either Intelligence, Wisdom or Strength for skillups.

For Weapons Skills, Swimming, other combat or "general physical" oriented skills, Dex determines the number of skillups.

Oagan
My wizard has his 8th prayer shawl. Other than smithing, the higher of INT or WIS. Since SK's are INT-based, we will generally have higher INT. Not to say that SK's can't be tradeskillers, but trolls/ogres tend to have much lower INT, so skill-ups are going to go slower. You're probably going to want to have some +INT gear to use while you're tradeskilling. There is a lot of player made jewelry pieces that can increase INT.

Dex does seem to help. Geerloks are also a good idea, and can be purchased rather cheaply. I believe it was shown that dex helps successses in Fletching, and can see why it would also possibly impact tailoring.

If you're not doing the prayer shawl or the PoP earring, I'd say it may be better to make a tradeskilling caster (erudite=highest INT) to master the tradeskills. If you're doing pottery, it will also help to have an enchanter, since they are able to enchant clay at a reasonably low level.

Don't kid yourself, however. GM tradeskilling in any skill is a time-consuming/expensive proposition. Brewing and Baking are probably the cheapest to GM. You can only have one skill past 200, until you use the PoP aa's, or are a Gnome and Tinker. A forager is also helpful in Baking.

At your level, Black Drake, Brilliance is the spell that will raise your INT. KEI/VoQ is the one to use 45/46+.
