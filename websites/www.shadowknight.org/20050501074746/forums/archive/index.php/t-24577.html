I gave it a try last night. Had the zone to myself, so I set up in the forest. XP was substandard (1% aa per) at level 59, although the fights were faster then in PoN, as way of example. Also, the mobs there only hit for 300ish and weren't very resistant. It had a slooooooooooooooooow respawn rate, however. Still there was lots of stuff to pull in the first area, although I wouldn't rate it very high on the sustained xp scale.

At that point I decided to check out the castle. Invised up and ran in. Everything was DB at level 59, but they would be between levels 55 (bodies lasted 30 minutes) and 58. There didn't seem to be a really good 'camp' spot, persay, because there didn't seem to be a great deal of mobs in general. Plus it was very linear in design, which might be fun for grouping, maybe, but definitely isn't condusive to duo'ing.

Anyhoo, explored most of the main castle, but was never able to find out how to get to the chessboard area.

At that point I decided to head back, happy with my little experiment. Of course, that meant zoning out. As I don't have the OT hammer (and the only set of puppet strings are 90k!!! right now on Prexus), I zoned out on the tree and popped in the middle of CS. I figured what the hell, and decided to try out Siren's Grotto. Neat place, and I was able to solo it without much of a problem while I ran my shaman over on my second computer. Killed the first 2 mobs, but then when I tried to pull some walrus man, he just kept pathing back and forth and not actually coming after me.

Being relatively impatient and growing bored with SG, I decided to give WW a try. Ran both character through SG and popped out in the Wastes. GOD does that zone have some bad pathing. I just stayed near the entrance for a few kills, and decided to take on a yellow con Ice Burrower. Of course, my shaman starts getting attacked through the ceiling by some wolf, and I didn't have root up for some reason, and I was tired, so I just let the shadowknight die and zoned my shaman. Ah, the beauties of that new guild hall. (even though I find the idea of the corpse summoning zone to be pretty lame in the grand scheme of things).

Anyway, don't think I'll head back to PoM any time soon.

Find Gillamina Garstobidokis near the zone-in in Splitpaw.

You say, 'Hail, Gillamina Garstobidokis'

Gillamina Garstobidokis says 'Greetings to you, fellow adventurer. Gillamina Garstobidokis, master mage at your service!' Gillamina bows. 'Beware, should you wish to venture here. In case you didn't know, the lair of Splitpaw has been overrun by fierce elemental creatures.' Gillamina looks down at the dead gnoll near her feet. 'Poor creatures. I am not a fan of the gnolls by any means, but it is still sad to see them all [slaughtered] in this manner.'

you say, 'Slaughtered?'

Gillamina Garstobidokis says 'Yes, the gnolls that once laired here have been destroyed by the elementals it seems. I am not quite sure what happened. These elementals intrigue me so. But I suppose you would like to know what I am [doing here]?'

You say, 'What are you doing here?'

Gillamina Garstobidokis says 'Good question! I have come here looking for a set of unique orbs that contain the essence of the various elementals that have invaded the lair. If you can find four of these orbs, one from a fire elemental, one from an air elemental, one from an earth elemental, and one from a water elemental, I will gladly repay you for your time. I wish to study these orbs and have not had much luck finding any unfortunately.'

You need to find four elemental orbs -- one of: Air, Water, Earth, and Fire.
Turning in these four globes results in an Elemental Stone.
