I'm going to go out on a limb here and advocate against spending a lot of time and effort training all your skills up early, and rather do it when (if) you need them. Here's my experience:

When I was moving through the early levels, I decided to just focus on 2-handed weapons. I liked the harder hitting weapons, and figured it'd be part of my "character", to use 2-handed weapons exclusively.

Well, along comes Telk, in ToV. Silly dragon drops a 23/23 1HB, nodrop, and I'm the only one there who can use it. I already had my epic at that point, so I was a little dubious about when it'd be useful, although I had a nice shield (Atrementous) to go with it.

My 1HB skill was pitifull. However, I had gobs of unused training points, so plugged about 50 into 1HB, and then went hunting. 2 days(RL, not played) later my skill was maxed. I thought, "Damn, that was easy. :P "

Since then I've also learned what situations it's better to use that hammer in instead of the epic, and thus I get use out of both.

One thing I'll admit, that the low delay of that hammer really cut down the time to train. If the delay were closer to 50, then it might have taken me 4 or 5 days (which, IMO, isn't a big deal).

Just some thoughts... I know most disagree... I think it's probably what some would call "six and one half-dozen of the other". :)
