I have played a cleric a bit and have tanked in places like CoD (and worse).

The first thing to recognize as a cleric is that Cheal has problems for those tanks that are "new" to that tier before the slow sets in. In these situations, a cleric may wish to HoT as the mob comes to camp and to spot heal until the slow lands.

As your level builds up (and eventually your AAs), you can get to only using Cheals (your mitigation and avoidance basically makes that unslowed bit a much smaller percentage of your hps).

I remember tanking in PoV for my first time when I was level 60. The golem tripled me and proced in the first round of combat and I lost 1/3 of my hps in that single round of combat. The first time a cleric sees this they tend to panic.

Now if a cleric doesn't know you (i.e. your in a pickup group), he should treat you as a new tank to that level until he knows otherwise.

Finally, the Greater Pusslings in CoD are mini-named and drop actual loot. They are not slowable iirc and will suck a lot of mana from your group.

Ognij
Level 61 helped a lot for pulling CoD as it added Festering Darkness to the spell line up.
This seems to generate less social agro and allowed single pulls to be made more easily.
Its quite a slow 'snare' too, so it gives the casters more time to debuff the incoming mob so that it is already slowed by the time it is in the camp. Drop a CD or two on the incoming mob as well and it should keep the agro on you and avoid the shaman/enchanter getting hit.

Sometimes targetting is difficult due to the cobwebs in there, a way around it is to cycle views using F9 so that you have a better viewpoint on the mobs.

Keep an eye out for the 'wanderer' in CoD that likes to appear at the 'crossroads'. Its easy to run into him on a pull sometimes, usually he can be avoided by careful planning, or FD if he is unavoidable.

If your enchanter has Dire Charm, the little rats make great pets (scrounging rat I think they are called). They often spawn to the right of the 'crossroads' and you can pull one back for the enchanter to charma nd add some reasonable DPS to the group.
