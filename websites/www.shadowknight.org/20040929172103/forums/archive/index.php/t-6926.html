was advised by all that if I were to solo more than group, I would need a decent sized mana pool so I split my creation points between INT and STR. So far, I haven't regretted this decision on where to put my initial starting points.

STA > STR. FYI, STR gear is relatively easy to come across. STA gear isn't. HPs help you tank. STR helps you deal damage, and your primary role, as a tank, is to absorb damage.

If you didn't put 5 points into AGI to raise it to 75, then you will cost yourself approximatly 30-35 AC at levels 40+. If your AGI isn't 75 due to gear, then get it there ASAP, and keep it there.

Personally, I *think* I put 5 in STR, 5 in AGI, then the rest in STA.

Note that by the time you reach 30, you should be starting to practice a 2-hander. That'll drop your STA fast. If your STA isn't over 100, you'll suffer penalties.

My initial starting stats were: STR 80, STA 80, AGI 75, DEX 70, WIS 83, INT 117.

I thought it odd that a majority of the SK's I did converse with stated that if they were to start another SK it would be an Erudite SK. Does this seem odd at all?

That is odd, though I think Erudite's a naturally good choice for my style of play. I like casting, so I like a larger mana pool. Additionally, there are more buffs to raise STR and STA than INT. I figure, I have more of a chance to maximize my abilities with an Erudite; I'd rather wind up with 255 STR, 215 STA, and 200 INT than 255 STR, 255 STA, 125 INT. I'm more well-rounded, I feel.

Having a larger mana pool also allows me stronger soloing skills in some regards; I feel it helps when breaking some tough spawns in areas you can't feign split, and that it allows me to escape from "oh crap" situations more easily when I'll have a few extra casts in me.

We also have better faction than all but human SKs...have a very easily accesible home city in Paineel (Nexus->Tox is a godsend..getting to Neriak or the ugly cities is much more of a pain)...oh, and we're damn sexy.
