Visiting Paladin here:

I have *always* held that all tank classes should bring to their group an equal-as-possible probability and potential reward of success. The magnitude of the variation in situational tradeoffs is much more important than a mere count of their occurances.

Ok, preamble stated, my point being:

There are only two factors. Group maximum capability and group efficiecy.

Currently in group settings SKs outperform Paladins on mobs that aren't stunnable (via immunity, mob level, or exorbitant resist rate). On single 'named' mobs that are stun immune the SK aresenal brought to the group as a whole has a decided advantage via the relative greater power of AoP, HT, personal DPS, etc. over the paladin's heals and anscillary abilities. On a string of same the bite spells offer far greater group efficiency benefits (taps/dps) than what a paladin could provide via his heals.

Currently SKs also outperform Paladins on the low-end where healing (and therefore the ability of stuns to meaningfully reduce the need for same) isn't a significant factor and 'dps' is the prevailing limiting parameter.

Currently Paladins outperform SKs on content other than the above.

As it relates to fight duration and therefore success the dps of a tank and their group is a relevent parameter. Plainly stated hypothetically if group with Tank A did 1kdps and Tank B did 1.1kdps then to have an overall 'balanced' case then Tank A would need to be more survivable than B on a per second basis. As a longer fight allows more room for RNG variances, repops, etc. it would be reasonable to conclude that the slower fight should be somewhat (if only to a small degree) safer than would be predicted by the exact equivalence of Xa^Ya = Xb^Yb (where "a" and b" are tanks, X is the per interval survival estimate and Y the fight duration in intervals).

I am not stating that the current situational strengths for knights are overall evenly balanced or in favor of either class. However if SK are to be generally better at the extreme high-end fight (from which all 'best' rewards originate) and at the low-end (to which all encounters eventually proceed to) I would expect that Paladins would possess a rather significant advantage in the transitional case.

However my preferance would be for SOE to grow some balls. Either state which content level which tank is to be good for and allow a X-class change or (much better idea) buff/nerf the tanks abilties such that from easy, moderate, to hard content all tanks offer to groups a very equivalent amount of capability.
