Sesshou
The pagefile is the virtual memory, basically it's hard drive space reserved for storing data that your RAM doesn't have room for. If you right-click on the My Computer icon and go to properties, it will open a "System Properties" window. There should be an "Advanced" tab (that's what it is under WinXP), which has a Performance -> Settings button on it. There's a section there on the "Advanced" tab of that window (under WinXP again) that refers to Virtual Memory. You can change that file to be larger or smaller, and you can set high and low ranges for the value. I would put the maximum at twice the amount of RAM in your system if you have the space, but put the minimum and default to a smaller number. I can't guarantee that making the pagefile larger will help, but it's a possibility that your computer was trying to dynamically resize the file and was having problems finding the space to do so, which would result in a lot of extra swapping to the hard drive. On that note, make sure you have a good amount of free drive space, in addition to making sure that that space is not fragmented.

Virtual Memory size is usually dynamic and doesn't need to be manually adjusted. I think your limiting issue is your processor. Look at a new Mboard + RAM set up. Keep the rest of your gear. Can go cheaper with an AMD processor, or spend a few more $$ and get a P4. I used to play on a P3 550 with 512 mb ram with a ATI 7500. Just upgrading to a P4 2.6 with 1 gig of ram doubled my FPS in games and allowed me to run full clip with no lag in bazaar (1 new model only though). Your processor / ram / Mother Board combo is what's slowing you down.

I've heard some good things about the latest ATI drivers, but with EQ that's a crap shoot on if they actually work. If all of a sudden you are having problems and nothings changed on your system, try to remember if it was from a recent patch. They like to dick around with the DX DLL about every patch and could of done something that screwed you up. Read the EQ tech forums and see if others users are having the same problems, or what they did to fix it.
