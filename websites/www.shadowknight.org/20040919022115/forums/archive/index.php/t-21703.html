I think DE's are well suited to a variety of SK that one might encounter, Polished, skilled, intelligent and underneath the exterior, wholly "eveel". Erudites and humans would probably fit into this play type as well.

I would also argue that Ogres and Trolls fit the sk bill as well. They are evil, just not as polished. I tend to RP my Troll as a calculating, intelligent, cunning, yet fear inspiring entity.

Historical as well as fantasy precedents exist for this. Typically any fighter, whether far eastern monk type orders, or Dark Age knight, who had devoted his life to warcraft was educated on far more than sword play. Geopolitics, religeous implications, social order, history of a sort, strategy, and in some instances basic science and math were part of the curriculum for the aspiring knight, assasin, monk, samurai etc.

I dont know if it still does, but descriptions of SK's back in the Kunark days included lines about being chosen at birth, consecrated to the gods od hate and fear etc, and beginning a lifelong journey of "learning" things like weapon skills, spells and exacting torture and death.

For fantasy evidence, look only to Tolkien (but there are other examples across different genre lines) when he speaks of trolls becoming more intelligent as the power of Mordor grows. And don't forget the well spoken Ogre (his name escapes me atm) in our own Ogreville who speaks of the hey-day of Ogre domination.

My intelligence has risen from my starting stats, and if for no other reason than that, I don't play Krool as Ja Jar Bink's lost cousin. He isn't nearly as sharp as a DE or an Erudite but he does not live in a fog of blood lust, booger and beer either.
