I'm 55 and can solo the jail cell area. I usually stick to melee mobs (most prisoners and the guardians). If you can't toe-to-toe them just dark em with your highest dark then load them up with your other DoTs. Stay out of their melee range and spear/tap them every once and awhile. Takes a minute or two, but at 55 most mobs are dead or close to it after just a lap or two around the cell block. Once they get low melee them to finish them off and to preserve mana.

As for the roamers, it really isn't a problem. The rats do not assist the prisoners, however the Guardians will and the rats will assist the guardians. So long as you keep the hall cleared of guardians, roamers should not be an issue.

A few tips for soloing in PoJ at 57:

1) If you stay in the first cell block area, then you wont have any adds. Below level 57, you will have adds.

2) As long as your resists are decent, the easiest prisoners are Zenok, Thrakk, and the shortie near Sslarm(sp?). They are casters.

3) Avoid Sslarm(sp?), sentrys, and the chealer prisoners.

4) Some of the prisoners HT, so send in Jobaro first.

5) Dont waste your time on the rats, they give very little xp.

6) Agro kiting takes too long, just tank them. Get Virtue and or KEI if needed. Bandaids and regen bp will help with downtime.

7) Fight in the jail cells against a wall, so that you can channel lifetaps if needed.

Good luck,
Dexk

At 57 you're nearly at the end of your least efficient period for soloing...Unfortunately, no matter how much more efficient you get, soloing for normal exp still won't really be a great idea at any level from 58 to 65. At best, it can be a decent option later on to do AA exp at odd hours.

All the same, your aggro kiting isn't going to come to fruition until you get better DoT and snare spells. Aggro kiting a mob around with Dooming Darkness, Boil Blood, and the 52/58 pet chasing it = something a Necro can do at L 39-44.

Fear kiting in a few select spots might still be decent, especially certain indoor zones where 'advantageous' fear pathing and good zem% are both availible. Finding the line between being clever and blatantly using an exploit is a good skill to learn. Even though the GMs aren't sneaking around spying on everyone like the old days, you don't want to be that guy getting ahead in EQ by hitting "Up, Up, Down, Down, Left, Right, Left, Right, B, A, Start"...although all weapons and 30 extra lives sounds pretty nice for this GoD stuff...
