Dakkin
From your post I assume you're talking about grouping, which means you're usually tanking so your main mana expenditure is on holding aggro and/or snaring.

It looks like you're using Festering and spears as your staple aggro; both are relatively inefficient in terms of the aggro they give you for mana expended.

On level 55+ mobs (i.e. exp groups), your best overall aggro spell is fear (100% resisted but still gives great aggro), and your best snap (quick) aggro spell is clinging darkness for when you don't have 2 seconds to land fear, or an unslowed heavy hitter might keep interrupting fear (or when some mobs might be lower than level 55).

Use these and you can drop Voice since you won't have a problem holding aggro.

The ideal in exp groups is to land fear while the mob's inc (or pull with it) then cast it whenever it refreshes. If there might be fearable mobs around, do the same with clinging darkness but slow down on the cast rate after the first few seconds.

Additional sources of mana regen are:
Druid Bot9 if available (8 mana per tick)
BST SD if available (9 mana per tick)
Mental corruption

As a side note, I'd suggest you put 2 points into Spell Casting Expertise AA (so fear and CD won't fizzle) and some more into mental clarity (every little helps).

Just my own opinions though; I know other people use different methods of holding aggro and do very well with them.

Naeldaar Blackhearted
CD isn't really a spell you cast for the snare (though it does stop movement entirely once the mob drops below about 9%), but rather for the aggro. If you're tanking, the 30 second duration shouldn't really matter, since chances are you're going to be casting it more than once every 30 seconds. Personally, I like to hit with at least 3 clinging as fast as they refresh, and then a clinging every 15-20 seconds as necessary to hold aggro. Early in the fight (usually after the first clinging gets the mob's attention) I'll generally toss my spear in for a bit of extra quick aggro, depending on my mana. This is especially useful pre-slow when interrupts on a longer cast like CD are more likely. My normal set up looks like this:

1 ToI (always)
2 Festering (always)
3 Clinging if I'm MT, Terror otherwise (when offtanking adds waiting for mez, dot = bad)
4 ZB
5 Spear
6 Aura of Pain if MT, Aura of Hate otherwise
7 Aura of Hate if MT and grouped with a chanter, switch between Aug. of Death/MC/Pact of Hate otherwise
8 Death Peace (always)

If I'm fighting 100% disease resistant mobs like in CoD or BoT south/NW tower, slots 4, 5, 6, 7 get set to Blood of Hate, Ignite Blood, Voice, MC.

I like to stay around 50m when tanking so I can tap 5 or 6 times before I OOM should I run into trouble, but I'll burn down to 20m then lower my mana usage over the next couple while I regen back up if I'm not. I generally use MC over SoC, depending on role and what I have in the way of mana regen - more is obviously better, get 9/symbol instead of virtue when you can, beast crack etc. I also have VoQ almost all the time, but I'll normally use pact instead of kei if VoQ isn't available and if I don't have other mana regen spells on - every point helps.

Generally I do more pop than ldon, so I stay mounted most of the time as it helps med between pulls without having to bother with sitting and standing (hard on food but easier on mana) - tanking while mounted takes some getting used to but it's pretty easy once you get into it. As for HT, I burn it as soon as it's up unless I'm raiding. The group I normally hunt with is dependable enough that we're always safe without me having to do anything exceptionally heroic, and having HT sitting doing nothing becomes nothing more than a waste of good dps.

Also, if you hate clinging's small snare and can't keep mana up with festering, consider using dooming as your hard snare instead - it's only 1% less movement reduction than festering, only costs 2/3 the mana, and lasts a lot longer than the average life expectancy of most pop exp or ldon mobs.
