Tukka
Well, I just recently managed to acquire a Royal Attendant Helm of Sand (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=44301), and while I am very pleased with the stats and such, much to my disappointment I discovered that it displays as a "cloth" (or "no graphic") item.

If I were any other race but erudite, this would probably please me. As it is, it is a huge bummer. For those of you who might not have noticed, erudites wear this absurd looking purple robe when we aren't wearing a piece of head armor. When we're completely unequipped it doesn't look so bad, but when we're wearing a breastplate, it looks absolutely silly. Erudites are the only race as far as I'm aware that wear anything on their heads when they're naked (aside from little cosmetic /face things like circlets and eyepatches).

Even worse, browsing lucy, I see that ornate (not that ornate would be an upgrade) and elemental SK helms (and I presume cleric/paladin helms) also are "cloth" graphics. Horrible. I don't know if this aesthetic theme carries over into GoD/OoW, but I doubt I will be getting upgrades from those expansions any time soon.

This really bugs me, as appearance is the primary reason I chose erudite as the race for my SK. Mind you, I chose the race before the release of Luclin and the huge foreheads and keyring helms--both of which I could tolerate, because otherwise it made our characters look even better--with this one glaring exception. I find it really galling that my character looks the way he does now. :(

Anybody agree? Got any paladin or cleric erudite pals who agree? Maybe have them e-mail Sony and /feedback. I know that there is an erudite Luclin model for the head which doesn't include the hood--I used to be able to get it to display (from my point of view at least) when I switched out my armor in the right order, but I can't seem to get that to work anymore. But in any event, it wouldn't mean any extra design work for Sony--all they'd have to do is make it so when an erudite is wearing a cloth head item, it displays the hoodless model.

Not Top 10 material, granted, but something that's pretty irritating nonetheless for those of us who are vain.
