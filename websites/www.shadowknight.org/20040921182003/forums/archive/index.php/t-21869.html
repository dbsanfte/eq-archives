Depends on context.

Divine Intervention - a buff that triggers when you would die, it has a chance based upon your CHA of healing you instantly so you don't die.

Damage Interval - a mob stat that determines how hard it hits. Multiplied by a random number and added to Damage Bonus (DB) for hit amounts.
Slein Jinn
Mob damage works on the modal Dmg*DI+DB. DI is a random intiger between 1 & 20, DB is a constant added to every connected swing, and Dmg and DB both are mob-specific.

Warrior mitigation innately multiplies the DI (or the Dmg - it makes no difference in the equation) by .95 and defensive/stonewall multiplies the DI or Dmg by .55.

EDIT: That's actually not quite the best way to explain it...

The innate warrior mitigation advantage commonly referred to as DI-1 mitigates as the following:
DI-(DI*.5)

The same is true of Defensive/Stonewall. Though the impact on the actual damage is the same, the latter formula allows for stackability, and the two do stack.

Divine Intervention... well, Lucy can tell you more about that.
