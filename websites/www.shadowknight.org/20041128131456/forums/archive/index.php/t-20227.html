Blackmerlin
Was reading about all the trouble highend sk have in god trials and the gap between warrior/sk getting larger and larger and was thinking about a an defensive way , that is sk-like but doesnt gimp old content.

Here is were i came up with the idea of an forced defensive proc.
It would be a instant cast (or nearly instant 0.1 - 0.5 sec) buff spell (or disc) that would turn our inate proc buff to proc on every hit we take from the target we are fighting. This buff (or disc) would consume 1000 mana per tick (starting at the moment where the buff is cast) and stop any manaregeneration through buffs(kei,voq,tranq,pot,...),rods,procs and recourse effects as long as it lasts.
This buff can be clicked off, it will then start to fade till the tick (which consumed the 1000 mana, is finished) - (if it would be a disc - it could be turned off and still would last till tick is finished).

Recast time of this buff would be 6 seconds -> you would be able to keep this up as long as your manapool lasts. As one relative long duration or in many 'one tick' - steps.

Its most efficient use would be with shroud of chaos/black shroud + soul abrasion since manaregeneration wouldnt be possible with mc proc line and it would only be a short duration dps increase for the cost of an whole manapool and only for the one sk who is actually tanking.

This buff would have one purpose - to help sk slightly against spike dmg - as it would be a nearly instant turn on/off ability - that wouldnt be able to be abused as to my knowledge.

What do you think about this ?

I was not suggesting an equivalent to defensive, but more of an short duration fighting style that would allow to reduce damageincome by a variable number (depending on aa's - soulabrasion,scf,theft of life,...).
If soulabrasion kicks in on every hit we take while this mode was active, this would be over 200 hitpoints healed on every hit taken and on a number of these hits there would be additional healing from crits. Looking at the mobs parsed in this trials (see Brael's parse in general) that would be roughly 10-20% less dmg taken in bad combat rounds with the occasional crits healing up to 50% per hit.

If this wont be enough there would still be the possibility to build in an increased crit rate up to effectivly doubling the received healing, but i think it wouldnt be balanced with 500 hitpoints healed per hit.
