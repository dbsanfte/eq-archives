Either will work.

The cleric has the potential to heal better, and supply the best AC/HP buffs.

The shaman can usually slow the mob, plus melee buff (agi, dex, str, sta), haste, heal, and has a pet to add to our pet for a little extra dps.

The shaman's slow will probably be necessary in the higher levels if you ever want to do tier 2 or better PoP zones.

Shaman's healing is not the greatest, but with the haste, slow, and buffs, they are usually the favorite melee duo partner.

03-16-2004, 05:05 AM
You can reach a point in gear on both characters where a cleric is a fine partner, even at 65, past teir one in PoP. I can tank unslowed mobs all day long without draining my clerics mana at all(minus stuff like wing bosses in BoT) up to tactics.

Cleric pros: Easiest to bot. Strong versus Undead. Res, Best HP/AC buffs. Best healing. Pacify line.

Cleric cons. Slow exp rate unless you're fighting undead. No haste. Slightly harder to travel with.

Shaman pros: Haste, Slow, better DPS all around(v undead it could go to the cleric if they have high enough mana regen). Easier to travel with. Good mana regen without purchasing outside buffs.

Shaman cons: Healing, Buffs and probably the biggest con with a shaman is it requires a whole lot more button mashing to match an effective level of play compared to an actual person playing them. Canni dance, slowing, Healing and DoTing can wear people out quickly when coupled with a character that needs somewhat frequent attention as well.

Overall I'd say shaman is the better partner for exp hunting. But either can work fine if you play out their strengths.

I concur. Don't duo at all unless you just can't avoid it or are just burnt out and want quiet. The experience grouping is vastly better.

I'd take the advice of what to do when time geared as only valuable to time-geared people. That gear so changes a toon's power, even a little of it, that the toon becomes capable of much different things.

I have a 65 shaman and SK, and can duo yellow mobs with them wearing bazaar gear. My SK has never looted a thing besides a sword from hedge maze that he doesn't even use. He has ornate hands arms and wrists, the rest fulvous hardscale plate made by my shaman. He does have a white dragon helm but otherwise nothing special besides utility items he doesn't even wear when fighting.

I duo yellows with him in Plane of Valor(the guys in suits) to get sacred waters for the aid gimel quest. He only has 28 AA, and my shaman has about 270, and my SK still manages to get along fairly well, though sometimes it's a close call, against yellows. That should give you an idea of the power of a shaman/SK duo. I can also solo zone trash in Plane of Fire, but the mephits in plane of water will wipe me out.

All in all, it can be quite a fun challenge, and really teach you a lot of tricks about pulling and things like that, to duo, and shaman/SK makes an incredible team. But...duo'ing is such inferior experience to grouping that it shouldn't be bothered with for most people, most of the time, especially when you get to 65, where you can get in groups that crank out an AA or more an hour. Not that many people can do that with a shaman/SK duo -- you need good AA and equipment on both toons before you can think of coming anywhere close to that level of experience.
