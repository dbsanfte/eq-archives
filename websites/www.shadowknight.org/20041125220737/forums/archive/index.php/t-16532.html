Murgutroid
That will never, never, never happen. Out of probably about a million reasons why, here's a few that popped into my head right away. In no particular order...

1. How many quests would be broken by this? If the big foreheaded types in Erud decided to finally rid the world of their heretical brothers in Paineel, where would Duriek and Gerot go? To an allied city? What if there weren't any left? Out into some dark corner of Toxx? Maybe, but if the devs wanted them out there, I think they'd be there already. It's a pretty minor piece of the puzzle, but getting inside the city to talk to Gerot is still part of the trick. Where would all of the other quest givers go?

2. Where would the newbies start if their historical home city fell? As it is now, there are only four dark cities left, counting Paineel. Neriak already has two races starting in it, what if Oggok went down? Could you put three races in Neriak? What if Paineel or Cabilis were taken out? Even if those races weren't KoS to the Inkies, how many could you squeeze into one city? What if all the dark cities were defeated at once? You know somebody would try it.

3. What if you were bound in a city that got taken out and you died? Death loop? Or would they move your bind point? If so, where? Someplace neutral like PoK? What if you don't have PoP? To whatever remaining allied city was still standing? What if you're KoS there despite the fact that your two races are on good terms? I know evil characters who've screwed their faction with another evil race by killing guards or other NPC's, or by hunting in CB when they were little.

4. Would the Outpost count? If it would, I think that would be one of the first places to go. All of those light loving, tree hugger types would be looking to avenge themselves on the Goon squad there. And that might really suck for anybody who relies on their OT hammer much.

5. What criteria would they use to decide if a city had actually been taken over? All of the NPC's being dead? That might not be all that hard in places like Oggok and Kaladim, they're not that big. But what about Freeport? They'd have to stretch the respawn rate in cities to several hours or more just to make sure that citizens weren't respawning behind you, making it impossible to conquer the larger cities. They could do that, of course, but if a raid failed, it would make a good chunk of that city useless to regular folks just trying to go about their everyday business there.

6. Who gets credit for sacking the city? What race would take possession of it, if multiple races were involved in it's demise? If all the Trolls, Ogres, Dark Elves and Iksar stormed Felwithe, what kind of NPC's would repop there? What if some turncoat High Elves helped? Would it go back to them? If a mix of light and dark races hit Gukta, would it just keep on as Gukta? Or would it become Grobb again? And if you limited a raid to just one race, the races with the highest populations would really be in the driver seat, with the more rare breeds getting kicked around a lot.

Other questions/concerns would include the fact that on those special days when these city wars are sanctioned, each city (and probably the newbie area(s) just outside its gates) would have to be made a PvP arena. That would almost certainly piss off a lot of players on blue servers who may not feel like playing PvP that day, but still want to play and have access to these zones. Plus, think of the disadvantage dark races would have. There are way more goodie-goodies than us, and it wouldn't be that hard for them to organize the forces to take down Oggok, Neriak, Paineel, Cabilis and the Outpost all on the same day. But think of how hard it would be to take out Freeport, Qeynos, Felwithe, Ak'Anon, Kelethin, Kaladim, Rivervale, Halas, Shar Val, Erud, Gukta and Firiona Vie all at once. Especially if PC's are allowed to help defend.

Besides, you can already raid city zones, if you want. You just can't keep them. And unless they add special NPC's with new quests or items to vend in the city after it's taken, why would you want to keep it? Other than taking Grobb back and chasing those slimy, bug-eyed, worm-eating freaks back to the mud holes and lilypads they crawled out from under, I can't think of any city I want that bad.
