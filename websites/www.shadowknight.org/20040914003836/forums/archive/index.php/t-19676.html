Elysea
/pet hold will make him stop attacking and return to what he was doing before he was told to attack. When I need to use him for pulling, I summon him, /pet hold, and let him follow me. I feign wherever I need to feign, and order the pet to /pet attack whichever mob I’m trying to get. As soon as his feet hit the targeting ring around the target, I hit /pet hold again and he turns around and returns to my feign spot with the mob chasing him. Once the mob is clear of his friends, I stand up and voila, I have the mob solo.

If you want to use /pet guard here for this technique, when you hit /pet hold, he should return to the spot you previously told him to guard . Think of /pet hold as a super-/pet back off + mem wipe.

Four things to remember: If you send in the pet to attack and he doesn’t immediately turn around when you hit /pet hold, it probably just means that he took a bash from the mob and is stunned. The command is working, just give Jabober a second to get his skull back on straight. Second, once you bring the mob back to your group, don’t forget to hit /pet attack again. It’s embarrassing to forget this. Third, it doesn’t always work, even if you feign before you send in the pet. The upshot is that if it doesn’t work, you should know right away. The mobs will all immediately monkey-pile on Jabober and you can let him die without consequence, just stay feigned. Fourth, an hp buff on your pet will help a great deal. Faith or Protection of the Nine is usually plenty, don’t ask your Cleric to waste a peridot on him unless you’re pulling something truly fearsome.
Thack
Actually, let me make a correction here. You do *NOT* need to feign to pull with pet, and if you are feigning, you are kinda wasting mana and time.

Pet pulling works well because of some changes that SOE made to pets a while ago. If your pet is low enough level, then if it attacks a mob, nothing around it will attack back. If you are fighing stuff that's light blue, you will get adds unless you pull up a lower level pet. Shamans and other classes that try pet pulling have to always use a lower level pet, because if they use a light blue or dark blue pet, they get adds just like anyone else that pulls.

Here are my opinions of pet pulling from quite a bit of pulling with my pet.

Pros
Single pulling most mobs that you will be fighting as a group. Especially if you don't waste time feigning, the pulls are almost as fast as just pulling with a spell.

Pet can be kept from aggroing on mobs that nuke you from a distance and making it hard to pull them close

In general pets become useful and worth casting.

Being able to clear rooms that wouldn't be possible in some circumstances. I've been in small groups (3-4 people) that have told me that we couldn't do a certain area without an enchanter. After we cleared the entire area of densly packed mobs, they ate their words and admitted how awesome I was. :)


Cons
We have very wussy pets. Ideally they pull best when they have a virtue and you have a healer healing them after every couple pulls. If you don't have a chanter in your group, this isn't bad though because it takes less to heal your pet than it would take a cleric to heal multiple people in a party when you get adds.

You have to tell your pet to attack. I lose a lot of DPS quite often because I forget to make pet engage after I backed him off.

You can't pull things that are too tough. Hard LDoN adventures for example. I have also heard that elemental level mobs can't be pet pulled easily either, although I havn't tried yet. Pet dies too fast even with virtue. Anything below elemental can be pulled though.

Sometimes if you try chain pulling and you aren't the main tank, the pet doesn't like to leave the first target you sick it on and even though you tell it to hit the new target it will sometimes go back to old pet.

Summary
I still feel that pets are well worth the AAs. I haven't found any method of pulling that is faster or more consistant. Even rangers pulling giants in BoT with harmony have resists sometimes and get adds. Pets never have this problem. If you don't pull, or if you don't care if you get more than one mob at a time, then don't get the AA for a while. If I was making another SK from scratch, I'd be doing the pet AA right after my horse, followed by the rest of my defensive AAs. Some will disagree, but in the groups I'm in, single pulling helps more than anything else, and I burn far less cleric mana if I only have one mob on me instead of two. Although it may be a pain to have clerics heal pet, I make up for it by always using Zevfeers to recharge any mana they may lose healing pet.

Pet doesn't proximity aggro if you are doing it right. I've only had one or two places ever that pet would pull more than a single mob, and with a little playing around, I could usually figure out why and get lower level pet, or do something else to get it back to single pulls. When I'm pet pulling, the ONLY time I feign is when something goes horribly wrong and pet is bringing back more mobs than what the group wants and I sacrifice my pet, feign, and then let mobs go back to their home location and try again.

I have been pet pulling for over a month now and loving every bit of it. I have pulled named mobs in BoT right out from a group of 5-6 mobs that would have aggroed if I had just pulled with spell or arrow, and I've even had a pet pull mobs right over the top of and through other mobs without any adds. In one example in particular, I used pet to pull a mob right through another mob, that when I had done the exact same pull a month earlier with pacify/harmony, the mob got close enough to another that I got an add from it. Pet pulling is much better and more reliable than harmony in most cases.

There have been times that I screwed something up and cast a spell on a mob too soon and got aggro on myself instead of my pet while it was still in aggro range of other stuff, and then I got adds that I had to deal with, but that was my own fault, and I feigned, let pet die, and got up and cast another pet after everything left.

To give you an idea of how little I have to feign, I pet pull almost single day, for at least a few hours, and on weekends even longer. When I'm pulling, I have very honestly considered getting rid of feign in favor of another spell while pulling because it's so unnecessary. I havent actually done it yet because feign has saved my butt so many times in the years I've played a SK that it's hard to want to give it up, but I can count the number of times I've had to feign on a pull on one hand and still have fingers left over.

I suppose that if you sit when pulling with a pet you can screw it up, but I've never tried that and don't really know why you'd want to. The trivial amount of mana you'd gain for the couple seconds that you pet pull is insigificant, especially if you use MC, like I do.

The main thing that I was trying to get across is this. Some people have the mistaken impression that you need to feign death, send pet in to attack, bring pet back, get up, and fight, when all you need to do is send the pet in and skip the feign death part. Feign can always help save your butt if you screw something up, but if you are pulling properly you won't need feign like some people felt, and if you are getting adds from the pet being too high of level, you are going to get those adds whether you are feigned or not, so there isn't any advantage to using feign regularly on pulls.
