The myth about the first person entering the zone is untrue.

It calculates the average lvl of the group and sets the dungeon accordingly. Kind of like this :

I everyone is 52-59 you will get mobs around lvl 52/53.

But if you have a group of 56-62 the mobs will be around 56.(and you will notice some will summon.

This is why it is very important to watch out when doing a hard mission. EX. If you do a hard assassination and your lvls are kind of spread(tank is 57, all others are 59-61) your tank is screwed. But if its the cleric or say mage thats lower you wont have as much to worry about.

Also carefull when getting a puller, thier pulling spells may not work on the mobs you are pulling. Level is important to watch when grouping.

My suggestion is to keep the lvls within 5 of eachother. Not the allowed 7. Unless your doing a group below say 45.

Ramgar SK
Why to avoid rescues:

The rescuee will attack/be attacked by anything it sees on its way out. This means you cannot pacify/invis by mobs at all if time starts to run out, like you can in an assassination.

They will not also follow you directly, and will stop and wander in rooms on the way out.

All other missions depend on the groups actions for completion, rescues depend on an NPC. Cleaning out the whole dungeon will win all other adventures, not a rescue. You can still fail trying to get them to follow you out.

Here's what I've observed in LDON. I'm currently working on ~100 missions and I've only lost on collections(2 before they fixed drop rates) and two really bad pick up groups. With that said:

1) 1st person in determines level.... NOT TRUE. It's all based on group average level. I know for a fact that if you get group average level to <63 it will be the second highest dungeon (hard missions not included). If you get 63 or over you are in the toughest normal dungeon.

2) Collections / slaughter are the easiest to win. Have fun with them. Assassinate is one of my favorites but finding the mob can occassionally prove challenging even with my favorite ranger (I hate guk's layout - rarely a problem though) Eqtoolbox has some decent maps. Little lacking on good miragul maps though. Rescues are my least favorite. Had a few issues with the rescued mobs pathing.

3) Not going to go back and dig out this gem of a quote about shaman not being set up for LDON but that's not really true friend. Shaman are pretty darn useful. Quite honestly any group makeup works unless you are doing 65 dungeons on the hard setting. Then it becomes pretty important on who you bring. Even then some of my shammy friends are preferred members /shrug.

Be clear about everyone's role BEFORE getting into the dungeon. The original post had some great advise. Still not sure if his/her bard friend quite gets it about pet classes and DPS but that's not important .:)
