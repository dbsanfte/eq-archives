I usually keep harmshield up for raids, in case I get called upon to pull a hard hitting enemy that isn't aggroed on anybody else or get asked to pull a large train in some of the easier areas. Aura of Hate depends on the group I'm in, if they want it I mem it (assuming we're fighting enemies that aren't immune to the nuke). Pets are situational, but with Pet Discipline I'd say if you are allowed to have it up, definitely have it up. Having a snare up is good, just be careful you don't dot something that needs to be mezzed. If you're fighting enemies that are immune to fear (basically any mob 55 or higher), mem fear and use it for aggro. Ignore the terrors and the voices unless specifically asked for voice. In general our DoTs aren't that great, if you mem any just use Blood of Hate. One or two lifetaps is a good idea in case you eat a riposte or are taking AE damage. Definitely Zevfeer's if you can land it. Spell lineup is situational in some respects, but you have a pretty good handle on it it seems.

For buffs, agility is nearly worthless and is often the first buff I click off. Strength is nice to have if you don't have max or nearly max strength (sorry, didn't check your magelo), as well as any stamina and dex increasing spells assuming you aren't maxed in those either. The number of buff slots you should leave open depends on the encounter, most times one or two is sufficient. Attack buffs are great to have, regen is nice but usually not necessary. Obviously you want any buff that gives you hitpoints, mana regen and haste, for hitpoints Po9, Fo7, Symbol, Brell, Cloak is usually the way to go, while for mana regen having both the Enchanter buff and the Beastlord buff is nice.
