Size means nothing as to what a guild can or can't do or how they do it. Really, it doesn't. You can't say guildX is better than guildY becouse they have 200 members.

Ancient Council on Saryrn has around 160 active members. But, we see maybe 30 or 40 online at a given time, if we're lucky, usually. Well over half of our guild is casual players with a core of about 20 powergamers and 30 or 40 more every-other-day players. Most of our members have families and don't dedicate 6 hours nightly to EQ. Heck, most of them don't even log in nightly, or daily, or whatever. We can go 3 or 4 days and only see 20 people on then all of a sudden, 60 will show up to a raid. It's pot-luck. Most of our raids average 35 to 50 people depending on the weekday and time of day/night and general luck.

As for accomplishments. We've done Cazic Thule with 48 people. We've done all of west wing temple of veeshan. Our first WToV named was Gozzrem. We had no DB casters in the guild and the night we finally won against him we had 38 people on the raid most of them under level 60 (12 were level 60 including me). The fight took 16 minutes (hahahaha, how gay). We've also done monk epic with 1 group all the way through, as well as Feydedar for druid epic with 12 people, woushi with 18, koralek dar with 5 people, lodizal with 3, Zlandicar with 23, doljo with 10, velkator with 28 and one GB caster, PoF DT golems with 13 and only one cleric, PoH mini's with one group even Maestro, Innoruuk with 18. Sometimes it's harder to get lower numbers. Zlandicar or something like him spawns and we can usually get 36+ people and usually do.

Rather than guild size I think it's more of a "who do we have" kind of thing. Is it the hardcore, know-it-all players showing up or is it the casual players who aren't as skilled? Is it all level 60's or is half your raid level 50 to 53? Who you have determines what numbers you need, not vice versa, and that determines how many people it takes you to kill a mob. It also dictates what strategies you use. Is it to be a duration battle or a DPS battle, for example. Through all of the server competition we finally, FINALLY found Lendiniara the Keeper alive this past weekend and for our first ever attempt, 0 deaths and she died in 7 minutes. Not bad I'd say. Especially considering that for once, our Kael aligned guild beat 3 other dragon aligned guilds to her who can walk right up to her without issue.

Accomplishments are marked by your guild and your guild alone. It's nice to know people are killing things outside of your guild and when they do it (if you need to keep track of death/spawn times) but other than that just worry about yourself and your guild. You'll be much better off for it in the long run.
