Hi there...

I'm currently a 54 ogah SK. Pulling.....

If i am to pull with spells, i use something with a fast cast time, that also has a small agro radius... Something like Disease Cloud.
However... with the new lines of spells that have been introduced i have found a bow to be faster and more efficient. Being an oger means a small mana pool... its hard enough to have enough mana for the things you want to do, let alone have to use it to pull with.

Pulling with a spell
- requires you to stand still
- requires a cast time
- nocks out your spell gems for a second or so

Pulling with a bow
- you can move while firing
- you dont even have to hit the mob
- you can cast strait after with no delay

I got my very 1st bow at lvl 52... at the moment i pull for groups in PoI and PoD mostly (where i spend most of my time nowadays). The tactic i use is simple (only good if your MT aswell) and effective.. wouldnt be possible if i was casting...
target mob, fire arrow, cast terror of darkness (agro increasor).
Its fast cast time, and the agro generated from the arrow damage, increased by the spell hitting the mob... means that the mob can be slowed before it even hits me when i get back to camp.

Personally, i prefer to use a bow due to: mana conservation; instant fire; mobility; and availabillity of the FD gem as soon as you see the mobs moving.

Thats my 2cp i hope it helps.

I used to carry a bow with me religiously, and arrows (or several stacks of arrows).

Once I got a really decent ranged item, though, I got rid of the bow and arrows. It freed up several inventory slots, and it made it so that I didn't have to swap my great ranged item with the bow all the damned time (translation = laziness).

I have used spells to pull once I stopped carrying the bow (since about level 45) and haven't looked back, and still am not the slightest bit interested in finding another bow - unless it has stats better than my ranged item, of course.

I usually use Abduction of Strength to pull, and found that its range is very good, fast cast time, and it only uses 5 mana - which means I'd have to cast it about 20 times before I could see my mana bar move. ;)

No worrying about getting/keeping arrows.
No worrying about swapping bow in/out with my ranged item.
And of course the benefit is the 'pre-emptive debuff', meaning that I have stolen the target's str before the mob is even to the group, which gives me time to cast other debuffs, as well.

Some folks swear by the bow, and use theirs religiously, and I respect that. I suggest you try both ways each for a few days and see what you're more comfortable with. ;)

ADVANTAGES of pulling with SPELL:
* Doesn't need Line of Sight - You can turn around and be ready to run while you cast.
* Works better when there is vertical difference between you and target - If you're on hilly terraine bows seem to get the 'target is too far above or below you' message.
* You don't need to swap out ranged items if your bow doesn't have major stats on it.
* You can't miss and aggro something on the floor above you by accident.
* You don't have to worry about running out of arrows
* You don't need a 'ranged attack' button

DISADVANTAGES of pulling with SPELL:
* You have to stand still while casting
* You have a delay before you can cast again
* Range on spells isn't as good as range expensive bow/arrow combos
* You use up mana and might have to rest occassionally
* Some mobs are highly magic resistant and spells might not cause much aggro for pulling

That seems to about sum it up for me. I own a bow, but I have hardly ever used it. I generally pull with spells, since it works most of the time. I'll generally pull with a strength tap spell or a lifetap or something that's a single shot, non-dot spell. If you pull with DoTs and have to FD, you have to wait for it to wear off before you can stand back up or you'll reaggro everything. Also if you pull with DoTs to a group with an enchanter or something, they can't mez things.

However, once I was hunting with a warrior friend of mine and he had put a lot of points into fletching and had a good bow and some really good arrows. Stuff that was out of spell range for me, and nearly beyond my clip plane that I could barely see... he could hit them with a bow. I think it's been fixed in a recent patch, but he claimed he could be so far from a target that he could kill it with archery and still be outside it's aggro range.

Outdoors bows might be better, but indoors it helps to not have the bother of setting up Line of Sight for a bow. You can cast around a corner, through a wall a lot of times, etc.

Like everything, it's situational.

- Minta
