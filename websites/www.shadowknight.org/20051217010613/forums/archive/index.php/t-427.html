Correct Sekmeth, it's something we grabbed along the way, halfway between an accident and a way to balance our extra abilities (leeching stats/doting).

It's only very recently that VI started actually increasing the hate directly as an obvious main concern, like with the enchanter trickster buff or with the effect on blade of carnage/hategiver.
When you look at our old spell book, ALL our spells have a primary focus, be it sucking life, slowing movement, doing damage... the taunt potential is just here to compensate that beneficial (for us) effect.
Later with kunark they gave us spells in pretty much the same lines, only more effective, but still none of them focused on agroing.
In velious we recieved Death peace and Shroud of Death, increasing our survivability.
In luclin we recieved low agro spells (torrents), a self buff...

None of our spells is designed as a taunt, they are all designed to enhance our melee abilities with spells.
Our spells happen do draw lots of agro, and we use it to our advantage, but we've never been designed that way.

We are designed to be independant killing machines, with a lower damage output, but able to do a lot without help (be it soloing, or without a cleric watching us too closely on raids), lifetaps, feign death, self buffs...
The same way paladins are designed to have a lower damage but are helping others survive, they got wave of prexus for this, and it just happens to increase their survivability as a side effect, but this was not the main concern.
