And once again, no one read the post... he was asking about mitigation. IE: How much damage you DON'T TAKE on a hit from a mob...

As far as I know, only AC and Class effect Damage Mitigation.

There is, I believe, a separate mitigation table for Plate classes now vs. monks, rangers, etc, so that we take less damage on average successful mob attack than the other classes, as is due to plate wearing classes. Actually, I think it might just be for Knight/Warrior classes. I don't know how it works for Clerics and Bards.

But I do know that this was talked about in the nerf on monks a few months ago.

AC can be affected by armor, level and to a smaller extent AGI.

But as far as mitigation differences for race, I have never heard that it makes a difference. Logically, this would be a pretty hefty advantage to give to a specific race, and would seriously imbalance the races. Of course, what you may be seeing as a "racial" mitigation penalty may indeed be due to the starting stats of the races. If you have seriously low starting AGI and do not raise it above the 75 point minimum for max. AC efficiency, then you may be noticing a pointed penalty to your mitigation. It might seem that it is a racial penalty, especially at lower levels, when gear stat boosts aren't able to overcome starting stat deficiencies, but it's probably not. If you (as a Troll SK) and your Troll warrior friend are both noticing bigger than expected/normal hits versus a similarly leveled and equipped character of a different race, check your AGI and your AC. Chances are you are either under 75 AGI or have significantly lower AC than the other character.
