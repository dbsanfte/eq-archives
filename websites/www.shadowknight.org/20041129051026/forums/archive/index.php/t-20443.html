int = int
fizzl = percentage of attempted casts fizzled
lv = spell level
fa = spell fizzle adjustment number
cast = number of cast attempts
spell = spell name

these were all done as a 65 SK.
what this shows:

higher int significantly decreases fizzle rate.
going from 119 int to 280 int decreased the number of spells fizzled from 19.39% to 5.83% on pact of hate.

fizzles use about 40% of a spells casting cost in mana... so rough approximations of mana lost:

20% fizzle rate: 9% mana wasted
10% fizzle rate: 4% mana wasted
5% fizzle rate: 2% mana wasted

so... going from 120 int to 280 int would probably end up saving you more mana than mana preservation IV.

Hi again,

Now, I am not meaning to be an asshole about this or anything, but since I was a professional statistician for several years it is something I still tend to take seriously. You have done a bunch of work here collecting data and, that is admirable. I am only trying to suggest some things that would prevent spurious conclusions here.

Just a clue here. I don’t want to tell people what to do and I would not be into doing all this analysis that people do on parses because I think it takes the mystery out of the game. However, if you are interested in improving your analysis, the first step would be to look into the analysis of binomial trials, otherwise known as Bernoulli trials. My cursory examination of your table lead me to believe that there is something other than intelligence involved in the fizzle rates depicted. Now that is not to say that INT is not a significant or even the primary exogenous variable that influences fizzle rates. I only say that it may not be.

As an example, you conducted 2,220 trials at the same INT level for nullify magic and shadow sight. Even thought the INT level was the same you recorded a difference in fizzle rates of more than 9.5 percentage points. On a cursory level this is an enormous difference and the difference would be exacerbated if you calculated the standard deviations of these trials. What this means is, that there is something else that is extremely significant in the calculation for fizzle rates imbedded in the game.

Anyway, I would be glad to give you suggestions as I think your objective is an admirable one.
