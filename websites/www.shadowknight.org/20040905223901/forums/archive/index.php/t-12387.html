Lizarde
I would agree with the people who advise that you try the one you think would be the most fun. Probably the biggest convenience you get with an Iksar is infravision. While you get spells to deal with this later, it's nice not to have to stop and cast them every now and then when you enter a dark zone.

Iksar regen is very nice, especially at low levels where mobs aren't doing a lot of damage. You can level very quickly because you spend less time resting between battles.

Iksar miss out on a number of nice plate armors until they get to their 30s and can start hunting for Ry'Gorr. Dreadscale is nice but is, IMO, a much bigger pain in the ass to quest than Darkforge. And later on, Iksar cultural is horrible compared to human cultural armor.

I think Iksar look cool, though I never really warmed to the plate look on Iksar SKs. Human's in plate look great, IMO. I prefer the Iksar look overall though, and never regretted my choice.

Faction is no longer an issue, especially with the PoP expansion. You don't need to do any faction work to train or bank in PoK. You didn't need to do faction work to bank in shadow haven or shar vahl. You only need to do a couple of really easy quests to buy/sell in shadow haven, and can buy/sell without any faction work in PoK or shar vahl.

Exp penalty can be a real drag. If you choose troll or iksar, be aware that you will level slower than others. That is probably the biggest reason why I would say go with the one that you think is most fun. Because if you don't enjoy playing an iksar, you're going to be spending 20% more time not enjoying him. :D
Toltanus Thule
I've played both, and in fact I have a 56 Iksar Sk laying around that I am thinking of resurrecting because Erudites are overrated IMHO..

Pros I have found with Iksar:
Regen, AC Bonus, Infravision.

Forage is forever stuck at level 50 and it's not to be used except in dire emergencies.

The quests in Cabilis are phenomenal, way better than the newbie quests that Human SKs get. There is a reason why people slaughter countless Sarnaks and Explorers just to be able to get inside Cabilis and that's Greenmist. But if you do the whole thing as an Iksar and use the quests as a guide to where to get XP, you will find it to be that much more rewarding. At least I did.

A lot of people think being KOS in the old world has a negative effect, but most of the places you are going to want to get to as a Shadowknight can be fixed with faction work, like Neriak. Aside from that, there is nothing the old world offers an Iksar that can't be bought in Cabilis or these days, in the Plane of Knowledge and it's a good enough excuse to just go and kill everything anyway. ;) I guess it would be cool to bank in places like Kelethin or Felwithe, but in all honesty, who the hell cares? If you really want it, the faction work is not impossible.

The armor that Iksars can't wear are offset in the low and mid levels by things such as the questable Dreadscale armor, which is equivalent to Darkforge, Greenmist armor, which is equivalent to Blood Ember, and in between you have armors such as Rygorr Chain that should do just fine for you, not to mention the Velious armor quests. You'll be fearkiting a lot anyway as you level and solo in the 40+ range, so keep that in mind when you think of how important armor is to you.

On the other hand, the new Iksar character models are about the ugliest thing around (and it's great how the Iksars suddenly go to looking NOTHING like the Iksar statues all over Kunark), and the armor looks like ass, including that they used the female helm look for the males. You can turn it off on your end, but if people have it on on their end, you'll look like a fag.

As far as humans, the whole vision thing can be overcome with judicious use of the gamma adjustor and you also get Infravision and Ultravision spells as a Shadowknight, so I don't think the whole vision thing is any kind of problem in the long run. You can wear just about all the armors Shadowknights can, you have more cities that you start non-KOS to, you can use the Outpost if you need to, you can faction yourself to non-KOS in Cabilis and work on Greenmist, and your new model and armor looks cool. Oh, and you level faster, about 20% faster, which is a yellow bubble faster.
