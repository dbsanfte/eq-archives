Things to know on raids.

1. Getup well ahead of posted raid time 2-1 hours before. Take a shower. Drink
some coffee, eat some breakfast... in other words give yourself time to wake up.

2. Show up at meeting point 1 hour to 30 mins before raid time. If theres a chat
channle join it and let raid leader know your there.

3. Sit down and listen. Nothing is more annoying than having scrolls of spam
run by when the raid leader is assigning groups. Know who is in your group.
Group leaders need to appoint somone in group to be secondary leader if they
go link dead... it's just a precation so no one freaks out during a fight.

4. Listen to pre-raid prep instructions.

5. Listen to your group leader. If your group leader says stfu then stfu, if your
group leader says sit your arse down then sit your arse down.

6. Sit your arse down. Do NOT run around, don't even move, hell don't even
BREATH. It distracts people that are keeping an eye out for wandering mobs
and those that are planing the next move. 'But we're in a safe spot.' FYI there
is NO SAFE SPOT. I don't care if a mob never even paths throu that zone...
SIT THE FREAK DOWN. This also goes for that duck-crackhead-n00b walk
people do. Cut it out, sit your arse down. People notice when you do it and
they remember and they think 'Gawd if he/she ever did that in such and such
zone we'd be dead.' So it distracts people and they begin a spiral of untrust
just because YOU couldn't sit your friggin arse down.

7. Spam. Keep it in groups. Only spam that should be flying across the chat
is MA's message, SA's message, Pullers message (including tagger), Debuffs,
Snare. No one cares if your assist message is cool or not and no one wants to
see it. Your assist keys should be this and only this: /assist MAname and
/assist SAname thats it, NO message, NO 'i'm helping MAname beat on a %t'
NO ONE WANTS TO SEE IT. Casters only call out messages IF it lands. Same
as debuffers and what not. Reason? Clerics and Casters can't see messages
when they have to scroll up to see what has been cast and clerics missed the
CH cycle message and most important you can't see that Mob_A has been
mezed message. Result DEAD people. So do yourselfs a favor and get rid
of the messages that go along with your assist key.

8. PAY ATTENTION. Can not stress this enough. If your afk don't tell just
the group tell the whole dam raid. If your in tell hell go afk and message can't
talk now. If your gettin to much spam from gu then say to gu /gu going off gu
for a bit. You should be on only ONE chat channel (unless you have one set
up for group leaders, puller team.. ect). Chat channel is NOT auct channel.

9. Down time, rest time. During long raids there will be down time and time
that half or all of the raid goes afk. Group Leaders if Move is called and
there is still someone afk then make sure it is known that person is afk.
Now for those that are afk and raid moves. DO NOT MOVE. Stand still.
Let them know you are back and someone will come get you or tell you if
the way is clear. If you move you might train raid killing them or get yourself
killed, so to be safe just STAND STILL.

10. LD's. Group leader ONLY call LD's, if it's the group leader that goes ld then
the person chosen to lead group call's ld. If you go link dead and come back
and everyone is gone DO NOT MOVE. Stand still. Let them know you are
back and someone will come get you or tell you if the way is clear. If you
move you might train raid killing them or get yourself killed, so to be safe
just STAND STILL.

11. Stick to the plan. On raids there are certain objectives. Once those objectives
are meet then you can change your plans according to mobs available and zone
loot. Changing plans mid-raid is a sure way to get you and everyone killed.
If plan is to go in and kill some green mob don't treat it as anything but a lvl 60
warrior/wizzard with mana burn. Even a lvl 60 can be zerg killed by green mobs.

12. Experience. Forget it. Don't even think of it, don't even mention it. On a raid
experience DOES NOT EXSIST. Whining about experience detracts from the
focus of the raid. It leads to rushed pulls and bad mistakes and eventually to
death and even raid wipe outs. If you have a problem keep it to your self and
take it up with the raid leader later.

13. Loot. Know what is in the zone for you. It's real simple. Find a web page and
print out the list of items that you can use for race/class. There is absolutely no
reason why someone should spend 3 mins spamming stats on a item that you
should already know about. Other part of this is KNOW what you want out of
the drop list. Lotto's should take NO MORE than half the mobs decay rate. Do
NOT whine and complain about how there are no drops for your race/class in
zone whatever. We do not care. Doing so makes you look bad to everyone and
the only thing we'll remember is that your a punk whining about loot, not if you
single handedly saved the entire raid.

14. Most important. HAVE FUN.
