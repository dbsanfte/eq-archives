Laronx
Listen up warriors, it is no secret that your class has problems right now, it sucks to be a wrrior, especially if you aren't the main tank in a raiding guild. We agree with you. We agree that you need a better role in xp groups, however, you do not need *our* role. There is no denying that most groups will turn away multiple warriors while waiting for a knight, and this is wrong. Honestly though, it has nothing to do with our tanking abilities, and is all about aggro. That's it, aggro, 100%. Warriors are still the kings of hp, ac, and mana efficiency for the healers, but only if aggro is maintained. Therin lies the rub, as knights are pretty close to you in your specialty, but you are nowhere close to us in ours. But is that really all there is to it?

I don't think so, but let me start off by saying this: We agree that groups holding out for knights to tank is wrong. On the flip side we believe it would be wrong if they held out for warriors as well. Ideally if a group is looking for a tank, the first one they come across should be fine, be it sk, warrior, or paladin. That being said, how many of you realize that a lot of groups hold out much more than that? I have seen xp groups take beastlords, rangers, and shaman over warriors without even blinking. It is really that bad. I also play a cleric with a group of freinds that gets together and just grinds xp and shoots the shit, none of which is a warrior or a knight, and it's almost a foregone conclusion that between the beastlord and the shammy, we don't really need a "tank" of any kind. Added to the fact that no matter what, once you have one of either (knight, war), adding a second of either is almost unheard of, yet how many times have you xp'd with more than one wizzie? more than one druid? a ranger and a druid? You get the point.

This points to the fact that even lock tight snap aggro probably won't change a thing. You need much more than that. I've said it before, and I'll say it a gain, it would be a disservice to you as a class to accept simply either copying one of our abilities, or nerfing us as a "fix" for your class. Changes are needed on a much grander scale for you, because believe it or not, knights are pretty balanced to every other class in the game. As a matter of fact, with the exception of warriors (and maybe that hit monks just a little *too* hard with the nerf bat..hehe nah!) the classes are all fairly balanced against each other. Also, we are balnced in relation to the monsters in the game, that means that we lose effectiveness across the board, even if we're soloing, even if we're in a group that has a freakin warrior MT and we arent taking shit from anyone, what it means is that group will kill slower, and thus be at a greater risk of dieing, and everyone loses in the end. They aren't going to screw all that up just for one class, and they fucking know it, which is why exactly jack shit has improved for you in 3+ expansions.

You need to fight for fundamental changes to the game, and stop singling out our class. I firmly believe that warriors can become a viable class, somehow, without infringing on knights at all.

I will give you a few examples of changes, these are fundamental changes to the game though and may seem kind of extreme, but that's what you need.

First, everyone agrees that the idea of knights being true hybrids was thrown out the window long ago. However, at a basic programming level, we still have a lot more in common with our warrior side. This shouldn't be, we don't share any kinds of ac/attk/etc. tables with casters, why should we share any with warriors. Warriors should get their own, their ac should be calculated differently, their ac should be calculated differently, as differently as an sk's is from a necros. This would stop any further benefit for knights when they are just trying to tweak melee. Not only that, but you should have a completely set of tables than other pure melee. Monks already have some seperate tables, so in effect it would seperate rouges, monks and warriors totally.

Necros do far more damage than sk's if each only use spells, and if the sk casts and melee's the necro still outperforms him in sustained dps, which is the advantage of being a "pure" class, and theoretically losing some utility. The same should be true for warriors, you should definately outdamage us if we are both only straight meleeing, which with the difference in skill caps, is basically the way it is. However, you should in all honesty do more *sustained* dps than us even when we use spells, as should be a benefit of you also being a "pure" class, it's that way in relation to the other pure melee, rouges and monks definately do more sustained dps, but they can't tank. That's not really that important though, and I'll explain why in a second.

I have lightly touched on two things I'd like to address at this point, the first, tanking doesn't stack. This is a problem, like I mentioned before, there is exactly no reason to have an extra warrior or knight if they aren't tanking. Yeah they can add a little dps, but you could pick 5 classes that you would put before us in that regard. Maybe offtank, but unless you're absolutely sure there will be many adds, a second shammy or bl could could tank infrequently enough, and they would be much more beneficial when they weren't by dotting, back up healing, slowing, etc. So theres another fundamental flaw, tanking needs to stack, and it doesn't, fixing this benefits any tank class, warrior paladin, or sk.

The second thing, I think in comparisons to what warriors can and can't do, I have mentioned every class in the game. There is a reason for that. Every class in the game has abilities that make them unique, and in certain circumstances the best there is at a certain thing. Yes, I know, warriors have defensive. I'll say this right now, it's a turd, I will do us all a favor and act like it doesn't exist, using it to argue as a favorable ability for warriors is wrong. Think of it this way, if disc makes you take half damage, than in theory you just cut the number of warriors you need in half as well. I'll get back to that point later. The point about abilities that I am trying to make is that even the other so called "pure melee" have abilities that make them more usefull than warriors. Monks have fd, mend, rouges have backstab, sneak, etc. And almost everyone does more damage than warriors. In every way warriors are inferior to someone, I don't even really know why anyone would roll one up at this point.

This discrepancy is all about utility. There are many different facets of utility, I will break them down here into the major categories.

1) The "save my ass" utility. This gives certain classes the ability to save them and only them in case of emergency. Some classes get a form of harmshield, that at least helps them get to the zone to save their own ass. Some classes get a self only gate to bind, if you're going to your bind anyway this is always the better option. Some classes get fd, others can at least root or snare a mob, some others can at least invis. The point is, that even though none of these are guaranteed life savers, every single other class has at least one form of it, at least you have a chance. What do warriors get? If you're lucky some jboots and an OT hammer...

2) The second is the "Shit hit the fan" utility. Not every class gets this one, but many do. Wether it's evac, group heal, LoH, or even the occaisonal AE blind or stun, almost every class has at least one ability that could be used in a pinch to make sure that even if your group can't kill it, it won't kill you. What can a warrior do? Die first most likely.

3) The next is the "OMG I PWNed that mob" utility. This is the burst dps utility. Manaburn, Harmtouch, Backstab, Dragon Kicks, AM3, even spell crits. These are the eye candy that makes killing stuff fun, and the majority of classes have some form or another of this ability, and the ability to have it reset fairly quickly. What do warriors have? That crappy disc that makes you hit a whole bunch of times for like 12-17 damage? I have never seen a warrior use that disc without it being followed by someone in the group typing LOL.

4) The next is situational utility. This is the ability for almost any class to join a group that already has someone filling the main role they are intended for and still reasonably contribute to the group. This includes changes in tactics that are available inherently from having a spell book. Any casting class has many more spells than they can mem, so if someone is already doing their main job, it is much easier for them to adjust their spell line up, and still bring sometyhing to the table that benefits the group. I will include the ability to cast pets in this category, but only for the classes that aren't inherently dfined by pets, necro, beastlord, mage, etc. For other classes that have low damage, in effect "extended dots" for pets, cleric hammer, shammy pet, sk pet, etc. This is situational, a lot of times these classes, if they have the ability to use their pets in group, they are most likely not performing their primary function, i.e., an sk might not have his pet up if he's pulling, cleric might not cast hammer if he is the main healer. What else can a warrior do if he's not the MT, umm exactly the same thing he would be doing anyway?


5) Next is general buffs. Finally, something warriors benefit from. Hp, Ac, haste, regen, Ch, this is the stuff that defines warriors. Unfortunately, even though warriors recieve none of the other benefits that I listed above, the one that they recieve the best boost to their best usefullness, *everyone in the game gets exactly the same benefit* This is why you see shammys tanking, they have decent ac, enough hp's that they can usually live at least long enough for the cleric to get a ch off, and they recieve every single one of the benefits I listed above.

So, even if you gave warriors the one unique ability of snap aggro, you would still be lacking in all around utility enough to still be getting passed over to tank. Then what? You have snap aggro, and the most hp/ac, but the LFG pally has enough hp/ac to tank, still can hold aggro well enough, and can heal buff, hmm, I see the pally still getting lots of groups. Or that LFG sk, he can tank well enough too, and we need a puller, or he can feed us mana, you still lose that group to the sk as well. Add to that, the sk and pally may well end up in the same group, but two warriors in the same group would still be useless. The sk and the pally can group because they have more utility, even if the pally is holding aggro, and being mt, the sk still has adds more to the group than a warrior would.

One simple "aggro lock fix" simply will not do it. You need much more than that, and it may very well be that whatever you gain would infringe on many other classes, I don't really see how it couldn't. I think all the other classes should be ready to accept this. I don't however feel you should "steal" a role from some other class, just so you can have a role.

I say you guys need a fairly substantial dps gain. Every class that can deal dps, with the exception of priests, has the ability in the right circumstance to be the one at the time doing the most damage. Why can't a warrior have a manaburn-harmtouchish ability? Maybe it could burn their hp bar like it does for wizzie mana. It could take 12 secs to do it, just to keep the cleric on his toes. Why can't a warrior have some form of pet? Almost every class has one in some form or another, why can't they have a war dog or something, that is only good for situationasl use. My idea would be to have the warrior pet basically do a "shield" type thing automatically when the warrior is attacked, so that it takes all the damage first and dies quick with a long reuser time, so if the warrior is MT it wouldnt be that useful, but in a secondary role, it would give them utility, which is how it works for any non pet defined class in a secondary role. Why can't a warrior have some kind of "save my ass" ability? Why can't they have a big badass attack, like backstab and monk kicks? Why does everyone get the same benefit from buffs? Why can't warriors get a skill that scales upwards as they level that makes them get an inherent bonus from certain types of spells? Maybe it should just cost healers flat out more mana to heal anyone but a warrior.

Should they get all of these abilities? Hell no. The fact is though that every has class has at least one of the main abilities I laid out above, and a lot have 2 and 3. As it stands right now, not only is there no reason to have a wariior in your xp group over a knight, there's really no reason to have a warrior in your xp group at all. It is time that warriors got to at least share in some of the basic utility. No other class should cry foul if they give warriors a few new abilities that happen to come from our bag of tricks. Keeping in mind they shouldn't gain so much of any one that it overshadows them, but a few from each to make a unique whole. Every class out there shares some of it's utility roles with another class, and it hasn't killed them, so let's not bitch when warriors get a few toys too. Yes, some of it may be eye candy, but I think one of the things that sucks about being a warrior is that no matter what, for the entire life of your character and your career as a warrior, you will push maybe 4 or 5 buttons. Let's face it, even some of the meaningless toys at least let you have a little fun, and that is after all why we play this game. I do think that a few fundamental changes will really benefit you though.

All in all these are the things I think will be most important to you becoming a viable class:

1) Make tanking stack. Do this by giving warriors at least enough utility to fill in some secondary roles if they are not MT. Also, they may want to make stamina actually mean something, maybe after a certain amount of time, your stamina is depleted and you have to rest. Either the group can do nothing, or the secondary tank can take a few. This would be equivelent to the cleric having to med and the druid taking over heals.

2) Make warriors the best tank again. On paper they are, in terms of the highest ac, and most hp's, but having the "most" really doesn't mean anything more than the 5 or 6 other classes that have "enough to do the job". Make the difference inthe way warriors take damage over anyone else totally different, make it matter. Make a warrior be so much more mana efficient to heal that it really matters.

3) Run screaming from defensive disc and never look back. At the very least it will increase the need for warriors on raids, and that will filter down to the rest of the game.

4) Give warriors a few toys and maybe a little survivability. This by no means should be the only or even a real important thing, but more stuff to do just makes the game more fun.

5) Make /shield better. This is one thing you have thats actually a decent idea, especially for xp groups, make it more worth using, maybe even more of a direct exchange of hitpoints between you and the person you are shielding. And here's a crazy idea- add some decent aggro to it. This could be a much more defining ability or warriors. You would have the ability to save a caster and grab aggro back. This could be your "shit hit the fan" utility, it should have a decently long refresh time, so that like most other classes "shit hit the fan" ability, and you can't really plan your group strategy around it.

5) Don't steal anyones role. Snap aggro is ours, it always should be. Perhaps there should be a way for knights to dish aggro more effectively. On a personal note, his is the one defining ability for knights IMO. It is the favorite thing about my class that as a shadow knight, I can do something so evil and vile to a monster that they just can't ignore it. Likewise for a paladin to call down a can of holy whupass that they just can't ignore. You shouldn't be trying to take our role anymore than you should be trying to become the main healer. Knights technically arent the best tanks, that should be your role and we don't want it. Unfortunately, we are the best tanks that can do a bunch of other stuff, and thats the problem. Above all they need to make being the biggest, toughest, meanest piece of meat on the block mean something.

I know this has been a long post, thanks if you suffered through it, it's nothing more than my opinions, if I really knew how to fix balance issues in video games I would be getting paid to do it. The main thing I hope you take from this post is that we support you wanting fixed, but in all fairness you signed up to be a warrior, and that means nthing but being the one able to take the most punishment out of all the classes, you should stop at nothing short of having that actually mean something again.
