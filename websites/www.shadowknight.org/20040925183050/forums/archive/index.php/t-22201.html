Marruk
No real point in buying plat. As was already mentioned, the newbie armor can carry you for a decent period, plus you can always earn a fair amount of plat in the course of your ordinary leveling; you just need to know what to look for.

Read up on your tradeskills and find out what are the most popular paths to quick skill-ups. Then find some tradeskill items used in those recipes that require low-level farming. Farm them while you still get exp for it, and then sell it in the bazaar (assuming you have SoL).

Animal pelts, spider/spiderling silks, swirling shadows, and many foraged items can make you enough plat to get some decent gear.

Rumor has it that having your epic will make it slightly easier to get epic 1.5 or 2.0. Given what a pain the current SK epic is, though, I'm going to risk doing 1.5/2.0 from scratch.

I'd advise against buying plat simply because it's soo easy to make a ton of it in game.

Levels 1-5, go kill spiderlings in Feerrott/Innothule Swamp/Everfrost and sell the silk for a small fortune.

Levels 6-18, go to Kurn's Tower and collect/sell the bone chips for another small fortune.

Levels 19-24 is East Karana time for spider silk. These also sell for a pretty plat or two.

Level 25-30 you can take on greyhoppers in Marus Seru and sell the hides.

Congrats, by the time you ding level 31 there should be a couple thousand plat in your bank account and several thousand plat worth of gear on your toon.
