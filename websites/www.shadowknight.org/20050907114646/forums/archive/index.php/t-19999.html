Originally posted by Dakkin
Same appears to work when taunting mobs off people who've been offtanking for a while. Taunt-and-hit on its own often doesn't help, but successful taunt followed by a good aggro spell works every time.

Not sure why this is.
Because of the way taunt works. A successful taunt is (current_highest_aggro)+1.

If you taunt successfully and attack, your aggro would be (former_highest_aggro)+1+(melee_round).

If you were to replace melee_round with a high aggro spell, like fear or CD, your total aggro would be substantially higher than if you just taunted and attacked.

Example 1:
Paladin offtanks mob, builds 3000 hate
SK taunts successfully, now has 3001 hate
Paladin swings a round of melee, has 3100 hate
SK swings a round of melee, has 3101 hate
Result: aggro ping-pong

Example 2:
Paladin offtanks mob, builds 3000 hate
SK taunts successfully, now has 3001 hate
Paladin swings a round of melee, has 3100 hate
SK casts fear, has 3500 hate
Result: solid aggro lead

Numbers are of course hypothetical, but do you see the point?
