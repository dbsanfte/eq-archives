Bertie was the hardest encounter we faced in progressing to the Elemental Planes.

His rampage is normal single target rampage, but his melee range is absolutely huge.

This makes positioning him a bit of a pig. What we did, which may not be the best way, but it worked.... SK with HS on, runs up to Bertie, moons him, then runs into the corner neat to the corner where we want him positioned.

Ranger then runs up to him, Warrior (RT) shoots an arrow to get second spot on rampage list, Ranger weaponshield tanks and builds some aggro. MA then runs up, and once CH chain is humming along, taunts off ranger.

During this team Bertie's rampage eats me, eats the ranger, then eventually chews into the /disc defensive warrior - this gives the CH chain on rampage tank time to wake up, have a shower, kick the dog, and start healing.

It isn't clean, it isn't pretty, but I did devise it myself and it works. I'd be interested to see how other guilds do it though.

Bert's a little bitch.

For the lifetapping bastards towards the end of the event... make sure you use DI on the tanks (duh), spellshield is teh win. And make sure that heal rotation is tight, tight, tight. A knight isn't gonna tank him well unless he's really loaded with VT gear or you're already elemental.... quad 1500 + 5k lifetap... he can do close to 10k damage in a round on a non-defensive tank. We usually lost our tanks on this guy at some point, until we got geared up a little more... but usually, unless you got serious adds, if you have enough dps for this event you can zerg him down before he gets you.

If the problem was adds... there's a pretty simple technique for getting the kings solo (or with only 1-2 adds). Won't say more... figure it out.

Bert himself is nasty as hell for a pre-elemental mob. The huge hitbox makes pulling and setting ramp a pain. Play around with it some. Rampage kicked our ass on that fight more than once. Use a defensive warrior on ramp, not a knight.... not until that knight is elemental geared.

This really is one of the coolest events pre-elemental.... it's just frustrating as hell to learn it sometimes... 2 hours then a wipe to bert ain't fun.... but really... if you can't handle the lifetap procs... pofire minis are gonna own you. And if you can't handle the fast paced pulls, you'll never get to spawn the bosses on air avatars or earth rings. If you get it, the frustration on bert will pay off in the end.

The Positioning of bert is important, true. But you need to SET RT badly at the start. Let the RT fire an arrow. Bert will do his AE thing, then charge. MT pampers him with arrows and gets aggro. position him in corner opposite to camp so clerics can heal from corners and not get hit by ae /weee for blocking ae .

The key to winning this fight is to get the adds out of the way. There can be up to 12 adds during bertox fight. Have a monk and a sk standing by, training away the adds that pop all the way into south corner, as deep as possible. monk feigns at the end or dies. SK doesnt attack bert (while raid is busy positioning etc) he is ONLY busy looking at the 2 exits so that the adds are NOT COMING BACK.

You dont want even ONE caster getting aggro (these have a weird aggro range, really. I have nearly walked past em and not get aggro in the king rooms, but once i stepped foot into bert room i had them charging from a entire hallway into the casters.

The *moment* the adds start coming back (and they will) Pop harmshield, charge into them , run as far back as you can with them, and simply die/fd.

Bert needs to be dead in that time (3 mins)


The last 5k hp tap king is annoying, but it can be done. We didnt use a WS ranger, but our tanks has over 10k pre elemental so far, so we kind of had the advantage there. Basically, he is like RD. eat him before he eats you, really. Max out dps, have a bard sing resist, spellshield, DI..there is just so much you can do. make SURE the clerics know the tank rotation. Set it beforehand. So if MT dies, he wont chew for multiple tanks.

ToT ROCKS for this, get it with leadership aa.

KEEP yourself to the MT chain, and you will be fine. Use SL chains, the fight should take longer then 1 minute.
