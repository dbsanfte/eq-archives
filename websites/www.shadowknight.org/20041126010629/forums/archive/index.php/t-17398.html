I think this needs to be examined. If we look at three scythe spells:

Level 49 cost 200 AP
Level 54 cost 410 AP
Level 64 cost 650 AP

The non-linearity really strikes me. Now for those level 65s, a mid-50s person receives around 20 AP per successful adventure. This means the level 49 spell cost 10 adventures, the 54 20 adventures (plus or minus). For a level 65, it seems to me that 13 adventures is whats required.

Given that I am not 49, it may be that 13 - 15 adventures is a good number for that level to obtain the level 49 Scythe spell.

So, my suggestion is that the cost of the level 54 spell be changed to around 300 APs. I am not clear if there are other inequitites in the system.

However, this does lead me to another question. A level 20 char receives about 1AP per adventure. A level 65 receives 51 point per adventure. The delta here is 45 levels and 50 points. In round terms about 1 point per level (1.1 is more accurate but I like round numbers for this example). So, to me a level 55 should receive about 30 points per adventure which would fix the cost of the level 54 spell.

Has anybody else noticed this lack of linearity?
