The biggest thing to know about this guy is that his hit box is retarded small. It makes him really, really hard, especially if you don't know about this prior. For this reason I tanked him as the Warriors had such a hard time finding his hitbox and not being able to get initial agro. With the new target circles it might not be such a problem but it totally screwed up our first couple of attempts.

Another problem that comes from his super small hitbox is that casters have to be in melee range to land spells. If you're not in melee range you get an automatic resist. Make sure that's clear to all debuffers and nukers. He is slowable but it is partially resisted.

Garudon has an AoE based Cold DD+Dot. Make sure everyone has a buff spot free for the debuff and max out resists. With Bard AoE resists and druid resists it is pretty easily resistable. Without bard resists it lands a lot as we didn't have a full time bard at the time.

From my experience with him there is no place to get where wall blocks his AoE. So take this into account. Have some heals massed or your healers will get tore up by the DoT. We semi corner tanked him best we could, but it doesn't exactly work right for whatever the reason. Have your best warriors shrunk and ready to AoE taunt if you do use a Knight to tank at first. Which worked for us best at first. I pulled him from theater down the column over to near the small steps that you use to enter the golem filled area. I casted CD/fear on him quite a few times on the pull to build up agro.

I didn't deal with the CH chain, so I can't tell you what pause to use there. I was at the time around 8K+ hps and 1500+ AC w/ shield fully buffed. He hits for max around 1000 or so I think. Don't exactly recall. Doesn't flurry, but does enrage.

Dknight pretty much told you what you need to know.

As an alternative to the long pull, you can have your raid hug the wall opposite the commanders and Garudon and clear the small room in the far corner. Have your MT engage and pull him there. Kinda gimpy but he walls easy enough in the broken portion and melee can still get to his asstastically small hitbox. This can't be stressed enough, it is small.

There will be a few spawns you need to clear first before you engage but they're about as tough as the mobs on the way to Garudon's area.

Garudon looks like an oversized Ashenbone drake, melees for 980, and aes for 650 initial dd + dot. Didn't notice any rampages or flurries but does enrage. Rough guesstimate of his hps would be close to 200k or so, maybe a touch higher but I didn't parse the fight so can't give you a true total. Don't recall the cheal timing for the fight but the MT wasn't in danger at any point during the encounter with 4 clerics. As for the AE, have druids/shaman/pallies patch heal groups as needed.

This is not a bad target to farm since I know on my server most guilds didn't bother with it. Has some decent drops for all classes.

Good luck and have fun.
