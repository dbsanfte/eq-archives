Watch your pulls so they don't get too long because if the group decides to creep up on you they'll go right to the doorway of your next packed room.

Reaching back in my memory for spells used at 55 so bear with me - this is just what I did myself at 55, not suggesting everyone else does it:

Manaregen at 55 is an issue and dooming darkness is heavy on mana to use for every pull.

I used BE gauntlets (mana-free light snare), and memmed engulfing darkness too.

Pull with the gaunts unless you need to snare/FD split.

If someone jumps a single mob inbound, chain cast clinging/engulfing alternately from your spell gems till it leaves them. They'll soon give up because playing chase-the-mob-back-to-camp isn't nearly as much fun as deliberately messing up someone's pull.

If the pull's a multiple, your light snared mob should arrive slightly after the adds, so muppet will jump the adds first. Then you can chain cast CD/engulfing on the MA mob if needed to bring it back to camp while they die to the adds.

Not sure if this helps but from memory it's more or less what I used to do at your level.

The lower planes are usually very easy hunting areas with rather static spawns or predicatable wanderers, so it is easy to be mediocre and survive. LDONS mobs are close to your level and often come in waves. That does not leave as much room for error, so player and group deficiencies become more obvious.
