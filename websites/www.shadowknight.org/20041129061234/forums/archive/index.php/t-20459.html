One of the points that I was trying to make is that a damage focus for your DoTs won't greatly increase your ability to get and keep aggro, and won't increase your ability to survive getting beat on. If it was only the damage dealt by our dots that created the aggro, then Ignite Blood would be better aggro than Festering Darkness and Clinging Darkness, and this simply is not the case. Also, resisted spells would not draw aggro the way they do, because they aren't doing any damage.

If you don't believe me, it's easy enough to experiment on this. Simply get the permission of your group mates to do a little testing of what works best for aggro, and replace Festering Darkness in your spell lineup with any other DoT that you have that doesn't debuff, ie Ignite Blood or Blood of Thule, and see if you can hold aggro just as easily. If so, then everything is well and good, but if not you might want to experiment with other spells.

In my experience, the most common spells used to keep and hold aggro are Clinging/Festering Darkness for the snare, and Fear (on level 55 and up mobs) simply because mobs get really mad when you try to fear them. I'm not saying that your way will not allow you to hold aggro in the places where you fight, because obviously it is. What I am saying, is that through experimentation you should find that a lower mana cost spell lineup will work as well, if not better, and allow you to do more things with your mana such as nuke, AC buff, attack buff and many other things. If your groupmates love you now, just imagine how they'll feel if you can do your job even better than before.

You are, of course, free to ignore any advice contained in this thread, and I'm sure that if you do so, you will still continue to be as successful in the future as you have been in the past.
