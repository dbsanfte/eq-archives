Korekil
I am sure people are aware of the recent statement from SOE about their intentions to address the disparity in power levels between casters and melee. I am not certain they mean purely in terms of DPS, but also in terms of survivability and utility.
As PoP stands at the moment, there are only three melee classes capable of soloing, and only twp can solo effectively. We can solo with an inefficient form of kiting and a lot of patience, Rangers can solo with AM3 and Trueshot etc, and Beastlords's can solo fairly well in the lower tiers I think.
As far as I am aware there are no other melee classes or hybrids capable of doing this. Paladins, Warriors, Monks and Rogues are purely group reliant really, as are we if we want effective exp.
All casters, with the exception of clerics, can solo fairly easily in PoP. Druids, Wizards, Shaman, Necromancer's, Mages and Enchanters can all solo pretty effectively, and they put out greater DPS in most cases than the melee.
Anyone got ideas on how they should go about this? Utility wise I think we are fine, but will we see more DPS or more ability for solo. Do you think they will balance melee classes seperately or just make an across-the-board change?
Personally I have no idea on how they will "balance" us. I would like to see an increase in melee skills, particularly offensive and 2 hand weapon skills, but I worry about a nerf bat :/
What I do hope is that they do not make it an AA change. I do not think classes should be balanced by AA as that only addresses the top percentage of the player base.

http://eqlive.station.sony.com/community/dev_view.jsp?id=55816

"We are going to look at improving the damage capabilities of melee classes, as they've fallen behind their magic-using counterparts at the very high end of the game. Some options being considered are:



Improve melee damage-increasing disciplines

Improve Planes of Power melee weapons"

I interpret this as a response to the numerous threads on The Safehouse and MB complaining that their damage does not keep up with casters who have FT15, focus items, aa's, etc that allows them to do damage without the traditional disadvantage of going oom. For example rogues have asked for Duellist to be made their equivalent of Trueshot and for their Throwing skill to be brought up to par with Rangers (both of which seem fairly reasonable to me). Monks have asked to be able to run faster than a speeding bullet and jump tall buildings with one bound, since they are, after all, kung fu ninja masters. Or something.

Note the update also talks about the very high end of the game. So some of the bonkers weapons and the mysterious melee focus effects found in Time may be part of this balancing.

I think enhancing melee soloing might occur as a side-effect of opening up the xp range on pre-PoP zones so that 65's can go beat up old world mobs for a little xp. But they really only tend to make design decisions that reduce the ability to solo, i'd be very surprised if they consciously set about encouraging it as an option.

Personally i think most classes are pretty well-balanced (everybody has the odd issue of course and always will) with mid-level, non-raiding warriors the only folk who really have a rough time of it. I believe giving warriors more variety in terms of their skills and abilities rather than choking them with more specialization is the best approach. As mentioned above (and every time it comes up) you can't really improve warrior aggro without potentially trivialising the game. Brik's thread has some good discussion going on about specific ideas he's suggested.
