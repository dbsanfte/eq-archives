Pre-PoP Advancements

General Abilities:

Innate Run Speed: A+, A, A
Run 1 will let you out run most mobs in indoor zones.
Innate Regeneration: B+
Regen 3 will hardly have a noticable affect when you are tanking. It is equivalent to mitigating 1/2 a DPS when tanking. It will decrease the time you have to wait if FDed in a sticky situation with low health, and it is a prerequisite for mind & body rejuvenation which has mana regen.
Innate Metabolism: B+
Decreasing your food consumption is convenient, it frees up inventory space and significantly lessens the cost of using stat food.
Innate Fire Protection, Cold Protection, Magic Protection, Poison Protection, Disease Protection: C-
Assuming you are aiming for the end game, resists are almost the only stat you won't max with gear, making them viable to spend AAs on.
Innate Charisma: D or B
Charisma can help if you tank with divine intervention, and most end game gear does not have enough to cap it.
Innate Intelligence, Stamina, Dexterity, Strength, Agility: D
If you're aiming for the end game, all these will end up capped by gear. If you are not aiming for the end game, you can still gain more power spending AAs elsewhere.
First Aid: D
Bandages are rarely useful.
Innate Lung Capacity: D
Rarely useful.
Innate Wisdom: F
The only alternate advancement ability with absolutely no benefit for shadow knights.

Archetype Abilities:

Combat Agility: A, A-, A
Combat agility has the bonus of slightly reducing interrupts while decreasing damage intake.
Combat Stability: A, A-, A
Combat stability makes your damage intake less spikey and more consistent.
Natural Durability: A-, B+, A-
When tanking meleeing mobs, natural durability is not as beneficial as combat stability and agility because the 10 percent improvement is only on your base hitpoints, and does not include buffs, so in a situation where you're taking a lot of damage quickly, CS or CA has a better chance of keeping you alive than ND. However, it also helps with spell damage while CS and CA do not.
Spell Casting Fury: C, B, A
This is the only ability that scales up. When you are going to get this, you should get it all in a row, because the first two levels are significantly less powerful/points than the third. This increases DD spell and proc DPS, is required for the 7200 damage harm touch, and stacks with theft of life.
Mental Clarity: B, C+, C-
Scaling costs without scaling improvement. If you're using your extra mana for damage, the most you should expect is about 1 dps per point of mana regen.
Combat Fury: B, C, D+
This is only a one percent increase in melee DPS per level, and has scaling cost without scaling improvement.
Channeling Focus: B-, C, D+
Scaling cost without scaling improvement. Will help you cast spells while getting beat on.
Spell Casting Expertise: B, D, D
The first level of this will prevent clinging darkness and fear from fizzling, which for many shadow knights are the staples of agro generation.
Natural Healing: C, D, D-
Scaling cost without scaling improvement. See "Innate Regeneration". Is a prerequisite for mind and body rejuvenation.
Fear Resistance: D+
Is a prerequisite for complete fear immunity.
Finishing Blow: D
Minorly speeds up killing low level mobs.
Spell Casting Deftness: D-
This will only slightly decrease the casting time of Augmentation of Death, Shroud of Chaos and Cloak of Luclin.
Spell Casting Subtlety: F
If you are in a raiding guild and you never tank this could be helpful, however it is counter productive if you are tanking or need to tank. Well placed FDs will be much more effective in non tanking situations.

Class Abilities:

Physical Enhancement: A+
You need one level in each of CA/CS/ND to get the full benefit, but this is the best improvement per AA point available.
2 Hand Bash: A or C
Bash slightly increases damage output, decreases damage intake if you stun the mob, and helps interrupt casters. C for trolls and ogres because slam is viable when not illusioned.
Pet Discipline: A
This keeps little Goner alive and well, and allows you to pet pull. If you consider this AA as letting your pet stay alive for most situations, it's by far the most DPS you can situationally gain using 6 AA.
Soul Abrasion: A-, B-, B+
With maxxed dex SA3 using SoC adds about 4.5 dps, and will mitigate about 5dps when tanking combined with SCF3 and ToL3.
Unholy Steed: A-
It's a very useful tool but doesn't really increase your power much. It speeds up recovery time from death, and is helpful for agro kiting and some pulling.
Leech Touch: B
This can be a life saver, however HT has to be available.
Improved Harm Touch: C
Not in itself very powerful, but it is a prerequisite for Unholy Touch and the 7200+ HT.
Fearless: C-
It's a useful ability when raiding fear casting mobs, but the prerequisite cost is very steep.
Body and Mind Rejuvenation: D+
It has a very steep prerequisite cost, and is only a minor gain in power.
Double Riposte: D+, D, D-
Scaling cost without scaling improvement. With how often you actually riposte an incoming hit, this is only an extremely minor increase in DPS, and only works while tanking.


PoP Alternate Advancements

PoP Advance:

Planar Durability: B-
this adds half as much HP per AA point as natural durability.
Planar Power: C
minor increase in stats.
Advanced Innate Strength, Stamina, Agility, Dexterity, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. Warding of Solusek, Blessing of E'ci, Marr's Protection, Shroud of The Faceless, Bertoxxulous' Gift:
see general innate stat categories.

PoP Ability:


Theft of Life: A+, B+, B
The low point cost of the first level makes it a great bargain, the second and third level have a lower points/gain ratio.
Unholy Touch: B
For general day to day fighting this isn't necessary, but can be invaluable in a raiding guild. It also allows you to solo some mobs you couldn't otherwise.
Speed of the Knight: C, B-, B-
About 3 percent improvement in melee DPS at the third level, stacks multiplicatively with Knight's advantage to add about 7.5% DPS together, and more swings slightly increases your melee produced agro. The first level is a lesser bonus than the next two.
Lightning Reflexes: B-
One percent increase in avoidance per level, regular combat agility is 2.5X the gain/point.
Innate Defense: B-
One percent increase in mitigation per level, regular combat stability is 2.5X the gain/point.
Knight's Advantage: B-, C+, C
About 3.75 percent improvement in melee DPS at the third level, stacks multiplicatively with Speed of the Knight to add about 7.5% DPS together. Cost scaling is greater than bonus scaling.
Consumption of the Soul: B-
Further increases the life saving capability of leech touch.
Touch of the Wicked: C+, C, C
This can be useful with leech touch. This shouldn't be considered until you have either UT3 or CotS3 or both. The first level puts HT closer to Unholy Aura disc timer, the third level lets every other HT have Unholy Aura without wasting much time.
Fury of the Ages: D+
0.5 percent increase in DPS per level, not a great bargain.
Coup de Grace: D, D, C
The final level of this puts it in a range where it might slay a fleeing mob worthy of being killed.
Flash of Steel: D-
see double riposte.
Mastery of the Past: D-
This doesn't help with any spells regularly cast by level 65 shadow knights.


Recommended Tanking AAs ordering.
CA1, CS1, ND1, PE, CA2, CA3, CS2, CS3, ND2, ND3, PoP AAs (PD/LR/ID).

Recommended Combat AAs ordering:
CF1, KA1, SoTK1, SoTK2, SoTK3, KA2, KA3, CF2, CF3, double riposte 1, FoTA1, FoTA2, FoTA3

Disclaimer:
These rankings are subjective, and alter with situation. The letter rankings comparing AAs in the same class are tiered to provide most benefit for the least AA to the best of my knowledge. Different classes of AAs, however, are far more open to situational differences, and debate. Feel free to make comments and suggestions.
