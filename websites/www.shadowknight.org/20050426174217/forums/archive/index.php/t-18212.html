On the 2nd map in the above post, I set up in the corridor immediately northeast of the shack. There will be 1 wanderer that I wait for to go on his way then I attack the static mob in the corridor. This usually brings 1 mob from up the slope, usually a dog. I dual box an SK and a druid so I charm the dog and use it to quickly kill the static mob. The wanderer will normally be close again at this point and I use the dog to kill him too, then break charm and kill the dog. The next mob I kill is a static mob south of my camp spot and east of the shack, unless it's another dog which I will charm for the next 2-3 kills. Next, while standing in the corridor I originally cleared, I cast on whatever is in the Alchemist Acolyte spot. He will run to me and about 50% of the time bring 1 mob from inside the hut with him. After these 2 are dead, I pull both mobs left in the hut.

If there are no dogs it's tougher for me but out of maybe 50 break ins in the last month I have only had the no-dog thing happen 1 time.

If the Grand Herbalist is up, have the highest level person invis and open the door then back up to the wall, cast on GH before door closes then run back in corridor. When I do this, he never brings adds even if they are dark blue to me. When this happens, I use the opportunity to split the spawns up so one thing spawns in the hut about every 5-6 minutes. With the group you listed, you should be fine if you have to tank 2 mobs. I would let the 62 necro pull and feign mobs off as you engage.

One other thing: most of the mobs down here are shaman mobs so have someone annul magic as soon as possible on each mob. Just getting rid of their haste makes them much easier.

Old school advice here, let the druid use ensnare as it lasts much longer than any darkness you would use, and then you don't have to worry about darkness wearing off while you are in the middle of battle spam. If a mob gets away down there with a 54 on its hate list, you are gonna have a pile of friends show up real fast.
