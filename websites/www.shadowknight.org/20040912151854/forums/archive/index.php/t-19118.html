A true cost/benefit analysis is not just pro's cons
It's analyzing the value of that you must put in and comparing it to the value of what you get out of it.
Just listing our useful skills is not enough for an analysis.
Just as an NPV calculation will tell you the time value of money so a good cost/benefit analysis of SK's should tell you the value of your time spent.

That being said the following is not a good cost/benefit analysis rather just rambling while I take a short break at work.

SK's require time building up spells skills to ensure that you can get less fizzles. So 1hr time + 25pp for int buffs and clarity = more likely to be able to FD and invis. - Worth time investment

SK's can wear armor from the highest class of armor in the game (plate). Plate is (relatively)expensive but adds (generally) better AC. - Worth money investment

SK's have pets that add damage and become the equivalent of a very mana efficient DoT, but can be hard to keep alive. - pick 'em

SK's sacrifice straight DPS/faction for verastility - Personal Preference

There's a reason people here kind of come across as assholes. And to be honest, I kind of like the atmosphere here.

We're evil classes. Don't expect people that are attracted to a dark knight to be the friendliest around. Our class is dark and scheming, we play dark and scheming, and we hold others at arms length.

We're self sufficient, and by extension, often loners.

We also take pride in the fact that, at least at one point, we were the least played class, and perservered. We resent the fact that when we became the 'favored class of the day', all the assholes quit their monks and druids and started SK's, and that these twinks often stunk up the class in general.

I think the old axiom from this forum is still true. If you have to *ask* if the shadowknight is for you, it's not.

We're not heros, we're not the best at anything. We're good at a lot of things, but since we lack a clearly defined role, we're often left behind for pure classes. We have to play many times harder than a warrior and play twice as well to be given a second thought as a tank in many raiding guilds... unless you just so happen to be short on warriors *and* paladins that day. Shadowknights take a hell of a lot of effort to be truly powerful.... but that truly powerful shadowknight is a rare and terrible thing to behold.

As a shadowknight, you'll spend hours and days with your /lfg tag on, passed up as a tank for warriors and monks, passed up as a puller for enchanters, monks, bards... passed up as dps by many classes... there's more than one point on the 'power curve' that we're waaay behind other classes.

You can't rely on the merits of your class to be popular. In fact, you probably won't be popular. You have to rely on your grit and skill to be favored. If you want a garaunteed raid role, play an enchanter or a cleric, or even a warrior. If you love getting groups quick.... cleric, chanter, bard is for you.

SK's are also very 'busy' classes to play, if you play them well. I hit most of the spells on my spell bar, most of the fights I'm in. You'll often be pulling, tanking, and snaring, so you dont' get to sit and get a rest at any point. As puller and tank in many groups, your skill will be key to that groups success. You will be adaptable, independant, self reliant, and have a lot to offer single group situations as an SK. You'll be relegated to a support role on raids, especially in larger guilds.
