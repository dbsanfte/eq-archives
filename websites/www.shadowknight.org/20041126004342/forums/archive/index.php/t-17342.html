A lot of attention has been directed at the balance of power between warriors and
their hybrid counterparts (Paladins and Shadowknights). The purpose of this post
is to present a compilation of evidence that may lead to an unpopular conclusion.
If an individual were to skip to the end of this post, and read just the conclusion, the popular opinion would be to disagree with it. However this Article is going to present both Historical facts from EQ and facts that are still very relevant today. I believe part of the problem lies with each class’s expectation of themselves.

Let’s start with the history of class "balance" between Warriors and Tanking Hybrids. Spare yourself the embarrassment of arguing against these points in the history section. They are not meant as genuine balance arguments today. I'm only bringing them up so the reader can have a good understanding of the evolution of this "balance" between the 3 classes involved.
The old saying was "75 percent Warrior and 25 percent (insert Cleric or Necro)" was what hybrids were. Hybrids would joke that that ratio is true, they had 75 percent of the melee-skills of a (Cleric or Necro) and 25 percent of the casting skills of a warrior. Shadowknights who were reasonably equipped during Classic, Kunark and Velious (Hereafter referred to as CKaV) would hesitate to cast several of their spells. Some spells like heat blood for example would eat up 2 or 3 solid rounds of melee ability (due to a bug that was later fixed pertaining to the melee round being reset after casting). On raids in CKaV, Shadowknights couldn’t use a majority of their spells for a couple reasons. Remembering that until PoP most PAL/SHD spells were just "Old-Cleric/Necro" spells, certain spells just didnt stick to the raid level mobs. If the Shadowknight's spells did stick, they would be overwritten by the Necro version anyways. Paladins had a similar problem. No raid functions without a cleric, therefore all paladin buff-spells (except Brells) were useless to the Paladin's group. During Classic and Kunark, most serious EXP groups also needed a cleric with few exceptions, making the Paladins line of buffs again, useless. In Kunark, the perception of most was that certain areas of Sebilis could not be done with a Paladin or Shadowknight. Remember that there was no "low-aggro-celestial-healing" spells, and no KEI. If a Paladin or Shadowknight was tanking, the cleric was pouring mana into them that they actually needed to med back. In these groups, Paladins and Shadowknight sat up at the entrance spamming "LFG" in the /ooc chat if they didn’t bring a group of existing friends. The tanking skills of Paladins and Shadowknights were severely lacking, their spells were unusable or trivial at best, and finally there melee damage was *not* 75 percent of a warriors. The problem lied in the skill caps. With weapon skills capped low, Paladins and Shadowknights suffered from the "Swwhish"- syndrome more than any other melee. If your not familiar with the "Swwhish"- syndrome consider yourself lucky. Being unable to connect a swing against higher level mobs was a widespread problem for Paladins and Shadowknights. With all these problems, Paladins and Shadowknights were not only the least desirable classes, but they paid *additional* experience for the honor of being so undesirable. During CKaV, there was only one tank, The Warrior. There were no substitutes.

As I stated earlier in this article, the preceding paragraph is history. To argue on behalf of Warriors or Hybrids based on just the preceding paragraph would be an embarrassment. Most of the aforementioned problems did eventually get corrected. Let’s take a look at Warriors versus Paladins and Shadowknights today.

On raids, Warriors are still the only true tank, this will never change. Its often not pointed out that the warriors disciplines scale upward with content. The "main tank" gets the advantage of taking less damage when he flips on his Defensive Discipline regardless of what encounter. Discipline defensive is arguably much better when used against Quarm than it was when used against Trakanon. Because it is on a percentage, it stays undeniably useful throughout all the Everquest encounters. This percentage works to a Warriors advantage, compounded with the Warriors rising hitpoints throughout the expansions it means that warrior can take more and more damage. When incoming damage is reduced by a percentage, you effectively get more use out of the hitpoints you have. If we arbitrarily added 1000hps to a Warrior, he will get more use from them than his Paladin or Shadowknight counterpart would. Warriors do keep a significant hitpoint advantage over Knights, however since it is based on a percentage Warrior's say the gap is "closing". Instead of having 20 percent more hitpoints than a Knight (common in CKaV) the Warrior HP advantage is down to around 12 percent. If a Warrior claims 12 percent is insignificant, you have to follow the history. The Warrior was the *only* tank through CKaV and was so far ahead of his Knightly counterparts that they were barely considered tanks. If tommorow SOE added an AA skill that gave 12 percent more hitpoints, you can bet the warriors would be the class most excited. Hitpoints are one of the few stats that really matter to a Warrior.

Which brings me to another Warrior advantage: Gear Selection.
In order for a Warrior to do his job he needs to worry about HPs, ATK and AC. When it comes to selecting gear, the warrior (usually) needs to select the piece with the most hps on it (this is the case with Time gear). Warriors can get geared well naturally without having to make tradeoffs. A Paladin may be interested in HPs, ATK and AC as well, but he is also interested in Mana Preservation, Spell Haste, and Improved Healing. A Shadowknight is interested in Mana Preservation, Burning Affliction and Improved Damage as well. On the FOH Message board in Kruegans post "Trail of Tears" he mentions a "Superknight" phenomenon. Where some people posting on behalf of warriors wrap-up all the powers of a Paladin and Shadowknight together as one "Superknight" then compare warriors to them. Not only does that happen, but often these same posters also assume the Paladin and Shadowknight has 2 sets of full time gear, one for max effects on focus effects and one for max hps. Then when convinent, The "Hitpoint" Knight template is compared to a real time Warriors illustrating only a small hitpoint difference (it helps their case) then when they mention spells they'll shift to a "Spellcasting/Max-Focus" Template when they discuss Knights spell casting ability. Gear choices are much harder for Knights than for Warriors. There is much more to balance. Warriors still have several advantages, lately those advantages have been "Swept under the rug" and the spotlight has turned on the Knights who finally got fixed when PoP was released.


Knights have enough hitpoints and AC to realistically "add-tank". There is one encounter in the plane of time that may have been designed by SOE for hybrids, but many guilds found a way to use Warriors on that encounter anyways. In small groups, Paladins and Shadowknights now bring more to the table than they did during CKaV. They offer "acceptable" tanking skills with one added benefit (SHD's add the benefit of tremendous aggro, and PAL's offer the benefit of decent support healing). If the healers have good mana regeneration and can afford to spend the extra mana healing a Pal/Shd then a small group will gain more from the Pal/Shd than they would with the Warrior. Currently in a 65th level, LDON normal mode adventure where the cleric (or alternate healers) have VOQ or KEI, most groups if given a choice of the 3-Tank classes would pick Paladin or Shadowknight. This isn't imbalanced in and of itself, its just a trade-off that most people at the moment will make.

Summary and Conclusion:
-----------------------

There are still many advantages to being a Warrior, some Warriors have done a great job lobbying for improvements to their class. The problem is that it is just that, lobbying. The advantages are written on the wall, and they stay the same throughout EQ. Some of them desire their "old-glory days" of CKaV where they were the only tank and Knights had zero value. Those days are gone, and now they have to deal with being the best tank of the three.. Instead of just being the only tank. The purpose of this article is not to have warriors nerfed, the purpose is to show the reader that the warrior situation certainly is not as grim as they paint it. Knights are finally a viable class, but that is what has come under fire. Some people posting on this issue seem intent on driving Knights back into the CKaV days. Even a certain uber-guild leader (who’s mother obviously used timeouts instead of spankings) proposed that when Knights put on a piece of gear they should suffer a penalty so they cant use the gear fully. When Knights were dead last on the list of desirable classes for grouping in CKaV the warriors did not seem to mind at all, now when they have a little competition for pickup groups their all aggravated. Add that to the fact they have *no* competition for raids, they *are* the tank.

You've already read the thousands of posts telling you Knights advantages over Warriors, be enlightened now as you read....
