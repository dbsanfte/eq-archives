KrukhanGarabash
04-02-2002, 05:24 PM
FR and MR. Even weak MR/FR (70-90ish) will give you adequate root and nuke protection, leaving the unresitable DoT line as your only problem.

-Go in unbuffed for easy dispelling of any DoTs or roots that land. You want to dispell big DoTs asap but don't stand there and eat nuke while doing so. If you give chase for a bit then stop and dispell you'll probably get it off before he realizes that you're not chasing/casting offense. 1 or 2 ticks of a 50's DoT's will add up to less than than a solid nuke.
-Dispel his buffs ASAP.
-Try to work in close and melee but don't blindly chase.
-Play with spell ranging. Stay just out of casting range and spam on clinging/englufing until he gets in range.
-Flutter him if you can since he probably won't be prepared to cure or resist it.
-If you get inside and tries to cast at point blank slash, bash then go for word if you think his MR is low enough. Not many people know that word has a 1ft knockback.
-Go for big base damage. You won't be in range to lang often so you want it matter when you are.
-Bash is your interrupt use it wisely, don't count on a fast 1h to do the job.
-If you're really confident you might try stand and see if he's willing to exchange nukes for taps/dispells...dispell, leech, tap, rush, ht. In my experience most don't realize or want to accept how much their nukes are being resisted down by until it's too late.

Don't expect too many wins though. EQ doesn't force you too commit to combat...So you can expect a losing caster to gate the instant he has 5 seconds of freedom or just flat out run and let latency do the work.

At lvl 50 druids are pretty tough, best you can do gear wise is load up on MR, BD/Diamond jewlery helps quite a bit, DarkForge armor has decetn MR as well.

1. If you stay snared for a long period of time you loose.
2. Most druid DOTs are magic based.

Keep the druid on the run as much as pssiblle. Only cast spells when the druid start to cast , IE druid is out running you due to sow, he gets ahead and start to cast, 1st thing to do is cancel magic him, if he continues casting after ur spells goes through cancel magic him again, or if u have been snared cancel magic ur self to strip the snare. A unbuffed druid will fall fast. So the key is to stay unsnared and keep the druid buffless especially sowless. Finally as always leave HT for finishing blow.. The rest really depends on ur MR if the druid can snare u every time he attempts you will loose, however the more often u resist the snare the better are ur chances.

Also darkness has 2 MR checks that need to pass in order for it to stick which means decent MR will almost always stop it from sticking. Use your mana to tap, yes i know at lvl 50 taps are weak but, taps are unrestable by players which means it will do full damage every time in pvp, making them by far the best DDs, also the added healing never hurts.
