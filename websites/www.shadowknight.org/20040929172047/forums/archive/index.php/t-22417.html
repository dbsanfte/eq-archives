Vaelenu
From your position, if you enjoy tanking I would complete the following AAs in this order as soon as possible:

Planar Durability 3 (HPs > all)
Combat Stability 3
Innate Defense 3
Planar Power 3 (assuming your stats are capping with buffs)

Then pick up the following to improve your dps via casting. Most of these will also improve your tanking ability.

Mental Clarity 1 (for 2 AAs it's a bargain)
Spell Casting Fury 3
Soul Abrasion 3
Theft of Life 3 (maybe look at getting 1 level of this earlier)
Unholy Touch 3

Then melee and 2 hander dps increasers:

Combat Fury 3
and (in any order)
Knight's Advantage 3
Speed of the Knight 3
2 Hand Bash

After that I would pick up these in any order:

Pet Discipline
Mental Clarity 3
Touch of the Wicked 1 (to synchronise your IHT with your disc)
Spell Casting Expertise (impossible to fizzle Fear and CD)
Channeling Focus 3

I haven't included any GoD AAs in my recommendations. While some of these are useful I feel unless you are gaining AAs super quick they just aren't worthwhile compared to other stuff you can get. If closer to the OoW release date you find you have a few saved up and can't decide where to spend them, consider hanging on to them till you see what OoW brings out, there might be some nice new ones although I doubt they will be cheap.

Slein Jinn
Tanking AA's are definately great and should be high on anyone's list of priorities, but certain AA's have an effect on you that simply reducing the damage you take cannot. Pet Discipline is one such AA; Unholy Steed is another. The increase in "raw power" or whatever may not be there, but the impact on your gaming experience, so to speak, is. Once you have AA's like Unholy Steed or Pet Discipline, you never know how you managed without them before.

Tanking AA's, DPS AA's, all that stuff just makes the numbers a little better on what you already do - while that's certainly important, it's very different from granting you something new and different (and for those too dense to figure it out: Unholy Steed is something new and different even from a 100k or dropped horse). I don't mean to sound like I'm trying to downplay the importance of tanking AA's - they're vital to being successful in your role - they're hard to compare to things like Pet Discipline though. It's hard to say which is ultimately "better" but you'll certainly appreciate Pet Discipline more once you have it.

Everyone's AA path differs based on their needs and play styles. An aweful lot of people put off getting AA's like Pet Discipline and Unholy Steed, though, and when they do get around to buying them, they smack themselves and wonder how they managed to get by. It's kinda like owning a CoS or HEB...

I'm not trying to say that everyone should dump 12 AA's in archetype and go straight to Pet Discipline and Unholy Steed... I'm just saying don't underestimate them (particularly the former) as so many do...
