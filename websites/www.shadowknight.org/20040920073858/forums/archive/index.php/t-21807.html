I'm at about the 680 mark for aa, but for some reason I still feel that there is soooo much to get yet. I'll comment on a few of the aa lines that you haven't completed yet though, hopefully it might help.

Double Riposte: I left this till quite late too, around the 300 aa mark if I recall. While it's true that a riposte isn't all that often which means a double riposte is even less frequent, if you tank I believe it's worthwhile. If you don't tank much (although I'm sure you do), then it has little value since to riposte you have to be getting hit. Also, for those of you wondering, you can't riposte a riposte. For a very short time after discs were introduced you could but that lasted only days (just after Kunark release I think).

Finishing Blow: at lvl 1 it's a fun toy but doesn't go off that much. Even up to lvl 3, where it goes off a bit more but has the same level cap (45 I think), it has limited value. The PoP ugrade, on the other hand does have a bit more value in that it raises the level cap of the mobs that can be FB'd. Up to the 2nd last level of Coupe de Grace, it wasn't doing much for me; but when I got the final level (up to 58 I think?), THEN it was working in LDoN normal adventures, PoTactics ogres, etc. and it was firing off left and right.

Intense Hatred: Levels 1 and 2 are relatively cheap, I spent my banked points on 1 & 2 on GoD release. I've just come back to it and am working on lvl 5 now. Like Double Riposte, DS doesn't do much unless you are tanking; and like small hp/ac upgrades, by itself 3 pts of DS doesn't mean much but it all adds up. At lvl 4 you have the same DS innately as you do with Banshee Aura, without the buff slot and spell gem, and it never wears off. For normal xp groups, I routinely have 70 pts of DS on plus 34 pt reverse DS. Sure getting hit for 900+ and doing 100 back doesn't mean squat, but getting hit for 60 and doing 100 might. It's unresistable free dps. It hurts to spend 12 and 15 points on it yes, but the lower lvls of it I think are worthwhile since it's a linear increase with increased costs.

That's all I can think of. You've got the good stuff already.
