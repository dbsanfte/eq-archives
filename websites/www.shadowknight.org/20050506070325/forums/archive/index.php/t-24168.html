For watercooling, try to avoid the fancy-ass kits you see from thermaltake, cooler bastard, and the like. Koolance is iffy, some stand by them, but I see them for the most part as iffy on the quality dept.

Here are a few good companies for parts

www.swiftnets.com -- Swiftech, my personal fave...pricey, but damn good

www.dangerden.com -- Danger Den, very nice stuff for overclockers

www.dtekcustoms.com -- DTek, best low buck radiator IMO, and decent stuff all around.

www.snt-systems.com -- US seller of Aqua-Computer, the rolls-royce of quiet watercooling....and pricey as a rolls too, but who can beat watercooled power supplies? :D

for accessories, parts, etc. (retailers)

www.sidewindercomputers.com

www.highspeedpc.com -- These guys have some nice vibration dampers for the Eheim brand water pumps, it really quiets them down, especially the Eheim 1250.

As far as how to do it, it's all plumbing...just with flexible tubes. Key here is to have enough flowrate to support the cooling you desire. There is lots of math involved here, bug me if you have to on this.

Questions are, how many components you want to cool, and is this for overclocking or quiet. Also what case is it going into, and are you willing to modify the case in any way (including cutting large holes, etc)? This will all help in component reccomendations.

BTW motherboard will have a huge impact on what CPU block to use....if it doesnt have the four mounting holes and is just a lugged socket, you have very few choices. Fortunately an athlon 64 as far as I know all the boards have this mount, or have a way to convert the clipping mechanism to a 4-hole mount.

Jovec
AMDs tend to run a lot cooler than Intel CPUs as they require much less power under load. If your only experience is with Intel CPUs, you probably don't need to go with watercooling for AMD, especially at stock clocks. It sounds like you also want to have your case unobstructed (nothing on the sides, don't sit it on carpet) and make sure you have ample intake and exhaust (i.e. airflow) through the case. Keep any dust screens clean too (dust, pet hair, etc can restrict airflow). Go with rounded IDE (or SATA) cables and keep them from bunching up around the main air circulation path inside the case. As for noise, low noise/zero noise fans seem to be rage these days.

As for KlaoofQuell's comment on the P4EE "smokin" on two instances, current HT tech doesn't help as much as you'd like (if that is what he had in mind) since HT P4 CPUs share a single set of execution units. Next gen stuff should move to complete dual cores on a single die.

AMDs top end isn't cheap, but generally, especially as you step down a little, the price vs. performance ratio goes strongly in favor of AMD for gaming (current benchmarks show AMD with a healthy lead regardless of price). Of course, the need to upgrade mobo and ram factor into pricing. Personally, I'm stuck with the p4 route, but I will likely move to AMD when my next upgrade requires all three pieces to be replaced.

And vilesoul, what game disappointed you with Nvidia SLI?

-Jovec

It takes time, and heat ouput and power consumotion increases are linear with your OC, so at the least it's predictable. Overclock a 2ghz chip to 2.4ghz and it will put out the same heat and have the same power consumtion as a 2.4ghz chip. Performance will most likely be the same as well, this has to do with chipmakers using the exat same core, if the architeccture is the same, pretty much the chip is the same, just some bridges at various points were either closed or opened to tell the chip to run at one specific speed.
