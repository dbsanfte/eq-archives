I was in the same spot, 65 shaman with all his skills and plenty of AA, and a mid-fifties SK, nicely buffed, using a SoD and a windblade and WMBS and black bastardsword, sometimes wearing fungi, sometimes not. We were pretty well set.

I didn't care for POI experience much. Actually, experience at that level is fairly slow everywhere even when powerleveling with a shaman because it's pretty easy to KS your SK at that level, and SK damage isn't exactly huge. For instance, leveling my monk was much faster and leveling my warrior was also significantly faster. You just have to suck that up a bit.

I found the best results in PON. Trees, spiders, birds. Relaxing enough and always plenty of them.

But you might want to consider doing kinda slowish experience in POI, POJ, and POD. That's because eventually you will probably want to go raid or get great(!!) experience in the Sol Ro zone, whether two-boxing or not, with your SK. You can get ornate there, spells, etc. But...you can't gate out! Problem!

So you can either spend like 500pp for a single gate potion, or go get thurgadin faction and build up pottery to a bit over 100 to make thurgadin gate potions(the reagents are getting harder to find because nobody really farms Velks/CC anymore), or you can kill the mobs in POI/POD/POJ that drop some of the pieces you need for the talisman gate necklace. Final product is the Binden Concertina -- very nice neck, and gate is a must-have for a non-gating class.

Anyway, since you very well might go back and camp those same mobs for almost no experience whatsoever later, you might want to kill two birds with one stone and go experience in those zones now, when the experience may not be the greatest, but at least you'll see slow progress. You're going to feel like you're really wasting your time if you come back there at 65 to camp the exact same thing.
