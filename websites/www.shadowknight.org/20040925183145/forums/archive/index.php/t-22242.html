Nocifer Deathblade
After I saw lot of problems in this SK forum and other class forums complaining about certain AA that are detrimental to them after some changes in EQ and they were stuck to those AA without any control over it.

That led to the suggestion of toggle of certain AAs.

I think the best solution for everything related to AA is to implement AA Removal System. If you happened not to like AA anymore, you can just erase AA back to its 0 skill and you won't get point for it but, at the end, you are free from it for good. For example, if you don't like SCS anymore, you can go to AA window and click on SCS and click ERASE and you get confirmation box saying, "ARE YOU SURE YOU WANT TO DO THIS?" then click yes. VOILA! SCS 3 levels went back to ZERO level. You won't get 12 AA back but you have nice control over it now. It is very similar to spell specialization erase system in case you made a wrong choice. You would have to regain skill back via practice. Same for AA, you would just have to earn more AA to buy other skills as usual but you have much more in control to remove that became detrinmental to your cause.

That way, SOE won't have to worry about fixing certain AA every time they became detrimental every major changes. I think its very easy to implement with new back-end AA system and I know SOE will LOVE this cuz it won't refund players AA to buy other AA skill immediately. SOE lose nothing and players actually benefit from this by eliminating any detrimental AAs with their full control without involving SOE to do it anymore. This would be useful feature for the future sake of EQ.

There is one problem I could see happening with an AA removal system. Most especially if the points were refunded.

That being AA that are deemed to be prerequisites for more advanced AA skills. How would this be handled? If you had the advanced version and took away the prerequisite AA, should that advanced AA still work?

Another scenario would be, what would happen if a player either by design, or by accident, went below the 12 AA required in Archetype, would that player then loose access to all of the class AA they had purchased?

Also, how would you handle all future AA purchases? Still count that the player at one point had the prereqs and allow them to purchase the more advanced AA or would the player have to rebuy those prereq AA again to have access to the advanced AA?

If points were refunded this could lead to some blatant abuse of the system. If not then there could still be problems with the way the prereq AA are handled. Makes the idea a whole lot more complicated in the end.
