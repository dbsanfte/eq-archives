Lunaticfringesk
Regardless of what any analysis is done when comparing race to race, one of the first and most important aspects to focus on is the xp penalty.

That is the great equalizer amongst all the races.

If all races leveled at the same pace, you'd be a fool to pick any race other than an Ogre, Troll, or Iksar, as those races are clearly superior to the remaining races due to special abilities (save for those who prefer certain intangibles, like looks, rollplay, cyber advantage, etc.).

That is why I will always chuckle when an "intelligent" race focuses on the fact that they have more intelligence as an advantage, yet get dwarfed in overall stats. Its foolish. To attempt to win the argument that one stat (in this case, intelligence) is inheritly better than another (say strength or stamina) is almost impossible to win.

For instance, an Ogre dominates a Dark Elf in terms of overall stats. To say, however, that the Dark Elf is better because he has higher intelligence is just silly.

To also argue that gear in the end game balances out all the stats is also foolish. I dont know of a single person that has maxed out all stats unbuffed. There is always room for improvement. At the end of the day, more points has to be superior to fewer points.

The point of this is that the primary benefit of playing a small race, say a Dark Elf, lies in the fact that they level faster then there large and / or Iksar counterparts. That is the primary benefit. It has little, if anything, to do in the fact that they are more intelligent.

For example, all things equal, a Dark Elf will always be higher level than an Orge. Now your choice between races becomes rather interesting. Would you rather have a race that will, in theory, be a higher level, but lack certain special abilities (i.e. regen, stats, stun, etc.) or get those advantages, but be lower level? Makes for an interesting decision.

Exception: In the very end game say, when a Dark Elf has to spend aa points on getting the ability to 2H bash, which an Ogre already has, the races almost balance out, except the Ogre still has a few advantages. Interpret as you wish....

So, when analyzing races, one of the primary questions should be, who levels faster. You'll find that, in general, the races that level faster are inferior, as they dont have certain special abilities. The fact that they level faster, however, is Verants way of balancing them out.
