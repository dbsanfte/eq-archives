You know, I've been wondering about that myself. The whole idea of an undead pet suiciding because it's master goes invisible seemed kind of screwed up.

The way I always thought it worked was that regular invisibility simply makes it so you can't be seen. But the undead don't really "see" you, they're sensing your life force, something that regular invisibilty can't mask. On the other hand, Invisibilty vs Undead only masks your life force and does nothing to physically obscure you. Which is why when you cast IU, the undead can't see you, but living NPC's as well as other players still can. So if you cast Gather Shadows, your pet, being undead and still able to sense you, should still know exactly where you are. Add to that the magical (mental, spiritual?) link you share with your pet, and you could argue that even IU shouldn't gank ol' Larry.

But if what Thacker said is true about the Necro exploit (and I'd bet my last bone chip it is), then I can finally see why it works the way it does...sort of. I suppose the reason it never occurred to me was that I never played a Necro, so the idea of my pet ever being able to solo anything that would give me xp also never occurred to me. But why don't they make invis work like FD? Put the pet on a timer and kill it if you stay invised for too long. They could even make up some explanation like "the magical aura that surrounds you and blocks detection of either your physical form or life essence interferes with the link between you and your pet, degrading it over time to the point where it is finally broken." I'd buy that. Close enough, anyway.

If they did it that way, you still couldn't let your pet do all your fighting for you while you slept, but you could call a pet at the zone-in (or other SS), park him there, regain your mana (really important for a Troll like me when your pet drains 30% of your mana pool), invis up, run to where you want to be and finally summon your pet. Three minutes should be enough, in most cases. Five would be better.
