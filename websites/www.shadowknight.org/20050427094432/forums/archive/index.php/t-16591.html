Experience penalty? The regen more than makes up for it. I have a 60 iksar monk and he leveled like lightning compared to my human monk. And also compared to my 65 ogre shaman, my ogre warrior, and my ogre SK. Regen is a huge help leveling pre-60.

Troll/iksar penalties are 20% and ogre is 15%. A 20% penalty really stinks. My ogres, with a 15% penalty, took much longer to hit 60 than all my friends did. Very often, in the same groups, they would join the group later than I did and leave earlier, but with more experience. That can lead to no small moping on my part sometimes. But the 5% penalty for being a troll/iksar compared to being an ogre is nothing at all -- certainly nothing when you have regen to compensate for it to help get you through the lower levels. And it doesn't suck at high levels either, though it's not as astounding as it is at the lower levels. Having taken my iksar monk to 60 and 18 AA's and leveled other toons up pretty high too, with and without fungi's, I respect regen a lot.

My ogre SK still tanks great at 59 with a fungi on, though he has a nice cultural BP with over 50 AC, no dex/agi penalties to it unlike the fungi, and 60 HP. DEX you can max get very high with a focus spell, and if you are a higher end toon, you may even be lucky enough to have a primal/prismatic to top out your DEX.

AGI can remain problematic, but luckily it rarely means much to the game, or to you personally unless you hit a "breakpoint." As far as the iksar goes. I have an iksar SK on the firiona vie server (which is the real reason I loiter and lurk here.) From a role-playing standpoint the iksar aren't so much evil in the same sense of the world as dark elves, ogres, and trolls, as they are very alien in their mindset, and utterly amoral. Iksar are very cold emotionally, being almost without them in the mammalian sense. What they value is honor, loyalty, and power. An iksar shadow knight spreads fear (which is what their god wants) merely by existing and pursuing their goals. Imagine a race of super-intelligent velociraptors (based on the ones in the 1st Jurassic Park novel, not the movie) and you get the sort of pack (expanded to their empire) and hunter mentality that pervades iksar racial psychology.

Its interesting, but on Firiona Vie, the all-iksar RP guild Skathris Ven'Sho, who are die-hard Cabilis loyalists, find themselves more often working with good-race guilds than evil-race guilds, although they aren't especially chummy with anyone obviously ;)
