Atlis
Maiden's eye is the opening to uber SK practice.
At any moment someone can and most likely will train ten skeletons, xi vius, wolves, and bats right through your hunt. This is where you learn to exp in the midst of catastrophe.
Practice your high end splits by using the goranga scouts anywhere in the zone. They're level 55, so decent exp.
You've been PL'd, try fear kiting. Learn what kind of mess you can make by fearing once too many times. Also, appreciate fear kiting while you can.
I don't care how many 65's you have, the SK is not something you learn in 2 weeks. SK is a completely self sufficient class in that it can survive and prosper in just about any circumstance, but it takes practice and skill to do. Quadding with a druid is not the same as kiting with a SK. Pulling with a monk is not the same as pulling with a SK. Tanking as a warrior is not the same as tanking as a SK. DPS, likewise, is not the same.
Once you graduate from Maiden's Eye, go to the exp zones you've gone to with your other 65 chars. Without knowing how to split higher end mobs, or how to get OR manage your aggro (getting PL'd says you haven't practiced tanking in groups enough), your exp zones will suck no matter where you go.

When I was level 59, a level 65 shadow knight asked me how to keep aggro. I hold him in the same regard as I do those who ask me for ports, rez's, and shaman buffs.
Stop PL'ing, learn how to solo cleanly, then practice grouping in low danger areas such as Plane of Justice before you hit zones that kill your groupmates in three seconds.

Expect a flame when you've done this 5 times and still do not know how to do it on your own.
