My personal preference was Kurn's until 20. The bonechips you collect will take you a very long way. I think I still have around 2 full backpacks full in the bank.

At 20, you can either head to Paludal, or to LDoN, PC will only take you to 26-28, and LDoN can take you to 65. The advantage of PC over LDoN is that you don't have to look for a group in PC. You can just solo along one side or the other. Loot is decent, with bronze ringmail and bronze weapons dropping. I stayed in PC until 27, soloing one of the bandit camps from 22 to 27. (but you can group for them, but xp will go way down at 24, grouped, but is still decent).

Then you can go to the Feerot (sp) and do the lizard camps (solo or group), or to Warsliks to work golras (solo), or to SK (solo or group) to work on gnolls, any of these will take you to 30, and all 3 of these have decent loot... (up to 5pp, and most weapon drops are fs).

At 30, you can head into Split Paw (solo or group), which can take you to 35, perhaps higher. (don't go past the double doors, without a full group, and a mezzer) Or, you can go to Butcher (beware the dorf guards) to the crazed goblin camp (solo camp only), which is 3 spawn, single pull, and can take you to 39 (the last level about half the spawns are green/light blue, but it's an easy camp, so I stuck with it until 39, using a ranger, and going toe to toe with the mobs. they only hit for a max of 90) with loot drops of up to 5pp, and perhaps some small ringmail, and/or a fs dagger. Also, at 30, there is the Overthere, and, for soloing/group, hit Timorous Deep. for the golras on the island in the south east corner, I think. If you do these golras, stay away from the named one (starts with a H, I think).

All of this will easily get you to 39, with enough money to get halfway decently outfitted, but, also keep in mind, most of this is solo.

If you want to continue soloing, you can head to "Sol B", also known as Nagafen's Lair, and work the window room, and perhaps, the pool room, also. XP is quite good here, but takes a major drop at 45. :( Again, up to 5pp, plus fs. You can take your group here, as well.

At 45-50 you can head to the Dreadlands, and either pick up a group, or make one. Either way, when I was here, we were chain pulling, and I was averaging a level about every 4 hours, right up until I dinged 50.

As far as this problem you are having with people just grabbing loot, and not sharing, set up a loot order, alphabetical works good, or you can go by level, low to high, or high to low, and request that the other party members turn on the loot split. On top of that, familiarize yourself with some/most of the "rare" drops in the area you are hunting, and roll for them, most groups I've been in just do a /ran 100, with either the high roller or the low roller being the winner.

As was mentioned earlier, an alternate route is to do mainly LDoN's, but with that, you are faced with trying to find a group. If you play odd hours like I do, a lot of times you are better off going to a normal group xp spot, or going to solo. (There have been times I've waited for up to 2 hours for a group, and then said screw it, and went to solo.)

Enough of my ramblings. As I said, this route will take you to 50, all of it grouped, or solo. your preference.

What are the loot rules? In groups there is no sharing. People just grab loot whether need it or not. Usually its worse with newer players here on Stromm because older players don't need loot as much.
Loot rules are usually decided at the beginning of a group session. I prefer the master looter set up. Master looting means one person is designated to loot everything for the group. When the group is ready to disband (or somebody is ready to leave but the group is going to stay together) the master looter divy's out equal shares of the loot gathered. Sometimes a master looter "accidently" goes link dead right before the loot is handed out. If they come back and pass around the loot, great. If they don't come back, add that person to a "will not group with again" list. From what you described though, it sounds like you have some loot happy ninja clickers. Over time you'll learn who loot happy and who isn't loot happy - group with people that have the same loot concepts as you do and the game will be more fun for you.
