/nod -- With more time spent in dungeons due to LDoN, I have started being more observant with LOS and casters (not to say I wasn't paying attention before, just that I'm paying more attention now).

- For both PC's and NPC's, if the caster begins casting and the target goes out of LoS, the spell will still land if the target remains within range.

- If the caster begins casting but the target goes out of range, the spell does not land (I realize this contradicts one of the posts above, but just my observation).

- The best way to pull a chain-casting caster to your group is to duck around a corner. (I haven't tried the sit-down thing yet).

- Most offensive spells that didn't used to have an LoS restriction now do, but some spells (not a usual staple pulling/aggro spell) still don't. ;)

- A casting mob and a non-casting mob on a pull isn't necessarily a bad thing, it can separate the two to make for easy FD splitting (as long as you time the FD, of course).

Many of you have known these since the Befallen days but I spent a lot of time outdoors (hung out with druids and rangers a lot).

10-16-2003, 01:35 PM
LoS was removed from mobs a while ago, I remember them announcing it.

Incorrect.

They fiddled with it a few patches in a row so you might remember the first one. They made changes to casters a while back.

OLD WAY
NPC: didn't require a line of sight to start casting or land the spell. Can't remember for sure, but thier range seamed to be HUGE also.
PC: didn't require a line of sight to start casting a spell but DID require a line of sight to land a spell.

CURRENT WAY
NPC: Requires a line of sight to start casting a spell. Does NOT require a line of sight to land a spell, must be in spell range.
PC: Requires a line of sight to start casting a spell. Does NOT require a line of sight to land a spell, must be in spell range.

Yes sitting down seams to have them charge about 2/3 of the time instead of chain casting.
