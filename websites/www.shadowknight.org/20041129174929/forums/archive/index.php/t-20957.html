I've noticed a lot of ideas for improving knight tanking capacity in general. First question: When using a shield in combat, weren't they designed to block attacks from enemies?

Suggestion: When any class has a shield equiped they get a "mitigation" bonus (much like warrior innate mitigation) based upon the AC of the shield. Meaning, the shield does add a certain amount of "armor class" or armor rating and the mitigation % could be based upon that.

So something like a 20ac shield isnt going to mitigate as much damage as a 70 ac shield. It should be a significant % as well, being as shields block / deflect quite a bit of incoming damage. Something like 25-50% of the AC value.

This would do a few things:

a) Increase the tanking ability of all "tank" classes across the board, from beginner to end game. Shields are available at all levels of the game.

b) Give options. Does a warrior want to duel weild for more agro, better damage or use a shield and a sword to increase is tanking ability? It would open up a bit of variation to classes on how people want to play in different situations.

c) Everyone is happy. Warriors can use a shield without feeling as gimped. They would still tank significantly better with stonewall / defenisve and their innate mitigation. Knights would gain the ability to tank for group trials in GoD, and other places without being a "detriment" to the group.

But that would be too easy and be to logical. Though, whats logical about an ogre wearing plate armor summoning skeletons and feasting on blood?
