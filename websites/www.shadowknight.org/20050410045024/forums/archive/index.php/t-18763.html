DConquest
Well, there's OT'ing which is described... but there's also another type of " mez tanking " that is very important. Load Fear, Terror, or any other aggro spell that doesn't involve a DoT and when a sizeable force of mobs are inc or about to pop on your raid, you keep aggro until the mob is mezzed. This gives time for the chanters to debuff / mez the mobs, and prevents possible Healer deaths.

Also, when there are a number of mez'd mobs in camp. It's a good idea to aggro the hell out of them, so they rush you on break rather than the more fragile caster.

Groff Gorespiller
Taunt and Terror line work wonders on Mezz'd mobs, but I wouldn't suggest fear at your level. It also helps to know the mobs you're looking at, as some may be immune to changes in run speed or magic and so dropping a couple of darknesses on them could be ok (think Mummy mobs in MMC adventures). You could also use some of the non-damaging debuffs for a little extra aggro, though our debuffs don't seem to do a whole hell of a lot, and you might not have the extra spell slots.

Typically, before breaking Mez, if I'm MA, I'll toss my two biggest terrors simultaneously with a taunt in between, then repeat and drop a CD. I have yet to let a mob get to the chanter after mez is broken.
