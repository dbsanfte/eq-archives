1. Depends. If you're working on a split of snarable mobs, and a successful split matters, use festering darkness. Non-snarable casters, use cancel magic. Where timing matters, use a bow. Pulling is a "learn by doing" thing. No hard rule for all situations.

2. CD and Fear. Since you played a warrior, you should know how taunt works (shhh, dont need to argue the validity of that =P). There is no snap agro that rivals the taunt button. For maximum effect, press taunt *then* cast an agro spell to give a big buffer.

3. Again, depends. Until you gain more familiarity with the class and can spot the places you should jump in, just do what runcade said. You have snares, FD, harmshield and massive agro generation at your disposal ... keep an eye out for where those would be useful in pulling situations. You're a 3rd rate tank with decent lifetaps and massive agro ... watch for where that would be useful in add situations. Dont think we need to give much away here, its pretty obvious when you keep your tools in mind and see what goes on in raids.

4. Soloing at 65 sucks ass, dont do it under any circumstances. Grouping, *shrug*, in-group buffs are fine. There should be no need to get external buffers in any situation. You come with your own C3 at all times (pact of hate).

5. There are no good GoD AA's. But theres plenty of worthwhile Luclin/PoP AA's that should keep you busy for another 200 points. No joke, take resists over that GoD crap.

6. www.eqwindows.com

Good luck. Its a damn fun, if misunderstood, class. No one is gonna hand you a role, you're gonna have to make it. Thats part of the fun. I highly recommend finding a veteran SK buddy on your server who doesnt mind fielding your questions in game.

I'd advise going somewhere fairly safe and practising how to FD split, etc- CoM is a good place for this.

When you do get comfy playing him and join pickup groups there are going to be people who are used to good SKs and will expect you to be able to pull well- and others who will have no idea that SKs can do anything but hold agro and look cool. Be ready for both instances.

2. What spells should i use for aggro control?
CD, Fear (Terror if the level of the mob is in question), and the non spell things like taunt, sit, etc. Getting agro is easy, agro control is the next step.


3. Whats the SK's main job during a raid usually?
Tanking, offtanking, tankmezzing, saving the chanter's ass, pulling, voicing, kiting, distracting, dragging.


4. What buffs are a must before a exp grind? (virt,kei, ect)
K/9s, C4 or C5 (Pact of Hate is 1 mana per tick more than KEI), shammy buff if you can get em, beastie crack, etc.


5. What are the good GoD AA's?
The ones they gave the Druids.

Culdahl
As an SK you will usually be MT and assisting someone else on pulls. Just do a /assist <puller> and cast festering darkness at max range on incoming pull. Unlike others here, I dont care much for the fear for aggro method....your 2 lower level "terror" spells work just as well (better in my experience). Also, make sure you cast your best "voice" spell on yourself and keep it up. You will also need to become familiar with FD splitting.....you can practice this on much lower level mobs solo somewhere so you don't wipe your group:-) Don't worry about learning to fear-kite as you will never use it at 65....even aggro kiting is a bit useless and i would definitely not suggest it until you are familiar with the character (don't want to screw around aggro-kiting BoT or PoS mobs:-). As far as buffs go, KEI is a must and any HP buffs you can get are good as well....I disagree with getting kazaad + Bo9 unless you have a 62ish cleric and druid in your group (these spells have very short durations compared to Virtue). As far as 2 boxing a rogue and SK, this would have been great for fear kiting pre-level 59, but is useless post 59...would suggest LFG with friends. Your job in a raid will vary.....you will likely not be the MA or SA, as a 65 warrior with /disc def is far better at tanking raid level mobs. You can use your group mana/HP taps to help your group in a raid, plus you can assist on corpse summons, aggro control, offtanking, etc.

Noctix
I can offer some unique perspective on your situation, as I am the only person I know of who 2boxes a SK and a rogue 100% of the time.

I really like running the SK and the rogue on the same machine using EQW. I set it up so that one window is almost directly on top of the other, with both windows at the same resolution. Since my SK is always MT and my rogue always wants to be behind the mob, my characters are always looking directly at each other. I find this very helpful for keeping my bearings and for mob positioning. Also, it doesn't take very long before you can judge whether the other character is out of melee range using this method. This allows for very quick corrections.

You will find that you can be much more efficient if you pay attention to character positioning before mobs get to camp. Get your SK facing the direction that the pull will come from, so you can pull it directly in with your agro spells. You will want your rogue out in front of your SK, facing the SK, so that as soon as the mob gets to the SK, your rogue is already in attack position. Remember to hide/sneak your rogue to help avoid agro from the incoming mob.

2boxing a SK and a rogue, you can learn a LOT about agro management. I keep agro primarily with CD, using spears for dps and a little extra agro. Since you have control of both the agro spells and evade, it is very easy to keep agro on the SK once you get the timing down.

The biggest challange during chain pulling is making the transition from mob to mob when there are runners. I have my rogue chase runners while my SK gains agro on the next mob. This takes some getting used to as you have to be able to make a lot of quick movements on both characters at the same time. Also, if there isn't another mob incoming when the last mob starts to run, make sure you get your SK down to med as quickly as possible while your rogue finishes off the runner. As MT, you don't get much med time so grab it when it's available.

I really love SK atk taps, it's so nice to have that permanent atk buff for my rogue. Also, since you always have a rogue for dps, your SK can really pay attention to agro, lifetaps, etc rather than being overly concerned about dps. I would say that AC/HP buffs for your SK and haste for both are the most important outside buffs. KEI is nice but you can always run with Deathly Temptations or Pact of Hate for mana regen. I very rarely have KEI on my SK and mana isn't really a problem.

I guess the biggest thing I can offer you is that in order to successfully 2box a SK with a rogue, you need to think of both characters as you "mains" rather than one as a main and the other as a bot. Both require your full attention at all times in order to run efficiently. At this point, this is second nature to me, I kind of think of my two characters as a single mob shredding machine rather than two individual characters. This is different than how most people 2box but, in my opinion, far more fun.

Good luck and if you have any other questions, please ask!

Edit: A couple more things...first, learn to play without ever using your mouse (during combat at least). It is a huge pain in the neck to find your mouse pointer every time you switch windows.

Second, the reason I said I position my windows *almost* directly on top of each other is so that I can see my backstab button when I am in the SK's window. You want to hit that button the second it pops so seeing it is very important. After a while, you will get a sense of timing and you will be able to switch windows, hit backstab, and go back to the SK without even thinking about it. But until then, make sure you can see that button!
