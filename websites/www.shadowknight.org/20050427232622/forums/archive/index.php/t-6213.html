The biggest problem with my race, dark elf (how come everyone seems to forget that DEs can be shadowknights, too?) is the lack of strength. Armor weighs a ton and can become a problem. Yet an SK without armor class is ... well ... almost as bad as a ranger.

My solution? I took a long term view and learned smithing. At level 40 (real level, not smithing; my smithing is 180) I now wear a full suit of DE cultural enchanted adamantite plate I made myself, which adds strength stats and weighs a lot less than other plate.

I wore sentient for awhile but DEs already start out with pretty high intelligence. Plus, the stat bonuses for INT for enchanted adamantite plate are better than sentient, and it adds strength, agility, dexterity and hella good AC without the heavy weight.

There are ways to compensate. You can both play up your strengths and shore up your weaknesses.

Lizarde
I think it is because people rarely choose their SK based on the starting stats. The race they choose is based on other factors, such as roleplay value or they just like the look. I chose Iksar because I liked how they look, no other consideration whatsoever.

From there, you seek ways to smooth out any perceived weakness in starting stats. If you're Erudite, for instance, you may want to improve your STR and STA to complement your INT. An Ogre may seek out more INT and mana items to complement their melee capability.

Also, there are some stats that you want raised to a minimal level, such as AGI to 75 (to avoid paying an AC penalty) and STA to 100 (to avoid paying a fatigue penalty). Thus classes who are deficient in those stats will look to increase them.
