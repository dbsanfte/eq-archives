Tukka
I don't know if this idea has been tossed around much, but I've had it in my head almost since I started playing, and unlike a lot of the ideas I had when I first started playing, this one still seems pretty good and hasn't been implemented yet.

It seems clear by now that Sony doesn't want to give us any new spell slots/gems to force all the casters to make a strategic choice about what spells they want to have memorized. It's a drag, but I can understand and agree the idea.

However, it makes rebuffing needlessly irritating. Having to de-memorize a spell, memorize a buff, wait for it to un-grey, cast, de-memorize it, and re-memorize the original spell is very annoying, but I don't think it puts you at that much of a tactical disadvantage... it just forces you to take a few more moments to rebuff that you otherwise wouldn't have to. An unnecessary pause in the action.

So I think they should give us two or three new spell gems, but to use them the spell you memorize in them would have to meet the following conditions:

- Spell is beneficial
- Base/minimum spell duration is greater than 8 minutes
- Recast time is less than 1 minute

I realize this wouldn't resolve any critical class balance issues (and would benefit us less than some other classes) and recent UI improvements have made switching out spells a bit less burdensome than it once was... but if they aren't going to give us any more normal/real spell mem slots, then I think they could throw us a bone and use this proposal or something like it as an incentive to buy a future expansion.

I use the Spell sets for buffing. I have a Buff spell set which I use when the group first buffs up or when we have a med break. When I need the short duration buffs I swap in the buff I want and then reload the spell set I've selected for the current group. Loading the spell set is faster as I only have so search for the one set entry. It also preserves the timing on spells such as Voice and Zevfeers.
