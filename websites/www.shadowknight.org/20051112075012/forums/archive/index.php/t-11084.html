We're fast hurtling toward an argue over the semantics of "Dynamic". Personally, I'm not whining over anything. I dislike what was done to Grobb and the inability to "save it". That's mostly an IC issue with just a hint of nostaliga for my favorite city way back when Mino axes were king and I personally petitioned the Tad Skin/Froglock Meat for a cruddy two-hander quest, as exploitable since it could be done ad infinitum.

I agree that having a real fight for Grobb, a newbie zone.. and having that fight dicate the varying placements of another newbie zone, would have been a nightmare. Starting cities should be more or less safe and secure once established, so that new players can get a handle on things without having to worry that the glut of info they've just digested is going to end up dissagreeing with them later. That's a good enough reason right there to make the true "newbie" guards more or less invulnerable or at least not worth the effort to slay.

What we have is the creation of a newbie zone, an area that by it's very definition is designed to be static and uniform across all servers. The location is a bit of shock as is the moving around of the Trolls, but for the most part we know it'll settle down into a same ole-same ole with new faces and old skin color. When it comes right down to it, nothing more "new" has occured then a dock suddenly appearing in some zone, leading to new places to explore.

The point is that this isn't really any more dynamic then any other expansion that has ever been added. In fact, in most of the in-game ways, it's going to have less of an actual impact on the way the game is played then just about any other expansion; well, given what little we actually know now.

All the truely game-changing, dynamic features are meta-game.

A new race with an old newbie zone isn't something that thrills me all that much and as far as I'm concerned, they could have left the trolls alone. Personally, I would have rather seen a new troll area (like the Coldains) that was under seige. Give me a place to explore and actively participate in what was going on.

To me, that's dynamic gaming and it's something that they have worked on in other infancy forms through Velious with the faction wars and later with Luclin in more active mob wars.

The thing is, that may very well be the case. I may just get my wish. None of us know what all the expansion may entail and in the end it may very well be the most exciting addition to date. I just don't personally find any of the in-game changes to be very dynamic or deserving of much in the way of "Hoo-hah!".

... yet.
