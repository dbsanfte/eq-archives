AFAIK:

SCF1 = 2% chance to perform a 33% crit
SCF2 = 4% chance to perform a 66% crit
SCF3 = 7% chance to perform a 100% crit

Keep in mind that it is also not a set amount based on the spell itself, but rather a % based on the damage dealt. So a 500dd unresisted with SCF3 would do 1k. However if the spell is partially resisted (say it would have only done 400 dmg without SCF) then the damage added by SCF scales down (spell would hit for 800, not 900 in this example with SCF3). Same is true in reverse with say a DD spell doing more base damage due to ID items and such.

That formula is incorrect lorddaeth. This is an almost direct quote from the Graffe forum. This question was about Innate wizard criticals, but I've modified it to be useful for us. The thread can be found Here (http://www.graffe.com/forums/showthread.php?t=13628)

The posted formula is correct, and well proven.

In the case of Crit and with a Focus:
(Base Spell Damage * Focus%) + (Base Spell Damage * Crit%) + Base Damage

The Crit does NOT crit Focused Damage, only the base damage of the spell. The Focus does not increase crit damage, only the base damage of the spell.

Focus and Crit are applied to base damage seperately, then summed for total damage.

Ice Comet : 1120 dmg.

So, Base Damage is 1120.
Add in a Max of (1120 * 0.20) from ID.
Add in a Max of (1120 * AA level (0.33, 0.66 or 1.0) from Critical AA

Theoretical Max on AA Crit with Improved Damage: 2464dmg without bard song.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Thus if you took good old Touch of Innoruuk you'd get the following :

Base damage : 470

470+(470*0.2)+(470*1) = 1034dmg Or 470 * 2.2 (or 2.35 if you're using bard songs into your calculations) since you can just add the modifiers together.
