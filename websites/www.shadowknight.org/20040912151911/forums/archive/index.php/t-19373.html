Khaguk
Only 8 spell gems... it's a constant trade-off between versatility and being effective in only one way, along with driving yourself crazy if you happen to keep switching spells during split-second downtimes.

Moreso in LDoN than other places, because there's no set time-limit aside from always wanting faster xp elsewhere, you really want DPS. Look at your spell line-up... this is what it would look like for maximum DPS, factoring in the new DD. We have to assume you don't need any FDs for pulling and are in a situation where you don't need it to save your own hide. Being able to keep yourself healed + having a class that can save you if an accident happens, for example. I'm not saying it'll be 100% safe, but...

1. ToI. You want a tap up there, it's mini burst DPS that can also save your skin if you need it.

2-4. Your 3 highest spears.

5. BoC.

6. ZB.

7. CD / fear.

These two aren't related to YOUR DPS, but... there's no reason not to keep ZB running. Effective FTX and regen XX to your groupmates is still DPS. If you can get by without snap aggro, heck, you can swap out the CD/fear for a spell that keeps dropping. If you have a chanter mana buff, then that's only really either your pet's buff (avoidable) or your spell proc buff.

8. BoD if it's necessary to keeping yourself running. However, assuming the SK is focusing on DPS, we can pretend that self-healing is a complete side-effect and not what we're shooting for. (Self healing's only really valid in LDoN normals, anywhere else with decent XP it's hard or impossible to pull off) That said, possibly stick in AoH here (trade-off between increased DPS or getting AoP's AC boost in here).

Now... there're some spells that die off fast. MC and pact of hate come to mind, as well as our self pet buff. You don't have an 'extra' slot up there, per-se, although the last one is the most variable, not as strictly tied to DPS. Depending on which spells you need to keep casting yourself, you'd need to either decide to keep switching out spell gems, or remove a spell from the above line-up (probably the lowest spear) and swap in the buff.

The problem with a DPS set-up like that? Your mana. You might find yourself slipping too fast. Losing mana over time is okay, especially in a LDoN, where if you end the adventure with mana remaining, that was (as a SK) DPS lost. In an exp group elsewhere, assuming a constant stream of mobs, variable shifting of the mana supply's okay, shouldn't keep yourself full so if there *IS* a break, you're not sitting there twiddling your thumbs...

It depends on the SK. YMMV, especially considering worn FT, AAs and focus effects.
