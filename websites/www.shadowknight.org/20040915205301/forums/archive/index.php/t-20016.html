Well, here is my thoughts on the group leadership now that it's been around for a while.

First of all, if you are going to do it, I would strongly suggest going with the HP add, Delgate MA, or maybe the atk one (though less emphasis there) and then working on the others. It's not so much that these are so awesome as it is that if you have skills that the entire group wants, it's going to be easier to get leadership and gain more skill. I chose to do Find Path to PC because of a couple friends I group with that are always getting lost. Although for me this was a very logical move based on the people I group with, there are at least 2-3 groups I've been with that someone else was working on leadership, but didn't have any skills. They weren't willing to turn leadership over for Find Path to PC, but if I had a more useful skill, they most likely would have. Basically you chose the skills that will make people hand you leadership for so you can gain more leadership in the end.

Second, you need to go all or nothing in leadership. I feel that a month or two down the road, leadership is going to be based on who has the most/best skills to offer a group. If you are just slightly working on leadership, then you aren't going to be leader much and you aren't going to get skills. If you want leadership skills, you have to work hard at them so that you will always be chosen if there is a conflict. Currently I have about 7 leadership points, and it's more than anyone else I've grouped with so far. If my prediction is right, then I will continue to get group leadership and there won't ever be any conflicts with other people wanting to be leader. If you have half a dozen useful group skills, and the current leader just has one, then the entire group is going to want group leader to turn it over and he won't have as much choice.

Third, leadership is only minimally helpful. 90 HP for 18 points is a lot of points to spend, and 90 HP isn't that many. Heck, you can get almost 250 HP from tribute (or 150 if you have capped stamina). Most of our AAs that give HP give more HP per level than this skill. 30 atk isn't a huge deal either. Aura of Hate, which you have to parse over a period of time to see any difference is over 100 atk. It takes almost 200 atk to have a difference that you can notice without having to parse. Yes, all of these can add up and it can help when you stack lots of little things, but just keep in mind that all of these skills are only of minimal use, so you need to decide for yourself if it's worth it. For every group leadership point you lose about one AA. For my 7 group points I could have 7 AAs. It's very debatable in my mind whether it's really worth it in the end or not. Personally I'm going to still keep going with it because for me I enjoy the group skills, and as many who notice my posts, I always tell people to do what is fun for you rather than what is going to be the most uber.

Fourth, Leadership seems to be based on the level of mobs that are being killed or number (not sure exactly on level, just a guess), but you gain leadership at the same rate at any level. You play a 15th level guy in groups and you will get leadership at the same rate as higher levels. To me, this says that leadership at pre-50 levels is more worth it than later levels in some ways. Anyways, it also means that if you are in a raid, you definately want group leadership. If you are group leader in a raid, you can get some awesome leadership points, even though exp is horrible. Last night my guild tried RZ a couple times and failed both times. Despite that, I earned two yellow bubbles of leadership exp, and not enough AA exp to really notice. You *DO* always want group leadership in raids. This is the one time that I can say that it is definately worth it 100% of the time. The gains outweigh the losses without a doubt (since there is practically no loss since you aren't really getting noticable xp anyways)

Just realized that I forgot to post some extra info I learned about group exp.

Group exp is based (from what I can tell) entirely off of the number of mobs killed that you get exp for. It is not based off of any percentage of exp like normal AA/exp. If you fight in LDoN, you kill more mobs that are worth less exp than BoT for example. You don't get as much AA as you would in BoT, but you will get more group exp because you are killing more mobs.

In addition, group exp seems to go up at the same rate no matter what level mobs you are killing. If you are 20th level and put exp to group, you'll gain it just as fast as if you are 65th level and put exp to group.

I have found that on LDoN adventures that I typically gain half a group point and a bit less than half an aa. This means that when I do two adventures I'll usually have about 1 group point and a bubble or so short of an AA. That means that over 10 adventures, I'll have about 5 group points and 4 AAs. Had I not had group exp going, I would have had about 5 AAs, so as I posted earlier, for each AA you lose in exp, you earn about 5 group points. That's really not too bad of a deal.

That means that the health ones that add 30 HP end up costing about 1 AA worth of exp. I don't think there is anyone here that would say that 30 HP isn't worth 1 AA. heck, you end up spending about 3 AAs for the same HP when you get planar durability.

Also, if you are raiding, especially raids like VT or such where there are lots of mobs killed, make sure you get group leadership. You get group points at the same rate for each mob killed in a raid as you would if you were in a group and killed those same mobs. I always try to get leadership in a raid that will be killing lots of mobs for that very reason. Few times i've earned a couple bubbles of group exp from a PoP raid.
