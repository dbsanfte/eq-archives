Originally posted by SkTyger
A few quick questions:
- What is Bind Wounds cap? 200? Just read that Beast Lords actually can go over 200, so can do extra binding (i.e. increased amount per bandage, up to 70% health...) So was wondering what the cap was on Sk's...

Our Bind Wounds caps at 200. This means we cannot, without AA skills, bind wounds up to more than 50% health. That natural ability comes at 201 BW skill. Maxing first aid gives us that ability to bind wounds up to 80% health. I'm not positive how many hp we heal with 1 bandage at 200 skill, but I'm pretty sure it's 50 HP. IMO, I think you're better off with a fungi, and sitting to med for the occasional combat taps.

- On ND, I think the description says that it is 2,5,10% extra hit points off of the 'stamina and stamina effect', I distinctly remember the wording for 'stamina effects'... What does that mean? If you have sta gear on, or a sta buff, does that also add on top with ND towards final hit points calculation?

ND bonus is applied to your total, unbuffed, hitpoints. This includes stamina gear, and stamina buffs. It does not include direct HP buffs. If you have 3000 hitpoints dressed and unbuffed, ND1 will give you a total of 3060 hp. ND2 will give you a total of 3150 hp, and ND3 will give you a total of 3300 hp.

- On Combat Agility, what happens when that works? I.e. is that like a new/seperate skill check added for another type of 'Dodge'?

From my understanding this improves the chance a mob will miss you by 2, 5, and 10%. IE, if a certain mob has a 50% chance to miss you, he will have a 51% chance to miss you with CA1, a 52.5% chance to miss you with CA2, and a 55% chance to miss you with CA3. This does not effect parry, dodge, riposte in any way, the hit/miss roll comes before the combat deflection rolls.

- CF still somewhat messed up, in that its not really worth going past CF 1 yet, is that right?

Supposedly: The first point adds a 2% chance, the second increases that to a 4% chance and the third raises it to a final 7% chance.

Reality: Parsings have shown very little improvement over CF1 from CF2 & CF3. If this has been fixed, I don't think anyone has noticed it yet.

- Does the 2HBash skill help/add Bash when you are in Skellie Form using a 2 Hander?

Yes.
