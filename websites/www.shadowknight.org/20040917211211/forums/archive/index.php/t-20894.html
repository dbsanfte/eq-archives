Actually, Festering Darkness (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3400&source=Live) has a faster casting time than Fear (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=229&source=Live). It is considerably less mana, however.

Festering has the added bonus of damage and snare. I have a decent mana pool and FT, so I use festering for aggro if mobs are unmezzable or there is no mezzer present. Otherwise, I use fear.

Now, if you're talking about aggro generation benefits only, you'll probably want to compare Clinging Darkness (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=344&source=Live) to Fear instead. It has even faster casting time, and half the mana of fear. But the added benefits are negligble: the dot is nothing, the snare is piddly and not very long lasting at all.

Aggro generation, in my experience, seems comparable between all three.

I hate to tell you you're wrong Squigle but:

Fear's much more mana-efficient than festering. In many high level groups you need to chain cast fear/CD to hold aggro so the casters can do decent DPS. Try this with festering and you'll go oom quickly.

I do use festering for DPS occasionally, but only as a move of desperation in low DPS groups. Its 8 DPS is neat for me but a joke to my caster and rogue friends who'd think I was mad if I claimed my DPS was useful, or even relevant, to most groups.

If you want extra aggro on mezzed mobs, it's far safer and more efficient to cast fear before you break mez than try to squeeze in a (comparatively) low-aggro nuke after you've dotted it.

And disarm gives negligible aggro compared with spells like fear and snares, or even terrors.
