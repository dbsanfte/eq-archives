A word of wisdom to any who might read - and yes, it may already have been said, but it doesn't hurt anybody for me to say it here.

The paladins are absurdly easy to split. Yes, yes, they are all clumped into a neat little group, but just run them off, play with them, etc. They do not path, and are non-aggro, so once you clear with FD you're fine to find a nice angle and play with them some more. I got the first 3 single by doing this, each of which dropped blood (dumb luck). My group was upset with the ease of the fight, so we pulled the other 3 all at once. The cleric tanked one, since she was bored and nobody needed a heal the whole damn time.
Croix VorAbaddon
Another thing to consider is that the pallies don't see through invis. Combine that with their normal non-KoS nature, and you have a winning combo:

Step one: DoT a pally
Step two: FD
Step three: Have someone else around, ranger, chanter, etc, invis you ((Popping with an insta click invis may work, but I don't have one to test with.))
Step four: Stand. Only the pally who was DoT'd should aggro.

If you can get away before it hits you, or casts on you, it'll follow alone. I've noticed the one frog doesn't seem to cast as much.

Now, of course, you could just pet pull em easily... but who wants to do things the easy way?
