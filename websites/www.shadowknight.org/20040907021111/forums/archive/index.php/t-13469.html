Vuloshek
When I was a gnome partnered with an ogre, the real difference I noticed in the low-mid levels was that he'd consistently do 10-15 more damage with a MoSS on average (someone hooked us up with MoSSes, we were new and didn't even know those were great weapons). He had as many STR items as I did at this point, so our strength gap was the same as it was lvl 1.

However, I had it in me to do some extra kiting if things went awry - generally, if he was kiting, it was 1 snare, 1 fear, fight until it dies whether fear breaks early or not, and that worked fine for him (and again, we had nearly equal INT items, so the INT gap was same as always as well). My downtime was spent medding, his healing. Pretty well equal; his edge was mostly that it was easier control-wise for him, but this justifies the XP penalty. My edge was that I could more consistently take on whites or yellows solo (extra mana to recast on resists), and I could still fight if I was a bit hurt so long as I knew I had mana. Of course, if things DID go terribly wrong, his chances of making it to the zoneline were a little better (but not much at all around lvl 20).

Our differences showed clearly when it was just us two. If I'd step back to lifetap while he tanked, I came back swinging with mana to spare and full life. When he stepped back in, he'd have 3/4 health and be LOM, but then again it didn't much matter, and I'd be about as hurt as he was after taking over tanking for that short time.

We didn't get far enough to do comparisons post-30, when magic starts to be more and more important.

This only proves 1 thing - a gnome SK and an ogre SK make a kickass duo. When you're medding next to each other, it's also pretty funny looking (I used to run back and forth jumping over him while he sat if he went AFK - it was hard to get over the crown of his head).

There are probably many ways a gnome can outdamage an ogre using magic, but it's not an issue. Either way, DPS isn't so great, it's all about control and tolerance - what can you do to prevent disaster, and how long can you take the punishment if you screw up. At least that's my take. All races are viable some just play differently. Biggest real differences are size and extra abilities.

Large size is a pain sometimes but if you carry shrink potions you can get around this.

Regen is huge (hence the experience penalty) for soloing SKs (my troll seldom needs to lifetap) but fairly useless in groups unless there is no true healer.

INT is not as big a deal as some make of it especially since they fixed Mental Corruption and C3 became so common. At 61 my int is 98 and I have no problems with lack of mana. I fear kite DBs one after the next without ever resting and have been doing so since the MC fix. Between Regen, MC and C3 my bubbles fill noticably during a kite and often I end the kite with more mana and health than I started it.

Bigger mana pool is nice when things go bad but solo SKs should FD as soon as it looks bad rather than trying to fight their way out anyway. In groups maintaining mana can be a problem but all tanks face this problem in groups where they never sit, large pool just means that it will be one more fight before you have to be careful how you spend mana.

So nothing matters but having fun. Although getting a plus to WIS on cultural armor don't help a whole lot.
