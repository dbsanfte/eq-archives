Slein Jinn
There are currently two tanking gaps between the three tank classes that must be addressed. One gap between Shadowknights and Paladins, and another between Warriors and Knights in general. These issues cannot be treated entirely independently from one another. Changes should return knights to preference in the offtanking role, establish equal desirability in a group setting, and bring the two knight classes into line with one another.

- Link the paladin Force of XXX line of stuns so that only one can be used at a time. This would give high-level Paladins a single line of stuns progressively upgraded to keep up with level increases. Upgrades should not increase in power, as the effects of stun spells scale to mob DPS.

- Replace the paladin Quellious' Word line (10.0 sec stuns) with a 0.0 sec stun with a high resist mod and low cast/recast time and a moderate mana drain. Approximate mana drain could range from the level 54 Quellious' Word of Tranquility at 65 mana to the level 69 Quellious' Word of Tranquility at 115 mana, with possible tweaking needed to the numbers depending on the final cast and recast times. These spells would fill paladin role of interrupting casters.

- Add a “Recourse Box” – a buff window specifically for recourse effects.

- Give Shadowknights a 20% accuracy debuff with high aggro. 18 sec duration, 12 sec recast, 1 sec cast, -55 resist check to magic, equivalent restrictions to paladin stuns. The high aggro is essential to prevent it from becoming a support spell – furthermore, it would be a welcome boost to aggro generation for Shadowknights.

This should be a new spell line rather than a single spell. Its max level should increase progressively along with paladin stuns, but its power should remain constant, as its effect scales with mob damage output.

Regarding mobs with hard coded to-hit ratios (I.E. evasive and similar are ineffective):
This debuff would need to be specifically coded to bypass the hard coded to-hit ratio, or its ineffectiveness on these mobs would need to be taken into account when creating immunities for the spell line. An equivalent portion of the mob population in a given area would need to be effected by the new debuff as is effected by stuns. The ideal solution would be to simply bypass the hard coded to-hit ratios on these mobs, but if that cannot be achieved, this must be considered to prevent Paladin stuns from having broader applications, creating an imbalance.

Note that a Paladin chain casting the remaining line of stuns would avoid 31% of melee damage, barring interrupts, fizzles, and resists. Shadowknight’s lower percentage of avoidance would be balanced by its greater reliability (duration exceeds recast by 4*cast time) and the spell Aura of Pain.

- Give knights two disciplines to return them to their offtanking role.
--- Melee immunity discipline: autoparry, 18-second duration, 40-minute reuse.
--- DI-mitigation discipline: 30-35% DI-mitigation, decrease melee damage modifier by 20%, 72-second duration, 4.5-minute reuse.

They should be on separate reuse timers, but the mitigation discipline may share the reuse timer of the current defensively oriented knight disciplines.

Note that the mitigation discipline would have a lower total active time than defensive, in addition to mitigating notably less. If used whenever available, Defensive is active 28.57% of the time; the knight discipline would be active 26.66% of the time.

- Allow Shadowknights access to the Stonewall AA ability.

- Provide Shadowknights with an upgrade to Death Peace. (Specifics as yet undecided.)

- Change the Malo lines of spells to debuff disease. It is the one resist that is not debuffed by the spell line and there is no justification for this. Disease is behind every other resist in the extent to which it is debuffed on raids, and it is the resist on which Shadowknights rely most heavily.

- Put Spell Casting Subtlety on a toggle; as it stands, purchasing this AA is currently detrimental to Shadowknights.



Paladin stuns are at the root of a lot of the current class balance problems for the tank classes. The number of usable stuns varies by mob level and they are extremely powerful against blue trash mobs and the like, are entirely useless against immune mobs, and address only part of the problem with others (incoming DPS vs. being one-rounded). The above changes would reduce paladin reliance on stuns and make their impact more consistent. Currently, the impact of stuns varies greatly from mob to mob depending on level, immunity, resistance, etc. Paladins would be compensated for the lost stun power with the much-needed tanking disciplines.

Shadowknights would be brought into line with paladins through the accuracy debuff, which would effectively parallel paladin stuns. These abilities, together with the rapid-fire mitigation discipline, would cement knights in their traditional offtanking roles while having little impact on the traditional warrior role of raid main tanking. All three tank classes would be viable for single group content.

Note that this would actually be an improvement for warriors in a group setting in terms of power relative to Paladins, as stuns in their current state are potentially very overpowered against common XP mobs.

Slein Jinn
I'm aware of that, and that's the problem. Deciding which situation is more balanced. From a raid standpoint, I'd say mana is the better option - higher regen rates and generally higher mana pools mean that it'd give the disc more staying power if it operated on mana, and that's in line with the offtanking role. It's also logical in that mana for hybrids is fairly analagous to endurance for melee's in that our other abilities are generally mana based and melee's other abilities are endurance based.

In the group setting, however, mana gives us an advantage. Our mana regen rates are higher than warrior endurance regen rates, so, hypothetically speaking, with each class using the disciplines in a group setting, we'd have an unfair advantage. Now we all know that in practice, neither disc would be used on XP trash very often, but the theoretical potential kinda has to be considered.

An argument in favor of mana regarding a group setting, however, is that, if it were endurance based, we could throw it around at will, saving only enough endurance for an emergency, because our other abilities are mana-based. A warrior cannot because running out of endurance limits his other abilities. As such, making it mana based would tie it to our other tools the way endurance based discs are tied to warrior tools (I.E. Incite line), which would be a positive parallel.

The difference in size of endurance pool vs. size of mana pool isn't really a factor so much, because the cost can scale accordingly - it's mostly a question of the balance impact of the regen rates and the fact that other relavant SK "tools" run on mana.
