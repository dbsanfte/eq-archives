Brabble
My advice would be:
1. Pull if you can and do initial melee damage.
2. Chain your cd/fast cast aggro spells
3. stand right on the mob's toes
4. Hold taunt until mobs turns (which you're doing now).
5. If you are not doing the pulling, make sure that the puller actually brings the mob to you, and not to some random location in the camp where the ranger gets two rounds of melee in before you can run across the room to engage mob. Make sure the group knows that where you are standing is where the fight will happen--no rushing the incoming mob.

Secondarily, if you're doing all that, don't worry overly much about letting the rogue tank for a bit at level 51. If the rogue and ranger are well geared, then they probably aren't dying before you get aggro back. If the cleric can stand the inefficient use of his healing mana, then if you're exp vs. blue mobs the group might be most efficient if all melee pull out the dps stops and get the mob dead asap.
They'll stop ganking your aggro when it gets 'em killed or when the cleric tells 'em to cut it out. Good players will stop earlier than bad players (rogues in particular are actually hurting their dps the longer the mob's on them), unless the good players are extremely well geared.
If you're doing your job, then dps classes like rogues and wizards have to do theirs: max dps until their aggro is just below the tank's. If they repeatedly fail to stay under your aggro, they're simply not doing their job.
I guess you could get an yak aug for your weapon, but it shouldn't be necessary, I wouldn't think.

Daywalka
Tip #1 - Don't use Voice on yourself, ever. It's a waste of a buff and a waste of a spell slot.

Tip #2 - Don't mash Taunt everytime it comes up. It gives you +1 Hate on top of what the person highest on the list has, which should be you. Save it for when you need it.

Tip #3 - It doesn't matter if someone snares over you. A Ranger, Druid, Necro can put their highest level snare on the mob if they want, your casting of CD for aggro won't be affected either way.

Tip #4 - If someone else in the group keeps taking aggro from you, by all means, let them have it. A death will go a long way in letting them see that stealing aggro is not a good thing.
