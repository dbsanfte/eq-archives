Some of you may have read this (http://www.avault.com/articles/getarticle.asp?name=mmogsell ) editorial regarding Blacksnow's case against Mythic entertainment. After reading the article I felt like I had to say something.

First off, I'm not a huge fan of EB players but I also don't think that people should be prohibited from buying or selling characters/items online. The problem I see with allowing the selling of in-game items is the possibility of commercial guilds developing. My fear is that individual players would find themselves competing with 'employees' for in game content.

I honestly can't say how much this might effect the balance of the game world but I believe it would significant impact on the middle to high level gamer. Don't get me wrong, I don't think any development company should have the right to forbid me to do whatever I want with my character or items. If I did sell my character (which I wouldn't), the person who bought it would still have to pay the monthly fee to play.

Development companies wouldn't lose money if they allowed the buying and selling of content for real dollars, but I do fear that the legitimate player would be effected.

If I ran a development company, I would not for bide real world transactions of characters and items, but I would feel the need to limit commercial opportunities.

I personally dislike the sale of game items or characters for cash.

It is bad enough to compete with people farming items to sell for plat, but generally those people don't make a full time business of it, since their goal is usually just better equipment for the character they are playing.

Individuals farming gear for RL profit go a step beyond that, they're competing with you in game to pay their rent. Most of these people aren't playing the game, their farming items to sell to you, items that you could probably have gotten on your own if they weren't farming them.

Companies who make a business out of farming and selling EQ items takes it the extreme. Now you potentially have groups of dedicated farmers who could totally dominate specific loot drops, pretty much requiring you to buy an item from them.

The only person these people are helping is themselves, at the expense of everyone else who plays the game.
