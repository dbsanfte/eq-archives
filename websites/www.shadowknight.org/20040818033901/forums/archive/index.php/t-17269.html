Stugein
The question I have for you is, "Do you like questing?". If so, then yes the Coldain Ring quests are totally worth it. If you don't enjoy questing, you probably don't want to do the rings or the shawl quests. Really, the rewards are sub-par until you get to the final few quests. Unless you are willing to put forth the effort to see all the quests through, don't bother.

Progression:
Ring #1 - AC: +1 Str: +1 Magic Resist: +1

Ring #2 - AC: +2 Str: +2 Magic Resist: +2

Ring #3 - AC: +3 Str: +3 Magic Resist: +3

Ring #4 - AC: +4 Str: +4 Magic Resist: +4

Ring #5 - AC: +5 Str: +5 Magic Resist: +5

Ring #6 - AC: +6 Str: +6 Magic Resist: +6

Ring #7 - AC: +7 Str: +7 Magic Resist: +7

Ring #8 - AC: +8 Str: +8 Magic Resist: +8
Clicky Shield of the Eighth (8 point damage shield)

Ring #9 - AC: +9 Str: +9 Sta: +9 Magic Resist: +9 HP: +50
Clicky Shield of the Eighth

Ring #10 - AC: +10 Str: +10 Sta: +10 Magic Resist: +10 HP: +100
Clicky Frostreaver's Blessing (10 hp/tick, 10 point damage shield, +10 ATK)

So you see, until you get to the last 2 or 3 quests, the rings really are not worth the trouble. However, if you are willing to put in the time and effort to fight it out to the end and complete all 10 quests you will end up with one of the best finger slot items in the game.

So sum up: If you like questing and are willing to see the entire thing through to the end, do it. The rewards are great (aside from the ring you can pick up some other really nice stuff along the way) and you won't be disappointed. If you don't plan on doing all 10 quests, then no - the first few rings aren't worth the effort. Go buy yourself some 6/65's and be done with it.

Duke Vendric Ravenkrest
The hardest part is the farming. Level 60+ can easily solo all the way to ring 7, and maybe ring 8 as well. Below that I dunno, as I never tried it below 60.

Basically just do all the farming beforehand. Get your cougar skins, and kodiak pelts, and tusks, walrus hides, skinning rock (ground spawn), etc. Check for tain any time you pass the bridge, get him out of the way whenever you see him since you don't hand him any ring to do that part. Same with warden for the shackle key.

Basically, though. Make the blanket, turn it in to Garadain, get ring 1.

Say 'what assistance' and he gives you an axe.

Run to Boridain, hand him the axe. Follow him and pre-clear any mobs in his path that are giant or orc. Aggro him to keep him in place if need be (don't have a DS on if you do this). You can pull him from spot to spot once you learn his path and all his stop/turn points.

Once Boridain is back at his spawn a kodiak spawns, kill it, loot hide, give that to boridain, he gives you an axe.

Hand the axe and ring 1 to Garadain and you get ring 2.

Take the skinning rock, tusk and walrus hide thingy and smith the coldain dagger (droppable, you could buy one). Hand that and ring 2 in for ring 3. If you did the pre-farming, you could get ring 3 right after you get ring 2 with no wait.

If you did Tain already, just hand in the hammer and ring 3 right away for ring 4. Otherwise go to Tain, say 'i will help' he instantly respawns. Invis up and wait. Target the named, run to max range and single pull him out. Solo him down, loot elixer. When Tain respawns say 'i will help' immediately hand him the elixer and take off, let him die again. Then go turn in the hammer and ring 3 for ring 4.

Now kill the rygorr messenger, or buy invasion plans, or you could already have them from pre-farming. Hand that to the scout behind Garadain's camp. Run to the front side of the camp, target Scarbrow, single him away. Let the dwarves fight the rest of the orcs. Solo scarbrow, loot the head, once Garadain respawns hand him the head and ring 4 for ring 5.

Go to icefang camp. At 10pm game time ghost dwarf spawns. Hand him ring 5, triggers a follow the wolf quest. You can ignore the wolf. Run to the orc camp beside the bridge, FD or invis and wait. Poxbreath will spawn inside. Target, run way off, pull him through the wall single, solo him, loot the head.

Then go to the rygorr fort (if you didn't pre-farm Rodrick) let the emissary run around and spawn Rodrick, kill rodrick loot head and dagger, keep both.

Go back to garadain, hand him rod's head and note off pox plus ring 5 for ring 6.

Go to the prison clear the house with the dorf in it. hand him the shackle key off the warden (warden can be single pulled through the wall, Kromrif Prison Guard is his PH). and ring 6. He hands ring 6 back. Follow him.

When he says something about an ambush, aggro him and run fast to the north (the shore). then follow the shore to Dobin's hut, run inside and once he's in with you, FD and wait. He will talk to Dobin then poof. Dobin asks for your ring, hand it to him and get a letter. Give the letter to Garadain and get ring 7.

If all the mobs are up you can do all of that in 5 or 6 hours. You can do multiple rings at the same time, actually, in 5 or 6 hours. I currently have ring 7 and the letter for another ring 7 from just 6 hours of time spent tonight, some of that was farming. Now I can simply trigger ring 8 war, then turn in the letter for a new ring 7. The plan is to MQ a ring 8 with a friend and have a ring 8 for me to clicky for DS. Ring 9 and 10 may follow later.

For ring 8, it's super easy. If you can clear the orc fort solo, you can solo ring 8. If not, you may need a friend to help. Duo is cake for a level 60'ish team. Anyway, clear the fort, hand garadain ring 7. Hand the not to gloridain, invis and run back inside the fort, single pull the chief (he's outside, in the outer wall). Kill him, loot head, invis and run back out. When garadain calls charge, hand him the head and get ring 8. Simple as pie.

So if you pre-farm and all the mobs are up, you can do rings 1 through 8 in a single night, easily. And solo most likely. Being able to self DS up to 24+ is worth the effort. Since ring 8's effect stacks with banshee aura and our hp buffs. +8 DS never hurts when you're just grouping, soloing, factioning or the like. And it's even a insta-click for crap buff on raids/pulling dispellers.

Anyway, good luck...

Duke Vendric Ravenkrest
You have plenty of time.

1: Turn in ring 7 to Garadain at his hut. He gives you marching orders.

2: Give the marching orders to Gloradin in the hut next door.

3: Invis and run into the empty orc fort, to the right as you enter, and wait in the clearing.

4: When the mobs spawn, pull the chief away from the mobs that spawned in the center of the fort courtyard.

5: Kill the chief, which can easily be done solo at 60+, and loot his head.

6: Invis and run outside to Garadain up on the hill now. He will walk back away from the fort eventually then call for his troops to "FALL IN". This takes a while. But, he then goes into a speech and moves them into position before calling the attack. You can hand in the head to get your 8th ring any time after he says "FALL IN".

Stugein
You can do very little of the 9th ring solo. You need Rodrick's dagger (which you probably already have) and the heads of 5 coldain traitors. Rodrick is cake, but you will need at least a good solid group for most of the heads (a couple of the traitors can't be pulled single, one involves running the plate cycle in Kael, etc.). Plus there is a tradeskill combine you must do (Tainted Avalanche Ale) that is trivial at 248 so you will need to train up brewing yourself or maybe find a gm brewer to make it for you (the ingredients for the ale are also a huge pita to get ahold of). If you find a high end brewer to do it for you, they will have to come with you to do the hand-in because the Ale is NODROP.
