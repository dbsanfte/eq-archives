For your second question: If a cleric yells for help after a failed mez I'd go for it and taunt the mob off him, but turn attack off and follow taunt with a terror immediately (terror works much better after a taunt) so the mob's safe to mez. It's all situational imo. First you have to look at your circumstances. Are you doing a LDoN Normal mission with a Chanter with as much hp as your normal Warrior? Are you on a Hard Mission where you chanter is going to get 1 rounded? Do you have another tank in the group? Is this a raid situation? How's the resists on the mobs? Are there mixed mezzable / unmezzable where you are fighting? What's the style of play of your Enchanter? These factors can make you have to adjust from the " norm ".

In groups, if it's normal LDoN I usually just charge the room and let the CC do their thing while I make sure everyone is assisting me on my Target. Nothing too complicated, LDoN normal is pretty easy for any group set up imo.

For Hard Missions, things get a little more complex. Higher lvl mobs = more chances for resists. 2+ mobs beating on you for 980+ involves a bit of healing and potential heal aggro. Most Chanters / clerics aren't going to survive long if they get aggro. That's why on Hard missions I try to keep pulls to a max of 2, and I have fear up. If I have to pull via Promitiy aggro I won't have time to establish aggro with snare. So, I call target, make SURE everyone understands this will be the target, melee / snare when it gets to me, then I turn off auto atk and fear the add. This is done very quickly, I don't want a loose mob to eat my healer / chanter, and I don't want issues with mis assists so I explain to the group what I"m doing.

If I know it's only going to be 2, I usually pull my target with Festering. The group knows that the 1st mob inc will be the mez target. That's usually a non issue.

If the pull is going to be 3, I give the chanter the targets and I pull mine with snare once again.

This is all assuming melee mobs that will charge you. If you have casters you don't even need to snare, they'll stop and try to cast while the melee charge. You can use your pet to single pull Casters in Hard though.
