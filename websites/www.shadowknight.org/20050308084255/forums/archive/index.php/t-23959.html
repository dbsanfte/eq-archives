Mardark
I finally put the 6600 GT card in my AMD system yesterday. Would not run EQ2 at all. Updated the bios on the motherboard. Updated video drivers. Downgraded video drivers. Nothing I seemed to try, worked. It would crash anytime I tried to run EQ2. It would run EQ1. It ran Doom3. It ran about everything else I tried, but would not run EQ2. I ran some benchmarks in Doom3, and performance was about 2/3 of what I expected.

Went to the motherboard web page, and got latest drivers they had for the motherboard there. Still nothing. I even tried to reinstall the OS, thinking something might have been messed up. I had to do a repair on it, after changing some hardware, because it wouldn't boot. So figured maybe soemthing was screwed up with it.

Then I went to the chipset manufacturer for the motherboard. I got their latest all-in-one drivers for the chipset. Figured I have tried everything else, what the heck. Viola. It fixed everything. Other benchmarks came up. And EQ2 ran just fine.

So if you stick in a 6xxx series nvidea card in your system, make sure everything is the latest you can get. Even the drivers for the chipset on the motherboard.

My P4 system worked fine, putting in the 6600 card right off the bat. The AMD system works fine now, after updating the drivers for the chipset on the motherboard.

I read alot of threads on the EQ2 boards, concerning problems with 6xxx series of cards. My conclusion is most problems are due to drivers for the chipset on the motherboard. I ran some tests using 5 different drivers for the video card (after fixing it), and all of them worked. But the chipset integrated drivers were the key.
