GubbaDaTroll
Here's what I would do:

Buy some svm-gear: Velium jacinth wedding ring*2 (svm+14 each), massive flameguard helm(+17 svM), maybe velium blue diamond bracelet and bloodstained mantle(+10 svM) .
Now you should resist most of his annoying stuns and blind-spells. If you want to cheat a bit, buy an SvMagic-potion from some vendor.
Next go buy some crystallized pumices(5 charges of nullify magic) from Shadowhaven. Zone to SH from bazaar and vendor in house up to left, left door, sells ´em. Why use pumices and not our own nullify magic spell you ask? Reason is you dont have to wait for the spell icon to ungrey if ye use pumice, so in theory ye can use one pumice empty in 10 secs, 5 charges 2 sec each.

Its also very useful if you know how to "Joust" .
Here's an link:
How to JOUST (http://www.shadowknight.org/forums/showthread.php?s=&threadid=13066&highlight=joust)
Ask some bored looking high level shadowknight in yer servers bazaar if he would have some moments to show you how to joust and train with ye a bit. If you know how, make an hotbutton for jousting that is /attack on, use disarm, /attack off in one macro. Pally without weapon is no threat.

When the battle begins: Tell your selfpetbuffed pet to attack the pally, he will prolly either use undead fear spell to send it away or kill it quickly with melee. Either way, pets only purpose is to distract the pally while you spam the paladin with nullify magic from your pumices. Unload atleast 1 pumice on him(5 charges).

Start jousting the paladin, hitting him with your 2handed weapon.
Eventually the paladin will try to use his lv 59 over time heal "Celestial cleansing" , which heals him for 700 dmg with 250 manacost in 4 tics(24 seconds) .
We dont want that , now do we.
Whenever you see the spellmessage "paladin soandso 's body is covered with soft glow", means he has casted the heal on himself.
Now you just use your crystallized pumice again on him, he should not have any buffs on so his heal icon is in first buffslot, and thus annulled after you unload an charge from yer pumice on him.

Keep on jousting, use pumice whenever he tries to use Celestial cleansing on himself and tap yer health back with Life leech and Spirit tap whenever you get below 80 percent hp.

Eventually he will go oom from uneffective CC's, stuns and blinds and becomes just weak warrior..well weak warrior with 2 lives. ;)

Aailin
Having a 65 Paladin with a several Hundred AA, I can tell you that with a 6 level difference unless he's a complete idiot you stand no chance of winning.

If you go the dispell route, and he has more 2 insta clickies (jboots/8th ring for example) add in his Yaulp which basically acts as one and your giong to spend so much time trying to dispell him that he's going to kill you before you even begin to do damage to him.

Also with a 6 level difference your going to have a very tough time landing anything on him.

The best advice I can give you, is to hit and run as much as possible. Shadow step, Joust just keep moving, while you keep your pet on him. Pet won't do much but it may keep him annoyed enough to turn and kill it. When he does that wade in and hit him until he turns on you then SS back away.

Truth is, even with the best advice from both pallies and sk's your not going to win. 6 levels is a huge differnce. Especially when your taking into account he is over 60. At 61 you get tremendous mitigation increases.

I'd imagine knowing your substantially lower then him his spell line up will probably include

Yaulp
Divine Aura
Force of Akilea (if he has it)
Quellious stun
Force
Stun
Root (or instill if he has it)
Celestial Cleansing....

He will probably go for the quick win early. He'll hit you with his post 60 stun and if it lands expect to be chainstunned to the point you can't move.

If he's any good he will be able to alternate his 2 4 second stuns/6 second stun/10 second stun and keep you locked down.


Its going to be tough even if you sub alot of AC/HP gear for SvM gear to resist because of the level difference.

If you manage to resist some stuns he'll just go to straight melee and use CC. He can also just DA to remem any other spells he wants since stuns aren't landing.

Personally, don't see anyway you can win this, but it will be a good learning experience anyway.

What I would do is this.

1. Chain pell him while he messes around with pet.
2. Have Both Spears up and nail him with both.
3. Pop off HT. He should be at 40-60% now.
4. He will begin casting CC now. Try to bash him to interupt.
5. If he gets CC off, pell it.
6. He will try to stun now. Have sow up and book it soon as you pell. go to the far end of the arena.
7. Pet should be alive still if you are lucky. He will waver in indecision now hopefully.
8. He will try to heal himself again now. Let him get it off.
9. While he has CC up, hit him with dooming dark.
10.Recast Spears.
11. Cast Spirit Tap.
12. He will be trying to get his stun off now again. Run in and melee hard.
13. Let him get his stun off. You should resist one if he trys to start chain stunning. The reason to do this is to trick him. He will not use LoH if he thinks that he can save it for when he really needs it. He knows that you have used your HT so he is not worried about being LoH happy. But...
14. Soon as you come unstunned, spear him with your 400 hp spear.
15. Bash him.
16. Recast the lower spear.
17. Now, if luck is on your side, he will be at 15% or so. He will want to use his LoH if he is watching his hp. Just hope you can kill him fast enough or you are screwed.
Nenadar W'Vern
To be honest, I think you do have a chance at beating him, even with the 6 levels difference.

What I would look at is his likely spell line up. Hes going to be trying to stun you mainly so he can get free hits and then hes going to be healing himself. He has a few dd spells but nothing as major. Maybe he will blind too.

He will be buffed, dispell him to remove hp buffs.

Keep your resists high so his spells dont land, stick to spears and tank him. He will concentrate on hitting you and healing himself. You just need to outdamage his healing. HT has 2 purposes, to either force him to use his LoH or as a finishing blow(its fun when they accidently LoH you which happens to me a lot on TZ).

If you can land a dark on him you have a very good chance. Always use clinging darkness, the lower the damage and movement hinderance the harder to resist, so clinging is best for this. Joust him, let him heal and waste his mana. Use a proccing life tap and life leech if you have it. Gradually wear him down, dont expect a quick victory. It will take you a long time but you can win, despite the level diff. As people have said above, just keep hitting him.

Environment also helps, where would you fight him?
