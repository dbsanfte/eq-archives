Introduction

Q : What are these Focus effects I keep seeing pop up on my equipment?

A : Focus effects were part of the so called Caster Balancing made by Sony Online Entertainment during the Luclin period. Mainly intended for the pure casters we as Shadowknights can gain great benifits as well by chosing the right focus for the job at hand. It can be very hard for your average player to gain the right types of Focus, and so SoE added an option for items summoned by the Magician class. All types of focus will be covered later on.

Q : Right then, so what kind of Focus are there?

A: There are quite alot by now, and more enter the game every expansion. Thus this is a work in progress and more items will be added when it becomes required. I will start with listing the "Basic" kind of Focus. Please note that Focus range from rank I to rank IV. Rank I effects level 1-20 spells, Rank II 1-44, Rank III 1-60 and Rank IV 1-65. Thus Ideally you'd want rank IV focus.

Also please note that the effect procentage ranges from 1% to whatever procent listed. So an Improved Damage IV would make direct damage spells do between 1% and 20% more damage. It is random everytime you cast a spell, so people generally consider Half the listed value as what you get out of it on average. Click the links below for the Technical mombojumbo from Lucy (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/)


Spell Haste (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3525&source=Live) - Helps you cast certain spells faster
Mana Preservation (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3537&source=Live) (MP) - Saves mana on all spells
Improved Damage (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3513&source=Live) (ID) - Helps your single target direct damage spells do more damage
Improved Healing (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3501&source=Live) (IH) - Makes your healing spells better
Burning Affiction (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3507&source=Live) (BA) - Increased DoT damage
Extended Range (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3510&source=Live) - Increases the Range of your spells
Extended Enhancement (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3504&source=Live) (EE) - Increases Buff timer
Reagent Conservation (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4016&source=Live) (RC) - Gives you a chance of casting a spell that requires a reagent without consuming said reagent.



Those are the basic focus you will see the most often. There are of course nearly a focus for every type of spell currently in Everquest, but these are the most common and widespread.
Since this is all about Shadowknights we should cut right to the chase and see what Focus we can make the best use out of.

Which Focus for the job?

Since we're all about two types of spells, namely Direct damage and Damage over Time (DoT) it's of course best to start off with focus that effect those two catagories. Every spell cost mana, so of course saving mana on every cast is something that could be useful as well.
Shadowknights have, as all hybrids do an innate spell casting haste on harmful spells. This innate ability starts at level 51, and scales all the way up to level 65, where it gives you a 45% haste on casting harmful spells. Thus I don't consider Spell Haste that important for us.

This leaves us with a number of choices for focus. We need something to help us Do more damage with DDs and DoTs, and we need to save mana. That leaves us pretty much with the basic Improved Damage, Burning Affiction and Mana Preservation.

Why Improved Damage?

We use Direct Damage to do more damage with our DD spells. We have a number of those and lifetaps are included in this catagory. The nifty thing about the ID focus and lifetaps are that it both increases how much our Lifetaps damage, but also how much we get healed by the tap. The Improved Damage focus also help our HT do more damage as our HT is considered a spell by the EverQuest spell engine. So thumbs up to this one, getting an ID item can and should be a fairly imporant task. Most SK's opt for the War Crested Ring (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=10947) with it's ID4. It can be a bit pricy, but it's a good goal to have.

Why Burning Affiction?

We creave more damage, thus it comes as no surprise that we should look to increase the potential of our DoT spells as well. BA helps us do that. The BA is checked for every tick (A tick is six seconds) so if you leave your DoT damage on it will produce different numbers almost every tick. More damage is always good, so a BA focus can be very worth it. While you are at it you could look for a way of increasing the DoT Duration with a Focus called Extend Torment (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4462&source=Live). That Focus requires the Lost Dungeons of Norrath Expansion though, and quite alot of Favor Points to buy though, so there's no need to search far and wide for equipment with that effect. Longer duration on dots = More damage, which was what we wanted all along.

Why Mana Preservation?

As hybrids our mana pool tend to be a bit lacking till we get into the Raiding game and all it's spendor of items. Thus saving mana on every spell can be a very nice thing indeed. More mana means you can cast more spells in the long run. Toss in a mana regen buff and some Flowing Thought items and you're a machine of spell casting death.

What should I focus on then?

Q : Cool, so Focus items can do alot for my shadowknight. Whats the catch?

A : Well, the "catch" is that you have to make some choices regarding your gear as you get better and better gear. We are mostly Tanks and Pullers so normally you'd Focus on AC, Hp and stamina when you chose your gear to help survive tanking and pulling better. So often you have to comprimise a bit till you can get good gear thru raiding that has the focus we need. The Lost Dungeons of Norrath Expansion can help the casual Shadowknight obtain such focus with some hard work and alot of Favor point spent, so there is light as the end of the tunnel there as well.

Magician Summoned Items

So you haven't quite made enough points in LDoN to deck yourself out with Focus items yet? No worries, by bothering your local magician a little you can get No Rent items that basicly do the same as those Focus obtained other places. On raids where you're not pulling or tanking there's no harm in sacrificing a little Hp/AC to gain a useful effect. But what do you ask them for?

Please note that I list the highest possible summoned items for the sake of simplicity. You can always find a magician around your level to summon a lower level version of the ones listed below.

Another thing to note about magician summoned focus items is that they follow other guidelines for when they stop effecting spells. You will have to look each of them up if you pick up lower level versions of those items. Follow the Lucy link on the top of this document and see what the magician spell list looks like.
