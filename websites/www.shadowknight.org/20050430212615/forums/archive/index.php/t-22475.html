Sewer 1 : you have to kill a boss mob that spawns after killing 9 stonemites in the last room.

Sewer 2 :you have to collect 4 different remains, depending on luck this can take a long time. After that you use the 4 items to spawn 4 nameds whom when you kill,spawn a willowwisp that starts moving towards a room called the crystal room, after the last wips arrives in the room, you got 30 mins to get to the room and hail and npc.

Sewer 3 : This ones a pain in the arse, clear all the way to last room, hand in quest peice you get after doing sewer 2 to the npc, this spawns special mobs in 3 different rooms in the other side of the sewers, kill mobs in each room till you get text saying that you found a tool, get all 3 tools and return to the npc in the last room.

Sewer 4 : Another pain in the arse trial, you need to clear to the last room, kill a named gello thing, that then spawns 6 wisps that disappear off to different parts of the dungeon, you need to collect 4 map peices from the 6 wisps and then turn in to npc near the ZI.

Considering, these were mainly RF groups with a few random people stuck in here and there, we are prolly better geared than you guys overall.

Regarding your group makeup, a chanter comes in very handy on some of them especially sewer 3 and 4.

You can also cut alot of time off sewer 3 if you got a rogue, since he can sneak all the way to the npc room and trigger the 3 rooms near ZI for you, sorta cuts the whole trial down to under an hour.

Darkmourn D`Estrellas
Just to clarify a bit, it's not 20 minutes. That would lead you to believe you could just start an instance and sit at zone in for 20 minutes waiting to see if named pop. You have to kill X number of mobs, which is typically around 20. It is not the same every time from what I can tell. After you kill the X'th mob, all the blank spots in the spawns fill in, and that is where the named can be. You'll learn to recognize which spawns are ones that signify the named could be up. Particularly the first time you pull a room that only has 2 mobs in it, and upon killing one of them suddenly 3 more and a roamer appear. Once you learn those quirks, it's easy. Oh, and a named can spawn on a repop of the "normal" mobs as well (the ones that are up when you zone in, before the "trigger" number is hit). But I can't guarantee that 2 of the same named can be up at the same time (never seen it happen) so you can't just keep killing near zone in and expect an eventual pop, if one is up deeper in zone.

Just for comparisons, I've done sewers, particularly Lair, a lot of times. I've seen Rigara like 4 times, compared to probably 15-20 each of the fly and the larva. Got the shield the first time. Each mob seems to have a common drop, so get used to a lot of stuff rotting. Out of all of those flies we've only had 2 spell haste augs, and the larva has only once dropped a FT2 aug, and twice the +15 atk aug. Oh, and a whopping total of 3 spell runes (6 if you count the 3 that dropped when the actual spell dropped instead of a generic rune).

The only way to know if a named is up is tracking (which sucks even for rangers in those zones) or have a rogue SoS through the whole thing and just look. To give an idea of tracking range, if you look at the map of the Lair there is a room directly east of the zone in room. Physically it is pretty close, but you have to fight around in a big U to get to it. A druid standing at the zone in spot after succoring was unable to track the named in that eastern room. So unless you've got a rogue, you just have to hope it spawns close.
