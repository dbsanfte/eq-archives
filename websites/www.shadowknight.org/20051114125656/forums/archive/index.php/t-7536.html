I always wondered why there wasn't an anti ranger the way that the sk is an anti paladin.....While too late for eq....perhaps in eq2, the TRADER class could be that class....hunting animals for their furs....ruining ecosystems with the runnoff from mines to get ore....clearcutting forests to stock their bakeries.....summoning elementals as cheap labor.....That would be the theme...

Their spell line would use a lot of components that dropped off mobs....or create reagents that were used in other classes spells...they'd have buffs that had side effects of reducing faction with natural critters while on....

Their weapons would all be clicky and procky. They'd get all the anti summon and control summoned spell line. They'd get access to all class/race based trade skills like tinkering, poison making, and alchemy.

Their nukes would be powerfull, but long recast (this is my BOOM stick), And in the form of summoned items with charges that used reagents which were also summoned. (the spell which summoned the item would have long recast time, while the time to use the charge and damage amount and type would be dependent on the item).

They'd have a version of charm which only worked vs sentient critters and had higher and higher cost gems as reagent (How much is your boss paying you? I already love the possible names for this "spell" line: Fasttalk, bribe, extort, blackmail.....).

Of course, a horse would be one of their class aa skills....and cart....

Clearly they'd be hybrid magician/rogue, much like the bard is hybrid chanter/rogue. But the playable races would be gnomes, dwarves, and humans. Perhaps dark elves, half elves, and erudites as well (heck, if paladins are erudite warriors, Merchants would be erudite rogues).
