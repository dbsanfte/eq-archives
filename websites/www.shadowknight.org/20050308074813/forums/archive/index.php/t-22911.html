Everyone is so proud with their "getting the breeder to spawn the adds" strats that they neglect to mention the firepower needed for it, and how easily it can catch a group offguard the first few times they try it.

Fine, do whatcha gotta do to get the spawns out of the way.

the two methods are:

1. aggro it, keep it aggroed till it craps them all out.
2. aggro it, feign, reaggro, feign, etc. until all are crapped out.

There are 6 total.

Getting him solo is easy.

But, it hits fastish. Low-time geared and under, without coordinated heals the healers might run out of mana as the thing has really high AC, and is very resistant to spells at 66. I would recommend melee DPS (rangers, monks, rogues) over spellbased.

We had a near 11k paladin tank, two 9500 sks, another SK, chanter, two druids, cleric, and one or two spell based DPS. The spell DPS was actually rather low, and the paladin had dropped alongside one of the SKs. The fight ended with most all of the people present low on mana and health.

I would have to agree that 3 groups+ would cinch it if you're pretime/ele.

Basically its a pretty good exp camp. The murks drop tradeskill augs, and the other named that pops behind the breeder spawn can drop 68 runes and a few attunables that are only a bit below ele level.

So, that's the main incentive to get a single group there. It's solid, steady EXP, about a yellow in 2 or so hours at 66. When the breeder pops, call in the troops.
