So let's analyze this further...

Some data:
Shiftless deeds (level 44 enchanter slow) has 12 disease counters
Forlorn deeds (level 57 enchanter slow) has 16 disease counters
Tigir's insects (level 58 AE shaman slow) has 16 disease counters
Turgur's insects (level 51 shaman slow) has 16 disease counters
Plangue of insects (level 54 disease shaman slow) has 16 disease counters)
Remove greater curse (level 54 cleric spell) is 5 attempts to remove 9 disease counters (this gives an indication of the 'weight' of a disease counter)

Disease cloud (level 9 SK spell) has 1 disease counter



What conclusions can we draw from this?

We know that all spells are made of several 'levels' (internal name meaning effect in this context) and the total result of each spell is a simple addition of all those levels.
This means not only the beneficial/harmful part, but also the duration, the stacking, the hate generated....
For example the enchanter spell bedlam raises your str by 50, lowers your agi by 25, and gives you a 350 hp rune, once you have spent the rune component by taking at least 350 dmg in nukes or melee, the whole buff fades, including the 50 str and the neg25 agi.

The hate component of slow spells is the combination of each level, the actual slow effect, and the new disease counter. What VI is going to work on is removing the extra agro from the disease counters, the agro from the slow component will remain.
It's unkown whether the hate generated by disease counters is a fixed amount or if it's proportinal to the number of counters
This in my opinion is the key point. If the hate is proportional to the number of counters, then DC will keep pretty much its efficiency as it'll only lose 1:16th of the agro slow spells have gained, in that case the agro of DC comes mostly from the other levels.
On the other hand, if the hate generated by disease counters is a fixed amount, then we cant tell for sure what the effect will be on DC, as it'll still keep the agro from the DD and the DoT on the other levels.

The agro of a spell is not based on its resist check at all, for example torrent spells have little to no agro and are all checking against disease. This change will not affect all spells that check against disease, the only part that will be affected is the disease counter, it is obviously present in several disease based spells, but is totally independant of the resist check (this is why it's present in slow spells which are magic based).
In the case of asystole and heart flutter, the first has 4 disease counters, while (surprisingly) the later has none. This patch might end up lowering the hate generated by the disease counter part of asystole (but the AC/str/DoT parts will remain the same, with equal agro), but should not affect heart flutter in any way.

Finally, another point that needs to be taken in consideration is the way VI will modify the way agro is generated by slow spells. They might simply lower the agro generated by disease counters, it's obviously the easiest solution, but this will raise some other 'problems', for example shaman's big DoTs that are curable (ie have disease counters) will have a noticeably lower agro. The other option is to lower the hate generated by the slow component by the amount of hate added by the disease counters, in this case the hate created by slow spells will be back to what it used to be, and none of our spells will be affected (slow spells and procs will ALL keep the same agro).


Conclusion: Disease cloud may not even generate most of its agro from the disease counter (and it certainly does not generate it just because it checks against disease), and even if it does, nothing allows us to say if fixing slow spells will affect the agro it can generate. Our class possesses many other ways to taunt with spells, and we are still able to create it from melee damage and procs when the mana conservation is a real problem (groups without a bard or enchanter). It's also true that we are able to replace that spell slot by other spells at least as beneficial (although in different ways i agree), i don't see any reason to be alarmed, and no reason to run around yelling nerf (after all a level 9 spell losing efficiency as you level is annoying but acceptable).
