The entire following is qualified. At the end of the day, the differences are extremely small, so if you like a certain intangible qualities (looks, ability to roleplay, etc), then disregard the following in its entirety.

Personally, if I was going to start all over again, I would NOT make an IKS SK again. It has nothing at all to do with finding suitable armor though. Nowadays, that just isnt that big a deal.

The main thing that I have against IKS is that regen, which is probably their greatest attribute, is also available to trolls. Trolls, however, have superior starting stats, but the same xp penalty. Also, trolls get to slam and the size drawback is really minor.

The AC bonus is pretty immaterial. You can hit the alleged ac softcap fairly easy, so who cares if a few more points of AC are granted. Sure, it will only help, but it just doesnt seem to yield that much benefit.

I guess what I am saying is that if you want regen, Id take a troll, not an IKS.

Personally, my favorites are ogres and gnomes, followed closely by trolls (yes, I said gnomes--I could care less about all the bashing that people do towards them).

As stated from the beginning, however, dont even sweat any race you pick. People are splitting major hairs when they talk difference between the races. Pick the one that you like the most (yeah, Id include intangibles in the equation) and have fun.

Originally posted by Zarrik
The only reason for not going iksar immediately is that it seems a pain to find suitable armor and I'm curious how much of a nuisance it is not being able to slam while weilding a 2hander.
It can certainly be annoying to miss out on some armor (blood ember, in particular, but also darkforge) as you level up. Erudite SKs can use those armors, but they also have a set of newbie armor (http://www.eqtraders.com/quests/paineel_SK.htm) that they can quest as they level.

I did the newbie quests for my dwarf cleric, and it is pretty good armor for the levels 10-20 or so, maybe even further along than that. The pieces tend to be pretty easy to procure for the most part (with usually one or two pieces that are a pain in the ass), and you can wind up with a full suit of matching armor by level 10-12 or so, or earlier if you have an alt get the pieces. Heck, most of the pieces can be purchased off of merchants, except for the handful of NO DROP parts that are usually required.

But as others say, think of what you value most, or what you would enjoy most. I went with an Iksar because I prefer the way they look, and would choose an iksar just about every time. If I didn't choose an Iksar I'd probably choose a human, for the same reason- I like how they look in plate armor.
