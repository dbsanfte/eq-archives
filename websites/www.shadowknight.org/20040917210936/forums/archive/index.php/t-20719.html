nimic
Natural durability will give you aproximately 300 hp at level 3, which is likely less then 1 hit from the mobs you are fighting. This makes it somewhat of a bad deal. However, ND3 provides more hp which is useful for taking spell damage - so it has its uses.

Combat Agility will prevent you from getting hit in some cases. Not getting hit is better then getting hit for less in my opinion, as avoiding damage is always better then getting hit for lower damage.

Combat Stability will help to alleviate damage spikes, which might make your health more predictable during a fight for the cleric to heal you.

I personally went for CA3 before CS3... However, the consencous seems to be to get them both, and just choose whichever one first that fits your play style best. Supposedly, they are both about equal in improving your tanking ability.

nimic
ND3 is 10% of your NAKED HP including any stamina bonuses.

So if you have 3000 hp naked, ND3 gives 300 hp.

If you have 3100 hp naked with max stamina, then you'll get 310.

It does NOT take into account any +HP items or buffs (other then STA buffs).

Yes CA gives a greater chance of being missed, and CS mitigates incoming damage... I am not sure specifically how CS mitigates, but I know its not as straight forward as it might seem. Do a search on combat stability, I know there have been a few good threads on it.
Dakkin
People usually recommend CA before CS and CS before ND for tanks (unless you're really low on hps to start with).

CA reduces the overall amount you get hit by about 10%. It also reduces the amount of proc damage you take from mobs by 10% (because mobs don't proc on misses), and ditto a 10% reduction in stuns.

CS and ND only affect the smaller proportion of hits that aren't avoided in the first place, and don't reduce procs so they're slightly less effective than CA.

ND only boosts your hps before pure hitpoint buffs by 10%, not your total hps, so is even less effective in reducing your overall damage taken while tanking than CS.

/shrug, those are just the tanking arguments. A raiding caster might well go ND3 before CA to be able to take more AE hits.
Josahd
ND gives you 10% more hp, NOT 300 or 350 or whatever more hp..... this is important, because the more hp you have, the more it will help you.

IT DOES affect hp on gear. Look for yourself... remove a +hp item when you have ND trained and you'll lose more hp than is listed on the item. A 100 hp item should give about 110 hp with ND3, even apart from any stamina on it (easier to see the effect if the item doesn't have any sta since that won't screw up the hp you're seeing). IT DOES affect all stamina effects. If you gain hp from a stamina buff, you'll gain even more hp due to ND.

The only thing it does not affect are direct hp buffs (Virtue, Symbol, 9, Focus, etc).

With that in mind, I'm not quite sure why someone would recommend ND more strongly if you don't have much for hp, since it'll be less effective when you have few hp.

Not quite sure why people like to downplay this AA lately. ND, CA, and CS each give about a 10% improvement to tanking ability... through mitigation, avoidance, or absorption (more hp to absorb damage). My preference is ND first, because more hp insulates you more from damage spikes, while the other 2 do not. The other 2 also only affect melee damage. Not that it matters, you should get all 3 as quick as you can.
