We generally aren't supposed to talk about it because it's likely not intended. But as mentioned, it's been discussed enough times here if you look for it.

If you FD first before sending the pet in, and spam /pet back off or /pet hold, the mob comes back single and other mobs will not assist. This assumes your pet has enough hit points or is buffed so that it makes it back to you.

Assuming that you FD'd out of aggro range, and the pet made it back to you alive, you can stand up and whack the mob (thereby saving your pet) and you have it single. Using FD takes care of any "your pet is or is not green to the mob" issues of singles, it ALWAYS comes back with a single. Have used this on King in Chardok, Protectors of Zek in Kael, lizards in Cazic-Thule, Black Reavers in CoM, etc.

If you stand up too soon; i.e. if pet is still in aggro range of the OTHER mobs or your pull is still in assist range of the other mobs, you will get aggro. If you FD'd and you had aggro already before sending in a held pet, you aren't guaranteed a single (depends on if your memwipe was successful -- wouldn't count on it). Sowing or Shrewing your pet helps, along with hp/ac buffs. And as mentioned above, healing the pet or otherwise runing it on the way back etc. will ruin a single pull.

If you set it up right -- /pet guard here at the right spot (behind you preferably but within pet command range) and with you out of aggro range, and you FD before the pull and not stand up until the pet has returned to you with the mob, it's never failed. Stand up too early because the pet died halfway or whatnot and you may or may not get a single.

I don't feel that it's a major secret/trick/exploit because generally it doesn't work on high level mobs, simply because our best pet (lvl 41 warrior) won't last that long against a hard hitting mob, even if it has virtue/7th/sta etc. buffs. On lower level mobs, you might lose a few pets trying to figure out the right spot to FD in before sending in your pet, and may end up costing you more mana to make the pets and buff them than it would to split the mobs the conventional ways. And on lower lvl mobs than that, you probably could just single pull without FD or the pet.

This does not enable us to do anything more than we can do already. In some situations, it can save a lot of time; especially in places where space is tight for FD pulling or when no pacify/harmony is available. Other times, the pet just won't live long enough for it to work. And other times, it's more trouble than it's worth.

Good place to "practise" your petpulling would be stalkers camp in PoN. You've got a lot of room to move, and good line of sight to get a feel for how quickly to pull your pet back. Agro range on the stalkers is large enough to get you used to where to FD.


A lot of it is getting used to having him just smack the mob then getting him out of there immediately- he can't take many hits and the more he is engaged with the mob, the harder a time you have pulling them both back.

The hardest part is getting your pet back alive, or just keeping him alive until you have a clean tag, and keeping control of him when he keeps wanting to turn and tank your mob- if you have a long way back to camp you may need to sit kite it to keep it from ripping up your pet.

I use it when I'm splitting something where the mobs take forever to path back, or there's a chance of a repop soon in my pull path, etc- it's basically just a timesaver then. Also useful in LDoN if all else is failing.
