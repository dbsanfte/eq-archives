We've talked about this before here, a while back. If you want to add /beg to your taunt macro lineup, simply do the following:

/attack off
/doability (beg)
/doability (disarm)
/attack on
/doability (taunt)


That should give you enough time after attack off to get the beg out while keeping attack on for the most part...

Just replace the parenthesied functions with their corresponding ability keys, or use the /alt activate commands if there is one for those abilities (never looked).

And you don't get ability failure messages ("You cannot perform this ability at this time...") when that particular line up goes off? Admittedly, I've not experimented with macro lineups different than what I posted, but I've noticed in the past that occasionally the script processes before the abilities do, in which case you sometimes have to add a pause in order to let the specific "doability XX" command process in full before the macro moves on to the next line. Because of the way that in game macros work, there isn't always sufficient time between the execution of one line and the next to fully execute the specific command, which will get you a failed message.

Most notably, if I use my taunt macro, then cast CD, I have to wait a few seconds after the taunt goes off (possibly because of clientside lag) in order to not have the spell cast interupt the disarm. Because of the way the taunt ability processes, there's a built in pause while the taunt check is performed which delays the actual execution of the disarm ability, even though the macro cycles through the commands almost immediately.

/shrug

I suppose it's really just habit, but I've always put the most restricted ability the deepest into a macro so that I don't have anything stepping on that process. Which is why there's a significant pause between acquiring PC target and acquiring assist target off of PC target when I run my Non-Tanking Assist macro. I was finding that /tar Player_01 wouldn't actually process fast enough to pick up the designated player before /assist tried to assist off of him. Add in a pause (mine is around 3) and I get a clean assist every time. Pause another 5 seconds before giving the /pet attack command for insurance that the pet won't go to the wrong mob and it works great.
