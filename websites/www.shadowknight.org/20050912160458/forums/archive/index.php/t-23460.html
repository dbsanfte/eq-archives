Personally I do this quite often, the people I group with tend to get a little cocky, but this is not hard at all. But first you need to realize that the chance of getting aggro on all before they get to you is very slim. Even if you mem more than 1 spear spell, there are a lot of mobs that will be in your faces before recast, but your group mates shouldn't take more than 1 hit.

First you need to get at an angle where you can see all mobs clearly, then shoot a spear off at the mob trailing last. And the moment the spear lands, assist the puller as quickly as you can. The chances are the one he/she has targetted is the one closer to him/her.
Once its in camp, time yourself carefully and attack it with attack, bash, taunt, and disarm while its still running its last few steps toward the puller. This will cause a "first attack" aggro effect, which will last for about 3 or 4 seconds. Plenty of time to get a fester darkness or heart flutter off. Once thats done get a fear off on the other mob (do NOT use dots on more than one mob unless you have no crowd control other than yourself!) and your more or less set.
Just keep alternating targets every 20 seconds or so to attack/taunt/repeat a darkness or flutter. Cuz lets face it, now that people have such a hardon for paladins they hate us, and love to give us that whole "SKs are so useless, I wouldn't have died if we had a paladin or warrior" bullshit.

If there are more than 2 mobs, yes, your group will take a few more hits. But it wont be that bad. If your fast enough you can get some aggro spells in before your group members can get anything in that would piss it off enough to cause aggro problems.

If there are more than 3 mobs, you *CAN* do it, but the cleric will be working, and you'll have to start hoping your defense is good enough. Just follow the previous steps.

If there are more than 4 mobs... rethink your pulling group members....

As for the targetting issue, pick your poison. Personally the target ring cycling feature worked great for me if things got too heated.
