Aegon Targaryen
In order to pick the right race _FOR YOU_, you must decide what you priorities are.

If you want to solo alot, consider the merits of the Troll or Iksar. Regen is super useful for reducing your downtime. If you play Troll, dump your points in Int, if you're a Iksar, dump your points in Sta.

If you want to look super-cool, and have plenty of mana to get you out of tight spots, play a Dark Elf or an Erudite. For points, Stamina is the way to go.

If you want to be the biggest, strongest, toughest tank you can get, play an Ogre. Get your agility to 75, dump the rest into Int.

If you can't make up your mind, don't care about being unable to see in the dark until 22, and want to avoid faction problems, play a human.

If you like being insulted, mocked and otherwise generally abused, play a gnome. I don't know what kind of shadowknights they make, but I do know they receive no end of crap.

So much for common knowledge, let me expound upon my personal experience, after playing 4 races of shadowknights (Def, Hum, Trl, Iks).

Humans: Don't. The ONLY bright side about being human is that the guards generally won't molest you, and you'll get marginally better prices for your loot from vendors. Being night blind is an annoyance that is only partly offset by monkeying with your gamma, and humans have the lowest carrying capacity of any shadowknight. Why is this? Because your walking speed is based on your agility, not your strength. This means you don't actually start moving slower due to being encumbered until your agility drops below 75. So a Dark elf who puts all his points in stamina can carry 100 stone of equipment before being slowed down, whereas a human who does the same can only carry 85. If you're not twinking, the situation gets worse, because the dark elf can wear lighter gear. This obviously stops being an issue once you grow out of your banded/bronze.

Dark Elves: You look really cool. So do the 200 or so other Dark Elves out there, but that's okay, you still look cool. Early on, you can actually lug about a lot of gear, see above. Later on, your mana pool can come in handy once or twice, but as many folks will tell you, mana regen is not dependent on the size of your mana pool. Does this mean having a reserve isn't good? No, but it does mean that most of the time your larger pool just means you'll be sitting for longer periods of time. Use this time to look in the mirror more often. After all, you look really cool.

Iksar: When I played my Iksar, you were shackled to Cabilis. No one else would buy from you, and this wound up being a big problem, because, if you're like me at all, you want to play with your friends, who are not all playing Iksar. With Luclin, Velious and PoP, this is a non-issue. When I played my Iksar, you couldn't wear plate. While this is still partly true, you can get stuff for your Iksar that both looks and acts just as good as plate. Regen, AC bonus, even foraging are great add-ons, and make Iksar one of my favorite races.

Troll: Trolls have some pretty big drawbacks, but some pretty big advantages to go with them. Bad fire resist is defintely the worst, make sure you remember this when picking your hunting grounds. Almost as bad is your horrid faction. Most merchants won't have anything to do with you, and you can forget about running to the guards for help. Oh and being large can be a pain indoors, especially now that Neriak, with its ridiculous number of choke points, is your home city. Now, if you can cope with these drawbacks, the advantages are huge. Regen is a great bonus, especially if you like soloing, but even in ordinary group fights, being able to bandage, tap and regen quickly lets your clerics save mana for your other, smaller group-mates. Slam is another winner, vastly increasing your effectiveness using 2 handed weapons. Fear kiting with a good 2 hander and slam is very, very effective. Finally, don't forget that trolls start with extra agility, which means you can carry more before being slowed due to encumbrance. This allows a troll to carry almost as much gear as an Ogre before dropping speed.

One last note: Many players will tell you that stats don't matter. At 60th level with raid buffs on, this is certainly true, but for most of your career, you can't rely on having everything maxxed to 255. Having more strength WILL make you do more damage. Having more stamina WILL give you more HPs. Having more intelligence WILL give you more mana, and make the accumulation of many skills faster. I personally think this makes trolls and ogres the best choice when picking your race, since their stats total up to so much more than their smaller brethren.
