If the only goal in EQ is pure damage output killing big mobs, then yes, the Shadowknight takes a backseat to other classes. Heck, the only melee damage dealer ya by pure damage output would be rogues by that standard.

But, there is so much more to SK's.

- No one, I mean no one besides matchup with the SK in PvP (assuming same level and relative gear comparisons). I destroy Warriors, laugh at rogues and chase silly casters until they finally unplug. SK's are the king of PvP.

- I'm sneaky. I have invisi, invisi to undead, levitate, and FD. There's very few places I can't explore in relative safety. (and don;t forget about the Circlet of Shadows!...invaluable!)

- I'm the only tank around that really doesn't require a healer to be effective (fungi, plus epic, plus minimal uses of lifetap abilities make this so).

- I can solo with ease and I make a great puller/tank for almost any group.

- I have the best horse (AXP) in the game.

Bottom line is, the SK is versatile and deadly. I don't have quite the damage output of the rogue on big mobs, but that's not the only measure of class effectiveness. Who can calulate how much my Shroud of Pain, Torrent of Hate, Torrent of Pain Vortex Shadow etc added to the raid's ability to kill the mob? That value the SK adds, is not easily quantitatively figured into that equation, but surely exists.

Long live the SK!
