Lunaticfringesk
The drawback of not being able to wear plate armor is probably not that huge of an issue at all. IKS not being able to wear plate armor is at least partially offset by the natural AC bonus that they get. At 60, this ac bonus varies when you compare it race to race, but seems to always be in excess of 50 more ac.

Furthermore, Dreadscale and even Ry'Gorr armor is easily useable to mid to late 40s and by that time you will be ready to start your Thurg armor quests in Kael. Another option, if you loathe killing in Kael, once you hit 45, is to go to PoG for armor drops. PoG armor is essentially the equivalent of Thurg armor.

From Thurg / PoG armor, you can graduate to SS or Kael armor quests (depending on your faction) if you can get into a guild that somewhat raids ToV on a fairly regular basis.

All of these armor drops are wearable by IKS and all are very good armor.

It isnt easy to get armor pieces better than Kael / SS armor. To get better, you are most likely killing upper-end raid mobs and will probably need to be in a good guild to do so. Guildless people will have an extremely tough time getting armor better than SS / Kael (hell, even those generally require several groups of high level characters to get).

If you are able to get into a very powerful guild (NToV and beyond), then most, if not substantially all, of the armor drops are wearable by IKS, which means, by extension, you will end up with a higher ac than most other SKs due to the ac bonus. This will be argued by many, however, as not being that huge an advantage due to the ac softcap (which you'll pretty easily hit once in full SS / Kael armor anyways, to say nothing of the armor obtainable that is better than that).

At the end of the day, dont sweat it at all. The drawback is mitigated. Some even argue that IKS are too strong due to their ac bonus.
