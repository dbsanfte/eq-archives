Razs Meows
I think that instant start/stop would make certain things too easy (ie, kiting stuff in raid encounters, PVP, etc.).

Instead of making it 1 huge AA, why not scale it?

Version 1 would reduce the acceleration and deceleration time to 2.5 seconds and allow lev / underwater / etc. steering. Also, maybe double damage if you hit melee while moving at top speed and a scaled % for when accelerating? (IE if top speed = 100% and does 100% extra damage, acceleration to 33% does 33% extra damage, 50% does 50% extra damage, and so on. Would *maybe* increase the chance to miss, but not by as much as the damage.). This makes 'Jousting' a bit more realistic.

Version 2 would reduce the acceleration and deceleration time to 2 seconds and allow you to have a 40% bonus to channelling or whatnot during deceleration. (You're not concentrating as hard on the horse or something).

Version 3 would reduce acceleration and deceleration time to 1 second, give an extra damage bonus modifier (15% of current damage bonus?), increase the chanelling % to 60%, and maybe allow sitting mana regen while meleeing. Also would allow all skills to be used while on horse back (ie, can't kick or backstab or use archery [while moving] on horse back).

Make it cost something like 8 / 16 / 24, and usable to all classes.

Then SK and Pallies would get a class specific rank in it, for whatever points, that increases the % of the damage bonus, upgrades the graphics, and maybe even grants an AC bonus (it is flippin' HARD to hit some one on a horse!).


That sound nice and nifty? It would give every one more of a reason to get horses, would actually allow all classes (except maybe bards still :P) to get a benefit from a horse, prevent any SK with a horse from insta-ganking people on PVP servers, and generally increase roleplaying aspects (even if they are dead).

When was the last time you saw a non-caster fight from horse back?
When was the last time you saw a non-pal / non-sk melee USE a horse?
Something needs to be done to make horses loved by every one.
