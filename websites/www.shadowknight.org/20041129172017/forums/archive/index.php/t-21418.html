Little is known about the EQ 2 sk yet. But first little bits of info got posted on the new updated eq2 website.

As you know EQ 2 class system is a class tree where to become a a shadowknight you'll have to start as a fighter, then become a crusaders and then a shadowknight.

So here's what we know of the progression, skill and spells...

Fighter Archtype:
Fighters use brute strenght and sturdy weapons to deal physical damage to their ennemies. Always at the forefront of combat, fighters stand toe-to-toe with opponents while keeping their allies from harm. No matter the risk, fighters never back down from a challenge.

Primary attribute: Strenght
Armor: Light
Weapons: All
Key Knowledge: Martial, Weapon Knowledge

Crusaders:
Crusaders are armored juggernauts that call upon divine powers to aid them in battle. Capable of dealing impressive physical damage, crusaders can wield a variety of weapons and shields.

Primary attribute: Strenght
Armor: Medium
Weapons: Axes, Swords, Polearms, Flail, Hammers, Mace, Staff and more.
Key Knowledges: Faith, Zeal, Weapon Knowledge

Crusader spells:
Bastion of Allegience: Spell that grants protection versus physical attacks to a party member. Use: Buff

Cry of Conviction: Spell that channels damage to all oponents in an area around the crusader. Use: Damage

Manifestation of Faith: Spell that wards against damage for a short period of time on a pary member. This ward cost the Crusader Health in addition to Power. Use: Damage

Shadowknight:
Shadowknights are insidious dark crusaders who use the power of evil to advance their cause. They live to inflict fear, hate and despair upon all who cross their path.

Primary Attribute: Strenght
Armor: Heavym Vanguard
Weapons: Axes, Swords, Polearms, Flails, Hammer, Mace, Staff and more
Key Knowledges: Determined Zeal, Sphere of Influence, Weapon Knowledge

Spells:
Shadowy Illusion: Spell that instantly transport the party out of a dangerous situation into a safe area. Use: Utility

Malevolent Soldier: Spell that summon forth a skeletal warrior that battles the shadownight's opponent. Use: Damage

Paragon of Malevolence: Spell that grants a deathly form to the shadowknight. This form imbues the Shadowknight with enehanced destructive capabilities and additional protection. Use: Combat buff

After reading that, it's becomming more apparent that EQ 2 isn't EQ Live as the SK seems to have little in common with the EQ Live version.

Quote from Moorgard regarding archtype roles:
Originally posted by Moorgard
Our method is to base the game on five roles, each of which provides unique and necessary functions. As I've said, the differences within an archetype are about how that job is done, not how well it is done.

Is this distinction enough for you? That's a matter of personal taste. Fighters take damage, deal damage, and hold a mob's attention. Every Fighter is charged with that responsibility, and must do it as well as any other. Some will use spells, some will use arts; some will use avoidance, some will use mitigation; some will wear heavy armor that makes them look like a walking tank, others will wear much lighter armor.

Will the sub-classes within an archetype look different? Yes. Will they have different types of quests? Yes. Will they share some skills? Yes. Will they have unique skills? Yes. Will one sub-class be better at its main role than another? No.
