Slein Jinn
There are currently two tanking gaps between the three tank classes that must be addressed. One gap between Shadowknights and Paladins, and another between Warriors and Knights in general. These issues cannot be treated entirely independently from one another.

- Link the paladin Force of XXX line of stuns so that only one can be used at a time. This would give high-level Paladins a single line of stuns progressively upgraded to keep up with level increases. Upgrades should not increase in power, as the effects of stun spells scale to mob DPS.

- Replace the paladin Quellious' Word line (10.0 sec stuns) with a 0.0 sec stun with a high resist mod and low cast/recast time and a moderate mana drain. Approximate mana drain could range from the level 54 Quellious' Word of Tranquility at 65 mana to the level 69 Quellious' Word of Tranquility at 115 mana, with possible tweaking needed to the numbers depending on the final cast and recast times. These spells would fill paladin role of interrupting casters.

- Add a “Recourse Box” – a buff window specifically for recourse effects.

- Give Shadowknights a 20% accuracy debuff with high aggro. 18 sec duration, 12 sec recast, 1 sec cast, -55 resist check to magic, equivalent restrictions to paladin stuns. The high aggro is essential to prevent it from becoming a support spell – furthermore, it would be a welcome boost to aggro generation for Shadowknights.

This should be a new spell line rather than a single spell. Its max level should increase progressively along with paladin stuns, but its power should remain constant, as its effect scales with mob damage output.

Note that a Paladin chain casting the remaining line of stuns would avoid 31% of melee damage, barring interrupts, fizzles, and resists. Shadowknight’s lower percentage of avoidance would be balanced by its greater reliability (duration exceeds recast by 4*cast time) and the spell Aura of Pain.

- Give knights two disciplines to return them to their offtanking role.
--- Melee immunity discipline: shield block (thus shield must be equipped), 18 second duration, 40 minute reuse.
--- DI-mitigation discipline: 30-35% DI-mitigation, 72-second duration, 4.5-minute reuse, shield required.

They should be on separate reuse timers, but the mitigation discipline may share the reuse timer of the current defensively oriented knight disciplines.

Note that the mitigation discipline would have a lower total active time than defensive, in addition to mitigating notably less. If used whenever available, Defensive is active 28.57% of the time; the knight discipline would be active 26.66% of the time.

- Allow Shadowknights access to the Stonewall AA ability.

- Change the Malo lines of spells to debuff disease. It is the one resist that is not debuffed by the spell line and there is no justification for this. Disease is behind every other resist in the extent to which it is debuffed on raids, and it is the resist on which Shadowknights rely most heavily.

- Put Spell Casting Subtlety on a toggle; as it stands, purchasing the AA is detrimental.



Paladin stuns are at the root of a lot of the current class balance problems for the tank classes. The number of usable stuns varies by mob level and they are extremely powerful against blue trash mobs and the like, are entirely useless against immune mobs, and address only part of the problem with others (incoming DPS vs. being one-rounded). The above changes would reduce paladin reliance on stuns and make their impact more consistent. Currently, the impact of stuns varies greatly from mob to mob depending on level, immunity, resistance, etc. Paladins would be compensated for the lost stun power with the much-needed tanking disciplines.

Shadowknights would be brought into line with paladins through the accuracy debuff, which would effectively parallel paladin stuns. These abilities, together with the rapid-fire mitigation discipline, would cement knights in their traditional offtanking roles while having little impact on the traditional warrior role of raid main tanking. All three tank classes would be viable for single group content.

Note that this would actually be an improvement for warriors in a group setting in terms of power relative to Paladins, as stuns in their current state are potentially very overpowered against common XP mobs.

Slein Jinn
Other tanking discs have some sort of penalty - requiring a shield for our tanking disc is logical. Warriors are forced to carry multiple weapon setups, it's fair and balanced that we would be too. It's also fair in that its a bit less beneficial in the level of the game where we have the least trouble - the low end. This isn't a matter of content or anything either, it's a product of our non-scaling lifetaps and other abilities; low end SK's, while still suffering a bit, don't have the same level of imbalance as higher end SK's who are competing with scaling abilities using the same non-scaling abilities the low-enders are.

Regarding giving it a 3min duration/10min reuse, that's exactly the wrong way of going about it. Then you have the exact "light defensive" we've been trying to get away from. We don't want a watered down warrior discipline, we need something tuned to our role. Warriors tank the big mobs; they need the long lasting disc. Knights are offtanks, we need something that can be used rapid-fire as new waves of mobs come into the camp. As I described in the original post, our discipline would have slightly less active time than Defensive/Stonewall, not to mention its lower mitigation percentage.

As for fear2, I thought alot about it and even supported a fear2 type spell for awhile, but if you look at the bigger picture, it just isn't the right way to go about it. Fear2 would just be propagating the problem - fear2 is essentially a stun, and stuns are why knights are such a mess. We need to reduce the impact of stuns, not expand it.

As for the accuracy debuff, I spent a decent amount of time trying to balance the numbers and I'm confident that that's the best percentage/duration/cast/recast from a balance standpoint. The cast and recast are the same as the line of pally stuns that would remain; the duration is sufficient to make it reliable, and the debuff percentage is just right to make it balanced factoring in Aura of Pain and it's slight reliability advantage over pally stuns. As long as equivilant restrictions were placed on it as pally stuns (could probably even tie it to a 0.0 sec stun, but that's taking the easy way out) it would be a very well balanced spell.

Shields have never provided extra defense, mitigation, blocking other than the amount of ac/hp extra they provided versus your 2 handed weapon setup. The desire to use to shield should be a geniuine one based on some serious mitigation benefits, not because we wanted to invent an aesthetic reason by tying them to our disciplines.

As to the reasons I've elaborated for not doing this, here's my list:

1) The SK class is already restricted to 2 handed and 1 hand/ shield combinations. We are talking about the addition of huge major disciplines to our class, yet this suggestion would restrict our limited fight styles further into one fight style. We are already restricted enough, further restrictions are NOT needed.

2) 1 handed / shield fight styles are currently popular on the upper end, yet there was a time not long ago when those weapons were completely shunned and hated by the community. I can remember those posts vividly when any number of prominent SKs would say how they shunned their one handers for 2 handers. If Epic 1.5 and 2.0 are going to be implemented as 2-handers again, we are going to hate ourselves for asking for this restriction!

3) Sure, warriors carry multiple weapons, and use many of them for various tanking situations and for utility effects. I've never suggested that we not also do the same, and I myself keep a bag full of weapons / shields for various reasons. However, no class has a restriction on a particular weapon setup and using a discipline. There should never be a suprise factor of not being about to use a discipline, because a particular weapon setup wasn't equipped.

4) Ease of implementation. Having a flat 30 percent mitigation discipline 72 sec duration / 4.5 re-use that ran off the endurance bar with no other restrictions is easy for them to implement. It works like all the disciplines, copy something from the warrior templates, change some numbers, add the books into game - bang all done.
