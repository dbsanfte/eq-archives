Yay, search is my friend! I found this explanation from Brael for you bud:

Sure Inferna. Warriors generate their aggro through 5 methods, these are: melee aggro, +hate procs, high aggro procs, provoke line, and taunt.

In order for voice (or any aggro mod ability) to affect something, it has to meet 2 criteria, it has to not put you at x posistion on the hate list and it has to have an aggro mod on it. To have an aggro mod a spell must not give a fixed value of aggro (direct hate spells). Looking through the list of Warrior aggro generation, melee aggro is affected, +hate procs are not, high aggro procs are, provoke is not, and taunt is not. I don't have the time to go through and calculate it all right now and it varies by the weapon and combat fx, but melee aggro is roughly 35% of a warriors aggro, procs are 45%, and incite is that final 10%. Out of those procs on a high end warrior only 3 of them should be affected (pestilence shock, eoe's proc, and ykesha), which is around 10/15 of their average ppm (depending on where you aug the ykesha) so 2/3 of their proc aggro is affected by it, or 30% of overall aggro, plus 35% of melee aggro, thats 65% of their total aggro which is affected by aggro mods. That 12% is barely able to be justified with Warriors insane aggro as it is, but how do you feel about it when you find out its really just 7.8% additional aggro?

That made a lot of sense to me when I read it. I don't waste a buffslot on Voice anymore.
