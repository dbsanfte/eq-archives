Actually EQoA, just like EQ, has it's share of l33t sP3ak3rz as well as normal folk. I do think however, that most of the short attention span crowd has moved off EQoA by now and onto the latest and greatest, what ever that may be.

The first Expansion for EQoA is due out soon. They are going to open the continent of Odus, introduce Ogres as a race, and add a new class that does not exist in regular EQ. Since it's launch they've added traidskills, an auction feature (I think it's supposed to be a bazaar type thing but I'm not sure, it's been a while since I've played EQoA), and a couple more servers.

One thing I disliked about EQoA (A LOT) was item decay. As you fight your weapons/armor take damage. You can have them repaired at a smith (located at most towns), for a cost. The problem is it is VERY costly to repair items. The result is, in most groups, tanks are given all vendor loot to pay for repair costs. Apparently this is often not enough since I often see my fiance running around without any armor fighting green cons to try to get Tunar (the currency in EQoA; there is no Plat, Gold, Silver, Copper...just Tunar) to repair his equipment.

There is no Experience loss in EQoA (no level loss), instead you gain an Experience Debt. Experience is represented numerically (i.e. instead of just seeing "You gain experience!", you will see "You gain 434,957 experience!"). After a death half of all your gained experience points goes towards paying off your debt. Rez spells can help pay off a certain percentage of your debt (interesting note, Druids and Shaman in EQoA can also rez, though their rez spells are significantly less than a Cleric's).

There are no corpses in EQoA, upon death you respawn with all of your gear and half of the money you had on you when you died (it is popular to bank money often for this reason, if you have no money on you, you won't lose any money upon death).

Warriors/Rogues/Monks all have "Abilities" that work exactly the same way spells work. As a matter of fact, some "Abilities" are shared between SK's, War's, and Paladin's (i.e. the Taunt spells). It is almost as if there are no "Pure melee" classes in EQoA.

Anyways, I know all of this wasn't asked for, just thought I'd share the knowledge. :)
