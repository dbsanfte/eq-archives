Groff Gorespiller
Never hurts to send a tell and see if the guy will come down on it. I have a friend who makes haggling into an art. Consequently, he has paid up to 5k under asking price for items that he wanted, just by being patient and persistant. Figure out what you want to spend and then send the guy a tell and ask if he'll accept it. Most of the people that have these quest items up are really just looking to make a quick profit off of someone who is desperate, but they realize that there isn't much of a demand for the item, and when you come right down to it, the thing is taking up bank space that could be used for more profitable (and better marketable) items.

Here in the US, we have been beaten down by the notion that what someone wants for something is what they'll take for it. We're not used to haggling, since most everyone buys their goods at large chain stores. The price tag says what the price tag means. However, in some other countries, haggling is a way of life, and EQ is not just a US game. Give it a shot, the worst that can happen (presuming you're polite about it) is that the guy will say "no deal" and you'll have to raise the rest of the cash or wait for another to come up at the price you want.

If you really can't afford to pay that much for it, another option is to become the newest close personal friend of a Necro who is working on his/her epic. Check your server boards for posts asking for Necro Epic help, then hop in on some raids. They'll eventually have to go get the Mansi Herb, and the GMS drops near the herbshack.
