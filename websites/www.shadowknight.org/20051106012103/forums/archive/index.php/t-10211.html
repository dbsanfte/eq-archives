I love to talk about PvP =) I have some xp at it, played on Rallos, but not as a SK. Im only 53rd lvl, so everything will come from what spells I have now and such.

With that out of the way, I will share what I have learned when dueling people of the same level.

First, Depending on what class I fight I have different gear set up.

Pure caster - all resist gear on, MR in my oppinion is most important. It seems that once you get your resists 150+, your pretty much set for any pure caster.

Pure Melee - Strait up AC

Hybrid - Here is where it becomes tough. Again I would say the if they cant reduce your movment rate your better off. So put all your +MR gear on and try to keep the AC up at the same time. Again 150 seems to be the magic number.

Jboots are a must!! Or Sow potions, but Jboots prefered.

Learn to straif like a rogue.

Shrink, makes you harder to target.

Work your hide skill.

Have Pumice Stones from DE City.

Max swimming skill.

Make sure that you leave the 1st 2 slots open on your buff line. Have nullify/cancel magic up.

Spell line up at 53 that I prefer:
- Vs. melee's w/o Pet's, and Wiz/Dru
- Shadow Step
- Abd. of Str
- Attk. Tap
- Invis.
- Lifetap
- Spear
- Darkness
- Cancel magic

- Vs. caster w/pet
- Same but change out:
- Attk. tap out, and Fear in
- If I had another spear I would put it in place of Str tap.

SK vs. **** Class strategy: ( Disclaimer - this is from my experiance's at 53rd level with no AA's )

- Vs. Warrior/Rogue/Monk
- Duel starts:
- sick pet
- shadow step
- try to snare ( repeat 2 times if resisted)
- If still resisted, Dot
- Get out Big damage 2hander
- Melee Kite
- shadow step/refresh Dot's/Life Tap
- When at 40%, Spear/HT.

- Vs. SK/Pally/Ranger
- Duel starts:
- Normaly peeps will try to snare/blind/stun right away - this means RUSH them and Bash.
Once they inturupted and dazed from bash, you snare them.
- Vs. Ranger - Melee Kite but stay very close, dont let them use their bow.
- Vs. SK - this will normaly show who has more mana. Im and Ogre and dont have crap for mana, so I pray that my MR is high enough that they cant get me darkend. If I get dark to stick to them, they can have all the mana in the world and I will pummle them to death with my Reaver.
- Vs. Pally - LONG BATTLE - Cancel magic is your friend, so use it to take away their hp buffs! You must run them out of mana in order to kill them. Again will show who has the bigger mana pool. Try to provoke them to use their stun spells, run around. Anyway you can get them to use mana try it. Try to save your mana for when they run out. So when they run out of mana from stunning you. All they are now is a under powered warrior.

-Vs. Necro/Mage/Wiz
- Spell line up for caster's/Duel starts
- Have pet targeted - Fear pet
- The caster will have casted on you, your pet will go on caster.
- When caster roots/snares your pet - cancel magic on pet, re-fear his pet.
- Life tap as needed - Keep his pet feared. Your pet will do a number on him.
- Caster gets to 60%, spear, spear, HT = Dead.
- Get out your fastest weapon ( I use Shard of Night for this) and get on top of them. - Vs. mage, stick with 2 hander, or try to dispell the DS.

- Vs. Shaman/BST/Druid
- Caster line up ( for druid have melee line )
- Fear pet, pet on caster, dispell roots/snares
- With these folks you must get in their and wack them a few times. Your pet will not do very well alone against them. Most Shaman/BST/Druids by this level have some good AC.
- I use fast weapon here also
- Must run then out of mana to kill them. Try not to use life taps, they are very mana inefficient.
- Dispell druids DS - if they are smart, they will have burried it deep in the buff line. This will be a pain in the ass, but well worth the effort. Possibly switch to 2 handed weapon
- When they get low mana, they will try running away to hide and med - no matter what, do not let them go if you can keep up with them, stay on them.
- SHM/BST are very dangerous class's because of
slow. Watch for the spell graphic's and make sure to get a bash in there.

- Vs. Enchanter
- Pre-Duel: Cast: Disease Cloud ON SELF!!
- Get MR as high as humanly possible
- Duel starts:
- I wouldnt use a pet, they will most likely charm it and use it against you.
- They will have a major rune on themselves, they will have excellent MR from self buffs.
- Pray that they are not faster than you, chase them down. I use 2 hander on them, drops the rune faster.
- As soon as you start to do damage to them spear and HT before they can re-rune. This may kill them, if it didnt pray that you can break thru again.
- They normaly dont have a way to heal themselves, so stay on them and try to break their rune again.
- Mean while they tash ya, mez ya. They think they can sit and med. Not! Remeber that Disease could, yeah it breaks the mez. You get a free full damage shot at them while they are sitting! ( Use your 2 hander for it )
- When you break thru their rune again, spear, and pray they dont get another rune off.
- This will be a very hard fight, no matter what you do.
- If they use a pet, you use a pet and treat them like a mage or necro.

- Vs. Bard
- Gah! I hate dueling bards!
- I have never won a duel with a bard.
- If they are not completely stupid, you will not be able to beat them unless your friggen lucky.

This is what I have found to work on these class's, Im not claiming they are the best way's but they seem to work!

Some tricks to the trade:
- When dueling out side, if you need to get away from them for some reason, try to get to the top of a hill before you shadow step. This will ensure that you make a good long shadow step.
- If you are dueling a pet class, and they are in your group. First thing you do is cast Invis vs. Undead on them. This kills pet instantly.
- When fighting outside:
- Know your opponents spells, tree's work great for those line of sight spells. Hills woork good also.
- Water is your friend, casters will have to come in after you in order to hit you with spells.
- Know your territory, have a spot picked out in case of a retreat. Summon pet there, and park him, if you dont plan on using him in he duel. That way when you retreat you opponent will have another thing to focus on instead of you.

These are just a few of the ideas/strategies I use when dueling 1 on 1. For mass battle's it becomes much more complex. I dont feel like typen all that out today, lol. I touched on it in the "I won my 10th duel today" post. Page 2, top of the page.

Ok folks, hope that I didnt sound like a complete idiot, and you all learned somthing. Take it easy.

I have a 55 Necro. And I've dueled a lot.

I'm very skeptical of your plan for dueling Necros. It sounds very idealistic.

Here's what I've done in the past against SK's.

Never duel in a confined space. Near water is the best, with DMF up.

1) The melee always tries to start the duel right up in your face. If you back up the SK will move up with you.

2) Let them start the duel "/d".

3) When they start the duel, target the SK pet and SS away. Then while the SK is spinning around trying to find you, which they always do, hit "/d", and hit the pet attack button. Then immediately target the SK and hit pet attack. Your pet now has it's marching orders for the whole fight.

4) The necro pet will munch the SK pet in a very short amount of time. Then take off after the sk.

5) I have 4 dots I use in duels, 1 nuke, and 1 life tap. E-Bolt (which is the devil himself if you've ever been hit with it), Splurt, Bond of Death, Dooming Darkness (fast cast), Torba's Venom (600 dd), and Drain Soul.

6) I usually lead off with E-Bolt, because that messes with a person's head. It has a very nice "AWWE $***" factor. Then dooming darkness (iffy chance of sticking). By then the SK is running at me full speed, I then attempt another SS. If I'm far enough way, I finish the dotting. Sometimes I eat the HT, sometimes not, depending on distance. I always keep Extended Range gear on.

If the SK turns his attention to my pet at all he's royally screwed.
The only trouble I have is when the SK Shadow steps away. That can suck in a large zone.

All this really depends on resists. Most players don't have high poison and/or disease resists.

Another thing is, the only SK pet that would have any bearing on the duel would be the 64th level one. The others are simply a non issue.

I don't duel as much as I used to because most players cheat. They either are 2 boxing a healer or have a retarded friend that heals them.

Some nice tips here =)

I just wanted to clarify Tumy’s resist check for Druids and Rangers; thier DoT’s are magic based, not disease.
Make sure to always have your MR up past 150 at the very least.
I have two sets of equipment on me at all times. When I go out to PK, I put my MR gear on. With a Shaman or Cleric’s MR buff (+40 MR) and a Shaman Null Aura potion (+15 MR), I have 220 MR.
Even then, It’s not guaranteed that you will resist a root or snare. I have, however, resisted Winged Death several times, which really makes the Druids angry =P

Dispell is your friend and your enemy. When facing Druids, Rangers and other classes that depend heavily on thier buffs, I always lead off with Dispell magic or use pumice. Dispelling a Druids, SoW, MR buff and levitate usually results in the gating real quick. Ranger’s damage shield, SoW, STR buffs etc. are what makes them so formidable.
SS and dispell a few times before engaging in combat.

Melee classes:

Jousting is a must, regardless of the level of your opponent. The less damage you take, the less chance of you getting killed by reinforcements that may have just arrived. Jousting allows you to see what’s coming or what’s waiting in the shadows for you. Force your opponent (if they are a melee class) to have to move around. I usually run past them and quickly turn around and bash. Then strafe around them so thier back is still facing me, then turn auto attack on. This will usually confuse them as they try to figure out where you are. If they do eventually face you, walk backwards then lunge at them again and repeat. A good 2H will work wonders when this tactic works. The other advantage of being behind them, is that they cannot disarm, reposte or parry.
If at the beginning of the fight, you dipelled any see invis buff they may have had up, use “hide” when you lunge past them to remove yourself from thier target. They will then have to retarget, which can be a pain if you are constantly moving and hiding.
Save mana and only use DoTs, Taps or Spear line when needed to end the fight or when they begin to flee.
I NEVER use HT unless they are very close to a zone and begin to run when low on HP. Save it for the difficult fights.

Caster classes

They can be deadly or easy depending on level difference and tactics.
Most will strafe or walk backwards forcing you move with them. With that darn melee bug which makes it almost impossible to land any melee damage because of the “You are too far away” or “You cannot see your target” bug. This is where your DoTs, Taps and spear line come into play. Keep close to them and unleash quick casting spells on them. With casters, it’s not the 200+ 2HS that will win the fights, but that quick 1HS and bash that will...most of the time. I have beaten red con Druids using a Lamentation and a shield. It is so fast, that they will have a hard time channeling a spell through. Never let them stop and begin to cast a spell. A HT at the beginning of the fight will usually make them think twice about fighting or make them gate. I wouldn’t advise this, as then if they return, after being healed by thier pocket Cleric, you will have lost your coupe de gras to end the fight in most situations.
Disease cloud cast on yourself and poison potions work wonders when facing an Enchanter. If they Tash you, quickly use your poison potion to dispell it. Disease cloud will prevent you from being mezed for more than a tic.
Fearing pets and staying close to the caster with a fast weapon is usually key to a successful fight.

Now, I’m no expert Pker, the above info is just from my experience. I have died many, many, many times, but have learned a little more with each defeat. Visiting other classes web sites and learning about those classes, will make you a more educated dueler/pker. Just remember, there is always a bigger, more experienced bully than you out there =P
