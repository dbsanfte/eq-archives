DrMobius
I see a lot of are saying you spoke to a GM, are you sure it was the actual GM? If you use the /petition feature in-game then you're talking to a <b>Guide</b>, not a GM. Guides get the scoop on information and have access to technical and bug boards so they can see the current issues and respond accordingly; but they are not GM's. A "GM" is an Everquest CSR (people working for and paid by S.O.E.). You used to see GM's around much more often, but now you're lucky to see them on for a few hours a day.

I am not saying your petitions don't get taken care of, however. When you /petition a Guide will read it, then contact you regarding the issue. Guides have rules they must abide by (a very big binder full of them) and procedures to follow. For matters concerning a NPC, item, or zone bug the Guide will escalate the issue for a GM to look at <i>when the GM is next online</i>. When a GM reviews the bug petition they will either contact you via email (if your email under registration is still correct), contact you in game, or hand the bug issue to the developers. In every case they'll delete the petition.

The /bug command sends your information directly to the developers. Their emails to you are not canned, and they are telling you the fastest way to get the issue resolved. Whoever said that "It's not on the top priority list" should be kicked in the pants as improving the gameplay is #1 priority. I do not have an IC yet, so I personally can not /bug for it but everyone here that has one <b>needs to log a /bug for it</b> if you want to see it fixed. Think of EQCS as a mob...you have to get high on their agro list. :D

DrMobius
Vinnayeny,

If you send a Guide a tell they'll respond as well. Its good to hear that the GM responded to you, but the process they have to go through remains the same. If you petition, send a tell to a guide, or send a tell to the GM (who, by the way, can not be located with the /w all gm command.../w all gm only displays guides) your issue will all be handled the same way. Whoever takes the petition or receives the tell will first determine whether the problem is a known issue (known meaning development already knows about it and is <i>planning</i> go fix it <i>sometime</i> soon) or a new issue. For known issues, like Innoruuk's Curse, they would apologize for the inconvenience and post it on a bug board for the developers to look at. If you were to just use /bug in the game it would go directly to the developers, which would save you having to be weeded out by the Guide's or GM's discretion.

Furthermore, contacting a GM or Guide directly is not the best way to go about complaining or reporting anything in game. The reason is all the petitions are tracked and recorded, tells are not. For the most part, if you contact a Guide or GM directly they'll ask you to petition with the issue so there can be trail of information. Another issue you may run in to by sending the GM's or Guides tells directly is they may tell you, "Yeah, I'll be sure to let the developers know of your problems" and then not contact the developers at the end of their shift because there wasn't a petition to remind them.

As I said, using the /bug command is the <b>best</b> and most sure fire way to get any issues of game mechanics or physics taken care of.
