Lizarde
From around level 36 or 37 I fear kite almost excusively. Therefore, being stunned isn't a real concern, unless I get adds or am running away, in which case not being stunnable from the front doesn't enter into the equation (Iksar SK here, currently 47).

Dunno how this changes as you level up, but in groups where I am tanking being stunned isn't a big deal either. For one, it doesn't happen often. Second, with a good group there's enough damage being dealt by everyone around me. All I need to do is keep the mob's focus (which has been made more difficult, but at this level is still very manageable).

Regen, however, does make a difference. But it is much more noticeable at lower levels. I've made an SK from just about every race at some point or other (except gnome I think). Take a human, untwinked, to your newbie area, and you're bound to have some downtime when you get that level 1 mob that you cannot seem to land a hit on (which can happen a lot at low levels :( ). Take an iksar or troll to their newbie area, and you'll find that downtime is almost nil for several levels.

At higher levels it isn't as noticeable because regening for 10 minutes can seem as long as regening for 20 or 30 minutes if you are impatient enough. :) But it does make a difference even then, in that you can get back into action quicker. But by then you're fear-kiting more, or grouped with a healer, and at those times you're more concerned with mana regen than HP regen. Except when things go wrong, anyway. :)

Bulvyne
Hmmm, after reading over your post again, I have to say that with a 60 Shaman babysitting, I don't think it really matters which race you pick! :)

You are most concerned about the pulling, especially at higher levels. Well, since most pullers I know pull and run, your back is to the enemy anyway, so the frontal-stun immunity won't rightly help much, as far as the pull goes, unless you plan on running backwards on all your pulls. :) Let's say you're pulling, and you get beat down by the mob you pulled (maybe you were snared or rooted or something). Let's say you're too far away for your Shammy to run up and heal you, so you are lying there FD on the ground with 10 hp left. The Iksar should regen faster than the ogre, even if both have shammy regen. And as someone mentioned, at higher levels the 'regen' really isn't that big a deal, the regen feels very nice at lower levels, mostly.

If you're an ogre, your shammy can shrink you.

Your shammy can uber-buff you regardless of race.

The only other real differences is that the Iksar will be hated everywhere (no big deal), and the ogre will probably have consistently better stats (because I think they start with a bit better stats).

The Iksar "can't wear Kunark armor" limitation really isn't a limitation. You can't wear Umbral from Fear, but Greenmist armor is arguably better anyway. And all the "high end" armor I've seen on raids can be worn by Iksar, too, so I don't think that Iksar really have any sort of armor "limitation", other than Blood Ember and Sentient (no big deal here either). The really important armors (TOV and up) are wearable by Iksar.

Other than that, differences are pretty much cosmetic. Having the Shammy along for the ride really opens the door for you to pick any race you want! :)

Iksar/Troll will get rezz'd less often after pulling something that the MA can't get off you in time. Ogre has higher base stamina, so will die less often due to instantaneous damage (nuking).

Either way, since you can box a shammie, those 3 are the best. The racial exp penalty won't factor in on you as noticeably since you don't have downtime.

Trolls can also wear the Ogre racial stuff (saweeeet, I know 8 )

Once you get to 30 or so, you can start working on Giant faction, and get some Kael armor MQ'd. At that point, the iksar's penalties die off.

Since you really need to plug all your starting stats into stamina with an iksar, the fatties actually have a similar starting int compared. (my troll has 82 base, Iksar has 85).

Please though, if you go Ogre, put 5 into dex and agi first, then the other 10 into int. A dwarven cleric friend of mine loses 41 AC for his agi falling under 75.

EDIT: Ogre, with dex and agi upped to 75, has 80 int. Troll 82, Iksar 85.

One more thing to consider, Iksar have racial bonuses to save vs fire, whereas trolls have negative 20 to fire saves. Also, with the new bash rules, keep slam in mind. It'll allow you to outdamage any paladin, and most rangers and monks until you hit post 50/AA, thereby causing more aggro to tank better.
