Paradox
I totally love my DE SK. I haven't been MT on raids because my guild has a few Warriors that are much higher level than I am. In groups I am mainly puller. I'll pull and dump aggro on to the MT, then go pull another with some taking as needed. I can be MT in single groups, but I don't like it. I'd rather be doing something more involved.

I can solo quite well. Pets help with this even though they seem second rate at times. Think of them as an extra DoT. Some may say that larger races are better at soloing because of more HP and regen, others say that having more mana for fear and spells are better. It all depends on you're type of game play style. Later on, the stats can be all balanced out with gear, so in the long run, it may not matter - again, you're game play style.

A group of my guildmates went to SkyFire the other day killing wyverns, mature wurms, soul defilers, etc. Most of the people who went were casters or casters with pets. There was one other melee with me and after the pull came in, it came down to me calling the shots on actually battling the mobs (basically fearkiting, knowing the pathing and taking as needed to keep aggro off the nukers. Knowing when fear would break, when to tell casters to back pets off and standing toe to toe with the mobs were my main duties.)

I split my starting points into STR and STA bringing them up to 80 each from 70. As a DE SK, CHA and WIS mean nothing. INT level is fine where at 109 starting. I didn't think DEX was needed at first, but I have found that items with a plus to DEX are easier to come by than STA - at least ones you would want anyway.

I am in no-way uber and I there is a lot more I have to learn, but I hope this little bit I could share helps.

Manslayer
Race is more or less irrelevant in the endgame. Most profiles end up looking very similar. I have no issues tanking XP stuff or offtanking mobs on raids. Warriors get to tank the big stuff.

With VoG, Focus, Talisman of the Brute, Avatar, and C3, you need 88str, 70dex, 115agi, 205sta, and 230int to get them all to 255 with these buffs. Of course this requires you to have primal, which isn't something you will get for some time as a new character so discounting Avatar add 100 to str, dex, and agi. As a dark elf even I would gladly trade a ton of physical stats atm for 20int, since that seems to me to be the hardest stat to max while keeping my hps as high as possible.

And none of this really applies to the getting to 60 part, just the once you get to a raiding environment, though honestly getting to 60 is alot easier in general now than it used to be due to more zones to xp in, easier to get better gear etc.
