In general if you want to hold the best aggro you load 2 or 3 good taunt spells and chain cast them so you do nearly zero DPS. Frequently this is on tougher named mobs where you've turned attack off to eliminate riposte damage.

Therefore the argument about which weapon is "best" for aggro holding is kind of academic.

But if you want to hold off on spell casting for a bit and let your weapon help, whichever does most DPS is best.

It doesn't hurt to get a good weapon and aug it with stun like Maicom says though, provided you leave your best DPS one without aggro proc so you can still use it on raids. I have a Ykesha auged one hander myself for any time I want a little bit of extra free aggro. Don't use it much but it's there if I want it :)
