I have noticed this prior to LDoN. Mobs that are of a certain type of intelligence (for lack of a better term atm) or animal-like follow different rules, and I have to treat them differently from other mobs to hold aggro the same way.

Here are a few examples inside and outside of LDoN:

Wrulons in Halls of Honor.
Spiders in LDoN Mistmoore (but NOT Guk).
Bugs in LDoN Tak.
Bugs in LDoN Guk.
Wolves in LDoN Mir.
Wolves in LDoN Ruj.
Pigs in LDoN Ruj.
Spiders in Plane of Nightmares.

I don't know how to explain it without coming off looking like I'm imagining things. But I have 150 LDoN successes (unlocked 4/5 themes) and xp'd in PoN/HoH a lot (have 400+ aa points).

Certain mobs I have more difficulty with than others, and the only common denominator is that none of these are humanoid looking mobs, they are either insects or animals. If I can somehow manage to get 2 CD's off on the mob BEFORE the mob hits camp, I have aggro lock. But if I only get off one CD or if I am not the puller, it's a different story. If I am not pulling, one CD and one spear will USUALLY do, but if I don't have time to get both of those off, the mob may turn to someone else.

Any other mob, a live frog, dead frog, sand elf, orc, goblin, observer, various other live and undead mobs -- NO PROBLEM, business as usual: one CD and it doesn't look at anyone else. Some other mobs, like the examples I listed above, are... different. I do have a harder (that does not necessarily mean that I am struggling) time to initially gain aggro. There is a pattern.

For example... let's say you are in HoH and a monk pulls a wrulon with a shuriken and does little damage (miss or 5 pts or something). You cast a CD on the wrulon while it's incoming. The monk stops about 20 feet in front of you but does not FD. Who do you think the wrulon whacks on? Yep, not you. It's whacking the monk. Now, let's say this time the pull is a Valorian Guard, exact same scenario. Who do you think the guard whacks on? Monk, WHAT MONK??? As far as the guard is concerned, there never was a monk. This is what I mean by different reaction by different types of mobs.
