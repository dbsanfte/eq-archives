i never have trouble getting/keeping agro. if you're doing the best you can, frankly there's nothing anybody will do that will keep agro from you... unless they're extremely stupid and taunt or chain a high agro spell (stun/slow/tash/snare).

tips:
use clinging darkness and fear. i keep both memmed if there's a mezzer, otherwise i just use clinging darkness. chaining cd & fear is probably the most agro you can produce over time, and its easily maintainable (20 mana for CD, 40 for fear)

if the mob turns from you, make sure you're close to the mob and click taunt. next attack 80% of the time that alone will put you back on top of the hate list. throw a clinging in after and you again have a nice agro lock.

if you have a chain puller, switch to the next mob when its incoming regardless of whether the other mob is dead. also if you have mezzed mobs in camp, switch to them before the first mob is dead and taunt -> fear -> taunt -> cd to have an agro lock on it for sure, or just taunt -> cd is sufficient most of the time.

know your key binds, and use them.. ie use F2, F3, F4, F5, or F6 to target the puller, and press 1 or whatever hotkey is your assist key, or set up a hotkey /target puller, /assist every time you change groups.

adjust the amount of agro you generate with your groups, IE generally 2-3 CDs is enough for me to have an agro lock for the entire fight, but when someone consistently is agroing above that i add another CD and i'm set.
Yabyab
From my own experience Fear generate less aggro than CD. But I know that some monsters react differently to some spells. But overall CD > Fear in terms of aggro. Thats more than you want really.

Always try to keep the slower as far away from the monster when pulling as you can (at least yuo have to be closer to the monster than the slower when slow lands). Range affects aggro ALOT.

For people fighting monsters of less than 10khp and grouping with kickass nukers. You will not keep aggro with CD/Fear(if they not immuned to it) this way. Because Damage Aggro is Scaled towards Total Percentage of Damage. The 6k nuke landing on a 10khp monster will hit 60% of its HP <-- this is a HUGE aggro. While a 6k nuke landing on a 60khp monster is just a 10% damage <-- this is not as bad. The damage done is the same but the aggro is different because damage Percentage is different.

I've noticed that CD, and most of other negative-effect spells generate less aggro the less HP your target has. For instance, CDing a Golem in PoV will generate solid aggro. Go to Crush Bone and CD a Legionaire, any wizard nuking for 150damage will overaggro you! thus, Terror line is more useful against Monsters with less HP.

Thats what I've observed through my hunting experience.
