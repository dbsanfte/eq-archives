This is sort of a carryover from the "Booties" thread.

I don't mind people who hang out in hopes of gaining a no drop item for a low level character so long as they don't make a nuisance of themselves. Depending upon how you look at it, they're still "earning" the gear by waiting around for it, even if not actually working to obtain it. Whether this is truly an efficient means of using your time is another matter open for debate.

If they do become a nuisance, then I'll tell them politely to stop being a nuisance, or we will destroy the no drop items. Beyond that, there's not much you can do to stop them because whether or not a given zone is out of their league, they have just as much right to be there as anyone else.

But then there are other situations. The Sword of Pain in Velk's for example, has become a very sore spot for me. I have spent a lot of time camping it recently, and had no luck. Gregendek continues to only want to drop that stupid book when I'm there (5 Gregs, 5 books). One night I had a prospective group member get hysterical that if one dropped he would get an equal roll on it for his twink. Fortunately, the rest of the group told him where he and his twink could go and we sought a different person to fill the group. I've had other groups outright tell me "no, you're a SK, we don't want you for the group, we want the sword for our twinks." I dropped down there the other day to uh, excercise the first rule of velk's club, to find a group there and a naked level 1 gnome SK standing around. Every time now that I have gone in there, if there's not a twink actively logged in waiting to loot one, someone in the group has a twink camped there and wants one. (Yes, I know, I just need to take in my own group for it, but we usually need 1 or 2 pickups to make 6, and we continue to run into these people who think their level 1 SK's "need" for the sword outweighs mine at 56.)

So yes the "vulturing" has gone too far. There are few real newbies anymore, and everyone seems to want their alts to be uber right out of the box. I have no problems with twinking, I have a well-twinked mage myself but I would NEVER put obtaining an item for my twink over a person in the group that can and would use the item. It's gotten disgusting, and I just wish VI would do something about it, like start making a lot more gear with level restrictions.

Where do you draw the line?

If someone is hanging around to loot no drop items, and the following conditions are met:

1) No one in the group needs it.
2) No one in the group's alt needs it AND are camped right there as to not slow the group down
3) The person is not annoying, an ass, or getting in the way

then I have absolutely no problem giving them a hand. The SoP example above is just sad, and takes it to an extreme. But I will not intentionally destroy or let rot something someone can use that's there, because at that point you're just bitter or a jerk. It's not fun to make people sad or mad because you intentionally destroyed something you had no use for and could have helped them out tremendously.

Is it more satisfying to work towards something? Sure. And there are PLENTY of opportunities in this game to do exactly that. So having some boots and gloves thrown your way is not going to a) ruin the game or b) turn the people into bad players. This is a marathon, and I'll take opportunities to help people along the way.
