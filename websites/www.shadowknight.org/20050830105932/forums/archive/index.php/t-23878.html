I'm writing this with the hope that EQ 1 will benefit in the area of population as well find shelter against possible patches that may make it more like EQ 2.

What follows is a brief overview of some of the features in EQ 2 and whether or not I found them to be positive or negative changes. I tried not to come off too upset over being disappointed but, I think I lost it near the end.

1) THE GRAPHICS ENGINE
Pro's
-Looks Great!

-Will scale with technology very well

Con's
-There's a memory leak some place.
After awhile, the screen gets to stuttering so badly that eventually you have to log out, close out and restart the client.

-Rendering distance.
Objects pop on to screen abruptly even if you drop all options and raise rendering distance to max. This is by far the most annoying to me. EQ 1 and WOW render far distances very well on my machine.

-You'll probably need to upgrade your hardware.

2) USER INTERFACE
Pro's
-The quest journal.
It's outstanding. I wish this would have been in EQ 1. I never would have left.

Con's
-It has an unfinished feel doesn't contain many of the features currently in EQ 1.

-Spell actions and a melee action now both run off power.
I think here again they gave into non casting whiners that it just wasn't fair that other classes got to take advantage of mana on gear but, they could not. Again they made the game fair and... it ruins it. Diversity, Individualism. Living with the choices you've made. You can't have it all doesn't detract from the game, it enhances it.

-All buttons be they actions or spells, look like square icons.
You basically end up with 36 buttons. Yay! Lots of ability. Zero feel. I liked having a spell bar and a hotkey bar. I liked the fact I was limited to the amount of spells I had access to and that it took strategy and time to memorize and setup my spells.

-Icons everywhere but, none that feel real.
The icons for items don't feel like objects at all. First of all, they don't have transparent backgrounds so they all look like square pictures instead of objects. Secondly, (this one is really irritating) they've restricted what you could do with icons. You can't put stuff on the ground. If you try to move an icon some place it doesn't go, it doesn't stay on your mouse it pops back to where it was before. This really adds to the feeling that you are not really dealing with objects in the game and for me that is frustrating. What's more upsetting is that this was clearly motivated by greed in that the less items lost to misplacement the less customer service they would have to perform. You'll see this thinking in a lot of how things work in the game.

-Hotkeys are not elaborate enough.
I don’t' get how to make them so that they can do the things they do in EQ 1. I don't get and I've been playing EQ 1 for 5 years. If I don't get it, It's too hard. And the manual is worthless even after is it was updated.

-The Map is weak.
(a) The map is not a scaled version of the actual terrain. Even UO had this. WoW has this.
(b) No directional pointer so you know what direction you're going.
(c) There's no creativity with the map. Why not tie it to a mapmaking skill? Make it so the more you travel with that map skill on the more clear it is. Wizardry 7 had this.
(d) Some zones still have no map even after you've been in them a great deal and leveled past them. This can make traveling to lower levels of those dungeons irritating.
(e) There's no way to edit the map or make notes
(f) Waypoints on the map don't work

3) COMBAT
Pro's
-HO's
These are very cool and really can make group combat interesting esp. at higher level's

Con's
-Kicking and bashing have time bars go across the screen.
This detracts from combat feeling real time like it is in EQ 1. This is basically a result of them meshing the combat actions with the spell actions.

-Grouped mobs cannot be pulled individually.
Again they took control away from the player and made a canned encounter. BORING!

-Encounters are locked.
You cannot help someone unless they /yell for help at which time they do not get exp or any other benefits from the kill and neither do you. I can use no other word here but, "Lame".

4) CHARACTER FEEL
(This set of issues is the main reason I'm choosing to go back to EQ 1)
Pro's
-Support for odd race/class combinations.
You want a Troll Paladin? Dark elf Ranger? You got it!

Con's
-There's no individualism or Class feel.
They did a couple things here that caused this;

There's no Race starting cities. They piled all the good class's in one city and all the evil class in another. So, right away any individual cultural feel is gone. This is more important to me then I ever thought. I forgot how much I loved the culture and history of both my EQ 1 Chars. Esp. my Iksar. There was a rich heritage and cultural history that really interested me and gave me a certain pride in playing my Iksar Shadow knight.

The Starting abilities are gone. Trolls no longer Regen, Large races no longer get slam, etc. What abilities are in the game are done through cheesy spells and do not feel like native abilities. There are slight venations in stats but, these mean very, very little as gear quickly makes up for deficiencies.

I really feel like they gave into whiners here. If you make all the world fair then you take out the diversity and without diversity the game is boring. Even with abilities there were still those who picked different races because they *liked* being a Human Paladin or other race class combo. They liked it because it had a feel to it all it's own.

Spells feel watered down. There's no specifically "evil" feeling spells for my class. They might have evil sounding names but, I could well imagine renaming them and assigning them to the paladin. I haven't checked but, I'm betting this is what they did. Gone are the days of Life Taps and skeleton pets.

5) QUESTING
Pro's
-Awesome quest journal.
-Very few broken quests.
-Many quests are well thought out and have neat and or otherwise interesting story lines.

Con's
-None that I can think of.

6) MISSING FEATURES THAT SHOULD BE IN THIS GENERATION OF MMO'S
(This one is a bit different. This item is more about my disappointment rather then pro's and con's)

-Voice communication
I had high hopes for this. The technology is there. If it had this I might want to stay. I know third party apps are out there but, everyone isn't using them.

-Seamless zoning
I know they claim that they can make more diverse environments this way but, I don't believe it. I see other MMO games without zoning and they do great with this and once you play a game that doesn't have zoning you will really realize how much you hate it. (WoW is my example here and not EQ 1)

7) HOUSING
Pro's
-Neat idea
-Can really be fun to decorate

Con's
-No windows
This could have been done and would have helped to make you feel like your not in a box. I'd like to go out on my patio and see the city.

-Even the *very* expensive ones are shoddy looking

8) SPELLS
Pro's
-None over EQ 1

Con's
-Confusing.
The spell descriptions don't tell you enough about what they do and upgrading the spells doesn't give you a clear enough indication what the advantage is of doing so.

-Redundant
Some spells are replaced with other spells and it's not clear if you should find upgrades for the older spell or if you're supposed to switch to the newer one.

9) THE MANUAL
Pro's
It's a manual /shrug

Con's
-Lacks Detail
The manual is not detailed enough and clearly shows signs as the rest of the game does that it was shipped before it was ready. Coat tailing on Halo2? Competing with WOW? Nice work! You shipped a turd.

10) GEAR & RESTRICTIONS
Gear is restricted by skill. At my level there is one weapon that is the best and I have no option of using a better one. There is no chance of rising above the developer’s expectations and being better then what they designed you to be. This makes for a canned setup yet again. I understand you don't want to totally unbalance the game however, allowing just a few of the most dedicated to outclass their predicted status gives the rest hope and feels less like a canned experience.

In the end, I would sum up the ruination of this game being caused by them "balancing" the game. If you want balanced, Play EQ 2, If you want fun, EQ 1 gets my vote

"1) Portal systems - for those too afraid or lazy to travel through dangerous areas to get where they want."

Screw that. I ran from Freeport to Qeynos just to see the sights, long after PoP came out. I did it by both routes, too. And I'm an Iksar who's only non-KOS in Rivervale because I enjoy beating the life from Pickclaws in occupied Runnyeye, because they say witty things when they die. That said, screw that. When I feel like running around, I run around. When I want to go somewhere to fight, I find it much easier to jump a book-port than beg like a sissy from some soft-skin just because SOE decided that none of the noble race can do ports. You can call me lazy for that, but I left Kunark the hard way, and I've done my time taking to my heels to get places, and the books just make it more fun to play since I'm not stuck blowing half of my time hoofing it to wherever I want to be, or spending time running endless faction so I'm not twenty minutes from the nearest friendly merchant.

"2) Graveyards - hate corpse runs? No problem if you're in a zone with a GY, just wait a few min."

Since that's only a few zones, and since many of those zones would be impossible to penetrate without fully-geared forces, it seems they put it in so that a wiped-out Manaetic Behemoth raid didn't result in making some random necromancer independently wealthy. Big deal that we don't have to spend two hours getting our corpses back from a failed raid. It doesn't affect game balance in any realistic way, and it's easier on everyone involved.

"3) Maps - forget the old days of loc numbers and printouts from eq atlas (or just learning a zone the old fashion way). Just pull up your map and you'll never get lost again... unless you happen to accidentally find your way into a real dungeon."

Cry me a river on this one. I went through reams of graph paper playing _The Bard's Tale_ and other games, and I found that it was not fun at all, but necessary. Now, I'd find it to be just a pain in my scaled ass to have to draw my own maps again, and I don't even have maps loaded for most zones, because I did learn it "the old fashioned" way. That doesn't mean that I'd wish that effort on anyone else. Having decent maps and the ability to tell what direction you're facing just means you can spend your time playing the fun parts of the game. Wandering around the Greater Faydark for an hour and a half trying to find something other than a zone edge or your last three corpses is enough to put anyone off of EQ.

"4) No more hell levels or staring at your spellbook all damn day (before level 30). I actually agree with those, but of course I did my time in both."

Boo hoo. I had to sit though acres of suck, too, but I always felt it served no purpose to make someone stare at the spellbook. With the introduction of camera changes, it becomes even less relevant (who meds in first person mode anyway?) so getting rid of this was long overdue. Hell levels were just due to the math for gaining levels, and again it wasn't planned, it was just an unforeseen side effect. New shadowknights still need to gain the same amount of experience to get to the same levels as the rest of us, it's just smoothed out more, so they don't seem to spend so much time in hell levels.

"5) New tradeskill interfaces. Pretty sweet, but I got really good at ctrl clicking arrow parts before they put that in. Definately made skilling up a lot easier, but in a good way I suppose."

How could the Legions of Cabilis possibly expect to conquer all of Norrath if we had to waste time with the old way of tradeskilling? It took away far too much time, and now I can just build my arrows and get back to spreading fear, instead of taking ten minutes to massage out my wrist.

The short story for me is that there are things that make the game harder, and there are things that make playing the game harder. I complain loudly when the first is interfered with, but I applaud when the second is fixed. It's tough enough to become universally feared and loathed in Everquest without interface and gameplay issues getting in the way.
