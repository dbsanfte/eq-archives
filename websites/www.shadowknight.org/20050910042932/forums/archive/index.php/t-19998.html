Originally posted by SkTyger
Does a successfull 'taunt' check lower aggro (or position in hate list) of others?
Lower other's aggro; no. Lower their position on hate list; sometimes, but it's relative.

A successful taunt puts you on top of the hate list by one point of aggro.

Say there's a warrior and sk in the same group, with 2dps and 2 support people. SK grabs initial aggro, hate list could look like this:

1. SK
2. Shaman
3. Ranger
4. Warrior
5. Wizard
6. Cleric

Warrior taunts:

1. Warrior
2. SK
3. Shaman
4. Ranger
5. Wizard
6. Cleric

So in that scenario, a successful taunt did lower relative positions on the hate list, but it didn't actually lower anyone's aggro. Make sense?

No, Mathas is correct on effects of taunt. It can instantly put you at the top if it is successful, hence it's usefullness. However, other high dps classes can quickly take it back if they don't control their aggro.

That's why CD and Fear work so well. It takes a shitload of dps to overcome the aggro generation of those. When we tank, we use the taunt button just like a warrior does when it pops up. However, keeping CD/Fear tossed in allows the real damage dealers to go full bore, and that means a mob is dead real quick, and the healers only have to worry about healing one person.

Successful Taunt = highest Hate +1.
Supposing you are highest on the hate list throughout the fight, all you are doing when mashing Taunt is adding 1 point to the Hate list; effectively just bringing yourself up one point each time.

While doing this, everyone else would potentially be generating more hate based on what they are doing, closing the gap. As an example, they may be gaining 700 hate each time they cast a spell but you are only gaining 1 every time you Taunt.
