Although I'm not sure if there are other AA's where buying it makes you better in some situations and worse in others (rather than just the same, like SU on live mobs for example).
Maybe... but, right... like I said... the way SCS works is without precadent (at least to my knowledge). If there were other AAs (or class skills like Offense, Dodge, Meditate, ect.) that worked like this, I could perhaps accept that logic, but why SCS should be the only passive AA/skill that you can't shut off that reduces your effectiveness in a very serious way, I don't understand.

The only skill that even comes close to being comparable to the SCS AA is Specialization*, which is explicitly designed to force you to make some kind of "choice". But specialization doesn't reduce your character's effectiveness in any way from what it is before you get the skill... it just makes you choose which skill you want to improve most (if my understanding of specialization is correct, it does in fact marginally improve even the spell skills you don't specialize in). And unlike the SCS AA, you can undo your spell specialization if you decide you made a mistake and want to change your mind.

* Or I guess the tradeskill specialization thing, but I can't see that as being a serious issue with the availability of New Tanaan and mules.

So if Sony is trying to "force a choice" with the SCS AA, it seems a pretty arbitrary and ill-conceived way of implementing such a choice.

Now I could be completely off the mark here, but I think the real reason they won't give us the option to turn off SCS is stubbornness and a degree of what you might call shame. They don't like the aggro kiting tactic, they don't like the way Shadow Knights use Clinging Darkness/Fear instead of their tailor-made aggro spells, but they can't figure out a balanced way to fix it. I think they consider the fact these things are done signs that they've failed in some aspect of game design, but can't really figure out what to do about it. So they put in a skill that gimps these tactics, that most of the classes that tend to want to avoid aggro will bite the bullet and learn, and that the classes that are "tanks"/aggro masters will just decide not to learn. They don't want to nerf us, but they will let us nerf ourselves closer to what their idea of our supposed roles/talents should be.

In any event, every line of reasoning I can imagine for them designing the skill this way demonstrates itself as being at the least, very weird, and at worst, very stupid. They could fix the skill and it would not have any serious effect on game balance either way, and it would make players happier.

It could just be that they're too lazy to do it, I guess, or that they have other priorities. But come on, we've had the ability for almost 3 expansions now.
