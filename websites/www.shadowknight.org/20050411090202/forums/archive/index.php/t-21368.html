Dakkin
Guk is a pain. Unless you badly need named drops from there or want excitement, avoid it on hards.

Pet pulling casters first works so long as there's no hasting or sowing mobs in the room, and you trust your group to stop the mob gating, and you're not doing it all the time in which case you'll have to keep recasting the pet when it dies.

If all else fails, put your group in a side room, then snare one non-caster and train the rest to zone or far enough away you can guarantee to FD safely.

Warrior tags the snared one and group kills it (with maybe one add or so unless you're incredibly unlucky) in the side room while you zone out and back in. Rinse repeat till the room's clear.

The logic of pulling non-casters first is they don't gate, so since you've trivialised the pull you get down to max. 3 mobs if one gates on you. (That's the way bards usually do it.)

Sadly Guk is full of unsnareable eyeballs so again, one more reason to avoid it.

Incidentally, if you're not tanking and the group has plenty of DPS you save a LOT of time if you reduce your role to lining up the next snared single mob just out of camp for the warr to tag the instant the current one dies, while keeping one free room to FD/tag in between the group and mobs.

I tend to get complaints when I do this though because it makes hard LDoNs so trivial (even bard pulling is exciting compared with tagging pre-snared singles for zero risk constant killing).
