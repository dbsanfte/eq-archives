Wizzy/SK is really not a great duo though. Until the wizzy can quad, she will slow you down. Once she can quad, you will slow her down. There really is no complementary overlap. If the two of you join a group together, then yes, you would be a good bodyguard.

Cirrean
You obviously intend to duo with your girlfriend's wizard right now, but there will surely be times when you want the ability to solo. The advice you've already been given is right on track about needing a mana pool to keep you alive via lifetaps. I knew I'd probably be soloing a lot, so I chose Erudite for race because of the INT, and have had to get a lot of stat gear to compensate...but I can fear-kite quite well. As a large race, you will have some benefits in the melee stats, but you're going to want a decent mana pool to stay alive or solo effectively.

Obviously, being an ogre partnered with a dark elf will work fine in that you won't be KOS to each other's starting towns, but if you are confident that you can get around safely, you might consider an Iksar. If you aren't twinked, you might be glad for the regen and AC bonus, plus innate foraging and swimming skill. With the Nexus and Plane of Knowledge available, getting around is extremely easy, so your KOS status isn't the roadblock it would have been in the past. If you don't want to be a lizard, maybe a troll would be good too. You lose the frontal stun resistance of the ogre, but still have regen.

Whatever you decide, having someone to team up with will be fun. Just realize that the wizard/SK duo isn't optimal compared to many others. I rarely turn anyone away from the SK class, but beastlord or paladin may be a better team-up class.

Shadowknights make a good duo combo with any healing class. The SK ability to tank compliments the priest's ability to heal nicely and their ability to snare and feign can help with runners and getting singles.

Wizards, on the other hand, don't make a good duo combo with any class in the game. :( As soon as you add a second class to a wizard, they lose their ability to use their 10 damage per mana Pillar spells and are reduced to their 3 damage per mana DD spells. A wizard really needs some form of external mana regeneration to perform well in a group (Clarity or Bardsong).

By grouping a wizard with a shadowknight, you fail to cover either one of their weaknesses. Sk's inability to downtime heal and the wizard's inability to quickly regen mana. On the one hand, you're not optimal and things will be slow going. On the other hand, it's not like there's a ton of classes that partner with a wizard any better. No manaless damage sources and no innate enhanced mana regen make the wizard a fairly ineffective partner when fighting single mobs, even while fear kiting or agro kiting.

It's one of the prices you pay for being a wizard. They arn't twinkable, they don't duo well, they don't powerlevel well. Sad thing is you'd both probably gain exp faster if you both just soloed seperatly while grouped and close enough to one another to get exp messages from eachother's kills. :p

Zretch
Bard, Enchanter, and Beastlord are really the 3 classes that can help give the wizard mana back to make them more effective. Of them, the Enchanter gets that ability the earliest by far with Breeze. Bard would be next with manasong, and finally Beastlord with the Purity line.

Enchanters probably compliment the wizard the best. Chanters can fear, DD, have a pet, and provide mana regen. Wizards can snare and DD. That opens up fear kiting.

I would say Beastlords pair up next best. The wizard has the ability to snare and root, where the beastlord lacks both. The Beastie brings slows, a very nice pet, and a pet heal. If you want a Wizard/Tank combo, the closest you're probably going to get is Wizard/Beastlord. As an added bonus, Ogres can be Beastlords! :D Yea, their bear is kinda dull, but it can get to be HUGE at higher levels. :)

IMO, a bard is the worst pairing of the 3, but still probably better than a Shadowknight. :) A bard kills very slowly but makes up for that fact by having no downtime, whether it's DoT kiting or Fear kiting. Speed up the kills and the bard won't be contributing much. Slow down the kills and the wizard really isn't bringing all that much. Swarm kiting is absolutly out of the question in a duo situation. In order for swarm kiting to work, the bard's pet needs to have agro. The minute the wizard snares, roots, or DDs even one mob in the swarm, that mob will peel off and go after the wizard.

I agree with the poster above. If you're just looking for "a tank that compliments a wizard", don't take a Shadowknight. I don't think you'll be happy. Give a Wizard/Ogre Beastlord a go and see how that works out. :) At low levels it should work out well as Beastlords are very good soloists and at higher levels Spiritual Radiance should help the wizard keep up some decent damage while the Beastlord plays pet cleric.
