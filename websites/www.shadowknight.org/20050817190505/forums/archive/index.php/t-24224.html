Ice side is pretty trivial if you have a mezzer with the high-level mezz. Until then, it's...challenging. There are no safe spots. The one-minute stun/mezz that mobs cast on you can be removed with RC. The AE stun isn't as bad as it sounds. I kinda like ice side, just because it's a little different. Plenty of named to choose from, and no competition.

For stairs camp, there are four PHs you want to pull.

Up the stairs, then right, there is an aneuk on the left-hand side of the room. He comes solo. PH for Aneuk Painwringer.

Right, then first left from camp there is a room with 3 mobs. One is an ikaav. PH for for Ikaav Neuromancer. All mobs in this room are single pulls.

Right, then second left from camp there is a room with a commanded chimera on the right side. PH for Companion Prefect. Comes solo.

Right, then right, there is a pyrilen in the middle of a large round room. PH for Pyrilen Something (too early to remember stuff). Wait for dogs to path away, pull the trash mob to his left first, and then single pull.

You'll need semi-decent DPS and not much AFK time to get all four PHs in a 19-minute span.

02-15-2005, 10:02 AM
Riftseeker's Beastiary (http://eqbeastiary.allakhazam.com/search.shtml?zone=265)

The stairs leading up to fire side is definitely the easiest camp, and there is really only one wandering mob you need to deal with. Clearing the PHs as described above you can get the Molten Metal Bracer (Channeler Pyren), Celestial Lorica (Companion Prefect), +65 Hp augs (Companion Prefect, sadly occupy same slots as LDoN AC augs), a fire DD proc augment (Ruiner Isaka), and spell runes. If your group doesn't have the highest DPS and clearing everything repeatedly is too much, you can still manage to pull the Companion Prefect (or PH) single right over Pyrilen mobs without them assisting by using a pet and/or Circlet.

Heading up the stairs all the way to statue will bring you to a camp with two easilly pullable named; the Researcher and Summoner (located in the middle of the round rooms adjacent to statue main room). They drops runes, one has +70 Hp aug, one has +70 mana aug. Sadly, they can charm, which is a hassle. If your group can handle alot of clearing, it is also possible to pull the Pyrilen Riftseeker PH (or Balancer Relik, same room) all the way back to statue camp but this again is a mob that charms and is a tough, longish pull in an area with lots of wandering mobs. Riftseeker drops 25 AC augs, however, so is worth camping all by itself. Statue camp is typically farmed most often for the glowing and greater runes.

Ice does seem to have more wanderers, but there are places you can safely camp once you have cleared though a bit. Like the fire side, I find the first flight of stairs to be the safest, easiest camp, but pulling phs back to the camp is a long pull. From here I have been able to grab Mistwalker (drops ring and shield) and Girplan Shardtender (drops 20 AC aug). The Discordling Shardmender PH (drops a belt) is also around here somewhere. Again, using pet and circlet will allow you to pull non-Gelidran PH mobs (Girplans, Ferans, or Discordlings) without the Gelidrans assisting. This will save you alot of work once you get the hang of it. Doing this I usually don't even bother clearing the wandering Gelidrans, so long as they don't see invis. I haven't worked my way in far enough to get to the Girplan Stonesinger camp, but the chalice is worth farming if you lack a Form of Endurance clicky.

I pull Mistwalker to the first flight of stairs leading down to the Ice side from the zone in.

Pull any see invis wanderers/statics along the way, then place a pet at the entrance to the round room where Mistwalker spawn. Go around the corner to the stairway leading back to camp and use F9 to do a quick check for wandering Gelidrans. Gelidrans don't assist the other creature model mobs, but they will proximity aggro so look out for them. What I do is pretarget Mistwalker, sacrifice my pet to aggro him and his buddies in the room (Girplan and Taskfiend model mobs), try to land snare on him and / or FD before getting beat down. If I manage to get him split then I Rule #1 and head back to camp with him snared in tow. I refresh snare on him to build aggro and keep him stuck to me in case I get aggro from something along the way back somehow and that's it.

I haven't got the shield yet but I've got the ring, and it is pretty handy if you don't have Qvic arms, Time BP, or Ikkinz Legs, etc.

Mistwalker is a challenge to pull unless you just clear the whole area and specifically camp him. It is a fun challenge though and if you practice at it you'll be able to get him or his PH singled regularly without even having to clear a single wanderer. However, if you are going to go through all this trouble, make sure you are clearing the PH for the Girplan Shardtender. Having four 20 AC augs > the Ring and Shield that Mistwalker drops.
