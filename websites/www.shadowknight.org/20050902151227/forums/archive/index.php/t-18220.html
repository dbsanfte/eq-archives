If you have any insta-clicks, use them in the first 2 slots of your buffs.
If he dispels you, click these to fill the spots instantly so your more important buffs are not dispelled.
Use terrain tricks to get rid of the pet..example…

If you are dueling in The Overthere, have levitation up and have the pet chase you to the edge of the big cavern in the middle. Let the pet drop over the edge while you come back.
The pet will not be able to get back to the BL unless the he summons it.

After you get rid of the pet, dispel the heck out of the BL.
As for melee, always strafe behind him and whack him. This will confuse the heck out of him. He will spend most of the fight turning around trying to locate you. Use spears in-between swings.
If he manages to outmaneuver you, Shadow Step, tap and try to melee again.
Finish off with HT.

Beastlord slows are all magic resist. Only shamans have disease resist.

Unless you have your horse, don't count on outrunning a beastlord, we can take hits and channel a SoW if need be. Disease and Poison dots can be nasty, but we inherit some of the best cures for both from shamans.

Warder quads, and the 62 warder hits in the neighborhood of 50-70 per hit and hasted is a real nightmare. Beastlords can pick procs, meaning dispelling a beastlord's warder can ironically be a bad idea, if they notice you are resisting the magic proc, they'll switch to cold, or to fire, or to disease...

Warders not only quad, but they bash, make sure you have improved channeling focus, or can keep away from them. Dumping mana on defeating the warder, or worse your harm touch, is exactly what the beastlord WANTS you do to do. They get out of your range, wait for the pet to die (it will be sowed of course, and that sow will be at the bottom of a LONG list of buffs, pets can have up to 30 and we have a wide variety of crap buffs we can drop on them.)

As far as dots on me go from NPC necros, I tend to ignore them as long as I am not taking melee damage. Self-buffed I can get to over 3khp and with race regen, aa regen, spiritual purity, and chloroplast, I heal fast without having to worry about mana.

Biggest risks are going to be getting blinded via FoL, so keep MR up. Don't let the BL kite you, you need to kill them, and they are (if they are smart) going to stay sowed and stay far enough away from you that you have to focus on their pet. Their dots, while not as nasty as yours, do pack a punch starting with Scorpion Venom at 62 (mind your poison resist.) Be especially careful of beastlords who are level 64 and have that pet and suspend minion. Mana dump to kill the first one and hope to defeat the beastlord can have you in for a very rude suprise.

Have some means of curing disease is a must, because while its magic resist, slow is still curable via cure disease. Be wary of Incapacitate as well, which can take an edge off your fighting stats.

Basically the longer the beastlord can have you dealing with their pet, the larger the likelihood they will win. Since the BL can sit safely and make new warders as often as necessary, and their warders will eat your pets level-for-level, for lunch.
