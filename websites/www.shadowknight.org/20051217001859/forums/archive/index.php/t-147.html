Damage bonuses are greater for higher delay, yes. However, for newbie use, personally I would recommend a lower delay 2HS for a couple of reasons. The first reason is that newbies miss a lot, so each time you miss or hit for minimum damage you're digging yourself a hole. Using a lower-delay 2HS will increase your reps and allow you more chances to hit. It will also allow you more chances to increase the skill.

The second reason is that at least until about 30 or so, even "low-end" weapons are still tremendously powerful compared to the sorts of weapons that existed when the game was released. Kunark-era and later weapons have disgustingly-powered ratios compared to what original EQ had. Even a "low-end" 2HS nowadays surpasses the uberest of the uber 2HS "back in the day." Notice there's just not much concern anymore about good ole Karg's Zweihander, once considered by many to be THE 2HS to have.

My suggestions for good 2HS that would be more than adequate for low level use and can be bought on the cheap:

Runic Carver: 18/37. Can be had for under 150pp or effortlessly camped by your mage outside Chardok. Might even look menacing in a gnome's hands.

Mithril 2-Handed Sword: 21/40(?) Built-in haste effect lowers its delay dramatically, giving this weapon the damage bonus of a "slow" 2HS with the speed of a fast 2HS. 500pp or so, varies by server. Downsides, it "looks gimp." Looks just like a rusty or FS, so if you want something that looks impressive, this isn't it.

As for the "dizok thingy" being high end, its a nice weapon, but I tried for almost 3 weeks to sell one and darn near couldn't give it away.

Be aware of the damage caps below level 20. I dunno what level your sk is, but before level 20, there's no point using a weapon with more than 14 damage.

Until level 10, weapon DMG is rounded down to 10. This means that nice 30/40 db rod really works like a 10/40 weapon.

Until level 14, weapon DMG is rounded down to 14. This means that nice 30/40 db rod really works like a 14/40 weapon.

So, if you've gotta have a 2 hander (say you want to work on skill), I'd go with that 20/30 centi spear, use it til 20, and then maybe pick up a DN lance (30 - 32 damage, 36 - 40 delay, worst being 30/40, best being 32/39 or 30/36).

the damage cap caps the damage rating of the weapon, not the actual damage you do when you hit

give a newbie str buffs up to 255 str, group with and cast torrent of hate, have a shammy cast avatar on em, get a ranger to cop em and they'll be hitting for WAY above the caps that some people think are the caps... that's because the damage rating of the weapon is what is capped. levels 1-10, weapon damage ratings are lowered to 10 (yields a 'max hit' of 21 in most cases) ... from 11-14 or 11-19, I forget which, the cap is raised to 14, so you can hit for up to 29 (14x2 +1), but stronger chars can hit over that, especially as they approach the level where the cap comes off, because their weapon skills are high enough to get them over the base
