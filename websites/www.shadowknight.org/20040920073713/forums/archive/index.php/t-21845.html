Catharsis
This is the best advice I can give to casuals to get something out of the game in later levels.

1. Do NOT go through the 20's, 30's, 40's without a guild. The lowbie family guilds is where you make friends. A network of friends is the single most valuable thing for someone with limitted play time.

2. Do diversify. Go to the empty dungeon zones with those friends from your guild when you get a decent block of time. Get experience with your group role, this expands to be your raid role. Get some loot in the process and vital experience in zones that arent outdoor pull kill pull kill snoozer zones where LFG people rotate in and out.

3. Keep your gear up. Do the quest armor in the 30's, whatever, just keep it up. Hunting in the non-traditional exp zones will get you drops and more cash than OT/DL (whatever is popular these days). Do all quests available to you as soon as you're of level to do them. Every level you should be able to say you're geared for your level.

4. Join a guild that is at your level of progression (either develop with your lowbie guild or move on when you outlevel them). Content skipping leads to frustration. There has to be an optional raid guild doing velious/luclin on every server. On TP theres quite litterally more guilds than the server population can support. There's something for everyone. Whatever, find a guild thats optional raiding, prefer dkp to merit because merit is tough on people who dont raid much.

5. Take advantage of the raised levels that trivializes older content. Named in places like NToV and Ssra named offer good gear and are waaaay easy post 60. And it takes very little time to mobilize and set up for these targets with all the new things like PoK stones.

6. Remember that there is no end. Don't look at what the top guild is doing or what their members are wearing. It will contribute to the mentality that somehow you being in that zone or having that gear means you win the game. It doesn't. They're doing the same thing you are ... trying the next available zone that they havent beat.

7. If you absolutely have no time to raid, ldon alot or fight in zones where there are drops. Research this. With all the sites out there theres no reason to not know where good items drop that you can either use or sell.

8. Do not rely on LFG. God damn, I never want to hear someone bitch about this again. Make your own groups. You open up that lfg tool and find some people. You do not need the perfect group. You do not need to go buy buffs. Get the people together fast, move fast and make the most of your time.

Just some thoughts off the top of my head.
