Yguana:

Paineel faction is *incredibly* easy to get for any race/class/religion combo. After a few hours of AOEing my TUNARE worshipping druid was able to hit amiable status (I did that for two reasons: sheer boredom and to help my SK get out of f#&king Paineel because the last two teleporters don't work). On the flip side I confuse the hell out of newbies when I decide to make an appearance.

I recommend gaining a few levels first (like at least get to level 30-45). If you hunt in The Warrens for a few hours one-shotting MOBs you'll be amiable in no time. You could do this before those levels, but you'll have to chase them down to land the killing blow when their hitpoints drop (everything runs). You can also collect teeth and do the molar quest in Paineel to get quicker faction boosts, but I didn't bother with it. I got to dubious in less than an hour and hit amiable rather quickly (I focused on killing the named kobolds in there instead of what I was initially doing... which was just killing everything in sight).

Freeport is also fairly easy faction. There are two ways to get faction... quick and dirty or drawn out but more extensive.

Quick and dirty: This will only work if wolf form still positively affects faction with the FP Militia. Have someone give you wolf form and turn in deathfist slashed belts to Captain Hazran in West Freeport. I think it takes 50-60 belts to get to amiable. This will raise faction with the Freeport Militia and some merchants, but you have to be careful and con everything, as there are a number of different factions in each zone.

Drawn out version: Head to Highhold Pass and slaughter gnolls and/or orcs, amassing stack after stack of orc and/or gnoll scalps. Once you hit apprehensive or higher faction with the Highhold Pass guards you can start turning in scalps to the two different NPCs (the orc one is on the orc side, and the gnoll one on the gnoll side). This will raise factions with a *number* of factions in HHP, Qeynos, and Freeport, without lowering any. That being said, it is also time consuming. Read on...

After you are done factioning in HHP you can go into the keep area and hunt goblins.. they are in your level range I think. You can also turn in goblin ears to a different NPC to get even more boosts to the above factions. You then have the option of going to East Commonlands and slaughtering orcs. This will boost your Knights of Truth (FP paladin guild) faction. You'll then have bank access in Freeport. Killing orcs there will also raise your Guardians of the Vale (halfling warrior guild) faction. However, don't ever expect to be able to safely walk through Rivervale.

As far as Neriak, you may be able to raise Dark Bargainers faction, as I think they start out non-KOS to all races. There is a necro (Lokar To'biath) in Neriak 3rd Gate you can turn red wine into to get positive faction with DB, Dread Guard Inner, and Dread Guard Outer. I don't know of any easy ways to raise the SK/necro factions, and I don't know what starting faction you have with them (I assume KOS).

I hope this helps.

In Freeport, another method of raising faction is the rogue note quests. If you make a level 1 Human Rogue Mule, you can have him go talk to one of the Guild Trainers under the docks, a female dark elf who's name I forget. Originally posted by jastrebi
What I would like to know is what towns would I have the best chance of gaining faction with as an Iksar SK and how would I get good faction with them.
The only town I worked up faction in was Neriak. All you need is two backpacks full of red wine, and someone to invis you in and out. You head to the zone that is furthest in (3rd gate?) and find an NPC named Lothar. He is in a room in a library of some kind.

He will /con indifferent, even to an Iksar SK. Start turning in the wine 16 at a time (or four at a time, people are saying that you can no longer hand in four stacks of four at a time). By the time you empty the second bag, you will be warmly/ally to the merchants and dubious to the inner and outer guards.

You will still be KOS to some factions in Neriak, including the warrior and cleric guild. I think that the guards in 3rd gate are on cleric faction, which is why I'd advise getting an invis on your way out. The warrior guild is in the same zone as the bank, but it is out of the way and not in your path to the bank, and I've never been attacked by them even though I am KOS.

There is one guard near the soul binder who is on warrior faction, but he is low level and should be green to you at 25. If he is green he doesn't attack unless you're sitting down. If he DOES attack, he is easily dispatched and the other guards will not assist him.

However . . .

With there being a PoP portal in Nektulos forest now, as well as in Freeport and Qeynos, I don't think I'd even bother getting Neriak faction. The Qeynos/Freeport portals are out of the way of danger, and thus easy to use for an Iksar SK. The Neriak portal would require you to get past some low level guards. If you don't want that hassle, just wipe out the halfling camps in the zone a few times and you should be dubious/indifferent to the outer guards, and they'll leave you alone.

Once you have PoP, faction concerns become almost zero. Once you have PoP and level 30 (FD/invis), faction concerns become zero. You have access to training, merchants, and banks in the Plane of Knowledge and Echo Caverns. You have access to banks and merchants in Shadow Haven, the bazaar, and Shar Vahl. Heck, with PoP you have easy access to Cabilis, as well.

Faction became much less of a concern with Luclin portals, and pretty much became no concern at all with PoP. The only exception would be as someone mentioned, if you want to do a quest or help a friend with a quest.
