Yes, a lot of this isn't anything new, but I'm going to try to be as thorough as possible. (Warning, very long.)

_________________________________________________


First, an overview of the issues SK's are having.
1) Shadowknight desirability during the PoP era stemmed from their reliable snap aggro. This was an effective counterbalance for warrior superiority in raw tanking. Warriors are now sufficient in this category, and even further ahead in raw tanking, dramatically reducing value of shadowknights, as warriors do the rest of the job notably better.

2) SK's raw tanking power is well behind that of warriors. Disciplines aside, warriors are roughly 15% better tanks than knights. This gap is then widened further by defensive, making warriors the only viable tanks for many encounters, and highly preferable tanks for most raid/named mobs. While there are many things a SK can tank, warriors can almost invariably do the job better. There are many situations where content is doable with a warrior, but not with a knight (or only by adding more to the size of the party).

3) Non-tank classes come very close to SK's in tanking ability. Opponents of knight upgrades often point out what knights can tank. DPS-oriented melee classes, rangers in particular, can fill nearly any knight tanking role. Rangers are very close in raw tanking power to SK's, have equivalent aggro capacity, and even have a distinct advantage in the form of weaponshield. As such, rangers are sufficient in terms of tanking for nearly anything a knight is - they aren't the best, but then again, neither are knights. Additionally, rangers can fulfill tank roles that knights cannot due to the power of weaponshield. That said, non-tank class tanking abilities are largely fine in my opinion. The problem is that the gap needs to be moved in between knights and non-tanks, rather than being between warriors and knights/non-tanks.

4) Shadowknights have effectively lost their pulling role. They are still capable of pulling, but other classes can do it more effectively. Bards, and to a lesser extent, clerics, enchanters, rangers, and druids, can pull much more effectively in many situations using different techniques than SK's. Monks, using exactly the same tactic employed by SK's, can pull more effectively in nearly all situations (especially those encountered on raids). While some shadowknights still pull for their groups/guilds, this is generally because of the skill of the player. Given an equivalent monk character, that same player would do the job more effectively.

5) Shadowknight utility is no longer a valid argument against more real power (if it ever was). Aside from the fact that utility is a poor substitute for raw power in most situations, SK's utility has been largely trivialized by item effects and endurance based skills.

_________________________________________________


An often proposed fix for knights is a defensive disc. Some among the knight community, along with a number of warriors, oppose this change, especially pre-GoD. Reasons sited generally revolve around the following.

1) Warriors only have one role (tanking), thus it's "fair" that they're significantly better at that job than knights.
Knights have always shared that same job. It is the core function of the knight classes. The difference is that they have traditionally been balanced in that role by having an advantage over warriors in one area (aggro) to counterbalance their deficiency in another (inferior raw tanking and the lack of disciplines). Warriors have made significant gains in the traditionally knight-dominated area, thus to maintain the balance, knights need to make gains in the other.

2) A defensive discipline for knights would make warriors obsolete as raid MT's.
On account of the fact that warriors have moved in on the conventional knight strengths of offtanking and small group encounters, it would not be unfair for knights to have some functionality in the established warrior role of raid main tanking. That said, giving knights a defensive discipline would still leave warriors as the preferred raid MT's. Knights lack such invaluable tools as furious and AE taunt, are roughly 15% behind in raw tanking, and are actually worse in aggro over time. Furious and AE taunt are hugely important for tank switches - AE taunt makes the point at which aggro is transferred predictable, and furious allows for a lot of added stability. Lacking these tools, the only spot in the tanking order knights could compete for would be the first spot. Warriors would still reign supreme for the latter spots due to the ease of tank switching, and being better overall even for the first due to better overall tanking and the value of furious in comparison to the slight knight initial aggro advantage.

3) Knights already tank fine pre-GoD outside of raid main tanking.
Sure they do for the most part; they tank exactly as well as they did when they were content, during PoP. The problem is that warriors now tank significantly better, making knights' sufficient tanking abilities obsolete. That said, there are some things that knights are insufficient for even pre-GoD and outside of the raid MT role. To kill the Daiku Overseer with a single group, a warrior is effectively required. For many bazaar/LDoN-geared groups (which are not out of place progression wise) in BoT, the wing-bosses are nearly impossible with knight tanks, especially compared with defensive warriors.

The areas in which knight tanking is "fine" already would not be significantly affected by a defensive discipline. Defensive would have little bearing on XP mobs, where knights are fine and to which warriors often point to try to say that knights are balanced. Defensive would have a limited effect on offtanking in pre-GoD content, as offtanking often requires tanking for much longer than the duration of a discipline. It would, however, have the positive effect of bringing knights into the same league of warriors in areas where defensive is relevant. If you look at where the balance problems exist, they are where defensive is applicable; knights aren't in bad shape where it is of minimal use.

4) Knight tanking inferiority is justified by their spellbooks/utility.
Most knight spells add minimal raw power. Many are nearly useless, and many are merely utility based. Utility does not make a class - it is nice to have, but it is no substitute for real power. Furthermore, much of that utility is available to other classes through item effects and endurance based skills. Many of those spells which do add real power are greatly overshadowed by the outright superiority of warriors in that to which they contribute. Others, such as feign death and healing, yield secondary roles, but still leave knights lacking as those roles are as dominated by other classes as tanking is by warriors.

Shadowknight spell DPS is not sufficient to be a valid balance issue with regard to tanking. Warriors are vastly superior DPS while tanking. When not tanking, they are better at the high end and at least comparable at the low end, particularly over longer periods of time.

5) Giving knights defensive would trivialize many old encounters.
Quite simply, it wouldn't. It would not raise the available tanking power, it would simply distribute it a bit more evenly among the three tank classes. Any content that would be "trivialized" by giving knights defensive can already be trivialized (and to a greater degree) by replacing that knight with a warrior.

_________________________________________________


Advantages of a defensive discipline as a fix:

1) First and foremost - scalability.
Defensive scales extremely well to content. Lifetaps, leechcurse, runes, etc. do not.

2) Would not dramatically impact the areas of the game where knights are fairly well balanced with warriors.
Knights are generally in reasonable shape in comparison to warriors where defensive is not a factor. Where defensive is applicable, serious issues are evident, however.

3) Would set knights apart from non-tank classes to some degree.
Non-tanks would retain the advantage of melee immunity disciplines for very short term tanking tasks. Knights would open the gap with non-tanks and close the gap (without overcoming it) with warriors on longer encounters, and remain largely uneffected in XP grinds and long-term offtanking roles.

_________________________________________________


Other things of note:

1) This is not a GoD problem. This is a class balance problem, which was highlighted by GoD.
Knight-warrior balance became an issue when the aggro gap was closed. A logical way to re-establish the balance would be to close the tanking gap. Hard-hitting GoD mobs simply serve to make this more evident.

2) There are supposed to be three tank classes: warriors, paladins, and shadowknights.
Right now, warriors are far and away the best tanks. Knights are situational seconds, with DPS-oriented melees filling the #2 spot in others thanks to their melee invulnerability disciplines.

So I've been trying to decide exactly what it is that will bring knights back up to par... not really anything too earthshattering, but I've not yet seen it really spelled out.


The best I've come up with is a set of two disciplines (separate timers from eachother and unholy aura):

One similar to weaponshield/furious/etc. - a short duration melee immunity for "oh shit" situations, fight stabalization, etc.
Parry or a shield block would be ideal - giving us ripost would be encroaching on warriors, especially considering its burst damage is somewhat analagous to HT.
Duration of 2-4 ticks.
Reuse upwards of 30mins.
Endurance based, vendor purchased tome.

A "mini-defensive" - notably less effective than defensive, but enough to put knights well beyond anything else; perhaps in the order of 25-35% off the DI.
Much shorter duration than defensive - somewhere around 60-72seconds.
Short reuse, in the area of 4.5 minutes.
Endurance based, vendor purchased tome.
This would be of minimal use as a raid MT, as it's simply too short, and warriors have comfortably better in terms of raw tanking. Add tanking doesn't generally require much more than a minute, however, and its short reuse would make it ideal for offtanking - it would be readily available as more adds came in. It would also be sufficient in duration for 1-group named.
At 72 second duration and 4.5 minute reuse knights would spend less total time under discipline than warriors, assuming both classes were using the disc every time it popped (warriors can spend 1min/3.5 disced, knights would have 1min/3.75).


Play with the numbers if needed, I havn't done alot of extensive math or anything on them at this point - I may at some point.


These combined would still leave us well shy of warriors overall. They would, however, put us clearly above DPS classes in virtually all tanking situations. Their specifics would be more tailored to the traditional roles of knights than would other options, implimentation would be fairly simple, they scale marvelously, etc.

Content trivialization would not be a problem, as anything that would stand to be "trivialized" by knights tanking with the new abilities would already have been trivialized to a much greater extent by warriors.

Do NOT put an aggro or casting time penalty on it. (Casting time penalty would basically be an aggro penalty anyway.) We need our aggro to perform our offtanking roles. Requiring a shield is fine; warriors are required to have two, sometimes 3 weapon sets for different situations - it's perfectly fair that we need different weapon sets as well.
