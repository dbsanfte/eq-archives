Kinda like driving across country to your folks house for the holidays, you enter town and think "Jesus, I am finally here", then you find out they moved to Canada.

Stats don't make the game. Yeah, they are important, but the definition of a "game" suggests that it should be something you have fun with. Minta
The 'end game', I presume... is the point where you can get planar armor, or nowadays Velious armor to make up for things.

I also disagree that gear will magically max out everyone's stats, but ... so what? What do stats really do for you? Much of who you are as an SK is defined by your class. Even if your stats are not maxed out, it is your damage table and your damage mitigation table as an SK that accounts for a lot of your effectiveness in a group. Give a wizard 255 strength and see how much good he does meleeing.

There are however things to worry about. If your melee stats are below 75, then you take negatives due to that attribute. Stamina below 75 is especially bad and, as I understand it, stamina below 100 will cause you to take penalties when your 'stamina bar' runs out. The stamina bar being that yellow one between health and mana in the top right of the screen. If you're swinging something like a Black Acrylia Halberd around, that goes down really quickly. With stamina over 100, even when the yellow bar hits the bottom, you should be okay.

As for Int? Unless you're doing tradeskills that shouldn't matter too much. Mana regenerates quite quickly compared to health, and other than for grabbing aggro or fear kiting, it doesn't seem like a big deal to me. If you're desperate, get clarity or sit down for a minute or something.

As for strength, other than being able to carry stuff (a weight reduction pack will help much more than a stat boost)... strength seems to add 1 ATK per strength point or something like that. Grim Aura adds 10 ATK and increasing your skill with a weapon adds much more.... unless you're talking about 100 points of strength, it doesn't seem like a big issue. Getting a faster weapon or a better damage ratio will do more than maxing out your strength.

As always, though... this is just my opinion. I'm probably wrong.
