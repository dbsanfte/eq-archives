Sylth
I've read alot of stuff about our pet, some people love him, some people hate him. Most recently, and especially with the flood of highly questionable pet centric AA's in GoD, there has been a growing desire among many in the community for the first sk-specific pet that is not a 20-level defunct necromancer hand-me-down.

I agree with this. However, I also see the problems and concerns the developers may have about granting us a level-appropraite pet.

As it stands, I see three main problems with our pets today:
1. They are too low level to land their procs on anything we would be fighting.
2. They disintegrate almost instantly if hit/riposted/damage-shielded/ae'd.
3. Generally because of 1&2 above, our pets are just not worth the expenditure of AA's to enhance them.

There are several ways the above problems could be addressed, and I'm not going into them, however, I have a suggestion that I've not seen presented before.

Rather than giving us just a higher level version of the existing pet, then tweaking down his damage, I suggest the following:

A new SK-only level 65 pet spell based (loosely) on the new necro swarm pet.

Summon Skeletal Archer.
Class: Sk
Level: 65
Mana: 750
Component: 1 Bonechip + 1 peridot (ala higher end necro pets)
Effect:
Summons a skeletal archer to aid the shadow knight in combat. The pet is perma-rooted in place, but can be summoned to the SK's side using Summon Companion. Rather than a lifetap proc, the Skeletal Archer pet will buff himself with Grim Aura and/or a version of Call of Darkness with the DoT aspect removed.
Pet Level would 60, same as 61+ Necro pet spells.
Pet would have 2500hp. (200 more than current pet, I believe)
Pet would hit for 150 Max. (Before the screams, remember archer pet, no dual wield, no double attack, no bash or kick).


The above pet, in my opinion, would not only solve virtually all issues we have with our current pets, but would do so in a creative way that would give us more options in gameplay. The above solution wouldn't just give us a better pet in all situations, but would force us to choose the best pet for a given situation. Certainly in the situation I've presented above, our current pet would be best suited for many situations, LDoN for example, or any else where your moving around alot.
However, the archer pet would give us a viable pet in raid and other situations. Out of melee range, and with the ability to position him strategically with summon companion to avoid AE's.

Additionally, while the archer's attack speed could be tuned to provide similar or only slightly higher DPS than our current pet, the subconscious satisfaction of seeing Jobaber crit for 270 instead of critting for 52 might tempt many more people into actually purchasing some currently worthless...I mean undervalued...AA's 8p

+++The explanation.

Strewn across several different threads are ideas to somehow incorporate our pet into a DI-mitigation ability we may get, along with the pet being a solution to our severe lack of dps compared to the warrior class. My proposed idea would incorporate both, and be a "nice" way to give us (if you do, please, please, do) a DI-mitigation ability and a dps upgrade.

I have been looking over the main arguements for why the pet should not be involved with any fixes we (hopefully) receive, and have taken these into deep consideration while mulling over this idea, so no, I'm not completely ignorant.

First off, as a disclaimer, I would like to say that I realize the sk class is NOT a pet class, and nor do I want us to be a pet class, but if we are going to have one, lets make it a bit more interesting?

+++Working this in with the current pet.

This ability should not require the sk's original pet to be "active" at time of use, but should not "kill" the pet when used, either. I believe that, either, A) The summoned pet should pop up just as an additional pet, along with the original active pet, or B) replace the current pet while active, then upon the minion despawning, original pet comes back. I believe having the minion pop up as an additional pet is much more plausible, and easier to program; I do not see how this would be unbalancing (having two pets for a short period of time).

+++The dps portion.

A 120 second reuse ability that summons a "minion of darkness" to our side that contributes to some actual damage. There are a few ways I see that could this be done, and the two most A) realistic, and B) useful, are:

+Idea A: Kamikaze pet. Minion is summoned, and "throws" itself at the intended target, doing an amount of damage proportional to the ShadowKnight's level.

Let's say that at level 65, it does 2,000 damage. Used exactly as it popped, every single time (the ability), this would provide an additional effective 16.66 points of dps. I don't believe that would be overpowering, and would bring us up more where we need to be with our dps.

+Idea B: Plain dps. Minion is summoned, and unleashes a death-like fury upon the target proportional to the level of the ShadowKnight using the ability.

Let's say that at level 65, it stays alive for 10 seconds. The pet does 200dps. This would contribute an effective 16.66 extra points of dps to the ShadowKnight using this skill if he could use it every single time it refreshed.

Now, I realize, that the animation for the pet "throwing" itself at the target would have to be a new one, and would require much more work than I assume you are willing to put in to this. However, I doubt any of the ShadowKnights would mind if it actually just spawned, took one slash at the target, and died with some nifty red particles flying out of it (perhaps Asystole's particle graphics) upon activation.

+++The mitigation portion.

Please note, that I think this could be used in conjunction with the dps portion (the timers on the dps portion would have to be reworked with the timers on the mitigation portion), or without it. Personally, I think it would be more useful to be used without it, on a seperate ability. Having these two on the same timer would be acceptable. That means we would have to choose between having the mitigation ability on the ready, or using the dps.

Activate ability, said minion pops up next to you, and a certain % of the enemies damage is directed to the minion. Now, the longevity of the minion receiving said diverted damage can be determined two ways. A) A defined hp pool for the pet. Personally, i think this would be bad, simply for the sheer lack of scalability. B) An indefinite HP pool, but a timer-limited lifespan.

Because I think idea A is not a particularly good one, I'm going to focus my arguement on idea B.

Using Slein Jinn's times for his proposed "knight-defensive" I think it would be fair if the pet lasted for 72 seconds, and refreshed every 270 seconds (4 and a half minutes), and mitigated 35% of the DI and provided 15% SpellShield (the mitigation and spellshield percentages are not of Sleinn Jinn's).

+++The disclaimer and other additions.

First off, as a disclaimer, I would like to say that I realize the sk class is NOT a pet class, and nor do I want us to be a pet class, and this will not make us a pet class any more so than we already are. Also, I do, firmly, believe that our tanking needs to be fixed BEFORE all else - including dps. This does not mean our dps can not be fixed also. And lastly, all numbers are easily tweaked, and nothing is final to me, they are simple suggestions, as is everything here, from a humble SK. Lastly, this is NOT meant as a fix for the paladin - sk imbalance, but as a fix for the knight - warrior imbalance.

Pet graphic: This could be something so simple as the exact same model for the summoned pet from "Opalescent two-handed Sword". Our elemental pet weapon.

Distributing this to Paladins as a fix to their tanking abilities in concern with warriors:

This can be achieved quite easily, with some slight variations of my proposed idea(s). Their "pet" could be a summoned flaming sword or some such, and effectively do the same thing with mitigation. And perhaps instead of my proposed dps idea - they could have some type of pet that "kamikaze's" its life (proportional to the Paladin's level) to the rest of the group in a nice, new, group heal ability. However, I make NO concrete idea for the Paladin's abilities in this matter, and speak my idea only in regards to the SK class, as I have no place regarding paladins. The above ideas are nothing more than suggestions in regards to paladins.

Problems:
+Parts of this could be tricky to code, and probably not worth doing compared to a simple slap-down discipline (in developer's eyes).

+The summoned pet's "push" or knockback abilities on raids.

+The summoned pet being large, obstrusive, a distraction, etc. etc.

Benefits:
+This would effectively solve BOTH our tanking concerns, and our DPS woes.

+It would give us something that isn't exactly a copy-paste of warrior abilities.

+It would be a CREATIVE solution, not just a 5-second solution someone thought of using 3 of their braincells.

+It would cause less (I believe) outrage from other class communities.

+It would be easily distributed to both the knight classes.

+It would put a (much needed) gap between Knight's and the other non-tanking classes that seem to now be so good at it.
