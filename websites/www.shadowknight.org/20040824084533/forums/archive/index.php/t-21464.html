The Army told members of Congress on Thursday that it will revoke the enlistment orders of any veteran who signed up based on mistaken information from recruiters.

"We will revoke the order if the soldier requests," an Army colonel said in a handwritten fax to U.S. Rep. Peter DeFazio's office.

DeFazio, a Springfield Democrat, asked that Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld rescind all enlistments made under the mistaken order. In the meantime, veterans need to request to nullify their enlistment.

The about-face follows several weeks of confusion involving the Individual Ready Reserve, a group of about 118,000 soldiers who had returned to civilian life after a stint in the Army but still had time left on their commitment.

After the Army said it would begin screening these soldiers for possible involuntary assignment to an Army Reserve unit, recruiters began contacting soldiers and telling them they had to choose between new assignments in the Army Reserve or National Guard by midnight Monday or be involuntarily assigned to an active duty unit. Army officials this week said that was a mistake.

It's estimated that more than 1,000 soldiers re-enlisted nationwide based on the false information, including more than 100 in Oregon.

Soldiers who want their enlistment revoked may call the regional office of the Army Reserve at (800) 347-2734, Ext. 1. Or they may call DeFazio's office at (800) 944-9603; staff members will help expedite requests, DeFazio spokeswoman Kristie Greco
