Your DE can't Slam with 2 handers equipped, and Bash is useful not only for interrupting casters but also adds extra aggro which is important at low levels now you can't buy KEI so mana's not limitless anymore.

I'd suggest getting a decent one hander and shield, but spending some time each level getting your 2Hs up with the polearm because you'll probably want to use 2Hs when you get high level (especially when you get the AA to let you bash while wielding 2 handers).

If you want a decent 2Hs now, maybe a Reaver or something like that with a decent ratio that's not level restricted. For later on, there's a thread in the equipment forum on this website that lists all the top weapons like WMBS, Windblade and so on.

Don't worry too much about it though; at higher levels your melee damage contribution is relatively small, and the wizard in your group will care less whether you're wielding a WMBS or a fishing pole. This is because he expects you to spend all your time spamming spell taunts on the mob to stop it ripping him to shreds while he chains his multi-K crit nukes on it.

I hope this helps - and happy to hear you're having fun with your SK :)

Iksar beserker club is a very fast club, 7/18. I think SK's can use it; I used it on my druid and shaman and monk and I think on my warrior too...forget if i did on my SK.

It's 7/18 and procs a very good buff that ups your ATK and defense and offense. Kind of an escape hatch, as far as boosting your defense when you're getting slaughtered. Cheap too.

It's good to have at least one very fast 1-hand weapon. Use it as soon as you ding until your skill in that weapon is maxed. This will help you level your offensive skill, not just skill in that weapon. Now, move on to your other weapons you need to work up your skill on. With offense raise via starting each level with your fast weapon, you will hit for higher amounts and more often with your other weapons. Now that, say, two-hand blunt thingy you have terrible skill in will at least have a chance to do something decent, since your offense has gone up a bit. Offense being maxed can make a very big difference every level in terms of your damage output, especially with weapons you stink at.

Find something very fast, and start each level with that. Don't stop until you've maxed that weapon for that level(five points per level). That way you will always have one reliable, fast weapon skill to use and, more importantly, always keep offense high, which applies to every single weapon you use. Emphasizing two-hand weapons can sometimes get you very far behind in offense.

Also...be sure you do a fair amount of MT'ing or even soloing, whatever keeps you getting hit. Each point in defense equals an increase in AC. For shamans, for instance(I don't know if SK's get a bigger bonus per point), each increase in defense is worth 2.5 AC. That means you can get 12.5 more AC per level if you max defense every level. Think of what adding 12.5 AC on one piece of armor you have would mean -- it could make a pretty junky piece look darn good, or a very good piece outstanding. Nothing to sneeze at, and it's FREE. So be aware that if you're always second banana in terms of being beat on, you'll might pay a price if your defense falls behind. And you cannot get defense higher unless you get hit -- no buying it at trainer, etc, like you can with weapons skills.
