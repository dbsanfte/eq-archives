Marruk
Vorrhees,

As was already mentioned in this thread several times, AGI increases your AC (although not by a very big amount once you get it past 75).

Higher AC = you get hit less.

So yes, maxxing your AGI will allow you to tank better. However, if it comes down to a choice between an AGI buff and a raw AC buff, chances are the AC buff will make a bigger difference than the AGI buff.
There is no evidence that AGI does anything other than increase your AC and increase you chance of skill-ups.
So yeah, max your defensive aa's and have a shaman cast Wrulans on you, and you won't get hit as much as you do without Wrulans. Just like you won't get hit as much with Virtue on as you do without.
I have seen lots of people who claim that AGI increases dodge and parry rates, or other claims along those lines. I've never seen anyone be able to show any evidence of that through parsing, however.
Just because I've never seen parsing prove it doesn't mean it isn't true, but the complete lack of evidence is good enough for me to click off Wrulan for an extra buff slot on every raid.
