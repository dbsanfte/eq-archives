Oberyn
Personally, I did LR first, and am currently working on polishing off ID.

I wish I had done it in the other order.

I notice less spell interrupts with LR against the mobs I solo for XP during the week. Definite difference there when I tank them, which is what I wanted.

When I group and tank in GoD sewers, which is about the top end stuff I can manage to tank (over 1K hits, unslowables) I wish I had done the mitigation discipline first, to smooth out the damage spikes somewhat. Makes it easier for the healer to predict and see whats going on, which makes me live longer, in general:P

I didn't think I'd be doing as much in GoD as I am, and playtime has mainly been limited to soloing and grouping on weekends the last while, so I did LR, thinking about the soloing.

Now, tanking the harder stuff, I should have done ID first.

Your mileage may vary, but think about what it is you do in game and choose accordingly.

And get both:)

Dakkin
LR is marginally better than ID, because ID can only work on the 70% or so of attacks that aren't already completely avoided with LR5. i.e. it's 30% less effective than LR.

Having said which, although overall improvement from ALL tanking AAs stacked is huge, LR5 is only a 5% increase in avoidance and ID5 a 5% increase in mitigation in the first place so neither will produce a perceptible improvement and you'd need a parser to tell the difference.

But if you're looking for maximum theoretical efficiency, go for LR first.
