Becoming A Ranger

Forward

This guide is geared towards players who have a good working comprehension of EverQuest game fundamentals but wish to achieve additional information on classes they might want to play. If you are a new player in EverQuest, then I suggest reading the EverQuest Manual and looking at our new player section before continuing.

If you there are any errors or comments for this guide, please send them to: news@eqanswers.com

Overview

Ho there friend! So ye have decided to follow the path of the Ranger, well let us sit awhile and speak on the nature of the wanderer. Rangers are the defenders of Tunare and her children. They protect the woods from outside harm as well as keep things running properly inside the woods. Bane of the lumberjacks, Hero of the faeries, you can expect much trial and tribute as a ranger.

Rangers are the true masters of the wild. Their skills range from exceeding accuracy with bow and arrow, blinding agility and ferocious strength in combat, intimate knowledge of the land and the creatures that inhabit it, and the ability to summon forth the great powers of Tunare and the magic of the land.

These pathfinders will scour the world, protecting the land from evil incarnate. Similar to their woodland brothers, the druids, rangers are capable of staying out on their own for long periods of time and with an array of buffing and healing spells can hold their own in combat. 

Rangers, however strong in combat, work much more effectively in a group. Their ability to track down elusive monsters and bring them back to fight is uncanny, and many groups would prefer the rangers scouting and pulling to a warriors super-human fighting ability. However, rangers are no match for warriors when it comes to dealing out damage, nor can they sustain even a fragment of what a warriors hardened body can endure and, though their spellcasting abilities are very effective, they must divide their studies between combat and casting so their minds cannot control near the amount of mana that a druid or mage can wield.

Myths and Controversy

Tracking is useless as it increases in skill.
After a bit of research, I noticed numerous rangers complaining about tracking becoming less effective as the skill increases. As the range of tracking at higher levels comes close to all of West Karana, some rangers are finding it harder to pick out those rare spawns and choice kill creatures from the garbage spawns . This is how tracking is designed. If you are standing on a well-traveled road you don't have much chance of picking out a wagon with a 1/2 inch wheel width. Traffic can destroy prints. Such is the way in EQ. If a tribe of kobolds comes along and stamps out the undead cyclopes’ paths, there is nothing you can do about it. Study the region you are in. Learn where your prey eats, sleeps, and hunts. Study the creatures’ surroundings. Does the hill giant have a camp of brigands willing to help him in a fight? Is there a private place he likes to walk and pick flowers? When the moment presents itself, slay your foe. Be prepared.

Ranger spells aren't very useful.
It is sometimes argued that rangers’ spell abilities are useless and incomparable to their druid brothers who share the spell table. Rangers don't get as much mana. They don't get the better spells at lower levels. They don't even get SoW until 39, but rangers have never and never will be considered casters. Ranger spells are not powerful, they don't work well for buffs, they don't heal as much as needed, and they don't do enough damage to be worthwhile. But they are exceedingly effective when added to a ranger's already excellent skill array. While druids may be able to SoW themselves and snare pull a creature with skill and speed, it also reduces their med-time and causes the group to wait longer after the battle. A ranger, however, can sneak in, snare pull the creature, and taunt it off the group while the druid meds up and nukes the creature to eternity. Harmony-pulling is an art that only true rangers can master but when properly done is one of the top pulling methods in game. Ranger spells are only as good as the ranger who is casting them.

Rangers don't get as good armor as warriors, paladins, or shadowknights.
It is true that ranger gear does not give as much AC as warriors, paladins, and shadowknights receive. Rangers have less HPs and less AC than any other hybrid class. But they make up for these flaws with better weapons and better spells. Rangers are giving access to an arsenal that would make paladins and shadowknights choke. And they get to pick two! So the next time you are watching a bucket head get pounded by a mob and not feel a thing, make sure you notice you hit 4 times as much as him.

(The BIG one) No defense after 50.
Well, it's true. Rangers have been up in arms about getting "pounded on by green *@#$%" and getting made fun of by other classes for their lack of shielding. But Kunark has not overlooked the poor ranger. I might suggest picking up a Green-Jade Broadsword and an Ornate Rune Blade and seeing who still makes fun of you. We are still the best damn fighters in the game, and no one deals out the damage like we can. So what if we have to step back every once and awhile and let the meat-shields have their turn. Appreciate the gifts you have been given.

Character Creation

So you are ready to create you first ranger. Well there are some key elements you should take notice of that are vital to this process. Race, location, and the all important stats. So before you go and create a wood elf ranger and drop all your points in agility, read this section.

Race

A large factor in creating your character is deciding what race to play. This is not only a personal choice depending on how you want to role-play, but it will also affect how you play. Each race has diverse starting attributes, location, and disadvantages over others. It is suggested you take a hard look at what one race offers over another, and most significantly if you think you will have fun with it.

Wood Elf
Wood elves are the protectors of the Forests of Faydark. If you should elect to be a ranger, you will join the elite ranks of Faydark's Champions . The leader of the illustrious band is Maesyn Trueshot of the Trueshot clan. He is a wise and honorable guildmaster and can train you well in the skills of a ranger.

Wood Elves begin in the tree-top city of Kelethin. It is a dangerous city, and many fledgling elves have fallen to their demise from the great platforms. But the height of the city also grants a decent amount of protection to the Wood elf community, using special lifts to access their city from the ground. Any type of invasion could be easily thwarted by simply piling the people onto the platforms and shutting down the lifts.

The great forest of Faydark provides ample game for the new ranger from the mischievous pixies to the foul orc stronghold of Crushbone. A wood elf ranger will have no problems finding quarry in the woods.

Humans
Humans, in their pursuit of knowledge and happiness, have integrated themselves into almost every facet of Norrathian life, including the arts of the ranger. Being a human ranger has its advantages and its flaws. Humans with their curiosity and natural learning ability can quickly master the skills taught to them and gain levels with ease. Humans are also much hardier and stronger than wood elves or half-elves. But humans are also as blind as barbarians when night falls and will have a hard time navigating the woods after nightfall.

The human ranger will begin in Surefall Glade, a most beautiful grove created by some of the most renowned rangers and druids in Norrath's history. Surefall Glade is a training ground for rangers and their druid brothers. Here they can learn the basic skills they need to begin their careers and may also pick up a thing or two from the great Trueshot.

Just outside Surefall Glade are the great hills of Qeynos. Here a new ranger may test his skills in combat against a variety of animals, villains, and even some darker beings like the Undead! As is the case with any surrounding, know your enemy. Spend some time studying the hills before you venture out too far from the safety of the guards.

Half-elves
Half-elves are a combination of the wood-elves and humans. They have some of the advantages of the wood elf like infravision and cat-like agility. They also have some human traits like strong, corded muscles. Yet they also share the disadvantages of both classes, getting neither the full effect of a wood elves infravison, nor the learning capacity of a human.

A half-elf ranger may begin in Kelethin or Surefall Glade. They may take advantage of the same training and experiences as a human might. All the same rules apply. The hills of Qeynos are filled with evil, vile creatures just waiting for a lowly being to wander too near their den.

Attributes

One of the most significant parts of character generation is choosing where to put your bonus points. This will let you further modify your character’s role to be more helpful in diverse situations. A Warrior might add more to his Strength, so he can cut deeper into his quarry. While a Necromancer would be much more interested in increasing his magical powers and spreading the evil deeds with Intelligence. A thing to keep in mind, you can even further increase your attributes with magical items found inside the game, so incase you make an oversight, it is not very hard to correct.

Strength (Excellent for Rangers)

Influences your maximum and average damage ratios, the more strength you have the more likely you will hit harder. Additional strength will let you learn your offensive skills faster and let you carry more weight.

Stamina (Valuable for Rangers)

Affects how many total hit points you have and how long you will be able to hold your breath under water.

Agility (Prime Requisite for Rangers)

Increases your percentage in how difficult it is to hit you, as well as how much damage you take when you are hit. Also affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills.

Note: This can be considered one of the most important attributes for rangers, more important than strength. Agility directly affects AC and indirectly affects dodge, parry, and riposte.

Dexterity (Useful for Rangers)

Influences how often your weapon proc (spell) will engage, and how quickly you learn weapon skills. Will also increase how much damage you hit with bows, and how fast you learn rogue skills.

Wisdom (Worthwhile for Rangers)

Directly affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, including rangers. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than intelligence.

Intelligence (Useless for Rangers)

Directly affects the amount of mana of magi classes, including bards and shadow knights. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills.

Charisma (Ineffective for Rangers)

Affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you. Also effects saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular).

Combat

Much of your time spent inside EverQuest will be involving combat situations. To be prepared for combat, one must know the basics of it and EverQuest itself, which we won’t describe here for obvious reasons. What we will divulge is common tactics used to take down foes more hastily and safer.

Solo

Soloing is an art that has only been perfected by some of the greatest players. Some of them are rangers.

To solo as a ranger, you must have your 1st circle of spells. Using snare and archery a ranger can effectively damage a creature without taking any damage himself. After thoroughly weakening a creature, you may proceed to melee the creature to death. Make sure you have an excessive amount of arrows and full mana for re-snaring and nuking at higher levels. Also make sure you are not pulling the creature through a heavily infested area. If correctly "pulled" off, this type of soloing can be very effective for a ranger.

Group

Although a ranger does well at soloing with an array of spells and ranged weapons, they excel in group combat much more. A ranger is one of the most effective taunter in the game, combining snares, taunting, and blinding speed and destruction in combat. Rangers are not tanks, nor should they be. Paladins and warriors have better armor and HP increases. Rangers are fast, strong, and very aggressive. A group with a ranger need not worry about the casters being swarmed when the spells start flying. A ranger can easily pull off an uber taunt before most casters even take a hit. But don't keep the creature on you too long, let the tanks do the tanking.

Tracking as a pulling tool.

A ranger's track is one of his most useful tools. When hunting in a large area like the desert or the plains, a ranger can track down prey and bring it back to the group for easy pickings. But a ranger must learn how to use track. When the tracking box appears, it will show all the monsters in the zone in order of spawn. This may not be very effective when first entering a zone as creatures in the very depths may spawn 1st and throw off your tracking.

Tracking is also range-based which means as you move about a dungeon, you may notice the entire list changing due to new creatures in the immediate area. This is helpful when you know where a creature is and want to check on it from a distance.

But the most important use of tracking is actually when your group has already broken a camp and is pulling to a safe point. Since tracking is spawn-time based, a ranger can tell the group exactly what spawns and when. Perhaps the group is not finished with its first pull and another creature has spawned in the immediate area. Tracking allows the ranger to see past walls and floors and inform the group of what is coming. It can also be used when the camp is broken and a puller is not being used. Tracking allows a ranger to check on the spawns without ever standing up. Many groups will accept a ranger for the sole purpose of having someone to track for them.

Snaring

The 9th level ranger spell Snare will be the single most used spell a ranger will ever have, whether you are kiting an orc across GFay or pinning down Butler Syncall in Castle Mistmoore. Snare has two effects. One, it cuts a creature’s movement rate in half. This means when a creature is running you down, it will look more like walking at you with bad intentions. It is also a highly effective taunting tool, creatures don’t like to be stuck in one place. A snared creature does not run from combat. Once it reaches its "wimpy" level (the point at which it attempts to run), it will simply cease attacking and look in the direction it wants to run. Meanwhile your fellow group members may peacefully hack it to pieces while it stands there.

Many players say that Root is more effective because it stops the mob entirely rather than slowing it down. This is somewhat true. Root binds a creature to the spot it is standing on, and as long as the spell is in effect the creature cannot move. But a rooted creature will fight to the death which means more HP loss to your team. Root also has an incredibly short duration and the creature will be at full movement when it wears off. So when you hit level 9, put Snare on a hot key because you will be using it ‘til your facing down Veeshan!

Harmony

Harmony is a lvl 22 ranger spell which does an area effect lull from the targeted creature. By using Harmony on a small crowd of monsters, a ranger may run in, attack one, and pull it safely to the group without any of the other monsters becoming aggressive. However, harmony is not a very durable spell. If you are good you may have up to 5 seconds to pull your intended target away. After that you will end up with more than you bargained for.

A common problem with harmony pulling is pathing. If a creature naturally paths a different way than the way you are pulling it, you will lose the harmony. For example, you cast harmony on a lizardman and kick his friend. His friend does not immediately come towards you but runs towards the wall then at you. When the creature runs towards the wall it will pass by its friend and cancel out the harmony and now you have two.

This is not as big a problem as it seems. A good way to learn a creature’s path is to cast a DD spell on it from a distance. Then watch. Does it come straight for you, or does it make a little side trip. If it comes right at you, harmony will work fine to pull it. If it moves one way or another, try pulling it from one of those directions. If you can't pull the creature from the left, try the right. All creatures have a path which can be harmony pulled to--you just have to find it.              

Spell Information

Here is a small taste of the spells you will acquire in your training.

First Rank (Level 9)

Snare- Cuts targets movement rate in half. Most effective ranger spell in game.

Flame Lick-(requires Fire Beetle Eye) AC debuff, DoT your first offensive spell.

Second Rank (Levels 15)

Burst of Fire- 17pt Fire-based Direct Damage Spell. A rangers first nuke and pulling spell.

Feet like Cat- Agility buff . Ups AC and defense abilities.

Third Rank ( Level 22)

Skin like Rock- AC and HP skin buff. Very nice for soloing but ineffective in groups.

Harmony- Area effect Lull. Outdoor use only.

Fourth Rank( Level 30)

Stinging Swarm- 120pt DoT spell. Useful in long , drawn out combat.

Strength of Earth- Strength Buff. Increases ATK power and weight allowed.

Fifth Rank (Level 39)

Spirit of Wolf- Doubles movement rate. Great for traveling.

Careless Lightning- Area Effect DD spell. Good for train stopping.

Sixth rank ( level 49)

Call of Flame- Ranger quest spell. 250 pt DD spell. Fire-based.

Wolf Form- turns yourself into a wolf and increases speed and ATK.

Conclusion

You have trained hard. Your mind, body, and spirit are united in perfect harmony. Your skills are honed; your morals straight. You have a path laid before you--a life of ritual, passion, and logic. You will fight many battles--some you will win, some you will lose. The fight is long and in the end, it’s only with yourself. So focus your mind, harden your body, and lace up your boots and go dish out some Tunarian justice!
