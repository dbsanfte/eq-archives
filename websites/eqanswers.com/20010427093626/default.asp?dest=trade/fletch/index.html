Mastering Fletching

Fletching is a skill that takes time, money and most of all patience. If you are interested in becoming a master Fletcher I would suggest being a Ranger, Paladin, Warrior, Rogue or Shadowknight else the skill isn't much use unless for profit. The skill you will find is much more helpful then just creating profit for your character, if he/she is one of these four classes. The benefits of having a bow and the correct skill in archery are tremendous, just due to the fact you can make your own bows and arrows.

Making the items

In order to start your career to becoming a successful fletcher one must have the essential components. However, even before hand you must at least put 1 point into your fletching skill, which can be learned from any of your guild masters. Fletching is not the hardest skill to learn in levels so I would suggest putting only one skill point in and then working with cheap arrow making.

The chart at the end of this page details the arrows you should make to increase your skill level. Using this chart at a given skill level provides best chance of success and the lowest cost.

Arrows

This is the cheapest and fastest way to gain the skills you need to obtain the title of a Master Fletcher or beyond. It is strongly suggested that you make arrows till a 150-skill level. Creating bows before that level is futile due to the high cost involved and low success rate.

The basic arrow is created from four components. The point, shaft, fletch and nock. Each different part of the arrow will effect a different attribute of the final outcome. The main two things you want to add are range and damage, as arrow type and delay isn't as important to the user.

Arrows - 1-16 - Beginner

Equipment needed
Fletching kit
Field point
Wooden shaft
Round cut fletch
Large nock

Buy 20 of each component. Move one of each (CTRL - Click stack) and place them into your open Fletching kit. After they are all inside hit combine. You will get a message saying either you are successful or have failed. Keep doing this in till you reach the trivial level of 16. Once that happens do the same exact thing only replace the large nock with a medium one. This will get your skill level up to 36 when it becomes trivial.

Arrows - 36-46 - Novice

Equipment needed
Fletching kit
Field point
Wooden shaft
Parabolic fletch
Large nock

Doing the same as the first time, only now replace the round cut fletch with a Parabolic one. After awhile this will raise your skill to 46.

Arrows - 46-56 - Intermediate

Equipment needed
Fletching kit
Field point
Wooden shaft
Round cut fletch
Small nock

Following as before, this will raise your skill to 56.

Arrows - 56-68 - Intermediate continue

Equipment needed
Fletching kit
Field point
Bone shaft
Round cut fletch
Large nock

At this stage it is possible to create a successful bow, but it is suggested that you keep on using arrows till you at least achieve the title of Master. Following as before, this will raise your skill to 68.

Arrows - 68-82 - Advance

Equipment needed
Fletching kit
Field point
Wooden shaft
Shield cut fletch
Large nock

Following as before, this will raise your skill to 82.

Arrows - 82-102 - Master

Equipment needed
Fletching Kit
Hooked point
Wooden shaft
Round cut fletch
Large nock

Congratulations!, you have achieved the title of a Master Fletcher. You can now start creating bows, or keep working with arrows. If you decided to work with bows instead, then you should be warned that you would still have a high percent rate in failure. If you choose to continue to work with arrows then follow the chart below. Doing the same things as before but changing one item in the process.
