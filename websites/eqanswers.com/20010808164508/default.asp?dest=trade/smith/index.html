Mastering Smithing

The wonders of taking ore and refining it into metal has been one of the longest trade skills known to man. Since the dawn of time man created protection for himself, may that be leather or fine steel plate mail suits, the point remains true. Man cannot live in battle very long without protection of the delicate skin that covers his body, for that reason one must create armor to protect against the claw of a dragon or a blade of a sword. Becoming a blacksmith is no easy task. Heed my warning now; it requires long hours and some fair amounts of money to get started in this skill. However, when one has become a true master smith, you will find a sense of accomplishment and wealth, as this is no easy task for any man or woman. This guide will show you the process of gaining skill and working with different types of materials to create useful and useless items. The main idea of this guide is to show you the steps on becoming a master smith and beyond, and to make sure you do it right.

Making the items

In order to start your career of becoming a successful smith one must have the essential components. However, even before hand you must at least put 1 point into your blacksmith skill, which can be learned from any of your guild masters. Because Smithing is not the easiest skill to improve in level, I would strongly suggest you put the whole 20 points into the skill before trying to attempt anything further.

You’ll also need a fair bit of money before we start out; around 200 platinum pieces to complete the task of becoming a master smith. If you do not have this type of funds, then I suggest either asking in the chat room/message board for more information or asking a friend for a lend.

Backpacks are also a prime item for you to smith because of the large amounts of spaces you’ll be taking up with ore and others components you’ll run out fast!

Finally the last suggestion is whatever character you are using to smith if possible, move him to the Freeport area. This is because Freeport is one of the prime areas for mastering blacksmith and will allow you to gain skill levels much faster then another area. Why? Because everything is at your fingertips, you have the forge; the ore and the other components needed to gain skill either in the zone or at max in another part of Freeport. This is a lot better than, say, Grobb while you have to run to Oggok for the forge.

Sharpening – Level 1-26 – Novice

Equipment needed
Rusty Weapons (1gp+ type, no daggers)
Sharpening Stone

If you follow the instructions above, you’ll have 20 points into skill already. This means you're only 6 points away from becoming trivial in shapening which won’t take very long. If you didn’t, then you’ll need to get a lot more weapons and stones to gain the skill to 26. The first thing you want to do is find rusty weapons. These can be bought from NPC/PC merchants but it's easier to just buy them up from all the NPC merchants around Freeport. Make sure the weapons are worth more than 1gp, or else they will give you little to no skill. Once you have gotten a nice collection of rusty weapons, head down to Groflah’s Forge in North Freeport near the Bards' guild. There is an NPC merchant who sells Sharpening Stones. Buy a bunch--around 40--since they don’t cost much. Then go near the side of the building to the forge and open it up by clicking on it. Remember to follow the trade skill rule of always putting one item in at a time; never stack them. With Rusty weapons, always put in one rusty weapon and one shaperning stone in at once only, no more. Keep doing this until level 26 in skill, and it becomes trivial to you. Then move on.

Scaler– level 26-41 – Junior

Equipment needed
One metal bit (two small ore + water = metal bit)
One Scaler mold
Flask of water

Since you're still at Groflah’s Forge, head inside and buy a bunch of ore and then some more water. Combine them, and keep doing it to make metal bits. Make an entire backpack full of metal bits. Metal bits aren’t that hard to create, but it gets really boring to click that combine button over and over. Plus you’ll need a lot of them so don’t sell them. Once you have a bag full of Metal bits head to Trader’s Holiday and meet a female merchant inside who sells cookie molds and other types of molds. Buy a bunch of molds from her (the cheaper the better) and then combine the above components inside the forge. Whatever item you get back (for some reason its random), sell to the merchant. Keep doing Scalers until it becomes trivial to you in skill, which is around level 41, then head on to the next thing.

Lanterns – Level 41-60 – Senior

Equipment needed

One metal bits (two small ore + water = metal bit)
One Lantern casing Mold
One bottle
Flask of Water

Start making some more metal bits if you don’t have any left from your Scaler project. The lantern mold can be found in the same place you can find the scaler molds, and the bottle, which is a plain old bottle, can be found in the same building only on a male merchant. Take the above and combine them back into the forage and keep working with them until you become trivial. Sell all the Lanterns back to the NPC merchants.

Skewers – Level 60-115 – Master

Equipment needed
One metal bits (two small ore + water = metal bit)
One Skewer Mold
Flask of Water

Finally, after a couple days you are now close to becoming a master smith. From now til you reach master keep working with skewers. This should put you to 115 before they become trivial to you. The Skewer molds and such can be found at the same merchants where you found the other molds, and of course you already have metal bits (or at least know how to make more). This should be really kept up, even though it is very boring. When you hit the trivial level at 115, you can jump right into making banded without a high risk of skill failure and that is gonna save you a lot of money.

Sewing Kits – Level 115-135 – Advance Master

Equipment needed
One metal bits (two small ore + water = metal bit)
Needle mold
Thimble Mold
Flask of Water

If you wish to increase your skill more quickly with less cost, I would suggest starting on sewing kits now til 135. This is a lot better then fiddling around with banded just because of the cost involved with creating banded armor. After the sewing kit becomes trivial to you, then switch to banded leggings and mantles and then finish off with banded cloaks. This will get your skill completely to the max, which is 175 currently.
