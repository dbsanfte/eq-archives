Becoming A Bard

Forward

This guide is geared towards players who have a good working comprehension of EverQuest game fundamentals but wish to achieve additional information on classes they might want to play. If you are a new player in EverQuest, then I suggest reading the EverQuest Manual and looking at our new player section before continuing.

If there are any errors or comments on this guide, please send them to: news@eqanswers.com

Overview

Bards are what keep Norrath fun and exciting. They use their voices and instruments to perform magical songs instead of casting intricate spells. These songs might help your party members or take down a foe. While the bard's main skill is songs, they are also proficient in combat skills.

The bard's role in a group later on in life is much like a shaman's. You are mainly for support, but you have some offensive attacks up your sleeve. For the first twenty or so levels, you also make an excellent fighter. Bards are a necessity for any adventuring party. You can get your party to its destination faster than any druid or shaman of the same level, and when you reach your battle grounds, you can sing songs to increase the morale of the party thus increasing the effectiveness of your party members throughout the fight.

Bards don't exactly have lines of spells like other classes but rather a random collection of all kinds of songs. You can increase the attack speed of your group, make them run faster than the wolves, soothe their wounds, clear their minds, and make them stronger. These are only a few of the buffing songs bards get. To recriprocate, you can play horrible sounds on your instrument to make any enemy within earshot wince in pain. You can also surround enemies in chains of music making them slower. You can even let loose a great bellow that will make your target reel in pain. And one of the greatest bardic abilities is filling your enemy's head with your beautiful music, making it follow your commands and fight for you.

Young bards (and very strategic older bards) will be able to solo creatures for experience, but the role of a bard is best used within a group of other adventurers. Bards can wear almost any type of armor including plate, but you will be hit constantly in groups... the monsters of Norrath apparently do not like our music! We can dish out much damage, but we simply cannot take the punishment from enemies very well.

Myths and Controversy

Bards get hit too much in groups.
This is actually quite true. Due to the constant buffing and melee attacking a bard does during battle, he will get hit by your target very much. There are many ways to get around this however. For instance, you can just get out of melee range of the mob, and play your buffing songs with the appropriate instrument. This is good for your party because using the instrument instead of singing and meleeing will increase the song's effectiveness. This will usually stop you from being hit. Another good remedy for this is to group with classes that can Taunt like warriors and paladins. This will keep the mob on them and not you.

Bards will always die during a large pull.
The only time when this is true is if a bard is playing his songs while something is being pulled. It is very important for you to wait till the very last moment before you start your songs, the very nature of the way Everquest handles the aggression list causes you to rise to the top in the beginning of the battle and there is not much we can do but be very careful with what songs we play and when we play them. Verant has recently made a slight change as to how bards and the aggro list works, at this time there has been a little improvement but overall there is still much danger to a bard when many creatures are pulled at once.

All your songs are weak compared to normal buffs.
With the exception of Selo's Accelerando this is true, but only when you are soloing. However, when you are in a group, the combination of not needing mana and the fact that your songs affect everyone in your group result in most of your songs being of better use and more economical than say a druid casting SoW on the entire party.

Character Creation

Creating a character can be a daunting undertaking, for it can be straightforward and complex at the same time. You can create your character depending on what type of situation or role you want to classify in. You can shape your bard to be an incredible fighter or a very agile and evasive singer.

Race

Another large factor in creating your character is deciding what race to play. This is not only a personal choice depending on how you want to roleplay, but it will also effect how you play. Each race has diverse starting attributes, locations, and disadvantages over others. It is suggested you take a hard look at what one race offers over another, and most significantly if you think you will have fun with it.

Half Elf
The best choice over all if you are looking for balanced stats and a small experience penalty. They also have the infravision of their pure blood relatives but do not have the high agility of wood elves nor the wisdom of high elves. They make very nice fighters with their high dexterity. They also have the choice of starting in Qeynos, Freeport, or Kelethin. I highly recommend not starting in Kelethin. It is a good city, but as a medium size it is hard at the early levels to find sufficient armor.

Human
This is perhaps the most uncommon race for bards. They are considered balance, but other races have better stats for bards. They have no nightvision and are medium sized. It was once thought they also gain experience faster, but this is untrue (halflings are the ones that gain it fastest). Humans can start in Qeynos or Freeport.

Wood Elf
This is a very popular race for bards. They are very agile which is very important. They do not have very high strength, but they wear small armor which weighs much less. They have infravision too. They can start in kelethin which is a wonderful newbie city but very overpopulated.

Attributes

One of the most significant parts of character generation is choosing where to put your bonus points. This will let your further modify your characters role, to be more helpful in diverse situations. A Warrior might add more to his Strength so he can cut deeper into his quarry. While a Necromancer would be much more interested in increasing his magical powers and spreading the evil deeds with intelligence. A thing to keep in mind, you can even further increase your attributes with magical items found inside the game, so incase you make a oversight, it is not very hard to correct.

Strength
Influences your maximum and average damage output in melee combat--the more strength you have, the more likely you will hit harder. Additional strength will let you learn your offensive skills faster and let you carry more weight. This is good for bards because they are in combat very often.

Stamina
Affects how many total hit points you have and how long you will be able to hold your breath under water. To some degree this is a helpful attribute for a bard. Increasing your hit points will permit you to sustain the blows from combat, but the increase is such a small difference it would not be recommended.

Agility
Affects how often your attacker misses you in melee combat. Agility also determines how much AC you get. Also, affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills. This is an extremely important stat for bards. If we get stunned, we stop singing, and a mute bard is a dead bard. You will want to dodge attacks very often. The AC will also help immensely in melee combat.

Dexterity
Affects how often your weapon will proc. It also affects how often your offensive melee skills raise. This also affects how often rangers will get critical hits with their bows. It is rumored that this affects how much we miss notes when starting up songs. This is a very important melee stat (like agility), so it is a very important bard stat.

Wisdom
This is the source of mana for priest classes like clerics, shamans, druids, paladins, and rangers. It also affects skill growth of some normal skills if higher than your intelligence. This affects success rate with trade skills also. This is very unimportant for bards.

Intelligence
This is the source of mana for magi classes like necromancers, wizards, magicians, enchanters, shadowknights, and more importantly bards. Bards use mana for only a few songs, so putting points into this is unnecessary. It is easier to just get items that raise mana directly. Intelligence also affects skill growth for many skills.

Charisma
Affects how much NPCs like you. With a higher charisma, you will get better prices from merchants and get higher faction with many races/classes. This also affects spells/songs that "mess with minds" like Mesmerize and Charm. This is very important for bards with Lull, Charm, and Fear songs. Having a high Charisma will let you charm nearly any mob close to your level.

Combat

Much of your time spent inside EverQuest will be involving combat situations. To be prepared for combat, one must know the basics of it and EverQuest itself, which we won't describe here, for obvious reasons. What we will divulge, is common tactics used to take down foes more hastily and safer.

Solo

Bards are one of the few classes that simply cannot solo for the first twenty or so levels. Some people think bards are strictly a grouping class. Bards do a lot for groups but can also solo very effectively. Here are some techniques you can use to solo as a bard.

Bellow-Kiting
This is a painstakingly long method of soloing, but it does work. You can do this starting at lvl 12 when you get Brusco's Boastful Bellow. First, find a nice, wide open zone (like the Karanas) and find a mob close to your level (whites and yellows work nicely at lvl 12). Put on Selo's Accelerando (you can also get a SoW from someone else to make it the whole thing faster) to find some prey. When you find something, Bellow it, and run around in a circle just big enough that it cannot hit you, but it's still in range for bellow. Now play Selo's once, then Bellow twice, then repeat the process. After a long time, your target will be dead, and you will be at nearly full HPs.

Fear-Chain
This is one of the best ways to solo. You can do this starting at lvl 26 when you get Angstlich's Appalling Screech. Again, find a nice wide open zone like the Karanas. When you find your prey (anything will work, but you will probably get many resists on whites), sing Chain on it to slow it down. Then when it gets close enough, play Screech to make it run away. Now you have a slow moving mob that won't touch you. Just keep twisting Chain and Screech while attacking the mob with melee weapons. Pretty soon, the mob will be dead, and you can celebrate in the nice solo exp!

Team Work

This is where the bard shines, the bard is the ultimate group member. As a bard you can make up for the absence of any other class in the group, you can be a tank, buffer, puller, healer, or evacer (good use of Selos). It is very important to know exactly how each of your songs work and in what situations they can be used for maximum benefit. A bards song selection changes often depending on level, group makeup, and zone type. No guide can tell you what songs you should be using, only experience can give you that kind of knowledge. Luckily since you are ready to get out of any situation in case it turns bad you should have no problem learning he ins and outs of your songs in different situations.

Conclusion

The Bard is by far one of the most interesting classes to play, and with most of Norrath's spell abilities at your command you can go anywhere and see most of the world without great personal harm. You will always be welcome in a group and at most levels considered the cornerstone to a truly well balanced group. Bards have huge role-playing potential and many of the early role-playing events were concerts put on by bards who had written actual songs and poems which they performed in places such as the Theater of the Tranquil in Freeport. Whatever path you take I wish you a song in your heart and a tune upon your lips!
