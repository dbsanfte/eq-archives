Becoming A Paladin

Forward

This guide is geared towards players who have a good working comprehension of EverQuest game fundamentals but wish to achieve additional information on classes they might want to play. If you are a new player in EverQuest, then I suggest reading the EverQuest Manual and looking at our new player section before continuing.

If there are any errors or comments for this guide, please send them to: news@eqanswers.com

Overview

The true knights of Norrath are the majestic paladins. They wander Norrath looking for good deeds to do for citizens and healing the wounded with every chance they get. The paladin can wield mighty swords to banish the evil legions that exist in every corner of the map and yet still use magics to smite the unliving. Your road may not always be easy, but in the end the rewards are many.

Choosing the way of the paladin is a big decision. As a hybrid between a warrior and cleric (about 3/4 warrior, 1/4 cleric), you will gain battle experience a little slower than other classes of Norrath. You also suffer from the fact that you can't fully play either the role of a cleric or a warrior. They do have some great abilities though.

Perhaps the greatest skill of the paladin is Lay Hands. It is a special skill we come into the world with which heals about 25 HPs per lvl of the paladin but can only be used once per EQ day (about an hour and a half in real time). It's a true lifesaver during heated battles. We also get the Bash skill (which hits the opponent with your shield stunning him and interrupting his casting) fairly early, and a very welcome addition to the paladin's skills is Double Attack (which lets you have a chance to hit twice per round with your weapon). These are great skills and are part of what defines the paladin class, but the paladin doesn't fully realize his potential until lvl 9 when we are granted by our gods our first spells.

Obviously our best line of spells is the heal series, which starts off at lvl 9 with Minor Heal (about 10 HPs) and ends at lvl 57 with Superior Healing (about 600 HPs). In addition to the healing line of spells paladins also have access to clerical buffs. We are able to buff ourselves with the Yaulp series (brief STR and AC boost) or others with spells that add to HPs, AC, STR, etc. The final line of spells paladins have are those of the Ward Undead series. These come in handy in undead dungeons like Unrest and Befallen since they are evocation spells which do direct damage to the undead target (such as skeletons and ghouls). It starts with Ward Undead at lvl 15 and ends with Expel Undead at lvl 54.

Lastly the single, greatest asset of the paladin is our selection of holy weapons. These are magical weapons which only paladins can wield and bring us great power in combat. One such holy weapon is Ghoulbane--a sword held in one hand with an effect of Dismiss Undead. Only the most honorable and powerful paladins are able to obtain such powerful holy relics.

Myths and Controversy

Armor of Ro is impossible to get
While this used to be true, Verant has recently changed the spawn times on the creatures involved in getting the Ro Armor. Ro is still difficult to obtain, but while it is probably the hardest quest armor to get, it is much easier than in the days of 40 hour long spawn times on Undead Cyclopes.

Paladins can't taunt off of other classes
Using normal fighing tactics this is usually true. Because of the high damage output of rangers, monks, and warriors, it is often hard to taunt creatures off them. However, the recent development of Stun fighting has shifted the balance since the Stun spell has a very high taunt factor. In addition Verant has also upgraded the paladin's taunt ability a short time back to compensate for their weakness in this area.

Paladins are just downgraded warriors who can heal
Many people believe that with the exception of the Lay Hands ability Paladins are nothing more than low HP warriors with the ability to heal. While Paladins do indeed have much less HPs than warriors naturally, it is balanced out by the fact that they can buff themselves to the point of ever having over 2,000 hit points as early as level 50. Paladins also have spells that increase their AC and lesser version of cleric ones that do a wide variety of things such as cure poisons, protect from disease, stun creatures, and blind them.

Character Creation

Now it is time to experience playing the paladin, but you must first create it. Creating your character can be simple and complex at the same time. You can build your character for different situations depending on what type you want to play. Some might want to tank like a maniac and pump up strength and agility. Others might want to be able to heal a whole lot and pump up wisdom (which is our source for mana).

Race

This is pretty much a personal choice. If you want to be a major healer, I would go with high elf. If you want to be a great fighter, I would go with dwarf. If you want to roleplay a human knight of honor and chivalry, choose a human. It all depends on how you want to play.

Dwarf
Dwarves are the children of Brell Serillis the Duke of Below. They are stout hardy folk who enjoy a good fight and are greatly in love with mining gems and working metals into everything from great weapons to works of art. A dwarf lives most of his life underground in the great city of Kaladim, and such a life often grants the dwarf the ability to see exceptionally well in the dark.

Dwarves are a great choice for the paladin who likes to duke it out with others. They have the highest base strength, stamina, and dexterity but a low base agility which means you will be hit quite often. They start on the continent of Faydwer in the city Kaladim which is a decent city and has a fairly undercrowded newbie area. The Butcherblock zone outside is great for many levels too. This is one of the most common races for paladins since they have such high starting stats aimed towards melee classes.

Erudite
While little is known about the Erudites, those who have gathered information about them seem to find evidence of a vast amount of knowledge and intelligence making them almost a superior race. The Erudites are mostly recognized for their magical abilities but more recently seem to be gaining good training in the field of melee combat.

Erudites are esteemed by many, but those who are envious of the Erudites’ immense power and intellect have shunned their race. Erudites detest those who follow the path of Innoruuk but rarely bring harm to others. It is important to watch for Heretics of Erudin however, as they have chosen a darker path. While Erudites have higher intelligence than humans, they share the human trait of near night-blindness.

Erudites start in Toxxulia Forest, a difficult setting for one new to the realms that are trying to gain experience. The Forest is a hard place to navigate, and when night falls, it is practically impossible to find your way around. Many Erudites leave the Forest for the more rewarding areas around Qeynos, only a short boat ride away.

Half Elf
Half elves are the cross-bred children of a human and an elf, and while they are accepted in both societies, they are not particularly liked in either. They make decent fighters, but unfortunately they don't, however, make good healers since they start off with the lowest wisdom. This means you won't have too much mana. They wear medium armor and have the infravision ability. They start off in Qeynos or Freeport which are on opposite sides of Antonica, and they are both great towns. Other than the low wisdom, this is a good race for paladins, though not a very common one.

High Elf
The name high elf signifies not only their stature, as they tower over many other races, but also their position within Norrath’s Society. Many observers view high elves as a race that prefers to ignore others and care mostly for themselves. The high elves focus solely on magical abilities and waste little time learning true melee, if they choose to learn at all. Their strong intellectual development makes them a perfect race for healing and offensive magic.

While high elves are liked by many of the realm's inhabitants, there are those who view them less favorably, resulting in a minor faction loss. High elves also have the ability to see through darkness, as they inherently posses Infravision.

The new high elf will first wander under the trees of Greater Faydark to begin combat and gain experience. The forest is a very popular spot for trying out those first weapons and spells, so it can become quite crowded with hundreds of elves sprinting under the leaves looking for elusive prey. A good solution to the dilemma of fewer battles is a short journey beyond the trees to Steamfont.

Human
The most populous race in Norrath is the human, as more and more players choose it. Now that the humans have grown in numbers, they also control much of the land and have primary authority over both main ports in Antonica. Humans can choose among all the classes except Shaman. Most people consider humans to be a balanced race, and playing a human has become a favorite especially among newer players.

Humans enjoy the high regard of most races except the evil ones. While the trolls and ogres will of course eat nearly anything including humans, the dark elves show some tolerance. Beware as the dark elves may attack if the human does not worship the darker gods. The most perceptible problem of being human is night blindness, and newer players will find navigation of the terrain in the dark to be difficult.

Either Qeynos or Freeport offers superb starting grounds for gaining experience rapidly. Many of the other races come to take advantage of the areas surrounding the towns. Freeport opens to a flat and desolate landscape which allows you to spot prey easily. Similarly, just outside Qeynos's gates is a large and grassy park with close proximity to the life-saving guards.

Attributes

One of the most significant parts of character generation is choosing where to put your bonus points. This will let you further modify your character's role to be more helpful in diverse situations. A warrior might add more to his Strength, so he can cut deeper into his quarry. While a necromancer would be much more interested in increasing his magical powers and spreading the evil deeds with Intelligence. A thing to keep in mind--you can even further increase your attributes with magical items found inside the game, so in case you make a oversight, it is not very hard to correct.

Strength (Valuable for Paladins)
Influences your maximum and average damage ratios. The more strength you have, the more likely you will hit harder. Additional strength will let you learn your offensive skills faster and let you carry more weight. This is an important stat for paladins, so you should put points into it for wearing that heavy plate armor.

Stamina (Valuable for Paladins)
Affects how many total hit points you have and how long you will be able to hold your breath under water. In melee classes it will also affect how quickly you lose stamina from fighting with a heavy weapon.

Agility (Valuable for Paladins)
This is another good stat for melee classes. It affects your AC and how often you get hit. Some races will really need this, and others already have high enough agility.

Dexterity (Valuable for Paladins based on weapon)
This stat makes your weapon's effect go off (referred to as 'procing') more often. It was once thought it affects how often you hit your target in melee, but Abashi has said it doesn't. It is also believed to make you recover from being hit more while casting spells. Not sure on this though. This ability is usually only useful for a paladin when wielding one of his holy sword which often have a proc.

Wisdom (Valuable for Paladins)
One of the most important stats for paladins--it directly affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, including rangers. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than intelligence.

Intelligence (Ineffective for Paladins)
Directly affects the amount of mana of magi classes, including bards and shadow knights. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills.

Charisma (Ineffective for Paladins)
Affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you. Also effects saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular).

Combat

Much of your time spent inside EverQuest will involve combat situations. To be prepared for combat, one must know the basics of it and EverQuest itself, which we won’t describe here for obvious reasons. What we will divulge though are common tactics used to take down foes more hastily and safer.

Solo

Paladins are like many of the other melee classes in that at lower levels they have the ability to solo, but once they reach level 15 or so, they must start seeking out groups if they wish to survive. Fortunately when a paladin finds that he can solo, he has much less down time due to the fact that he has the ability to heal himself and can regenerate mana at quite a fast pace. A paladin at higher levels will also have the advantage of many stat-increasing buffs, and once he has access to the Symbol line of clerical spells, he suddenly has the ability to increase his hit point up to 575 points with a single buff. A soloing paladin also has the distinct advantage of using Lay Hands (see Special Information below) if he finds himself in a situation where he has attacked something he can not handle.

Group

Paladins are a great asset to any group. In battle it truly shines through that they are 3/4 warrior, and when going up against undead or magic users they have a few tricks up their sleeves that can tip the balance of battle in their group's favor. When fighting casters for instance most paladins prefer to use a one handed weapon while bashing with a shield and casting stun--usually if you bash sucessfully enough times and don't fizzle too many stuns, you can make sure that caster doesn't get off a single spell. Another bonus is that with a paladin's ability to heal and buff, he not only does good in melee combat but also can fill the void if a group finds itself without a cleric.

General

In essence paladins fight like most other classes do, and even though you can cast spells, melee is still your most important goal in battle. Paladins live and die by their equipment--without the right weapon and the knowledge of how to use it, you will be of no use to your group no matter how well you think you can heal, or how good you are at pulling off a LoH right before the evacer dies. Some basic tips that will keep you and your group alive are as follows:

  • Always remember to taunt, even if you are currently the target of the monster's aggressions.
  • When fighting a caster make sure to have stun memorized and use it as soon as you see the spell effect going off.
  • Watch your hit points and those of your team during battle--sometimes the main healer doesn't notice when someone is about to die, and you may need to heal him instead.
  • Be careful when and where you cast Flash of Light. Inside a dungeon a running monster at full health usually means a train and quick death for you and your party.

Chain Stunning

A newly discovered ability that paladins can use in battle is chain stunning a creature--not just a caster but any monster that you are fighting. Between the Stun spell, bashing with a shield, and the few new Kunark weapons which proc Stun spells, the creature you are fighting could be dead before getting off more than a couple of blows or a single spell. The only downside to this method of fighting is that it quickly drains your mana, leaving you unable to heal or to keep casting stun if it is a long, drawn out battle.

Helping Citizens

You must remember that you are a paladin, a holy knight whose solemn duty is to protect others and defend the weak. Many paladins take it upon themselves to always be on the lookout for someone in need of help; a lower level player about to die and in need of a heal, a group who has pulled one too many creatures and are having trouble holding them all off, or just buffing other players to help them out in future combat.

One Hand Slash or Two Hand Slash?

This one's a tough choice which is usually dependant on what equipment you have access to. While 2HS does more damage (especially when you gain Double Attack), wielding a 1HS gives room for a shield. If you are facing mobs that often cast harmful spells, 1HS and a shield are most likely your best bet since Bash interrupts the target when they are casting. Unfortunatly a lot of this really matters on what weapons/shields you have. Obviously if you have Soulfire you would want to use that rather than a 1HS. But if you are lvl 25 and have a Ghoulsbane and Bladestopper, you would use that over your old FS 2 Hander.


Special Information

Lay On Hands (LoH)

Lay on Hands is the ability that sets the Paladin apart from everyone else. With the single click of a button you can save a comrade from near death or heal yourself giving you time to either run away or finish the battle when you otherwise would have not. As powerful as it is there still is a downside: after using it you need to wait a day of in-game time (1 hour) before you can use it again. At most levels Lay on Hands will heal practically all your hit points, it isn't until you get to the later levels (45+) that it only heals a portion. Even then it still serves its purpose--to save you or a group member's life in the time that it take to hit a button.


Spell Usage

Paladin healing spells make them decent at soloing, as I have already stated. And if you are actually able to take down a mob yourself, your downtime is fairly quick. You get the Meditate skill (which lets you regain mana faster) at lvl 12. Before 12 it's still kind of slow, but after you have Meditate you can heal and get back into the fight faster than others can. Another spell you should use often is Yaulp. It doesn't take long to cast and doesn't use much mana, so why not use it? Everyone can use that extra STR and AC. The Ward Undead series is extremely useful if you hunt in undead dungeons a lot. There aren't too many of them, but if you travel to one, you can do some major damage. Ward Undead does about 40 pts. of damage to the target. After casting that several times, it really adds up. The bottom line here is always stay buffed and conserve some mana for quick heals when you are hanging on by a thread.

The following list will deal with some of the important spells a Paladin will gain over his career:

Level 9

Yaulp (Self Only Buff)
This spell is ideal to cast constantly during battl. It costs barely any mana and adds +10 points to STR and +6 to your AC. A small bonus, but every bit counts and at the higher levels, the amount of mana this requires to cast is considered paltry.

Flash of Light (Pulling Spell)
At the early levels when you are dealing with very few agressive monsters that are spread out with a large range, this spell can be used as a pulling spell. It should never be used in a dungeon though since it will often cause the creature to go running away for a few seconds, more than enough time to bring friends back.

Level 15

Lull (Reduces Aggression)
Once you start hunting in groups this spell becomes invaluable when you want to pull a single monster out of a group (orc, goblin, or derv camps). All you need to do is cast Lull on all monsters but the one you want and then have someone attack the monster and pull him away from his friends.

Ward Undead (Direct Damage - Undead)
This is your first spell in the Ward Undead line. It is one of the few spells that paladins get which directly cause damage. However, the spell can only be used on undead creatures and does between 36-41 damage depending on your level.

Level 22

Invigor (Replace Stamina)
If you wield an exceptionally heavy weapon and tend to fight in long battles, then this spell is very important. As time goes by in battle your stamina (yellow bar under health) will drop, and you will hit less and do less damage. One casting of this spell will replace all your lost stamina.

Root (Stop NPC Movement)
If used correctly this spell can be the ultimate taunting tool, all you have to do is root the monster and have everyone else back away. Since you are the only one standing near the monster still he will attack you, giving the others time to heal up, run away, or attack the monster from a distance with spells.

Level 30

Stun (Stuns Target)
One of your most important spells--casting this spell will stun a target causing it to stop casting or stand still for either 5-6 seconds or when someone attacks it. More information on use of stun provided in the Combat section under Chain Stunning.

Symbol of Transal (HP Buff)
One of the most powerful buffs you will ever get--the Symbol spells add greatly to your hit points giving as much as 525 extra hit points at the higher levels.

Level 39

Greater Healing (Heals HP)
One of the best healing spells you will get as far as mana cost goes--this spell will heal 300 hit points total and is considered by some as a smaller version of LoH at the level you get it.

Cancel Magic (Debuff)
This spell is a tactical one--it can be used to remove one or two spell effects on an NPC or Player. It can be used to take a damage shield off a monster or remove a harmful spell (like a DoT) from a teammate.

Level 49

Holy Might (Damage Stun)
This spell is an upgraded version of Stun in that not only does it stun the target, but it does between 29 and 70 damage as well. The damage might be worth it, but in the end you can stun more often if you use the original Stun spell.

Revive (Resurrection)
This spell is useful if a fallen comrade has a long way to run from his bind point back to his corpse and there isn't a cleric around to do an experience resurrect. When cast on a corpse it will, with the owner's consent, gate the person back to their corpse.


Conclusion

The paladin is a fun class to play, especially if you like to roleplay. Being able to melee AND heal effectively is a very nice combination. If you want to solo, this class isn't for you. However, if you like to play a support role in a group, this makes a great class. It may seem horrid at first when you are simply a weak version of a warrior but keep playing, and you will mature into a glorious knight. Have fun and always remember to help others.
