Becoming A Magician

Forward

This guide is geared towards players who have a good working comprehension of EverQuest game fundamentals, but wish to achieve additional information on class’s they might want to play. If you are a new player in EverQuest, then I suggest reading the EverQuest Manual and looking at our new player section before continuing.

If you their are any errors or comments for this guide please send them to: news@eqanswers.com

Overview

Summoners of Norrath. They are the great and Magnificent Magicians, which roam the land weaving its four aspects of water, fire, air and earth together. Creating Magical beasts, items of great power and weapons of better or equal strength of the strongest steal. From merely thin air.

The Path of a Magician is one of great honor and status. If you choose to become, and not stray, you will soon feel the true power that they have access too. The world of Norrath to some extent is one of a ‘balanced’ world, and to where there is power, there are also disadvantages.

A Magician’s power is very similar to that of a wizard, they are typically an offensive magic class. They do however; have a few tricks up their sleeve. With a swift of the hand, the Magician can conjuror powerful elementals that will answer to your bidding. These can be one of the most powerful assets to the Magician, as the elemental will never flee from a fight, and never complain during your adventures. They are servants of their master, and will do anything you please, including dying for you. This makes Magicians a somewhat offensive magic and melee package, letting you control both sides of the combat. Though, don’t think you’re as equal power of a warrior and wizard together. You are not, and it would be foolish to think so.

There are many different spells that the Magician has access too, making her a fairly interesting class. The main force behind the Magician is the offensive spells, very much like the wizards but with a few disadvantages. The biggest issue is slightly higher casting times, and lower damage to mana ratio. We also can’t do the most damage in one casting like the Wizard, however combined with a elemental can do the same if not more damage then one Wizard.

Summoning spells is the next type Magician’s receive. Allowing them to conjuror up many special and magical items. Some of these may come in handy in times of need and others may seem utterly pointless. One of the great assets of being a Magician is your summoning spells will let you stay out longer then another class.

Some compare the Magician to the evil twin. The Necromancer, but I distress. I agree both the Magician and Necromancers can stay on their own for a long period of time, but Magicians also have the ability to jump in a group and actually offer a great deal of fire power to it. A Magician is not just purely a solo class if you don’t want it to be, but we can do solo extremely well.

Myths and Controversy

Pets are an aggravation after level 30.
A very common compliant among one’s who have never played a class which main focus is the pet ability. The concern is that pets will wander off due to bad pathing, or pursue after a fleeing NPC only to attract more then what you bargained for. One way to prevent this is making sure you know the area in which you are hunting well enough, that you are familiar with any pathing problem areas. If for some reason your pet has gotten lost inside a dungeon, then the best solution would be to gate or exit the zone and re-enter. Doing so will prevent any future trains on yourself and others in the area.

Pets and Experience
Another frequent myth is that pets take large amounts of experience from the Magician or group she is playing with. This is absolutely not true. Your pet does in fact take some experience, but so little it is hard to notice. Adding another group member would take large amounts of that experience pool towards having a pet. So always remember to summon a pet in all situations, and if your group complains, explain to them why its good to have a pet.

Magician’s needed to Research their pets.
Not completely false, but it is not entirely true either. Magicians can research their pets if they choose, but it is not necessary like with the Necromancers. The Magician has the choice of buying elementals all the way up to level 50. However, the catch is you won’t always have the choice of the elemental. For example, vendors may only sell the level 39 Earth spell, and only Fire at level 44. This can prove difficult in some situations, with not being able to customize the pet for the playing field. But, we still have the option of researching the Elemental we desire. 

Summoned Items are worthless
You’ll find this comment among many Magicians, saying a lot of their summoning abilities seem to be not worth the time and mana. Though this has change to some extent due to the new Expansion: Ruins of Kunark, where many of our items come in handy. Verant has also upgraded the older summoning spells, making them more useful to groups and Magicians.

Character Creation

Creating a Character can be a daunting undertaking, for it can be straightforward and complex at the same time. You can create your character depending on what type of situation or role you want to classify in. With a Magician, there are many different types of roles you can play, but two of the most common is a group player and soloist. 

Race

Another large factor in creating your Character, is deciding what race to play. This is not only a personal choice depending on how you want to role-play, but it will also effect how you play. Each race has diverse starting attributes, location and disadvantages, over others. Typically evil races are harder to play then good ones, but the final decision is left up to you. It is suggested you take a hard look at what one race offers over another, and most significantly if you think you will have fun with it.

Dark Elf
They live within the dark dwellings of the underground city only known as Neriak. Some say Dark elves are the true masters behind Innoruuks malevolent plans “as with their high intelligence make them exceptional strategic planners against the ugly humans” 

The Dark elves are not very well liked among most races, and will generally be attacked on sight. They are however blessed with the ability to see through darkness as if it was daylight, a gift that is called ultra-vision.

The area surrounding Neriak, contains many species that would be worthwhile to any new dark elf adventurer eagerly trying to achieve experience. The forest is also protected by many patrolling guards and summoned creatures making it relatively safe for a new Dark elf.

Erudite
While little is known about the Erudites, those who have gathered information about them seem to find evidence of a vast amount of knowledge and intelligence making them almost a superior race. The Erudites are mostly recognized for their magical abilities, but more recently seem to be gaining good training in the field of melee combat.

Erudites are esteemed by many, but those who are envious of the Erudites’ immense power and intellect have shunned their race. Erudites detest those who follow the path of Innoruuk, but rarely brings harm to others. It is important to watch for Heretics of Erudin however, as they have chosen a darker path. While Erudites have higher intelligence than humans, they share the human trait of near night blindness.

Erudites start in Toxxulia Forest, a difficult setting for one new to the realms that are trying to gain experience. The Forest is a hard place to navigate and when night falls it is practically impossible to find your way around the forest. Many Erudites leave the Forest for the more rewarding areas around Qeynos, only a short boat ride away.

Gnome
A Gnome characteristically is short with a sparse hairline or even a shiny pate. Gnomes have very inquisitive minds and have a knack for all things mechanical. It has been suggested that Gnomes are most likely responsible for inventing most of Norrath’s superior inventions, including the chronometer. While their very high intelligence supports the use of magic among Gnomes, many have found them to be among the finest warriors in the land.

Gnomes are usually found going busily about their business, harming no one unless provoked. They have a deep interest in inventing that is supported by their natural ability to tinker items – they are the only race with its own trade skill. Gnomes also have enhanced night sight with their inherent infravision.

Gnomes live inside the underground metropolis of Ak’anon, and outside is the mountains of Steamfont. Newer players will do well to begin leveling up just outside Ak’anon in Steamfont. Many from Faydwer journey to the hills and geysers of Steamfont to hunt its rich surroundings. You may find yourself skirmishing over quarry with wood elves instead of gnomes.

High Elf
The name High Elf signifies not only their stature as they tower over many other races, but also their position within Norrath’s Society. Many observers view High Elves as a race that prefers to ignore others and care mostly for themselves. The High Elves focus solely on magical abilities and waste little time learning true melee, if they choose to learn at all. Their strong intellectual development makes them a perfect race for healing and offensive magic.

While High Elves are liked by many of the realms’ inhabitants, they’re those who view them less favorably, resulting in a minor faction loss. High elves also have the ability to see through darkness as they inherently posses Infravision.

The new High Elf will first wander under the trees of Greater Faydark to begin combat and gain experience. The forest is a very popular spot for trying out those first weapons and spells so it can become quite crowded, with hundreds of Elves sprinting under the leaves looking for elusive prey. A good solution to the dilemma of fewer battles is a short journey beyond the trees to Steamfont.

Human 
The most populous race in Norrath is Human as more and more players choose it. Now that the Humans have grown in numbers, they also control much of the land and have primary authority over both main ports in Antonica. Humans can choose among all the classes except Shaman. Most people consider Humans to be a Balanced race and playing a Human has become a favorite especially among newer players.

Humans enjoy the high regard of most races, except the evil ones. While the Trolls and Ogres will of course eat nearly anything including Humans, the dark elves show some tolerance. Beware as the dark elves may attack if the human does not worship the darker gods. The most perceptible problem of being human is night blindness and newer players will find navigation of the terrain in the dark to be difficult. 

Either Qeynos or Freeport offer superb starting grounds for gaining experience rapidly. Many of the other races come to take advantage of the areas surrounding the towns. Freeport opens to a flat and desolate landscape, which allows you to spot prey easily. Similarly, just outside the Qeynos’ gates is a large and grassy park with close proximity to the life-saving guards.

Attributes

One of the most significant parts of character generation is choosing where to put your bonus points. This will let your further modify your characters role, to be more helpful in diverse situations. A Warrior might add more to his Strength so he can cut deeper into his quarry. While a Necromancer would be much more interested in increasing his magical powers and spreading the evil deeds with intelligence. A thing to keep in mind, you can even further increase your attributes with magical items found inside the game, so incase you make a oversight, it is not very hard to correct.

Strength (Ineffective for Magicians)
Influences your maximum and average damage ratios, the more strength you have the more likely you will hit harder. Additional strength will let you learn your offensive skills faster, and let you carry more weight.

Stamina (Valuable for Magicians)
Affects how many total hit points you have, and how long you will be able to hold your breath under water.

To some degree this is a helpful attribute for a Magician. Increasing your hit points will permit you to sustain the blows from adversary casting classes, but the increase is such a small difference it would not be recommended.

Agility (Valuable for Magicians)

Increases your percentage in how difficult it is to hit you, as well as how much damage you take when you are hit. Also, affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills.

I would suggest putting your last five points into this attribute as it will let you stay in melee combat longer, which is helpful inside close quarters, and especially during the younger levels.

Dexterity (Ineffective for Magicians)

Influences how often your weapon proc (spell) will engage, and how quickly you learn weapon skills. Will also increase how much damage you hit with bows, and how fast you learn rogue skills.

Wisdom (Ineffective for Magicians)

Directly affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, including rangers. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher then intelligence.

Intelligence (Valuable for Magicians)

Directly affects the amount of mana of magi classes, including bards and shadow knights. Also affects how quickly you can learn many skills.

Here is the most influential attribute you can place points into, as it will directly affect your mana pool. More mana equals more firepower to take down opponents, and occasionally letting you take foes much higher level then you, or even save your life with that last bit, to gate.

Charisma (Ineffective for Magicians)

Affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you. Also effects saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

Elementals

Here is where the Magician’s strength lay. The Elemental is a great damage output, and is one of the most effective damage over time "spells" you could own. However just because an Elemental is disposable, do not treat it as such. If you get in trouble, and need to flee, go right ahead and run to the zone, but if you think you can win the fight, stick it out with your Elemental. Better yet, know what you and your Elemental can handle, work with him more like a human player, rather then a slave. Protect your Elemental as he protects you, and soon you will find it easy to stay with the same Elemental for hours without it dying (that is if you don’t log or zone).

A detailed run down of the strength and weaknesses of each Elemental type.
By Geoffrey Zatkin, Lead Magic Designer for Verant

The Earth Elemental is the elemental that you want to be using for serious battles. It has THE most hp of any pet. It also has a massive strength (about equal to an Ogre). Earth elementals deal THE most damage of any pet. Their weakness is that their Agility bites. They will get hit by anything swinging at them.

The Air Elemental is the elemental that you want tanking for you. It has an incredible high agility, making it much harder to hit than any other elemental. It has good strength (about equal to a Barbarian). It deals good damage, is hard to hit, and has the invisibility ability. This means you can use "/pet back off" to good effect. The Air Elemental backs off, and assuming that monster does not follow (it goes after another party member), the Air Elemental will turn invisible, guaranteeing that it will not be hit while it heals up.

The Fire Elemental is the elemental you want to use against creatures with very quick attacks (Bixies) or against massive amounts of smaller creatures (clearing away the hall trash). Fire Elementals have good strength (about equal to a Barbarian) and good agility (about equal to an elf). They have the least HP of any elemental, but compensate with an innate Fire Shield that damages an attacker every time the attacker hits the elemental. This means that the elemental AND taking damage is hitting a creature every time they hit the elemental.

Water Elementals are the composite elemental - they combine a little bit of the best of all of the other elementals. Their strength is second only to an Earth Elemental (about equal to a Troll), their agility is good (about as good as an elf) and their HP is just under that of an Earth Elemental. They are the 'balanced' elemental. Water Elementals also heal faster than any other elemental.

Combat

Much of your time spent inside EverQuest will be involving combat situations. To be prepared for combat, one must know the basics of it and EverQuest itself, which we won’t describe here, for obvious reasons. What we will divulge, is common tactics used to take down foes more hastily and safer.

Solo

Only certain classes can become an effective soloist and a Magician does do it quite successfully. The life of someone who chooses to be by herself is one of immense risk, but also of great reward. You can reap much more experience faster, letting you build your levels more rapidly then someone who says inside the warm refuge of a group. Of course, without that group you have no one to save you, and soloist tend to die much more often then someone inside a group. The second disadvantage is, most high-level items are found on creatures that require a group’s effort.

Group

Fighting inside a group can have many advantages, taking down higher-level creatures, resulting in more experience for some classes, better loot and the protection of having a group behind you. Some people also like the group social aspect of it, and find adventuring solo can become very boring and tired sum over time. If your class has the ability to solo well, and you are after experience then you may want to go with that. There is no doubt, going solo will result in faster and more experience then inside a group. Sooner or later though everyone will need to group, as after level 40 everything becomes too hard to take down by a single person.

General

This most frequently used tactic throughout levels is your basic nuke while your pet tanks. This has been a tired and tested method and is the staple among many Magicians. Though this can work fairly well in the correct situation, it is mostly used among the lower levels. Once you get above level 30, your pet cannot sustain the amount of damage a same level NPC would dish out. In the amount of time it takes you to nuke it to death, your pet will be already dead. Another recent tactic, is medding while your pet tanks, and throwing him abundant heals, though this may not be the most effective, some people find some luck with it.

Team Work

One of the major advantages you can gain is working with your elemental (“Pet”) as a team and not just as dispensable mana. You should know what you and your pet can take, what pet to use in different situations and how each one works accordingly with yourself. Of course, your pet can be used as a lifesaver in most situations, so you’ll also want to know when to run like hell. Always make sure your pet is fully healed, and buffed at all times in certain areas like dungeons and high traffic train zones. You never know when the next time you’ll go into combat, and having your pet ready will most likely save your life. Do not leave your pet if you see him dying, have some faith in him. If you treat your pet right, you will soon find you’ll be able to keep them alive for a long time. 

Pet Kiting

A very common occurrence with Magicians a tactic entitled “Kiting”. If you haven’t heard, kiting in essence is running in front of a NPC while damaging it. This tends to be done in very open areas where you can run for a long period of time without attracting a lot of attention, of other NPCs. The modified version for Magicians, is using a command called “/pet notaunt” this will make your pet taunting ability turned off. Starting a fight with a nuke, and then following it up with a few more, making the NPC very aggressive towards you. Once you have gotten it down to half, send in your Pet, he will continue to hit it from behind while the NPC chases after you. A note of warning, do not attempt this unless you have some sort of Speed Spell. It works best when a druid can cast SoW on both yourself and your pet.

Spell Usage

As a Magician you can go through mana very quickly, due to the nature of Magician’s nuking abilities. It is outmost important that you have knowledge of how much mana you have and how much each spell will cost you. You will also want to leave just enough mana incase you need to gate out of a sticky situation. Never go into a fight with things much higher then your level unless you know for sure you can get through its magic resistance.

Damage Shields

This is a very effective way in raising the odds of winning a fight. A damage shield will inflict damage on anything that cause’s you on your pet damage. In other words, if a NPC hits your pet, then it will automatically hurt the NPC. This can be an extremely useful tactic while fighting multiple NPCs or when you are fighting something that hits very fast but for lower damage. Always cast a damage shield on your pet before a fight while soloing, it can turn the odds and greatly speed up the fight.

Anti Magic

The Malosi line of spells can become very useful down the road while fighting even, yellow, and higher-level NPCs. It will lower your targets magic resistance, resulting in less chance for your spell to get resisted. Remember to always practice alteration as much as possible to higher the skill level so that Malosi will not fail.

Bandage

The skill is the most underused skill in the game I believe, but it’s a great addition to the Magician, and a savior at higher levels while solo. Magicians can summon bandages in large qualities, up to 20 at level 49, and with a high level skill in bind wound, it will take you a couple minutes to get back to half health, rather then 10-15minutes of sitting. Practice this skill as soon as possible, I know its boring at first, and may not "seem" to be helping much, but trust me in the long run it does.

Pet Commands
Once you have a pet, you can instruct it to taunt your target, attack, follow you, guard a location, or protect you as follows:

/pet as you were - Put the pet at ease (cancels all previous orders).
/pet attack - Tell pet to attack designated target.
/pet back off - Tell pet to stop attacking.
/pet follow me - Tell pet to follow you (deactivates once you cross playing zones).
/pet get lost - Order a pet to kill itself.
/pet guard here - Tell pet to guard its current location (it will attack all players and NPCs).
/pet guard me - Tell pet to attack anyone that attacks you, or that you attack.
/pet report health - View pet's current health (% remaining).
/pet taunt - Tell pet to taunt your current target (toggles on/off).
/pet who the leader - View pet's commander (useful for enchanters).

Spell Information

Here we will describe the first two ranks of spells you are eligible to buy, including which ones to buy and the ones to avoid. You can find all these spells at your local guildhall, or near it.

First Rank (Levels 1-3)

Burst of Flame (Single Target Damage)

This is one of the Magicians starting spells, and will act as your main form of damage till level 4. Since you automatically start with this spell, there is no need to buy it.

Flare (Ball of Light)

To some extent this is a very useless spell during the early levels. It has been said among others that this spell has some use to it, with locating targeted NPCs or players. When you cast this spell, a bright ball of light will shoot forth from your hands, and go in the direction of your target, or where you are looking. It is advised not to buy this spell, unless you feel some need to. 

Minor Shielding (Increase HP, AC)

This is one of the Magicians starting spells, and will increase both your Hit points and Armor class. It is crucial to keep this spell on at all times during lower levels, as without a pet, you will be taking melee damage. Since you automatically start with this spell, there is no need to buy it.

Reclaim Energy (Reclaims Pet for Mana)

When you get the ability to summon pets at level 4, this spell will allow you to desummon them and gain a certain amount of mana back. At lower levels, it does not have any noticeable effect in regaining mana, and only when you get up in the higher levels do you notice the difference. If you need to get rid of your pet, then use the command /pet get lost. It is advised you do not purchase this spell.

Summon Dagger (Summons Dagger)

When you create a Magician you start with a Dagger, which is good enough to get past the dreaded three levels till you receive your pet. However, if money is not a problem, then buying this spell will let you create daggers with a lower delay. Resulting to some degree, more damage over time. It is advised not to purchase this spell.

Summon Drink/Food (Summons Food)

A must buy for any low level Magician. I would only suggest buying these two spells, as they are extremely useful at all levels. You will soon find after level 3 you do not come back with food and water when you die. Once you run out, you will start noticing hunger pains and decrease in stamina and Agility. What is even worst for Spell casters is when you go hungry for a long period of time, you will no longer regain mana back. Summon Food, will summon one black bread, and drink will summon one globe of water.

Note: The best suggestion is to save all your money so that you can make sure you’ll have enough for an elemental and burn once you get to level 4. Without those, leveling will be near impossible.

Second Rank (Levels 4-7)

Burn (Single Target Damage)

The second spell in the series of fire based direct damage is Burn. This is a must buy, and with the higher damage output of 11-13dmg, will let you take down harder NPCs, faster then Burst of Flame. It is strongly recommended you purchase this spell.

Elementalkin Air/Earth/Fire/Water (Summon Elemental)

At last, you now have the ability to purchase and summon an Elemental. One of the furthermost spells for you is this one, it will significantly aid you in your combat ability, and many times your pet will let you gain levels much faster then someone without. It is with out most importance you purchase this spell.

Fire Flux (Area Effect Damage)

Not the best choice for a low level Magician, and a very dangerous spell if used improperly. It will damage anything around the caster, including your pet, so it’s advised not to cast this near stronger NPCs, such as Guards. A useful spell if you are in melee combat with more then one thing, but then if you are doing that, you’ll end up dead sooner or later. It is recommended not to purchase this spell.

Gate (Teleport to Bind Spot)

Many have claimed this is the best spell for any Casting class in the game, and it is easy to tell why. Gate will teleport you back to your bind location, or home point if you haven’t binded elsewhere. Not only is this spell good to travel back to where you were binded, but it can also get you out of some of the worst situations. Gate is a spell that tends to be always ready to use with other Casters, and you should have it memmed at all times. It is highly recommended you purchase this spell.

Sense Summon (Senses Summoned)

This spell will point you in the direction of a Summoned being. Most likely your pet, but will also point you towards other pets. Making the spell utterly useless in finding a rogue pet, but most people use it to increase Divination Skill.

Summon Bandages (Summon Bandages)

A very underused skill goes along with this spell, as describe above, Bind wound also needs bandages to work. Magicians start with the ability to summon two bandages at level 4 and will increase by 1 with every two levels you achieve. It is highly recommended you purchase this spell.

Summon Wisp (Summon Light)

This spell will summon a light source equal strength of a normal light stone. A useful spell for Magicians who are blinded at night, or to give them away to your human friends, other then that, there is not much use for this spell.

Conclusion

The Magician is a wonderful class to play, and one with many possibilities to role-play. It gives you a challenging experience but also fun at the same time. Becoming a Magician, and staying loyal to the ways, is a great honor, and you will soon earn the respect among your peers. The Magician class is not an easy road to travel, which goes for every other class inside EverQuest. The most important part is to pick a class and race you are willing to play to its full extent, and enjoy your time doing so.
