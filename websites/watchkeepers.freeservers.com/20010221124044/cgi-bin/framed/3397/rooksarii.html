Background:

    The young half elf’s eyes open slowly. Yet again it is time to find a new profession! Only yesterday he was a tailor, and due to an oversizing mishap for a grumpy dwarven warrior, he is now a retired tailor. Rooksarii could not understand his misfortune. Out of all the residents of Freeport, Rooks seemed to be the best at jumping from trade to trade. But what else was there for him? He had no mother or father to guide him into a profession. Indeed, the only parents he has known were the priests and priestesses of Erollisi Marr, and the goddess herself. Despite his never-ending admiration, friendship, loyalty and, of course, love for them all, he could not stomach the life of a priest. Rooksarii was a fellow of action. Though he would gladly spread the word of Erollisi to all he met, he could not handle the countless hours of prayer and meditation. Thus, the half elf with the purple eyes prepared himself for another trade........he heard that the O’donnars where looking for an apprentice brewer..........

    Although his life sounded pretty clear and unfettered, Rooksarii was troubled by a great deal of sorrow which he always kept inside himself. There were only two keepsakes that were left with him at the Temple of Marr all those years ago. One was a mask, divided into four sections, 2 black and 2 white, arranged in a checkered pattern. The other was a medallion depicting that same mask with a falcon perched upon it, clutching a rose in one talon and a saber in the other. This was all he knew of his parents. Though the priests and priestesses of Marr were the kindest foster parents he could have asked for, he had no brothers and sisters to call his own. The truth was that the Erollisi order had mostly human orphans, and no elven or half elven ones. Furthermore, few if any of the priests could tell him what life was like having the blood of an elf and a human course through him. And then there were the rumors.......

    Upon reaching the age of 10, Rooks was allowed to go into the market place to help one of the Brothers of Erollisi fetch some reagents for the incantations of the head priests and priestesses. He was filled with joy, for this was his first trip outside of the temple courtyard. The day drew on quite well until one of the Freeport Militia caught a glimpse of his deep violet eyes. " Time to die scum!!" barked the guardsman, blade raised and ready to end the young half elf’s life. Petrified with fear Rooks stood there, the color of his eyes blurred by the tears that began to fall from his eyes out of sheer fright.

    "Hold your ground guardsman!", the brother yelled

    "Are you mad?!?! That is a dark elf! Enemy to all of Freeport!", screamed the guardsman, eager for the kill and the bounty it would bring him.

    "Do you not see that the boy’s skin is of the color of human flesh? Not the deep hues of the dark elves!"

    "But those eyes!!!!"

    "A miracle of Erlollisi!"

    "A damnation Cazek Thul!"

    "Heathen!"

    "His days are numbered!"

    Then silence.............................

    Rooksarii awoke in his room at the temple. As he opened his eyes he saw an Wood elven woman standing above him. Brother Draden, the one who defended him, entered the room and softly said, "Rooks, in light of what happened, we are sending you to the Sister isle until the guards can be tamed, this is not your fault, people can be cruel sometimes....." The woman spoke, "Hello there young Rooksarii, I am Shorana, I shall take you to the Isle of Erollisi. You will be able to train in whatever you wish, or you can simply play about the island, you are young so there is no need to worry about any real training!" By nightfall, Rooksarii was fast asleep in one of the huts upon the Isle of Erollisi. During his one month stay there Rooks actually found pleasure in studying and training in daggers and rapier. When the time came to return to the city, Rooks could never really feel comfortable walking past the members of the Freeport Militia, not even to this day.

    What am I? Who am I? Countless thoughts raged through his head. On his walk home after being fired from the O’donnor’s ( one mead, two meads, twenty meads, whats the difference?) Rooksarii recalled all those questions that have troubled him since that day. It didn’t help that his skin seemed to darken a bit with age, almost a bluish color.......but that may have been his paranoia kicking in. The fact remained that he had a past, but no history. The mask and the medallion rested in a small box beneath his bed at the inn, holding all the secrets to his past and yet never sharing any of them.

    As he began to open the door to the inn, Rooks heard the sound of confrontation coming from the Southgate commons. Quickly and quietly he made his way to the gate and looked around the corner to the west. There he saw 3 of the Militiamen brutally kicking and beating a man one third the size of just one of them. Rooksarii felt he had to do something, to save a fellow being and to simply enrage the Militia.

    Rooksarii made his way to stand in front of one of the cactuses on the grounds, and once he knew all was set, he made his move.

    "Now look at what we have here. Three hulkish brutes proving to all the world that they can injure a smaller being. My hat goes off to you!"

    "Who goes there?" exclaimed the one of the guardsmen," Show yourself and I’ll let ye die quickly!"

    "Ahhh, more eloquent speech could not even be heard in Erudine! Tell me, was one of your parents an ogre and the other a troll?"

    "It was with that statement that the all three of the guardsmen began a steady trot towards Rooks with their weapons drawn. Seeing that they were on their way, Rooks set his plan into action.

    Now Rooksarii loved having the blood of elves running through him. This allowed him a certain degree of night vision not possessed by the Human guardsmen. As they charged him, he deftly moved the south, leaving the cactus he was standing in front of to the guards. Rooksarii circled around in an arch to the place where the fallen man lay, scooped him up and drug his powerless frame into the wall!

    It was several minutes before the beaten man came to, his wounds bandaged thanks to Rooks and his two day employment at an infirmary (a foot of bandage, a yard of bandage, what was the big deal?) When the man finally awoke, the first thing he did, without question, was draw a dagger and hold it tightly against Rooksarii’s throat.

    "First question, how do you know of these tunnels: Second, who are you and third why have you kept me alive? ANSWER!"

    "Well, second first, my name is Rooksarii, I used to play around in Freeport and I found these tunnels. Lastly, I didn’t much like to watch three men beat another being to a bloody pulp...ruins my day ya know."

    "Hmmm, by the looks of ya you couldn’t have fended them of with battle prowess, and I don’t feel magic running through your bones, so it must have been through trickery. Though you seem by no means the master of such arts, I feel with training you may just lift you to that level. Interested in a job?"

    From that day forth, Rooksarii was employed as a rogue.

    It seemed amazing to Rooks that here he was trying to make ends meet with every profession out there, and his one true calling was that of a rogue. To the amazement of all the guild members, Rooksarii flew through all the initiation stages not only faster than ever before seen, but with flying colors to boot. Within no time at all, Rooks was smuggling, picking pockets and "Liberating Treasure" with the best of them. Rooksarii did not consider what he did theft, for he never took a job unless the target was a stolen or illegally gained item itself, and he was to return it to the rightful owner. After some time, Rooks did not ask for money for such jobs, the thrill was all it took to repay him.

    All when on like gnomish clockwork for Rooks (occasionally there was a disturbance, but after much cursing and such, it worked just as good as it did before). There came a day however that would greatly impact his life. While wandering through the Freeport tunnel system trying to figure out if his map was accurate, a gust of wind from far down the tunnel blew his map into the water and the past forever. Now, being the calm fellow he was, Rooksarii proceeded to systematically try and find a way out of the tunnels........

    "HELP!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!"

    He called up every drainage pipe he could find. It was many hours until he finally heard a reply. The voice on the other end urged him to tell it where he was, or at least what the area around him looked like. Within minutes, before Rooksarii stood a fellow by the name of Drubone. Little did Rooks know, he had not only found a life long friend, but he had also found a mentor, teacher and a brother at arms and in spirit.

    On the way from the tunnels to the tavern, Drubone and Rooks traded joking banter of how Rooks had become trapped in the tunnels, and of life and professions. Drubone was full of not only useful and helpful hints regarding the life of a rogue, but his stories of far off lands and strange and bizarre creatures lit a fire within Rooksarii. As the night wore on, so grew this fire.

    When Rooks said his good-byes to Drubone and rested comfortably upon his bed, his inner being was ablaze with wanderlust. Why am I just sitting here, wallowing in my lack of knowledge of the world and myself? All these years have gone by and all I have to answer them with is more questions.

    If any where in that room at that time, they may have mistook Rooksarii’s eyes for two blazing purple flames. With not another thought, Rooksarii leaped from his bed and drew forth his old tailoring kit, still scratch from where that dwarf threw it at him. He found all the loose clippings of puma and lion skin he had collected off the tailoring shop floor and within the hour, he had himself a full leather armor suit! Quite good he thought! Why should he stay in the same town wondering where he is from when he can go out into the world and find out! He picked up the snake fang Drubone gave to him out of friendship, slung a backpack over his shoulder and left his inn room. All that would serve as an indication that the room once belonged to Rooksarii was the empty box under the bed. That night, there was a large coin purse left upon the steps of the Temple of Marr with a note that simply said "Thank you all". An innkeep received 3 months of back payments and an extra one for putting up with a half elf more than he should have. And the guards at Southgate related a story of how they slew an insolent whelp defending a rogue many years ago. All were unaware of the shadowed figure slipping out of the city wearing a black and white checkered mask, and adorned with a pendant depicting the mask, the falcon, the rose and the blade..........
