KINDARRA ANNPURNA

Character Name:

Kindarra Annapurna

Class:

Druid

ICQ:

NA

Background:

Well friends, I shall begin at the beginning, as any story should, and tell it as it should as my grandmother told me as a child.

Many thousands of seasons ago, our elven ancestors left their homeland in the Faydark spurned by revelations from Tunare that they were to spread Her word throughout the known lands and to the new race of men, humans. These pioneering wood elves were not just simple druids and rangers but bards, rogues, warriors too joined them in their journey across the Ocean of Tears, as did a select few high elves mostly warriors and clerics who found wisdom and strength in the Word of Tunare.

Upon landing at a new dingy settlement now known as Freeport, the ancient name long forgotten as the town was reputedly raised many times by orc raiders and other un-natural disasters caused in part by the lawlessness of its early citizenry, they were dismayed by the dust and clutter of the human settlement. Despite the inherent beauty of the surrounding desert and sandstone formations and the vibrantness of the deciduous forests and rolling hills of what we now call the Commonlands, the locals lent not an ear to Tunare’s teachings.

At the peak of their doubt whether these hu`mans could understand the way of Tunare, the leader of the trek, the legendary Ranger Kodiak Willowwhisper, had a vision from Karana, that the One would be sent to show them the way in this new land. The next morning, he woke feeling refreshed, and while foraging for their breakfast in the hills someone shouted in excitement, ‘Look, up there, on that hill.’ Suddenly the fog parted from around the figure on the hill and everyone gathered in and gasped at the sight. There stood without a doubt a human woman, gowned in free-flowing verdant robes, with silver shoulder length hair that sparkled in the stars and danced in the wind. In his excitement, Kodiak dropped the basket of berries he had just picked, and forgetting himself, ran up to her as a child would. Kodiak was tall for a wood elf, but had to look up to meet her compassionate gaze. She took his hand into her hands and simply said, ‘I am called Annapurna. Our new home shall be near the Plains of Karana, Surefall, a sacred grove that is waiting for our arrival.’

The band of wood elves and high elves found that their long voyage west across Antonica was free of the dangers of roving bandits, goblins, or the hideous creatures that filled the sky that they had heard talked about with so much fear in Freeport. All arrived safely to the grove. Annapurna never did answer when anyone asked about her past or where she came from or if she missed her family. She always sweetly smiled and said that they were her family. She never spoke of the future or the past, always of the present, and never was a woman of many words, but she did not need to speak as anyone could understand her by just feeling. Sure enough, she was family after the marriage with Kodiak was consecrated and they were blessed by children. Some elders of the band thought it to be impossible, yet there they were, bouncing babies who were neither human nor elf but both. Finally a semblance of village had formed in the grove, and the shouts of half-elf children playing could be heard.

The inhabitants of the grove had found a friendly ear in the human settlement of Qeynos, a simple folk who lived off the bounty of the sea and the farm.
