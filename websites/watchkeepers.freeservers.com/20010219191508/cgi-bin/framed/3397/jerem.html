JEREM ELEGOTH

Character Name:

Jerem Elegoth

Class:

Cleirc

ICQ:

NA

Background:

The Summons



“Excuse me sir?”

A voice, somewhere close, talking to him?

“Sir?  Are you Jerem Elegoth sir?”

A name, was it his?  Yes.

“You see, I have a message for Jerem Elegoth, and I am wondering if you are him?”

Jerem Elegoth.  It had been a long time since he had been called by his full name; so long he had almost forgotten it.  He slowly lifted his head from his crossed arms, wiping drool from his unruly beard.  He rubbed his eyes and peered into the darkness of the Lion’s Mane tavern.  He blinked and tried to focus on the voice, tried to understand who was speaking to him. He was confused, and reached for his glass trying to stall for time so his mind would clear.

“If you could please answer me sir, I am in a bit of a hurry, and the message is important.”

The glass was empty, and a quick check of his belt pouch revealed that his purse was not far behind.  His eyes finally cleared and the child standing before him came into focus.

“Jerem Elegoth.  Yes, I suppose that is me.  What do you want,” his voice was a low growl, his words slurred by the drink from the night before.

“I have a message from the Church of the Prime Healer, from the High Priestess herself.”

The High Priestess?  Sobriety came quickly and brought a severe headache with it, this was the last thing he needed right now.  He had been hiding from the cursed High Priestess for months and had no desire to see her now.

“Tell her I have no interest in what she wants to say, tell her to leave me alone,” he scowled at the child hoping to scare her away.

“I do not think she will accept that answer Sir.  She said it was urgent, she said she must speak to you.”

Jerem laughed loudly, his head instantly regretting it, “She wants to speak to me, or she wants to speak to Him?”

“I don’t understand sir, she said she wanted to speak with Jerem Elegoth and that I would most likely find you here.”

“Of course, there is no way you could understand, and I hope you never have to.  Tell your blessed High Priestess that He will be there as soon as He can, she will just have to be patient.”

“But she wants to see you….”

“She will understand the message, it is what she wants.  Now be off.”

Jerem watched the child run from the tavern as he waived his hand for the serving wench.  The High Priestess had not specified that he meet with her sober; it was time to drink, and drink hard.




The two paladins deposited their burden at the feet of the High Priestess, a low groan escaping from the dirty rags that now lay on the floor of the temple.  The Priestess sighed loudly and kneeled, quietly praying to the Prime Healer for strength.

“Jerem, I do not understand.  Why do you do this to yourself?  Why after being given such a great chance do you harm yourself like this?  Sometimes I wonder why you were chosen, and then I see the sickness within you and I understand.”

“Why must you continue to try and save me Jahnda?  Why can you not just leave me alone and let me be?  I did not ask for help, I did not desire any of this.  I only wanted to die; I wanted it to be over.  The pain is to much, the burden to great, please, leave me be.”

Jerem looked up through the tears in his eyes and began to shake as he saw Jahnda smiling down at him.  He did not deserve any of this, why did she insist on drawing him back to the church.

“I help you because you are in need Jerem.  I help you because He has seen something within you worth saving.  He has chosen you to help our lands.  He has chosen you to be his messenger to me.”

“Why can He not use the fish?  You yourself said He used to use the fish, that is their purpose on this land.  Why has He chosen to torment me when the blasted fish were created to deliver His message?”

“I do not know Jerem, He has not told me why, only that it must be so.  I am sorry if this saddens you Jerem, but somewhere inside I know there must be a good man, someone worthy of saving.  You have lost your way and He wishes to help you find it; I wish to help you find it.  You will understand someday, you will see it in yourself eventually.”

“Bah, you care little for me,” Jerem stumbled to his feet, “you only wish to speak to Him, to use me to listen to His words.  Fine!  I give up!  The sooner this is over the better, the sooner I can return to my drink.  Come to me Nife!  Come to me blast you!  Speak with your followers, then leave me alone!”

Jerem dropped to his knees and gave in.  He stopped fighting the presence that had lingered on the edges of his consciousness for the last week.  He had known Nife was there all along, waiting for him to give in.  Jerem bowed
his head and gave the god free passage, gave him entry into his body and his soul.  A great feeling of peace came over him, a sense of forgiveness and of healing; then all went black.




Jerem squinted into the sunlight passing through the window over his bed. His head was clear and his body at rest, no signs of the hangover he deserved were present, Nife had mended his body once again.  He lay back on the soft pillow and tried to weep but could not; something inside of him was different.  Jerem sat up, confused, trying to determine what was wrong with
him, what was different.  The door opened and Jahnda stepped in, a smile on her face.

“Good morning Jerem.  How do you feel?”

“What have you…..what has He done to me?”

“He has started the healing process my friend.  He has softened the old memories.  He has given your body new strength.  He has strengthened your mind.  He has chosen you to be one of his vessels in this land, he has chosen you to join the others and help Him heal the wounds of Norrath.”

“What do you mean?  I do not want any of this, I just want to…..”, but he was wrong, he felt different inside.  The memories were no longer strong; the pain they had caused him was less.  For the first time in ages he could almost think clearly, could almost focus without the faces of the dead haunting his mind.  In some strange way he had been healed.

“Nife is with you Jerem, you are a new man.  Through you he spoke to me as he has done before, but last night was different.  He has chosen you to be one of his champions, this time he has not left you, like before.  This time He is still within you.  He believes that as he heals you, and others like you, he will also heal the lands of Norrath.  He has begun the process with you Jerem, now you must continue his work and end the suffering of Norrath, only then can you be truly healed and suffer no longer.  You have been given a second chance.”

Jerem could feel it now, somewhere deep inside, a sense of intelligence, a presence within his mind that was not his own.  Jahnda spoke the truth, Nife was with him.

“But I…..how can I…..I don’t want any of……please I just…”

“Rest Jerem, sleep.  When you awake you will start your life anew, and leave your past behind.  Sleep Jerem Elegoth, and heal.”

Jerem tried to argue, he tried to fight back, but it was impossible.  All he wanted to do was sleep.




Jerem stepped away from the temple teleporter and turned to look at the High Priestess.  He reached down and smoothed the creases of his new temple garb, adjusting the club that now hung from his belt.  The first words of Nife were now in his head and he was ready to begin.

“I am proud Jerem Elegoth, I know that you will work hard to bring His will to our lands.  I know now that the healing of Norrath can begin.  Find others such as yourself to aid you in your quest.  May Rodcet Nife heal all wounds.”  Jahnda bowed deeply.

“I will do my best Jahnda, and I know Nife will aid me when I am in need,” Jerem held a hand to his heart and bowed before Jahnda.  With that he turned and headed into the busy streets of Qeynos, Jahnda waving as he left, a look of hope in her eyes.

Jerem turned the corner and looked over his shoulder to ensure the temple was out of sight.  He reached into his pocket and fished out the gold coins Jahnda had given him to help him start out.  He weighed the coins in his hand, three gold pieces, a hefty sum for one used to poverty such as he.

Jerem laughed to himself as he walked down the street towards the Lion’s Mane tavern.  Three gold coins was just enough to get him good and drunk.
