SMOKEYES DREAMNIGHT

Character Name:

Smokeyes Dreamnight

Class:

Rogue

Background:

After a long journey across the Commonlands of Antonica and risking the dangers of the patrols of Nektulos Forest you come to the gates of the underground city known as Neriak, home of the Teir'Dal, the Dark Elves. The guards at the gate seem to be on their toes so you bide your time hiding in the shadows.

As you sit there and wait you think to yourself, "I wonder if she is here… I wonder if she is as they say she is?"

The person you seek is said to frequent a bar in the Commons Quarter called "The Blind Fish" in the city proper. You hope that the map you took off that orc courier is correct about the hidden tunnels to the bar and that the gnome you spoke to on your way here didn't lie about the patrols in that area. You feel the hair on the back of your neck stand…

A rustle of bushes to the side draws your attention. A young Teir'Dal female stumbles out of the under brush and staggers to the gate. Blood covers her robes and a score or more gashes cover her body, she falls to the floor as the dagger slips from her grasp. In a weak voice she calls out "Sargtlin! Ogglin tir elgg!"

As the last word escapes her lips three zombies burst from the tree line and with ravenous claws tear the spark of light from the young elfs' eyes and rob her of her final breath. The creatures flesh dangle from decaying bones as the feast upon their fallen prey.

The guards seem to delight in the show as the step forward to engage the undead, weapons glowing in the moonlight. Two of the zombies cease their feasting and move slowly towards the guards and the third continues dining upon the now dead elf.

Now is your chance… You move with the speed and grace of a hunting cat towards the gate as the sounds of battle come from behind you. You enter into the realm of the Teir'Dal, the realm of death and hate.

Seems your information was correct, you make it to "The Blind Fish" with only a brief encounter with a troll merchant who seemed to only care that you didn't try to steal any of his, or was it her, wears. Pulling your cloak up you step into the bar.

Smoke fills the room of both levels of the place, the smell of hundred of rare herbs mingle with pipe tobacco and incense to create a smell that is very soothing and seems to remind you that you need a drink. You walk down the small flight of stairs to the lower level and gaze around at the windows that cover the north wall. Seems the bar is underwater and the view is from the windows is that of an underwater landscape. It is breath taking and scary at the same time. An impishly happy giggle draws your attention to a far corner of the room.

There leaning on a brewing barrel holding a large sewing kit is a young female Teir'Dal. She is dressed in a rage tag set of armor, most of which seems to be some type of metal links. The armor is crafted so well that if forms to her body as though it were her own skin. Strapped to her hips are two very nasty daggers, a glowing rapier and a whip covered in spikes. Her body is as if sculpted from fine onyx, ever muscle tight and smooth as thought a master craftsman spent his life forming a living being from the dark stone. Her hair is as white as the pure snow that falls in the cold winter's morning and looks as though it might be just as soft.

"Are you going to order or take up space? Cause if it's space ya be wanting there is plenty of room on the other side of the window for your kind." Says the barmaid in a voice that is soft as a flute yet with an undertone of a dagger in the night.

You didn't even hear her walk up on you, you didn't think you where there that long looking at the young Teir'Dal. You take a second before you ask for a white wine and hand over what you know to be double the price and hope that the barmaid doesn't call the guards about your presents in the city. She returns a moment later with the drink and no change smiles and walks away. Seems you are safe as long as your coin holds out.

Pulling the hood of your cloak down more and taking a sip of your wine you go back to your observation of the lady who held your attention so completely before.

She is smiling at a young female Halfling standing before her dressed in what appears to be freshly made set studded and reinforced leather armor. Every piece unmarred by battle or time, from boots to cap. Crafted to fit the halfling almost perfectly, leaving some room for hidden equipment it seems.

"Thank you Miss Smoke! Tis a grand set, how much is it that I owe you?" the young halfling ask with a smile of gratitude on her face as bright as a new day's dawn.

"For you young one, just the cost of materials. You have paid enough of a price come'in all the way here to get a set. I would have been back in young home in a few days, but I do admire your efforts in come'in here just to ask for my help. So for that I shall only charge you half." Smoke says in a voice that is almost a whispering yet reaches every corner of the room. "Besides, you are a sister of the arts and of Bristlebane. You should be take'in care of cause no one takes care of us so someone has ta. No go before someone sees you here, not safe for your folk here about."

"Travel safe Miss Smoke, I will remember your kindness and generosity." With that the halfing bows and hands Smoke a pouch of coins and sneaks off towards the back door.

With an almost childlike smile and the speed of a sprite, Smokes' hand flashes towards the halfling and she turns to go. With the expertise that must come with hours of practice she does a reverse pick pocket and places the pouch back into the halflings backpack as she makes her way to the door.

"Travel safe and dream well young one. May your skill prevail where others fail." Smoke says while holding back a giggle.

She places the sewing kit back into her case and turns to open the panel on the brew cask. With a sparkle in her eyes she reaches in and removes five small white bottles from the cask and places them into her pouch.

With one single flowing motion she flings a sixth white bottle right at you "So, are ya go'in ta paint a portrait or just start at me all night friend?" Her eyes lock with yours…

Her eyes… This is the first time you have seen them, peering right into your soul and stealing a single breath from your body. At least some of the tails are true; her eyes are lovely as life, yet dark as death. A swirling mass of day and night, a patch of darkness that seem to have the light of a thousand candles flickering in the breeze of eternity just at it's edge. You wonder if you will ever get that breath back, yet knows that she has stolen something that can not be regained.

She walks towards your table with the grace of a breeze on a spring morning, pulls a chair from the next table and sits before you. Her eyes never leaving yours. "So what is it I can do for ya young friend, why is it ya searched me out in this dark place?"

You find it difficult to speak looking into her eyes yet you can not look away. "Are you the Teir'Dal rogue known as Smokeyes Dreamnight?"

"Yes I am. Now I am sure that you didn't sneak past guards and travel halfway from where ever to ask me just that." She says with a smile that her eyes also mimic.

Clearing your throat you say why you are here in a quiet whisper. "My guild has charged me with a task. We are collectors of history and keepers of truths. Your name is known by many, most call you friend. You have help many, even at great cost both financial and spiritual. You are no ordinary Teir'Dal as most have portrayed your kind, nor are you common for one of your profession. We would like to know if you wish to share your story with history and us?"

Her eyes… Those eye that seemed to hold the mystery of life only a second ago, the eyes that held your life with in them are not the same anymore. There is no sign of life, no sign of death. They are now a void, which seems to devour everything, yet reflects all. "It is time…"

"My true name is Cy'eti Maiveldrin, the name give'in to me by my mother upon my birth. It means "Onyx eyes of deaths shadow". My family name is Firreth'Mai'Atria, which means "Deaths dark dancers" in the tongue of the common man.

"My family are true followers of Innoruuk. Necromancers, Shadow Knights and Clerics all. Proud and feared for their power and connection to the Prince of Hate.

"My mother is a High Necromancer named Ama'Reth, feared by her apprentices and the family for her power. My father is one of Innoruuks' more powerful knights named Mal'Thal Ael'Ual, he stands at my mothers side out of loyalty, fear and lust. Both to serve the will of the God of Hate. Together they sired six offspring. I am the second child, yet eldest living child.

"My eldest brother, Ty'Rath, was sacrificed by my parents hands at Innoruuks will. And together they shared his heart in a ceremony of death and hate. The last thing my brother saw was that of our parents eating of his still warm and beating heart. I watched it all; I held the dagger they used.

"My younger twin brothers sat and watched in glee at the sight and reveled in the blood that spilled from the altar, even at the tender age of six they knew their place in our realm. My sister didn't understand, but sat in awe at the magic power that was unleashed by this ritual; I feel that might have damaged her for she was only four seasons old.

"He was only sixteen upon his death, it was his birthday. His age of ascension to life as a full member of our society. This was his gift from my parents. It was my thirteenth season of life, but I did not want to be part of this life.

"Every day I wake with the look my brothers eye held upon his death, his and the others that followed. Each life my family sacrificed a piece of me was lost with them.

"My other siblings seemed to flow with the power of our dark god. The twins at the age of eight seasons were sent to the Lodge of the Dead with my father to start they're training. They were to follow in my fathers' path and become knights of our dark god. I knew I would never see them again for once one became part of the lodge they are lost to the world.

"My sister never did recover from the exposure to the magic unleashed the day of my brothers death. At the age of seven seasons she was already able to manipulate magic even apprentices only dream of. Yet she knew neither what she did nor how she did it. My mother, pregnant with my yet unborn sister, was very happy with my other siblings… Then there was me.

"I did not share their blood lust, their love for our dark ways. I did not feel the hate that was the foundation of Teir'Dal race. It was my sixteenth season of life and I had yet to swear my life to their god, nor chosen a path to follow, much to my parents disdain.

"When one of my kind turns sixteen season old there is a ceremony known as Iaire'Lanna, 'Daughters chosen day'. A coming of age ceremony. The time of my ceremony was near and I felt that I could not take part of it…

"For most of my younger years I would play hide and sneak with our family guards, they always had a hard time catching me. That helped play a part in what I knew I had to do. I had to escape, I had to find a life, I had to leave these walls and find what it was that would make me feel whole. I gathered together some thing I thought I would need and climbed down into the cellars. Sneaking past a few of the more inept guard I went into the 'stalls' my family kept our slaves in and that is when I saw something that brought a spark to my heart.

"On a recent raid on a wood elf camp, my families forces took a few slaves to help in the mines and fill in the places left open by the most recent ceremony. There were five adult males, three females who would no doubt be give to the commanders of the house guard and a teenage male about my age. It was his eyes that froze me in my tracks. Even in that dark place, with death all around, I could feel the light in those eyes.

"He was rather small even for a wood elf, a wiry frame with tight muscles. His cloths, even though caked with mud and blood, hug loosely from his form. Short blond hair was matted to his head, the color of hay in the morning sun. His eyes so deep and alive, were the color of the morning sky and held a life of their own. Even in this place of death there was joy in his eyes.

"I watched from the shadows as the guards move the prisoners into the cells. The young elf stood away from the other, it was almost as if they didn't want him with them. The guards locked the others into the cells leaving the young elf in the middle of two of the guards. The leader of the raiding part said 'He is too small for anything… Kill him and throw his body to the ghouls.' One of the guards drew his sword as the other forced the elf to his knees.

"Stop!" I yelled as I drew myself from the shadows, I do not know why I felt compelled to do that, but something inside me wanted to save this elf. The guards were startled for a moment, once they saw who it was they stayed their actions.

" 'Mistress Cy'eti, this is no place for you, a lady of your blood.' He gave pause for a moment 'Unless it is you who wishes to kill this decrepit thing.' The head guard finished. I could see that he was afraid of me, even though I showed no powers they must have thought I was hiding what I did have. After all, they could never catch me so I guess they thought I had the power of shadows. They called me 'Shadow Child' when they didn't think I was in earshot.

"No, let him live. I want him for myself… I am a lady of the house and my will is law is it not." I told them and took a step forward. "Release him to my watch." They were a bit in shock, but agreed to do as I wished. I looked down at the elf; he was staring right into my eyes... I could feel his fear and something else, he was smiling at me with his eyes.

" 'Thank you Lady with the eyes of smoke.' He whispered once the guards were not looking. He spoke our tongue, it was something I did not expect. Nor did I expect such a title.

"I took him to the bath house out in back of the family house. The guards still watching and whispering from the distance. I wonder what they thought I was going to do, it did not matter to me. Something about this elf had taken a hold on me, what it was I did not know, nor did I care. I took him into the house and closed the door, at this time of night there was no one there. I undressed him and help him into the tub and started to wash him.

"Something stirred within me as I massaged the kinks out of his shoulders. I felt something new, something I have never even felt before. A warmth where my heart, my soul, was hiding from the horrors around me. I looked into his eyes once more, there was life. I have seen wood elves before but never felt this. This one was more then an ordinary elf, he was part of me. A fire I needed to be whole, even in this place where nothing but pain existed I found something I needed. I took a sponge and rinsed the rest of the dirt from his hair. He turned and our eyes locked once again.

" 'Why did you call me that?' I ask as I looked deep into his soul. His inner fire mingled with my soul. 'You had to have understood what the guards called me.'

" 'Because I saw you in the shadows, and when you came up to save me from death. I felt your presents and knew who you were.' He reached out and held me in his arms. 'When I looked into your eyes I knew there was something with in you. Your eyes, your soul has the light of joy yet the darkness of a thousand deaths. You area a mystery to your own world and your eyes show that.'

"He gently pressed his lips to mine, I knew I should have pushed back but something about him, he was part of me. I wanted to be whole, I wanted to be part of his world. He held me close and pulled me down into the bath with him.

" 'You are one with the shadows, not evil but will never be part of good.' He stroked my hair and held my face in his hands. 'You are the candle to the star, The Ladies favored…' His eyes locked with mine. 'Your are the mist that surrounds truth, the light that covers a lie.' He gently removed my gown and held me, eyes never leaving mine. 'You are the loved and hated by all…'

"My head started to swim, I was part of him, part of his world even for this short time.

" 'Bristlebanes's Lady has chosen you my love. The hand for night and shadow, the hands of friendship and the wielder of the dagger in the dark. You are Eti'firret, 'Death Shadow'. Your path is now chosen Lady of Smoke. Embrace your future and know that it is your will.' The sound of his voice and the look in his eyes told me my life was now chosen, and I welcomed it. I gave into my feelings of love, I embraced my destiny and knew I would never be."

"My mother watched from the shadows and knew what she must do.

"After that night I set the young wood elf up as a house boy till I could find some way to get him to safety. I did not see him after that night knowing that to do so would insure his death. My ascension was a week away.

"The night before my formal ascension I knew that I had to get away before it was too late, before my soul, my now free soul, would be given unto the god of Hate. I ready myself for the escape and set out to the young wood elf's room. My stealth had improved for some reason, it was as if I was smoke drifting through the halls, unseen and unheard.

"The door to his chamber was locked so I pulled a pin from my hair and set to the lock. With a light click the tumblers moved into place and released the door. But once I opened it I saw that my elf friend wasn't there. But my sister was.

" 'Mother says you are to come with me.' Her eyes glowed brightly in the dark room. Due imparts to the magic that coursed through her veins. I was about to scold her for being in here, for giving demands of her elder sister, when she said 'Mother said your time is now and you must come.' And she headed out the door to the family temple.

"I followed, I do not know why I did. A feeling started welling deep inside my breast. I do not know if it was fear or excitement. I just felt change within me. I knew something was about to happen, something that would change me completely.

"The door to the temple opened at our approach and we entered. The light of thousands of candles and incense burners lighted it. The tapestries of hate covered the walls and the smell of old blood hung in the air. My sister walks up the altar to my mothers' side, two hideous ghouls at either side.

" 'Welcome my daughter to Iaire'Lanna. Come and become part of your destiny.' She held the dagger in her hand and offered it and beckoned me to the altar. 'Come my daughter and take this gift and be one with your own kind, the true masters of all!' The doors to the temple closed behind me. I turned to look and saw four rotting corpses putting the bar on the door. They were all wood elves.

"I was trapped, it seemed that I had lost what I had found. But I knew I would not go out without facing 'our dark god' and spitting in his face. With the fire I had found I marched up the steps to the alter and grab the dagger from my mothers hand and look down into the face of my 'gift', it was then that I knew I was truly lost. I looked down into the eyes of the young wood elf. His body was ridged and lifeless but his eyes were still alive. I saw fear and something more in them.

" 'Did you think I would not know!' my mother cackled at me, I could feel the hate welling with in me, I wanted blood. 'Did you think you could ever hope to be not of my control!' Rage, pain, and anger… The fire within me grew a fire more powerful then I could imagine. 'Yes my daughter! Feel the hate! You are ours! Now and always!' I thrust the dagger into my mothers' heart…

"I felt the hot blood running down my hand. Felt the beating of the heart that I impaled my dagger in, taste the blood that had sprayed onto my face. But something was wrong, I heard laughter? My mothers and sisters laughter. I felt a warmth swell inside of me as I looked at my hand still clutching the dagger, the dagger that was hilt deep in the wood elf's' chest.

" 'It is your destiny now my love. You are the Lady of Smoke, The Ladies Chosen. Neither alive nor dead, awake nor asleep' A tear of blood rolled down his cheek. ' Dream well, for dreams are your reality.' His eyes locked with mine as I watched the fire drain from them, they never closed.

" 'NO!' I turned to my mother, to rip her heart from her with my hands. I lunged to my mother, but the ghouls had attacked me before I took my first step and froze me with the power of their unlife. The chill of death grabbed me. 'It was my path to chose! Why!?' I screamed at my mother.

" 'It was your choice my dearest daughter. This is what you wanted and what I wanted. This was your sisters' ceremony. She was the one who controlled your mind, she is one of Innooruuk children and the Eldest daughter of our family now. You were her gift and now you are no more.' With that she held my sisters' hand and walked down the steps, once at the bottom she turned to me once more. 'Farewell daughter, you are nothing but mist in my presence and smoke in my memory.' As they walked to the door the wood elf zombies opened it to let the mother and daughter out then turned and walk up the steps to where I was frozen by the ghouls' touch. I welcomed death for I knew I had no life in me anymore. And I never even knew his true name.
