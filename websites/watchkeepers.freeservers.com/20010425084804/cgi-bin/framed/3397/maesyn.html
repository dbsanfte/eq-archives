Background:

    Born to a life of wealth and privilege that most children of Freeport could only dream of, Maesyn and her twin sister Lini could have been considered by most to be spoiled children. Never did they go to bed with pangs of hunger, or fear what creature might awaken them in the night. In the house of their father, Ruben Shadowdancer, the twins grew into fine young ladies.

    They were educated by tutors from all corners of Norrath, in the delicate arts that their father deemed suitable: sewing, dancing, cooking, and singing among others. He asked only that they never question his word, and that when the time came, they marry and give him grandchildren.

    Under the loving iron thumb of their father, Maesyn’s mother had no say in the family affairs. When they were in their early teens, their father began to bring around young men that he deemed suitable for them to marry. The lasses bore these visits well, but one young man’s wooing words struck a chord in Maesyn’s heart one day. The lad went on and on about how he would protect her and save her from the trials and tribulations of the world. Maesyn was not so deluded that she didn’t realize that she was a spoiled merchant’s daughter, but at that moment she wondered if there was anything more to her shallow existence. She spoke in depth about this with her sister, and they sought out the monks of the Ashen Order and began to train their bodies.

    The years passed easily, and the girls grew into beautiful women and their bodies into deadly weapons. The secret was kept from their parents until they were brought on a mysterious trip to the Tree City of Kelethin. Her mother bade her to dress in her finest gown, and Maesyn was led into a decorated grove. There was a wedding to occur, and it was only as her father led her down the aisle to the groom that she realized that the wedding was to be her own. Her father released her and she looked into the eyes of her intended…. What she saw there was no desire to be married, and Maesyn let out a booming laugh of relief for it. Her betrothed, a young wood elf named Larch smirked back at her, and then they moved in sync to vacate the premises.

     Maesyn slept under the stars of Faydwer that night, and in her sleep she found what she had been seeking since she first doubted her existence, she found some self-respect. Lini tracked her down the next day, and the twins returned to their parents, informing them of their training with the Ashen Order. Their father was dumbfounded. He threw down curses upon their heads, and ranted about the worthlessness of bearing daughters. Maesyn just bore his words with a slight smile. She was in another place now, with a new beginning ahead of her. And so, with the training of the monks of the Ashen Order, and the wisdom of Quellious at their side, the twins headed off into the world.

    And how will one find the spoiled merchant’s daughter now? Free. But even freedom has its price, and Maesyn has paid dearly for it. But those are other stories, for other times.
