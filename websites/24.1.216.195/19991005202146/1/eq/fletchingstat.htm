Bowyering


It's pretty simple here:
Insert a Stick - Hicory, Elm, Ashwood... and some String - Hemp, Linen, Silk and you have a bow.
Don't try to add the whittling tool unless you make an Elm or better bow it just eats the components.
Haven't tried a Plane or Cams yet - as I KNOW I will fail.

Now you may know as a bowyer that the demand for bows is weak.  Most people will want to dabble in archery - rangers esp.  But they freak at even the most modestly priced Elm bow (3-4 pp) many don't understand the proper use/advantage of having bows around. So you can squeak out an existance on Hickory or just use Bowery to improve the bows you and your close allies use.  I basically train up my arrow making then attempt a few bows.  With the successes I trade out my 2 warrior friends and 1 rogue friend bows.  I always try and make the next better bow.  This means I don't have trivial numbers for Bow types but a good example is:

First bow made Hickory-Twine skill 12 first try.
Next 2 Hickory bows - Skill 21ish both no failure
All 3 of my Elm bow attempts have been at skill 25.  I had a Twine fail.  Then suceeded on a Twine and a Linen.  I will be trying a Silk soon.

Line quality - As you go up in quality line 2 things happen - DMG goes down 1 point and speed goes up 4 points.
Whittling blade - I have not successfully used a whittling blade, but as an avid bowyer I pour over many leads to what it does.  I did find a page that says a blade has a similar effect as a line upgrade.  -1 damage, -4 speed.  When I can confirm this I will update the page.

Archery

Basics of archery - WHY?
Why would anyone pay a minimum of 1s for purchased arrows and send them into never, never land by shooting them at a MOB?

I'll give you my groups reasons.
We are pretty much a bashem group - Enchant, Bard, Rogue, Monk, Warrior.
With the Rogue and the Warrior having bows. we can do the following:
I as a Bard with maxed out Hand to Hand can play a super healing song on my mandolin while dishing out a constant stream of 4-5 fast DMG. - not enough to get the MOBs attention
The Monk, Rogue and Warrior step in and Bash away.
When The Rogue or Warrior gets dinged up quite a bit they step out behind the Bard and heal up... all the while twanging away at the MOB.
When they heal up some they come back in and the other leaves.

Why does this work so well?  The monk and bard hit alot, but nothing amazing in actuall DMG per hit. So for the most part the MOB ignores us with the Backstabing rogue or the Tauting Warrior all over him.  We also share damage quite evenly - if taunt is done well - and can use the bard's even healing after the battle for quick recovery.

Apparently there is a melee weapon that is an archery weapon.  pine needle/tiny dagger (1dmg/33delay)?  This can be used to raise your neglected archery skill - but remember only the monsters that give exp will raise your combat skills, so find a tolerant group or bang on someone elses monster. The location of these pine needles is in Kithikor woods.. Look for the 4 building camp on the west side. The NE cabin has them.. THEY SELL FOR 6c.  DO NOT PAY ALOT FOR THESE.  They help raise archery tremendously.  I have 3 arching playmates that maxed their skill in about an hour with one of these.
