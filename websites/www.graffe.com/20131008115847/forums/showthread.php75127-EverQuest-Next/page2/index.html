    Originally Posted by PPatty

    Aren't all mmos like that now? I remember, even in vanilla wow (not really played it since), you could just run and try everything because there was no death penalty at all. The cost of coin was a joke (until higher levels, but penalities should go from the beginning, not just the end). Every mmo since EQ has followed your wish. Not really rewards in terms of dying, but certainly not penalized.

    And without a penalty at death, there is no "risk", certainly not if you are rewarded upon death either. And what affection value do you get from the loot/reward if there is no risk in obtaining it? Guess it would be similar to buying items from an auction house.

    EDIT - Remove action houses. Remember selling from the EC tunnels? Slate to tunnel, skellies to tunnel. I sold so many stein of moggoks there. People would freak the f* out if they didn't have any automated selling abilitiy, but I do think that going away from the completely automated auction houses with UI huds, no personal interaction, min maxing sales/profits, would benefit the community in the long run. MMOs of today is like a subway cart in the morning heading to work. Dozens of people around you, 95% with their own headphones, looking at their own screen/pad/phone, and they will get jumpy if you try to interact with them. I am in my bubble. Do not disturb. Simply make MMOs less realisitic, and make interaction among people normal again.

    - End edit.

    Things I think would help build a community
    - Death penalities and a harsh world that can be lessened by the community (EQ had rez + corpse summon, find, etc).

    - No maps. Learn directions, ask for directions. Quite often in EQ you got help/helped find camp sites, etc. Early EQ I can still remember the layout of the zones. No clue at all in the later expansions where all I stared at was an arrow on a map. And exploring was fun, but felt dangerous.

    - Dangerous zones, goes in with the death penalty. There might be a wonderful level spot or named with loot in the middle of it, but there should be a risk getting there.

    - NO DYES!!!!!!! ffs. Let loot become identifiable again, to where you can see what someone is wearing and think "ohh". Early on in EQ, a cleric with a purple breastplate = someone who had raided PoH and most likely knew what they were doing. After dyes, bronze breastplate + purple dye = PoH. This isn't so much a community builder as a .... I don't even know. I hated dyes when implemented in EQ, and I still hate them. Feel free to add them to crafting, but it should never be possible to get a loot drop and dye that.

    In my opinion, the only way to make a community is to build a world which not only requires it, but helps you. In EQ, if you played your class badly, the game (or should I say, the servers) were so small that you would be noticed. Buy your character? PL much? The game was hard/difficult/grindy enough to where you would be forced to learn your class by the end. Solo was hard there, so that was something that came with learning your class. Kiting, pet chaining, etc. That seems to be the biggest difference of the mmos since EQ. Where solo is first, then groups second. Switch that around again, and communities will build. They will have to. Granted, doing this would no doubt lower the sub numbers, and as such, I doubt we will see a game like that again until someone with enough money can make it without a publisher, as easier = more subs.

    Re: EverQuest Next

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    People would freak the f* out if they didn't have any automated selling abilitiy, but I do think that going away from the completely automated auction houses with UI huds, no personal interaction, min maxing sales/profits, would benefit the community in the long run.
    WoW had the Trade channel. It's a cesspool of humanity. If you want the personal touch, try to contact people who buy your stuff, or who are selling stuff, and upsell them/haggle with them. I entered into a 2000 g/day contract with one guild leader by doing just that. The tool is not the end of the process.

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    Death penalities and a harsh world that can be lessened by the community (EQ had rez + corpse summon, find, etc).
    Requiring people to cling to one another to make any progress in a game world is not community. Communities should form by choice, not by force. I think everyone here who raided in EQ knew at least one jackass warrior or nimrod cleric that they never wanted to be guilded with, but had to in order to have numbers for raids. That's not the sort of memory a good game should make. Note that there's also one very bad consequence of making a game promote strong bonds: when the leaders of a group leave your game, the rest of that group tends to follow. And I'm not sure that guild leaders are the audience you want to strive to please.

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    No maps.
    I'm pro-maps, but with two caveats: one, you need to explore to open up the map. Two, there should be features in the world that don't show up on the map (or at least the big world map), so there's still an element of "how do I get there" and exploration has value. Think things like important ground spawns, including resource nodes.

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    NO DYES!!!!!!!
    Is it OK for your character to look like a joke because of all the mismatched armour pieces they're wearing? When I joined my raid guild in EQ, one person told me I'd done a great job dressing for shock value, from all the garish colours down to the Viking helmet on my wee dwarven head. The thing was, I wasn't dressing to shock. I had on the best possible pieces someone without a raid guild could have. And I looked like crap. I agree with you that the freedom to make yourself bright pink is one a game can live without, and even WoW transmog allows for some pretty silly crap. But I think there has to be some way for a player to at least get their gear looking matched, like a "you can transmog armour to a given pattern based on your ilvl" or something like that. Because even with artists trying to make all gear from a given tier look relatively matched, you still get some clown costumes.

    Originally Posted by Droigan
    In my opinion, the only way to make a community is to build a world which not only requires it, but helps you.
    I believe that a game should help the player form bonds if the player wishes to form bonds, but should never mandate them save at the very highest levels of the game. Take WoW. You have the option of forming RealID friends with anyone in the game, which allows players from different servers to group together, chat, and raid all but the most current content. But you don't need friends to do those things. You do need friends for organized raiding, and for high end PvP (Arena/Rated BGs), but you can putter along one tier below that, see almost all the content, and be happy. Friends make some things easier, and they make everything more fun, but they aren't mandatory to play the game.

    Also worth noting: the EQ community was fairly toxic. For people who did any kind of raiding, you had your guild, which you stayed with whenever possible; the competition, whom you hated with a passion for all the dickish things they'd do to you at the drop of a hat (and which you'd do to them if you got the chance); and everyone else, whom you didn't know and didn't want to group with because they likely sucked. Everything revolved around fierce competition for limited resources, which created conflict and hatred. I like helping people - it's why I was a healer in EQ. Hoping the group next to us will wipe so my group can pick up three more spawns and a named? Screw the system that makes me think that way. I can say honestly there's very little about EQ that I miss in any way, shape, or form.
