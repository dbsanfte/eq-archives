Absor Responds To Wizard Concerns:
Some wizard questions were answered by Alan on the Developer Forums -

While I'm on this hotbutton Wizard topic - Wizards all over would really appreciate it if you would give some very straightforward yes/no type answers to the following questions, to help them decide whether to delete their account now or not, so stop beating around the bush...

I'm not beating around any bushes. I'm telling you what I've told you before, until we're done balancing things there is nothing to tell. But I'll give you several probably and maybe answers, if that helps. Just remember that until all the class balance issues are done, NONE of this is a promise.

1 - Will our mana regen improve?

Probably not.

2 - Will our dmg/time improve?

Probably not. Heck, wizards have a great damage over time ratio and this isn't even something that we're looking at improving.

3 - Will our dmg/mana improve?

Bane spells. Other than that, I don't know.

4 - Is our single most primary attribute now "Masters of Transportation" for balancing purposes?

Isn't now, hasn't been in the past, probably won't be in the future. Just because you are the 'best' at travel does not mean that travel is what you do best.

5 - If the above isn't a clear yes, is Damage considered the top priority for us, or at least even with transport?

There is no 'top priority' for any class. That's very misleading question.

Let's look at another class, to help keep perspective.

When talking about balancing, let's say, Magicians, yes, the first thing we'll probably look at will be their pets. But will that be the 'priority' for balancing that class? Who knows. What we'll do is look at everything that the class can do, find the holes, and see if we want to fill them. If we do, then yes, it's likely we'll again look at pets for filling those holes.

But you can't fill damage dealing holes with teleportation spells (in much the same way that you don't use a broad thing like mana regeneration to fill damage dealing holes).

For some reason people think that we are using the new teleportation spells to as a balancing tool, despite the fact that I've mentioned that these spells are going in as part of a long term plan unrelated to class balance. Yes, we're including them in the package that is the wizard when we look at the class as a whole, but it's pretty silly to even suggest that we would use a teleportation spell to help wizards sustain their damage potential against high hit point NPCs…

Thanks,
Cabot of Tholuxe Paells

Ok, that was of topic and I ranted a bit to boot. Sorry about that.

Alan

Translocate Bug:
If you are experiencing it, you are not alone. This bug acts exactly like the group bug where it shows a person in group but they cannot join. In order to stay away from bugging yourself and others, I suggest waiting for your first translocated person to reach their destination, give you a confirm, ungroup, then you can invite more. If you invite someone while a recent translocated person is still zoning, the probability of getting group bugged is high.

Thanks to Ellissa of Tunare for the heads up.

Patch Message:
Todays patch has been placed. Here's some things that directly effect us as wizards -

  • Translocation spells: Five new Wizard 'push' spells have been added (Translocate: Fay, Tox, North, Combine and Group). Translocate spells send the group to the location, while the Wizard remains behind. Translocate Group sends everyone in the group (except the Wizard) to their own bind points. Translocate Group can be found on NPCs in Velious (and they won't give them up easily). The others are available on an exclusive vendor. All Translocate spells (even the original Translocate) have confirmation boxes that must be accepted before the target will be sent. And Wizards can no longer Translocate themselves.
  • Some lower 39th and 44th level Velious spells have had their drop rates increased.
  • The Eye of Zomm series of spells will no longer work in Kedge Keep. This change was made to prevent an exploit.
For the full patch message, click here. I do suggest reading the full message. There have been a lot of changes made.
