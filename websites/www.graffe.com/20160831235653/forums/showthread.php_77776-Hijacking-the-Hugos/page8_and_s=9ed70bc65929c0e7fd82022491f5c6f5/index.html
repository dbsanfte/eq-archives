    Re: Hijacking the Hugos?

    Originally Posted by Chu
    The worst trolls are almost universally hard-right conservatives, in other words, and they generally care about their pet causes with a breathtaking fervor that their enemies can’t possibly hope to match.
    Originally Posted by Delores
    Which part gave you the most problems?
    Ok let's see:

    The worst trolls are almost universally hard-right conservatives

    This statement presumes the speaker has all knowledge of all trolling. Chu's evidence doesn't even scratch the surface. He also uses a semantically loaded term, which shifts a bit even in the few paragraphs where he uses it. In the previous paragraph he defines trolls more or less narrowly as anyone who vote-brigades for spurious reasons. Then in the very next sentence he changes the definition to say: in other words, and they generally care about their pet causes with a breathtaking fervor that their enemies can’t possibly hope to match.

    So which is it? They do it for the lulz or because of the breathtaking fervor of their causes? Those are mutually exclusive. Unless getting a fat loser on a date with Taylor Swift is somehow a right-wing conservative clause.

    A statement this ambiguous and inconsistent simply cannot be true. It's pure rhetoric, playing directly to reader's existing prejudices to associate "right-wing" "trolling" "pet causes" and to mock them as irrationally fervent. It's propaganda, and it's not just a simple hyperbole for effect, it's dishonest propaganda that deliberately tries to shape perceptions away from more reasonable truths.

    Finally, the title of his article calls the Sad Puppies deranged trolls. From what I can tell (the article rambles) the sum total of Arthur Chu's evidence supporting the name-calling in his thesis is this paragraph:

    To vote on the Hugos you have to either know and care a ton about science fiction–or you have to be convinced that science fiction is part of the vast liberal conspiracy arrayed against you and make a disingenuous post calling you and your friends “Sad Puppies” over said liberal conspiracy. $40 is a lot of money to pay to express your opinions, even strongly held ones, about fiction you love–but it’s a cheap price to stick it to liberal pro-diversity elitists you hate.

    Freeping with clicks in order to troll the liberals is one thing–freeping to give money to a horrible person to troll even harder, another. But giving money en masse to an organization you dislike so you can subvert and take over that organization? Who’d go that far?
    And he links to Larry's original 2013 post about the Hugos, which omits all of the back and forth that has happened since that initial post and either way, certainly does not convince me that Larry is a deranged troll, not that such a thing is provable anyway given the ambiguity of those terms. Hence: accusations of name-calling, because that's what it is.

    Then there's the whole story about the Washington Monument, which, like most of the article, has nothing to do with his accusation that the Puppies are trolling the Hugos.
    "Browbeat - Slate's Culture Blog" (again, bolding mine). You know what a blog is, right?
    The distinction really does not matter if people treat it as a news source rather than op-ed. It's also not relevant to the point about the effect of leveraging the reach and credibility of sites like Slate to support only one side of the debate.
