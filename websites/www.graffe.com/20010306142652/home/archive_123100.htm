Absor on the Status of Lures:
Absor took some of his time to post what is going on with our lure line of spells on the Official Gameplay Forum -

I just wanted to summarize what is happening and what has happened with Lure spells (hopefully I understand it).

Three weeks ago, the resist rates for NPCs in regards to Lure was fixed. This means, basically, that they will resist Lures that they are supposed to resists (a fire creature might now resist a fire-based Lure that it did not resist before). This change really shouldn't have affected most wizards, once they realized the change and adjusted tactics accordingly. It is my fault for not informing you of the change. For that I'm sorry.

However, all the talk about changes to this line of spells brought about a more in-depth look at how they work, leading to this post by one of our programmers (given in full here for your enjoyment):

"After looking closely at the way saves are done when the target is higher level than the caster, it appears that Lures would get saving throws in situations where they should not. I'm fixing it now, it should go on test shortly.

So to make everyone happy, the target of a lure spell will NOT get a saving throw addition if it's higher level than the caster. I thought this already was the case, it was not.

Currently on live some NPCs are getting saves versus lures that they should not be getting."

This change, however, will not take effect until we patch next. With the holidays on us, it's hard to tell when that will be.

So, over all, Lure should actually become a more powerful line of spells, for those that are fully aware of their proper use…

Alan

New Spell - Velium Chill of Al`Kabor:
After the patch I did the thing that GZ tells me not to do and I disected the spell data file. Looks like there is a new spell for us -

  • Spell Name: Velium Chill of Al`Kabor
  • Illusion: Elemental
  • Decrease Hit Points (HP) by 1700
  • Level: 60
  • Range to Target: 200 feet
  • Skill: Evocation Allowable Targets: All
  • Resistance Check: Cold
  • Mana Required: 450
  • Spell Duration: Instant
  • Casting Time: 7.0 seconds
  • Spell Recovery: 2.50 seconds
  • Spell cast on you: Your skin freezes as you are hit with a chilling blast.
  • Spell cast on someone: Soandso has been frozen by a chilling blast.
  • Spell fades: Your skin returns to normal.

Now as for the illusion stat of elemental, I already sent a query to GZ on it. It reads that the target will turn into an elemental much like the necromancer's 'Ignite Bones' turns their target into a skeleton. Don't expect him to reply though. Some things he just doesn't want to give info on.

More on Lures:
More info has been addressed about our Lure spells, this time from a programmer at Verant -

There is a nasty rumour floating around that something will be changed in the overall way that NPCs save against the Lure series of spells this upcoming patch. This is not true. Lure saves at one time were supposed to be like lifetap saves, but this did not happen. They are currently more like Dragon Breath saves, which is better for the caster, worse for the target. They will continue to be that way!

About 3 weeks ago dragon breath saves were adjusted, and that resulted in some few NPCS saving slightly better saves vs certain lures, but this change happened about a month ago on test, 3 weeks ago on live, and lures will continue to work as dragon breath type saves, that will not change.

In an irc chat it was incorrectly stated, that Lures would be treated as lifetap saves, let me repeat, this is not true. Lures are treated as Dragon breath type saves both before and after tonights patch.

- Uzun

Abashi on Lure Spells:
For about a week now I've been playing ping-pong with e-mails to Geoff Zatkin and the live team at Verant concerning our Lure line of spells. With help from Pluton, we were able to feed them with good info on what was happening to us. Since that time, a chat has taken place and EQ Stratics posted the chat log. This is what was said -

Thundolf: (Azz) There has been some concern expressed on the Gameplay board over recent reduced effectiveness of wizard Lure spells...with tine for a long, reasoned response by Absor regarding enchanter illusions of all things, why is it nobody has looked at this question, even if only to say "we'll check it out?"

Abashi: Basically: The Lure spells were meant to save like lifetaps. Unfortuantely they were saving like dragon breath (dragons get better saves). They were fixed, they now save like lifetaps as was intended. It should have been preannounced, but was missed due to its low game impact. It really only affects raids on things that are immune to certain types of magic.
