    Re: Destiny Impressions - Things you may want to know

    The rumor is Activision altered the story because using the Traveler as a marketing symbol of hope/communion was very effective, but I think people might be pushing too hard on that idea. It really isn't that unusual for a story to have a radical redesign before it becomes a finished product, and we still have too little information to go on. As I said, I'm skeptical, but could see it working. People have really taken a hold of the idea and some pretty terrible theories have started cropping up as a result.

    There is one simple explanation for why the missions are so simple and why there is so little exposition in the game: it was built in such a way that you could 1) play most of it co-op, 2) repeat the same content (grind) forever with little to no hassle. Does that excuse not also having story missions actually be story missions? I don't know. At the very least they could have named the story missions something else, and have story missions that are basically just optional cutscenes explaining what the hell is going on.

    As I replay them I start to piece more and more of it together, but some things are still vague. I haven't read a theory for what is at the heart of the black garden for example. I guess I could google it and find a post on reddit that explains everything, just like with the Vault of Glass (which was pretty cool), but that's just kind of ridiculous.


    Edit: Something else that bothers me. When you finish most story missions you are given an item with subtext implying what an amazing discovery something is, you then take it back to the tower and are given a shitty gun as a reward and that's it. It is never brought up again. This is crazy.

    Re: Destiny Impressions - Things you may want to know

    One of the confusing aspects of weapons is that the amount of damage done on weapons doesn't scale. A level 1 and level 20 Exotic weapon do exactly the same amount of damage (before the upgrades).

    A level 1 Dreg and a level 20 Dreg have the same amount of Health. However, they have different damage reduction. Higher attack allows you to overcome that damage reduction, until you're doing maximum damage. This means that you want your weapon level and character level to be closer the higher level you are, but it isn't a huge deal below level ~15 or so, because the attack power scale isn't very high until 15-20.

    Re: Destiny Impressions - Things you may want to know

    I think Venus might be the best planet for patrols. Almost all of the areas can be quickly accessed with the speeder bike, while on Earth there are a lot of buildings with tight corridors to move between. Earth is still the best place for public event farming though I wager. The moon's problem is half of it is the Hellmouth dungeon thing. The only outside area I really like there is the area with all the Fallen to the left of the Crota temple. I like Mars too, but haven't spent much time there. I probably should, I need a heck load of relic iron.


    You can get an upgrade to your speeder bike by finding the five golden chests on Earth. They aren't that hard to find, you should only need help with one of them and there are guides on the internet that can help. As far as I can tell, after the next two upgrades come from spending 175ish crucible or vanguard marks, and then one last upgrade by spending a ton (25+, don't remember) of strange coins at the weekly Xur vendor. Neither of these options is recommended if you haven't geared up first, since both these currencies are needed to buy gear and you can only get a limited amount each week.

    The upgrades themselves are noticeable but not particularly remarkable. It's a slight improvement rather than a significant one.


    Edit/Correction: You apparently get a speeder upgrade for doing the first Mars mission. I forgot about it because it's equivalent to what you get out of the gold chests on Earth.
