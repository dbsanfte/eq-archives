Insta's (aka bookmarks) are your friend. It's tedious but you can make them on your own. An “insta” is a bookmark that lands you right on top of a station so that you can instantly dock, or a stargate so that you can instantly jump to the next system. These are vital for lowsec and 0.0 space. Slow-boating to a gate or a station will get you killed.

I’ve never bought bookmarks off the escrow market, and I’ve heard many a tale of pilots being scammed out of their isk for worthless bookmarks so buyer beware. I got mine from my corp, any corp competent enough to live in 0.0 space will most certainly have bookmarks you can copy.

To make your own, pick your destination like a stargate. Set your auto pilot and warp to the gate. As soon as you come out of warp and you hear “approaching stargate” disengage your autopilot, and you’ll continue to approach the gate. Left click the gate you’re approaching – this will put a square around the gate. Then double click just below that square and you’ll then be headed past the gate while still being aligned to it. Once you’ve passed the gate, stop your ship at exactly 15km from the gate. You’ll know the distance because the gate should still be targeted in your overview. Once you’re at 15km, open the People and Places window and select places, then click the add bookmark button at the bottom of the window. You can label it whatever you’d like but usually people label it as Gate XYZ.546 or something like Konora Gate ---> XYZ.546 gate.

The reason for the second example is that Insta’s only work if you approach them from the alignment they were saved in. Yet another reason why Eve is so complex and realistic. For example if there are five gates in a system, then there would be a different bookmark from each gate to whatever. Not to mention one for each station, or safe spot.

Also, what are 0.0 sectors like for solo players? Is it attack on sight if you're not part of the corp that runs the system?

Non aggression pacts and positive standings (which would tell other pilots you’re friendly) are rarely given to individuals, but some alliances do permit smaller corps to move out their regions and hunt / live in those areas. Each region of Eve is different, as well as each alliance’s policies.

If you find yourself in that situation, run like hell if you can, and don’t bother squawking in local. There’s a saying, “If it aien’t blue; its goo.
