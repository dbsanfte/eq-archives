    The Orange T4 robe cost 70-90G on the Market.....
    But the mats cost 20G... anyone talked about thieving assholes crafters ?
    You're fighting 4 separate problems: gold duping, time to acquire materials, rediculous material pricing and burnout. I play on targonor. Where the prices are the opposite: orange robes would only sell for about 20G with the prime silkbloom bales running 60G apiece.

    I've done group harvesting with other folks, and I saw only 2 malachite samples (your T4 robe upgrade needs one) drop in a 2h30m session of non-stop harvesting. In general, I price items about 2x the price of the rare (or most expensive) material. Even with the boost in harvesting nodes just before SoE took over, you are looking at 1-3 hours of harvesting to get a single prime silkbloom bale. Prior, you could only get prime items as part of work orders or quests.

    The coin duping is such a problem that many crafters are looking up profiles on vgplayers to see if you got your gold legitimately, or bought it online, before making something. Many of the lvl 50 crafters - that still play - only take orders from other crafters. The price of gold has shot up about 10x since the 5/11 patch, so it looks like they got one of the main dupes.

    Tailoring prices are borked as the material costs are so high that you can't sell the finished item for anywhere near the price of the rare/ultra rare. I don't bother deconstructing gear for powders, and just get a couple from work orders. Hearing other outfitters report their results of spending 10-20G and getting less than 3 useful powders from deconstruction, which is rather demoralizing.

    The recipe to upgrade material is also a drop item. A copy of the silkbloom armor upgrade recipe runs about 20G on my server, and most tailors won't pay that (my toon wears plate, so I sure won't), so they hope they'll get the recipe before they hit 40 tailoring. And not all of them will ever get that recipe. I mostly make cloth crafting gear upgrades, and I spent 30G getting my recipes. I also had to borrow money to get them, as they are even more rare than the cloth upgrade recipe.

    That robe you're whining about has several man-hours of effort in getting the parts to make it. Suck it up.

    Don't like the cost of crafted gear? Start crafting yourself.
