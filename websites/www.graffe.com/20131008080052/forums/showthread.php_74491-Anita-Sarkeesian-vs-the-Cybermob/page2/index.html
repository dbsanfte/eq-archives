    Re: Anita Sarkeesian vs the Cybermob

    I started to reply to this a few days ago, but didn't have time to watch the videos and got distracted before compiling all my thoughts.

    I think the first video was good. This surprised me because I had seen quite a bit of complaining about it across the internet before you linked it. Now I may not end up agreeing with some of her other videos/arguments, but I don't think she said anything in this one I wouldn't have said myself. She even points out damsels in distress aren't always sexist or have no value.

    Second video I think is arguing just for the sake of arguing. I think for similar reasons to why people have been dismissing her video in the first place. They have baggage with this woman because of things she has said in the past and so they made up their mind ahead of time to disagree with anything she had to say. Several of his arguments are poor and only serve the purpose of attempting to discredit her. He addresses points that have nothing to do with this video at all. Bring those up later when she releases the video claiming strong female characters are actually men. (it's one of the planned videos I believe)

    The few points he does make don't disprove anything she is saying and would be better served as a counterpoint. Yes games are made to serve an audience, and yes early games had to boil a plot down to 7 seconds, but that doesn't change the fact that women have been objectified in video games because they are designed specifically to appeal to boys and men. I do not understand why people feel threatened by this idea, or why they think video games need to be a boy's club. Stronger characterization benefits all of us.

    You don't have to make silly pink barbie games to get girls interested in video games, you just have to have a video game that doesn't tell them they are an object to be won. You can even still have sexy female characters, if their entire existence isn't defined by being oggled. I don't know if she would agree with me, but both Heart of the Swarm and the new Tomb Raider feature strong female leads. It is marketable and he is wrong when he claims it is not viable. I think the fact that he would boil down her argument to a desire for "the patriarchy to fund my feminist games" to be particularly ridiculous.

    Heck, even ignoring recent games, or games with a female protagonist, there have been several games that resonated with female audiences. I don't feel like starting a kickstarter to fund researching them, but I would bet they are primarily games that don't objectify women, or at least do it less than others. Heck, what's the most popular genre with women? I would argue rpgs. Rpgs also tend to have women more frequently featured in prominent roles. After that I would guess adventure games for similar reasons.

    I admit, the scene in Double Dragon Neo did occur to me as well, but I don't think it actually disproves her point. I also had a hard time taking his argument about family values seriously because he kept looping the video of Marian's panties being flashed at the camera during her abduction. Kind of amused he used footage from "Two Best Friends play", which is where I learned about Double Dragon Neo in the first place.
