Absor on the Status of Lures:
Absor took some of his time to post what is going on with our lure line of spells on the Official Gameplay Forum -

I just wanted to summarize what is happening and what has happened with Lure spells (hopefully I understand it).

Three weeks ago, the resist rates for NPCs in regards to Lure was fixed. This means, basically, that they will resist Lures that they are supposed to resists (a fire creature might now resist a fire-based Lure that it did not resist before). This change really shouldn't have affected most wizards, once they realized the change and adjusted tactics accordingly. It is my fault for not informing you of the change. For that I'm sorry.

However, all the talk about changes to this line of spells brought about a more in-depth look at how they work, leading to this post by one of our programmers (given in full here for your enjoyment):

"After looking closely at the way saves are done when the target is higher level than the caster, it appears that Lures would get saving throws in situations where they should not. I'm fixing it now, it should go on test shortly.

So to make everyone happy, the target of a lure spell will NOT get a saving throw addition if it's higher level than the caster. I thought this already was the case, it was not.

Currently on live some NPCs are getting saves versus lures that they should not be getting."

This change, however, will not take effect until we patch next. With the holidays on us, it's hard to tell when that will be.

So, over all, Lure should actually become a more powerful line of spells, for those that are fully aware of their proper use…

Alan

Looking for Spells?:
I think we are all looking diligently for our spells. Currently, some lower level spells (Wakening Lands Portal, and Enticement of Flame) have been dropping off of the wurms. Here's an excerpt from an e-mail I received -

"The Wakening Lands Portal is dropped from guardian shardwurms in the cave following the glowy stream out of Thurgadin City in The Great Divide. The scroll is no drop, but was a fairly common drop."
Here's the trick about our new portal spells. In the patch today, they said "Dragon Circles have been fully implemented. In short, you will not be able to receive a teleport to a dragon circle unless you've been to the circle before and carry a soulbound souvenir that spawns at the circle." What this means, for example, is that if you scribe the Wakening Lands Portal and wish to use it, before it will even work, you will need to travel by foot through the giant city of Kael. Once through Kael, you will end up in the Wakening Lands. There you must find the dragon circle (approximately -3000, -3000) and loot a tooth from the middle of the circle (spawn time is about 20 seconds). Once you have this tooth in your inventory, it will be proof that you have been there and you can now use the portal. Every member of your party must have one of these teeth or 'tokens' in order to be taken to the appropriate portal.

Have fun running through Kael! I took a wrong turn and got ambushed by 4 named casters that see through invis. Owiee! Thanks to Kenju Weeple [Coven Nex] of Druzzil Ro for the spell drop info.

Wizard Spells:
Besides the Iceclad Portal and Gate being for sale in the mainlands, the only spell I could find for sale in Velious was O'Keils Flickering Flame (Level 34, Damage Shield by 6, Increase Fire Resist by 10). Easiest way to get it is to gate into Iceclad Ocean (Iceclad Gate or Portal), use Abscond to take you to the docks, exit the dock and turn left, following the waters edge until you can see the igloos up on the hill. Inside one of the igloos, you will find a gnome in a bright blue robe named Xorbinasticalus Zimralicus (+4631.04, +1315.80, +73.88). He sells it for about 4pp.
