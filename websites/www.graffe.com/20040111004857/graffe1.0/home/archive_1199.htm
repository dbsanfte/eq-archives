Here's some good reading for you. NeuroS T'zzorM of Xegony created a list of questions for Geoffrey Zatkin (GZ). These are questions and topics that are always abundant on the major message boards. If you all can take your comments to the Rant Board, my e-mail box will thank you greatly ::smiles:: Here is a copy of the e-mail:
Q: Have the mana/damage efficiency linked to the level of the wizard. There by increasing all DD spells useable by a wizard at that level to the mana/damage efficiency of the wizards largest DD spell.an example: At level 50 IC and rend both have a mana/damage efficiency of 2.8. I am suggesting that all DD from 1 to 50 be increased to that efficiency rating. That would increase the max damage of Shock of Ice (level 34 spell) from 320 to 448 for a level 50 wizard.
GZ: If the wizard was level 28 with a top spell of frost shock with a mana/damage efficiency rating of 1.7. Therefore all lower level spells would have a mana/damage rate of 1.7. We specificially designed our system to do the opposite. Otherwise, why would you ever want another flame spell after level 1, since that one would upgrade as you gained in levels. Instead, we have spells that grow with you to a certain point, but then cap. This lets you move on to the next more powerful spell when you advance a level.

Q: Also with this change the restance rates of lower level spells would need to be placed on a sliding scale. So the lower the spell rank less chance of the mob resisting the spell.
GZ: We already have a bit of this built in. If you use a lower level spell, your chance of fizzle is almost 0, you do that spells max damage, and we knock a bit off of the creatures resistance. Not a lot, but a bit.

Q: Give the wizards DD spells an across the board increase in damage. We are advertised as the "Masters of Damage" yet at various levels other pure casters and Hybrid casters can do as much or more damage then us.
GZ: DD damage? Not really. Wizards have [1] Better spell casting times [2] better spell recasting times [3] a better mana to damage ratio (always) [4] more damage per DD spell then any other DD spell of the same rank. Remember, this is DD - not DoT, Bolt, etc.

Q: Perhaps raise the resistance cap for wizards from 6 or 1.2 to 8 or 1.3. This would allow a lower level wizards to be of use against the harder mobs, where they normally would not.
GZ: The one big weakness of the Wizard is their lack of indirect offense. This can be overcome with help - a Bard, Enchanter, Magician or Shaman can lower the resistance of monsters, making it easier to effect a red. Wizards can also quest for a magic staff that helps with this.

Q: Either totally remove or add upgrades to our one and only damage shield spell. This most have been meant as a joke... One damage shield for 2 pt that last what 20 seconds and no upgrades...
GZ: Wizards were not slotted to have gotten Damage Shields. If I had known how many people would bitch about it, it would have been removed in early beta.

Q: Revert to Root line of spells back to there former power. I can't believe they did this to us. I would rather trade in all of my Root serious spells for the 1st level Druid Snare spell which is a hundred times more effective. You wanted us to group up and then you complain that the root spell is to powerful in group combats... Next our DD will be to powerful. Did you bother to think that I may have to depend upon that root spell to hold a wandering monster so I can zone???
GZ: Not sure what you are talking about. Root hasn't changed in a long, long time. I even doublechecked with the AI guy - no changes in how Root works vs monsters. If you are talking about PvP, it got changed a tiny bit - and we playtested it a lot to a level that we felt was balanced.

Q: And lastly has anyone else noticed that whilst wizards don't get a stun effect off there DD spells, all other pure casters get stun effects(enchanters at level 1) much earlier, others slow their opponent down or gain life whilst doing damage?
GZ: There are two types of stun spells in EQ - knock out of casting stuns (what wizards get on most of their spells) and stun spells that actually keep the creature from doing anything (wizards get 2 of this type). As for "all other casters" - Clerics & Enchanters get a stun, Druids get a knock out of casting stun that only works outdoors, Shamans & Necromancers & Magicians get none. I might be forgetting something, but that is what comes immediatly to mind.

Q: Jipp, Yes I want to be the most powerful damage spell caster in the game. That is what I was lead to believe the wizard class was! The title of Master of Offensive Magic and the line about having no rivals in this area kind of sums it up. Wizards were suppose to be the Masters of spell damage, we don't have any cool pets, no Buffs, no DOTs, no Debuffs, no Heals, no worth while Damage Shield. Those where are sacrificed to concentrate wizards in the fields of Damage and teleportation. We can teleport all over Norrath and the planes, to bad we are out gunned by other caster at our own speciality.
GZ: Wizards get what is advertised - the most powerful direct damage in the game. For this, they give up a lot of other things.
A Magician with a pet can outdamage a Wizard. A Necromancer with DoT's and a Skeleton will eventually outdamage a Wizard. However, with just direct damage, a Wizard will outdamage everybody, hands down. They do more damage in less time then any other class. They also get a few nifty things that most other classes don't - Teleport being one of the major ones. Wizards are good at DD, AoE, Teleport, self protection (shieldskin, resistant skin, etc), Root and the occasional stun. Wizards also get 3 types of damage spells (magic, fire and cold), which means that they can effect almost every monster in every zone of the game. They also get their DD spells upgraded every level - no other class gets that. This means that the Wizard always has access to their most powerful spell at every spell rank.
Now, this isn't going to be popular, but... I've read a lot of discussion boards. Every class asks for things to be added, and every class thinks that they are the least powerful. Go read the Necromancer or Magician boards - or the Shaman or Cleric. Pick one and read - lots of suggestions on how to make the classes more balanced towards their class.
EQ classes are not going to change from what they are now. We will add a few spells and little abilities as we go along, but nothing that will effect the balance of the classes or balance of the game. If you like Wizards as they are, you will continue to like them. If you think Wizards aren't that powerful, or you like the style of play of another class, realize that you may want to switch to that class.
I feel that Wizards are well balanced. I have come to this conclusion from over a year of playing a Wizard. I have high level characters of every spell casting class in EQ. From a balance point of view, in my personal opinion, Wizards are well balanced. This is an opinion, and you may disagree. But from personal play experience, Wizards are pretty damn cool.
Geoffrey C. Zatkin
Arch-Mage of Norrath
Game Designer: Verant Interactive

Sarumaan sent this in concerning the Ice Comet Quest:
"I am a level 25 wizard, and since I'd recently heard that any level wizard could collect the 10 rods and create the staff of the wheel I decided to give it a go myself. While trying to obtain the Erud's crossing rod, which I think is definately that hardest to get, I found a nice trick that might be worth trying if you have a high-ish swimming skill and feel lucky. I had died 4 times trying to get the rod in Erud's crossing and was close to giving up, as the killer sharks seemed to be there EVERY time I took the dive, and would attack me immediately, and could see through invisibility, so I decided to make one last charge. There are a lot of pesky zombie sailors running around the island and a few of them spawn next to the boat that is just infront of the rod. As I jumped into the water a few of them attacked me, but i kept swimming, and they followed me down and to my utter surprise, the killer sharks started attacking the zombies instead of me, which gave me the time I needed to grab the rod and swim like hell towards shore. I owe my life to one of those zombies :) ....so, it may be worth while dragging a few zombie sailors down after you to distract the sharks while you make off with the rod. It worked for me :)
Sarumaan The white, formerly 26th, now 25th lvl wizard."
Thanks Sarumaan!
