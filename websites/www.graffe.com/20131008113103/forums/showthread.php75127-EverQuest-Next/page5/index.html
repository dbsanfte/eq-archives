    one thing in EQ Next they will need to decide and they will have to decide it as early as possible is do they want PVP. Much of the ire towards PVP in WoW comes from the fact they have to constantly tweak things for the sake of Arena and whenever they tweak for arena something is always damaged... Its like EQ, Every patch broke bards somehow.

    Re: EverQuest Next

    Things I would like to see include:

    1 When you die, you do not loose experience, but you loose 5 levels of weapon skills.
    2 When you die, your gear still needs looted from your corpse.
    3 Resurrections summon corpse and returns all lost weapons skill levels except 1.
    4 Only players can resurrect (no NPC)
    5 Corpse summons only summons corpse no skill returns.
    6 Corpse summoner NPC only summons corpse, no skill returns.
    7 Biggest positive faction builders are group and raid quests (make it a bit easier).
    8 Player selling system is an auction based system like they use in Rift. You put your items on the auctioneer and set the buy out price and/or min bid price which cannot be changed until the auction timer for that item has expired. Items not sold at end of timer are sent back to the player in the in game mail system, same as when an item is sold the money is sent by the in game mail system.
    9 No mercenaries! Solo players have pets available instead to fill that role. Pets are not allowed in groups or raids.
    10 Player characters have modes for solo, group and raid and depending on the mode, they will have different abilities, spells and skills available to them, with the goal of providing a balance to game play from solo to raid. If you are in a raid, you are automatically in raid mode, if you are in a group you are automatically in group mode and likewise solo if not grouped or in raid modes and you cannot change the mode manually.
    11. Quests be categorized as raid, group or solo with the majority of the quests being solo.
    12. Dyes be rewards from quests so they are available, but not commonplace.

    Oh! And for experience gain... I think they need to use a multiplier system. For example, level 1 you need 100 exp points. Level 2 you double that to 200 exp points. Level 3 is double level 2 so it's 400 exp. point and so on. This will slow down leveling and thus the game yet have no hell levels and no level cap. No need for alternative advancement, make those things tied to your mode and level. Similarly, your base spells increase by a percentage by level and spell skill proficiency so you won't need as many spells that do the same thing.

    Those are some of the things I would like to see. I like the old school EQ, but I just do not have the time for a game that is a huge time sink anymore.
