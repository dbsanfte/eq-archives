Crusaders of the Wind
Crusaders' Status
Clerics, Shaman, and Druids belong to the Crusaders of the Wind. The highest of these is the Genesis Crusader of the Winds. He or she is most responsible for the rest of their division. Directly under the Genesis Crusader is the Exodus Crusader of the Winds. He or she has nearly the same responsibilities as Genesis, and assumes the role fully when Genesis is unavailable. All rank decisions are made by the division's officers, and the Guildleader.

As for the rest of the Crusaders division, this is the structure:

  • All new members are given the rank of Emissary.
  • After Emissary, you can be promoted to Ambassador.
  • And after Ambassador, you can be promoted to Crusader.
Decisions are based on (but not exclusively on) the following criteria:

  • Impress higher ranking members with loyal role playing. If you are devoted, you will get their attention.
  • Provide aid or equipment to help your fellow guildmembers. Examples would be escorting a younger member through dangerous territory or offering the Genesis Crusader a gift that the guild can use, like armor, etc. The High Court will determine what to do with the equipment. (This alone will not get you to officership though, you need to stress roleplay.)
  • Defeat a Shadow Strider in a battle of wits. Must be witnessed by another.
  • Defeat a Shadow Strider in a fair duel. Must also be witnessed. By fair we mean equal to or very close to your level.
Higher ranks must perform more of the above to advance. If you are an Ambassador, you should play at least 5 hours a week. If you are a Crusader, you should play at least 10 hours a week. Exceptions can be made if you are having RL difficulties.

Once you are a Crusader, you can advance to the rank of Exodus or even Genesis if the positions are open. Only Sugan can bestow that honor, and only Crusaders will be considered for officership.

Disrespect to higher ranking members is punishable by demotion and/or disbandment.
