The Quest for McMerin’s Feast, aka Cannibalize 2

This is the quest for the level 39 shaman spell, Cannibalize 2. Do not be confused by the name, as only the physical scroll for the spell is named “McMerin’s Feast”. Once scribed in your spellbook, it is called Cannibalize 2.

This spell is only available through this quest. However, the quest is extremely difficult to accomplish at level 39. At least one of the parts is a challenge even for a group of level 50s. You may wish to buy some of the parts and to solicit help from stronger friends. There are shamans who will tell you that you are better off just toughing it out with Cannibalize 1 until you reach level 54 and can use Cannibalize 3. That’s an awfully long time to wait given how important cannibalizing is to shamans. But the fact that such advice is even given should indicate how much effort this will take you at 39 or even well into your 40s. If you find that you cannot do it until you are in your low 50s, it is almost certainly well worth it unless you level very quickly.

Barbarians, vah shir, and guktan (frogloks) start in Firiona Vie by speaking with Marlyn McMerin in the shaman guild on the dock, at approximately -3800, 1700. If you hail her she will explain her dilemma. Tell her that you will search for rare alchemy components.

Trolls, ogres, and iksar should start the quest by speaking with The Great Oowomp at the ogre camp in Timorous Deep. He is on the same island where the boats come to and from Oasis and Overthere. Ask him about the McMerin clan. He will profess to a bit of thievery and offer to give the spell to you in exchange for collecting the same things that Marlyn requests.

Note: Iksar should be careful, as other ogres nearby may agro. If that happens the Oowomp will agro as well. You may wish to bring along some help to back you up. Then, if you get agro, be sure to kill the Oowomp first and stall for a minute or two before killing the others. The Oowomp’s respawn time is a little over 5 minutes, so you can then turn in your items and scoot out of there before the others return. The Great Oowomp is level 34; the other ogres should be the same or lower. Just be sure your helpers know that you will take hits to The Overthere faction for killing these ogres.

The four pieces you must collect are:

  1. Strange Ochre Clay – This drops in City of Mist from Army Behemoths, the big, brown golems. It is droppable but lore. That means you may be able to nab this in the Bazaar or even from other people hunting in City of Mist. If you hunt for it, the behemoths spawn throughout various parts of the City of Mist, including the area before and after the entrance bridge. Be careful searching for them, as you can sneak past the ghosts with invisibility to undead but will be spotted by the behemoths. The behemoths themselves are approximately level 40-45.

  2. Shaman Powder – This piece drops off of the froglok yun shamans in Trakanon’s Teeth. They are most commonly sighted around the aquaducts outside the exterior wall of the Sebilis ruins as well as in the area outside the ruins that house Emperor Ganak in the southwest of the zone. You will save a great deal of time if you bring a tracker along to help find them. When you do find one, either pull it to the zone wall to fight or be prepared for adds, as there are a lot of hungry critters wandering around out there. Do not be confused by the shaman powder that drops off of other varieties of froglok shamans and is white and no drop, or the shaman powder off of nok shamans that is black and no drop. The one you need is black and droppable. Here again, you may have success in the Bazaar or in asking other people in the zone. The yun shamans are also approximately level 40-45.

  3. Greyish Bone Chips – These are the hardest part of the quest. They drop off of the Skeletal Warlord in Karnor’s Castle. Alas, your chances of getting a group here at level 39 are slim to none. The warlord can spawn in place of most of the ghosts and skeletons throughout the zone. With the frequency of trains in Karnor’s, more than one shaman has obtained the bones by hanging out at the zone and waiting for someone to train the warlord there. The bones are no drop, so you cannot buy them. But as the quest is their only use, you may wish to /ooc periodically to advertise that you need them. If you find someone else who has a spare, they are multiquestable, as long as the other person has sufficient faction with your quest giver. The warlord is approximately level 45.

  4. Crushed Diamonds – There are two ways to get this last piece. One of them is extremely easy and very safe, while the other is long and complicated. Needless to say, the easy way is recommended.

    1. Easy Way: Pick them up off the ground at -8850, -6040 in Timorous Deep. This island is the iceberg home of the golras. They do not see invis. It is very easy to spot, a little brown bag sitting on the ice. From anywhere in the zone you can just fire up levitate and invis and run to that loc. If you are unfamiliar with the area, be careful in the middle of the zone where there are sunken pirate ships containing undead pirates, as well as a big patch of space through which you cannot pass. (That is where the boats zone to and from Firiona and Overthere.) The closest island to the golra iceberg is the elven outpost. From there you would run due south (again, levitated and invis) and will see the bag sitting on the northeast side of the iceberg island. The Crushed Diamonds will respawn 8-12 hours after being taken. If you get to that loc and do not see them anywhere, they have probably
      been picked up recently. Try again later.

    2. Difficult Way: Buy a dufrenite from any of several locations, including the vendors in most home cities that sell the vendor potions and crystals. Bring an enchanter, necro, or bard to the huge spires in Dreadlands, near the functional wizzie spires. On the ground under those spires there are some pyramids containing various nasty creatures. One of them is the Gem Cutter Skeleton. He may wander in and out of one of the pyramids. He will be kos, so charm him and give him the dufrenite to receive your Dread Diamond. Then head to Kaesora or Trakanon’s Teeth and start killing spectral guardians until you get a Spectral Pestle. Combine it with the Dread Diamond in a Medicine Bag to get your Crushed Dread Diamond. The combine is trivial at an alchemy skill of 70. If you fail you will get the Dread Diamond back but not the Pestle. I told you this way was difficult.

Turn in the four quest items to Marlyn McMerin or The Great Oowomp and voilà! You can now feast upon your hit points more efficiently than ever before.
