Satori Apocrypha's Guide to Level 20

To state the obvious, warriors are the least independent of all classes. They don't have any special abilities. They soak up damage, and they deal it out. I personally don't see this as a disadvantage - no one can do what they do better, and as the game progresses, it becomes harder and harder to get by without one. What is important to understand when playing a warrior, though, is that you can't do very much solo. When looking for a place to hunt, you need to think about how easy it will be to find other people to group with so that they can heal, nuke, mez, stun, or fear - anything to keep your downtime to a minimum. This is what I have kept in mind in creating this guide. Places that I might go with my magician, I would not go to with my warrior at the same level, it's as simple as that. 

Levels 1-5:
These levels are the same for pretty much every class. Hunt in the newbie area closest to you. Find the quests tailored for your level and do them as much as you can. Bone chip quests, for instance, are great for faction and exp and can be found in most cities' cleric's guild. Check every guild hall that you are accepted in for quests - the more you do, the faster you will get through these levels, and with less tedium. 

Levels 6-10: 
I am a big fan of the Butcherblock Mountains for Dwarf, Elf, and Gnome Warriors. There are usually clerics and Druids around to group with, and enough players to group with. Dwarf Skeletons, Giant Scarabs, Giant Bats, and Emerald Drakes are all great experience at these levels, as are the several goblin camps spread around the zone. Crushbone off of Greater Faydark, while annoying, is also an excellent dungeon, and the CrushBone Orc belts and Legionaire Shoulderpads quests are outstanding for experience, cash and faction. Though frequently ignored, I have found that an enterprising player can organize a group for Orc Camps in the Lesser Faydark as well and gain a great deal of experience there, unhindered by other groups. 

The most common place for Dark Elves and Humans from Freeport to persue these levels are the Western Commonlands. This zone is very noisy, and has a high density of players, but if you are industrious, you can get through your levels quickly here. Bears, Snakes, and Orcs are the common targets, and groups are fairly easy to get into or form here. Non evil characters can do very well in Misty Thicket, as well. There are always a ton of druids here, and if you can convince one to partner with you, the result can be very productive. 

Everfrost, however, will probably always be my favorite place for the early levels. It can support a character from level one to level 25 in theory, and best of all it has a feel completely different from other zones. The people here, on my server at least, always seem to be more friendly. It should be noted, however, that there are Barbarian guards at the exits of Mountain valleys to the Tundra - so evil races would not have an easy time of it here. 

Some other viable hunting grounds at these levels would be the Feerrott, Innothule Swamp, the Southern Desert of Ro, and Befallen. I think that South Ro is the most underutilized of these, but it is a long and dangerous run from a bind spot. 

Levels 11-15:
Misty Thicket continues to be good hunting right up to level 14 or 15. You can still hunt in Butcherblock, as well, but the experience gain will proceed more slowly for most. I found that Lesser Fay and Steamfont were outstanding, but the most common place to find people leveling for these levels is North Ro. All races can hunt here with equal risks. Evil and good races alike can bind in West Freeport, away from the guards without much trouble. Finding a group at these levels isn't usually too difficult, either. 

The yard trash in Unrest also makes great prey at this level range. The trains are bad, and pathing is strange here, meaning that you can be run over by mobs significantly tougher than you without much warning, but I have not had too much trouble hunting here when I kept off the main thoroughfare. 

Crushbone Castle remains good hunting at these levels as well. Slavers and Legionaires are fairly plentiful, and the quest still provides great experience up to about level 14. 

There are bandits in the Karanas that are about synonymous with Dervs. These bandits drop red sashes that can be turned in for a quest in Qeynos. The rewards are bronze weapons, outstanding faction increases, and a not insignificant amount of cash. This is probably my favorite hunting spot for this level, but it should be pointed out that pickup groups are not at all easy to find at this level. If you want to venture out on these dangerous plains to hunt these bandits, bring some friends with you - you won't find many peers out there. 

There are orc camps that people frequently miss in Lake Rathe and also in Dagnor's Cauldron. These aren't the most convenient spots for people in this level range, but I could see them being great experience for the right team. 

Levels 16-20: 
Hands down, the most popular place for people to do these levels, regardless of race/class is Oasis. Its crowded, but usually underutilized. The coastline is usually the safest place to be, and hence it is where most people hunt. There are crocodiles here, and they are often killed as fast as they spawn. Deeper in there are countless orcs, however, that are usually not hunted as heavily. A well organized group can hunt these by pulling them to a safer location, and the experience really flows here. 

South Karana starts to become an interesting zone around level 16, also. While I personally cannot stand this zone, and it really seems to have been designed with Druids in mind (read: an abundance of kiters) there are a lot of things to hunt, and spread out over a great deal of space. I have heard of many people staying in this zone without ever leaving all the way up their mid thirties, and with the introduction of an upgraded dungeon, I suppose it could be possible to stay even longer now - but I don't recommend it. Get out and see the world! Adventure is what the game is all about. 

The Rathe Mountains, while rarely hunted by people as low as this range, do have several mobs that spawn away from the Hill Giants and Cyclopses that provide good experience. These are Drakes, and if you can organize a party ahead of time you can hunt undisturbed here. 

High Hold Pass is also a popular hunting ground at these levels, and the orcs and gnolls spawn quickly. When hunting here, you are providing a service to lower level travelers, too, as this is one of the only ways to get from west to east on the continent of Antonica.
