Shamans, more than any other class, seem to have the greatest diversity in the way they have to play as they advance. Here is an overview of how I've played through level 28.

Levels 1-4

Start building up a bit of cash. Buy or make patchwork armor for yourself. Get a rusty warhammer or worn staff as soon as you can, as that club you start off with is horrid. Since cash will be tight, buy Burst of Flame and Strengthen before your other spells.

At this point, you're almost as good as a fighter at melee. Keep yourself and everyone else in strengthen and inner fire. Your mana is best used for healing in and out of combat. Burst of flame can be used to kill off fleeing critters, and to lure.

I found grouping even at level 1 to be very enjoyable. I managed to hook up with 2 fighters were taking on a hoard of creatures, and we got to level 4 extremely quickly.

Levels 5-8

If you haven't gotten yourself a good weapon by now, do so. If you're lucky, you might be able to buy a Fine Steel 2H staff, or Fine Steel Warhammer. Buy Frost Rift, Summon Water, and Gate first. At level 8 be sure to get meditate, it raises quickly. On that same trip, if you can afford them, buy Spirit of the Wolf and Light Healing too.

You're still a decent melee fighter at this point, but now you have a good amount of mana too. Keep your tanks buffed with Strengthen, Inner Fire, and Feet Like Cat, and keep any mages buffed with Dexterous Aura.

In a group, using Frost Rift to finish off running opponents works really well, and is indispensable when those critters form trains (BlackBurrow). Your healing spell will start to feel innefective during combat.


Levels 9-13

Buy Spirit of the Wolf and Light Healing first. In fact, buy them before you level and scribe them as soon as you do! The other best spells include Summoned Food, Tainted Breath, and if you're a Barbarian, Serpent Sight.With Light healing you can effectively heal in combat again, and this will become your primary role. Tainted Breath is a great damage spell for long fights, if you have a fighter who can get the critter off you.

Unfortunately your instant damage spell is pretty inneffectual. You'll also notice that you're not nearly as good at melee as the fighters in damage dealing now, but you can still help them out, and take some damage.

Keep everyone buffed with wolf constantly, mages with dex, fighters with just about everything else, and yourself with everything. Serpent Sight is nice to cast on your puller at night if he doesn't have infravision.


Levels 14-18

Buy Spirit Strike, Walking Sleep, and Turtle Skin, first. You should also be getting yourself in a set of banded, if you haven't already. Look into getting a magic weapon such as a Runed Totem Staff, Glowing Crook, or Screaming Mace.

Walking Sleep is a great debuff. If grouped with a fighter, this can make both your lives a lot easier as you watch the mob fight in slow motion. Your healing spell will start to feel inneffectual, but in its place, you have Spirit Strike, an impressive direct damage spell.

In groups you will find you're spending an incredible amount of mana on buffs, especially on melee types. But don't be stingy. You're buffs in many cases are keeping everyone alive. A good buffing strategy is to buff yourself with something first, then buff everyone else who's appropriate with it. When it wears of you, it will be wearing off
everyone else, and it's time to rebuff again. This also minimises the amount of re-memorization you have to do with buffs. I call this buffing a "round" of something.


Levels 19-23

The best spells are Healing, Malise, and Endure Magic. However, since by this point you will likely be fighting a ways away from your hometown, it might be better to just save up and buy all your spells at once. Start collecting magical items that enhance your wisdom, charisma, or resistances. Look into getting a bigger melee weapon such as an Ogre War Maul.

Again, you're a combat healer. Your direct damage spell is still pretty good though. Endure magic is wonderful in helping to prevent members of your group from being rooted or charmed, and helps you to resist many damaging spells as well. Malise can help casters in your party to get damage through much more often to yellow and red creatures, and should not be underestimated.


Levels 24-28

Tons of great spells at this level. The best ones being Cannibalize, Regeneration, Frost Strike, and Evenomed Breath. Keep collecting more magic items to upgrade your collection.

Regenerate is most useful on yourself, since you can use Cannibalize to prevent any "spillage". Regnerate constantly. Cannibalize is nice for regaining mana faster while regenerating, as well as to get that extra
bit of mana you need to deal a final Frost Strike. One cute trick, if grouped with a cleric, he can effectively "give" you mana if you Cannibalize down to 1 bar of health, then he can cast Greater Heal on you. Outdoors, Spirit of the Cheetah is a nice escape spell. It lasts about 45 seconds, and will extremely quickly get you away from
anything. It takes a while to recharge, but Zoning will do this instantly. Kiting is much easier at Level 24, since you can gain life while running away, then stop for a second convert that life into mana.

You also have Envenomed Breath, a powerful DoT spell. If kiting, make sure you save a bar of mana for regeneration though, it leaves quickly.

Regenerate is the most efficient healing you have. It takes a while, but if you're low on mana, and your melee guy is low on HP, stick regen on him while you meditate. With a bit of luck, he'll still be regennerating during your next fight too. Your buffs, as always, keep getting bigger, more powerful, and more expensive. Fortunately they
last longer too. I traditionally keep myself in 10 buffs (5 stat buffs, 2 armor buffs, Endure Magic, Regeneration, Spirit of the Wolf)
