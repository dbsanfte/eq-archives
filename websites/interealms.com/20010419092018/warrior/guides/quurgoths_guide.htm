The Big, Bad, Stupid Meanie: Playing a Troll or Ogre Warrior, Levels 1-18

The Warrior is the backbone of any fantasy world, and this is true for Norrath. The pros and cons of playing a warrior are simple. On the pro side, we are strong and thus good with weapons, and can use nearly all weapons and armor. We have a ton of hit points, and can double attack/dual wield for excellent damage potential over time. We are desired in almost any group out there. On the con side, we do not solo well after level 20 (although it can be done). We lack spells of any sort. We get no pets, no healing abilities other than bandages, and without a healer around we require a lot of downtime between battles. If you like to wade into battle, take and deal mighty blows, and depend on others to save your hide, this is the class for you.

Why Should I Choose a Troll or Ogre Warrior?

Answer: For whatever reason suits your fancy. Evil race warriors have some obvious advantages to the power gamer: they are the strongest (do the most weapon damage) and have the most hit points (by virtue of having the highest Stamina). They can Slam, which is the same as Bash but doesn’t require that the warrior hold a shield. They can see in the dark. Having pointed out the reasons a k00l d00d might choose this path, however, let me say that role-playing a Troll or Ogre is some of the most fun that can be had in EQ. Role-playing these characters is easy: they are extremely stupid (with the lowest intelligence in the game), and they love to bash and eat things. Other characters enjoy interacting with role-players, so play your role to the hilt. Role-playing also carries rewards other than pure enjoyment: I can’t count the number of times I have received great buffs or excellent transaction prices because I requested in character “Duz youse got anny buffs fur dis Troll?” or “Me wants da 5pp fur da bone chippies, but if youse not rich me kin take da 3pp.” I get a lot of “LOL” (laughing out loud) responses, and almost always get the buff or outrageously good prices.

Character Creation

The debate about which attributes to increase has been held at length elsewhere, but my conclusion is that the best you can do for yourself is to put nearly all your points into Strength, with the remaining points in Stamina. This will ensure you can wear heavy armor, can use heavy weapons, will hit for high damage, and will have a goodly number of hit points. This will make soloing through the early levels easier, and will allow you to do your job admirably in the higher levels.

One other alternative, which I am exploring with a secondary character, is maxing out your Dexterity. This allows for frequent Critical Hits and Crippling Blows, and also leads to much more frequent weapon procs (very handy at higher levels).

Hotkeys

You will need to set up hotkeys (an art in and of itself) to your taste. Regardless of your configuration, you will NEED to learn Sense Heading and Taunt, which means you must hotkey them and spam (hit repeatedly) them during every battle. Sense Heading is fairly self-explanatory. When it works, you know which direction you’re going. Taunting is one of the primary skills for a tank in a group. When you taunt a creature, it becomes enraged at you and attacks you. This is good: you don’t want a mob (Monster Or Beast) attacking your puny spell-casters and healers. Your job is going to be making sure you are the primary attacker, soaking up damage and allowing your group to stay alive. This is more fun than it might sound, and it’s what a warrior is all about.

Any time you get into a group in which you are the primary tank, make sure you ask “has everyone set up an assist hotkey?” This will remind players to hotkey /assist so that they are all fighting what you are targeting. This is very important, as taking the mobs down one at a time is MUCH more efficient than fighting individually.

The Early Years: Learning to Bash Dem Baddies (Level 1-7)

The early years (level 2-7) of a Troll or Ogre warrior’s life should be spent in Innothule Swamp, the newby area immediately outside the Grobb, for Troll, or in the Feerrott, for Ogres. Either way, your first task is to read the note in your inventory (right-click on the note to read it) and follow the instructions. You will then receive some experience and your guild tunic, which will serve as your armor until you get better items from corpses. I recommend saving your skill practices for now, or putting them into kick.

After getting your tunic, find and kill whatever creatures con white or lower. My own recommendation is that you kill decaying skeletons whenever possible, since the bone chips they drop can be sold to necromancers and shadow knights. When you have 20 chips, do a /who necro, which will give you the names of all necromancers in the zone. Then /tell anyone you find on that list that “Me gots bone chippies fur sale. Duz youse wanna buy dem?” You can often get 5pp per stack this way, which is a ton of money for an early character.

Killing the various newby-level mobs in your newby area should suffice for getting you through level 6 in no time. If you can group with a shaman, do so, as this will reduce downtime, and there is a 50% bonus added to all experience gained in a group.

No Longer A Child: Becoming a Warrior (Level 7-13)

At level 8 (or sooner, if you have a group), it’s time to head for the Feerrott and my favorite place in Norrath for making money and gaining experience. As you zone into the Feerrott from Innothule, turn left and follow the zone wall until you reach the river. Follow the river until you reach the second bridge. Cross the bridge, and turn right, staying VERY close to the water’s edge. When you get to the zone wall, turn left. From Oggok, simply follow the path straight out from Oggok, turn right at the river after crossing the bridge, and turn left at the zone wall. There is the PROMISED LAND.

This corner of the Feerrott contains a set of three stone structures. Around each of the first two structures you see there will be 3 skeletons each, with a somewhat tougher zombie spawning in place of a skeleton on occasion. The corner (farthest) structure will have 5 skeletons/zombies. If you are careful, you will be able to fight one skeleton or zombie at a time for as long as you wish. The bone chips will accumulate rapidly, and the Feerrott almost always contains a few camps of high-level types, so you will have a ready market for your chips. My own warrior accumulated over 100pp in two levels by slaughtering skellies here. Other loot (rusty weapons, zombie skins, etc.) can be sold in Oggok. To get to Oggok from the skeleton camps, head back to the river and follow it to the large bridge. Cross the bridge and continue straight to the zone wall. Turn left at the zone wall, and you will find Oggok. Merchants there will sell/buy with both Trolls and Ogres.

When you no longer get experience from the skeletons and zombies, or when you’re bored of that camp, you can turn your attention to lizard men. The lizard men in the Feerrott are nothing like the Iksar, so don’t worry about taking faction hits when fighting them. The lizard men are a good source of experience for levels 9-12, and their tails and meat are quest loot. Be warned, however, that the Lizard Man Mystics are tougher than the Lizard Man Warriors. The best spot to kill lizards is outside Cazic Thule, a dungeon for groups of mid-20 level types. To get to Cazic Thule from the skeleton camp, follow the river to the large bridge. Cross the bridge, and continue following the river in the same direction. When you reach the second bridge (which is smaller and brownish in color), cross the river again and head left. You will find a gigantic stone structure with five small pyramids in front of it. Each of the pyramids will have a lizard man warrior or mystic atop it, with occasional wandering lizzies nearby. Bash them to death, as this is a great spot for your level.

Keep the tails and meats, as they can be turned in to the warrior trainers in Oggok. One of the trainers wants four tails at a time, and in return will give you a faction adjustment (important for Trolls), rawhide or patchwork armor, and experience. The trainer next to him wants four meats at a time, but will not offer the quest to Trolls. If you are a Troll, you will need to find a newby to group with you and give the meats to the Ogre. This will give you experience, armor, and faction, but you can let the newby keep the armor as a reward, or pay them a plat. They need the money. These quests will also adjust faction for Clurg, which is important in getting the Grobb Cleaver quest later on.

Getting Equipped: Level 13-18

At level 13, you have received your Dual Wield skill. This allows you to wield 1H weapons in both hands. Pour every practice skill point you have saved into this skill, and you will start to become a storm of blades. For the next few levels you will only occasionally get attacks off with your secondary weapon, but as your skill with DW increases you will get more and more attacks. This is even more valuable at level 15, when you get Double Attack. Again, pour all your practice skill points (save your points from level 14!) into this new skill. This will allow you to occasionally get two attacks per round, or even four attacks per round with Dual Wield.

By now, you will be ready to head out into the great big world. My own suggestion is that your first order of business be to acquire some better armor, and possibly weapons. Remember, AC is the most important stat, so always put armor ahead of weapons. The best place on my server (E’ci) to buy items is the tunnel between North Ro and E. Commons. To get there, zone north from the swamp to South Ro, head north by northwest, zone to Oasis, head north, zone to North Ro, head north by northeast, and you will find the tunnel entrance. Once there, buy as much bronze plate armor (or banded, if no bronze is available) as possible. You might also buy Langesaxes, which are magical swords with a good damage/delay ratio. Bronze plate armor should cost 2pp per AC, banded closer to 1pp per AC. Langseaxes might be available for 20pp each, give or take some plat.

Once you are equipped, you are ready to head to Oasis and battle Orcs, Drybones Skeletons, Caimans and Crocodiles, Mummies, and a host of other baddies. By now you will be more likely to fight in groups, which will reduce downtime dramatically. You will also begin to learn and understand your role as tank, using your taunting skills, huge reserve of hit points, and blades of death, all while your other non-tank group members cower in the second line, away from the fun.

Oasis is a great place to adventure, as there are always many people there, and there is a good range of bad guys. A word of warning: the Oasis is also populated by a few baddies that can kill you in seconds. If anyone shouts about a Sand Giant, Lockjaw, or Spectre, get as far as possible from the action. These guys will kill you before you land a single blow.

Conclusion: Have Fun!

By now you have advanced far enough in your bashing to have developed your own strategies, roles, and network of friends. Perhaps you have joined a guild, or maybe you are still soloing. Regardless, remember that EQ is a game and should only be played if you are having fun. My tank is by far my favorite character, and I hope this guide has given you some ideas for your own enjoyment.
