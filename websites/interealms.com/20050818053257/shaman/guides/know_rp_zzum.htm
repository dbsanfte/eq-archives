Many of you may think you have a good idea of what a Troll shaman is all about, but I'll bet few of you realize that the Troll shaman is, bar none, the most evil, insidious creature in the game. I'm not making that up. Verant themselves state this. When you create your shaman, be sure to read the paragraph that Verant wrote to help us with roleplaying. In our lovely hometown of Grobb we are known only as "The Dark Ones." We are more feared here than any warrior or shadowknight, for we and we alone possess the true power of Innoruuk. We are also know for our fascination with torture. True, all trolls enjoy a good torturing now and then, but nobody on the face of Norrath enjoys it like a Troll shaman. Poison and disease are our absolute favorite things. We love to feel poison coursing through our veins and disease wracking our bodies allmost as much as we enjoy inflicting that same pain on other creatures. Malady and deadly poisons are the true tools of power that Innoruuk has granted us. Most Troll shaman worship Innoruuk, and those who do not are regarded as primative and unfocused. It is our intense loyalty to the lord of hatred, and our twisted ways of serving his will, that make us the most vile biengs on the face of Norrath. Even the dark elves fall short of us in the evil department, sorry inkies, but this is one area we have locked down. ;) You may think that the Troll shaman is "smarter than your average troll." Well, you're wrong. We are just as simple minded as our warrior and shadowknight brethren, but we are simple with a delightfully evil twist. It's that simple fact that makes this class so fun to roleplay.

VIEWS ON OTHER RACES

Ogres: As shaman, we cant help but respect ogres, not only for thier size and brute strength, but for the fact that they are usually our brothers in battle. Ogres are allways welcome amongst the Troll shaman, allthough ogre shaman are usually viewed as inferiors, because of their allmost exclusive servitude of the god of war, Rallos Zek.

Dark Elves: Shaman most definately have a respect for the Tier'Dal, for their ability to master magiks in ways that we as shaman, can not. The Tier'Dal also worship Innoruuk allmost exclusively, which makes the shaman feel even more at home near them. Shaman are aware of the fact that some Tier'Dal view the trolls servitude of Innoruuk as a mockery. Shaman also notice that most Tier'Dal are very arrogant, but tend to put these things aside in favor of keeping the Dark elven race as an ally in the war against the light.

Humans: Ahhh...the humans. Troll Shaman tend to despise humans because they realize the position of power humans now hold over most of the planet. The fact that several powerful humans wander around the Innothule killing young trolls does not help either. Human meat, as a rule, is no delicacy, but, when you're starving in the desert of Ro, a good leg of hummie doesnt sound too bad. Hummies also tend to cry, and beg for their lives more than some other races, especially when they feel our poison sweeping through their bodies and their pathetic eyesight begins to fail..........

Halflings, Dwarves and Gnomes: Trolls find the shorter races of Norrath quite amusing. Shaman especially like halflings. Now, it is true that a good roast dwarf is a tasty treat, (just ask Carver Kagek in grobb.;), but nothing beats a halfling. Haflings tend to be a bit softer than the other races as well, and nothing squeals like a diseased halflie. ;)

The Elven Races: I don't mean to generalize the three groups of elves here, but I'm trying to write this from a troll's point of view, and to a troll, an elf is an elf. Elves are all regarded as weak, pointy-eared hummies, allthough their skin is a bit tougher. The shaman do not fully understand the elves, nor do they seek to. An elf succumbs to Innoruuk's infection just like anyone else, and for a shaman, that is all one needs to know.

Barbarians: The hardy northlanders are a bit of a paradox to troll shaman. To all other trolls, barbarians are just strong, battle-bred hummies, but it is not that simple for troll shamen. Barbarians, you see, are the only "good" race in Norrath that practice Shamanism, and this perplexes troll shamen. It is because of this that troll shamen have a kind of respect for the barbarian peoples, and will deal with them in buisness. However, when Innoruuk's will moves us, a barbarian shaman will go just like the rest..........slowly.

In conclusion, I hope I have helped out any aspiring troll shamen out there. There are strikingly few of us around these days (most shamen tend to be barbarians...blech..). If you want to crusade for the light, then by all means, start a barbarian. But if you want the same shaman powers with a major evil twist and some fun roleplaying potential, then I would suggest you try out a troll. It's not for everyone....but its definately for me! I hope to see many fledgling troll shamen running around soon. Gbal the shadowknight, Xaleos the wizard and myself could allways use a little more help carving up the Commons Guards. :)

Zzum Hammerfyst, Troll Shaman of Erollosi Mar
