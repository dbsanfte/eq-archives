From the unedited diary of Jorgurdar, Shaman of the Tribunal:

   Indeed! In fact I do have the spell scribed and memmed. It seems that after talking to the Herb Vendor in Firiona Vie, she wanted me to search for components for her alchemy practices. I, of course, was more than willing to help.

   It turned out she needed 4 components. Clay Ghiosk, Powder of Yun, Crushed Dread Diamonds, and (what she said was the hardest) Bones of one from the Bath of  Obolus. I was quite baffled, because I had never heard of any of these.  So, off to the wilderness I went. I had heard from a friend that there was a  place nearby they called the Dreadlands, and did the name ever fit. This place had these nasty spiders that looked like an evil Dark Elf experiment gone bad.  They are half (lower) spider and half (upper) dark elf, and cast wizard and  druid spells. There were Dovlrogs of the most difficult kind, wearing bronze and ruined scale armor on their mean dog bodies. Also, I saw oversized monkeys that  folks called Brutes and there were mountain giants too! And these birds that  looked nothing like the aviaks I knew. The veterans of this land called them "chickens"! (quite tasty when cooked, I had heard). 

  Well, after spending some time fighting the hordes of evil in the dreadlands, I ventured to a crumbling castle called Karnor's Castle. Once inside, our small band of travellers found the castle overrun by even more powerful Dovlrogs and undead of every kind. Detached hands and spectres and vile skeletons of all kinds. And what luck! After killing a skeleton, I sifted thru the bones of that skeleton (which, by the markings of its tattered uniform, seemed to be a warlord) and found some bizarre Greyish Bones. I asked my groupmates if I could just stash them away, since they looked like garbage to everyone else. As soon as I picked the bones up, I felt a bond to them and knew I couldn't drop them if I wanted to. I later found out that these were the bones of obolus I was looking for! 

  After the storming of the castle, we all retreated to the comfort of the local tavern in Firiona Vie and there is where I heard of a large group of people heading to the mysterious City of Mist. A city that was overrun with undead minions and the evil black reavers. It was the black reavers and their evil master Lord Ghiosk that the group was after. At the mention of the name Ghiosk,  my ears perked up and I started wondering if I might be able investigate this city with them. After all, it might hold the secret to the Clay of Ghiosk that the Herb woman was looking for. Fortunately for me, I have compatriots in Clan Bravewill, who were the people going, and they had heard of me and wished for me to join them. I was quite honored to join them. The next morning, we sneaked into the City and along the way were met with little resistance, and even killed a behemoth of the local army, and upon inspecting his corpse, I found some Strange Ochre Clay. I put this clay in my pocket, as I could have an enchanter cast his Identify magic on it and learn what it really was, as I had learned to do with the Bones I had found in that vile castle. It seems in the newly discovered lands of Kunark, some things are not as they appear. Well, Lord Ghiosk fell to the victorious Clan Bravewill and upon returning to the elfie outpost of Firiona Vie, I happened upon an enchanter who identified the clay as the Clay of Ghiosk I needed. I was half way through my quest. 

  Well, many days went on and I joined up with many others to fight the minions of evil outside of that outpost. It seemed I had inadvertently joined up with the hearty band of rabble at the outpost. Upon my travels thru one of the nearby swamps, I had heard that there were Froglok Shamans in the area. Thinking maybe I could visit them and possibly exchange greetings and maybe learn new shamanic magics, I went in search of them. And, oh, how I did find them. They were quite hostile and wanted to kill me as soon as they saw me. Not as nice as their apparent relatives in the ruins of Guk on Atonica I had once visited. Realizing that these frogloks were tainted by the evil of Kunark, I enlisted a small band and started to slay the ones who tried to come too near me. On the corpse of one of the shamans was an odd powder. Remembering that the Herb woman wanted some powder I searched out for another enchanter who after identifying the powder, told me it was Nok and Tal powder (see, I had two packets of it), but nei ther was Powder of Yun. But I was sure I had stumbled onto something. 

  Overhearing my conversation with the enchanter was a ranger named Brilon who informed me that there were also more of these Kunark frogloks in a jungle-like land called Trakanon's Teeth, named after the vile dragon Trakanon. He said he would venture to Trak (as we were prone to call it) and help me find more frogloks. I had learned that most people see frogloks and think that if you see one froglok, you have seen them all. Not so true. I have learned how to distinguish them from their markings. Well, anyway, upon entering Trak, the ranger came upon the trail of a froglok. After tracking it down, it definitely had the markings of no Froglok I had ever seen. 

  Then it attacked! He gave up a good fight, but in the end was not even able to withstand the power of a real Shaman! Methinks that the frogloks do not train their shamans as well as we train our barbarian ones. As it turned out, this shaman also had some powder, which I pocketed, and again felt that special bond to the powder, knowing I couldn't drop it. This time my luck was in. Upon returning to Firiona Vie, the powder turned out to be Yun Powder, and I realized that the Froglok must have been a Yun Shaman. 

  I was quite happy having found 3 of the 4 components for the Herb woman, and yet was still plagued with not knowing where in the world to find the Crushed Dread Diamonds that she wanted me to find. I had looked all over the Dreadlands and didn't find any clues, until I saw my friend Hiker the Druid. Ahh, Hiker. He is a good friend I hadn't seen in forever it seemed. We sat and talked of old times and I realized that the last time I had seen him was when we first teleported to  Kunark and went to search the lost valley on the western edge of the dreadlands.

  I had forgotten all about the lost valley! I remember we were killing some skeletons in an undead city that had pyramids bigger than any I had ever seen before. And one skeleton that we had sent back to its maker the locals had told me was commonly known as the Gem Cutter Skeleton. I actually remembered that skeleton, so after a little discussion with Hiker, we figured that the gem cutter skeleton might hold the secret to the Crushed Dread Diamonds. 

  So off I went to the pyramid city, and sure enough the gemcutter skeleton was there, but it wanted to fight with me instead of talking. It was quite weak, so it didn't hurt me at all, so I asked Hiker to locate me an Enchanter (thank The Tribunal for Enchanters!) to charm him, so he wouldn't be so aggressive to me and maybe we could talk. I knew that enchanters had this powers, see, because by now I had spent so much time with them. So, Hiker used his druidic teleportation powers to travel around the world looking for people he might know, and I used my powers to send a telepathic message to all of the dreadlands, seeking an enchanter.

   It turned out a powerful shaman (yes, even more powerful than me!) was married to an enchanter, but was away for a little time. Then Hiker came back with an enchanter he had found too, named Kissie, who charmed the skeleton and sure enough, this tortured soul (the skeleton, not Kissie) wanted to finally talk. It had knowledge of, what it called, sparklies. Weny piped up then and asked it for a dread diamond. (turns out that the Herb vendor woman had enlisted quite a few volunteers to look for her herbs) The Skeleton said that for a Dufrenite, it would hand over a Dread Diamond. This time Kissie had to leave, but Weny's husband had finally arrived, so he kept his charming magic on the skeleton while Weny and I went to town to buy a dufrenite. 

  When I gave the dufrenite to the skeleton and received the diamond, the skeleton told me it was much more valuable in the crushed form, and the only way to crush it was to use my own alchemy skills and grind it with a spectral pestle. Weny had said that spectral pestles were found in the dungeon of Kaesora and had special magic that if you went to sleep, they would disappear, and also formed that familiar bond where you could not give them to others. I was quite distraught, for I, because of lack of funds, had not advanced my alchemy skills much at all. But Weny was quite proficient, and off to Kaesora she went, while I made a makeshift shop to sell my potions I made in the practising of the alchemy skill.

   Two days later, Weny comes back with an amazing tale of how she killed spectral guardians who had pestles on them and how she had crushed the diamonds. I congratulated her on completing the herb woman's quest and Weny then presented me with an extra packet of Crushed Dread Diamonds that she had made. It turns out there were no magical bonds to the diamond or the crushed diamonds, so she was able to give them to me! I was quite excited to be able to complete the quest and when I went to the herb vendor woman, after giving her the components, she presented me with the scroll for the spell McMerrin's Feast (cannibalize II) 

  I was quite excited to have this new spell that had haunted me for weeks now. I am also happy I helped Weny get the spell too, because, as it turns out, she was the first ever to scribe the spell.

  Anyway, that is my tale. I would like to thank everyone who helped me obtain this, including Brilon, Hiker, Peazoup, Kissie, Aliannor, and Congratulations and thanks Weny.

CAN 2 Quest Notes:

Just a few things that haven't been pointed out per se about his insanely difficult quest:   Perhaps worth posting somewhere for those trying to do it?

  1.  Two of the items you need are no-drop.  They are Greyish Bone Chips (from the skeletal warlord in Karnor's), and Froglok Yun Shaman Powder (from froglok yun shamans in Trakanon's Teeth)  2.    Two of the items you need to get are NOT no drop and can be traded.  They are Strange Ochre Clay (drops from army behemoths in The City Of Mist), and the dread diamond - both in it's crushed and uncrushed form.  (The dread diamond is obtained by getting a necro or enchanter to charm the skeletal gemcutter in The Dreadlands in the old combine outpost area, and giving him a dufrenite.  The dread diamond is crushed by combining it with a spectral pestle [dropped from spectral guardians in Kaesora - these spawns right at the zone entry off and on - so no need to go in that deep if you don't want to.  They also spawn in the church area in the bottom of the place.] in a medicine bag.  Note that the spectral pestles seem to drop off of about 50% of the spectral guardians in Kaesora, and they are lore so you can only have one at a time.  If you fail your alchemy skill roll in attempting to combine, you WILL get your dread diamond back.  It's possible to succeed at crushing the diamond with alchemy skill as low as 33 as far as I have confirmed personally.  <Succeeded after three tries with 33 skill>

  3.    You can do this quest with apprehensive faction and succeed.  I did so with a higher level mule myself as I found it easier to obtain many of the harder items that way.  For those that wish to go this route note that you must obtain the greyish bone chips and the froglok yun shaman powder with the character that is going to be turning in the items eventually.  For the other 2 whomever can get them, as they can be traded.

  4.    The spell you recieve for this is labeled McMerin's Feast - don't worry - it's cannibalize II when you mem it <g>  It is NOT no drop - so the whole mule idea works fine.  Simply have the mule hand the spell over to your shaman self. 

  5.    The greyish bone chips and strange ochre clay can usually be obtained eventually just by hanging out at the Karnor's and City Of Mist zones and asking folks if you can loot it if it's killed near the zone <in the case of the skeletal warlord>, or if someone that has some clay can hand it to you when they zone out <in the case of the City Of Mist>

  6.    The froglok yun shaman powder you'll probably need to bring people with and hunt one down yourself, I had little luck with asking to loot this stuff off of someone's kill.  A tracker will be VERY useful here - as they really aren't all that common.  Careful - Trakanon's is not a very friendly zone - lock n' load.

   Just stuff that I noted while doing the quest myself.  Hopefully these might help people actually working on this quest get the spell %)  It's possible - just nastily difficult.

   Abdiel Al`Azard - 37 Iksar Shaman - Bertoxx

First off, this quest is NOT an easy task at all. it seems more level 50+ then 39.  For starters you need some Clay of Ghiosk, which comes from city of mist in emerald jungle.  The mob is  an army behemoth that is level 42 with about 4000 hit points.  Second you need grey bone chips. These come from the Skeletal Warlord in kanor, which can be pulled from moat, still another amible foe.  Third you need the yun shaman powder.  I got this near the ruined city of sebilis, it comes off yun shamans which are level 37 - 41 and have about 3000 hps, they hit for  lower-mid 90s so be carefull.  Last the ever elusive crushed dread diamonds.  Go buy a dufrenite.

  Go to Dreadlands.

  Have someone charm the Gem Cutter Skeleton that spawns in the mass of wizard teleporters in Dreadlands.  Now that you have indifferent faction to the charmed skeleton, you can hail him and ask about dread diamond(s) Give him a dufrenite.  You get Dread Diamond (Lore item)

  Head back to Trakanon's teeth

  Kill Spectral Partisans (30 - 34 about 1300 hit points) until you get a spectral pestle (lore, no drop, no rent) Combine the pestle and the diamond in an alchemy bag and hope your alchemy skill is high enough to succeed.  I did it on the 2nd try with 30 skill, it becomes trival at about 70.  If you fail the alchemy check, you get the diamond back and have to try again. Go to step 8.  If you succeed the alchemy check, you get Crushed Diamond(s) (lore item).

  Return all 4 items in firiona to the lady who gives the quest for Cannibalize II: Decrease Hit Points (HP) by 67 (L39)

  Increase Mana from 28 (L39) to 33 (L60) Enjoy!

  Few notes:

     The Spectral pestle comes from both kaesora and trakanon's teeth, I found trakanon's teeth much easier to get it from.  The shaman powder is black, and identifys as Powder of Yun.      Make sure you have enough faction with firiona vie (or the ogres if you are evil) when you turn in the items!  The spell is not no drop, so you can trade it if you want.  Dread diamonds, both crushed and normal are tradeable, along with the yun powder.  If you hang out in karnor some, you can usally get bone chips. 

  Be safe, and good travels.
