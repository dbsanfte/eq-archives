A GUIDE TO SOLUSEK A

by Faenor Ravenwing of Sanctus Libra

Deep in the sinister Lavastorm mountains lies a dungeon inhabited by the fire goblins and the gnomes of the Solusek A mining company. This is a great place to hunt for players between levels 20 and 35 or so and yields some excellent loot for those levels.

Unless you are an evil/neutral race and you like to hunt gnomes, your main prey in Solusek A will be goblins. They range from ‘Young Goblins’ to ‘Cinder Goblins’, ‘Fire Goblins’, ‘Inferno Goblins’ to ‘Solusek Goblins’. Young goblins are around level 20 and Solusek Champions around level 30. Coming face to face with an Inferno Goblin Wizard can be unnerving to most players!

The most intimidating feature of Solusek A however is the entrance. A narrow bridge crosses a lava pool into the zone proper. Walking across this bridge scares a lot of people (some people I know still insist on levitation before crossing the bridge) and running across it back to the zone with some furious goblins in tow can be very dangerous. Falling into the lava below is very bad since you will most probably die down there and will have to mount a rescue operation involving levitating over lava and other unpleasantness. I have never fallen in myself but I have seen plenty of people drown a fiery death.

Fortunately there are other escape routes leading into Solusek B, the lair of Nagafen the dragon. This sounds far more dangerous than it is, the dragon himself is far removed from any of the Solusek B zones that connect to Solusek A. However you always run the risk of meeting a train of far worse monsters at the other end. One lava duct crawler is far worse than 5 enraged goblins let me tell you! However I have zoned into Solusek B many times from Solusek A and rarely did I encounter anything dangerous. So when faced with the decision to zone back into Lavastorm across the lava bridge or to zone into Solusek B a player has to do what they feel comfortable with. Also depending on where you are in Solusek A you might not have a choice other than to flee to Solusek B.

There are three areas to Solusek A each with its own difficulty level. First there is the ‘north area’ that houses mostly cinder and fire goblins and is suited for levels 20 to 30 or so. The ‘south area’ is where the goblin king and the bartender are and contains mostly inferno goblins. This area is for levels 25-35. Then there is the gnome area. I haven’t explored this area much, there is an actual castle full of gnomes and their clockworks.

The gnome area

If you are a ranger you really shouldn’t hunt gnomes since you need to venture deep into Ak’Anon for the Rain Caller quest. Also there really is no need to kill the little guys. However if you want Ivy boots you do need a piece of melatite that can be found on clockworks. Killing clockworks will lower gnome faction and if any gnomes see you killing their machinery they will attack. So if you do want to get your own melatite be very careful and try to keep the killing to a minimum. A better plan would be to try to buy a piece from another player or if you are really lucky (like me) you have a friend who finds a piece for sale at a merchant! To raise gnome faction, kill minotaurs in the cave in Steamfont. I haven’t spent much time around the gnome castle. One thing of interest is the Lava Elemental that sometimes drops the prized Runed Lava Pendant (the regular drop is an inferno shield potion), a very nice neck piece for melee types. (ac5, str+6, vs. fire +5). Getting this is another story since it is surrounded by gnomes who will not tolerate anyone killing their elemental.

The clockworks and unfortunate gnomes I fought helping someone get his melatite were around lvl30-35.

The following special mobs can be found in the gnome area:

Guardian Model EXG, this drops a Charred Guardian Shield (ac10, wis+9) or a Charred Guardian Breastplate (ac16, str+3, sta+6) as its rare drop.

Captain Bipnupple, he drops a very heavy Brick of Blue Ore or a Gnomish Environmental Suit (ac9, vs.fire+10,vs.cold+10,vs.poison+10) as his rare drop.

Gabbie Mardoddle, he drops a Mithril Quill (piercing, dmg 4, del 19, hp+20) or a Molten Cloak (ac4, int+4, wis+4) as his rare drop.

On goblins

These are nasty little creatures that are social (i.e. they will help each other) and since there are goblin shamans in the dungeon, there is a good chance that they will run fast when about to die. For some reason goblins and other non-player shamans can cast sow indoors. Needless to say having a runner is usually the cause of trains and death. So use that ever so important snare since it will be a lifesaver in this dungeon like in most others.

Some goblins also back stab which is dangerous when fighting more than one or when running to the zone. If possible, keep your back to the wall when fighting since being back stabbed for 120 points while in a heated battle is not good!

The casters (wizards, shamans and mages) will also see through invisibility. This means corpse retrievals and exploration missions are severely hampered. Not good being seen while deep inside the dungeon and having to race to a zone with a train of 20 goblins in tow.

One other annoying thing is that all goblins in this dungeon are over level 17 which means they will attack anyone no matter what their level. So don’t expect a free walk down to the king’s room when you are level 35, even the young goblins will hurl themselves at you with blind fury.

One important item that is dropped by some Inferno goblins is the Heart of Fire. This is part of the Ivy Sleeves quest that rangers will do at some point in their lives. I found that the best part of the dungeon to find these hearts is the south side. The other item needed for this quest is a rune of ivy off Grimfeather which is a particularly nasty Griffon that roams around North Karana.

The entrance

Past the lava bridge is a small room that is usually empty since most people entering the zone will have to kill the goblin in it to pass by. This room has a window that most small and medium sized races will fit through. It requires some fiddling with jumping and crouching but it’s better than crossing the second part of the bridge. This window is actually quite important since goblins do not go through it but around across the second lava bridge instead. This buys you a few extra seconds to navigate the bridge to the zone. If a wizard is chasing you, take extra care since their force shock spell could actually knock you off the bridge…

Once in the little room you can head straight out onto a ledge overlooking a pit with large double doors on the opposite side. These doors lead to the jail/bar/king area that is more difficult than the north side of the dungeon. More about it later.

To the right a passage will take you straight to a Solusek B zone border. Since the Lavastorm zone and this Solusek B zone are pretty much the same distance from this point you can make a decision here when running for the zone. Either head left to Solusek B or straight through the little window room to the Lavastorm zone. Zoning into lavastorm means crossing the bridge but pretty much guaranteed security at the other end, zoning into Solusek B means no lava to cross but a possible Kobold at the other end in Solusek B that will kill you faster than any goblins ever can. There is also a camp spot there called ‘the pit’ by some people so if you are pulling a train to zone and decide to head for the Solusek B zone make sure you announce it so the people camping there know what is about to hit them.

If you go left after entering the central area and left again across the tiny bridge over the lava (this one is not dangerous) you can head straight north into goblin territory or north-east towards the gnome castle.

Before your first serious expedition into Solusek A I strongly suggest you get a map of some sorts. The dungeon has lots of twisty passages and a large number of fake walls. Explaining these in words would get too confusing. I used Everlore’s excellent map for all my Solusek A adventures that can be found here: http://www.everlore.com/geography/solseka_map.asp

The north side

This part of the dungeon is great for levels 20 to 30. Take a left after entering the central area and another left across the small lava bridge. Head right from the little room and then left through the fake wall, down the stairs following the passage. There are several spawn points along the way of young, cinder and fire goblins so you may have to fight your way there if the dungeon isn’t busy. From here you can go into the Foreman room (through another fake wall) and past the Foreman room into the Kobold Predator room. A bit further north is the three door room that leads west to another Solusek B zone, north to the Efreeti and Captain area and east towards the gnomes.

A breakdown of the named spawns and camp spots:

  • Goblin Foreman – this is a room with 3 goblin spawns in it. Sometimes the Foreman spawns and he will drop an Obsidian Scimitar (1HS, dmg7, del27, agi+6) or the Foreman Tunic (ac10, str+3, wis+3, wt.0) which is a nice item for druids. You will need a good group of mid-twenties players to hold this room. It is a decent place for pulling from the rest of the dungeon but depending on how busy it is a much better spot for pulling from the immediate area and the Foreman room is the zone to Solusek B just around the corner.
  • Solusek B zone – if you take the corridor north just before the foreman room you end up in the 3-door room. Heading west from the 3-door room you end up in a small corridor that leads to a Solusek B zone. This is a pretty safe zone since the Solusek B area it leads to is a bit off the beaten path and doesn’t see trains very often. But you can never be 100% sure you won’t find a Greater Kobold waiting for you at the other side so be aware of that. The proximity to the zone and some prime spots in Solusek A like the Foreman room, the Kobold Predator and the Efreeti area make this a very good spot to camp. If the dungeon is not that busy a good group can control most of the north west area from this spot. With a good puller you will have a pretty much non-stop stream of goblins at your disposal.
  • Kobold Predator – past the foreman room a corridor slopes down and curves around into a larger room with a few goblin spawns in it and the Kobold Predator on a pedestal in the middle of the room. The Predator drops the Kobold Hide Boots (ac5, agi+3, hp+10) or the Obsidian Shard (piercing, dmg 6, del 25, effect obsidian shard) as it’s rare drop. Fighting the predator is a nice prelude to your later exploits in Solusek B after you hit 35 or so and you fight Kobolds mainly. Their howl will soon become a very familiar sound indeed…
  • Singe – the 3-door room is also the spawn point of a fire drake named Singe. It roams however around the north west part of the dungeon so needs to be hunted down once spawned. Singe drops the Obsidian Bead Hoop (ac2, agi+3,int+3) and the Drake-Hide Belt (ac5, dex+3,vs.fire+5) as it’s rare drop.

From the 3 door room with the Solusek B zone to the west, the Foreman room to the south and the corridors leading to the Gnome area to the east you can head north to the Efreeti / Captain area. The Efreeti is guarded by 2 fire elementals that have nasty fire damage shields. When pulling these we used to have a rule that nobody attacks until they have been dispelled. When you are a fast and furious dual wielder, a high damage shield can hurt! It is hard to get the elementals without the Efreeti (or vice versa) so if you decide to pull them make sure your group can handle the 3 of them. This also a time when you can actually use that ‘ward summoned’ spell since it does decent DD damage to the elementals that are summoned beings.

The Efreeti drops Platinum Armbands (ac0, str+3, wis+3) or the Obsidian Flamberge (2hs dmg20, del50, effect Obsidian shatter) as his rare drop.

At the northern most point of the dungeon is the Fire Goblin Captain’s room. He drops the Charred Boots (ac5, agi+5, vs.fire+5, vs.poison+5) or the Charred Gauntlets (ac7, dex+3, int+5) as his rare drop. I have never actually been to the Captain room so I can’t say that much about it but like the rest of the north area I expect a good group of mid 20’s to be able to do it.

Around the Captain / Efreeti area you will also find Kindle, a fire imp who roams around. He drops an Obsidian Ring (ac3,dex+3,hp+10) or a pair of Impskin Gloves (ac2, int+5, mana+10) as his rare drop.

The south side

When you are up for a real challenge and you have a good group in their low 30’s you can try to tackle a room in the south side of the dungeon. After entering the zone you can use the pit in front of the big gate as an initial pulling point to clear the way to the room of your choice. The south side contains inferno goblins mostly, plus the highest level goblins in Solusek A, the Solusek Goblins. Their champions are fierce melee fighters and a Solusek Mage can give a level 40+ character a hard time.

The Bar

After you go through the doors and past the gate you can head left towards the Bar. This is a great room to camp in and pull a good part of the south side. It has 4 spawns plus the bartender or his placeholder behind the bar and the Goblin Drunkard in a room off the side.

Holding the bar takes a good group effort with a skilled enchanter or bard for crowd control. Spawns need to be timed and pulls can get messy due to roaming goblins and some pathing issues. However I made an ungodly amount of experience in the bar room over the course of a few evenings in my low 30s.

A few tips for the bar room. First of all, the bar can be used to your advantage. When dealing with multiples and one of your casters gets attacked by a goblin, get them to hop across the bar. The goblin will not hop across but take the long way around, giving your caster a chance to get a spell off and hop across the bar again to repeat the process. It may be a bit of a cheat but in a 5-goblin battle it may be the only thing that keeps you alive.

Also, fight as close to the bar as possible. You don’t want someone straying too close to the side rooms by accident and attracting the spawns in there. Also, when pulling from the side rooms, make sure to shepherd the goblin into the room through the side door. If you run too fast, the goblin will take the long way around (through the corridor) and will attract any goblins in the corridor or the rooms close to it.

The bartender drops the Drake-Hide Leggings (AC5, dex+5, cha+5) or the Drake-Hide Sleeves (AC4, sta+3, hp+10) as his rare drop. The drunkard in the next room is a mage (he has a pet sometimes) and drops the Frothy Goblin Tonic (regeneration effect) or the Staff of Fire (dmg 5, del 25, vs.fire+5, effect burst of flame) as his rare drop.

The King

When you head right after entering the south side through the gate you go through a fake wall into a long corridor that angles to the left. At the end of the corridor is the so-called King room. This room can also be approached from the other side by taking a left directly in front of the fake wall, past the goblin merchant. This merchant will not attack and if you sneak up on him you can actually sell there.

The king room is possibly the hardest room in the dungeon (apart from the gnome castle) since it has the Goblin King (which is a heavy caster) and the Solusek Champions in it. The king himself spawns in a little room at the west side of the King room with 2 other goblins. You can pull him and his companions into the long corridor before entering the bigger part of the room with the sunken floor. Be careful with the initial pulls, getting a fully spawned king room all at once is enough to obliterate the strongest of groups.

The king drops the Ring of the Goblin Lords (ac4, str+3, int+3) or the Sceptre of Flame (1HB, dam 7, del 29, vs. magic+5, vs.fire+5,mana+15) as his rare drop.

The Jail

At the east end of the King room area is a stairway going down to the jail area. This is a nice bit of the dungeon with jail cells containing goblins. Don’t be fooled though, the cell doors open up and the goblins will come out to beat on you. At the end of the corridor with the jail cells is a room where the Inferno Goblin Torturer sometimes spawns. He drops a Turquoise Eyepatch (ac2, sta+2,int+2, cha+2) or a Momento Box as his rare drop. This box is a 6 slot small item container that is rumoured to have 80% weight reduction. I have never seen one of these so I can’t confirm that.

High Shaman

At the very southern end of the dungeon is the High Shaman room. This part of the dungeon is pretty confusing with a lot of corridors and rooms so make sure you have a map ready. There is also a Solusek B zone around here. This is actually a very dangerous zone since it zones to the so-called ‘bat bridge’ of Solusek B. This is a prime sonic bat / lava beetle / lava duct crawler pulling spot and any of these will kill you without breaking into a sweat.

The high shaman drops a Glowing Stone Band (ac2, serpent sight) or a Platinum Dragon Totem (hp+30, mana+50) as his rare drop.

Conclusion

Solusek A is a good zone to hone your group’s skills and it will prepare you nicely for the more difficult zones like Solusek B, Mistmoore Castle, Lower Guk and Kedge Keep. Being able to break a full spawn and hold a room like the Bar or the King Room for a longer period of time is a good challenge and can be very rewarding in both experience and items you find. I spent a lot of time in Solusek A between my 28th and 34th levels and made a lot of money off the special items and the fine steel weapons the higher-level goblins drop. And some of the really nice items like some of the weapons and armour pieces are still being handed down in our guild as heirlooms almost.

However it can be a harsh zone as well. Getting overwhelmed in the king room can be devastating to your group and due to the high amount of goblins that can see invisible, corpse retrieval can be very difficult. (I remember doing a few mad corpse-dragging runs, buff up completely at the zone and just run for it, shouting train all the way down J ). A party member capable of evacuation should always have this spell up when fighting here, I remember a few tense times when evac saved all our lives and we hugged our druid afterwards. Also, an enchanter or bard with good crowd control skills can make a huge difference.

Enjoy,
