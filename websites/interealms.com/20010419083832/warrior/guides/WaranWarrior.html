A quiet night sky falls over Freeport. The starts shining brightly upon the adventurers that pass through the city. As the dark night falls, Waran sits in the Arena amongst his trainers that have trained him in the art of battle all through his years. As he sits, he removes helm and begins sharpening his newly discovered blade from the new lands with a sharpening rock. Up and down the edges, he takes such great care in perfecting his weapon to meet his own requirements. Visually, you can see the wear on Waran’s armor. A couple of dents, scratches, and indentations here and there, its not that obvious, but quite a bit of difference than the armor you see younger Warriors start out with. Waran starts to reflect on his battles and adventures in his life. Exploring Blackburrow for the first time, seeking revenge on the Snow Orc Shaman in Everfrost with Xirius, his first trip across Antonica to seek out his future LOA brethren, his first trip into Crushbone, his first Hill Giant kill, all these images flood through his mind as he concentrates on repairing his swords and armor.

As he continues, there is a noticeable running noise coming from the Arena entrance. At once, all the guildtrainers give out an audible sound of "Ugh.." as the young Warrior heads towards Waran and the guildtrainers. Waran looks up to see who is giving the trainers so much pain in training him and was quite surprised to see who it was. Young Warazan clambers up the steps, fumbling over his own two feet, towards his father and the guildtrainers, practically bringing every armor piece, weapon, sword, item, you name it, with him in his stuffed pack. He, on many instances, had asked every guildtrainer in Freeport which was better than which. Waran stands up from his armor and newly sharpened weapon, moving toward the guild head master. All that he does is nod to the head guild trainer, letting him know, nonverbally, that he would take over training him since he was back in Freeport from Kunark….

Warazan could sense that his father was not pleased with him and followed behind him with his head down in shame. Waran heads back to his items, puts his armor on and heads to one of the side rooms in the Arena where he can talk to Warazan alone. As Waran enters the room, he puts his things down and quickly turns around to Warazan, seeing him with his head down. "Warriors never look down in shame son, pull your chin up" in a very strict tone, Waran says to his son. "You’ve been aways so long poppa, sisy Cali be too bigs for me ta fight with her no more, and me haven’t seen momma since I be born pracktikly!" Warazan mutters. Waran looks at his son intently with increasing concern, "Who has been training you and what have you been doing?" he questions. "Well….me got sleepy….been sleepin and kinda missed all dem training tingys….didn’t mean ta, just couln get up., Warazan says. "Let me start with the basics then, since it appears you’ve decided to sleep instead of attending formal Warrior training. Question 1. What is a Warrior?" Waran begins. "Someone who fights a lot?" Warazan says in a snap. Waran grins as he looks at his son and says, "Is that all we are?" Warazan completely confused, scratching his head, only answers with silence. "You definitely have much to learn my young Warrior son, lets begin"…

A Warrior is a master of combat. We are always in the thick of battle. Fighting and taunting monsters; battling fiercely at every moment. We can use any weapon, from blunt weapons, piercing weapons, throwing weapons, bows, and slashing weapons, we can wield them all. We don’t put our noses in books, we have time to enjoy our surroundings, always alert, always ready for battle.

When you decide to take your Oath of a Warrior, there are 3 stats that you must focus on when you begin. Your Strength (STR), Stamina (STA), and Agility (AGI) are the three most important stats for a Warrior to have before he chooses to follow the path of the Warrior. Your STR determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills. Your STA affects how many hit points you have and how long you can hold your breath underwater. Your AGI affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit. You will have +25 points to add to your base stats, so depending on what you want to do with your stats, divide them accordingly. When I decided to take the Oath of the Warrior for the Wolves of the North, I divided my points mostly into Strength and Stamina, leaving my Agility lower than usual. I do get hit more because of this, but that is the way of the Warrior. You will get hit…a lot! You have the most hit points than any other class in Norrath and rightly so. You are the damage absorber, or "sponge" to take the hits while others in your group fight by your side and blast and melee right along with you. That is your job in any group that you are in.

"Warazan are you listening?" Waran asks intently. With a huge yawn Warazan says, "Yeah poppa, me tryin ta listen ta ya". Waran gives Warazan an icy glare and says "You better listen to me, because I expect to see what you’ve learned from all this afterwards." Warazan groans and says, "Ok poppa"…

One of the most important skills to train in is Taunt. When at your specific guild trainer, at level 1, you will have 5 training points after handing in your guild note. You should train in 1 Handed Slash/2 Handed Slash, kick and taunt, most importantly. Funny story is, I had assumed Taunt was an automatic thing and was using it, untrained, all the way till my 20s until a very nice Dwarven Warrior by the name of Bojo informed me of my mistake. At later levels you will use Taunt very often to "piss off" the different monsters that are attacking your group to attack you instead. This is a very useful skill, especially if there is a healer or caster in your group yelling and screaming at your group "ONE ON ME, ONE ON ME, GET IT OFF!" You should be able to taunt it off the frail casters with relative ease once your skill is high enough. Use Taunt all the time, especially when you have a full group and are fighting 1 monster, taunt taunt taunt, to increase your skill. At later levels, you will use taunt selectively in different situations. You should learn how to manage group damage, as is, know when to stop taking damage, sit back and let others take some of the damage, then join the fray again and finish the mob off. Taunt should not always be used in some situations, you’ll have to determine when to taunt and when not to taunt. As another note, after you skill increases to the point where it costs money to train in them, don’t waste your money, it will go up automatically. Later, when you get a bow and arrows, you should train in Archery, as it is your effective way of pulling monsters for your group to kill. Train in that and fletching where you can make your own arrows rather than buying them. I suggest going to http://interealms.com/ranger/guides/arrowsintro.html to find info on fletching arrow combinations, Sunmoon has quite a nice list there.

"Have you trained in Taunt, son?" Waran asks to make sure his son is still awake. Warazan says, "Me thinks so, me not sure what me trained in, but me thinks dat be one of dem" Waran rolls his eyes and continues…

Now, one question a Warrior always has is, Which weapon should I practice in and fight in a majority of my time? The generic all around answer is "Train in them all". You never know what amazing weapon will come your way down the road, and you don’t want to be in a position to not be able to use a weapon because your skill is nonexistent. This is something I probably should have followed myself, but nonetheless, train in them all, you will thank me later. I didn’t train in 2H Slash until my early to mid 20s because I didn’t see the point. Boy was I wrong. It took me at least 10 levels and tons of experience points to lift my 2H slash skill to where it should be and where it is today. What I usually do to increase both skills is when I reach my halfway point in my experience bar, I switch to my other discipline of weaponry. This tactic works very well.

"But what I do wit all dis stuff me gots in mi’pack? Which be da one me should use?!?" Warazan says eagerly. Waran lifts his pack and dumps its contents on the ground before them. It’s filled with rusty swords, bone chips, cat pelts, goblin ice necklaces, and other assorted items. "Sell them son, you’re keep rats in here too?!?!" Waran says jokingly. Warazan stands before his father in his full bronze, slouching a bit, still acting if he was still asleep standing up. Waran lifts him from his slouch and says, "STAND WARRIOR", echoing through the halls of the Freeport Arena. With guild trainers peeking around the corner, Warazan immediately stands at attention. Waran moves around him in a circle, adjusting his posture. "By Rallos boy, ye true Warrior posture needs some work!!!" he says. "Ok, I guess that will do for now, you must not just train to be a warrior and continue in your battles, you must look and feel the Warrior spirit within you. Let it fill your mind and soul. You must follow Rallos Zek and he will give you the power to do this" looking into Warazan’s eyes as he says this. "Let’s begin son…"

With the sun rising slowly upon the Freeport sky, Warazan heads out of the room with his father behind him. As he is walking out, Waran grins and kicks Warazan in his arse, with his son falling to the ground, to have a little fun with him. Waran winks at the guildtrainers to show them that Warazan’s father was still able to give him a kick in his arse for slacking in his Warrior ways. Warazan blushes among all the laughter in the Arena, while his father comes to led him a hand getting up off the ground. "Learn to have fun young Warrior, learn to laugh and learn to laugh at yourself" with a mighty Warrior laugh. Waran and Warazan both head out of the Arena, with the wind at their backs and their blades drawn. To new battles and new adventures!!
