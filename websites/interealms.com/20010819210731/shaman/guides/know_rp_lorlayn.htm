I am a Barbarian Shaman on Mithaniel Marr who has left Halas and am currently adventuring in the Rivervale area. To try and give my character a little more depth I play with the following attitudes:

I don't like humans -- I stayed in Everfrost for my first nine levels and couldn't believe how many humans I would find hunting our animals. As if it isn't bad enough they have spoiled their own lands... must they invade ours as well?

I cast any spell I have on a fellow barbarian for free. I will jump into a fight to aid a brother or sister of our race. I will discount any items I auction if a barbarian is the purchaser. I believe that unity is our greatest weapons against invading humans. I do accept donations from our people, but only after some protest.

I will generally cast beneficial spells on players of the race of the place I am adventuring in. Thus while I am in Rivervale I help the halflings as much as I can. I consider this a natural duty of being a guest to their land.

I will cast spells for a minor donation on higher level characters (free if they are trying to recover their corpse), but the price goes up (steeply) if they have a human in their party and much more steeply if the human is a Qeynos native. I never state a price for my services unless a human is present, I just ask for a reasonable donation and leave it on the head of the asker to do what is just.

I feel the barbarian's are a proud race and refuse to beg for help and have taken the time to lecture others of our race who did. This has provided some wonderful role-playing moments in the game.

I intend to cast Bind Affinity for free for all but humans. This just fits with my own personal "it sucks to die policy". However, I will never bind anyone but a barbarian in Halas without a substantial donation. There is no need to encourage people to invade our homeland.

I have a natural affinity for elves, wood elves and non-human druids. These people seem to respect nature even if they don't understand its relationship to the spirit world.

I don't believe in haggling. When an auction call is placed, I ask the person for the lowest price he will take. If the price is not fair, I say no and seek the item elsewhere. I believe that all objects have a value and it is not just to ask more than that value. However, I do admit that some objects are more valuable to others then they might be to me. So asking too much for an item, does not generate any ill will.

I like to group and of course any spell I have is free to a group-mate or a friend, but I do tend to mutter in character if a human is in the same group I am in. (I generally let the human know out of character that I bear him no real ill will its just my character).

Lorlayn of Mithaniel Marr
