The Shaman's Guide to Research: Another Source of your 50-60 Spells

Having trouble getting your 50-60 spells? Research them! Well, okay, you can't actually do the research yourself. Only the int casters (enchanters, magicians, necromancers, and wizards) can actually do research. But you can gather the ingredients for them to make you your very own copy of Levitation, Acumen of Dar Khura, or yes, even Torpor!

How does research work?
Int casters are able to start researching at level 16. Their skill advances much like alchemy, with the ability to gain another 5 points of skill each time they level. The highest skill a researcher can have is 200, although the use of skill modifiers like the Geerlok Automated Quill will allow their effective skill to be higher than 200.

A researcher creates new spells by combining their ingredients in their research tome. Each of the four int caster classes has some spells under 50 that are only available by researching. None of those spells is No Drop, however, so many of them buy those spells from other people rather than investing the time and money to become researchers themselves. Just like any other tradeskill, it is a lot of work. Also, keep in mind that just like other tradeskills, researchers can fail their attempts. These recipes all have very high trivials, some over 200. So be prepared for the possibility that you may need to gather components more than once.

My researcher has never made 50-60 spells. Is it the same?
Researchers usually can only make spells for their own class. The exceptions to this are 50-60 priest spells and 50-60 spells that they share with other research classes. So any researcher can make any 50-60 spell usable by a cleric, druid, or shaman. They will need to use the advanced research container available from talking with Researcher Alvarik in the Gulf of Gunthak. In addition to the dropped ingredients, they will also need a parchment and quill available from a variety of vendors.

So where do I get the stuff my researcher needs?
The dropped ingredients come from creatures of approximately the same level as the spell being made. In cases where the same component can make spells of different levels, it is the lowest level of spell that the item can make that determines which creatures will drop it. So if a component is used to make both a 51 and a 58 spell, it will drop off creatures that are around level 51. However, the higher level spells usually have at least one or two components that are exclusively for high level spells and therefore exclusively available in difficult places. Some components, for example, have only been reported from the sewers of Cazic Thule or in Vex Thal. Yes, Vex Thal. But as none of the components are No Drop, the Bazaar is always another possible source.

Once you have the drops that your researcher needs, pick up a piece of parchment and a quill from one of the vendors listed in the Supply Vendors in the link below.

I can get all my 50-60 shaman spells this way?
Almost. Ancient spells for any class, including shamans, cannot be researched. In addition, Primal Avatar is not researchable because it is No Drop. The scroll that is created by a researcher is identical to the one that drops. So it wouldn't do anyone any good if an int caster researchs a shaman-only No Drop spell. You will still need to go to Sleeper's Tomb for that one.
