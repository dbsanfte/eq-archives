A viewer sent me this reply he received from GZ concerning the balance issues with the classes, Wizards in particular. I apologize to the submitter for taking so long to post this, but I wished to get GZ's permission to do so. So, here it is in all its glory.... please take all discussion to the Rant message board. Geoffrey Z: "I honestly feel that most spell casting classes are pretty well balanced. Wizards complain a lot that they don't have the versatility at higher levels - but I also get an equal number of e-mails from Druids, saying that they wish that they were more specialized. Most people won't be happy with this - they feel that their particular class is underpowered. We let Wizards have DoTs, and then Necros and Shamans say they aren't needed, etc.). We are in a pretty good balance state right now... Geoffrey C. Zatkin"

- 11:30am PST
Recieving alot of emails asking my further thoughts upon Asheron's Call. I posted this to the General Message Board a couple days ago and I'll post it here so you can read.

"What I found to be the greatest aspect of being a caster in AC was the thinking that went along with getting your spells. You dont need to goto your vendor and buy the spell.. you go get components (much like the research practice in EQ.. yet in AC they are purchasable from the magic vendors), once you get the components you can create your spells if you know the right ingredients and order they go in. Now.. in game right now, the higher level players are not telling their ingredients unless you trade for info.. but you know what, Jodowein (this was a person that I posted under) is right.. there will be sites that will list this info quickly. Then again, I have also been told that what works for one mage may not work for another.. so that spells will be totally user specific. (Kinda nice to see a spell that only you can cast).

As for the Graphics.. not that great at all. But also keep in mind that this is a BETA. In most betas, the graphic enhancements come last. So from the way it is now, I have a feeling that it may have the ability to surpass EQ.. even with their lame D3D. (This may be rivaled again when the expansion for EQ comes out and we all get more polygons... just wait to see).

The best thing I noticed.. NO ZONES. You computer 'reads ahead' for the landscape and loads them as you run. The more you play, the faster the game will become due to it caching all of the graphics. The closest thing to a zone is called a 'portal'. The neat thing about portals is that you can still chat while in one.. you don't sit there with your computer locked up wondering if you are going to be looking at your login screen or desktop anytime soon. =)

All in all, the game has great promise. For a game slated to be released in December.. they have quite a in depth game in their hands. Who wouldn't with the financial backing of Microsoft capital. So my final note is.. don't sweat trying to get into AC, If EQ would focus on quests, like the name suggests, they would have no worry about people defecting. Damn I hope a developer at Verant reads that."

Now for some additional thoughts. When I beta tested for Everquest, I was simply blown away. Graphics, user interface, size of the world, etc., all amazed me. When I fired up Asheron's Call for the first time, I did not have that feeling that Everquest gave me. Everquest still has the ability to pull me in and make me feel like I am actually within the world... this is something Asheron's Call did not do for me. Keep in mind this is just my opinion and many others out there may feel different. Don't judge the game just from my point of view, wait until you can play it for yourself before making a decision. - Graffë

- 5:15pm PST
I've been in contact with Geoffrey Z (Lead Spell Designer/Programmer for Verant) as of late and I thought some of you might find our conversation of interest. This is concerning our Resist problems we as Wizards face:

Graffe: The largest complaint I receive is our +6 level damage cap. This is hard for me to respond to the viewers with due to the fact that I don't have the exact numbers on why this happens. What they are complaining about is that if we cast on a beast that is of 6 levels higher than us, we get a full resist. This greatly reduces our use in a higher class group. I do know that when I fire upon a red and it resists the spell fully, it still takes that resist as a full hit and adds me to its hatred list. Here is the scenario that I have personally experienced. I cast Thunder Strike on a mob (which would normally do 250 damage at my current level) and it resists the cast fully. If I do not wait for the tanks to raise its hatred level to them and I cast it again for a full resist, it will come directly for me. From higher level wizards, I hear that the Fire Giants within Solusek resist everything they cast (that comes from two wizards, one level 49 the other 50). I was told by my level 49 contact that this is due to an exploit that could be taken advantage of. My suggestion is that the resist cap is removed, but do not let us hit for our full damage... lessen the damage on tougher creatures so that we can once again be useful within a group. Not only used for evacuation purposes.

Geoffrey Z: It actually isn't a 6 lvl cap - it is >5 lvls OR more than 1.2 of the casters level - so at higher levels, you can effect things 7-9 levels above you. Anything which falls into the above catagory gets a much better saving throw - not 100%, but much better. This was done for balance reasons - most classes use indirect magic to get around it. The Wizard does have trouble in that they do not get any good indirect magic (healing, damage shields, quickness spells, etc.). It is one of the downsides of playing a Wizard. Have a good one. :)
