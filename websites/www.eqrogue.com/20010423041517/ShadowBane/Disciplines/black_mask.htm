The Black Mask, Emissary of Death

Rogue Races Available:  Aelfborn, Aracoix, Elf, Human, Irekei, and Shade

Rogue Classes Available:  Assassin, Bard, Scout, and Thief

Post - Initiation: Rank 1

    There are six rules we who wear the mask live and die by, a set of traditions we call the Code, hammered out by the first master Black Masks of old. If you've ever hired one of our blades, then you already know some of The Code, but we don't tell our patrons everything. There are six tenets to The Code - learn them now.

The Rule of the Choosing
    A Black Mask may refuse any contract or the terms of any contract without loss of prestige, and need never give his reasons. 

The Rule of Commitment
    Once a contract has been accepted, a black mask may not accept another - the contract ends with the mask's death or with the target's. The killing of a man, especially a great man, is no light matter - a Black Mask's commitment to his profession must be absolute, or he (and by extension, all under the Mask) are nothing. 

The Rule of the Blade
A Black Mask kills only with a knife, and only works alone. If a man wants a murder mistook for an accident, let him hire a common thief or a poisoner. If a man wants the death of his enemy to send a message, he calls for a Black Mask.

The Rule of the Sheathe
    While pursuing a contract, Black Mask will kill only the contracted target and none other, save in the direst need. The mark will be killed one time, and one time only.  We are men, not beasts. To us death is the highest art. 

The Rule of One Shadow
    A Black Mask must render aid, safe harbor, and assistance to any identified Black Mask who asks, save in violation of the Rule of the Blade. The same shadow must hide us all. We may be many, but the Mask makes us one.

The Rule of the Mask
    A Black Mask will never reveal his identity, the identity of his patron, or that of his agents or any other Black Mask to anyone. Secrecy is the darkest shadow of all, and our very lives depend upon it.

    So you see, even we Black Masks have our honor, and our own type of glory. But know this - our glory is as silent as our tread. You've made a bit of a name for yourself, and you've got talent. But if you wear this mask, that ends. You'll never get to boast of the jobs you've done, or tell even your wife or lovers just how skilled and crafty you are.

If you do, I'll be coming in the night for you.

Rank 2

    There is far more to the Black Mask's art than an agile tread, a black suit, and a blade. Any man can kill another with a knife. Any rake, rogue, or warrior knows where to strike to kill a man quickly. Ours is the hidden blade - no castle, wall, or army can save our target from his fate. 

    To excel at our craft, to become truly great, one must study.  No knife is sharper than knowledge.  No mask more deceptive than Truth.  Travel to distant lands, and learn the ways of the people there, how they dress, and how they speak. Disguise and evasion will bring you closer to your targets than a quiet pair of boots, and even vigilant guards learn not to look at butlers and chambermaids. I spent eight moons finishing a contract, once - disguised all the while as a poor butler from Ethyra. Two months of cleaning stables won me duties inside the manor of a merchant prince, where I spent the next months winning the trust and respect of the chamberlain. My knowledge of poetry (as well as other… finer arts studied in my travels) won me the heart of the merchant's wife. Once I had access to his bedchamber, the merchant's doom was sealed. Had I not known the ways, fables, and speech of Ethyra, never could I have finished the job.

    This is not to say that your should neglect the rogue's arts - stealth, agility, and a keen blade are the foundations of our success. The Rule of the Blade demands that we kill with knives - use the straight sort like those made in Rovayle. A long, light stiletto that can pierce a man's heart or liver in the blink of an eye is best. And always, always use venom on your blade. While an expert hand can kill with but a single strike, only a fool leaves anything to chance. Be careful when buying poisons - the loose lips of an alchemist can betray your secret profession and lead vengeful enemies to your doorstep. If your mind is keen enough (and your purse deep enough) go study the distiller's craft and become an alchemist yourself. If you depend on anyone for anything, you open a window to betrayal. 

    Finally, to carry out your work you will need an agent, an intermediary. Every large city has one: someone who's looked death in the eye, and can win the ear of the Black Masks for anyone who would hire our knives. Agents are often old slayers past their prime, who wear the mask no more in favor of a more mundane profession. They'll bring you word of any prospective contracts so that you never need meet your would-be patrons in person. Some agents work in secret, hidden behind a web of rumors, secret messages, and night meetings. Others are open in their affiliations with us. After all, who would dare do harm to such a man and risk the ire of our knives?

Rank 3

    Poison is the Black Mask's greatest ally, but its use requires special diligence and discipline. The venom that coats your blade will make your every blow a lethal one, but beware - the poison cares not who it kills, and more than one novice has died from a tiny scratch of his own blade.

    As any alchemist will tell you, the world is brimming over with poisons of all sorts: berries, beasts, spiders, even the trees in the foggy swamplands of the far South beyond Irydni all hold toxins than can kill a man, render him lame, or cast him into slumber. By long standing tradition, Black Masks use three types of venom - all are among the most potent that can be delivered by a blade, and the easiest to obtain. Each is very different, however, and requires its own discipline and cautions. 

    To kill your target, use the mixture known as pellegorn. A mixture of adder's venom and hemlock mixed with crushed thiroo berries, this potent poison is rendered into a thick paste that sticks well to a blade and is easily applied. Beware, however, for pellegorn loses its potency within an hour if exposed to heat or the sun's light. Also, use it sparingly, for the paste has a distinctive odor. Even a small dose will kill, but too much spread on your blade will smell so strongly that your target may be warned.

    A favorite among Black Masks, the venom of the galpa spider sends its victim into a deep slumber. A single drop of galpa will suffice, and the sting can be delivered through a pin or needle. Judicious use of galpa will allow a Black Mask to disable guards or other foes while still obeying the Rule of the Sheathe. When you procure a vial of galpa venom, mix in two drops of your own spit: this will temper the poison, making it useless against you (and hence removing any danger of you pricking yourself by accident). Galpa will keep for a month if stored in a vial, but becomes inert quickly in air.

    The other poison most favored by men behind the Mask is buchinine, or gorgon's venom. This insidious oil seeps into the very flesh of the victim, stiffening their sinews and muscles. The victim remains awake and aware of everything around them, but is frozen in place, as if they had been turned to stone. The experience is terrifying, and never forgotten. If your patron wishes you to deliver a message to the target before the kill, immobilization with Gorgon's venom is the preferred method. It also works well on men and dogs. Note, however, that buchinine will only stay potent if it is kept warm. Keep it in a brazier over dim coals, and wear the vial under your armor, next to your skin. 

Rank 4

    To wear the Mask is to embrace a way of life, a double life that few of us were ever ready for. In all things, let the Code be your guide - every one of them is there for a reason, or for more than one. I myself was once contacted by a patron (through my agent, of course) and asked to kill a prominent man that lived in my city, a man who just happened to be me! I refused the job, of course, and gave no reason. My agent and I had a long laugh over the whole affair. Too bad the would-be patron didn't find it half as amusing.

    Always be sure to research your targets extensively before accepting a contract. There's a song the troubadours of Mellisar sing about the Black Mask who was hired to kill the corrupt sheriff of a faraway county in a time long before the Turning. The price was so high that the Mask eagerly took the contract, only to discover soon after that the sheriff was his long lost brother. The Mask agonized about the whims of Fortune and then, depending on which version of the song you prefer, either killed his own brother without hesitation or killed himself, holding true to the Rule of Commitment. Either ending is very tragic. The fact that the song's composer died soon after writing it for betraying working secrets of the Black Masks makes it even more popular.

    Etiquette and custom influence a Mask's work profoundly, and vary wildly from place to place. In the merchant cities of Tariponto a Black Mask is expected to fulfill a contract by the next full moon, and agents work openly, posting the names of targets on poles in the city plaza. 'Ponti Masks tend to be arrogant artistes - they think giving the target warning makes the job that much more challenging, demanding more of their skill. Conversely, in the caravan town of Berenger hired killing is strictly outlawed by the order of the High Templars, and according to them, there are no Black Masks in the city at all. Wise men, however, know better. Anyone needing our services drops a potsherd into the Well of Tears at the edge of town, with the target's name and an offer of payment scratched into it. If the Mask accepts, he'll send word to the applicant, and undertake the job. The Berengar Mask keeps his own schedule and sets his own terms, having to avoid the attentions of both the Templars and his targets.

    If you come to a new city, beware the different customs, and seek out the agent or agents there. Know them by the cut of their dress - they will favor black hose or leggings, and will always drink with their left hands. When you meet such a man, ask him for directions to the next town while glancing left and scratching your head. If he is an agent, he will answer by saying "Funny, how the road is never so straight as it seems." Once you have identified each other, inquire of him (in private, of course) how business is in town. Some Masks are very territorial, and don't tolerate free blades in their cities. 

Rank 5

    It is now time to initiate you into the deepest secrets of our order, for you to learn the true face behind the ebon mask. The Deepest Shadow is a council of master Black Masks, who stay in contact with each other and look out for the best interests of our order as a whole. They decide if any leaders are especially deserving of death, or whether a contract against any man might prove bad for the order in the long run. 

    But wait, you say - the Black Masks supposed to be aloof and neutral, catering only to the highest bidder and caring little for politics! True, but only up to a point. Only a fool has no interests. Would we stay in business long if a new High King were to draw the Crimson Blade, or do you think the Lords of the East would have any place for us once they'd conquered the Several Kingdoms? I think not. Too much peace breeds complacency, while too much war gives rivals another outlet for their enmity. Both are bad for business. Better to keep the petty kings and lords vying for power, but afraid to unsheathe the bloody sword of war. That way, they will always look to us as a hidden ally, an easy way to overcome their enemies. And the people do not suffer, at least so long as it serves our ends.

    For the Deepest Shadow knows, from bitter memory, that just as one man's death can prevent a war, one man's death can easily start one as well. And wars are always bad for business. Well, almost always…

    But the Deepest Shadow does more than steer the tide of History. Their first and most important task is to keep the Black Masks in order, and punish violations of the Code. Usually, if a Black Mask steps outside the laws of our order, he is warned. Once. Then, a Mask in a neighboring city will be contracted to find the offender and bring him to face Justice. Thus the Shadow culls the unfit from the lesser ranks, without even their knowledge.

    In the times before the Turning, death was a welcome punishment for those accused.  The ultimate punishment is Sanction, in which the offender's identity is made known to every member of our order, and the Rule of the Sheathe is removed.  The accused will be hunted by each and every Black Mask, forever.  Such a thing has not been done in thirty years, since The Spider violated the Rule of One Shadow by killing a family rival who asked him for safe harbor. The Spider was one of the greatest Masks who ever lived - the hunt was long, but retribution finally fell upon him. He remains in our safe keeping.  Take heed of this warning.  You have not heard his screams. , I have.

    A Black Mask doesn't kill because he enjoys it, or even if it might make his job easier. He kills his target and only his target because that's his job. We're no army (you might be surprised to learn how few of us actually wear the mask), and we can't walk through walls. Heartless? Hardly. Soulless? Maybe.

We are the Hands of Fate.
