Alchemist, the Blood Scientist

Races Available:  Aelfborn, Elf, Human, Irekei, and Shade

Classes Available:  Assassin, Bard, and Thief

    The unenlightened and uneducated presume that Alchemists are just another variety of Wizard, who spend long hours brewing strange potions and distilling chemicals. While we do work long and hard in our laboratories, we are not magicians: we are scholars, and we work not to cast magical spells or effects, but to better understand the basic nature of Life itself. The heathen tribes of the Horwathi Steppes have an ancient saying: "the Blood is the Life." Centuries of trial and experiment by master Alchemists have proven the humble proverb true. Alchemy is the study of Blood, and its power to transmute the basic elements of the universe into Life itself.

    For many years, Alchemists have known that the blood that flows in our veins is the medium through which our bodies are kept whole, soft, and limber. Talmiaches of Karaton, the greatest Alchemist who ever lived, was the first to prove that all true living things have some form of blood, be it the red blood of men or beasts, the sap of trees and plants, or the noxious ichor of the Chaos-tainted. Non-living objects like ponds, stones, and metals have no blood, and therefore do not live. Other master Alchemists have since proved that it is the action of the blood that allows living things to digest the elements they consume in the form of food, water, and breath, and thus stay alive.

    As there are four elements in the universe, so are there four components to all blood, and the proportion of their mixture will vary from individual to individual and species to species. If you would learn our ancient wisdom, learn well the ingredients, so that their names are as second nature to you.

    Maegus, the Spirit Water, is a clear fluid which carries life and youth to all parts of the body. Without water and the maegus to carry it, all living things quickly wither and die.

    Halgon, the Healing Unction, is red like the purest flame. It draws in the heat from the sun's fire and directs it throughout the body, melting away sicknesses and refashioning injuries, allowing them to heal.

    Timar, the Thought Wine, is the bright blue product of elemental air. Timar allows the will and thought of the Spirit and Mind to travel throughout the body, telling organs to function and limbs to move according to our volition. Timar is the noblest and most critical of all the blood components: a man without water will die in days, but a man without air will die in moments.

    Bile, or the Dark Sediment, is the fluid that results when the three ingredients above mix too freely with the earth of which our bodies are composed. All living things create bile as a consequence of their existence, but if their bodies are balanced they will naturally shed their bile and remain pure. Imbalanced beings will slowly accumulate bile in their blood, and must beware of it, for raw bile is pure and potent poison, which can kill anything if the dose is high enough.
