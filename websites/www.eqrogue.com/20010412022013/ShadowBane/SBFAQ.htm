Excepts from the Stratics SB FAQ and Elidrin's Comments

How many characters can I have per account?:

   Right now Wolfpack is aiming for around five, but will not commit to any number.

   Don't worry, though, you won't be limited to one. Damn!

Will character advancement be level based?:

    In a word, yes. Your character will have an attribute called level which will denote his or her general strength.

   However, this is not the single determining factor in your character's success since skill will be very, very important.

   I wonder how level and skill levels come into play, and just where disciplines fit into it.

How do I acquire experience points?:

    The only way to get experience points is to kill players or monsters.  YES YES YES Finally

   It is not definite how experience will be split between groups, but all that will be taken care of before the game is released.

Is there a level or skill cap in Shadowbane?:

    This really hasn't been discussed much. If there is a level cap, it will be fairly high (probably in the 100 range, but Wolfpack hasn't committed to a number). As for a skill cap, that is completely up in the air. They have mentioned the idea of a "soft cap", though, which means it becomes exponentially more and more difficult to reach a higher level and you gain less and less of a reward as you hit that level. In that light, people won't be striving to hit that next level anymore, thus the idea of a "soft cap". Basically this puts a limit on power leveling due to it becoming tedious even if you had a full group constantly working. In addition I understand at a certain point XPs only come from players further reducing the ability to power level.

How customizable will character appearance be?:

    Very--in fact, Wolfpack thinks they can make it deep enough so that no two characters look alike! Social Interaction is the key point of Shadowbane, and Wolfpack feels that being able to create your character with as much freedom as you want is important in expressing your personal character. Rest assured that things such as height, build, hair color, facial hair, skin tone and hue, and scars will be completely customizable. Well, maybe you can't be a seven foot, pink Dwarf, but you get the idea. I wonder how disguise (Assassin) will work with this?

Will I be able to wear cloaks and robes?:

   Well, that part is sketchy due to the 3D Engine. Robes and Cloaks are very difficult to create properly due to the way they naturally flow (much like hair). In addition, like hair, it is one of the things that has been baffling programmers and artists alike for a very long time now. Rest assured that Wolfpack will do its best to make the possibility a reality, but they will only implement them if they can do it right--they don't want to throw it in "just because". Anyone here want to wear a robe?

Are the attributes (strength, dexterity, etc) that you choose static or do they improve with use?:

   There has been no information released about this. We do know that the attributes will be Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, and Spirit. There isn't even any info on how the attributes affect your skills

Will my character need to eat?:

    If eating is in Shadowbane, it will only enhance gameplay, not become a tedious "necessity". It has not been confirmed if it will be in Shadowbane, though. So much for poisoning food and drink =(

Will male and female characters have different attributes?:

    Yes, they will both start off slightly differently with men concentrating more on strength while women will be high in spirit and agility/dexterity. However, there will be no cap limiting the genders--women will just have to work harder at gaining strength (due to lower testosterone levels) and men will have to work harder at gaining speed. *Contains himself*

Will all characters start in GM towns?:

    Yes. Since guild cities could fall and change power, all characters will start in GM-Towns which will never grow or decline in size.

Interactive Map FAQ

Basic description: 

    A set of 3 maps showing how the political structure may change over 3 months. 

    Each city is represented by shields

So what am I looking at, here?

This is example of the Shadowbane “World Map.”  This map can be used to track the changes in the political landscape of Shadowbane over a period of time – in this case, how the beta world might look after an elapsed time of three months. I really need to find a way to copy the maps.

What are all of these shields for?

Each of the Shields represents a City -- a collection of houses, castles, and other fortifications that are created, populated and governed entirely by players just like you.

Who decides how the Shields look?

Players do!  When a group of players comes together to form a GUILD, they use an interface to create their own heraldic Coat of Arms.  This crest is then used on the World Map to show all Provinces that they control (either through occupation or pledges of fealty.) The actual crest will be made by me when I start the guild in game, the question is what to make it look like, we know we got the rose, the question is 1 color or 2 color background and what pattern and color. I'm partial to a black rose on a solid red or gray background.

What is a Guild and how do I create one?

A Guild is a collection of players that choose to play together for any number of reasons : political affiliation, mutual protection, or just simple camaraderie. They are created in Shadowbane through the use of a GUILD CHARTER.

There are 10 different types of Guild Charters, each representing a different type of organization.  The 3 types of guild charters which apply to us are listed at the end of this update. Mercenary Company, Thieves Band, and the Barbarian Clan.

Can you explain how the World Map works?

Sure.  The World Map has two modes – the World view and the Regional view.  The World View only shows the largest Cities in each World (i.e. the Cities with the largest population of players.)  The Region view, however, will show all of the Cities located in each Region, regardless of size.

How do I get from the World level to the Region level?

Simple.  As you pass your mouse over each Regions, it lights up in red.  Just click on it and you’ll be presented with a detailed view of that Region.

How do I get back from the Region level up to the World level?

Clicking on the “Back” button will take you back up to the World View.

Can I get more detailed information on the City-States?

Yes.  In the Region View, clicking on any Guild Crest will give you detailed information about that City, such as: creation date, player population, guild control, and kingdom allegiance.

Is this the Beta world?

Yes, it is -- but the player cities being shown aren’t real, as beta hasn’t started yet.  This World, “Aerynth”, is the first of the Worlds we’ll be presenting as we move through our beta process.

So can a map like this one can be viewed in the game?

Yes. I love this.

As players create and destroy cities, will this Map be automatically updated?

Yes!  That’s why we placed all of the guild crests on the map – to show how the political landscape of the World will change based on player actions.  This is also why we put up “earlier” screen shots of the map, as well – to show the game changing over time.

So will you be updating this Map to show the Kingdoms that players create in beta?

Exactly.  Once the beta gets rolling, we’ll update it with actual data taken from the game. I can't help but wonder how often.

Is it just me, or do some of these guild names look familiar?

They should!   While most of the guilds shown on the Region view were made up by individuals here in the Wolfpack office, some of them were filled out by pre-existing Guilds (from other games) that volunteered to be in our beta process.  In the latter case, we had these Guilds provide us information on their name, crest, population and motto.  We thought this would give the Map a more realistic look and feel, and better illustrate what we’re building.

I noticed that different colors are used to outline different crests on the City information screen.  Why is this?

There are actually four different guild crest outlines : cobalt blue, copper, silver, and gold.

Any crest with a blue outline represents a SAFEHOLD (also known as a ‘GM-run city’).  This is a protected town that new players can start in, fashioned by the world designers and static (meaning they always exist in the world.)  Players cannot engage in combat while in a Safehold and surrounding areas, and these towns cannot participate in Guild Wars.  Safeholds tend to be located near low level adventuring areas.

What do the other metallic outlines signify?

The other colors relate to player-run towns.  The metallic color of copper, silver, or gold depicts the political significance of the City.

Copper crest outlines represent independent CITY-STATES.  These player-run Cities owe allegiance to no one.

Silver crest outlines are used to depict PROVINCES.  A province is a City-State that has pledged fealty to another, and now existing as part of a larger Kingdom.

Gold outlined crests are used to represent the capital city of a KINGDOM.  (Any City-State with one or more sworn Province is considered to be a Kingdom.  These Provinces, however, do not have to be adjacent on the Map or even exist in the same Region.)

So how do I create a City?

There is an object in the game known as a “Tree of Life” – an enormous tree of white stone covered with archaic runes.  To start a new City, players must find one of these objects and “plant” it in the ground at a suitable location.  (With the exception of adventuring areas and the boundaries of other Cities, almost all of the World is considered viable for city building.)

This Tree acts, quite literally, as the heart of your newly-formed City.  It serves as a respawn points for your guild members on death, it lends both protection and fortification to the buildings in your town, and stands as a physical representation of your burgeoning new Kingdom.  As long as your Tree remains healthy and whole, your City will thrive.

How do I protect my Tree, and my City?

There are a large number of defensive measures that can be acquired in-game to help you defend your town.  Guards can patrol your streets, archers can man your ramparts.  Castle walls can be used to fortify your perimeter, and druids enticed to nurture and protect your Tree.  Guild leaders also control a “Kill on Sight” list, informing the guards which players and guilds should be considered enemies of the state.

The best defense of all, though, will be your relationship with other players.  Banner men and political allegiance will weigh heavily in the balance of feudal power. We must be careful to not make those KOS lists and if we do our information about those cities will have to be COMPLETE in order to get our assassins in and out.

Can I lay siege to another guild’s town?

Yes, you can.  As a word of warning, however – as you might expect, the burden of work is definitely on the aggressor.  It’s much easier to defend a castle than it is to attack one.  Sieges will often become long and arduous affairs, requiring potentially days or weeks of real time game play before any real headway is made.

While full scale siege will certainly happen activities like border raids, assassination attempts, political treaties and reconnaissance missions will likely be much more common. This is where we come in.

Why so much emphasis on player interaction?

Other games, to date, have relied too much on the ‘single-player’ design mentality.  We didn’t just want to build a game; we wanted to build a living simulation.  Shadowbane is a cooperative and competitive model of a feudal society.  We believe that this will create the ultimate roleplaying experience – that the opportunities for human interaction brought about by such a dynamic environment will far surpasses what one might come to expect from a more static, repetitive model.  In short, we wanted to give people more than “just a game.”

We wanted to give you the opportunity to Change the World. *Grins*

How does guild leadership work?

There are four levels of authority within every guild :

The GUILD LEADER is in charge of the guild.  He (or she) is responsible for all areas of guild management, including such things as : initiating and banishing members, promoting and demoting player ranks, and controlling the City Tree itself. Me

Additional, secondary guild leaders can be named to the guild’s INNER COUNCIL.  Inner Council members have access to the same commands as the guild leader. People I trust implicitly, in both actions and wisdom.

Most players within the guild will be ACTIVE members.  This gives them the right to use the guild channel and facilities, invite new players into the guild as petitioners, and vote on all guild matters.  They also remain under the protection of the city guards while in town. Everyone else.

Lastly, when a new player is invited into the guild, that player is given PETITIONER status.  Petitioners are given limited access to guild facilities channels. The new people

Players who are not currently in a guild are considered ERRANT.

So how does a petitioner become an active member of my guild?

Well, that depends on how you want to manage your guild.  Guild Leaders and Inner Council members have the ability to initiate petitioners at any time (or to banish active members, for that matter.) We need to have a long discussion on this.

Does that mean that, as guild leader, I have to initiate all of my players by hand?

Only if you choose to.  For guilds that want a more automated approach, there is also a WHITEBALL/BLACKBALL system that can allow active members to vote on the admission of all petitioners. Personally not sure how this works, sounds like a private vote.

What if I don’t like my guild?  Can I quit?

Of course.  Members of a guild may quit at any time. Anyone ready to wimp out yet?

How can I effectively manage my guild if everyone is simply an “active”?  That seems like a very flat organization.

There is also a title/ranking system within the Guild, which allows your guild leader to assign different ranks to different individuals.  There is no pre-requisite for any given rank; these titles are used for roleplaying and managerial purposes only.

These title also vary based on the guild type.  For example, within a Noble House, players can be given the titles of Vassal, Scion, Champion, Baron, Count, Duke and King.  Within a Mercenary Company, the titles would be Soldier, Man-at-Arms, Veteran, Captain, Commander, and Warlord. None of these sound right for us...

How does the Fealty system work?

The same way the guild system does!  A guild that is landed (meaning that the guild directly controls a Tree of Life) may take pledges of fealty from other guilds.  These guilds, like players, begin as PETITIONERS and can be later initiated as a fully active,  SWORN guild.  SWORN guilds are considered extensions of the guild itself, and as such have access to make use of the City and its facilities.

In addition, members of Sworn and Sovereign (landed) guilds receive a bonus to their recovery rate, subject to the health of the Tree to which they are bonded. This may pose a problem for us.

What if my guild wants to change Nations?  Can we quit?

Of course.  Guild leaders may opt to change allegiance – or declare independence – at any time. Heh heh

So what are my chances of taking over the World?

To be honest, no one knows.  It’s certainly possible.  Can one guild do it?  Possibly.  A group of guilds working together?  Maybe.  What’s the recipe for world domination?  Coercion?  Political power? Economic might?

You can certainly change the world, but taking over is another matter entirely.  One thing IS quite certain, however.

Joining a Thieves' Band allows rogues to hone their skills to near perfection, and in some corrupt cities these organizations have grown so powerful and influential that they operate in the open, with an air of legality.
