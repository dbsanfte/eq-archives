Elidrin's Shadowbane Reference

Let me begin by stating this is by no means complete.. I'm still working..

A 

  • Aerynth - A great city with a vast library lost during the turning.

  • Age - The world's history is broken up into 5 periods: The Age Before, The Age of Twilight, The Age of Days, The Age of Kings, and The Age of Strife.

  • Age Before - The first of the five Ages. The time in which the All-Father created the world, only myths exist of this time.

  • Age of Days - The third of the five Ages. The era is marked by the counting of time, this is assumed to have been begun by the humans due to their short life spans. It is also rumored that before this time their was no sun by which to measure days. It is also the point in time in which the deathless empire discovered the runestones. It is also marked by the discovery of the magical art of Sorcery. It is believed that the War of the Scourge happened during or at the end of this age.

  • Age of Kings - The Fourth age and an age of war, between the humans and elves as well as humans against each other. The entire age is one of war amongst the Kings of the Humans while being defeated by the Elves do to lack of unity. Until the rise of Cambruin. It is late in this age that the amazons revealed themselves to be more than a rumor by attacking raiding and pillaging the southern lands of the One Kingdom, the Amazons call it the Great Hunt. The Age of Kings is marked by the death of Cambruin and The Turning.

  • Age of Strife - The fifth age and the age we live in now.

  • Age of Twilight - The second of the five Ages. The age in which the elves reached their pinnacle of civilization and magical arts, specifically wizardry, channeling and summoning. During this time the elves unlocked the secret that all things have a true name. This era is consider by many to close with the awaking of the dragon and the creation of the human race by the All-Father.

  • Air -  Material Element of Life. Air is essence of the intangible the path of knowledge, thought, and inspiration. It is also associated with and sight, sound, flight, and motion. Air is the direct opposite of Earth.

  • Almeus the Young - Tutor of Zaristan and one of the greatest Wizards Humankind has ever known, discovered the principles of Sorcery after he seized the throne of Brethild. From arcane grimoires of the Deathless Empire the Wizard learned of the Elvish efforts to create Runestones, and took up the cause himself. Even Almeus could not succeed in fashioning new Runestones, but his attempts prompted other discoveries which revolutionized magic throughout the World. While trying to find the ideal medium from which to fashion Runestones, Almeus determined that certain objects and materials hold an inherent "resonance potential," a sensitivity to specific magical auras and energies. In time, he found that objects placed at the intersections of Ley Lines slowly accumulate magical force, called Mana by initiates. Almeus devised ways to tap into the stored Mana within these objects, as well as other ways to trap mana within the substance of particular grades of stone or metal. Thus were born the Rings, Talismans, and Powerstones so prized by Wizards and Channelers alike. Two centuries later Almeus devised the first Sigils.  

  • All-Father - The "One True God" of the Shadowbane realm.

  • Amazon - Also known as the Daughters of Phaedra. This is an all female human culture whose jungle ancestral home Delgana and the surrounding Emerald Canopy was lost during the Turning. Known for their huntresses and Furies it is believed that the amazons where once members of the Northmen tribes (Barbarians), led away long ago by Phaedra seeking peace away from the war mongering males. In order to proliferate and continue their line and culture the Amazons have been known to kidnap female children during raids. The greatest raid of the Amazons however was not for children but rather to save Braialla from the destructive hands of men, this was known as The Great Hunt which ended on the Day of Woe.

  • Animation - The magical art of giving "life" to an inanimate object. According to the Book of Staves the All-Father created the Human race using this art from the elements of Air, Earth, Fire, Spirit and Water. 

  • Archon - The first three beings created by the All-Father from the mists of the Void.

  • Artifact Lore - Bard ability, The ability to examine an item and know its history and reputed powers.

  • Assassin - Not content with stealing gold or plunder, these grim professionals steal lives instead, killing for their wages, trading blood for gold. Some Assassins are ruthless predators, who've developed a taste for blood and thrill at the act of killing, while others are grim professionals, bringing a kind of honor even to the direst of professions. There are some Assassins who are less direct, however, and began their dark careers not as Rogues but as Wizards, and use magic instead of a dagger. These dark magicians have practiced the art of murder so long that every Assassin learns spells of stealth and shadow, evasion and death. In any battle, the mightiest weapon is one that strikes unseen. If history has taught anything to the peoples of the World, its grim lesson is that anyone can be killed, if the killer tries hard enough. In these days since the Turning, death has lost its permanence but not its sting: though the Champion of a guild may only stay dead a few hours after he tastes an Assassin's blade, his absence may well turn the tide of a Guildwar. Truly, the Age of Strife has kept Assassins busier than ever, and as long as there is profit in it, these harbingers of death will lurk in the shadows, waiting to strike.

  • Aurochmengr - Barbarian word for Minotaur.

B

  • Barbarian - Humans of the cold wastes of the north. Also called Northmen. Considered by most, uncivilized.

  • Bard - Every Bard is a master of tale-telling and music. Around hearth and table Bards work their wonders, spinning tapestries of poetry and music, and keeping the memories of ancient days alive. Their ancient craft was born among the skalds of the Northmen (Barbarians), but now Bards can be found among all the peoples of the World.  But there is far more to a Bard's life than singing and playing. Bards must record the events of the present as dutifully as they remember the past, and therefore every Bard is a vagabond, a chronicler in search of a new epic. Bards are welcomed everywhere, for a good song and a moment free from care have become precious things in this troubled Age. Bards also carry news and tidings from distant places, which the folk of remote towns crave more than ale. Through the work of Bards deeds seen become sagas told throughout the World. So the works of the strong and the honorable are known and revered, and glory is heaped on the names of the brave. A Bard's knowledge of the sagas has other benefits as well. Bards know how every hero triumphed or failed, and remember every trick and secret employed by giants, sorcerers, witches, and heroes. There is a power in words, names, and songs, and Bards know much that is hidden.

  • Battle of Krollenford - Bard song telling of when Duke Leonard's men held the ford against a horde ten times their number. 

  • Beast Lords - Also called Beast Spirits. Revered by Rangers and Druids. Names and Types currently unknown. However there may be a connection with the Great Lioness of the Barbarian religious histories.

  • Bellowstone, Therron - The dwarf who according to dwarvish legend observed the creation of the human race and carried the knowledge back to his people, thus creating the art of Animation.

  • Black Mask - Discipline of Assassin, Bard, Fury, Huntress, Scout, Thief, and Warlock. The black masks are a group of hired killers who follow a group of set rules unswervingly: The Rule of the Choosing, The Rule of Commitment, The Rule of the Blade, The Rule of the Sheathe, The Rule of One Shadow, The Rule of the Mask

  • Blessing of the Healing Hands - Crusader ability, believed to be a heal touch.

  • Book of Staves - Religious Book of Creation for the Church of the All-Father, assumed Human (although not barbarian), perhaps Dwarvish or Centaurian, definitely not Elvish in origin.

  • Braialla (The Green Mother) - Mother of all life. The world. Patron goddess of the Amazons, Druids and Rangers.

  • Brethild - Believed to be the first human city.

C

  • Call Lightning - Druid ability, believed to allow the druid to call a lightning strike down from the heavens.

  • Call Storm - Fury ability. Rumors speak of Furies linked together can use this ability enhance it's effect.

  • Callynthir - Tae ap Arpalonamil Refence. Enchanted spear which wounded the Wand'rer. Believed to be a weapon of great magic.

  • Cambruin - The great human leader from the Kingdom of Caledorn who united the human race against the elves late in the Age of Kings. Believed by many to have been an Archon or the All-Father incarnate. Cambruin created the Knights under the belief in the five virtues of the All-Father. He is also credited with forming the One Kingdom. Cambruin fell at the battle of Kierhaven to a traitors blade, Shadowbane. His death is marked by The Turning.

  • Centaurs - Half Man / Half horse, the race predates the human Race.

  • Channeler - A type of spell caster who can draw and focus power directly from the Tapestry of Creation. Through the use of Mage Sight these a casters can actually see Ley Lines and speed, slow, or redirect their energies. Channelers devote themselves to one of the four elements, unlocking the utmost secrets of matter and its composition. The Art of Channeling is still practiced to this day, and its masters are able to call fire, walk upon water, cause earthquakes, or ride the winds like eagles. 

  • Charm - Bard ability, The ability to replace a targets free will with your own.

  • Chaos - Universal Material of constant change and instability. Associated with demons.

  • Church of the All-Father - Human church dedicated to the worship of the All-Father. Also called the Holy Church.

  • Church of the Cleansing Flame - The days since the Turning have seen the rise of a new religion, a faith that has made its presence felt in every corner of the sundered World. Nearly a century ago, a group of learned theologians left the Holy Church of the All-Father, claiming that it had lost its way and was incapable of redeeming the World in this Age of Strife. They founded the Temple of the Cleansing Flame, devoted to the redemption of all peoples through the eradication of evil, sin, and heresy. In the decades that followed, the followers of the Flame have carried on their battle for righteousness with all the ferocity and intensity of the fire that they venerate. The church is made up of Confessors and Templars. They believe that fire must be used to burn away all that is impure. It's Confessors and Templars traveling the world on their crusade. The church demands its followers to follow the Twelve Articles of Faith. 

  • Colossus - The greatest of all "animations" a giant murgolem carved from the face of a mountain. This creation took Therron Bellowstone over 200 pears to complete. It is believed that the golem still stands guard over the lost dwarven city of Grimbord.

  • Confessor - Confessors are the priests of the Cleansing Flame, charged with winnowing the righteous from the wicked, and exposing evil and sin wherever they may lie. Motivated by the Twelve Articles of Faith, they have continued the High King's crusade against the Elves, and have added many other races to the ranks of the sinful. Namely: Orcs, Irekei, and "Heretics" They pray to the All-Father daily, and their powers arise from lengthy invocations of the Archons and the Blessed Saints such as Saint Malorn the Just. Confessors put the spiritual well-being of their flocks far above their physical well-being, testing and rewarding the faithful rather than feeding or clothing the poor. Confessors can usually be identified by their red robes.

  • Crafter - A dwarven word for one who uses the Animator Discipline.

  • Crusader - When the hordes of Chaos shook the World at the beginning of the War of the Scourge, Saint Adelmo the Patriarch called a mighty crusade against the unnatural invaders, and placed the All-Father's blessing upon any who would take up arms in the Church's defense. From that day to this, they have worn white for purity and blue for the All-Father's strength, as vast as the endless sky. Also known as the Soldiers of Grace. Crusaders serve as guardians and defenders of cathedrals, or soldiers in the Patriarch's grand army, defending the body of the Holy Church from any who would oppose it. In the days since the Turning, the Soldiers of Grace have a new duty as well. The most stalwart Crusaders are elected to venture out into the world, seeking news of the All-Father's fate, and helping the innocent wait out the dark times until He may return.

  • Cuthric Rune Thief - Hero of a Bardic saga, the Northman who tricked the Giants into revealing the secret wisdom of the All-Father. I find this tale odd in that it was the Elves who are credited with the discovery of the runestones.

D

  • Darkness - Also called The Dark, Universal Material of death and destruction. Associated with the Unholy Legion.

  • Day of Woe - Amazon term for The Turning.

  • Deathless Empire - Common reference to the elvish empire during the age of twilight and age of days.

  • Delgana - The lost valley of the Amazons within the Emerald Canopy. Home to the Amazon culture since the day Phaedra led them there, lost on the Day of Woe.

  • Disguise - Assassin ability, Simply the ability to look like someone else.

  • Disjunction - Prelate ability, unknown effect.

  • Dragon, The - A legendary beast that almost destroyed all life on the world.

  • Druid - The most ancient and mysterious of priests are the Druids, an ancient sect of priests who worship the primal Beast Spirits and Braialla. The Druids hold their rites at ancient stone circles lost in the deeps of the forest, far from the prying eyes of Man. Their rites and rituals are the echoes of ancient ceremonies intoned by the Elves under the First Twilight ages ago. Through the power of their faith, Druids attune themselves with the world around them, utilizing the hidden connection between all living things and the World itself. Plants, beasts, and the weather all respond to a Druid's magic. No beast of field or wild will ever seek of its own will to harm a Druid, and many serve them as their eyes, ears, and protectors. During the Age of Kings, the Druids and Rangers were ruthlessly persecuted by the Church of the All-Father and Cambruin, who saw the Druidic religion as the source of the Great Betrayal of the Elves. Driven into hiding, the Druids have worked in the shadows ever since, healing lands blighted by war or the touch of Chaos, destroying creatures that disrupt the natural order, and trying to minimize or offset the damage done by civilized peoples to the world. Above all, the Druids work to restore the Sacred Balance. 

E

  • Earth - Material Element of Life. Earth brings strength, fortitude, stability, and permanence. It is used to mend, heal, harden; make an object brittle, wither it, or even transform its basic composition, turning wood into iron, or iron into sand. Protection, health, and wealth are also associated with Earth. It is the direct opposite of Air.

  • Elements of Life - There are five elements from which all living things are made. The elements of life are all of the material elements plus the fifth element, spirit. 

  • Elves - One of the first sentient creations of the All-Father. This race predates the human race.

  • Emerald Canopy - Amazon term for the jungles of their lost homelands which hid Delgana from the outside world.

  • Excommunication - Confessor ability.

F

  • Fire - Material Element of Life and Animation. Fire has the power to create, inspire, or destroy. Destruction and transformation lie at the heart of this element. Do to the warlike nature of the age of strife many channelers concentrate upon this element, some believe too many. It is opposed to water.

  • Fists of Heaven - Prelate ability, unknown effect

  • Focus - Warlock ability to channel their mental abilities to affect the thoughts of others. From observation and communication to mental assaults.

  • Fury - Both loved and feared by the Amazons they protect, Furies live almost feral lives at the edges of Amazon settlements, living like hermits in caves or secluded huts. The power to channel and shape the Fury is born of pain and sacrifice, so the furies deprive themselves of all luxury and refinement. Fasting, meditation, and even self-mutilation are the keys to their power. Furies adorn themselves with dozens of fetishes, handmade talismans, and striking body paint. The Furies learned long ago how to work together to produce greater magical effects. Three Furies casting together are perilous indeed, and the High Circle of Furies can call up a storm so terrible that it can lay waste to an entire region. Though they look and act like primitive Healers or shamans, Furies do not worship the forces of weather and lightning - they command them, with as much detachment and scholastic discipline as any Wizard or Priest. Previous to Lyestra Furies where known as weather witches. Legend has it that the ancient Furies could also call forth power from earth and fire, but that these ancient secrets were lost with their homelands. Whispered tales speak of the earth furies still live but are quite insane, chained within deep caves unable to die. From the very foundation of the Amazon Empire, the Furies have served as advisors to the Amazon Queens. Their cryptic words carry much weight within Amazon society, and over the centuries the Sisterhood of Furies became the power behind the Amazon throne, for no queen had the will or courage to dispute them. The few Amazons who were left behind at the borders of their former Empire turned to the Furies for guidance, and now the weather witches are the overt rulers of the wandering Amazons who remain in the World, searching for their lost sisters.

G

  • Gallorian Yndrialle - The first Channeler, elvish. Who noted that all material elements are constant within the Tapestry of Creation. In order for there to be rain in an area water must leave an another area. He referred to this as "...the turning of a vast wheel."

  • Gend - Town/City/Province of unknown race (Assumed Human), legends speak of the warrior archers of this land being able to fire twenty arrows with deadly precision in the space of a minute. 

  • Giants - Basically a enormous Man, this race predates the human race.

  • Gift, The - Warlock term for their mental powers. Their power is unique in that they do no draw power from a natural loophole nor from the tapestry of creation. By focusing their gift they are able to cause spell like effects.

  • Golem - A type of animation, the word is believed to be associated with earth, but may be any element.

  • Great Hunt - Amazon term for their assault on the One Kingdom late in the Age of Kings.

  • Great Tapestry - Book of Staves Reference, also known as the Tapestry of Creation. 

  • Grimbord - A lost dwarven city, believed to be guarded by the Colossus.

  • High Circle of Furies - A organization of Amazon spell casters.

  • Karl - Barbarian word for warrior.

  • Kenaryn - Tae ap Arpalonamil Refence. Companion to the Wand'rer, assumed to be the Archon Kenaryn the Hunter. 

  • Kenaryn the Hunter - Book of Staves Reference. The Second of the Archons made by the All-Father, assumed to be associated with the providing of food. Possibly a Centaur.

  • Kierhaven - The only elvish city to survive Cambruin's campaign against the elves during the Age of Kings. The site of Cambruin's death.

  • Kolaur,Prince - Tae ap Arpalonamil Refence. Demon defeated by the company of the Wand'rer. Actually slain by Thurin. Original owner or guardian of the Orb of Ebon and Callynthir.

L

  • Last Hunt - Amazon term for the Age of Strife, in which they intend to kill all men who would harm or own the world, Braialla rather than "enjoy" it.

  • Levitation - Warlock ability - Believed to allow the warlock a limited form of flight.

  • Ley Lines - A mystical thread of energy, also known as Threads of Creation, first discovered by the elves during the Age of Twilight. These threads are believed to connect all things living and spiritual, making the Tapestry of Creation. The five elemental forces, Earth, Air, Fire, Water, and Spirit, continually bind and twist to form every object and creature in our World, and the Ley Lines serve as the conduits for their motions. Mages are able to tap and focus this energy called mana from the intersections of theses threads.

  • Lioness, The Great - According to many barbarians it was she who gave man fire to wage war against the Kings of the Animals: Snake, Boar, Wolf, and Bear. 

  • Lyestra - It is said that a millennium ago an Amazon sculptor named Lyestra discovered the energy locked within everything, energy that can be used for creation and transformation, but which can only be released through the destruction of the vessel that holds it. The Amazon witches named this force the Fury, and adopted its name as their own. This may be the same mystical science used by Almeus. 

M

  • Mage Sight - Channeler term - The ability to see thru the veil of illusion and gaze upon the tapestry of creation.

  • Malog the Warrior - Book of Staves Reference. The Third of the Archons made by the All-Father, assumed to be associated offense, aggression and war. Possibly Elvish. 

  • Mass Entangle - Druid Ability, believed to allow the druid to call upon plants to slow and hold their enemies.

  • Manisfestation - Channeler term for the physical effect of energy passing thru Ley Lines forming a perceivable effect within the Veil of Illusion. 

  • Material Elements - Common reference by casters which includes Air, Earth, Fire, and Water. Used by Channelers and Animators in their professions.

  • Mellissar - Town/City/Province of unknown race (Assumed Human), renown for producing great swordsman/fencers. It is said they train daily for 8 hours honing their skills. 

  • Mental Projection - Warlock ability. Believed to allow the warlock to communicate with others without being heard, may also allow minor illusions (Unconfirmed speculation)

  • Mind Trap - Warlock ability. Believed to allow the warlock to put the target into a form of paralysis, his mind separated from his body. (Unconfirmed speculation)

  • Minotaur - Race best described as a large man with the head of a bull. The debate of whether this is a race twisted by chaos (See Twisted Breeds) or a race from another realm is currently unknown.

  • Murgolem - Possible dwarvish word for Earth Golem.

O

  • One Kingdom, The - The unified human kingdom formed by Cambruin during the Age of Kings. Spread from the jungles in the South to the Ice in the North, with legions of Knights as the High King's defenders and enforcers.

  • Orb of Ebon - Tae ap Arpalonamil Refence. A dark orb containing 3 smaller orbs of stone and ice. This is believed to be our universe.  According to the Tae ap Arpalonamil the Wand'rer claimed the best of the orbs as his own to rule.

  • Ordeal of the Irons - Church of the Cleansing Flame term for a form of torture used by confessors upon suspected heretics. Involves the cutting and burning of flesh.

P

  • Paolus - First King of Brethild. Usurped by Almeus the Young.

  • Passwall - Assassin ability, The ability to move through a wall or wall-like barrier.

  • Patriarch - Head of the Church of the All-Father. The word implies that women are not able to hold this position.

  • Petty Kingdoms - Reference to the unaligned human kingdoms before the rise of the One Kingdom under Cambruin during the Age of Kings.

  • Phaedra - The First Huntress, who led the Amazons away from men and their strife to the Emerald Canopy and Delgana.

  • Poison Blade - Assassin ability, The ability to properly place a venom or toxin upon a bladed weapon so that it renders its effects upon the victim to maximum effect.

  • Prelate - Through all the long history of the World the Prelates of the Church of the All-Father have sung His praises and served as the keepers of the Holy Word. Their Faith has brought the Church through four Ages of war and cataclysm, and they have stood fast even in the wake of the Turning, and now offer the last hope for peace in a darkening World. The Prelates still hold true to the Book of Staves, the All-Father's great book, and believe that all of the His children must stand together against the forces of Chaos and Darkness, or all will surely be lost. Aided by the stalwart Crusaders, the clergy of the Holy Church strives to bring unity and healing to a World rent by strife. Many Prelates serve as functionaries within the Church hierarchy, running cathedrals and monasteries, preaching to their flocks, and helping the poor and sick. As the Cardinals debate endlessly over the fate of the All-Father and what role the Church should play in the Age of Strife, some Prelates have decided to that the time for action has come. These missionary Prelates wander the World, spreading the All-Father's gospel while combating the forces of evil. Many lend their spiritual aid to virtuous heroes, hoping that their efforts can bring Order out of Chaos, and restore the Harmony lost so long ago. 

  • Priests - Many simple healers go on to devote themselves to one of the Great Faiths, pledging their life and efforts to advance the causes of their chosen path, and are rewarded with even greater powers and miracles. The Holy Church of the All-Father draws the greatest number of healers into its ranks, with its legions of divine saints and hierarchies of angelic lords offering a vast array of powers and miracles to Prelates who believe. The ancient and secretive Druids devote themselves to the Balance and the Mother of the World. Rumors abound of other healers who follow the teachings of the Elemental Hierarchs, the Beast Lords, or even the wicked doctrines of the Lords of Chaos. Faith is the source of a healer's power, and the final measure of his capabilities. In the darkness that followed the Sundering, a new Faith was born: the Temple of the Cleansing Flame, devoted to avenging the High King's death and revering the High King and Saint Malorn, the Temple's founder. Whereas most healers devote themselves to healing the hurts of the world and trying to end strife, the fanatics of the Temple believe that strife will only end when all who are evil, impure, and unfit have been destroyed. Their crusade in the All-Father's name threatens to ignite the weary World like a firebrand.

Q

  • Queen Candrae - Character in the Bardic tale of Sir Hurrigan.

R

  • Ranger - Warriors of the Sacred Balance, fighting against the forces of the Unholy Legion, The Twisted Breeds, and any other who would bring harm to Braialla. Created by the elves in the age of twilight, they later recruited worthy humans. Now the organization primarily consists of humans. Early in the age of kings rangers, knights and crusaders fought side by side until Cambruin waged war against the elves. The rangers refused and Cambruin denounced them and their religion forcing the rangers and druids into hiding within the One Kingdom. The rangers have only now since The Turning returned to fight openly. The symbol of the ranger is the Scorpion. They also have a deep running distrust for Irekei, probably dating back to when the Irekei broke away from the elvish community.

  • Release Fury - Fury ability, believed to be a way for Furies to release magical energies stored within talismans and fetishes.

  • Resurrect - Prelate ability, assumed to return a person to their dead body.

  • Rule of the Choosing: A Black Mask may refuse any contract or the terms of any contract without loss of prestige, and need never give his reasons. 

  • Rule of Commitment: Once a contract has been accepted, a black mask may not accept another - the contract ends with the mask's death or with the target's. The killing of a man, especially a great man, is no light matter - a Black Mask's commitment to his profession must be absolute, or he (and by extension, all under the Mask) are nothing. 

  • Rule of the Blade: A Black Mask kills only with a knife, and only works alone. If a man wants a murder mistook for an accident, let him hire a common thief or a poisoner. If a man wants the death of his enemy to send a message, he calls for a Black Mask.

  • Rule of the Sheathe: While pursuing a contract, Black Mask will kill only the contracted target and none other, save in the direst need. The mark will be killed one time, and one time only.  We are men, not beasts. To us death is the highest art. 

  • Rule of One Shadow: A Black Mask must render aid, safe harbor, and assistance to any identified Black Mask who asks, save in violation of the Rule of the Blade. The same shadow must hide us all. We may be many, but the Mask makes us one.

  • Rule of the Mask: A Black Mask will never reveal his identity, the identity of his patron, or that of his agents or any other Black Mask to anyone. Secrecy is the darkest shadow of all, and our very lives depend upon it.

  • Runestones - The fragments of the saga that the All-Father wrote in stone foretelling the destiny of the world. The elvish masters learned to tap into their power. Some Magi, not content with harnessing the Stones' power, tried to fashion new Runestones of their own. Their efforts all failed, and most ended in disaster. Until Almeus the Young. 

S

  • Sacred Balance - Druid and Ranger term - Also called the Great Balance. The cycle of Life, Death, and Transformation, the basis of the  at the heart of nature. 

  • Saint Adelmo the Patriarch - Saint of the Church of the All-Father. The priest credited with the creation of the Crusaders during the War of the Scourge.

  • Saint Anselm - Saint of the Church of the All Father. Famous for writing "One branch from a tree may be broken, but a dozen bundled tight will resist the strength of a Giant. So must it be with all the Children of the All-Father: with the hideous forces of Darkness and Chaos arrayed against us, how can the His children endure if they fight ever amongst themselves? We of the Holy Church have always sought to unite the children of the World, and our efforts are needed now more than ever."

  • Saint Eliander - A saint of the Church of the All-Father who endured the lashes of his tormentors and came through unmarked.

  • Saint Hugert - Unknown saint of the Church of the All-Father.

  • Saint Hugert's Psalm of Divine Protection - Crusader ability

  • Saint Kellast - Saint of the Church of the All Father. Famous for saying of the darkness of the turning "...this must not endure."

  • Saint Malorn the Just - Founder and patron saint of the Church of the Cleansing Flame. Kindler and Keeper of the Flame, through him Confessors can work mighty miracles of fire, smiting the unrighteous and unholy, or keeping shadow at bay.

  • Saint Meynard's Chalice - Historical Artifact (Possibly fictional) that according to legend Sir Diarmid the Bear-Cloaked quested to find.

  • Scout - Every Scout is a master of finding their way, even under the direst conditions, and can make good speed over the roughest terrain. Scouts are also superb trackers and foragers, though most never learn much of the ways of beasts. A Scout's greatest assets, however, are their senses. A good Scout can spot potential foes hundreds of yards away, and most have honed their senses to such a degree that it is almost impossible to catch a Scout unawares. While capable in a fight, most Scouts on a job are loath to reveal themselves, bypassing trouble or looking to their skill at stealth to keep them from harm. A Scout's skills and quick wits are his only protection from death in the wild, and those who live to an old age are as wily as any Ranger. While Scouting is considered a legitimate profession by most, all Scouts are still Rogues at heart, and Commanders who treat their Scouts badly should beware. Scouts hire out their skills to any who can afford them. While questing heroes maintain a high demand for Scouts' services, these Rogues of the wild are found far more often in the employ of armies. Good reconnaissance can be the key to victory in battle, and most Commanders refuse to take their armies into hills or forests without a pack of Scouts to serve as their eyes and ears.

  • Shield of the Martyr - Crusader ability.

  • Sigils - Arcane marks based both upon the Runestones and the arcane geometry of the Tapestry of Creation. When properly enchanted, a sigil acts as a trap for magical energy, a net that can bind a spell and hold it indefinitely. 

  • Sir Diarmid the Bear-Cloaked - Hero (Unknown race possibly Barbarian) who according to bard song single-handedly slew four great Giants while questing for St. Meynard's Chalice.

  • Sir Hurrigan - Hero of Bardic tales, who stood alone against a host of Minotaurs to save Queen Candrae the Fair.

  • Song of the Lion - Amazon ability, believed to raise the moral and/or fighting ability of other amazons nearby. (Unconfirmed speculation)

  • Spirit - Element of Life. No Channeler nor Animator to date has learned to Master the Spirit, and thus has never been able to grant true life to any animation.

  • Storm Cry - Barbarian ability, believed at this time to be a way call down a bolt of lightning from the heavens. (Unconfirmed Speculation)

  • Tae ap Arpalonamil - Elvish epic "The Far Wanderings of the Great Father",describing how elves came to be in command of the world we know, "in the beginning". Contained with in it are the histories dealing with the Wand'rer, believed to be the first elf; as well as Kenaryn possibly the first centaur, and Thurin who may have been the first dwarf. The elvish epic speaks of him traveling with Kenaryn, and Thurin and "others" who left heaven to travel the void where he faced and defeated Prince Kolaur and his horde. Thus claiming the Orb of Ebon. During this battle the Wand'rer was wounded by Prince Kolaur's spear "Callynthir" before being slain by Thurin. 

  • Tapestry of Creation - Also known as the Great Tapestry. The weave of Ley Lines that connect all things in our universe, from the physical to the spiritual.

  • Templar - The soldier priest of the Church of the Cleansing Flame. Blessed by their beliefs they are completely immune to fire. Each Templar is branded on the chest as a sign of their devotion.

  • Thane - Dwarvish word of king.

  • Threads of Creation - Channeler term, See Ley Lines.

  • Thurin -Tae ap Arpalonamil Refence. Companion to the Wand'rer, assumed to be the Archon Thurin the Shaper. slayer of Prince Kolaur.

  • Thurin the Shaper - Book of Staves Reference. The first of the Archons first made by the All-Father, assumed connected with building and construction of physical things. Possibly Dwarvish.

  • Tree of Life - Also known as Guild Trees. Once a person's name is scribed upon this tree they are immune to the permanency of death.

  • True Name - Book of Staves Reference. The names given by the All-Father to all things, in theory buy speaking the true name of an object, living or nonliving will cause it to become manifest (Summoned). 

  • Turning - The catastrophe which shattered our world upon the death of Cambruin and the abandonment of the All-Father. Marks the end of the Age of Kings and the beginning of the Age of Strife.

  • Twisted Breed(s) - A collective reference to the creatures of Chaos. Normally used in reference to Grobolds, Orcs, Ogres, and Trolls

  • Universal Materials - Law, Chaos, Light, and Dark the contents of the void. From these insubstantial materials did the All-Father create our universe. Therefore each of the Elements of Life are made up of each in varying degrees.

Warlocks are a new form of caster created during the Turning, little is known of their true abilities but rumors attest to every thing from ESP, Clairvoyance/audience, to the mental destruction of their enemies. Warlocks refer to their powers as the gift.
