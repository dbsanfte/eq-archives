Aelfborn - The half breeds, the Aelfborn are the sterile offspring of Elf and Man. It was once said that the offspring of man and elf were the vessels of chaos, creatures of spirit rather than soul. For this reason, Aelfborn children are often marked with protection runes to stave off the madness that is rumored to come from years of exposure to human society. Aelfborn lack the immortality of both races -- they age as men, yet can produce no offspring.

Aracoix (restricted)-  Civilized and noble, the Birdmen are a race rarely understood by Man. When first encountered, Man believed them to be primitive and tribal, but time has proven this to be a completely incorrect assumption. Hunted by the Elves to a point of near extinction, the few Aracoix who remain are fierce warriors and brilliant hunters, but disdain the use of Magic beyond the use of artifacts and relics.

Elves - Mournful descendants of a Forgotten Empire. Arrogant and cruel, the Elves were put down by their own slaves, the trained animals known as Man. What few Elves still remain wander the world as outcasts, rather than Kings - their legendary immortality now a curse, as they watch their world crumble and wait to die. The remnants of the greatest Elven families can still be seen among those who wander, borne out through years of ancestry in each elves' skin tone : White in the oldest families, ruling in the cold and desolate Northlands; Brown, for those born to the wild and Lords of the wood; Green for the coastal families who mapped the once-great Seas.

Humans - Once the mere slave race of the great Elven Empire, the Humans have prospered through our broken World and hold tightly to the few vestiges of civilization which remain. Inheritors of a world they barely understand, most of the other races consider the Humans to be the cause of the Turning, and their constant warring a direct cause to the destruction of our World. The truth or falsehood of this claim has never been determined, but even among men, the argument rages on.

Irekei - The "Devilmen", little is known about these strange, red-skinned tribes of the desert. Common folklore claims them to be the descendants of a long lost family of Elven nobles, banished to the Burning Lands for their ferocity and warlike nature. Known primarily for their teachings in the mystic arts, they are also known to be cunning assassins. It is said that Irekei children are trained in the use of knives from birth.

Shade - Labeled a perversion by the Church of the Sun, it is impossible to fathom whence came the strange subrace of men known as "Shades." Often called the "Children of the Damned," Shades are born as human children, normal but for cold demeanor and chilling appearance - for their skin is ashen grey, and their pupiless eyes the color of bone white. No one knows why Shades walk among us, but it is clear the cause - when a human father dies with the child still in the womb, there is a likely chance - but not a certain one - that the child will be born as a Shade. Inexplicably, there are also no written accounts of such births before the Turning.
