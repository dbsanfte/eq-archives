Order of the Black Rose Trade Skill Guide - Dyes

    The rogue class has the ability to create metal coloring  dyes. Sadly these dyes can only be used to color Fine Steel Plate Armor made by black smiths. This armor is unusable to rogues, how ever no one said we couldn't make a plat or two. This process is keyed to our Make Poison Skill (Level 20 minimum). Rumor is dyes stop skill progression at 35. THIS IS A RUMOR AND UNCONFIRMED AT THIS TIME. 

    This guide is broken down into 3 parts. Buying the appropriate material. Creating the dye. Using the dye on armor.

Purchase of dye making and armor coloring supplies:

    Mortar and Pestle (Combinable Container): Rogues make the dye extract in the same  container they use for creating poisons.

    A Flask of Water: Any tavern, most guild houses.

    Resin: High Keep (Near the Bank) Steam Font (Windmills)

    Empty Vial: You cannot use poison vials, nor any vial made through pottery. It must be bought. See Resin for where to buy.

    Medium Clay Jar (Combinable Container): This is made thru the Pottery skill. The Jar is lost upon creation of the dye so you might need several.

    Jar of Acid: High Keep (Near the Bank) Steam Font (Windmills)

    Jar of Lacquer: High Keep (Near the Bank) Steam Font (Windmills)

 

Dye Creation (Make Poison):

    To create the dye you will need one of the following color items depending on the color you want.

        Permafrost Crystals Most creatures in Permafrost

        Evergreen Leaves On the ground in Greater Feydark

        Sarcoscypha Fungus Mushroom men and Fungus men (Guk)

        Russet Oxide Earth Elementals (Najena)

        Iron Oxide Earth Elementals (Najena)

        Charcoal Mist and Air Elementals (Najena, Commonlands)

        Jack o' Lantern Fungus Jack o' Lanterns. (Unrest).

    Next you will need a Mortar and Pestle (Container), A Flask of Water, and an Empty Vial.

    Put the Color Item, the Flask of Water, and the Empty Vial in the Mortar and Pestle and hit combine.

        This will form what is called an Extract. Only use 1 of each do not stack anything.

    The Extract is then combined with Resin to determine the shade of the Dye. 

        The number of Resins used determines the color of the dye. This is done in a Medium Jar.

            1 Resin = Dark Color

            2 Resin = Base Color

            3 Resin = Light Color

        Simply place the Extract and the appropriate amount of Resin into the Medium Jar and hit combine.

 

    With luck and skill you know have a Dye. Which can be sold to a Blacksmith, or Warrior type.

 

Using Dye to Color Armor (Blacksmithing):

    Only Fine Steel Plate Mail created by a blacksmith can be dyed at this time.

    To dye the armor simply place the armor piece into a forge, along with the Dye, a Jar of Acid, and a Jar of Lacquer then hit combine.

    

    With luck and skill you know have a piece of Fine Steel Plate Mail dyed to the proper color.

 

    Note: You cannot change the color again, once dyed the color is unchangeable.
