With the calendar meeting coming up next weekend, I just thought I'd post some thoughts...

Today, I joined NoN/SA (Shadow Alliance not Second Age) for their first POH run ever. They needed a puller and so I went up to teach their monks how to do it.

When we zoned in at 7am EST we found a group of mostly UDL along with some relentless, a second age and a steel wind (probably some others I am forgetting) clearing the plane. They had managed to pull the smithy house along with most of the clerics in the zone by the time we got there. They then left the zone to us.. I assume it was their bed time as their numbers were dwindling.

Later on, after we had cleared to Maestro, a group of 20 UDL logged on and started pulling the houses on the east side from NE corner. They cleared entire east side and then we both started pulling from the Smithy which at that time had respawned.

Overall, I was impressed w/ UDLs behavior... obviously there was some animosity towards them as they had cleared out over half of the loot dropping mobs. But UDL didnt yell back on shout or ooc. In fact, despite all the animosity towards UDL - they offered to res us after first attempt on Maestro and feed our clerics mana.. They offered up any loot which they couldnt use so it did not poof.. and they kept us well informed of where they were so no one accidently trained.

Now, the fact of the matter is that UDL had every right to be there in Hate according to the PNP. Another fact is that everyone, including myself resented them being there... Why? Because the existence of FFA guilds heavily penalizes people who choose to obey the rules of the calendar.

NoN/SA lost out on over half of the loot dropping mobs today... They're all in crafted, and oracle robes, and various mid level armors... They're planes capable... and they won't be in Hate for another month.

When the calendar works it works well.. Ideally, everyone would get their 1-3 days in a plane uninterrupted and have rights to all of the loot. In theory, this is great. In reality, the planes remain empty some days because of lack of people/ability. In reality, calendar obeying guilds lose out on a big piece of something which they wait a month or more for because a FFA guild zones in. Worse yet, we have calendar obeying guilds "piggy backing" with FFA guilds and remaining calendar obeying guilds...

The calendar is not a viable tool with just 1 capable FFA guild - let alone 3 or more. The solution? Its simple. Either the FFA guilds have to respect the calendar, effectively becoming part of it, or the calendar has to go away.

Next Sunday at the calendar meeting... I urge everyone, even the FFA guilds to attend. Discuss not what dates we will go by on the calendar, but draw some lines for an organized FFA. FFA does not have to be a kill stealing trainfest. FFA w/ respect for all is the way to go. Treat the planes like you would any other dungeon... draw the lines.. pick the camps... I'd like to see the planes as someplace I could go for a few hours on a tuesday night and kill some revenants.. not a weekend ordeal where everyone is pushed to their limits in order to get as many clears in as possible.

Think about it.

FFA guidelines, while a noble idea, are probably ultimately doomed to failure.

Reason being, that once FFA kicks in, so does a survival of the fittest situation. In that kind of environment, any kind of rules we impose will be out the window fast.

Another, more technical problem would be enforcement. The penalty for disobeying dragon/plane rules was loss of calendar days or a rotation spot. The loss of days meant they either played by the rules or were basically railroaded into FFA... not everyone can handle FFA, thus most people tried to abide by at least the letter if not the spirit.

What's the penalty for not following a loose set of FFA rules? Nothing. While the calendar penalties are weak at best when regarding certain guilds, FFA penalties would be totally nonexistant. History has shown us that CTC pressure alone is not enough to break the will of most of our FFA guilds... and I can't see too much CTC outrage regarding the breaking of FFA rules, anyhow. At least, not enough to dent an entire guild's resolve.

I would also be interested in hearing a list of FFA rules to be followed that did not coincide with PnP... how would they differ? I can't come up with anything that I would viably consider telling 30-odd FFA guilds and expect them to follow.

That said, I am not personally against an FFA, as frankly when it comes down to it, it won't hurt me at all. Will it hurt a lot of people? Probably. I'm sure FFA of any kind will screw the guilds who can't get the spawns quicker than the competition.

It also means less administrative overhead for me ;) Back to being just a regular whore I suppose.

I imagine there will be sufficient opposition to FFA at the next meeting. However, we need more than a majority vote. We have to keep relatively close to unanimity, or we end up with small guilds that have 1 or 2 days planes time having to put up with half their spawns cleared when they zone in.

I do find some people's change of hearts interesting, though. The planes calendar really hasn't changed at all in months, yet I see people like Laerwyn suddenly crying foul and pointing out the evils of organized EQ. Weren't you vehemently attacking everyone with that exact same viewpoint a couple months ago? Funny how attitudes change when relative power shifts due to a new guild.

BTW, Ryoohki, that last paragraph wasn't directed at you. I'd guess some people would assume it was, as you were the one who brought up the FFA motion, but I wanted to make it clear that I understand you legitimately are concerned. I don't necessarily agree FFA is the right direction for the greater good (depends where your priorities are), but there are certainly longstanding problems with the calendar.
Always have been, and probably always will be...

Especially when people vote "no" to address planes issues at every meeting, despite my urging.
