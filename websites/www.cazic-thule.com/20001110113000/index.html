Abashi Speaks on Casual Players
Kwill | 11/9/2000 - 8:55

Abashi posted on the Everquest General Discussion boards today about the experience of the casual player in response to someone who felt that the only way to have sucess was to belong to a large guild:

There's no question that "success" in EQ revolves around building a proper network of friends. That however applies to those who play 2 hours every other day as well as those who play 10 a day. Those who play less often just define success differently. ...Rather than define it on the basis of how much time one plays, I'd rather define it based on the playstyle of the person playing. It's perfectly possible for someone who's a 10 hour a day player to be a casual gamer, as much as it's possible to define a 6 hour a week player as a powergamer.

EQ supports the casual gamer just fine, as the focus of the casual gamer is not consumption of content, leveling, and item acquisition; It's to "have a good time", by adventuring with friends, exploring areas, and overall experiencing the game. Leveling and item acquisition is ancillary to the goal of having fun; It just happens as a result of their adventuring. -Gordon
Go here for the discussion thread.

SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT ANNOUNCES "PLANETSIDE(tm)," THE WORLD'S FIRST MASSIVELY MULTIPLAYER FIRST-PERSON SHOOTER

Makers of Best-Selling EverQuest(r) Bring Expertise to a New Genre

SAN DIEGO, Calif., November 8, 2000 - Sony Online Entertainment Inc. is pleased to announce the development of "PlanetSide(tm)," the first massively multiplayer first-person action game in PC gaming history.

"We're leveraging our experience with EverQuest into an entirely new genre. Imagine a soldier-to-soldier combat and vehicular warfare game set within a massive persistent environment," said John Smedley, President and CEO of Sony Online Entertainment Inc. "'PlanetSide' is a logical extension of Verant's online capabilities."

In the game, players assume the roles of mercenaries and reside in a distant galaxy where little semblance of law exists. In PlanetSide, these mercenaries rise within the ranks of one of four specialized corporations that offer both character development and professional growth.

"'PlanetSide' expands the first-person action genre into an entirely new universe," said Kevin McCann, producer of the game. "Both team-minded players as well as independent rogues are going to find a place within this game, which represents the culmination of years of Verant's work."

"We've created a persistent world where you work to capture bases and increase your worth over time - a place where you can return again and again, where your accomplishments are recorded and translated into statistics, merits, and other rewards."

More About "PlanetSide" The "PlanetSide" continents measure approximately 64 square kilometers, varying from arctic wastelands to scorching deserts. Players work to capture various technological bases - using both soldier-to-soldier as well as vehicle-based combat - to eventually influence the control of the continent toward their chosen corporation.

There are four primary corporations that offer professional growth opportunities and steady income: the mining industry, the technological industry, the science and research industry and the military industry. These corporations "sponsor" players - offer a starting apartment and a Personal Computer Terminal where they can access job assignments, news, and e-mail.

"PlanetSide," published by Sony Online Entertainment, is due to ship the third quarter of 2001 for the PC platform.

    Best of the Best Wizard Tournament
    Kwill | 11/7/2000 - 9:06

    Lady Daegarmo has announced the rules and details of the Wizard Best of the Best Tournament, to be held November 9th through the 17th on the live servers. On each server the winner must take two out of three matches. The final round on the test server will be held on November 19.

    Lady Daegarmo writes: The Arena is off limits to unauthorized pvp 15 minutes before and 15 minutes after the event. This is to ensure that the contestants and spectators are safe. During this time, play nice policy is in effect regardless of server.

    Both wizards will be summoned out of the zone, and summoned back before the start of the match to clear any previous bugs with permanent spell effects.

    This is a test of magic, not melee. No melee combat of any kind will be allowed in this event. No ranged weapons of any kind will be allowed in this event.
    Both wizards, once debuffed and resummoned shall be asked if they are ready for the match to start. Once the match starts, a timer will start of 30 seconds that the wizards are allowed to cast on themselves, and quickly rememorize the spells. Mana used for buffs will not be restored. An explosion will start the 30 second period, and a second explosion will start the match. A single “Fault” will be allowed (casting before the explosion), and an event warning (not an official warning) be given to the offender. A second “Fault” per match is disqualification. We do realize that this is a “Quick Draw” match and that players will be trying to time the explosions as close to the mark as possible to gain advantage.

    No activatable items of any kind shall be used in the match. This includes any right click items. No use of Invert Gravity shall be allowed, this is the only spell being removed from the event, because it can knock the wizard out of the ring.


    Check out all the rules and regulations at the Harpy's Head Tavern. Wizard Best of the Best Tournament

    Word from Casters Realm has it that the Monk Epic Quest has been completed on the Test Server by Wakefield of the Tribe Guild. Interestingly enough, it isn't a weapon!

    Rather than the standard uber-weapon that has been given out to most other classes thus far, Verant tossed Monks a very nice hand slot item with very impressive stats and one heck of a spell effect. I am assuming its a right click effect because I recall Abashi stating on the boards that procs couldn't be assigned to anything but weapons at this time. I could be wrong tho ;).

    Here is a brief run down of the stats:
    15 AC +100 HP +10 AGI +20 STR +10 DEX +10 STA +10 SV COLD +10 SV DISEASE +10 SV FIRE +10 SV MAGIC +10 SV POISON

    Effect: Celestial Tranquility.

    It is believed that the effect is self-only and gives the wearer 40% haste buff, 40 Strength and 12 AC.
    Sounds like any Monk willing to do the quest is going to make out with a VERY nice item.
