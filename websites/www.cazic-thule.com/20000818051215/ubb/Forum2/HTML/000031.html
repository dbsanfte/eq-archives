Leizav stepped off the docks of Butcher Block, and nodded in mock honor to a Feir'Dal paladin nearby. What does the bastard son of a whore and a priest of Innoruuk really know about honor anyway. Taught to steal, lie and murder by one and disciplined in the ways of hate by the other. Too bad they both discovered just a bit to late how well they had taught the son. Leizav shuddered, that was a cold chapter in his life, better to not remember the screams of his "loved" ones.

Passing by the simple minded paladin who was decieved by the palty high elf form Leizav wore, he stepped onto the orange sand right off the beach of the Ocean of Tears.

Leizav despised this continent of Norrath above all others. It was here he was trapped and made to bend to the will of another. Walking past the towering stone walls of the shipyard he passed several cheerful dwarven guards. Of all the races on Norrath, Leizav actually admired the dwarfs for their hot tempers. He felt bad for the dwarves themselves though..minds clouded by the thoughts and wills of "good" gods.

On through the mountians he walked, sometime near when day filtered to night he let his illusion drop. As Leizav approached the forest of Fay, he felt the day shift completely to night more quickly than it should. A crackling in the air behind him and a heat intense enough to be the very sun told him he was no longer alone.

Behind him stood a figure cloaked in a robe of bonechilled spider silk, with eyes that burned fiery green. "Greetings Leizav, my...father...He called you into his service I believe." Leizav was at once chilled and burned by the voice that spoke. It rasped like burnt bones dragged across a stone crypt floor, yet was shrill, constant. strong, and made him shiver. This...thing..before him was not mortal.

"Of course I am mortal" the figure spoke, pulling the words from the enchanter's mind. "I'm just not mortal in a way you'd comprehend, but besides that, you are here for my Ilharn's bidding, correct?"

Visibly shaken by this creatures words, Leizav questioned "You are the wraithe Vaziel then, son of Mayong the vile lord of Mistmoore." The figure hardened, then let out a brief chuckle. "Word of who I am has reached Neriak then I see, it's of no consequence though. Yes, I am Vaziel the "wraithe"" at this he chuckled again "and have you any doubt I am HIS son?"

"No, no " stammerd the enchanter,trying not to look at the odd entrancing eyes on the robe of his counterpart "I was simply making sure" he responded quickly.

"Very well, enough prattle, I am here to "fetch" you as it were" Vaziel cleared his throat and began to speak the words of magic. In the next instant the enchanter and wizard(for thats what he appeared to be) were in a great hall, lined with beautiful statues of historic heros long since dead. Shadows flitted in and out of the dark corners of the old place. It smelled of death, old and long forgotten.

The wizard stepped forward, cleared his throat and spoke in a voice tinged with not a small amount of fear, "Father..I have done your bidding and brought unto you the young enchanter who you caught here in guise weeks ago." Leizav cringed at that, he remembered when his illusion had dropped at the whim of the vampire lord as he was searching for treasure. THere were few people in Norrath Leizav feared, this odd wizard and his father were definatly two of them.

A voice like booming thunder in the darkest hours of the night spoke, "Leizav..Elgg'Del'Khaless, slayer of his very parents, I, Lord Mayong Mistmoore, have a "request" you will fulfill for me...In the westernmost part of Norrath there is a tomb of a city, overrun by devils and elementals. Here you will find two imps, these two are favored by the dark gods of Norrath. I require from them each a drop of blood. No more than that." Leizav was at once put off guard by the voice which came from everywhere in the hall, yet no where. He responded weekly "I am but a lonely enchanter, my spells are powerful, true, but how am I to..." he stopped abruptly as the very shadows of the room took shape to form an elven figure, that of Mayong. The wizard lowered his head, walked over and took his place beside his father's right side. "You will go to the city of Freeport, there you will gather with you a band of travelers to aid you in your quest. Do not let them know of the true reasons you make your way there." spoke the lord, "and you..my son will watch over him until the business is finished." The vampire cast a glance at the wizard who nodded to an unspoken command. "Leave now, and do not fail me..." At that the air became chilled and the vampire faded in the darkness as he faded so the heat returned to the hall.

The wizard walked up to stand in front of the enchanter. "Know this elf...I have my own agenda, looking after it involves you..for now." As he finished his words his eye began glow an fiery red.

Leizav awoke in the early morning to the sounds of the water front, children playing, men shouting commands in gruff voices. He stood from the bed he woke in and walked to the window...ahh Freeport he thought, what a strange dream...
"Twas no dream enchanter" came that voice from a dark corner in the room "and so you don't think it is again, I give you this "gift"" the wizard rambled off sacred words of magic and Leizav's arm began to tingle. Seconds lately the wizard was gone with the haunting voice trailing into the ether saying "I am watching you Leizav, do not fail my father......"
Shaken, Leizav looks to his arm and finds a black rose seared into his flesh. The revenant Vaziel's own crest.
Leizav turns to the window musing over what has just transpired. "Well, I guess, Norrath for me has just gotten a bit more interesting...."

The long twilight sun swept across the front of the castle keep. It was morning now for the wizard known as Vaziel. Occasionally he would wake up very early and enjoy the day's sunshine, but those times were becoming fewer and more far between. The hours kept by his father prevented him from wasting too much of his waking time gawking at such things as the simple sunlight.

This night was different. His father had givin him command to gather a young enchanter who Vaziel had caught snooping the castle grounds earlier in the year and bring him from the Butcher Block mountains to his fathers court.

The wizard suspected something was amiss the day before when he noticed his father, the vampire lord Mayong staring intently at him while the wizard was studying his spellbook. A conversation had taken place when the wizard closed his book and rose to leave. "Did I dismiss you from my presence son?" Mayong thundered. "No, you did not father, I merely took your stare to mean you wished to be left alone" the wizard replied, visibly shaken. The vampire waved his hand at his son in apology, he had quite the temper, especially with things concerning his son.

Mayong had been cheated by the god Solusek, his offspring, Vaziel, had his life essence stolen and replaced with that of Solusek Ro's. Forever changing the young would be powerful necromancer son of his into a raw controller of the elements known to many as a wizard.

Tonight his father seemed agitated though by the wizard. Vaziel had heard the story of Solusek many times and had even fallen victim to his father's rage once or twice barely surviving the beatings he endured. Fearing this was the case, another re-telling of the story, he made his move to the door of his father's study.

"I know how to redeem you" came his father's voice just as he reached the door. Vaziel froze, fearing that by redemption his father meant the son's usefulness had ended and his life was forfeit. He awaited the inevitable words that would pass from his father's lip banishing Vaziel to whatever hell or plane his father sent his creations to.

"I don't know if I could banish you like so many of those fledglings you've witnessed" said reading his son's mind "and I don't wish that at all. I fear for you my son, These past 20 years I've seen your elven form age. Although slower than others, you will still feel the touch of death." the vampire paused. "Although attempting to turn you into what I am would not work, I have learned there exists on Norrath another type of unliving creature. The problem is that the place they dwell in his holy ground. Neither you nor I could go there. I would use the old dwarf for this, but you did right in destroying him in the caverns of Permafrost for falling prey to the Ring of Scale." The vampire mused on this thought for a bit. "Know this though son, not only do I require these creatures for you, but for a "friend" of mine also who grows weak with age and undeath. Find me that young enchanter, the one soulbound to me for his trepasses here, send him his summons to my court."

"As you command my father" was all Vaziel could say, when he first rejoined his father he would have dared quetioning, but he had learned that fate proved to be most informative.

So here he was, awoken early in the evening by the gypsies who guarded the keep during the day. Supposed to "fetch" this enchanter for his father. Thoughts buzzed right and left in his brain. What is this thing his father wanted him to become, and who was his father's "friend". Again, the thought came to him...time will tell...

Porting himself to the Butcher Block mountains Vaziel followed the Teir'Dal in Feir'Dal form for a few minutes, then let his presence be known with an interesting new spell his father had givin him. Oddly all the spells his father gave him worked slightly differently for the son, this spell was designed to chill those in the effect area to the bone, for the wizard it acted as a blast furnace to all in the effect, but with no physical damage, such was the touch of Solusek Ro..

He greeted the young enchanter, and learned that Neriak did indeed know who he really was now. With a few words he ported them away to his father's great hall. There his father commissioned the enchanter with the task of retrieving the blood from undead devilish imps in the ruins of the Erudite capital. Also, not a surprise to the wizard he was picked to oversee the endeavor personally. After taking the enchanter to Freeport and reminding him(somewhat painfully) the stakes, the wizard did not for the first time in 20 years return to his father's home, but instead visited a Neriak inn as an unknown wizard, member of the spurned. He sent out letters to the friends he needed to see again, a druid with a kindred heart as dark as the wizard's own, a polite reaver of Innoruuk, and a mercenary warrior to name a few and spread no word of his arrival to enemies who would get some surprise visits.

Shallondra sat quietly in the inn, sipping her tea, journaling the day’s events, paying little mind to the help shuffling around her. The candles on each table flickered softly, illuminating the room in a soft glow as the sunlight of the day retreated. A sigh escaped her. It was late. Her benefactor in the temple would not be pleased, should she realize Shallondra was out so late and not meditating in her bedchamber.

Again, as many nights of late, she swore quietly to herself. Why was she trapped inside the stifling walls of the temple? She never understood how she came to be there. No one would tell her anything. When she asked she was reprimanded thoroughly and completely for voicing anything near the subject. She learned years ago that it was a line of questioning that was not allowed. Always, she wondered why. It was a hole in her history that would remain unanswered, she knew.

The door swung open quietly, followed by a cold breeze. Shallondra shivered and looked up. A robed figure stood in the doorway briefly, and then silently moved into the shadows of the room. Quickly she looked away. The breeze from the open doorway carried the smell of magics and herbs. This same figure had shown at almost the same time each night in recent days. Always moving to the same corner of the inn, followed by two, sometimes three others close behind. Many nights she felt the urgency to leave and return to the temple, before she was missed. Tonight, she paused for a bit longer. The robed stranger intrigued her young mind and sense of adventure. Who was he?

Shallondra looked blankly at the pages of her journal. Why did he come each night? Somehow she knew there was a mystery and danger about him. She turned slowly to spy him out of the corner of her eyes. The barmaid was setting a refreshment in front of the robed stranger, blocking her inquisitive eyes. The barmaid moved away and she found herself being regarded through narrowed, cold eyes, glinting menacingly from the candlelight on the table.

Shallondra turned around abruptly. The shackles rose on her neck and her cheeks flushed in embarrassment. How horrifying! He’d caught her. She could feel his eyes on her still, even with her back turned. Shivering, she gathered her things, shoving her journal into her bag quickly and scurried out the door. She ran down the back street towards the temple quickly, then stopped around the next corner and took a deep breath.

Silly girl! She thought to herself. What is wrong with you? Shallondra sighed. She must be losing her mind. She heard the night birds singing their farewell songs for the eve.

“Oh dear… it’s late, the mistress will surely take a whip to my back this time!” Shallondra swore at herself for getting carried away in idle fantasies. She hurriedly ran off towards the temple of the God of Hate, Innoruuk.

A week or so had passed since his father's command, Vaziel had watched with interest the young enchanter as he set out from Freeport with at least one companion in tow. The next stop for the young enchanter was Neriak. Here the enchanter was having difficulty raising a band of travelers, as the Teir'Dal all demanded to know the true purpose of his quest, which of course he could not tell. The wizard himself still awaited return of messages from trusted companions of him.

Ever reminding the enchanter of the quest and that he was being vigilant, Vaziel would materialize every other day as the enchanter awoke and the wizard prepared for his slumber. Occasionally they would speak, other times the enchanter would simply plead that he was trying his best.

Vaziel had time...the enchanter however did not.

During the time while Vaziel waited he frequented an inn known as the Blind Fish. He had busied himself by bringing the agents of Mistmoore who had fallen in disfavor with his father here. He thought it appropriate to buy them a drink or meal before they last breathed Norrath air. On one of these occasions, he had noticed a beautiful young priestess begin to take an interest in him. He had seen her here more than once, and even caught her gazing at him intently at one point. She was strangly powerful for one so young, for whether she knew it or not, she kept her thoughts from him. Few possessed this ability and most had to work at it, the wizard knew many tricks of the mind, some of those who he called friends had learned how to escape his mental scrutiny after years of his company. Frustrating as this was, he knew it mattered not, as these were people he placed his trust in. This young cleric however, was different. What if she could read my thoughts came a horrifying thought. No, the wizard decided relaxing a bit, she appeared much to comfortable to be delving through the depths of the wizard's mind. Things stored in his memory would drive an ordinary mortal mad. Sights he had seen, atrocities he had witnessed recently, which were fresh in his mind from his stay with his father.

Intriqued by her he would follow her to the temple grounds, only to be held back in his pursuit by the hallowed ground. Perplexed by the the cleric that haunted his daytime slumber, the wizard made a desicion to greet this young one and learn who she was and were she was from, and not un-importantly why her thoughts were veiled from him. Was it the work of Innoruuk, who even though the wizard pledged no allegiance to, he was a puppet of none the less.

Waking much earlier than his usual sundown, revenants can walk the day altough their blood much prefers the night, he donned his red training robes, those that denote an apprentice of the spurned, made his way to the inn and sat much nearer her table in the lighted area of the inn. He waited there for her, confident she would come soon, as it seemed her custom to have a drink before retiring. He ordered a warm elven wine, not to drink...he never drank, but he would hold it in his hand and feel the warmth die from it.

Here he would wait.

The note had been delivered in typical Neriak style, by bribing an off-duty member of the Indigo Brotherhood to play errand-boy. Nodding his thanks with impassive features, the knight tossed a pouch of plat toward the ill-bred warrior and waited until the hulking brute wandered away to spend both this coin and his stamina in the loving and well seasoned embrace of Mare X'Lottl.

Still, their constant fascination with drink and rutting made them easily bribed to run trivial errands.

Done musing over the nature of the errand boy, Noamu'Chath turned his gaze to the fine scroll. Murmuring the incantation that would cancel any lingering magic and any unpleasant traps, he waited until he felt the surge of magic flow through him before he snapped the nondescript wax seal and unrolled the fine soft skin.

Reading quickly the coded phrases inscribed in spidery script, he smiled grimly and then rerolled the scroll neatly, admiring the way that the halfling tottler's skin had been so elegantly tanned to provide such a supple and smooth surface for writing. Tucking the hide into his belt with a quickly hidden grin, he strode toward the Third Gate and the Lodge of the Dead for a discussion with his liege lord.

Shallondra looked out the window at the blazing orb in the sky, assessing its place. She closed her eyes briefly, feeling the warmth of the sunlight on her face. It would be a warm evening, she thought to herself. She looked down at the moat surrounding the temple. Her eyes narrowed. Trapped. She smiled grimly. They could not hold her spirit anymore with physical restrictions. She even felt that her benefactor knew of her escaping in the afternoon, and said nothing. Somehow, the high priestess knew Shallondra did not belong within the temple’s walls. Her spirit would not allow itself to be slaved.

Taking a deep breath, she looked behind her at the journal lying on her bag. It was a bit early to sneak out. Smiling mischievously, she threw her journal into her bag, slipped out of her room and down the hallway, cautious to remain in the shadows.

Laughing as the temple receded in the background, Shallondra ran down the back streets of Neriak, eyes already searching for the inn she liked to sit in and journal. She liked the Blind Fish very much, mostly because the help there said nothing about whomever choose to frequent the establishment. No questions, this is what she needed to protect her haven. This was the one place she could truly relax now, be herself. The temple was stifling.

Shallondra rounded the corner and approached the entrance of the inn. Suddenly she stopped, gazing at the door, feeling a dark presence inside. Her breath caught in her throat. He was there. The arcane wizard was already there. She could feel it, a cold shiver passed through her. He was early, almost two hours early. Why?

Taking a deep breath, she approached the door, her senses heightened at the presence of this powerful one. He was very powerful, this too she could feel. How, she did not know, oft she had the ability to sense danger around her. Did this mean she was in danger? One way to find out.

Shallondra pushed the door open just enough for her to slip inside. The door swung shut behind her. She looked around briefly; she did not see the intriguing stranger. Sniffing in disgust at her foolishness, she moved further in to the dim room towards the table she normally choose to sit at during her time there. She glanced at the barmaid and motioned her to bring her drink. The help knew her now, needing not to ask what it is she wished. She pulled out the chair, expecting to see the barmaid place the drink in front of her.

Frowning, Shallondra looked up just in time to see her tea set in front of the shadowed figure in the corner. Opening her mouth to object and thrash the girl for the mistake, the words fell short. There he was. The air from the room seemed to be sucked away, she could not breath. He was waiting on her arrival. Why? The question echoed in her mind.

Slowly, the robed figure leaned forward, so the light from the nearby window illuminated his face. Gaunt. Almost death. Afraid, yet enthralled at the same time, Shallondra stepped forward. Her eyes locked on his, captured, she wanted to look away but was at once unable to.

Gathering her courage, she bowed respectfully and murmured, “My apologies, master, the wench made a mistake with my refreshment.”

His eyes narrowed a bit, and then he smirked. “No mistake, yathrin. I wish you to join me.” He deftly waved his hand, indicating she sit at his table, where her drink had been placed.

Shallondra swallowed. She could not refuse, do that and she would appear rude. She moved to the chair, sat and said, “Thank you, you are kind.”

His eyes remained locked on hers for a moment in silence, then he asked, leaning forward a bit, “What is your name?”

Shallondra looked away for a moment, confused, wondering why he was asking. Perhaps he would report her transgressions to the temple. She looked up again, seeing him watching her again, waiting for her answer. No reason to fabricate a story, she thought, he already knew she came here in the late afternoons and probably more. Instinct told her that.

Taking a deep breath, she replied, “I am Shallondra Bel’la, master.”

As Knok sat in the inn, drinking his ale, as he has for the past years in excess, a traveller came into the bar. Knok sat in his designated table and watched as the traveller began asking the barkeep questions. Intrigued by the wanderer Knok listened attentively. At last the traveller left and Knok was still very curious, but his curiosity came and went as his sanity.


Knok was a self loathing bastard child of a necromancer. He often thought about his past, his future which seemed to be bright. He was a young Teir'Dal with courage and a flair for adventuring, but his roots or lack thereof soon overcame the Magician. He chose the path of summoner of the arcane arts in spite of his mother, who wished the child to continue in her necromancy. He always enjoyed controlling the conjurations of his. He soon became adept at the art, often summoning things as a child to impress his young friends. He ran away from his home at a very young age and soon traversed Norrath to seek his fate. Gaining in power he often would return to Neriak only to talk with his master, then soon leave with disgust as he thought of his heritage. All was going well, until Knok suddenly felt a pulling or tugging inside of him. He had an urge to return to his home, to find his roots. This reawakening inside of Knok bound him to return to his mother whom he left so long ago.


Upon returning to Neriak he learned that his mother had passed and that she did so in deep depression. For there were rumors that she had had two children, and neither of them recognized their mother. A grief stricken old necromancer woman who passed away with no family. This thought has haunted Knok for the past two years. And during this time he had not left Neriak, he just sat and drank, and waded in his self-pity. This once promising young Mage, had become a babbling drunk. Many believed he blamed the death of his mother on himself, and carried this burden, and drank to remove himself from the truth.


This traveller however had brought Knok out of his drunken stupor, he couldn't explain it, but he felt that this nomad was somehow important in his search for his heritage. He soon got up from his table and asked the barkeep who the man was. At this the barkeep responded "A foolish man, who is seeking men for a quest, however he won't explain himself, so no one will listen to him." Interested Knok returned to his table.

The shadowknight bowed deeply murmuring his understanding and obesiance to his lord, before backing his way out of the private audience chamber. The very air within the Lodge of the Dead seemed sullen, watchful as he made his way toward the quartermaster. The stained walls of the vast building could tell the tale of many a betrayal if only they could speak in words men understood. For the members of the lodge served the Queen herself, unwaveringly in the silent and deadly struggles for control of the City. The sacred art of the necromancers brought the blessings of the Father of Hate himself upon their cause, while the dedication of the warrior-mages, the Shadow Knights enforced the will of Christianos.

In return he was given a journey pack and the appropriate supplies for a foray into the wilderness. No questions were asked, no answers were offered. The token spoke volumes of the support behind this mission.

Hefting the bag to his shoulder, Noamu'Chath made his way toward the small private chapel that served the knight's quarter of the Lodge. A single soft luminescense filled the room, illuminating the solid walls with a faint blue glow. Removing his helmet, the Shadow Knight knelt, bowing his head in prayer before the altar of Innoruuk.

Some time later, his devotions completed, he made his way toward the seedy tavern near the vaults in the Common quarter. He'd tossed a simple grey cloak over his shoulders to partially hide the jet black platemail from casual sight. One table was occupied with several common laborers swigging ales at an alarming rate. A drunken man muttered and mumbled at his perch near the wall. Several tables were unoccupied save the one in the darkest corner. There a hooded figure and a female were engaged in some conversation, a kettle of tea resting neatly nearby.
