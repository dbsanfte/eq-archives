Raiding Rules and Procedures

  • The Divine Prophecy has various guides and procedures for different raids that can be completed.  We strongly encourage all members to visit the Knowledgebase and review these guides.

    To ensure efficient raiding, this page is designed to provide the core basics to all guild-sponsored raids.

    Core Raiding Rules Loot Rules

    Core Raiding Rules

    Have Your Own Means Of Egress

     

    We usually don't have a wizard. We seldom have enough druids. Add to that unexpected things like server crashes, power outages, ISP outages, prolonged LD. You get the point. Gate potions are cheap and easy. Its better to have a gate potion and never use it then to be stuck somewhere with no way out.
     

    Be On Time


    For scheduled raids make your own arrangements for transportation to the raid site. If time is tight, do it the day before the raid. Its ok to ask in guild chat for a port, but don't get upset if no one can come get you.

    Reduce Spam


    Spam as little as possible. Ask for buffs with tells. Talk to friends in tells. Don't send tells to the puller or RL asking them to pull "your" mob. Stay off /OOC and shout.  If you wish, can set up your own spam channel.

    Group Leaders Are To Keep Track Of Their Group

     

    Let someone know if you have a LD or AFK person.  As group leader this is your job. Also let the person handling grouping know if someone left the raid. DO NOT start picking up people on your own.

    Pay Attention

     

    Missing something sometimes means all the difference between a close call and a wipe.

    Be Respectful Of Grouping

     

    Don't whine about your group. Its ok to ask if the group maker realized you don't have a healer. Its not ok to go on constantly because you don't have a cleric. Hate raids have been run without a cleric in the past.

    Death Happens

     

    Don't come if you can't afford to die. We all die at raids. We've never lost a corpse yet. We haven't missed a Rez since our first epic attempt.

     Stand Still

     

    If your not absolutely sure where your going stand still. Its a lot easier to find you if your where we were, versus where ever you happened to run off to.

    Control The Buffing

     

    Never heal or buff a FD puller unless he knows your doing it, and okays it. Otherwise you'll pick up his aggro and if he FD's the camp will be trained or you'll be summoned. Or DT'ed in the case of Fear.

    Loot Categories
    Greed Roll - in which everyone rolls, and does with the item as they wish.

    Can and Will Use - If the raid leader calls a Can and Will Use roll then characters attending the raid roll if its an upgrade for them. Because not everyone at a raid rolls for a this, these items won are not to be sold or given to alts. One possible exception to this is if your using the item you won to trade up to an even better item. You were given the item to make the character attending the raid do a better job. The exception to the attendance rule is when the raid leader asks you to bring a specific character and you get permission to roll for the character you wanted to bring, and then you can roll only that character.

    All rolls will be lvl X 10 to 1000 unless otherwise specified.

    Camped and Reserved Loot - In some cases Loot may be reserved. Including but not limited to this are epic drops, camped rare spawns and upper extreme items like Breast Plates. In the case of camped spawns normally only the "key" item will be reserved.

    Cash Loot - This goes to the guild to cover cost involved in raiding.
     

    Have Fun

     

    You can be organized and have fun. If fact a well run raid is tons more fun than chaos. The loots much better too.

     

    Loot Rules

    Who Can Roll

    1.) You may only roll if you are a full member of DP, not a probate.

    2.) You may only roll if you CAN and WILL use the item. If you have equivalent or better gear, you should pass. It is HIGHLY recommended you do some research on common drops in a zone we are raiding so you will know what gear there will be an upgrade for you.

    3.) Do not roll on an item with the intent of selling it unless the raid leader announces a GREED ROLL.

    4.) Do not roll for an alt who is not on the raid unless you were specifically requested to play your main instead of your alt by the raid leader, and he has agreed you may roll for the alt instead.

    5.) Botted alts being used on the raid are allowed to roll for items they can and will use. This assumes the bot is actually contributing to the raid and not just along for the ride.

    6.) Regardless of how many characters you are playing (botting), you will only get one roll per item at a raid (you choose who rolls). If any of your characters win something, you are not allowed to roll on anything else until everybody else wins something or the raid leader makes an exception.


    How To Roll

    1.) Do not roll until the raid leader calls for it. Rolling early will invalidate your roll and you may not be allowed a second one on that item. Repeated violations could mean no more rolls for you at all on that raid.

    2.) Random 10 X level to 1000. If you random incorrectly you MUST roll again even if you like the result of the incorrect roll. It is highly recommended you create a hotkey for randoming.

    3.) Do not spam rolls or roll "for the fun of it" on an item you won't or can't use. It is often difficult to get all randoming done before items rot, and frivolous rolls compound the problem.


    If You Win

    1.) You may not roll on another item on that raid until all in your class have won something. There may be some exceptions allowed by the raid leader for differing types of items.

    2.) Passing down items you are no longer going to use to other guild members is a nice gesture, especially if the item is something you won on a previous guild raid.


    Exceptional Items

    1.) Some loot will not be rolled on at all but will be distributed by the officers. Common examples are epic items, uber spells, reserved items, and so on.

    2.) If someone has camped a mob specifically waiting for it to pop (such as Lodizal) he or she may reserve the key item from that mob, but other loot will be subject to the above rules.
