Horse Racing Quest...

This is new, so thinks are bound to change.

Basic idea is the players start in Tar Valon, using the unique "gleaming white stallion" that is there. Once started (you're placed at the stable in Tar) you race to the destination (random place somewhere in med). There is an enrollment fee, 50,000 - and you can bet on enrolled players (both enrolled and non-enrolled people may bet). So for players there's 3 potential pay offs, the winning pot (taken from the enroll fees) and bets you've placed, and if you're racing, a small pay off from any bets placed on you.

There are a number of commands:

help - the always present help spam
help betting - help spam on placing bets
enroll me - Before you can enroll, you must Bank Transfer me 50k. I will MATCH all enrollment fees so the pot grows.
mount up - Places you in a holding room (which exits to stables on startup) IFyou wish to bet on yourself or other players, then don't "mount up" until after you're all done betting (you have to give me money to bet)
dest - show the destination (where I'll be) and the current enrollment pot
who <String> - Find a player(s) enrolled in the quest via a specific string to search for. You want to bet, but on who? Instead of me spamming 20+ names on quest or telepaths, you can tell me "who <some string>" to search for. The Search is CaSe SeNsItIvE Examples:
who J - would list anyone with a name starting with "J"
who gar - would list anyone with "gar" in their name
check <Name> - Check name is useful to see how many people have bet on a player. "<Name>" must match exactly the mud name of the player. Note: the more bets on a player, the better your pay out, if the player wins in the top 10.
my pot - Used by enrolled players, see how many players have bet on you, and your cut of those bets (1/5th) This is what you will win, regardless of what position you finish in. Simply just finish and win this amount.
bet <Name> - To Bet on someone, you must FIRST give me coins "give xx coins Omawarisan". Bets are anything from 5k-100k. After you have given me the bet, tell me "bet <Name>" to bet on a player.
return bet - If you gave me coins for a bet, but have not yet selected a player and wish to re-adjust or cancel it use this command.

IF YOU DIE, when you CORPSE just tell me dead horse I'll reanimate you and give you a mount. You must be a Corpse and enrolled in the quest.


PAYOFFS:
For Racers I'll pay the top 10 finishers a sum of the total enrollment pot, naturally the better positions earn more.
For Racers: If people bet on you, you get 1/5th of the bets, as long as you finish, regardless of your finishing position. You can even bet on yourself :p
For Betters: I will pay betters who bet on any of the top 10 finishers. The more bets on a player, the better your payoff. Your bet is a formula based off the total bets placed on a rider, the riders position in winning, and your bet amount. Naturally the more you bet, the more you might win.

BETTING:
You can bet ONCE for any or all players enrolled in the game. Anyone can place bets, racer or not! (If you are a racer you can bet on yourself and any other competitors). Bets are 5k-100k .. any value in that range. The more you bet, the more you might win back. Basically 1/5th of your bet is given to the rider..(as long as the rider finishes). 4/5ths of your bet is used in the math formula for bet payoffs. Your total bet amount is also used in the bet payoff formula. You MUST be online to win.

Riders be warned, if you haven't "mount up"'d and the race starts, you cannot race! You "miss the gate call". If you dilly dally around trying to place bets and miss the final call .. you're out (you can still win bets you placed though). I will quest out warnings before "opening the gates"..

Races will always start in Tar Valon, but can end anywhere in med that a horse might travel.

Note: there are 2 horse types in Tar Valon, Unicorns and Stallions.. You must use the STALLION. Arriving at the destination mounted on anything other will prove fruitless. You MUST "mount up" to race. No sneaking around with stallions beforehand.
