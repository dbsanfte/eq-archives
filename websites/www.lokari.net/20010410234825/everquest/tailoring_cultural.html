    • Halfling Cultural Armor

    Ingredients for Halfling Cultural Armor

    Halfling cultural armor requires the following special ingredients:

    • High Quality Metal Bits

      Smithing - This can only be made by Halfling Smiths, and must be made in the Rivervale forge. Combine two pieces of High Quality Ore and a Flask of Water. The High Quality Ore is occasionally found on Runney Eye goblins. It can also be found on goblins in Permafrost. This becomes trivial at a smithing skill of around 80.

    • Vale Sewing Kit

      Smithing - This can only be made by Halfling Smiths, and must be made in the Rivervale forge. Combine High Quality Metal Bits (quantity unknown), a Needle Mold, a Thimble Mold and a Flask of Water. This becomes trivial at a smithing skill of around 160.

    • Acorn Oil

      Brewing - This can be created by a member of any race, using any Brew Barrel. It is not restricted to Halflings or Rivervale only. Combine a Flask of Water and a Misty Acorn in a brewing barrel. The acorns are random ground spawn in the Misty Thisket. This becomes trivial at a brewing skill of around 103.

    • Imbued Plains Pebble

      Cleric/Druid - Plains Pebbles are foraged in the various Karana zones. They must be imbued by a Cleric or Druid who worships the god Karana. Imbued Plains Pebbles add STA and WIS attributes to armor created with them.


    Vale Studded Leather

    • Vale studded leather armor is created by combining the appropriate tailoring pattern with Acorn Oil, some quantity of Metal Studs, and a Brute Hide. The Brute Hides drop off Voracious Brutes in the Dreadlands and Skulking Brutes in Warsliks Wood.
    • You can optionally include an Imbued Plains Pebble, which will give the items you create additional stamina and wisdom enhancements.
    • These items can only be crafted by a Halfling Tailor, and must be combined in a Vale Sewing Kit.
    • The non-imbued variety becomes trivial at around skill 115, the imbued variety at skill 122.
    • The non-imbued variety is wearable by races : HUM, ERU, ELF, DEF, HEF, DWF, HFL, GNM, and classes : WAR, CLR, PAL, RNG, SHD, DRU, MNK, BRD, ROG, SHM.
    • The imbued variety is wearable by Karana worshipers only, of races: HUM, ERU, ELF, DEF, HEF, DWF, HFL, GNM, and classes : WAR, CLR, PAL, RNG, SHD, DRU, MNK, BRD, ROG, SHM.

    • Vale reinforced leather armor is created by combining the appropriate tailoring pattern with Acorn Oil, some quantity of Steel Boning, and a High Quality Brute Hide. The High Quality Brute Hides drop off Vicious Brutes in Warsliks Woods and the Frontier Mountans.

    Wood Elf Cultural Armor

    Ingredients for Wood Elf Cultural Armor

    Wood Elf cultural armor requires the following special ingredients:

    • Mithril Bits

      Smithing - two small pieces of mithril ore, morning dew. Must be made in the Fier'dal Forge in Kelethin.

    • Mithril Boning

      Smithing - small brick of mithril ore, file, morning dew. Must be made in the Fier'dal Forge in Kelethin.

    • Mithril Studs

      Smithing - three mithril bits, file, morning dew. Must be made in the Fier'dal Forge in Kelethin.

    • Fier'dal Sewing Kit

      Smithing - one mithril bit, needle mold, thimble mold, flask water. Must be made in the Fier'dal Forge in Kelethin. Creates a ten-slot Fier'dal Sewing Kit that only works for the cultural armor. You'll still need a regular sewing kit to make conventional items.

    • Oak Bark Tannin

      Brewing - A flask of water and a piece of Oak Bark, combined in a brewing barrel. The Oak Bark can be foraged in Kithicor and Warslik's Woods, and also occurs as a ground spawn in Butcherblock. Becomes trivial at brewing skill 103.

    Mithril Studded Leather

    • Mithril studded leather armor is created by combining the appropriate tailoring pattern with oak bark tannin, some quantity of mithril studs, and a Sabertooth Tiger Hide. The items must be combined in a Fier'dal Sewing Kit, and the sabertooth tiger hides drop from Kunark Sabertooth Tigers. You can optionally include an Imbued Emerald, which will give the items you create additional charisma and wisdom enhancements.
    • The non-imbued variety becomes trivial at skill 108, the imbued variety at skill 122.
    • The non-imbued variety is wearable by races : ELF HIE HEF, and classes : WAR CLR PAL RNG DRU BRD ROG.
    • The imbued variety is wearable by Tunare worshipers only, of races : ELF HIE HEF HUM, and classes : WAR CLR PAL RNG DRU BRD ROG.

    • Mithril reinforced leather armor is created by combining the appropriate tailoring pattern with oak bark tannin, some quantity of mithril boning, and an Excellent Sabertooth Tiger Hide. The items must be combined in a Fier'dal Sewing Kit, and the excellent sabertooth tiger hides drop from Kunark sabertooth tigers.
    • Both the imbued and non-imbued varieties become trivial at a skill greater than 135.
    • The non-imbued variety is wearable by races : ELF HIE HEF, and classes : WAR CLR PAL RNG DRU BRD ROG.
    • The imbued variety is wearable by Tunare worshipers only, of races : ELF HIE HEF HUM, and classes : WAR CLR PAL RNG DRU BRD ROG.

    Erudite Cultural Armor

    Ingredients for Erudite Cultural Armor

    Erudite cultural armor requires the following special ingredients:

    • Terrorantula Silk Swatch

      Tailoring - Presumably this is made by combining two Terrorantula Silks in a tailoring kit. The Terrorantula Silk drops from Terrorantula, a rare giant spider in the desert of South Ro. It is unknown if this requires an Erudite Sewing Kit or if it will work in a regular kit.

    • Purified Mana

      Enchanter - Uses the level 49 spell "Purify Mana", one Poison Vial and four Rubies.


    Steel Silk Armor

    • Steel Silk Armor is created by combining the appropriate tailoring pattern with some number of Terrorantula Silk Swatches, a Vial of Purified Mana and Platinum Thread. The items must be combined in an Erudite Sewing Kit. These recipes are unconfirmed, and the trivial levels are unknown.
