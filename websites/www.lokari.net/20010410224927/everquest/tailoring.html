    General Tailoring Notes

    • Verant is somewhat inconsistent in their use of "skin" and "pelt". For the most part they are interchageable terms - if you have a "skin", but a recipe calls for a "pelt", generally you can just go ahead and use it.

    • When combining stackable items (such as studs) in a sewing kit, don't stack them! Put each item in its own slot.

    • A smith can make an item called a "skinning knife" for you. You can use this knife to convert a high quality skin to medium quality, medium to low, and low to ruined. Just place the skin and the knife in your kit together and hit combine. The knife is not used up in the process. Note that this does not work with the named skins (like lion skin, or shadow wolf skin) - the knife is destroyed when you try an illegal combination.

      For those inclined towards smithing, the recipe for the skinning knife is - 1 small brick of ore + 1 dagger mold + 1 hilt mold + 1 water.

    Trivial List

    The "trivial" levels for items made in Everquest indicate the point at which you can no longer increase your skills by making that item. You can still experience failures making trivial items, but the further beyond the trivial point you get, the fewer failures will occur.

    Some of these trivial levels are pretty certain, some are just estimates, based on conflicting information. Unfortunately, I can't test out trivial levels without starting up a new tailor every time an item is in question! Just bear in mind that these are close estimates, and some items may be off a few points.

    Leather Armor

    Tailors can create three kinds of leather armor – patchwork, studded and reinforced.
    The size of the armor made depends upon the kind of pelt used, not the size of the sewing kit.

    Silk Armor

    Ingredients

    All silk-based items require at least some of the following ingredients:

    • Silk Swatch

      Tailoring - These are made by combining two spider (not spiderling) silks in a sewing kit. Swatches become trivial around skill 17; however, they never fail.

    • Heady Kiola

      Brewing - Combine two kiola saps, one water flask, and one bottle in a brewers barrel. This item requires only a low skill at brewing, so any tailor should be able to make them as needed. The cost to make them is just under 3 gold each. Heady Kiola becomes trivial at brewing skill 46.

    • Viscous Mana

      Enchanter - It uses the 12th level enchanter spell Thicken Mana, which is available in the Brownie village in Lesser Faydark, and on an island in the Ocean of Tears. Casting the spell requires the material components of a poison vial (made by a potter) and a pearl.

    Raw Silk Armor

    • Raw silk armor is wearable by Monks, Mages, Necromancers, Wizards, and Enchanters, of any race. Raw silk armor is made by combining silk swatches with the appropriate pattern. All raw silk items have a weight of 0.4. Raw silk becomes trivial to create at skill 36.

    Cured Silk Armor

    • Cured silk armor is wearable only by monks. Cured silk armor is made by combining silk swatches and heady kiolas with the appropriate pattern. Remember that the heady kiolas and the silk swatches must not be stacked. All cured silk items have a weight of 0.1. Cured silk becomes trivial to create at skill 82.

    Wu's Fighting Gauntlets

    These magical glowing silk gloves are Monk only, AC 4, with a weight of 0.1, and give off light as a greater lightstone. The recipe is 1 glove pattern, 1 silk swatch, 1 greater lightstone, 1 vial of viscous mana, and 4 heady kiolas. Do not stack the heady kiolas. These become trivial to make at skill 88.

    Quest Recipes

    • Shadow Silk - Temple of Solusek Ro, primarily Necromancer

      1 Shadow Wolf Pelt, 1 Silk Swatch and 1 Scroll of Gather Shadows. The product is used in various quests.

    • Large Shadow Silk - Temple of Solusek Ro, primarily Necromancer

      2 Shadow Wolf Pelts, 1 Silk Swatch and 1 Scroll of Gather Shadows. The product is used in various quests.

    • Inky Shadow Silk - Temple of Solusek Ro, primarily Necromancer

      1 Shadow Wolf Pelt, 1 Silk Swatch and; 2 Scrolls of Gather Shadows. The product is used in various quests.

    • Second Rank Skullcap - West Cabilis, Necromancer only

      Ask Master Xydoz in the Tower of Death about Second Rank Skullcap. He will state that he seeks a Faded Tapestry. You need to be adept at tailoring and acquire two pieces of the now destroyed tapestry and repair them. The pieces are the Torn and Ripped Tapestry, which you find off of Sarnak Hatchlings as rare drops in the Lake of Ill Omen. Tailor the two pieces together (the result is a mended tapestry, not a faded one, but it works) and hand them in for your Apprentice Skullcap - 2nd Rank.

    • Sparring Armor - East Cabilis, Monks only

      Master Bain gives you a Leech Husk Pouch for you to fill with leech husks. Once done, you are asked to combine the skins and take the Full Leech Husk Pouch to a local tailor by the name of Klok Mugruk. He will then hand you a ready made Training Bag Husk and tell you that you need to get three Piles of Granite Pebbles and then you can tailor a Monk Training Bag for your caste. When you combine these together, you get a Monk Training Bag. Hand this back to Master Bain and he will reward you with a random piece of Sparring Armor.

    • Robe of the Lost Circle - South Karana, Monks only

      Brother Qwinn in South Karana seeks the return of the Code of Zan Fi. It is dropped by Targin the Rock, a rare spawn in the Sol B throne room. Bring him the Code and a purple Silent Fist Headband and you will get a special needle. Brother Zephyl in Rathe Mountains wants the Idol of Zan Fi, which is in the possession of Brother Raster, who spawns in the Mino Elders room in Lower Guk. Returning this Idol, plus a Red Sash, results in a special tailoring pattern. These two items need to be combined in a sewing kit with a swatch of Shadow Silk and a Scroll of Jonathans Whistling Warsong, which is bought from the Bards Guild, to make a monk only item called the Robe of the Lost Circle. This robe is Magic, Lore and No Drop. The stats are AC 15, STR +5, STA +5, AGI +5, DEX +5, HP +50, and Wt 0.5.

    • Patched Gnoll Fur Bundle - Halas, any class

      On the second floor of the Halas bank, hail Cappi McTarnigal. He will ask if you're a member of the White Rose. Tell him you are - he will ask you for mammoth calf hides but at the same time his fellow trainer, Vsanna MacGibbons, mentions gnoll fur. Ignore Cappi after this. Ask Vsanna "What gnoll fur", and she tells you to take four patches of gnoll fur and sew them together, then take four of this product and sew them together, and finally take four of that and sew them together. Yes, that's right, a total of 64 patches of gnoll fur are required. This creates the Patched Gnoll Fur Bundle, which you can either turn into Vsanna for the Fang of the Wolf, or keep it to wear - the bundle serves as a cloak, AC 4, SvCold +5, Wt 3.0, wearable by all.

    • Blanket - Eastern Wastes, any class

      Garadain Glacierbane in the Eastern Wastes asks you to make him a blanket so he can stay warm. In a large sewing kit combine two High Quality Tundra Kodiak hides and two High Quality Snow Cougar pelts. The blanket this creates is no-drop. Giving this blanket to Garadain results in a Copper Coldain Insignia Ring, faction changes, and some experience. The blanket becomes trivial lower than skill 82.

    Tailored Containers

    • Bearskin Potion Bag

      One grizzly bear skin and one silk cord. When you put 5 or 10 shaman-made potions into the bag and click the combine button, they will be stacked.
      Weight - 2.5, Slots - 10, Holds - Small, Weight Reduction - None, Trivial - 68.

    • Fleeting Quiver

      Recipe and statistics unknown. This is presumed to be a permanently broken item.

    • Handmade Backpack

      One high quality bear skin and one backpack pattern. These are the first Holy Grail of tailoring, and are consistent money makers.
      Weight - 3.0, Slots - 10, Holds - Large, Weight Reduction - 10%, Trivial - 88.

    • Tailored Quiver

      One high quality cat pelt and one quiver pattern.
      Weight - 0.4, Slots - 6, Holds - Medium (arrows only), Weight Reduction - 22%, Trivial - 115.

    • Tailored Large Bag

      One silk cord, and one low quality bear skin.
      Weight - 0.8, Slots - 6, Holds - Large, Weight Reduction - 10%, Trivial - 41.

    • Tailored Large Belt Pouch

      One silk cord, and one low quality cat skin.
      Weight - 0.6, Slots - 6, Holds - Medium, Weight Reduction - 10%, Trivial - 37.

    • Tailored Small Bag

      One silk cord, and one low quality wolf pelt.
      Weight - 0.4, Slots - 4, Holds - Medium, Weight Reduction - 10%, Trivial - 31.

    • Tailored Wrist Pouch

      One ruined wolf pelt and one silk cord.
      Weight - 0.3, Slots - 4, Holds - Tiny, Weight Reduction - 10%, Trivial - 26.

    Other Items

    • Leather Padding

      One silk thread, and one low quality skin (wolf, cat or bear). Creates a Leather Padding, which is a stackable item used in smithing. It doesn't matter which kind of skin you start with - the end results will always be the same. Becomes trivial at skill 31.

    • Silk Bandage

      Two silk threads, unstacked. Silk bandages weigh 0.2 each, as opposed to a normal bandage at 0.5. Becomes trivial at skill 21.

    • Silk Cord

      Three silk threads, unstacked. Becomes trivial at skill 26.

    • Silk Thread

      Two spiderling (not spider) silks. Becomes trivial at skill 15, but like Silk Swatches, they never fail.

    • Tailored Whip

      One medium quality skin (wolf or cat - a medium quality bear skin does not work), four heady kiolas (unstacked) and one whip pattern. A tailored whip has damage 7, delay 33 and weighs 6.0. Becomes trivial at skill 36.
