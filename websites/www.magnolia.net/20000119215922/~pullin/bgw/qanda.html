07/25/97 Questions answered by Dr. Muzyka

I have just lately taken a look at Interplay's Fallout demo. It brought up , as I see it, an important issue. In Diablo, your character remained in the center of the display at all times. This means that the terrain was always scrolling. With Fallout, you can walk anywhere on the current display and then scroll the terrain manually. This interface did not seem as nice or logical as Diablo's. Which will you use? If you use the Fallout method, will you add a "mode" that allows the interface to act like Diablo's? Also, do NPCs always remain next to you are do they get strung out sometimes?
You can control any of the six characters - your first, plus 5 PC's. This is inherently different from the control in Diablo and Fallout, which were both single character games - that is, you controlled one PC in your party. You can center on one of your characters with a double left click; otherwise the field of view scrolls as you wish. It would be very frustrating if it didn't, due to the large size of the party (up to 6). When you walk, the terrain does scroll, to answer the first part of your question. It follows your selected character unless you choose (via double left click) a different one, where it will follow the other. A single left click causes the display to stop following the selected character but does not center the view on another.
If you have made any descisions about network/internet play, please drop me a line.
We're doing the net play - probably in the main game. We're working on it now, and we'll see when it gets done.

07/18/97 Questions and answers with Dr. Ray Muzyka, CFO and Joint-CEO of Bioware Corp

In previous AD&D computer games, the player could modify his character's attributes to the max (ie STR 18(00), INT 18, WIS 18, DEX 18, CON 18, CHA 18). Will you allow this or will you make the player take a rolled value?
You will get a roll and be able to modify the rolled numbers to the racial max/min for each attribute.
In creating a Priest (Cleric) class character, will there be a choice of god to follow or will you simply choose an alignment? If you choose a god, will this effect the game in any way?
We might - still debating this though.
Will the thief only be able to open locked doors and bypass traps or will he be able to actually pickpockets?
Pickpocket of course too! Why else would you want to be a thief?
Will there be a way to speedily rest and memorize spells (bypass realtime for the sake of having to wait through this boring action)?
Yes - you hit a "Rest" button and the resting occurs instantly - unless you are attacked overnight, where you go to the main game view.
Will you go as far as having items make saving throws?
Hmm. Maybe.
You have prerendered screens. Does this mean that you do not have special effects such as moving flames in a fireplace?
No, we have fire in the fireplaces and a lot of other ambient animations.
Will you release a DVD-ROM version of this game?
Probably.
Is LAN and modem support a given?
Probably - we're negotiating this now.
In your multiplayer model, will you have server based characters or local characters? The local characters in Diablo allowed for much cheating.
Probably local, but this could change. Having a server base would imply that we're going to have a server...and this is probably not gonna happen for a LAN- or TCP-LAN based game...

07/16/97 Discussion between Crispy and Scott Grieg (head programmer)

noticed when looking at and comparing the thumbnail versions of several of BG's screenshots (http://www.alcyonis.fr/~garulfo/baldur/) that the degree of angular rotation of a few of the rooms and buildings seems to vary. Some seem to be about 90 degrees different, while others only vary by maybe 5 or 10 degrees. Will there be a random layout to the buildings in the cities and towns in this sense, giving a more "sprawled" look? Or could this have just been the difference between two separate areas of the game or even a change in the game's engine?
Actually the game engine allows for buildings of any shape to be placed at any angle, even large round ones. Just wait until you get a look at the city Baldur's Gate itself. It has buildings cramed together, on irregular streets, just like a medeval town.
What happens when your character walks behind a building? Do they disappear and will there be separate, back entrances, for example?
The character is obscured by the building, but character becomes transparent so you can still see who it is. We are trying to avoid placing back entrances, so you won't have to constantly wave the cursor over buildings looking for doors.

So if you want to scan in a picture of yourself, or just paint on a pair of elf ears, you will be able to do this.

07/12/97 Questions and answers with Dr. Ray Muzyka, CFO and Joint-CEO of Bioware Corp

Interface
Is the user interface customizable? A screen shot on the French site showed icons down the left side of the screen while another at Thanos web site showed the same icons in the upper right hand corner.
No - these are different stages in the concept of the GUI. Of note, we originally planned on a customizable interface but decided that this was a waste of resources - we instead decided to optimize the interface and increased the number of customizable buttons (e.g. hot keys for quick spells, quick items, quick weapons, quick party formations).
How exactly does the interface work? Do you use the mouse to click in the screen for movement/attacks/NPC interaction?
Yes, but there are many hotkeys as well.
Diablo for instance, only had a few weapon animations (sword, mace, bow, staff, axe). Will BG show more? Will BG show a different animation for each weapon?
Yes. There are a fair number of weaponsin the game (dagger, longsword, broadsword, two-handed swords, staffs, clubs, flails, maces, morning stars, bows, crossbows, spears (non-throwing only), ?maybe a few more I can't remember). Yes, they all look different, most with different movements and all with different weapons in hand. And magic weapons and special weapons LOOK different (glow in dark, different colors, etc.)
I have seen screen shots where armor has a greenish tint and shots where armor has a bluish tint. Is this configurable by the player or does this denote different types of armor?
It denotes different armors. But you can change your skin color, hair color, and two user clothing colors on the fly. We reserve certain color ranges for indicating armor types and leather/trim colors.
One of my main gripes with Diablo was the inventory. Will we have to divide our gold up into sections within our inventory as with Diablo? Will inventory be limited by weight as per the rules or by size as with Diablo?
No gold dividing up (yuck). Weight is a factor (you can't load a weak character with too much stuff, and every item does have a weight), but you can store items in the "group pool" temporarily (you can't use them there but can pull them out later for use by individual characters). The group pool is limited by numbers of items, and each player can carry only a certain number of items as well (limited by a certain number of slots per character).
Can you save a game wherever you are? When you save, how complete is the state information? Will bodies left in a certain area still be there when you come back to it much later? Can you save during the middle of an encounter?
Yes, save anywhere. We keep track of a certain number of bodies (like bullet holes in Duke Nukem). Yes, I suppose you could save in the middle of an encounter (but you'd have to exit to the options page to do it).
The area in BG seems to be quite large. Will there be quick ways of traveling across country?
Yes, a world map will let you traverse the country to areas which you had already visited.
Will there be a mapping function for dungeons (if there are any) or will you be forced to do your own mapping?
The maps for dungeons will be the same mapping system as everywhere else in the game - the map will be progressively revealed as you move around. This is intentional - we want the amount of notes you have to keep kept to a minimum. Mapmaking in the game would be quite painful due to the size otherwise...
The French site mentions something about no tiling. Do you really not use tiles?
No. It's all prerendered, no tiles anywhere.
Gameplay
At the Tavern, I saw you post that the game could be paused to go into turn-based mode. Can this be done in the middle of a fight? If so, once paused, can you simply change the script for one of your NPCs and then go back to realtime mode?
Yes, that's the point - to be able to turn the game from a real-time game to a turn-based game on the fly. Realtime scripting - I hope to have this but I don't know if we can do it for sure. But we're going to try.
Which of the following will you have:
Random encounters.
Rarely only.
Set encounters.
Mostly these.
Random treasure. (if random, do you use all items from the DND tables or a smaller subset?)
Rarely only.
Set treasure. (Any artifacts? Any newly create magical items?)
Mostly this. A lot of new items - and magical items which are "standard" (most are this type, since this game is for 1st-6th level characters) all are going to be named (if we have time) and will have some background history as well which will be accessible in the inventory page.
A store in which magical items can be bought.
Of course! But only a few can be bought...most must be earned...
In some of the later AD&D computer games, there were puzzles that really didn't make sense and therefore were rather hard. Will BG contain puzzles? What kind?
We don't like puzzles that don't make sense. But ones that do sound ok to me...riddles, and logical puzzles. Plus ones where you have to do a little research in the towns and dungeons of the region.
So far, most of the screenshots I have seen have been outside or in a building. Are there any underground areas? If so, can you send us a couple of screenshots of this?
Yes, there are dungeons. Lots of them. Will try and do so.
In Diablo, the only ranged weapons were bows. Will you include the ability to throw daggers, darts, axes, etc.?
Yes, but probably not axes - we're not sure how you keep getting more axes to throw. Daggers and darts, if you have a lot you can throw. Spears. no, since again you'd have to have a lot to make it worthwhile.
In some of the gold box games, they implemented a "vault". This allowed the party to store gold and extra items. Are you going to have an equivalent function?
? I'm not sure what you mean. There will be areas that are safer than other to dump stuff, certainly. But if you just dump a bunch of stuff in the middle of nowwhere, it might disappear.
The vault was simply a place in a town or, as in Pools of Darkness, Elminster's pocket dimension where you could leave items and gold and come back to get them later. A totally safe place.
Maybe. I don't know yet.
Can the player totally wipeout all encounters in the game? Or will there always be some random encounters?
Yes, there will always be some. But not too many.
How will you handle resting? Can the party always try to rest? Or will there be areas where they cannot even try to rest?
Yes, you can rest anywhere (no Dark Sun rest camp sites!). But some areas will be better to rest in than others, else you might get rudely interrupted.
If you "finish" the game with a character, can you reload him and start over?
No. Start over? With a 6th level character? But you can transfer that character to the next game and to the mission packs. Plus probably as many of your party as you'd like to transfer. Actually the mission packs are a little different - you can load the mission pack either while you are still playing the main game, or afterwards - it plugs into the main game.
Will the player be given specific quests by a NPC and then rewarded on a successful completion?
Yes. The game is party based (6 characters in your party).
One of the cool AI things from Pool of Radiance was the ability for random encounter size to be determined by the number of characters in the party and their levels. Will you implement something like this?
Yes. But set encounters may or may not have variable numbers of foes. Some (even though not really random per se) may vary the number of enemies, while others are set encounters.
Can you remove an NPC from your party after you have allowed him/her to join? If you can remove this character, will he remain in the current spot always? Will he disappear? Will he return to his original location?
He/she proceeds to his post-game location. Where he or she may or may not want to join you again.
Do NPCs move around on their own? If you played the game twice, would it be possible to find the same NPC in 2 different locations?
They move in limited ways. Only if we tell them to. Usually we don't (it's harder to find them then!)
Can you fight NPCs that are in the pool of characters that can join your party? Can you attack someone who is in your party? In combat, can you "accidently" hit one of you NPCs with a spell or weapon? If so, does he turn against you?
Yes. Yes. Yes. yes, but only if he's pissed off enough (he'll warn you that he's getting upset).
What about fighting in town? Is there a penalty for this? Can you attempt to break into stores or other buildings at night?
Of course! But guards might start noticing you and townsfolk might not want to talk to you afterwards. Evil doesn't ALWAYS pay, but it might in the short term!
Rules
How detailed is the rule base you are using? Do you get down to the weapon speed level? Do you count certain item's AC bonus to apply only to its area(for instance: Would a helm give you an AC bonus of 1 or would it only protect your head?)?
Weapon speeds: yes. Spell speeds: yes. Helm: AC +1.
Can you give us a list of character classes/races that the game will support?
Classes: Fighter, Ranger, Paladin, Bard, Thief, Mage, Druid, Cleric; plus multi-classed non-humans (for a total of about 18).
Will you include special character classes like the ranger, bard, and druid? If so, will these classes include their special abilities like use of 2 weapons without a penalty, playing music as a spell like effect?
Yes. 2 weapons: no (I wish we could!). Bard song: yes.
Will special spells like enlarge, fly, and animate dead be implemented? Will these spells actually change the character's look(ie grow larger, appear above the ground)?
Some. Some aren't possible due to engine limitations. But most are. Yes, they will alter appearance if it's appropriate - but only again if allowed by the engine. I'm not yet sure about resizing characters - we're trying this now but the bitmaps don't look too good all the time.
Will you include special character items (ie an item that can be used only by a Lawful Good character)?
Yes, but remember this is a fairly low-level game, hence we don't have a lot of high-level items. No artifacts, for example.
Will all character classes be able to use 2 weapons in combat with the appropriate penalty on the 2nd hand? Will the warrior class increase attacks per level at higher experience levels (ie 7-12 lvl - 3 attacks /2 rounds)? If so, how will this appear different in realtime? Will his weapon just swing faster?
2 weapons: no. Increased attacks: yes. It will be seen as more actual effective attacks each simulated round of (approximately ) 3 seconds.
Will you require spell components for the magic user?
No.
Will the characters automatically go up levels or will they have to train?
Automatic. But this is a good idea - we may implement the training if we can think of a reasonable way to do it.
Will magic users get new spells automatically when they go up a new level or will they have to find all of their spells?
Find em.
General
Will there be familiar characters from the Forgotten Realms novels in the game?
Yes.
I have seen 2 conflicting reports on the number of cds. 3 and 5. Which is it or do you not know the answer yet?
5. I think :)
Can you take screenshots from within the game?
Hmm. WE can, but I'm not sure if we will have dumps in the release.....this is an intriguing idea. I think we will!
Gamespot's current estimate for this game is December. Is this about the right time frame?
Yes, more or less. QA is our main worry, along with the multi-player game - we're not sure when the multiplayer game will be done. If it is in the main release (my preference) it may take a month or two more. If in a mission pack, this will not impact the main game. We're working on it now and discussing plans for implementation dates with Interplay now.
Quoted from Thanos site: "What are the minimum system requirements for the game. It will run on a P75 with 16 meg and a 2 meg video card - but we're planning to implement better features for players with better systems (a faster computer - say a P166 - and more memory - say 32 meg - will allow for more animation and reduced latency). MMX is also being implemented so as to allow for larger areas of animation on MMX-based systems." What exactly do you mean by "better features" and "larger areas of animation"? Can you give us an example of what you mean?
Well - we're working on this now. More variety in the people populating a region, better frame rates, larger areas of animation for effects, faster effects, etc. The exact increases in performance are what we are working on now - we really need to try it to know what we will get on a faster machine with more memory and MMX. This influences the design implementation as well - we will design features for better systems to show off the better effects!
