This page contains the Brimstone Charter.  The Brimstone Charter is our set of rules and regulations to help keep Brimstone flowing smoothly.  In Addition to the rules and regulations, you will find a Point Schedule which outlines the process for collecting points and what they are used for.  The easiest way to split this charter up is to define three key fields we consider the most important: Duty to Brimstone, Duty to Allies, Duty to Norrath.

Duty to Brimstone

The first and foremost priority is to your guild.  Your guild is the collection of people who work with you towards a common goal, the people who accompany you on quests, your trench-mates in the thick of battle, the people who patch you up after your toughest ordeals - both physically and mentally.  Optimum Brimstone is one thing you should strive for on a daily basis, Optimum Brimstone is not a guild, but a Family.

How to Serve Brimstone (Point Schedule)

Everything you do to contribute to Brimstone will not go without recognition or reward.  Though there isn't a strict schedule naming every task and each allotment of points (as there are too many to possibly list), we can give you a general idea of how points are earned and what they are for.  One (1) Point is worth .01 Copper.  Basically, points can be cashed in for goods and services at Brimstone's expense.  When you earn X amount of points, we will find that special item you're looking for, providing you have enough points to "pay" for the item based on fair market value.  For Example, if you have 25,000 Points, you can buy from Brimstone a Stein of Moggok (Market Value is 250 PP).

Examples of Point Earning Activities

Recruitment (100 Points - 50 Points for Assisting Officer)
Event Participation (20-75 Points)
Weekly Character Updates (20 Points)
Forging an Alliance (500 Points - 250 Points for Assisting Officer)
Monthly Basic Participation (50 Points)

All of these contribute to the good of Brimstone, and you are rewarded for your performance.

Consequences of Treachery or Laziness

By joining Brimstone, you promise the following:

To Participate in Events (Not all Events, but provide notification of ones you cannot attend in advance).
To keep us Updated as you increase in level (One Update per weekly roster update).
Not to "Guild Hop" to serve other guilds by collecting information (Spying).
To Interact with other guild members in a peaceful demeanor through Guild Chat and Message Boards.
To Respect all Officers, Command Officials, Alliance Representatives, and Allied Guild Members.
To Perform as a member of a Neutral Guild by not PKing without Just Cause.
To occasionally visit the Guild Website for changes and new information.
To show your Pride by wearing your Guild Tag "<Brimstone>" at all times (unless asked not to for a GM Event or any other similar purpose).
To Help other members of Brimstone whenever possible.
To bring swift reckoning to our enemies by dispatching them with extreme prejudice.

It may seem like a lot to do, but it's all very basic and easy if you really want to be a member of Brimstone.  We can be lenient and accommodate you, but we will not be taken advantage of.  If you fail to maintain these simple practices, you will be removed from Brimstone.  Period.  Anyone found committing acts of treason against Brimstone will be added to the Enemies list and considered Kill on Sight for a period of time no less than 2 weeks and no greater than 2 months unless your actions warrant a stiffer penalty.

DUTY TO ALLIES

Our cardinal rule when interacting with Allies is simple:  Treat everyone wearing the guild tag of an Allied Guild as if they were wearing a Brimstone guild tag.  They are our Brothers and Sisters and deserve your respect.  If there is any discrepancy between you and a member of an Allied guild, do not take the matter into your own hands, get in touch with the first available Brimstone Officer so we can take care of the situation ASAP.

If you wish to cite a member of an Allied Guild for outstanding performance by providing services or any other helpful actions, let an Officer of Brimstone know.  I'm sure their Officers and Guild Leader would love to hear about their Members Kindness.

If you are being PK'd by a suspected Allied Guild Member, bag your droppables and take a screen shot of being slain.  If you are given just cause by a character not wearing a tag of an Allied Guild to PK them, and it is later revealed that you have slain a member of an Allied Guild, be prepared to return any Loot.  To protect yourself from situations like this, be sure to have screen shots of an provoking behavior on that character's part.

Regard any orders from an Allied Officer as you would an Officer of Brimstone as long as the order does not go against any of our policies, or violates your better judgment and common sense.  If ordered to do something you feel is absurd, simply say "I respectfully decline your request, please feel free to discuss this with an Officer of Brimstone.  Thank You."  Name an Officer that is available, there is no need to be rude.  If you follow this simple procedure, you have done no wrong in Brimstone's Eyes.

Anyone who is overly rude or brash towards Allies, or caught committing crimes against an Allied Guild will be ejected from Brimstone and sentenced to a period of time on the KoS List depending on the severity of the offense.

DUTY TO NORRATH

We are a Neutral Guild.  We do not hunt PKers at Random, and we certainly do not PK at Random.  We engage in PvP Combat only when provoked through various forms of harassment such as extreme profanity, disrespectful emotes, Kill Stealing, etc.  Before you even take a step to initiate combat for any of these reasons or one similar that isn't listed, have screen shots of the Provocation.

If you find yourself in a group that is being attacked by a known PKer or PK Guild, feel free you come to the aid of your party.  If they are NOT known PKers, DO NOT engage in combat.  Put some distance between yourself and the skirmish and send a request for a clarification through Guild Chat.  The reason we ask you to do this is so you do not bring Brimstone in to an already heated Guild War, or tarnish Brimstone's image by assisting yet unknown PKers in a Kill.  If you are unsure, ask another member of Brimstone, preferably an Officer.

We encourage the use of Role-playing to better enjoy the aspects of EverQuest. Just keep in mind that as you Role-play, you are Role-playing as a member of a Neutral Guild with Allies, regardless of how much you hate Trolls, Humans, Enchanters, or Warriors.

We ask that you try to exploit as little as possible, never is preferable.  Taking advantage of in-game bugs to further yourself is dishonorable.  We won't kick you out for exploiting, but ask if you have to, keep it at a minimum.

Be respectful to other players, don't Spam in OOC, AUCTION, or SHOUT.

Follow the Play Nice Policies set forth by Customer Service Reps and enforced by GMs.  Even though we're on a PvP Server, Corpse Camping, Bind Point Camping, Zone PKing can be used to your advantage when dispatching our foes, but NOBODY likes having it done to them, so just keep that in mind.  We'd rather not lose people cause they get their accounts termed over something trivial.
