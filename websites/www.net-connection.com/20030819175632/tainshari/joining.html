Joining Tain Shari

Times change and we change with them. When Tain Shari began it was made up almost entirely of members from the same geographical area, namely Huntsville, Alabama. However we are now opening the guild, on a limited basis, to members from other areas. It is not our goal to grow to be a huge, "uber" guild and so we plan to take only a few new members. Our first preference is to fill slots we are low on: warriors, enchanters, rogues, and clerics though anyone is welcome to apply. Preference will also be given to those we have played with in the past. Please note that our guild plays exclusively on the Test Server.

Note: You must be at least level 45 to join.

If you are interested in joining our guild, please read the guild rules listed below and fill out the guild application. Our guild rules are in a state of development. What you see posted is the most current version. After filling out the application, you will be invited to play with us for one month to see how well we fit your needs and you fit ours.

After the"get to know you" time of playing together the guild will decide whether to invite you or not. The decision to allow new people into the guild will be made by the guild membership committee, which meets each Monday at noon. Applicants will be notified as soon as possible after the discussion.

Guild Leadership

Tain Shari was founded by Sulin, who is no longer active. It is led by a committee of the original guild officers who meet each Monday at noon to discuss guild issues.

What We Are Looking For In Members

  • Maturity - We don't care how old you are, we care how old you act.
  • Fellowship - We are all very good friends and we only want new guild members who will also be our friends.
  • Responsibility - This goes back to the maturity part. If you say you'll be there, be there. When you have a guild as small as ours, every person really counts.
  • Compassion - Our members are noted for traveling halfway around the world to rez a guildmate or help someone move to a meeting spot or help with a body recovery.
  • Humor - Laughing is good, we do it a lot. We like to torment each other but never in a bad way.
  • Respect - We all respect each other. We know our strengths and our weaknesses and we accept those. We treat each other as we wish to be treated.
  • Honor - Our motto is "Honor Knows No Bounds" and we really do try and play by that.

What We Are NOT Looking For

  • Loot Mongers - In our guild we don't mind sharing items but if loot is your driving force in the game, we are not the guild for you.
  • Whiners - Nobody likes a whiner, why should we?
  • Elitists - While there is nothing wrong with guild pride, we have no use for those who are seeking L33t guild so they can get phat lootz. If you are just looking for a guild to "get your epic" please look somewhere else.

What We Offer To Our Members

Friendship. Good, mature, dedicated game play. Help in achieving your goals in the game. Help out of a jam when you need it. Laughter. Fun. Someone to die with in a valiant effort and someone to rez you afterwards. Pride in accomplishing things in a smaller group than most would believe possible. Friends you will still want to talk to long after Everquest is history.

Guild Rules

Attendance - If you say you will be at a guild raid/event/gathering, we expect you to be there. We play on set nights as a guild and the plans we make involve everyone who says they will be there. On other nights we usually just play with whoever shows up. You are not required to play with the guild every time, but when you're needed we expect you to come and help out. The nights are:
Monday - Guild Play Night. What we do is usually decided by the officers at the Monday lunch meeting. Plans are posted on the message board and this is also where you post places you would like to go and things you would like to do.

If your plans change post it to the message board, email a guild mate or tell someone in the game. Failure to show up 3 times without letting anyone know can mean removal from the guild.

Trade Skills - Our members have a wide variety of trade skill available to share. As with most folks on Test Server, we do not usually seek to make a profit and only ask to be reimbursed for the actual cost of an item. If you want to chip in a little extra to further the skill level of the character making the items, it is totally up to you. You can refer to the skills page for a list of who does what and how well.
Loot - We have always had a very informal loot policy, but with the guild growing we have to firm it up a bit. The nightly raid leader determines the looting policy for that raid, and if they do not wish too set one, it falls back to the most deserving gets the item. We also have a pass down rule, this means that when if you upgrade a piece of equipment on a guild sponsored raid then if possible you pass down the piece upgraded to others who may need (kinda like how they pass tink bags down on dragon raids). With maturity, loot is not a problem and it is much more common for our guys to try and give stuff to others than take it for themselves.

Things That Will Get You Kicked Out

1) Cheating, either each other or the game
2) Failure to attend, repeatedly, without letting someone know
3) Embarrassing the guild in public (acting like a wanker in shout and OOC or by your actions)
4) Stealing from anyone in our guild or out of it
5) Lying in the game to anyone in our guild or out of it

Some Things That Will NOT Get You Kicked Out

1) Having your tail fall off in the middle of a fight
2) Not agreeing the Juzzie is a very "pretty trollie"
3) Mocking Freaya's pants.
4) Calling Crouching Lizard he.
5) Telling Ivar you still think his hat looks like a sombrero
6) Refusing to submit to Durgle's PayPal requests
