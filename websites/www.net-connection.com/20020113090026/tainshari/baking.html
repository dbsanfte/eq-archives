Amethysax's Big-Budget Guide to Baking

 

This guide is designed for the baker who is looking for the fastest possible progerss and assumes that the chef has plenty of funds (plenty meaning around 30pp depending on INT and CHA. You probably won't need that much, but it can't hurt). Start by putting 20 skill points into baking, if you don't have 20 skill points it just means that your start will be a little slower. Buy a portable spit or have one made by a blacksmith. This lets you cook in the wilderness (or in the same Inn where you are shopping) instead of travelling to a town everytime you want to whip something up. Make sure you have several backpacks, the ingredients you will be working with will be bought in quantity for the fastest progress.

Levels Instructions
1-22 *

Sandwiches and Eddible Goo. For sandwiches you need meats from bears, rats, mammouths, wolves and bread. On most servers you only need to go to the vendors in the newbie areas to get all the meat you need for this. Put 1 meat (do not use "chunk of meat" as it will not work) and 1 bread in your spit and combine to make a sandwich. If plentiful sandwich meat isn't available, change over to making edible goo. Rat ears are easy to get and fire beetle eyes are sold by the Erudite at Inn 3 in East Commons. Put 1 ear and 1 eye in your spit and combine, this gives you 1 eddible goo.

*You only have to get 2 points this way if you have put the 20 starter points in, so should not take long.

 

 
23-45 Batwing Crunchies. Move to East Commons. From the vendor in Inn 1 buy frosting, lots and lots of frosting. I bought it in 160 lot increments but those with higher intellegnce will go up faster and may not need as much. Then move to Inn 3 and buy an equal number of batwings from the Erudite. Combine 1 batwing and 1 frosting in your spit to make a batwing crunchie.

 

 
45-82 Fish Filets. From the vendor at Inn 1 in East Commons, but the usual 160 jugs of sauce, then move to the dock in Freeport and buy fish (not the fish scales). Combine 1 fish and 1 jug of sauce in your spit to make 2 fish filets. These sell for more than they cost and you should be able to recoup the money you have spent getting this far. Go back and forth until you get to 82 skill level.

 

 
82-135 Fish Rolls. Buy your usual 160 fish at the docks and head back to East Commons Inn 3. Buy batwings. Combine 1 wing and 1 fish to make a fish roll. Go back and forth until you get to 135 skill level.

 

 
135 - ??? Pies, pies and more pies. Skill points really slow down at this point. You need to make dough in your mixing bowl (1 flour, 1 milk, and 1 egg - any type) then put 1 dough, 1 berry or fruit and your pie tin in the spit and combine. The tin comes back so you only need one.

Following this procedure, you can go from 0 skill as a baker to well over Master level in less than 6 hours. The cost is roughly, 20 pp for a dwarf with a 65 INT and a 45 CHA.

The benefits to baked food as opposed to bought food. Well ......

  • It's lighter. Bought food weighs roughly 1.0 while baked food weighs 0.1, so you can carry 10 homemade sandwiched for every 1 purchased ration. Monks love this since they are always watching their weight.
  • Some quests, especially in the Kunark zones, require a baking skill to complete.
  • It allows you to turn loot that you would normally leave on the body or destroy into something you can sell for a profit or use.
  • It gives you something to do while your waiting for the boat, riding on the boat, or meditating.
  • There are rumors that someday Varant will send a famine upon the land and vendors will no longer sell food. This will make it a very valuable skill to have.
