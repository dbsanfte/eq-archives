Vivid
unregistered 		posted 02-07-2000 10:42 PM           
This has obviously been beaten to death, but I think I’ve got a few constructive ideas that I’d like to suggest. There are several problems with the class as currently implemented, and there are a large number of spells (including several entire lines) relegated to the back pages of the spellbook. Don’t get me wrong - I do NOT think that all of these should or need to be implemented, I am merely making suggestions for possible changes, any combination of which would make the class more playable, and would not unbalance the game.

IMHO, the pet nerf is mostly irrelevant - as most postings (though not all) say they don’t use the pet anyway. I still do, but I personally feel that replacing the lost attacks with a commensurate amount of hit points (or at least AC, he IS carrying a shield, after all) would be much more useful - our pet dies almost instantly in most circumstances. I have had a green spider kill mine - without poisoning him, when he (the pet) was a high blue. Mages and Necros can both heal their pets, which have 150-200% the hit points of ours to begin with.

The non-attacking pet idea I have seen a few times is brilliant, and I believe not unbalancing at all - perhaps replace Sight spells with second pet line (or just toss current pet line, or alternate the two) I believe this would be a much more group-friendly pet an Enchanter could cast to save their ***es in a dungeon where a normal pet or charm is suicide. Some possible ideas for the pet are as follows:
Higher HP and AC than regular pet - perhaps around Mage pet.
Higher Taunt skill - equivalent to or just under Ranger of similar level. It should be something that can be turned on or off.
Has hate list much like monsters, but based on damage done to CASTER. This determines whom it taunts. Either that or have /pet commands work to direct its taunt.
Minor damage shield, not as much damage as regular pet but enough to be useful. Possibly even a buff we have to cast separately, castable only on this pet (but please give it a reasonable duration). I question whether it should stack with other damage shields, that might get abusive.
This could be something that uses Enchanting skill - perhaps component is a magicked up tiny dagger.
Ideally it should ALWAYS stay right next to me, as any good damage shield should. Permanent /pet guard me mode.
Animation could be a ball floating over my head. Ideally something small that doesn’t get in the way of targeting – we’re the only class that has to target that critter in the back corner over there rather than just the one in front the tank is beating on, DURING combat, and the pet REALLY gets in the way. (BTW - am I the only one with problems targeting through 3 tanks, 3 pets, and 2 MOBs? Especially when one of the mobs you want is right on top of the other one?)
One last thought - perhaps an enchanter pet heal spell on this pet would be allowable

Main problem with Charm as pet alternative is high resist and extremely short duration. It seems necessary for this spell to be useable (without frequent deaths) to do at least one of the following:
Put a minimum duration on the spell
or Make the random duration component a MINOR + or - from a base time computed by casting skill, Resistance and Charisma. Not a 1 second to 10 minute range (heavily weighted toward the 1 sec side)
Reduce resistance – Tash helps but resists are still high & duration still low.
Give warning before it breaks.

One possibility to make an improved charm less overpowering is to require a component to cast, as with pet spells. Perhaps a 1-2 gp gem (bribing the MOB? and a more expensive one for the higher level charms. After all, we are getting a pet out of it, however briefly.

The constant thread I’ve seen about higher level creatures resisting spells constantly (Due to a recent patch, even despite 200+ charisma reported by some posters. Also experienced personally with lower level creatures) worries me considerably. If I do an AOE mez on even 3 or 4 creatures, and 2 resist, I’m dead before I have the chance to recast. Granted Color series helps, but if they resist that too? And then I also have to run far enough to avoid my own spell and recast AOE mez if there’s more than 1 or 2 left, all within the few secs Color gives me - not likely. I think that one possible answer to this problem is an AOE Tash spell. Obviously it should not be as effective as the single target spell, but at least enough to be useful in making AOE mez have a chance of not killing me with the 3 blues that resisted.
I agree with all the postings on the Strength debuffs being largely useless except PvP. One possible solution - have the MOB start out with a certain amount of weight on them. If they’re wearing no armor, and have no encumbrance, how is it that they have a better AC than a warrior and hit for more damage? Are all NPC’s monks? Are they not carrying any food or loot (even if we don’t get it ? If they start out with 60/80 encumbrance like most players do, then these spells might pack some punch.

A few other comments:
Tashania reduction from 65 to 31 is ridiculous. At least leave at 45 or 50.

SLTW (49th) should at least do equivalent speed increase to the Shaman Alacrity (44th) spell, currently it is running a poor second to it (based on an analysis I read on a shaman board). I like the idea of all the haste items, since this spell is probably short duration and extremely mana heavy, but if this and clarity (replaceable by Bards) were Enchanters’ main assets for planes runs, then we are seriously missing something at higher levels. I quit using my cleric because I foresaw a future of buffs and bandaids - please don’t make me be solely a crack vendor or I’ll have to switch again. Maybe wizard - DD & teleports.

I would like to have Illusion spells mask religion (at least partially - I got whacked in Neriak in DE form just by going vis, they don’t like Quellious much there , but I don’t believe they should mask any faction from past deeds - we would also lose good faction that way, and it is something you can adjust yourself usually with time & effort. However, since the Alliance / Benevolence series works on EVERYONE with the same faction group within the zone, a partial workaround is to pick the ones who hate you the most & hit them with it. Then hope there’s not more than one group that hates you, and always zone invis.

Granted we’re a great moneymaker class (if you’ve got 4 hours to run for a Stein or a high level wiz friend, or 5000pp to become a jeweler, and you’re on a newer server where they aren’t ubiquitous & cheap yet), but money does not equal exp and levels. As many have said before, I’d hate to solely use my enchantress as a twinker for my real characters. I don’t think we’re to that point yet, but we do have our weaknesses.

And lastly, I don’t mind adding things to other classes, but it does seem like most of the unique strengths of the Enchanter class are being farmed out to the other classes (especially to necro, which everyone I’ve spoken with refers to as the God class, except necros). If our class strengths are no longer ours alone, should we not gain some of the other classes strengths to compensate? After all, the reason WHY our DOT and DD stink is because we have crowd control spells, which now the necros are usurping.
