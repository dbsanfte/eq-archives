PSX Designer
unregistered 		posted 02-01-2000 07:53 PM           
I can’t say that I am surprised! Enchanters are one of the rarest classes in the game, we lack the numbers to make enough of a stink to be noticed and on top of that we generally don’t complain. This is a dangerous combo, because Verant can basically nerf us at whim with little negative response and this provides no deterrent to do so. Enchanters are slowly receiving several nerfs here and there, though they are no too bad yet, given time they will pile up more and more. It was my opinion that if enchanters didn't complain and give Verant grief they would appreciate it and be fair to our class. This is not the case, enchanters need to start making their voices heard, before the next nerf and the next nerf and the one after that. We are already becoming underpowered quickly, and from what I hear we are quite worthless in the planes and with dragons. I will list some changes I would like to see made to our class to bring us back up to par with other classes. Its long so skip it if you don't like to read.

1. Charm spells need a minimum duration. Keep the current maximum time and formula of charm, just add a duration minimum. Maybe one-half second per level of the enchanter. High-level charm spells need to have their caps raised, so they become effective on the planes. It seems the majority of monsters on the planes cannot be charmed because they exceed level 50. Obviously charm should never affect dragons or gods in any way.
2. Whirl till you hurl and DDD need to have longer and more reliable duration. The terrible duration and mana cost makes sure this spell is never used. The visual component of the target whiling needs to be fixed also or a message stating the target has stopped spinning needs to be added.
3. Illusions need to be upgraded to be more than just a gimmick, you don’t want shrink to be a gimmick why should an illusion be? I suggest the illusions should mask religion and raise all faction to at least indifferent for the appropriate race. If an illusion masks your identity then it should mask your past deeds, because they do not know it is you. I suggest the following additions to illusions, I believe they are not too powerful or unbalanceing considering the buffs that exsist. Barbarian: +2 str and +2 stam, Dark Elf: +3 agi, +2 int, -2 cha and hide, Dwarf: +2 str, +2 stam and –1 cha, Erudite: +4 int, +2 wis and –2 str, Gnome: + 1 agi, +1 dex and +3 Int, Half Elf: +2 agi, +1 str and +2 dex, Halfling: +3 agi, +3 dex and hide, High Elf: +2 int, +3 wis, +3 cha and –1 str, Human: +2 all stats, Ogre: +5 str, +5 stam and –5 cha, Troll: +3 str, +4 stam and –4 cha, Wood Elf: +2 Agi, +2 Dex, +2 wis, -1 str, forage and hide, Skeleton: see invis, enduring breath and no need to eat, Earth Elemental: +10 str, proc root and +10 AC, Air Elemental: +10 agi, levitate and proc stun, Water Elemental: +25 vs cold, enduring breath and +5 str, Fire Elemental: damage shield and +30 vs fire, Dry Bone: see invis, enduring breath and no need to eat +20 vs fire, poison and disease, Spirit Wolf: slower version of sow, +25 vs cold, +5 agi and dex, Werewolf: +20 str, +25 magic, poison and disease and proc screaming terror.

4. Pets could be upgraded by adding some hot points, attack speed or some interesting weapon procs like color flux or whirl till you hurl.
5. Memorization spells need to affect large targets. I understand why a stuns or bashes should not work on giants, but memorize affects the mind directly and color flux stuns visually, so unless the target is blind it should be affected too.
6. Curse of the simple mind and mana sieve are not very effective against npcs if at all. Since npcs have much larger mana pools and intelligence, then maybe the spells should have double to triple the effect against npcs only.
7. Area affect memorization needs an upgrade or a gain in duration as the enchanter reaches higher levels.
8. I think weapon and item enchantments should be scrapped and redone. Creating a perm ant items means that some point in time that item will no longer be in demand and that spell will become useless. When was the last time you got a buyer for Wu’s gloves now that the market is flooded? It would be nice if our enchant spells gave a proc to a weapon or shield that lasted for an hour or a stat/ resistance bonus to armor, that way the enchantment would be needed again in the near future. Other nice temporary enchantments that could be bestowed to items might be levitate, enduring breath, greater light source, DOT, DD, whirl till you hurl. It could be as simple as the enchanter hold the item while casting the enchant spell, and then they click on a spell in their memorized slot to add this effect to the item for a period of time. Higher level enchant spells would yield longer duration times.
9. Minor illusion should act like invisibility, making the caster indifferent to non-undead mobs. We get invisibility at level four any way. The obvious disadvantage is we cannot move while it’s in effect. At the moment I see no advantage to this spell, except being a goofy party trick and self inflicted root spell.
10. It would be nice if taper enchantment had a duration, so it could be casted on a target and any undesired spells there after would be affected/tapered. This would make it into more a mini buff, as of now it useless. Most enchanters don’t have this spell memorized and when a situation arises, by the time you sit and memorize it the negative effect is gone.
11. Strength debuffs are very ineffective most against NPCs. The maximum damage needs to be lowered not just the average damage. Most enchanters I know don’t use the strength debuffs because by the time they cast a few of them the monster is dead and the ones that live long enough to debuff and not effected by them. I suggest you keep the same effects the strength debuffs have now, but add a reduction in their maximum damage by one point for every five points of strength they are drained.
12. The Lull line of spells needs to be enhanced in resist rates and duration. To cast these spells is pure suicide, as they will 80% of the time result in pulling the whole camp. We are supposed to be the masters of crowd control, but we fail miserably here. Druids just plain make us look incompetent here, I don’t want to reduce the druid effectiveness, but I want enchanters to be the best at crowd control across the board.
13. Another minor gripe it were are supposed to be the best at buffs and debuffs, but shamans’ slow line of spells are better than our languid line of spells.
14. Our quickness line of spells need to be upgraded in some way, there are so many haste items for fighting types now, that our quickness spells seem to be much less desirable or useful.
15. Another minor request is for Illusion fire elemental and feed back to stack. Right now there is a bug that has them subtract from each other, resulting in a damage shield that causes 2 points of damage per hit.
16. Cancel magic should be changed so they only take down negative effects when cast on yourself or another friendly grouped pc, but it should work normally against enemy monsters and PCs. This would help create more of a difference between cancel magic and strip enchantment series.

Thanks for reading this, sincerely Senior game designer for several platforms.

First one very important point, which baelish's comment suggests he may not be
aware of. Augment Death is a scaled haste spell. It starts at quite a low increase,
and increases in strength as you gain levels. So while it is close, but not equal to,
Swift Like the Wind at level 50, when it is first gained at level 39 it is not even
close. The speed advantage of enchanter pets is maintained through their
career, not just at level 49.

On a different note, I would like to raise one point that many of you may
not have noticed, or may have forgotten.

This is not the first time that enchanter pets have been overlooked.

Remember the first pet nerf ? Where 44 and 49 pets which were overpowered
due to a *coughcough* "data entry" error were reduced ?

Guess who got overlooked ? 8)

Magician: 66 max pet -> 60 max pet
Necromancer : 62 max pet -> 55 max pet
Enchanter : 60 max pet -> 60 max pet

So prior to the speed nerf, it was theoretically possible for an enchanter pet
to outdamage a similarly equipped necromancer pet, and it *always*
outdamaged a magician pet. After the speed nerf, the enchanter pet will
still hit for more damage than the necro pet, but this will be compensated
slightly by the necro pet's drain proc.

This is not to say that the enchanter pet does not suck - it has 1/2 the hp
of a necro pet and 1/3 the hp of a mage pet and is uncontrollable - but
it is still something to remember as I strongly suspect this plays a part
in verant's decision..

I actually fight exclusively in groups but for very few occassions like camping ancient gator. And i use besides clarity and augmentation (which sucks compared to shaman buffs but at least stacks) mostly tashani, single and ae mes and ae stuns.
