MAGICIAN RESEARCH

While being a magician is a fun-filled career with many easy methods of acquiring items by simply summoning them, there are times when our dedication to our art is tested. This test comes in the form of research. Many of the spells that we need to be the most productive in what we do need our time and energy to be made a reality.

A beginning magician needs to acquire an elemental grimoire.  These can be found at their local guilds.  In this container, one can combine the many items required to make a spell.  There are many books we can purchase from local vendors throughout Norrath in cities where Magician guilds and libraries exist that tell us the ingredients we need for each of these spells. These books, however, are written in other languages than our own. Generally, they are in the elder forms of foreign tongues.

Luckily for young magicians, we have provided lists with the components required for these spells, saving them time so they can get to work faster.

Each spell needs components that are not easily attainable through merchants. The acquisition of various old world words and needed items can be viewed as fun-filled quests. Generally, the components can be found on monsters equal to the level at which the spell is granted. Another option is to find another person who possesses the item we need and trading them something of value for it.

Once all items needed for the spell are attained, they must be put into the Elemental Grimoire and combined. Should the combination work, there will be a brand new scroll the magician can then scribe into his or her spellbook.

To raise skills, and reduce failures when combining, a Magician can repeatedly create lower-level spells as practice, making a profit in the process from other magicians to whom the spells can be sold. There are also Quests involving the concordance of research, which can help build a higher skill level to insure a better success rate with the later spells.

For many, the practice of research is fun and enjoyable; for others the idea is simply an annoyance. There are many magicians who practice research as a trade skill for profit, from whom others can purchase the spells without ever touching the skill.
