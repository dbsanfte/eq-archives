Privacy is a RIGHT.

RIGHTS cannot be taken away. You cannot be forced to sign away a RIGHT through coersion (ie by accepting the EULA).

EULA's with clauses that strip away rights will not stand up in court.

A simple analogy goes like this:

I need a plumber. There is only one plumber in my town. The plumber says I will fix your toilet but you must agree to my contract (similar to EULA) which states that:

Upon completion of my plumbing task you will let me **** you analy.

Well he is the only plumber in town, so I sign the agreement. Upon completion I decide that I don't wish to be ****ed up the butt and refuse.

The plumber can take his signed contract to court but its highly unlikely the judge will order me to bend over and take it up the arse!

A RIGHT is a RIGHT. Removal of a right through coersion is illegal. Signing a contract (EULA) does not make something illegal legal. period!

Varent is treading very dangerous waters here. They may get away with scanning processes on the system which is running the game. They may even be able to disallow the game to run if certain tasks are running.
