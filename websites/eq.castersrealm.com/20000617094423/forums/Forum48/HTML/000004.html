The main thing about PvP is that you are fighting a live, intelligent (well...) and unpredictable target, a target that actively hunts you down across zones, or the entire world for that matter. PvP is for people who don't think the greatest joy in life is killing bigger and bigger bats and rats, just to get some items to make them even better at killing... er... bats and rats. No AI-controlled bat or rat can ever provide the challenge a living, breathing human can. And most players on the Zeks believe that you aren't really playing multiplayer until you actually go head to head with other players - normal EQ is basically people playing beside each other, against the AI. PvP is truly interactive in a way multiplayer with PvP-restrictions never can be.

My 2 cents

I agree that pvp has a greater level of strategy involved, especially at the lower levels. I play on TZ and have found that it is basically dark vs light in terms of teams. As a lower lvl char. you have to learn what areas are safer and therefore less likely to be inhabited by DE's and ogres hunting for lighties. It also pays to make friends with higher lvl characters who are close by in case you get jumped by a gang of DEs. Actually the roleplaying experience is superb most of the time, unless you get two people acting like imbeciles and abusing the ooc. I've had many high level char help me in a pinch, or give me a buff as they pass by. I've also met very honorable DE who have also helped me at times. I met one lvl 30 DE magician who summoned a staff for me and buffed me before warning me that I was in an unsafe area. He told me to be safe so that we could meet as equals on the battlefield someday. How cool!
