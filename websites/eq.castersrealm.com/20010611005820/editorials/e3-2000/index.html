EVERQUEST AT E3 2000

EVERQUEST AT E3 2000
As the blur of the last 3 days wears off I am sitting down and beginning to absorb everything that went on at E3.  

Verant Interactive had two booths.  Both were located in the South Hall of the LA Convention Centre.  The main booth was as the major part of the Sony Interactive Display.  

The Everquest / Sovereign display was pretty crowded from the beginning to the end of the show and displayed very well that EQ is still well and alive.

While I was at the booth Will Wright (creator of "The Sims") actually stopped by to show William Shatner (reknowned for many things including his role in "Star Trek").  They had a pretty good look at the game and seemed to like the new Iksar's in particular.

Keith Parkinson was supposed to sign some of his lithogram posters for Ruins of Kunark.  He got stranded at the airport however due to a strike and the public seemed to be just as happy to get them signed by the development team.  The crowd was about 15 people deep until they ran out of posters. 

Other give-aways for people were pewter figurines and shirts, and many peolpe were very happy to get various EQ things autographed.

The Sony Interactive Booth Everquest on Display

The Sony Interactive Booth Poster Signing

Most of the Everquest development team were there.  John Smedley, Brad McQuaid, Bill Trost, Jeff Butler, Geoff Zatkin, Matt Yaney as well as the majority of the EQ Live Team and a number of GM's.  In all an excellent opportunity for people to meet those behind the game and ask their questions.

A large number of players (including a good number of designers for other games at E3) huddled around each other discussing the game.  It was a great time to meet people and discuss quests, spells, 
Geoff Zatkin speaks to Fans Geoff Zatkin and Bill Trost
Whilst a large number of visitors were already fans of EQ, a large number were not.  On the large plasma HDTV screens above the booth, members of the public watched (many with mouths agape) as raids went on in the City of Mists, and the Dreadlands.

They also had some of the sound track blasting out of their speakers attracting people from all around the area.

Some of the Verant staff were showing off the GM clients displaying how they can keep track of what players are doing.

They also showed how they can run quests by taking the form of other monsters within zones.

The team mainly showed off the new Kunark zones, explaining the changes made in the expansion such as moving trees, flowing water, high poly counts on monsters, and more.

The game was shown on PIII 733 machines with 256 Meg RAM, and 19 inch Flat Screen displays.  The internet connection to the machines was a bit up and down with the embrassing "You have been disconnected" appearing as the convention centres routers flapped, but overall most people at the booth were able to see real EQ on real servers in action.

Mmmmm Plasma Displays A GM controlled Skeleton

Two Verant Staff at Play More Plasma Screen Observation

Also on show were the new Everquest collector figurines which will be available for purchase in stores in coming months. I believe they will also be available to purchase through the EQ Online Store.  The detail on these little figurines is quite extensive, and add-on packs with armor and weapons will be released over time.

Right now there are three completed.  I saw some design sculptures for the Froglok figurine which looked pretty good too.

I also got a chance to meet Rick Schmitz and Richard Johnson (the two artists behind most of the creatures in EQ).  We were all pretty impressed with the figurines, especially the troll.

In all, meeting the dev team was great fun, and it was good to see that a game which has been out for over a year was attracting just as much attention as the new releases being launched at E3.

E3 Report by Baelish, May 14 2000.
