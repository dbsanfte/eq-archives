I dont understand the problem with the change to NPC speed based on weapons. Yes, it makes pets hit slower with a dagger, but it also makes monsters hit slower with a dagger. Yes it lowers the amount of damage that a pet can do over the same amount of time, but if you think about it in terms of damage of a weapon, why in the world would someone wielding a dagger hit for as much as someone wielding a two handed sword, but faster? That makes no sense whatsoever, not that this game is entirely based on logic, but its still one way of looking at the change.
I, for one, love the new change, I play a Level 50 Cleric on the Tribunal, and one of my roommates is a level 50 enchanter. Believe it or not, he likes the weapon change as well. Enchanters are NOT dependent on their pets in any way in a group, and solo the strength of an unbuffed pet is pitiful anyway, so the amount of damage they do is entirely based upon the buffs you give them. I've watched him solo monsters before comparing his pet with daggers to his pet normally, and it takes him approximately 10% more mana to kill a monster with the new speed change, if the monster has no weapon. If the monster has a dagger, he actually saves mana with the new system.
It seems to me that people are forgetting the main purpose of an enchanter, crowd control. Pets just get in the way of crowd control, I cant count the amount of times that he used to use his pet while learning to play the class and have a mesmerize resisted only to get the pet on the enemy thus making it impossible to mes until a) the pet died or b) the monster died. The pet was more trouble than help, the best enchanter pets are charmed pets because they can be controlled. I hate taking the unsympathetic point of view, but this change seems to have a relatively small impact on the enchanter class and I do not see what all the fuss is about.
