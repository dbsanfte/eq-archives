I played a High Elf enchanter on Bristlebane for four months (time played was 37 days). Wasn't fun. Switched to a cleric in late November. Like a whole 'nother game. Since then, I've discouraged people from starting enchanters, and I'd like to share with you why. I keep some very good records when I play, so I've got some supporting numbers to my comments (contained below).

To me, the over-arching problem with the enchanter class is that they are not viable in groups until Clarity at 29. From levels 12 through 21, I wandered zones like a nomad in search of a group for weeks at a time. I'd often get on at 9:00 p.m.and wander zones for an hour or two sending direct tells, doing /ooc statements and shouts looking for a group. Most nights I never got a response. Actually, almost every night I never got a response. Eventually, I'd get trained and die or try to solo a high blue (simply because I found something that I might be able to kill for experience) and die. Bristlebane is an old server and a popular one. There are many tanks with crafted armor at level 5. They don't need an enchanter until it's time for Guk. There are a ton of Necros on Bristlebane. Most get groups whenever they want. Crowd control is just not an issue when the server has three times the number of players on them compared to what they were designed for. Debuffs? The MoB doesn't live long enough for debuffs or weak enchanter DoTs to matter, and everybody knows this. I remember one guy shouting back, after my fifth shout for a group in one hour in South Karana; he said, "You'd get a group if Enchanters helped create more MoB crowds and not just control them." Of course he was joking, but the point is that when there are bitter kill stealing fights all over, what is the point of an enchanter until the higher levels?

Now, I've got a lot of numbers here on my resistance rates. I'm going to spare you the details and simply give you the totals: From level 14 through level 21, I was resisted 42% of the time. Imagine how much mana that is. Imagine how many taunts that is. Imagine how many times I died from that. My intelligence was high. My charisma was high. I used Tashan and then the 20th level version when I got it. I rarely casted on yellows or higher. But the resistance made every spell cast a gamble. Of course, as part of that need to keep my intelligence and charisma high, I sacraficed AC, like any caster would. But the enchanter's hit points are low. The stamina is low. A few blows, and I'm dead. What's going to help me, that weak pet?

When you look at an enchanter's spell list, it is impressive, but much of the spells are useless. As for the decent ones, well, when I've got to stack Tashan, Disempower, Languid Pace, Enfeeblement, a DoT and maybe something in the color line so that my pet with Quickness, Strengthen, Mist, and Rune One on him can maybe take down a low blue Orc, with my tanking for the pet as well with the same buffs on me, is this really viable? I med for three minutes to get my mana up to do another Orc or Aviak and just hope to goodness that Languid Pace or Choke isn't resisted. And the duration on my buffs got shorter twice as I played. Again, I have numbers on this, but I won't bore you with the details. I play with a stop watch, I know they did. That's even more mana gone.

I'm not questioning the ability of enchanters once they hit level 29. They eventually become a rival to a Necro as the two classes rise in levels. What I'm saying is that the class needs a little help between levels 9 and 28. Here are my suggetsions:

1. Enchanters need a really nice spell to get them into groups earlier than level 29. Quickness at 16 is OK, but the duration is very short, and most tanks can heal up their lost hit points between respawns anyway. Group mez is nice at 20, but until you're in Guk or something like that, you're not seeing huge pulls. You're seeing groups take turns on ones and twos. The 24th level spells are a bit weak in my opinion. What enchanters really need is a well-known spell that gives a good benefit, and they should get it at level 12. I think it should be a weak form of Clarity. Something that is perhaps one-third as strong as Clarity but strong enough to make enchanters desired before 29.

2. Enchanter buffs should have their duration times returned to what they were. If not for levels 29 or higher, at least for levels 1 through 28. Enchanters have so many debuffs, so many utility spells, so many crowd control spells, so many buffs, that it really is a problem when buffs are so short, to say nothing of Whirl and even our regular Root being so short.

3. To help enchanters get experience if they're on a server like Bristlebane and to help leverage the value of those illusion spells, there should be more quests suitable for levels 8 through 28. Whether these quests give great items or not isn't the point. They should give decent experience.

I guess what I'm saying is that there are two enchanter classes. One enchanter class is the 29 and higher class. The other enchanter class is the 1 through 28 class. I think that Verant has considered the 29 and higher version of enchanters to be a bit too strong. In reducing them slightly over the past six months, they reduced the much weaker 1 through 28 level enchanters as well. The magician class was, for all practical purposes, incomplete when the game started. I've seen a lot of good work done to help boost it up and make it more viable -- even though they still have that incredibly tough research burden. I'd like to see that same kind of careful, well-thought out work done to help enchanters in their teens and in their 20s.

However, I don't think that I'll ever go back to my enchanter. The problems and frustrations that I had with the class were serious. I died 104 times in getting to level 21. At that point, I looked at the level 24 spells, saw that they weren't really that good, and I quit him. I just could not go on. I could not find a group. I could not solo. With eight levels to go before Clarity, I simply gave up. I started a Halfling Rogue and did not twink him. I got him to level 10 in less than 2 days time played. He died once over those 10 levels. I sold all of my enchanter's stuff (good stuff, too). I bought a bunch of cleric stuff. Now I only play a Dark Elf Cleric (on Bristlebane). He's 18th level and he has died seven times. I've got just over 4 days time played on him. He's twinked, yes, but he's a survivor. The defensive ability of the cleric is incredible, and a heal is never resisted. You can see, now, why I think the enchanter class has some serious problems through level 28. This latest reduction in the pet's ability is merely the latest in a series of problems for the class, in my view.

Thanks for your attention.
Dacian, Bristlebane, Retired
Antonescu, Britlebane, Healing Group Members since 1/1/00.
