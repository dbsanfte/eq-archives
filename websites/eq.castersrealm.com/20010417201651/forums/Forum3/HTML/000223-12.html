I want to talk about using pets and in particular about soloing.

No, I'm not particularyly ticked off that our pets don't attack faster with daggers. Frankly, I always thought that was something of an explioit (ie, a bug that would eventually get fixed), and although I did take advantage of it a few times, it was by no means a regular occurence.

What I am ticked off about is all these people who are saying "You stupid enchanter, learn how to play your class, you shouldn't even be using your pets, and you shouldn't be soloing."

These people have really lit a fire under me tonight. They A) Have never played an enchanter for any significant amount of time, B) play an enchanter but get powerleveled and twinked by their friends, or C) have played an untwinked, unpowerleveled enchanter, and the reason they don't see the same problems I do is that the stupid random number generator has been giving them all my good rolls and me all their bad.

On soloing: I hate to solo. I absolutely despise it. I think soloing does nothing but bring out the worst in the game. But you know what? Probably 3/4 or more of my entire character's life has been soloing, and probably 90% of all of my experience gain has come from soloing. Why is that, Myrrn? Why do you solo so much if you hate it? Answer? Because 90% of the time I don't have any choice! I spend hours and hours at a time sometimes asking, no, begging, for a group to take me. Generally, the times when groups really want an enchanter are in very high mob count situations, where an enchanter is a requirement. But guess what? These situations are the exact ones that have made most of my experience gain come during solo times. Because these situations require near-perfection out of everybody in the group, and frankly, there just aren't that many groups that are that good around. Yes, I've been in a couple, but very few, so either there just aren't that many, or they all already have their pet enchanters, and want nothing to do with me.

On pets: Guess what, if I'm soloing, I need a pet, and I need a good one. Once upon a time, I could solo yellows and even a rare red with a pet, but now I'm starting to have trouble with blues, and every once in a while a green will get the best of me, even with a fully buffed pet. Don't give me that bs about soloing with charm. Soloing with charm not only requires practically perfect circumstances (2 mobs of near the same level at the same place at the same time), but it is nearly a death wish as well. Yes, I love charm, and when I can find groups, I make quite frequent use of it. But quit trying to tell me that it's the silver bullet that makes soloing easy, because all that tells me is that you either don't know what you're talking about or you're the one that's been stealing all my good random number streaks.

On a closing note, I saw somebody say "welcome to the same game melee classes have been playing all along", and I just had to laugh.

Because, you see, just last night, while I was in my second hour of asking for a group and attempting to do a bit of soloing (which basically consisted of fighting near the zone and zoning when my pet died and I was out of mana, I don't think I killed even one single mob in the entire hour), I watched a warrior (same level as me) solo things that made mincemeat of me, even with a pet. And we're not talking soloing just one and then resting for 20 minutes, we're talking taking 3 or 4 in a row before having to rest. So don't give me that bs either, because I'm not hearing it.

Do I love playing an enchanter? Absolutely. Because when things go well, they go very well. I just wish "when things go well" was a little more often. Have I cancelled my account once already for almost 3 months, and am I on the verge of cancelling it again, because of the utter frustrations of "when things don't go so well"? Absolutely.

Although not an enchanter myself, I have known and hunted with a number of enchanters, and got to know their tactics and abilities well.
I agree with Abashi's position that the enchanter is not a pet-class. However, I have to say that the dependence of the enchanters to their pets is far greater than that of the shadow knight, or the shaman.
Shadow knights are primarily a melee class. They are self-sufficient with a sword and some armour, and can survive whether they cast spells or not (besides, they don't get spells till lvl 9).
Shamans do not depend on their pet--ever--. They don't even get it till lvl 34, so it stands to reason that they 'must' have some other abilities to help them advance till that point.
Enchanters on the other hand are weak, in both melee, and magic combat. Certainly they can charm, blind, stun...So? Once those spells are done, the enchanter will have to run, or die, as she's unable to do the damage needed to finish a kill. The few damaging spells they get are mana-hungry, and slow to cast. That's where their pet gets in, to keep them from harm while they cast.
Sure, an enchanter is in his best element in a group--I know I love having one in my group--but does that mean they have to be helpless without one?
Abashi says that Enchanters were not compensated because they could make their pets faster...
Abashi, with all due respect, I think you're a little mixed up here...
On one hand you talk about how each class has to be good at what they specialize in... So necros get pets, strong pets, with spells...They get the ability to heal those pets, and to buff them as well. Because that's what 'they specialize in'.
Well, enchanters specialize in buffing and crowd control. They specialize in making things faster (or should we take Alacrity out of the game too, because it makes monks too fast?). Their pets cannot be commanded like Necro pets can. They cannot be healed by the enchanter. They have half the hit points and the armour class of other classes' pets. All they get--Wow!--are the (mana-costly, and of short duration) speed buffs that the enchanter can give. And not like those spells are specifically 'pet-spells'. They are the same spells the enchanter will cast on a Necro's pet, or on the monk or warrior that is tanking.
'That' is the enchanter's specialty. Why should they be nerfed because of it?
Let the enchanters be a viable class.
You want to take away their pets? Then give some compensation for it. Make the charm spells harder to resist, or harder to break. Give them fixed duration, rather than random. Give the enchanter some way to do damage, in an effective way.
That's my point of view.

The Enchanter at least has the chance to. Anyway, at 12 we also get Choke, Languid Pace, Ebbing Strength, and Whirl, all of which I have used and enjoyed. Between Tashan-Color Flux-Whirl-Color Flux I have had a monster not attack at all. I have on occasion had Whirl last the whole rest of the fight. How is this spell broken? I haven't even noticed particularly bad resists on it. Languid Pace seems to resist far more. Somehow I have managed to make it up to 16, nearly 17, without taking advantage of the dagger trick, so it must be possible. And as for nobody wanting an enchanter in a group before Clarity, this hasn't been the case for me. I generally have no trouble picking up a group when I want one, and I have cultivated several friends I play with regularly from the groups I have been in. I have never met a group that turned me down because I was an enchanter. The only time I had difficulty gaining a group was between 10 and 12. Should the game not be frustrating? Not in my opinion. I don't feel Enchanters are broken in any way. There are a few things that would be really great if they were done, several of which have been mentioned in this thread. But I see nothing that makes the Enchanter class "unplayable".
