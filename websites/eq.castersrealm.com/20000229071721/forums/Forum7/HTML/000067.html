If NPC wizards are improved with this patch then you're right. From Verant's history, it is hard to say if this effect will migrate to the NPCs. NPC warriors didn’t get critical hits when warriors were given the ability, for example.

However, please bear in mind the advantages of having an innately lower resist rate on high-level wizard spells.

First, the tashan/malise line takes both time and mana to cast. Further, the malise line is frequently resisted and so requires multiple casts. Many shamans will work their way up the malise line on tough mobs just so they can have a hope of getting malosi to get through.

This is one of the reasons why the SoTF is a decent item. The reduction isn't great but it costs us absolutely nothing to get the reduction. The recast time is zero so you can cast is repeatedly until it sticks (I've been told it will never stick on the highest mobs, however). You'd be a fool not to have and use the staff simply because of the benefits outside of plane/dragon raids. Every little bit helps and this little bit is *free*.

So, getting some inherent fraction of the benefit of these spells (undoubtedly a small fraction given Verant's position on wizards as essentially balanced) does have the advantage that we don't have to waste the mana or time casting the debuffs. Finally, bear in mind that this naturally lower resist rate will almost certainly "stack" with the other debuff lines. If we had malise, we that would be the extent of it. Now malise will continue help us as well.

This is a meaningful advantage *depending* upon the actual magnitude of the change in our spell resist rate. I just fear that it will be an imperceptible farce.

Ehle, lvl 38 Wiz (Xegony)
