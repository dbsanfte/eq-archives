INTRODUCTION

The Magician epic quest reward is a level 46 spell, Summon Orb. Once you receive the reward, you scribe the spell, which you then may use to SUMMON your epic weapon, the Orb of Mastery. The nice side effect of this is that when you die, you may SUMMON ANOTHER EPIC WEAPON BEFORE YOU RETRIEVE YOUR CORPSE! Plus, it doesn't require any reagents like malachite to summon, so you essentially get a free pet for 500 mana (not to mention a kickass primary slot weapon).

To get the Orb of Mastery (or really, the Summon Orb spell), you turn in the four Elements to the Master of Elements in the Plane of Sky. Each of the four Elements (Wind, Water, Earth, Fire) is itself a sub-quest, where you must turn in three or four items to an NPC, who will reward you with the corresponding element. (Earth and Fire each require four items; Air and Water each require three items.) All but three of the 14 items are simple drops from mobs; some high-level and rare, some lower-level and common. Two of the 14 items are your lower-level focus items from the Temple of Solusek Ro. The last item, the Power of the Orb, is an item for the Element of Fire sub-quest. The Power of the Orb itself requires 3 sub-items to be traded in; each of those 3 sub-items needs either 3 or 4 items. A tree diagram looks like this:

Do not confuse the Power of Water, Power of Earth, etc. with the Element of Water, Element of Earth, etc. The "Power of" items are for the "Power of the Elements" sub-sub-quest of the Power of the Orb sub-quest, and the "Element of" items are the four items you turn in to the Master of Elements to GET YOUR EPIC QUEST REWARD.

A WARNING: DO NOT COMPLETE THE ELEMENT OF WIND QUEST UNTIL LAST OR YOU WILL POP THE MASTER OF THE ELEMENTS AND HE WILL DEPOP BEFORE YOU GET THE OTHER ELEMENTS DONE. This means that you should have completed and acquired the Element of Fire, Element of Earth, and Element of Water, BEFORE you go to the Plane of Sky and turn in your items for the Element of Wind.

A note: Some of the other epic quests (Cleric and Monk, for example) require you to do things in a certain order. You must get item A, give it to mob B, which will cause mob C to spawn, who you kill for item D, and so on. The magician epic has items that can all be acquired in ANY ORDER. The only time that "order" matters is, as noted above, when you turn in the Element of Wind items, because this causes the Master of Elements to spawn. (There are one or two instances with the Power of the Orb sub-sub-quest where you are "supposed" to talk to someone who gives you an item to give to someone else, but, these SHOULD BE optional. Do them anyway! It's epic, man!)

Also, in the cases of items from the Plane of Hate and Plane of Sky, you need to be level 46 to even be teleported into those zones. For your own safety, we recommend that you are at least level 47, and preferably 49, before you enter the planes.

Some terminology:
always drop - This refers to items that will always drop when you kill the mob. There are possibly some cases where the drop rate might be high but not 100%, but when I mark something as "always drop", it means that every time I've killed the mob, it's dropped the item. More to the point, there are no recorded instances of the mob NOT dropping the item. This doesn't mean that it won't happen, just that no one's yet seen it happen.
common drop - At least 20% of the time. While one in five may not seem all that common (some "common drops" are 100% of the time!), since most of the mobs here are rapid spawns, you'll end up with the item in short order assuming you camp the mob for a little while.
semi-rare drop - 10-20% of the time.
rare drop - 5-10% of the time.
very rare drop - less than 5% of the time.

Keep in mind that these are GUIDELINES; since we don't have enough data to be positive about them, the terms are assigned to tbe best of our ability.

* Burning Embers (Burning Wood: "Nezekezena", "Gylton", "Phenoz", rare)


Picture courtesy of Varia Darselai of The Tribunal

The Burning Embers are a confirmed drop off Nezekezena, Gylton, and now (thanks to Orko Soulblighter) Phenoz, another named wurm in Burning Wood.

Reports indicate that if you go to Nezekezena's spawn location (about (-450, -2550) in Burning Wood) and kill everything in sight, eventually Nez or his (her?) placeholders will start spawning with a cycle time of 90 seconds. People have evidenced acquiring Burning Embers at a rate of 1 or 2 in a few hours' time with this method. Phurzikan and Nezekezna both spawn on this spot, and they are both strong; sometimes Nez or Phurz will spawn two times in a row, so your group had better be strong enough to take them.

Nezekezena, if not killed when she spawns, will wander sometimes along the east and south walls of the zone, so if you can't find her near her spawn, look there. A tracker would definitely help.

Thanks to Kelston Bernstar for contributing info about Nezekezena and Phurzikan (Phurz does not drop the Burning Embers so he is not listed here).

 When you're in your 20s, Najena is a good place to go for loot and exp. It's a "doable" dungeon, meaning a single group of mid-to-high 20s (or even low 30s) can crawl through the dungeon, clearing it out room by room. A single crawl, even if you kill every mob in the dungeon, is not likely to net you all four pages; I spent a good 5-6 hours there both at levels 40 and 46, killing literally every mob in the dungeon, before I gathered all four words. Keep at it, and you'll get it. Note that the words can drop off both the goblin magicians found in the northern caverns, as well as the Dark Elf magicians, and even a few of the named mobs in the zone. I personally looted the Wind words off of Rathyl.

If you're higher level like I am (45+), it's relatively trivial to solo literally the entire zone. Just run about killing everything you see, and eventually you'll get all the words. It's not really worth it to bother with Najena's room, because you have to resummon a pet to get in there (pets will not be able to leave the pool that leads to Najena's room). At any rate, the spawn time in this zone is around 18 minutes, and it will take about that long to hit all of the other spawns you need to hit. These are:

1. BoneCracker and the necromancers in his room.
2. Trazdon and the magician[s] in his room. (I don't know whether Trazdon is capable of dropping the words, because I only killed him once and he didn't drop them that time, so it's hard to tell.)
3. The magician at the front gate of the castle area (where the entry caverns end).
4. The magician in the room behind her.
5. The two magicians in the Crystal Room.
6. The two necromancers and Drelzna (or her PH, another Necro) in Drelzna's room.
7. The three magicians in the antechamber outside Rathyl's room.
8. Rathyl or his placeholder (a magician) in Rathyl's room.
9. The three goblin camps in the north caves. One is in a cul-de-sac to the west; the other two are in rooms to the east and north.

If you hit these spots in this order, you will sooner or later get all the words. Be wary of other people camping the dungeon for exp and loot; don't run over their camps. But do shout to the zone that you're looking for the Words, and if people could please not loot them and tell you, that would be nice. A friendly group of low-20's adventurers were camping Trazdon's room when one of the magicians there dropped the Water words (the last one I needed); I gave one of them all the platinum I had earned in the 2 hours I spent trying to get the Water words, and she was incredibly grateful and excited (at my level, 46, the 12pp was nothing; if I'd had 50pp I would have given all that to her).

You will need some keys to get around the dungeon. The best order for this, is thus: Go to BoneCracker's room and kill him. He will drop the "Dull Bone Key" (DBK). Then go to the nearby Ogre Captain, and kill him, for he drops the "Shiny Metal Key" (SMK). The SMK opens the cell outside Rathyl's room; the DBK opens Rathyl's room itself. Kill Rathyl or his placeholders until Rathyl drops the "Bloodstained Key" (BSK) (this key is NORENT so if you go linkdead or camp, you will need to get it again). The BSK opens the door to Drelzna's room. Drelzna drops the "Golden Crescent Key" (GCK), but this only leads to Najena's room, and you don't really need to go there, so just stick the GCK in a bag and forget about it unless you really feel like killing Najena. The GCK is also NORENT. (The DBK and SMK are NOT NORENT, you can hang onto them forever, and probably should if you have to camp this place more than once.)

Because there are so many mobs that can drop these words, we will not list them all here. Suffice to say that few, if any, of them have more than 600 hit points or hit for more than 50 damage max. The magicians and necromancers will all have pets and will cast their appropriate class spells, but otherwise are trivial to kill at high level. They can be difficult at lower levels, however (fighting 3 necros solo at 39 can get you killed if you aren't careful).

The named mobs are, as expected, somewhat more difficult, but not much.

A quick summary of which pages dropped of what mobs in Najena, although I don't know whether there's any correlation:
  • Water dropped off a magician in Trazdon's room.
  • Wind dropped off Rathyl.

  • Fire dropped off a goblin magician in the north caves. I believe I also saw fire drop off one of the magicians, but I saw it drop off the goblins at least 3 times.

The alligators have historically had the same loot table as the dark elf magicians in Najena, so this is understandable. Whether there are any other mobs in Cazic Thule that have the words is unknown, but unlikely (your best bet for the words is still Najena).
Kelston Bernstar tells me that he has never heard of the pages dropping off Trazdon or "a necromancer" (this seems like it would include Najena herself, as she is a necro), but I still keep them in the list on the off chance that they have the pages in their loot table as a rare drop.

& Tears of Erollisi (Overthere: "Captain Rottgrime", always drop)



I joined up with a group of people from various guilds who had banded together to get the good Captain dead, dead, dead, dead. In fact, they got him four times, and the fourth time, the Tears of Erollisi went to me. Killing the Captain isn't hard, you just need lots of tanks and clerics. Casters who have pets can help, but I don't think any other spells will be useful against him.

Pulling him from the outpost in the Overthere is difficult; the two times I was present, a dragoon was pulled along with him. He is usually standing near the southeast corner of the Outpost, along with six undead marines, who are level 50. You'll probably want a monk to feign pull the Captain, and it will be much easier to do at night when the dragoons are out on patrol (fewer things to aggro). I don't know the exact method that was used however.

You will take a Venril Sathir faction hit for killing Rottgrime; apparently it's a pretty sizable one, so be careful if you're used to being able to travel there (evil races and classes, for example).

* Pegasus Feather Cloak (South Karana: Quillmane, very rare drop)


Picture courtesy of The Mage Compendium

Quillmane is a rare spawn, but he can spawn anywhere in the zone. Most commonly he is found in the interior of the zone, away from the zone walls, but he can be out there, too. He wanders almost constantly, and is killed almost instantly by any who see him (because the cloak you need for the quest is All/All and has unlimited charges of Levitate...). This sucks, but it means it takes some real effort to get the cloak.

Because Quillmane spawns randomly and wanders, the best way to get the cloak is to go to South Karanas with as many high level rangers and druids as you can gather. More than 5 would be overkill, since you want to be grouped with them all. Basically, what you should do is summon your pet of choice, get a SoW, and then run around the zone killing anything that wanders. Static spawns like the Aviaks at their tower or the Tesch Mas Gnolls outside splitpaw, need not be killed (Quillmane doesn't spawn there, and there will always be people camping the Aviaks and Gnolls anyway). Anything else that wanders -- centaurs, wolves, lions, elephants, aviaks, gnolls, treants (although there is a faction issue there) -- is fair game. You want five trackers with you because they can always home in on a mob and kill it, and they can do this constantly -- the instant something shows up on tracking, they will go after it and kill it. And the reason you want to be grouped is, if someone in your group does see Quillmane and kills him, then you will be able to get to the corpse and loot it before it opens up and some ninjalooter steals your cloak!
Even one high-level tracker with you can make this an easy enough camp. (To put it another way, one tracker tracking and killing mobs, and Quillmane when he pops, is better than wandering about hoping to encounter Quillmane by yourself!) If you have even one or two ranger or druid friends who can help, then your life will become immeasurably easier.

Nevertheless, even with your entire guild scouring the zone for Quillmane, expect to spend at least several hours on getting the cloak. If you're doing it solo, then may Mithaniel Marr protect your soul...
