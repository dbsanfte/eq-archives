NOTES

The Planes

This isn't self-evident since some people simply don't know about it. The Planes of Sky and Hate are not connected to any other zone through normal means; you cannot zone into them the same way you zone from Freeport to East Commonlands. There is only one way to get to the Planes: the level 46 wizard spells "Alter Plane: Hate" and "Alter Plane: Sky". These spells teleport the wizard and his group to the port-in house in the Plane of Hate, or the first island in the Plane of Sky. Note that it uses up a reagant (a "Fulligrans Soulstone of Innoruuk" for Hate, and a "Cloudy Stone of Veeshan" for Sky) which each cost 125pp.

Other Notes

Some of the other epic quests (Cleric and Monk, for example) require you to do things in a certain order. You must get item A, give it to mob B, which will cause mob C to spawn, who you kill for item D, and so on. The magician epic has items that can all be acquired in ANY ORDER. The only time that "order" matters is, as noted above, when you turn in the Element of Wind items, because this causes the Master of Elements to spawn. (There are one or two instances with the Power of the Orb sub-sub-quest where you are "supposed" to talk to someone who gives you an item to give to someone else, but, these SHOULD BE optional. Do them anyway! It's epic, man!)

Also, in the cases of items from the Plane of Hate and Plane of Sky, you need to be level 46 to even be teleported into those zones. For your own safety, we recommend that you are at least level 47, and preferably 49 (as our level 49 pets are MUCH more powerful than our level 44 pets), before you enter the planes.

As a result of this, it is impossible to complete the quest before level 46 (and since the result is a level 46 spell, even if you somehow managed to get it before you were level 46, you could not scribe the spell or summon the Orb anyway). You can talk to most (if not all) of the quest NPCs, and turn in any items you DO acquire for the appropriate reward, but since you have to be 46 to use the spell (and you have to be 46 to go to Hate and Sky to get their corresponding items), there is little point in worrying about it until you are level 46.

Some Terminology

  • always drop - This refers to items that will always drop when you kill the mob. There are possibly some cases where the drop rate might be high but not 100%, but when I mark something as "always drop", it means that every time I've killed the mob, it's dropped the item. More to the point, there are no recorded instances of the mob NOT dropping the item. This doesn't mean that it won't happen, just that no one's yet seen it happen.
  • common drop - At least 20% of the time. While one in five may not seem all that common, since most of the mobs here are rapid spawns, you'll end up with the item in short order assuming you camp the mob for a little while.
  • semi-rare drop - 10-20% of the time.
  • rare drop - 5-10% of the time.
  • very rare drop - less than 5% of the time.
Keep in mind that these are GUIDELINES; since we don't have enough data to be positive about them, the terms are assigned to tbe best of our ability.

Spawn Times

All spawn times are in REAL TIME, NOT GAME TIME. E.g., a spawn time of "1 day" means 1 regular real life Earth day, not one day in Norrath (which is 72 minutes in real life).

To get the Orb of Mastery (or really, the Summon Orb spell), you turn in the four Elements to the Master of Elements in the Plane of Sky. Each of the four Elements (Wind, Water, Earth, Fire) is itself a sub-quest, where you must turn in three or four items to an NPC, who will reward you with the corresponding element. (Earth and Fire each require four items; Wind and Water each require three items, for 14 items total.) All but three of the 14 items are simple drops from mobs; some high-level and rare, some lower-level and common. Two of the 14 items are your lower-level focus items from the Temple of Solusek Ro. The last item, the Power of the Orb, is an item for the Element of Fire sub-quest. When you're in your 20s, Najena is a good place to go for loot and exp. It's a "doable" dungeon, meaning a single group of mid-to-high 20s (or even low 30s) can crawl through the dungeon, clearing it out room by room. A single crawl, even if you kill every mob in the dungeon, is not likely to net you all four pages; I spent a good 5-6 hours there both at levels 40 and 46, killing literally every mob in the dungeon, before I gathered all four Pages. Keep at it, and you'll get it. Note that the Pages can drop off both the goblin magicians found in the northern caverns, as well as the Dark Elf magicians, and even a few of the named mobs in the zone. I personally looted the Wind page off of Rathyl. (Ualdail of Fennin Ro confirms that he looted the Water page off of Rathyl incarnate, who can spawn right after you kill Rathyl.)

If you're higher level like I am (45+), it's relatively trivial to solo literally the entire zone. Just run about killing everything you see, and eventually you'll get all the Pages. You can even go to Najena's room, since the new spell Summon Companion allows us to pull our pet out of the pool near Najena's room once we're done killing her and her friends.

The spawn time in this zone is around 18 minutes, and it will take about that long to hit all of the other spawns you need to hit. These are:

  1. BoneCracker and the necromancers in his room (you only really need to hit this once, for the key that BoneCracker drops), plus the Ogre Captain (again, only once, for the key he drops).
  2. Trazdon and the magician[s] in his room. (I don't know whether Trazdon is capable of dropping the Pages, because I only killed him once and he didn't drop them that time, so it's hard to tell. But the magicians certainly do drop the pages, so it's worth a trip.)
  3. The magician at the front gate of the castle area (where the entry caverns end).
  4. The magician in the room behind her.
  5. The two magicians in the Crystal Room.
  6. The two necromancers and Drelzna (or her PH, another Necro) in Drelzna's room. (As necromancers do not drop the pages, this is not strictly a necessary trip, though Drelzna does drop the key to Najena's room. Fun Fact: Did you know that Drelzna used to drop Journeyman's Boots as her rare drop?)
  7. The three magicians in the antechamber outside Rathyl's room.
  8. Rathyl or his placeholder (a magician or various elementals) in Rathyl's room (and Rathyl reincarnate, if it spawns).
  9. The three goblin camps in the north caves. One is in a cul-de-sac to the west; the other two are in rooms to the east and north.
If you hit these spots in this order, you will sooner or later get all the Pages. Be wary of other people camping the dungeon for exp and loot; don't run over their camps. But do shout to the zone that you're looking for the Pages, and if people could please not loot them and tell you, that would be nice. A friendly group of low-20's adventurers were camping Trazdon's room when one of the magicians there dropped the Water Page (the last one I needed); I gave one of them the 12 platinum I had earned in the 2 hours I spent trying to get the Water Page, and she was ecstatically thankful.

You will need some keys to get around the dungeon. The best order for this, is thus: Go to BoneCracker's room and kill him. He will drop the "Dull Bone Key" (DBK). Then go to the nearby Ogre Captain, and kill him, for he drops the "Shiny Metal Key" (SMK). The SMK opens the cell outside Rathyl's room; the DBK opens Rathyl's room itself. Kill Rathyl or his placeholders until Rathyl drops the "Bloodstained Key" (BSK) (this key is NORENT so if you go linkdead or camp, you will need to get it again). The BSK opens the door to Drelzna's room. Drelzna drops the "Golden Crescent Key" (GCK), but this only leads to Najena's room, and you don't really need to go there, so just stick the GCK in a bag and forget about it unless you really feel like killing Najena. The GCK is also NORENT. (The DBK and SMK are NOT NORENT, you can hang onto them forever, and probably should if you have to camp this place more than once.)

Virtually nothing in this dungeon has more than 600 hit points or hits for more than 50 damage (except the Widowmistress, a high-30s dark elven rogue who is *incredibly* fast and *incredibly* dangerous, and spawns in the northwest corner of the dungeon, beyond all the goblin camps). The magicians and necromancers will all have pets and will cast their appropriate class spells, but otherwise are trivial to kill at high level. They can be difficult at lower levels, however (fighting 3 necros solo at 39 can get you killed if you aren't careful). The named mobs are, as expected, somewhat more difficult, but not much.

A quick summary of which pages dropped of what mobs in Najena, although I don't know whether there's any relationship between them:
  • Water dropped off a magician in Trazdon's room.
  • Wind dropped off Rathyl.
