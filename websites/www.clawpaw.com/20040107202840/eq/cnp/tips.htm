Tips in Role-playing

 
  • Instead of role-playing hitting someone, instead role-play the attempt to allow someone to counter the action.
  • Instead of *laugh* as a emote, try : or /emote laughing madly. Elaborate so that it aids the person role-playing with you in emoting back. (Example: John laughs.  vs. John laughs as Joann gets hit with spear in the bottom. "Lass, I think that gnoll likes more of you than you're willing to endure! Should I leave you two alone?")
  • Don't ignore the poses of others. If you pose tossing a cold fish (while on a fishing trip in Cobalt Scar), to Jack, you wouldn't like Jack to smack you in the head and not react to your pose of the tossing fish. So be kind and allow others the same courtesy.
  • Realize that just because a person role-plays being a jerk or stuck up (like a High Elf might), doesn't mean anything other than THAT IS HOW THEIR CHARACTER IS. In real life it's a totally different person. Realize this going in and coming out every day. You're dealing with characters run by people, if something a character is to much to deal with, try oocly speaking with the player and see what can be worked out. You can't always get out of what you've done to alter things but if advances or personal issues cloud actions, then we need to talk things out and openly.
  • When a loot is set up, we may set up the /random rolling for the loot but loot always goes to the person who can use it on the scene/group. If Jack and Mel both can use the shaman item, then only they will roll and the rest of us can role-play whining all we like and they can roll, winner can role-play snatching it or whatever they like to claim it, try to stay ic unless the ooc message system is being used.
  • If you're role-playing and others around you get rude and say to take to tells, ignore them. They're all talking ooc and spamming casino's and we don't complain, let them. We are Claws N Paws and we have standards and we have fun. 
  • If you're role-playing of a ... sexy nature between 2 people, please keep those out of reach of others. We don't want to know how you stick your tongue down xx's throat. We don't care if you do it, whatever floats your boat, but the rest of the world doesn't have to know. Kissing and hugging and snuggling and innocent poses are fine, it's the intimate ones that we don't wish shared with others. Keep that private! :)
  • Address your emotes. Say 4 people in a group. John, Sally, Bob and Moe. Saying 'John tickles you like mad' makes Sally, Bob and Moe wonder who you're tickling! 'John tries to tickle Sally without mercy! His fingers working feverishly to try to get through that thick leather armor of hers to get to her skin!' That emote helps everyone understand WHO is doing WHAT to WHOM.
