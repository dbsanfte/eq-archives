Project Entropia Upgrades to NetImmerse 4.0
Integration of the latest version of NDL's engine nears completion

Gothenburg, Sweden - May 30, 2001 - MindArk AB confirmed today that
it is in the process of upgrading Project Entropia, its innovative
science fiction-based persistent state online world, by implementing
the NetImmerse 4.0 engine, the newest version of the 3D development
toolkit from NDL.

“It's never a simple process to make major technological changes in
mid-project,” stated Marketing Manager Patric Sundstrom. “However,
our technology group is completely confident that providing support
for bump-mapped reflection mapping, hardware character skinning,
environment skinning and other features will allow for a definite
step forward in terms of Project Entropia's graphical presentation.
We expect implementation of NetImmerse 4.0 to be completed fairly
soon, and we plan to release a new generation of screenshots soon
after to show the difference.”

Project Entropia is a revolutionary new form of Internet-based
interactive entertainment that will be made available at no initial
cost and with no subscription fees to millions of Internet users
around the world. In the roles of human colonists on the virtual
planet Calypso, they will be able to meet and socialize or adventure
into wilderness areas where they will face challenges from wild
indigenous creatures and invading robots.

Information about Project Entropia can be found on the official
website at http://www.project-entropia.com/ and prospective beta
testers can apply at
http://www.project-entropia.com/betatest/apply.asp?topic=betatest

MindArk (http://www.mindark.com) develops technology solutions that
address the entertainment needs of millions of Internet users. With
novel applications in the interactive entertainment sector and
browser infrastructure, MindArk is a world leader in expanding the
new universe.

I'd like to see Tazoon rich and alive, with NPCs going about their business somewhat like they do in Shenmue. I'd like to see homes, small businesses, fruit markets, kids playing in the streets, policemen on horseback and many more things incorporated to make the city feel alive and real. With that being said, Douglas Shuler made a few comments on Tazoon's architecture and history on AEs boards.
Tazoon is the capital of the Central Empire and is run primarily by Humans, although their neutrality allows a large diversity of other races to enter freely. Because Tazoon has been around for so long, it has become an ecclectic collection of architectural styles, representing a wide array of styles over centuries of development. It's a melting-pot of culture, and the locus for most trade in Istaria.The style we've chosen to go with on Tazoon specifically (and not ALL Human cities) is one of stately grandeur. It's influenced by two distinct architectural palettes, Italian and Greek. The Italian influences include terra cotta roofs, eggshell white walls, and bas relief decor along the edges of many buildings. The Greek influences include domes, pillars, open structures, and general scale, as well as extensive use of marble as a building material.
Comparisons to Theed from Phantom Menace are inevitable, as are those to Dinotopia. This is because they drew similar inspiration from the same historic sources. They were not the first to use this collection of styles, and we will not be the last.

As for the other Human cities; you'll see different looks based on their individual locations since they're influenced by thier neighboring races. It's my intention to make each and every city of Istaria a unique place with unique layout and design, so you can tell you're in a different place, and will have interesting places to explore. I'm putting a lot of time into it, so you won't have 'cookie-cutter' cities merely used to resupply between play sessions.

ACTOOL.. An interview with its developer Cameron Cole
Posted on Monday, May 14, 2001 12:32 PM EST by Pretzel

First of all I would like to say that Cameron Cole was the AC Macro development Pioneer and his "ACTOOL" was and is used by 99% of all AC macroers because of its ease in coding and stable platform. I myself use it daily and wanted to learn a little bit about him.

Q: What got you started into macroing

A: To tell the truth, I have never really macroed. I started writing AC Tool
in order to fletch some arrows for a character. I stopped playing the
character soon after I started and thus my macroing career never took off.

At first, I looked around and couldn't find any program for AC that did
macros at all. That is when I decided it would be nice to contribute
something to the AC world and dedicated some serious development time to AC
Tool. Before long, AC Tool was simply theraputic programming that took my
mind off of my day-to-day development job. In the later parts of AC Tool's
development, it was the users that kept me going. Several even contributed
to the program itself. This was my fuel so to speak. As long as users were
using, I was writing.

Q: What do you see in the near future of macroing

A: Macros as they pertain to AC: I think the limit for macros has almost been
met. Further refinement will come, but the macro engines available now are
so powerful that almost any task can be accomplished inside of AC. I expect
to see more and more impressive macros, but the engines that drive them will
most likely not change other than minor enhancements and bug fixes. It is
already possible to completely macro a character from 1st to 70th and
beyond. What more could a person ask for?

Macros as they pertain to future software: It is my hope that as the newer
online games come out they embrace macroing. There is nothing wrong with
macroing contrary to what some believe. The only problem with it is that it
isn't a level playing field. Everyone should have access to a scripting
engine inside a game. Personally, I believe the next generation games
should come with some sort of scripting language built into the game itself.
Lets be honest, how many people want to spend an hour fletching arrows? It
is just silly to think anyone will want to do that. If macroing is
considered in the game development process, it is likely that balancing
issues around it will be worked out instead of patching or worse banning
after the fact. Macroing should be a feature not an offense.

Q: Tell me about some of the greatest macros you have come across or
coded.

A: I guess I can let the cat out of the bag since the macro will most likely
never see the light of day. I have seen (even played with it so this is no
myth) a macro engine that was the ultimate melee bot. A friend of mine
built a character that was basically impervious to Olthoi in the Olthoi
Vault. This macro engine was based loosely around his character and idea
for the most part. What it did was kill Olthoi, charge items, buff, heal,
pick up items, recall to town, restock supplies, and even logged back on
when it got disconnected. The macro made about a million an hour in
unassigned exp. Not a lot, but considering it needed no human control and
could run indefinately it was marvelous. The last version I saw still had
timing and recognition bugs, but I believe it was about 80% the way there.

Q: What is entailed in the aerlinthe macros?

A: You are asking the wrong guy. I just write the engine. I have never really
played around with actual macros.

Q: What are you currently working on as far as AC macros, plugs, or
platforms?

A: I have handed my torch over to the next generation of macro writers. David Smith fROM onlinegamegimp.com(Portal Bot and Robotica fame) has taken over the development and
maintenance of AC Tool. He has done a phenominal job on all the products.
The AC Script guys have definately made a huge advancement in macros as
well. So to answer your question, I am waiting on AC2 and the next
generation of macro engines.

Q: How do you feel about Drain and or Hunting (smart) macros?

A: Let me describe my basic philosophy about playing games before I answer
this. I play games for me and not for the stress of keeping up with the
Jones. That said if someone wants to macro a character up so they can get
to the island, doesn't bother me one bit. As long as it doesn't
significantly impact my playing, I don't care what Joe Blow wants to do with
his 10 bucks a month. I say macro AC if that is what you want to do. To be
perfectly honest, I dislike the players that steal my kills or leech off of
my exp MUCH more than any macroer. Macroing has such a minor impact on AC
and the players of the game.

Q: Is there anything thats not possible by macroing?

A: With AC, yes. However, AC macroing is really only limited to what the
client and user can do since the AC servers maintain the state of the
character. I think of it as the matrix. Some rules you can bend and others
you can break, but some rules are non-negotiable because that is the way the
system is written. I don't expect to see any Neo's running around
deflecting arrows anytime soon, but you never know.

Q: Do you have a favorite AC Easter Egg?

A: Not really. Actually, I have always wondered what Cameron's Curse was. I
hear there was a spell named after me, but I have never actually seen it.
If I had a favorite AC Easter Egg, it would be to see a screen shot of that
spell. Not really an easter egg, but it would be my favorite if it is
exists.

Q: Do you currently macro? If so which macros etc?

A: Don't macro and as I mentioned early never really did. I was in this for
the fun and conversation. Interesting side note was that I continued to
support AC Tool six months after I stopped playing AC. That is how
wonderful the macro users were to just be around.

Thanks for sending over the questionnaire. Let me know if I can answer any
other questions. If this does see "press", send me a link. I would love to
see the entire article.

Thanks again, Cameron.

If you have an interest in macroing for that trade mule or think you have what it takes to make a full on melee bot check out Camerons site:
http://www.cameroncole.net/ac.html

A small interview with Dave from : http://www.onlinegamegimp.com/
Q: What got you started into macroing?

A: I was bored and have no life. The normal prerequisite for any serious AC development.

Q: What do you see in the near future of macroing?

A: ACTool was really hot during its prime, but I see game interaction being the future, since obtaining your stats, monster name, etc are vital for advanced macroing. ACScript fills that gap nicely, but its syntax and nonintuitive structure confuse old-time macroers who aren't interested in diving into hardcore scripting. Robotica was intended to fill the gap, but I overcomplicated my tool a bit and may perform major surgery on it shortly. Choices are always a bonus: ACScript, ACTool, Robotica, Decal, ACHook custom programs, EZClient.. numerous options with limitless possibilities. Our 3rd party devs just rock, no other
MMORPG has this luxurious a selection of tools.

Q: Tell me about some of the greatest macros you have come across or coded.

A: Classics would be Madar's Magic defense, Oceana's fullpack tradeskill, and single tab fully beneficials. Oceana is my hero for various scripting needs. She'll adopt a macro, then improve it substantially and make the old one obsolete. Just gotta love that Oceana girl!

Q: What is entailed in the aerlinthe macros?

A: When critters start draining back, it's time to ditch ACTool and use programs that react to character changes. Like healing on low health, switching from drain to harm at target points, splitting peas when outta comps, refreshing buffs upon expiration. Not rocket science by far, but merely planning specific goals and reacting to environmental factors.

Q: What are you currently working on as far as AC macros etc?

A: People using scripts all seem to have a dozen common goals in mind - DrainBot, PortalBot, ArcherPerch Bot, MeleeBot, etc. I'm realizing that creating quality, dedicated programs to accomodate each niche might be a superior alternative to generalized scripting languages. Then release the code, and let users customize. Of course, I could be wrong (yet again).

Q: All of us on FF saw your dedication to the community with the addition of Gand and Angel. Tell us about angels setup (the PCBOARD on plywood) and what kept you there for those long months etc.

A: FrostFell never had a portal bot, and I kept reading how elite other server's bots were so decided to make my own. I wrote the MT guy asking for code and he refused, so I broke out the Big Chief pad, and maroon crayola, and designed a free bot, planted him in Ayan then prayed people would actually use him. One thing I learned from having a high-traffic bot is how generous people are at heart. Even though Angel was free, folks would dump countless trade notes on me. After filling up three mules with D's to the point I had no room for my armor and wands, I turned off GiveItems on Angel and people STILL tried donating cash. That says a lot about FrostFell inhabitants - there's an ample supply of asses, but nice players outnumber them immensely.

Q: From a coders point of view is there anything thats not possible by using the packet sniffers? What type of things are possible?

A: Sure, all client-side activity that's not sent downstream would require research to find the bytes in AC's process, but ultimately there's no limit. Newer devs bring a fresh prospective to the table, and make cool new discoveries. On every occasion, us older guys can pickup and enhance our tools. When combined, we all help each other out and everyone benefits. Marvelous example would be Cibo's XML. Inside are contributions from hundreds of people. Scramble it all together and we have this big team of folks dedicated to making everyone's gaming experience better. Teamwork - it's what makes all things possible :)
