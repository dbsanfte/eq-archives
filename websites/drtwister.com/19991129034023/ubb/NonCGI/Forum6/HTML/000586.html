If things like how fast your SoW casts for is kept on your computer, along with how many hps a buff gives you, then I'm betting that something like duration of a spell is also kept on your side. Very interesting....let's look at it.

You and your level 50 friend both switch your spells data around the same, then you get him to cast a couple level 50 buffs on your newbie. But instead of letting the spell duration last for ten minutes, you switch it to 255 minutes. Or whatever your little heart desires.

Now you have a level 1 newbie running around with 500 hps that'll last your for 10 hours of gaming, should make leveling a little bit easier.

Not good enough?

How about a little regeneration. Same thing as before, both switch, cast, now your level 1 newbie has 500+ hps that're constantly regenerating for 10 hours. Should make leveling fly by.

Not good enough?

How about you toss in a damage shield? Your level 50 druid buddy tosses one on you, both with your duration times switched, and now you're not only having 500+ hps that're constantly regenerating, but you're also dishing out 24 points of damage to everything that touches you. Should be able to make level 20 in 20 hours or less with the above three all in effect.

Still not good enough?

This post was just to shine some light on the potential of what Stormlord was saying. Everyone seems to be posting that they can't figure out how to make their hit points go above 37, while no one seems to be exploring the huge potential of the can of worms that he's opened. There're so many useful spells that're out there that only last for a short duration, making them useless on the whole to leveling. But make them practically permanent? Well there you have it.

A couple classes have a brief invulnerability spell. Bingo. Set this to 25000 seconds instead of 25, and you can now you can go pick a fight with a sand giant...or solo a dragon. Hell, I'm almost tempted to go play with it so I could smack Lady Vox around a couple times and get myself a Mistwalker but...nope, not quite enough Plus she's got them friggin heals, way too much effort ;p
