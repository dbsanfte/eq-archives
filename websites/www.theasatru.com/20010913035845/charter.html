This Charter exists to remind us of, and to tell others of the principles which make us what we are, and to show us what to strive for as we move on into the future.

WE ARE, above all else, a Fellowship, and we strive at all times to maintain the family atmosphere which makes appropriate the use of the word in our guild's name.

WE DESIRE to empower our members to exercise their personal freedoms, including the freedom to determine their own comfortable level of participation in the guild, the freedom to have characters as members in other guilds, and the freedom to play the game in the way which makes them most happy.

WE DESIRE to grow, in a limited and positive fashion, by the formation of personal relationships between our members and other players, and use of good judgement in inviting to join us those people we feel would fit well within our family.

WE DESIRE to have within our guild, an environment which encourages frequent and open communication between members, and enables us to be successful in the organization of raids, and other guild events.

WE DESIRE to have a good reputation amongst other players and guilds, and to be held in esteem by those whom we also hold in esteem.

TO MAKE THESE DESIRES COME ABOUT:

WE SHOULD participate as far as possible in the guild's events and hunts.

WE SHOULD make full use of both the message boards and guildsay in order to maintain the relationships which are such an important part of the guild family.

WE SHOULD be judicious and thoughtful in our sponsoring of new members.

WE SHOULD at all times behave in accordance with Verant's Code of Conduct.

WE SHOULD at all times strive to demonstrate to our guildmates and to the world, the following qualities:

Nobility of heart - so that dishonour never taints our reputation.

Gallantry of spirit - helping those in trouble, defending the weak, and
      putting others first.

 Respect for others - always remembering that people's time is precious,
      and never forgetting the person behind the character.

 Courtesy - treating other people as we would wish to be treated, never
      nagging or making repeated requests for assistance, and taking the time
      to be polite.

 Integrity - being honest in our dealings with others, both inside and
      outside the guild.

Loyalty and devotion to the guild and our guildmates .
