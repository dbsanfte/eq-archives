Policies:

1. If a member is found to be in serious violation of aforementioned ideals in the charter, then the guild senior council reserve's the right to vote on dismissal from guild. The idea of the game is to have fun and we'll not lose out on fun of thegame by dealing with any persisting problems in the guild.

2. Though we try to group and work together as much as possible there are times when a person is busy doing something else and at no time is anyone obligated to participate in a raid, provide a teleport, resurrect someone's corpse, power level someone, camp a item for another, etc.

3. Rude behavior, being extremely annoying, cybering, flaming or any other inappropriate behavior on guildsay or the guild forum page will not be allowed. Disciplinary action will include a warning, and may finally result in removal from the guild. Obscenities, are not grounds for dismissal if they are not used for malicious purpose. However, constant use of obscenities that would be censored on normal USA TV, will not be tolerated. While it is understandable that mis-tells and heat of the moment explicatives happen, constant abuse will create an uncomfortable environment for many.

Reinstatements to the guild -

4. Any members removed from the guild, that wish to rejoin, must have their membership voted on. They need to receive more then 80% of the votes to be reinstated. If a person is reinstated any future infractions will disallow them from guild membership.

5. Any member whom removes themselves without violating our charter can rejoin without having to reapply, under the following guild lines:

.....a) Reguilding can occur automatically if a member deguilds to temporarily join another guild for purposes of a raid.

.....b) An explanation to the guild should be made in a public post in members section, as to why he/she deguilded themself and wish to reguild. We will not remove access immediately upon a deguild, unless that person requests it to be done.

.....c) A period of 3 days for a discussion among current guild members will take place before reguilding. There may have been an underlying problem with the deguild that needs to be addressed.

.....d) If anyone publicly or privately (with anonymity) tells an officer they would like a vote to occur, a vote will be taken before the reguilding.

..........d-1) If policies were not broken, a plain majority vote in the members section can allow reguilding.

..........d-2) If policies were broken, an 80% majority vote in the members section can allow reguilding.

6. Anytime investigations show there were no infractions of our policies and charter there will be no formal recording of the incident.

7. In summation if you are having problems with a fellow member please take it to tells, say, or group. Please try to avoid inappropriate behavior at all times. Please advise any of us officers of the problems. Please feel that we are not difficult to reach out to. We are here to help and serve you. We want you to contact us!

Loot Policy :

Raid items are being awarded by officers. In most cases officers will ask those present, who need the item, and then will discuss amongst themselves the people that need the item, what they have been awarded lately, and how much it will help them if they are awarded the item. We will try to distribute items so everyone gets something at sometime but also to those who need it the most and help the guild the most.

If you attend our raids, please ask ahead of time what loot will and will not be available if you are outside of our guild. As of right now there is no clear cut responce.

You may only request items for the toon present unless all present pass.
Exception:
If you were going to take a different toon of the right level, and were asked to bring another, you may roll on the toon's items you did not bring, and not on the items for the toon you did bring. You must state this intention before the raid to the raid leader or loot dealer.

Ninja Looting will never be tolerated under any circumstances. This doesn't mean a multi-questable item can't be looted prior to a complete wipe-out. However, if you do not have rights to loot a no-drop item it is better to let the item rot then loot inappropriately.

Privacy Policy :

1. All Forum's that are not public need to remain confidential among the people who have access to them. Anyone found relaying information may have their access to the offending forum removed.

2. The content of members only forums should not be discussed with those outside the guild. An exception can be made for guild events that you would like to invite someone from the outside on.

3. Applicant review should not be discussed with the applicants.

4. Officer forums should not be discussed with general members (in detail.) Fact finding of course may need to be done.

5. Council information that is sensitive should not be discussed with anyone not in council.

6. Of course we can not mandate keeping secrets from your spouce or signifigant other. In general it is probably ok to talk about topics as long as specifics are not given out.

New Member Policy:

First all applicants must have a sponsor. This sponsor will be responsible for making sure you have the opportunity to join us on some events, groups, etc. and it will be the responsibility of that sponsor to report to us and recommend you for membership. A member can only sponsor one person at a time. All applicants need to apply on our site (soon to be coming to a theater near us). Once the application has been completed there will be a 3-4 day comment period on that application from all or any members. If two or more members have a concern the enforcement committee will step in and do an evaluation. More then likely, if two or more people have a concern they will not be accepted. If there are no concerns, they will be an "interim" guild member. They will have our tag, participate with us in any and all events, and get to know us. This "interim" period will last 2-4 weeks. During this time further comments will be able to be made either in support or rejecting this candidate. If the overall message is positive they will be moved by the membership committee to full status and have access to the full boards. If negative, the will be moved to the enforcement group for enforcement.

The only exception (always seems to be one eh?) is for mass recruiting. The mass recruiting/guild merger policy is:

An officer of Asatru meets with the merging guild's leader, or whomever they deem is in charge of the merger from their end. they draft a 'plan' which contains all the relevant info about the merger, such as: names of the people being merged, how long we 'grandfather' people in, which (if any) officers keep an officership in Asatru, and whether the merging members are automatically full members or trial members.

after that has been completed, it's put to both guilds for review, and both guilds comment on it and suggest changes. it's up to the officer from Asatru to change what they deem necessary, but the officer from asatru and the officer from the merging guild have to let each other know what changed.

after 3 or so days of review, it gets changed into a poll, and 2/3 of the votes need to be in favor of the merger. the voting should take at least 5 days, to give everyone a chance to make up their final mind and for those that don't check the board as often.

if approved, officers can start inviting the former guild members and the date on the grandfather clause is set. after that date, additional members need to go through the application process. if the plan isn't approved, the officer from asatru and the officer from the othe guild revise their plan and re-submit it to both guilds for a review.

Powerleveling Policy:

We as a guild encourage all main characters to level themselves as opposed to power-leveling. We will conduct level appropriate raids and guild events to support our members and encourage them to learn how to handle the differing challenges incurred by them. We will, on an individual basis, power level and alternate character to support the needs of the guild. All request for such support should be posted for the membership committe to handle.

Raid Rules and Regulations:

The following rules are are binding on all parties in an Asatru-lead Raid, may the participant be a member of Asatru or not.

1. If a Raid is scheduled for a specific time, it will start at that time. Neither sooner nor later. Exceptions will be made if the case justifies it. This, however, does not include waiting for people or the Raidleader thinking he/she cannot wait.

2. If a participant is late for the raid, whatever circumstances may have caused it, he/she will be brought in if the situation allows this. Should this not be the case, the participant can either wait till we are able to get him/her in or cancel participation in this raid. If the participant is late, he/she goes to the bottom of the reserves. Reserves are taken in order of need, not necessarily in order of signup.

3. The commands of the Raidleader are to be followed at any times. Failure to do so will be followed by a one time warning and if that does not help it will result in the banning from this or even further Asatru-lead Raids. Nobody is perfect, but if a participant is willingly rejecting orders or not listening to them we need to kick him/her out to ensure that the raid is not being put into danger by that person. See the Raidleader as your commanding officer and follow his/her commands to the point. A raid is not a democracy.

4. The commands and procedures of the Raidleader are not to be questioned in /ooc /shout or /say at any time. If you have an important information or complaint, please send a tell. This does not include tells like "Hey, our group needs another wiz to effectively KS the others." or anything to that effect. If these tells are persistant, those participants will be set on ignore for the rest of the raid.

5. The /shout channel is SOLELY reserved for the Raid-Leadership. Any violations of this rule will be answered with a one time warning and finally with a banning from this raid. If you have something to say, no matter how important you think it is, use /ooc. Try to keep /ooc clear of unneeded spam, we need to be able to see important information etc.

Words of advice: The participants should follow these steps to reduce lag and spam so they can spot the important orders/information easier:

a. Set 'Sky', 'Particle effects' and 'Level of Detail' and if it need be,'Player names' to OFF
b. Deactivate 'others hits', 'Damage shields' (as long as you dont have a debuff job),'Bard songs'.
c. type /serverfilter on
d. Set your /say color to something better spottable than white/black.
e. Some situations are easier to master if you are using 3rd person perspective, try it.

This should greatly reduce your lag and chance to go LD (except 5.), so try to follow those points.

These rules have not been made to reduce your fun, but to actually increase the chance of a successful raid.
