As a raiding guild the following guidelines must be used for the safety of all, and to insure a quality raid.

1.  Show up on time, be there and be ready to go. Don't make people wait for you.

2.  Pay attention. At any moment a raiding force can move, jump into battle, or need your attention. Please inform group leaders if you must go afk.

3.  NEVER send a direct tell to a raid leader. They are juggling 20 things at once. Any questions or comments should be told to your group leader or an officer. They will keep raid leaders informed.  Continued abuse will be warned.  Repeated abuse will be grounds for guild removal.

4.  Know your zones and mobs. Most prepared wins. If you don't know before a raid, search the web, or ask someone. Knowledge is key.

5.  Know your class. We strive for smart players, not pure brute force. Knowing your class expectations in any given battle is key.

6.  "ASSIST"  Always wait for the Main assist (MA) or Second assist (SA) to call for you to help on a mob.   the MA and SA are deciding what mob to fight next, what mobs to unmezz, gaining taunt agro. Any jumping the gun can kill an entire raid, or at least make it more complicated.  Always assist from behind the mob unless directed otherwise.

7.  Please try to attend the majority of a raid. All raids will have an estimated duration. if you can safely join a raid after it has begun that will be your responsibility to get to us.  we cannot stop the raid to add each new person, but if you can get to us you are welcome to join.  if you must leave a raid early please plan for that in advance, i.e. bring gate potions, inviso potions, etc.

8.  Keep raid channel clear for information. Use guild, group, and say for conversations. Also, try and keep emotes and food/drink spam low on a raid.

9.  Be patient. Sometimes a raid can be slow, or there may be a lot of deaths. Patients will help keep things moving along faster then complaints.

10.  All peridots, port stones, pearls for CoH and coffins will be supplied by the guild raid fund.  Please provide other items such as fish scales, batwings, etc.

11.  Be ready for the CAMP! call. Never know when we will be in a spot where running./evacing can't be done because of logistics. So, when told by a leader to CAMP! do it without hesitation.  Good policy is to camp for at least 3 min.  You can also come back into raid chat with an alt. and check to see if its safe to log back in.

12.  Planes of Power raid feature will be used on ALL guild raids.  Therefore it is a requirement that all members have Planes of Power.

All raid guidelines are just that, a guide. But, continually ignoring raid policies can be cause for a warning, and in an extreme case be grounds for removal. We want everyone to have fun, but also need to keep some kind of Order.

 


Loot Rule/Policy-

As a raiding guild we will need to keep a large raid attendance to accomplish goals. Thus, the system employed is Merit/NBG (need before greed).  Also, you must be of proper class/race to use an item.   

1. Merit means that a particular item has been designated for a certain guild member.  This item will not be /rolled on by raid members.  A merit decision will be decided at the time the item drops (some places we will have no idea what drops and therefore cannot claim the merit in advance).

2. NBG (need before greed) means that you “can and will use” the item, its not for twinking an alt or friend and its not for selling for the pp.  A player can only win one item per raid until everyone has won an item. Once all have won, the cycle can start again. 

3. Pre-claimed means that a raid leader claims an item for a particular person at the time the raid is posted or announced.  Only 1 item per raid can be pre-claimed by a raid leader.  No one else will get a chance to roll on this item. 

4. If no player is able to use an item or no one wants a particular item, then the whole raid can roll for twinking or selling. 

5. Only time any of these rules may differ, is on class specific items.  Then, those who have previously won may or may not be given a chance to win this item.

6. In some cases, a raid will be designed to get one class or character upgrades. This will be done so the guild as a whole can advance in power, and abilities. These types of raids will not be common, but will be needed in some cases. any raid of this type will be clearly posted as such a raid.

7. Once a loot decision has been made at a raid, it is Final.  There is to be NO discussion during the raids about any loot decision.  Save any comments or criticisms for the message boards or guild meetings.  Anyone continually abusing this rule will be removed from the guild. 

8. 2-boxing or dual-boxing (playing 2 characters at one time) is allowed.  Thus, those players that 2-box during a raid will be allowed to roll for each character they are playing.  Both characters are helping the raid and therefore should be allowed to roll.  Normal loot rules apply to 2-boxers, except only one character can be considered for a merit per raid.

9. If you are asked to bring a different character to a raid by the raid leader, you may roll for either your original character or the new one.  NOT BOTH.  You must specify to the raid leader which character you will be rolling for at the beginning of the raid and you may not switch at any time who you are rolling for. 

10. If you bring an alt. character to the raid just because you want to play someone different, then you roll for the character you are playing.

11. Recruits do not get the chance to roll until all members have passed.  Sorry but we must think of current members first. 

12. If a player is pre-claimed an item they may roll on other items until their pre-claim item drops (in case their item does not drop).  Once the pre-claimed item drops that person cannot roll on any item even a BP, after all you got what you really wanted on this raid.  Exception:  if the pre-claimed item is guaranteed to drop, i.e. talisman from Harla Dar, then the person that is to get that item will not be allowed to roll on any other items unless “all roll” is called.

13. All merchant fodder and cash loot will go to the guild raid fund to help pay for peridots, pearls, coffins, portal stones, etc.

14. Raid leaders will decide the looters for each raid. Items that are tradable will be held till the end of the raid.  NO DROP items will be announced and rolled when possible.  Pay attention to the leaders for the loot drops and when you may need to roll on an item.

15. If anyone leaves a raid early, and something drops that they would have been merited, an officer will hold the item for that player in his/her bank. If the item would have been rolled against someone else with same merit, an officer will roll for the player. If an item becomes an open guild roll, player forfeits loot rights. Note, you must have been at the raid for a majority of its duration.

16. ANYONE can lead a raid.  Raid leader gains pre-claim rights and allows you the opportunity to participate in the Loot Council, if you so choose.

17. All raids will use the above rules.  Any raid under any other rules is not considered an official OFC raid.  Your attendance does not reflect on the guild or its loot and policies.

18. When upgrading items, members should consider the extent of an upgrade.  If an item is a small upgrade for yourself, consider passing on the item so someone that is lesser equipped may win.  “Share the Wealth.”
