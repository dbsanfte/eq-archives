Koada’Dal Mithril Armor Overview

Here are the recipes for Mithril plate armor.  If you find one of these recipes are not correct, please let me know.  To create this armor, you MUST be a High elf, and you have to use the Koada’dal forge (building right next to the bank). Fortunately, you don’t lose your components when you try an incorrect recipe (how I wish that was true for the rest of the forges), so you can experiment to your hearts content. If you try an incorrect recipe, close the forge and reopen it and all your components should be in there.  The component recipes use morning dew instead of water, and most of the final recipes use moonlight temper (which you can find about on the tempers page) and an elven smithy hammer.  The Mithril chainmail is caster wearable.

Component Recipies

  • Mithril rings = large brick of mithril ore + file + morning dew
  • Mithril chain jointing = mithril rings + elven smithy hammer + file + morning dew
  • Folded sheet of mithril = block of mithril ore + elven smithy hammer + morning dew
  • Sheet of mithril = 2 small bricks of mithril + morning dew

Note on enchanted mithril: An enchanter must enchant the large brick of mithril ore BEFORE you start working with it in the forge.  After the enchantment is done, working with the ore is done in the same way.  Enchanting metal does not restrict the diety of who can use that item.

Mithril chainmail

Mithril chainmail piece = appropriate chain pattern + appropriate number of mithril rings + silk swatch + elven smithy hammer + moonlight temper

  • veils, bracelets and neckguards = 1 set of Mithril rings
  • Coifs, sleeves, mantles, skirts and gloves = 2 sets of Mithril rings
  • Tunics, capes and leggings = 3 sets of Mithril rings
  • There is no known boot recipe

Enchanted mithril chainmail

Enchanted mithril chainmail piece = appropriate chain pattern + appropriate number of enchanted mithril rings + silk swatch + elven smithy hammer + moonlight temper

  • veils, bracelets and neckguards = 1 set of Enchanted mithril rings
  • Coifs, sleeves, mantles, skirts and gloves = 2 sets of Enchanted mithril rings
  • Tunics, capes and leggings = 3 sets of Enchanted mithril rings
  • There is no known boot recipe

Stats:

  • Tunic: 14 AC, +4 wis, +4 int, +2 save vs Magic, 1.5 weight, wearable by all, casters included and all good elves (half elf, high elf, wood elf)

Mithril platemail

Mithril platemail = appropriate plate section mold + appropriate number of folded sheets of  mithril + mithril chain jointing + leather padding + elven smithy hammer + moonlight temper

  • Visors, collars, bracers and boots = 1 Folded sheet of mithril
  • Helms, pauldrons, girdles, vambraces and gauntlets = 2 Folded sheets of mithril
  • Breastplates, greaves and cloaks = 3 Folded sheets of mithril

NOTE:  For the imbued versions of mithril weapons and shields, USE Moonlight temper instead of morning dew and add ONE imbued gem.

Mithril weapons

  • koada'dal falchion = curved blade mold + hilt mold + pommel mold + folded sheet of mithril + morning dew
    • Stats: 7 dam, 24 delay
    • Can be imbued with: (add imbued gem and change morning dew to moonlight temper)
      • Imbued Emerald - This will restrict the finished armor to followers of Tunare.
        • Adds +3 str, +3 wis

Mithril shields

  • woodlander shield = kite shield mold + 2 sheets of mithril + elven smithy hammer + moonlight temper
    • Stats: 11 AC, 10 weight
    • Can be imbued with: (add imbued gem)
      • Imbued Emerald - This will restrict the finished armor to followers of Tunare.
        • Adds: +2 str, +2 wis, +2 sta
