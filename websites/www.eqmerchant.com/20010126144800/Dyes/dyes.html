The art of Dying Fine Plate Steel

Here is the most recent information that I have.. again, if you have actually verified something different then what is here, please let me know!

Basically, here are the steps... I’ll describe them in passing here, and in detail later in the page:

  1. Get your components.
  2. Have a shaman with Alchemy (or a rogue with Poisonmaking) make your dye extract.
  3. Get a potter to make clay jars.
  4. Combine the dye extract with resin to create the “shade” of color that you want of dye.
  5. Laquer your Fine Steel Plate with the dye, acid, and laquer.
  6. Wear your resulting Colored armor with pride!

Assembly Instructions

STEP ONE

  • Take the component, give it to a shaman who has alchemy or a rogue who has poison making.  He will combine these components (for a shaman he combines in a medicine bag):
    • 1 Component (like a Permafrost crystal)
    • 1 Flask of water (NOT summoned)
    • 1 Empty vial (Loc) - Don’t use the poisoning vial!
  • and that will make 1 Dye extract of the corrosponding color.

STEP TWO

  • Have a master potter make a medium clay jar. Using a potter’s wheel, combine:
    • 1 Block of clay
    • 1 Flask of water
    • 1 Medium jar sketch
  • and that will make 1 unfired Medium clay jar.  Take the jar and fire it by combining in a kiln:
    • 1 Unfired medium clay jar
    • 1 Quality firing sheet
  • and that will make a Medium Clay Jar

STEP THREE

  • Take your medium clay jar (which is a container with a combine button) and combine the following:
    • 1 Dye extract (of the appropriate color)
    • 1-3 resins (1 resin for the “Dark” color, 2 resins for the “medium” color and 3 resins for the “light” color)
  • and that will make a Vial of dye
  • Anyone can combine the dye extract with the resins, there is no skill check.

STEP FOUR

  • Combine the following in a regular forge:
    • 1 Piece of Fine steel plate armor
    • 1 Vial of dye
    • 1 Jar of laquer (Loc)
    • 1 Jar of acid (Loc)
  • and that will make a piece of Colored Fine Steel Plate.
  • Anyone can do this... you don’t need the blacksmith. There is no skill check.

Important things to note:

  • You dye the armor AFTER it has already been forged... NOT during!  Make sure you have a completed normal looking piece of Fine steel plate armor before proceeding with dying.
  • You can never change the color of the armor after it has been dyed.
  • Making Clay Jars is trivial at 35.
  • Making the dyes is trivial to a rogue with 38 poisonmaking.

About the colors

The colors are as follows:

1 Extract + 1 Resin = Dark Color Dye
1 Extract + 2 Resins = Color Dye
1 Extract + 3 Resins = Light Color Dye

Basically, every color follows the same conventions:

The dark color dye is a light shade of the color, surrounded by heavy shadows around the edges.
The regular color dye is the most vibrant, pigmented color available.
The light color dye looks like the regular color dye, with the brightness turned up.

Here are some examples:

Permafrost Crystals
DARK BLUE: Looks like a light shade of blue with heavy shadows along the edges
BLUE: Looks like Warrior crafted armor
LIGHT BLUE: Looks like Teal or Aqua Blue

Sarcoscypha Fungus
DARK RED:  Looks like a light shade of red with heavy shadows along the edges
RED: Looks like Rubicite - Bright Red
LIGHT RED: Looks Hot Pink

Evergreen Leaves
DARK GREEN: Again, looks like light green with heavy shadows along the edges
GREEN: Looks like the green you would find on scarab helm or Hero Bracers
LIGHT GREEN: Look Florescent Green

Iron Oxide
DARK DARK RED: One person said it looked like the Bloodstained Tunic, another said it looked like a “hideous shade of brown”.  Your milage may vary.
