Above all else Prophecy is a group of friends. For the most part Prophecy works and plays very well together. People share ideas and knowledge, we compliment one another's abilities, and we enjoy each other's company.

    The officers of Prophecy, in order to maintain our friendly atmosphere more completely, are putting forth this "Code of Conduct".

    The officers of Prophecy intend to lead by example. We will live this code of conduct as fully as we are able. Additionally, we will work to the greatest of our abilities to deliver the most enjoyable guild in Everquest. We will be as fair as humanly possible in resolving disagreements, we will listen to suggestions, and we will always strive to please those people who can be pleased.

    In return, we expect the members of Prophecy to live by this code of conduct. No new member will be admitted to the guild that lacks these characteristics. Any existing member who demonstrates a pattern of failing to exhibit these qualities will be, with due process, removed. The officer corps will carry out judgment in these cases.

    This is not to say the officers are going to be heavy handed. To the contrary, we are members of the guild, too. Our enjoyment stems, in part, from a general feeling of goodwill within the guild. It is our intent to remove bad apples, allow no new ones, and cultivate an atmosphere of brotherhood. Our goal is an overwhelming percentage of satisfied members.

    For the benefit of each individual and the guild as a whole it is each member's responsibility to evaluate whether they fit within the bounds of Prophecy or not. If you don't fit you won't have fun, nor will other members of the guild.

    CODE OF CONDUCT

    Show integrity
    Each member must demonstrate a higher level of integrity than is generally present in the server population. We strive to be the best. Mediocrity will not suffice. Don't train on others; don't group with those that do, and so forth. We are known by whom we associate with. If you do something that is reprehensible it affects us all. Others will remember your guild tag longer than they will remember your name.

    Have fun and don't impact others' fun
    This is a game and it is played for fun. The more you enjoy it, the more you will be enjoyed. It is an escape for many. If you are having a bad day, have died too many times, or the boss (or instructor) is climbing down your back, please don't lash out at your friends in the game. It is not right for one to degrade the enjoyment of another. Besides, the person you take it out on might have been the one to pull you back out of the doldrums.

    Be polite
    In an effort to not impair someone else's enjoyment of the game all members of Prophecy will make a concerted effort to be polite to others. Don't hide behind the anonymity of the web. Imagine that you are standing in front of the person you are speaking too. If you have an issue or a problem it will be addressed and every effort will be made to work it out. Don't present it from a position of anger and your concern will be heard and addressed. This includes in game and out, in guild and out.

    Show respect
    Each of us has talents, view, and capabilities worthy of the respect of the others. Your disagreement with another does not mean that the other is less talented or knowledgeable. Swearing in anger at someone and inflammatory comments will not be tolerated. Perhaps the other is wrong in this case, but he is still worthy of your respect.

    Share
    Part of the fun is sharing ideas, items, stories, and knowledge. The more you share with others, the more likely it is they will share with you. We have a tradition of passing items around to other guild mates before we sell them. It is not mandatory, but how you deal with items demonstrates your priorities. It's fun to gain new things and it's fun to give them, too.

    Loyalty
    Each one of us needs help and support from time to time. The more you provide to your guild mates, the more likely you are to receive help when you need it. It may be assistance camping an item, help dealing with a kill stealer, or help with a corpse recovery. Help out; you'll enjoy it.

    Do your best
    We all continue to learn. You are not expected to play flawlessly. Nor should you expect others to be without fault. If you die because you didn't get healed in time or if someone evacs at the wrong time or if someone over pulls please give them a break. It may be you next time.
