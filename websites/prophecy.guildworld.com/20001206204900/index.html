Prophecy, a Bertoxxulous Guild

A streak of flame lit the night sky briefly, and a shadow slashed across the moon, there and gone in an instant. A hush fell across the land and the heavy sweep of giant wings came faintly into the small clearing beneath ancient oak trees.

The old shaman pulled her heavy shawl around her shoulders and leaned forward over the small fire which burned in the pit in the center of her hut. She dipped wrinkled fingers into one of the many pockets of her apothecary pouch and pulled forth a pinch of dull red powder. This she sprinkled onto the coals of the fire, staring at the bright flash of light which resulted, inhaling the heavy fumes engendered by the incineration of the mystic substance. She began to chant the magical words passed down to her by her mother, who learned them from her mother before her, who learned them from her mother before her, so on back into the mists of time.

The fire seemed to waver and dance, lose shapes and then gain them. Suddenly the fire went flat, and hung perfectly still above the coals in the pit.

In a trance, the old woman saw the distinct forms of people coalesce - a paladin swung a mighty sword, a hard eyed rogue plunged a dagger into the back of a stricken froglok, a bard strummed his lute before a crowd of attentive listeners, a cleric faced down a horrific undead warrior, a wizard stood poised in front of a giant, a monk stared defiantly into a long dark tunnel; faster and faster came the images in the flames. A ranger stood in a barren rocky land, a druid in wolf form bared long fangs in a snarl, a warrior fought a huge sharp toothed bat, a mage powdered a piece of stone to summon forth an elemental, a shadowknight laughed as she delivered a killing blow to a kobold, an enchanter stood among many motionless monsters while a necromancer sent his animated skeleton against one. More faces, gone as fast as they appeared.

Suddenly the fire blazed up, frightening the old shaman, and a pair of angry golden eyes the size of dinner plates glared at her. She jumped up, knocking over a stool, but the eyes were only the last offering of the fire's magical vision. The coals in the firepit glowed brightly, no more than coals now; the visions were fading already.

Somewhere miles away, a terrified screech rose to the sky and was abruptly cut off. A few minutes later the woman heard the rapid beat of wings again. She stepped outside the door of her hut and saw the huge shadow of the dragon winging away to the north, something clutched in one of his mighty talons.

She was satisfied with this night's vision. She addressed the rapidly diminishing form.

"Your time will also come, this I have seen."

Thus was uttered the Prophecy.
