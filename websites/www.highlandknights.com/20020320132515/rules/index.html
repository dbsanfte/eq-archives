1. The highest expectation is to have fun and enjoy your adventures in Norrath. A member of Highland Knights can do this without kill stealing, training other citizens of Norrath with mobs, or doing anything else that might prevent others from having fun or enjoying their adventures.

2. You must protect your clansmen at all times, putting their needs ahead of your own.

3. You must be hospitable to all strangers. Always giving a hearty welcome and helping them in any way you can.

4. You must be honest and always have fair dealings. Your name and the name of the clan must always be one of honor.

1. Any member of the guild can organize a guild raid. For a raid to be considered as a Guild Raid, it must be approved by an officer.  (This is as simple as posting a request on the Message board, from there it will be approved.) If a grouping is considered a guild raid then the guild looting rules apply.

2. During the raid, there's NO useless chatter. Someone not paying attention could cause the rest of us to get killed and destroy the raid.

3. No extended periods of AFK. You gotta pee? Pee before hand. Need to walk the dog? Walk the dog before hand. You need to go hang out with the wife/girlfriend? Put her to bed beforehand. Holding up 20 people for your laundry isn't cool.

4. You must pay attention to the raid organizer at all times and keep up with what the organizer says. If you don't keep up, and you get left behind, you might a) die, b) start a train and kill us all and everyone else in the zone, c) lose your corpse. All 3 of these options are unacceptable.

5. Loot will be random AFTER the raid is over (Unless the item is NO DROP. In that case it will be rolled for immediately), for EVERYONE who can use the item. It will be done by a master looter, who will then random the item by use of one roll. The master looter will be the leader of each group. To avoid confusion, the master looter will deposit his/her own items in the bank prior to the hunt.

6. There will be no bickering/arguing about anything up to, and including loot. The raid leader's say is final.

7. There will be one person assigned as the assist person. To make it as easy as possible, create a hotkey that says "/assist ". Keep hitting that hotkey as soon as the assist person says that a target has been acquired.

8. The message board will be used to organize the groups beforehand. If a raid is announced, please post a message stating your class/level to register for the raid.

9. During a raid, guild chat will only be used by the leaders of the groups. All other conversation must take place in group chat.

1. Highland Knights are a "By Recommendation" only membership guild. This means that no one will be tagged simply because they want to join the guild. Any player wishing to become a member must first find a "Sponsor". Any current regular member of the guild may be a sponsor. Once a sponsor has been found, the Sponsor must contact an officer and make his sponsor status known.

2. The recruit must be within the guild's level requirement. The minimum level that may be recruited into the clan is currently level 20.

3. Grouping - As a general rule I would say about 2-3 hours real time grouping is a minimum for a prospective recruit, with a good portion of that time spent in battle. The guild member who recommends a recruit for evaluation shall be the Advocate to the Guild concerning that recruit.

The items in the treasury will be stored by the guild treasurer on a "mule" character created specifically for this purpose. These items will be available for you to purchase at discounted prices if you wish and some of them are potential gifts for clansmen who show courage, loyalty, hospitality and honesty, the qualities we look for in our guild.

There will be no dues as such. Instead, funds will be generated in three ways.

1. Guild Fund Hunts. Periodically, guild fund hunts will be organized as a way to raise money for the guild while at the same time providing an opportunity for fellowship and fun. Groups will form to hunt profitable monsters with one guild officer or designated leader per group. To avoid confusion, all guild members should deposit their own coinage in the bank prior to the hunt. The groups will venture out, slay monsters, and gain experience and loot. Upon returning to town, all members should sell all loot and give the coinage to the guild officer or leader of their group, who will in turn give this coinage to the guild treasurer’s “bank” character for deposit. During fund hunts, special items found as loot should also be turned in and can be sold by the guild treasurer or stored for later use. If sold, the member who originally found the item will be given first opportunity to buy it from the guild.

2. Item Recycling. Useful items can be donated to the guild for sale to guild members. When the purchasing member outgrows the item, he or she is expected to donate the item back to the guild to be resold to another member. This gives members a chance to buy needed items at discounted rates, while at the same time raising money for the guild and promoting fellowship among members. The donating clansmen will, of course, receive recognition for his or her generosity.

3. Clan Coin Donations. Any donations made by a clansmen will be posted in the clan forum in recognition for his or her generosity.

Gift = Item awarded to a guild member for demonstrating courage, loyalty, hospitality and honesty. This could be via an accomplishment at a guild raid or quest. An item can also be gifted for a prize in a competition or given to a member under extreme circumstances such as a lost corpse. Gifted items are yours to keep, use, and sell as you wish.

Guild member may nominate members of the guild, and may also nominate Non-members, for acts deemed worthy of recognition. Non members will be officially recognized by the guild master. (example: non member grouped with members risks his life to save our members in a combat situation. This is Highland Knights behavior, and recognizing the individual may also become a good recruiting tool)

Every Clan member has the ability to become an officer. Officers are selected from the clan amongst people who have shown themselves to be courageous, loyal, hospitably and honest. Every officer has an important job they are responsible for. If an officer position becomes available in the clan, nominees will be selected and then voted upon by the clan.

The officer roster is dynamic, and any officer, can be decided to be replaced, can ask to be replaced so on.

People can talk to the guildmaster if they have interest to become officer and have tasks that need to be done/responsibilities to accomplish

Non-officer members are just as important in this clan. We encourage all to take an active role in the clan, for you ARE the clan. When important issues arise, members will indeed be an integral part of decisions that have to be made.
