Outline of application procedure: (updated 03/09/04)


1. Person interested in joining the guild fills out an application after reading our charter and all information located on our guild information page.

2. The application is received and reviewed. If the application is turned down a letter will be sent to that applicant within several days.

3. If application is accepted, the applicant will be assigned to a mentor and considered a recruit. The mentor will contact the recruit and help the recruit learn about the guild.

4. After the mentor finds the recruit worthy and the recruit has obtained 5 sponsors from the guild to support his/her entry, an Officer of the guild will be contacted to conduct an interview.

5. Recruits will soon after receive word if they have been accepted or not.

6. Recruits once initiated into the guild, will remain on probation for 45 days. During this time if no complaints were filed against the member and the mentor has given an acceptable recommendation, they will no longer be a recruit.

Frequently Asked Questions about joining the guild:


1) Who can join the guild.....what race, class, ect..?

Any Race and/or Class can join the guild, as long as they swear to act as a Knight and follow the code of a Chivalrous Knight (CHARTER) at all times.


2) Is there a minimum level requirement in order to join the Knights of Reverence?

Yes. Level 50 is the current minimum level to be eligible for entry into the guild.

3) How do I join the guild?

Anyone interested in joining the guild MUST fill out an application located on our website.

4) What is a Mentor and why do I need one?

A mentor is an active member of the guild who have volunteered to help new recruits during their transition into guild life. The mentors will answer any questions and make sure he/she spends some time with the the recruit. Upon receiving a good recommendation from the Mentor and have gotten 5 members of the guild to sponsor them for entry into the guild, they will be recommended for officer interview by the Mentor.. Also remember a mentor is not only there for recruits prior to initiation, but are there to help and answer questions when needed.


5) What is the interview all about?

During the interview we will be covering the following basics:

a. To tell you a bit more about our guild than is posted anywhere.

b. To make sure that we are the type of guild you are looking for.

c. To make sure that you are the type of member we are looking for.

d. To answer any Questions you may still have.



6) I think I may have a special situation as far as recruiting goes. Who do I contact?

By all means feel free to contact Salanie at Salanie@kor-guild.com.
