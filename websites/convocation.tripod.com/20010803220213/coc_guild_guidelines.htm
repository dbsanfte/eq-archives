The Original Guild Charter

 

The Guild Goals .

  Our main goal is to improve the quality of the game for all our members by providing an in-game community much similar to a large family, if you are having more fun and enjoying Everquest as a guild member then that means we are meeting our main goal and thus making the game much more fun than it is, we do not like to burden our members with too many rules as the whole point to playing a game is to have fun and HAVING to do this or that can get annoying fast.  We all have a good reputation for helping others, though not an obligation, most of our members will gladly give their lives to save each other and even help out strangers in need, if you are someone who thinks twice about risking their precious XP for another guild member then this guild is definitely not for you.  We also help each other obtain valuable gear and very much dislike people who will lie and take gear from the guild only to sell it or use it for their own ends. Another of our goals though it has proven to be very difficult is to promote role-playing in the game, we don't role-play when talking to each other to facilitate communication but we all try to be a little bit more in character when talking to people outside of the guild.  In closing if you want a large group of friends to share and enjoy Everquest much more and you are someone who shares our goals then please feel free to let us know in game that you want to join us, and to everyone who is already a member, thank you for making the guild such a good community!.

 

 

The Ranks and Responsibilities.

- Guild Leader role and responsibilities.

  The Guild Leader has the ability to add and remove members from the guild as well as declare wars vs. another guild (which we don't do) or disband the guild. The Guild Leader has all the responsibilities of the Officers plus having to keep track of everything that goes on in the guild and deal with it appropriately, if any member has a personal issue with any other member he/she should first talk to the Guild Leader to find a solution, pretty much anything guild related is a responsibility for the Leader.

- Officers role and responsibilities.

  The officers have the ability to add members to the guild, the guild is a democracy and for all guild decisions all of us have to agree for it pass, deciding if an applicant joins the guild, changing rules or policies, serving as referees when two or members have a problem, organizing events and keeping up with what is going on in the guild are tasks of the officers.

- Becoming an Officer in the guild.

  The Officers and Leader talk a lot as you can see in our message board, any member who is cooperative, gets along with everyone and acts when it is needed, and most important of all that we know quite well, will very likely be asked to help out the guild by taking on the responsibilities of an officer, this is how Fizzlefrazzle, Filiztyn and Neonod became officers.  Nobody should expect to become an officer simply because of their experience level or time as a member in the guild, most important of all you don't ask to become an Officer but rather we will ask you to become one.

 

 

New Members Guidelines.

  As a new member you have to do a number of things, don't worry you don't have to pay any fees or anything like that, but there are things that you are expected to know and do, so here they are :

 

  1. Send in your email address a list of all the characters you play including race, level and class, who invited you into the guild and what you expect from the guild and any miscellaneous information to Gorme at his e-mail address gorme_stouthead@hotmail.com, also please specify if You want any of the information sent to be confidential to the other guild members.  You MUST send in your information within a reasonable amount of time, if you don't we can't contact you by e-mail and we will assume you do not care enough about the guild to be in it, which means you could be considered for an Officer vote to be removed.
  2. Introduce yourself to the guild through guild chat to as many members as you can that you don't know yet, socialize, use the chat a lot, be polite (no YELLING or bad words please) and make sure everyone knows who you are, with so many members and each having from 2 to 5 characters it is hard to tell who is actually a new member. Participation is the key.  Any NEW member who rarely uses guild chat and does not take the time to socialize with other members at all will get our attention fast, what's the point of joining a guild to play separate from it?  We don't want people who like to play alone all the time in this guild.
  3. If you have any questions, or if you cant find anyone to team up with you please ask in guild chat as that is what it is for and the whole point in being in a community is to participate.
  4. If you need an item or gear the guild has enough resources through each individual that it is conceivable to get good gear when you REALLY need it simply by asking, what I mean by good gear I am not talking about an executioners axe or full crafted armor, but rather anything from leather armor and a shield to a  full set of banded or bronze plate mail and a fine steel weapon perhaps.  Do not expect any gear from anyone, nobody HAS to give you any gear, do not be pushy and be warned if someone thinks they can get free gear from our guild then leave they are in the wrong place!!!!! NO BEGGING ALLOWED!!!
  5. If you have a problem with another member in the guild, please first contact me Gorme either by email at gorme_stouthead@hotmail.com or within the game using the /tell command instead of accusing and pointing fingers in the guild chat channel, we don't expect this to happen often but people are people and some just don't get along with others, we will do our best to resolve the issues.
  6. Voice out any suggestions or comments in guild chat or directly to me Zumlin, we have plans to raid dungeons, do some kind of event, etc but we have not gotten too much into those sorts of things, so let us know what you think, have fun!!!
  7. Learn the Guild Rules and most importantly have fun!
 

 

 

The Guild Rules.

  This are the Guild Rules as of April 18th, 2000.  This rules are not set in stone and in fact will have to change constantly, they are here mainly to serve as guidelines and help organize the Guild, please feel free to comment on what you think about any or all of the rules as they are meant to make YOUR time in the guild more enjoyable.

 

  1. No begging :  No member should beg for anything in the game, nobody has to give you anything nor do anything for you, constantly begging in-game will get an Officer vote on you to be removed.

  2. No Racism of any kind :  This is a pretty clear one, we play to have fun not to bring the Real World issues into the game, if you have a serious problem with ANY particular Ethnic/religious group (i.e. you can't get along with them) you don't belong in CoC, any non-game racial/ethnic/religious insults from a member will get you removed from the guild with no vote at all.

  3. Bad Language :  This one is more of courtesy to other members rather than what you can or can't do, everyone uses bad words, it is just a matter of knowing when to not use them.  As there are members who will get offended by them do not use bad language in guild chat, an occasional one in extreme situations is understandable (i.e. You just died twice in a row) but a constant use of them even when asked by any other member to stop becomes harassment and will get you an Officer vote to be removed.

  4. Double Guilding :  You may have other characters in other guilds, however you may not hide who your primary character is from this guild this way (your primary is your Highest level character on the server), this is important due to our looting policy, if you try to actually deceive the guild in this manner you will get a very serious Officer vote for removal.

  5. Anonymous and Roleplay :  We do not hide who we are as it hampers the process of getting to know each and every single member in the guild.  You must do your best to NOT use Anonymous modes in-game as they make you not show up when other members use the " /who all guild convocation " command, ROLEPLAY mode is a lot more desirable than ANONYMOUS mode as it does show your guild name (but it still makes it so you DON'T show up with the /who  all guild command) however it is understandable that some classes or just due to your level you have to be Anonymous in heavy traffic zones (Shamans, High level Clerics and Druids) so if you must be anonymous then use the Roleplay mode. 

  6. Guild grouping :  If there is a member needing a group and they can get XP and you are able to invite him/her YOU must do so (if you are group leader for example), a large reason of being in the guild is to be able to team up more easily, if we start excluding guild members from camping a room in Lower Guk or Sol B because we prefer other non guild members players, then what's the point of being in the guild? Use common sense of course, a full group made up of only warriors is not a wise idea.  Again if a member repeatedly excludes other guild members from his/her group they will get an officer vote for removal.

  7. Normal Loot :  In Guild Groups* we use auto-split on and either take turns of do a FFA (free for all) which means everyone just uses common sense looting and loots without asking.  We absolutely do not tolerate greed among members, hearing somebody have an argument about who looted that Fine Steel Weapon will really get the officers attention quickly.

  8. Special Loot :  In Guild Groups* special items (Sword of Ykesha, SMR, etc...) go to the member in the group (or groups if there is more than one of them participating on a raid) who's primary character (his/her highest level in the server) actually NEEDS and will use the item, if there are two or more members in the group/groups who need it, then a lotto only between those who needed will be used.  If no participating  members NEED the item then a full group lotto should be used.  NEVER should a "twink" character receive an item that another members primary character needs and participates in the group/groups unless everyone agrees to it.  A side not here :  Deceiving the guild as to who is your primary character will get a very serious Officers vote for removal.

  9. Non guild members on guild groups : It is every single members responsibility to point out how our looting policy works BEFORE something is dropped, most players expect a lotto for items, but as you know that is our last resort for distributing items, so make sure to point out to them what is going on, and more importantly make sure you have an idea who is going with you if you are planning on camping a valuable item, imagine 5 guild members being "ninja looted" out of some valuable by someone we tried to be helpful with and invite him/her into the group.  This rule basically means that if there are non-guild members in a Guild Group then an understanding of looting should be reached BEFORE a special item is found.

  10. Secondary Characters : You can have secondary characters guilded at any time by any officer, however you MUST constantly inform the other guild members who you are, if you make secondary characters you must abide by the looting rules in guild groups also.


    ---------- REFERENCE -------------

    * A guild group is one that has more than 50% of guild members, 2 out 3, 3 out 5 or 4 out of 6 is a guild group.
    Also remember that if more than one guild group is participating all groups treat themselves as one for looting purposes.

 

 

The Guild's Original Charter.

  Our Goal in Norrath is to spread chaos, conflict and hate. Rules are followed only by those weak enough to not be able to follow their own path, this makes for weak individuals who rely on others for protection, when the time for all out war comes to Norrath all of this weaker individuals will be the first to fall to the more powerful and organized factions throughout Norrath, by promoting conflict the most fit to rule will all come forth and make themselves known to us, when the time for the last great conflict arrives we will know who will be our rivals and we will know their strengths and weaknesses and defeat them to claim the most precious jewel that is rightfully ours, Norrath, just as our Gods have made us foresee us throughout time.

  Our group consists of members from many different races and trades throughout Norrath; from the dark and dangerous swamps of Innothule to the glorious Elven city of Felwithe to the underground Dwarven city of Kaladin and the dark and secluded Neriak; from the fiercest of warriors to the more arcane magic users and necromancers to our precious priests who guide us on the paths our Gods want us to be. We are everywhere.

  Our role as the Convocation of Chaos is to gather as much power and wealth as possible before the last great conflict arrives and to make it known throughout the lands that we intend to use this power for our own purposes creating tension and conflict, it is us who will bring forth the tide of war and it is us who will be victorious.

Here follows the basic beliefs that all of our members share:

  1. All rules and orders from any kingdom or organization other than the Convocation of Chaos itself are irrelevant, to follow them would be to betray our beliefs and weaken us. We make our own rules and we make all others follow them.
  2. All items and artifacts we gain will have priority within our group before anyone else, this does not mean we give away our valuables to other members, it simply means that we make sure to provide our whole group with needed gear before selling it to outsiders.
  3. We share information only within our group, knowledge is power, to give it away is to make us weaker and abandon our goals. 
  4. We do not begin wars with other groups without careful planning, a sudden conflict without preparation in our part is foolishness and will only serve to prove us inferior to others.
  5. We share our skills and trades with our own group for the minimal fee, we do not charge our members and they wont charge us anything other than materials needed, the more wealth we accumulate as a group the stronger we will be.
  We help train and improve all our weaker members without regard for the trainers personal gain other than the gain that member being trained will provide to the group, it is critical we all stay within the same skill levels and abilities.

This is all that is needed to be known to be a part of us,

for glory and wealth and power and fame,

Zumlin Axethrower.
