What is The Verant Barrier?
Is there a way around the Barrier?
YES!  With the addition of chat channels, Verant really has allowed a  loophole for the small, family style guild to function without giving up their core beliefs.   We may move at a slower cycle than other Raiding style guilds, but we do have progress…we do move…and we do conquer in areas that Verant seemingly has not planned for us to have success in.
YES!  As we mature in real life, so do our in game characters.  We learn that working hard and together far out weighs the fast, furious and at all costs mentality.
YES!  The Verant barrier is only a perception – and if we don’t let it become our reality, then we can move mountains.  Verant designed this game seemingly with the idea that there is strength in numbers… But we know different.  The real strength lies not in the quantity of members we have but rather in the quality of our family bonds.
YES!  Because when it’s all said and done, WE HAVE THE WILL!!  And where there is WILL there is a WAY!!

  The City of Mist story
When Carpe first started going to City of Mist, we did everything wrong.  If we were lucky, we might have cleared the courtyard and got some Shaman scrolls.  Our trains were record breaking, our CRs lasted for hours.  But the guild was relentless, and would not stop trying for the brass ring (in this case, Jade Reavers).  We didn’t know where to stand, so we fell in the water and died as the mobs pounded on us.  And let’s not forget the time we attacked Lhranc (61-63 mob)?  Our entire raid wiped out in less time than it takes to type “RUN!”

Today, Carpe OWNS this zone.  We no longer need four groups of people to take out all 6 Black Reavers - we can do it easily with two, and sometimes just one.  And we can pull all 6 Black Reavers and have to wait till the next spawn cycle starts.  Not only that, so many people in Carpe could LEAD this raid - why?  Because you have gone to so many of them that you know instinctively what to do - where to stand, when to attack, when to back off, when to pull more mobs.  And we do it without having the “perfect group.”  Because of our efforts, we have been rewarded with Jade Reavers.

City of Mist is a SUCCESS STORY for Carpe Diem.  This is what you are all capable of.
And that is only the beginning!!!

The Hole
When Carpe first started going to The Hole, we spent most of the raid just trying to get in between the rock and the hard place!  No rogue for picking the lock… no key… and most of us having never been there before!  We spent months learning how to “hug the wall”, listen to commands, and work as a team.  Our corpse recoveries always lasted far longer than the actual raid.  But the guild was determined to not let this zone conquer us!  We died and died and died some more – but we learned.  We slowly but surely started to see that we could not rely on brute strength to take out the many mobs – we needed more than that.  We had to plan, practice, research, and plan some more.

Today, while Carpe doesn’t own this zone, YET, it doesn’t strike fear into us like it once did.   If anything we have become more determined to make this another success story for our guild. We have come a long way as a guild due in large part to our efforts in this zone.  We have learned that with patience and persistence, anything IS possible!

Our Needs
  September 2, 2002
1.  Let’s focus on our guild relations
Let’s make sure we are having fun again in Norrath, and that our guild has high guild morale.  People should be able to do what they want while in Norrath - no mandatory raiding.
Structure Official Raiding - Max official raids scheduled monthly are 2 Planes Raids, 2 Major Epic Mobs, 4 Velious Faction/Quests or Special Needs, and 2 Good Will Events; no more than 2 raids per day scheduled on the calendar, impromptu raids encouraged.
YOUR 2 Days a week - No official raids will be scheduled on these two days - members are encouraged to do whatever it is they like best.
Focus on our Members - people want experience - let’s give them that.  Not every event has to be about loot. Group together, talk, horse around, and soon exp will only be an added bonus to the true interaction we share.
Officers especially encouraged  to attend scheduled raids (if they can – real life still comes first!)
Chain of Command - bring issues to the officers - don’t expect us to all be mind readers.  Carpe Diem is YOUR guild. Carpe is now and always will be what it’s members make of it.  And officers – be sure to post about concerns or ideas… we cannot read your minds either!!
Theme of Carpe
The "theme" of Carpe is one that you will find repeated throughout life.
You will get out of Carpe as much (or as little) as you put into it.
This means:
    -if you want events - attend the ones that are planned and help plan the ones you want.
    -if you want people to help you obtain items then you need to help others obtain items too.
    -if you want people to speak to you in guild chat or tells then you have to return the kindness.
    -if you want to be seen and heard and acknowledged be sure that you are seeing and hearing
      and acknowledging those around you.
    -if you want respect for your abilities you need to be sure that you respect others for theirs.
And if you feel that you ARE doing all of these things and yet you are still unhappy, then you need to seek out an officer or a guild leader or someone that you feel comfortable with and you need to ask them what it is that you can do to create the atmosphere within YOUR family that you want. For that really IS the duty of each member - to help foster the sort of atmosphere that they are happy in. And the reason we are all in the same guild is because our idea of what sort of atmosphere that is is very much the same.
Carpe is now and always will be what it's members make of it.
