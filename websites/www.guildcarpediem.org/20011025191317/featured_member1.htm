I started my online gaming experience with Ultima Online quite sometime after it was released. A real life friend purchased it and asked me to join him in the world of Britannia, and the game was fun.....for a time. I soon realized how much of an impact other people had on each other in this type of game. Hard earned gold that took many hours to obtain was taken away in seconds by greedy children that knew how to "hack" the trade window. I was stolen from, ruthlessly killed and laughed at by other players that were far stronger than I. My account was quickly cancelled with an oath to never return to this type of game.

When Everquest was released....I laughed! Their was simply no way I was returning to the type of game where people preyed on each other. I had been looking for something, anything, where people HELPED each other. It didn't exist. All people cared about was killing each other and taking things that had taken others days or even weeks to acquire. More power to them....but I was not going to be involved.

It was not until later that I realized how the Everquest servers were set up. This threw new light on the situation and after some very careful research about this game I decided to give it a try. My friend purchased it as well and together we first set foot upon Norrath. At first things went well but after achieving a few levels together we found we would be needing others to group with in order to progress. Two more friends of ours decided to buy the game and join in our adventures and once again the game became enjoyable. It was something to look forward to in the evening hours =)

We had no idea what lay ahead for us in the weeks ahead. We found out in order to fight successfully each of us had to be there. The warrior (tank), cleric, enchanter and wizard. If the warrior had a date that night we found there was no reason to play at all as we simply could not survive. The next night the enchanter was absent and we were overwhelmed by sheer numbers of MoBs and slaughtered. Simply put we could not all play at the same time, therefore why play at all. We simply could not achieve anything unless all members were there to contribute. We attempted to add other members to our group but we leveled far too slowly for one of them and she decided to travel another road. Yet another member was forced to quit Everquest due to lack of time to play. Even my friends, with the exception of one, became casual gamers only playing once or twice a week and then one of them left for good. I became forced to rely on pickup groups in order to gain any experience and most of the time all these groups did is argue and bicker among themselves. The game was no longer fun =(

However, something had happened while fighting in a pickup group near the spires in South Karana. Something had happened to me that was very strange. I had found a friend. As most of you know I don't post very much and I talk even less so most people find me to be very anti-social. I have had some really bad experiences with people so I have found it best to just not talk much. My newfound friend seemed not to mind and unlike me it seemed everyone in Norrath was her friend. She was known and loved by all. I had met Rainfeather.

In the days ahead, she introduced me to two other wonderful people with whom I would begin to share a strong friendship with. Their names were Supajeenyus and Melodious Sunnyskies. I readily accepted an invitation to a guild they were starting and asked that my friend, (the one that had always been there for me) be allowed to join too.
