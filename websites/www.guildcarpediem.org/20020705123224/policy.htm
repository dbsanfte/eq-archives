Theme of Carpe

The "theme" of Carpe is one that you will find repeated throughout life.

Quote:

You will get out of Carpe as much (or as little) as you put into it.

This means:

    -if you want events - attend the ones that are planned and help plan the ones you want.
    -if you want people to help you obtain items then you need to help others obtain items too.
    -if you want people to speak to you in guildchat or tells then you have to return the kindness.
    -if you want to be seen and heard and acknowledged be sure that you are seeing and hearing
      and acknowledging those around you.
    -if you want respect for your abilities you need to be sure that you respect others for theirs.

And if you feel that you ARE doing all of these things and yet you are still unhappy, then you need to seek out an officer or a guild leader or someone that you feel comfortable with and you need to ask them what it is that you can do to create the atmosphere within YOUR family that you want. For that really IS the duty of each member - to help foster the sort of atmosphere that they are happy in. And the reason we are all in the same guild is because our idea of what sort of atmosphere that is is very much the same.

Carpe is now and always will be what it's members make of it.

Each officer is responsible for making sure that Carpe adheres to the policies that have been set forth and the general ideals behind what we stand for.

Most importantly - all officers must work together and with the guild leaders.

 

RAID ETIQUETTE

1. First and foremost, be on time. It is highly recommended that you get there at least a half an hour before raid time to make setting up groups easier and possibly send in a scouting group ahead of time. We have plenty of porters to help get people to raids. However, if no porters are on, it is your responsibility to get to the raid, whether that means donating for an outside-the-guild port, or running it the old fashioned way.  Please do not come on at the scheduled raid time and expect someone to come fetch you. It's not going to happen.  The event will have an official starting time called by the leader - that means that unless the leader says so, no others may join after that time.

2. When you get to the appointed meeting spot, keep your mouth shut and your ears (eyes I suppose in this world we type in) open. Have patience with those setting up the raid, and show some respect. No wandering around trying to get in some exp while we wait, no dueling, no goofing off in general. If you guys want play days, we can set those up. Raids are when things get down to business.

3. Use the designated chat channel for the raid. We may have guests from outside the guild on the raid, and they need to be kept just as informed as we are. Even if the raid or event is only going to be Carpe only, it helps to have all directions given in the designated chat channel with questions being asked in guild (if only Carpes are attending). I would prefer that if groups have been formed, you direct your questions to your Group Leader as they may have an answer for you without involving the entire raid. Do not hound the Raid Leader or your Group Leader with tells. They have enough to deal with during these situations. That being said, if you have a question please ask it. Better to be bothered with a question than to have to do an extensive or even a single corpse run.

4. Once groups are called, buffing will start. Do not start hounding the chanter for clarity, or the cleric for symbol, or anything else. If you are a buffer, and have questions as to what buffs are needed for the current situation, ask. Otherwise, get buffing. If someone misses a buff after it seems that everyone is ready, let your Group Leader know. They are the ones that will keep the raid leader apprised of the readiness of the group.

5. Know your role! We have an entire section dedicated to knowing what your role in a raid should be. If you have questions about what you should be doing, ask ahead of time. As always, direct questions to your Group Leader first.

6. Pay attention! Nothing is more frustrating than having to repeat instructions five times because someone is not paying attention to the task at hand. Listen closely when the plans for the raid are being laid out. If you have questions, direct them to your Group Leader. If we work together, things will go a lot more smoothly.

7. Do not go AFK for extended periods of time. If you need to go afk, let your Group Leader know. GL's let the raid leader and their group know if they need a quick afk.

8. Do not train the raid. If you are pulling, and think you've bitten off more than the raid can handle, die. Plain and simple. Do not bring the entire mess right on top of the rest of us. We should have clerics available to rez you and get you right back in the raid. The death of one is a lot easier to recoup that the wipe out of an entire raid.

9. Do NOT whine.  Period.  Plain and simple.  I do not want to hear someone whining that they are not getting exp.  If you want exp, go find an exp group and grind, that's not what we are raiding for. Loot? I don't even want to hear any complaining about it.  ML's will be assigned for each and every group. What does that mean? That means that one person per group will be looting the kills that their group got the exp for. The only loot that will be given out during any raid will be No Drops. Droppables will be dealt with at the end of the raid unless otherwise stated by the Raid Leader. Ninja looting will not be tolerated. In general, Carpe's loot policy is Need Before Greed. If any loot rules will be in effect other than our standard NBG policy, they will be stated at the beginning of the raid.

Guild Chat

Guildchat should be used in such a manner as to keep all of the ideals of Carpe in mind.

Friendly chatter is encouraged but please maintain dignity and respect in your actions toward guild mates.

Cursing is allowed (please use /filter if you do not want to see cursing) but with extreme moderation.

Slandering a fellow guild mate is not allowed under any circumstances nor is expressing sentiments of hate, prejudice or overt and continuous sexual content.

At all times, guildchat should be respectful. Playful chatter is encouraged so long as it does not at some point become heated arguing.

How do you know if your idea of playful chattering has gone too far? Others in the guild are trying to change the subject. The tone of the entire conversation has changed from fun to unease.

As a general rule, guild discussion of an idea is great. But when it boils down to just two or so people arguing in guild chat about something the rest of the guild either does not want to participate in or has already attempted to change the subject on, then those two people should take it to tells.

As many of us know, this game is for relaxing and having fun. How fun is it to log into the game and watch as people continually argue over every comment mentioned? How relaxin is it to see other guild members talk down to one another? How enjoyable is it to be forced into the position of either turning off guildchat entirely to avoid a few who refuse to show respect for the entire guild?

If any guild member has an issue about the content of guildchat they are to:

a: Take it up with that member IN TELLS in a civil manner (it is however, appropriate for guild members/officers to SAY in guildchat to take it to tells...if the offending party does not, send them a private tell, otherwise see point b)

b: If resistance is met, contact an officer in game or email the guild leader immediately upon logging. You are responsible for maintaining your OWN dignity in all matters of guildchat. Do not continue to participate in negative modes of guildchat...contact an officer.

NOTE: If you exhibit disgraceful behavior in response to disgraceful behavior you can be subject to similar consequences.

 

Guild Bank

 A member may not check out an item until he or she has been in the guild for at least 2 weeks. Guild acceptance date is determined from the date that you register your character on the guild roster.

If an exception is to be made, it will only be made by the guild leaders.

A member my not donate to the guild bank until he or she has been in the guild for at least 2 weeks.

The guild bank is for the benefit of all Carpe members. If there are items within the bank, which you feel would benefit you, please post to the forum in the HELPING HANDS section.  Aamon is the guild banker and will do everything within his power to get the item to those who are in need.

Please remember that all items donated to the guild bank are property of Carpe Diem and will be remain guild property even if loaned out. The bank works off of the honor system. If you ask to be loaned an item, it is expected that when you no longer need the item, you will return it to the bank so that it may benefit another member in need.

Respect

Manners and respect are one of the cornerstones in Carpe Diem . All members will behave in an honorable way and show fellow members respect. All members are required to treat all other non-guild players with similar respect. If there is an issue in which a guild member feels that there is a lack of mutual respect between other members or other non-guild players, they are required to take the matter to an officer or to the Guild Leader.

All guild members should keep in mind that: Your disgrace is the guild's disgrace. Your honor is the guild's honor. Try to, as much as possible, to take the high road in your dealings with players with whom you have a disagreement - regardless of it they are guildmates or allies or just other players.

Porting

Being able to port is a class ability. Being a druid or wizard means that you will be asked often for ports by both guild members and non guild members alike. While I cannot set policy for non guild members, I will clearly state for the record the policy for Carpe members.

1. Porting guild members is not required. Just because your character has the ability to port and your wear the guild tag does not mean that you are now a slave to the guild and all members who want a port.

2. Polite requests for porting should be honored when it is convenient for the person doing the porting. We are a family and family should help each other out. But once again, it is not required.

3. Abuse of members who can port will not be tolerated. Just because a guild druid or wizard is on does not mean that you (the non porting member) can badger and beg and plead until you get your way. If you ask in guild for a port and are told to either wait or that it is not possible at this time, there is no reason to be rude, angry, and/or argumentative. And constantly asking to be ported here and there is a form of abuse. You can tell in guild chat if a guild porter is busy or not. If they are, then seek other ways to travel. Take a boat. Run. Walk. Ask a non guild porter. Send tells to our allied guilds. No matter what, be respectful...to our guild, other guilds, and total strangers. Porting is not owed to you for any reason.

4. For those members who can port and are willing to do so, charging a guild member is considered poor taste.

Resurrections

2. Polite requests for resurrecting should be honored when it is convenient for the person doing the resurrecting. We are a family and family should help each other out. But once again, it is not required.

3. Abuse of members who can resurrect will not be tolerated. Just because a guild cleric is on does not mean that you (the non resurrecting member) can badger and beg and plead until you get your way. If you ask in guild for a resurrection and are told to either wait or that it is not possible at this time, there is no reason to be rude, angry, and/or argumentative. And constantly asking to be resurrected is a form of abuse. You can tell in guild chat if a guild cleric is busy or not. If they are, then seek other ways of being reunited with your corpse. We all would love to get our experience back each time we die, but sometimes you take the good with the bad and just be more careful next time. No matter what, be respectful...to our guild, other guilds, and total strangers. Resurrecting is not owed to you for any reason.

4. For those members who can resurrect and are willing to do so, charging a guild member is considered poor taste.

5. Resurrecting for guild members will only be done once a character is over the level of 25 unless the cleric is willing to do so or you have no hope of getting back to where your corpse is located.

Example of when you will not be resurrected: In these cases, it is assumed that the cleric is not in the same zone you died or near the same zone. If the cleric IS in the same zone or near enough to the zone, you may ask but it is not required that they do so.

For instance, if your level 8 character dies in Erudin for whatever reason, and they are bound there, do not call on the guild cleric. Get your corpse. Go kill something and get your exp back.

If your level 8 character dies in Erudin and you are bound in Butcher Block, do not call on the guild cleric. Take a boat. Get a port. Run. Walk. Get your corpse. Go kill something and get your exp back.

If your level 15 character falls off the ramp on the way to HH, do not call a guild cleric.

I hope that you are getting the picture of when not to call on a guild cleric for a resurrection.

There are times when exceptions are made - and if you feel that your case is an exception then ask in guild chat if a guild cleric could help you. If you are told no, there will be no begging, guilt tripping, or tantrum throwing. You are not owed a resurrection.

Consequences

1. You will be given a verbal warning via a private tell stating how you have violated the rules.
2. The incident will be recorded in the officer's section of the forum entitled Time Out.
3. An email will be sent to you with a listing of all the rules as they appear on the site. You are to respond to that email letting us know that you have indeed re-read the rules.

Second infraction: (Within a 30 day period from the first infraction)

1. You will be given a verbal warning via a private tell stating how you have violated the rules.
2. The incident will be recorded in the officer's section of the forum entitled Time Out.
3. You will be contacted by either Melo or Supa in game and via email to discuss why you are continuing to break guild policy.
4. You will be placed on probation.

Third infraction: (Within a 30 day period from the first infraction)

1. You will be given a verbal warning via a private tell stating how you have violated the rules.
2. The incident will be recorded in the officer's section of the forum entitled Time Out.
3. You will be contacted by either Melo or Supa in game and via email to discuss why you are continuing to break guild policy.
4. You will be disbanded from the guild.

Notes:

If you have a second infraction that is more than 30 days apart from your first infraction, you will be treated as if it is your first infraction. However, if a pattern develops, or the infractions are severe enough, appropriate actions will be taken.
