"Caaaaarpe...Caaarpe Diem.... Seize the day, boys.... make your lives extraordinary...." Dead Poets Society

Carpe Diem – Seize the Day – is not a casual philosophy but more of a command of the soul.  Living in the moment is what it is all about.  Life is short and in the end, happiness is only what we make or do not make of it. Carpe Diem is about actively pursuing that happiness rather than sitting back and waiting for it to find you. Carpe Diem is a place to create, discover and have fun -- that is its mission. It is a place to discover, or perhaps rediscover, the still small voice within, that voice of inner knowing that so easily goes unheard in the din of daily life. Carpe Diem is a place to bond with people who care. It is a place for contemplation more than competition, meditation rather than agitation, a place to revive the spirit. It is a place to have fun, to laugh both on the outside as well as the inside. It is a place to remember how to play, how to let go and experience the joy of oneself and the enjoyment of others. Courage is precisely what it takes. If you believe enough in something, it can be achieved. The power in Carpe Diem is not only the province of those with a cause, it is in everybody.  It's the strength to support someone who flies in the face of popular thought. It's the ability to grasp every opportunity that presents itself. It's the courage to feel and care.  We are all extraordinary.

What We Expect From Our Members:

First and foremost our guild is a family and every member of the guild should take pride in that fact. With that in mind, we expect all members of Carpe Diem to actively participate in the guild. Because we believe that most characters within the game of EverQuest in many ways are just a reflection of the person playing, we view our guildmates as friends. To that end, we should stop at nothing to help our guildmates out in time of need, never let our pride get in the way of another’s happiness, and above all else, show respect to each and every person not just within our guild but throughout Norrath. 

Furthermore we believe that Honor (Respect, Admiration, Credit, Reputation), Valor (Spirit, Bravery, Courage, Nerve, Boldness), and Loyalty (Faithfulness, Devotion, Allegiance, Trustworthiness, Reliability, Dependability) should be at the very foundation of every decision we make and everything that we do. These very basic principles are what make us strong as a guild and will make us known and respected throughout Norrath. In order to achieve these principles, one must strive to fight for justice with a fair and even hand, be willing to sacrifice -  even at the cost of ones own life, and show compassion towards those in need.

Carpe Diem Sodalitas was never founded to be a "raiding" guild.  We do not have plans to one day be "uber" in the usual sense of the word.  Our goal is to maintain a level of closeness that cannot be matched by many in Norrath.  Carpe Diem has been specifically designed to be a guild in which we can all appreciate each other for who we are and appreciate the experiences and interactions we have within the game.  Each and every one of us likes to see new places, do new things and get experience but that is not always our main focus.  "Seizing the day" means different things to different people. To some it may mean guild raids, to others it may mean just sitting somewhere and chatting all night. However, as a whole, Carpe is not a guild that focuses its attentions on raiding.  Because our philosophy is what it is, we have a closer bunch of people than most guilds who truly enjoy each others' company. Sure sometimes there are frustrations but most of the time we are experiencing them together which gives us something to bring us closer as a guild.  Will we raid? Yup! Sure we will. Will we get experience? Yup! Sure we will. Will we have a good time in the process?  Absolutely!  There will always be sights to be seen, many mobs to fight, and in time, we will see it all and do it all.  But in the meantime, it doesn't hurt to stop and smell the roses.

What We Expect From Our Officers:

What is a Guild Officer?

We see the function of a guild officer to be something of great responsibility. Being an officer is not something that someone should enter into lightly because it means always putting the needs of your guild members ahead of your own. A guild officer should be nothing more than an extension of the guild leader. This means that an officer should be intimately knowledgeable of the charter and rules of the guild. They should be made to feel comfortable in enforcing them and while they should not wear their rank on their shoulder, they should both be mindful and proud of the fact at all times. An officer should feel comfortable in assisting the leader in leading the guild and ruling with a fair and even hand. Being an officer is not a popularity contest - and having the title of "Officer" does not immediately make you popular. With that said, it is important that the officer be liked as well as respected. They should not be afraid to address issues and enforce the bylaws of the guild even if it is an unpopular decision. An officer should be able to not only take directive but give it as well. Likewise, an officer should always be aware of guild needs and wants and help to contribute in any way possible to realize them.

An officer should be mindful of the following guidelines at all times:

1. Above all else, keep in mind that Honor (Respect, Admiration, Credit, Reputation), Valor (Spirit, Bravery, Courage, Nerve, Boldness) and Loyalty (Faithfulness, Devotion, Allegiance, Trustworthiness, Reliability, Dependability) are the basis for everything that we do. This means that an officer should strive to maintain a very high level of these three traits not only within himself/herself, it also means they must strive to maintain that same level within the members of the guild.

2. Members needs should always come before an officer's needs. If a member needs something and it is a reasonable request an officer's duty requires them to drop what they are doing (if what they are doing is not guild related) and handle it. Reasonable requests are requests such as resolving disputes between members, disputes with other guilds or players and other issues that might help out a guild member in need. It does not require officers to do things such as port on demand, camp things that lower level people might not be able to get, give things that an officer might have that someone else wants etc. These acts are things that are to be done at the discretion of the officer and are not required.

3. An officer should take an active role within the guild that goes beyond what they might do as a typical member. This means actively involving ones self in guild chat, any events that the guild plans, and helping out with any special programs that the guild might have (such as guild bank etc). In the event that an officer cannot make a guild planned event, they should be sure to be very active within guild chat enticing other members to come to the event.

4. An officer should be very active on the forum. Merely checking the forum regularly is not enough. An officer should feel free to post new topics - even if it is something simple in order to help spark conversation. Since our guild is based on a family type philosophy, it is important that our family speak with each other. And it is very important that our family see that officers are very actively involved.

5. An officer should take part in all officer meetings. Granted, sometimes real life issues come into play, however, officers should make it a priority if to attend the meetings as this is one of the few chances everyone gets to handle the issues that are presented to the guild at that time. We do not have meetings all that often so it is extremely important that the officers make use of the Officer's Forum. It is our connection to each other. If there are issues that need to be addressed, ideas that could be of use to the guild, or requests for anything from each other - the Officer Forum is the place to do so. Just an hello is sometimes all that is needed.

6. An officer is a friend to the guild. This means that they are a friend to all those in the guild. An officer only needs to exercise his status as an officer or someone in a position of authority in the event of a problem. Otherwise it is the duty of each officer to strive to establish lasting friendships with other members of the guild. This includes making sure that all members feel valued and appreciated within the guild. Saying hello to them when they log in and goodnight when they log off is simply not enough. As officers, we need to try to establish a bond with each member of our family. We also need to encourage all members to actively want to establish that bond between themselves and every member of the guild.

7. An officer should feel like an extension of the guild leaders. This means that they should not be afraid to enforce rules as handed down by the leaders. Furthermore an officer should not hesitate to carry out any request given by a guild leader. An officer is expected to think for him/her self. This means that the guild leaders will rely on the best judgment of an officer and fully support the officer in any decision made by that officer.
