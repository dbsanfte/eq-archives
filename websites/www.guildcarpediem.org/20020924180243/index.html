Carpe Diem – Seize the Day – is not a casual philosophy but more of a command of the soul.  Living in the moment is what it is all about.  Life is short and in the end, happiness is only what we make or do not make of it. Carpe Diem is about actively pursuing that happiness rather than sitting back and waiting for it to find you. Carpe Diem is a place to create, discover and have fun -- that is its mission. It is a place to discover, or perhaps rediscover, the still small voice within, that voice of inner knowing that so easily goes unheard in the din of daily life. Carpe Diem is a place to bond with people who care. It is a place for contemplation more than competition, meditation rather than agitation, a place to revive the spirit. It is a place to have fun, to laugh both on the outside as well as the inside. It is a place to remember how to play, how to let go and experience the joy of oneself and the enjoyment of others. Courage is precisely what it takes. If you believe enough in something, it can be achieved. The power in Carpe Diem is not only the province of those with a cause, it is in everybody.  It's the strength to support someone who flies in the face of popular thought. It's the ability to grasp every opportunity that presents itself. It's the courage to feel and care.  We are all extraordinary.
