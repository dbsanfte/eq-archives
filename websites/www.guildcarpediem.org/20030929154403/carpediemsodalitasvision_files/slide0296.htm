What is The Verant Barrier?
 
Complaint:
“I am in a Family Guild but we never raid the big zones.  I want my Hate armor,  Velious Gear, and my Epic Weapon.  Maybe I will take my Main to an ‘uber’ guild and just leave my alt here.” 
The EQ Design Issue:
Big zones and Epics require participation from a lot of people… requires getting people together… generally requires cleared schedules… generally requires some sort of mandatory raid attendance (if not enough people attend, total party wipeouts will happen or raids get cancelled)…generally requires little to no “real life” obligations.
The Problem:
People in Family Guilds don’t like to be told what to do when they are in Norrath.  “Mandatory attendance to raids?  No way!” Nor do they want another full time job called EQ.
“I am in a Raiding Guild, and although we get to do all the great zones, people are very rude to each other - there is no sense of camaraderie or respect…I’m just another number…they have 10 shaman already…I am not needed or wanted.”
The focus for most Raiding Guilds is success - anything other than success is secondary.  There are time limitations that you have when chatting - success of these big raids requires everyone to be focused and on their toes - and the communication in Raiding Guilds is to make sure you are GTG. 
If Raiding Guilds need to communicate attack orders or group formation in a constant sea of raiding, the chatting and “warm fuzzies” becomes much less of a priority.  The focus to “get it right!” is often times taken as a personal attack.
