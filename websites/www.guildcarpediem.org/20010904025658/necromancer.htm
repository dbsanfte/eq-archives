The Dark Side

 

The art of reaching beyond the grave and commanding the dead is the primary skill of Necromancers.  They don't tra-la through the woods with the bunnies like Druids or dabble in the "let's all feel good about ourselves" magic like Enchanters.  Nope, Necromancers go straight for the throat with spells of destrcution and the dark ,evil acts of raising the dead.  That's actually not a bad career in Norrath, where there are always plenty of dead around, and plenty of creatures that need killing.

A powerful Necromancer is powerful indeed and fears very little.  At a high enough level, one could stand before the gates of a hostile city, idly chatting with anyone inclined to a little light conversation, while his pet single-handedly destroys the guards who have come to take him down.  Which is a good thing for him, because most cities will indeed be hostile to his presence, and most guards will treat him as an enemy of the state.  The lesson here is two-fold:  a Necromancer shouldn't go visiting until he's good and ready, and shouldn't get his feelings hurt when no one likes him.

Necromancers do not put a lot of weight into this whole business of "good" magic and "evil" magic.  Essentially they consider research to be a noble goal, and have no problem with the ends justifying the means...especially when the means involved are the Necromancers.  Still, the rest of the world is king of hung up on the "white" and "black" business of magic, and that's that.
