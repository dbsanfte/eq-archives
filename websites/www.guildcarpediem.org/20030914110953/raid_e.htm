RAID ETIQUETTE

1. First and foremost, be on time. It is highly recommended that you get there at least a half an hour before raid time to make setting up groups easier and possibly send in a scouting group ahead of time. We have plenty of porters to help get people to raids. However, if no porters are on, it is your responsibility to get to the raid, whether that means donating for an outside-the-guild port, or running it the old fashioned way.  Please do not come on at the scheduled raid time and expect someone to come fetch you. It's not going to happen.

2. When you get to the appointed meeting spot, keep your mouth shut and your ears (eyes I suppose in this world we type in) open. Have patience with those setting up the raid, and show some respect. No wandering around trying to get in some exp while we wait, no dueling, no goofing off in general. If you guys want play days, we can set those up. Raids are when things get down to business.

3. Use the designated chat channel for the raid. We may have guests from outside the guild on the raid, and they need to be kept just as informed as we are. Even if the raid or event is only going to be Carpe only, it helps to have all directions given in the designated chat channel with questions being asked in guild (if only Carpes are attending). I would prefer that if groups have been formed, you direct your questions to your Group Leader as they may have an answer for you without involving the entire raid. Do not hound the Raid Leader or your Group Leader with tells. They have enough to deal with during these situations. That being said, if you have a question please ask it. Better to be bothered with a question than to have to do an extensive or even a single corpse run.

4. Once groups are called, buffing will start. Do not start hounding the chanter for clarity, or the cleric for symbol, or anything else. If you are a buffer, and have questions as to what buffs are needed for the current situation, ask. Otherwise, get buffing. If someone misses a buff after it seems that everyone is ready, let your Group Leader know. They are the ones that will keep the raid leader apprised of the readiness of the group.

5. Know your role! We have an entire section dedicated to knowing what your role in a raid should be. If you have questions about what you should be doing, ask ahead of time. As always, direct questions to your Group Leader first.

6. Pay attention! Nothing is more frustrating than having to repeat instructions five times because someone is not paying attention to the task at hand. Listen closely when the plans for the raid are being laid out. If you have questions, direct them to your Group Leader. If we work together, things will go a lot more smoothly.

7. Do not go AFK for extended periods of time. If you need to go afk, let your Group Leader know. GL's let the raid leader and their group know if they need a quick afk.

8. Do not train the raid. If you are pulling, and think you've bitten off more than the raid can handle, die. Plain and simple. Do not bring the entire mess right on top of the rest of us. We should have clerics available to rez you and get you right back in the raid. The death of one is a lot easier to recoup that the wipe out of an entire raid.

9. Do NOT whine.  Period.  Plain and simple.  I do not want to hear someone whining that they are not getting exp.  If you want exp, go find an exp group and grind, that's not what we are raiding for. Loot? I don't even want to hear any complaining about it.  ML's will be assigned for each and every group. What does that mean? That means that one person per group will be looting the kills that their group got the exp for. The only loot that will be given out during any raid will be No Drops. Droppables will be dealt with at the end of the raid unless otherwise stated by the Raid Leader. Ninja looting will not be tolerated. In general, Carpe's loot policy is Need Before Greed. If any loot rules will be in effect other than our standard NBG policy, they will be stated at the beginning of the raid.
