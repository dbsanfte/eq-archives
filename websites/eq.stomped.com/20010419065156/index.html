    April 18, 2001

    I guess after every EQ website in existance leaked the news about the new expansion, Verant figured it may as well come clean, so here is an official press release I got in my mail today:

    SAN DIEGO, CA – April 18, 2001 – Sony Online Entertainment announced today its new expansion pack, EverQuest: Shadows of Luclin for the Internet’s most popular role-playing game, EverQuest. The new expansion pack will introduce EverQuest fans to an enchanting moonscape packed with new adventure zones, new items, creatures and spells, a new playable character class and more. Supported by a complete graphic overhaul, Shadows of Luclin will allow EverQuest players to witness their world in unprecedented splendor.
    “Shadows of Luclin will give EQ fans a beautiful uncharted territory to explore,” states Jeff Butler, Producer, Sony Online Entertainment. “Whether you’re a devoted long-term fan looking for new challenges or just discovering online gaming for the first time, Shadows of Luclin is filled with enough captivating adventures to support all levels of EverQuest players.”
    The Shadows of Luclin takes voyagers to a satellite hovering ominously above the world of Norrath. An area of great mystery, magic, and perplexity, Luclin is divided into a land of light, ruled by Discord, and a land of shadow, ruled by Order. Each side of this amazing world will present many new secrets, treasures and unique monsters.

    Shadows of Luclin key features:

    New adventure zones, including a new starting city
    All new, highly detailed player-character models
    Massively enhanced 3D graphics engine
    An all-new player character race
    Challenging new quests, items to trade and sell, NPCs and more

    “EverQuest: The Shadows of Luclin is due to ship in Q4 2001. For more information visit the official Shadows of Luclin Website at www.everquest.com/luclin.

    Continuing to grow with top-selling expansions The Ruins of Kunark and The Scars of Velious, EverQuest proves to be the largest fantasy world ever created online. During peak periods, more than 88,000 adventurers and dragon slayers have explored the world of Norrath simultaneously. The active global EverQuest subscriber base is comprised of players from various countries throughout the “real” world, including the U.S., England, Canada, Japan, Taiwan, Korea, Saudi Arabia, France, Italy and Australia.

    The Station (www.station.com) is the official provider of EverQuest, with software updates and news about the game, message boards and other community features, as well as an easy-to-use billing system for EverQuest’s monthly subscription fee of $9.89.

    Continuing to deliver break-through interactive gaming, Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), the online gaming division of Sony Corporation and Sony Pictures Digital Entertainment, is a worldwide leader in developing and publishing online entertainment. With more than 9 million registered users, SOE’s website www.station.com features EverQuest®, the most successful massively multiplayer online role-playing game available, and such popular attractions for non-paying, casual users as Jeopardy! Online and Wheel of Fortune Online. SOE is committed to developing cutting-edge online games and communities such as Planetside™, TanarusÔ, Sovereign™, Infantry™, Cosmic Rift™ and Star Wars® Galaxies™ and distributes online entertainment from Sony Pictures Entertainment and Sony Computer Entertainment America. SOE is headquartered in San Diego, California, with additional development studios in St. Louis and Austin.

    Also, when Verant eliminated their forums, they also unfortunately eliminated the Newbie Zone, which was a very useful area for new players to go to ask questions and find out about the game from a regular group of informed and helpful players.  Fortunately, I have been told that the regulars at the Newbie Zone took it upon themsleves to establish their own forum. Click here to check it out.

    Finally, we are ready to open a new, much anticipated, feature for the site:  [tada]  Classifieds (platinum piece based of course).  Illia has been working hard on this feature for a while and parts of it have been up for a few weeks, but now it is hopefully ready for prime time.  If you were playing around with it and added items for sale that you don't have, please go in and delete them or you will start to get e-mails about them you don't want.  (In fact, I would suggest everyone wait another day until you e-mail the sellers on the items).  Here's how it works.

    Sellers:  You sell items through the characters feature.  You must list your character as public to sell items.  In the character screen, you can add items for sale by typing in the name if the item and hitting "attach more items".  Pick the item you want to sell from the list and change it to "Put up for sale" and add a price to the slot next to it.  Leave any other items at "discard".  Then click OK and that item will be added to your for sale list.  Keep doing this for each item you want to sell.  Be sure to delete an item after you make the sale or you will keep getting e-mails on it.  Also, make sure that the e-mail address you have listed is the one you want people responding to.  When you get an e-mail from a potential buyer, you simply have to arrange the time and place in the game where you want to make the sale.

    Buyers:  If you click on For Sale in the index under equipment, you will get to a list by server of all the items people have listed for sale under their characters.  This list gets updated every hour.  If you see an item you like, click on the person's name and it will bring up the character profile with every item he or she is offering.  Above the item list is that person's e-mail.  You can arrange the time and place and final price of the sale through e-mails.  You can also find out who is wearing and selling an item from that item's individual page.  This is particularly useful if you have done a character upgrade search to find the items that are the best upgrades for the equipment you are wearing.  Right now you need to click on the pricing data link.  We will add a more intuitive one soon.

    This is a great way to get rid of items or find items you want without going through the time and effort of /auctioning them.  It is also a great way for craftsmen to advertise their wares and for players to find craftsmen on their servers.  If you are a jeweler, tailor, smith, etc., I would suggest you add all the items and prices for your craft under your character so that you can arrange to make your sales at your convienence and that of your customers.  There is also an added feature that you can see everyone who is wearing each item by clicking on the pricing data in the item screen.  So, for example, if you wanted to know everyone who has gotten their fiery defender (at least those who have made their characters public), you would click here.

    You are bound to discover a few bugs as this gets used, so feel free to send in bug reports or suggestions on how to improve the feature.  I am guessing that most of the people who posted items in the auction were looking for something more like the current classifieds.  Thus, we are going to take down the auction section.  If you posted something up there, don't worry.  We will leave it up for another week or so, but please don't post any more auctions.  Use the classifieds instead.
