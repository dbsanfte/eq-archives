Friday, April 5th, 2002 - Regarding version 3.0...

After much deliberation, I've decided that I "will try" to finish tweakEQ (eqcolors 3.0), assuming I can find the sparetime to do so, the only obstacle I have really is "the time to do it".

To touch a little on the history of this program; EQcolors version 1.0 was a simple little utility that I shared with friends, eventually I was talked into releasing it to the public, the same thing happened with version 2.0, only I took the program a little further with features, and placed a more detailed graphical user-interface on it, version 2.0 really didn't take much time to create, though I had it tested vigorously for several months, version 3.0 is a "far more ambitious undertaking", even the screenshot posted here (above) is dated, it does not reflect the scope of the program in it's current state, you'd be amazed at what all can be done in regards to settings with EverQuest now, and I'm not just referring to the eqclient.ini file (don't worry it's perfectly legal, you cannot modify anything that VI wouldn't want you to).

I've started and stopped development on version 3.0 several times now, due to the amount of time it was consuming, the complexity of it's major new features, and enhancements is a major time sink, especially for something originally intended as a "casual utility", anyhow, version 3.0 has gone far beyond version 2.0, in ways that are a hard to describe, unless you actually see it in action, it would be a real shame if I didn't at least make an attempt to finish what I started, only time will tell...

Monday, February 4th, 2002

It's official, I've made my decisions on several issues regarding the program:

EQcolors will now be known as TweakEQ; the functionality of this program has gone far beyond a simple eqclient.ini editor, it has many new advanced system-level features, that you would only expect to find in high-quality commercial configuration tools.

The program will be initially privatized, meaning that I will not release it to the public when it's finished, "which is very soon pending the Bazaar UI addition in EverQuest", I simply haven't decided what I want to do with the program yet, I know it's being used by a rather large population of EverQuest players, but I get very little feedback from them.
