abuse

Alcoholic Marriage

I said, "I do"
to midnights

full of worry
about your drunk driving

to morning
bus rides back to
work without
a wrinkle
In your
sheets

vomit
lumped beside
the kitchen
trash can

Like some gag shop
Sunday surprise

to black feelings
stirred deep into
breakfast coffee

sipped alone
at the Old South
Pancake House

while your shadow
sleeps off Saturday
on the divan
at home


Sack Man

Goodwill pile

Ode to a Bag Lady

Think of me
whenever you

empty a can
of Diet Coke

read Newsweek

or wipe with toilet paper

think of me
pawing the dank depths
of Safeway’s dumpster

for wilted lettuce
stale rolls
and molded cheese

I am the
grey bundle

trundling it all

along Miracle Mile
In a Kroger cart
looking for a

safe place
to curl

the night

while you
In your creamy satin
negligee are
home

fluffing
your

down pillows



Sanguis eius super nos et files nostros


What lizard demon lurks
clawing at your mind

what voodoo chant
flagellates your ear drums

what black goat
bleats into your brain

why do you fetal curl
against the world

is there any censer I can swing
to smoke the Lucifers out

any ashes I can sprinkle

water to wine

Jesus Christos
let me in

between your winding sheets

give me a chance
to cuddle

your soul




Candle in the Wind



black
back lit
by the sprinkle of stars
and glittered neon of St. Thomas
holds six carnivaled
cruise ships
at bay

boats full
of wrinkled
moneyed bodies
and corsaged honeymooners
swaying to the
steel band
calypsos

while I sit above
alone

on the hotel terrace
under the rustle of palm fronds
sipping endless banana daiquiris
deep into the dark
trying to escape

the paper
cut of

divorce
