Twelve-Step Program

by Ryan Williams

A self-addiction recovery group
meets twice daily in the afterworld. Fresh escapees from life
see the cliche flash,
lacking only the lose ends tied Hollywood tight.
The vision is incomplete,
like a botched piece of pottery,
denied form,
reduced to mush,
slipping through the fingers of the creator,
who's angry over free time wasted.
In twelve steps they are taught to stop whining and deal with it.


Brief biographical information: I live in Decatur, Illinois, and I am sixteen years old. For the past 36 months, I have had an irregularly appearing opinion and review column in the Decatur Herald and Review, my local newspaper (this Thursday my most recent column, a review of the movie "Waterworld", will be published). I have won or placed highly in youth writing contests on the local, state, and national levels with my short fiction, poetry, and essays.
