Tinkering Journal: Noabit
Shop Safety
This is just a note to all tinkerers, to mind basic safety principles when working with high explosives inside the city gates. It is also an invitaion for the ambitious to complete the research of 2 of my pet projects. The first is nearly complete, a few more tests and we may have it, the 2nd may be on permanent delay due to my lack of adherence to basic safetey measures.

Heres what happened. Early one sunday morn, while wonder thru Norath, I had a flash of inspiration to build a device that would allow tempory invulnorability to enhance escape from a dire situation. The device I had imagined would be similar to idol the head Ork used to drop in High Hold back in me younger days. I used a rebreather as the basis for my design, since the extreme heat I anticapted would necessitate an automated breathing apperatus. I began with standard rebreather parts, adding to them a large power suply, a left over black box from my ealier land mine expereminents, and a Lava Bolt spell, the Magemechanica guild relunctantly donated for the experiment. I then heisted, no! BORROWED, the copper tubing from the public still and had the armor clockwork drill a hole in the black box and forge the copper tubing to it. As i had done in previous experiments, I mounted the power supply firmly inside the box with gnomish bolts, the switch poking out one end. I then carefully placed the volitile spell inside the box and connected it to the power supply with 2 short pieces of metal twine wrapped in bandages.

It was so exiting! almost test ready. I then fastend the the tube up one side the corla mesh, and assembled the rebreather. next, I mounted the breather on a outdated clock work procured from the city and got set to flip the switch. Well, how was i suposed to know that lava confined in one direction could cause so much damage! After all, I cast the thing on those stupid bugs in in solusek often with little effect!

Well anyways when I flipped the switch, the effect was instantaneous, the lava shot thru the tubes and out between the crack between the mesh and the helm, with such force that it ripped the head clean off the clock work. The copper tubing got so hot that holes burned through it, spraying lava all over the room. The rest of the clockwork melted into a pile of rouble and became welded to the floor. What a mess. Oh, and the civic leaders have now come down on me! Even my guild has is reviewing my possible dissmisal! Naturally noone has noticed that the detached head of the clockwork is still intacked, but WHATEVER.

In any case, I'm done, no longer allowed to tinker inside the city, I am not inclined to bring anymore glory to Akonon, so You all are welcome to the the materials left in my lab. Good Luck!, oh and btw I will not be releasing the blue prints to my automated clockwork transporter!
