Hey Kritta, what's Tinkering?

Her little legs carried her through the hills of Steamfront and towards the dark and forgotten caves of the minotaurs. She found herself following the dusty path upto the first of the large windmills. Skirting around to the left she continued on, her boots making only a faint whisper as they struck the ground in a rhythmic cadence.

Passing by the third windmill she heard her name on the wind and came up short, blades drawn and knees bent into a warrior's crouch. Trained eyes scanned the world around her and gave no hint at the origion of the call. Slowly she turned, her eyes scanning the area between the tall windmills with nervousness. It was then when she spotted him, poking his head out from around the heavy wooden door leading into the third windmill. "Kritta, come inside, little one." And with that the heavy door swung nearly shut as the gnome vanished from sight.

She moved to the door and squeezed between the barrier and the doorjam, entering the mechanical wonder. Slowly she sheathed one blade at her hip and the other rested along her back. Curiousity had always been her weakness, and just what the gnome wished had her full attention...

Hail and well met! My name is Kritta Didymus and I am a Mistress Tinker. It was many, many seasons ago when Bom Knotwood pulled me aside and sat me down. Oh he spent many a day, and a lot of patience, training me oh how to combine gears, sprockets, wire and other goods into helpful contraptions. If you wish to follow the same path and become a master tinker as well, there are a few things that you will find helpful to know.

First of all, have a full money bag. Metal of all kinds seems to be expensive as you increase your skill and work on more difficult items. Becoming a master wasn't very difficult or time consuming ... but raising my skill over 122 has posed quite, well, trying. Time and coin are needed here, but the gratification is well worth the effort. You will need a toolbox (or deluxe toolbox) to do your combining. The regular toolbox can be purchased in the third windmill in Steamfont, from Mr. Bom Knotwood.

Ingredients: The various parts and pieces can be found in Bom Knotwood's pockets, as he keeps most of what you will need in stock. Quite handy and he tends to give kind prices. A couple of the items do stack, such as gears and sprockets, but the more advanced the ingredient the more likely that it won't be stackable, so stock up on large bags or even the 10 slot deluxe toolboxes found in IceClad.

Recipes: Once you have your books you will find you have many of the recipes that have been handed down over the years. These books are sold by Rylin Coil who tends to hang out by the old water wheel in his home town of Ak'Anon. An additional book is sold in Katta Castellum by the sweet Zyleea Geerlok, a very warm hearted gnome who also sells upper end ingredients to tinkerers.

Selling Wares: Personally I have found that being known as a successful tinker works the best for getting requests for handtinkered items. I found it nearly impossible to sell rebreathers, Geerlok devices, fireworks and other gnomish goodies via /auction, but rather find that most of my sales come from orders people place when they hear your name or find out who a friend has purchased their item from. If you wish to find a trade that will make you rich, tinkering is not what you seek. Do it for enjoyment or you will easily become bored and frustrated with this skill.

Note: For recipes check out the listings at www.tinkering.org/recipes.html.

Oh I could go on and on, but I feel that would be a waste of my time and yours. You know the basics of tinkering now and should you decide you wish to follow down the same path as I, just keep your chin up and learn to enjoy it. Feel free to ask questions and let me know if I can be of any help.

Kritta Didymus, Mistress Tinker
