Applicants:

During the first fortnight of your new membership you are considered as being on a probational basis. During this period it is not uncommon that new applicants are approached by either the Guildmaster or an officer and called upon to prove his or her faith in the virtues of the Aegis Lux.

Such a reckoning can be done either through a quest set forth by your superiors, a line of questioning or merely reflected by the behaviour you have exhibited during your 30 days of probation.

If the reckoning goes well, you are accepted as a fully-fledged member of Aegis Lux. If the officer or Guildmaster finds that the applicant have exhibited behaviour deemed unworthy of guild-membership, the applicant must again enter a 7 day probation, after which the member will enter a new reckoning or be duly expelled.

All applicants will advance to 2nd. Circle members, after a successful reckoning.

(See Initiation for further information)

Sponsors:

Applicants may seek a sponsor within the guild. Sponsors are members of the guild who take it upon themselves to help and guide new members in the ways of Aegis Lux. Sponsors are responsible for the integrity of the new member and it is their duty to help the Council evaluate the new applicant after the 30 days of probation are over.

Sponsors may be either 2nd. Circle, 1st. Circle or even Council members.

If an applicant does not follow the virtues stated in the 5 Basic Truths, then it is the Sponsors responsibility to help the applicant align him- or herself. Sponsors should not enter this stewardship lightly, but only when they truly feel that the new applicant is worthy of joining Aegis Lux. The Sponsor may be held partly accountable for any transgressions made by his or hers applicant. A Sponsor must at all times seek to impart the 5 Basic Truths and his or hers class-creeds (if applicable) to the Sponsor.

 

2nd. Circle

2nd. Circle members are fully fledged Aegis Lux members, with all the responsibilities and honour that follows. 2nd. Circle members are the mainstay of the guild. Being a member of the 2nd. Circle grants you certain privileges that applicants don’t have. For instance recommendations can now be made from 2nd. Circle members. There is no longer any form of probation for a 2nd. Circle member and lifelong membership is guaranteed as long as one upholds the 5 basic truths.

2nd. Circle members may advance to 1st. Circle if the Council deems them worthy and if they meet certain requirements (see below).

 

1st. Circle

1st. Circle members are the highest ranking common members of the Aegis Lux (with only Guildmaster and Officers ranking higher). Members only attain this rank if they exhibit extreme involvement in guild matters, show mature behaviour and meet the following requirements.

Practical Requirements:

1.      Must be least level 50

2.      Should be in possession of quested items that show a high degree of dedication to ones character and class
 

3.      Must receive the recommendation of no less than 10 common members.

4.      Must have participated in at least 50 % of all major guild events.

The abovementioned criteria are set forth for two main reasons. First of all to stop every member from automatically graduating to 1st, Circle upon gaining level 50. Second to make sure that 1st. level members represent the members who have worked hard both for the guild and for the EverQuest society as a whole.

Benefits:

·          1st. Circle members acquire upon graduation the title of Sir or Lady.

·          1st. Circle members may freely distribute from the Guild Treasury and Library.

·          1st. Circle members can, in absence of Council members, rule in guild disputes.

Responsibilities:

·          1st. Circle members can be called upon to rule in guild disputes, but ONLY in the total absence of Council members. The ruling empowerment will always take place beforehand, by a Council member, and will be stated in the Private Message boards.

·          No 1st. Circle member may adopt ruling power without a direct empowerment from a Council Member. Transgression will be viewed as a bannable offence.

·          1st. Circle members must respond to administrative requests made by Council members.

·          1st. Circle members represent the highest rank amongst common members in Aegis Lux and should therefore always act with extreme integrity. Transgression can result in demotion to 2nd. Circle.

How to initiate Graduation and Evaluation:

·          If a 2nd. Circle member feels he or she is ready to attain 1st. Circle Ranking, they should contact a Council member. The Council member will acknowledge the verbal application and ask the applicant to post his or hers request to the Private Boards.

·          The applicant will then need at least 10 positive recommendations on the post, before the application is considered valid.

·          The Council member will then start the evaluation process and make sure the graduate is made aware of all requirements, benefits and responsibilities.

·          The evaluation process can be either short or long depending on the level of dedication from the graduate and whether he or she has already met the requirements.

Evaluation period:

·          The period will normally run over 1 month, in which the applicant should gather his 10 votes and the Council will judge whether he or she is worthy of obtaining 1st. Circle.

The Council always has the final say:

·          It is always the Council, which in the end decides whether or not the graduate is worthy of obtaining 1st. Circle Ranking. Should the Council feel the graduate is lacking, although he or she meets all practical requirements, the Council will explicitly explain how the graduate can better their chances. Not all members are worthy of 1st. Circle, but potential graduates should always be informed in what way they are lacking should they be denied graduation.

 

Class Leaders:

Class Leaders is the elected focal point for all things regarding their particular class. They act as wise men (or women) who will answer questions concerning class-specific items, quests, spells or tactics. Class Leaders will also be responsible for promoting their particular class, both within Aegis Lux and outside.

How to become a Class Leader:

The Council will no longer appoint Class Leaders directly. They will only address the need for one and then post the open position on the Private Board. All those who feel they have something to contribute may sign up for the job.

Accessibility:

Class Leaders need to readily accessible through the boards or in game.

Should the Class Leader be temporarily unavailable it is his or hers responsibility to appoint another temporary Class Leader for the duration of the absence.

Information:

Class Leaders need to provide information related to their class.

Please try and make a post every week or so, with something specific to your class. This does not have to be a huge quest, but could as well be something specific; An item, new spell, where to get spells, fighting techniques, grouping techniques, places to hunt related to levels, location of vendors in different zones, raids to be organized for particular items etc. Should you want a news item to be posted on the front page of the website, simply contact an Officer and they will post it.

Quests and Raids:

It is important that the Class Leader shows initiative in creating Class events, cultivates Class development and recruiting, and generally be a visible member of the Aegis Lux society.

Do not mistake this for promoting one Class before everyone else, but healthy competition and good-hearted banter between classes are encouraged.


Guild Ranking:

A member that wishes to apply for Class Leader needs a minimum ranking of 2nd. Circle. If no other members in the class wish to apply for CL and you are of applicant status the Council and other CL will take that into consideration.

The Class Leader is not higher ranked than the 1st. Circle member, but is rather an advisor to members who seek guidance in matters of their Class and a promoter of said. The Council recommends that Class Leaders are either 2nd. Or 1st. Circle members, since Officers doubling as Class Leaders shouldn’t be the norm. This should in no way hinder Officers from applying, since the most qualified should always be appointed, but if evenly matched, a 2nd. Or 1st. Circle member will be preferred.

Dedication:

The Council will depend on you to know your Class well and will be referring questions from members or players in general to the particular Class Leaders. Showing a high degree of knowledge and insight, as well as being able to communicate well, is an advantage and will be expected. Just like any position gained, if you are not doing what is expected, you will be approached to see if there is an issue that the council or other CL can help you with. If these issues can not be resolved, you will be asked to resign and the position will be open for new applicants.

Have fun:

That’s it..have fun…We all play this game differently and we all approach the tasks given us in different manners. All playing styles are welcome and above all it is important to have fun doing Class Leadership.

 

Event Masters:

The Event Master is a person who will function as the hub of coordinating events. The will be a High Level event Master (orchestrating events for levels 35 and up) and a Low/Medium Level Event Master (orchestrating events for level 15 to 35).

It is their job to make sure larger Events are posted on the Events Section, gather data and generally make sure the preliminary preparations for an event runs smoothly. Members are encouraged to approach the Event Masters with ideas for new gatherings, quests or socializing and the Event Master will then help with coordinating and advising. It is NOT the Event Master who will lead the Raids or Events unless specifically noted. The originator of the Event will normally hold this position.

Class Leaders and members should use the Event Masters as means of communicating larger scale events via the Events Section and the News section on the website, for which only they and Officers have access.

 

Officers:

The Officers are the actual highest-ranking members of the Guild (ranked just below the Guildmaster). They are in charge of day-to-day operations and has the ruling power and Guild Invitation capabilities. There are also special areas of responsibility for the Officers, some of which are stated below.

Officer roles and responsibilities:

Treasurer:
The keeper of common treasure, to be distributed ad hoc amongst new members. It is the responsibility of the Treasurer to keep a record of all common items in his or her safekeeping. These items are primarily meant to be distributed to lower ranking members as either part of an initiation, a quest or in recognition of noteworthy behaviour.

Initiation Officer:
Responsible for initiation. This role can change among officers. All officers are by definition responsible for answering questions from potential applicants and be helpful in any way to people asking questions regarding the guild.

 

Guild Master:

Are the founder and leader of Aegis Lux – The shield of Light. The Guild Master always has the final word in any guild dispute. He is also the only one able to completely disband the Guild.
