Applicants:

During the first fortnight of your new membership you are considered as being on a probational basis. During this period it is not uncommon that new applicants are approached by either the Guildmaster or an officer and called upon to prove his or her faith in the virtues of the Aegis Lux.

Such a reckoning can be done either through a quest set forth by your superiors, a line of questioning or merely reflected by the behaviour you have exhibited during your 14 days of probation.

If the reckoning goes well, you are accepted as a fully-fledged member of Aegis Lux. If the officer or Guildmaster finds that the applicant have exhibited behaviour deemed unworthy of guild-membership, the applicant must again enter a 7 day probation, after which the member will enter a new reckoning or be duly expelled.

All applicants will advance to 2nd. Circle members, after a successful reckoning.

(See Initiation for further information)

Sponsors:

Applicants may seek a sponsor within the guild. Sponsors are members of the guild who take it upon themselves to help and guide new members in the ways of Aegis Lux. Sponsors are responsible for the integrity of the new member and it is their duty to help the Council evaluate the new applicant after the the 14 days of probation are over.

Sponsors may be either 2nd. Circle, 1st. Circle or even Council members.

If an applicant does not follow the virtues stated in the 5 Basic Truths, then it is the Sponsors responsibility to help the applicant align him- or herself. Sponsors should not enter this stewardship lightly, but only when they truly feel that the new applicant is worthy of joining Aegis Lux. The Sponsor may be held partly accountable for any transgressions made by his or hers applicant  A Sponsor must at all times seek to impart the 5 Basic Truths and his or hers class-creeds (if applicable) to the Sponsor.

 

2nd. Circle

2nd. Circle members are fully fledged Aegis Lux members, with all the responsibilities and honour that follows. 2nd. Circle members are the mainstay of the guild. Being a member of the 2nd. Circle grants you certain privileges that applicants don’t have. For instance recommendations can now be made from 2nd. Circle members. There is no longer any form of probation for a 2nd. Circle member and lifelong membership is guaranteed as long as one upholds the 5 basic truths.

2nd. Circle members may advance to 1st. Circle if they are deemed worthy by the Council and meet certain requirements (see below).

 

1st. Circle

1st. Circle members are the highest ranking common members of the Aegis Lux (with only Guildmaster and Officers ranking higher). Members only attain this rank if they exhibit extreme involvement in guild matters, show mature behaviour and meet the following requirements.

Practical Requirements:

1.      Must be least level 40

2.      Must be possession of quested items that show a high degree of dedication to ones character and class

examples could be:
 
- Soulfire (PAL)
- Raincaller / Orc Impaler (RNG)
- Singing Steel Armor / Mistmore Battle Drum / Gypsy Lute (BRD)
- Totemic Armor / Slime Coated Harpoon  (SHM)
- Crafted Armor (WAR)
- Ravenscale Armor (ROG)
- Cleric Named Armor pieces (CLR)
- Paw of Oppolla (DRU)
- Robe of The Lost Circle (MNK)
- Researched the Ice Comet Spell (WIZ)
- All 4 Pet Foci items (MAG)
- Coin of Tash (ENC)

The specific nature of items are subject to change upon the Councils discretion. The above items are merely meant as guidelines and should reflect the player’s intimacy with the EverQuest game mechanics.

The possession of the items above does NOT automatically generate a promotion after an application for 1st. Circle (see below) neither does the lack of Class armor or a specific item deny you the possibility to graduate. The Council will tell you if they feel you lack something in this regard.

The items mentioned above are merely examples on the type of equipment the Council could demand you should possess or quest for before being deemed worthy.

3.      Must receive the recommendation of no less than 5, 2nd. Circle members.

4.      Must have participated in at least 50 % of all major guild events.

The abovementioned criteria are set forth for two main reasons. First of all to stop every member from automatically graduating to 1st, Circle upon gaining level 40. Second to make sure that 1st. level members represent the members who have worked hard both for the guild and for the EverQuest society as a whole.

Benefits:

·          1st. Circle members acquire upon graduation the title of Sir or Lady.

·          1st. Circle members may freely distribute from the Guild Treasury and Library.

·          1st. Circle members can, in absence of Council members, rule in guild disputes.

Responsibilities:

·          1st. Circle members can be called upon to rule in guild disputes, but ONLY in the total absence of Council members. The ruling empowerment will always take place beforehand, by a Council member, and will be stated in the Private Message boards.

·          No 1st. Circle member may adopt ruling power without a direct empowerment from a Council Member. Transgression will be viewed as a bannable offence.

·          1st. Circle members must respond to administrative requests made by Council members.

·          1st. Circle members represent the highest rank amongst common members in Aegis Lux and should therefore always act with extreme integrity. Transgression can result in demotion to 2nd. Circle.

How to initiate Graduation and Evaluation – and finally the Circle Quest:

·          If a 2nd. Circle member feels he or she is ready to attain 1st. Circle Ranking, they should contact a Council member. The Council member will acknowledge the verbal application and ask the applicant to post his or hers request to the Private Boards.

·          The applicant will then need at least 5 positive recommendations on the post, before the application is considered valid.

·          The Council member will then start the evaluation process and make sure the graduate is made aware of all requirements, benefits and responsibilities.

·          The evaluation process can be either short or long depending on the level of dedication from the graduate and whether he or she has already met the requirements.

The Circle Quest:

·          When all practical requirements are met, the Council then initiates the applicants Circle Quest. Depending on the outcome, the Council then decides if and when the applicant graduates to 1st. Circle. The applicant must complete a Circle Quest set forth by the Council.

This Quest would normally involve a test of both faith and strength on the behalf of the applicant. The Circle Quest is normally rather involved and takes a lot of planning on the part of the Council. The applicant must bear this in mind when applying for graduation. Be patient.

·          It is always the Council, which in the end decides whether or not the graduate is worthy of obtaining 1st. Circle Ranking. Should the Council feel the graduate is lacking, although he or she meets all practical requirements, the Council will explicitly explain how the graduate can better their chances. It is the Councils responsibility that no potential graduates are left without means on how to obtain graduation.

Class Leaders:

Class Leaders is the elected focal point for all things regarding their particular class. They act as wise men (or women) who will answer questions concerning class-specific items, quests, spells or tactics.

Class Leaders will also be responsible for promoting their particular class, both within Aegis Lux and outside. It is important that the Class Leader shows initiative in creating Class events, cultivates Class development and recruiting, and generally be a visible member of the Aegis Lux society.

The Class Leader is not higher ranked than the 1st. Circle member, but is rather an advisor to members who seek guidance in matters of their Class and a promoter of said.

The Council recommends that Class Leaders are either 2nd. Or 1st. Circle members, since Officers doubling as Class Leaders shouldn’t be the norm. This should in no way hinder Officers from applying, since the most qualified should always be appointed, but if evenly matched, a 2nd. Or 1st. Circle member will be preferred.

Officers:

The Officers are the actual highest-ranking members of the Guild (ranked just below the Guildmaster). They are in charge of day-to-day operations and has the ruling power and Guild Invitation capabilities. There are also special areas of responsibility for the Officers, some of which are stated below.

Officer roles and responsibilities:

Event Officer:
The organiser of Events. This role can change between officers. This role functions primarily as the acting co-ordinator of events. Every member of the guild can approach the Event officer with ideas for fun, questing or plain social events.

Treasurer:
The keeper of common treasure, to be distributed ad hoc amongst new members. It is the responsibility of the Treasurer to keep a record of all common items in his or her safekeeping. These items are primarily meant to be distributed to lower ranking members as either part of an initiation, a quest or in recognition of noteworthy behaviour.

Initiation Officer:
Responsible for initiation. This role can change among officers. All officers are by definition responsible for answering questions from potential applicants and be helpful in any way to people asking questions regarding the guild.

 

Guild Master:

Are the founder and leader of Aegis Lux – The shield of Light. The Guild Master always has the final word in any guild dispute. He is also the only one able to completely disband the Guild.
