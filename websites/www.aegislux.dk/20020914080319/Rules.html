No Discussions

The loot master’s decision is final. There will be no discussion during the raid. No one will spam the raid leader, the puller, the main healer or the Officers during the Raid.

In depth
If you have a problem with the rules, please talk to one of the officers in-game (before or after a raid, not during), or mail to either Errol or Monrad. On a particular raid you feel you aren’t getting what you deserve, please consult the rules below. It is these the Loot Master are following and it is these the rest of the raid is following, so must you.

The purpose of this rule is to avoid any arguments to arise during the raid.

The Loot Master

Maybe you’ve been on a few raids where the classes on the raid handled their own lottos. Not here. One person handles the distribution for all drops, including zone/cash drops right through to class specific no drop armor. For example, if you decide to take it upon yourself to run all druid lottos and you’re not the Loot Master, you’ll be asked to leave the raid.

When an item is dropped and you want to be considered, you must then send a tell to the Loot Master.

The Loot Master will then make a decision based on a random roll, the drops of the Raid and your current loot status.

When an Item Drops


When an item drops the loot master will call for interested parties, which then must give a tell to the Loot Master. The Loot Master will then determine which of the interested participants will get to loot the item.

It is quite possible that the Loot Master will have to judge between two or more equally deserving persons. If both are entirely equal, a Random Roll will be used by the Loot Master to decide where the loot falls.

Class/Multiple Class drops:
If an item drops for a class (or classes), it will be a consideration between the members of that class (or classes) that require that item or can use that item. The only exception to this is if one of the people who can use the item opts to count themselves out of the consideration by choice.

In depth
By “require”, we mean people that do not already have this item and will actually equip or use the item, or need the item to fulfill a quest. We are not interested in people getting items so that they can be immediately traded or placed in bank vaults to rot unused.

There is always going to be some debate as to whether all classes that can physically use the item should be allowed to. To be fair, we are going to say that people who can use an item (judging by the description on the item itself) can be considered if they are going to use it.

However, we would like to heavily stress that if you know this is an item that you’re not really going to use and that a particular class is very excited about getting in on, please reconsider whether you really want to be considered or not. Use some common sense about how people are going to perceive your actions. If you’re a wizard and a sword drops that’s great for rangers and druids but happens to be ALL/ALL, do you really want to enter that consideration just because you can? Be considerate of others and use good judgement.


The KAG’s (Keep Alive Guy/Gal)

What benefits the Guild as a whole

The most important classes for any guild who wishes to make Raids are Warriors, Enchanters and Clerics. That means that Aspiration’s main KAG’s will be the first to get any major upgrades to his or hers equipment. This primarily goes for “prime” items (such as dragon haste CoF, RBB, Breast Plates, or other unique items), but on all other items (ie. helmets, legs, arms, gloves, bracers, shoulders etc) that are not rare or unique goes to lottos.

Its gonna benefit us all that our Warriors don’t die too fast, our Enchanter can stay alive and that our Cleric doesn’t drop after the first CH aggro. Its real simple. If you do not agree with this, do not attend our raids.

Bear in mind that all “prime” items doesn’t automatically go to the KAG’s. The Loot Master will evaluate its benefits and whether it constitutes a decent upgrade before assigning it to either the KAG without a loot or if its free for all. The KAG rule is simply there to ensure that we keep our KAG’s well-equipped, to benefit the overall raid-capability.


Exceptions

When raiding with allies we cannot hog items for our KAG’s. During multi-guild Raids led by Aegis, this rule is suspended.

Cash For Clerics

All cash items (jeweled necklaces, opal bracelets, etc. - basically things that sell for a lot but can’t be used for any other purpose such as jewelcraft) is passed on to the Loot Master.

He will make sure no one is unduly taxed economically for attending our Raids.

In depth
Ever play a cleric and have to pay 11 plat per stone every time you use one to cast Symbol of Naltron on people? That’s a lot of money. Very often, giving all the gems found to clerics still doesn’t cover their cost of casting Naltron for the day. If anybody has a problem with this rule, that’s just tough, quite frankly.


One Item Per Person Per Raid

(With Some Exceptions/Exclusions)

If you win an item, you are out of consideration for the remainder of the raid. If something else drops later in the raid and you would prefer to have it more than the item you won, you may enter consideration for the second item only if you can “lotto” off the first item you won to the rest of the raiders.

This is to ensure everyone has a chance at getting a drop.

If the first item that you won is a no drop item, it cannot be traded out of your inventory and is therefore not something that can be “re-lotto’d”.

In depth
Some may argue that with the case of plane armor, you can’t trade back armor (say a bracer) that you might not want if something better drops (say a breastplate). This is your problem and yours alone. If a bracer drops and you want to exclude yourself from consideration because you might want to get in on a possible BP drop later in the day, so be it.

The choice and suppositions are yours to make. The One Raid, One Item Rule still apply and overrule.

If you win a bracer and a breastplate drops later, sorry, but that’s the luck of the draw.

Exceptions
Please note, that if 4 clerics participate, and they have all gotten a drop and a 5th. item drops, its open season again for all 4 clerics. Nothing will rot. We just start the cycle again.

Also, KAG’s are the most important. So on Aegis only Raids they may get more than 1 item before anyone else.

In the case of certains items dropping that are considered to be common, exceptions may be made. For example, for a while Fluxbladed Axes (ranger only, no drop) were quite common on fear raids. Many people weren’t counting them towards being taken out of ranger armor considerations. If an item drops that all the members of a class feel is so common that they wouldn’t want to be considered for, for fear of risking their placement towards armor drops, due to the One Raid, One Drop Rule, it will NOT be counted towards armor drops and will be considered on equally between those that need it.

But, it is always the Loot Masters decision that is final.


Epic Drops

Epic Drops automatically goes to the class who can use it. In the case where there are several of the same class present, a combination of lotto and an evaluation of how far each member are in their quest, will be used.


Dragon and God Drops

Dragon/God drops (that includes Dracoliche and Maestro as well, for example) be considered separate from all the other loot.

Just because you won armor or a rare mace doesn’t mean the “losers” of the previous considerations get to call in on something better that drops off a god or dragon. This is everybody’s priveledge and it’s a rarity to get in on these drops, so everybody should be included, regardless of what you’ve gotten so far in the day.

In depth
However, let’s say you’re a player who’s the only one of your class in the zone and five pieces of armor have dropped for your class and because you were the only one of your class, you got them all by default. That’s great for you, but how is it going to make others feel when you enter a God or Dragon lconsideration?

The god drops are open but if you’ve been winning things left right and center, try and show a little restraint when it comes to the big draw. People might see you as greedy as a result - whether you want to cross that line of perception with people is your own choice to make.

Loot and Twinks

It is always the Primaries that gets first loot Rights. Twinks and alts will only be considered when all primaries have looted. If you feel this is unfair, don’t bring your twink on a Raid.

Again the Loot Master may decide something in each specific case, but the ground rule is - twinks gets last.

In depth
If it is a multi-guild Raid, twinks of Aegis will still be last in line for drops. This is to show our friends good faith and common courtesy. It can be argued that our allies might have joined the raid with a twink, but they still get rights before our own secondaries.


Addendum: Nothing Rots, Ever.

Some people have read these types of rules and seen enough complexity to somehow believe that there is a chance something will rot because we are tied down in rules. This simply is not the case. If anybody has gotten this impression, it is false.
