Member Relations

This guild was founded and operates on the principal of helping.  As such, there are certain guidelines by which members should follow regarding other guildmembers.   This is not an attempt to tell any player how to play their character nor an attempt to dictate how one should relate to others.  Just some suggestions and my ideals on how I would hope we all treat each other. One note: Yes, it would be ideal to provide items, services, and aid for no fee or cost at all, but I realize this is not always practical and not everyone is so wealthy they have money to burn freely. 

Relating to each other
Items--You will not ask any other member for items!! Please respect the High Level Member.  He/she wishes to play the game and is often times saving and working hard to obtain some item of their own! Trust that when they have the opportunity, they will donate items to the guild armory.
Camping for Rare Items--Certainly you may call upon someone for assistance, but realize you are cutting into THEIR playtime as well as your own.  If they decline to assist, say thank you and move on.  For all you know, they just spent the last 3 days camping to help someone else and now they are playing for themselves. Do not beg, plead, or otherwise attempt to change their mind.
Services--Again, please respect that they are playing the game as well! Asking for a gate, bind, or whatever is acceptable, but if you get a "No, I am busy at the moment" please tell them thank you and move on. Do not beg or plead. Ask once and respect that answer!!
Bottom line--Asking is acceptable (except for items) but respect the answer given and move on! Realize there must be a reason they cannot honor your request at this time and move on.
Services
Spellcaster--For the casting of spells which require only mana to cast, it is hoped this service will be performed for no cost to another guild member (such as Binds) For the member being bound, it is acceptable to offer a tip for the service, especially if the spellcaster had to wait for you, come find you, or was otherwise inconvenienced.  If the spell cast required some type of component that was consumed, then it is reasonable to ask for the cost of the component or replacement.
Trade Skills--Making a profit on a trade skill is difficult, as is raising the skill in the first place.  Understanding this, I would hope the Tradesman would only charge another guild member the cost of the raw materials, or simply ask the guild member to provide the raw material.  This helps both the Tradesman as well as the guild member.
Corpse Retrieval--I would hope guildmembers would be ready, willing, and able to answer the call to aid a fallen member.   Everyone dies and we all know the pain and frustration that accompanies death and the possible loss of all items. I would hope a guild member would stand ready to do all that is within his or her abilities to aid your fallen comrade if you are on the same continent and able to get to them in a timely manner.  This is when belonging to a guild matters the most.  You may be able to aid in others ways as well, even if you cannot physically go to their location.  You may be able to offer advice, directions, or you may know someone in the area who can help them.  The key is to communicate.   Do not let your guild member feel he or she is all alone.
Funds and Items
Please do not ask another guild member for money or items.  See the Guild Armory.  This way, higher level characters would have a central way to distribute loot items they do not need to sell and the lower level characters would have a single point of contact to obtain certain items (and you avoid begging from others)
Rare or hard to obtain items obtained solo (see the loot policy for details)--Up to the sole discretion of the guild member.  He or she may sell it for maximum profit to a non-guild member, sell it for some profit to a guild member, donate it to a guild member, or whatever.  I would hope if you opt to sell the item, you do allow your guild mates the first opportunity to purchase.  Offer a discount if you like, but with a Rare or hard to obtain item, it is understandable to offer no discount.
Party formation--Naturally, it is preferable to form parties with your own guild mates first.  However, I realize this isn't always possible.  Besides, grouping with non-guildmembers helps let our guild be known to others.  This is going to be a tough area and will rely on many judgment calls I am sure.  I just ask you be as considerate of the non-guildmembers as you are towards guildmembers.
Late comers--Should you form a party with non-guildmembers and begin adventuring, then a guild member arrives and wishes to join, please be considerate of the non-guildmembers as well as your guild member.  I know I certainly would not like being in a group, deep in Sebilis, only to be asked to disband immediately so someone else could join.  In this type of situation, I would expect the party to at least escort the non-guildmembers to a safe location before disbanding to allow the guild member to join.  Consider the time invested by this non-guild member.  Did you all just arrive at a good location that took a while to get there?  Has this person been an asset to your party so far?   This is going to be a fine-line, I know.
