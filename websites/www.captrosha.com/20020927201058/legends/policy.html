Legends Loot Policy


This policy will be administered by the guild officers. Loot under this policy will be distributed to primary characters only, except secondary's may receive items when no mains have use for them, as long as the secondary is useful in guild raids. Each player will designate a primary character.

This policy applies to all items and spells (“Items”) obtained by members of the guild. Items obtained by members will stay in guild, and not be traded or sold outside the guild except in accordance with this policy. With regards to items obtained solo or in pick-up groups. The item is yours to decide to do with as you wish. If you feel it's better served in a trade for something for your own character, so be it. We will recognize your right to determine what is best for your character.

Items obtained by Legends groups (defined as a group with 6 members) will become property of the guild. Items will be dealt with by the officers and appointed Guild Traders--with items being distributed to those members who contribute to the guild on a consistent basis. Members who turn over items they obtain solo or in pickup groups are considered before those members who do not. In lieu of items, donations of gems or platinum is also considered as contribution. We also recognize the freedom of guild members to gift others with items (members or non-members).  Simply inform a guild officer of such gifting and it will be considered as contributing to the guild as if you gave the item to the guild.

Any person found to be actively and consistently avoiding grouping with other Legends in order to bypass the policy may be asked to leave the guild since it is obvious they are only out for their own welfare and not the good of the guild. (This will only happen after discussions with the individual and the guild leader and officers...it will not happen arbitrarily and without warning).

Officers will determine who receives Items with preference always being given first to characters who can and will use said Item who were present when such Item was obtained.* Any item that you are replacing with an Item received under this policy will be given to an officer for redistribution to other primary characters in the guild who can and will use it. Failure to turn over an item requested for redistribution will forfeit the person's right to the new Item.  Exceptions to this will be considered by the officer's on a case by case basis.**

Distribution of items is not going to be an exact science, but we are going to do our best to be fair to everyone.  Factors that will be used to determine who gets an item include, but are not limited to: level, time in guild, availability for raids, donations of items to the guild, donation of time to the guild, and attendance at guild events.

Items should not be sold or traded outside the guild unless no primary (or useful secondary) can use, and always with officer consent. If you leave the guild, to the extent possible, guild Items are to be returned (in other words, if it is not No Drop it should be returned). If you receive an Item and then trade it because an irresistible trade offer comes along, the new item(s) are considered property of the guild.

Certain guild officers will create characters to act as the bank for the guild.  These characters will hold excess items for the guild. In addition, one of the characters created by the guild leader will hold money for the guild for use in trading, for raids and to support our craftspeople. Donation of platinum to the guild is voluntary.

Excess Items will be traded for items/spells the guild needs. Certain members of our guild are very gifted in trading. Officers will designate these members as "Guild Traders" who will trade on behalf of the guild and have access to the excess items and spells in the Guild Bank.

*This generally means that, for example, an enchanter at a raid will always get the particular item before one not at the raid.

**Why on a case by case basis? Whether or not something replaces something else can be a murky question. Does a 2-handed weapon replace a 1 handed weapon? Does a +mana item replace an ac item or a resist item?

So, what does this mean?


If you are grouped with 6 or more Legends, Items obtained are property of the guild and the guild officers will determine what to do with the item. Also, you will receive the benefit of guild items based on your contributions to the guild. If you give freely, expect to receive freely. If you rarely give, then do not expect to receive items in return. If you give assistance teleporting people, assisting in corpse recovery, or on raids, you are contributing (contributions can be items, plat, gems, TIME, in-game assistance, but are not limited to this list).

What the hell? Why would I do this?


It's simple. A group is more powerful than an individual. Have you ever wanted to make some trade for some item yet you lacked the plat or all the required items the person trading the item asked for? Imagine the power of 80+ people behind you, acquiring the platinum and items to make that trade! Some will likely view this as a restriction on their ability to trade or acquire items. I submit to you that it is a more powerful way to acquire items!

What if I don't want to follow this policy?


Then it is probably best for you to move on to another guild. There will be no hard feelings and I will ask all guild members to treat you with the respect of one who wore the Legends name.

Who is responsible for this?


Ultimately, Vestrial  as the Guild Leader. If you wish to rant, vent, or blowup at someone, then Vestrial is the person to whom you need to direct the above. You should  know the guild officers  have put a lot of thought and effort into this and feel this is the best thing for this guild. As already mentioned, a great many people ALREADY follow this policy. We are just making that the official policy.
