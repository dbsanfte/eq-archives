  Announcing The Intel "Arm Yourself for Adventure" Game Room

Intel will be sponsoring a player room for the Dallas Fan Faire where you and your friends can play EQ together! Finally, you will have a chance to play together in the same room! Not only can you play with your friends, but you'll also get to check out some great top-end Intel based PCs!

You will be able to sign up for time in the Computer Room when you check in. Space will be allotted on a first come, first serve basis. In addition, the guild with the greatest number of members present will be given a prime time spot so they can all play together!

If you plan to take advantage of this opportunity, please be sure your characters are where you wish them to be before you arrive at the Fan Faire. Playtime will be somewhat limited, and it would be best if you did not have to waste your time traveling to meet up with your friends!

Our thanks to Intel for bringing this new and exciting activity to the Fan Faire!

  Native Armor Class Bonus for Beastlords
Absor confirmed that the Beastlords do in fact have a native armor class bonus. In a recent Developer's Corner post, Absor clarified that this native bonus is not affected by encumberance as it is with Monks. But, he also states that the Beastlord AC bonus is not as good as the Monk's bonus. For more information, check out Absor's comments.

  ATI Graphical Issues
We are aware that some people are experiencing problems with the latest drivers from ATI. While we are working on the problems, ATI users having graphical issues should also submit a Problem Report with ATI at http://support.ati.com/problem/win9x.html. This will ensure that the engineers at ATI are also notified of any problems using their drivers. Our programmers will then be better able to collaborate on a solution with ATI.

  Notice to Consumers - Windows 95 EverQuest Players
If you are running under the Windows 95 operating system and, therefore, can no longer play EverQuest, and your account was in good standing as of December 3, 2001, you may be entitled to receive a refund of the purchase price for the EverQuest product(s) you recently bought and any subscription money paid for the period December 4, 2001 onward. To learn more about this, and to determine if a refund is desired, please read the special Consumer Notice page we established to address this concern.

  Unable to Download Files with the New Patcher?
If you are having difficulty downloading files with the new EverQuest patcher, download this file into your EverQuest directory (the default location is C:\Program Files\EverQuest\). Unzip the file, replacing your old EverQuest.exe file with this newer version, and your problem should be solved.

  No 3D Devices Found Error
This error will appear when a customer has DirectX 8.0a installed, but the game is trying to use a DirectX 8.1 compliant driver. The solution is to upgrade to DirectX 8.1.

  DirectX
Because Windows 95 does not support DirectX 8.1 and our new and improved EverQuest engine requires the use of this version of DirectX, we can no longer support Windows 95.

We apologize for this inconvenience but in order to play EverQuest and enjoy the benefits of the new engine you must upgrade to Windows 98 or greater and install DirectX 8.1.

  Windows 98 or Higher Users! Important DirectX Info
IMPORTANT UPDATE:

EverQuest's new graphics engine requires that players upgrade their DirectX drivers. The following will point you to the drivers you need:

Windows 98 or higher users must upgrade to DirectX 8.1 drivers to play EverQuest. The DirectX 8.1 drivers are available from Microsoft.

For help, consult our guide to installing DirectX drivers.

  How to Upgrade Your DirectX Drivers
We are receiving many technical inquiries regarding recent changes we have made in preparation for the launch of the new EverQuest engine. There is an extended downtime scheduled from 12:00 pm PST on December 3, 2001 through 8:00 am PST on December 4, 2001 to patch the Shadows of Luclin and upgrade the existing EverQuest Client. We believe this patch will fix most of the problems that you are experiencing and will add some great new features to the game.

After tomorrow's patch, EverQuest will require DirectX 8.0a to play, although version 8.1 is recommended. DirectX 8 upgrade is easy, and the gains are exceptional.

For information, consult our guide to upgrading your DirectX drivers.
