Bordael's Update - March 2003

Alan "Absor" VanCouvering has graciously donated this space to me this month. Allow me to start by first offering a bit of an introduction: I bring you word of a brave new game—its name: EverQuest® Online Adventures™, or simply EQOA for short. EQOA is a first for console gaming, a true massively multiplayer online game (MMOG--we love our acronyms) filled with thousands of other adventurous souls for you to meet and explore with. It is set in the recognizable world of Norrath, but 500 years before the original EverQuest®. At this time the elves, dwarves and gnomes have yet to depart for the distant shores of Faydwer. This era is known as the Age of Adventure, an age where great mysteries and dangers lay hidden throughout the young continent of Tunaria, as it is called during this time.

As for myself, I am known in the lands of Norrath as Bordael, but you may come to know me as Alex von Minden, the community relations manager for EverQuest Online Adventures. My duty, like that of my good colleague Absor, is to bring to you the latest news of developments to EQOA as well as to gather your feedback for consideration by the EQOA development team.

We invite you to join us in the exploration of this exciting new gaming experience. Below are links to many details about the EverQuest Online Adventures universe.

We look forward to seeing you.

Very truly yours,

Alex von Minden
Community Relations Manager

EverQuest®: The Legacy of Ykesha™ Launches!

The very first downloadable EverQuest extension has generated a lot of talk in the EQ community, and at last it is available and live!

Sales of the extension were phenomenal, with more than 200,000 pre-orders eclipsing even EverQuest®: The Planes of Power™ and its terrific first-week sales! There are going to be a lot of Frogloks in Norrath.

Some users are concerned that this downloadable extension will be unmangeable over a slower dial-up connection. We took measures to minimize the pain of such a large download. Prior to release, we gradually made available the files needed for the extension. Also, your download is managed through your EverQuest client front-end, which can handle the long process of downloading while you sleep or while you're at work. Plus, we'll be offering a relatively small number of The Legacy of Ykesha CD's through retailers soon, though they may not be available everywhere.

The launch itself went off without a hitch. As a matter of fact, we experienced our highest simultaneous user count ever that evening. Players rushed the gates to check out the new playable race, the Froglok and other new features The Legacy of Ykesha introduces.
