        The day we had spent together at the Lake of Ill Omen was wonderful, as is all the time I get to spend with Lady Siniana. Our Lovemaking had been strong and exhaustive but also very refreshing. It had been far too long since we had seen each other and the short time we spent that day had gone by far too fast. The next morning I had to return to the mainland on Guild business, Siniana had accompanied me back to ease the long boat ride. We spoke of many things during that ride, the Love we shared, our future together, but mostly I remember just being with her. Watching her face light up when she smiled, her little mannerisms as she spoke, I had not realized how deeply I had fallen for her until just then, I couldn’t imagine life without her. At the Butcherblock docks we had said our goodbye’s and planned to meet up later that evening in the Faydark Forest, at a special spot we had once shared a picnic at very early in our relationship. I had told her that I would arrive by dusk and if she could have a camp set-up perhaps we could spend some more "Quality" time together, she had smiled and laughed when I had said that. We had kissed before parting and she tasted so sweet that I barely had the resolve to leave. Sensing that I may be hesitating, she gave me a small kick in the rear to get me going "Now you hurry along" she had said smiling, "I will be waiting for you tonight." With a smile and a wink I had turned to run for the city of Kaladim, already thinking about my evening ahead with Siniana. The business I had in Kaladim was long and boring, and many were my thoughts of my Lady that day. Finally the business was finished and I began the run to the Faydark. An hour or so later as I approached the meeting place I could see no light from a campfire. As I entered the small clearing I could tell that someone had been here for a short time, but had left some time ago and not returned. It did not look like there had been a struggle, but I could not be certain. I looked around for signs as to what happened, but found nothing out of the ordinary. I started to fan out searching for something, anything that might indicate where the Lady was or why she had not come back. As I searched, the sun faded over the horizon and my chances of finding anything were quickly diminishing. Just as I thought it was hopeless I saw the light from a small campfire, moving towards the light I made my way through the brush quietly. As I approached I could see a lone figure sitting by the fire, he had on dark cloak with a hood so I couldn’t see much of him, but I could see enough. He was a darkie I could see that, and the colored piping around the edges of his cloak indicated that he was a Templar. Thoughts rushed through my head, what was a dark elf Templar doing here, out in the middle of the lesser fay? And where was Siniana? This was too much of a coincidence, I figured there was a good chance that he had something to do with her disappearance. Moving around behind him I jumped from the bushes and ran towards him, hoping that I had enough surprise on my side to reach him before he could take action. He must have heard me coming for he was ready, he stood quickly turning towards me, a short sword in his hand. I could see the white of his teeth from the light of the fire as he faced me, I expected him to cast a spell or two right then but he held off. Perhaps he thought I was just a lowly thief or robber and did not wish to waste his mana on me, whatever the reason I am thankful for it. I closed the distance between us and he lunged at me with his sword. I had been receiving training in the Ancient arts of Combat for some time now and instantly my training took over. I stepped to the side, off the line of his attack and grabbed his wrists with both of my hands. Lifting his arm, I stepped under and through, twisting his wrist back towards him, I could see the look of surprise on his face as I moved. I needed information from him, so I did not wish to harm him, at least not yet, I put him into a wristlock and forced the sword out of his hand. I heard him begin to mutter an incantation under his breath, quickly pulling my dirk from its sheath, I slid the blade up under his chin and pressed it to his throat, "Quiet Templar", I said, "One more word from you and I will take off your head." I drew the blade across his neck slightly to let him know I wasn’t kidding, his chanting stopped. "Lay down on the ground" I said, he laid down slowly and I put my knee into his back to keep him from moving around. "Now, I need some information from you Templar, so I am going to ask a question and if you respond with anything other than an answer you are going to offend me, and I am in no mood to be offended right now." I said calmly, while watching him carefully. I took my dirk and set the tip of the blade right at the base of the back of his neck, "And If you try and cast on me I am going to clean my blade with your brains, you understand?" he nodded slightly to indicate his understanding. "I am looking for a friend of mine, a paladin, a Lady paladin." I said dryly, not wanting him to know what my feelings for her might be. "She was to be in this area to meet me tonight, yet I cannot find her, and I think you might know where she is." He wiggled slightly and I stuck my knife tip into his neck enough to draw blood, "Don’t play games with me Templar, think carefully on your answer." He hissed back at me, "I know nothing about it, you fool, don’t you know who I am?" "You are nothing more than a dead man if I do not get the answers I seek." I replied coldly. Grabbing the back of his hair with my free hand I pulled back his head and moved the point of my dagger to the opening of his right ear. "Now, I will ask you once more Templar, and if you lie I swear by all that’s Holy I will kill you, where is the paladin?" He shouted back, "I don’t know what you’re talking about." Damn, I thought, this is getting me nowhere fast and the Lady may need me. "You have no idea who I am do you?" The dark elf half shouted from underneath me. "My friends are very powerful, they will kill you……..I will kill you when you let me up." He was ranting but I was barely listening to him anymore, grabbing a nearby rock I smashed him in the back of the head, knocking him unconscious and relieving myself from having to listen to anymore of his nonsense. Rolling off the darkie I sat thinking about the situation. Where was the Lady Siniana? And what, if anything, did this dark elf Templar have to do with her disappearance? I searched through the Templars belongings, looking for some indication as to whether he had anything to do with what was going on. Finding nothing, I sat down, still unsure if he had been involved. Looking over at the limp form of the Templar I realized I wasn’t getting any answers here, involved or not I cannot waste anymore time. I thought to myself, what do my instincts say? My instincts told me she wasn’t here, not in this forest, not even on this continent, and I have learned to trust my instincts. So, leaving the Templar to his fate, I circled back to the meeting place just to make sure I hadn’t missed anything. Giving the place one more quick search, I headed off running towards the docks, listening to the sounds of the night, and praying that my Lady was alright.
