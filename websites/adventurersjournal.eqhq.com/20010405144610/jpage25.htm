                It has been days since he has seen another human. Since he left the outpost of Fironia Vie he has seen neither man nor woman. He noticed tracks from travelers long since passed, but has seen no signs of recent travel. He has been searching the area for his Love, the Lady Siniana, but so far, with the exception of the wildlife, he has found nothing. Now, up ahead in the distance he could see a small plume of smoke rising into the air. The smoke looked like it was from a fire of some sort and he decided to head in that direction to check it out.

A few miles later, from a ridge that overlooked a small valley he could see the small shack. It sat alone in the middle of small clearing. Normally he probably wouldn’t have looked twice at it, but out of the chimney came a steady stream of black smoke, which meant someone was home. And whoever was home might be able to help him. Heading down the ridge he moved cautiously closer, he still had a ways to go, but better to be safe than sorry.

Inside the little shack, the dark witch stirred her brew, the smoke from the fire she had made billowed up and around the kettle, then out through the chimney. She had seen him coming in one of her crystals and had been preparing ever since, making up a spell that would be needed soon. The witch had lived most of her life here on the green, alone, just the way she liked it. But recently she had been thinking about her life and where it was going. You see, the dark magic that she worked had taken its toll on her body and left it mangled, and somewhat twisted. Although she was only twenty-five seasons old, she looked to be fifty or more. She knew she would not live forever, as a matter of fact, at the rate she was abusing her body she expected to live no more than another twenty seasons or so. So recently, she had been thinking about a child, someone to pass along the secrets of the arcane that she had learned in her life. Someone who could carry on her name and memories, and more importantly someone she could teach to be a being of pure evil and hatred. She had considered hiking into town and stealing a young child, one young enough to not have many memories, but that was risky business that could get a person killed, even a person as skilled in the dark arts as her. Recently she realized that she was carrying a seed of her own, If only she could get a man to help her conceive the child out of the seed that she carried then that would be better. Then, out of the blue came this man who now approached her shack, "what a stroke of luck" she thought, "Luck nothing, it is a gift from the dark Gods themselves" she smiles at the thought of it. She could see through the crystal that this man was a half breed and probably wouldn’t have anything to do with a dark elf such as herself, so a spell was needed to change her appearance to something a little more pleasurable to his eyes. She continued stirring the brew while she checked the crystal to keep an eye on his progress. "Yes" she said, "keep coming my handsome stranger, I have a little surprise for you, Haaaahaaaahaaahaahahahaha" The spell that she cooked was almost done now, just a few more minutes and she could use it.

Approaching the shack, the ranger stops next to a tree just to watch for a few minutes. There are no signs of life at all, except for the smoke coming from the chimney. His instincts are sharp and he cannot detect anything wrong here, all seems relatively safe. He decides to wait for a bit and just watch from the trees, perhaps someone will come out and he can get a look at who might be living here.

The witch watches him from her crystal, he has stopped and seems to be just looking at the place for now. "Cautious one, eh?" she says to herself, "That is no matter" She smiles and takes a scoop of the brew she has been cooking and pours it into a powered mixture in a mug on the table. The mug hisses and smokes when the brew hits it and a puff of thick white smoke rises from it. Saying an incantation over the mug, she takes it and drinks it down quickly. As soon as she drinks it, she feels the change in her body and smiles while walking towards the door.

Suddenly the door opens and the ranger sees a beautiful half-elf woman standing in it. She is young and strong, very well built. Her blonde hair flows down around her body to her waist. She walks out the door and begins walking towards the stack of wood that is near the shack. After seeing her, Temyez begins walking towards the shack, before he has a chance to say anything the woman looks up at him and cries out "Oh no! please don’t hurt me, no, please!" dropping the wood the she cringes away from him in terror. Lifting his hands, Temyez tries to smile, "Its ok, I won’t hurt you. Please don’t be afraid." He says to the girl, "My name is Temyez D’argos, I am looking for a friend of mine, please don’t be afraid. Let me help you" He walks slowly up to her and kneels to pick up the wood that she has dropped. The woman watches him carefully, still afraid. Standing up with the wood he steps back and looks at her, the woman just sits there unmoving, staring at him with wide eyes. "M’lady, I would stand here all day if it made you more comfortable, but this wood is kind of heavy." He says to her with a smile and a wink. "I assure you M’lady I mean you no harm." The witch stands up slowly and stares at him "M’lady, If I was going to harm you I would have done so already. Now please, I only wish to help you and then perhaps ask some questions. Then I will be on my way." The witch looks at him for a few more moments then smiles slightly "Ok" she says softly, walking back towards the shack. Temyez follows her through the door with the load of wood he carries.

The interior of the shack came as somewhat of surprise to him, dead scorpions and lizards hung upside down near a window to the right. A huge cooking pot hung over the fire with some unknown brew bubbling inside of it. A shelf near the back was lined with skulls of various animals, and jars filled with many different substances all labeled for future use. On the table there were more jars and vials filled with powders and substances, knives and utensils sat in disarray next to a mixing bowl with something half made in it. A cloth blanket hung in a doorway separating this room from another back room, probably a place to sleep. The place was dank and dark and instantly reminded him of any one of the many dungeons he had visited in his lifetime. He set the wood down near the cooking fire and stood up looking around "You live here?" he asked somewhat surprised. "Oh yes" she replied, looking at him and seeing his expression she went on "Oh, I am sorry about all this, I lived here with my old mother, all of these things were hers…….but she’s gone now, and I haven’t really had time to clean out all of this junk yet" she lowered her head, while her finger traced designs in some powder that was spilled on the table. "I am sorry for your loss" he replied. "Its ok, it was some time ago. Besides I don’t want to talk about her anyways, I want to talk about you." She raised her head and looked directly at him, her demeanor seemed to instantly change from a scared, shy little girl, to a confident woman who knows exactly what she wants. She went directly to him now, not afraid at all, her finger softly traced a design on his chest plate while she looked up into his eyes "I have never been with a real man before" she said with a sly smile. Her small body rubbed against him provocatively as she smiled at him. This caught him completely off guard, one minute she had been afraid to even be near him, now she wanted to be on him, he tried to say something "I ….. Uh ….. what" She smiled even larger now, her leg rubbing into his groin, her tongue darting out to lick her lips "You can have me now, I want you" she purred, while her hands grabbed him by the shoulders and tried to pull him down so she might kiss him. "No" Temyez cried out, pushing her back away from him "That is not why I am here" She looked at him with a surprised, sad look "But don’t you want me, don’t you find me attractive?" He crossed the room to the other side of the table "No, my heart belongs to another and while she lives I WILL not Love anyone else" The witch shrugged her shoulders, surprised that any man might turn down the opportunity to be with a beautiful woman, "we shall see", she thought to herself. The witch began to cry, lifting her hand to her face "Oh I am sorry", she cried "I have just been so afraid, so lonely" tears rolled down her cheeks. With her other hand she scooped up some of the powder in one of the piles on the table. Temyez was shaken by her sudden changes in emotion, he didn’t know what to make of it, he slowly moved towards her wanting both to comfort her and keep his distance. Quickly she raised her hand and blew the powder into his face. He pulled back and turned instinctively but not before getting a lungful and faceful of the powder "What? …. " he sputtered out between gasping and coughing, his hands flew up to wipe the dust from his eyes. When he turned back he suddenly felt sleepy, he fell to his knees, and for a moment he thought he saw an old woman standing where the beautiful girl used to be. "That’s funny" he thought, then everything went black and he fell to the floor.

The old witch cackled and laughed, as she danced around the shack "Won’t have a young girl will you. Hahahahahaha Never Love another, eh? Hahahaha ………….. We shall see, Hahahaha…. We shall see……….." Picking him up off the floor she threw him onto the table "If you won’t give me what I want voluntarily, then I’ll take what I need forcefully" she said, and went about her dark work. Hours later when she had taken what she needed from him, she held up a vial filled with the life giving liquid that would nourish her seed "Yes, you will bring my child to life" Putting the vial carefully into a jar of liquid, she turns back toward the ranger laying on her table. "And for making me take what I needed the hard way and denying me any fun you will be punished" She thought about what he had said before she knocked him out, "I will never Love another" he had said "Well now, my handsome young ranger, you will never Love at all" she smiled evilly as she decided upon his punishment "I will take your heart and soul, my ranger friend, and you will never Love again, Hahahahahahahahahahaha" She proceeded to work over him casting spells and saying incantations. When she was finished she smiled wickedly and put him into a cart. She wheeled him away and took him to the west for some distance. She found another old abandoned shack some distance from hers and dumped him inside. "When you awake my heartless friend you will remember nothing about our meeting, nor will you remember anything about why you are here, you will only want to return home" she quickly wrote a note and pinned it to him. She grinned as she looked at him for the last time, rubbing her belly she said "Stay well, young father, Perhaps someday you will meet your son or daughter" She started laughing as she walked away, and you could hear the laughing continue as she faded into the distance.

Days later, when he awoke, he felt somewhat disoriented and couldn’t remember where he was or what he was doing here. He looked around at the abandoned shack that he was in, but couldn’t remember anything. He felt funny inside, he couldn’t quite describe it but it was different. He looked down and saw a note pinned to his shirt, he pulled it off and read "South to civilization, south to Fironia Vie" He stood shakily and felt very thirsty. Looking around he saw his pack and dug out some water drinking it down greedily. This was a very strange feeling he had inside, almost like he was empty, like he had been hollowed out somehow. He shrugged it of, after all it didn’t mean anything, he was alive and seemed healthy, that was all that mattered anyways. For a moment he considered why he was here, and what had brought him here, Bah, it didn’t really matter anyway. Standing up and collecting his things he stepped out into the cool night air. Looking up into the sky, he saw the moon and stars glowing like gems in the blackness, normally he might have thought of how beautiful it all was or how he missed someone or something, but tonight he thought of nothing when he looked into that lovely sky, only which way is south. As he began to walk into the night, there was no whistling or singing as was his custom, nothing but his quiet footsteps and the sounds of the night.
