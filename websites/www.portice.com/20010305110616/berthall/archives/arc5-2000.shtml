Gordon on Spell Drops
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 Hello all,

I've noticed a number of threads here talking about spell-drops in certain areas of the game, and I thought I'd clear up a few misconceptions.

1. The chances of getting a spell are higher in some zones than others. Some zones (Karnor's comes to mind) don't have spells because there's enough loot there already.
2. Spell-drop chances are not completely random. In other words, you may have to go to several different zones to get all of your spells.
3. Not all spell drops are on the "random" tables. What I mean here is that sitting at a dungeon enterace or exit and pulling from the closest room will not guarantee that you get all of the spells in the zone/dungeon sooner or later. You might have to venture in further.

I hope this might clear up some of the ideas about spells out there. If anyone has any other questions or concerns, please post in this thread and I'll see if I can address your issue.

-Gordon

Food for Thought: Risk in PvP
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 I often get email from a few folks who make some very good arguments concerning risk in PvP. Basically, the standpoint is that if the environment does not contain risk, there is no sense in engaging in it. "Item loot" itself does not handle this risk, as players of casting classes are not affected to the same extreme as melee characters.

One argument is that we should implement experience-loss in PvP. I like this idea, unfortunately some core issues with the way life in EQ works makes implementing this unreasonable. For anything that couldn't be handled in code, an additional load would be placed on the CS staff. Believe it or not, even though people playing on PvP servers should be more tolerant of interaction with other players, 90% of the petitions there are for some kind of perceived harassment.

The core EQ system, meaning, the things that define EQ as EQ, do not allow some of these issues to be resolved without drastically changing the PvE portion of the game which we want to remain intact.

That said, I still firmly believe that some form of risk associated with PvP needs to be in game. Since that risk cannot be experience-based, and item-based risk doesn't hurt people who can bag their items or fight without them, I'm considering the following option: Making it so that everyone, regardless of class, can only bind in cities on the PvP Servers only.

City-only binding (again, on PvP servers only) would allow people to, for instance, eject an opposing force from a zone, and provide the additional risk of making all of them run back to recover their corpse and engage again.

What are your thoughts on this? Do you feel that PvP needs more real and equal risk associated with it regardless of class?

-Gordon

Bug while followiing mobs
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 Have you ever gotten a mob so low on life they try to run away? When they run, lets say they hit a tree or some other obstacle. What happens? You can still see the Mob but you cant hit them, then a couple of seconds later they appear about 10 feet ahead of where they really are.

Basically, you see them hit the tree and go around it, but what really happens is they walk right through it and it takes a couple of seconds to refresh. Does this bug anyone else???

This happens due to the way collision with objects works server-side versus the prediction engine of the client. Basically, the server doesn't acknowledge the object (a tree for instance) so has the NPC walk through it. The client on the other hand sees the NPC hit the tree, and figures that he NPC will get stuck walking up against it.

In a few seconds, the server resync's the client and everything is updated. It's one of the minor drawbacks to the system that we adopted to minimize the effects of internet latency and reduce bandwidth usage.

    Numbers over 1000 are indeed calculated.

    Back in beta, for a time, AC and ATK were displayed with two numbers each, showing the different components of the factor. For instance, AC was displayed with both a damage avoidance and a damage mitigation factor. This was highly confusing to a majority of the players, so both numbers were combined for the purpose of display.

    Basically, the two numbers were added, then divided by a theoretical max that one could attain at the time, yielding a percentage. It's this percentage that is displayed.

    Now, when we brought new items into the game that raised the true theoretical max (as opposed to the calculated theoretical max), and espicially when we brought out Kunark, we could have raised the calculated theoretical max so that the percentage would always be below 1000, but that would have resulted in most people seeing their AC drop, making them think that we had somehow nerfed it. So we left the calculated max the same, yeilding numbers greater than 1000.

    For actual combat calculations, the values you see are still reduced into their specific componenets which aren't capped with exception of lower level twinking-impact caps that we've referenced before.

    The concept of risk and reward is very important to the game of EverQuest. Ideally, situations that provide a greater reward should also provide substantial risk. Over the past several weeks, we've been identifying areas in game where risk and reward are at a disparity, and making necessary adjustments.

    In most cases, those adjustments are in favor of increasing the reward for a specific action. For example, we recently increased the experience awarded in many Kunark zones to offset the sometimes-considerable risk associated with adventuring there. We also do this quite frequently by increasing the reward associated with a quest by enhancing the item, as we did last week with the Burning Rapier quest.

    However, in some cases there are instances where reward is far in excess of the risk involved, and we will be correcting these issues like those above as we come across them. For example, there are many cases where a solitary and stationary outdoor creature has a "fast spawn", yielding the reward of fast experience-gain without the commensurate risk.

    Though it is not our policy to announce changes to individual NPCs, we did want to let you know that you will see risk/reward disparities corrected over the next several weeks.

    PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Character Transfers

    As many people playing upon the PvP-Teams servers are aware, Vallon Zek and Tallon Zek have different rules regarding looting rights from PvP combat. Vallon Zek allows the victor to loot all coin plus one item from the fallen, whereas Tallon Zek allows only coin-loot with no right to loot items.

    Due to the fact that the looting rules were changed on Tallon Zek post-production, we've decided to allow characters on both servers a one-time character-move. Registration for the move will open at 6:00pm PDT on Friday, June 9th, 2000, on both servers, and will be open through 12:00pm PDT on Monday, June 12th, 2000. You can register for the move by executing the "/movelog yes" command during the registration window. There is no limit to the number of people that we will allow to move between Tallon and Vallon Zek, so there is no reason to rush to enter the command.

    Please remember that once you register for the move, your decision can not be rescinded. The actual character-move will take place sometime next week, at a time to be announced later. Please keep in mind that corpses are not moved in a character-move, so we'd recommend against leaving unclaimed corpses around after Monday of next week if you've asked to be moved.

    Questions about the move process will be addressed in the Gameplay Message Forum available via www.everquest.com.

    - The EverQuest Team

    Gordon on the Resurrection Issue
    [ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

    		 In a recent thread on this subject, Abashi responded:

    "I believe I mentioned before that this isn't a bug. Player characters get one chance to accept a resurrection. If they refuse, they can still be transported to the site with a resurrection, but won't get experience."

    That is completely accurate, but does not precisely capture the entire issue. If a player does not accept the Resurrection (or Resuscitate) for ANY reason they have completely lost the opportunity to receive a restoration of experience. Those reasons can include:

    • Being AFK and the cleric not bothering to check until the player is ready.

    • Going LD while the Res is being cast.

    • Zoning while the Res is being cast.

    • Saying no because you have no idea what spell was cast or upon which corpse (nobody contacted you ahead of time).

    • Being unfortunate enough to have someone open a trade with you (interferes with the dialog box).

    • The zone repops while waiting for the Res (all information gone).

    • My favorite of all, some well -or not so well- intentioned cleric casting Revive upon your fresh corpse. That's right, you can absolutely remove the possibility of a player receiving an experience res by tossing a good old Revive upon them. There is NOTHING the recipient can do to protect themselves against this. Nothing.

    The list above is intentionally left short; I'm sure the more imaginative among us can come up with many more examples.

    In discussions about this issue on the EQ-Cleric site some clerics pointed out that nobody was absolutely entitled to an experience ressurection. I agree and disagree with that statement. In my opinion it would be more accurate to state that:

    "Nobody is entitled to having a level 39+ cleric Rescusitate or Ressurect them, but if a player were fortunate enough to have one willing to do it, then they are absolutely entitled to being able to receive it successfully with regard to the game mechanics." In other words, if I have a cleric willing to Res me then I absolutely expect to get the experience restoration even if I miss the dialog box through being afk, crashing, zoning to avoid another death, or any other of the numerous possibilities. And as a cleric who provides many ressurections every night, I expect my recipients to always receive the experience I am willing to restore for them, even if I forgot to warn them I am casting (it's happened), they miss clicked (it's happened), they've gone link dead (it's happened), someone opened a trade window with them (it's happened), they went AFK after confirming with me (it's happened), the zone repops (it's happened), or any other conceivable reason.

    The solution to this is simple, although the programming may not be.

    • A player is eligible to receive an experience resurrection no matter how many times they click NO (or do nothing) until they either click YES, or their 2 hour time limit is expired.

    • If a zone repops please save the information required to provide the resurrection. I'm sure it's doable, albeit possibly difficult.

    • If a player goes linkdead AFTER clicking YES, either provide the experience anyway, or if that's not possible then allow them to still be eligible upon their return to the game.

    I agree with many who have stated before that NOBODY is 100% entitle to an experience resurrection from another player. I've said no so many times that I made a macro for it =P. However, if two players are willing to complete the transaction (a cleric and the recipient) then the game mechanics should be designed to ensure that the experience should be properly returned, regardless of the game/real life problems (e.g. ISP failure) that frequently occur. Furthermore, now that this issue is widely known, I must admit to being somewhat concerned about the possibility of malicious Clerics casting Revive upon people.

    I fail to see how Everquest is enhanced by allowing the situation to remain status quo.

    We've reopened this issue and are looking into the requested design change. Initially, some possible UI changes suggested looked optimistic, however we need to investigate the issue further before committing to a solution.
