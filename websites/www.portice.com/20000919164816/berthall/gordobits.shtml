Invis Unstackable

 >>>I play on the tribunal and informed a guide about the presence of this bug and was told they are investigating the problem. The bug is as follows, Normally i as a cleric could cast invis vs undead on myself and them have REGULAr invis cast upon and they would stack just fine. Since the last patch the msg "You are allready invisible" is given when attempting this. Could you please give an update so we know if this bug has even made it too the right people and when it might be completed. Thank You Much

It's not a bug. No class (except for rogues) can be invisible to both living and undead regardless of spells or abilities. So even though the spells would appear to stack, the recipient of the spell would only be under the affect of one spell.

A few weeks ago, I asked the coder to make the spells unstackable so that they didn't give the impression that being invisible to both is possible.

-Gordon

Why is Cannibalize a taunt?

 >>>Here i am helping my girlfriend level in FM...she(lvl 28) is fighting things and i(lvl 53) am just casting my slow on them and healing when she needs it. I can be sitting down while she beats the thing to half-way of it's health and it doesn't even notice me, but the second i stand up and cannibalize the things turn on me no matter how bad she taunted it and beat it when i have not casted anything on it but slow at the beginning of the fight

Since you cast a combat speed debuff on the creature, you were put on the creatures hate list. When you then cast cannabalize on yourself, you are casting a beneficial spell on someone that the creature hates, which causes the amount that it hates you to increase.

-Gordon

Simple idea to reduce customer dis-satisfaction

 I believe a large part of the perceived customer service problem is that, well, the process is pretty opaque. You /petition, and don't know how many guides/GMs are on (because they're anon) and don't know how many are in front of you in the queue. You ask a GM in chat, and don't know when he'll be able to answer, or even if he saw you. Etc.

Me: /petition I fell under the world and died
when zoning into Qeynos Hills, and want my corpse
and EXP back.

Server: Your petition is number 99 out of 101;
there are currently 2 GMs and 8 guides online; we
estimate your petition to be answered in 45-70
minutes.

... 10 minutes later ...

Me: /petition

Server: Your petition is number 49 out of 200;
there are currently 1 GM and 2 guides online; we
estimate your petition to be answered in 80-120
minutes.

The information is easy to calculate for the server based on the current petition queue and average petition response rate for GMs and guides; add a little slop to the estimate and you have a good time range to print.

Well, I would like it.

Interesting idea. I don't see any issues that would prevent us from putting something like this in. I'll chat with the team about it and see what they think. And for future reference, can we speak of "increasing customer satisfaction" rather than "reducing customer dis-satisfaction" in the future?

-Gordon

On combinable containers "eating" items...

 Hello all,

The original thread on the Partisan pack shifted later to a topic about combinable containers in general. I wanted to separate out the two issues and discuss them individually. I've started a separate thread on the issue concerning the unannounced change of the partisan pack, which is a bag given by an NPC for the purpose of completing a quest.

In this thread I'd like to discuss my comments where I said:

"Concerning items in combinable containers, I think that I'm going to chat with the programmers Monday and see if they can make the containers eat any items in them upon logoff/Linkdeath. I think that there'd be no question of the intent of those bags then."

After reviewing this post that I made earlier, I do agree that it appears to be a threat when viewed out-of-context, and understand the general reaction that followed. The point of the post was to respond to multiple posts from people earlier in the thread who had no idea that they shouldn't use combinable containers, particularly free bags given by NPCs to complete quests, for general item storage.

I'd thought that the number of people using the bags for this purpose were the exception rather than the rule, an observation that was apparently mistaken given public response to my post. Given that public response, we won't be taking any steps to "destroy" items in combinable containers upon logout or linkdeath.

I do apologize for the way my response was phrased and hope everyone understands that neither I nor other members of the tuning team actively seek out ways to make the lives of the players more difficult.

-Gordon

20 Questions for Gordo

 >>>1. Camping. Why couldn't the "long portion" of the camp be put at the OTHER end of the camp? Perhaps the NPC needs time to "craft" the quest item and changes in some way to show that they are ready to give the reward. Let the other items spawn almost continuously, and people would NEED to camp anything but the reward PC.

It's a doable idea, but wouldn't this be just a camp by another name? Whether I'm camping a creature that I want to kill, or one I want to talk to, it's still a camp. In this case, I can see 20 people standing around an NPC waiting for him to "be ready" and fighting over who can hand him the items fastest.

>>>2. Are there currently any suits of armor that give some "hidden" effect or bonus by virtue of wearing the entire suit?

No, there are not. It's a good idea though that might have some application for later implementation or future products.

>>>3. Are there currently any weapons in the game that have any "hidden" effect?

Not really. What an item can do is expressly stated upon it. We do have some plans for more cursed weaponry in the future, of course we just might have to be creative to "hide" the effect of the curse.

>>>4. If the answer to 2 and/or 3 is "no", are there any plans to add this feature to the game?

See above.

>>>5. You once said that GZ was going to conduct some tests with the wizard's spells vs. the other caster classes. Have these been completed?

Actually I believe that Brad said that, and it is indeed our opinion that offensive power is appropriate for the class.

>>>6. Why, exactly, was the definition of the wizard class changed on the everquest.com page under the classes section?

What change and when was it made? To my knowledge, it has read what it reads now since release. I believe that there was a change at one time in the beta period where it was changed from "damage" to "magical damage", but that's all I believe happened. Was there something else?

>>>7. Are there any plans to introduce items that directly influence/enhance trade skills? (Gnomish Jeweler's Loupe, Dwarven Smith's Hammer, Elven Sewing Needle, etc.)

Do you mean items that allow you to make better items with trades? It's a good idea, but we no firm plans in that direction.

>>>8. Why, since dwarves in fantasy literature (and even in EQ) are the finest smiths in all the realms, is there no ore in Kaladim? It even has a mine.

Good question. There's no ore in Kaladim? Hmmm, I'll check into that to see if it was a design or an oversight.

>>>9. Why can't enchanters be given a high-level illusion that allows them to change into the nearest ANIMATE object?

Races are a component of faction, and you adopt whatever race-faction you emulate. There's a lot of special case-coding to handle the animate races that players currently emulate. Opening that up to nearly everything else would be extremely problematic, if not impossible.

>>>10. Are there any plans to introduce a spell or item that absorbs magic directed at the wielder, perhaps converting it directly into mana/hit points?

There is already a bard song that has a damage shield that absorbs magical damage. There's also a cleric "reverse damage shield" that heals anyone that hits the target. That's the extent of what we have, and nothing new along these lines are planned.

>>>11. Are there any plans to introduce a spell or item that REFLECTS spells back at the caster?

No.

>>>12. Why can't the monk-only weapons that look like gloves be worn on the hands, and add directly to the ATTACK stat instead of requiring them to be 1h blunt?

Items cannot be set to use hand-to-hand skill, and combat can't go to an "equipment" slot to get combat statistics.

>>>13. If Verant doesn't like "spoiler sites", then why are there no spell descriptions anywhere in the game?

I think that you've answered your own question. We don't like spoilers. We'd prefer people figure these things out on their own and maintain their own knowledge-base.

>>>14. Will players ever be able to construct golems? (A.k.a. summon a temporary "golem-type" pet through some complicated quest)

We could certainly, by use of trade skill or quest, award an item that summoned a pet of any particular type, however we have no plans to do that at present.

>>>15. Necros can charm the undead; enchanters can charm...well...everything. Will Magicians ever have the ability/spell to charm elementals or other summoned creatures?

No, we have no plans along these lines at present.

>>>16. Are there any plans for an expansion geared toward the 10 - 30 range of player rather than the higher levels?

Velious is geared towards a higher level-range, however Kunark has support for all level ranges.

With Velious, the thinking is that there is already plenty of content to support the continually migrating portion of the customer-base that is in the 10-30 range.

I'm unable to talk about any post-Velious plans at this time.

>>>17. The Trackers' Water Extractor - This is currently broken, is it not? The purification tablets do not seem to exist, and the NPC that mentions them won't respond.

I inquired about this time time back, when Kunark first launched, and it was verified at the time that the tablets do exist. If I need to check into it further, please let me know.

>>>18. Bandages vs. Aloe Swatches vs. Maneater Plant Salve vs. Tailored Silk Bandages: Is there any difference other than price i.e. More healing, faster healing, etc.

No difference at this time.

>>>19. Are there any plans to give clerics any type of special ability that is tied to whatever deity they worship?

No, there are currently no plans along these lines. However, there are plans and implemented pieces of deity-specific quests that return deity-specific rewards.

>>>20. Are there any races and/or classes that you, personally, would like to see added to the game?

If we added a warrior/wizard hybrid, could we call him a "fire fighter"?

-Gordon
