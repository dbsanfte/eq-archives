Why is Cannibalize a taunt?

On combinable containers "eating" items...
[ comments] Submited by Portice

 Hello all,

The original thread on the Partisan pack shifted later to a topic about combinable containers in general. I wanted to separate out the two issues and discuss them individually. I've started a separate thread on the issue concerning the unannounced change of the partisan pack, which is a bag given by an NPC for the purpose of completing a quest.

In this thread I'd like to discuss my comments where I said:

"Concerning items in combinable containers, I think that I'm going to chat with the programmers Monday and see if they can make the containers eat any items in them upon logoff/Linkdeath. I think that there'd be no question of the intent of those bags then."

>>>6. Why does sitting increase the aggro distance on enemies, especially green ones?

It really doesn't, however, sitting PCs are automatically added to the hate-list of any NPC in the awareness radius. Normally, an NPC that isn't mad at you, but would attack you anyway, becomes aware of you before they react to you. So, in other words, if the awareness radius is 200 ft, and the reaction radius is 100 ft, a wolf would attack a standing person once he crossed into reaction range. However, when the person is sitting, they are added to the hate list at the awareness range. The ranges themselves don't actually change.

This is intended.

You'll never get spun if you are simply facing the creature. You only get spun if you are turning at the time you are stunned. I've always thought that this was a neat effect.

Monster Exp Info
[ 0 comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 Information on Monsters
When you move the cursor over a monster, you see the name of its class. Behind the name in red appears the monster's hit-point bar graph. It shows the percentage of hit-points remaining. The name of the monster is color-coded. White is a standard monster type, blue is a champion, and gold is a unique monster.

Monster Difficulty and Experience in Multiplayer games
In multiplayer, when a Monster is created, it checks to see how many players are in the game. The Monster's Hit Points are multiplied the number of players in the game: (Life = Hit Points * Number of Players in the Game). The Monster's Base Experience will be determined by the following formula:

Experience = X + (n-1)*(2/3X)

X = base experience
n = # of players in the game

This will determine the base experience of a Monster.

Monster Level vs Player Level
Monsters that are a higher level than your Character have an easier time hitting you and you will have a harder time hitting them. You will have an easier time hitting monsters that are a lower level than you, and they will have a harder time hitting you.

Monsters on Nightmare Difficulty
On Nightmare Difficulty, Monster hit points, Armor Class, Experience, Damage, Chance to Hit, and Resistances all go up.

Monster level goes up, according to this formula :

Nightmare Monster Level = Monster Level + 25
So, a level 2 Skeleton in the regular game is level 27 in Nightmare.

Monster skill levels go up +3.

Unique Monsters gain one bonus ability.

Monsters on Hell Difficulty
On Hell Difficulty, Monster hit points, Armor Class, Experience, Damage, Chance to Hit, and Resistances all go up.

Monster level goes up, according to this formula :

Hell Monster Level = Monster Level + 50
So, a level 2 Skeleton in the Normal game is level 52 in Hell.

Monster skill levels go up +7 in hell.

Unique Monsters gain two bonus abilities.
