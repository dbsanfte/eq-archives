4-28-2000
2 Patch Messages Today
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 April 28th, 6:00am
-------------------

*Patch Today*

Several changes were implemented as part of today's patch:

*Spells*

- "Scale of Wolf" is now group-only. You must be grouped with the target in order to cast this spell upon them. This change was made due to the fact that Scale of Wolf overwrites Spirit of Wolf, which may be more desirable in some situations.
- The "Cannibalize II" spell has been enhanced.
- Corrected a bug with "Song of the Sirens" that caused an unintended level-cap to affect the spell. Bards should now be able to charm everything that they once could with this spell.

*Binding in Timorous Deep*

Due to a feature within the zone, players can no longer bind within Timorous Deep. This functionality was intended in the zone prior to launch, as binding presents a possible balance issue. Players who are already bound in this zone will retain their binding location until they bind someplace else.

*Iksar Quests*

A few items associated with the Iksar newbie warrior quests have been repaired. The EverQuest team would like to extend thanks to those who reported the problems.

*Iksar Factions*

As announced in the patch server message yesterday, this morning we corrected a bug in Iksar faction that allowed them to travel outside Kunark without fear of being attacked by most things. In the event that any young Iksar find themselves in a bind/death loop, please /quit, log in another character, and /petition for assistance.

*Server Splits*

The recently announced server splits will be completed roughly two hours after the other servers come up from this patch. Movelog registered accounts from Veeshan and Bristlebane will be moved to Saryrn, and Xegony and Bertox to The Seventh Hammer. As requests to move were very strong, we were not able to move everyone who requested. We appreciate everyone's understanding.

Thank you!

- The EverQuest Team


April 28th, 5:30pm
--------------------

*Registration System*

This patch corrects an issue that caused some overseas customers creating new accounts to have problems accessing Kunark content. Thank you and enjoy the weekend.

- The EverQuest Team

Brad on Framerates
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 Just to clear up this rumor:
The trees swaying have NOTHING to do with framerate.

Two things affect framerate: number of polygons drawn, and fill rate (the number of objects in front of other objects in front of other objects, all of which have to be drawn).

Framerate goes down in a forest because of the additional polygons needed to respresent all the trees, as well as the trees behind the trees behind the trees having to be drawn.

Anyway, there are three general ways to increase your framerate (starting from easiest to hardest):

1. Adjust your clipping plane down under the display options section.

2. Buy a faster 3D card.

3. Upgrade your CPU/Computer

The level of 3D sound can also affect overall framerate, depending on your system's configuration (which sound card, etc.) so you might want to experiment with those settings as well.

Bottom line, Kunark pushes the EQ engine a lot harder, hence the better visuals. Even though this was perhaps a non-issue for you with the rest of Norrath, when you're on Kunark you might now need to adjust your display settings.

-Brad

ps. just to be very clear, the animating trees do not measurably affect your framerate.

pps. framerate is how fast your screen is updated and has little to do with 'lag', which is generally another word for 'latency' which has to do with internet performance (timing issues, sending data between your client and our servers).
hope that helps

Gordon on Veeshan's Peak
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 Veeshan's Peak
Just for a bit of clarification, back when the planes were brought online, John Smedley commented that people just don't die in planes, they die hard. Veeshan's Peak makes the planes look easy. There exists a non-negligible chance that not only will you die there, but you might not be able to raise the necessary forces to allow recovery of your corpse. It is *very* dangerous.
In order to avoid any appearance of impropriety in the zone, as sometimes follows when we've assisted in corpse recoveries in other zones due to claimed bug-deaths, we're not going to risk that for Veeshan's Peak. Veeshan's Peak is incredibly dangerous, and in order to guarantee that everyone is operating under the same set of conditions, we've elected to make the zone "enter at your own risk".

That said, there are no known bugs in the zone that would cause the death of a character, and it was tested extensively in the Ruins of Kunark beta. We will certainly investigate and eliminate any bugs that are reported, but we won't be reimbursing items or experience, nor will we be moving corpses or people within the zone.

Kunark Goes Live
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 *Kunark Goes Live*

In case the announcement from the League of Antonican Bards wasn't to your liking, we are very pleased to announce that the EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark expansion has been implemented on the live servers. Adventurers who have installed the expansion and registered their account key may now visit the new lands, exceed level 50, create Iksar characters, and access Kunark-related content in general.

Here is a short list of some of the new features available to those who have upgraded:

- Players may now reach level 60!
- Over two dozen new zones to explore!
- Tons of new quests, items, and spells to complete, acquire, and learn!
- All-new creatures and weapons, with new art too!

There are two minor issue that is known and that we are still in the process of rectifying: Action-based sounds for the Iksar and some other Kunark creatures are still being worked on. In other words, you might hear a giant mosquito "walk" by you, and your Iksar might sound curiously like a human when he is hit with a sword. In addition, there is some additional work required with a couple of the starting Iksar quests. This may cause you to reach a point where you cannot proceed further in a quest. This will not however cause you to turn in items and be denied your reward, so feel free to explore these new quests. We will be finishing them over the next week.

We would like to offer special thanks everyone who has been patient over the past couple of weeks while we've readied EverQuest for this awesome event. It has been quite a challenge for our team to get everything ready for launch, and your support has been instrumental in this process. We'd also like to thank the beta test team, who in the face of all of the new content managed to keep their thoughts between themselves and the Dev Team, while working to make the game better for everyone.

If you ordered Kunark on or before 4/19/2000 and do not receive your Kunark CD by today, we will be posting instructions later today on how to claim the shipping refund as well as the free month of EverQuest promised last week.

Thank you,

The EverQuest Team

Multiguild Communications?
[ comments] Submited by Webadmin

 I'm currently engrossed in a discussion on the Station (G_Lightyear) about what was initially a discussion about how a /guildalliance flag would allow guilds to ally themselves for the purposes of merging communications during attacks.

Rather than continue to add hacks to what I am beginning to feel, as I think about it more, is a fundamentally flawed communication system, I've sort-of-proposed a replacement communications system for Everquest that I'd like to see them look into implementing.

In order to further this discussion, get in some fresh ideas, and solidify the proposal before submitting a more solid and well-thought proposal to Verant for implementation, I'd like to get some others' opinions on what the ramifications of this communications system would mean for the average player.

I believe that the proposed modification would:

* Put all of the communications mechanisms which would exist into an in-game, roleplayable experience,

* Truly offer individuals control over whom they can communicate with and how,

* Give individuals new and powerful capabilities in ways that break the boundaries between groups, individuals, etc.,

* Open up new areas of spells and magic by allowing personal connections between players that can be affected through magic cast through the link.


I'd like everyone to have a look. Rather than simply "bump"ing the message, I'd be interested in seeing what people really think, whether they'd use it, whether they'd prefer the existing system, where they think it would be difficult, and where they think it would be better. I can certainly see some drawbacks; drawbacks I haven't actually stated, but I think are obvious. However, I think the benefit, in the end, would be an Everquest that seemed a lot more like a role-playing experience to the average person.

Have a read, give it some thought, and help to improve Everquest by investing some of your own experience into the discussion.

The discussion on the Everquest boards can be found by going to here

Sincerely,
Meeror
