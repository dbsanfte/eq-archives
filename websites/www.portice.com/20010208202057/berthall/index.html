  More On the Donal's BP
  02-01-2001
  More On the Donal's BP
[ add comment ] submitted by: Porticemaster
  I wanted to add a little more detail and some of my thoughts about ripple affects whenever significant changes are made to a game like this. When we increased the level cap, there were unforeseen consequences. And so we had to make adjustments. This understandably upset some players -- in fact, any change to the status quo can be upsetting to many. Now that we've increased the HP cap, there are also some unforeseen consequences. The breastplate is just one -- the perceived problems you've posted about class balance and usefulness as a result of the HP cap removal might may well be other consequences (and we're already starting to debate and discuss them).

Undoubtedly, we'll have to make more adjustments, bringing the game back closer to balance. Some of these changes will probably be negatively perceived by some players, and hopefully others will be positively perceived. But, honestly and bluntly, the short-term happiness or disappointment is secondary in our decision making process. We fix and adjust and balance, especially after changes like this, to ensure the overall health of the game, because we fundamentally believe that our focus on the game's overall health and balance is what brings long term happiness to our playerbase. The alternative, really, is a static, unevolving game. If we don't try things out that we never planned to do, like increasing the level cap, or the MOB HP cap, then we risk a stagnant game. The genre is still new, and yes, in a sense, EQ will never be out of 'beta'. It's an experiment in many ways, because something like EQ hasn't really been done before -- there are very few games out there even close to EQ in terms of goals and scope.

The same goes for items. We've tried some new item ideas out in an attempt to make the game more interesting and varied. These include this breastplate, the manastone, mistwalker, etc. And yes, unfortunately, these also caused unforeseen consequences.

I submit, however, when considering the big picture and the game as a whole, it's better that we try this stuff than not. I also believe in my heart, all things considered, that EQ is a far better game today than it was 6 months ago, and waaaay better than it was when we launched. And, overall, I know it will continue to improve, and so also will our future games be all the more better given what we've learned, both from our successes AND our failures and mistakes.

I guess what I'm trying to say, or do, is manage expectations better, going forward. Perhaps another MMORPG will come along that never nerfs, never makes mistakes, but still evolves and expands, with a perfect dev and testing team, eager to try new things, but somehow impervious to failure.

But I really doubt it... years of MUDing and game development experience tell me otherwise. And, in any case, until then, we're going to march forward, doing our best, with a strong commitment to continue to both improve EQ and also expand EQ, be it with more content, more options, improved technology, etc.

Heck, we're already talking about some major additions to gameplay (not just new content) that we're going to make after we make a serious stab at getting the classes balanced in the next few months. I can't go into details yet, but I think they'll be great. But I also think, realistically, that we'll face challenges associated with the changes - in any case, though, we are going to continue pushing EQ beyond its original scope and vision.

I appreciate all of you who've stayed with us this long, and I encourage you to stick with us going forward -- we certainly couldn't have done it without you And while I wish some of your expectations were a bit more realistic and forgiving, I'm at the same time very glad we have this board so you all can keep us on our toes -- it's an invaluable resource.

take care,

- Brad

Brad later returned to make a few more comments:

TTuuaann: We do play the game. Really. I promise We have people on the dev team, in testing, in customer service, in operations, in upper management, etc., who love and play the game, who play hour after hour, who work on the game all day, and then head home to play it into the wee hours of the night.

We have power gamers, role players, and casual gamers. We have low level players, mid level players, and high level players. We even have people in many of the well-known 'uberguilds', and let me tell you, I really wish sometimes I could let you know who they were -- it might be a big surprise But so that they can do their jobs without the perception of favortism or even cheating, I can't.

In fact, at this point, there are probably more dedicated EQ players on the EQ Live team than there ever has been, and I believe we're more in touch with the game than ever before.

So, there you have it. Yes, we make mistakes and screw up in our attempt to make EQ an even better game, but it's simply NOT because we don't play it.

We DO play it.

Something else occured to me after reading through the posts.... Our position is that 5 or so mobs need to be adjusted post-nerf. Others disagree and claim that much more of Velious 'requires' the BP.

Let's say we're wrong and there are many more mobs that are simply too tough without the BP. Think on that a minute -- that just overwhelmingly strengthens the argument that the BP needs to be changed.

The more Velious content that requires the use of this item, a rare drop off of Trakanon, the more it would be true that the majority of our playerbase could never enjoy Velious. We can't have one rare drop off of Trakanon be a gating factor for our player base to enjoy Velious (whether it's fewer mobs as we believe, or more as others may believe).

So, we'll change the BP, and adjust mobs as necessary. NO one item should be necessary to be victorious in an expansion, or even significant parts of an expansion... especially not a rare drop on a formidable dragon many players will never encounter.

- Brad

  Donal's Breastplate Changes Coming
  01-30-2001
  Donal's Breastplate Changes Coming
[ add comment ] submitted by: Porticemaster
  We've changed this item before because we thought it was overpowered. Unfortunately, it is still overpowered. Let me explain why.

This item is a breastplate that is only usable by clerics. If you right click this item, it will cast a Complete Heal on your target with a casting time of 30 seconds. It uses no mana to produce this effect.

A group of two characters, one cleric wearing this breastplate and one warrior, can defeat any opponent, as long as that opponent can not kill the warrior in under 30 seconds.

Let me restate that.

One cleric and one warrior, using this one magic item, can defeat ANY opponent or any number of opponents that are not capable of killing the warrior in under 30 seconds.

I hope that the impact that this game dynamic has on the game is obvious to everyone. If we allow this item to remain in the game as it is, we will have to change the game so that monsters are capable of killing a warrior in 30 seconds. 15 seconds if the group consists of two clerics with this breastplate and one warrior, and it gets worse from there...

Of course, if we make the world so hard that this breastplate is not overwhelmingly powerful, we would have to, essentially, rebuild the game, rebalance the classes No single item should be so powerful that the balance of the game revolves around it. Adjusting the game to fit this item is not a choice. We need to change the item to fit the game.

We have had a fair amount of internal debate over this item. It really comes down to two choices. Eliminate the item entirely, or change the way it works. We would rather change the way it works. After much debate and some conversations with players that have characters that use this item, we decided on the following change.

The item will cast a new spell (to be named later). The effect of this spell will be to completely heal the target. The spell will also leave a buff on the recipient that lasts somewhere between five and ten minutes (we are leaning toward a compromise of 7.5 minutes). This buff will have some small positive effect (perhaps a 1 point regenerative effect), but as long as this buff is on a character, he can not receive the effect of this spell again. This buff will not be removable until its duration runs out.

In practice, what this will mean is that the cleric with a Donal's Breastplate will be able to heal anyone he wants every 30 seconds. But he will not be able to heal the same person twice unless 7.5 minutes has passed between healings. The breastplate will still almost completely eliminate downtime due to hit point loss, and it will no longer destroy the game dynamic of EverQuest.

There is one concern that we have as to this implementation, and I'd like your feedback on it.

There are some cases where a character would have all of his buff slots full and be unable to gain a new buff. This new Donal's effect would be, essentially, a buff. We can either have it overwrite a current buff, or have the spell fail if the character has a full load of buffs on him. I can see arguments for either implementation, and I want to hear your thoughts on it.

Yes, we know this is a 'nerf'. And, frankly, we're willing to nerf this item to save the game as a whole. We are sorry about ever creating an item that is so easily unbalancing to the game, and we're sorry that we have to change it again. For now, I can't tell you when this change will take place. It won't be until after we've finished the work that has us on hold for updating the game. When I have a better timeframe, I'll certainly let you all know.

Alan

  More Favortism on TT? or is it Legit..
[ 1 comment ] submitted by: Iila BellaRose
  It would appear that Verant didn't learn their lesson with the whole Bunnies of Chaos thing. Either that or it has just been so long that they hoped everyone had forgot.

It appears that a small group of players on Terris-Thule got some pretty special treatment from someone at Verant. Special treatment in the form of high level VP and Plane of Air items. You can read the whole thread right here.

http://boards.station.sony.com/everquest/Forum2/HTML/050912.html

http://boards.station.sony.com/everquest/Forum2/HTML/050542-6.html

All of the problems center around the player of the characters Hathorne (L60 shaman), Langley (L60 magician), and Hath (L60 warrior), all reportedly leveled to 60 in the same manner it is done on the Test Server. This player, who has been described as a "non-paying account", seems to have some special tie with Verant Interactive, perhaps an employee or relative of an employee (he has told his guildmates he is the son of a Verant employee). The problem this player brings to the game environment has been to in some manner, repeatedly, *create* high-level items and introduce them into the game environment. On our server, Venril Sathir has been killed many times. However, we are a social community, and everytime it dies, everyone can easily find out who killed it, what it had, and who got the drops. At the time Hathorne began wearing his pair of JB Greaves, they had only dropped 3 times on the server, and all 3 shamans with them were public knowledge. Now, at this time, Hathorne had also never attended a VS raid with anyone that was successful, and neither has his guild. These greaves did not come from Venril, so where did they come from? Over the winter holdiay, Hathorne mircaulously began wearing a Jaundiced Bone Breastplate, again without ever attending a Trakanon raid. All shaman BPs dropped by Trakanon are well known, and none of them was ever traded or received by Hathorne. In the same manner, his warrior now has a cobalt breastplate, and this weekend, Hath began wielding the warrior epic weapon, even though his guild cannot get to Spiroc Island in PoA, he hasn't been there with any guild on TT that can, and he's never killed the Chardok Queen.

The item creation issue has been strongly contested by many players in the higher level community. To see this player and his group of friends run around in high-level, often VP or PoA equipment, is absurd, and cheapens what others work for and earn with skill and teamwork. The issues of why this is a severe game-inbalance should be obvious. For example, in an experience group with Hathorne, he told the group that a cleric was not needed. While a shaman can heal certain groups without a cleric, no other shaman possesses a 1-Handed Blunt Weapon with infinite charges of instant-cast Complete Heal, as Hathorne did that night. I have never even seen such an item located in EverQuest, and yet this and other items regularly appear in this player's arsenal.

Despite many players asking for some sort of explanation, Verant has remained silent on the issue thus far. Surprise, surprise. If this is true then it certainly wouldn't be the first time that some players got special benefits for knowing people at Verant. The problem is that players who work hard, very hard, for their loot don't really appreciate it when they see others running around with high level items that they didn't have to work for. When you make it so exceptionally hard to get these items and then just start handing them out to friends, someone is going to notice.

Hopefully we see some sort of statement from Verant about all this soon.
