Invis Unstackable

 >>>I play on the tribunal and informed a guide about the presence of this bug and was told they are investigating the problem. Why is Cannibalize a taunt?

 >>>Here i am helping my girlfriend level in FM...she(lvl 28) is fighting things and i(lvl 53) am just casting my slow on them and healing when she needs it. I can be sitting down while she beats the thing to half-way of it's health and it doesn't even notice me, but the second i stand up and cannibalize the things turn on me no matter how bad she taunted it and beat it when i have not casted anything on it but slow at the beginning of the fight

Since you cast a combat speed debuff on the creature, you were put on the creatures hate list. When you then cast cannabalize on yourself, you are casting a beneficial spell on someone that the creature hates, which causes the amount that it hates you to increase.

On combinable containers "eating" items...

 Hello all,

The original thread on the Partisan pack shifted later to a topic about combinable containers in general. I wanted to separate out the two issues and discuss them individually. I've started a separate thread on the issue concerning the unannounced change of the partisan pack, which is a bag given by an NPC for the purpose of completing a quest.

In this thread I'd like to discuss my comments where I said:

"Concerning items in combinable containers, I think that I'm going to chat with the programmers Monday and see if they can make the containers eat any items in them upon logoff/Linkdeath. I think that there'd be no question of the intent of those bags then."

After reviewing this post that I made earlier, I do agree that it appears to be a threat when viewed out-of-context, and understand the general reaction that followed. The point of the post was to respond to multiple posts from people earlier in the thread who had no idea that they shouldn't use combinable containers, particularly free bags given by NPCs to complete quests, for general item storage.

I do apologize for the way my response was phrased and hope everyone understands that neither I nor other members of the tuning team actively seek out ways to make the lives of the players more difficult.

>>>6. Why does sitting increase the aggro distance on enemies, especially green ones?

It really doesn't, however, sitting PCs are automatically added to the hate-list of any NPC in the awareness radius. Normally, an NPC that isn't mad at you, but would attack you anyway, becomes aware of you before they react to you. So, in other words, if the awareness radius is 200 ft, and the reaction radius is 100 ft, a wolf would attack a standing person once he crossed into reaction range. However, when the person is sitting, they are added to the hate list at the awareness range. The ranges themselves don't actually change.

Client (polygons) and server load. A zone currently needs to support X people, Y pets, and Z NPCs. As long Y=X, and Z is constant, the numbers remain manageable under most circumstances. As soon as Y=2X or 3X, it quickly becomes difficult, and the server would have trouble keeping track of them all.

Pet power was set based upon this one-pet limit. If we allowed multiple pets, their power would drop significantly.

Bug while followiing mobs

 Have you ever gotten a mob so low on life they try to run away? When they run, lets say they hit a tree or some other obstacle. What happens? You can still see the Mob but you cant hit them, then a couple of seconds later they appear about 10 feet ahead of where they really are.

Basically, you see them hit the tree and go around it, but what really happens is they walk right through it and it takes a couple of seconds to refresh. Does this bug anyone else???

This happens due to the way collision with objects works server-side versus the prediction engine of the client. Basically, the server doesn't acknowledge the object (a tree for instance) so has the NPC walk through it. The client on the other hand sees the NPC hit the tree, and figures that he NPC will get stuck walking up against it.

In a few seconds, the server resync's the client and everything is updated. It's one of the minor drawbacks to the system that we adopted to minimize the effects of internet latency and reduce bandwidth usage.

>>>What are your thoughts on no_drop? Am I missing an important reason why no_drop should still be in game for all but the most uber/critical items?

NODROP was originally created with the intent of requiring that someone be at a specific event in order to reap the benefits of it. Quest items are a good example of what NODROP was intended for. We also expanded upon this initial design as an anti-farming measure.

Of course, with all of the new lands, items, areas, creatures, etc., that Kunark brought to the table, and with the introduction of contextual random loot, the need for anti-farming measures is decreasing. Most of the items in Kunark are not NODROP unless part of some quest.

We probably won't, however, go about removing the NODROP tags from existing items.
