When Changes Should Be Preannounced
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 There were quite a few complaints when the dark elf camp was placed in Lesser Faydark, and when wolf form become KOS in cities, and for numerous other changes. Gordon has defended the changes, saying the world changes--you can't expect everything to be static. Unfortunately, both Gordon and many of the complainers have been wrong. The rule should be this: a change is NOT announced IF AND ONLY IF that change would be something reasonable in the fantasy world.

Example: Verant was right in not announcing the new dark elf camp. It is perfectly reasonable for dark elves to move into that camp.

Example: Verant was wrong to not announce the wolf form change. There is no way that suddenly all the NPCs in all the cities, good and evil, would decide they no longer like wolves.

>>>The rule should be this: a change is NOT announced IF AND ONLY IF that change would be something reasonable in the fantasy world.

I'll buy this to an extent. It's a fairly good measuring stick. It doesn't quite fit every situation perfectly but it's probably as good as a written rule as we can apply. I think that there are more cases where we would decide to preannounce something that this rule says we shouldn't have to.

Also, one thing to point out is the difference between announcing something and preannouncing it. We will typically try to pre-announce (meaning: Give advance notice of) changes that are likely to get characters killed.

-Gordon

A plea for the survival of Bertoxxulous
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

As we all know, many newcomers to EQ just click "PLAY," which sends them to the default server. Once, the default was the server with the lowest population. Player of McManus Lai'Dir, warrior of the Bertoxxulous server.

*Patch Day*

Greetings all! The following changes have been implemented on the Live servers:

We apologize to warriors that currently own this weapon.

A data error with the Symphonic Saber, causing it to report "Item out of charges", has been corrected. Due to the dynamics of this particular issue, current owners will need to contact a GM to trade this item out for a new, properly working version of the item.

The Burning Rapier (rogue weapon) has been enhanced. In addition to an increase in statistics, it received a stronger spell effect. The lightsource effect on the weapon has been removed due to an aesthetic rogue desire to be "sneaky"

The effect on the Hangman's Noose will now work properly.

*General Changes*

- Some NPCs in Dalnir were casting spells much too powerful for their level. They have been adjusted.
- Pathing information has been updated in Highkeep. NPCs and pets should have a much easier time of navigating the zone.
- Some Quest-NPCs were not spawning properly in Rathe Mountains. These issues have been corrected.
- A handful of problems reported with research practice via the Concordance of Research have been corrected. All practice-rune recipes should now work correctly.
- Gorilla's in Burning Wood now have a new texture set that properly reflects their undead status.
- Fishing should now work properly in Kunark zones.
- /yell now reports the proper direction to people hearing the yell.
- Corrected a bug that gave some races the impression that they were allowed to start their character in Cabalis. They were never actually allowed to create the character there, however the indication that they could has been fixed.
- Corrected sound issues that were causing some crashes to the desktop in certain cases.

We are in the process of patching music files (*.XMI) for zones in which music does not currently work. There are 55 small files (around 2K each) that will be patched over the next couple of days to enable music in all of the affected zones.

The zone of Timorous Deep has been tuned and itemized. Certain aspects of the changes have made certain areas of the zone more dangerous, but the reward should be well worth the risk. Feel free to visit this zone and search out its treasures at your leisure. Note: These changes went in on Monday.

Upon investigation, we discovered a deficiency with the amount of experience being rewarded in many Kunark zones (mostly dungeons). The affected zones have been updated, and adventurers should notice the greater experience reward in return for the increased risk and challenge associated with these zones.

- The EverQuest Team

Gordon on Ranger Skills
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 Why then is it so difficult to at least admit the possibility that the design team may have erred when they did not give rangers ANY defensive skill increases post 50? Why can't you at least admit that perhaps the thousands of Rangers who are being pounded by enhanced Kunark Mobs while being condemned to having Level 40 Defense skills might know something that you and your design and testing teams don't? Why can't you at least check it out?

Because we sat down as a group and specifically designed out all of the aspects of the level 51+ skills and abilities. It wasn't an oversight, but something that we planned.

As for having "level 40 defensive skills", it doesn't work like that. Proficiency in a particular skill is not merely a function of skill points, but a function of level and stats as well. In other words, just because you stop gaining in one particular part of the equation doesn't mean that your proficiency hasn't changed. For instance, a level 60 warrior with 200 1HS hits better than a level 40 warrior with 200 1HS.

-Gordon

Gordon on Guards
[ comments] Submited by Porticemaster

 As we are sure many people are aware, there are guards that exist specifically for the purpose of giving new characters a place to run in order to be saved from a certain death. However, as many people have mentioned, these guards are frequently either dead, or engaged by players choosing to hunt them. This makes things very frustrating for newer players, and with the large influx of new players due to the release of EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark, we've decided to make a change.

Though we do not have a problem with players choosing to hunt guards, the newbie guards themselves, specifically the ones at the gates of cities and in the vicinity of newbie zones, need to remain alive under most circumstances. To that end, tomorrow morning we will be buffing up these newbie-protecting guards, making them immune to magic, and giving them some pretty good healing properties in order to make them less attractive to people hunting them.

In addition, though we will leave the guards inside cities as-is from the perspective of power, we've also found a problem with the respawn rate. Currently they are typically on six-minute timers, making them spawn much faster than an equivalent NPC would in a dungeon. We will be normalizing the respawn time of guards inside cities to bring them in line with dungeon NPCs.

-Gordon

 I'd like to be able to have more spell slots so I don't have to memorize spells everytime my buffs run out.

No plans to do this. The limitation to the choice of spells available to you at any given time promotes strategy. Some classes have a harder time with this than others. Wizards, for instance, usually don't have a hard time making their choices, where enchanters (and necromancers from personal experience) will always have an ongoing debate as to what the best spell lineup is.
