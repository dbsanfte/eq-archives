Introduction Caeron3000, formerly known as Infinium, is a Massively Multi-player Online Role-playing game which is still in early development, that promises to turn the genre on its proverbial ear. Magique plans to implement features that have never been heard of in the CRPG frontier. The Story Caeron3000 takes place on six planets in the Caeron solar system in the Andromeda galaxy. One of these planets, called Andraeus, has just been through a civil war which threatened to destroy the planet. On one side of the conflict were those who embraced technology, on the other were those who rejected it. The result of the technological and arcane war were 5 distinctly different races, and where once a technological conflict separated them, now their racial differences substitute. A few hundred years have passed since that last dreadful war, and now a new threat arises to take its place. The Hawkeen, from the nearby planet Rethulim, have set their sites on conquering Andraeus. Already they have a foothold on Andraeus, and war descends upon the planet once again. Gameplay Caeron3000 will implement some truly unique features, one of them being a classless character system. Instead of the old class systems - where you could play a warrior, thief, ranger, mage etc. - you aren’t limited to such strict interpretations in Caeron3000. YOU choose what direction your character will take. This adds a totally new dimension to the role-playing experience, as you can advance any of your skills as the game progresses, provided that you can find a trainer. Another breakthrough aspect of C3K is that there will be an implementation of character death. That’s right folks, no more endless respawning, you can die permanently. However, to make the game fair Magique has designed a process to lower the likelihood of dying permanently by instituting the concept of cloning centers. You must regularly go to these cloning centers to ensure your next death won’t be your last. Along with these cloning centers you can buy insurance to make sure all the items you had when you died get back to you. No more having to go collect your belongings off your old corpse! Magique is also introducing their new Life-Link Artificial Intelligence system. Thomas Scott, President of Magique Productions, describes this system thusly, “Simply put, non-player characters (NPCs) in the game have a link to real lives. They are not just static automatons that stand there waiting for you to click on them for information. Nor will they simply march back and forth in a repetitive pattern performing the same mundane task, oblivious to their surroundings. With Life-Link, each NPC has his own life that is very much like the lives of the characters that are controlled by real people.” NPC’s have a number of different variables that determine how they act, what their doing and so on. If an NPC’s hunger variable gets too low, it will go buy food. If it’s tired variable gets low, it will sit down and take a rest. Conclusion Caeron3000 is one of the most promising and unique of the MMORPG’s in current development. If Magique can pull off what they intend to, Caeron3000 could be the most remembered and most played games of its kind in the future. And from what I’ve seen so far, that future looks pretty encouraging.  ShockForce - Bolt Action Software Details

Shock Force puts the player in control of a futuristic hovertank against other players from around the world in one of iMOL’s online arenas. Each arena is presented as a battleground on the surface a planet. Each arena is capable of handling up to 64 players. Players choose one of two sides and attempt to destroy the other side’s bases to take control of the planet. Orbiting above the battlefield are spaceships which supply each side. Supply is controlled by one player from each side by taking control of the "Uplink". The Uplink allows the player controlling it to determine what supplies are dropped to his side, to order the spaceship’s movement across the battlefield, and to call down orbital bombardments on enemy positions. It is possible to "warp in" other spaceships to reinforce your side, dropping more supplies and gaining an advantage in the orbital battle between the spaceships themselves. Spaceships are engaged in a continuous fight above. Evenly matched, battles between spaceships can only be won if more than one ship gangs up on an enemy. Otherwise, the battles are stalemates. Players can place "skypumps" on the surface, allowing more spaceships to warp in, increasing the odds and gaining an advantage over their foes.
