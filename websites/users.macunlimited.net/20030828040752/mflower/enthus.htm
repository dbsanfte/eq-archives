A short introduction, for the uninitiated

Vibracathedral Orchestra is Julian Bradley, Neil Campbell, Bridget Hayden, Adam Davenport and Michael Flower. They have been playing as a quintet since September 1998, although previous to this they were playing in private as two separate trios, sharing a common member in Michael Flower.

The music is usually built around fixed tonal drones - improvised, but not "improv" - more like dream music, trance music, whatever you like to call it - all played live, and usually recorded direct to two-track, the best way to capture the vast swirl and coagulation of the sound. There is a certain "primitivism" to the whole approach, a desire to cut to the heart of that which is vital and exciting about the music.

There are no fixed instruments for any of the band members, no "virtuosity", no "solos" - just elevated group dynamic. Accordingly, instrumentation is vast and quasi-orchestral, including:

  • Strings (guitars, violins, cello, japan banjo, mandolin, tanpura)
  • Wind instruments (clarinet, recorders, flute, whistles, trombone, shawm, various home-made reed devices)
  • Percussion (drums, hand bells, shakers, glockenspiel)
  • Keyboards (organ, toy piano, an arsenal of car boot sale bontempis and casios)
  • Voices (generally wordless unamplified accompaniment to thicken the drones)
  • Electronics (analogue synth, various home-built electronics and effects boxes, tapes)
