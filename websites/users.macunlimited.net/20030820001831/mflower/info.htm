News - August 2003

Seems like an eternity since we last updated, but we wanted to wait until we had some good things to lay on you (ok, maybe we're just lazy too). First big news is that we'll be touring the east cost of USA in early September with Sunroof!, due to the enthusiasm, savvy and hard work of Mr Tom Greenwood. Many thanks big man. Unfortunately, the band will be playing without Neil Campbell, who's too embroiled in bringing up baby to travel (how rock'n'roll), but they will be roping in some guest stars along the way to fill the big fat gap he leaves. Details so far are as follows:

thur 4 sept - boston ma, venue tba, w/ sunburned hand of the man

fri 5 sept - florence ma, newgrass center for underground culture, w/double leopards

sat 6 sept - northampton ma, hampshire college

mon 8 sept - philadelphia pa, khyber pass, w/ jack rose.

wed 10 sept - baltimore md, talking head

thur 11 sept - fredericksburg va, venue tba, w/ pelt

fri 12 sept - brooklyn, ny free 103 radio

sat 13 sept - nyc, venue tba, w/ samara lubelski

There may be another gig in NYC, and there will certainly be other bands on the bills, so check local listings for further details.

To celebrate the tour, our kindly patron Bill Kellum at vhf records will be releasing special tour CD, "wings over america", comprising 35-40 mins of music each by Vibracathedral and Sunroof!. It will be in edition of 500, so there should be some left over for non-east coast USA residents - keep an eye on this site after the tour dust has settled.

We've also finally finished our new full-length CD for vhf, entitled "the queen of guess". It'll be out in october and is quite a spangled beast over its nine tracks, which include a couple of longer pieces for all the hardcore out there.

Check the PRODUCT page here for details of a couple of recent releases, including "hex hostess", a CDR culled from our short european jaunt last March.

What else? The Julian Bradley "companion as glamorous as sleeping on wheels" CD is finally out on nice belgian label Veglia. Mail JB himself for details. More stuff in the pipeline from Julian too.

For any Neil Campbell solo or weird collaboration stuff (including disks with Rob Hayler, Ian Middleton, Campbell Kneale, etc etc) e-mail here. Campbell also gets to blether to Jon Dale over long-distance phone line in an interview just published in the fine fine Perfect Sound Forever online magazine, as well as talk about a few records he thinks are quite good in a recent Dusted feature (nice pic of him and his boy Noah on there too - aaaaahhh).

And news is that Bridget Hayden has been working on some solo recordings, ostensibly for release by Mental Telemetry. Keep your eyes + ears peeled!

And check the SOUND page some time next week for new MP3 downloads, most of which aren't intended for release anywhere. We wanted to include them with this update, but time time time isn't on our side, so rather than delay details of the american tour, we'll have a mini-update next week.
