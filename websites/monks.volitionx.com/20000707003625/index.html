Feb. 2, 2000 / 2:00pm CST (by Jekhar)

I got back from my vacation, which was quite enjoyable. Unfortunately, I've fallen quite behind in the news. Here's what little I know:

The monk fist haste patch is in, only affects your fists (not weapons), and starts at level 20. Your delay drops by 1 every 5 levels, with a delay of 30 at level 50. So at level 50, your fists are a 14/30 weapon.

The Plane of Air is open, but very difficult. It's a quest based zone, and quite expensive to traverse. There are six new quests for monks there, but I don't know anyone that has completed them. A good link I found for the quest information is at EQ Casters.

Also found on the plane of air are three new monk items. The Whitened Treant Fists are a 1HB weapon for monks, and currently look like a snow cone, although that should be changed soon. The stats on this weapon (14/28) make it the second best dual wieldable weapon in the game, second only to the Fanged Skull Stiletto. It is also better than our level fifty fists. This weapon was found on the Heart Harpy.

Brass Knuckles are found off a dragon-level efreeti named Noble Dojorn. Their stats aren't nearly as good (10/25), but they are used for one of the monk quests (Test of Fists).

The Belt of Tranquility is a new monk haste belt, found off Gusts of Wind I believe. Reports are differening, stating that it has the haste somewhere between a Flowing Black Silk Sash and a Cloak of Flames. All melee classes can find one of these belts in Plane of Air.

The Monk cap for One Hand Blunt has been increased as well. I've heard of someone getting a skill of 240 in this. It is rumored that it caps at 255, but I have heard no reports to confirm this. Two Hand Blunt cap has also been increased.

Finally, I've completed my Fire Giant Guide. This is the first "Detailed Guide" for a specific camp or adventure. It is mainly geared towards pullers, but hopefully it'll help out anyone wanting to do a Fire Giant run. I plan on doing more of these soon, with the next one likely being the Efreeti Lord run.

Jan. 12, 2000 / 4:3412pm CST (by Jekhar)

Ack, been way too long since last update. The big news of course, is that monks are getting a attack speedup. I'll quote Abashi's post on this rather then try to explain it. I wanted to get more details on the actual numbers before posting news on this, but numbers don't seem to be forthcoming:

Given some of the issues we’ve seen from the public lately in regards to Monks, we’ve worked with a few for the last 3 hours on an enhancement. Monks now receive a built in haste effect beginning in the high 20s. The haste effect increases with level, and stacks with haste-items and haste-spells. At 50th monks will find they do substantially more damage per minute.

I suppose this is a bit of a blessing and a curse, since doing more damage (over 15%) will make the creature more likely to turn on you, but we think it will be well received. This should reaffirm Monks as the masters of melee damage. I was even able to solo an experience-giving creature at 50th without using the mend skill.

Barring any problems on test, this should make the live servers at the next patch. Let us know what you think.

-Gordon

So I suppose it wasn't a pure solo. I'm going to have to go back in, try it again without the buffs but use mend if need-be. Oh, the mob tested was a 37th level Hill Giant.

- Gordon

Abashi, have they also changed it to where a monks delay can go below 20?

Yeah, it can go much lower than that now. The cap at 20 was based on the max effect of any haste-items and haste-spells combined. The built-in monk haste is computed before that. Though I of course won't give you the exact numbers, it can go substantially lower than 20.

- Gordon

Finally, the biggest news is a new monk quest that's been discovered. This is the new quest for the Treant Fists (AC 8.  + 3 Dex and + 5 HP). Here's a brief rundown on the quest:

  • Talk to Grim Oakfist in Kithicor and ask him about [Treant Fists]. Grim says he gave them to Master Puab in Freeport Ashen Order guild. (Not necessary)
  • Talk to Puab in the Ashen Order. Ask him about [Treant Fists]. He will tell you to give a coin to a banished Kerran monk in Kerra Isle. This kerran is called "a banished Kerran" and is on the heretic island in Kerra Isle (off of Tox Forest). He will tell you to find the remains of Thipt, who died in the Runnyeye area. He gives you a container to combine all of Thipt's remains (4 slot box).
  • Head to Runnyeye. You need the following 4 items: Ribcage Chest Armor (Goblin Alchemist in Misty Thicket, spawns around the goblin camps), Fractured Femur (Goblin Captain in Runnyeye, spawns near the king room), Catskull Cap (Gelatinous Cube in Runnyeye, spawns in the underwater passage behind the king room), Humerus Handled Mace (Goblin Alchemist in Beholder Maze).
  • Combine all the items in the box and return them to the banished Kerran. He will give you a note to give to Puab.
  • Give the note back to Puab and recieve the Treant Fists. Pretty nice item, especially for those monks that like AC.

All items in this quest are NO DROP, so you must do this entire quest yourself. Besides hunting the wanderer in beholder maze, most of the quest is pretty easy, without too much waiting. I haven't gotten the gloves yet, but I'm told they give the appearance of Maroon Plate. They should go nicely with the Robe of the Lost Circle. I just hope that somewhere there is a quest for maroon plate boots to complete the maroon look.

Dec. 15, 1999 / 2:43pm CST (by Jekhar)

Gordon Abashi, the new PR guy at Verant, responded to some questions asked about mobs seeing through Feign Death even after a successful feign. Here's the question, with the response in italics:

One thing on the "new" FD, aside from the nerfing it represents to +35 monks. There are a lot of times when mobs under level 35 will see through a successful feign (i.e. one with no "you have fallen to the ground" message). We knew of situations when this would happen before...lag, caster mobs casting before feign and then the spell hitting after feign and breaking it, etc. But now, a lot of monks are feigning with no lag, on low level non-casters...no failure message, and the mob continues to beat on them.
I strongly suspect this is a bug, or at the very least unintentional. There has never been an indication, not even in your explanation, that mobs get anything like a "save v. successful feign." This never happened before the first FD nerf patch.

Abashi, is this a bug or a permanent part of the nerf?

It's not a bug. In addition to the skill check for person who is feigning, each creature who is aggro'd on him or passes by has a *small* chance to see through it. It's the equivalence to an intelligence check. Some just won't fall for it.

The percentage changes haven't changed.

-Gordon

Dec. 13, 1999 / 7:56pm CST (by Jekhar)

I'll start off the news section by briefly going over all the relevant news from the last couple of weeks:

As many of our high level monks have noticed, the skill cap for Double Attack has been increased. Previously, it was maxxed at 200. Reports state that monks are now capping at 210. This cap is attainable at level 41. This should help slightly with our damage output. It's been reported that the correct skill cap is 225, and it's still a bug, but this has yet to be confirmed.

The Guise of the Deciever, the mask that allows players to turn into a dark elf, is no longer dropping. Instead, a Rogue/Bard only mask that must be worn to be used is dropping. Many monks were looking forward to becoming a dark elf monk, but this doesn't look likely for many of the newer monks.

The Robe of the Lost Circle has had its stats modified to make it more worthwhile. It used to be 9AC, 5AGI, 5STA, 15HP, 0.7weight. Now it is 12AC, 5AGI, 5STA, 25HP, 0.7weight. The quest has also been made slightly easier, in that Targin the Rock in Solusek B is now spawning alot more often. He spawned 5 times in 5 hours that I was there on Friday night. I'm not sure on Raster of Guk, although I did manage to get an idol after less than an hour of waiting.

Splitpaw has been upgraded to a 30-40 level dungeon. It's got some great experience, but no known good items for a monk. The new snakeskin mask might be considered useful for its 5 magic resist, but I believe there is another mask that gives the same (Lizardscale?).

Yeti, the Monk of Karana, a frequent poster on the monk message board, recently did a study of monk fist delay and how it is affected by haste items. To quote him:

I did stopwatch tests over 10 rounds of combat to determine delays prior to the last patch and got these numbers:

Naked monk: 36
Monk+FBSS: 30
Monk+Swirlspine: 30

I repeated these tests this weekend and got these numbers:

Naked monk: 35.5
Monk+FBSS: 29.0
Monk+Swirlspine: 30.75
Monk+Cloak of Flames: 26.4
Monk+CoF+FBSS: 26.4
Monk+CoF+SS: 26.4

Resulting hastes (rounded):
Swirlspine: 13%
FBSS: 18%
CoF: 25.5%

So:
The last patch gave us a slight decrease in HTH delay.
The swirlspine belt was nerfed.
The CoF doesn't stack with either of those belts.

Ah, I've finally gotten the site up in a working condition. Here's a little information on the site itself:

The main goal of this site is to have a dedicated news and information resource for monks. There are plenty of good monk information sites out there, most notably the EverQuest Monks forums, and eqstomped's monk page. Hopefully not too many people will be turned away by the lack of pizzaz, but heck, I'm just a programmer, I can't make spiffy graphics. The site is seperated into 6 major sections. This, the main page, is the news and update page, and everything new will be listed here. The forums link will take you to Korzon's monk forum, the best forum for monks. The equipment will take you to the Stomped equipment page. Information will contain quest information, essays, and studies on the monk class. The spawns for the Idealist, Realist, Herbalist and Tactician are wrong, so if anyone has time, please mail me with the correct spawn locations. The name of the Site, "Relentless Monk", comes from my guild's name (Relentless on Cazic Thule). Plus I thought it sounded cool, and I couldn't think of anything better. My name is Sandeep Shekar, I'm a programmer at Volition, Inc. I play a (currently) 41 monk on Cazic Thule named Jekhar Shadowfist, as well as various other characters, including a 48 Wizard.
