<Brewing Guide| Monk>

WARNING: This guide is meant for Monks who want to brew Heady Kiolas consistently as an ingredient for tailoring Cured Silk or Wu's Gauntlets. This contains a fair bit of spoiler information. It will take you to Brewing Skill 68, at which time you will be successful at brewing Heady Kiolas 90% of the time. Some information for going past 68 is also included.

Since I have a real life weakness for B&B (Benedictine & Brandy, angel nectar brewed by the Benedictine monks), Sindi the monk thought she should pursue the time-honoured monk tradition of Brewing. Most of this I've discovered through trial and error with several characters as well as perusing ALL the websites I could find.

Brewing Locations:

  • Freeport: West Freeport, brew barrel outside of Hog Caller's Inn. East Freeport inside Traveler's Holiday
  • Qeynos: South Qeynos in the Fish's Ale and in the Lion's Mane
  • Rivervale: Along the wall behind the Fool's Gold, on the way to the Druid Guild (Tagglefoot Farm) from the bank
  • Highpass Hold: Tiger's Roar Inn (you can fish out the window too).
  • HighKeep: 2nd floor

Ingredient Locations:

  • Snake skins: West Freeport is great. North Qeynos, East Freeport, and Misty Thicket are acceptable locations.
  • Fish: Fishing poles and bait can usually be bought from a vendor near every body of water. Fish is sold at the Port Authority in East Freeport, the building by the Qeynos boat dock, Fiddy Bobick in the building by Rivervale dock, Tiger's Roar Inn in Highpass Hold.
  • Grapes and Bottles: Available in most Inns that sell alcohol
  • Water: Lots of places, but I'll make some special notes. You MUST use store bought water. Foraged or summoned water will not work. If you are making Fish Wine in East Freeport, buy water from the building just before the gates out to the E FP desert. Voleen's Bakery in South Qeynos has water. Daleen Leafsway by the Druid Guild (Tagglefoot farm) and Fiddy Bobick at the docks in Rivervale are both convenient to the brew barrel.
  • Ale and Short Beer: Kristy Charcoal stands in the main path near the Militia House from West Freeport to North Freeport. Voleen's Bakery in South Qeynos has a female vendor. Golden Rooster in Highpass Hold. The gaming room in HighKeep 2nd floor has several vendors. Ale ingredients are not sold in Rivervale.

 

General Recommendations:

Training: Put 1 point into Brewing if you want to, but it is not necessary. Monks have precious few training points to waste.

Item Selection: Use <CTRL> <Left Click> to select 1 from a pile of 20 or less stacked items. You don't have to slide that frightful slider.

Intelligence and Wisdom: As with any skill you want to raise quickly, put on any Int and Wis items/jewellry that you have. It makes a big difference. Don't drink and brew, it lowers your Int.

Charisma: Wear any Charisma items you have while buying ingredients and selling drinks back (this includes sashes and headbands). It can substantially cut your costs.

Ale step: Don't skip the Ale step to get from skill 62 to 68. It only takes a few minutes, and makes a significant difference in your Heady Kiola failure rate.

Brew barrels and Ingredient locations: Print out complete list from www.eqtraders.com

Going past Skill 68: I've added some notes at the end of this guide. Monks are at a disadvantage because we don't have Foraging skill, so the most important recommendation is to make friends with Foragers who will collect Vegetables, Fishing Grubs, Fruit, and Morning Dew for you (Morning Dew is for Elven wine, it can be foraged in zones with vegetation early in the morning).

 

Method 1: Time consuming, VERY low cost

Sindi started brewing at level 3, so I did not have the cash to spend on a trade skill like some other people do. This information also assumes that you sell back all your brews to the vendor, and don't indulge in a little drinking.

Bog Juice: Skill 1-21

1 snake skin, 1 water, 1 bottle

Go out and kill several hundred snakes to get their skins (takes about 200 skins at newbie levels of Intelligence). Save eggs for personal food or sell to a baker (they need them for dough). Save any extra snake skins in the bank (more on this later), OR, keep brewing with the snake skins to make the money that must be initially invested into Fish Wine.

Cost: Close to break even (loss at first), net loss a few gold pieces.

 

Fish Wine: Skill 22-62

1 fish, 1 water, 1 grapes, 1 bottle

Grab a fishing pole and fish for hours (takes about 500 fish at newbie Intelligence). Recoup money spent on fishing poles and bait with the daggers and sandals, sell fish scales to casters. Try not to get discouraged, as you will only succeed 25-30% of your attempts at first. You will need approximately 7-8pp to get to 45 skill, but after that you will recover most of that. Recommendation: Spend some of your newbie life (levels 1-3) fishing (even after you reach your level's skill cap), and sock away the fish in the bank until you have some money to invest in this stage.

Cost: Your average cost/attempt is about 25cp at 21 skill to start, so you will lose money at first but start making a profit around 45 skill, overall net loss a few plat (2-4).

Notes for skill 21-62:

  • DO NOT make Short Beer like everyone says, it is a money pit.
  • DO NOT make Mead (trivial at mid forties), you lose money on it even when it is trivial.
  • DO NOT make Heady Kiola, even though it trivials at 46. At 5gp per attempt it is a money pit, be patient and get to skill 68.
  • DO NOT make Short Ale (trivial at 51), also a money pit.
  • Although Ale SEEMS to be profitable because it produces 3 (like Fish Wine), if you run the numbers it turns out that Fish Wine is less costly.
  • At 50 skill Bog Juice is about 95% success rate, so if you saved those extra snake skins then you can now indulge in some drinking at low cost.

Ale: Skill 62-68

The first brew without any harvested items, but at 62 skill you succeed about 70% of the time, thus recouping your costs overall.
Cost: break even

TOTAL COST: Under 5pp. Break-even or a small profit is possible with lots of Int, Wis, and Charisma items.

 

Method 2: Less time consuming, fairly costly

I still suggest not putting points into Brewing, because our points are precious. Sure, it will take you an extra 10 minutes, 100 mouse clicks, and 1gp, but you won't Flying Kick yourself when your Defense Skill is stuck at 229 for a month.

Skill 1-21

Bog Juice or Short Beer. You can auction for snake skins at 20 for 1pp, most newbies are quite willing to supply you with them since they fetch a lousy price at the vendors. Check the newbie vendors and buy up all the snake skins first. They are also sold in the Wizard's and Enchanter's Guilds in Erudin. (Short Beer if you plan on going to the Vodka stage, bank it and see below. Short Beer needs 1 Hops, 1 Barley, 1 Malt, 1 Cask).

Skill 21-31

Short Beer or Fish Wine. Buy the fish instead of Fishing. (Short beer for Vodka)

Skill 31-62

Fish Wine. This is by far the most economical drink to make even with bought fish, versus your other choices of Mead, Gypsy Wine, Short Ale and Ale.

Skill 62-68

Ale.

TOTAL COST: I haven't figured this out exactly, but it's somewhere in the 20-30pp range, and about 40-50pp if you make Short Beer.

 

Heady Kioals

Now you can make them with confidence. Kiola Sap is sold at almost all Inns on the alcohol vendors. The same vendors sell bottles. 2 Kiola Sap (unstacked), 1 water, 1 bottle in the brew barrel and Presto! Heady Kiola.

 

Good locations for Tailoring & Brewing simultaneously:

If you want to tailor your own cured silk, I recommend building yourself a Spider Suit of items with Save vs. Poison. Not many people want to go spider hunting for experience, so you will be soloing a lot. If you see a high level (45+) Necromancer, ask them to cast Dead Man Floating on you, it puts a ton of points in your save vs. poison column. Don't forget your bandaids.

  • West Freeport / East Commons area- spiders, wolves, lions, bears in E Commons; snakes and brew barrel in W FP, but you have to buy patterns in E FP.
  • East Freeport / Northern Ro area - spiders, pumas, secondary patterns (bracers etc.) and large sewing kit in N Ro; snakes, brew barrel, fish and fishing, primary patterns (tunics etc.) in E FP.
  • Qeynos area - snakes, wolves and bears in N Qeynos and Qeynos Hills; spiders, lions, bears, secondary patterns and large sewing kit in West Karana; primary patterns, brew barrels and supplies, fish and fishing in S Qeynos.
  • Highpass / East Karana area - (NOT recommended for under level 17, when you get Feign Death) Carrions, Crags, lions in E Karana; brew barrels, supplies, fish and fishing, secondary patterns (Serpent's Supply) in Highpass and Highkeep.
  • Rivervale / Misty Thicket area - snakes, wolves, bears, spiders, large sewing kit and secondary patterns in Misty; brew barrel, grapes, bottles, fish and fishing in Rivervale (NO hops etc. here).

 

Continuing your Brewing career above 68 skill:

Make friends with Foragers. Ask them to save their vegetables and fishing grubs for you, you are going to need ALOT of them, first of all the vegetables to get you through the vodka stage, and then you'll need even more vodka (and the fishing grubs) for Hulgarsh and Bleeding Brains later. Pick up all those fishing grubs that are left on the ground and bank them.

Start with Gnomish Spirits (rat ear, spider leg, rice, bottle). Trivial at 95. Kill rats and spiders or buy rat ears and spider legs (save spider legs from the spiders you hunt for silk). Don't bother with the Tumpy Tonic stage (trivial at 86), for 2 reasons - it is Horribly expensive, and Horribly awkward. Kiola Nuts are only available on Sister's Isle in Ocean of Tears, and they are unstackable. It requires several trips through OOT to buy as many as you need. Each nut is almost 2gp. I bought 100 nuts, got 9 skill points, wasted 20pp and a lot of time. I would only suggest it if you are extremely bored, have money, and wish to endlessly practice scaring the boat with Instill Doubt.

Now you need the vegetables. Make Vodka (Foraged Vegetables, Water, 2 Short Beer unstacked, bottle), Vodka, and more Vodka (trivials at 123). Bank it all. After all, you want to make neat drinks with it later, right?

Fierce Brew to 135, Hulgarsh, Ginesh, and Bleeding Brains after that. Impress your friends and make their world sway.

Cheers!
