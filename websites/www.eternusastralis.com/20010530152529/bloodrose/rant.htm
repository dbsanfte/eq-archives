so. i'm always getting these tells from people who have either just started playing the game and have chosen the path of an enchanter or more commonly people who know me that have decided to start an enchanter twink. "got any advice for a young enchanter?"

damn straight i do.

MEZ.

that's our most important job, though more mana goes to buffing. an enchanter's job is crowd control. there is no class better at it, i don't care what those little flaming bards say. =P i also emphasize learning the durations of your mezzes. know when stuff's gonna wake up and prevent it from doing so. i get irritated working with chanters who let their mobs wake up. they're not supposed to do that. i primarily use entrance and enthrall. i actually can't think of the last time i had dazzle memmed... unless deceiver was playing me =P.

for some reason a lot of people don't seem to realize that MEZ IS FIXED DURATION. cool huh? my primary mez is entrance, which lasts 72 seconds for 85 mana. recast is 1.5 seconds after the 2.25 you gotta wait for all spells to recycle. my secondary mez for raids and stuff is enthrall which lasts 48 seconds for 50 mana and recast is one quarter of a second. basically instant recast. it's slightly more mana efficent and the recast is better than entrance but the duration is just too short and makes me uncomfortable. more than 3 or 4 mobs and you aren't going to be able to keep them all asleep when you factor in a resist or two. with entrance i can turn stuff off and sit around and med a bit and get back up and refresh every 60 seconds. makes my life easy cos i just watch my second hand on my clock go around once.

and then there's dazzle. 96 seconds for 125 mana and 2.75 second recovery after spells recycle. i don't like the duration/mana ratio on this at all and the recast is just too long. the additional 24 seconds over entrance just isn't worth it to me and the whole 2 or 3 days i used dazzle i had mana problems. entrance is just easier to time and costs less. i can keep 3 mobs asleep with entrance without really losing any mana if i sit and med between. BUT that's just me, my experience and my comfort zone.

what was my point? oh yeah. learn your mez, whichever one it is. get used to timing it and know the mana cost. if you have your timing down you can med a lot durring battles. usually if they pull 4 or 5 mobs i'm back to 80 or fm by the time they have killed the last one just cos i know how long i can safely med between recasts. theft of thought helps too =P

okay, so anyone can hit their friggin mez hotkey and make something go to sleep if they don't get a resist. in actuality the most difficult part of being an enchanter when it comes to fighting in dungeons is targetting quickly and casting under attack. you gotta pick the one mob the tank isn't on and then click on just the right pixel to get his friend next. pay attention to the little glowing names above the mobs' heads. if you have 4 froglok dar knights that's the only bloody way you're gonna know if the one that's still awake is the one you have targetted. if i'm having a problem targetting the one that's still awake, i'll cast a color stun (the level 4 stun is my favorite, 1 second cast time and lasts 4 seconds and costs 20 mana), esc my target, then wait for the one that's awake to hit me. if it's one of the quicker hitting mobs cast another stun (i always always have at least 2 color stuns memmed, the level 4 and the level 52) and then mez.

color stuns will save yer ass about as often as mez will. however, used incorrectly they'll also get you killed. they vary in cast time, range and mana.

Name Level Cast Time Duration Range Mana Cost Re-cast
Color Flux 4 1.0 seconds 4 seconds 20 feet 20 12 seconds
Color Shift 20 1.5 seconds 6 seconds 25 feet 40 12 seconds
Color Skew 44 2.0 seconds 8 seconds 30 feet 60 12 seconds
Color Slant 52 1.5 seconds 8 seconds 35 feet 125 12 seconds

See the pattern? they get longer in duration and larger in range but longer in cast time before color slant. color slant is a nasty mana cost but i use it anyway because the shorter cast time makes a difference. because it's 1.5 seconds instead of 2, i can cast color flux then immediately cast color slant and everything stays stunned. i cannot emphasize how useful the level 4 stun is. the range is small but that's actually a good thing. it keeps you from aggroing whatever else is in the room being fought and just gets whatever is on you at the time off for a second. it'll unstun just as you finish casting mez but if you've moved during the 2.25 seconds you're waiting for the spells to recycle you should be able to cast mez before it gets to you to interrupt. even if it gets to you you can usually regain thru 1 or 2 hits.

sans resists, if you have basically any 3 stuns memmed you can keep things perma-stunned.
