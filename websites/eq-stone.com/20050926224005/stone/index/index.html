All Good Things Must Come to an End...
  Just over three years ago, eight friends embarked upon a new journey in EverQuest. Our goal was to create a guild that could be our home; a guild that could become a home for more people over time.

On October 1, 2001 we formed Stone as a guild of close friends that simply wanted to log on every night, have a common chat channel and find mobs to kill together. And just maybe we could make this guild into a force that could allow us to experience new things in the game.

Over time, more people have come to belong to the Stone tag and make this guild their home as well. Together, we've been able to see zones and mobs that many of us never truly believed we would. We've collected loot that we used to believe was unattainable. I'm thankful I was a part of that journey. But what I'm truly grateful for is the friends I've made along the way. Ten years from now, I won't remember the name or stats of the armor my toon wore, but I'll remember the adventures I was on when I got it. I'll remember how my friend Souleach pulled together 6 groups of people, FAR more than Stone had in it's ranks, to help me kill Faydedar for my final epic fight. I'll remember that time in Plane of Fear when a Vermiculated Tunic dropped and Tutatus let me loot it. I'll remember when 3 groups of my friends gathered in Iceclad Ocean to help me babysit the General so I could get my shoulder piece. I won't remember the stats on them, but the memories of those events will be as clear to me as when I got my first car.

EverQuest is just a game, but our experiences in this game are real. We share this game with real people, and that makes it worthwhile. I don't have that car anymore, and I don't have that epic sword when I turn off my computer, but I still have the memories of the people that were with me when I got them. And I'll have these very real memories of Stone for the rest of my life. Those memories are priceless to me, and I wouldn't trade them for anything. To everyone that shared this journey with me, THANK YOU! You have made this game well worth the money to me, and I hope I had some part in making it worth the $13/mo to you.

Every member should realize that they contributed to making our original goals a success, AND THEN SOME!

Tonight we say goodbye to our home of the past 3 years. We do this with dignity. We will all be able to remember our time in Stone with pride. Tonight, we should not mourn the loss of the tag, rather celebrate our successes. We've had a hell of a ride. No, we MADE a hell of a ride!

ROCK ON!!!
