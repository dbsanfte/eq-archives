Loot Policy

Stone is a family guild that in the past would award loot based on a few factors. We looked at whom ever benefited most from any given item upgrades and also who was attending the raids and would put the items into use for guild progression. It was our understanding that if we help each other and those of us that needed the items the most and would use them the most, we would all get stronger.

We have worked extremely hard to maintain a family feeling with a larger guild, growing slower than any other raiding guild. As a Raider, Applicant and Initiate you could not receive any loot under this previous system. This insured that people wanted to be apart of the family, not just apart of the looting.

Awarding loot in this manner worked well while climbing through Kunark, Velious and finally Luclin where we became a Vex Thal capable guild. Once we were in Vex Thal, looking forward the the elemental planes, we began to realize we needed to modify our loot policy. Accommodation of the sending in /tells for loot was becoming more then just a challenge, with our increased numbers and attendance, it was a nightmare.

Loot Council (LC) worked tirelessly to find a balance to keep everyone content. Only to find in the end that striving to make everyone happy was impossible. The time allocated for making a loot decision was very minimal. Referencing Magelo's, who received last drops, seniority, and which member deserved an upgrade for a particular slot was a difficult and often thankless job.

It was our intent to maintain a fair loot distribution to those members who faithfully attend raids and those who, due to their play time, showed up more sporadically.

How can a few people balance the distribution of loot fairly and keep track of who has attended more raids? Simple answer, they cannot.

We decided to modify our loot policy to Stone Kill Points (SKP). This followed our original ideas of keeping the items in the hands of those using them to make Stone stronger as a whole.

As with all things EverQuest, SKP came with The Good, The Bad and The Ugly.

The Good: Everyone who shows up to raid is accounted for and has equal chance of being awarded loot directly proportional to their contribution to the growth of Stone.

The Bad: Your not there, you get left behind in the upgrades. Solution: Be there as much as possible.

The Ugly: Negative 40 SKP adjustment when you become a full member as a way of saying thanks to those that paved the way before you.

Stone has many people that monitor SKP and we have the ability to track every entry. The SKP site is public and visible to all. It can be found on the front page of the Stone website.
