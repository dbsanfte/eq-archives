1. SUMMARY

The Order of the Dragon is a guild that represents the best of the races and classes of Norrath. We are committed to upholding the highest ideals of Truth, Justice, Honor, Strength, Loyalty, Brotherhood and Sisterhood. We are united together to fight for the interests of the Guild and to uphold the ideals we represent; to help other members in times of need; to foster an environment of friendship and camaraderie; to be a guiding, helpful force to others in need; and to serve as role models for others to emulate. We are a neutral Guild and believe everything exists as a necessary and integral part of the whole, each thing as a check and balance for the other. For what is good if there were not evil, or happiness without suffering. By maintaining balance throughout the world, we are better able to appreciate that which we have. All who possess these same ideals, goals, and beliefs, regardless of Race or Class, are friends of the Order of the Dragon.

2. OVERVIEW

History tells us that in aeons past, Veeshan, the Crystalline Dragon and Ruler of the Plane of Sky, came upon the world and found it pleasing. The world then was pure and uncorrupted. She deposited her brood and thus began the age of scale. Great dragons roamed the lands and flew the skies. They were powerful beings of great intellect, wisdom and strength. The beauty and majesty of the world was unparalleled. In time, other gods took notice of Veeshan’s discovery and came unto the world. These gods created the elder races of Norrath and in turn, evil was brought into the world as well. In time, the dragons were driven from mastery of the over-world and the lesser races soon laid claim to all the lands. They spread across the length and breadth of Norrath and with their growth so too grew evil upon the lands. The world was brought into balance. Today, there is great need upon Norrath for the revival of the virtues of Truth, Justice, Honor, Strength, Loyalty, Brotherhood and Sisterhood. A need to restore Norrath to the same beauty, majesty and grandeur that Veeshan first saw in the world. A restoration of the balance of all things. A world uncorrupted by pettiness and greed. The Order of the Dragon was formed to uphold these ideals, to maintain the balance of all things, and to stand as role models for others to emulate. To best uphold our guiding ideals, we are duty bound to develop our abilities and skills to be the best of our classes and races and to be recognized by others as such. There is also a recognized need to have a place where one can find fellowship, camaraderie, and loyalty. The Order of the Dragon is a guild that will always put other members needs and interests before their own and provide aid to other members in times of need, no matter the risk. All members are duty bound to render aid to fellow members when called upon to do so. The Order of the Dragon is more than a fellowship of adventurers. We are a family: brothers and sisters bound in the pursuit of common goals. There is also a great need upon Norrath for teaching and guidance of the young. There are many inexperienced adventurers who could benefit greatly from the helpful guidance of the experienced, and in turn, may develop to further the cause of the Guild. As representatives of the best of the classes of Norrath, we are duty bound to recognize the worthy and needy and render assistance in the best interests of the guild. There are many races and classes upon Norrath, all with their own personal goals, abilities, and objectives. The Order of the Dragon is a guild of neutral alignment. We do not discriminate based on Race or Class. Any who strive to follow the ideals that we represent are friends of the Guild. As representatives of the best of the classes of Norrath, full membership is open to all that agree to uphold the ideals and bylaws as put forth by the Guild and have achieved a minimum of 20th rank. Additionally, all prospective members prior to initiation into the ranks of the Order of the Dragon must complete an apprenticeship period and a quest which will be assigned by the Guild quest officer.

 

3. BYLAWS

The Guild is to be formed by a minimum of 10 members. These original members are to be referred to as "founding members". All founding members will have officer designation within the guild. Additional officers may be designated as needed through distinguished service to the guild by majority vote of all officers.

The following positions and responsibilities are to be created and assigned to officers within the Guild. New positions will be designated and created as needed and as voted by unanimous vote of all officers.

Guild Leader

Responsibilities: Assume leadership role of guild. Serve as central point of contact for officers, mediate discussions, Sole and final decisions regarding alliances and declaration of war. All other issues are subject to majority vote of officers.

First Officer

Responsibilities: Assist guild Leader in duties and serve as Leader when leader not able to be present.

Treasurer

Responsibilities: Maintaining Guild treasury including monetary funds and items. This may be accomplished through the use of proxy player to maintain separate bank account. (proxy player is alternate identity solely for the purpose of maintaining separate guild account).

Craft Master

Responsibilities: Fostering and developing master craftsmanship skills for the guild. Provide instruction in the various crafts and create crafted items as needed.

Web Master

Responsibilities: To create and maintain an active, up to date Guild web page. The contents of the Page are to be decided by unanimous vote of all officers. The overall design of the page is at the sole discretion of the Web master.

Recruiting officer (3)

Responsibilities: 3 officers shall be designated as recruiting officers. They are tasked to actively seek out prospective new members that can further the goals of the guild. All new recommendations for membership shall be first approved by at least 2 of the 3 Recruiting officers BEFORE being presented to council at large.

Quest Officer

Responsibilities: Develop, monitor, and assign quests to prospective members. Guild leader or First officer, prior to assignment, must approve all quests.

 

All members are duty bound to abide by the following codes of the Guild:

TO RENDER AID to other members of guild when in need and when called upon to do so. No matter the risk or circumstance. Any officer may make a general call for aid for a member. Members in need must contact an officer to petition for aid. All members, by default are to look out for the interest and well being of fellow members and render local assistance when necessary. To ignore a call for aid is grounds for probation or dismissal from guild, pending majority vote of officers.

TO FIGHT for the best interests of the Guild. Members are encouraged to group together and journey the length and breadth of Norrath.

TO UPHOLD THE ideals of Truth, Justice, Honor, Strength, Loyalty, Brotherhood and Sisterhood.

TO DEVELOP SKILLS to be the best of the races and classes of Norrath. In so doing, the goals and ideals of the guild are furthered.

TO BE HELPFUL to any in need. This aid may take the form of guidance, money or items as deemed appropriate by the member.

Additional ByLaws will be added as need upon majority vote of all officers

 

4. GENERAL POLICIES

New members

The recruiting officer(s) will recruit new members as the need arises to maintain a balance of skill sets within the guild and as merit indicates. Potential new members may also initiate a request to be admitted.

The potential initiate is to be assigned an apprenticeship and is required to journey with several guild members so his / her suitability and compatibility with the guild may be assessed. The apprenticed individual will be assigned to an appropriate guild member. That member is responsible for determining the successful completion of apprenticeship. Additionally, all members must be afforded the opportunity to meet the candidate. In addition to completion of an appropriate apprenticeship period, all candidates must complete a Quest in the name of the Guild as assigned by the Guild officer.

In extreme cases when aiding an exceptional individual of lower than 20th rank, a member may petition for Apprentice status for the individual of less than 20th rank. If approved by unanimous vote of membership council, the apprenticed individual is by rights a provisional guild member while performing apprenticeship and is entitled to all rights, responsibilities and benefits, but without carrying the guild name. An individual accepted into Apprentice status is to be trained according to the Guilds ideals and Bylaws, and upon successful completion of Apprenticeship (as determined by mentor), guarantees guild membership when appropriate level achieved. In RARE circumstance, an individual who has successfully completed an apprenticeship, may be granted guild entry prior to attainment of 20th rank only with the UNANIMOUS vote of all officers.
