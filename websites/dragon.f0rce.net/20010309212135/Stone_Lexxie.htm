The following is  the text from a wedding ceremony that is so well done, I felt inclined to leave it here as an inspiration to all how know true love in their lives.

 

Soandso, the best man has Groom and Bride stand near the
wizard ring of Kelethin, with the guests in a circle around
them. Soandso instructs everyone to disarm their weapons and
notifies Cleric when all is ready.

Cleric gates into the circle right in front of the wedding party.

Welcome, dear friends, to the joining of Groom and Bride.

Let the four directions be honored, that love, peace, power, and
radiance enter our circle.

With the blessing of the hawk of dawn soaring through the clear blue
sky, we turn to the east and call upon the spirits of air.

With the blessing of the great stag in the heat of the chase, we turn to
the south and call upon the spirits of fire.

With the blessing of the koalindl of wisdom who dwells within the sacred
waters of the pool, we turn to the west and call upon the spirits of
water.

With the blessing of the great bear whose strength flows from the fruit
of the forests, we turn to the north and call upon the spirits of earth.

May the harmony of our circle be complete.

May we all be united in perfect love.

Who walks the path of the moon to stand before all present to declare
her sacred vows?

Bride: I do.

Who walks the path of the sun to stand before all present to declare his
sacred vows?

Groom: I do.

Will you love survive the clear light of day, continuing to breathe life
into your relationship?

BOTH: It shall.

Then accept the blessing of the element of air in this place of spring.
May your union be blessed by the light of every new dawn.

Will your love survive the harsh fires of change to continue to burn
even brighter?

BOTH: It shall.

Then accept the blessings of the element of fire in this place of
summer.  May your hearts and home be filled with warmth.

Will your love survive the ebb and flow of feeling, continuing to be a
well spring?

BOTH: It shall.

Then accept the blessings of the element of water in this place of
autumn.  May the cup of your life be filled with love.

Will your love survive the times of stillness and restriction,
continuing to anchor your hearts together?

BOTH: It shall.

Then accept the blessings of the element of earth, in this place of
winter.  May your union be strong and fruitful.

As the grass of the fields and the trees of the forest bend together
under the pressure of the storm, so you two must bend when the wind of
change blows strong.

May you both stand strong in each others strength. As you give strength,
so you will receive strength. As you give love, so you will receive
love.

Together you are strong in love, may your love feed you and keep you
well.

Groom and Bride, do you have the symbols representing the
eternity of the great mysteries of life?

BOTH: We do.

Before all present exchange your rings.

May the warmth and light of your union be blessed.

Cleric binds Groom and Bride together at the wrists with a garland
of colorful ribbons.

Do you swear upon the land your mother to be true to your promises?

BOTH: We do.

Then seal these promises with a kiss.

In the name of Tunare, Mother of all, I now pronounce you husband and
wife! May you walk together life's long path.

We thank the powers of love and peace for their presence in this circle.

Let the spirits of the four directions be thanked for their energy and
blessings.
