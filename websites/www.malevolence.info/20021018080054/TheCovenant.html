Malevolent Intent is a guild that lives up to the true form of the word guild.  We work together as a team.  All decisions made for the guild are voted in by the guild officers.  The Guild Leader Title has no significance here, as ALL decisions are based on a majority vote.  As a member one can feel secure that any issues brought to the officers will be voted in with many different views in  mind.

As a guild, we aim to help everyone achieve their goals and bearing that in mind we have come up with the following guild rules for all events:


  



Malevolent Intent  is a merit based guild. Those who contribute and participate the most will prosper. There are four primary factors in deciding merit, as decided upon by the officers:

- 1 - How often a member participates in raids.
- 2 - What class will best benefit from the particular item.
- 3 - How drastic an improvement in the member this will make.
- 4 - How much a member contributes to the guild, on and off the battlefield.

When an item drops that interests you please quickly /tell an officer that you are interested in that drop. All officers will then quickly decide who gets that drop based on the above criteria. We cannot promise that mistakes will never be made. We can, however, promise to do our best in being as absolutely fair as possible.  We also have an officer in the guild which is in charge of keeping track of all members stats, we will know ahead of time who is in most need of a particular stat.  Hopefully this will help to keep balance among our members.  Because of this it is vitally important that when you join this guild you meet with an officer and go over equipment and character info.

Posted guild raids are MANDATORY to all those who are online.

There will be 1 raid leader per raid, everyone is to listen carefully and do as the raid leader instructs. Should anyone consistently fail to listen that during a raid, that person may be asked to leave and will not be allowed to raid until the next event.

******SPECIAL NOTE******
Please be advised that no alternate character will ever be considered for item drops as long as a primary character is in need on that particular raid. Should an item appear that is classified as "no drop" which no primary character needs then alternate characters may give an officer a /tell for that item. Tradeable items will be considered on a case by case basis, and the same criteria as above will apply.

******Regarding Epic Weapons******

Epic fights will not be planned for any guild member until that member has proven themselves to the guild, through strong participation and dedication. To reiterate, we want to help each and every one of you become the best you can be, but we must make this rule to avoid bringing in the kind of player that will make use of our guild to better only his or herself and then move on to the "uber-guild-de-jour"

If you feel you have raided and contributed to the guild the best you could, give an officer a /tell stating exactly what you would like the guild to help you with concerning your epic fight. At that time the officer will present your request to the other officers on the officer chat handle. A vote may be taken, and a raid planned.

Please keep in mind, though, that those who help themselves will get the most help in return. Feel free to call for assistance on anything that requires only a small group to accomplish. The ranger epic task in West Karanas comes immediately to mind, as a good example.
