I want a Viper. No, that's not right, I need a Viper. It's beyond a simple wish. I have to have one. I have Viper plastic and metal model kit cars. I have Viper Hot Wheels. I will buy a 20 pack of Matchbox cars if it has one Viper in it. When I start a new game of Gran Turismo, the first car I go for is the Viper. Steppenwolf's song, 'Magic Carpet Ride' is now the Dodge Viper theme song. When I hear it on the radio, it takes me away to the magical happy place where I too own a Viper.

The fact that the car I drive now is a Dodge Shadow doesn't help things very much. I've tried pretending that my little red 4 cylinder turbo is a 450hp, V-10, blue and white striped monster. The fantasy usually ends when I lose a drag race to a kid on a moped delivering newspapers. I was pretty insulted because he kept on throwing papers to his customers while we raced. So, later, I took masking tape, wrote the word 'Viper' on it and placed it over the 'Shadow' name plate on the back to intimidate those nasty moped drivers. No one but my 4 year old cousin was fooled. Of course, he eats things he finds between the sofa cushions and the only thing he's ever driven is his Power Wheels Bigfoot. I am proud to report that my car smoked his Bigfoot's ass. He might have won, though if he hadn't been trying to run over his neighbor's cat. He also laughed at me when I tried to collect on the bet we'd made. He told me that he didn't have $80,000 and that I was a "stupid fart head". I don't care who you are, there's no arguing with logic like that, so later, we made a double or nothing bet on a game of Pokemon Snap and he kicked my ass and broke even.

So anyway, I've come to the conclusion that a Dodge Shadow is no substitue for a Dodge Viper.

Right now, Vipers are just slightly out of my price range along with every other car that's not at least 10 years old. Part-time jobs that you put up with while you're in college just were never meant to give people the means to buy $75,000 sports cars. Also, once I make it through the hell that is an engineering degree, I'm thinking that the first $75,000 I earn should probably go towards something I can live in. And don't think that I've not tried to figure out a way to put my bed into the trunk of a Viper.

So, what I've done is make this little page in the hopes that it inspires a passing Texas billionaire or the ex-CEO of one of the richest companies in the world or even a game designer who thinks he's a king, to charitably reach out, sign a blank check and send it along to me with all the best wishes.
