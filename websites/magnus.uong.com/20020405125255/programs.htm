Our Programs
Youth Philanthropy Worldwide's programs help young people recognize that they can make a difference. We provide a meaningful introduction to important issues in international philanthropy and get young people thinking about how they can make a difference by supporting local leaders and community based initiatives in a spirit of mutual respect and learning.
"It was fun to talk about different groups. For one group we were arguing about whether to give it money because if the group got caught doing this illegal process [teaching girls in Afghanistan], the teachers might be put to death and the money would be taken, which would be risking our money. But we decided that these people were rising their lives for girls to learn, so we could risk the money."
--Mary, Grade 6
Philanthropy Workshops - Our first workshop explores women's rights issues across the developing world.
The heart of the workshop is the grantmaking exercise. Working with a hypothetical budget, participants play the role of grantmakers and review proposals from real community-based organizations in the developing world. The funding requests reflect the wide diversity of women's rights concerns around the world: from a night school for domestic workers in Mali to a street theatre group educating Pakistani women about their rights.
Programs for National Youth Organizations - YPW works with national youth organizations to adapt to our materials for use in their programs. We are currently working with the Girl Scout Council of Santa Clara County to pilot an International Women's Rights patch for Cadette and Senior Girl Scouts, which we eventually plan to bring to the national level.
