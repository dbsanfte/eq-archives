YPW Founder Biographies


Esther Hewlett

Esther Hewlett's primary occupation has been raising her children with her husband in Palo Alto, California. Her second passion has been promoting female human rights around the world. Esther serves as the chair of YPW's Advisory Committee.

From 1990-1998, she served on the Board of Directors of the Global Fund for Women, an international grantmaking organization, based in San Francisco, that gives small grants to grassroots women's groups all over the world. She has visited Global Fund grantees in Bangladesh and Nepal (1991); in Uganda and Zimbabwe (1998, along with her older daughter, who was seventeen at the time); at the NGO Forum at the Beijing conference (1995, also with her daughter); and in Mali (2000, with the Flora Family Foundation) as well as Cedpa grantees in Egypt (2000).

Currently, Esther serves on the Board of Directors of Cedpa in Washington, DC, and Cap and Gown (Women's Honor Society) at Stanford University. She has previously served on the Board of the Flora Family Foundation in Palo Alto, and as co-director of the Noon Chorus at Addison Elementary School when her daughter was a student.

Esther received her BA in Chinese from Stanford University in 1970 and her MA in Asian Studies from the University of Berkeley in 1973.


Anne McCarten-Gibbs

Anne has worked in the nonprofit world for over 10 years. She serves as YPW's Executive Director. Anne is also an active volunteer in Berkeley schools.

Most recently, Anne operated a successful consulting practice serving nonprofit organizations. Her clients included the Global Fund for Women, the Urgent Action Fund for Women's Human Rights, and the Global Fund for Children. From 1997-1998, Anne was the Communications Officer for the Global Fund for Women, responsible for the organization's web site, annual reports, newsletters, and other communications with supporters and the public. Her work won two Wilmer Shields Rich Awards for Excellence in Communication from the Council on Foundations in 1999.

Prior to joining the Global Fund, Anne examined and wrote about K-12 education for EdSource in Palo Alto, about child care policy for Mathematica Policy Research in Princeton, New Jersey, and about the history of US foreign policy for the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace in Washington, DC.
