Philanthropy Workshops

Youth Philanthopy Worldwide's workshops provide a meaningful introduction to important issues in international philanthropy and get young people thinking about how they can make a difference through fundraising and activism.

Our first workshop is on Philanthropy for Women's Rights.

The heart of the workshop is the grantmaking exercise. Working with a hypothetical budget, participants play the role of grantmakers and review proposals from real community-based organizations in the developing world. The funding requests reflect the wide diversity of women's rights concerns around the world: from a night school for domestic workers in Mali to a street theatre group educating Pakistani women about their rights. These grant proposals come from the Global Fund for Women, one of our grantmaking partners.

Unable to fully fund the requests they are reviewing (normally six), the group usually begins a passionate discussion about the work of the grantseekers and their relative merit. Through this discussion, the group comes to a consensus about how to allocate the grant money. Some workshops may allow the participants to allocate actual funds, when the participating organization has provided them.

The grantmaking exercise is followed by an in-depth discussion on fundraising. When a young person has a passion to make a change or right a wrong in the world, how can she raise money to help make that happen? Participants discuss stories of real life kids their age who have made a difference - by sending teddy bears and encouraging words to hospitalized children, speaking out against industrial pollution in their community, or building a nation-wide campaign to honor a feminist trailblazer.

Underlying the entire fundraising discussion is an important environmental activist who founded Children for a Safe Environment when she was nine years old: "I want other girls to know that it doesn't matter whether you are rich or poor; every girl can make a difference."* Finally, the group spends some time on the nuts and bolts developing a budget for one of the fundraising ideas.

These one-time workshops can take from one hour to half a day depending on which components are included.

* From Extraordinary Girls, by Maya Ajmera, Olateju Omolodun, and Sarah Strunk Shakti for Children, 1999.
