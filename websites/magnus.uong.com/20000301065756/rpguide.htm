Magnus' Guide to Roleplaying

This guide is meant as a companion guide to Mushashi's Guide to Ultima Online writing. (found at Musashi's Page ) Its intent is to help the masses be more like, or at least understand the typical roleplayer in Ultima Online. The latter may be nigh impossible but we're being optimistic today.

 

-What is roleplaying?

That's a question that has as many answers as Richard Garriot has sports cars. Generally, roleplaying is pretending to be something you're not. Whether you decide to play an intellengent, witty, brave, or a well respected character, the choice is always up to you.

-Why roleplay?

Roleplaying adds a whole new element of the game that you may never knew existed before! When you roleplay, you'll suddenly find your mind turning and thinking as you try to come up with rational "RP explanations" for ICQ (telepathy), mules (weakminded brothers and sisters in your care), and your secret PK character (evil twin). It's a whole new experience!

-How do I get started?

The most important thing that you need is a name. There are several options in this case. Perhaps the novice roleplayer will want to start simply. You can take a character from a well known piece of fiction and roleplay as the character. For example, you decide to name your character Stone Cold. You make wrestling his primary skill and say "Hell yeah!" (Capitalization and punctuation not required.) a lot. This is all there is to roleplaying!

A more advanced option is to make up your own name. This usually involves throwing letters together in a way that looks medieval or magical. A good tactic is to use a lot of AE and AEI combinations in your name. Examples: Kaeriae, Ryaeinaie, Fraeittraein, etc.

Another common practice is to overuse apostrophe marks. Use them whereever and whenever. They will give your name a much more unique look and let everyone around you know that YOU are a true roleplayer.

If you're really creative, you might decide to give your character a last name. This is really only for veteran roleplayers as last names are generally a sign of a hard core rper. (Unless of course, the last name you choose is Knocka, Pimpstah, or Dawg.)

-Ok, I have a name, now what?

You should probably work on a background story now. Mushashi has covered the background story in his writing guide but if your not the writing type, here are a few simple rules to remember about your character's background.

-You are an orphan. (Everyone is.)

-You must have a hometown. It doesn't matter if your character lives in a house blocking the entrance to Destard, pick a town that you like and call that your hometown.

-You have a feeling that there is something special* about yourself.

*Special meaning spoken of in prophecy, savior of the world, etc.

-When in doubt, resort to being the strong silent type.

-Ok, got the background, what do I do when I play?

Roleplaying requires that you learn to use bad olde english. This isn't as hard as it sounds if you just follow a few rules. Use 'thee' and 'thou' a lot unless you're pretending that you're Scottish or Irish and then you can just use 'ye'. Put a 'th' or 'eth' on the end or every verb and that's all there is to it. When you get practice, you can start working in words like 'forsooth', 'partake', and 'verily'. The best part is, you don't even have to use them correctly in the context of a sentence! Just use them and you'll look like you know what you're talking about!

Now that you're speaking like a native, there are a few things you must do playwise. First of all, you must give up your 'vendor buy the bank guards' macro. You may, however, macro each command to a seperate key in a badly composed sentence. (Remember the rules from above.)

Emote! Emote! Emote! This is a subtlety that passes so many players by. Other players want to see *stares with a piercing stare that sends shivers up your spine*, or *stands quietly with hands in pockets*, or even *laughs in a most evil way*. If you do nothing more than set up a macro that emotes *clears throat* and *nods in agreement*, you'll earn the respect of many a player! Again, no humorous emotings allowed. Examples of emotings to avoid: *Relieves himself on a nearby tree*, *Fiddles with nipple rings*, *Hums Nirvana songs*, *Does the Macarena*, and *Lights a fart*.

You may not run, unless you are sure that no one else can see you! You may not macro either unless no one can see you.
