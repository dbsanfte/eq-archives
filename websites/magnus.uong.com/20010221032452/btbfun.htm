I think it was the summer of 1996 when I had the extreme misfortune of buying Beyond the Beyond. BtB is one of those games that is so bad, you play it for hours and just shake your head silently at how bad it is. You hold on to a belief that nothing could be so bad all the way through, it has to get better. The game never got better and, after two weeks of playing, I completed BtB and felt like less of a person for it.

I don't really remember much about the story since I've done my best to forget everything about the game including the title, but I'm pretty sure it had something to do with a kid who used a big sword and saved the world by collecting 8 magic things and beating some bad guy in a dark cape at the end. Along the way, you met new people who would join your adventure. Oooh like one of them was a really big dude who carried an axe. Another was a babe in revealing armor and a loin cloth, who had big boobs and knew lots of magic. Really original stuff. And if that weren't enough, the game also featured "full 360 degree combat" according to the back of the box. What this translated to is really unconvincing two dimensional sprites and a rotating landscape. It looks as cheesy as it sounds.

For years now, I've thought that the only use I'd ever find for the game ever again would be as a bookend for my other Playstation games. I just hope it breaks their fall should some set of circumstances cause them to jump off their shelf. Such situations do occur when you have a cat, as I do, that is more hyperactive than a room full of three year olds on Koolaid and Barney videos. Enough about cats and Koolaid though, I finally had a good time PLAYING BtB! (Cue the sinister BtB bad guy music.)

 This is where it all begins. Up until yesterday, if you had asked me what you should do at this screen, I would have told you to turn the power off and snap the CD in half. That was before I understood the simple way to enjoy this game. It's so simple, I can't believe it took me more than four years to figure it out. What is the secret that breathes life back into one of the worst games ever made?

Name yourself "mydick".
Yes! It really is that easy. This sets up dozens of magic moments like the one depicted to the right.

The melding of a bad script and a clever, albeit juvenile, name never produced such fantastic results as in Beyond the Beyond. The simple name change transforms the game. The story transforms from boring as an english teacher to engaging, seductive, racy, and hilarious. The plot transforms to one of child abuse, bisexual orgies, and cross dressing. So drastic is the change, so much better does this make the game, I am convinced the programmers intended the game to be this way all along! I've thought of submitting the name change as a trick to various gaming magazines.
