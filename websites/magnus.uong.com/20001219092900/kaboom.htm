Before I begin, I must ask all reading this to observe a moment of silence for one of the greatest games ever made. Out of the hundreds and hundreds of video games I've played in my life, I can count on one hand those that are on the same level as Kaboom. This article took me three days to complete because I started playing Kaboom in order to get screenshots and spent 2 and a half days playing it. So, with that said, begin the moment of silence now, and no cheating. I'll know.

Ok, now that the necessary repects have been paid, I'll move on. It was during the retelling of the history of my life that I began to get all nostalgic. I dragged out the old Atari, blew out the Kaboom cartridge, found my paddle controllers and played until the wee hours of the morning. The game is still that damn good.

Kaboom is a simple thing. If you can sit upright for fifteen minutes and keep from drooling on yourself, you can play Kaboom. I bought the game at a garage sale for one dollar. It came with no instructions but I was able to figure it out.

The guy at the top is the villain. His sole purpose in this game is to move back and forth and drop bombs in short rounds. The hero (that's you) moves the wheel on the paddle controller back and forth and tries to catch the bombs on yellow tires. As you catch more bombs, this guy goes faster and drops more bombs per round. If you miss a bomb, you lose a tire. The game keeps going on like this until you lose all your tires or the end of time, whichever comes first.
As far as I know, there's no story or backplot or prequal or strategy guide or any of those other things that all games today seem to need.

The Freddy Krueger looking guy is the only villain you ever see. Usually, his expression is an icy frown but when he eventually wears you down, he allows himself a smile for a job well done. The bastard. What motivates him? Does he hate the Activision logo at the bottom? Has he got something against yellow tires? Who cares?

These days, classic games mean easy money for game companies. They can take an old idea, make it into a bad 3d game, market it to kids who think an Atari 2600 is for playing 8 tracks and whamo, instant cash. There have been dozens and dozens of remakes of old classics. Games like Frogger, PacMan, and even Pong have been given face lifts and sold to young kids looking for awesome graphics and old kids looking to relive their first childhood. And while I pray that Kaboom is never re-released as a STUNNING 3D GRAPHICAL ADVENTURE!, I can't help but wonder what Kaboom would be like if some of the major game companies today got their hands on it.
