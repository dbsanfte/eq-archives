From the Global Fund for Women web site, May 2001

Global Fund Teaches Girls How to Give Grants

As part of Take Our Daughters to Work Day, the Global Fund for Women welcomed 14 girls from the Bay Area to our San Francisco office on April 26th for an orientation and workshop on grantmaking and fundraising. Daughters and family members of staff joined 8th graders from A.P. Giannini Middle School in San Francisco.

"It made an impact on me. I want to be one of those women on the Global Fund staff."

– Catherine Riley, participant
This is the second year the Global Fund has participated in the Ms. Foundation for Women-sponsored Take Our Daughters to Work Day, a national program to build girls' self-esteem, introduce career options, and present female role models.

As "program officers for a day," the girls were given summaries of six proposals from women's rights groups, ranging from a disability rights group in Kenya to a girls' education group in Pakistan. The girls were presented with a real-life scenario in which they received $51,500 in grant requests and had only $40,000 to give away. They discussed questions such as whether 30 single mothers rebuilding their hurricane–destroyed homes in Nicaragua should get more or less money than a group in Tajikistan hoping to open a business center for women.


"56% of girls surveyed in the U.S. said it is true that girls are expected to speak softly and not cause trouble."

– Girls, Inc.

"This exercise created a sense of awareness of how interconnected our global community has become — that women in Afghanistan and Mali are in some way linked to girls in California," commented Esther Hewlett, workshop leader.

One participant, eight-year-old Sagarika Ravishankar, was inspired enough to go back to her friends at school and start a club to raise funds for women's human rights, especially the women of Afghanistan. She and her friends have brainstormed ideas for raising money, including bake sales, art sales and lemonade stands. Sagarika has raised $23 to date and hopes to donate the money to an Afghani women's group recommended by the Global Fund.


Global Fund staff would like to thank Esther Hewlett and Anne McCarten-Gibbs for once again organizing Take Our Daughters to Work Day.
