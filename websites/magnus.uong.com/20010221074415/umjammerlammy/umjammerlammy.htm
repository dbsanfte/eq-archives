Over the past couple of years the "music game" genre has been introduced and expanded. These are usually games where the control pad acts as a musical instrument as you play your way through the game. They don't require much in the way of musical knowledge though. They're usually just really advanced versions of the old Simon hand held games as you press buttons in the order called to you to play a song. That's a good thing for me since I have zero musical talent and the rhythm of a, um well, of a skinny white college student.

Enter, Lammy from Um Jammer Lammy. She's a lamb and she plays guitar in a band called Milkcan. I bought this game roughly a month ago on a whim. Since then, the little lamb pictured to the left has played her way into my heart, because her game is one the most addictive I've ever played. The addiction factor is right up there with Tetris, laser light pens, painkillers, Rice Krispy Treats, pornography, and those damned Hanson fan fiction sites. Add to that the fact that Lammy is cute as a button and you have no reason left not to own this game.

She plays a wicked electric guitar too.
I'm one of those wierd people that reads the instruction booklet before they start playing a new game. After looking through the first couple of pages about the girl band named Milkcan and rolling my eyes a few times, I thought for sure I'd just wasted my money. I just knew I should have bought Super Mega Ultra Power Duper Street Fighter EX 3.1415 squared: Turbo Cool Edition. Either that or Mega Man XYZ 14. Oh well, I'd already opened it by then and couldn't take it back.

I was expecting a wierd cross between one of those lame Nickolodeon cartoons and Britney Spears. And the two things that make Britney Spears appealing to me would most certainly not be included. I was bummed. I prepared myself for songs about butterflies and cute guys and other stuff that teeny bopper girlie bands like to sing about.
I will say now that it defies all logic, rational or otherwise, that a game centered around the lead guitarist of an all-girl band can be this bad ass. It really should be illegal to make a cool game about a girl band. It makes it really hard on me for one thing. Do you know how embarrasing it is to admit that your new favorite game is one in which you control a female lamb? Pretty damn embarrassing.

What I'm getting at is, most of the really kick ass games out there have gritty storylines, and swearing, and soldiers who blow things up, and heroes that don't shave. If women are characters in one of these games, they're always nearly naked and have breasts that are bigger than their heads.

These are games that you proudly stand up and announce that you love. That is, if you aren't already ashamed of spending more money on video games than dates at age 21, and I'm not.
Um Jammer Lammy has none of that macho stuff. The storyline is as wacky as they come. The only thing even resembling a fight comes in the opening cinema when a bully tries to steal Lammy's lunch and she beats him up with the power of her guitar riffs. Don't roll your eyes, I already told you it was silly.

There is only one soldier and he's a senile old man. He's also the only human in the whole game and it appears that he does, in fact, shave. He doesn't swear, but he does scream and head bang.

Lammy's no Lara Croft either. As you can see from the picture on the right, she's about as flat chested as you can possibly get. She's practically two dimensional, as a matter of fact.


If a rapping onion isn't original, please tell me what original means.

 So what is it about the game that makes it such a blast to play? It could be the original characters. To the left, that's Chop Chop Master Onion. He's a rapping, kung fu sensei with an onion for a head. I thought that was pretty original.

It could be that I love the game because I've always had a desire to play lead guitar for a rock band in front of millions of screaming fans. Worry not though, my aforementioned rhythm problem prevents this dream from ever becoming reality. As a matter of fact, the only time I've ever "played" an electric guitar, there was only one screaming person and it was my friend's mom. She said something like, "You'd better cut the damn volume down or I'll make you boys take that outside!" Um Jammer Lammy may be the closest I ever get to my fantasy.
Maybe the reason I love the game so much is the fact that Lammy and I share the same hairstyle and wardrobe. I always sport the eternally messed up hair and if you ever see me wearing something other than a t-shirt, blue jeans, and sneakers, I'm probably at a funeral and you can be sure there's a change of clothes waiting in my car. When it's time for my funeral, jeans and a t-shirt are what I wanna go in. Either that, or my Ernest P. Warrel mask.

Whatever the reason is, Um Jammer Lammy like, um jams! The music is so catchy, I've been singing the songs for days now. The game has recieved the first, "Damn, you mean it's dark outside already? How long have I been playing?" award I've given this year. It's been responsible for making me nearly late to class twice. "Yeah Dr. Anderson, I'm sorry I disturbed your class, I was really rocking with my lady Lammy."

But, with this page, I am now over the embarrassment that loving this game initially brought. I know that there exist men who can look badass in a Power Puff Girls t-shirt. So too, I shall look bad ass once my Um Jammer Lammy t-shirt arrives. Allowing 5 to 7 days for delivery and I am only one business week away from announcing to the world, "I love Um Jammer Lammy and I am badass!" Oh what a great day that will be.God, I'm such a geek.


Lammy, today is your lucky day!

 So, anyway, the one thing that Lammy does not have as far as I can tell is an internet fanboy page. If you've never used the internet to search for information on a video game, let me explain to you what a fanboy page is. Some games are just so totally, and awesomely cool that they inspire young men (little boys) and young women (little boys) all over the world to fire up the AOL HOMEPAGE MAKER, collect pictures from other internet sites, type up the game's manual along with their review of it, and post their tribute to said game for all to see. They register it with every search engine you can possibly think of and then folks like you and me stumble across these masterpieces such as Cloud46's FF7 page or The Sonic Shrine. And believe me, I don't care who you are, or what game you just programmed, it isn't worth shit until LimpBizkit42@hotmail.com makes a fan site and calls it a shrine. And now, the true purpose of all this rambling reveals itself! I am writing this to move Um Jammer Lammy from the status of great game to that of classic game. Without further adieu (sp?) I proudly present to you, quite possibly, the only, and therefore, the best, Um Jammer Lammy fansite.

ENTER Lammy75's Um Jammer Lammy Shrine!!
