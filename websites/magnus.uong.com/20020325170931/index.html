Introduction

 

Youth Philanthropy Worldwide's programs help young people recognize that they can make a difference. We believe that increasing the next generation's awareness, commitment, and sense of partnership in the global community is a critical step for building a peaceful future.

YPW's programs

Inform young people about the challenges faced by other neighbors in the Global South
Engage their energy and creativity to make a contribution
Create real connections with community initiatives around the world

Our programs focus on real challenges facing communities in the developing world. Partnering with well-regarded grantmaking organizations, including the Global Fund for Women and the Global Fund for Children, we introduce young people to the style of philanthropy exemplified by these grantmakers -- supporting local leaders and community based initiatives in a spirit of mutual respect and learning.

Our goal is to create high-quality programs and resources and make them easily available to young people through the World Wide Web and print. By forming strategic alliances with organizations that work with youth nationwide, we can bring our programs to large numbers of young people.

Our current programs are:
Philanthropy Workshops - These workshops provide a meaningful introduction to issues in international philanthropy and get young people thinking about how they can make a difference. Out first workshop explores women's rights issues across the developing world.

Programs for National Youth Organizations - YPW works with national youth organizations to adapt to our materials for use in their programs. We are currently working with the Girl Scout Council of Santa Clara County to pilot an International Women's Rights patch for Cadette and Senior Girl Scouts, which we eventually plan to bring to the national level.

"It was a good learning experience for me because I learned what it takes to fund projects to support what girls' and women's groups believe in. I wouldn't mind working...to help those who are in need of help."
