Patch Message From 3/28
Here is the latest patch and what it contained:
IMPORTANT NEW MESSAGE

***Combine Button Containers***

First of all thank you to everyone who posted the problem with the
Velious Tailoring recipes and other recipes that appeared to be broken
after the patch.

With the most recent patch, we fixed a bug that is causing some
confusion with these recipes.

There was a bug that allowed certain recipes to work in any container of
its type, where they were intended to work only in special containers of
that type. For example, there are recipes that were intended to only
work in the Coldain Tanner's Kit that could be completed in any Sewing
Kit. That bug was repaired along with some other minor bugs associated
with combine button containers in this last patch.

Please make sure that you are using the proper container as instructed
by in game instructions or npc's when combining trade skill components
or quest components in any combine button container.

The Velious Tailoring Recipes are currently functioning in the Coldain
Tanners Kit. We apologize for the inconvenience.

- The EverQuest Team

EQ Boards Change
Verant has changed the way the EQ boards work. Here are the details:
As you have seen we have made some significant changes to these boards. Abashi has posted the changes and the reasons behind these changes.

What does this change mean? Well it means that I and my team will be taking the emails that you send us and posting some of them along with answers and updates. It means that it will be significantly easier for you to find the answers to your questions. It means that the spam, flames and other messiness will no longer be here.

You will not be able to post on this forum. When you fill out the form it sends an email to me. Please be polite and informative. I need locs, zones and all the information necessary for me to reproduce your bug.

It also means that because I do not have to moderate a board I will have more time to answer questions. It is still not my primary responsibility, but I think that more questions will be answered and more information will be available.

What we ask you to do is to read the posts to see if your question is already answered, and if it is not please use the form provided to email us. We will take the most commonly asked questions and post them and the answers. Absor is doing this to a much more extensive scale in another section.

Please only send bug related questions. Anything development related or customer service related will not be answered. Also, anything vulgar or inappropriate sent could have consequences on your EverQuest account, so please be polite. Remember that I have to read these emails.

We hope that this new format will let us communicate with you guys more clearly. By not having to dig through hundreds of posts we hope you will find the information you need more quickly and find that information more concise.

Please do not be offended if your question is not answered. I or my team read each email but we do not have time to respond to every email.

We are excited about this new system and look forward to answering your emails.

March 25, 2001
EQ vs the brave new world(s)
Source: EverQuest General Discussion Forums
Source: EverQuest General Discussion Forums.

Hello all. I played EQ from the tail end of Beta until about 4 months ago. I finally quit for a number of reasons, but the #1 reason by far is that it's impossible to get anything done in the game without taking a week off from work and sitting at my computer the whole time. Literally hundreds of players on each server doing the exact same quests competing for the exact same spawn that for the most part pop in the exact same place time after time. The whole game became one big exercise in boredom and frustration. Add into that the fact the Verants customer service makes the phone company and post office seem downright friendly & helpful and the situation gets even worse.
Well, EQ and Verant are about to be in some deep [****] now, because their virtual monopoly on the mmorpg is going away. I've been playing the Beta version of Anarchy Online, and MAN what a game it is. Extraordinarily customizeable characters allow you to actually recognize your friends by sight most of the time; the skill and experience system allow for so much flexibility that two characters started the same way at the same time can be totally different in two weeks; NO CAMPING, the spawn and loot are randomized so that you don't have to; you can play equally well solo or in a group, and get something done even if you don't have 48 straight hours to sit and wait. And the customer service actually PROVIDES customer service!

This might end up being a good thing for EQ in the long run, since with such powerful competition they will have to dramatically improve the game just to keep the players they have now, let alone attract any new ones. But I think its more likely that EQ is going to end up fading away into history, as a once great and innovative game that's long past its prime.

Actually, comparing EQ to other games, and going over the positives and negatives, and different approaches and philosophies is fine. Please, though, I hope nobody viloates their NDAs that exist with some of these games in beta.

Anyway, the description you gave of AO is so general, as noted by Logan9, that it doesn't really separate itself from other games (or promises of other games) that are either out or coming out (I'm not saying the game's do or don't -- just the description you gave).

First, a skill based system (no classes). Actually, EQ is in the minority here -- most MMOGs use or intend to use skill based systems. This could be a thread unto itself, going over the advantages and disadvantages of class vs. skill systems. IMHO, they both have their plusses and minuses, and here at Verant/SOE, we're working on some games that are class based and others skill based. In a nutshell, classes are easier to balance and easier to create interdependece with. But they are also less flexible, and they can be more prone to 'class envy' issues after launch. Skill systems, on the other hand, are more flexible, but harder to balance, and can lead to 'tank mage syndromes' and serious lack of interdependence problems. Also, if complex enough, they can intimidate new users. (For the future, we've some ideas on several of our products, to address the deficiencies of both systems, as I'm sure our competitors do as well).

No camping and random spawns... well, again, this is pretty general. We've tried to do more randomization recently, and with decent success. The trick is, IMHO, to reduce camping but not have so much randomness that you lose context, storyline, and setting. And, again, this could be a huge thread too.


Ah, the solo vs. group issue. I've really yet to see this one solved. It's pretty tough, because first you want to encourage grouping and interdependence because it forms bonds and community, which are vital in MMOGs. Second, if you create something challenging for one player, a group of players will demolish the encounter. The solution is probably to somehow make sure you've content for both, but of course the reward for the 'solo' encounter cannot be nearly as great as the reward for the 'group' encounter. This is another great topic for debate.

And no comment on customer service -- first, it's a beta and there aren't a lot of people involved, and second, people have all sorts of different ideas as to what customer service is, what to expect, etc.

- Brad

Stats to stay the way they are
source:EverQuest General Discussion Forum
Ok, this has got to be the fifteenth time i've posted this. I've recieved over 100 responses on all posts and still no response from Verant. Here goes:
When I started my Magician, I was un aware of the whole int/wis caster thing. I had no idea what I was supposed to invest my extra points into, so I put most of them in wis and str. Now, 40 levels later i'm paying for it. Spending thousands of PP on items that boost me +5 int here and there, when I could have an extra 20! I need my epic just to make my int normal! Would it be at all possible to give us some way to redo our base stats? A quest or something? I mean, if its that much of a pain I would pay RL money for the service, and alot of other people would too.

This issue has been answered several times, usually every month or two. The short answer is no, we won't create a way for people to redo their base stats. Fortunately there is no decision that you can make at character creation that results in having a "useless" character at later levels. You may very well have to work a little harder to get your stats where you want them, but you aren't that far behind those who put their 20 points directly into INT.

- Gordon
