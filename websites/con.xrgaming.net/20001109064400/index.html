November 08, 2000
Velious Party Successful Press Release
Verant sent out the following press release about the success of their recent launch party!
Sony Online Entertainment Inc. is pleased to announce that the official launch party for fans of "EverQuest(r): The Scars of Velious(tm)" was an unmitigated success. "Velious" is the second expansion pack for the most successful online role-playing game in history, and attracted over one thousand EverQuest fans in Las Vegas on November 4-5. "Velious," the add-on to the best selling "EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark" product, ships this December.

"It was definitely a party to remember," said Jeff Butler, producer of "Scars of Velious." "We were overwhelmed by the enthusiasm of our fans - they make the games great and make our effort worthwhile. I was flabbergasted that fans came from all across the country, from Virginia to California, to attend our party!"

The attendees to the party were the first to explore the new continent and mingle with the creators of EverQuest(r) at The Bellagio Hotel and Planet Hollywood in Las Vegas. Memorable events included:

* Over fifty EverQuest guilds assembled at The Bellagio Hotel and met their members in person, some for the first time. The largest guild consisted of twenty fans.
* Thirty fans arrived in full costume to portray their favorite characters from EverQuest, including wood elves, barbarians, Iksars, and warriors.
* The fans benefited from numerous giveaways and drawings during the festivities. Prizes included the Scars of Velious expansion pack, hand-painted pewter figurines, lithographs of Velious artwork, NVIDIA video cards, and one-year membership of EverQuest sponsored by Ignited Minds.

About "EverQuest: The Scars of Velious" Starting this December, "EverQuest: The Scars of Velious" will take hundreds of thousands of EQ players to an all-new frozen continent. In addition to perils inherent in the environment, players will meet with new NPCs, cast new spells, handle new weapons, and don upgradeable armor.

"EverQuest: The Scars of Velious," is the second expansion pack for the most successful online role playing game in history, and takes the more than 300,000 current EverQuest players on dozens of new quests and adventures. The pack features 16 new advanced adventure zones, numerous new creatures and spells and weapons in an enhanced 3D game engine.

"Velious" is located south of Antonica, and features ice caves, crystal caverns, dungeons and frozen towers. The new cast of visually stunning non-player characters includes frost giants, ice dragons, bloodthirsty orcs, cave bears, Snowfang gnolls, storm giants and vicious ottermen. The continent features three times the amount of polygons and twice the detail of the current EverQuest world.

Unlike its predecessor, "The Ruins of Kunark," this expansion pack requires players to own a copy of "EverQuest" and is designed for higher level characters.

Best Of The Best (Wizzies)
Here is the official word on the BoB tournament for Wizzies:
Best of the Best – Wizards
Rules and Information Packet

For wizards 54+

Single Elimination 1 vs 1 competition for all rounds but final round.

Final round (to determine 1st and 2nd place on both test and live) will be 2 out of 3 matches.

Dates: November 9th though the 17th on Live Servers, times will be posted at least 48 hours in advance.

Final is on Test Server, November 19th at Noon, all servers will be represented. Wizards must be played by the owner of the account, no “Ringers” will be allowed. Any account found with someone besides the registered and original owner of the account playing that character is breaking the EULA as set forth by Verant Interactive.

Any use of exploits or bugs will result in immediate disqualification from the event, and is a break in the rules of conduct as set forth on www.everquest.com.

Guides and GMs are considered judges and therefore restricted from attack. The Arena is off limits to unauthorized pvp 15 minutes before and 15 minutes after the event. This is to ensure that the contestants and spectators are safe. During this time, play nice policy is in effect regardless of server.

Both wizards will be summoned out of the zone, and summoned back before the start of the match to clear any previous bugs with permanent spell effects.

Both wizards, once debuffed and resummoned shall be asked if they are ready for the match to start. Once the match starts, a timer will start of 30 seconds that the wizards are allowed to cast on themselves, and quickly rememorize the spells. The judge of the match will count down on every 5 second interval until the time is up, and at that time offensive spells maybe used. Mana used for buffs will not be restored. The offensive spells start at the 30 second mark regardless if wizard is ready for the next stage.

You cannot cast an offensive spell during the first 30 seconds, even if it would land after the explosion.

An explosion will start the 30 second period, and a second explosion will start the match. A single “Fault” will be allowed (Casting before the explosion), and an event warning (not an official warning) be given to the offender. A second “Fault” per match is disqualification. We do realize that this is a “Quick Draw” match and that players will be trying to time the explosions as close to the mark as possible to gain advantage.

No activatable items of any kind shall be used in the match. This includes any right click items.

No use of Invert Gravity shall be allowed, this is the only spell being removed from the event, because it can knock the wizard out of the ring.

Both wizards shall stay on the tiled area at all times, you may not stand on or behind the firepots. You may not stand on or in the throne. Use of yonder is of high risk, if you go out of the ring you maybe disqualified. Binding in the ring and gating to a different part of the ring is no longer allowed in any VI Event.

This is a test of magic, not melee. No melee combat of any kind will be allowed in this event. No ranged weapons of any kind will be allowed in this event.

Spectators shall remain seated and not casting during the match. Any spell casting shall be considered aimed at a participant, and either harmful or helpful to someone in the match. Anyone who casts any spell during a match can be death touched (for no xp loss) and removed from the arena.

Spectators shall not use shout for any reason, but use /ooc instead. This is so that those involved in the match use shout, and the channel can be turned off to reduce distraction to the wizards involved.

Spectators shall not interfere with this event. This includes helping or assisting a person involved in the match in any way.

Rallos Zek Specific Rule : There shall be no looting of any item at an event, this includes spectators.

Tallon Zek and Vallon Zek Rule : You may request to have your faction restored to the condition it was in at the last available save. This is to insure that no unnecessary faction hits are taken at an event. There is no entry fee for this event, and the only prizes will be titles awarded to the winners. Title for winning at the server level will be the level 51 title for wizards. The title for the 3 runners up will be the level 56 title. The grand champion shall have his level 60 title given to him on his own server.

Those champions who go to test are bound by additional rules. The character used for the event cannot adventure on test for any reason. Handing off even the smallest items to a player on test is forbidden, as well as considered duplication of items.

The transfer of characters to test is midnight (give or take an hour) PST the night before the main event.

Lake Rathetear will be shut down several hours before the main event. The only characters allowed in the arena on Test for the main event will be the champion wizards, the judges of the event and the event staff, other members of Verant Interactive, and members of the press who sign up with Lady Daegarmo at least 3 days in advance via email.

All other characters on test found in the Arena during the main event will be summoned out of the zone and binded so they cannot return.

VI may limit the amount of people in the zone to control conditions of lag or frame rate for the participants. The event shall start open seating, but may close off during time. The Event Staff may use any sort of decision on who stays and who leaves to ensure the event is a success. This should not be implemented until a minimal of 150 players in the zone have been reached.

The Event Staff reserves the right to change the rules of the event at any time before, during, or after the event in the case of an unforeseen issue with the match.

Lady Daegarmo
Online Events Coordinator
Verant Interactive (Server event times and dates will be posted tomorrow)

Everlore's own David "Gaazoo" Bieranowski has just put the finishing touches on his Scars of Velious report from the recent Las Vegas launch party. This is an excellent report and will give you a good idea of what to expect from the new highly anticipated EverQuest expansion pack, a highly recommended read, here's a snip.

The Iceclad Ocean was a series of icy island and a large body of water.     Is EQ For High Levels Only?
    Some recent posts on the Verant boards seem to think so. Here is one such post and Gordon's reply:
    I don't know if it's only me noticing or I'm just quite delusional, butafter Halloween`s little event (Thank god I wasn't in Lake of Ill Omen),I am noticing more and more that EQ is turning into a highbie game.Case and point:
    Events: Dragon in LOIL the 31st, Highbie = event, Lowbie = fodder.
    Werewolves in WC, Highbies = event, Lowbie = fodder
    Expansion coming out: 35+ only (45-50+ realistically ?).
    New quests:Ultimate weapons (55-60)
    New trade skills: 45+ only (Yeah I'd like to see a 20-30 level do fineplate/cultural)

    The list goes on.

    Now I'd just like to see if there is a general feeling if this is trueand whether people think it's good or bad.

    [GORDONS REPLY]
    Please remember that the lower levels are a transitional period. Peoplemove through them, and once they are through them, they move on to otherplaces. For this reason, there's always been suitable in-game contentfor lower level folks, as people keep moving out of the lower levels.It's the high level game that is most in need of new content due to thegrowing number of "members" of that range.

    That said, we'd certainly like to see more dynamic events for the lowerlevel folks. We have the ability now to limit who can attackgm-controlled NPCs, and it would definitely be nice to employ that more.Of course, that's gm-controlled NPCs only. Computer controlled NPCsremain attackable by everyone.

    Scars of Velious Event.. A Report!
    Syndicate member, Bhri attended the Scars of Velious party this weekend and sends in this post party report!
    EVERQUEST SCARS OF VELIOUS
    Held in Las Vegas, NV

    For those that wanted to sign in early, the Planet Hollywood was the place to meet. After no time at all, it was standing room only. About 800 people showed up for this, many of which also headed to Game Works and had fun drinking till late in the night. The atmosphere was full of chatter as many people met for the first time.

    The main show was held in two Ballrooms inside the Bellagio Hotel and Casino. The halls were crowded with many folk until noon when the doors were opened. Upon entering, everyone got a key chain and T-shirt to go along with the nametags identifying who you were. There were 24 computers set up around the room, for people to try out the new lands. Around 1,800 people showed up for this event and there were not enough chairs for them all.

    Verant made a good show of names for this, with people like Brad McQuaid, John Smedley and Gordon Wrinn. The game programmers and artists were there along with the model Denise Harris, dressed up as Firiona Vie. Many of the Lead GM’s and GM staff were there helping also.

    A movie was played on two large screens for all to see. We were shown many new lands, races, creatures and armors (6 for each class BTW). Awards were given to the most people for a server, which Povar won. Along with many drawings for prints and other memorabilia, there was a large dinner planned later in the evening.

    I got to meet several other people who play on Tarrew Marr, including the GM Shanta. I have to say, it sure is nice to put a face to a name.

    Another nice note to make, for those who plan in the future. The next EQ event will be held in Baltimore, Maryland. And unless they change the dates, February 2 and 3rd should be it.
