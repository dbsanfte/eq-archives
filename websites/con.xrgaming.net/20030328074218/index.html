Kwip's Third Annual MS Walk-a-thon Story and Pledge Request
Posted by Sneezy at 11:47am on 03/18/2003

If you've played Asheron's Call or Dark Age of Camelot, you might be familiar with Kwip, the guy who brought us such classic comedies as The Ninja Story or Roleplaying on Merlin. If you aren't familiar with his writings, then check out the lists at the bottom of this post. Go read a few of them if you haven't. Even if you don't play any of these games you'll still get a laugh out of them, guaranteed ;)

In 2000 Kwipette (Kwip's wife) found out she had Multiple Sclerosis. Each year since then Kwip's been doing an annual MS Walk-a-thon and requesting pledges from the gaming community. Well, it's that time of the year.

You can read his plea for help here.

Here's an excerpt:

Gamers are vile, childish, immature people that lurk in their parents' basements and are completely socially underdeveloped. So says the media. But you know what? The first year we did the Walk, our local newspaper ran a wonderful piece on us, talking about how our fellow gamers from all over the world came together for this worthy cause, pitching in to help a friend that most of them had never met, barely knew - that only shared a joint love of games.

Wanna know how many national/international news agencies picked that story up? No, come on, I'll give you three guesses and the first two don't count. What's that? Zero, you say? Why, good heavens Miss Moneypenny, you're CORRECT! Nobody seemed very interested in a bunch of gamers doing something amazing for a great cause. Nope, not the television, not the newspaper, not the radio - zip. But if I went on a shooting spree, what do you want to be they would've been slobbering at the muzzle to cover all these 'violent' games I play? Oh, and don't forget demonic - we can't overlook the e-vul influence of these games! The good will, kindness, charity, and amazing coolness of our fellow gamers is always overshadowed by a few mentally unstable people whose families are desperate to find a scapegoat to lay the blame upon and an over-zealous media that loves to expose the 'dark side' of gamers.

Well, not this time. This year, I want to ROCK PEOPLE'S FACES. And I want your help. See, you guys have always been there for us. After I wrote that first story, the outpouring of support was overwhelming. And last year, despite my screwing up the prizes I set up for people, we still got a good response. But it wasn't as good as the first year, and that's got me nervous this year. I'm afraid that I'm going to pour my heart into this, and there's just not going to be the concern there once was. I'm afraid that the MS story is just going to be another internet 'has-been,' relegated to the same shelf as the groundhog page or the dancing baby. I know I can't make people care, but I can sure as hell try.
So let's get the word out, show our support, and help Kwip make this the most successful Walk-a-thon yet!

An Interview with Gina Ferguson, aka "Heartseeker" of Frostfell
Posted by Warcry News Network at 1:05pm on 02/05/2003

Gina Ferguson is a talented artist that runs the web site Nevermore Designs. In addition to her artwork, she also plays Asheron's Call, taking on the persona of "Heartseeker" on the world of Frostfell. I recently had a chance to interview with Gina about her work.

Is your artwork a profession, hobby, or both?
Well it is actually both. I use my art skills in my everyday work. Its not the same as doing a image, but it is something I still enjoy doing. The images I create are a hobby for now, but I hope to someday make it more then that.

How did you come up with the site name "Nevermore Designs"?
Well I am a huge Edgar Allen Poe fan. I wanted a site name that would stand out and something I could really work with a unique design and after a bit of thinking about names Nevermore popped into my head and it stuck.
