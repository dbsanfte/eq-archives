Shadows of Luclin Impressions
Jonathan went to E3.. he saw SoL.. and here is what he thought of it:
EVERYTHING HERE IS SIMPLY MY IMPRESSION from what I
was told by Verant employees. I do not guarantee the
authenticity of either the information I received, nor
do I take responsibility for my own, potentially
erroneous interpretation of any aspect of Luclin that
they discussed with me. I was told that Luclin is
still very far from release and many details have not
been finalized. I would not be at all surprised if
many of the features/details I mention below change
before Luclin is released.

Onto the info:

* The Beastlord class will be heavily pet dependent and
will probably, at the highest levels, create a pet
that is at least 10 levels lower than your character.
On the other hand, the Beastlord will have a variety
of buffs that will work only on his own pet (though
he will have other buffs that can be used on other
characters/pets) which could effectively increase
the capability of the pet by a factor of 10 levels or
more.

When I mentioned that this would cause problems with
aggro (i.e. low level pets causing everything green
to jump it) I was told that aggro is being redesigned
to take buffs and overall strengths of the pet into
consideration.

* Beastlord pets quite possibly will not be able to
persist through zoning due to the unusually large
difficulty of getting npcs to zone.

* Expect some aspects of Beastlords to be very similar
to existing hybrids, except with Shaman spells.
(At the same time I heard someone say that they'll
be totally different from hybrids with a lot of
unique spells, and more of a pet class, so I'm not
sure they're too solid on this.)

* Beastlord pets will be unique and will not be the
same as Shaman pets, or any other pets currently
in the game. The pet selection will vary depending
on the race of the Beastlord.

* The Bazaar zone will be in a central city located
on Luclin that is neutral to all races. The city
itself will be bordered by four different dungeons.

The Bazaar will feature a variety of named and
differently colored tents to make it easy to tell
players where you will be meeting them, and will
feature a bank. Additionally, the city will be, or
be right next to, the primary entry point for
Luclin, to make getting there convenient.

* Similar to Velious, there probably won't be any
factions that are correlated with factions in
the rest of Norrath, with the possible exception
of Kerran/Var Shir (since they are related.)
This is because Luclin has been cut off from
the rest of Norrath for quite some time.

* You will be able to play from level 1 to level
60 without ever leaving Luclin.

* It is quite probable that existing characters
will be given a one-time-only option to pick
a new face when Luclin is released.

* On a somewhat related note, Var Shir (Kerran)
models will not only feature different faces,
but entirely different matching skin patterns. In
addition to the tiger Var Shir pictured in
the E3 screenshots, there may be black panther
Var Shir, lions, leopards, etc.

* I asked about the alternative character
development and was told that there would be
new skills available for characters who are
level 60. To get and increase these skills,
you will need to spend experience you get
that is over the experience you needed for
level 60. (Currently any experience you get
over level 60 isn't really important, aside
from the death penalty.) You may also be
able to spend your post-60 experience on
perks like purchasing a title ("Bob the
vindicator", etc.)

* Armor now can have its own geometry. If
you take a look at the screenshots from E3,
you'll notice that human armor plates go
beyond the shoulders of the player model.
Expect to see armors that significantly
alter the appearance of a character due
to the ability to create different shaped
armor.

* The trees in Luclin very possibly will not be
animated, as people have pointed out from the
screenshot. An artist I spoke to said that this
is due to the mostly negative reaction to having
tree animations in the previous expansions.

Trade Fair Tonight on Rodect Nife
Here is a news blurb for those who play on RN Server:
Noradvo is hosting a open Guild Event and Tournament/Trade Fair Rodcet Nife server Friday May 18th, 8pm to 11pm est. in The Arena (entrance in Lake Rathe). Anyone that would like to join us for some friendly PVP fun is welcome to attend.

Events will include:
* PVP grudge matches - Who would you like to take a few shots at ?.
* Team PVP - we will split all participants into equal teams. (2, 3, 4 teams)
* Falling ladder tournament - We start with the two highest level Adventurers dueling and the winner stays in the ring. The next contestant immediately challenges until last man is standing.
* Free-for-all - mayhem at its finest
* Tactics battles - Tanks vs casters, Lots of Lowbies versus single Uber char, etc.
* Anything else we can dream up

Yes you will DIE, perhaps several times over. Your friends will kill you, repeatedly. And they will enjoy it. They will laugh and jeer (all in good fun) but there is a bright side. You will get to slaughter them in return. Everyone can bind in the Arena itself outside of the PVP area.

For those that get there early we will also be having a Trade Fair those with equipment sitting in the bank that they don't need. Or for those trade skill artisans that have unique wares to sell. Bakers and Brewers are encouraged to come and sell some delicacies to our tournament contestants and spectators. So get there early to get some good deals.

Sorry but the plans for the official "I went to the Arena and all I got was this lousy Bronze T-shirt" were shot down.

Noradvo is the official guild for the Norrath Adventurers Order. We consist of a group of friendly adventurous players that are after some fun and camaraderie. We consist of a large number of twinks and semi-retired players wanting to enjoy some time away form the pressures of Uber Guilds, camping and over-grinding. If you are interested in a low-pressure fun-seeking guild you are welcome to apply.

Our web page is at www.noradvo.com or you can send an email to Draco@Noradvo.com.

An Interview
Fanstory.com has released a new interview and they sent this news blurb:
Sing Lung is an editor in "real life" and the editor-in-chief of our new online magazine fanMAG. In the interview she offers advice on how writers can improve their work. She also talks about her motivation for her book Path of the Dragon and her love of massive multiplayer gaming.

An Excerpt:--

---------------

FanStory.com: How would you suggest a writer improve his writing?

SingLung: Learn to read critically, and learn to take criticism. Read and review as many stories as you can. Pay close attention to how the author writes -- what pulls you in to the story, and what knocks you out of it. If you don't like a story, figure out why. When you get a critical review, give the reviewer's suggestions some serious thought (as long as they are constructive). Try them out. You might be surprised if you do -- or you might find a better solution. When a critique puts me on the defensive or makes me feel compelled to explain my story, that tells me I should pay attention to it. It doesn't mean the reviewer is necessarily right, it just means I might not be getting my point across as well as I thought.

------------

Different Titles...
for male and female high level chars. Is this possible? Alan speaks:
I'm not sure I want to do this, because I don't know if we'll be able to use this information (and because I can only imagine the volume of e-mail I'm asking for here…). But what the heck.

Let us assume for the moment that we were going to have separate titles for male and female characters post 50th level. What would you suggest?

Let's toss in a few rules to make this easy. The 51-54th level title will be a one-size-fits-all title, to help reduce the number of acceptable names we need. The 55-59th title and the 60th title will require both a masculine and feminine version of the same title, not completely separate title. So witch and warlock might work, where warlock and sorceress would not. (Though I am would be tempted to leave the Monk ones as they are, in a way I can't see Monks distinguishing their masters by sex like that…)

Some titles are reserved (though I don't know all of them), such as Lord and Lady, because they are used as special rewards.

Let me list the current titles:
BARD – Minstrel – Troubadour – Virtuoso
CLERIC – Vicar – Templar – High Priest
DRUID – Wanderer – Preserver – Heirophant
ENCHANTER – Illusionist – Beguiler – Phantasmist
MAGICIAN – Elementalist – Conjurer – Arch Mage
MONK – Disciple – Master – Grandmaster
NECROMANCER – Heretic – Defiler – Warlock
PALADIN – Cavalier – Knight – Crusader
RANGER – Pathfinder – Outrider – Warder
ROGUE – Rake – Blackguard – Assassin
SHADOWKNIGHT – Reaver – Revenant – Grave Lord
SHAMAN – Mystic – Luminary – Oracle
WARRIOR – Champion – Myrmidon – Warlord
WIZARD – Channeler – Evoker – Sorcerer

The other possibility would be to just replace the masculine titles with others, so send in proposals about that too.

But keep in mind that I'm doing this before talking with the team about possibilities for making changes, so nothing may come of it. If you don't want to send in suggestions that might have no chance of seeing the light of day, then don't send them in.

Also, please keep in mind that we have to worry about titles that are directly connected with real life groups. We don't want to offend…
