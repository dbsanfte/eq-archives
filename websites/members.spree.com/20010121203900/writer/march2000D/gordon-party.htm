 The Christmas Party
© Donald Gordon 
It was cold when I walked to your house
the night black with stars,
and I wore the collar of my fleece
Elvis Presley side up.

The garden was wet with mud
and discarded children's toys.

I knocked on the steamed up kitchen window 
and you squinted a wave.
On the table
stubby bottles of continental lager
sat clustered
in primary coloured
cardboard cases.

but we drank Australian fizzy wine 
and crème de cassis
sticky-sweet like ribena.

It was another year
and like me 
the house was down at heel,
cluttered with the spoils of your auctions.
And what lot number was he?

But the wine did a good line in small talk.
And in the male bastion of the dinning room
the conversation ebbed and flowed on cars and TV.

The cheese on the table
smelt of your illicit sex.
But you both played your Christmas charades
to perfection.
And by the way,
is adultery a book, a film or a play?

The children surfed your new satellite TV,
while we watched our lives
drifting into mediocrity.
