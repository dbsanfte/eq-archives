 What's On The Web For Women
© Colleen Moulding

Compared with just a couple of years ago, women are very well catered for on the Internet. There are many sites full of information on health, business, homemaking, relationships, parenting fashion and lots more.

One of the newest sites for women is Beme.com This stylish site has sections for news, consumer, home life, entertainment, culture and trends containing up to the minute articles, opinion, comment or reviews.

Another site creating a buzz at the moment is Handbag.com with it's mix of features, news, chat and discussion groups. There are also free homepages and e-mail, ask the experts, polls and shopping. A good read.

For women in business there is no better starting point than Digital Women. This excellent resource has business tips, business ideas, local chapters, message boards, newsletter and classified ads, but most of all it is a place to network with other women in business. There is an exceptionally good online e-mail list where you can contact a brilliant group of knowledgeable, helpful and supportive women who nearly always know the answer to your business problems or will go out of their way to find out for you if they don't. It is also a place to share resources, inspire each other and share successes too.

There are also several useful sites online for the growing community of Mompreneurs and work-at-home moms. Work At Home Moms is a jolly site with lots of advice, cartoons, message boards etc. to help you cope with the wahm lifestyle. Bizymoms.com is another great resource for wannabee work at home moms. Liz Folger achieved her goal and shows you how to do the same, and Homeworking Mom.com has been helping mothers to become homeworking mothers since 1984. They have questions and answers, how to keep kids happy while you work, telecommuting, homeworking horoscopes, newsletters and links.

If your toddlers need taming or your teens are getting into trouble or you just need some ideas for getting one whole night's sleep a month, there is plenty of parenting help and support on the web. Parentsoup was one of the first places for parents to discuss, advise or problem solve for each other with plenty of expert advice on hand too. There is also lots of sound advice and support at The Cyber Mom.com

Fun and common sense advice whether you are falling in or out of love can be found at You Married Him In The Beginning deals with dating to proposals, In The Middle, with marriage and kids, and At The End with separation, divorce and loss of a loved one. Debate, articles and contests too.

If you haven't found anyone to fall in love with yet, perhaps you need some help with your flirting technique. At Flirtzone you can find articles and tips on how to flirt successfully, plus workshops, personal coaching, newsletter and social events.

For women who love to decorate I would recommend The Painted House from Debbie Travis' television show of the same name. Here you can learn how to do all the paint effects used on the show plus crafts, stencils and home ideas galore. At Home Decor Crafts you can find lots of easy, inexpensive ideas to add zing to your home, including crackle glazing without a kit, making simple tassels and decorative rope cording.

And if just reading about all that hard work has got you feeling a little peckish point your browser at Recipe Land.com where you can search the database of over 25,000 recipes for anything from candy to casseroles, dumplings to dog biscuits. Or try out some of Morten's Irish Recipe Collection before settling down at Pete and Johnny's Cocktails and Confessions site.

Happy surfing sisters!



Colleen Moulding is Editor of All That Women Want.com http://www.allthatwomenwant.com a magazine, web guide and resource for women everywhere.
