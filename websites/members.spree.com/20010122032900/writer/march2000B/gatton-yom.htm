Yom Kippur (9/19/99)

© Phil J. Gatton

lunchtime tomorrow's gonna suck

the besieged day planner has rolled to the day of atonement again

looks like a war zone

full of successful assaults on my idleness

skipping breakfast one day is no big deal, but

come noon there will be expectations

i forget friends and family and various obligations

but i never forget to eat

replaced this sundown to sundown with

what i've done this year to consume me

that gnawing feeling will consume me

i confess i'll be thinking about eating

pitting the enzymes against the overmatched calendar

hoping it will lose

like the idle calls from mother

saying "sorry i missed you"

meaning "why the hell can't i reach you?"

like cousin Mary

who knows i accept her as she is

but doesn't understand why "i can't talk right now"

means forever

like Jennifer saying "don't be a stranger"

meaning i should visit her in the purgatory

i put her in

though i still seem to miss her more than she misses being almost missus

like Debbie waiting for me to figure out she is the answer

when she isn't

and stomach will grumble like a an angry document shredder

waiting for the next chance to devour something

like a Tuesday or a Wednesday

or a beautiful mid-September

or a lunch hour

normally, i would substitute sugar for sleep

caffeine-free now, but bubble-sugar cold hand

mouth

repeat MOUTH

maybe i'll talk my way through the morning

today it will go dry

i will stay tired

exhausted from the overextension

installing software with a ruptured disk

being at least three people means

getting away with letting at least two assholes out

simultaneous parallel-processing dreams

pain like multiple syringe wounds

not really afraid to fail

just afraid to not have an excuse for it

like "i was busy doing something else"

"i wasn't eating my meat, ma'am,

because i was concentrating on the vegetables"

i'm sorry; i wasn't thinking

so that's the contrast today

i have had way too much on my plate

to be this famished

stalks and sprouts and sandwiches of guilt

over a full, and now protruding, belly

a buddha sits still in perfect suchness

while i spin like an electron, like a rotisserie microwave

generating meals of self-satisfaction

and simple sweet soul self-defeat

24 hours go too fast

to fast

keep the cabinets closed

the trick is not to hang on until dusk

but to learn not to fill the plate again

not to fill my mouth with the salt

and wonder why the cold sore stings

not to fill my mouth with starch

and wonder why i'm pressing so hard

not to fill my guts with meaningless matter

and wonder why i'm heavier

i remember being lighter

feeding off the air on a pre-autumn day such as this

i could draw sustenance from bird calls

and train trellises and oak leaves

i could plug in my aural IV

or turn up the car stereo

and be well-fueled

the sheer geometry of my surroundings

used to be of more import than three trapezoidal meals per day

song as rhombus

poem as pentagon

iamb as line segment

these were meant to fill me, compose me

teach me not to merely subsist

lunchtime tomorrow's gonna suck

but for once, my plate will be empty

and i will have my seder with my pen
