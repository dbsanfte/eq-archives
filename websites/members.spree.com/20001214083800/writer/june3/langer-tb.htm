 THEATER BALCONIES
© Susan Langer 


	
There is an old theater beside my house.
Since the age of six, when it is not open, I sneak
inside through the side door which is slightly ajar.
I enter into it’s huge silence. It is so black inside
that I can only stumble and feel my way along
with my hands. Cold cement touches my fingers
from the balcony wall as I move toward my
hiding place. I stumble along, tennis shoes
sometimes sticking to the floor from sticky candy
or soda which has spilled. There is an
overwhelming musty odor of old carpet, and wood
seats mixing with the sweetness of sugary candy
and saltiness of old popcorn . I take a deep breath,
feeling at once safe among my familiar smells. My
eyes slowly adjust to the pitch blackness
surrounding me as I lower myself into the corner
of the third balcony, first row, last seat. 

	
Once when I was seven, I heard strange
noises as the side door creaked open and someone
entered. They quickly moved through the
auditorium  to the outside lobby of the theater
busying themselves with a task of one sort or
another. Then , I thought it was safe to hide in the
first balcony, first row, first seat. Now, I know
that I am safer when I hide up in the third
balcony, first row, last seat. It is at least 45 steps
up to the third balcony. I count them each time I
visit my secret hiding place. It would take them at
least one minute to reach the third balcony. By
then,  I could move to the third balcony, 10th row,
6th seat or possibly to the 16th row, 8th seat. My
choices are endless and all provide me with a
sense of safety and refuge...

	


	
I crouch down by the floor and feel the
stickiness of the corner. Once safely tucked into
my familiar space, I can dream and  make believe
because my imagination is endless. I can be
anyone that I want to be, go anywhere that I
want to go. I can be a princess, or a movie star, or
a famous pianist, or a little girl who is loved by
her parents.
	

	
This is a safe space. I am safe to dream,
fantasize, be alone. No one will bother me here.
No one will hurt me here. No one knows that this
is where I hide when they can’t find me. It is my
place. I know that when I am here I am
completely safe from any pain.  I wish that I could
just stay here forever. But I know that when it
gets dark the theater opens. Then  I must go home,
But that is hours away.

	
I play for awhile. I imagine that I am in a great
concert hall and I am singing a beautiful song. Today, I
only pretend, singing in my heart, because I can’t take
the chance that they will hear me and discover my
hiding place. The audience applauds and stands to their
feet. They love my song. They love me. I have done well.
Once, I went up on stage and sang out loud. But my
heart was pounding so loudly that I could not hear if
they were coming. Pretending in my heart is safer. I
don’t want to lose my hiding place in the third
balcony, first row, last seat

	
... I tire of my game of make-believe. It is so
dark and peaceful here in this place. I close my
eyes and relax, breath  in, breath out, breath in,
breath out.........I sleep.
