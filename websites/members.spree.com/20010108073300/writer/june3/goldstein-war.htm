 The War
© Allison Goldstein 


I remember when mother
sent the men out to war
Men vs. The cold machines
uniforms missing the shiny buttons
I still see them in my dreams
so long ago, so long ago it seems

Now you can watch my fever grow
and you can see my eyes begin to glow
great green, green as the mist 
outside my window pane
full of pain
I'm full of shame
riding on the backs of iron horses they came

I shot out on my metal cross
turned upside down in industry
with hammers bashing in my brains
my red blood turned nearly black
with oxygen
as black as the roses hanging upside down 
for years to come,
outside my window's pain
where it rains bombs and all their 
beautiful colors
that burnt out my eyes
and the metal went through my palms and ankles
and I thought that I would die
but did I die?
 no sir, I only survive and survive

The bombs that rain the fire of colors
fall outside the metal rooms
and the men keep shooting and fighting and stabbing
and I wish I was out there so I could die too

But no,
the little girls must stay inside,
the echos of metal banging against 
the rain and clouds and fire of colors

			
I barely remember, how is the sky?
when the stars talked back and the grass grew high
and I breathed air and my lungs filled with joy
then the industry metal and copper came
and killed my soft and my air
The metal remains
and its smothering and its cold
where did all the color go?
			
Now I only dream,
dreams in black and white, dark and light
where are the colors?

My mother industry,
the cold against my thigh,
hums from the machine sing me to sleep
and I sleep on shells of shotguns
my pillow, some say its an old nuclear bomb
but its so soft compared to the walls,
they always vibrate
from the rain and fire and men blown against it,
I can hear them crying in my dreams

And in the last dream I saw the
colors of fire
and held a stranger in my arms
and he cried too,
cried steel and bullets
they clanged when they hit the ground
I can still hear the old familiar sound,
but I can no longer see
			
I feel the walls closer
to my 16 fingers and 22 toes
between the metal and my room 
with all the other broken girls
because mother won't let us die
industry loves us
and we must live so long to keep her running

So we sleep on shotgun shells
and nuclear bombs
			
and cry steel bullets
and dream of colors 
and try to claw at mother to let us out,
Out where mother industry

		
can't keep us safe
so we die
and the men who shoot and stab and kill
might hit us
and we will leave the land of 
cold machines
and find somewhere soft
and see all the colors,
well, I won't see
but I bet I could pretend when I feel
the soft something and hear no 
longer the dreaded humming mother
telling me to go to sleep

Sometimes I want to scream!
can't scream
Oh God -God don't want to scream
1 scream bounces off 50 metal walls
and in 250 corners
and off 10,000 shot gun shells
and then off 350 nuclear bombs
and off 400 scared broken girls 
waiting to get fixed,
they all scream too and it gets louder
til you feel sorta numb
and the red liquid drips from your ears
No-No don't scream
mother doesn't scream
she only hum, hum, hums

go to sleep and I'll fix you
that's mother
That's what mother always says
and one day we'll wake up
and we'll all be better
and one day when we grow up
industry will be everywhere
and the war will be over
			
and man will die,
all except 400 ex-broken girls
and some metal walls
echoing their screams
again and again.
