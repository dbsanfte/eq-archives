Gary Pence I'm 38 years old and I've been writing poetry off and on for several years, but only began taking it seriously about 4 years ago. I do much of my writing late at night while listening to music. My earlier works are written in traditional rhyme, but recently, I have switched to writing in free verse. Free verse allows me the freedom to express exactly what I am feeling without the limitations that structure and form bring about.

Editor of Spanish edition of Niederngasse, a bilingual monthly poetry magazine which appears online and is printed twice a year. Literary award, University of Puerto Rico for prose poem, 'Lluvia en negro.' Poetry in English published in Niederngasse, A Writer's Choice Literary Journal, 2nd River View, Disquieting Muses, The Thin Wire Journal, The Crystal Oasis, and accepted for publication in The Writer's Quill. In Spanish, publication in Mizar, Entre Amigos and Niederngasse, Spanish Edition. Poetry published in various print journals in Puerto Rico and Miami. Also practices law when not writing poetry.

Elisha Porat

the 1996 winner of Israel's Prime Minister Prize for Literature, has published more than a dozen volumes of fiction and poetry in Hebrew, since 1973. His works have appeared in translation in Israel, the United States, Canada and England. The Messiah of LaGuardia, his first collection of stories in English Translation, had published recently in Mosaic press, Canada.

Elisha Porat was born in 1938, to a "pioneer" family in Palestine-Eretz Yisrael, ten years before the independence of state of Israel, in 1948. In the early 1930th, his parents were among the founders of Kibbutz Ein Hahoresh, on the coastal plain of Israel. He draft into the Israeli army, the IDF, in 1956, and he served in a frontline reconnaissance unit and fought in both the Six Day War in 1967, and the Yom Kippur War in 1973.

As a liflong member of his Kibbutz, Elisha Porat has worked as a farmer as well as a writer. His labours in the Kibbutz fruit, mango and avocado fields have distinctly influenced his writing. In addition, Elisha Porat currently performs editorial duties for several literary journals.
