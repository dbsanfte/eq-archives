Sandra VonLienen (she prefers Sandy)resides in central Illinois. Mother of three, she began writing at 14 for friends in school, then through high school. After that, she wrote off and on, until finding Poet's Place on Aol. Her first publish was in the premier edition of Emotions Magazine, October 1997. Since then, she has been published and featured on several internet mags and newsletters including Internet Poetry Magazine (Premier Edition featured poet), Poetic Voices, Poetic Whispers, Poet's Review, Purple Inks, and Writers Quill. Sandy has also just received notification for 8 of her pieces to be published in an anthology, LINKS, by PoetWorksPress and had her first overseas publish in Sept/December 1999 issue of Writers Cauldron. Sandy currently has three chapbooks self-published for to use while on tour with a group of online poets and has published chapbooks for a few of her fellow poets. At the moment, most of her writing involves doing reviews and interviews for Robin Travis, editor of Poetic Voices. She believes there is a poet in all of us. We just need the encouragement and courage to try. Visit her web site.

Jim "Weisskid" Weiss I am a 68 year old, retired chemist with a wife, three children, a pug dog, and seven grandchildren who has had an abiding interest in history and politics among other things. I would very much like for my grandchildren to live in a community free of racial strife. The current controversy over flying the Confederate battle flag over the SC statehouse turned my attention away from ancient civilizations in the Middle East back closer to home. People detached from the issue of Southern pride versus Black pride may find the whole topic difficult to understand, but in the South nothing but college football or the 2nd Amendment rivals it in importance. It seems to me that if the modern Southerner really thought about it, he would be thankful that the South lost the war when it did because things could have gone on longer and gotten much worse as I tried to illustrate in the story. What I wonder about is how could anyone take pride in a heritage that included slavery?

Wacco Paul Wilson Wacco Aeron Paul Wilson Born last day of June 1951, I am a mixture of clashes. Born on a reservation in Montana and raised in southern Colorado with a northern Cheyenne mother and a Celtic/Welsh father my life has been exciting and at odds with itself. To explain the matter of my names, all I can say is my parents were also at odds. Wacco is from my mother and is Cheyenne for "Heavens Wind" or the equivalent, Aeron was my fathers choice and is of the Celtic/Welsh heritage. Paul is my Christian given name picked from the biblical apostles and Wilson simply my father's family handle. To confuse myself as well as my readers, I write under all my names and different combinations of them. Keeps me interesting critics say. I am a trained historical researcher, Work in Archeology, Ethnology and several other sciences as well as the paranormal to support myself. Writing has always been my passion, writing simply for the love and freedom of expression in words. I have written numerous poems, factual and fictional short stories, historical articles for local newspapers, magazines, minor book publications and the internet, as well as a full length historical/ fiction/action novel titled "The Existence Factor". I have done critiques on articles, stories, books and poetry for private writing clubs. I am known as "The Colorado Campfire Story Teller" to numerous boys clubs and outdoors organizations in my area and have a collection of these true stories of life experiences circulating in various published forms. In short I am excited by life, like people and love children of all ages...to me, 48 year olds are still kids with me being the biggest of them all...smile. I love adventure and seek it out wherever I go, always looking closely at the excitement, passion and emotion of a moment to capture with my words. I have written on nearly every subject and genre I can think of including science fiction. My hobbies are writing, painting, woodcarving, model railroading, miniature military figures, history, poetry, archeology, paranormalstudies, Arthurian romance literature, Astronomy, mysteries and anything else I can think of that touches my imagination with excitement and intrigue. I have a website called "The Storyteller's Hearth" where more of my stories and poetry can be read.
