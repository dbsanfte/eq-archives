Quick Overview of Basic Grammar

What is a noun ?

Basically it is the name of a person, place, idea, or thing. A common noun refers to any person, place, thing, or idea. I.E.-

  • girl
  • pony
  • barn
  • milk
  • religion
  • depression

    Proper Nouns refer to particular places, people, things, ideas, etc. I.E.

  • Leroy
  • Monticello
  • Texas
  • Baptist

    Nouns can be used as the subjects of sentences or as the objects of verbs and prepositions.



    WHAT ARE PRONOUNS ?

    Pronouns take the place of a noun. I.E.

  • Betty talked to her doctor before he had had the time to examine her test results.



    WHAT IS A VERB ?

    Verbs show a state of being or an action, sometimes they also refer to the time of being or action. I.E.

  • She refused to accept his reasons. (past)
  • Tom needs to be understood. (present)
  • Rose will enjoy her new job. (future)



    WHAT IS AN ADVERB?

    Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs. They are used to specify how, when, where, and how much. I.E.

  • The dog howled loudly as her owner walked away.
  • It is much colder that I thought.



    WHAT IS AN ADJECTIVE ?

    They specifiy color, size, shape, number, etc. They are used to describe nouns. This is called “modifying”, thus adjectivies are “modifiers”. I.E.

  • A blue glow shined from the lower portion of the new dwelling.
  • The three old owls lived in the huge new barn.



    WHAT ARE CASES ?

    Case is the form of a noun, or pronoun which shows its relationship to the other words in a sentence. We have 3 cases in English.
  • nominative - used for the subject of the sentence or clause
  • objective - receives the action of the verb or noun
  • possessive - showing who owns something

    Nouns don’t change their form except in the possive case when we add an ‘s. Pronouns have different forms for each case.



    WHAT ARE PREPOSITIONS?

    Prepositions show how a noun or a pronoun relates to another word in the sentence. I.E.

  • James came storming into the party.
  • In this case, we were wrong.
  • She ran behind the leader the whole race.

    If a preposition is used with a verb, then the combination of the verb & preposition usually have a different meaning than the verb alone. I.E.

  • I have to lookinto your eyes.
  • Have you come to any choice in


    WHAT ARE CONJUNCTIONS?

    Conjuctions are connectors. They join words, phrases, and/or clauses. Coordinating conjunctions connect elements of a sentence of the same value; single words, phrases, or clauses. These are :and, but, for, or, nor, either,yet, so, neither, and so that.

    Subordinating conjunctions join 2 clauses, the main clause and the dependent clause (or subordinate).
  • These are:because, although, since, until ,while and some others which place a condition on the sentence.
