 How To Get Whatever You Want Out Of Life
© Barbara J. Kenzik

It seems every time I pick up a magazine or visit the bookstore I am bombarded with information on "How To Get What You Want Out Of Life.

I wonder, does anyone really get everything they want out of life, or are they actually putting the 'cart before the horse'.

To paraphrase John Kennedy, 'ask not what someone can do for you; ask what you can do for someone'.

1) What do you have that other people want or need? A skill, talent, - a product?

2) If you don't have these things, how can you get them? Surely you have some life and/or work experience that can offer a clue.

3) If you do have these things, why should someone buy from you instead of the guy down the block? What are you offering/doing differently that makes you unique?

When I tell people I wrote a small book on wardrobe planning, I get a lot of blank stares. But wardrobe planning is something everyone should take seriously. After all, the three basic needs in life are: food, shelter and clothing - which translates into shopping, something women do a lot of. And yet they continue to complain that they 'have nothing to wear'.

My 'skill or talent' is in observing that many women shop impulsively and without a plan. This can be expensive! In my book, I created the 'Rule of 3' and, using my retailing expertise, developed a simple guide so that the 'secret' to wardrobe planning is no longer a secret. I saw a need (women who struggle to get dressed each day) that no one else seemed to be addressing, and I offered a solution, in the form of a product, a small book. What makes my product/idea different is that it is so simple, people say, 'why didn't I think of that'. And it is for the most part mistake-proof. You can't mess up. I get a great deal of satisfaction from hearing women tell me they were able to make some major improvements to their wardrobes in just a short time.

Money is nice, but doing something constructive for someone wins hands down every time. Try it. See if you can come up with an idea or product that addresses an everyday problem and maybe it too will bring you 'whatever you want out of life'.




Barbara J. Kenzik
