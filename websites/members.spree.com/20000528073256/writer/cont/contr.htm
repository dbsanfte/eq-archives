Nathan Rabe

I was born in India in 1957. I am currently living in Oxford, England but my home base in Melbourne, Australia. When I'm not writing I manage relief operations throughout the world. At the moment I am Extremely busy with the Kosovar crisis but have also worked in Africa, Asia and the Middle East. My first novel, The Book of Accounts, is near publication here in the UK. It is a political thriller based in Iraq. I'm currently working on my second novel, The Shah of Chicago. I am married with no children

Three collection of poetry published plus non-fiction books. Collection of short stories due out soon. Editor of Southern Ocean Review, on-line and paper magazine of writing.


Joy Reid

began writing poetry seriously in 1997. To my surprise, I became successful quickly, so much so that my work now appears internationally in fifteen hard copy magazines and over seventy zines. I like to write about anything which captures my attention using it as a means to explore my response to whatever the stimulus might be. My writing tends to be visual. Snobbish as it may sound, I can't bear overtly cerebal poetry which makes no attempt to engage the senses, but philosophises on the condition of mankind - blah! I would like to revitalize interest in poetry among ordianry Australians - wish me luck.


Scott Reid, MA,

teaches poetry workshops in the San Francisco Bay Area. He currently teaches The Albany Poetry Workshop in the East Bay As an adjunct instructor in the Special Education & Seniors Program at Santa Rosa Junior College, he presents autobiography writing courses for Sonoma County elders. His poems have appeared in Blue Unicorn, The Berkeley Poetry Review, Tomcat, Switched-On Gutenberg, Morpo Review, and First Leaves. He has received writing fellowships to attend writers' conferences at Squaw Valley, Napa Valley, and the Bread Loaf Writers' Conference. He lives in Healdsburg, California.

Jerry Reynolds

There's a primitive tribe that carves huge birds from milky white wood and leaves them hanging in the jungle to surprise and delight whoever stumbles along. They do this to keep their souls from getting bored and drifting away. They call their artistic endeavors "toys for the soul". My writings are my "toys" and the InterNet is my jungle. Should I fail to surprise and delight any unwary surfers, I'll settle for becoming rich and famous.

Margaret Catherine Rigsby

lives in northeastern Alabama where she shares domicile with five exotic birds and two eccentric cats. Margaret has been writing since her earliest remembrance of the day she realized she could not draw to express herself. She has studied at Calhoun Community College and the University of Alabama at Huntsville. Her webpage

Her work has appeared in various anthologies and small press publications. Her most recent hard-bound publication, discounting poetry anthologies, were three poems in Proteus Literary Magazine/Spring 1998 which was published by St. John s University in New York. Margaret is honored to have received various literary awards, the most recent being the Golden Brutus 1998 Comedy Award presented by the website Remember the Flame for her poem, I don t want to do the twelve steps, and Mona's Flame Award for Outstanding Poetry for my two poems, "Hungry Soul," and "King-Size Bed," on March 13, 1999.

Most recent publishers are, A Writer's Choice Literary Journal, Mind Fire Poetry Journal, Whisper's On-Line Magazine, The Amateur Poetry Journal, PoetryDownunder, Perspectives, NetSister's Newsletter, Sonata, malryn magazine for the arts and m.e.stubbs poetry journal, a quarterly. She has two poems coming out in an anthology to be published by Maelstrom.

Margaret is also a member of the Alabama Writers Forum which supports the Alabama State Council on the Arts as well as a consumer and advocate for the Mentally Ill.
