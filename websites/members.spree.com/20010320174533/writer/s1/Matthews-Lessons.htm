 I've Learned: I Can But...I Shouldn't - What Stops Personal Growth?
© Philippe Matthews

For the past fifteen years, I've interviewed several success gurus and celebrities. I've met hundreds of people who have expanded their mindset from why to why not and I've discovered a major truth in why people who can and should succeed don't - they don't believe they deserve to be there! Did you know a lot of people believe they can but they don't believe they should? There are people who feel they could succeed but feel they don't deserve to succeed.

You can't succeed until you feel you should succeed. Why do people work so hard to reach a goal only to stop when the achievement of the goal is clearly in sight? Why do people back out on solid business deals that could make them independently wealthy? Why would we sabotage a positive relationship with someone who clearly loves us? Why do we say I should but I can't? Powerful questions with a devastating answer which is why we must develop an understanding of the law of "Have." You must begin believing that you already have success and that it is your divine birthright to have it. You must be thoroughly convinced that success and achievement is the natural order of your life and that there should not be any contradiction to this conviction.

THE LESSON OF EXPECTATION

Eliminate all doubt that you can't have something, be something or do something. You must completely surrender to the law of positive expectation. Peak Performance Trainer, Brian Tracy in an interview said this: "The law of expectation says that you get what you expect. Not what you want, but what you expect. If you expect good things to happen to you, then good things tend to happen to you. Positive, successful people have an attitude of positive expectation. They're constantly expecting to do well." Develop on all mental and spiritual levels that success is your destiny!

Expectation narrows the gap between what you believe you deserve and desire and the fulfillment of that belief and desire causing you to attract your desires like a magnet attracting metal. Your entire life is solely based on your expectations. If your expectations are low, life is a struggle. If they are in the medium range, you will enjoy mediocrity, because that zone can appear comfortable. But if your expectations are high, inspiration burns high, enabling you to move above the subtle influence of mediocrity.

Simply stated, what you expect becomes your reality as well as what you don't expect becomes your reality too! In other words, you want to have a clear and concise assessment of what you want in life, or you may end up with the opposite of what you want. The law of expectancy does not accept an inert mind. You are always expecting something, whether you are conscious of it at the moment or not. What you fear the most is an expectation. What you don't feel you deserve becomes an expectation. So by all means, consciously expect good things.

An intriguing example that demonstrates the power of expectation and how it deeply influences the direction of our life comes from a true story of a coal mining disaster in Germany when a small group of miners were trapped underground after a massive cave-in. They realized that the air in the mineshaft would last for only a limited number of hours. As it turned out, just one member of the group was wearing a wristwatch, and he began to announce the time while the men anxiously awaited rescue. To sustain hope in the others, he didn't announce the right time, however; he called out one hour whenever two hours had passed. Six days later, a rescue team found the trapped miners; amazingly, they were all still alive with one exception -- the man who wore the wristwatch died.

The man literally conditioned and expected himself to die after a certain amount of hours had passed, causing the expectation of his death to become a self-fulfilling prophecy. This story represents a prime example of how so many of us accept a concept or idea, expect it to occur and by default, run right into it in our future.

To give you another example of how what happens when you expect failure, let me tell you about a blind date I didn't feel I deserved to be with women who were attractive. I was eighteen years old, and looking for lust in all the wrong places. For one, I didn't feel I deserved to be around beautiful women so deep within I expected a lower standard of happiness in my relationships.

A friend of mine I worked with at the time told me of this young lady she wanted me to meet. She told me about the proverbial "beautiful personality" she had which should have triggered in me that I was being had by my own low self-expectations. When I talked to this young lady with the beautiful personality on the phone, she told me she was drop-dead gorgeous, but she had been in an accident a week or so earlier. Ding, ding, ding, ding! No, I didn't see this one coming.

I agreed to meet with this drop-dead gorgeous woman with the beautiful personality for lunch. I told her she would recognize me from my clean-shaven head and six feet two inch height. She told me, I'd recognize her from her low-crop hairstyle and flowery dress she was wearing. When I arrived, there stood a woman who had patches of her hair missing and told me that she had been in a car accident a week earlier and wore a cast from a broken ankle and arm. Surprisingly, she had several teeth missing. I asked her if this was a result of the car accident. She said, "no, I got into a fight with my sister and fell, knocking out my two front teeth." This also accounted for her low-cropped hairstyle; her sister had pulled most of it out during the fight.

So, here I was with this toothless, woman on crutches with less hair in places than I had. What I found hilarious is that her nickname was "Lucky Lucy!" The law of low expectation had its nasty, cynical grips around me and I could not break free for that entire day. All I could do was say to myself, "I told ya so" because it's what I expected even though I could have done better. Since I believed I could not get anything better, I programmed my self-image to go out and attract exactly what I truly expected to have.

Lesson Learned: Believe in yourself. Expect the best and realize that you deserve the best. You were not put here to live up to low expectations!
