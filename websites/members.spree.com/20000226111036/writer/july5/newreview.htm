Things I Remember
© Patrick Ellam
Minim Books
Library of Congress Catalog number 97-076275
Available from:Minim Books Website

An interesting and lively book from Patrick, with never-before told stories of his days as an English spy, and many other fantastic tales.

It provides us with a look back at this century written by an exceptional man at the age of 78. Many of the tales he has related to us couldn't be told for the past 50 years- but are ours now to read and live vicariously through Patrick's eloquent prose sprinkled with his British humor.

Patrick tells us stories of the 2nd World War in which he served in the British Expeditionary Force in France where he led a force of 200 in defending London against the German bombings.

Then, still seeking more action Patrick volunteered to parachute behind enemy lines. To prepare he went to spy school learning the valuable skills of forgery, burglary, hand to hand combat, knife fighting and many other things we see in the movies. But this wasn't a movie it was his life!

The work he did there behind enemy lines in Germany was top secret and as such could not be revealed until now.

This is a fast paced, exciting, funny, and terrifying look into the world of espionage, life, and the amazing spirit of Patrick Ellam.


Shadow of the Serpent: A Coyote Moon Story
© James Joseph
Audenreed Press
A Division of Biddle Publishing Company
ISBN: 1-879418-80-0
$14.95
Available from:Audenreed Press

Shadow of the Serpent: A Coyote Moon Story is one of the best Native American books I've read. Those of you who know me- understand that that is quite a statement as I've read well over 200 of them, and published my own native american haiku. I'm not an expert in the field- but pretty close.
The book is set in prehistoric North America and Joseph weaves quite an extravagant story of ancient civilizations, nomads, and the ever-present struggle for control of land and domination over others.
This work could be called action/adventure as it contains plenty of both, but it also mixes fact & fantasy which leads us on a spiritual journey as we view what we have made of this world. The parables contained within the text are insightful, and deeply provocative. James has encased these moral gems into a very well crafted narrative which is exciting, enticing, and eloquent. He brings us a closer look at ancient rites and rituals and helps us to see the need for such in our present world.
This book gets my highest recommendation- a bear paw salute !!


The Wide Open Door
©Tav Sparks
Hanford Mead Publishers
ISBN # 0894868675
$12.00
Available from:Hanford Mead Publishers...
The Wide Open Door connects The Twelve Steps of Alcoholics Anonymous to some of the world's greatest spiritual philosophies - Jungian psychology, yoga, the Tao, tribal rites of passage, and more. The book invites us to rediscover the Source behind the Steps through direct personal experience within ourselves and with our Higher Power. The author discusses the experiences of surrender, death and rebirth, and wholeness. He also describes Eleventh Step techniques, those inner experiences which maintain and deepen recovery.

Having read many, many self help, meditation, and Twelve Step books I am somewhat of an expert in this genre. Tav gives us a unique melding of spirituality, the Twelve Step tradition, psychology, and mysticism. Through his insight he helps us to see the Twelve Steps in a new light, a more profound understanding of the traditions. Blending these with a mixture of the Tao, yoga, rites of passage, and Jungian psychology provides a much deeper understanding of addiction and recovery. The Wide Open Door is perhaps the most important contribution to the addictions field in years.

This is a deeply personal and caring work blending ancient truths with modern psychological knowledge, a definite must for anyone in recovery, or those of us who care about someone in recovery.
