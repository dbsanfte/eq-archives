 The Little Engine that took Action, with Faith and Trust
© Julie Jordan Scott

Trust, faith and action go hand in hand against fear, denial and stagnation.. Which would you rather shape your life?

As little children learn to speak, they simply speak. They do not become concerned that their sounds are gibberish, gobbledy gook and make no sense. Adults delight in each sound. Never do adults say, "Hey cut that out! We can't understand you anyway so why bother?" Our enthusiastic response inspires language learners to continue.

In the famous story, the Little Engine that Could, the hero was an initially uninspiring little train, who courageously took on a job that was bigger and more challenging than his norm. He literally thought himself over the mountain. Who can forget the infamous, repititious line of that feisty little rail car: "I think I can, I think I can, I think I can."

He believed he could: he had faith in his ability. He trusted that even when the picture is contrary to his faith and belief, he could use that faith and belief to overpower the stagnation that is so everpresent with fear and denial.

Can you hear it? As his wheels start chugging, slowly at first, the faith and trust are building. The motion is starting. That alone begins to nullify the inertia of the fear and denial.

I (pause) think (pause) I (pause) can (pause) I think (pause) I can (pause).

What the Little Engine had to decide was what would continue to propel him, just as you need to decide what will turn the tide for you.

Is it making lists, putting it in black and white the answer for you? Will that make your faith and trust come alive to fuel your action?

Perhaps for you it is talking it out with a group of close friends and allies, seeking counsel, and hearing from their perspective to validate your faith and trust and then take action accordingly.

Another method might be gathering more evidence of others successes (perhaps from history or doing a web search). What you learn may give your faith and trust the reasoning to take action or it may give you another message, to say try something else. The key there is you are taking action, making something happen that was not happening prior to setting the wheels in motion.

The little Engine climbed over the mountain because he thought he could and he took action towards it, whether or not it appeared possible to the outside world. He did not allow fear to take root, creating quicksand in his train- soul.

"Thinking is easy, acting is difficult, and to put one's thoughts into action is the most difficult thing in the world." are the words of Johann Wolfgang van Goethe. How has this statement been the truth in your life? How come? And how can being like the Little Engine make putting your thoughts into action more easily and with greater power?

Without action, nothing will happen. Without momentum, little will happen. Without taking the leap, a year from now you will be where you are now simply with a calendar with a different year listed across the top.

In the words of Dale Carnegie, "Action breeds confidence and courage. If you want to conquer fear, do not sit home and think about it. Go out and get busy."

I think I (pause) can. I think I can. IthinkIcan,IthinkIcan,IthinkIcan.

You can.
