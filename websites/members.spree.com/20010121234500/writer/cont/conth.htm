Maryann Hazen-Stearns Maryann Hazen-Stearns is currently enjoying her new position as Poetry Editor of the award winning internet magazine, "The Writer's Hood". She's always looking for submissions. She's also a Poet, Medical Transcriptionist, wife and mom. Mary has been published internationally in hundreds of magazines and anthologies in electronic and hard copy format and has also won several awards and contests. She has five self-published chapbooks which may be viewed at her site: http://www.geocities.com/faerhart/LimboStick.html . A selection of her poetry may be heard in an audio edition of "Ygdrasil." She's made guest appearances on her local TV network, "The Helen Winer Show." Mary has been interviewed in magazines such as "Perihelion," "The Writer's Hood," "Pillow Screams," and "Gotta Write Network." She will be receiving the "Silver Rose Award for Poetic Achievement" in the near future. Mary also judges a monthly writing contest for "Inscriptions," that is, when she has not entered the contest herself! Her poem "Seeing Keisha See" placed 3rd in the "Preditors & Editors Best Poem of 1999" Readers Poll and she placed 4th in "P&E Best Poet on the Internet 1999" as well. Mary loves Renaissance Faires, birdhouses, her pet hamster and reading poetry, fantasy, SciFi & Horror. Other hobbies include needlepoint and gardening. She has an awesome tin collection and avoids the kitchen as much as possible.

Jean Hull Herman

Jean Hull Herman was born and raised in Wilmington, Delaware. She graduated from Allegheny College in 1966 with a Bachelor's in English Literature. She earned her teaching accreditation for senior high English at the University of Delaware in 1969. She taught at Seaford High School. She was on staff at the start-up of Delaware Technical and Community College in 1968, developing courses for GED certificates and nurses' training. Recently she has resumed teaching at Del Tech. Her work last year was rewarded with a Teacher of Note article in the college's student newspaper.

MÖBIUS: The Poetry Magazine, was born in 1982, and is published semi-annually, at Memorial Day and Thanksgiving. Under Jean's direction, the magazine grew from a local pamphlet featuring twelve local artists, to an internationally recognized anthology. The Netherlands, Sweden, Japan, Switzerland, Germany, Great Britain, Mexico, New Zealand, Peru, Argentina, Australia, the Canadian provinces, and all American states, have been represented. In the past year, poetry from all seven continents (yes, Antarctica!) found its way to the magazine's pages.

Jean continues her affiliation with the American Association of University Women (as a lifetime member) in Wilmington; she had been elected President of the Grosse Pointe Branch. She is a member of the Delaware Press Association. She is a member of the Diamond State Branch of the National League of Pen Women (just elected Secretary of the Board for two more years). She attends the Philadelphia Writer's Conference on a scholarship from the Diamond State Branch of the National League of American Pen Women. Her poetry has been published in a variety of small press magazines, including Bound and Penny Dreadful.
Art Hernandez Was born in Cuba, December 6, 1965. Granted political refuge, my family and I fled to Madrid, Spain, where my twin sisters, Gloria and Jackie, were born. We then migrated to the United States, Cincinnati, Ohio, where my brother, Jimmy, was born . We moved to Tampa, Florida, where I had a poem published in the annual school poetry book at Alexander Elementary. I graduated high school in 1984; afterwhich, I went to Tampa Technical for two years and received an A.S. Degree in Computer Engineering Technology. I am married to a lovely lady, her name is Donna; she's blessed me with three children (Nikita, Melissa, and Nicholas). My hobbies include reading, writing, sports, and computers.
Fiona M Hess was born in Johannesburg, South Africa in 1974. My family moved to Cape Town shortly afterwards and that is where I have lived up till 1999. I completed BSc (Hons) Geochemistry and MSc Chemistry degrees at the University of Cape Town. Thereafter I left for the sunny skies of Perth, Australia and worked as a laboratory assistant at Murdoch University. I love sport and do a lot of cycling, swimming and play squash. In March 2000 I head back to Pretoria, South Africa where I will start my first 'real' job. I have written poems episodically from the age of about 16. They are mostly written in response to people or events that have touched my life.
