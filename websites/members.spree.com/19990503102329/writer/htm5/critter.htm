UP, UP... ,BUT NOT ALWAYS AWAY!

Dee Walmsley
Copyright Dee Walmsley

Kids do it for fun at birthday parties . Merchants use it as an advertising gimmick. Our own government does it in celebration. At one time or another we may have all done it ignorant of the consequences. I am talking about the release of helium balloons into the atmosphere.

How, one might ask, is that doing any harm? What goes up must eventually come down and the problem, is where. Air currents frequently float these helium- filled potential killers out to sea where they burst and enter the water. Hungry sea turtles, mistaking the floating balloon for a jelly fish, snap up the balloon, string and all. The string becomes entangled in their intestines causing a slow agonizing death.

Turtles are not the only marine life affected by this simple toy. A young sperm whale was found dying from a balloon stuck in its stomach and three feet of purple ribbon wound throughout its intestines. Balloons have been spotted as far out to sea as 200 miles, generally singly but clusters have also been sighted.

What is the alternative? For the merchants, hand out balloons but let the kids have the fun of blowing them up. As for the other events, would not the release of thousands of lady bugs have a greater positive impact to our environment? Think of the beauty and fun of releasing butterflies in celebration. Plant a tree, or visit a wood-lot . If the event involves children have them make some pine cones stuffed with peanut butter and bird seed to take home to their back-yards. Why not give nature a hand? The next time you feel like celebrating, think of some ways to share your happiness with the environment, like freeing those little bugs to do what they like best ....eating up aphids.

I am a wildlife rehabilitator having raised raccoons, possums and squirrels for release back into the wild. I founded and currently chair an Oil Spill Society which trains locals how to care for oiled birds and beaches in the event of an oil spill and teaches school children of the impact of oil in our environment. I have been doing this for 9 years. I have a monthly nature column which is published in 3 local magazines I also conduct Living with Wildlife lectures in local schools. I have been fortunate enough to have produced /scripted a number of videos for cablevision on oil spills and urban wildlife. I have just completed two videos for children on oil and the marine environment and a public service announcement for the Scleroderma Foundation. I am the coordinator for a tutoring group for the learning disabled and produced a video on that subject "Look a little Deeper". I am now persuing a career as a freelance writer. I am working on a non-fiction novel about raccoons and a fiction novel The Hunter and the Rehabber. I enjoy writing about nature with a message.
