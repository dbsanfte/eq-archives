Enchanted Melody 

~ A DAYLIGHT DREAMING ~


Take me away to the sweet land of Fae
Where dreams will all come true
Goodness, and beauty reign night and day
Magick, no evil stew!

Enchanted forest, sing your song for me
Melody of romance
May true love come, a heart who will be
My own eternal dance

Oh shelter me my kin, my Faery friend
Give your love your kisses
To my lone heart, for tenderness to mend-
I will be your Mrs.


Part 1 - Late Summer 
The seasons of life ebb and flow
Some lift you up,
While others bend you low
Treasure each one,
We need them all to grow.

It was a rare summer indeed, for the Fae families of the enchanted forest. The harvest was abundant, cellars full and over flowing. Wheat was stored for grinding in the months ahead. Timber - a plenty for warmth, and building - was split, smoothed and stacked away. Vegetables, and fruits, both canned and fresh stored in the cellars.

Valenia's grandma Zola, and mother Delina, had the gift of healing. Herbal medicine was also stored if needed for illness and birthing. They attended all Fae births in their village, blessing mothers and all wee younglings. Legends tell of Fae birth rites, a magical joy, an innate goodness passed from generation to generation.

Hardy laughter echoed through the trees. Good cheer abounded with much hustle and bustle as they prepared for the coming winter festival. This was to be the largest, most celebrated in the realm.

Valenia, their own fair maid was now promised to Prince Nyklus. They would celebrate their wedding rites during the height of the gathering. The Fae people held a winter solstice festival each year. King Augus, and Queen Nera joined them every fifth year. This year was an exception with their son's wedding, they would come two years in a row. A festival castle was built over the summer for their visits. The people were proud of the workmanship built with both stone and wood. They also built a huge portico near the entrance for all the celebrations.

This was where the wedding rites would be held, at the edge of the meadow with the castle up on a knoll. Incense cedar, spruce, and many other varieties of pine surround this meadow and glen. Year round, severe weather skirts the Fae forest making ideal conditions. They enjoy the changing four seasons in all their beauty with ideal temperatures. Winter was especially exquisite in beauty, with crystal frost forming a light coating on everything until the glen sparkled like diamonds. Snow fell, yet never more than an inch or two. The entire village resembled a wonderland. Certain varieties of flowers thrived, Pansies, Chrysanthemums, and the sacred miniature white orchids. With a light sprinkling of magic, and shelter from trees, to break the wind blooms were huge!

Earlier in the summer, when Valenia and Nyklus were out walking they heard the sound of hoof beats. A herd of unicorns came running toward them. Even at a distance Val could tell that Leap, [short for Leopold] her beloved childhood friend was in the lead. They stopped short of them near a grove of conifers to graze on grass. Val's heart was pounding as she told Nyklus she had not seen him since he left last spring. Just then he left the others coming toward her. Following behind him were his mate, a small female and their little foal. The baby reminded her of Leap when he was young. Leopold put his nose in her hand, she gave him a few pine nuts, [her favorite snack] he knew she kept them in her pocket. He nudged her in fondness and recognition. Valenia was so happy to see him and his family. The herd stayed near the meadow for a few days where she visited often with Nyklus watching in fascination. Valenia was content to know he was happy with the herd and his own family.

This time of growing love between Val and Nyklus was precious. Two lives growing into one toward their joining. They had spent time in both the glen, and at the main castle to learn more of their new lives together, so much to learn. There was time to listen to each other, and for their friends. Then, there were times of silence, when quietness was the beauty of their language. No words were needed, love filled the spaces. Feeling went beyond what words could express.

Dak, Valenia's brother, and wife Cynan had adopted a puppy from friends. Animals ran free and enjoyed life with the Fae. Trina, another friend had given Valenia and Nyklus a kitten. This growing kitten traveled back and forth with them as they were both fond of her. She was calico, and they named her Bits, most often calling her 'little Bits.' They had another pet, Nyklus gave Val a horse as an engagement gift. She'd never ridden and enjoyed having Nyk teach her to ride. No less did he enjoy teaching her, she was a natural. He chose a young sturdy mare from the castle stable. She was both gentle and beautiful with a gold coat, and white mane and tail. Her name was 'Neea'ba' which means swift beauty in the ancient Fae tongue.


Part 2 - Love's Full Joy 
It takes two for soaring heaven
with wings touching, rejoicing.
Sublime eternal love,
their souls reaching, joining.

Weather cooled once again in the glen as fall came, and went, bringing color to the trees and foliage. As winter neared the level of excitement grew into joyous celebrating. A party for the engaged couple was planned. Everyone brought hand made gifts made with love. This was a traditional joining party complete with favored dancing, food, and drinks.

While all the busy preparation went on, other dramas were unfolding. Trina who watched Jen from afar for as long as she could remember tried her best to keep her secret. Jen was popular, and nine years older. She imagined he never noticed her in the back ground. Trina was painfully shy and almost never let her feelings show. She pictured herself, as drab, heavy, and not very attractive. She did admit that her eyes were large and round! Her father once told her she had eyes like a young deer. Trina's mother said they were her best feature.

In fact Jen noticed her, oh yes. He went out of his way to find reason to catch a glimpse of her. This was not easy as she was an only child of an older couple. They lived at the edge of the village.

Once he saw their cottage roof was in need of repair. He offered to help Trina's father fix it. But that was earlier in the year, several months back. Jen purposely worked slow to have an excuse to be near. Her father invited him to stay for an evening meal, for he was genuinely fond of him. Trina hardly spoke, and kept to the kitchen, it was natural for her to help her mother. She liked cooking, and keeping the home. When ever she thought no one noticed, she'd look at Jen through heavy lashes. Clearly she adored him, and had for a long time.

He found her shy ways appealing, and thought her form lovely in every way. The fact that Trina saw herself as heavy, caused Jen's eyes to follow her every move. Full womanly curves kept him busy finding excuses to help out. He dug the garden up for their small winter crop which was near the cottage. Trina helped by weeding, she reached across to grab a weed and accidentally touched his hand. Good gravy he thought, she blushed bright as beets! Feeling bold Jen took her hand and squeezed it gently. He smiled into those big eyes of hers, she forgot to breath. He bent down and just brushed his lips across hers. Without thinking, speaking in a whisper Jen said, "I love you Trina." In silence, she raised on her toes, she kissed him with a passion which surprised them both!

Love seemed to bloom in mid air, as though it were catching. Even Bouck, Valenia's youngest brother was eyeing 'every' young girl in the glen! Cynan, Val's sister by rites, and best friend was with child now. She was so happy, and wore her clothes loose for comfort. Dak strutted about like a Peacock on parade. He was clearly about to bust his shirt buttons with pride. Their wee one would arrive soon. Cynan was sorely disappointed, due to her advanced condition. she couldn't be Val's 'Matron of the Joining.' One evening Dak consoled her with an idea. "Why not suggest to Val that she ask Trina?" She thought that was a great idea! "Oh Dak of course, it's the perfect answer." With that she hugged him to her. Well, as close as they could!

There was no way she would be able to travel alone to the falls for the sacred white orchids. She could however help Trina weave them and feel part of the rites. This morning Valenia was coming over to visit. Cynan could hear her humming to herself before she saw her. On meeting, they affectionately kissed each other on the cheek in greeting. "Hello how are you feeling?" Val asked. "I feel like a big fat cow," she answered. They both laughed, and went inside to relax for a while. Val spoke first, "We both wanted you to be 'Matron of the Joining' but you aren't able. I've been thinking with your approval, I'd ask Trina to be 'Maiden of the Joining' what do you think?" "I think she said giggling, I was about to suggest the same thing!" "Well, this isn't the first time we've had the same idea" Val said. Then they were both laughing!

So it was settled, and when Val asked Trina if she would, she had tears in her eyes, saying "I'd be most honored." Valenia was sure to explain exactly how, and where to find the falls and over which bridge to go. They both knew that Jen would be following and watching over her. Most certainly after the accident that happened to Valenia when she went to cut the sacred orchids for Cynan and Dak's joining.

Jen had a tendency to move slow when it came to decisions. However, after he heard of Trina's intended walk to the falls as 'Maiden' he made a swift decision. The white orchids were brought back with out delay or problem.

Part 3 - The Royal Joining 
In winter's glory love was set,
amid the crystal frost-
Sacred flowers, eternal love,
Entwined their hearts embossed.

All things in their time come to pass. They don't however always go according to perfect plans. Not even in the enchanted Faery villages. Never before were the heavens more lovely than on this day of the 'Royal Wedding Rites.' There had been rain, followed by a sprinkling of snow. The sun triumphant burst through as final preparations were finished. Color streaked the sky with lavender, pink, and yellow clouds. Across this crystal palace of wonder two rainbows in full spectrum of color arched over the meadow.

For well over a week there had been celebrations of all types in this winter festival. It was now time for the Kings trumpets to sound. There was an honor guard of soldiers representing his Royal Highness. Other regiments remained behind to guard the main palace. The Royal couple would arrive there tomorrow morning to spend there special days alone together in wedded bliss. The palace maids had been cleaning, and fussing over their rooms for days. They would have satin sheets embroidered with their initials. Fresh rose petals would be sprinkled over their bed linens shortly before they entered their rooms. Huge bowls of fresh fruit were being readied, and all variety of beverages and food.

Everything was in readiness, King Augus and Queen Nera were seated in splendor on the portico. Valenia's family were invited to join them, those member's not taking part in the rites. Delina, Zola, Dak, and Bouck sat next to them. Beautiful floral ropes were draped all around the meadow and along a satin walkway.

Fanfare of trumpets blared through the air. The King shouts, "Let the wedding begin!" All the village girls under six years in matching pink velvet go first tossing rose petals. They are followed by Jen 'taking Valenia's father's place, dressed in blue velvet as were the men in both families. Valenia wore an exquisite white velvet gown. Following them are Cynan and Trina with the circle of love between them also dressed in pink velvet. As Valenia nears Nyklus, their eyes meet in their own thoughts. He reaches for her hand, and the crowd roars and cheers loudly in approval. Together they walk alone toward an altar of trimmed juniper loaded with cut flowers. On their right and left are lit candles, rows and rows of them all glowing in the crystal light of noon. They turn to look into each other's eyes, still holding hands in Fae custom. Then Trina comes forward to wrap them in the traditional circle of sacred orchids tying them together. Jen smiled to see her so lovely fulfilling her role as 'Maiden of the Joining.' Valenia speaks first in her soft lilting voice. "To you Nyklus I give my heart to hold forever more. I promise to adore, and love you with my whole being. You honor me, and I honor you for as long as the stars shine will my love be. I pray that I may bring to you many children all blessed with the light of creation. I hope they will be beautiful as you are my Nyklus."

Then it is Nyklus turn to speak to Valenia. "My dearest love Valenia, thank you for being my mate and for the most precious gift of all, your love. I accept the sacred trust of your heart and will treasure it gently and tenderly till the sun and moon cease to be. Indeed it is you who honor me with your life. You have agreed to share this gift with me. I hope never to cause you a moments care. May our children, and their children some day out number the grains of sand near the great sea. Light grant us growing old together in love, as on this day." This ended their vows! Within the traditional moment of silence as the couple kissed, a scream comes from Cynan's throat. Dak ran from the portico in swift steps. He took her in his arms as she said, "I am sorry my child comes!" The King rose and said, "hold your cheers please." Silence remained until Cynan was safe in their home along with grandmother Zola to help with the birthing.

Joining hands Queen Nera and King Augus raise them high saying, "Our children are of one flesh now. Here after they will be known through out the land as Their Royal Highness' Prince Nyklus, and Princess Valenia!" Cheers rose once again. After the noise diminished, He said, "May the feasting begin." What a day it is, with fun had by all. Nyklus and Valenia were allowed to come and go, to be alone to share this over whelming love and excitement. In their small home, Cynan looked up at Dak, and Zola with tears and pain of birth saying, "I'm so sorry, I ruined the joining." "Little mother to be Zola said, you did nothing of the sort." Listen, the celebrations continue. Dak brought all the prepared clean bedding and supplies they'd set aside for this moment. Zola said, "here Cynan take this hold it under your tongue to ease your pain my daughter." When the next pain comes push with it to help your wee one. In three more pushes there was a new little boy! "Good girl" soothed Zola as she wiped Cynan clean with soap, and warm water, drying her. Dak held his baby boy and cleaned him off as Delina came through the door. "Oh how beautiful he is she cooed at the proud new parents. A knock on the door sounded. Dak said, "Yes" and Valenia begged to come in. It was Cynan who weakly said, "Come." Val took one look before her tear filled eyes over flowed down her cheeks in happiness. I'm an aunt! What will you name him she asked? Well, Dak said, "we decided on Taylif after our father if given a boy." Perfect they all said in unison!

And so it was on the day of the Royal Wedding they had a new member join the family of Fae. Many traditional ceremonies would be ahead soon as Cynan felt stronger. Nyklus and Val left the festivities to be alone for the night. After their morning meal, with their families they left for the palace. Jen made an announcement with Trina blushing at his side. He cleared his throat loudly, and said "come summer there will be another joining." Trina says she'll be my mate! Congratulations were shared with all.


Part 4 - Sinister Evil and Hope 
A demon's hunting foray,
Till evil eyes see prey -
Misfortune lead astray,
May mercy light their way.

With the winter festival pronounced closed for another year, life began to return to normal. Of course that didn't include Valenia, and Nyklus still at the castle, nor Cynan and Dak. They were still walking on clouds. Wee Taylif was the most perfect youngling ever born‚Ä¶they both agreed on that. It was about this time that some very strange things began to occur. For one thing there were claw scratching sounds, rustling, stirring in the underbrush and leaves at night. The dogs whimpered, and other pets cowered in their beds instead of barking or defending their domain. Sounds which even grandmother Zola and Delina could not discern from any they'd ever heard before. The Fae kept their doors, and windows closed tight not just from the cold, [for winter still seasoned the air] but fear was the feeling most often felt! This unknown drove the men crazy. Zola cast a spell of safety, but this evil was stronger than her spell. Sounds continued, blood curdling screams heard only in the night.

Jen worried about Trina, and her parents living out so far. He asked them to move into their home till they could end what ever manner of evil this was. They agreed, mainly for Trina's sake not fearing much for themselves. That night they heard an animal cry out, then silence. Dak would not leave Cynan, and Taylif unprotected so he didn't accompany the others. Jen, Bouck, and several other men armed with sheathed swords of magic went out to scout the glen that night. Holding lanterns they found nothing but bloody remains. Whatever this was, or they were, the victims were torn to shreds. A meeting was held, patrols set up and word sent to the palace for help by carrier pigeon. The return message said a group of men were on their way accompanied by the ancient Fae wizard Braegon to help solve this danger. No one knew how old he was, but all knew his magic was strong.

The village council ruled no one was allowed to go anywhere alone. The women ad children were always guarded by men. Wood cutting ceased for now, all stayed near the village.

That very night the patrol came back with tales of huge lizard like creatures with dark gray skin, and long saber like teeth. They were seen flying in over the meadow, their wings bat like webbed in between. This fanned everyone's fear all the more. Apparently they were nocturnal, but the Fae were careful at all times. Jen knew if all was well the men should arrive from the palace in the morning.

And glory to be for sure, about an hour after day break horses were heard, they were almost there. Jen and the others hoped they could capture one of these monsters so they'd know what sort of evil this was. The lantern light they held up seemed to reflect off shiny scales.

The men and Braegon were set up to stay at the castle on the knoll. When the men were called in by him, he asked them to sit down and listen. "I have brought strong magic, we will stop this evil." After they explained all they knew Braegon spoke again. "This evil is caused by the others, the people who fly in metal machines, and send their younglings off to war. They have sprayed poison on the forest and it has drifted over onto us. This poison cause's a mutation of genes changing normal creatures into vicious killing machines. I know too from what species they come, they were ordinary harmless lizards. However with genetic mutation of their genes they grow into these flying dragons who crave blood and flesh to eat. Tonight I will take you Jen, and Zola with me, do not be afraid. This evil will end soon. Jen asked Zola to carry the lantern, he wore his sword, with Braegon leading the way. He said, "Do as I ask." At midnight they moved to the center of the meadow. He asked Zola to hold the lantern high as possible, from the pouch around his neck he removed the potion he'd mixed. Braegon said, "Listen, they come, be ready." Draw your sword Jen, when I say now raise it up high in a circle. Silence, moments stretched into long minutes.

"NOW" he said. As Jen's sword circled light zinged from it like stars bright as day. Braegon threw the potion up and it spread wide in the light. He yelled in the old Fae tongue "GLY-GETHID" meaning 'death to evil'! The light and potion spread till it was seen all over the glen above the tree tops. The next day the children asked where the monsters were, they wanted to see one. Braegon laughed and said, "I'm sorry younglings but they are gone now and will never be seen again. We hope the 'others' will not spray poison again over some other creature and change it into something evil.

Later in their home, they could scarcely believe it was over. Zola said, "This will be a story for you to tell your children, and grandchildren about Jen." In fact I'd guess it will be a story all will tell in future legends. The weeks passed and winter came to a close with the opening of buds on the Fae fruit trees. The orchards looked like huge floral gardens in pink and white all delicate and pretty. Soon the ceremony for little Taylif would begin. Now that danger was past, the royal couple would come. Nyklus would not take a chance with her though he wanted to come with Braegon and the others.

Old Chadman the record keeper was talking to Zola only this morning about the delay of birth rites. He was none to happy about it either, though he knew they must wait till danger passed. He kept records of all births in the 'Book of Faery Life.'

The day was warm on this ceremonial day of spring. Valenia and Nyklus arrived yesterday, and were settled into the castle. Delina and the other ladies had worked preparing all types of food for afterward. The village all followed little Taylif's family and quite a procession it was. Cynan carried him in her arms, as they walked to the Mist river. He was four months old now, they formed the circle around Taylif, Cynan, Dak, and Zola. Cynan removed his clothes as Zola held out the special aromatic herbs. She rubbed them over his cute little body, he laughed up onto her face. Zola thought to herself, how blessed I am this is my first great grandchild. My little Taylif who looks so like his father. Holding him high she said these words: 

"Those whose eyes see love
Are blessed beyond compare-
Fulfillment for your life,
Your heart we prepare."

With that Taylif was dipped in the water three times and then before he shivered Zola gave him to Cynan who wrapped him in his drying cloth. Old Chadman smiled as he wrote Taylif into the 'Faery Book of Life.' A cheer rose into the air from the village voices. They sang songs of old as they all walked back up the hill to enjoy the feast. Aunt Valenia held Taylif close walking next to Uncle Nyklus who was smiling with thoughts of his own children to come.

Joy Peace and Prosperity reigned for years and years and years. Oh, did I tell you? The Faery spirit never ever ever dies!
