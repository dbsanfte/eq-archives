 The Courage to Change
© Linda Hunt

So many people think they haven't got what it takes to operate a home based business… I was one of those people but my drive to be my own boss, set my own hours and not have to worry about my children all day long was enough to set me on my path to financial security. The other great advantages of course is more time with my family and the best thing of all, personal satisfaction and achievement.

It would be foolish of me to tell you that it was a piece of cake because it wasn't and still isn't. It takes a lot of hard work, determination, patience, revisiting and revising your goals, and the guts to hang in there. The courage to ignore people when they tell you you're crazy and it's not going to work can be difficult, but they don't have to pay you're bills right!

The truth is they probably envy you. In my experience most people fear success because with success comes change and that can be too much for some people. So before you totally ignore them ask them want they want out of life, I'll guarantee most people will say - more money, more time with their family and so on. Sound familiar, one thing you can do to help them out is invite them along to see what you're doing and how it can help them.

That's one of the most satisfying and rewarding aspects of this business I find is helping others achieve their dreams. I used to worry so much what other people thought about what I was doing, and soon realised it was getting me nowhere. I was still stuck in the same space, still feeling frustrated, I soon realised I had to stop worrying (as quite frankly, it is really none of my business what other people think of me) and focus on what I wanted and go for it. That's exactly what I'm doing and I love it!

I read a brilliant saying once: Choose a job you love and you'll never have to work a day in your life.

Right now I'm sitting here in the comfort of my home while my daughter is asleep, I've made myself a nice pot of coffee and I'm writing this. When I worked for a boss I very rarely got time to sit down and have a coffee, and certainly never got the opportunity to be creative. This brings me to another important point- this whole business thing has given me the chance to explore areas of myself I didn't even know existed for example, being creative - writing copy and designing web pages, developing my own system to work within and so on. It's so very exciting and rewarding.

Fortunately for me I'm the sort of person that welcomes change, I hold on to this thought… I don't want to go through the rest of my life wondering what it would be like to have more time, more money and a feeling of total satisfaction with what I'm doing'. In addition to that a feeling of contribution. I want to be living it every day of my life.

You can do it too, you just need belief, belief in yourself, to be clear about your dreams and goals and courage…the courage to change.
