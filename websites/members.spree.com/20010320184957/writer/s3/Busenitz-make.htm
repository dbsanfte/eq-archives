 MAKE PEOPLE LIKE YOU
© Arlen Busenitz

Discover The Hidden KEY to making Friends and Building relationships!

Would you like to make friends quickly? Do you desire strong relationships? Do you wish that people would instantly like you? In this article you will discover the hidden key that will allow you to make friends quickly and create strong relationships.

What is this key that will allow you to build business contacts, make friends, and make people want to be around you? The key is this: "MAKE OTHERS FEEL SIGNIFICANT." That's it! So simple but yet so powerful. If you make the other person feel important, he/she will love you and want to build a relationship with you. How do you apply this secret? Below are many ways in which you can apply this simple principle and reap the benefits many times over.

When you meet someone, greet them on the phone, or pass them on the street, act ENTHUSED to meet them! Give them a "winning" smile. Cheerfully greet them with "hello (name)". Let your voice show that you are glad to see them. What if you DON'T feel like it? Who cares! Act enthusiastic to meet them and you will BE enthusiastic to meet them, and they will feel important. They will be glad to see YOU.

You have probably realized that puppies make friends very easily. When they greet a person they smile, wag their tail and are absolutely happy to see you and want to be your friend. The result is that most people love puppies.

Publicly Praise your friend. This is simple. Say to a coworker or to Fred when he is in company "Fred is (you are) doing a really good job with meeting his deadlines. This will make Fred feel like the "king of the hill".

One of the best ways to make a person feel important is to praise him. I am not talking about flattery. When you are walking down the street and see a person washing windows, say "you are doing a good job". If a friend or coworker does something good TELL IT TO HIM/HER.

Show appreciation. When someone does something for you, show sincere appreciation. Say "thanks for the drink of water", "thanks for holding the door open, it is very kind of you." The person will walk off with his head in the clouds and so will you. One of the keys for a successful marriage is showing appreciation for the little things that the one spouse does for the other. Whether it is cooking a good meal, being home on time, or doing a good job cleaning, show appreciation.

Acknowledge the other person. If you are walking down the street, or are in an elevator, look the person in the eye for a second and say hello. Make small talk.

Let people know you care. Send a person a card when he is sick. Send an online greeting card. Ask a person how he/she is doing.

Do something special for the person. Call them. Take them out to eat. www.WorldsBestFriend.com has come up with a unique, special and free way to make a person feel special and to assure they remember YOU.

Maintain eye contact when talking and listening. This shows you care about the other person and it makes him/her feel important.

This last one is the most important to making friends, and building relationships. If you apply this, people will love you. Get the other person to talk about himself or his interests and then LISTEN. Show interest in what the other person is saying, listen and ask questions to keep him/her talking. He/she will love you.
