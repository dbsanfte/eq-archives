Sylvia Leigh

Sylvia Leigh has always expressed a deep love for poetry and an even deeper desire for writing it. She has written poetry for the past several years, but only recently attempted publication...and published she was in the November 1998 issue of The Literary Journal's Special Romance Issue! She is a teacher by vocation and has studied Music extensively. She shares her home near the ocean with a loving family, two dobermans, and her pet finches. Sylvia devotes most of her time to her children and is an avid-supporter of home-schooling for quality education.

D. Alexander Levy

I started writing about five years ago. This was in a period in which I was ready to graduate from school, and I had to wait for a few mandatory classes to be available for me to enroll. The concept was simple. I take dance, piano, singing, and poetry. These are such things I should learn so I can be a well-rounded person. I could also have my choice in women; they dig a good dancer and a sensitive poet, right? As with many things in life, things never go as planned. I learned the stupidity of that logic and the value of these classes for myself. And ever since, I have found that reading and writing poetry has made me a better person, a modern day Renaissance man. I can always get the girls later, right? Visit Alex at his website Gefilte's Shop of Poetry and Prose

Duane Locke

[Duane Locke, Ph. D. in Renaissance literature, lives alone and isolated in an old, two-story, decaying inherited house, in the sunny Tampa slums The egregious ugliness of the neighborhood has recently been mitigated by the esthetic activities of the police department. The police have pasted bright yellow and orange posters on each post. The posters advertise that the location is a shopping mall for drugs and proclaim "no loitering," Thus everybody in the neighborhood jogs while carrying cell phones. Duane lives as a stranger and alien, not comprehending the customs, the costumes, or the language, some form of postmodern English. He has had over 2,000 poems published in over 500 print magazines such as APR, Nation, Literary Review, Black Moon, Bitter Oleander, but is not certain if he has any readers since people rarely read poems in print magazines. His 14th and latest book of poems is WATCHING WISTERIA, and can be ordered, see www.vidapublishing.com or call Small Press Distributors, 1-800-869-7553.]

Lloyd Michael Lohr

I write poetry with the viewpoint of the ancient world in both my heart and mind. My chosen mindset is that of the Goddess-based cultures of the Bronze Age to the Iron Age world of the Indo-European Celts. My poetry is intended for all those who look to Mother Earth and Sister Sky for inspiration regardless of their spiritual bent. As far as my fiction writing goes, it is everywhere. From humorous science fiction and horror to serious action/adventure and fantasy. I have just started to dabble in mystery fiction. There are too many muses to ignore. Finding the time to appease them all can be an arduous task. I am mostly Celt by blood and by creed. My family is of both Scottish/Pictish and Breton stock. I am supposed to have this crazy uncle who was born on the Isle of Man and had his ear torn off in a pub fight in London, but I have yet to verify this. My family also has a smidgen of Welsh and a touch of German in us as well. To all my Welsh soulmates out there, "Ysdryd Tracwyddol y Keltiad Yddraic Coch Ddyrg Cychwyn!" I am a member of the Academy of American Poets, the Science Fiction Poetry Association, the British Science Fiction Association, the National Writers Association, the Horror Writers Association and the Science Fiction Club Deutschland. I have had 84 short stories and poems published in 17 countries and translated into 12 languages. Publication credits include: Vermont Living Magazine, The Zone (United Kingdom), Antipodean Science Fiction (Australia), Planet Magazine, Solar X Science Fiction Magazine (Germany), Fahrenheit Magazine (Poland), AlthernaTerra SF (Romania), Via Galactica (Croatia), Nocturnal Ecstasy Vampire Coven Journal, The Harrow, Monochrom SF Magazine (Austria), Amberzine/Amber Stories (Czech Republic/Slovakia), FrightNet, Mobius; The Journal of Social Change, The Mentor (Australia), Ygdrasil, A Journal of Poetic Arts (Canada), imelod (Canada), Ghastly Magazine, Illya's Honey Quarterly Journal, The Nocturnal Lyric; Journal of the Bizarre, and The Phoenix; International Journal of Heart and Spirit (Canary Islands/Spain). I hold B.A. and M.S. degrees and I am a graduate of the London School of Journalism. I am currently attempting to earn my Ph.D.
