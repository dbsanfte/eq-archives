Tips For Self Copy-Editing

Leslie Blanchard
Copyright Leslie Blanchard 1996


The fastest way to follow the suggestions shown is with your word processor. Use your “find” feature and enter the following things.

Do a search for “the”, have you misspelled it by typing in “their” or “there” ? Did you mean to enter “There” and only entered “the”? How many times have you used “the” in a single sentence?

Check for adverbs that end in “ly” by typing in “ly” on your search feature. Adverbs that end in ly are more likely to be “telling” words as opposed to “showing” words. Instead of saying “Joe angrily threw his hat to the ground” try being more descriptive. “Joe’s face was beet red, the veins at his temples and along his neck were bulging, as he grasped the hat in his fist and hurled it to the ground”, paints a better picture doesn’t it?

Search for opening quotation marks. What verbs have you used in place of the word “said”? You should replace as many of these as possible. Don’t fall to using verbs such as “mused”, “quipped”, “cried” “yelled”, “laughed”. etc. Remember to show instead of tell. Keep it simple and use “said” as much as possible.

Do a search for double periods.

Do a search for parentheses. These are often over used in places where a comma would work.

Look for the word “that”. Try removing it from any sentence in which you find it and see if the sentence doesn’t work just as well, or better.

Try not to use connectives such as : but, so, and, because, then, however. Many times where these appear you can use a period and end the sentence instead. Then capitalize the next word - making two good sentences out of one clumsy one.

Search for I. This is often overused and used in the wrong place as well. Should it be me or I? Be sure to double check this.

Run a search on all the different pronouns. She, her, him, he, his, theirs, etc. Pronouns can serve to confuse a reader - be sure to use names or otherwise clarify as much as possible.

Search for your lead character’s name. How often do you see it? It should be mentioned every 2-3 paragraphs.


Try these tips, and your copy-editor will have far less red ink on your manuscript !
