 A Conversation between Joshua, the Anointed, and Judas, the Assassin From Keroith, on the Occasion of the Miracle of the Feeding of the Multitude.
© Brian E Turner

Joshua is sitting alone on top of a hill looking down on the scene below. Five thousand men like flowers in the long grass beside the Lake of Galilee. Judas climbs up and sits at his feet.

-How is it going down there, Judas.

-They've all been fed, Rabbi. There's even twelve baskets-full of leftovers.

-Where would they get baskets from then?

-If we had baskets there'd be enough to fill twelve.

-And there's twelve tribes in Israel.

-Rocky's been telling everyone it's a miracle.

-Well isn't it?

-I saw how it was done, Rabbi.

-Oh, did you now?

-Yes, Rabbi. I know you do miracles but this wasn't one of them. You gave half a loaf to one man. He passed it on to his neighbour, then he took a loaf from his own bag and broke it in two and shared that. It was a loaf he had brought. Pretty soon everyone was sharing out the food they had brought with them. You knew that would happen?

-Of course my friend.

-Then how is it a miracle?

-If a man comes away on a day trip at short notice he is bound to quickly place some prepared food in a knapsack, perhaps more than he needs. But then a few, of course, will forget. So if a man sits amongst five neighbours thinking that he had to share with all of them he believes that he will go short. But I know that there is only one man there who is without food and if they share all will have plenty.

-Yes, I believe you are very astute, Rabbi. But it is not a miracle.

-To have all these men together, sharing everything with their brothers, in peace and friendship, so that there is an abundance, what is that then?

-Of course. A miracle, Rabbi.
