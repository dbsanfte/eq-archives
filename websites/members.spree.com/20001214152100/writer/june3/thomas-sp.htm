 SPIRITUAL MARKETING: THE ART OF MAGICAL OPPORTUNITIES

© Mary Anne Thomas

Throw out all the "must-do's" on your marketing list! This issue of Mindzine is going to show you a quick and easy way to bring magical, new opportunities right to your door.

First, let's make a list of all the things about marketing that we don't like . . . things that are merely "jobs to do" and aren't any fun. Feel free to add your own comments to the list I'm compiling from my own experiences and the experiences of my students.

DON'T LIKE:

 1) The uncertainty of wondering whether a marketing idea will work
2) Getting my hopes up about a new marketing plan and being
disappointed 3) Never having enough clients or sales
4) Feeling rejected by the marketplace
5) Spending money on marketing that doesn't work
6) Knowing that I have to market, but having no enthusiasm for the task

Now, what would we like to have instead?

DO LIKE:

1) Marketing that brings me monetary rewards
2) Marketing that's easy to do
3) Marketing that excites me and makes me eager to do it
4) New clients/students/customers to love
5) Clients/students/customers who approve of what I'm doing
6) Acclaim for my talents, abilities and gifts
7) Support for my new ideas
8) Magical, wonderful opportunities that arrive on their own

Now we're ready to create!

First, let's find words that describe our new desires. Some of them appeared in our "do like" list, and so we'll start there:


Rewards
Easy
Exciting
Eagerness
Love
Approval
Acclaim
Support
Magical
Wonderful
Opportunities

Next, let's add to our list by looking up synonyms for the words we've already identified.

Here are synonyms for the word "rewards." Do they appeal to you?

Prize
Bounty
Appreciation

How about these synonyms for the word "easy"?

Effortlessly
Freely
Smoothly

Let's add both groups to our list!

Finally, we're ready to make the concepts behind these words part of our everyday life with the Dictionary Game*. With the help of the Dictionary Game, our minds are able to focus on words long enough to get the creative job done. To play the Dictionary Game now, just read along with me:

Reward: something given in return for good; something offered for a service

Easy: causing or involving little difficulty or discomfort; requiring little effort, thought or reflection; not severe; not difficult to endure or undergo; readily available; pleasant; marked by peace and comfort; not hurried or strenuous; free from pain, annoyance or anxiety; not burdensome; fitting comfortably; allowing freedom of movement; attained naturally

Excite: to rouse to an emotional response; to stimulate; to arouse the activity of

Eager: marked by enthusiastic desire or interest

Love: to hold dear; to cherish; to like or desire actively; to feel affection for

Approve: to have or express a favorable opinion of; to accept as satisfactory; to give formal or official sanction to

Acclaim: to shout praise or applause

Support: to promote the interests or cause of; to pay the cost of; to maintain; to keep going

Magic: having seemingly supernatural qualities or powers; giving a feeling of enchantment

Wonder: a cause of astonishment or admiration; marvel; miracle; the quality of amazed admiration

Opportunity: a favorable juncture of circumstances; a good chance for advancement or progress

Prize: something exceptionally desirable

Bounty: something that is given generously; a reward, premium or subsidy; a grant; a payment

Appreciation: a favorable critical estimate; an expression of admiration, approval, or gratitude; an increase in value

Effortless: showing or requiring little or no effort

Freely: without hindrance; with freedom from external control

Smooth: free from difficulties or impediments

Do you feel excited? Optimistic? Relieved? That's your mind at work . . . with the help of the Dictionary Game . . . bringing magical opportunities to you without the use of physical effort.
