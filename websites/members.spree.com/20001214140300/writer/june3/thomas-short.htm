 A SHORT-CUT TO PROSPERITY

© Mary Anne Thomas

A desire for money, more than any other desire, creates spiritual growth. Why? Because lack of money makes us address the lack of love in our world . . . and makes us focus on our primary purpose for this lifetime . . . which is to bring love into a world that is starving for it. It makes no difference which angle of lack you choose to address in order to fulfill your purpose. All angles of lack lead to spiritual growth. Whether you're reacting to a lack of money, a lack of friends, a lack of praise, a lack of clients, or a lack of direction in your life . . . you are attempting to correct the physical and spiritual voids in our world.

Yes, lack is a spiritual guide for this era, perhaps our most effective and brilliant one. It forces us to fight back . . . and end the grip of control that lack has on our world.

Let's fight back by creating prosperity today!

With this issue of Mindzine and its use of the Dictionary Game* . . . we're going to focus on prosperity long enough for it to be created in your life. Play the Dictionary Game by reading along with me right now:

Prosperity: the condition of being successful or thriving; economic well-being

Success: favorable or desired outcome; the attainment of wealth, favor, or eminence

Wealth: abundance of valuable material possessions or resources

Resources: a natural source of wealth or revenue; a possibility of relief or recovery

Fortunate: receiving some unexpected good

Abundant: marked by great plenty; amply supplied

Thrive: to grow vigorously; to flourish; to gain in wealth or possessions; to prosper; to progress toward or realize a goal

Sufficient: enough to meet the needs of a situation or a proposed end; qualified; competent

Surplus: the amount that remains when use or need is satisfied; the excess of receipts over disbursements

Plenty: plentiful in amount, number, or supply

Increase: to make greater; to augment; to multiply

Gain: resources or advantage acquired or increased; profit

Have: to hold or maintain as a possession; to hold for one's use or at one's disposal; to acquire or get possession of; to receive; to accept; to experience; to entertain in the mind; to allow; to partake of

Enjoy: to have for one's use, benefit, or lot; to take pleasure or satisfaction in

Do you feel a difference? Uplifted? More certain of prosperity? That's your Dictionary Game at work . . . bringing the concepts behind your words to life.

Don't let lack defeat you! Let lack be your teacher . . . rather than your jailer. Let it lead you to the conditions your soul is trying to create . . . and bring those conditions into being with your Dictionary Game.
