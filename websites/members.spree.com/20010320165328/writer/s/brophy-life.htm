 My Life Altering Experience - A First-Person Story
© Larry Brophy

My life has recently gone through a dramatic change. I've been an entrepreneur and businessperson most of my adult life and thought I had my life priorities pretty well in hand. I was - and still am - deeply involved with an Internet e-commerce business based upon the work I'd been doing for the past seven or eight years.

By fluke circumstance I met a man by the name of Mitch Axelrod who introduced me to a science - axiology - and tool - the Hartman Value Profile - that I'd never heard of. And, because I've been interested in personal development and human potential for many years, I decided to take the Value Profile myself.

When I took the Value Profile I expected to learn a few things about myself. What I got totally knocked me out. It was infinitely more than I'd expected.

I discovered that although I was crystal clear about many things, I wasn't living my life in the manner that I REALLY wanted. What I read and learned about myself struck a chord within me that I just couldn't ignore. When I saw what I was paying attention to in my own life - actually, what I was NOT paying attention to - it was as if my eyes were fully open for the first time to what it was I should be doing with my life.

The person that introduced me to the Value Profile, Mitch Axelrod, provided a feedback session that gave me a most valuable insight into my own strengths and - dare I even say it - weaknesses. (Mitch would gently call it my "developmental areas"). I didn't even know how 'lucky' I was to have him help me through this experience.

What I didn't know is that Mitch is the 'consultants consultant'. Jay Abraham, one of today's most respected and sought after marketing consultants, says of him, "If you want to increase business, call Mitch." Neat coincidence, eh?

The benefit of the Value Profile to me is immeasurable. I learned where my true values - and strengths - lie. That knowledge has encouraged and empowered me to follow my true joy. That's in helping others through their own gateway of understanding so they can live a fuller, more productive and joy-filled life.

Harvey Schoof, a business associate of Mitch's who's been working with the Value Profile for the past 12 years, describes the purpose of the Value Profile like this. He says, "This instrument is designed to help you understand how you think and why you do what you do. Why you're getting the results you are in your life. You already know what results you're getting - you don't need a profile to tell you that.

"You need to understand WHY things are happening so you can start to understand what you can use that will help you be more effective. And, what things you need to work on or get help working on that are potential problem areas for you."

I found that the Value Profile is designed to teach you how to maximize your strengths to get more out of life emotionally, psychologically, spiritually, physically, personally and financially.

For me - and, as I've learned, for many others that have had this experience - knowing your own 'values' is the gateway of opportunity for living a memorable life. It's the bridge from where I was to where I want to be.
