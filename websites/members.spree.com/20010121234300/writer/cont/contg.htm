Marty Gallanter

Marty Gallanter’s writing “compelling.” His articles have graced the pages of the New York Times, the Chicago Tribune, the Baltimore Sun, Parade and TV Guide. He’s appeared on Saturday Night with Connie Chung. And he’s written for more than one hundred Jewish-American publications.
Gallanter regularly appears on radio and television, and often lectures at universities throughout the country. He also writes a syndicated newspaper column about the Internet.
Gallanter has been a publicist, writer and fundraiser for more than twenty years. He is currently the Director at the Dakota State University Foundation in Madison, SD. He previously worked the Director of Fundraising for the Legal Aid Society of New York and for the United Jewish Appeal. His web site is at http://www.themadhatter.net

Phil Gatton

ASSOCIATE POETRY EDITOR A Writer's Choice Literary Journal Phil is an electrical engineer, musician, songwriter, and poet. He is currently the president of the Board of Directors of the Louisville Poets' Guild and is co-editing the group's LPG Anthology '99 with Ryk Whyte. His poetry works have recently appeared in the Louisville Eccentric Observer, the Prism quarterly, the Poet's Cut, The Miserere Review, and A Writer's Choice, and was recently selected for inclusion in Moonshade Magazine. He has written and performed with a variety of bands and musical collaborations, including Alex Kravitz (Chicago, IL), No Comment, The Primates, Red, Buckethead (Austin, TX), Millions of Illegitimate Children of Elvis (Cincinnati, OH), Allah Don't Surf, XOX, Muffy Panics, Bucket, 24, and The Harlots, as well as studio projects "Something Wrongfully Right" in 1997 with Muffy Junes and "Taxi" in 1998 with Tom Cantrell, and the LPG-TV theme music. He has composed songs and poetry since age 4. He hopes to have a combination music/spoken word compact disc ready for release in fall 2000.

Dave Gitomer

born on 4/14/54, I prefer to be called Dave. I have been writing for 3 years on the Internet. though I do have BA in economics, I have taken to writing poetry. I took it upon myself to teach myself the craft and work hard at it. of late I have had some success in being published. I did copyright my first volume of poetry "THE MOON AT NOON, REFLECTIONS ON AN ICY POND". I have done a small self published chapbook with graphics called "SECRETS OF THE ORIENT OR COPING THROUGH LIFE VIA POETRY". I do performance poetry and have been on Internet radio two times on Galinski's GO POETRY! channel, I have read and been featured in Borders Book Stores on Long Island NY under the auspices of the Long Island Performance Poets Association. I did contribute to a CD-ROM on how to write poetry published by Kurweil's Technology. I have been published in chapbook anthologies like Memorandum, PIF, Stand Alone, Silver Blue Dementia, Writers Brew, and Chainsaw Orgasms. I am on many web sites including the Guggenheim Museum's Reverie, Brooklyn Poets, and many more. I often post to assorted newsgroups like rec.arts.poems, alt.poetry.comments, and assorted Zen/Tao groups. I enjoy showcasing my works on America Online chat forums, and have been featured on Poetic Spotlight. as far as my interests, I like to read books on philosophy, especially those with an Eastern slant and enjoy The Best American Poetry anthologies, (published by Simon & Schuster). I have done live poetry contests (slams) on line (where I usually participate as a judge) and once did the NEWRICAN CAFE on the lower east side of Manhattan

Jennifer Gordon Gray

is a writer who has worked as a writer, editor and photographer in the newspaper industry since 1983. She is also a correspondent for several publications serving members and friends of Soka Gakkai International, both in the United States and the United Kingdom.

She has been published in numerous literary magazines and has won several publishing and literary awards.

Jenny is also a horse-driving and dressage enthusiast, and owner of a homebred Oldenberg filly, Caledonia. She was a staff worker for the 1996 Olympic equestrian games in Atlanta, working on the fields of play for both the dressage and three-day-event competitions.

Most of these essays were written in a six-month odyssey at Howeford Cottage in the north of Scotland. In moments of occasional sanity, Jenny can be found hiking anything with a slope, and hugging her cat, Bear.

She has a new book out Thru Bodhisattva's Eyes a personal journey in Scotland. C. 1998 TartanPony Publishing.

Yes, it's Buddhist inspired, but far from preachy or esoteric. This work gets right to the core of human matters, and offers hope, not indoctrination. And because it reflects life, it "ain't all pretty," still the sick heal, the hero lives, and the reader leaves each page feeling like life isn't an impossible dream.
