 Just One More Step
© Michael Pollock

The mountain stood ominously, towering above her. In her head, the thoughts of self-doubt ran unchecked. "Who do you think you are? You're not a mountain climber. You're not even an athlete. You can't do this. Go back home and start cooking dinner for John and the kids." This was to be Anne's maiden climb to the top of Mount Destiny. Actually, it was her first real mountain climbing experience altogether.

Anne was many things, but not a mountain climber. A wife and mother of two beautiful little girls, she spent most of her days climbing up and down the stairs of their home, cooking dinners and scaling the mountains of laundry that only a growing family of four can produce. Challenging yes, but seeming mole hills as she gazed up toward the jagged cliffs and snow capped peak that appeared to touch the heavens. "I can't do this," she thought to herself.

The idea of climbing Mount Destiny sounded very exciting four months ago. Anne had been looking for something to break the monotony of the daily chores that came with being a domestic engineer. She loved her family and the life they had built together, but she yearned for something more. She craved a challenge that would test her strength, not only as a woman, mother and wife, but as a human being. Mount Destiny was just the challenge, or so it seemed a few short months ago. Today though, even with all her preparation and training, the idea of climbing a mountain was more than Anne could grasp.

"Okay, is everybody ready," shouted Denise, the expedition leader. Anne slowy walked toward Denise to explain that she just wasn't ready to climb the mountain today. What would she say, she wondered. How could she explain that this was a little more than she was ready for? Then she stopped. Her mind suddenly flashed back to last year when her youngest daughter Lisa was just learning to walk. "Come on honey, you can do it, just one more step," she remembered saying to Lisa.

The words echoed again and again in her consciousness. "Just one more step, you can do it, just one more step." Anne looked down at her feet. Then she looked at a spot on the ground that lay two feet in front of her. She stepped toward that spot. Then she took another step and another and the next. Suddenly her whole perspective changed. "I don't have to climb a mountain today," she thought. "I just need to take one more step."

Anne no longer looked up at the huge mountain. She focused only two feet in front of her. Step by step, up Mount Destiny she ascended. She never once looked up at the peak. She knew it was there and she knew she was headed in that direction, but she only focused on the next step.

Several hours later, Anne stood at the peak of Mount Destiny and gazed out over the land below. Tears filled her eyes. An overwhelming feeling of joy and accomplishment swept her entire body. She had done it. She had conquered not only the mountain but also the fear that just a few hours before had nearly sent her home in defeat. Step by step, Destiny was hers.

Are you like Anne? Do you gaze up at Destiny, hoping to conquer it. Do you yearn for a greater challenge - one that will test your strength as a human being? Perhaps you want to make a job change or start your own business. Maybe you'd like to climb a mountain, further your education, begin an exercise program or work on improving a relationship. But - like Anne - do you tell yourself "I can't climb this mountain, go home."

Spend this week thinking only about the next step in your climb. Shift your focus from the entire mountain to the spot two feet in front of you. You don't have to climb the mountain today. Just take one more step. Maybe that step is polishing your resume' or visiting the library to research the business you want to begin. You could visit the community college in your area to see what classes might interest you. Instead of trying to run 3 miles for the first time, take a brisk ten minute walk around the neighborhood. Just take one more step.

We all have plenty of mountains to climb and sometimes it all seems a little more than we're capable of doing. But step by step, Destiny can be conquered. Just one more step.

It's your life. Create it the way you want it!
