 Why Does Life Begin at 40?
© Tony Brown

We've all heard the expression, and, when that day looms up, we start to use it ourselves and accept it as a Truth. However, nobody has ever fully explained why it is true.

It intrigued me for years until I uncovered the answer in a most unexpected place. It lay hidden between the pages of Napoleon Hill's masterpiece, "Think & Grow Rich" ! There under the chapter entitled "The Secrets Of Sex Transmutation" I found this remarkable quotation :-

"I discovered, from the analysis of over 25,000 people, that men who succeed in an outstanding way, seldom do so before the age of forty, and more often they do not strike their real pace until they are well beyond the age of fifty. This fact was so astounding that it prompted me to go into the study of its cause most carefully.

This study disclosed the fact that the major reason why the majority of men who succeed do not begin to do so before the age of forty to fifty, is their tendency to dissipate their energies through over-indulgence in physical expression of the emotion of sex. The majority of men never learn that the urge of sex has other possibilities, which far transcend in importance that of mere physical expression.

The majority of those who make this discovery, do so after having wasted many years at a period when sex energy was at its height, prior to the age of forty to fifty. This is usually followed by noteworthy achievement.

The lives of many men up to, and sometimes well past the age of forty, reflect a continued dissipation of energies, which could have been more profitably turned into better channels. Their finer and more powerful emotions are sown wildly to the four winds. Out of this habit grew the term "sowing his wild oats."

The desire for sexual expression is by far the strongest and most impelling of all the human emotion because, when harnessed and transmuted into action, other than that of physical expression, it may raise one to great accomplishment."

I not only found this interesting but was impressed, not to mention greatly encouraged by the whole thing. He was talking here about the use of sexual energy as a 'mind stimulant' and listed all ten of the other mind stimulants known to man as:-

1. The desire for sex expression.
2. Love
3. A burning desire for fame, fortune, power or financial gain.
4. Music
5. Friendship between either those of the same or opposite sex
6. A Master Mind Alliance based on the harmony of two or more people who ally themselves for spiritual or temporal advancement
7. Mutual suffering, such as that experienced by people who are persecuted
8. Autosuggestion
9. Fear
10. Narcotics or alcohol

He cited many examples of men and women who have attained the status of genius as a result of using the 'artificial' mind stimulants of narcotics and alcohol. Men like James Whitcomb Riley who did his best writing whilst under the influence of alcohol.

Perhaps that's where he saw "the ordered intermingling of the real and the dream, the mill above the river, and the mist above the stream". However, such men have often destroyed themselves in the process.

Nature has prepared her own potions with which men and women may stimulate their minds as you can see from the list above. The desire for sex expression heads the list because it is the most powerful of all. No one of them, nor all of them combined, can equal the driving force of sex when properly channelled.

Napoleon Hill explains later in that chapter that :-

"Through these sources one may commune with Infinite Intelligence, or enter at will, the storehouse of the subconscious mind, either one's own, or that of another person, a procedure which is all there is of genius."

Between the ages of about thirty and forty, a man or woman begins to learn, if they ever learn at all, the art of sex transmutation, that is, the turning of sexual energy into others forms of productive effort. "Throwing yourself into your work" might be one way of expressing this.

This discovery is often made by accident and we could remain totally unconscious of it. We may simply observe that our powers of achievement have increased between these ages and never become aware of the cause.

The truth is that Nature begins to harmonise the emotions of love and sex in the individual between these ages so that they may draw upon these great forces and apply them jointly as stimuli to other productive action.

It is encouraging to learn that most people don't even enter into highly creative pursuits of any kind before the age of forty and that the average person reaches the period of their greatest capacity to create between the ages of forty and sixty !

This period must not be approached with fear and trembling, but with new hope and anticipation. We must get out there and make some new plans.

The pages of history are full of people who started to succeed after the age of forty but here are just a couple of more recent examples.

I just finished watching a TV show with highlights from "On Tour With Tina" a documentary of Tina Turner's "2000 World Tour" which she is doing with Lionel Richie as the co-star. Tina's solo career didn't even get off the ground till she was aged 40 and here she is rocking them in the aisles at age 60 and still looking good on it too !

Tom Jones made his TV debut with "It's Not Unusual" in 1969 and went on to star in Las Vegas along with big names like Elvis Presley and Frank Sinatra. Now in his late fifties, he has just launched his new album "Reload" and is busy successfully promoting it all around the world. The list just goes on and on and it's not all show-biz either.

Just look around, there are examples everywhere. Life really does begin at 40. Now, instead of wondering how or why ? we now know that this is how it was meant to be and that "Great Beginnings" and 'Great Comebacks' are not only possible but quite normal, at any age whatsoever.

I don't know about you, but I think this is really great news !
