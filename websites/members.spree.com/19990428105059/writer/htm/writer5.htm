General Tips For Dealing With Publishers
Copyright Leslie Blanchard

Usually, the editor is right. Many writers complain to no end about editors, but if their publication is doing well - then the editor must be doing something right. If they reject your writing you should accept this as a good decision, and try to figure out why the work was rejected.

Fear is your enemy. Fear of failing will almost certainly guarantee your failure - because you either will not try, or you will not give it your best effort.

Everything counts. When I get a letter or query from a prospective client that contains errors in grammar, or spelling errors-it may well change my opinion of the writer. I may even toss it in the trash. Neatness, correct formatting, following the editors guidelines, spelling, punctuation, legibility, accuracy, all of these things are important. If you want to be a professional writer you must act like one and present yourself as such.

Multiple submissions are smart business when appropriate.

Don’t ask an editor if their readers would be interested in a certain subject. At this point editors are the readers, so be direct and talk to them.
