Sean Bamman Born & raised outside of Washington, DC in Arlington, VA. Ryan Barber I believe I have always been a poet, it just took a long time for me to proclaim it to myself. I am a seeker, tying the truths of the world so that nothing can be excluded. I imagine this is a longer than lifetime pursuit. Poetry is my way of expressing life, revelation, and passion with some semblance of clarity. For now, I manage & buy for an outfitting store, own & operate a climbing shoe resoling business, & a myriad of other odd jobs to keep the loose ends tied. I live when I am in the mountains, church if you will, & pay for my stay in society by working in Albuquerque.

Don Barbera A man for all seasons, Don has been a journalist, editor, news announcer, disk jockey, photographer, photojournalist, feature writer, pants presser for Lee Jeans, delivery car driver, television personality and systems consultant. At times he has been sinner & saint, rich & poor, lost & found & always involved in what is going on. For redemption, Don teaches part-time as an English professor at a local Dallas university. Don has been a professional musician for more than 20 years. His loves are painting, drawing & writing.
He is educated. He admits to degrees in journalism, business, English & other areas but sees little value in them other than he managed to persevere to get his sheepskin (fake) -- but his mother loved it.
Don is irreverent, testy, salty, holier than thou, opinionated, soft, hard as nails, inquisitive & totally undeterred by establishment procedures. Writing: The majority of Don's work has been in the corporate designing training curriculum, marketing collaterals, corporate presentations, speeches & public speaking. However, before joining the corporate world Don wrote & edited for a small newspaper The Pittsburg Morning Sun in Pittsburg, Kansas. He later worked as a reported, editor & photographer with the Tulsa Tribune. During that time, he was also a stringer for the Wichita Eagle & the Kansas City Star. Don's other work includes numerous appearances in small art & poetry magazines and various opinion columns.

Michael Barnett considers himself to be a "defrocked" astrophysicist, having graduated with degrees in physics & astronomy. In order to live, he took a job as a software engineer & has been at it since 1979. He used to write to help himself out during times of depression. Interestingly enough, his life has apparently been good for the last fifteen years. Of course, having a family with two small children does make it slightly difficult to keep up with any literary pursuits. So perhaps some encouragement from some fans would spur his creative juices. Michael had originally published "Toll Road" in the "The Cat's Eye", a defunct ezine. He has recently published some of his science fiction endeavors in the Planet Magazine ;, which currently contains a story "That Takes The Cake". His Web site has more of his writing, including juvenile science fiction, astronomical non-fiction, & religious humor pieces

Melissa Beynon Melissa is a 23-year-old college graduate in Ohio. She has a sincere love for all genres of fiction & is rarely seen without a book in her coat pocket or purse. John Birkbeck My main literary interest is in poetry, & I'm concerned with the great range of what is being written in the present. In my own experience of reading, & in writing for other web-zines, world-wide, I've come to see that there is a lot of poetry being written in in these times. And a lot of it is simply fantastic! What is incomprehensible to me is that, although the market for poetry seems to be a hard sell, & that there seems to be a shrinking venue for poetry, in the paper world, there are at the same time, more people than ever reading and writing it. The glory of the Internet is that it's possible for publishers to provide venues for poetry at a fraction of the cost of putting out a publicaton on paper. Thus, these publications can afford to take risks on publishing the new and the unknown and the untried voices out there, that a paper journal, with rising costs of materials and overhead, are sadly, unable to do.
Darlene Bishop began writing at age 10 when she submitted a short story to Golden Books for publication. She neglected to include her return address so she doesn't know if they accepted her manuscript or not. That didn't stop her, though. As a self-educated writer from her early teens on, she devoured multitudes of books on writing principles and practiced every writing exercise in each of them. During college, she majored in business with a strong emphasis on writing. Later, her skills were used extensively in varied jobs from executive assistant to desktop publisher, and then to managing editor for a small, local newspaper. Darlene's writing background includes articles for newspapers, magazines and online publications, press releases, newsletters, website content and much more. A number of her short pieces have been published in various offline publications, including Woman's Day, Penny Press, and Dell. She typically seeks to write for business, however, rather than publication, and has formed her own successful writing and editorial services company, Copeland & Lane (http://www.copelandlane.com), as a vehicle for employing these much needed skills.
Dave Bishop
