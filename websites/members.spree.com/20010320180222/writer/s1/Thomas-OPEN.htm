 OPEN THE DOOR TO GOOD NEWS
© Mary Anne Thomas

Friday is judgment day, the day we tally our results for the week. If we've made visible progress toward our goals, Friday is a good news day. If we haven't produced results, Friday is a bad news day -- one that produces worry and anxiety until Monday arrives with another chance for success.

This past Friday looked like a bad news day for me. I had submitted my workbook, "An Adventure of the Mind," to publishers three weeks before. I had no offers and no signs of interest to show for my efforts -- and, like all writers, the more I waited the more I feared rejection.

"What can I do to produce some quick results?"

"Celebrate," was my heart's response.

"There's nothing to celebrate!" I yelled. "That's the problem!"

"But you still want to celebrate," my heart said, and it was true. I didn't want to face another long weekend of worry.

"Will the Dictionary Game* help me?" I decided to find out.

First, I compiled a list of words that mean "to celebrate." I used my synonym dictionary and thesaurus to do it, and here's the list I came up with:
Applaud
Cheer
Favor
Admire
Endorse
Commend
Praise
Esteem
Revere
Honor
Bless
Notice
Proclaim
Promote
Publish

Next, I played the Dictionary Game with my list of words. The Dictionary Game is a new mind-body-spirit tool that helps you focus on your wishes and make them manifest. Here's how it works: With enough focus, the feelings behind your wishes are activated, or animated. When feelings are animated, they're able to attract other feelings just like them until they form a mass. When a mass is formed . . . it becomes visible in our physical world. That's the goal of all mind-body-spirit work . . . to make your feelings, your wishes, and your desires visible . . . and the Dictionary Game allows you to accomplish it.

I played the Dictionary Game by looking up the definitions of the words I compiled. To play the Dictionary Game with me now, just read along with me.

Applaud: to express approval by clapping the hands

Cheer: gaiety; animation; happiness; anything that gives joy or happiness; a shout of approval, encouragement, or congratulation

Favor: something given as a token of love, affection, or remembrance; advantage; benefit; approval or support; sanction

Admire: to regard with wonder, pleasure, and approval; to have a high opinion of; to esteem or respect

Endorse: to give approval of or support to; to sanction

Commend: to represent as worthy, qualified, or desirable; to recommend; to express approval of; to praise

Praise: an expression of warm approval or admiration; strong commendation

Esteem: to think of with respect; to prize; favorable regard

Revere: to regard with awe, great respect, or devotion

Honor: nobility of mind; probity; esteem; reputation; good name; credit; glory; fame; distinction

Bless: to invoke divine favor upon; to honor as holy; to endorse or favor, as with talent

Notice: respectful attention or consideration

Proclaim: to announce officially and publicly; to praise; to extol

Promote: to contribute to the progress or growth of; to further; to advocate; to popularize by advertising or by securing financial support; to move forward; to advance

Publish: to bring to the public attention; to announce; to be the author of a published work

These were the words I needed to hear! Within minutes after playing my Dictionary Game, I felt like calling one of the editors who was reviewing my book. I got a great reception! He said, "I've read it. I like it. I haven't made a decision yet though. Can I have until Monday to give you something?" It was more encouragement than I had ever received from an editor, and my good news didn't stop there. An hour later, I walked to the post office in our little village to pick up my mail. In my box was an unexpected check for $800. Bob and I went to our favorite Chinese restaurant for lunch to celebrate our good news and we took the rest of the day off to plan a relaxing, worry-free weekend.

Go after the things you need! Some of your results will arrive right away and some will take a little longer, but with the Dictionary Game you'll find a new way to make things happen . . . a way that brings lots of good news.
