 BEYOND THE GREAT GLASS DOME
© Anar Azimov

CHARACTERS:
1.Young Man
2.Young Woman

(FAR GUNS REPORTS. NEAR CRYING OF A CHILD. SUDDENLY IT STOPS.)

YOUNG MAN. But I shan't talk about children. The sky,the city, the twilight. It is large and transparent. Trees between grey and nicely-grieving houses are in a fragile chaos of the autumn lace. The sky is white-blue as if the water colour were on glass. It is light-grey in the cloudy west. This block was rather calm, nobody shot or made barricades, houses were unbroken yet. Fights went on in the western side, near the railway station.

There were two more blocks to the theatre surrounded with the trees. The green foliage indeed would completely hide its two storeys in summer. There were neither actors nor spectators there, but some tens of people took their refuge there.

(RIFLE REPORT) The sniper hid behind the opposite corner of the house. Two more blocks of open space. I jumped over the law fence of the park and squatted avoiding to touch the cold stones with my back. The damned war...I saw her on TV. A crowd ran across the street. It is a surprise that TV still works here. And it works well though the operator was killed, but the tape was brought to the studio, nevertheless.

Click - and people run, click - and they stand still, click - and they back quickly! (LAUGHS NERVOUSLY.) I couldn't understand what she looked for in this damned city! It is because of her permanent ability to be where it is needed not to be! One more shot - the wall was a very good shelter, but the instinct forced me to bow. (RIFLE REPORT.) Generally many of my ancestors died of tonsillitis. Ancestors of mother, I mean. I am thinking whether my unknown hunter changes the side.

YOUNG WOMAN. What are you doing here?

YOUNG MAN. I am here for some hours,I'm all frozen. Listen, I know you seem to me, but that is because you are in this small and empty theatre, don't you think?

YOUNG WOMAN. What "don't you think?"?

YOUNG MAN. How "what "don't you think?"?"?

YOUNG WOMAN. "Don't you think?" that I am there or that the theatre is small and empty?

YOUNG MAN. Both. Don't you think so?

YOUNG WOMAN. You know the second, but I shan't say the first.

YOUNG MAN. I am going to save you.

YOUNG WOMAN. And what if I am not there?

YOUNG MAN. But what if you are?

YOUNG WOMAN. But I am not.

YOUNG MAN. No, you are. (WITH NERVOUS IRONY.) What's about weather? It is fine, isn't?

YOUNG WOMAN. Yes, it is not too bad.

YOUNG MAN. But it is cold a little.

YOUNG WOMAN. Probably, it is.

YOUNG MAN. It was warmer yesterday.

YOUNG WOMAN. So it was.

YOUNG MAN. But what about tomorrow?... Listen, I want to save you!

YOUNG WOMAN. Anyhow you don't love me.

YOUNG MAN. Why do you think so? I love you!

YOUNG WOMAN. No, you don't.

YOUNG MAN. I do.

YOUNG WOMAN. No, you don't.

YOUNG MAN. I do.

YOUNG WOMAN. You can't prove that. You can't prove that. You can't prove that.

YOUNG MAN. Should I?

(ENGINE SOUNDS OF CARS APPROACHING AND GOING AWAY.)

I didn't know which of them, drivers and passengers, would help. The sniper could shoot everyone who would be too courageous or insufficiently coward. (PAUSE.) Listen, I go to this damned theatre though you aren't there at all!

YOUNG WOMAN But you've said I was.

YOUNG MAN Would you be so kind to stop making fun of me? (RIFLE REPORT.) I didn't see him at all because I hid at once when he had shot.

YOUNG WOMAN. So you are going to save me.

YOUNG MAN. Yes, I am.

YOUNG WOMAN. But you love only my body.

YOUNG MAN. It is the great news for me that you don't like me to like your body. I don't like that you don't. You liked it before. You liked everything.

YOUNG WOMAN (NERVOUSLY.) I would like you to stop. I want you to stop.

YOUNG MAN. You can want nothing, you are my dream.

YOUNG WOMAN. Nevertheless...

YOUNG MAN. So I want you not to know.

YOUNG WOMAN. Don't you want something else?

YOUNG MAN. May be I shall be killed, don't you know?

YOUNG WOMAN. Why did you come here? Don't you like your hometown?

YOUNG MAN (IRONICALLY). My hometown...

YOUNG WOMAN. Forty five.

YOUNG MAN. Sixteen.

YOUNG WOMAN. Thirty eight.

YOUNG MAN. Twenty four.

YOUNG WOMAN. Seven.

YOUNG MAN. Twenty three.

YOUNG WOMAN. Ninety six.

YOUNG MAN. Twenty two.

YOUNG WOMAN. Eighty nine.

YOUNG MAN. Twenty one.

YOUNG WOMAN. You are winning again! (IMITATES A FLIRTING PHONE CONVERSATION.) Don't you want to say me something?

YOUNG MAN (IMITATES A FLIRTING PHONE CONVERSATION). You know what I want to say.

YOUNG WOMAN. And what?

YOUNG MAN (ACQUIRING A TASTE OF THIS NEW ROLE). Stop it! "And what?"... What "and what?"?

YOUNG WOMAN (IN HER NORMAL VOICE). It is O.K. (IMITATING AGAIN.) I am waiting.

YOUNG MAN. You'll see...

YOUNG WOMAN. What shall I see?

YOUNG MAN. I've just finished to talk.

YOUNG WOMAN. It seems to me I guess what you mean.

YOUNG MAN. You needn't guess, you know.

YOUNG WOMAN. I swear I didn't see him since the high school time. Trust me, please!

YOUNG MAN. I trust nothing but proofs.

(THE GAME IS OVER, THEY LAUGH TOGETHER.)

YOUNG MAN (SERIOUSLY). Didn't you really see him since the high school time? (PAUSE.) Why did I think her to be there? It seemed to me: some dark-grey paint is poured on the light water-coloured dome; condensed as a huge and heavy mass it suppresses the fragile glass - the dome is broken, the liquid mass falls down...

YOUNG WOMAN. It's going to rain...

YOUNG MAN. You imagination is too rich. Don't you listen, "It seemed to me".

YOUNG WOMAN. Let's not quarrel...

YOUNG MAN. Oh, Great Glass Dome! It is infinite and can't be named. It is a formless form, an image without essence.

YOUNG WOMAN (ARTIFICIALLY). Philosophy is so interesting...

YOUNG MAN (IN THE SAME EMPHASIZED MANNER). Thank you very much for the conversation. I have to go, good bye, see you later.

YOUNG WOMAN (IN THE SAME MANNER). Oh, please don't leave us now.

YOUNG MAN (IN THE SAME MANNER). No, unfortunately I should go right now. (SERIOUSLY.) No, I am staying.

YOUNG WOMAN. In this case I'll go.

YOUNG MAN. No, stay, please. (PAUSE.) A sudden blow moved naked branches. Something was blown up somewhere. The sniper kept silent, and I was about to lose my patience. (PAUSE.) Don't you know what's behind the theatre?

YOUNG WOMAN. A market is.

YOUNG MAN. And what is behind?

YOUNG WOMAN. A railway station may be.

YOUNG MAN. And what is behind?

YOUNG WOMAN. I don't know.

YOUNG MAN. But I do. Something else is. Motionless moments of absent eternity. A moment... five... ten...seventeen, twenty, and so on.

YOUNG WOMAN (IRONICALLY.) Philosophy again.

YOUNG MAN. But you wanted it.

YOUNG WOMAN. I won't you to wake up.

YOUNG MAN. Woman never knows what she wants. And I want you.

YOUNG WOMAN. But you've said you want me not to want.

YOUNG MAN. I joked. Why don't the guns shoot? (PAUSE.) The sun sent its rays in through a hole within the cloudy curtain, and its shadow sliding away between trees opened their green variety. I liked to caress her. I dressed her with my caresses, and my desire to undress her increased. But later, when she finished her tuneless shouts, I realized how near and far she was. We approached to each other with painful pleasure...

YOUNG WOMAN (OBVIOUSLY FEELING WHAT HE SAYS). What kind of pleasure?

YOUNG MAN. I may be killed.

YOUNG WOMAN. Won't you marry me?

YOUNG MAN. Why do you need it, I am just with you.

YOUNG WOMAN. And won't you leave me?

YOUNG MAN. No, I shan't.

YOUNG WOMAN. Don't you deceive me?

YOUNG MAN. She wants to live with a man whom she doesn't trust! No, I shan't deceive you. And if I did - you wouldn't need me. But you don't trust me already.

YOUNG WOMAN. Sometimes you seem to be wild to me.

YOUNG MAN. May be I am. What's over the dome?

YOUNG WOMAN. Something else you told.

YOUNG MAN. Shall I live if I die?

YOUNG WOMAN. You shall of course, but you shouldn't.

YOUNG MAN. It would be better to wait for a truck and quickly cross the street behind it. And to finish the matter at once. But no car went. Suddenly a far and low noise of an engine interrupted my thinking. (CARS NOISES.) Three trucks appeared from nothing. They approached to the house behind the autumn lace of the trees from the opposite side, and then they stopped.

YOUNG WOMAN. They came to save us from the theatre. Be careful.

YOUNG MAN. Why should I be careful if I won't die? Or else you love someone else?

YOUNG WOMAN. Don't be so crazy.

YOUNG MAN (IRONICALLY). But God speaks through mad person's tongue...

YOUNG WOMAN. I don't see anything funny.

YOUNG MAN. My words are human, but not divine.

YOUNG WOMAN. I need truth, but not words.

YOUNG MAN. I need knowledge, but not truth.

YOUNG WOMAN. I know the only truth that you won't marry me.

YOUNG MAN. Yes, it is true, but why don't you like it?

YOUNG WOMAN. I love you. YOUNG MAN. I do. I love myself within you. Come back, please. (PAUSE.) She felt well with me - I knew that. Feeling exactly the frequent and invisible changes of her mood I was now sharply passionate now shyly gentle. I accompanied her playing my different roles so well that I didn't know what was real.

YOUNG WOMAN. Stop your buffoonery! "She... she..."

YOUNG MAN (SERIOUSLY). As you like it. I just can everything. What's after? Sunny slices danced on the wall of the opposite house. It was a mirror brought and broken. People were going out sitting into the truck's bodies. (SERIOUSLY.) Even if you were there, you aren't now. I've lost time!

YOUNG WOMAN. With me?

YOUNG MAN. Oh, I don't know, I am confused. But you aren't here!

YOUNG WOMAN. I don't think so.

YOUNG MAN. But who is there?

YOUNG WOMAN. Not me...

YOUNG MAN. But who?

YOUNG WOMAN (GENTLY). And then we sat in the kitchen drinking coffee and talking.

YOUNG MAN (ANGRILY).And then we sat drinking coffee and talking in the kitchen! And the same with everyone before and after! Like a theatre, and nothing more!

YOUNG WOMAN. Listen, may be the sniper is gone.

YOUNG MAN. What if I cross the street quickly and attack the sniper? Cross and attack. I even don't know who is there.

YOUNG WOMAN. Wait for a truck to cross, please. Or else it would be better for you to go back through the park?

YOUNG MAN. "For you"?

YOUNG WOMAN. No, for us.

YOUNG MAN. Does she love me? I'll marry her. But does she love me? And why does she love me if she does?

YOUNG WOMAN (GENTLY). Keep cool.

YOUNG MAN. The sun set down. Short-sighted useless traffic lights looked ahead. It would be dark soon, and they would throw coloured stains to the pavement and dig deep black ravines in a red trunk. But really who is this invisible sniper? Whom shall I kill? Whom shall I think about if I survive? But I should stop this awful infinite sequence of masks and find a final form.

YOUNG WOMAN. Tell me, don't you want to kill me?

YOUNG MAN (CALMLY, NOT SURPRISED BY HER QUESTION). No, because I know you, but the sniper is unknown. And may be that's why I should kill you. You exist in my soul already, let you be there. Oh, what am I saying!

YOUNG WOMAN. You do.

YOUNG MAN. Yes, I. Even if I were blind, surfaces would bring me boredom. But I am grateful to you for everything happened, aren't I?

YOUNG WOMAN. And me too.

YOUNG MAN. However it may be, we shall go back together through the park, shan't we?

YOUNG WOMAN. Yes, we shall.

YOUNG MAN. We shall live feeling each moment of time as it passes by. Life is a play, an infinite play, an infinitely interesting play, and this sniper plays with us, too, and he isn't dangerous! (RIFLE REPORTS. BROKEN GLASS NOISE. SLOW DANCE MUSIC.)
