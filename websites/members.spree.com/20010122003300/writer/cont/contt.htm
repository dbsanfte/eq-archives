John Tannock was born in Toronto, Canada, and, perhaps, more importantly, into a universe that I didn't understand. Let me say right up front...this bothered me from the get go. Even as a child I quickly realized that, even with their love and life earned wisdom, Parents could offer no insight as to the nature of human consciousness in terms of my purpose. From an early age, it seemed clear to me that most "Humans" were content to place the unanswerable upon a secret shelf entitled "Don't look here...you'll just get hot and bothered and your brain might explode" and go about the simple business of being. For some reason, I could never adopt this head-in-the-sand approach to not having answers. I wanted them. I still do. I won't stop trying to find them. Call me crazy (many have and I have accredited documentation to that effect), but I just want to know what is going on with me, the Universe, God, Everything. Sue me. I won't care. Maybe you're not even real. Maybe I'm not. Cogito Ergo Sum. But what am I? Visit his Web Page.

Eye became interested 6 or 7 years ago, in the principles and utilities of magick and how it can be used to æffect reality. M'eye studies have had a profound effect on m'eye writting, in that Eye have found, that expression through the written thought is much the same as expression through the magical vehicle of a spell. They both work to transport the psyche out of the mundane and into the supernatural. Eye have come to understand that each and everyone of us is entered into this thing called 'life' for a purpose, what Eye have come to understand as a direction... If we learn to find where we are going, we accomplish our destiny, but it is also possible to miss the point and journey off in a direction undetermined... A majority of people move through life as such... Undirected... M'eye writting reflects what Eye have learned, what Eye understand that wisdom to be, and how Eye intend to use it in an effort to accomplish what Eye feel Eye am here to do. Life is a learning process, and we must first learn to understand ourselves, and through the written expressions of thoughts, Eye have learned how to know and understand who Eye am... Perhaps what Eye have learned about m'eye self can be used to assist others in their search for themselves...

Joseph Trocino

Personal: Born and raised near New York City in Elizabeth New Jersey, July 2nd, 1945. Summers on the Jersey shore at Belmar, New Jersey. Grandparents immigrated to the United States during the 1900’s. Married 32 years to Sherry M. Trocino, 3 children

Education, licenses and skills: BA, Geography, University of Maryland. Graduate Studies in Urban Planning, The George Washington University. Lively speaking skills in Spanish and Italian. Learning Papiamentu, the language of Aruba, Curacao and Bonaire.

Military Service: Veteran, U.S. Navy Submarine Service (reserve).

Business: Director of Operations and Marketing, Aruba Trade Zone Services, NV., the operators of a foreign trade zone located at Queen Beatrice International Airport, Orangestad, Aruba, Dutch Caribbean.

Career Highlights: former Director of Planning and Zoning Administrator, Loudoun County, Virginia. Former Chair, Loudoun County Economic Development Advisory Committee. Many volunteer service areas.

Political: Twice elected Councilmember, Leesburg Town Council.

Publications: Frequent fiction and nonfiction articles including, most recently, an article in the magazine, "Virginia City and Town," and an op-ed essay in the newspaper, "Loudoun Times-Mirror." In 1999, Four fiction stories and a poem were published in both print and electronic magazines. One, "In Aranjuez With Your Love" was award winning. The March 2000 issue of the Loudoun Arts Magazine will feature my nonfiction article spotlighting the work of Virginia artist, Octavia Frazier.

The non-working Joe: Given time enough, I travel and explore. If nothing else, I am a geographer. I love snow skiing, as well as snorkeling in blue-green waters. I write poetry, short stories and essays. I think of myself as an excellent cook. I look for people to chat with where ever I am.

My Quote : "Reality is for those who lack imagination."

My Father's Quote : "No one, on their deathbed ever said, "I wish I had spent more time at the office!" I agree.
