Recollections of a Bygone Era

© Stephanie J. Baldock 

 Mama boiled starch in a soup pot and stretched Daddy's
dungarees over metal frames to put creases in them.
She sang with the radio while she ironed shirts and
shirtwaist dresses fresh from the refrigerator.
Laundry was a never ending chore in the days when
you scrubbed clothes over a washboard, so
Mama was always scrubbing or sewing -- and
Lord, my mama could sew! -- whether she stitched
play clothes from discarded pillow ticking or 
sewed for aristocratic old biddies with
haughty posture and pinched noses.
They seemed ever so tall when they
looked down their noses at poor folks like us.
Still they came from their Oak Cliff mansions
to our little Row Houses because
our mamas washed or cleaned or
sewed a fine stitch where the fit was always
"just right" after a little bit got
"took in" or "let out" here or there. 
I remember our tiny, whitewashed house on its
non-descript street where all the other houses were
as similar as the up and coming families that inhabited them
-- daring to dream of lifestyles that would 
set their forefathers minds agog.
I remember horsehair sofas and formica dinettes as
vividly as I remember that old stray dog that
slept under our porch and once bit the postman.
Like so many my age, the Future  is uncertain but
the Past is the Good Old Days.
