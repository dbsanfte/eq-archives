 A Vacation Date - for Spiritual Renewal

© Ruth Marlene Friesen

Need an alternative vacation idea? Something opposite to seeing more big cities with malls full of people, all with hands out for your money?

Need to renew your spirit where it is just you on this big rugged earth-ball, and God up in Heaven? A place where it is easier to ask Him earnest questions directly and expect to sense a reply?

(Gulp!) Calls for a wilderness place, doesn't it? But you need a good quality bed at night, and some basic amenities. Where!? How does one insure such a spiritual renewal really happens?

May I make some suggestions?

Thank you.

If you already do journalling most every day the need to get the main points of your life worked out will not be as urgent. Instead you might start with re-evaluating your priorities.

If you're new to this, plan a vacation this year where you can spend at least a few hours alone, outdoors, cut off from human contact if possible, and take an honest, gut-wrenching look at your life before God. From His point of view, what have you become? Do not fear the truth, for it will set you free. At least, if you are humble and admit to God that you need help. It is at exactly that point that help miraculously appears. It may be instantaneous, or simply start there and grow.

Right now my mind is on a mountain retreat, but you could also go for a sunrise walk by a wheat field on the prairies, and have such an experience.

Unless you are able to vacation alone, consider a package where your family members can have their own adventures, or luxuriate in a mineral hot springs.

Just this year I've made new friends and learned of their wilderness vacation packages, where both you and your other-extreme family members can all be made happy. It has just opened, so I know there may be room for short-notice bookings. It's a five-day wilderness vacation at Meadow Creek in the Canadian Rockies, just a bit north of the American border.

Their guests are kept in a fine hotel on the shore of Kootenay Lake. There is a hot springs nearby, with caves to explore. Meadow Creek Wilderness Vacations arranges to pick up your party each day and take you to see the sights, all over the mountains in 4x4 all-terrain- vehicles, and bring you back at the end of each day to the hotel with the pool and all its fine facilities.

Your package can be tailored, so you might ask them to drop you off with a blanket and a picnic at some lovely view spot, and let the rest of your party go boating, water-skiing, or exploring Sandon, a ghost town, and an abandoned silver mine nearby, while you sort out the questions, priorities, and projects of your life.

If you have been living at 300 mph, you may need to just lie back and unwind first. You'll get sleepy if not used to sitting still, so have a nap. It may take until the second or third day, when you have spelled out all that is on your mind, that the answers begin to come.

Even so, for a thorough spiritual retreat you must involve God and yield to His greater purposes. It is after all His universe, and all its principles, whether mechanical or spiritual, have a beautiful synergy. To get in sync at that level is where you will find true fulfillment and wholeness.
