Seth Abramson-a 1998 graduate of Dartmouth College, Seth is a second-year student at Harvard Law School. A life-long resident of Boston, Seth began writing poetry in the fall of 1998, soon after his immersion into the somewhat stifling world of legal thought and writing. His work has been published or will soon be published in a number of print and on-line poetry journals, including The Melic Review, Savoy Magazine, Tintern Abbey, Eclectica, The Fish Eye Review, and The Southern Ocean Review. Seth recently received an Honorable Mention Award in Suite101.com's 1999 National Poetry Month Contest.










Bill Adams -Wm. F. Adams, Jr. (Bill) is an aging father of five, who lives in the hills of NorthWest New Jersey and writes in an attempt to reconcile what he knows with what he sees: "it is maddest of all to accept the world as it is, and not as it should be." Don Quixote/Cervantes









Vasilis Adams A. I am an ESL (English as a Second Language) teacher. I have been teaching English full-time for the last forteen years. Prior to that I worked as a Technical Specifications Writer for seven years and as an Engineer for five years. I was born in Thessaloniki, Greece. I went to university in the United States where I received a B.Sc. degree. My writing credits include published fiction and non-fiction appearing both in Greece and in the USA. Stateside publications I have written for are Greek Accent, National Herald (Proini), and Crosscurrents. In Greece I've been published in 30-Days, Key Travel News, Greece's Weekly, Athena Magazine and had a weekend travel column in The Athens Star newspaper.

Yona Alon Yona Alon, an Israeli poet writing in Hebrew, was born in Kibbutz Ein-Shemer in 1935. In 1958 he moved to Jerusalem, where he lives today. He studied in the Rubin Academy of Music in Jerusalem, and learned philosophy from Prof. Yeshayahu Leibowitz and Prof. Marcel Dubois.
His books:
Philosophy - 1. There are no wicked in the world (1968). The book offers a new moral theory which eliminates the concept of wickedness.
2. The social moral vs. the existence moral (1975). The book develops and widens the theory offered in the first book.
Poetry:
1. Light (1976).
. 2. Beethoven - letters for the Immortal lover (1990).
3. The death of summer (1998).
4. A year's diary (1999).
The book was translated to English by Dr. Ben Schapiro. Website: http://www.migvan.co.il/magicpage/Cult-Rec/yona/english.htm

Norm Anderson Norm grew up in a small town 18 miles west of Cleveland, OH called Avon Lake. He was born Nov. 10 1957. He liked to play baseball and golf. Norm says he was a jock growing up. First poem ever written was to a girl friend in college 20 years ago. He attended Cuyahoga Community College, majoring in baseball with a minor in beer. A freind of his told him he could probably play more baseball for Dallas Baptist University so in 1980 Norm moved to Texas. Norm graduated from DBU in 1984. Majored in beer this time, just kidding -Major in PE, with a BS in education. He met his wife in Dallas but she is from Buffalo, NY. She already had a daughter- Alison from a previous marrige, however Norman has alwasys considered Alison his child since she was 14 months old when Norm and Marley wed on the Fourth of July 1982. Their son Casey was born in 1983. The family moved to Buffalo in 1990. Norm has worked more jobs than he would care to mention. Presently he is working on a cartoon, screenplay and book. His dog Pepper that likes to lift his leg and pee on the curtains. Norm is currently selling computers wholesale. Normans hobbies are reading, golf, writing, and hanging out with the kids. Diane Amlin Ashley I am a homemaker, wife, mother, and proud grandmother of eight. My ethnic heritage is both Scandinavian and Native American Indian. I was born and educated in California. I've enjoyed writing and reading from my youngest years. There are two volumes of poems, the bulk of which are unpublished. A few have appeared in both our local newspaper the "Record Gazette Senior Scene," and "Angel News" a monthly newsletter. Writing is a passion as natural as breathing, and full of surprise. I find life rich and write like a growing child, open to hold words yet giving them away.
