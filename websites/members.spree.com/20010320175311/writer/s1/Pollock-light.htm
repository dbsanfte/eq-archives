 The Guiding Light
© Michael Pollock

It started out just like any other day. David boarded his treasured sailboat and headed out of the harbor for a day of sailing. As he passed the faithful lighthouse on his way out to the open ocean, his heart began to dance over the feeling of freedom that came with a day on the ocean. He knew it would be a great day.

After several hours of sailing a few miles offshore, David began to sense a change in the air. He'd always been good at sensing an oncoming storm. He decided to start heading back to the safety of his harbor. He searched for his compass to get an idea of which direction lie the shore. The compass was nowhere to be found. A little concerned, David then referred to the navigational instruments of the sailboat. That was no help as he neglected to get them repaired after they stopped working a few months back.

As the sky darkened and a dense fog set in, David's concern grew to nearly a panic level. He was lost at sea. He had ventured out of site of the shoreline, had lost his compass and his navigational instruments were broken. The sea began to roll, tossing David and his tiny sailboat about like a cork. What would he do now, he wondered. How would he ever make it back to his safe harbor.

Suddenly, David remembed the lighthouse that marked the entrance to the harbor. Although he had no idea what direction it lie, David turned his boat toward a direction that just felt right and true. As he continued in blind faith, he peered out at the dense fog, searching for the trusty lighthouse.

Off in the distance, he heard a bell that sat atop a bouy marking the entrance to his harbor. He then saw a light, at first very small and dim, but which grew brighter as he moved toward it. In a few more minutes the light grew very bright as David's lighthouse emerged from the fog, bringing with it direction, clarity, safety and comfort. David was home. He was safe.

Sometimes our lives seem very similar to David's day on the ocean. Everything starts out pretty normal and fun. Then the weather changes and the fog sets in. Perhaps it comes in the form of a relationship problem, a crisis at work, financial concerns or a health problem. You look for direction and guidance but can't seem to find your own internal compass. You are lost and being tossed about on the sea of life.

Suddenly, you remember you have your own trusty guiding light. Maybe it's your personal mission statement, your relationship with a Higher Power or - in my case - my Life Purpose. In blind faith, you turn in the direction that feels right and true. You proceed through the fog, searching for an answer. The light, at first very dim, begins to brighten. Eventually, it emerges from the darkness bringing with it clarity, direction, safety and comfort. You are home.

Spend this week inspecting your own navigational instruments. Check to make sure you have your own compass ready for when the fog of life sets in. Ask yourself what it is that gives you guidance and clarity. Do you have your own personal mission statement, Life Purpose or relationship with a Higher Power? If so, can you see it from where you are? If not, do you know in which direction it lie? Write your thoughts down on paper or in a journal. If your guiding light is not visible to you, make a plan to clarify it. Talk to a close friend, a Coach or your Pastor. We all need our own lighthouse for navigating the sea of life.

Have a great week. Send me an email and tell me what your guiding light looks like.

It's your life. Create it the way you desire!
