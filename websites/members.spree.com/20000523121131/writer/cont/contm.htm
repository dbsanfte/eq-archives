Jerome Mandel

sometime Chair of the English Department at Tel Aviv University, is the first-place winner of the P.E.N.-UNESCO International Short Story Competition for 997. His stories have appeared in Midstream; The Missouri Review; Short Story International; War, Literature, and the Arts; The Morpo Review; PEN-International; PEN-Israel; arc--The Journal of the Israel Association of Writers in English; and in Nativ (in Hebrew). The Israel Federation of Writers Unions published his collection of short stories, NOTHING GOLD CAN STAY: 18 STORIES OF ISRAELI EXPERIENCE, in February, 1999. The father of two grown children, he lives in Ra'anana with his wife who also teaches at Tel Aviv University and writes on Hemingway. Jerome's book a short story collection, NOTHING GOLD CAN STAY: 18 STORIES OF ISRAELI EXPERIENCE can be ordered either directly from the publisher by FAX at 972-3-691-9681, on the net from books@booksinternational.com editors note: Watch for my review soon- it's a great book!

Kim Mayhall

i'm a typical insane poet who has written since she was four....i have lived many life times in the short time i've captured the name of kim and know that poetry comes from something that isn't explained in our language....i have been attending school forever it seems....and have been published quite a bit but refuse to believe it is because of any thing other than just pure luck.......so I would say to anyone who has the inkling to write should write and write a lot.....if about nothing if anything at all....because the secret to life lies between that whisper and the infinite giggle of time.....teeehee........oh and i have 4 kids...should that explain my insanity? Anastasia (Stazja) McFadyen-Stazja McFadyen

lives in Austin, Tx. with her hard working husband Cody, musician son Mike and best dog in the world Chelsea. She is a board member of the Austin International Poetry Festival, and executive officer in the Austin Poetry Society. She hosts the weekly Saturday Afternoon Poetry on the Porch at Quackenbush's Coffee House, and the monthly East Side Black & White poets at Ebony Sun Java House. Online, she publishes a weekly poetry e-newsletter, "Map of Austin Poetry", archived on the Austin International Poetry Festival web site at http:www.hyperweb.com/aipf Stazja has featured at poetry readings throughout central Texas, and at venues across the country. Her poems have been published in various magazines and anthologies, internet poetry magazines and web sites, and two chapbook collections: If You Can't Eat 'em, Join 'em and Dream Songs.She was voted "Favorite Featured Poet" in Poetry Super Highway's 1998 contest. She and L.A. poet Larry Jaffe are co-editing Heritage Blue, an anthology of 17 Austin and Southern California poets reading at Heritage House, an Austin historical landmark. Profits from Heritage Blue, scheduled for April 1999 publication by PoetWarrior Press, will be donated to Heritage House for restorations.

Eric Morrissey

I'm a 51-year-old with three children and three grandchildren. I'm originally from Cwmaman, South Wales, U.K., but now living in Cardigan, West Wales. I didn't start writing poetry until well after my fiftieth birthday. I have not had any tuition in creative writing, so am entirely self taught in this respect. I've traveled fairly extensively, whilst in the Royal Navy and since leaving in 1968. My hobbies are fairly diverse, from Falconry to marathon running. When training for a specific marathon I run between 120 - 130 miles per 6 day week when fit. Sundays I rest, unless there's a race (of anything up to half marathon distance), in which case I run that too.


Victoria Mosley

" My poems are really about relationships , the times of transition in one's life when there's death , divorce, and everything seems to be falling apart . But things are never unremittingly bleak , I write intense love poems too "

Performance poet Victoria Mosley was born in Kent , and grew up in Singapore and Hong Kong .

Returning to Britain to read English Literature at London University , she dropped out after a year to work as a production assistant on short films and rock videos .

Moving to a clifftop farm on the Isle of Man she bred Arabian horses- , and helped produce a literary magasine. She then lived in France , and later Devon , where she joined the Company of Poets and gave readings of her work at the Dartingtom Art Gallery ,Wessex Poetry Festival,Cheltenham Festival , The Bug Bar , Brixton . She makes regular appearances at the Poetry Cafe Covent Garden , Poetry bookshop Brixton , has appeared at the Comedy House Stockwell .

She is a founder member of Poets of the Pulse and will be working closely with them , ( Philip Law ,Adam Horovitz, Ian Winn, Michael Groce , John Citizen ) over the next year , touring Theatres , schools , hospices festivals , wherever poetry is needed .

She now lives with her three children in Dulwich .

She has published in numerous small press mags and anthologies .

Victoria Mosley's first collection of poems The Dry Season will be published on November 2 1998 by Odyssey Poets at £6 .95 . To order a copy or for further information about Victoria Mosly contact : The Creative Umbrella 0171 323 5993
