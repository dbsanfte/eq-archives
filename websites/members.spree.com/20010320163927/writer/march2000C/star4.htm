 Star Chasers/On the Road to Paradise
Dedicated to Jack Kerouac
© Sean McLaughin

Chapter 3 Enter the Beast (cont.)

And upon saying so he takes hold of the knife with one hand, in other hand its a fist full of creamers concealed by clenched fist which he presses up against eye…and CAPACK! - knifes the containers and the white eyeball goo shoots out all over the place. This receives a warm greeting of laughter from companions, and a cold look of 'oh my god how immature' from the girls. Oh well, Ahhhh (stretch,yawn) time to unwind with the comfort of the inanimate serenity of this diner. A train tea-pots far away, as it blows so does Donny's heart, sighing on out, sipping on coffee…silence and sweet gentle water of mind flowing right on out of the gates lifted and on towards the sea. Thoughts a blazin with romance all gone wrong with the wind, funny to think about broken-hearts, his heart broken long before any girls ever had anything to do with it. Not long ago it was baseball, break my heart base-ball, home-team ra-ra baseball, home-runs, sexual metaphors, tough guys to soft, America at its idealist. Brotherhood as well as real brotherhood - with brothers from the hood all united in that field of dreams. Well donny's dreams like America's well too short-lived, - with the glory and sadness of one day - eyes of affection from every which direction fixed on you! - in middle on that pitching mound ready to showcase that laser arm, the one all the scouts dream about according to dad sitting amidst the crowd drunk, hollering profanities, 3-2 count crowd on the edge of their seats - let her rip…High-school…gassinip!!! Strike-three your out!!! Mileage, bloody tear-stained mileage that ol tendonitis shoulder that ol tendonitis heart - kicked on, spit at by some short perverted coach - the kind of pig that actually undressed down to the complete buck while cutting one of his broken hearted youths of America -(one of Don's close friends) all lined up single file outside his office waiting anxiously for that moment of clarity…baseball, break your heart baseball.

"Donny…Donny… whoa you were kind of spaceing out there, what's on your mind man?"

"Oh nothing just thinking about our past time that's all."

"We should jet my friends, this place is kind of freek'n me out already, pretty soon everybody's gonna start turning into werewolve's" Anounces T-bone and so their off again. As they exit stage left Donny hollering "Hey… Charity…see you in Paradise love!" and blows her a kiss.

They head back to the dove, fire her up, and they're off again in search of a place, any place remotely safe-looking to sleep. The clock staring on out glowing 9: 35 - 'can't believe it's so late, we need to find some shelter immediately'. The road twisting and turning into more and more darkness betwixt tall owl trees, wind picking up, cars dissipating on road, soon its empty street loneliness of late night, and lost! Suddenly the trip feeling hopeless, scary, out in the middle of Pennsylvania nowhere where the beast will surely feast on our souls! So they turn to the radio for some comfort…radio dial twists, on comes some evangelist preaching "We shall confess our sins. We shall pray to be cleansed. We shall pray to be filled with the holy spirit, if we are going to be any use to God…it will only be through the power of the spirit…" and on and on as the dove rolls deeper and deeper into fog-laden woods and hills secluded. To cope with the fear and disorientation the heroes pull into a drive-through beer garage and pick-up some tough-guy beer, IBC its called. Then its back to gravel road, clock now glowing 12:00, 12:30, 1:00 and on pass the monkey's brass a quarter to his…late, need to stay awake, find some-place to sleep…soon!

"Uh should I uh turn here?" confusion, confusion, weary-eyed confusion.

"Um, let's just go down a little bit further…Whoa! Look at that freek'n road!" comments Donny observing a black path ahead leading across some old country bridge that looks just like one from a Scooby-Doo rerun where the thing keeps turning around and around sending the Scooby van back and forth in circles. Brake-lights illuminate interior of car SCREECH red-alert!!

"Oh shit, I can't do it, I can't trust that son of a bitch bridge." Says the Captain nearly pulling into a mud-filled ditch, so instead its u-turn, fire the dove back up and onward.

T-bone begins dozing off in the back seat as Donny and the Captain sail onward down the abandoned country roads dimly lit by archaic lanterns illuminating the way. They slowly approach some giant of a building high above perched on hill -passenger side of dove. The Captain brakes to take a look at the strange sight which turns out to be a babtist church with a big glowing sign in front reading: 'Jesus is passing through here tonight.'

"whoa what the?! That's a pretty bold statement huh Insano?"

"I'd say! Gosh what the hell's going on!"

Just then T-bone alerts back to senses and some tail-wagging mutt comes a strolling down the middle of the road ahead playing chicken with the dove.

"OH SHIT" bursting from back seat out of nowhere "It's the fuck'n Beast!!! And with that they take of roaring down the road like Mario Andrettie not looking back, flying as fast as the Dove will take them to a safe camp-ground, any camp-ground, the first camp-ground they encounter which happens to be the Deer Cove, and unfortunately for them none were barking. (instead just a hideous dog)

They pull into the driveway entrance to the cove wearing their road-wearied looks of exhaustion. Tires chomping up gravel - the only sound to be heard throughout the deserted parking lot entrance. They approach a dimly lit office, only a mellow-yellow lamp on table and vase in window, the office just beyond the mammoth wooden Gates of entrance to the grounds - the gates look like that giant portal from King- Kong (which happens to be the first movie Donny ever saw in this life - no wonder!). Insano scans the surroundings uncertain what to do next, he brakes to read the hand-crafted sign on the office window: 'To rent a lot register at the office bla bla bla …gates will be closed at 12:00, and opened again at 6:00 a.m.' - "hmmm that's funny". So the captain parks the car abruptly, acknowledges companions who haste not and mozy on up to the creaky wooden steps to the porch of the office. Off in the distant darkness of the camp- grounds - the sounds of tree-muffled laughter, fires a popping, and several rusty bicycle chains turning. Donny approaches the door to the office, twists the knob, again, unsuccessful…peers in through window…taps on glass…no response.

"Uhh…okay…hmm…what the hell?!…" completely drained from the perils of the first day's excitement Don just opts for silence, and awaits a suggestion from the others. T-bone of course contently saying nothing simply yawning and lighting up another smoke.

"F it let's just head inside, if we see someone then we'll tell em we tried at the office right?" suggests the Captain.

So much for a warm welcome…any-how the setting seems right for the weary wanderers, who all agree that it is simply another example of the beast (who evidently now seems set on doing whatever it takes to prevent this journey from happening swiftly) playing tricks on them, pretty soon he'd surely have his way with them -probably inside the cove in the pitch-black darkness of night. Back to the car, start her up, and they roll quietly down the gravel paths alit by firelight, pass sites occupied by trailors, many of them appearing to be permanent arrangements with mail-boxes and names like the 'MacGregors' or 'the Glick's' carved into wooden addresses. T-bone notices a spot of serenity off to the side of the main trail (leave it to him to find these spots, after all it's where he lives his life), Donny and the Captain agree, so they pull into the desolate strip of sites estranged from the rest of the Cove. The strip nearly separated from the grounds by a patch of trees stemming outward from the boardering forest. They pull into a spot beneath a broad tree - site 417. The Capt. pops the trunk, grabs the tent, the beer, cracks one open and begins to set up shop. Donny unravels his loyal hammock and runs it tree to tree between the car and tent. When the tent is erected the three tired gypsies decide its time to get drunk.

Don digs into the bag of snacks and fires together a sloppy P.B. sandwich to accompany his beer. He walks over to their picnic table and plops himself down, the Captain like a true skipper charts a map with flashlight in teeth, trying to formulate some sort of coherent plan of attack for tomorrow. T-bone decides he's had enough for the day and passes out in tent, soon he's snoring.

"Cheers Don, here's to the f'n Beast who's apparently decided to molest us this trip."

Then the two of them sigh and begin to take comfort in the stationary environs of their surroundings. They rip into a sloppy beer-drinking binge, whacking down bottles at a time, the whole time spilling drops all over themselves - beer induced laughter percolating throughout the misty pine scented night, carried away forever into the Allegheni breeze. Time standing still…home and sorrows far-away…school and responsibility even farther away somewhere down the end of the line - manana. The two drunkards now atop the picnic table begin hollering the Captain's trademark beast yodel's to the wind. Donny now a laughing hysterical mess of sentimental fever.

"Man I miss working with you, I miss hearing these beast cries all the time! God, those were the days Insano, some of the best times I'll ever have man, you just don't realize it until their in the rearview mirror! DAMN YOU BEAST!"

When the last of the beers is put to rest the Captain retires to the tent, and Donny clumsily climbs into the swing-swaying hammock where his poetry awaits. His hazy-head a throbbing ba-boom-boom- boom with the dizzying hammock. Alone in the dark of the Alleghini's so far away from all the bloody pain of things remembered, yet now somehow closer than ever all opened up inward in this black of night - wish you were…wish you were so close to me. Soon Donny's solitary laments yield to acute awareness of moment as a gut-wrenching sound comes a groaning across the sky. The sound begins as a sort of cough or choking, but it melts into a hideous crackle and continues for an uncomfortable amount of time.

"What the ?! hey did you here that?" asks Insano from within the tent. The sound continues…

"God, that has gotta be the most hideous sound I have ever heard in my entire life!…Oh yeah Donny, I almost forgot to tell you about the beast's pets that inhabit these parts, the ones that you might want to be on the lookout for."

"Oh yeah what's that?"

"The blood-thirsting tree-sloths of the Alleghini's!"

Laughter, but only temporal as the sounds continue to pile on layer upon layer a mad rhapsody.

And so Donny sways amidst the symphony of nocturnal moonlight, 1,2,3 cough cough, 1,2,3, cough, then CA-BOOM gunshots in the distance, cough, CA-BOOEY and echo fading out into the mist rain sssssssssssssssssss and crickets…and then screaming from the heavens come the echo booming radar howls of early morning owls, ping-ponging tree-to tree throughout the Cove. Donny falls victim to the overwhelming tingles of the experience, he grabs the flashlight and begins to write
Donny hero's soliloquy

Ah alive, always forever, this is where I long to be, I think of home behind and that alternative world of my father's. A world I'll never own, because time- she already owns it, and me, I ain't gett'n any younger. A world I loath to return to, a world of constant dramatizations of those glorious wonder years no more, drama, dramatizations, recycled yesterday's prancing about their stages of despair, tired of reinactments, tired of virtual net waves and the underground chambers of solitary hearts, tired of fear and detachment, tired of being surveyed by surveyors of solo-psistic boundaries, tired of that splinter's labyrinth of no escape. I think of Mills, friend and personal hero (his story a whole other one in itself), Mills out West with his sweet-heart Rossie-Rose road wandering like modern day Kerouac's and Cassidy's, that's it! That's the road I yearn for, a dramatization I can handle - young Jacks road resurrected, that road never-ending - Sal's paradise, a road uninhibited, an empirical road of Allegheni breeze- blowin, moon glowin, owl-booming sound waves of this moment of forever, - SURF AND RIP THE CURL- and ride onward to places where we rarely go, onward, upwards and then keep on going! Till sitting atop this world with legs tumblin free dipped in blissful candy-cane kisses and tastes and warm embrace the sun on our face to love, sweet helpless love, and when I get there I'll be ready for the long-awaited embrace.


Chapter 4 Into the Dream city

Donny falls asleep in Hammock and the dreams continue (Meanwhile the skeleton break-dances wildly at an underground rave).

Hush little ones and don't say a word…close your eyes now and sleep…Into the darkness of all our nights we go, with Donny as our guide…good-luck Don, may you return safely…oh are you bringing them back?…sailing you say…well try not to get too lost…just try to make sure you follow the right illusions…which ones are the right ones…well, only you can decide that.

Welcome fair reader to the city of Dreams, the portal between this and that, the place where the wild things are. The other part of the script, where nothing is true, because everything is permitted. Welcome to the home of forever, as well as the home of the Fear - The Beast's palace upon the hill in the dark of night void where I sleep fear - A thousand crystal-light mosaic's of oscillating werewolves and Nothing's from the Never Ending Story fear, the I love you no more funeral goodbye of tomorrow never shines - fear, always the fear. Let the dreams begin…


Godzilla-beach, rocky-rolling wave crescendo of early evening dusk. Quite a nice gathering, dream-impression of some historically-driven/holiday-like occasion. Milky haze, crimson sky splattered with gleaming stars, blinking like Christmas lights. Wandering the festive beach alone - and somehow separated from rest of the gang - whoever that is today. Suddenly as forthcoming as the autumn leave- rolling wind blows a cool, crow-cawing gust of shivers. Sudden awareness of - fear - that impending presence that breathes beneath the surface. Walking faster and faster, with each dizzy step scanning wildly for signs of companions through laughter, children playing, and on. Then its "Ohhh Ahhh" the crowd in unison everyone gazing up at the sky. Frozen in slo-motion steps gazing upwards at the stealth-like aircraft or star, black in color with red-marks like a black-widow. The aircraft dashing through the sky so freely, so uninhibited, so unscientifically…amazing. Then dashing to a complete stop, suspended in the center of attention, high above in scarlet-sky, Time frozen…all eyes fixed in silence
