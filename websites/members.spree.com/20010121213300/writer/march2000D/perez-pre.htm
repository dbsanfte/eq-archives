 pre-valentine's day musings

© Silvia Antonia Brandon y Pérez 
salvage (SAL-vij) noun
   2. The act of saving imperiled property from loss. The property so saved.
   3. Something saved from destruction or waste and put to further use.

and so, my soul, what price salvation
how dear the turning of the knife
what wondrous compensation
shall suffice
to turn despair to joy
and having done the turning
to capture joy and freeze it
carefully wrapped in foil
preventing loss of flavor
and vitamin content




she carefully pared the carrots for the stew
told him clearly he was
naught but a bit of nonsense
a lackadaisal organic misfeasance
lacking spine or drive 
or the wherewithal for life
absent the eversuffering spouse
the caring child
or the laboring mother
and then she set a place for one
white napkin neatly folded on the right 
of the plate, fork, spoon 
a small  delphinium in the bud vase
to the left of the wineglass
(a dry chablis, well-chilled)
and served him dinner
a clean and seasoned plate
with figured artichokes 
fresh hot Cuban bread
with virgin olive oil and garlic
(a dash of arsenic
in the black bean soup)
and cream cheese flan 
with mantecado ice cream




the end of the century approaches
with undue haste
as I watch snow fall
and traffic congest
and children slash tires
in the old neighborhood
lately mixed, with poverty
and tension for all


what price, what salvage
shall soul's redemption cost
what underpinnings shall entice
the anima to stay another year
to stop awhile, and write a line or two
to smile at children's jokes
and muse at life, and death, and god
