 Suspended Concentration
© Cicely M. Nevitt 
this is the night before my second
test in classic mythology and I 
think on all the threads of pantheons
which bind my feet to this desk in

dread from the kitchen and I need to
learn how to cook in the bedroom as
soon as I get my own place and wait
for when the what (?) stops here to pay 

for your mileage's ease. springtime comes
and you learn to throw something away
like those poems which go nowhere and
motivation's transition from homework

to the hot steam of opening inside so that 
when it turned cold again (louisville oh
louisville) my body was so disappointed
that I slept twelve hours two nights in a

row because the radioactive thermostat
of my sex fell asleep on an iceberg and
made up in z's for what she lacked in xxx's
and I caressed my t's and simmered like texas

in a churning pot of summer on its way
with doldrums beating the heat of my pulp
and the meat of fruit and water so now I am 
losing the ability to focus and dreaming the

song of the locus in the sweetest measure of one
long song of a poem defying punctuation and
academic concentration and oh gods I would
rather write eight hours a day and take breaks

for lovemaking than squeeze my poems into
praying for more free time and assigning my
right to whenever I can tap the hydra and quiet
the perilous siren to run to the notebook

for in each page is a new god waiting to be
spoken to when this classic mythology isn't
due and my sexual appetites are whetted through
like the cradle basket bones ribbing this heart's lonely

home.
