Ronald Nash

Ronald Nash was born in 1950 in Cincinnati, Ohio. He served four years in the U.S. Army and received his undergraduate degree in Marketing from San Jose State University. He is now retired, living and writing poetry in the Appalachian mountains of Kentucky.


Michael J. Nedell

Michael is the founder of The Burlington Free Word Association, a non-profiting litterary guild whose goal is to disseminate literature in impoverished areas. He is the author of the novel 'Saint Michael's Letters to the Aesthesians' published in September 99 by WPC/the minimal press. He is 34 years old and lives in Burlington, Vermont.


Cicely M. Nevitt

Cicely Nevitt is an associate at a political marketing and research company, an undergraduate student at the University of Louisville, and a poet. She enjoys the vast possibilities of poetry and its manifold outlets of communication. The Louisville Poets' Guild, of which she is a wayward member, is an eclectic group of wordsmiths who converge to immerse themselves in the essence of poetry. She savored her first experience with being published in the Louisville Poets' Guild Anthology '99. She believes that when we are writing about those things which move us, we are writing our own truths. There is joy in that recognition.


Cheryl Nicholas

Cheryl Nicholas is a freelance author and editor as well as registered nurse. She lives with her teenage daughter in a small town nestled in the foothills of the Cascade Mountains. Her inspirational essays have been published in various Christian periodicals as well as electronic media. Her motto: The event is meaningless. It is what we do with the event that sets our spiritual path.


RHONDA L. NOLAN

is a part-time freelance writer living in southern Louisiana. Her personal website is RHONDAVOUS: Romance by Rhonda

She works full-time as a Community Outreach Specialist doing public relations and education, writing, editing, publications, graphic design, web site development, public speaking and fundraising.

In addition to a B.A. in Journalism and an M.A. in Speech Communication from Louisiana State University, Rhonda is a former college instructor and semi-professional actor. She attended the Professional Actor's M.F.A. Training Program at the University of Missouri-Kansas City.

"Although I consider myself primarily a romance writer, my stories are often non-traditional in nature. I think there is more to the genre than a "formula." I have also written romantic comedy, romantic fantasy, futuristic romance, poetry and general fiction. I do not particularly care for the stereotypical supermarket paperback romance. My biggest ambition is to get published and eventually write my first novel."

She loves red wine, pink roses, shades of burgundy, magnolias, hardwood floors, Pre-Raphaelite artwork, Europe, and her orange tabby named "Kee."

"If you aren't passionate about something in your life, then you aren't really alive. I am passionate about being passionate!"

Rhonda designed her fiance's web site, Gefilte's Shop of Poetry and Prose, a collection of original, contemporary poetry by D. Alexander Levy. She is also the designer, owner and editor of a new romance e-zine, Potpourri Romance Online Rhonda is seeking unpublished short fiction and poetry for this zine! Submission guidelines can be found at the site.

Her short stories have been published many times on the Internet. Rhonda won a Golden Quill at The Short Story Workshop (where she a co-founder and staff member there) for "The Truth About Caleb" and "Shades of Burgundy." "Shades of Burgundy" also won Short Story of the Month in December, 1998 at St. Rose Press. Other publications include: The Fiction Network, The Inditer, The Short Story Collective, The Reading Garden, SOON Online Magazine, Short Story Writer's Showcase, The Writer's Quill, The Short Story Page, The Miserere Review, and the ever-popular, Writer's Choice Literary Journal.

In addition, her poetry and short stories have been published in print magazines such as Emotions and The Threshold. Rhonda's tribute to her late grandmother, "Butterfly Eyes" is part of Shards an anthology of womens literature by DLSIJ Press. All proceeds from this e-book go to the Breast Cancer Fund. Rhonda L. Nolan is the editor of Potpourri : Miscellaneous & Sundry Romance Online

Opelupuakea

I was born an old soul never understood kids my age, hung around adults to listen to their conversations on many topics. Self learner - life student of human nature. My ancestors come from Europe and finally landed here with a native. My bloodline is as eclectic as my interests.
