 ...EVERYTHING BUT HEADS
© Anar Azimov

CHARACTERS:
1.Woman of 50
2.Young Man
3.Young Man's Girl
4.Intellectual Man
5.One More
6.Non-Intellectual Man
7.Girl With Watch
8.Sleeping Girl
9.First Woman
10.Second Woman

A flat. A window with the landscape of a modern city on the backstage. A clock hangs at the edge of the stage so that the audience may see the back of the clock. Enters Woman Of 50. Her hair is grey, her gown is old-fashioned. She approaches to the edge of the stage and looks as if into the mirror. She touches the wrinkles of her face.

WOMAN OF 50 Have I seen it yesterday or is it new one? If it is, I've got one day older. If it isn't, yesterday I wasn't younger than now. Nothing can be better for me. (With despair.) Nothing. (Pause. With abrupt coquetry and relief.) Because even a wrinkle would be a big harm for such a young and pretty woman as I am. (With more coquetry.) I even want to be old and ugly because if I were, wrinkles wouldn't trouble me. If I were... (With despair.) My son... (Moves aside "the mirror" as if leaving. Fast blackout. The heels of Woman's shoes sound as if she is going upstairs. A door unseen in the dark is opened and closed. The light is gradually back to the maximum. On the backstage - stairs to the gallery of the second floor, a big mirror is downstairs. The clock is on the wall. It doesn't work. Young Man stands downstairs. He has a red belt. Young Man's Girl stands upstairs at the door. Young Man turns around himself and at last looks up.)

YOUNG MAN Here you are. Come down. (She comes rather loudly. Young Man angrily whispers.) Quiet, he may hear us. (Young Man's Girl starts going on the tiptoes.) Do you really understand it is necessary to do?

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL I don't know why me.

YOUNG MAN (with hysteria and humour). Because of similarity.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL I always knew you see no difference between me and her.

YOUNG MAN Not me, but nature does. (Takes a gun from his pocket.) Take it. (Young Man's Girl startles and looks at the gun as if she is hypnotized.)

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL No, I can't.

YOUNG MAN You said nothing like that before, but now you do. How can you say you can't?

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL It is sin.

YOUNG MAN It's sin to do what my mother does.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL It's crime.

YOUNG MAN It won't be crime if nobody sees what we do.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL (almost losing her mind). Oh, no...

YOUNG MAN Oh, yes, and if you are so weak...

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL (being more quiet, but with some fear). ...you'll do it yourself?

YOUNG MAN What?

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL You'll kill your mother yourself?

YOUNG MAN Your question is not correct. Of course, you may kill yours if you want, but we are not brother and sister.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL This nuances are not for me. I have no philosophic education.

YOUNG MAN You don't need it. (Approaches to her.) Anyhow I love you...

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL And why do you?

YOUNG MAN It doesn't matter.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL And when?

YOUNG MAN Later.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL And what now?

YOUNG MAN Now take it. (Puts the gun into her hand; the pause.)

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL And what if I'm weak?

YOUNG MAN So prove you are stronger.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL (with irony). Than you are?

YOUNG MAN Than she is.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL So what?

YOUNG MAN Then I'll know my love is not a mistake.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL (going upstairs as a robot). You haven't said me what her sin is.

YOUNG MAN Go. (Young Man's Girl goes upstairs. She enters the room and closes the door. A woman's wail and a shot sound. Young Man covers his ears with his hands. Simultaneously with the shot the light is flashing one time and off again. Gradual increase of light. A city park. Bushes and trees. Far noise of cars. Intellectual Man hangs on an oak with his belt, which is fixed as a tail so that he faces the audience.)

INTELLECTUAL MAN I think it is not correct to consider similarity as a factor of interaction because two things are different as they are not the same one.

(The light is off and on during a moment. Intellectual Man hangs sideways to the audience, and One More hangs on the oak in the same way so they face each other.)

ONE MORE Don't you accept the principle of relativity?

INTELLECTUAL MAN On the contrary, this principle disables similarity as two things can't be similar just because of relativity of their possible similarity.

ONE MORE How do you refuse similarity if you accept it?

INTELLECTUAL MAN I accept only relative similarity so I don't accept it at all.

ONE MORE How do you accept similarity if you refuse it?

INTELLECTUAL MAN Relative similarity is not similar.

ONE MORE Then why do you name it so?

INTELLECTUAL MAN I must confess I thought about it for a long time, but I haven't come to any exact conclusion.

(Non-Intellectual Man enters from behind a bush)

NON-INTELLECTUAL MAN Hush! You may scare all girls. (Looks at somebody and hides back.)

INTELLECTUAL MAN You see, this maniac has come again.

ONE MORE He is not, and you know it well.

INTELLECTUAL MAN I just know only that he is not a maniac because he doesn't rape women. But I also know that he hides here daily at the same time and reasons it by his attention to another half of mankind, and you should agree that it looks like a mania.

NON-INTELLECTUAL MAN (from behind the bush). You better look at yourself, you philosophers!

(Intellectual Man and One More look at themselves.)

INTELLECTUAL MAN I see nothing new.

ONE MORE Me too.

INTELLECTUAL MAN We don't have a mirror, but we can look at each other.

ONE MORE (to Non-Intellectual Man). You have truly defined our profession.

INTELLECTUAL MAN But we live here nothing to disturb us.

ONE MORE And nobody.

INTELLECTUAL MAN And any strange commentaries are completely unnecessary.

NON-INTELLECTUAL MAN (from behind the bush). You'll feel them later.

INTELLECTUAL MAN He says the same things every day. We shouldn't attend to him.

ONE MORE Yes, all the more that you daily say that we shouldn't attend to him.

INTELLECTUAL MAN Yes, all the more that you daily attend to the daily nature of this my offer.

ONE MORE Yes, all the more that...

INTELLECTUAL MAN We better stop here.

ONE MORE As we do it every day.

INTELLECTUAL MAN As we do it every day.

NON-INTELLECTUAL MAN (from behind the bush). Listen, if you want to hang, you are... (Slow black-out. Light is slowly on with increasing chaos of car hooting. A street bench. A bus faces the audience. Driver's place is empty. The First and Second Women sit inside. They have gloves and hats. The First Woman is thick and small, The Second Woman is thin and tall. Girl With Watch sits so that the audience can see her back only. Sometimes she impatiently looks at her watch. Sleeping Girl sleeps in the bus. She has a small balloon. Long pause.)

FIRST WOMAN What a traffic jam!

SECOND WOMAN Oh, yes...

FIRST WOMAN Sorry, may I ask you?

SECOND WOMAN You are welcome.

FIRST WOMAN Where have you bought your nice shoes?

SECOND WOMAN Oh, I don't know.

FIRST WOMAN Do you mean you have forgotten?

SECOND WOMAN I didn't buy them.

FIRST WOMAN Oh, yes, I see...

SECOND WOMAN Do you like them?

FIRST WOMAN I like the heels first of all.

SECOND WOMAN I like yours more.

FIRST WOMAN Let's exchange?

SECOND WOMAN What a good idea! (They exchange their shoes.)

FIRST WOMAN How is it?

SECOND WOMAN It is great, and I like your gloves too!

FIRST WOMAN Really? Let's do the same with our gloves.

SECOND WOMAN You are genius! (They exchange their gloves.)

FIRST WOMAN How is it?

SECOND WOMAN It is great. (Pause.) Your hat... it is... so...

FIRST WOMAN Do you like it? Let us do what I want to offer. (They take off their hats.)

SECOND WOMAN (looking at First Woman's head). Stop!

FIRST WOMAN Do you like it too?

SECOND WOMAN Yeah, yeah, yeah!

FIRST WOMAN Just do it! (They exchange their hats and wigs. Pause. They gently look at each other.)

SECOND WOMAN Your head looks so well in my hat and wig.

FIRST WOMAN Your feet look so subtle in my shoes.

SECOND WOMAN You are so tall.

FIRST WOMAN You are so small.

SECOND WOMAN Thank you.

FIRST WOMAN Thank you too.

FIRST WOMAN AND SECOND WOMAN (happily). What a traffic jam!

(A running man's steps sound. Black-out. Light. Intellectual Man and One More on the trees.)

NON-INTELLECTUAL MAN (continuing his interrupted speech). ....welcome, but shut up, eh?

INTELLECTUAL MAN (neglecting Non-Intellectual Man, to One More). I should note you were wrong to a certain extent.

ONE MORE By the way, you too.

INTELLECTUAL MAN Really? What do you mean?

ONE MORE No-no, you first.

INTELLECTUAL MAN You are very nice. But later I'll surely listen to you.

ONE MORE Of course! Nobody and nothing will disturb me not to deprive you of an opportunity to know my opinion.

INTELLECTUAL MAN If you prove similarity, you shouldn't distinguish between "nobody" and "nothing".

ONE MORE Why to exaggerate so much?

INTELLECTUAL MAN Who will need us if we don't?

ONE MORE Who needs us now?

INTELLECTUAL MAN But what about tasty remains of sandwiches and cakes brought by our old women fans?

ONE MORE They come to sit on these benches and to read detective stories that's why they want us to shut up.

INTELLECTUAL MAN I shall think on it.

ONE MORE It is nothing to think unlike your recent intention to look at me instead of yourself that completely refuses your refusal of any similarity.

NON-INTELLECTUAL MAN (standing from behind the bush). You both are the same for me! (Runs away.)

ONE MORE You see, he has left again.

INTELLECTUAL MAN What if he is back?

ONE MORE He will be tomorrow.

INTELLECTUAL MAN What if he is back today?

ONE MORE And what?

INTELLECTUAL MAN And he will try to cease our hanging.

ONE MORE What do you mean?

INTELLECTUAL MAN To violate the relative balance.

ONE MORE What do you mean?

INTELLECTUAL MAN To make us to fall down.

ONE MORE Do you mean he will shoot?

INTELLECTUAL MAN Why not? But you shouldn't be afraid of it as balance is based on similarity which is absolute for you.

ONE MORE But you can't hang as you don't believe.

INTELLECTUAL MAN But I said balance is relative. That's why people now hang now fall down.

ONE MORE We shall throw bananas of syllogisms and coconuts of paradoxes before he can sight.

INTELLECTUAL MAN Be closer to the ground, acorns are the only fruit we have.

ONE MORE We shouldn't spent acorns because old women don't come daily. As to balance it is generally eternal. If we fall down, someone will surely... (looking at Non-Intellectual Man coming with a sling) surely...

(Non-Intellectual Man slings. Quick blackout. Load gun report. Light is quickly on. The flat of Woman Of 50. The stairs on the backstage. Young Man is ahead squeezing his ears with his hands and waits. He lowers his hands carefully looking at the door. Woman Of 50 opens the door and comes. She throws the gun on the floor. Young Man is horrified.)

WOMAN OF 50 So what?

YOUNG MAN You've...

WOMAN OF 50 I've done it for you.

YOUNG MAN (automatically). You've done it for me.

WOMAN OF 50 I only want you to be happy.

YOUNG MAN Happy?

WOMAN OF 50 I've done it because you may be happy only with me.

(Long pause.)

YOUNG MAN Are you really right?

WOMAN OF 50 Was I ever wrong?

YOUNG MAN How could you get it?

WOMAN OF 50 What? The gun?... I am stronger and younger than you think.

YOUNG MAN Poor baby...

WOMAN OF 50 You had to think on it before.

YOUNG MAN It's terrible... Mom, help me...

WOMAN OF 50 My poor son, come to me. (She wants to embrace him.)

YOUNG MAN Thank you... I didn't know what you are...

(Woman Of 50 embracing Young Man with one hand wipes off her make-up - she is Young Man's Girl. She takes a step back, and Young Man is terrified with this transformation. She pushes him, and he falls down.)

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL You didn't know who I am...

YOUNG MAN How... how could you make fun of me so much!

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL Yes, I've made fun of you.

YOUNG MAN I was about heart attack.

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL Your heart is strong.

YOUNG MAN (stands up). All's well which ends well. (Trying to embrace Young Man's Girl, but she pushes him again, and he falls down one more time. He stands up.) O.K., you are right, but we musn't lose time, we must hide her...

YOUNG MAN'S GIRL A good son must bury his mother himself.

(Exits. Pause. Young Man looks up. Long pause. He imitates some previous situations with calm hysteria.) Go. (Goes upstairs instead of Young Man's Girl with an imagined gun in his hand. He can't force himself to open the door, goes down, and expresses his own waiting. Repeats the same several times with the "go" word every time more and more nervously. Being tired he sits down on the floor.) If only someone shot me... (He looks at the gun and startles. He carefully takes it. Impulsively shoots to different sides as if he runs from his own hand so that the audience can see his side or back. One of bullets breaks the mirror. He faces the audience and shoots. A broken glass sounds. Quick blackout. Light is quickly on. The initial decoration of the performance. The window with the city landscape is broken. He tries to shoot again, but the gun doesn't shoot. He throws gun on the floor and exits. Blackout. Light. The park. Intellectual Man and One More sit on the ground and count their bruises.)

ONE MORE Hitting the belt. What can I do now?

INTELLECTUAL MAN (looking at his torn belt). What can we do?

ONE MORE What will be tomorrow as this day is not like all the past ours?

INTELLECTUAL MAN You've just forgotten your past. May be it is a hint for us to return to civilization.

ONE MORE To civilization?

INTELLECTUAL MAN I would like to bathe and to change my clothes.

ONE MORE Oh, yes... INTELLECTUAL MAN You said that balance is eternal, so, by your logic, someone will surely take our place. (A rustle sounds behind them. They turn back. Intellectual Man with hesitation.) It is too fast. (A red belt is seen behind the foliage, someone hidden by trees hangs it on a bough.)

ONE MORE What is he doing?

INTELLECTUAL MAN I have the same question.

ONE MORE We should ask him.

INTELLECTUAL MAN I think I know. (Shouts.) Stop it!

(Both throw themselves to the supposed suicide. Blackout. Light. The same flat. Stairs on the backstage. Enters Young Man's Girl in Woman Of 50's gown. She indifferently looks at the imagined window with broken glass and at other traces of shots (the mirror and so on). She wipes off her make-up and takes off her wig - it is Woman Of 50. She slowly goes upstairs, enters the room, and closes the door. Quick blackout and light again. The window with broken glass on the backstage. The bus inflated as a balloon appears behind the window. Only its lower part (below windows) is seen. The bus starts and flies to the horizon so that it is not seen whether something or somebody is inside. Blackout. The bus is on the ground in its initial position. Running man's steps sound. Enters Non-Intellectual Man. He approaches to the bus, opens the driver cabin, takes the sling from under the seat, and runs away.)

SECOND WOMAN Your hands are so pretty.

FIRST WOMAN Let's exchange.

SECOND WOMAN What do you mean?

FIRST WOMAN I just mean what I say.

SECOND WOMAN I didn't even suppose, it would be great, thank you very much!

FIRST WOMAN But...

SECOND WOMAN What? Please say, don't shy.

FIRST WOMAN I would like to be as thin and tall as you are.

SECOND WOMAN As to me, I am also bored to be as I am.

FIRST WOMAN So let's do it!

SECOND WOMAN Great! And don't forget about clothes; my gown will be too narrow for my new body.

FIRST WOMAN You know, let's exchange everything but heads.

SECOND WOMAN Did I say you are genius?

FIRST WOMAN It seems you did. This traffic jam seems to be long, let's go to that entrance.

(Exit both. Girl With Watch stands up, leaves the bus, and faces the audience. It is Young Man's Girl in a gown relevant to her age. She looks at her watch, hesitates forcing herself to make a step in one direction and stopping at once. Several far reports come from the same direction. Sleeping Girl's balloon bursts. Sleeping Girl remains sleeping. Girl With Watch startles and quickly goes in the contrary direction. The bus as if being inflated slowly goes up and flies away. Enter First and Second Women. They have exchanged everything but heads. They look at the flying bus, but are not surprised too much. They look at each other, exit again, and re-enter in 10 seconds as they were at their first appearance of the performance. They look at each other and sit down on a street bench. They start a dialogue identical with their first one. Non-Intellectual Man hiding behind a tree looks at them. Parallel blackout and increase of urban noise to the extent of rattle drowning the speech. At once the rattle is off.)
