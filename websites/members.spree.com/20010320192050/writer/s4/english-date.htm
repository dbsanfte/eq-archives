 Dating Yourself
© Regena English

Today we're going to talk about dating. Whaaaat a spinster talking about dating? Yes dating! When was the last time you dated yourself? If you're like many women you don't step one foot out on Saturday night without a date. That's really sad when you think about it. I can remember when I was sixteen years old my friends and I would stay home on the weekend when we were dateless. It was very important to not be seen out and about alone.

The only times it were safe to be seen without a boyfriend during the weekend was when all the "dateless" girls gathered. Yep, we were a "pack" of giggly girls searching for potential boyfriends everywhere we went. Mind you, I had some of the best times as a teenager with my "pack" of "dateless" friends. That was then and this is now. Well it's time to let go of the mentality we need someone at our side in order to enjoy our hard earned weekends. After working forty or more hours per week our weekends should be carefree and restful.

We're now mature, vibrant, and fun loving women who don't need someone to hold our hands before we will allow ourselves to experience the pleasure of our own company. So you don't know how to date yourself? Let me tell you about my dates, yeeessss with myself. My ideal date would start with a long pampering bath in some of the best smelling gardenia bubble bath on the market. On date days I usually feel ultra feminine so I dress accordingly. I don't take any shortcuts when dressing for a date with myself. If it took me an hour and a half to dress for a man it would most certainly take me two hours for myself.

Yes I do primp and spin in front of the mirror. Why? I want to look DELICIOUS to myself. Ok I'm a bit weird but lets not stray from the theme of this article. Next, the evening starts with me attending a play, usually a comedy. I prefer comedies because I spend my days being serious and could use some hearty laughs by the weekend. Afterwards, I would stop at a nice sit down restaurant. My restaurant choices depends on what I feel like eating at that moment. Chili's, Bennigan's, Ruth's Seafood, or Bonnie's Steak House one of Houston's best steak restaurants. No McDonald's or drive thrus for me on this night. Why?

It wouldn't be something I would tolerate on a "real" date with a man so why should I settle when dating myself? The key to dating yourself is the same as if you were dating another person, to have a good time. Don't stress yourself with concerns as to who's looking at you or what they must be thinking about you dining alone. Relax! Who said you can't have fun by yourself, it sure wasn't me. Now I know there has to be another purpose for this article besides dating yourself?

Yes. Our preoccupation with the opinions of others. Dating yourself forces you to face those criticisms you think others are making about you. It's when you face them you realize it was all in your mind and not what people were actually saying. When you can handle your own pessimism about not having a mate you'll be able to triumph in dealing with the harsh judgments from others. Ask any woman what her ten worst fears would be and being alone in public would surely be among them.

So many women are fearful of being without a date they would rank it as a tie with having their dresses stuck in their pantyhoses in public. Ladies, take chances and experience new things. So what if you have to experience them alone. What truly matters is getting the most enriching experiences from life that's possible. Don't be the dying person saying what-ifs and should-haves, life waits for no one so LIVE NOW!




Regena English at leatherspinsters2@mailcity.com a leather spinster, editor, conference organizer, and the inspiration behind the creation of the web's first publications dedicated enitrely to happily unmarried careerwomen.

The Leather Spinsters Newsletter at http://www.leatherspinsters.com/archives.html and Leather Spinsters on the Web Ezine at http://www.leatherspinsters.com/ezine.html were both created primarily to provide emotional, physical, and spiritual support to unmarried careerwomen over the age of thirty, those women who are at peace with their choice to live without mates. Secondarily, Miss English is committed to shattering the myths surrounding spinsterhood, asexuality, and what most single women really want from life.
