 Have We Grown Up Yet?
© Pamela Geiss

When you were a little tot, the first time you got close to the stove or the hot water tap, your Mother probably said, "No,no. Hot." And you looked at her and then touched it. Why? Because you didn't know what the word "hot" meant. However, AFTER you touched it, you had a pretty good idea. Chances are you didn't touch anything hot for at least a week or so. But, maybe a month later, you might have done it again if you had forgotten. Usually that's all it takes. Then you grew up.

Or did you? How are you spending your time marketing your product(s) on the Internet? Do you ever find yourself doing the same thing the same way, and getting the same results? If the results are good, then you are doing something right. But if the results are small or negligible, why are you continuing to use the same methods? Is it because you don't know any other way? Or is it because you're a creature of habit?

If you're a creature of habit, you need to change your ways. The Internet is changing daily. In order to be competitive, businesses must be aware of the changes and have the capacity to change with them. If you don't, you will surely be left behind. This holds true even if what you are doing is working now. You need to keep up with your statistics. If your orders go down, you need to "tweak" your system until you find what works. What works today may not work at all tomorrow.

If you're not a creature of habit but just don't know what else to do, you need to find help. There are all sorts of free ebooks, informative sites and other successful business owners out there that will be happy to advise you. For the most part, business owners remember what it's like to have "been there". Everyone started out at one time with nothing. Everyone also started out with no computer knowledge or Internet knowledge. I will readily admit that if it hadn't been for some good souls on the Internet giving me advice, I would probably be doing something else right now. I believe we must give what we have received. It's in sharing with each other that we all become richer - not just in money but in knowledge. And knowledge is power.

So, the next time you get ready to promote your product or business, think about your results the last time and see if you might need a little "tweak". Don't keep burning yourself on that stove!
