 It is not only a matter of Intelligence
© Paul Chika Emekwulu

In the fall of 1996, as I was signing my books at Waldenbooks in Shawnee, Oklahoma, a woman in her fifties (I guessed) walked up to me and asked me, "Why should I buy your book?". "Because it will tell you how to publish your your won book, because the New York Giants will not do it for you, I replied.

I told her my story ( I don't know how many times now) of how I submitted one of my books to these giants all to not avail, but when I published it myself, it was adopted by the school system.

"I don't have the intelligence to get my books into the school system," she continued.

"It is not only a matter of intelligence. It is also about having a message. It is also about having a viewpoint, a viewpoint you believe in, and excited about and of course, want to share with others."

Elementary, middle, junior and senior high school teachers are searching for answers. They are looking for solutions to problems posed by single family homes throughout the United States, solutions to problems posed by gang membership among the inner-city youths etc.

There is an ongoing national and local dialogues on race relations. Do you have an opinion? What's your perspective? Teachers are looking for solutions to problems associated with understanding certain concepts not only in mathematics, but also in other school subjects. They are looking for anything that could help, anything that could offer some solutions, not necessarily complete solutions.

Those in higher education are looking for solutions through research, through programs on multiculturalism. Because of this, they are looking for resources. May be your book is what they are all waiting for. Maybe your perspective is what they want to listen to. If yes, why can't your perspective be expressed in a book form? Can you help? If yes, how?

It is not only a matter of intelligence. It is also a matter of having a viewpoint, not only a viewpoint but a consumable one.
