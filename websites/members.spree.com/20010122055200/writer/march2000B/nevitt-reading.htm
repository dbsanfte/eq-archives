 The Inward Reading
© Cicely M. Nevitt 
You read it and it plunges to your depths. 
You read it and it is oxygen for your fire.
Never has it been this opportune.
Endlessly the cycle catches light.
The tacit essence in what is written
Is an intrinsic trickster. All words
Are gathered like precious talismans. 

When I heard my voice, it was alien.
That ship that precedes has been too far
Ahead for too long and we've only written
Letters. There, on that first reading, you 
Let go of your voice and it toddles along
Like a child and her first few steps.

You read it and you wring your hands.
You read it with heavy lidded eyes.
Never has it been this intimidating and
The chimes are ringing in the throated
Strings of countless poets playing music
For the ear.  

That one, he's a beatnik. 
This next one is a performer.  
Right here is a circle in a square peg. 
Such sense it bore and
Such absolute contours when dropped
Like a tear from the mind's eye. 
The words pebbled out on the sleeping
Water with sequential tranquility. 

You've got a lot to learn but plans are
Hatching in the restless nest of the throat. 
There's good work to be hovered over by
Those who were lending libations while you weren't
Watching. Honored promises are more pressing.  
There are times woken desirous of solidity in unattainable
concepts: talent, perfection, ease, consistency.    

Talent is the palpable effort of caring. Her real
Name is work. She is both fragile and resolute. 
Ashen eyes of doubt and the red knots of frustration 
Allude the celebration within the tangled empty.
When you get there you will keep on going.
What will be given is the pure discernment of 
Companion souls to those just beyond, listening 
To that deafening echo of the inward reading.
