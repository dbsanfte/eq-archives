Hazel Edwards Provoked by experience, the YA thriller "Stalker'(Lothian) offers a different challenge from Hazel Edwards' cake-eating hippo character who turned 21 recently. Books co-authored with psychologist Dr Helen Mc Grath include 'Creative Kaleidoscope',(Horwitz), 'Friends' (Choice) and the forthcoming 'Difficult Personalities'(Choice) Short fiction such as 'A Hairy Question' and Kalo Li's New Country' (Blake's Sparklers)are amongst her 130 publications. Avraham Fox I am a University of Wisconsin-Madison student that studies abroad currently at Jerusalem, Israel. While abroad, I am a freelance writer whose essays were published in the Madison Capital Times and the Oshkosh Northwestern. I also write periodically as a guest columnist for the Daily Cardinal; a University of Wisconsin-Madison newspaper.
I studied creative modes writing with University of Wisconsin emeritus Dr. Lee DiSalvo. She inspires me to think outside the conventional box of thought. Some of my poetry was published, and awarded merit within mainstream poetry contests.

Jay Frankston Jay Frankston was raised in Paris, and came to the U.S. in 1942. He became a lawyer and practiced in New York for 20 years reaching the top of his profession and writing at the same time. In 1972 he gave up law and New York and moved to California where he became a college instructor. He is the nationally published author of several books some of which have been condensed in Reader's Digest and translated into 15 languages.
