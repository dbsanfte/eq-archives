Trudi Blue T I was born in Koln, Germany. My family left when I was a very young child. We ventured on, across the Atlantic on a huge ship - then traveled on to British Colombia, Canada by the railways. My passion for nature & the animal kingdom stem from my daily walks into the deep forest behind our house ( log cabin ). I frequently interacted with the Black Bear & all the Winged Ones. I found an abundance of serenity in the deep woods. I also frequently visited the Indian Reservation near by. My beliefs are closest to the Lakota, for this tribe has the upmost reverance for our Mother, the Earth, the Animal kingdom, the Elderly, the Children, and the Elements. I believe in simplicity and what truly dwells inside the heart and soul....the eyes have it! My first love is Music....I find music to be very inspirational for my writing And illustration. The Arts are very important to me. The freedom of expession is a priority of mine. The material rewards are not that important. I believe in creating, doing, being, living ART! The creation of art itself is the reward and being able to share it with others. My writing usually takes on a BEAT vibe... the Beat Poets have grabbed me, also the unique poetry of E. Poe and Blake. There are many artists that have inspired me and continue to do so. I have felt ART flowing through my veins for many years and it is a gift to me that I may share it with you.........so let us do some jazz and beat...enjoy the Blues beat.......read like jazz and drift on out peoples!

Gregg Bridgeman born the son of an Army officer and traveled all over North America and Europe growing up. He attended Berea College and North Carolina State University before deploying as an activated Army Reservist to participate in Operation Desert Shield/Storm. For the next two years, Gregg served as a Sergeant in a Special Operations capacity in Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Kuwait, and Turkey. Since returning home in 1992, he has worked for IBM, GTE, Knowledge Source, and ADS Consulting. Gregg writes mostly science fiction but occasionally dabbles in other genres. His poetry has been published in such places as A Writer's Choice Literary Journal Online, Inkwell, Peer Amid, and the X-er's, In Our Own Voice poetry anthology. He wrote a series of articles entitled, The Elements of Formal Style, which he hopes to compile and publish as a short instructional book someday. "There are a lot of books and manuals available to help new authors, but few of them really motivate and none really address the Formal Style of writing, which appears to be going the way of the dinosaur in today's literature." Today, Gregg travels all over North America as a certified computer consultant and instructor. Currently, he dedicates all of his spare time to completing his science fiction novel, The Dama Project, and editing the work of another mainstream author.
Scott Brining I live in Louisville KY, but originally hail from Cleveland OH. I am 26 and have only in the past year re-discovered writing poetry. I belong to the Louisville Poets' Guild, which is a free poetry group which contains many talented and decidedly different styles of poets. I also frequent a local open mic called the Java House, which showcases local poets in a very intimate setting, however it usually draws a huge crowd. I write mainly for myself, as a release of whatever thoughts i may be having at the time. However, the forum of poetry allows me a semi-safe way of essentially, sharing my diary with the rest of the world! I am most fond of using what i call 'visual poetry', which to me means, trying to paint the most vivid poem that i can, for the particular subject that i happen to be writing about. I also enjoy dabbling in photography. i participte in local theater productions as well. in my free time i enjoy learning new software applications (i am designing a web page), mountain biking, camping and hiking.

Lida Broadhurst Ever since I was in high school writing poetry & prose have been important in my life, but at the same time I did not always want to share my thoughts. After my husband and I retired, I felt more confident and able to send my thoughts on cats, kids, love, and city life out to magazines. A few years ago, I began writing darker work about odd twists on myths or the monsters who inhabit our world. Now I just accept whatever thoughts arise at midnight, be they humorous, warm, loving, or horrific. I am grateful that almost 150 pieces of my work have been printed in publications like "Zuzu's Petals", "Tight", "The Orphic Chronicle", "Star*Line", "69 Flavors of Paranoia", & many other small press magazines. This year is the first time one of my poems received a Rhysling nomination.

Jeff Brown was born in Arlington, Texas in 1973. At the age of eight, he began writing short stories in a backyard tool shed and, by twelve, had completed two collections of short stories as well as several novels and screenplays. Jeff graduated from Baylor University in Waco shortly after the Branch Davidian crisis for which that area is notorious. In 1996, Jeff's first book of poetry, Slow Christmas, was published by Brownflower Press. Later that same year, Jeff moved with his wife to Cambridge, Massachusetts where he founded a poetry workshop, published his second collection of poems (An Exciting Wonderful), and began to consider himself a "new surrealist". In June 1998 Jeff moved back to Texas to take up residence in Austin where he recently completed work on his third book of poems, Lucifer Starfish, a book dealing with the nature of good and evil. Jeff has spent the last six months working as an elementary school art teacher in the Austin Independent School District and preparing his fourth collection of poems, Crutch-Makers And Insects for publication some time this fall. Jeff's poems have appeared in various publications as well as several online forums such as Taverner's Koans, Spoken War, A Writer's Choice Literary Journal, and PIF Online. In June of 1997, Jeff was featured in real audio at the Ann Arbor Poetry Forum, and he is currently being featured (also in real audio) at Audionet Books.

Salvatore Amico M. Buttaci currently is an English teacher in a Garfield, NJ, middle school and adjunct English professor at Bergen Community College. In addition to English degrees, he holds an MBA in Marketing and Management. From 1974 to 1988, he was the Editor of NEW WORLDS UNLIMITED, an annual poetry anthology that showcased the poems of aspiring and professional poets from here and abroad. From 1995 to 1997 he was the Editor of POETIDINGS, the newsletter of the New Jersey Poetry Society, Inc. Currently he is Children's Editor of poetrymagazine.com. His work has appeared in THE WRITER, CHRISTIAN SCIENCE MONITOR, WRITER'S JOURNAL, FEELINGS, CATS MAGAZINE, AQUARIAN WEEKLY, as well as on Internet publications. He has won numerous awards for his poetry, including the 1995 $250 Empress Award. Four of his songs, which he wrote with his brother Alfonso, were in a 1989 movie FORTRESS OF AMERIKKKA. For a copy of his newly published book A FAMILY OF SICILIANS: STORIES AND POEMS, send $15.95 check or money order payable to Salvatore Buttaci, P.O. Box 887, Saddle Brook, NJ 07663-0887.
