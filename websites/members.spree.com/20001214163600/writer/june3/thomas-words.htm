 THE POWER OF WORDS TO CREATE

© Mary Anne Thomas

For almost two thousand years, philosophers have known that our thoughts create everything in our physical world. In the 3rd century AD, Plotinus told us: "Ultimate reality consists of forms; what exists is ultimately mental. Thus for something to be created is for it to be thought." Plotinus's teachings influenced the two greatest Christian philosophers, St. Augustine and St. Thomas Aquinas, and in the view of Christian theologians Plotinus was responsible for the astounding growth of Christianity.

Over the centuries, spiritual students took Plotinus's teachings and Christian theology to heart -- and have experimented with ways to focus on specific forms. Most recently, we have tried our hand at creating form "intentionally"; by "intentionally" focusing on the thoughts, ideas and material objects we want to bring into reality. A problem quickly occurred:

Sometimes focusing works; sometimes it doesn't

Why? New insights from quantum theorists (our modern philosophers) tell us:

Focus must be held for a specific length of time so that the atoms behind thought can create a mass

Now, a new technique allows you to achieve this crucial step -- to hold your focus long enough for the atoms behind your thoughts to move . . . to attract similar thoughts, to create mass, and to become visible in physical form.

Grab a dictionary.

Let's look up the following words, one after another, and read their dictionary meanings. Online dictionaries are best because they allow you to hold your focus longer and with more precision. I'm using the AOL version for this exercise -- the Merriam-Webster.


1) Fulfill
2) Attain
3) Fruition
4) Accomplish
5) Realize
6) Manifest
7) Deliver

Fulfill: to make full; to put into effect; to execute; to bring to an end; to satisfy; to convert into reality; to develop the full potentialities of

Attain: to reach as an end; to gain; to achieve; to come into possession of; to obtain; to come to as the end of a progression or course of movement; to come or arrive by motion, growth or effort

Fruition: pleasurable use or possession; enjoyment; the state of bearing fruit, realization

Accomplish: to bring about; to bring to completion; to fulfill; to succeed in reaching a stage in progression; perfect

Realize: to bring into concrete existence; accomplish; to cause to seem real; to appear real; to convert into actual assets; to bring or get; to conceive vividly as real, to be fully aware of

Manifest: readily perceived by the senses and especially by the sight; easily understood or recognized by the mind; obvious; evident

Deliver: to set free; to aid in the birth of; to bring; to come through; to produce the promised, desired, or expected results

What happened when you read these dictionary meanings? Your feelings shifted, didn't they? They moved? That's because the atoms behind your thoughts moved . . . and are now forming a mass. Exciting, isn't it?

This exercise is called The Dictionary Game, and it has the power to:


1) Bring your wishes into the physical world
2) Break you out of any emotional prison
3) Bring satisfaction and gratification to any part of your life that contains "lack"
4) Reverse the motion of "frustration" --and turn it into "fulfillment"
5) Save us all from unnecessary heartache

In future issues of Mindzine, I'll show you how to use The Dictionary Game and make it one of your favorite, most powerful spiritual tools. I'll share stories from my life and from the lives of my students . . . as we all work to bring our dreams to life.
