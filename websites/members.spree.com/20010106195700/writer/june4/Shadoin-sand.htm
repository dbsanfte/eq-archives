 THE FLAT RIVER SANDPIT

© Phillip D. Shadoin

Not far from Rock Creek, on the Flat River, around 1930 a couple of smart fellas from the city decided to excavate large quantities of sand for the cement that made the new highways crossing the state. This sand dredging excavation was a successful business all the way through the early 60's when the interstate highway was completed then the need for that much sand in that part of the state dried up. The hole left by the excavating process did not dry up however. No, in fact it filled up. After 3 years, the sand pit reached a sort of stasis and was completely filled with clear water and soon after, young aquatic life. Some of the folks in town thought it would make a nice park and lake, much to the disappointment of the local teens who used it as a swimming hole and make-out place.

It made quite a nice park and lake. And the deep cool water was ideal for growing very large fish of all manner of species. Young children and old men fished the reeds and shallows for the sunfish and bluegill, adventurous young men fished the various points for largemouth bass, and wily stodgers fished the depths for lake trout and muskie. A very fine body of water indeed, and it did itself proud with the quality of its' fish.

Rock Creek has always had a generous supply of youths who enjoy the outdoors and the adventures to be had there. Even now, there are scads of boys who prefer real-live fishing over the hand-held computerized variety. These boys congregate at the old ball diamond each morning after breakfast and play baseball until the sun gets high and hot, then they jump on their fancy mountain and BMX bikes and peddle like heck over to the sandpit for a dip in the swimming area. Sufficiently cooled, they usually can scrounge up enough money for a big bag of nacho chips and a soda, which they consider fine luncheon fare, and then their free and daring spirit takes them on a daily adventure. They know enough to stay off the Rock Creek. The Locals would take turns blistering their hides if they caught the boys doing anything worse than jumping from the railroad trestle into the trestle pool or riding innertubes down the small rapids that connect the best trout spots. No, they tend to emigrate up the lesser creeks and the Flat River itself.

The Flat being in fact so flat, you can most often walk entirely across it and not get the top of your socks wet. There are far more interesting places for boys who haven't yet met puberty and have the hearts of conquerors. Muddy Creek is a great example. It cuts deeply through farmland, with its high banks and overhanging trees, it's a natural tunnel that through the whole summer will stay cool and shaded. And perfectly quiet and out of the way of parental supervision. It's the kind of place a girlie magazine stolen from an older brother or a cigarette stolen from a careless parent, can be shared amongst curious kids. And it is no surprise that the boys show equal false appreciation for both vices, not knowing what to make of a grown naked woman or a lit cigarette. It was quite natural then that the boys eventually tire of the hide and seek games, the dirt clod fights, and the games of ninja-war and TV wrestlers-who-takes-on-the-beings-from-outer-space. And when they tire of these noble pursuits, they find some trouble to get into.

It was the James boy who brought up the idea of trying to see a real-live girl naked, not some picture in a book. Little James was approaching puberty, as his changing voice and too-short pants legs would attest. The other boys, feeling similar stirrings in their hormones, agreed to take part, if a good plan could be developed. The ground rules of a good plan are simple: It has to work and it couldn't get them into trouble. So the boys thought and planned and eventually discarded all the ideas except one.

The idea they decided to use was to ambush a swimmer back at the sandpit and somehow remove her clothing. And in doing so, it had to look accidental, and somehow all of the boys had to be able to be on hand to reap the rewards. The girls who were already in college were immediately crossed off the list. They could hurt a guy if they wanted to. The high school girls were also eliminated because they had big scary boyfriends who would love to pummel some wise-guy punks for trying to mess with their girls. That left the girls who were their own ages, and already twice as far along the maturity scale as the perpetrators. They narrowed the choices down to the three girls they would most like to see bare and who they thought they could fend off in a fair fight. Now they had a target, they needed a method.

It was young James who thought up the idea if "fishing" for bikinis. Since fishing wasn't allowed in the swimming area, an accomplice would be needed. It was the Matthews boy who was selected to be the underwater counterpart. A thorough discussion followed. It would be too obvious if one of them reeled in the prize, so an dupe was going to be needed. The boys decided to reconnoiter the area and see if something might develop.

JoeHoehn was sleeping in his jonboat when the boys arrived back at the fishing area on the sandpit. And his jonboat wasn't too far from the swimming hole. Perfect. Now little Matthews was to swim out to JoeHoehn's boat, release the bail on the reel, grab the hook and tow it back in, then sort of hangout by the outer buoys until a victim got within his range of underwater swimming ability so he could swim over hook her suit and swim away without being detected.

Being detected would pretty much destroy the whole plan. So another participant would be needed to occupy the victim in a water fight or other disturbance so that she would be less likely to detect somebody hooking her suit, and hopefully not her skin. The James boy took upon that task with a boy named DP.

The luck of the young put all elements in place for them so soon that they didn't have any time to chicken out. Matthews swam out and released the bail without JoeHoehn even stirring. He then took off JoeHoehn's bobber and bait and wrapper the line around a small stick to tow it by. It wouldn't do if JoeHoehn woke early and upon discovering his bobber missing, set the hook on young Matthews.

DP and the James kid began to "herd" Sissy Taylor towards deeper water by getting into a furious water fight. Sissy was only trying to avoid drowning as she waded deeper and deeper, the two boys constantly maneuvering to keep her from returning safely to shore. Matthews was successful in hooking Sissy's flowered suit without her detection. Another case of blind luck. He surfaced near the two combatants and they suddenly decided the water fight was over in a fit of nervous giggling. Now if JoeHoehn would wake up!

Caleb, another of our youthful delinquents, shouted from his shoreline position, "Hey JoeHoehn, HOWSTHEFISHIN?!"

This of course stirred JoeHoehn, and he immediately noted the absence of his bobber and began to reel, not even noting the open bail. When the slack was reel in, JoeHoehn had a helluva fight on his hands. He was absolutely sure he had hooked a 30, no 40, no 50, NO 60 POUND MUSKIE! He was going to be famous. He reeled for all he was worth. Missy Taylor felt a tug at her bikini bottoms and immediately she wheeled to see which little dweezil was going to "get it!" But she found it hard to wheel around. She even found it hard to walk. In fact, she was slipping a little deeper into the water. She panicked and yelled for help. At this point, the boys knew the plan had gone awry and their best option was to quietly slip away. But being young boys, they had to see it through. This was the most exciting thing in Rock Creek at the moment, and they want to be there to witness it.

JoeHoehn and Missy were at an impasse of sorts when JoeHoehn noticed his boat was drifting closer to the swimming area. Shouts from the bathers to warn him only got back his assurances that a state record muskie was going to be landed here. Today. NOW if he could help it.

Missy could still feel something powerful pulling at her bottoms, but she didn't dare let go of the sides to reach back and determine the culprit. Now Missy's friends get involved. They answer her pleas for help, not at first knowing what the problem is. Missy was reluctant to shout it, so precious seconds were wasted while the rescuers "come HERE!"

Upon hearing the dilemma their friend is in, they immediately determine the cause. JoeHoehn is barreling down on his prize as fast as he can reel. The rescuers immediately wheel around to face JoeHoehn and unleash the full fury of twelve year old womanhood. JoeHoehn is taken aback. Enough so the rescuers are able to get Missy out of her bikini bottoms. They fly out of the water and nearly slap JoeHoehn as they whiz by his head.

The full stages of shock, realization, and humiliation cross JoeHoehn's visage in seconds. He is far more embarrassed than the young girl, fighting furiously to get the hook out so that they can be returned to the rightful owner.

Missy quickly replaces her bottoms and strides out of the swimming area, clutching the sides to keep them in place. Back at her bag, she slithers into her shorts and T-shirt and goes straight home for a hot bath and comfort from her thoroughly confused mother.

JoeHoehn immediately paddles out of the swimming area and heads to the boat ramp to get the heck out of there. It is a full week before he ventures into Clem's feed store and the full scale razzing he knew all along he had coming.

The culprits got away with their plan for exactly three days. Then the brag bug got the best of them and in telling the story to boys who weren't there, they were overheard. All of the parents were notified and duly chagrined. No one saw much of those boys for several weeks. And when they did, they were usually hard at work mowing or painting or stacking wood.

Little Matthews mastered the art of riding his bike standing up. He had plenty of practice. He also had no desire whatsoever to sit down for more than a week.
