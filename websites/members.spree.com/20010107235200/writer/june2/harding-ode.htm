 ode to samuel barber's adagio for strings
Jay T. Harding 

vinyl tiling the dining room on a winter slippery saturday
reveals a creviced square piece of wood, which discloses upon prying
an old heater vent leading into the basement, just above the furnace,
and I stare straight down into it for the longest while,
imagining a chute curved toward the washer, weaved 'tween  existing 
metal tubes that bring blessed warmth through the home
of the brave, self-doers armed with 'do-it-yourself' pamphlets, 
a curious mixture of instruments new and old: some broken beyond their worth
yet still stashed away in the pregnant toolbox like a place of honor
for giving their lives for us, and, of course, vision.
just in front of the cumbrous upright piano -
it will squat there with its broad back against the kitchen wall
until the rest of the room is done, 
then herniatingly rolled onto a placed patch of carpet before finishing the floor 
then somehow, by some miracle, get it back - 
but, just before this behemoth seeped
the unmistakable pungency of cat piss, deep into the thick-planked floor, 
loosed one black night of lazy urgency 
undiscovered before now, and as deb
patiently fills up a rectangle tupperware bin with hot, soapy water,
and sops the violated area to hopefully draw it up and out, 
her legs straight out in a "V" - 
i can't do that; its indian style or a stool for these legs used to bending-
as she treats the soaked wood, a rare silence swells between selections
on the tape, and as i lay on the floor, 
my head close to the bowling ball sized hole between levels,
the strangest thought came over me, twisting a line from some movie:
"clark kent can't be superman, you fool!
superman doesn't wear glasses!"
and i laugh outloud, the sound bouncing off basement walls
like someone else's timbre from far away,
then adagio for strings begins its subtle stirrings
in answer.
the stereo's volume rests on eight to combat, 
the whirring blowdrying of a now soaked floor,
the psychotic dogs that bark at every outside twigsnap, and
the boy-man's wrestling video blasting insults out open windows upstairs,
and the violin's familiar lofty flight drives out all distractions
as it steals my willing heart, 
dancing with it, quivering to match my own
as its notes ascend to celestial heights, 
teasingly, 
caressing soul's cheek 
like a nipple answering a hungry mouth's nudgings,
pulling me along, a willing slave
to heaven's own door
where the trembling notes linger
forever . . . . . . .
before tumbling down again into silenced clover
with muffled sighs bringing me back
unwillingly to the hardwood floor,
only to be nudged upward again
like a blazed-winged phoenix from the ashes of my tattered mind,
fanning the fire so slowly ghostly smoke turns the final refrain
into a cemetery scene,
leaving me prostate, staring at the crypted din below,
wishing i could telekinese the repeat button
without moving from this prayer spot
to allow adagio to take me out of myself again 
as it always does,
stringing me home like a traveler,
taking the rope bridged way back
over chasms of echoed laughter.
