ABOUT POETRY
Leslie Blanchard
Copyright Leslie Blanchard


Poetry is the language of the soul. It encourages each of us, writer and
reader alike, to be true to the depths of our beings. It guides us to the
places where the goddess resides in our psyche.

Although there generally is little financial reward to having poetry
published, the reward of seeing our own truths and deepest form of
expression in print is often enough for many of us. I believe these
remunereations are totally significant and worthwhile.

There is a poet inside us all, a visionary whose voice deserves an outlet.
A soul whose truth needs to be validated, appreciated, and enjoyed by
others.

ABOUT POETRY

In the academic world there are many debates, many arguments, and many
viewpoints as to what makes a piece of work "poetry". If you investigate,
you'll find that there are as many different definitions of poetry as there
are definers. At a Writer's Choice we have our own criteria as to what
defines poetry.

Meter and rhyme, or the lack thereof, do not determine for us what is or
is not a "good" poem. We believe that poetry cannot be evaluated totally
on the basis of its form, pattern, or even technique, even though all of
these components are important in composing and evaluating it.

We are a diverse lot, each of us truly unique in this world. We urge our
writers to listen to their inner voices, to develop, and celebrate that
uniqueness - to sing life as it is for you. Nurture your innermost
impulses; free yourself from the restrictions of specific forms and
techniques. Freeing yourself from these constraints will keep you out of
the ruts and will bring you to a higher understanding of what poetry is
and is not.

Just as a book is not made by its cover, poetry is not made by its form
or technique. The words are the real book, as they are the real poem.
Form and technique are merely outer wrappings for the essence of the poem.
True, if the wrapping doesn't fit quite right, the poem will suffer. But
in adopting this attitude,you will see that simply mastering the techniques
of form is not the same as mastering the art of writing poetry.

EVALUATING POETRY

The real strength of a poem lies in its metaphorical power. In the poem's
intrinsic ability to imply or suggest, rather than stating directly. A
strong poem should invoke emotion in the reader, it should communicate a
depth of feeling, a small hint of truth to the reader.

One question will lead to the true measure of a poem, no matter the style
or form - " How well is it said?" If what the poem has to say is muddled,
trite, vague, ungrammatical, or garbled, then the poem fails. If what it
has to say is conveyed in a pompous, precious, overly sentimental,
commonplace, or obsolete language, then the poem fails. The problem is
often grammatical. New poets tend to start at the wrong end of the
spectrum, striving to write great poetry before learning the basic elements
of grammar, sentence structure, punctuation, word usage, and spelling.

If a poem is a web of fragmented sentences, misspelled or misused words,
overworked adjectives, weak passive verbs; if it is oversupplied with
"so", "and", or it is cluttered with the decaying bones of dead language,
then it fails.

Learn the rules of grammar. Break them by choice - not out of ignorance.

Is It Worth Saying?? We all know that love can hurt, wound, mortify,
uplift, invigorate, turn lives around, overjoy, etc. We all know that
nature is overwhelming, beautiful, frightening, stirring, and so on. If
we examine ourselves honestly we will see that even truths like these are
no defense in the realm of poetry. Although truth is no defense for poetry,
poetry must deal with truth. We must tell it indirectly, figuratively,
metaphorically, symbolically . It must be overrun with imagination, born of
originality, and live in honesty.
