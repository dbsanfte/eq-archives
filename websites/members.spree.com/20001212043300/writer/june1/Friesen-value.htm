 The True Value of Financial Struggles
© Ruth Marlene Friesen

Ever notice how quickly we blame God when we have troubles we don't understand? On the other hand, if we credit Him with all the good things that happen to us, we gradually come to recognize that even our negative experiences are good gifts from God.

It is harder to tell while you are struggling in a situation, but look back to ones you had a few years ago. Didn't some good come out of that? Perhaps you had some illness or pain as a child that set you apart from other children, but during that time you read a lot and developed certain interests that are key to your present life.

Or, you had an ill parent and had to learn to be a caregiver for your siblings, and perhaps the parent too. Now you are a responsible adult who is not afraid to take on big jobs and see them through. Would you trade this for carefree youth if it meant you were a flighty, unreliable adult?

Maybe you were poor, but longed to give gifts so much that you struggled to learn some craft skills just to make special presents for birthdays and Christmas. Now you have reputation for your sewing and people eagerly open your gifts, knowing there will be something of your quality workmanship.

You get the picture? You could add examples now, right? Me too. Many of them. I had that sick parent and had to take responsibility. I taught myself to make teddy bears so I'd have a gift to take to the baby showers.

So - if you are trying to run an internet business and it is not prospering as it should, is God really bopping you on the head? Or, is He giving you opportunity to grow new skills and character traits? I believe He is!

There are the beatitudes of Matthew 5, and the fruit of the Spirit in Galatians 6; love, joy, peace, patience, gentleness, purity, faith, meekness (reverence), and self-control for starters. Though I've been learning lessons in all of these for most of my life, I'm still at it.

At the end of January 1999 I got a new computer and went on line with high hopes.I had to learn Windows 98, emailing, online movements, web page design, and now I've been adding marketing and ezine production too! But last May I had my wrist in a cast, my niece was here for the summer, and I had duties at church. I fell for a get-rich- quick scheme and was off on a tangent a while this winter. Sometimes I've wondered "Am I ever going to get this business to fly?" My Dad asks, "When are you going to make that heap of money?"

Yet by all those delays I have learned to do a higher quality of work, discovered the world of marketing and promotion, to be patient with the newbies - and myself, to discipline my time and some organization. Also to discern the shoddy and what is of better quality. Now I realize that a sudden big success would have made me a flop. I would not know how to cope with all that's with it. I started with faith in God for miracles, and now realize that He did me a great favor, allowing me these struggles. I have skills that form a good basis for success, and the knowledge to bring it in.

I'm trying to be humble too, but look out the miracles are going to be astonishing! Same for YOU. Give it time, and spend that time learning many things. Later your joy will be richer - for your success will be stronger and more lasting.




Ruth Marlene Friesen, Editor of A Journal of God's Provision - Digest.
