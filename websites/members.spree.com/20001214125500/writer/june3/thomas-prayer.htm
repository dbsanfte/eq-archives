 The Power of Creative Prayer

© Mary Anne Thomas

Why do some prayers work better than others do?

It's a question we've all asked. Some prayers make us soar while others leave us feeling lifeless. The words of the Magnificat are a good example. I hear "My soul doth magnify the Lord" and my heart makes a beeline for heaven. "Our father, who art in heaven," on the other hand, has always made me feel like a piece of concrete. Embarrassing, but true.

Do our experiences with prayer mean that some prayers work better than others do? Yes . . . and these experiences also reveal a secret about prayer no one else knows.

What is a prayer but a wish from the heart? Whether you say a prayer, release an intention, or visualize a desire . . . your wish asks the forces of creation to help you obtain something important to your happiness.

Have you noticed that no two wishes are alike? You might desire better health because your asthma has kicked up. I might want an increased income to expand my classes on manifestation. A woman who wishes to be a mother, and who has faced disappointment month after month, might cry out for help becoming pregnant.

If no two wishes are alike . . . how can two prayers be alike? They can't be . . . and this fact reveals the secret about prayer.

Prayers work differently because the wishes in each person's heart are different.

Your heart is unique. It carries with it a unique gift for mankind . . . a gift that is identified by the wishes in your heart. Take another look at your desires. Don't they define forms of love? With better health, won't you have more energy to achieve your goals for happiness? With more money, won't you be able to develop talents and programs that will help others? With a child by your side, won't you be able to beam more love into the world?

Our heart's wishes frame our gifts . . . but how do we give our gifts?

With words . . . because words have the power to create

To find out how powerful your own words really are, try this simple exercise. Grab a dictionary . . . and open it to any page. Which words grab your attention? Make a note.

Now, ask a friend to look at the same page. Ask him or her this question: "Which words on this page jump out at you?" Your words will differ from your friend's words. Why?

Because your heart is different from your friend's heart

Which prayers, then, work best? They are the prayers written with words from your own heart.

Put the power of creative prayer to work for you

1) Start a prayer journal. On each page, identify one of your heart's wishes. What do you need? Is it money, better health, the resolution to a relationship problem? Relief from feelings of rejection? A deeper connection to your own spirit and guides?

2) Write a short paragraph about each wish. Why do you want it? What will it do for you? What will it do for others? Speak from your heart . . . using your unique words.

3) "Send" each prayer with a short paragraph of appreciation. How will you feel when you receive your wish? The answer will launch your prayer . . . and make sure it reaches the ears of those who will help it come true.
