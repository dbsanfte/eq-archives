Seth Abramson a 1998 graduate of Dartmouth College, Seth is a second-year student at Harvard Law School. A life-long resident of Boston, Seth began writing poetry in the fall of 1998, soon after his immersion into the somewhat stifling world of legal thought & writing. His work has been published in a number poetry journals, including The Melic Review, Savoy Magazine, Tintern Abbey, The Fish Eye Review, and The Southern Ocean Review. He recently received an Honorable Mention Award in Suite101.com's 1999 National Poetry Month Contest.
Bill Adams an aging father of five, lives in the hills of NorthWest New Jersey & writes in an attempt to reconcile what he knows with what he sees: "it is maddest of all to accept the world as it is, and not as it should be." Don Quixote/Cervantes
Vasilis Adams A. I am an ESL (English as a Second Language) teacher. I've been teaching English for the last 14 years. Prior to that I worked as a Technical Specifications Writer for 7 years & as an Engineer for 5 years. I was born in Thessaloniki, Greece. I went to university in the U.S. where I received a B.Sc. degree. My writing credits include published fiction & non-fiction appearing in Greece & the USA. Stateside publications I have written for are Greek Accent, & Crosscurrents. In Greece I've been published in 30-Days, Key Travel News, Athena Magazine & had a weekend travel column in The Athens Star newspaper.

Charles Albano enjoys writing poetry& articles addressing diverse topics. His website, Adaptive Leadership, contains poems he has written about business leadership.

Diane Amlin Ashley is a homemaker, wife, mother,& proud grandmother of eight. Her ethnic heritage is both Scandinavian & Native American Indian. Born & educated in California.
