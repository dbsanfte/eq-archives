 Badmouthing Politicans
© Carl Weisbrod, Ph.D

I get real angry at politicians to the point I've started stereotyping them all as bureaucrats...with all due respect to hard working bureaucrats everywhere (is hard-working bureaucrat an oxymoron?).

Wow...first paragraph and I've already offended a bunch of folks.

But I want to go on record of being dissatisfied with politicians on three counts--follow thru' with me on this.

Forget about the Clinton sex scandal, for heaven sakes...that's minor compared with the things I'm going to talk about.

During my career I have been acutely aware that the American diet is directly related to the major cause of death in our society.

The number-crunchers at the CENTERS FOR DISEASE CONTROL AND PREVENTION know this, and it's known also by statisticians with epidemiological studies such as Framingham and Cornell-China Diet studies. Everything points to the fact that over half of the population of most developed countries are eating themselves into an early grave.

In the 60s and 70s, Senator George McGovern headed a Senate Select Committee that looked at the benefits (and lack of) of the American diet. They gathered together all of the research data that was available from the 40s, 50s, and 60s. McGovern, and the other members of the committee (Including Ted Kennedy and Bob Dole), released a document that became known as the McGovern report.

So nobody can plead ignorance, because our political leaders put it writing way back in the 70s. I've got to say it: "Thanks George, at least you made the attempt."

But then McGovern and the rest just let it drop, and steered clear of the issue pretty much from that time on.

If you've had a bout with a carcinoma cancer, heart disease, or other circulatory problems, or have a loved one who has succumbed to one of these problems (and that covers 99% of us), you might want to ask our politicians "why weren't you more aggressive in telling us about the dangers of the hi-fat/protein diet?" They still aren't telling us the whole truth.

The second thing is the national debt. It grew and grew until the danger was so obvious that the politicians had to say something. Most of the rhetoric tended to be the finger-pointing variety, and promises that "they" are the only ones that can fix it. Yeah, right!...like the folks that caused the problem are going to fix it?...you believe that??

The economy is better today than it has been in years. Who do you think is responsible for that? I hope you haven't bought into the claims that it's the Clinton presidency or the Republican Congress.

If you look at what is causing a great deal of the healthy cash flow in this country you will see that a couple of the players are Microsoft and R.J. Reynolds-Nabisco.

Now notice what our leaders are trying to do as we speak...they're trying to attach themselves to the udder of these two wealthy CashCows...aren't they?

Of course we're told that their only purpose is to stop children from smoking and to make sure that Bill Gates isn't building a monopoly...you believe that??

They knew that smoking was killing folks by the millions but didn't get too whipped up about it until they realized these companies had even more easy-to-grab dollars, although they've been milking smokers for decades...folks, by the way, who will end up with tremendous health problems and contribute to the economy by dying before drawing out their social security money. And of course teenage smoking has been increasing from the time Surgeon General, C. Everett Koop started his 1980s campaign to reduce teenage smoking.

Drugs/alcohol is another area that our politicians have consistently screwed up--that's so obvious I don't even want to talk about it.

Finally in July of 1998, President Clinton started a muffled mumble about the y2k problem (the Millennium Bug), something that could cripple main frame computers worldwide on December 31, 1999. But why did they ignored y2k warnings that were first identified more than a decade ago? Does "no money" and "no votes" seem like possible reasons?

[ed. note: refer to "Surviving Y2K" linked to the index page at http://askcarl.net or http://breakfree.net]

I've been called anti-American for my rant-and-rave criticism of the bureaucratic system. I don't think I'm anti-American. I am, however, anti-politician... without regard to their party affiliation.

I believe we've spawned a system that selects leaders for their skill in telling only that "truth" which benefits the potential for their re-election. Also, without regard to all the tears shed at the recent death of two Capitol Security officers, I think politics naturally selects folks with a cleaverly covered up mean spirit. Good heavens, look what they do to each other!

Politically designed semantic rules includes the phrase "politically correct" which is a rebirth of the Nixon-era phrase "the necessary lie."

My suggestion is this: Pay little attention to our political leaders and instead gather information from multiple sources.

The Internet is a godsend for this. Don't worry about all the junk that exists here--with a little experience you will learn to separate the wheat from the chaff.

For example, don't listen to the stuff about the sorry state of HMOs and the medical system. Instead, do what it takes to NOT NEED the medical system. Anyway, Western medicine is a disease-model system attempting to function in a chronic degenerative disorder world

.

Without a doubt, you will need medical doctors around if you are either injured or infected with a virus, bacteria, or parasite; but little help is offered for the prevention of chronic disorders such as atheroscleroses and the common cancers (lung, colon, breast, and prostate). That is, of course, unless you delay taking care of yourself until it's too late.

The few brave MDs who have spoken out about 20th-century prevention are scorned by their peer group. You will find a sampling of these folks at http://www.vegsource.com, One of my personal heroes is physician Bill Harris, who can be found at both the askcarl and breakfree websites.

But the quintessential doc thinks of prevention as such things as a mammogram, ct scan, and the physical exam. These things are detection, not prevention. Which do you think is better...to find a cancer growing in your lung (detection) or quitting smoking (prevention)? Which is better; to have bypass surgery, or prevent deposites of plaque in a coronary artery?

Okay here comes the selling part. http://askcarl.net is not a place for me to sell you audio cassettes. It is instead a place where I have put my 30 years of experience helping folks do better in various aspects of their life.

Think of these three realms:

The Physical/Intellectual/Emotional.

The physical realm must focus on prevention of chronic degenerative diseases. To do that, I have put my best efforts on audio cassettes and workbooks. Examples are smoking elimination, weight control, and exercise approaches.

In the intellectual realm I offer approaches for such things as charisma, the home-based business, and lifestyles (of the poor and unknown).

The Emotional realm I have programs such as stress management and getting over a broken heart.

I have tried to make this site as interactive as possible. The only way possible is to make myself available using the AskCarl Confidential Board, and at no charge beyond the price of the audio cassette program.

In some ways, I have found that an online interactive approach is more powerful than in-office psychotherapy, and far less expensive.

Folks have time to focus in on the message they send me, and I have time to do any necessary research for the best answer--something that seldom happens with $100/hour in-office sessions. It's also far more confidential than the usual clinical session. Over "my dead body" will there ever be insurance company folks, office file clerks, or lawyers peeking at your file.

So, what more can I say? Go to the AskCarl Board and talk to me...I will most likely answer you on the same day, even on week ends and holidays--I work every day.
