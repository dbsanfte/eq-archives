Quick View - Parts Of A Sentence

1. PHRASES -

A phrase is a group of words that are closely related but have no subject and no predicate. Phrases can be used as a verb, a noun, an adjective, or an adverb. I.E.
  • Noun
    Waiting for the postman’s delivery kept me home all morning.
  • VERB
    That letter could have been sentsooner.
  • ADJECTIVE
    That house with the large flag flying on the roof is where he lives.
  • ADVERB
    The price of fruit is higher out of season.
    If a phrase is essential to the meaning of the sentence it is a restrictive phrase. A phrase which is actually a parenthetical comment is called nonrestrictive and is almost always set off by commas.

  • RESTRICTIVE
    The books on my shelf are used by several employees.
  • NON RESTRICTIVE
    I imagine, by the way, that she will win the election.



    2. SUBJECT AND PREDICATE

    A sentence should express a complete thought and contain a subject and a predicate. If the subject or predicate is missing - it must be readily understood from sentences that surround it.
    The subject of a sentence must be a person, object, or idea that is being expressed. A predicate is an explanation of the condition, action, or effect of the subject. In the sentences below the subject is in regular type and the predicate is in italics -
  • Finding a job is a difficult thing these days.
  • The pre-Easter sale will be starting tomorrow.
    Subjects are nouns, pronouns, or phrases used as nouns.
    Predicates are verbs and the words used to explain the action or the condition.



    3. CLAUSES

    A clause is a group of words that has a subject and a predicate. A main clause can stand alone as a complete sentence, while a subordinate clause is incomplete and is used with a main clause to express a related idea.

  • MAIN CLAUSE
    This is the lady that gave me the sled.
    I enjoyed using the sled when the weather permitted.
  • SUBORDINATE CLAUSE
    I enjoy playing with the sled, which I bought last week.
    Whenever she is here, I like to read poetry.
