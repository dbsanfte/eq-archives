Raid #06 - Lady Vox - Jun 27, 2001
Report from raid leader, Brewall Rainsinger, of the Crimson Talon.

LADY VOX IS DEAD

Vox dies yet a sixth time at the hands of Crimson Talon and other hearty adventurers who joined us in battle.  Interest in the raids is very high, and I had to turn away no less than 12 people.

Word has definitely gotten out about the Vox raids, and I am amazed at how many people showed up early. Signing up on EverquestRaids is no guarantee that you will have a spot, nor does it indicate the exact starting time. Quite a difference from a few weeks ago when I had cobbled together just 38 people, and felt lucky to get that, now, tons of people are showing up. To anyone I had to turn away, I appologize, I have to limit attendance otherwise things are out of control, people start getting immature, and people don't listen to my instructions.

The two buff stations worked out well again. People are really getting the hang of it. Great opportunity for capitalists to sell peridots. Always seems to be people around without them.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Pulling Vox's guards without agroing Vox can be tricky. Nothing worse than having Vox agro and start summoning people from goblin king area to their death - and I've seen this happen on one bad raid I was on. If it takes a while, it is well worth it.
2. As raid leader, I average 1 tell every 10 seconds and I still have to do groups, buffers, and a number of other things. If you are in a temp group, find out if any are new attendees and help them along if you can, explaining loot, warning about traps, and flow helps a lot.
3. If I say raid is full, it is full. Sending 6 more tells won't help. I try to accomodate long-time attendees and work in classes I'm short on, and try to be as fair as possible.

Excellent job by all listening to instructions. Pulling Vox out into middle of room was very well executed and excellent job surrounding her. Vox did not manage to get off any CH, great job seiving and stunning!

Special thanks go out to Altus and Physst for pulling of the Ice Giants and Vox's guards. This is an extremely difficult and important task and I certainly appreciate their efforts. And to the buffers that evening as well: Khallin, Barelil, Kauf, Vyce, Galadriad, Bolgor, Mysyn, Cilden, Saleel, Guldaenya, and Nandarr (and to the bards, necros, and magicians doing the feeding too). For all the first time buffers that were there, great job!

WHAT WILL BE DONE DIFFERENT FOR THE NEXT RAID?
1. Confirmation that on list for raid. I will hard cap Vox at 45 and Nag at 40, and keep a few spots open for classes the raid is short on. Hate to tell people no, but going to have to do it. Server gone from 0 kills in 2 months to 6 kills and the word has gotten out that my raids work. Going to be especially tough on Naggy, he is a lot easier than Vox.
2. Suggest that Clerics/Enchanters sell peridots for 14pp. At levels 45+ everyone should be carrying a stack of 20 with them at all times. Good habit to get into, if you accept less from guildmates/friends, that is your decision. Hate to see cle/enc tie up tons of pp in peridots for those that don't carry them.

OLD INFO ON WDS:
3. As promised when I first started setting raids up, the WDS would open up once the original 4 bards had won scales. I did not foresee 2 dropping last night and did not have my spreadsheet ready for caster/bard odds. On the next raid, the WDS will be open to casters with the following conditions:
-If 3 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the others will be casters+losing bards.
-If 2 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the other will be casters+losing bards.
-If 1 scale drops, the roll will be bards+casters. However, I will multiply each bard's raid points by a factor of 5. I have targetted the factor so that the collective numbers that bards have is approximately 40%-50% of the total pool of numbers on the roll. So bards have a 50% chance of winning, otherwise a caster wins it. Many of the bards that have won are continuing to attend with hopes of getting other dragon items, so it may be that the only people rolling on WDS are casters.
