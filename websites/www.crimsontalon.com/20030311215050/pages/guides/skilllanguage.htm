EverQuest Language Guide
Last update: 12 November 2002

EQ LANGUAGE GUIDE - Written by Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule
This guide covers all you want to know about the languages in Everquest, where to find them, and how to learn them.

THE LANGUAGES
There are 25 total languages in EverQuest and are split among those that the player-controlled characters speak and those that can only be obtained through training.

PLAYER LANGUAGES
Easiest to find and to train in.  Find any player of the corresponding race/class and have them train you to level of mastery:

Barbarian
Combine: (Vah Shir have master skill)
Common Tongue
Dark Speech
Dwarvish
Elder Elvish: (High Elves have skill of 41)
Elvish
Erudian
Gnomish
Halfling
Lizardman: Language of the Iksar.
Ogre
Tier'Dal (Dark Elvish language)
Thieve's Cant (All rogues have master skill)
Troll
Vah Shir

NON-PLAYER LANGUAGES
With these languages, it is impossible to create or find someone based on their race to train you in these languages.  The only way to obtain them is to find someone who already knows them or put training points in them and/or find partners to practice with:

Dragon: level 20
Elder Dragon: level 30
Elder Tier'Dal: level 30
Faerie: level 20
Froglok: level 20
Gnoll: level 10
Goblin: level 20
Old Erudian: level 10
Orcish: level 20

Note: level is when most classes are able to train in that language. May vary based on class & trainer. Not all languages are taught at all trainers.

RULES TO LEARNING LANGUAGES
1. You can only learn by listening to someone else with equal or higher skill than yourself.
2. You can train someone else up to 1 point higher than your skill.
3. You must be in same zone as someone else to learn.
4. EQ does not recognize any skill increases until you zone. If you are teaming with someone else, you will need to zone to get the increases recognized.
5. If you are teaming with someone else to learn a language, you will each need to zone every time the other person's skill is increased (see rules 3 & 4).
6. Can't train in some obscure languages until after level 20 and some after 30. This prevents people from creating new characters for sole purpose of language training.
7. You can identify a language being spoken to you at a skill of 25 (it will no longer be "unknown language")

HOW TO LEARN FROM SOMEONE WHO HAS MASTERED A LANGUAGE
1. Get some cheap int or wis gear. Try to get stat over 100.
2. Have the person you are going to learn from set up hotkey with 5 lines of group say spam. I recommend a few letters per line. Some people say that it works faster with long lines of text, but from my testing, long lines only increase lag. Each line should have something different, otherwise, the server may filter the message because it looks similar.  I use a social hotkey of:
  /gsay abc123
  /gsay efg456
  /gsay hij789
  /gsay klm123
  /gsay nop456
3. Form a group, have other person change language to desired one to learn. If you do not form a group, the messages to other players are filtered for spamming.
4. Have them spam you over-and-over with the hotkey.
5. Skill point increases in languages do not show the numerical value when you get an increase, but you will see a message that your skill gets better.
6. Typically takes about 10 minutes to go from 0 to 101 (master) with an intelligence of 100.

HOW TO TEACH EACH OTHER A LANGUAGE (WHEN YOU CAN'T FIND A MASTER)
1. Get some cheap int or wis gear. Try to get stat over 100.
2. Have 1 person put at least 1 point into the language (e.g. PlayerA trains 1 point).
3. Have person you are going to learn from set up hotkey with 5 lines of group say spam. I recommend a few letters per line. Some people say that it works faster with long lines of text, but from my testing, long lines only increase lag. Each line should have something different, otherwise, the server may filter the message because it looks like you are spamming.  I use a social hotkey of:
  /gsay abc123
  /gsay efg456
  /gsay hij789
  /gsay klm123
  /gsay nop456
4. Form group, have other person change language to desired one to learn.
5. Player knowing language will spam the other person until they get to 1 point higher than the teacher (e.g. PlayerA, with skill of 1, spams PlayerB with skill of 0, until PlayerB gets 2 skill increases. PlayerA skill is 1, PlayerB skill is now 2).
6. Both players zone, and original learner spams the teacher until that person gets 2 skill increases (e.g. PlayerB, with skill of 2, spams PlayerA with skill of 1, until PlayerA gets 2 skill increases. PlayerA skill now 3, PlayerB skill is 2).
7. Repeat 5-6 until both players at 101. 

TIPS
1. To see list of languages you know, use the "/language help" command to see list of languages and corresponding numbers.
2. You can see the language you are currently speaking with "/language 0"
3. You can change the language you are speaking with "/language #", where # is the language number.
4. If partnering with someone to learn languages from scratch, try to do several languages at one time. Zoning takes majority of time.
5. Don't try to train languages in busy zones. Longer load time when zoning into crowded areas and more spam.
