Raid #11 Lord Nagafen
Raid Date: July 19 2001

Report from raid leader Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Lord Nagafen felt the cold grip of death tonight as a band of adventurers led by Brewall descended into his lair.  

Originally scheduled for Wednesday, Lord Nagafen decided not to return to his lair that night, and instead sent Ragefire. Perhaps the two got into a time conflict and that is why Nagafen was late. In any case, he had spawned by Thursday morning and needed to be slain.

Large numbers of people were present and ready to join the raid. I appologize again to anyone I turned away, but it is impossible to include everyone. I tried to be as fair as possible picking people, and I do this by taking everyone's name and then going through the list and prioritize people who have been on a lot of raids and then necessary classes. I turned away 11 people in Sol B and even more as the raid progressed.  The 11 people I turned away at the start were given an attendance point which helps you get into future raids. Keep trying to get in and your persistance will pay off, a lot of prior attendees level past 52 are are unable to attend.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Collecting everyone's name takes a while. I never know who is going to show up and I have to enter all this into my spreadsheet.
2. I take extra time and double-check level when epic items are at stake. This is important for Paladins (50+ requirement for book) and Bards/Warriors (50+ for red dragon scale).
3. Once names entered, takes little while to work out who is needed. I work out all the groups and adjust if we are short in critical areas. This means I might take a first-time shaman, enchanter, cleric, or druid over a multi-attendee melee if we are short in these classes. It is exceedingly rare that I ever turn away shm, enc, clr, and bards, since all help the raid go faster and more successfully.
4. I don't lock the list until critical class mixture is met. If cannot get these classes, I take additional people to compensate. So, the 36 number is ideal, but could be higher or lower based on what classes show.

When in a temp group, find out if anyone in your group is new. Help them along and answer their questions and tell them how raid generally works. Saves them from sending me tells. Find out where they heard about raid and if they have read loot rules and the dragon guide.

Excellent job by all listening to instructions, especially during giant pulls. If you have never done a dragon, pay attention to those that have, there is a reason for everything.

Special thanks go out to Altus and Dainin for coordinating the Giant pulling. This is an extremely difficult and important task and I certainly appreciate their efforts. And to all the buffers that evening as well: Khallin, Barelil, Evelyn, Kauf, Lheal, Aorthin, Cilden, Tadyan, Svenia, Windarii, and Dunskye. For all the first time buffers that were there, great job!
