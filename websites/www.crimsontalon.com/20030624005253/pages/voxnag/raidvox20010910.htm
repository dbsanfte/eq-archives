Raid #24 Lady Vox
Raid Date: September 10 2001

Report from raid leader Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Vox dies at the hands of Crimson Talon and other hearty adventurers who joined us in battle.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Significant lack of clerics or enchanters delayed us quite a bit this time. By 9:30pm, had only 1 cleric in permafrost (other than a level 60 to help buff) and 0 enc levels 48-52. Took a while for all to arrive, my thanks for coming to the raid.
2. Pulling Vox's guards without agroing Vox can be tricky. Nothing worse than having Vox agro and start summoning people from goblin king area to their death - and I've seen this happen on one bad raid I was on. If it takes a while, it is well worth it. Need to have entire lair clear prior to attacking Vox, especially her two priests.
3. As raid leader, I average 1 tell every 10 seconds and I still have to do groups, buffers, and a number of other things. If you are in a temp group, find out if any are new attendees and help them along if you can, explaining loot, warning about traps, and flow helps a lot.
4. I cap Vox attendance at around 48 people. With 70+ people wanting to go sometimes, I have to pick and that can take a while. This is not the time to be asking how to get to Permafrost, I'll take the person standing right next to me first.

WHAT WILL BE DONE DIFFERENT FOR THE NEXT RAID?
1. [old] People need to report in when I shout for names. If you do not send in your name, you are not even on my radar to be assigned to a group. It takes me a while to go through tells, and I rarely miss someone.
2. [old] Suggest that Clerics/Enchanters sell peridots for 15pp. At levels 45+ everyone should be carrying a stack of 20 with them at all times. Good habit to get into, if you accept less from guildmates/friends, that is your decision. Hate to see cle/enc tie up tons of pp in peridots for those that don't carry them.

OLD INFO ON WDS:
3. As promised when I first started setting raids up, the WDS would open up once the original 4 bards had won scales. I did not foresee 2 dropping last night and did not have my spreadsheet ready for caster/bard odds. On the next raid, the WDS will be open to casters with the following conditions:
-If 3 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the others will be casters+losing bards.
-If 2 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the other will be casters+losing bards.
-If 1 scale drops, the roll will be bards+casters. However, I will multiply each 50-52 bard's raid points by a factor of 5. I have targetted the factor so that the collective numbers that bards have is approximately 40%-50% of the total pool of numbers on the roll. So bards have a 50% chance of winning, otherwise a caster wins it.
