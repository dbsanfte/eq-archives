Raid #05 Lady Vox
Raid Date: June 16 2001

Report from raid leader Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Vox dies yet again at the hands of Crimson Talon and other hearty adventurers who joined us in battle. Interest in the raids is very high, until Brewall started raiding her, she had not gone down for several months, and the attendees were in blood lust. The last five Brewall led raids have eased the oppression of this cruel dragon over the lands of Everfrost, as well as, liberated even more of her property.

Word has definitely gotten out about the Vox raids, and I am amazed at how many people showed up early. Signing up on EverquestRaids is no guarantee that you will have a spot, nor does it indicate the exact starting time. Quite a difference from three weeks ago when I had cobbled together just 38 people, and felt lucky to get that, now, tons of people are showing up. To anyone I had to turn away, I appologize, I have to limit attendance otherwise things are out of control, people start getting immature, and people don't listen to my instructions.

The two buff stations worked out well again. People are really getting the hang of it. With only 2 shamans and no 52+ clerics, I had to get inventive with the buffs, but still worked out very well.

Prior attendees are blessing on the raid, and if you wonder why things slow down from time-to-time, I have to deal with some pretty bizarre problems. This raid had quite a few:
1. Someone did station one buffs, got 14 buffs, and then went to station two and tried to get those...
2. Had 2 people fall in pits. Nothing I can do until after Vox is dead. Asking me multiple times when I will get it doesn't help. My answer is always - after the fight - and don't fall in pits!
3. Someone complaining that group disbanded them. Then I find out it was because they missed their group buffing and got 'disbanded' when group camped out.
4. Over ten people who didn't have peridots or any platinum to pay. Very annoying to deal with on an open raid.

Excellent job by all listening to instructions. Pulling Vox out into middle of room was very well executed. She did get off 1 CH during the fight, but it was a losing battle for her.

Special thanks go out to Altus for pulling of the Ice Giants and Vox's guards. This is an extremely difficult and important task and I certainly appreciate their efforts. And to the buffers that evening as well: Khallin, Bolgor, Loen, Mysyn, Aprisle, Taraddar, Galadriad, Venswayla, Espin, Xiaoling, and Eirye. For all the first time buffers that were there, great job!

My computer is really screwed up right now, and I locked up hard twice last night. Going to get a new hard drive and rebuild and reinstall everything. Died hard right when I was about to do loot, but at least it didn't happen during fight.

WHAT WILL BE DONE DIFFERENT FOR THE NEXT RAID?
1. Confirmation that on list for raid. I will hard cap at 40, and keep a few spots open for classes the raid is short on. Hate to tell people no, but going to have to do it. Server gone from 0 kills in 2 months to 5 kills in 2 weeks and the word has gotten out that my raids work.
2. Will kick "BOOK DROP" button. Vox 0-4 on book drops and I'm really feeling sorry for paladins. Vox should drop one every time in my opinion.
3. Suggest that Clerics/Enchanters sell peridots for 14pp. At levels 45+ everyone should be carrying a stack of 20 with them at all times. Good habit to get into, if you accept less from guildmates/friends, that is your decision. Hate to see cle/enc tie up tons of pp in peridots for those that don't carry them.

OLD INFO ON WDS:
5. As promised when I first started setting raids up, the WDS would open up once the original 4 bards had won scales. I did not foresee 2 dropping last night and did not have my spreadsheet ready for caster/bard odds. On the next raid, the WDS will be open to casters with the following conditions:
-If 3 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the others will be casters+losing bards.
-If 2 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the other will be casters+losing bards.
-If 1 scale drops, the roll will be bards+casters. However, I will multiply each bard's raid points by a factor of X (will work on this week to come up with factor). I will target the factor so that the collective numbers that bards have is approximately 50% of the total pool of numbers on the roll. So bards have a 50% chance of winning, otherwise a caster wins it. Many of the bards that have won are continuing to attend with hopes of getting other dragon items, so it may be that the only people rolling on WDS are casters.
