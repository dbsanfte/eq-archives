Raid #03 Lady Vox
Raid Date: May 29 2001

Report from raid leader Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Vox dies a third time at the hands of Crimson Talon and other hearty adventurers who joined us in battle.  Vox had not gone down on the server for several months and the last three Brewall lead raids have eased the oppression of this cruel dragon over the lands of Everfrost, as well as, liberated more of her property.

Word has definitely gotten out about the Vox raids, and I was absolutely shocked how many people showed up. Less than two weeks ago, I had cobbled together just 38 people, and felt lucky to get that, now, tons of people are showing up. Next raid will have a limit on people.

Definitely going to change way people make the list, and it will be better for everyone involved if I do. Too many people causes a lot more work (and time) to do a raid and almost results in mob mentality, and instructions not being followed. I know it can be dull at start during setup, but pullers and myself are hard at work. I average 1 tell every 10 seconds AND I'm trying to work out groups, buffers, tactics, and other things.

From now on, when people arrive in zone, they will need to send me a tell, AND receive a message back from me that "YOU ARE ON LIST". It may add to my tells, but I was actually getting people just arriving in zone claiming they had been there for a long time (I wrote down every name when I first logged into Perma) - I turned some away for this reason. Trust me, I don't like turning anyone away, but just going to have to do it next time. I also turned away some below 45 that were on last raid, but this time we had plenty of 45+.

Vox was definitely tough this time on pulling guards. Hate it when she agros and then summons the pullers, and I'm sure the pullers do to. This next time, I'll include a message that Altus is puller, will use shout, and everyone should help him in any way he asks.

Two line buffing worked out better than I anticipated time-wise, but it still seems that some people are missing buffs. Seems like I can lead an ogre to a beer but can't make him drink. I thought the way I ran through each line and then did a recap was a good format, but next time, I just will just jump to a recap messages and make people write it down. No excuse for not getting 14 buffs.

Absolute biggest problem of the fight was that someone did not listen to instructions, got feared and pulled back a ton of mobs from the pits. If I could figure out who they were, they would not be welcome on additional raids. I had planned to use reserve group 5 to take out any respawns (I thought maybe 1 up) and instead they were decimated by the pit mobs. Then, the bears wiped out a couple enchanters, allowing Vox to retain mana for CH. To top it off, the bear AI must then have looked for person doing most shouts, because I had 4 on me and couldn't get them off. Needless to say, with 75hp left, I did my last shouts, did a suicide AE 350hp dmg to all the bears and promptly died when Vox was at 20%.

Special thanks go out to Altus (CT guild leader) and Physst for pulling of the Ice Giants and Vox's guards. This is an extremely difficult and important task and I certainly appreciate their efforts. And to the buffers that evening as well: Sileguy, Aorthin, Rekinom, Khallin, Lazuli, Saleel, Tomwin, Remuvdar, Barelil, Syncere, Hierl, and Mysyn.

WHAT WILL BE DONE DIFFERENT FOR THE NEXT RAID?
1. Confirmation that on list for raid. I will hard cap at 40, and keep a few spots open for classes the raid is short on. Hate to tell people no, but going to have to do it. Server gone from 0 kills in 2 months to 3 kills in 2 weeks and the word has gotten out.
2. Rule #2 is going to be: don't ever bring a mob back from pits. Rule #1 is to keep Vox off walls.
3. Will kick "BOOK DROP" button. Vox 0-3 on book drops and I'm really feeling sorry for paladins. Almost as bad as me hosting 4 raids and not winning a scale yet...
4. If multiple scales drop, I will automatically recieve one, others all up for random (vox drops 0-3). Already, 3 bards that I had never met before I started the Vox raids have won, and will definitely ease the loot presure on me. With many more bards in attendance (only 4 on 1st raid), I will also increase my own raid points to still allow for chance someone else may win, but my odds get no worse than this last fight. And, yes, even after I win a WDS, I will continue to host raids.
5. Suggest that Clerics/Enchanters sell peridots for 14pp. At levels 45+ everyone should be carrying a stack of 20 with them at all times. Good habit to get into, if you accept less from guildmates/friends, that is your decision. Hate to see cle/enc tie up tons of pp in peridots for those that don't carry them.
