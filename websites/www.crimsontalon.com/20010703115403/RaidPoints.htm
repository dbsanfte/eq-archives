Vox & Nag Loot Rules & Raid Points
** Last updated 01 July 2001

INTRODUCTION:
Everyone on a Crimson Talon Vox/Nag raid has a chance at winning loot. Items are open loot, with no reserved or claimed items. This is typical of our regular raids and our big raids. For our typical raiding during the week, we distribute loot to any attendee based on need before greed, we don't use points, and no special treatments.

Dragon raids are a bit different in that it takes more than just one guild to pull one off, and have to do multiple times in order to get a chance at item. This is the reason for the Vox/nag rules and points, with often 40+ attendees, numerous guilds, unguilded individuals, epic items, uber-loot, everything needs to be clear so that it is fair and avoids confusion.

This page will: (1) Detail all loot rules, (2) List everyone's raid points to date, (3) Talk about Brewall's future raiding plans, and (4) Detail how the point system works.

HOW IS LOOT DONE? DO I HAVE TO DO A RANDOM?
The raid leader assigns everyone a sequential number based on their name (done alphabetically) and does a  "\random 1 N" and the person whose name matches the number wins. This allows people who participated in raid, but are still being rezed/LD a chance at loot. Some items are simply 1 person = 1 number, for special items it is 1 person = 1 number + raid points. This is done so everyone has a chance at an item, but multi-raid attendees have improved odds (reflecting their prior risk and raiding effort). After each raid, all raid points and a recap of the loot numbers for each item rolled is posted to the Crimson Talon website if anyone wants to check later. Those that have attended prior raids can attest to my honesty in handling loot - 3 bards I did not know prior to the Vox raids have wons scales, and as raid leader, it was the 4th raid before I won one (and was the main reason I started the raids).

HEY BREWALL, HOW MANY TIMES DO YOU PLAN ON KILLING VOX?
For those that don't know me, I'm a 52 bard that hopes to see an epic some day. I lacked the 100k+ pp to buy a white scale and killing the only alternate source (Gorenaire) is a remote possibility at best when I hit 60. I waited around for Vox raids, but no one was making an effort to kill her. After two months of Vox living happily every day, I decided to do something about it. I went through every piece of Vox raiding information I could find, combined it with my knowledge and expections of how a Vox raid should be done, wrote a guide, and scheduled some raids. Now as a host, I find myself constantly improving my guide (found on this site in the guide area) after every raid with my own observations and suggestions from others.

Thrice I had been a victim to my own idealistic open looting rules (before I finally got a WDS), and am probably only one of the few bards on any server to hold THREE (3!) white dragon scales and then hand them to others. If anyone doubts my integrity and fairness in handling loot, I think this alone says that I am honorable and trustworthy. Granted, I could make a mistake when doing the numbers, but not intentionally, and my spreadsheet that calculates the numbers is extremely solid.

I plan on holding Vox raids until I, at a minimum, get a white scale and a mcvaxious horn (is the only upgrade to store-bought brass instrument which I still use). After that, I will likely do several more to get other bards a WDS, and melees & casters a RBB/Eyepatch/WDS (many CT members have been attending raids and have a decent total pool of numbers, bound to win sometime with enough raids). Perhaps I will also get a RBB, but time will only tell on that. I probably play EQ as it was meant to be played, I rarely camp for experience and spend my time exploring as much of the game as I can (which is easy to do a bard speed+track), because of this, I level pretty slowly, so 53 is still a ways off. When I do get to 53, I will likely push to get to 55 and then host other big encounter raids.

I'll continue to keep scheduling the raids when I believe Vox will be up and can get enough attendees. The raids are always listed at Everquestraids.com and usually on a Fri/Sat/Sun. If I miss a week, the risk of Vox not being up is too great or I'm on vacation somewhere. She is on a random 7-10 day timer, and I got burned on raid 2a when we waited for hours after more than 8 days passed.

In the event of an emergency patch, I will reschedule raids to maximize kills. There may be a raid that day or the following day.

When I do stop having raids, I will write a hosting guide, a listing of all the keyboard shouts I use (over 40), how to form groups, tactics (especially when short on classes), and the spreadsheet I use to do groups & loot.

RAID POINTS
Everyone attending the raid is given 1 Raid Point. The Raid Leader (Brewall) is given 2 extra Raid Points for leading the event. For history buffs, eighteenth century pirate captains usually divided treasure out equally to all crew members, and they themselves took a 3x share. Rather democratic in my opinion, and I follow the same tradition, giving myself just 2 extra shares/points, far more fair than reserving items.

For those that have never hosted a raid, doing so is an enormous amount of work. I average 1 tell every 10-15 seconds for almost an hour before the fight and still have to work out groups, buffers, final strategy, and making sure the loot calculations are correct. I don't like to guarantee items, instead preferring everyone to get a chance at it, but as a raid leader, will have slightly better odds to reflect the work to put raids together.

The raid leader, at their discression, can award additional points to individuals for service above and beyond the call of duty.

What are the points for?
Dragon raids take a lot of people to pull off and usually only a few (very nice) items drop, and even then, not all the time (RBB & Eyepatch). Attend 1 raid, you may not win anything, attend more than 1, you stand a better chance.
To pull this off, I give out raid points to encourage people to attend more than one raid. The more you attend, the better chance you have at winning nice items, but there is still a chance for a new attendee to win as well (why attend is you stand zero chance at winning?). People who have attended more raids have taken more overall risk. Someone who has attended 5 raids should stand a better chance at winning an item than a first timer. Plus, multi-attendees are more experienced, need fewer instructions, usually get better resistance gear, and ease the work done by the raid leader.

How do the points help?
Each time you come to a raid you get 1 point. The points are used to give you an extra number in the rolls for the good loot items. The first time you come to a raid, you have 1 number, people who have attended a prior raid have 1 number + 1 number for each raid point.

Ugh! Sounds complicated, Give me an example.
It's actually very easy. Say JoePaladin attends raid #1, he gets 1 raid point. No book of scale drops on raid #1. JoePaladin and MaryPaladin come to raid #2, and if a book of scale drops, JoePaladin would get two numbers (#1 and #2), MaryPaladin would get one number (#3). If they were the only paladins on the raid, the raid leader would do a \random 1 3 to determine the winner. Mary still has a chance at the book, but Joe has a better chance reflecting his prior attendance.

If Joe lost to Mary on the roll for raid #2, he has a lot of motivation to come to raid #3 (3 numbers now on rolls: 1 number+2 raid points), and to bring along some friends to make sure the raid isn't short of people because he has some good odds at winning. Hopefully, Mary will also return to the next couple raids to help fellow paladins who did not win a book.

What items are the points for?
For the Books of Scale, Dragon Scales, Runed Bolster Belt, Cloak of Flames, and Tobrin's Mystical Eyepatch. If you win an item, you go back to 0 points. Once you win an item, you are ineligible to receive another of the same item ever. You do not lose your points if you win another item (pouch, bracer, horn, etc). Paladins are faced with a decision if they win a RBB/CoF since they would lose all their points, thus reducing odds on future book drops.

All other items are 1 person = 1 number.

The raid leader, at their discression, can use their points on these 1-point items to increase odds. If they win, loses all points to date (e.g., bard hosting raids has a WDS and decides to use points on future rolls for horn to increase odds). This still allows others chances at the item, and keeps the raid leader motivated to not freeze out new attendees of same class.

How is the White Dragon Scale treated?
For bards, this is the critical item needed for the epic quest.  Personally, I hate the way VI did the bard epic, it hurts casters and it hurts bards. On other servers, it has meant that casters that win the WDS have been turning around and selling it for over 100k to high level bards instead of turning it in for a cloak, and yes, the epic would be worth that much.  I've tried to come up with an alternate system that is fair to bards and yet benefits casters as well.

*For the first couple raids, the rolls for the WDS will be bard only (level 50+). DONE, 5 BARDS HAVE SCALE.
*These bards are expected to use the WDS for epic. If they ever sell the WDS, they will never be eligible to roll again for a WDS. They will also never be eligble to receive another non no-drop item used in a quest on any future raid I host. If you plan to sell it, refuse to roll and wait until opened to casters.
*Casters will get a raid points for each raid.
*(Old info) Until all the bards from the first raid have won a WDS (2 bards still have not won), the roll will remain bard only. A new bard coming to the raid at this point would still have a chance to win, but at lesser odds because of the accumulated raid points of the other bards. I'll note that the odds are still better than waiting till level 60 and taking out Gore...
*Now that the remaining two original  bards have won, the roll is open to casters.   However, bards will get their raid points factored up by 5x to improve their odds.   Remember, raid points are used! So, on raid #4, a caster who has attended every raid will have 5 numbers, a first-time caster would have 1, a first-time bard would have 5 , and a third time bard would have 15. The bards from the first raid plan on attending a few more for chances at McVaxious Horn, so this may wind up being open to casters soon. If multiple scales drop, I will do 1 exclusively to bards and others to casters (losing bards in on this roll). If 3 drop, 1 will go to bards, 2 to casters (losing bards will be in roll as well).
*To avoid scalping the WDS to high level bards (for 100k+), I will allow high-level bards to offer a cloak trade at the conclusion of the fight (if a caster wins). A cloak of equal or greater value for the WDS will be traded 1-for-1 for the scale.  I have already received offers for trading White Dragon Scale Cloaks, Heirophant cloaks, and a Crimson Robe of Alendine when we get to this point! If more than one bard is present offering to make the trade, I will randomly select one. I don't want to see high level bards trying to outbid each other on this.

*53+ Bards & Alternate characters. I've gotten a number of messages from high-level bards (and those with alts) in the 45-52 range wanting to know how to get the scale. They have a number of options to get a scale:
1. Attend raids and take chance (like everyone else) on winning a scale. If you win (and tell me you are the alt), I will not force you to take a trade if a high-level bard is present and wanting to trade. Showing favoritism to someone because they have an alt that is a high-level bard wouldn't be fair to people on the raid without an alt. I've kept my bard at level 52 for over 4 months to try to get a white scale. This works if you have a caster alt / puller alt.

2. Come to raids with a group of friends (casters). If any of them wins the roll (and tell me that they are part of your odds), I will not force them to take a trade if a high-level bard is present and wanting to trade. Getting a group of 4-5 casters on a few raids to attend would give you a nice chance at the scale.

3. Get a greater or equal cloak and offer it for trade at end of the fight (to raid leader). I will ensure that the items are traded.
