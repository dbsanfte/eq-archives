Lord Nagafen Guide
Last update: 04 January 2002

Written by Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Greetings all. I've written this guide to explain how a textbook Naggy raid is done. This is the compilation of my observations on both good & bad raids and numerous "guides" that I have found that were often lacking. For many players, dragon hunts are their first foray into large scale raids and if you have never done one before, this guide will explain a lot of what is going on, and what you need to do. Most of all, be patient. Pulling off a large raid can take a bit of time, especially if the raid is short of certain classes.

WHAT YOU NEED
1. You should be between levels 45 and 52. Clerics as low as 39 (with complete heal) are also useful.
2. An unbuffed Magic Resistance of 50 and a Fire Resistance of 100. The higher the better.
3. Bring two peridots: one for cleric and one for enchanter. These will be used for beneficial spells.
4. Check with raid leader on how loot will be distributed.

HOW TO GET THERE
1. Closest Druid portal: Lavastorm
2. Closest Wizard portal: SE corner of Nekulos Forest

IMPORTANT THINGS TO REMEMBER
1. Naggy is a level 55 Dragon Warrior.
2. Naggy's most deadly weapon is his AE fire breath and AE fear spell. Wear as much MR/FR gear as possible; sacrifice all other statistics (yes, even AC) to get these as high as possible.
3. Check your resist gear. If you don't have much, consider acquiring: 2xEarring of Fire Reflection (AC1 FR5 ~26pp at Lavastorm gypsies), Gold Diamond Mask (FR10 CR10 MR3), Gold Hermatite Necklace (FR7), Gold Emerald Bracelet (AC4 FR6), Platinum Emerald Ring (AC4 FR10), Hand Woven Legging (AC8 FR10), Rune Boned Fork (MR10 CR10 FR10), and Crown of the Froglok King (AC12 CR20 FR20).
4. Many of the mobs in Solusek have high fire resist - including Nag, use cold based spells where possible for minimum resists.
5. In order to cast any spell on a dragon - YOU MUST BE WITHIN HIS MELEE RANGE.

STAGING PHASE
1. When you are in Lavastorm or Solusek, report in to the raid leader.
2. The raid leader may tell if the raid is short of certain classes. Check with friends if asked.
3. Turn off anonymous when you show up.
4. The raid leader, pullers, and group leaders will use shout channel. All other communication should be on the OOC channel.
5. It may take a while for the raid leader to work out final groups and the attack plan, especially if the raid is short of people in critical areas (clerics, enchanters, bards, nukers).

ASSEMBLY OPTIONS
1. Assemble in Lavastorm and fight en-mass through giants or meet at stone spider room.
2. Single late comers may need to be CoH if past giants. If multiple people, they can form temporary groups and fight back to staging area outside Nag Lair. If need be, spiders can be pulled to assembly area to keep path free.

KILLING GIANTS PHASE
1. Once enough people are present, final groups will be assigned.
2. Unless you are being directed to pull, stay back.
3. The main and secondary assists will be named. Create a hotkey for each.
4. The raid leader and backup leader will be named. Take instructions from either leader.
5. Form into final groups. Write down all members on paper for future reference (with so many people, it may be difficult to target someone with a mouse, so just use the "/target [name]" command).
6. The pullers will begin pulling the giants to the groups. This phase is often tricky and the puller may die. All these mobs are on an approximate 8 hour timer. When the last giant is killed, the groups will move to the safe spot in the hallway near Nag's chambers. Once this phase starts, please do not go go AFK.
7. Nuking casters will bind in the camp spot, so that in death, can mem critical spells and jump back into the fight. In the past, players sometimes left a couple coins on the ground to prevent empty corpses from vanishing if dying again. An alternate approach was to have everyone in group create a "/split 0 0 0 6" key, which can be used prior to someone coming back into the fight.
8. Raid leader should go through the entire attack plan and buffing information at this point.

BUFFING PHASE
1. A list of casters will be named #1-14 and will correspond to the buff number.
2. Go to your graphic and filter options and turn everything off or to minimum (spell effects, level of detail, damage shield messages, etc).
3. All non-buffers will be stripped of all buffs.
4. Once stripped, all buffers & all bards will disband from the groups. Temporary buffer groups will be formed. Suggest that you do 2 buffing stations inside the dragon skull room. Do station #1 at left pot and station #2 at right pot. If 3 is possible, do it near the teeth. Clarity should be kept on all buffers and teamed with bards. If there are 2 or more necros, keep chloro and bard singing mana/heal so that the necros can pump mana). Groups with magicians and necros should be buffed last to allow them to feed mana longer.
5. The raid leader will call out a group to get buffed. This group should move to the buff groups and get all of the buffs listed in the next step. Give a peridot to the cleric and the enchanter when ready for those buffs.
6. The buffs should be done in mana/time order to maximize buff time.
  #01 caster - 1 junk buff (e.g. resist cold)
  #02 caster - 1 junk buff (e.g. resist poison)
  #03 caster - 1 junk buff (e.g. resist disease, strength)
  #04 caster - 1 junk buff (e.g. ultravision)
  #05 shaman - talisman of altuna - HP238 (250mana - 72min)
  #06 shaman - stamina - STA37 (~100HP) (100 - 63min)
  #07 cleric - shield of words - AC32 (300 - 72min)
  #08 cleric - heroism - AC20 HP400 (140mana - 72min)
  #09 cleric - symbol of naltron - HP525 Peridot (315mana - 54min)
  #10 enchanter - rune IV / V - DMG620/700 Peridot (350mana - 110min)
  #11 enchanter - group resist magic - MR55 (200mana - 36min)
  #12 enchanter - haste / clarity - (185mana - 16min/75mana - 27min)
  #13 druid - circle of winter - group FR45 (150mana - 36min)
  #14 druid - chloro (200mana - 15min)
  #15 open - for bard resist song
7. Once buffed, the group should disband, OOC done, and camp out.
8. When back at the server selection screen, go to the chat area, and go to the server chat (Cazic-Thule in my case). Wait here until someone gets on and says everyone should log back.
9. Once everyone has been buffed, the temporary buff groups will form groups of 5, buff themselves, and recover mana.
10. Someone will camp and tell the chat room that the attack will start momentarily. Give group leaders a 30 second head start.
11. When you log back in - turn on WALK. Nagafen will cast fear, and it is best to be walk mode. If you have run mode on and he fears you, you may wind up in lava.  Note: VI changed this code, when feared you will always move at a set speed, therefore, it no longer does any good to turn on walk.
12. Reform final groups.
13. Any class that has a pet should summon one.

ASSAULTING NAG
1. Only the raid leader and/or backup leader will shout attack instructions. Smooth raids are ones where everyone knows what to do and follows instructions.
2. [RL: Prepare for attack on the count of 3!]. Bards should start to play Elemental Rhythms with a drum (which should add approximately 71MR, 71CR, and 71FR).
3. [RL: Group 1 and 2 attack]. The main tank groups will rush Nag, activate disciplines (if you have them), and attack. Bards will not attack with hand-to-hand (insignificant melee damage and Nag ripostes can cause significant damage). The safest place for a bard to fight will be directly below stomach near his hind legs. If lag is minimal and it is possible, twist Guardian Rhythms ( 71MR and 10AC), or other buffs.
4. Goal is to push Nag from the bridge to the back wall. This creates more distance from the lava in case someone is feared, and allows clerics to heal from bridge without being in AE range.
5. [RL: Group 3 and 4 attack]. If a lot of melees are present and you have 4-5 groups, two more groups can be sent in once Nagafen is slightly back from the bridge and there is a little more room to work with.
6. Once Nagafen is on back wall, surround him on 3 sides and keep him there.
7. Healers for the tank groups will remain near the bridge.

EARLY INTO FIGHT
8. [RL: Debuf now].  Shamans should immediately begin to attempt to slow/debuf Nag and should OOC successes ("Slowed", "Malosied", etc). If any 53+ characters are present on the raid, they can attempt to attack/debuf/slow at this time. Bards may consider adding a debuf to twist (1 does cold resist, 1 does magic resist).
9. Necros should avoid spells that turn Nag into a skeleton. This makes it very difficult to find the mob with so many people.
10. Healers should remain back at the bridge until needed. If there is only a single person in the group taking damage, they should move back to the bridge and get a heal. This allows the healers to stay out of breath range. If a number of people have taken damage, the healer should come to the group. The safest place for a healer to stand is on the bridge outside breath range.
11. Healers should pay particularly close attention to the bard in their group. Bard resistances are preventing an enormous amount of AE fire damage, and bards have the fewest HP of all melee/hybrids. Bards are the only class that will not come back to the bridge for healing if they are the only one taking damage. Bards are keeping groups alive, keep them alive.
12. Healers/Paladins should call out in OOC any LoH or CH.
13. Necros should pump mana to clerics.

AT 60% HEALTH:
14. [RL: Pets attack now]. Pets are called in to attack.
15. [RL: Tash now]. 1 enchanter should Tash and should OOC "Tashed" if successful. If Nag agros and stays agroed on the enchanter, they should not be healed (hate will be transferred to healer and healer will die). Heal the enchanter if they live.
16. Enchanters will have hard time landing any damage spell on Nagafen. Best use may be to cast rune on Nagafen's current target. 

AT 50% HEALTH
17. [RL: Nuke now]. Nukes and casters will be called in. Remember to get within melee range or your spell will not land. Nuke until Nag is dead.
18. [RL: Reserves Attack now]. Any melee groups held in reserve should attack.
19. If a caster dies, memorize critical spells and rejoin the fight. All rezzes will be done when Nag goes down.
20. If a bard is nearly dead and cannot be healed, they can move away from Nagafen and suicide with Denons Desperate Dirge (dragon targeted) for 300+ damage.

AT 20% HEALTH
21. [RL: Wizards Nuke now]. Wizards are called in at 20% health for nuking.

NAG DEATH
1. Nag's corpse should not decay for 30 minutes until after he dies.
2. The group leader of the killing group should inspect the body and tell the raid leader which items are present.  Some raids will have a trusted member lock the corpse until the raid leader can get to the corpse.
3. No one else should inspect the body. The raid leader will loot and distribute items.
4. All resurrections will be performed at this time. Temporary groups may be assigned with clerics & bards to minimize time.

LOOT RULES
1. Depending on the raid, loot rules can differ widely. Be sure to check before joining the raid. Some items may be claimed by the raid leader or hosting guild.
2. Typically, each class eligible for an item is assigned a number and a random is performed to award the item. If eligible, raid leaders and group leaders should state which numbers they have prior to the roll.
3. Although some items are all/all, some should be restricted to benefit the classes that could use the item the most - as opposed to being up for sale at the EC bazaar minutes after the fight ends:
a. Cloak of Flames: Melees / Hybrids
b. Gauntlets of Firey Might: Monks
c. Red Dragon Scale: Bards & Warriors for epic quest.

MISCELLANEOUS NOTES
It is possible for a level 53+ player to directly assist in a Nag fight, but it is moderated by getting expelled by Nag (other than buffs prior to a fight). If you get placed on his hate list, you will be expelled.
1. Casting a spell on Nag will cause you to get expelled. The spell may or may not land. Spells that cause waves of damage appear to work - subsequent to the first wave.
2. Healing people fighting Nag will result in an expulsion. The heal will generally take, lower level heals (non-CH) will sometimes allow you to cast more than one prior to expulsion.
3. Getting too close to Nag may result in an expulsion.  The agro radius is quite large.
4. Some groups have used CoH (Call of Hero) to heal people. Since CoH wipes a persons hate list, someone can be summoned, have CH cast on them, and return to the fight. Since the hate list was cleared with the CoH, the healer is not placed on hate list.
5. Some raids have used 53+ characters to keep any adds from bothering the raid group.
