Dragon Raid #19 - Lord Nagafen - Aug 15, 2001
Report from raid leader, Brewall Rainsinger, of the Crimson Talon.

LORD NAGAFEN IS DEAD

Lord Nagafen felt the cold grip of death tonight as a band of adventurers led by Brewall descended into his lair.  

Large numbers of people were present and ready to join the raid. I appologize again to anyone I turned away, but it is simply impossible to include everyone. I tried to be as fair as possible picking people, and I do this by taking everyone's name and then going through the list and prioritize people based on: classes, raid+attendance points, and then levels. I turned away 15 people in Sol B and even more as the raid progressed.  The 15 people I turned away at the start were given an attendance point which helps you get into future raids. Keep trying to get in and your persistance will pay off.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Collecting everyone's name takes a while. I never know who is going to show up and I have to enter all this into my spreadsheet.
2. I take extra time and double-check level when epic items are at stake. This is important for Paladins (50+ requirement for book) and Bards/Warriors (50+ for red dragon scale).
3. Once names entered, takes little while to work out who is needed. I work out all the groups and adjust if we are short in critical areas. This means I might take a first-time shaman, enchanter, cleric, or druid over a multi-attendee melee if we are short in these classes. It is exceedingly rare that I ever turn away shm, enc, clr, and bards, since all help the raid go faster and more successfully.
4. I don't lock the list until critical class mixture is met. If cannot get these classes, I take additional people to compensate.
*5. I'm usually buried in tells during a raid, and get average 1 every 10 seconds during raid nights. At the start of most raids, I'm 10+ pages behind on tells (and I am extremely quick entering data and a touch-typist as well). I don't mind receiving tells, but make them informative. Tells like "Me want on raid" are of no help, I can't enter you without class and level and because I'm pages behind on tells, I can't do a \who without risking losing my place. Also, tells that "I need temp group" will not be returned - if you need a temp group, ooc for people looking for groups to turn on lfg and form one yourself. The best raid attendees are those that make it easy on the raid leader. There are a lot of people that are looking for something to do, ask if someone knows how it usually works and chat with them.

When in a temp group, find out if anyone in your group is new. Help them along and answer their questions and tell them how raid generally works. Saves them from sending me tells. Find out where they heard about raid and if they have read loot rules and the dragon guide.
