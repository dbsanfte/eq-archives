Raid #09 Lord Nagafen
Raid Date: July 11 2001

Report from raid leader Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Lord Nagafen felt the cold grip of death tonight as a band of adventurers led by Brewall descended into his lair.  

Originally scheduled for Saturday, VI's patch on Tuesday created another opportunity for dragon killing. If it's patch day, chances are, Brewall will be killing a dragon somewhere.

Large numbers of people were present and ready to join the raid. I appologize again to anyone I turned away, but it is impossible to include everyone. I tried to be as fair as possible picking people, and I do this by taking everyone's name and then going through the list and prioritize people who have been on a lot of raids and then necessary classes. I turned away 13+ people before the fighting even started and even more later on as people getting on later would send me tells.  I do track those that tried to attend and although you don't get a raid point, it does get factored in when I pick people.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Collecting everyone's name takes a while. I never know who is going to show up and I enter all this into my spreadsheet.
2. I take extra time and double-check level when epic items are at stake. This is important for Paladins (50+ requirement for book) and Bards/Warriors (50+ for red dragon scale).
3. Once names entered, takes little while to work out who is needed. I work out all the groups and adjust if we are short in critical areas.

When in a temp group, find out if anyone in your group is new. Help them along and answer their questions and tell them how raid generally works. Saves them from sending me tells.

Excellent job by all listening to instructions, especially during giant pulls. If you have never done a dragon, pay attention to those that have, there is a reason for everything.

Special thanks go out to Altus and Physst for tremendous job pulling the Giants. This is an extremely difficult and important task and I certainly appreciate their efforts. And to all the buffers that evening as well: Khallin, Kauf, Galadriad, Greymask, Aprisle, Zulon, Cerritus, Loen, Ciladin, Azenyth. For all the first time buffers that were there, great job!

OTHER NOTES
* I am awarding Ixnus a point for showing up for raid and then stepping out so that another paladin needing a Book could get a shot at it.
* I am awarding Nhadya a point for taking time to shuttle at least four times to get everyone out of lair.
* Gauntlet's of Firey Might is specified in my raid rules as monk-only roll. The item COULD be used by melees & hybrids, but the 10ac makes it inferior to virtually all Kunark, Plane, and even Solusek Ro quest armours. The resists would be nice for dragons and special occasions, but isn't an everyday item. For monks, it has decent AC compared to their Kunark armour and it is 0.3wt. Therefore, for monks, this is an everyday fantastic item, for everyone else, something that is either sold or put in bank for special raids. I think my decision to do monk only is very rational. If you can make a strong case how your class could use the item just as well, please start a discussion on the bulletin board.
