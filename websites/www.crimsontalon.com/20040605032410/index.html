Experience, Equipment and Tactics
Posted by: Barelil on Wednesday, May 26, 2004 - 03:49 PM (1 Reads)

Oh, the ever evolving Everquest.  I still remember the first time we attempted Dozekar in the Halls of Testing in the Temple of Veeshan.  We thought we were ready, but we found ourselves lacking as Dozekar was nice enough to show us.  Our raid party was probably 50 strong on that night and our spirits high.  We went home that night in defeat.  We did not give up though.  Dozekar had to die and eventually did at our hands.  Of course the level cap at this time was still 60, Luclin had just come out, and no one had entered Vex Thal yet.  

The other night, almost 2 years since we had last stepped foot in HoT, a small group of 10 decided to pay old Dozekar a visit.  With only a cleric, a shaman, and a paladin to offer any form of healing, we managed to beat that old lizard into submission and reap the rewards of our efforts.  It is truly amazing what a difference 5 levels, pre-elemental gear and sound tactics can do against old challenges like those.  Dozekar used to be a mob that guilds used to test their metal.  Now he is just another trophy tucked away in the closet.

Congratulations Altus on getting the Platinum Tear that you needed for your quest.

Rallos Zek: The Enigma
Posted by: Chantress on Thursday, May 20, 2004 - 02:31 PM (5 Reads)

The elemental planes lay before us with only one barrier to entry...Rallos Zek the Warlord.  You may think that Crimson Talon has been latent in its activity as we have posted no news lately, but we have not.  We have been hitting the usual backflagging targets.  Can anyone say three cheers for killing Grummus for the 27th time?  along with a cornicopia of other targets as well.  There stands only one that has gotten the best of us three time thus far.  Let me tell you about it.

Rallos Zek #1- Recipe for disaster:  Take one game that has been in beta testing for about 5 years.  Sprinkle in 72 highly trained people at dealing with said beta version of the game.  Sprinkle in one DX9 upgrade.  Results should be explosive and unpredictable at best.  Such was the case on the first RZ attempt.  We get everything in place.  Everyone is set up perfectly.  The pull is executed flawlessly.  Tallon Zek warps on the pull into the casters, thanks a million Sony.  Wipe raid number 1 in 5 seconds flat.  His AE sucks....hardcore.

Rallos Zek #2- After working with the same effeciency, and this time without the warp, we easily kill both the brothers and the fake RZ.  Mind you, most guilds take months just to get this far.  72 casual players that have RL lives and dont raid hardcore got to the real deal on the second attempt.  Now let me tell you a little about that.  We get in the pit and get set up.  We get enchanters with pets.  We are ready to rock and roll.  Rallos Zek INC!!!  For some odd reason he eats the first enchanter he sees(me) so that puts one hasted pet onto the raid.  Then we get him positioned and start to rock his world.  All said and done...Rallos Zek the Warlord was trembling at 24% health on only our second attempt.

Rallos Zek #3- Pets are the Suck!  Read everything about attempt number one setting up.  That all happened again.  Long story short.  Pet owned us and pulled TZ into the casters.  Guess what....we didnt live through it.  OMG that are the suck!!!!!!.  Anyhow.  He will be roadkill before long.  We will see to that.  Stay tuned for more RZ action as we add him to farmed status in the VERY near future.

Whew!

HoH Trial 1. Halgoz Rellinic and Freegan Haun fell quickly yielding an Intricately Inscribed Band [Missycat] and a Warder's Ornate Broadsword [Celeste].  We also rolled off some ornate armour that was donated by generous guildmembers that have been spending time in Sol Ro's Tower. Ornate Silk Robe Pattern [Darkenloth], Ornate Chain Coif Pattern [Florain], Ornate Silk Sleeve Pattern [Aniin], Ornate Chain Glove Pattern [Ruzamm], and a Cloak of Greater Pernicity [Wynndy].

Aerin'Dar and Rydda'Dar. Definitely a dragon bashing mode this evening when we mericilessly killed two of these beasts in one night. Yielded a number of flags to those that were missing these annoying flags. Some nice loots: Valorous Quarterstaff [Jkar], Honorable Longsword [Pizacatto], and Ivory Etched Aegis [Valtyr].

Terris Thule. We hadn't done her in a while and she was due for a dirt-nap. Not the smoothest kill, but she fell to us never-the-less. Loots of Indigo Snake Bracelet [Bianka], Fearsome Midnight Cloak [Duudah], and Ornate Silk Glove Pattern [Darkenloth].
