Raid #21 Lady Vox
Raid Date: August 22 2001

Report from raid leader Brewall Rainsinger // Bard // Cazic-Thule

Vox dies at the hands of Crimson Talon and other hearty adventurers who joined us in battle.

One of the most interesting nights ever. Mob placement, mob types, pathing, and roamers are all different now. Physst has pulled from the lair 10+ times and realized that things were a lot different this time around.  On top of that, Vox agro range seems to have changed as well. This made pulling that much more difficult and resulted in Vox appearance in the buff area. Took a while to get her back into the lair and to get people rezzed. Still, we were able to kill the beast in the end.

To say that people are interested in killing dragons is an understatement. More and more people show each raid and it's difficult to pick those that make the raid list.  These are truely open attendance raids, there are typically 20+ guilds in attendance, and usually only 5-6 from Crimson Talon, as many are now beyond the 52 dragon cap.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Pulling Vox's guards without agroing Vox can be tricky. Nothing worse than having Vox agro and start summoning people from goblin king area to their death - and I've seen this happen on one bad raid I was on. If it takes a while, it is well worth it.
2. As raid leader, I average 1 tell every 10 seconds and I still have to do groups, buffers, and a number of other things. If you are in a temp group, find out if any are new attendees and help them along if you can, explaining loot, warning about traps, and flow helps a lot.
3. If I say raid is full, it is full. If I say it is too late, it is too late. Sending additional tells won't help. I try to accomodate long-time attendees and work in classes I'm short on, and try to be as fair as possible. Sometimes, I just can't add people and keep things going smoothly. Adding too many to raid risks huge LD problems.
4. I cap Vox attendance at around 48 people. With 70+ people wanting to go, I have to pick and that can take a while. This is not the time to be asking how to get to Permafrost, I'll take the person standing right next to me first.

WHAT WILL BE DONE DIFFERENT FOR THE NEXT RAID?
1. People need to report in when I shout for names. If you do not send in your name, you are not even on my radar to be assigned to a group. It takes me a while to go through tells, and I rarely miss someone.
2. Suggest that Clerics/Enchanters sell peridots for 14pp. At levels 45+ everyone should be carrying a stack of 20 with them at all times. Good habit to get into, if you accept less from guildmates/friends, that is your decision. Hate to see cle/enc tie up tons of pp in peridots for those that don't carry them.
3. Definitely have limits now on people and enforced them last night. No LD and lag was quite reasonable.

OLD INFO ON WDS:
4. As promised when I first started setting raids up, the WDS would open up once the original 4 bards had won scales. I did not foresee 2 dropping last night and did not have my spreadsheet ready for caster/bard odds. On the next raid, the WDS will be open to casters with the following conditions:
-If 3 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the others will be casters+losing bards.
-If 2 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the other will be casters+losing bards.
-If 1 scale drops, the roll will be bards+casters. However, I will multiply each 50-52 bard's raid points by a factor of 5. I have targetted the factor so that the collective numbers that bards have is approximately 40%-50% of the total pool of numbers on the roll. So bards have a 50% chance of winning, otherwise a caster wins it.
