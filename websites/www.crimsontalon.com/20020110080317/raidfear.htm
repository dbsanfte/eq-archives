Plane of Fear Raid Guide
** Last updated 02 October 2001
** Written by Brewall Rainsinger of Cazic-Thule

INTRODUCTION
The Plane of Fear is a level 46+ zone that normally requires a raid to complete. It is the home of the god Cazic-Thule and his minions, including Dracoliche, Fear, Dread, and Terror. This guide covers everything needed to successfully raid Fear, please read it all before attending a raid.

The information in this guide is a compilation of numerous guides I have read along with my observations of both good and bad Fear raids.

MAP
Everlore Map

SETTING UP
The Plane of Fear can be accessed through a tunnel in Ferrot located in the South-West corner of the zone. The entrance itself is hidden on the zone wall, but can easily be found by starting in the zone corner and running east along the hill.

Fear is well-known for break-in difficulty and getting the raid started. Break-in will take quite a bit of time and will involve numerous deaths. Once the raid has started, it is very important that everyone know exactly what to do. Wipe outs are very common if people are not paying attention.

Tanks should bind just inside Ogguk at the entrance. Someone should have an extra character there that can SoW or give out SoW potions to anyone that has died so they can run past the bouncers. This character will also need to be able to cast ultravision or infravision for night-blind tanks.

Casters should bind by the rock outside the Fear portal. This area has no wandering mobs.

Note: Cyndereela is a necro that guards the portal and is of little direct danger. The biggest problem she poses is that she sees invis and an attack from her will strip you of invisibility that you need when entering Fear.

BREAK-IN OPTIONS
There are three major theories on the easiest way to break into fear. Each balances length of run to camp spot versus numbers of mobs. The longer run / higher risk break-ins should only be done with experienced Fear players, just one person who brings a big train can wipe out a lot of staging work.
1. Start at west camp. This guide covers this process. Short run from portal, but have to clear more mobs. Two different methods.
2. Start at southwest camp. Longer run from portal, but fewer mobs to clear. Run W, then run S high on wall.
3. Start at north camp. Longest run from portal, but fewest mobs to clear. Run W, then run N high on wall.

PREPARING TO ENTER - The Scouts
Break in begins with two people that can feign death. One will enter the zone and feign just outside the portal to the west! Feigning slightly to the west will make it easier for people zoning in to orient themselves on which way to run. When the area is clear, the second scout will enter, turn west, run down the hill, and feign at the bottom near the wall. This is where the initial battle will take place, this is often called the "West Camp" or "WC" for short. Ideally, these monks will have rez-sticks in event of catastrophic event during the raid (e.g., server reset in middle of a raid).

PREPARING TO ENTER - Groups
Break in groups are usually formed at this time and should consist of tanks and clerics. These groups are not built to share experience, just to break into the plane. Once the raid is going, groups can be redone. There are numerous suggestions on break-in groups and tactics:
1. 2 tank groups: 5 tanks & 1 cleric || 5 tanks & 1 cleric
2. 2 tank groups: 5 tanks & 1 cleric || 4 tanks, 1 cleric, & 1 enc
3. 2 tank groups: 5 tanks & 1 cleric || 2 tanks, 2 cleric, & 2 enc
4. First wave of all FD classes to WC. They quit to eliminate any residual agro. They return and FD in camp spot and await main groups of tanks. Second wave of 2 tank groups #1-3 from above is then sent in.
5. Have casters duel outside portal area. Get a couple coins upon death and mem biggest nuke spells.

PREPARING TO ENTER - Buffs
Everyone about to enter Fear should consider the following buffs:
1. Talisman of Altuna (shaman)
2. Stamina (shaman)
3. Invisibility or Superior Camo
4. See Invisibility
5. Symbol (cleric)
6. Resolution (cleric)
7. Shield (cleric)
8. Levitate or Dead Man Floating
9. Haste or Clarity
10. Rune
11. Magic Resistance
12. Spirit of Wolf (deactivate upon reaching WC)
13. Chloro
14. Damage Shields
15. Str/Dex/Agi buff 

FIRST BREAK IN - "FIGHT AND DIE" APPROACH
This is the most common tactic to breaking into fear. It features breaking in, fighting (killing a couple mobs), dying, rebuffing, and repeating). Once groups are set up and buffed, the first rush to the West Camp will begin. Goal is to get a strong beach head in the zone and clear out roaming mobs near the WC. There are about 12 roamers that will need to be killed, and this does not include other roamers near the portal or the adjacent areas. Agro ranges in Fear are absolutely huge.
1. The two FD monks will give all-clear. These monks will not fight during any of the break-in attempts.
2. Raid leader will have all extra monks and shadow knights zone-in and run to the WC. They will FD at the WC and then quit to avoid any residual agro. Wait a couple minutes and come back on. Remain FD at the WC for the main assault groups. Their job is to help with initial fights, and FD when badly injured.
3. When all clear is given again, the first two groups will zone into Fear.
4. When you zone in, you turn yourself west (orient yourself with the FD monk who is lying west of the portal) and run straight over them to the west zone wall to the WC.
  a. If you are attacked on the way to the WC, stay there, fight, and die. If several of you are attacked, get on the same mob.
  b. Critical that you do not bring mobs to the WC. Do not run to camp if you are attacked. You can be pulled to SS for a rez sometime later.
  c. Do not run around the zone if you are attacked. This can stir up the zone with chain agros and it may take a long time to get your corpse back.
  d. If a scareling teleports you away from the camp, get your bearings on the camp. If you are agroed in any way, stay there (get a loc). If you are clear for 15+ seconds, slowly make your way back to the WC. Never bring a mob with you.
5. Everyone needs to stand extremely close to one another in the WC. Do not stray more than 1-2 steps from anyone else. Need to occupy as little space as possible to avoid wanderer agro.
6. Eventually a wandering mob will agro and will attack the WC camp. Goal is to kill as many of these mobs as possible. Each one killed makes subsequent attempts easier. Everyone needs to attack the same target.
7. Once you are in WC, turn on walk and turn off SoW in the event that a turmoil toad attacks and fears you.
8. The raid leader may then wait for all clear again and send in naked nuke casters as a second wave.

SUBSEQUENT BREAK IN'S - "FIGHT AND DIE" APPROACH
It is exceptionally rare that the first break-in attempt succeeds. There are simply too many mobs to be killed and too many things that go wrong.
1. Anyone that dies should make their way back to the portal and get buffed again. Best to keep a magician outside that can summon basic weapons, 5 food, and 5 water. Someone also needs to have extra gems for cleric and enchanter buffs. Extra SoW person in Oggok makes this much quicker process.
2. With enough people assembled, wait for the all-clear and repeat the attack.

FIRST BREAK IN - "FIGHT AND FLEE" APPROACH
This tactic is not a widely practiced break-in method. It features breaking in, fighting & nuking, and then evacing. Goal is to kill roamers and adds one at a time.
1. One FD monk near the portal and is responsible for telling everyone when to zone. A second monk will be located on the west wall. Everyone should have a hotkey set up to assist this monk to make target selection easier.
2. 2-4 groups, all with an evac person and mezzer, will zone in at once, run to west wall with any adds. Everyone should stay extremely close to one another. Kill 1 mob, while enchanters chain cast AE mez. At first sign of trouble evac or when 1 mob is killed - a train is coming - get out before it arrives!
3. Repeat process of damage/kill, evac, damage/kill, and evac. Can never evac too early for first few mobs while it is still crowded. Repeat until portal mobs are dead, then work on west side.
4. For those that die, see "Subsequent Break In's - Fight and Die Approach" above.

THE WEST CAMP
Once the 11-13 wanderers have been killed near WC, the plane is effectively broken, but it will still be risky for a while. Scouts should give all clear when portal wanderers are away and additional groups can move to the WC, rez, and begin clearing the rest of the plane.

With everone in the west camp, you have made past the first stage, but the raid is still extemely risky. There are still a lot of wandering mobs just outside this area. Everyone needs to remain very close to one another. Snare is extemely important at this time.
1. Stay in WC for about 30 minutes killing any wandering mob that is nearby.
2. If there is another mob close to camp while puller is further out, the closer mob should be killed first.
3. Pullers should clear any wandering mobs that are near the portal area.
4. Pullers should clear any wandering mobs that are between WC and the SW corner.
5. Camp will move to the SW corner to add some space so that people don't need to be standing on top of one another.

THE SOUTHWEST CAMP & Killing Dracoliche
1. Any remaining nearby wanderers are killed.
2. Wanderers near the Fetid Fiend house to the north are pulled.
3. Fetid Fiend house is pulled, and then any wanderers to the north of the house.
4. Pull east along the south wall. Best to chain-pull this area.
5. Pull the area past the portal area to get the tentacle terrors.
6. Have everyone rebuff and pull Dracoliche.
  a. Summon pets if you have them
  b. Critical buffs are save disease, save poison, and magic.
  c. Biggest weapon is AE Fear and Rotting Flesh (poison based) DoT.
  d. Cazic will be agroed when Draco is attacked. It is important to kill Draco as quickly as possible because Cazic will continue to death-touch people every 30 seconds throughout the battle. When battle is over, log out. Someone from kill group should check corpse first.
  e. Undead spells will not work on Dracoliche.
7. Pull from far north, but not past the fire wall.
8. Pull from east. Pull any wanderers, then the gorgons, the frightfinger house, and check again for wanderers.
9. Move camp to South Camp

THE SOUTH CAMP & Killing Dread, Terror, and Fright
1. Pull Dread and his 2 Amy knights/warriors.
2. Pull Terror and his 2 Amy knights/warriors.
3. When Cazic-Thule moves away, pull Fright and his 2 minions.
4. Pull remaining mobs on south wall.
5. Move camp to Southeast Camp.

THE SOUTHEAST CAMP
1. Pull wanderers on East wall.
2. Pull static spawns on East wall.
3. Move camp to Northwest Camp.

THE NORTHWEST CAMP & Clearing the Temple
Pulling the temple takes quite a bit of planning, but with the zone virtually empty of all other mobs, there is a large area to the south for kiting mobs.
1. Get highest level necro available to go to the portal area and  cast a fresh SoW on them. They should then cast harmshield upon themselves, run into the temple to agro every mob (harmshield will absorb the harmtouches), run to South wall (with a huge train of mobs), and then gate out as quickly as possible.
2. Once mobs start returning to the temple, have a freshly buffed person kite some of the stragglers around the portal hill.
3. While they are being kited, and all the other mobs have returned to the temple, have a puller grab a couple and bring them back to the camp. Repeat this until all kited mobs are dead.
4. When the kited mobs are all dead, repeat the agro process from step 1. Hopefully, all the harm touches have been expended with the initial necro clearing. If in doubt, repeat necro process from step 1, if no harm touches are left, someone else with SoW can re-agro the temple.  
5. Repeat until temple is clear.
6. Have rangers check zone for any remaining mobs other than Cazic.

CAZIC-THULE
1. Remove all buffs and get 4 junk buffs.
2. Get shaman, druid, cleric, & enchanter buffs.
3. Get levitate or dead man floating last.
4. Leave spot open for bard resist magic song.
5. Select someone to eat the first death touch. Have them hail or cast on Cazic.
6. As soon as Cazic is attacked, he will summon all remaining mobs in the zone to his aid. Enchanters should charm these mobs and use them as pets to attack Cazic.
7. When the sacrifice is killed by the first death touch, the tank groups should rush in and attack.
8 Cazic dual hits in the 300s-400s, grav-fluxes almost constantly, will dispell buffs, and will death-touch every 30 seconds.
9. At 50%, casters will nuke hard, and with Cazic's low magic resistance, will have an easy time.
10. Everyone needs to stun, bash, and slam Cazic as much as possible to disrupt his casting.
11. Cazic is vulnerable to fear spells. Fear him as much as possible during the fight and DoT him heavily.

LOGGING AND ZONING
1. The only exit from Fear is porting or gating. There is also a portal in the middle of the temple that will take you out of the zone.
2. Melee characters may want to have a gate potion for emergencies.
3. Once the zone is clear, the raid leader may instruct everyone to log at: Ferrot camp, SW corner, or N camp (usually called the SS).

THE MOBS
All mobs are on a 13-14 hour spawn cycle except for Cazic & Draco who are on 1 week cycles.
Amygdalan Knights - Shadowknights. Harm touch and mana drain. Drops Umbral armour (SK).
Amygdalan Warrior - Warriors. High MR. Drops shield and whip for bard epic quest.
Boogeymen - Ghoulroot. Drops Blighted armour (NEC).
Cazic-Thule - See above. Numerous drops.
Dracoliche - See above. Numerous drops.
Dread - Blind. Numerous drops.
Fetid Fiends (Male) - Disease. Drops Valorium armour (PAL).
Fetid Fiends (Female) - No special skills. Drops cleric girdle .
Fright - Blind. Numerous drops.
Frightfingers - Wizards. Wizard spells. Stun as much as possible to interrupt. Drops Carmine armour (WIZ).
Glare Lords - Encanters. AE debuf / they do not charm.
Gorgons - 15 second stun. Drops Vermiculated armour (DRU).
Nightmares - Blind & Fear. Drops cloak.
Phantasms - No skills. Drops nothing.
Phoboplasms - AE 1 debuf. Drops any armour from either plane.
Scarelings - Rouge. 400hp backstab, shadowstep you, and run at SoW speed. Extremely important to kill these as quickly as possible. Drops rogue rapier .
Shamains - No skills. Drops ranger axe.
Shiverbacks - No special skills. Drops Shiverback armour (MNK).
Spinechiller Spiders - Cold DoT 300hp in 30sec. Drops Cryosilk Armour (WIZ, ENC, NEC, MAG).
Tentacle Tormentors - Lifetap, throwback, AE debuf. Keep back to wall when fighting these and stun as much. as possible.  Thorny Vine Armour (RNG) & druid belt.
Terror - Blind. Numerous drops.
Turmoil Toads - Fear. Drops turmoil warts and shaman belt.
Worry Wraiths - Damage shield. Need to be dispelled quickly. Drops nothing.

CLASS NOTES
Bards - Able to charm any mob. Bards should not be in pull group or they will die from any adds that come back. Keep MR song ready for Gorgons, Nightmare, Scareling, Tentacle, and Toad fights. Keep DR song ready for male Fetid Fiends. Mez song works well to interrupt casting mobs and to place mobs that are pushed into trees during fights where people aren't listening.
Druids -  Animal charm is powerful tool to get a hard hitting level 50 mob as an ally. Important to snare mobs. Nuke Scarelings hard to kill them quickly.
Enchanters - Able to charm any mob. Group Resist Magic is vital buff to keep up.
Magicians - Key to early part of raid with summoned items. Call of Hero can be used to rescue those that were teleported away from the camp.
Necromancer - Undead charm is powerful tool to get a hard hitting level 50 mob as an ally. Fear works well on mini-bosses and on Cazic (need to be snared first). Many fights are long and DoTs are very useful.
Rangers - Important to snare mobs.
Wizards - Do more casting on dangerous mobs, such as Scarelings and Tentacle Tormentors that teleport party members away from camp area. These mobs need to be killed as quickly as possible.

PULLER NOTES
1. Chances of death are very high if you have little experience pulling in Fear. If you don't like dying, don't pull.
2. Mob agro ranges are huge in fear and pathing is quirky. Most good pullers are patient, they move out and wait a bit for any wanderer to agro. If within 30 seconds, nothing comes they move further out.
3. Get as much MR as possible. Gorgons stun, Knights harmtouch, and Toads can fear.
4. If you think you may have a lot of agro, quit out. Better for raid to wait for you to come back than to have Cazic start death-touching people.
5. 51+ monks with stonestance will die far less frequently. Use it when FD fails and multiple mobs are on you.
6. Don't turn spell effects off. Turn on lowest setting if you need to. Much easier to spot some of the smaller mobs that cast.
7. No one should cast a spell on you unless you are back at camp. If they do cast on you while you are out and a train of mobs come, they should run away from the camp and die. Do not have a bard in pull group; if multiple mobs come back, they will head straight for the bard because of song taunting.
8. Be certain you have gotten all wanderers before you move the camp.
9. If things are going very smoothly and people are getting bored, bring back 2-3 mobs each time.
10. Don't train the camp. Far easier to just get you rezzed than having camp wiped out.
11. Chain pull wherever possible. Especially so with the scareling tower.
12. May want to get item to allow you to permanently see infravision or ultravision in the event your nighvision buff is dispelled.
13. Keep 3-4 junk buffs to protect good buffs from being easily dispelled.

MISCELLANEOUS NOTES
1. In the event of a wipe-out, like any other raid, get a couple FD scouts there first, followed by cleric to rez, enchanter to clarity, bards (as many as possible, mana song stacks), and then start main rezzing everyone else. If mobs attack during this process, bards should protect clerics while they camp.

LOOT RULES
Full Loot List: Allakhazam's List
1. Loot rules vary widely on plane raids. Before joining one, check what the rules are. Rules could vary from completely closed loot, unneeded items only are open, all items but BP/epic/god are open, to all items are open.
2. Some multi-guild raids take turns at loot based on how many groups each guild brought other raids just may have all the classes do a random. If random is done, I suggest setting seed at start of raid and having each person set up a hotkey with the random roll to keep things going faster.
3. Some raids have loot coordinator that will track items you have won and limit number of items for the raid until everyone else has won. Sometimes winning a BP/Epic/God item will limit your ability to win additional items that raid.
4. Some raids give points for every hour you are on the raid to increase odds as items and carry-over time from prior raids.
5. Most raids will have a gem collector that should receive all gems to cover costs of peridots used for buffing. Good gems (blue diamonds/diamonds) can be randomed off at the end of the raid, or made into jewelry and randomed off.
