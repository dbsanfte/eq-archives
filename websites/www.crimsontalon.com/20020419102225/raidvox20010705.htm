Raid #08 - Lady Vox - Jul 05, 2001
Report from raid leader, Brewall Rainsinger, of the Crimson Talon.

LADY VOX IS DEAD

Vox dies for a seventh time at the hands of Crimson Talon and other hearty adventurers who joined us in battle.  Interest in the raids is very high, and I had to turn away no less than 15 people.

Word has definitely gotten out about the Vox raids, and I am amazed at how many people showed up early. Signing up on EverquestRaids is no guarantee that you will have a spot, nor does it indicate the exact starting time. Quite a difference from a month ago when I had cobbled together just 38 people, and felt lucky to get that, now, tons of people are showing up. To anyone I had to turn away, I appologize, I have to limit attendance otherwise things are out of control.

The two buff stations worked out well again. People are really getting the hang of it. Great opportunity for capitalists to sell peridots. Always seems to be people around without them.

If you ever wonder what is going on or why things are taking so long, just keep some things in mind:
1. Pulling Vox's guards without agroing Vox can be tricky. Nothing worse than having Vox agro and start summoning people from goblin king area to their death - and I've seen this happen on one bad raid I was on. If it takes a while, it is well worth it.
2. As raid leader, I average 1 tell every 10 seconds and I still have to do groups, buffers, and a number of other things. If you are in a temp group, find out if any are new attendees and help them along if you can, explaining loot, warning about traps, and flow helps a lot.
3. If I say raid is full, it is full. If I say it is too late, it is too late. Sending additional tells won't help. I try to accomodate long-time attendees and work in classes I'm short on, and try to be as fair as possible. Sometimes, I just can't add people and keep things going smoothly.

Excellent job by all listening to instructions. Pulling Vox out into middle of room was very well executed and excellent job surrounding her. Vox managed one CH, but did her little good.

Special thanks go out to Altus for pulling of the Ice Giants and Vox's guards. This is an extremely difficult and important task and I certainly appreciate his efforts. And to the buffers that evening as well: Khallin, Zulon, Kauf, Galadriad, Loen, Lilcure, Mysyn, Cilden, Larea, Aprisle, and Laurax. For all the first time buffers that were there, great job!

WHAT WILL BE DONE DIFFERENT FOR THE NEXT RAID?
1. Hard cap of 42 attendees for next Vox raid. People have been upgrading their CR and getting much better at attack. Rumors of 'Vox upgrade' I had been hearing were false. She is no harder than before.
2. Going to have better method for doing bards offering trades. Made mistake this time by missing Lovern (alt is Larea) on the first roll. Appologies to Kauf, but by including raid points in ability to make trade, stand excellent chance the next time a caster wins.
3. Suggest that Clerics/Enchanters sell peridots for 14pp. At levels 45+ everyone should be carrying a stack of 20 with them at all times. Good habit to get into, if you accept less from guildmates/friends, that is your decision. Hate to see cle/enc tie up tons of pp in peridots for those that don't carry them.

OLD INFO ON WDS:
4. As promised when I first started setting raids up, the WDS would open up once the original 4 bards had won scales. I did not foresee 2 dropping last night and did not have my spreadsheet ready for caster/bard odds. On the next raid, the WDS will be open to casters with the following conditions:
-If 3 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the others will be casters+losing bards.
-If 2 scales drop, 1 will be bard only, the other will be casters+losing bards.
-If 1 scale drops, the roll will be bards+casters. However, I will multiply each bard's raid points by a factor of 5. I have targetted the factor so that the collective numbers that bards have is approximately 40%-50% of the total pool of numbers on the roll. So bards have a 50% chance of winning, otherwise a caster wins it. Many of the bards that have won are continuing to attend with hopes of getting other dragon items, so it may be that the only people rolling on WDS are casters.
