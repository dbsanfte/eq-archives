The Black Market

While it may seem Unfair that the primary tool of the reanimator has been declared anathema, a moment's observation will confirm that this has not in any way prevented the necromancer from purchasing what is required. Clearly, there are vendors who carry such accoutrements.

How is this accomplished? The method is at once both absurdly Simple and deviously Effective. All staves created for merchant sale are required by King's decree to show the profession for whom they are intended, the better to avoid accidental or intentional deception in sales. Necromantic staves have no such marker, only runes suggestive of their intended use. Thus, should the merchant's inventory be inspected by one of the King's guard, the forbidden tool will appear to be merely a “found” item acquired in trade – with no indication that there are hundred more hidden in a padded box beneath the floorboards.

The savvy Necromancer will, of course, be intimately familiar with these macabre runes and have no trouble in distinguishing which staff may be best suited for personal use. The only challenge is in finding the merchants who ply this proscribed trade.

The Shores of Atlantis

Although the Avalonians may appear somewhat Lax in their treatment of contraband, those who have taken up residence in the Atlantean ruins are the embodiment of Disinterest. Focused as everyone is on their own Projects, a merchant could likely sell Arthur's very bones without fear of notice.

Ruins of Atlantis
Name Alifa
Locale A tent in the archaeological dig near the Hall of Heroes.
Favorite Color Deep indigo
Hobbies Smiling at passers-by, listening to the Soothing sound of waves lapping upon the shore, admiring pieces of newly-uncovered Atlantean inscriptions, waiting for the slightest Opportunity to snatch the halberd from its rack and Show these people the true meaning of misery.
 

The canny may wonder at their observation that the Inconnu do not make trade in their one most Needed piece of equipment; and indeed, any Necromancer is exquisitely careful to avoid concurrent possession of more than a few ensorcelled staves, certainly never keeping the inventories required of a successful merchant. When questioned upon this fact the Inconnu remain silent, offering only the shadow of a smile before turning away.
