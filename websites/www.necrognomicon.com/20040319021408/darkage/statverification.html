What Transfers to the Pet, and When

"Necromancer" refers to the necromancer without a pet. "Shade" refers to the necromancer while a pet is under command. "Pet" refers to any of the summoned undead. Question marks signify information that is believed to be true, but has not been adequately tested.

It is accepted as given that Piety, Empathy, and Charisma have no effect on any ability of the necromancer, shade, or pet.

Bonuses and Effects from Items

Most item bonuses are applied to the pet Continuously and affect the pet while the item is worn. A few bonuses only apply on the Summoning of the pet, and the item can be removed without ill effect afterwards.

In general, weapons only work normally for the necromancer; the shade is unable to melee and the pet does not use the weapon or inherit any of the weapon's qualities (DPS, Speed, Quality, Condition, Enchantment Bonus, Proc, Charges). The same applies to armor, which works normally for the necromancer; the shade can't be hit in melee and the pet doesn't use the armor or inherit any of the armor's qualities (AF, Absorb, Quality, Condition, Enchantment Bonus, Reverse Proc, Charges). Any general bonuses, such as a bonus to Strength, that a piece of armor or a weapon may possess are applied as is normal for the type of bonus.

Items used directly (charged items, /use, /use2, /interact) function properly for the necromancer but most cannot be used in shade form. Exceptions exist, such as water-breathing potions. Effects from items which automatically apply to the item's user do not transfer to the pet.

Caveat Magus

In some cases, the details concerning when and if something transfers to the summoned pet have been known to change without warning or comment. In other cases, even keen discernment may be unable to determine whether or not an effect transfers. If personal experience suggests something working in a different manner than presented here, trust experience.
