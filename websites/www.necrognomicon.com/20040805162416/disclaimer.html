And while we're OOC...

Over the last year, there's been a lot of talk about the general degradation of the necromancer as a viable class. Powerful for 50 levels despite all of the changes, and then a steep decline in usefulness after 50; a decline so steep as to be crippling. Weak spell upgrades. Useless spells. Fragile pets. A general inability to contribute to most fights. A tapering off of power when other classes grow tremendously. A laughable epic quest. The conversion of a first-rate damage-dealer to a third-rate support role.

I share your pain, truly. But, my friends...

Get over it. These attitudes are not welcome here.

While I acknowledge that there certainly are problems, I've heard from too many necromancers who haven't been crippled by them. There are ways, and there are ways. If your ways aren't working, find better ways.

It's also wise to keep in mind that many changes will undoubtedly occur over time. While changes tend to be both good and bad, I believe the trend will be in our favor. To be completely honest, I believe the trend will be greatly in our favor.

If you have a problem with that, well, write your own book.
