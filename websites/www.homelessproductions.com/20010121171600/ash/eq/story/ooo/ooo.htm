Order or Chaos?

My guildmaster has always told me that there are many different powergroups in our world, and that they all deserve respect...even if the said powergroup is "evil". I suppose my guildmaster believes in balance, and as such, evil is a nessasary part of life.

There was a time that I might have scoffed at such a remark. Until I met Helgrim, of the Order of Oblivion. They want chaos to spread around the world, reguardless of the fact that it says "order" in their name. Maybe they should call themselves the Chaos of Oblivion...hmmm.

At any rate, I was approached by Helgrim, and he asked me to join his party, as they wanted to hunt giant spiders, and were in need of a wizard.

I had to think long and hard about this, as I knew I would be helping them improve themselves, however, I needed a party to gain better insight into my art, so I accepted.

During the course of our adventure, we cam across a ranger named Rhinno, and a bard named Persecutis. I did not get along well with Persecutis, with all his singing and shouting while we were fighting, but I can not deny the fact that it inspired us somehow.

Our adventure was short lived. We began by slaying spiders, and grizzley bears without much trouble. After one close encounter with a grizzley bear, I turned and saw the biggest creature I have ever seen. A hill giant.

It was huge, towering well above the trees, and swinging a hammar, made from an ancient oak, and a large boulder. Everywhere he went, destruction was wrought. You might think that they would be easy to outrun...on the contrary, they are extreamly quick, as two of my comrades soon found out.

I backed up, and hid behind a tree, hoping the stupid giant didn't notice. Helgrim, who also saw the beast shouted, "Rhinno, Pers...RUN!!!"

The two took heed to the warning, however it came a bit too late. I watched in horror as the giant swung once with his hammer, and took the lives of both of my friends. Although Rhinno came back to join us, I fear we may have lost Persecutis forever.

After a bit more hunting, Helgrim said he needed to go to Qeynos for supplies. Rhinno and I decided to travel together a bit more, and hope for better luck.

Upon seeing a giant spider, we attacked, and prevailed with little difficulty. However, as I bent down to see what was in the belly of the beast, I saw some strange colored hair creeping up on us. A Scarecrow!

I tried to back up, but the creature was much too fast, and it slayed me with one swipe. Rhinno valiently tried to heal me before I fell, but a brown bear kept him from accomplishing his goal. It was not long that I appeared-yet again-in front of Qeynos.

What did I learn from this journey? I suppose that the world really is a balanced place, for Helgrim was not much different than I, a humble follower of the Karana. I also learned that caution is much advisible, even when traveling in a party. Each time we were careless, someone died. Therefore, I will always watch my back.

Rhinno and I decided after the last incident to part ways, however, we will join up again some other time.

I traveled back to Qeynos in reletive safety, and went back to my Innroom, only to find a letter from my good friend, Aldarin. He told me he found a little known spot in which to hunt Gnolls. I suppose I'll try to contact him, and see what this is all about.
