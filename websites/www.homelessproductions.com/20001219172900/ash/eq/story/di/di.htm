Trials

I have done it!

The night was dark when it happened, and things looked rather bleak. My newfound friend, and fellow hunter, Rhinno, and I were chasing a grizzley bear near a farm in the Wester Plains of Karana. We should have been afraid of the strong beasts, but we were flowing together, and fear seemed a mile away.

Fear of giant spiders was a thing of the past. We were able to take them anytime we wanted, with Rhinno slashing off legs, and me burning the hair of their backs. We were quite a team.

But, as I was saying, I did it! After much ado, we finally killed the grizzley bear. It was a sight to see it fall, after all the hard work. My mental powers were drained, and I was fairley injured, but the victory was sweet.

We found an abandoned farmhouse nearby and decided to rest inside of it. I began to meditate, and the world around me went dark, as I entered the soft, comforting blackness.

Suddenly, a flash of pain enveloped me, and my darkness turned a glaring red. I felt as though I was flying in the air. My flight ended with a thud, and a huge pain in my back, even as I heard a familiar voice shouting, "Gil, wake up and run!"

I opened my eyes to see that yet another grizzley was trying to kill me, no doubt the mate of the one we killed. I had just enough mana for one spell, and I knew that one spell would not kill the bear--at least not any spells that I knew of. I quickly cast a Fade spell, and hoped that I would teleport far enough away from the beast to survive.

Apparantly, my prayers were heard, for I got away, completly unsure of Rhinno's fate. But, as I was running away, clouds came, and darkness set in, and I became hopelessly lost. Feeling abandonded, I frantically searched for one of the towers. The Plains of Karana are not a safe place for an injured wizard, alone. If not bandits, then hill giants, treants, or scarecrows. There are several things one can meet his doom in the wild plains.

But soon, I came upon two young fighters, who had been attacked by a giant spider. They were shouting for help, and had I not come, would have surely died. I knew that if they died, the spider would come for me next. Since I was already injured and very low on mana, I should have run away, to face danger another day.

But I did not.

I came to the fool's rescue by casting one shock of fire spell, and then leaning into Melee with the giant snake fang that Duca had brought me. I slayed the giant spider in no more than two or three minuets. The fighters, greatly appreciative, escorted me to the nearest tower.

I collapsed on the floor, greatful to still be alive, and feeling pretty good about rescuing the young men. But something was troubling me: Rhinno!

I was unable to find him, and I wondered what had happened to him. As I finished my meditation, I heard a familiar voice behind me. "Good to see you again, Gilgadet."

Of course it was Rhinno, and he was ok. Apparantly, the grizzley bear started to follow me, but when I cast the Fade spell, it changed it's mind, and chased him. There was a farmer nearby who helped Rhinno by attacking the bear, even as he ran away. I suppose we were both lucky that day.

After a few more moments of speaking to each other, I told Rhinno that I needed to go see my guildmaster. I wanted to find out if there were any spells that directly effected bears, or if there was any room to study such things.

After the long trek to Qeynos from the plains, and upon reaching my guild in South Qeynos, I saw my guildmaster. He smiled and said, "Gilgadet, you have passed the trials of the seventh circle! For your courage and willingness to help those weaker than yourself, you have entered the next stage of your journey!"

I was very pleased, as I knew that in the 8th, there were a multitude of new skills and spells to be had. Unfortunatly, the cost of research is great. I was able to buy only one spell that I have been practising since early this morning. It is a weapon of devistation called Fire Bolt. I will have to be careful with this one.

And so you see why I was so jubilent in my claims of, "I did it!". It is not often that one passes into a new section of life. A promotion. And when one does, it is a means to celebrate. Perhaps, if I didn't have all this research to do, I would. However, the path I have chosen, or rather, the path that my mother has chosen for me, is a difficult one. But if I am to protect myself from those that would harm me, this is exactly what I must do.
