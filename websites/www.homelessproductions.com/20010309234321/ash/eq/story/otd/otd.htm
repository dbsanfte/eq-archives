One of Those Dayz

WHAM! Today I was hit in the head with harsh reality. It was just one of those days...or so I hope. What began as a simple attempt to further my studies into the arcane arts became a matter of life and death...or death and death...

It all started in Qeynos hills. I heard from a traveler that there was a child missing in Qeynos, and that a guard there was offering much money for someone to look into it. Since my childhood was so jaded, and cold, I could not bear to hear of such atrocities. So I decided to go into the city and look into it.

After much digging around, I met a fisherman near the bank. He told me that the child had fallen into the aqueducts, but also warned me of many monsters lurking below the city. I had heard tales of monsters living down below, but passed them off as rumors and sagas to scare children from diving into the depths. As it turns out, the rumors are true.

On first look, it is simple. The first few rooms are full of spiders, and rats, much like most basements in Qeynos. Upon swimming further, however, there are some fearful creatures.

I was first attacked by a giant translucent cube which shivers and shakes, and makes strange sounds, as it sucks its prey inside. It somehow became aware of me, even though it has no eyes, and started trying to suck me into itself. I cast several Fire Shocks, and a few Numbing Colds, and finally put it to rest. From it's insides, it dropped several silver and copper counts, along with the bones of it's past prey.

I began to get confident in my abilities. Perhaps too confident. I ran through the maze, and became very disoriented and lost, all trying to find some evidence of the girl. It was not long before I was hopelessly lost.

I sat against a wall down there, trying to figure out how to get back out. But there was no hope in that. So I took to walking through the tunnels, and swimming in a general direction, hoping against hope that I could find a way out. It was not long before I ran into a fearful creature, who identified himself as Dracos.

Before he began savagely attacking me with a rusty two handed sword, he said, "forgive me". I might have been able to take him, but his sword was laced with a deadly poison.

I fell to my death upon the floor of this sewer, not sure what to do, and only hoping that the gods granted me yet another life. Luckily, I soon found myself standing in front of Qeynos, the city to which I am bound. I ran back to the aqueduct entrance, and dove in, hoping to find my body, and promising myself that I would pay more attention this time to where I was.

Unfortunately, I became just as lost as the last time, and I could not find my body. I was hungry, cold and tired, and I almost swore I would never help anyone out again. That was when I ran into a necromancer named Somatosis.

I had heard that necromancers had an uncanny ability to find peoples dead bodies. I suppose that is a good thing--at least it was in my case. I had always heard, though, that it was a very easy process. It was not.

It took us a few hours to locate my body, and when I found it, I was horrified. My own face was puffy, and my body reeked of decomposition. The poison from the feared Dracos had turned some of my skin green. I quickly took my meager belongings, and hoped for the best.

As to finding the way out, I was lucky. I fell into yet another pool of water, and was sucked out of the aqueducts, and thrown into the ocean west of Qeynos. I decided to give up the quest for the girl for another day.

I quickly rested up, and got my spells ready, and went to Qeynos Hills. I was intending to buy some blank spell scrolls for my guild, but I got sidetracked at the ruins. There were a few skeletons running around, and not a lot of people, so I decided to help in the fight. I was not even scared of Dread Skeletons, because I had gotten away so easily before with the use of my Fade spell.

That turned out to be my mistake. An acquaintance of mine, Faklor, was being attacked by a dread skeleton. I didn't think he could survive the onslaught that they bring, and I thought I could get away. So I chased it down, and attacked it, to get it off of him. I turned and cast the spell, but to my dismay, it did not work.

I tried again, but still: nothing. The skeleton behind me continued to stab me in the back with his sword. Pain enveloped me, and once again--I was standing in front of Qeynos.

I sighed, thanked the gods again, and ran back to the ruins to get my body, fully intending to get to the Glade, and buy those spell scrolls. However, upon recovering my body, Isis, a monk of an unknown religion, told me that Faklor had run off to the Gnoll caves. I thought he might need some help, so I ran off after him.

This mistake put me in the most feared position of my whole life. I made it there just fine. I was standing near the entrance to their lair, and I saw a hollow tree. Curious, I walked slowly to the entrance...the ground beneath my feet gave way, and I fell about twenty feet, into ice cold water. I swam to the side, and looked around.

Everywhere I looked, I saw Gnolls. From scouts to Elite guards, I was completely surrounded. I had learned some tricks from Chrom back when we were younger, and I snuck around everything as best as I could. I was not spotted. Suddenly, just as I peered over the top of some rocks, I saw twenty Gnolls running. I figured they were running toward the entrance, so I followed them.

There, I met a few people nice enough to help me out of the caves, and for once during this adventure, I did not die.

In fact, I even made a little money. But not nearly enough to come that close to death. I will not return to that hideous place alone for quite some time. I also learned, just as our Guildmaster often said, overconfidence is a wizards worst enemy.
