Adventures With Aldarin

The old man once again sat in his rocker, on the old wooden porch and stared off into the distance. I awaited his words: The continuation of the story of Gilgadet.

But the words did not come.

Instead, he handed me a book. It was Gilgadets journal, and other pieces of paper, written by others. The old man got up and walked away, leaving the story of Gilgadet to me.

I began to read:

It was not long ago that I stumbled upon a great man named Aldarin. It was simply by chance that we met at all. I was in Qeynos hills, looking for wolves, when I was suddenly attacked by three Gnoll Watchers. Aldarin came to my rescue with a few arrows planted in the right spots, and a few quick strikes by his axe. It was obvious that with my magic, and his blade, we could make a little money together, not to mention share some good times.

After a bit of rest, Alderin asked if I wanted to go to the ruins and fight some skeletons. We had heard rumors of the vile beasts attacking any who went to see the beauty of the ruins.

I had not been to see them myself, for I have spent far too much time studying magic, to prepare myself for the coming attack of my father. To finally be able to see these places that I have heard about brings me great joy!

Our first few fights with these skeletons brought us nothing. They were quite weak, and the time was unspectacular. However, it was not at all long before we had a battle with a fearsome beast known as a dread skeleton.

It was quite a bit more powerful than it's cousins, and we came upon it battling a noble warrior. We stood in fascination for a few moments, even after the warrior shouted for help. We joined in the fight, and got in a few good hits, too. It was not long, however, that my mental powers were too drained to do battle with it.

I almost collapsed with exhaustion just as the warrior whom we were helping fell to the ground, dead. Knowing that my new found friend would be getting attacked next, I sprang after it, and attack with my feeble dagger.

It did no good. The skeleton slashed Aldarin one final time before he too, fell to his death. Knowing that I was next, I ran towards the tunnel to the Glade. With the feared skeleton at my back, I dodged his blows, and made it to the tunnel, just as two of the posted guards attacked and killed it.

Turns out this was my lucky day. I sat and waited while Aldarin was restored. It is said that cats have nine lives....I wonder how many we get.

At any rate, after the incident, we went our separate ways, knowing that one day we will travel together again. I also learned a lesson....Always have a Fade spell memorized.
