Karana: Rain on me Again

The rain came down harder than it had earlier in the morning. The wizard Carlion grumbled, and shoved his sleeping bag into his backpack.

"Does Karana not keep his servants dry, even as the rest of us suffer in this blasted rain?"

"Nay, friend. The power of the storms are quieted by His might," said Kelethan, a cleric of Karana. Indeed, he felt empowered by the wind and the rain.

"Do not fret, friends, I know of a cave not far from here where Karana will not touch us," I said, hoping silently that my chain mail would not become rusted.

After a quick packing session, our camp was clean, and we left in the dark of night towards the cave that I knew of.

Our adventures had begun after we met at a place called Crow's in Qeynos. The wizard had spoken of the sac of a Giant spider, needed for spell research, and he said he would pay me greatly for helping him quire some. I knew Kelethan from previous adventures, and knew that one who could cure poison would be valuable against such beasts.

Just after we arrived the the Western Plains, it became dark, and we decided to set camp, as opposed to hunting in the twilight. Unfortunately, it began to rain, lest our leaving camp, and looking for our cave.

We trudged through the mud that the plains had become, wishing for a bit of shelter--except for our Cleric, who worshiped the bringer of storms: Karana.

It was not long, however that the mountains became visible--even for a human, like myself. I knew our cave was not far.

"It is in those mountains,"I said.

Kelethan seemed less than enthusiastic. "Might we dawdle a bit, and play in the rain?"

"Ye gads, cleric!"

The mage seemed more than mildly irritated. And perhaps that's why he didn't notice the young kodiak bear sneaking up on him.

The bear swiped twice with his claws, and the wizard fell to the ground. I could not tell if he was alive or dead, and at that time, I didn't care.

"Fall back!" I shouted to the cleric, even as the bear swiped at him.

I stepped into the bear, knowing that it had a huge advantage over me. I swiped with my longsword, and slashed it's face. It roared and stepped into me, raking it's claws. I thought he was going to hit me in my face, but I threw my shield arm over my head, and the claw impacted there.

The force of the blow knocked me to the ground, even as our wizard friend was standing. Out of the corner of my eye, I noticed Kelethan beating the huge bear with his mace. The bear let out a roar, and that instant, I was able to stand.

I began to slash the bear again, a bit bruised, but not seriously injured. Time after time I stabbed the beast, but still, it fought. I was hit several times, and I thought it would get the best of us, yet. Suddenly the air around us got extremely cold, and my sword knocked ice off the bears skin.

A loud shout ended with the bear exploding into fire. I knew our mage was alive and well at this point. It was not long that the bear decided he had enough, and he tured to run, only to get hit by Kelethans mace once again. The bear did not stand a chance.

After we killed the beast, we skinned it, and once again set out for our cave.

"Be a bit more cautious," the wizard told me.

"Aye. But remember that I can not see as well as you, half-elf."

The words visibly hurt the wizard, but I assume, since he needed our help, he did not speak out against me.

We finally came to the cave that I spoke of, and went inside to set camp once again.

"Ahh," said Carlion," This is much better. Tis warm, and the rains can not touch us!"

"Aye. It is better," I said, watching Kelethin, and knowing that he would much rather be outside in the weather.

Kelethin looked about. "What is in here, anyway?"

I shook my head, "Not sure. This is as far as I have ever been."

"WHAT?!" shouted our wizard, "How do you know its safe?"

"Shhhhh! Do not yell, for if it's not safe, you've surely alerted whatever might be in here." I said. Concerned, I stood, and held my lantern high, to better light the dark cave. I saw nothing, so I sat back down.

"How could you have led us here?" whispered Carlion.

I calmly wiped water off my longsword, "I have camped here many times, without any trouble, wizard. I doubt that we will have any now."

My words were followed by the sound of footsteps, and a loud voice shouting something in a different language. I looked up and saw a magic user (I know not what type) in the process of casting a spell.

"Duck!" I shouted, even as I dove behind some rocks.

Kelethan also saw the wizard and ran towards the wall on his left, where big boulders stood, to shield whatever spell the wizard might cast.

Carlion, the slow, irritable wizard, had no chance to do anything. He turned just as a bolt of fire engulfed his body, sending him to an early grave.

I realized at that point that even though the wizard yelled, drawing attention to us, that I had made the mistake of not researching the cave enough. His death was upon my hands.

Angered, I jumped over my shielding rock and attacked the wizard ferociously. Kelethin followed my lead.

For ever slash of my blade, the wizard lost a spell. He got off a couple, but they had little effect on me. He finally resorted to trying to stab me with his dagger, but if you've ever seen a fight between a dagger, and a sword, you know who has the clear advantage.

With a mighty thump with his mace to the back of the wizards head, Kelethin slayed yet another beast this day. Upon search of the wizards body, we found some strange rings, which glowed in the darkness of our cave.

"Bandits!" said Kelethin.

"Aye. Where there's one, there's two or three. We should get out of here while we can." I said.

"Agreed."

We left the cave the way we came in, knowing full well that we were lucky this day. And ever since, I have lead a party with much more organization, and caution. Indeed, a great lesson was learned that day.

By Gunther the Warrior, of Qeynos
