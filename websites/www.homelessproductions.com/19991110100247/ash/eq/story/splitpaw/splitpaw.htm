Split-Paw

"Are you sure we can trust him," the warrior monk Adar asked Kiedatar as he pulled the barbarian aside.

Kiedatar glanced over his shoulder to where the dark-elf wizard Auman sat silently meditating. He had met a couple of dark elves before, and neither of them had lived up to their race's dark reputation, so he was inclined to believe that Auman was indeed as he stated: an agnostic who had foresworn the dark gods of his people.

The barbarian shrugged. "I believe him. Besides, without his aid, we will never be able to destroy Shaman Lenrel, for the high shaman's spells are formidable."

"Don't you think it is suspicious that he stumbled upon our camp just when we were about to go on this raid? What if the inky betrays us and runs? Or worse, aids the gnoll shaman?"

Kiedatar shook his head. "He won't. While he may not be actively pursuing the same ends that we are, the means to both our ends happens to coincide at the moment, so I say we work with him."

Adar scowled. "All right, but if we are betrayed, I'm holding you responsible, barbarian."

"Okay," Kiedatar said, not mentioning that if they were betrayed they would be unlikely to survive the experience.

"Tell Fonan and Tiedan to prepare themselves. Remember, follow me in closely and hopefully we'll overwhelm them before they can counter-attack."

Akdrada the Terrible looked at his cards, his canine features carefully set to betray nothing of his hand to his brother Judriya.

"I'll raise your bet by ten silvers ... and I'll throw in that halfling prisoner I caught yesterday."

"Ak, don't tell me you're down to betting your prisoners again," Judriya barked in a tone that sounded suspiciously like a whine. "I've already got more than I can eat in a week from our game two days ago."

The gnoll noble looked at his brother. "In any case, I'll see your ten silver and throw in that case of ivory we captured from that caravan last week. That should cover the halfing, agreed?"

Akdrada opened his mouth to protest, but was interrupted when the door to the room shattered open.

"For the North!!!" A deep voice cried as a giant specimen of a human came charging in, axe held high. Behind the huge human, three smaller humans dressed in the simple clothing of fighting monks piled in behind him.

Akdrada responded quickly, grabbing the table and flinging it toward the charging barbarian. His swift response saved his brother's life. For the axe that would have clove into Judriya's head was driven deeply into the table's edge instead.

The big man tried to pull back his axe for another swing, but it was stuck in the table. Judriya, having recovered from the surprise of the attack, saw his opportunity and swung his morning star at the human. The giant man jerked to the side, taking only a grazing blow along his shoulder.

Quicker than Judriya would have though possible, one of the smaller men darted forward and chopped a hand into his throat. The gnoll gasped as he felt his supply of oxygen cut off. He dropped his morningstar and began clawing at his throat.

"Nice work, Fonan," the big man shouted as he cleared his axe from the table and drove it into the chest of the choking Judriya.

Akdrada knew he was doomed as he watched his brother's lifeblood splatter across the room when the big man withdrew his axe. He was barely managing to hold the other two men off with his sword for the fools seemed to disdain the use of weapons and fought with hands and feet only. Still, now that his brother was down, it was only a matter of time before the other two joined in and he was slain.

There was only thing that could save him now.

The other door in the room opened and three more gnolls poured in. Akdrada's heart leaped in triumph. If he could only keep the humans at bay for a few moments longer, they would be destroyed. Captain Skedrik and High Shaman Lenrel had joined the battle along with his cousin Lharp.

Shaman Lenrel barked out a few words of power, and fire flew forth from his hands burning the two humans that had slain his brother. The smaller of the two screamed and dropped to the ground writhing in pain. Akdrada barked a triumphant battle cry and renewed his efforts agains the two men fighting him.

His triumph was shortlived, however, for mere seconds after the high shaman had cast his spell on the two humans, another voice began to speak. The dark sibilant words were in a language that Akdrada knew to be dark elven for the gnolls of Splitpaw had sold slaves to the elves for many years.

At first, Akdrada thought one of the dark elves had come just in time to aid them, but upon completion of the spell, Captain Skedrick's head blew up splattering brains and gore across the room soaking all of the occupants. They were doomed. Akdrada turned to run but a fist hit him in the back of the skull knocking him to the ground. The last thing he saw was High Shaman Lenrel making a desperate attempt to shield himself from the dark elf's magicks.

Then the world came crashing down on him in the form of a steel shod boot. Akdrada's body twitched once and lay still.

"He'll live," Tiedan said as he finished wrapping a soft guaze bandage around Fonan's fire blistered scalp. "He'll be in pain until we can find a cleric to heal him, but he will live."

"Just kill me now," Fonan groaned. "Especially if you're going to talk about me as if I were not here."

The dark elf ,Auman , came striding out of the shaman's room. He looked suspiciously at Kiedatar and the three monks.

"Did any of you see a staff? About so long with a light blue crystal on the top?"

"A staff?" Adar asked. "What staff?" "The staff of...," Auman stopped. "It is no matter. I doubt these gnolls even have it. Or perhaps one of the other Splitpaw shamans possesses it. Or maybe one of their necromancers."

The dark elf went back into the shaman's room muttering to himself. From the sounds of it, he was tossing the room again in search of the staff.

"Kie," Adar said, "I don't suppose you could use your axe to remove the head of this shaman like you did that last gnoll? This will prove to the elders that we are worthy enough to achieve the next headband."

Kiedatar shrugged and did what the monk had asked.

These monks seemed a little too pre-occupied with status, or so he judged from their obsession with these headbands. The dark elf also appeared to have his own agenda. Kiedatar could hear him in the shaman's room, tapping on the walls looking for secret passages. The northman was discouraged. He had hoped to find something to indicate that the gnolls of Splitpaw were in league with those of Black Burrow and what the purpose of that union was, but they had unearthed nothing of the sort. They would have to plan a raid on one of the other leaders of the Paw, but that would have to await another day.

"Time," the voice of Mogok whispered in his head. Mogok was a shaman of the People, whom the tribal leaders had sent south with Kiedatar to investigate the Black Burrow-Splitpaw connection.

They had left him outside the lair to watch for possible gnoll reinforcements and to cover their escape if things went sour.

"It seems your little raid did not go unnoticed," Mogok continued, using the power of Farspeak to communicate with his countryman.

"A company of gnolls just ran by armed to the teeth." "Time to go," Kiedatar said to the others.

"Just one moment," Auman's voice came from the other room. "There is something funny about this rock, I think it might open a secret passage or something."

"Now, elf!" Kiedatar snapped as he walked into the room. The dark elf whirled his eyes blazing with fury. For a moment, Kiedatar thought the wizard would cast a spell, but the elf grimaced and stopped himself.

"All right, everyone gather here." The three monks joined Kiedatar and Auman. The elf cast one more dark look at Kiedatar then spoke a few words. The air grew hazy for a moment and when it cleared, Kiedatar found it quite difficult to see his comrades.

"Now, let us get out of here before their comrades arrive," Aemon's voice suggested.

-By Kiedatar
