Kiedatar

It was a nice day out, the day that I met Kiedatar. I was hunting lion's in the Western Plains, and I saw him.

He was a large specimen, even for a barbarian, and I heard his laughter ringing out over the plains, even as he slaid yet another lioness. I was a bit injured, and my mind was very weak from the casting of spells. So, I ran over to where he was, and asked if he knew where the nearest tower was, so that I could rest.

He, thankfully, said he knew where one was, and told me to follow him. We were almost to the second tower, or T2, as the locals call it, when we were ambushed by a lion, and his mate. Kiedater valiantly fought the lion, even though he was severely weakened from his past battles.

Unfortunately, the lioness took advantage of my weaknesses, and she slayed me with but a few slashes of her claws. I appeared-yes, yet again, in front of Qeynos, the city to which I am bound.

I ran back to the plains as fast as I could, but still, it took almost an entire day, and I wondered about the fate of my barbaric friend. It was not long that I found out.

He too, was slaid by the lion, and we bonded in a way that can only be found in mutual death. He and I decided at that point to travel together, and, as he put it, "get back at some of this wildlife."

What happened next surprised even me. As we hunted lions and lioness', we began to speak of our past. Not mine nearly as much as his. Nay, I did not tell him of my father, or his campaign against me, but I did speak a bit about my guild. But, as sad as my story is, his was just as sad.

His beloved teacher, apparently a great Barbarian warrior, disappeared several years prior to our adventure. He went into some hills west of Halas called the Everfrost Peaks, to do battle with a great Ice Drake, that was threatening the town. Everyone was awed by his courage, and his willingness to help his people. However, no one ever saw him, or the Ice Drake again.

Inspired by his master, Kiedater decided to become much like him. Brave, honorable, and strong. He gained a great distrust in magic, and shunned most magic users. I was honored that he even kept company with me.

By the end of our adventure, I discovered that he eventually wants to assemble a party of great warriors, and go find his master. I can only hope that when the time comes, he views me as honorable and strong enough to go with them. This would make my Guildmaster proud.

At any rate, our conversation soon went to different things. We spoke much on religion. I think that all the gods of Norrath are the same...or at least use the same source of power. It seems that Karana and his Great Spirit have much in common, although there are enough subtle differences to make the justification that they are two completely separate entities. This, however, was not as important as our discussion of the "evil" in our land.

I did not recount my travels with the Order of Oblivion, rather, I played the devil's advocate, and told him that the evil of man is much more frightening than the evil of the gods, or the evil of nature. Such atrocities as the Scarecrow, or the Treant is apparently the work of man--or madmen.

Ahh. I digress. Our evening ended after making about five gold apiece on high quality cat skins. I went to T2 to rest with him, and when I woke up in the morning, he was gone. I can only hope I see him again...Indeed, for a barbarian, he has much knowledge.
