GUILD RAID RULES v 1.0
 

aid Rules are made for various reason including the safety and prosperity of the guild and its members. By attending a guild raid, it is expected that you have read an understood the entire guild raid rules and abide by them. They are not to be interpreted or deviated from unless the Raid Manager and or the Raid Officers agree to any changes to the rules and let all raiding members know of the change(s) ahead of time and the reason for the change(s). Failure to follow any of the rules will have you banned and or removed from the guild event and further action may be required included a warning from the guild leadership and under severe conditions guild removal.

 
RULE 1: Attendance
  Guild Raids are a way to help the guild and its member acquire experience, loot, and or show other game players who we are via aide stations and the like. In order to be successful you need to have guild members' show up to these events. So while this guild is not a dire hard raiding guild it will require members to attend every 1 in 3 guild Raids for there level and if applicable. Failure to do so will have you placed on the inactive roster list and after 30 days of inactiveness you will be removed from guild with or without warning. Once you become inactive you will immediately become active once you attend a raid again. (See Guild Rule 15)

RULE 2: Raid Leaders
 
The Guild has set up Raid Leaders (RL) to prepare, plan and execute guild raids. There are two types of Raid Leaders: The Raid Manager (RM) and four Raid Officers (RO). The RM is the overall RL and is in charge of coordinating and appointing the RO's. The three RO's are set up based on level ranges and are as follows: Levels 20-35; Levels 36-50; Levels 51-65. Each RO will plan and coordinate and eventually execute a guild raid for their level range. When level ranges overlap both RO that the overlap effects and or a RM will become involved. Each RL will be given a user name and password to use to post to the guild's Event Page

RULE 3: Scheduling Raids
  RL have the responsibility of keeping the guild informed of upcoming raids and making sure they get the correct and needed information about the raid. The following information needs to be listed for any and all guild raids:
    1. A date should be established as well as a time to start raid. All times listed should be in EST. It is usually best to include an Assembly Time (AT) and a Raid Time (RT). The AT is to be established so ports can be arranged, groups constructed and travel to the raid site done. The RT is the time you should arrive in the raid zone.
    2. A Raid Zone should be picked and maps should be provided telling what part of zone the raid will focus on. An Assembly Point should be established to meet at before the raid begins so ports can be arranged and groups made.
  3. Raid Time Length should be established. This is used as an estimate and can always vary but please put a minimum amount to time need for the raid to happen.
    4. Raid Leader in Charge of Raid shall be listed either a RM or RO.
    5. Bind points for melees and non-melees should be suggested before the raid starts.
    6. Level range of raid established. (Please be reasonable and conservative)
    7. Character classes or at least class preferences should be stated for a successful raid to take place unless it is open to all.
    8. Goals of Raid clearly indicated, such as an experience, loot or epic raid.
    9. Strategies of the raid if any would be helpful to raid members and should be made available prior to the start of the raid. Please do some research before attending and or planning a raid.
    10. Each Raid planned should have a sign-up sheet on so that interest level can be determined and raid can be called off or postponed if necessary. Please do this on guild Forum Page under "Raids" topic, set up a new subtopic under the following format: Date-Zone-Signup i.e. 10/21/2002 Raid at City of Mist Sign up

RULE 4: Attending Raids
  Before a player attends a raid they are asked to be properly prepared. By doing so they will help the raid be successful and prevent delays and failures to happen. As a rule of thumb you should follow these simple guidelines:
    1. You should sign up for the raid at the guild Forum Page under the "Raids" topic for the specific raid you will be attending.
    2. Research the zone and raid objectives set by the RL.
    3. Print out any maps or zone strategy guides needed before attending raid.
    4. Obtain any items needed for raid before the raid time and have on you. Items like gems for buffs, batwings for levitation, fish scales for enduring breathe, food and water, and any keys need to gain access to zone.
    5. Be on time for raid
    6. Take Screen shots and after raid submit them to photos@gloryoftheglade.com
    7. Prepare to have fun!

RULE 5: Executing Raids
  The RL has the responsibility to run and execute the guild raid. In order to do this the RL, either a RM or RO, must take charge and raid attendees my listen to their orders and follow them. They are responsible for the following:
    1. Taking attendance of guild members and making a record of those who attended the raid along with the time each member spent at raid.
    2. Make group leaders and group assignments. Once group creation, roles need to be CLEARLY defined. If there are multiple groups, there still needs to be one Main Puller, one Main Assist, etc.
    3. Make sure everyone has items needed for raid and knows what objectives are.
    4.. Set up a raid camp.
    5. Setting up a raid channel and password and distribute to raid members.
    6. Assign a Main Looter (ML) and any Assistant Looters (AL) if needed.
    7. Distributes Loot (see below for Looting Rules)
    8. Post results of Raid on guild Forum Page with a list of items looted and money earned along with guild members attending. Any screenshots from raid may be forwarded to photos@gloryoftheglade.com

RULE 6: Raid Looting
  Looting for a guild raid will be on a need before greed basics. The RL will assign a Main Looter (ML) who has the job of looting all corpses and hold all items and money acquired from the guild raid. Only the ML may loot during a guild raid. If there is a lot of corpses to be looted and or a lot of items to be held or ML already has a lore item and can not hold another, then at the RL discretion they may appoint an Assistant Looter (AL) to help the ML out. However, the AL will only loot when directed by a RL or ML to do so and will stop this when directed too. They are only there to assist the ML not to be the ML. Ninja Looting is not accepted and will not be tolerated by any members (see Guild Rule 7). At the end of the raid or perhaps during some point during a longer raid or if a no drop item is found the RL will direct the ML to start to disturb money and or items among the raid members in the following way:
  1. All money acquired in a guild raid will be distributed as follows: 10% of all money looted will be donated to guild bank for future use, the rest of the money is to be divided equally among raid attending members with consideration given to those who have spent money on gems and or items need for raid. These members should be compensated before money is divided.
  2. All gems usable for making resistant gear i.e. blue diamonds, rubies, etc are to be given to the guild bank to hold for a guild jeweler to make resistant gear for guild members to use during raids.
  3. All items are distributed by need over greed and will be given to members with main characters over alternate characters. Item distribution is done by placing a claim for an item.
  4. No Drop Items will be distributed to the class and race that may use it. If you meet these criteria you may place a claim to the item. Exceptions if raid was held for a specific individual or individuals that already are granted these items in advance. They may loot them if RL is aware of this situation.
  Placing a claim to an item is handled in the following manner. The RL will announce what item is being distributed and rolled for you will then send the RL a /tell that you would like to acquire the item. The RL will then make a list of players that wish to place a claim on the item being distributed. The RL will make the list in the raid chat channel or applicable public channel to let the whole raid party know of the list. It will be formatted as follows: 1) "Player A" 2) "Player B" 3) "Player C" etc…Once the list is made the RL will announce last call to place a claim on the item. Once all claims are made the RL will roll a random die to the amount of bidders to determine who the winner of the item is.

RULE 7: Link Death
  If you so happen to be unlucky and go link dead during a raid please make every effort to come back to the game as soon as possible. If you go LD during a raid the following rules are in effect:
    1. If the site you went LD is a safe spot for the raid party to hold up at but not safe for the LD member to come back alone then the raid party will remain at the LD site for 5 minutes.
    2. If the site you went LD is an unsafe spot for the raid party they are to move out of area to closes safe point and wait for you to come back for a period of 5 minutes.
    3. After a 5 minute wait period has past for a member to return from LD the RL will then continue the raid an assign either a guard or guards to wait behind for the LD member to come back. After 15 minutes have past the guard(s) shall be recalled by the RL to rejoin the raid and your character will be declared lost due to LD. Please do not get mad if you log on and die. We will make ever effort possible to help you regain your body either at the end of a raid or during the event if applicable. We will not let you go unassisted in your CR.
    4. If the site you went LD is a safe spot and in the opinion of the RL that the LD member can safely rejoin the raid once they relog then the raid will continue as planned.

RULE 8: Raid Camp
  If the proposed raid camp is already taken, overcrowded or is invaded by other players, groups or guilds the RL will be the only qualified person to speak up and talk to the parties involved in the dispute of a camp. Please do not take matters in your own hands the RL will be representing the guild and all its members so please do not make a scene and act like a respectful player and person. If you are sent tell by non raid members concerning the camp please have them direct to them to the RL and make the RL aware of this so they may handle the situation. The RL may choose to change a raid camp and or whole zone if the raid camp is not available.

RULE 9: Raid Disputes
  Any an all disputes involved in a guild raid will be made directly to the RL. This includes disputes with raid and non-raid members concerning raid rules, looting etc. Please be clear and concise with your dispute and if possible keep it to the end of the raid. All disputes will be noted by the RL and in turn reported to the RM. The RL will try to settle the disputes and if unable too then RM will be asked to settle the dispute if applicable. Exception, The guild's Order of the Glade may have final say and take action if necessary too. All rules of the guild and its hierarchy take precedence.
