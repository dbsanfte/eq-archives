Hearts of Heroes Raids          
 

As with our Charter, this document may be changed for the good of the guild by ideas and suggestions. Bear in mind that changes will be voted upon by the Guild Officers.

  Raid Etiquette

 

Raids are a big part of the game. It takes organization and participation in order to properly raid and members are strongly encouraged to participate. Most importantly, raids are for fun!!! It is a time when the guild can come together and do something in large numbers. Raids build morale within the Guild.

Raids are not designed to gain experience or to see how much loot can be carried away. Raids are designed for the guild members to accomplish a goal or goals acting as a team. Sometimes, raids are organized to obtain difficult items from high level dungeons. Try to think about "team" when you come to a raid. Don’t come to a raid thinking about what you can personally get...Your turn will come!

-          Arrive on time for a raid. Smart players arrive early. This means getting ports to the location in advance. If you are a porter, try to offer ports an hour early if possible.  

-     Pay close attention to the Raid Channel chat and do not SPAM it with unnecessary conversation.   Be ready to respond promptly
to the Raid Leader's requests for information on Classes and Levels.

-          Be ready. Have enough food and water. Outfit your character for the raid in advance.  
    Some Raids have Special Requirements... If you need to equip for Magic Resistance for instance, do so before arriving...
    Do your homework :)

-          Get a bind nearby the raid location ahead of time.

-          Proper planning on everyone’s part will allow raids to get started as soon as possible.

 

Raid Rules

 

-          The Raid Leader is in ultimately in charge of the raid. The Raid Leader must only answer to the Guild Leader. This is necessary to set the hierarchy of command.

-          The Raid Leader will make group assignments and name Group Leaders.

-          Group Leaders must only answer to the Raid Leader or Guild Leader.

-          All participants at any raid/special event must listen and follow orders set forth by the Raid Leader at all times to avoid unnecessary confusion. Failure to do so can delay a raid start or get us all killed during a raid.

-          Cooperate with  and follow instructions from your Group Leader.

-          Limit tells and unnecessary chat during a raid. This just adds to more confusion. Be a good listener. If you need to ask a question use the chain of command. Send a tell to your group leader. Only group leaders and the Guild Leader should send tells to the Raid leader. Remember the leadership is constantly coordinating with each other on the Leaders channel. Do not make their job any more difficult.

-          NO SPAMMING ON RAID CHANNELS.

-          Don’t complain about the group you are in. Groups are designed to be balanced by class for survivability first then by level if possible. Remember experience is not the prime directive of a raid.

-          Group Leaders will loot all corpses. They will ensure fair splits when necessary. I.E.: someone has to leave early, raid is over, or they must lighten their load. No drop items will not be looted. The Raid Leader will be informed of a no drop and he or she will determine who needs it.

-          Items of value will be rolled for by all the raiders after the raid. In other words; turn in items of value to the Raid Leader. Listen for any special raid instructions depending on the raid. Normally, only characters who have a need for the items will be allowed to roll.

-          Do not pass the (point) person or  (puller) without instructions to do so. Passing the pull spot can  result in entire group or even raid party death. Assist, assist, assist, and if all else fails assist some more. If you break a mezz  you may get us all killed. If you sit to meditate when it draws aggro, you will get aggro :)  If you are running to the raid pull spot and get aggro, stand and fight.. DO NOT TRAIN YOUR GROUP OR THE RAID PARTY.

-          Do not evac unless instructed to do so by the Raid Leader. If the Raid Leader gives the “CAMP OUT” command, this is for rezzing Clerics and Paladins only. Everyone else fights to the death to allow the rezzers to escape. Die with honor and bards may sing praises of you one day, fail and it means a messy corpse run for all. Clerics and Pallies will bring up alt toons and return when the all clear soggnal is given to rez up. 

Raid Roles

  • Several KEY positions will be filled for each raid.  If you seek one of theses important assignments, contact the designated leader for that Raid.   Raid Leaders can and will change as we gain in size and experience.  This will give opportunity to as many interested members as possible.

    • Raid Leader:     In charge of Raid event

    • Group Coordinator:     Group Assignment, Balancing, Locations

    • Loot Coordinator:    Loot pickup, banking, Quest items, Rolls, Distribution.

    • Supplies/Logistics:    In game support, food, drink, bandages, holding coin, gophers

    • Chat Moderator: Establish chat channel, relay messages, monitor raid, guild, & zone chat.

    • Recorder:    Track players, events, loots, surprises. Submit report to webmaster.

    YOUR ROLE:  Participate fully, be a team member, help others by giving what you have to offer.  If you can summon or donate some nifty stuff to help outfit others then please do so.  Let the raid organizers know what your specialties and constraints are.  Be patient as we get better. As with any skill, training up in the art of Raids takes practice and dedication.
