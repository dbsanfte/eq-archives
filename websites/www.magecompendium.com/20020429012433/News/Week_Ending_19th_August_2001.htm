Topic :  Pet levels

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Absor there was indeed a nerf to Invoke Death. The 55 hitting pet is now conning even to a 39 and it used to be level 41.

All the Pet NPCs are named a certain way which reflects their level. Last week I was cataloguing the stas of the pets in game and I guess I accidentally edited the wrong NPC, when I went to set it back I set it's level to the level indicated in it's name. At some point in the past the level of the 49 necro pet was changed to be higher than the one listed in its name. This caused the max level of the pet as well as its average level to be lowered by 2. I have corrected the mistake I made in the pet's level, and we will be updating this on the servers this afternoon. The update will not require the servers to come down. I'm really sorry for this mistake, and hope I haven't inconvenienced anyone too much. Thanks to everyone who reported my error so quickly.

- Lawrence Poe

Let me give a fake example. I'm oversimplifying this to help make it clear.

Let's say there was a pet named Bob4. That name would indicate that he was of the type Bob and is intended to be level 4.

At some point in the past it was decided that Bob needed to be 6th level. Because the name Bob4 is referenced in a lot of other places in the game, changing the name might not be the best idea, so someone just raised Bob4 to level 6.

When Lawrence noticed the error he'd made by accidentally editing Bob4, he went back to make Bob4 4th level, as his name indicated.

From looking at the data there wasn't an easy way to know that Bob4 should have been 6th level.

But when we delved into it and tapped into Gordon's impressive database talent we were able to look and see that prior to Lawrence's revision, Bob4 had indeed been 6th level.

Again, we're sorry for the error. And thanks for pointing it out.

- Alan

Topic :  Login problems

 

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

If you're having a problem logging in and the system is telling you that your Game Card does not exist, there's a way around the problem.

Find the testeverquest.exe file in your EverQuest directory and run that. After the Test patcher downloads files to you, click cancel (you don't need to actually log onto the Test server, just download the files). Then log into the game as you normally would (you'll probably end up downloading a few more files).

This should let you in the game again.

We're sorry for the inconvenience.

- Alan

This came off the official EverQuest message boards.

Why are quests in EQ taking more and more people to complete? It seems to me that this game has gone overboard with making content for the 50+ people in very large guilds.

Only 10 to 15% of the people who play EverQuest will be able to get any of the better quest items and I am not talking about the UBER items.

I hope to see when SoL comes out that quest can be done with just one person, or at the max of one full group.

Luclin is indeed being designed moreso for one or two groups of players and focused overall less on raids. It will also provide content for all levels, 1-60.

I do think, however, that Velious’ attention to raids and other epic battles was and is important. While it is true that not everyone will reach 60 or have the time to involve themselves in large guilds, the playerbase as a whole still enjoys that content vicariously. MMORPGs need rumors and legends of awesome, virtually unbeatable foes against which mortals send armies. Just looking around at various message boards you can find all sorts of players debating the high-end of the game. In fact, when we sometimes change aspects of the high level game we get all sorts of feedback, both negative and positive, from players who aren’t even close to experiencing the content we changed. Why? Because it’s a goal for them – they plan on encountering that content one day, no matter how far in the future.

I remember playing a certain MUD several years ago and being low level and in a newbie dungeon. I overheard some mid level players talking about another group who’d allegedly fought a dragon. Immediately I approached them and asked them if there were really dragons in the game, and if so why haven’t I seen any? Where were they, I inquired, and what were they like?

Well, not only did they have no clue how to get to where dragons were, they also let me know that it would take months and months of hard work before I’d even have a chance to encounter a dragon. That was an important day for me. It was at that moment I knew the MUD was for me – a game that had legends and rumors and where experiencing the ‘end-game’ was something to really strive for.

So while I know we’ve been criticized for paying too much attention to ‘uber guilds’ and their needs and wants, I do think there needs to be a balance. There should be content at the high end for single groups, massive raids, and everything in between. And to that end, Shadows of Luclin should complement the Scars of Velious quite well.

When SW:G comes out, some EQ players will switch over and play it full time. Others, like myself, will purchase it and play both. You might get more people to continue to play, and pay, for both if you give a monthly fee discount if a player has both accounts active. Maybe make the EQ fee $5 for players also paying for SW:G. Any chance of this happening?

While it’s certainly far too early to announcing pricing plans, we are definitely thinking about how it might work with the suite of titles we’re planning on having one day. I think there probably will be many players who might be interested in multiple premium titles, and I think we should have pricing that is attractive to them. I think its both in our interest and the players out there that they are each able to experience EverQuest, SW:G, Planetside, and Sovereign and decide which, if not all, they are interested in playing.

I think it’s related to risk vs. reward. Mid level dungeons, especially on older servers where higher-end items have trickled down, just don’t have treasure that’s appealing enough to lure players into a more risky area. In addition to making sure there are several high-level dungeons on Luclin, we also plan on converting some of the existing and underused older dungeons to high-level zones. We realize that it’s not just treasure, but also distance that can frustrate low and mid-level players and that some zones are just too far away. Around the time of Luclin’s release or soon after, several of these zones will be changed such that higher-level players are their target audience.
