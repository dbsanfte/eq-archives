Date :   07/07/2000

 

Topic :  Hell Levels

 

This came from the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Given my time here at Verant, I think I can finally articulate this in a more accurate manner. I think "bug" is probably not the correct term to describe why hell levels are there. "Unintended Mathematical Consequence" would probably be a better description. Trying to put it in easy terms:

Basically, everyone knows that the experience needed at every level is a curve. Well, it's not so much a curve, as it is a series of straight lines. As you level, the "curve" gets steeper. However, due to the formula used, in the first level after the curve gets steeper, you must "make up" all of the experience as if the curve had been that steep all along. As such, a "Hell Level" is the first level where a new experience curve took affect. Ideally, it would have been nice if the formula spread the additional experience required over all the levels of the next range, rather than requiring it all in one level, but the outcome is the same.

As for 50+, it's probably safe to say that experience required in those levels won't be on the same curve as 45-50, and as such, any time the curve steepens, the "Hell Level Phenomenon" will be present for the first level of the new curve.

Basically, this is just the way it is, and we won't be readjusting the experience curves. They haven't been changed since release, and no one ever said that progression would be linear.

- Gordon

Topic :  Patch

 

July 5th, 4:00pm

Karana, Bertox, and Test Server Split

Hello all,

At some time between 6pm and 8pm PDT (GMT-8), we will open /movelog registration on Karana, Bertox, and Test, to allow players on those servers to request that their account be moved to the new split-server, Terris-Thule. Players on the affected servers will be informed via server broadcast when registration is open. Players wishing to have their characters moved to the new split server will need to run the command "/movelog yes" after registration is opened.

Notes:

- /movelog only needs to be run from ONE character, and will count for all characters on that server on that account.


- Players who have requested a move should make sure all of their corpses are looted by the time the server is brought down tomorrow morning (9am PDT). Corpses will not be duplicated in the new world, and anything left on a corpse left behind by anyone who moves will be lost.


- We may or may not be able to move everyone who requests this action, based upon the number of total requests that we receive.


- The GM/Guide staff will not be able to tell you whether or not you have been selected until after the move is actually completed.


- Test Server Players with artificially buffed characters on their account *MUST* delete those characters prior to registering for move. Having artificially buffed characters moved to the live servers will result in action against the player's account.


Item Changes Pending in Next Patch

In the next patch, keys for Charasis, Sebilis, and Howling Stones will all be made NODROP if not currently set with that flag. This means that people holding more than one key will not be able to hand out their extras after the patch.

At the same time that this change is made, we are also implementing a new change that "binds" these items to your character. Should you die with one of these keys in your possession, it will not be left on your corpse, but will stay with you when you return to your bindpoint.

This change was made in light of the difficulty of the key-quests, in order to eliminate the need to do the quest multiple times prior to entering the dungeon.

- The EverQuest Team

Topic :  The Source of Verant's Problems

 

This came from the EQVault news page.

 

Now Abashi has stated that rogues are "the necromancers of melee" and later clarified that he means that they are on the upper end of the balance scale, which would seem to indicate that he considers necromancers to be nearly perfectly balanced

Actually, no. I would consider necromancers to be "on the verge of being overpowered". By "overpowered" I do not speak of pure offensive power, but everything that makes up a class that figures into the balance equation. "Perfectly balanced" lies somewhere between "overpowered" and "underpowered" in this sense.

By saying that rogues are the "necros of melee", I was not speaking of versatility nor solo capability, but rather that rogues are on the verge of being overpowered every bit as much as necros are. The original vision for rogues was more of a "thief" than they are now. However inability to fully implement some of the skills we had planned (sense/disarm traps), we decided to fit them more into an "assassin" role.

Now since they could not fit Verant's niche monks invented a new, valuable and effective niche for themselves and became EQ's master pullers, bringing themselves back into balance with the EQ's environment

I've taken quite a bit of heat from some players about some comments that I made about not liking feign-pulling. Quite simply, when feign-pulling, a monk uses a skill that we created for something other than its purpose. Feign Death was supposed to be an escape skill, not a skill that allowed easier breaking of camps. Also consider that feign-pulling doesn't make sense from a roleplay perspective (we like it when things make sense in this fashion). Can you see a creature saying, "I'M MAD AT HIM, I'M GOING TO KI...wait... he's dead now.. I guess I'll go ba...AH HA! HE'S NOT DEAD, I'M GOING TO KI...wait...no... dead again....(ad nauseam)"

Verant needs to realize that they have not created the perfect gaming environment, and therefore need to alter their perception of some of the classes and bring them in line with the world they have created, not the world they had envisioned.

We're well aware that the game isn't perfect. After all, what game can be ? That said, what good would we do as game designers if we did not have some idea for the way we wanted the game. People will continue to make the decision as to whether the game we offer is one that they are willing to play.

- Gordon

Date :   04/07/2000

 

Topic :  More on Test Player Wipe

 

This came from the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Hello all,

Many of you are probably familiar with the situation that resulted in characters from Test being moved to Torvonnilous and other servers. For those who are not, let me do a quick recap:

A few weeks ago we did a character wipe on the Test server in order to cleanse the testing platform. After doing this wipe, it was apparent that many people on the server did not have a reasonable expectation that this was a possibility. As a result, in the interest of providing good customer service, we decided to back out the player-wipe, change it to an item-wipe, and give the players on Test an option to move to one of the production servers.

During this process, some mistakes were made on our part. In these plans, we neglected to place several “exceptions” for valid destinations for Test characters. We moved around 20 characters to Torvonnilous, a “young non-split” server. People often start on new non-split servers because they want a fresh environment. There is often a “race”, so to speak, shortly after the server comes up to be the first character or group to reach high levels, get good equipment, and do the dragons and planes. There are many people who enjoy this fresh environment as it gives them the opportunity to become a well-known and respected person on their server.

It was inappropriate for us to inject established higher-level characters into the young environment on Torvonnilous. Transfers of this type, though not particularly relished by the people on the destination servers in general, should be limited to established and “older” servers, including split-servers, that are not in the midst of establishing their “server community”. A well-established community on older servers can take an injection of new people without a harmful effect upon that community, however the community on the younger servers is fragile and deserves protection.

In a previous message on the subject that I posted earlier today, I commented that it was “too late” to address this issue. However, for the reasons above, we’ve modified our decision in regard to the Test move. We will be removing the characters from Test that were moved to Torvonnilous, and placing them back on Test for the time being. Owners of those characters should be contacted by email in the near future.

We’d like to thank everyone for their patience and understanding in this matter.

Thank you,

Gordon Wrinn
Internet Relations Manager
Verant Interactive, Inc.

Topic :  /petition

 

This came from the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Hello all,

We've been working for a while now at improving the level of customer service that we provide to our customers who are in need. We've been hiring more GMs and Technical support staff, and have also created several different types of GMs to help service the players.

For instance, we're in the process of differentiating between GMs who are there to develop and provide dynamic content (Events, Quests, etc.) as opposed to those who are there to provide administrative services (Customer service, assistance, and take disciplinary action).

Another drive that we are on is to reduce the average response-time to /petitions in game. This is one area that the customer-service staff needs your help in. We need to reduce the number of petitions for issues that GMs and Guides have no control over. The customer-service staff and the design teams are two different groups of people, and neither is responsible for the actions or work-load of the other..

It's not unusual on these boards or others to see someone putting together a drive to /petition ideas or dissatisfaction with some aspect of gameplay when they log in game. This only extends the amount of time that it will take the Customer Service staff to get to people who need some sort of immediate help in game. Though many customer Service Reps try to extend the courtesy of discussing issues regarding game mechanics with those that petition, the CS Reps are not responsible for the direction that gameplay goes. Given this, /feedback, or placing your ideas in public forums such as this one is the best way to go.

I'd like to ask for everyone's cooperation in helping the CS staff get to people who need assistance quickly. You can do this by only petitioning when you need assistance for something in game at that moment. Ideas and suggestions should be submitted via /feedback, which are reviewed by the development team regularly. Please help both your fellow players and us by using /petition responsibly.

Thank you,

- Gordon

Topic :  EverQuest Client Changes

 

This came from the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Hello all,

I thought I'd drop a line and let everyone know about some exciting changes that we are working on:

First, the next EQ client will, unless a problem is discovered in testing, include a slash-command that will allow players to turn off display of corpses. This will be a client-side option that will just repress the rendering of the corpses themselves. This will allow corpse-related actions (like sense-corpse) to be taken even while corpse display is repressed.

The command (/hidecorpses) will have three possible parameters:

None - Shows all corpses
All - Hides all corpses but that belonging to the player.
All but group - Hides all corpses but those belonging to the player and group members.

As asking people "Has anyone seen my corpse?" is an issue that we feel builds the feeling of community and encourages people to help each other out, the command will reset to showing all corpses upon zoning. Also, as a note, in its current implementation, any corpses created after the command is run will be shown.

This command should help people who experience framerate problems after zoning in to areas known to cause a large number of corpses.

The second change has to do with quested keys for entrance into the keyed dungeons on Kunark: Sebilis, Charasis, and Veeshan's Peak. It's apparent that the risk of dying in these zones is too great to risk going with only one key. Furthermore, it's become standard practice for people to leave a corpse at the entrance of these dungeons with the spare key.

As we feel that the idea of dueling and getting killed just to leave a corpse for a bank isn't in the best interest of role-playing or our vision for the game (e.g. It's cheesy), we've decided to nerf this practice Our plan is to have keys for these three dungeons stay with the owner when they return to their bind point. This new ability that we are working on also gives us the opportunity to explore "soulbound" items in the future, but no promises just yet on that.

- Gordon
