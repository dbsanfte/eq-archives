Topic :   Travel From, To and On Kunark

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

I think if we put enough cool stuff, especially treasure, on Kunark, you'll come.

Here are the reasons for 1 teleport in (and 2 ports for ships) :

1. Kunark is supposed to be a remote newly re-discovered continent.

2. Exploration is key to a game like this.

3. Just like in fantasy novels and such, I want it to be an accomplishment to travel deep into Kunark.

Is all this just 'waste your time' like someone asserted? No, it's an attempt to capture an important aspect of 'High Fantasy', namely remote areas, distant lands, rumors from those who've returned from a long journey and their intriguing tales.

Am I naive ? Perhaps, but I don't think so. Some posts have asked what the 'vision' is. Well, there are a lot of aspects to the 'vision' of EQ, but a good way to describe the vision is that it's our intent to create a 3D virtual world that captures the elements of traditional fantasy novels as well as to re-capture that feeling of playing AD&D or some other paper and pencil game around the table with your friends.

The goals : adventure, danger, friendship, exploration, a strong sense of accomplishment and pride in both your character and the knowledge you've gained over time.

Does EQ meet all of our goals perfectly ? Heck no, but it's close, and it will get closer over time.

If no one ever goes to Kunark and we're convinced it's because of only one teleport point will we add more ? Of course, but let's get there first.

I know one of the arguments as to why some zones on EQ are under populated is their remoteness. I don't buy it though. Some of the great dungeons in the game are not remote, yet still under populated. And Cazic Thule, very remote for good races (I remember dying and running from Arena or Freeport to Cazic-Thule), was packed when there was Rubicite there.

My conclusion ? Compelling loot, familiarity, and critical mass. I just drove through LA yesterday and it was packed -- horrible traffic, people everywhere -- definitely 'overcrowded'. Yet further up the coast, hardly anyone around. People go to LA to live and work for a reason, and I think it's critical mass of people as well as resources (jobs, etc.).

EQ zones are the same way. When we re-itemize Najena and Permafrost, people will go there (IF the loot is deemed good enough). Will it take some time to build critical mass there ? Probably.

And if we put compelling 'loot' in the depths of dungeons in Kunark, will people go there? I believe so, yes. Will they make the long journey there ? Yes. And there are binding points elsewhere on the continent for melee classes, so all a party has to do is commit themselves to Kunark for a while and essentially adventure there for some time before returning to the 'known' lands.

Again, that was the point -- create a wild and remote continent full of adventure, quests, and treasure for the brave and hardy.

Wrong or right, jury's still out. Let's wait and see.

- Brad McQuaid

Date :    21/03/2000

 

Topic :   Patch

 

March 21st, 11:00am

***UPDATE MESSAGE***

This morning's update consisted primarily of server-side changes to improve performance of the game engine in general. However, there are several changes to note:

*Spell Changes*

- Skin Like Nature has had its damage shield removed and has regained its regeneration component. We're certain this time :)
- Many Shaman buff spells have had their recast delay removed. You can now cast them on multiple people without having to wait between each cast.
- The Wizard spell "Rend" was improperly saving versus cold. It now saves versus Magic. 

*Bug Fixes and General Enhancements*

The spawning problem in Solusek's Eye (SoldungA), related to the spawn of the Clockwork gnomes, will be fixed later today in a zone patch. 

The +/= key will no longer take screenshots. 

You can now adjust your clipping plane by using the SHIFT/- and SHIFT/+ key combinations. 

You can now view the Message of the Day by typing /motd.

Corrected a bug in the /follow process that would cause your character to loop around to the right at times when following someone.

Corrected a bug that would cause item-loss associated with trading a container marked LORE. 

Corrected some grouping bugs that had the potential to cause the client to crash. 

*Feign Death and Pets*

The Feign Death spell will no longer wear off. This change had been made due to an issue that was discovered that allowed Necromancers to solo creatures while feigned and AFK. The change had some unintended effects, as we discovered that there are a few cases where someone could be legitimately feigned for a longer period. Instead of having the spell wear off, any summoned pet will automatically suicide 2 minutes after its owner feigns. This is the solution requested by most of the players who voiced an opinion, and should serve the same goal as the original change.

- The EverQuest Team

Topic :   Magic vs Muscle

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Why are melee classes second rate to every caster in the game. There is not one melee class that can solo a mob above 40th level that is within 5 levels of him. Yet there are casting classes above 40 that can solo mobs 2 levels above them. Verant continues to state their overbalancing policies when comparing like classes but they ignore the fact that melee vs caster is extremely unbalanced for the most part.
Soloing is not always the best way to compare classes but in the case where say a level 45 ranger tries to take on a mob of level 42. The mob would wipe the floor with him. Now substitute the ranger with a necro/mage....easy battle for those classes. I have been in Nag's Lair on several occasions and have seen Mages camping one room while their pet is in another. Is there anyway with my ranger I could do that alone...heh no way.

It has also been said by Verant that casting classes die a lot more than melee classes and therefore they should be able to bind in more locations. How can that be? I group regularly with several casters. If anyone dies in our group it is me. I am the tank therefore when things get hairy I am the first to go down.

The above two scenarios gives casters a huge advantage over melee classes with maybe an exception to wizards at higher levels.

The soluction? Buff the melee classes some. Here are some suggestions.

1. Let us be capable of binding in the same locations as casters...I still don't understand this one. My gaming time is just as valuable to me as a casters.

2. Make the melee stats STR/DEX/AGIL actually mean something. WIS/INT are noticable improvements to casters when they get stat increasing items. You have explained to the casters what these do for them but seem to hesitate when explaining STR/DEX/AGIL to the classes that use these stats most.

3. Raise Skill Caps, or better yet change them for melee classes.

Give warriors and monks 7 improves/level for their melee skills...they should be better at melee than other classes at the same level. At higher levels warriors should be uber tanks and monks should be incredible damage dealers.

Give rogues 6 improves/level for melee skills....make throwing a viable option for them and monks.

Ranger/Paladin/SK....keep the same 5/level skill increase but get rid of the lower skill caps in melee. Giving the other classes more improves/level will ensure they are better at melee.

Stop saying that ranged attacks by melee classes are too powerful. Fix this. Archery/throwing should cause just as much damage/hit as does melee. A well aimed arrow is no different than a well placed thrust or slash.

Give critical hits for standard melee to monks, warriors, and rogues. Why they don't have them is beyond me. They all are masters at melee.

Raise all skill caps to level 50 for all melee classes. Monks/warriors would cap at 350

All these requests seem reasonable.

Today I'm going through and trying to find some of the largely subscribed posts that I've not yet commented on.

I've read the original author's post:

1. Let us be capable of binding in the same locations as casters...I still don't understand this one. My gaming time is just as valuable to me as a casters

This is a dead horse that I don't think I need to go into again. Just to attempt to put it to bed, widespread binding for melee characters isn't in our plans for the game.

2. Make the melee stats STR/DEX/AGIL actually mean something. WIS/INT are noticable improvements to casters when they get stat increasing items. You have explained to the casters what these do for them but seem to hesitate when explaining STR/DEX/AGIL to the classes that use these stats most.

I've also discussed stats to the maximum depth that I intend to. These stats do mean something. You need to decide for yourself if what they mean is important enough for you to invest in them.


3. Raise Skill Caps...or better yet change them for melee classes.

Skill-caps are completely arbitrary. We could have made most of them cap at 100 instead of 200, but 200 was the number that was chosen. The skill caps alone are not the sole indicator of how well you hit, but only a part of it. Other things like your level versus the level of the critter you are trying to hit are also taken into account.

Raising the skill caps for the 1-50 game is not planned because the game was balanced with the caps being as they are set now. The end result would be that even if we did raise the caps, we'd have to decrease the impact of level and other issues in order to maintain balance.

d) Stop saying that ranged attacks by melee classes are too powerful. Fix this. Archery/throwing should cause just as much damage/hit as does melee. A well aimed arrow is no different than a well placed thrust or slash.

I've also discussed this in other threads. Melee is a primary form of combat, as is spell casting. Throwing/archery is not, and never will be a primary form of combat. We don't wish to have people standing back in this game and plugging away at critters, doing as much damage as melee, yet without significant risk, and where the only balancing factor is cash. Spell-casters have mana to mitigate these issues, there's no similar meter by which to control archery or throwing. Though many good suggestions have been submitted, we consider ranged combat done with respect to the mechanics of it.

Give critical hits for standard melee to monks, warriors, and rogues. Why they don't have them is beyond me. They all are masters at melee.

Warriors will continue to be the only class that gets criticals from normal combat. With things as they are now, we consider melee to be essentially well-balanced.

All these requests seem reasonable.

Sure, all of these suggestions are reasonable from the standpoint of what could be improved, but we need to look at what is necessary, not what is reasonable. We receive many reasonable suggestions every day (several thousand), however most of them aren't needed. We feel that the melee/caster disparity is pretty much covered at present. Melee users continue to be desired in groups, at all levels, which was our intent. They are considered “fun-to-play” by those who enjoy playing in well-balanced groups. In short, melee classes are not broken. They are looking good.

- Gordon

JSmedley says, "We use 2 major ISP's (UUnet and AT&T Cerfnet)"

JSmedley says, "we've had to build this all up from scratch, and we don't try to hide the fact that we've been learning as we go to some extent"

JSmedley says, "I think we have this down pretty well now and we will work at it every day"

JSmedley says, "until we think it's as good as it can get."
