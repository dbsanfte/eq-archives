This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

I'm going to take a moment here to eat some crow and commend Verant on Kunark.

Kunark is split into two apects: Levels past 50, and the Kunark zones. Both have been extremely well done, and both exhibit an intellegence and creativity that your past work didn't even hint at. Now we've gotten to the subject of my post : What about the rest of the game ? Kunark is stunning but there are still zones in the "old game" that aren't even near fully utilized.

Will work on the "old game" be abandoned in favor of doting on the new and expensive-to-create Kunark ?

First, thanks for the compliments -- Kunark was an opportunity to learn from our mistakes and do better. And in the future, we'll top Kunark

Now, to your questions.

1. No, we will not be updating the graphics on the old zones. This is primarily for two reasons: a. Resources are limited and I really think most of our customers would rather see new content as opposed to re-doing older content, and b., as was mentioned in this thread, it's not really appropriate to change the system requirements on the existing lands, which upgrading the graphics significantly would. That said, we have at least one minor way we're going to be upgrading the original world soon.

2. The 'old game' will definitely NOT be abandoned. The EQ Live team will continue to address crowding issues by revamping the lesser used dungeons and other zones. In addition, many of the zone mechanics that are being tried in Kunark, should they prove successful, will be used in the older zones when they are revamped as well.

3. The Hole is still on target for May, probably late May. After the Hole comes the Warrens, which is also on Odus. And we will continue to add free zones after that.

4. Yes, I think slowly but surely existing NPCs will be made more interesting, possibly involved in quests and storylines.

Hope that helps,

- Brad McQuaid

Topic :  Patch

Spells

Scale of Wolf" is now group-only.  You must be grouped with the target in order to cast this spell upon them.  This change was made due to the fact that Scale of Wolf overwrites Spirit of Wolf, which may be more desirable in some situations.

The "Cannibalize II" spell has been enhanced. 

Corrected a bug with "Song of the Sirens" that caused an unintended level-cap to affect the spell. Bards should now be able to charm everything that they once could with this spell.

Binding in Timorous Deep

Due to a feature within the zone, players can no longer bind within Timorous Deep.  This functionality was intended in the zone prior to launch, as binding presents a possible balance issue.  Players who are already bound in this zone will retain their binding location until they bind someplace else.

Iksar Quests

A few items associated with the Iksar newbie warrior quests have been repaired. The EverQuest team would like to extend thanks to those who reported the problems.

Iksar Factions

As announced in the patch server message yesterday, this morning we corrected a bug in Iksar faction that allowed them to travel outside Kunark without fear of being attacked by most things.  In the event that any young Iksar find themselves in a bind/death loop, please /quit, log in another character, and /petition for assistance.

Server Splits

The recently announced server splits will be completed roughly two hours after the other servers come up from this patch.  Movelog registered accounts from Veeshan and Bristlebane will be moved to Saryrn, and Xegony and Bertox to The Seventh Hammer.  As requests to move were very strong, we were not able to move everyone who requested.  We appreciate everyone's understanding.

Thank you !

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Just for a bit of clarification, back when the planes were brought online, John Smedley commented that people just don't die in planes, they die hard. Veeshan's Peak makes the planes look easy. There exists a non-negligible chance that not only will you die there, but you might not be able to raise the necessary forces to allow recovery of your corpse. It is *very* dangerous.

In order to avoid any appearance of impropriety in the zone, as sometimes follows when we've assisted in corpse recoveries in other zones due to claimed bug-deaths, we're not going to risk that for Veeshan's Peak. Veeshan's Peak is incredibly dangerous, and in order to guarantee that everyone is operating under the same set of conditions, we've elected to make the zone "enter at your own risk".

That said, there are no known bugs in the zone that would cause the death of a character, and it was tested extensively in the Ruins of Kunark beta. We will certainly investigate and eliminate any bugs that are reported, but we won't be reimbursing items or experience, nor will we be moving corpses or people within the zone.

Should you not put this in a patch message ?

Probably not. There are people that purposely avoid the message boards and news sites due to their desire to discover everything on their own. I don't want to put up a patch message that "forces" them to view the info. Also, we usually don't put up patch messages for info that only affects a very small number of players, in this case the top 10% or so.

I expect that the info will spread to those concerned anecdotally prior to anyone entering the zone. There is also sufficient in-character warning in-game about this as well.

I’ll give it some more thought though.

Topic :   Kunark Framerate

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

Just to clear up this rumor :

The trees swaying have NOTHING to do with framerate.

Two things affect framerate: number of polygons drawn, and fill rate (the number of objects in front of other objects in front of other objects, all of which have to be drawn).

Framerate goes down in a forest because of the additional polygons needed to represent all the trees, as well as the trees behind the trees behind the trees having to be drawn.

Anyway, there are three general ways to increase your framerate (starting from easiest to hardest):

1. Adjust your clipping plane down under the display options section.

2. Buy a faster 3D card.

3. Upgrade your CPU/Computer

The level of 3D sound can also affect overall framerate, depending on your system's configuration (which sound card, etc.) so you might want to experiment with those settings as well.

Bottom line, Kunark pushes the EQ engine a lot harder, hence the better visuals. Even though this was perhaps a non-issue for you with the rest of Norrath, when you're on Kunark you might now need to adjust your display settings.

- Brad McQuaid

PS. just to be very clear, the animating trees do not measurably affect your framerate.

PPS. framerate is how fast your screen is updated and has little to do with 'lag', which is generally another word for 'latency' which has to do with internet performance (timing issues, sending data between your client and our servers).
hope that helps.

Topic :   Pets

 

This came of the official EverQuest message boards.

 

There's a couple of issues that have several threads running behind them.

 

It might be easier for me to address them at one time :

 

Spirit of Scale :

We'll be making this spell group-only (e.g. must be grouped with target) shortly. This should alleviate people's concerns about it being used offensively to harm other players.

Backstabbing Pets :

Up until the level 50+ pet spells, all pets were based off of warriors. We now have the ability to base pets off of other classes, and that's what's been done here. There are now rogue pets, wizard pets, and monk pets, which represent additional abilities for the pet-users above 50th level.

Rogues need not worry that a backstabbing pet somehow reduces the desirability of their class. First, the pet does not have the backstab skill that a rogue of that level would have, so they won't hit as often nor will they do as much damage. Furthermore, they don't have all of the stat bonuses that increase the rogues effectiveness. Certainly they can be buffed up, but they'll never come close to having a real rogue. Finally, rogue pets will never have the ability to double-backstab as rogues do.

Rogue pets won't be picking locks either, and there are quite a few places to do that in Kunark.

- Gordon

As a Wizard, I am professionally interested in all things having to do with ethereal transport. As such, I was drawn to rumors concerning firepots throughout the lands of Norrath. These rumors spoke of an ancient transport system "for those of evil race", which could be accessed through having the appropriate "key", and inserting it (or using it) with the appropriate "firepot" in a Norrathian city, to be instantly transported to another city of Norrath, without the need of spell, Wizard or Druid (shudder!).

Knowing that most ancient magic was not related to the evils that plague Norrath, I have long discounted the likelihood that such devices, if they existed, were for evil races' exclusive use. Evil races are prone to destruction, rarely creativity of any sort! However, it has come to my attention, that such teleportation devices do, in fact exist, and can be utilized by all races. However, their utility is, at this stage of my investigation, limited to travel from one hub point to twelve (12) of the cities of Norrath.

This hub point is deep under the Timorous Deep, located at -12260, +4365, -277. It came to my attention, that the top of a classic Combine Era Wizard Spire, with its familiar four tines, was observable above the waves in this depth of Ocean. If you swim down directly between the four tines, you will find a square opening on the ocean floor. Descending, you will find yourself suddenly in air, in a square room, with three firepots along each wall. In the center burns a magic fire of blue, such as can be seen at certain ancient Elvish and Combine towers (e.g. Oasis). It was immediately apparent that the style of each was different, and each was familiar to a particular culture of Norrath.
