Pottery
By Faelene

Pottery is the art of making jars, bowls, and pots out of clay and other natural materials. However, for the most part, the only people for whom pottery is useful for are bakers (who cook things with it), and smiths (who make the pottery into stuff for bakers).
Recently Rogues have been able to make poison, and they require vials which can be made using pottery -- so now there is more use for pottery. There are a lot of things that you can make out of pottery that are just as good as smithing them, However, they can be a quite a bit more or less expensive, depending on what you are making.

I've worked on pottery for quite awhile now, and one of the things that I must say is nice about pottery is that it, well, WAS not very expensive -- The most expensive block of clay is about one gold, a glass shard is about one gold, a High Quality Baking Sheet goes for about 5 silver, and the water flasks and sketches cost about 1 silver each. However, a patch released awhile ago adjusted the prices of some of the sketches to an astronomical high, making it very unreasonable to make unless you are certain of success.

The second most expensive items in pottery are often the metal bits, which must be fashioned by smiths. Also, some sketches cost more than the others (Pie tins, muffin tins, etc). But enough rambling, let's get on with the basics..

Put about 5 points of skill into pottery if you want. You'll also want to buy a Basic Pottery book for about a gold(?) or so. In it, you'll find many basic recipies for clay items. Early on, I would recommend making Medium Clay Jars -- They're relatively cheap and easy to make(at least for me). To make a Medium Clay Jar, you'll need the following (approximate cost):

Medium Clay Jar sketch -- 1 silver
Flask of Water -- 1 silver
Medium Block of Clay -- 5 silver
Quality Firing Sheet -- forgot

At least, I *think* that is the recipe. Check the Basic Pottery book to make sure, then add ONE unit of water, ONE sketch, and ONE block of clay to the potting wheel. Do NOT add the Sheet yet. Combine the three items, and hope for the best. If it said "the items does not combine in those quantities", either you added too much/not enough ingredients, or maybe the recipe was wrong.
Anyway, if the items combined correctly, you should have a nice, unfired clay jar in your inventory. Once you're done with the potting wheel, go over to a Kiln, which usually (but not always) is near a Potting Wheel. Make sure it's a Kiln -- if you try to bake the clay in an oven, or a forge, you'll probably end up ruining the jar. Put one firing sheet and one unfired jar into the Kiln, and bake. If successful, you should have a medium-sized jar, capable of holding items. Medium and Large Clay Jars are useful and valuable for newbies to have in areas where containers are harder to find (Freeport for example).

Keep making these jars. If you wish, you can also try other things, but don't try anything too difficult. At 50 skill I was struggling to make pie tins and muffin tins, and Medium Bowls proved almost impossible to make. Stay small, with jars, skewers, and ceramic lining. Once you get tired of making jars (personally I prefer ceramic lining because you can stuff them into containers, unlike
jars... never tried skewers, though), try ceramic lining. You should sell your products to either bakers or NPCs in order to fund your pottery trade. The ceramic lining requires:

Ceramic Lining Sketch -- 5 silver
Flask of Water -- 1 silver
Small Block of Clay -- 1~2 silver
Quality Firing Sheet -- forgot

A bit more expensive, but worth the extra money, in my opinion. Continue making these until you reach the point where you get the message, "This item is trivial for someone of your skill level to make.." when you're using the *potting wheel*. Baking the item in the kiln is rather easy, generally, baking a clay item will result in success, especially later on. At this point, hopefully your skill will be at around 30. I began making pie tins and muffin tins at around 40, and I was soon able to raise my skill to 100 quickly. However, the main caveat with this is that with such a low skill, you will be prone to failure, and every failed attempt will cost TONS of platinum now. I was able to raise my skill quickly, but that's due to high intellect and low prices. I would highly suggest finding an alternate item which would allow you to raise your skill until you hit at least 50, since many of the sketches cost a fortune. In any case, here are the recipes...

Muffin Tin Sketch -- 1 platinum(!)
Flask of Water -- 1 silver
Medium Block of Clay -- 5 silver
Metal Bits -- 1~2 gold
High Quality Firing Sheet -- 5 silver

Pie Tin Sketch -- 1 platinum
Flask of Water -- 1 silver
2 Medium Blocks of Clay -- 1 gold
High Quality Firing Sheet -- 5 silver

One of the best reasons to make Pie Tins instead of Muffin Tins is that you don't need Metal Bits -- however, you will need to buy lots and lots of clay to make up for it, as clay doesn't stack. This is one reason why pottery isn't a huge money maker -- you have to run back and forth gathering trade items, wasting time all the way. Also, the more advanced items cost a whole ton to make now. In any case, I would stick with the cheap items, until it becomes trivial to make.

The profitability of pie tins were exploited, leading to a price hike to the sketches, but some conniving people used the patch to gain money by buying pie tin sketches, making heaps of platinum after the prices were raised. I wish they didn't have to do something so drastic. Smart move for the rich boys and bad move for just about everyone else. Oh well..

One more thing... I started this business in North Freeport, so I shall list the areas where I found the items:

Sketches -- In North Freeport in building near Bank; also can be
found in Southern Desert of Ro.

Clay -- In East Commonlands inside a building close to the inn nearest town (merchant name is Merra Clayfinger, though he is often not open for business). Also can be found in the Southern Desert of Ro, and in the Blacksmith shop in North Freeport.

Flask of Water -- Blue house next to Sketch shop in North Freeport; or just about anywhere.

Firing Sheets -- (Most irritating items to get) in Southern Desert
of Ro, or East Commonlands, from that slacker Merra Clayfinger.

Metal Bits -- Buy from PC blacksmiths, or make yourself if you are
a smith.

Glass Shards -- Buy from Merra Clayfinger or from Southern Desert of Ro.

Whew! Well, that's all the information I can think of right now. I base my work in Northern Freeport, as most of the items I need are there, though I must travel a bit to gather firing sheets. I am now a master potter, and use my skill to replace my baking goods when I foul up. Information, corrections, and other information regarding this document can be sent to [StephenY@hawaii.rr.com]

I would appreciate seeing this document being distributed to EQ sites across the web so that people will be able to begin practicing and offer their own experiences regarding this overexploited and now overpriced trade skill.

-Stephen Yasunaga
aka Faelene, on the E'ci server.
Happy potting! And try baking sometime, it's fun.
