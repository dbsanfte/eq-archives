Bashing/Kicking
By Dextor Malthorn

Many warriors find out early that Kicking does more damage than the Slam/Bash skill. This is in fact true and many warriors never give Slam/Bash a second thought. Wrong! Bad move! Permit me to explain.

Kick is good when your in a straight knife fight with the exception listed below.

I used Slam ( A skill give to large races) and Bash interchangeably. The only difference is that Slam does not take a shield and so can be used with two handed weapons.

The more commonly know use for Bash is to stun your opponent. I use this frequently when my victim has decided to run. At 28th level I stun about 50% of the time when I hit. The means my victim dies before getting as far and to his reinforcements. (can you say TRAIN!)

The less well known and by FAR better reason to keep your Bash skill up is that when it hit it will Always(99%) interrupt a spell while being cast. At high levels the caster spell can hurt us far faster than some brute. Don't Kick or bash, just melee with the caster. When you see him begin to cast...BASH! If you hit him before he completes his casting his spell Will Be interrupted. With the most aggressive casters you can prevent 50% of the spells from being cast. This is because spell recover time is less than bash ecovery time. Two warriors trading off bashes can stop 90% of the casts, only failing when the bash misses. This can be a life saver in a group and most people don't even realize what's happening.

I always get a kick when I here someone say, " Did they make these guys easier?" Happy Hunting.
