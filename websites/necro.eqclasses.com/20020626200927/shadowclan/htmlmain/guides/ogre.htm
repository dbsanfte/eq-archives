Ogre Description
Ogres are extremely large, strong and simple-minded creatures, who are constantly looking for ways to fill their big hairy bellies. Since they are usually the largest creatures around, they tend to assume that "bigger is better" and tease or bully those smaller than themselves. Their home city of Oggok is a series of caves carved out of the living rock and is full of simple stone structures. Most Ogres are easily amused, easily fooled, easily frustrated and quick-tempered (especially if they are hungry). Ogre society basically revolves around proving who is bigger and/or stronger and Ogres often get into fights over the smallest things. Ogres don't like to actually think to solve their problems. It is much simpler (and more fun) to smash whatever (or whoever) is causing the problem. Problem not solved? Add one more Ogre to try to  smash the problem (repeat until the problem is solved or you run out of Ogres). This constant fighting and the fact that most Ogre games are amazingly destructive and violent are two causes of the Ogre race's devastating strength and combat skills. Once enraged an Ogre can think of NOTHING other than crushing the source of his or her frustration. Do NOT get in the way of an angry Ogre…

Ogres do most of their thinking (when they do think) on the subject of FOOD and DRINK (and in the case of Shadowknights, planning cruel new tortures for their victims). Generally when Ogres run out of things to eat in and around Oggok, they will raid the settlements of the smaller races, destroying the buildings, eating the livestock and quite often eating the farmers who raised the livestock. With their bellies full, the Ogres happily travel back to Oggok singing songs and bragging about who did the most damage, who was the most cruel, who ate the most food, and who drank the most! Unfortunately, most other races don't seem to appreciate this type of behavior, so Ogres are quite unpopular with most other races. Also contributing to their lack of popularity is the fact that Ogres don't seem to care too much for personal hygiene. And what do the Ogres think about this?

The Ogre Warrior

Ogre Warriors are the biggest of the big, the baddest of the bad, and the strongest of the strong. Unfortunately, they're also the dumbest of the dumb. It is rare to find an Ogre Warrior that doesn't need help to count higher than the number of fingers on one battle scarred hand. But, most Ogre Warriors just don't care about all that thinking stuff. As long as there is food in their stomachs and things to crush beneath their huge smelly feet they are content. Ogre Warriors live for the opportunity to prove their physical superiority to all others and will usually charge into battle without any type of a plan other than to physically overwhelm their opponent.

Because of their size and strength Ogre Warriors generally feel that they have no need to use magic spells (besides those scrolls are too tough to read, anyway.. stupid words). However, they don't mind being healed, and actually like being magically strengthened (even if they are not really sure what is going on, magic makes pretty lights). Ogre Warriors will use magic weapons and armor, if they feel it helps them smash things better.

Ogre Warriors crave destruction almost as much as they crave food. This works itself out nicely because Ogre Warriors generally try to eat whatever (or whoever) they destroy. Special delicacies for an Ogre Warrior include: Dwarf heads, mixed with whole Gnome's by the handful (Mmmmmm). Elves tend to be a bit too spicy (especially dark elves) and trolls can cause a bad case of indigestion. But, a nice plump halfling or human bar-b-qued on the grill is a special treat no Ogre would pass up. Unfortunately, most Ogres don't eat that well all the time, and so they have to satisfy their insatiable hunger with whatever animals they can kill (and any other object that looks tasty, for example: colorful rocks). Additionally, some Ogre Warriors have been known to supplement their diet with massive amounts of alcohol.

During their youth, all Ogres (male and female) are "groomed" to become warriors. Young Ogres are taught "da hedbut game" as a way to build community, establish a social structure, and prove their strength (without killing each other). This practice is rumored by some to be one of many probable causes of the Ogre race's generally limited intelligence. Ogre Warriors continue to practice "da hedbut game" through adulthood just for fun. Young Ogres that are exceptionally smart (poor things) or exceptionally evil (blessed by the Shadow god) tend not to stay long in the warrior class. These Ogres eventually become Shamans or Shadow Knights. But, as many a smushed opponent has learned, having a true Ogre Warrior on your side in a fight can often mean the difference between being victorious and being dinner!

How to role play an Ogre Warrior:

-Don't be too bright (duh!) Remind people that you are hungry every so often (Maybe they'll give you food!)
-Occasionally recklessly charge into battle! (Me gunna STOMP YU GUUD!!)
-Try to impress others with your physical strength and size! (Me BIG agh STRUNG! yub, yub!)
-Insult/try to bully those you think are weaker or shorter than you (Puny liddul weeklins!).
-If you become too much of a bully, expect to pay the consequences! (You have been warned)

Da hedbut game

Run/jump at another Ogre warrior (run and hit space bar!) Try to make your heads meet!

"Dat's fun! Me kronk yur hed in!! Hu, Hu, Hu, Hoooo!!!.... Du agin!!!"

The Ogre Shadowknight

Background: We are mystic warriors, devoted to our gods and Soonag our master to let blood and bring glory to the Greenbloods. We view Cragneks with disdain, grudgingly maintaining a low profile until we grow into our powers.  At that point we let our clear superiority shine through.Grevak, Soonag's right hand man we treat as our master as well.  Glory is gained through their eyes.

Characteristics:  Bloodletting, torture, and death are our stock in trade. We toy with mortally wounded enemies and take great pleasure in the pain of others.  We value weapons over armor hoping to inflict as opposed to receive.  Glory and recognition is attained through helping our brother Greenbloods by improving their weapons and ability to hurt, maim and kill. We view our own injuries as an opportunity to suck the life force out of our enemies.  We quest to achieve a state of pure evil.  Shadowknights make great leaders on the battlefield since they are a little (very little) more intelligent than the warriors.

Suggested RP devices:  Possible emotes for casting include:

"Me take dis life forze for Kazik Thule."  (prior to steal strength)
"May da dizeazed breath ob Ralos Zek cuurupt yur body" (prior to disease cloud)
"Ralos Zek show me to de flesh" (prior to locate corpse)
 

How to role play an Ogre Shadowknight:

-You demand respect and those that do not give it soon find themselves on their back.
-Use whatever means necessary to accomplish your goals....values? Is there such a thing?.
-As a warrior would say he is strong from clomping things you may take the fact that you fight for your god and that is where your strength comes from.
-Approach every battle and fight without fear....but there is no need to be stoopid either *grins*

The Ogre Shaman

Most Ogre shamans are slightly smaller and slightly smarter than their warrior and shadow knight brothers. Despite being slightly smaller, they are still a powerful force to contend with because of their magic ("mojo") use. As children Ogres who become shamans are picked on by their larger brethren for being smaller and smarter than most. So, they tend to be a bit solitary and bad tempered. However, as they grow older they often form close bonds with other Ogres and Trolls who appreciate their mojo abilities. Some have even been known to befriend dark elves, if only because they don't taste good and are fun to tease.

Ogre Shamans are often seen slightly behind the battle lines muttering mojo words and making sparkly lights to smite their foes and strengthen their kin and allies. Being Ogres, they still occasionally go berserk, forget to use their mojo, and run headlong into battle at the side of the warriors.

Because of their faith and the strength of their god the mighty Cazic-Thule, Ogre Shamans can at times be almost as effective in melee as their larger kin. But when the berserker rage is over, they have to pray to regain their strength. The strong religious faith shamans possess also makes these Ogres even more superstitious than most, and is responsible for the many odd rituals which they tend to keep secret from the other Ogres.

As with the religious types of many races Ogre Shamans are fond of drinkee and are well known as the best brewers among the dark races.

While they enjoy the hedbutt game, Ogre Shamans prefer the slightly more cerebral sport of dwarf tossing. It's an exciting sport, although at times the contestants get a bit too excited and eat the dwarves. At this point, whoever eats the most dwarves is declared the winner.

How to role play an Ogre Shaman:

-Pray a lot
-Drink a lot
-Threaten to use any dwarf you see in a game of dwarf tossing
-Be bad tempered
-Be superstitious
-Make fun of your less intelligent kin as they will surely mock your small size, (Remember you are still an Ogre and not too bright yourself though!)
