Troll Description

malicious, angry, primitive

Trolls are vile creatures born in the harsh environment of the Innothule Swamps. They are primitive savage creatures, evil intelligent beasts. They prefer to be solitary, and have an extreme dislike for following the orders of others. Trolls speak a primitive tongue that is hard to understand even by those who are well versed in the language. They distrust other races thinking that they can only trust themselves.

Trolls are the monsters that mothers tell their children about while putting them to bed. They view both animals and intelligent creatures as an equal source of food. Trolls are careful to stay down wind while stalking their prey and can move with surprising stealth for there size and bulk.

They prefer dark and dank environments. And are always on the look out for caves and crevices to call there own. Humans refer to such places as "Troll Holes". Troll Holes are filthy places were few living things will go, a Troll will live out of a single cave or crevice until the entire surrounding area has been depleted of food.

On the battlefield trolls are usually disorganized and chaotic. They rarely follow orders and when they do it's usually by accident. They are terrifying opponents to face, their predatory instincts often drive them to attack the weakest members of an opposing army, which is often the mages and clerics. Many also fear them for their regenerative abilities... it is said that a troll can never truly be killed. That given enough time they will always come back no matter how horrible the injuries... they're right.

The Troll Warrior

In the primitive and instinctive minds of Trolls one word stands out above all the rest, "survive"... survival at all costs. The best way to stay alive is to kill the competition, these are words that the Troll Warrior has taken to heart. Troll Warriors are just as clever as their Shadow Knight and Shaman counterparts. But they have an advantage, Warriors are more willing to work as a team. Warriors will often times form small hunting packs that seek out and destroy the "competition". Every member of the pack looks out for himself and only himself, but there is still much safety in numbers. Once the pack grows tired of killing it will disband and its members will usually forget each other.

Groups of five or six Troll Warriors (occasionally accompanied by a Shadow Knight or Shaman) roam the countryside killing the weak and the sick. If they can catch you, you're food. This is part of the reason trolls are so hated, packs are dangerous. While leaders are rare within a pack the members seem to fight with one mind, all traveling in the same direction and ALWAYS fighting the same opponent, targeting the small and weak first. Children do not leave their homes when a pack is in the area. And when they do they are rarely seen again.

How to role play an Troll Warrior:

-The enemy must die, first the weak then the strong.
-Who is more important than yourself? No one!
-Be more willing to work in a group, but stay an individual.

The Troll Shadowknight

Troll Shadow Knights are the very definition of independence. Even when in a group they will often stand apart from the crowed thinking and plotting. Unlike other Trolls, Shadow Knights have a thirst for power and even less respect for authority than most Trolls. For some, their disrespect is so great that they disdain the very gods that give them power. If a Troll Shadow Knight could figure out how, he would rule the world. And if he couldn't rule it, he would destroy it. Troll Shadow Knights are more intelligent about worldly things such as mechanics and magic than most warriors, they are also by far the most arrogant of Trolls. Some even refusing to speak any language other than there own, being unwilling to degrade themselves so much. Many Troll Shadow Knights use magery in battle to weaken and cripple their opponents before the first blow has been thrown. Troll Shadow Knights enjoy the suffering of others, and are not above torture. In fact, they're quite good at it.

How to role play a Troll Shadowknight:

-You are a dangerous loner with no respect for any authority but your own
-You will put more thought into an attack than most trolls
-You will never show weakness in front of anyone, including yourself

The Troll Shaman

The world is a strange and magical place for a primitive race of people, but the Troll Shaman has the answer to all its mysteries. Troll Shaman are con men with magical powers, unfortunately they believe there own con. They seek to explain the forces of nature through their gruesome rituals. Shaman make other Trolls uneasy, which is exactly how they want it.

They can read ones future by looking at the palm of his palm, spitting into a bowl of bile, studying the directions that bodies fall on the battle field, casting bones on the ground, contemplating their naval, looking at the stars, dreaming... There are hundreds of ways to see the future, and the Shaman knows them all.

As if that weren't enough every Troll Shaman has a spirit helper, this spirit helps guide the Shaman into understanding his visions. Most spirits helpers are either animals or Trolls long since dead. Shaman will often times speak to their helper as plainly as they would to anyone. Their helper can tell them many things and will often times be a great source of inspiration to a Shaman.

When a truly grand vision is necessary a Troll Shaman will mix a powerful drink known as "Grol", it is made from various mushrooms and berries that are found within the swamp. After drinking it, if the Shaman survives he will often times have the truly amazing vision he was searching for, but will also have lost a little bit of his sanity as well... Perhaps forever.

How to role play an Troll Shaman:

-You must search for answer to your questions, at all costs!
-Listen to your spirit guide, he would never lie..
-If you don't know it, Fake it!
-You may now knowingly worship your god, revere your spirit guide above all!
-Beware the power of Grol, if it is taken to liberally one may forever loose there sanity and be forever trapped in a "vision".
