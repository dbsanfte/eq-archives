This has been my new home as of late. 3 main areas I would suggest to hunt. Near the zone to Mons Letalis, near the zone to Scarlet Dessert or by the temple (to get to the temple go to the zone to Mons, and head straight north). Of the three, try to get the zone to Mons for reasons listed below.

Always pull to as close to a zone wall as you can. There are random moving critter traps that can pop a sand worm or Rock Golem looking thing on you. Both are immune to snare. Both can wreck your day if you are in a tough fight or having a tricky pull. Pulling near the zone wall is your best bet to avoid these, and near the zone to Mons, there is a gradual slope near the zone wall, that seems to be free of these critter traps.

Everything here seems to have about 5K HP. The snakes have a bit more. The snakes and skeletons are considered undead, and can be Thrall'ed, Shackled or Undead DD'ed by necros. The MR on pretty much everything is pretty pathetic, and most spells stick, with 2 excepetions. Ebolt (and I would assume anything poison based) is hard to land, so do not waste your mana. Also, everything here is immune to stun, so for crowd control, use Paralyzing Earth, then get your group to move out of its range.

Hunting by the temple gives you the chance of a few nice items, but stuff near the temple seems to be a lower level then most other stuff in the zone. By the zone to Mons, everything is dark blue to me, and by the temple it is 33% light blue.

If you have every hunted City of Mist, you now what the critters here are about. Hit for 120 or so, do not walk too fast. No casters. Solo, root-DOT works well. Cascade, Ignite Blood, Splurt pretty much kills them, with a Bond or Deflux to top them off. With a group, turn your pet taunt to off, and spend your time healing the main tank while he/she grinds. Stuff will drop faster then most of your DOTs, and since Ebolt does not stick, I usually find myself Shadowbonding the tank then getting in a couple Defluxes for a fight.

Oh, and one thing that is fairly important. The skeletons do not seem to return to their homepoint after agroed. If you FD with 5HP under a skeletons feet, you are going to be there until someone comes by and helps you out. Also, if you train the zone with skellies, they will be there when you zone back in. Oh, and one more weird thing. Even though the snakes are undead, Gather Shadows seems to fool them, espectially the ones around the temple.

Cheers,

My knowledge is only limited to the Mons zoneline area... I might venture out later.
