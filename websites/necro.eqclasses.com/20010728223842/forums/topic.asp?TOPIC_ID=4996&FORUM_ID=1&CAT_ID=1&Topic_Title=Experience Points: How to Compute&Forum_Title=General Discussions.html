All you need to know about Experience.

This article deals with how to compute experience.

Here are some basic constants:

Class modifiers:

Warrior, Rogue: .9 for level calculations, 1.0 for experience awards
Wisdom Based Casters (Shaman, Druid, Cleric): 1.0
Intelligence Based Casters (Magician, Enchanter, Wizard, Necromancer): 1.1
Monks: 1.2
Hybrids (Shadowknight, Paladin, Ranger, Bard): 1.4


Race modifiers:

Halfling: 95
Human, Erudite, Wood Elf, Dark Elf, High Elf, Half Elf, Dwarf, Gnome: 100
Barbarian: 105
Ogre: 115
Troll, Iksar: 120


Hell modifiers:

Level 1-29 : 10
Level 30-34: 11
Level 35-39: 12
Level 40-44: 13
Level 45-50: 14
Level 51: 15 *every hell follows*
Level 52: 16
Level 53: 17
Level 54: 19 *double hells follow*
Level 55: 21
Level 56: 23
Level 57: 25
Level 58: 27
Level 59-60: 30 *uber hell*


Group bonuses:

We've always been the first to say that EverQuest is designed to promote grouping. Those who prefer to solo should be able to do so with some restrictions, but probably will not be able to go everywhere and do everything like a well-oiled, or like multiple well-oiled groups. This is a main foundation of EverQuest, and is what drove our decision to implement a class-based system where classes have strengths and weaknesses that complement each other.

Solo: 1
2 man: 1.02
3 man: 1.06
4 man: 1.1
5 man : 1.14
Full : 1.2

This bonus is applied to the total experience reward for killing a creature prior to distributing it to the group.


Group Distribution:

Your level / (All group members levels summated (including yours))

ZEM (Zone Experience Multiplier)

Currently, every creature in the game of a given level yields a fixed amount of "Base Experience" (experience before it is allocated to group members). That base experience is then multiplied by the ZEM which is set to a value in correlation with the risk in a particular zone compared with the reward; at least, that is the goal.

Most outdoor zones: 75
Most dungeons: 80
Some kunark/velious dungeons: 85
Underused zones (kedge, hole): 100

Regardless of zone, at level 1-6 you get a ZEM of 100


Zones which have modified ZEMs:
Droga increased by 12%
Nurga increased by 12%
Solusek's Eye (SolA) increased by 13%
Najena increased by 13%
Befallen increased by 13%
Paw increased by 13%
Permafrost increased by 13%
Kaesora increased by 18%
Qeynos Catacombs increased by 20%
Runnyeye increased by 20%
Kerra Ridge increased by 20%
The Hole increased by 25%


Formulas:

Experience needed to level (lifetime):

(Level to attain^3) * Class Mod * Race Mod * Hell Mod


Experience needed in a given level:

(Lifetime experience to level x) - (Lifetime experience to level x-1)


Experience given by a mob:

(Mob Level^2) * Class Mod * ZEM * Group Bonus * Group Dist


Pets:

If any single pet can do > 51% damage to a mob, he will get half experience. This will rarely happen in a group unless you are using some high level charmed pets. It *does not* matter who gets the final blow or anything else you hear, its just tied to whether or not a pet does more than half damage.

Pet and you solo:
Pet does >= 51%, pet gets half experience
Pet does < 50%, pet gets 0% experience

Pet in group:
Pet does >=51% total damage, he takes experience as if he were a member of the group.
Pet does < 50% damage, pet takes no experience.


Examples:

Lvl 30 DE SK needs 41,580,000 exp lvl 1-30.
((30^3)*1.4*100*11)


from lvl 29-30 DE SK you need: 7,433,400 exp
((30^3)*1.4*100*11)-((29^3)*1.4*100*10)


a DE SK killing a lvl 28 mob solo outdoors gets 82,320 exp
((28^2)*1.4*75*1*1)

a lvl 31 DE SK in a group of lvls 29, 30, 38 killing a lvl 35 mob in a dungeon will get 36550 exp
((35^2)*1.4*80*1.1*.242) .242 = 31/(31+29+30+38)


From here you can extrapolate many quantities such as kills to level, time per kill, and time to level. If you take experience to level for Levels 1-2, 2-3...59-60, you will also see why hell levels exist, just a mathematical effect of the way they chose to manage these equations.


Pre experience-patch, class modifier was not part of the experience equation. Now it is and it has the effects of negating the class modifier in the experience needed to level.

The class penalties were not *removed*, they were *compensated for* by giving even splits of experience in groups and multiplying each player's experience received by the same class modifier that increases his experience required to level except for rogues and warriors.

Again, it was not taken out, it was merely compensated for.

Summary
(Stolen from the January 14, 2001 Producer's Letter)

Classes no longer have a true class-based experience penalty, making it easier for people to play the class that they want to play, rather than the class that they feel compelled to play due to faster advancement.

Race based penalties are no longer shared with the group, and in truth are not severe enough to greatly impact the advancement speed of any particular race. Even the race with the worst penalty will level no slower than a human monk does now, and in truth will level faster due to the other changes that went in.

The grouping bonus increase, in addition to the other changes, makes grouping much more efficient, without reducing the current efficiency of soloing for those who prefer that route.

Experience modifiers turned up in the other zones should encourage people to spread out, have more fun, and allow those staying behind in other areas to have more fun themselves since the area is less crowded.
All players, assuming that they have an experience penalty, or ever group with anyone who does, will level faster.
