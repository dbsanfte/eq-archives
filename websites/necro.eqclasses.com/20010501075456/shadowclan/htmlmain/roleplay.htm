Battle Etiquette
  • Don't take someone else's kill. If someone else is already fighting something then don't jump in unless invited or unless they are in trouble. If it looks like they are in trouble, ask them if they need help. If you actually do wind up jumping in and saving their butt, the simplest way to do it is to target them and use the /assist command. This command allows you to attack whatever they are attacking. 
  • Don't block someone's escape route. This is a sensitive subject to anyone who has tried to flee the terrors of the dark deeps and found themselves blocked by YOU. This can put you on the "bad" list of someone for weeks on end.  Although EQ is non pvp oriented, the monsters can be so hard that having a  friend around just when you were least expecting them can save your bacon. People who I thought were strangers have saved me several times just because I was nice to them at one time or another. 
  • Don't be greedy. For example: there are 3 female barbarians standing around in the snow shivering in their mini kilts and halter tops and one of them has 3 bear cubs attacking her. One of the other wild women tries to attack one of the bears and gets yelled at by the one wearing the "monster magnet" perfume for stealing one of "her" kills. This is just not right. Just because the monsters like the taste of your blood doesn't mean that you need to hog all the action. See 1.2 about the "what goes around comes around" principle.

Loot Rules
  • SINGLE GROUP:  If on an ALL CLAN hunt... any loot acquired will go to a member in the group that will USE IT.  If more then one will use, then will random amongst the ones able to use it.   Else it stays in clan for auction or passing on to someone who can use it in clan.  If not a clan group then random as usual.
  • Multi-Group raids:  On any raid which requires more than one group of 6, and which the Shadowclan dictates the loot policies, the following rules apply:

    Need Over Greed:

    When an item drops, all those who are present that can use and would     use the item will /random for it, being careful NOT to /random until the raid leader has said so, with the victor getting the spoils. Once a person has won an item on that particular raid, they are excluded from future /randoms on that raid until everyone of that class has attained an item. The raid leader will call out the numbers to be used in the random.

    Example:
    Lifebane is frolicking through the plane of fear with Shadowclan and some invited friends when the Uber Robe-o-death drops. Usable by Necromancers only, Lifebane looks around and sees that there are 2 other necros, Kimmurl and PhatLewtBob (an invited friend of the clan). Bruthah the mighty finishes looting his own corpse for the 15th time that hour, and calls for all necros who would use the Uber Robe-o-death to /random. He then supplies the numbers to /random between (he calls out because he is the raid leader) this time Bruthah, in his infinite wisdom, chooses 20 and 1020. Lifebane types /ran 20 1020 and comes up with a 983 (we still think he rigged it). Kimmurl is busy flogging Verrin and /randoms a half-hearted 265, and PhatLewtBob comes up with 762. Hoowah! The elder has finally won something! Lifebane moves over to the corpse and picks up his prize, and with a glazed look heads off into a dark corner…. Twenty Minutes later, The group leader that had slain the latest beastie loots it (only group leaders loot corpses on raids of this nature) and calls out that "Hoowah! Dos Critter has the Bracer-of-helpImlostinthewoods!" This item is usable by wizards, enchanters, and necromancers. Bruthah the mighty, while looting his corpse again, calls out for the people of those classes to /random for it and chooses, in his infinite wisdom, the numbers 300 to 1300. Braiel, altho falling into the enchanter class, decides that she has no use for the bracer-of-helpimlostinthewoods and says that she will not participate in the /random for it. With high hopes, Drezzden, Kimmurl, and PhatLewtBob all roll for this with Kimmurl coming out the victor. Note that Lifebane did not /random for this item. He got his loot for this trip…  Several hours later, Bruthah is looting his corpse again when an exclamation goes up of "Hoowah! A necro only whack-bonk of power!". PhatLewtBob wins this by default as both Kimmurl and Lifebane had already won their items. If another item dropped that was necromancer only, the process would begin again with all 3 necromancers /randoming for it.


    Recap:
  1. Loot is determined by a /random roll, with the highest roll winning the prize.
  2. Only group leaders are to look into corpses or for the loot.
  3. Everyone that is on the raid is entitled to a fair chance for loot, both Clan members and invites. Latecomers may not be eligible or suffer a penalty, as determined by the raid leader. On the other hand, those that participate on the "break" of a particular raid may be entitled to additional benefits, as determined by the raid leader and stated BEFORE the raid. (this can be +200 to rolls, etc)
  4. If an item drops that your class can use but you do not want, you are not required to random for it. You can also roll and if you win it, offer it to another on the raid that can use it.
  5. You are only eligible to /random for an item if:
    a)       You have not already won something on that trip or 
    b)   All those that could use the item had already won something on that trip.
  6. There may be special situations where an item will be given to a particular person without a /random. Example: an epic quest item drops and it's the last piece that a particular person needs for the quest, this must be stated by the raid leader PRIOR to the beginning of that particular raid. 
  7. When in doubt, the raid leader has final say, backed by the attending guild officers.   If you have a question, refer back to this rule.

Notes and Exceptions:    

  • God Items:  All non-epic God items will be straight randomed to all that can use it regardless if they had already won an item. (God's only spawn once a week, and their items are not counted as plane gear.)
  • Epic Quest Items:  These quest items will go to the highest leveled race/class that needs it the most.
  • Non-Rare Gems/items:  Non-rare gems and items (such as emerald rings, pages, etc.) are looted by group leaders. Group leader then give these gems/items to the group cleric to cover the expenses of their reagents required to buff the tanks.  Pages and Words are handed out to the appropriate caster class.
  • Rare Gems:  Diamond, jancinth and black sapphires are zone randomed by the group leaders and then group leaders random within there party.
  • Breastplates and Robes:  Do NOT count towards the "1 Item Only" rule.  They are the exception. 
    Example:  If "Necrobob" wins a Bracer and then a BP drops later, "Necrobob" can also /random for the BP.  If "Necrojohn" wins a BP first however, "Necrojohn" can not roll for the Bracer if it drops afterwards. The Breastplate/Robe counts as the 1 Drop IF it is won first.   So it is possible that one Raid Member can win 2 pieces before anyone else wins one IF the BP/Robe was the second item won.  But, from prior experience... this is a one in thousand scenario.
  • The Final Piece of a Set:   If an item drops that would complete a persons full set of his class's armor, then that person gets that drop by default. The exception to this rule is a Breastplate or Robe. Those are /randomed as usual.  Though this may sound unfair at first, remember that the person who needs only one item will not touch the other items of that class that may drop.  So others of same class have better chance to obtain those pieces.
  • Plane of Fear:  Cryosilk pieces will be randomed to Enchanters and Mages first.
  • Plane of Hate:  Cryosilk pieces from chests will be randomed to Necros and Wizards.

Twinking (Giving lower level players items which they can not attain at their level) & Powering (Powerleveling)
  • Shadowclan does not promote the blatant twinking or powerleveling of younger/ lower level characters.  This means that no one in Shadowclan is required to "twink/plevel" a younger member.  
  • A Clan member can give an item to someone they think has earned it or deserves it, but is not required to do so.  
  • Likewise a clansman may choose to aid a fellow clansmen in their hunting, BUT it is not required nor expected from anyone.  This is a free choice made by the Member.
  • Item Prizes from contests and quests, and items gotten through guild auctions are 100% acceptable since the items were obtained through clan in rp fashion or in a manner which benefited the clan (auction funds go to clan).
