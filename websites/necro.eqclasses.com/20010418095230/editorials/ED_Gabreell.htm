Helpful Tips for Necromancers 

Player Editorial from April 28th - Gabreell the ShadowQueen

 

I've been playing a Necro in EQ since release back in March of 99. In that time I've discovered for myself and learned from others, many nuggets of wisdom which have been invaluable to me as a Necromancer. Perhaps they will help you as well. Read on, and glean what you may:

--Did you summon a pet, and get attacked before you could issue him the weapons he needs? Well, he's now too busy fighting that mob to accept your trade. This used to irritate the heck out of me because I couldn't give him weapons until the fight was over. No problem. Just tell him to back off (You have a hotkey for this right? Good..) and instantly give him the weapons. You have to give him the weapons VERY fast after you have him back off, but it's works. You can give him 2 weapons at once, and he will duel wield them fine. Even if he goes back to attacking, the trade window will stay open.

--You feigned death with 12 HP's and all the mobs left, but if you stand up you know they will come rampaging back to you with a bloodlust quenched only by your screams of death (Wow, that sounds like a necro more than a dar ghoul knight *giggle*). No problem. After you successfully FD (Feign Death), just use the /q command to go linkdead and don't log back in until your character is *completely* logged out of EQ. This should take about 2 or 3 minutes. Log back in and you will be standing up, but the mobs that you successfully FD'd against have now forgotten you. Remember to Gate fast, or invis up fast, you are weak. It helps if you try to FD in a semi-safe place where there are no stationary or wandering mobs. I have no problem doing this usually. If you know you aren't going to make it, just go to a safe place and FD + /q.

--Did you bind in a spot you thought was safe, but now you are coming back in the middle of some KOS mobs? Or, did you accidentally train some KOS mobs back to your bind point, and are being attacked as soon as you log back in? (Yes, embarrassingly enough, I've been stupid enough to do this.) First of all, don't panic and try to get away--You'll probably only die many more times. Instead, use the wonderful /q command as soon as you load back in, while you are being wacked on. This will keep you from loading back in again in that spot after you die. Now either wait for the KOS mobs to leave before you log in again, or log in as another character and petition a GM for help.

--Remember, if you plan on fighting in a zone for a long time, make sure that you keep re-summoning your pet until you get the best possible summon for your pet's level(s). There is a HUGE difference between the worst summon, and the best summon. For instance, the level 49 pet spell can summon an undead companion NPC that can be between level 37 to level 41. The level 37 pet only hits for 47(max), and has MUCH less HP's than the level 41 pet which hits for 55(max) and has MUCH more HP's. This isn't the only reason to have the best summon (Highest level pet). Also, keep in mind what it was like when you were level 6 trying to melee a red level 9 mob. Remember how much you'd miss in combat compared to when you'd fight a blue mob? Well, that was because of the level difference. Level difference means EVERYTHING in EQ. It means more than equipment, and it means more than skills (In most situations). Get that highest level pet and he will be missing your opponent in melee MUCH less than the lowest level pet. Plus he will be doing more maximum damage over time--Trust me. Even if it takes 20 re-summons to get that pet, it's worth it if you will be fighting in the same place for awhile.

--Are you trying to get the best pet for your level, but they all con blue at your level? You can't figure out which summon level you have by just conning the pet, so be creative. First of all, it's important that you remember what the max damage is for the pet you've summoned. The level 49 pet hits for a max of 55 for instance. After you summon the pet, have it fight something easy that is close by. If you send it to fight a low green, it will hit for it's maximum quite often. Take note of what it is hitting for and you will be able to judge it's level easily. No mobs around to fight right now?--Have a friend that is close to the pet's level con it and tell you what level it is. No friends around that are close to it's level?--Duel a friend, and take note of what it hits for at it's max. This will take some testing because the pet will NOT hit for it's normal max vs another player in a duel. I know for a fact that the best level 49 pet hits for 33 max in a duel. After you see what level the pet is, have it back off, then have your friend log out and in so that you will no longer be in duel mode (Being in duel mode can cause problems when you are fighting things together.).

--Running out of bone chips? No problem (For me anyway). I've played on many servers as a necro, and I have never had a problem coming up with bone chips. I have *never* camped bone chips myself to harvest them. What I'll do is take 30 minutes and sit in Nektulos during prime time playing hours (8PM EST is good.) auctioning that I'll buy bone chips for 2 or 4 pp per stack of 20. The newbies will flock to you to sell their bone chips. I've even had low level necros sell me their chips at that price. I stock up on a couple hundred bone chips and I'm set for a month. Lets face it, after level 20, you should have no problem coming up with 20 or 30 pp to stack up on bone chips, so don't be greedy : ). If you are low level, just go to a zone that has a lot of decaying or regular skeletons running around (Nektulos and EC come to mind) and auction every 4 minutes or so that you'll buy bone chips. You can usually get them for 20 per 1 pp if you are patient.

--Use PE(Paralyzing Earth). People give PE a bad name, saying it's no better than root. All I can say, is that they are SO wrong. I've had PE last consistently for minutes at a time. It consistently lasts longer than Root. It may cost 100 mana as opposed to 30 mana for root, but it is well worth it IMO. After all, you are a necro, you should always have plenty of mana.

--Use Dooming or Engulfing Darkness to stop running mobs instead of Rooting or Nuking them. There are many reasons for this. Some mobs like to run when they get to about 5% HP's or so. This is a very bad thing, because they usually alert some of their buddies and cause a train. Stopping runners is a MUST. Learn to do it, or die. This is where darkness comes in. If a mob is "darked", when it tries to run away it will not be able to run at all. Instead it will stand still, not attacking, just letting you whack on it with spells or your pet. The problem with root is threefold. Number one, unlike Darkness it has a variable duration so the mob may break root and start to run again. Secondly, when a mob is rooted it will not just stand still when it tries to run (Like it does with darkness), it will attack you. Third, root will nullify the effects of your darkness spells (Both the DOT effect, and the movement speed reduction effect.) so tell those wizards in your group to PLEASE stop rooting. Remember, darkness has a pretty high resist rate. Just make sure to start trying to Dark a monster when it's still at half HP's. I like to start darking a mob when it's at 75% then re-dark it when it's at 30% or so just to be extra safe. Darkness does not cost a lot of mana to cast, and Necros are mana machines. Also, don't even bother with Cascading Darkness. It's basically a useless spell. It costs WAY too much mana, and is often resisted. Dooming Darkness costs much less mana, lasts the same amount of time, and I'm has the same (If not very close to the same) movement speed reduction factor. If you want the damage that Cascading Darkness does, just use another DoT.

--Use lich-type spells (HP for mana line) and Lifetap DoT spells (Leech, Vampiric Curse, Bond of Death, etc.) in conjunction. This is probably your most efficient way of doing damage (With the possible exception of Fear Kiting outdoors.). The Lifetap DoT spells have decent damage to mana ratios and will heal you from your lich type spells. This is a potent combination. You can't beat it's efficiency. Throw in a manastone (If you are lucky enough to have one.) and you have some INSANE efficiency. How does 330 almost unresistable damage for around 90 mana sound?

--Invest in HP items after you have 200 INT. HP items are so nice for a necro to have. Remember, for a necro HP=Mana=Damage. They are all interchangeable for a necro when you factor in the Lich line of spells, Lifetap DoTs, and maximum HP's. Consider your HP's mana, because they basically are. A good way to invest in your HP's is to ditch your INT rings and pick up some Adamantite bands (75 HP's each and -10 vs magic) for 150 extra HP's. Also, some jewel-crafted Platinum Fire Opal rings that give 55 Hps each work well if you don't like the negative magic resist factor of the Adamantites. Is there a cleric around? Beg or pay him/her for some HP buffs : )

--Learn to use ST (Screaming Terror) and root (Or Paralyzing Earth if you have it). If you ever end up with 3 or 4 mobs, you will need to learn how to neutralize them, taking some of them out of the fight while you dispose of the others. Necros make decent enchanters if they are well-played. I have solo'ed up to 4 or 5 Dar Ghoul Knights after a bad pull (Yes you can pull as a necro if you have good pet control.) by making judicious use of ST and root. ST one mob and have your pet back off immediately so that he only attacks the other mobs and doesn't force the mob you casted on to break ST. Remember, you can root a mob while it is ST'd and it will not cause it to break ST. Keep as many mobs from fighting as you can. Keep them contained while you concentrate on one mob at a time. Take out casters or healers first. If there are no casters or healers, take out the weakest melee mob next (Since it will drop the fastest) then take out the stronger ones. This is what works best for me.

--Your pet can't taunt that mob off of you because you applied too many DoT's too quickly? No problem. Just FD (Feign Death) for a bit while your pet builds up some hate on their aggro list. After they are good and pissed at your pet, stand up and continue to help. Remember, you can only FD for 2 minutes before your pet will suicide.

--Learn to use the Pact of Shadow (Transfers your HP's to target) line on your pet. Keep your pet healed with your HP's, and lifetap your opponent to gain back your lost life--Aren't Necros great?! : )

--At lower levels, don't camp items, and don't spend much time focusing on your equipment. All you need to do is level. Levels are key in EQ. That 8 hours you spent camping the Black Tome w/ Silver Runes with a group in Najena yielding no EXP and a few deaths would have been MUCH better spent leveling up somewhere else. Remember, when you become higher in levels, you can camp whatever items you need very easily, and platinum will be much easier to obtain so that you can buy those items you want. Stop wasting time chasing after low level INT items. If you see them auctioned for a good price, and you have the money, then go ahead and buy it. Otherwise, just keep going for EXP. Trust me, it's a much better use of your time. I had to learn this the hard way with my first level 50 Necromanacer.

--Need weapons for your pet? Well, now that they have nerfed the delay factor of weapons for pets, you can use any pair of 1H weapons to get your pet into duel-wield mode. Damage and delay means nothing. Now just focus on cost and weight of weapons. I still use daggers because they are light and fairly inexpensive. If you go to an area where fishing poles and bait is sold, and where people fish a lot, you can often pick up A LOT of rusty daggers from that vendor. People get rusty daggers from fishing, then sell them to a vendor. They are cheap and light. If you can't find any rusty daggers, many inns sell regular daggers for around 2 plat. If I'm going to be fighting a lot of high level mobs in the same zone for a long time, I like to use a Gnoll Hide Lariat for my pet. It procs a stun and goes off pretty often in a pet's hand. They cost around 25 pp each and can be bought easily from players in South Karana. I like to stock up on them whenever I'm in that area.

--Use Harmshield offensively. If your pet is taking a beating, and you need a little extra edge to finish off that tough mob, try this--Cast Harmshield, and sit at the mob's feet. Aggro'ed mobs *HATE* it when you sit down. It will normally stop focusing on your pet and attack you for the duration of the Harmshield. Harmshield often just sits there refreshed for hours at a time--You might as well make good use of it. This is a strategy that few necros know about. Even fewer know how well it works. This strategy goes well with the next tip...

--Tank for your pet. Even at level 50, I have been in rare situations where I had to tank for my pet. Sometimes it becomes obvious that your pet is not going to live much longer no matter how many times you pact/heal it. The mob is at 20% and you only need a little more time. If you have a lot of HP's, get in there an take some hits for your pet like you did back when you were level 3 : ). It works. It also helps when the cleric is trying to pull of a CH(Complete Heal) on your pet, but the pet is about to die. It can buy you those extra seconds that often determine the outcome of the battle.

Wow, this post ended up being A LOT longer than I had planned : )

I hope these tips can help you out someday. Remember, much of what I wrote is only my opinion. They are things that have worked for me as I've learned to play 2 necros to level 50 on Xegony and The Nameless Servers. If these tips don't fit in with your play-style, just ignore them : )
