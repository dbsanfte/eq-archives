Iksar Description (top of page)
Iksar History

            The Iksar are a proud race of lizard-like creatures created by the great god Cazic-Thule far back in the Elder age, near the dawning of time. In the early days, the Iksar were tribal in nature, forming small pockets of themselves upon their vast continent of Kunark. After many many years the Lizards came together into a vast empire that spanned the entire continent. This was done under the war hero turned emperor, Tharakus the Fist. The emperor was a cruel and jealous sort, and ruled with an absolute power. None dared oppose him. It was this simple fact that kept him from bearing offspring (he was concerned that they would mature and covet the seat of power for their own) and so when he died many years later, the great empire fell apart to the squabbling of many warlords, all seeking the title of “Emperor”. Eventually the peoples of Iksar spread out and were lost to antiquity. Some formed great City-States such as Cabilis while others traveled nomadically.  Even today traces of this vast power can be seen in all reaches of Kunark.

About the Iksar Race:

              War and Bloodshed are the bread and wine of their lives. As such only the classes geared towards warfare are available to the Iksar: Warrior, Shadowknight, Monk, Necromancer, and Shaman.

Roleplaying the Iksar: 

            The Iksar are an intelligent race of Lizards whose entire history and daily lives involve fighting, warfare, and conquest. They are undoubtably some of the most powerful melee fighters in the world. They are proud bordering on arrogant, and tend to be viscious and cruel. This stems from their belief that they were the first of all intelligent races on Norrath. They have no great respect for those who wield magic (other than shaman, that is “god magic” and therefor sacred) as they consider them physically weak, and strength is all to the Iksar. Even amongst their own kind Necromancers are outcasts, tho still respected for the power they wield. They do not tolerate failure or weakness in any form, and either offense is often met with a swift death. Quick to anger but with long memories to bear grudges, they are not someone you want against you. Iksar tend to become venerable (read: weaker) at or near 140 years old, at which point they will leave their family, lands, and titles behind and go off to die in private, with dignity. Ritual suicide is not uncommon amongst the elder of the Iksar, and is considered preferable to being seen weak and frail with age.

Iksar Shaman: (top of page)

The spiritual leaders of their culture, the Shaman play a unique role. They act as spiritual guide and leader, as well as strengthen the troops with their great and powerful magic. They are a powerful addition to the forces of the Iksar and are respected greatly by their peerage.

How to role play a Iksar Shaman:

  • Your magic can easily swing the tide of any battle. You have the ability to “buff” the melee fighters to be far better than they could normally be.
  • One of your most powerful line of spells allows you to slow a creatures attack.
  • You possess extremely powerful Damage Over Time spells (or DOTs) that can cause considerable damage to your opponent.
  • You can cast spells that will heal yourself or others.
  • At higher levels you can summon a pet to aid you in your battles.
  • Wisdom and Strength are important to the shaman.

Iksar Necromancer: (top of page)

Shunned by even their own people, the Iksar Necromancer cares little for his people or their opinions of him. Power is all that he seeks, power over the living through the use of the dead. He knows that his own people fear him, and he derives a great pleasure and satisfaction from this knowledge.

How to role play a Iksar Necromancer:

  • You can summon an undead creature to fight your battles.
  • You can cast powerful magics that tear the lifeforce away from your opponent to heal your own wounds.
  • You can cast Damage over Time (DOTs) spells to destroy any who dare oppose you.
  •  Intelligence is of great importance to the Necromancer.

Iksar Warrior: (top of page)

 “Grunt”, “Meatshield”, “Tank” and proud of it! The warrior has the advantage of being able to take and deal out a tremendous amount of damage. Very few adventures can meet with success without a warrior in their midst.

How to role play a Iksar Warrior:

  • You have access to the best armor and weapons in the game
  • You are a necessity in nearly every group
  • You have the ability to “slug it out” better than any other class
  • You have more hit points and armor class than any other class by far
  • Strength is of great importance to the warrior

Iksar Shadowknight: (top of page)

The shadowknight is a powerful union that is halfway between the necromancer and the warrior. Shadowknights are able to wield excellent weapons and wear protective armor, but have the added bonus of being able to cast Necromantic spells (tho at a lower level than a true necromancer)

How to role play a Iksar Shadowknight:

  • A Melee fighter that can also cast spells
  • Has the ability to focus energy into a harmful blow to his opponent called “Harmtouch”
  • At higher levels can summon pets to aid you in battle
  • Strength and Intelligence are of great importance to the Shadowknight

Iksar Monk: (top of page)
Iksar are the only race other than human that can be this powerful class. Monks are the undisputed champs of dealing melee damage. Having a wide array of extra abilities makes this a fun and powerful class to play. Unlike the warrior, monks do not encumber themselves with heavy equipment, preferring to be agile and light.

How to role play a Iksar Monk:

  • You deal out more melee damage than any other class. Period.
  • You have the ability to focus your spirit to mend your own wounds.
  • You can “feign death” fooling your opponent to leave you alone.
  • You are one of the best classes to “Pull” in the game. Meaning bringing creatures back to your group to fight.
  • You require very little in the way of equipment to be effective. Your body is a weapon.
  • You rely on evading damage rather than absorbing it like the warrior. The downside is you do not have many hit points.
  • Strength and Agility are very important to the monk.
