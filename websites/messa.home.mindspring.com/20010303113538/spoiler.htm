*Unrest*

How to tackle the basement in Unrest:

Let's start with some assumptions:
1) All spawns are 19 min 40 sec apart.
2) Try to take on no more then two creatures at a time.
3) You have to be able to kill hags quickly, these are by far the deadliest mob in Unrest.
4) Your party consists of at least a Warrior, Cleric, Wizard and at least one of extra, preferably a Cleric, a Caster, or a Druid.

Attached is a bitmap that contains a map with all the spawn points, the green dot is where the spirit in Unrest (has Dwarven Work Boots!) spawns the green line is the line that the mobs walk on when coming to you or retreating.

The first thing you have to do get to the spirit at point 11. Most people think that training the entire mob out and having your party run in is the best way to do this, I have a different way and it gives you a chance to set the time between spawns.

Setting up of the basement begins once you get to point A. Try to clear all creatures up to that point. Remember always target Hags first followed by mortuary fungus. Once you have cleared up to point A. have your party med up and get all healed up (you will have 19 min to do this so take your time).

Once everyone is ready attack the creature at point 5, normally a hag, a GDB, or a Fungus. Right away run down to point 6 and attack the creature there. Now wait 2 min at point 6 medding. At this point you'll see 2 mobs on either side of a pool of blood, Do not walk around the pool there are traps with fungus in them.

Attack the mobs at point 7. And again wait 2 min and have your casters med up. Then move down to point 8 and attack the mob there, wait at point 8 for 2 min. Move on to point 9 and kill the mob there. Retreat to point 8 and med to full health, this will normally take about 8 to 10 min, Now you are ready to take on the spirit. Attack the spirit at point 10, sometimes a TT will be at point 11, be sure to stand with your back against the wall if the TT throws you into the pit at point 13 you WILL die.

Once you have killed the spirit kill the TT but try to melee it, you'll need this battle to last about 2 min. If your party is up in health and mana take on the TT on the ledge in the pit, have a caster cast a spell or have a warrior shoot an arrow.

Do not try to take on the pit at this time, wait till after the next spirit spawn. You tend to have about 2 min between battles, have your tank run all the way back to point A pulling whatever is there to point 11. If you can keep your battles staggered to 2 min you will get a gap of 6 to 8 min before the spirit spawn, this is a good time to try to take the pit out. It is a good idea to clear the pit before the spirit spawns so when the TT spawns afterwards and you get thrown into the pit you won't get nailed by 4 mobs.

NOTES:
1 ) Always keep the path clear all the way back to point A, it will normally take you this much time to kill the spirit once he starts retrieting.
2) Always know how long you have to the spirit spawn down to the min.
3) Order to attack things: Hags, Sprirt, Fungus, TT, GDB, Barbes, Bats, Monks.
4) Sometimes a hag will spawn in place take this as an opportunity to clear even further back to the stairs out of the basement.
5) Casters: Don't cast unless it's needed, with 2 min intervals you will need the mana. When a hag spawns "Open the can of Woop ass."
6) Clerics: Your primary responsibility is to keep the warriors alive.
7) Warriors: KILL KILL KILL. =)

I knew that Llrodd, now an undead skeleton, waited by a well on the first level of Befallen. I also knew that Commander Windstream was known to appear in a secret room on the lowest level of Befallen. In our time there I had gained 2 pieces of the fabled Mallet from the remains of Elf Skeletons who inhabited the entry room of the 3rd level, Windsteam must hold the 3rd piece, a container needed to combine the other 2 pieces into
the Thex Mallet. I positioned my group just inside the secret room next to Windstream's Throne. I took myself to the well on the first level and waited for Llrodd
to appear as I knew he must. My wait was short. I gave the note to Llrodd and he screamed at me to seek Windsteam, hurry!! hurry!! 1 2 3!! My group shouted to me that Windsteam had appeared and I was needed on the 3rd lvl. Knowing I could not make it to them swiftly enough by the normal route I  cast invisible to undead on myself and leapt down the well straight to the 3rd lvl. I was damaged by the fall but regained my feet and sprinted straight ahead to where my group was engaged in furious battle with Windstream. Joining the fray I struck and struck again at the nightmare before me till the zombie warrior was hacked to pieces. In a pouch on his remains was the 3rd piece of the Mallet. Quickly we fled Befallen to the sand dunes of the West Commons. My hands were trembling and I put the 2 pieces of the Mallet into the magical container and closed the lid. Magical energies flared inside the box and as the light faded away I opened the smoking container to behold the Thex Mallet, fully reformed. I gave all of the treasures we had gained in the dungeon to my group, keeping only the Mallet for myself. Rushing back to Neriak I made my way to Loveal S'Nez and bowing low, presented him with the Thex Mallet. His joy that day knew no bounds and my standing with the dead soared to new heights and I was favored of the Queen of Neriak, The Lady Chistanos. For my unswerving loyalty to The Dead, S'Nez did gift me with The Ring of The Dead, a magical band that would bind my soul to any point I chose. A powerful item for a Shadow Knight indeed. I hasted downstairs to Xantis Ixtax and presented her the ring and 10 pieces of gold whereby she charged the Ring for me. It has served me well and whenever I am in Neriak I have it recharged that I might use it again. Thus ends my tale of the Lore of The Ring.

*Items from Dwarven Smith on Sister Isle*

There may be a few who  don't know yet, so here goes.  A person can conceiveable get a dwarven ringmail tunic  ac 12 +8 sv  vs magic fire and cold for just 4 gold.  Yep, thats right, 4 gold.  Talk to Moodoro Finharn (Crow's Tavern, Qeynos) and give him 4 gold, sometimes he will give you a knight card.  Save up the knight cards and take them to a dwarf on the siterhood isle in ocean of tears.  Look for a dwarf mechant in a small tent, give him the knight card and you can get several nice items, including dwarven chainmail, black iron boots and different bits of chainmail armor.  You can also get rusty weapons to, its a luck of the draw...

*Testament of Vandeer*

Go to Crows in Qeynos and talk to Modooro, for 2 gp, he will sell you the Book, go to HHP and kill the named smuggler (dressed like a guard) and he has a page.  If you go to Highpass and ask about The Testament of Vanear he tells you that he did indeed buy page 30 from a man named Moodoro some time ago but that it was recently stolen by a man wearing the uniform of a Qeynos guard and that this guard was still in Highpass, because he could "feel it". He also says that Moodoro also had page 34 but refused to sell it to him. If you go into highhold pass and go toward the orc side, you will see a lake or pond or whatever. Jump in and swim around until you come to a hidden camp of smugglers. Only one npc there has a name, which is Dyllin Starsine. Kill him and you get page 30 (and you also get a sealed letter which is for the Muffin for Panos Quest in Freeport).  Beware that any smugglers around him will come to his aid.  Faction hits when you kill him are:(-Corrupt Qeynos Guard, +Guards of Quenos, -Kane Bayle, -Circle of Unseen Hands, -BloodSabers) Go back to Qeynos and talk to Modooro again, he will offer to play cards with you for 4 gp a shot.  Play with him till you get the Jester card, give it back to him and he gives you the last page.  Combine them in the book and you have the Testament.  (I think, can't remember now, but I think that he gives you the first page needed when you first talk to him as well.)  It is no drop.

*Brazen Brass Kilij*

In the wood elf city on the same platform as the druid guildhall is a weapon shop, outside the shop, is a npc that if you hail him will tell you about kilij's plans and if you bring him the plans and three enchanted gold bars he will make a weapon.  The plans come from a sand giant in So Ro, hes not named but he is in the twenties instead of the thirties like most sand giants, about six weeks ago we killed him twice while killing sand giants in the span of about two hours and got plans both times. Get an enchanter to enchant three gold bars, give the npc the bars, dont stack them, and the plans, in return you recieve the Brazen Brass Kilij, magic, 10 damage, one handed slash, 35 delay wt 8.5, class warrior bard and rogue, race all.

*Bypassing East Commons*

Before you zone into the East Commons from the West Commons get as high as you can on the mountains on the north side.  After you zone into East Commons turn and go straight up over the mountain.  This will cause you to fall off the world and will warp you to the end of the desert at the beginning of the East zone.
