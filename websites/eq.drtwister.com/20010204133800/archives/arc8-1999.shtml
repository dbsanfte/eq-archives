This has to do with exploring the zones of EQ while offline via the tutorial. I wasn't too clear the first time I posted about this, and I know the message board can get kind of cluttered and hard to read sometimes, so I will post a revised version of how to do this here. I'll try to be as straitforward and detailed as I can without confusing anybody, but sometimes I can get carried away, so who knows. If you still have problems after reading this, post a message on the message board and see if ya can't get some of those folks to help you out. That method usually takes much less time than waiting for my *cough* response. So, anyhow, without further ado, here are some revised instructions(which includes a way to view zones offline with names longer than 6 chars).

1) First of all, make a copy of tutorial.exe and save it as tutorialorig.exe or whatever you wish, unless you feel like redoing this every time you want to explore a given zone(since EQ will patch your tutorial.exe to the original).

2) Open up tutorial.exe in your hex editor. If you don't have one, get one at bpsoft.com.

3) Go to line 00197790 in the code. It should look like this;
00197790 65722E2E2E0A0000536F616E64736F00 er......Soandso.
00197790 7475746F7269616C000000004552524F tutorial....ERRO
If you don't know how to go to a certain line in the code, do a search(alt+f3 in hex workshop) for "tutorial". There should be 4 or 5 matches, and the line I posted above should be the last. Just keep on using the "Find next" command until you come to it. You should now be at the line I posted above.

4) See where it says "tutorial...."? That is the key to our mission. Simply change "tutorial...." to "hateplane..." or "freeport....". Go to the space where the first "t" is, make sure Overwrite, and not Insert is on(in hex workshop, it will say which is on the bottom right hand corner. Press the "insert" key just above the delete key on your keyboard to change this. See the note on the bottom of this post regarding zone names.

5) Now, save tutorial.exe, exit the hex editor, and run tutorial.exe. You now should be in the zone you wrote in. If you aren't, and the program gives you an error, delete tutorial.exe, make a copy of the backup, paste it into your EQ directory, rename it to tutorial.exe, and repeat steps 1-5 over again. You probably accidently didn't do something correctly.

6) When you want to play online, rename tutorial.exe to something else, and your backup to tutorial.exe. Now, you don't have to do this, but if you don't, it will be patched to the original state, and you'll have to redo what you just did if you want to keep exploring that certain zone.

Now, there is another way to do this that I know works, but it is harder to do, and requires more editing, not to mention being more limited.
