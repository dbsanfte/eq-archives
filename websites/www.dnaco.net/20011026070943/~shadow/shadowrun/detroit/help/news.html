NEWS DETROIT SITE HISTORY
^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^
 
The setting: The brisk evening air blows wild through the hibernating 
branches of the tree's surrounding the medium sized ranch style house.  A 
boy and his 4 friends muddle over piles of paper and scattered dice.  One 
of the boys who'm everyone called Lucifer sat behind a open notebook on 
it's standing edge.  Each person laughing and eating some form of 
Dorito's were all at the table for one common reason.  To play Shadowrun...
 
The reason: Lucifer sat at the table and gazed upon his friends and 
smiled.  "I love Shadowrun."  He loved Shadowrun so much.  Why?  So many 
reasons come to mind that it's hard to say.  The most prodominant of 
these being the creative thought process, the gathering of friends, 
sharing of ideas.  All these fit the glove.  
	Turning his head he surveyed his friends.  Havok stumbled over a 
LINUX Unleased book on or about page 600 or so.  His friend Tracey sat 
there laughing in a very odd way and drawing her character in stick 
figure art.  While Eric sat there filling his head with more Shadowrun 
information than Mike Mulhvill prolly knows.  
	During this time of thought Lucifer was attending school at the 
local community college along with Havok and Eric.  Havok and Lucifer 
each heading in the same educational direction.  CPS.  Eric off on his 
own world learning, Vulcanology, Intro to the Internet and History of 
Dinosaurs.  Oh..  He's a libral arts major.  Go figure. 
	Lucifer decided one day while playing on Shadowrun Seattle that 
this online experience was so great and shown him so many new ideas and 
aspects to the Shadowrun universe that it needed to be expanded.  Seattle 
was begining to become to small for it's exponential growth.  So the idea 
of California Free State was thrown into the mix as a prospect for a new 
Shadowrun Site.  Lucifer decided that the check points and playability 
and restrictions of the city made it to much of an unhospitable place.  
So after a few weeks of thinking he figured it out.  Detroit, MI.  Why 
Detroit?  Lucifer lived near Detroit and worked in Detroit.  The city has 
a upbeat edge and enough crime to fill the holes that society has left.  
A perfect setting for the next site.
	To make this site he knew while he could go it seperate of 
Seattle.  He would have no code support.  For systems such as the matrix 
and  astral that can become quite a headache.  Why would people want to 
play in your city when you don't have the code quality of Seattle?  They 
wouldn't.  So we needed to have Seattle hold our hand as we strived to 
get on our feet.  A post was mad one late December 96 night.  The post 
alerted hundreds.  The first man to speak up in a loud voice.  Shayd.  He 
asks a simple question.  "Why?"
	The question is answered with an open heart and Seattle offers 
it's help.  Sometime in December 96 Shadowrun Detroit goes up on a site 
provided by a friend of a friend.  Willie lets us use his site.  Time 
passes the site becomes to small for our needs.  We lose our home and 
roam the streets begging for room and board.  Megalinx take us in.  Only 
for a short time though as this place wants rent.  So then we go to our 
father of sorts.  Boss cat Morris.  He offers us a machine and all we 
have to do is pay for the electricity at a one time charge of 16MB of 
ram.  We pay and bingo we have a house again.
	Time goes on and things get shaky.  New staff and new ideas are 
coming to quickly.  The site begins to have growing pains.  Lucifer 
leaves for personal reasons and Dan takes control.  Dan and Geoff and 
their staff begin to make this place into a streamlined code enviroment 
with equal RP opportunity.  However as this new staff and hence new era 
come to their peak the troubles again persue the site like a hunter.  The 
site again goes through it's diffuculties.  Staff members leave for 
undisclosed matters and new faces step in to take their place.  A new man 
takes the stand.  Zeke.
	Zeke had been with the original staff since close to day 1.  He 
brings in his new staff and moves Dan's papers out of his desk.  This 
staff falls short though.  While stronger perhaps in Roleplaying ability 
it's code skills faulted and mayhem ensues.  Code falls apart on a daily 
basis.  Those who did have some remedial coding ability did their best to 
hold the site togeather and for a time did.  But the code patches began 
to stop up the plumbling.  Things got worse.  
	Now we come to today.  Lucifer has returned.  Taking the position 
as Head Director and asking Shayd for his services the site begins to 
take a distinct form again.  Code being repaired.  Spelling being fixed 
(Heh.)  The city again growns.  The familiar wind blows again through the 
humble december tree's outside Lucifer's new house.  This time last year 
he began a new idea.  This time this year he sets out to finish it.  He 
can't do it alone though.   He needs his friends.  He needs his staff.  
But even we can't make this place great.  We still need you.
 
So we go on...
 
Lucifer

NEWS BUILDING POLICY
 ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^
 
1) All building is to be done by special Builder characters. Player
   Characters may not own ANYTHING.
 
2) All Builders must append their name with the letters BLDR.
 
3) Builders may NOT build residences for their PCs, or other PCs
   without direct permission from a Wizard. (This applies to Staff, too)
 
4) Builders may not make personalized items, nor may they create
   restricted items, such as guns, armor, credsticks, vehicles, etc.
  
5) Construction is by assignment ONLY. If you have a good idea,
   mail a Wizard, to see if the idea is workable.
 
6) ALL assignments MUST come from, or be OK'ed by, a Wizard. Period.
 
7) All building will be done from the room provided for you off the
   Building Nexus. No JUMP_OK or FLOATING rooms are allowed.
 
8) All buildings MUST be inspected before being linked. Inspection
   consists of a member of the Magic Staff checking aura quality, and a
   Wizard checking overall quality. Please do NOT
   page staff members to inspect your building.
 
9) Upon completion of a project, mail a Wizard with the name of building,
   dbref, person who assigned you the project, and any other information 
   you want the person inspecting to know. You will receive mail when the
   inspection is complete. If you don't receive mail within a week, resend
   the inspection request, or page an ON duty Wizard.
 
10) Please do not bother a staff member if your building is not linked
    right away. Mail A Wizard if you think someone forgot.
 
11) No linked buildings may be modified, without direct permission from a
    Wizard.

Required attributes for exits:
        @Desc, &AURA
        @Succ, @Osucc, @Odrop
        &STAFF_NOTES, &CREATOR
 
If the exit will ICly have a lock on it, add the following:
        @Fail, @Ofail, &LOCK_RATING
        LOCK_RATING is the rating of the maglock or other system.  Please put
        a note in the STAFF_NOTES with details on the lock or system. 
 
If an exit is to blocked against Astral Movement, it MUST have:
        &BARRIER =
 
If the exit is a solid door, it MUST have:
        &IS_SOLID =1
If it's something thick enough to be soundproof, add:
        &IS_SOUNDPROOF =1

NEWS BUILDING ROOMS
 ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^
All rooms MUST have the following attributes set:
        @Desc, &AURA
        &CREATOR, &STAFF_NOTES
        &SECURITY, &SECURITY_RATING, &MAGICAL_SECURITY
        &WHERE_PLACE, &WHERE_ZONE
 
Keep your descs clear and concise. Try to portray a complete image of the
room, including where exits and 'places' are. Please watch your grammar and
use a dictionary. This makes the job of the person inspecting your work 
much easier. A good practice is to write your descs/auras in your favorite
word processing program and run a spell check upon completion.
 
Auras give an accurate idea of the emotional content of the room. Remember 
an aura is the ONLY description an astral person sees, so make the main 
features of the room clear for them as well. See 'news building astral'
for more info.
 
CREATOR is you. Put your name here. Please use correct capitalization of
your name. eg. NakedBLDR

NEWS BUILDING ROOMS2
 ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^ ^
STAFF_NOTES is an attribute staff uses to note things about the room for
judging purposes. If there is something noteworthy that a staff member
should know about the room you can place it here, but the majority of the
time just set it to 'none'.
 
SECURITY is a listing for the Judges that explains in relative detail the 
security and other features of the room. Considering that Shadowrun is a 
violent world, this attribute is useful in the event combat or other 
nastiness occurs in your room. Statistics should be cleared with the 
appropriate RPAdmin. Remember, the more detailed you are, the better the 
chance of the Judge handling the call as you believe your building's 
personnel would.
 
SECURITY_RATING delineates Knight Errant response time and is a scale from 
AAA to Z. See 'news building secratings' for more info.
 
MAGICAL_SECURITY is the astral security of the room. It's like security 
for the netherplane.

When creating auras for Rooms and Objects there are a few simple things
to remember:
 1) Astral space is made of moods, emotions, textures and tones. It is
    perfectly alight to include feelings in your astral descriptions.
     For example:
        A person on the astral plane looking at a letter or sign would see
        the emotional content of the item, not the actual words.
 2) Man-made objects show up on the astral plane. They are generally
    shadows of the real thing, with magical influences showing up.
 3) Background count is important. A Room can have a general emotional 
    feel to it based on past events that have influenced the etheric space.
    However, permanent background count is very rare.  Please consult a
    magic admin prior to placing a permanent background count on a room.
 
Well thought out astral descriptions will add to overall quality of the MUX,
and you never know when you'll want to play a magician.

Having a building built for you by a construction company on the MUX costs 
the following amount in IC funds: 

LOW LIFESTYLE AREAS:
 
 Simple, one-story structure is 50,000 =Y=
     Rooms for buildings are approximately 20x20x12' and are 1,500 =Y=
     Each additional stories are 10,000 =Y= per floor.
 
 If a building already exists, adding to it has a 10,000 =Y= charge, plus
 room costs, etc. 
 
 RENTAL
 
 Rental costs are ten percent of the building's total cost PLUS any IC
 security a player wishes. 
 
 EXAMPLE:
 
 Joe wants to build a two-story house with four rooms.
 50,000 for the building
 10,000 for the second story
  6,000 for the four rooms
 Total is 66,000 PLUS any security costs. Rent for this building would be
 6,600 numen per month plus any security. 
 
 This is for LOW LIFESTYLE CONSTRUCTION. The following are price increases
 for other lifetime areas. 
         MIDDLE - LOW cost + 40 percent
         HIGH - LOW cost + 80 percent
         LUXURY - LOW + 160 percent.
 Please remember that the staff of the MUX will work with you on prices,
 and financing is available.

Charlie to get his? Simple: Charlie was not well known. Charlie spent
more time out of the trix then inside of it. He doesn't go out and explore
the matrix systems, figuring out the insides and outs of each. And he
rarely triggers, not from skill, but from lack of time.
     Bob on the other hand spends at least some time in the trix every
time he logs on, whether it is simply cruising the shadowlands or actually
exploring. He makes himself known, in the matrix ways, and is someone who
triggers, not from lack of skill, but excess time in there (it happens).
