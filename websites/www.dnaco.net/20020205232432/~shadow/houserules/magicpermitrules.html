Magical Permits Rules and Decking Requirements

 

 

Legal Penalties for Illegal Magic Use/Possession

(T) Class E Magic

Possession:__________10,000/1yr

Transport:____________20,000/1yr*

Threat:______________40,000/1yr

Use:________________40,000/5yrs

Intent:_______________40,000/15yrs

 

(T) Criminal Acts Using Magic

As per criminal offense (Assault, Murder, Invasion of Privacy, etc)

 

Above type (T) offenses cover the possession, transport, and use of magical items and spells ways not covered by standard criminal law, such as casting non-harmful spells (Mask, Detect Life, etc) at Forces greater than 3. Given the difficulty of proving such activity, as recordings of astral events are thus far impossible, prosecution for casting spells at excessive Force is uncommon at best. However, threat of hostile spellcasting/conjuration while in the possession of Foci of significant Force is considered a separate crime from the initial threat.

 

Transport, in this case, governs the transportation of hardcopy spell formulae (electronic/text and medicine shield varieties), foci, conjuring materials, as well as hermetic circle and shamanic lodge materials. Spells known constitute free speech, and can be transported legally, regardless of Force, for obvious reasons. Transport penalties listed are for offenses entirely within the UCAS. Offenders found to be transporting Class E Magical supplies across international borders face a mandatory sentence of 100,000Y and 10 years in maximum security prison, most of which will likely be endured while wearing a magemask.

 

Threat consists of any criminal threats made against an individual or group while the perpetrator is in possession of any items that fall under the category of Controlled Magical Paraphernalia. This includes conjuration materials and ritual materials.

 

Use, in the case of spell formulae, does not include teaching the spell, as dissemination of information is protected under the First Amendment to the UCAS Constitution, and specific spell formulae do not, unless otherwise specifically indicated, present a threat to National Security or the National Defense. Please note this is the case in the UCAS specifically, and in other nationalities/sovereign territories, such protection is not guaranteed. In the case of ritual and conjuring materials, use is defined as any usage of said materials for a single magical process in excess of legal levels. In the case of Foci or Hermetic Circles/Shamanic Lodges, use consists of any usage of items of these types which exceed legally mandated limitations, as it is impossible to regulate/monitor how much power from these items is used during a specific magical process.

 

Intent, as always, consists of situations where an actual threat has not been made, and illegal usage has not yet taken place, but the decision to use such items in an illegal manner can be established.

Getting a Permit:

        In order to register for any of the following types of permits within the UCAS, the licensee must be able to provide the following: A legal UCAS SIN or Resident Alien ID, a current valid mailing address, and a clean criminal record for the past three years. Renewal of permits and licensure is the responsibility of the magician/PC/Player, not Staff.

 

General Practitioner's License: While the UCAS does not require all magically active individuals to register with the government, it does require that all active practitioners do so. In many ways, this is intended to be analogous to handgun laws. Ie: it is not illegal to have a hand. To put a gun in it, you require a permit.

        Cost: 1500Y

        Background Check: Last 3 years.

        Renewal: Annually

        Ritual Sample: No

        Allows: use of magic under F4, eligibility for other permits

Decking Requirements: Level 6 sin, Hack the Accounting and Administration hosts of Lone Star or Knight Errant for 6 file successes in each host.

 

 

Ecclesiastical License: As many shamanic magicians' magical paradigm and spiritual views are heavily interrelated, many early shamans in the old US defended their Lodges from claims of being a 'danger to the community' by suing for protection under the guaranteed freedom of religion clause in the Constitution. After the formation of the UCAS, the new government decided to sidestep the issue completely, according that shamanic lodges are protected on religious grounds, but that the construction of a Lodge of greater than 3 KWE, roughly the equivalent of a 3-meter Hermetic Circle, requires the owner to obtain a special License. Location of the lodge is required to be revealed, and the law stipulates that any Lodge powerful enough to require licensing must be at least 100 yards from any residential structure, school, or government building.

        NOTE: Federal Law stipulates that in order for a religious group to qualify for tax-exempt status, the tenents of their faith must be on file with the UCAS Gov't. For non-tax-exempt religious communities and individuals, such registration of their beliefs is not, and cannot be mandated by law, though a request for that precise information is present on the application as an optional question. Nor can licensure be denied based on the tenets of one's faith. Many states and municipalities, however, levy significant 'Cross-Cultural Awareness' taxes on licensees who do not answer the optional question regarding the tenets of their faith.

        Cost: 2,000Y (3,500Y for not revealing path)

        Background Check: Not beyond 3 years.

        Renewal: Annually.

        Ritual Sample: No

        Allows: Construction of Shamanic Lodges over F3.

Decking Requirements: Level 6 sin, 6 file successes in LS or KE Accounting and Administration, and 5 File successes in Department of Licensing (to stand in for 'church' registration).  ADDRESS of the Lodge is important.  If the Address does not match to where the physical location of the Lodge is, the permit will be voided.

 

 

Research License: Sometimes considered purely the hermetic's answer to the

Ecclesiastic License, this form of license allows a hermetic magician to construct Circles exceeding 3 meters in diameter. It also allows both magicians and mundanes to possess Hermetic Libraries above Level 3. Higher Rating Libraries are considered to be verging into 'controlled' magical territory, even as the UCAS's inclusion of the Bill of Rights pays lip service to the concepts of Free Speech. Intended to allow for research and scholarly pursuits, as well as continued innovation, this permit does not allow licensees to exceed legal limits on any matter except Hermetic Circles and Libraries. Those wishing to conduct research into specific types of High-Powered magic also require the necessary permits for such magic, as listed in the professional practitioner's permit (below). NOTE:  This permit is highly irregular for one outside the jurisdiction of a university and/or corporation to possess. Unaffiliated individuals applying for said permit will have to submit letters of intent to the authorities and go through a research board review in order to ensure that their research is not of a detriment to the community or individuals within said community.

        Cost: 2500Y (1500Y Extra for Board Reviews)

        Background Check: Yes

        Renewal: Every year

        Ritual Sample: No

        Allows: Construction of F4+ Hermetic Circles and Libraries

Decking Requirements: Level 6 Sin, 6 file successes in LS or KE Accounting and Administration, plus an Appropiate System Hack for Five File Successes (see below).

 

 

Educational License: Dissemination of information is protected under the First Amendment to the UCAS Constitution. However, in order to hold a job as a legal magical instructor requires licensing. The UCAS Department of Education's minimum requirements are a Master's Degree in Education (Or in relevant course material, dependent upon university requirements), and successfully completing competency examinations to demonstrate reasonable familiarity with the subject matter. These examinations are often closer to a psychological evaluation than an aptitude test. A background check for criminal activity is also standard, though a criminal record does not preclude the issuance of the license. Most UCAS states, and many municipalities, add additional requirements onto these.

        Cost: 2500 Y (Many universities pay these for their professors)

        Background Check: Yes

        Renewal: Every 2 years

        Ritual Sample: No

        Allows: Employment as a magical instructor

Decking Requirements: Same as Research, but a seperate hack is required for each one of these permits.

 

 

Professional Practitioner License: The default 'Magician's License', this license enables a magician to exceed legal limits on specific magical effects and/or items in the performance of his or her professional duties. Examples include: Illusions for entertainers, defensive magics and anchored spells such as Armor, Detect Bullet, Detect Enemies, Anti-Bullet Barrier, etc for bodyguards/security professionals, Foci, Conjuring/Ritual materials and Lodge/Circle materials for Talismongers, etc. NOTE: Each type of professional practitioner license applies to Power Foci as well as specific spell foci and spell category foci. Weapon Foci and Foci not pertaining to the types of magic allowed by the specific License type are not legal. All foci above F3 MUST be registered. Unless otherwise indicated by the specific type of Professional License, the costs/statistics for all are as follows:

        Cost: 5000Y

        Background Check: Yes

        Renewal: Yearly

        Ritual Sample: Varies

        Allows: Varies

 

NOTE: These are subcategories of the Professional Practicioner's License. You may be charged for each if you choose to get more than one from this category.

 

 

        Enchanting License: Enchanting itself is not illegal. However, in order to legally produce materials and foci in excess of legally controlled levels, the enchanter must have this form of Professional Practitioner License. When selling or distributing legally made Class E items, the enchanter is obligated to ensure the possession of a legal license to own and/or transport such items. Failure to do so warrants immediate revocation of professional licensure and criminal charges for Transporting the indicated items.

                Ritual Sample: No

                Allows: Possession and Transport of Foci, Spell Formulae,

& Magical Materials in excess of F3

Decking Requirements: Level 6 sin, 8 file successes in LS or KE Accounting and Administration, and 5 file success hack in the Department of Labor's main host.

 

 

        Entertainment License: An addendum to the Professional Practitioner License, this license recognizes the usage of detection, illusion, and non-volatile manipulation magics in the entertainment industry for the production of special effects. It also authorizes the licensee to own, carry, and utilize Foci, Spell Formulae, and Magical Materials in excess of Force 3 -in the performance of their professional duties. All performances and recordings utilizing illusionary content for entertainment purposes must provide clearly posted written warning available to prospective attendees in advance of payment. Failure to do so will void this license and constitute criminal Intent.

                Ritual Sample: No

                Allows: Possession, Transport, and Use of controlled Magical Paraphernalia in job-related activities.

Decking Requirements: Same as enchanting license

 

 

        Healthcare and Therapy License: This type of Professional Practitioner License covers the use of Healing and Manipulation spells by members of the healthcare community, including doctors, nurses, paramedics and DocWagon HTR team mages, as well as Foci and Formulae related to those types of spells in the execution of their duties.

                Ritual Sample: No

                Allows: Possession, Transport, and Use of controlled Magical Paraphernalia in job-related activities.

Decking Requirements: Level 6 sin, 8 file successes in Administration, Accounting, and Secure Records in LS or KE, and a hack of an appropiate Health Outfit for a cover story (See below).

 

 

        Security/Investigative License: The only license that allows the possession of Combat Spell and Elemental Manipulation formulae and Foci, this is the type of Professional License used by security companies, corporate sec personnel, bodyguards, private investigators, and both DW and Krash Kart HTR Combat magi. (Note: DW and Krash Kart HTR mages usually possess this in addition to the Healthcare and Therapy License above.) This license authorizes the bearer to possess all types of spell formulae, foci, etc, including those above F3. It is also the only type of professional license to require renewal of ritual sample every 3 months.

                Ritual Sample: Yes

                Allows: Possession, transport, and use of restricted magical paraphernalia in execution of job duties.

Decking Requirements: Level 8 sin, 10 File successes in Administration, Accounting, and Secure Records of LS or KE, and an appropiate hack of a sponser corp/group.  Must be renewed every six months!

 

 

Controlled/Dangerous Magic License: This is a separate license issued to practitioners whose normal thaumaturgical duties place those around them at risk. The applicant must show a legitimate, legal purpose for the usage of potentially dangerous magics during the execution of their normal employment or community service duties.  This evidence must be countersigned by the applicant's employer and/or community leaders.  Should the applicant receive this license and an accident occur or harm come to bystanders for some other reason, the countersigned would assume equal legal responsibility. As a result, very few community leaders will vouch for the 'community service' activities of someone without considerable proof of competence and good faith. 'Community Leaders' in this instance refers to local heads of religious organizations/orders, local, state, and federal government officials, and/or the head of the local chapter of the municipal police department or contracted designee. This license is required in addition to the Security/Investigative License by most security companies, for insurance purposes.

        Cost: 2500Y

        Background Check: Yes

        Renewal: Yearly

        Ritual Sample: Yes (every 3 months)

        Allows: See above

Decking Requirements: Same as Security/Investigative

 

 

Focus Registration: All Foci above F3, as well as all Weapon, Combat Spell and Manipulation Spell foci, MUST be registered. In addition, all weapon foci are subject to the normal registration procedures for a weapon of that type. If unregistered, no professional license in the world is going to keep you out of jail for packing one. If a permit is not available for that type of weapon, the Focus cannot be registered. In order to register Foci a magician must possess a valid permit for the Focus's use, as well as the prerequisite General Practitioner's Permit.  When registering a focus, one needs to describe what the focus looks like (is it a watch, ring, keychain, staff, etc?), its KWE rating, category, and spells bonded (if applicable).  Those who wish to use expendable foci do not need to register these expendables and pay fines for each, but do need to pay a flat fee every two years.

        Cost: 50Y Per Focus

        Expendable Foci Cost: 1500Y

        Background Check: No

        Renewal: Every 2 Years/By weapon type

        Ritual Sample: No

Decking Requirements: Level ? sin, 5 file successes in Administration, Accounting, and Secure Records of LS or KE.

      Non Dangerous foci: Level 6 sin.

      Dangerous Foci: Level 8 sin.

      Note: Some of the foci cannot be given permits. Those are any without the P in their legality code.

'Hack an Appropiate System':  These hacks will be made up by the players and judges.  This will allow the PCs some ability to customize and not do a cookie cutter permit hack type thing.  Each player may deciede their own cover story and the Mjduge will assign a system to hack to get that cover story, and Gmnote it when they hack their permit. 

      This sort of thing has to be handled carefully. A PC wanting a security permit COULD hack the very easy Stuffer Shack system. But if they go and use lethal force and then try to hold up the permit, they might get caught in a backlash when someone finds out Stuffer Shack Security can't use lethal force.  Whereas a player hacking MCT to get a cover story could probably pass that (of course, if that person caused trouble on Ares grounds and held up their permit, they would be in just as much trouble)....

 

'Reverse Hacking of Permits': Suppose you find a magician you dislike. Suppose you happen to find his SIN and the permits linked to it. You could hire a decker to go in and wipe out his permits, or alter them or alter them or what not.  This will be allowed, but there are no specific rules for it.  Each run will probably be customized according to how the victim got their cover story in the first place. It'll be very easy should the magician in question have a legal sin. A hacked/fake sin will require much more work. 

 

This will allow for some more work, and some cat and mouse games.
