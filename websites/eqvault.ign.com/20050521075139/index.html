  May 18 Patch Message
Print News | Mail News
EverQuest - Live Update - 05/18/05
----------------------------------

*** Spells ***
----------------------------------
- With the last patch, we made a change to the way that intoxication effects work so you are no longer completely immune to them if you have maximum alcohol tolerance. Instead, you now mitigate the effects based on your alcohol tolerance skill. Due to this change, intoxication effects have been reduced or removed from a number of detrimental NPC spells where they would now cause the encounters to be more difficult than intended.

- Storm Guard and Frost Shield now have 60 minute durations.

- Increased the range on Kessdona’s Roar.



*** Missions ***
----------------------------------
- Skrepiz Plaguebringer will now only appear when you have completed all previous requirements in the Rise of Marnek mission.

- If you are on the mission to Unrest, Nisha Lightbrook will now respond to your first hail instead of requiring two hails for a response.

- You will no longer be able to loot the notes for the Hate's Fury mission out of order.


*** Items ***
----------------------------------
- Gloves of Wicked Ambition now have +5 to stun resist, +6 to dot shield, and +2 to mana regen.

- Mirrored Mask now has a type 8 augmentation slot, by request. Now players can bind a focus augment to the item if they wish.

- Gem of the Spring Wind from the good DoN camp is now named "Shard of the Spring Wind" and is now a type 7 and 8 augmentation.


*** Alternate Abilities ***
----------------------------------
- Raised the cap on AA's so that those people who were maxed out can receive all of their Vet rewards.
- The shape changing effects of the Jester of Bristlebane will now disappear after 10 minutes.

*** Code ***
----------------------------------

Windows 98/ME:

- Users with these operating systems should no longer experience the bug where there UI renders but the 3D scene does not when they first enter the game.

-- The EverQuest Team

Sat 14 May 2005

  Clarification on Veteran's Rewards
Print News | Mail News
The CS Team has been working on fixing some of the problems with the veterans reward system, which went live Friday. Here is the latest from Kytherea at EQLive:

Due to all of the information that our community has submitted, we have determined two problems with customers not being granted all of their veteran rewards.

First, as many of you know from tech support, there was a billing discrepancy on accounts before 4/01, when our billing system changed. Our policy is that customers are enabled from their account creation date up to 4/01 as we feel this is the most appropriate solution for our customers without reliable data. This data has been live since 5/12 and was not the cause of some of the issues today. What we didn't determine and fix until now is that some customers were not being credited with time enabled until their subscription cycled after 4/01. This is most likely the cause for customers who are not receiving all the rewards they are entitled to, including the reward for the next eligible year as a bonus for subscribing to the Sixth Anniversary Promo year.

We have also identified that in some special billing circumstances, such as changing from regular account subscriptions to All Access or Legends, that the system was not crediting the appropriate subscription time accrued. This issue has also been addressed for those customers.

We thank you very much for all of your information as the only resolution was due to contacting customers in the community to track these specific circumstances down. Please log out of the game completely, repatch and log back in; your veteran rewards will be automatically updated to any characters that are enabled for veteran rewards.

In addition, we have not forgotten about those customers who's subscription time accrued is being miscalculated due to being exempt for any period of time. This is still being worked on as well.


She also addressed the issue of splitting off a character from your main account, with less pleasant news: "there is nothing we can do about a character that split off from the account getting the rewards. I'm sorry!"

If you are having problems, be sure to log out all the way, type /veteranreward when you get in, and look under your AA tab for the veteran's AAs.

Sun 17 Apr 2005

  New EQ Producer Craig Knapp
Print News | Mail News
Craig Knapp is the new EQProducer and as an introduction shares some of his ideas in a producer's letter to the players:

EverQuest Producer's Letter
Greetings Norrathians!

First off I wanted to take some time to introduce myself as the new producer of EverQuest. EverQuest has been a large part of my life for the past three years; including the past two years as the game's Associate Producer. During my time on the EverQuest team I've come to realize one of the great things about EQ is the friendships and the community that we’ve all come to love. We all remember the first time we logged into EQ. If your experience was anything like mine, you were shocked that so many windows could fit on one screen! Thankfully, most of us found our way out of the initial shock and found folks who showed us the ins and outs of the game. Many of these in-game friendships have grown into friendships outside of Norrath and for that I thank EverQuest.

We all know that EverQuest is not just a game. The game and its community are both a large part of all our lives. Our team takes great pride in this fact, but at the same time we are humbled by the responsibilities that come along with it. As developers, our primary responsibilities are to make an entertaining game and to support the thriving EverQuest community. Only by working closely with you, can we keep EverQuest successful for years to come.

As I hope you have noticed, our team spends a great deal of time on message boards and we are constantly gathering what's important to you and bringing that information to the forefront. We continue to develop procedures that facilitate that process, but allowing our community to thrive requires an ongoing dialogue with you. For instance, class re-envisionment has been, and continues to be, a hot topic. The recent patch represents many of the initial changes we’ve made together, and we welcome your continued feedback on them. Our goal is to get these changes out as quickly as is reasonable, but to pace ourselves knowing full well that this will take many iterations and updates. This continues to be a top priority and we've already made many changes based on your feedback. Your continued feedback allows us to make EverQuest a better game.


His letter continues on to discuss server merges, instancing the Plane of Time, and more hints of "ambitious plans" in the next year.
