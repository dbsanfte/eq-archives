Friday, 18 January, 2008


  Should AA be Revealed in LFG?  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
The number of alternate advancement points of any given character should give you an indication of how well they play, right?

Not necessarily. But some folks would like to have the number of AA listed along with a level in the "looking for a group" tag, so they know if a person has the extra advantages that many AA points give.

Others argue that the number of AA is meaningless and will cause some people to be ungrouped if they don't have a certain number.

The developers considered the addition of showing AA along with level some time ago, and then ultimately decided not to implement it.

Where do you stand on the issue? You can join the discussion here.

Tuesday, 15 January, 2008


  Massively Interview with Smedley  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
Massively.com has published an interview with SOE President John Smedley, which includes comments about EverQuest, as well as comments concerning plat farmers, which are much more of a problem for SOE than you might think:

Massively: While we're talking about EverQuest ... I think some players are at the point where they don't understand why you're still running the game? SOE has this reputation for keeping games like Vanguard and Matrix Online going, and we were just talking about the economics. What is it about these games that make them worthwhile to keep them going?

John Smedley: Great subscriber bases for EverQuest and EverQuest 2 and Galaxies, pretty much. I will tell you, I'm as surprised as anyone that the longevity of EverQuest. Our first spreadsheet had this thing running for about two years, and even that we thought was a little optimistic. Nine years later you go, "wow". There's still a large community there. As long as people pay us money and we love what we do, we'll keep running it.

The challenge is that, with EverQuest and EverQuest 2 on the shelves together ... why would people want to play a nine-year old game? Honestly the acquisition in EverQuest has slowed down a lot, and it's hard. We've refocused on one expansion a year with these games now, I think we let quality slip too much when we were doing two, and I'm happy with where we are on that. We have a great player base and we're making some money so there's no point in not continuing.

Massively: Earlier you mentioned the problem of farmers with regards to Station Access. I know that's something the company feels very strongly about?

John Smedley: I think the issue of farming is higher on the radar now than it ever has been. The behinds the scenes things are really frustration. A lot of these farmers are essentially stealing from us. What they do is they charge us back all the time. They use a credit card –sometimes stolen, sometimes not – to buy an account key. They use the account for a month, and then they call the credit card company and charge it back. We have suffered nearly a million dollars just in fines over the past six months; it's getting extremely expensive for us. What's happening is that when they do this all the time, the credit card companies come back to us and say "You have a higher than normal chargeback rate, therefore we'll charge you fines on top of that." We're really trying to get on top of that. We're taking our current efforts up about five notches to Defcon 1 on this issue. They bug us even more than they bug our customers, and we're definitely taking steps to implement rigorous anti-farming efforts.

It's actually really amazing to sit and watch these people work. I've personally sat with them as they're tracking a farmer, and you'll see a mob spawn – this guy's got a bot that within half a second has them moving towards the creature even if it's halfway across the zone. It's a serious problem.

There's much more to the interview, so check it out!

  Hotfix Today: Guild Banners  [ Comment ]
Print News
1/11/2008

*** Hotfix ***

- In order to prevent an exploit, we have temporarily disabled the ability to summon players to guild banners and fellowship camps in many instances, primarily raid instances. Once we have a long-term fix in for the exploit, we will return the banner and camp functionality to its previous state.

- The EverQuest Team

Rashere adds:

"I obviously can't go into the details behind the exploit, but its not related to hacking. Folks found a loophole in the way banners work and were exploiting it to bypass raid lockouts. Code is working on closing that loophole, but we needed to shut it down sooner than they'll be able to get a fix in place. You can still plant banners and get the effects, you just can't teleport to them in those instances for the time being."

Friday, 4 January, 2008


  Unannounced Changes to Crystallos Zone  [ Comment ]
Print News
Players are reporting a "ninja nerf" to the Crystallos zone --- the wyverns are now immune to snare, thus keeping players from "kiting" them (kiting is the technique used by certain classes that involves snaring and dotting a mob, inflicting damage to it while it runs around the zone after a player, slowly dying).

Why? Developer Nodyin had this to say about the change:

"I wouldn't say I'm anti-kiting (I have played a Wizard to level 76 so far, started with him in 1999) but it's not appropriate in the hardest zone aimed at group play currently in the game."

The Beastlord class AA can still be used to snare unsnarable mobs, but that is going to change soon too, according to Nodyin in the same thread:

"I'm pretty sure I saw that on a list of things to fix. Just FYI."
