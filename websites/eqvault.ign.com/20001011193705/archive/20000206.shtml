Gordon Infobits
The daily tradition continues. Here are some excerpts from board postings made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the official forums today:
    Stat System
    The Stat system in game is not broken and does work as intended, but we prefer not to release in-depth information on game mechanics. We, or more precisely, I regret releasing the information that I did, as I am now fully convinced that releasing this type of information detracts from the explorative aspect of the game, like any spoiler.

    As far as the strategy guide goes, we unfortunately didn't have creative control over its development, and as such didn't have the amount of input into it that we would have liked. As a result, some of the information contained in it might not be entirely accurate. Of course, as the game is dynamic and subject to change, any static printed material goes out-of-date fairly quickly.

    More on Statistics
    oshlarian,

    Are you really going to keep bumping every single thread on the board with "He thinks we are going away, Guess again?".

    No, I don't think you are going away, nor do I want you to. Regardless, I am NOT giving out any specific information about stats, even if you do "bump till doomsday" as you suggest you will.

    On the Mistwalker
    This is correct. The Mistwalker's proc rate has been restored to its previous value. The pet now automatically dissipates after one combat round.

    I can't think of any situation where the MW could have done 2000dmg Elderan. The NPCs would automatically turn on any Mistwalker on their list and kill them in 1-2 combat rounds anyway. Now that the mistwalker automatically disappears after once combat round, that special code is no longer necessary.

    The only situation where'd they'd last longer than this is if multiple mistwalkers were present. Even in that case, I can't see them getting anywhere near that much damage in.

    Please keep in mind that the latest change to the mistwalker was not a balance issue, it was to stop a potential problem that could occur if several people with Mistwalkers got together. With maxed stats, it would be possible for them to nearly insure that a mistwalker was always present, thus mitigating the majority of damage that a NPC has the potential to do.

    Shaman Pets
    [Why don't Shaman pets (which are all wolf spirits) come with inherent Spirit of Wolf?] Hmmm, good question. Since shamans can cast SoW, I really wouldn't see any problem with this, and it seems to make sense.

    I'll bring it up in the tuning meeting.

    Fennin Ro Split Logistics
    Moving 1 account is not a technical challenge. Moving 5000 accounts (about half of the number of active accounts on Fennin) is not a technical challenge. Moving 5000 accounts after pairing them up into guilds, verifying with all said-guild members that they indeed want to move, accounting for un-guilded groups that want to stay together and THEN conducting the move while both servers are down, *IS* technically *and* logistically infeasible. Moving in this fashion would, based upon my estimates, take over 100 man-days (1 man-day = 8 man-hours).

    We have to do it in a way that can be scripted in order to have any chance of doing it in a limited amount of time, hence our decision to move volunteers without regard to guild or group.

Kunark Infobits
Gordon Wrinn responded to several posts on the Kunark forum over the last 24 hours. Here's what's been said:
    Will Kunark Allow More Character Slots?
    No. Kunark is an expansion to the existing game. It contains landmass, content, and an additional race. The core game of EverQuest really isn't changing as a result of it exept to include quests related to Kunark and the boats to get there. Hence, the 8 character limit per server won't change as a result of the expansion.

    On Kunark Cover Art
    You can thank Keith Parkinson for the work. He's the same artist that did the original EverQuest box and poster art.

    The box cover really only represents about a third of the complete work. I've got a full-sized poster hanging up in my office. It's all good :)

    The Continent of Kunark
    Kunark itself is a huge continent. It has more zones than Faydwer with landmass rivaling Antonica. There are a good number of dungeons and areas targeting level 50+ players.

    Since EQ was live while Kunark was in development, we've been able to implement the zones with a better idea of how people will play the game. I think that everyone will be very pleased.

    Level Limits on Items
    We're really against the idea of hard level limits on items (e.g. You have to be level X to put on item X). It's a really artifical way to control twinking. We prefer soft level limits, where a person cannot obtain the full benefits of a piece of equipment until a certain level. As an example, a level 5 person in full plate really isn't much more effective than a level 5 person in patchwork. It's not like they can take on a level 10 critter and expect to live. Sure, you have to deal with "looking" at the person in this armor, but it really isn't affecting game balance.

    Cleric Spells
    They expand easier than you think. Resurrection for example can get better. I've seen GZ's spell list and it looks nice. The casters in general will see a lot of new things as well as some familiar themes carried on.

    Spells for Level 50+ Characters
    Though we'll keep to our philosophy of not releasing information about things that people should be allowed to discover on their own, I'll say that new spells make sense, since the current spells were meant to be effective versus lower level NPCs.

Gordon Infobits, Part II
Since our first Infobit news story today, Gordon Wrinn has responded to a few more important posts on the Gameplay forum. Here's the skinny:
    Server Transfers
    The only character-moves that we have planned currently are for the Fennin Ro split-server, to go up hopefully around the end of next week. Aside from current players who volunteer on Fennin, no other characters will be moved. After we see how the Fennin split goes, we may consider bring up additional split-servers for some of the other more crowded servers, but it's too late to talk about that.

    In any case, our current plans do not include making special arrangements for entire guilds. It would require far too much hands-on effort and be too prone to mistakes to consider it.

    Looting Bug
    This problem is in our bug-list and on the programming list for repair. Unfortunately I don't have an ETA for you.

    Zone Issues
    Odus is getting attention. Paineel will be up soon, with the Hole to follow after that. We're also working on another zone for Odus that I can't comment on at this time. We're also going to be revamping Kerra Ridge to try and get some people in there.

    Odus is, of course, meant to be less populated than the main land. Erud lead his people there to escape the lesser races of the mainland and give his people an opportunity to study magic and pursue enlightenment.

    Catacombs Status
    All the entrances to the Qeynos Catacombs have been fixed on test and are planned for implementation in the next patch.

    Fraud in Trade Skills?
    It's already been discussed. We want to get away from the idea of implementing so many "Is he doing his job" messages. Currently on the test server, we've added messages to the group for looting. We've further been asked to add messages for taunting, the evade skill, and pickpocket.

    At some point we have to suggest that people learn who they can trust. Many craftsman on the servers have established a well-earned reputation for honesty, and earn a good deal of business because of it. Would it be fair to them to eliminate the "trust" factor and have it so anyone could deal with any craftsman and be sure they won't get cheated? Even if we did include the message, what would stop someone from just taking the item and telling you, "tough luck"? I imagine the next step after that would be a call for us to develop some kind of secure-crafting mechanism in the game.

    We want to let people earn their reputations.

Gordon Infobits
Internet Relations Manager Gordon Wrinn adressed several topics on the forum today on the Gameplay forum. Here's the scoop:
    Cleric Quest Armor
    There isn't a lack of concern, it's just that getting the armor available has a higher priority right now than going through and taking another look at the AC, Weight, and Stats, the first two of which are probably more in need of adjustment than the latter. I commented in another thread that we were ok with the idea of a cleric choosing between being a "Battle Cleric" or a "Wisdom Cleric" and that remains true. However, those who choose the "Battle Cleric" (and by that I mean AC over Wisdom) should receive a definite AC advantage.

    On Bristlebane Problems
    Actually, that's taking place tomorrow.

    For those just tuning in, Bristlebane has had some really strange problems for the past 3 weeks. Among these are strange lag-spikes and some slow spawn-times in a couple of zones.

    When we first started looking into this problem, we found some bad data in a couple of areas that we felt was contributing to the spawn-time issue. This data was replaced with correct data taken off another server, but that didn't solve the problem. We've also double-checked all of the data on the server and made many adjustments to try and track down what is causing the problem. Due to these attempts to fix the slow spawn-time and strange-lag problem, the server has been taken down several times since the problem was verified.

    Tomorrow, we're going to replace some major hardware components, which will hopefully fix the problems and have things back to normal. We apologize to everyone who has had to put up with this, you have every right to be upset.

    Rain Spells
     The rain spells aren't bugged. There is a maximum number of targets that all AoE spells can affect, as well as a maximum amount of damage that they can do. This was put in back in beta when people were training a ton of creatures and rain made them as easy to kill as any one creature.

    There are 3 different types of AoE spells in game. Their types are listed below in order of how many creatures they can affect and how much damage they do:

    1. Rain
    2. Radiate out from Caster
    3. Radiate out from Target

Customer Service Policy
Gordon Wrinn responded to a post on the subject of Customer Service over on the Gameplay forum. Here's excerpts from the original post accompanied with all of Wrinn's comments:
    Really i was just wondering if we (the customers) could get a look at the Customer service policy.

    We're currently in the process of revamping our Customer Service Policies. After we get them ironed out and in print, we'll release an updated GM/Guide FAQ here on the web page.

    1) Verant doesn't have a policy in place. If you go from server to server, sometimes from one guide/GM to another on the SAME server, they will give you vastly different interpretations of the same "rule." This means that they don't have a strict set of rules in place

    We do have policies in place, but there must be an avenue for resolution in the event that the written policies do not apply. Senior Guides and GMs have the authority to implement ad hoc server policies for issues that aren't covered by the policy manual. The unfortunate side-effect of this is that it causes "Server Drift", where rules and policies begin to diverge between the servers.

    The Customer Service Policies must undergo a periodic rewrite in order to implement those ad hoc policies across all of the servers, and decide which one is best in the case where they contradict each other.

    2) If they let you SEE the rules up front, they wouldn't be able to change them and tell you "it's always been the rule." Also, if you know the rules, you can look for loopholes and ways to exploit their own rules. Verant would rather keep the rules vague and nebulous than risk someone making a mockery (imagine that...) of them.

    There's no malice or bending of reality that is going on here, it's just a process of identifying specific actions as part of an inspecific infraction. As an example of this: Harassing other players is illegal. But what specific actions are considered Harassment? As people come up with new ways to harass each other, those specific actions are identified and fall under harassment.

    This isn't a simple process. Take the system of laws in the U.S. as an example. Great care has been taken to identify specific infractions over the 200+ year history of our government, yet there are still actions which are covered under a blanket infraction. Take for instance the charge of "Disorderly Conduct". They (the lawmakers) don't necessarily define every possible action a person can do as part of the law.

    -Gordon

EverQuest Issues
Gordon Wrinn made a large post on the Gameplay forum tonight briefing us on the current status surrounding many of the hot issues that EverQuest is currently embroiled with. Here's the post:
Hello all,

Every Monday here at Verant we hold our weekly meetings to go over the current outstanding projects and make plans for the week going forward. At this week's meeting, we discussed a number of issues that I'd like to bring to your attention and open for debate.

Skin Like Nature:

Last week a change to the Skin Like Nature spell was implemented. The change replaced the "HP Regen" component of the spell with a "Damage Shield" component.

We also introduced a bug that caused the damage shield to stack improperly. The Damage Shield values are being subtracted instead of being added. The Damage shield bug will be fixed in the next patch for all stackable damage shields that had this problem (there were a few). However, we'd like to open the damage shield's replacement of the regen component for debate.

The regen component on this spell was replaced with the Shield because it allowed the Enchanter-Gasping and Necro-Heart series to overwrite SLN. Unfortunately, directly fixing this issue is not possible, as it would require a rewrite of the spell-interaction/stacking code. This would undoubtedly introduce other problems that we don't want to risk at this stage. By replacing the regen component with the damage shield, SLN is no longer overwritten by those other spells.

As it seems that many people were upset by the change, and since the change was not made for balance reasons, wed like to get general consensus on what should be done with the spell. The options available are 1) Leave it as it is now (Damage Shield), or 2) Return it to the way it was (HP Regen).

The Mistwalker:

We are currently reevaluating the decision to reduce the proc percentage for this weapon. Were looking at the number of Mistwalkers on each server, and the possible effect should we return it to the way it was. We are also looking at other ways to avoid the exploit that was possible before, that would allow us to return the weapon to the way it was. Much thanks to everyone who sent in suggestions that would make this possible. I cant comment further on it at this time, but I just wanted to let those concerned know that the issue was still open.

Tallon Zek & PvP Looting:

There is unfortunately no reasonable way for us to eliminate the equipment dependence disparity between melee and caster classes. This equipment dependence disparity creates a loot disparity on the two servers. Casters can bag all of their equipment and still be 90% effective in PvP combat. Melee, on the other hand, cannot. During the next patch, tentatively scheduled for 2/16/00, we will be changing the looting rules for the Tallon Zek server from Coin + 1 item to Coin Only. This change is being made as a Test to determine what looting rules are actually preferred by the majority of people who play on PvP. Well be examining server load over the next few weeks to determine the changes impact on population. Vallon Zek (the other PvP Teams Server) will remain Coin + 1 item.

We expect that this change will lead to a new influx of people playing melee characters on Tallon Zek. Please keep in mind that in the event that the change shows itself to be detrimental to server population or morale, the server may be changed back to Coin + 1 item loot.

Alchemy and Poison:

Alchemy and Poison are in the process of being revamped on the Test server. Changes are scheduled to go live on 2/16/00, but dont quote me on that just yet :)

Rogue Enhancements:

Some interesting rogue enhancements are being developed on the Test server with plans of going live on 2/16/00.

The chance of scoring a critical hit with a throwing weapon is being increased dramatically. In addition, a thrown weapon that hits the target from behind will have a chance of scoring a Deadly Strike. Additional throwing weapons will be released at a later date to make throwing even more viable.

In addition to the throwing changes, a new ability (much more exciting than throwing) is being granted to the rogue class. The ability, called Evade, is tied to the Hide skill. It is used by targeting a creature and pressing the Hide button. If it is successful, it will dramatically reduce how much the targeted creature hates the rogue. The Rogue will be given a message indicating success or failure.

This new ability is, for all intents and purposes, a reverse taunt, and will help the grouped rogue shift the targeted creatures focus back to the tank after landing all of those hits. This is not like feign death, which makes the creature forget you entirely, but rather just makes it hate you less. In addition, it only works against the targeted creature.

The skill is partially implemented on the Test server. Currently, getting hit by a creature makes the rogues Hide button gray out. Once this is changed so that it doesnt gray out upon getting hit, it will work as intended.

NPC Warping:

We are dedicating a good number of man hours this week to tracking down and eliminating the bug that causes a fleeing creature to teleport away.

-Gordon
