  Update Notes for GU 46  [ 2 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Game Updates June 17,2008
HIGHLIGHTS

During "Gathering Tempest" (GU46): The ranks of Freeport’s Academy of Arcane Science and Qeynos’ Concordium have been stretched thin as they attempt to investigate the mysterious and dark storms that have erupted across Norrath. Those wishing to volunteer their services for the investigation are encouraged to speak with the Concordium Pupil Adepts or Academy Apprentices that have been sent all across the world.

There is a great commotion stirring around Bog Mountain, goblins from all nations appear to be drawn to the halls of Runnyeye Citadel! High level groups can now investigate, should they choose, the new dealings with the goblins in Runnyeye: The Gathering!

Poisons now have the option to auto consume when they run out. Check out the "POISONS" section for details.

Carpenters, inspired by some of the elaborate cloaks adventurers have discovered, have started learning to create similar tapestries for the home. They have also acquired a few new recipes inspired by Kelethin and Maj'dul craftsmanship.

  Rytan on LL Raid Difficulty  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
Should the Living Legend promotional raids be geared for any level of guild or players? The community can't agree whether the entire playerbase should be able to participate in these live raids ("Marathon" just ended, with few guilds being able to get much past level 4, and some casual guilds not even making past level 1).

The smaller, family guilds are unhappy they can't participate; the hard core raiders argue that there's less content for high end players, and not everyone can participate in every single piece of content in the game. The hard core raiders are branded as "elistist" while the smaller guilds are accused of being unreasonable in their demands. So what does the SOE team have to say?

Rytan, Lead Designer, posted in response:

"It's important to note that there's two zones being redone that will go out towards the end of the promotion that are group content, and there's always the hotzone bonus exp, and the new armor dropping. We've tried to appeal to as many different playstyles as possible with this promotion, but not every piece of content is going to appeal to everyone."

I interpret this as agreeing that not everyone can handle the live raids, that's intended, but there will be special content for those that cannot so everyone gets to do something.
