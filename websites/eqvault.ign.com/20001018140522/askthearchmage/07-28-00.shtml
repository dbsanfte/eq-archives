Ask the Arch Mage: 07-28-00

Author: Geoffrey Zatkin   Organization: Verant Interactive, Inc.

Quick Bio:

Geoffrey Zatkin aka “Geoffrey Z” aka “GZ”
Game Designer
3 years at Verant
B.S. in Psychology (not computer science)
Works with the other game designers at Verant Interactive (so can ask questions around)
Specialty of the EQ Magic System

To submit a question email it to archmage@vaultnetwork.com. When submitting a question please keep the following in mind:

  • No “Why is X class better then my class” questions.
  • No “Why can’t I have a tank-mage” questions.
  • Look around and make sure that you haven’t seen any answer to your question.
  • The more off-beat the question, the more likely he will answer it.

Tricky Names

Hey GZ - I've been looking through some of the spell listings and town names in EQ and have noticed some pretty interesting things. We all know that Qeynos is SonyEQ when reversed. From spell listings I've concluded that you MUST play an Enchanter. ;) Both of the high level animations are re-arrangements of your name: Yeogreff's Animation and Kintaz's Animation. Do you indeed play an Enchanter ingame?

GZ ANSWERS

Oh no - found out at last. Yes, I do play an Enchanter. Actually, I have fairly high level versions of all of the robed and priest classes, and a pretty suave Bard as well.

When naming the spells, I had a lot of fun - and some good help/suggestions from a few members of the EQ team. One of our Game Designers, Brian Canary, helped out a lot with the initial names for the pre-alpha spell list - back when we amazed everybody on the team by having more then 400 different spells. “Pogonip” is my favorite spell that Brian named.

I have always enjoyed anagrams - and I have a fun time slipping them into the EQ world upon occasion. Looking through the names of characters in our world, spell names, monster names, etc. is likely to yield a fair number of anagrams, puns, and other light bits of playful fun. EverQuest is, after all, a game, and games are meant to provide multifaceted entertainment value. Playing with words is, for me, an attempt to make EverQuest even more fun.

This being said, another reason for playing with the names of NPC’s and spells is that, after adding more then 1300 spells and naming thousands of NPC’s, I began to run dry on naming inspiration. There are, after all, only so many different ways of saying the word “fire.”

Words of Wisdom

Does wisdom have any influence on resisting mental spells such as charm and fear, or is it solely dependent on magic resistance?

GZ ANSWERS

Unlike AD&D, in EverQuest a character's wisdom has absolutely no impact on her saving throw verses mind effecting spells such as Charm and Fear. These saves are dependent upon the target’s magic resistance and the difference in levels between the caster and the target.

This being said, the “wise” player will have cast spells or will be wearing items that raise her magic resistance when she is planning to fight creatures that cast Charm and Fear style spells.

As a side not, having a high magic resistance not only makes it harder to be fear/charmed, but it also means that the duration of such spells will, on average, be shorter. Magic resistance helps your periodic save to break charm style spells as well as helping your initial save.

Singing Blues

If you have more than one bard in the group (which I have had a bunch) can both bards uses Chords of Discord and damage the same mobs? Or are these songs limited like regular spells appear to be? [I'm pretty sure Hymn of Restoration does not heal faster, but that too is a question; any chance for a chorus to be more effective than a soloist?]

GZ ANSWERS

Most Bard songs do not stack on top of each other - once you have a song being sung to you, having more than one person singing the song does not give any further effect. With the way EQ Bardic magic was built, Bards are more soloists then chorus members. Any song with a duration (that leaves an icon) will not stack - so no, having two Bards using the Hymn of Restoration will not make you heal faster. Having two bards using Chords could be more effective then a single Bard singing it - monsters do occasionally save verses the song. It would probably be better for the multiple Bard party, however, to sing different songs.

This being said, the following is a list of Bard songs that do not have a duration (they leave no icon), and can therefore be played by any number of Bards at full effect.

  • Alenia`s Disenchanting Melody
  • Brusco`s Boastful Bellow
  • Brusco`s Bombastic Bellow
  • Cassindra`s Chorus of Clarity
  • Denon`s Desperate Dirge
  • Denon`s Dissension
  • Lyssa`s Cataloging Libretto
  • Lyssa`s Locating Lyric
  • Melanie`s Mellifluous Motion
  • Song of Dawn
  • Song of Highsun
  • Syvelian`s Anti-Magic Aria

Wind and Brass

Bards get the chance to learn the Wind and Brass skills before they actually get a song that uses them; is there a way to practice these skills before the songs that use them are gained?

GZ ANSWERS

Other then from your Guild Master, nope. You get the skills a bit early in preparation for learning the songs.

Warriors and Weapons with Healing Procs

Has there ever (or will there ever be) a weapon which can be used by warriors that has a healing "proc" (effect)?

GZ ANSWERS

I can think of at least one item off the top of my head (a certain whip) which Warriors can use that has a life drain proc associated with it. This being said, we will never put a huge healing effect on a melee class weapon for the same reason that we would never make a spell that turned the caster into an excellent melee character, or a wand that allowed a melee types to cast Ice Comet - class distinction. Each class has certain areas in which they are very powerful and certain areas in which they are weak. Our class based system is part of what makes partying so powerful in EQ - other members of the party have strengths that your character does not, and your character has strengths that shore up other classes weaknesses.

Mages and Imaginary Spells

Will mages ever get the 49th level summoning that we were told we'd get? My suggestion would be [insert spell idea here].

GZ ANSWERS

I love these ones - for the record, Mages were never told that they would get any additional item summoning spells. Or if so, I sure didn’t say it. When we launched EQ, there was a summoning spell for about ½ of the Mage’s spell circles. As the game developed, I continued to add additional item summoning spells every other month or so. By logical progression, there will probably be a 49th level item summon added at some point to the game. I have received lots of excellent suggestions - at this point it is deciding what the Mage class needs that will not change the functionality of being a Mage or overpower the Mages with a radical new ability.

Research in Kunark

Why was the research completely ignored for the expansion? As a mage, our research is frustrating as is and when I understand it will not be needed for 50+ spells, I was disappointed that all my "hard work" would not be rewarded. Personally I was hoping that the 50+ spells would be a Research and Quest combo where we'd search for lost tomes and artifacts that would combine into our expansion spells.

GZ ANSWERS

This just goes to show that you can’t please everybody, no matter what you do. When I asked around to different players about parts of the magic system that they liked/disliked/didn’t ever want to see again, most people were not very fond of the research system. Because of the player feedback, I purposefully left research out of the expansion pack. Most people in beta and that I polled were very happy to have research left out of the expansion.

Then we got the opposite side of the coin, as expressed in the question above - people who, while stating that research was “frustrating” (one of the main reasons that I removed it), still wanted to see it in the game. I don’t know what to say on this other then that I was trying to make the most people happy that I could by removing a frustrating aspect of the EQ game experience.
