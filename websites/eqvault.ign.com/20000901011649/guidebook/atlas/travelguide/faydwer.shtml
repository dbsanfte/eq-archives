Travel Guide: Kelethin - Felwithe - Kaladim

Author: Faenor (N/A)   Organization: N/A

For those who live in one of these cities and want to do some great travelling and exploring at relatively low levels (4 and up) I suggest a trip between the cities of Kelethin, Felwithe and Kaladim. Kelethin and Felwithe are both in Greater Faydark, Kaladim lies in the fertile hunting grounds of the Butcherblock Mountains.

If you are a newbie in Kelethin and have reached level 2 or 3 you can safely travel to Felwithe. Make sure you travel when it is light, as soon as you leave Kelethin the forest gets very dark and getting lost usually results in nasty encounters with Orcs and such. Type /time to find out what time it is, I don’t suggest travelling between 6pm and 5am. Start out at the main lift, the one with the torch and the Priest of Discord next to it. A path leads up a hill from this lift, follow the path until you see the second lift, just past this lift another path starts, this path will lead all the way past Felwithe, past the Wizard Spires and to the entrance to Butcherblock Mountains. It is quite a long walk so do not get discouraged when all you see is an endless expanse of forest. Just make sure you keep following the path! When you see an interesting creature like a wasp or bat to kill (assuming you are low level) think twice before you run off to slay it. You might get disoriented in the heat of battle and it is so easy to loose your bearings amidst the giant trees of Greater Faydark. Trust me, getting lost usually leads to death and an impossible to find body.

After a while, the path will come to a branch leading to the left, ignore this one, it’s a dead end. Shortly after the first branch you will get to a second branch to the left. It should be a bit more crowded here since you are really close to Felwithe now. The music will change and if you go left here you will very soon see the magnificent city of Felwithe. The area right in front of the gates is usually populated by bats and wasps and the city guards offer good backup in case you get in trouble. Felwithe itself is also worth a visit, it is not as dizzying as Kelethin in the trees but it is certainly interesting for shopping and sightseeing.

Before you head off to the wondrous Butcherblock Mountains, make sure you realise that when you die there, you will re-spawn in your home city so if you are an elf this means Kelethin or Felwithe. Walking back to your body without any weapons or food can be very time-consuming and nerve-wracking. Make sure you are at least level 5 and have some backup money and perhaps equipment in the bank. Also, finding some friends to travel with always makes it safer. Instead of taking the left branch into Felwithe, keep going straight until you reach another branch to the left. This path it will lead to the entrance of Lesser Faydark though which you can reach the Steamfont Mountains and the Gnome city of Ak’Anon. Unless you feel adventurous (the path to Steamfont is reasonably safe area but Lesser Faydark has some fairly high level creatures roaming around it) keep going straight to the Wizard Spires. These mysterious monoliths are rumoured to be used by higher level wizards for magical purposes. Keep following the path and keep an eye open for any stray Orcs that might wander around here. At low levels you don’t want to encounter any Orc Centurions. I did once find an Orc Hatchetman here, I quickly slew it and pried an Orc Hatchet from it’s dead claws. These hatchets are part of a Kelethin Ranger quest.

There will be one branch to the right before the path ends, ignore this since it is a dead end and Orc Centurions tend to be around there. Go straight for a little longer until the path ends and you should be at the entrance to Butcherblock. This is a great area, when I reached level 5 I spent many hours amidst the crowds at the Orc camp near Kelethin, getting killed numerous times by gangs of Orc Centurions and spending lots of time in anguish looking for my body. After a while I decided I had enough and headed for Butcherblock. This place is much brighter and open than Faydark and it much easier to navigate. Also, there are lots of lower level monsters like Skunks, Spiders, Snakes and lower level Goblins like the goblin whelps.

The whole way from the Butcherblock-Faydark gate to the gates of Kaladim is relatively safe. There are two distinct dangers to look out for though. First off, there is Corflunk the Ogre who prowls the area close to the Faydark entrance. He will attack anybody and is fairly tough (level 8 or so). I was once ambushed by him and an Goblin Shaman, needless to say I died a swift death. There is also another Ogre, a female Shaman named Zarchoomi who is tougher than Corflunk and has some nasty spells in her repertoire. There are also several Goblin camps with Goblins ranging from levels 5 and up. Some of them roam around and they will attack you without provocation.

A path leads away from the Faydark gate, follow this past two small houses where you can find a merchant. It continues to an area called The Crossroads. This is a popular spot to hang out and hunt monsters from since there are two powerful Dwarf guards and the Dwarf hero Nyzil Bloodforge who kill anything hostile that comes near them. So if you find yourself being chased by two rabid Goblins, run back to the guards and you will usually be fine. At the crossroads, the path continues past a Goblin camp (sometimes dangerous Goblin wizards and shamans appear there) and ends at the entrance to Dagnor’s Cauldron. Stay away from there until you are much more experienced. At the crossroads turn right instead and soon you’ll come across another house with a guard in front of it. Just left of this house is a Goblin Warrior camp and past this a Dwarf Bandit camp. Keep following the path though, it is quite a long way still and passes several guard houses until it slowly turns left, past a few merchants and finally to the towering gates of Kaladim, the Dwarf city. Here you can marvel at the city built in massive caves inside the mountain and visit the palace of the Dwarf King. Very impressive. The area around the Kaladim gates is populated by low-level creatures and the guards are always close by in case you’re in trouble. The path continues past another guard house to the docks where the mighty ships that travel to the city of Freeport can be boarded. These docks are also a favourite fishing spot, the sunsets are magnificent.

If you are a Faydark elf and feel to urge to explore, Butcherblock is a great place to roam. It offers plenty of hunting for levels 3-10 (and some special areas like the Chessboard for higher level players) and the presence of guards in a couple of places makes it safer than the deep forest of Faydark. If you are a Dwarf from Kaladim, visiting the High Elf city of Felwithe is an experience and navigating the tree platforms of Kelethin a thrill. In Faydark you can find many Rangers to help you find creatures with their Tracking skill. I have travelled with many Dwarfs around Butcherblock and always had a great time hunting with them. Beware though, Faydark is a very dark place and it is easy to get lost. Also, I have helped many a lost Dwarf find their way around the myriad of walkways and platforms that form the fair city of Kelethin.

For shorter travel times in Greater Faydark, those who feel comfortable leaving the path and cutting straight through the forest (really the toughest aggressive creatures you might encounter are Orc centurions who pose no threat to those who are level 8 and above) I will give you the directions from the various zone gates to Kelethin which is in the centre of Greater Faydark. From Butcherblock head northeast, from Lesser Faydark head north-Northwest and from Felwithe head west. Especially if you arrive from Butcherblock, you can shave a considerable amount of time off your trip since the path circles halfway around the zone before ending at Kelethin. Take care however, it is easy to get lost in the expanse of the forest. If your sense heading isn’t up to par you might want to stick to the path.

My final words: don’t be afraid to travel! I have spoken to many newbies who were reluctant to travel because of the unknown dangers that lie ahead. Once you are familiar with the game and have gained a level or two you can easily travel from Kelethin to Felwithe. And if you feel confident or have found some companions, the trip to Kaladim can be exciting and full of wonder. If you stick to the path and remember where the closest guards are, you should be allright.

If you find yourself around these areas and are in need of help or a travel / hunting companion, look for me on the Solusek Ro server.
