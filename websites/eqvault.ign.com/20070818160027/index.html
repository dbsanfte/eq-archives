Test Dowload Creates Human Models in Error  [ Comment ]
Print News
Rashere has posted a response on the EQPlayers forums concerning complaints that the incorrect (and mostly disliked) new human models went live for some people:

"The human models aren't ready and aren't live yet. What you're seeing are models that you downloaded when you logged into test from your live EQ directory at some point in the past. That downloaded the new human file into your folder. We haven't updated the global memory in a while, so the new human wasn't in there until today which is why its just now showing up.

If you have a clean install (ie. you haven't patched test on top of live), you won't have this problem. For anyone else, we're putting up a new version of the global file that will make that model not load on live servers so you won't see it. It should be up on the patcher in a few minutes.

Alternately, if you don't want to wait for the patcher, just delete the following files from your EQ directory and then relaunch the game:

hnm.eqg

hnf.eqg

Rashere


Message edited by Rashere on 08/14/2007 16:56:02."

Update: Ozyen-Stromm also posted that you may want to try "Deleting hum.eqg and hum_anims.eqg."


-- Kwill @ 5:13PM Tue 14 Aug 2007

TCG battles will be a great way to pass the time while playing if you are waiting for friends or that elusive NPC.

Once the game is open to everyone, booster packs will only be available via NPC drops and through the station store. I would encourage everyone to go visit http://www.station.sony.com/ and follow the Legends of Norrath link to find out more information including links to the exclusive loot cards found in both EQ and EQ2."

Interview with SOE Studio Head Beliaeff  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Columns
Gamasutra has published an interview with Sony Online San Diego studio head Nicholas Beliaeff, in which he talks about SOE's commitment to EverQuest as their oldest title, among other topics.

Beliaeff points out that they have no interest in moving EverQuest players to EverQuest II, they are committed to the game, and that "It's a tremendously healthy game. We're coming out with our next expansion in November. We have our next [just-held] fan fair in August in Las Vegas, to meet with the people who play that game. It's going strong, and showing no signs of going away."

Also of note is the idea Beliaeff expressed that if a player base grows too small for a game title, as happened with Cosmic Rift, Infantry, and Tanarus, they simply make them free. It seems that no matter what happens to the EQ playerbase, the game will never actually be shut down, at least in today's management climate. Good news!

More on Legends of Norrath Tradeable Card Game  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live

John Smedly, President of SOE, posted these clarifying comments concerning Legends of Norrath on the EverQuestII boards:

"I wanted to get a few comments out regarding LoN.

1) This is a first and foremost a card game - and you don't have to spend a dime if you don't want to. We're giving away free starter packs to all EQ and EQ II subscribers

2) If you don't want to spend money on cards - you can get booster packs as drops in-game just by killing stuff. This means that you can also get the same in-game loot items without spending money.. including the rare stuff.

3) The game is intended to bring a different kind of gameplay into both EQ and EQ II - we want to offer greater variety of gameplay in addition to the normal expansion packs. Are we going to make money from it? Yes.

4) You will NOT be able to trade the in-game items. We are spending a lot of time and effort fighting plat-sellers and we didn't want to put in a mechanic that attracts more of them. THIS INCLUDES THE BAZZAR SERVERS ON EQ II (i.e. too bad.. no selling these items period).

5) This is not some slippery slope towards selling items directly in EQ & EQ II. We had a cool idea for a card game set within the lore common to both games. Yes, we chose to include in-game loot as part of the fun rewards you could get when you open booster packs. However, as I said... you don't have to spend a dime to get exactly the same things. I should also point out that these items are similar to Veteran rewards or pre-order items in that they tend to be more fun.. not some big powerful in-game item."

You can see the entire presentation of the new product and more in the Community Address by Smed at Fan Faire on YouTube.
