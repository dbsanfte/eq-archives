  Purple Shinies and You  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
For those who don't know, the World Event Sinking Sands quest reward excavation hat (10 D.I.R.T.Y. coins at Jimbo the vendor) does more than just to look good -- it shows you purple shinies at the excavation site when equipped in the regular helmet slot.

You will only find half (six) of the purple shinies, however. This has caused some annoyance in the community, but it wasn't done deliberately to frustrate people; it was designed to be part of the idea that as the excavations continued, more exciting trinkets would appear, as developer Gninja explains in his comments below:

"Unfortunately its a bit too late to change how the collection works but to clear things up.

In part 1 of the event the first 6 spawn.

In part 2 all 12 spawn.

Now the reasoning behind this was basically that the more rare trinkets were a bit deeper in the sand that was dug up. Sorry you all didn't care for it done that way but as I said before it's a bit late to change it now. I will definately take it into consideration for the future though."

The other half will appear in the next couple of weeks after the next update. Don't worry about missing out on getting them all done before that, though. Gninja says:

"I have taken everything said into consideration and after the event is over the purple shinies will continue to spawn for those who have gotten the Excavation helmet. So no rush in thinking you MUST get it done before the event is over."

So you can collect the first six now, and wait for the other six, or just collect them all after the next update. Thanks for listening, Gninja!

Pros and Cons of the New Exp Bonuses  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News

Checking the official boards lately, there seem to be innumerable threads on the pros and cons of the new enhanced experience gain for levels 20-70.

If it's not complaining, it's people coming up with various solutions on how to get around this change.

Some people are convinced the exp bonus is causing people to level up before their skill ups are complete for each levels.

Dev Illucide posted a gentle correction on the above thread to some of the misinformation flying around:

"We've talked about re-working the way skills are earned so that they're on an inverse exponential curve (the further away from your "current" level, the faster they raise), but we haven't done it yet. I'm not entirely adverse to doing it, it just requires more time, and a little more thought as to whether that's the route we'd like to travel.

However, I would appreciate it if folks wouldn't throw misinformation into this kind of topic.

There is no way that a character can, during a standard level, not increase their core abilities to cap, provided they are actually using those abilities. Yes, if you turn in a few dozen collection quests and go from 35 to 40 doing so, then you're giong to be behind the curve, and it'll be rough for a few critters to catch up. But that's about it. There are some points to be made for mages who don't normally get hit very often, and their defense/focus not raising as quickly as they might like, but it's not actually really all that difficult to raise those (trust me, I've leveled a few) either. =)

In addition - none of the levels from 1-60 even had achievements in mind when the creatures were balanced for those level ranges. So in that respect, all content sub-60 is already skewed towards current player's favor (and by quite a bit). So it would be pretty nice if folks took that into account when they bring up being "behind" now when they're levelling.

Also would be nice if everyone remembered that we put in the increase to achievement gained while mentoring too, but I'm not going to ask for too much all at once or anything!"

As with any game-changing decision, there are growing pains. For those who like the journey through the content, it does appear to take some creative thinking to level slowly now, but it can be done (turn off experience, for instance). For the majority of players, it's now less tedious to get through the lower levels that many people have already experienced a few times already.

I seem to recall that turning in collections, discovery experience, and killing named mobs were all methods to level faster and gain AA. Now that those strategies aren't as important, due to the recent changes, the game can turn back to combat as the prime way to level a character -- which will give needed skill-ups.

New Zones in SoD  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Here is a preview of some of the new zones coming your way with the release of Seeds of Destruction:

The Void
Because of Discord's interference in the timelines of Norrath the world has been destroyed completely. The only objects left are the drifting asteroid shards called The Void. Sparking time portals in The Void drift in the ether and lead to the various rifts in time: the Plane of Time, Bloody Kithicor, the Field of Scale, Oceangreen Hills, the Bloodfields, the Invasion of the Plane of Earth, and realm of Discord. The only living creature in The Void is the god Zebuxoruk; torn from the flow of time by the combined might of the elder gods of Norrath.

Oceangreen Hills
Oceangreen was made up primarily of lush green grassland and gently rolling hills. To the north, the Jaggedpine forest hampered travel towards the barbarian Northlands. To the east, a set of very high and steep hills cut off all access except for a tunnel that led to Blackburrow, home of the gnolls, and a narrow valley that led off to the great plains which are now known as the Plains of Karana. On the western borders of Oceangreen, several sets of steep hills existed in the northern region and an ocean bay in the southwest. The region was also the home to several different clans of Gnolls. However, only the largest of these clans, the Sabertooth clan, was violent towards human settlers.

Oceangreen Village
The village is currently under attack by a force of Bertoxxulous worshippers and hordes of undead that spread disease and decay, infecting all life in the area. Luckily for the villagers a force of heroes has risen up to help defend the village. These heroes are lead by a charismatic man named Antonius Bayle.

Temple of Bertoxxulous
The worshippers of Bertoxxulous have lived here secretly for decades, but have only recently become emboldened enough to spread their influence to the surface. Their once troublesome interferences have festered into a full blown plague that now threatens all of Tunaria. They must be stopped if the future of humanity is to remain intact.

GU 49 "Raising the Banner" is Live  [ 6 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Official News
SPECIAL EVENTS
During "Raising the Banner," a team of dedicated excavators and researchers have begun to uncover a remarkable find in the dunes of Sinking Sands. They have numerous tasks for any recruits willing to help defend the site, excavate relics, maintain their assisting clockworks and provide crafted supplies for the team. They cannot fathom how important this project is and how it is fated to reshape the world of Norrath for years to come!

GUILD HALLS
Construction of the Guild Halls has been completed. Guilds of qualifying levels can now purchase halls in a variety of locations. Smaller halls can be found in North Qeynos, South Freeport, Kelethin, Neriak, and Gorowyn. Medium halls are found in South Qeynos and North Freeport, and large halls are found off the shores of Antonica and Commonlands. Click on the corresponding doors, or mariner bells on the docks, to access these new structures.

With the work completed on the coastal fortresses in Antonica and Commonlands the involved parties have retired to their cities and the guards have been recalled to the barracks.
