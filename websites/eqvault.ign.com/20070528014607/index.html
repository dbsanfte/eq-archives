  Parkinson's Fantasy Art at the Art Institute of California  [ Comment ]
Print News
SOE is inviting all EverQuest fans to a gallery opening for the exhibition "Masters of Fantasy Art," featuring the paintings of Keith Parkinson, when it arrives in San Diego on Tuesday, June 5.

After five months of gallery shows across the country, The Masters of Fantasy Art - A Tribute to Keith Parkinson, is heading to the Art Institute of California, San Diego.

The tour began in January, alongside the launch of Vanguard: Saga of Heroes, as a tribute to the late Keith Parkinson, Art Director for Vanguard. SOE partnered with the Art Institutes to bring the show to various schools that offer game design and game art programs. The tour has had successful shows at the Art Institutes in San Francisco, Las Vegas, Washington D.C. and New York where thousands of people, many of whom are avid gamers, have experienced Keith's art up close and personal. In addition, the tour has received media coverage in all markets, including multiple live hits on a DC morning show.

SOE invites all players to come to a special opening: "In honor of the tour returning home for a two month show at the Art Institute of California, San Diego, we are hosting a gallery opening on the evening of June 5th and would love to have SOE players stop by to see the art hung in a gallery setting and show their support for both Keith and Vanguard."

Thursday, 24 May, 2007


  Dragon Politics  [ Comment ]
Print News
The anticipated return of the dragon Kerafyrm, who wants to destroy the world, has got the lore masters in the EQ community talking. What will his role be in the world order? Is he the avatar of Veeshan, or just waiting for his moment to unite the dragons for world domination?

There are clues all over Norrath concerning the lore of the world -- in libraries, in talking to NPCs, and in signs and symbols in the artwork and architecture. The development team spends a significant amount of time fleshing out the world of Norrath and there is backstory everywhere for players to discover, and the return of Kerafyrm ties in many old threads of story. You certainly can play the game without knowing much about what's going on, but you might be surprised how much backstory and detail there is. Check it out sometime in the Library Forums discussions.

  Changes to AA System Coming to Test  [ Comment ]
Print News
Developer Nodyin posted on EQPlayers:

"In the next few days we will be putting a new version of the AA window on the Test Server for you guys to check out and play with. We have spent some time trying to streamline the look and functionality of the AA window to cut down on the number of confusing tabs in the window and to make it easier to find the information or AA you are looking for. Below is a summary of the changes:

Reduced the tabs to 3 - General, Archtype, Class.

Added new information to each AA including an Expansion and/or a Category for the AA (Such as Luclin or Veteran Reward).

The column headings can be clicked to sort the AAs in alphabetical order by that column. So you can click on the title, the cost, the expansion, etc to see the AAs sorted by that column.

Several filters exists including: Can Purchase (The AAs you qualify to purchase based on level and prerequisites), Expansion and Category.

We hope that this will make the AA system easier to use and it will definitely make it easier for us to upgrade and add AAs in the future."

He also added:

"There are a lot of behind-the-scenes changes that are happening to support the UI change that will make my life a lot easier when making AAs. I'll have more to say about this."

  To Fizzle or Not To Fizzle?  [ 2 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Features
Should caster fizzles be part of the EverQuest game? Would removing them be game breaking?

Maddoc mentioned in the developer chat on May 15 that fizzles will not be removed from the game, and after extensive testing he assures the player base that the fizzle rate is steady at around 2% (although his actual number reported of 2.7714% is more like 3% in my book).

Player Aanelar argues that fizzles are annoying and an archaic part of EverQuest, better consigned to "it seemed like a good idea at the time" dustbin. Other players don't agree, and see it as balancing -- as must the devs, since they claim it is working as intended.

Read the arguments in the link above and decide for yourself!

  SOE Purchases Sigil Games Online  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Features
In move that probably isn't that surprising, SOE has purchased Sigil Games Online, which produces Vanguard.

Previous to the sale, SOE hosted the game but did not own the key assets. This move will make the Sigil employees part of the SOE group, and according to the press release, Brad McQuaid will stay on as a consultant and "creative advisor."

The press release:

May 15, 2007 San Diego, CA – Sony Online Entertainment LLC (SOE), a global leader in the online games industry, today announced that it has completed a transaction to purchase key assets of Sigil Games Online, Inc. Through the deal, SOE now owns Vanguard: Saga of Heroes, Sigil’s tentpole property, a highly-successful massively multiplayer online game. The Sigil acquisition also builds on SOE’s successful strategy of cultivating game development studios around the world to further boost the company’s capabilities. The announcement was made by John Smedley, President, Sony Online Entertainment.

As part of the acquisition, a majority of the Sigil team will join SOE, integrating into SOE’s worldwide group of studios which includes offices in San Diego, Seattle, Austin, Denver, Los Angeles and Taiwan. The acquisition will strengthen SOE’s position as a leader in the online gaming space by expanding the company’s portfolio of gaming offerings.

“With an unparalleled team and exceptional product, Sigil has been a creative force in the online games space. We look forward to continuing the vision for Vanguard and to continue to deliver some groundbreaking game play,” said Smedley.

In May 2006, SOE acquired the title’s rights and the two companies agreed to co-publish Vanguard. The MMO launched in late January of this year and has sold nearly 200,000 retail and digital units worldwide. Sigil CEO and Vanguard creator, Brad McQuaid will become a consultant with SOE as Vanguard Creative Advisor.

Vanguard: Saga of Heroes takes place in the fantasy setting of Telon, a vast and ancient world of magic, intrigue and adventure where thousands of players from around the world have formed a steadfast community exploring the grandeur of a world crafted by the seminal fantasy artist Keith Parkinson. The game introduced many groundbreaking new features and an art direction second to none.

Do You Read the Quest Texts?  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
When you are playing, do you take the time to read all the text for the quests you are completing?

Some players are discussing whether they do or don't on EQPlayers. Developer Nodyin posted that he spends a lot of time on the lore and the text, so he wishes people would read it!

Some people commented they would like to read it, but are too busy avoiding aggro mobs to be able to take the time to do much more than type the trigger words. Others say when the entire raid is clicking, it's hard to take the time to read it.

The interesting thing about reading quest texts is that you learn a lot more about the lore of Norrath, which is actually fairly complex. Every quest has some kind of story that tries to immerse players in the world, from kill ten rats to epic quest arcs.

  SOE Previews New Station Launcher  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
SOE recently hosted a "Game Day" in New York City, where they unveiled not only the Secrets of Faydwer expansion for EverQuest, but a new Station Launcher feature.

The new paradigm for gaming is community building, and the previewed Station Launcher hopes to promote community across SOE games, as well as cut down logging in time by offering simultaneous patching for all its games.

Player Profiles for All Games
Using the the station access concept already in place ($29.95 for access to all SOE games), the new Station Launcher will streamline access to all SOE's games. All SOE games features will be run through the Station Launcher, and as part of that, a player can create a persistent profile that will show characters and names for each game that a person plays. As part of this feature, the Station Launcher will also show where your friends are logged in at any given moment, so you can keep in touch across games. Cross-game tells are also in the works.

Simultaneous Patching
The Station Launcher will also allow for simultaneous patching of SOE games. Instead of logging in and waiting for a game to patch, the new Launcher would have patched each game for you while you were away as the patches became available. No more waiting on long patches (or even short ones) when you log in.

A couple of nice features designed to encourage people to play other titles in the SOE library, without worrying about lengthy patches on log in, as well as the ability to sustain friendships that are often lost when players switch games.
