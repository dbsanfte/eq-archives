Tuesday, 3 Apr, 2007 - EQ Vault News


  Missing Character Issue Post Patch  [ Comment ]
Print News
It was like a nightmare .... finally the red sticks were gone, but my main character, Syncere, was not on my character list! Where was she? My character list was full, stating I had 8 out of 8 characters (didn't I used to have 10?!), but no main? How could this be? Did I delete her by accident? Impossible.

I immediately logged on an alt, starting complaining to my guild mates in Crimson Talon, and petitioned. There was nothing in the knowledge base discussing the problem, at all. What could have caused this? My guild assured me that SOE could get my cleric back, even if she had been erased (how could that have happened, though?)

So I started doing some digging. In EQ2, if your character disappears, it might be because you cancelled Station Access and lost your extra character slots. Since I should have had more character slots than I had today, maybe deleting a character to make room for another would work. Following this train of thought, I deleted a level 3 alt I once made, since she had no gear to speak of and was expendable. And there she was, my cleric, magically appearing at the bottom of my character list.

I logged her on to let my guild know the raid cleric was not gone forever, and almost immediately got a petition from the GM. The response time was under 30 minutes between the time I petitioned and got a personal response -- very nice. I was very gratified that the GM was so responsive to my issue. Turns out this was not just my problem, it was a lot of people's. Many (or all) of those people who had 10 character slots filled up lost two of them as a result of the patch to remove the stick figures.

Some April fools joke, eh?

To resolve this issue, petition, and if you must have your missing character(s) back immediately, you can delete one (or two). For those who cannot delete one or more of their 10 characters to get an important one back, they must wait to have their issue resolved through technical support. Of course, no one should ever have to delete a character as a result of a technical problem (in my case the deletion of a level 3 alt was not a big deal, but that's not the point).

Doing a little more research, I found a thread on the technical support board addressing this issue. It is most likely the result of the patch to remove the stick figures. If you had ten character slots on your EverQuest account, you are probably down to eight, and the ones at the bottom of the list will not appear. I am told that Technical Support is working on a fix for this issue.

I don't know a worse feeling than logging on and having a beloved character mysteriously gone. Luckily, Syncere was just hiding. I do hope that they post something about this in the knowledge base, and not just in the tech support forums.

It is unfortunate that a lighthearted patch for Bristlebane Day ended up being not so funny for a lot of people. Let's hope this gets resolved soon.

-- Kwill @ 10:12AM Tue 03 Apr 2007

New Technology Revolutionizes EQ! Special Update  [ Comment ]
Print News
Hope you enjoy the new and improved look of the EverQuest models! We here at EQVault have to applaud the team for their modern, minimalist vision, stripping the game of the old school conventions and ushering in a whole new world of stark character models, casting aside outdated ideas about forced raids and loot, and introducing the realism of locked doors with no keys. And with the spell "Charm Door" they finally gave enchanters something to do.

Here is the official announcment from the EQ Team:

We are pleased to announce that we have developed new technology that allows us to change the character models for all races at the same time. This includes the following:

New Graphics Engine: We completely overhauled the EverQuest graphics engine... yes again. All character models have been retextured using this new technology and the results are beyond anyone's wildest dreams.

Dynamice effects for animations: Each movement of the model now acts as a separate entity, allowing more life-like animations.

Enhanced weapon visage: While we didn't change the look of the weapons, they sure look better on the new character models.

  Alliance Raiders Guild on Luclin  [ Comment ]
Print News
The latest addition to the Luclin guild list is The Alliance Raiders, a guild that encourages family and social guilds to raid with them:

"We are a raiding guild, on the Luclin server, that hosts open raids several times a week. We are currently working on flagging and epics for our extended family.

Most raiding guilds refuse to work with family or social guilds. If they do raid together, the raiding guilds frequently demand the lion's share of the loot. Alliance Raiders has a different attitude. We believe that any person willing to work should have an opportunity to advance their characters, regardless of their affiliations.

As with most raiding guilds, we do have a DKP (Dragon Kill Points) system. However, our system is open to anyone who raids with us. Whether you are a guild member or not, the more you raid with us, the more loot you will receive."

Check their website for more information. Thanks to John P. for sending this in!

  EverQuest Trivia: Jenniy's Cuirass  [ Comment ]
Print News
A very astute player reminded Merloc, item designer, that the Fabled Jenniy's Cuirass does not have the same stats "STR 8, DEX 6, STA 7, CHA 5, WIS 30, INT 9," as did the original. Those particular stats matter because when put together, they spell out the phone number 867-5309, made famous by the Tommy TwoTone song, "867-5309/Jenny."

Merloc replied that he hadn't meant to change them.

In 1982, the "Jenny" song peaked on the Billbord charts at number 4, making Jenny's phone number forever a part of pop culture.

You can read about 867-5309 on Wikipedia, which includes a mention of the EverQuest item.

  Anniversary Edition Expansion Pack Released  [ 4 Comments ]
Print News
It has arrived ... 375 zones and 50,000 items! That's a lot!

EVERQUEST THE ANNIVERSARY EDITION ANNOUNCED BY SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT AS EVERQUEST CELEBRATES ITS 8TH BIRTHDAY

EverQuest The Anniversary Edition includes all expansions ever created for EverQuest making it the largest compilation of MMO expansions ever.

SAN DIEGO, CA March 16, 2007 Sony Online Entertainment LLC (SOE), a global leader in the online games industry, today announced EverQuest The Anniversary Edition, a new compilation collection for the Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game (MMORPG), EverQuest. EverQuest The Anniversary Edition will only be available at participating retail locations and is scheduled for release on April 23, 2007, for the suggested retail price of US$19.99.

After eight successful years of EverQuest, we wanted to celebrate with the release of a very special Anniversary Edition, which consists of all the expansions ever created for EverQuest, including the most recently released expansion: The Buried Sea, said Clint Worley, Producer for EverQuest. The Anniversary Edition is not only chock-full of content, but priced to allow players both old and new to join in the online experience known as EverQuest.

Coinciding with the anniversary celebration, for a month starting on March 16th, EverQuest will be treating players to new in-game content including dozens of Luclin Fabled NPCs, a new scavenger hunt for players of all levels, and the return of the Fabled fiends of years past.

EverQuest continues to provide players with a massive amount of content, more than any other MMO in the market, and is still continuing to grow, eight years later, said Michael Lustenberger, Vice President of Marketing, Sony Online Entertainment.

About EverQuest The Anniversary Edition

EverQuest The Anniversary Edition is a compilation of eight years of content. Players can adventure online in worlds populated by hundreds of thousands of other players as they journey across massive continents filled with vast dungeons, eerie crypts and mysterious underwater landscapes. They are able to choose from 16 unique races and 16 distinct classes, including the recently created Drakkin race, a race formed of a dragon breed. With more than 375 zones to explore and more than 50,000 items to earn and collect both magical and mundane, including artifacts of great power, characters can shape the universe in EverQuest.
