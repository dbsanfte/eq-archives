Player Guide: Guide to Monks

Author: Tabor Ali (N/A)   Organization: N/A

Greeting Friend, so ye seek to follow the hallowed path of the monk eh? Forsaken most all of your worldly possessions for the hope, small as it may seem, that one day, trained and honed of body and mind, ye may show your foe the skilled fury of our noble class?

At first glance I would ask you, why do you seek to join us?

A monk is indeed a powerful class, but does not provide the instant gratification that practitioners wielding their magics may contain.... We cannot stand up to a flurry of assaults, taking blow after blow to our unshielded body as can our better armored brethren…

What draws you to us?

Ahh.. You seek unmatched offensive power in melee when given no other options?

You seek the abilities to separate you, and allow you when working with others to safely draw foes out of hiding, and into your battle party’s grasp?

Well, that you shall find within our world.

None can deliver the furry of assaults with the tenacity or lethality of a monk, and with a few tricks taught only amongst our ranks. Live to see the next battle.

Step inside our temple young one, and let us being your training.

Character Creation:
I have seen tons of debates on this topic, I will go with what I have found, but I also advise checking message boards and reading on your own. Everyone seems to have his or her own opinions.

To begin with you have the option of two races now, Humans and the newer Lizard men: The Iksar.

The human seem to be based upon offensive prowess, alas, it is not a huge difference until the later levels, but trust me, its there. Just not as LARGE of a difference as some would like… While the Iksar are akin to a more defensive posture, their scale providing an AC bonus second to none, coupled with the fact they possess keen vision AND the ability to regeneration like the Trolls of Grobb, making them a powerful combination as well. Though as a drawback, the Iksar are welcome nowhere, save for their home city, so keep that in mind

After picking your class take a look at your starting stats, and here is what I have found each to mean.

Strength: Ahhh, good ole brawn, your oomph, your chutzpuh. Strength determines the maximum weight you can carry before being overburdened, and, provides you with a meager damage bonus to whatever weapons you may be using. It also directly affects your ATK value, which is used for combat roles. Now, the amount you can carry. As a monk, is moot, because once you go over 15 weight, you take a noteworthy hit to your Armor Class, keep this in mind, it is crucial to not go over 15 weight unless you are prepared to pay the consequences. I found that a higher strength didn’t really do much for me as a Monk; again, this is up for debate in many places. I am of the mind, that slightly more damage is not the way to go, at the beginning; Str is an easy stat to gain items for should you discover they are needed.

Stamina: This is a relative measure of your endurance, and as such directly affects your base Hit point to start out, though it’s a very small effect. It also dictates how long before your little yellow bar tells you that you are tired as hell. I have found that a few points here, gave me a couple more HP, I lived a bit longer at the lower levels, and thus, thought I made the right decision. Again, this is what I found and by no means the end all, be all of what to do.

Dexterity: Hehe, When I used to play AD&D a while back, Dex was my thing, had to have a good Dex, I always dug the acrobats… Sadly, in EQ, Dex means a lot less then you might think… It was posted that all it really does is directly effect skill progression with offensive skills. Meaning, a higher Dex will cause you to see:

‘You have gotten better at Hand to Hand (add one to current skill)!’

a little more than someone with out. To me, that was a good thing, so I tossed some points in here, because Monks get a ton of offensive skills, that need to rise quickly to keep us doing peak damage. It also factors into ranged combat, so if William Tell is who you idolize. hehe.. I must admit, as I monk I do use a lot of throwing weapons..=)

Agility: This is the Defensive inverse of Dex, this effects defensive skill progression. Again, I find this very helpful being that Monks are all about avoiding damage, not negating it. We can get out of the way really well, with a plethora of get-out-of-the-way skills, but when we get thwacked, its usually rather hard…Ugh.. So I put some points here with the mindset that the faster I could learn to get out of the way, the better off I would be. Also of note, it provides a small AC bonus. Again, just a perk in my opinion.

Intelligence and Wisdom: Um.

If we were smart, we would wear plate armor and hide behind the warriors.

If we were wise, we would not be out there at all.

That being said, I didn’t worry about either.

Note though, if you choose to take up a trade skill, your success/failure rolls are based off your skill. Skill advancement checks are made off of Int or Wis I have found, whichever is higher. Keep that in mind if you are career minded.

Charisma: What can I say, we are monks, and our actions speak rather than our words. Have you ever smelled someone after a workout with no deodorant? I have yet to see deodorant from vendors, and Monks are always in the heat of battle.

You do the math.

Ladies love our abs.. (or Men, if you are a lady).. We don’t really need this skill; it affects merchant’s prices somewhat from my understanding, not a huge deal being that we can’t carry much anyway. Don’t forget that 15 weight dangit!

Wooo, ok. Give yourself a name, pick a deity and time to get to work!

I don’t consider deity choice much of anything honestly. Humans get Quellious or Agnostic. Iksars go with Cazic. I haven’t found it effecting game play directly of yet. Though, maybe one day Verant will make me eat those words..=)

NOW:
I don’t give a hoot where you start. Some people will argue that as a human starting here is better than there. (hint: You have very few choices if your an Iksar and only slightly more as a Human)

blah blah blah.. Everywhere has innate advantages, and you will not be doing any super power leveling with this class, unless say, you have a level 50 cleric following you around. If you do, then that being the case, it really doesn’t matter where you go now does it? Hell, go kill Nagafen.. More power to you..

Start wherever looks interesting to you and begin your journey.

Starting Out:
Ok, find that dang elusive guild master. A little hint:

If you are Human, he is the guy with the sweet washboards abs.
Iksar: the guy squatting in the middle of the circle near a ton of merchants.

Give him your note and train a few things as you see fit, here is what I always tend to do, you get 5 points, here is how I use them.

2 points to Hand to Hand
1 point to Kick
2 points to Sense Heading

These are by no means the Bible of what to do, but let me explain my thoughts:

Some people say that you would never train points into offensive combat skills, like 1h slash, 2hblunt, Etc. Etc., because they will rise naturally in combat, and thus you are wasting them. They are correct, they will, but I am very impatient and have found if my new little monk runs out for combat, with nothing in hand to hand, his first fights look a lot like this:

Tabor misses
Tabor misses
Tabor misses
Tabor misses
Tabor misses
Etc.
Etc.

So, I add a pair of points, and find a small, but make me smile difference. Same thing with kick, it’s a nice little offensive bonus.

Sense heading is the bane of my existence. I put 2 point there because if I don’t. EQ likes to spite me and make me press that button 10000 times to get Sense heading to (1). Then another week of Sundays to get to (2)… Again, I am not patient.

Small Blurb on Skills:

Ok, lets take it from the top:

Hand to Hand:

This is your bread and butter. Your skill here is your fundamental monk mojo. Keep it maxed. With the expansion to Kunark, weapons have been found that will out damage Monk fists, but starting out, you will not have access to these, so put them out of your mind to begin with. For Humans, here is what you have to look forward to, sorry Iksar, I don’t know your damage tables.

Level (dam/delay)
1 (4/36)
5 (5/36)
10 (6/36)
15 (7/36)
20 (8/36)
25 (9/35)
30 (10/34)
35 (11/33)
40 (12/32)
45 (13/31)
50 (14/30)
60 (14/26)

So Humans, as you can see, starting with 4 dam, you gain a point every level 5 levels until 50, and your delays drops a point every five levels, starting at 20 to 50, at 50, its drops 2 every 5 levels to 60.

A level 50 monk is no joke with his/her fists; they throw around some serious heat.

Dual wield:

Good skill, several classes get it, only we get it to start out I believe. (Rangers, Warriors and rogues all get it). What happens here is, this skill’s levels effects a roll that is made during combat to determine if you will attack with your other hand, or whether you will continue to dig in your nose with it while fighting with your main hand.

Pretty cool skill, if you make the roll, you swing with this hand. Giving you two attacks that round, and more attacks mean more damage. Well, in theory anyway..=)

Double Attack:

You get this skill at level 15, another goodie. This skill allows you to swing twice with the same hand, in the same round. Now, as your dual wield skill progresses and get higher, eventually you will start to double attack with frequency with both hands (starting around level 30) giving you four attacks per round, and that’s not counting your special attack at the time

Special Attacks:

These are the staple of the Monks mojo, we get special graphics. Wooooo.

el 1-4 Kick:

What can I say, take your foot, and place it with some force upon said target, nice little addition to your damage.

Level 5-10 Round Kick:

Oooo, fancy new spinning graphic, does better damage than kick, AND is a magic attack, meaning, should you desire, you can attack things that would scoff at normal weapons. Like I said, pretty nice damage, little slow on the delay side though

Level 11-20 Tiger Strike:

Ooo, little crouching Ninja smack.. About the same damage as Round Kick, and much faster delay, always a good thing. BUT, this strike is not magic in and of itself, so now, you are going to need magic gloves to lay the smack down on certain baddies.

Level 20-25 Eagle Claw:

Do Eagles have claws? Maybe its Eagle Strike….Er.. In either case, its fast, its hard, and you will be happy when you get it. Though, it’s the same graphic as the Tiger Strike.

Level 25-30 Dragon Punch:

Now see, here, being a fan of Street Fighter, I was hoping for a Ryu looking jump up in the air, flaming fist sort of…Well, lets just say I was mildly saddened. Great skill, wonderful damage, nice addition to our arsenal.

Level 30 - Flying Kick:

You made it, this is the one, at higher levels, you can do over 100 point with this, and it looks really cool. A little slow on the delay, so the skill rises painfully slow at times I have found, but Ooooo is it worth it.

Note:

I have found that it works to go ahead and toss your old skill out as you get a new one. At first, its going to be rough, you will do less damage at times and miss a whole lot, trust me on this one though, work that new skill constantly. Though, in a mild addendum to this line of though, post 50 Monks get new disciplines that do use skills such as Eagle Claw, Round Kick and Tiger Strike, so this my mean as time progresses you may want to keep those skills on a somewhat increasing level.

Mend (small cheer in background from author): I cannot say enough good things about this skill, though at some point, it will kill you. Sorry. What it does it heal a good chunk of HP, I would say roughly a third, and it recycles every 5 minutes. Sound good?

Wait..

When you start off, you need to press this button every time you can, need it or not, because you will fail constantly, and at time, you will fail AND hurt yourself taking damage instead of healing it. Once your skill get to about 50, you can stop the constant presses and use it a little more as needed as at 50 it is a little more reliable. And that’s the skill level at 50, not your level.=)

Feign Death (huge cheer in background from Author)

I get all misty when I think of how much I love this skill.

What it does it cause you to stop what you are doing, and keel over, giving an Oscar caliber performance Siskel and the late Ebert would both give two thumbs up, causing whatever was hitting you to sniff, and leave you be, and if whatever you are fighting is under level 35, they will never remember they were mad at you. Good trick.

Now the bad side:

You get this skill at level 17, its painfully low level when you get it, meaning, you will never successfully feign to begin with. You can toss some skill points into it, or just practice it constantly like I did. Either way, it will get there and save your monk arse many times.

I won’t go into feign pulling, I will leave that part for you to figure out, but pulling is what brought monks back into being. Monks are the best pulling class in EQ, bar none. There are occasions where other classes are even, but monks can pull anywhere, and anything and do it with less risk of training then anyone else in my opinion. Feign death would be the reason.

Closing:

In closing I would like to say, enjoy your character; play him for the sheer fun of being a monk. Those being said, if you seek uber power at lower levels, pick a Necro, or Mage, because you aren’t either. You are limited in your soloing ability (I say limited, because it can be done), and groups honestly, are just more dang fun. At times you will feel really underpowered and dismayed, but don’t give up, it’s a great class and you will be highly rewarded at the upper levels for sticking with it

Good luck young monk, Godspeed..

And may your foes always feel the skilled fury of a trained Monk.

Tabor Ali
