  Workaround for Elddar Grove Quest  
Print News | Mail News
Developer Gordish posted on EQLive:

"Currently on live there is a bug in the Elddar Forest mission "Key to Corruption" on the step where you need to find Orgaf. The problem is I moved him further away from the named orc raid because it was causing some funky agro problems having a quest giver be so close to.

If you get stuck on this until the next time we can update tasks to the live servers, you can get the update if you go to location positive 1603, negative 1435. Then run over to Orgaf who isn't to far away from that spot.


-Gordish the Gnomish Hero"

Thursday, 23 March, 2006


  Test Server Has All Expansions, Double Experience  
Print News | Mail News
All Expansions Come to the Test Server!

In addition to the other nifty bonuses for playing on the Test server, now anyone playing on Test can also experience all the expansions as well! Simply download all the expansion files by making sure you have all expansions checked when you log into Test and you will be able to experience the entire game.

This benefit to all Test server players is in addition to:

Double XP

Ability to test buff your character to 25

All Veteran Rewards regardless of account eligibility

Need only one flagged raid member to enter a flagged area

The bonus access to the expansions while on Test will not give you access to all the expansions on Live servers though. You will still be required to purchase any expansion you wish to use on a regular Live server.

Come on by Test and check it out!


-- Kwill @ 1:55PM Thu 23 Mar 2006


Chat Logs for March 22  
Print News | Mail News
You can see the entire log below.

As a summary:

Plane of Rage progression is bottlenecked, admit the developers, and they are looking into it. The progression NPC will stay up longer to address the issues and they may change it further still.

Endurance regeneration is a "hot topic" for the EQLive team and is "discussed on a constant basis." All regen is being looked at, but there is no time estimate when these tasks will be done.

Coldwind Blackfoot, DoD quest NPC, will return to the game as soon as they finish testing him. They will keep people posted on the EQLive Boards.

Poison making is being looked at for a major upgrade and is high on the list of things to do.

No new spell designer has been hired, but the EQLive team are all chipping in to work on spells. Adjustments will come slower until they hire someone specifically for spells.

Progressive trade skills quests are being "kept in mind" but no specific plans are being made to add more to the game.

There is no answer to the question will there be new player models. They do have "something in the next couple of months that should be of interest" said Producer Raghnell.

Keridon, the designer of the Creator mission in DoN, will be looking at it "from top to bottom" and consider the respawn problem.

The changes to bows by Maddoc was the one that would "positively impact the greatest number of players and negatively impact the least number of players."

Baseball's Curt Schilling to Endorse EverQuest  [ 4 Comments ]
Print News | Mail News
Curt Schilling, World Series Champion and MVP Pitcher for the Boston Red Sox, just signed an endorsement deal with SOE to attend Fan Faires and other events to promote SOE's EverQuest games.

In a recent press release, SOE announced that as spokesman for SOE, Schilling will make personal appearances, attend media tours and give opportunities for fans of both games [EQ and EQ2] to meet him.

"I'm excited about this partnership with Sony Online," said Schilling. "I've been playing video games a long time. I've been a fan of Sony Online since the first time I logged into the original EverQuest. That passion has only increased since the launch of EverQuest II."

Schilling may be one of today's top athletes in professional baseball, but off the field he plays EverQuest II.

"EverQuest II provides me an opportunity to remain in touch with my family during the incredible travel schedule of a major league baseball season," Schilling said. "One of the greatest elements of EverQuest II is that I can play when I'm on the road. My son Gehrig and I play together when I'm not working and he's not in school.

I enjoy the anonymity and the ability to interact on a personal level with people I wouldn't normally meet or know," Schilling said. "That, and the game is a riot. There's no end to it, the world is enormous, the possibilities are endless, and above all else, it's a place to go and relax amongst friends. What I can't get enough of is your ability to interact with real live people. The game's about teamwork, and knowing the right way to pick and play your class properly in such a way that it complements a group. That's why EverQuest has stayed so popular."

So yet another reason to attend Fan Faire!




-- Kwill @ 9:57AM Thu 16 Mar 2006


  

Wednesday, 15 March, 2006


  Nodyin on Prophecy of Ro  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
Developer Nodyin had this to say on the EQPlayers boards about why he thinks PoR is a great expansion:

"Fun features. The spheres/auras are a lot of fun. Arcstone has several roaming Spheres that move back and forth over the island. They're awesome to see and when they pass over you the gameplay changes for a few seconds. They're also in seveal missions (Spheres) and play a big role in Devastation.

Destructible objects and Traps are really good too. One big bonus on Traps is that we've been freed as designers from some of the limitations of the previous Trap system. We can now create content we couldn't create before. Any time a limit on creativity is lifted I think we all win.

Good Missions. I happen to think the missions in this expansion are really well done. Those that don't like Monster Missions might disbelieve this but in my opinion we took it to the next level with Monster Missions and really started to explore the potential of this feature. I mentioned the "Braaaaaains!" mission in another thread and I'll bring it up again - where else can you play as a zombie rampaging through Rivervale?!!

Good single group, small event and raid content. I think there is a lot going on for players of nearly all power levels starting at level 45 and going through the hardest of the hard core 70 raider players. "

-- Kwill @ 9:38AM Wed 15 Mar 2006
