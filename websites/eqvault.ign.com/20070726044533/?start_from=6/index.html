Print News
Besides EQ, what games do the EQ developers play? Lead Developer Rashere posted what he's playing right now on a thread on the anniversary edition release:

"I pick up pretty much every big title that hits the stores and have a fondness for quirky games as well (go go Cubivore). Besides just loving games, I also like to see what games are doing right, what works, and what doesn't.

Another Guild Summit?  [ Comment ]
Print News
Should there be another guild summit at SOE, as there was in 2004? Although some players would like to have one, or think it's a good idea, Developer Nodyin says it's not going to happen because of the feedback they get at Fan Faire:

"We also have a lot of panels at the Fan Fair that generate changes based on the questions and responses that we get there, sometimes even during the panel itself! There are also a huge number of changes and modifications that take place because of issues raised on these boards or via direct contact with players on Test and Live servers in various situations. I'm not against meeting players in any circumstance (I actually welcome it) but I'm not sure we need another summit right now."

As a regular attendee of Fan Faire, it's true that the developers take the time to interact with players for the entire event. They take many questions and answer them as best they can, and write down the things that they want to look into. So if you have concerns and would like to talk to the SOE staff, think about attending Fan Faire, which is fun as well!


-- Kwill @ 6:23AM Wed 25 Apr 2007

Friday, 20 Apr, 2007


  Damage Over Time Spell Hate Changes  [ Comment ]
Print News
Rashere posted:

In the near future, we're going to make a change that will reduce the amount of hate generated by DoTs so I wanted to give a head's up. For most damage spells, they generate aggro based on the base amount of damage they do. DoTs do this as well, generating hate based on their damage every tick, but they also generate initial aggro worth one tick of damage when they first land. This means that they basically have one extra damage tick's worth of aggro at all times.

We're changing things so that you won't gain aggro based on the DoTs damage until it actually does its first tick of damage which will bring them in line with other damage spells for the amount of hate they generate.


-- Kwill @ 11:44AM Fri 20 Apr 2007

Raiding Game Commentary  [ Comment ]
Print News
Is the raiding game the crowning glory of EverQuest, the one aspect that sets it apart from other MMOs? Yes, says player Nendiilya, in his editorial posted on EQOutrider, but some aspects of the raiding game need to change if EQ is to keep up with the current state of MMO play, he argues.

Nendiilya has long been a top end raider in the game and community correspondent to the EQ team.

Read his article here.

Edit: I just found an article by Jeff Vogel, game designer, that adds to Nendiilya's commentaries about some of the problems with raids in EverQuest. Vogel makes the general point that killing trash mobs is boring, and shows a lack of creativity from designers. Both writers are coming at the same problem, one as a player, the other as a designer, and they come to the same basic conclusion.

Thursday, 19 Apr, 2007


  Devs on Top of Zone-Based Economies  [ Comment ]
Print News
A player posted on EQPlayers that the drop rate for gems had been significantly reduced as a result of the new hot-zone status for Crystal Caverns. Rashere responded to his allegations:

"I routinely run zone-based economy reports.

I just queried the data for the zones you mentioned, and neither the coin-drops nor the cash-loot have been changed enough to make any significantly measurable impact on money entering the world.

When it comes to money entering the world, we have a very strict guideline for all level ranges of mobs, a guideline which we follow to the letter with very few deviations (hill giants, for instance, are one of the anomalies).

Specific to your inquiry on Crystal Caverns, its conversion to a hot-zone, and that being the cause of coin drops being significantly reduced, I'll check with Nodyin to see if he may have done anything to cause that particular issue. ....Nope! None of the NPCs were modified in any of these zones. The new drops are additional but didn't remove any current items from the zones."

The thread quickly morfed into a "yeah, how come we can't make more plat of the mobs in all the zones," to which Dev Prathun replied:

"In original EQ, when we were walking uphill both ways in the snow, we managed to buy our spells without level 4 mobs dropping 4-12 platinum worth of items. It was rough, but somehow we got by."

A few more players chimed in, a bit less sarcastically, that yes, indeed it was hard to make platinum in the days when you had to fish or sell hides in East Commonlands in the open trading mart.

So, all's right with the world, and we had a thread where three developers chimed in to talk about an issue.

9th Year Veteran Reward on Test Server  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Tadena posted:

"The New Vet Reward is on Test if you wish to see it and feedback on it

New AA called "Armor of Experience"

This Ability grants you several layers of protection from harm. As you take damage each layer will absorb five strikes before failing. As your armor fails you will take more and more damage."

Developer Nodyin explained: "It's a melee guard that goes through 4 stages of protection from 100% down to 25% before fading."

Nodyin also explained a little more about it in the same thread:

"In designing this AA I was very conscious of balance concerns. This AA is actually unique in EQ up to this point. I had to have several changes made to the way the spell system and some of the spell effects worked to get this to happen. Part of the issue was changing the spell system so that we could create this AA in a way that would both scale from level 1 to 75 and that wouldn't be overpowered at any point.

Does it have a gameplay impact? Yes, but not to the extent that it will change the balance of content. Is it useful? I think so, and I hope you agree."

The refresh time was four hours intially, but has since been changed to 20 hours as are the other veteran reward AAs.

Wednesday, 11 Apr, 2007


  Former IGE VP Tapped for SOE  [ 4 Comments ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
David Christiansen, former Vice President of Business Development for IGE, will now be the Vice President of Business Development & International Operations for Sony Online Entertainment. In this newly created role, Christensen will be responsible for generating strategic global growth opportunities and securing key business alliances for SOE, with an emphasis on the European and Asian markets. In addition to his business development responsibilities, Christensen is tasked with managing SOE’s international operations group. Christensen will be based in SOE’s new Los Angeles studio and will report to John Needham, Chief Financial Officer and Senior Vice President for Sony Online Entertainment. Christensen is a veteran of the video games industry with a broad range of business experience, including development partnerships, acquisitions, licensing and business affairs.

SOE's official press release states that "prior to joining SOE, Christensen served as Vice President of Business Development for IGE where he drove the company’s relationships with the massively multiplayer publishing community."

MMO players are certainly familiar with IGE, since it is the largest company behind real money transactions, or selling gold in online games. I am not sure what "relationships" the press release is referring to, since most MMOs are still calling real money transactions a black market, and certainly buying platinum and powerleveling services is not condoned in EverQuest.

In past years, IGE bought up many of the EverQuest fan sites, including OGaming, and used them as advertising platforms for the gold selling market.

All is fair in business, however, and I am sure Mr. Christensen can figure out ways to make SOE, including EverQuest, much more profitable using his prior experience in global marketing. The irony, however, is inescapable.
