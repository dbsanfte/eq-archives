  Station Exchange Unveiled: Marketplace to Buy and Sell  
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Did you ever think it would happen? SOE today unveiled their Station Exchange program, where you can buy and sell items, plat and characters for real world currency in EQ2 starting in June.

In a bold move to legitimize and standardize an underground industry that has been going on for years, SOE again has shown that they are indeed always on the cutting edge of the gaming world.

EQLive will not be part of the initial pilot program.

The FAQ on the program is below. Initially it will be restricted to two servers that will be "exchange enabled" so the entire game world will not be affected.

Sunday, 17 Apr, 2005


  New EQ Producer Craig Knapp  
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Craig Knapp is the new EQProducer and as an introduction shares some of his ideas in a producer's letter to the players:

EverQuest Producer's Letter
Greetings Norrathians!

First off I wanted to take some time to introduce myself as the new producer of EverQuest. EverQuest has been a large part of my life for the past three years; including the past two years as the game's Associate Producer. During my time on the EverQuest team I've come to realize one of the great things about EQ is the friendships and the community that we’ve all come to love. We all remember the first time we logged into EQ. If your experience was anything like mine, you were shocked that so many windows could fit on one screen! Thankfully, most of us found our way out of the initial shock and found folks who showed us the ins and outs of the game. Many of these in-game friendships have grown into friendships outside of Norrath and for that I thank EverQuest.

We all know that EverQuest is not just a game. The game and its community are both a large part of all our lives. Our team takes great pride in this fact, but at the same time we are humbled by the responsibilities that come along with it. As developers, our primary responsibilities are to make an entertaining game and to support the thriving EverQuest community. Only by working closely with you, can we keep EverQuest successful for years to come.

As I hope you have noticed, our team spends a great deal of time on message boards and we are constantly gathering what's important to you and bringing that information to the forefront. We continue to develop procedures that facilitate that process, but allowing our community to thrive requires an ongoing dialogue with you. For instance, class re-envisionment has been, and continues to be, a hot topic. The recent patch represents many of the initial changes we’ve made together, and we welcome your continued feedback on them. Our goal is to get these changes out as quickly as is reasonable, but to pace ourselves knowing full well that this will take many iterations and updates. This continues to be a top priority and we've already made many changes based on your feedback. Your continued feedback allows us to make EverQuest a better game.


His letter continues on to discuss server merges, instancing the Plane of Time, and more hints of "ambitious plans" in the next year.

Veteran's Awards  
Print News | Category: EQ Live
SOE will be offering a new program called "Veterans Awards" starting May 13th -- the longer you have been an active subscriber, the more rewards you will get in the game. Here are some details:
Good News for Veteran EQ players!

As part of EverQuest's 6th-Year Anniversary celebration, the game team is giving current EQ players** one special Veteran Reward for every year that you've been an active, paying EverQuest subscriber in good standing! This is very good news for six year vets! It means that you'll be getting six Veteran Rewards for up to six of your characters!

If you are a former EQ player, any active subscription time previously accrued on a paying account in good standing will be credited toward your veteran reward when you re-activate your paid subscription.

Note: you'll find the Veteran Rewards tab in the game's AA user interface.

Even better news: From April 11, 2005 to May 11, 2005, any current EQ player can purchase a 12-month EQ subscription for a special price ($99.99 plus applicable taxes) resulting in a potential savings of more than $50 off of the standard, monthly subscription fee. Game software is sold separately.

Tuesday, 29 Mar, 2005


  Brenlo's comments on server moves  
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Brenlo offered this explanation on the EQLive website concerning the server moves:

"Merging servers does not free up staff. It does not save us money on bandwidth. What it does do is get healthy populations on the servers that needed a boost. We set a number that we consider to be a healthy population and merged the servers based on that criteria. Low population servers are merging with higher population servers, mid population servers are merging together.

Yes it will be disrupting and we realize that, which is why you will have the option to move, to almost any other server you desire. "

The higher population servers are merging with the lower ones.

Saturday, 19 Mar, 2005


  Class Revisioning: First Ideas from the Team  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
EQLive has posted the first proposed gameplay and class changes to re-envision the class structure. Some of these will go live in the April 6th update.

Some of the proposed general changes:

--Casters will gain spells every level
--Adding healing potions
--loss of the plus mana cap
--more solo abilities at the cost of group friendly attributes

Some of the proposed class changes:

Bard: increase instrument mod cap
Beastlord: one new slow spell and increased healing
Beserkers: lower endurance cost
Clerics: full spell lines for Mark of Vengance and Mark of Retribution
Druids: innate abilities to change from defense to offense
Enchanters: change mana drain limitations; changes in charm
Mages: summons spells go directly in player hands; useful pet items
Monks: reversing AC mitigation reduction
Necromancers: more combat utility; reduced pet heal cast times
Paladins: more main tank abilities
Rangers: improved spell damage and healing
Rogues: fix Assassin's Feint; throwing quivers with haste
Shadowknights: more main tank abilities
Shaman: increase healing; extend Spirit of Leopard and Panther spells
Warriors: lowered endurance cost, increase knockback
Wizards: improved dps, fix manaburn

There are more changes for each class, so read the entire message below. And discuss!
