Gordon Answers Expansion Spell Question
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:27 PM PDT
Gordon took the time today to respond to a question centered on the expansion spells. Here's the original post followed by Gordon's response:

    I was just wondweing if, Level 50+ Spells are going to drop on L45+ Mob's in the Old Dungen that have 45+ Mob's like SolB, Pema, and Lower Guk? Are Spells going to be Exclusive drops in Kunark Lands?

    No, the spells are only going to drop in Expansion areas. We wanted to encourage people to spread out to the new lands, and also didn't see any reason to have people who didn't purchase the expansion in dungeons fighting for them when they cannot use them.

Lord Sahvo Weiss, Duke of Saronthal and leader of the Council of Aeton,
gripped the reins of his white steed and, placing a foot into a stirrup,
hefted his heavily armored bulk onto the horse. He adjusted the pack behind
the saddle and, waving his page over, accepted the long pole atop which
blazed his emblem in white and gold. Speaking in a booming voice, he
shouted, "Open the gates!"

As the massive wooden gates began to part, he turned to his wife and son who
approached from the side.

Emma Weiss, his wife of seventeen years, grabbed his hand. "Be careful, my
love," she admonished and then smiled. "I know you will be victorious once
more." Sahvo leaned down to steal a kiss from her and then placed his hand
on the head of his son, who looked concerned.

"Be strong, my son. Have faith in Mithaniel Marr that good shall overcome."
Straightening in his saddle, he added. "The day I fight for a cause that
is not just is the day I shall fall, and not before."

Without another word, he urged his mount forward into the morning sunlight
outside his keep. Arrayed on the hill before his home was a legion of his
guard, sunlight flashing off the polished steel of helmets and swords, each
man's shield bearing the emblem of Saronthal in white and gold. At the
sight of their duke on his steed, they raised their swords as one and
cheered.

His steed reared at the sudden onslaught of noise, and he patted its neck
gently to calm the aggressive animal. He raised an arm and quiet fell.

His voice carried on the wind as he shouted, "Today we march for Freeport to
join the armies there for the battle to come. In one week, we shall shed
blood over the Desert of Northern Ro, the blood of any whom would stand in
defiance of justice and honor! We fight to recover a vital artifact which
will bring us one step closer to the Straylight Gem, once step closer to
harnessing that power for truth, and not evil!"

He pitched the end of his banner into the dirt so that it stood without his
help, the banner snapping in the breeze. With his hand, he pulled his sword
from its scabbard and raised it high above his head.

"In one week's time, we shall all march into battle, but we do not march
alone! Mithaniel Marr will be there with you, at your side, the power of
his might on your blade and his truth in your heart! Believe in what is
right and we cannot fail! I am proud to stand beside you in battle and I
salute you as the best warriors in the realm!"

The cheers from his men rose as he resheathed his sword. Grabbing the
banner from the dirt, his horse broke into a gallop and took him to the head
of the regiment. "To Freeport we march!" he said and they began the
journey.

Skill Increases During Duels
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:50 PM PDT
Does your combat skills increase while in a duel? Gordon answered this question on the Gameplay Board recently:

    Skills are meant to be increased by fighting creatures that actually intend to kill you, thus imparting *risk* in exchange for the *reward* of a skill increase. Allowing people to skill up while dueling would allow someone to neglect a particular weapons skill all the way up to the high levels, then build it up from nothing without risk.

    -Gordon

It is now my sad duty to post the following letter sent to me by the people of Fennin Ro:

Gathered at their favorite hunting grounds in Rathe Mountains "The Folk"
slayed giant after giant. "What is that" Xiandru Cloudwalker yelled to his
group. A GIANT of giants was bearing down on the small band. Spell after
spell hit the great beast. Slowly "The Folk" beat it down.
Xiandru's blasts of power shook the beast to his heels while Celina and
Tylon chopped away at his exposed legs. But the beast would not be put
down. A shimmer blue light encircled his body and he gained momentum and
became more invigored.

Again "The Folk" beat him down. The clerics, Landeth, Zardac, and Samsara
could barely keep up with the heals. Pandorae and Eynel were vigorously
trying to gain enough strength to slow the beast while Celebairn rocked it

members of the guild were not strong enough to face such a beast. But
slowly he was beat down again. With a sliver of health left and all of "The

the ground as the Giant toppled over him.

Again and again the clerics tried to raise their companion with no success.
In a last ditch effort they all joined hands to channel their energy through
Samsara's spell of life. A burst of light appeared before the group. Soft
and gentle the light floated above the ground. Tunare in all her splendor
stood before the humbled group.

"To each of you I bestow the gift of everlasting friendship. Treasure this
gift above all others and remember I am only a thought or memory away"

Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:29 PM PDT
Here is the latest round of posts made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board:

  • How to you record text in EQ in a LOGGED.TXT File?
    The /log command will toggle text logging to the EQLOG.TXT file in your EQ directory. It defaults to *off* each time you log on to EQ, so if you wish to log everything, you'll need to turn it on each time you log on.

  • Aegis of Life/Spawn Comment
    The EQLive guys said that the spawn is merely rare the last time I brought the issue up, but I'll have them check it again since people are still reporting that he's not spawning at all.

  • Rules for GM Resurrect
    The position of the body after a 10K fall on zoning is the only method available to determine that a 10K fall is what actually happened. Moving the body in essence destroyed the evidence that would have allowed the Guide to perform the Rez.

  • Placeholders
    Placeholders are just another way to set up spawns and have their appearance tied to some random value. They also require that something be killed, avoiding a situation where someone just has to wait around long enough.

    There are situations where long spawns are appropriate, and other situations where placeholders with a percent chance of spawning a certain mob is appropriate. It just depends on the effect that the game designer is going for.

  • Kly Overseer Answer
    There are cases where particular NPCs can be given access to a spell that is normally not part of their repertoire. I'm not sure what the deal is in this particular case, but I'll check into it.

  • Trading Accounts Legal?
    Transfer of accounts between individuals is prohibited by the license agreement. This applies whether the account is transferred for cash, another account in kind, or any other form of consideration.

  • Necro Rez Useless?
    [Quote from original post: Necro rez spell is totaly useless]

    There's a difference between "useless" and "costly". The spell definitely has use, but as it is a tremendous ability, it is very costly. I would agree that in many cases this spell isn't the best choice in how to proceed, but I can also think of several where it's certainly better than nothing.

  • Test of Flight and Steel Problems
    The entry fee was set up as a linear value for this go-around. One difficulty with lowering the entry fee is that it results in a lot of more people wanting to enter. With so many people on an average server at peak, the tournaments can get a bit difficult to run. I used to run pool tournaments for upwards of 200 people. It gets rough very quickly.

    We might try a scaling entry fee next time, but we'll see how it works out.

  • Level 51+ Spell Question
    They are both supposed to save vs. magic. Draught of Jiva has an additional effect associated with it. I'll give other readers here a chance to jump in and comment on it rather than releasing the info myself.

Verant on Addiction
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:16 PM PDT
Gordon addressed the issue of "EverQuest Addiction" on the Gameplay Board. Very interesting post, I believe this is the first time anyone at Verant has spoke about this:

    Does Verant employ any professional consultants from any area like Psychology, Sociology, Psychiarty, etc to offer opinions on the matter?

    Not specifically for that purpose. One of our game designers does have a degree in psychology, and most of our people have degrees, of which psychology and sociology classes are often included in the curriculum, but we don't purposely design for addiction, we design for excitement and fun, which are characteristics that some find addicting.

    Does Verant plan to limit play time in any way, shape or form?

    No. We provide a persistent world where one can feel free to come and go as they please. It is in the end the responsibility of each player, whether an adult themselves, or whether the account was purchased by a responsible adult, to regulate their own playtime to what works for them.

    Does Verant reccommend a certain play time limit for its players? Does Verant believe that there is such a thing as excessive play time?

    There is certainly such a thing as "excessive play time", but it's not a rigid value that we can set, but is more subjective in terms of each individual player. Certainly playtime becomes excessive when gameplay begins to interfere with responsibilities, every day life, and commitments to a job or family.

    Has Verant received any complaints from parents of children that play EverQuest, or from players themselves regarding playtime?

    Not to my knowledge, of course it wouldn't make much sense for parents of minor players to hold us responsible for a service that they pay for with their credit card.

    Just like television, sports, and computer usage in general, parents are ultimately responsible for the content to which they allow their children access. Though there are companies that specialize, for instance, in selling parental control that limit TV viewing times for children, no one really holds the TV networks at fault for sending out the signal 24/7.

    In any case, any activity that provides some form of fun, excitement, or stimulation can be taken to an extreme. "Games" by their nature provide those factors. Though we are certainly proud of the success of EverQuest and amount that people play and enjoy the game, we certainly do not want to see people hurting their families, careers, or other relationships over it.

    Please play responsibly.

    -Gordon

    In order to insure class interdependence, and also provide and balance abilities available to the different races, we have to place some limits on the class selections that a person can make. We draw these lines based upon what makes sense from a roleplay standpoint, as well as to insure that the different classes available to each race is spread out fairly well.

    Anyway, your question above was, "Why do you penalize the players so heavily time wise for dying". Setting aside for the moment that I don't think that calling the penalty a penalty of time is appropriate since there is no "end" to the journey in the game, "heavily" is a subjective term. What one person thinks is a heavy penalty might be no-sweat for another. Since people have differing opinions on what these subjective terms mean, we set a value, and provide methods that allow the mitigation of the time involved.

    The concept of risk and reward is very much part of the core of EQ. In general, one taking greater risks should have the ability to reap more rewards. Players determine what risk is acceptable to them when they play the game. The player who chooses to play without clerics or paladins around to do an XP rez face greater experience losses on death, but reap the reward of having less restrictions on the way that they play. Conversely, the person who chooses the reward of power-soloing to into the mid-50s will face the risk of greater experience losses on death since they don't have friends to rez them.

    There have been a lot of posts here recently from people who have asked us to lessen the risks involved in the game without reducing the rewards. All the while, they have been in control of the amount of risk that they subject their characters to. I understand that it is human nature for people (including myself) to want to have their cake and eat it too, but rewards without risks make for a boring game.

  • Damage Shields
    Damage shields still allow interruptions to take place.

  • PoF Items Question
    Considering the numbers of new items from Kunark that are neither nodrop or lore, we probably won't be applying those tags to anything that has been missing them in game all of this time.

    For those on the PvP servers where loot is still a possibility, it's basically another case where you decide what risk is good for you and play the game to that end. If the possibility of losing an item in PvP combat is too great, it is you choice as to whether or not you want to wear it in a situation where PvP is possible.

    Cases like this where players make a decision about the level of risk that they are willing to accept are quite common in game.

    Considering how easy it is to reach 200+ int, I'm surprised that people would KS a rare spawn to get an item that is a mere +1 INT better than something else easily obtainable by them. If the fact that casters want to wear this ring is partly responsible for the speed that jboots are coming in game, we probably wouldn't want to change it.

If the risk of dying without a rez is too great to be palatable, then players need to take steps to mitigate that risk.
