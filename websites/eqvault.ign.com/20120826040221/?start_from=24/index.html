  Legends of Norrath Set #5: Ethernauts  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
The newest Legends of Norrath tradable card game set, "Ethernauts," was released this week. It is of especial interest to lore fans in EQII because it explains some of the backstory of the next EQII expansion, "The Shadow Odyssey."

According to SOE Denver's press release:

"This set kicks off the newest Legends of Norrath story arc, starting 4,000 years ago when void storms and terrible creatures threatened the very existence of Norrath. Only a handful of brave heroes, the Ethernauts, kept the world from extinction. Players will experience this epic story through Legends of Norrath, taking this knowledge into the present EverQuest II timeline, where strange void storms trouble Norrath once again."

“Ethernauts provides gamers the opportunity to play as the great heroes of Norrath,” said Scott Martins, director of development for SOE Denver. “This set will give players the whole story behind the events in the upcoming EverQuest II The Shadow Odyssey™ expansion launching in November. Ethernauts provides gamers the back story and history they need to fully unravel the mysteries of The Shadow Odyssey!”

The set has more than 280 new cards, adds new races, and new loot cards for EQ and EQII.

This deck also marks the debut of a Starter Pack. This card set is capable of making four different starter decks, replacing the individual starter decks for the four previous blocks of card sets.

Updgrading Older Spells  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
Trying to upgrade spells at the lower tiers can be quite frustrating these days. Some lower level masters are very rare, mostly because people get to level 80 quickly and the spells from the older content don't find their way to the broker. If they are on the broker, they are too expensive for most people to buy. Getting a group to try to loot these spells can also be difficult. Some players have suggested alternative ways to upgrade spells, such as questing or letting the tradeskillers make master spells.

Developer Aeralik is willing to try to fix the problem, but it won't be a fast process:

"This is something I would like to look into fixing slowly over the coming months so that you can upgrade old spells without having to go back to really old content or pay a fortune for on the broker. However, it will likely be a case by case kind of thing. Obviously some things are easier to do than others but often percentage based skills like the brawler heals or scout hate transfers reach a point that makes them difficult to upgrade further which means we might have to explore other adjustments to them."

  September 3 Update  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Get ready for voice chat! EQII already has it, and the players love it. Most groups use it, and there are even general voice chat channels.

*** Highlights ***
- Station Voice: Station Voice is a voice chat service powered by Vivox and included with certain SOE game subscriptions. Station Voice allows players to talk person-to-person in-game at no additional cost. Players can go far beyond basic real-time chat with the usual headset and microphone setup that is commonly used today, and will be able to access a suite of voice communication tools.

Upcoming features are scheduled to include voice mail, the ability to receive external calls, and voice fonts to synthesize player voices into in-game character voices. Station Voice will be hosted and managed on the Vivox Network. Game software and headsets sold separately. Station Voice is also present in EverQuest II and Star Wars Galaxies.

Features at Launch in EverQuest include:
-- Talk person-to-person in game with any microphone and headset combo.
-- Chat with your party, guild or any other group in game.
-- Create automatic channels for guilds, raids and groups.
-- Play and communicate without worry of a significant increase in game lag.
-- Manage group/user participation with moderator controls.
-- Individually mute and set volume per user Create automatic channels for guilds, raids and groups.
-- Set up easily without the need for separate software, accounts or registration.
(For details on Station Voice see below under the UI changes.)

- Weightless Coinage: Coinage no longer adds to the weight of player characters. Adventurers all across Norrath rejoice... especially monks!

Mage Power Pool Issues  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
Some astute players have discovered that the mage power pool formulas have a glitch: at 1350 intelligence points, the affect of more INT is to diminish the power pool, rather than add to it.

Up until 1220 INT points, the mage's power pool keeps growing. After that, there's a plateau with no change. At 1350 INT, the power pool sees the effects of diminishing returns, as it diminshes each time INT is added. Check the link above for an excellent graphical representation of the phenomenon, as well as many examples from various players that document the problem.

This issue is a function of the hard cap (15 x level + 20 is what was reported) for intelligence stats. Clearly the math for INT stats should not resemble a curve, but a climb upward and then have no negative effect after the cap is reached.

The devs have no comment on the issue so far.

What does it mean to you? Players should be aware that adding too much INT can have diminishing returns at the present time, so keep under that 1350 INT point for the optimal power pool.

  Bonus Exp Weekend Update  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Did it seem like the bonus experience this weekend wasn't working? That's because on some servers, it wasn't turned on.

After some people noted there didn't seem to be a bonus, Kirstie from EQ2Players realized it hadn't taken effect on some servers:

"I'm going to need to take complete responsibility for this.

Although I turned the bonus XP on with the best of intentions there was a quirk in the system that I wasn't aware of and I didn't personally go check to insure that the bonus had taken on all servers.

Given that I'll be making sure we do another bonus weekend and I do apologize to everyone. - Kirstie .. the Humbled."

The good news is that she will be enabling it again for everyone next weekend, adding Monday since it's a holiday:

"We will definitely be having some bonus XP this weekend and it will include the full Monday, Sep 1st since I know a lot of people have that day free. - K"
