EQ Legends Q&A
- Aurum @ 6:14 PM PST
Alan spent a good deal of time answering some questions about EQ Legends that players sent into the Developer's Corner this afternoon. As you can tell, some players seem in favor of of this new addition to the EQ universe while others do not:

    I guess I knew that this announcement would bring a lot of questions. :)

The Live team has done nothing but grow over the last few months and at the same time we've also built the Legends content team. We have also been hiring new Customer Service people incrementally to cover the Legends server.

    d a bunch of web-based features (and tons of other stuff I'm surely forgetting to mention).

Certainly a lot of that content will be exclusive to the Legends server(s), but we'll also use some of it on Live servers after it's run its course on Legends.

    Obviously we see this as a win-win situation.

Changes Coming to the EverQuest Patcher
- Aurum @ 6:10 PM PST
Alan made an announcement this afternoon concerning the EverQuest Patcher. Here's the scoop:

    On Monday the 5th we'll be making a change to the way our Live and Test patching works.

    A little while ago we combined our Test patcher with our Live patcher. The intent was to remove the confusion that having two patchers can cause and to encourage more people to play on Test.

    Well, we're going back to the old way. We are going to split the Test patcher and the Live patcher back into two separate programs.

    This means that in order to access the Test Server you will need to run TestEverQuest.exe, which you will find in your EverQuest directory. To play on the Live Servers just start EverQuest as you always have.

    There are a few advantages to separating the two patchers. Splitting them up makes it a little easier, as a player, to be sure that you are headed to the right server when you double click the icon. It's also a little more intuitive for those that want to run the game in two directories.

    Using two directories for EverQuest does use up a lot of hard drive space. However, for those with enough space that play regularly on both Test and on Live servers, this is a safer and faster way to work. Using the same directory means that each time you change servers (Live to Test or Test to Live) you will have to patch. The data on the Test Server is very rarely the same as that on Live servers.

    And for us internally it makes it easier to test new patchers. It gives us one more step for testing. Now we can push a patcher to the Test Server and try it out there after internal testing and before it goes to the Live servers.

    In any case, we've decided to move back to the old way. Starting on Monday, February 5th, 2002 you will need to run TestEverQuest.exe to patch and play on the Test server, and Everquest.exe to patch and play on the Live servers.

    * Patch Message Addendum *

    The command line argument "/test" should no longer be used to play on the Test Server. Anyone using this should remove this argument from their shortcut. Please manually select "TestServer" from the Advanced Options window to use the Test Server. On Monday, February 05, TestEverQuest.exe will once again be used to patch and play on the Test Server.


    - The EverQuest Team

More Mage Q&A
- Aurum @ 7:50 PM PST
The one update to the Developer's Corner came to us in the form of a Magician Q&A follow-up:

    I want to thank the many of you that sent in calm reasoned responses to my last comments. Because there were so many of them, and most of them were rather lengthy (like the one from Citen below), I'm not going to post a bunch of them. In fact, I'm only going to post one today.

    But I want to address a few points that came up.

    Yes, I most certainly could look up all of the NPCs that can be Dire Charmed based on level and make my own list. But I wanted to address an issue that I suspected was happening. While a lot of folks sent in lists of NPCs that were rather accurate and useful, some folks sent in lists with NPCs that were significantly too high level for Dire Charm to affect. Many included NPCs that, while charmable with that ability were in zones where using those NPCs for gaining experience wouldn't be too useful. This indicates to me that while some folks know the limitations of Dire Charm, some don't. Now this is perfectly understandable, as not that many characters have attained the ability, and it's not available to Mages at all.

    Most of the rest of the kinds of comments you sent in are covered below. It's long, so I'll make comments within the text.

    Greetings.

    First off, Id like to apologize for the countless emails im sure you get from every other mage in the world that is vague, haughty, and angry. They serve no purpose but to make mages look bad.

    Despite how I sounded, it's not really a problem. I understand being passionate about the game. If I didn't, I wouldn't be here.

    Now then. First off, I'm not level 60, and I don't have my epic. Im just an average little 52 mage trying to grind my up through the levels. I haven't parsed my earth pet's damage versus a similar level warrior or poured over the mana ratio of mage and druid nukes because thats really not my idea of fun. The following opinions are just the impressions I have gotten from playing EQ for about 2 years now.

    That works. Most of your comments matched up well with what others wrote.

    The main 'problem' with mages is their lack of place in a group. This problem becomes compounded in the 50+ levels, but it does exist before then. The cause for this problem, despite what lots of people say, is something thats not really within your sphere of control. Because for such a long time, mages havent trully had a specific group role, at least none like a cleric, enchanters, or wizards role, most players have relagated them to a 2nd rate class..a class you put in your group because you can't find anyone else.

    Pre-50 I don't think Mages have a problem finding a place in groups. There may be a few places that, due to pathing issues, Mages tend to avoid, but if the player is good, friendly and knows how to work with a group, finding groups pre-50 isn't all that bad. As many noted, the Mage can solo pretty well pre-50. This does tend to mean that Mages will solo. That means that groups don't see too many Mages. Which leads to them not knowing what a Mage can do, which might lead to Mages finding trouble grouping. But pre-50 the issue isn't with the class, it's with the social dynamics of the game.

    Post 50 you are talking more and more about raids (at least you were until Luclin). And I get the sense that when Mages say that they are never wanted in groups, it's 'raid' groups that they are talking about, not so much experience groups. We can talk about that 2nd rate class comment a bit later…

    Now, some people say lots of people don't have a definate role in a group, but I have seen that most indeed do. For example:
    Clerics offer supperior healing and buffing abilities.
    Shaman's offer slow spells, haste buffs, and a very good line of stat buffs.
    Druid's offer snare, tracking, healing, buffing, magic damage, and transport/evac abilities.
    Warriors offer supreme tanking and melee damage dealing power.
    Rogues offer very good melee damage and scouting abilities with hide/sneak.
    Monks offer very good pulling skills and melee damage.
    Rangers offer tracking and snare, along with some good unique buffs and melee damage output.
    Shadow Knights offer good pulling skills and very good aggro management abilities.
    Paladins offer very good tanking skills and superb stun abilities, which can be very useful against fast mobs or caster mobs.
    Wizards offer supreme magic damage output and transport/evac abilities.
    Necromancers offer supreme DOTs and their darnkess snare line.

    I can hear members of some of the classes you mention above bursting to disagree with your evaluation, especially if you are talking about raids. For an experience group, I'll accept your summary.

    Now then, that leaves Magicians.
    What do Magicians offer?
    #1. Melee Damage through their pets. Pets are intended to be and are our main form of damage output. But, this benefit is often taken away because of pathing issues. And while pathing is not a massive problem in every single zone in Everquest..its such an annoyance in so many that i simply refuse to bother with it most times. I just use Summon Companion. I summon a pet, /pet guard here. When a pull comes, i stand near mob, Summon Compantion, /pet attack. If the group moves I either resummon or Summon Companion. Now then, yes, this does avert pathing, but it is a trully cumbersome process, and I doubt was the whole intention of the spell.

    I'm not going to try to tell you that the pathing in EverQuest is perfect. We are well aware that it's not. You, however, do seem to have a workable solution to some of the problems. The number of zones where pathing would exclude the use of pets, though, isn't some laundry list that never ends.

    A few places I know pathing to be bad, at least as far in pets are concerned:
    Plane of Hate: Most all of the 2nd floor, and a good portion of the first floor. 2nd floor pathing in particular is bad. It is very very common to see mobs or pets heading in very strange circle patterns that eventually reach the raid party, but not before picking up a few friends on the way.

    Hate, the most commonly mentioned bad pathing zone. We've fiddled and fiddled with it, and it still sucks.

    Ruins of Akheva: The hallway off to the right from the main entrance hallway has some places where if a mob is directly in front of me, the pet will go behind the mob and come back around to the front of it to attack.

    Fair enough. But you're talking about some spots in one location of that zone. That certainly doesn't condemn the whole zone. Most of the problem zones are like that, bad spots where pets are a bad idea. Again, not a great thing.

    I have no idea how the pathing in everquest works, or if it can really be 'fixed'. If it can't be, then a better workaround for pet owners should be found. Possibly having pets follow the owners like players follow each other, possibly giving a spell that allows the owner direct control over the movement of the pet for a short period of time.

    In cases where you know the problem spots, Summon Companion works just fine to solve this. As for other solutions, I'll certainly bring it up to the team.

    #2. Magic damage through nukes. Personally, Im rather happy with how my nukes stack up. Not as good as a wizards. Druids have similar damage spells, but that has to be taken in context of the fact that the druid usually has other uses for his mana besides just nuking. He might need to watch health bars for heals, have mana to refresh sow or other buffs, have an emergency supply for an evac, etc. So while if a mage and druid both only nuked, the druid would be equal or better, this situation is not going to happen a lot. The only thing I would like to see in mage nukes are some cold based spells, nothing spectacular. Possibly summoning gusts of intensly cold planar wind with a small stun in addition to damage.

    The idea of giving Mages a broader range of damage types keeps coming up. Regardless of any claims to being the "masters of elements", broad damage range is the purview of the Wizard. I don't know if you should expect to see other damage types.

    #3. Utility Summons. I think this are has improved with Luclin. The Elemental Blanket and Defender are both great items. The Shard of the Core is useful now with the NO DROP tag removed. And summoning arrows and daggers will be useful as long as monks want to throw sharp things at giants. The improvement I'd like to see in this area would be utility summons for the mage besides food/water/bandages/beer (BTW thank Brell for Bristlebane's Bundle). My idea is a line of summons similar to the Beastlord line of Spirit of XXX spells, which imbue thier pets with various procs or abilities. Id like to see the mage be able to summon special weapons for each of his pets that enhance his natural abilities. These spells would be rare drops or quested, as they would be powerful and rare scrolls. Possibly a dagger of air with a small haste percentage for air pets, earthen blades (too bad that name is already taken) which had STR HPS and AC. Liquid spears with a very small slow (1-2% maybe-The idea being the mob cannot move as fast when experiencing such intense cold). Fire hammers with an atk bonus or possibly debuff. These spells could take the natutal strenghs of the various pets and amplify them, thus making them more useful overall, and making it easier to both solo and be effective in groups.

    All interesting ideas. One of the most common suggestions for improving mage pets, along with the idea of just buffing them up straight out.

    #4. CoTH. I don't have this spell yet, so I can't really comment on it much. I can see how this spell has been turned around from its original intention. Mages defend it so fiercily because many see it as thier saving grace, the spell that gives their needed role in a group. If Mages could be given a broader and more definate role, then many would be so angry when it is tweaked back to its original intention.

    Well, broader might not be a good idea. Mages really do three things, summon stuff, pets and damage (ok, and debuff). I'm not sure that I like the idea of broadening the abilities of any class, as that almost always leads to infringing on the stuff that others do. Unless that broadening was into a completely new direction, and new directions are hard to find. I'd like to see suggestions for improvement more like your #3, ideas that build on existing areas for the Mage.

    So, my overall point is this: Mages need a role in groups, and to do this we need to have reliable damage output from our pets. This can be aquired by somehow dealing with the various pathing problems so people no longer have a fear of pets causing trains and death. Improved summons for our pets can make them even more effective, and thus more desiarable for groups.

    All in all that seems like the way to go if improvements are to be made. I would disagree with the idea that there would be no fear of pets causing trains and death, but it sure would be nice if such things were only cause by intentional game mechanics rather than weird game code.

    Thank you very much for listening to your players and dealing with the bad seeds among those players.

    Sincerly,
    Citen Clanbreaker
    52 Mage of Brell
    Karana Server
    Illuminatus Guild, VFA Alliance
    "Cause a Home just isn't a home..without a Gnome"

    Thanks for the nice letter.

    Keep sending in ideas and comments folks.

    Alan

      Normally this isn't something I'd do. But, well, I'm going to outline the changes we're making to this quest in rather close detail. But first, let me try to explain the concern that players had with this quest.

      With arguably the most popular Epic Quest item, the Cleric trying to gain his Epic item usually finds plenty of people to help in the process. Clerics are commonly the first in line with guilds to help with their epic quests. So Clerics tend to get further in their epic quest faster than many others. This has lead to a very nasty 'bottleneck' for Clerics trying to complete the quest. The Ragefire portion of the quest has been creating a lot of static among players.

      So we have decided to make a change to the end portion of this quest. To reduce confusion and to help people understand why we're making this change, I'm going to give out a few specific details. These changes will go Live with the patch tonight.

      For those that are not interested in quest details, consider the rest of this post SPOILER INFORMATION:


      First, the 'merchant' version of Zordak will now appear at a different location in SoldungB. He will appear once each day (except after a server comes down, then he will spawn in one to three days). The appearance of this NPC will no longer require the absence of Lord Nagafen.

      The cleric still turns in his Shimmering Pearl to Zordak. But now the merchant Zordak will return a Swirling Pearl to the Cleric and disappear.

      At that point the Cleric will need to head out and look for him. A killable version of Zordak will appear independent of the pearl turn-in every few days (except after a server goes down, then he will appear a little faster). Killing Zordak will transform him into dragon form, and the Cleric (with help…) must kill him immediately.

      The new dragon Zordak will drop a heart that is a container that requires the Swirling Pearl to be combined in it to create the Heart that is turned in to Omat.

      The rest of the quest remains the same.


      The goal of these changes is to reduce the problems with the bottleneck that existed before. They will not remove that bottleneck, but hopefully will remove the nasty competition for a single spawn with so many people willing to be… rude to each other.

      Alan
