Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:44 PM PDT
A few posts were made today on the Gameplay Board by Sony's Gordon Wrinn:

  • eBay
    [Quote from original post: sorry phelan..but that is not good enough. Before everyone goes and flames this "musk" you need to know it is him. ANYONE at all with a FA coold sell their account and try to MAKE it seem like it was this guy...hmmmmm would maybe casue people to try and bid more.]

    Very true, and it's the same issue that we face daily. We've already had several cases of people putting up auctions, making it appear to be someone on their server who they don't like, in an attempt to get them banned.

  • Does EQ Support Dual Processors?
    Though EQ will run on a multiprocessor machine, any performance increase will be negligible. The application, though multithreaded, wasn't built specifically to take advantage of multiple processors.

  • Jumping Into the Hole
    [Quote from original post: No one said not to levitate in... Read the patch message again it didnt say.]

    The patch message included:

    "As a note, jumping in The Hole from Paineel is NO LONGER A SHORTCUT TO TOXXULIA FOREST. The GMs will not recover corpses for people who fall, or jump, or attempt to levitate into The Hole."

    I felt that this was as clear as I could make it without completely spelling it out. The risk of exploring new zones is quite high, and it should be. It really wouldn't be appropriate for us to describe in detail everything that can kill you.

  • Rubricite & Manastones
    [Quote from original post: I was wondering why Verant doesn't put rubicite back in the game? It is so rare now that the price is inflated too high for its worth.]

    Prices never inflate too high for its "worth". The economy doesn't allow that to happen, as no one will pay more than it is "worth" to them. As for putting it back in, I suppose that it's possible that it might make an appearance or two.

    The manastone, however, will not go back in. The manastone stopped dropping due to balance issues associated with it. Rubicite stopped dropping due to rampant camping.

  • If I do "this" will I get in trouble?
    It basically falls under fraud or harassment. Someone is bugging you for a port, you agree to port them, you port them someplace else. You do something other than what you agreed to do. Next thing you know the guy is dead, and lost experience due to something that another player did intentionally. It's basically the same as purposely training someone.

    Now if someone is harassing you with non-stop agressive begging, and refuses to leave you alone when you tell them to, please /report and /petition a GM.

  • Experience Loss & Death
    Experience loss on death isn't calculated as a number of bubbles, but rather a numeric value. That value, is however, capped at the higher levels. In other words, a level 60 person will appear to lose less experience (comparing "bubbles") than a level 55 person.

  • Manastones and The Hole
    The Hole shouldn't be marked as a "Kunark" zone, so the manastone should work fine at the moment.

Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 8:06 PM PDT
Gordon made a few more late night posts in the last hour or so. Here they are:

  • Druid Bug
    Thanks for the note about the regen problem. I've confirmed it and sent it off to the test team. I'll check into snare, but I'm pretty sure that there's some case-specific code that reduces its resistability.

  • Veksar
    [Quote from original post: Where is the underwater dungeon in the Lake of Ill Omen? Now that you have completed The Hole, will you be working on Veksar?]

    Veksar is a possible future expansion area. It's not on our schedule at present.

  • Revive Spell Issues
    [Quote from original post: Has anything been done to prevent problems with clerics randomly casting revive on folks to intentionally/unintentionally depriving them of getting experience restoring ressurections or any at all?]

    Not yet, but we are working on a plan. In the mean time, anyone doing this intentionally is subject to discipline for harassment. You should use /report and /petition in the event this happens to you.

  • Haste Sashes
    [Quote from original post: I realize I will start a fire storm by this post but I decided to ask it anyway. Last night a member of my group told me that Gordon and company were going to reduce or already have reduced the effect of haste items by 10%.]

    We do not have any plans along these lines. Hate items and such appear to be working pretty well in game. With the release of Kunark and the subsequent raise of the haste-cap, we've made room for more and better haste items and spells.

    I think we're safe for the time being :)

  • The Neat Idea: Caught in a Lie?
    [Quote from original post: Is today's patch that "neat idea"?]

    Yes, this is the idea. We've been discussing it since before Kunark launched. Obviously it would have been nice if we would have put it in back before anyone surpassed level 52, but we decided to hold off for a while and see what the impact of growing numbers of high level folks would have.

    [Quote from original post: Did anyone at verant consider ways to expand EQ without messing with the level limit?]

    Sure, but as the majority of players polled expressed an interest in an increased level limit, we decided to go in that direction.

Gordon Infobits
[9 Comments] - Aurum @ 4:10 PM PDT
Here's the first round of posts and excerpts made by Gordon Wrinn on the Gameplay Board today:

  • Dragons: Solutions Not Complaints
    We're always open to better solutions to the problem. Of course in this case, I don't think that anyone will be happy with anything that reduces their ability to contribute once they get too high in level.

    One suggestion I've seen was that people's ability to affect dragons should be scaled back as they go up in level, such that perhaps a level 58 warrior couldn't do as much damage as a level 52 warrior versus a dragon. Though this would most likely accomplish the same goal, I don't know how well it would fit into the world.

  • More Dragon Issues
    [Quote from original post: And where is it we find the "other dragons" that have the unique loot that you have places ONLY on these two dragons?]

    The same loot? I doubt you find that. However there's nothing to stop you from trading the loot you find on other dragons for some of the loot you can get on Vox and Nagafen.

  • Gobblins Banishing Players
    Yes, we are aware, and it is not the intent. It will be fixed this evening. SolB is fine, however.

    Sorry for the problem.

  • Zone Level Limits
    For zones, you are correct, we aren't interested in putting level ceilings on particular zones. Having an NPC use different abilities based upon the level of the attacker is something else entirely.

    "You are too strong to enter here" doesn't fit well into the environment.

  • Find Other Dragons
    The change wasn't made to restrict people over 52 from fighting dragons, but to restrict the level of assistance they can render fighting these TWO dragons. Other dragons in the world are not affected by this change.

    I realize that this is not a solution that will make everyone happy, but the situation the way it was wasn't making everyone happy either. This change should help give everyone a chance.

  • Can't Zone Into Permafrost
    It's a small bug. Only Vox is supposed to have that ability. We'll be bringing down permafrost this evening to fix it. Nagafen's Lair is working correctly.

    I apologize for the problem.

  • Druids, Animals and Charm
    Though I'd certainly like to see more critters out there marked as "animals" to allow druids to charm them, we don't plan on giving druids a way to permanently charm an animal, as they are not a pet class. The ability to have a pet, for any period of time, certianly makes them stronger for that period, so I hope you consider it a benefit.

    As a note, we have introduced an animal-only resistance debuff which should help with some of those charm times. However I suspect that you might need to check out some Charisma gear to extend the duration of your charms.

  • Locked Doors: Another Options
    [Quote from original post: A better solution would be to not allow NPCs to walk through locked doors at all. How come that rat in Befallen can open a door (locked or not) anyway?]

    The problem is that spell effects don't collide with objects or world geometry. In other words, as was done at one point in beta, people could cast rain spells and such and have it affect NPCs on the other side of the door, with no risk to the caster.

  • Dragon Complaints
    [Quote from original post: Why won't Gordon/Abashi comment on dragon complaints?]

    I've commented on several other threads as well. You might want to check out the "search" funtion, it can help you find the active discussions which we are participating in.

    It's not unusual for situations like this to result in dozens of threads where everyone is asking similar questions or making similar arguments. In these cases, it makes the most sense for me to only concentrate on a couple of them.

  • Lockpicking Negatively Affects Players?
    [Quote from original post: Well, I don't know about your servers, but on mine there are probably 5-6 level 50+ rogues.]

    With the lockpicking change, 3 of the 4 level 50+ dungeons in the game now require a rogue to access all/part of them. Now the 2 to 3 50+ rogues that are online at any given time have absolute control over where the other 100's of level 50+ players can go.

    Players have always shown a remarkable ability to adapt to changing conditions. I doubt it will be long before we see a surge in rogue population in response to this change.

    I think everything will work out well :)

  • Nagafen/Vox
    [Quote from original post: You never once said we'd be punished for levelling beyond 50th]

    This is a special case. As I stated in the patch message, our goal was to avoid having to raise the bar for existing players. Had we been able to make no changes, and still have the result be that the 40s and low 50s were killing the dragons, we would have done so. However every week I've gotten more and more reports of high-50s folk coming in with a small number of people and besting the dragons.

    We still think that the lower level folks deserve a chance.

    [Quote from original post: After FoH killed Trakanon, we saw that he shared the same loot tables as Vox and Nagafen.]

    No, he doesn't. I looked at the items that they got off of Trakanon, and they are certainly different than anything that has been found in game.

    [Quote from original post: It does not say that characters lvl 53 and over would be BANNED from Vox & Nagafen raids.]

    Very true, and I'll let the players figure out exactly how it works. I still think that they'll be able to assist in some fashion or another, but it's not going to be the same old strategy.

  • FA Quest/Dragons
    [Quote from original post: Was the FA Quest, taken into consideration, before this Dragon *enhancement* was done?]

    Well, we haven't done anything to restrict level 53+ paladins from looting the items, so I wouldn't say that it's been placed out of reach. That said, we are going to discuss this issue further in our next tuning meeting.

  • Shrink Anywhere Potions
    I'll see if I can walk this issue through. I apologize that it's taking so long to get this change made.

  • Bug While Following Mobs
    This happens due to the way collision with objects works server-side versus the prediction engine of the client. Basically, the server doesn't acknowledge the object (a tree for instance) so has the NPC walk through it. The client on the other hand sees the NPC hit the tree, and figures that he NPC will get stuck walking up against it.

    In a few seconds, the server resync's the client and everything is updated. It's one of the minor drawbacks to the system that we adopted to minimize the effects of internet latency and reduce bandwidth usage.

    June 22nd, 9:00am
    -----------------

    ***Today's Updates***

    *The Hole*

    We are pleased to announce the opening of The Hole, a high-level dungeon on the continent of Odus. The Hole is a complementary addition to EverQuest, available to all subscribers regardless of their Kunark status. The files necessary to enter The Hole are available via the "Optional Patch" button at the bottom of the patch program interface. The files are quite large (about 9MB total), so if the download is going slowly, you may want to postpone downloading the files until later.

    As mentioned above, The Hole is a high-level dungeon, designed to support characters starting at about level 40. It should be regarded as extremely dangerous. As a note, jumping in The Hole from Paineel is NO LONGER A SHORTCUT TO TOXXULIA FOREST. The GMs will not recover corpses for people who fall, or jump, or attempt to levitate into The Hole.


    *Vallon Zek is now Coin-Only*

    As we announced earlier this week, due to the number of people specifying interest in "coin-only" loot on Vallon Zek, versus those wishing to retain item- loot, we have implemented "coin-only" looting rules for PvP combat on Vallon Zek.


    *Resurrection*

    Previously, players had to be resurrected within two hours after death in order to receive any experience back as part of the resurrection. Due to the increased size and difficulty associated with Kunark zones, we've raised this limit from two hours to three. This should assist players and make it easier for them to obtain an experience-returning resurrection within the time limit.


    *Warping*

    We put in some code that should help decrease the "ghosting" (NPCs appearing to be some place they aren't) being reported in some of the larger Kunark zones. Please let us know how this goes.


    *Locked Doors*

    We noticed few weeks ago that it is common practice to use NPCs to open locked doors rather than by using the services of someone that is able to pick locks. In order to protect and increase the value of the abilities of the lock-picking classes, NPCs have gained the ability to walk through locked doors without opening them.


    *Vox and Nagafen*

    Over the past couple of weeks, we've received more and more reports of these dragons being bested by smaller and smaller groups of higher-level (high 50s) adventurers. As we've always felt that Dragons should be special encounters that require a large number of people, we were left with some choices.

    One choice available to us was to increase the power of the dragons to a level where the level 60 folks would find them challenging. This would have the disadvantage of forever placing them out-of-reach for those who did not buy Kunark, and place them further out-of-reach for those who did, but are just now approaching the levels where they could go on Dragon raids. Another possible solution was to make the dragons flee (depop) when engaged by a more powerful group of people. This would have the undesired effect of allowing some people to deny others the experience of fighting a dragon.

    Instead of raising the bar as mentioned above, we've instead decided that dragons, or at least these two dragons, should have the magical ability to pick their own fights. Players of level 53 or above will now encounter some difficulty when attempting to assist in combat with these dragons. This should help 40s and low 50-level characters with their chances to encounter a dragon without worry of getting forced out by much higher level players. In addition, it also has the added effect of creating a natural progression from the younger dragons of Antonica to the elder and more formidable dragons of Kunark.

    -The EverQuest Team

    Gordon Wrinn made the following announcement on the Gameplay Board concerning the Test Server:

      Hello all,

      After a good deal of discussion on the Test server status, we've decided that having the right or the ability to do something doesn't necessarily mean that we should. Test has served well in this capacity since release, however item loot issues and other factors have reduced Test's ability to do this. Given these issues, we still feel that a complete character-wipe represents the best possible solution for continued functionality of the Test server. However, this is a case where, in the interest of keeping our current testers and players satisfied, that we need to compromise on the solution.

      After our discussions today, we've decided that an item-wipe, without deleting characters, will accomplish the majority of our goal to restore the Test server as a functional testing platform. Given that, the EQLive and Operations teams are currently at work restoring all characters that were wiped yesterday, with the exception of characters known to be artificially buffed. This process should be completed later today.

As Brad mentioned in his message earlier, we do recommend that those who are on Test more for playing, and less for Testing, seriously consider moving when we open registration. The possibility of a future wipe, whether it is a player or item wipe, is not necessarily unlikely in the future.

      We hope that the players on Test will find this compromise acceptable and continue to participate in the testing process. Furthermore, we apologize for the insensitivity of our previous decision to completely wipe the server.

      On behalf of the EverQuest team,

      Gordon Wrinn

    Should you gain experience when you die? This very unusual thought was posted on the Gameplay Board recently. Here's the original post followed by Gordon's reply:

    More of Brad on Player Wipes
    - Aurum @ 7:26 AM PDT
    Brad McQuaid, EverQuest's Executive Producer, posted the following message last night on the Gameplay Board regarding the pwipe ont he test:

      Despite the Pwipe, more than 95% of the average population on the test server were playing tonight anyway.
      They were there, starting anew, despite the huge time loss due to the PWipe.

      We also received a lot of heartfelt emails (and very few flames).

      So, after discussing this issue with 180 or so players on Test tonight, we've decided to seriously consider the following 2 options:

      1. Do an item wipe on Test (i.e. restore the characters but without items).
      2. Move on a voluntary basis characters from Test to Live servers. The characters would NOT go across with items.

      It is also possible we will do both.

      I got a lot of good feedback and passionate opinions and responses tonight, and I really appreciate it. The spirit of the vast majority of the players on Test is truly amazing and I admire you all.

      While a total player wipe with select individuals being buffed to test mid and higher level zones might be clinically best for the Test Server, I think it's more fair to the players and testers there to do one or both of the options above.

      In closing, if we choose option 2 (or both 1 and 2) I would really encourage those players on Test who are there more for playing and less for testing to seriously consider moving.

      Another wipe, player or item, is not necessarily unlikely some time in the future.

      Your comments and feedback are welcome,

      -Brad
