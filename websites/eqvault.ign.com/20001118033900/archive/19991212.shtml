Abashi on Bind Afinity and Deadlines
Abashi made a good-sized response to a post on the Gameplay forum. The topic for this one is the spell Bind Afinity and the issue of deadlines. Here's the thread:
    If Verant were to let casters bind anyone, anywhere, the gameplay of EQ would be greatly enhanced. For one, casters would not have to wait for their tanks to return to the dungeon, creating much less downtime and adding to the enjoyment of the game. In addition, melee classes would no longer run the very high risk of being killed while returning to the action, and being forced to suffer an even higher XP loss than the caster.

    This is a very old argument. I could talk about the fact that death is supposed to be inconveniencing. It’s supposed to be something that you really don’t want to happen. I could talk about how this design is just as inconveniencing for the casters as it is the melee types. I could talk about value added to druids, wizards, clerics, and necromancers who all provide post-death services to characters. I could talk about balance issues in regards to having it so anyone can die and be right back in combat 30 seconds after it happens. I could talk about a lot of things.

    When it comes down to it, some classes can do things that other classes can’t. That is the simplest explanation I can give without going into how a change in bind would cause a dramatic shift to many dynamics in the game. Some people don’t play melee-types just due to this disadvantage. Other people don’t play casters just because of the fact that a caster can’t take a hit. It’s all a matter of personal preference. Is a given disadvantage too large for you to play the class? It’s up to you to choose.

    Second, why can't Verant stick to their deadlines? John Smedley reported that a new server would be up in early November. Of course, after we make demands when the deadline has come and gone

    We have had a tendency to be overconfident with our estimations, and that is not intentional. Believe me, with the amount of flack we catch when we miss a deadline, it would be much easier to get it right the first time. Sometimes though, schedules must be adjusted, and there is no way around that. I should mention that John Smedley said that a new server would be coming up “soon”, not “in early November”. In addition to getting a rein on our overconfidence, we need to be a little less free with the term “soon”. We are working on this and you should see some definite improvement.

    What makes it even worse is that Verant never bothers to inform us why they have missed the deadline. . It is a known fact among players that Verant consistently lies about their deadlines

    As far as the new server goes, I told you that we missed it, and I told you why we missed it: Someone that needs to be here cannot. What else would you like to know? Yes, we missed the deadline. I first said it would be up by the end of this week, and had to move it off to early next week. But as to the suggestion that we somehow had some malicious intent in missing the deadline by a day or two at most, that’s just silly.

    I hope this clears things up.

    -Gordon

A Word of Caution
A word of caution from BB:
    There is currently posted on some of the EQ sites a reference to a Guild Merge Petition that is going around. I have one concern with the way that the Group Merge Petition is being done. During the process of registering your vote, they require you to enter your Station Name, the server you log into, and your email address.

    Part of security is keeping both the username and password secret. If some should decide to use the list of station names for malicious intent, they would be well on their way to breaking into numerous accounts - they would already have a list of several hundred accounts and the servers they belong to, to try logging into. At that point it's little more than using a tool to try various passwords against these valid server/station combinations. Should they gain access to these accounts the paying customers could lose items, characters, all that they had worked and payed for.

    A better way for people to request this change is for them to use the in game feature of /feedback to pass on to Verant their wishes.

    I don't want to accuse the person/people running the petition of anything, but I think that everyone should be aware of the risk they run by giving out this private information to an unknown 3rd party. To this extent, I am forwarding this message to several of the more popular EQ sites, as well as to Verant and the person who has setup the petition.

    - BB

Abashi on Quests
Abashi, aka Gordon Wrinn, posted the following about EverQuest's Quests on the official EQ forums:
    There's actually several different types of dynamic quests that run in EverQuest, however they could be broken up into two main groups: Minor and Major.

    Minor quests do not run across all servers simultaneously. They are scheduled based upon availability of actors and petition load. They actually run quite frequently, but have a relatively short duration and typically don't show up on the news sites since they are such a common occurance.

    Major quests run across all servers. They are likewise scheduled into a window of time where they must run. This is to minimize the chance that people find out about the quest in advance and just "show up" at the place where it starts (or ends).

    The weakness in this system is that the majority of quests tend to run during peak hours. The reason behind this is that more people tend to be available to run the quest during that time, as much as there are more people available to play. We're certainly interested in running more quests during off-peak hours and are working to bring more people into the Guide Program who can guide at those times. In fact, if you have the strong customer service skills, the knowledge of the game that we need, and the ability to guide during off-peak hours, I invite you to fill out the Guide Program application at http://guideapp.989studios.com.

    As to the schedule for when these quests run, I'm sorry but that will never be publicized in advance, as the whole server would show up to participate. As much as we'd like to involve everyone on the server at one time, that usually doesn't work out well for the participants.

    Come on now. I think Verant of all people are forgetting that this IS A GAME. Everyone keeps saying, "This is just a game, live with it," including Verant.     I think some people don’t understand the impact of a healthy (or unhealthy) economy on a game of this type. Let me see if I can explain:

    Money keeps coming into the world, and isn't going out. As more money enters the world, its value decreases, causing prices on items, rare or otherwise to go up. This continues until such a time that money is considered "worthless". The economy then devolves into the barter system, which is inherently inferior, as people cannot obtain trade-worthy items without first having other items to trade.

    This is why so many items are sold "For Trade Only" in game, and prices on items that ARE sold are tremendously high. This is why we are so concerned about the economy, as people are linking their sense of satisfaction with the game indirectly to the value of money. The introduction of gate potions, weightless bags, and pricey spell components into the game is having a positive effect on the economy. Money is going out of the world, and the prices on items traded between players are dropping.

    The economy of EverQuest is repairable, and is slowly getting better. The trouble we are faced with is repairing it by adding things that take money out of the economy. It could be fixed nearly overnight if bank vaults were taxed regularly, but we'd prefer to find a way to do it without adding something that that will upset the customers.

    The effects of the additions we make to the game on a server’s economy are noticeable first on the younger servers, and more slowly on the older ones. But I assure you that the collective economies of the servers of EverQuest are getting better, and people will begin to notice their positive effects soon.
