  New Server Announcement!  [ 2 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Official News
The long awaited post was made on Friday, May 1st announcing what rule-set the new server would be. Be sure to follow the link to the official post as a great deal more is mentioned aside from the new server:
We recently did a poll for a new server set through the in-game polling system. We have done many polls in the past through the webpage but there are plenty of players that play the game and don’t go to the webpage for information on it. We really wanted to get everyone who plays to cast a vote to accurately represent the players’ choice.

The winning server rule set was… 51/50 server!!!

We are pleased to see everyone cast their votes and can honestly say that we are very excited to see this server roll out to the EQ community. We are starting the work on getting the backend setup and ready to go, and will announce the details soon. Source

Thursday, 30 April, 2009


  IGN Vault Changes  
Print News
The following is a letter from Staci "Rosethorn" Krause, IGN Vault Editor in Chief.

Hi everyone,

There are some big changes afoot at IGN and for the first time, Vault is really being affected by them, but I think in a very positive way. The most important change to relay is that I am no longer the Editor in Chief of Vault Network. Instead, Teddy Pierson, who has previously been in charge of Vault community sites, will be taking the helm once again. He will continue to run the Planets and all future IGN Community sites as well as the IGN Community Site Manager. My position has changed and I am now the Community Outreach Manager for all of IGN. You are part of that community, so I’ll be doing plenty of reaching out to you as well. The MMO community is an integral part of the IGN Community as a whole and I hope everyone will embrace the idea of being an important part of a larger community.

My new position retains some of my old responsibilities, like getting together with developers to host developer chats or developer diaries. But some new exciting things will happen now that I can focus on community initiatives, like hosting competitions, and I plan to include the MMO community in those initiatives. I have some big plans for the future of IGN community and that includes every one of you.

You can read about all the staffing changes on Jeremy Dunham’s recent Letter from the Editor. Also, to follow all the community initiatives I’ll be working on, add me to your blog friends. Join me in welcoming Teddy back to the fold – his job has just gotten so much bigger!

Thank you everyone for your support the last couple of years and I just know IGN Community Sites have a bright future.

Staci “Rosethorn” Krause



-- Jube @ 9:53 PST

Fan Faire Registration Now Open  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
Join SOE at Bally’s Las Vegas for four days of revelry and celebration at Fan Faire 2009. This year marks the 10th anniversary of EverQuest, and SOE will celebrate this incredible milestone with its loyal fans June 25-28.

For $89 (plus any applicable taxes), attendees will receive an All-Access pass to an expanded Fan Faire weekend that includes:

  • Sneak peeks at new games in development
  • Special guest appearances
  • A chance to purchase EverQuest: The 10th Anniversary Collector's Edition
  • Book signing session with master artist Donato Giancola
  • Developer forums
  • In-game tournaments
  • Activities such as live quests tied to SOE’s current games lineup
  • Live, playable demos of upcoming SOE titles
  • Gifts and in-game items
Attendees also will hear about exciting new announcements from SOE President John Smedley during the Annual Community Address, meet and mingle at the Kickoff Reception, feast at the Grand Banquet, show off their creativity at the Costume Contest and enjoy the good times at the EverQuest 10th Anniversary Bash!*

Those who pre-register 10 or more guild mates online will receive 50% off an All-Access pass. (Offer does not apply toward walk-in or day-pass registrants.)

If you can't make it for the entire weekend, day passes are available for $39 (plus any applicable taxes), and you'll still be able to participate in the daytime activities, such as forums, roundtables, live quests and tournaments.

*Attendees must be 21 years or older with valid identification to attend certain evening events.


-- Jube @ 5:11 PST

SOE Player of the Year 2009 Contest  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
At 2009 Fan Faire, in addition to meeting fellow gamers and the creators of your favourite SOE games, you'll also have the chance to be honoured as "SOE's Player of the Year". The contest will run from April 17 through May 10, allowing you to go online and nominate the most dedicated SOE player (including yourself) for the title. Who's deserving and why?

Do you know someone who is the poster child for your favorite SOE title? So dedicated to the game, he or she walks around with bloodshot eyes, sleep deprived, wearing his or her favorite wrinkled SOE T-shirt? Does this player stand out as the first person to go beyond the call of duty to help fellow community gamers in and out of the game? If so, you may have the perfect candidate, and we want to know more!

To enter the contest, simply submit a 300 word (or less) letter or role-playing story by e-mail to SOEContests@soe.sony.com explaining why this exceptional player deserves to be honored as Player of the Year. You may also include a link to an image (screen capture, artwork, video, or photo) to go along with your entry. See the Official Contest Rules for submission requirements.

At the end of the contest, up to two lucky winners will receive a trip to Vegas to join us in the festivities. So good luck to everyone, and we look forward to seeing you at Fan Faire!
Read full contest details!
