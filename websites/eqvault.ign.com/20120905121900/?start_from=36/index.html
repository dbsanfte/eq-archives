Patch Notes for May 29  [ Comment ]
Print News
*** Highlights ***

- Summer is coming. Keep watch on the EQPlayers website and the LaunchPad over the next couple of weeks for news about some exciting activities.

- The experience gained formulas have been modified to present players with a much smoother increase in the number of kills required to progress through a level as they gain more levels. This change has the side effect of reducing the number of kills required to gain a level in certain scenarios between levels 50 and 75.

*** AA ***

- Rank 4 of Divine Stun is now available as a Secrets of Faydwer AA.

*** Miscellaneous ***

- Improvements have been made to load balancing for instance servers that should improve peak-time performance of instances.
- Corpses should once again disappear immediately rather than fading away slowly after looting when using /hidecorpse looted even if all the items have been removed from the corpse.

- The EverQuest Team

Possible Performance Increase  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
Vhortex posted on the EQPlayers boards that:

"For anyone wanting a increase in performance please go ahead and try this!

- Go to: Options > User Interface
- Target Ring : Turn off ---------> ( THIS SEEMS VERY TAXING ON MY SYSTEM)
- Target Highlight : Turn down all sliders (Optional)

I also disable the "Names" of the NPC's and put them to "target/mouse over only" so they dont appear.

I found this issue by pure chance. I was eating in front of my pc, and targeted a group of centaurs for no reason. I was at 60FPS but by targeting the group of 4 centaurs I droped to 38 FPS! I didn't move the camera anything, I just clicked on the centaur and BAM."

According to people who responded to the thread, this method doesn't seem to work on every computer. The only way to tell if it works for your system is to give it a try.

  The Commission System  [ 6 Comments ]
Print News | Category: General News
This week I featured an article on the AA mirror, and mentioned that if you weren't a level 75+ crafter, you could get one through the commission system, which was introduced in live update #29.

If you aren't familiar with how the commission system works, you can see a very clear explanation at the EQ2i wiki site.

Basically, it works like this:

The crafter and the customer meet at a crafting station. The crafter targets the customer and begins to craft the chosen recipe.

At this point each person sees a different window: the crafter sees what the customer offers, and the customer sees what the crafter is going to make.

The customer is presented with the option to add something to the window, such as the crafting components and/or money. It's the customers decision who gets the by-products of the crafting session.

Something must change hands, even if it's one copper, to complete the transaction.

Once the transaction is accepted, then the crafting session proceeds. Both parties can see how the crafting session is progressing because the two trade windows stay open.

When the item is complete, it goes in the customer's inventory, and the payment goes to the crafter.

DOE Artwork in Digitial Art Book  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
The publication Expose is an annual publication that features digital art from all over the world, and in the 6th edition, SOE has six pieces included.

Rystall, an SOE staff member, reports on his blog:

"We just got our pre-publication copy of EXPOSÉ 6. SOE will be very well represented in this upcoming international annual from Ballistic Publishing.

There were six pieces chosen that were created for Legends of Norrath; five as card images and one for marketing key art.

The chosen entries were:

Ariseph, Mistress of Verse, Mayong Mistmoore, and Queen Cristanos Thex by Derek Herring
Gnoll High Shaman by Patrick Ho
Play Dead by Roel Jovellanos
Legends of Norrath - Oathbound ket art by Clint Langley."

This writer had the pleasure of speaking to Derrek Herring about his art for Legends of Norrath. My interview with him can be found in my article on LoN for the second issue of EQuinox, the official EQII magazine.

Mirror, Mirror on the Wall  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
A very handy item that is a must-have for those who like to group and solo play is the Mirror of Reflected Achievements.

This item, artisan-crafted using a level 75 recipe from Reflections of the Grandmasters, allows a player to store one achievement profile that can be switched out for the one they are currently using.

If you aren't a crafter, you can commission one.

Instructions on how to use the mirror are here at EQ2Wikia.com.
