Sunday, 21 May, 2006


  Prathun's Comments on Deathknell  
Print News | Mail News
Prathun, the designer of the Deathknell zone, addressed these comments on the EQ boards to the critics of the current version of the zone :

"I've gotten enough feedback on the first event in Deathknell to realize it's a tad on the hard side, even after the latest changes that removed the Enforcer's ability to feign off tanks. Though some guilds have expressed an interest in keeping it as-is, I think the event could benefit from another round of tweaks.

First, and most importantly, the knockback AE needs to change. Getting knocked up in the rafters and becoming irreversibly stuck in geometry isn't good and neither are 4 overlapping damage AEs firing simultaneously. I've created a new spell for the event that has a much smaller range and much lower upforce component. With careful placement you should be able to avoid having it overlap and hit the same people multiple times.

Second, the vampire adds spawn with twice the frequency during the last half of the event and this is unnecessarily harsh. Considering the chaos that occurs during this part of the fight, and the fact that crowd control is already locking down adds from the first half, I've reduced the frequency at which they spawn instead of increasing it.

Third, there will now be a bit of a warning before the Enforcers attack, and their activations are staggered and sequential. You shouldn't need a stopwatch to know that four raid-level NPCs are about to select random members of your guild for termination.

Due to the spell change, this isn't something that can be Live copied, unfortunately. It will have to wait for the next major patch. And if the gung-ho guilds are determined to demonstrate that the event is beatable in its current state, they have that opportunity.

Thanks-
-Prathun"

Even if your guild isn't working on defeating this end-game zone, it's nice to know the devs are listening to the player base and making changes accordingly.

-- Kwill @ 4:11PM Sun 21 May 2006


May 19 Producer's Letter: Combine Server  
Print News | Mail News
From Chris Lena:

Producer’s Letter – May 19, 2006


Hello Everybody,

The big news these days seems to be the progression server officially named The Combine. First off, I am overwhelmed by the interest and although I know it won't satisfy everyone's desires it does look like the idea is appealing to a large number of people. It is great to see the excitement out there and not just with our active players but hearing a lot of people talking about reforming old guilds and coming back to the game.

The response has also, quite honestly, got us a bit worried. We are working on taking a few steps to make sure we can handle the possible load. Primarily we are going to cap the maximum online population. We consider this a much better solution than the inevitable zone crashes that would begin when we start to hit higher loads. The last thing we want to do is limit the amount of players that can participate but we have to make sure we maintain a good play experience as well. Over the past few years the servers have stayed very stable so they can handle some pretty big populations so the server will still be plenty full.

We understand that there are many players that have become attached to their character name. In an effort to allow people to reserve their name before the launch of the server we are going to set up a limited version that will allow you to log on and create a character. You will not be able to enter the game world itself but you will be able to pre-create your character and reserve your name. Having the name you want is even more important when you consider that this server will be freshly tracking all item discoveries and leaderboards on our EQPlayers service. Who will be first to discover the highly sought after Fire Beetle Eye!?

There will be a lot more to talk about as we get into our beta and closer to the launch of The Combine server.

Chris "Raghnell" Lena
EQ Producer

Test Server Update May 15  
Print News | Mail News
*** DirectX Upgrade ***

Continuing our efforts to keep pace with current technology, EverQuest will be upgrading to DirectX9.0c to allow us to take advantage of many of the new features and fixes since our last upgrade in the middle of 2003. This patch upgrades our Test server to DirectX 9.0c, everyone on Test Server will need to upgrade to the latest DirectX run times. This can be done by simply going to Microsoft's link and following the instructions there.

Please post any problems you encounter on the Tech Support forum. If possible, please include what graphics card you have installed and what driver version you are running with in the post. We will be monitoring this forum very closely to catch and fix any related issues as quickly as possible.

*** EQPlayers Upgrade ***

EQPlayers is being upgraded to track additional data, in order to test this functionality the Test server will not be connected to the live EQPlayers service. We expect this to last approximately 1 month.

During this period your existing data will still be available for you to view on EQPlayers, but changes to your character will not be reflected in EQPlayers until this testing period ends.

We try to ensure that Test server is connected to EQPlayers as much as possible, but occasional testing requires us to temporarily disable the service. We apologize for any inconvenience.

Friday, 12 May, 2006


  New Expansion: Serpents Spine?  
Print News | Mail News
It's all just guesswork now, but we have a couple of clues that may or may not pan out into where the new expansion will be located.

Intrepid amatuer EQ sleuths have uncovered SOE registered the domian name "serpents spine."

So what and where is the "Serpent's Spine"? Loremasters say:

"The Serpent is the great river that flows south from Halas. It quadrasects the Plains of Karana, is joined by the runoff from Winters Deep, and flows south, separating the Desert of Ro from the more fertile lands to the West, finally emptying into the Gulf of Gunthak. The Lifefire River is an offshoot of The Serpent.

Serpent's Spine is the name of a low mountain range that runs parallel to The Serpent. The Gorge of King Xorbb runs through the Serpent's Spine to connect to the Plains of Karana. The town of Highpass is located in the mountains, and the goblin town of Runnyeye is located under them."

This area would also include the commonlands tunnel and the zone of Befallen, as well as Kithicor and possibly even Rivervale.

Additionally, it makes sense that a new revamp of old zones would connect to the newly redesigned Desert of Ro.

It's all speculation at this point, but perhaps the SOE presentation at E3 will give us more information and prove the guessers right or wrong?
