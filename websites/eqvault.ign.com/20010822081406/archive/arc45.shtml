Gordon Infobits
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:43 PM PDT
Gordon roared back on the boards to help fill in for Absor until he finishes his training. Here's the latest excerpts from the Gameplay Board:

  • Cleric Epic Weapon Nerf?
    We have no plans along these lines. The weapon does indeed have stat ratios that were excessive given our original plan, but as these are rare epic quest rewards, we've elected to modify the other weapons in a similar direction.

  • One Buff Wearing Out..But Which One?
    While printing a line to the chat window is not something that we've considered up to the present, we do have a number of plans for the interface, including reducing the dependancy on the low-res screen, if not removing it entirely.

  • Cleric Temple Ressurections
    As I've mentioned before, we'd prefer that services that can be rendered by players *be* rendered by players. Allowing NPCs to provide similar services would not promote grouping and socialization in line with what we've wanted for the game. Were we to allow NPCs to do anything of this nature, it would certainly be at a cost far in excess to that a PC would charge, in order to ensure that a PC is always preferred as the renderer of a service. In other words, something like 500pp (a drop in the bucket for a high 50s player), wouldn't be expensive enough. Perhaps 5000pp would be more in line.

    That said, it's still not something that we plan on doing.

  • Overcrowding in the Planes
    [Quote from original post: However, this is one of those things that always annoyed me. EVERYTIME someone brings up how crowded servers are at peak times.. they raise their target. Everytime. It started up at 1000.. when the first servers started breaking that it went to 1500. Then 2000. Now it's 2500 because of the "extra content of Kunark." ]

    No, the target was never 1000 players at peak. Servers with 1000 users at peak typically have such low usage off-hours that the environment is not enjoyed by the players. Around 1800 is a good goal, and makes for a nice server environment all the way around. However that remains a target. The servers were playable at 2000 users at peak prior to Kunark launch, and became even more so after launch. With Velious coming, I expect that it will help releive a lot of the pressure that is being felt.

    I do worry though that more zones tend to become unused and everyone will gravitate towards the zone or two with the best loot.

  • On Getting Your Issues Addressed
    Thanks for posting Zoldak. I think that you've laid out some good guidelines, and I especially like your 4th post. I guess it comes down to the question, why do we have representatives posting on this board? It's because we feel that good can come out of it.

    There's much good that can come from maintaining an open line of communication with your customers. The point that there ceases to be any advantage to having these boards, or the point that they do more harm than good, is the point that we'd shut them down. These boards are not something obligatory like tech or billing support, it's something we've decided to do because we feel that maintaining the community is important.

  • Sky NPC Rumor
    I'm not sure where the rumor came from, but no, we've no plans to have items dynamically changed to "NODROP" status.

  • Favorite Fantasy Books
    I most recently re-read Piers Anthony's "Incarnations of Immortality" series. I read them as a teenager and enjoyed them yet again a few weeks ago. I've also enjoyed Terry Brook's '...of Shannara" series, and of course Tolkien.

  • Rotting and Ressurection
    [Quote from original post: If I die, according to the book that Verant released with the Kunark upgrade, I have 7 days REAL time to loot my body before it decays if I'm logged into the game, and 24 hours if I'm not logged in, yet players I encounter firmly believe it's the opposite .. 24 hours if I'm logged in and 7 days if I'm logged out]

    Your observations are correct. The values printed in the manual released with Kunark were incorrect. The corrections are listed in the EQROKManualsupplement.doc in your EverQuest directory.

    [Quote from original post: About rezzes, it was changed a while back to be 3 hours from 2 hours. But I was always of the understanding that it was 3 real life hours. However, I was told by another player that they had died, logged out, came back some 15 or 16 hours (real life hours) later, and got an exp rez. ]

    I believe that the 3 hour rez timer is online. We'll check into this tomorrow and Absor or I will get back with you.

  • Scythe of Shadowed Souls
    There's nothing wrong with a free high-end darkness spell, and that spell is no more resistable than any other darkness spell being used by the players.

    I've always liked darkness for pulling, and the chance to cast it without mana is pretty nice. I'd use the weapon's effect in concert with screaming terror when applicable, in case it's resisted, but that's just me. Much like anyone else, I doubt my necro will get the weapon any time soon.
    --
    I should comment that spell is not variable damage, but all-or-nothing like other DOT spells (with exceptions for PvP and moving creatures like all DOT spells). I'm not sure where the figures came from in the original log, but I imagine that some readings might have been taken mid-tick.

  • Naming Policy
    The naming policies aren't perfect, however nothing created or implemented by man *can* be. They are however the best we've got, and have been very successful over all. They have been so successful in reducing the amount of player dissatisfaction in regard to offensive or inappropriate names that it's a foregone conclusion that all Verant games will have one, though they may be enforced under different rules depending on the game.

    I'm sorry that you feel that your change was inappropriate, and am equally sorry that it wasn't caught sooner in your characters life, however we've committed to enforcing the naming policy for the betterment of all the players.

  • Protecting But Still Getting Killed
    While bard songs are still inappropriately breaking Hide and Sneak (a problem which we intend to fix soon), I don't believe that player-cast spells still have that problem. If people are experincing player-cast spells (rather than just songs) still breaking hide and sneak, I'll see if I can get that repair as part of the same project.

  • Best Level to Stop Playing
    [Quote from original post: You camp one spawn for hours until you make a level or two and have to move to the next higher spawn. And with every level the time you need to spend camping the same spawn becomes longer and longer]

    If that's how you're playing (e.g. camping for experinece) you might consider modifying your playstyle. While camping for expereince is often considered the "fastest" way to level, it's also widely accepted as a boring way to play the game. MMOGs are flexible in that many different playstyles are supported, even those that are not as fulfilling. Choosing to play the game in that manner, however, does indeed remain a choice.

    I wouldn't be one to tell you that you are playing the wrong way, as there are indeed players who enjoy playing in the style you've illustrated. However if I may be so bold, you do not appear to be one of them. I suggest that you consider making friends, moving around a bit, and by all means, adventuring. Try new and exciting things, rather than giving in to the urge to sit in one place and mindlessly level.


Discussion of the Week: Expectations
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:30 PM PDT
Gordon started a new Discussion of the Week this evening on the topic of expectations:

    Ok, it's been a while since we had one of these, but reading the posts lately I thought it might be a good time to discuss expectations.

    It's been surmised by many gurus in the MMOG world that the most important job of a game developer is to manage expectations. MMOGs are fairly new in the grand scheme of computer games, and I think that there are still many people who do not know what to expect, or have expectations that could be considered "unreasonable" on a large scale, while seeming perfectly "reasonable" to the individual.

    To date there has not been an MMOG market leader that has had "good customer service" from the perspective of many of the people playing the game. Yet when these games are no longer the market leader, their customer service image somehow improves. Is this indicative of policy changes in regards to no longer being the market leader? I don't think so. I think that overall the policies remain the same, and that those who are left once a game leaves the limelight have expectations more inline with what is considered realistic by the people playing the game.

    The expectations of those playing EverQuest are as diverse as the makeup of the player base. We have people who are brand new to MMOGs, those that are veterans, and those that are somewhat in between. Customer service, however, must keep in mind that service provided to one player does not only affect that player in the MMOG environment, but everyone else, either directly or indirectly as well. For instance, a policy to give items whenever a claim of loss is presented can have a dramatic effect upon the game economy, and the game overall, if abused.

    No, we do not think that all players are liars like has been suggested in the past, however the question is, what percentage does it take to ruin a game? Many of the complaints received by GMs, and on this board as well, are rooted in player interaction. Soandso stole a kill, or ninja-looted, or cheated in a transaction, or harassed, zonecamped, bindcamped, hurled profanity, etc. With the number of player-interaction related complaints, is it not reasonable to assume that the percentage of those who take advantage of a situation is high enough to have a negative impact upon the game if those same people’s actions are rewarded? I believe that is a reasonable assumption, so why does the expectation exist?

    While I’ve used an example regarding expectations and customer service, it’s not limited solely to that. Expectations regarding the game exist in every facet. Some people expect to be able to acquire every item, complete every quest, to be the center of the world, the hero, and to in short, win the game. While these are good goals, the same expectations are often shared by others as well. In fact, if one person can achieve all that’s been mentioned, it’s very easy for all or a large number of people to have those same expectations. What happens when two people’s expectations conflict directly with each other? What happens when it’s 200K people who all have conflicting expectations? Can we make everyone the center of the world? No, because the center would become quite crowded. All we can do is attempt to manage the expectation, and provide content that encourages people to be satisfied or modify their expectations in a direction that does not conflict with the expectations of others.

    Meeting everyone’s expectations are the domain of the single player game. They are designed to make the player the hero or the center of the world. In single player games, one person “cheating” affects no one else. It’s even considered normal since virtually every game out there comes out with “cheat codes”, the use of which carries no stigma. Everyone is equal, because what I do on my PC in the privacy of my own home has no bearing on what you do on yours. You do not exist in my world, nor I in yours.

    Take class issues as an example. Suppose we have 4 classes, A, B, C, and D. (note: I’m avoiding actual class names to try and keep things on track). If A > B > C > D, A is perfectly happy with its relation to all others, while B is happy in regards to C and D, while C is happy in regards to D, and D is unhappy. Elevating D to the point that D > A upsets the other 3 classes, while pleasing D. This process can continue ad infinitum. Of course the easy solution would be to try and come up with A=B=C=D, however “equality” cannot be achieved without truly making all of the classes identical, at which point we would not have classes. If there are going to be differences, which is of course our goal, there will always be those with the opinion that the differences themselves are not equal. That is of course why we strive for balance, but while “equality” is a statement of fact, “balance” is an opinion that we have.

    I’ve used two examples, one of customer service and the other of class balance, to illustrate situations in which expectations can and do conflict. I don’t want to turn this into a class balance or customer service discussion, but would like to keep on the subject of expectations in all facets of the game. I normally end my opening post of one of these threads with a question, and the obvious question here is “What are your expectations?” However, I think that that question would yield replies along the lines of “I expect to be able to find a group”, or “I expect to be rezed if I die to a bug.” But this question isn’t that simple, and all I can ask when you reply is that you keep in mind that for every expectation that you have, there’s someone else with an equal and opposite expectation.

    There’s no perfect formula for meeting or exceeding expectations. Be certain that if there was, we would certainly employ it. Just try to look at your expectations not on the individual scale, but on that of the game as a whole. What happens if your expectation becomes policy? What happens if your expectation is applied to everyone playing the game? Are any one person’s expectations right? Is anyone wrong? I think that the answer lies somewhere in the middle. It’s not black and white or right or wrong. It’s not that simple.

    -Gordon


Official Board Rule Reminder
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:17 PM PDT
Gordon reminded us of the rules Verant enforces on the official board system in the following thread he started on the Gameplay Board:

    Hello all,

    I've been a little disappointed at the reception that's been given thus far to Absor. Rampant and insults seem to have returned, meaning that we're going to have to again ramp up the enforcement of the rules again. We've let things slide a bit over the past week, and cannot allow it to continue. We will not let these boards once again deteriorate to what they once were back before we added accountability to the posts people make here.

    I haven't had as much time as normal to spend reading and responding to issues here given my new responsibilities, but I'm going to make the time to come back and help move things along while Absor comes up to speed. Please be aware that we will continue to enforce the board rules, isible under the FAQ at the link at the top of the page.

    Common courtesy and decency are not concepts reserved for us as a company towards you as the players. Continued flaming and insults are not acceptable content here on the boards. We want Absor to be more of a "man of the people" than I was able to be, because that is what the players have indicated that they want, but please remember that this is a two way street, and only you can make this happen.

    Thank you,

    -Gordon
Here's post #2:
    I'm not necessarily saying that anyone is giving Absor a hard time, just that he's been searching for positive/courteous/well-written posts to reply to, and that it's been exceedingly difficult because someone always seems to come in and mess them up with flames.

    Flames don't help anyone.

    -Gordon
Here's post #3:
    Making decisions that are contrary with the opinions of certain groups of players does not indicate a lack of respect, which I believe is where most of the complaints along those lines come from. Sometimes we have to make those decisions in the best interest of the players overall.

    People will not continue to play the game if it becomes too easy, advancement becomes a joke, or if there are rampant imbalances. They play the game to be challenged, and if we do not provide that challenge they will go elsewhere. Doing what we need to to maintain that challenge is not a lack of respect, it's good business.

    In any case, I don't want to get into a chicken and egg argument. If you feel that you've been disrespected, I'll accept that you do indeed feel that way and apologize for whatever it is that caused you to feel that way. The point however remains that flames, insults, and disrespect have no place on these boards, and we'll need everyone's help in that area.

    -Gordon

News From Absor
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:00 PM PDT
Alan announced some news regarding future patches and changes coming in the month of November in the following announcement posted to the Gameplay Board:

    Hello all,

    I have some news for you.

    Some time during the first week of November we will be deleting all guilds with less than ten members. Remember, these members must be from separate active accounts. We will be deleting guilds with less than ten members on a monthly basis after that.

    As another reminder, there is a new step required for the creation of new guilds. When the guild leader goes into the game to talk to a GM about the final steps for creating the guild (step 7 of the process listed on the Official EverQuest site), please be aware that you will be required to have at least ten members of the guild on line for final guild creation. The official EverQuest web site will be updated in the next few days to reflect this new requirement for guild creation.

    I also have news about the next patch. We will be patching on Tuesday, October 31, 2000. Because this will be a large patch, we will be starting earlier than usual and will take longer to complete. I do not have an exact start time or duration for this patch yet. Here are a few of the things that will be in the patch:

    An update for the data structures to allow for more in-depth quests.

    The DirectX 7a update.

    An update that should reduce the amount of NPC warping.

    An improvement to the movelog function that should reduce the chances of requests being dropped from the logs.

    NOTE: After this patch, you must DirectX 7a installed to play EverQuest.

    There will be more information in the patch message.

    Some of the changes we are making could use some stress testing. If you have time before the next patch, please take a few minutes and play on the test server a bit, we would like to see how these updates react when the servers are under full load. Please take special note of any video or sound related problems and send a detailed note to eqtesting@verant.com.

    For those of you that have not played on the Test Server before, you can access it by running the program called Testeverquest.exe. You can find it in your EverQuest directory.

    Alan
Here's a follow-up:
    More in-depth how? Like just more items to fetch for Mr. Quest NPC? Or what?

    Basically, there will be more ways that an NPC can react, and more things that they can react to. I can’t go into greater detail than that.

    So another week with several epic quests diabled? Epic quests that we were promised up and down were working in the first place....

    I’ve just checked, and the epic quests should all be enabled and ready to go after the patch.

    And as for guilds…

    We had to set a size limit for guilds. The rule has always been ten people to form a guild. We probably should have been enforcing this more stringently from the start, but there were other priorities. Too many guilds does cause a performance problem, and forcing people to stick to the rules should solve that problem.

    If you are forming a guild and need to meet the ten member on-line requirement, if you can get them together during the hours that the GM is on-line for your server, just petition. A guide can verify the ten people and they can take that information to the GM. Your guild members can leave after that. So you do not have to wait for the GM that is actually going to do the guild creation, so your wait time shouldn’t be too long.

    If you cannot organize your guild to gather during the regularly scheduled time for the GM of your server, you can contact eqguilds@station.sony.com. We will work with you to make your guild.

    DIRECTX 7.0A!?!?!

    We are testing with Windows 2000 and it is working fine with DirectX 7.0. From what I have been told there is no significant difference between 7.0 and 7.0a as far as EverQuest is concerned.

    Alan

About DataRate.txt
[13 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:35 PM PDT
Gordon spoke about the datarate.txt file found on the EverQuest client in a recent response posted to the Gameplay Board:

    Please can I have the number into this file explained to me?

    Someone stated that it would help with dumps to Servers Selection Screen with cable modems.

    I have the same problem.

    It's a way for you to tell our server how fast you can accept data. The default setting of 5 is roughly where it should be for a 28.8K modem.
    If you are running something faster than than, feel free to bump it up. If you start having system problems, getting knocked off, or anything of the sort, bump it down a notch.

    I ran 9 on a 512K DSK connection without a problem. Your miliage may vary. Don't set it above 9 or below 3.

    The main benefit of bumping it up is faster zoning, and less of a chance of getting kicked off in high-bandwidth encounters (such as dragon raids).

    -Gordon

    I doubt it.
