Thursday, 10 May, 2007


  Multi-Tasking Players  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
Zatozia has a poll up on EQPlayers that asks, what do you do while you are playing EverQuest?

If you look at the current results, just a little over half of the people that responded are doing something else -- either watching TV or a movie, or even working!

37% responded that they don't do anything else while playing.

She didn't include listening to music, probably because it's not nearly as distracting? I personally listen to music when playing, and I am sure a lot of other people do as well.

It seems, though, that many players find that just playing doesn't require their full attention, hence the addition of TV or movie watching. What does this say about the game? Or about us, the players? Too much down time? Too much repitition that doesn't require absolute attention from the player? Or just people that like to have lots going on at the same time? The answers to the poll might be just as interesting as the poll itself.




-- Kwill @ 1:24PM Thu 10 May 2007

  The Buried Sea Wrap Report  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
RPG Vault has published a really sweet report with three members of Sony's team talking about the latest expansion, which takes us to a lost city beneath the waves. Go check it out...
During the creative and brainstorming phase of this project, we had two primary goals based on the feedback we'd been receiving from our player base. One was to offer fun and interesting content for casual players, and the other to offer challenging single group and raid content for our advanced ones. Our need to accomplish these two goals mixed very well with our ideas to bring two overall themes to life. One was pirates, and the other was the Combine in an underwater theme that revolved around some interesting characters from EQ's history. We also wanted to address the Mayong Mistmoore story arc that's been alive and well for some time.

  Google Ad Help  
Print News | Category: Community News
The Vault Network is asking the community to help us preserve our "Don't Destroy the Game" philosophy and mantra. Our parent company IGN has partnered with the good guys over at Google to run its "Google Ads" across all of IGN's sites, which includes the Vaults. With that comes the chance of Google running a gold seller-type ad that might slip by the existing filter we help set up to ensure these ads are blocked. Because we told Google that these gold farmer type ads suck and don't belong on the Vault Network. They were more than happy to work with us to keep such ads off our sites. So, we will be watching to make sure we remove any that happen to slip through and muck up our stuff!

There is a gold lining to this situation (pun intended!). We have a system in place where any community member can submit an ad they might see via a simple and easy web form. You only need to provide one of two pieces of information: Name of Ad and/or the Link for the Ad -- that's it. Click here to learn more and where to submit.
