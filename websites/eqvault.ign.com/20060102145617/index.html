EQ Classic Style Group Forming on Prexus  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
Despite the fact that I might have poked a little fun at the "bring back a classic EQ server" peeps, I see that some folks are giving a "classic-style" play mode a try. If you are interested in playing like it was back in the early days of EQ (old world zones, no mad twinking, no power leveling, no use of the PoK books and so forth), you can join them:

Deadcool posted on EQLive:

We are doing it on Prexus Server. Many of us have discussed it, and that's what we came up with. It's a long road ahead, you guys. This is not going to be easy, but perhaps, it will prove our desire to SoE, and even if it doesn't, we will definitely have a lot of fun hanging out in the old world zones.

We are going to start new characters, then get the heck out of the mines of Gloomingdeep, as quickly as possible. We will all begin working out of Western Antonica. That is, Freeport, The Commonlands, Nektulos/Neriak, The Desert of Ro, and eventually we will move inland toward the Karanas, and so forth. Start your new char on Prexus, and begin playing legit, as in, no twinking. If you must give yourself a slight edge, that's acceptable, but don't show up at level 5 with level 20 equip. Just a few pieces of coinage to get your spells, and maybe a few pieces of decent-for-your-level gear.

You may begin soloing if you can't get any kind of group together. West Freeport is good for the first few levels, and East Commons is great for soloing, until about level 10 or so. By that time, we will have a lot more organization.

As soon as you start your new character on Prexus, log out of Everquest and come here, post your name on the Prexus server, so that we can all get a hold of each other.


I wish you all the best of luck in giving this a try.

Known Issues with EQPlayers.com  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
The new service SOE has unrolled, EQPlayers.com, is now live. Baelish has posted some known issues and general information about the service on the EQLive website.

Greg "Baelish" explains: "As a general note, once again I'd like to re-iterate if your character is not showing correct information please run the /eqplayersupdate command, give it an hour or so and then if you still have issues please email us.

Also, another reminder that EverQuest has NO database as a game. The tools to collect the hundreds of files, run the game logic against them to then generate the information we display is very complex. The game team and web team have tried hard to ensure accuracy, but it's entirely possible that there are small things that aren't quite right. If you let us know about them (you have a lot more eyes than we do!) we'll be sure to get them straightened out.

As always if you have anything you consider a bug or feedback for how we can make the service better please email eqplayersqa@soe.sony.com."

Downtime Explanation  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
Greetings Folks!

As I am sure you have noticed we had to do a quick update again this afternoon to fix the bug with NPC spell casting. Let me step back and try to give a more thorough explanation of why we ended up with the amount of downtime we had this week.

EQPlayers is a huge separate but connected system to the EverQuest game. Because of this there is a lot of messaging going on and the amount of traffic is dependent on the amount of information that is being dealt with. The consequence of this is that some bugs did not become apparent until we hooked up the system to all of the live servers. Although EQPlayers has not been available for you this week it has been running with all of the live data. Even more unfortunately, some of the leaks and crashes were not seen until the other ones were discovered – much like the layers of an onion. This is what has caused the repeated updates. Although not immediately obvious, this morning’s update dealing with the NPC spell casting bug is also EQPlayers related. As you all know EverQuest is a large and complicated system.

This being said I am not making excuses but I wanted to communicate the reasons. I do believe that EQPlayers is a great addition to the game and will be a great service to all of you. We do strive to maintain a stable and reliable service to our customers and I hope you agree that this week has been the exception and not the rule.

Once again, please accept my apologies for this week’s downtime.

Chris Lena ~ Raghnell

SONY ONLINE ENTERTAINMENT ANNOUNCES EVERQUEST® Titanium
AN ALL-INCLUSIVE COLLECTION OF EVERQUEST

SOE Also Launches New EverQuest Players Service

SAN DIEGO, CA – December 12, 2005 Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming, announced today that it will be releasing EverQuest® Titanium, an all-inclusive collection consisting of EverQuest Classic and the first ten expansions. EverQuest Titanium carries a suggested retail price of $19.99 and will be available at retail beginning January 11, 2006. In addition to the release of EverQuest Titanium, SOE will launch EQ Players, a website that will enhance the services provided to EQ players.

EverQuest Titanium offers the original classic game, as well as all ten expansion packs: The Ruins of Kunark®, The Scars of Velious®, The Shadows of Luclin®, The Planes of Power®, The Legacy of Ykesha™, Lost Dungeons of Norrath™, Gates of Discord™, Dragons of Norrath™, Omens of War™ and Depths of Darkhollow™.

"EverQuest Titanium will allow players to experience more than 375 areas of exploration and excitement in the online world of Norrath," said Chris Sturr, Sr. Brand Manager, Sony Online Entertainment. "This five-disk collection is essential for both classic EverQuest players and those interested in entering into the EverQuest experience for the first time as it will include in game items such as a collapsible roboboar and a frozen crystal shard." (Please note: items are only claimable in one satchel for one player character.)

In addition to EverQuest Titanium, SOE is launching EQ Players, a website designed to extend the player's interaction with the game outside of the game world. This new service gives players more ways to access, track and expand their in-game characters by obtaining character statistics, profiles, item information, in-game activities and more all in one place. The website, www.eqplayers.com, will launch on December 15, 2005, and will have profiles for more than 4.9 million characters and more than 335 million total inventory items. EQ Players will be available for EverQuest players to try, at no additional cost, until December 29, 2005, after which time some EQ Players features will carry a monthly fee. In addition, as a special offer, SOE will reactivate previously closed EverQuest accounts for 21 days of free gameplay but the former EverQuest players must take advantage of this offer before January 13, 2006.

  Guk Monster Mission Changed  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
For those of you who might have tried the Guk Monster Mission, "Train!" in the past, it has been significantly changed since the patch (without a notice in the patch message). Pre-December 8, the mission could be completed by one or two people training the Guk intruders to the zone. In fact, it was probably the most popular AA generating "/afk" monster mission out there, since it could be soloed with all other group members AFK. After the patch, the mission was supposedly the same, but with the addition of every group member being required to loot a shard from the chest that dropped the mission loot. After the December 9 patch, more changes were introduced, making the mission a completely different encounter. Currently the mission is geared toward an entire group fighting the Guk intruders, according to Developer Celrak, who posted this update December 9 on EQLive:

"I'm sure many of you realize that this mission was not intended to be done solo. In lieu of this, a few changes were made to ensure that you need the better part of a group to take on the different enemies you fight during the mission. The mission still functions in a similar fashion, but you may find that you need to beat these NPCs up a bit more than in the past to get them to flee your home of Lower Guk. Additional changes have been made in regards to your Froglok regeneration (hp/mana/endurance), starting hitpoints, mana, and various spells which should be seen in the next update."

Unfortunately, people playing the mission posting to the EQLive forum thread on this topic are stating that it is almost impossible to complete as it works currently. So if you choose to start this mission, please be aware that it is much more difficult to complete.

Legends Service To Be Discontinued  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
EQLive has posted that the premium Legends service will be discontinued as of February 1, 2006.

The Legends service is offered on the Stormhammer server. The letter states that it has not been decided whether Stormhammer will become a regular server or players will be moved to other existing servers. The letter states the decision will be partly up to the community. A poll will be posted shortly in game that will allow subscribers to choose whether Stormhammer will be shut down or kept as a regular server.

Stormhammer has been floundering for population for quite some time now. This decision will probably come as no surprise to the players who subscribe to the premium service, which promised more GM attention and more events on the server for a higher subscription fee.

Comments on Holiday Events  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
The holiday events have a problem: the shared task given out by MC Tinkerton actually only gives one person the task, instead of the group.

Oshran wrote: "The fact that only one player in a group can have the mission at a time is not intended. This should be a normal group task where up to 6 players can work together. The problem is already fixed on test and should be fixed on the live servers asap (likely within the next few days)."

Kytherea posted later in the thread on EQLive "This should be fixed tonight."

People also were irritated that anyone can loot the chests that spawn from completing the task.

Kytherea posted, "There should be multiple chests spawning as a result of completing this event, and yes the chests are available to everyone."

Oshran commented on EQLive:

"The party favor chests that spawn on mission complete are for anyone in the area. Think of it as a piñata. The New Year gemstones (which are much nicer than anything you'll find in the free party favor chests) go only to the individual(s) who complete the quest. "

Some people complained that the Bristlebane Party Platter prize was unfair to tradeskillers who worked hard to get the skill to make this item.

Rashere added:

The [Bristlebane's Party Platter] BBPP (neat acryonym) is only a 2% spawn chance on the chests. It's the uber-rare best of the best present the machine gives out.

As to the FFA loot...its a holiday event. Sharing, caring, feel good happiness...the party machine is spreading joy and presents to everyone around when someone finishes the quest. The person who actually completes the event gets a totally different prize.
