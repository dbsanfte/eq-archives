Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:33 PM PST
Proving that no illness can keep a good man down, Gordon Wrinn was back on the boards today responding to the concerns that the players of EverQuest have raised. Here's his latest from the Gameplay board:

    Spell Feature Request
    [Quote from original post: Feature: Allow the ability to cast spells directly from spellbook, with certain circumstances, and at a large casting time delay penalty (a penalty similar to the meditation time of the spell, or more, would be appropriate). ]

    The spell-system of EQ is built around memorization, and would not easily adopt changes to the core architecture such as this. Also, the effort required to implement something along these lines wouldn't really return any advantage substantial enough to justify it, since any net-gain in time or delay would have to be zero in order to maintain balance. In other words, even if we did it, the penalty associated with casting directly from the spell-book would have to be equal to the time it would take to forget a spell, mem/cast/forget a new spell, and re-mem the first one.

    Warrior Vision
    [Quote from original post: Verants Vision of warriors was to make them the one class that was totally dependant on other classes.]

    That's a pretty half-empty way to look at the glass, coming from the class that is considered the most valuable in the game by most people I've seen polled. Of course that means "most valuable to the group", which if it is the "group" part of that that you disagree with, I think anyone could have told you in the first days of EverQuest that Warriors weren't exactly designed to be solo.

    Corpse Issues
    [Quote from original post: Gordon, you have stated that Verant's opposition to a /drag command (drag corpse without looting rights) is that you want people to develop "trust" within the game. Yet, we constantly see server banner messages that shout "Trust NOBODY!!! Don't give ANYBODY your password!!!".]

    Passwords (out-of-game) and corpses (in-game) are two separate things, so the argument really doesn't apply.

    Here's the deal. If you consent the wrong person, he's going to steal from you. Don't do that, and if you do, and he does, then report him to a GM.

    You are fully in control of who you give consent to, and always have been. The /corpse ability (dragging) is relatively new to EverQuest, and was put in to help people get their corpses out of inaccessible areas without having to wait for a GM/Guide. At this point, it works well for that use and we decline to invest further time into it.

    Stating it as plainly as possible, we consider the issue closed.

    Pet Bug?
    [Quote from original message: Anyone notice how when you kill the master of the pet, the pet doesnt also die like our pets do?]

    It's not a bug, but rather another case where NPCs do not follow the same rules that PCs do, such as not being able to drown, fall for damage, etc.


Aradune Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:27 PM PST
EverQuest's Producer, the tireless Brad McQuaid, has been on the Gameplay Board addressing a wide range of issues pertaining to EverQuest. Here's a rundown of the posts he's made over the last 24 hours:

    Beyond EverQuest
    About as specific as I can get at this point is that the EQ Live team will be revamping existing zones and then building some new ones. We still have the Hole coming, and then some zones after that (there's quite a few areas we can and hopefully will flesh out on Odus, Antonica, and Faydwer -- you can see them if you have any old maps about).

    Beyond that? Lots of other EQ related stuff is coming, but it's too early to say much, unfortunately. Suffice it to say Verant and Sony are VERY committed to the EQ franchise and are in it for the long haul.

    Overcrowding and Level'ing
    Just an FYI, people did not level faster than we thought they would. They're pretty much right on schedule. MOST players are 25-35. We KNEW hard core power gamers would be ahead of the curve -- if you look up the old posts by me about how long it would take a player to get to level 50 I used the words AVERAGE player.

    The overcrowding origin lies more in more units sold than thought and WAY less people quitting than we thought, plus some issues in terms of finding places to put servers and getting enough bandwidth to support the rapid growth.

    Brad on GM Events
    [Editor's note: This was in response to a thread wanting people to sign if they wanted more GM events]
    I definitely want more GM events too!

    bump!

    On Diablo II
    Just wanted to say that I'm definitely going to play Diablo 2 (I can't wait -- hurry up Blizzard!) but not cancel my EQ account :)

    On Warriors
    Not gonna give Warriors self-healing... not gonna do it... dead horse with a high MR here (no ressurecting!).
    --
    The reason is our Vision(TM) of a Warrior (which certainly isn't original, but rather is quite traditional): They hit stuff with weapons, and they get hit.

    They're not supposed to have any magic skills.

    They are supposed to be dependent on others for healing, magical protection, travel, etc.

    It's all part of the class interdependence that is key to the game. Giving Warriors the ability to heal themselves beyond Bind Wounds would, in my opinion, blur the distinction between classes and harmfully homogenize class balance.

    Bottom line, if you want to melee and still cast spells, play a hybrid.

    Why Can't Verant Admit Their Wrong?
    Wrong? We're wrong on lots of things, we make errors, we underestimate, etc. etc. EQ is a very big learning experience for us, to say the least -- there's no manuals on how to do this stuff and OSI ain't talking :)

    I don't think we've ever not acknowledged that we're wrong when we think we are, nor been afraid or reluctant to apologize (alchemy! :))

    We listen too, as I posted elsewhere -- that's why we're here posting, and that's why you see changes IN-GAME based on feedback on message boards and such.

    I'm sure you can find instances (dunno about a bazillion :)) where you think we were wrong and we don't or where we didn't change something some people thought we should, but I can also find examples of the opposite (not a bazillion either :))

    Brad's Complete Charcter Name
    My complete name and title is:
    Aradune Mithara, Ranger Lord, Outrider of Karana

    The Plan
    The current plan is to turn on the Expansion a couple of days max before commercial release (April 24th).

    At this point I can't say when people will get their upgrades, but our intent is obviously before the 24th.

    We've already started the downtime to upgrade our servers for the Expansion -- we are taking down approximately 2 servers each business day for a time, upgrading them, and then bringing them back online.

    On Trade Skills
    I definitely think trade skills could be enhanced quite a bit, especially at the higher levels. We plan on doing so, too, but it will probably take us a while :(

    Rangers Are Not Happy
    What? How can this be? Everyone says that you're my pet class and all I do is pamper you guys (because I play a ranger myself, btw.)

    Et tu, Brutus?

    Kunark Zones
    No, no, there are all sorts of zones on Kunark, from newbie zones all the way up to 50+ zones. There should be stuff to do on Kunark for all levels, don't worry.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:17 PM PST
Verant's Gordon Wrinn has been posting up a storm today on the Gameplay board. Here's a rundown of some of today's posts:

    Making Rare Items "Uber" Lore
    This was suggested under another named called 'Lore for Life' some time back, but I think I like UBER-LORE better :)We discussed it, and unfortunately it's not possible given our current architecture. We'll certainly keep it in mind for future products though.

    On Wizards
    There have been a ton of responses, in various forums, in response to wizards concerns. Over two dozen in fact. The ones who have seen them just didn't like the response. Basically, there's nothing wrong with wizards. There's no complaint that they've made which can't be solved by being in a well-balanced group.

    The posts in the wizard threads are pretty much the same over all. Here's a list of the standard requests:

    1. Give us Self-Clarity
    2. Give us Pets
    3. Make us get resisted less
    4. Remove the Spell Damage Cap
    5. Raise our Mana/Damage Ratio
    6. Give us a DD spell of each type at each circle.
    7. Make AoE spells not suck.

    It's a wonderful wish list, but that's what it is. Just like the wish lists that every other class has. Now we are going to have a new type of spell for the Wizard as part of the expansion, but that's only being done as a benefit, not to correct any deficiency with the class.

    On Augment Death
    How on earth does making the Augment death spell last 15 minutes for 100 additional mana affect mages whatsoever, let along do so at the "expense" of mages. You can't buff necro pets. And even if you are comparing the final burnout spell with augment death, your spell still costs less mana and does more.

    On "Switching Languages Faster" Suggestion
    I saw this idea suggested last week, and I've forwarded it to the guy who does that kind of thing. It's not on the programming list yet, but we'll see how it goes.

    New Servers and Splits
    Yes, [Druzzil Ro was the new server promised after Luclin was launched] that's the one we meant. The split was successful enough that new servers will probably be splits as well for the foreseeable future.

    More about Druzzil Ro
    The arrival of Druzzil Ro allowed us to collect a good deal of data that should help any future splits go off without a hitch. As such, future servers will most likely be used for splits to some extent.

    Bugs and Hell Levels
    Given my time here at Verant, I think I can finally articulate this in a more accurate manner. I think "bug" is probably not the correct term to describe why hell levels are there. "Mathematical Consequence" would probably be a better description. Trying to put it in easy terms:

    Basically, everyone knows that the experience needed at every level is a curve. Well, it's not so much a curve, as it is a series of straight lines. As you level, the "curve" gets steeper. However, due to the formula used, in the first level after the curve gets steeper, you must "make up" all of the experience as if the curve had been that steep all along. As such, a "Hell Level" is the first level where a new experience curve took affect. Ideally, it would have been nice if the formula spread the additional experience required over all the levels of the next range, rather than requiring it all in one level, but the outcome is the same.

    As for 50+, it's probably safe to say that experience required in those levels won't be on the same curve as 45-50, and as such, any time the curve steepens, the "Hell Level Phenomenon" will be present for the first level of the new curve.

    Basically, this is just the way it is, and we won't be readjusting the experience curves. They haven't been changed since release, and no one ever said that progression would be linear.

    Blackmail
    I saw a comical parody of a Get Rich Quick scheme on TV the other day. It had your typical mid-20s guy who says something along the lines of "Just 3 short years ago I was waiting tables, but now I'm rich! Just send me $50 for my book, 'How to get people to send you $50 for a book'".

    Anyway friend, we don't do blackmail.

    [Statement claiming Verant bans people for reporting bugs and exploits] This is simply not true. We embrace people who report exploits, we don't ban them.

    Cygne1, can you please stop spreading this B.S.?

    Why is Invisbility so Random?
    To keep you on your toes.

    [Responding to a comment asking for the spell to be changed back the way it was] There's no "way it was" to put it back to. It's no different then it was in mid-beta when its duration was randomized.


The Vision
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:16 PM PST
Gordon Wrinn addressed the vision of EverQuest in a response he made to a recent post on the Gameplay board. Here's the thread:

    What would it take to demonstrate a flaw in the Vision

    The Vision(tm) has flaws, sure. We never said it was perfect, but we do believe it is in the best interest of the game, and fulfills its goal quite well.

    What would be necessary for you to question and possibly adjust the Vision of EQ?

    We adjust our vision as necessary to fit the circumstances at hand. Much like the game itself, our vision must be dynamic and flexible, thus capable of change.

    If you took a poll about a vision issue, and 99% of your customers said "you are wrong" would that do it?

    I think it would be fair to say that 99% of our customers think we are wrong about *something*, but that doesn't make those issues a deal-breaker. How specific would your question be? If the question was "Do you think your class is powerful enough?", what percentage would answer "no"? I think you'd get probably about 70% no, 30% yes to that question. I can't really think of a question that would garner 99% support of nearly 200K people.

    But ok, if we are hypothesizing, if 99% of the customer-base were to suggest one specific change, we'd seriously consider it. Of course, we frequently implement changes proposed by 1 player as well, so it's not to say that there's some "support ceiling" that must be hit before we consider your suggestions.

    If you hired professional unbiased playtesters to assess the game and they advised non-vision-compliant changes, would that do it?

    No one person makes decisions about the game here. Sure, we have people (Like Brad) who function as the vision-keepers, but they don't dictate unilaterally. Those "professional unbiased playtesters" would have just as much of a say as anyone else.

    If the decision-makers themselves played the game on live servers and found that some aspect of the vision was making for lots of unhappiness, would that do it?

    The decision-makers, the 15 or so people on the tuning team, do play the game. Some of us are complete EQ fanatics. They have as much input into the game as anyone else on the team.

    If players put together carefully reasoned arguments, backed them up with detailed statistics, and comparisons to other successful games, would that do it?

    This happens all the time. The suggestions that come from places such as this board don't magically appear in game. We discuss them and decide what is or is not good for the game. If it is good, we weigh cost versus benefit, and then decide whether or not to implement the suggestion based on that.

    There's no shortage of suggestions out there. We get in excess of 10K suggestions every day, with at least a few hundred being pretty much unique.     On Overcrowding
    I'm reposting something I wrote tonight in responce to another thread in order to make sure no one misses it. The thread is/was about overcrowding and the math involved in determining how many servers we need and such:

    ------

    Actually, I think the math is as follows:

    (a) Target peak usage per server : 1800
    (b) Current number of servers : 28
    (c) Average total users online during peak hours : 50,000

    And I'll also add some additional but not necessarily relevant to the math data, which we've released before and which continues to grow quite rapidly:

    (d) Current number of subscribers: 196,000

    So, that means about 26% of our player base plays during peak hours. 25-30% is, btw, pretty typical for MMORPGs. I just wanted to go over that briefly as well since I've seen all sorts of formulas and calculations posted in overcrowding discussions.

    Anyway, back to the relevant math:

    c / a = 27.7, being the number of servers we'd need to have to maintain Target peak usage per server.

    Now, before everyone gets too excited or upset at this math, there are definitely a few issues here to go over:

    First, the peak usage per server is NOT equal. Some servers go over 2000 while some average 1200 or so.

    Second, one can argue the 1800 target number in a couple of different ways.

    When we first started thinking about target server capacities (pre-beta) we said somewhere between 1000 and 1500. As we entered beta and these numbers became less theoretical we changed our target to 1500. Then, as the game matured after launch and the level spread grew (meaning there are lower level players, mid, and high, spread out over varying zones, depending generally on the intended level ranges of those zones) we changed our target peak usage number to approximately 1800.

    But there are some problems with that, of course. First, when we launch a new server, it tends to fill up with, of course, newbie players (in terms of their level). This means low-level zones are full and mid to high level zones empty. So you have worse crowding until people spread out level-wise. Second, you could simply debate whether 1800 on a mature server is really a good number.

    In principal I think it is IF people are really spread out. And, of course, they're not. Some dungeons and outdoor areas have a LOT more players than others even though both have similar target level ranges.

    So, this is why that even if you do the correct math (which I believe I've done up above), which concludes with 28 servers we're just about where we should be, there is still definitely a crowding problem.

    I will reiterate our plan to deal with the problem:

    1. Launch at least one more server and fill it with players from the more populated servers. These players will be taken from the group that plays during peak hours so that we don't pull people who play during off peak-hours and risk creating an under-population problem. Looking at the numbers right now I think the Druzzil Ro endeavor was fairly successful: Fennin Ro has 1670 players right now (with about 47K online), while Bristlebane (a server we didn't pull players from) has 1867.

    2. Continue to re-tune and revamp underutilized zones. We need to entice our players, compelling them to spread out to many of the zones that just aren't used much (for example, Najena, Permafrost, Mistmoore, Kerra Ridge, etc.)

    3. Launch the Expansion which will increase the size of the world by over 33% and add more than 25 new zones (all with different level spreads, ranging from newbie zones all the way up to level 40-60 zones).

    So, in summary, that's our plan and that's how we're looking at the situation and how to address it. Your feedback and comments are welcome.

    [This was part of a server wide broadcast from Aradune on Friday, March 26, 1999.]

Gathering II Report

The first report of the Gathering II, the real-life event that took place in St. Louis last Saturday, has been posted by MakeItSimple.com. The most interesting pages cover the Demo of Ruins of Kunark and the Q&A with Verant Interactive. Here's a few excepts:

    Graphic Enchancements
    The graphic enhancements were the first thing we noticed, and while not making it a whole new game, they're pretty impressive. Screenshots can reveal the increased poly-counts and texture sizes, but once you actually see the game in real-time, you get a full understanding of the enhanced graphic features. New animation effects are evident in objects such as trees that sway in the wind, or the shuffling of the Forest Giant's vest as he moves about.

    GZ Keeps His Cool
    We also managed to catch Geoff Zatnik (GZ), who designed the EQ spell database among other things, talking in-depth about current issues and upcoming features. He was given a particularly hard time by a certain attendee who felt that the Wizard class was underpowered. Despite this person's repeated complaints and attacks, not once did Geoff lose his cool, tactfully addressing each and every point with a rational explanation. His dialogue was a real testament to the professionalism and expertise of the Verant staff.

    Hell Levels
    Although they wouldn't confirm this, they did say that every level after 50 would be the equivalent of a "hell level". Currently, the "hell levels" are 30, 35, 40, and 45, which take considerably longer to advance through than the other levels.

Gordon Infobits
[Post a Comment] - Aurum @ 6:50 PM PST
Gordon Wrinn, of Verant Interactive, was busy on the boards today. Here are quotes and excerpts from his latest posts:

    Up'ing the group limit to 8
    I think that this would tend to exacerbate the problem more than solve it. My *pet* idea for getting people to spread out would be to make heavily camped zones dynamically turn PvP when too many people are in them. Somehow I don't think the tuning team, not to mention the customers, would go for that one.

    NPC Binding
    Being honest with you Krux, the idea of making NPCs so they can Bind PCs is really a dead horse. I've seen all kinds of reasonable proposals, but the truth remains that limited melee binding is not an oversight, but part of the plan.

    Languages
    I'll forward this to the client programmer. I think it would make a bit more sense for the language to be changed with a "/language" command that takes the language name as a parameter. It might make a nice change if his schedule frees up for a few hours.

    Monk Issues
    Someone sent me an email yesterday outlining some pretty compelling issues with some of the monk stuff. Specifically mentioned was the fact that a lot of the pre-plane stuff has better stats than shiverback. I'm looking this info over now.

    As for AC in general, Monk pieces will always be pretty low in AC due to the fact that monks get an AC bonus just for being a monk. But I do think that the plane gear should probably be better than the mundane stuff.

    Kunark Pricing
    Discounted compared to the cost of buying a brand new boxed set.

    But if the complaint in general is that people who have been playing for a year will pay more money for EQ+expansion than new players will pay for EQ+expansion, this is true, and an acceptable standard in business.

    Analogies work best for this:

    1. Take for instance if you buy a brand new car as soon as the model-year is available. You'll pay more than someone who buys the same new car prior to the next model-year being released.

    2. People who bought a DVD player as soon as they went on the market will pay more than people who buy them now.

    3. HDTV compliant sets are pretty pricey now. I'm waiting a while. Hopefully I'll not only get a better price, but a better set.

    4. My computer at home is a PII-450. I can still buy a brand new PII-450, and it will cost me much less than my computer did originally.

    The additional money you pay when something first comes out is a premium for being among the first to own it when its brand new.
    --
    Oh, sorry, I didn't mean to dance. We're still waiting on the pricing to be finalized.

    Potion Issues
    There were a couple of things such as this that were suggested back when I was settling into the job here.

    Having meaningful healing potions out there that can be bought from a vendor would reduce class-interdependence. Yes, as you said, your suggestion is a money sink that would help all classes solo better, but the truth is that that is a money sink that we've specifically decided not to take advantage of. We don't allow people to buy healing, nor do we allow them to buy experience. That's why the healing potion is basically useless, and it's also the reason why we've adjusted the experience given out by some quests.

    Why Servers weren't selected for the Transfer Option
    It depends on the time of day really. There were any number of servers we could have chosen for the other two besides Fennin that we took people from.

    Togor Issues
    I understand where you are coming from, and you make a lot of sense. The issue is that an upgrade to Togor's is planned for the expansion, and it will *probably* be better than the this one was. However the level to which that would have been better would be too small to be worthwhile had Togors not been adjusted.

    Strength has a similar issue, except its problem was a pure error in the entry of spell data as opposed to an overpowering, as in Togor's case. To illustrate this, a spell can have X effect, plus Y effect per level. When these spells were being entered in batch, the previous spell that was added had a "Y" value, but a much lower value of X. When Strength was put in, the existing Y value wasn't reset to its default value.

    Cleric Armor
    The AC of the cleric class-specific armor is being increased based upon a spreadsheet that was sent to me by one of the players.

    I think I mentioned last week that we were going to take another look at some of the class-specific armor.

    Spawn Times
    Actually, spawn times across servers are all somewhat independent, based upon the load in the zone (and other zones running on the same piece of hardware). The servers automatically throttle how much they have to keep track of so that they don't peg their processor usage, which would be *really* bad.

    The problem on Bristlebane a bit back where the spawn times in LowerGuk were 3 times normal had to due with this same issue. Of course in that case it was due to a bad piece of hardware.

    On Alchemy
    The latest changes to alchemy were a complete overhaul. I wouldn't be surprised if it still needs a tweak or two, specifically in the area of prices. However, we'll let people settle in and wait for the initial shock wears off before making any firm plans.

    I should probably note that I've gotten email both ways. Some people think it's fantastic now and needs no changes. The some think that the potions that can be made are great, but the prices need a tweak, and some people think that it's completely useless.

    Shaman Issues
    These are some compelling arguments about the shaman spells. I'll bring them up at the tuning meeting. What will happen if we decide not to change them is that you might not get as many spells as were planned in the expansion, but it's a thought.

    Be honest with me for a second, is the shaman class really "destroyed" by a 5% ranged reduction in the attack-speed slowing of Togor’s and a 20-some point reduction in Strength?

    As for the clipping plane, I'm still trying to get with the client programmer about it. I'll get back with you.

    Smushu + Torakk = g47
    Of course. This change was made due to a balance iss.....er... wait.. I have *no* idea what this is about... :)

    More Rogue Issues
    This was not an "either/or" situation. Poison is still on the plate. However we were looking for other ways to reduce the perception that rogues "sucked".

    That's where evade came in. One of the major complaints I saw was that rogues constantly caught the critter's attention because of all the damage they did, and ended up dead faster than the cleric could heal them, thus making the cleric OOM and unable to support the real, thus killing the whole group.

    Testing showed this to be a valid issue, one that the monks (the other high speed melee damage dealers) didn't have to contend with due to feign death. Just giving rogues Feign Death would have been pretty easy, but we didn't want to just copy the monk. The Evade ability integrated into the Rogues hide fit the bill pretty well, and from what I see on the Safehouse, they are very happy with it. Most of them have even seen their damage potential go up due to the number of additional backstabs they get in, and not nearly as much running around screaming "TAUNT IT!! TAUNT IT!!" like they used to :)

    The hotkey lock problem is also still on the table.

    GS Interview: "Making items not class-specific will decrease camping"
    Brad's out of the office today, on his way to St. Louis for EQ Gathering II, but I'll put my money on that being a typo. Considering that the changes that have been made to the game to reduce camping has included the introduction of class-specific items, I think that's a fairly safe bet :)

    Rechargable Items
    We're actually going to start experimenting with some recharable items soon. The difficulty is making it sufficiently meaningful that it still counts. For instance, if an item off of a deity gives 5 charges of Clarity, we don't want to make recharging it so easy that it hurts the enchanter. Many of the charged items out of the Plane of Sky will be rechargable. Based on how those go, we might make additional items rechargable.

    On Quests
    Some quests do have level limits necessary to *start* the quest. However once someone knows it they can do it over and over again as long as their faction is good.

    In hindsite, we would like to have included not only level limits, but a way to keep track of the quests completely to disallow quest farming. We'll certainly keep these issues in mind for future products.

    Roleplaying Servers
    We consider all servers to be RP servers since this is a role-playing game. Role-playing is a personal decision that each player makes for themselves. The joy for role-playing should be role-playing itself, not any tangible benefit handed out by the game. That's why when people say "There's nothing to encourage role-playing", I get very confused, as there is everything to encourage it, except perhaps ones own propensity to become self-conscious.

    Now, if you are asking about an "Enforced Role-play" server, we'd consider it as soon as someone comes up with a definition for role-play that would allow us to enforce it. We catch a lot of flack for our naming policy, which is much clearer than any enforced role-playing server could be in my humble opinion.

    Now if we are talking about an un-enforced role-play server, I'd have to say that that would do no better than the servers we have now, which are all basically un-enforced roleplay servers. Perhaps in name alone, it would support further role-playing, but I'm sure that many people of all play-styles would join it, if for no other reason than it had less players for the first few hours.
