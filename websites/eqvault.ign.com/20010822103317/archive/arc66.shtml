Aradune Infobits
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:58 PM PDT
EverQuest's Excutive Producer Brad McQuaid went on a posting spree today on the Gameplay Board. Here's what was said:

  • BoC Issue Update
    Smed and I are personally investigating this issue.

    Any discovery of misconduct will be dealt with.

  • On Boards & Abashi's Job
    Actually, it's not his job to reply to many emails. As he's posted in the past, it's just not an efficient way of communicating issues and messages to our player base.

    He does, however, read his email and acts upon it accordingly.

    As for Timnar, I really don't know what to say. EQVault and other sites that summarize Abashi's posts day after day are FILLED with his constant responses to questions and comments. The pages scroll and scroll by.

    Not only that, he often posts late at night and on weekends, even though such dedication is not required of him.

    Finally, interacting with the dev teams is indeed time consuming. Abashi attends scheduled and unscheduled tuning meetings, interacts with programmers, testers, and GMs, follows up on issues, etc. etc.

  • Burnout Effect
    Well, it's our opinion that 'challenging = fun for many people'. It's also our opinion that 'too easy = people becoming bored and leaving'. And finally, since we're doing word equations, 'unbalanced = no fun = dead game', 'monty haul = short term lots of fun, long term total boredom.'

    That's why we're so intent on keeping the game balanced and challenging. So much so that we're willing to risk some of our player base being upset with us in order to maintain the overall health of the game. It's the 'needs of the many outweigh the needs of the few' mentality.

    Is the game too challenging, slow, time consuming, etc. for some people? Undoubtedly. Is EverQuest for everyone? Heck no. Are there alternatives out there for people who prefer different playstyles? Yes (and I very much disagree with the 'we have a monopoly' assertion -- we have maybe 50% of the MMORPG community -- that's hardly a monopoly). Will there be more alternatives in the future making it such that MMORPGs can be even more niche? Definitely.

    We wanted to make a game based on interdependence, a class system... a game focussed on character development and item acquisition... a challenging game where one can feel very accomplished... a game that kept people's interest month after month, even year after year.

    We wanted to capture the compelling and intense qualities the best PvE MUDs had acheived, and then bring that experience to a large commercial market.

    And we did, and through luck, hard work, and passion, it turns out a LOT of people wanted a game like EQ. It was certainly the game we wanted to make... I remember when we were hoping that EQ would garner 70,000 users and how we hoped it would at least be marginally profitable.

    Now, at 270,000 users and growing we're pretty pleased, but still not satisfied. We can and will do better, and we will continue to make EverQuest a better EverQuest. Why? Because we think it's a fun game, and apparently a LOT of other people do too.

  • More on Posting Spree & Boards
    Thanks, greater lich :)

    As for my posting, well, that involves some really difficult and as of yet unaswered questions.

    For example, do these boards hurt or help? We've set up boards because of our commitment to communicating with our customers and for feedback. But the signal to noise ratio isn't so good.

    Does it help that we have Abashi here posting as a full time job? Many vocal posters, posting on boards we provide, complain and complain that we don't listen, that we don't care, and they even personally attack Abashi.

    All on our boards, being read and responded to by Verant/SOE employees.

    Would it really help if I posted more? Would people really appreciate it?

    Are there really a greater number of lurkers than posters, the 'silent majority', who appreciate the information we post and the fact that ideas do get communicated to us and often reflected in the game?

    Or have they all left because they're sick and tired of weeding through whine after whine?

    We're going to be making some changes soon, pretty radical ones, in order to get these boards to the point that they both serve the customer and Verant. I hope it works, but then it may fail, in which case I doubt the boards will be around long.

    We'll certainly give it a shot though.

    Personally, I'd love to start posting again more often.

  • Verant and Future & Current Projects
    We continue to develop Star Wars Online for Lucasarts.

    The game is coming a long great, but it's simply too early to talk much about it and yes, Lucasarts will be involved with the PR once it's time to start releasing info.

    As for future EverQuest products, well, as was mentioned, it would be foolish to start promoting a product that's some time away. It would be just as foolish, though, for us not to be looking into the future and preparing new titles, whether they be more expansions, sequels, and/or completely different products.

    I will say that Verant/SOE is developing SEVERAL massively multiplayer titles (including several unannounced titles, as well as SWO, EverQuest: The Scars of Velious, and Sovereign) and it is our intent to eventually cover as many genres and themes to which we feel 'massively multiplayer' is applicable.

    We're very excited over here, because we've only just begun :)

  • Quest Changes
    Yes, it's still our intent that those changes occur.

    The majority of recent and future quests should be made multi-step, which each component not taking so long that it's hard for a casual gamer to eventually complete the quest.

    If you know of any fairly recent quests where this is not so, please post them and the EQ Live team will take a look at them.

    And a major upgrade to many aspects of the Trade Skills system is still on its way (the main thrust being to not only add more products, but to make existing products more viable and valuable on older servers).

  • Posting Spree
    Yeah, I'm in a wierd posting mood.

    It hits me once in a while, though less and less lately.

    Hopefully the board changes in the near future will make a difference.

  • Responding to a Player Quitting EQ
    I'm sorry to see you go, and I appreciate your feedback as it relates to some of EQ's weaknesses.

    I hope you'll return one day, either to EQ or one of its successors.

  • PC Gamer EQ/eBay Article Screen Question
    It's a very old screenshot -- in fact, I think it's pre-beta.

    I would assume someone at the magazine wanted an EQ screen shot of a well equipped level one character and they looked into the archives and found this old shot.

  • Monty Haul Server Suggestion
    In a nutshell, the reason we wouldn't want to do this is that it cheapens the game.

    We don't want a monty haul server, we don't want a 'real easy/level fast/collect the phat l00t' server.

    It's the same reason we don't have level-up potions or gates or some such on Test.

    It's the same reason I throw a fit when Veeshan's Peak screen shots are shown, and why we don't like spoiler sites.

    It cheapens the game. It makes the sense of accomplishment a player gets on a regular server signficantly less.

    And, as mentioned, it would also just fill up with the lazy power gamer.

    People would rip through the content on the easy server, cheating themselves of a good sense of accomplishment, and leave.

    I'm sure the flames will follow this... 'you are just greedy and want people to play a long time!'

    Well, yeah, in a sense. One of the challenges to creating MMORPGs is PACING the game. Players can always consume content faster than a team can create it... you know, the 'it takes 6 months to write a novel and 2 weeks to read one' argument.

    But another key aspect to PACING an MMORPG is balancing risk and reward and character advancement such that players get a deep sense of accomplishment. Yes, this keeps players around longer AND makes a better game, IMHO.

    And that was our goal -- to create a persistent, massively multiplayer game, where people would play for months, even years. We did not set out to create D2, great game that it is.

  • Reporting Bugs and It's Rewards
    Ah, this rears its head again.

    No, you are not banned for reporting bugs. You are banned for exploiting them.

    Reporting a bug to a GM will not get you banned. If you need to test the bug several times to make sure it is a bug, petition, seek aid, and let us know what you're doing.

    And yes, the offer still stands -- if you find a dupe bug we will reward you. The reward varies, but usually includes a title.

  • Real Reason for EQ Burnout
    Paranoid4, I agree with some of what you're saying, and we will be making some changes very soon (within the next 2 weeks) that will hopefully remove the noise from the boards so that we can concentrate on well thought out and constructive criticisms, suggestions, and feedback.

Gordon Infobits
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 6:48 PM PDT
Here's the conclusion of today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Group Aggregate Damage
    [Quote from original post: Verant claimed quite loudly that Group Aggregate Damage would unbalance and ruin the game.]

    Ok. No one here ever said anything like that to my knowledge. It just couldn't be done at the time that it was suggested. It was later implemented because it was a good idea. There were absolutely no reservations about it here at the time that it was done.

    [Quote from original post: It was you personally who assured me on this board that group aggregate damage would be tested and removed because it was a bad idea. Go ahead Gordon. Refute that. ]

    And I never said anything like that either, considering that the Group Aggregate Damage change went in some time before I started working here.

  • Skeleton Naming Bug?
    It's technically a bug. What it will take to fix it though is another issue. I don't believe that the issue has been *waived*, which still leaves it on our "to be addressed" list.

    With the number of outstanding bugs that have been fixed over the past few weeks, I wouldn't be surprised if you see this one addressed soon.

  • Sense the Dead Problems
    I'll bring these issues up at our next tuning meeting. I know that we passed on the issue before, but I'll have another go at it :)

  • Response to "Solution to Class Envy"
    You seem to be advocating constant and persistant upgrades for each class as times goes on. What point do we stop? Never? At the same time do we constantly upgrade NPCs so that adventuring never becomes a joke? If so, is that a "nerf"?

    As much as I'd like to be able to tell everyone that we will keep making every class more and more powerful, I can't do that. Sure, in the short term following each enhancement, people of that class would be happy, but then we'd have to deal with things like, "Their last enhancement was better than our enhancement" and "Their last enhancement now makes us useless". Sooner or later we'd end up with a game that is pitifully easy and without challenge. That's when we'd end up with everyone quitting.

    Very soon, we're going to have to start freezing various aspects of the game in order to allow our designers to concentrate further on content additions. This has already happened to some extent with the announcement of the experience systems and with issues such as melee binding. We cannot get into a situation where we are making enhancements to a class based upon a shift in relative power in regards to an enhancement to another class. We could make changes of this nature forever, or until the game was broken, and we'd still have people who were upset about their classes.

    I'd like to ask some very straight-forward questions:

    Do you think that there is any change or series of changes that we could make that would eliminate class-complaints from the board? I'm not even talking about the odd complaint that comes up every once in a while that no one agrees with, just the ones that result in dozens of threads and hundreds of posts per day.

    Satisfying one group of people who are all of the same mind is easy. Satisfying 14 groups who's desires are often in direct conflict with that of another group's is impossible.

    Here's an example, I read a post today where one player said that Hybrids should all have all of the skills of the warrior, including hitpoints, and the strength of armor and weapons, in addition to the casting abilities that they already have. This is supposed to "make up" for the experinece penalty. Any warriors here agree with that?

  • Math .25+.75=1 AND 1+ Exp Penalty=1, Explaination Please?
    Additional or lesser experience requirements do not directly relate to class-balance.

    They are mere modifiers, of the same significance as things such as "faction problems (or the lack thereof)", "quest availability", "item availability", and "racial skills".

  • Class Inter-Dependancy
    While I appreciate the paralells you are trying to form, let me point a few things out:

    [my quote]: If... I can get a bind by some method other than using another class.

    This is a factual statement that clearly defines a proposed change. There's no subjectiveness to get into the of carrying the logic further. There's no element of opinion or bias in the statement itself.

    [your quote]:If...This game ever becomes more fun and less frustrating and has less downtime. And the PVP problem is fixed. And classes are balanced. And people can bind anywhere with potions or NPCs. And anyone could heal using Bind Wounds skill to 100% health.

    "more fun" is subjective. People have differing ideas of "fun". What one person finds fun, another person might find tedious. The game provides many different aspects, most of which should be found as "fun" by most peopel who continue playing beyond their free month.

    "less frustrating" is again subjective. What you find frustrating may be considered "fun" by another, and vice versa.

    "less downtime" is a matter of perception. If you view any time that you are not actively enaged in fighting creatures as "downtime" then there is probably too much of it for you. However others view what you refer to as "downtime" a chance to "socialize", "form relationships", "reflect upon current events", "roleplay", "build character", "craft items", "get a snack" and "use the facilities".

    Of course, if pure, unadulterated action is your chief concern, you can get very close to that with certain group makeups as well.

  • More PvP Issues
    [Quote from original post: First of all, the team servers are wrecked by the people that want it 'light races vs dark'. Well, I don't like this, and in reality, these people are 'wrecking' the team servers already. 'Light vs Dark' is cross-teaming in the extreme]

    They might be "wrecking" your idea of the ideal situation, but if that is how the environment is operating on the team servers, it probably means that that is how the majority of players sees things there.

    [Quote from original post: I don't really care if the majority want it 'light vs dark']

    Everyone is certainly entitled to their own idea of what should be going on. Maybe you can gather the like-minded people and see if you can convince others to play the game in that fashion. I don't think that we, as the designers, will be able to force elves and halflings to go to war.

    [Quote from original post: Stop it already with how RZers will wreck team servers...]

    Well perhaps an influence of RZ players might actually help mold the server to your desire. They would be coming from a server where anyone can attack anyone, and would probably be more likely to attack someone outside of the "Dark vs. Light" situation.

  • Wurmslayer
    The Wurmslayer is indeed being changed to a slashing weapon. The game designer who created the item mistakenly believed that Paladins could use piercing weapons. There are some other piercing weapons that have paladin's included as a valid class. Paladins will either be removed from the list on those items, or the items will be changed to slashing.

  • Subscriber Numbers
    We're still running over 70% retention after nearly 17 months of operation. That's fairly substantial and unheard-of in the industry.

    [Quote from original post: It was in a recent interview with Brad, I think it was PC Gamer. He said something alone the lines of like 70% retention rate. He thought that was acceptable. I don't, when I worked customer service our goal was in the high 90s of retention and satisfaction. And it is possible, I worked at a few telecomunications companies that had a customer base of millions.]

    Average retention rates over time for subscription services vary by industry. I'm sure that telecom and ISPs might enjoy higher average rates. MMOGs differ in this respect.

  • More Variety
    [Quote from original post: Imagine a world where there are 30 different choices for race/class and more than 3 combinations are actually useful!]

    13 races and 14 classes gives us an incredible amount of variety. I can only imagine the difficulty that would come with trying to make 30 races and 30 classes, each of which have to have their own "place" in the world. People already apply the famed "Master of..." title to each class. I would think that extending classes out to 30 would only make them more specifically qualified for certain situations and less qualified on the whole.

    [Quote from original post: Finally, after we actually have a little variety, maybe people will stop whining "its not fair, he is stronger, he has more, i want more, waaaaahh" because different people will be fighting different things, so they will have nothing to really compare with.]

    I think that you underestimate the resolve of players to "compare".


Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:22 PM PDT
Here's a few of the latest posts Gordon made today on the Gameplay Board:

  • Verant Math
    [Quote from original post: Seriously though. You're correct. In PnP games, hybrid math works something like 1 + 1 = 1.5, but here they seem to twist math in ways that physicists must dream about.]

    PnP games however consist of a few friends sitting around a table, none of whom have to worry about getting left behind or chosen over another based upon the power of their class. To compare MMOGs directly to PnP games, it would be like 30 friends sitting in a room, all wanting to play, with only a few of the most powerful being allowed.

    [Quote from original post: How is it that you can combine a warrior who is great at taking damage and a druid who is good at dealing dmg and buffing and come out with a ranger. Every hybrid is the same way.]

    If a ranger had all the skills and abilities of both warriors and druids, who would ever select a warrior or a druid for the group? The same situation would be present for the other hybrids. 1+1 cannot equal 2 in this case. The goal however is to make .25 + .75=1, and we've done a fairly good job of that.

  • Beating the Game
    [Quote from original post: See, there *is* a way to beat this game. It'a either to a)Reach level 60]

    Considering that there were tons of people at 50th level when that was the cap, and the fact that they didn't quite once reaching 50th, that's pretty strong evidence that it doesn't constitue "beating" the game. There's not that many level 60s yet, but even when there is I don't think that that will be a large quit-point either. People quit when they decide they are done with the game, regardless what level they happen to be at.

    [Quote from original post: Believe it or not, we DO NOT LIKE PUTTING 1000 hrs into a character! ]

    Perhaps you may not enjoy it, but there's everything to suggest that a *lot* of people *do* enjoy long-term character development. The simplest evidence is the fact that many people do put thousands of hours into their characters. I know dozens of people with well over 100 days played. There's even more, by several orders of magnitude, with over 50 days played. I have nearly 1000 hours of playtime on my character and have enjoyed them to a great extent.

    [Quote from original post: But with 14 classes I think you would get plenty, if not more replay value(subscription renewals in Verantese) and alot more satisfied customers if you cut down the exp requirements significantly. ]

    We don't expect people to be able to max out characters in all 14 classes. That's the thing about massively multiplayer games - they have to have more content than anyone can ever expect to consume. If you reach a point in the game where people have consumed all of that content, then yes, they've beaten the game.

  • Mesmerize Line for Clerics?
    A mesmerize line wouldn't be appropriate for clerics. Clerics are already considered immensly useful to the group, and are likewise appropriatly balanced.

  • Cleric Suggestion Response
    [Quote from original post: The problem at high levels (51+) is that we can't get groups if you're a hybrid or generalist or we have to wait for the cleric and enchanter to log on before we can do anything.]

    People will and do make due with what they have in the absense of the "perfect" arrangement. Farming out existing class-specific skills to other classes thus reducing the use of the original classes is not really a viable solution. Players need to learn to adapt to the situation and overcome the obstacles with the resources at hand.

  • Future Events
    We're actually going to have some new events that are going to start in the near future. Events will be held on each server to determine the best of a particular class, and all of the winners will be transferred to Test to compete there. It should be pretty interesting to see who the best players out there are :)

  • Response to a Suggestion
    Container code, particularly when dealing with nested containers, is very touchy business. Each additional level of nesting provides additional problems that need to be addressed. The way containers are handled now is part of the core foundation of EverQuest, so it's highly unlikely that it will be expanded to allow nesting.

  • Tuning Meeting Aftermath

Gordon Infobits
[7 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:30 PM PDT
Gordon was back on the boards in full force today as he responded to several different player-generated posts on the Gameplay Board. Here's the lowdown:

  • Wizards Post Locked
    We have to shut down threads when they exceed 250 posts. 300 is really the key number but the chance of corruption of the boards goes up quite a bit after that. I didn't realize the thread had already doubled the usual size, so just caught it now.

  • A Radical Test
    [Quote from original post: Ok, I would like to propose a radical idea. Your dev team will probably hate me for suggesting this, but its one way to maybe get something done about all the problems facing players in EQ. I suggest that Abashi and some of the dev team take the top complaints people are having, wizards, monks, etc..., and give them exactly what they want.]

    Just to preface my answer, I'm going to ask everyone to keep an open mind and not take offense at anything here. Most of us have played MMOG's for some time. Whether in the commercial setting, or in the form of MUDs, some of the issues we deal with are not due to any one particular person, but common problems that will be encountered any time someone tries to develop of a game of this nature.

    On to the subject at hand. This would be a radical idea. Let's assume for a moment that we can get those classes to agree on what they want and make the change. Should we decide to "revert back" later, how upset would people be then? Next, lets understand that some other classes would feel slighted by the changes made to these classes. They'd then expect to get what they want. Then other classes that weren't involved in the first two changes would figure that they also deserve what they want. Then we'd be back to the first two classes that feel slighted as it is. This would continue until we put our foot down and said, "No more".

    But we don't need to try that, because it's already been done. Over the past 7 months, tons of positive changes have been made to the classes, with very few negative ones. Any positive change made to one class satisfied them for the time until a few months down the road when all of the other changes were factored in. Several of the loudest complaints I'm hearing now are coming from the same classes that had complaints that we fixed months ago. The feeling of, "It's our turn now", keeps coming around again and again. We're now at the point to put our foot down as mentioned above.

    We can't afford to go making changes that we know will unbalance the game just for the purpose of reducing complaints, when the situation remains that there will always be issues that the players feel need to be addressed. There will always be some inequality felt by some classes, because we've designed the game not for equality but for balance.

  • Key Quest Broken?
    [Quote from original post: Why is it that the keys for Sebilis have both halfs of the key on 2 different mobs in the same zone that are always up and always drop each half, YET the keys for Charsis are on 2 different mobs, 2 different zones ( both green to the folks that would be going to charsis) and both mobs DO NOT always have the key componet ?]

    Concerning the critters being green, when designing dungeons, even new dungeons, we have to take into account the way that it fits within the entire game. Certainly there are a bunch of high level people who find those creatures green, but the person who is advancing through the game and encounters them while adventuring probably will get some experience off of them. They'll then have these components once they reach level to enter that dungeon. Every time we put in something new, there's always a rush to get it. It takes a little while, but it balances out before long.

    Concerning the difficulty of getting the items, all I can say is that the quests are different. Ones easier, and the other is harder. It's based on whatever the quest designer had in mind at the time. I don't think that we'd be interested in making one easier just because of the other.

  • Why don't SKs get Gate?
    Gate is reserved for pure caster/priest classes. Consider "Shadowstep" as an extra ability that they garner from their parent class.

  • Insight
    [Quote from original post: I'm just alittle curious when I look and see that clerics get equal AC to Paladins and ShadowKnights plus get complete heal on their Breastplate versus a 45 min, self only lvl 24 necro spell, major sheilding and symbol of pinzan which requires a component. I'm not complaining as I would surely love to have a cleric that can sit back and start casting CH when at half health over and over.]

    In this case, the cleric item is giving them access to a spell that they normally have, that just costs them mana. The casting time on the item is sufficient to ensure that it's not exactly a combat heal and doesn't replace the casted heal itself. It's also an extremely rare item off of a really tough creature. The breastplate itself doesn't really expand the definition of what a cleric is. They still remain a healer. I would admit that you'd have a point had we put some type of damage spell on it.

    [Quote from original post: What is the reasoning behind having a set bonus applied to weapons. Should the bonus not be adjusted to the damage of the weapon?]

    The damage bonus itself was originally put in without any regard to weapons, use, or damage. We later upped the per-swing damage bonus for 2H weapons. These weapons, the 3/10 and 4/11 and other really low delay weapons out there are basically broken. We're still fighting over whether or not to make a retroactive change to them.

  • Summoning More Pets
    [Quote from original post: Why can't pet classes summon more than one pet at a time?]

    Client (polygons) and server load. A zone currently needs to support X people, Y pets, and Z NPCs. As long Y=X, and Z is constant, the numbers remain manageable under most circumstances. As soon as Y=2X or 3X, it quickly becomes difficult, and the server would have trouble keeping track of them all.

    Additionally, as the number of things in the zone increases, the client then has to draw that many more polygons.

    Pet power was set based upon this one-pet limit. If we allowed multiple pets, their power would drop significantly.

  • Death Penalty
    Everyone keeps quoting the worst case scenario in regards to the death penalty. You can reduce that substantially by getting a res. Yes, I understand that a res might not always be available, but assuming that you have friends, it's probably available enough that you can mitigate the death penalty to a great extent. Death is supposed to be very inconveniencing and something that you don't want to encounter. It wouldn't be a risk if this weren't the case.

    The experience system in game is considered frozen, meaning that no further changes will be made to how experience is earned, dealt out, or lost.

  • Runaway Pets
    Runaway pets in dungeons (though preferrably not due to pathing problems) is one of the designed dangers of using pets. Owners need to be espicially careful that their pet doesn't run off, upset a few critters, and bring them back home to the group.

Brad on EverQuest Burnout
[3 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:51 PM PDT
Verant Vice President Brad McQuaid added his two cents on a board thread talking about EverQuest burnout. Here's his complete post:

    I wrote a post a while back on MMORPG burn-out -- it's probably still out there somewhere.

    Abashi is correct -- with most games, you start playing and either get hooked or not.

    If not, you shelf it/return it, and move on, perhaps only slightly annoyed that you bought a game you didn't care for.

    If you're hooked, you play it through, complete it, and move on, looking for another good game.

    With an MMORPG you play, and you play, and you keep playing, until you're burned out. Burn out can come from boredom (you've done it all, or done all you care to do), or from aspects of the game that annoy you that you overlooked when it was new but now aggrivate you more and more, etc.
    Also, there aren't very many choices out there right now, and MMORPGs are forced to try to appeal to as many people as possible. That means there are people playing these games that only like certain aspects of the gameplay. Basically, they choose the MMORPG that most closely matches their disires and tastes and, in a sense, tolerate it. As the market matures, the players mature, the game space expands, etc., players will have more choices. MMORPGs wil be able to be more niche, appealing to more specific groups of customers. This, I think, will help with some of the burnout issues.

    In any case, as MMORPG designers, one of our core challenges is to figure out ways to keep most players entertained over the long haul either indefinitely or at least until we can provide an alternative (sequel, different product, etc.)

    That isn't always possible, though, and so we are at times faced with upset ex-customers who have a bad taste in their mouth even though they've played the game for over a year and logged 100+ days of playing time.

    It's not a new phenomenon, of course. The same stuff happened with MUDs and other similar games that a. have no ending and b. are very compelling. Slowly but surely, people become upset and either quit or keep playing, remaining upset.

    Some of you might say, well that's because these games aren't any good or some such, and that as soon as a truly 'great' MMORPG comes along, there will be no burn out or hard feelings.

    Perhaps. But I doubt it (though I'd love to be wrong). I think any game that can keep a players attention for month after month, even year after year (take a look at UO), is simply a GREAT game.
    That said, MMORPGs are still first generation. We all have a very bright future ahead of us and I'm sure many design hurdles will be overcome.

    -Brad

Gordon Infobits: Afternoon Edition
[12 Comments] - Aurum @ 1:21 PM PDT
Gordon's been busy this past afternoon answering a few posts on the Gameplay Board. Here's the scoop:

  • The Feeling When You Quit EQ
    [Quote from original post: I was thinking about this...

    .. any other game I have played, it's fun, you and your friends enjoy the rush, but eventually, you stop playing. If the game was good (example: Diablo) you are excited about the follow-up game (example: Diablo II)... and you have a pretty good feeling about the the game company that created the game (in this case, Blizzard).

    So why is it that when people quit playing EQ, they are frustrated, angry, upset, etc? No sense of satisfaction, more of a sense of a waste of time. Also, it seems, that most who stop playing EQ have bad feelings about Verant.]

    I've given this issue a good deal of thought myself. I think that it is a situation that is unique to massively-multiplayer online games. You see, there's no way to "win" these games. The closest you could come to winning would be to do everything that there is to do, and get everything that there is to get. Since *no one* could ever do that, no one ever gets close to the idea of winning.

    With single player games, there's often a linear storyline with one main plot. There might be a few branches with subplots, but the main idea remains. Completing these games give you a feeling of "wow, that was a good game", but you still put them on the shelf and will probably never play them again. If you finish the game, you are probably still "happy" with the game, or otherwise "satisfied" when you complete it. There's a good chance that you wish there was more game to play.

    MMOGs have to provide all of their content up front, as soon, there will be people at all stages of advancement playing. There can be no main "plot" to the entire purpose of the game, only a bunch of smaller challenges for you to encounter.

    People play MMOGs until they are no longer satisfied with the game, then they quit. Since quitting basically requires boredom of dissatisfaction, people don't remember the game as fondly as they would a single-player game that they've just completed. As game developers, our task is to keep adding things to the game to keep people interested and satisfied. Of course, it takes an author months to write a novel, but only a few hours to read it.

    We're faced with the same dilemma when it comes to adding to the game.

  • Current Subscriber Base
    We're running over 270K active subscribers as of this morning. Roughly 80% of those play in any 48 hour period. When we ran the poll on the dragon issue, I believe it was done on a Wednesday, and we had over 180K responses in the 24 hours that it was up.

    I'm not sure what you are saying here.

    An active account is an account that can log into the game and play a character. This is every paying account, whether they are currently being billed or whether they are on their free month.

    Don't confuse active accounts with peak usage. Peak usage is only a percentage of active accounts.

    In the meantime, remember that the blue Abashi is the real Abashi :)

  • The Economy
    The fact that prices are dropping indicates that the value of platinum has gone up. Money sinks only serve to reduce the amount of money in the world which would cause a further lowering of prices. The existing money sinks, and the reduced cash drops in many areas has done a good job of increasing the value of platinum.

    Prices are also dropping due to the fact that there are more of the items in game, meaning that supply is exceeding demand. It's a bit harder to do something with this, unless we develop an "item sink".

PvP Rulesets
[38 Comments] - Aurum @ 3:42 PM PDT
Gordon made a lengthy post on PvP rules on the Gameplay Board a short time ago. He also asks for thoughts and comments so don't hesitate to speak. Here's the complete post

    Before I get into the subject of this post, let me define the rulesets in play on each of the PvP servers:

    Rallos: Complete PvP. Players can attack any other player within a 4 level limit. Looting is coin plus one item, providing that the item isn't bagged, NODROP, or carried in a melee slot. There are no faction penalties for engaging in PvP combat.

    Tallon/Vallon: PvP Teams. Players can attack any other player within an 8 level limit, assuming that the target is on one of the 3 opposing teams. Looting is coin-only. There *are* faction penalties for engaging in PvP combat.

    As many here have noticed, there have been drives for each of the PvP servers, most recently Rallos, to get the server switched to coin-only loot. However, though the same drive once existed for Vallon, after the change was made, there was likewise a drive to switch it back to item-loot. This suggests that while there are people who enjoy coin-only loot, there are also people who enjoy item-loot. The difficulty that we are faced with is trying to make everyone happy. Simply implementing majority will on each server doesn't accomplish that objective, as it addresses the needs of the pro-coin crowd without taking care of the pro-loot crowd. As much as both groups come here insisting that "everyone" wants what they want, I don't think that anyone can truly deny that both groups exist.

    Right now, we have two servers where coin-loot is in operation, and one where item-loot is the rule. That layout is roughly equivalent to the percentages that enjoy coin-loot versus item loot. The third who enjoy item loot, though technically the minority, are still a significant portion of the gaming populace, and as such should have their concerns addressed as well. Rather than just changing Rallos to coin-loot, it's been suggested that we allow people a one-time chance to move between the PvP servers. Though we are not yet prepared to commit to a plan of that nature, I do want to get everyone's opinion as to whether or not they would find such a plan acceptable.

    If we were to allow moves, a two-step process would be used. First, Rallos people would be given the option to move to Vallon or Tallon (by choice), then Vallon and Tallon people would be given the opportunity to move to Rallos. If moved, players would suddenly find themselves in a new ruleset, subject to every facet of that ruleset.

    Former Rallos players would find themselves on teams, in an environment that discourages socialization outside of your team. They would suddenly be subject to attack in an 8 level limit, and would take race-based faction hits for any PvP kills made. Players with strange deity selections (e.g. Half elf worshipping Innoruuk) would suddenly find themselves homeless for all intents and purposes.

    Former team-server players moving to Rallos would have their ability to engage in combat restricted to a 4 level limit, and be subject to attack and able to attack anyone in that range. Any race-based faction penalties assigned while on the Team servers would follow to Rallos, and would not be reset.

    Players on all PvP servers would encounter an influx of characters of all levels, who wouldn't be completely familiar with any server-specific player-originated rules of etiquette that may be in place. It is possible that aspects once understood as a "given" within the server community might break down and be no more.

    It may sound like I'm trying to discourage acquiescence to this change, however I just want to be sure that all aspects of the proposal are understood prior to anyone giving an opinion on the matter. If done, the change would likely be the biggest to be made to any server community in EverQuest, and we want to be sure that everyone is well-informed.

    Thoughts?

    -Gordon


The New Item-Reimbursement Policy
- Aurum @ 3:38 PM PDT
Gordon posted the following on the Gameplay Board about the new item reimbursement policy:

    Hello all,

    I'm sure that most everyone is aware of the new item-reimbursement policy for loss due to bugs. For those that aren't, here's a quick recap:

    Our intention is to handle all claims in a timely manner. Upon receiving a claim of item loss due to a bug, the GM will begin the investigation process (checking logs, backups, etc). This usually takes roughly 30 to 60 minutes to complete. The GM will then immediately take action and reimburse the item(s) based upon the outcome of the investigation. Our customers diserve courteous and prompt service, and we'll do everything in our power to insure that they receive it.

    One thing that we should have made clear in the announcement is that unfortunately, this policy is not retroactive, meaning that those who experienced loss prior to the time that the policy was announced (7/18/2000) would not be covered by the new policy.

    As mentioned above, we do need to perform due diligence when investigating the claims, and though we will make reimbursements any time that we lack proof of any misconduct, we still need to have that information (backups, logs, etc.) present to perform the search.

    -Gordon
