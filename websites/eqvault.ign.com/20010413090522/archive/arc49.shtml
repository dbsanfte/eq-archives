August 1, 2000 - August 6, 2000

Sunday, August 6, 2000


Gordon Infobits
- ChrisLTD @ 2:41 PM PDT
The Official Forums are a busy place and Abashi does the best job he can addressing relevant issues. Here for your reading pleasure are a summary of his recent board adventures:

  • Gordon: Fatties are getting the shaft in equipment
    There should be some advantages for those who choose to forego the immense advantages that come with choosing to be a large race.

  • Gordon- Vermaculated armor once again
    This issue has been addressed with the Live Team several times, and the outcome is that the stats on these items are intentional. As such, we're closing the issue.

  • Moss Covered Twig
    [Quote from original post: Can you confirm or deny if you are going to nerf this weapon yet?]

    We still haven't made a descision regarding these extremely low-delay weapons yet. We'll be sure to publicize it when the decision has been made.

  • Lore Items..
    [Quote from original post: Supposedly an item with the LORE ITEM tag is supposed to have info on it that can be revealed with an Identify. But most lore items either dont need a description because it is inherent in the title, or are lore simply to prevent a user from having more than one at a time(magician summoned staffs come quickly to mind)]

    I think that we're going to begin adding actual "lore" to those lore items soon. We have a writer on the EQ Live staff who will probably take that up soon.

  • Gordon/Abashi/Verant Can this be done?
    [Quote from original post: Let everyone "do" a dungeon at the same time.

    Give the dungeon a maximum number of players (like a chat room has a maximum number of participants)

    This should be in multiples of 6 (for groups)

    If more players want to enter a dungeon than its maximum, the server creates a second copy of the dungeon for the next set of players.]

    We actually want to encourage people to spread out in the world and experience new things. Spawing multiple copies of dungeons would work against this goal. Another issue would be that people might have a hard time getting into the same "copy" of the dungeon when they wanted to. Finally, we would lose control over the rate at which items enter the game. I can see a situation where large guilds would purposely create multiple copies for the purposes of camping the same thing in those multiple copies.
    It's not a good solution for a MMOG.

  • Gordon Look! :) Mana Regen Again ....
    [Quote from original post: I've noticed that it is taking FOREVER to med with my skill stuck at 255 (or 252?). We continue to get more mana intensive spells and more mana past the level 50 mark, but I am stuck with the same med capability I will have at level 60 that I had at level 50. So is there a fix proposed for this? How about an item that casts... or has the effect of clarity? If you can do the effect of SoW for J Boots.... why not clarity for another type of item? Seems only fair since the value of SoW is not near what clarity is anymore.]

    The "solution" for what you are mentioning is grouping with classes that have the ability to increase your regen rate. Bards and enchanters can do this for you, and can certainly increase it at a rate beyond what it was at level 50.

    Putting in other ways to increase mana regeneration would unbalance the game. The methods available to increase the rate of regeneration do so to the greatest extent that we've designed for the game.

  • Too many people on a sever? What to do now?
    [Quote from original post: Do you think there is a way to make respawn times faster on 'full' servers?]

    Unfortuantely, that's not the answer to overcrowding. Lack of things to kill in a particular area is one of the things that gets people to spread out to some of the other areas in the game. There is now more than enough land and things to kill in the game to support well over two thousand people, though as we mentioned before, we're not raising the official peak-usage target.

    The other issue is that dungeons are laid out so that if they are "done" (rather than camped), several groups can move through them and get enough time to rest. Increasing spawn times, by even a minute, upsets that balance and actually promotes camping.

  • The easiest way to silence post-50 monk complaints
    [Quote from original post: Simply put, give us triple attack at 60 like the warriors. We will be able to outdamage them again by a significant margin. Our lower ac won't be as big an issue because groups will want us again for our damage output.]

    Triple attack is basically just "something neat". I don't understand why this is a frequent point with monks, as it really doesn't fire that often for warriors to really swing balance. If monks want it just because it's a skill that they don't have, that's really not a compelling reason to add it.

  • Magicians
    [Quote from original post: Can us mages get some "fun" spells put into the game? Perhaps find a way to spice up the class without unbalancing it?]

    I thought we did some of that with the bundle/pack spells that we threw in. They are really there just for fun, as you suggest. If you can think of some other "fun" spells, we could throw those in as well

  • Post 50 cleric spell order screwed up -- clerics constantly told Verant, and still!
    They were initially correct towards the end of the RoK beta, however at some point they got messed up. You are right that a few are out of order, for instance, with group heroism coming before group resolution.

    Rather than just put them in the right order, which would be considered as a nerf by some, we're going to just change a spell or two to something more appropriate for the level. For instance, I believe that we are changing "United Resolve" to a "group symbol" spell that only uses one component.

  • RUMOR!! If it's true I will never play EQ again
    [Quote from original post: according to people I know and things that have been derived from the test server atmopsphere Feign death has an added recast time to it, so feigns cannot be done in succession.]

    Interestingly enough, that was a suggestion that was made here on the boards a while back to eliminate feign-pulling: Make it so that the recycle timer doesn't start until you stand up.

    In any case, we haven't implemented it. I checked out feign quite a bit during the last great-feign debate, and also just checked it now, and it appears to be the same to me.

  • Abashi - Whats the ETA on the Disease line being upgraded?
    I'm not really sure what may have been indicated in the past in regards to this line. As of now, the disease line is considered adequate. The extended duration of the spells was intentional, and is balanced with the environment.

  • The only Thing I have ever or will ever ask of you Verant/Abashi
    [Quote from original post: PLEEEEAAAAASSSSSEEE! Could you make it so my Lupine claw gauntlet's are visible when I wear them! No stat change is nec. my monk would look sooo cool woth them!! Other than that I love the game the way it is, even with the parts that drive me crazy and make walk away to another room so I don't hit my monitor, just to end up coming back and playing for another six hours!!!!

    Gauntlets please!!!]

    As they are hand items, they cannot appear as a model held in the hand such as thulian claws.

  • Gordon, if you would be so kind.....
    [Quote from original post: Pestilence Scythe, 2H slash ok? SK NEC WAR

    but Necro's can't slash ( I've had this thing in my bank for like ages and its no drop ( so should I keep it for some slashing fun one day when necro's can slash? or just give it to my pet for a mean beat em up till I log? )]

    The inclusions of necromancers on this item was most likely a mistake. That said, they look pretty cool when wielded by a caster. When the SolRo quests first came out, the first thing I did with my necro was to run out and get a Harvester

  • Abashi: Casual Player Server? Ideas from a power gamer.
    [Quote from original post: How do you know its 20 hours a week? Please define the source of that datum.]

    It's actually over 20 hours per week, much closer to 23. The data is fairly easy to come by, as we can tell who is logged into the servers at any given time, and how long they are logged in.

    [Quote from original post: That 20 hour figure is fine if you reduce the weak to two days. I play about 20 hours a week myself, but that's based on the fact that I put in all 20 in one session, and then take a long break. Not everybody is as fortunate as me to have a nice schedule.]

    I know what you are saying. That's pretty typical of the average player. The average player typically plays a few hours each day with one long session once or twice a week.

  • Deleting petitions.
    [Quote from original post: No doubt many people including myself have made petitions, only to be able to resolve the situation themselves or with help from other players moments later before a GM was able to reply.
    Unfortunately once the problem has been solved, the petition is still waiting, and the sender has no way of removing the petition from the que, or informing the GM`s that they no longer need assistance, which no doubt is a waste of they're time, and players who still need help are left waiting longer.

    Is there any chance that a means of deleting petitions sent would be added to the game?]

    That's a good idea. I'll mention it to the team.

    Players can currently only file one petition per character. A "delete petition" command (e.g. /delpetition) would just inform the server that you are no longer in need of help allowing the server to expire the petition.

Gordon Converses with the Masses
- ChrisLTD @ 2:03 PM PDT
Here (for your ultimate viewing pleasure) are posts from Gordon Wrinn, located on the Official EverQuest Forum:

  • Gordon (honest question) How important are the PvP servers to Verant?
    We certainly care about PvP. We have a significant portion of our playerbase playing on PvP servers. The difficulty is coming up with a way to handle PvP that "everyone" likes. As you know, "everyone" is a hard group to please. Some want no looting. Others want to be able to loot everything. Some think that there should be experience loss on PvP deaths, others see that that would cause major problems without changing some other aspects of the game greatly.
    We do have some constraints we have to operate under, the main one being that EverQuest is and will always be primarily a PvE (Player versus Environment) game. To that end, that aspect of the game cannot be changed greatly purely for the sake of PvP.

    There's almost as many ideas on how to "fix" PvP as there is players. Most issues really don't have a solid consensus. So what aspects of PvP are there that you like in EQ? Dislike? How do you propose that they be addressed?

  • Abashi, doesn't your reasoning concerning hybrid mana also apply to ranger defense?
    Though the rangers didn't get an increase in defensive skills, you'll notice that they got larger increases in offensive skills than other classes. From 50 to 60, the balance swung in favor of being more offensively oriented. Where one area received no increases, another area received more increases than normal.

    This isnt' related to the hybrid mana issue.

  • Here is probably the best suggestion I've seen
    [Quote from original post: I'd like to see a command /Notes
    that would open a window that looks like a bug form or feedback report. In this window you can
    put in any notes you would like to take and be able to edit them at will. This file could be stored in your main EQ directory locally.
    I think this is probably one of the handiest and most usefull things Verant could do. It would be simple to implement. Of course something I think
    could be added at the same time is an Automap feature as in M&M once again easy to implement.]

    Good idea, I'll mention it to the guy who's doing some updating to the client.

  • Abashi: Suggestion for a new board rule
    I am going to add a section to the FAQ for finalized issues in the near future. The first release was very broad in scope, with very few specific issues covered. It was basically trying to cast a wide net for a multitude of issues that come up frequently.

    Future releases will add sections that are more specific in nature, but we have to be wary of "finalizing" things too quickly. Nothing is more embarassing than writing an issue "in stone" only to come back and change it later Things like expanded melee binding, and experience curves are indeed written in stone. I wouldn't want to put things in stone lightly.

Gordon Infobits
- ChrisLTD @ 7:26 PM PDT
Abashi made the following posts on the Official EverQuest Forum:

  • Abashi listened to hybrids finally! Thanx
    [Quote from original post: Abashi THANK YOU for putting a larger mana pool on test server. Please consider making this live as it will not address the xp issue, but it certainly will go a long way to fixing it. Also consider unnerfing the spell mana requirement, they should take the same mana as the parent class.]

    Just to stress, this is in testing and may or may not make the live servers. I thought I'd announce it to give the players some idea of what we are working on. Just to elaborate further on what is in testing:
    1. The mana pool for a hybrid will be the same as the pool for the parent caster, assuming the same amount of int/wis. This is balanced by the fact that there are not nearly as many +int/wis items available for hybrids as pure casters, and that they'd have trouble equallying out in that department. Previously, hybrids had the equivalent of 8 levels less mana than a caster of the same level assuming the same amount of int/wis.

Official Board Policy Update
[10 Comments] - ChrisLTD @ 11:34 AM PDT
Straight from Abashi on the Official Boards:

Hello all,
We're quite pleased with the way the boards are working out so far, and hope that they will continue in this fashion. That said, there are a couple other issues that we need to address in order to facilitate the board's continued usefulness:

Nerf Requests: We're going to begin closing requests to nerf others classes. It becomes a fairly common occurance when it's apparent that we aren't going to upgrade another class. Nerf requests are very inflamatory and are a breeding ground for all-out flame wars. As we don't want to have to liberally ban people if it can be handled via other means, closing the threads is probably the best action we could take.

Dead issues: We'll also begin closing threads, explicitly or implicitly addressed to us, that we consider dead issues, issues that we've addressed already and consider "locked down". You all can feel free to continue discussing the issue amongst yourselves, but if it's a "Verant please read!" thread, and we consider the issue closed, we'll close the thread as well. We're going to do this in hopes of making room for new threads on other subjects that just can't seem to draw much interest on their first go-around. Rather than have a dozen threads pop up asking us to continue explaining the issue, this might be the best course of action.

In addition, I'm going to create a "dead issue" section of the FAQ for these things as they continue to come up. We realize that not everyone can read everything on the boards every day, so we should have the data available someplace.

Concerning the "dead issue" stuff, please take note that it only speaks to threads addressed to us. Like I said in the original post, feel free to discuss it amongst yourselves.
What I'm trying to avoid is this:

1. Post shows up asking for an issue to be addressed. We address it.
2. More posts show up asking for it to be addressed further, we clarify.
3. Yet more posts show up demanding it be addressed. We run out of ways to address the issue and sooner or later stop responding.
4. More posts show up asking why we're ignoring the issue. We post that it's already been addressed several times.
5. More posts show up saying that it wasn't addressed well-enough to begin with, yet we've said all that we can say on the issue.

Where does it stop? A solution is needed, and this one seems better than having a bunch of "why are you ignoring..." posts on the front page.

-Gordon

Gordon Infobits
[3 Comments] - ChrisLTD @ 2:17 PM PDT
A number of posts were made by Verant's Gordon Wrinn on the Official EverQuest Board. Here's the story:


  • Tired of total lack of courtesy
    [Quote from original post: Verant PLEASE, this is not good business and its downright messed up. Give players time to get to a safe spot. AT the minium players should have 5 minutes to log out. 10 - 15 being a more appropriate time]

    There is supposed to be at least a 5 minute warning in all cases of an intentional server downing. Of course, it is possible that some of the notifications are missed due to zoning or fighting. at the time that the warning messages are sent. Others might log in after a message and feel that they weren't given a warning. I myself have sat on a server and watched warnings be issued every minute for 5 minutes, only to go into the chat-server and hear from people who didn't see the warning.

    That said, if it's apparent that at least 5 minutes warning isn't given in the case of an outage, please let us know what server the problem occurred on so that we can take steps to ensure that proper warning is given in the future.

  • Hybred Idea
    [Quote from original post: Hybreds are supposed to be warriors and casters right? So why can't we attack while casting a spell. I mean you still couldn't move or be hit. But why didn't my paladin ever learn to hit things while casting?]

    Casting takes a certain amount of concentration, which is why casters get interrupted while being hit. Of course that's an RP reason. From a game design perspective, nothing hits while in a casting delay, not even NPCs. Though I like the idea, this is a core mechanic of EverQuest, and isn't really likely to change.

  • Questions comments and ideas :)
    [Quote from original post: Why does mana not have a numeric value when everything else does]

    We've spoken about the mysticism before, and in regards to your argument, we actually publicize far more numbers in game than we originally would have liked. The fact that we did publicize those additional numbers doesn't necessarily mean that we should publicize the next thing that seems "logical" given that argument. At some point we either have to stop releasing more numbers, or just release them all. Though there are certainly people who would like to see numbers on everything, we're of the mind that the game shouldn't be reduced to pure mathematics in the eyes of the players.

  • Wake of Tranquility
    Gthen,
    When GZ was designing the spell layouts, he made a list of all of the spells that he wanted to implement, and then set it up so that a certain number of spells were implemented in a given level. Though it may appear to make sense for a particular spell to be given a few levels earlier or later, it would upset the way that the spells are allocated.

    We actually made a mistake at one point, giving group resolution after group heroism. I'm not sure how that happened, but rather than shift the spells back to where they should be, we're actually going to replace one of the spells. I'm mentioning this to illustrate how important it is that we provide spells at the level which they are provided.

  • Help Melee classes restore some semblence of pvp-balance
    [Quote from original post: Please, make pumice stones self-only and return them to the merchants.]

    We are giving that suggestion some serious consideration now. Though it is unlikely that we would completely remove the casting delay should we decide to do this, we would probably reduce it significantly.

  • What constitues proof to the GM?
    Most bugs that would result in a resurrection leave some sort of signature that would allow the GM or Guide to verify the incident and make the correct decision. For instance, falling for 10K damage leaves a line of text that can be /reported in order to provide "proof".
    The particular incident that you are mentioning however is not a bug. Centaurs by their nature are ornery critters, and have a very small percent chance of just going wild on someone. Of course this can be very inconvenient when there are other centaurs around, as their anger is contagious.

    I think that one of the reasons people think that this is a bug is that this NPC personality trait isn't widely used in game.

  • /who GUILD all and /roleplay
    [Quote from original post: Well, I dont know if anyone noticed, but unlike the patch msg announced, people who are in /roleplay do NOT show up on /who GUILD all. I dont want to sound offensive, but maybe you guys should double or triple check patch content to make sure everything is there?]

    Yeah, you have a point I sincerely apologize for announcing a patch change that didn't take place. As it turns out, one file necessary for the /who all "guild" to work on /roleplayers wasn't included in the patch package. It will be in the next one.
