Mon 29 Aug 2005

  Dev Comments on the future of LDoN
Print News | Mail News
In response to a thread on the EQLive boards, Zajeer wrote concerning the LDoN system and its rewards:

"I've got plans for sometime in the future to evaluate the point system in LDoN and make adjustments, especially at the low end. Ideally, we'd like players to walk out of the tutorial at 10, do the various quests in POK until 20, and then hit LDoN for a good portion of their 20-40s for directed play. To accomplish this, however, the point system (or the cost of items, really) needs to be changed so that you get enough points to buy items before you level. Currently in LDoN, players will outgrow the items they are trying to save up for by doing LDoN adventures, because they level up faster than they can save.

I'll be looking at the post-50 LDoN points/rewards, but my main goal is to make LDoN a viable place for new players to meet up and play in."


-- Kwill @ Mon 29 Aug 2005

Fri 26 Aug 2005

  Server List Complete!
Print News | Mail News
Look on the left hand menu, and you will see that under the topic "server guild guide" almost every EQ server is listed there. Click on the links and you will see many of your server's guild recruiting requirements and a link to each of their websites.

How does this work for you?

--if you are considering another guild or joining a guild, easily see what guilds might suit you best and if you meet the requirements for a particular guild without clicking back and forth to many different websites.

--if you are a guild leader, see what other guilds post in terms of rules, recruitment policies, and other information.

--new to the game and thinking about joining a guild? Find out what it takes both in character progession (gear, AA's, etc.) and behavior to join a guild. Not every guild is right for every person, so investigating what different guilds are about before you apply is a very good idea.

--wonder what you should be looking toward for your personal character goals? The high level guilds all have class requirements, and you can use those AA, gear and resists requirements to plan your own progression!

--see what guilds are doing on your server and how many are playing in the end game content. For more information along these lines, you can also check out eqrankings for public information on guild kills, magelo profiles, and other statistical information.

-- Kwill @ Fri 26 Aug 2005

Sun 14 Aug 2005

  The Change in Frogloks
Print News | Mail News
From EQLive:

Evil Frogloks!
The last few weeks have seen a number of events unfold across the EverQuest servers, particularly relating to Grobb / Gukta, and who will retain ownership of it.

The events played out over a period of roughly five weeks, during which the Orcs mounted an attack against the Elves of Faydwer, the Barbarians launched an assault against the mobilizing forces of Ykesha, and the Dark Elves called on the power of Innoruuk to create an evil curse to deploy amongst the Froglok, all of which ultimately resulted in the Fall of Gukta (also referred to as the Retake of Grobb).

The deployment of Innoruuk's Curse is of special note, since it caused the once unified Armies of Gukta to turn on each other, corrupting their minds, and giving them access to types of magic previously unknown to the Guktans. Those corrupted by the curse began to turn to Necromancy and other similar dark arts.

The manifestation of this curse is portrayed in the ability for players to create Froglok Necromancers, Shadowknights, Rogues, and Clerics, all of which who follow Innoruuk due to the curse.

-- Kwill @ Sun 14 Aug 2005
