  Guild Recruitment Festival Begins  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
This weekend there will be in-game festivities for the Guild Recruitment Festival on all servers, specifically, meeting places in Qeynos and Freeport where you can meet with prospective guilds and guild members. So get ready to hook up!

For those who reside in Kelethin or Gorowyn, you are going to need to travel over to Freeport or Qeynos ...guild folks, maybe offer escorts to the lower levels who might not be able to make it?

EQ2Players posts:

"The Living Legacy summer events are just kicking off, and now that you’re back, we want make it easy for you to enjoy the rest of the summer celebration by linking back up with old friends or by helping you make some new ones. To help provide you with guidance, we have organized a Guild Recruitment Fair. We have called upon all the EQII legends that are “living the legacy” by running guilds to gather around and recruit new blood.

This is a perfect opportunity for you to meet in-game and learn more about the guilds on your server. EQII has a highly developed guild tool system to help you find the right guild and a massive guild community base for you to continue your adventures in a group environment. With guild halls quickly approaching, and several new raid and group zones being released this summer, you’ll need all the help you can get.

Event Details

For PvE servers, visits these locations:

Antonica - For those of the good alignments, come meet at the Claymore in Antonica to exchange information and get to know your fellow Qeynosian or Kelethinian!

Commonlands - The Overlord decrees the Crossroads as a suitable meeting location for this year's Guild Faire. Do what's right for Freeport and the allies of the Overlord, and ensure only the strongest of guilds brings honor to Freeport!

For PvP servers, visit these locations:

Qeynos - For those of the good alignments, come meet at the Claymore Plaza in North Qeynos to exchange information and get to know your fellow Qeynosian or Kelethinain!

Freeport - The Overlord decrees Execution Plaza in West Freeport as a suitable meeting location for this year's Guild Faire. Do what's right for Freeport and the allies of the Overlord, and ensure only the strongest of guilds bring honor to Freeport!

The in-game festivities are scheduled Saturday, June 28 through Sunday, June 29; however, you can get ready for the event by visiting our Guild Listing page (COMING SOON!!) to learn more about the participating guilds."

What Class is Best for Raiding?  [ 5 Comments ]
Print News | Category: General News
Is there a best class for raiding? A new player wants to know, what class should I choose if I want to raid?

As you might guess, the answers run the gamut from "play what you like" to "don't make a brawler" and everything in between.

Player Rijacki said it well:
"Remember, there are only 24 positions in a raid. If you take two of one class, you don't have room to take one of another class. It's all about balancing the buffs (what a class brings to a group or the raid), the debuffs (how the target can be prevented from doing it's thang), the damage output (how fast something will die die die), and minimising damage input (no one likes a wipe).

Basically, find a class YOU like and figure out how to play that class to its best."

An interesting discussion on raid classes in general, worth a look if you enjoy the endless debate about which class is "the best."

Voice Chat Preview  
Print News | Category: Official News
Posted on EQ2Players:

"There has been some buzz building for a while that we've teamed up with Vivox to bring you in-game voice chat. That time is nearly upon us. With Game Update 47, voice chat will be available throughout EverQuest II!

However, we really want this to be a high-quality system that you love to use! That's why, just days after Game Update 46 has gone live, we're bringing in-game voice chat to the Test server! We want your feedback!

Here's a little preview of how EverQuest II's voice chat feature will work.

The game automatically creates voice channels for Guilds, Guild Officers, Groups and Raids. You can also create your own custom password-protected user channels similar to how the in-game text chat currently works. These channels are available through a drop-down box in the new Voice Chat window. The Voice Chat window also shows the participants currently in the channel and an icon color indicates who is speaking."

The link above takes you to pictures and a lot more information on how the voice chat will work, in detail. Their set-up includes troubleshooting for your microphone! The system does have cross-server capability.

It looks sweet, and SOE is encouraging everyone to try it out on the test server. This would replace Ventrilo and Team Speak as the voice chat of choice if it works as described.

  Legends of Norrath Releases 'Oathbreaker'  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category:
WITH MORE THAN ONE MILLION LEGENDS OF NORRATH™ MATCHES PLAYED TO DATE, EVERQUEST® FRANCHISE ONLINE STRATEGY GAME INTRODUCES OATHBREAKER TO THE VIRTUAL PLAYMAT

Release Precedes $100K World Championship Event at Gen Con Indy 2008


DENVER, COLO. – June 19, 2008 – Sony Online Entertainment LLC announced the release of Oathbreaker, the fourth set of the EverQuest® franchise online strategy game, Legends of Norrath™. Oathbreaker continues to draw upon the influence of SOE’s groundbreaking EverQuest® franchise, adding fun and familiar elements, including items and characters beautifully illustrated with high-fantasy flair. Oathbreaker will enthrall players with a tale of corruption from within, and redemption that comes at a terrible cost, providing an unprecedented level of drama in the ongoing saga. While Oathbreaker brings the story of your Oathbound hero to a dramatic finale, new gameplay builds on existing functionality and allows for even more creative strategies to emerge.

"In just over nine months since launch, Legends of Norrath has had more than one million matches,” said Scott Martins, director of development, SOE Denver. “And, with the introduction of Oathbreaker, many more matches are on the horizon. There are more ways than ever to enhance your strategies, explore different decks and try out new combos. We're eager to introduce the new raid and open up cooperative play. And, with the Monthly Sprint Tournaments that started in early June, there are more opportunities for players to climb the rankings and earn special promo cards and free loot.”

Not only does Oathbreaker complete the story arc which originated with LoN’s first set, Oathbound – it’s also the last LoN release before the World Championship event at Gen Con Indy, August 14-17, where up to 32 finalists are slated to compete for up to $100,000 in cash prizes. July 12-13 marks the last opportunity to compete in a monthly online Championship Qualifier tournament before the World Championship event. At Gen Con, two days of qualification heats, open to all Gen Con attendees 18 and older, will take place before the final championship day.

Developed at SOE’s Denver studio, Legends of Norrath is the only online strategy game based on and playable from SOE’s popular EverQuest® and EverQuest® II MMOs, as well as from a stand-alone client using a free SOE Station Account (available at www.station.com). Players can challenge opponents on their game server and across other EQ and EQII servers.

Oathbreaker’s features include:

New Cards – More than 250 new cards for your online arsenal! Collect all 210 base set cards, four starter decks featuring 16 new quests and three random rares, 10 scenario rewards, fantastic new loot cards for EverQuest and EverQuest II and lots of new promotional cards.

Play For Free - SOE is providing all registered LoN users even more complimentary content and bonus features in the game client, including ten new scenarios that tell the tale of Oathbreaker and a brand new raid. Go to www.legendsofnorrath.com for complete details.

New Raid Play – In the Antechamber of Fate, up to four players can face off against the powerful island guardian Chel’Drak, the dragon turtle!

New Gameplay – “Charge” is a new keyword that provides additional bonuses to cards while players have, or get rid of, their charge tokens. Re-linkable navigation offers greater flexibility in play by allowing linked cards to be linked again to a new target.

New Faces - Delve into new avatar races (Froglok and Erudite), unit types, legends, keywords and abilities. Explore more of your favorite EverQuest and EverQuest II classes with the introduction of “unarmed” Monks, Coercers, Templars and Beastlords (with their warder pets)!

Oathbreaker cards will be available via digital download from SOE’s Station Store in both 58-card digital starter decks for $9.99 (plus any applicable taxes), and 15-card digital booster packs for $2.99 (plus any applicable taxes).

To get started, players can download the client for free, test their skills 24 hours a day against AI and real opponents, and then head over to the tournament lobby to compete. Monthly Championship Qualifier eligibility limited and subject to Official Rules. Residents of certain territories are not eligible to participate in LoN tournaments with rewards. Please see the EULA for details. For a schedule of events, rules and further details, see the Events Calendar and the Tournaments page.

For more information on Legends of Norrath, visit the website at www.legendsofnorrath.com. New assets for Legends of Norrath Oathbreaker can be found at www.SOEpress.com; interviews are available upon request.

Blurring Reality and Fantasy?  [ 2 Comments ]
Print News | Category: General News

A recent thread has cropped up complaining about the story line of the slavery of the Frogloks and equating it to condoning real life slavery.

Of course, that led to references to the classic thread "Please remove Shark Fin from the game", where a player argues that in the real world, cutting the fins off sharks is cruel to the animals because they die slowly, so therefore even fantasy games shouldn't feature the practice.

Does content in a fantasy world mean that the designers are approving of the same thing in reality? Should we make fantasy worlds the way some folks think the world should be, or is it okay to base some story lines and situations on events and practices that occurred sometime in human history, good or bad?

Granted, the two situations here don't allow the player much choice to choose good or evil -- if you want to make certain things, you need shark fin, and if you want to raise faction in Kunark so you can run around without a hassle, you need to aid some nasty characters -- but by participating in the game, you aren't necessarily stating you believe such things are right in real life. Other game scenarios allow you to choose your moral path within the story.

The world of Norrath is very different from real life. The stories may borrow events and situations that have parallels in world history, but these events are part of a greater fantasy world that has no basis in reality whatsoever. If you play a necromancer, are you an evil corpse summoning magic user in real life? I doubt it!

Should we take what we participate in seriously? Of course. Should we cease having fun because a fantasy situation reminds us of something abohorrent in real life? Maybe. But please, take it in context. Imagination is not reality.

Today's Patch Message  
Print News | Category:
6/19/2008

Here's a sneak peek at the Game Update Message for Thursday.

*** Items ***

- Scorpion sandwiches no longer have a weapon delay on them. They do, however, still have scorpions.
- The Temporal Chrysalis will no longer disappear if it is used before its proper time.


*** Quests and Events ***


- The lockout for Goru Uldrock, the Reincarnate is now spelled correctly.
- Killing Emperor Draygun should properly update his task once again.


*** Tasks ****

- Fixed the spelling of the quest NPC in the task Simple Arrow for Crescent Reach.


*** NPC ***

- Fixed the reset issues with Bargangle Tinkerson in Meldrath's Mansion.



- The EverQuest Team

Developer Nodyin added:

"This is an odd patch. We have some stuff that just *has* to go out in the spells file for some events that will happen in the next few weeks but we can't push out all the spell changes we have because of the focus effect changes we're working on over on Test. So we had to do a special merge of just a few changes into the spells file we'll be pushing out tomorrow to just get the stuff we needed without pushing in-flux focus changes.

There are also a number of pretty minor bug fixes we're doing on the design side. "

And Developer Ngrath explained why they mention the smallest details:

"People complained when we did not put stuff we did in the patches... so we put the changes we did in the patches, even the minor stuff.

The real simple stuff like spelling, item flags and such very simple things, are almost never the reason for a patch, and are just included to do what you guys wanted... tell even the little things we did."
