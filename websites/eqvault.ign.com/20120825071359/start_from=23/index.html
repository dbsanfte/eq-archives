Developer Comments on GU 49  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
Developer Ilucide has written a clear explanation on EQ2Players about why the new experience gains are being put into the game, which you can read below.

Basically, the average time to get a new character to level 80 is currently 260 hours. (Did you know they kept track of this stuff?) Most of that time was the journey from 20-70. Ilucide comments, "What we didn't change much were the bookmarks, which is to say, we didn't change the 1-20 experience much, nor did we change the 70-80 a whole lot either." The goal for GU 49 is to reduce the time to get to 80 at about 100 hours of play.

He argues that the changes are not about making the game easier, but rather having a better experience through the mid levels from 20-70. It's also a way to get veterans to help out newbies, he explains in his post.

You will also be rewarded for getting characters to the level cap, and having alts. In GU 50, they are planning on implementing a 10% account-wide xp bonus for every character you have at level cap (obviously level 80 right now). This bonus will stack for each character you get to 80, up to a maximum of a 50% bonus.

The reality of the mid-level game today is that there are few opportunities for the grouping we had when the game first came out. With most players maxed out, the lower tier levels are often empty. The leveling of alts is also a goal of many players, and many of them don't want to do the same content over again at the original pace. This change will shift the popular areas to the 70+ game, but will probably help with more opportunities to group at that level, since so many players will be working on these levels at the same time.

For those people who enjoy the journey, it's still possible -- you can turn off combat experience, or mentor lower level players through the content.

The economy certainly will be affected. With the increased drop of adepts and the speed of leveling, prices on vendored items are bound to drop. There just won't be the need for the mid-level gear that will be replaced rapidly. Tradeskillers will have to grind through the mid-levels as well so they can produce goods that will sell at the T8 tiers.

Some players are quite upset with the change, and wish they would allow it to be an optional experience bonus. The developers had an interesting dilemma. The reality of the game's population had to be weighed against the wishes of some players to enjoy the entire game, rather than just race to the level cap. Clearly they decided that it is better to push players through the mid level content than force them to linger through older tiers.

      Advice on Farming AA  [ Comment ]
    Print News | Category: General News
    There's plenty of folks out there who haven't got maxed alternative advancement points, and are wondering just how to get the necessary AA for their characters.

    Join the AA farming advice thread on EQ2Players and see what strategies other players have come up with.

    The most efficient way is probably to solo through quests. Try some of the longer quest lines with friends, too. Don't forget all those grey quests you haven't done -- you can turn on all quests on your options so you can see grey quest givers.

    The Halflings in Enchanted Lands also give AA -- but not Rivervale, sorry.

    Preview of Befallen  [ Comment ]
    Print News | Category: General News
    The haunted citadel of Befallen, Halls of the Forsaken, awaits adventurers in the Commolands when the Shadow Odyssey expansion goes live.

    "Once there was a grand fortress of benevolence beneath the Commonlands. Now, there is a cursed dungeon. This is Befallen, the ancient and haunted underground citadel that once was home to the now defunct Order of Marr's Fist. They built this fortress to serve as the launching point for their efforts to defend the Commonlands against orcs and giants. In this, they were successful... Sadly, their victory was short lived and a powerful evil invaded the fortress and brought about the horrific deaths of the noble knights. "

    Much of the story of Befallen, with some ties to the haunted stronghold of Stormhold, lies in the tale of Gynok Moltar:

    "...Gynok Moltor was once a Guard Captain of Qeynos. Cursed by the High Shaman of the Blackburrow gnolls, Opalla, he was exiled from Qeynos. As he traveled Norrath, those around him fell victim to the curse and Gynok spiraled down into despair and self-loathing. Still a fearsome warrior, despite the curse, Gynok's prowess in battle inspired the knights of Marr and helped them renew their battle for the Commonlands."

    Dev Gninja says: "The true story behind Gynok Moltar lies in Befallen. Me thinks you might learn more there. His ties to Stormhold lie primarily with the BBC [bone bladed claymore sword]."

    Wednesday, 10 September, 2008


      Today's Update  [ Comment ]
    Print News | Category: Official News
    The development team has fixed some problems players were complaining about this week -- namely, the Iksar crest color problem and the rare spawn table of the bighorn sheep in the new Everfrost quest for the charm item. Thanks team for responding so quickly!

    The furor over the Everfrost quest, "Peace with the Valkeries" brings up an interesting problem. Despite it being a lower level grey quest for anyone over 50, the charm slot reward is better than many T8 items available for that slot. Many high level players are flocking to Everfrost to get this charm, which caused hard feelings on some servers when the people who the quest was intended for had to compete against everyone else for rare spawns (that would be the sheep, now fixed to include the common dasher sheep). The inclusion of more spawns to free up the bottleneck in the zone doesn't fix the problem of itemization for this particular quest reward, which seems a little skewed. Far be it from me to complain, though, since I already did the quest to get it for my level 80 character!

    Here's the patch message:

    GAMEPLAY

    Iksar crests are now using the correct texture.
    Addressed a server stability issue.
    Mages should now find a rare drop on Meng BahDashs loot table.
    Defeating the taskmaster no longer allows respawn in Travenro's chamber in Veeshan's Peak.
    "Peace with the Valkyries" in Everfrost now updates off of both the sheep and the dashers.

    SPELLS

    Mystic

    The Spirit of the Bull line should now toggle correctly when cast.

    Wizard

    The Surging Tempest line of spells should now stack properly when cast by multiple wizards.
