Game Mechanics
Gordon posted a reply to a player-made post regarding some of EQ's gameplay mechanics on the official boards. Here's the thread:
    Oh, I was worried for a second. People say "confrontation" and I assume that they mean that someone was wronged or did something bad :)Ok, on to your issues:

    I asked him: "So what your saying is, in the world of Norrath if your not able to recieve harm from your enemies, your "cheating" or breaking the rules."

    He said: "Well, look at this way, while your up on the roof the monsters don't know that you exist."

    Well actually, the creatures don't know how to get to you, and since they don't know how to get to you, they *settle* for attacking the people in your party. Though this may sound like a reasonable tactic, it's really not. The existence of a world-geometry feature shouldn't make you, for all intents and purposes, invulnerable to attack. If we didn't make acts such as this illegal, *everyone* would then do it, and then the game is a joke.

    A standing rule in EQ is that any creature should be able to get to you anywhere. If they can't, then it's a bug (in this case, a pathing bug). Now it could be looked at in one of two ways, either the creature doesn't know how to get to you and that's a bug, or you were allowed to get someplace that you shouldn't have, and that's a bug. In either case, using a bug for personal gain is exploitation, and that's why a warning would be issued.

    But by the Guide's logic ("...the monsters don't know that you exist."), zoning with a train or a single creature on your rear is just as illegal.

    Zoning is an acceptable and intended method of breaking combat. It's not a bug that the critter doesn't follow you through the zone. It is a bug that he doesn't go up on top of the house.

    Gordon, please explain where I am wrong

    If AI (artificial intelligence) ever gets to the point that it is as adaptable as human intelligence, then you wouldn't be wrong, as the critter could figure out how to get up there. Until then, we have to make allowances for the fact that a computer will never be as smart as the player encountering it (most of them anyway :).

    From now on, if you find a place of saftey, other than the next zone over, from your enemies... then by all means use it! This is a smart idea because you have a better chance of living and defeating your enemy.

    Well, that would get you booted from the game, per my comments above.

    From now on, so that law abiding citizens of Norrath will not get into trouble with a Guide: all known "safe areas" where creatures cannot harm you should be posted on the EverQuest web site somewhere, and should be regularly be checked in-game by Guides for exploitation

    Well they are checked as people /petition to report exploitation. Quite frankly, we don't want the guides to have to waste time by popping from one possible exploit site to another just to make regular rounds. If someone reports exploitation, then we'll go there. We might also stop by if a new area has opened up and become a free for all.

    As for listing all of the places on the site, I'll be honest, it won't happen. To use an analogy (and I love analogies), suppose a bank security system has a bug where all the doors and the vault unlock from 2-3am. Would they announce this to everyone? No, but they would still bust the guy who "wanders" into the vault completely by accident :) Yes, I know, they'd post a security guard too, but you get my point.

    Neither can I see Verant doing... but hopefully some middle ground can be met, and action can be taken that matches the general idea of this post. I honestly didn't know that was an illegal spot to be, and got an official warning from a Guide

    The middle ground is that we give people the benefit of the doubt and issue a warning. The guides will tell you, much as this one did, that anything not being able to get to you is a bug, therefore standing in someplace that insures this to be so is exploitation.

    You needn’t worry about getting a warning for something like this. It happens to many people. We only worry about people who get several for similar infractions.

    -Gordon

Origin of EverQuest
Verant Vice President Brad McQuaid talked about the origin of EverQuest in a thread discussing the Dikumud issue on Usenet recently. Here's the complete post:
    Ruadh, not sure who you are, but anyway, Graelov was his name and he's still on the EQ team (not sure why you called him my nemesis).

    Anyway, just to clear the whole Sony/989/Verant thing up (I talked about this at GDC too):

    EverQuest began as a project in March of 1996 when Steve Clover and I were hired by John Smedley, then Director of Development, at Sony Interactive Studios America (which is a division or some such of Sony Computer Entertainment America). Over time, the EQ team grew to over 30 people involved with the project. Some time later (the exact date escapes me) SISA changed their branding name to 989 Studios (and 989 Sports). Then, in January of 1999, the PC/online portion of 989 Studios (meaning the entire EQ team as well as everyone else working on PC stuff) spun off into their own company called Verant Interactive, Inc. We then finished up EQ and released it in March 1999. Sony still owns EQ, but we work on it and maintain it.

Gordon Infobits
Gordon Wrinn has been very active on the official boards today. Here's the scoop on all the latest info:
    Verant's plans to deal with inaccuracies in the current manual
    We will have a new printed manual in the boxed set. I'm not exactly sure if an electronic version of it will be patched but I'll ask around. As for inaccuracies in the FAQ, as I mentioned a few days ago, the FAQ will be updated to reflect the current state of the game.

    EverQuest's Client Price
    [Quote from original message: Well, Everquest has been out for 6 months now and they still want $40 for the software ]

    Well actually it's been out for a year, and yes, we still want $40 for the software :)

    On Pets
    [Quote from original message: Wouldn't it be possible that, with a level 50+ pet spell, it can be called something like "Summon undead familiar", which would randomly summon an undead pet base on the zone you're in]

    Yes, that would be possible, but not every zone has undead models and textures in it, aside from the skeleton. Now we are working on some cool things, but as I mentioned before, it's too soon to comment.

    Response to a Suggestion
    [Quote from original post: My idea is very simple, and would benefit every single person in EQ. Instead of having ONE main window that displays ALL of the data in EQ, make two. One for spells, melee dmg, etc and one for JUST /say, /groupsay, /guild, /ooc, /shout, and emotes.]

    We've definitely seen this suggestion before, but due to the same limitations that keeps us from reworking the entire UI for higher resolutions, we won't be able to employ it. The interface as it stands is locked.

    Star Wars Patch Message
    The patch server is one method we use to give information to our players that they might find interesting. It's very clear at the opening that it's a press release for a different game, so I doubt we're wasting any more than a second or two of your time.

    Lack of Updates on "In Develoment" Section on EverQuest.com
    This is on my plate for an update. I'm getting information together as we speak.

    Listening to Players & Loot We listen to our players, but as we've mentioned before, we want the loot and its location to have a context within the game. For instance, when you see someone with a cool sword and you ask, "Wow, cool sword, I bet you had to kill something nasty for that!", we don't want the response to be "Nah, just killed a bunch of undead frogs and one of them finally dropped it".

    Doing Dungeons
    It's a vicious circle. No one can do a dungeon because everything is camped. Everything is camped because no one will do a dungeon.     Observer Staff Nerfed?
    Combat dynamics (hit/miss) are not weapon-specific. The only thing that matters on a weapons is damage, delay, and weight. Your hit percentages aren't at all based on the weapon itself.

    Customer Service
    The number you provided is for the Station's Customer Service, and yes, they do billing and take care of problems with your station account.

    The EverQuest Customer Service department, the people you communicate with when you have a problem with a character, is set up for online-service, not phone-based service. If you need Customer Service for EverQuest, it is provided in-game or by email (eqmail@verant.com). If you can't get in-game, it is then a technical issue that can be resolved by tech support, who, as you mentioned, does answer their phones.

    To provide you with the short answers you are looking for, no, our customer service department does not generally take phone calls. As such, the talk-time statistics that you are requesting are moot.

    Getting Rid of Pets?
    We are certainly serious about class balance, but the amount of complaining that we see really isn't tied to the balance of the classes, but the perception of that balance.

    Eliminating pets would cause all sorts of problems, upset a lot of people, and unbalance the classes to boot. I don't think we'll be pursuing that route.

    Random Chances
    Random chances are random chances. In other words, if the thing were to go 22 hours without dropping a cowl, it is no more or no less likely to drop the cowl on the next spawn. Each spawn gives an equal percentage chance that the item you want will be there. In other words, a person with 22 hours at a spawn has no greater "rights" to the spawn than anyone else. The previous kills that you made did not count as any work towards getting the item.

    Sharing Spawns
    We do realize how long it takes. If I had my way, many items would be made much more rare than they are, to the extent that no one would camp for them. People would then do the dungeons, and if they got something cool it would be a real reward.

    No, we're not going to force you to camp. However if you insist on camping, then you'll need to deal with others who insist on camping in a civilized manner.

    These rules change nothing for people who already work well with others. For instance, lets say you are camping something that always has a list going. If someone new comes in, putting them on the list is perfectly acceptable, much the same as you did before.

    These rules don't let them come in and demand that they get the next loot, they only say that you need to work something out, or a GM/Guide will work it out for you.

    Is CoB Still Bugged?
    Yes. The bug is on the list, but I don't have an ETA for when it would be fixed. If anyone has any way to reproduce the bug with any reliability whatsoever, that would go a long way to getting it fixed.

    CoB Cont.: Why Don't GM's Compensate Players For Experience Loss?
    There's no way to tell with any certainty that this happened. I would recommend that if it happens in the future, you do a cancel-magic on it.

    On Dragons
    We could probably do something like this if Dragons were truely rare. Nothing in the game is this rare though, not even the Gods. Of course if we were to make them rare enough to support something like this, I imagine that we'd upset more than a few people.

    More on Kill-Stealing/Play Nice Policies
    [Quote from original post: EQ is its own society as you have stated, it's own world then what type of society is it? captalism? communist? socialist?] Actually, those are economic principles.

    These new rules merely provide guidelines for people to follow. They encourage people to work out disagreements themselves, and state that any party to a disagreement can call in an arbitrator if they can't work something out.

    Oh, and I should mention that the way things used to be were more of a feudal system bordering on anarchy than anything else.

    New Policy Continued
    The purpose of the new rules is totally the opposite. It's to protect the average player from the people you mentioned. It encourages people to work out issues on their own, and gives the GMs and Guides an avenue to follow to get involved if necessary. The time of the CS Staff person saying "I'm sorry, but we can't get involved in these issues" is over.

    People in general have asked for a higher level of customer service, and we're going to try to give it to them.

Producer Letter: Lets Play Nice
The new Producer Letter detailing the new GM/Guide policies has been posted on the official site. Here's the letter in it's entirety:
    3/14/2000

    Hello Everyone,

    Back in October, Jeff Butler, EverQuest’s Customer Service Manager, outlined some new Customer Service Guidelines in the Producers Letter. Shortly after these policies were publicized, we announced that their implementation would be postponed pending some revisions necessary to insure enforceability and consistency across the servers and different levels of our Customer Service organization. Last week, our policy team completed their public revision of those policies, and we are ready to implement them at this time. Before we get in to the wording of the "Play Nice Policies", I’d like to take a moment to discuss their spirit, and why we feel that they are necessary.

    EverQuest continues to astound everyone involved with it almost daily. Nearly one year since we opened our doors, EverQuest boasts a community of current players numbering nearly 200,000, most of whom still play on a daily basis. In all actuality, EverQuest has gone beyond what could be described with a term such as "community". We are all, in fact, part of the EverQuest Society.

    Like any society, each person has the ability to place his or her mark upon it. The vast majority of people in our society do their best to insure that their mark is positive, by abiding to the laws that we, much like the government, bring forward. Some of you choose to become pinnacles of honor, dignity, and respect in your individual communities by forming guilds, promoting honorable actions by your members, and by supporting EverQuest on your web-sites.

    Also like any society, we have our underbelly, a relatively small number of people who live to prey upon the honorable. It is frequently the goal of these people to see to their desires, no matter the effect of their actions upon others around them. They are the ones who claim ownership of servers, zones, or spawns, and cause or threaten harm to anyone who does not share their disregard and contempt. They are the ones who live, not to enjoy the game with everyone else, but to enjoy at everyone else’s expense.

    For the first few months after EverQuest’s release, we felt that a policy of non-interference in many of these matters was warranted. However, we continued to lose good players. This was not due to any deficiency or dissatisfaction in the game, but due to dissatisfaction with the treatment that they received from their fellow players, and the perceived inability of our Customer Service department to intervene. Late last year, we made a commitment to our players to begin playing an active role in many of these situations.

    The intent of these policies is to provide the players with general guidelines for what is or is not acceptable behavior in EverQuest, and give them with the opportunity to work out differences prior to involving the EverQuest Customer Service Staff. Naturally, in a game as multifaceted as EverQuest, we are not able to cover every possible issue that could arise as part of these policies. In these cases, it is the spirit of a rule that will prevail over any discrepancies in the letter.

    Play Nice Policies: A revision to the GM/Guide FAQ and the Rules of Conduct.

    The policies below are now in effect and will be reflected in the GM - Guide/Conduct FAQ in the near future.

      Kill Stealing
      Kill Stealing will now be regarded as disruption and will result in disciplinary action when witnessed by any EverQuest Customer Service Representative (EQCSR). The EQCSR will review these situations on an individual basis and issue a decision which is considered binding upon all parties involved. Kill Stealing is defined as the killing of a mob for any reason that is already fighting or pursuing another player or group. The intent of this rule is discourage and make note of habitual Kill Stealers, not to punish those who honestly try to work together or those who make an honest mistake. Its enforcement by the EverQuest Customer Service Staff will reflect this philosophy.

      Contested Spawns
      There are cases where two or more groups wish to kill the same thing. In these cases, the groups are required to compromise. If an equitable compromise cannot be reached between the players prior to EverQuest Customer Service Staff involvement, the EQCSR will mandate a binding compromise. Refusing to abide by a compromise mandated by an EQCSR will be considered disruption. It is therefore strongly suggested that the groups make every attempt to reach a compromise that they can live with prior to involving an EQCSR, who may mandate a compromise that does not suit you to the extent that a player-devised compromise would.

      Note: A "group" in this case is defined as a party of one or more characters that are united in a common belief or goal and are capable of completing that goal.

      Foul Language
      Excessive use of foul language in an inappropriate context, including swear words, real-world racial slurs, and other language that is not consistent with the fantasy environment and designed to hurt, will be considered a disruption. The existence of the filter (/filter) is not a license to be profane.

      Harassment
      Harassment is defined as specifically targeting another player or group of players to harm or inconvenience them. Harassment can take many forms, as it goes to the state-of-mind of the person or party on the receiving end of the action. However, in order to account for those who are excessively thin-skinned, the EQCSR involved will make a determination as to whether or not the average person would feel "harassed" and act accordingly.

      Zone/Area Disruption
      Zone/Area Disruption is defined as any activity designed to harm or inconvenience a number of groups rather than a specific player or group of players. This includes things such as:

      • Monopolizing most or all of the kills in an area.

      • Deliberately blocking a doorway or narrow area so other players cannot get past.

      • Refusing to cooperate with the other parties at a contested spawn site after having been instructed to do so by an EQCSR.

      • Making excessive and inappropriate use of zone-wide communications (/shout, /ooc, /auction).

      Reports of Fraud
      Fraud in all transactions between players will result in disciplinary action when confirmed by an internal EQCSR. Fraud is defined as falsely representing one’s intentions to make a gain at another’s expense. Examples of this activity include but are not limited to offering to recover possessions from the corpse of another player and refusing to return that property to it’s owner as well as using flaws in a secure trade window to deprive someone of one of their items.

      Guilds
      Disciplinary issues involving guilds will also be addressed on a broader basis. Guilds whose members habitually violate any of the Rules of Conduct or Play Nice Policies may be disbanded. In addition, monopolizing numerous spawn areas with the intent to exclude other players will not be permitted. If investigated and verified by an internal EQCSR, monopolizing will result in the disbanding of the guild.

      Training NPCs
      The intentional training of NPCs will result in immediate disciplinary action when witnessed by an EQCSR. We are aware that accidents often happen causing unintentional trains, and will scrutinize each report of this activity closely.

      Abuse
      Though the following have always been in the GM/Guide FAQ, this letter is probably a good place to reiterate some of the items that we consider abuse:

      • Hate Mongering - participation in or propagation of Hate literature, behavior, or propaganda related to real -world characteristics.

      • Sexual Abuse or Harassment - untoward and unwelcome advances of a graphic and sexual nature. This includes virtual rape, overt sexual overtures, and stalking of a sexual nature.

      • Attempting to Defraud a CS Representative - Petitioning with untrue information with the intention of receiving benefits as a result. This includes reporting bug deaths, experience or item loss, or accusing other players of wrongdoing without basis for it.

      • Impersonating a Customer Service Representative - falsely representing yourself to another player as a Guide or a Verant Interactive employee.

      • CS Personnel Abuse -- sending excessive /tells to a CS Representative, excessively using say or other channels to communicate to a CS Representative, making physical threats, or using abusive language against a CS Representative.

      • Using Threats of Retribution by GM Friends - attempting to convince another player that they have no recourse in a disagreement because favoritism is shown to one of the parties by the Verant Interactive or Guide staff. It is recommended that players make judicious use of the /report command when reporting abuse. The proper course of action is to file a /report, then file a petition informing the EverQuest Customer Service Staff that a /report has been filed.

      PvP Servers
      The PvP Servers are a special case for many of the policies listed here. When one makes the decision to play on a PvP Server, they are in essence agreeing to play on a server where compromise is decided on the basis of who has the power to kill whom.

      By the same token, we expect that the people on those servers will apply PvP combat in all situations where it is called for, as a resolution to the problem. As such, the EverQuest Customer Service Staff will decline to intervene in cases where a PvP alternative exists, such as at disputed spawns where the parties involved have the ability to attack each other.

      Though the PvP environment does eliminate a few of the problems experienced on the normal servers, there are a few more PvP-specific problems that are created.

        Causing Experience Loss

        Intentionally causing experience-loss to other players in the PvP environment is illegal in all cases and will result in a warning when witnessed by an EQCSR. This includes things such as intentionally training NPCs, and purposefully allowing an NPC to get the final blow in combat. We do understand that there are cases where the experience-loss is unintentional, and will take no action unless a person earns a reputation of causing unintentional experience-loss.

        Much like how the Kill Stealing rule applies to those on the blue servers, this rule is designed to discourage and make note of those who do it habitually, thus betraying the spirit of PvP. As a note, the EQCSR will not in any case provide a resurrection for someone who was caused experience loss in PvP, intentionally or otherwise. This is one of the dangers of playing on a PvP server.

        Bind-point/Corpse Camping

        Killing someone over and over again while at his or her bind-point or while they are in the process of retrieving their corpse is illegal and will result in a warning when witnessed by an EQCSR. You are expected in all cases to give someone a reasonable amount of time to recover their corpse an leave the area prior to attacking again.

        Conversely, the person recovering their corpse is expected to do so and retreat to safety promptly. In other words, sitting next to your corpse and taunting someone while daring them to attack you so that you can /petition them is bad form, and would result in a warning if witnessed by an EQCSR.

        The EQCSR in attendance will decide what is reasonable in these cases. It is highly recommended that the people involved make equitable arrangements prior to involving the EverQuest Customer Service Staff.

      Roleplay
      Though EverQuest is a Role-playing game, the claim of "Role-play" will not be accepted in defense of any of the antisocial behaviors mentioned above. As an example, you are in no case (PvP or otherwise) allowed to "Train" a guard onto an enemy in protection of your homeland. In another example, a rogue is not allowed to steal someone’s corpse under the guise of role-playing a thief.

      By all means we want to encourage you to play your role, we just cannot allow that role-play to be done at another’s out-of-game expense.

    To quote the Producers Letter from October 20, 1999, Verant GM-Administrators review records of disciplinary incidents on a daily basis. Action is taken based on the severity and nature of the offense, and/or the number of warnings issued to the customer. These actions may include but are not limited to, temporary suspension or permanent banning from the game. Be advised that you may not receive any notification or warnings, in-game or otherwise, prior to disciplinary action being taken against your account.

    We ask that everyone give these new policies some time before judging their effectiveness. Our intent is to further our philosophy that no one player should ever be allowed to intentionally ruin the gameplay experience of another, a philosophy to which we are firmly committed. These policies will continue to adapt as we learn new ways to deal with particular issues, and discover new issues that need to be specifically addressed.

    Thank you,
    Gordon Wrinn
    Internet Relations Manager
    Verant Interactive, Inc.

Too many seek the glory counted in gold and silver. Too Many seek glory, not honor or valor, in wielding lost treasure of hoards forgotten and claimed by rebel wurms.

The Dark One follows no god, no he brokes no interference from mere mortals, but if I am to honor My Father's Will, My Vows to Him, The Duke of Below, this Childer of Underfoot, will stop him before it is too late, or die forever in trying.

Upon my warnings to the crowd, he turned to stare upon the presumptuous golden dwarf, who glared up at him.

"You seek powers that are not yours to Claim Mayong. Ye should be dust long forgotten."

Mayong sneered at me, and then turned to his servant ordering the death of the dragon once he gained the knowledge of the shard's likely location. Rainy's Reed and song of prophecy had already these clues, but the bright song of this elven lass surely burned too deep for Mayong to hear the warnings amongst the clues and words. I hummed the tune and spoke her words, though no bard, my voice is pure as stone unweathered, deep and foreboding. Mayong shivered and looked upon me once more and then departed as his servant fled to do his bidding. The curious, the honorless, followed like sheep to Daugnor's Cauldron where the Rogue doth hide.

The Rogue's price was surely met, and one inured to evil most foul, surely claimed Galendore's belt as a trophy, and perhaps even fell sway tot eh Dark one's charms and imbibed the blood of the eternal one, becoming bound to him for eternity as a slave, his soul ever forfeit.

I traveled not with the sheep or to do an evil ones bidding. I had a Dwarf's task to do. Good had fallen in service to her god, and I was empowered to restore life, where death had laid claim once before.

The young dragon was beyond my reach and the powers of Brell, for the magic bestowed upon the Faithful could not pierce the Veil...yet. This was small comfort that the veil was yet strong enough to protect us from the wakening wurms sleeping where their Queen and God had left them on their own.

Soon the Dragon faded to mist and departed to where dragon's go when they die. Perhaps to his All-mudder and Queen-god. Then this lore is still lost to me yet. But Much lore of Norrath and Dragon's I still hold too dear to tell easily, then again these are desperate times.

The Bard I tried to raise several times, each resurrection was met by a force that drained me of all magic and left me as weak as a gnome newly birthed. I tried several times, but even use of Brell's own holy waters would not raise this hero anew. I fear that the blade of Lord Galendore was one most foul and drank more than the young lass's blood. That Blade will too be destroyed as will the servants of Mayong and Himself.

A Child of hate appeared upon the grasses of Steamfront in full light of day, to see what the chaos was about. I told her and others gathered there, the lay and reed of Rainy Dragonfriend as best I remembered it. Tears coursed my craggy visage as anger, despair, hate, and then hope as a course was set upon me as clear as a mithril vein in the dark of below. I sang, I retold of the dangers.

The Portal goes both ways. And a Dragon's Memory is forever. And Of Vengeance they do dream.

For Faydwer is in Danger I know. For upon the grassy knolls of Steamfront lays the corpse, a moldering skeleton of one such great beast, sure to have kin who will one day seek revenge.

Kaladim, home to my kin, was torn from a Dragon, to be claimed as our own. By a hero of Olde, dwarven stout. The Hero Jendl Mizebrite, with his Hammer crushed its skull. For this Our Home is so named in his Honor. KALADIM I seek. For the Hero, maker of the great avenger axe, lost an arm in this battle. Kaladim the hammer that slays dragons. This I seek to save my Kin, to save Faydwer, and to save Norrath.

Should I find Kaladim, it may not be enough.

For this I ask all who hear my tale to heed that which brought you and yours here to these lands, once claimed by the Queen's own children. (Hush, now speak not her name, lest from her troubled dreams awaken too early for us to have a chance. Hush now and listen)

Long ago, Brell came to Norrath, from deep below, the dragon's home and carved the Vault so his children may have way into this world. Many were these children, first among them the Dwarfs, my kin.

Soon the other gods came to Norrath, and he counseled with each, to forge the First Pact of the Gods. The Great Mother, Tunare, and Prexus, and Rallos Zek were the second to come, all agreed to the pact except Rallos Zek.

The World was divided up for the purpose of keeping the Wurmqueen in Check. Each created creatures to watch the dragons and remain ever vigilant. The Dwarves, the Kedge, and the elves, Rallos created the Giants to Defend the land. (ohh how the mighty have fallen, but that is another tale, hush let me quench my thirst and I will continue)

Then came jealous Innoruuk, stealing Tunare's children and twisted them to his whims, thus came the dark elves. The Teir`Dal. Bristle bane and Cazic Thule cam next. Rallos Zek ever suspicious remained watching from afar. Soon they too were brought in to Honor the PACT. To watch and guard all of Norrath from the Dragon Kind.

Then there were great wars, too long to recount here, and many of the elder races fell from such graces as can dimly be remembered, or even imagined.

Soon the last of the Gods came to the lands. Mithaniel Marr God of Valor, And Erollisi Marr Goddess of Love, their children the Barbarians came to the land, and then came the age of blood.

Not much is known about the coming of the Humans, and perhaps the gods wish it so, but they are the rumored children of the lost Combine Empire. Much is lost still, and yet to be found.

Then Came Solusek Ro, jealous too of the elves, he burned their home in Tunaria forcing them to flee to Faydwer. (Tunaria is the olde name for Antonica)

Of the Necromancers and erudites, I speak not of yet, but their wars have wounded Norrath to the deep, and our Father's Vault is uncovered and disturbed. This is a danger to Norrath still, but that hole yet contains its dangers from all.

I remind you, of the story of Rainy, of the Shard of Nature unknown, of Mayong's desires to claim its powers as his own.

He meddles in powers that we the elder races were set upon this land to guard against.

For should this Shard of crystal (yes I know what this shard is indeed, for I read the histories and have broken stone since I was an infant, tis something every dwarf suffers in the mines at one time or another, Hush I cannot say more right now, lest that dark one learn more to this mystery too soon)

Mayong seeks many things we may not understand, but he who forsake the light of day, and the breath of light, for something darker and eternal, seeks even more power. Greed we can all see and understand, Though immortal, Mayong suffers this flaw too well, as do many others.

Should he gain this shard, he could by design or chance, awaken the Wurmqueen from her slumber to the woe of all in Norrath. Or perhaps he seeks an ancient Dragon to become his slave, and to consume it's blood, and twist a dragon's power to his own? Mayhap. If so, woe to us all.

Fear and Greed lead to jealousy, the dark one wants to be counted among the gods. The shard is his key. He uses servants, vampires, fools for tools, and waves power and treasure to the easily blinded to serve his means. (No, I have never been fooled foul dark one)

We could argue the theories for an eternity, but we should fail in our task.

What task? I spoke of it before. I am a keeper of the Pact of Gods. Ever watchful, ever cautious, the dragons stir. Dark Evil Plans mischief on a scale that the gods should be worried.

So, by the Pact of the Gods I call upon the Elder Races to Send forth their Clerics, their shaman, their Druids and their holy me and women to me.

One we form our Council, we shall call a truce between the races, till the Pact be honored, and Mayong is Dust forever, or we lie rotting in the sun. And Should the Dragon;s awaken, before this war is done, our strength's will be needed to protect that which we took for our own.

I related this amongst other facts to the crowd where I stood vigil by the fallen hero, slain by the blade dark and foul of Lord Galendore.
