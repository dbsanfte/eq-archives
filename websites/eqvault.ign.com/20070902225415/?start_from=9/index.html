9th Year Veteran Reward on Test Server  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Tadena posted:

"The New Vet Reward is on Test if you wish to see it and feedback on it

New AA called "Armor of Experience"

This Ability grants you several layers of protection from harm. As you take damage each layer will absorb five strikes before failing. As your armor fails you will take more and more damage."

Developer Nodyin explained: "It's a melee guard that goes through 4 stages of protection from 100% down to 25% before fading."

Nodyin also explained a little more about it in the same thread:

"In designing this AA I was very conscious of balance concerns. This AA is actually unique in EQ up to this point. I had to have several changes made to the way the spell system and some of the spell effects worked to get this to happen. Part of the issue was changing the spell system so that we could create this AA in a way that would both scale from level 1 to 75 and that wouldn't be overpowered at any point.

Does it have a gameplay impact? Yes, but not to the extent that it will change the balance of content. Is it useful? I think so, and I hope you agree."

The refresh time was four hours intially, but has since been changed to 20 hours as are the other veteran reward AAs.

  Former IGE VP Tapped for SOE  [ 4 Comments ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
David Christiansen, former Vice President of Business Development for IGE, will now be the Vice President of Business Development & International Operations for Sony Online Entertainment. In this newly created role, Christensen will be responsible for generating strategic global growth opportunities and securing key business alliances for SOE, with an emphasis on the European and Asian markets. In addition to his business development responsibilities, Christensen is tasked with managing SOE’s international operations group. Christensen will be based in SOE’s new Los Angeles studio and will report to John Needham, Chief Financial Officer and Senior Vice President for Sony Online Entertainment. Christensen is a veteran of the video games industry with a broad range of business experience, including development partnerships, acquisitions, licensing and business affairs.

SOE's official press release states that "prior to joining SOE, Christensen served as Vice President of Business Development for IGE where he drove the company’s relationships with the massively multiplayer publishing community."

MMO players are certainly familiar with IGE, since it is the largest company behind real money transactions, or selling gold in online games. I am not sure what "relationships" the press release is referring to, since most MMOs are still calling real money transactions a black market, and certainly buying platinum and powerleveling services is not condoned in EverQuest.

In past years, IGE bought up many of the EverQuest fan sites, including OGaming, and used them as advertising platforms for the gold selling market.

All is fair in business, however, and I am sure Mr. Christensen can figure out ways to make SOE, including EverQuest, much more profitable using his prior experience in global marketing. The irony, however, is inescapable.

Buried Sea Review by Loral  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Loral from mobhunter.com has written a strong commentary on his most recent impressions of The Buried Sea expansion, Mobhunter: A Late, Incomplete, and Pessimistic Review of the Buried Sea, and while you may not agree with his conclusions, he makes some good points about the nature of video games in general.

He writes, "My time is the most important commodity I have. I only have a few hours every week to play these games. More and more hobbies take up that time and I have to decide exactly where my time is best spent. "

I think that gets right down to it, when all is said and done. Games must make us feel like the time spent playing them is worthwhile, otherwise, what is the point? Most of us do only have a limited amount of time to spend on entertainment, and it's up to the game designers to make sure we enjoy ourselves.

An excellent review as always from Loral. Check it out.

Missing Character Issue Post Patch  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
It was like a nightmare .... finally the red sticks were gone, but my main character, Syncere, was not on my character list! Where was she? My character list was full, stating I had 8 out of 8 characters (didn't I used to have 10?!), but no main? How could this be? Did I delete her by accident? Impossible.

I immediately logged on an alt, starting complaining to my guild mates in Crimson Talon, and petitioned. There was nothing in the knowledge base discussing the problem, at all. What could have caused this? My guild assured me that SOE could get my cleric back, even if she had been erased (how could that have happened, though?)

So I started doing some digging. In EQ2, if your character disappears, it might be because you cancelled Station Access and lost your extra character slots. Since I should have had more character slots than I had today, maybe deleting a character to make room for another would work. Following this train of thought, I deleted a level 3 alt I once made, since she had no gear to speak of and was expendable. And there she was, my cleric, magically appearing at the bottom of my character list.

I logged her on to let my guild know the raid cleric was not gone forever, and almost immediately got a petition from the GM. The response time was under 30 minutes between the time I petitioned and got a personal response -- very nice. I was very gratified that the GM was so responsive to my issue. Turns out this was not just my problem, it was a lot of people's. Many (or all) of those people who had 10 character slots filled up lost two of them as a result of the patch to remove the stick figures.

Some April fools joke, eh?

To resolve this issue, petition, and if you must have your missing character(s) back immediately, you can delete one (or two). For those who cannot delete one or more of their 10 characters to get an important one back, they must wait to have their issue resolved through technical support. Of course, no one should ever have to delete a character as a result of a technical problem (in my case the deletion of a level 3 alt was not a big deal, but that's not the point).

Doing a little more research, I found a thread on the technical support board addressing this issue. It is most likely the result of the patch to remove the stick figures. If you had ten character slots on your EverQuest account, you are probably down to eight, and the ones at the bottom of the list will not appear. I am told that Technical Support is working on a fix for this issue.

I don't know a worse feeling than logging on and having a beloved character mysteriously gone. Luckily, Syncere was just hiding. I do hope that they post something about this in the knowledge base, and not just in the tech support forums.

It is unfortunate that a lighthearted patch for Bristlebane Day ended up being not so funny for a lot of people. Let's hope this gets resolved soon.


-- Kwill @ 10:12AM Tue 03 Apr 2007
