    Release Notes  [ Comment ]
    Print News | Mail News
    Here are the release notes from Tuesday's update:

    Tuesday July 20th Update

    - Augments can now fit into multiple slot types. On an augment the text could read "This augment can fit in these slot types: 3, 4".

    - Zoning pets will now properly save their taunt toggle settings.

    - Spell damage messages done to yourself will no longer be displayed for others. Cannibalize and Mana Convert spells were examples of this.

    - Summoning a Sand Giant in Oasis with Monster Summoning will no longer cause your pet the next time you zone to be HUGE! Monster Summoned pets will no longer save their size

    - Pets will no longer poof when you get Call of Hero'd.

    - Weapon augmentations will again proc properly

    - The EverQuest process is now more friendly. There is also a slider bar to set your Max. FPS. in the Options window under display settings. This will free up more resources while running EQ. Also, when EQ loses focus it will use less resources.

    - New commands for consent:

    /consent group -- flags your current corpses so they can be dragged by all of your current group members

    /consent raid -- flags your current corpses so they can be dragged by anyone in your raid (including people who join the raid later)

    /consent guild -- flags your current corpses so they can be dragged by anyone in your guild

    /deny group -- removes the flag

    /deny raid -- removes the flag

    /deny guild -- removes the flag

      Station Launchpad on Test  
    Print News | Mail News
    Starting soon, EQ will be loading up faster than ever as the Station Launchpad comes to EQ!


    Hey folks,

    Our platform group has put together a new version of Launchpad, just for EverQuest. The new Launchpad system, which is used for all other SOE games, is now being applied to the Test server for you folks to try out. Please log into test, using the Testeverquest.exe icon from your EQ directory and download the Launchpad front end.     Dev Response on Transferring No-Drop Items to Char  
    Print News | Mail News
    Prathun responded to a request to add the ability to transfer No-Drop items to characters on the same account.

    First of all, no_drop equipment is no_drop for a reason. The character holding the gear should be the character who was there to loot the corpse, and was presumably also a participant in the battle that resulted in the item. Got the Belt of Dwarfslaying? You killed Dain. Got Quarm's Hammer? You killed Quarm. Passing that gear onto characters that have never seen those mobs or those zones cheapens value of the accomplishment.

    Secondly, allowing the transfer of no_drop items eliminates an important aspect of player progression. If every alt on an established account begins their life with a Plane of Time gift pack, then they don't have a whole lot of gear upgrades to look forward to.

    Most importantly, your proposal would allow the sale and trade of no_drop equipment. It wouldn't take someone long to realize that they could load up a mule with high end no_drop gear, and then move said mule to another account.

    -Prathun


    You can find the entire thread on the official boards Here
