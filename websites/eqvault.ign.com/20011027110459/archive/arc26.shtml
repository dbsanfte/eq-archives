Move Log Reminder From Alan
- Aurum @ 7:20 PM PST
A Move Log reminder was written and posted to the Gameplay Board by Alan today:

    Folks, please wait until the move log has been opened before trying to use the command. If you use the command before it is opened, your request will not be captured by the log. So if you have already executed the move log, you will need to do it again during the proper window. Here is a copy of my recent post about this:

    We will be opening a new server and moving characters from the E'ci and The Rather severs to this new server upon the request of the customer. If you choose to move your characters to the new server, please be aware that all of your characters on the old server will be moved to the new server.

    To request to have your characters moved to the new server, all you need to do is type "/movelog yes" into the chat window while playing a character on that server during the time that the move log is open.

    We will be opening the move log for these two servers on Friday, March 16th, 2001 at 6:00 pm. PST (which is Saturday morning at 2:00 am GMT). We will be closing the move log on Sunday, March 18th, 2001 at 11:59 pm PST (7:59 am on Monday the 19th GMT). The new, yet unnamed, server should be opened some time during the week of March 19th. We'll announce the exact date as soon as we can.

    There are a few things to keep in mind when choosing to move your characters to the new server. The most important thing to keep in mind is that character corpses will not be moved with the characters. So it is important to make certain that you have looted all of the corpses that you want to loot before the characters are moved.

    Also, characters names are unique on the new server. Because characters will be moving from two separate servers onto the new one, there may be cases where two characters with the same name are created on the new server. Because character names must be unique, one of the two characters will be chosen at random and have an 'x' added to the end of their name. If you find that one of your characters has had an 'x' tacked onto their name after the move and you would rather have a new name, please contact your GM by using the /petition function. The GM will help you to change the name of your character.

    Guild structures do not follow the characters to the new server. We will be unable to reestablish guilds until at least a week has passed after the split. On any day after the new server has been up for eight days, the guild leader can /petition for a GM to help them reestablish the guild. Please do not petition for this before seven days have passed, we will not be able to set up your guild before then.

    We will not be moving characters back to the old server, so make certain that you want to move your characters before you type "/movelog yes". And we will not be moving characters for those that fail to execute the movelog command during the stated window of opportunity.

    Alan


Few More Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 7:18 PM PST
Here is the latest from the man himself:

  • Paladin Epic Quest Problems
    Immortalus wrote:

    [Quote from original post: As of now, the quest involves getting a book from vox and nagafen (and of course, the "new dragons". The only problem is, while almost every other class has a reasonable easy time getting their epics, Verant has made it very, very difficult for paladins. Problem being, since you implimented the level limit on Vox/Nagafen, you have hence punished us paladins who have lvled up. So, basically, if we can manage to get the levels together to kill Gorenaire, etc, then MAYBE we might be lucky to get a book drop. This is ridiculous.]

    One of the designers pulled me to the side and explained something to me about this whole situation.

    Let's look at the whole picture here.

    Most people seem to agree that the way it was before the caps on Naggy and Vox was just fine. The quests were hard, but not too hard. Drops were not too infrequent.

    For characters of the right level, that challenge hasn't changed, so that should still be fine.

    Now, consider that it takes about the same number of people to kill the tougher dragons as it does to kill the old ones (35 or so at the low-end and as few as 18 at the high end). Also realize that the books drop at about the same rate off the old dragons as they do off the new ones.

    So, basically, Paladins have twice as many opportunities to get these book as they did in the past. I would like to know how this is grossly more difficult than any other epic quest?

  • Multi-Questing Ruined on Test?
    We have not put any new code on Test that has anything to do with multi-questing. I'm not saying that we won't, but we haven't at this time made any decisions about any such code, and none have been implemented on Test.

    I just thought I'd let you know.

  • New Threads Drawing Attention to Existing Threads
    Starting new threads to draw attention to other threads is against the board rules.

Alan On "Trival Combat"
- Aurum @ 7:18 PM PST
A note was posted by Alan Vancouvering today concerning "Trival Combat" and other such code:

    I just wanted to pop in here and let you all know that the plan for such code changes, like the 'Trivial Combat' code idea, is to go forward with them. It is unlikely that we'll move that code or others like it back into the existing game.

    The plan is to try the idea out and see how it works. We want to know what you think of it (after trying it out) and then decide if we will include the code in future content. Yes, there is a very small chance that we'll find some code that is so overwhelmingly great that we'll use it in the old world. But we will be reluctant to do so because gauging the impact of a change like that on old content would be all but impossible.

    Alan

Patch Message: 03-14-01
- Aurum @ 5:35 PM PST
Along with a new patch comes a new message explaining what went in and what was changed in Norrath:

    March 14th, 3:00am
    ------------------------------

    ***EverQuest Turns Two***

    Two years ago this Friday, EverQuest was launched, forever changing the course of online gaming. Today EverQuest boasts nearly 360,000 active subscribers, and a two-year retention rate of 65%. Peak usage tops 89,000 simultaneous users with average peak usage at roughly 87,000. The EverQuest community continues to grow daily.

    We've put up a page on the EverQuest website to help celebrate EverQuest's achievement and to thank our loyal fans. Over the next couple of days we will be posting interesting content from EverQuest's humble beginnings, as well as a few surprises. Check it out! http://everquest.station.sony.com/2ndanniversary/index.jsp

    *Ever Changing, Evolving, Expanding*

    The Warrens:

    As part of our two-year celebration, we're pleased to announce the release of "The Warrens", a free expansion zone located on Odus. "The Warrens" is a low-to-mid level dungeon and the home of the Kobolds on Odus. You can patch the Warrens by running the "Optional Patch".

    In addition to filling-out the continent of Odus, "the Warrens" will also serve as a platform to try out some of the new ideas brought forth by members of the team and the public aimed at addressing some common gameplay issues. We would like to see what options might be open to and enjoyed by the players in the future.

    The first issue addressed in the Warrens is the problem of higher level characters competing with lower level characters for loot on lower level creatures. To address this issue, in the Warrens, a group that "outclasses" a particular foe will not receive any loot that is MAGIC, LORE, or NODROP. All other loot will drop. "Outclassing" a foe is defined as having a group makeup such that no one in your group receives experience from the kill. This can happen if, for instance, the foe is "green" to all group members, or if the group makeup is of such a wide level range that the system determines the fight was unbalanced using regular experience rules.

    Any time that an NPC would have dropped special loot and does not due to it being outclassed, a message indicating that the combat was "trivial" will be displayed to the group.

    As time goes on, we will be implementing new gameplay subsets and adjustements in the Warrens. Please be sure to let us know what you think!

    The Planes:

    The Planes of Fear and Hate have been updated as part of our two-year celebration. Adventurers in the Plane of Fear will notice that some high-level foes are now available. Rewards for former "boss npcs" have been moved down to underlings that are roughly as powerful as the old bosses were.

    The Plane of Hate has also received a large update, but this one also includes the opening of a previously undiscovered part of the zone, now full high-level expansion-oriented content. Much as with Fear, rewards from bosses have been moved.

    Epic Quests:

    As part of the Plane updates, we've made several adjustments to Epic quests for a number of classes. Previously, questers of many classes were put in direct competition with regular non-questing adventurers for a few coveted spawns. In these cases, a new step has been added to separate the "Epic Encounter" NPCs from the "Epic Quest" NPCs.

    For those who are mid-quest, all quest requirements have remained the same. In other words, if you turn the same items into the same NPC, the same reward applies. What has changed is that some components may be in a different location from where you expect them. It is recommended that you check with the last NPC in line or other adventurers to find where you need to go next.

    ***Patch Day***

    Along with the big announcements above, today was a patch day as well:

    - Fixed a bug that allowed exploitation of the period of time between character control being established and loading of character faction tables.
    - Fixed the Dagger of Frost. Its special item flags were improperly removed in the last patch.
    - The spell, Mark of Karn, has been enhanced to heal more per hit.
    - The effect on the Celestial Elixir Breastplates has been changed to the enhanced Mark of Karn. The casting time on these items has also been changed to 9 seconds.
    - NPCs in Thurgadin will no longer stop traveling when you /hail them.
    - Fixed a problem present in Nagafen's Lair and the Plane of Hate that caused framerate to drop over time.
    - Shift-clicking on a stack of items will cause you to pick up the whole stack. This is in addition to CTRL-clicking on a stack, which will cause you to pick up one. These both also work for coins now as well as items.
    - In addition to the above, holding down the shift key while clicking the "Sell" button in the vendor screen will cause you to sell the entire stack of items if a stack is selected. For example, you can sell 20 peridots by selecting the stack, and shift-clicking "Sell". I'd recommend though that you give them to your neighborhood cleric first though :) The ability to buy stacks is on the list for implementation in the near future.
    - There are a ton of new recipies for trade skill users out there. Check your books.
    - There are a bunch of new quests as well.
    - It appears that we fixed a bug with the Mend skill that caused it to be unavailable for a few seconds after it was supposed to be ready for use. Let us know if there are any further problems.
    - Foraging items will now yield a message giving you some idea of what you've foraged.

    Please remember that the /movelog for the Rathe and E'ci is scheduled for this weekend. Please read the message below for details on the /movelog.

    *A Message From Customer Service*

    We regularly receive requests from customers that want their characters moved from one server to another, or from one account to another. Unfortunately, due to the EverQuest EULA, the number of requests that we receive, the desire to maintain stable server communities and economies, and other problems associated with character moves, we can not move characters across servers or accounts.

    It is important for the integrity of the server that items from a server remain on that server. We also feel that it is important that characters that have built up a reputation, good or bad, have to live with the consequences of their decisions, and are not allowed to retreat from that reputation by changing servers.

    The only exception to this rule is when we open a Movelog for moving a large number of characters to new servers. At no other time will we move a character in such a fashion.

    - The EverQuest Team

Note From Alan About Moving Characters
- Aurum @ 6:37 PM PST
Alan dropped the community the following note on the Gameplay Board regarding player transfers:

    It seems that we are receiving a lot of requests from customers that want their characters moved from one server to another, or from one account to another.

    We do not move characters across servers or accounts. The only exception to this rule is when we open a Move Log for moving a large number of characters to new servers. At no other time will we move a character in such a fashion.

    It is important for the integrity of the server that items from a server remain on that server. We also feel that it is important that characters that have built up a reputation of any sort are not allowed to retreat from that reputation by changing servers.

    So, please, do not ask us to move a character.

    Thank you.

    Alan

    Seaforth wrote:

    [Quote from original post: 1) I want a clear answer as to why do we need the new naming policy? Obtusely we don’t from a law perspective as VI has stated online to many players. “your name is copyrighted we are going to change it”. When from a legal standpoint no name is copyrighted and if trademark is sooooo close a sir name would make it legal. Unless the name was “Elvis” and the sir name was “Presley” and even then it is not graphic in nature in EQ.]

    Very little about the naming policy is actually new. Most of the changes are clarifications. The only really new part is that we will be deleting offensively named characters without warning the player. I have to admit to being somewhat confused by the rest of your post. But I will inform you that several words that players choose to name their characters are indeed copyrighted. And names can be copyrighted, I believe that the Tolkien estate has managed to get a copyright on the name 'Tolkien'. Though I might be wrong. And there might be times when a GM tells the player that his name is in violation of the naming policy for 'copyright' reasons, when in fact he or she means to say that the name is part of popular fiction or some other rule. The point is that the name violates one of the naming policies, and that's what matters.

    [Quote from original post: 2) I want to see something on how it came about?]

    I gather that you are talking about how the naming policies were decided. I wasn't working here at the time, but I know the general story. It's pretty simple. The decision was made to support roleplaying in EverQuest. One of way that roleplaying would be encouraged (I could argue all day about the fact that it can't be enforced, only encouraged, but that's not the topic here) was to insist that they name there characters in such a way as to not only avoid generally nasty names, but to create names that would fit into the world of Norrath. So the naming policy was written. It's pretty much the same now that it was when the game opened two years ago.

    [Quote from original post: 3) I want an answer as to why GM’s are singling out people. or so it seems. some servers really harsh; some don’t care. Some border on discrimination. This problem needs to be solved now ether you enforce the rules or you don’t.]

    GMs don't single people out. They just don't have the time to do that. They change names that break the rules when they see them or when they are petitioned.

    [Quote from original post: 4) Lastly, I really want to know, VI build this game based on other Mud’s/RPG’s of the past. Those people now play EQ and many brought their guilds and names from other games to EQ, these are online personalities some characters have had for years!!! Did VI even think of those people and guilds when this rule came about after 2 years of play? I bet not, considering somewhere near of the guild names and characters on most servers come from popular fiction or contemporary names.]

    Many of the people that built EverQuest came from the places you are talking about. And, again, these rules have been in place since the game opened. I think it's a shame that there are people that think they are new. This indicates that we need to find a better way to get these rules to the players so that they are aware of them.

    msully wrote:

    Baven, do me a favor next time. Don't start your posts by referring to anyone in such a way as to relate them to those that were responsible for the Holocaust. This makes it more likely that your thread will be closed than read, and while it might seem to make your point in a stronger fashion, it only proves to the reader that you are unaware of the depth of the wrongs that were done at that time when you compare them to anything ever done that relates to a game. And, well, it's pretty offensive to those that you refer to in such a way.

    Baven wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Starting with the Donal's Breastplate. I agree the item was originally overpowered. However when you realize this you have no one to blame but YOURSELF Verant.]

    We do take blame for that. And we fixed it. We know changing this upset some people. But it needed to be done to prevent the trivialization of encounters in the game, and the game itself. Because I see so many people point this out in just the way that you have (basically saying that you didn't like it but you understand why it was done), I tend to think that this is not really a point of contention.

    [Quote from original post: Moving on to the breastplates from Velious.]

    This is where you get confusing. You at one point state that nobody uses this item for anything but major raids, and then tell me that they aren't useful on those raids because the NPCs hit too hard for them to be of any value.

    The fact is that with the Celestial Elixer chestplates a cleric could keep a continuous 300 hit point heal on a target for no mana cost, and still toss out a complete heal every 30 seconds or so without breaking that chain of healing. The item was broken. Not to the degree that the Complete Healing item was, but to a degree that it needs to be fixed.

    You also said:

    [Quote from original post: Now I look and cry at the Velious cleric BP's and watch the 60 second cast time for the same spell....sixty seconds.]

    This is a temporary change. We want to find a new effect that will aid the cleric at the level needed to get the item that won't be broken for gameplay purposes. Until then, yes, we've 'nerfed' the item pretty severely.

    [Quote from original post: Lastly, and most importantly.... the agro healing.]

    [Quote from original post: Please do not tell me its mismanagement of agro/hate.]

    Well, then I guess I can't tell you anything… Because that's exactly what it is. Unlike prior to the change, where the cleric was just about the only class that could have major impact on the survivability of an NPC and yet the NPC would never notice that he was doing anything. We've adjusted the amount of 'hate' generated by healing spells. We're happy with the way they work now, and, though it might change again in the future, it seems unlikely.

    [Quote from original post: What is the point? It is a simple question of motives. Are you trying to push people away from this game? Or just clerics?]

    Of course not. It is never our plan to 'push people away' from EverQuest. As a company and as game builders, it will always be our desire to pull people into the game. Some of that job, however, might mean that we have to change something that current players don't like. We always consider what players will think when we make a change. But it can't be the only consideration. Especially when with any decision we make there will be people that like the changes and those that don't. We need to look at the game as a whole and make that game the best game we can.

    [Quote from original post: You took their duty, and then you made it unable to be fulfilled.]

    And I suggest that we did not. We did make it a little harder. But it's certainly not something that can't be done. We've turned it from 'way to easy' to 'just hard enough'.

    [Quote from original post: I watched today as my best friend in EverQuest whom I have known since day 1 walked away from the game, and all his friends that play the game because he believes (as well as many others, including myself) that Verant is an Evil Empire that is bent on doing what they want, when they want, as they want.... instead of what most businesses do, what their customers want.]

Some customers want exactly the opposite of what other customers want. It is simply impossible for us to just do what the customers want. We must, instead, do what is best for the majority of customers where possible, and always do what is best for the game. I still firmly believe that people want to play a compelling and interesting game.

    So sometimes we do things that will upset some players. And this is one of those things. But the changes we have made certainly do not invalidate the cleric as a class. We have done nothing that makes it impossible, or even really hard, for a cleric to do what he needs to do.

the rosters
by the values entered in this field. Prior to this release, all custom
User Fields

* Fixed "overflow" error in Deeds Journal when member's Deed Tally

* Fixed Cellpadding and Cellspacing on roster reports where they were
swapped.
* Fixed extra "report.
* Fixed "Undefined error [invalid key] populating member property
controls!"
error when adding a new member to a new blank database. Finally!!
* Fixed "Undefined error 3061-Too few parameters" when sorting roster by
Dues Owed.
