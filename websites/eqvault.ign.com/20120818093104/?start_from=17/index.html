SOE Scare For A Cure  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News

The SOE Austin office brought this year's horrors into real life by participating in the Scare for a Cure World of Horrorcraft Haunted House event in October.

Scare for a Cure

Scare for a Cure is a non-profit organization that hosts a haunted house event every year. The net proceeds are donated to cancer-related charities; this year, proceeds help college-bound students whose lives have been affected by cancer.

The theme for this year's haunted house event was World of Horrorcraft, bringing game companies across Austin together to throw the scariest event of the year.

Michael Daubert, the Studio Art Director of the Austin studio, was heavily involved in organizing Sony Online Entertainment's involvement in this year's event. Speaking about the event, he says, "SOE is very proud to have a studio in Austin , where the community really embraces the gaming industry. By participating in various community events and charities, we feel that we can give back to the community that has given us so much already. A lot of our employees have had family members and friends affected by cancer, so Scare for a Cure was especially important for us to participate in."

Among the volunteers were Star Wars Galaxies artists Christine Chugon and Scott Downey, Producer Christopher Field, and Lead Designer Jesse Benjamin, who donated their time to build and participate in one of the scariest haunted houses Austin has ever seen!

Building the Horror

The haunted house itself took about 6 weeks to build, with over 100 volunteers. The SOE room took about five weekends alone to build.
