Player Guide: Validus's Warrior Guide

Author: Validus (validus00@hotmail.com)   Organization: N/A

INTRODUCTION

Warriors may very well be one of the most important backbones of a diverse and strong group. Because we indulge all of our discipline in the field of battle, hacking, bashing, and taking in damage from the enemies, we lack the ability to cast magic. Although the class is easy at times, requiring no understanding of the complexity of magic. Warriors simply set out, hunt and kill, even though much of a warrior’s life may very well be dedicated to resting for health to recovery if the warrior chose to combat alone…

Warriors are magnificent even though every class is unique in its own way. In battle, no class is parallel to warriors’ ability to tank (to take in damage), even though we might not be the class that can deal out the most melee damage. Warriors are gifted with the most health points and usually the highest armor class. At high levels, warriors are a key element to a successful group. Casters depend on our taunts for survival in tight quarters. Rogues rely on our distracting the enemy for successful backstabs. Although we might not be as balanced as a paladin without the ability to self heal and buff, we are simply unmatched as the Norrath’s best tanks.

MELEE ATTRIBUTES

These attribute information will apply to warriors, and may apply to most hybrids and other pure melees. I’ve done some research what extent stats help. Many players have conducted their own tests by buffing the tank and killing around the same level to see the effects of the buffs on combat performance. I did not conduct these tests myself but I did read all of the posts in the forums to make these conclusions. Almost all of the tests were conducted without any biases and most of them support each other in terms of data and conclusion. The most popular conclusion seems to be that although stats increase can improve performance, only armor class seems to have the most significant effect. Here it is:

AC - It is the MOST important attribute to have high. A little difference in armor class can affect battle performance very much. In fact, it is the key element to become a good tank. Warriors are only good for tanking. Period. Warriors are not out there to do the most melee damage. It’s also because we usually can’t out damage monks or possibly a rogue in a group unless we are equipped with the best weapons. If a group wants massive damage, leave that job to a caster. Our job is to make the mob (mobile creature, i.e. non-player creature) focus their attacks on you and you only! Believe me, the group will be more impressed with your ability to quickly taunt away the mobs from the healers and casters and thus saving the group, than to see you nuke mobs with a big sword. A mere 30 AC difference can make a lot of difference when players are constantly fighting mobs that can double attack for 75 dmg, or even 125 dmg in Guk. Tanks need every AC they can get. Just remember, you want every AC you can get. More is always best. We are blessed with the opportunity to get the most AC than any other class out there with items like the warrior only crafted armor, which has the highest AC amongst class quest armors.

STR - It helps warriors to have a higher average and maximum damage with weapons. It may very well be the most important attribute next to AC to increase for warriors. DEX may help weapon proc, but it doesn’t help enough to be the primary focus. STR buffs seem more consistent and thus more helpful in the long run. STR also helps all classes to carry more items without being encumbered. Being encumbered will decrease AGI, AC, and speed. Encumbrance is something all classes want to avoid at all times. Every disadvantage should be avoided.

STA - This attribute helps to raise more health points per level. From one of the forums, it was revealed that health points for level 40+ warriors might be calculated using this formula, “level X (27 + STA/11)”. Allowing warriors to gain 50 health points per additional 11 STA at level 50. It may also help traveling and fatigue in battle. Many have speculated that fatigue in battle really has to do with the weigh of the weapons instead of stamina. But I haven’t seen enough evidence to conclude anything about fatigue yet.

AGI - This stat helps to increase armor class. Other than this benefit, tests haven’t concluded much about it. Every few agility points increase the overall armor class by 1. Lower level warriors have reported saying that their higher agility seems to have helped them to dodge/parry drastically. But as Verant has reported, AGI and DEX does NOT affect skills like dodge, parry, and riposte!!! Buff tests by players ALSO conclude the same finding by testing agility buffs on high-level tanks. At higher levels, this stat doesn’t help much. However, tests show that AGI will hurt armor class a lot if it drops below 75. When your health drops below 1/6, 1/4th of your agility drops. Thus, with a normal agility of 99, even if your agility drops down 1/4th due to low health, it will not stay below 75 and will help better survival. Other than that, it doesn’t seem really necessary to increase agility beyond that. If you intend on engaging in long battles or are constantly draining your endurance bar (fighting in Kedge Keep for example), you should increase AGI even more. AGI of 133 is a good number for that because even if your health drops ¼th due to low health AND 0 endurance from long battles or swimming, you will still have an agility of above 75. Armor class should NOT be sacrificed for higher agility!! Or any stat!! Ever!!! Agility also helps to increase defensive skills for melee classes.

DEX - Contrary to what player guides have reported about this stat, dexterity does NOT affect chances to hit or miss. It does not affect double attack. Not only has Verant stated this fact, but also enough buff tests by players have concluded the same thing. In another words, higher DEX will not help you hit more accurately. However, for warriors, dexterity will help us get more critical hits/crippling blows. I’m not sure to what extent dexterity helps critical hits. Dexterity also affects weapon procs (process effects). There’ve been a lot of tests on this one. The common conclusion seems to be that dexterity helps procs up to 100 DEX. In fact, dexterity of 100 is sufficient for good procs. Then, it doesn’t seem to make enough difference until around 150. Even at 150, it doesn’t seem to be that significant of attribute to be focusing on. In fact, there’ve been so many posts about why players don’t notice an increase in the frequency of procs when they received high dexterity buffs from a shaman. I personally have not noticed a significant increase in the number of procs with Ykeshas with higher dexterity. I’ve had fights where I’ve gotten more procs with lower dexterity. Also, player buff tests also pointed out that an increase in dexterity only seems to affect a slight increase in procs in the primary weapon but not the secondary weapon. Because procs are so unreliable, dexterity shouldn’t be focused as much as AC or strength is. Procs also seem to depend on the weapon. I.e. you often get more procs with Ykeshas than you might get with Executioner’s Axe. Haste also seems to increase procs as you get more chances of getting the weapon to proc with more attack. DEX also helps to improve weapon skills for warriors.

WIS/INT - It really does not help a warrior’s life that much. It has no tremendous effect to improve melee performance, although Intelligence helps to raise skills fasters. Wisdom only helps a warrior to fail less while working with trade skills. As I said before, these two stats are usually useless for warriors; however, a warrior with low intelligence and wisdom would never become successful in trade skills. An alternative for this may be purchasing wisdom and intelligence increasing items for the sole purpose of trade skills.

CHA - It only helps us to get better deals with a vendor. Again, it has minor effect for warriors.

RACES

There are a lot of choices for races when it comes down to making a warrior. What players have to realize is that there is NO best race for warrior since it’s obvious that every choice has its advantage and disadvantage. It all comes down to what you want in a warrior. If you want lethalness and cuteness at the same time, go with a gnome warrior. If you want slam, pick a barbarian, ogre, or a troll. Different races also gain inherent save versus resists. Ogres are unstunnable by certain races and certain mobs. Slam, something that you might want to consider when picking your race, is more useful than kick not only because the skill cap of bash is a lot higher than kick, it is also usually preferred at high level combat. But again, there are plenty of successful warriors that cannot slam. Verant recently stated that there might be Fiery-Avenger-type weapons for other melee classes such as warrior later. Fiery-Avenger-type weapons allow the player to slam while wielding that weapon without a shield, even if the player isn’ t blessed with a natural ability to slam. So, if you’re lucky, you might be able to slam eventually even if you’re not a big race. The importance here is that you be comfortable with whichever race you pick. Don’t let them tell you what race you want. I’ve always been barbarian warriors and that’s what I enjoy being.

SKILLS

Most melee skills are automatic. For example, you will not be able to attempt to dodge, reposte, double attack or critical hit on command. Melee skills that are manual for us warriors are kick, bash (slam), taunt, sense heading, bind wound, and disarm. Warriors get kick, taunt, sense heading, and bind wound at level 1. Kick is a very nice skill for extra damage. Kick does more damage than bash, but bash stuns and damages the opponent at the same time. Since kick and bash are both damaging skills, you can only bash or kick in one round. You may not do both in that round until the time recycles. As for taunt, this is may be the most important skill a warrior develops. In a group, a warrior without the ability to taunt off mobs is a useless warrior. You should always get this skill maxed out for that level, even if you are solo’ing.

Sense heading is another skill that should be focused on also. It really helps while traveling. But also when you have to zone quickly, it just might save your life when you can immediately determine if you’re heading towards the nearest zone. Train it. Especially if you’re resting, you should constantly practice this skill. A compass is an alternative solution to this. Bind wound is another important skill. For every 4 skill levels, you can bind for 1 health point. The cap of bind wound is 100 for warriors, meaning that we can “heal” ourselves for 25 health points for every bandage used. The only problem with bind wound is that you cannot bandage yourself or anyone beyond 50% health. Regular bandages are somewhat expensive. Summoned bandages are the cheapest to obtain but disappear when you log off. They cost perhaps 2-4pp for every 1 stack or 2 stacks of them (20-40 bandages). Sometimes more expensive, sometimes less. Note that bandages can be summoned by magicians, but please don’t be going around harassing magicians for bandages, even if you intend to buy from them.

Also note that warrior is the only class with the ability to go into a berserker frenzy when its health drops too low (to around 1/4th of the total health bar). When this happens, strength goes up by 10 points. Other than this, I don’t notice any other change in our stats.

Most skills seem to cap at level 200. However, I believe that dodge caps at 140 as opposed to 200 like the rest and I think that kick caps at around 150. Offense and defense caps at skill levels higher than 200, perhaps at 210 or maybe higher. The formula to find the cap of melee skills is “5 X (class level + 1)”. I believe there are no level caps for trade skills and all trade skills cap at 200.

Warriors get bash and dodge at level 6. Note that races with slam can’t really do much damage until bash is available. From level 1-5, slam will only do 1 point of damage. After getting the bash skill, bash becomes slam. As bash skill increases, slam also increases both in success rate and damage. At high levels, the lower skill cap of kick suggests that kick will miss more than bash will. Bash is also extremely helpful at high levels in its ability to slow down runners (fleeing mobs) and interrupt casters and, thus, saving the group at times. If you can slam, try getting both slam (bash) and kick skills maxed up for each level. When a mob is stunned, they won’t be able to attack. Kick may do more damage than bash does, but, then again, a real warrior’s duty isn’t to deal out the most damage but to increase the survival of the group. As for dodge, it is an avoidance skill as the name suggests.

There’s also a strategy to bash while dual wielding for smaller races. It's actually kind of hard to do. First, you will have to make an icon of the secondary weapon slot and the inventory slot with the shield. What you do is switch shield with the 2ndary weapon after the delay of bash is over and you have already slashed the secondary weapon. When it permits, quickly bash, and then quickly switch it back. You'll have to time it well. But if you have haste items, I don't think anyone will be able to do this technique adequately with the weapons going off so fast. It's definitely an alternative for you "smaller" races. Bash is also a lot better than kick because bash max skill cap is at 200 while kick is at around 150.

At level 10, warriors get parry. This is another avoidance skill and may be better than dodge. We then get critical hit/crippling blow at level 12. As I’ve said before, critical hit is an automatic skill. Critical hit allows the warrior to deck out around double the normal damage of 1hs, 2hs, kick, bash (slam), or range attack. When warriors frenzy, critical hits become crippling blows. Crippling blows, like critical hit, can deck out to around double to triple the normal damage of attacks. Verant stated that such automatic skill is related to the dexterity the player has. I don’t know to what extent. Also, warriors get our special berserk when our health bar drops below.

At level 13, we get our famous dual wield skill. Warrior is one of the only five classes that can dual wield (Warrior, rogue, monk, ranger, and bard). Dual wield’s formula seems to be “(skill level + class level)/600”. Although I can’t say which is better, two-handed slash or dual one-handed slash. Which of the two skills can do more damage all depends on the weapon you have. I.e., Langseax of the Wolves is better than 2 Langseax’s, 2 Ykeshas is better than Executioner’s Axe... etc. There is also a strategy behind the two skills. Since fast damage has higher chances of interrupting casters, 1hs (one-handed slash) seems to help in battles against caster opponents. However, 2hs can do a lot of damage in a short time, and thus increases the damage of our critical hits/crippling blows by a lot more than 1hs can. Also, dual wielding 1hs weapons, players can slam magic creatures (creatures like willowisps and ghouls) without magic armor. But with 2hs, players will have to have a magic shoulder piece in order to slam magic creatures. Also remember that you need to wield magic weapons to be able to damage magic creatures.

At level 15, we get double attack. This gives us the ability to attack twice with 2hs weapon in one round, or 3 to 4 attacks while dual wielding two weapons. Like Verant stated, double attack and most skills don’t depend on attributes such as STR, DEX, and/or AGI. They seem to depend only on the skill level. Once again, a lot of buff tests seem to conclude the same thing. The formula for chances for double attack seems to be “(skill level + class level)/500”. I’m not sure about the cap for double attack but I have gotten the skill level to go up to 205. Perhaps the cap is at 210 instead of 200.

At level 25, we get riposte, which is another defensive skills that enable us to evade and counterattack for one attack. At level 29, the off-hand gains the ability to double attack, meaning that you’ll to be able to attack for up to four times a round, instead of up to three times a round at level 28 and lower (there is a chance of dual wielding and a chance of double attack for each hand, giving the player the ability to attack four times a round.) Then, at level 35, warriors get the disarm skill. This is a very nice skill considering that it takes away the mob’s ability to deck out more damage with a weapon. When a weapon is disarmed from a mob (NPC), the weapon drops to the ground in the form of a bag. In pvp (player vs. player), if a player disarms another player, that disarmed weapon goes into the inventory of the weapon owner instead of falling onto the ground. From my experience, I’ve never disarmed magic/lore/nodrop weapons from a mob, whereas in pvp, I’ve been able to disarm magic/lore weapons, although I’ve never tried disarming nodrop weapons from players. Haven’t been able to doesn’t mean you can’t… It only suggests you have had bad luck. The skill works very occasionally, sometimes it succeeds on the first try, sometimes it never succeeds in a long battle. I’m not sure what helps the disarm skill, but the skill level sure helps the success rate.

CONCLUSION

The path to becoming a successful warrior is rough and hard. But the result can be very rewarding knowing that you have just saved your entire group. Warrior at times may be the first to fall in a group. When our health is dropping fast, there is really little we can do besides sticking it in and taunting the mobs off the healers for them to meditate and get enough mana. The live of a warrior may not be exciting at times as you patrol the same routes over and over again to search and pull the same mobs. But if you work hard enough, you may prove yourself extremely worthy of honor and gain much fame, as you become widely known as an altruistic and self-sacrificing tank. It may bring you much satisfaction to be embellished and protected with the best plate armor across Norrath and to be wielding the extremely destructive weapons. Warriors are also the only class that gets a huge variety and the best of weapons, envied by almost every class. When it comes down to pure melee power and strength, no other class comes close to our class. If you do choose to follow the path of warrior, bring pride and honor to our name. Best wishes in your journeys.

Validus, Rallos Zek
Aureus Wolfheart, Fennin Ro
