New Update by Shawn Lord on the Developers Corner  
Print News
The lead designer of EverQuest has posted a new update on the Developers Corner. This round one of the main topics is communication between the developers and the players. Interesting read for sure. Here's a clip:

Please remember, even if we're not posting or discussing certain topics in the forums, we're listening. The team not only reads your posts, but discusses them internally on a daily basis. Unfortunately, many of the requests and issues you present to us do not have easy solutions. With every change, update, and addition, we must consider all of the possible ramifications on nearly 8 years worth of content and systems. Because of these risks and the shear scale of the game and our player base, we have to take our time with certain changes.

So go give it a read!

EQ-Squared.Com - Guild Info  
Print News
EQ-Squared.com also had a representitive at the SOE fansite weekend. They cover some guild related things in this article. It's worth a read for sure. Here's a snippet:

All guilds will have at launch 4 hard-coded ranks. Leader, Officer, Member and an Initiate. I raised some concerns about this, that many people really like to make there own ranks and privileges. The dev said that it may change, but most-likely not before release.

EQ2 Ogaming Impressions  
Print News
The reports from this past weekend keep flowing like Guinness when I hit a local establishment. But I digress, EQ2 Ogaming gets in on the action with their weekend roundup. Leonai_Art talks about his experiences with SOE and EQ2. Here's a San Diego flavored clip:

They literally did build this game from scratch. From the game engine to the graphics engine, this is completely built with and by the folks at Sony Online Entertainment. The issues of graphics and gameplay upgrades are a thing of the past. The engine that they have created was designed so that they CAN expand and update it to keep up with the technology of tomorrow. The entire process over the past 4+ years of development has evolved so incredibly much, that it seems to be something that IS part of the process.

EQ2 Warcy Talks Music And Sounds  
Print News
Woot, a personal favorite topic of mine, let's get right to it, shall we? EQ2 Warcry did a write up about the music and sounds in EverQuest 2. Here's a surround sound enabled sample:

You no longer have to go to a trainer and chain click on 'Orcish' 100 times. Instead, you learn languages by doing quests and by crafting. Furthermore, there is actually a reason to have these languages now. When fighting in groups often you will be up against AI groups, meaning a PC (player character) group against an NPC (Non-Player Character) group. Let’s say your cleric is overhealing like I do. The Orcs will all yell "GET THE HEALER" in voice over ... they will usually then continue to kill the healer. If you understand the orcish languages you will hear "Get the healer" in English, if you do not understand it you will hear "GRARBEN SNOBINDEDOBIN GAGURBEN


Uh, whoa! Now that's some wicked cool stuff right there. Yes I have an odd sound fascination, sorry.

Moorgard Talks Artisans  
Print News
Over on the official forums the man they call Moorgard talked about Artisan advancement. Here's a freebie:

However, some components needed to make higher-tier versions of items will only be found in dangerous parts of the world. For this reason, harvesting skills are given to adventurers so that they can confront those dangers and obtain the rare components. Those components are useless, of course, until you bring them to a talented artisan who can turn them into something useful.

Thus artisan skills are not dependent upon adventurers obtaining those harvestable components for advancement, but they are necessary for crafting the best items

EQ2 Caster University Experience  
Print News
No, this has nothing to do with Jimi Hendrix, sorry. Azhreal from Casteruniversity.com decided to give his story of what went down in San Diego this past weekend at the SOE fansite event. Here, take this free sample:

The one thing I love about this game is how npc’s grab your attention when you get within range of them if they have something you should hear, or maybe a quest they would like you to do for them. For instance, right when I arrived off the boat I was instantly greeted by a man who told me I needed to talk to him. If this wasn’t implemented you would just have to run around hailing every single npc until you found out what you needed to do. After several hours of game play I was amazed at how different this game is compared to all the other MMORPG’s I’ve played in the past. Finally immersion, great questing, little downtime for more focus on actual battles, and constant feeling of achievement is rolled into one game.


A recurring theme i'm noticing is that immersion seems to be done very well in EQ2. Can you say woot? I knew ya could ...

-- @ 18:01 PST


EQ2 Warcry Interview  
Print News
EQ2 Warcry scored an interview with Chris Cao and Glen Snider. They discussed the market system in EQ2 a bit, here's a snippet:

The second way to sell your wares is in the city market. From what I understood it really reminded me of the FFXI system of the "auction house" only without the auction. Basically you give your item to a trader npc, set the price and let him sell it. A percentage of the sale will be taxed and that money will go towards the city value rating (which I didn't fully understand). The only downfall to this is that you can only see things for sale inside your city.


"Greed is good." As far as i'm concerned I have little to no concept of this stuff. My gamestyle is "hit stuff hard and loot it." But this does interest alot of people, so enjoy. Did anyone get the greed is good quote? If so you win nothing except my "grats!"

Hannar From EQII.Com Visits SOE  
Print News
Hannar from EQII.com was one of the lucky people who got to visit SOE in San Diego this weekend. You can read all about his trip right here. Because i'm such a nice guy, i'm gonna hook you up with a sample:

So the guys at SOE fire up our fresh “SOE Fansite” accounts in game and take down all of our names. We’re then transported into a scene that I’m sure most of us have seen 100 times by now, the EQII tutorial. While little if anything has changed from what we’ve seen before, seeing it in person and being able to control the action was something far more compelling than what I had previously expected. The game is absolutely beautiful; by far the best looking game I’ve ever played. And on the machine I was given, it ran flawlessly. Some of our machines varied in specs, but all were mid to high range gaming machines. If you own something that you would consider to be in that class, congratulations.

  Weekend Recap  
Print News
One of the things I have always disliked is the way many fansites simply shut down for the weekend. Because of this, I want to try to keep things moving on the weekends – at least a little. So beginning today I’m going to start a weekend recap every saturday.

This weeks version is going to be a bit short due to the transition process that’s still in the works – but here we go!

Here at EQ Vault this week we had a Q&A with Alan Crosby dealing with the recent Guild Summit and a few exclusive Omens of War screenshots. We also had the first chapter of a new Fan Fiction series entitled “Silent Agreement”

Around the web this week a new update in the Developers Corner describes the changes to Tavci. Stratics has a sneak peak at Magician Epic 2.0, and PlanetEQ also has a couple exclusive Omens screens.

Expect this to be a bit larger next week. If you have any suggestions about this shoot me an e-mail.
