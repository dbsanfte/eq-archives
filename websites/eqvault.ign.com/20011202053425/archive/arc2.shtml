Couple More Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 6:19 PM PDT
There are a couple of new posts from Absor to report tonight:

    Quest Info Collection
    I just wanted to pop in and say thanks for all the input so far (well over 150 comments in two days…).

    Please try to be detailed when you can, and please don't send in suggestions for new quest ideas. Please be as accurate as you can with NPC names… you get the idea. :) The more information the better, the more accurate the better.

    Oh, rumor has it that any item with a LORE tag on it is meant for a quest. Can you confirm/deny this?

    Alpheus Trueflight
    37th season Ranger of Norrath
    Seekers of Lore
    The Rathe

    That's not really true. Items are marked lore for the sole purpose of making it so that a character can only have one of them at a time. This is often something that we like to do for quest items, though.

    Which reminds me of one more thing. Please don't send in comments like "I have this item that is marked Lore but I can't find the quest that it goes with." :)

    Thanks again everyone. Keep sending them in. We'll make time to go over them after Shadows of Luclin ships.

    Quivering Veil of Xarn
    Quivering Veil of Xarn is an escape spell....how can I use it when I have lich 100% of the time up and it does not overwrite it?
    It means that I would have to click down lich and cast Quivering Veil of Xarn......but thats takes way too long.

    Please I do NOT ask for it to stack, all I ask that Quivering Veil of Xarn should OVERWRITE any other form-change spells.

    Thank you for your time.

    Sumamael – Karana

    We understood the request that it overwrite Lich. As I said, we'll have to look into that later as it would require an exception in code for this spell, not just a change to the spells themselves (I suspect that didn't make as much sense as I think…). Basically, you're asking for a single spell to be exempt from the existing rule system for all spells, and that would require some code to be written for that spell, not just a change in the spell database. That won't happen right now, our programmers are very busy. And before making any exception to any rule we'd want to look at it with a bit of care.

    The spell is now much more available than it was before. Not all necromancers have lich up 100% of the time. In any case, the stacking restriction existed before we took away the night only restriction, so you couldn't have used it with Lich up anyhow. And we'll look into the idea of allowing it to overwrite other transformation spells (and that would mean ALL transformation spells, I suspect) in the future.

Lady Daegarmo Returns
- Aurum @ 6:11 PM PDT
It was made known on the Developer's Corner that Lady Daegarmo has returned:

    Trumpets sounded at the approach of a white horse, colors of purple and silver could be seen atop of a lance’s banner in the distance. The great arena opened its doors, this time to its returning champion.

    Once on the ground, one could easily see that it was Lady Daegarmo, her armor unpolished, and showing signs of battle. Whatever duties she had to the all mother, they must have been hostile.

    The eldest of Paladins spoke “For the past year I have ridden, at the request of Tunare herself, on a great crusade against evil. I have pleased my goddess, and she has released me of my duties to her crusade!”

    The tired Knight of Tunare opened her old office door, almost cautiously, as if the Lady had seen some very dark nights, to find it exactly the way she left it. Everything was cared for, and unharmed.

    For the night she slept, very uneasy, as even the most seasoned veterans are after seeing the battlefield. Some thought to wake her from these nightmares, but no one had the heart to wake her.

    At the morning’s light, she awoke, and walked out to meet everyone at the Tavern. Everyone stared as they waited for her to speak, but she took her time getting comfortable in a chair. After being served a juice, she spoke only a single sentence, and it set people into motion.

    “I have returned home, let the games begin!”

    Lady Daegarmo
    Senior Events Master
    Sony Online Entertainment

Annoying Agents
- Aurum @ 7:49 PM PDT
This information was sent our way by Rylic:

    I dont know how many of you got to read this, but I don't think it has been posted here before. If so my bad.


    "Annoying agents

    Customers may not have any sure way of knowing what information that update agents retrieve and transmit, so they may be left to rely on vendors' assurances that only necessary update information is collected. If you're a trusting consumer, you may be at risk when sneaky vendors use the back door to surreptitiously extract and analyze information in your computer—your Internet browsing habits or PC configuration.


    CNET and TechRepublic

    This article first appeared on CNET's Enterprise Business site. TechRepublic is part of the CNET family of Web sites dedicated to educating and empowering people and businesses in the IT field.


    "Though application vendors in every category use auto-updates, game vendors seem to stand out for their stealthy data-collection practices. For example, Verant Interactive, a division of Sony Online Entertainment, was collecting information about additional applications running on users' computers while they were playing its online game EverQuest: The Scars of Velious. Confronted with evidence collected by technically savvy players using network sniffers—and anticipating a flood of angry responses from its customers—Verant changed its policy. "It really woke us up," says Verant's COO, John Smedley. "We have now opted to walk a straighter path."

    Similarly, the online game site Battle.net, operated by Blizzard Entertainment, was snooping into players' PCs to retrieve key configuration information, such as players' names, browsers, and e-mail addresses, as well as files from competing gaming Web sites. Like Verant, Blizzard said that it was all a misunderstanding and that the purpose was to improve customer service. "

New Addition to "What Inspires Your Character?"
- Aurum @ 8:01 PM PDT
The only big update to report from the Developer's Corner us another installment to the What Inspires Your Character? thread:

    Wow, avoid the boards for two weeks and I miss a great question!! I hate moving.

    Moving can be fun too! Just think, you get new phone books!

    ANyway, I am glad to see this question because too many times I have been asked why I play a femae character when I am a male IRL (some didn't figure that out till I flat out told them, lol, I guess I did too good of a job for awhile).

    Allow me to give my answer.

    This should be interesting… This is a topic debated a lot on message boards. I don't really want this thread to turn into a debate over the merits/issues of playing characters of a sex other than your own. But let's stray a little off course anyhow.

    When I am creating a character for any game (Online or P&P--P&P the most notable), I sit back and I think about what kind of character I want to play. What mindset and thought process do I want to slip into this time? I often take my roleplaying approach akin to acting or writing a novel: who am I/main character, and what type of person do I see him as?

    The birth of Kiriani Greywynd was quite unexpected for me. As I sat watching the install of EQ the day it launched Live I was teeming with many thoughts as to what types of characters I could play. I had decided to go with my old favorite, a wood elf ranger (which I was VERY happy to find I could do in EQ).

    I had tales forming in my mind already. I wanted this hero to be a lone wolf of sorts-- hating cities, quiet, often going solo except around friends. At the same time, I wanted him to be a kind and gentle soul, but when confronted with trouble or those who defile nature (bei it hunting for sport, destroying woodlands, etc) his retribution was fast and furious.

    But then an image popped in my mind of a picture I drew long ago, and a short story I had written for a high-school project. It was a woman wielding twin blades, crouched down and examining the tracks before her.

    Suddenly, everything in my head changed. I began picturing a woman who was all alone in the world not by choice, but by fate; her mother was dead, her father unknown, and no relatives to her knowledge. she was somewhat the outcast except among a few rangers. She believed in the ranger's cause, and became one herself. But simply protecting her homeland was not enough for her, she felt the compulsion to explore, and help others around the world who could not help themselves. FOr her, serving her goddess and aiding those in need was all that mattered. She would fight for any cause that was just.

    All the while she would be troubled inside, unsure of herself, who she was, or where her destiny lay. She was warm and friendly, yet distant, often withdrawn. Her actions would speak for her. She was a thrill seeker, but sought her thrills in pushing her skills to the limit and in helping where help was needed. She loved to explore and see new areas and kingdoms, even if she did think cities (expcept Kelethin) were a blight on the landscape. She would also be a leader. Not a political leader, but a leader of action. The kind that would take charge of a party of adventurers in the midst of a dungeon or battle, not the kind to lead guilds and political groups.

    Then it occured to me that I had never played a female character in my entire history of roleplaying (and I've been roleplaying for over 15 years now). It was a challenge. COuld I slip into this character like many actors their roles? COuld I think enough like a woman to not appear a freak? TO this day I finally thank god for not having a father or any brothers when I was growing up....having three sisters and your mother around constantly helped observe female behavior :) (although I STILL don't understand women, LOL).

    From that moment on I was inpired to create an almost Anti-Xena, heh heh. I wanted to make her a strong female character, but not so feminine she looked out of place (and made me feel awkward for that matter) as a ranger.

    I think I've done it rather well :)

    I follow that process when creating a character for all games though. I form a mental image, then begin fleshing out the details in an almost "20 questions" approach.

    Many people look at me when I show up to roleplay and take a step back saying "whoa, dude, you have too much time on yer hands" as I pull out a 10 or so page history/description of the character I am playing. I do it because I think the more information you have for your character, the more that character becomes like a real person and believable, which makes it that much more fun to roleplay.

    I've found that I like writing, and that's at least part of the reason why I enjoy making character backgrounds. Sure, it helps me to get to know the character, and it allows me to help fill some of the awkward times in the game by putting the character's history to work (in town and at a lagging point while the party tries to decide what to do next? That's when my characters run off to fulfill personal needs, ones that I know about because I know the character pretty well, even it's my first time playing him).

    For Kiriani Greywynd I have done the same thing...written out a history, a personality, and I try to stick to that (exceptin /ooc and /gu) as tightly as I can within the constraits of the game. So far, nobody has complained, and in fact, have encouraged me :) THat alone makes me feel it worth it to keep going with her.

    Kiri Greywynd's player :)
    Sayryn

    Thanks for the comment.

    Alan

Information About Horses
- Aurum @ 5:28 PM PDT
Alan unloaded a bit of information about the horses that will be available in the upcoming Luclin expansion on the Developer's Corner:

    I may be repeating things that some of you have already read about elsewhere, but it won't hurt to repeat some information here.

    Twink_a_Saurus wrote:

    I have a few questions about horse.

    First, I know they will be an extension of your body. Can you enter old world dungeons with them?

    If so will you have to be shrunk to fight cast etc? A man on a horse in a tunnel in Sol B won't cut it.

    When you die does the horse die too?

    Once you buy the horse can you ever dismount and feel the grass between your toes?

    Just wondering.

    Ok that's all for now.

    Most likely horses will not be allowed to function indoors. There are just too many situations that could make problematic for horses indoors, not the least of which might be stuffing the rider into the roof of a low ceiling. They will probably disappear when you zone into an indoor location.

    When you die the horse will probably disappear. He will also probably disappear when you dismount, but you will indeed be able to dismount. You'll be able to 'call' your horse back when you need it (which will also cause you to mount the horse).

    Palmetris wrote:

    I was just wondering if horses can be mounted anywhere in the world or just on Luclin.

    If you purchase Shadows of Luclin then you will be able to ride your horse in any outdoor zone, old world or new. However, those that do not have Luclin and all the new graphics it includes (as well as those that own Shadows of Luclin but choose to disable the new graphics) will not see your horse. Only those using the new expansion will be able to see horses.

    Where are the new Dwarves? Also, will they be riding full size horses, scaled down horses (ponies?), or some other animal altogether?

    Aalek
    51 Dwarf Paladin
    Torvonnilous

    We'll have ponies for those that can't reach up to a regular horse's saddle.

    Oh! I also wanted to respond to those that had thought that the horses in some of the earlier screen shots looked a little odd. We had noticed that and had fixed them up just about the time that those shots came out (remember, print magazines have to get these shots a while before the magazine hits the stands). I'm sure you'll be seeing more horse shots before too long, and you'll be able to see the difference. I must say, I was impressed with the very detailed diagram sent in detailing horse anatomy… I'm sorry that I can't find the diagram or the name of the person that sent it in. I was impressed with the effort you put into it. :)

    Alan
