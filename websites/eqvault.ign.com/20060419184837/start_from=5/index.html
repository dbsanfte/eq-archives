  Warhorse Screenshots  [ 3 Comments ]
Print News | Mail News
Two screenshots of the new Prophecy of Ro warhorses, available only in the retail package:





I see on the EQLive site the horse also comes in dark brown (bay).

The rumor is that the horse will run at the speed of the roboboar, which is the slowest mount speed.

If you enjoy roleplaying, a slow warhorse makes sense. The modern draft horse, such as the percheron, is descended from the ancient "warhorse." A knights horse was bred to be big boned, able to carry large amounts of weight, and to withstand attacks from foot soldiers as well as other jousting knights. They were never swift. It was not until the development of the light horse calvary that the lighter breeds were used to create a swift, sensitive horse that could gallop quickly.

  The Age of Mayong Mistmoore  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
From EQPlayers:

A New God
With the final devastating blow, Mayong Mistmoore's blood spread across the enchanted floor of Dreadspire Keep. In his last breath, the lord of the vampires issued a promise, "My age begins."

Finally shedding the remains of his mortal coil, centuries of research and experiments culminated into achieving his life's goal -- to ascend and walk among the gods. With the last act of unleashing Mistmoore's soul, Norrath's mortals released an evil demi-god among the unsuspecting planes of the pantheon.

And as Mistmoore's soul completed its metamorphosis, the Demi-Plane of Blood became fully realized as a crimson realm of death and darkness. Even Ro's shimmer, the great Norrath sun, was lessened as the vampire's plane bled through the pantheon, reeling and reforming in ethereal space.

Norrathians thirst for glory and power -- a reality the vampire relied on -- brought Mayong to godhood. For hundreds of years, Mayong laid the groundwork for his own ascension, carefully and purposefully. He enshrouded himself in mystery and myth, creating an insatiable curiosity in historians and fame hunters the world over. Few had ever seen the vampire, but everyone believed in him. That was crucial to Mayong's designs.

Most know that the gods of the pantheon are strengthened and empowered by those who revere and believe in them. So, as Norrathians accepted Mayong's invitation to Dreadspire Keep, they unwittingly acknowledged his power and became disciples who fueled the enchantments that surrounded him and his abode.

When the last drop of the great vampire's blood trickled to the floor, the enchantments took on life at his death. The awe of his power and pride in his defeat shown by those who revealed him provided the last ingredient to lift his dark soul into godhood.

Now, Mayong's cackles resonate through the immortal realm.

The wise and learned of Norrath share chagrin at this turn of tides in the pantheon, for they suspect what most certainly do not -- Mayong's ascension is but a stepping stone for a larger, more devious plan than any could imagine. Beyond the mortal lands, in a realm of pure magic, a goddess has foreseen a world plunged into darkness and chaos. Worse still, she feels her own kin's suffering.

And while Norrath's lauded heroes -- Firiona Vie and Lanys T'Vyl -- and their armies remain unfound, the world and its future lie in the hands of those who set the vampire free.

  Video Game Brains  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
According to the latest research, Kwill is right (see Kwill's Quill for that reference) and video games really do teach you things, really well! That hasn't escaped the keen eye of people who want to educate you.

From a recent presentation by Merrilea J. Mayo, from The National Academies:

"Our Dubious National Future in Science and Engineering . . .And How Video Games Might Help."

"By both quantitative and qualitative measures, the U.S. is losing ground rapidly to technologically-competent countries driven by intense, well-planned science, technology, math and engineering (STEM) education strategies. The U.S. desperately needs a strong, considered approach to the development of scientists and engineers ­ and, more importantly, a technologically literate public. However, in an education system composed of locally-administered fiefdoms, national solutions are difficult to impose.

A striking observation is that the number of active subscribers to a single online video game is approximately equal to the yearly output of scientists and engineers graduated by all colleges and universities in the U.S., across all disciplines. This national reach, completely outside the maze of our public and higher education systems, could make video games a “bronze bullet” for solving our nation’s educational crisis. Further research shows that the learning paradigms espoused by cognitive scientists are all well represented in the video game genre, and, moreover, the chemical state of the brain while playing video games virtually guarantees superior encoding of learned information.
Time on task is huge (for college-bound high school students, the same as homework time), and games can easily be structured to adapt to the specific learning path of the individual. The possibility that games might be a “bronze bullet” for remedying our national education woes has driven a fast-growing movement of “Serious Game” researchers who are intent on taking this technology to the next level."

Okay you uber future scientists, here comes the "bronze bullet" -- taking all the fun out of video games and making them "educational." Let's hope they leave some of the fun in it... When serious people notice how a fun activity is actually an effective learning tool and is taking wayyy too much of our precious time that could be spent getting a degree in engineering, you can surely expect a visit from a scientist near you.

Good going to our game designers for making something so fun, and so engaging, that the number of MMO players exceeds people getting degrees in science. Hmm, play EverQuest or take a chemistry exam ... wonder why you spend as much time playing EQ as doing your homework!

The "researchers" might also remember that the cost of getting a degree in chemistry is a lot more than $14.95 a month and you can't do it at home with your friends.

They also might want to examine their assumptions that everyone who plays video games would enjoy doing a quest to discover the periodic table and having to solve differential equations before they could whack the boss mob. That's like making cold medicine taste like bubble gum. Games are fun because they are leisure activities.

Would you still be playing EverQuest if you were made to do it?

More Expansion Information  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
Rashere has given us a little more information about the expansion on the EQPlayers forums:

"It made the most sense to group revamped zones together so you're travelling from one to another instead of just seeing them scattered willy-nilly.

To answer a few more questions:

It's a brand new horse.

We're not increasing the level cap this expansion.

There won't be a whole new host of AAs this time. We try to space out AAs a bit and just did a large batch with DoD. The vast majority of people aren't anywhere near maxed and that ever-moving top end gets really intimidating. Doing it ever other expansion lets people catch up a bit and keeps that gap from growing too wide. You may see a few new AAs to support and expand on the new features, though.

The zone count in the press release doesn't include the revamped Freeport and Ro zones. Those aren't part of the expansion and will be available to everyone.

...and as Nodyin said, the new Ro looks fantastic. You'll see shots of it soon, I'm sure. Best part about it, in my opinion, is that it's instantly recognizeable as Ro."

  New Expansion Announced  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
The official word is out: Prophecy of Ro is coming!

SAN DIEGO, CA – January 3, 2006 – Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming, today announced EverQuest® Prophecy of Ro™, a new expansion for the incredibly popular PC Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game (MMORPG), EverQuest®. EverQuest Prophecy of Ro is scheduled to be available at retail and for digital download from Sony Online Entertainment in February for the suggested price of U.S. $29.99.

“EverQuest Prophecy of Ro will give players the opportunity to interact with and transform the online world of Norrath like never before,” said Chris Sturr, Sr. Brand Manager, Sony Online Entertainment. “In addition to a variety of new features, EverQuest Prophecy of Ro continues the fascinating storyline as new and old players alike explore, battle and adventure through EverQuest on more than 30 new missions encountering new creatures and environments throughout the journey.”

Players will be able to interact with the world thanks to three new features. The first, spheres of influence, can be used to thwart your enemies or aid allies. The second feature is a trap system that will let players deceive opponents with player-set traps. And for the first time in EverQuest, players will be able to destroy objects in the world; doors can be bashed in, walls can be toppled and towers can be destroyed, leading to a new level of interactivity within the game and opening up a new realm of design.

About EverQuest Prophecy of Ro

The gods have returned and the mortal realm shifts with their influence. But a new presence walks among the gods, a malevolent force released into godhood by the follies of mortals. Unbound by the laws of the pantheon, it leaves corruption in its wake and threatens to bring the entire pantheon crumbling down. The only hope for Norrath hangs on the very mortals who have unleashed this upheaval. EverQuest Prophecy of Ro includes the following features:

Player-set Traps: Control your battles before they even begin by deceiving your opponents with a new arsenal of player-set traps!
Spheres of Influence: Transform the world of Norrath by creating magical spheres of influence to thwart your enemies or aid your allies. These artifacts will create a localized area where the rules of the game can change.

Destructible Objects: Interact with and destroy pieces of the game world leading to a new level of interactivity within the game. Chop down doors, tumble walls, and destroy encampments!

Buff Filters: Decide which buffs you would rather auto-decline with the new buff filtering system!

8 New Bank Spaces

New Missions: Battle your way through over 30 new missions.

§ New Creatures, New Environments and New Encounters: More than 25 unique creatures, 7 Zones, and 6 Small Instances.

§ New Spells, Tasks, Disciplines and Trade skills

The new expansion will be downloadable via the EverQuest Station Store for U.S. $29.99 (plus tax where applicable). Retailers will be offering a boxed version that will carry a suggested retail price of $29.99, as well as a special in-game item, a Warhorse mount, for players to enjoy. Additionally, a reduced price of $26.99 will be available to consumers who pre-order and purchase the digital download version direct from Sony Online Entertainment. For more information on EverQuest: Prophecy of Ro, please visit: http://everquest.station.sony.com/por/.

EQ Classic Style Group Forming on Prexus  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
Despite the fact that I might have poked a little fun at the "bring back a classic EQ server" peeps, I see that some folks are giving a "classic-style" play mode a try. If you are interested in playing like it was back in the early days of EQ (old world zones, no mad twinking, no power leveling, no use of the PoK books and so forth), you can join them:

Deadcool posted on EQLive:

We are doing it on Prexus Server. Many of us have discussed it, and that's what we came up with. It's a long road ahead, you guys. This is not going to be easy, but perhaps, it will prove our desire to SoE, and even if it doesn't, we will definitely have a lot of fun hanging out in the old world zones.

We are going to start new characters, then get the heck out of the mines of Gloomingdeep, as quickly as possible. We will all begin working out of Western Antonica. That is, Freeport, The Commonlands, Nektulos/Neriak, The Desert of Ro, and eventually we will move inland toward the Karanas, and so forth. Start your new char on Prexus, and begin playing legit, as in, no twinking. If you must give yourself a slight edge, that's acceptable, but don't show up at level 5 with level 20 equip. Just a few pieces of coinage to get your spells, and maybe a few pieces of decent-for-your-level gear.

You may begin soloing if you can't get any kind of group together. West Freeport is good for the first few levels, and East Commons is great for soloing, until about level 10 or so. By that time, we will have a lot more organization.

As soon as you start your new character on Prexus, log out of Everquest and come here, post your name on the Prexus server, so that we can all get a hold of each other.


I wish you all the best of luck in giving this a try.

Known Issues with EQPlayers.com  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News
The new service SOE has unrolled, EQPlayers.com, is now live. Baelish has posted some known issues and general information about the service on the EQLive website.

Greg "Baelish" explains: "As a general note, once again I'd like to re-iterate if your character is not showing correct information please run the /eqplayersupdate command, give it an hour or so and then if you still have issues please email us.

Also, another reminder that EverQuest has NO database as a game. The tools to collect the hundreds of files, run the game logic against them to then generate the information we display is very complex. The game team and web team have tried hard to ensure accuracy, but it's entirely possible that there are small things that aren't quite right. If you let us know about them (you have a lot more eyes than we do!) we'll be sure to get them straightened out.

As always if you have anything you consider a bug or feedback for how we can make the service better please email eqplayersqa@soe.sony.com."
