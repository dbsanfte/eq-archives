  Moorgard On Buffing Others  
Print News
On the Official EQII Forums, Moorgard had something to say in regards to buffing others:
Quote: Moorgard
"....Rules are not impediments to fun; often they define the game itself. Locked encounters are in place to keep our game challenging. A rule? A constraint? Sure. But often our greatest satisfaction is achieved by overcoming limitations placed before us.

Consider this a challenge to see what you can accomplish with the abilities of you and your groupmates rather than you, your groupmates, and any random person on the server who will sell you buffs.
============================
Moorgard
EQII Community Guy"

Go check out the whole post! :)

Game Zone Interview With Heather Sowards  
Print News
Game Zone did an interview with Heather Sowards who just happens to be the audio director of EverQuest II. If you're a sound freak like me, you'll like this. If you aren't a sound freak like me, just play along. Here's a bit from it:

Question: EverQuest II purports to have the largest audio tracks of any game in the MMO genre. How many hours of recorded audio are we talking about and how many voice actors were involved in creating this?

Heather: The voice-over is a huge amount of the audio content in EverQuest II. So far we have already recorded over 200 actors. We have been recording non-stop for the last 3 months and have accumulated hundreds of hours of voice recording.

EQ2 Gamergod Q And A - Part 2  
Print News
Gamergod has part 2 of their interview with the EQ2 development team up. Here's pint's worth from it:

Gamergod: Travel in EQ2...will this game start with a better travel system, something like the current EQ has, with many options to move around the game world? Or can we expect the same vast open spaces to be explored by foot as in the good old days?


EQ2 Dev Team: We've designed a simple system of travel for our two starting cities so that players can easily find each other and get where they need to go quickly.

Hm, sounds like mass transit in the newer Norrath. Should be cool to see how that plays out.

Andy Sites Interview - Part IV  
Print News
Computer and Video Games has Part 4 of the interview with Andy Sites up today as promised. See it here. Here's a sneak peak:
"Just lately we've seen a number of big-name projects being cancelled. When news of another one going under breaks, how does that affect you guys?

Sites: Initially it's very frustrating because we still believe at this point that the more quality MMOs that come out, the better. Every time a new game comes out, we maintain our subscriber base, but they gain a new one and that just helps to grow the market, it gets more people interested in MMOs. So the more quality games that come out the better, although there is something to be said about there being something like over a hundred MMOs currently being in development."
