Lords of EQ Preview From ECTS!
Our own Silirrion was busy again, with a full length preview of Lords of EQ, direct from ECTS in London!
August 28, 2003 - Our very own Craig 'Silirrion' Morrison was busy at London's ECTS today and brought home a full preview and six screenshots of Lords of EverQuest; SOE's upcoming RTS title. It is a wonderful read, and very informative for those looking forward to RTS in Norrath!

If anyone else has any comments on this article, please check out our brand new Lords of EverQuest Board!


Lords of Everquest Preview
by Craig 'Silirrion' Morrison


James Parker, lead design on the upcoming Everquest based RTS Lords of Everquest, was kind enough to spend some time with us going through what we can expect to see in this new direction for the franchise.

With titles like Blizzard's Warcraft III setting a very high benchmark, Lords of Everquest will have its work cut out to convince sceptical reviewers that its not a thin license cash-in. However, aside from the strength of the license itself (everything is immediately recognisable to fans of Everquest) the game has a very intriguing unit development system that James is confident will win the game fans from those die hard RTS fans who have seen everything before.



To zerg or not to zerg...

Zerging, anyone who has played an RTS online knows the tactic all to well. Throw masses of cheap quickly produced forces at your opponent and smothering them quickly. The ease with which it's possible in almost each and every RTS since the original Warcraft has long been a bone of contention amongst fans of the genre. The unit development system in LOE is designed as the latest attempt to counter the most loathed of online tactics.

Each and every unit in LOE will be able to earn experience. Your armies will have a leader, the Lords of the title, and these will function just like the generals in Warcraft III, but the earning of experience goes all the way down to each and every unit. This means that you can build up very effective teams of units as they level up, for example a team of four or five units that have been levelled up will be able to cut down an entire army of level one fodder mobs. In this way, anyone thinking of using the zerg tactic may only end up making his or her opponents army much, much stronger.

You will also be able to 'knight' units once they reach level six, this will allow you to give them extra abilities and build up squads that are almost akin to groups of EQ players. You could very well build a team of a warrior, Shadow knight, cleric and wizard and send them out to tackle hordes of enemies on their own.

Familiar faces

As mentioned above all the units are recognisable to players of Everquest, this even extends to the names of necromancers pet skeletons and the like. Little touches like this abound, that will immerse players of Everquest into the new game, without making the storyline incomprehensible to anyone who hasn't ever played the MMORPG.



James is confident that the game will appeal to fans of the genre every bit as much as fans of the original Everquest. The graphics are top notch for the style, and the various races look instantly familiar.
