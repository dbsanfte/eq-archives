SOE Press Release

Special plans for Lost Dungeons of Norrath, the latest EQ expansion!

Sony Online Entertainment ANNOUNCES GIFT WITH PURCHASE PLANS FOR THE RETAIL VERSION OF EVERQUESTý: LOST DUNGEONS OF NORRATH(tm)

- EQ Players Receive A Rare In-Game Magical Item And Collectible Keychain With the Boxed Retail Version of the New Expansion -

SAN DIEGO, CA - July 17, 2003 - Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), a worldwide leader in massively multiplayer online gaming, announced today that the boxed retail version of EverQuestý: Lost Dungeons of Norrath(tm) will contain both a special "in-box" gift as well as an "in-game" item (while supplies last). Lost Dungeons of Norrath, the sixth expansion to the hugely popular Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game (MMORPG), EverQuest, is expected to ship to North American retail stores this Fall.

"This is a real first for us, since we've never given away an in-game item before" said Don Vercelli, senior vice president, sales and marketing, Sony Online Entertainment. "It's very important for us to support our retail customers and we feel that the combination of both the in-game item and the keychain in the box will be a great incentive for EverQuest fans."

For the first time, Sony Online Entertainment is offering both a "real world" item and an "in-game" item to those who purchase the boxed retail version of Lost Dungeons of Norrath. Inside the retail box is a unique, collectible EverQuest-branded keychain, while the in-game item is the magical "Satchel of Abu Kar."

What is a Satchel of Abu Kar? Not much is known about the ancient adventurer, Abu-Kar, beyond a few rare weight-reducing containers that are occasionally discovered. Now rumor has it that a buried storehouse full of these fine satchels has recently been unearthed by the Wayfarers Guild. This satchel will carry eight small-to-large sized items, and magically reduces the weight of everything within by 25%. The satchel will be a single non-drop "lore" item that can only be granted to one character avatar per EverQuest account.



About Lost Dungeons of Norrath
Just when you thought you'd covered it all -- five massive continents, the moon, and the homes of the gods themselves -- a new series of adventures leads you into some of Norrath's greatest sources of speculation and mystery. The story that began in Broken Skull Rock now yields new clues about an event threatening to change the face of Norrath. Assist a band of adventurers on their quest to unearth long-hidden artifacts of power from forgotten dungeons, secret catacombs, and long-buried ruins. A mystery is unfolding and a new age of exploration and adventure has begun! Lost Dungeons of Norrath also features:

Explore more than 40 diverse dungeons set in 5 new environments. Emphasizes personalized group encounters without interruption from other players. Enjoy the excitement of "dungeon crawls" that are tailored to your group. Discover new environments, objects and interactive items for the hardy adventurer. Reap the benefits of your adventures with an all new reward system that puts the choice in your hands. New tradeskill recipes to produce hundreds of new items. Encounter a host of new challenging monsters and NPCs.
Unearth arcane new spells, mighty weapons and other hidden treasures!


About EverQuest

Developed and published by Sony Online Entertainment Inc. (SOE), EverQuest launched in 1999 and has since become both a gaming and cultural phenomenon. The game has sold more than 2 million copies while continuing to grow with award-winning expansions The Ruins of Kunarký, The Scars of Veliousý, The Shadows of Luclin(tm), The Planes of Power(tm), and The Legacy of Ykesha(tm), making it the largest online 3D fantasy world ever created. During peak periods, more than 118,000 simultaneous adventurers have explored the fully detailed world of Norrath, filled with monsters, magic, adventure and more. The active global EverQuest subscriber base of more than 430,000 players is comprised of people from 40 different countries including North America, Europe, Asia and Australia. Sony Online is committed to bringing EverQuest to various entertainment platforms, releasing titles such as EverQuest Online Adventures for the PlayStationý 2 computer entertainment system, EverQuest for the Pocket PC and EverQuest: Hero's Call for wireless phones. This Winter, SOE will launch the highly-anticipated sequel, EverQuest II, on PCs around the globe.
