  SOE Raises 20k For Charity  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Sony Online Entertainment LLC (SOE) joined forces with players of its popular online video games to support the Child’s Play 2009 Winter Charity Drive. The fundraising efforts culminated this week with a donation of more than $20,000 to the Child’s Play charity.

The month-long fundraising campaign supported Child’s Play; an organization that’s purpose is to improve the lives of hospitalized children across the nation. SOE donated $10 each time an EverQuest® or EverQuest® II player bought a specific Child’s Play virtual item on the Marketplace with Station Cash, SOE’s recently introduced in-game currency. The in-game items purchased by players are theirs to keep within the online virtual worlds.

“The ability of our players to make a donation of this magnitude in these tough economic times is beyond anything we could have hoped for,” said John Smedley, president of Sony Online Entertainment. “As we continue to diversify with programs like Station Cash we will be able to offer unique items that benefit not only our players, but other partnerships and organizations such as Child’s Play in this case.”

Founded in 2003, Child’s Play is a game industry charity that is dedicated to providing children with toys and age-appropriate games at more than 40 hospitals worldwide.

For more information, visit www.childsplaycharity.org and www.station.com.

  VN Board Upgrades Coming Soon  
Print News
Soon IGN will roll out a number of changes to our boards. Along with some general improvements to the interface we are providing features previously locked behind subscription to all of our users, while providing new features to our subscribers. Keep a lookout for the following changes in the near future.

Features Now Available to All Users
  • Registered users can read private messages from anyone and everyone.
  • All users can read Insider-exclusive boards but only Insiders can post there.
  • All users can use emoticons and mark-up code in their posts.
  • Every registered user has access to icons (80x80 pixels).
  • We have reduced posting time limit to 15 seconds between each post to speed things up.
  • Private messages are open to everyone after 20 posts, but Insiders can use private messages immediately.
  • Updated Quick Post feature with bug fixes, a new sliding interface, and new look for quick comments.
New Insider Only Features
  • Insiders can have colored user names. This applies to the text but not the border.
  • Insiders can now upload our new biggie-sized "MegIcons" (120x120 pixels)
  • Insiders can upload a small signature picture which will appear at the bottom of their posts.
  • Insiders, VIPs and above can give themselves catchy titles under their username.
General Upgrades
  • The oft-requested Icon submission tool that allows users to bypass our old approval system.
  • We've removed the "your message has been posted" page between messages to make posting faster.
There are a few things to note about the new changes. The larger MegIcons will only be available to the user who upload the icon themselves; they are not stored in a shared pool like the old icons.

  Examining Freeport  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
On EQ Players they bring us a pretty extensive look at Freeport. If you're a newcomer to EverQuest then this may help you get a better grip on one of, what I would personally consider, the most confusing towns in the game. The article covers pretty much anything you can think of.

Freeport is a city that is in constant turmoil. All races have visited Freeport at one time or another and from here one can board a ship that will take them to the farthest lands. Freeport is a wretched hive of scum and villainy, but can be a ton of fun if you are smart and careful.

Freeport has been embroiled in a power struggle for some time, particularly between the Priests of Marr (Knights of Truth) and the Freeport Militia. The opening of the Planes of Magic, Music, and Rage caused an even greater struggle as both controlling elements of the city vied for control of the portals to the planes. All major factions in the city wanted to achieve power and to this end, they plotted and schemed. The city also found renewed threats from the Deathfist Orcs as well as other races and factions outside its walls. The Coalition of Tradefolk, among others, has been frantically trying to keep trade routes open and the economy running smoothly.

Meet the Community: Lrbearclaw  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
Every week SOE highlights EverQuest community members. If you are interested in being highlighted in a Meet the Community, look for instructions here!

Meet Lrbearclaw Twiceborn
Server: The Tribunal
Guild: In Motion

Where in the real life world do you live?
Cadillac, Michigan

How long have you played EQ?
Nearly 6 years (Started April '03)
 
What other MMOs have you played?
Beta Tested Fairyland and play EQ2 off and on (more off than on)
 
What type of work do you do?
Unemployed for now, actively seeking work.
 
How long is your typical gaming session when playing EQ?
Varies, can be 2 hours or 10. Depends on if I'm in a good group or if we have a raid.
 
What does a typical EQ day consist of for you?
Again it varies, talk to friends, LFG for hours while soloing in Katta, grouping with guildies or my father and/or sister, raiding with the guild.

What was the race and class of your very first character?
Human Ranger (he's my main still)

What got you into gaming and what sort of games do you like the most?
Honestly, I was raised a gamer. I used to LOVE Jungle Hunt on the Atari 2600. My favorite games are Adventure, RPG, and puzzle.
 
What is your highest level in EQ?
Just hit level 78, working to get 81 for my instant DoT and then I will AA more.
 
What race and class is your main character?
Human Ranger (Surefall Glade, represent!)

What do you like the most about the class you have chosen for your main character?
I enjoy the fact that I can fill-in a few roles but not be expected to do one job. I can pull, I can tank but those are NOT my job. But that is also a double-edged sword.

Does your main character worship a deity?
Karana
 
How did you choose your main character's name?
In a nutshell? In a D&D like game Christians are called "LightRaiders" or "TwiceBorn", mine was a Bearknight named 'BearClaw'. That name was taken by a Shaman so I took the abbreviation for LightRaider and tagged it on. Thus his name Lrbearclaw Twiceborn.

Which classes in EQ have you NOT played?
Bard. I've had alts of all other classes and decided I like meleeing toons.

What is your favorite expansion?
SoD, I LOVE the mercenaries... hmm though LDoN was/is a blast... it's a hard call.

What is your favorite zone?
The original Blackburrow, spent the better part of a year there (no maps, didn't even know about the Far Clip Plane... so I learned the layout the hard way).

What is your favorite NPC?
Captain Tillin in South Qeynos (sorry, inside joke with my Dad). Seriously, Fippy Darkpaw, that guy is SO tenacious it is inspiring.

Which expansion do you like the least?
Secrets of Faydwer. I don't like how it screwed up stats on items.

Which zone do you like the least?
KT, that place is a freaking deathtrap.

Which NPC do you like the least?
Tovax Vmar in Qeynos Hills... I kill him on sight as revenge

What is the most platinum you have ever spent on purchasing a single item for one of your characters?
25k on a Porous Loam that a friend made into a Sublime augment for me a couple weeks ago.

Do you have an item you no longer use but keep around for nostalgic reasons?  If so what is it and why do you keep it?
Yes, I have a bag full of old gear. It was from the original skeleton army in Butcherblock (anniversary event before the scavenger hunts were introduced). ack then they were upgrades and I earned them by pure luck. They and Lrb's newbie sword (Pine Scout Longsword) act as reminders of how far I have come in game and almost none of it was by myself. Many friends have come and gone, but they all made their mark on my Ranger AND myself.

What do you like best about EverQuest?
The community for the most part. There are some bad apples, but when you need help and ask you almost always find people willing to assist you.

What do you get out of playing EverQuest?
Friendships. People I would never have met and are now like family.

Describe your best experience from playing EverQuest?
It had to be my first day, made it from Surefall Glade to North Qeynos at level 2. I met a few people that became friends in game for many years. I watched them in awe. A Shadow Knight killed the priest of Discord while the Enchantress watched two Warriors dueling. They would back off and regen while a Shaman healed them. I sat and watched them and after 15 or so minutes they saw me. The Wizard ran to the "Bazaar" (somewhere I never heard of and saw about a year later) and came back with 3 items Lrb wore for a LONG time (the tunic alone was worn into my 40s).

Do you run or work for an EverQuest Fan Site? 
Afraid not, to the shock of all who know me. Perhaps one day.

EQ Feb. 11 Game Update Notes  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Here are the highlights of the Wednesday, February 11, 2009 Game Update.

  • Buffs that have a levitation effect are no longer stripped when you enter zones marked as no-levitate. When you enter a no-levitate zone, you will receive a message that the levitation effect has been disabled. Additional effects provided by the buffs (if any) will remain enabled. This means that levitation buffs that have other effects connected to them (i.e. Spirit of the Eagle's speed boost effect) can now be used in a no-levitate zone.

  • The armor sets for Seeds of Destruction have been modified to improve the balance of stats for the individual pieces while adding more consistency within the sets. Some of the changes include an improved consistency between chaos" and "order" sets, the addition of Focus Effects to Tier 3 Raid breastplates, the addition of Slot 15 to Tier 3 and 4 for group and raid armor, and the improvement of the Heroic stats.

  • Tradeskilled Pure Powersources can now be unlocked with many of the flagging SoF and SoD raids. Past kills will work to unlock these items.
