Correctly Updating EQPlayers  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category:
There's been some unhappiness concerning EQPlayers updates, so Absor came on the EQPlayers boards and explained how to make it work right:

I suspect that many of the issues being reported regarding guild status and Living Legacy information may stem from some confusion about how the EQPlayers update works. This is our fault for not making this clear earlier, and for that I apologize.

In order for your EQPlayer data to be updated, you MUST have your EQPlayer Auto Update turned on. Just issuing the /eqplayer command IS NOT sufficient to update your data.

To update your EQPlayer data you must:

1. Turn ON EQPlayer Auto Update

a. This can be found in the General tab in the options window (ALT-O)

2. Issue the /eqplayer command from your chat window

3. Turn OFF EQPlayer Auto Update (if you wish it to be off)

Again, I apologize if this information was not clearly stated before. I admit that I didn't know this until just this morning [June 10].

NOTE: it will still take some time for EQPlayers to update your information after following the steps above, so please do not expect the information to change immediately after the update is done. Please allow some time (24 hours for Living Legacy leader boards) for the update to complete.

This Week's Raid Notes  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category:

As part of the Living Legend events, this week's live raid, which ends Sunday the 15th, is called "Marathon":

Get Ready To Face I.R.A.

The Ak'Anon Strike Force is looking for a few good adventurers to help them with their latest project. In their efforts to defeat Meldrath once and for all, these brave gnomes constructed an unstoppable clockwork juggernaut: I.R.A., the Infinitely Regenerative Automaton. Before they unleash I.R.A., however, they need to test it out. Are you up for the challenge?

Rules: When players activate the raid scenario, I.R.A. will spawn along with some smaller clockwork brethren. Every time a raid force diminishes I.R. A. to 5% of its total hit points, it will deactivate. This signals the end of a wave. I.R.A. will reactivate automatically after two minutes, initiating a new wave. The way to "win" this raid is for a player raid group to withstand the highest number of waves on a specific server. Players can end the raid at any time after each wave.

Rewards: Individual rewards for this raid event will be calculated based on the best attempt for each player. Every participant in a raid group on each server will receive a clockwork abacus that is used to track their progress. Once the results for all raids have been tabulated across all servers, a scaling item reward* will be given to players based on their relative performance in the raid.

The first round of this raid scenario ends on 6/15, so get ready to have some fun! Check the leaderboards table of guilds & players to track your progress and compete with your friends.

*One per character, non-tradeable. Sony Online Entertainment, in its sole and absolute discretion, will determine raid performances and corresponding rewards.

As the events open up, you can read all about them on the 'Events' tab of the EverQuest section of the Living Legacy website and track player and guild standings for each server for each event. The standings are updated once every 24 hours.

Monday, 9 June, 2008


  Brasse's Press Tour Commentary  [ Comment ]
Print News
Dwarf comic goddess Brasse was also on the Runnyeye/Void Storm press conference with me, and you should be sure to check out her comic regarding the event!

She wrote an excellent write up of the event, with more screenshots than I have because sadly almost all mine all malfunctioned.

You may wonder if she exaggerates with her comic .... nope. As our noob press characters, who are leveled to 80 but aren't actually geared past level one, zoned into RE, poor Grimwell went link dead, leaving us there at the zone in. Basically we were stranded, naked and alone, in a level 80+ zone. Kind of like being on "Survivor." Which none of us did for long.

I assumed we had been put there to add another feature to the zone, possibly "The Undead Press Room" where we could haunt and generally harass raiders with our mental damage procs. Sort of like the Estate of Unrest, only not as interesting a cast of characters. A group of "mocking fan site" mobs, dancing like those kids in Unrest, could have its amusement value.

Anyway, turns out we were rescued after all, and transported to the Community Lounge, which is very exotic and has lots of pillows so we could bring all the info about the fun in store to you.
