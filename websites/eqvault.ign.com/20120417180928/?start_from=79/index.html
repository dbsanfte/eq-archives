  Speculation, Speculation...  [ 12 Comments ]
Print News | Category: General News - Vault News
It's no secret that SOE has been putting out expansions rather quickly. There was some discussion saying they were rushing things and not doing a great job of making sure the majority of the big bugs were taken care of prior to launch... then came Echoes of Faydwer. Though there were a few bugs still in the expansion they were not huge and the entire thing was rather well polished. It was with EoF that SOE announced that they would be taking a little more time with thier expansions to provide a better expansion for the final product. It worked out well. Now some have found that a new name has been trademarked by SOE.

"Rise of Kunark"

Does this mean that will be the title of the next chapter in the Norrathian story? Maybe... Maybe not. Only time will tell but there is much talk about it and we all have our speculations about what is coming next. If it is indeed Kunark many questions will be finally answered such as What happened to Cabillis, Are the Burning Woods STILL Burning?, and the all famous Trakanon... he was undead after all so is he still around?

Hopefully soon we can get some official word on this from someone over at SOE but for now we will just have to put our hopes for that Fungi Tunic on the back burner.

Stratics Chat Logs from February 21 Chat  [ Comment ]
Print News
The developer chat last night has been published on the Stratics site.

Of note is the introduction of Zatozia, otherwise known as Lydia Pope, the new EverQuest community manager. Kytherea has moved on to be the International Community Relations Mananger, and we here at EQVault wish her the best of luck and welcome Zatozia!

Other highlights include:

Prathun on SK direct damage: I'll have to look into the reasons why the shadowknight's DD spells have limitations that other comparable classes do not and see if those are appropriate and intended, or relics of an old way of thinking and no longer necessary. Thank you for pointing that out. =)

Merloc on AC for Guild Banners and TBS AC: We don't have any plans to add AC to Guild Banners for now... I am not sure what is meant by 'general low AC in TBS', several of the items in TBS are higher AC than any other item for that slot... The Katta Progression Ring was addressed, and the 25 AC was considered appropriate.

Vahlar on the difficulty of getting the Serpent Seeker's Charm: The Serpent Seeker's charm and, in particular, the recent changes reflect the difficulty of getting the item to its full power. The difficulty of the quest and the reward is balanced in such a way that you're rewarded for the content you are capable of doing. I have heard that Ambersnout's difficulty might need a second look, but overall, we're happy how the item develops as it stands. We'll keep an eye on it, however.

Zajeer-Dev on Soltaris Raids: While guilds definitely have been getting access to Solteris, I have a good feeling they won’t be finding the events easy to do. The first event in the zone, for example, is supposed to be harder (and is harder) than the final bosses in TSS. And the difficulty only ramps up from there. I’ll give the next guild a cookie if they can beat the 6th raid in the zone in the next 3 months :)

Prathun on the wizard spell Beam of Solteris: The recourse is only supposed to fire once, so the side effect of killing the caster against multiple mobs was unintended. I'd like to rework the spell so that's not a possibility.

Vahlar on continuing storylines: The storylines we've been developing over the past few years are definitely not over and will be addressed as we go on. I'm mostly the gatekeeper for that lore and I do present storylines to the team when we discuss future content.

Zajeer on the TSS Shaman raid class item: Yes, this will be going in very soon. We'll try and update Frostcrypt with the item this week. It's a shoulderpiece.

Rashere on updating Lost Dungeons of Norrath: We don’t have any plans to update LDoN at this time. At this point its simply older content and just like other old content, while it servers a purpose for some players, the newer stuff holds better rewards.

Merloc on focus items for TSS: That's at the top of my list after TSS / TBS weapon tuning, hopefully in the next week.

Rashere on anniversary events: The team is hard at work introducing Fabled NPCs to Luclin right now. They'll be joined by all the Fabled from the past as well as additional events that will play out throughout our anniversary.

Rashere on the broken Sebilis key: I've been working on this exact problem today and think I have a solution now. Once this chat is done, I'll be verifying it and getting the fix into QA. For reference, this only seems to affect people who are trying to get into the zone using only the idol and don't have it on their key ring yet. So, it should be fixed on live servers very soon, although it will require a full patch to get it out there.

Prathun on monk Echo of Misdirection: Though the monk Echo of Misdirection ability uses the mesmerization effect, it was never intended to be used as in-camp crowd control. I will actively discourage its use for that purpose as it infringes on the role of Enchanters. For now, I'm happy with how the ability works.

Prathun on Demi-plane focus Chromatic Arc V: I've changed Chromatic Arc V to decay at level 72.

News Article Highlights MMO Addiction  [ Comment ]
Print News
I saw today that Yahoo.com's featured "human interest" story was an article about addiction to MMO's, entitled "Massively Addicted."

The main point of the story, which profiled World of Warcraft players (although EverQuest is mentioned!), is that MMOs can be addictive to some people.

A psychiatrist compares MMOs to compulsive gambling, and there's mention of how the game mechanics stimulate the dopamine centers of the brain, which can cause addition.

Mostly though, the author presents the idea that MMOs are addictive because they are fun. In point of fact, playing often is more interesting than other things people have to do, thus causing people to choose being online rather than say, going to work or going to the family reunion. That is a much more complicated and interesting issue.

John Smedley, in one of his earlier blogs, defended the game companies and put the blame for poor choices on the players, not the game designers. I disagreed with his insistence that the game companies had no blame at all, because MMOs are designed to be immersive, and that is a choice they make that encourages extreme play behavior. Still, I am not one of those people who think everyone who plays is going to end up an addict.

In reading the article, I found a couple of intriging things about the WoW community that are in contrast to the EverQuest community. The main subject of the story, an ex-WoW player and uber guild leader, claims that after leaving the game, everyone in his guild forgot about him. Of course, he played 180 days and only two years ... a drop in the bucket for a lot of EQ old schoolers. Nevertheless, forgetting someone once they stop playing is just not EverQuest community behavior -- I know many people who do not play EQ anymore that are still remembered and liked by their former guilds and friends, and who would never be "just another guy." Our community is more tight-knit than that. The second is that the author of the article could get not one person to talk about marriages that came about due to online friendships -- and that's another phenomenon that's well documented in the EQ community. These two examples reminded me of how the EQ is known for being a group oriented, community-centric game while World of Warcraft is for the more casual, less engaged player.

Because of its intensity, challenge, and world size, EverQuest is the granddaddy of games when it comes to players who were "addicts," as the issue first garnered attention from EQ players. Now, when WoW has eight million players, or so they say, the issue comes to light again. The light at the end of the tunnel this time, though, is that along with the bad, researchers are finding that there's a lot of good that comes from having online friendships and experiences in MMOs.

Some people may be addicted, but a lot more are having a great time. If you are addicted, of course get help. Doing anything too much is probably not good for you. But for the majority of the rest of us, MMOs are a great lifestyle. Just remember, if they are posting warning signs about how something is bad for you, it must be pretty fun!

  TBS Currency  [ Comment ]
Print News
Confused over the new currency in the Buried Sea Expansion? Here's quick run-down:

Raid Currencies: Phosphites and Phonhenes.

Group currencies (64+ level): Orux and Doubloons.
Orux are obtained from doing the instances in Katta Castrum.
Doubloons are obtained from doing the missions in the Buried Sea.

These Buried Sea specific currencies can be used to buy armor and other items in this expansion.

Player Guide to the Katta Portals  [ Comment ]
Print News
Kumbaja has posted a helpful guide to the Katta Castellum portals, including a map and explanation of how they work.

"The city of Katta Castrum is constructed in a set of 3 concentric circles.
...
Distributed across the city, there are a number of teleportation pads, which allow you quicker travel from one area to another. There are two teleporters inside the library/temple in the center of the city, two more portals in the Sanctum area, 4 portals in the Inner circle, and 8 sets of 3 portals each in the Outer circle. Also, part of the sets in the Outer circle are 4 special portals that take you to the 4 habitats, Jewel of Atiiki, Thalassius, Zhisza and Silyssar.

In order to be able to use these teleporters you must first complete a number of quests to attune the portals to yourself. These quests are given by Archarcanist Kirstmycl at the southwest portal in the Sanctum. The quests ask you to visit certain portals and collect keys from them. The keys will spawn on your cursor when you step on those portals. Be aware though! You may get ambushed at some of the portals.

Each portal (or set of portals) has a unique symbol attached to it.
Navigating from portal to portal is done by clicking or stepping on the symbols on the teleportation platform. (The exception are the portals in the temple, which aren’t a platform but rather a tube of spinning rings)
Not all portals are directly connected to each other, so you may need to use 2 or 3 portals to reach your destination, depending on where you want to go."

Thank you Kumbaja for posting this helpful guide! There is much more information in the original link.

Soloing for TBS Points?  [ Comment ]
Print News
Rashere explains in this thread that the spendable points in the Buired Sea expansion cannot be gained in solo play. EQ remains a group-based game:

"It was a conscious decision to put points only on missions. EverQuest is, at its core, a group-based game and that's not something that is easy to change even if we wanted to. It's built into EQs foundation so it behooves us to encourage and support grouping.

That being said, we did take steps to make it possible for the solo player to reap the rewards from the point vendors by allowing them to gain the faction required to unlock tiers of items from quests and by making the points tradeable, but we have no plans to make the points accessible via non-mission content."
