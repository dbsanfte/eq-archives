  Live Event Quest Info  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
GU 47 is coming soon (I hope). Allakahzam's has posted more information on their wikia about the Void Storms World Events.

For the upcoming events, "Storm Break," citizens will now become Shadow Assailants.

Those who follow the path of light should take the South Queynos Concordium Tower's blue portal to check in with Librarian Dungil.

Those who follow the dark should head to the Academy of Arcane Science in North Freeport to check in with Librarian Motius Feldarn.

During Storm Break, Void beasts will be dropping some kind of shredded filament that the concordium researchers are very interested in. Concerned citizens will be turning in any they can find for possible rewards.

Don't forget to finish up the quests for the current rewards on the live servers before GU 47 goes live! Not much time left ....

  Shadow Beasts on Test  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
Ouka, from the Kithicor server, is a lore master, and always has interesting things to post about the back story of what's going on in Norrath.

This week, he posted this comment on the lore boards about some happenings on the Test server:

"I skipped over to Test last night to see what new things were being added in with the next live events. Lots of new Kelethin-related furniture, both a black and a purple formal ensemble for females (the Purple one is extremely sexy). A new dark grey monk-bruiser only Gi (yay!) Some other stuff that is far less interesting (In-game voice chat.)

I went outside to Antonica to see what was going on. The Void tempests have turned into full-blown portal storms, where void creatures are emerging at will.

If you kill the void creatures, after about 5 or so, the storm itself becomes attackable. If you destroy it, it may leave a void shred left behind. You take these shreds to Librarian Dungil in Qeynos to recieve more Concordium Appeciation Notes.

If you're very lucky (and I mean very lucky, these guys are ****ing rare,) then you'll encounter a Shadow Beast, your first look at the Grathok species.



At this point, the model is only being used to represent the "Shadow Beast" that emerges from the void portals. They're not identified as Grathok yet, so it's not possible to tell if these new creatures are intellegent or sentient at this point, but it seems pretty obvious that they are or will be in the next expansion." -- Ouka

Thanks for the post!

  Blog Shocker: Raid Caps Next Expansion  [ 4 Comments ]
Print News | Category:
In some startling news, Rytan and team have announced in the SOE dev blog that raids will be limited to a cap of 42 players in the coming expansion:

"In an effort to allow everyone the opportunity to experience as much content as possible we’ve decided to lower the maximum players in raid content to 42 for any new content that is released in the future. Please note that this will not have an impact on the player caps for existing content. They will remain the same as they are now. We will be monitoring this change going forward and undertaking any needed balance changes that become necessary.

As always our goal is to have a game that’s both challenging and fun for everyone, and believe this change will be a move in the right direction. Thanks for continuing to provide the feedback and support to make EverQuest the best game it can be.

Rytan and the EQ Dev team."

The question is, why? The problem, if you don't see it, is that with raid force hard caps someone gets left out. There just isn't room for marginal or redundant classes. Yes, many servers have guilds that can't field a 54 player raid force anymore, but that doesn't mean hard caps are the answer. Those guilds who field a raid force over 42 people for the current content will be forced to drop 12 people if they want to experience the new content.

Perhaps it will allow more players to experience raid content, but at what cost?
