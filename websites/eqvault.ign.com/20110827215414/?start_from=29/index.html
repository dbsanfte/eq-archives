  Legends of Norrath Live  [ Comment ]
Print News
LEGENDS OF NORRATH™ NOW AVAILABLE FOR ALL EVERQUEST® AND EVERQUEST® II PLAYERS

New Online Trading Card Game Playable Within and Across Online RPG’s

Subscribers to Receive Free Starter Deck

Legends of Norrath™ Oathbound, the online trading card game based on the rich fantasy worlds of EverQuest® and EverQuest® II, is now live and available for all EQ and EQII subscribers. Playable from within either of the two online games, players can take on challengers in casual gameplay or test their skills against AI opponents, across all servers, within both live games.

Oathbound is the first release in the Legends of Norrath storyline, featuring more than 375 digital cards, available in both 55 card digital starter decks for $9.99 (plus applicable taxes) and 15 card digital booster packs for $2.99 (plus applicable taxes). In the three-part Oathbound storyline, players become part of a secret war between the forces of Order and Chaos, preventing the realm of Norrath from the brunt of a bloody and eternal war.

In addition to player vs. player combat, Legends of Norrath provides the complete online card gaming experience, featuring a sophisticated, re-playable AI system for single player campaigns, easy to use management tools and numerous Casual and Tournament game lobbies. Victory can be achieved by completing quests or reducing your opponent’s health, enabling players to employ their own unique strategies for victory. The game also features a robust loot system; more than 30 loot items each for EQ and EQII will be available, including player mounts, potions, clothing, weapon adornments and spells.

To get started on the fun, EQ and EQII players will receive a free starter deck and game client to play. Non-subscribers will also get a chance to compete against the EQ and EQII community on September 12, when a free stand alone version of the game is scheduled to be available.

For more information on Legends of Norrath, visit the website at www.legendsofnorrath.com.

Call for Volunteers  [ Comment ]
Print News
Kwill is lonely! No, not in that way, but I sure could use a contributor or two to help out with EQVault.

I specifically could use:

-- people to write play guides. Do you know how to level a character up, defeat a certain zone, or make money? Write a short article and get it published here!

-- people to help keep the guild listings updated. It's a big job for one person, but easy enough for a couple of peeps.

-- ideas for new features. Think the site is missing something? Be the first one to add it!

-- someone to cover Legends of Norrath.

Why should you help out as a volunteer?

It's a great way to get your foot in the door in the video game industry. Companies love it when you have worked on a fan site. This site is powered by one of the top media companies in the business, IGN Entertainment, so it looks good on a resume!

Writing practice. Wish you could be a writer? The best way to get there is to write, and write some more. Get published here, painlessly!

Be part of the EverQuest community. Fan site contributors are a special breed of devoted fans. And we are way fierce! Come join the supercool club!

Please email me at kwill@vaultnetwork.com if you have an interest in helping. Thanks!

Producer Tidbits  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category:
Clint Worley has some tidbits for this week [I have added some emphasis on certain comments -- Kwill]:

Producer Tidbits 9/4/07

Legends of Norrath and raiding- Can't we all get along?

I have seen a few posts about players getting frustrated that LoN is distracting focused Raid attempts and even smaller group outings. We did discuss this initially and we came to the conclusion that it is much easier to treat the players as adults than to put training wheels on LoN. Meaning, if you decide to raid with someone that you do not know, there are inherent risks associated with it. One such risk could be needing said "unknown player" to help and they are playing LoN instead.

Some players would like to have an icon or some indication that players are playing LoN but that is not the direction we want to take the game. I would suggest that all players take the time to know who they are playing with and make sure that LoN rules are clear in critical situations.


Dice, Dice, I need some dice over here!

The dice will be rolling out in the update tomorrow. I am sure that the players will have a good time with them. For those of you that were not at this years Fan Faire maybe next year! You will see players with their own Stones of Fortune being used to get random buffs.


Did I lose a claim item around here somewhere? Hey there is a new claim window!

In an effort to support the LoN roll out that is happening this week, we redesigned the claim window. Now you should find it much easier to use. I hope that now everyone will be able to find all of their claimables and hopefully give CS a little break Also, I want to be sure to clarify that this is a new window that handles claim items only. Please do not confuse it with a new item window.


I run EQ in 1600x1200 on my main machine and 1024x768 on the other 5 boxes!!!

There is a change to the resolution options in the latest update. You now need to be in full screen mode to change your resolution size under the options menu. While in windowed mode, you can simply drag the window border to re-size the window.


I had the day off and the server was down, what gives?!?

Yesterday we had to bring down the FV server to get in a hotfix to some of the loot drops tied to LoN. Firiona Vie has a special rule set and it generally allows players to trade items that are normally no trade on other servers. That is a specific setting that must be set and it was overlooked. That means that players were able to trade LoN redeemed loot on FV. Once this was pointed out to the development team we made an immediate fix. I apologize for the downtime and assure you that the change to LoN loot on FV was the only change that made it in yesterday.

Our end desire is to have LoN be uniform across all servers and all games. Having the rule set be unique on FV was an oversight and required a fix. We appreciate your understanding and we are glad that we caught in the Beta phase prior to Live launch.


Raids are waiting for the foolish and brave alike!!!

We are already starting with the Raid tuning on the Secrets of Faydwer expansion. A special thank you goes out to those players for taking the time to work with the developers on these insanely difficult challenges. I am sure that you will all learn quite a bit about the next expansion but I can assure you that we will have a few surprises for you right after launch.


Secrets of Faydwer- Unfolding the Lore Series

In an effort to help players understand the expansion better, the EQ designers have been working with community to get out all of the lore from the next expansion. I would recommend that you all go check out the latest update in the series. It is a cool insight to the work that we put into the design of each zone and storyline and it also has screenshots from the next expansion for you to check out.


Is it a bird, is it a plane, noooooo it is the new LaunchPad!

As you all know now, we made the move and many of you are pleased. With everything we as devs do there were good comments and not so good comments but it was an overwhelming success. You should notice faster download times and more functionality as well. We are enjoying it from our end and we actually look forward to update days now. (I know, I know, I probably just jinxed myself but hey, it is working and the team loves it. What else can I say?)

RPG Vault Roundtable: Online Worlds  [ Comment ]
Print News

A group of game developers share their thoughts on the global online worlds market in this edition of RPG Vault's Online World Roundtable No. Five.

How does the global market influence game design? Can a great game cross international boundaries? The Western-based gaming industry so far has paid little attention to the global space, but that's not going to be the case for much longer. In the near future, the pipeline from Asia to the United States and other Western countries is going to start flowing our way, rather than just the one way to Asia as it has in the past, these designers predict. This theme of expanding markets and a two-way pipeline was also emphasized at this year's Game Developer's Conference in San Francisco.

Look for more "Freemium" games (free to play plus premium virtual items) to be produced for the global marketplace, since that model has been so successful in Asia.

The article is a good read with lots of interesting comments from cutting edge game designers for those interested in the future of online gaming.

Legends of Norrath Live on September 5  [ Comment ]
Print News
Legends of Norrath™: Oathbound is releasing live in EverQuest® and EverQuest II® on September 5th, 2007!

Legends of Norrath is an online-only trading card game, based on the richly-detailed sword-and-sorcery world of Norrath from the massively multiplayer online role-playing games EverQuest and EverQuest II. Playable from within the two MMOs, Oathbound, the first set for Legends of Norrath features over 375 digital cards and is available in both a 55-card digital starter deck and 15-card digital booster packs. Play as one of many Oathbound avatars and decide your own fate as you choose between the paths of good or evil.

Features Include:

EverQuest® and EverQuest®II Interactivity: Legends of Norrath works both with and within your existing EQ and EQ®II games. Launching directly from your in-game menu, players can challenge others from either game. Also included are randomly inserted reward cards from potions to mounts that will enhance your EQ and EQII MMO game.

Complimentary Starter Packs: EQ and EQII players will be awarded a complimentary random Oathbound digital starter deck.

Single-Player Campaigns or PVP: Test your skills against a sophisticated, re-playable AI system and earn special reward cards by completing campaigns... or challenge players all over the world in online game lobbies where you can play both casual games and high-level tournaments.

Sophisticated Online Play: Legends of Norrath provides you with all the tools needed for the complete online TCG experience. Featuring tools perfected through five previous collectable trading games, utilize the Deck Builder, Collection Manager, Trade Lobby, Tournament Lobby and numerous Casual Games Lobbies where you can find opponents any time of the day or night.

Multiple Ways to Win: Win by completing a series of four quests or reduce the health of your opponent's avatar to zero. Be the best ultimate strategic mastermind as you choose your path towards victory.

Original Fantasy Artwork: Featuring original interpretations by experienced fantasy artists, players of EverQuest and EverQuest II will find themselves reacquainted with legendary EverQuest and EverQuest II characters, creatures, locations, and items. In addition, witness new avatars and minions introduced for the very first time.

Get great rewards in EverQuest® and EverQuest II®: With each booster pack of Legends of Norrath cards, you will have the chance of receiving special cards that grant you actual in-game items that can be claimed by one of your characters.

  Secrets of Faydwer Lore Part 2  [ Comment ]
Print News
This installment of lore from the Secrets of Faydwer expansion, due to be released in November, profiles the Gyrospire Towers – Beza and Zeka. Check out the screenshots at EQPlayers.

"Gyrospire Beza and Zeka are two of three towers made by Meldrath specifically to guard his keep and serve as a flanking force in attacks. Gyrospire Azia was destroyed in the attack by the Gnomes from Ak`Anon while Gyrospire Beza and Zeka both remain functional. Beza is currently the oldest tower. It was the second Gyrospire created, and has become the "lead" tower. Zeka is the newest tower, and one of Meldrath's experiments at an ultimate weapon. At the top of the tower is a large steam and magic powered catapult that can be used with devastating ability, though it is a bit cumbersome and ends up best used as a siege weapon. There were many failures and many tower designs before Zeka, but they all failed spectacularly. This final tower is a success.

Despite attempts to have the towers completely clockwork controlled, Meldrath found that some times a more creative brain is required. While clockworks dominate in Gyrospire Beza there are gnomes, brownies, and minotaurs in the zone as well. Gyrospire Zeka is a different story. The Captain of the tower, a steamwork, took it upon itself to remove all living creatures from its tower. It has such a hatred for anything living that it has systematically killed everything from Meldrath's gnomes to the rats in the walls. Nothing lives within this tower. The only living being that it will tolerate in its domain is Meldrath, and only because its programming to not harm Meldrath is so deep it has not been able to alter it. Meldrath tolerates this destruction because to him even his living servants are just tools to use and discard. "

My first thought here was brownies! Not those evil little brownies again!

  Loral's Review of GenCon  [ Comment ]
Print News
Loral from Mobhunter has posted a report of his visit to GenCon. I am a big fan of Loral's writing and was pleased to meet him and his lovely wife at the GenCon SOE Meet and Greet this year.

Of interest is Absor's comment on Loral's report, found below it in the comments section:

"Heh, I'm being bashed for disliking casual player, yet I am the most casual player you've ever met. I just don't have any delusions about what casual means. I don't expect to be fight Kerafyrm with my casual-geared characters, so I don't stress over the fact that I can't.

It's certainly understandable if some folks won't find a lot of immediate use from the expansion. I understand that. But the reality is that we have to keep the top level folks busy for a year. The casual folks should still have a fair amount of content to devour, especially when the level cap goes up. This one has to last a year before the next one, and with a standard expansion cycle to build it in, we had to do what makes the most sense for the game.

And to be clear, there is one zone in location that moves, and it moves because it's attached to the bottom of a walking steamwork. The rest are stationary."

And yes, GenCon is a happy fun time for nerds, both male and female. The crowd's fascination for scantily-clad elf maidens with flying hair, dragons, over the top medieval costumes, swords, and intense game playing can't be overstated! Along with traditional Ren Faire sword and sorcery, there are Bible-based games, collectable cards for horse-crazy little girls, and vendors selling tshirts with obscure gamer jokes. It's great fun and worth the trip, as Loral points out in his article.

  Secrets of Faydwer Lore  [ Comment ]
Print News
Get the skinny on the lore behind the next EQ expansion, Secrets of Faydwer, and check out the screenshots.

"The clockwork armies of the evil gnomish necromancer Meldrath the Malignant gather in the Steamfont Mountains, awaiting his order to invade the kingdom of Ak'Anon. The launch of his great air fortress Mechanotus has torn open the earth, exposing new paths into unexplored regions of the ancient continent of Faydwer.

Rediscover Faydwer as you adventure in the nearby Loping Plains, home to the soul eating wereorcs of Bloodmoon Keep. Strive to end the torment of humans and elves cursed for centuries in the Hills of Shade. All the while, an ancient menace stirs east of the Dragonscale Hills, where the prismatic dragon Kerafyrm the Awakened plans his revenge. "

On an interesting note, those who play EQII will recognize the Loping Plains as a zone name from that game -- also the home of some of Faydwer's werewolves and vampires.
