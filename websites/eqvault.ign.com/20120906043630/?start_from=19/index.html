Five Year Veteran Rewards: Official  [ 12 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Looks like the Five Year Veteran Reward page has been fixed, so you can go see the official version.

They are:

Every year right around EverQuest II's birthday, we give our awesome fans some birthday presents! Well, this year is no different! Please note that these rewards will be active on your account when The Shadow Odyssey goes live.

This year we have the 5-year Firework! When activated this groovy bit of gnomish tinkering (that actually works without blowing you up!) sets off several different fireworks at once.

Since fireworks weren't enough sparkle for this year's birthday celebration, the team has decided that everyone needs a little extra twinkle in their lives. Thus we bring you, the Pixie on a String. You'll have your very own pet pixie to take with you everywhere!

For those days when you want a little extra bang for your buck, we also present you with: Draft of the Sage (x3) and Draft of the Expert (x3). These experience boosting potions will give you a two hour surge of energy for those extra long crafting stints or dungeon runs.

Last, but certainly not least, the Orb of Concentrated Memories. This mysterious orb will refill your adventure vitality once a week.

Happy Birthday EverQuest II and to all of you who make Norrath home.

New Maps for TSO  [ 6 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Official News
November 18 will give us new maps ... check them out here.




From SOE's description:

"The map system has been completely reworked and there's a new main screen with a fresh new look. All of the overland maps have been redone as well! The various city scenes are more detailed than ever before.

There's also a new POI (point of interest) directory. It works with outside POI's as well, and this will be beneficial to folks who use add on's such as EQ2Maps. You'll be able to drop custom POI's into your map directory and have them appear. Your POI will also blink on the main map, and set a waypoint while in the same zone.

To compliment the new functionality, you can also search the map. There's a wildcard function as well so you can either check specifically for a merchant or just search for shops in general and have them appear before you.

One of the things that was always limiting in the past was the inability to navigate maps outside the zone in which you were. That is no more!

Now you can go to the main map and search throughout Norrath. You can use the drop down menu at the top, or you can simply click the name of the region you wish to explore.

The main map screen is also sizeable so you can make it as small or large as you like and even run through game with it always on your screen.

There's also a minimap now! It can also be sized as well as adjusted like all your other UI windows to be as opaque or transparent as you like. It will show you the zone you're currently in, and will even give you an arrow pointing to your destination if it's off the screen! Another feature of the minimap is the ability to scroll in and out by using the mouse wheel. You can also move it to different locations on your screen by holding down the shift key and dragging to your location of choice."

For the majority of us that use EQ2Maps I understand that they are working on an interface with the new map system.

  Brasse's Press Tour Comic  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: General News
I wasn't in Brasse's group for the press tour -- I was put with the serious people, like Michael Zenke from Massively and the Grumpy Old Gamer -- but she's got the press tour experience just about right in her Press Tour Comic.

Yes, people wander around and get lost, don't have their GM invulnerability tunics on and die (and in one case, die with GM buffs!), pull thousands of mobs at once, get zoned randomly, and in my case, lose the ability to hear on voice chat so the dev has to type stuff (cringe).

Press tours are always pretty much like a really disorganized raid. Kiiara, Community Manager, always keeps her cool, mostly, and gets everyone where they need to go. So go have a laugh at Brasse's comic, since it wouldn't be nearly as funny if it wasn't true.
