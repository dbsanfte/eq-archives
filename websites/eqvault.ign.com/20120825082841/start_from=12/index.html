  EverQuest II Producer's Letter For January  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
EverQuest II Producer, Bruce "Froech" Ferguson, talks about The Shadow Odyssey, fighter class changes, Erollisi Day and upcoming game updates in the Producer's Letter for January 2009. Check out the letter in its entirety below.

Hello there! Hard to believe it's the end of January already; it still feels like we just shipped The Shadow Odyssey just last week. And here I am again, with a brief update for all of you folks who love EQII so much.

First, let me thank you for your support on The Shadow Odyssey. It truly was a labor of love for the entire team, and we've been enjoying your comments about it, and watching you devour the content we toiled so long over. But now that we've got that out the door, we're looking ahead. Last summer, at Fan Faire, we discussed making some changes to the Fighter classes, in order to bring them back in line with our original vision, where all fighters manage their own aggro, instead of relying on other group members to channel them some hate. Making some of these changes will broaden the desirability of all fighter classes, and help increase their role in groups, without altering others.

Print News | Category: General News
Gamasutra features an interview with SOE president John Smedley, in which they chat about the challenges of operating a game that turns 10 years old this March. Yup, that would be EverQuest! They also go into Station Cash and consoles.

  The Fighter Revamp  [ 4 Comments ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Over on EQ2 Players they talk about the fighter revamp, GU #51 and the change to aggro management. Read all about it below!

GU #51, Lucan’s Art of War, will bring a sweeping change to aggro management and how hate is gained.  This is a significant change and we want to make sure that it’s as clear as possible.

Why Are These Changes Being Made?

Over time, mechanics have shifted from tanks grabbing and holding aggro because they’re the biggest and baddest thing making the mob angry, to tanks grabbing and holding aggro because they’re being fed hate from other people making the mob angry.  In short, the emphasis has been on passive hate gain rather than active hate gain.

The goal is to shift back towards active hate gain and make holding a mob’s attention a more interactive process for the tank. 

In order to make this happen, stances are becoming more meaningful.  Being a tank in defensive stance will now mean more.  Instead of being a damage dealer with a little extra mitigation, you’ll now be a wall of rock standing between the mob and the squishies.  Your taunts will be more effective than just saying, “Your father was a hamster and your mother smelled of elderberries!”

Celebrating the Year of the Ox  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Official News
Gung Hei Fat Choi! Gosh, I'm not sure that I really want to know what that means. Anyway, the Chinese New Year is here and SOE invites you to celebrate in your favourite SOE game. New clothes are usually worn to signify a new year. The colour red symbolizes good luck. So get in game, make your own good luck by wearing a red robe, dress or armour. Pass out celebratory foods and treats, decorate your home with red items, start a parade, enjoy some fireworks and all that good stuff!

Were you born in the Year of the Ox? If so, you are said to be:

Born leaders who will work hard to achieve their aims. Dependable, good organisers and not easily influenced by others. Patient, loyal to their friends and expect loyalty in return. Tend to have lasting relationships.
