Interview with EverQuest Developer Holly Vahlar Longdale

EverQuest Developer Holly “Vahlar” Longdale:  Strong Vision
By Mindy “Kwill” Basi

Holly Longdale is the Assistant Lead Developer for EverQuest and the undisputed maven of game lore on the EQ team.  An EverQuest development team veteran, she is responsible for writing much of the tens of thousands of words of lore that keep the game of EverQuest a coherent story, a universe that invites the player in.  Without the extensive lore and story lines that make up the virtual world of EverQuest, the game would be shallow indeed. But Holly is more than just the lore-keeper: she’s also technically proficient and is a main designer for many of the EverQuest zones, including two of the newest zones for The Serpents Spine expansion: Crescent Reach and Stone Hive.  At the end of August 2006, just before the release of the Serpent’s Spine expansion, Holly and I toured her two new zones and spoke on the phone about her ideas for game design, how her zones come together, Norrathian lore, and what makes her zones a “Holly zone.”

Holly’s style is detail oriented, organized, and logical.  Her strength in creating coherent story lines is evident in her zone designs, which have a seamless feel between the action and the environment where it happens.  She describes her zones as “methodical,” and this is reflected in the logical progression of the player through the environment.  Everything makes sense as both a part of the story line and for playability, down to the smallest detail.  Her zones are immersive and interesting and strive for that “wow” factor.

Each of Holly’s zones has an inherent flavor, from the broad strokes of color and construction that give the zone its feel and texture, to the smallest details in different areas that add their own story to the whole.  Crescent Reach, the starting zone for the new Drakkin race, reflects the arrogance and power of the Norrathian dragons and their creations.  Crescent Reach has both a past and a present represented in the design, which Holly uses to tell the story of the zone.  Originally an area inhabited by the ancient intelligent ogres, the ancient city and surrounding areas has been claimed by dragons and the race of beings they created, the Drakkin.  With its large open areas and dragon-sized platforms, Holly has designed the zone to illustrate the character of the dragons and the Drakkin that serve them.  Along with quests and lore, the complexity of the dragon culture is reflected in the artwork, symbolism, and natural-material based structures that they have added to the ancient ruins of the ogre city.

 

In another zone in the new expansion, the Stone Hive, Holly again expresses the culture and character of the beelike race of the Bixies through artwork and lore. The honeycomb designs, the organization of workspaces, and the emphasis on bright colors and living flowers emphasize the importance of nature and a colony-like organization in this regimented, hive-centered civilization.

When designing a zone with her team, Holly takes the time to work with the artists and other team members to ensure that the representative raw materials used to create buildings and cultural artifacts in the zone are found in their natural states in the zone environments.  The Bixies use honey and amber to create their living spaces, and so flowers and the honey produced from them are a dominant theme in the zone.  The mysterious chambers featuring bees encased in amber also give a hint of the lore behind the zone.  In Crescent Reach the ancient ogres left a huge city structure carved into a cliff wall, which the Drakkin have adapted to their own needs, using natural products found in the zone -- reeds, wood and leather – to create their own artifacts and structures, reflecting Drakkin cultural ideas and values.  Holly’s zone layout expresses the idea that Norrathian dragons are majestic, roam freely, and are proud of their clans and heritage and share that with their Drakkin creations.

Adventurers in Holly’s zones can learn a great deal about the history and culture of the creatures that live there, and participate in the story that unfolds around them. From the dark secret of the Bixie hive to the imposing elder dragons with their own agenda that inhabit Crescent Reach, Norrath immerses the adventurer in story and history.

Each design element in a zone designed by Holly and her team is meaningful, from the symbols of Veeshan found in Crescent Reach to the inner workings of the Stone Hive.  If there is a symbol on the floor, or a strange artifact, you can be sure that it relates to the larger story or the culture of the zone’s inhabitants.  It may not be immediately explained – Holly hinted that they keep some lore secret for later – but you can be sure that she knows its purpose.

Holly draws consistently upon her grounding in the classical fantasy genre that is the backbone of EverQuest.  She populates her zones with creatures and civilizations that stem from fantasy literature, but with her own creative twist.  In the Depths of Darkhollow expansion, she did extensive research on werewolves so they would be instantly recognizable to the audience, but then took those ideas and made her monsters unique to the EverQuest universe.  Werewolves were an important element in DoDH, she explained, because they kept the feel of classical fantasy in addition to the more exotic new creatures, such as the Illsalin, introduced in that expansion.

Along with designing new races and creatures to populate the world, Holly also works on the development of individual non-player characters such as the evil Lanys T’vyl.  As a writer, Holly uses her real-world experiences to make her fictional characters in the game believable.  They have personalities and histories, making them memorable as individuals in the Norrathian story.

Holly explained to me that her designs for a zone evolve out of a process of continuing to write EQ lore, background research on the internet, and extensive reading on the details she’s interested in including.  Her research could be on interesting architecture all over the world, the lore of fantasy creatures, or about plants and animals.  When she feels that she understands her subject matter, Holly beings to create, working closely with the team artists and the other designers, a world that expresses her vision in intricate detail that is unique to EQ.  Holly strives to keep the EverQuest trademark of classical fantasy as a basis for her designs while introducing other creatures and ideas to keep it interesting.  She told me that she never loses sight of her original vision of where the game and her zone should be going, and reworks a zone until she feels that it lives up to her standards:  a fun zone to adventure in, a feeling of immersion, and a continuation or addition to the Norrathian story.

In the past, zones were designed and then populated with creatures.  In today’s EQ, zones are designed around the creatures that live in them.  Although her emphasis might be on lore, her zone design is grounded in playability and character progression.  The EverQuest team has learned what makes the game playable and logical from their experiences both good and bad from past expansions, she explained, and has taken great care with new content to make the lore of events accessible and sensible for the players.  Events, group content, and raids all reflect the overall story line.  Holly, along with the rest of the EQ design team, favors making themed content that allows for a challenging and entertaining playing experience within the zone environment.  In particular, Holly tries to design small group events to convey the flavor of the zone and reflect the overall theme and immersion in the environment.  Crescent Reach’s design and details reflect the neatness and order of Norrathian dragons, but if a group of adventurers explores the city, they will find a forgotten space that holds an adventure concerning the last vestige of the ancient city’s inhabitants.

The experience in Crescent Reach reflects Holly’s welcoming philosophy for lower level players.  The team this time around had an emphasis on bringing back players to EverQuest that may have left the game.  The team has worked to make this particular expansion very inviting to people restarting the game from scratch.  Holly explained that she appreciates that new or returning players making a new character may not want a difficult challenge at first, so her newbie experiences are straightforward and easy to understand.  In Crescent Reach, she designed the zone so the lower level quests have clearly outlined player progression and use directional signs (in English) and pathways that invite players to a successful game experience.  For higher players, the technical aspects of the higher level sections of the zone and adjacent zones are more difficult and exploring is more challenging. A well-designed zone, in her mind, is inviting to adventure in and a place where it’s worthwhile to spend time and return to.  Much of Holly’s time is used to make sure that the details are in place to express her ideas in a zone, and that the playability has depth and interest as well as challenge.  Working with her team members, she brings her interest in lore to life.

The beauty of a well-designed zone, which is the trademark of all the EverQuest developers, is one that incorporates the lore and logic of a fantasy universe.   The goal is for a unified world that is logical enough to be believable as a place that could possibly exist.  This should be apparent whether or not any particular player ponders the meaning of the dialogue or just goes to adventure in search of experience and nice loot.  This philosophy of immersion and depth of story is expressed when a player enters Holly’s Stone Hive zone: the adventurer can clearly see that this culture of bees has existed before they arrived and will continue after the adventure is over.  Even if most players don’t spend time gawking at the details, most do notice when the story isn’t there or the zone doesn’t make sense for the game.  Equally as important to the playability of the game, the environment and behavior of the creatures in any particular place reflects the story line and draws the player inside to be part of that environment.  A beehive is a very different place to adventure in than a dragon cave.

But any one zone can’t stand alone if the game world is to exist as a whole.  One of Holly’s tasks as a game designer, and as Assistant Lead, is to make her zones and others dovetail with the work of equal caliber being done by the rest of the design team, but still express her individual vision and ideas.  When the details are right, each zone has a unique flavor that is recognizable as the work of an individual, but still is part of an expansion and the rest of the work by designers that has gone before. This creates a world that is seamless, a feat that the game of EverQuest has managed to repeat for eight years and over 200 zones.  Each designer adds their own personal vision to the playing experience to make the game a place where players adventure in a living, growing fantasy world, where the whole is much greater than the sum of its parts.

So how do you know you are in a “Holly zone?” It will have a strong vision of storyline; attention to the artwork and details that support that story; have a very logical and organized progression through the zone environment; and will use the zone’s lore in small group events around the zone to explore the backstory. The interaction of players with the NPCs and the environment makes Holly’s design vision unique and playable.  Her designs, seamlessly integrated with the zones designed by the other members of her team, make up the complexity of the world that is Norrath and encourage players to experience the game in all its wonder.
