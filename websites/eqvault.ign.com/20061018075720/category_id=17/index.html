  EverQuest: Depths of Darkhollow Review  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News | Category: Community News
GameChronicles has published a pretty good review of EverQuest:Depths of Darkhollow. Go have a look.
What would a new expansion be without new missions to go on, though? Rest assured, there are quite a few, many of them available in new underground zones. These new missions span a decently wide range of levels, so the new content doesn’t cater to just older players, and can be accessible right away.

Still, I think Sony knows that the staying power of EverQuest comes from keeping old players hooked, not really recruiting new players. Some of the higher level missions are very difficult, not only to play, but to even get to, as I was told from some of the old hands. Some of these missions may be a bit too difficult, and tiresome and frustrating at times. Still, if you like a challenge, that’s something EverQuest continues to deliver.

Monday, 21 November, 2005


  New EverQuest Novel  [ Comment ]
Print News | Mail News | Category: Community News
For those of you who like to read fantasy set in the world of EverQuest, the second book in a series edited by R.A. Salvatore is now out, in paperback: Ocean of Tears by Stewart Weick.

Steward Weick is the author of a number of fantasy books, including the "Clan Novel Saga" series.

I have no information on whether the author plays EverQuest or not, or how true this book is to the lore in the game.

Although the book project of setting well-written fiction in the EQ world sounded like a worthwhile endeavor, trying to write about a fictional world from game lore designed for playable encounters in a persistent world is more difficult than it seems. The first novel, Rogue's Hour, did not seem to bear the slightest resemblance to EverQuest or the Norrath that I spend time in with my avatar. The lore used in the book was shaky at best. Let's hope as the series continues the books are more true to the lore our game designers and developers use. It would help if the books were written by people who play EverQuest and understand the basic nature of Norrath and the encounters that take place there.

You can purchase the book at any retail book store. Amazon also has it in stock, identified as "EverQuest II: The Ocean of Tears." I did not find it listed when I searched the SOE online StationStore.
