So you don't have to scour the boards yourself I have compiled Abashi's posts on the Official Boards:

  • Abashi - A warm "Thank You" From Hybrids
    [Quote from original post: Wisdom and Intelligence have no effect on the mana pool of a shadow knight.]

    Indeed it does (Intelligence anyway), and now it will have the same effect as it does for a pure caster of the same level.

    [Quote from original post: Does this also mean that stats will have a greater effect on hybrid mana, just as it does on the parent class?]

    Buffs that increase the stat used to determine mana (int or wis depending on class) will indeed affect the mana pool.

    [Quote from original post: I'm a Bard. I'm a Hybrid. I have an experience penalty. This change doesn't do doodly for me.]

    Only two bard songs use mana currently, and one of them may be moved to a "resing timer" in the future. The change really doesn't affect bards, as they aren't dependant on mana for most of what they do.

    [Quote from original post: This thread is what I hope to be a large "Thank You" card for Abashi. Yesterday he announced that the hybrid mana pool has been increased on the test server, which will possibly lead to the same on the live servers. I consider that a great leap towards our salvation.]

    Well it's live now, and you're welcome

  • Verant - The truth comes out.
    I really don't think that there's ever been any secret that the rangers strength was offensive rather than defensive. The term "light tank" has been used in the past, but that's when people were using "tank" for both dealers and takers of damage. At the time, there were several rather lengthly threads where everyone here on the board agreed that the term "tank" should only apply to damage takers.
    Their role hasn't changed mid-stream. What did happen is a further shift towards offensive from defensive between 50 and 60. However this same trend was happening in the prior levels and just continued into the post 50 ones.

  • I Chalange VI to take a poll on the ballance issue
    How do you propose to word such a poll? Something as broad as "Do you feel your class is powerful enough?", or "Do you feel that your class fulfills it's place in the game?", or something narrower and class-based? Class-based polls really wouldn't be very useful since we'd have no way to determine whether the person answering the poll is "qualified" (in a general sense) to speak on the issue. For that matter, we'd have a problem determining what "qualified" means.

    Then there's the matter of human nature. Can we agree that it is in the nature of an EverQuest player for them to want their class to be "better" in some fashion, regardless their current status? For instance, taking a common thread here on the boards, if magicians think their direct damage spells should be better than the druids, and druids think their direct damage spells should be better than the magicians, how do we determine who represents whom in such a poll?

    It's not an easy thing to determine all of the player desires and implement them in a way that doesn't conflict with other players desires.

  • If im 52 and want to change my class why not?
    [Quote from original post: I don't see why im not allowed to do a quest to change my class/race since in RP terms this would be perfectly acceptable]

    I really don't think that this would be acceptable in "RP terms". Your character has spent his whole life learning the way of the wizard, there's no reason that he should suddenly forget everything that he knew about being a wizard, and become another class, say a rogue, and be nearly as advanced. In the real world, it would be roughly equivalent to a master electrician suddenly becoming a doctor.

  • Will Anything Convince Verant That Wizards Need More Mana and A Faster Regen????
    [Quote from original post: We all know the arguments about why wizards, as the *most* mana dependent class in the game need a bigger mana pool and a faster mana regen. ]

    Clerics could also say that they are the most mana-dependant class in the game. Neither class is in need of bigger mana pools or faster regen.

    [Quote from original post: Daily, I contact friends who have quit their wizards because the down time for a wizard is longer for any other class, wizards do not fill a needed group role, ]

    Evacers and nukers are often needed roles. I've seen posts from wizards saying that they can't get groups. I've seen the same from druids. Yet I've also seen posts from everyone else who says that an evacer is a must for a group. Wizards are also the only class that can get people to 2 out of 3 planes. They have a lot going for them.

    [Quote from original post: So, I'm asking abashi and aradune straight up: Will *ANYTHING* convince you that wizards need a revamping which should include a larger mana pool, a faster mana regen rate and more damage output? ]

    No, not at this time. Wizards already have more efficient direct damage spells than other classes, they get more different types of direct damage spells, their spells have better casting and recast times, and their spells are inherently less resistable. Not to mention that you have concussion which will take care of those aggro problems when used properly, and harvest and manaskin, which when used together can easily eliminate 2 "Meds" per hour.

  • Probably been addressed but here goes... Changing your religion
    [Quote from original post: Ok is it possible to change your diety?
    If it isnt.. WHY NOT?]

    No.

    Deity selections are designed to have positive and negative effects upon what a character can do in game and how the NPCs react to them. We want that change to be permanent, as we don't want to see a situation where people switch deities based upon what is convenient at the time. For instance, if a really cool deity-specific quest or item comes in game, we don't want to see people flocking to a new religion.

    As it was established that a change in religion would not be allowed, the game architecture was built in a way that now makes such a change impossible. Your faction (which comes from your race, class, and deity) is determined when you first start your character and can't be changed on the fly.

  • Question about new text colors
    There's some messages that aren't included in the current scheme, spell termination notices are one of them. The color for them is currently specified server-side. They'll be adjustable in the near future, we just had to get the base framework in before those changes could be made.

Here you will finda summary of the posts made by Abashi on the Official EverQuest Forum:

  • My experience with the "New" Verant Customer Service
    [Quote from original post: So I ask you Abashi, you call this "fair" treatment? Consistent treatment? You ask for names all the time I give you names.]

    The GMs have to do the best job they can given the information available to them at the time. As I wasn't there at the time, I really can't comment upon what actually happened. However, I think that it would be prudent to say that if everything were as clear in game as your accounting of it seems, then there would have been no reason for you to come to the boards with this.

    Things, however, are rarely this clear in practice. Had decisions gone in another direction, there would most likely have been someone from the other side of it here to take issue with the way it was handled. The PvP environments are very difficult to manage. One would think that people on those environments would be more open to conflicts with other players, however that's not the case. A full 90% of petitions on those servers deal with some type of PvP harassment issue. As I said in the begining, GMs have to make the best decision that they can given the information at hand at the time.

  • Beautiful commentary on Verant's "it's a grouping game" logic
    [Quote from original post: Why do you assume that people won't interact unless they're 1/6th of a siamese sextuplet?]

    We don't, we assume that they will "group" even if the game mechanics don't support it. Once this happens, if the game doesn't support and provide challenge for the group, they won't enjoy themselves.

    Since they (being the majority of player) are going to group anyway, we decided to make sure that the game engine was primarily in support of it.

  • Planning ahead
    [Quote from original post: Why is the code so unflexible that your only answer is "That isn't possible" every time someone suggests an improvement or change?]

    It's not inflexble, it's just not infinitely flexible. At some point in development you have to begin placing limits on what you want to accomplish. If you don't place those limits on what the software will handle, you end up with "feature creep", where feature after feature is layered onto the product until the point that forward progress stops. One decision often affects several other subordinate decisions.

    Certainly, it's easy to look back a year down the line and say that several things should have been done. Afterall, hindsight is 20/20. As it is, we can do nearly anything with the code. The question remains, do the benefits for what we could do outweigh the time, effort, and the problems that result from doing it. Sometimes that answer is no.

  • Zone camping at the teleport points.
    [Quote from original post: As I understand zone boundry camping and teleport location camping is in violation of the current rules.
    Is there anything that can be done to remove these individuals that continuously do this?]

    This behavior is not allowed on the PvP servers. If it's happening, you should /petition to get the incident witnessed. Assuming that it can be witnessed, appropriate action will be taken.

    [Quote from original post: Does a character enter the game before control is available?]

    Only in cases of excess internet latency.

    [Quote from original post: Serveral Guilds are known to condone this behavior.]

    If that's found to be the case, the guild will be disbanded.

  • Annoying thing about /hidecorpses

The rule I've heard most is everyone will get XP if the highest group member level is 1.5 times the lowest group member level or less, or if the difference is 3 or less.]

    The easiest way to tell is to kill something. If everyone gets a "gained experience" message, then they all get their full share of experience. Contrary to popular belief, experience isn't scaled after the fact.

  • Abashi, Why are these 2 spells like this?
    [Quote from original post: Invoke fear is a level 34-49 spell (depending on class) and takes 100 mana with a cast time of 2.5 seconds, and has a maximum duration of 42 seconds.
    Trepidation, level 56-57, takes 200 mana, with the same cast time as invoke fear, but lasts for a maximum of only 36 seconds. Why is this? The later version (which takes more mana, and has the same cast time) actually lasts for a shorter amount of time, and has no benefits over the older one.]

    There are other ways that spells can be improved beyond that done by casting time, duration, and mana usage. There is something about Trepidation that makes it better than Invoke Fear.

  • Abashi Why do dark races get so little option on classes?
    [Quote from original post: Abashi Why do dark races get so little option on classes?
    If you look at it. light races can play any single class they want to play. Human SK human Necro. but i notice that dark races get left out. they get left out in the hybrid aspect very badly. here is why.]

    "light" races accounts for 3/4ths of the races available in game. It would make sense for them to have more classes available to them. Of course it's not really fair to include humans in the list as they can be anything but a shaman.

    In any case, the game really isn't "light" vs. "dark". There isn't an alignment system that differentiates between the concepts of "good" and "evil". The faction system in game is far more complex than that, and makes more sense overall. Would dark elves consider themselves evil? Probably not. From their point of view the high elves probably look evil. Ogres and Trolls just don't have very discriminating diets, and there's no "evil" in consuming your prey. But I digress...

    The class restrictions were decided based on what made sense, and what would be balanced. This places restrictions on what combo you can be.

  • Mana Regen ABASHI please read
    [Quote from original post: So before you say that it will ruin the balance of the game by putting mana regen items in the game, look around and see what kind of hitpoint regen items you have out there. Hitpoints are the same thing to a Tank, as Mana is to casters. WHy not give an enchanter a Complete Mana reload spell that would act like a Complete Heal?]

    HP Regen and Mana Regen are two different things. Mana is, in the end, the standard that defines what groups can do and how often they can do it. HP regen is really only important in the absense of mana, as with mana you can instantly heal someone.

    If you want to increase the rate at which you regen mana, you'll have to see one of the classes that can help you in that department.



  • Question About EQ Graphics
    The world has to look the same (e.g. same geometry, same textures, same models, etc.) for everyone who can view the particular thing in question at any given time. With Kunark, we decided to increase the quality of the graphics, which is why we increased the system specs for the Kunark zones. EQ Proper, however, must remain the same in order to support those having the original system specs.
    [Quote from original post: My point is that the textures are stored on your machine therefore if you buy Kunark you have agreed to the upped specs and the new world textures even though they are not Kunark zones affect only your PC not other peoples, so I think upping the texture size for Kunark in every zone is a very feasable proposition. However for all i know they may already do this. My question again is if they dont why not?]

    I suppose that given enough man-power and time, we could create seperate profiles depending on system specs, however we'd prefer to focus on new content, rather than revamping old graphics.

  • If Verant Wants Us to "Do Dungeons" Why are Enchanters Required?
    [Quote from original post: Now all groups are 100% enchanter dependent if they want to do a dungeon.]

    They are only perceived that way because they are the "best" option. People often have the tendency to view the best option as the *only* option, which isn't the case. If you look into the classes, and their respective abilities, I think you'll find others who can do the job to a sufficient extent.

    The same issue exists with the cleric. Is the cleric absolutely essential for every group? No, but they are the best option. There are other classes that singularly, or in tandem with another class of that or a similar type, can fulfill the role to an extent. I, for instance, have seen all-hybrid groups tear up dungeons.

  • Abashi-Please explain how FD pulling is against the Vision

  • Gordon (honest question) How important are the PvP servers to Verant?
