Sneaking and Soloing
A couple of excerpts to bring you tonight. Gordon Wrinn, of Verant Interactive, made a couple of interesting posts regarding two different matters on the Gameplay forum tonight:
    Sneak and Hiding
    Rogue sneak/hide is the same as invisibility for primarily one reason, game balance. Think of PvP where rogues could run around completely invisible with absolutely no chance of being seen by anyone. It wouldn't exactly be fair. Then we'd have to create a "see hidden" spell, which at that point it might as well be stay the way it is.

    As for the problem buffs revealing you, we've brought it up at the tuning meeting and are looking into it. The same problem exists for those who are invisible.

    Soloing in EverQuest
    Some classes can solo, some cannot. The weapons delay change did not remove soloability from a classes’ repertoire. It might have decreased their ability slightly, but they can still solo nearly as well as they could before. Mages actually received a big boost in their ability to solo. Of course they still won't solo as well (in my opinion) as the other classes who have the ability to heal themselves.

    Soloability does NOT mean that you can go everywhere and do everything. It does mean that you can go somewhere and do some things without group members. Unless a dungeon is extremely crowded, you will probably never be able to go very deep into a dungeon while solo, and will probably not see the planes. That is how we encourage grouping. If solo players could do everything that a group of 6 can do, there would be no sense grouping.

    If you want to play solo, you'll have to play a soloable class. That's really all there is to it. We don't have any intention of making non-solo classes soloable, any more than we have the intention of removing the soloability of any class who can solo by design. Adventuring alone *is* supported.

    EQ gets points for its magician and necromancer pets--a cool idea that's well executed. Druids can also charm animals and use them as pets for as long as the charm spell lasts. Gordon on Shamans
While player discontent over their class' skills and abilities is an old subject, much controversy has descended around the Shaman's class as of late. Gordon Wrinn addressed this matter in the following post to the Gameplay forum:
    Yes, there are a lot of posts. Of course, most of them are bumps pointing out how many posts there are.

    Here's some answers to some of the issues I've seen:

    1) Alchemy: We have two game designers that are actively revamping alchemy. I'm expecting it within the next patch or two.

    2) Shrink: The shrink spell will remain how it is now (indoor only). There will be a shrink potion craftable using alchemy that will shrink you everywhere. Yes, I've read all of the complaints one way or another about the way we are doing this. Thanks for your input.

    3) Charm: Shrink was the substitute for charm. Charm was removed from all the classes who had it post beta, except the enchanter. Even the bard version of charm is capped so that it does not work against creatures higher than a specified level. I've read all the complaints stating that you should have charm. Thanks for your input.

    4) Sense Magic: No class has received the substitution for Sense Magic yet. This isn't a shaman-specific issue. I have no ETA on when something like this will be implemented.

    5) Pets: You aren't a pet class. You just happen to have a pet. We've no short-term plans to implement focus objects for Shamans.

    6) Stats: I'm not getting into the stat discussion. Your buffs are powerful and they do matter. Beyond that, you'll need to experiment on your own.

    7) Miscellaneous "XXXX Sucks" posts: Armor, Cannibalize, spells in general, class in general, etc. Join the club. Get enough people together and the "consensus" will be that every thing about every class sucks. Everyone wants their class to be more powerful and your class to be weaker. The general consensus around the office is that the Shaman is one of the best-designed and implemented classes in the game. The Alchemy revamp should seal this.

    Now, if someone wouldn't mind making an outline of the issues herein that I missed and posting it in a new thread, I'll see if I can address them.

    -Gordon

Abashi Infobits
I've compiled the a list of quotes and excerpts from some of the posts Gordon Wrinn made today on the Gameplay forum. Issues addressed in this round include Nerfs, PvP combat, more nerfs and a few other things. Here's the scoop:
    'Skin Like Nature' Nerfed
    Sure enough. I just spoke to GZ and confirmed that the damage shield replaced the regen component. This was done because a series of spells possessed by necromancers and enchanters replaced the SLN effect with a damaging DOT. By replacing the regen component with a shield, SLN will stay in effect when one of these other spells is cast upon the target.

    It wasn't a balance issue, but the patch message should have reflected this information. Fixing it would have required rewriting the spell-stacking code in its entirety, no doubt introducing a bunch of problems which is something that we don't want to do for 2pts of regen on one spell.

    Is two points of regen at a level that you can cast Chloroplast really that big of a deal?

    About Clarity
    Clarity has definitely not had its duration decreased in at least the past 2 months. In fact, no spell has received a duration decrease during that time. I believe that about 6 months ago, all buff spells were adjusted for duration, mana usage, and casting time. Some went up, others went down.

    I'll check into this tomorrow, but as I said above it has not received a recent decrease.

    Solo Classes
    [Does EQ have classes that can solo?] Yes, and this is still true. I really don't see the problem here. There are still solo-friendly classes. There will remain solo-friendly classes. If anyone doubts it, name a level. I bet there's something that can be killed that gives experience.

    PvP Combat
    One of the foundations of EQ is that no one has to engage in PvP combat unless they specifically make the choice to place themselves in that position. People still want to be a part of guilds without participating in PvP. To tell people that they must participate in PvP if they wish to be a member of any guild wouldn't be very fair to them.

    This is why we provide the PvP servers. People can play on those and then have the option of spontaneous attack.

    On Rogues
    I've read thousands of posts on rogue issues, as well as the petition. Poison is being overhauled, but I don't have an eta as to when it will be done. I'd like to see more pickable doors, but we aren't going to see them in the existing world, only add-on zones will have them. Sense/Disarm traps will never be usable, and I'd just like to see them removed from the display, but that's a bit of a pain to do.

    I've got two things that I plan on bringing up in the next tuning meeting. One has to do with making throwing useful for the rogue, the other I can't comment on yet, but it is a suggestion that has been in *many* of the rogue threads.

    After we get some disposition on these two issues, we can see where we can go from there. It's a slow process, and I know you've been waiting for something for a long time, so I appreciate your continued patience.

    Sword of Runes Broken?

Abashi Quote and Excerpts
Verant's Internet Relations Manager Gordon "Abashi" Wrinn has been hanging around the Gameplay forum all night. Here's what he's posted in his most recent messages:
    Shaman Spell Resistance Nerf?
    I'd be interested in knowing what GM said these things since no changes to resistance has been made in a *LONG* time, except last week when the panic/spook series became LESS resistable.

    The Public Vote
    But I do want to make it very clear that EQ is not and never will be a game designed by public vote, and I'm sorry if we ever gave you that impression. We will make the changes that we decide are in the best interest of the future of the game.

    We are happy to listen to the opinions expressed here, and integrate ideas from them into our vision, and run a poll from time to time to determine majority opinion. However in no circumstances will we pre-commit ourselves to following what decision majority opinion turns out.

    We cannot expect majority opinion to represent the best interests of the game as a whole, as those opinions tend to be issued from a personal point of view. For instance, the majority of people think that their class is too weak. The majority thinks that your class is too strong. If we post a poll entitled "Who should have the strongest pets", we would expect the results to be roughly equivalent to the percentages of people playing each pet using class.

    That is why we are the sole arbiters of what "should", what "shouldn't", and how it is "supposed" to be.

    Monk Delay decrease: Only HTH, or applies to all weapons?
    It only applies to hand-to-hand. The innate delay on the weapons in use overrides the increase in attack speed granted a few levels ago. I should also mention that the 1HB weapons are being created with this in mind.

    Dungeons and Overcrowding
    Or we could start moving the most popular items from Lower Guk to some of the under-utilized dungeons. We just can't throw carbon copies of the existing items into other dungeons. People will still go where it's easiest. Lower Guk is too easy for the items available there.

    Of course, any dungeon will become easy when you throw in enough people. That's why the Plane of Sky is designed how it is.

    On Enchanters
    Enchanters are not a pet-class. They are a class that has a pet, much like Shamans or Shadowknights. The only pet-class affected adversely by the delay change was the necromancer, which is why we went and added the additional spells.

    Sure, enchanters and shadowknights were affected as well, but their pets are not a big enough part of their class for us to feel that their power was decreased by a level significant enough to justify some additional compensation.

    Cleric Armor
    A couple of us have been talking about class-specific armor over the past couple of weeks and will bring it up in a tuning meeting soon.

    As far as cleric armor is concerned, I'm ok with the stats, but less so with the AC. The idea of making a cleric choose between being a Wisdom Cleric and a Battle Cleric seems to be a good trade-off, but the AC advantage should be pretty good if they choose Battle Cleric.

    Bard Weapon Effects Broken? (Click here for more details)
    I've sent this off to our Test team. A bug was introduced about two weeks ago that wouldn't let weapons randomly proc while a bard was singing. This was fixed in a patch that took place a day or two later. However, I suppose it's possible that the same issue may exist with an activatable item like that below. After I find out if it happens, I'll see if we have any plans to address it.

    Server Overcrowding and Class Balance

Ask Hook
Former iD Software employee Brian Hook updated his Ask Hook Column today and responded to the comments iD Software's Graeme Devine made last week about online worlds, such as EverQuest, Ultima Online. Here's an excerpt:
    I'm not sure if you've noticed this or not, but gathering experience and 'nifty little flairs' are, in fact, huge motivations for many players. For many people there is a certain obsessive compulsive streak that makes you want to finish "just this one mob" before you call it quits for the night. You NEED to have those magic slippers. You MUST hit level 33 by Monday. You know the drill. These people aren't being forced to play the game at gun point. The beauty of a game like EQ is that there is a visible progress indicator (your inventory and experience bar) that always makes you want to play "just a bit longer".

    In other words, there's a reward system in place for playing. There is a persistent nature to your character, and thus a sense of investment and identity that makes you WANT to continue playing. Believe it or not, this isn't a bad thing. It sounds more like you don't "get" these games (which is valid). But your complaint is akin to you saying "The main game-play in the current crop of FPSes, that the way the game plays, it provides little motivation -- beyond running around shooting people and trying to stay alive -- to advance the game."

    Is this reward enough for playing? Obviously not for everyone. It appeals to a small but important part of the market, but it's not going to pull in the Myst crowd by any means. There is a cap to the number of people willing to pull an all nighter to get Efreeti boots or that are willing to take a number to get in line for mob 4012 in Crushbone. The RPG industry as a whole is working to broaden the appeal of massively multiplayer games to include casual gamers, pure role players, pure power gamers, and especially those players that have never experienced or possibly enjoyed a role playing game of any kind. Accessibility is key, and appealing to a specific compulsive personality type is not going to be enough to broaden our user base.
