Game Update  
Print News | Category: EQ Live

Game Update: All EverQuest Live Servers (except Al'Kabor) will be brought down at 5:00 am PDT on Wednesday, May 7, 2008 for a Game Update. The servers are estimated to be down for 6 hours. (PDT is -7 GMT: 6:00am MDT, 7:00am CDT, 8:00am EDT, 12:00 GMT).

SoF Evolving Items Reminder: With Wednesday's Game Update, we changed variations of the same Secrets of Faydwer evolving items that were not on lore groups to now share lore groups.

What this means is that if you somehow acquired two versions of the same Secrets of Faydwer evolving item, one of them will be removed after the patch due to the duplicate lore status. In order to select the version that you wish to keep, you should delete the version that you do not need. This change should only affect a minimal amount of people.

Saturday, 3 May, 2008


  Faction Discussion  
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Player Elislrac posted, please take faction out of the game, we hate it.

Developer Nodyin responded,

"It seems to me that "meaningful" faction in EQ means 1 of 2 things:

Stuff that used to kill me doesn't kill me anymore because I raised my faction (or the opposite, of course!)

Stuff that these guys over here didn't want to talk to me about before they now want to talk to me about now because I raised my faction (or the opposite here, too I guess.)

The first use of faction hasn't been used much in EQ lately and that may be an opportunity to explore. Making more of the world "safe" for your character is a goal that some people find to be fun. But when we look at the second case I guess I have a counter-question for you all:

If you don't unlike unlocking augments, weapons, spells, etc based on faction why would you like unlocking quests? More generally what types of content are acceptable to be hidden behind a faction "veil" so to speak?"

Join the thread on EQPlayers to catch the lively discussion about what faction is, and if it indeed can be overdone?


-- Kwill @ 7:56 PST

  SoF Armor Tiers  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
Curious as to how the Secrets of Faydwer armor tiers actually work? check out this this thread for some clarification. The basic types start at attunable, upgrade to steam core and then upgrade to essence armor.

Barnard summed it up this way:

"Tier 1 is the non-visable slot pieces that drop in zones like Dragonscale Hills or Loping Planes.

Tier 2 is the visable armor that drops in zones like bloodmoon, fortress mech, steam factory, etc

Tier 3 is the tier 2 armor with a core added.

tier 4 is the tier 3 armor with an essence added."

Print News | Category: Community News
Started with a one line post on the EQII boards, the rumor that the next EQ expansion would be titled "The Seeds of Destruction" found their way to the EQ community.

Elidroth, EQ Designer, was quick to deny any truth to player guesses about what would be included in this next expansion. He sternly pointed out:

New Lead Rytan Speaks Out  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News

Ryan "Rytan" Barker posted his credentials to the EQPlayers boards as one of his first public posts in his new position as Lead Designer:

"I'll probably put up a blog post at some point with a little more in depth info, but here's a short and dirty of my dev resume.

Started as an apprentice designer towards the end of Luclin. Populated Akheva ruins, several areas of Ssratemple and other areas. During PoP I took over player spells, and did design work in Bastion of Thunder, Plane of Storms, and Drunder. From here on it's a flurry of spell design, random quests/zone content and some raids all the way through Prophecy of Ro. I also did a lot of the work for spirit shrouds and monster missions initially. I was heavily involved in the melee revamp somewhere in there too. For about 9 months after Prophecy of Ro shipped I switched teams internally and worked on Dark Kingdom as Nanyea mentioned, although it wasn in SD not Seattle. After that shipped I came back and helped out with content/quests/raids for the Buried Sea and have been doing content/quests/raids on TBS, and SoF.

I think you'll find that Travis and I share a lot of basic game design philosophies, so I wouldn't expect anything too far out of left field from me that's going to shift the core game of EverQuest. I think (hope) that most people that have dealt with me through the many betas I've been involved with would say positive things. I'll let you all be the judges on how effective I am at the helm in the coming months. I'll do my best not to disappoint!"

I am looking forward to working with Ryan. He will do an excellent job as lead designer, I am sure.

Frodlin, the Warrior Community Representative, had some positive things to say about Rytan's performance in the past, on the same thread:

"Rytan is the guy who's been on EQ the longest of the whole team. He was essentially one of the first guys forced into the design job when the Verant/McQuaid team took off. He really took off well, and became the spell designer for a number of years. Anyone talking with him for any time about EQ knows, the guy is an absolute machine, he literally knows more about the inside of EQ than anyone I know. When someone asks him if something can be done, he will literally take a trip into the engine in his mind, and after figuring out the possibility, will then answer.

Some of the great things he's done:

- Making many Dragonscale Hills mobs melee soloable. That was a HUGE thing, and in my view, it made SoF as an expansion for me. Never had I seen a designer design content with melee being able to solo it in mind, but he did.

- I'm fairly certain he was instrumental in the design of the endurance system, as well as many of the aggro changes to spells and END based skills that we see today.

- He knows EQ lore, and sometimes factors it into his decision making... this is a big thing in my book, there's only 2 other people I can think of who are more in tune with EQ lore than Rytan.

- His work on pets really gave pet classes something good this expansion.

Rytan is one of the designers who is very open to listening to folks, and not just brushing them aside."


-- Kwill @ 8:05 PST


Lead Designer Rashere Resigns  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: Community News
Posted on the EQ Developer blog:

A goodbye post from RashereIts amazing how fast time can pass. It feels like just a few days ago when I first walked through the doors here but now, more than 5 years later, its time for me to move on. Ill be leaving SOE at the end of the month and wanted to take this opportunity to thank all of you, the EverQuest players, for your dedication and friendship over the years.

Looking back over everything that EverQuest has done in the last 5 years is enough to make my head swim, but the one constant throughout that time has always been the fans. Between fan faires, community summits, and the forums, Ive met more dedicated and knowledgeable players than I could ever count and many of you have become good friends. That level of interaction with players is something you dont often get to experience in this industry and Im certainly going to miss it.

EverQuest has always been a pioneer in the MMO industry and it has been a distinct honor to be part of that legacy and work with the wonderful team behind the game. I leave EverQuest in the very capable hands of Ryan Rytan Barker and I have no doubt that hell continue its success long into the future.

Thanks you all for the great memories and I hope to run into everyone again in the future.

Travis Rashere McGeathy
Lead Designer
EverQuest

Turns out he will be joining Curt Schilling's team, along with other former EQ designers:

MASSIVELY reports:

"As we reported yesterday former EverQuest Lead Designer Travis McGeathy announced his departure from SOE. On a message board post he alluded to a "cross country trip." With former SOE employees taking cross country trips tending to end up at 38 Studios, Red Sox pitcher Curt Schilling's start-up, we reached out to the hurler himself for comment.

Curt confirmed to us via e-mail this morning that Travis will indeed be joining SOE alumni Steve Danuser, Ryan Schwayder, Billy Ahlswede, Aaron Carlson, Niraj Desai, Eric Theisz, and Michael Woods in their Maynard, MA offices. Jeez, Curt, wouldn't it just be easier to buy SOE at this point? Curt did not immediately respond to a follow-up on what exactly Travis will be doing."
