    [Quote from original post: As the recent commentary seems to indicate this is not actually just a thread about Magicians and Summoning Stuff, but also one about class balancing quibbles, as with all the '[insert class name here] Talk' threads, I have a few thoughts as well here.]

  • Shadow Knight Talk, Part 2
    As a starting note, all of us, players and Verant staff, really need to keep in mind that we just recently made a huge improvement to all hybrids by removing the experience costs. That must be kept in mind at all times when thinking about any further changes to hybrids.

    Foxglove1 wrote:

    [Quote from original post: Number one. Quit sterotyping SK's as Ogres or Trolls when making items. You ever seen a Erud or DE SK try to wear a full set of BloodEmber armour. Weights a bloody ton!!]

I see your point, but that's the sort of argument that works both ways.

    RunDMZ said:

    [Quote from original post: I'd ask why we're getting Summon Corpse? I don't recall us ever asking for it and it only seems to be pissing the Necro's off.

    The last HT fix took care of most of our problems.]

    Well, that's a good point. The reason for giving Summon Corpse was to make them more wanted in the big raid situation, more or less. Keep in mind, this is the Summon that requires the huge coffin. It's not like the shadow knight is going to be carrying a ton of these things. Having said that, I'm not real sure about that change either. And I may as well bring it up to the team.

    -Dr wrote:

    [Quote from original post: 1) Unable to tank any uber mobs at all , no defensive disc nor any legit offensive disc to use on any uber mobs.]

    At all? That's pretty dramatic. Shadow knights get plate, they have fairly good defensive skills… Are you perhaps exaggerating here? No, a shadow knight can't tank like a warrior, but they shouldn't either.

    [Quote from original post: 2) lvl 60 disc is arguably less usefull then the lvl 55 disc (harmtouch disc)]

    If that's true, then perhaps we should reduce the usefulness of the level 55 discipline… Ok, just kidding. Along with problems, I also need suggested solutions. Ones that make sense.

    [Quote from original post: 3) tied with paladins for lowest dmg output of any melee class by a VERY large margin.]

    Heh, not really. Firstly, shadow knights are hybrids, not a melee class. It's perfectly acceptable that a melee class like the rogue be doing more damage than a hybrid in combat. Rogues don't get spells. And rangers are the melee damage dealing hybrid. Paladins and shadow knights are far more defensive in their abilities. And, well, we could talk about how shadow knights compare in melee damage to bards…

    Keep in mind that shadow knights are a hybrid, not a melee class. They will, therefore, not be equal to the melee classes in straight combat ability, that is the price you pay for the ability to cast spells.

    But perhaps we should evaluate the level of combat ability, probably for all the hybrids.

    [Quote from original post: 4) all spells resisted on uber mobs (other classes have this complaint)thus the penalty we pay for having spells (less melee) is negated]

    Ah, I guess I should have read 4 before commenting on 3. :) Fair point. But you've also had the advantage of those spells all the way up to that very final uber encounter. And some of your spells still work (pets, for example) in a limited way. But, as I said, a review of the combat abilities of hybrids is probably in order.

    [Quote from original post: 5) Epic quest issues (not sure if you wanted these or not)

    please see the many threads on this but basically drops off Cazic thule are rare not to mention CT is a farmed god not a triggered spawn, CT is not any harder than the general in Kithicor, in fact prolly easier to kill but because he is farmed you never see him]

    True, I don't really want to talk about Epic Quests. But I'll make note of your concern, and we'll leave it at that for now.

    [Quote from original post: 6.5) the illogical extra low cap on piercing

    I admit to being slightly baffled as to why you even care about your piercing skill… But I don't know why it's capped low. My guess would be that it was not considered to be a 'knightly' skill, and that shadow knights needed to have some limits on what they could do in comparison to warriors, so dropping the piercing cap seemed like the right thing to do. I can say that I agree with that idea (especially since it was my reasoning for it…)

    [Quote from original post: 7)Cash sinks of SK quests.

    Why does lifeleech cost 3kpp to do again? a lvl 50 spell that cost 3k pp AND items to turn in,
    what makes people think SKs are so rich that they can spend 3kpp just to buy a lvl 50 spell?

    Epic quest costs at least 1900pp to do.]

    I dunno. But, well, is this out of line with what other classes are paying for things? Perhaps you are unfamiliar with the costs of researching even the lower level spells for casters… I'm not sure that these costs are out of line. Especially for the Epic.

    [Quote from original post: 8) Skell form

    not a major issue and i realize you can get skell form items but these are all/all items. The ability to take the form of a skeleton soldier is very cool IMO from an RP prespective necros can do it, chanters can do it, but knights of the dead cannot take skell form unless they have an ALL/ALL item ]

    *shrug* Dunno. Interesting idea, and I'll put it on the list. But since it's not a particularly critical thing…

    Soliptic wrote:

    Had to look this spell up. It seems pretty mild. I think the trouble here might be that a shadow knight wouldn't get it until a bit later than a necromancer. So a fairly weak spell would seem even weaker.

    But I'll add it to the list.

    [Quote from original post: 2) Slightly higher level pets. Nuthin big here, I love having pets, and they are useful as an escape mechanism in a pinch, but make them just a bit more useful, raise thier rank maybe 1 more notch (Level 8 pet at level 9, etc) and they'd be a whole lot more useful]

    Ok, by overwhelming demand, I'll add a request for better pets. But keep in mind that, well, I can certainly see a reluctance to make them more powerful. Consider that they are primarily a soloing tool for the shadow knight, and we're not real excited about encouraging soloing. And the shadow knight can tank for his pet, using the pet essentially like a DoT. So they don't need to be all that tough. But I will add it to the list.

I intend to repost my lists when I've got them all done, and we can further refine them at that time. Right now, I need to get the rest done.

    Xilith said:

    [Quote from original post: What we propose is a change of some kind to the level 60 discipline Leechcurse that will allow us to substitute for a warrior against raid npc's for a time shorter than a warrior and/or not as well. The leechcurse discipline currently lasts 20 seconds with a 72 minute reuse delay, during which 1 hp is healed for every point of melee damage dealt to the npc. When they hit for up to 2800 points a round, this does not extend our tanking abilities even enough to be healed once.]

    Ok… I'm listening… :)

    [Quote from original post: Given the short duration and high reuse time on the ability, here is a possible solution I see (this is my personal opinion, and does not represent other players) that keeps the current flavor and would prove effective in letting us tank as a warrior using defensive for a short time would be to calculate a factor that scales with an npc's level, and multiply our melee damage times that to determine the amount leechcurse heals. Say for instance level/10. Against level 60 npc's that would make Leechcurse heal 6 points for every point we deal. This brings it approximately up to a warrior using defensive on large encounters, yet scales down on normal mobs to not be unbalancing. The precise factor would need to be determined after tuning.]

    That doesn't work for me… Firstly, it requires that the software look at the enemy to make calculations. It's always better to do these things internally, looking at the caster. After all, the monster level should pretty much scale with the character level…

    Unfortunately, this is a level 60 discipline, so it can't really scale with the character…

    Well, I'll add this to the list as is, unless someone comes up with a better implementation.

    [Quote from original post: 4. Bug: Vampiric Curse (level 57 lifetap dot) and Soul Consumption (The effect on the SK epic weapon) should stack with the cleric Regen-based heals, such as Celestial Elixir. This evidently is something due to be fixed for other such stacking issues.]

    Might be a bug, might not. I don't know. But I'll add it to the list.

    [Quote from original post: Overall, most SKs are now happy with the balancing that has been done lately. If the SK (and Paladin) tanking ability against high level npc's is restored to original levels, the majority of the players would have no complaints.]

    This seems to be the primary thing to look at, the tanking ability of higher level shadow knights. Keep in mind that, to me at least, 'tanking' is the ability to take a beating without falling over dead. Damage dealing is a separate issue. And shadow knights should be good tanks, and not so good at damage dealing. That is, at least, my opinion (there may be a good argument that shadow knights should be moderately good at both tanking and damage dealing, falling between paladins and rangers…). Those that want to see more damage from their shadow knight would have to be willing to give up an equal amount of tanking ability, and most don't want that. I see the three hybrids (excluding bards in this instance) as the two sides of the warrior coin, so to speak. The ranger gets most of the damage dealing ability of the warrior, but trades out some of the defense for his spells. Shadow knight and paladins get most of the tanking ability of the warrior and lose some of the damage ability in exchange for their spells.

    That's how I see it at least…

Essentially, if a necromancer gets a spell at 24th level and a shadow knight gets it at 30th, that's a way to control the power of the spells the shadow knight gets. It's essentially 6 levels weaker. Increasing the mana cost for these spells is a way to control the volume of spells, especially since the mana pool for hybrids was brought up recently.

    But I accept that we should probably review the way that all hybrids get their spells. Others at Verant have already suggested that. So it's on my list.

  • Wizard Talk, Part 2
    Well, that was a lot of reading. Besides one meeting, all I've had time to do today is read these class threads and write up very long replies. So let's get on with it!

    In general there are few 'balance' issues in this thread. There are several problems, but things (even seemingly important ones, like the whole Admiral Akbar line of spells) aren't balance issues. And I want to look at the balance issues first.

    A bazillion hit points: The biggest balance issue that I see is the problem that we've given the purely mana dependent wizard in Velious. Once the mana is gone and the enemy is only partially wounded, the wizard is no longer contributing to the fight (except that he is meditating in preparation for helping out later). I see this as the single most important issue facing the wizard class as a whole. And, as I'm discovering with most classes, it's mostly a post 50th level issue.

    I've seen two basic approaches to a solution to this problem. Unfortunately, both are very dramatic (though it may require a dramatic change to rectify this issue). One is simply to increase the wizard's mana reserve, the other is to create a new line of spells that does a percentage of damage based on the current hit points of the target. Honestly, I don't like either one. Raising the mana pool would be a terrific balancing problem. This not only increases the potential damage output of the wizard while he's fighting the bazillion hit point enemy, but at all other times as well. I can't really see this working.

    I admit that the % damage spell idea is interesting. And it does target the problem a lot more specifically. This also seems like an idea that could cause troublesome balancing issues, but far fewer than the mana pool increase. It would require a ton of work, assuming that this sort of spell is even possible…

    There is a third idea that I have, but I can't tell you about because it is based on things that you folks don't get to know (sorry). But it is not too far from the percentage damage idea anyhow.

    Regardless, this is the main concern that I have about balancing the wizard class. And it will probably remain at the top of the wizard list.

    The other more general issue is about the whole level limit cap on wizard (and everyone else's for that matter) damage spells. I can see how this would impact a wizard more than others, since they have little to do when they can't blast things. I'm not sure if this should be changed, but I'll bring it up.

    With the list below, a + means it's on the list, a – means that it is not on the list, and a ? means that I don't think I understand the problem and need more input.

    Specific Spells:

    + Al'Kabor line of spells: The consensus is that this spell line is far less useful than the parallel line (pun!) of pillar spells. It seems that the benefit if a larger area of affect and a single extra target is not worthy of the increased mana cost. Frankly, it seems that there are no wizards casting this spell in Norrath. Heck if I know how to fix these. I'm against any call to simply remove the spell, that does little to actually help anything. Increasing its radius could be very messy. Adding more targets might be the best solution.

    + Flaming Sword of Xuzl: Again, I'm against just eliminating what is already there (keep in mind that ideas like 'take away this spell and give us this one instead' don't really work well). Though I also agree with many of you that wizards shouldn't have pets. I do like the idea of allowing this thing to work sort of more like a DoT (as with the clerical hammer spell). Making it immune to spells and riposte might work to make this spell more useful. In any case, it should be looked at.

    + Harvest: The feeling is that this spell doesn't 'ramp up' properly for levels over 50. Some want to see this spell recast faster, and I'll suggest it. But keep in mind that this is, essentially, increasing the wizards mana pool, which is an idea that I am against in general.

    + Rain Spells: I agree that we should think about taking pets out of the equation. I'm not convinced that more needs to be done than that. We also might need to look at the resist rates on these spells (in conjunction with everything else). But I was thinking that we should consider increasing the over-all number of waves a bit (I mean, really, like by 1) but leaving pets in the formula. This would increase the relative power of wizards. But that also may not be the best thing to do (since there are people that play magicians too…). In general, this series should be looked at.

    - Okiel’s Radiation and the other one: Sure, not the greatest spells. But I can't imagine suggesting that they be made better. So let's just leave them as they are.

    + Atol's Apectral Shackles: As a level 51 spell, it fizzles too much, reportedly more than Fetter a level 58 spell. I agree that we should review the whole thing with high fizzle rate spells.

    ? Snare/Enstill: It sure does seem odd to suddenly get a group snare… Tell me, would you rather have a group or a single target snare in that spell slot?

    + Invisibility to Undead: I, honestly, don't know why wizards have this spell. I want to find out why.

    Teleport Spells:

    + West Karana: Move this portal to the other side of the zone. This has all of the technical nightmares that I pointed out before (complete rebuilding of the zone and time to download it). But heck, it's on the list.

    + Cazil Thule Gate: Mana of you think this is in too dangerous of a location. I'm not sure that there really should be any promise that the place you end up in should be safe. Use this spell when you know you're gonna die, and any destination is better than where you are. But I also see your point.

    + Fade/shadowstep/yonder: These spells have problems going vertically. I'm not sure that this can be fixed, but if it can, it should be.

    + Evacs: I agree that it would be nice if Evacs would take link-dead characters. I have no idea if this is possible, but I can certainly ask.

    + Evacs and Abscond: I will check into the whole thing about these spells clearing or not clearing aggro lists.

    + Abscond: This is another spell that fizzles too frequently.

    Items:

    - The Plane of Growth robe: Honestly, I'm fine with longer casting times on free direct damage spells. Even more so if it's a Lure.

    + Epic Quest: I'll ask them to review the Cazic skin and the whole difficulty of this quest. But keep in mind that I am in favor of these quests being very hard.

    + Concussion spell scroll: This is believed to be too hard to obtain. But, consider that this is a great spell, and having to earn it is a good thing to me. The question is, is it too hard to get.

I'm not sayin it should work flawlessly, but that kind of spell seems to hit 2 of our supposed class-specific strengths (knowledge of the attack coming, and transportation away from the attack) and helps us be a bit less of a liability in groups. Maybe at high levels it could even have mild hate reduction a-la Concussion. Hell, gimme a whole blink line of defensive spells of varying proc frequency and effectiveness.
    This might also address the fact that we got no hp/ac and no pet to help with our defense."

  • Cleric Talk, Part 3
    Well, I think now is a good time for a summary of what I've seen in this thread(s).

    Firstly, there seems to be an agreement that clerics are fairly solidly balanced. Nobody wants dramatic changes (ok, some people do…)

    First, let me touch on some broad subjects.

    Druid and shaman healing: If we decide to increase the ability for these two classes to heal, it will be because the change is needed in order to balance the game. We will not change this balance in such a way as to make a cleric unwanted. Solving a balance problem by creating another one isn't what we intend. We are very aware of the concerns that cleric players have about this, and it will be at the forefront of the discussion when the time comes.

    Clerics are boring: Well, that's hard to argue. But think about it a little. A cleric in the high level game might do a lot of sitting and a lot of healing. But that's pretty much what all casters do at those same levels. The only difference is that the cleric has to choose who to heal, the wizard has to choose who to blast. Being a good cleric is just as challenging at higher levels as being any other caster. Or any other warrior-type for that matter, who only has to decide which opponent to beat on from one moment to the next…

    Ok, so I'm oversimplifying the whole thing. But while the gameplay is a bit different for each class, it's also similar in many ways. And if you are not entertained by the way clerics play on the big raids, you might not be any more entertained if you were playing some other class.

    Having said that, I'm all for finding ways to make things more interesting for clerics at higher levels. And I was looking for those types of things specifically as I went through the threads. Honestly, I saw very few ideas that would make clerics more fun. I'm still hoping for more ideas along those lines.

    Now for the specific suggestions. To simplify this, I'll tag a + in front of ideas that are certainly on the list, a – in front of those that will not make the list, and a ? in front of those that I still don't fully understand. But keep in mind, these are just ideas to take to the team. Being on the list is very far from being in the game…

    Combat/Skills:

    + Defense skills: Several of you have asked for higher dodge and defense skills. I think I can accept those ideas.

    - Attack skills: I'm not too keen on suggesting higher attack skills.

    + Bash: On a related note, some of you would like to be able to bash. I think this is a pretty good suggestion.

    + Bind Wounds: You'd like to have your skill cap on this skill increased. I pretty much agree.

    Spells:

    + Death Pact / Divine Intervention: for the cost (5pp), you think that these spells work too infrequently and don't have a large enough effect when they do work. In general the agreement seems to be that you want a percentage heal from these spells (25/50), and a much higher chance of it actually going off. Certainly something worth looking into.

    ? Aegolism: You seem to want this reviewed. It might be too expensive (10pp) to cast, but I don't think so. And it saves you one spell slot, which is always valuable. It basically seems to do what it was intended to do, save time and spell slots. I'm going to need more information about what is wrong with this spell before I'd even bring it up.


    + Symbol of Naltron:. You feel it is too expensive. Perhaps moving the reagent for this spell to something that costs about half as much (5pp), or increasing it's duration would ease the monetary burden.

    ? Shield of Words: The complaint here seems to be that there is a double mana cost for a 6 point armor class increase. This exactly follows the pattern of the spell line, so I'm not sure why this would need to be changed. Though perhaps it would be more appropriate to offer a larger armor class increase considering that the level increase, though the same, represents a much harder level range.

    + Unswerving Hammer of Faith: Perhaps this spell should be re-evaluated as if it was a DoT. I am very disinclined to ask for the spell to be removed, and just as reluctant to change the type of pet it is (some have suggested that it become an enchanter-style pet). And keep in mind that removing this spell would NOT mean that we would add a new spell in its place… So let's look at re-evaluating the casting cost as if it was a DoT, or something along those lines.

    + Mark of Karn: We should probably take another look at this spell. I'm not sure that I've seen a suggestion here that I like that much. Though I do kind of like the idea of reversing the way it works, basing the amount healed on blows taken rather than dealt. It helps to avoid some of the problems.

    + Divine Light: One of those spells that fizzles too much. I'm in favor of looking at all the spells that fizzle too much.

    + Hero/Heroic Bond: It appears that Heroic Bond, the group version of Heroism, won't overwrite itself or Heroism, It probably should.


    + Fortitude: This spell gives lesser buffs than its predecessor, in exchange for a longer duration. You think it should at least give the same or greater buffs as well as the longer duration.

    + Hammer of Striking and Hammer of Requital: You would like to see these improved somewhat, stat-wise. I'm not sure about giving them any sort of proc, but if so a proc versus undead isn't too bad of an idea.

    + Abolish Disease: You want the 49th level shaman spell. I have not personal opinion on this, but I'll add it to the list.

    + Invisibility (personal only): I'll mention this. But, well, I don't support it. I understand that you find yourselves running to do resurrections, but I just don't see it as the right thing to do. Yes, I also think that wizards should never have been given invisibility to undead…

    - Deity specific spells or abilities: A wonderful idea, one that probably should have been used from the beginning. But it's way too late now to be trying to balance a whole set of new spells like this.

    + Self-only Gate to Temple: Another very interesting idea. Also one that it is probably too late to introduce now.

    - Hold/Mez Undead: Not a bad idea, but once again, I'm not looking for new spell line ideas, just fixes for what is already in.

    - Targetted Divine Aura: Flat out, no. There would be way, way too many possible abuses to this spell, sorry.

Absor Infobits
- Aurum @ 4:43 PM PST
Some big announcements and "Talk" class threads you should all read are contained in today's edition of the Absor Infobits:

  • Wizard Talk
    All right, due to the impression that you all have given me, it seems that I need to cover wizards next…

    I’ve already made several notes to myself about wizards, and I may as well start out this discussion with those notes. Keep in mind that these ‘talks’ are geared toward making sure that the classes are ‘balanced’. Please work toward that goal when posting (yes, even if that means you suggest a ‘nerf’). But I’m also interested in hearing about bugs and such too, I’m just not going to focus on them.

    Here are the notes that I already have. Keep in mind that these are only my notes, and just because I’ve listed something does NOT mean that I like the idea or that the designers are working on making changes based on these notes. This is just what it is, a list of ideas. I’ve also added my notes to you all under the header of Note:

    AoE spells: Mentioned elsewhere… (earlier on my list, probably under mages: basically the idea here is that maybe pets should be removed from the equation).

    Gaze/Glimpse: Due to the clipping plane, these spells don’t really allow further seeing, just zooming in (which makes your resolution worse anyhow).

    Note: this may not be ‘fixable’ at all due to the way the game works. It’s not like we can force your PC into a higher resolution when you use this spell…

    Fade/Shadowstep/Yonder: There seems to be a problem with these spells. Since they will not teleport you up, they don’t work too well in hilly terrain; because the spell only sends you a few feet (stopping when it reaches a hill or building).

    Note: Again, this may also not be something we can change, but it’s on the list.

    Bind Sight: Seems to cause viewer to see the target as running into walls or spinning in circles if target goes up an incline.

    Enstill: It seems that this spell is not a more useful or more powerful root, it’s just more expensive.

    Cazic Gate: At level spell is attained, using it will most often cause death because the caster appears amidst many aggressive monsters that /con white to them.

    Note: We’ve actually discussed this before. If I remember correctly, the general feeling was that having a dangerous gate like this wasn’t necessarily a bad thing. But it is also still on my list.

    Tishan’s Relocation: This spell evacuates you to a very dangerous location, a monster crossroads in the Skyfire mountains. Could the location be moved, or was it intended that this be a very dangerous evacuation spell?

    Note: See note about Cazic Gate.

    Concussion: A level 39 spell dropped by a creature in a location that requires a party of level 50s to handle. Should it be in such a location?

    Icestrike effects: Graphical effect looks like fire.

    Lightning Bolt effects: looks exactly like Fire Bolt.

    Other lightning and cold effects: It seems that there might be other spells that have the look of fire when they should not.


    Having just re-read this list, I see nothing at all related to actual balancing stuff. This is all nit-picky stuff about minor effects and bugs and such (with the possible exception of Area of Effect spells, changing those would certainly have an effect on class balances). So, please, try to focus on balance issues…

  • No New Updates For A While
    For at least a few weeks we will be doing some internal work that will prevent us from making any updates to the game unless they are critical (and there are no such things that we know of at this time).

    We will still be taking in reports about bugs and other suggestions for change (such as the Class Talk stuff), but we won’t be making any changes to the game. And the two-week timeframe is only a guess at the moment, it could go longer.

    While this means there will be no changes to the game during this time, we will still be maintaining the servers, the physical hardware, and all other aspects of the game. We are just not changing any aspects of the game software itself.

  • Plane of Mischief Open
    I just wanted to clarify that. Despite all of the confusion about it on our end, this plane is open and available. I’m sorry about the conflicting tales you might have heard from our end.

    Again, Mischief Plane is open. But, so far, nobody has been able to outsmart the master of trickery to find the way in.

  • Cleric Talk
    I’m doing this one next simply because I’ve already done the other two ‘healer’ classes, and having input from you all about clerics would help round that all out.

    I’m not sure that I really see anything major that needs to be addressed about clerics. But I’d like to hear your opinions about what you would like to see changed, what’s broken and all of that. Primarily I’d like to focus on ‘balance’ issues, but I’m always open to other input as well. Just so you know, the only notes I have on clerics to date is:

    Celestial Healing and Celestial Elixer now stack with all regen spells, including Bear Form and Skin Like Nature. But it will not stack with Lich and other spells in that line. = fixed

    So let’s hear it.

Discussion: eBay and Intellectual Property
- Aurum @ 6:12 PM PST
We've all heard the news by now, eBay has removed all EQ auctions presumably at Verant's request months and years after they should have done it if they were going to in the first place. Tonight Verant's Alan VanCouvering finally address the heated subject on the Gameplay Board in a somewhat cryptic response:

    Unfortunately, at the moment I'm not in a position to really discuss this from a company point of view. And until I'm able to do that, I'm going to withhold any personal comments as well.

    But I’m sure we could have an interesting debate on the topic. :)

    Alan
Regrettably that thread was closed minutes later, likely due to flames.

This raises all sorts of questions and issues about EverQuest and intellectual property. Most, if not all, of which are resolved via the little contract we all agree to upon entering the game. For better or worse.

I've received a lot of comments about this story. Most of them can be summarized by the following comment an anonymous person sent in:
When a player sells on eBay, they are merely getting paid for time to acquire an item, then transferring the item to another EverQuest character in exchange for real life money for that time.

What's so complicated/wrong about that?

Verant's so called 'intellectual' property remains on the same server, untouched and unharmed in any way! Verant does not own, and can not own the time in which players spend playing this game. Verant is simply wasting their resources with this topic, and could be working on way more important gameplay and development issues.
The question posed in the latest Player View Discussion is whether this was a good move or not. More discussion and talk about this issue can be found there.
