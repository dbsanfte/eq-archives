Gordon Infobits: Late Night Edition
[5 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:39 PM PDT
Here's even more posts from off the Gameplay Board that Verant's Gordon Wrinn has made in the last few minutes:

  • Experience Loss for Thought
    [Quote from original post: Verant: Considering the steep experience curve and the very real chance of losing your corpse for post 50 players]

    Well I wouldn't call it a "very real chance", I'd call it a non-negligible chance. Given sufficiently talented, knowledgeable, and powerful compatriots, it probably won't be an issue. The posts about this over the past few weeks have just been to remind people that the possibility does exist, as it has always existed, even though the skill of players up to this point has often made that possibility trivial.

    [Quote from original post: My idea - - No experience penalty when you die if your 51+. That doesn't seem to unreasonable does it! Though it probably doesn't fit your Vision(TM) which is predicated upon slowing people's progress so they will have to pay your salaries for yet another month. All i want is 5 good reasons why this isn't a good idea (and that Vision(TM) crap doesn't cout).]

    I guess I should ask for 5 good reasons why there shouldn't be experience loss at 51 when there is at 50. The reasons for experience loss at 51+ are the same reason as there is experience loss at any other level excluding levels 1-5. We do allow players to mitigate this chance to a mere fraction of the game design by providing resurrection. Of course you need to make friends with other people in game to have this benefit, which I don't think is unreasonable.

  • Monk Items
    The weight on many of the monk staffs has been reduced and will go live the next time that we patch items. As for the armor issues, that's still on the plate for evaluation, but I don't have an ETA on it at this time.

  • Is EQ supposed to be fun?
    [Quote from original post: Isnt everquest supposed to be fun?]

    Of course that's the goal, but as "fun" is entirely subjective, with each person having their own idea of what "fun" is, we try to provide as many different definitions of "fun" as possible in game.

    There are certain ideas of "fun" that are incompatible with other ideas. For instance, some might think that it would be fun if they were provided with items, levels, skills, and abilities with little or no effort. While we think that some might think this is fun in the short term, we firmly believe that those things will be more fun, and fun for a longer period of time, if they are earned rather than given.

    Much in the same fashion that sunny days aren't appreciated without the cloudy ones, the amount of fun in the game is related to other factors that some might consider aren't fun. For another real world analogy, I spent the last 8 years in Arizona. There, with over 300 days of sunshine per year, we learn to appreciate the cloudy ones.

    EQ is very much like this. Without the rarety of items, the amount of fun that people have by acquiring items is reduced. Without some downtime, those non-stop periods of edge-of-the-chair excitement that everyone has experienced would not be as special. Without the difficulty of advancement, advancement would not be as special.

    Fun remains subjective, and will be subjective for every game, especially in MMORPGs where the goal of the developer is to attract long-term subscribers. People will quit these games and go on to the next one when the fun that they get from playing it is no longer worth the things that are necessary for the fun to exist in the first place. Our goal of course is to make sure that it takes a long time for the average person to traverse that course.

Gordon Infobits
[4 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:07 PM PDT
Here's today's edition of the Gordon Infobits:

  • Game Enchancement Ideas
    [Quote from original post: 1) A hot button similiar to /pet report health that reports mana for players.]

    There's really no reason that a player would be able to look at another and determine how much mana they have left. If you take health as an example, someone should be able to look at someone and see how bad off they are based upon cuts, bruises, etc. But mana is magical power controlled by a character, it's not something that can be observed.

    There's also the point that mana management and coordination are certainly an important part of the game.

    [Quote from original post: 2) A message for when you combine items. I don't know how many times I have accidently hit combine on a box, whether it be a sowing kit or Elemental Grimoire or whatever, and lost items that I spent alot of time collecting. A message similiar to the one you get when you are about to destroy an item, just aimed to making sure you definately want to combine these items.]

    We've also ruled out this enhancement in the past. The possibility of destroying items unintentionally is the only thing that keeps players from using combine-boxes to store items that they weren't made for. The only way we'd look into making a change of this nature is if we also spent a good deal of time changing the way combineable containers work so that you can only put items in them that you mean to combine right away.

    [Quote from original post: 3) I know it's been said before, but a command to send a message to everybody on your friend's list. Not sure how far you guys are coming with this, but the sooner the better. ]

    We were planning on implementing a /ftell option, but have taken it off the list pending further discussion. I'll get back with everyone when we make a final determination.

  • Experience Reward Per Critter Broken?
    Though the formula you've provided is incorrect, you are correct in your assertion that experience rewarded does not take into account the special skills or abilities of an NPC.

    This does make some NPCs harder or easier to take down with differing strategies, and is as designed. We don't differ experience rewards on a per-creature basis, and it's not really possible to dynamically account for the different strategies that someone might use. For instance, it is a lot easier for a necro to take something down by kiting it than it is for a warrior to melee it to death in many cases, but at the time of death, the creature doesn't know if it's been kited or if it's been meleed to death to make an additional experience reward valid at that time.

  • The Longest GM Quest
    Very interesting and thank you for bringing it to my attention. I'm aware of several server-specific subplots that occurred during the time of the Lanys quests.

    As you may or may not know, Lanys fought the chosen of Tunare, Firiona Vie in the woods of Kithicor. Firiona almost won, but as she was about to strike the killing blow, Laarthik K`Shin, Lanys' teacher and protector pulled a blue orb from his breastplate, which weakened the barrier between the Plane of Hate and the mortal realm. Innoruuk's wrath filled the forest as Laarthik called upon him to save Lanys. Lanys was lifted up to the Plane of Hate, and Innoruuk's wrath killed all but the hardiest in the forest.

    It was later rumored that someone led a crusade to return Lanys from the Plane of Hate to the mortal realm, so you may very well get a chance to continue your quest.

  • Experience Loss Regarding RL Time Loss
    [Quote from original post: Abashi. Can you comment on EXP loss reguarding RL time loss.]

    This is really a matter of perception and I'm afraid that I don't think that there is a way to explain it that will sit well with everyone.

    There must be some advantage for not dying. As such, there must be a disadvantage for dying. The disadvantage for dying must be sufficient to make the advantage for not dying worthwhile. Now, "sufficient" and "worthwhile" are subjective terms that everyone views differently. Since these terms are subjective, we assign values that we believe are sufficient, making the rewards worthwhile, and present that to the players as part of the game.

    [Quote from original post: To clarify my question, what i am asking is a statement saying that yes, Verant thinks that it is okay to take an hour or so worth of work away from a player when they die.]

    I wouldn't look at it as "taking an hour away". In general, you (meaning the players in general) had fun during that hour. We can't take away the fun that you experienced. Now maybe some feel that they cannot have fun until their experience is back to where it was before they died, but again, that is a matter of personal perception.

    I choose to play the game to have fun. During that process I also gain levels, skills, and abilities at whatever rate I happen to get them. I don't go hunting for the best experience, but rather for the most fun.

    Other people play with different priorities. It doesn't make any one person's choice right or wrong, it is just the style that they choose. We believe that most people feel that something earned is sweeter than something given, which brings us back to the subject of "worthwhile" mentioned above.

  • Reducing Class Arguments
    Our goal is to reduce the number of class-specific discussions that we get into, rather than increase them. Class-specific issues, by their nature, are often no-win situations due to the fact that we believe that the classes are balanced, and some players disagree with the assertion.

    There are often cases where there is really nothing that we can say that will help people feel better about their class, and trying to do so just breeds further bad feelings, a mistake that I've made in the past. While I'm not opposed to having periodic real-time chats, and both Brad and I have been known to drop into the IRC from time to time, I wouldn't want to make it a class-specific affair, or for class-specific issues in general.

  • Why Post?
    You have a point.

    It is true that there are a lot of people who post on this board for the pleasure of tearing us down publicly, but there is an even greater number of people who lurk here or post infrequently in hopes of having a specific issue important to them addressed or answered. I get emails every day from people who read the forum, support what we are trying to do here on the board even if they don't agree with us on every issue, but who won't post for fear of being flamed.

    The people who do nothing but flame everything I or anyone else from Verant says will always be here as long as we continue to tell the truth, thereby getting into situations where we often have to tell people things other than that which they were hoping for. I personally don't see most of them any more, and only respond to a flame or insulting post when the issue contained within is of importance to most and there isn't a non-flame version of the post readily available.

    I'm still considering asking the moderators to begin strictly moderating the board to try and pull it back into the fray, but doing so might be fruitless until we get the board restricted to current EverQuest players. I'd certainly like to see a little more courtesy here. I'm not looking for people to agree with us all of the time, just for people willing to give it an honest chance.

  • Moving From Rallos Zek
    At this point we're not planning on allowing moves between RZ and the team-servers, as the rulesets are incompatable, as are some of the settings within the character file. On RZ, all players basically have their PvP switch on. On TZ and VZ, that switch is off and the ability to handle attack permissions is controlled elsewhere in code. There's also a philosophical issue with the advantages and disadvantages of having your character grow up on a different ruleset. For instance, we wouldn't consider allowing people to move from a non-PvP server to a PvP server or vice-versa.

    It's something we'll have to give continuing thought, but that's our plan at present.

  • Indirect PK
    I'd have my money on an AE spell as well here. NPCs have the option to cast any spell available to a PC of their level, and could very well strike back with an AE spell. Non-combatants can be hit by the spell as well, in the same fashion that dragons hit everyone around with their AE, even if certain people aren't fighting.

    [Quote from original post: I didn't know that npcs hit back with AE spells, if so that is a blatant bug as it can cause people to be killed on non PvP servers.]

    It's not a bug, but anyone purposely trying to take out other players in this manner is subject to action by the GM staff. Since you can't reliably get an NPC to cast an AE spell versus a DD spell, it will probably be a very rare occurrence.

  • Two Quick Notes
    I checked into the blood ember weights when dealing with the darkforge armor issues. Blood Ember weights are correct and are the tradeoff for the exceptional AC.

    I'll check into the Vamp embrace issue tomorrow.

  • Frogloks in Swamp of No Hope
    The escaped frogloks are not 100% spawns. In other words, they have placeholders that you need to kill in order to get them to spawn.

    Start killing everything and you might see them pop up a bit more often.

  • "What's Wrong With EQ" List
    I'll support this list, but I'd like to see it limited to "broken", as in quest that do not work. "Too hard for level" and "Horrid rewards" are subjective labels, and though are often on target to a certain extent, don't have the signal-to-noise ratio that black-and-white things like "broken" or "not broken" does.

    Also keep in mind to provide good information for locating the specifics of the quest that you are claiming is broken. There are literally thousands of quests in game, most of them have different names that people give them. No one here at the office can quote every one without some serious investigation.

  • Monk Issues
    [Quote from original post: All we want to know is the damage and delay of our fists from 50-60 so that we know if we can even come close to doing our best damage with our bare fists- not an unreasonable request, I must say, considering the fact that all other melee types get to knw their damage and delay with any given weapon.]

    Ok, I guess I can buy that. I think that it would be fair to say that assuming that a monk has maxed out all related skills, that an armed monk, wielding looted weapons appropriate for their level will generally be better off than an unarmed monk, but not to the extent that an unarmed monk is "useless". Human monks hands will cap at 14/28 at 60th level, with Iksar monks being slightly slower.

  • Ancient Cyclops/Boot Spawn Rate
    We're basically happy with the rate that the boots are coming into the game at this point. I know that these are items that everyone would like to have, but that's not how we have it set up at this point. Many of the suggestions that I've seen would only further the idea that these items are entitlements.

    The ancient cyclopse is really our only way to control the rate that the boots come into game, which keeps the item valuable.

    [Quote from original post: bump, because Abashi usually only reads the first two pages :-P]

    Actually, I just got this off of page 10.


PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Change Pending
[1 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:00 PM PDT
Gordon announced new information concerning some changes coming to the Race War servers. Here's the post from the Gameplay Board:

    Hello all,

    Today we've been brainstorming about what has become known as the PermaMez bug present on the PvP-teams servers. Without going into specifics on how the bug is used, basically, a player can use the interaction between illusions and the mesmerize spell to cause an opponent to be permanently mesmerized. If you play on the PvP Teams servers, you are probably somewhat familiar with this issue.

    The issue that makes the bug possible is deeply woven into the fabric of how illusions work, making it something that cannot be addressed directly. Initial ideas (ruled unacceptable) on how to fix this issue included:

    1. Turning off illusions entirely on the PvP Teams servers.
    2. Making stun and mez spells invalid in PvP combat.
    3. Making an illusion cause a team-switch. In other words, if a dark elf cast "Illusion: Human", they would become a human in all senses, able to attack and be attacked by people not on the human team.
    4. Making an illusion cause a team-switch, but only for the purpose of Mez and Stun spells. This idea would lead to situations where one person could attack someone who couldn't attack them in return.

    The final solution that we've arrived at is the best method that we've thought of to address the issue, and the one that will most likely go live unless someone here can bring something to our attention that we've overlooked. The solution is:

    On PvP Teams servers, casting a *non-beneficial spell* upon another *player-character* will result in any active illusion upon the caster, even illusions that have other effects, being canceled.

    "Illusion" in this case is defined as any spell effect that makes you look like something different than you actually are. This would include all of the enchanter illusions, as well as spells such as treeform, wolf form, the Call of Bones series, and Item-activated illusions such as the Guise of the Deceiver, etc.

    If anyone has any questions or comments on this change, or can think of any issues that make the solution worse than the problem it solves, please post follow-ups in this thread.

    Thanks,

    -Gordon

    It would, however, be interesting to know how many are in this position currently.

  • Who Dun It?

    Knowing the name of the people who play wizards, necros, warriors, or SKs isn't as important as the fact that we do have people here in general who are familiar with the aspects of each class.

  • Abashi Quote
    The post that was replied to was about the experience curve, not a class. The quote, "with a ranger of all things" was speaking to the high experience requirement of the class, not the class itself. Had the first character been a SK or Paladin, the quote would have been similar.

  • Root and Snare
    [Quote from original post: Shame on you, Abashi...you're a necro, and you know this to be false. Dark a mob and send your pet in, then run away and cast a dd or a dot at it...why is the mob running at you now?]

    If you look at the mob, it will usually turn, stop, and hit the pet a few times, then turn and continue pursuit. It doesn't just chase after the necro ignoring getting hit in the back by the pet. It's more than the taunting being done by the pet. The NPC wants to kill the necro, but by virtue of being under a slowing spell settles for hitting the pet every few steps.

    The effect is not as pronounced (or as useful) as when rooted, but it exists.

  • Changing Servers
    Over time, servers develop their own atmosphere or community based upon the personalities of those playing on them. With a relatively small population of players on a server, people build (or hurt) their reputations, and live and work in concert with others that they have in essense "grown up" with.

    We feel that this community is one of the things that is responsible for the success of EQ. People are not faceless characters cloaked in anonymity, but instead must reap the rewards or consequences of their actions. This, over all, tends to encourage people to behave more honorably in their adventures.

    Based upon the same logic that we use in regards to not changing names unless they violate the naming policy, character moves are likewise prohibited as it allows someone to escape their reputation, and also breaks down the positive aspects of a community.

    We have in the past allowed characters to be moved under certain limited circumstances but that started causing more problems than it solved, and we stopped doing them. The only reason that we allow them now is for the purpose of a spreading out population, based upon the logic that enough people are moving from the server that reputations tend to follow the move. We are also planning on allowing some PvP players to move to another ruleset in the near future, but that is the extent of the program.

  • Experience Loss
    [Quote from original post: Pre-50 Exp loss in Kunark Way too high. I died 3 times in Kunark on Monday, and lost an entire bubble of exp each time. Considering that it took me nearly 1-1 1/2 hours per bubble, this is waaay too much.]

    Experience loss on death isn't tied to locale. It's only a function of your level and the total amount of experience that you have. In other words, had you died somewhere in EQProper, you would have lost the same amount of experience that you did by dying in Kunark.

  • Group Healing
    [Quote from original post: Quick question for any one whom plays a healer.

Kunark and the Casual Gamer
[2 Comments] - Aurum @ 7:34 PM PDT
Gordon responded to a post stating that Kunark is not for the casual gamer. Here's the argument followed by Gordon's response:

    Kunark sucks for casual gamers NO chance of getting lvls NO chance of getting Spells

    Kunark dungeons generally give more experience than dungeons in the main land. We've also implemented the ability to get *good* loot randomly, eliminating the perceived need to "camp" for items.

    Both of these issues point to Kunark being better for the casual gamer.

    Now, assuming your statement is about levels 51+, experience is slow for everyone there, and spells drop slowly and randomly, meaning that someone who fights in a dungeon 2 hours per day has just as much of a chance of getting a spell as a guy who plays 12 hours per day does in that 2 hour period.

    -Gordon
