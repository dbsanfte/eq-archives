  Do You Read the Quest Texts?  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
When you are playing, do you take the time to read all the text for the quests you are completing?

Some players are discussing whether they do or don't on EQPlayers. Developer Nodyin posted that he spends a lot of time on the lore and the text, so he wishes people would read it!

Some people commented they would like to read it, but are too busy avoiding aggro mobs to be able to take the time to do much more than type the trigger words. Others say when the entire raid is clicking, it's hard to take the time to read it.

The interesting thing about reading quest texts is that you learn a lot more about the lore of Norrath, which is actually fairly complex. Every quest has some kind of story that tries to immerse players in the world, from kill ten rats to epic quest arcs.

  SOE Previews New Station Launcher  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
SOE recently hosted a "Game Day" in New York City, where they unveiled not only the Secrets of Faydwer expansion for EverQuest, but a new Station Launcher feature.

The new paradigm for gaming is community building, and the previewed Station Launcher hopes to promote community across SOE games, as well as cut down logging in time by offering simultaneous patching for all its games.

Player Profiles for All Games
Using the the station access concept already in place ($29.95 for access to all SOE games), the new Station Launcher will streamline access to all SOE's games. All SOE games features will be run through the Station Launcher, and as part of that, a player can create a persistent profile that will show characters and names for each game that a person plays. As part of this feature, the Station Launcher will also show where your friends are logged in at any given moment, so you can keep in touch across games. Cross-game tells are also in the works.

Simultaneous Patching
The Station Launcher will also allow for simultaneous patching of SOE games. Instead of logging in and waiting for a game to patch, the new Launcher would have patched each game for you while you were away as the patches became available. No more waiting on long patches (or even short ones) when you log in.

A couple of nice features designed to encourage people to play other titles in the SOE library, without worrying about lengthy patches on log in, as well as the ability to sustain friendships that are often lost when players switch games.

-- Kwill @ 9:18AM Fri 11 May 2007

9th Year Veteran Reward on Test Server  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Tadena posted:

"The New Vet Reward is on Test if you wish to see it and feedback on it

New AA called "Armor of Experience"

This Ability grants you several layers of protection from harm. As you take damage each layer will absorb five strikes before failing. As your armor fails you will take more and more damage."

Developer Nodyin explained: "It's a melee guard that goes through 4 stages of protection from 100% down to 25% before fading."

Nodyin also explained a little more about it in the same thread:

"In designing this AA I was very conscious of balance concerns. This AA is actually unique in EQ up to this point. I had to have several changes made to the way the spell system and some of the spell effects worked to get this to happen. Part of the issue was changing the spell system so that we could create this AA in a way that would both scale from level 1 to 75 and that wouldn't be overpowered at any point.

Does it have a gameplay impact? Yes, but not to the extent that it will change the balance of content. Is it useful? I think so, and I hope you agree."

The refresh time was four hours intially, but has since been changed to 20 hours as are the other veteran reward AAs.

  Producer's Letter: Buried Sea Expansion  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
From Clint Worley, the new EQ Producer:

Producers Letter January 29, 2007

I wanted to start off this Producer's letter by saying THANK YOU to all of the beta testers that are actively participating in The Buried Sea beta. I know that jumping into a work in progress can be frustrating at times, but the teamwork between the developers and the players really makes EverQuest into the classic that it is. Special thanks go out to the guilds that are helping with the larger tuning tasks.

Being able to participate in beta tuning is a huge job for both the developers and the testers involved. I know that many of you want to help out with each beta and we welcome the extra help. We wanted to get in a few new guilds to help us this time around. We tend to stick with guilds that have proven in past beta's that they are professional, on time and willing to work with our developers to ensure a better game for everyone else. If you have not participated in beta tuning with the developers, don't worry. We plan to cycle in new guilds when needed and will send out open calls in a similar manner as we did on The Buried Sea. The guilds that are listed below have all contributed to the tuning passes we are doing on this expansion.

Midnight Tempest - new beta testing guild from our board recruiting
Silent Redemption - new beta testing guild from our board recruiting
Fire & Fury
The Relentless
Silent Resurgence
Township Rebellion
Triality

Our internal Quality Assurance department is hard at work checking all of the new zones, quests, items and additional content that will roll out in The Buried Sea next month, and the work is right on schedule.

I plan on having a weekly feature on the webpage leading up to the launch of The Buried Sea as well, which we kicked it off on Friday with a feature on the Guild Banners. If you haven't seen it - go check it out! They are really cool and they should help guilds out quite a bit by reducing downtime and travel time.

I have been reading the boards regarding me taking over the reigns [reins! -- Ed.] as Producer, and I appreciate the votes of confidence, and also understand the skeptics. The development team and the community have seen many people come and go, and I was aware of that when I joined the team. I am looking to bring a new angle to EverQuest. As I have said before, my intentions are not to change the game, but I do believe that there are quite a few things that could be done to make playing it more fun.

While I can't comment on promises made in the past, I can listen to you now and work with you to make this game a place that you love to come back to everyday. Changes can take some time but making sure that the right changes happen is far more important than rushing anything. Just remember that I am listening, so keep posting!

Clint "Jourdian" Worley
EverQuest Producer
Sony Online Entertainment

  Guild Banners Explained  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Part of the new Buried Sea expansion, coming out in February, is the Guild Banner feature. The developers have posted a page explaining how they work on the EQPlayers site (with pics!).

In a nutshell:

Banners were created to help late arrivals get to a raid.

The basic banner is free to any guild. Advanced banners can be purchased in the guild hall and have extra features. Guilds can customize their banners with emblems and colors.

Guild members purchase special stones in the guild hall to use the banner after it's been placed.

Only one banner can exist in the world at a time.

The banner is planted by the leader or an officer, and stays in place for one hour.

There's lots more detail on the page linked above!

  Interesting EQ Lore  [ 1 Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
With a game as old as EQ, you know there have to be a lot of rumors, hoaxes, mysteries, and other strange things connected with the game. The folks over at EQPlayers have started a Collection of Strange Information thread that tries to write down some of these oddities.

Here are a few Player -Wycca came up with:

- The "penalty" box with no exits GM's used to summon misbehaving players to in Qeynos Catacombs

- The old message boards that used to exist in Qeynos (disabled with Dx8 revamp I believe)

- Mayong Mistmoore actually existing in Mistmoore (claimed he existed during Beta and was never live, his model is Maestro's model)

- Rubicite Armor, Lustrous Russet Armor - old armor sets that were removed, probably not as well known now as they were in 00/01

- PoFear before limitations imposed, lvl 5 ogres in the zone waiting to pick up armor drops

- Rumors of a hidden area in Neriak 3rd Gate, alternatively accessed via a chamber pot which lead to a secret evil transportation network (there is a hidden tunnel back there, leads nowhere), also linked to the King and Queen actually appearing in Neriak 3rd gate

- Mysterious unfinished zone lines in SolA (it warps you back to where you started), theres a few others spread out across the game (old Oasis had a zone line by Orc Highway, Stonebrunt mountains has several), hot topics back in the day

- The Hole has a big gate and a zone line that leads nowhere, where is the Plane of Underfoot?

Vanguard: Saga of Heroes Early Access to Live Servers Begins Friday, January 26th

Sony Online Entertainment and Sigil Games Online announced today that early access for Vanguard: Saga of Heroes will commence January 26 at approximately 10:00 a.m. PST. Gamers who placed pre-orders either at retail or digitally will get to experience Vanguard: Saga of Heroes before the games official launch on Tuesday, January 30th. Those with a quick mind can use diplomacy to convince kings to move armies and change the course of history. With player-crafted boats, flying mounts, and customizable player housing, Vanguard offers players a world of their own choosing.

  SOE Contest for Recipes  [ Comment ]
Print News | Category: EQ Live
Have a good fall/harvest recipie? Enter it for a prize in game!

Kytherea posted on EQPlayers:

November Forum Challenge

This week in the US, many people will be celebrating Thanksgiving with family and eating awesome food like: turkey, ham, mashed potatoes with gravy, stuffing, pumpkin pie and lots of other good stuff. Many cultures around the world celebrate similar holidays centered on harvest time.

The forum challenge for November is to post a recipe and picture from your culture that is used to celebrate harvest time. (It does not necessarily have to be a recipe your family serves)

The categories for this challenge will be:

Most unusual recipe
Most appealing recipe
Recipe most likely to have the most leftovers if served in your house


The Harvest Challenge will start today as of this post and run through Monday, November 27th at noon PST. You may submit your recipe and picture to cdelzer@soe.sony.com. Please include your forum handle and what culture uses this recipe for its harvest celebration as well as the category you wish to submit your recipe in.

On Monday, November 27th sometimes after noon PST, I will post the recipes on the Just for Fun forum and start a poll for each category so the community can vote on the recipes. One challenge winner from each category will receive a special forum title and an in-game prize for one character of their choice.
