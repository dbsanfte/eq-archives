In times of turmoil such as these, it is our call to carve order from chaos.  There are many forces in Norrath vying for dominance, but we will have our will known.  We are a guild not bound by race, but unified in mind and manifest in strength that flashes in greatness on the battlefield.  Our bond to our guild mates is supreme and the power we thus possess exists is used to promote order and enforce all that is good.  Our deeds speak of a heritage from which grand stories are told.  Through blood spilt and souls transmuted, our fates are bound one to the other.  Let all in Norrath come to respect the name: Dragon Knight!
 
Raids
Many in Norrath are content to spend vast amounts of time with safer endeavors, such as camping and farming. We, the Dragon Knights, prefer the invasion, the Raid! The challenge of a raid gets the blood boiling and burns away the excess of the body and the mind. Through this we elevate the group as a whole to a single fighting entity capable of seeking new and ever more challenging exploits.

This is not to discount the need for updated gear and equipment. Indeed we pull together to gain those items. And we cannot discount the experience needed to attain the next level of progression. Indeed we pull together to this end also. But, be you mindful, these are but secondary to the Raid.
 
Time Zones
So that all may attend we make effort to schedule raids appropriate to our members. Though days of the week are important in this regard, it is our experience that the time of the event is critical to a successful campaign. Most of us are on the Pacific Coast (PST) while others may be found oversees in Europe. And yet some others are closer to the East Coast. On this I must be blunt, most raids are scheduled on Wednesday or Friday at 8pm PST. Weekend raids will generally start around 11am PST. There will be exceptions as we cherish our existing members for whom this is a problem. But, I ask that you seriously consider this when seeking membership.
 
Loot!
There have been many duels fought, friends parted, and tears shed over the right to jump in and claim the prizes of war. Alas reward is sorely missed for a battle well fought. 'Tis a tough and difficult decision to award a glorious item to one and and not another. The High Council has debated this manner for many moons seeking to be fair and just, being true to our fellows in arms. One cannot discount long standing and unflagging devotion. One cannot discount the merit that is bestowed to one just entering our ranks. We strive together as a family and we cannot deny reward to any of our number. In this vein, the Loot Officer of the High Council will enforce our policy and bestow items according to the policy set forth by the High Council.

For most items, those discovered during normal course, a simple lottery is exercised. Members included will generally be limited to those who are participating in the raid and of a class best suited to the items use. Of the members meeting this criteria, ones who have previously won (any item) will be excluded until all members of the class have been awarded an item. Once all members of that class have received an item (any item) all are allowed back into the lottery for the next item. Members who will not put the item to use are asked to voluntarily exclude themselves from the lottery. In the event an item drops which may be passed from one to another (droppable) and all participating member most suited to its use own the item, then that item may be awarded to either another member in participation, held for a member not participating, or deposited in the Guild Treasury at the discretion of the Loot Officer.

On occasion some in attendance will not be a Dragon Knight, but this does not exclude them from the treasures of war all together. At the discretion of the Loot Officer these people may be included in an item lottery. However, this will likely be done only after attending members have received such an item. Indeed, many fine people have been awarded items on our journeys.

Precious and rare loot (e.g., Rare Dragon Loot, BP, Robe, Rare weapon) is seldom discovered and is cherished above all else. All our members fight hard and die for the cause, but some more than others. Some uphold our honor daily while others are seen less often. Extraordinary effort and contribution to the guild cannot be discounted. For this reason rare items are awarded based purely on Guild Seniority. Seniority is based upon many factors and all must be counted: long standing, participation, and contribution. The High Council decides seniority based on all of these things and rare items will be awarded accordingly.

Finally, if an item is awarded but no longer needed by that individual (and the item is droppable) then we ask that the item be returned to the Guild for re-distribution to another member. If you find such an item in your inventory please notify the Loot Officer for guidance.
 
Joining Dragon Knight
Times change... What once was, is no more - and in its place we strive to achieve something greater. We strive for something more complete and whole, more perfect...

Dragon Knight now stand as a proud symbol in our land. The name Dragon Knight inspires many to rally to our side to push the evils of our land deeper into the shadows. Brave adventurers from across Norrath, who have proven their valor in battle and devotion to our cause, are welcomed into our fold where they proudly stand with us. Our unity makes us stronger. Now, reaching to greater achievements, we strive to mold and temper the steel of our friendship. This, so that we may pull even closer together in spirit - creating a bond no war, nor death, nor evil god may pull apart... 

CLASS RECRUITMENT -- With increased size is achieved strength - to a point. And at that point, additional size becomes a weakness. A balance of classes must be struck. In order to strike the right class balance, we are now choosing to severely limit additions to our guild so that this necessary goal is met. Dragon Knights has some of the most skilled players in the game of the following classes. Thus, Dragon Knight is currently not accepting applications from the following classes: Druid, Necromancer, Paladin, Ranger and Shadow Knight.  All is not bleak however - for many weary travelers well deserve a place in our midst. A new cycle for acceptance has been enacted and, though the journey may be longer, still provides light in the lonely darkness. Dragon Knights are recruiting noble players in the following classes: Bard (30), Cleric (100), Enchanter (80), Magician (80), Monk (40), Rogue (40), Shaman (30), Warrior (75) and Wizard (50). The number in parentheses is the Recruiment Priority Index. The higher number in parentheses, the greater is our interest in applicants of that class.

LEVEL -- We strongly encourage all potential Dragon Knights to attain level 55 before seeking acceptance. If you are a member of a class with a high RPI score then exceptions may be made to the level restriction, on a case by case basis, and in concert with the guilds need for that class. In the members section of our site you may notice several people not of this level. Be at ease however in the knowledge that they are being trained by sponsors within the guild.

GUILD AFFILIATION -- Dragon Knight will not consider the application of any player that is guilded.  Only those who have been confirmed to be guildless in game will have their application accepted for consideration.

SPONSOR -- For those wishing to gain acceptance to Dragon Knight, a guild sponsor should be found. Our members do not take this responsibility lightly to be sure. I will leave it in your care to determine the best way to achieve this friendship and trust. Dragon Knights hold certain values in very high esteem, such as honesty, honor and integrity, and it is these values which provide a basis for acceptance.

APPLICATION -- Once a sponsor is found you should feel free to email your application to Envino@aol.com. Be sure to use the form found on the applicants section of this message board. Use this link to view the form: http://pub9.ezboard.com/fthedragonknightsapplytojointhedragonknights.showMessage?topicID=370.topic  Once the High Council finds nothing wanting in your application, you will be notified that your status is that of a formal applicant and your evaluation process will begin.

EVALUATION PERIOD -- Following the custom of time immemorial, it is required that potential recruits join us on the field of battle. In battle, the true nature of the warrior is revealed and it is the core of the individual we wish to know. Applicants should not expect to share in loot that is attained in any of the raids they attend during the evaluation process.  In the two week period from the notification of your promotion to Applicant status, you are encouraged to attend all raids and make every effort to team with our members. Actively seek Dragon Knights for company so that we come to know each other. In this, rely upon your sponsor to make your introductions. If your sponsor is not available seek out our Recruit Coordinator, Garro. At the end of these two weeks Garro, our Recruit Coordinator, will post a thread with your name in the Review section of our message board. During this time our members will be invited to formally comment on your worthiness to join us. In this arena your past deeds may come to boost your effort or deny it. After another two weeks of taking comments on your suitability, Garro will post your name for a vote.  A single no vote will result in your application being rejected.  Harmony is greatly prized among our guild members.  If your vote goes well, Envino will inform you of your probationary membership in Dragon Knight and you will enter the final evaluation period wearing the guild name above your head. For the next 4 weeks we will get to know you as well as we can. After 4 weeks, if all goes well, you will become a full voting member. Thus, the entire evaluation period should last two months.

Please take heed, it is not an easy path you set out on when you endeavour to join Dragon Knight. Few will succeed. However, the quality of the guild must be maintained. If you are denied acceptance based on your class or level, it may well be that we open our doors to you in times yet to come. For those who choose to endure this process, the rewards will be great and in our midst you will find peace and brotherhood.
 
Sponsors
As a Dragon Knight you know the fabric that binds us and the quality of people who now fight at your side. Through the pain of battle you have learned to trust us, even though death may encroach steadily nearer. Even in the most dire of times we stand as one and endure. We seek the greater good in all things.

Perhaps you find these qualities in another, not of our guild. If you come to trust them as you trust us, if you see the true heart of a Dragon in them - then you may stand proudly at their side as a sponsor. It is the wish of the High Council that you carry the title of Dragon Knight for at least one month before becoming a sponsor and that you only sponsor one person at a time. As a sponsor you are asking us to depend on them as we do you.

In becoming a sponsor you are also accepting additional responsibility. A sponsored applicant likely does not know many of us - so we ask that you introduce them to others. If the applicant is to attend raids or general activities they must know of such things. Please keep them informed and make every effort to include them as appropriate. Ensure that they understand our charter and that any questions they may have are answered. These things we place in your care until the application is decided.
