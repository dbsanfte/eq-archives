Introduction To Portfolio

 

Portfolio is an on-line interactive information management tool designed to give students, faculty, and staff the opportunity to create and distribute their educational records and other supporting documents.  Currently available to all U of M students, faculty, and staff, Portfolio provides a dynamic, efficient mechanism for integrating official as well as self-reported academic, career, and personal information.  It is an on-line, integrated database where a variety of student records, work samples, and planning documents are electronically housed.  Users can easily store, view, and selectively share this information with others anywhere, anytime via the web.

 

Portfolio provides an efficient way to document and reflect upon personal information, educational accomplishments, career goals, skills development, professional practices, and recognition you have received. You can retrieve and view your documents at any time and organize components of it into folders for others at the U of M to access. You can also view folders that other Portfolio owners have created for you.

 

Portfolio can be accessed from any Internet browser at http://portfolio.umn.edu Each user can log into his or her personal Portfolio account by entering his or her U of MN Internet ID (E-mail username) and password.

 

Portfolio has three functions: Enter, Share, and View. 

  • Enter allows you to record and update information and reflections about yourself, attach additional materials, and access your data in the U of M Student Administration System.  
  • Share assists you in creating customized folders of selected information about yourself, previewing folders you plan to share with others at the U of M, and giving others access to folders you want to share.  
  • View facilitates your access to information that others at the U of M have shared with you and allows you to add comments to additional materials they have allowed you to see.

Portfolio Organization

Portfolio is divided into the following six categories in order to ensure that the development of a Portfolio will cover a full range of learning activities:

 

  • Personal Information
  • Education
  • Career
  • Skills
  • Professional Practices
  • Recognition

 

Within each of the six categories there are many data elements (see U of M Portfolio Data Elements).  Most data elements are text fields in which the user is given prompts to enter information.  These are self-reported data elements.  It is up to the individual to complete these accurately.  An advantage of the self-reported data elements is that the user can upload multiple files and make one or more hypertext links to any of the self-reported data elements.

 

Data elements populated with information from the U of M Student Administrative System (SAS) contain current, live information.  See the list below for data elements generated by SAS.

 

The list of self-reported and SAS data elements is not intended to be exhaustive.  For example, in the career section many students may want to include one or more resumes.  To accommodate the need for including a variety of documents, each Portfolio category has a Documentation data element.  This element is a multiple field, and the user can enter as many documents as he or she would like in each section.

Enter

This is your personal file for entering information and reflections and for attaching additional materials.  Enter allows you to enter your information by category.  There are six categories, each of which contains a number of subcategories.

 

Information is entered by subcategory.  You may access subcategories in any order. Each time you finish entering information in a subcategory, click the Save button to save the additions or changes you have made to your Portfolio.  Some subcategories, such as Registration Record display data from the U of M Student Administrative System, and other categories, such as Academic Plan, display self-reported information.

 

Below is an example demonstrating how to put information into an element in portfolio and attach a file.

 

Enter Portfolio at http://portfolio.umn.edu  You will be asked to log in as a U of M user or a Guest. 

 

 

 

You will need to log into Portfolio using your U of M (x.500) username and password.

 

 

If you need assistance logging in, call your campus Help Desk or ADCS at 612-301-4357.

 

 

 

Once you have logged in to Portfolio you will see your name at the top of the screen.

 

 

 

 

Once you click on Enter you will have six categories where you can enter information.

 

 

 

Expand one of the categories to see sub-categories (elements) where data can be entered.

 

 

Open one of the sub-categories and enter information.  When you are done entering information, click Save at the bottom of the page.

 

 

Uploading Files

You can upload files to your Portfolio elements (sub-categories) by using the Material Manager function.   To do this go the green Material Manager box on one of the elements and click on the upload button.

 

 

 

Once you have clicked on the upload button you will be prompted to browse for the file you would like to upload. 

 

After you have found the file you would like to upload, click open.

 

Once it is opened, the file will automatically be placed in the box next to the word browse.  This step allows Portfolio to access the file for uploading.  Once the file is in the box, click upload to finish uploading the file to your Portfolio.

 

 

When a file has been attached (uploaded) to an element, it will show up in the Additional Materials box.

 

 

Each user has 20MB of space for there data.  This can get used up quickly when large files are uploaded to Portfolio.  To save space in some cases it may be a better idea to make the files available on the internet somewhere and then attach the URL to the file to the element.

Uploading a URL is a very similar process to uploading a file.  Using the URL button will allow you to enter information about the URL you want to attach.

The Material Manager

The Material Manager provides the ability to view and manage the files and URLs in your Portfolio on the same page.  The Material Manager is accessible through the green box in the Enter section of portfolio.

 

Some of the functions available through the material manager include deleting uploaded material from portfolio, attaching existing materials to new or existing elements, uploading new material with or without attaching them to an element, and seeing where the different upload materials are attached.

 

Share

This Portfolio function allows you to create and preview customized folders that offer different views of the information, reflections, and additional materials you have stored in Enter.  It also lets you specify who can view these folders.  If you want to view a folder you have created, you must give yourself access in Share.

 

The customized Portfolio folder can provide a three-dimensional view of you to others with whom you choose to share the information, thereby providing them with a wider range and depth of information about you and your skills.  It can also be used to distribute information to several people to facilitate working together.

 

To use Share, create a new folder or select an existing one. Select from the menu of subcategories in which you have entered information and reflections or attached additional materials. Determine the information to be included in the customized folder. When prompted, type in the user names of individuals to whom you want to give permission to access the folder.

 

To share information with other individuals, first click on the Share tab or click on Share from the Welcome page.  Then click on create.

 

 

Clicking on the Create button takes you to the page where you will name the folder you are going to share and where you will identify with whom the information will be shared.

 

 

After you have named the folder, identified who the folder will be shared with, added them to the list, and created a brief description of the folder, click Next. 

 

 

After you click Next, you will see a list of all of the elements (sub-categories) that are available for you to share.  By clicking the box next to the element, you are indicating that this piece of information will be included in the shared folder.  After clicking on all of the elements you would like to have included in this folder, click Save at the bottom of the page.  The users you indicated now have access to view the information you just shared with them.

 

 

An email notification will be sent to each individual to whom you granted access to the shared folder.

 

 

If you have shared the folder to someone outside the University by entering their email address for the first time, The email they receive will also contain instructions and a username and password for accessing portfolio.

 

View

 

View lists folders to which other Portfolio users have given you access.  Click on individual folders to open them.  Clicking on subcategories within each folder will display entered information and reflections, open additional materials attached to subcategories (using existing software on your hard drive), or download the additional materials to your desktop.

 

Inside the View function, a specialized Portfolio folder, the Academic Profile, containing only the U of M system information is made available to assigned U of M advisors.  It is the prerogative of the student to share additional information with his/her assigned advisor. 

 

To view folders that others have shared with you, first click on the View tab or on View from the Welcome page and then click on the underlined folder name.

Student Administrative System (SAS)

 

All records displayed in the Academic Profile of Portfolio are from the University of Minnesotas Student Administrative System (SAS).  This is a centralized PeopleSoft Campus Community database, housing student and human resource records.  The display of these records in the Academic Profile is dynamic; current SAS records are shown in the Academic Profile.

 

If there are errors in these records, people assigned to work with U of M system records should be contacted.  Most departments and collegiate units have one or more people assigned to work with these records.  Some SAS demographic information may be viewed and/or updated on the web at University of Minnesota Personal Information

(https://onestop.umn.edu/personalprofile/staff/nameInformation/viewNameInformation.shtml).

It is also possible to use this web site to suppress personal information.

 

Suppression of Records

 

Any student member of the U of M community can request that his or her public information is suppressed.  This means that according to federal and state law and Regents policy regarding private student information, advisors and other U of M employees may not release any information about this student to a third party.

 

It is possible to suppress records in one of two ways.  You may suppress address and phone or you may suppress all personal information.  The U of M Personal Information website above allows for either of these suppressions.

 

A request to suppress student records shows up in the Academic Profile in two ways.

 

After clicking on the name of a student whos records have been suppressed, a suppression screen will appear.  In order to see the advisees records you must read and click that you accept the conditions of student data privacy.

 

 

 

 

At the top of each page of this advisees Academic Profile you will see a suppression statement, which reads:  This student has suppressed his or her information. 

 

 

 

 

Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)

 

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) of 1974, also known as the Buckley Amendment, helps protect the privacy of student records.  FERPA provides for the right to inspect and review education records, the right to seek to amend those records, and the right to limit disclosure of information from the records.  The intent of the legislation is to protect the rights of students and to ensure the privacy and accuracy of records.  FERPA applies to all institutions that receive federal funding.

 

The Office of the Registrar has published a concise document summarizing FERPA regulations as they apply to the University of Minnesota.  For a copy of this brochure contact Tina Falkner at rovic001@umn.edu.  Also, the Universitys official policy on student records can be accessed at http://www1.umn.edu/regents/policies/administrative/StudentRecords.pdf

 

All faculty and staff should understand FERPA and the impact it has on student records.  Many colleges require advising staff, professional and faculty, to annually sign a FERPA agreement.  Please check with your college office regarding this form. 

 

Why should students build customized Portfolio folders for advisement?

One of the unique strengths of Portfolio is that it provides one mechanism for sharing system (SAS) and self-reported information anywhere, any time.  This feature can be used to great advantage in advisement because students can share integrated information otherwise unavailable to their advisor.  For instance, a student can write out a four-year academic plan using the Academic Plan template in Portfolio.  The student can then attach URLs for the catalog description of the major/minor degree requirements as well as class schedules.  Other self-reported documents can include career plans, autobiographical statements, photographs, assessment scores, resumes, co-curricular activities, and academic work samples.  In addition to official web pages, such as student affairs offices and policies, students can upload documents such as degree audit reports (APAS) to their Portfolios for sharing.

 

These, and other self-reported documents, can be shared with an advisor only by using a customized Portfolio folder.  This is a custom-built folder created for an advisor.  It contains elements the student wants his or her advisor to see, and it can contain any number of SAS and self-reported elements.

 

The customized Portfolio folder can provide a three-dimensional view of the student to the advisor, thereby providing an advisor with wider range and depth of information for making recommendations.
