It's stories like what Lum just posted that make me absolutely sick. GM's should NEVER, ABSOLUTELY NEVER, have the power to destroy items like that at whim.

I recall a UOHoC conference a while back, we were pestering DD in the moderation channel about giving players the ability to destroy items. The entire dev team was trying to find ways to eliminate the amount of "stuff" in the game and we (those of us in the channel and not working for OSI) felt that a "delete" key would be the right move.

You know what DD said? I don't think I can remember the EXACT words, but it went something like this: 'I've tried/seen abilities like that in other games, and I must say that I don't like it. It's one thing when someone kills you and loots you, it's quite another when they taunt you over your corpse by deleting each and every item on you.'

DD was denouncing the destruction of in-game personal property as a criminal act, literally likening it to the worst forms of anti-social behavior possible. And here we have GM's who simply click and destroy people's stuff, and throw out the cop-out of "My boss told me too."

This, frankly, is bullshit. I've seen A LOT of people's unique items destroyed. I've watched this policy of no hued-items play itself out from conception and I can tell you that nothing in the game, save the bug-exploiter, has been worse for the game.

Why was this policy implemented? Simple. GM's were getting too many "service calls" from newbies who were being lured to their death by players who were clad in special clothing/armor/weapons. Should that situation ever exist on Siege Perilous.... FUCK NO.

I'd like to continue, but words are really failing me right now. This is a classic example of a system with it's head up it's ass. In fact, I'd like to award my own special, Heads-up-ass award to any GM who supports this policy, and especially to the GM who did that to you Lum...
