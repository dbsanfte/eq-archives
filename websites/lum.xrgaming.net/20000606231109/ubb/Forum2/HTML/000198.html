Well, I used to work for an ISP which used Exodus as a peer provider. Basically, they have the fuckiest internal routing I've ever seen, all kinds of looping routes.

At best, this adds HUGE amounts of transit time to the packets. At worst, the packets become looped and you have no connection at all. They also are very lossy for some reason [I suspect network hardware overload is the culprit]; I never clearly could get this out of them. But Exodus is one of the worst co-location deals I've seen and working with ISP's I've seen a few.

Supposedly Exodus has POP in dallas, I don't know why the hell they'd be routing you to china and back but a lot of that has to do with where your ISP and their ISP meet. If it takes your provider 6 hops to get to the top level to exchange with another network there is nothing that exodus could do about that and that's what a lot of problems are caused from so watch for that.

That still does not explain why I'm obtaining 67 ms of lag from within Exodus' OWN network [I'm getting about 20 ms up to the alter.net exchange, which isn't too bad, if I was getting 20-30ms more going through Exodus' network I'd not complain 60 ms is acceptable ping time for UO]. I've got like 14 hops 7 of which are producing 30ms, why is exodus producing 67 ms of lag? If UUNET can do it why can't exodus? :]

If OSI had UUNET as a provider, my connect would be in the 40-50ms range.. damn I wish.. :]
