Krazi -

I'm not OSI - but I can tell you a couple things about your letter.

Housing is a mess, always has been a mess, and quite likely - always will be a mess. But, that said - It's SO much better than it used to be.

We ran a little site back when housing had yet to be addressed - many good ideas got kicked around.

One was 'off the map' housing. Some kind of place where even if it was to YOUR own house, you could go that wouldn't clutter up the rest of Britannia. Hell, I don't care, call them suburbs. I was personally told at that time (at a luncheon) that this COULD NOT be done do to the infrastructure of the game. Course,at the exact same time, I was told the exact same thing about contents not downloading to you for every house you passed until you went over the steps. As you probably know, this WAS done, and quite well, and helped with lag considerably.

This is not a new concept at all, a lot of text based RPG's have private areas for each user that are basically not in traveled areas, i.e., 'off the map' so to speak.

As to new players having a harder time - they will ALWAYS have to catch up. Don't look at UO as a race, or a competition, look at it as a virtual framework in which to live. Does a child have an unfair burden because it's parents were born 20 years before he was? It's much the same difference. UO is a PERSISTENT world. Elder players having more, being able to do more - evens out (well except for houses as you noted) once a character gets their skills up.
Some people look at hitting GM in something as the 'end'. I look at it as the beginning of my adult life - at THAT point, you have matured your character to the point in something where you are a fullfledged adult in the community.

Already on Seige Perilous - there are a few GMs - One I know in particular is a smith. This took a coordinated effort between over 20 people to get to this point in that amount of time....but guess what, some people are screaming their heads off it's somehow 'not fair'.

Fair isn't what you can accomplish thru cooperation or hard work being more than what someone ELSE is willing to invest. Fair is them having the same opportunities to do the same exact thing.
