A time machine appears before you. It's controls are set for the year 1996. The place? Austin TX. Mr. Peabody wants to take you to start of the Ultima Online project.

With all you now know, what are you going tell RG about how to make UO?

The dev team thinks the time machine is really cool. They'll implement anything you think will be a good idea.

Your mission? Give them a grocery list of things that will make UO a better game in the year 1999.

Here's my list;

A) Raph is allready warning folks that people in a virtual world are assholes. I need to impress upon them that the problem will be worse than they suspect. Much, much worse. I will tell them that they *must* start off with a zero tolerance policy twoard the idiots. Dupers? No leanancy, no amnesty. Give 'em the boot....and their little dog toto too. Yeah, they'll lose that 10 bucks a month, but it'll keep two people from quiting, and convince two more people that the game is worth buying. That's a net profit in my book.

B) Take the people responsible for the skill system and give them a good beating. I'll then pour water on them, get on on thier feet and give them another savage beating for good measure. Then I'll discuss RoT, and clue them in to the notion that people do not like having to click on the same thing a million times....and those that do are not the kind of people fit for polite society.

C) OSI will be putting out wantads thanks to me. They'll be looking for storytellers, who will be responsible for creating involving storylines and quests. These story tellers will be empowered to make changes in the landscape. The game will still involve baking bread, but damn it, there *will* be adventures for all who wish them.

D) GMs will be hired from the ranks of grumpy men who sit on park benches in austin throwing things at the squirrels. Most of them will be ex-cops with a real bad attitude. We will never be friendly with them, but at least we know they will be fair and not easily corruptable. The kids who were hired in the "real" timeline will be shipped from school to one of Kathy Lee Giffords sweat shops instead of the comfy GM jobs they now hold.

E) The early days of UO were a blood bath. It's easy to look fondly upon them as the "good old days of the dreads" but they were only good days for the dreads doing the killling. The bloody first days are what still fuels the PK debate, and what led to the attitude of the general public that UO is a 2-d quake. PKing is a needed element of the game, but it needed to be toned down. Thanks to the time machine, I'll be able to do that. Perhapos with the proper foundation set, stat loss would eventually be able to be phased out. (point A tells me otherwise, but I'm an optomist!)

F) UO will eventually make 1.5 million a month. Take an extra few months to improve the infrastructure. If you have to, push it back to Q1 1998. UO has allways recieved bad press instead of the "journalistic" blowjob that EQ gets. Let's fix that now.

G) Lord British must die. If someone isn't going to activly play Lord British, than a new king must be found. Someone who won't become a running joke. An Ultima game has a king who's part of the story...not one who does his "where's waldo" immitation.

H) Did I mention a zero tolerance policy on cheats and assholes? Good. I need to say it again.

I) One more time won't hurt. Kick the fuckwads out NOW before they become a problem.


That's my list. I now turn the time machine over to you good folks at Lums. Go nuts guys and gals....and racoons.

------------------
The Great Bob
