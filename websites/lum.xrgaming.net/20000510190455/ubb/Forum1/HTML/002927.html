The news of the layoffs in Austin today were a bit juicy, but can anyone honestly say they were a suprise? Origin, by the actions they've displayed over the past two years, had shown a complete change in focus.

No longer is this the same company that once produced the great Ultima series (through U:7 part 2), or the Wing Commander series or any of the host of independent games that they did (System Shock, UW's, Bioforge, etc.) Instead the company is now being run by the accountants at EA.

A single player game can not, and will never be able to make the amount of money a game that runs off of subscriptions can. The reasons are obvious, but looking past that for a moment, can anyone honestly say that Ultima Online is a BETTER game then Ultima's 5 or 7???

What I'm getting at is although the recent layoffs and change in focus over at OSI are good buisness decisions - are they beneficial to those of us that enjoyed the Origin games of old?

The answer is actually yes. By casting away all of the talent that originally made Origin a name-brand, they have freed those former Origin employees to use their experience gained while working at OSI to start their own companies and continue their single player stories there.

Warren Spector (System Shock, Ultima Underworlds & Crusader) has recently joined Ion Storm in producing what will be the immersive game of 2000: Deus Ex.

Doug Church (Also of System Shock & Ultima Underworlds) is now at Looking Glass, which had released a sequel to System Shock last year and just recently released Thief 2.

And Finally,

Chris Roberts (Wing Commander's I-IV) & Erin Roberts (Privateer 1 & 2) started up Digital Anvil and are just about ready to release their first two games in what promises to be another grand series - Starlancer & Freelancer.

Should I be sad for the days when an Origin game held the promise of a good plot? In a nostalgic sort of way we probably all will be, but the talent is still out there - its just not at OSI anymore.
