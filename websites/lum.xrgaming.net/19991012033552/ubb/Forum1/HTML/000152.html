I would concur on the statements on Cable Modems. Cable Modem's work much like LAN's; the more packets flying around your local loop, the more lag you get. If some twink is running a Quake server from his parents computer - expect to see your ping rates go up. But - I still stand by my statement: Path&Hops = LAG, not how fast you can ping your ISP. We ALL must suffer with GLOBALCENTER. One thing I have noticed with RoadRunner; large file download's are exceptionally fast. I downloaded 23MB's worth of IE5 this week; it took around 10 minutes. It's great for downloading .jpg's as well. That 20MB+ Ultima9 walk through? I sat there and watched it tick away - very fast.

Installers: Well, Oceanic has an odd system of installing Road Runner. First, they send out a guy from Digital Equipment Corp (now Compaq) to install your NIC card; a ISA 10baseT Ethernet adaptor. Next day, The "Cable Guy" brings out your Motorola CyberSurfer (I think) Cable Modem; runs the coax lines, and installs the sofware on your computer. I already had a NIC card installed, so I saved $50 and skipped the friendly guy from DEC. The Cable Guy seemed competent.

Tech Support: TimeWarner, the Road Runner peps', run their tech support out of two big Network Operation Centers; one in Dallas (I think) and another somewhere else. These guys are generally knowledgeable; and there open 24/7...
