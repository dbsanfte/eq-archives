VERANT LOVES YOU *BUMPFUZZLE*
Sunday, June 18, 2000 - 9:58 AM [Filed by lum]

This is a long post, but mainly because I'm "enshrining for posterity" some postings which tend to mysteriously disappear. Deal.

Some interesting posts on Whineplay this weekend - apparently a guide in EQ named "rpgadvocate" finally had enough, and has been coredumping all over the message base. Abashi asked him Friday to email him privately, apparently to ask him what was what. His response, which he made public, early Saturday morning:

What follows is the portion of my response to the email Abashi sent me, basically asking WHY I believe GM abuse occurs on a regular basis.

Keep in mind that while the anecdotes cited within are real. My take on their sevrity is just my opinion. I would request that if you have a rebuttal, do so in a non-inflamitory manner. Intelligent discussion is of the utmost importance.

I. THE CUSTOMER IS ALWAYS WRONG: ITEM REIMBURSAL SHINANIGANS

An abuse in this area is what really set me off and made me decide to begin my "stop GM abuse" campaign when I left the Guide program, and to a more limited extent, in the program. A level 40ish character (I forget the details. It's been eight months) petitioned that he had lost ALL of his items to a bug. Since Vyrinn wasn't scheduled to be on for a couple of days, I told him to email Vyrinn (apparently, this was against policy. More on this later) and that he would get back to him ASAP. One week later, the guy petitions again, saying that Vyrinn never contacted him back. Next time Vyrinn got on, I asked him to check the logs RIGHT NOW. Well guess what? The logs only go back one week, and Vyrinn decalred that no verification was possible since the logs could not be checked. In this situation, Vyrinn should have reimbursed the items, but failed to do so. Why? Because the default attitude is that a player is scamming for a reimbursal. This character had SIXTY DAYS /played! Talk about stepping on someone's emotional investment! All done in the name of "game integrity!" I vowed that such a thing would NEVER happen again on my watch.

II: RETURN TO SENDER - HOW VERANT LOVES TO IGNORE EMAIL

How does GM abuse result from Verant's email network? Well, GM (and Guides) often redirect valid requests to various Verant email, when they know full well that those emails won't be answered. You wouldn't believe how many times I received a petition regarding how a guide or GM told him or her to email one of the following email addresses, only to receive an autoresponse, NEVER followed up by a response from a real person. Those emails follow:

questhelp@verant.com
eqmail@verant.com
eqcs@verant.com

Furthermore, GMs abuse players by making it MORE DIFFICULT to get redress for problems, either real or perceived. Policy, at least on Tarew Marr, was that Bob Painter's (Vyrinn's) email was NOT to be given out except by Vyrinn himself. Instead, we were to redirect to one of the emails above if the issue needed further examination and a GM was not present.

What are the practical consequences of this setup? The most noticable is that players who have completed quests, but not received the appropriate items often have to wait a long time to get their items, if they get them at all. If you ask a Guide or GM for help, you just get redirected again.

III: WHAT YOU DON'T KNOW *CAN* HURT YOU, AND THE PLAYERS!

This is one that I experienced directly a few times as a Guide. Because Guides don't have access to timely information about game bugs and enahcnements, they will often abuse players without even KNOWING it, not to mention the hundreds of times when guides have to admit that they don't know something that they SHOULD know. The most glaring example of this kind of behvaior involves a bug with an NPC named "scraps" in East Freeport. If scraps was aggroed for any reason, he would not "un-agro" correctly, causing players uninvolved in the inital agro to be killed. For WEEKS guides didn't know this was a bug. At least 2 people a day were killed by this bug, and guides could do NOTHING about it! Terrible!

Interesting to note is that Verant says the onus is on the player to prove a bug death, but players have few tools to prove such deaths.

While the guides weren't abusing consciously in this situation, it is still abuse, as it denied players resurrections to which they were entitiled.

IV: YOU HAVE BEEN SCREWED BY THE GODS! - HOW PLANES POLICIES PROMOTE GM ABUSE

About midway through my Guide career, it was declared that Guides were not to perform resurrections or CR on the planes (and now I assume Veehsan's Peak) for "some reason" (more on this later). Again, the practical effects of this were particularly glaring in one routine case that because of the rules, forced me to abuse a player. A player claimed he had fallen for 10K in fearplane. Okay, that's common enough. I zone into fear, and, sure enough, the corpse is at the safe coords. I tell him I verified the bug, but that I couldn't help until I got a GM's permission. With the resurrection time limit in force, I couldn't guarantee a resurrection in the time alloted. At first, I followed the policy, for about an hour, but after repeated tells from him and his friends, I relented and gave the resurrection, thinking that anyone would think that player satisfaction is more important than policy. Too bad Vyrinn found out... I got a stern reprimand for that one. Score one for the policy-bots.

V: ............................. - ABUSE BY SILENCE


Policy encourages Guides and GMs to be short with customers, oftentimes leaving them feeling like their issue is of little importance to the GM or guide answering the petition. I remember a thread posted on the board about how GM Vlashire failed to give a player a erfectly reasonable surname that the /surname command wouldn't allow. Vlashire wouldn't explain WHY this was the case, and would not respond to tells. There are too many reports of such behavior to beleve they are all lies. Besdies, it's IN THE POLICY! Why do I believe the Vlashire story? Because it was backed up by several screenshots and because Vlashire subsitiute-GM'ed on TM for a couple days, and he seemed very gruff and strict.

VI: YOU WANT THE TRUTH?! YOU CAN'T HANDLE THE TRUTH! - INTERNAL ARROGANCE

Another problem that promotes GM abuse is somewhat related to #3 above. Questioning superiors about what's going on, so that one can better serve the player population is often met with a rebuke or stony silence. Remember the planes policy stuff above? Well, myself and a couple of guides asked GM Lokkison WHY this was implemented, and Lokkison's response was, in essence "Because we said so. Isn't that enough for you?" No it is NOT enough, because players want to know what's up too. Failing to give resurrections for such vaccuous reasons as "GM Lokkison said so" is abusive in the extreme.

Luckily, GM Viik was kind enough to tell some of us what was up - a week and a half AFTER implementation.

Such arrogance with regard to asking tough questions INCREASES GM abuse overall and should be discontinued immediately.
