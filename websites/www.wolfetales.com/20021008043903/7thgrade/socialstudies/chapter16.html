CHAPTER 16       THE CIVIL WAR       1861-1865

 

The Beginning of the Civil War

The Civil War began at Fort Sumter in Charleston, South Carolina harbor in April of 1861.  The South fired the first shot on the American flag.  The action unified the North

 

 

Choosing Sides

Choosing sides was difficult. 

 

Some men left the Union army because they came from the South.  They were loyal to their home states.

 

Many Northern men enlisted because the South had fired on Union troops.

 

The Civil War was fought among brothers and friends, fathers and sons.  The decision to support the South might mean you would fight against a friend or relative on the Union side.  The same was true if you supported the North.

 

States Choose Sides 

When the war broke out only 7 of the 15 slave states had seceded or left the Union.

 Others left when President Lincoln called up troops for the Union Army.

 

Virginia was split in two.  When Virginia voted to secede people in the western part of the state separated and formed the New State of West Virginia.  It remains West Virginia to this day.

 

The map on page 442 of your text shows the new state of West Virginia.

 

Union and Confederate Resources 

Both sides thought the war would end quickly.  Both sides were wrong.

Both were equally determined and loyal to their beliefs.

 

The Union  Strengths 

More people available to fight and work.

 

More raw material and factories for production

 

More Money in the bank to invest in the war effort.

 

More miles of railroad tracks… Made moving troops easier.

 

Large Navy that could blockade Southern ports and keep the South from getting supplies from Europe.

 

            Confederate Strengths

            Geographic advantage of fighting on its own soil. Familiar land features.

            Friendly people in the area to feed them and hide them from Northern soldiers.

           

            A history of long military tradition. They new how to be soldiers.

           

Major supplier of raw cotton to the textile mills in Europe  (thought Europe would come help them because of King Cotton.)

 

They truly believed in their right to form their own country and their right to separate from the Union.

 

 

Differences Between Lincoln and Davis

 

            Lincoln   Abraham Lincoln.  President of the United States

            Little political or military experience.

            Patient and thoughtful, he learned as he went.

            He was good with people

            Chose strong men to for his cabinet who would help him.

            He was quick to make decisions.

 

            Davis Jefferson Davis  President of the Confederate states

            Public service experience and hardworking

            Disagreed with Generals and advisers.

Didn’t pay attention to other important problems in the South

Was not a good leader when it came to economics. (food shortages- supply shortages for his troops)

 

The War Takes Shape

The South’s plan was mainly defensive.  Keep the Union from taking their lands.They would also try to break the Union blockade.

 

The North had a 3 part plan

One – Continue to try and capture the Confederate capital  of Richmond Virginia

 

Second – Gain control of the Mississippi and Tennessee river valleys in order to divide the south.

 

Third – Union ships would blockade the Southern coastline to prevent goods from leaving or entering.

 

First Battle of Bull Run  (South Won)

This battle was fought close to Washington DC.  The Union thought the Confederates would lose the battle but General Thomas J Jackson came with reinforcements and they refused to give in.  He and his troops stood like a stone wall against the Union.  (Stonewall Jackson)

 

North retreated back to Washington DC…

The South did not follow them.  Instead they stopped to celebrate their victory.

Outcome might have been different if they had followed them to washing ton and taken the city.  The war might have ended right there and the South would have been the winner.

 

 

Two Northern Victories

General Ulysses S. Grant Captured two Confederate forts in Tennessee.

 

Fort Henry and Fort Donelson. He took 14,000 prisoners at Fort Donelson.

Confederates withdrew from Kentucky and middle Tennessee.

 

 

Shiloh Tennessee – The Confederates retreated when Northern reinforcements came so the North claimed the victory.

 

At Shiloh - 13,000 Union troops died… 11,000 Confederate troops died

 

Union leaders wanted Grant removed because of all the deaths but Lincoln supported him.

 

Confederacy and Foreign Support

King Cotton Diplomacy – Europe wanted the cotton produced in the South. 

South thought they would receive aid and support from England and France..

New markets opened and Europe was able to get its cotton from India and Egypt.

France would not help the South because England did not.

 

Britain was only willing to give support if the Confederacy was winning battles.

 

Robert E Lee – was a Southern General… He was against slavery and secession… Lincoln offered him a position in the Union army but he was a loyal Southerner.

Defeated the North at the Second Battle of Bull Run.

 

Battle of Antietam Neither side won.
