The Growing Crisis

 

 

Sutter’s Mill

 

--Gold was discovered at Sutter’s Mill in California in 1848.

 

--By 1849 about 80,000 people had come looking for gold.  They were called the FORTY-NINERS.

 

--Important because it gave California enough people to be eligible for statehood.

 

The FREE – SOIL MOVEMENT

 

Wilmot Proviso (A proviso is condition in the form of an amendment.)

The Wilmot Proviso was a bill to create a fund for buying land from Mexico.  The Wilmot Proviso would have outlawed slavery in any land we got from Mexico.

 

All Northern Senators but two voted…

All Southern Senators but two voted…

 

--The bill was defeated but… the idea of outlawing slavery in lands we got from Mexico went on.

 

FREE SOILERS –People against the spread of slavery.  ( Some thought that having slaves meant that jobs would be taken from white workers –competition from slave labor)

 

Popular Sovereignty  Sometimes called squatter sovereignty – was proposed as a way to settle the question.  It would have allowed the voters in an area to decide if slavery would be permitted.

 

California applied for statehood and had the right to decide if it would be a free state or a slave state.  It applied to be a free state.

 

Balance is upset

Up until now there was a balance of 15 free states and 15 slave states.  When California elected to be a free state the balance was upset.  The voting power of the free states would be greater than that of the slave states.

 

Compromise of 1850   to settle the issue (4 main parts)

--California became a free state (North happy)

--territories gained from Mexico would be free to decide by popular sovereignty (South  happy

--Slave trade was banned in Washington DC  (North happy)

--Stricter Fugitive Slave law –required all states to help return runaways (slave or free                                 state)

 

Resisting the Fugitive Slave Law

--Judges got 10 dollars for deciding an accused runaway was a slave

--Only 5 if they set the person free

--It denied the accused slave a trial by jury. (They couldn’t tell their side of the story.)

 

The law was unpopular and so it became impossible to enforce in the North.

 

--Personal Liberty Laws were passed in Vermont that helped slaves get lawyers  and released them from local jails.

 

Harriet Beecher Stowe – Wrote Uncle Tom’s Cabin -  She became famous in the North and hated in the South.  She wrote the book to show that slavery harmed everyone, black and white, North or South.  It showed the suffering slavery caused.

 

“With one wild cry…she vaulted…onto the raft of ice beyond… she leaped to another and another cake…. Her shoes are gone… blood marked every step…till dimly…she saw the Ohio side, and a man helping her up the bank.”  Harriet Beecher Stowe  …from Uncle Tom’s Cabin

 

Whigs and the Democrats – were the two major parties in 1852 election

Franklin Pierce (Democrat)  won

 

Steven Douglas – From Illinois – Douglas brought a bill to Congress to organize the Nebraska-Kansas territories.  He wanted to build a railroad that would begin in Chicago Illinois.  It would run through Kansas and end in California.

 

The Kansas-Nebraska Act gave something to the South.  It meant that the states formed from these territories would decide by popular sovereignty to be slave or free.  Before the act, they would have formed into free states because of their location.

 

People on both sides of the slavery issue rushed into Kansas to vote because they wanted to influence the outcome of whether or not Kansas would be a free state or a slave state.  They were not residents but they voted anyway.

 

Election of 1860  Abraham Lincoln becomes president.

Lincoln’s election had an immediate effect.  South Carolina and six other states seceded from the Union and formed their own union. 

 

Representatives from these states met and formed the Confederate States of America.  The wrote their own Constitution.   It guaranteed the right to own slaves. 

 

Jefferson Davis became the Confederate President.

 

Fort Sumter  - Charleston Harbor – Only important military post the United States had in confederate territory.  South Fired on Fort Sumter on April 12, 1861.   Lincoln called for volunteers to put down the rebellion.  The CIVIL WAR had begun.

 

 

 

Chronology of Events

 

Congress passes the Kansas-Nebraska Act in 1854.

 

Proslavery forces attack Lawrence Kansas in 1856  (Bloody Kansas)

 

The Supreme Court delivers the Dred Scott decision in 1857

Dred was a slave who lived with his owner for 5 years in a free area.  When his owner died he was encouraged to take his case to court.  The supreme court ruled that no black, slave or free, could be a citizen of the United States.  Thus a black could not sue in federal court.  Further the decision defined slaves as property and owners could take them anywhere without restriction.

 

John Brown leads antislavery forces in a raid on Harpers Ferry in 1859

 

South Carolina secedes from the Union in 1860
