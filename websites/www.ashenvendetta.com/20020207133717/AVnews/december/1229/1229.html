Here is a rather long personal retrospective.

Looking back to where Ashen Vendetta was a year ago, I realize not only how far we have come (in terms of teamwork and success), but how far the game has progressed. For better or worse, Everquest continues to be a big part of all our members lives (sometimes to detriment to school or work!) and we can all agree it has been one of our favorite hobbies since its inception.

This year we continued to build on what started as nothing more than a few friends and an idea of fun first; after all that is why so many of us continue to log in. This group continued to grow, and eventually became not only friends, but veterans of the high level game. Let me reflect on some of our highlights.

In December of last year we were still killing Trakanon fairly regularly, working towards entering Veeshans Peak. Velious was out but we stayed with our goal, even if other folks on the server were getting somewhat better equipment for much less risk. In March, just one month after entering the zone, we killed every dragon there.

In hindsight I'm glad we stuck to our goal; people do not get remembered for pharming the first set of PoG armor, but I can assure you my fellow guildmates will never forget killing Phara Dar for the first time. It was more sweet, not for being the first (there are many guilds out there that have been first more times), but for rewarding us all for the hard work and sacrifice. Also, watching all those foolish naysayers eat their collective words. To this day, only 2 guilds on Innoruuk have cleared this zone. Just 2.

In the months that followed, realization was that we had only really scratched the surface of Velious. Most of the encounters were much different than that quick bloody battles of Kunark, these fights required the now infamous main tank. Mobs were given huge huge amounts of hitpoints. The officers and guildmembers hashed out strategies on the fly and we got to work. We also recruited some very good people, that are still with us today. A consequence of super SoV encounters; one that has added greatly to our guild.

Compared to the other top guilds on the server, we were not as well equipped, or uber mobs slain; what we did not lack was determination and teamwork. Many folks said we could not handle the beasts of Velious. Those creatures started to drop like flies at our hands. The summertime began with mobs like Dain, Vindicator, and all of the West Wing Temple of Veeshan dragons.

Also, who can forget the infamous Yelinak slayings of early summer. AV and our friends The Pantheon were mocked and what not, for two guilds that often before fought and raced against each other for mobs, working together to do something big. Everyone knew the mob (Yelinak at the time was nothing like he is now) required less people, but almost everyone from both guilds were excited about working together. Some people, in both guilds did not like this idea. That is why it was something unique, something I will never forget.

Still there were some that continued to use every opportunity to try and discredit us, many of them at one time were friends that had moved onto other guilds. We did joint raids a few more times, but two guilds with much different styles of combat and leadership just didn't work well in an encounter sense, but we all appreciate the fun and friendships forged those days.

Since then we have killed Yelinak with just our guild more times than I can remember. The dragon has been upgraded time and time again; and still he fell. Today, Yelinak is the hardest dragon known (besides the unkillable Sleeper or Jaled) in my opinion, much tougher than Vyemm. With only 43 people, last night we dropped Lord Yelinak with zero casualties.

The end of the summer found us finally gathering enough sleeper tomb keys to finally enter and face the warders. Those key gathering days were hectic, and quite monotonous; we had no luxury of the old 24 hour Klandicar and Zlandicar spawns. We fought over those two dragons frequently with other guilds. Then Klandicar was upgraded, and we still beat his ass into the dirt. What is most enjoyable is that AV got most of its keys for much harder foes; Sontalak, Lendinaria, and Yelinak. We fought hard for every key.

We knew that eventually the Sleeper would be freed. It was important for us to take part in such a server wide event, do tackle Ventani first. The time had come, once again, to reap the rewards and thrills of doing something epic, to see those hard many months of gathering sleeper tomb keys pay off.

Unfortunately, we had decided on a whim to kill him, not really thinking about a strategy ahead of time. It took three fights to realize that Ventanni was not quite the monster we thought before. We had a plan, a plan that we executed flawlessly. The excitement and pride was palpable, every guildmate both there and supporting through guildchat (those not lucky enough to have a key yet) added something. With the dragon dead at our feet, we watched with delight as The Sleeper tore through our ranks and continue on to other zones.

Many say it was because of the new patch that allowed him to be slowed that we won. Our clerics had quite a lot of mana at the end of the fight. Ventanni wasn't slowed until he was at 45% health or so. Only a handful of people died. It truly was one of the most epic battles I have ever been apart of in EQ, and one that any witnessed would not soon forget.

The end of the year was mainly killing in The North Temple of Veeshan, the SoV faction leaders, and trying out strategies on the ultra mobs (AoW, Tunare). The release of SoL has thrown us a curve, as I'm sure it has for many other guilds, but I look forward to the new challenges this expansion holds for us.

I'm very grateful to be apart of Ashen Vendetta, part of a solid group of friends and seeing so many great victories. As Everquest continues to evolve, we will see many good people come and go. As long as the game holds something special for all of us, may we continue to see goals met, and new boundaries crossed in 2002.

Best wishes for the new year to all of my fellow guild brothers and sisters, and to those of you who stop by and read all of my silly ramblings!

-Irongut
