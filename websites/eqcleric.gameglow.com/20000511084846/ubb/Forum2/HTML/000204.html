I never knew how good the spell flash of light was. I am kicking myself for not discovering it until 33. This has got to be the most overlooked useful cleric spell out there.

When soloing hard hitting mobs and blasting sometimes I would have problems with root sticking or breaking and getting the recast off under the mob's beating.

I am a big stun fan and really utilize them.
Normally I would stun, back up and root.

Well my divs only around 30 at level 33 but it works like a champ.

1) It has a very fast cast (like stun)
2) It is very low mana (like stun)
3) the effect lasts a lot longer then stun

here's what I've been doing:
1) enstill or root
2) wrath
3) flash of light (while wrath recycles)
After that I just keep blasting. I will cast FOL again when it wears off. If root breaks and mob is still blind he'll run around in circles so I have time to root again. If somehow the mob actually gets to me and starts beating on me then I'll FOL and step back a few feet and root easily or stun/FOL/step back/root.

Normally I would end solo fights with 2 bubs of health. Since inserting FOL I rarely lose even 1 bub!!

Also, last night I was jumped by 2 high blue mobs that I had no way in hell to beat. I flashed both of them and had about a 15 second head start running to the zone. Left them far behind.

FOL would be great when grouping with enchanters if they know you are going to be using it. Something gets on enchanter, just FOL, enchanter step back and mes. It gives the enchanter more time to get off a longer casting spell. It doesn't seem to aggro the mob as much as a stun either.

I can't wait to max my div and start playing around with the 34th upgrade to FOL.
