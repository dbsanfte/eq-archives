I would say High Elf with 130 Wisdom and the other 5 points put into Strength. Small armor is far easier to come by, weighs less, and High Elves have their choice between small and medium. They also have Infravision. They have a far easier time having enough mana due to their high wisdom. It is far easier to add 5 Strength than it is to add 3 Wisdom.

Humans are a bit blind. As a Human I have run from a small tree at night thinking it was a cyclops and run into a cyclops thinking it was a small tree. No night vision is truly painful when you have to hunt undead at night. Humans have a maximum of 110 base Wisdom. Humans can only wear Medium armor. The exception to the armor is the quest armor which is the same weight as small plate. For the rest, I don't think the extra 5 or 10 Strength is worth the extra weight you end up carrying as a Human. I play a Human Cleric, but have a lower level High Elf that I tried out. The High Elf was far superior. The main thing was that the High Elf had the mana to finish the fights, not just get most of the way through them before running dry. Unless you can twink hard, I would say go High Elf or Dwarf. Since you don't want a Dwarf, then I would say High Elf. Dark Elves are nice, but the Clerics have a lot of extra work to do on faction. Halflings are nice also, but the armor for females is often off. The feet of the Halflings can be a bit embarassing. To me, the naked feet are a bit of an added pain when you die. It might be untroubling to some, but death is annoying enough without that to me.

Gnomes are both weak and the lowest base wisdom that you can get. The Ultra vision might be nice though.

Primarily, skill will help, but stats will limit you. Dwarf Clerics should have all their points put into Wisdom, and the other 5 put into Agility or Strength. All other races should put all point into Wisdom, and the other 5 left over put into Strength. Some people will disagree with me, but that is my very strong opinion.

It seems to me many Clerics get discouraged at about 20 and 30. I will tell you some things to help you get through.

Clerics have a very hard time from about 19 to 23 and 29 to 33 due to having no greater spells than Druids or Shamans, and not having Spirit of the Wolf. Once you hit 34 though, you have Superior Healing. 600 hit points for 250 mana as opposed to the 300 hit points for 150 mana of Greater Healing. That is 1800 hit points healing for the price of 1500. When you hit 39, you get Complete Healing. 400 mana for however much damage someone has taken. So if you do a 1,000 point heal, you are about even with a Superior Healing. Any damage over 1 k is all cake. A level 45 Warrior has so many hit points, and are so hard to hit that they can live a long time getting pummeled. Better, they get harder to kill below 35% health, where others get easier to kill below 35%. This will let a group with a 39+ Cleric and a 40 ish Warrior kill often even if the Cleric doesn't have Clarity. Some Clerics here have posted about healing their Warrior after multiple pulls rather than healing them and then regaing the mana for the next pull like everyone else has to. I have done this and had more mana at the end of a fight than I started with. Complete Healing is the thing that sets us apart from any other Class. Not Resurrections, not four stuns, not our armor (it doesn't help that much really) and not any bonus to our Healing abilities.

Everywhere you turn, it's grumpy drunken dwarves haggling with merchants and aloof high elves staggering home encumbered with a backpack of two fine steel rapiers.

Not that many Gnome, Erudite and Humans, though, because some people like concentrating on the 200 Wisdom aspect of things. Personally I chose the character that looked the most Noble, apart from that tarty appearance in Banded or Chain (Bleh!)

Human clerics are perfectly feasable. I've made it to 178 Wisdom, AC 806 Self-Buffed, +95 mana. Strength is a bit low still, at 92. (Only strength item is the mask). I consider myself somewhat underequipped for my level, but I am very happy with my belongings.

I still need some Efreeti boots, a Girdle, Ivanders hoop and maybe a range slot item and I'll be kicking into the magical 200 and still enjoying that high necked solid metal look that typifies us humans. (running around in the gold with black breastplate at the moment - looks very nice and formal, but I haven't set foot in the planes yet)

Night vision is mainly a problem in the Karanas when it starts raining. There is that Serpent Sight band from the High Shaman in Sol B, but that needs about 3 or 4 30's to do safely. You will need to lose a +6 wis ring, though, any time you use it, so you swap it on and off as required. I camped him on Sunday Night (3 people in Sol A - 49, 45 and 51st) for about 3 hours until he popped and dropped it. I understand they are about 500 plat.

For the previous levels, I've been using a Lightstone, a Screaming mace and a Charred Guardian shield which are all light emitters (but the effects don't multiply together)

I've found human clerics tend to be a bit of a Armour over wisdom cleric, party due to the extra strength and partly from necessity.

If I had to start again, I would still be a human (110 wis / 85 str), but I'd also be tempted to go Gnome or Erudite(!), just to be different from most other people out there.

Oh, and I have *never* had a group complain about my setup, although about 14 levels ago a Snooty High Elf said that with all that armour I shouldn't hit my 'Cleric under attack' button, and just soak up the attention of the Cavalier and his wizard friend.

Stats are pretty obvious to everyone.
Erudites and humans have no night vision, dark elves have ultravision, all the other clerical races have infravision. (vision can be a real pain sometimes, try to get the ring from Sol A, and/or find a mage to summon you a heat/cold stone when you log)

Few of the things you'll make will be useful too.
Then comes the look. High elves and dwarves look awful, must be to counter their awesome stats. The other races look cool to very cool in plate (erudites have a very unique look).

Having a 41st level human cleric, I would say I have some experience.

The Vision Thing: Get Lightstone or a GSL or a magical item that acts as a GSL. The first few levels are gonna suck, but you will get over it. Glowing Band is a nice item (Serpent Sight) which I use. At 14th, you can cast Halo of Light. One thing not mentioned here is simply ask a magician for a heat or coldstone. I still do this at 41st so I dont have to change out a Jasper Ring. Hunt in dungeons since most of these are light up nicely and XP is better anyways.

Wisdom: Max it out (110) and dump last 10 points in strength. This way you can actually wear armor (unlike a wimpy high elf at low levels). Yes it will be harder to get your wisdom to 200, but you can do it and frankly you should wear AC armor as well. I can get mine to 195, but keep it at 191 so I can wear a high AC breatplate (Blood Stained Tunic collecting dust). One insight spell by a 39+ Enchanter and poof! 201 Wisdom. Going by the eqcastersrealm's mana calculator, the difference in mana at 50th level between 200 and 180 (a good goal for human clerics) wisdom isnt even a Superior Healing. So dont obsess over wisdom like most people do.

Religion. I would suggest Rodcet Nife due to the Aegis of Life quest. But now the quest is so nerfed this isnt a big deal. I picked Mithaniel Marr and was regretting it but after I saw the Defense of Marr shield on EQ'Lizer I am VERY glad with my choice.


I love playing my human cleric and unlike others wouldnt go back and make a dwarf or high elf cleric. This game is not just about stats but also role-playing what you like, style and uniqueness. With enough hard work, any race can make a good cleric (even a gnome).

Something that hasn't been mentioned yet is the equipment flexibility you get at later levels when you start with a high wis.

I chose high elf, and I am at a point now where much of my wis gear has been traded out for AC, hps, and mana. Other races do not have this advantage since they must work fairly hard just to max their wis.

I have traded out my earrings with black sapphire, my CGS with Clay guardian shield, wis rings with plat fire opal, etc.

When you start with a high base wis, you will give yourself much more flexibility at later levels with equipment.

And remember... a cleric is only a caster in platemail .. dont worry too much about str, dex..and not even agi or sta too much. Agi only marginally helps AC after you pass the magic 75 barrier..and dex really doesn't help a cleric at all. Sta is ok, but again not worth spending alot of points on.

I would recommend a race with high base wis so that you can wear gear at higher levels that will help you more with AC, hps, and mana.
