I think we're getting caught up in something unrelated to EQ. There is a difference between morality and respect. In EQ, morality doesn't have much of a place since it's not real, nor do we have any means of enforcing it. Respect, however, is more universal and that is what many players lack.

Let's say you and your friends are playing basketball. To do so requires a court and there are many public courts available (in the US, anyways). Let's say someone needs to get to the other side. It's a public court and everyone has a right to be there but the respectful thing to do is to walk around the edge instead of through the middle where the game is being played. To walk through the middle says 1 of 2 things:

1. I don't care if I destroy your game (apathy)
2. I am more important than you (elitist)

It's true everywhere you go, in every culture there is. You either don't care or you are more important than everyone else. And people take notice. It doesn't matter whether or not they think you are moral or not. You can be quite moral and people will still hate you if you have an attitude.

I think the translation to EQ is obvious.

So, when someone on a PvP server refers to another server as a "carebear" or "my little pony" or whatever else you wish to call it, it's taken as an insult, regardless of your intent. It fall under the elite attitude of "my play is better than yours". It implies that we need to be coddled or nursemaided through the game instead of taking the combat route when that is not the case at all. Even more insulting is the fact that I do not think most of them have any real PvE experience. Cali would indeed correct when she labels such a person as "prejudiced".

Is PvE perfect? Of course not. But I don't think anyone can make a case for PvP being that much better. Players there have different options than us but they have different problems too.

Bottom line: when you say "blue" servers are "carebear" it's about as accurate as my calling TZ and VZ "team ganking".

I've seen a few posts in this thread about "my right to have fun", "playing the game as VI intended" and the roleplaying aspects of the game. After you've seen every zone and after you've killed every manner of monster or beast, there are only two things left to keep someone playing:

Competition and the social aspects of the game.

Lets face it, after level 45 or so, there's not much left undiscovered. There are new dungeons and dragons and named uber-mobs, but there's no mystery there. No excitement for it's own sake.

And roleplaying gets old very quickly. It's a static world, like a virtual high-school. A few people come and a few people go, but the setting never changes and the routine is the same day after day. After dealing with a die-hard roleplayer for about 15 minutes, I'm ready to /ignore them and move on.

All that's left are interpersonal relationships and striving to be better than someone or something (competition).

After 58 levels I've gotten to know some people pretty well. I know about their wives, pets, children and their job. Sometimes we get together and chat. Sometimes we get together and kill stuff. Most times we end up doing both. Everquest becomes mere scenery for our virtual life.

I've also realized that there are only a few fundamental tactics involved in winning a battle. Once those tactics have been mastered, encounters are simply a matter of devising a strategy built on those tactics and executing it properly.

So why do we continue to play?

For me, it is 70% competition and 30% relationships. How about everyone else?
