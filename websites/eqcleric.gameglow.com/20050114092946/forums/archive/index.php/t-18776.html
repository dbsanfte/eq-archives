The character creation is pretty amazing. You can make nearly anything you can think of, just stand in Atlas Park and look around a while to see how versatile the creation design is. If you have a character in mind from a comic book, you can come within throwing distance of that character (sorry, no Cthulu squids).

The game really is fairly easy. When you have a controller to lock down mobs, you don't have any sort of "I said not to touch the damn thing! Weren't you listening?" sorts of stress you get from EQ. Taking out a locked down mob is a good thing as it keeps the mob from going for the controller if it gets loose. Teams fights against the bad guys are a free for all at lower levels (but fun), though I imagine people will learn more control later on. In fights it seems to be more "What do I want to do?" rather than "What can I do?". You are far less squeezed into a mold in CoH, though it is a bit early to be certain.

The assist method is also superior. Target whoever you want to assist and use your ranged attacks. If the hero you are assisting will need a heal after the attack, hit your heal ability and it will start as soon as the ranged attack is finished. No twitch timing involved.

Going LOM as a healer in a fight? You can rest once (long recharge time) when the team can do without you a bit. We had 3 defenders in a group of 8 last night (I think I was the only Empathic healer doing the big heals) and they were able to take up the slack when I couldn't heal. Since it takes time for a power to cycle and become ready for reuse, you have time to use your attack powers however you choose, and still do your healing job.

The biggest weakness seems to be a lack of tanks, rather than a lack of healers. Tanks probably die a bit too much due to inexperienced healers and the lack of Empathic Defenders (with a CH that costs them 20% of their own health that cannot be healed back for about 30 seconds).

When making my empathic healer, I probably made a mistake by chosing "radiation" as my damage powerline (yes, you can chose what sort of resists you have to deal with). Radiation lowers the defense of its target, and seemed like a good bet at the time because I expected more melees. Instead, this game has more caster types than tank types being created (that I have seen), so something that increases the effectiveness of ranged powers hitting would probably be better (or even psi to ignore defense, though you are unable to do any damage to mindless machines with psi).

As far as the interface, it was awful until I remapped the movement keys, autorun, and screenshot keys to what I was used to using in EQ. The /bind commands provided earlier in this thread helped immensely as well.


P.S. Infinity server, though I am still experimenting with characters to find the type I like the most.

The binds/macros in the links are some more complicated ones that you might be interested in later. Forget if it was mentioned in the thread previously but /bind and /macro work exactly the same except for one minor difference, /bind hooks the command to a key or combination of keys on your keyboard while /macro makes a button you put on your little tray and can click.

Here's a basic example:

/bind numpad1 "powexec_name Hover"
This makes it so every time you hit '1' on the number pad it will execute Hover. The nice thing about calling it by name is that it doesn't matter where you drag the icon on the bar

/bind numpad4 "g Mesmerizing $target - please refrain from waking them.$$powexec_name Mesmerize"
The "g" at the beginning of the message make it always say the text in group chat no matter what the default is. $target says the name of target. The "$$" is the separator between commands.

/bind h "+up$$powexec_name Hover$$powexec_name Hover"
This one is handy. The "+up" makes you jump before it turns on Hover so you have a little distance from the ground if you're trying to avoid the bad guys. Not 100% why the second execute of Hover is in there since I took it from someone else but I believe it just makes the command *not* toggle. When you hit the button Hover will always be on, even if it was on when y ou hit the button again.

/bind LSHIFT+LBUTTON "powexec_name Teleport"
I haven't used this one yet but it seems like it'd be a major bonus. I don't have teleport but I believe currently you have to click on teleport then click on the screen where you want to teleport. This speeds up the process. If you hold down the left shift button and click the left button on your mouse it will start teleporting without having to do the two-step process of click on power then click on where to go.

/macro moron "local Hey $target, Good job moron!$$thumbsup"
Notice the /macro. This will create a new button to click and I believe place it on your mouse pointer. The button will be labeled "moron" (there isn't a lot of space so keep it short, also there is currently a bug that will keep you from logging in if you make a macro named "color" or "colors"). The button will say in the local channel (like /say from EQ) "Hey %target, Good job moron!" and then do the thumbs up emote (the guy made this macro for the idiots who come along and finish off the pack of mobs you're working on).
