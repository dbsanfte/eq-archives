Just going to post this overhere so it doesn't get glossed over since this thread seems to be the new "what clerics want" thread.

First post:
I know a way VI could fix the entire cleric's-can't-solo issue in one fell swoop, and they wouldn't even have to add any new spells (though an undead snare or slow would be N-I-C-E). There is even a basis for it looking at AD&D, which is what EQ is supposed to be based on, originally. Druids and shaman would be unable to complain, and fun would be restored to the life of high level clerics everywhere. What is this ultimate panacea? Quite simple, really:
Tag all heals except CHeal (and WoR, obviously) as damaging to undead.

And there would be singing in the streets, birds would chirp and rainbows would fill the sky. That one simple little thing would once again open up the end game for clerics. We would no longer be considered by raid leaders only as "do I use him in a Cheal chain or to throw spam heals??" Soloing would be as viable for us as any other caster class, especially the priest classes. There is a solid reasoning for it-the divine power that can heal the living can equally destroy the undead. We would have usable AoEs (AoE heal = AoE undead nuke now).

Oh yeah, and one more thing-fix the freeking symbol quests that aren't working/put in ones for the religions that don't have them. So far, this and diety-specific jewelry/idols are the only thing differentiating the choice of gods for us. Throw everyone this little bone, at least.


Second post with a bit more info on my reasoning added:
I contend that clerics/druids/shaman (being priests) *should* be the most powerful class when it comes to killing undead, with clerics being slightly ahead of the rest of the pack. The turn undead AA ability was a step in the right direction for us, but I think it needs to be expanded upon.

/roleplay on
We weild the power of our gods-a cleric should be a truly awesome force when faced with a horde of undead. Any person devoted and pious enough to have reached the upper levels of the priesthood should be a shining beacon of light (or menacing beacon of the dark forces for out evil brethren) in the dark, evil places where the undead hold sway. Undead beings should cringe at the sight of us and whither at our holy touch, ripped apart by the very power that serves to mend the wounds and stem the bleeding of our living companions.
/roleplay off

I would also contend that Torpor would be a better spell than CE. Think about it-Torpor is 300 less damage for 100 less mana, but also adds in snare/slow/mana reduction. Nature's Recovery/Touch for druids would also be amazing spells-1Kish DD and 900 DoT. Though I agree that all these spells would be truly awesome against undead-I also feel that they should-level 59+ clerics/shamans/druids should be able to rip through undead foes as easily as necromancers destroy living foes.

When you consider the fact that these spells only work on a limited set of creatures (undead) and that our spells vs live mobs would still be much less than optimal, you're not really unbalancing us when compared to other classes, just opening up moer options for us. Wizards would still be the kings of DD vs live mobs (and vs undead in many cases), shaman/necros/mages would still have the edge on us in damage due to having useable pets. Druids/wizards would still be the masters of quad-kiting and transportation.

In other words, you would be expanding the role of all the priest classes (since druids and shaman also get heals) and opening up some more options for both soloing and grouping for clerics.

All without needing to add new spells, abilities, etc-you would just need to add an extra bit to the spells that currently exist.

First of all, let me say that I realize we're seeing these proposed changes totally out of context (with respect to both other changes to clerics, and changes to other classes) so take the below with a grain of salt.

Stuff you asked about:

Smite changes - Good stuff. A power increase, albeit a small one, to our nukes, which could do with an upgrade. As a level 40 I can still solo low blues (And occasionally do, as a break from the leveling grind.), and this will make that a bit easier and is certainly welcome. On a related note, a similar small upgrade to our undead DD line would be welcome, to keep those two lines of spells balanced with respect to each other. All that said, a magic resistance debuff of any kind would be highly desirable. Even aside from DDs, having my root resisted 5 times in a row, and the 6th breaking after 2 seconds makes me wish I hadn't tried to cast it at all. And that's on a blue.

Resurrection Changes - Mostly good stuff, but I'm not sure about all of it. As a L40, I don't have the "good" XP rezz yet, but I'm looking forward to it. That said, remember that rezz is one of our "prestige" abilites, and an XP rezz even moreso. I think I'd rather wait until 49 for my next rezz than get an "in betweener" at 44. Also, as an impoverished cleric I often rezz for donations, and I'm a tad worried about what a spell every circle would to the market for rezzes. However, this would certainly give lower level clerics a power boost, which I'm in favor of. So I'm of two minds on the issue. Either way, I'm in favor of the L14 no xp rezz spell. Put it in there.

I also agree with several people above that this seems to have two intended effects, and may only accomplish one of them. It will almost certainly make dungeon exploration more convenient and thus popular at lower levels. But I'm not sure it will do much to make clerics more desirable than drus/shams as healers in lower level groups. (Begging a cleric who's not in your group for a rezz is currently the norm, I don't see that that will change much.)

Either way I like the idea of the XP percentage showing in the confirmation window. There's some confusion now, and if the number of types of rezzes is increased, it will certainly get worse. The trend seems to be toward giving the players more information anyway.

As to the paladin rezzes, my thoughts sort of depend on what else you guys are doing. By themselves, I'm against them. CHeal and XP rezzes are really the only unique aspects of the cleric class. They're the only things we get that no one else does. Giving XP rezzes to paladins steps on our toes, with regard to class abilities and uniqueness. That said, I'm all for broadening the classes somewhat (see below), and if the cleric class is broadened some then I'd be fine with paladins being broadened to include XP rezzes.

Stuff you *didn't* ask about, but that I'll talk about anyway:

Defense - Our dodge skill cap should almost certainly be higher. Int casters get the same dodge cap, yet we're a plate class and supposed to be able to melee somewhat. By the same reasoning, a *small* increase to the caps on our weapon skills and offense and defense seems in order.

Group Buffs - I'm still amazed that clerics don't get a group buff anywhere in the spiritual armor line, and we don't get *any* group buffs until level 52. Certainly we should get some group buffs by level 39, and perhaps even as early as 9 or 14. And a group buff anywhere in the spiritual armor line would be appreciated.

On a related note, another line of buffs usable on others (just 1 more) would be awfully nice. Anything really; short duration (5-10 min) small increase in chance to hit, short duration small increase in damage per hit, anything.

Uber level heals - Now that I've gotten Complete Heal, I really don't have much to look forward to in the way of heals other than Celestial Heal. The higher level heals, Remedy, etc., don't really offer much other than a tiny gain in efficiency (a couple of percent). I'd happily settle for those heals having the same mana/hp efficiency as Superior Heal in exchange for a significantly faster cast time. Similarly, below raid levels our AoE heal spells are very specialized. You need at least 3 group members to be wounded, and wounded enough for that amount of healing to be efficient in order to use it. Even something minor like increasing the range would make the earlier spells in this line see more use.

Class Roles - I realize that EQ is a class-based game, but somewhere between initial design and now the classes have gotten EXTREMELY narrow. Clerics are perhaps the biggest example of this, we really only do one thing well enough to speak of: Heal. I understand your desire to maintain inter-class dependencies, that's why I agree that clerics should not get an invis spell. However, one of the things that makes a game fun is versatility, the ability to do something new every day. I think the overly tight class roles are the main reason most people have so many alts. I'm not saying every class should be jacks-of-all-trades, but a little diversity of function would be welcome to most people. Well defined roles is a good thing, being straitjacketed into those roles is not.

Soloing - Clerics are the worst solo class in the game, and this has several effects other than the obvious "we need to group for XP." First of all, while I wouldn't want to use soloing as my primary method of gaining XP, it's a nice change from grouping for variety's sake. And while at level 40 I can just barely solo low blues, it's a near thing (half health and OOM when I'm done with one). In 5 more levels, I doubt I'll be able to solo XP giving mobs at all.

Second, lack of soloing ability makes doing quests more difficult. Many mid-level quests involve camping mobs that don't provide very good loot/xp other than the quest item. I hate to ask several other people to group with me so *I* can get a quest item, with little potential reward for them. And often without a group I can't even get to the mob in question in a dungeon. This actually gets *worse* as I level past the intended level range of the quest. I'm too high to group with people who could get the minimal side rewards of camping the quest mob, and people of my level now get no rewards for the camp. And I still can't solo the quest mob.

Finally, it contributes to the "impoverished cleric" syndrome. Yes, in part clerics are poor because they have to buy spells and plate-class armor. But we're also poor because we can't solo mobs for loot. Ignoring farming of high-demand items (like the FBSS), which I'm against, many classes derive a significant portion of their income from soloing green mobs for their standard loot (plat, weapons, etc.) so they don't have to split it. Clerics can barely do this with LOW greens, and even then it takes too long to kill a mob for it to be effective.

(Sorry for the long rant. I hope Verant keeps asking players for their opinions, and that this initial information overload doesn't dissuade you.)

Jahaar the issue with enforced reference isn't so much the damage efficency, its the fact that stuns of this nature are of no particular use in the high end game.

There really are two types of stun, those for emergency and control, and those for preventing gaters.

Control ones are handled fine by stun command, and sacred word.

Gaters however are more problematical. The recast time on stuns is so high that in order to actually keep a mob stunned for its last 20% of its life, it takes a substancial number of stun spells, which often a cleric can ill afford to use and provide healing coverage.

Enforced reverence would be much better served as being a gate preventer, in order to do this it would require fast cast time, damage is of little importance, and it would have to have a fast recast time/perhaps even none. Since this could get abusive I would suggest removing the stun component completely from the spell and make it a spell interupter only.
