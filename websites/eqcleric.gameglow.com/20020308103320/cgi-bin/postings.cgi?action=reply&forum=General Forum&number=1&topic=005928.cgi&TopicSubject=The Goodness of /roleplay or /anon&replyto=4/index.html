Nazrin WraithforgeCrispus,
The answer is pretty simple actually as you did state the /anon command makes you anonymous so it does not show up you Class/Race/Level/Guild/where when someone does a /who in the zone you are in. It also means that the only way someone could find out if your on is too either have you in their friends list as this will show you as being on just now where you are. Also if someone has your name they can do a /who all and see if you on and again will not know what zone you are in.

The /roleplay is really used like a /anon but this actually leaves the Guild tag up so you can do a /who all and it will show you in the list, many guilds prefer this as it shows active members when a who is done on the guild.

The use is really upto the player but majority of these two commands are used by Druids and Wizards and not Clerics after all we are honorable and tend too show ourselves to the public at large. I'm asuming since you've asked that you don't know what roleplaying is, if i'm wrong then just slap my upside my head:P Roleplaying games started off as the tabletop kind, games like Dungeons & Dragons, Rifts, All Flesh Must Be Eaten, Call of Cthulu and so many more.

The basic idea behind it is you make a character (just like in EQ) and then you try and figure out a personality for your character and usually keeping in mind their stats is good too. Is he good or bad, does he have any mortal enemies, is he strong or weak, smart or not so smart, quick or slow, etc.. Then you try and act out that cahracter while playing within the rules of the game. For EQ you would probably do other things but similar to tabletop games, for instance dwarves and elves while not hating each other dont' get along unless trouble is around, then they work together very well. High Elves think they are better than everyone else and would never stoop so low as to group with any of the evil races. Gnomes are always curious and in some games they invent very strange and often explosive machines, Paladins are mortal enemies with Shadow Knights, and good clerics would distrust any necromancer.

But in EQ it makes things kind of hard, although some people do it, to not group with certain classes or races, and to stay in a role, especially in the middle or combat and such so most people don't do it or those that do only do it in certain situations. Some people create stories around their character, I remember one person, I forget the URL, that had this huge story for her elven cleric, and it was a great story too by the way. You have a personal choice here. On one hand, some clerics can't stand the number of res requests they get and go anonymous. On the other hand, some don't, either because they don't mind so much, or out of a sense of duty, or what have you. It's a choice you have to make for yourself (usually when you hit about 39th).

I do not go anon. I get requests for resses all over the place. I have a policy on doing them, and it depends on how busy I am and where they are, and I stick to it. On one hand I get tired of the requests sometimes, especially because some people are rude and ungrateful.

On the other hand, some people are kind, wonderful, thankful, appreciative, and generous. And those people are a pleasure to interact with. And sometimes you will be rewarded if you are willing to go out of your way to help someone. (My top reward to date is a robe of the ishva.)

You can go anon, and avoid the requests. In doing so you avoid the hassle, but also the pleasures. The choice is up to you.
