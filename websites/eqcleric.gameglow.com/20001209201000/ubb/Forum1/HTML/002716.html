5. Group exp is not based on level (within reason).

The exp gained by each group member, as long as each member is reasonably close in level to each other, is computed by proportionally dividing the exp per kill by the total exp of each group member. There is no accounting for the level of each person when determining the split, unless certain people in the group are far too low level (a level 50 and a level 20 for example). This leads to a few direct conclusions:

*People in hell levels do not take a disproportionate split of a group's exp.

*Classes will larger innate exp penalties do not take a disproportionate split of a group's exp.

*People with larger innate exp penalties level slower than their groupmates, even when fighting the same creatures. They gain exp at the same rate and will have the same total exp after fighting the same creatures together, but each one will be at a different level or at least a different percentage into a level with the same amount of exp points.

*Certain classes need to play more, outside of their normal groups, to keep up with their regular groupmates. A Troll Shadow Knight will need to play *a lot* longer than the Halfling Warrior he normally groups with to remain at the same level over any signifigant amount of time.

*Someone who is grouped with another who is slightly lowerl level but fighting things that are green to the higher level person and blue to the lower level one is still taking exp from the group. Therefore, the high level person isn't just "helping", he's stealing a lot of exp which isn't going anywhere because the mob is too low level for him to gain anything.

_____________________________________________

The above statement is from the guys who have been figuring out how experience is done in the game. If the first paragraph is true then there first two conclusions are wrong. Conclusion three is only valid if you group with people of the same exact experience not level. If indeed experience is divided up proportionally by the amount of experience each person has then classes with experience penalties do take more experience from a group. Lets look at this by a simple example. At the beginning of level 21 a Troll SK has 13.44 mil exp pts, and a Dwarven Cleric would have 8.0 mil exp pts. So if group experience is divided proportionally the equation would look like this :
Exp given to A = Experience earned from monster* (Total exp of A / (Total exp of A + Total exp B)). If the Troll SK and Dwarven Cleric were grouped and they killed a monster worth 100,000 it would look like this: Troll gets = 100,000 * (13.44/ (8+13.44)) = 62,687 The cleric would get 37,313. However the cleric only needs 1.261 mil to get his next level so he just got almost 3% of that the troll needs 2.118 mil to level so he got almost 3% as well. So effectively they would level at about the same rate if grouped. What this implies (if it is correct and I have no way of really knowing) is that if you are grouped with people of the same level you will gain levels at about the same rate. Just out of curiosity I did a little experiment, I have two networked computes and using my wife’s account created two newbie chars a warrior and paladin. The warrior would take 900 to get to level 2 and the paladin 1400 to get to level 2. I played them until the warrior leveled (took about 10 mins) and sure enough once the warrior leveled the paladin was around 65%. So I ungrouped the warrior and played the paladin until he got level 2. Then regrouped and started playing again. To get to level 3 the warrior is going to need 6300 more exp and the paladin will need 9,800. If the experience id divided solely based on level when the warrior hits level 3 the paladin should be about 65% again. I have not finished this experiment yet but so far the experience is almost identical. It should be clear once the warrior hits level 3 how valid the equations are since at a couple of bubbles its hard to tell.

As you can see I got really curious about this once I started reading the thread. I do not know how valid any of the assumptions are and would be really interested in what others have observed. From around level 35 to 50 I spent most of the time grouped with a Monk, Ranger, Bard, Paladin and Wizard, myself being a dwarven cleric. Interestingly enough we all leveled at about the same rate as the proportional experience theory implied. If the experience was divided evenly based on level I should have leveled much faster than the Ranger, Bard and Paladin but I did not.

What does everyone else think?
