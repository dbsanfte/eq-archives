GryfaliaWell, nothing personal, but at level 8 I
wouldn't expect you to know anything about
Kedge Keep, and at the same time I wouldn't
expect you to be asking for a rez.

But this person WASN'T level 8. Indeed, I
know that he was above 30 and I think from
recollection higher than 40 (not sure). At
that point you SHOULD know something about
Kedge Keep, not necessarily from having been
there, but from discussions people have.

It's an underwater dungeon, kinda underneath
Dagnor's Cauldron. Many things see invis and
since it is so unfriendly an environment, you
don't just visit, you go there for a reason.

/who name all is one of the basic commands
in the game. At a base level /who tells
you the names of everyone in zone (and, if
they aren't roleplaying or anon, their
class, level and guild.). Doing a /who name
would give you the information on just the
one person. Doing a /who name all would
give you that information regardless of where
you and I are, AND tell you where I am.

I actually don't mind rez requests, and have
only have a couple people give me a hard time
for not being able to get to them.

And, in a nutshell, my attitude towards these
subjects is that people who request a rez
need to remember that they are not the top of
my list when I am doing something, and they
move further down my list depending on what
I am doing. If I am just sitting in EComm
selling stuff, well, just wait a minute for
me to finish this transaction and tell me
where the corpse is (or get me a port or
whatever). If I am soloing it merely depends
how deep I am in whatever dungeon I am. I
don't want to have to work my way back down
to the basement of CC. If I am in a group
in a hard to get to place (the planes, Kedge,
Kobolds in Velk's Laboratory) it's VERY
unlikely that I will drop what I am doing and
help you out. And a little research on your
end (the requestor) to look where I am saves
me from having to say what, at a higher
level, you should be able to figure out for
yourself.

I suppose I find it similar to warriors who
