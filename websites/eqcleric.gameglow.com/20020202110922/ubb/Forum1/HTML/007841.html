Something that's really nice is to add additional mappings to your numberpad keys. That way, you can cast 1-8 gems by using the corresponding numbers on the keypad. I put sit on 0 on number pad, spell book on the .(del) key, con on + key, and attack on the enter key. Mapping these keeps them mapped for all of your characters, so it helps on my wizard and shaman, too. The enter key is nice on my monk as it's not easy to accidentally attack something. Also makes it easier to twist bard songs.

If I'm playing my cleric on a second computer, I usually make custom hotkeys on my second computer for each person. Using CH for the tank, DL for the "semi-tanks" and remedy for the enchanter or caster types. Similar to the way some of the people have it:

/sit off
/target tank
/g CH on %T
/cast 4 (CH on mine)

I just change that up for each slot, usually have SIT on 4 and Personal on 5 (I just like to be tortured and see how slow my xp is moving hehe).

"Real" Fear-Kiting is best accomplished through the use of a snare-type spell. When I say that I have been doing a lot of Fear-Kiting, I probably should have also clarified (if I didn't) that I was partnered with a Ranger. Have the Ranger Snare whatever mob, and then cast Fear (Invoke Fear at my age) on the mob. This will in turn cause the mob to run away from you and the Snare will ensure that it doesn't run away too fast. In zones where you are not likely to pick up additional mobs by aggros, this is the most efficient manner of saving mana. When I partner with someone else and the mob is feared, they are essentially never attacking who you are supposed to be healing, so it saves on mana and downtime. I should note (if you aren't familiar with kiting) that fearing a mob you can't control is dangerous to your health if it has friends nearby that will jump in the fight. It is (however, much more difficult) possible for a Cleric to solo Fear-Kite if they have some sort of speed enhancement to keep up with the mob. However, this is not desireable to Fear-Kiting without some sort of snare involved.

I noticed everyone has a sit key and most have a book key...

You can configure your keyboard so you don't waste hotkeys on this. (Do this in your option menu.)

I have 'z' set for spellbook. 'x' set for change screen (typically your 'F10' key). 'c' is sit/stand. and 'v' is duck. I may have had to switch other keys around, like the advancement menu defaults to v, i made it alt-v, etc.

So, if I need to look at my book, I hit 'z'. If I am finished memming a spell, but I don't want to stand, I hit 'x'. To sit/stand, I hit 'c'. And to interrupt a spell I hit 'v' twice.

I usually only use one set of hotkeys and my hotkeys 1-7 are set to spells 1-7.

My spells are almost always:
1. CH (Hotkey is macro with /g Complete Healing %t in 10 seconds).
2. Cel Heal (Again, with /g message).
3. Remedy (Again with /g message)
4. Stun (Swap with group heal if hunting un-stunable MoBs)
5. Root
6. Swap (maybe DD, maybe Zing, buff spells go here too).
7. DA
8. DB

When I cast DA, I switch hotkey from spell 7 (DA) to spell 8 (DB) and vice versa. (Although looking at Roguefox keys, I think I am going to switch to a macro that /g Invuln 18 seconds, /cast 7, /cast 8. Good idea.)

My last 3 hotkeys vary a lot and I will change them out. Usually 8 is inv slot with jboots in it. 9. Is /assist. 10. Is my /loc, senseheading key (Macro that does /loc and /doability 1).

In raids, I usually have 8,9,10 all set to target players. 8 Target MA cleric, 9 target MA, 10 target SA.
