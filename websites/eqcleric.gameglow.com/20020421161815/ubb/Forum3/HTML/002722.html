All epics should be equal in ease to attain or in difficulty to obtain. Unfortunatly at this point they can not be radically changed.

The cleric epic attainability can be put on par with some of the other classes merely by increasing the spawn rate of ragefire. If he was garunteed to spawn randomly within 3 days(72hours) then the bottleneck for the cleric epic would be done away with. In the process of this remove all loot from ragefire except for the cleric item, similar to the spawned fay for druids. This would ensure that only clerics are intersted in ragefire being killed. Here's what I think should be done with Ragefire:

The problem: Ragefire spawns every 3 days (give or take), and is heavily contested. Making him a faster spawn isn't going to happen since Verant don't want epics entering the world any faster than they do now.

(I'm not sure I understand the logic there, since Bergurgle and Gimblox are already somewhat rare, but it's true that there's a big backlog of clerics with their pearl, me included).

So...

Change the Ragefire to a non-KOS human merchant in Skyfire. Make him always up.

Add an extra stage where the cleric needs to collect several (4 maybe?) uncommon/rare drops from regular, xp-giving (at 50+) mobs in various locations around the world. One of these items would be a container.

Combine all items in the container, and hand this to the merchant. He despawns, and respawns as the KOS version. Fight occurs as previously. As soon as (or very shortly after - less than an hour) the dragon dies or despawns, the merchant form respawns non-KOS. If you fail this encounter you need to go camp the items again, but you keep the pearl.

Ragefire still drops the heart container and you still need the swirling pearl to combine it with.

This moves the bottleneck (i.e. limiting the rate of epic creation) to collecting the uncommon drops needed to hand in to the merchant. However, this bottleneck becomes much less painful since you can camp these items with a regular XP group.

Possible places to get these items could be Skyfire, Sol B, Burning Woods, Timorous Deep, Kedge... looking for zones with some tough mobs and a fire/water theme here.

I think pretty much everyone agrees that the big problem with the cleric epic is that it's a poorly designed quest. There are a lot of very easy steps (only Ixiblat and the Overking need a significant force), with one enormously contended rare spawn. Remove that contention by making Ragefire always available, but lengthen the quest.

How does that sound?

Problem with requiring pieces from other mobs, and zordak being an hourly spawn is that it only slows down particular clerics. The rate at which the epics enter the world is limited by the slowest single component.

Assume for the moment that the mobs dropping each of rare-piece-1, rare-piece-2, and rare-piece-3 spawn times are 8h, 12h, and 24h respectively. If the mob can spawn every 20 minutes, say, then even if the item from the mob is rare, the completion time -could- be 20m. The maxiumum number of a particular item that could potentially enter the world is the important number to consider for limiting rate of entry into the world.

While Joe Questor is off getting rare-piece-1, John Questor could be getting rare-piece-2, Jane Questor could be getting rare-piece-3. Queueing them through, 1 person is ready for zordak every 24h (the longest spawn time of the bunch). During that 24h, 2 people could have gotten rare-piece-2, and 3 people could have gotten rare-piece-1. There's a backlog at rare-piece-3 that grows over time, but that backlog is insignificant compared to the current zordak backlogs.

Unless one of the pieces enters the world at the same rate as zordak currently spawns, the rate at which epics enter the world is decreased. Thats what VI claims not to want. They want 1-2 epics entering the world per week, maximum. The only way they can ensure that is making one of the spawns occur at most twice a week. End result is that without doing substantial rewriting of the quest, there will be a single piece that ends up contested.
