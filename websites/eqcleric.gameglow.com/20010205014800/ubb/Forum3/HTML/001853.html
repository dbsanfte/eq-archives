WARNING. Long..;-)


Ok, here's Gryfalia's Sure Fire way to get Lava Rocks…

Ask around a lot.

No really, that's the basis of it. But to be more precise:

1) I already have a reputation as a smith on my server. I get requests all the time asking for armor from people I've never met before. Which means that people believe me when I ask about Lava Rocks
2) I make sure that people KNOW what lava rocks are for. I mean, currently we only know of 4 zones to get them in. Do the people that frequent those zones KNOW that they are worth something to you? Or do you have people leaving with a stack of 8 from Sol B selling them to the druid merchant (true story…the pain).
3) Hunt in a target rich environment. This is NOT Sol B or Skyfire. While there may be some specific mobs in these zones that drop them, these aren't exactly safe, no risk areas to look. Najena is the best for this, especially because I don't think anything will aggro players of your level. I still get the occasional Guard Officer or TT latching on to me and attacking. But as long as you keep killing the elementals off you WILL get Lava Rocks from the Fire Elementals. Not a lot, and not really fast, but hey if no one else is there you can hit the rare spawns too and grab some goodies to sell.
4) Create a mule. I have the hardest working mule in the game. This intrepid little DE made his way from Neriak (dying once to a shadow wolf), through EComm and NRo (with an Invis), through the oasis, the Deep and into the Outpost. I set up a banking account and stocked him with a ton of bat wings, food and drink. Then I sent him into Skyfire. Not only to Skyfire, but I found the pickings to be better at the Burning Woods zone, so I walked him over there! No mean feat, I might add. And except for one unfortunate death to a Gnawer while watching a group kill it, he has been perfectly safe (well, almost fell to his death, but…). He has managed to snag me at least 15 rocks. I go into him from time to time, advertise that I have food, drink and bat wings if anyone wants them (for free). I also advertise that I want Lava Rocks if anyone has them. Answer the inevitable question about what they are for and sometimes you will get a tell asking for food, sometimes you will get someone handing you a couple lava rocks (2 on Monday, 1 more yesterday). Make sure you bring a lot of bat wings. They are popular..hehe. Then, either get a guildmate to port you out, or find some nice Druid (Hey Toil!) to port them out for you and meet them with your main. Thank them profusely. Pay if you want. Whatever, just get them where you can use them.
5) Get lucky. Had someone just give me a rock yesterday because they liked the look of my armor, asked about it, and said 'hey, you mentioned Lava Rocks…I was wondering what they are for. Here!'. Proceed to cheer wildly.

I have found that is also VERY important to NEVER stop trying to pick up Drops of Mercury. I thought I had more than enough, but when they started Imbued up and my mule in Skyfire started producing, I quickly found I did NOT have enough and was lucky to find someone who had 8 for me (traded off all my swirling mists..10 of them, for 8 Drops, 3-4 charcoal, 20 resin and some other stuff..they were getting out of the dyeing business..making me one of that last ones).

And, of course, assume that failure will be your new friend.
That list above of armor I have doesn't mention the 5 failed Boots (simply amazing), 1 Vambrace, 1 Cloak and
a couple others. Those are EXPENSIVE failures. And now that I am starting to look for jacinths, the failures will
be measured in the thousands of PP. When I made the cloak last night I had 168 Str, 213 Wis and a skill of 185.
Still failed the first one. I have found that EVERY time I camp out and come back in, I have successfully made
the next piece I try. Probably sheer dumb luck, but who knows?

Oh yeah, and don't accidentally put it into the wrong forge. I did do that little move once when I came
back in. Didn't look at the forge I was using when I came in..hehe. OUCH Another 300PP down the drain or so.

Now I need to figure out what prices to sell things at. What do people think? Looking at the stats I listed in the above post, what are realistic prices?

Here are my ideas: [NOTE, nothing can cost less than 200PP - 135PP or so for Ruby, 35 per sheet of Brellium,
25 for a Chain jointing (stupid Hammer is still destroyed when making it) and then the Temper value, but that's
a wierder issue…Also keep in mind that these are all for Small followers of Brell ONLY]

Bracers: 10AC, plus 2 Wis, Save Fire 1
I have no idea who would pay over 200PP for this. Dwarven Clerics should just do the Reverent quest twice and paladins have better choices. Uses 1 Sheet
Visor: AC 7, unknown wisdom (no one has made it, likely 2 or 3), Save Fire 1
Hard time justifying buying this either. plus 7 Str/Wis is very nice..;-) One Sheet
Collar: AC 9, plus 3 Wis, Save Fire 1
Ok, now this is a nice item. It's one sheet of Brellium, and MUCH better than BI Medallion. 14 AC better that is. Cost to make, about 200PP (ignoring the temper). I think I could get almost 500PP for this, low failure rate.
Gauntlets: AC 12, plus 4 Wis, Save Fire 4
Very nice item. Better than the quest armor hands down. 2 Sheet item, so 235PP cost w/o temper included. Hard time thinking of a better hand item. Medium failure rate. I think 1000PP not unreasonable, perhaps more.
Vambraces: AC 12, Plus 4 Wis, Save Fire 4
Wow, another item that is better than the quest armor due to both plus wis AND high AC. 2 Sheet item, Medium failure rate. At least 1000PP again.
Pauldron: AC 12, unknown Wisdom, Save Fire 4
hard to pick this over the Adamantite Epaulets. Not sure plus plus 3 AC is worth losing 3 Wis. Likely Medium difficulty 2 sheet item. Hard to sell at a good price vs AE.
Helm: AC 14, Plus 4 Wis, Save Fire 4.
How can you NOT love this bad boy? Same Wis as the Runed Circlet, add some useful fire save, kick the Int out and LOOK AT THAT AC! I will take this over a Nightshade Wreath any day of the week (less, 13 AC, 1 Wis..hmm). 2 Sheets, likely medium difficulty. I would say at least 1000PP, perhaps more.
Girdle: AC 10, unknown Wisdom (likely 3 or 4), plus 2 fire Save.
Never made one, don't know how hard it is. Everyone seems to speak very highly of the Girdle of the Diligent (when you already have enough AC). This 2 sheet item has 1-2 less Wis, but the same AC and some Save Fire.
Who knows? If it IS 4 Wis, I think the value may be high, perhaps 2000 or so.
Boots: AC 12, Plus 4 Wis, Save Fire 2
Ok, IMPOSSIBLE to make. I am not the only one reporting this..hehe. BUT, these things ARE amazing. 1 sheet. I would say at least 3000PP (seriously). perhaps more. Compare to Golden Efreeti.
Cloak: AC 11, Plus 4 Wis, Save Fire 3 (I think)
Who needs a mammoth cloak? Good AC increase, 1.6 additional weight, change cold plus 5 to fire plus 3. Mammoth is worth around 2250 or so, I would say this should be more. How much more? 3000PP selling price. 3 sheets.
Breastplate: AC 22, Plus 4 Wis, Save Fire 4
Not hard for me to make, but others have reported 10+ failures in a row (WOW). Compare to the el-boring Bloodstained Tunic. Was already offered 3K for it, didn't take it. I would say 3-4K (AND some lava rocks..hehe). 3 Sheets
Greaves: AC 14, Wis plus 4, Save Fire 4
Made in frist try (somehow). This things kick the Gatorscale leggings to the curb also. Simply amazing. I would say at least 2000 due to what I assume is a normally high failure rate and high AC. 3 sheet.

WHEW, that took some doing. Hope you can actually make it all out. Just remember: None of this includes the getting of the lava rocks. I would almost charge these prices AND a lava rock or two. And I would strongly suggest making the armor BEFORE selling it. The chances of chain failing are out of this world.

I think that I would try to 'stock' Gauntlets, Vambraces, Collars, Helms, BPlates, Greaves, Boots and perhaps a Girdle.

Of course, when pondering value remember that this is a limited market. Which means 2 things. One is that there are fewer people that want it, so demand won't be too high. BUT, on the flip side, you need to remember that once they get it, they won't need it ever again. So, limited market, but really wanted by those who can use it, but only until they have it.

Well, that's enough of that. Thanks for reading this and whatever input you may have!

Ok, in a nutshell here's the list of costs:

Large Brick of brellium Approx 14PP or so (not sure)
Block of Brellium Approx 36 PP (again, or so)
Dwarven Smithy Hammer 11PP (currently you lose one each time you make chain jointing)
Dwarven Ale Basically nothing
Imbued Ruby Ruby is about 138 in Ak'anon with good faction, imbuing should be free
Leather Backing: I made mine, about 2-5PP purchased
Earthen Temper: Oi. This one sucks. 1 Dwarven Ale (see above), 2 Drops of Mercury (I pay 5PP
each for them, get them a lot in Steamfont), 1 Lava Rock - See other posts about this, it's tough.
If I am lucky I pay 25PP for them, I get a lot free. But the Lava Rocks ARE the bottleneck in this
process (well, and being able to buy lots of Rubies).

Now, each item needs this in the forge to make it:
1 Earthen Temper
1 Dwarven Smithy Hammer (this one is NOT destroyed)
1 Brellium Chain Jointing (1 Hammer which IS destroyed, 1 Large Brick of Brellium, 2 Ales)
1 Imbued Ruby
1 Mold (from 7.7 GP to 21PP at best charisma)
1 Leather Backing
1-3 Folded Sheet of Brellium (1 Block of Brellium plus 1 Dwarven Ale and a hammer which isn't
destroyed). Each Sheet is about 36PP (cost of the Block).

So, those are the items and their base costs, here are the amount of sheets needed per item:

1. HELM 2
2. BREASTPLATE 3
3. VAMBRACES 2
4. GREAVES 3
5. GUANTLETS 2
6. GIRDLE 2
7. BOOTS 1
8. CLOAK 3
Multiply the amount of Sheets by the sheet cost (36PP), add the Ruby cost (138), the Temper cost
(unknown), the Backing cost (Free for me), the Mold cost (7.7Gp-21PP) and the Jointing Cost
(about 25PP) and the cost of the hammer you DON'T lose (well, no cost cause you can reuse it) for
your final price.

Then expect to fail at least 50%. Hehe, did manage to make a second helm yesterday. Sold my spare
Imbued Breastplate for 3500PP and was SOME pleased. Now I just need to get my 2 Lava Rocks off my mule, make Tempers, and make 3 more items (have 1 more temper on my main).

Next time I hear an Enchanter complaining that their Enchant Vellium is a rare drop, I will moon im.
This stuff is SO much harder to do all things considered, it isn't funny…
