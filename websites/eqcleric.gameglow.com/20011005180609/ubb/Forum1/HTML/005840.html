Yes, it's also my guild's practice to give quick cash items, notably gems, except for high ticket items like diamonds, to clerics in the group/raid. My guildmates also contribute peridots and/or cash on an individual basis.

I've only joined in once to help out on an epic (my cleric's been semi-retired for awhile, and only recently been playing a lot) and the enchantress who it was for handed me a stack of peridots at the beginning of the raid.

When I first raised the issue (many had no idea how much peridots cost), we discussed establishing a guild fund for this sort of thing, but ultimately decided that it wouldn't be fair to folks who had gained no benefit from whatever raid required peridot symbols. It's since worked out pretty well, with the added side-benefit, now that my guildmates are more aware of the cost, of being encouraged to use Pinzarn instead for routine stuff.

Our guild (The Guardian Lords from Povar server) has an extensive guild treasury. On a guild-sponsored raid all non-rollable items are sold by a designated treasurer and that cash is used to replace the stock of reagents used for the raid (eg portal gems, buffing gems).
