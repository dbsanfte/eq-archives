Hi Jahaar,

When I saw an official EQ person here, I just had to sign up for an EQCleric account. I love playing as a cleric (it's the only character I play), so I'm very interested in any changes that affect me.

I think the changes to allow lower level clerics to cast ressurections is generally a good idea. I remember when I was a low level cleric, and groups actually preferred having druids or shamans as their healer. Hopefully this will help make clerics a more important class for low and mid level groups.

However, I think there are too many new ressurection spells that are being introduced. I think introducing the improvements to the ressurection spells once every two spell circles (ie 10 levels) like our other spells is a good pace. So instead of seven pre-49 ressurection spells, I would suggest having four. This would help avoid confusion over the amount of exp gained from the ressurection, and reduce the chance of players constantly telling the cleric to "Wait, let me see if I can get a better rez"... and getting upset if the cleric gets tired of waiting and goes on with his/her business.

The changes to the general DD spells are also nice. I usually don't memorize them because the damage vs mana costs never seemed to make it worth it, but it's nice to know that if I did, it'll go off sooner. On a side note, I do memorize the AOE nuke spells, like Upheaval when I'm in a AOE group. I would love it if the AOE and Undead nuke spells were similiarly changed, if only to maintain their relative value to the general DD changes.

I have to agree with the concern that others have had regarding clerics in the post 60 world. I'm having a much harder time getting into exp group now with other Level 60's. Several classes seem to be able to replace clerics frightening easily. I'm finding that the groups can get away without needing the cleric's Complete Healing spell.

The most damaging to me is the level 60 Shaman's ability to heal more mana effeciently then clerics with their Torpor spell.

The Druid's Protection of the Glades is considered superior to the cleric's Aegolism buff because of it's six point mana regeneration.

Even in the 50 - 59 exp groups, it seems as though a 60 paladin is preferable to a cleric since paladins can ressurect and heal quite effeciently. Paladins possess both AE Heals and Heal over Time spells, although it is one spell circle behind clerics for a given level. Groups then gain another tank with decent damage output.

I'm sorry, I didn't mean for this to go into a rant about clerics. I'm thrilled that someone is at least looking making some some changes to our class, but at this point I'm very concerned that it might be "too little and too late". The other classes are very close to making the high level cleric an extinct class for exp groups. Raiding is the last domain where clerics are still considered to have a vital role.

First off, thank you for actually listening to our suggestions. The top frustration for us has been having to yell and scream to be heard. I think we all prefer this forum. You might even lure Rayne back if this continues

Some comments...

Resurrection
Yes, all the 50+ people worked like crazy to get to get what reses we do have. But I honestly believe this is a good thing. I remember when the nightmare of playing through dungeons and having a tank die. Good example would be Unrest. Nice 15-30 dungeon where the closest zone you can bind is Kaladim or Greater Faydark. Any melee dies you're looking at 10+ mins of downtime. Not to mention the frustration of having lost that exp and possibly begging a cleric for some of it back.

This drastically increases the odds that not only will a lower level cleric (who must compete with Shamen and Druids and their SoW spell) get a group but that they will actually be useful.

This will also encourage people to dungeon crawl. I personally like this.

Here's a radical idea to couple with this... make corpses of people lower than 50 have only 2 hour res timers. This makes it less likely that people will pester clerics half a world away for a res when they can find a lower level cleric to provide one.

Direct Damage

Clerics are the poorest class offensively. The Vision(tm) regarding clerics has always seemed to be "Clerics MUST group for experience, travel, etc" In other words, you need a group just to be able to move around.

Our DD lines have been rather lacking. Clerics have magic based DD spells and nothing but magic based DD spells, our spells have a high recast time and finally we have the shortest primary DD series.

Strike Level 1. 5-9 damage for 12 mana 0.75 efficency
Furor Level 5. 14-19 damage for 20 mana 0.98 efficiency
Smite Level 14. 74-79 damage for 70 mana 1.1 efficiency
Wrath Level 29. 198 - 218 damage for 145 mana 1.5 efficiency
Retribution Level 44. 496 - 519 damage for 240 mana 2.16 efficiency
Reckoning Level 54. 630 - 670 damage for 250 mana 2.7 efficiency
Judgement Level 56. presumably 905-925 damage for 325 mana 2.84 efficiency

There are 2 areas in there where clerics go for 15 levels without a nuke upgrade. I still don't understand that.

Here's my suggestion for the DD line to make it more curved and better spread out

Strike 1. No change
Furor 5. No change
Smite 14. No change
New nuke 24. 120-130 damage for 100 mana 1.3 efficiency
Wrath Level 34. 300 damage for 175 mana 1.7 efficiency
Retribution No Change
Reckoning No Change
Judgement No Change

Fixes the curve for clerics and allows for a steady increase of cleric nuking capabilities.

My next suggestion is to add another resist DD series for clerics. Nothing to outperform our current series but it would allow us some more lattiude. Fire based nukes would work. Without any sort of debuff, we would not be infringing upon druids.

Lastly, stuns... the changes you proposed for Enforced Reverence really miss the point. The issue is not the DD component, it's the fact that clerics at 58 really can't stun much. That has apparently been given to paladins instead. Try casting Enforced Reverence on a variety of mobs with a level 58 cleric and you'll see what I mean.

Instead of nerfing paladins, how about this. Make Enforced Reverence a Lure based stun. Since stuns can be turned off on a per mob basis this would allow us to once again stun mobs. Since it's such a poor nuke (with the damage increase it's still only gets an efficiency of 1.5 plus it has a recycle time of 24 seconds) it also doesn't infringe upon INT caster nuking. I certainly don't think it's unreasonable to think a 58 cleric should be able to stun most of the mobs out there.

I hope there are more changes but, for now, these are awesome.
