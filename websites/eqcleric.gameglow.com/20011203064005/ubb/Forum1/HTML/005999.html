First, I am a regular follower of these message boards but rarely post. I am a level 58 cleric on the Terris Thule server. I enjoy the game a good deal and have been playing it fairly regularly since April 1999.

Now, I kinda wanted to get the sentiment of other 50+ clerics out there on guilds and raiding. I was in a fairly large and powerful guild on my server until about 2 weeks ago. The guild's expectations were that if you are online, you are expected to attend all of the raids. However, I did not follow that tenant to the letter and only attended 2-3 raids a week, since that was about all I could stand to do. We had a parting of the ways, and I have been guildless in recent weeks.

I have spent the last 2 weeks sorting out my feelings on guilds and raiding and I am still not sure where I stand, and wanted to hear other cleric's thoughts. Since being guildless, I have done a couple of raids with other guilds, and I still get rather bored with the whole thing.

Typically for me, a raid consists of begging some poor druid for a port which usually takes a good bit of time, then making my way to the location in question, then waiting a good deal more for everyone else to show up. Next, we buff everyone up, then make our way to the target mob, then we wait a bit, and then buff again. Then several hours after all this started, the raid leader call the attack. Everyone assaults the mob, I throw a heal out, get summoned and die fairly quickly. Then I wait a bit more for a resurrection and if the raid was successful, loots are awarded to the people or put in a guild bank. Now, if the raid was not successful, then it's corpse recovery time, and a couple more hours of buffing and waiting.

I am sure my attitude and my description of raiding seems kinda gloom and doom. Just for whatever reason, the raid feels more like a job and less like an adventuresome game. A raid is a fun diversion from exp groups when not done on a daily basis, but once I start attending them daily, I get that second job feeling overcoming me. I am not sure if what I am missing is a connection to the people on the raid. In my old guild, I always felt the people did not really care too much for me, and I do not really think I honestly cared that much for them either. So maybe I am not overcome with joy when people reap the rewards of raids, because I do not have a real connection with them.

On a lighter note, I did have fun on a mini-raid of sorts the other night. I have a very good friend named Darkpurse on my server. She is a rogue that needed help with her rogue epic. She needed help killing the guy in steamfont the other night and the general in kithicor this coming Saturday. I really did enjoy helping her and felt good seeing here progress.

So, I guess what I would like to ask at this point is, how does one find joy in raids? Am I looking at things in the wrong perspective, or is it just certain classes do not enjoy raids as other classes would?

Raids can definitely lead to burnout. I'm by no means a veteran, but here are a couple of thoughts, for what they're worth.

First, do raids in moderation. Since you've done so many for so long, you could even take a break entirely for a while. I try to spend no more than 25 or 30 percent of my time doing raids, with the rest for normal grouping or whatnot.

Second, find a guild where you genuinely like the people you're with. In my opinion, this is more important than uber-ness. When you're with friends, seeing them get loot is almost as satisfying as getting loot yourself. This makes raiding a lot more fun, since you'll often walk away with nothing for yourself.
