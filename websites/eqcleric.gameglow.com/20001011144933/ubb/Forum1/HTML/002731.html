Gnomes have low starting wis, but that can be made up for in items. Alot of people tend to want to play the "best" race, and while it is still up for debate, gnomes are not usually considered to be in the top 3. Make a gnome. Stats don't really matter, as it all works out in the end.

As far as DE Clerics and quests, well, you can't be a Dark Elf Cleric and be agnostic as well. In order to be a cleric at all you have to pick a god. A cleric is a priest, and it is hard to be a priest and not have a god.

Put as much as you can into Wisdom, and the rest into Strength. Gnomes are the least Wise of the Clerical races, and among the least strong. It will be painful in the long run being one. High Elves can have the highest base Wisdom, but pay for it in Strength. Personally, I think they are the best race. Dwarves are best for Strength and Wisdom though. The Agility is what they put the extra 5 into.

I highly advise putting 25 points into Wisdom no matter what race you chose however. Others will say different though.

Let's not forget one of the other overlooked races for being a cleric: Erudites.

Picked Erudites because they seem to be pretty rare in the priestly category (not many Erudite Pallys out there either). In fact I only know of 3 other Erudite clerics on Cazic Thule. Ran into one in Kunark and he stopped me in my tracks with "Hey! I'm the original Erudite Cleric!". We got a good laugh out of that one

Basically Erudites are on par with High Elves except you have no infravision or exp penalty and can wear anything humans can (like the Aegis of Life ).

But pick a race according to what appeals to YOU. Don't let any of the advice here be the sole reason you pick a particular race. Look around. Talk to people who play one already.

Then pick an Erudite

If you want quests, human cleric is the only way to go, they have more clerical quests than the rest of us combined.

If you want stats, high elf or dwarf is the way to go, depending on whether you want wisdom or strength.

If you want handy abilities, halfling is the way to go, they get both sneak and hide.

If you want the best of all, Tier'Dal, hands down. Because, um... we rule!

But there's no reason not to go with a gnome, starting stats are pretty meaningless once you start picking up magic stuff.
