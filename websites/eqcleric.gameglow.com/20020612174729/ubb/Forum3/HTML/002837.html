I don't know how to word the second reason my guild was there the whole time. I was/am the only cleric in guild to get my epic. It was excitement, the fear of uberguild_01 coming in to kill him, the attitude of showing everyone that we were serious in our camp, and that we were not giving up.
I do know that the cleric that camped RF a couple of spawns after me was the only one in his guild to sit in SF for the week--he'd had RF taken from him once before, and still sat in the zone by himself.
I don't know what kind of guild you are in, but truely, all of us sitting in SF for a week was absolutely great for our morale as a guild. Our younger folks got to hang out with the "ubers", lol. Lots of people got spells. We all got some exp.
I guess what I am saying is that IMO its totally up to you what you do with your time while you are there. You might want to let folks know what server you are on, as every one has different rules.

I started my camp immediately after Ragefire was killed by another guild. I stayed there for 6 days. No one else challenged my camp. I got him on Sunday, June 2. After the patch on June 5, a guildmate cleric went to camp Ragefire. There were three other clerics there. It seems that most clerics will try to get the short, post-patch camp rather than the longer one. It was just a convenient week for me to spend there so I did it. Had some guildmates do the camp for me overnight and I slept well except the night before he spawned. It was getting closer and closer and I was nervous. The first night, I logged when I went to sleep. No one was at the camp when I woke up. The second night I just went afk (the guild had killed Talendor so I felt safe) and was still alone (and alive) in the morning. The next three nights were when guildmates held the camp while I slept. This involved giving my login info to someone else. I am not suggesting that anyone do it. I trust my mates completely and had no problem doing it.

As for knowing the time of death, that doesn't matter as much as knowing the day of death. He doesn't spawn a multiple of 24 hours after a death. It wasn't a multiple of 12 hours either.
