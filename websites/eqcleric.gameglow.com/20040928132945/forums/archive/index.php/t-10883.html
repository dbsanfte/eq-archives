Dis, Dat, and the OTHER Ding about Siren's Grotto.
This contains no maps, as I have yet to see a map of the
zone that actually works, all things considered. Going there
and looking around is the best way to see it….

Critters: [Note that only the Seahorses and the Servant Swordfish
see invis naturally (and perhaps the neriads).]

Enthralleds:
Ulthorks - Easiest, drop Ulthork Tusks for quest in Cobalt Scar
Bulthar - Easy, drops Bulthar Trunk for crappy version of CS quest, more MR
Molkors - much harder, healer, cast all cleric spells inc Complete
Heal. Drops Molkor Hide for Prayer Shawl 7. Very High MR!
All the Enthralleds Drops low level gems, common drop of quest
gems/pretend quest gems, rare drops Netted Kelp armor (good Monk
or int caster gear). They have a TON of HP and hit somewhat hard.

Servant Walrus/Sea Lion/Manatee:
Easy to kill, a ton of HP, no drops of importance. Non-agro until they
see you fighting another resident of SG near them.

Servant Swordfish: See invis, hit hard and fast. Hard to Slow/Mez.
Lots of HP and very Social. Drop the Swordfish Bill, Fresh Fish, and
the important (and no drop) Swordfish Tooth for the Prayer Shawl quest.

Coldspine Seahorse: See invis, hits VERY hard and VERY fast (no, not
dragon hard or anything, but OW). Also hard to slow and mez, very high
MR. Very social. Have several good drops, including the ancient fishing
pole for EB, Ancient Seahorse Hide Cloak, Seahorse Spine Bracelet,
Scales which are just PP loot and Dragonbade Herb (which is No Drop and,
I assume, for the Dragonbane spell quest).

Watery/Icy Servants: The BAD BOYS of the zone. Well, the Watery aren't
bad, but the Icy will just make you hurt all over. Icy do a huge AoE with
500 or so damage, and debuff one spell. They do it a lot. They can also
enrage. The Watery does too, but it only debuffs its target, and not as
often, but it slows too. Add to that real CRAPPY loot and they suck. BUT
at least they are easy to mez. But if you hunt sirens, you will have to kill
them.

Various Neriads - Haven't hunted them enough to know if they see invis.
Don't know much about them, just recall them being Melee only.

Various Sirens - Priestesses are healers, the other two main types (Enticers
and Seductresses) are enchanters. They cast slow ALL THE TIME. They
also charm a LOT (very hard to resist) and nuke too much AND cast nice
4 spell debuffs. Luckily not a lot of HP, but they hit hard. They WILL run
at the end of battle, unlike pretty much everything else in here (The Seahorses
kinda run). They drop a HIGH percentage of quest Chest gems, as well as normal
high value gems (Sapphires, Ruby, etc) and siren parts for baking. They also have
a conch shell horn which is total crap, 2 different runed sea shells (1 with Cloud
and 1 with EB) which are for the Coldain ring quest and the shawl quest
respectively, a Runed Shell Necklace with 5 charges of EB (don't need scales), an
Ornately Runed Shell Necklace for the Sea Dragonscale Bracer quest, and
Immaculate Shell Horn which is a VERY high end Bard Brass instrument (adds
21% to brass IIRC) and Walrus Skin Drums (Percussion, adds about 30%, for
travel spells they are BETTER than Selo's Drums - tested by a Bard friend of mine
and posted by others).

There are several nameds, but in all my time there I have NEVER seen one. Weird
eh? But they Mistress and the other high end named Sirens are supposedly very
much an OW situation…

My personal Location Descriptions:

This is what I call things, don't care what they call them on your server, but
for this to make sense ya gotta read it using mine. (left and right are in reference
to the direction you are facing when you look into the zone from the CS
Zone area.)

CS Zone - area up to and including the one Enthralled that spawns here.

Front Pool - The water just past the CS Zone - kill Seahorses in here for you
Seahorse Spined Bracelet and Ancient Seahorse Hide Cloaks (amazing).

Left Tunnel - The tunnel that goes DOWN to your left when you jump into
the Front Pool from the CS Zone. Probably 4-5 seahorses total by the
time you get to the Tunnels.

Right Tunnel - The one that heads to the right, guarded by 3 Seahorses (split
in 1 group of 1 and 1 of 2) and a wandering Servant Swordfish.

Ledge - if you go through the Right Tunnel and, after killing the mobs there
you can jump on the Ledge (up and behind you once you go through the tunnel).
Mobs in the water leave eventually when you leave the water.

Waterfall Tunnel (dunno, the name works) - The tunnel just to the RIGHT of the
Left Tunnel. Guarded by 2 seahorses outside and 1 inside. NOTE that often the
inside seahorse will be bugged and trapped on one of the 2 spits of lands next
to the Front Pool.

Left/Right sides (using the terms just for this message, don't normally name them) -
The 2 spits of land on either side of the Front Pool. Each has 1 Enthralled and 1
Servant (Manatee/Sea Lion/Walrus).

Bridge - Well, uh, the bridge you see there, and both sides of it. There are about 5-6
Enthralleds up there, 1 is a roamer, the other ones are statics on the left side. There are
2 ways to get there. One is swimming through the Right Tunnel, jumping up on the
land to the back left of you, killing the one enthralled at the top of the ramp and then
going up the land tunnel. You will see the bridge once you turn left. Pull to that spot,
it is very safe. The other way to get on the bridge is left to the more adventurous
reader. I did it this way before I learned the easy way (it’s far more fun). The other
end of the bridge drops into the first pool of water that you hit if you go down the
Left tunnel.

Main Waterfall - The huge one you see from the front. Almost always a bugged
Servant Swordfish trapped at the top or bottom. There is some way that small
characters can get up it, but that's boring and kinda buggy.

Escher's Waterfall - Named because the water flow in the zone is impossible.
This is the waterfall you jump over to leave the Waterfall Tunnel.

Upper Pool - Pointless cept as a place to land when dropping off Escher's Waterfall.
Note that there is 1 Enthralled and 1 Servant just to the right when you drop. The
bugged Servant Swordfish is often seen here too. There is a small spit of water
that heads into the Well too. It has a camp of 2 Servant Swordfish. If you are
hunting the Swordfish Tooth for Shawl step 7 I STRONGLY suggest bringing
a chanter and killing these 2. Nice and easy.

WW Zone - This includes all the area around the top of the well and all the little
back tunnels/maze area. Includes, lesse, 5 Servant Walrus/etc and about 8
Enthralled's, and 1 more of each on the other side of the Upper Pool. Note
that you can often get trained by people doing the SoW and run method of
getting through the zone. Always killed Enthralled's before you mess with the
Servants, cause they Servants are only XP, nothing more, and won't agro unless
you are fighting another Grotto Resident within earshot. One good group can
easily kill all the enthralleds, and start on the servants.

The Well - 2 parts, the Top well contains 2 of the Enthralleds mentioned in the
WW zone above. The Bottom of the Well is a large area filled with a HUGE
amount of sirens, Icy/Watery servants, Enthralleds, and some random others.
SOME people call this the Temple. I don't because there is another area that
I call the temple. If you are hunting Sirens, this is the place for you. More
on this later.

The Tower - When you go down the Right Tunnel, you hit the area with the Tower.
If you go to the Bridge using the method I described and turn right instead
of left when you go up the ramp, you will be IN the Tower. If you are not
invis at this point you will be DEAD. The tower contains at least, hmm, 10
Sirens and 3 Icy/Watery Servants packed in like Sardines. There is a back
exit which heads down into the Tunnels.

The Temple - if you keep swimming past the Tower and down slightly to your
right, you will pass into another large area. Note that any large area of water
will have Coldspine Seahorses who hit hard. And they see invis AND ARE
ALWAYS AGRO. /conning them from the surface will get indifferent, but
they more certainly are not. Anyway, surface in this area and you will be at
what I call the Temple. Another small room packed with about 8-10 Sirens
and several Icy/Watery Servants. VERY hard to pull safely.

The Tunnels - Several ways to get to them. I suggest heading down the Left
Tunnel described above and just keeping left. You will eventually end up
in a long set of both underwater AND above water tunnels. Lots of Enthralleds
and some Icy/Watery servants and sirens/neriads. If you follow the Tunnels
to their end, you will be in a watery room which, when you go up the ramp,
puts you in the back area of the Tower (again, being invis will keep you alive
here). Halfway through there is a tunnel that leads to the Temple.

There are more watery tunnels that I don't wanna try to describe. Just realize
that you CAN get to the water in the Well from the water around the Temple.

Things to bring with you: Some way to breathe underwater and some way
of going invis would be nice. Resist gear vs Magic and Cold are good too.

NOW - if you just wanna get through…

Get Invis, Sow helps. Jump into the water and head to the 'Left Side' I mention
above. Watch out for the 2 seahorses guarding the Waterfall Tunnel entrance.
If you are careful, you can jump into the water BEHIND the two seahorses
and head up the Waterfall Tunnel. They have agroed me ONCE doing this.
If they do agro, just move faster.;-) There is 1 more Seahorse in the tunnel
usually, just swim as far down and to the left as you can past it. Again, if you
agro, just keep swimming fast. DA if you can clerics. Then the tunnel
will head UP and you will find yourself at the top of Escher's Waterfall.
Except on VERY rare occasions the seahorses will just leave now.
Jump off the waterfall (reinvis if needed) and head left out of it, then just keep
left as much as possible to get out. The tunnel you want on your left may
be partially hidden by a large rock, but once you find it the only thing
you will run into are 2 servant Walrus/Sea Lion/Manatee and perhaps 1
wandering Enthralled. Keep left until you zone out. You can be a pansy
and wait for a Succor, but why?

Places to hunt:

CS Zone and the Front Pool. Kill the three Enthralled spawns and the
2 Servant Mammals (ah, better than typing Sea Lion/Walrus/Manatee
all over). Then kill between 5 and 10 Seahorses if you have the
firepower. Note that you CAN kill 2 at a time without mez, but it is
tough. Slow is good too.

Tower - Not worth it, easier places to hunt for sirens.

Bridge - Kill the ones on the bridge, then if you are VERY lucky and
good you can try to get some from the Tower.

Temple - again, hard to hunt. Very packed.

WW Zone - best place for Enthralleds, and therefore Crushed Gems of
all varieties. Netted Kelp a big plus as well as close zone out. Either hunt
right out in front of the well in the open area (watching for the 2 close
statics at the top of the well and the wanderers) or pull to the area in
the tunnel near the WW zone with rocks in the corner. Good
place, far enough away from the Servant Mammals so they don't agro.
Painful on the longer pulls tho.

The Well - Ah, the Well. Getting down isn't too hard, just make sure
that none of the water spawns at the bottom see invis. If they do you
can try 2 different things. 1) is swim all the way down and fight them
as low as possible, avoiding agroing the Sirens. 2) is to stand on the
top of the well, target one of the sirens and stun her (and run back
quickly). Note that this will create a train of between 1 and 6 critters.
If you get more than 3 you best not just have a chanter, but a GOOD one.
Normally only one of the 2 you can see will come up, with support. If you
survive killing those, stun the other one and kill whatever she brings with
her. THEN you can swim down and kill whatever is still in the water.
THIS IS RISKY. Don't try this method unless you have SoW, high HP
and a large tolerance for risk.
Anyway, then invis everyone, swim down and head out into the icy area.
This means you swim to the bottom of the column of water, then swim
over a little, then up. You will be standing on ice when you surface
and have lots of baddies all around. They don't see invis tho. Then
face the large statue you see and turn right 90%. Head to that wall, then turn 90%
again. Head to the corner. Closest thing you see should be 2 Watery/Icy servants
near you at the entrance to a small alcove. As long as you stay away from them
you will be safe, invis or not. Then get your highest puller to start carefully
pulling the sirens you can see. I was warned that the pathing was terrible, but
in reality it wasn't that bad. There are little tricks you will learn and keep
see invis up for various servants, they can be invis. We found that you can
pull the sirens on the weird stair-step thingies by merely walking to the
end of them. Also note that you NEED to snare or kill fast so they don't
run and get you killed.

The Tunnels - you can hunt the tunnels for a wide variety of prey, from a good
amount of Enthralled, to a couple icy/watery servants, neriads, sirens and
whatever else you can find. Most everything. Just make sure you are
careful with AoE spells so you don't agro from above, and keep your head
about you..;-) Don't accidentally walk into the Temple or the Tower
uninvis. That's bad.

That's about it. Siren's Grotto is the best place, bar none, to hunt for
quest gems in the game. Faster drop rate, interesting other drops and
some quest drops, as well as usually being alone (or at most 1-2
other groups). The zone can hold at LEAST 6 strong groups without
anyone poaching, and probably more if they have the guts.

Remember that the zone strips lev off and it cannot be cast inside
either. And always be careful around the seahorses…
