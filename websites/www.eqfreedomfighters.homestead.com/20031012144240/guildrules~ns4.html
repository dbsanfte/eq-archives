Recruitment

Freedom Fighter is undergoing a rebuilding stage and will be allowing members in guild below level cap if they show truth, respect, and honor. Encouragement of grouping with mates is a must and knowing that the guild is limited to what it can do currently needs to be accepted.

Minimum Levels: The minimum level to join is 45 for most classes. The minimum level is 40 for Bards, Clerics, Enchanters, Magicians, Monks, Warriors, Druids, Rogues, Shamans, Rangers and Wizards.

Recruiting Officer: The recruiting officer will receive and review all applications. The recruiting officer has the ability to decline an application if that officer feels that the applicant would not fit into Freedom Fighter standard. The recruiting officer will make sure that all applications are processed and that all invites are handled in a timely manner.

Petition: Prospective members must post a petition to join the guild in
Welcome Hall that says "You ready to be a Freedom Fighter?"
Prospective members must also complete the Application found on the Guild Website. Applicants under the lvl cap, are not required to fill out the online application, at this time. Thank you for your interrest in FF.

Application Process: The application will be reviewed by the guild and positive and negative vouchers will be collected. This can last up to a month depending on play times, groupings, etc. It is recommended that the prospective member group with guild members as much as possible during this time period. After the review period and voting, gathering of vouchers etc., prospective members will be invited into the guild providing that no more than two (2) negative vouchers were given and majority votes. At this time, one (1) alternate character may also be invited into the guild.
Please note; that an invite can take up to 1 month to do depending on play times, vouchers etc. Please be patient through the process and group with as many members as possible to establish friendships and bonds. Once you have applied to guild you are welcome to join guild events and raids for screening process and vouchers.

Invitation: All officers will review the veracity and validity of all vouchers throughout the process. The contacting officer will also keep the prospective member informed as to where the process currently stands. Any officer, after consultation with the Guild Leader and the Recruiting Officer, can initiate a formal invite or denial to the prospective member.
We will try to do invites at guild functions when possible.

Probation: Upon invite the prospective member will be in a 21 day probationary period. During that time the prospective member must attend a minimum of two (2) guild-sponsored raids and group with fellow guildmates as often as possible. Additional positive and negative vouchers will be collected. At any time during this probationary period the prospective member may be guild removed if more than two (2) negative vouchers are collected.

Alternate Characters: After the probationary period is over, additional alternates may join the guild. The main character must announce in guildchat that they have an alternate they wish to have invited. They must announce the alternate character's name in guildchat. An officer will then extend the invitation.

Guild Readmittance: Players who have left the guild in good standing (i.e., non-probationary members who were not guild removed by an officer) may apply for readmittance to the guild. On the first request you will be readmitted with no questions. On the second and subsequent requests you must go through the application process just as new members do.

Exceptions: Exceptions to the above will be made on a case by case basis as determined by the officers. When the officers deem a case is worthy of an exception, the case is put forward to the guild for approval.

Discipline

If a guild member acts in a dishonorable fashion, he/she will be required to give a formal appology to the person or guild involved and suffer a penalty as determined by the officers.  One example of a penalty is to perform community service, thus bringing honor back to the guild, by offering free buffs or by helping a newbie.  Disciplinary action will be discussed and voted on by the officers on a situation by situation basis.

Failure to appologize, or perform the community service will result in immediate guild removal.

Inappropriate outbursts in Guild Chat will not be tolerated. Continued failure to heed warnings from officers will result in a 30 day probation.

Problems between guildmates should be taken to tells, or to an officer.  Having a guildmate on ignore  is grounds for instant guildremoval.

Guild Wars

We do not promote guild wars. However, we will participate in a guildwar for fun.

Power Leveling and Begging

Power leveling is a gift from one person to another. The guild will not power level anyone asking for it in guildchat. Begging for items and coin from ANYONE is not allowed. The guild bank is available to those in need of help.  See the guild bank rules and policies.

Kill Stealing / Camp Stealing / Ninja Looting

Everquest does not tolerate this behavior, and neither does the Freedom Fighters.  If you kill steal by accident you should humbly appologize, show respect and make amends.

Before invading a camp you must do a camp check.  If the camp is open, take it.  If the camp is not open, either move along, or ask to share.  NEVER camp steal.

          Do not hog loots and be respectful of others.  Best rule to go by in a group of 6 loot every 6th kill or establish loot order so there are no misunderstandings. This shows you as an honorable person.

When in the field, you carry the name (Freedom Fighter) above your brow.  You will show honor, truth, might, and respect.  Remember the pride we share with one another, and offer a helping hand when possible.  Going /anon is not allowed on a permanent basis.

Guild Chat

Guild chat is a private form of communication between guildmates. It will not be used as a form of blackmail by any means.  If a guildmate wishes to vent frustrations in guild chat they should feel free to do so without fear of being reported to Verant.  Please be respectful and try to not offend anyone.  Never be afraid to talk in guild chat.  Feel free to turn of guildchat temporarily, but let everyone know that you are. If you have a problem with something that was said in guild chat, speak with an officer immediately.  What is said in guildchat STAYS in guild. Out of respect and honor, you should not in any way, shape, or form share any comments or discussions viewed in guildchat with any persons not in guild.  Making issues public on public boards is grounds for immediate guild removal and will be considered an act against the guild.

Rules and Guidelines of Services to Guildmates

Proper etiquette - ask in guildchat who can perform a service (e.g., port or resurrection) when needed.  Personal tells to a guildmate requesting a service should be kept to a minimum, if done at all.  If you no guildmate  is able to do the service for you at that time, then /shout /ooc /auction for the service. DO NOT repeatedly ask for the service in guildchat.

Remember, guildmates should not feel obligated to provide services when they are trying to achieve their in game goals.

Rules of Quest Help and Etiquette

If you need help doing a quest, you are to request for help on the message board.  Guildmates will not harrass each other when help cannot be provided.  All efforts should be made to insure the help is there and replacements are found as needed.  Spur of the moment quest help can be done, but please note that if not planned before hand you could be setting yourself up for disappointment.

Speak with an officer or raid leader for assistance in quest research, planning, and raid leading.  These requests must be posted.  Remember, planning is the key to the success of your adventure.

Rules / Guidelines to Follow in case of Emergency

Emergency situations can and will occur when the guild is needed to help a guildmate.  Follow these guidelines in case of an emergency.

See who in the zone can help, shout/ooc if necessary.

See who in guild is close to help you.
Determine wheither or not a GM needs to be called and if a guild mate can come be a witness to your situation.

Announce that all options have been considered and a Guild Rescue is necessary.  At that time the guild will see what it can do to help. Sometimes, even when a GM is called, the guild can manage to help while the guildmate waits for a response. If all options have been considered and no solutions found, then guildmates can pull together to answer the emergency call.

Treatment of Fellow Guildmates

Mistreatment of fellow guildmates is considered an act against the guild, and disciplinary action will be taken.

If you have questions of proper game play etiquette, review the
Rules of Conduct provided by VERANT.

Disciplinary action will be by the officers on a situation by situation basis.

Farming of Alliance Mates to join Freedom Fighters is not allowed.  If you are an alliance mate wishing to join Freedom Fighters you must have approval from your guild leader in addition to the standard procedures outlined above.  Special circumstances may be taken into consideration.  Officers will have final say after discussion of the proposed membership with guildleaders, vouchers, and guild.

Rules of Conduct /Guidelines are subjct to change.  If you are asked on message boards or /guildmotd to come and read this page, you are required by guild  to do so.

No Excuses for not knowing guild rules /guidelines will be tolerated.  You are required by guild to view this site for guild information, news, and rules.  Joining Freedom Fighters is acceptance of you following these rules and guidelines and viewing this web site.

Officer Duties

When a situation arises requiring Officer intervention, these procedures are to be followed.

Officers shall consult with all individuals involved to obtain as much information as necessary regarding the situation.

Officers shall discuss the problem and gather ideas, comments, and suggestions on possible resolutions.

If necessary, a vote will be taken to determine a final decision on the matter. Majority vote decides the final decision.

An officers will present the decision to all parties involved.

If the situation concerns the entire guild, the matter will be taken to message boards.  A similar process shall be followed as outlined above.

If for any reason a decision cannot be made in a timely manner the Guild Leader shall have final say and pass judgement.
