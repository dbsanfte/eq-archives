The Rathe Council - When? Why? and Most Importantly, What the Fuck Is Wrong With You?
So tonight we took our first shot at the latest revision of The Rathe Council. We had 80 people. A number I had hoped I would never see again from our first encounter with said number when Emperor Ssra death touched.

Here's a simple question. Since when did this game turn from fun to torture?

It is obvious this designer in particular does not play Everquest. I know this because nobody could design an encounter like this, PLAY at said encounter, and come out (1) Sane and (2) Thinking the encounter was properly tuned.

No, we didn't win tonight. Will we win? Can we win? Yes, I'm sure both of those things will happen. What I have to ask myself, though, is at what price? It was made clear to me by at least 30 of my guildmembers tonight, whether in chat or in tells, that they were no longer enjoying this game. More specifically, this encounter BY ITSELF has completely fucked their entire opinion of the game from beyond disgust to pure, unbridled anger and hatred.

You have to understand, we've been trying to defeat the encounter for MONTHS. Through artificial roadblock after artificial roadblock, we have played ball. We have reluctantly rolled with the punches. We have waited, patiently, as a paying base of customers, for what we were assured would eventually happen. That is, the Rathe encounter would be properly revised and the Plane of Time would be completed by the time we would have reached that stage in the game. So far, despite at least three KNOWN revisions, The Rathe encounter is still far from properly tuned.

First of all, this is just another Pantheon God encounter. One of Four. We already know at least one item that this encounter will assuredly net, the Plate Breastplate, a loot counterpart that drops off the other 3 Pantheon Gods (Fennin - Leather; Xegony - Silk; Coirnav - Chain, Rathe - Plate). We also know it has the usual 20% chance of dropping elemental legs. So the loot is going to be the same shit we get off the other gods. There is the added PITA, with the Rathe, of having to clear Earth A, to key up for Earth B. Then to top all of this off, we get to play with an encounter that is not just mind numbingly boring, but more difficult than all 3 of the other Pantheon encounters combined. Huh? How does this make sense? It doesn't make sense.

The Rathe encounter absolutely must be reworked downward again. There is nothing that can possibly explain how demoralizing it is to have to deal with this encounter in its current form and see three hours of work completely obliterated from a chain reaction of events in mere SECONDS. The fact that this encounter could easily take upwards of three hours, though, is the real kick in the ass. Yes, we are done with those type of encounters, aren't we? No, we're not done by a damn sight. Instead, what we have here is the most god awful and horrible encounter that could ever possibly exist in Luclin x500. Thank you for learning balance. When you create a marathon encounter, you also need to create a method of being able to recover from disaster which does not set you back three hours. Sorry, this is a game, afterall, and a game by definition should illicit HAPPINESS. Instead, now after a Rathe raid, I get to spend half the evening talking people down off ledges and telling them to put their fucking weapons down.

Way to go, guys, you've done it again!

(1) Remove the fucking port. Why is this stupid ability even still there? HOW does it make sense with the revisions that were made? It FUCKING DOES NOT.

(2) Reduce their hit points again by another 30%. There are 12 of them geniuses, get creative.

(3) The resistances of The Rathe need to be brought back down TREMENDOUSLY. I cannot tell you how fucking tedious it is to have to pull these stupid fucking pygmies. Let alone having to deal with enchanters crying about resist after resist after resist after resist on a fucking mob that has had its resistances already debuffed a metric fuckton.

(4) The melee ability of the mezzables simply needs to be toned down. Also, dumbasses, when you state that you don't want a CH chain on a mob, perhaps you should realize that you've also just at least DOUBLED the number of clerics that should be necessary for any fucking encounter, EVER. Yea, you do not need a CH chain to keep up a tank on a Rathe member under 6%, but with Porting and other idiotic happenings, you're going to want to keep at least 2 clerics and a druid behind to handle healing your offtank. The melee ability of the non-mes needs to once again be toned down, and IMO, significantly.

Essentially the encounter should not require (1) More than 72 people (realistically no more than 60 people) and (2) Should not ever take longer than one hour start to finish. These changes cannot wait, they need to go in yesterday.

At this point I pity the guildmember that is not there for flagging when we finally beat this encounter. I have all but assured everyone that we willl never go back again. Great encounter... /clap
