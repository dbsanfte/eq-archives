But beneath his grime covered blond hair, his steel gray eyes sparkled and a smile of future delight partially erased the look of death that usually covered his weather browned face. When he sighted Vixen approaching, his smile broadened and he raised his bloodslimy gauntleted hand in greeting.

Her incessant hunger temporarily sated, Vixen Faen Tlabbar, vampire priestess of Lloth, lay by the side of her huge lover, nibbling on his neck. She purred in Rellikk's ear with evil glee, as she pressed her ebony body close to his,

The vampiress slid up on top of her evil lover and teased, "Oh, most wicked son of Bane, your treachery and cunning is exceeded only by your prowess as a lover." Rellikk's deep growl echoed like thunder on the massive stones, as he pulled Vixen's voluptuous body close, ready for more....

RELLIKK, The Legend of Neverwinter that Bards sing about:

NURGLING 

Nurgling is the Great Lord of Decay who presides over physical corruption, and morbidity. Disease, and putrefaction attract his demons like flies to a rotted corpse. For his amusement he devises foul, and diverse contagions that he inflicts upon the mortals of Neverwinter. Many of the most horrible diseases are Nurgling's proudest creations. Mortals who perish in their grip are claimed by the Plague Lord so that their souls are caught in the eternally bubbling foulness of his realm. It is said that the fate of those who die of the foul disease Nurgling's Rot is to serve the Lord of Decay forever in the most disgusting form of a demon called a Plaguebearer. Huge black flies lay their filthy eggs on these foul demons, and clumps of maggots crawl, and clamber over their putrid hides, feeding upon the putrescent matter that drips from their oozing sores. When the flies hatch, they swarm around the Plaguebearer in a buzzing cloud of vileness, and will turn upon, and attack its enemies.

The Plaguebearer's body is swollen with contagion, and churning innards spill from tears in their rancid skin. It has a single baleful eye, and from its head sprouts a long horn. In its clawed hand the demon carries a sword with a distorted barbed edge. This is the Plagesword of Nurgling whose touch brings disease, and death to mortal creatures.

The gods immense body is bloated with corruption, and exudes a sickly, diseased stench. His skin is greenish, leathery, and necrotic, its surface pock-marked with running sores, swelling boils, and infestations. His inner organs, rank with excremental decay, spill through the ruptured skin to hang like bunches of scrofulous grapes around his girth. From these organs burst tiny demons which chew, and suck upon the nauseous jucies within. This then is the appearance of the KAAOS god Nurgling, though mere words cannot do justice to his truly monumental foulness....
