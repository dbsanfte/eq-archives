  Gaming Utopia: Will it Exist?

  Has anyone ever thought about where MMORPG's are going today? Are they progressing or are they on a downward spiral that will eventually lead to the death of online gaming as we know it?

  I think it is the latter.

  This is a very harsh statement, but I will explain to you now why this "Utopian Game," the pot of gold at the end of every gamers rainbow, will NEVER be born. It is the masses upon masses of IDIOTS and c001 dUd3s that are running rampant in every online game today. It is the droves of preschoolers running around defacing Massive Multi-user Online ROLE-PLAYING Games. I almost think that the TV Raters have a good idea. Rate Video games, anything that requires an Adult rating, should only be played by Mature adults. Sorry Kids, the playground is closed.

   Role-play in today's Online ROLE-PLAYING Games is not existent. This is due to the overwhelming rush of underage gamers. "Role-playing is Gey0r" Is imagination a thing of the past? What people don't understand is that role-play is not a bad thing.. it doesn't take away from the enjoyment of the game. It GREATLY enhances it.

  "I don't like to role-play, why do you want to make me. Go play on a Blue (non-PVP) server if you want to role-play."

  Whenever I hear this statement. I want to grab the person who said it, turn them upside down, and ram their head into the ground until their eyes pop out. This has got to be the most absurd statement anyone playing a MMORPG can ever utter. PvP (Player vs. Player) is dependent upon Role-play, and Role-play is dependent upon PvP. You CANNOT have one without the other. Well.. that is a bold statement.. but the first is true. You cannot have PvP without Role-play. Without role-play.. PvP becomes Pk'ing (Player Killing)

  OH my.. (gasps) I said the "Pee-Kay" word. There is a HUGE difference in the two. They are not the same. I get equally upset when someone calls me a PK'er. I am not a friggin PK'er, I have never been a PK'er, and I will never be a PK'er. For the last 10+ years, I have role-played that same character, the same way, and have done the same things. I kill people because I am evil. I role-play an evil character that thrives on the destruction of everything and everyone around him. One of the other problems I see with games today is that online gaming began under the genre of AD&D (Advanced Dungeon & Dragons). AD&D had set "alignments" that your character followed when created. You could be Good, Neutral, or Evil (with a few sub Alignments in between: Lawful and Chaotic). My character, being the Chaotic Evil God of KAAOS that he is, kills at random just for the joy of killing and collection of souls for the spritzers. >;) In today's games, your opponents have no way of knowing your "Alignment" so they just have to judge you by your actions. If my gnomish Warrior was to walk up to someone, and just whack the crap out of them, Am I a PK'er. YES! Why? Because I attacked you. I am an EVIL person. I will just soon kill you as to look at you. This action does not make me a PK'er. If real-time combat allowed for Role-play (which it doesn't) I would role-play it.. but with the system games have now, there is no possible way to role-play this before the situation occurs. This must transpire AFTER the damage is done. By then it is too late. I am labeled a PK'er.

  This is what people do not understand in games today. Just because I kill you, does not make me a PK'er.. it could be that I am actually role-playing my character the same way I have always been. This brings me to another point..

  With the introduction of Real-Time combat.. the need for a centralized, Server based, Messaging center is more than ever. I could post my role-play stories, my character bios, tales of my other slayings on this board for all to see. People would begin to recognize names and guilds and Role-playing PvPers would have a chance to live again. There are message board upon message boards flung all over the 'net. Hell.. there are more SB message boards than I have ex-girlfriends (and that is A LOT). If a gaming company was to make an area, on their game site, that allowed for Private Guild areas, General Tales, and Guild forums, the sense of community in that game would grow by leaps and bounds. Players would have one place to go to find what town KAAOS overran that day, Who lost the DeathMatch that was fought outside the TheMercs outhouse. People would have one place in which to LIVE the game they play. It would allow those less fortunate, who do not have 23 hours a day to play, to keep up with what was going on in the game. One kicker though... Players would need FREE REIGN over these boards. Flaming, Challenges, Stories. EQ's Whineplay board is a prime example of what NOT to do. "Flame and you will be banned?" What the hell is that. Flaming is part of the game. It is part of human nature. Competitiveness is the KEY to playing a MMORPG. Without this, you might as well unplug the DSL and play it solo. Granted.. we don't need four letter words flying all over the place, any intelligent person can flame without resorting to profanity, but flames make the game more fun, for those flaming and those reading and getting a chuckle out of 'em. To accomplish this would be so easy, I don't understand why NO ONE has even attempted it. You would need 1 staff member to moderate the boards. Hell.. I would VOLUNTEER to do it if a gaming company would put out the effort. I did this for 5+ years in Neverwinter Nights as a staff member. I moderated the entire Message board area. The Public Forums, The Guild Forums, and the Private Sanctioned Guild Forums. It was really not that difficult. Delete a post here and there, issue a warning here and there, but for the most part things went pretty smooth.

  In closing.. I would like to say that there is still apart of me that thinks that maybe someday, my (and most others) idea of a Utopian RPG will come to be. My only fear is that *I* will have to fund the damn thing, and until I can figure out a way to "Pee-Kay" my grandmother, that isn't going to happen :)
