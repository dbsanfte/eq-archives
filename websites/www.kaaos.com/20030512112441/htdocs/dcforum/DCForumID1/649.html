4. "RE: Yes, Questions"
I’m not convinced that PvP needs to be turn-based to be good. Although I certainly think that every combat system will be more tactical in a turn based environment rather than real-time. But turn-based adds a whole set of problems for developers, primarily due to the sheer number of gamers now.

The problem with PvP today is developers are not even trying to make a good PvP game. I sure hope they aren’t trying because if they are trying and still turning out the shit they are turning out they are some sad mother fuckers.

I’ve left an assload of posts regarding how I feel PvP needs to be approached from a development standpoint so I’m not going to rehash this here. If you want to read more on my thoughts about PvP you can search this forum for my posts or you can go to http://www.falseprophecies.com and search for my articles there.

As far as nNWN, the oNWN fan base is actually rather small in the grand scheme of things. Bioware did just fine with nNWN. It’s just not oNWN and it’s not a PvP game. For what it is though, it was a pretty revolutionary product in the RPG genre.

I’m not happy with the third edition AD&D rules. They tried to simplify AD&D in an attempt to increase their market share. The third edition rules are better for pure adventuring, they are shit for PvP though. Wizard of the Coast will be the first ones to tell you as much. They said they realized it’s not balanced for PvP.

AD&D’s second edition rules aren’t even that good for PvP. Melee is not balanced with magic. It’s a much better set of rules than anything else out there though but it starts to get pretty out of whack after level 12 or so. The second edition rules worked nicely in oNWN because there was no melee in PvP and because the highest level you could attain was level 11. It’s pretty funny actually, that the game was never meant to have PvP, that PvP was an accident. It happened to be pretty fucking good because of the fact that it was an accident and because it just so happened that the max level was 11. I’m sure if they had tried to develop the game to have PvP they would have fucked it up.

There’re lessons to be learned there though. Everyone spouts off about ancient history and people shouldn’t dwell on the past. However, history is important, many things can be learned from the past. Sometimes you should look back before you look forward.

Gaming is about revenue, sure. If it wasn’t, there wouldn’t be anybody developing games. That’s not necessarily a bad thing. The problem for us, as gamers, is that the companies producing these games are taking the easy way out. They invest in proven earners. That’s why there are so many clones out there (take a look at the FPS and RTS genre). You won’t find many companies willing to take a risk, not as long as the gamers are gobbling up the crap that they feed them. You can’t blame them though, hell, money makes the world go ‘round. They take risks and they risk taking food off their table.

Whatever, in the end, we all die and nothing else matters anyway.

10. "RE: Yes, Questions"
Well aside from your time investment statements, which are a catch-22, I would agree with what you said. That time investment thing works both ways and when it comes right down to it is a very poor way to measure skill in an online game. Your example of 2-3 kids coming online and beating you simply because you invested 20x the amount of time playing that game is not the right way to look at it. The problem with many of these games is that there are so many time restraints, you have to play X hours to do this and then X more hours to do this and on and on. The time investment factor does not reward skill or strategy at all. All does is reward people with all the time in the world and no lives (normally young kids whose parents are footing the bills). Now if those kids are intelligent, strategic, down-right bad asses then they should not have to play the damn game for 6 months just to be able to compete competitively. Again this is not the view of most of the gamers of today. They seem to like these endless treadmills ala EQ, DAoC, AO2, et al. We want a game where you do not have to invest countless hours playing to attain an avatar of such and such level. In the old NWN you could max your character in literally a couple hours. Of course that did not make you an expert at PvP. All that got you was the level playing field in terms of Hps and spell selection that everyone else had. After that the time investment did become a factor in PvP terms.

The other factor that goes hand in hand with the time constraint is the endless amount of differentiating items that are available. The more items you have in a game the less balanced the PvP system is going to be. You simply cannot plan for a PvP system when you have 1000's of items available for your characters. We messed around with the new NWN for awhile in PvP terms but we restricted the item usage to a set number and types. Too much customization waters down the skill level required for those involved in PvP.

You are hearing from the minority here. If you go ask others on the Net you are going to hear the exact opposite in most cases and that is ultimately what is ruling today's games. The gaming companies are in it to make a buck and thus they cater to the masses.
