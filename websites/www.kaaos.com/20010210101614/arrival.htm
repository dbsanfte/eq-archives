THE ARRIVAL

Sitting upon a large grey stone, an old man looked down at the mass of children, who has gathered around him, who had come to hear one of his tales. A long, knotted grey beard hung down passed the wrinkled skin of his throat, the only hair that could be seen on his head. The old man's eyes could barely be seen behind the thick lenses of his glasses, glasses which lay in the shadows cast by the wide brimmed hat he was wearing.

With the slowest and most subtle of movements, the old man's frail hands opened the cover of a weathered tome. The children murmured amongst themselves, but one glance from the old man silenced them. As the old man began to tell his story, a story of woe and sorrow, of pain and anguish, the sky above them began to cloud over with dark clouds and the ominous sound of thunder could be heard off in the distance.

With a storm brewing around them, the old man continued his tale, and the children listened with rapt attention, and more than a couple of them bit their lips as shivers were sent through their bodies from the old man's words. Nodding to himself before continuing, the old man recounted the scariest story he could think of while beads of sweat began to appear on his leathery brow. For, this story brought fear to all, the old, the young, the strong, the weak.

All feared the horrid tales of KAAOS.

The old man paused in mid verse, leaving the children to their own thoughts as he remembered the tales he, himself, had heard when he was a child. Many thought that these stories were just used to put a scare in children, but the old man knew better. His fingers unconsciously let go of the tome he had held in his hands and they moved to his cheek, tracing a long purple scar which had been concealed in shadow.

As his fingers touched the scar, he heard his father's words echoing in his head and in front of him, he no longer saw the massed children, but instead he saw himself sitting before his father as his father recounted this same tale. He listened now as if he had never heard this story before.

The old man listened to his father telling of how the forces which he called KAAOS had once existed in some far off realm. KAAOS, as they called themselves, was a band of the most twisted and warped that any had ever come in contact with, their power being so strong that they were seen as gods and worshipped as them, as well, his father explained to him in the flashback. When they had broken the spirit of those who had lived in that far off realm, KAAOS and a lesser faction began to struggle for control.

The one faction wished to move on, to continue the way of bloodshed and domination against new peoples instead of staying among the spineless. The other faction wished to stay in the same realm where the people lay cowering already. The forces of KAAOS, having made their decision, left the realm of the weak and moved on to new lands where a challenge existed. The leader, one named Malhavok, had brought the most evil and most vile lot with him, leaving the other faction and its weaker members to go their own way.

Thus, KAAOS was born anew, as the movement ended up in the elders cleaning house and weeding out of the weaker members who didnt have the heart. The other faction, chuckled the father as he spoke to his son, became a ragtag band of the slow of wit and weak of soul, who some have heard of as The Mercs. They always strived their hardest to be like their rival guild KAAOS, but they lacked the strength or the wit to survive such a task, and thus they sank into near anonimity.

His father continued to tell the story, his smile gone from his lips now. While the mercs were always stuck in their own private cesspool of mediocrity, KAAOS flourished. Whereever they went, rivers of blood flowed under their feet. Those who stood before them were crushed and broken, there souls damned to eternal torment at the hands of these Gods of KAAOS.

Then that fateful day came, when the realm that they had conquered was thrown so far into anarchy and chaos, that all of the people were either slain or forced to flee. Thus, that realm ceased to exist and KAAOS was forced to find a new home. A tear began to fall from the corner of the father's eye as he talked to his son.

"My son," he said, "KAAOS has come here. None of these lands are safe. Even Lord British and his followers are no match for them. We now enter into a dark time.. a time of pain. A time of fear. A time of death." The old man was suddenly awoken from his flashback as a child shook his hand violently. The old man tried to speak, tried to form a reply, but all he could do was gasp for breath and clutch his chest. As he fought against the clawing hand of death, his body began to shake in fear as visions of these dark clad Gods of KAAOS shot through his head.

Then abruptly, he fell backwards, sprawled on his back ontop of the rock which he had once sat upon. The book fell to the ground with a dull thud and at that instant lightning flashed across the sky and the storm came. Rain and hail began to pummel the earth, causing the children to flee back to their homes, but no child would ever be the same. No child could ever be the same after listening to the story of the old man and after seeing the utter look of terror and anguish that was written upon his face as his life and soul were taken from his body.

Even as the children were running from the storm's destructive power, a new sound could be heard by those who listened. Amidst the thunder and confusion, a low, amused laughter could be heard.. and everyone who heard it knew where it was coming from. As fear swept through the people, the Gods of KAAOS laughed. KAAOS had finally arrived.
