Rebirth: The making of a GOD

The well was part of the Hell Hole. It, like the fortress, followed the gods to any universe they sat foot. The contents of this well, was a black liquid with the consitency of normal water. But there likeness ends. Each drop of liquid was a soul. A soul harvested by a member of KAAOS. Its taste was death, its fumes where madness. For eons, the well had existed. And for eons, the souls had swam in the waters. Insane and unrecognizable from the person they once where, they only thought of death. The escape from this horrible prison.

The figure that stood gazing into the pools depths, need not fear any of the dangers of the well. He in fact, was an immortal. Just like the rest of those that inhabit this fortress. Today tho, his reasons for using the well were different. Today, he had to find another host.

For centuries, he had skipped from host to host. It started when the mage called forth a great demon from the abyss. His incantations had been correct, and his runes had been perfect. When the fiend appeared, it was bound fully inside the circle of diamond fragments.

"WHO IS THE FOOL THAT AWOKE ME FROM SLUMBER!" the demon questioned commandingly.

With a quick gesture and quickly muttered words, the demon slammed to the ground. Raising to a crawling postion the fiend growled with anger and scanned the room for his assailant.

"Quiet slayve. It is I who have summoned thee, and it is I who shall ask the questions." commanded the bonemage. Altho, answers happend to be the last thing he sought.

The mage began casting spell upon spell of protection. For an hour this went on the demon could do nothing but watch. The magic that held him firm prevented him from doing anything more then speaking.

"Why have you summoned me... master..." The demon spat in contempt when the mage had stopped his marathon casting.

"You see, its quite simple. I've broken my staff and I need a new one." he stated matter-of-factly.

"You see, its quite simple. I've broken my staff and I need a new one." he stated matter-of-factly.

"What in the abyss does that have to do with me?" the demon was more confused then ever.

"Your spine will serve as a fine replacement." The mage said and began casting another spell. The force from the blast rocked the tower and the demon was slammed down once again. Only this time, it was completely froze.

The bonemage strolled thru the protective circle and placed his hand on the demons back and the flesh began to melt away. He could smell the charred flesh in an instant. There. There it was. He grasped the spine and pulled it free with no more effor then to pull a blade of grass from the ground. The demon made not a sound. It simply couldn't.

The spine was crooked and had chunks of flesh hanging from it. Rann straightend the staff and said a few arcane words and the staff became rigid and glowed with a faint red light.

He walked back out of the circle and turned to regard the still form of the fiend.

"You may return to your plane now." he said and spoke the words that would break the summoning and send the demon back to the abyss. That is when things went wrong.

For a split second, the shield was broken as the gate to the abyss opened. And a second was all it took.

The demon ripped one of its on claws off and like a bolt of lighting, flung it towards the mage. It struck just below the heart. Normally this would not be a fatal wound, but the claw began sinking into the bonemages flesh.

Darkness. Pure nothingness.

He finally regained his site to see his former body lying on the floor with the bonestaff in its grasp. Horror filled him. Then turned to hatred. Then, to instinct.

The form rose steadily thru the tower. Up it went until it found its target. An infant. No more the 2 days old. He knew what he had to do, and he had to do it fast. The unlord forced his nothingness into the childs mind. Once in, he devoured the soul of the newborn.

Perhaps if he had known it was his own son, he may have had an instant of regret. But he did not care. He would live. And he would grow.

Those first days had been the worst but over time, the process was refined. He could take a host-child, and will it to grow to adulthood in no more then a week. If pressed, a day. It took power, but not as much as to drain him.

But like anyliving thing, decay and extreme age take their toll. A good host would last perhaps one hundred maybe two hundred years. A weak one, seven or eight. His current host had lasted a mere two. But the mage thought that was fine, considering the lengths at which he pushed the flesh.

Already, the flesh had began to rot. his face was no more then a skull with pieces of putrid flaps of meat hanging from it.

It didn't matter. Tonight he would find a host. Tonight he would feast.

The image of a child appeared in the well.

The mage grinned.

"Tonight, I shall be reborn."

Rann
Bonemage of KAAOS
