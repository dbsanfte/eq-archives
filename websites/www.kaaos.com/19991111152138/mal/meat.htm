Subj: The Meat Puppet Date: 96-02-10 11:29:54 EST From: Hedron

Hedron and his meat puppet, Kaelith, wound their way down the narrow and jagged hallway, more like a split in the living stone than anything created by man.

Hedron's long stride soon takes them back out into a cavernous and more normal hallway (for the Hell Hole), and right up to a small, fire blackened door. Without breaking stride, as the walk up to it, a finger on Hedron's left hand twitches, and the door flies open. They march in.

They march in.

My God, what in all the HELLS do they march into? This one room is some kind of twisted reality. Some kind of gross warped joke played on the essence of life. The Main Pit had been a sight, but this? This was painful for Kaelith to look at. Except he couldn't NOT look at it. There was no light in the room. Not a shred except for what beamed in through the doorway from the flickering torches in the hall.

But somehow, that didn't matter. Even without the light, the contents of this room burned into your brain. The room echoed with such pain and hurt that it needed no light to push itself into your head like a branding iron.

And no blinking of eyes, no covering of faces, no hiding could shut it out!

It didn't seem to bother Hedron. In fact, he didn't even seem to notice. As he walked into the room, the Twisted Cross of the False Prophet of KAAOS rose into the air and beamed a dark and evil light around the room. It silhoueted an evil sleeping form stretched out on a blood stained table. Again without breaking stride, Hedron's foot rose up in a vicious kick, that slammed the table out from under its occupant, crashing it into a wall and splintering half of it into sawdust.

In the same moment, Hedron's voice rang out, "Malhavok, get up you sleeping worthless curr! It's time for my meat puppet's check up!"

This was Malhavok's Lab. Once Doc Krypto's. A legacy of pain and death hung in the room like a thick, oily, evil smoke.

Malhavok's body, of course, was undisturbed by the destruction of the table, or the ear-piercing yell of The False Prophet. He hung there in mid-air as though nothing had happened. With a slow stretch, he turned and sat up, placing his feet on the floor. Seeming to take no notice that there was no table to be sitting on.

"Hedron, anybody ever tell you that you don't sleep enough?" growled out Mal.

Ignoring the comment, Hedron turned and swung the Twisted Cross, embedding one tine of it into the solid stone wall. It flared into brightness, lighting the rest of the horrid room as Hedron plopped down into a chair.

"As expected, I slaughtered the mortal, Kaelith, in a Deathmatch, Mal. And I cast the spells and rituals as you instructed to breathe life into him. All is as you predicted. He serves as a half-aware Zombie, able to initiate his own actions, yet unable to resist my instruction when I give them.

"I have brought him here for you to test the bindings that hold his life in his body, despite the magic of the Deathmatch chamber. And I have brought him here for you to test the binding that hold his will in my grip. Malhavok, will it last a full cycle of the moon as you said before he dies?"

Kaelith the Meat Puppet stood drooling and idle, listening to the words of the Twisted Cross wielder, but unable to resent them.

Mal, with an evil smile, stood and stepped toward the slave. - Hedron False Prophet of KAAOS

Subj: Re: The time is now.... Date: 96-03-23 15:41:16 EST From: ITB Circe

The night air outside the hell hole was filled with its usual foul stench. Only so much blood can slick the walls of a pit like this before the stench starts to show. But the Gods of KAAOS paid it no mind. To them the corrupt smell of death was just the sweet air of home.

Inside, it was the night of gathering. The Gods were arriving in their usual haphazard manner, flashing in from thin air, blasting in through a hole blown in the raw stone, staggering in drunken from the outside, or even just waking up from the floor they had passed out on the night before. Screams of mortals being tortured for pleasure in the background filled the ears for miles around. Any half-sensitive mage could feel the magical presence from across the realms and stayed clear.

THIS my eager readers was the KAAOS Hell Hole. A coming together of evil that cannot be ignored. Cannot be forbidden. Cannot be contained. But you know that. You've all dreamt about being God-like enough to attend and survive.

Tonight was a little different, though. Tonight, Hedron (one of the more ancient and powerful of the Gods) had promised to re-open his infamous "Meat Puppet Theater." But not a word was spoken of who he had wrested a soul from.

The carousing reached epic heights as it does at every Hell Hole. Gobbets of torn out flesh flew through the air, dripping blood on those beneath its path and evil laughter rang from every wall. Magical energies buffeted the Gods as they slammed bolt after bolt of raw power into each other as minor disagreements flared into death struggles.

But this was the way of the immortals.

Still, up to this point, all was as usual. It was in the moment that Hedron, cloaked in black and grey, walked phased into the air near the mouth of the Hell Hole that things became unusual. A quiet... or at least what passes for one in the Hell Hole... descended upon the crowd.

Hedron stood in front of all, capturing the attention of the killers by his silence. A smug little grin tainted his lips.

"LADIES AND GENTELE-IMMORTALS! TONIGHT, AS PROMISED, MEAT PUPPET THEATER WILL BE PUTTING ON A SPECIAL PERFORMANCE! FOR THE AMUSEMENT OF THE GODS AND GODDESSES, I PRESENT----

ITB CIRCE IN HER DEBUT PERFORMANCE OF THE MEAT PUPPET THEATER!!!"

With a flash, Hedron disappeared, and the doors at the mouth of the Hell Hole slowly began to drift open. Darkness lay beyond and courtesy of Hedron's magics, not even the eyes of the immortals could penetrate that darkness. From somewhere inside of it a woman's hand pushed its way out. Grabbing the edge of one door the figure pulled itself into the light.

Tanned and oiled flesh began at the tip of her toes. The Gods' eyes difted slowly up her shapely leg, tracing the curves over her flat belly, over her breasts, and up the face of one of a member of the only guild in the realms to even pretend to compete with the Gods of KAAOS.

Writhing and and slithering to some inner song that none but she could hear, Circe danced her way into the great hall. The torch light shined off the hot oils that had been applied to every inch of her, and her lush body undulated beneath the scraps of clothing that Hedron had applied to her like garnishes on a meal. Her blonde hair was brushed forward, partially hiding her face. This was the only sign of the shame that must have been tormenting her in the inner secret portion of her will that Hedron did not own completely through the ritual of her True Name.

From a dark corner in the back Malhavok, former GM of KAAOS, watched the little scene as Circe's body (as controlled by Hedron) serpentined past the tables of drunken Gods. Hedron mysteriously appeared in even deeper shadows behind Malhavok, unnoticed at first. He leaned forward with a grin.

"Mal, how do you like my little puppet?" whispered Hedron. His expression was gleefull at the thought of what he was making her do... what he would make her do still. His eyes held that sick shine that they always manifested as he walked into his torture rooms.

"She's... suitable, Hedron," whispered the ancient evil back. He paused, diabolical thought racing across his expression. "You must understand I can give you no true opinion without closer observation. Much closer. Perhaps in my lab."

"Certainly, Malhavok. And before the night is done, I can say that she will be available for close observation. She'll be visiting your lab. Of that you should have no doubt." With a tight little smile Hedron leaned back into the shadows and disappeared again.

And the spectacle went on....

the spectacle went down...

And hope died in Circe's breast as she became the plaything of the cruelest being she had ever known.
