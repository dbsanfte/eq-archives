  Mirage moved slowly through his new home, a grim smile crossing his face as he surveyed his new surroundings. The steel grey stone walls, the dark torch-lit hallways, and the shadowy faces of his brothers and sisters welcomed him to his new home. Absently, he touched his forearm and winced as his fingers brushed against the brand that marked him. They had saved his life and now they offered him a place to call home and other beings who would be his family. Looking down at his forearm, Mirage's smile disappeared as he examined the mark of the Bond.

The mark of House ITB.

 

Shrugging off his leathery, silver lined, pitch black cloak, Mirage thought back to this days happenings, and with those thoughts, came doubt. For the last three years, his brothers and sisters of the Bond had sheltered him, taught him, and trained him, his loyalty had never faltered. Mirage was in the Bond and the Bond with in Mirage. Shaking his head slowly, visions began to return to him..

 

...Merculus spoke a quick phrase, spitting out the final syllables of his spell. The grey skinned figure dove aside as the lightning bolt sizzled past his falling body. Mirage rolled back to his feet, speaking the arcane words of protection his brothers had taught him. Taking the great hammer from his belt, he spun towards his attacker.

Merculus laughed dryly at the figure before him. Knowing that this boy was no match for his powers, he sat back and watched. He fingered the weapon in his hands, an almost nonchalant look on his face as his eyes met with the boy who's soul he was about to take.

Speaking slowly, muttering words of power, Mirage drew closer to his attacker, his ignorance shielding from the fear he should be feeling. As he prepared to launch his first offensive, a searing pain ripped through his being, shattering his protective spells, leaving him stunned. Glancing upwards, Mirage saw nothing in front of him, nothing except the shimmering of some sort of magic. Falling to his knees, he fought to turn around, to protect himself from the killing blow that was sure to follow.

Laughing openly now, Merculus grinned from ear to ear as he watched the scarlet lifeblood of the boy in front of him run down his back. A hint of white, the white of bone, between the torn flesh, proved the strength of the KAAOS God. Raising his weapon with ease, he prepared to claim the soul at his hands, in the name of KAAOS.

As Merculus brought his flail high over his head, the twang of a bow, the twang of many bows, halted his killing stroke. Turning he watched a flight of a dozen arrows cascade through the air towards his wide open body. He looked back to his prey, then back to the arrows and sighed. He spoke again, his magic surrounding him, shielding him as arrow after arrow shattered against the invisible barrier.

Growling, Merculus kicked the kneeling form in front of him and spun back to his new threat. Growling again, streams of flame streamed from his fingertips and bolts of pure energy lanced towards his hunters. Knowing that he would be hard-pressed after expending much of his strength, he let with loose all of his power.

Mirage slumped down momentarily, then began struggling to his feet as screams of agony cut through his ear drums. Standing shakily, he saw his attacker massacring six tall figures. The lone figure was taking on six men, and he was only at half strength! Mirage stood in awe of his power, captive to his might, entranced by his strength. Then he saw the two hidden figures moving through the shadows, swords in hand, nearing his attacker. He knew he should flee, that he would quickly be dispatched in his weakened form, but he also realized he should be dead. Very dead. Looking back towards Merculus, envy of his power in his eyes, Mirage attacked.

Merculus wearily laughed at the charred and broken bodies laying in front of him, thinking to himself.. "Shit. Only seven souls? I must be tired." Hearing a scream come from almost right on top of him, he dove headlong out of the way. A blade slashed right where his head would have been, but cut only air.

Laying on his hands and knees, nearly exhausted, Merculus watched his prey move towards these two men. His eyes met Mirage's momentarily and this time, Merculus could not take his eyes off of this boy. He saw such hatred in those eyes, such contempt, such blood-thirst, and.. such Envy. He knew right away that this boy wanted power. Finally breaking free of his daze, Merculus realized that the fight had ended. The boy in stood in front of him, blood spattered all of his face. A puddle of crimson began to gather around his feet from the gaping wound on his back and his knees looked as if they would buckle any moment.

Mirage said nothing as he fell. Then all went dark around him. When he awoke, he was weak, but healed and the KAAOS God, Merculus was no where to be seen.

 

He woke from his dreamlike trance and shook his head. The Bond had claimed how powerful they were, yet with all of his training, Mirage was no match for this God. All he could think about was how much he wanted that power. And with those thoughts, his loyalty to House ITB began to fade.

 

As the months passed, Mirage began to grow distant from those he had called family. He began to train on his own, staying away from the place he used to call home. Somehow he always knew where to find those he sought. He always knew where to find KAAOS and he met with them willingly.

This continued for weeks, until his family began to grow suspicious of him. Mirage knew that the time had come. House ITB had offered him a place to stay, a place to learn, but they could only teach so much. They had not even come close to rivaling the power of those of KAAOS he had met. Mirage knew that his quest for power would always end in the same spot. The KAAOS Hell Hole.

Taking a dagger from belt, he brought the blade to his forearm. Staring directly at the mark of House ITB upon him, he ground the length of the blade into his skin, and snarled as the blade bit into his flesh. Moving the dagger back and forth, blood streaming down his arm and between his fingers, he systematically flayed the marked skin from his arm. Tossing the dagger aside and wrapping a bandage around his forearm, Mirage left his home behind. Left his family behind.

Left the Bond behind.

 

His power grew quickly over the next few weeks, and he was "welcomed" into the KAAOS Hell Hole. Taking on the prefix "Kal" -meaning King and God in high common-, Mirage found out the truth. The truth that his old family had hid from him. The truth that House ITB was weak and that they could never match nor rival the power of KAAOS. Everything they had told him had been bullshit and he felt no remorse about his betrayal. "Let them rot in their cesspool of stagnation and under-achievement," he thought. Shrugging, he strode away, in search of blood.

 

Kal Mirage
The Hammer of KAAOS
