6. "RE: Interesting new gaming vision...."
"it looks like most of us are quickly running out of even slightly original options"

I don't agree with this. As far as the combat system itself has been done, there are a ton of options that either haven't been tried or are ideas that have laid dormant for far too long. The things that you could do with a combat system go so much farther than what DAoC has done with melee (currently the most advanced melee system, imho) or what any game has done with magic.

And then there are overarching ideas that seem so obvious, yet haven't really been done. Up until SB, the concept of taking and owning guild fortresses had been pretty much a dead topic. Lineage had it a bit and LoE had tried to do something like it, but it was an unknown concept to the vast majority of american audiences. EQ and AC were both steps back from UO in fortress taking, since you could actually own houses in UO. Since SB reintroduced this idea, DAoC has blatently stolen it, Dominus online has as well, and most games that are coming without this idea are considered inferior. Ideas exist, many many ideas exist.

Here's one small idea that I have yet to see in a game: Failing quests, with consequences. Too often, you get a quest and then you level up 40 lvls and then go back to the quest. With the ability to fail a quest, you have to do the quest when you get it or else consequences will happen. An example would be something simple like delivering a package. If you brought the package to the destination withing 1/2 an hour, you get rewarded. If you keep the package for more than 1/2 an hour, you get it's contents, but the original giver of the package sends a bounty hunter after you and your faction is screwed.

"I read up on azmo's theory, and it appears that even you didn't find it all that logical a couple of weeks ago."

Well, yes and no. I didn't understand exactly how far to the top he was planning on going when he said top-down development. I took him as going to the very top, but what he was really saying is almost exactly what I was saying. The combat system comes before the world, but not before you have all the parts in place to make a good combat system. Really just bad communication, not a disagreement.


And don't take my criticism of Dominus Online as me wanting it to fail. I'm pretty cinical so I expect it to fail miserably, but I'm praying with everything that I've got that it actually will be a good game. I also didn't notice any new ideas coming from Dominus Online, but I didn't exactly look too hard. I did read that article you linked to, I'm not really seeing great new ideas. They talk about the focus on PvP as if it was something new, but that's exactly what SB has said since the beginning. Dynamic quests have been done in AO. Special attacks for training has been done in DAoC. The skill system sounds a lot like UO, with a small twist (which is a new idea).

Overall, I'm still not impressed and I still see too many holes. It doesn't look promising.
