A BRIEF HISTORY OF KAAOS


Part I: "The Creation"

"In the mind of the Evil One, lurked the blackest of hates..."

The initial concept of KAAOS took form in 1992, on AOL, in Neverwinter Nights. A bored assassin named Malhavok, and an evil companion named Chaosium grew weary of Neverwinter and it's environs. They sought to create a Guild of the purest evil, and a blossoming new land called Yserbius, on The Sierra Network, beckoned them and their creation. But what to call it? Early on, the name 'Guild Of Death' was tossed around. We would call ourselves GOD's. (See? Didn't come from where ya thought. ;p). But that was replaced when the suggestion Killing As An Organized Sport was mentioned. Besides, KAAOS sounded cooler than Guild of Death. ;)

They journeyed to Yserbius with 4 or 5 stalwart friends and set up shop in the Hell Hole. Early on, even before the game went "live", the role-playing citizens of TSN (now known as the financially troubled ImagiNation Network) had but two choices in Guilds: the goodies known as Soul's Brigade, and the evils known as KAAOS. Soul's Brigade arrived first...setting up a large foundation of members, before KAAOS' arrival. There was no "game" yet to speak of, but the role-playing message boards were alive with tales of how Soul's Brigade or KAAOS would rule the new realms. Time passed and Malhavok and his few companions dabbled in the fine art of "member acquisition". The creator of Soul's Brigade, SoulForge, had a grand vision of domination, but his pride, lack of diplomacy skills and ego all played into the hands of KAAOS. First, the Soul's Brigade's AGMs saw the glory of KAAOS, then the other members became enlightened. Ulitmately, a mere week before The Shadow of Yserbius went live, Soul's Brigade disbanded and KAAOS grew strong, taking only the most select members.

Many guilds claimed to have been the "first" guild in TSN's Shadow of Yserbius, including many stragglers from the defunct Soul's Brigade (now a "Soul-less" Brigade). Believe me when I tell you, from a purely historical view, KAAOS was the *1st* fully formed, organized Guild to set foot into TSOY. Malhavok and Chaosium personally made the very *1st* non-staff posts on the message boards inside the game. Take it to the bank.

PART II:  "KAAOS worship begins"

Time passed on TSOY and KAAOS grew. PVP skill, and getting the best weapons were the main ways to show guild dominance in those days. KAAOS had plenty of both (as usual ;>). Members came and went as they pleased, and achieved all in the name of KAAOS. Tuesday night Hell Hole meetings were loads of fun and KAAOS was active. The infamous "Say something Evil!" initiation process insured only the truly worthy were accepted into the ranks of KAAOS. Other guilds formed...Aegis, TheMercs, the Bhall's, KOY (TSOY's version of COR), and others all took up residence in Yserbius and one year of battles, bragging, adventuring and rp fun passed quickly. The entity known as KAAOS thrived.

But soon, after only 1 year, many grew tired of Yserbius' limited gameplay and poor interface. An upgrade appeared on the horizon, but Malhavok longed for the better combat/PVP engine and superior quality of AOL's Neverwinter Nights. There was no real "area" for Guilds to announce themselves and become sanctioned on TSN (and there STILL isn't today) and no message board area to call your own. It was pretty damn lame (and still is). However, Yserbius was much cheaper than AOL in those days. He decided to wait for the upgrade and see if it would disuade him from returning back to Neverwinter.

It was during this period of time, KAAOS went from 30 members down to 18, and only half of them were active. The game was boring them into inactivity. Malhavok knew something must be done quickly to save his creation, and assured everyone better times were ahead. The 1st "upgrade" to Yserbius came out amid little fanfare, and was a major disappointment. Few interface improvements, no new classes, and worst of all...PVP was BANNED. That was the last straw. Mal was heading back to Neverwinter.

Mal announced his intentions and found many were willing to move KAAOS to Neverwinter, but many who had staked their claim to Yserbius, were NOT. In a bold move, worthy of legend, Malhavok usurped the Throne of KAAOS and announced KAAOS would be moving to Neverwinter. What transpired was a wonderfully bitter battle! Two divided factions formed. Those siding with Malhavok, and those wanting to stay in Yserbius. Mal had carefully hand-picked those he would take to Neverwinter. Of the 19 members, Mal took the 10 most active: Merculus, Dark Anjel, War Lance, Doomers, Feebinator, and others took the KAAOS war machine to Neverwinter Nights and set up the Hell Hole.

Part III:  "KAAOS takes over Neverwinter Nights - Fucking Wheeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeee!!"

KAAOS started as a non-sanctioned guild and struggled to make a name for itself in Neverwinter. Malhavok's reputation from his former presence in NW preceeded him, and he quickly developed a large following of old time, veteran NWN'ers. A mere 5 months after applying for non-sanctioned guild acceptance, KAAOS was awarded Sanctioned Guild of Neverwinter status and was well on it's way into the Neverwinter history books. While KAAOS thrived in Neverwinter, tales of the remnants of KAAOS on Yserbius reached Malhavok's ears. A weak and liberal recruitment process? No more Hell Hole meetings?! No more live evil sayings?!? No more KAAOS DeathHunts?!?! RULES and LAWS for KAAOS?!? WHAT the hell was going on!??! Malhavok was not amused. He severed all ties with Yserbius's KAAOS and thought no more of those who had lost the TRUE spirit of KAAOS.

Today in Neverwinter, KAAOS continues kicking ass. In Neverwinter, Player Vs. Player combat is a game within itself, and requires not only luck and the best equipment, but a strategy aspect worthy of anyone willing to take the time to learn the finer points. As in the old days when Doomers, Merculus, Chaosium, War Lance and Malhavok struck fear into the hearts of Yserbius, the names of Rellikk, Slayve, Doc Krypto, Stilborn, Mad Drunk, Kal Mirage, Xardak, P1rate and Hedron do the same to Neverwinter.

When Neverwinter ran Quests 2 or 3 times a year, the newly formed KAAOS ruled. Taking 2nd place in their first realms-wide quest, KAAOS went on to dominate the next 4 realms-wide Quests and take 1st place! KAAOS won every Quest for a year straight. Soon after KAAOS' stranglehold on these events, realms-wide Quests started becoming rarer and rarer, down to only 1 a year. (Some say, the constant dominance of KAAOS caused the powers that be to stop sponsoring Quests altogether. Hehehe! ;>).

With Quests no longer occupying folks' attention, PVP Tournaments soon became all the rage. Now with 4 major PVP ladders, one look at the Trophy Room of the Gods, and you'll see the tradition of KAAOS excellence remains intact as the Gods and Goddesses rule nearly every one. With over *9* PVP Champions (the most by FAR over any other guild) KAAOS seeks to accept only the most skilled and dedicated players. Testimony to this, KAAOS recently won a Neverwinter-wide Guild PVP Championship, a tournament where 20 other Guilds' teams pitted their PVP prowess against each other in battles to the death to determine the best. KAAOS not only won the Championship, but remained *undefeated* throughout the entire tournament!

!!!(Worship it)!!!

Part IV:  "The Future of the Gods"

Sir Rellikk now leads the Gods and Goddesses of KAAOS into prosperous existance. Having retired as GM, Malhavok now dedicates much of his time to insuring the recruitment process of KAAOS remains intact and the guild's reputation as the "Ultimate Evil in Neverwinter" remains strong. Tuesday night Hell Hole's are still a highlight and a tradition, where soul spritzers, troll weed and the promise of "LIVE EVIL!" bring as many as 20 KAAOS Gods and 10 worshippers (a.k.a. KAAOS wanna-be's), to the private room where KAAOS holds court.

Now, Neverwinter is gone...crushed by corporate America. Rest assured the Gods of KAAOS will continue.  Wherever they decide to next reign, they will go in full force.
