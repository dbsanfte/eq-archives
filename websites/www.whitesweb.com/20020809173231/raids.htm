RAID RULES

Organization: (Answer1: when a guild raid fails it is usually due to poor organization)

 

  1. Leadership: The will be one and ONLY one raid leader per raid. The raid leader is pre chosen before the raid is scheduled. DO NOT follow anyone else besides the designated raid leader as this could lead to getting the guild killed. DO NOT suggest alternate directions or places in group or guild chat during raid as this can distract guild members and lead to getting us killed, if you have a suggestion send it in a tell to the raid leader. Before a raid leader can lead a raid he/she must have a printed copy of the area we are raiding and advanced knowledge of the monsters to be confronted. If leading a raid say MOVING in guild chat before moving and say TANKS in guild chat when you see mobs that need to be killed.

  2. Following: Follow ONLY the pre chosen raid leader. There are two main guild orders issued by the raid leader. 1st Before movement from sitting the raid leader will say MOVING in guild chat, at that point you stand immediately and follow. 2nd when the raid leader says TANKS in guild chat all tanks move forward and initiate combat. DO NOT make suggestions to go in a different route or fight different mobs in guild or group chat, instead send a tell to the raid leader. Stay close to the group(s) at all times. DO NOT wander off, check on mobs, make pulls, or look for camps unless asked to by the raid leader.

  3. Movement: Everyone follows behind the raid leader. THE ORDER IS, Raid Leader, Tanks (all melee classes), Healers, Evacs & Mez/Mana (clerics, druids, bards & enchanters), Nukers (wizards, necromancers, magicians)

  5. Grouping: When using multiple groups each group will have a leader who will be a tank if possible. The group follows the group leader and the group leader follows the raid leader staying in the order described above in movement.

Looting: (Answer2: this can ruin the raid after mobs are dead if not done properly)

 

  1. ML Rules (Master Looter): Cash off mobs, non magical items and non quest items are all ML<Master Looter> loot. During the raid each group will have a designated looter. At the end of the raid all items and cash looted we be divided up by the Master Looters and split between all participating members left at end of raid. If you leave early, disband, or log out before loot is split you forfeit your share of the loot and it gets split among remaining members <people who go LD will be waited on>. If you absolutely have to leave during a raid and want your share of loot or a specific item we will either roll for the item you are interested in then or give you a small share of loot dropped. During a raid please loot with split off.

  2. Drops Over 1000pp: All magical drops and quest items worth over 1000 platinum pieces will be rolled for (/random 0 100) OR distributed by the raid leader. General protocol for distribution is, the highest level member that needs the item of the items usable classes will be rewarded first. If there are multiple "highest" level members that are the same level of the items usable classes those members roll (/random 0 100) and the highest number is rewarded the item. (example: if the items usable classes are WAR, SHD, PAL and the highest level in our raid out of those classes is our 55 PAL and 55 SHD, the 55 PAL and 55 SHD would roll off for the item.)

  3. Drops Under 1000pp: All magical drops and quest items worth under 1000 platinum pieces will be rolled (/random 0 100) for by all participating members.

  4. Treasury: Although this rarely happens. If we get a very good class specific item or quest drop and we have no member present of the usable class it will be held in guild vault for the highest level guild member of that items usable class.

Dragon Raids

Dragon Raid Looting System: Our guild does not use the DKP system (dragon kill point)

The Term Dragon Raid also includes other high level (55 to 70) mobs.

Our guild uses "highest level priority" for these raids. 

Any item dropping from these mobs goes to the highest level participating member that can use the item and hasn't already received one on a guild raid. If multiple people tie in levels, the member who has been in guild longest gets the item. If the highest level member has already received the same item on a guild raid he/she is out of consideration for this item and it is passed down to the next highest level. If the Guildmaster is present he will distribute the items and this system is to be ignored.

Specific Person Raids

Specific Person Raids are when we all take a member of our guild to get a specific item.

These quests include Epic Weapon Quests

If we are helping someone with their epic then expect to die, we are not there for loot or exp. We are only there to help that special person get their epic and sometimes it requires sacrifices.
