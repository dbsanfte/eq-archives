Acumen Mask

This quest originates by talking to Vilissia within the Temple of Solusek Ro.

Vilissia says 'The Acumen Mask, as its name suggests, confers to its wearer certain
powers of insight and knowledge.

Vilissia says 'I can make for you an Acumen Mask, but you will need to bring me the
proper components. I will need a Glowing Mask from a Skeletal Monk in the Caverns of
Guk, A Patch of Shadow from our mortal enemies the Shadowed Men, a Darkbone Skull
from a Darkbone Skeleton in the Estate of Unrest and a Bonechipped Mask from a Goblin
Headmaster in the Ocean of Tears.

Patch of Shadow- this is dropped by Shadowmen (roughly level 25 -30), the place I hunted
them to get it was in west commons in the corner of the zone wall, if you follow the right
zone wall from Kithicor when facing outwards. you will only see their name and the
weapons they carry because they are transparent. (Get this item last. All Shadowmen
items are NO RENT which means they will dissapear from your inventory once you log out.)

Darkbone Skull- found off of a GREATER darkbone skeleton (roughly level 27-28) in
Unrest. Note- I went around blasting normals for an hour then killed a greater in a train
and it had it :P (Many of these spawn near the bloodpool and the Ghost in the basement.)

Glowing mask- this is found in Upper Guk, NOT Unrest. If you go towards the main lower
guk zone there is the place that you cross a plank (it is also filled with Heart Spiders),
jump in the water and search around for a hidden room. Within that room you will find
Tempus, a Dark Elf Necromancer (roughly level 25 -26). The Skeleton Monk (roughly level
24-25) with the mask will be next to him (if you attack the monk, the necro and his pet will
join in). I have been told by some that the Necro will not join but he will attempt to heal
the Skeletal Monk. (note:Glowing Mask has been found upon the Scryer in Upper Guk and
did work as a component for the quest) (If you cross the wooden plank in
the Spider room, look to your left and you will see a ladder that leads to a ledge
and another below that leads from the ledge to the water. Swim to the very bottom there
and follow the wall to the right, you will find the room up in an 'air pocket' there.)

Bonechipped Mask- This is dropped by the Goblin Headmaster (roughly level 24-25) on the
Isle Goblin island in the Ocean of Tears. This is the same island the Oracle is on (known to
some as the Oracle Island). If you still don't know where this is, keep following the pier on
the Sisters' island and swim out into the ocean until you see the island. The Headmaster
spawns in front of a small hut and drops the mask fairly often (2 out of 6 times was my
experience). Verant was furthermore merciful - he spawns every 5 to 10 minutes. He has
approximately 900hp. The shamans that spawn near him go down easily, it might be a
good idea to take them out first. (Follow the water's edge around the Goblin island until
you see a stone ruin. There is a Goblin hut near that ruin and the Headmaster spawns in
front of that hut.)

Nostrolo Tambourine

Nostrolo Tambourine Quest Walkthrough
Quest Level
This quest may be started as a level 1 bard, simply gather all components from other players (none are NO DROP) and catch a ride to Fironie Vie to talk to the banker. If you wanted to quest this yourself, it is feasible around level 35. Some coins I would suggest getting a group together.


Quest Walkthrough
Talk to Wellford Fargin, the banker in Firiona Vie, and ask him about his hobby. He will tell you that he used to collect rare coins and gems. If you say that you will help him, he wants you to first replace his prized Star of Odus which was stolen from him.

Here's how the conversation goes:
You say, "Hail Wellford Fargin"
Wellford Fargin turns his frown around and begins to smile. "Oh! Hello. It has been quite some time since someone has been polite and greeted me. Most adventurers are quite rude. To think I gave up my [hobby] to work day and night here!!"
You say, "What hobby?"
Wellford Fargin says "I used to collect rare coins and gems. I came to Fironia Vie to do just that, but I never have the time to [collect rare coins] when I am always here working."
You say, "I can collect rare coins."
Wellford Fargin says "If you want to help, you can begin by replacing my prized Star of Odus which was taken from my vault when I stepped out to dine. Do so, and I may trust you to collect the coins. I will also reward you with an item a composer left here."
You may easily purchase the Star of Odus for around 105pp from a few of the jewelers in a jewel shop in Erudin. Come back to Fironia Vie and hand the Star of Odus to Wellford and he will hand you back the "Tin Box", which has 6 empty slots. Be sure you have an empty slot because this box requires a slot all by itself. He will then tell you about the other six coins he has been looking for.


Identifying your coins
Start collecting these coins in various parts of Kunark. They will all say "An Ancient Coin", so use your Level 14 song Lyssa's Cataloging Libretto to correctly identify your coins. Simply pick up one of the coins with your mouse cursor, and play the song. You will see a message identifying the coin in hand. These coins are not LORE and NO DROP, so this means you can auction for them, and may hold multiple coins of each.


Coin Values
Be sure to say "Looking for ancient coins", as many people will just give them to you for free. Some people might be greedy and will want to charge 100-200pp for them. Hopefully you'll get most of your coins for free, and pay only 30-40pp for the rest. The Kaesora coin might be a bit expensive, I paid 100pp. It really all depends on how much you want to pay, and if you can wait for a good deal.


The 6 Ancient Coins
Ik Gran - drops off the Captain of the Guard in the City of Mist
Terraz Ton - drops off the Failed Raider in Kaesora
Danak Gran - drops off the Scrounger Scorpion in the Overthere
Veksar Gran - drops off the Bloodgill Goblins in the Lake the Ill Omen
Royal Crown of Dalnir - drops off the Kly Implicator in Dalnir
Torsis Ton - foraged coin in the Emerald Jungle
Completing the Quest
Once you have obtained all six items, put them in the tin box and hit combine. It will change into a chest and if you hand this back to Wellford, he will reward you with the Nostrolo Tambourine. This instrument will add about +25% to percussion songs. It is a nifty new percussion instrument that actually looks like a tambourine when held. Unfortunately, we still strum it like a lute.






Obtaining the 6 Coins
Ik Gran Coin - drops off the Captain of the Guard in the City of Mist
The Captain of the Guard is a high 30's undead skeleton that can spawn nearly anywhere in the first half of the City of Mist zone, but pops most frequently at the bridge gate or in the courtyard, and sometimes the temple. He seems to drop the coin every time. Just hang out at the zone or /ooc asking for it, usually no one needs the coin. - (Info submitted by Swyshing)


Terraz Ton Coin - drops off the Failed Raider in Kaesora
This coin seems to be the hardest coin to get.

The failed raider is a random spawn in the Kaesora Library. If there are people in the library, they usually let you have to coin if they don't have a bard with them. You have to kill everything in the library and the failed raider will spawn eventually. While I was there, he spawned 1 time in a 3 hours period of time. - (Info submitted by Tirade Bunzofsteel)


Danak Gran Coin - drops off the Scrounger Scorpion in the Overthere
Grab a group and descend down the canyon. Stay near the canyon entrance. Start killing Scorpious, and eventually a scrounger will spawn and they always drop a coin. These scorpious will start out with a Harm Touch which seems to be unresistable which will bring you down a few bubbles. If you want to solo, I also hear it is effective to charm two so they use their HT on each other. There may be more scroungers near the temples, but it might be hard to grab just one and kite it out to safety.

Torsis Ton Coin - foraged coin in the Emerald Jungle
This is one of the items that may be foraged in the Emerald Jungle Zone. It will be very hard (but possible) for you to forage it yourself as a bard. There are a few options for you to obtain this coin:
Have a friendly druid agree to teleport you into Emerald Jungle (you must be in a Kunark dungeon for the druid to evac you here). Then, the two of you just forage until you get the coin. It might take some time. In my case, it took a 45 druid an hour and a half to forage one coin.
Create a young Iksar, run to this zone, and start foraging. Many bards have done this successfully and have gotten their coin within minutes.
Run to the zone yourself. Try and forage up the coin yourself. It has been reported that a bard has indeed foraged up this coin, so if you have time to kill, you might want to try yourself. It will just take longer, as you'll get many failed forages... Emerald Jungle is a deserted place many times, just people teleporting in to get to The City of Mist, but if you notice a druid or ranger in the zone, you can ask them to help you forage. Be careful in this zone though, there are level 35-45 mobs that will hunt you. Emerald Jungle is alot like Jurassic Park in that respect. Most mobs cannot see through Invisibility or Superior Camo, except for the undead skeletons.

Incandescent Wand

This is one of the three Incandescent items your can get from Sultin in the library. (You can also get the Rod of Insidious Glamor quest from him.) Stats: 2 Dex, 5 SV Magic, weight= 1 stone. Effect: Color Shift. Procs at 20, which is when you get the spell anyway.

I recommend doing all 3 quests at once, and bring along your buddies if you're like me, level 30. I brought a 51 Warrior (or she brought me, I still can't tell.)

For the wand, though, Sultin wants 2 things: a pouch of silver dust and a fully enchanted wand.

The wand drops from a Silvered Guard in the Temple of Cazic Thule. There are only two of them, and they guard the stairs that lead to the the mystical hole thingy. They spawn about every half hour. Invisibility is your friend! We (ok the Warrior) killed six before the wand dropped.

Take your wand to Esmirella in the North Karana gypsy camp. Give her the wand and 50gp and she will enchant it for you. She then tells you to take the wand to Raine Beteria in the Erudin Library to get it "fully" enchanted.

If you're lucky, you can get a port to Toxx from the NK. Run north, cross the bridge, run north some more, and voila, Erudin. I had to cast Illusion: Erudin to get a hugh enough faction for her to talk to me. Make sure she replies when you hail her. Repeat the process you followed to enchant the wand the first time: give her the wand and 50gp. She gives you the "Fully Enchanted" wand.

The pouch of silver dust is dropped from Dusty Werebats in the Estate of Unrest. They are in the basement. Inside the house, head left, take the first left door and turn right. There should be a bookcase. Click on it. It opens the secret door to the basement! My Warrior brought out 4 bats and we got the pouch.

Take the wand and the pouch back to Sultin in Solusek Ro (along with all the items for the other two incandescent items if you were bright), hand them over, and you get the Incandescent Wand. Ta da!

The other two can each be purchased for 50 golden coins each - one from Mizr N`Mar in the Neriak City Library and the other from Madam Serena in Qeynos.

Illusion Iskar Spell

This is the quest you need to get the level 20 enchanter spell Illusion Iksar. The items required are:

-Powder of Nok (from froglok nok shamans north of the docks in Firiona Vie)
-Heart of Ice (iceboned skeletons in Lake of Ill Omen)
-Sabertooth Mane (sabertooth tigers in Lake of Ill Omen)
-Ton Warrior Totem (froglok ton warriors in the froglok city in the Swamp of No Hope. Fairly rare spawn).

Return all four items to Luminare in the enchanter section of Firiona Vie (the part where you buy the other enchanter spells) for the spell Illusion: Iksar.
