Built-in Quest  XP Shortcuts

**There is no skill increase that would usually occur while fighting, and melee.

This can hamper a character’s fighting prowess. A chanter with haste can usually overcome this.  A caster must cast his 1st lvl spells until he reaches a proficiency where he doesn’t fizzle all the time. 

•  You can do the Erud’s Tonic Quest in Erudin for a little xp at newbie level.  

•  Cb Belts are easy to obtain and can jump a characters up levels at a time.  Turn them in in Kaladim.

•  Greater Light stone quests, in oasis gypsy camp.  Buy GLS’s en masse at 7 pp each, turn in.  You will be awarded with xp and with a Tome of Research you can sell back for a little more than 7pp (charisma a factor).

 

CLERICS

STRENGTHS:

•  AC Buffs – “Armor of” Series

•  HP Buffs – “Symbol” Series (costs a Gemstone)

•  Combo Buffs – “AC & HP” Buffs

•  “Healing” – Masters of Healing

 

WEAKNESS:

•  No Damage Shield – plain and simple

 

COMPENSATE BY:

•  Full HP Buff, gives you more slack to heal with

•  You will Heal more, you have no choice

•  Ask/pay any level druid/chanter/wizard with a damage shield to join you for a bit

-Druid do not have to be high level –They get first shield at 9th

•  Group your subject with a druid (9th or higher)

•  With higher level casters get a tank to group with them and instruct them to

nuke at 40% health

•  Try to focus on healing one individual, less work and maximum return

•  baby root mobs which come close to camp, for perspective pulling

 

Target Subject Classes

-Any class, levels 1 - 12

- Past 12th,  Melee-ers of Any type –Strongest with higher level melee-ers

- Past 12th,  Casters can be helped by rooting Mobs and healing and group with 1 Meleer only

DRUIDS

The Art of the Damage Shield (and mana efficiency)

•  Keep a damage shield on tank/subject at all times

•  Tell them to agro as much as they can to be more mana efficient

•  They do not need to hit as much as they need to get hit.

•  This tool allows them to finish Mobs en masse.

•  They must learn to use Agro radius for them, instead of the normal tendency to avoid it.

•  The subject must make all kills count, don’t let them get away

•  Must develop a technique to spot finish off mobs which are about to break and run.

  

Pets and Power Level ing

•  Use pets, buff them and damage shield them

•  Pet’s eat 50% of the xp if they do more than 50% of the damage.

(So basically Your subject will spend 50% xp no matter what)

•  When grouped, a pet only eats 1% of xp no matter what.

•  If they are losing 50% xp anyway make it work for your subject, make them use the pet and group with one melee-er, you get double the damage output verses the same xp cost.  You gain the ability to keel more stuff!  Use it.

•  Do not be afraid to allow subject to Fear kite, but throw in a stun

or two to allow the tank to catch up with it for hits.

 

Healing while Power Leveling

•  ALWAYS attempt to fight Animals, they do not agro the healer no matter how much the subject is healed.  This is our boon!

•  Anything else you will agro, use baby Root to root mobs or ignore there blows

if you have to heal a lot.

 

At higher levels Power leveling is a pain, why?

 

Level 1- 20 are relatively easy if you have a damage shield.

•  You are in control because the mobs are insignificant.

 

After 20 it becomes increasingly harder:

•  Mob hit points start to increase

•  Mob damage starts to increase

•  Mobs become more lethal to the subject in numbers

•  More mana usage, Meelers are easier if they have HP buffs:

•  Simply put, it cost nothing to swing your arms.

• Just heal them

•  Buy a pillow of meditation, cause you’re gonna be on your butt a lot!

 

Casters are the hardest:

•  Plain and simple it cost mana from their smaller mana pool to kill stuff

•  Expect downtime with casters

•  Group them with one lucky melee-er and PL them both, it is more efficient than abiding by the built-in downtime rule and you won’t be as bored.

•  Casters are hard to level, but not impossible.

 

When can they group with me and we just fight together?

 

Approximately 1.5 times their level (rounding down) is who they can group with.

30th cleric can group with a 45th tank.

20th druid can group with a 30th tank.

40th Wizard can group with a 60th tank

 

Q: Do they get super good Xp distribution?

A: Varies, but it beats just sitting on your thumbs.

Controlling Your Subjects

•  Follow them around, and make sure they can survive the encounters.

 

•  When Power leveling maintain control at all times, be aware of mobs and other players

 

•  Do not let your subject KS, this will become a problem entirely by accident sometimes.

 

•  You are responsible for this individual’s life/death and behaviors.

 

•  Instruct them when it’s okay to continue and keep them aware of your mana usage.

 

•  If your subject has a pet and keeps bugging you cause he is out of mana, tell him your are in control and will heal the pet. Tell them worry, when you are oom. You must assume he always has no mana, and treat it accordingly.

 

•  If the subject does not listen, LEAVE.  You do not owe anyone anything. Do not feel obligated.  This is a gift, and if you are abused or unappreciated leave. You have no one to blame but yourself if you stay.  Tell them you may or may not continue at a later time, that will put them on their best behavior.

 

•  If you have invited a tank to group with your caster, and they are not following instructions disband them and find another.  Trust me, someone will follow instructions to the letter in exchange for power leveling.
