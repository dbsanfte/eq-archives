Guild Charter


The Elder Ones:

The Beginning:

When I started to play Everquest in the Beta 3 everyone in the game helped each other. Questions were answered, information was shared. They rotated kills and loot, they would heal and buff you. I enjoyed playing in Everquest a lot back then and when beta 4 came too. When the Final edition was released I got it as soon as possible and started too play again. The game is everything it was it was in beta and more, but somehow the way people play the roles has changed dramatically. They don't care about each other, steal kills, won't heal when needed. The players would rather let you die to get the experience than help you. The players are starting to steal kills rather than rotate on kills. I find this a great loss since I not only liked the game but the way it was played by most people too.

The Idea:

Gladly I found some people that felt the same way as I did and my hope started too rise again. There's still good people out there. People whom Do help, heal, buff, and answer questions. Playing with these people, fighting along side them brought some honor back into the game. This is how the idea to make a guild started. A guild with people, who just like me, don't like the 8year old mentality of play that 90% of the people do at this moment. We want a guild with the honor to help, answer questions, and to share any information that we may have.

The Guild:

So here it is! The Elder Ones! A group of people, that became close friends while playing together in Everquest. All think that the way the game is played now by so many people is not the right one. Our objective is as follows:

1. To try and restore some of the lost honor
2. To answer any questions that others may have
3. Heal where necessary
4. Help in a battle where necessary to safe a life
5. To convince others to play the same way

In Game:

In the game "The Elder Ones" will be seen as a group of close friends, who put his or her life at risk too save that of someone else's. People that always try too help everyone were they can in every way possible. Who put pleasure of "role" playing above gaining levels and money. It will be a small guild: a group of close friends that all know each other and play together, rather then some big guild of 100+ people where 80% of participants don't know each other. No ranks: we don't want to make an army. We want to be a group of people sharing the same goals and ways. Sure new people can and surely will join, but not because they walk up to us and say: "Hey I would like join your guild, but because they become friends while playing with us and share the same goals. What's the use of a guild or its goal's when you never teamed with each other to achieve the goals or have fun together?
