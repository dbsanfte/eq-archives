We like to think of ourselves as a relaxed guild that enjoys the company of each other and having fun.  The Damned is not a guild of strict, militaristic rules. However guidelines and policies are needed in order for the guild to operate smoothly and fairly.

 

If any of these guide lines seem harsh or unjust, please bring up your concern with an Officer or the Guild Master.  These guidelines may be amended at any time if they are found to be unjust or in need of clarification.

Guidelines

 

We look down upon the following:

v     Unwanted harassment in any form.

Jokes are great as long as the person you are having fun with (making fun of) is laughing too.  No jokes about real life race, sexual orientation or religion should be told.  EverQuest is a role playing game, so keep it RP. (Make fun of Halflings all you want. JK any form of in game race/ religion prejudice is ok as long as it is not outright bashing.)

v     Kill Stealing

There are plenty of mobs in the game for everyone to hunt.  There is no reason to kill steal.

v     Purposely training a player / camp

This is simply rude and disrespectful and gives the player and the guild a bad name.  It is understandable that trains can occur when a player is running to the zone, however a simple /shout of train to what ever zone you are running to will at least give people a heads up you are heading their way with death on your tail.

v     Ninja Looting

If you did not kill the mob or win the roll the item does not belong to you.  Players do not forget ninja looters and will often tell their friends and guild mates of the jerk that ninja looted their <insert coveted loot item here>.  Players also do not forget what guild the ninja looter was in, so ninja looters not only give themselves a bad name but also give the guild a bad name.

v     Excessive swearing

Don’t swear in /shout or any type of zone wide chat.  It just makes you look stupid and you may offend a lot of the zone that way.  Also try to limit the amount of swearing in guild chat.

 

We also support the rules set forth by Verant along with their “ play nice policies”.  Game Masters and Guides are to be treated with respect.  They have a very hard job and the last thing they want is to deal with a jerk.  If you don’t like the GMs or Guides, don’t deal with them.

 

If a member of the guild is found to be acting against any of our policies or the polices of Verant, they will be subject to an investigation and ruling by the Officers and the Guild Master.

Policies

 

            Ethics

 

The Guild understands that:

 

·        Real life comes before the guild and before EverQuest.

Things happen in real life that makes it so a person cannot play.  And in general real life is more important than EverQuest (although I know a few that would refute this).

·        The current group you are in comes before the guild.

If you are in a group, that group comes first.  If you wish to leave the group to join some guild mates or to leave for a hunt, please excuse yourself and attempt to find another person to take your place before you leave the group.  However it is also expected that if a planned guild hunt is taking place, all applicable guild members are expected to attend and have made prior arrangements to arrive on time.

·        In most cases the guild comes before self. 

The thought behind this is that every member of the guild is a servant to the guild.  They are to do what is in their power for the betterment of the guild.  However no one is expected to give more then they can.  Just remember the guild will give back as much as it receives.  So give us your friendship and loyalty and we will give back comradery and respect.

·        Members will work together for the betterment of the guild.

 

The Guild expects that:

·        Members actively involve themselves with the guild.

Active involvement means talking in guild chat and seeking out groups with guild members.  Not only is grouping with your fellow guild mates fun, it also improves how the guild works together in combat.  And you never know, you might learn something. (Even if you are a quiet person at least say hello when you sign on and ask how everyone is doing).  If a member feels that they are going to take an extended time away from EverQuest or for some other reason are not able to play, they should contact an Officer or the Guild Leader and place a post on the forums telling of their absence.  No in depth reason is needed, after all, you are entitled to your privacy.

·        Members respect each other, as they want themselves to be respected.

·        Members respect other players and other guilds.

You never know when you may encounter a potential guild member or guild alliance.

·        Members will address any issues with an Officer or the Guild Master.

If a member is experiencing any issues within the guild and in some cases outside the guild in the scope of the game, this is to be brought up with an Officer or the Guild Leader.  Any issues that could be volatile should not be brought up in guild chat, because this will only lead to animosity and /gu spam.

·        Officers act responsibly and with honor and respect.

The officers are second in command. They are to respect the members of the guild and treat them fairly.  In return they are to be respected by the members and each other.  The Officers are to support the Guild Leader and act as council.  The Officers are the eyes and ears of the Guild Leader and therefore their loyalty is to the Guild Leader and the betterment of the guild.

·        The Guild Leader is to act with respect and fairness.

The Guild Leader is to respect and treat all members of the guild fairly.  The Leader is also expected to show the utmost respect to other guilds and players in EverQuest.  The Guild Leader should not make a decision till all sides have been heard and thought out.  The Guild Leader has the last say in all matters.  The Guild Leader is to receive the highest amount of respect from the members.

Loot

When in a group with no other guild member then the leader of that group decides how the loot will be split up.  If it is a rare drop item the fair way to decide who gets the loot is to roll for it.   The person who wins the roll owns the loot.

·        When grouping with a group of a few guild mates and outsiders, the same rule applies as above.

·        When grouping with a full group of Damned, then the loot scheme is as follows:

~ Rare drop items go to person present in group that can use it.

~ If no one in group can use it then the item goes to another main in the guild that can

~ If no ones main in the guild can use it, then the item goes to the alternate character of a person in the group that can use the item.

~ If no one can use the item, then the group can decide whether to pass the item on to whoever wants it or to roll for the item.

·        Whenever you enter a group, ask them how they are doing the loot and follow their rule (unless you want to amend it because you are a Monk and don’t want extra weight or if the loot order seems very unbalanced).

Disagreements and Warnings

 

            Disagreements

 

If at anytime a member has a disagreement with another player whether it be a guild mate or not, they should attempt to solve the disagreement first themselves.  If this cannot be done in a sane manner then, depending on the status of the other player, a course of action should be taken.

·        If the other player is not a guild mate

Then that person should be ignored.  If they have cause you large amounts of distress then a Game Master of Guide should be petitioned.

·        If the player is a guild mate

Then an Officer of the Guild Master should be contacted.  The Officer or Guild Master should then hear both sides of the story and a decision will be made on how to deal with the issue.

 

At all times members are expected to act responsibly and respectfully.  If you are being harassed by any other player, use /log to create a log of all chat that is seem on your screen.  This log may be useful later to prove harassment or other charges.

 

            Warnings

 

If a member of the guild is found to be disobeying the rules set here or those set by Verant, they may either be deguilded on the spot or investigated.  Investigation is the chosen course for any reports or unjust or rude behavior.  If after an investigation a member(s) is found to be acting against the policies of the guild and / or the game, then an Officer or the Guild Master will speak to the member(s).  The member(s) is to be told what action is not approved by the guild and why it is not approved.  They will be asked if they can change their future actions.  If not then they will be asked to leave the guild.

Rite of Passage

 

To be considered for a membership within the Damned is relatively simple.  If a person outside the guild is interested in joining, they should contact a member of the guild or an Officer.  They may also post a message on the forums or send and e-mail to an Officer or the Guild Master.  If the potential member is a friend of a member of the guild, we ask that the member bring up the person’s name in guild chat or with an Officer if they are not sure what to do.

 

 

These are the general requirements that should be met in order to become a member of the Damned.

 

·        Potential member should contact the guild and or be mentioned to the guild by a current member.

·        The potential member should introduce themselves to the guild by sending tells to the Officers or Guild Leader.  If no Officers or the Guild Leader is not on line at the time but a regular member is, the potential member may send them a /tell.

·        Members of the guild are expected to respond to a /tell from a person saying they are interested in joining.  If the member is not sure what to say they can always give the name of an Officer and the Guild Leader or direct them to our web site.

·        Any potential members should be directed to the Decree of the Damned so they are aware of the guild policies and agree to abide by them.

·        The potential member should be at least level 20.

·        The potential member should group with the guild on at least three occasions so they can learn more about the guild and the guild can learn more about them. This includes one time with an officer present.

·        A potential member must have at least 2 member sponsors and one officer sponsor before they can be guilded and if applicable a final vote will be counted in guild chat to make sure there are no objections.

·        If the guild agrees to tag a potential member, any officer may tag them once the word is out that they are approved.

·        After a player is tagged, they will be on a probationary period for 1 month.  This time period may be shortened or extended at the discretion of the Guild Master.  After this time, any alternate characters the new guildy wants guilded, regardless of level, can be tagged.

·        It is also required that all new members create an EZBoard global account for the message board and actively participate on the board.
