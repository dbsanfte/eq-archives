[quote]Pirub9: You are wrong. You do not own intellectual rights unless you claim them. Trademarks, copyrights, and other forms of patents are only as strong as the desire to enforce them. Basically if you don't defend them, you don't own them.[/quote]

Only trademarks need to be defended. If trademarks are not vigerously enforced then you lose trademark protection.

However, IP/copyright rights are granted to the individual as soon as they create the work in question. If you do not defend/enforce your rights, it doesn't matter; you don't lose them...you just don't gain anything.

Patents are the same way; if you don't enforce a patent, it doesn't go away...you just don't get royalties you could be getting.

The only thing that registering a copyright gives you is that it makes it easier to enforce/sue in the case of a violation. In some cases, should the copyright not be registered, (altho I'm not sure of the specific conditions) a lawsuit against someone for copyright violation can only result in a cease-and-desist settlement and not monetary compensation...monetary compensation for copyright violation can only be calculated/granted from the date of registration, not the date of creation. (but I think that varies dependent on media type)

Standard disclaimer: IANAL, but I know some.
