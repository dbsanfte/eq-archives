Scruffy was bored... Scruffy made a FAQ about Luclin...
---
FAQ - OVERVIEW
---
Question: WTF is EQ: SoL?
Verant: EverQuest: the Shadows of Luclin is an expansion to the world-renowned game EverQuest.
Translation: It's a way to make more $$ off of EverQuest.
---
Question: What's in Luclin?
Verant: Here is a partial feature list. Please note that this feature set is subject to change prior to release.
Translation: Some of these features may be removed before release. Remember how Velious and Kunark were supposed to be in the original EQ release? This is just like that.
Verant: A massively enhanced 3D graphics engine that makes use of newer technologies allowing us to support higher poly counts and more detailed textures.
Translation: We're putting more graphics into our 6 year old 3D Graphics engine that is still buggy as hell. We're also integrating new technologies to make more people crash on load.
Verant: Over 25 new zones including a new starting city
Translation: 25 new zones that may or may not make it to release. If they don't it will be downloaded 2 years later (See: Paineel, Hole, Warrens, StoneBrunt). We're also putting in a starting city for that race that should have been in the original EQ release, the Kierrans.
Verant: All new, highly detailed player-character models
Translation: That will not show up and probably break every non Luclin player's client. Plus, watch those framerates because this is bound to slow down even the fastest 3D Card with our uber broken engine.
Verant: An all-new player character race: The Vah Shir (Kerrans)
Translation: Finally, we got around to putting in a race that was supposted to be put in on EverQuest's Release 2 years ago.
Verant: An all-new player character class: The Beastlord
Translation: We're putting in a new class that is a combination of the broken ranger class and the broken shadow knight's pets.
Verant: Additional non-level-based advancement options for those at level 60
Translation: More ubermobs for uberguilds. We couldn't let FoH down, could we?
Verant: New quests, items, NPCs, and more!
Translation: More broken quests, broken items, broken NPCs, and more broken stuff. Of course, we'll fix anything that broke and gives player an advantage, but we'll ignore anything that gives the player a disadvantage.
---
Question: Will I need an ubersystem to play Luclin?
Verant: Yes, Luclin is a very ambitious title. In addition to a rich in-character feature set, Luclin will sport some of the most stunning visuals yet to be seen in online games. In order to achieve this, the minimum system specification for Luclin will roughly coincide with that of games being released today.
Verant: The system specs will be released closer to the expansion's release date.
Translation: We're tinkering with the 6 year old EverQuest engine to put more visual effects into it. Unfortunatly, since our engine sucks, you're going to need a Uber System to play Luclin.
---
Question: When's Luclin going to be relased?
Verant: Luclin will go live in December of this year.
Translation: We're trying to get Luclin released, completed or not, just in time for the Christmas shopping season. We hope that a release during this time will maximize our wallets.
---
Question: Will there be a beta?
Verant: Yes. Luclin will be tested via a closed beta system expected to begin around August. People will be invited individually based upon past demonstrated desire to test and assist in EverQuest's development. As such, people who actively play on the Test server will get priority.
Translation: We're going to do a closed "test" around August. You need not apply though if you aren't a: A) VAK; B) VAK Test Server Player
---
Question: How will I get Luclin? Will it be sold like Velious?
Verant: Yes, Luclin will be sold; however the precise format of sales is still to be determined.
Translation: We're going to sell Luclin like Velious because we got a lot of money from the sale of Velious to all EverQuest players, unlike the Kunark upgrade which you could buy bundled with the original EverQuest package. We're working on ways to maximize profit with this expansion, so we don't know how we're going to sell it exactly yet.
---
Question: Will I need to buy Luclin for each account?
Verant: Yes, you will need to buy a copy of Luclin for each account that you wish to access Luclin content.
Translation: You didn't think you we were going to let you upgrade 4 accounts with one box? Come on, we need to make some $$ from this!!
---
WHINEPLAY/GAMEPLAY
---
Question: Where is Luclin?
Verant: Luclin is the name of the ringed moon that orbits Norrath which currently can only been seen via the Plane of Sky. We expect that Al`Kabor will soon pierce the veil of shadow that hides it from view elsewhere in Norrath.
Translation: The current moon will be magically transformed during a patch by changing the texture from Sky.s3d/Moon.bmp to Sky.s3d/Saturn.bmp
---
Question: What level range will Luclin support?
Verant: Luclin will support players of all levels, much like Kunark.
Translation: Luclin will support players of all levels in theory, but they will have to run through several high level zones to get there. For example, think of the Firiona to Lake of Ill Omen sprint for level 20's.
---
Question: Is there Life after level 60?
Verant: No, but we are planning on including some non-level-based advancement options for those who wish to advance their characters further. The advancement will be measurable but subtle, so-as not to give anyone the feeling that they are required to advance as many felt upon the release of Kunark.
Translation: We're putting more ubermobs with uberloots that HOPEFULLY FoH will not be able to kill. But knowing FoH, they'll be dead 2 weeks after release.
---
Question: Will Luclin Players that weren't suckered into Kunark and Velious be able to reach 60?
Verant: Yes.
Translation: We needed to put in some incentive for people that weren't suckered into Kunark and Velious!!
---
Question: How do you get to Luclin?
Verant: Giant steam-powered gnomish rocketships…
Translation: No way to translate a bad joke... I'll just put it this way: "Who's your daddy?"
Verant: Just kidding. Keep an eye on the Journal of Al`Kabor. Something tells me he may have some idea about how it's accomplished.
Translation: Keep reading our website, sucker. We'll tell you when we're ready to release this thing.
---
THE RACE FORMERLY KNOWN AS THE KERRANS/THE VAH SHIR
---
Question: WTF are the Vah Shir? Is that like Venril Sathir?
Verant: The Vah Shir are a race of noble cat people that were wiped off Odus and deposited on Luclin many years ago in the test of a great weapon by the Erudites. The weapon, aimed at the Vah Shir home city of the same name, flung the entire city and the inhabitants therein through space, leaving what is now known as The Hole. Without their nobility, the remaining cat people, dubbed "Kerrans" by the Erudites, were easily rounded up and segregated to Kerra Isle.
Translation: In our "story", the Kerrans were shot all of the way to the moon by Erudites many years ago (giving a new meaning to the saying: "To the moon!!"). The Erudites basically transported an entire city to the moon, leaving what is now known as "The Hole" (contrary to the "story" that the hole was created during the civil war between Erudites and the Erudite Necromancers at Paineel (See story below) - What's a few more holes (pun intended) in the EQ "storyline").
A brief history of the Hole: The Hole was created at the end of the Erudin/Heretic war, when the Heretics used their magical powers to punch a hole in the earth deep into the depths of Odus. Within this deep shaft they built their city of Paineel. However, they penetrated too deeply and, unknown to them at first, they had touched upon the Plane of Underfoot itself, which had been cast onto the earth in eons past. In the years after the city was built, they became aware of the enemy below them and attempted to block them in, but with the arrival of the great Master Yael, the elementals and minions of Brell swept through the city on one gruesome night, destroying everything in their path. The Heretics were forced to retreat to their highest outpost, the site of the current city of Paineel. (From EQAtlas.com)
---
Question: Will I need to get Luclin to play the Race Formerly Known as Kerrans?
Verant: Though everyone's client will download the Vah Shir character model in order to allow viewing of those of the Vah Shir race, you will need Luclin in order to play one.
Translation: Even though you'll have almost everything you need to play the Race Formerly Known as Kerrans besides the home zone, you'll not be able to play them unless you buy Luclin. Think of it as another incentive to purchase Luclin.
---
THE BEASTRANGER/THE BEASTLORD
---
Question: What is BeastRangers?
Verant: Beastlords are essentially hybrid class with a heavy reliance upon pets. More information will be available as we finalize the details.
Translation: Beastlords are rangers with pets. More details coming.
---
Question: Will old races be able to be BeastRangers?
Verant: At minimum, the Vah Shir will be able to be Beastlords. It is very likely, though not guaranteed, that we will open the class up to other shamanistic races.
Translation: The Race Formerly Known as Kerrans will definatly be able to be BeastRangers. If we're feeling nice, we'll allow other races to be BeastRangers too.
---
Question: Will you need Luclin to play BeastRanger?
Verant: Yes. Even if we make the Beastlord available to existing races, you will need Luclin in order to play one.
Translation: You need to fork over YOUR cash to play BeastRanger. Did you think you were going to get something free out of this?!?!
---
NEW UBERPOLY CHARACTER MODELS/NEW CHARACTER MODELS
---
Question: Will there be new uberhigh-poly character models?
Verant: Yes. The character models we are building are far more detailed, sporting a much higher polygon count and a greater texture depth. They seem more lifelike.
Translation: We're adding more polyies to make everyone upgrade their computer. But, the nice side effect is that they all look nicer.
---
Question: Will there be new faces, face choices, and is my old character screwed?
Verant: The new models will have a greater number of face options available. For those who already have characters, we will be creating faces for the new models that closely resemble the ones you chose for your old models. We may also allow players with existing characters a one-time choice of a new face for each character, but that's not guaranteed at the moment.
Translation: We're putting in new faces and we're HOPING that the face you get on the new model will be pretty much the same as the old face you choose. But, if they don't come out too well, your old character is pretty much screwed. But, maybe if we're feeling nice, we'll put in a way to change your face one time only.
---
Question: I don't have an ubercomputer!! Tell me that there will be an off switch!!
Verant: While we sincerely doubt that will be the case, we will include an option to continue display of the old models.
Translation: We're going to try to put an off switch in time for release, but it will probably be hard to set (think turning on the Velious textures).
---
Question: Are you redoing all player races?
Verant: We're also planning on making new models for anything that players can become.
Translation: We're planning on making new models for EVERY PC race. We want to make the system requirements as high as possible.
---
Question: Will there be more animations to slow down my CPU?
Verant: Yes, we're doing all new combat and social animations, including differentiating animations based upon what you happen to have in your hands at the given time. In other words, a kick carrying one weapon will look different than a kick while carrying two weapons.
Translation: After 2 years of saying "We can't do that", we did the impossible: We made the slash weapon animation look different than a blunt weapon animation. Think of it as another incentive to buy Luclin. We want your $$!!
Verant: In addition, we're working on another aspect of animation that will simply blow you away. Unfortunately we can't talk about it just yet.
Translation: We're working on an ubersecret project that will be causing EVERYONE to crash to desktop when loading EverQuest. More details to come.
---
THE END

------------------
-Scruffy the MEANIE
