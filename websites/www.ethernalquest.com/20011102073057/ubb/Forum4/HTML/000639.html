If you use the search you would see that there has been a lot of discussion on the linksys 5 port hubs.

They are in actuality switches.

To get the linksys to work as a hub, everything must be at either 10mb or 100mb. Since the Cable Modem is running at 10mb and your computers are running at 100mb (most likely) the hub is acting as a modified switch.

You will need to do 1 of 2 things to get it to work with that hub.

1. Remove the cable modem from the hub, get another net card and put it in your linux box, and then plug the cable modem into your linux box. (That is assuming that your 2 current net cards are 100mb).

2. Get a hub that isnt a switch.

Sounds like you could try to jack into one of the regular ports instead of the uplink port. It may work, but usually the uplink port requires a 'reversed' cable.

The problem with Lynksys hubs is that the uplink port is switched. The rest are actually broadcast(what you want.)

At worst it won't work, at best you have a quick fix till you get a real unmanaged hub.

3com had an all in one network kit available over a year ago that contained a 5 port hub and two NIC's for about $50 dollars. They worked perfectly, and ran my network at home without any problems, I have since upgraded to a 100Mbs AT hub, but the 3com cards were 10/100 and still work great.

You might also look for an Allied-Telesyn brand hub. They are cheap <$100 for 8 port, durable(metal cases) and ultra-reliable(no failures in 20 hubs in years of use in an industrial, static ridden, dusty hell-hole.) Though, I did lose one to a roof leak =)
