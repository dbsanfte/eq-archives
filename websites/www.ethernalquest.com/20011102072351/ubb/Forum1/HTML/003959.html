Remember when "The Lion King" scandal was hot, and everyone thought the scene where Simba throws himself down and the smoke/flower petals billows up, and people SWORE it had a subliminal message that said S E X? Only it turned out what it said was S F X for the special effects department?

Or how everyone USED to describe aliens as small brightly colored creatures, until a few descriptions of them as tall with big eyes got publicized widely? Now every redneck that sees a bright light in his pickup truck describes what he saw as tall, thin, with big black eyes?

It's called "group induced hysteria/perception." It means you think that's what it is because other people think that, and they told you. In order for your brain (as a social creature) to feel accepted, it distorts previously loosely held beliefs into other related securely held beliefs. You don't just "think you saw a face", you are sure you saw the devil's face.

Hmm the devil's face? Biblically Beezelbub, Bealzebub, Lucifer. Sounds menancing, right? Only cause you've been taught that, and relate those words with evil now. Actually, the names are supposed to relate to beauty, glory, and power. Biblically, Lucifer aka the devil was the second in command, just slightly above the archangel Micheal and Gideon.

Where did the old guy with horns come from? Actually, it's a blending of norse and greek mythology and christian superstition. The little half goat half boy thing (I forget the name, it isn't a nymph...) was originally the greek god Pan. It symbolized debauchery and mischevious intent, thus it slowly began to represent evil, and thus the switch to devil was not a far jump.
