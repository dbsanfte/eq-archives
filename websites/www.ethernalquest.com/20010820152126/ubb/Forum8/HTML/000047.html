Decaying Skeleton 		posted 07-05-2001 03:30 PM            
One thing that is absurdly weak in most multi-player games (especially RPGs) is that the player INSTANTLY knows everyone and every thing.

You come upon a ghoul (which you've NEVER seen before), which looks just like three or four other monsters that you have seen, and viola! you know it's a ghoul. Same with basilisks... A Barbarian from a continent away comes to Butcherblock (level 2, never been here before)... and looks, and sees a LOWLAND BASILISK! Look, it's a Gnoll Commander, a Seafury Cyclops, Ambassador Dvinn (apparently known by name, like I mysteriously know each and EVERY Dragoon by name).

When I enter any zone, I instantly know every player's name and every NPC too.

Faction is a good idea, but unrealistically handled. If I've killed a wandering Ogre in zone X and my Wizard taxi's me across the world to say... Ogguk, within the same hour, how are those OTHER Ogres going to know? (just an example)...

Merchants should have families or at least towns should be more legitimately structured. Any decent RPGamer KNEW that Verant / Sony was unable to have houses or apartments for players... why? Because the NPCs don't have any. For each merchant, there should be at LEAST 3 rooms in the town to support them. For each non-food merchant, there would be 3-4 non-merchant NPCs like farmers and hunters (where does the food resource come from otherwise?).

Having merchants open at all times is rather senseless. There should be some all-night "grocers" but they should be RARE and expensive. Merchants should NOT be able to handle more than 2-3 people at a time.

The game should not EVER be balanced for the computer. It should be balanced for either :
fairness,
logic / realism, or
the player.

Felwithe is a great example. There are like what... 20 shops (15-20?)... and about 40 merchants and a slew of guards. Where do they all live? Where do their families live? Wouldn't it be better if EVERY town was significantly larger and had a logistically tenable economy?

If you will have languages supported in a game... then make them useful. When I go to towns, even if I don't speak the language of that race, I understand everyone! That is stupid.

That's all stuff that is both easy to fix and rather increase playability and fun.

Ey Belinar, It's me, Jakob, yer programmer
Just wanted to say that I would also like a Sci-fi game, but I personally think that the combat rules should be written from scratch, without needing any influence from any other.
I just had a cool thought, what 'bout making a MMORPG in the HG Wells: "the war of the worlds" universe, being about marsians taking over the world =)
And for the graphics, you shouldn't use Luclins graphics engine, that would cost ALOT!!! It would be better to write a portable OpenGL graphics engine, so that the game would be playable on Windows, Linux, Unix, BeOS, QNXrtp etc, EQ uses Direct3D, wich is not very portable.
As to my ideas about the game, we could write a landscape engine without zones, using fractal calculations to make a relatively low resolution heightmap, maybe 1 metre res, look natural.
I also think that the game would not be a role playing GAME, but a role playing ENVIROMENT. Maybe even the players would start in a empty world, regardless of if it should be fantasy or sci-fi, and then the players should build houses and castles, and make their weapons (I have even written a test program that calculates the metal used and your skill, to make a weapon with different stats). And no stupid rules like "wizzies can't have two weapons" or "of course you can't use that a big sword, you're a druid" the game could be an enviroment where the players are the most important things, and not the rules.
I am currently writing some network code, but I don't know how usable it is on Windows, but we can port.
As for the homepage, I think we should use some better program than FrontPage. I use Dreamweaver, that is much better, and for the forums, I could write one much better than that shitty Yahoo.
We can discuss more when we get together on a chat room, but it would be great if I had a telnet server ready so that we could write some snippets of code, and try out some approaches to the landscape engine...
