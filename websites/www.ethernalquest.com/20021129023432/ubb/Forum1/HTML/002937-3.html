Well, let's look at all the possible ways one could get caught using GHU ...

1. Verant scans Task List (apps running). Fortunately, it's probably illegal, and they backed down from checking your Task List. Doesn't mean they're not; in fact, I think if they're smart, they would, to flag people as "possible" hax0rz. They never mention it, just use it as a basis for investigation (kinda like law enforcement can't use certain information *directly*, but can act on it in other ways).

2. Verant sets server-side checks, and flags your account when your actions don't "check out". Arguable whether or not they've done this for the speed hack, but overall, that's a lot more work for already over-worked servers and their processors. Sure, they limit effects/timing server-side, but I'm thinking they don't report anomolies [in such a way that it flags your account].

3. You brag about it online in some way. Honestly, I think 9 out of 10 people suspended/banned are caught in this fashion. Whether it's showing off super-speed to someone, selling 200 Yew Leaves a day [SEQ], selling piles of rare Foraged items or whatever, one's own big mouth is the most likely way to get oneself in hot water.

4. Someone observes you doing something normally impossible for your character's race/class/level. Giving my Bard perma-speed song isn't so noticable -- giving it to my Warrior sure would be!

Keep quiet when you're on EQ about your exploits. Good buddy of mine had the activatable speed hack -- only used it when he needed to get away from mobs and he was low health (which normally slows you down). So even at a sliver of health, he was running a little faster than the mob. Hard to notice, unless you really think to look (after all, other character's health bars aren't updated to 'observers' too quickly). Subtle. He's been using mem hacks for over a year, and no troubles at all, knock on wood!
