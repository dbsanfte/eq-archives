Code of Conduct

There are not many societies that can survive without some form of rules and codes of conduct to keep justice and peace. I wish there was some way to keep from having to make rules for the guild, but there isn't. SO, without further notice, here are the rules set forth for the guild, Light of Pure Order. These rules are subject to be changed and revised as seen fit by the officers of the guild.

Guild Rules | Officer Rules

Guild Rules:

 1.  There is to be no begging or bothering of guild members for equipment, cash, ports, or
        power leveling in guild chat. If you need a favor, then you can ask for help, but do not
        bother other members in guild chat.
 2.  Power Leveling is not condoned by the Light of Pure Order. If you wish to seek someone
        to PL you, please do not spam the guild chat.
 3.  Finally, all "Play Nicely" Rules and regulations set forth by Sony and Verant should be
        followed without a second thought. If there is something that you don't agree with that
        Sony and Verant have ruled against, leave it as something you don't like. Don't use the
        guild to try to overthrow the rules.
 4.  All members should act in ways that will bring only a good reputation to the guild.
        Everyone that sees you, sees the name of the Guild. Anything you do that is harmful to
        your character's image, is also harmful to the Guild's image.
 5.  Guild members must play their guild character once a month. The guild will remove all
        inactive characters on the 15th of every month. If a character has not been played since
        the 15th of the month before, it will be removed from the guild, on the spot, no
        questions asked. If you have alt characters that you do not play much, please be sure 
        to mark them, i.e. <Daemc's Alt> on the guild management window in EQ. Alt
        characters that are marked on the guild mangement window, are immune to deletion, 
        unless the main character has not been played since the previous deletion.
 6.  All members are to uphold the decisions of the Officers online. Anyone that does not
        obey a officer's orders, will be put on probation until a trial can be held. 
 7.  The decision made by officers should be final, and should be obeyed as if they are Law. 
        If you feel that a officer's decision is wrong, then you may request a trial for the decision 
        by the High Council.

Officer Rules:

 1.  No Officer will abuse their power.
 2.  Officers will NOT remove members without giving them a proper trial.
 3.  If there is a problem within the guild, Officers have to do what is needed, to solve the 
        problem.
 4.  If the officers can't solve the problem at that time, then the High Council will hold a trial.

All of the above rules are strictly enforced. Anyone caught breaking the rules will be given one verbal warning. After a verbal warning has been issued, another violation of the rules will be removal from the guild. If a member feels that they are being treated unfairly, then a council of members will meet and discuss the situation and a decision will be made. The ruling of the council is final and no one will change what the council decides. Serious violation of the rules will be subject to notification of a GM for assistance, and possibly, removal of the violator.
