Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana Server:

Comments: Second only to the 5th level Accelerando song, this song will make you the life of any party that has tank characters. Useful for topping off health after a battle while the healers meditate. In combat, keep in mind that every point that is healed by this song, will piss off a monster as much as doing 1 point of damage to it. For example, if you are healing 6 players, for 5 points each, the monsters you are fighting will react as if you were doing an extra 6*5=30 points damage to each of them every pulse. Use with caution, monsters may start to concentrate more of their attacks on you.

Strategies:

Will stack with the Druid/Shaman regeneration spell lines.  Be sure the regeneration spell is cast first.
[Kaearia] People often ask whether you should play this song during combat.  The answer is, it depends.  :) Offensive songs like Anthem or Bellow will make the fight shorter (assuming you are the winner).  Defensive songs like Hymn and the Rhythm series do not shorten the fight, but allow you to survive longer.  All other things being equal, it is better to shorten a fight than to increase the duration you can survive (shorter fights = less chance of a wandering mob joining in).  But if you're already singing your "best" offensive songs and can sing one more, then it may be more helpful to sing Hymn rather than a weaker offensive song.
[Kaearia] It may not make sense to sing this in combat when only one or two people are taking damage.  But if everyone is significantly wounded, the healing effect can easily outweigh the benefit of other songs.  e.g. at L36, it heals 15 points per pulse with a lute.  Since the song continues for 3 pulses by just playing it once, if all 6 party members are wounded, you will heal 15*6*3 = 270 points by "strumming" this song once.  That's almost as much as a greater heal every 18 seconds.  So be ready to use this song if changing battle conditions warrant it.
[Mark] I was grouped with a rouge in N RO, I wanted the derv's to attack me so the rouge could get more backstabs. The other songs didn't seem to piss of the mobs enough for them to stop attacking the rouge but when I started playing the Hymn of restoration it worked well. They would attack me until the rouge would backstab then after 2 or 3 pulses of the song they would start attacking me, just in time fro the rouges backstab. (This is also true for any song with a High Taunt factor, also the taunt power is level dependent).
