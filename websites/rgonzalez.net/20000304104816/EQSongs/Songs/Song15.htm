KELIN's LUCID LULLABY

Level: 15

Effect: Enthrall

Skill: String

Target: Area Effect

Location: Kelethin

Does not stack with: Overrides Enchanter's mesmerize line of spell

Taunt: High

Quantified: N/A

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server.

Notes: This song is affected by your charisma.

Comments: This song used to Nix the hate list as well, but no longer seems to in testing, or in reports from the field.  Also Kaearia has done some extensive log analysis and the resist numbers from GZ seem to be off as well. 

Strategies:

GZ did a public test of this song on the Veeshen server. Several bards posted reports of the test.
[GZ] Minimum resist on any mob is about 25%. About 35% on whites, much higher on reds. Good charisma lowers the chance of resist.
[GZ] Lullaby is a 1 (to about 1.5) tick song. It is not effective when twisted with another song. If you plan to use this song as an Area Mezmerize, use ONLY this song. The length is about 3-5 seconds long only.
This song can be extremely powerful when used correctly. The secret is to educate your party prior to using it. All party members need to focus on a single mob at a time, melee and casters. The best way to do this is to have everyone use the /assist [playername] command.  Make sure the caster now that using AE spells will mean a dead bard, as when they wake up they will express their displeasure on you.  The cleric needs to keep a very close eye on your health bar as well.
This song can be used to interrupt casting NPCs as well.  Some higher level NPCs cast so fast that they are immune to this effect.
Another good use of this song is when partnered with a nuker class.  As the nod effect clears the hate list allowing the caster to get off nuke after nuke without getting stomped.
[Elthan] This song can be used for the "Heroic emergency escape", The heroic Bard nods the bad pull allowing the rest of the party to flee, this will normally result in a dead bard so try to die in a spot that is easy to recover from.
[Thaddius] Guest editorial from a Cleric regarding Lucid Lullaby
[Kaearia] Addendum to the "Heroic emergency escape."  The bard does not have to stand there and die after his party has escaped.  If you keep Lullaby playing as you run out, the mobs that nod will stand in place, and thus fall behind.  If you're lucky, this thinning of the ranks will give you enough time to reach the zone.
[Kaearia] Using a lute helps the effectiveness of this song.  It was not so apparent in my teens, but by my 30s, the improved effect was obvious.
[Kaearia] If you leave the song playing, it pulses every 6 seconds.  If you stop and restart it every time you see the "nods" messages scroll by, you can get the time between pulses down to about 3 seconds.  This will interrupt more casters, nod more mobs that have woken up, and in general make the song work much better.
[Kaearia] Two bards playing this song in the above manner can do an incredible job holding a train.
[Kaearia] You can nod runners, which will sometimes give a druid or ranger enough time to snare them.  Unfortunately, the rest of your party will probably be trying to kill the runner, meaning they'll hit it and immediately wake it up again, at which point it'll start running again...
[Kaearia] You will take the faction hit for mobs that you've nodded.  This can be a quick and easy way to build up faction.  Go to a newbie zone (e.g. Blackburrow) and put the trains to sleep while the newbies kill everything.  Only problem is that green mobs tend to run when at half hp.

Cautions:

I have received several reports that this song no longer clears the Hate list.
[Patch] This song now correctly displays a message if the target makes the second resist check.
Word of warning, just like Chords of Dissonance, this song will affect everything in the area. Please do not use this song next to a guard, or he will start attacking you.
Each mob affected has two chances to break out of the Lullaby every pulse. The first is a standard resist, which nullifies the song outright, and the second is the chance (which varies based on Bard CHA, instrument used, level spread, etc) to just plain ignore the song.
Even though Verant claims to have limited this song to 4 mobs at a time, nearly every Bard I have spoken to says it will sleep more.
[Kaearia] Lullaby will override an enchanter's mesmerize (when two mesmerizes of different duration conflict, the shorter wins, and Lullaby is about as short as they come). In some really rare instances (e.g. lower level enchanter having problem with resists, or trying to interrupt a spell casting mob you're fighting) you should use Lullaby. But in general, if you have an enchanter, you should leave the mesmerizing up to them.  Their mesmerize spells last at least a minimum duration.
