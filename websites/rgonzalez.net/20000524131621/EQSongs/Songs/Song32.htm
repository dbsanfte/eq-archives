CASSINDRA's CHORUS of CLARITY

Level: 32

Effect: Direct Mana Restoration

Skill: Singing

Target: Party

Location: Kelethin, Qeynos

Does not stack with: N/A

Taunt: Low

Quantified: Provided by Kaearia

L29 enchanter Clarity was about a 30% reduction in med time.
L32 bard Clarity was about a 33% reduction in med time.
L40 bard Clarity was about a 38% reduction in med time.  
These figures are probably +/- 3% or so due to inaccuracies in eyeballing the mana bar, but it really seems like a straight level% conversion.

Some additional timing numbers (provided by Kaearia):
Time for cleric to med back one bubble 
~100 sec - no songs 
~63 sec - playing just Clarity 
~63 sec - stop/starting Clarity with each "mind clears" message 
~63 sec - playing Clarity + Hymn 
~76 sec - playing Clarity + Hymn + Accel 
L39 cleric, L40 bard.

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server

Comments: This song is a direct mana restoration, this means that it only restores mana when you see the "Your mind clears" message. So weaving this song can decrease its effectiveness.  Now that said it it possible to weave Clarity with one song with little loss of mana restoration vs playing this song by itself (see timing numbers above). Our songs have a 3 second casting time and a 6 second natural pulse time.  So weaving Clarity with one song will still result in clarity pulsing about once every 6-7 seconds, this can and will be affected by your manual dexterity, missed notes, connection and machine speed.

Strategies:

I have found it useful to weave this into combat, especially in large parties with only one healer.  I will also weave this with only one song, if any of the caster go below 1/3rd mana.
If you are feeling real lucky you can weave Clarity with 2 other songs and still get nearly the full effect.  Play Clarity - song 1 - Clarity - song 2 - Clarity  - song 1 - clarity - etc...  Personally I have a hard time keeping the 2 extra songs up, but you might find it useful.

Cautions:

The range on this song is short, so depending on the situation you may need to withdraw from battle if the casters need mana in battle.  Personally I tell the casters it is their responsibility to stay in range if they want mana.  Especially in dragon runs or other multi-party events, as all those casters look the same to me ;)
[Ziann] Occasionally this song will produce phantom mana, so when mana levels are critical it may be a good idea to have casters cast a low cost spell to force their client to sync with the server.
The phantom mana effect can happen if you are weaving or playing straight Clarity.  It seems to be user specific, so it may be a lag effect.
