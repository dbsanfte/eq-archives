KELIN'S LUGUBRIOUS LAMENT

Level: 8

Effect: Lull (reduce's targets aggressive radius)

Skill: String

Target: Single

Location: Kelethin

Does not stack with: N/A

Taunt: N/A

Quantified: N/A

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server:

Notes: This song is affected by your charisma.

Comments: This song causes a NPC to 'become sad' (lulled) and ignore you. This is the first Bard song that is not continuous.  Lulled NPC's have there aggro radius greatly reduced, but not eliminated.

Strategies:

This song allows you to single pull from a group of NPC's.  This makes breaking up a NPC camp much easier.
When using this song lull the NPCs farthest from your puller.  Try to avoid pulling past lulled creatures as they may come even if successfully lulled.
[Gendal] Since this song it string based, using a string instrument greatly increases it's effectiveness.  Gendal also reports that at level 50 this song will make you the puller extraordinaire for breaking the spawn on difficult rooms that would normally take 2 parties to break.
When you have the choice of pulling a melee mob vs a casting mob, always pull the melee mob first.  The reason is that casters like to stand off and launch a nuke or two before closing the distance to your party.
[Maravana] Lament (Song 8) is also good to use to move through dungeons with greatly reduced bother from aggro green mobs and/or dealing with casters and the like who can see through an invis. Permafrost is a good example of a place where multiple greens will annoy and sometimes even overwhelm you. You suggest conning a mob when using clouding to see if it is still aggrro. If it is, (and of course depending on the geometry of the place) you can drop clouding, lament the mob, then start clouding again greatly increasing your chances of slipping by.  The lament may be resisted but it is still an increased chance of avoiding a fight.  I have done this in lower Guk getting to the live castle from the upper Guk/live side zone line. With undead, the lament tactic alone may work although I haven't tested it.
[Kaearia] If you are Lamenting a group of mobs with the intention of eventually pulling them all, start by Lamenting the lowest level ones first.  This decreases the chances of Lament failing and everything charging at you.  By the time you get to Lamenting the higher level mobs, the lower ones are already Lamented.  If it should fail and the higher level mob(s) charge you, the lower level ones will usually not follow.
[Kaearia] Lament (and lull) work on wandering mobs.  If you can Lament it before it gets within aggro radius of you, often it will just walk by without attacking.
[Lundriel] Lament targeting takes place as soon as the song is selected not when the song completes. As soon as the song is started you can go ahead and target your next mob. This makes lamenting multiple targets much easier.

Cautions:

This song has no effect if the mob is already aggro'd on you.
Sometimes when a mob resists it will get upset, and he and all his friends (including successfully lulled friends)  will come to express their anger on you.  Sometimes when they resist they will just stand there and ignore you.
[Kaearia] The duration of this song seems to be random (although loosely based on your charisma and whether or not you used an instrument).  So after you finish Lamenting, don't count on everything still being lamented.  In other words, expect it to work, but be prepared for the worst.  :)  This also means you should fight your pull far far away from the camp, well out of the aggro radius of the Lamented mobs.
