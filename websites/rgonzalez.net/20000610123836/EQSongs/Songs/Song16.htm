TAREW's AQUATIC AYRE

Level: 16

Effect: Water Breathing

Skill: Wind

Target: Party

Location: High Hold

Does not stack with: N/A

Taunt: Nil-Low

Quantified: N/A

Analysis by Rokenn Swiftsong, Karana server.

Comments: This is the first continuous song that uses Wind Instruments like minotaur horns or flutes (minotaur horns are wind instruments. All other types of horns are usually brass). This song allows your entire party to breathe underwater.

Strategies:

Since this is your first wind based song expect to have a lot of trouble starting it up till you get your wind skill up.  Find a safe spot in town, start up the song with a Flute equipped and wait 3 to 4 hours, you should max out your wind skill in that time.
[Sartori] This song will not cancel out other water breathing spells/effects, making it a good back up in Kedge Keep.
[Kaearia] You will need your wind skill maxed for the invisibility song (level 19), so it makes sense to practice this song with a flute while waiting on the boat, practicing skills, etc.

Cautions:

[Offkee] If you are playing this song , the water stones and other water breathing effects will not work for you, you must end the song, hold your breath while using the stone or have water breathing cast on you.
The major drawback is that the range is too small in many cases. If your group plans on doing a major underwater excursion (Kedge Keep) this song should NOT be your only means of water breathing.
[Kaearia] The only advantage to playing this song with a flute is that it will increase your wind instrument skill.
