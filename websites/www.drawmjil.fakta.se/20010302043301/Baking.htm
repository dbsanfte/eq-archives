Why do you want to Bake? Well, there are a couple of answers to that. First of all, it's great fun to give new and exotic dishes to friends, and believe me, giving 'Gnome Kabobs' to a gnome can produce a GREAT reaction. Second, it's a rather cheap skill to become master in. Third, it is defiantly needed for a quest in Grobb.

Ogres really are the worst race (except for trolls -Chernabog's remark) when it comes down to trade skills, they lack very much in both INT and WIS, the first important for skill advancement, the second for skill success. So you should really not aim for a jeweler career or some other expensive trade skill.

Drawmjil had his own VERY good reasons to start out his baking career, which you can read all about in his diary (Dates October 17 - 19). I will try to describe here how Drawmjil got from skill 0 to skill 103 in a very short time, with a very limited budget and NO hunting for ingredients.

First of all, get around 15 practicing points to spend on baking, you can spend less, but that will mean more time and more money. Collect around 15-20 platinum pieces. Do NOT think that you will have the patience of fishing your way up to master chef, this is tedious enough for an Ogres just to mix everything about a 1000 times.

Ok, what we are going to concentrate on here are TWO dishes. First one is fish fillet. You will be making these until you are at skill level 82. Drawmjil also got a friend to buy a spit (portable oven) in Freeport, which increases the speed, since you don't have to run all the way to an oven. Start by putting all practice points you have into baking (I recommend 15, but it could be more or less depending on how much you are willing to spend).

First go to Bup, he is located in The ferrot at loc 1171, 122. Buy as many Jug of Sauce as you can carry. Then go to Chef Dooga, she has an in in the same place as the warrior guild is located. There you can buy fresh fish. If you have a spit you can start making fish fillets right here, otherwise there is an oven next to Crunga & Erung in an unmarked shop down the hall from armor merchants. To make a fish fillet you put one jug of sauce and one fish in spit/oven and press combine. Believe me, you WILL fail often, especially in the beginning. A tip, hold down LEFT-CTRL when picking up a stacked fish/sauce, that will make you pick up ONLY ONE, so you don't have to bother with the slider. Well, you just do that over and over until you reach skill 82, then fish fillets will become trivial. Sell the fillets to merchants, Dooga or Bup depending on where you are at the moment (you will run between them allot).

Ok, you are now at skill 82, that might be enough for you, if not (Drawmjil wanted to be above 100 in skill), you should now move on to Fish rolls. Same procedure, but instead of sauce, you mix one fish and one batwing in spit/oven. You might think that this will take allot of bat bashing, but there is a person who sells unlimited batwings in Oggok (lucky us Ogres). His name is Hrak and he is in the second room in the mountain when going to the Shaman guild in Oggok. Just repeat the procedure above and produce fish rolls. Fish rolls are not as profitable as fish fillets, which is the reason for making fillets all the way up to 82. If you have allot of money you can do fish rolls all the way, since you wont have to zone as much buying ingredients. Fish rolls can take you as high as 132 which should be QUITE enough. 

Well, that's it. Hope you can enjoy baking as much as I do, it really is fun, and now for the good part. The trolls have a quest called skeletons in the closet, which is to my knowledge the only quest in the game which requires cooking. What they need to complete the quest is something called a butchers apron. You get it by giving chef Dooga 10 gold and 3 pickled froglocks (1 froglock meat + 1 vinegar + 1 jug of sauce). The apron itself if a good newbie armor, and could be sold to troll shamans doing this quest.
