"I can't believe this is happening," the Halfling Druid muttered to herself as she tried to wring out the water from her clothes, "how many times does that make? Four? Four times I've fallen of that blasted boat." Katani muttered to herself some more then found a spot on the ground where she could sit in the warmth of the sun. Her companion, a Wood Elf Rogue, finally glanced her way and tried to stifle his laughter. He had purposely jumped overboard to try to save his friend when she fell into the Ocean of Tears, but by the time he had gotten to her the boat had sailed on without them.. too far for them to swim to.

Shadmel looked over his maps for a third time and narrowed his eyes. "There is no island in this location." Katani looked up at him confused, then looked around at her surroundings. "er, Shad? What do you call this place then, cause it sure looks like an island to me." She peered at him closely wondering if her friend had been knocked on the head during their swim from the boat to the shore. Shadmel sighed heavily, "I'll go look around some more, see if I can find anything." The rogue suddenly was gone, having hidden himself and preparing to sneak away "Just you wait one second!" Katani exclaimed as she scrambled to gather her belongings, "Don't you dare leave me alone here." Katani camouflaged herself and together she and Shadmel began exploring the island.

After several hours of searching the coastline, they finally made their way inland. Their supplies were being depleted and they needed to find shelter, food and water soon. Dusk soon came upon them as they finally found a small cave. They would have to wait till morning before venturing out again. As the settled in for the night a sudden burst of light appeared. They found a small tunnel that Shadmel had to duck for in order to make his way, but Katani could easily walk through so she took the lead.. eager to see what lay ahead. As she found herself nearing a large cavern she suddenly stopped and swallowed hard... afraid to even cast the spell to camouflage herself. Shadmel finally caught up with her and at the sight before him immediately hid himself. Then, seeing how Katani was exposed he stopped hiding and stood in front of his halfling friend. Too stunned to move, the druid and rogue could only stare up into the eyes of a huge silver dragon who had observed their every movement.

"All is well little ones. I wish you no harm." The Silver Dragon spoke, her voice gentle and kind. "I am only resting from a long journey. Come, sit with me a while and tell me of your world."

The soothing voice of the great dragon did much to sooth their fears, so Shadmel and Katani approached the Silver Dragon and told her the stories of Norrath. The legends, the gods, the quests and what could be found in the various parts of the world. The telling of their tales lasted several days, during which the Silver Dragon provided food and drink to sustain them. The great dragon seemed to take great interest in learning everything the two was sharing with her.... then finally, after three days, the Silver Dragon told them why she wanted to know.

"I come from another world, a world that has been destroyed. Only myself and a companion of mine live. At least, all that we know of. The Black Dragon, Nightwing, and myself, seek others of our kind. I had hoped to find some here, but this land is too vast and full of mysteries that I fear I can not search all before I must rejoin my companion who searches elsewhere."

"We can help you!" Katani cried, overcome by the sadness in the Silver Dragon's voice. "Yes, we will do all we can to help you!" Shadmel vowed with such conviction that the Silver Dragon looked at him closely. The rogue could see the scrutiny in her eyes. "Yes, I believe you would." The Silver Dragon knew Shadmel was a rogue, but she saw within his heart truth, goodness and honor.

The Silver Dragon spoke a few words and suddenly Katani was holding an ancient tome. "This that you hold, bears the history of my world up to it's last moments."

"And this... " she began as she started another spell "is a gift so that you can search even further."

Shadmel and Katani looked at each other, but did not understand what the gift was. "You will know in time what I have given you, as you read the histories of my world" The great Silver Dragon then cast another spell as she bid the two farewell.. "I will see you two again."

Suddenly, Shadmel and Katani found themselves on a boat heading for Greater Faydark... and to what they will call the Silver Dragon Clan.

A Few Weeks Later.....

Katani caressed the ancient leather bound tome respectfully then opened it's pages to read yet again of the Great Dragons. The words, she memorized every day, though she knew the entire tome by heart.

This day, she turned to the pages which told of the Silver Dragon's return to her world and found destruction..... an image of a crystal on one page caught her eye as it had many times before. She spoke the words which released it's magic and she watched again the scenes inside the crystal.

Two figures stood side by side... without speaking yet deep within each other's thoughts. "Gone...everything...gone" The female didn't notice a tear as it fell unchecked...emotion burned within her yet her face was a mask, devoid of the pain and heartache that still violently swirled inside her. "We are all that are left you and I... and we shall be the last of our kind" His mind did not betray any resentment towards her or himself for being unable to propagate. His Life mate had passed on and he could have no other to replace her. He had learned many lifetimes ago that nothing can be changed, you can only go forward. The female stirred beside him as she caught his last thoughts "Only go forward??? and What do we have to go forward to???" He felt the intense bitterness in her thoughts but remained a tower of strength.. she needed him now more than ever and he would do all he could to complete her teachings. "Teachings? and Why should we continue with the Teachings? There is NOTHING for us now.." She cast her eyes across the desolate planet....nothing lived in this wasteland, yet she could still see with her inner eye... the homeland of her family... "It is time... we must go" He gently urged her on....knowing that soon even they could not escape the same fate as the rest. Each felt within them a tiny flicker of cold and heat that sprouted out as it grew stronger. From the female, an aura of silver surrounded her then branched outward filling the area with a brightness that was blinding. Her companion became encompassed by a wide thickening cloud of darkness. As light and dark merged, a spectacular sight appeared as they became one and created a world of intense gray. Almost immediately the light and dark began receding and growing smaller and smaller till none of the light and darkness remained. In their stead was a silver dragon and a large black dragon. The silver female dragon sighed, a bitter sound that showed her frustration "There is nothing for us now, What are we to do??" "You will learn, as I said before you must continue your teachings, .and we shall add new ones for you to be able to exist among others" "Surely there are more like us?" She asked hopefully, yet she knew what his answer would be. "There may be similarities but none... in any dimension of the universe, are like us" His mention of dimensions reminded her of the last of her Teachings, she had just begun to travel to other worlds with her Th'ras'AK when they were sent word that their world was being destroyed. They had arrived too late to save any of her kindred. She looked back at their world, now destroyed, a last look before they would leave their home forever. They lingered... wanting to prolong this moment for as long as they could... they would never be back... and there was nothing to come back to. "We can open doorways into other worlds...can travel through dimensions... but can not erase what happened here... " She felt as though her body would crumble from the sadness within her. "Time has been... and shall always be our greatest enemy" He replied harshly... and she looked up with surprise...but he continued.... "The past is gone...our future shall never be... you know that you and I can not hope to continue our race. It is best that we move on... we can not change what has been..." "No! I will carry this within me till the dying breath leaves my body!" She declared as she promised herself that somehow she would continue the legacy of her race. "Our race is dead! There is nothing that even you and I together can do to change what has happened." He knew her power was strong... and his own was even stronger... and when combined they created an unequaled force of power...but this was beyond their powers... they could do nothing to change the past. "Time...as you said... Time is our greatest enemy... But someday " "Someday you will have moved on. You may keep the legacy of our race...but that is all you shall ever have" "Then in my heart and soul I vow that the legacy of our race... the history of our past shall remain within me... and for as long as that may be I shall embrace history for with it I shall someday find a way to control time... and someday I shall restore our future." Together, the male, Nightwind of the Black Clan from the Land of Shadows, and the female, Firesky of the Silver Clan from the Land of Moonlight, traveled through space and through many dimensions, searching for others like themselves. During their journeys they found many races who were destined to share the same fate as the home world of the Ancient Ones. Nightwind and Firesky vowed to save as many lives as possible, and swore they would return someday with the help they needed to protect them.

Katani closed the book as Shadmel approached. "You reading that again?" "No!" she muttered defensively. "Only watching this time." Shadmel spoke the words that he now understood.. the words which were the gift of the Silver Dragon that he had been able to decipher from the ancient tome.. Shadmel became Dillin and sat down beside Katani, put an arm around her shoulders and squeezed. "Their tale is sad.. I know.." but the Silver Dragon is out there. She asked of us a great and noble task which we shall endeavor to complete. If there be any of her kind alive, we shall find them. More and more join us.. more eyes and ears to learn of any dragons." Dillin hugged Katani and retrieved the book from her to hide it in it's special place. He, too, caressed it's cover gently, in reverence to the great mysteries and life of the Great Dragons.
