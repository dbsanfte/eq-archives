I.  Guild Name and Game Systems of Operation

The name of the guild that this charter establishes will be Silver Dragon Clan (SDC). Silver Dragon Clan will operate chapters on the following Online Role-Play Game systems / Servers:

A.  EverQuest (EQ)

      1.  Saryrn Server - Primary Server

      2.  Other servers may be added at a later date.

B.  Star Wars Galaxies (SWG)

      1.  Primary Server to be determined upon game release.

      2.  Other servers may be added at a later date.

II.  In Game Vs. Mundane Considerations

EverQuest and Star Wars Galaxies are online games that allow for the creation of fictional In-Game characters that are operated by a mundane player via a computer connection to the game system.

It is important to note, EverQuest and Star Wars Galaxies are not real life and are just games. As such Silver Dragon Clan is a guild comprised of In-Game characters, not the mundane player operating the characters, and is not intended to be a forum for mundane and / or personal issues.

While Silver Dragon Clan will take advantage of mundane resources and systems that allow a player to operate multiple characters, each character will be considered to be part of a family of characters controlled by the same player unless other wise noted. 

III.  Guild Goal / Mission

The overall goal of Silver Dragon Clan guild is to promote fun, adventure, excitement and enjoyment for all members of the guild.  To this end, the mission of Silver Dragon Clan will be to sponsor events, forums and other creative outlets for involvement by all members of the guild.  Members of Silver Dragon Clan will be united by this common goal and mission while following the policies and rules set forth in this charter.

IV.  Membership Qualifications and Application Process

A.  EverQuest Membership Qualifications

      To qualify for membership in an EverQuest Chapter, the prospective character must meet all of the following requirements:

1.  Must be a character on an EverQuest server where Silver Dragon Clan has an operating chapter. The character may be any class / race / deity combination available in the lands of Norrath.

2.  Must have achieved a minimum of 15th level / season, regardless of the level / season of any other family member character that may presently be a member of Silver Dragon Clan.

3.  Must agree to follow all guild policies as outlined in this charter and all other guild forums and resource systems, including respective End User License Agreements.

4.  If the player has other characters in Silver Dragon Clan, those characters must be active members in good standing.

5.  Must complete the Application Process outlined in this charter.

B.  EverQuest Application Process

      To be guild invited as an active member of Silver Dragon Clan, the prospective character must complete the following steps of the application process:

1.  Complete and submit a membership application, found in the "Seeker’s Hall" Section of the Silver Dragon Clan web site.

2.  Create and submit a character listing on Guild Magic web site for inclusion in the Silver Dragon Clan member roster. Creation of Guild Magic, free account is required if not previously created for another character.

3.  Create a message forum posting in the "Application / Joining Information" folder of the Silver Dragon Clan message forum stating intent to join the guild and that the character is seeking a sponsor. Include in the post the character name, class, race, level, past guild membership and any other information that should be known about the character. Creation of a free message board account is required if not previously created for another character.

4.  Begin the sponsorship period and complete any quests given in preparation for sponsorship, while awaiting a response to message board posting from a sponsor.

5.  Work in conjunction with a sponsor to complete sponsorship process.

C.  Star Wars Galaxies Membership Qualifications

      To be determined as game release date approaches.

D.  Star Wars Galaxies Application Process

      To be determined as game release date approaches.

V.  Sponsorship Process

Silver Dragon Clan members are united by the quest given by the Silver Dragon to seek others of her kind. The goal while undertaking this quest is to promote fun, adventure, excitement  and enjoyment among the members of Silver Dragon Clan.

As part of this quest and goal, members strive to work together to obtain the skills necessary to explore all areas of the game worlds where Silver Dragon Clan has a presence. In the process of working together, members of Silver Dragon Clan form a community of friends and kinship.

Silver Dragon Clan has established the sponsorship process to allow prospective and active members of Silver Dragon Clan to get to know each other. This period is also designed to allow active members to evaluate whether prospective members display qualities that will enhance the ranks of Silver Dragon Clan.

To begin the sponsorship process, prospective members must create a message forum posting as described in step three of the application process. This will serve as an introduction by the prospective member to the active membership of Silver Dragon Clan. The prospective member will next have to await a response to the message board posting by an active member that will agree to serve as a sponsor.

The posting will remain active on the message board for two weeks pending a response from a sponsor. If a response is not received during that time period, the posting will be removed from the message board. This should not be viewed as a rejection of the prospective member, but rather as an indication that the prospective member may need to spend some time befriending and getting to know the active members of Silver Dragon Clan. It is advisable that the prospective member knows a few active members very well and feels comfortable that one of those active members will be a sponsor prior to beginning the application process.

The message board post will be copied into the "Members’ Area" of the message forum for active members to discuss sponsor assignments. When the active members have had an opportunity to discuss sponsoring the prospective member (See Sponsor Requirements below), a sponsor will be selected and will post a response to the prospective member’s original posting.

During this time period the prospective member should take the opportunity to begin interacting with the active membership of Silver Dragon Clan. Also at this time the prospective member may also be given a quest to complete that will bring them in contact with other members of Silver Dragon Clan. This quest will not be so hard as to be unattainable at the prospective member's present level, but will require some assistance from active members of Silver Dragon Clan. This quest will be published on the message board and active members will be encouraged to assist.

Once an active member responds as a sponsor, the prospective member will be able to continue to fulfill the remaining requirements of the sponsorship process. At this time the prospective member will be given limited access to the Members' Area folders so that they may communicate with other members of the guild.

To allow the active members to further evaluate the prospective member’s acceptance as an active member, the prospective member, with the assistance of their sponsor, will participate in hunting groups, raids and guild events as listed below. These requirements must be met after a sponsor has been selected, previous interactions with active members prior to selection of a sponsor will not be considered retroactively.

A.  Participate in one large guild raid or event. The prospective member’s sponsor must accompany them at the raid or event for it to be counted towards the requirements. The sponsor must also post to the appropriate message topic in the Members' Area folders for the prospective member to receive credit for the raid attendance.

B.  Participate in at least two smaller guild hunt events or general hunting group. Regardless of the group type, the prospective member must have hunted with at least three different  active members for this requirement to be met. Each of the three active members must post to the appropriate message topic in the Members' Area folders for the prospective member to receive credit for participating in the hunting groups / events.

C.  Have a brief interview regarding membership and involvement in Silver Dragon Clan with one of the guild officers or guild leaders.  The time and location of this interview may be arranged via the guild message board or in game tells so as to be convenient for both the prospective member and the guild officer / leader. 

The requirements listed above are intended to provide a guideline of the minimum participation required, while more participation is not required, it is strongly encouraged. The main objective to be considered is the opportunity for the prospective member to interact with the highest possible number of active members of Silver Dragon Clan.

The sponsor and all other active members will provide feedback regarding interactions with the prospective member by posting to the appropriate message topic in the Members’ Area folders. The active members should also reflect their impressions of their interaction with the prospective member and their opinion of whether or not the prospective member should be promoted to active membership in Silver Dragon Clan. If further feedback and evaluation is required, the sponsor will be informed of the necessary, additional requirements.

It should also be noted that the prospective member, the sponsoring member and all active members contributing to meeting the requirements of the application process may not be from the same family of characters controlled by the same player in any way.

Once all the requirements of the sponsorship process are completed, the prospective member will be promoted to active membership in Silver Dragon Clan. Arrangements will also be made at this time for the member to be guild invited and receive the In-game guild tag.

After the character has been promoted to active membership they will serve a thirty-day probationary period. During this probationary period if the guild leadership determines it necessary, the character may be removed from Silver Dragon Clan without warning and further will not be required to honor any policies in this charter pertaining to an appeal or mediation.

VI  Sponsor Requirements.

To be considered as a possible sponsor, members of Silver Dragon Clan must meet the qualifications listed below:

A.  Be an active member in good standing and not on probation.

B.  Not be currently sponsoring another prospective member with any character member.

C.  Must be able to commit the time to serve as a sponsor and mentor to the prospective member.

D.  Aid the prospective member in fulfilling the requirements to attain active membership as outlined in the Sponsorship Process section of this charter.

E.  Act as a mentor to the prospective member by providing answers to questions, helping them to become familiar with Silver Dragon Clan and making the transition to active membership.

F.  Act as a liaison between the prospective member and other active members to coordinate completion of quest, hunt, raid and event requirements, as the prospective member will have limited access to the Members’ Area folders of the message board.

VII. EverQuest Membership Requirements

To maintain an active membership status, each character member must meet and maintain the requirements listed below.

A.  Each member will follow all rules and policies set forth in this charter and all other guild forums and systems.

1.  Members will follow all conditions as set forth in the Verant / EverQuest Terms of Service Agreement (EULA displayed while loading game program) and all other Terms of Service agreements of other systems used in conjunction with membership in Silver Dragon Clan (IE: Guild Magic, SDC Web Portal, Etc.).

2.  Members will follow and adhere to the provisions set forth in the Verant / EverQuest "Rules of Conduct", "Play Nice Policies - Activity within EverQuest" and "Message Board Conduct" policies, located on the main EverQuest web site (To view these policies in more detail - visit the web site by clicking  here). 

As there are certain sections of these policies that can be misinterpreted or can lead to problems / disputes between game characters, please note the following excerpted sections, the guild's interpretation notes and policy adjustments.

a)  Kill Stealing

1) Excerpt - "Kill Stealing is defined as the killing of an NPC for any reason that is already fighting or pursuing another player or group that is prepared to engage that same NPC without that group's specific permission."

2) Interpretation Note - Some players have varying definitions of what is a NPC - A NPC is any in-game character that is controlled by the game system and not another player. in-game monsters, commonly referred to as MOBs are also considered NPCs. While the policy can be interpreted as: "You should not deal the killing hit to the NPC / MOB without the group's specific permission", it should be noted that the character that damages the NPC / MOB the most receives the experience gain for killing the NPC / MOB. This can cause complications between characters.

3) Policy Adjustment - Silver Dragon Clan members shall not engage, cast on or melee any NPC / MOB that another character is preparing to fight or is actively fighting unless they are a part of the same group or have received specific permission to do so from the other character. Permission can include the person using the /yell command, using the in game channels (ooc, shout, etc.) to announce a train to the zone that they need assistance in killing. If you are not absolutely certain that the character is in need of assistance do not in any way engage the NPC / MOB.

b) Ninja looting

1) Excerpt - "Ninjalooting is defined as taking special equipment from a fallen creature without the express permission of those who killed the creature if they are in attendance."

2) Interpretation Note - Items in the game can be used for many purposes and as such have varying value to each character. What may be appear as an item to be sold to a vendor by you may be a required component of a quest or tradeskill of another character. Also the person that killed the NPC / MOB whose corpse is there in front of you may still be engaged by the remainder of the swarm that they are fighting.

3) Policy Adjustment - Silver Dragon Clan members shall not loot the corpse of any NPC / MOB that they or a member of their group did not kill unless given specific permission to do so from the character that killed the NPC / MOB.

c) Contested Spawns / "Camping"

1) Excerpt - "There are cases where two or more groups wish to kill the same NPC or hunt in the same area. In these cases, the groups are required to compromise.

If an equitable compromise cannot be reached between the players prior to EverQuest Customer Service Staff involvement, the EQCSR will mandate a compromise. Any such compromise is final and not open to debate. Refusing to abide by these terms will be considered disruption and may result in disciplinary action.

It is therefore strongly suggested that the groups make every attempt to reach a compromise that they can live with prior to involving an EQCSR, who may mandate a compromise that does not suit you to the extent that a player-devised compromise would.

Note: A "group" in this case is defined as a party of one or more characters that are united in a common belief or goal and are capable of completing that goal."

2) Interpretation Note - Many misunderstandings occur in EverQuest over this "Play Nice" policy and opinions vary greatly as to whether or not Verant allows "camping" of specific NPC / MOB spawns. Based on this policy and the 'kill stealing" policy mentioned above there arises the opportunity for many different interpretations. Based on the two policies it will be the guild's interpretation that Verant does not recognize or allow a character exclusive rights to claim a specific spawn but rather that characters compromise and share spawns. However many players do recognize the concept of "camping", therefore some communication will be involved regarding the contested spawn so that a compromise may be reached. Please see the policy adjustment below for Silver Dragon Clan's exact policy.

3) Policy Adjustment - If a character / group is hunting / "camping" a spawn site and is at that location prior to your arrival, then per the 'kill stealing" policy do not interfere with the character / group's hunting ground / "camp". The Silver Dragon Clan member should open a dialogue with the character / group as to what type of compromise may be reached. If the Silver Dragon Clan member is the character at the spawn site first then maintain your hunting ground / "camp", but be open to compromise. Because spawn site recycle times and size of the spawn site can vary greatly there is no set policy that can be defined here that will address this issue. The general Silver Dragon Clan policy will be to work with others to reach an equitable solution. If an equitable solution can not be reached then assistance should be sought from an EQCSR. Any solution mandated by the EQCSR will be followed by all Silver Dragon Clan members, regardless of their opinion of the mandated compromise. This policy is not intended to direct Silver Dragon Clan members to seek assistance from EQCSR staff in every incident that a solution can not be reached, common sense and courtesy should be a guiding factor in disputes of this type. It should also be noted that excessive requests for mediation by EQCSR staff can directly affect the reputation of the guild and the Silver Dragon Clan member and is addressed by other policies in this charter.

d) Foul language and abusive behavior

1) Excerpt - "Foul language is not permitted, in any language.

Excessive use of foul language in an inappropriate context, including swear words, real-world racial slurs, and other language that is not consistent with the fantasy environment and designed to hurt, will be considered a disruption. The existence of the filter (/filter) is not a license to be profane."

The guiding factor here is that if it is something most people would not say or do in front of their parents, grandparents or religious leader then it is best left unsaid or undone.

Excessive use of foul language, behavior or actions of an inappropriate context, including swear words, real-world racial slurs, and other language that is not consistent with the fantasy environment and designed to hurt, will be considered a disruption. The existence of the filter (/filter) is not a license to be profane. VIII.  Membership Hierarchy, Ranks and Titles

Silver Dragon Clan is structured on a rank hierarchy, and will recognize two ranks for each member. The first rank system will be based on the In-game rank structure of each game system and the second rank system will be based on membership in Silver Dragon Clan. Additionally certain ranks and guild positions will carry an associated title that will be bestowed upon members by the Dragon’s Council of Silver Dragon Clan.

A. EverQuest In game Ranks

EverQuest allows the In-game ranks and guild powers as listed below.

1. Member - The In-game rank of Member is the standard rank allowed for guild members by the EverQuest game interface. This rank allows characters to display the guild tag following their name above the In-game avatar to identify them as a member of the guild. Also it allows them access to the guild chat channel. The majority of Silver Dragon Clan members will be given this In-game rank.

2. Officer - The In-game rank of Officer is the next available rank allowed by the EverQuest game interface. In addition to the abilities allowed by the rank of Member, this rank allows control over adding and removing characters of the In-game rank of Member.

3. Leader - The In-game rank of Leader is the final available In-game rank allowed by the EverQuest game interface. This rank allows all the abilities of the In-game ranks of Member and Officer as well as control over adding and removing characters of the In-game rank of Officer.

Guild members of the In-game rank of Officer and Leader will have the ability to control membership in Silver Dragon Clan. While this ability is given by the game interface, members of these ranks will follow the Silver Dragon Clan policies in this charter in conjunction with exercising the abilities to control membership.

B.  Star Wars Galaxies In-game Ranks

To be determined upon game release.

C. Silver Dragon Clan Ranks

Silver Dragon Clan allows the guild ranks and associated level of achievement for each member as listed below.

1.  Dragon Spirit - This rank is bestowed by the Dragon’s Council upon previous members of the Silver Dragon Clan that are no longer able to travel with us and assist us in quest given to us by the Silver Dragon. This rank is honorary in nature and given at the Dragon Council’s discretion.

2.  Dragon Companion - This rank is bestowed by the Dragon’s Council upon those that are not currently members of the Silver Dragon Clan, but assist the members of the Silver Dragon Clan in their travels and the quest given to us by the Silver Dragon. This rank is honorary in nature and given at the Dragon Council’s discretion.

3.  Pending Follow Up - This rank is bestowed to those that are placed in a pending / inactive status while the Silver Dragon Clan is awaiting follow up from the character.

4.  Dragon Applicant - This rank is bestowed upon those that are currently beginning the application process to join the Silver Dragon Clan.

Members of this rank will not be allowed to participate in guild raids and events.
Members of this rank will not be allowed to receive assistance from the Silver Dragon Clan Armory, Library, Stores or Treasury.
Members of this rank will not display the guild tag, or hold any In-game guild rank.
Members of this rank will receive promotion to the rank of Dragon Recruit upon completion of the first portion of the Application Process.

5.  Dragon Recruit - This rank is bestowed upon those that are currently completing the sponsorship process prior to becoming an active member of the Silver Dragon Clan.

Members of this rank will be allowed to participate in all guild raids and events (based on raid / event level requirements and scheduling).
Members of this rank will not be allowed to receive assistance from the Silver Dragon Clan Armory, Library, Stores or Treasury.
Members of this rank will be required to maintain all accounts established as part of the application process.
Members of this rank will not display the guild tag, or hold any In-game guild rank.
Members of this rank will receive promotion to the rank of Dragon Initiate upon completion of the probationary period of sponsorship.

6.  Dragon Initiate - This is the first rank tier of the Silver Dragon Clan. It is bestowed upon those members that have completed all requirements to become an active member of the Silver Dragon Clan. 

Members of this rank will be allowed to participate in all guild raids and events (based on raid / event level requirements and scheduling).
Members of this rank will be allowed to receive assistance from the Silver Dragon Clan Armory, Library, Stores and Treasury as outlined in the Guild Vaults Policies section.
Members of this rank will be required to meet all membership requirements for their respective game system and maintain all accounts established as part of the application process.
Members of this rank will display the guild tag and hold the In-game rank of Member.
Members of this rank will receive promotion to higher guild ranks at the Dragon Council's discretion.

7.  Higher Guild Ranks - The second through eighth rank tiers will be bestowed at the Dragon Council's discretion. These ranks are bestowed upon those members that have earned promotion through their actions and deeds in the quest of the Silver Dragon while maintaining the requirements of an active member of the Silver Dragon Clan. The ranks and respective rank tiers are as follows:

Dragon Seeker (Second rank tier)
Dragon Apprentice (Third rank tier)
Dragon Acolyte (Fourth rank tier)
Dragon Defender (Fifth rank tier)
Dragon Disciple (Sixth rank tier)
Dragon Guardian (Seventh rank tier)
Dragon Priest / Dragon Priestess (Eighth rank tier)

Members of these ranks will be allowed to participate in all guild raids and events (based on raid / event level requirements and scheduling).
Members of these ranks will be allowed to receive assistance from the Silver Dragon Clan Armory, Library, Stores and Treasury as outlined in the Guild Vaults Policies section.
Members of these ranks will be required to meet all membership requirements for their respective game system and maintain all accounts established as part of the application process.
Members of these ranks will display the guild tag and hold the In-game rank of Member.
Members that achieve the eighth rank tier will receive promotion to the Dragon's Court at the Dragon Council's discretion. 

8.  Dragon’s Court - This is the ninth rank tier of the Silver Dragon Clan. It is bestowed upon those members that have earned promotion through their actions and deeds in the quest of the Silver Dragon while maintaining the requirements of an active member of the Silver Dragon Clan.

Members of this rank have earned the honor and privilege of having a special title bestowed upon them by the Dragon’s Council.
Members of this rank will be allowed to participate in all guild raids and events (based on raid / event level requirements and scheduling).
Members of this rank will be allowed to receive assistance from the Silver Dragon Clan Armory, Library, Stores and Treasury as outlined in the Guild Vaults Policies section.
Members of this rank will be required to meet all membership requirements for their respective game system and maintain all accounts established as part of the application process.
Members of this rank will display the guild tag and hold the In-game rank of Member.
The Dragon's Council may assign members of this rank special duties and / or responsibilities as a requirement of retaining this rank and associated title. 

9.  Dragon’s Council - This rank includes the Dragon Elders, Dragon Lords and Dragon Ladies of Silver Dragon Clan. These members will form the administrative council of Silver Dragon Clan. 

Members of this rank will be allowed to participate in all guild raids and events (based on raid / event level requirements and scheduling).
Members of this rank will be allowed to receive assistance from the Silver Dragon Clan Armory, Library, Stores and Treasury as outlined in the Guild Vaults Policies section.
Members of this rank will be required to meet all membership requirements for their respective game system and maintain all accounts established as part of the application process.
Members of this rank will display the guild tag and hold the In-game rank of Member.

D.  Silver Dragon Clan Titles / Guild Positions

Silver Dragon Clan allows the guild titles and associated positions for specific members as listed below.

1.  Special Titles - Special titles will be bestowed by the Dragon’s Council to those members that have achieved the Silver Dragon Clan rank of Dragon’s Court. Members of the Dragon's Court may request a special title upon achieving the ninth rank tier. The Dragon's Council will consider the requested title and make the final decision as to whether the title will be bestowed. IX.  General Guidelines and Rules

The following are the original guidelines and rules of Silver Dragon Clan. While they may be addressed in other sections of this charter, they are included here to maintain the original concepts and beliefs by which the guild was founded. The individual items in this section will be superceded by all other sections of this charter should a conflict in policy need to be resolved.

1) Role-playing is strongly encouraged.

2) Guild members should aid each other as often as reasonably possible. This includes helping each other with corpse runs, acquiring items, porting, resuscitating, and grouping together.

3) No Begging from other players or members for aid, whether it be items, money, ports, resurrections or help in leveling. Begging is highly frowned upon, especially begging from those who generously give of their time to help others anyway.

4) No kill stealing. You may loot only your own (or your party’s) kills. Members should attack only those mobs that are not currently in conflict with another character. This does not apply if the other character is a member of your party or if the person is requesting assistance.

5) Members will control their pulls at all times. If you or your party must run from a fight pulling a mob train, you should attempt to warn others of pending train through use of the appropriate chat channel such as /shout. You should also take a route which will not endanger others along your path of escape.

6) No looting during battles. Make sure all of your party members are safe before you take loot. Also you should follow any party or raid looting agreements and procedures.

7) No inappropriate language over chat channels (i.e.: Auction, Shout, OOC, Guild, Etc.) , in deference to other players and Verant policies.

8) If guild members want to be anonymous in EverQuest, they are required to use the /role command, not the /anonymous command, so that they will show up on guild who lists.

9) Alts (alternate characters) are allowed in the guild at any level. Players are also welcome to join Dragon Companion Guilds on other Servers.

10) Auctioning items to guild members is allowed, it is preferred that a discount be given to guild mates whenever possible, but is not required.

11) No PvP characters in the guild at this time. (At a later date we may allow PvP characters for Guild Wars or specific guild events)

12) All Guild Members are to encourage fun, excitement and adventure for everyone!

13) Guild Members will follow all Verant policies, including the established Play Nice Policy.

14) Guild Members will adhere to the following Guild Golden Rules:

A) At all times let your actions honor the Silver Dragon.

B) Guild Members shall not undertake any action that would be detrimental or harassing to another player in the lands of Norrath.

C) Guild Members shall take no action that will reduce the honor or reputation of their own or the guild’s name.

X.  Guild Families

While Silver Dragon Clan will take advantage of mundane resources and systems that allow a player to operate multiple characters, each character will be considered to be part of a family of characters controlled by the same player unless other wise noted.

A.  A family will be comprised of a minimum of one character and a maximum number of characters to be arranged between the player and the guild leaders (the guild leaders reserve the ability to limit the total number of characters a player can consider to be part of one family).  If a player chooses to have a family of two or more characters, the player will designate one character as the main character to be considered the leader of the family, all other members of that family will be considered alternate characters. 

B.  The actions of each member of a family will affect and impact the status of all of the members of that family of characters, unless otherwise noted. Therefore each family member will not be required to maintain guild requirements separately but rather as an overall family effort. By the same token if the actions of a family member warrant disciplinary action such as probationary status, all members of that family will be affected and be considered to be on probation.

The overall goal of Silver Dragon Clan guild is to promote fun, adventure, excitement and enjoyment for all members of the guild.  To this end, the mission of Silver Dragon Clan will be to sponsor events, forums and other creative outlets for involvement by all members of the guild.  Members of Silver Dragon Clan will be united by this common goal and mission.

4.  Find the hidden guild member.

XIII.  Guild Loot Policy

Silver Dragon Clan will recognize the following policies as they apply to looting corpses. These policies are not intended to supercede any other agreements that guild members may agree to as part of being in a group. Guild members should follow these policies if no other agreement is in effect.

A.  When hunting in a group, regardless of whether the group is all guild members or a mix of guild and non-guild members.

1.  Members will not loot any corpse that was not killed by themselves or another member of their hunting party.

2.  Members will not loot any corpse while any member of their hunting party is fighting.

3.  Members will share loot equally and fairly so that all members of their hunting party have the opportunity to profit. 

Some examples of looting styles can include:

a)  Appointing a primary looter that will loot all money and at a break will divide the money equally between party members using the /split command. Looter will turn of autosplitting.
b)  Loot items by rotating the loot order alphabetically by party members' names. 

4.  Members will use the /random 100 command (character receiving highest number) to determine who will receive items sought by more than one member of their hunting party.

The overall consideration in looting is that all hunting party members receive the opportunity to profit from the available loot dropped by MOBs killed.

B.  When hunting at a guild hosted raid / hunt the following loot system is the preferred method:

Silver Dragon Clan will follow a "Need before Greed" philosophy. This means that equipment / armor / quest items will be distributed to the event members based on specific order of precedence and benefit. The precedence and benefit order and distribution method is as follows:

1.  Loot gathering method:

a)  Each raid / hunt group will have a designated looter. Looter will turn off autosplitting
b)  All loot from corpses killed will be looted only by the designated group looter.
c)  Items that will be distributed during the loot distribution phase will be reported to the raid / hunt host as they are looted.

2.  Loot distribution method:

a)  All money looted will be distributed to the members of the specific hunting group members using the /split command.
b)  All loot items will be given to the raid / hunt host for distribution.
c)  All consumed spell components (gems, etc.) will be distributed to the raid attendees in the following manner:

1)  Raid attendees that were present that used their own supplies for spells, receive 25% to replenish their supplies.
2)  Dragon QuarterMasters receive 25% for guild vaults.
3)  Group looting the components receive 50%.

d)  All loot will be distributed by the raid / hunt host to all raid / hunt attendees using the highest roll of a /random 100 to determine the recipient.
e)  All loot will be distributed in the following order of precedence, based on item stats (All attendees will receive items before moving to the next level of precedence):

1)  Raid / hunt attendees of that class / race / etc. that were in attendance and hunting at the raid / hunt that needs the item.
2)  Raid / hunt attendees that have an alternate character that supported the raid / hunt in some way that needs the item.
3)  Raid / hunt attendees that have an alternate character that did not attend / support the raid in any way that needs the item.
4)  Dragon QuarterMaster seeking the item for the guild vaults.
5)  Raid / hunt attendees that can not use the item but plan to sell the item.

3.  Planning notes - The raid / hunt host will coordinate the possible loot from the raid / hunt and all requests for loot in advance of the raid / hunt as part of their preparation of the raid / hunt and post the information as part of the raid / hunt announcement. This will allow a clear understanding by all raid / hunt attendees and reduce confusion during the raid / hunt itself.

XIV.  Power Leveling  / Low Skills Policies

Silver Dragon Clan recognizes that players do engage in power leveling of characters.  While Silver Dragon Clan will not prohibit this practice, guild members will be expected to follow this policy.

Power leveling through questing will leave the character lacking in many needed skills such as melee or casting abilities. If a member engages in power leveling they will make sure that their skills are brought up to a sufficient level as to not be detrimental to other members of their hunting party. 

If a member's skills are not sufficient for their current overall level due to power leveling or lack of practicing that skill then they will not participate in any guild events requiring them to fight, until such time as their skills are sufficient. (ex: If the character is a level 30 druid but their melee and / or casting abilities are those of a level 10 character due to power leveling, then they will not participate in any guild event that will require them to melee.)

Also it is understood that some skills are not kept even with other melee and casting skills. Such as keeping one handed slashing maxed while not raising two handed blunt. Members will not use any weapon that they are lacking the skills to use, during guild raids / hunts unless all other members of the group are warned in advance and agree to allow them to do so. They will also be prepared to switch back to their most proficient weapon at any time so as not to endanger the other members of their hunting party. (ex: The member is a level 30 druid with a one hand slashing skill of a 10th level character and two handed blunt skill of 28th level, then they will use two handed blunt while fighting unless all members of their party agree that it is ok for them to try to raise one handed slashing skill).

The main consideration is that members with inadequate skills do not endanger other members of their hunting group.

XV.  Honor System

As is the case of the of the internet and online role-playing games it is very easy to remain anonymous or to hide certain information regarding themselves. Silver Dragon Clan acknowledges this ability and will foster an atmosphere of privacy of personal facts between all members. However, part of administering the guild requires that certain information be disclosed per the policies set forth in this charter. Silver Dragon Clan will expect all members to honestly represent themselves to the guild and its' members. 

Due to the nature of the game systems and internet facilities used in administering the guild there is no easy way to double check the honesty of all the information that people may convey regarding themselves or their characters. Silver Dragon Clan will accept the information provided by members based on the honor system. Information provided and conveyed by members will be considered to be accurate and honestly provided.

Further as is the case in any game there are times when there are incompatibilities between different players and characters that lead to situations where conflicts between characters arise. Silver Dragon Clan is understanding and open to providing characters another chance if the circumstance of the situation allow. There are however some situations where this is not possible, leading to the association ending and a parting of the paths occurs. This sometimes is an amicable separation and sometimes is not so amicable. 

It is understood that some players take advantage of the ability to hide their identity by creating a new character to escape or hide a bad reputation they may have gained with a previous character. 

Silver Dragon Clan understands that all of the above mentioned situations occur and prefer to trust players are honestly representing themselves, however Silver Dragon Clan reserve the right and ability to remove any member that it discovers to have misrepresented themselves.

XVI.  Disciplinary Actions

All members of Silver Dragon Clan will be expected to follow the rules and policies set forth in this charter. Also members are expected to follow the rules and agreements associated with the game systems and outside resources. Should a member not do so, the need for disciplinary action may arise. This section will govern the manner in which Silver Dragon Clan will conduct disciplinary actions.

If a situation arises where disciplinary action must be considered, the members of the Dragon's Council will  review the information of all parties to make a decision if disciplinary action need be taken. The preferred method will be for the council to meet in an in-game chat channel to review the information from all parties. All council members directly involved in the situation under review will be removed from participation in the decision making process. Further the Dragon Elders will limit their participation to coordinating the discussion and moderating the chat channel. Upon reviewing all available information the council will make a decision on the matter and if necessary will decide on the appropriate action to be taken as punishment.

The Dragon Elders will serve as the appeals council in the matter. The Dragon Elders will have the authority to overturn or adjust the decision of the Dragon's Council at their discretion.

Disciplinary actions may include but are not limited to the following and will affect all characters in the member's family of characters.:

A.  Demotion in guild rank.

B.  Removal of guild title.

C.  Probation status

D. Guild removal

XVII.  Leaving the Guild

Silver Dragon Clan recognizes that there may arise the situation where members will leave the guild. Members may choose to do so either voluntarily or involuntarily.

A.  Voluntary removal from Silver Dragon Clan

Silver Dragon Clan understands that members may choose to leave the guild for various reasons. If a member decides to remove themselves from the guild, they will need to inform the Dragon Elders prior to using the /guildremove command.

The Dragon Elders would like to be aware of why members choose to leave to determine if there is an area of dissatisfaction that may need to be addressed. This also allows the member to maintain an amicable relationship with the guild as there may be other members of their family of characters that they would like to keep in Silver Dragon Clan.

From past experience, the Dragon Elders have found that members choosing to leave Silver Dragon Clan for another guild, often find that they wish to return. By discussing the matter with the Dragon Elders arrangements can be made for a leave of absence if necessary so that guild ranks may be maintained.

Dependent on the circumstance surrounding the members decision to leave Silver Dragon Clan, the Dragon Elders reserve the right to require the member to remove all characters of their family from Silver Dragon Clan.

If a member chooses not to notify the Dragon Elders that they are removing a character then they may proceed in using the /guildremove command, however they will be expected to do so for all members of their family of characters. If they do not remove all characters, then any remaining characters in Silver Dragon Clan are subject to guild removal with out warning or notice.

Unless otherwise informed by the Dragon Elders, any members leaving Silver Dragon Clan will lose any and all guild titles, ranks and status. If the character then chooses to rejoin Silver Dragon Clan, they will be required to complete the application process and sponsorship period. Further the Dragon Elders reserve the right require additional tasks of the previous member as a condition of their application process.

B.  Involuntary removal from Silver Dragon Clan

As a result of disciplinary action a member may be required to /guildremove their family of characters for a specified period of time or permanently.

If it is determined that a member must do so, then the member is expected to carry out the /guildremove command for each member of their family of characters under the supervision of a member of the Dragon's Council. If the member refuses to honor this requirement and members of the Dragon's Council are required to issue the /guildremove command then that member will forfeit the ability to rejoin Silver Dragon Clan with any character in the future.

XVIII.  Amendments to this Charter

Silver Dragon Clan is in a constant state of growth and change. As such the need will arise for certain policies of the guild to be changed. 

The charter will always be accessible on the guild web site for viewing and review. Once the changes are made notification will be posted to guild members on the guild message board. The changes will go into effect upon posting, however a grace period of seven days will be allowed to give members an opportunity to become familiar the new requirements.
