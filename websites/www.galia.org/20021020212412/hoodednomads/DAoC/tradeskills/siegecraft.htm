This page is a result of my on-going explorations and testing with the siege warfare elements. Please note that these are all via the "purchase method" of obtaining the components. Unfortunately I am not that skillful yet in creating these components.

Main siegecraft information was gathered from: http://support.darkageofcamelot.com/guides/siegecraft/, which I found via the Dark Age of Camelot Tradeskills site

Basic Information

Level requirement: Lvl 1 in Siegecrafting - there is a misconception in popular belief, you need to be high level in different tradeskills to create the "components" of the siege equipment. However, once you have all the components ready, you only need to be lvl 1 in siegecrafting to create the siege equipment. I believe the higher in Siegecrafting you are, the faster you make the siege equipment.
Where to buy components: unfortunately, the only place I've found this is at the furthest frontier keep, which in Midgard is at Bledmeer Faste. Now I think, if you control the similar keep in Albion or Hibernia, then you can get siege components there too, once the vendors are available.
Commands: currently, there seems to be a bug once you create the siege equipment. You are automatically in control, however the control box doesn't pop up. Type "/control release" to remove control, and then target and type "/control" to take control of the siege equipment. A control box will pop up with the following commands:
ARM - click this right away to get your siege equipment ready
LOAD - this command is only for ammo using equipment (ballistas and catapults), grab and drop the bolt/stone onto the siege equipment, THEN click LOAD to load the ammo. NOTE: don't drop more than one piece of ammo onto the equipment, unfortunately at the moment you cannot pre-load them, although it looks like you may able to do it in the future.
AIM - click on a target you want to hit, you can use F5 to target ground (move ground targetting by holding the F5 key and using UP and DOWN arrow keys) or directly selecting a target such as the keep door or enemy players, THEN click AIM to position your equipment.
FIRE - after the appropriate amount of secs, sometimes around 5 secs or so, click FIRE to launch your ammo or battering ram. Immediately click on ARM to get ready for the next round.
/repair - the siege equipment will normally decay in time, and will also receive damage when hit by enemy players. Use this command to repair it. Currently, you don't need wood to repair the siege equipment, BUT there is a limit as to how many time you can repair the equipment. I usually wait till it's around 1/4 health when I use this command. It takes less than 10 secs for me to fully repair the siege equipment back to full health. However, I am not too sure on who can repair this, since this is a weapon. I currently am lvl 245 in weaponcrafting, and I read somewhere that you have to be around lvl 300+ to repair the weapon. Try it out and let me know what you find out.
Movement: YOU CAN'T!!! Please make note of that. Once you deploy siege equipment, that's where they stand. So special care and planning need to be made when picking a spot to deploy at. And don't forget to have people defending your newly created equipment. Trust me, you will be attracting a lot of attention.
