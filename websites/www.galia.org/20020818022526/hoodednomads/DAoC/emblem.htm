Xocomil 10 2 16 8 After some deliberation and discussion w/ Zerahemla, I've come to my decision on the guild emblem. For the pattern I like the V shape mainly because it takes the emphasis off of the colors and places it on the symbol itself. Colors were a difficult decision. I like reds and blues, but I don't want nomads to become sniper bait because of their shields and capes. Any advantage we can take in the frontier should be pursued. For this reason, I chose darker colors. For the primary color I like the 16. It can symbolize our mystery as the Hooded part of our name does. Shadows are usually shades of grey and rarely ever black. As a Nomad, I see myself as being mysterious, but not to the point of darkness... For the secondary color, I chose 8. Purple has always signified courage and valor. All Nomads show great courage and valor in battle. One thing I always notice is that Nomads are the last to flee. It was obvious last night in the frontier who the Nomads were helping people in danger even though our own party was in danger from another pull. Waiting for our healer to get free when we knew we were dead. I watched our little hunter buddy flee many times when things looked grim. As Nomads, we stand until the last man is free, even at the cost of our own life. This is why I chose the purple. The symbol was tough. I wanted to choose an eagle or the eagles wings because they would be a symbol of our nobility, but then I thought more about it and settled on symbol 10, the wolf's head. Thinking about wolves I can draw quite a few parallels to our guild. A wolf is a careful hunter. Carefully choosing his prey and bringing it down with thought. A wolf is equally dangerous by himself or in a pack, but prefers to fight in a pack to add to his strengths. Wolves can be heard calling to each other to find out where they are and what they are doing. Finally, in mythology, wolves are feared by their enemies and respected by their allies. I think this symbolizes us as a guild. I tried to choose something that would be nice to look at while at the same time giving us something symbolic to rally around. There were other choices that I considered because they looked cool, but in the end, I think what will be more important is what our symbol stands for. It will give us a common link rather than some cool symbol that attracts people looking for something pretty to wear w/ their armor.
