9-11-02

It’s been a year. Not a day or night has gone by that I haven’t thought about that terrible day last September. I felt lost because I didn’t understand why someone would want to cause such pain. I’ve spent a large amount of time in the past year following politics, viewing the many, many hate websites, and feeling helpless as a nameless nobody who can’t change a thing. In the meantime our world continues spinning, people keep doing horrendous things to others. Has anything really changed?

As a show of remembrance I asked my fellow gamers to write down how this past year has changed for them individually. I wasn’t interested in what the celeb of the week, or some political bigwig on the tube had to say. I wanted to hear from my friends, the regular joes of our world.

Needless to say I didn’t get much of a response. Maybe it is still too painful to put into words. Maybe with the TV saturation nobody really had anything left to say. I was disappointed until I received an email from Cendor. He had taken the time to open his world to the rest of us. Thank you Cendor, sometimes it just takes one to make a difference J

Cynder

Cendor’s Words

2001 was not a good year for me at all. My father passed away in April. My mother had 2 back surgeries. My aunt had heart surgery and I had 2 high school friends commit suicide on top of the September 11th events. On September 11th I had actually stayed home sick from work and woke up to the news talking about the twin towers. I was half-asleep when I looked at the television and seen the second plane hitting. All sorts of emotions went running through my head. Shock, confusion and horror were the biggest ones.

I didn’t know what to do or who to call being as most people were at work that I knew so I logged into Everquest to talk to some of my online friends about what had been happening. Of course all the zones I was in people were talking about it in ooc, shout, and say chat channels. You name it! So I was talking to some friends in my current guild at the time and come to find out about a half dozen of our guildmates lived in the very vacinity that these events were happening so everyone was waiting to hear from them to make sure they were safe. So back and forth I went from the computer to the TV just waiting for news on what was going on. As the day went on our friends and family all began getting together not knowing what to expect. Once it was released it was terrorist and possibly middle east involvement that’s when things commercially in the area started to go crazy. People were flocking to grocery stores and gas station (which decided to raise their gas prices to over $6.00/gal). Luckily I had just gotten groceries and filled up my jeep so didn’t have to deal with all that. As the days went on it really impressed and pleased me just how the US citizens all over were responding to helping with donation and with labor not to mention just a general increased friendly attitude to one another.

 Time has passed now in the last 12 months and most people that can have gotten back to their everyday life. I think everyone has changed at least a little bit. I had quit playing games 4 to 12 hours a day and started getting outside and enjoying what I use to, just the peace and quite of the woods. I’ve gotten back into hiking and biking which has not only made me happier but I’ve been getting in much better health again as well. I plan to continue this trend and use online gaming more as a hobby again than as a second career. Just because Dragon's Bane s online that does not mean I don't think of you all as dear friends. We form bonds both in games and out in our family/community and no matter what game or guild I might currently be in. I will always from this day on consider myself a DB member. And since I’m not currently gaming with any of you I just want you all to know I do miss that part of the experience dearly.

Cendor

Stay safe and stay sane J
