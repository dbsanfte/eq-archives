QUAKE: End of Dragon's Bane Quake Team| by Sandman
Dragon's Bane does not run any Q3 teams any more. As far as I know, very few members even play Quake any more. Membership started to wane about a year ago, leaving the clan with only a handful of active players. Attempts to merge with other clans, recruit new players, and jump-start players with sporadic activity all failed. The last of what was left of DB Quake life fell out of existence about 6 months ago. It is hopeful that if Doom3 is ever released (maybe in another year), then it will spark an interest amoung DB players, but no one knows for sure. It's been a good run, but the time has come to close downt the DB Quake teams formally.

Time to get Organized| by Burk
My fellow DB members, are you tired of pick-up groups at DL leaving you without a bard or healer? Ever finally get a group, go out to emain and get rolled, just to have half your group quit? Even better, quit to join their guildies? Well, I say it's time we get organized for some serious RvR action. I'd like to see DB get build a RvR team that is:

1. Willing to meet at least once or twice a week. I'd like to see us do it as often as possible, but I think 1-2 times a week is a great start.

2. Made up of the right level 50 class mix. I dont mean to sound abrasive, but a group of 3 lvl 44 NS, 1 lvl 38 bard, 2 lvl 47 rangers etc just doesn't cut it for a good RvR group. I'd like to see lvl 50 toons across the board. If you're not level 50, and are seriously interested, we can discuss how to best get to 50 (we have a couple chanters, dont we :p)

Buff bots will provide buffs to group from DL or crunchy etc.

3. Well organized
Everyone needs to understand his/her role in the group. I'm no RvR guru, but I do know this. 90% of the problems I have while in pick up groups involve 8 people that each have their own game plans.

4. Quitters not invited
I expect to get rolled. I expect many of us wont have purge, and a nice AOE mez will be the death of us a time or two. That's life - especially when fighting RR5+ mids/albs. I expect for my "one of many plans of attack" plan to flat out suck. I expect for myself and my guldies to make mistakes, and make them often. What I dont expect is for our group to get upset and quit. This is a learning experience, and if you really, really, really suck at RvR, there's nowhere to go but up :p

We have the best guild in Hib, imho. You will find no better people. RvR should be a fun aspect of the game, but we should play to win, and maximize our ability to do so. I think organiziation is a key to success in RvR, and without it, (with the exception of some of our exceptional stealthers), we are forever bound to the 1 in 15 shot of a decent RvR group. My ranger, Burk, has racked up 40K RPs in a single night while playing in a balanced group. Other nights, we just go out to get rolled while other groups mop up on RPs.

I really see potential for a DB RvR group. We can have great fun, doing everything from venturing to Dodens as a group and clearing the dungeon for giggles to hit and run ganking in emain.
