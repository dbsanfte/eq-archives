Demons Inside

I think everyone has his or her demons. Finding an appropriate outlet for one of my own has actually been quite easy J

I try to be nice to everyone I come in contact with. I find it hard to say what I feel when dealing with someone that bugs me. In the past this has gotten me into some tight spots. To an icky person my niceness deems me a niave rube or some one too stupid for my own good. I haven’t changed my niceness but I have seriously reduced the people I come in contact with.

I think one of my demons is a direct result of feeling I always have to be nice. This demon shows up in a wicked form whenever I get a phone call from a telemarketer. I have tried to do other things to avoid these countless wasted calls but in the end I guess I was driven to the dark side.

Side note: Caller ID doesn’t always work to screen out these stupid sales calls if you occasionally get long distance calls that you want to pick up but show up as an unknown caller or information unavailable.

First I tried to be nice. My first mistake J.  Try telling one of those callers right off the bat that you don’t want to waste their time and that you never buy anything anyway. They may hang up but then they just give your number over to a higher-powered sales person who then calls you back over and over and over.

Second I changed my phone number. Not such a hot idea considering the replacement phone number was previously listed to someone who had obviously changed his or her phone number for the same reason I did. Now instead of getting telemarketers who were asking for me I was getting just as many calls for the Shandlers. The funny thing was even when the caller was informed they had the wrong household it didn’t matter and they started into the sales monologue barely missing a beat.

Third I changed my answering machine message to inform those calling for the Shandlers that “this was no longer their number” and “no, I wasn’t interested”. This did not solve the problem as most people who get an answering machine rarely listen to what is being said and betray their ignorance by leaving a message for the very people which they had just been informed are no longer at the number they dialed.

Forth I invested in the “Telezapper” http://www.telezapper.com , a little device that fools computer calls with a tone that makes the computer think the number it called is disconnected. Your number is supposedly taken off the active list and no more calls. This works amazingly well but does nothing for those companies who still have their sales reps manually dial. So even though my calls have been drastically reduced a few still trickle through.

My defining moment or the day my demon was unleashed I had answered the phone and gotten a sales lady asking for, yes, you guessed it, the Shandlers. This is how the call went.

_______________________________________________________________________

Sales Twit : Hello. Is this the Shandling residence?

Me : I’m sorry. You have the wrong number.

Sales Twit : Did I dial  ***-****?

Me: Yes you did but you are calling the wrong number.

Sales Twit : But then that would be the right number.

Me : (big sigh) No you didn’t dial the right number. You said you were calling for the Shandlers. This is not their number so you are dialing the wrong number if you wish to speak to the Shandlers.

Sales Twit : Okaaaaaaaaaaaaaaay.

_____________________________________________________________________

I think it was the tone on her last word that sent me over the brink. Twas at this point I decided I was going to waste the time of every telemarketer who called my house starting with this one. Poor woman J. Maybe it was repressed wickedness or maybe too much Jerky Boys and Crank Yankers. I couldn’t help myself.

_____________________________________________________________________

(continued)

Me : This is a private line to a medical facility.

Sales Twit : What medical facility?

Me :  A private medical facility. The nurses don’t want us to answer the phone but we do sometimes anyway. (I then make meowing/snorting sounds into the reciever).

Sales Twit: (total silence).

Me : Are you there? I have boogers. Do you want to make pants-pie with me?

Sales Twit : (click).

_____________________________________________________________________

That was how it began. Now I look forward to the very few calls I still get every once in a while. I have found that there is actually great fun in tormenting telemarketers. Doing the crazy person is fun but I have also done the little old lady who has to talk about all her ailments (wonderful time waster) or the little boy who wanders off to get mom and keeps coming back to the phone to tell the caller to keep hanging on.

For those of you in the same boat who don’t have the time to waste wasting the telemarketers time there is another option. It’s an organization called Private Citizen, Inc. http://www.private-citizen.com . It is a nation wide organization that for a minimal yearly subscription adds your name and number to a multitude of telemarketing Do-Not-Call lists. After you inform a company that they are not to call your number they are bound by law not to. If they do call you after being told not to you can sue them. Private Citizen has done this on various occasions and gotten settlements believe it or not.

I think I will hold off on the Private Citizen option though. Releasing my demons is much better fun J.

Stay safe and stay sane (most of the time anyway)
