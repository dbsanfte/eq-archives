The Road to Hell


We all know the cliche about the road to hell. (If you don't, you really need to get out more)

Some interesting dialogues have taken place in the past week in response to my last column. "Interesting" is an interesting word. There's an ancient Chinese curse: "May you live in interesting times." Sounds relatively innocuous, but it's still a curse. If only we could all be so polite in our
disagreements and yes, hostilities.

I wish to repeat that this column was never intended for anything but good. Doesn't mean it turned out that way, alas. So anyhow, I started down this road. It's very hot here where I've ended up.

The following is an excerpt from a letter received here at QQ HQ.

"Over the past few weeks I've noticed a trend developing on Quellious Quarters. Particular threads are developing a masked (and sometimes blatant) negative and hostile tone. Some remain open for now, some have been locked. What's interesting is that the majority of posts that have required a lock were in direct response to an editorial written. Guaranteed, none of the posts began with a staff member initiating the message, and most of your staff have made the attempt to minimize the possibility of a flame war, but the fact remains that if the editorial hadn't been written, there would have been no reason for so many to passionately respond to it. It's hardly commendable that one attempts to stop a fight after they were the ones who (whether with conscious intent or not) initiated it. In regards to the non-editorial based threads, I'm left curious as to whether your posters now believe because of the editorials, there is an allowance to vent their own issues publicly.

Masked anonymity is a dangerous thing. It's been written in rebuttal that no names, no guilds, no groups of people were ever mentioned. One could easily take the editorials written as fictional, merely providing and provoking scenarios for each of us to reflect on. Very true, very honorable. However, that's not the case. What is written about is not fictional. They are actual events involving actual people on Quellious. There are in excess of 400 members on your board. If those 400 members had only 10 friends they communicated with, that would be 4000 people who are potentially having a dialogue about the posts and issues being discussed on Quellious Quarters. Over double of what our current server population is. It's naive to believe that because names aren't being mentioned, that it's acceptable and people aren't being affected by what's written.

This is a fact I can validate. I was a direct topic of one of the editorials written. After a few days of its posting, I received a number of tells from people who should not have known of this particular issue, who were not involved, and who had no true business knowing.

When there are three sides to every story (trite cliche, but valid), it's a difficult thing when one entity of an issue has an open and public reign to express their view. I can completely understand those who posted in response, revealing themselves from anonymity, because the anonymity wasn't really there to begin with; not in whole. No one argues because they believe they are wrong. Whether right or wrong, people fight because they feel justified in their beliefs, their issues. Most people, when provoked, will defend themselves. How they elect to do so is a personal choice."

This letter drew me up short. Never in my life had I ever been damned with faint praise to such devastating effect. The calm, reasonable tone got through to me where irate bluster bounced off my armor.

Indeed, as the letter writer says, how people respond when provoked is a personal choice. I made my choice. I chose wrongly. Perhaps I underestimated how much other people talk in-game about their private conversations with others. Perhaps I underestimated how many people read this drivel I spew. Perhaps I thought people like this controversial/confrontational stuff. After all, millions of people watch that crap Jerry Springer puts on TV every day.

What I never intended was for things to turn out the way they did. What I envisioned when I wrote my last column was pretty much this: me, knowing what it was about, one other individual knowing what it was about, and twelve people reading it and thinking "what's that all about?"

Never in my wildest dreams/nightmares did I imagine such a public outcry over this. Thin disguise of no names being mentioned or not, the fact remains that only two people were directly involved in the incident that inspired my last column. One of them (me) aired my feelings on it here, as
many read. I never once imagined so many people would be able to lock onto it and know exactly what it was about. So obviously the other person told a lot of people, whether before or after my column was posted, it doesn't matter. That was not a result I ever envisioned. Don't get me wrong, I'm not meaning to imply that the person I disagreed with was wrong to tell anyone else. It simply never occured to me that they would.

I'm a human being and far from perfect. Due to my background I have in me a very strong grain of "I don't start fights, I finish them." In this case it was a fight that never should have been fought. The term "Pyrrhic victory" comes to mind.

And so today, here and now, I use my public forum to abase myself before my readers. I was wrong. I won't admit to being wholely wrong, because I still feel I was not. However, I was wrong to base an editorial off one single event that was so specific as to be so easily identified. That was an abuse of my position. I really had no idea how widely read this was. And that, my friends, is as close to an apology as I can make. I shall be more careful in the future. If indeed there is a future for Soapbox.

Tamas
