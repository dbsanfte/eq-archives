No Experience Necessary

Argenbright Security has immediate openings for:
Pre-Departure Screeners $12/hr
We offer excellent benefits including:
Health-care Package, Vacation/Holiday Pay.
Must be 18 yrs old with a complete verifiable background check, and pre-employment drug screen.
College students, retirees/Senior citizens encouraged to apply.
No exp. necessary, training provided.

The ad listed above is a current one for Denver Airport as of November 6th, 2001. I called Argenbright and the only changes that had been made was the age requirement was lifted to 21 and proof of a high school diploma. That was it. No requirement to have a P.E.R. card (Permanent Employee Registration Card) that is applied for under the Private Detective, Private Alarm, and Private Security Act of 1983. Needless to say I wasn’t encouraged. Perhaps the “training” they are providing isn’t sufficient. Talk about understatement.

I think everyone has heard about the incident about the Nepal forestry student (with an expired visa) who tried to bring a number of knives among other things aboard a United flight leaving from O’Hare Airport this past weekend. Apparently probation for the company entrusted with the security for our skies has not instilled the same concern (which should have been already present) for safety that ordinary citizens whom are not trained or paid have shown in the last 2 months. It was just dumb luck that the young man (now detained) was searched a second time before he got on his United flight and the additional 7 (out of 9) knives, mace, and stun gun were discovered. After the horror of September I don’t care what his excuse or intentions were and neither should anyone else.

What do you do when a company gives you bad service? How many 2nd, 3rd, and 4th chances do you give them? Personally (and I do take it personally) I don’t give second chances. Screw me once, shame on you. Screw me twice, shame on me. Our government has decided to leave the security of our airways to private companies. Fine, I can deal with that but why not give the private company whom is repeatedly screwing up the boot for good and completely. I’m sure there are other private security companies standing in line for these contracts and would be ready and able to provide the service they are hired for. It is plain to see (especially after September 11th) that Argenbright Security is not doing all it needs to and most likely never will.

We, as Americans, have been told that it is up to all of us to protect our country. I agree with this. It is not only the responsibility of our authority figures such as the police, FBI, and armed forces but all of us. Argenbright needs to take a walk and we, as citizens, need to make our opinion heard loud and clear. For those politicians who support the current security company in question need to be remembered next time you cast your vote. The airlines want us to fly again. The government wants us to fly again. Hell, even the President wants us to fly again (he told me so, okay, not personally but he did say that). Want us to fly again? Then make it safe. No probation’s, no excuses, no room for error. We just can’t risk it anymore.

It only takes a small amount of common sense to figure out what are attractive targets to any terrorists wanting to create a big bang. Our nations busiest airport located within miles of our nations tallest building, Hellooooooooooooo! Even I, a lowly nobody haus-frau, figured that one out.  I would have expected O’Hare to be one of the most secure airports in America especially after the tragedies in New York and Washington D.C. I guess I was wrong. Somehow I don’t think I’m the only one.

My last column (Fright TV) I talked about the “at risk” factor. You might be asking yourself why would I be personally concerned about this since I don’t travel. Your right, I don’t travel but my family and friends do. My 11 year old son is coming home later this month for Thanksgiving vacation. He is scheduled to fly United. I miss him terribly but I want him to be safe. Do I feel lucky? Should I even roll the dice?

Stay safe and stay sane J

Cynder
