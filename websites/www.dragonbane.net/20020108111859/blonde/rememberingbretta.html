Remembering Bretta

One year ago, June 12, I learned that one of my oldest friends had died. As I sit here I wonder if the person or persons who were with her that night are thinking about her…

I think about her every day. That’s a sad thought if only for the reason I should have done so when she was alive, instead of so frequently after her death. We were the same age and met when we were 13 or so. I remember sitting with her one night talking about how old we would be in the year 2000, and what we would be doing. When your young it’s easy to believe that death is afar off distant future.

As we got older and traveled different roads we would occasionally run into each other at the oddest times and most unexpected places. We would always lose touch again as old friends do, only to meet up again a week, a month, or a year later like we had just seen each other the day before. It’s sort of spooky when I think back on those times, she usually breezed into my life just when I needed a friend the most. She made me laugh when the thought of smiling was inconceivable.  She taught me not to take myself too seriously without even knowing it.

I won’t see her again. A year later and I still have to tell myself that. I blame the absences that peppered our friendship. I still expect to see her someday just around the next corner. I have had a lot of my friends die but whether it was due to suicide (majority), accidents or sickness there was a finality to their deaths.  There are still so many unanswered questions about Bretta's death, too many to close the book.

Brettas’ body was found in a wooded residential area partially unclothed. She had been there for approximately a week before being discovered by a local walking his dog. An autopsy report stated she died due to a heart defect facilitated by the use of cocaine on the night she died. The police reports added she did not die where her body was found.

Some might say that “she used drugs she took the risk”. I’m cool with that, it’s true,… BUT the thought that gets me is Bretta died or was dying as the person or persons she was with decided that it was easier to dump her body like yesterday's garbage than to try and seek medical care.  If the government needs a new warning campaign strategy against drugs, this would be it. If the drugs your doing don’t kill ya, the cowardly assholes your with probably will…

Bretta was a human being. She was my friend. She didn’t deserve the indignity of her death.
