DAOC: Lowdown on Perfector ML3 Ability |by Vercinorix
I just did some testing of this with my BM and druid... druid was out of group. Looks pretty cool!

Sphere of Rejuvenation
Casting time: 15 sec
Recast Timer: 5 min
Power Cost: 20% power
Duration: 10 min
Radius of Effect: 250, no drop off
Effect: On a level 50 druid with 36+12 effective regrowth healed between 72-121 hits per tick, ticks every 5 seconds. Healing happens whether you are in combat or not, and apparently doesn't make a diff as to how much is healed. When I stood in the effect while fighting, my out of group druid got exp from the fight, so it looks like aggro will be generated from it. The message you get is: You are healed for X amount of hits by <name of caster of the sphere>.

The visual effect created is a downward spiral of stars on each pulse... the spell creates a fixed node on the ground which looks identical to the housing hookpoints. Please also be aware that multiple Spheres WILL stack effects. I haven't had a chance yet to see whether the sphere will also heal pets.

Von's Nightshade Tips for DAOC| by Vonhinten
Well... here's some of the general rules and processes I use when I'm out RVRing:

- Use /colornames so that all enemy stealthers pop into view the red named. This has made a large impact in my own playing, as I no longer have to _read_ a name to find out if I should engage, but instead just identify a color. It's much less work for your brain, and doesn't waste those fractions of seconds deciding what to do. Those seconds matter a great deal if you run into more than 1 enemy stealther. Your brain can instantly identify multiple enemies with red names, and you can choose what to do from there.

- Strafe during your fights, timed between swings so you don't lose rounds. This is a good way to avoid the mercfiltrator's Dual Shadow's style (front positional, big damage), and also makes it tough for the enemies friends to sneak in and PA you, mid fight. If you're in a fight that's looking favorable for you, and you see another enemy stealther pop into view, quickly break away from the fight so their PA misses, then pop AP (if you have it), and go back to fighting the same guy. Optionally, if you have a piercer in your other mainhand with a DOT on it, switch to it, and smack the other stealther once to apply it, then switch back to the first stealther. The DOT will tick on the 2nd stealther while you're fighting the first one,
and when it comes time to switch to the 2nd stealther, you'll already have a good 300 - 400 damage on him.

- Always keep your enemies in your evade area. You can strafe, you can back up to pull them to your front, but never let them get outside your evade area. You'll live a lot longer in fights where you've got multiple enemies on you if you're able to evade.

- Use Snow Shower (CD back positional) if you have it. This style snares an enemy, like garrote, only it uses less endurance, and does a reasonable amount of damage.

- If you sprint to catch someone, then manage to snare them, turn sprint off! So much wasted endurance before I realized that if you're turned sprint on, even moving at snared speed, it's taking sprint endurance!

- Always use positionals when you can. If you backstab someone, follow Thigh Cut up with wasp's sting, hornet's sting.. if they keep running, use Snow Shower to snare them.. positionals use less endurance, and do more damage typically than anytimes.

- Don't stand close to AMG doors if you can help it, and especially not when Albs are approaching. Enemies like to AOE the doors with dots and mez, and can ruin your day if you're near. Nothing's worse than being AOE Mezed in front of the door and then getting PA'd by an enemy stealther as he comes
through. If you get caught in this scenario.. don't wait to see if you'll live.. purge and run to hide. You might get caught by TS, but if you're sneaky you'll live.

- Carry lots of endurance and health potions. Get used to using them, minimizing the time it takes to stop combat, hit the potion, then start combat again. The least you should expect to lose is one round of combat.

- Use AF charges. I personally have an AF proc on my offhand, but since I've slowed down in my playing, it's more beneficial to use charges. These make a pretty big impact on encounters with tough infs.

- Be conservative with purge. Use Purge only to save your life, not to help you get RP's. If you go after a minstrel and you get stunned and/or mezzed and the minstrel takes off, unless you fear there's another stealther near that will take you out, don't bother purging just to catch up to them. It's not worth it.

- Disease has a snare component.. If you're diseased and trying to run away, it might be wise to purge the disease. Even with the small snare that this has, it's enough to get you caught. Get into the habit of checking your buffs region when you're trying to run away, and note whether you're diseased. It's really a judgement call whether you feel you're far enough away to not have to purge it, but be aware of it anyway.

- If a pet class is chasing you, try to lead the pet through agro mobs. If you're lucky, mobs will agro the pet and slow it down. It will also put the enemy in combat mode, and they'll lose speed. Sprint away if you can.

- Don't try to kill the following classes, unless you expect to die: Bonedancer, Spiritmaster, Savage, Bezerker, any hammer spec (2H or polearm), Friar.

- I use str/con on mainhand, dot on offhand, and dot on 2nd mainhand. I don't use 3 poisons per fight. I save the 2nd mainhand for long fights or multiple enemies. If you know you're about to fight multiple enemies, put a str/con on the 2nd mainhand, and be sure to hit the 2nd target with it early. This will allow you to last longer, as the target's damage output will be lessened.
